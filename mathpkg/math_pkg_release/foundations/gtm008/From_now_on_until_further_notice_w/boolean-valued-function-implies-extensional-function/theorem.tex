Let $u, v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ be definite and $f \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ such that $\llbracket f: u \rightarrow v \rrbracket = 1$. Define $\varphi: \mathcal{D}(u) \rightarrow \mathcal{D}(v)$ by the condition

$$\llbracket \varphi(x) = y \rrbracket = \llbracket \langle x, y \rangle \in f \rrbracket$$

for $x \in \mathcal{D}(u), y \in \mathcal{D}(v)$. Then $\varphi$ is extensional and $(\forall x \in \mathcal{D}(u))[\llbracket f(x) = \varphi(x) \rrbracket = 1]$.
