This theorem establishes the crucial correspondence between external extensional functions on definite sets and internal Boolean-valued functions. Given a definite domain $u$ and a definite codomain $v$, any extensional function $\varphi$ between their domains can be lifted to a Boolean-valued function $f$ satisfying $\llbracket f: u \rightarrow v \rrbracket = 1$ and agreeing with $\varphi$ pointwise with Boolean value $1$. This is fundamental for constructing functions in the Boolean-valued universe.
