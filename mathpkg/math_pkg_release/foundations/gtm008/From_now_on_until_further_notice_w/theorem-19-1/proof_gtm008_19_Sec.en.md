(By the method of forcing.) Let $M$ be a countable transitive model of $ZF$ such that $\langle M, \in, B^M \rangle$ is elementary equivalent to $\langle V, \in, B \rangle$, let $G$ be $P$-generic over $M$ (where $P$ is the partial order structure in $M$ associated with $B^M$). Then, by Theorem 18.2

$$P(\omega)^M \subseteq P(\omega)^M[G].$$

However, if $M[G]$ satisfies $V = L$, then $M[G] = M$. This is a contradiction. Therefore $M[G] \models V \neq L$ and hence $[V \neq L] = 1$ in $V^{(B)}$.

Exercise. Give a direct proof of Theorem 19.1 in $V^{(B)}$.

Remark. We shall now present new proofs of the independence of certain axioms from the axioms of $ZF + AC$ by using the models $V^{(B)}$ rather than the technique of forcing.
