An extensional function is a function $g: d \to V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ defined on a subset $d$ of the Boolean-valued universe that respects Boolean-valued equality: if $x$ and $x'$ are equal with Boolean truth value $\llbracket x = x' \rrbracket$, then $g(x)$ and $g(x')$ are at least as equal. Extensional functions are the "real" functions that correspond to functions in the sense of $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ when going from definite sets to definite sets (Theorem 16.8). This notion is crucial because functions in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ behave extensionally.
