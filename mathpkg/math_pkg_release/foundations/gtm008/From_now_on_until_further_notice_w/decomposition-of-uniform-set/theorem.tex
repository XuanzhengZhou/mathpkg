Let $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ be uniform. Define $v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ by
$$\mathcal{D}(v) = \{y \mid y \in \mathcal{D}(u) \land u(y) = \sup(u)\}$$
and $(\forall y \in \mathcal{D}(v))\; v(y) = 1$. Let $b = \sup(u)$. Then
$$\llbracket u = b \cdot v \rrbracket = 1.$$
Thus every uniform set is equal (with truth value $1$) to a scalar multiple of a definite uniform set.
