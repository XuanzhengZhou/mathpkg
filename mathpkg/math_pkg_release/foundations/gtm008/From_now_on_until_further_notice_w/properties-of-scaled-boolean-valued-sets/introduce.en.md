This exercise establishes the basic properties of scalar multiplication $b \cdot u$ in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$. Part 1 shows that membership in a scaled set $b \cdot u$ is equivalent to the conjunction of $b$ with membership in $u$ (Boolean truth: $b \cdot \llbracket v \in u \rrbracket$). Part 2 gives a formula for Boolean-valued equality of scaled sets: $\llbracket b \cdot u = b \cdot v \rrbracket$ equals $\neg b$ (the case when scaling kills both sides) plus $\llbracket u = v \rrbracket$ (when the scaling factor $b$ holds). These identities are used throughout the chapter, particularly in the proofs of Theorems 16.22 and 16.20.
