The Maximum Principle is arguably the most important theorem of Boolean-valued set theory. It states that existential quantifiers in the Boolean-valued universe always admit concrete witnesses: for any formula $\varphi$, the truth value of $(\exists u)\varphi(u)$ is attained by some specific $v$. This eliminates the gap between "it is true that there exists" and "there exists a witness," which is central to the Boolean-valued approach to forcing. Corollary 16.3 provides the technical form needed for constructions: given a partition of unity and a family of candidate witnesses, one can mix them into a single witness that matches each candidate with the corresponding Boolean weight.
