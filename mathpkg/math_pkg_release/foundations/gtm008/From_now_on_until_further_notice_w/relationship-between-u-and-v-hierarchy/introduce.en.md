Theorem 16.6 establishes the essential equivalence between the two Boolean-valued hierarchies $U^{(\mathbf{B})}$ and $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$. Statement 1 asserts that every $u \in U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is Boolean-valued-equal to some $v \in V_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$. Statement 2 provides the converse. Statement 3 notes that $U_{\alpha+1}^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is always complete, a property that does not hold automatically for $V_{\alpha+1}^{(\mathbf{B})}$. This completeness makes the $U^{(\mathbf{B})}$ hierarchy technically convenient, as it follows from Corollary 16.3 applied with $d = U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$.
