Theorem 16.28 is the converse of Theorem 16.8. While Theorem 16.8 shows that any real extensional function $\varphi$ between domains of definite sets gives rise to an internal function $f$ in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$, Theorem 16.28 goes the other direction: given an internal function $f$ in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ with $\llbracket f: u \to v \rrbracket = 1$ where $u$ and $v$ are definite and uniform, there exists a real extensional function $\varphi: \mathcal{D}(u) \to \mathcal{D}(v)$ such that $\llbracket f(x) = \varphi(x) \rrbracket = 1$ for all $x \in \mathcal{D}(u)$. Together, these two theorems establish a complete correspondence between real extensional functions and internal Boolean-valued functions between definite uniform sets.
