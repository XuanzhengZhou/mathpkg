First, by Theorem 16.11, we may assume $u$ is extensional (replace $u$ by an extensional set that is Boolean-valued-equal to it). Then by Theorem 16.20 applied to the extensional set (which is uniform by the property $u(x) = \llbracket x \in u \rrbracket$ for extensional sets), there exist $b = \sup(u) \in B$ and a definite uniform $v$ such that $\llbracket u = b \cdot v \rrbracket = 1$.

More precisely, after making $u$ extensional, one verifies that extensional sets satisfy $u(x) = \llbracket x \in u \rrbracket$, which implies $u$ is uniform. Then Theorem 16.20 gives the decomposition directly.
