The supremum $\sup(u)$ of a Boolean-valued set $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is defined as the join (Boolean sum) of all values $u(x)$ for $x \in \mathcal{D}(u)$. This element of the complete Boolean algebra $B$ captures the "total truth" of membership across the domain of $u$. The supremum plays a key role in the decomposition theory of Boolean-valued sets: it provides a scalar $b \in B$ such that $u$ can be factored as $b \cdot v$ where $v$ is definite and uniform (Corollary 16.21). The supremum also relates to the Boolean truth of $u = 0$ via $\llbracket u = 0 \rrbracket = \neg \sup(u)$.
