This theorem establishes that the $U_\alpha^{(B)}$ and $V_\alpha^{(B)}$ hierarchies are essentially equivalent. Every element of $U_\alpha^{(B)}$ can be matched with an equal element in $V_\alpha^{(B)}$ with Boolean value $1$, and vice versa. Moreover, $U_{\alpha+1}^{(B)}$ has the advantage of being complete, meaning that all Boolean mixtures of its elements are contained within it. This completeness property makes the $U$ hierarchy technically convenient for certain constructions.
