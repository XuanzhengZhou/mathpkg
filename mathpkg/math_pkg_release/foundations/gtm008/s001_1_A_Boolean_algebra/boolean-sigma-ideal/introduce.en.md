A Boolean $\sigma$-ideal is a Boolean ideal that is closed under countable suprema. Whenever a countable collection of elements belongs to the ideal $I$, their join (supremum) must also lie in $I$. This property generalizes the notion of a $\sigma$-ideal in measure theory to the algebraic framework of Boolean algebras, and is essential for the construction of quotient $\sigma$-algebras.
