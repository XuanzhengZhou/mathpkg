This theorem establishes that the Boolean truth values of bounded formulas (formulas whose quantifiers are all of the restricted form $\exists x \in y$ or $\forall x \in y$) are invariant under passing from a complete Boolean algebra $B$ to a larger complete Boolean algebra $B'$ extending it. This absoluteness property for bounded formulas is crucial for independence proofs, as it ensures that the truth of such formulas does not depend on which ambient complete Boolean algebra is chosen.
