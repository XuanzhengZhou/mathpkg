This theorem establishes the fundamental properties of the canonical embedding $y \mapsto \check{y}$. Parts 1 and 2 show that membership and equality between standard sets are reflected as Boolean truth values exactly: the Boolean value is $1$ when the relation holds in $V$ and $0$ otherwise. Part 3 shows that within $V^{(2)}$ (the Boolean-valued universe over the two-element algebra), every element is equal (with value $1$) to the check of a unique standard set $v$. Thus $V$ is faithfully represented in $V^{(2)}$.
