The canonical embedding (or "check" map) $y \mapsto \check{y}$ embeds the standard set-theoretic universe $V$ into the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$. Each standard set $y$ is represented as the constant function with value $1$ on the domain $\{\check{x} \mid x \in y\}$. This embedding lives in $V^{(2)}$, the Boolean-valued universe over the minimal complete Boolean algebra, and hence in $V^{(B)}$ for any $B$. The check map allows us to interpret statements about standard sets within the Boolean-valued model.
