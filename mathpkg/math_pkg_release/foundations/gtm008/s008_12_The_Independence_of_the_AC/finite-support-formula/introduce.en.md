Every formula $\varphi$ in the ramified language has only finitely many "relevant" indices from the countable index set. For any formula, term, and condition, there exists a natural number $m$ such that any permutation of $\omega$ that fixes the first $m$ indices pointwise also fixes the formula, term, and condition. This finite support property is what makes symmetry-based independence arguments possible in the forcing construction for negating the Axiom of Choice.
