When $B$ is a complete subalgebra of $B'$, the Boolean-valued universe over $B$ naturally embeds into that over $B'$. Moreover, the Boolean truth values of atomic formulas computed in $B$ agree with those computed in the larger algebra $B'$, since the Boolean operations (sum and product) over a subset of $B'$ yield the same results in both algebras. This embedding property is fundamental for relating different Boolean-valued models.
