$$\prod_{m < \omega} \sum_{n < \omega} b_{nm} = \sum_{f \in \omega^{\omega}} \prod_{n \in \omega} \sum_{m \leq f(n)} b_{nm}.$$

Similarly, if $\omega_{\alpha}$ is not cofinal with $\omega$, B satisfies the $(\omega, \omega_{\alpha})$-weak distributive law iff for every family $\{b_{n\xi} \mid n \in \omega \land \xi < \omega_{\alpha}\} \subseteq B$

1. $$\prod_{n < \omega} \sum_{\xi < \omega_{\alpha}} b_{n\xi} = \sum_{f \in \omega_{\alpha}^{\omega}} \prod_{n \in \omega} \sum_{\xi \leq f(n)} b_{n\xi}.$$

Remark. If $cf(\omega_{\alpha}) > \omega$, the right-hand side of 1 is equal to

$$\sum_{n < \omega_{\alpha}} \prod_{n < \omega} \sum_{\xi \leq n} b_{n\xi}.$$
