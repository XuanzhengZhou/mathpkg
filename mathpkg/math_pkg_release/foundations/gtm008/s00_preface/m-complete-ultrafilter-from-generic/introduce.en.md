This theorem (spanning Theorems 2.15 and 2.16 in the text) establishes the crucial link between generic sets and $M$-complete ultrafilters. Given a $P$-generic set $G$ over $M$, one can construct a proper $M$-complete ultrafilter $F$ on the Boolean algebra $B^M$ of regular open sets. The construction is $F = \{b \in |\mathbf{B}| \cap M \mid b = b^{-0} \land b \cap G \neq 0\}$. This $M$-completeness is essential for the Boolean-valued model to satisfy the axioms of ZF.
