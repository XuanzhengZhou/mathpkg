The notion of $M$-completeness relativizes completeness to a standard transitive model $M$ of ZF set theory. A Boolean algebra is $M$-complete if suprema exist for all subsets that belong to $M$. Similarly, an $M$-complete homomorphism preserves suprema and infima of subsets in $M$, and an $M$-complete ideal is closed under suprema of its subsets that lie in $M$. This relativization is crucial for forcing constructions where the ground model $M$ does not contain all subsets of the Boolean algebra.
