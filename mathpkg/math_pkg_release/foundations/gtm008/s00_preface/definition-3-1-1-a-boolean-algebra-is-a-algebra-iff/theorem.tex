1. A Boolean algebra $B$ is a $\sigma$-algebra iff

$$(\forall A \subseteq |B|) \left[ \bar{A} = \omega \rightarrow \sum_{a \in A} a \in |B| \land \prod_{a \in A} a \in |B| \right]$$

2. A Boolean ideal $I$ is a $\sigma$-ideal iff

$$(\forall A \subseteq I) \left[ \bar{A} = \omega \rightarrow \sum_{a \in A} a \in I \right]$$

3. A homomorphism $f$ on a Boolean $\sigma$-algebra $B$ is a $\sigma$-homomorphism iff

$$(\forall A \subseteq |B|) \left[ \bar{A} = \omega \rightarrow f\left(\sum_{a \in A} a\right) = \sum_{a \in A} f(a) \land f\left(\prod_{a \in A} a\right) = \prod_{a \in A} f(a) \right]$$
