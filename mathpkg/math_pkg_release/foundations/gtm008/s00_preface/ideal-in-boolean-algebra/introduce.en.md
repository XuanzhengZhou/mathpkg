An ideal in a Boolean algebra is a nonempty subset closed under addition and downward-closed under multiplication (if $a \in I$, then $ab \in I$ for any $b$). Ideals correspond to the kernels of Boolean homomorphisms and are the algebraic counterpart of filters. The classification into proper, principal, and trivial ideals is fundamental for the quotient construction and the study of maximal ideals.
