$I$ is an \text{ideal} in the Boolean algebra $B$ iff $I \subseteq |B|$ and:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $0 \in I$.
\item $a, b \in I \rightarrow a + b \in I$.
\item $a \in I \land b \in |B| \rightarrow ab \in I$.
\end{enumerate}
An ideal $I$ is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a \text{proper ideal} iff $1 \notin I$,
\item a \text{principal ideal} iff $(\exists b \in |B|)[I = [b]]$,
\item a \text{trivial ideal} iff $I = \{0\}$.
\end{enumerate}
