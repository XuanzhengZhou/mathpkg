This theorem shows that intersecting a complete Boolean algebra with a standard transitive model $M$ of ZF yields an $M$-complete Boolean algebra. The operations and constants, being absolute for transitive models, restrict properly. For any subset $A \subseteq |B| \cap M$ with $A \in M$, completeness of $B$ combined with $M$ being a model of ZF ensures the supremum belongs to $M \cap |B|$.
