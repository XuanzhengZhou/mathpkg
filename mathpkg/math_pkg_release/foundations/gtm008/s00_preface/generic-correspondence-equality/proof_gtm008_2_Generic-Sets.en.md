**Proof.** If $p \in G$, then $[p]^{-0} \cap G \neq 0$ (since $p \in [p]^{-0} \cap G$). Therefore $[p]^{-0} \in F$, so $p \in G'$. Thus $G \subseteq G'$. By Theorem 2.17, $G'$ is $P$-generic over $M$, and by Theorem 2.18, $G'$ is maximally compatible. Since $G$ is also maximally compatible (Theorem 2.18), we must have $G = G'$.
