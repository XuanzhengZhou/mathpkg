When the ground model $M$ is a standard transitive model of ZF containing the partial order $P$, generic sets over $M$ automatically satisfy a strengthened form of genericity. The strong sense requires that for any $a, b \in G$, there exists $c \in G$ with $c \leq a$ and $c \leq b$ (the set is directed downward). This theorem is important because it ensures generic filters have the finite intersection property needed for the forcing construction.
