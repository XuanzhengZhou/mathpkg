This theorem establishes the reverse direction of the bijective correspondence. Starting from an $M$-complete ultrafilter $F$, constructing the generic set $G$, and then reconstructing a filter $F'$ from $G$, one recovers the original $F$. The proof shows $F' \subseteq F$ by noting that elements of $F'$ lie above some $[p]^{-0} \in F$, and then equality follows because both are ultrafilters (and hence maximal).
