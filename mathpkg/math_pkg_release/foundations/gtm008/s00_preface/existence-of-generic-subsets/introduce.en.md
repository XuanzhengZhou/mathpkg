This theorem asserts the existence of generic sets when the family $A$ of dense sets is countable. Starting from any element $a \in P$, one constructs a descending sequence by successively meeting each dense set in the countable enumeration, then takes the upward closure. This is a constructive existence result (using countable choice) that does not require the full axiom of choice and is a precursor to the Rasiowa-Sikorski theorem.
