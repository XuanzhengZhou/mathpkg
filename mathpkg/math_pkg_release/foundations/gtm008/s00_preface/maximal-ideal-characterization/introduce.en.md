A proper ideal $I$ is maximal (cannot be properly extended to another proper ideal) if and only if for every element $b$, exactly one of $b$ and $-b$ belongs to $I$. This property is the ideal-theoretic counterpart of the ultrafilter property. Maximal ideals correspond precisely to the kernels of homomorphisms onto the 2-element Boolean algebra.
