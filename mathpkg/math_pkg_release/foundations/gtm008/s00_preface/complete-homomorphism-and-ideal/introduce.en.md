A homomorphism between complete Boolean algebras is called complete if it preserves arbitrary suprema and infima, not just finite ones. Similarly, an ideal is complete if it is closed under arbitrary suprema of its subsets. These notions are essential when working with Boolean-valued models where the completeness of the algebra guarantees the existence of truth values for quantified statements.
