This theorem establishes that the natural order on a Boolean algebra endows it with a lattice structure: $0$ is the least element, $1$ is the greatest element, $ab$ is the greatest lower bound of $a$ and $b$, and $a+b$ is the least upper bound. These lattice properties are fundamental to the study of complete Boolean algebras.
