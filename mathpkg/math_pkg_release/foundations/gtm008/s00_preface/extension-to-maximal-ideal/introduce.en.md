This is the Boolean-algebraic analog of the Boolean prime ideal theorem (which is equivalent to the axiom of choice). Every proper ideal can be extended to a maximal ideal. The proof uses a transfinite construction along a well-ordering of the Boolean algebra, adding elements that do not cause the ideal to become improper. This theorem is essential for the construction of ultrafilters and generic sets.
