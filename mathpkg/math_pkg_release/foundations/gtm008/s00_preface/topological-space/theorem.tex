The structure $\langle X, T \rangle$ is a \text{topological space} iff $X \neq 0$ and:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T \subseteq \mathcal{P}(X) \land 0 \in T \land X \in T$.
\item $A \subseteq T \rightarrow \bigcup (A) \in T$.
\item $(\forall N, N' \in T)[N \cap N' \in T]$.
\end{enumerate}
$T$ is a \text{topology} on $X$ iff $\langle X, T \rangle$ is a topological space. If $a \in X$ and $N \in T$ then $N$ is a \text{neighborhood} of $a$ iff $a \in N$.
