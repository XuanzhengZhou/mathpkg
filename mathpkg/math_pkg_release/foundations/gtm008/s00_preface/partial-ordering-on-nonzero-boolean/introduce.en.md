This theorem states that removing the zero element from a Boolean algebra yields a partial order structure (since $0 \leq x$ for all $x$, the natural order restricted to $B_0$ remains a partial order). Moreover, two nonzero elements have disjoint downward closures exactly when their product is zero. This condition connects the order-topological notion of disjointness with the algebraic notion of orthogonality.
