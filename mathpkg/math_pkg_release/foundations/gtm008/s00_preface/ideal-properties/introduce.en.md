This theorem notes two elementary consequences of the ideal axioms. First, ideals are downward closed under the natural order: if $b$ is in the ideal and $a \leq b$, then $a$ is also in the ideal. Second, the only ideal containing $1$ is the entire Boolean algebra, which is why proper ideals are defined by the condition $1 \notin I$.
