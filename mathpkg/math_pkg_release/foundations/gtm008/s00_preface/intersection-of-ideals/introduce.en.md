This theorem states that the intersection of an arbitrary nonempty family of ideals in a Boolean algebra is again an ideal. The proof follows directly from the ideal axioms: $0$ belongs to every ideal and hence to the intersection; closure under addition and downward closure under multiplication are preserved by arbitrary intersections. This result is used in the construction of the ideal generated by a set.
