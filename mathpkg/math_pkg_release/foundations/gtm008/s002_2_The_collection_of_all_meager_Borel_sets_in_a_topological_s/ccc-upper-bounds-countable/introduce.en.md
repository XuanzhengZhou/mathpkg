Theorem 3.27 is a powerful consequence of the countable chain condition: for any subset $E$ of a c.c.c. Boolean algebra, one can find a countable subset $D \subseteq E$ whose upper bounds coincide with those of $E$. The proof uses Zorn's Lemma to extract a maximal pairwise-disjoint family from the ideal generated by $E$, then applies the c.c.c. to conclude countability. The result implies that arbitrary Boolean sums in a c.c.c. algebra are essentially countable.
