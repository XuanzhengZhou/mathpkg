For any open subset $A$ of a topological space, the interior of its boundary $A^- - A$ is empty. Dually, if $A$ is closed, the closure of the interior of its boundary $(A - A^0)^{-0}$ vanishes. These two simple facts are key building blocks used to establish that the collection of all meager sets forms a proper $\sigma$-ideal, since they imply that the difference between a set and its regular open representative is nowhere dense.
