Tychonoff's Theorem (Theorem 3.32) is one of the most important results in general topology: the Cartesian product of an arbitrary family of compact spaces, equipped with the product topology, is itself compact. The proof uses the finite intersection property characterization of compactness together with Zorn's Lemma to extend a family with the finite intersection property to a maximal such family, then projects onto each factor to obtain limit points.
