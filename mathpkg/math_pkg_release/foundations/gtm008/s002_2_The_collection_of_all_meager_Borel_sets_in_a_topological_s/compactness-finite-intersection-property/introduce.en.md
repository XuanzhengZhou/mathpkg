Theorem 3.17 provides the dual characterization of compactness in terms of closed sets: a topological space is compact precisely when every family of closed sets whose finite subfamilies have nonempty intersection has a nonempty total intersection. The forward direction uses the fact that the complements of a closed family with the finite intersection property form an open cover with no finite subcover -- a contradiction. The converse direction is proved by contraposition.
