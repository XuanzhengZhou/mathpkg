Corollary 3.11 sharpens Theorem 3.10 by showing that the open set in the Baire property representation can always be chosen to be regular open. The proof replaces an arbitrary open set $G$ by its regular open hull $G^{-0}$, absorbing the difference $G^{-0} - G$ (which is nowhere dense) into the meager correction terms. This refinement is essential for establishing the isomorphism between Borel sets modulo meager sets and the regular open algebra.
