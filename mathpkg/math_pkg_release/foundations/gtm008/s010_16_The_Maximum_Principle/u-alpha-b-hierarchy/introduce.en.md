Definition 16.5 introduces the hierarchy $U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ as an alternative to the $V_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ hierarchy for Boolean-valued sets. At successor stages, $U_{\alpha+1}^{(\mathbf{B})}$ consists of all functions from the entire $U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ into $B$, making each element a function with full domain $U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ rather than an arbitrary subset. This ensures that $U_{\alpha+1}^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is complete — a key property used in later theorems about uniform and extensional sets.
