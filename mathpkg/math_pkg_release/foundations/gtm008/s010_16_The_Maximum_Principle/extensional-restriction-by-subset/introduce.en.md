Theorem 16.22 is a technical tool for manipulating Boolean-valued subset relations. Given an extensional set $v$ and another set $u$, let $b = \llbracket v \subseteq u \rrbracket$ be the Boolean truth value of "$v$ is a subset of $u$". Multiplying $v$ by $b$ yields $v' = b \cdot v$ which is guaranteed to be a subset of $u$ (with truth value $1$) while remaining extensional. Moreover, $v$ and $v'$ are equal exactly to the extent that $v$ was a subset of $u$, i.e., $\llbracket v = v' \rrbracket = b$.
