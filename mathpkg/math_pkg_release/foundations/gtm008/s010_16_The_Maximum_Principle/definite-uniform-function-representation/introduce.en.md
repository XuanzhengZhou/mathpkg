Theorem 16.28 is the converse of Theorem 16.8, completing the characterization of Boolean-valued functions between definite uniform sets. Together, they establish that Boolean-valued functions $f: u \to v$ (with $\llbracket f: u \rightarrow v \rrbracket = 1$) between definite uniform sets correspond exactly to real extensional functions $\varphi: \mathcal{D}(u) \to \mathcal{D}(v)$. This correspondence is foundational for understanding the structure of $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$: it shows that, at the level of definite uniform sets, Boolean-valued functions have no "extra" information beyond real extensional functions on the domains.
