Theorem 16.26 applies the subset characterization (Theorem 16.25) to compute the Boolean truth value of rank membership in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$. Part 1 expresses $\llbracket u \in R(\check{\alpha}) \rrbracket$ as the sum of equality values with elements of $V_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$. Part 2 notes that if $u$'s domain is contained in $V_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$, then $u$ is certainly a subset of $R(\check{\alpha})$. Part 3 extends the subset formula from Theorem 16.25 to the rank context, providing a set-sized sum representation of $\llbracket u \subseteq R(\check{\alpha}) \rrbracket$.
