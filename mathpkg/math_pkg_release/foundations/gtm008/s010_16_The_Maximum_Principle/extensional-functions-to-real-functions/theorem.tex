Let $u, v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ be definite and let $\varphi: \mathcal{D}(u) \rightarrow \mathcal{D}(v)$ be an extensional function. Then there exists $f \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ such that

1. $\llbracket f: u \rightarrow v \rrbracket = 1$,
2. $(\forall x \in \mathcal{D}(u))[\llbracket f(x) = \varphi(x) \rrbracket = 1]$.

Conversely (Theorem 16.28), if $u, v$ are definite and uniform and $\llbracket f: u \rightarrow v \rrbracket = 1$, then there exists a real extensional function $\varphi: \mathcal{D}(u) \rightarrow \mathcal{D}(v)$ such that condition 2 holds.
