Theorem 16.8 establishes a fundamental correspondence: any real extensional function $\varphi$ between the domains of two definite Boolean-valued sets can be lifted to a Boolean-valued function $f$ in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ that maps $u$ to $v$ with Boolean truth value $1$. Moreover, $f$ agrees with $\varphi$ pointwise in the sense that $\llbracket f(x) = \varphi(x) \rrbracket = 1$. The converse direction is given in Theorem 16.28, and together they characterize Boolean-valued functions between definite uniform sets as precisely the real extensional functions on their domains.
