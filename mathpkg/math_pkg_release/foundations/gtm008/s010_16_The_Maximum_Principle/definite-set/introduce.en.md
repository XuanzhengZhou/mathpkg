A definite set $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is one where every element of its domain $\mathcal{D}(u)$ belongs to $u$ with full Boolean value $1$. In other words, the membership function $u(x)$ is identically $1$ on the domain. The standard example is $\check{k}$ (the canonical check embedding of a ground-model set), which is always definite. Definite sets correspond to "ordinary" sets with no Boolean fuzziness in their membership relation and play an important role in relating Boolean-valued functions to real extensional functions.
