If $\mathbf{B}$ satisfies the c.c.c. and $cf(\aleph_\beta) > \aleph_0$, then
$$b = \sum_{j < \aleph_\beta} b_{ij} = \sum_{j < \gamma} b_{ij} \text{ for some } \gamma < \aleph_\beta,$$
because the c.c.c. implies that any antichain in $B$ is countable, and with $cf(\aleph_\beta) > \aleph_0$, the supremum over $\aleph_\beta$ indices is achieved at some $\gamma < \aleph_\beta$. One can then construct $\bar{q}$ and $\Lambda$ satisfying the splitable condition.
