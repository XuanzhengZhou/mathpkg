Theorem 23.32 establishes a connection between the countable chain condition (c.c.c.) and the splitable property: if a Boolean algebra satisfies the c.c.c. and the cofinality of $\aleph_\beta$ is uncountable, then the algebra is $(\aleph_\alpha, \aleph_\beta)$-splitable. The proof uses the fact that under c.c.c., any supremum over $\aleph_\beta$ indices with uncountable cofinality is already achieved at some smaller index, allowing the construction of the required $\bar{q}$ and $\Lambda$.
