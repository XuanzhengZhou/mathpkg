Definition 23.35 introduces the $(\omega_\alpha, \omega_\beta)$-Weak Distributive Law (WDL), a natural generalization of the $(\omega, \omega_\alpha)$-WDL defined earlier (Definition 20.1). This law expresses a commutation relation between products over $\omega_\alpha$ indices and sums over $\omega_\beta$ indices: the left-hand side commutes the product and sum in the natural order, while the right-hand side expresses this as a sum over all functions from $\omega_\alpha$ to $\omega_\beta$ of products of partial sums. The WDL is closely connected to the splitable property.
