Definition 23.29 introduces the $I$-$\Delta$ sieve law ($I$-$\Delta$SL), a strengthening of the ordinary $I$-sieve law that restricts attention to sieves defined within $\Delta(B_0)$ and requires the sifted element to also lie in $\Delta(B_0)$. This definition is used in the proof that Boolean-valued models satisfying certain conditions are models of ZF+AC.
