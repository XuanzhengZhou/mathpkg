Let $I$ be a set. $B$ satisfies the $I$-$\Delta$ sieve law ($I$-$\Delta$SL) iff there exists a complete subalgebra $B_0$ of $B$ for which
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B_0$ is a set,
\item for every sieve $\langle b', \{b_i \mid i \in I\} \rangle$ defined in $\Delta(B_0)$ there exists a sifted element in $\Delta(B_0)$.
\end{enumerate}
