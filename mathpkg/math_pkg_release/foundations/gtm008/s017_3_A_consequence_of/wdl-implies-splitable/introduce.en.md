Theorem 23.36 shows that the Weak Distributive Law implies the splitable property. Given the WDL identity, one can expand the product of sums as a sum over functions, and since some term in this sum must be nonzero (as the whole sum equals $b > 0$), one obtains the function $f$ and the set $\Lambda$ witnessing the splitable condition. This result connects the algebraic distributive law to the combinatorial splitting property used in forcing arguments.
