Define $\Gamma_\alpha \triangleq \{q \in P \mid q = \bigcup_{\beta \leq \alpha} q^\beta\}$. Then
$$P_\alpha \triangleq \langle \Gamma_\alpha, \leq \rangle.$$
$B_\alpha$ is the Boolean algebra of all regular open sets in $P_\alpha$. For $\alpha < \beta$ define $j: \Gamma_\beta \rightarrow \Gamma_\alpha$ by
$$j(p) = \bigcup_{\gamma \leq \alpha} p^\gamma \quad \text{for} \quad p \in \Gamma_\beta.$$
The map $j$ is an open continuous map onto $\Gamma_\alpha$, inducing a complete monomorphism $i: B_\alpha \rightarrow B_\beta$, so $B_\alpha$ may be regarded as a complete subalgebra of $B_\beta$ for $\alpha < \beta$.
