Definition 24.3 introduces the level-$\alpha$ components of Easton's forcing. For each ordinal $\alpha$, $\Gamma_\alpha$ consists of those conditions in $P$ that only involve coordinates up to $\alpha$, and $P_\alpha$ is the corresponding partial order. The Boolean algebras $B_\alpha$ of regular open sets in $P_\alpha$ form a directed system via the projection maps $j: \Gamma_\beta \rightarrow \Gamma_\alpha$, which are open, continuous, and onto. This induces complete monomorphisms $i: B_\alpha \rightarrow B_\beta$, allowing $B_\alpha$ to be treated as a complete subalgebra of $B_\beta$. Each $B_\alpha$ is a set, which is essential for the Easton pair machinery.
