1. Let $G_1 = (G^\Delta)^{-0}$. Then

$$G = G^\Delta^* \subseteq (G^\Delta)^{-0} = G_1^*.$$

If $G_2$ is regular open and $G \cap G_2 = 0$ then

$$(G^\Delta \cap G_2^\Delta)^* \subseteq G^\Delta^* \cap G_2^\Delta^* = G \cap G_2 = 0.$$

Therefore $G^\Delta \cap G_2^\Delta = 0$ and hence $G_1 \cap G_2^\Delta = 0$. Furthermore

$$(G_1^* \cap G_2^\Delta)^\Delta \subseteq G_1^{*\Delta} \cap G_2^\Delta = G_1 \cap G_2^\Delta = 0.$$

Thus $G_1^* \cap G_2 = 0$ and hence, by Theorem 5.15, $G_1^* \subseteq G^{-0} = G$. Consequently

$$G^\Delta = G_1^{*\Delta} = G_1$$

i.e., $G^\Delta$ is regular open.
