A partial order $P$ is called \textit{fine} if, whenever $q \not\leq p$, there exists an element $r \leq q$ that is incompatible with $p$. This condition ensures that the basic open sets $[p] = \{x \in P : x \leq p\}$ are regular open in the order topology. Many partial orders arising in forcing constructions, including those associated with Boolean algebras, satisfy the fine condition.
