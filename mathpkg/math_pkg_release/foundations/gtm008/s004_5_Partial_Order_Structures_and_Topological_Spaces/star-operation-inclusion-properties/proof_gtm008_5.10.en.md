(1) Let $a \in G_1$. Then $[a] \subseteq G_1$ (since $G_1$ is open), so $N(a) \subseteq G_1^*$. But then $[a] \subseteq G_1^{*\Delta}$ (since $G_1^*$ is an open subset of $\mathcal{F}$). Consequently $a \in G_1^{*\Delta}$.

(2) Let $F \in G_2$. Then $(\exists a \in F)[N(a) \subseteq G_2]$ (since $G_2$ is open). Hence $(\exists a \in F)[[a] \subseteq G_2^\Delta]$. Since $G_2^\Delta$ is an open subset of $P$, this means $(\exists a \in F)[N(a) \subseteq G_2^{\Delta *}]$. Therefore $F \in G_2^{\Delta *}$.
