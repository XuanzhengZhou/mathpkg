Theorem 7.10 establishes two important properties of $Df(\mathbf{A})$: (1) it is itself a set (not a proper class) when $\mathbf{A}$ is a set, and it can be defined by a formula from the parameters of $\mathbf{A}$; (2) it is absolute with respect to any transitive model $M$ of ZF containing $\mathbf{A}$. These properties are essential for proving that the constructible hierarchy is well-defined within ZF.
