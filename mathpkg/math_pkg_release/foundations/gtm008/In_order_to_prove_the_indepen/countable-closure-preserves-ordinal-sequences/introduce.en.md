Theorem 12.5 states that if a forcing notion $\mathbf{P}$ has the countable closure property (every countable compatible set has a lower bound), then the generic extension $M[G]$ contains no new $\omega$-sequences of ordinals. This means that countable sequences from the extension were already present in the ground model $M$. Such forcing notions are particularly useful when one wants to add sets without disturbing countable combinatorics.
