Theorem 20.6 shows that the existence of a strictly positive $\sigma$-measure on a Boolean $\sigma$-algebra forces the algebra to satisfy the $(\omega, \omega)$-weak distributive law. This is a key structural result: measure algebras, already known to satisfy the c.c.c., automatically possess a stronger distributivity property. The proof uses $\varepsilon$-approximations via the measure to construct bounding functions that witness the weak distributive equality.
