The weak distributive law is a weakening of the full distributive law for complete Boolean algebras. Instead of requiring the product of sums to equal the sum over all choice functions of the corresponding products, the weak distributive law restricts the choice functions to those in a specified cardinal-indexed function space. The $(\omega, \omega)$-WDL and $(\omega, \omega_\alpha)$-WDL are central to the study of forcing extensions, as they characterize when certain cardinal cofinalities are preserved in Boolean-valued models.
