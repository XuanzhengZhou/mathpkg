Theorem 20.4 characterizes the $(\omega, \omega)$-weak distributive law in terms of the dominating relation on the Baire space: a complete Boolean algebra satisfies the $(\omega, \omega)$-WDL exactly when it forces that every function $g: \omega \to \omega$ in the extension is pointwise dominated by some ground model function. This means that the $(\omega, \omega)$-WDL precisely controls whether new "fast-growing" functions from $\omega$ to $\omega$ are added in the Boolean-valued extension.
