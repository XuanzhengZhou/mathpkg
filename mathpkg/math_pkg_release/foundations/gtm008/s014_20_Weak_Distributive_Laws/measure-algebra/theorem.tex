A Boolean $\sigma$-algebra $B$ is a \textbf{measure algebra} iff there exists a strictly positive $\sigma$-measure $m$ on $B$, i.e., a function from $|B|$ into $[0, 1]$, the closed interval of real numbers between $0$ and $1$, such that
$$
(\forall b \in B) [b \neq 0 \rightarrow m(b) > 0] \wedge m(1) = 1
$$
and
$$
(\forall b \in B^{\omega}) \left[ (\forall i, j < \omega) [i \neq j \rightarrow b_i \cdot b_j = 0] \rightarrow m\left( \sum_{i < \omega} b_i \right) = \sum_{i < \omega} m(b_i) \right].
$$

\begin{remark}
A measure algebra always satisfies the c.c.c. and hence it is complete.
\end{remark}
