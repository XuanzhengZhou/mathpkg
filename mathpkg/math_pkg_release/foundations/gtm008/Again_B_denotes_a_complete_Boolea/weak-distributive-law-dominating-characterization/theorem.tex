Let $B$ be a complete Boolean algebra.

\textbf{$(\omega, \omega)$-Weak Distributive Law:}
$B$ satisfies the $(\omega, \omega)$-weak distributive law $(\omega, \omega)$-WDL iff for every family $\{b_{nm} \mid n, m \in \omega\} \subseteq B$,
$$\prod_{m < \omega} \sum_{n < \omega} b_{nm} = \sum_{f \in \omega^{\omega}} \prod_{n \in \omega} \sum_{m \leq f(n)} b_{nm}.$$

\textbf{$(\omega, \omega_{\alpha})$-Weak Distributive Law:}
If $\omega_{\alpha}$ is not cofinal with $\omega$, $B$ satisfies the $(\omega, \omega_{\alpha})$-weak distributive law iff for every family $\{b_{n\xi} \mid n \in \omega \land \xi < \omega_{\alpha}\} \subseteq B$,
$$\prod_{n < \omega} \sum_{\xi < \omega_{\alpha}} b_{n\xi} = \sum_{f \in \omega_{\alpha}^{\omega}} \prod_{n \in \omega} \sum_{\xi \leq f(n)} b_{n\xi}.$$
