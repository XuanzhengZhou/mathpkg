This theorem establishes that every measure algebra satisfies the $(\omega, \omega)$-weak distributive law. The proof uses the $\sigma$-additivity of the measure to approximate infinite sums by finite partial sums to within any $\varepsilon > 0$. This result is important because it shows that forcing with a measure algebra (random real forcing) preserves the property that $\omega_1$ is not cofinal with $\omega$.
