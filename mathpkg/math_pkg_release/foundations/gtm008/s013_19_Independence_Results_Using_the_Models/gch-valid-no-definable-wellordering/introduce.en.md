Assuming the Generalized Continuum Hypothesis, there exists a complete Boolean algebra $B$ such that GCH remains $B$-valid in the Boolean-valued model $V^{(B)}$, yet the statement asserting the existence of a definable well-ordering of the power set of $\omega$ fails to be $B$-valid. This theorem, which parallels Theorem 11.7, establishes the independence of the existence of a definable well-ordering of $\mathcal{P}(\omega)$ from ZFC + GCH. The Boolean algebra $B$ is taken as in Theorem 19.4 (the regular open algebra of the partial order used for the independence of $V = L$), and the proof uses the rigidity property of $B$ to show that no definable well-ordering can exist in $V^{(B)}$.
