Both class-sized Boolean-valued universes $V^{(B)}$ and $V^{(\tilde{B})}$ satisfy the Axiom of Union. For a given $u$ in the Boolean-valued universe, the union $v$ is constructed by taking $\mathcal{D}(v) = \bigcup_{y \in \mathcal{D}(u)} \mathcal{D}(y)$ and defining $v(x) = \llbracket (\exists y \in u)[x \in y] \rrbracket$. The verification that this $v$ indeed satisfies the union axiom relies on the quantifier formulas of Theorem 23.7.
