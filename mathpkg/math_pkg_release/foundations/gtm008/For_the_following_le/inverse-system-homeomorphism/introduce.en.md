When a family of topological spaces is organized into an inverse system via the natural projection maps, and the index $\kappa$ has cofinality at least $\lambda$, the inverse limit of the finite partial products is homeomorphic to the full $\lambda$-product. This theorem bridges the abstract notion of an o.c.o. inverse system (the topological dual of a direct system of Boolean algebras) with the concrete $\lambda$-product topology. It is essential for constructing limits of Boolean-valued models, where the limit of the associated Boolean algebras corresponds to the limit of their Stone spaces.
