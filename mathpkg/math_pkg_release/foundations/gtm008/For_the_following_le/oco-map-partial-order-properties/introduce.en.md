An open continuous onto (o.c.o.) map between partial order structures (viewed as topological spaces with the down-set topology) is necessarily order-preserving. Moreover, the image of the principal down-set $[x]$ equals the principal down-set of the image $[p(x)]$. These properties show that o.c.o. maps are precisely the continuous homomorphisms between the Stone spaces of Boolean algebras, and they provide the geometric characterization of complete monomorphisms between complete Boolean algebras.
