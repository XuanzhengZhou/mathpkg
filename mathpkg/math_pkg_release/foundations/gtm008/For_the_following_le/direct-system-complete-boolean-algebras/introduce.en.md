A direct system of complete Boolean algebras consists of a $\kappa$-indexed family of complete Boolean algebras together with complete monomorphisms $i_{\alpha\beta}: \mathbf{B}_{\alpha} \to \mathbf{B}_{\beta}$ for $\alpha \leq \beta$, satisfying the identity and composition laws. By identifying each $\mathbf{B}_{\alpha}$ with its image under the connecting maps, one can view the system as an increasing chain of complete subalgebras. The union $\bigcup_{\alpha} B_{\alpha}$ then naturally carries a Boolean algebra structure. Direct systems are the algebraic counterpart (via Stone duality) of inverse systems of topological spaces, and their limits are used to construct large Boolean algebras for forcing and Boolean-valued models.
