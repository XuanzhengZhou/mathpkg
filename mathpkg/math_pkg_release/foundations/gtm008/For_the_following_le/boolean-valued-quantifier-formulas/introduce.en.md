This theorem extends the fundamental truth definition of Boolean-valued logic to class-sized Boolean algebras. The Boolean truth value of $(\exists x \in u)\varphi(x)$ is the supremum over all $x$ in the domain of $u$ of the meet of $u(x)$ (the "degree to which $x$ belongs to $u$") with the truth value of $\varphi(x)$. Similarly, the Boolean truth value of $(\forall x \in u)\varphi(x)$ is the infimum over $x$ in the domain of $u$ of the implication $u(x) \Rightarrow \llbracket\varphi(x)\rrbracket$. The existence of these suprema and infima requires the completeness of the Boolean algebra (for $V^{(\tilde{B})}$) or is justified by bounding arguments (for $V^{(B)}$).
