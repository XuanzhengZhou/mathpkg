In the theory of Boolean-valued models, generalized product spaces over a cardinal index set are fundamental. This theorem establishes that for such a product space, each coordinate projection $p_{\alpha}$ is an open, continuous, and onto map (o.c.o.), and the projections satisfy the natural compatibility condition $p_{\alpha\beta} \circ p_{\beta} = p_{\alpha}$. These o.c.o. maps form the morphisms of inverse systems of topological spaces, which correspond via Stone duality to direct systems of complete Boolean algebras.
