This theorem establishes that applying the star and delta operations in succession yields a superset of the original open set: $G_1 \subseteq G_1^{*\Delta}$ for open subsets of $P$, and $G_2 \subseteq G_2^{\Delta*}$ for open subsets of $\mathcal{F}$. These inclusions are the starting point for proving that the maps $G \mapsto G^{*\Delta}$ and $H \mapsto H^{\Delta*}$ form closure operations whose fixed points are exactly the regular open sets.
