A structure $\mathbf{A}$ is a substructure of $\mathbf{B}$ if the domain of $\mathbf{A}$ is a subset of the domain of $\mathbf{B}$, and the interpretations of predicate and constant symbols in $\mathbf{A}$ agree with those in $\mathbf{B}$ on the smaller domain. This is the standard model-theoretic notion of substructure, used throughout the development of relative constructibility to define hierarchies of transitive structures.
