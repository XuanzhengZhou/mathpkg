The $B$-valued interpretation extends the Boolean truth values from atomic formulas to all formulas by interpreting logical connectives as Boolean operations: negation as complement, conjunction as meet, disjunction as join, universal quantification as product (generalized meet), and existential quantification as sum (generalized join). The equality interpretation must satisfy the substitution property that equal terms can be substituted in any context without decreasing the truth value. This forms the semantic foundation for Boolean-valued model theory.
