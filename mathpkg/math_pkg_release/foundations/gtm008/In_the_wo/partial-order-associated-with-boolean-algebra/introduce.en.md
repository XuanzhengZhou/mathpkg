Every Boolean algebra $B$ gives rise to a canonical partial order structure $\mathcal{P}$ by removing the zero element and restricting the natural order. This associated partial order structure provides a bridge between the algebraic theory of Boolean algebras and the order-theoretic/topological theory of partial orders. It is a fundamental construction used throughout Boolean-valued model theory.
