For languages with equality, a $B$-valued interpretation must satisfy additional axioms ensuring that equality behaves as a generalized equivalence relation valued in the Boolean algebra $B$. Reflexivity requires identity to hold with truth value $1$, symmetry requires the truth values to be equal, and transitivity is expressed as an inequality of Boolean products. The substitution axiom for predicates ensures that equal objects have equal truth values under any predicate, up to the product of the relevant equality truth values.
