Bounded formulas (those with only bounded quantifiers) are absolute between the universe $V$ and any transitive class $M$. This is because the truth of bounded quantifiers depends only on the elements of the sets involved, which are the same in $V$ and $M$ when $M$ is transitive. This absoluteness is the foundation for proving that the constructible hierarchy correctly computes simple set-theoretic properties and that forcing extensions preserve certain classes of statements.
