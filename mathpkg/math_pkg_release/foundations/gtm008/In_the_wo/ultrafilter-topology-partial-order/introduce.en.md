Given a partial order structure $P$, one can construct a topological space whose points are the ultrafilters on $P$. Basic open sets are of the form $N(p) = \{F \mid p \in F\}$ for $p \in P$. This space is always $T_1$, and when $P$ is the partial order associated with a Boolean algebra (i.e., $B - \{0\}$ ordered by the natural order), the space is Hausdorff. This construction is central to the Stone representation theory connecting Boolean algebras to topological spaces.
