A $B$-valued structure is complete if it has a "mixing" or "amalgamation" property: given a partition of unity in $B$ and a corresponding family of elements of $A$, there exists an element $a$ that is "equal to $a_i$ with truth value at least $b_i$" for each $i$. This is the $B$-valued analog of the ability to paste together local data. Completeness is essential for constructing $B$-valued models of set theory, particularly for verifying the axioms of Separation and Replacement.
