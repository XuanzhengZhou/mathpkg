The reflection principle states that for any existential formula, there are arbitrarily large ordinals $\beta$ such that any existential statement true in the full model $\mathbf{M}$ is already witnessed by some element in $M_\beta$. The proof uses the fact that satisfaction in $\mathbf{M}$ is expressible by a formula of $\mathcal{L}_0$, and then takes the supremum of the witnessing ordinals for all parameter tuples from $M_\alpha$. This is the key technical step in proving that the limit model satisfies the axioms of ZF.
