For closed formulas (sentences with no free variables), the Boolean truth value is absolute -- it does not depend on any assignment of values to variables. This means that a sentence is either satisfied (truth value $1$) or not satisfied in a given $B$-valued structure. This is the Boolean-valued analogue of the classical fact that sentences have a definite truth value in a structure, and it is essential for independence proofs: one shows that $\|\varphi\| < 1$ in $V^{(B)}$ to prove that $\varphi$ is not provable from ZF.
