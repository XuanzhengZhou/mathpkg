This theorem is a strengthening of the result that $V = L$ fails in the generic extension. Using the finite-support automorphisms $\pi_k$ of the Cohen poset, one shows that any definable well-ordering of $\mathcal{P}(\omega)$ would be invariant under enough automorphisms to force a contradiction. The key identity $\tilde{a}(\pi_k''(G)) = \tilde{a}(G) \Delta k$ shows that the generic real can be modified by any finite set, destroying any putative definable well-order.
