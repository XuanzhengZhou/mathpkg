This exercise asks the reader to provide a direct proof that the Axiom of Choice holds with Boolean value 1 in $\mathbf{V}^{(B)}$, without relying on the homomorphism $h$ from Theorem 14.22 or the result $V = M[F]$ from Theorem 14.24. The challenge is to construct a Boolean-valued choice function directly from a family of nonempty Boolean-valued sets, using the Axiom of Choice in the ground model $V$.
