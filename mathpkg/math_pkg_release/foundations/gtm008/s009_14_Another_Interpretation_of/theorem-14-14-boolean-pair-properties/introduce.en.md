This theorem justifies Definition 14.13 by showing that the Boolean-valued constructions of unordered pairs, singletons, and ordered pairs are equal (with Boolean truth value 1) to their standard set-theoretic counterparts. This means that for reasoning inside $V^{(B)}$, we may freely use the standard pair-set notation without worrying about the Boolean-valued nature of the construction.
