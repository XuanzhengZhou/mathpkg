An atom in a Boolean algebra is a nonzero element $b$ such that the only elements below $b$ are $0$ and $b$ itself. A Boolean algebra is called nonatomic if it contains no atoms. The atomic/nonatomic distinction plays a key role in determining whether the generic filter $F$ can lie in the ground model $M$.
