This theorem establishes that if the Axiom of Choice holds in the ground model $V$, then it also holds with Boolean value 1 in the Boolean-valued universe $\mathbf{V}^{(B)}$. The proof relies on Theorem 14.24: choose $N$ and $\mathbf{B}$ as in Corollary 14.23 (in the system GB + Mathematical Induction). Then $N$ satisfies AC, and so does every generic extension $N[G]$ for $G$ that is $\mathbf{P}$-generic over $N$. By Theorem 14.24, $[[AC]] = 1$ in $\mathbf{V}^{(B)}$. This result is important because it shows that Boolean-valued models preserve choice.
