For a complete nonatomic Boolean algebra $B$, any attempt to characterize a subset $S$ by the meet of its elements together with complements of non-elements yields zero. This implies no subset can serve as a complete ultrafilter, which is why nonatomic complete Boolean algebras lack complete ultrafilters -- a crucial property for independence proofs via forcing.
