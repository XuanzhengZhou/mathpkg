This theorem is one of the two main goals of Section 14. It establishes that in the Boolean-valued universe $\mathbf{V}^{(B)}$, the predicate $M$ -- which interprets the ground model -- satisfies all the properties of a standard transitive model of ZF containing all ordinals, with Boolean value 1. This follows from the transitivity of $M$ (Theorem 14.10), the fact that ZF axioms relativized to $M$ hold (Theorem 14.phi), and that all ordinals are in $M$. This result is fundamental for interpreting forcing within Boolean-valued models.
