This theorem establishes the fundamental properties of the homomorphism $h$ constructed from an $N$-complete Boolean homomorphism $h_0: \mathbf{B} \to 2$. The eight properties show that $h$ provides a surjective correspondence between the Boolean-valued universe $(V^{(B)})^N$ and the generic extension $N[G]$. Properties 1-2 preserve equality and membership, property 3 shows $h$ fixes the canonical names, property 7 is a Tarski truth lemma, and property 8 shows the image lies in $N[G]$. This is the key bridge between Boolean-valued models and forcing.
