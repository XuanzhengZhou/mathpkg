Theorem 10.12 provides a crucial refinement of the relationship between forcing and generic filters: if $p \Vdash (\exists x)\varphi(x)$ and $G$ is $P$-generic over $M$ with $p \in G$, then there exists a condition $q \leq p$ in $G$ and a constant term $t$ such that $q \Vdash \varphi(t)$. This theorem is essential for establishing that the forcing relation commutes with the existential quantifier in the presence of a generic filter, and is used to prove that truth in $M[G]$ corresponds to forcing.
