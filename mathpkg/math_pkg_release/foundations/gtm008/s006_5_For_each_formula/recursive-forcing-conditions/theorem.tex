Let $k, k_1, k_2 \in V$ and $t, t_1, t_2$ be constant terms. The forcing relation satisfies:

1. $p \Vdash \neg \varphi \leftrightarrow (\forall q \leq p) \neg(q \Vdash \varphi)$.
2. $p \Vdash \varphi_1 \land \varphi_2 \leftrightarrow p \Vdash \varphi_1 \land p \Vdash \varphi_2$.
3. $p \Vdash (\forall x)\varphi(x) \leftrightarrow (\forall t \in T)[p \Vdash \varphi(t)]$.
4. $p \Vdash (\forall x^{\alpha})\varphi(x^{\alpha}) \leftrightarrow (\forall q \leq p)(\exists q' \leq q)(\forall t \in T_{\alpha})[q' \Vdash \varphi(t)]$.
5. $p \Vdash V(t) \leftrightarrow (\forall q \leq p)(\exists q' \leq q)(\exists k)[q' \Vdash t = k]$; in particular $p \Vdash V(k)$.
6. $p \Vdash F(t) \leftrightarrow (\forall q \leq p)(\exists q' \leq q)(\exists k \in K)[h(f_0(k)) = 1 \land q' \Vdash t = k]$.
