Theorem 9.7 provides the computation rule for bounded universal quantifiers in the $B$-valued model: the truth value of $(\forall x \in a)\varphi(x)$ is the Boolean product over all $x \in M_{\alpha}$ of the implication $\llbracket x \in a \rrbracket \Rightarrow \llbracket \varphi(x) \rrbracket$. This is the $B$-valued counterpart of the classical equivalence $(\forall x \in a)\varphi(x) \leftrightarrow (\forall x)(x \in a \to \varphi(x))$, and is a crucial tool for handling bounded quantification.
