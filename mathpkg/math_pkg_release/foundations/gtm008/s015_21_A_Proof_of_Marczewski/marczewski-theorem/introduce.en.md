Marczewski's theorem (also known as the Marczewski–Szpilrajn theorem) bounds the cardinality of a family of pairwise disjoint product-open sets, each having finite support and each factor drawn from a small prescribed base. The theorem states that if a product $\prod_{i \in I} X_i$ has bases $b_i$ of size at most $\gamma$ (with $\gamma$ infinite) for each factor, then any family of pairwise disjoint basic open sets with finite supports has cardinality at most $\gamma$. This result is the central combinatorial ingredient in proving that the Boolean algebra of regular open sets of a product of spaces with small bases satisfies the $\gamma$-chain condition.
