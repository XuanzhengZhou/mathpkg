The Erdős–Rado theorem provides a cardinality bound for a family $A$ of sets in terms of two parameters $\alpha$ and $\gamma$ (with $\gamma$ infinite). It states that if every member of $A$ has cardinality at most $\alpha$, and every quasi-disjoint subfamily of $A$ has cardinality at most $\gamma$, then the whole family $A$ has cardinality at most $\gamma^\alpha$. This theorem is a central tool in infinitary combinatorics and is used in this section to prove Marczewski's theorem on the $\gamma$-chain condition of product spaces.
