Corollary 21.4 gives a sharp cardinality bound for an index set $T$ when there exist families $\{A_t\}_{t \in T}$ and $\{B_t\}_{t \in T}$ satisfying a separating condition: each $A_t$ is disjoint from its own partner $B_t$, but intersects $B_{t'}$ for all distinct $t, t'$. The key improvement over Theorem 21.3 is that when each $A_t$ is finite, the bound simplifies to $|T| \leq \gamma$ (since $\gamma^n = \gamma$ for finite $n$ when $\gamma$ is infinite). This corollary serves as the final combinatorial step in the proof of Marczewski's theorem.
