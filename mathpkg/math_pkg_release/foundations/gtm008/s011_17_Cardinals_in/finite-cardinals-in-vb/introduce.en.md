This theorem is the converse of Theorem 17.1 for ordinals $\alpha \leq \omega$. It asserts that every finite ordinal and $\omega$ itself are guaranteed to be cardinals in the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$, with Boolean value $1$. The proof relies on the $(\beta+1, \beta)$-distributive law, which holds in any complete Boolean algebra $B$ when $\beta$ is finite.
