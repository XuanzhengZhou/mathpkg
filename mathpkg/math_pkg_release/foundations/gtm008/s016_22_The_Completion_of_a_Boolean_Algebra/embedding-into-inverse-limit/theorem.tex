Let $X = \varprojlim_{\alpha < \kappa} X^{\alpha}$ be an inverse limit of topological spaces with o.c.o. projections $p_{\alpha}: X \to X^{\alpha}$ and $p_{\alpha\beta}: X^{\beta} \to X^{\alpha}$. Let $Y$ be a topological space and $q_{\alpha}: Y \to X^{\alpha}$ be o.c.o. maps with $q_{\alpha} = p_{\alpha\beta} \circ q_{\beta}$ for $\alpha \leq \beta < \kappa$. Define $q: Y \to X$ by $(q(y))(\alpha) = q_{\alpha}(y)$ and $X_0 = \{q(y) \mid y \in Y\}$. Then $X_0$ is dense in $X$ and $q: Y \to X_0$ is o.c.o.

If, in addition, $Y$ satisfies the following condition

$$(\forall y_1, y_2 \in Y)[y_1 \neq y_2 \rightarrow (\exists \alpha < \kappa)[q_{\alpha}(y_1) \neq q_{\alpha}(y_2)]],$$

then $q$ is a homeomorphic map from $Y$ to $X_0$.$$
