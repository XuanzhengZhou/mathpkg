$j``P$ is dense in $\tilde{B}$ because $\{[p] \mid p \in P\}$ is a base for open sets in $P$. Repeating the proof of Theorem 1.30 we see that $\langle \tilde{B}, j \rangle$ is a completion of $B$. If $\langle \tilde{B}_1, f \rangle$ is any completion of $B$, $S = f``(B - \{0\})$ is dense in $\tilde{B}_1 - \{0\}$. Thus by Theorem 22.5 the Boolean algebra of regular open sets of $S$ is isomorphic to the complete Boolean algebra of all regular open sets in $\tilde{B}_1 - \{0\}$, which is isomorphic to $\tilde{B}_1$ by Theorem 1.40, and hence also to $\tilde{B}$. This gives an isomorphism $k: \tilde{B} \rightarrow \tilde{B}_1$ as required by the theorem.
