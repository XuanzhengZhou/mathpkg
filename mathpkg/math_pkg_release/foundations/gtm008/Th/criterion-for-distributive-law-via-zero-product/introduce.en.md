This lemma provides a simplified criterion for verifying the $(I, J)$-distributive law in a complete Boolean algebra. It states that in order to prove the $(I, J)$-DL for a family $\{b_{ij}\}$, it suffices to show that if the product along every selection function $f \in J^I$ is zero, then the product of the sums over all choices is also zero. This reduces the full distributive law check to a single implication involving Boolean products and sums.
