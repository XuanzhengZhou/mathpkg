If $\gamma \in \sup(A)$ then $(\exists \alpha)[\gamma \in \alpha \land \alpha \in A]$. But $\alpha \in A \rightarrow (\exists \beta)[\beta \in B \land \alpha \le \beta]$ i.e., $(\exists \beta)[\gamma \in \beta \land \beta \in B]$. Therefore $\gamma \in \sup(B)$ and hence $\sup(A) \le \sup(B).$

Remark. That Proposition 8.6 can not be an iff result is established by the counter-example

$$\sup(\omega) = \sup(\omega + 1).$$
