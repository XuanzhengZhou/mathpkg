** If $A$ is a standard transitive model of Axiom 2 and if $a, b \in A$, then $\{a\} \in A$ and $\{a, b\} \in A$. Then

$$[\langle a, b \rangle]^A = \{x \in A | [x = \{a\} \vee x = \{a, b\}]^A\}$$

$$= \{x \in A | x = \{a\} \vee x = \{a, b\}\}$$

$$= \{x | x = \{a\} \vee x = \{a, b\}\}$$

$$= \langle a, b \rangle.$$

**Remark.** From Proposition 13.26 and the substitution property, Proposition 13.10 it follows that ordered triples, ordered quadruples, etc. are absolute with respect to standard transitive models of the Axiom of Pairing.
