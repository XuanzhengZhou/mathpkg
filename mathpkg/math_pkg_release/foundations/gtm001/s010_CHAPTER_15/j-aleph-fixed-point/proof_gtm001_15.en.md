**Proof.** If $K_1' \aleph_\gamma < \aleph_\gamma$ and $K_2' \aleph_\gamma < \aleph_\gamma$, then by Propositions 15.8 and 15.1(2), $\aleph_\gamma = J'\langle K_1' \aleph_\gamma, K_2' \aleph_\gamma, K_3' \aleph_\gamma \rangle < \aleph_\gamma$, a contradiction. Hence $\max(K_1' \aleph_\gamma, K_2' \aleph_\gamma) = \aleph_\gamma$, and from $J_0'\langle 0, \aleph_\gamma \rangle \geq \aleph_\gamma$ we get the result.
