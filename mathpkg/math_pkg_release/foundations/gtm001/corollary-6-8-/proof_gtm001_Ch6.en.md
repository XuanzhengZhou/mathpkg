(1) If $A = \{ \langle \langle x, y \rangle, \langle y, x \rangle \rangle | x, y \in V \}$, then $A$ is single valued and hence by Proposition 6.7, $A“a$ is a set. But $A“a = a^{-1}$; therefore $a^{-1}$ is a set.

(2) If $A = \{ \langle \langle x, y \rangle, x \rangle | x, y \in V \}$, then $A$ is single valued and $A“a = D(a)$.

(3) If $A = \{ \langle \langle x, y \rangle, y \rangle | x, y \in V \}$, then $A$ is single valued and $A“a = W(a)$.
