The definition **Let $\Pi = \langle \eta_i, \pi_{ij} \rangle_{i \leq j}$ be a direct system of order preserving maps with an index set $\langle I, \leq \rangle$. Then $\lim_{\substack{\text{i} \in I \\ \text{i} \neq j}} \Pi \triangleq \langle \langle M / \equiv, < \rangle, \pi_{i\infty} \rangle_{i \in I}$ where $M / \equiv, <$, and $\pi_{i\infty}$ are defined as follows** (Definition 16.25.) is a fundamental notion in axiomatic set theory, introduced in Chapter 15 of Takeuti and Zaring's *Introduction to Axiomatic Set Theory* (2nd ed., 1982). It forms part of the formal development of Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory with classes (GB or ZF).
