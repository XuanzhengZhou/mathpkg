Proposition 5.18 asserts that there are no finite membership cycles: it is impossible to have $a_1 \in a_2 \in \cdots \in a_n \in a_1$. The proof uses the Axiom of Regularity: if such a cycle existed, every element of the set $\{a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n\}$ would intersect the set nontrivially, contradicting Regularity.
