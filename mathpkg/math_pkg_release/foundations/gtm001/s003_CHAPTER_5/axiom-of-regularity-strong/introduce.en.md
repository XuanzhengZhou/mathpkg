The strong form of the Axiom of Regularity (Axiom 6') extends the weak form from sets to arbitrary classes: every nonempty class $A$ contains an element $x$ that is disjoint from $A$. The weak and strong forms are in fact equivalent, though this is proved later. The strong form yields an induction principle (Proposition 5.22).
