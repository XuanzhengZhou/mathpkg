By definition, inaccessible implies weakly inaccessible. Conversely, if $\aleph_\alpha$ is weakly inaccessible, then for any $x$ with $\text{Inf}(x) \land \bar{x} < \aleph_\alpha$, we have $\bar{x} = \aleph_\beta$ with $\beta < \alpha$. Since $\alpha \in K_{\text{II}}$, $\beta+1 < \alpha$, so $\aleph_{\beta+1} < \aleph_\alpha$. By GCH, $\overline{\mathcal{P}(x)} = \aleph_{\beta+1} < \aleph_\alpha$. Thus $\aleph_\alpha$ is a strong limit cardinal.
