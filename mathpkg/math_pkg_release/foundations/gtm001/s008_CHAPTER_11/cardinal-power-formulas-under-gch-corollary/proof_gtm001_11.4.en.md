These follow directly from Theorem 11.21. For (1), if $\aleph_\alpha \leq \aleph_\beta$, then $\aleph_\alpha \leq \aleph_\beta \leq 2^{\aleph_\beta}$, so by Theorem 11.21 with $b = \aleph_\beta$, $\overline{\aleph_\alpha}^{\aleph_\beta} = 2^{\aleph_\beta}$. (2) is the case $\alpha = \beta$. (3) follows similarly.
