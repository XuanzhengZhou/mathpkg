If each element of $a$ is well founded and $x \in a$, then $(\exists \alpha)[x \in R_1^\prime \alpha]$. If

$$F'x = \mu_\alpha(x \in R_1^\prime \alpha)$$

then since $F$ is a function $F''a$ is a set and indeed a subset of On. Therefore $\cup(F''a)$ is an ordinal. If $\beta = \cup(F''a) + 1$ then $F''a \subseteq \beta$, i.e., $x \in a \rightarrow F'x < \beta$.

By Proposition 9.10

$$R_1^\prime F'x \subseteq R_1^\prime \beta.$$

Also $x \in R_1^\prime F'x$ and so $x \in R_1^\prime \beta$. Thus $a \subseteq R'^\prime \beta$ and hence $a \in \mathcal{P}(R_1^\prime \beta) = R_1^\prime (\beta + 1)$.
