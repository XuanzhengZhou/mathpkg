(1) If each of $t_1$ and $t_2$ is a constant term or a variable, then $(t_1 \in t_2)$ and $(t_1 = t_2)$ are formulas of $\mathcal{L}$.
(2) If $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are formulas, then $(\neg \varphi)$ and $(\varphi \vee \psi)$ are formulas.
(3) If $\varphi$ is a formula, then $(\exists^\alpha x_i \varphi)$ is a formula.
(4) If $\varphi(x_i)$ is a formula without free variables other than $x_i$ such that $g(\varphi(x_i)) < g(\wedge^\alpha)$, then $(\hat{x}_i^\alpha \varphi(x_i))$ is a constant term.
(5) Formulas and terms are only those obtained by a finite number of applications of (1)–(4).
