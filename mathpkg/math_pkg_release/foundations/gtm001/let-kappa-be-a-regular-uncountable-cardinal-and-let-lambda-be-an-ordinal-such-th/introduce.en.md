The lemma **Let $\kappa$ be a regular uncountable cardinal and let $\lambda$ be an ordinal such that $\kappa < \lambda$. For each set $X$ with $X \subseteq \lambda$ and $\overline{X} < \kappa$, there exists a subalgebra $Y$ of $M^{\lambda}$ such that $X$ is a subset of $Y$, $\overline{Y} < \kappa$ and $Y \cap \kappa \in \kappa$** (Lemma 17.6.) is a fundamental notion in axiomatic set theory, introduced in Chapter 17 of Takeuti and Zaring's *Introduction to Axiomatic Set Theory* (2nd ed., 1982). It forms part of the formal development of Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory with classes (GB or ZF).
