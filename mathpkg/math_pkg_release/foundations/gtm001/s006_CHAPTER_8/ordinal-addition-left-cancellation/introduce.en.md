Ordinal addition satisfies a left cancellation law: if $\gamma + \alpha = \gamma + \beta$, then $\alpha = \beta$. The converse is trivial. This follows immediately from the left strict monotonicity of ordinal addition (Proposition 8.4). Note that right cancellation does not hold in general, as shown by $1 + \omega = 2 + \omega$ but $1 \neq 2$.
