In ordinal exponentiation, $0^0 = 1$ by definition. For $\beta \ge 1$, $0^\beta = 0$ (zero raised to any positive exponent is zero). The ordinal $1$ is the identity for exponentiation in the base: $1^\beta = 1$ for all $\beta$.
