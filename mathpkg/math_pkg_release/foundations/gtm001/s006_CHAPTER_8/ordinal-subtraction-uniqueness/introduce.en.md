If $\alpha \le \beta$, then there exists a unique ordinal $\gamma$ such that $\alpha + \gamma = \beta$. This ordinal $\gamma$ is called the difference $\beta - \alpha$. Ordinal subtraction is thus a well-defined operation whenever the subtrahend is at most the minuend, and the result is uniquely determined.
