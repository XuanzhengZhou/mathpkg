**Proof.** This follows immediately from Definition 8.15: since $\beta \in K_{\mathrm{II}}$, $\alpha \cdot \beta = \bigcup_{\delta < \beta} \alpha \cdot \delta$. If $\gamma < \alpha \cdot \beta$, then $\gamma \in \alpha \cdot \beta = \bigcup_{\delta < \beta} \alpha \cdot \delta$, so there exists $\delta < \beta$ such that $\gamma \in \alpha \cdot \delta$, i.e., $\gamma < \alpha \cdot \delta$.
