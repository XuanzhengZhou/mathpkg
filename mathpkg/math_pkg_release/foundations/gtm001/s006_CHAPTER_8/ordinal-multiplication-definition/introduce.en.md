Ordinal multiplication is defined by transfinite recursion, similar to ordinal addition. Multiplying by $0$ yields $0$. Multiplication by a successor $\beta+1$ is defined as $(\alpha \cdot \beta) + \alpha$, i.e., repeated addition. For a limit ordinal $\beta \in K_{\mathrm{II}}$, the product $\alpha \cdot \beta$ is the supremum of all partial products $\alpha \cdot \gamma$ for $\gamma < \beta$. The definition is justified by Proposition 7.44 (transfinite recursion).
