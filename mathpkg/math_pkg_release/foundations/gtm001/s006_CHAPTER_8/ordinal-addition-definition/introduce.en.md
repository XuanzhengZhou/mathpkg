Ordinal addition is defined by transfinite recursion on the second argument. For a fixed ordinal $\alpha$, adding $0$ yields $\alpha$. Adding the successor $\beta+1$ is defined as the successor of $(\alpha+\beta)$. For a limit ordinal $\beta \in K_{\mathrm{II}}$, the sum $\alpha+\beta$ is the supremum of all partial sums $\alpha+\gamma$ for $\gamma<\beta$. This recursive definition is justified by Proposition 7.44 (transfinite recursion theorem).
