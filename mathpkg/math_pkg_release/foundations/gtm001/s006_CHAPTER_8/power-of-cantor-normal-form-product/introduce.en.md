When a term of the form $\alpha^\beta \cdot m$ (with $\alpha \ge \omega$ and $m \in \omega$) is raised to a successor exponent $\gamma$, we have $(\alpha^\beta \cdot m)^\gamma = \alpha^{\beta \cdot \gamma} \cdot m$. For limit exponents, an upper bound is given. This proposition is used in estimating powers of Cantor Normal Form expressions.
