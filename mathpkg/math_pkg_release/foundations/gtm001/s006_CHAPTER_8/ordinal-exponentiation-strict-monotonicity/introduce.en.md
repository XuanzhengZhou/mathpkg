If the base $\gamma$ is strictly greater than $1$, then ordinal exponentiation is strictly monotone in the exponent: $\alpha < \beta$ implies $\gamma^\alpha < \gamma^\beta$. The proof uses the fact that for $\gamma > 1$, $\gamma^\alpha \ge 1$ and $\gamma^\alpha \cdot \gamma = \gamma^{\alpha+1} > \gamma^\alpha$.
