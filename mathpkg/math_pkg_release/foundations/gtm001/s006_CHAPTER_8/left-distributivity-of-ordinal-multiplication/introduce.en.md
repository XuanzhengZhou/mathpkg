Ordinal multiplication distributes from the left over addition: $\alpha \cdot (\beta + \gamma) = \alpha \cdot \beta + \alpha \cdot \gamma$. However, right distributivity does NOT hold in general. A counterexample: $(\omega + 1) \cdot 2 = \omega \cdot 2 + 1 \neq \omega \cdot 2 + 2$.
