Ordinal exponentiation is defined by transfinite recursion. For any $\alpha$, $\alpha^0 = 1$. For a successor $\beta+1$, $\alpha^{\beta+1} = \alpha^\beta \cdot \alpha$ (repeated multiplication). For a limit ordinal $\beta$ with $\alpha \neq 0$, $\alpha^\beta$ is the supremum of $\alpha^\gamma$ for $\gamma < \beta$. If $\alpha = 0$ and $\beta$ is a limit ordinal, then $0^\beta = 0$.
