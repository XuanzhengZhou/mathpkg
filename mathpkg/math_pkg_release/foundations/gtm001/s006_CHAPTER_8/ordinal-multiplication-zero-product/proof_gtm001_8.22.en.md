**Proof.** If $\alpha = 0$ or $\beta = 0$, then from Proposition 8.18, $0 \cdot \beta = 0$ and $\alpha \cdot 0 = 0$. If $\alpha \neq 0$ and $\beta \neq 0$, then $\alpha \geq 1$ and $\beta \geq 1$. By Proposition 8.19 (with $\gamma = \alpha$), $\alpha \cdot 1 \leq \alpha \cdot \beta$, i.e., $\alpha \leq \alpha \cdot \beta$. Since $\alpha \geq 1$, we have $1 \leq \alpha \leq \alpha \cdot \beta$, hence $\alpha \cdot \beta \neq 0$.
