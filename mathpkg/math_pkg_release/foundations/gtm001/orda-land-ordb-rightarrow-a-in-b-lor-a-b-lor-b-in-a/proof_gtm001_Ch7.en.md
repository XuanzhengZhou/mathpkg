We first observe that $A \cap B \subseteq A \land A \cap B \subseteq B$. If $A \cap B \subset A \land A \cap B \subset B$, then $A \cap B \in A \land A \cap B \in B$ (Propositions 7.9 and 7.7) hence $A \cap B \in A \cap B$. But this contradicts Proposition 5.18. Therefore $A \cap B = A$ or $A \cap B = B$, i.e., $A \subseteq B$ or $B \subseteq A$. Hence, by Corollary 7.8

$$A \in B \lor A = B \lor B \in A.$$ $\square$
