Remark. Earlier we used $a^n$ to mean the $n$-fold cross product of $a$ and in our discussion of ordinal arithmetic we used $\alpha^\beta$ to mean the $\beta$th power of $\alpha$. Now we propose a third use, namely that $a^b$ shall denote the set of mapping from $b$ into $a$. We will rely upon the context to make clear what we intend when we use this notation. That will be much simpler than creating new notation.

It is a temptation to add a fourth usage and define cardinal powers by

$$\bar{a}^{\bar{b}} = \overline{a^b}.$$

But we will suppress that urge and not add an addition ambiguity to an already overworked notation. We will however talk about cardinal powers even though we will not introduce a notation for them. Thus when we write $\aleph_\alpha^{\aleph_\beta}$ we will always mean the set of functions from $\aleph_\beta$ into $\aleph_\alpha$. For the associated cardinal number we will write

$$\aleph_\alpha^{\aleph_\beta}.$$
