If $B \in A$, then $B$ is a set. If in addition $A$ is transitive, then by Definition 7.1, $B \subseteq A$. But $B = A$ implies that $A \in A$ which contradicts Corollary 5.19. Consequently $B \subset A$.

Remark. In spite of our claim that we are going to define ordinal numbers to be sets we begin by defining ordinal classes.
