Let $\Pi = \langle \langle \delta_i, \alpha_i, P_i \rangle, \pi_{ij} \rangle_{i,j \in I}$ be a $\kappa$-direct limit system, and $Z \subseteq L_\kappa$.

(1) $\Pi \subseteq Z$ iff $(\forall i \in I)[\delta_i, \alpha_i, P_i \in Z] \wedge (\forall i, j \in I)[i \leq j \rightarrow \pi_{ij} \in Z]$.

(2) $\Pi$ is $Z$-well-founded iff there are no sequences $\langle i_n : n < \omega \rangle$ and $\langle \sigma_n : n < \omega \rangle$ such that $i_n \in I$, $\sigma_n \in Z \cap \delta_{i_n}$ and $(\forall n < \omega)[i_n \leq i_{n+1} \wedge \pi_{i_n i_{n+1}}(\sigma_n) > \sigma_{n+1}]$.
