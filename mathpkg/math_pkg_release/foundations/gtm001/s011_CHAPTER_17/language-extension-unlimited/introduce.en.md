This definition extends the limited forcing language $\mathcal{L}(M, \mathbf{G})$ to include unlimited quantifiers $\exists$ and $\forall$, along with a predicate $M(t)$ expressing that the denotation of $t$ belongs to the ground model $M$. The unlimited quantifiers are coded using the same coding scheme as the logical symbols. This extension allows the expression of full first-order statements about the generic extension $M[G]$ within the forcing language.
