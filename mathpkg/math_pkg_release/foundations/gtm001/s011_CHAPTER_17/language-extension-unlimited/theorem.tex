The language $\mathcal{L}(M, \mathbf{G})$ is extended with unlimited quantifiers. We add a new predicate symbol $M(t)$ meaning "$D^G(t)$ is a member of $M$." The unlimited quantifiers $\exists$ and $\forall$ are coded as $\ulcorner \exists \urcorner = \langle 3, 0 \rangle$ and $\ulcorner \forall \urcorner = \langle 3, 1 \rangle$.
