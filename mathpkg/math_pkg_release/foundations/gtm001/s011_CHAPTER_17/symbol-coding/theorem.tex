The symbols of $\mathcal{L}(M, \mathbf{G})$ are coded by members of $M$. For example:

(1) $\ulcorner \in \urcorner = \langle 0, 0 \rangle$,
(2) $\ulcorner \neg \urcorner = \langle 0, 1 \rangle$,
(3) $\ulcorner \wedge \urcorner = \langle 0, 2 \rangle$,
(4) $\ulcorner \vee \urcorner = \langle 0, 3 \rangle$,
(7) $\ulcorner \mathbf{G} \urcorner = \langle \ldots \rangle$, and for $k \in M$, $\ulcorner \mathbf{k} \urcorner = \langle 1, k \rangle$.
