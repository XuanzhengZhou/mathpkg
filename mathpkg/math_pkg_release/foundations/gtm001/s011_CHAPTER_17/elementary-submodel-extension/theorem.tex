Let $Z$ be an elementary submodel of $L_\kappa$ such that $X \subseteq Z$ and $\overline{Z} = \overline{X}$. Then there exists a $Z'$, an elementary submodel of $L_\kappa$, such that

(1) $Z \subseteq Z'$, $\overline{Z}' = \overline{X}$; and

(2) for any $\lambda \in S$, if $\Pi \subseteq Z$ is a $\lambda$-direct limit system which is not well founded, then $\Pi$ is not $Z'$-well-founded.
