For certain atomic limited sentences $\varphi$ of $\mathcal{L}(M, \mathbf{G})$, a limited sentence $E(\varphi)$ is defined:

(1) If $\varphi$ is $t \in \hat{x}_i^\alpha \psi(x_i)$ and $g(t) < g(\hat{x}_i^\alpha \psi(x_i))$, then $E(\varphi)$ is $\psi(t)$.

(2) If $\varphi$ is $t_1 \in t_2$ and $g(t_2) \leq g(t_1)$, then
$$E(\varphi) \text{ is } \exists^\alpha x_i(x_i \in t_2 \land \forall^\alpha x_j(x_j \in t_1 \leftrightarrow x_j \in x_i)),$$
where $\alpha = \rho(t_1)$ and $x_i, x_j$ are the first and second variables not occurring in $t_1$ or $t_2$.
