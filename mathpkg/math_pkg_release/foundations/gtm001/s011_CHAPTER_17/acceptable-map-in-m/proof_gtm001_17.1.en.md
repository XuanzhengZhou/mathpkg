Note that each of $F_0, J, C_i, T$ and $K$ is absolute with respect to $M$. Let $F_1 = J, F_2 = T, F_3 = K$ and $F_{i+4} = C_i$ for $i < \omega$. We define a sequence $\langle X_n \rangle$ within $M$ by induction on $n$, closing under the operations $F_k$. Since each operation is absolute for $M$, the resulting structure that defines $\pi$ is entirely computable within $M$, and thus $\pi \in M$.
