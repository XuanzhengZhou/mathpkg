Let $N = \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ and let $\{S_1, \ldots, S_t\} \in \mathbb{L}_n$ be orthogonal. Write $S_h = [a_{ij}^h]$. Let $V = N \times N$. We shall say that two distinct elements $(i, j)$ and $(i', j')$ of $V$ are first associates if any one of the following three conditions holds:

(a) $i = i'$;
(b) $j = j'$;
(c) for some $h \in \{1, \ldots, t\}$, $a_{ij}^h = a_{ij'}^h$.

Otherwise $(i, j)$ and $(i', j')$ are second associates. From this 2-class association scheme, we form a 2-class PBIB-design satisfying E6 in the following way. Let $(i, j) \in V$. The following sets are the blocks incident with $(i, j)$:

(d) $\{(i, j'): j' = 1, \ldots, n\}$;
(e) $\{(i', j): i' = 1, \ldots, n\}$;
(f) $\{(i', j'): a_{ij'}^h = a_{ij}^h\}$, $h = 1, \ldots, t$.
