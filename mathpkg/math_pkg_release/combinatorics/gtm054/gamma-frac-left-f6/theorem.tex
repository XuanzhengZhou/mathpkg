Suppose that $\Gamma$ is without isthmuses and that $Z_1, \ldots, Z_{\nu_2(\Gamma)}$ is a simple planar imbedding of $\Gamma$. If for some $k \in \mathbb{N}$, $\rho^\perp(Z_i) = k$ for all $i = 1, \ldots, \nu_2(\Gamma)$, then the planar imbedding is said to be $k$-covalent. If for some $k \in \mathbb{N}$, every simple planar imbedding of $\Gamma$ is $k$-covalent, we say that $\Gamma$ is $k$-covalent. We say that $\Gamma$ is isocovalent if it is $k$-covalent for some $k \in \mathbb{N}$.

Examples. The circuit $\Delta_n$ is $n$-covalent. $K_4$ is both 3-valent and 3-covalent. A multigraph with $\nu_0 = 2$ and $\nu_1 \geq 2$ is $\nu_1$-valent and 2-covalent.
