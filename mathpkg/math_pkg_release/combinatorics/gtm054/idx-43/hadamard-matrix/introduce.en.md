A Hadamard matrix is an $m \times m$ matrix with entries $\pm 1$ whose rows are mutually orthogonal, i.e., the inner product of any two distinct rows is zero. Hadamard matrices are closely connected to symmetric block designs and have applications in coding theory, statistics, and combinatorics. The order $m$ of a Hadamard matrix with $m > 2$ must be divisible by $4$.
