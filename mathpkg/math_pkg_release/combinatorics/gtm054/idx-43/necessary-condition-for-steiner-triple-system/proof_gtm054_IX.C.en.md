For a Steiner triple system with $k = 3$ and $\lambda = 1$, Equation C8 becomes $v - 1 = 2r$, and so $v$ must be odd. Solving for $r$ and substituting into $vr = bk$ (C7) gives $b = v(v-1)/6$. Since $b$ must be an integer, $v(v-1)$ must be divisible by $6$, which together with $v$ being odd implies $v \equiv 1$ or $3 \pmod{6}$.
