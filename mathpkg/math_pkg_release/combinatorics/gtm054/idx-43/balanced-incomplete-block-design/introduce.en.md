A balanced incomplete block design (BIB-design) is a $(1, 2; 1, 2)$-design, the most broadly studied class of designs. It is characterized by five parameters $b, v, r, k, \lambda$, often called a $(b, v, r, k, \lambda)$-configuration after H. J. Ryser. However, these five values may not uniquely characterize the design up to isomorphism.
