H. J. Ryser refers to a BIB-design as a $(b, v, r, k, \lambda)$-configuration. While these five values name a design, they need not characterize it uniquely up to isomorphism. For example, there are two nonisomorphic $(26, 13, 6, 3, 1)$-configurations.
