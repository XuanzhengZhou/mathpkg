Clearly $\Lambda_0$ has blocksize $k$. Also if $s_1, s_2$, and $s_0$ are the selections of $\Lambda_1, \Lambda_2$, and $\Lambda_0$, respectively, and if $S \subseteq V$, then $\bar{s}_0(S) = \bar{s}_1(S) + \bar{s}_2(S)$.

We call $\Lambda_0$ as in the above proposition the sum of $\Lambda_1$ and $\Lambda_2$ and denote it by $\Lambda_1 + \Lambda_2$. This leads at once to the concept of the $n$th multiple $n\Lambda$ of a design $\Lambda$, obtained by replicating each block $n$ times. If $\Lambda$ is not the sum of two other designs we say $\Lambda$ is indecomposable. Otherwise we say $\Lambda$ is decomposable.
