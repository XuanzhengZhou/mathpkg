A \textbf{region} of a topological imbedding of a graph $\Gamma$ in a surface $S$ is a connected component of the complement in $S$ of the image of the topological imbedding of $\Gamma$. If every such region is homeomorphic to an open disk, the topological imbedding is called a \textbf{2-cell imbedding}.

By abuse of terminology, the vertices and edges of $\Gamma$ are identified with the images under a topological imbedding of their corresponding subspaces in a topological realization of $\Gamma$. If $R$ is a region, the set of edges, each of which belongs to the boundary of both $R$ and some region other than $R$, corresponds to a cycle of $\Gamma$, called the \textbf{cycle of $R$}.
