When a graph $\Gamma$ is topologically imbedded in a surface $S$, the complement of the imbedding decomposes into connected components called regions. When every region is homeomorphic to an open disk, the imbedding is called a 2-cell imbedding. Each region $R$ determines a cycle of $\Gamma$ consisting of those edges that belong to the boundary of both $R$ and some other region. This framework is foundational for understanding the relationship between graph embeddings and the topology of surfaces.
