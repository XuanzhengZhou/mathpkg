An almost factorable graph is an odd graph that is as close to having a 1-factor (perfect matching) as possible. Specifically, for every vertex $x$, the subgraph obtained by removing $x$ admits a perfect matching. The complete graphs $K_{2n+1}$ provide the simplest examples of almost factorable graphs.
