For a set $U$, the collection $\mathcal{E}(U)$ consists of all subsets of $U$ whose cardinality is even. This subset of the power set $\mathcal{P}(U)$ plays a fundamental role in the algebraic study of finite sets, where $\mathcal{P}(U)$ is viewed as a vector space over the field $\mathbb{K}$ with symmetric difference as addition. The space $\mathcal{E}(U)$ provides a natural example of a proper subspace of $\mathcal{P}(U)$ and serves as the foundation for studying orthogonality and dimension formulas in this combinatorial vector space setting.
