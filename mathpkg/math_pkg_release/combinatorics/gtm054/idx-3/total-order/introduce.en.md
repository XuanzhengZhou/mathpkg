A total order (also called a linear order) is a partial order with the additional property that every pair of elements is comparable. In a totally-ordered set, any two elements can be ranked relative to each other. Chains in a partially-ordered set are subsets that become totally ordered under the induced order, and the study of maximal chains is central to the combinatorial theory of posets.
