This theorem states the familiar dimension formula from linear algebra in the context of the power set vector space: the sum of the dimensions of the join and intersection of two subspaces equals the sum of their individual dimensions. As a corollary, when the subspaces have disjoint foundations (forming a coordinate sum), the dimension is additive, i.e., the dimension of the direct sum is the sum of the dimensions.
