A maximal chain in a finite partially-ordered set is a chain that cannot be extended by adding any element while remaining totally ordered. Such a chain necessarily contains both a maximal element and a minimal element of the entire poset; otherwise the chain could be extended by appending a larger or smaller element. Maximal chains play a role analogous to bases in vector spaces, providing a way to navigate from minimal to maximal elements through a sequence of covering relations.
