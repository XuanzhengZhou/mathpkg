This proposition states that the refinement relation on the set $\mathbb{P}(U)$ of all partitions of $U$ satisfies the axioms of a partial order. Combined with the previously established properties that refinement is reflexive (B3) and antisymmetric (B4), and the transitivity established in Exercise B5, refinement qualifies as a partial order. This places $\mathbb{P}(U)$ alongside $\mathcal{P}(U)$ (ordered by inclusion) as a naturally occurring partially-ordered set in combinatorics.
