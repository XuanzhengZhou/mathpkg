Elementary sets are the minimal nonempty members of a collection $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets closed under symmetric difference (a subspace of the vector space $(\mathcal{P}(U), +)$ over $\mathbb{F}_2$). They play a role analogous to atoms in a lattice: they form an incommensurable family that generates the entire subspace. For example, in the full power set $\mathcal{P}(U)$, the elementary sets are the singletons; in the collection of even-cardinality subsets, they are the $2$-element subsets. The concept of elementary sets is foundational for studying the structure of set systems via their minimal generators.
