\textbf{Sperner's Theorem.} Show that any largest incommensurable subcollection of $\mathcal{P}(U)$ has cardinality

$$\binom{|U|}{\left\lfloor \frac{|U|}{2} \right\rfloor}.$$

(Hint: Let $\mathcal{C}$ be the set of $(|U| + 1)$-chains in $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$. For each $S \in \mathcal{P}(U)$, let $\mathcal{C}_S$ consist of those chains in $\mathcal{C}$ of which $S$ is an element. Begin by showing that if $\mathcal{S} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(U)$ is incommensurable, then $|\mathcal{C}| \geq \sum_{S \in \mathcal{S}} |\mathcal{C}_S|$.)
