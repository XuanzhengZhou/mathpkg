In a power set $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$ partially ordered by inclusion, any nonempty subcollection that is closed under intersection must contain a unique minimum element with respect to inclusion. Dually, any nonempty subcollection closed under union must contain a maximum element. This simple consequence of closure properties is fundamental for establishing the existence of extremal elements in set-theoretic structures.
