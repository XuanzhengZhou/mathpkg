In a partially ordered set, a successor of an element $v$ is a strictly greater element $w$ with no intermediate elements strictly between them. This corresponds to a covering relation in the Hasse diagram of the poset. The concept is essential for constructing minimal directed graph representations of partial orders, where only successor edges need be included to recover the full order via transitive closure.
