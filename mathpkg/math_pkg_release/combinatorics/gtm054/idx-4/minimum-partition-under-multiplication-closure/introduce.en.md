In the lattice of partitions $\mathbb{P}(U)$ of a set $U$, ordered by refinement, any nonempty subcollection that is closed under the operation of partition multiplication necessarily contains a minimum element. This corollary extends the set-intersection closure principle to the realm of partitions, where the multiplication of two partitions yields their greatest common refinement.
