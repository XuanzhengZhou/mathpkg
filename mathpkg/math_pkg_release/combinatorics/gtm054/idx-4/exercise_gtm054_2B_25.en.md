Given a partially ordered set $(V, \leq)$, let $D_0 = \{(v, w): w \text{ is a successor of } v\}$. Show that the partial order determined by the directed graph $(V, D_0)$ is precisely $(V, \leq)$. Moreover, $D_0 \subseteq D_1 \subseteq D$ if and only if $(V, D_1)$ determines $(V, \leq)$, where $D = \{(v, w) \in V \times V : v \leq w\}$.
