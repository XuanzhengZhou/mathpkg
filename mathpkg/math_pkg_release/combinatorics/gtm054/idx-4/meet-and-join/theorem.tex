Let $(U, \leq)$ be a partially ordered set. For $x, y \in U$, the \textit{meet} of $x$ and $y$, denoted by $x \wedge y$, is the maximum element of $\{z \in U: z \leq x \text{ and } z \leq y\}$, if it exists. The \textit{join} of $x$ and $y$, denoted by $x \vee y$, is the minimum element of $\{z \in U: x \leq z \text{ and } y \leq z\}$, if it exists.
