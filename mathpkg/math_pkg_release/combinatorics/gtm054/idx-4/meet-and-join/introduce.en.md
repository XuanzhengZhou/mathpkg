The meet (greatest lower bound, $\wedge$) and join (least upper bound, $\vee$) are the fundamental binary operations of lattice theory. When they exist for every pair of elements in a poset, the structure becomes a lattice. The meet of two elements is their largest common lower bound, while the join is their smallest common upper bound. These operations generalize the intersection and union of sets in the Boolean lattice $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$.
