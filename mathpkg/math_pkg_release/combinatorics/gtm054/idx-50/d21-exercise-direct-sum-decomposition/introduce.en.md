This exercise demonstrates that the direct sum operation on matroids behaves componentwise for all four fundamental equivalent formulations: independence systems, basis systems, spanning systems, and rank functions. It shows why matroid direct sum is the appropriate notion, and why one cannot substitute "independence system," "basis system," or "spanning system" for "matroid" in the direct sum theorems D18 and D19.
