This exercise shows that the class of complete matroids is closed under the operation of taking minors. A complete matroid is one in which every subset of the vertex set with size not exceeding the rank is independent; proving that this property is inherited by minors provides a useful structural result analogous to the fact that complete graphs have only complete graphs as minors.
