A matroid $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ with rank function $r$ is called $m$-separated if it admits a nontrivial partition of its vertex set where the rank deficiency across the cut equals $m$. This generalizes Tutte's notion of $m$-connectedness for graphs to the matroid setting. The first condition requires both sides of the partition to contain more than $m$ vertices, ensuring the separation is nontrivial; the second condition measures the rank deficiency across the cut.
