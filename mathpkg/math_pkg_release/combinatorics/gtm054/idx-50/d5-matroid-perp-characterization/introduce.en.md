This proposition provides a direct characterization of the collection $\mathcal{M}^\perp$ of minimal sets that meet every basis of a matroid $\Lambda$, without going through the intermediate collections $\mathcal{I}(\Lambda)$, $\mathcal{B}(\Lambda)$, $\mathcal{B}^\perp(\Lambda)$, and $\mathcal{I}^\perp(\Lambda)$. It states that $\mathcal{M}^\perp$ equals the collection of minimal sets in the family $\mathcal{A}$ of all subsets of $V$ that intersect each cycle $M \in \mathcal{M}$ in a number of elements other than one. Moreover, every set in $\mathcal{A}$ can be expressed as a union of sets from $\mathcal{M}^\perp$.
