Let $\Gamma$ be a Moore graph of type $(\rho, 2)$. Thus $\rho = n_1$ and by Theorem E15 it suffices to prove that $p_{11}^1$ and $p_{11}^2$ are constant. Let $x_0 \in V$ and let $V_0, V_1$, and $V_2$ be as above. Let $y \in V_1$ and $z \in V_2$. Due to the structure of a Moore graph, $p_{11}^1(x_0, y) = 0$ and $p_{11}^2(x_0, z) = 1$. The result follows since $x_0$ was chosen arbitrarily, $y$ was an arbitrary first associate of $x_0$, and $z$ was an arbitrary second associate of $x_0$.
