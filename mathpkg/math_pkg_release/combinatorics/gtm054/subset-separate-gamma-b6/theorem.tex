$$S' = [S'' \cap (V' \cup D')] \cup \{(a, x): a \in A; (a_j, x) \in S'' \text{ for some } j = 1, \ldots, g(a)\} \cup \{a \in A: \{a_1, \ldots, a_{g(a)}\} \subseteq S''\}.$$

Hence $|S''| \geq |S'|$.

We assert that $|S''| \geq m$. Were that not so, we would have $A'' \not\subseteq S''$ by B6, and consequently $A \not\subseteq S'$. Also $|S'| < m$, implying, by the way that $\Gamma'$ was constructed from $\Gamma$ and by the hypothesis, that $S'$ fails to separate $Z$ from $A$ in $\Gamma'$. Hence there would exist an AZ-path $\Pi'$ in $\Gamma'_{(S')}$ whose initial vertex is some $a \in A + (A \cap S')$. Also $\Pi'$ contains an edge $(a, x)$ for some $x \in V'$. It follows that for some $j \in \{1, \ldots, g(a)\}$, neither $a_j$ nor the edge $(a_j, x)$ is an element of $S'$.
