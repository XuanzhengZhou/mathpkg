Let $X$ be an $n$-set and let $S \in \mathcal{P}_k(X)$. How many permutations of $X$ fix $S$ pointwise? How many fix $S$ (set-wise)? How many map some given point $x \in X$ onto some point of $S$?

For $m, n \in \mathbb{N}$ it is conventional to write

$$\binom{n}{m} = \begin{cases} \frac{n!}{m!(n-m)!} & \text{if } m \leq n; \\ 0 & \text{if } m > n. \end{cases}$$

Observe that $\binom{n}{m} = \binom{n-n}{m}$ if $m \leq n$.
