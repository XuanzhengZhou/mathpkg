The Five-Color Theorem strengthens the Six-Color Theorem by reducing the upper bound on the vertex chromatic number of planar graphs from six to five. The proof uses a more refined argument: if the chromatic number were greater than five, one can construct a planar graph where a vertex of degree five forces a contradiction via Kempe chain recoloring arguments. This is the strongest result provable by elementary methods without computer assistance; the Four-Color Theorem requires substantially more machinery.
