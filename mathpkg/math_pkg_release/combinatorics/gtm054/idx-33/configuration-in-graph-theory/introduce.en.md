In the Appel-Haken approach to the Four-Color Theorem, a configuration is a pair $(\Gamma, S)$ consisting of a graph $\Gamma$ and a subset of vertices $S$ satisfying two conditions: the subgraph induced by $S$ (the "boundary") is an elementary circuit, and the subgraph induced by the complement of $S$ (the "interior") is connected. Configurations are the building blocks of the unavoidable set method used in the computer-assisted proof.
