This exercise asks the reader to prove a structural property of graphs that admit triangular imbeddings: every such graph without an isthmus is biconnected, and except for the triangle $K_3$, is even triconnected. A triangular imbedding is a planar imbedding in which every region is bounded by exactly three edges, making it the densest possible planar embedding. This connectivity result is important in the study of the Four-Color Problem, as triangular imbeddings play a central role in reformulating the conjecture.
