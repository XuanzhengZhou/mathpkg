This exercise asks the reader to prove that planar graphs with girth at least 6 (i.e., no cycles of length 3, 4, or 5) are 3-colorable. The proof technique parallels those used for the girth-4 result (Corollary B7), leveraging lower bounds on face degrees in planar embeddings to force sufficiently small average vertex degree, enabling an inductive coloring argument.
