This proposition provides a reduction step for proving the Four-Color Theorem. If a planar graph has a vertex of degree at most 4, and the graph with that vertex removed is 4-colorable, then the original graph is also 4-colorable. The proof uses a 5-coloring argument and Kempe chain techniques similar to those employed in the proof of the Five-Color Theorem. This result implies that any counterexample to the Four-Color Conjecture must have all vertices of degree at least 5.
