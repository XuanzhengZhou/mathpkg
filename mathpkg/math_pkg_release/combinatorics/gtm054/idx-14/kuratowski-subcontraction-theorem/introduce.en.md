This proposition, labeled G11 by Graver and Watkins, is a Kuratowski-type theorem for the subcontraction relation on graphs. It states that the only nonplanar graphs that are minimal with respect to subcontraction (i.e., every proper subcontraction is planar) are the complete graph $K_5$ and the complete bipartite graph $K_{3,3}$. Together with its converse, this yields that a graph is nonplanar if and only if it can be contracted to $K_5$ or $K_{3,3}$. The proof uses triconnectivity (from Exercise G7a), properties of separating sets (G7b), and a planar embedding argument via cycle spaces.
