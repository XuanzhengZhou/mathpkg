For $S \subseteq X$, the characteristic function $c_S \in \mathbb{Q}^X$ is defined by
$$c_S(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } x \in S, \\ 0 & \text{if } x \notin S, \end{cases}$$
where the symbols $0$ and $1$ are understood to be the rational numbers $0, 1 \in \mathbb{Q}$. For a singleton $\{x\}$, we write $c_x$ in place of $c_{\{x\}}$. The function $c_\emptyset$ is the additive identity of the vector space $\mathbb{Q}^X$, and $c_X$ is the multiplicative identity of the algebra $\mathbb{Q}^X$. The symbols $0$ and $1$ are also used to denote $c_\emptyset$ and $c_X$ respectively.
