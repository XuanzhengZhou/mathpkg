In the context of $\mathbb{Q}^X$, characteristic functions $c_S$ are defined analogously to their counterparts in $\mathbb{K}^X$, but with the crucial difference that the values $0$ and $1$ are now understood as rational numbers rather than Boolean elements. This shift has algebraic consequences: $c_\emptyset$ becomes the additive identity of $\mathbb{Q}^X$ and $c_X$ the multiplicative identity. The notation $c_x$ is used for singleton characteristic functions, and the set $\{c_x : x \in X\}$ plays the role of a distinguished basis for $\mathbb{Q}^X$, mirroring the standard basis of the free vector space on $X$ over $\mathbb{Q}$.
