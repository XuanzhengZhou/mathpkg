This corollary gives the classical formula for $D_n$, the number of derangements (permutations with no fixed points) of an $n$-set, obtained by setting $r = 0$ in Proposition E16. The ratio $D_n/n!$ is the $(n+1)$-st partial sum of the power series for $e^{-1}$, so the probability that a random permutation is a derangement approaches $1/e \approx 0.368$ as $n \to \infty$.
