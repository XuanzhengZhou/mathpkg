If $V$ and $E$ are sets and $f: E \to \mathcal{P}(V)$, the \textbf{transpose} of $f$ is the function $f^*: V \to \mathcal{P}(E)$ given by

$$f^*(x) = \{e \in E : x \in f(e)\}.$$

The transpose satisfies the fundamental duality:

$$x \in f(e) \iff e \in f^*(x), \quad \text{for all } x \in V,\; e \in E.$$
