The Principle of Inclusion–Exclusion, in its most general form for systems, counts vertices belonging to exactly $r$ blocks (or blocks of exactly size $k$) in terms of cumulative counts over supersets. Part (a) uses the dual cumulative selection $\bar{s}^*(A) = |\bigcap_{e\in A} f(e)|$ to count vertices, while part (b) uses $\bar{s}(S)$ to count blocks. The two forms are dual under the transpose operation, and both follow from the Möbius inversion formula E6.
