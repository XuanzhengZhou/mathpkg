This proposition counts permutations of an $n$-set with exactly $r$ fixed points, generalizing the derangement number $D_n$ (which is the case $r = 0$). The proof applies the Principle of Inclusion–Exclusion to the system where vertices are permutations of $B$ and blocks are elements of $B$, with a permutation belonging to block $b$ if $b$ is a fixed point of the permutation.
