A \textbf{partition of the positive integer $n$} is a selection $s \in \mathbb{S}(\mathbb{N} \cup \{0\})$ such that
$$\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} i\,s(i) = n.$$

If $|s| = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} s(i) = m$, then $s$ is called an \textbf{$m$-partition of $n$}. The value $s(i)$ is the number of parts of size $i$ in the partition.
