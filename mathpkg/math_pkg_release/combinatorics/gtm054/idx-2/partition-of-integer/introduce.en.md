A partition of a positive integer $n$ is a way of writing $n$ as a sum of positive integers, with the selection $s(i)$ counting how many times the integer $i$ appears as a summand. An $m$-partition uses exactly $m$ parts. This definition arises naturally from counting isomorphism classes of set partitions: each isomorphism class of an $m$-cell partition of an $n$-set corresponds uniquely to an $m$-partition of $n$.
