Euler's totient function $\varphi(n)$ counts the positive integers up to $n$ that are coprime to $n$. It is a fundamental arithmetic function in number theory, satisfying the multiplicative property $\varphi(ab) = \varphi(a)\varphi(b)$ for coprime $a, b$, and the product formula $\varphi(n) = n\prod_{p\mid n}(1 - 1/p)$. The book uses it to demonstrate the power of the Principle of Inclusion–Exclusion.
