This theorem derives the classical product formula for Euler's totient function $\varphi(n)$ using the Principle of Inclusion–Exclusion. The proof constructs a system where vertices are the integers $\{1, \ldots, n\}$ and blocks correspond to prime divisors of $n$, with an integer $b$ belonging to block $p$ if $p$ divides $b$. The totient $\varphi(n)$ then counts the number of blocks of size 0 (integers coprime to $n$).
