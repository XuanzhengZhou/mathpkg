A normal set is a special type of extremal vertex subset that plays a central role in Tutte's theory of 1-factors. It satisfies three conditions: it is extremal (achieving equality in the deficiency bound), its vertex set together with its neighbor set spans the entire graph, and each component of the subgraph induced by the set is itself almost factorable. Normal sets provide the structural foundation for constructing maximum matchings in general graphs.
