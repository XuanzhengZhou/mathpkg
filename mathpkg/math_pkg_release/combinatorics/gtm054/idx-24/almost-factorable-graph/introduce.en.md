An almost factorable graph is an odd graph that is as close to admitting a 1-factor as possible: while the graph itself cannot have a 1-factor (due to its odd number of vertices), every subgraph obtained by deleting a single vertex does admit one. This concept is essential in Tutte's characterization of graphs that admit 1-factors, as almost factorable graphs arise as the connected components of the subgraph induced by a normal set. The complete graph $K_{2n+1}$ is the canonical example of an almost factorable graph.
