A finite projective plane is defined within the framework of symmetric block designs as one with $\lambda = 1$ and at least 4 points. In this geometric language, vertices become "points" and blocks become "lines." Every two distinct points determine a unique line, and every two distinct lines intersect in a unique point — the classical axioms of projective geometry.
