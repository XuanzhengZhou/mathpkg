Proposition D3 characterizes finite projective planes via three incidence conditions: (a) any two points determine a unique line, (b) any two lines meet in a unique point, and (c) there exists a non-degenerate quadrangle (four points, no three collinear). These are the classical axioms of projective geometry. The proposition shows that any $(2;2)$-design (where each pair of points is on exactly two blocks) with $v \geq 4$ satisfying (c) is in fact a projective plane with $\lambda = 1$.
