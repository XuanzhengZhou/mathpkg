This proposition shows that the Kronecker (tensor) product of two Hadamard matrices is again a Hadamard matrix. Specifically, if $H_1$ is $m_1 \times m_1$ and $H_2$ is $m_2 \times m_2$, then $H_1 \times H_2$ is $m_1 m_2 \times m_1 m_2$ and satisfies the Hadamard condition $(H_1 \times H_2)(H_1 \times H_2)^* = m_1 m_2 I$. This closure property, together with the base case of a $2 \times 2$ Hadamard matrix, yields the existence of $2^n \times 2^n$ Hadamard matrices for all $n$.
