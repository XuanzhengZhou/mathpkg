[m.10]. Let $m$ be a positive integer, and let $\Gamma = (V, \mathcal{E})$ be a graph with $\nu_0(\Gamma) \geq m + 1$. The following 2$m$ statements are equivalent:

A. $\Gamma$ is $m$-connected.

$B_k(k = 1, \ldots, m)$. Given $\{a, z_1, \ldots, z_k\} \in \mathcal{P}_{k+1}(V)$, there exists an openly disjoint $m$-family of $a\{z_1, \ldots, z_k\}$-paths of which $m - k + 1$ are $az_1$-paths and one is an $az_i$-path for each $i = 2, \ldots, k$.

$C_k(k = 1, \ldots, m - 1)$. Given $S \in \mathcal{P}_k(V)$, and $\{a, z\} \in \mathcal{P}_2(V + S)$, there exists an openly-disjoint $(m - k)$-family of az-paths in $\Gamma_{(s)}$.
