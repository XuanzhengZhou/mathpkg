Proposition C9 establishes a chain of inequalities connecting the vertex independence number $\alpha_0$, the vertex covering number $\beta_{10}$, the edge independence (matching) number $\alpha_1$, the edge covering number $\beta_{01}$, and the total number of vertices $\nu_0$. These inequalities hold for any graph without isolated vertices. The proposition also characterizes when equalities occur: equality in any left-hand inequality forces equality in all three left-hand inequalities, while equality in any right-hand inequality (or $\delta \leq \alpha_0$) forces equality in all three right-hand inequalities and $\delta = \alpha_0$.
