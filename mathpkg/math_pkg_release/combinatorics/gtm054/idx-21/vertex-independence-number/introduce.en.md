The vertex-independence number $\alpha_0(\Gamma)$ of a graph $\Gamma$ is the maximum cardinality of an independent vertex set in $\Gamma$. Also called the internal stability number, it is a fundamental graph parameter that measures how many pairwise nonadjacent vertices can be selected. Together with the edge-independence number $\alpha_1(\Gamma)$, it quantifies the independence structure of the graph and plays a key role in matching theory and extremal graph theory.
