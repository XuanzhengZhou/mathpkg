The deficiency $\delta(U)$ of a vertex subset $U$ in a bipartite graph is defined as $|U| - |N(U)|$, measuring how many more vertices $U$ has than its neighbor set. A negative deficiency means the neighbor set is larger than $U$, while a positive deficiency indicates that $U$ lacks enough neighbors to be fully matched. This quantity is the key ingredient in the statement of Hall's marriage condition and the Main Matching Theorem.
