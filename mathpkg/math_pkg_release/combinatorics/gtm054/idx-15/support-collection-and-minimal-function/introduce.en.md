For a subspace $L$ of $\mathbb{Q}^X$, the collection $\mathcal{N}(L)$ consists of all supports $\sigma(h)$ of functions $h \in L$. Within $\mathcal{N}(L)$, the elementary sets form the subcollection $\mathcal{M}(L)$, and a function whose support is elementary is called a minimal function. These notions are central to the structure theory of subspaces of $\mathbb{Q}^X$ and lead to the $\mathcal{M}$-decomposition theorem.
