By condition (c) of the $\mathcal{M}$-decomposition, $h_i(x)h(x) \geq 0$ for all $x \in X$ and all $i$. This means that all the $h_i(x)$ have the same sign as $h(x)$ (or are zero). Therefore the sum $h(x) = \sum_{i=1}^m h_i(x)$ is a sum of terms with identical signs, which implies $|h(x)| = \sum_{i=1}^m |h_i(x)|$.
