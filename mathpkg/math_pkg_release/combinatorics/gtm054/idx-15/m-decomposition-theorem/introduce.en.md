The $\mathcal{M}$-Decomposition Theorem (Proposition A6) is the central result of Section 15. It states that every function $h$ in a subspace $L \subseteq \mathbb{Q}^X$ can be expressed as a sum of minimal functions $h_i$ whose supports are elementary sets contained in $\sigma(h)$, and whose signs never oppose that of $h$. This decomposition is fundamental to the analysis of subspaces of $\mathbb{Q}^X$.
