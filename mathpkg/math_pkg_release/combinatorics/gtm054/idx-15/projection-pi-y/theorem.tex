For each subset $Y \subseteq X$, define the projection $\pi_Y: \mathbb{Q}^X \to \mathbb{Q}^Y$ where $\pi_Y(h)$ is the product $hc_Y$ for each $h \in \mathbb{Q}^X$. Thus $\pi_Y(h)$ is the restriction $h|_Y$ extended by $0$ to all of $X$. The image of $\pi_Y$ is identified with $\mathbb{Q}^Y$, and its kernel is $\mathbb{Q}^{X \setminus Y}$. Thus $\pi_Y[L]$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{Q}^Y$ whenever $L$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{Q}^X$.
