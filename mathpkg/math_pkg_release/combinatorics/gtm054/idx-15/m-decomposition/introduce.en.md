An $\mathcal{M}$-decomposition of a function $h \in L$ is a representation of $h$ as a sum of minimal functions whose supports are elementary sets contained in $\sigma(h)$ and that never oppose the sign of $h$. This terminology is introduced after Proposition A6 and is used throughout the subsequent theory of subspaces of $\mathbb{Q}^X$.
