Let $L = \{f \in \mathbb{Q}^X : \sum_{x \in X} f(x) = 0\}$. Then $L$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{Q}^X$ and $\mathcal{M}(L) = \mathcal{P}_2(X)$ (the collection of all $2$-element subsets of $X$). An elementary function $f$ with $\sigma(f) = \{x, y\}$ has the form

$$f(u) = \begin{cases} \pm 1 & \text{if } u = x \\ \mp 1 & \text{if } u = y \\ 0 & \text{if } u \in X \setminus \{x, y\}. \end{cases}$$

Thus $L$ is unimodular. Moreover, $L^{\perp}$ is the subspace of constant functions in $\mathbb{Q}^X$, and $\{\sigma(f) : f \in L\} = \mathcal{E}(X)$ (the collection of all nonempty subsets of $X$), while $\{\sigma(f) : f \in L^{\perp}\} = \{\varnothing, X\}$.
