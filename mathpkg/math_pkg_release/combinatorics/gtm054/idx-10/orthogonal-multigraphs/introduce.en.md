Two multigraphs sharing a common edge set are orthogonal if the cycle space of one equals the cocycle space of the other. The relation is symmetric: $\Gamma \perp \Theta$ implies $\Theta \perp \Gamma$. Orthogonal multigraphs have no isthmuses and, if neither has isolated vertices, they are either both biconnected or both not biconnected. This concept generalizes the classical notion of a dual graph, and Whitney characterized planar multigraphs as precisely those admitting an orthogonal multigraph.
