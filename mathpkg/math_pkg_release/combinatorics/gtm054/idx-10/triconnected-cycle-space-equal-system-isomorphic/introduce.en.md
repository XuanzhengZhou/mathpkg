For triconnected multigraphs, the cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)$ uniquely determines the multigraph up to system-isomorphism, provided the second multigraph has no isolated vertices. This is a fundamental uniqueness result: the algebraic structure of cycles alone is sufficient to recover the combinatorial graph structure when the graph is sufficiently connected.
