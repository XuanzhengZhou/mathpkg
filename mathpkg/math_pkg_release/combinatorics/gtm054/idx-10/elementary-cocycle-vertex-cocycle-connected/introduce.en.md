In a biconnected multigraph, vertex cocycles can be distinguished algebraically from other cocycles. If an elementary cocycle $C$ is removed and the orthogonal complement of the resulting submultigraph remains connected, then $C$ must be a vertex cocycle. This provides a combinatorial criterion for identifying vertex cocycles purely through connectivity properties of the cycle space.
