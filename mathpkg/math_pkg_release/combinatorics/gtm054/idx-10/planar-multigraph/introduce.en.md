A multigraph is called planar if it admits a planar imbedding -- a sequence of cycles that span the cycle space with each edge belonging to exactly two of the cycles. This combinatorial definition of planarity, due to MacLane, is equivalent to the topological notion of being embeddable in the plane without edge crossings. It forms the foundation for the algebraic approach to planarity developed in this text.
