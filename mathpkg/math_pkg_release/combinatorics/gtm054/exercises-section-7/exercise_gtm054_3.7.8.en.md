Show that if $\Lambda$ is allowed to be infinite, then the two-way implication in Corollary E5 need hold in only one sense [L. Babai, L. Lovász].

Consider an $st$-path $s = s_0, s_1, \ldots, s_n = t$ in $\Lambda$, and let us say, for definiteness, that $s \in V$. One easily verifies that for any $(p, q) \in G(\Lambda)$, the sequence $q(s_0), p(s_1), q(s_2), \ldots, p(s_{2i-1}), q(s_{2i}), \ldots, p(t)$ or $q(t)$ is also a path in $\Lambda$.
