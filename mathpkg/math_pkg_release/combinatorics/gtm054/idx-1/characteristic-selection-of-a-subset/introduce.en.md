The characteristic selection $s_S$ is the selection analog of the characteristic function, taking values in $\mathbb{N}$ instead of $\mathbb{K}$. The distinction is subtle but important: $1+1 = 2$ in $\mathbb{N}$ but $1+1 = 0$ in $\mathbb{K}$, so the algebraic structures differ. The correspondence $S \mapsto s_S$ gives a natural injection of $\mathcal{P}(U)$ into $\mathbb{S}(U)$.
