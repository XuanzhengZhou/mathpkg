A partition of $U$ is a collection $\mathcal{Q}$ of nonempty, pairwise disjoint subsets of $U$ whose union is $U$. The members of $\mathcal{Q}$ are called cells. The set of all partitions of $U$ is $\mathbb{P}(U)$. If $\mathcal{Q}$ has exactly $m$ cells, it is an $m$-partition, and $\mathbb{P}_m(U)$ is the collection of $m$-partitions of $U$.
