The transversal matroid of a set system $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ is the matroid on $V$ whose independent sets are exactly the transversals (systems of distinct representatives) of $\Lambda$. By E1 Lemma, the transversals form an independence system, and the transversal matroid is the unique matroid having this independence system. Transversal matroids form an important class of matroids arising naturally from combinatorial matching problems and are closely related to matching matroids of bipartite graphs.
