The proof proceeds by successive applications of the various parts of D13 (duality commutes with restriction and contraction) and D14 (composition properties). By repeatedly applying the identities in D13, one can push contractions to the left and restrictions to the right, eventually obtaining the desired factorization. The key step uses the fact that $(\Lambda_U)_{[T]} = (\Lambda_{[V \setminus U]})_T$ and similar identities to reorder the operations, ultimately expressing $\Lambda^{(n)}$ as $(\Lambda^{(0)}_{[T]})_{V_n}$ for appropriately chosen $T \in \mathcal{P}(V_0)$ containing $V_n$.
