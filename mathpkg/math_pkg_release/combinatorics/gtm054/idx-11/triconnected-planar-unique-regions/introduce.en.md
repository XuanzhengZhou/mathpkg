Exercise E20 (essentially Whitney's Theorem for planar graphs) asks to prove that for a triconnected planar graph, the set of regions in any planar imbedding is uniquely determined — there is essentially only one way to embed the graph in the plane. Moreover, there is a unique connected dual multigraph $\Theta$. This is a fundamental result in topological graph theory: triconnectivity guarantees a rigid planar embedding, whereas graphs of lower connectivity may admit multiple distinct planar imbeddings.
