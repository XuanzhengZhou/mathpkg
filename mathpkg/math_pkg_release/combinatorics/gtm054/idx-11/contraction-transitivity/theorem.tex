Prove:

(a) If $\Gamma_2$ is a contraction of $\Gamma_1$ and if $\Gamma_1$ is a contraction of $\Gamma$, then $\Gamma_2$ is a contraction of $\Gamma$.

(b) Every contraction of $\Gamma$ may be obtained by starting with $\Gamma_0 = \Gamma$ and iterating operations of three types: (i) $\Gamma_i = (\Gamma_{i-1})_{(e)}$ for some edge $e \in E_{i-1}$ that is an isthmus; (ii) $\Gamma_i = (\Gamma_{i-1})_{(e)}$ for some $e \in E_{i-1}$; (iii) the contraction of $\Gamma_{i-1}$ obtained by identifying two vertices $x, y \in V_{i-1}$ incident with a common edge, so that
$$V_i = \{ \{w\} : w \in V_{i-1} \setminus \{x, y\} \} \cup \{ \{x, y\} \}$$
and
$$E_i = E_{i-1} \setminus f_{i-1}^{-1}[\{x, y\}].$$
