A subcontraction of a multigraph is the multigraph-theoretic analogue of a graph minor: one first takes a contraction (collapsing connected vertex subsets) and then restricts to a submultigraph (deleting some edges and vertices). Every submultigraph is trivially a subcontraction by taking the trivial contraction. This concept is used throughout Chapter E to analyze how planarity behaves under graph-minor-like operations.
