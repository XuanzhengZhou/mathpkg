This exercise establishes two key properties of geodetic graphs. First, the geodetic property is lobe-local: a graph is geodetic precisely when each of its lobes is geodetic. Second, a biconnected geodetic graph cannot have cycle space dimension exactly 2 — meaning the cycle space dimension for such graphs is either 1 (a single circuit) or at least 3.
