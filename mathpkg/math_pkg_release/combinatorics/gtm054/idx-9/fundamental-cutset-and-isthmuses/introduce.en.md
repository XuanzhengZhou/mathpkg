This exercise relates the fundamental cutset (fundamental system of neighborhoods) of the cycle space $\mathcal{L}(\Gamma)$ to the set of edges and isthmuses. It states that the fundamental set of $\mathcal{L}(\Gamma)$ consists of all edges that are not isthmuses — equivalently, $\text{Fnd}(\mathcal{L}(\Gamma)) = E \setminus I$ where $I$ is the set of isthmuses. This highlights the structural role of isthmuses in the cycle-cocycle duality.
