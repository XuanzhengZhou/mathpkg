A tree is a connected forest — a connected multigraph with no cycles. Trees are the fundamental building blocks of multigraph theory: they are minimally connected (removing any edge disconnects them) and maximally acyclic (adding any edge creates exactly one cycle). Every connected multigraph contains a spanning tree, which serves as a scaffold for understanding its cycle and cocycle structure.
