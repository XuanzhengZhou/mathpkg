A forest is a multigraph whose cycle space is trivial — it contains no nonempty cycles. Every forest is necessarily a graph (no parallel edges), since parallel edges would form a 2-cycle. Forests generalize trees by allowing multiple connected components, and together with their spanning subgraphs, they form the foundation for understanding the cycle structure of arbitrary multigraphs.
