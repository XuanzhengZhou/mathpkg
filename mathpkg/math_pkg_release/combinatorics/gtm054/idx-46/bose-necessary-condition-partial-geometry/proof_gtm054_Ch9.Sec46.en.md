The first quantity $k(r-1)(k-1)/t$ equals $n_1 = r(k-1)$ from Proposition F8, which must be integral as it counts first associates. Note that $k(k-1)(r-1)/t = (v-k)$ from Proposition F5(d), and since $v$ must be integral, this quantity is integral. The second quantity $r(r-1)(k-1)/t$ is the dual counterpart obtained by interchanging $r$ and $k$ (Proposition F7), corresponding to $n_1$ in the dual geometry.

The third quantity equals $\alpha$, the total number of lines. By Exercise F10,
$$\alpha = \frac{rk(r-1)(k-1)}{t(k+r-t-1)},$$
which must be a positive integer since it counts the lines of the incidence structure. Thus all three quantities must be positive integers for a partial geometry with parameters $(r, k, t)$ to exist.

This condition is not sufficient: for $(r, k, t) = (3, 11, 1)$ we obtain $v = 231$, $b = 63$, $\alpha = 55$, yet no partial geometry with these parameters exists (Bose and Clatworthy [b.12]).
