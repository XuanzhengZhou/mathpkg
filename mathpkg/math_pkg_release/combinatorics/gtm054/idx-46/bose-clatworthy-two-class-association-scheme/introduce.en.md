In the theory of association schemes, verifying constancy of all intersection numbers $p_{jk}^i$ typically requires checking eight separate functions. Bose and Clatworthy proved that for a two-class scheme, it suffices to check only two: $p_{11}^1$ and $p_{11}^2$. This theorem provides a substantial simplification of the definition of two-class association schemes and is the key technical tool used to prove that Moore graphs of type $(\rho, 2)$ are strongly isovalent.
