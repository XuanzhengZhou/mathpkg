The dual of an incidence structure is obtained by interchanging the roles of points and lines. This proposition shows that the class of partial geometries is closed under duality: if $\Lambda$ is a partial geometry with parameters $(r, k, t)$, then $\Lambda^*$ is also a partial geometry with the parameters $r$ and $k$ swapped while $t$ remains unchanged. The proof follows from the symmetry of the defining axioms F1-F3 under point-line interchange.
