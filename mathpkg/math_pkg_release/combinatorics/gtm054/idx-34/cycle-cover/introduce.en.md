A cycle cover of a graph $\Gamma$ is a sequence of cycles $Z_1, \ldots, Z_m$ from the cycle space $\mathcal{X}(\Gamma)$ (together with the empty set) such that each edge of $\Gamma$ belongs to at most two of the $Z_i$. This concept generalizes the idea that region boundaries in a graph embedding cover each edge exactly twice. The cycle cover formalism provides a purely combinatorial approach to studying graph embeddings on arbitrary surfaces, relaxing the condition that all cycles must be homotopically trivial.
