The modified charge function $h = f + g$ combines the base charge $f$ with the Appel-Haken discharging function $g$. The key property is that $\sum_{x \in V} h(x) = 12$, which follows from the definition of $f$ and the charge conservation of $g$. In the Appel-Haken proof, one aims to show that $h(x) \leq 0$ for all $x \in V$, which would contradict the positive total sum of 12, thereby proving that no minimal counterexample to the Four-Color Theorem can exist.
