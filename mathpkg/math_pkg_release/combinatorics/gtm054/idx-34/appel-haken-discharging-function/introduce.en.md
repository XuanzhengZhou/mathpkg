The Appel-Haken discharging function $g: V \to \mathbb{Q}$ is a key ingredient in their proof of the Four-Color Theorem. It removes one unit of charge from each 5-valent vertex and redistributes it equally among the neighboring vertices of valence at least 7. The total charge over the entire graph is conserved: $\sum_{x \in V} g(x) = 0$. This averaging technique allows one to derive a contradiction from the assumption that a minimal counterexample to the Four-Color Theorem exists.
