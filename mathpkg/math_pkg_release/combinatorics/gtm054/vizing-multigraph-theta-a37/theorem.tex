(V. G. Vizing, [v.1]). For any multigraph $\Theta = (V, f, E)$ with selection $s,$

$$\chi_1(\Theta) \leq \hat{\rho}(\Theta) + \max\{s(U): U \subseteq V\}.$$

To see that Vizing’s theorem cannot be strengthened, consider the multigraph obtained by replicating exactly $r$ times each edge of the complete graph $K_3$. Then since any two edges are incident, $\chi_1 = \nu_1 = 3r = 2r + r,$ but $\rho = \hat{\rho} = 2r.$
