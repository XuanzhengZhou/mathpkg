A graph $\Gamma = (V, \mathcal{E})$ is a comparability graph if there exists a transitive, antisymmetric relation $R$ on $V$ such that $\{x, y\} \in \mathcal{E}$ if and only if either $(x, y) \in R$ or $(y, x) \in R$. Using Dilworth's theorem IVG7, show that every comparability graph is color-perfect, and hence perfect.
