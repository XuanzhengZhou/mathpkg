This exercise explores the construction of 2-cell imbeddings of a graph in orientable surfaces by varying the cyclic permutation (rotation system) at a single vertex. Given a specific graph $\Gamma$ with a vertex $a$ of degree $\rho(a)$, there are $(\rho(a)-1)!$ possible cyclic permutations at $a$. Part (a) asks to find the five alternative imbeddings when the permutations at other vertices are held fixed, and to determine which are toroidal. Part (b) asks for a general upper bound on the number of distinct 2-cell imbeddings of an arbitrary graph in orientable surfaces.
