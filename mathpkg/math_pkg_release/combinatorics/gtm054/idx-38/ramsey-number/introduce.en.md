The Ramsey number $n(q_1, \ldots, q_m : s)$ is the threshold such that any coloring of the $s$-subsets of a set of size at least $n$ with $m$ colors must contain a $q_i$-subset all of whose $s$-subsets receive color $i$, for some $i$. It generalizes the pigeonhole principle ($s=1$) and the classical graph Ramsey numbers ($s=2$). A central theorem of Ramsey theory asserts that all Ramsey numbers are finite.
