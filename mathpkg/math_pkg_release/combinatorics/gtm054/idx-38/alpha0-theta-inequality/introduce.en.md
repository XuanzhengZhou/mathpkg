Corollary A5 follows directly from A4 by applying complementation. Using $\alpha_0(\Gamma) = \omega(\Gamma')$ (A2), $\theta(\Gamma) = \chi_0(\Gamma')$ (A3), and applying $\omega \leq \chi_0$ to $\Gamma'$ gives $\alpha_0(\Gamma) \leq \theta(\Gamma)$. This shows the size of a largest independent set cannot exceed the minimum number of complete sets needed to cover the vertices.
