By two successive applications of Lemma F24, we infer the existence of a connected Platonic graph $\Gamma$ of each type.

If $\rho(\Gamma) = 3$, let $x_0$ be an articulation vertex of $\Gamma$. Since $f^*(x_0)$ is not elementary, while $|f^*(x_0)| = 3$, at least one of the edges in $f^*(x_0)$ is an isthmus. But by the definition of a Platonic graph, $\Gamma$ contains no isthmuses. If $\rho(\Gamma) > 3$, then by Lemma F24, there exists a Platonic graph $\Theta$ such that $\Theta \perp \Gamma$. From Table F16 we see that $\rho(\Theta) = \rho^\perp(\Gamma) = 3$. Hence $\Theta$ is biconnected and it follows that $\Gamma$ is biconnected.
