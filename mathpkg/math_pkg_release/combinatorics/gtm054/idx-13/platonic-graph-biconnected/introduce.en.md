This lemma establishes that every Platonic graph is biconnected, meaning it has no articulation vertices and remains connected upon removal of any single vertex. The proof proceeds via the orthogonality lemma (F24), reducing the problem to the $\rho = 3$ case where a direct vertex argument eliminates articulation vertices. For higher $\rho$, the existence of an orthogonal Platonic graph with $\rho^\perp = 3$ allows the biconnectivity property to be transferred back to the original graph.
