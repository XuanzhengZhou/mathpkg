Let $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ be a system, and let $s, t \in V \cup E$. An $st$-\text{path} is a sequence $s = s_0, s_1, \ldots, s_n = t$ of elements of $V \cup E$ such that:

(a) any three consecutive terms of the sequence are distinct;

(b) $\{s_{j-1}, s_j\}$ is an edge of the bipartite graph of $\Lambda$ for $j = 1, \ldots, n$.

A path consisting of a single vertex or block is called \text{trivial}. A path is \text{elementary} if all of its terms are distinct.
