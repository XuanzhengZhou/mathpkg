An $st$-path in a system $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ is an alternating sequence of vertices (elements of $V$) and blocks (elements of $E$) such that consecutive terms are incident in the bipartite graph of $\Lambda$, and no three consecutive terms repeat. This generalizes the notion of a path in a graph to the hypergraph setting. A trivial path consists of a single element, and an elementary path has all terms distinct.
