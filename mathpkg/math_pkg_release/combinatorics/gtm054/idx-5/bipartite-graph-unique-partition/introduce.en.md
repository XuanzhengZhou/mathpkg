Exercise C25 states that a connected bipartite graph has a unique bipartition of its vertex set (up to swapping the two parts). This is a classical result in graph theory: in a connected bipartite graph, once the part of one vertex is fixed, the parts of all other vertices are determined by the requirement that edges only cross between the two parts.
