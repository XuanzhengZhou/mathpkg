Let $(V, \mathcal{A})$ be a set system. A triple $(A_1, A_2, y)$ will be called admissible for $(V, \mathcal{A})$ if $A_1, A_2 \in \mathcal{A}, A_1 \neq A_2$, and $y \in A_1 \cap A_2$. The 4-tuple $(A_1, A_2, x, y)$ will be called admissible if $(A_1, A_2, y)$ is an admissible triple and $x \in A_1 + (A_1 \cap A_2)$.
