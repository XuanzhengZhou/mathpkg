Euler's Theorem (1736) is one of the foundational results of graph theory. It characterizes Eulerian multigraphs -- those that admit an Euler circuit (a closed walk using each edge exactly once) -- as precisely the connected multigraphs in which every vertex has even valence. The proof given here is algebraic, using the cycle space formalism. The traditional proof is constructive, but the algebraic proof reveals a duality with the bipartite characterization theorem (A13).
