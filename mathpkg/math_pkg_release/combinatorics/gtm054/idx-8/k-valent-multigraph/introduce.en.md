A multigraph is $k$-valent (or $k$-regular in standard graph theory terminology) if every vertex has the same valence $k$. A multigraph is called isovalent if it is $k$-valent for some $k \in \mathbb{N}$. These are the natural analogues of regular graphs in the multigraph setting.
