A matroid $\Lambda$ is representable over a field $\mathbb{F}$ if it admits a Tutte subspace over $\mathbb{F}$, or equivalently (by Proposition F4), a Whitney function over $\mathbb{F}$. This means there exists a subspace $\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathbb{F}^V$ whose minimal nonempty supports correspond exactly to the cycles of $\Lambda$. Of particular interest is representability over the field $\mathbb{K} = \text{GF}(2)$; matroids representable over $\mathbb{K}$ are called binary matroids. The cycle matroid of any multigraph is binary, and consequently its cocycle matroid is binary as well. A matroid representable over every field is called regular.
