Let $\mathcal{T}$ be a unimodular Tutte subspace over $\mathbb{Q}$ for $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ and let $\mathbb{F}$ be an arbitrary field. By Proposition IVA19, $\mathcal{T}^\perp$ is a unimodular subspace of $\mathbb{Q}^V$, and by Proposition F6a, $\mathcal{T}^\perp$ is a Tutte subspace over $\mathbb{Q}$ for $\Lambda^\perp$.

Consider a mapping $h \mapsto \bar{h}$ from $\{h \in \mathbb{Q}^V : h[V] \subseteq \{-1, 0, 1\}\}$ into $\mathbb{F}^V$ (cf. §IVA) whereby $\bar{h}(x)$ is the reinterpretation of $h(x)$ as an element of $\mathbb{F}$, with the understanding that $-1 = 1$ if $\mathbb{F}$ has characteristic $2$. Clearly $\sigma(h) = \sigma(\bar{h})$.

Let $\mathcal{S}_1$ denote the subspace of $\mathbb{F}^V$ generated by $\{\bar{h} : h \in \mathcal{T}^\perp,\; h[V] \subseteq \{-1, 0, 1\}\}$. The minimal nonempty supports of functions in $\mathcal{S}_1$ correspond to those in $\mathcal{T}^\perp$, which are the cocycles of $\Lambda$. Thus $\mathcal{S}_1$ is a Tutte subspace over $\mathbb{F}$ for a matroid $\Theta_1$ having the same cocycles as $\Lambda^\perp$, whence $r(\Theta_1) = r(\Lambda)$. By Lemma B29, $\Lambda = \Theta_1$. Thus $\Lambda$ is representable over $\mathbb{F}$. Since $\mathbb{F}$ was arbitrary, $\Lambda$ is regular.
