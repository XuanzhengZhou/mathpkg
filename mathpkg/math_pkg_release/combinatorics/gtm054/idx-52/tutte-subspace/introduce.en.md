A Tutte subspace provides a vector-space encoding of a matroid over a given field $\mathbb{F}$. For a matroid $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ on ground set $V$, a Tutte subspace $\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathbb{F}^V$ is a linear subspace whose minimal nonempty supports coincide exactly with the cycles (circuits) $\mathcal{M}$ of $\Lambda$. This concept, introduced by W. T. Tutte, gives an algebraic characterization of matroid representability: a matroid admits a Tutte subspace over $\mathbb{F}$ if and only if it is representable over $\mathbb{F}$ (equivalently, it admits a Whitney function over $\mathbb{F}$). The dimension of the Tutte subspace satisfies $r(\Lambda) + \dim(\mathcal{T}) = |V|$.
