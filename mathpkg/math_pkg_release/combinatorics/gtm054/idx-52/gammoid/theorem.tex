Let $(V, D)$ be a directed graph and let $Z \subseteq V$. Let $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ be the strict gammoid determined by $(V, D)$ and $Z$ (so that $\mathcal{I}(\Lambda)$ consists of subsets $A \subseteq V$ for which there exists an $|A|$-family of pairwise-disjoint $AZ$-paths). Any submatroid $\Lambda_U$ for $U \subseteq V$ is called a \textbf{gammoid}.
