A binary matroid is a matroid that is representable over the two-element field $\mathbb{K} = \text{GF}(2)$. This is the simplest nontrivial representability class. The cycle matroid of any multigraph is binary (as shown in Example F1), and consequently the cocycle matroid of any multigraph is also binary. The class of binary matroids is characterized by an excluded-minor condition: a matroid is binary if and only if it has no $U_{2,4}$ minor. Binary matroids play a central role in matroid theory, sitting between graphic matroids and regular matroids in the hierarchy of representability classes.
