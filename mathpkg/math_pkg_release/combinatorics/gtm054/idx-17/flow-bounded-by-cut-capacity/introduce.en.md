This proposition establishes the fundamental inequality between flows and cuts in a network: the flow through any edge $e_0$ cannot exceed the capacity of any cut through $e_0$. It provides the "easy direction" of the Max-Flow Min-Cut theorem by showing that every flow value is a lower bound for all cut capacities, making it a key step toward the duality between maximum flows and minimum cuts.
