The Max-Flow-Min-Cut Theorem is the central result of network flow theory. It states that for any directed edge $e_0$ in a network, the maximum possible flow through $e_0$ is exactly equal to the minimum capacity of any cut through $e_0$ (or $k(e_0)$ itself, whichever is smaller). This theorem establishes a fundamental min-max duality between flows and cuts and serves as the point of departure for the subsequent chapters on matroids and transversal theory.
