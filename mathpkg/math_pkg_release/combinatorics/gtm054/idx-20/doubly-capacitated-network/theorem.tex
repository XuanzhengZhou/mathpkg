A \textit{doubly-capacitated network} is a triple $(V, k_1, k_2)$ where $V$ is a directed graph, $k_i \in \mathbb{Z}^W$ for $i = 1, 2$, and
$$k_1(e) \leq k_2(e) \quad \text{for all } e \in W.$$
The functions $k_1$ and $k_2$ are called, respectively, the \textit{lower} and \textit{upper capacities}.
