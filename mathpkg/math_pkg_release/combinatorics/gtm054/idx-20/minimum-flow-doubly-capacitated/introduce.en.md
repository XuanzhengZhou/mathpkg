The notion of a minimum flow through a specified edge $e_0$ is the natural dual of the maximum flow concept. While the maximum flow gives the greatest possible throughput on $e_0$ consistent with feasibility, the minimum flow gives the least. If the lower capacity function $k_1$ itself happens to be a flow, then it is automatically a minimum flow through every edge. This concept is motivated by the lower bound $k_1(C; e_0) \leq h(e_0)$ from Proposition G2, which suggests a Min-Flow Max-Cut theorem (G4) analogous to G3.
