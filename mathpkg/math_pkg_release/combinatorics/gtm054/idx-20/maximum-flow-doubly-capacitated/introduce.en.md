In the doubly-capacitated context, a maximum flow through a specified edge $e_0$ is a feasible flow whose value on $e_0$ is at least as large as that of any other feasible flow. This definition extends the classical notion of a maximum flow to networks where each edge carries both a lower and an upper capacity bound. The existence of a maximum flow is guaranteed by the Max-Flow–Min-Cut Theorem (G3), provided the network admits at least one feasible flow.
