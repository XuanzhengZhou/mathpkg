A $\{0,1\}$-matrix is a rectangular array whose entries are restricted to the values $0$ and $1$. Such matrices arise naturally as incidence matrices of set systems, graphs, and combinatorial designs. The term "line" collectively refers to the rows and columns of the matrix, providing a convenient vocabulary for discussing incidence relations among the entries.
