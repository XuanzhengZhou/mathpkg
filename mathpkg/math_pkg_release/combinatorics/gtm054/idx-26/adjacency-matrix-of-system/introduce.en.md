Given a set system $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ with incidence matrix $M$, the product $MM^{*}$ yields the vertex-vertex incidence matrix, also known as the adjacency matrix of $\Lambda$. The entry $a_{ij}$ counts the number of elements $e \in E$ whose image under $f$ contains both $x_i$ and $x_j$, equivalently the size of the intersection of the pre-images $f^{*}(x_i)$ and $f^{*}(x_j)$. This generalizes the familiar adjacency matrix of a graph.
