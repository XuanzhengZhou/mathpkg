This theorem, attributed to Watkins [w.3], establishes a quantitative relationship between the strong linkage condition $O_n$ and vertex-connectivity. If a multigraph $\Gamma$ satisfies $O_n$ (meaning any $n$ distinct source-sink pairs can be connected by $n$ openly-disjoint paths), then $\Gamma$ must have connectivity at least $2n-1$. For example, $O_2$ implies $\kappa(\Gamma) \geq 3$, so the graph is at least $3$-connected (tri-connected). The proof uses induction on $n$ with the openly-disjoint family machinery from Proposition C9.
