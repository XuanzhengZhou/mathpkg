If a multigraph $\Gamma$ is $m$-connected, then removing a single vertex or a single edge reduces the connectivity by at most one: the resulting subgraphs $\Gamma_{(x)}$ (vertex deletion) and $\Gamma_{(E)}$ (edge deletion) are each $(m-1)$-connected. This proposition quantifies how connectivity degrades under elementary graph operations, and is important for inductive arguments about connectivity.
