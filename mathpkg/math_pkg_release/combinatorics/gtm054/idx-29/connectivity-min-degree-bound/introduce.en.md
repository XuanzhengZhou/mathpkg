This simple inequality states that the vertex-connectivity of any multigraph cannot exceed its minimum degree. To disconnect a graph, one must isolate a vertex, which requires removing all of its neighbors — so at most $\check{\rho}(\Gamma)$ vertices suffice. This provides a readily computable upper bound for $\kappa(\Gamma)$ and highlights the relationship between local structure (vertex degrees) and global robustness (connectivity).
