Let $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ be a $(t;)$-design. Prove:

(a) If $\lambda(t) > \bar{s}(V) > 0$, then the subsystem $(V, f|_F, F)$ where $F = \{e \in E: f(e) \neq V\}$ is a nondegenerate $(t;)$-design.

(b) If $\Lambda$ is not degenerate, then $\Lambda$ is a subsystem of some degenerate $(t;)$-design with vertex set $V$.

(c) Let $W$ and $F$ be any disjoint sets such that $|W| > t$ and $|F| > a$. Then there exists a function $f: F \rightarrow \mathcal{P}(W)$ such that $(W, f, F)$ is a degenerate $(t; a)$-design.
