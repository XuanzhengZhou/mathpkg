A symmetric $\lambda$-linked design is a nondegenerate, nontrivial $(2;2)$-design that fails to be a $t$-design for any $t$. These designs occupy a distinguished position: they have regularity of pairs both on the vertex side and the block side, but are not hierarchical in the sense of $t$-designs. They provide an interesting class of counterexamples to the natural expectation that $(t;a)$-designs should automatically be $t$-designs.
