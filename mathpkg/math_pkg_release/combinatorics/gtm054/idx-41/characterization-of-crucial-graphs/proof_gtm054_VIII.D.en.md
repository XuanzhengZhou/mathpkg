**Proof.** If $\Gamma$ is crucial, then certainly $\nu_0(\Gamma) > 1$. Thus if $x$ is a vertex of $\Gamma$, then $\Gamma_{(x)}$ is perfect while $\Gamma$ is not perfect. By the theorem,

$$\nu_0(\Gamma) = \nu_0(\Gamma_{(x)}) + 1 \leq \alpha_0(\Gamma_{(x)})\omega(\Gamma_{(x)}) + 1$$
$$\leq \alpha_0(\Gamma)\omega(\Gamma) + 1$$
$$\leq \nu_0(\Gamma).$$

It is easy to see that for any crucial graph $\Gamma$, $\alpha_0(\Gamma) \geq 2$ and $\omega(\Gamma) \geq 2$.
