Exercise D20 asks the reader to prove that every comparability graph is color-perfect, and therefore perfect. A comparability graph is defined by a transitive, antisymmetric relation on the vertex set. The proof uses Dilworth's theorem from Chapter IV, which relates the size of a largest antichain to the minimum number of chains in a partition.
