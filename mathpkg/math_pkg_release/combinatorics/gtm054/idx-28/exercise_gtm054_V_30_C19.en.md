Construct a graph $\Gamma$ such that for each $\{a, z_1, z_2, z_3\} \in \mathcal{P}_4(V)$, there is an openly-disjoint $6$-family of $a\{z_1, z_2, z_3\}$-paths, two of which are $az_i$-paths for each $i = 1, 2, 3$, but $\Gamma$ is not $6$-connected.
