For a graph with vertex-connectivity $\kappa(\Gamma) \geq 2$, the largest integer $\zeta(\Gamma)$ such that any set of $m$ vertices is contained in some elementary circuit satisfies $\zeta(\Gamma) \geq \kappa(\Gamma)$. This follows from Theorem C11 and relates the circuit-containment capacity $\zeta(\Gamma)$ to the standard vertex-connectivity measure. The inequality is sharp, as demonstrated by complete bipartite graphs $K_{n,n+1}$ where equality holds.
