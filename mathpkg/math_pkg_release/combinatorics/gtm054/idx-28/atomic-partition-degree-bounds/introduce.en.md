When a graph admits the atomic partition, every vertex degree is bounded both above and below by expressions involving the atom size $\alpha$ and the connectivity $\kappa$. The lower bound $2\alpha \leq \rho(x)$ follows from the fact that each atom lies in the neighborhood of another atom, while $\rho(x) \leq \kappa + \alpha - 1$ comes from counting vertices inside the atom and in its neighborhood.
