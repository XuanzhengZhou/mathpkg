If $\Gamma$ contains a vertex of valence 2 or less, and if $\Gamma$ is not one of the "forbidden" multigraphs ($K_1, K_2, K_3, B_2, B_3$), then by E5, $\tau(\Gamma) \leq \kappa(\Gamma) \leq 2 \leq \gamma(\Gamma)$. Hence assume that the smallest valence is at least 3. We may also assume $\nu_1(\Gamma) \geq 4$, since the only multigraph with smallest valence 3 and $\nu_1(\Gamma) < 4$ is $B_3$. By Exercise IIIA6a, $\Gamma$ contains a nonempty cycle. Let $Z \in \mathcal{X}(\Gamma)$ such that $|Z| = \gamma(\Gamma)$. The circuit $\Gamma_Z$ is clearly an elementary circuit. Taking the submultigraph $\Theta$ induced by $Z$ shows that $\Gamma$ is $\gamma(\Gamma)$-separated, whence $\tau(\Gamma) \leq \gamma(\Gamma)$.
