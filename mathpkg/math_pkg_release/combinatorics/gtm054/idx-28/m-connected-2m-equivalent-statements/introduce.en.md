For a graph with sufficiently many vertices, $m$-connectivity is equivalent to $2m$ statements about the existence of openly-disjoint path families. The $m$ statements $B_k$ generalize Menger's theorem by specifying the distribution of paths among multiple targets. The $m-1$ statements $C_k$ strengthen the attachment-vertex formulation of connectivity. This theorem, due to Mesner and Watkins, provides a partial converse to an earlier generalization of Whitney's theorem.
