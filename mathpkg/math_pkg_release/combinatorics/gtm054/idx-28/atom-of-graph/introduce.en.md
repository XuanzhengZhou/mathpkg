An atom of a graph is a fragment of minimum cardinality, denoted $\alpha(\Gamma)$. Atoms play a crucial role in decomposing the connectivity structure of a graph: distinct atoms are always disjoint, and each atom is either contained in any given fragment, contained in its complement, or contained in its neighborhood. This trichotomy property makes atoms the irreducible components for analyzing graph connectivity.
