An immediate consequence of the atom-fragment containment trichotomy (D6) is that distinct atoms of a graph are always disjoint. Since each atom is itself a fragment, the trichotomy applied to one atom $A$ and another atom $B$ considered as a fragment forces $A$ and $B$ to be either identical or disjoint.
