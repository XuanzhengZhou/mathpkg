Schur's result follows as an immediate corollary of Proposition B12. Let $\mathcal{Q} \in \mathbb{P}(\mathbb{N} \cup \{0\})$ be a finite partition with $|\mathcal{Q}| = m$. Choose $k \geq n(3, \ldots, 3: 2) - 1$ (with $m$ arguments, all equal to $3$) and restrict $\mathcal{Q}$ to the finite set $\{1, 2, \ldots, k\}$. Applying Proposition B12, there exists $Q \in \mathcal{Q}$ and $x, y \in Q$ (with $x, y \in \{1, 2, \ldots, k\}$) such that $x + y \in Q$. Thus the required triple exists in the original partition as well.
