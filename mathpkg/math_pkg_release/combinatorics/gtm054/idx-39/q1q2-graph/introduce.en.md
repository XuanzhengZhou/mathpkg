A $(q_1, q_2)$-graph is a graph whose clique number is strictly less than $q_1$ and whose scattering number (vertex independence number) is strictly less than $q_2$. In the context of Ramsey theory, $(q_1, q_2)$-graphs are precisely those graphs on at most $n(q_1, q_2) - 1$ vertices that witness the extremal value. This terminology is introduced as working language for the study of Ramsey numbers in Section VIII.C.
