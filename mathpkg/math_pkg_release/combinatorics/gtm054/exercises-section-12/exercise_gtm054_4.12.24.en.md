Let $\Gamma$ be a connected planar multigraph. Let $Z$ be a cycle of $\Gamma$ which is not a region of some planar imbedding of $\Gamma$, and let $U$ be the set of vertices incident with edges in $Z$. Show that if $\Gamma_U$ is an elementary circuit, then $\Gamma_{(U)}$ is not connected.

IIIF Euler’s Formula

We have seen by example in the previous section that a planar multigraph need not uniquely determine which cycles will be regions of a simple planar imbedding. It does, however, determine the number of regions in any simple planar imbedding.
