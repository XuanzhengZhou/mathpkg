This proposition establishes the fundamental row-sum identities for the intersection parameter matrices $P_i$ of an $m$-class association scheme. For a fixed pair of $i$th-associates $\{x, y\}$, every $j$th-associate of $x$ must be some $k$th-associate of $y$. Summing $p_{jk}^i$ over all relation classes $k$ therefore counts the total number of $j$th-associates of $x$, which is $n_j$, except when $j = i$ and $y$ itself is counted among the $i$th-associates, yielding $n_i - 1$. This identity reduces the number of independent parameters in an association scheme.
