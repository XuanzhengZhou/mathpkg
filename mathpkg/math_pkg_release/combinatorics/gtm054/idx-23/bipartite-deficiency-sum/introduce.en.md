This proposition connects the graph-theoretic deficiency $\delta(\mathbf{B})$ for arbitrary graphs with the bipartite deficiencies $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ familiar from the Main Matching Theorem. It shows that for a bipartite graph, the graph deficiency decomposes as the sum of the two one-sided bipartite deficiencies, establishing a precise bridge between the bipartite matching theory and the general 1-factor theory.
