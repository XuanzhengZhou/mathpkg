We call a subgraph of $\Gamma$ which is a forest or tree a subforest or a subtree, respectively. If $\Gamma$ is a subforest (subtree) of the multigraph $\Gamma$ such that $\nu_0(\Gamma) = \nu_0(\Gamma)$ and $\nu_{-1}(\Gamma) = \nu_{-1}(\Gamma)$, we call $\Gamma$ a spanning forest (spanning tree). It follows directly from Corollary C5 with $j = 0$ that every multigraph contains a spanning forest and that every connected multigraph contains a spanning tree.
