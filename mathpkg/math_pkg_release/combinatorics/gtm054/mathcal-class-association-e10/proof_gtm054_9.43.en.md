Let $\{x, y\} \in Q_i$. Of the $(n_i - 1)$ $i$th associates of $x$ other than $y$, precisely $p_{ik}^i$ of them are $k$th associates of $y$, for each $k = 1, \ldots, m$. If $j \neq i$, then of the $n_j j$th associates of $x$, precisely $p_{jk}^i$ are $k$th associates of $y$, for each $k = 1, \ldots, m$.
