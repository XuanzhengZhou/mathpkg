For any graph $\Gamma$, prove that either $\Gamma$ or $\Gamma'$ has diameter at most 3. (If $\Gamma$ is not connected, its diameter is understood to be $\infty$.)

A subset $S \subseteq V$ will be called a complete set if $\mathcal{P}_2(S) \subseteq \mathcal{E}$; i.e., $\Gamma_s$ is a complete subgraph. A largest complete set in $\Gamma$ is called a clique, and $\omega(\Gamma)$ will be used to denote its cardinality. A largest independent subset of $V$ will be called a scattering.
