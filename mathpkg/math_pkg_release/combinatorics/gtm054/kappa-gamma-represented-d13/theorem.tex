Let $\kappa(\Gamma)$ be represented as $\kappa = m\alpha + b$ where $m \geq 1$ and $0 \leq b < \alpha$. Then for any vertex $x$ in an atom,

$$\rho(x) \leq \frac{m + 1}{m} \kappa - 1 - \frac{b}{m}.$$

Let $\Gamma = (V, \mathcal{E})$. Then $\Gamma$ is $\rho$-valent with $\rho = 3n - 1$. The elements of $\mathcal{S}_0(\Gamma)$ are of the form $V_i + V_j$ where $j - i \neq 0, \pm 1$ (mod $p$). Hence $\kappa = 2n$ and the atoms are the sets $V_i$ ($i = 0, \ldots, p - 1$). Thus $\frac{3}{2} - \rho/\kappa = 1/2n$, and $n$ may be chosen arbitrarily large.

Clearly every graph which is vertex-transitive admits the atomic partition. Such a graph is, moreover, $\rho$-valent, and so the last two propositions and corollary apply in particular to vertex-transitive graphs.
