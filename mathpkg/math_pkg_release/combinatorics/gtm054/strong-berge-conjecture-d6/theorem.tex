In the remainder of this section we prove the Berge Conjecture (D1) and we obtain some further properties of crucial graphs. Most of these results are due to L. Lovász [ℓ.3] and [ℓ.4], but their presentation here has been considerably transformed. Those lemmas below which we have ascribed to Lovász constitute various fragments of his original proof of the conjecture.

Let $\Gamma = (V, \mathcal{E})$ be any graph, let $W$ be a set, and let $g: W \rightarrow V$ be a function. We define the Lovász graph $\Lambda$ determined by the triple $(\Gamma, W, g)$ to be the graph $\Lambda = (W, \mathcal{F})$ where

$$\mathcal{F} = \{F \in \mathcal{P}_2(W) : g[F] \in \mathcal{E} \cup \mathcal{P}_1(V)\}.$$
