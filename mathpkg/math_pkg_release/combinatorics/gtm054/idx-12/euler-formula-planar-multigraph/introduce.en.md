Euler's Formula is one of the most celebrated results in graph theory and topology. It relates the number of regions $\nu_2$, edges $\nu_1$, and vertices $\nu_0$ of a planar multigraph. For a connected planar multigraph, the formula simplifies to $\nu_2 - \nu_1 + \nu_0 = 2$, which is the classical form. The more general form $\nu_2 - \nu_1 + \nu_0 - \nu_{-1} = 1$ holds for any planar multigraph by incorporating the number of connected components $\nu_{-1}$.
