This definition introduces the dual analogues of valence regularity for planar multigraphs. A $k$-covalent planar imbedding is one in which every region is bounded by exactly $k$ edges. A multigraph is $k$-covalent if all of its simple planar imbeddings share this property, and isocovalent if such a $k$ exists. Examples include the $n$-circuit $\Delta_n$ (which is $n$-covalent), $K_4$ (which is $3$-covalent), and multigraphs with $\nu_0 = 2$ and $\nu_1 \geq 2$ (which are $2$-covalent).
