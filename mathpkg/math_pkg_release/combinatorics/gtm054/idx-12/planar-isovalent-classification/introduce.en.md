This proposition provides a complete classification of planar isovalent multigraphs without isthmuses or isolated vertices that admit an $m$-covalent imbedding. The parameters must match one of seven types: two infinite families (Types I and II, parameterized by $k \geq 2$) and five sporadic types (Types III--VII) corresponding to the Platonic solids. The proof proceeds by solving the Diophantine system derived from Euler's formula, exploiting symmetry between $\rho$ and $\rho^{\perp}$, and analyzing the cases $\rho^{\perp} = 2, 3$ with $\rho \geq \rho^{\perp}$. The remaining three types arise by symmetry.
