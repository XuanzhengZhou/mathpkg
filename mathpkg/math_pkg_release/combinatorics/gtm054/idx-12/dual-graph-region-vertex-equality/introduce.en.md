This proposition establishes the fundamental relationship between a planar multigraph and its dual. After removing isthmuses, if $\Gamma_{(I)}$ is dual to $\Theta$, then the number of regions of $\Gamma$ equals the number of vertices of $\Theta$. Additionally, the covalence of each region in the planar imbedding corresponds precisely to the valence of the corresponding vertex in the dual graph. This is a key structural insight that underlies planar duality theory.
