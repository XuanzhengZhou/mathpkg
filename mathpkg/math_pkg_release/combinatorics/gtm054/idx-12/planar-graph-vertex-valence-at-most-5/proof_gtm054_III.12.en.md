Every nonempty cycle of a graph $\Gamma$ has covalence at least 3. By Corollary F7b, $\rho^{\perp}(\Gamma) \geq 3$. From Proposition F11(a), $\rho(\Gamma) < 6$. Since the average valence is strictly less than 6, there must exist at least one vertex whose valence is strictly less than 6, i.e., at most 5.
