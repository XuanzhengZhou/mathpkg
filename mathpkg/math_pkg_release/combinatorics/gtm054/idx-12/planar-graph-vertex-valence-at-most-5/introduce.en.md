This is one of the most useful corollaries of Euler's formula for planar graphs. Every nonempty cycle of a planar graph has covalence at least 3, implying $\rho^{\perp} \geq 3$. Combined with Proposition F11(a), which guarantees $\rho < 6$, it follows that some vertex must have valence strictly less than 6, i.e., at most 5. This fact is the starting point for proofs of the Six-Color and Five-Color Theorems.
