A subset $A$ of the edge set $E$ of a set system $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ belongs to the cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Lambda)$ precisely when the symmetric difference (sum) of the images of its edges under the incidence map $f$ yields the empty set. This characterization gives a concrete criterion for recognizing cycles: the boundary sum of the edges in $A$ vanishes. It complements the definition of the cycle space as the orthogonal complement of the bond space $\mathcal{Y}(\Lambda)$.
