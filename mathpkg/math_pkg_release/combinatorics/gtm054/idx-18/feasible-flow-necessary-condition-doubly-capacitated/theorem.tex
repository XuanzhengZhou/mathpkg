For a doubly-capacitated network $(V, k_1, k_2)$ and any cut $C$ through $e_0 \in W$, if a feasible flow $h$ exists then

$$0 \leq k_2(C; e_0) - k_1(e_0) = \sum_{g_U(e)=1} k_2(e) - \sum_{g_U(e)=-1} k_1(e).$$

Denote this quantity by $k(U)$. The condition $k(U) \geq 0$ for all $\varnothing \subset U \subset V$ is necessary and sufficient for the existence of a feasible flow.
