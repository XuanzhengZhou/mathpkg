Theorem G3 extends the max-flow min-cut theorem to doubly-capacitated networks. Provided a feasible flow exists, the maximum possible flow through any designated edge $e_0$ equals the minimum of the upper capacities of all cuts through $e_0$ (together with the capacity of $e_0$ itself). The proof adapts the standard argument from Theorem C6, with a modified notion of unsaturated path that accounts for the lower capacity bound.
