Let $f(x) \in \Phi[x]$ be a separable polynomial and $P$ its splitting field over $\Phi$. The Galois group of the equation $f(x) = 0$ over $\Phi$ is the Galois group $\text{Gal}(P/\Phi)$, viewed as a permutation group on the roots of $f$. The equation is solvable by radicals if and only if this group is solvable.
