Let $E$ and $P$ be two fields. The set $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ of homomorphisms of the additive group $(E, +)$ of $E$ into $(P, +)$ is a group relative to the composition $A + B$ defined by

$$\epsilon(A + B) = \epsilon A + \epsilon B$$

for $\epsilon \in E$. The zero of $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ is the mapping $0$ such that $\epsilon 0 = 0$ for all $\epsilon \in E$, and $-A$ is given by $\epsilon(-A) = -\epsilon A$.

If $\Delta$ is a third field and $A \in \mathfrak{L}(E, P)$, $B \in \mathfrak{L}(P, \Delta)$, then the resultant $AB$ defined by $\epsilon(AB) = (\epsilon A)B$ is an element of $\mathfrak{L}(E, \Delta)$. Both distributive laws hold: $(A_1 + A_2)(B_1 + B_2) = A_1B_1 + A_1B_2 + A_2B_1 + A_2B_2$, and the associative law holds: $(AB)C = A(BC)$.
