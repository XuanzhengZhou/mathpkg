The formalization of Sturm's theorem via resultants and subresultants provides an algebraic (not analytic) method for counting real roots. The resultant $\text{Res}(f, g)$ is a polynomial in the coefficients that detects common roots. This algebraic formulation is essential for quantifier elimination and the Tarski-Seidenberg decision procedure.
