Let $f, g \in R[x]$ over a real closed field $R$. The resultant $\text{Res}(f, g)$ vanishes exactly when $f$ and $g$ have a common root. The Sturm sequence can be formalized using subresultants and the Euclidean algorithm with signs. The generalized Sturm theorem computes the number of roots of $f$ where $g > 0$ (or $g < 0$) via sign variations in the Sturm sequence.
