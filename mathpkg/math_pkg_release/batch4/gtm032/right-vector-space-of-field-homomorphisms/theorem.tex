The set $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ can be regarded as a right vector space over $P$ by defining, for $A \in \mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ and $\rho \in P$, the product $A\rho$ as

$$\epsilon(A\rho) = (\epsilon A)\rho, \quad \epsilon \in E.$$

Equivalently, $A\rho = A \circ \rho_R$ where $\rho_R$ is the right multiplication by $\rho$ in $P$. The additive group of $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ together with this scalar multiplication satisfies the vector space axioms over $P$.
