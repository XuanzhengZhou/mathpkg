Let $\mathfrak{B}$ and $\mathfrak{C}$ be commutative rings, $\Phi$ a subfield of $\mathfrak{C}$, $t$ an element of $\mathfrak{C}$ which is algebraic over $\Phi$ with minimum polynomial $f(x)$, and $s$ an isomorphism of $\Phi$ into $\mathfrak{B}$. Then $s$ can be extended to a homomorphism $S$ of $\Phi[t]$ into $\mathfrak{B}$ if and only if there exists a $u \in \mathfrak{B}$ such that $f^s(u) = 0$. If $u$ exists, then $S$ is uniquely determined by $t^S = u$, and the number of such extensions equals the number of distinct roots of $f^s(x)$ in $\mathfrak{B}$.
