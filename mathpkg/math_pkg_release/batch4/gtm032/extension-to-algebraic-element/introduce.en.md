This theorem describes the extension of isomorphisms to simple algebraic extensions. Given an isomorphism $s$ from the base field $\Phi$, one can extend it to $\Phi[t]$ where $t$ is algebraic with minimum polynomial $f$, by sending $t$ to any root of $f^s$ in the target ring. The number of possible extensions equals the number of distinct roots of $f^s$, which is at most $\deg f$. This is foundational for understanding embeddings of algebraic extensions.
