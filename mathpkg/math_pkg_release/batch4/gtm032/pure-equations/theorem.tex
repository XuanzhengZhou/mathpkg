Let $\Phi$ be a field containing a primitive $n$-th root of unity, and consider the pure equation $x^n - a = 0$ with $a \in \Phi$. Its splitting field is $\Phi(\sqrt[n]{a})$ and its Galois group is cyclic of order $d$ where $d$ divides $n$. More precisely, if $\sqrt[n]{a}$ denotes any root and $d$ is the smallest positive integer such that $(\sqrt[n]{a})^d \in \Phi$, then the Galois group has order $d$.
