Pure equations $x^n = a$ over fields containing $n$-th roots of unity have cyclic Galois groups. The Galois group order is the smallest exponent $d$ such that $a^{n/d}$ is an $n$-th power in $\Phi$. This is the simplest case of Kummer theory and provides the building blocks for solvability by radicals.
