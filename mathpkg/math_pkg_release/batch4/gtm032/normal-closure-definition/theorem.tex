Let $P/\Phi$ be a finite dimensional extension. A \text{normal closure} of $P/\Phi$ is an extension $N/\Phi$ such that $N \supseteq P$, $N/\Phi$ is normal, and no proper subfield of $N$ containing $P$ is normal over $\Phi$. If $P = \Phi(\rho_1, \ldots, \rho_m)$ and $f_i(x)$ is the minimum polynomial of $\rho_i$ over $\Phi$, then a splitting field of $f(x) = \prod f_i(x)$ over $\Phi$ containing $P$ is a normal closure.
