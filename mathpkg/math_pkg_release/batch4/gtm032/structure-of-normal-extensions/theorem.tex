If $P/\Phi$ is finite dimensional normal, then $P = \Gamma \otimes_\Phi \Sigma$ where $\Sigma/\Phi$ is Galois and $\Gamma/\Phi$ is purely inseparable. Here $\Gamma$ is the subfield of purely inseparable elements over $\Phi$, and $\Sigma$ is the maximal separable subfield of $P/\Phi$.

Conversely, if $\Gamma/\Phi$ is a finite dimensional purely inseparable extension and $\Sigma/\Phi$ is finite dimensional separable, then the tensor product $P = \Gamma \otimes_\Phi \Sigma$ (over $\Phi$) is a field, and it is normal if $\Sigma/\Phi$ is Galois.
