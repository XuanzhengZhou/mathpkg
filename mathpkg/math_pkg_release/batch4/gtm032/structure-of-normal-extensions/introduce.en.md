Every finite normal extension decomposes as a tensor product (over the base) of its maximal purely inseparable subextension and its maximal separable subextension. The separable part is always Galois. This structural theorem completely describes normal extensions in terms of the simpler building blocks: Galois extensions and purely inseparable extensions. The converse also holds: the tensor product of a purely inseparable extension with a Galois extension is a field that is normal. This decomposition is essential for understanding the architecture of arbitrary field extensions.
