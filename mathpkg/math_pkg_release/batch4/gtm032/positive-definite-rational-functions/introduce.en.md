Artin's solution to Hilbert's 17th problem: a positive definite rational function over a real closed field is a sum of squares of rational functions. The proof uses the Artin-Schreier theory: if $f$ were not a sum of squares, one could construct an ordering of the function field in which $f$ is negative, contradicting positivity.
