The Galois group $A(\Phi)$ of a field extension $P/\Phi$ consists of all automorphisms of $P$ that fix every element of the base field $\Phi$. Together with the invariant subfield construction $I(G)$, this defines the two maps of the Galois correspondence: $G \mapsto I(G)$ and $\Phi \mapsto A(\Phi)$. The fundamental properties $I(A(I(G))) = I(G)$ and $A(I(A(\Phi))) = A(\Phi)$ show these maps are inverses on their respective images, laying the groundwork for the Galois correspondence theorem.
