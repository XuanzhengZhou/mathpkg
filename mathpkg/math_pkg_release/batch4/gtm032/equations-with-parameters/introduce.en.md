The study of equations with parameters over real closed fields leads to quantifier elimination: any first-order formula is equivalent to a quantifier-free formula (Boolean combination of polynomial equations and inequalities). This has profound consequences: the projection of a semi-algebraic set is semi-algebraic, and the theory of real closed fields is model-complete and decidable.
