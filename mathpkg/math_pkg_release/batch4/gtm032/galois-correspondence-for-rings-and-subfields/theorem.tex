The mappings $R: \Phi \mapsto \mathfrak{L}_\Phi(P, P)$ and $F: \mathfrak{A} \mapsto \{\alpha \in P \mid \alpha_R A = A \alpha_R, \, A \in \mathfrak{A}\}$ are inverse bijections between the collection $\mathcal{F}$ of subfields of $P$ of finite co-dimension and the collection $\mathcal{R}$ of subrings $\mathfrak{A}$ of $\mathfrak{L}(P, P)$ satisfying conditions (i)-(iii) of Theorem 2. That is,
$$R(F(\mathfrak{A})) = \mathfrak{A}, \quad \mathfrak{A} \in \mathcal{R},$$
$$F(R(\Phi)) = \Phi, \quad \Phi \in \mathcal{F}.$$
