Given a group $G$ of automorphisms of a field $P$, the elements fixed by all automorphisms in $G$ form a subfield $\Phi = I(G)$, called the subfield of $G$-invariants. A subfield arising in this way is termed "Galois in $P$". This defines one direction of the Galois correspondence: from groups of automorphisms to subfields. The reverse direction associates to a subfield its Galois group, i.e., the group of automorphisms fixing that subfield pointwise.
