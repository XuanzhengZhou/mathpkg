Let $G$ be a group of automorphisms of a field $P$. The set of $G$-invariants of $P$ is

$$\Phi = I(G) = \{\alpha \in P \mid \alpha^s = \alpha \text{ for all } s \in G\}.$$

Then $\Phi$ is a subfield of $P$, called the subfield of $G$-invariants. A subfield $\Phi$ of $P$ is called Galois in $P$ if $\Phi = I(G)$ for some group of automorphisms $G$ of $P$. In this case, $P$ is said to be Galois over $\Phi$, or $P/\Phi$ is Galois.
