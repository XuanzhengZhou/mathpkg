Let $\Phi$ be a field and $f(x) \in \Phi[x]$ a non-zero polynomial. Let $P/\Phi$ be a splitting field of $f(x)$ with roots $R = \{\rho_1, \dots, \rho_m\}$. The Galois group $G$ of $P/\Phi$ induces a permutation group $G_f$ on $R$ via $s \mapsto s|_R$. The mapping $s \mapsto s_f$ is an isomorphism of $G$ onto $G_f$.

The group $G_f$, considered as a subgroup of the symmetric group $S_m$ (by identifying $\rho_i \mapsto \rho_{i'}$ with $i \mapsto i'$), is called the Galois group of the equation $f(x) = 0$ over $\Phi$. It is uniquely determined up to isomorphism.
