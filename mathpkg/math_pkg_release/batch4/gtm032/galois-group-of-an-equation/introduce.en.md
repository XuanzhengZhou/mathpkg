The Galois group of a polynomial equation $f(x) = 0$ is the permutation group induced by the Galois group of its splitting field on the set of roots. Since the splitting field is generated by the roots, the action is faithful, giving an isomorphism between the abstract Galois group and a concrete permutation group. This identification is the bridge between field-theoretic Galois theory and the classical theory of equations: properties of the equation (such as solvability by radicals) correspond to group-theoretic properties of this permutation group.
