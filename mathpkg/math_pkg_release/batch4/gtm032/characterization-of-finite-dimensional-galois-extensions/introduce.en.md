A finite-dimensional field extension is Galois precisely when it is both normal and separable, or equivalently, when it is a splitting field of a separable polynomial. This characterization bridges the abstract definition (fixed field of a group of automorphisms) with the concrete construction (splitting field). A key consequence is that in a Galois extension, the minimum polynomial of any element factors completely into distinct linear factors over the extension, and its roots are precisely the Galois conjugates of that element.
