A finite dimensional extension $P/\Phi$ is Galois if and only if it is a splitting field of a separable polynomial over $\Phi$. Equivalently, $P/\Phi$ is Galois if and only if it is separable and normal.

Moreover, if $P/\Phi$ is Galois with Galois group $G = \{s_1, \dots, s_n\}$ and $\rho \in P$, then the minimum polynomial of $\rho$ over $\Phi$ is
$$h(x) = \prod_{j=1}^{r} (x - \rho^{s_j})$$
where $\rho^{s_1}, \dots, \rho^{s_r}$ are the distinct conjugates of $\rho$ under $G$. In particular, this polynomial is separable and splits completely in $P[x]$.
