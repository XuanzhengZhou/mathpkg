This theorem generalizes the construction of the field of fractions. Given a subring $\mathfrak{o}$ of a field $P$ and a multiplicative subset $M$ containing $1$, any homomorphism $s$ from $\mathfrak{o}$ that does not vanish on $M$ extends uniquely to the ring $\mathfrak{o}_M$ obtained by adjoining inverses of elements of $M$. The extension sends $\alpha\beta^{-1}$ to $\alpha^s(\beta^s)^{-1}$, and it is an isomorphism precisely when $s$ is.
