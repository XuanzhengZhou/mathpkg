Let $\mathfrak{o}$ be a subring of a field $P$ and let $M$ be a sub-semigroup of the multiplicative group of $P$ contained in $\mathfrak{o}^*$ and containing $1$. Let $s$ be a homomorphism of $\mathfrak{o}$ into a field $P'$ such that $\beta^s \neq 0$ for every $\beta \in M$. Then $s$ has a unique extension to a homomorphism $S$ of $\mathfrak{o}_M$ into $P'$, where $\mathfrak{o}_M$ is the subring of $P$ generated by $\mathfrak{o}$ and the inverses of the elements of $M$. Moreover, $S$ is an isomorphism if and only if $s$ is an isomorphism.
