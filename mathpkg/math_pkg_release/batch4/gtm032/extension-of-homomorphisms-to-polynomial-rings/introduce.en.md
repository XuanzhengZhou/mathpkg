The universal property of polynomial rings states that a homomorphism from the coefficient ring $\mathfrak{A}$ to $\mathfrak{B}$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism from $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, \dots, x_r]$ to $\mathfrak{B}$ once images for the indeterminates are chosen. For subrings of the form $\mathfrak{A}[t_1, \dots, t_r]$ (where the $t_i$ are algebraic elements), the extension exists iff the relations among the $t_i$ are preserved. This theorem is fundamental for constructing homomorphisms between field extensions and is used throughout the book to extend isomorphisms to splitting fields.
