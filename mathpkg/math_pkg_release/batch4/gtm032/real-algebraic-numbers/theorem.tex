The field $\mathbb{R} \cap \overline{\mathbb{Q}}$ of real algebraic numbers is the real closure of $\mathbb{Q}$. It consists of all algebraic numbers that are real (i.e., satisfy an ordering extending the usual ordering of $\mathbb{Q}$). It is elementarily equivalent to $\mathbb{R}$ and serves as the prime model of the theory of real closed fields.
