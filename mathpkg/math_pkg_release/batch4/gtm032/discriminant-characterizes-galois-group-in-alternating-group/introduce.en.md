Theorem 1 provides a computable criterion to determine whether the Galois group of a polynomial consists entirely of even permutations. The discriminant $\delta = \prod_{i<j} (\rho_i - \rho_j)^2$ is a symmetric polynomial in the roots, hence expressible in terms of the coefficients. The square root $\Delta$ generates the fixed field of the intersection of $G_f$ with the alternating group $A_m$. Thus $G_f \subseteq A_m$ iff $\delta$ is a perfect square in the base field. This is particularly useful for cubic and quartic equations.
