There exist polynomials over $\mathbb{Q}$ of any degree $n$ whose Galois group is the full symmetric group $S_n$. In particular, the general polynomial $x^n + t_1 x^{n-1} + \cdots + t_n$ over $\mathbb{Q}(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$ has Galois group $S_n$. Hilbert's irreducibility theorem implies that there are infinitely many specializations to $\mathbb{Q}$ with the same Galois group.
