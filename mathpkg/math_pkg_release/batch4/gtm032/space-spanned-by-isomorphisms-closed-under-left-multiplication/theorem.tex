Let $s_1, s_2, \dots, s_n$ be distinct isomorphisms of $E$ into $P$ and let $\mathfrak{A}$ be the set of endomorphisms of the form

$$s_1 \rho_1 + s_2 \rho_2 + \cdots + s_n \rho_n, \quad \rho_i \in P.$$

Then $\mathfrak{A}$ is a subspace of the right $P$-vector space $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$. Moreover, if $\epsilon \in E$, then for any $A = \sum s_i \rho_i \in \mathfrak{A}$,

$$\epsilon_R A = \sum_{i=1}^{n} s_i (\epsilon^{s_i} \rho_i) \in \mathfrak{A}.$$

Thus $\mathfrak{A}$ is closed under left multiplication by arbitrary $\epsilon_R$, $\epsilon \in E$.
