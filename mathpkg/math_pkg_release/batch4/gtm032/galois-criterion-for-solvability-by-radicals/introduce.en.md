Galois' criterion: A polynomial equation is solvable by radicals precisely when its Galois group is a solvable group. The proof involves analyzing radical extensions and their Galois groups, showing that solvability by radicals corresponds to the existence of a tower of cyclic extensions. This is the theorem that launched modern algebra by connecting field theory with group theory.
