Lemma 2: Cyclic extensions always admit normal bases. The proof identifies the generator $s$ of the Galois group as a linear transformation and uses the fact that its minimum polynomial equals $x^n - 1$, together with the existence of a cyclic vector for $s$ (an element whose orbit under $s$ spans the whole space).
