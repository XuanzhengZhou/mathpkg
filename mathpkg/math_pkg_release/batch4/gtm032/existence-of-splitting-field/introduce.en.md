Every polynomial of positive degree admits a splitting field. The proof proceeds by induction on $\deg f$: if $f$ has no irreducible factor of degree $> 1$, then $\Phi$ itself is a splitting field; otherwise, one adjoins a root of an irreducible factor to obtain a field in which $f$ has a linear factor, then applies induction. The degree bound $[P:\Phi] \leq n!$ follows from the iterative construction.
