Let $\Phi$ be a field of characteristic $p \neq 0$ and let $\rho$ be algebraic over $\Phi$.

(i) $\rho$ is separable over $\Phi$ if and only if $\Phi(\rho) = \Phi(\rho^p) = \Phi(\rho^{p^2}) = \cdots$.

(ii) If $\rho$ is purely inseparable over $\Phi$, then its minimum polynomial has the form $x^{p^e} - \alpha$ for some $\alpha \in \Phi$ and $e \geq 0$. Conversely, if $\rho$ satisfies $x^{p^e} = \alpha \in \Phi$ for some $e \geq 0$, then $\rho$ is purely inseparable over $\Phi$.
