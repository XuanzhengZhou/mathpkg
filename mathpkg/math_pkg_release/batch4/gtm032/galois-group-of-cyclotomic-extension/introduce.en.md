The Galois group of a cyclotomic extension (splitting field of $x^n - 1$) embeds into the group of units modulo $n$, because any automorphism must send a primitive $n$-th root of unity to another primitive root, i.e., to $\zeta^k$ where $\gcd(k, n) = 1$. This shows the Galois group is always abelian, and cyclic when $n$ is prime. This theorem is the starting point for the study of abelian extensions and is essential for the proof of the Kronecker-Weber theorem and for Galois' criterion for solvability by radicals.
