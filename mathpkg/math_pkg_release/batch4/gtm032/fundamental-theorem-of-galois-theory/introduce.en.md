The Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory is the central result connecting field theory and group theory. For a Galois (i.e., separable splitting) extension $P/\Phi$, there is a perfect inclusion-reversing correspondence between intermediate fields and subgroups of the Galois group. The degree of an extension equals the index of the corresponding subgroup, and normality of the subgroup corresponds to the intermediate field being Galois over the base. This theorem transforms problems about fields into problems about finite groups.
