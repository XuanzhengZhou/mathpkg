For a simple algebraic extension $\Phi[t]$, the dimension equals the degree of the minimum polynomial of $t$. Using the division algorithm in $\Phi[x]$, every element of $\Phi[t]$ can be uniquely expressed as a polynomial in $t$ of degree less than $\deg f$. Thus the powers $1, t, \ldots, t^{n-1}$ form a basis. This is the fundamental structure theorem for simple algebraic extensions.
