The Jacobson-Bourbaki correspondence is a fundamental structure theorem establishing a bijection between finite-dimensional right $P$-subspace-subrings of $\mathfrak{L}(P, P)$ and subfields of $P$ of finite co-dimension. It generalizes the classical Galois correspondence by replacing the group algebra of automorphisms with more general rings of endomorphisms. The proof constructs a dual basis for $P$ over $\Phi$ using elements of $\mathfrak{A}$, and shows $[P:\Phi] = [\mathfrak{A}:P]_R$. This theorem is the foundation upon which the entire Galois theory in Jacobson's treatment is built.
