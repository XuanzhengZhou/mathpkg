Combining Dedekind's Independence Theorem with the dimensionality result for $\mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P)$ yields a concrete upper bound: a finite extension $E/\Phi$ of degree $n$ admits at most $n$ distinct $\Phi$-isomorphisms into any extension $P/\Phi$. This bound is sharp for Galois extensions, where the number of automorphisms equals the degree. The corollary is essential for proving the uniqueness of splitting fields and establishing the Galois correspondence.
