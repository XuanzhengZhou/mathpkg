Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a subspace of the right $P$-vector space $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$. For a fixed element $\epsilon \in E$, define the mapping $f_\epsilon : \mathfrak{A} \to P$ by

$$f_\epsilon(A) = \epsilon A \in P.$$

Then $f_\epsilon$ is a $P$-linear mapping of the right vector space $\mathfrak{A}$ into $P$, i.e., $f_\epsilon \in \mathfrak{L}_P(\mathfrak{A}, P)$, the dual space of $\mathfrak{A}$ over $P$. Specifically,

$$f_\epsilon(A + B) = f_\epsilon(A) + f_\epsilon(B), \quad f_\epsilon(A\rho) = f_\epsilon(A)\rho$$
for all $A, B \in \mathfrak{A}$ and $\rho \in P$.
