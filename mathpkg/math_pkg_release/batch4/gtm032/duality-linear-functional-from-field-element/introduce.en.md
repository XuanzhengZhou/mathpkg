Every element $\epsilon$ of the field $E$ induces a $P$-linear functional $f_\epsilon$ on any subspace $\mathfrak{A}$ of $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$, defined by evaluation: $f_\epsilon(A) = \epsilon A$. This establishes a duality between $E$ and $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$, where elements of $E$ act as coordinate functions on the space of additive homomorphisms. This duality plays a crucial role in the proof of Theorem 1 and in the Jacobson-Bourbaki correspondence.
