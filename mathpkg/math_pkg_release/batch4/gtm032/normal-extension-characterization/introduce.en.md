This theorem gives three equivalent characterizations of normal extensions. The first is constructive: the extension is generated by roots of a polynomial. The second uses isomorphism invariance: any embedding of $P$ into an extension fixing $\Phi$ maps $P$ onto itself. The third is factorization-theoretic: irreducible polynomials with a root in $P$ split completely. These equivalences are fundamental for understanding the structure of algebraic extensions.
