Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be a commutative ring, $\mathfrak{A}$ a subring, $s$ a homomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ into a commutative ring $\mathfrak{B}$. Let $t_1, t_2, \ldots, t_r \in \mathfrak{C}$ and let $\mathfrak{R}$ be the set of polynomials $f(x_1, \ldots, x_r) \in \mathfrak{A}[x_1, \ldots, x_r]$ such that $f(t_1, \ldots, t_r) = 0$. Then $s$ can be extended to a homomorphism $S$ of $\mathfrak{A}[t_1, \ldots, t_r]$ into $\mathfrak{B}$ such that $t_i^S = u_i$ if and only if for every $f(x_1, \ldots, x_r) \in \mathfrak{R}$ we have $f^s(u_1, \ldots, u_r) = 0$. When this condition holds, the extension is unique and is given by
$$S: h(t_1, \ldots, t_r) \mapsto h^s(u_1, \ldots, u_r).$$
