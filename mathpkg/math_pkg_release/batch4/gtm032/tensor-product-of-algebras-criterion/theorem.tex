Let $\mathfrak{A}_1$ and $\mathfrak{A}_2$ be algebras over $\Phi$, and let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an algebra over $\Phi$ containing subalgebras $\mathfrak{A}_1^{s_1} \cong \mathfrak{A}_1$ and $\mathfrak{A}_2^{s_2} \cong \mathfrak{A}_2$ such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] elements of $\mathfrak{A}_1^{s_1}$ commute with elements of $\mathfrak{A}_2^{s_2}$,
\item[(ii)] $[1_1, 1_2]$ form a basis for the vector space generated by these subalgebras,
\item[(iii)] $\mathfrak{A}$ is generated by $\mathfrak{A}_1^{s_1}$ and $\mathfrak{A}_2^{s_2}$.
\end{itemize}
Then $\mathfrak{A} \cong \mathfrak{A}_1 \otimes_\Phi \mathfrak{A}_2$ as algebras. Equivalently, if $\{e_\alpha\}$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{A}_1$ and $\{f_\beta\}$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{A}_2$, then $\{e_\alpha f_\beta\}$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{A}$.
