Finite fields exist exactly for prime power orders. The cardinality must be a power of the characteristic since a finite field is a vector space over its prime field $\mathbb{F}_p$. Uniqueness follows because any field of order $p^N$ is a splitting field of $x^{p^N} - x$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$.
