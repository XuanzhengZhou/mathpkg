The separability degree $[K:k]_s$ (the degree of the maximal separable subextension) and the inseparability degree $[K:k]_i$ multiply in towers of finite extensions, exactly as the total degree does. Moreover, $[K:k] = [K:k]_s[K:k]_i$, decomposing the total degree into separable and purely inseparable factors. This multiplicative structure is fundamental for understanding arbitrary finite extensions and provides a framework for analyzing the interplay between separable and inseparable parts.
