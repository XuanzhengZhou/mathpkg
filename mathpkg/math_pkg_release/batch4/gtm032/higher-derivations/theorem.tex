A higher derivation of rank $m$ of a field extension $P/\Phi$ is a sequence $D = (D_0 = \text{id}, D_1, \ldots, D_m)$ of $\Phi$-linear maps $P \to P$ satisfying the generalized Leibniz rule:
$$D_k(ab) = \sum_{i=0}^{k} D_i(a) D_{k-i}(b).$$
Higher derivations provide a finer invariant than ordinary derivations for studying inseparable extensions of exponent $> 1$.
