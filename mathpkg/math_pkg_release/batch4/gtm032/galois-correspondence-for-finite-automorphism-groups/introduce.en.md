Theorem 5 establishes the fundamental Galois correspondence for finite automorphism groups. It provides a complete lattice anti-isomorphism between subgroups of a finite automorphism group $G$ and intermediate subfields between $\Phi = I(G)$ and $P$. The theorem implies $[P:\Phi] = |G|$, that normal subgroups correspond to Galois intermediate extensions, and that the Galois group of a Galois intermediate extension is isomorphic to the quotient $G/H$. This theorem, derived from the Jacobson-Bourbaki correspondence, is the cornerstone of classical Galois theory.
