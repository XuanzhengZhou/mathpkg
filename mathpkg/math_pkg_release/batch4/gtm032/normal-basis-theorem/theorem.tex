Let $P/\Phi$ be a finite dimensional Galois extension with Galois group $G = \{s_1, \dots, s_n\}$. Then there exists $\rho \in P$ such that $(\rho^{s_1}, \rho^{s_2}, \dots, \rho^{s_n})$ is a basis for $P$ over $\Phi$. Such a basis is called a normal basis.

Equivalently, as $\Phi(G)$-modules, $P$ is isomorphic to the group algebra $\Phi(G)$, where $G$ acts on $P$ via the Galois action and $\Phi(G)$ acts on itself by right multiplication.
