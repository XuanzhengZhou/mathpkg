Combining Dedekind's theorem with Theorem 1, distinct isomorphisms of $E/\Phi$ into $P/\Phi$ are $P$-independent elements of $\mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P)$, whose $P$-dimension is $[E:\Phi] = n$. Hence there can be at most $n$ such isomorphisms. This bound is sharp and is attained precisely for Galois extensions.
