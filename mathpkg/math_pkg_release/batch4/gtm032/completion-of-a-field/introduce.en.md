The completion of a valued field fills in "gaps" analogously to constructing $\mathbb{R}$ from $\mathbb{Q}$. Complete fields have much nicer properties: Hensel's Lemma holds, polynomials factor nicely, and extensions are better behaved. This is the starting point for local field theory.
