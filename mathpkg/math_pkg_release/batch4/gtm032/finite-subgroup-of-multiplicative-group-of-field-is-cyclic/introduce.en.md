A fundamental fact about fields: any finite subgroup of the multiplicative group is cyclic. The proof uses the property of finite abelian groups that an element of maximal order generates all elements whose orders divide that maximal order. Since a polynomial $x^m - 1$ has at most $m$ roots in a field, the maximal order must equal the group order, forcing the group to be cyclic. This lemma immediately implies that the multiplicative group of any finite field is cyclic, which greatly simplifies the structure theory of finite fields.
