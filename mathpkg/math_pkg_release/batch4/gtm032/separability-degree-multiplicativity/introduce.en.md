The separable and inseparable degrees are multiplicative in towers of finite extensions. The proof reduces to two cases: purely inseparable extensions (where the separable degree is $1$) and separable extensions (where one uses the lemma on the tensor product decomposition of normal extensions). This multiplicativity is essential for the structure theory of algebraic extensions.
