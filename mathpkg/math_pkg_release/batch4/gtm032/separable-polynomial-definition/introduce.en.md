A separable polynomial has no repeated roots in its splitting field. Separability can be tested algebraically via the formal derivative: $(f, f') = 1$. In characteristic $0$, all irreducible polynomials are separable. In characteristic $p$, an irreducible polynomial is inseparable exactly when it is a polynomial in $x^p$. This notion is fundamental for Galois theory, as Galois extensions are precisely those generated by separable polynomials.
