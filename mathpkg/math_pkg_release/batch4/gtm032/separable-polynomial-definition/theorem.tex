A polynomial $f(x) \in \Phi[x]$ of positive degree is called \text{separable} if its roots in a splitting field are all simple (i.e., of multiplicity $1$). Equivalently, $(f, f') = 1$ where $f'$ is the formal derivative. An irreducible polynomial is separable if and only if $f' \neq 0$. An algebraic element $\rho$ over $\Phi$ is called \text{separable} if its minimum polynomial over $\Phi$ is separable.
