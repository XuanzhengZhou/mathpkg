The crossed product construction generalizes group rings by introducing a factor set (2-cocycle) that twists the multiplication. Crossed products are central simple algebras and every central simple algebra is similar to a crossed product. Factor set equivalence classes form the second cohomology group $H^2(G, P^*)$, which describes the Brauer group.
