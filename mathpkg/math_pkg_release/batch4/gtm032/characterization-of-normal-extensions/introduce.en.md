Theorem 10 provides three equivalent characterizations of normal (finite-dimensional) extensions: as splitting fields of polynomials, as extensions where every $\Phi$-isomorphism into a larger field is already an automorphism, and as extensions where every irreducible polynomial with a root in the extension splits completely. The equivalence of these conditions is fundamental for understanding the structure of field extensions and for proving that separability and normality together characterize Galois extensions.
