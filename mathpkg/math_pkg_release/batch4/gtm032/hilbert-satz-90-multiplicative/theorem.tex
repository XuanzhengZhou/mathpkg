Let $P/\Phi$ be a finite dimensional Galois extension with Galois group $G$. A mapping $s \mapsto \mu_s \in P^*$ satisfying Noether's equations $\mu_{st} = \mu_s^t \mu_t$ for all $s, t \in G$ is called a crossed homomorphism. Then every such mapping has the form $\mu_s = \gamma(\gamma^s)^{-1}$ for some $\gamma \in P^*$. In particular (Hilbert's Satz 90): If $G$ is cyclic generated by $g$ and $N_{P|\Phi}(\mu) = 1$, then $\mu = \gamma(\gamma^g)^{-1}$ for some $\gamma \in P^*$.
