This lemma states that finite subgroups of the multiplicative group of any field are cyclic. The proof uses the fact that in a finite commutative group there is an element whose order is the lcm of all element orders, combined with the bound on roots of $x^m - 1 = 0$ in a field. For finite fields, this implies $P^*$ is cyclic, so every finite field is a simple extension of its prime field generated by a primitive root.
