Hensel's Lemma is the non-archimedean analogue of Newton's method. It allows lifting approximate roots to exact roots in complete valued fields. This fundamental result underlies much of $p$-adic analysis and algebraic number theory, enabling factorization of polynomials and analysis of extensions of complete discretely valued fields.
