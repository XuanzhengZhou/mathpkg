Let $K$ be a field complete with respect to a non-archimedean valuation, let $\mathfrak{o}$ be its valuation ring, and let $f(x) \in \mathfrak{o}[x]$. If there exists $a_0 \in \mathfrak{o}$ such that $|f(a_0)| < |f'(a_0)|^2$, then there exists a unique $a \in \mathfrak{o}$ such that $f(a) = 0$ and $|a - a_0| \leq |f(a_0)|/|f'(a_0)|$.
