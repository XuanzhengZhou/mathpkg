Let $E/\Phi$ and $P/\Phi$ be field extensions and assume $[E:\Phi] = n < \infty$. Then

$$[\mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P) : P]_R = n.$$

Moreover, if $(\eta_1, \eta_2, \dots, \eta_n)$ is a basis for $E/\Phi$, then there exist elements $E_1, E_2, \dots, E_n$ in $\mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P)$ such that

$$\eta_i E_j = \delta_{ij},$$

and $(E_1, E_2, \dots, E_n)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P)$ over $P$. Every $A \in \mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P)$ can be expressed as

$$A = \sum_{i=1}^{n} E_i (\eta_i A).$$
