When the domain field $E$ is finite-dimensional over a common subfield $\Phi$, the space $\mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P)$ of $\Phi$-linear transformations from $E$ to $P$ has right dimension over $P$ equal to $[E:\Phi]$. An explicit dual basis $\{E_i\}$ is constructed relative to a given basis $\{\eta_i\}$ of $E/\Phi$, satisfying $\eta_i E_j = \delta_{ij}$. Every $\Phi$-linear map $A$ then admits the representation $A = \sum E_i(\eta_i A)$, which is a key tool in the Jacobson-Bourbaki correspondence.
