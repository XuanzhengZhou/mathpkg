When the base field contains all $n$-th roots of unity, the splitting field of a pure equation $x^n = \alpha$ is a cyclic Kummer extension. The Galois group embeds into the cyclic group of $n$-th roots of unity via the action on a chosen root $\rho$. The order of the Galois group is the smallest $d$ such that $\alpha^d$ is an $n$-th power in $\Phi$. This is the foundation of Kummer theory, which classifies abelian extensions of exponent $n$ over fields containing $n$-th roots of unity.
