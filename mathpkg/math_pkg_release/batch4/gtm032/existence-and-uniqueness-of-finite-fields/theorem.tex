For every prime $p$ and integer $N \geq 1$, there exists a field with $p^N$ elements, and any two such fields are isomorphic. The field of order $p^N$ is the splitting field of $x^{p^N} - x$ over $\mathbb{F}_p = \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$. Its elements are precisely the roots of this polynomial, and its multiplicative group is cyclic of order $p^N - 1$.
