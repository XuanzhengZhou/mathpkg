Finite fields (Galois fields) are completely classified by their order: exactly one exists for each prime power. They are denoted $\mathbb{F}_{p^N}$ or $\text{GF}(p^N)$. The Frobenius automorphism $x \mapsto x^p$ generates the cyclic Galois group, and subfields correspond to divisors of $N$.
