Dedekind's independence theorem states that distinct field isomorphisms from $E$ into $P$ are linearly independent over $P$ when viewed as elements of the right $P$-vector space of additive homomorphisms. The proof proceeds by induction and uses the fact that if a non-trivial linear relation exists, one can construct a shorter one, leading to a contradiction. This is a cornerstone of Galois theory.
