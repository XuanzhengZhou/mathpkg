Noether's equations $\mu_{st} = \mu_s^t \mu_t$ define 1-cocycles of the Galois group $G$ with values in the multiplicative group $P^*$ of the extension field. Theorem 18 (often called the "Theorem of the 90" in reference to Hilbert's Satz 90) asserts that every such cocycle is a coboundary: $\mu_s = \gamma(\gamma^s)^{-1}$. The proof uses Dedekind's independence theorem to find a suitable $\gamma$ via a linear combination $\gamma = \sum \beta^s \mu_s$. This result is fundamental in Galois cohomology and algebraic number theory.
