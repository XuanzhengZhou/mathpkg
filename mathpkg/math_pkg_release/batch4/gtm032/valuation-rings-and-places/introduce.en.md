Valuation rings provide a ring-theoretic characterization of valuations: a subring is a valuation ring exactly when it contains at least one of $x$ or $x^{-1}$ for every non-zero field element. Places are the geometric counterpart, mapping "most" of a field to a residue field. This equivalence unifies algebraic, arithmetic, and geometric perspectives.
