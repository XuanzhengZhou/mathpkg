A cyclic algebra is the simplest non-trivial crossed product, associated to a cyclic Galois extension. It is generated by the field $P$ and an element $u$ satisfying $u^n = \alpha \in \Phi^*$ and the commutation relation $u\rho = \rho^g u$. Cyclic algebras are fundamental in the theory of central simple algebras and the Brauer group.
