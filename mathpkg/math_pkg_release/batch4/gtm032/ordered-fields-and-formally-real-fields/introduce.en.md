The Artin-Schreier theorem bridges algebra and order: a field can be ordered precisely when $-1$ is not a sum of squares. This purely algebraic condition characterizes orderability. Real closed fields are the maximal ordered fields, analogous to algebraically closed fields for the property of admitting roots of odd-degree polynomials rather than all polynomials.
