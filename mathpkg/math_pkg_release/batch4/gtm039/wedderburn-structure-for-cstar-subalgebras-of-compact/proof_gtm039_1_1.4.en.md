Write the identity representation of $A$ as $\sum^\oplus n(\zeta) \cdot \zeta$, where $\zeta$ runs over equivalence classes of irreducible subrepresentations. The map $T \mapsto (\zeta(T))_\zeta$ is an isometric $*$-homomorphism of $A$ into $\sum \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H}_\zeta)$. Since each $\zeta(A)$ is irreducible in $\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H}_\zeta)$, $\zeta(A) = \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H}_\zeta)$ by 1.4.2. Compactness ensures $\lim_{\zeta \to \infty} \|\zeta(T)\| = 0$, giving surjectivity onto the direct sum. $\square$
