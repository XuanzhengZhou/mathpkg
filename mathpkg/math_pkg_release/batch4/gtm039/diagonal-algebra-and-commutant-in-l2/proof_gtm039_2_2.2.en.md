Clearly $\pi_\mu(C(X)) \subseteq \mathcal{Z} \subseteq \mathcal{Z}'$. For $T \in \mathcal{Z}'$, let $g = T1$. For bounded $\xi$, $|\int g\xi\bar{\eta}d\mu| = |(L_\xi T1,\eta)| = |(T\xi,\eta)| \leqslant \|T\|\|\xi\|_2\|\eta\|_2$. This forces $|g| \leqslant \|T\|$ a.e., so $g \in \mathcal{B}(X)$ and $T = L_g$. Strong density: bounded Borel functions are $L^2$-limits of continuous functions. $\square$
