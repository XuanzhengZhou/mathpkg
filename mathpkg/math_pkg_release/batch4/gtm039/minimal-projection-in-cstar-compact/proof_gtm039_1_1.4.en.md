Every nonzero $C^*$-algebra of compact operators contains nonzero projections (by the spectral theorem for compact self-adjoint operators). Let $E$ be any nonzero projection in $\mathcal{A}$. If $E$ is not minimal, there exists a proper nonzero subprojection $F \in \mathcal{A}$ with $F < E$. Since $E$ has finite rank (compactness), repeated decomposition yields a minimal projection in finitely many steps. $\square$
