If $\text{sp}(S) = \text{sp}(T)$, the functional calculus gives $*$-isomorphisms $C(\text{sp}(S)) \cong C^*(S)$ and $C(\text{sp}(T)) \cong C^*(T)$, both sending the identity function to the respective operator. Composing yields an algebraic equivalence. Conversely, any $*$-isomorphism $C^*(S) \to C^*(T)$ carrying $S \mapsto T$ preserves spectra, so $\text{sp}(S) = \text{sp}(T)$. $\square$
