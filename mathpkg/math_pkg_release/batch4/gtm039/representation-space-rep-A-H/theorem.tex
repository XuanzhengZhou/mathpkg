Let $A$ be a separable $C^*$-algebra and $\mathcal{H}$ a separable Hilbert space. Then $\text{rep}(A, \mathcal{H})$ denotes the set of all representations of $A$ on $\mathcal{H}$, endowed with the weakest topology making all functions $\pi \mapsto (\pi(x)\xi, \eta)$ continuous for $x \in A$, $\xi, \eta \in \mathcal{H}$. The subspace $\text{irr}(A, \mathcal{H})$ consists of all irreducible representations.
