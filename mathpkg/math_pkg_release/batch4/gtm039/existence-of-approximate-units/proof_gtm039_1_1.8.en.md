Let $\Lambda$ be the directed set of all finite subsets of self-adjoint elements. For $\lambda = \{x_1,\ldots,x_n\}$, define $e_\lambda = f_n(x_1^2+\cdots+x_n^2)$ with $f_n(t) = nt(1+nt)^{-1}$. $0 \leqslant f_n \leqslant 1$ gives $\text{sp}(e_\lambda) \subseteq [0,1]$. For $\lambda \leqslant \mu$, $f_m \leqslant f_n$ (when $m \leqslant n$) implies $e_\lambda \leqslant e_\mu$. For $x = x^*$, $\|xe_\lambda - x\|^2 = \|(e-e_\lambda)x^2(e-e_\lambda)\| \leqslant \|x^2(e-e_\lambda)\|$. Since $(1-f_n(t)) = (1+nt)^{-1} \leqslant 1/(nt)$, the right side tends to 0. $\square$
