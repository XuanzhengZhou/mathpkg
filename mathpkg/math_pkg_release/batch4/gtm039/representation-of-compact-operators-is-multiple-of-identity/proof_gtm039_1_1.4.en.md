Let $\pi$ be any representation. By 1.4.4, $\pi = \sum_i \pi_i$ where each $\pi_i$ is equivalent to a subrepresentation of the identity representation. Each nonzero $\pi_i$ is irreducible (as a subrepresentation of the irreducible identity representation), hence equivalent to the identity representation. Choosing unitaries $U_i$ implementing these equivalences, $U = \sum^\oplus U_i$ gives $\pi \cong n \cdot \text{id}$. $\square$
