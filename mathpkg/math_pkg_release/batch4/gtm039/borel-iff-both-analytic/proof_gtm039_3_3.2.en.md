If $S$ and $P \setminus S$ are analytic, the separation theorem (3.2.2) gives a Borel set $C$ with $S \subseteq C$ and $P \setminus S \subseteq P \setminus C$, so $S = C$ is Borel. The converse follows from 3.2.1: Borel sets are continuous 1-1 images of Polish spaces. $\square$
