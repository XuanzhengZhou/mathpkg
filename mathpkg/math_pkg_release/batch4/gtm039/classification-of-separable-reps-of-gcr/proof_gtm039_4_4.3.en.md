Every representation is type I (2.4.1). By 2.1.8, decompose $\pi = \sum_n \pi_n$ with $\pi_n$ of multiplicity $n$, where $\pi_n \cong n \cdot \sigma_n$ for multiplicity-free $\sigma_n$. By 4.3.4, $\sigma_n \cong \pi_{\mu_n}$ for some measure $\mu_n$, unique up to equivalence. Disjointness (2.1.5) forces $\mu_i \perp \mu_j$ for $i \neq j$. Equivalently, $\pi \cong \int_{\hat{A}}^\oplus m(\zeta)\cdot\zeta\,d\mu(\zeta)$. $\square$
