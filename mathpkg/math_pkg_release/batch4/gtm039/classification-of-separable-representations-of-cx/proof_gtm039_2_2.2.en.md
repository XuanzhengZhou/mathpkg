By 2.2.3, every multiplicity-free representation has a cyclic vector $\xi$. Define $\mu(E) = (\pi(\chi_E)\xi,\xi)$ via the spectral theorem. The map $Ug = \pi(g)\xi$ extends to a unitary $U: L^2(X,\mu) \to \mathcal{H}$ intertwining $\pi_\mu$ and $\pi$. Thus $\pi \cong \pi_\mu$. The general case follows from the central decomposition 2.1.8: every representation is $\sum n \cdot \pi_{\mu_n}$ with $\mu_i \perp \mu_j$. $\square$
