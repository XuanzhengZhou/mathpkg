For $\|z\| \leqslant 1$, the Schwarz inequality gives $|f(z)|^2 \leqslant f(z^*z)f(e)$. Show $f(z^*z) \leqslant f(e)$, i.e. $f(e-z^*z) \geqslant 0$. For $x = x^*$ with $\|x\| \leqslant 1$, $e-x = y^2$ (functional calculus). Since $f(y^2) = f(y^*y) \geqslant 0$, $f(e-x) \geqslant 0$, yielding $\|f\| \leqslant f(e)$. The reverse inequality is trivial. $\square$
