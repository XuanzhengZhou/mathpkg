Let $\pi$ be a type I representation of a $C^*$-algebra $A$ on a separable Hilbert space. Then there exists a unique sequence $\pi_\infty, \pi_1, \pi_2, \ldots$ of mutually orthogonal central subrepresentations of $\pi$, with $\pi_n$ having multiplicity $n$, such that $\pi = \pi_\infty \oplus \pi_1 \oplus \pi_2 \oplus \cdots$.
