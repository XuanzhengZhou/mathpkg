If $\mathcal{Z} \subseteq \mathcal{A}'' \subseteq \mathcal{Z}'$, then the center of $\mathcal{A}''$ is $\mathcal{Z}$. For $x \neq y$, pick a Borel set $E$ separating them. $\chi_E$ is in $\mathcal{Z} = \mathcal{A}'' \cap \mathcal{A}'$. This forces the fiber representations at $x$ and $y$ to be disjoint. By Corollary 4, $\mathcal{A}''$ consists precisely of all multiplications $L_F$ with $F(x) \in \pi_x(A)''$ a.e. $\square$
