For a sequence $T_n \in \mathcal{A}$ converging strongly, a diagonal argument using a countable dense subset of $\mathcal{K}$ yields a subsequence such that $\pi(T_n)(x) \to F(x)$ strongly for almost every $x$. The key estimate uses the uniform boundedness of $\{\|T_n\|\}$ and the separability of the range Hilbert space. $\square$
