Let $\mathcal{F} = \{f(P): P \text{ Polish}, f: P \to X$ continuous 1-1$\}$. $\mathcal{F}$ contains all closed sets. Closed under complements: the complement is also the 1-1 continuous image of a Polish space. Closed under countable intersections: use the diagonal product construction. Closed under countable disjoint unions: use the direct sum of Polish spaces. $\square$
