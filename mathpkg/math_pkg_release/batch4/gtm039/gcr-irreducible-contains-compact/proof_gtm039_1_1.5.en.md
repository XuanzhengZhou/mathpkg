$\pi(A) \cong A/\ker\pi$ contains a nonzero CCR ideal $J$ (since $A$ is GCR). The restriction of the identity representation of $\pi(A)$ to $J$ is irreducible by 1.3.4 (since $\pi(A)$ is primitive). Since $J$ is CCR, its image is an irreducible $C^*$-algebra of compact operators, which by 1.4.2 must be all of $\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H})$. Hence $\pi(A) \supseteq \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H})$. $\square$
