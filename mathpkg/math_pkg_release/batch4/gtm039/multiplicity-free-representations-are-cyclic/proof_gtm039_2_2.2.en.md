Take a maximal family $\{\xi_i\}$ of unit vectors with $\pi(A)\xi_i \perp \pi(A)\xi_j$ for $i \neq j$ (Zorn's lemma). The cyclic subrepresentations generated by $\xi_i$ are mutually disjoint and multiplicity-free. Their central covers are orthogonal. If there are at least two distinct $\xi_i$, the commutant would be non-abelian (they'd generate a non-commuting partial isometry), contradicting multiplicity-free. Thus there is exactly one cyclic vector. $\square$
