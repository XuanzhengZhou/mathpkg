Consider $\alpha: \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H}) \to \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{K})$. View $\alpha$ as an irreducible representation of $\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H})$ on $\mathcal{K}$. By Corollary 2, $\alpha \sim \text{id}$, so there exists a unitary $U$ with $\alpha(T) = UTU^*$. For $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{H})$, restrict to $\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H})$ to get the unitary, then extend by weak continuity. $\square$
