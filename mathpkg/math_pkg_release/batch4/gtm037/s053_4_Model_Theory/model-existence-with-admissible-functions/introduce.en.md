Theorem 18.9 refines the Model Existence Theorem by incorporating admissible functions that control the growth of the constructed model. For any consistency family $S$ with admissible functions on a rich expansion $\mathcal{L}'$, each member $\Gamma$ of $S$ possesses a model whose universe is generated by the new constant symbols $C$, guaranteeing $|A| \leq |\text{Fmla}_\mathcal{L}|$. Moreover, the construction provides a chain of intermediate sets $\Delta \in S$ mediated by the admissible functions $f_\alpha$, establishing a tight relationship between the consistency family and the theory of the resulting model. This theorem is a key technical tool for deriving cardinality bounds in model-theoretic constructions.
