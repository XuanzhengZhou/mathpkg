Let $\mathcal{L}'$ be a rich expansion of a first-order language $\mathcal{L}$ by a set $C$ of new constant symbols, and let $S$ be a consistency family for $\mathcal{L}'$. Let $\langle f_\alpha : \alpha < |\text{Fmla}_\mathcal{L}| \rangle$ be a system of admissible functions for $S$. Then for each $\Gamma \in S$, there exists an $\mathcal{L}'$-structure $(\mathfrak{A}, a_c)_{c \in C}$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $A = \{a_c : c \in C\}$, and consequently $|A| \leq |\text{Fmla}_\mathcal{L}|$;
\item[(ii)] for each $\alpha < |\text{Fmla}_\mathcal{L}|$, there exists $\Delta \in S$ such that
$$
\Gamma \subseteq f_\alpha \Delta \subseteq \{\varphi : (\mathfrak{A}, a_c)_{c \in C} \models \varphi\}.
$$
\end{enumerate}

In particular, $(\mathfrak{A}, a_c)_{c \in C}$ is a model of $\Gamma$.
