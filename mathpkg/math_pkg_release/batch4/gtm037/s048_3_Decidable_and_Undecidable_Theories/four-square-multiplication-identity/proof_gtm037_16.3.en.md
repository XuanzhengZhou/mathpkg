The identity is verified by direct expansion of both sides. The left-hand side is the product of two sums of four squares. Expanding the right-hand side, each of the four squared terms yields cross terms that, when summed, reproduce exactly the expanded product. The identity is a classical result due to Euler and can be checked by straightforward algebraic manipulation.
