Lemma 16.3 is the classical Euler four-square identity: the product of two sums of four squares is itself a sum of four squares. This purely algebraic identity is computationally straightforward and plays a crucial role in the proof of Lagrange's four-square theorem. In the context of mathematical logic, the identity is used to show that the set of natural numbers is definable in the ring of integers $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$, which ultimately establishes that the theory of $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$ is inseparable.
