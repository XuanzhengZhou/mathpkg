Theorem 16.1 establishes that the fundamental arithmetic theories $R$ (Robinson's minimal arithmetic), $Q$ (Robinson arithmetic), $P$ (Peano arithmetic), and $N$ (true arithmetic) are all inseparable. A theory is inseparable if the sets of Godel numbers of its theorems and of sentences whose negations are theorems cannot be separated by a recursive set. Inseparability implies essential undecidability (no consistent extension is decidable) and hence undecidability. This follows immediately from Theorem 15.19, which connects spectral representability to inseparability.
