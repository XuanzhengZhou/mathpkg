By Theorem 15.3, any inseparable theory is incomplete. Theorem 16.1 establishes that $R$, $Q$, $P$, and $N$ are inseparable. Therefore $R$, $Q$, and $P$ are incomplete. Moreover, if $\Gamma$ is a recursively axiomatizable consistent extension of any of these theories, then by the properties of inseparability, $\Gamma$ is also inseparable and hence incomplete.
