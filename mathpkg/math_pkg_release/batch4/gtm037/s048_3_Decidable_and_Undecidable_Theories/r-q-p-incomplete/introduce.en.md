Theorem 16.2 states that the theories $R$, $Q$, and $P$ (as well as any of their recursively axiomatizable consistent extensions) are incomplete. This means there exist sentences in the language of these theories that are neither provable nor disprovable. The result follows by applying Theorem 15.3 to the inseparability established in Theorem 16.1. In particular, this gives a proof of Godel's first incompleteness theorem for Peano arithmetic $P$.
