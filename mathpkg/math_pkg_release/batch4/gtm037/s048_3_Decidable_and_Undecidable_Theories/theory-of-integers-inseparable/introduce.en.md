Theorem 16.7 states that the complete first-order theory of the ring of integers $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$ is inseparable. The proof uses Lagrange's four-square theorem (Theorem 16.6) to define the natural numbers $\omega$ within $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$, thereby constructing an effective interpretation of the theory $N$ into a definitional expansion of the theory of $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$. Since $N$ is inseparable, Theorem 15.16 implies that the theory of $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$ is also inseparable.
