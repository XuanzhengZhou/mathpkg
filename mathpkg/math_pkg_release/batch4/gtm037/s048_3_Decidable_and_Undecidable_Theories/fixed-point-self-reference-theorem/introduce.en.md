The Fixed-Point Self-Reference Theorem (also called the diagonalization lemma) is a fundamental result in mathematical logic. It states that for any theory $\Gamma$ in the language of number theory $\mathcal{L}_{\text{nos}}$ in which every unary recursive function is syntactically definable, and for any formula $\varphi$ with at most $v_0$ free, there exists a sentence $\psi$ such that $\Gamma$ proves the equivalence $\varphi(\Delta g^+ \psi) \leftrightarrow \psi$. Here $\Delta g^+ \psi$ denotes the numeral for the Godel number of $\psi$. This theorem provides a rigorous formalization of self-reference and is the key technical lemma used in Godel's first incompleteness theorem.
