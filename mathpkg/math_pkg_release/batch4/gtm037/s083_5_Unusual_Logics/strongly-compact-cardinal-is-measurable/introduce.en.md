Theorem 31.16 from Ebbinghaus, Flum, and Thomas's *Mathematical Logic* establishes that every strongly compact cardinal is measurable. This places strong compactness above measurability in the large cardinal hierarchy. The proof constructs an $m$-complete ultrafilter on the cardinal using properties of the $m$-topology on product spaces and the existence of $m$-complete prime ideals extending $m$-complete ideals. The result is a key step in relating compactness properties of infinitary logics to large cardinal axioms.
