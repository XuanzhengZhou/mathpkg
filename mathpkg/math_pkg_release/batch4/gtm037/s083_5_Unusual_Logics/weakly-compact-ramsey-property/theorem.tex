For a strongly inaccessible cardinal $m$, the following are equivalent:
(i) $m$ is weakly compact;
(ii) $m \rightarrow (m)^2_2$ (the Ramsey partition relation);
(iii) every tree of height $m$ with levels of size $< m$ has a branch of length $m$ (the tree property);
and several other equivalent conditions involving linear orderings and model-theoretic compactness.
