For strongly inaccessible cardinals, weak compactness is equivalent to several combinatorial properties: the Ramsey partition relation $m \rightarrow (m)^2_2$ (any graph on $m$ vertices has a homogeneous set of size $m$), the tree property (every tree of height $m$ with small levels has a cofinal branch), and model-theoretic compactness. These equivalences connect logic, combinatorics, and set theory through the notion of large cardinals.
