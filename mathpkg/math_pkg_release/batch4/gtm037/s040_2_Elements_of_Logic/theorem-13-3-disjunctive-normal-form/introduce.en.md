Theorem 13.3, the Disjunctive Normal Form Theorem, states that for any set $\Gamma$ of formulas and any quantifier-free formula $\varphi$ whose negation is not a tautology, $\varphi$ is logically equivalent to a disjunction of conjunctions, where each conjunct is either a member of $\Gamma$ or the negation of a member of $\Gamma$. This theorem is a key technical tool in decidability proofs via quantifier elimination, as it allows reducing an arbitrary quantifier-free formula to a normal form built from the basic formulas of the theory. The proof is referenced as Theorem 8.38 in the text.
