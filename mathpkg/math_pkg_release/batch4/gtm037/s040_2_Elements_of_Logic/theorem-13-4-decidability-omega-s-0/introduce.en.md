Theorem 13.4 establishes the decidability of the first-order theory of the natural numbers with successor function and zero constant $(\omega, s, 0)$. The proof uses the method of elimination of quantifiers: one first singles out a class of basic formulas (equations of the form $s^m v_i = \sigma$ where $\sigma$ does not involve $v_i$), then shows that any formula is effectively equivalent under the theory $\Gamma_1$ to a Boolean combination of basic formulas. Since it is effectively decidable whether a Boolean combination of basic formulas holds in $(\omega, s, 0)$, the theory is decidable.
