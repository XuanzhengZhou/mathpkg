Definition 13.1 formalizes the notion of a decidable theory in the framework of effectivized first-order languages. A theory $\Gamma$ is called decidable precisely when its image under the Godel numbering $g^{+*}$ forms a recursive set of natural numbers. This concept is central to Part III of Monk's book, where the goal is to determine for various mathematically interesting theories whether there exists an effective procedure to decide the truth of any sentence under the theory's semantic consequence relation.
