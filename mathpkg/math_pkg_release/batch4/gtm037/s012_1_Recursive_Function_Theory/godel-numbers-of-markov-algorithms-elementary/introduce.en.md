This lemma establishes that the set of Gödel numbers of Markov algorithms is an elementary (first-order definable) relation in arithmetic. This is a crucial step in the arithmetization of computability theory: by showing that the encoding of Markov algorithms is elementary, one can reason about algorithms within formal arithmetic. The proof uses the characterization that $n$ encodes a Markov algorithm if and only if $n \geq 2$ and every exponent in its prime factorization encodes a valid algorithm row.
