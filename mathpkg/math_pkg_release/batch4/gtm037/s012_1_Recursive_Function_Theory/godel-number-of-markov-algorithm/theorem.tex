If $A$ is a Markov algorithm as in 4.1, its Gödel number, $gA$, is the number
$$\prod_{i < m} p_i^{t_i},$$
where $t_i = 2^{g a_i} \cdot 3^{g b_i} \cdot 5^{c_i}$ for each $i \leq m$, with $p_i$ the $i$-th prime, $g a_i$ and $g b_i$ the Gödel numbers of the words $a_i$ and $b_i$, and $c_i \in \{0, 1\}$ indicating whether the row is terminating.
