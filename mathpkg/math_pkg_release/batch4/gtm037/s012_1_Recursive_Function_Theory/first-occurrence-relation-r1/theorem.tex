Let $R_1 = \{(m, n, p, q) : m, n, p, q \text{ are Gödel numbers of words } a, b, c, d \text{ respectively, and } (a, b, c) \text{ is the first occurrence of } b \text{ in } d\}$.

Here $(a, b, c)$ being the first occurrence of $b$ in $d$ means that $d = \operatorname{Cat}(\operatorname{Cat}(a, b), c)$ and no proper initial segment of $a$ contains an occurrence of $b$ in $d$.
