A free Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ with free generators $X$ satisfies the universal mapping property: any function from $X$ into an arbitrary Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{B}$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism from $\mathfrak{A}$ to $\mathfrak{B}$. This mirrors the notion of free objects in universal algebra. The free Boolean algebra on $X$ is unique up to isomorphism and can be constructed explicitly as the Lindenbaum algebra of the sentential language with $X$ as propositional variables.
