This lemma formalizes the principle of transport of structure: given a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ and a bijection $f$ from its underlying set $A$ onto a set $B$, one can define Boolean operations on $B$ by pushing the operations of $\mathfrak{A}$ forward through $f$. Explicitly, $b +' c = f(f^{-1}(b) + f^{-1}(c))$, and similarly for the other operations. The resulting structure $\mathfrak{B}$ is a Boolean algebra and $f$ becomes an isomorphism.
