This theorem establishes that the countable atomless Boolean algebra is unique up to isomorphism. The proof applies the back-and-forth method (Theorem 9.47) to the relation $R = \{(\mathfrak{A}, \mathfrak{B}) : |A| = |B| = 1 \text{ or } \mathfrak{A} \text{ and } \mathfrak{B} \text{ are denumerable and atomless}\}$. This is the Boolean-algebraic analogue of Cantor's theorem that any two countable dense linear orders without endpoints are isomorphic.
