Exercise 9.70 from Chapter 9 of Monk's Mathematical Logic, completing the Stone duality picture. A Stone space is defined as a compact Hausdorff space with a basis of clopen sets. The clopen sets of any Stone space form a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{G}X$, and there is a natural homeomorphism between $X$ and the Stone space $F\mathfrak{G}X$ of its clopen algebra. This establishes the other half of Stone duality: the category of Stone spaces with continuous maps is dually equivalent to the category of Boolean algebras with homomorphisms.
