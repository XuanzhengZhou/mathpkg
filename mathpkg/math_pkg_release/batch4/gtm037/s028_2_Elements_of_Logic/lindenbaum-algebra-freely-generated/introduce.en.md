This theorem establishes the fundamental correspondence between sentential logic and Boolean algebras. The Lindenbaum algebra $\mathfrak{M}_{\mathcal{P}}$ of a sentential language $\mathcal{P}$ (the quotient of all sentences by logical equivalence) is a Boolean algebra freely generated by the equivalence classes of atomic sentences. Moreover, distinct propositional variables yield distinct equivalence classes. This means that the category of Boolean algebras is, in a precise sense, equivalent to the theory of sentential logics.
