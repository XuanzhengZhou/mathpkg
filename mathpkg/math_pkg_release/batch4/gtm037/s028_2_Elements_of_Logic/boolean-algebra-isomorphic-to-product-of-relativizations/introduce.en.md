This proposition provides a fundamental decomposition theorem: every Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ is isomorphic to the direct product of its relativizations to an element $a$ and its complement $-a$. The isomorphism sends each $x \in A$ to the pair $\langle x \cdot a, x \cdot -a \rangle$. This decomposition is used in the proof of the isomorphism theorem for denumerable atomless Boolean algebras (Theorem 9.48) via the back-and-forth argument in Theorem 9.47.
