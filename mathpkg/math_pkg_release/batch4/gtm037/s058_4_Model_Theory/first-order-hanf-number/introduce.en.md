This corollary applies the Hanf system framework to first-order logic. The relation associating a set of sentences $\Gamma$ with its models and their cardinalities forms a Hanf system, and its Hanf number is $\omega$. This formalizes the upward Löwenheim–Skolem phenomenon in the language of Hanf systems: if a theory has a model of cardinality at least $\omega$ (i.e., an infinite model), then it has arbitrarily large models.
