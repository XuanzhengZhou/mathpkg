This corollary of the downward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem (Theorem 19.18) characterizes universal validity purely in terms of countable structures. It states that a first-order sentence is universally valid if and only if it holds in every countable structure of the language, regardless of the cardinality of the set of formulas. As an application, the Skolem paradox is obtained: if ZF set theory has a model (which must be infinite), then it has a denumerable model, even though one can prove the existence of non-denumerable sets within ZF.
