This corollary of the upward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem establishes a striking dichotomy for first-order sentences. Any sentence either has no infinite models whatsoever, or it has infinite models of every possible infinite cardinality. This means that first-order logic cannot distinguish between different infinite cardinalities — if a sentence admits, say, a countable model, it also admits models of cardinality $\aleph_1$, $\aleph_2$, and beyond. The discussion following the corollary introduces the notion of the spectrum $\mathrm{Sp}\,\varphi$, the set of finite cardinalities of models of $\varphi$, which exhibits more nuanced behavior.
