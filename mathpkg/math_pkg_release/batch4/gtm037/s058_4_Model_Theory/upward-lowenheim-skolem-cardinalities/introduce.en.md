This corollary of the upward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem (Theorem 19.24) states that any set of first-order sentences possessing an infinite model has models of every cardinality at least as large as the cardinality of the set of formulas of the language. Combined with the compactness theorem, this yields a powerful conclusion: if a theory has arbitrarily large finite models, then it has models of every infinite cardinality above the threshold $|\mathrm{Fmla}_{\mathcal{L}}|$.
