This is the fundamental theorem about Hanf systems. It states that for any Hanf system $R$ (a ternary relation whose first projection is a set), there exists a cardinal $m$ serving as a "threshold": if some $\Gamma$ witnesses the relation with a parameter $n \geq m$, then $\Gamma$ can witness the relation with arbitrarily large parameters. The proof constructs $m$ as the successor cardinal of the supremum of local bounds $f_\Gamma$, where each $f_\Gamma$ is either $0$ (if $\Gamma$ already admits arbitrarily large witnesses) or the least upper bound on witnesses for $\Gamma$. The replacement axiom ensures this supremum over a set is well-defined.
