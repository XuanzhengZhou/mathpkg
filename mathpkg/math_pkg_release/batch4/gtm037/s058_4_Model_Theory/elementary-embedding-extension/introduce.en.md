This proposition shows that every elementary embedding can be factored through an elementary extension followed by an isomorphism. Concretely, if $f$ is an elementary embedding of $\mathfrak{A}$ into $\mathfrak{B}$, one can find a structure $\mathfrak{C}$ that is an elementary extension of $\mathfrak{A}$ and an isomorphism $g$ from $\mathfrak{C}$ onto $\mathfrak{B}$ extending $f$. The proof follows the same technique used for Proposition 19.7, constructing $\mathfrak{C}$ by replacing the image of $f$ with an isomorphic copy within the domain of $g$.
