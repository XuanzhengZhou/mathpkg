Theorem 26.41 is a crucial technical result in the Keisler-Shelah proof: under appropriate cardinality conditions, a set of formulas that is finitely satisfiable can be simultaneously realized in an ultraproduct. This bridges the gap between the finite satisfaction used in the consistency definitions and the full realization needed for the isomorphism construction.
