Lemma 26.37 is a key technical step in the inductive construction for the Keisler-Shelah theorem. It asserts that if we have an $m$-consistent triple with an initially empty $G$ and a large $F$ (size exceeding $m$), then we can add any finite set $G$ of functions (with domain $n$ and bounded range) while removing only a small subset $F'$ (of size at most $m$) from $F$. This ensures that as we enrich $G$ during the construction, we lose only a controlled amount from $F$.
