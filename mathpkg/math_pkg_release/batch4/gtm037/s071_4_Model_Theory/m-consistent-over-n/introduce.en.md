Definition 26.33 introduces the technical notion of an $m$-consistent triple $(F, G, D)$ over a cardinal $n$, which is the central combinatorial device in the proof of the Keisler-Shelah isomorphism theorem. The definition ensures that functions in $F$ take on many distinct values and agree with functions in $G$ at a "large" set of places (relative to the filter $D$). Intuitively, $F$ is large, $G$ is small, and members of $D$ are "large" subsets of $n$.
