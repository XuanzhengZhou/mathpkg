This lemma shows that in the Keisler-Shelah construction, the filter $D$ can be extended to decide membership for up to $m$ subsets of $n$, while maintaining $m$-consistency and losing at most $m$ elements from $F$. This property is essential for eventually obtaining an ultrafilter with the desired saturation properties.
