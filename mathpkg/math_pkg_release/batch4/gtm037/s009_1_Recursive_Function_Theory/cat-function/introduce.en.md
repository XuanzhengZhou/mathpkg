This definition introduces the concatenation function $\operatorname{Cat}(x, y)$ that merges two G\"{o}del-encoded sequences $x$ and $y$ into a single code representing their concatenation. The formula shifts the prime indices of $y$'s encoding by $\operatorname{lh}(x)$ positions and multiplies by $x$, effectively appending $y$'s sequence after $x$'s. This operation is essential for building up complex sequence encodings from simpler parts in the arithmetization of Turing machine computations.
