$$f_0 g e = \begin{cases} f_0(2e) & e \ge 0 \\ f_0(-2e - 1) & e < 0 \end{cases} = \begin{cases} 2(e + 1) & e \ge 0 \\ 0 & e = -1 \\ -2e - 3 & e < -1 \end{cases} = g(e + 1);$$

$$f_1 g e = \begin{cases} f_1(2e) & e \ge 0 \\ f_1(-2e - 1) & e < 0 \end{cases} = \begin{cases} 2(e - 1) & e > 0 \\ 1 & e = 0 \\ -2e + 1 & e < 0 \end{cases} = g(e - 1).$$

Thus $f_0$ and $f_1$ are elementary, being defined by cases with simple arithmetic operations.
