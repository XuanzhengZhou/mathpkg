A class $K$ of $\mathcal{L}$-structures is elementarily closed if it is closed under elementary equivalence: whenever a structure $\mathfrak{A}$ is elementarily equivalent to some structure $\mathfrak{B}$ in $K$, then $\mathfrak{A}$ itself must belong to $K$. This property is central to the characterization of elementary classes.
