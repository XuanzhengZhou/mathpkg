Theorem 25.28 gives a preservation-theoretic characterization: an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ can be embedded into the $\mathcal{L}$-reduct of some model of $\Gamma$ (over an expanded language $\mathcal{L}'$) if and only if $\mathfrak{A}$ satisfies every universal $\mathcal{L}$-sentence that is a logical consequence of $\Gamma$. This connects the semantic notion of embeddability with the syntactic notion of universal consequence, and is a key tool in the study of universal classes and preservation theorems.
