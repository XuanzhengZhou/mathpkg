Theorem 26.2 establishes a chain of implications among four important properties of classes of $\mathcal{L}$-structures: elementary $\Rightarrow$ projective $\Rightarrow$ closed under ultraproducts $\Rightarrow$ compact. Moreover, if the class $K$ is elementarily closed (closed under elementary equivalence), then all four conditions become equivalent. This theorem, due to Keisler and Shelah, provides a fundamental structural characterization of elementary classes and is a key ingredient in Lindström's theorem characterizing first-order logic.
