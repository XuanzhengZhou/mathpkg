A class $K$ of structures is elementarily closed if whenever a structure is elementarily equivalent to a member of $K$, it is itself in $K$. This condition is essential in the characterization of elementary classes: if $K$ is elementarily closed and compact, then $K$ is an elementary class (Theorem 26.2).
