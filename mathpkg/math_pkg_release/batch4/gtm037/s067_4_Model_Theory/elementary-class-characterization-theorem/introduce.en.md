This theorem establishes the fundamental hierarchy of classes of structures in model theory. Elementary classes are projective, projective classes are closed under ultraproducts, and ultraproduct-closed classes are compact. When $K$ is elementarily closed, all four conditions become equivalent, giving a semantic characterization of elementary classes. This result is foundational for the Keisler-Shelah theorem and related work on characterizing first-order logic.
