Monadic second-order logic extends first-order logic with variables ranging over arbitrary subsets of the universe. This theorem shows that, like weak second-order logic, the validities of monadic second-order logic are not recursively enumerable. The proof uses the fact that $Q^m$ characterizes $(\omega, +, \cdot, 0, s)$ up to isomorphism.
