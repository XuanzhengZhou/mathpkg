This theorem establishes a fundamental limitation of weak second-order logic: the set of logical consequences of the weak second-order theory $Q^w$ (which characterizes $(\omega, +, \cdot, 0, s)$ up to isomorphism) is not elementarily definable. Consequently, the set of valid weak second-order sentences is not recursively enumerable, and no reasonable complete proof theory exists for this logic.
