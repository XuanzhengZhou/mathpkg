The theory $Q^m$ is formed by taking Robinson's $Q$ and adding the monadic second-order induction axiom: if a subset $P_0$ contains $0$ and is closed under the successor function $s$, then $P_0$ contains every element. This axiom expresses the full induction principle using quantification over arbitrary subsets. The categoricity of $Q^m$ -- that $(\omega, +, \cdot, s, 0)$ is its only model up to isomorphism -- demonstrates that monadic second-order logic can uniquely characterize the standard natural numbers, something impossible in first-order logic by the compactness theorem.
