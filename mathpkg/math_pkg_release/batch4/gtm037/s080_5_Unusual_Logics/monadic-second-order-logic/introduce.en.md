Monadic second-order logic strengthens first-order logic by adding variables $P_0, P_1, \ldots$ that range over arbitrary (not necessarily finite) subsets of the universe. This is a proper extension of weak second-order logic. The negative results from weak second-order logic carry over to the monadic case, but require new proofs. The key tool is the theory $Q^m$, which adds to Robinson's $Q$ the full monadic second-order induction axiom. Like $Q^w$ in the weak case, $Q^m$ is categorical -- its only model up to isomorphism is $(\omega, +, \cdot, s, 0)$ -- from which the failure of the upward Lowenheim--Skolem theorem and recursive enumerability of validities follow.
