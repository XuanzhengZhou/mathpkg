$Q^m$ consists of the theory $Q$ together with the full second-order induction axiom $\forall P_0 [P_0 0 \wedge \forall v_0 (P_0 v_0 \rightarrow P_0 s v_0) \rightarrow \forall v_0 P_0 v_0]$. Up to isomorphism, $(\omega, +, \cdot, 0, s)$ is the only model of $Q^m$. The upward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem therefore fails. By the same argument as for Theorem 30.1 (using Tarski's undefinability), the set of validities is not recursively enumerable.
