Theorem 30.4 extends the undefinability result to monadic second-order logic (where variables range over all subsets, not just finite ones). The theory $Q^m$ (arithmetic plus full second-order induction) is categorical: its only model is the standard natural numbers. This yields the failure of both compactness and the upward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem, and the non-recursive-enumerability of validities.
