Theorem 30.1 establishes a fundamental limitation of weak second-order logic: the set of Godel numbers of sentences valid in the unique model $Q^w$ is not elementarily definable in standard arithmetic. The proof uses the categoricity of $Q^w$ together with Tarski's undefinability theorem. Since $Q^w \models \psi$ iff $\models \bigwedge Q^w \rightarrow \psi$, the set of all weak second-order validities is also not elementarily definable, and by the earlier result 14.16, it is not recursively enumerable. This means there is no complete, effective proof theory for weak second-order logic.
