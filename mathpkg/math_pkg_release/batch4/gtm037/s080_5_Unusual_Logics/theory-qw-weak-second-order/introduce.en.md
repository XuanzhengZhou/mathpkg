The theory $Q^w$ is formulated in the language $\mathcal{L}_{\text{nos}}$ of number theory by adding a single weak second-order sentence to the theory $Q$ (Robinson arithmetic). The additional sentence asserts that every element belongs to some finite set closed under the predecessor operation. Remarkably, this single weak second-order axiom is sufficient to characterize the standard model $(\omega, +, \cdot, s, 0)$ up to isomorphism, making $Q^w$ categorical. This categoricity result is the key to proving that many desirable properties of first-order logic fail in weak second-order logic.
