In \textbf{second-order logic} (full) we augment a first-order language with variables ranging over relations of arbitrary arities on the universe. Formation rules and satisfaction and truth rules extend naturally. For simplicity we may delete variables ranging over operations, retaining only those which range over relations (plus all of the usual first-order set-up). This is not an essential restriction, since $m$-ary operations can be considered as special $(m+1)$-ary relations.

The negative results concerning monadic second-order logic extend automatically to full second-order logic. However, there is a reasonable proof theory with respect to a weakened form of validity.
