Full second-order logic extends first-order logic with variables ranging over relations of arbitrary arities on the universe. This is the most natural and powerful formulation, subsuming both weak and monadic second-order logic. Operations can be treated as special relations, so restricting to relation variables is not an essential limitation. The negative results (failure of compactness, upward Lowenheim--Skolem, recursive enumerability) all extend to this setting. However, unlike the weaker variants, full second-order logic admits a reasonable proof theory with respect to a suitably weakened notion of validity (Henkin semantics), explored further in the theory of types.
