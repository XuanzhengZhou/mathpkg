Lemma 26.20 states that for two denumerable $\mathcal{L}$-structures, the existence of an $\omega$-sequence (an infinite Ehrenfeucht–Fraïssé back-and-forth system) implies that the two structures are isomorphic. This result bridges the game-theoretic notion of an $\omega$-sequence with the structural notion of isomorphism: if player II can maintain a winning strategy through all finite stages, then the countable structures are actually the same up to renaming. The proof constructs an explicit isomorphism by enumerating the elements of both structures and using the back-and-forth property of the $\omega$-sequence to match them.
