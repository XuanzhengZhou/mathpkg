If a class $A$ of number-theoretic functions is closed under primitive recursive operations (in particular, under composition and primitive recursion), then $A$ is also closed under the summation operation $\sum f$, defined as the bounded sum $g(x_0, \ldots, x_{m-2}, z) = \sum_{y < z} f(x_0, \ldots, x_{m-2}, y)$. This is a key closure property used to show that many arithmetically defined functions remain within the class of primitive recursive functions.
