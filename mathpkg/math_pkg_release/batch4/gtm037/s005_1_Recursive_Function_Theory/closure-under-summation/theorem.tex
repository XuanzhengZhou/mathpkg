Let $A$ be a class of functions closed under primitive recursive operations. If $f \in A$ is $m$-ary, then the summation $g = \sum f$ defined by

$$g(x_0, \ldots, x_{m-2}, z) = \sum_{y < z} f(x_0, \ldots, x_{m-2}, y)$$

is also in $A$. In particular, the class of primitive recursive functions is closed under bounded summation.
