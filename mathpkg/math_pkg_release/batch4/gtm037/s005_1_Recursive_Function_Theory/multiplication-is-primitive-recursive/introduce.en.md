Multiplication of natural numbers is primitive recursive. It is defined by primitive recursion with the constant-zero function $C_0^1$ as the base case, and the recursive step adds the multiplicand $x$ to the previously computed product $x \cdot y$. This uses the already-established primitive recursiveness of addition and the closure of primitive recursive functions under composition.
