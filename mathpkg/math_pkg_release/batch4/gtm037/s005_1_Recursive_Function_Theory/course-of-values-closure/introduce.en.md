Proposition 2.32 establishes that within any class $A$ closed under primitive recursive operations, a function $f$ belongs to $A$ precisely when its course-of-values function $\tilde{f}$ belongs to $A$. This equivalence is important because it allows one to replace course-of-values recursion (which refers to all previous values of a function) with ordinary primitive recursion on the coded sequence $\tilde{f}$, thereby showing that course-of-values recursion does not lead out of the class of primitive recursive functions.
