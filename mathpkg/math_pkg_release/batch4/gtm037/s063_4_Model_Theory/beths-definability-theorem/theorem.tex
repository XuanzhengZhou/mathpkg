Let $\Gamma$ be a theory in $\mathcal{L}$, and assume that $\pi$ is a nonlogical constant of $\mathcal{L}$ such that

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] if $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are two models of $\Gamma$ such that $A = B$ and $\sigma^{\mathfrak{A}} = \sigma^{\mathfrak{B}}$ for any nonlogical constant $\sigma \neq \pi$, then $\pi^{\mathfrak{A}} = \pi^{\mathfrak{B}}$.
\end{enumerate}

Then $\pi$ can be defined in terms of the other nonlogical constants, i.e.,

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(ii)] if $\mathcal{L}'$ is the reduct of $\mathcal{L}$ obtained by deleting $\pi$, then there is a theory $\Delta$ in $\mathcal{L}'$ such that $(\Gamma, \mathcal{L})$ is a definitional expansion of $(\Delta, \mathcal{L}')$.
\end{enumerate}
