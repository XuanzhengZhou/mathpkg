Lemma 22.7 is a combinatorial extension lemma used in the proof of Reznikoff's theorem. Given an independent set $\Delta$ and a set $\Gamma$ of no larger cardinality whose consequences are contained in those of $\Delta$, one can extend $\Delta$ to a larger independent set $\Omega$ that still captures all consequences of $\Gamma$. This lemma allows the inductive construction of independent axiomatizations for theories of arbitrary cardinality.
