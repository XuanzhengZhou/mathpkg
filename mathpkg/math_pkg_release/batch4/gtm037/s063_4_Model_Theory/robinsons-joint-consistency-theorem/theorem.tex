Let $\mathcal{L}_0$, $\mathcal{L}_1$, $\mathcal{L}_2$ be first-order languages such that $\mathcal{L}_0 = \mathcal{L}_1 \cap \mathcal{L}_2$. Let $\Gamma_0$ be a complete theory in $\mathcal{L}_0$, and let $\Gamma_1$, $\Gamma_2$ be consistent theories in $\mathcal{L}_1$, $\mathcal{L}_2$ respectively such that $\Gamma_0 \subseteq \Gamma_1$ and $\Gamma_0 \subseteq \Gamma_2$. Then $\Gamma_1 \cup \Gamma_2$ is consistent.
