Craig's Interpolation Theorem states that if a sentence $\varphi$ logically implies $\psi$, then there exists an "interpolant" sentence $\chi$ whose nonlogical constants are common to both $\varphi$ and $\psi$, and such that $\varphi \rightarrow \chi$ and $\chi \rightarrow \psi$ are both logically valid. This theorem is fundamental in model theory and underlies results on definability, conservative extensions, and the elimination of superfluous notions in logical theories.
