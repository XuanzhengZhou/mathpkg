The Feferman-Vaught Theorem is a fundamental result in model theory that reduces the truth of a first-order formula in a generalized product to the truth of an associated formula in the index structure. For every formula $\chi$ in the product language, one can effectively construct a partitioning sequence $(\varphi, \psi_0, \ldots, \psi_n)$ such that $\chi$ holds in the product exactly when $\varphi$ holds in the index structure, evaluated at the sets of indices where each $\psi_j$ holds. This provides a powerful tool for analyzing the first-order properties of products and leads to many preservation and decidability results.
