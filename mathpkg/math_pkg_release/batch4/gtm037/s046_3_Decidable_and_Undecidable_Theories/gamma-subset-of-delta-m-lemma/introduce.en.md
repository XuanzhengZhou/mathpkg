This lemma establishes that for every index $m$, the constructed set $\Delta_m$ contains the original theory $\Gamma$ as a subset. The proof uses the consistency of the prefix union: given any $\varphi \in \Gamma$, when $\varphi$ is enumerated as some $k(n)$, the consistency of $\Gamma \cup \{g^{+-1}f(m,i) : i \leq n\}$ ensures that the condition for adding $k(n)$ to the range of $f(m,\cdot)$ is met, so $\varphi$ enters $\Delta_m$. Consequently, $\Gamma \subseteq \Theta_m$ as well.
