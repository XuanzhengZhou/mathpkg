Gödel's $\beta$-function encodes a finite sequence $y_0, \ldots, y_{n-1}$ of natural numbers into a single number $x$, allowing retrieval of the $i$-th element via $\beta(x, i) = \operatorname{rm}(\operatorname{Exc} x, 1 + (i + 1)Lx)$. The construction uses the remainder function together with the Exc and $L$ pairing decoders. The $\beta$-function is elementary, and its fundamental property --- that every finite sequence can be so encoded --- is the content of the $\beta$-function lemma.
