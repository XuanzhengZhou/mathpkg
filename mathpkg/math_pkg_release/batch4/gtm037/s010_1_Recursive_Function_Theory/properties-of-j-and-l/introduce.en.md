This theorem establishes the fundamental properties of the $J$-$L$ pairing system. Parts (i)-(iii) show that $J$, $L$, and Exc are elementary functions with simple recurrence relations. Parts (iv)-(v) confirm that Exc and $L$ correctly decode the components of a pair encoded by $J$. Part (vi) asserts that $J$ is injective, making $(J, \text{Exc}, L)$ a complete elementary coding scheme for ordered pairs of natural numbers.
