The first-order theory of the set algebra structure $(\mathbf{S}I, \cup, \cap, \sim, 0, I, F_I)$, where $F_I$ is the collection of all finite subsets of $I$, is decidable. This follows from Theorem 23.12, which provides effective combinatorial conditions characterizing when any formula holds in such a structure.
