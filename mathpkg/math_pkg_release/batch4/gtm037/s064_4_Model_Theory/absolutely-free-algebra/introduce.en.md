The absolutely free $\mathcal{L}$-algebra $\mathfrak{Fr}_\mathcal{L}$ is the term algebra over the language $\mathcal{L}$. Its universe is the set $\mathrm{Trm}_\mathcal{L}$ of all terms, and operations are interpreted syntactically by concatenating the operation symbol with its arguments. This construction is analogous to the "internal" model built from constant terms in the completeness proof (Section 11.12), and it plays a central role in the proof of Birkhoff's HSP theorem, providing a free object in the category of $\mathcal{L}$-structures.
