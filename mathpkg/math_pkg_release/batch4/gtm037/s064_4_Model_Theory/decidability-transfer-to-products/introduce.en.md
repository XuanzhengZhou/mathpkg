If the set of sentences valid in a class $\mathbf{K}$ of structures is decidable, then the set of sentences valid in the class $\mathbf{PK}$ of all products of members of $\mathbf{K}$ is also decidable. The decision procedure reduces the problem to checking a certain sentence in the theory of Boolean algebras, specifically whether it holds in all atomic Boolean algebras. This is a key Feferman–Vaught type result about the effective transfer of decidability under the product operation.
