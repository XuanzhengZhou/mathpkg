This proposition establishes the fundamental correspondence between congruence relations and homomorphisms in algebraic languages. Every congruence relation $R$ on a structure yields a homomorphism onto the quotient $\mathfrak{A}/R$. Conversely, the kernel of any homomorphism is a congruence relation. Moreover, every surjective homomorphism $f: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ induces an isomorphism $\mathfrak{B} \cong \mathfrak{A}/\ker f$, giving a first isomorphism theorem.
