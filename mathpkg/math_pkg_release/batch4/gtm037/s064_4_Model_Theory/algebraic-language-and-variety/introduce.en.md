An algebraic language is a first-order language with no relation symbols, only operation symbols. In such a language, an equation is an atomic equality formula, and an identity is its universal closure. A variety is the model class of a set of identities. Two fundamental operators on classes of structures are introduced: $\mathbf{S}$ (substructures) and $\mathbf{P}$ (products). Note that $\mathbf{PK}$ is always nonempty, even if $\mathbf{K}$ is empty, since the empty product (with $I = 0$) is admitted.
