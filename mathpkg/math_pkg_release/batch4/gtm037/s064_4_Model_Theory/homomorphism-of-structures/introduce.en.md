A homomorphism between $\mathcal{L}$-structures is a function that commutes with all operation symbols and preserves all relation symbols (in the forward direction). This generalizes the algebraic notion of homomorphism to arbitrary first-order structures, including those with relation symbols. Homomorphisms are fundamental to the study of varieties and are closely related to the operator $\mathbf{H}$ (homomorphic images) that, together with $\mathbf{S}$ and $\mathbf{P}$, characterizes varieties via Birkhoff's HSP theorem.
