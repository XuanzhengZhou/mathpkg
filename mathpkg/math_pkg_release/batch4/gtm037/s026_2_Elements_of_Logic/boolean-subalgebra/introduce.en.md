A subalgebra of a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{B}$ is a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ whose universe is a subset of $B$ and whose operations agree with the restriction of the operations of $\mathfrak{B}$. A subuniverse is simply the underlying set of a subalgebra, i.e., a subset containing $0$ and $1$ that is closed under the Boolean operations. Any subuniverse of $\mathfrak{B}$ is the underlying set of some subalgebra of $\mathfrak{B}$.
