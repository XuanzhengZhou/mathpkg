Let $\mathfrak{A} = \langle A, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1 \rangle$ and $\mathfrak{B} = \langle B, +', \cdot', -', 0', 1' \rangle$ be Boolean algebras.

We say that $\mathfrak{A}$ is a **subalgebra** of $\mathfrak{B}$ if $A \subseteq B$, $0 = 0'$, $1 = 1'$, and for all $x, y \in A$:
$$x + y = x +' y, \quad x \cdot y = x \cdot' y, \quad -x = -' x.$$

For any $X \subseteq B$, $X$ is a **subuniverse** of $\mathfrak{B}$ if $0', 1' \in X$ and $X$ is closed under $+', \cdot', -'$.
