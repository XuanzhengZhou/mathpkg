A Boolean algebra homomorphism is a function between two Boolean algebras that preserves all the Boolean operations and constants. Homomorphisms are the natural structure-preserving maps in the category of Boolean algebras. An isomorphism is a bijective homomorphism; two Boolean algebras are isomorphic if they are structurally identical as Boolean algebras, differing only in the naming of their elements.
