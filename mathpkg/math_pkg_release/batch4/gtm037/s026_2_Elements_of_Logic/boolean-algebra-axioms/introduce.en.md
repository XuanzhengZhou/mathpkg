A Boolean algebra is an algebraic structure abstracting the properties of a field of sets. The operations $+$, $\cdot$, and $-$ correspond to union, intersection, and complementation respectively, while $0$ and $1$ correspond to the empty set and the universal set. Axioms (i)-(v) capture commutativity, associativity, absorption, distributivity, and complementation, forming a complete axiomatization of the equational theory of fields of sets.
