A Boolean algebra is an algebraic structure $\langle A, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1 \rangle$ where $A$ is a nonempty set, $+$ and $\cdot$ are binary operations, $-$ is a unary operation, and $0, 1 \in A$, satisfying the following axioms:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $x + y = y + x$ and $x \cdot y = y \cdot x$;
\item[(ii)] $x + (y + z) = (x + y) + z$ and $x \cdot (y \cdot z) = (x \cdot y) \cdot z$;
\item[(iii)] $x \cdot y + y = y$ and $(x + y) \cdot y = y$;
\item[(iv)] $x \cdot (y + z) = x \cdot y + x \cdot z$ and $x + y \cdot z = (x + y) \cdot (x + z)$;
\item[(v)] $x \cdot -x = 0$ and $x + -x = 1$.
\end{enumerate}
