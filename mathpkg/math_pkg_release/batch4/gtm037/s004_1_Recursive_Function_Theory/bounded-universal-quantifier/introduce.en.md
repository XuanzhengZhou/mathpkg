The bounded universal quantifier preserves $A$-relations when $A$ is closed under elementary recursive operations. Given an $m$-ary $A$-relation $R$, the set of tuples $\langle x_0, \ldots, x_{m-1}\rangle$ for which every $y < x_{m-1}$ satisfies $R(x_0, \ldots, x_{m-2}, y)$ is itself an $A$-relation. This follows from the bounded existential quantifier result by expressing the universal quantifier in terms of the existential quantifier and complementation.
