A relation $R$ is called primitive recursive when its characteristic function $\chi_R$ is a primitive recursive function. This definition extends the notion of primitive recursiveness from functions to relations, providing a uniform way to study the computational complexity of predicates. Together with closure properties of the class of primitive recursive relations under Boolean operations and bounded quantification, this forms the foundation for the study of primitive recursive definability.
