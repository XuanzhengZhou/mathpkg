The exponent function $(a)_i$ extracts the exponent of the $i$-th prime $p_i$ in the prime factorization of a natural number $a$. For $a = 0$ or $a = 1$, the exponent is defined as $0$ for all $i$. For $a > 1$, $(a)_i$ returns the multiplicity of $p_i$ in the prime decomposition. This function is essential for Godel numbering and for encoding sequences of natural numbers as single integers via prime power decomposition.
