Proposition 2.29 gives a constructive characterization of primitive recursive functions: a function is primitive recursive exactly when it can be generated by a finite derivation sequence starting from the basic functions (zero function $\varnothing$ and projections $U_j^n$) and applying composition and the two forms of primitive recursion ($R^m$ and $R^0$). This is analogous to the characterization of elementary recursive functions in Proposition 2.3 and highlights that primitive recursion adds a strictly more powerful generation scheme to the elementary operations.
