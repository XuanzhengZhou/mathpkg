Proposition 20.8 gives a clean nonstandard characterization of boundedness: a real sequence is bounded (in the standard sense) precisely when its nonstandard extension assigns a finite value to every infinite index. This mirrors the limit characterization of Proposition 20.5 and reinforces the principle that standard analytic properties of sequences translate naturally into properties of their values at infinite indices.
