The standard part map $\operatorname{st}$ assigns to each finite nonstandard real $r$ the unique standard real $s$ that is infinitely close to $r$. This map is a fundamental tool in nonstandard analysis, bridging the nonstandard and standard worlds. It allows nonstandard proofs to yield standard results by "rounding off" infinitesimal differences. Every finite nonstandard real has a unique standard part, and each $\simeq$-equivalence class contains at most one standard real.
