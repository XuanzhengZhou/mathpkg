An element $\varepsilon \in {}^*\mathbb{R}$ is called \emph{infinitesimal} if $|\varepsilon| < r$ for every positive standard real $r \in \mathbb{R}^+$. Every infinitesimal is finite. A sum or difference of infinitesimals is infinitesimal, and the product of an infinitesimal and a finite nonstandard real is again infinitesimal.
