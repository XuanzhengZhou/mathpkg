Infinitesimals are nonstandard real numbers whose absolute value is smaller than every positive standard real. They form an ideal in the ring of finite nonstandard reals. Infinitesimals are the key innovation of nonstandard analysis, allowing rigorous arguments that replace the classical $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ formalism. The algebraic closure properties — sums and differences of infinitesimals are infinitesimal, and products with finite elements remain infinitesimal — make them a well-behaved class within $^*\mathbb{R}$.
