Proposition 20.10 gives a mixed standard/nonstandard proof of the Intermediate Value Theorem. The proof defines $c$ as the supremum of points where $f$ is negative, then uses a positive infinitesimal $i$ to probe both sides of $c$ in the nonstandard extension. The nonstandard values $^*f(c - i)$ and $^*f(c + i)$ (or values arbitrarily close) force $f(c) = 0$ via the continuity of $f$ and the properties of the infimum.
