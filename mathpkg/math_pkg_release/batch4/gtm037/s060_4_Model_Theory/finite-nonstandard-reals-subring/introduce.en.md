The set ${}^t\mathbb{R}$ of finite nonstandard reals is closed under addition, subtraction, and multiplication, forming a subring of $^*\mathbb{R}$. The infinitesimals form an ideal within this ring. This algebraic structure is fundamental to nonstandard analysis: the quotient ${}^t\mathbb{R} / \{\text{infinitesimals}\}$ is isomorphic to the standard real numbers $\mathbb{R}$, with the standard part map serving as the quotient homomorphism.
