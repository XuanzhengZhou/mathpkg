This proposition translates the characterization of tautologies from Lemma 10.20 into an explicit recursive (in fact, $\Sigma_1$) formula in the language of arithmetic via Godel numbering. The key idea is that $n$ encodes the set of subformulas of the formula numbered by $m$, and $f$ encodes a candidate valuation on those subformulas. The bounded existential and universal quantifiers make the overall definition recursive, showing that the property of being a tautology is decidable by an effective procedure, despite the fact that the naive definition involves a universal quantifier over all truth assignments.
