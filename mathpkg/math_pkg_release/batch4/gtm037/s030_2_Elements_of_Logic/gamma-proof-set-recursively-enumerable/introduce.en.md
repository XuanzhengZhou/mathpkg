This proposition is the recursively enumerable analogue of Proposition 10.27. If the set of hypotheses $\Gamma$ is only recursively enumerable rather than recursive, the set of $\Gamma$-proofs remains recursively enumerable. This is because the conditions for being a proof involve an existential search over the components of the sequence and over the elements of $\Gamma$; when $\Gamma$ is r.e., the combined existential search over a finite proof sequence and an r.e. set yields another r.e. relation. This result is a direct corollary of the proof of Proposition 10.27.
