This definition extends the Godel numbering from single expressions to finite sequences of expressions. Using standard prime power coding, a sequence $\langle \varphi_0, \ldots, \varphi_{m-1} \rangle$ is encoded by the product of $p_i^{g^{+}(\varphi_i)}$, where $p_i$ is the $i$-th prime and $g^{+}(\varphi_i)$ is the Godel number of the $i$-th expression. This encoding is injective and allows the theory to arithmetize the notion of a formal proof, since a proof is precisely a finite sequence of formulas satisfying certain syntactic conditions.
