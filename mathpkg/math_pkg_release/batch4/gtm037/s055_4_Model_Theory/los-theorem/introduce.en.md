Łoś's Theorem, also known as the Fundamental Theorem of Ultraproducts, is a cornerstone of model theory. It states that a first-order formula holds in an ultraproduct of structures if and only if the set of indices for which the formula holds in the factor structures belongs to the ultrafilter. This theorem provides the essential link between the algebraic construction of ultraproducts and logical truth, enabling compactness arguments, the construction of nonstandard models, and the transfer of first-order properties between structures and their ultrapowers.
