This proposition constructs a regular ultrafilter over the set of finite subsets of an infinite set $I$. The construction uses the family of sets $G_X = \{Y \subseteq I : X \subseteq Y, Y \text{ finite}\}$ for each finite $X \subseteq I$, which has the finite intersection property. The resulting ultrafilter has the key property that every countable intersection of elements from the generating family is empty, making it a regular ultrafilter. This construction is essential for obtaining lower bounds on the cardinality of ultrapowers.
