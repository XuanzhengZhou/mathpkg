This proposition gives a condition under which the reduced product of a family of sets has a fixed finite cardinality. If almost all factor sets (with respect to the filter $F$) have the same finite cardinality $n$, then the reduced product also has cardinality $n$. The proof uses a sentence involving equality only that characterizes structures of size $n$, together with Łoś's Theorem to transfer cardinality information from the factors to the reduced product.
