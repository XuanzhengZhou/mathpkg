This lemma forms the technical core of the proof that any two elementarily equivalent structures have isomorphic ultrapowers. It constructs, via transfinite induction up to $\mathfrak{p} = 2^{|n|}$, a hierarchy of partial isomorphisms $H_\gamma$ and associated filters $D_\gamma$. The induction is organized into three cases: base case ($\gamma = 0$), limit ordinal case, and successor case ($\gamma = \beta + 1$), where the successor case handles two sub-steps to ensure that conditions (3) and (4) about formula preservation are satisfied. At the final stage $\mathfrak{p}$, the filter $D_\mathfrak{p}$ becomes an ultrafilter $E$, and the relation $K$ defined from $H_\mathfrak{p}$ yields a well-defined map between the reduced products that preserves equality.
