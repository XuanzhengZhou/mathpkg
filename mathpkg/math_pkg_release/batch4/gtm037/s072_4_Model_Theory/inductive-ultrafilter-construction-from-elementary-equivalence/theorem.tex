Let $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ be two structures that are elementarily equivalent. For a given natural number $n$, there exists a transfinite inductive construction of relations $H_\gamma$ and filters $D_\gamma$ (for $\gamma \leq \mathfrak{p} = 2^{|n|}$) satisfying conditions (1)--(7) such that:

1. $E = D_\mathfrak{p}$ is an ultrafilter over $n$.
2. $K = \{([a]_E, [b]_E) : a H_\mathfrak{p} b\}$ is a well-defined relation with domain $^n A / E$ and range $^n B / E$.
3. $K$ preserves equality: for all $a, a'$ with $a H_\mathfrak{p} b$ and $a' H_\mathfrak{p} b'$,
   $$[a]_E = [a']_E \iff [b]_E = [b']_E.$$
