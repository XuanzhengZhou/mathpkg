In the $m$-interpretation of a $\mathcal{Q}$-language, the quantifier $\mathbf{Q}v_i\varphi$ is satisfied when the set of witnesses $a \in A$ for which $\varphi$ holds has cardinality at least $m$. When $m = \aleph_0$, this reads "there are infinitely many," making the $\mathcal{Q}$-language resemble weak second-order logic. This interpretation provides a natural framework for studying compactness properties with respect to cardinal bounds.
