Exercise 30.18 uses the previous exercise to show that the two linear orders $(\omega^\omega \cdot \omega \cdot 2, <)$ and $(\omega^\omega \cdot (\omega + \omega^*), <)$ are weak second-order elementarily equivalent, even though only the first is a well-order. This demonstrates that the property of being well-ordered is not definable in weak second-order logic.
