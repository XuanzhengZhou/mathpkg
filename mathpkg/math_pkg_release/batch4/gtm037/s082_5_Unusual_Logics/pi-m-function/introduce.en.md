The cardinal function $\pi m$ is the minimal number of cardinals strictly less than $m$ whose product reaches at least $m$. It satisfies $\aleph_0 \leq \pi m \leq m$ and interacts naturally with cardinal exponentiation and cofinality. This function plays a crucial role in the compactness theorem for $\mathcal{Q}$-languages under the $m$-interpretation, where $\pi m$ determines the degree of compactness.
