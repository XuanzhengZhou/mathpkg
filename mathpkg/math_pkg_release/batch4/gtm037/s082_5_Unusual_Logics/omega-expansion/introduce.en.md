An $\omega$-expansion enriches a first-order language $\mathfrak{L}$ with a distinguished unary relation symbol $\mathbf{N}$ (intended to denote the natural numbers) and a countable sequence of new individual constants $\mathbf{c}_0, \mathbf{c}_1, \ldots$. This expansion serves as the syntactic foundation for $\omega$-logic, allowing formal reasoning about the standard natural numbers within an extended first-order framework.
