Scott's Isomorphism Theorem is a fundamental result in infinitary logic: for any countable structure $\mathfrak{A}$ in a countable language, there exists a single sentence $\psi$ of $L_{\omega_1\omega}$ (the "Scott sentence") that characterizes $\mathfrak{A}$ up to isomorphism among countable structures. The proof constructs a transfinite sequence of formulas $\varphi_a^\alpha$ (the Scott analysis) that capture the back-and-forth equivalence type of each tuple.
