Corollary 30.14 follows from Fuhrken's ultraproduct theorem using the standard ultraproduct proof of compactness. In the $m$-interpretation, any $\mathcal{Q}$-language is $(\aleph_0, \pi m)$-compact: every countable set of $\mathcal{Q}$-sentences such that every finite subset has a model also has a model. For the $(\exp \aleph_0)^+$-interpretation, this yields $(\aleph_0, \aleph_1)$-compactness.
