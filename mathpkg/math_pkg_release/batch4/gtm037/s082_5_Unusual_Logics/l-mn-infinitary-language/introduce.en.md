L_{mn} languages generalize L_{\omega_1\omega} to arbitrary infinite cardinals m and n. They allow conjunctions and disjunctions of size less than m, and simultaneous universal quantification over fewer than n variables. The case m = n = \omega recovers ordinary first-order logic; m = \omega_1, n = \omega gives L_{\omega_1\omega}.
