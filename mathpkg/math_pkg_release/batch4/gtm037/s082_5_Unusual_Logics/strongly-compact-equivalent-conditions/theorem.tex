For any infinite cardinal $m$, the following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $m$ is strongly compact;
\item[(ii)] every $m$-complete, $m$-distributive Boolean algebra has an $m$-complete ultrafilter;
\item[(iii)] in every $m$-complete field of sets, any proper $m$-complete filter extends to an $m$-complete ultrafilter;
\item[(iv)] in the field of all subsets of a set, any proper $m$-complete filter extends to an $m$-complete ultrafilter;
\item[(v)] any $m$-product of $(m, \infty)$-compact Hausdorff spaces is $(m, \infty)$-compact;
\item[(vi)] any product of $(m, \infty)$-compact Hausdorff spaces is $(m, \infty)$-compact.
\end{itemize}
