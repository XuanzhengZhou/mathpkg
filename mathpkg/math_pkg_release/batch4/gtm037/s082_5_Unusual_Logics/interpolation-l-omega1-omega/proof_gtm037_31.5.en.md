The proof is almost identical with that of 22.1, and we omit it. The argument proceeds by considering the set of nonlogical constants occurring in $\varphi$ but not $\psi$, and those in $\psi$ but not $\varphi$. One constructs a consistency family and applies the model existence theorem (Theorem 31.3) to show that if no interpolant exists, then $\varphi \wedge \neg\psi$ would be satisfiable, contradicting $\models \varphi \rightarrow \psi$.
