$\omega$-logic extends first-order logic with a distinguished unary predicate $\mathbf{N}$ (interpreted as the natural numbers) and constant symbols $\mathbf{c}_i$ for each natural number. An $\omega$-structure is one where $\mathbf{N}$ is exactly the set of denotations of the $\mathbf{c}_i$ in their natural order. $\omega$-logic provides a framework for formalizing concepts that refer to the standard natural numbers within a structure.
