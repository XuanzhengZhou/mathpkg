Combining Lemma 31.6 with the fact that strongly compact cardinals are regular (proved via a similar $L_{mm}$-sentence construction), this theorem shows that every strongly compact cardinal is strongly inaccessible. Since the existence of uncountable strongly inaccessible cardinals is not provable in ZFC, it follows that the $(m, \infty)$-compactness of $L_{mm}$ for $m > \aleph_0$ is also not provable.
