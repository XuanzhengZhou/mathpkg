Ulam's Theorem establishes that every measurable cardinal is inaccessible (and in fact much more — it is a strong limit cardinal and regular). This places measurable cardinals firmly in the large cardinal hierarchy, showing they cannot be proved to exist in ZFC alone. The proof uses the $m$-additivity of the measure to show $m$ cannot be singular and cannot satisfy $m \leq 2^n$ for any $n < m$.
