Let $\mathcal{L}'$ be an expansion of $\mathcal{L}$ by adding a new binary relation symbol $R$ and a new ternary relation symbol $S$, and let $\mathcal{L}''$ be an $\omega$-expansion of $\mathcal{L}'$. A translation $*$ is defined from weak second-order formulas to $\mathcal{L}''$ formulas. The translation of $F_i\sigma$ is $SF_{i0}F_{i1}\sigma$, and $(\forall F_i\varphi)^* = \forall F_{i0}\forall F_{i1}(RF_{i0}F_{i1} \rightarrow \varphi^*)$. By induction on formulas, one shows that $\mathfrak{A} \models \varphi$ iff $\mathfrak{A}^* \models \varphi^*$. If $\mathfrak{A}$ is a model of $\Theta$, then $\mathfrak{A}^*$ is a model of the translated theory $\Omega$. Conversely, any $\omega$-model of $\Omega$ is isomorphic to $\mathfrak{A}^*$ for some $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$.
