Let $L$ be an $L_{\omega_1\omega}$-language, and let $L'$ be an expansion of $L$ by adjoining a countable set $C$ of new individual constants. A consistency family for $(L, L')$ is a family $S$ of countable sets of sentences of $L'$ such that for each $\Gamma \in S$, conditions (C0)--(C9) of Chapter 18 hold, as well as:

(C3') If $\bigwedge_{i < \omega} \varphi_i \in \Gamma$, then $\Gamma \cup \{\varphi_i\} \in S$ for each $i < \omega$.

(C4') If $\bigvee_{i < \omega} \varphi_i \in \Gamma$, then $\Gamma \cup \{\varphi_i\} \in S$ for some $i < \omega$.

Additionally, the notation $\varphi^\rightarrow$ must be extended to infinite conjunctions and disjunctions:
$$\left(\bigwedge_{i < \omega} \varphi_i\right)^\rightarrow = \bigvee_{i < \omega} \varphi_i^\rightarrow;$$
$$\left(\bigvee_{i < \omega} \varphi_i\right)^\rightarrow = \bigwedge_{i < \omega} \varphi_i^\rightarrow.$$
