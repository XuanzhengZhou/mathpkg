The proof proceeds by induction on $\varphi$, following the pattern of Lós's theorem. The only nontrivial step is the case $\varphi = \mathbf{Q}v_j\psi$. In the forward direction, if the ultraproduct satisfies $\mathbf{Q}v_j\psi$, then there are at least $m$ witnessing elements. In the reverse direction, if the index set where $\mathfrak{A}_i \models_m \mathbf{Q}v_j\psi$ is in $F$, the key point is to show that the product of at most $m$-sized witnessing sets over a small index set yields at least $m$ elements in the ultraproduct. This uses $|I| < \pi m$ and the definition of $\pi m$ to bound the size of the product of sets each of size $< m$.
