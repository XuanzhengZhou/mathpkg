Theorem 30.11 establishes that any weak second-order theory $\Theta$ over a first-order language $\mathcal{L}$ whose models are all infinite can be represented as an $\omega$-projective class. The proof constructs an expansion $\mathcal{L}'$ with new binary and ternary relation symbols, an $\omega$-expansion $\mathcal{L}''$, and translates second-order formulas $\varphi$ into first-order formulas $\varphi^*$ in $\mathcal{L}''$, reducing weak second-order satisfaction to $\omega$-model satisfaction.
