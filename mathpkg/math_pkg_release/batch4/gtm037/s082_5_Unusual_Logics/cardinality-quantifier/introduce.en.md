The cardinality quantifier $\mathbf{Q}$ enriches first-order logic with the ability to express that there are at least $m$ elements satisfying a formula. When $m = \aleph_0$, $\mathbf{Q}v_i$ means "there are infinitely many $v_i$," and the logic resembles weak second-order logic. For larger $m$, the $Q$-language provides increasingly expressive cardinality bounds, and interesting compactness and definability results emerge depending on $m$.
