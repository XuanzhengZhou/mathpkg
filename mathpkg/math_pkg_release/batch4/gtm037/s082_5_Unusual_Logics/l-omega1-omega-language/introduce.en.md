The language $L_{\omega_1\omega}$ extends ordinary first-order logic by allowing countable conjunctions $\bigwedge_{i\in\omega}\varphi_i$ and countable disjunctions $\bigvee_{i\in\omega}\varphi_i$. This added expressiveness allows natural formalization of concepts like "the natural numbers are characterized" or "Archimedean-ordered field" without circumlocutions. The compactness theorem fails in $L_{\omega_1\omega}$, but much of first-order model theory remains available.
