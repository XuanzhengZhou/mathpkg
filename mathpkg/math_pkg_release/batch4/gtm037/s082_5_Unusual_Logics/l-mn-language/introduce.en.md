The language $L_{mn}$ generalizes $L_{\omega_1\omega}$ by allowing conjunctions and disjunctions of size $< m$ and universal quantification over $< n$ variables simultaneously. This family of infinitary languages, parameterized by two infinite cardinals $m$ and $n$, provides a fine-grained framework for studying the interplay between syntactic complexity (bound on formula size) and model-theoretic properties such as compactness. The case $L_{\omega_1\omega}$ is the special case $m = \omega_1$, $n = \omega$.
