An $\omega$-structure is an $\mathfrak{L}'$-structure in which the distinguished unary relation $\mathbf{N}$ is interpreted as the set of natural numbers $\omega$, and each constant $\mathbf{c}_i$ is interpreted as the corresponding natural number $i$. This ensures the structure faithfully represents the standard natural numbers, making it suitable for $\omega$-logic semantics.
