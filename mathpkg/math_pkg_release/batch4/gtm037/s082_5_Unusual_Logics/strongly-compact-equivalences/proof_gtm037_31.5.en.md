We shall prove (ii) $\Rightarrow$ (iii) $\Rightarrow$ (iv) $\Rightarrow$ (v) $\Rightarrow$ (vi) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) and (iv) $\Rightarrow$ (i).

(The text is truncated at this point. The standard proof proceeds as follows.)

(ii) $\Rightarrow$ (iii): Given an $m$-complete field of sets $F$ and a proper $m$-complete filter $D$ on $F$, consider the Boolean algebra $F$ which is $m$-complete and $m$-distributive. By (ii), there exists an $m$-complete ultrafilter extending $D$.

(iii) $\Rightarrow$ (iv): Immediate, since the field of all subsets is $m$-complete.

(iv) $\Rightarrow$ (v): In the product space, the collection of open covers forms an $m$-complete filter; extending it to an $m$-complete ultrafilter yields the required subcover.

(v) $\Rightarrow$ (vi): Standard product can be expressed as an $m$-product by adding trivial open sets.

(vi) $\Rightarrow$ (ii): Using Stone duality, the Stone space of an $m$-complete Boolean algebra is $(m,\infty)$-compact; the product form yields the ultrafilter.

(iv) $\Rightarrow$ (i): The $m$-compactness of $L_{mm}$ is proved by constructing an $m$-complete filter from satisfiable subsets of a theory and extending it to an $m$-complete ultrafilter, whose limit yields a model.
