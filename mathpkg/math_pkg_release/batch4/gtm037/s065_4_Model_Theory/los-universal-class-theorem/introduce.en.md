Lós's Theorem (Theorem 25.2) characterizes universal classes semantically: $K$ is axiomatizable by universal sentences if and only if $K$ is closed under taking substructures and ultraproducts. This is a prototypical preservation theorem, establishing a precise correspondence between syntactic form (universal sentences) and semantic closure properties.
