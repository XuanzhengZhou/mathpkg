Recall from Chapter 11 (11.14) the notion of a reduct of a structure. For each member of $\mathrm{Up}\,\mathbf{S} K$, it is an ultraproduct $\prod_{i \in I} \mathfrak{B}_i / F$ where each $\mathfrak{B}_i$ is a substructure of some $\mathfrak{A}_i \in K$. Since the ultraproduct construction respects the substructure relation, this ultraproduct is isomorphic to a substructure of the ultraproduct $\prod_{i \in I} \mathfrak{A}_i / F$, which belongs to $\mathrm{Up}\,K$. Hence the inclusion holds. $\square$
