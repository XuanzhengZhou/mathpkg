Łoś's theorem gives a semantic characterization of universal classes: a class of structures is axiomatizable by universal sentences precisely when it is closed under taking substructures and ultraproducts. This is one of the earliest and most important preservation theorems, showing a tight connection between syntactic form (universal quantifier prefix) and semantic closure properties.
