This corollary of Łoś's theorem shows that the class of all substructures of reducts of a first-order axiomatizable class is always a universal class. This has important applications: for example, the class of all semigroups embeddable in groups, the class of all rings which can be ordered, and the class of all rings embeddable in fields are all universal classes.
