The direction $\Rightarrow$ is given by 24.2 and 24.9 (equations are preserved under $H$, $S$, and $P$). Now assume $K = \mathbf{HSP}K$. By 24.11, $K$ is closed under $H$, $S$, and $P$, hence also under $\mathbf{Up}$. Let $\Gamma = \{\sigma = \tau : \sigma = \tau \text{ holds in every member of } K\}$. We claim $K$ is exactly the class of all models of $\Gamma$. Obviously each member of $K$ is a model of $\Gamma$. Now let $A$ be a model of $\Gamma$. By 24.21 it suffices to take any finitely generated substructure $B$ of $A$, say generated by a finite nonempty set $F$, and show $B \in K$.

Choose an assignment $x$ so that $\text{Rng } x = F$. Then for every $b \in B$ there is a term $\sigma$ such that $\sigma^{\mathfrak{B}}x = b$. From the fact that $A$ is a model of $\Gamma$, one shows that $B$ is a homomorphic image of $\mathfrak{Fr}_\mathcal{L}/\equiv_\Gamma$, which by Lemma 24.20 is in $\mathbf{SPK}$. Since $K$ is closed under $H$, $B \in K$, and the proof is complete.
