In the theory of Markov algorithms, a "word" is a finite sequence of symbols from the alphabet $\{0, 1, 2\}$. A word $a$ is said to occur in a word $b$ if $a$ appears as a contiguous substring of $b$; that is, there exist (possibly empty) words $c$ and $d$ such that $b$ is the concatenation $cad$. This basic notion of substring occurrence is fundamental to defining how Markov algorithm rules are matched and applied to an input word.
