The successor function $\sigma(x) = x+1$ is one of the most fundamental number-theoretic functions. In the theory of recursive functions, it serves as one of the initial elementary functions from which the entire class of elementary functions is generated. It corresponds to the successor operation in Peano arithmetic and is computable by the simplest of Turing machines.
