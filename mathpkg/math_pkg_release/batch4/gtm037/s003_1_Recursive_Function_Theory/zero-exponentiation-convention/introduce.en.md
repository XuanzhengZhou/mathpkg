These conventions define the values of exponentiation and factorial when zero is involved. Specifically, $0^0$ is defined as $1$, while $0^x=0$ for all positive $x$. The factorial of zero, $0!$, is also defined as $1$. These conventions ensure that exponentiation and factorial are defined total functions on $\omega$, which is necessary for them to be considered elementary recursive functions in the framework of Proposition 2.8.
