The proof of Proposition 2.3 follows from Definition 2.1 and the usual set-theoretic interpretation. If $f$ is elementary, the construction sequence witnessing its elementariness provides the required finite sequence $\langle g_0,\ldots,g_{k-1}\rangle$. Conversely, given such a sequence, induction on $i<k$ shows that each $g_i$ is elementary, so $f=g_{k-1}$ is elementary. The conditions on each $g_i$ (being one of the basic functions 2.1(1)-(5), or obtained by composition, summation, or multiplication) mirror exactly the definition of the class of elementary functions.
