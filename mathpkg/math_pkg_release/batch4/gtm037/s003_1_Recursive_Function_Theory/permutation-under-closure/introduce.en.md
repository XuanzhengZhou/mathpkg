If a class $\mathcal{A}$ of number-theoretic functions is closed under elementary recursive operations, then permuting the arguments of any $m$-ary function $f\in\mathcal{A}$ yields another function in $\mathcal{A}$. The proof is simple: the permuted function is expressed as a composition of $f$ with appropriate projection functions, and closure under composition ensures the result remains in $\mathcal{A}$.
