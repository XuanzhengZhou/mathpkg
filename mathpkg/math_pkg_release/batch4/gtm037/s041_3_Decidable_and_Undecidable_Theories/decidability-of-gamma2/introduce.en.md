Theorem 13.9 establishes that the theory $\Gamma_2$, which is Presburger arithmetic (the first-order theory of the integers with addition, order, and congruence relations), is decidable. By the quantifier elimination result of Lemma 13.8, every sentence of $\Gamma_2$ can be effectively reduced to a Boolean combination of basic sentences. Since the truth of basic sentences (equalities $m = n$, inequalities $m < n$, and congruences $m \equiv_n p$ of constant terms) can be decided by elementary arithmetic, there is an effective decision procedure for the entire theory.
