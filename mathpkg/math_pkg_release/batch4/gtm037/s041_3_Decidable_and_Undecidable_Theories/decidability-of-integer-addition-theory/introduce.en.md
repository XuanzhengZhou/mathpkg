Theorem 13.10 states that the first-order theory of the integers with only the addition operation $(\mathbb{Z}, +)$ is decidable. This is a core result in mathematical logic, establishing that the additive structure of the integers, unlike the full structure with both addition and multiplication (which is undecidable by Gödel's incompleteness theorems), admits an effective decision procedure. The result follows from the decidability of Presburger arithmetic $\Gamma_2$, which includes addition along with order and congruence relations.
