Let $\Gamma$ be a strong theory in a language $\mathcal{L}$. Assume that $\Pr$ is a formula of $\mathcal{L}$ such that $Fv \Pr \subseteq \{v_0\}$ and the following conditions hold for all sentences $\psi, \chi$ of $\mathcal{L}$:

(i) if $\Gamma \models \psi$, then $\Gamma \models \Pr(\Delta g^+ \psi)$;
(ii) $\Gamma \models \Pr(\Delta g^+ \psi) \rightarrow \Pr(\Delta g^+ \Pr(\Delta g^+ \psi))$;
(iii) $\Gamma \models \Pr(\Delta g^+ (\chi \rightarrow \psi)) \rightarrow (\Pr(\Delta g^+ \chi) \rightarrow \Pr(\Delta g^+ \psi))$.

Furthermore, let $\varphi$ be a sentence of $\mathcal{L}$ such that $\Gamma \models \Pr(\Delta g^+ \varphi) \rightarrow \varphi$. Then $\Gamma \models \varphi$.
