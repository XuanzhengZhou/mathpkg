The proof proceeds by straightforward induction on the construction of the operation symbol $\mathbf{O}$, following the recursive clauses of Definition 17.4.

**Base cases:** For the primitive symbols $0$, $s$, $+$, $\cdot$, $|-|$, $[/]$, the result follows directly from the axioms (i)-(viii) of $\mathcal{P}$ together with the induction schema.

**Induction step for composition $C_m^n$:** Suppose $\mathbf{O} = C_m^n(\mathbf{Q}; \mathbf{P}_0, \ldots, \mathbf{P}_{m-1})$ and the result holds for $\mathbf{Q}$ and all $\mathbf{P}_i$. Then $\#\mathbf{O} = K_m^n(\#\mathbf{Q}; \#\mathbf{P}_0, \ldots, \#\mathbf{P}_{n-1})$ by definition. Given $\#\mathbf{O} x_0 \cdots x_{m-1} = y$, the definition of Kleene's $K_m^n$ yields intermediate values such that $\#\mathbf{P}_i x_0 \cdots x_{m-1} = z_i$ for $i < n$, and $\#\mathbf{Q} z_0 \cdots z_{n-1} = y$. By the induction hypothesis, $\mathcal{P}$ proves $\mathbf{P}_i(\Delta x_0, \ldots, \Delta x_{m-1}) = \Delta z_i$ and $\mathbf{Q}(\Delta z_0, \ldots, \Delta z_{n-1}) = \Delta y$. The defining axiom for $C_m^n$ then yields $\mathcal{P} \models \mathbf{O}(\Delta x_0, \ldots, \Delta x_{m-1}) = \Delta y$.

**Induction step for $\Sigma$ and $\Pi$:** Similar arguments using the defining axioms for summation and product operators, together with the induction schema of $\mathcal{P}$.

**Induction step for $R_{\mathbf{O}}$:** Handled analogously, noting that the defining axiom (xv) translates between the relation symbol and the corresponding operation symbol evaluated to $1$.

Since every operation symbol of $\mathcal{L}_{\text{el}}$ is generated by these constructions, the induction is complete.
