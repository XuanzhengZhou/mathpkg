Theorem 16.56 establishes that the theory of two equivalence relations over the universe is finitely inseparable from the theory of a single symmetric binary relation. The proof proceeds by interpreting the symmetric binary relation into the theory of two equivalence relations. Given a finite model $\mathfrak{A}$ of $\Gamma'$ (with two equivalence relations $\mathbf{L}^{\mathfrak{A}}$ and $\mathbf{M}^{\mathfrak{A}}$), one constructs a finite model $\mathfrak{B}$ of the theory of symmetric binary relations by using choice functions on equivalence classes to define new unary operation symbols, yielding the desired finite inseparability.
