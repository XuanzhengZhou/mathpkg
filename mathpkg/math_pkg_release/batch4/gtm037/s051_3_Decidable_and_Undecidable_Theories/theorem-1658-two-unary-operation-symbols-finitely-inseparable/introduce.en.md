Theorem 16.58 completes the classification of languages by finite inseparability of their logically valid sentences. Together with Theorems 16.52 and 16.54, it establishes three sufficient conditions for the set of validities to be finitely inseparable: the language has a relation symbol of rank at least 2, an operation symbol of rank at least 2, or at least two unary operation symbols. If none of these conditions hold---meaning the language has only unary relation symbols and at most one unary operation symbol---then the set of logically valid sentences is decidable. This theorem thus characterizes the boundary between decidability and undecidability for pure first-order languages.
