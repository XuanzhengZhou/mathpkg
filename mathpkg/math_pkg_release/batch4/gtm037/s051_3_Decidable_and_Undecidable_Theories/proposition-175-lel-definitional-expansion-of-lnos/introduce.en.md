Proposition 17.5 asserts that the pair $(\mathcal{P}, \mathcal{L}_{\text{el}})$ constructed in Definition 17.4 is a definitional expansion of $(\mathcal{P}, \mathcal{L}_{\text{nos}})$, the base theory in the language of number theory. This means that every new symbol introduced in $\mathcal{L}_{\text{el}}$--the absolute difference operation, integer division, and the relation symbols $R_{\mathbf{O}}$--can be eliminated via explicit definitions without changing the set of provable sentences in the original language. The proof proceeds by showing that $(\mathcal{P}, \mathcal{L}_{\text{el}})$ is obtained as the union of a chain of definitional expansions $(\mathcal{P}, \mathcal{L}_{\text{nos}}) = (\mathcal{S}_0, \mathcal{L}_0), (\mathcal{S}_1, \mathcal{L}_1), \dots$, where at each step a single new symbol is introduced with a defining axiom satisfying the existence and uniqueness conditions required for definitional expansions.
