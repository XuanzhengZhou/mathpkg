Theorem 16.65 states that the first-order theory of a single binary relation axiomatized as a partial ordering is finitely inseparable, and consequently undecidable. This result is striking because a partial ordering is one of the simplest relational structures studied in mathematics--requiring only reflexivity, antisymmetry, and transitivity--yet its theory already lies on the undecidable side of the boundary. The theorem illustrates how quickly decidability can be lost even with minimal axiomatic requirements.
