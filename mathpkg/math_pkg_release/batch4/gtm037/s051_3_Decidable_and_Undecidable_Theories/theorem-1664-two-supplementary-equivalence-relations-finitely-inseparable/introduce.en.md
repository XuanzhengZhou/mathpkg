Theorem 16.64 concerns the theory of a structure equipped with two equivalence relations $E$ and $F$ that are supplementary: for any two elements $x$ and $y$, there exists an element $z$ such that $x E z F y$. This supplementary condition creates a rich combinatorial structure that renders the first-order theory finitely inseparable and therefore undecidable. The result is part of Monk's systematic investigation into which minimal relational/functional structures generate theories that are undecidable or finitely inseparable.
