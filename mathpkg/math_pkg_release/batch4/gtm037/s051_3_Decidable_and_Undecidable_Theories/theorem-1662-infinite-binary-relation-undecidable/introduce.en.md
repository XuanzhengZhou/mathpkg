Theorem 16.62 asserts that the first-order theory of a single binary relation on an infinite domain is undecidable. This result illustrates how even a relativley simple relational structure can generate a theory whose set of logical consequences is not computably enumerable. The theorem fits into the broader investigation in Part 3 of Monk's book, which systematically analyzes which languages and theories are decidable and which are undecidable or finitely inseparable.
