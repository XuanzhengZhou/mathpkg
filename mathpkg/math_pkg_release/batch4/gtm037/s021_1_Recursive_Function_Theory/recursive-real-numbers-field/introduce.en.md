The recursive real numbers --- those real numbers possessing a computable Cauchy sequence representation --- form a countable subfield $F$ of $\mathbb{R}$. All rational numbers are recursive, and $F$ is closed under the field operations. This provides the foundation for computable analysis, where one studies the effective content of classical real analysis.
