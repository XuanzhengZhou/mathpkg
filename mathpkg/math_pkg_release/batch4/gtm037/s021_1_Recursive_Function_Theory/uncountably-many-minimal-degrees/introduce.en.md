The poset $(D, \leq)$ of Turing degrees contains $2^{\aleph_0}$ distinct minimal degrees. This theorem demonstrates that the structure of degrees directly above $0$ is already extremely rich, having the cardinality of the continuum. The construction of continuum many minimal degrees is a classic application of forcing or priority methods in computability theory.
