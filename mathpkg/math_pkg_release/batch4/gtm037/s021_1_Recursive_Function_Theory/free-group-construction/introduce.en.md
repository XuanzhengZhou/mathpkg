The free group $F_X$ on a set $X$ is constructed by taking finite words over the alphabet $X \cup X'$ (where $X'$ provides formal inverses) and quotienting by the congruence generated by the relations $aa' \equiv 0$ and $a'a \equiv 0$. Under this construction $F_X$ becomes a group, providing the framework for the word problem for groups.
