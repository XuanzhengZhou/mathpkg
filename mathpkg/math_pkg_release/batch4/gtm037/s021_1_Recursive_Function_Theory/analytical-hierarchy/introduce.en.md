The analytical hierarchy classifies second-order definable relations according to the complexity of their defining formulas. The $\Sigma_m^1$ relations are defined by an existential function quantifier followed by $m-1$ alternating function quantifiers and a final number quantifier, all in front of a recursive matrix. The theory of this hierarchy shows considerable similarity to the classical topological theory of analytic sets.
