Let $A$ be any set, $f: P \to A$, $g: A \to A$, and $h: A \times A \to A$. Then there is a unique function $\overline{F}: \text{Sent}_{\mathcal{P}} \to A$ such that $\overline{F}(\langle s \rangle) = f(s)$ for $s \in P$, $\overline{F}(\neg \varphi) = g(\overline{F}(\varphi))$, and $\overline{F}(\varphi \rightarrow \psi) = h(\overline{F}(\varphi), \overline{F}(\psi))$ for all sentences $\varphi, \psi$.
