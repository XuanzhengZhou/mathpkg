A Diophantine relation is one definable by an existential polynomial equation over the natural numbers: $R(\vec{x})$ holds iff there exist parameters $\vec{y}$ making $P(\vec{x}, \vec{y}) = 0$. The MRDP theorem establishes that the Diophantine relations are exactly the recursively enumerable relations, providing a purely number-theoretic characterization of computability.
