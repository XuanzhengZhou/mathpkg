The set of all functions from $\omega$ to $\omega$ admits an equivalence relation defined by relative computability: two functions are equivalent if each is Turing-reducible to the other. The resulting equivalence classes are called Turing degrees of unsolvability. These degrees form a partially ordered set $D$ under the relation $\leq$ induced by relative recursiveness, with the degree of recursive functions (denoted $0$) as the least element. The structure $(D, \leq)$ has cardinality $2^{\aleph_0}$ and constitutes the central object of study in degree theory, a branch of computability theory concerned with classifying problems by their computational difficulty.
