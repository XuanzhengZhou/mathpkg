ISOL is closed under $+$ and $\cdot$. For any $\alpha, \beta, \gamma \in \text{ISOL}$ we have:
(i) $\alpha + \beta = \alpha + \gamma$ implies $\beta = \gamma$;
(ii) $\alpha \cdot \beta = \alpha \cdot \gamma$ and $\alpha \neq 0$ imply $\beta = \gamma$;
(iii) $(\omega, +, \cdot)$ is a substructure of $(\text{ISOL}, +, \cdot)$.
