Corollary 5.11 establishes the existence of a universal Turing machine $M$ that can simulate any other Turing machine. Given the Gödel number $e$ of a Turing machine $N$ and an input $x$, $M$ halts precisely when $N$ halts on $x$, and produces the same output. This universality phenomenon is a direct consequence of the Normal Form Theorem and is a cornerstone of computability theory.
