The Iteration Theorem (also known as the s-m-n theorem) is a fundamental result in recursion theory. For any $m,n \geq 1$, there exists an $(m+1)$-ary recursive function $s_n^m$ that uniformly transforms the index $e$ of an $(m+n)$-ary partial recursive function, given fixed parameters $y_1,\ldots,y_m$, into the index of the resulting $n$-ary function. This theorem underpins most deeper results in recursion theory, including the recursion theorem and Rice's theorem.
