This lemma shows that the class of Turing computable functions is closed under the operation of composition. Given Turing machines $M, N_0, \ldots, N_{m-1}$ that compute the functions $f, g_0, \ldots, g_{m-1}$ respectively, one can construct a composite Turing machine that first evaluates each $g_i$ on the input, then feeds the results into $f$. The explicit machine diagram is given in the proof.
