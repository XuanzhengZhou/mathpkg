The union of a directed set of $L$-structures is a natural construction in model theory. When $K$ is a set of $L$-structures directed by the substructure relation $\subseteq$, the union $\bigcup K$ forms a well-defined $L$-structure. Operations on tuples are evaluated using any member structure that contains all the arguments, and relations are simply the union of the corresponding relations across all members of $K$. This definition is fundamental for Tarski's elementary chain theorem and for constructing elementary extensions via unions of chains.
