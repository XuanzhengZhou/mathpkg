Proposition 19.35 states a basic property of the union of a directed set of $L$-structures: each member structure is a substructure of the union. This follows directly from the definition of $\bigcup K$. When $K$ is directed by $\subseteq$, the operations and relations of any $\mathcal{A} \in K$ are coherently embedded into $\bigcup K$, making $\mathcal{A}$ a substructure.
