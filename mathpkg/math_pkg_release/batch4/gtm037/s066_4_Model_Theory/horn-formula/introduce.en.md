Definition 25.12 introduces Horn formulas, which are the syntactic building blocks for quasivarieties. A Horn formula is either an atomic formula, a simple implication (a conjunction of atomic formulas implying a single atomic formula), or the negation of a conjunction of atomic formulas. Horn formulas generalize equations and are used to axiomatize quasivarieties, which are classes of structures closed under substructures, direct products, and ultraproducts. The three forms correspond to the three types of clauses in a universally quantified Horn sentence.
