Lemma 25.21 is a technical lemma used in the proof of Lyndon's theorem on positive sentences (Theorem 25.22). It states that if a structure $\mathfrak{B}$ satisfies all positive consequences of a theory $\Gamma$, then $\Gamma$ has a model $\mathfrak{A}$ that can be positively embedded into $\mathfrak{B}$ (denoted $\mathfrak{A} \preceq_{\mathrm{pos}} \mathfrak{B}$). The proof is a compactness argument: one considers $\Gamma$ together with the negations of all positive sentences whose negations are consequences of $\Gamma$, and shows this set is consistent.
