For any $x \in \mathfrak{P}_{i \in I} (\mathfrak{C}_i \upharpoonright \mathbf{U})$, let $fx = [x]$, the equivalence class of $x$ under $F$ in the structure $\mathfrak{P}_{i \in I} \mathfrak{C}_i / F$. It is easy to see that $f$ induces the desired isomorphism. The induced map is, in fact, only in a subtle way different from the identity map.
