Proposition 25.7 establishes a commutativity relationship between taking ultraproducts and taking reducts of structures. Specifically, if each structure $\mathfrak{C}_i$ satisfies the $\mathcal{L}$-closure conditions for a set $\mathbf{U}$ of function symbols, then the ultraproduct of the reducts is canonically isomorphic to the reduct of the ultraproduct. The isomorphism is induced by the natural map sending an equivalence class $[x]$ in the ultraproduct of reducts to its equivalence class in the reduct of the full ultraproduct. This result is a technical lemma used in the study of universal classes and closure conditions in model theory.
