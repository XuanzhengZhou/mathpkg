This is a direct corollary of Theorem 25.2 (Łoś's theorem on universal classes), which states that a class $K$ is universal if and only if $K$ is closed under substructures and ultraproducts, and $K$ is axiomatizable. The condition $SK = K$ captures closure under substructures and isomorphisms, while $K = \mathrm{Mod}\,\Gamma$ expresses axiomatizability. See the proof of Theorem 25.2 for the full derivation.
