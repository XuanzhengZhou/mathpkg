Theorem 25.22 is Lyndon's preservation theorem for positive sentences, analogous to the Łoś--Tarski theorem for universal sentences and the Chang--Łoś--Suszko theorem for universal-existential sentences. It characterizes the model classes axiomatizable by positive sentences (formulas built from atomic formulas using $\wedge$, $\vee$, $\forall$, $\exists$, but without negation) as precisely those classes closed under homomorphic images. The trivial direction (i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) is proved by induction on positive formulas, showing that if $\mathfrak{A} \twoheadrightarrow \mathfrak{B}$ via a surjective homomorphism, then satisfaction of positive formulas is preserved from $\mathfrak{A}$ to $\mathfrak{B}$. The converse uses Lemma 25.21 and a diagram argument.
