Theorem 25.16, due to Chang and Łoś--Suszko, characterizes the model classes that can be axiomatized by universal-existential sentences. It establishes the equivalence of three conditions: (i) the class $\mathrm{Mod}\,\Gamma$ is characterized by a set of $\forall\exists$-sentences; (ii) the class is closed under unions of directed subsets (with respect to the substructure relation); and (iii) the class is closed under unions of chains of type $\omega$. The proof of (i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) uses directedness to find witnesses for the existential quantifiers, while (iii) $\Rightarrow$ (i) constructs an elementary chain alternating between models of $\Gamma$ and structures satisfying the universal-existential consequences.
