Definition 25.15 introduces universal-existential formulas, which have a quantifier prefix consisting of a block of universal quantifiers followed by a block of existential quantifiers, applied to a quantifier-free formula $\psi$. Every universal sentence is trivially universal-existential (with $n=0$ existential quantifiers). Unlike universal classes, classes characterized by universal-existential sentences are not generally closed under substructures. The Chang-Łoś-Suszko theorem (Theorem 25.16) characterizes such classes in terms of closure under unions of directed subsets or chains.
