Theorem 15.18 connects the notion of implicit definability from Chapter 14 with undecidability. It states that if a theory in the language of number theory is rich enough to weakly syntactically define every recursive set, then the theory cannot be decidable. The proof is a classic diagonal argument: assuming decidability, one constructs a recursive set $A$ of Gödel numbers of formulas that do not prove their own diagonalization, then uses a weak syntactic definition of $A$ to derive a contradiction.
