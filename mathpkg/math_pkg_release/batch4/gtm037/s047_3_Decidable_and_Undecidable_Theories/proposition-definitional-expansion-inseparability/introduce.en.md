This proposition establishes that definitional expansions preserve several key logical properties between a theory $\Gamma$ and its definitional expansion $\Gamma'$. Specifically, inseparability, essential undecidability (under certain conditions), and finite inseparability are all invariant under definitional expansion. The proofs use the translation between sentences of $\mathcal{L}$ and $\mathcal{L}'$ via the definitional expansion map, together with the theory of effective inseparability from Section 6.
