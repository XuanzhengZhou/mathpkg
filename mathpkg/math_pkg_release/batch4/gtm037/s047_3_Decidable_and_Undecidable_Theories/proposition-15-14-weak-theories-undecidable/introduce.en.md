Proposition 15.14 provides a tool for proving that relatively weak theories are undecidable. If a finitely axiomatizable and essentially undecidable theory $\Gamma$ can be consistently combined with a theory $\Delta$ in an expanded language, then $\Delta$ must be undecidable. The proof constructs an intermediate theory $\Theta$ consisting of logical consequences of $\Gamma \cup \Delta$, which is essentially undecidable (by inheritance from $\Gamma$) and a finite extension of $\Delta$, thus transferring undecidability to $\Delta$.
