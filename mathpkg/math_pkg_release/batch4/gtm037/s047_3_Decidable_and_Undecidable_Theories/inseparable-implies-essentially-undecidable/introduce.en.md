This proposition establishes that every inseparable theory is essentially undecidable. The proof uses the recursive inseparability of the set of Gödel numbers of theorems of $\Gamma$ and the set of Gödel numbers of sentences whose negations are in $\Gamma$. Since any consistent extension $\Delta$ preserves this inseparability property, the set of Gödel numbers of $\Delta$ cannot be recursive, implying $\Delta$ is undecidable. This result provides one of the main pathways for proving essential undecidability.
