Proposition 15.13 shows that undecidability transfers upward through finite extensions: if a finitely extended theory $\Delta$ is undecidable, then the original theory $\Gamma$ is also undecidable. The proof relies on the fact that for a finite set $\Theta$ of sentences axiomatizing $\Delta$ over $\Gamma$, a sentence $\varphi$ belongs to $\Delta$ if and only if the implication $\bigwedge\Theta \to \varphi$ belongs to $\Gamma$, which reduces decidability of $\Delta$ to decidability of $\Gamma$.
