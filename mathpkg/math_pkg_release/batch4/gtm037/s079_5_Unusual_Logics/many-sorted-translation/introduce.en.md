This proposition establishes a natural reduction of many-sorted logic to ordinary (single-sorted) first-order logic. With each many-sorted language $\mathcal{L}$ one associates an ordinary language $\mathcal{L}^*$ that contains the original relation and operation symbols, plus unary predicates $\mathbf{P}_s$ for each sort $s \in \mathcal{S}$ to indicate membership in the corresponding universe. The translation $\varphi \mapsto \varphi^*$ maps many-sorted formulas to ordinary formulas by relativizing quantifiers to the appropriate sort predicates. Every many-sorted structure $\mathfrak{A}$ gives rise to an ordinary $\mathcal{L}^*$-structure $\mathfrak{A}^*$, and satisfaction is preserved under the translation.
