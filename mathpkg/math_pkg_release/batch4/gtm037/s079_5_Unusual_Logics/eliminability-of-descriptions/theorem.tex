Let $(\mathcal{L}, \tau, \mathbf{O})$ be a descriptive triple, $\Gamma$ a set of sentences in $(\mathcal{L}, \tau, \mathbf{O})$, $\sigma$ a term of $(\mathcal{L}, \tau, \mathbf{O})$ and $\varphi$ a formula of $(\mathcal{L}, \tau, \mathbf{O})$. Then

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] if $v_k$ is a variable not occurring in $\sigma$, then there is a formula $\psi$ not involving $\tau$ and with $\mathrm{Fv}\,\psi \subseteq \mathrm{Fv}\,\sigma \cup \{v_k\}$, such that $\Gamma \models \sigma = v_k \leftrightarrow \psi$;
\item[(ii)] there is a formula $\varphi^*$ not involving $\tau$ and with $\mathrm{Fv}\,\varphi^* \subseteq \mathrm{Fv}\,\varphi$ such that $\Gamma \models \varphi \leftrightarrow \varphi^*$.
\end{enumerate}
