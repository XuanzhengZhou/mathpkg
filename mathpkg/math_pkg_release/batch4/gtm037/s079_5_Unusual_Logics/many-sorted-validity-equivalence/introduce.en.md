This corollary to Proposition 29.28 states that a many-sorted sentence $\varphi$ is universally valid (true in all many-sorted structures) if and only if its translation $\varphi^*$ is a logical consequence of the sort axioms $\Gamma$ in ordinary first-order logic. This reduces the notion of many-sorted validity to ordinary first-order logical consequence, allowing metalogical results about ordinary logic (such as compactness and completeness) to be transferred to the many-sorted setting.
