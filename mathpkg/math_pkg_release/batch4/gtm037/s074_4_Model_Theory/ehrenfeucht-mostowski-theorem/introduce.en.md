The **Ehrenfeucht--Mostowski theorem** is a foundational result in model theory that guarantees the existence of models with large sets of indiscernibles. Given any theory $\Gamma$ with an infinite model and any simple ordering structure $\mathfrak{A}$, the theorem constructs a model $\mathfrak{B}$ of $\Gamma$ such that $\mathfrak{A}$ forms a set of indiscernibles for $\mathfrak{B}$. Moreover, every automorphism of the ordering $\mathfrak{A}$ lifts to an automorphism of the model $\mathfrak{B}$, and the cardinality of $\mathfrak{B}$ is bounded by $|\mathrm{Fmla}_{\mathfrak{B}}| + |A|$. The proof proceeds by a Skolem expansion and an iterated compactness argument, using Ramsey's theorem to isolate an infinite set of indiscernibles from the given ordering.
