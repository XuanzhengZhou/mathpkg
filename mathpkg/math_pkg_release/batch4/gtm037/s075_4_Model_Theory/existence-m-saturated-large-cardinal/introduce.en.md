This theorem addresses the existence of $m$-saturated structures when the cardinal $m$ strictly exceeds the size of the language. In this regime, the cardinal arithmetic condition $n^p \leq n$ from Theorem 28.6 is no longer needed, and the existence of an $m$-saturated model follows under simpler hypotheses. Together with Theorem 28.6, this completes the picture of when $m$-saturated structures exist: the difficult case is when $m$ is small relative to the language size.
