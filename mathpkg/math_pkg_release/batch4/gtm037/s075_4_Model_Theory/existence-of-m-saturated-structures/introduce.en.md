This theorem gives sufficient conditions for the existence of an $m$-saturated model of a theory $\Gamma$. Given three nested languages $\mathcal{L} \subseteq \mathcal{L}' \subseteq \mathcal{L}''$ and cardinals $m, n \geq \aleph_0$ satisfying a cardinal arithmetic condition ($n^p \leq n$ for $p < m$), if $\Gamma'$ is a consistent extension of $\Gamma$ and there are at most $n$ $1$-types over $\Gamma$ in each restricted language $\mathcal{L}_B$, then $\Gamma$ has an $m$-saturated model of cardinality at most $n$. The proof uses the model existence theorem together with a careful construction via admissible functions.
