This lemma (denoted by (*) in the text) provides a construction that is central to an alternative, more natural proof of the existence of saturated models (Corollaries 28.8 and 28.14). Given an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ of size at most $2^m$ where $m \geq |\mathrm{Fmla}_{\mathcal{L}}|$, there exists an elementary extension $\mathfrak{B}$ of size exactly $2^m$ that realizes every $1$-type over any small ($\leq m$) parameter set from $\mathfrak{A}$. The proof applies the compactness theorem by introducing new constants for each pair $(X, \Delta)$; it can also be proved using ultraproducts (Exercise 28.39).
