This proposition collects four elementary properties relating $m$-saturated structures to $n$-saturated structures for smaller cardinals $n$. Part (i) shows that $m$-saturatedness implies $n$-saturatedness for all $n \leq m$. Part (ii) handles the limit cardinal case, where $m$-saturatedness is equivalent to $n$-saturatedness for all $n < m$. Part (iii) characterizes $m$-saturatedness in terms of $0$-saturatedness of expansions by fewer than $m$ constants. Part (iv) states that expanding an $m$-saturated structure by fewer than $m$ constants preserves $m$-saturatedness.
