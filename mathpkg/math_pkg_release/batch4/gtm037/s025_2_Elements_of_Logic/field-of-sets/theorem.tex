A field of sets is a set $\mathcal{A}$ such that $\bigcup \mathcal{A} \in \mathcal{A}$, $\bigcup \mathcal{A} \sim X \in \mathcal{A}$ whenever $X \in \mathcal{A}$, and $X \cup Y \in \mathcal{A}$ whenever $X, Y \in \mathcal{A}$. We also say that $\mathcal{A}$ is a field of subsets of $\bigcup \mathcal{A}$.
