A field of sets is a nonempty collection $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets of its own union that is closed under relative complements and finite unions. This is the concrete prototype of a Boolean algebra: the union of all members belongs to $\mathcal{A}$, the complement of any member (relative to the union) belongs to $\mathcal{A}$, and the union of any two members belongs to $\mathcal{A}$. Fields of sets motivate the axiomatic definition of Boolean algebras.
