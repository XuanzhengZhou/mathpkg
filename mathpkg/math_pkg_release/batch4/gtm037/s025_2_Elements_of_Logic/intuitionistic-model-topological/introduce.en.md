An intuitionistic model provides a mathematical (topological) interpretation of intuitionistic sentential logic. Sentences are mapped to open sets of a topological space $X$ via a valuation function $f$. The logical connectives are interpreted as set-theoretic operations: conjunction as intersection, and negation and implication as interiors of appropriate complements and unions, reflecting the intuitionistic requirement that truth be "locally verifiable" via open sets.
