Taking negation, conjunction and implication as primitive, the intuitionistic sentential logic is axiomatized by all sentences of the following kinds:

\begin{align}
&\text{(I1)}\quad \varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \varphi);\\
&\text{(I2)}\quad [\varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \chi)] \rightarrow [(\varphi \rightarrow \psi) \rightarrow (\varphi \rightarrow \chi)];\\
&\text{(I3)}\quad (\varphi \rightarrow \neg \varphi) \rightarrow \neg \varphi;\\
&\text{(I4)}\quad \neg \varphi \rightarrow (\varphi \rightarrow \psi);\\
&\text{(I5)}\quad \varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \varphi \wedge \psi);\\
&\text{(I6)}\quad \varphi \wedge \psi \rightarrow \varphi;\\
&\text{(I7)}\quad \varphi \wedge \psi \rightarrow \psi.
\end{align}

Detachment is the only rule of inference. $\vdash_i \varphi$ is defined analogously to the classical case.
