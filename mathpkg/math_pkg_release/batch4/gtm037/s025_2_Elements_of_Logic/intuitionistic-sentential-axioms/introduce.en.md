The intuitionistic sentential logic is an axiomatic system for propositional reasoning that rejects the law of excluded middle. It takes negation, conjunction, and implication as primitive connectives. The axioms I1 through I7 capture the intuitionistically valid sentence forms, with detachment (modus ponens) as the sole rule of inference. The system is strictly weaker than classical logic; notably, $\neg\neg\varphi \rightarrow \varphi$ is not provable.
