The axioms of modal logic are all sentences of the following kinds:

\begin{align}
&\text{(M1)}\quad \varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \varphi),\\
&\text{(M2)}\quad [\varphi \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \chi)] \rightarrow [(\varphi \rightarrow \psi) \rightarrow (\varphi \rightarrow \chi)],\\
&\text{(M3)}\quad (\neg \varphi \rightarrow \neg \psi) \rightarrow (\psi \rightarrow \varphi),\\
&\text{(M4)}\quad \Box \varphi \rightarrow \varphi,\\
&\text{(M5)}\quad \Box \varphi \rightarrow \Box \Box \varphi,\\
&\text{(M6)}\quad \Box (\varphi \rightarrow \psi) \rightarrow (\Box \varphi \rightarrow \Box \psi).
\end{align}

The rules of inference are detachment (modus ponens) and necessitation: from $\varphi$ infer $\Box \varphi$.
