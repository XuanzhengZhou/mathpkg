A Boolean algebra is an algebraic structure $\langle A, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1 \rangle$ where $A$ is a set, $+$ and $\cdot$ are binary operations, $-$ is a unary operation, and $0$ and $1$ are distinguished elements of $A$, satisfying the following axioms:

\begin{align}
&x + y = y + x, && x \cdot y = y \cdot x;\\
&x + (y + z) = (x + y) + z, && x \cdot (y \cdot z) = (x \cdot y) \cdot z;\\
&x + (x \cdot y) = x, && x \cdot (x + y) = x;\\
&x \cdot (y + z) = (x \cdot y) + (x \cdot z), && x + (y \cdot z) = (x + y) \cdot (x + z);\\
&x + (-x) = 1, && x \cdot (-x) = 0.
\end{align}
