Suppose $X \subseteq \mathbf{P}^{\mathfrak{A}}$ with $|X| < m$, and let $\mathcal{L}'$ be an $X$-expansion of $\mathcal{L}$. Assume that $\Delta \subseteq \text{Fmla}^1_{\mathcal{L}'}$ is such that each finite subset of $\Delta$ can be realized in $(\mathfrak{B}, x)_{x \in X}$. For each formula $\varphi$ of $\mathcal{L}'$ let $\varphi^*$ be obtained from $\varphi$ by relativizing all quantifiers to $\mathbf{P}$. Then each finite subset of $\{\varphi^* : \varphi \in \Delta\}$ can be realized in $(\mathfrak{A}, x)_{x \in X}$. Since $\mathfrak{A}$ is $m$-saturated, there exists $a \in A$ realizing $\{\varphi^* : \varphi \in \Delta\}$ in $(\mathfrak{A}, x)_{x \in X}$. Since each $\varphi \in \Delta$ is a formula with one free variable and includes the condition that the realizing element satisfies $\mathbf{P}$, we have $a \in \mathbf{P}^{\mathfrak{A}} = B$, and $a$ realizes $\Delta$ in $(\mathfrak{B}, x)_{x \in X}$. Thus $\mathfrak{B}$ is $m$-saturated. The condition $|B| \geq m$ follows from $|\mathbf{P}^{\mathfrak{A}}| \geq \aleph_0$ and $m$-saturation.
