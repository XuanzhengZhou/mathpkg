Theorem 28.23 provides an interesting structural consequence of universality: if $\mathfrak{A}$ is $m^+$-universal and has cardinality $m$ (at least the number of formulas in the language), then $\mathfrak{A}$ is isomorphic to a proper elementary substructure of itself. This gives an application of saturated and universal models to the study of elementary chains and self-embeddings.
