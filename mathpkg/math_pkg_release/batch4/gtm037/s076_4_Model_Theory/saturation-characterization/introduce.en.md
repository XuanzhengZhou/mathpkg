Theorem 28.21 combines the preceding lemmas to give a clean characterization: for a first-order language with fewer than $m$ formulas, an $L$-structure is $m$-saturated if and only if it is both $m$-universal and $m$-homogeneous. This equivalence is fundamental in the model theory of saturated structures and connects three central large-model properties.
