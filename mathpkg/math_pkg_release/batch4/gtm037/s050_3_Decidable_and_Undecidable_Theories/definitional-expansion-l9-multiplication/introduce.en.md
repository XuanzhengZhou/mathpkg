Definition 16.45 introduces the language $\mathcal{L}_9$ as a definitional expansion of $\mathcal{L}_8$, obtained by adding a new binary operation symbol $\cdot$ (intended to represent multiplication). The defining axiom specifies the value of $v_0 \cdot v_1$ in terms of the ternary relation $\pi(v_0, v_1, v_2)$, which expresses the graph of multiplication. The axiom covers the ordinary case where the product exists via $\pi$, as well as three degenerate cases involving the special element $\{1\}$ and the condition $\Omega v_0$, ensuring that multiplication is totally defined in the expanded language. This definitional expansion is a step toward formalizing Robinson arithmetic Q.
