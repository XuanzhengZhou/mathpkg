This definition introduces two notational conventions for finite sequences. The power notation $a^{(m)}$ denotes the constant sequence of length $m$ consisting entirely of the element $a$. The concatenation $xy$ joins two finite sequences $x$ and $y$ end-to-end. Additionally, a singleton sequence is identified with its sole element, so $a$ and $\langle a \rangle$ are used interchangeably. These notations are essential for describing tape configurations and computation traces in Turing machine theory.
