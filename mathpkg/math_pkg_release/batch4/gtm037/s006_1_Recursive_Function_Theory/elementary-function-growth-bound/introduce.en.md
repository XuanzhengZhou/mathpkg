This lemma establishes that every elementary function $g$ is bounded above by a fixed iterate of the Ackermann function $a(-, m)$ applied to the maximum of its arguments. The integer $m$ depends only on $g$, not on the arguments (provided the maximum argument exceeds 1). The proof shows that the set $A$ of functions possessing such a bound is closed under all elementary recursive operations: the constant functions, the successor function, the projection functions, addition, multiplication, exponentiation, composition, and bounded sum. This result is central to the characterization of the class of elementary recursive functions as precisely those functions bounded by some iterate of the Ackermann function.
