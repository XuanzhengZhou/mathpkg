Theorem 29.16 establishes an even stronger upward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem for logic without equality: any structure can be elementarily extended to any larger universe. This is striking because, unlike the classical case, finite structures can be elementarily extended to infinite structures. The proof uses a simple projection map to define the relations on the larger set.
