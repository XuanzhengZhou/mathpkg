This theorem collects the essential properties of the Skolem form $\varphi^S$ of a formula $\varphi$ relative to a Skolem expansion. Part (i) states that the Skolem form logically implies the original formula. Part (ii) gives the converse implication under the assumption that the structure is a model of the Skolem set.

Part (iii) establishes that the Skolemized theory $\Gamma'$ (axiomatized by the Skolem forms of sentences in $\Gamma$) behaves well with respect to models: expansions of models of $\Gamma$ satisfying the Skolem set are models of $\Gamma'$, and conversely the reduct of any model of $\Gamma'$ is a model of $\Gamma$. In particular, $(\Gamma', \mathcal{L}')$ is a conservative extension of $(\Gamma, \mathcal{L})$.

Part (iv) is a useful model-existence criterion: a formula has a model precisely when its Skolem form (which is universal) has a model.
