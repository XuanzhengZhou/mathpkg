Let $(\Gamma', \mathcal{L}')$ be a definitional extension of $(\Gamma, \mathcal{L})$, with notation as in 11.29. Then for any formula $\psi$ of $\mathcal{L}'$ there is a formula $\psi'$ of $\mathcal{L}$ with the same free variables as $\psi$ such that

$$\Gamma' \models \psi \leftrightarrow \psi'.$$

If $\Gamma'$ is an effective definitional extension of $\Gamma$, then such a formula $\psi'$ can be effectively obtained from $\psi$.
