Definition 11.1 introduces the fundamental semantic notion of an $\mathcal{L}$-structure for a first-order language $\mathcal{L}$. An $\mathcal{L}$-structure consists of a nonempty domain $A$ together with interpretations of every operation symbol and relation symbol of $\mathcal{L}$ as actual operations and relations on $A$. This notion provides the bridge between the purely syntactic language and its mathematical meaning, serving as the basis for the truth definition and the completeness theorem.
