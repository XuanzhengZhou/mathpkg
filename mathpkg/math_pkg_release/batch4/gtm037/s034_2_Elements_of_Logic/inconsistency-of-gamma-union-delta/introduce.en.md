Lemma 10.92 provides an equivalence characterization for when the union of a set of formulas $\Gamma$ with another set $\Delta$ is inconsistent. It states that $\Gamma \cup \Delta$ is inconsistent precisely when there is a finite disjunction of negated members of $\Delta$ that is syntactically provable from $\Gamma$. This lemma serves as a technical tool for deriving further results about inconsistency of sets of sentences.
