Show that there is a sentence $\psi$ such that

$$\mathcal{P} \models Pr(\Delta_{g}^{+} Pr(\langle \neg' \Delta_{g}^{+} \psi \rangle)) \leftrightarrow \psi.$$

Show that neither $\mathcal{P} \models \psi$ nor $\mathcal{P} \models \neg\psi$. [The intuitive paradox is as follows. Let $\varphi$ be the sentence

It is true that it is true that this sentence is false.

Since $\varphi$ is just a slight reformulation of the liar, a paradox arises as usual.]
