Let $\mathcal{L}$ be a first-order language. An expansion $\mathcal{L}'$ of $\mathcal{L}$ is rich by $C$ if $\mathcal{L}'$ is obtained from $\mathcal{L}$ by adjoining a set $C$ of individual constants with $|C| = |\text{Fmla}_{\mathcal{L}}|$. We shall denote $\mathcal{L}'$-structures frequently by $(\mathfrak{A}, a_c)_{c \in C}$, where $\mathfrak{A}$ is an $\mathcal{L}$-structure. A primitive term of $\mathcal{L}'$ is a term of the form $\mathbf{O}c_0 \cdots c_{m-1}$ where $\mathbf{O}$ is an operation symbol of rank $m$ and $\mathbf{c} \in {}^m C$. In particular ($m = 0$), any individual constant is a primitive term.
