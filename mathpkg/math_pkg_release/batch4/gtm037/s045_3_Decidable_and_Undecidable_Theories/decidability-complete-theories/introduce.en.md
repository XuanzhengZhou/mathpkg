This theorem characterizes decidable complete theories: a complete theory is decidable precisely when it is recursively axiomatizable. The forward direction is trivial since the theory axiomatizes itself. The converse uses the completeness of the theory to produce a decision procedure: given a sentence, enumerate all consequences of the recursive axiom set until either the sentence or its negation appears. This is a logical version of the fact that a set is recursive if and only if both it and its complement are recursively enumerable.
