This lemma, known as consequentia mirabilis ("the astonishing consequence"), states that if a formula implies its own negation, then its negation must be true. In other words, a self-refuting statement is false. The proof combines the deduction theorem with double-negation reasoning, and the result is an important derived rule in classical propositional logic.
