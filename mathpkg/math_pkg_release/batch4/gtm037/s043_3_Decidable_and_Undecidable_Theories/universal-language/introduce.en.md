The universal language $\mathcal{L}_{\text{un}}$ is an effective first-order language with infinitely many relation symbols of every positive arity but no operation symbols. For each positive arity $m$ and index $n$, there is a distinct $m$-ary relation symbol $\mathbf{R}_n^m$. This language provides the setting for spectral representability of number-theoretic functions, where finite models of sentences in $\mathcal{L}_{\text{un}}$ encode function values through the cardinalities of distinguished relations.
