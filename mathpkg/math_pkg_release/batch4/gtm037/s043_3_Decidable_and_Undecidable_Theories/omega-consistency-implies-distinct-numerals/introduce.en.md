This proposition establishes that any $\omega$-consistent theory in the language of number theory automatically proves the distinctness of numerals for distinct natural numbers. For $n \neq p$, the theory $\Gamma$ must contain $\neg(\mathbf{n} = \mathbf{p})$. This property is essential for the theory to distinguish different natural numbers and is used as a hypothesis in Theorems 14.5 and 14.6.
