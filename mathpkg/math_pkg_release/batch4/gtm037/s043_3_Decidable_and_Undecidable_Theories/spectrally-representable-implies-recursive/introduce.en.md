A number-theoretic function $f$ is spectrally representable if there exists a sentence $\varphi$ of the universal language $\mathcal{L}_{\text{un}}$ such that in any finite model of $\varphi$, the cardinalities of certain designated unary relations encode the input-output behavior of $f$. This lemma establishes that every spectrally representable function is recursive, connecting spectral definability to computability. The proof appeals to the weak Church's thesis, using the finiteness of the relevant vocabulary and the effectiveness of searching through finite structures.
