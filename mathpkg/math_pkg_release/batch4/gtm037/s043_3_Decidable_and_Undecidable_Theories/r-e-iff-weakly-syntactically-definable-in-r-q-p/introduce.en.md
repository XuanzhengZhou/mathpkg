This corollary extends the characterization of recursively enumerable relations to the standard arithmetic theories. A relation $R$ is r.e. precisely when it is weakly syntactically definable in any of the theories $R$, $Q$, or $P$. Since $Q$ is finitely axiomatizable and $P$ is Peano arithmetic, this provides multiple logical characterizations of recursive enumerability.
