This corollary combines Theorems 14.5 and 14.11 to give a complete logical characterization of recursive functions: a function is recursive precisely when it is syntactically definable in the weak arithmetic theory $R$. This establishes that syntactic definability in $R$ captures exactly the class of computable functions, providing a logical equivalent of recursiveness.
