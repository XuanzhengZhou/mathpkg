Let $R$ be the theory in $\mathcal{L}_{\text{nos}}$ axiomatized by the sentences $\mathbf{n} + \mathbf{p} = \Delta(n + p)$ and $\mathbf{n} \cdot \mathbf{p} = \Delta(n \cdot p)$ for all $n, p \in \omega$, together with $\neg(\mathbf{n} = \mathbf{p})$ for $n \neq p$, and $v_0 \leq \mathbf{n} \rightarrow v_0 = \mathbf{0} \lor \cdots \lor v_0 = \mathbf{n}$ for each $n \in \omega$. Then:

(i) $R$ is recursively axiomatizable.
(ii) $R$ is $\omega$-consistent.
