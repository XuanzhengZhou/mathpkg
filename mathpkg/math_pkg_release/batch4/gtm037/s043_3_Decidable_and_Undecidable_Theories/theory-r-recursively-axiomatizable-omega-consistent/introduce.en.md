Theory $R$ is a weak arithmetic theory axiomatized by the correct atomic sentences about addition and multiplication of numerals, the distinctness of distinct numerals, and the boundedness principle that every number less than or equal to $\mathbf{n}$ must be one of $\mathbf{0}, \ldots, \mathbf{n}$. This proposition establishes that $R$ is both recursively axiomatizable and $\omega$-consistent, making it a suitable base theory for the syntactic definability results in this chapter.
