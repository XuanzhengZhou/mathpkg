A theory $\Gamma$ in the language of number theory $\mathcal{L}_{\text{nos}}$ is $\omega$-consistent if it is impossible that $\varphi(\mathbf{x})$ belongs to $\Gamma$ for every natural number $x$ while simultaneously the sentence $\exists v_0 \neg \varphi(v_0)$ also belongs to $\Gamma$. This condition is stronger than ordinary consistency: it forbids a situation where a formula holds of each numeral individually yet its existential negation is provable. Omega-consistency plays a crucial role in Gödel's incompleteness theorems.
