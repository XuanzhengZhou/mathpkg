Three fundamental arithmetic theories are defined: $Q$ (Robinson arithmetic), a finitely axiomatizable theory with seven axioms covering the basic properties of successor, addition, and multiplication; $P$ (Peano arithmetic), which extends $Q$'s axioms with the full induction schema for all formulas; and $N$ (true arithmetic), the complete theory of the standard model $(\omega, +, \cdot, \mathbf{s}, \mathbf{0})$. These theories form an increasing chain in terms of deductive strength: $Q \subseteq P \subseteq N$.
