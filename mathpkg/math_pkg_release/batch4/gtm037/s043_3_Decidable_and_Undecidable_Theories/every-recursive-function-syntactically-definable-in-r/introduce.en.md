This theorem, one of the two main results of this section, establishes that the weak arithmetic theory $R$ is sufficiently strong to syntactically define every recursive function. The proof uses Julia Robinson's theorem (3.48) and proceeds by showing that the class $A$ of functions syntactically definable in $R$ contains addition, multiplication, projection functions, and is closed under composition and inversion. Together with Theorem 14.5, this yields a logical characterization of recursiveness.
