Theorem 10.38 gives the first formal expression of the principle of substitution of equals for equals in the object language. If each of the $m$ arguments of an operation symbol $O$ is pairwise equal to a corresponding term ($\sigma_i = \tau_i$ for all $i < m$), then applying $O$ to the $\sigma$'s yields a term equal to applying $O$ to the $\tau$'s. The proof proceeds by successive applications of Lemma 10.37 together with a suitable propositional tautology.
