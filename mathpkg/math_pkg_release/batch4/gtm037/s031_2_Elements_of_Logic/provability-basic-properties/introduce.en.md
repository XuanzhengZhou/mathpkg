Proposition 10.33 summarizes three elementary properties of the provability relation $\vdash$ that are used frequently without explicit citation throughout the development. Part (i) is monotonicity: adding premises preserves provability. Part (ii) is finiteness: any provable formula has a proof using only finitely many premises. Part (iii) is the cut rule: provability is transitive across intermediate sets of formulas.
