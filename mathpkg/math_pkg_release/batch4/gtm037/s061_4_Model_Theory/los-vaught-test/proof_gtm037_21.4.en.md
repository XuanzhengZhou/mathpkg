We need to show that any two models $\mathfrak{A}$, $\mathfrak{B}$ of $\Gamma$ are elementarily equivalent. By the Löwenheim–Skolem theorems there are $\mathcal{L}$-structures $\mathfrak{A}'$, $\mathfrak{B}'$ of power $m$ with $\mathfrak{A} \equiv \mathfrak{A}'$ and $\mathfrak{B} \equiv \mathfrak{B}'$. Thus $\mathfrak{A}'$ and $\mathfrak{B}'$ are models of $\Gamma$, so $\mathfrak{A}' \cong \mathfrak{B}'$ by the assumption of $m$-categoricity. Hence $\mathfrak{A} \equiv \mathfrak{B}$.
