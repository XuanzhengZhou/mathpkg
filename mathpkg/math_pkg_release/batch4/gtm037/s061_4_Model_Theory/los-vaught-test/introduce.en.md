The Łoś–Vaught test provides a practical sufficient condition for proving that a first-order theory is complete. If a theory has only infinite models and is categorical in some cardinality at least as large as the number of formulas in its language, then the theory must be complete. This test has been applied to show the completeness of many important theories: dense linear orders without endpoints, atomless Boolean algebras, and divisible torsion-free Abelian groups.
