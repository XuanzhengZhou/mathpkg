An $m$-elementary extension is a refinement of the usual notion of elementary extension, introduced by Dana Scott. Instead of requiring agreement on all first-order formulas, it only requires agreement on formulas with at most $m$ quantifiers. This graded notion provides a finer tool for analyzing the logical complexity of embeddings between structures and has been used to prove decidability and completeness results for certain theories. *(Note: the source text for this definition is partially truncated; the reconstruction follows standard model-theoretic usage.)*
