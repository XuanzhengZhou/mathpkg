For any consistent first-order theory, being categorical is equivalent to being $m$-categorical for some positive integer $m$ with all models having that finite cardinality. In particular, no consistent theory with an infinite model can be categorical; categoricity in first-order logic is thus a trivial notion that only applies to finite theories. This is why the cardinality-relative notion of $m$-categoricity is the meaningful concept in model theory.
