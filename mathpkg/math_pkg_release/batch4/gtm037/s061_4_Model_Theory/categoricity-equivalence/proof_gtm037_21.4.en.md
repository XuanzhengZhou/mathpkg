Obviously $(ii) \Rightarrow (i)$. Now assume $(i)$. Since any two models of $\Gamma$ are isomorphic, they all have the same cardinality $m$, and $\Gamma$ is $m$-categorical. Suppose $m \geq \aleph_0$. Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a model of $\Gamma$, and by the upward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem let $\mathfrak{B}$ be an elementary extension of $\mathfrak{A}$ of power $> m$. Then $\mathfrak{A} \equiv \mathfrak{B}$, so $\mathfrak{B}$ is also a model of $\Gamma$, contradiction. Hence $m < \aleph_0$.
