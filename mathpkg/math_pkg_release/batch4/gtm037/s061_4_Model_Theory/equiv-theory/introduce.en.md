$\Gamma_{\text{equiv}}$ is the theory of a single equivalence relation, axiomatized by reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of the binary relation symbol $\mathbf{R}$. An $m$-basic model is a model where, for each cardinality $n \leq m$, there is at most one equivalence class of that size. This theory serves as an illustrative example for the $m$-elementary extension method: the theory of one equivalence relation is decidable, contrasting with the undecidability of the theory of two equivalence relations (Theorem 16.56). *(Note: the source text for this definition is partially truncated; part (ii) has been reconstructed from standard usage.)*
