(i) $\Gamma_{\text{equiv}}$ is the set of all logical consequences of the following sentences, in a language with a single binary relation symbol $\mathbf{R}$:

$$\forall v_0 \, \mathbf{R}v_0v_0$$
$$\forall v_0 \forall v_1 \, (\mathbf{R}v_0v_1 \rightarrow \mathbf{R}v_1v_0)$$
$$\forall v_0 \forall v_1 \forall v_2 \, (\mathbf{R}v_0v_1 \wedge \mathbf{R}v_1v_2 \rightarrow \mathbf{R}v_0v_2)$$

(ii) For $m \in \omega \sim 1$, a model $\mathfrak{A} = \langle A, \mathbf{R} \rangle$ of $\Gamma_{\text{equiv}}$ is called an $m$-basic model of $\Gamma_{\text{equiv}}$ provided that for every $n \leq m$, $A$ has at most one equivalence class of size $n$.
