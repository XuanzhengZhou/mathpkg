The Soundness Lemma establishes that syntactic derivability implies semantic entailment in sentential logic: if a formula $\varphi$ is provable from a set of premises $\Gamma$, then every model that satisfies all formulas in $\Gamma$ also satisfies $\varphi$. This lemma is the easy direction of the completeness theorem and is verified by checking that the logical axioms are tautologies and that the rule of detachment preserves truth.
