Definition 8.34 introduces finite generalizations of conjunction ($\bigwedge$) and disjunction ($\bigvee$) for arbitrary finite sequences of sentences. These generalized connectives allow compact formulation of results such as the Generalized Deduction Theorem (Lemma 8.36) and criteria for inconsistency (Theorem 8.37), where a finite number of premises must be conjoined.
