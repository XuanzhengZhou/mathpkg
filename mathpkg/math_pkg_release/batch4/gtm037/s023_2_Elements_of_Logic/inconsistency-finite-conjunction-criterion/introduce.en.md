Theorem 8.37 provides a useful syntactic criterion for the inconsistency of a set $\Gamma$ of sentences: $\Gamma$ is inconsistent if and only if there exists a finite number of sentences $\varphi_0, \ldots, \varphi_m$ from $\Gamma$ such that the conjunction of their negations is provable. This connects the semantic notion of inconsistency with a finitary syntactic condition involving the generalized conjunction.
