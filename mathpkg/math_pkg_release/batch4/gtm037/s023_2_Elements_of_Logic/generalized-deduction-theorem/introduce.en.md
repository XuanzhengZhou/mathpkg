The Generalized Deduction Theorem (Lemma 8.36) extends the ordinary deduction theorem from a single hypothesis to an arbitrary finite set of hypotheses. It states that a finite set of premises $\{\varphi_i : i < m\}$ entails $\psi$ exactly when their finite conjunction implies $\psi$. The proof proceeds by induction on the number of premises, using the ordinary deduction theorem and the fact that certain propositional schemas involving the generalized conjunction are tautologies.
