The two-element Boolean algebra $\mathbf{2}$ is the simplest nontrivial Boolean algebra, consisting of the set $\{0, 1\}$ with operations defined by the standard truth tables. It corresponds to the algebra of classical propositional logic, where $+$ is disjunction, $\cdot$ is conjunction, and $-$ is negation. Every maximal ideal $I$ in a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ yields a quotient $\mathfrak{A}/I$ isomorphic to $\mathbf{2}$, making $\mathbf{2}$ the unique simple Boolean algebra.
