Given an ideal $I$ in a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$, the quotient algebra $\mathfrak{A}/I$ is formed by taking equivalence classes modulo the relation $xRy$ iff $x \cdot -y + y \cdot -x \in I$. This relation captures the idea that two elements are equivalent when their symmetric difference lies in the ideal. The operations are defined on equivalence classes by lifting the original operations from $\mathfrak{A}$, and the definition is well-defined because the relation $R$ is a congruence with respect to the Boolean algebra operations.
