Let $I$ be an ideal of a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A} = \langle A, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1 \rangle$. Let $A/I$ be the set of all equivalence classes under the equivalence relation $R$ (defined by $xRy$ iff $x \cdot -y + y \cdot -x \in I$). The equivalence class of an element $a \in A$ is denoted by $[a]_I$, or simply $[a]$. Operations $+', \cdot',$ and $-'$ on $A/I$ are uniquely determined by the stipulation that, for all $a, b \in A$,

$$[a] +' [b] = [a + b],$$
$$[a] \cdot' [b] = [a \cdot b],$$
$$-' [a] = [-a].$$

We let $\mathfrak{A}/I = \langle A/I, +', \cdot', -', [0], [1] \rangle$.
