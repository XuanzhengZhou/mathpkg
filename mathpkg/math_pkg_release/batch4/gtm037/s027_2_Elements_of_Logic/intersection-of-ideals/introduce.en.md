If $\{I_j : j \in J\}$ is a nonempty collection of ideals of a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$, then their intersection $\bigcap_{j \in J} I_j$ is again an ideal of $\mathfrak{A}$. The verification is straightforward: the intersection is nonempty (it contains $0$), downward closed, and closed under joins because each $I_j$ satisfies these properties. The same holds for filters by duality or by a direct symmetric argument.
