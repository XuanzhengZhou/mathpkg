The direct product of a family of Boolean algebras $\{\mathfrak{A}_i : i \in I\}$ is formed by taking the Cartesian product of their underlying sets and defining operations coordinatewise. This yields another Boolean algebra. When all factors are the same algebra $\mathfrak{C}$, the product is denoted $^I\mathfrak{C}$. The direct product is a fundamental construction that corresponds, under Stone duality, to the disjoint union of Stone spaces.
