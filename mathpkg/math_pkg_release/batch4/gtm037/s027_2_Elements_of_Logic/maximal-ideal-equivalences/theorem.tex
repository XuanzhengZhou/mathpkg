For any ideal $I$ in a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ the following conditions are equivalent:

(i) $I$ is maximal;

(ii) $I \neq A$, and for any $x, y \in A$, if $x \cdot y \in I$ then $x \in I$ or $y \in I$;

(iii) $I \neq A$, and for any $x \in A$, either $x \in I$ or $-x \in I$;

(iv) the algebra $\mathfrak{A}/I$ is isomorphic to $2$.
