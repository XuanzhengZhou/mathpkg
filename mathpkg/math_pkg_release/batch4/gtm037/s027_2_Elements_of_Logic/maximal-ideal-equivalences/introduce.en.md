A maximal ideal in a Boolean algebra can be characterized in several useful ways: by the prime condition ($x \cdot y \in I \implies x \in I$ or $y \in I$), by the property that for every element $x$, either $x$ or its complement lies in $I$, and by the fact that the quotient algebra $\mathfrak{A}/I$ is isomorphic to the two-element Boolean algebra $\mathbf{2}$. These equivalences provide multiple tools for working with maximal ideals and connect the algebraic notion of maximality with the logical notion of completeness (every statement or its negation is provable).
