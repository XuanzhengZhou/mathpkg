$(ii) \Rightarrow (i)$: The power set Boolean algebra on an $m$-element set has $2^m$ elements.

$(i) \Rightarrow (ii)$: Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a finite Boolean algebra of power $n$. By Proposition 9.29, $\mathfrak{A}$ is atomic. Let $m$ be the number of atoms of $\mathfrak{A}$. By the representation theorem for finite Boolean algebras (every element is uniquely the sum of the atoms below it, see Proposition 9.30), $|A| = 2^m$. Hence $n = 2^m$.
