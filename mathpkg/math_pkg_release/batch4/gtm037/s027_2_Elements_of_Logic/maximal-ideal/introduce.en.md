A maximal ideal in a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ is a proper ideal that cannot be extended to a larger proper ideal. Dually, an ultrafilter is a proper filter that cannot be extended to a larger proper filter. Under the ideal-filter correspondence, maximal ideals correspond exactly to ultrafilters. Maximal ideals are also called prime ideals because they satisfy the prime condition: if $x \cdot y \in I$, then $x \in I$ or $y \in I$.
