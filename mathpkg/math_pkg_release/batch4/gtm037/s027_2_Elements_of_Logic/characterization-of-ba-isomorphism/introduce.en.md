A map between Boolean algebras is an isomorphism if and only if it is surjective and preserves the order relation in both directions. This proposition gives a useful criterion for checking that a Boolean algebra homomorphism is in fact an isomorphism, without needing to verify that all three operations (join, meet, complement) are preserved. The condition $x \leq y$ iff $fx \leq' fy$ ensures that $f$ is an order-embedding, which together with surjectivity implies $f$ is a Boolean algebra isomorphism.
