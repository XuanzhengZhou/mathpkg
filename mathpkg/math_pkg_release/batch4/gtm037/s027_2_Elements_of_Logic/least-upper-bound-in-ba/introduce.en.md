The least upper bound (or sum) $\Sigma X$ of a subset $X$ of a Boolean algebra generalizes the notion of arbitrary disjunction. Not every subset need have a sum; Boolean algebras in which every subset has a sum are called complete Boolean algebras. When the sum exists, it is unique. The dual notion is the greatest lower bound (or product) $\Pi X$, defined analogously. These operations satisfy distributivity laws such as $a \cdot \Sigma X = \Sigma \{a \cdot x : x \in X\}$ whenever the sums exist.
