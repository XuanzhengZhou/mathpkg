Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a Boolean algebra, $X \subseteq A$. The least upper bound of $X$, if it exists, is the unique element $\Sigma X$ such that $\forall x \in X (x \leq \Sigma X)$ (i.e., $\Sigma X$ is an upper bound of $X$) and $\forall y \in A [\forall x \in X (x \leq y) \implies \Sigma X \leq y]$.
