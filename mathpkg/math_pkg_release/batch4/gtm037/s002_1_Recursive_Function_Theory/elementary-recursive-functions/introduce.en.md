Elementary recursive functions form the smallest class of number-theoretic functions containing addition, multiplication, absolute difference, integer division, and all projection functions, closed under composition and bounded summation. This class captures most effective functions encountered in mathematical practice, though not all computable functions. It serves as a stepping stone toward the larger classes of primitive recursive and general recursive functions, which progressively capture more of the intuitively computable functions.
