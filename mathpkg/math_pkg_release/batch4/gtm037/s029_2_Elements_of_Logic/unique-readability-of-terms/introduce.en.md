Proposition 10.12 establishes the unique readability property for terms of a first-order language. Every term is nonempty, and each term has exactly one of two forms: either it is an individual variable $\langle v_m \rangle$, or it is the application of an operation symbol $\mathbf{O}$ to a sequence of subterms. In both cases the decomposition is unique -- the index $m$, the operation symbol $\mathbf{O}$, and the subterms $\sigma_0,\ldots,\sigma_{m-1}$ are all uniquely determined. This guarantees that the syntactic structure of terms can be parsed unambiguously.
