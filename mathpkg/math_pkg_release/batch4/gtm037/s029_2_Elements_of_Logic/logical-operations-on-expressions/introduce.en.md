This definition introduces the standard logical operations on expressions as formal syntactic constructions using the logical constants $L_0,\ldots,L_4$. Negation, disjunction, conjunction, universal quantification, and equality are taken as primitive, while implication, biconditional, and existential quantification are defined in terms of the primitives. The operations are then lifted to G\"{o}del numbers via the prime-power concatenation function Cat, allowing syntactic manipulation to be carried out arithmetically in $\omega$.
