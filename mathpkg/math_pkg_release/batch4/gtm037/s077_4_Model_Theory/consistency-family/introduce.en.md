A consistency family is a family of sets of sentences satisfying closure conditions (C0)-(C8) that form the basis for Henkin-style model existence proofs in first-order logic without equality. The conditions ensure that maximal elements of the family behave like Henkin theories: they are negation-complete, closed under subformulas, and witness existential quantifiers. Condition (C7) handles limit stages in the construction, while (C8) bounds the number of new constants used.
