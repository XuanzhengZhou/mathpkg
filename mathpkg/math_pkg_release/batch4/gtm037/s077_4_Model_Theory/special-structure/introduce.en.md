A special structure is a weakening of the notion of a saturated structure that avoids dependence on the generalized continuum hypothesis (GCH). For successor cardinals it coincides with saturation, while for limit cardinals it is defined as the union of an elementary chain of increasingly saturated substructures. Special structures suffice for many model-theoretic applications where saturated structures would otherwise require GCH.
