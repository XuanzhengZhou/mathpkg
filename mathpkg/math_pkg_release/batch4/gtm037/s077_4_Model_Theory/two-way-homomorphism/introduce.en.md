A two-way homomorphism is a pair of homomorphisms going in opposite directions between two structures, both of which are surjective (onto). This notion is used in logic without equality to study when two $\mathcal{L}^{\text{e}}$-structures are essentially the same up to a congruence relation interpreting equality. If an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ satisfies the equality axioms and $f$ is a two-way homomorphism, then the kernel of $f$ can serve as an interpretation of the equality symbol.
