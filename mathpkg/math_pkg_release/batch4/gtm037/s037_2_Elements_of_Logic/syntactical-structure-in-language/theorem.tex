Let $\mathcal{L} = (L, v, \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{R})$ and $\mathcal{L}' = (L', v', \mathcal{O}', \mathcal{R}')$ be two first-order languages. A \text{syntactical $\mathcal{L}$-structure in $\mathcal{L}'$} is a quadruple $\mathfrak{A} = (\chi, f, R, \Gamma')$ such that $\chi$ is a formula of $\mathcal{L}'$ with $\operatorname{Fv} \chi \subseteq \{v_0\}$, $f: \operatorname{Dmn} \mathcal{O} \rightarrow \operatorname{Dmn} \mathcal{O}'$ and $\mathcal{O}'_{f\mathbf{O}} = \mathcal{O}_{\mathbf{O}}$ for each $\mathbf{O} \in \operatorname{Dmn} \mathcal{O}$, $R: \operatorname{Dmn} \mathcal{R} \rightarrow \operatorname{Fmla}_{\mathcal{L}'}$ and $\mathcal{R}'_{R\mathbf{R}} = \mathcal{R}_{\mathbf{R}}$ for each $\mathbf{R} \in \operatorname{Dmn} \mathcal{R}$, and $\Gamma'$ is a theory in $\mathcal{L}'$.
