A syntactical $\mathcal{L}$-structure in $\mathcal{L}'$ provides a syntactic counterpart to the semantic notion of a model. It consists of a defining formula $\chi$ (with at most $v_0$ free), a mapping $f$ on operation symbols, a mapping $R$ on relation symbols, and a theory $\Gamma'$, all subject to compatibility conditions that mirror the arity requirements of the original language $\mathcal{L}$ within the target language $\mathcal{L}'$.
