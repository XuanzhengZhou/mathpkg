The proposition is clear from the definitions. Since $\mathfrak{A}$ is an effective syntactical $\mathcal{L}$-structure in $\mathcal{L}'$, the formula $\chi$, the map $f$ on operation symbols, and the map $R$ on relation symbols are all recursive. The translations $\sigma^{\mathfrak{A}}$ and $\varphi^{\mathfrak{A}}$ are defined by recursion on the structure of terms and formulas respectively, using only the recursive data $(\chi, f, R, \Gamma')$. Consequently, when composed with the Gödel numbering $g$ and its inverse $g^{+-1}$, the resulting partial functions are partial recursive on the Gödel sets $g^{+*} \operatorname{Trm}_{\mathfrak{L}}$ and $g^{+*} \operatorname{Fmla}_{\mathfrak{L}}$.
