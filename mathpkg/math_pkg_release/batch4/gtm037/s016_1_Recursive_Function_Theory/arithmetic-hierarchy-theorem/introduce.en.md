Theorem 5.36, the Hierarchy Theorem, is the central result of the arithmetic hierarchy. It shows that the hierarchy is strict at every level: for each $m, n > 0$, there exist $n$-ary relations that are $\Sigma_m$ but not $\Pi_m$, and dually relations that are $\Pi_m$ but not $\Sigma_m$. Moreover, there exist relations in $\Delta_{m+1}$ that are neither $\Sigma_m$ nor $\Pi_m$, proving that each level of the hierarchy is a proper extension of all lower levels. The proof uses the universal relation $R_m^n$ from Theorem 5.35 and a diagonal argument to construct explicit counterexamples, establishing that the quantifier-alternation complexity of arithmetical relations forms a genuine, non-collapsing hierarchy.
