An $n$-ary relation $R$ is in $\Sigma_{m+1}$ iff there is an $(n+1)$-ary relation $S$ in $\Pi_m$ such that for all $x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1} \in \omega$,

$$(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}) \in R \quad \text{iff} \quad \exists y \in \omega[(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}, y) \in S].$$

Dually, an $n$-ary relation $R$ is in $\Pi_{m+1}$ iff there is an $(n+1)$-ary relation $S$ in $\Sigma_m$ such that for all $x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1} \in \omega$,

$$(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}) \in R \quad \text{iff} \quad \forall y \in \omega[(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}, y) \in S].$$
