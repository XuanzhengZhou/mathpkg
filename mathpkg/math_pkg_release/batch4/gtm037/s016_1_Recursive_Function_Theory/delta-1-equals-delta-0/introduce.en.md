Theorem 5.34 states that the first level of the arithmetic hierarchy, $\Delta_1$, collapses to the class of recursive relations $\Delta_0$. In other words, any relation that is definable both by a $\Sigma_1$ formula and by a $\Pi_1$ formula is already recursive, and conversely every recursive relation is $\Delta_1$. This is a fundamental result connecting the syntactic complexity of definitions (quantifier alternations) with computability (recursive decidability). The theorem is used repeatedly throughout the development of recursive function theory.
