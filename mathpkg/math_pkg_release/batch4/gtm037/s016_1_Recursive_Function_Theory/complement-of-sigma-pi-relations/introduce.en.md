Proposition 5.32 establishes the fundamental duality between $\Sigma_m$ and $\Pi_m$ classes via complementation. It states that a relation belongs to $\Sigma_m$ if and only if its complement (relative to ${}^n\omega$) belongs to $\Pi_m$. This duality is a direct consequence of De Morgan's laws for quantifiers and is central to understanding the structure of the arithmetic hierarchy. It implies that the classes $\Sigma_m$ and $\Pi_m$ are mirror images of each other under complementation.
