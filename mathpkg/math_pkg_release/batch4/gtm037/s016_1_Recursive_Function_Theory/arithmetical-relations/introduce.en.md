An arithmetical relation is any $n$-ary relation on the natural numbers that belongs to one of the classes $\Sigma_m$ or $\Pi_m$ for some $m \geq 1$. Equivalently, a relation is arithmetical if it is definable by a first-order formula in the language of arithmetic. Since there are only countably many arithmetical relations, most number-theoretic relations are not arithmetical, highlighting the vast majority of undefinable relations over $\omega$.
