Corollary 5.2 establishes the relationship between the two notions of computability. Every (total) Turing computable function is also partial Turing computable, which is immediate since a total function is a special case of a partial function. Conversely, if a partial Turing computable function happens to be total (its domain is all of ${}^m\omega$), then it is Turing computable in the original sense. Thus the two notions coincide on total functions.
