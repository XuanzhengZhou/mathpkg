$(m, n) \in R_4$ if and only if $m$ is a Gödel number of a Markov algorithm, $\ln n \geq 1$, and

$$\forall i < \ln n \div 1 \left[ (m, (n)_i, (n)_{i+1}) \in R_2 \right]$$

and

$$(m, (n)_{\ln n - 1}, (n)_{\ln n}) \in R_3.$$

The relation $R_2$ and $R_3$ are previously established as elementary, and the quantifier is bounded, so $R_4$ is elementary.
