An $m$-ary partial function on the natural numbers $\omega$ is a function whose domain is a subset of ${}^m\omega$ (the set of all $m$-tuples of natural numbers). The domain can be empty, finite, or infinite. When the domain equals all of ${}^m\omega$, the partial function is called total and coincides with the ordinary notion of an $m$-ary function. Partial functions are essential for formulating results about computability where certain inputs may lead to non-terminating computations.
