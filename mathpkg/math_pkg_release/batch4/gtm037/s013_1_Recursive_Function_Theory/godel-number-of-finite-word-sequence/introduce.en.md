The Gödel number of a finite sequence of words $\langle d_0, \ldots, d_m \rangle$ is defined as the product over $i < m$ of primes $p_i$ raised to the power $g_i d_i$, where $g_i d_i$ is the Gödel number of the $i$-th word. This encoding allows finite sequences of words to be represented by single natural numbers. The relation $R_4$ captures the computational semantics of Markov algorithms: a pair $(m,n)$ belongs to $R_4$ precisely when $m$ encodes a Markov algorithm $A$ and $n$ encodes a computation carried out under $A$.
