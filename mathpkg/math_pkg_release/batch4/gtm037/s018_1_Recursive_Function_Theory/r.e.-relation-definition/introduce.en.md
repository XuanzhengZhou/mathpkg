A relation $R \subseteq {}^m \omega$ is recursively enumerable if it is empty or can be enumerated by $m$ recursive functions acting coordinate-wise on $\omega$. This definition extends the notion of r.e. sets (the case $m = 1$) to finitary relations, providing a uniform framework for studying the enumerability of multi-dimensional predicates in recursion theory.
