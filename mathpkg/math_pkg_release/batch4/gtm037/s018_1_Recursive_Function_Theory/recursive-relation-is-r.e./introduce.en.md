Every recursive relation is recursively enumerable, establishing that the class of recursive relations is contained in the class of r.e. relations. Moreover, for every arity $m \geq 1$ there exists an $m$-ary r.e. relation that is not recursive, showing the inclusion is proper. This generalizes the one-dimensional result that the recursive sets form a proper subclass of the r.e. sets.
