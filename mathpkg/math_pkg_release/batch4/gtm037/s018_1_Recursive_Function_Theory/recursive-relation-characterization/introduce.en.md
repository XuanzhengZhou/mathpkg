Theorem 6.15 generalizes the characterization of recursive sets (Theorem 6.6) to finitary relations: an $m$-ary relation $R$ is recursive if and only if both $R$ and its complement are recursively enumerable. This is the relational version of Post's theorem, establishing that decidability of a relation is equivalent to the semi-decidability of both the relation and its complement.
