Theorem 21.32 gives a purely mathematical (non-syntactic) sufficient condition for a theory $\Gamma$ to be complete. Condition (i) requires that every finitely generated substructure of any model embeds into any other model. Condition (ii) is a stronger "amalgamation-like" property: if $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$ are models of $\Gamma$, any finitely generated substructure $\mathfrak{B}'$ of $\mathfrak{B}$ embeds into $\mathfrak{A}$ in a way that fixes the intersection $A \cap B'$ pointwise. The proof proceeds by first showing $\Gamma$ is model-complete via criterion 21.27(iii), then applying Proposition 21.31 to deduce completeness.
