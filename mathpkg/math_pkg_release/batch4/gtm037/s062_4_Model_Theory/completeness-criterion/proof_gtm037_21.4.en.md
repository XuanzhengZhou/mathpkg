First we show that $\Gamma$ is model-complete; to prove this we apply 21.27(iii). Assume that $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are models of $\Gamma$, $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$, $x \in {}^\omega A$, $\varphi$ is a universal formula, and not $(\mathfrak{B} \models \varphi[x])$; we want to show that not $(\mathfrak{A} \models \varphi[x])$. Say $\varphi = \forall v_{i_0} \cdots \forall v_{i_{m-1}} \psi$ with $\psi$ quantifier-free. Then there is a $y \in {}^\omega B$ with not $(\mathfrak{B} \models \psi[y])$ and $y_j = x_j$ for all $j \in \omega \setminus \{i_0, \ldots, i_{m-1}\}$. Let $\mathfrak{B}'$ be the substructure of $\mathfrak{B}$ generated by $\{y_{i_0}, \ldots, y_{i_{m-1}}\} \cup \{x_j : j \in \omega \setminus \{i_0, \ldots, i_{m-1}\}\}$. By condition (ii), there exists an embedding $f : \mathfrak{B}' \to \mathfrak{A}$ such that $f \upharpoonright (A \cap B')$ is the identity. Applying $f$ to the witnessing tuple yields an assignment in $A$ showing not $(\mathfrak{A} \models \psi[f \circ y])$, hence not $(\mathfrak{A} \models \varphi[x])$. This establishes model completeness of $\Gamma$ by criterion 21.27(iii). Since $\Gamma$ is model complete and condition (i) implies the joint extension property, Proposition 21.31 yields that $\Gamma$ is complete.
