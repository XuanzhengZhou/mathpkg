Proposition 21.31 states that if a theory $\Gamma$ is model complete and possesses the joint extension property, then $\Gamma$ is complete. This provides a useful criterion for establishing completeness of a theory via model-theoretic properties. The proof is analogous to that of Proposition 21.29, as noted in the text.
