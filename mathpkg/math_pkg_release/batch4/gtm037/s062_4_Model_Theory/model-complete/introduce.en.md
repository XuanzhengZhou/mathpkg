Definition 21.20 introduces the notion of a model-complete set of sentences. A consistent set $\Gamma$ of sentences is model-complete precisely when the substructure relation between any two models of $\Gamma$ coincides with the elementary substructure relation. This means that for models of $\Gamma$, being a substructure automatically implies being an elementary substructure. Model completeness is a key property that simplifies checking elementary equivalence and plays a central role in subsequent results about completeness of theories (cf. 19.20).
