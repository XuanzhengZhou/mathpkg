Proposition 21.21 characterizes the set $\Delta$ of formulas generated from atomic formulas and their negations by closing under disjunction, conjunction, and universal quantification. The proposition states that every formula in $\Delta$ is logically equivalent to a universal formula with the same free variables. The proof is straightforward: letting $\Theta$ be the set of formulas that admit such an equivalent universal formula, one observes that $\Theta$ satisfies the closure conditions defining $\Delta$, hence $\Delta \subseteq \Theta$.
