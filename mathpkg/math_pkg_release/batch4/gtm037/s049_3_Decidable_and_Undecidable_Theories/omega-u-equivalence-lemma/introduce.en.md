Lemma 16.20 states that in the language $\mathcal{L}_3$, a set $v_0$ satisfies the ordinal-like predicate $\Omega$ if and only if its successor $\mathbf{U} v_0 = v_0 \cup \{v_0\}$ also satisfies $\Omega$. This lemma establishes the equivalence of $\Omega$-satisfaction between a set and its $\mathbf{U}$-image, which is crucial for building the natural numbers in the formal set theory. The proof uses the properties that $\mathbf{B}$, transitivity, and $\mathbf{W}$ are all preserved in both directions under $\mathbf{U}$.
