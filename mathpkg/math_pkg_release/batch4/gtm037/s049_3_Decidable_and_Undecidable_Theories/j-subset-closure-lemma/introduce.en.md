In the formal language $\mathcal{L}_1$, if a set $v_0$ satisfies the predicate $\mathbf{J}$ (meaning that intersections can be formed for subsets of $v_0$) and $v_1$ is a subset of $v_0$, then $v_1$ also satisfies $\mathbf{J}$. This lemma shows that the property of admitting intersections is hereditary downward with respect to the subset relation.
