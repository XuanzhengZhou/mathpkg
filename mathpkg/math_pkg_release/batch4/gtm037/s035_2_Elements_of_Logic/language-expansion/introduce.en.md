This definition formalizes the process of enriching a first-order language with additional operation symbols, relation symbols, or (in the effectivized case) additional computability data. An expansion $\mathcal{L}'$ of $\mathcal{L}$ retains all original symbols and adds new ones. For structures, an $\mathcal{L}'$-expansion of an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ is a structure $\mathfrak{B}$ for the richer language whose reduct (forgetting the new symbols) recovers $\mathfrak{A}$. This concept is essential for the completeness proof: to construct rich extensions, one expands the language with fresh individual constants (Henkin constants) that serve as witnesses for existential sentences.
