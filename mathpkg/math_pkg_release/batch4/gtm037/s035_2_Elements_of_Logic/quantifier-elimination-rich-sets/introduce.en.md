This proposition formalizes the central role of rich sets in the Henkin completeness proof: every sentence is provably equivalent, modulo a rich set $\Gamma$, to a quantifier-free sentence. The proof proceeds by induction on the structure of $\varphi$, using the richness property to replace each existential quantifier with a suitable constant substitution. Combined with completeness (decidability of all sentences), this allows the construction of a term model in which truth reduces to provability, ultimately yielding a model for the original consistent set.
