This definition extends Tarski's semantic notion of truth from terms to arbitrary first-order formulas. Given an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$, each formula $\varphi$ is assigned the set $\varphi^{\mathfrak{A}}$ of all assignments $x \in {}^\omega A$ that satisfy $\varphi$ in $\mathfrak{A}$. The definition proceeds by recursion on formula structure: atomic equalities and relation applications, Boolean connectives via set-theoretic complement/union/intersection, and universal quantification via the modified assignment $x_a^i$. A formula $\varphi$ elementarily defines $\varphi^{\mathfrak{A}}$, and we write $\mathfrak{A} \models \varphi[x]$ when $x$ satisfies $\varphi$.
