This theorem provides an important method for constructing new effectively inseparable pairs from existing ones. If $A$ and $B$ are effectively inseparable and a recursive function $f$ maps $A$ into $C$ and $B$ into $D$ (with $C$ and $D$ disjoint), then $C$ and $D$ are also effectively inseparable. This closure property under recursive inverse images makes effective inseparability a robust notion that can be propagated through recursive reductions.
