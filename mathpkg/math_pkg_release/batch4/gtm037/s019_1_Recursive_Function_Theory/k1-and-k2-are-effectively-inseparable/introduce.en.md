This theorem provides a concrete and natural example of an effectively inseparable pair of sets. The sets $K_1$ and $K_2$ are defined using the Kleene $T_1$-predicate in a symmetric manner, and their effective inseparability is witnessed by the simple recursive function $f(e, r) = 2^r \cdot 3^e$. This construction serves as a prototype for many other effectively inseparable pairs that arise in logic.
