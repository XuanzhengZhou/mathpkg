An $m$-sequence (or $\alpha$-sequence for $\alpha \in \omega \cup \{\omega\}$) is a system of relations $\langle I_k : k \in \alpha \rangle$ that refines the notion of elementary equivalence $\equiv_m$ by explicitly recording which tuples from the two structures are regarded as equivalent. Condition (3) is the back-and-forth extension property: from any $I_m$-related $m$-tuples, any additional element on one side can be matched on the other side while preserving the relation at the next level. Condition (4) ensures that $I_k$-related tuples satisfy the same atomic formulas, anchoring the entire construction in the atomic diagram of the structures. This definition generalizes the classical back-and-forth systems used in Fraisse's theorem.
