Let $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ be $\mathcal{L}$-structures for the same language $\mathcal{L}$. The relations $\equiv_m$ are defined by induction on $m \in \omega$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{A} \equiv_0 \mathfrak{B}$ iff $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are $\mathcal{L}$-structures and either $\mathcal{L}$ has no individual constants or else it does and $\mathfrak{A}^- \cong \mathfrak{B}^-$.
\item $\mathfrak{A} \equiv_{m+1} \mathfrak{B}$ iff for every $a \in A$ there is a $b \in B$ such that $(\mathfrak{A}, a) \equiv_m (\mathfrak{B}, b)$ and similarly with the roles of $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ interchanged.
\end{enumerate}
