Let $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ be $\mathcal{L}$-structures, and let $m$ be a positive integer. If there is an $(m + 1)$-system of partial isomorphisms of $\mathfrak{A}$ into $\mathfrak{B}$, then Player II has a winning strategy for the $m$-elementary game over $\mathfrak{A}$, $\mathfrak{B}$.
