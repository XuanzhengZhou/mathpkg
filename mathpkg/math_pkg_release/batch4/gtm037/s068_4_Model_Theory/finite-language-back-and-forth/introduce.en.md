Lemma 26.7 provides a sufficient condition for the existence of an $(n+1)$-sequence in terms of agreement on Boolean combinations of $\Sigma_n$ and $\Pi_n$ formulas (the class $\Delta_n^n$). For languages with finitely many relation symbols and no operation symbols, if two structures agree on all $\Delta_n^n$ sentences, then there exists an $(n+1)$-sequence witnessing their back-and-forth equivalence up to level $n+1$. This lemma is a key technical step toward Fraisse's theorem, linking syntactic equivalence at a given quantifier complexity to the semantic back-and-forth relations.
