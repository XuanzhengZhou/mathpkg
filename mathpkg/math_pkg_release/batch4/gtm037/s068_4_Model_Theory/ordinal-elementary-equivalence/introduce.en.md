Lemma 26.15 characterizes elementary equivalence of ordinal structures $(\alpha, <)$: two ordinals are elementarily equivalent if and only if they are congruent modulo $\omega^\omega$, and if either is below $\omega^\omega$ then they are equal. The proof proceeds by showing that each ordinal below $\omega^\omega$ is elementarily definable -- specifically, one can define in the language of order the formulas expressing "$v_0 - v_1$ is a successor," "$v_0 - v_1$ is divisible by $\omega$," and inductively "$v_0 - v_1$ is divisible by $\omega^m$." Using these formulas, one can express that the difference of two ordinals equals any fixed ordinal $\delta < \omega^\omega$, establishing that the Cantor normal form below $\omega^\omega$ is captured by first-order logic.
