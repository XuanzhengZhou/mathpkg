The ultraproduct construction is one of the central tools of model theory. Given a family of $\mathcal{L}$-structures indexed by $I$ and an ultrafilter $F$ on $I$, the ultraproduct quotients the Cartesian product by the equivalence relation of agreeing on a set of indices belonging to $F$. Relations are interpreted via the ultrafilter: a tuple of equivalence classes satisfies a relation exactly when the componentwise condition holds on a "large" set of indices. When all factor structures are identical, the construction yields an ultrapower, which is a key ingredient in the proof of the compactness theorem via Los's theorem.
