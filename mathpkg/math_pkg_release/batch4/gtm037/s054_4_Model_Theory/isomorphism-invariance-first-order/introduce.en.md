This fundamental proposition establishes that isomorphic structures are elementarily equivalent: they satisfy exactly the same first-order sentences. More generally, the truth of any formula under an assignment is preserved when the assignment is transported through an isomorphism. The proof proceeds by induction on the complexity of terms and formulas, reflecting the compositional nature of first-order semantics. This invariance justifies why most model-theoretic notions are defined only up to isomorphism.
