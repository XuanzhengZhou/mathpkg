Let $I = \{i_1, \ldots, i_n\}$ be finite and let $F$ be an ultrafilter on $I$. Since $I = \{i_1\} \cup \cdots \cup \{i_n\} \in F$, by condition (ii) of Proposition 18.17 (the prime property of ultrafilters), one of the singletons $\{i_k\}$ must belong to $F$. Then for any $a \subseteq I$, we have $a \in F$ iff $\{i_k\} \subseteq a$ iff $i_k \in a$. Hence $F = \{a \subseteq I : i_k \in a\}$, which is the principal ultrafilter generated by $i_k$.

For the second claim: if $F$ is the principal ultrafilter generated by $i \in I$, then the map $[x]_F \mapsto x_i$ is an isomorphism from $\prod_{j \in I} \mathfrak{A}_j / F$ onto $\mathfrak{A}_i$. Indeed, $x \equiv_F y$ iff $x_i = y_i$, so the map is well-defined and injective. It is clearly surjective. That it preserves operations and relations follows directly from the coordinate-wise definitions in the ultraproduct.
