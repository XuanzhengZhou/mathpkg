This proposition records three elementary properties of filters. A proper filter (one not containing the empty set) automatically satisfies the finite intersection property: the intersection of any finitely many members is nonempty. Every filter over $I$ contains $I$ itself. And a filter contains the empty set exactly when it is the improper filter consisting of all subsets of $I$.
