A principal ultrafilter is the simplest kind of ultrafilter: it consists of all subsets that contain a particular fixed element $i \in I$. Such ultrafilters are generated by a singleton set. In model theory, ultraproducts taken with principal ultrafilters collapse to the factor structure corresponding to the generating element, making them trivial and uninteresting. The existence of nonprincipal (free) ultrafilters on infinite sets requires the Axiom of Choice and is essential for nontrivial ultraproduct constructions.
