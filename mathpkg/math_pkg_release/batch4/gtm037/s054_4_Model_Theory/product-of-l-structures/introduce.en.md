The product of a family of $\mathcal{L}$-structures generalizes the Cartesian product from algebra to first-order structures. The universe is the Cartesian product of the individual universes, and both operations and relations are defined coordinate-wise. This construction is fundamental in model theory for building new structures from existing ones, and gives rise to direct powers when all factors are identical and to binary products for two-factor cases.
