Given an ultrafilter $F$ over an index set $I$, we can define an equivalence relation on the Cartesian product of a family of structures by declaring two tuples equivalent when they agree on a "large" set of indices (one belonging to $F$). This proposition establishes that this relation is not merely an equivalence but a full congruence: it respects all operations of the language. This fact is the essential algebraic prerequisite for constructing ultraproducts, where we quotient the product by this congruence.
