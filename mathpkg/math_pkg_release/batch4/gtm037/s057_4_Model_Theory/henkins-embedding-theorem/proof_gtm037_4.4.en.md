Let $\Delta$ be the $\mathcal{L}'$-diagram of $\mathfrak{A}$, where $\mathcal{L}'$ is the $A$-expansion of $\mathcal{L}$ obtained by adding a new constant symbol $\mathbf{c}_a$ for each $a \in A$. Let

$$\Gamma = \{\exists v_0 \cdots \exists v_{m-1} \, \theta(\mathbf{c}_{b_0}, \ldots, \mathbf{c}_{b_{m-1}}) \mid m \in \omega, \, b_0, \ldots, b_{m-1} \in A, \, \theta \text{ is the conjunction of a finite subset of } \Delta\}.$$

We show that $\Gamma$ is consistent with the set of sentences true in all members of $\mathbf{K}$.

Take any finite $\Theta \subseteq \Gamma$. Let $b_0, \ldots, b_{m-1}$ be all of the new individual constants that occur in sentences of $\Theta$. Let $\mathfrak{B}$ be the substructure of $\mathfrak{A}$ generated by $\{b_0, \ldots, b_{m-1}\}$, or let $\mathfrak{B}$ be any finitely generated substructure of $\mathfrak{A}$ if $m = 0$. Set $\mathfrak{C} = (\mathfrak{B}, l_a)_{a \in A}$, where $l_a = a$ for $a$ of the form $b_i$, and $l_a$ is any element of $B$ otherwise. Since $(\mathfrak{A}, a)_{a \in A}$ is a model of $\Delta$, it follows that $\mathfrak{C}$ is a model of $\Delta \cap \Theta$.

Now by the hypothesis of the theorem, let $f$ be an embedding of $\mathfrak{B}$ into a member $\mathfrak{C}'$ of $\mathbf{K}$. It is easily verified that $(\mathfrak{C}', f l_a)_{a \in A}$ is a model of $\Theta$.

Thus by the compactness theorem, $\Gamma \cup \Delta$ has a model $(\mathfrak{D}, k_a)_{a \in A} = \mathfrak{D}$. From Proposition 19.10 we see that $k$ is an embedding of $\mathfrak{A}$ into the $\mathcal{L}$-reduct of $\mathfrak{D}$, and the $\mathcal{L}$-reduct of $\mathfrak{D}$ is (isomorphic to) a member of $\mathbf{K}$.
