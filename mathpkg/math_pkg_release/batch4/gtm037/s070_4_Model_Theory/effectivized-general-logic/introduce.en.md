An effectivized general logic strengthens the notion of a weak effectivized general logic by requiring three recursive operations on Gödel numbers of sentences. Condition (1) demands a recursive complementation function $\mathrm{Comp}_L$: given the Gödel number of a sentence $\varphi$, it effectively produces the Gödel number of a sentence whose class of models is the complement of that of $\varphi$. Condition (2) requires a recursive intersection function $\mathrm{Int}_L$ producing the Gödel number of a sentence whose models are the intersection of the models of two given sentences. Condition (3) ensures that the set of all Gödel numbers of sentences (over reducts of the universal language) is recursive, providing a syntactic decision procedure for well-formedness.
