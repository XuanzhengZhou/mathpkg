$L_{\mathrm{fo}}^{\mathrm{eff}}$ is the effectivized version of first-order logic, obtained by endowing the standard first-order logical apparatus with a recursive Gödel numbering. Starting from an effectivized first-order language $(\mathcal{L}, g)$, where $g$ provides Gödel numbers for expressions, the numbering of sentences $h$ is defined as the restriction of the extended numbering $g^{+}$ to the set $\mathrm{Sent}_{\mathcal{L}}$ of all sentences. This definition plays a key role in the effective version of Lindström's theorem, which asserts that any effectivized general logic satisfying appropriate conditions is effectively contained in $L_{\mathrm{fo}}^{\mathrm{eff}}$.
