Lindström's theorem is a fundamental characterization result in abstract model theory. It states that first-order logic $L_{\mathrm{fo}}$ is the maximal general logic (up to equivalence) that satisfies both the downward Löwenheim--Skolem theorem and the countable compactness theorem. More precisely, if $L$ is any general logic extending $L_{\mathrm{fo}}$ in which every $L$-elementary class with an infinite model has a countable model, and every countable set of $L$-sentences that is finitely satisfiable is satisfiable, then $L$ is equivalent in expressive power to first-order logic. This theorem establishes first-order logic as the unique logic satisfying these two desirable properties, explaining its privileged position in mathematical logic.
