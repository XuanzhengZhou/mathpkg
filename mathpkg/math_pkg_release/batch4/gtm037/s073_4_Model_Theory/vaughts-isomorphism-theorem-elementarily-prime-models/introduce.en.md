An elementarily prime model of a theory $\Gamma$ is one that embeds elementarily into every model of $\Gamma$. Vaught's isomorphism theorem states that for a countable language, any two such models are isomorphic. The proof constructs a back-and-forth system using $m$-atomic formulas: for two elementarily prime models $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$, one builds an $\omega$-sequence of finite partial isomorphisms where each pair of corresponding tuples satisfies the same $m$-atomic formula over $\Gamma$. This yields a total isomorphism between $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$, establishing categoricity at the level of prime models.
