For any natural number $n$, $\mathrm{Fmla}^n_{\mathcal{L}}$ denotes the set of formulas whose free variables are among $\{v_0,\ldots,v_{n-1}\}$. Given a set $\Delta$ of such formulas and an $n$-tuple $a$ from a structure $\mathfrak{A}$, we say $a$ realizes $\Delta$ in $\mathfrak{A}$ if every formula in $\Delta$ is satisfied by $a$. The structure $\mathfrak{A}$ realizes $\Delta$ when some $n$-tuple does so, and omits $\Delta$ otherwise. The $n$-type of $a$ in $\mathfrak{A}$ is the complete set of $n$-variable formulas satisfied by $a$, capturing the first-order properties of that tuple within the structure.
