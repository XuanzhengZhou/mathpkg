**Exercise 8.55** studies three-valued logic with truth values $0$ (falsity), $1$ (truth), and $2$ (indeterminate). The truth tables for negation and implication are given. Part (a) asks to show that a certain sentence is never false but not always true in three-valued logic. The exercise then describes an axiom system with 8 axiom schemas (A1)-(A8) and detachment as the only rule of inference. Subsequent parts develop the model theory for this three-valued logic, including a proof by induction relating truth values to set membership, an analog of the consistency theorem (8.29), and part (e) asks to define functional completeness for three-valued logic and show that the given connectives are not functionally complete.
