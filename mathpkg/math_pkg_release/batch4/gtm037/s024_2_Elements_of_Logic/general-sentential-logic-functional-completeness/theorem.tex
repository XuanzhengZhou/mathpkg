Let $\mathit{neg}$ be the truth function corresponding to $n$ in any sentential logic, and $\mathit{imp}$ that corresponding to $c$. Let $\mathcal{P} = (K, P, f, g)$ be a general sentential logic such that $K \cap 2 = \emptyset$, $P$ finite,
$$\mathit{neg} \in \{\mathcal{T}_{\varphi} : \varphi \text{ a sentence of } (K, 1, f, g)\},$$
and
$$\mathit{imp} \in \{\mathcal{T}_{\varphi} : \varphi \text{ a sentence of } (K, 2, f, g)\}.$$
Then $\mathcal{P}$ is functionally complete.
