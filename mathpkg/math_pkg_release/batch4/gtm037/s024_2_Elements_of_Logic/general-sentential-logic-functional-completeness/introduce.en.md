**Lemma 8.42** provides a sufficient condition for a general sentential logic to be functionally complete. If the truth functions for negation ($\mathit{neg}$) and implication ($\mathit{imp}$) can each be expressed as the truth function $\mathcal{T}_{\varphi}$ of some sentence $\varphi$ in the one-symbol and two-symbol fragments of the logic respectively, and $P$ is finite, then every truth function can be simulated. This lemma serves as the key stepping stone to prove that specific connective sets (negation with conjunction, negation with disjunction, and the Sheffer stroke) yield functional completeness.
