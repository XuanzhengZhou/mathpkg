**Exercise 8.57** provides a short introduction to intuitionistic sentential logic, following the Brouwer-Heyting-Kolmogorov (BHK) interpretation. According to intuitionism, every mathematical assertion must be expressible as "I have effected a certain construction $A$ in my mind." The exercise explains the intuitionistic meaning of the logical connectives: $\varphi \wedge \psi$ (both can be asserted), $\varphi \vee \psi$ (at least one can be asserted), $\neg \varphi$ (a construction leading from the supposition of $\varphi$ to a contradiction), and $\varphi \rightarrow \psi$ (a construction that, when joined to the construction mentioned in $\varphi$, yields the construction mentioned in $\psi$). This provides a philosophical foundation distinct from classical truth-functional semantics.
