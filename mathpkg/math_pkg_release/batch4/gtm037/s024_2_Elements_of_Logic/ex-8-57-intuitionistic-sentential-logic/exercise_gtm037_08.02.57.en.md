(Intuitionistic logic). According to intuitionism, every mathematical assertion, to be called mathematical and not theological, should be expressible in the form "I have effected a certain construction $A$ in my mind." If $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are assertions, then:

(1) We can assert $\varphi \wedge \psi$ iff both $\varphi$ and $\psi$ can be asserted.

(2) We can assert $\varphi \vee \psi$ iff at least one of the sentences $\varphi$ and $\psi$ can be asserted.

(3) We can assert $\neg \varphi$ iff we possess a construction which leads from the supposition of $\varphi$ to a contradiction.

(4) We can assert $\varphi \rightarrow \psi$ iff we possess a construction which, when joined to the construction mentioned in $\varphi$ (which is not, however, assumed to exist), leads to a construction of the sort mentioned in $\psi$.
