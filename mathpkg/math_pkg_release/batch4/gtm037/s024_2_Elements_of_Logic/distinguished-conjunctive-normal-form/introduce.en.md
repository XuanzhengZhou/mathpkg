The **distinguished conjunctive normal form** theorem states that every sentence of a sentential logic that is not a tautology can be expressed equivalently as a conjunction of disjunctions of sentential symbols or their negations. This is the dual counterpart of the distinguished disjunctive normal form, obtained by duality. The expression $\bigwedge_{i \leq p} \bigvee_{j \leq m} \psi_{ij}$ is a conjunction of $p+1$ clauses, each clause being a disjunction of $m+1$ literals, where each literal is either a sentential symbol $\langle s_j \rangle$ or its negation $\neg \langle s_j \rangle$.
