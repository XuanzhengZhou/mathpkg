**Exercise 8.54** describes an alternative approach to defining provability ($\vdash \varphi$) in which rules of inference take precedence over axioms. In a sentential logic with negation and disjunction as primitives, four rules of inference are defined: (1) Association — reassociating disjunctions, (2) Commutation — commuting the order of disjuncts, (3) Double negation — introducing or eliminating double negations in various positions, and (4) Conjunction — a rule involving triples. The exercise asks to investigate this rule-based definition of provability.
