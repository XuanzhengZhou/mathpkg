Negation and disjunction give rise to a functionally complete sentential logic. That is, if $\mathcal{P} = \langle \{n, d\}, P, f, g \rangle$ is a general sentential logic with $fn = 1$, $fd = 2$, and $g$ given by the usual truth tables for negation and disjunction, and if $P$ is finite, then $\mathcal{P}$ is functionally complete.
