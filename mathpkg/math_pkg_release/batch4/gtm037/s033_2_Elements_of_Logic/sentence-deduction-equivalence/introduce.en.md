Theorem 10.85 establishes that for sets of sentences, the two deductive relations $\vdash$ (ordinary provability) and $\vdash''$ (a restricted variant) are equivalent. The direction $\Gamma \vdash'' \varphi \Rightarrow \Gamma \vdash \varphi$ is immediate since $\Gamma\text{-Thm}'' \subseteq (\Gamma\text{-Thm}) \cap \text{Sent}$. The converse is proved by constructing a set $\Delta$ containing all formulas whose universal closures are provable under $\vdash''$ from $\Gamma$, showing that $\Delta$ includes the axioms and $\Gamma$, is closed under generalization and detachment, and therefore contains all $\Gamma$-theorems. This result is a key technical lemma used in completeness proofs, as it allows one to freely choose between different formalizations of the deduction relation.
