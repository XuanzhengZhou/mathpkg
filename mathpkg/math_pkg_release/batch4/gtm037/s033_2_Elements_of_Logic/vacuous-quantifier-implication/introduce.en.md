When a variable $\alpha$ does not occur free in a formula $\varphi$, the universal quantifier $\forall \alpha$ can be distributed over an implication $\varphi \rightarrow \psi$ to yield a logically equivalent rearrangement $(\varphi \rightarrow \forall \alpha \psi)$. This property is essential in first-order predicate logic for moving quantifiers across logical connectives and plays a crucial role in prenex normal form transformations, where quantifiers are systematically moved to the front of formulas.
