Adjacent universal quantifiers in first-order logic commute: asserting that all $\alpha$ and all $\beta$ satisfy $\varphi$ is logically equivalent to asserting that all $\beta$ and all $\alpha$ satisfy $\varphi$. This commutativity property, expressed as $\vdash \forall\alpha\forall\beta\varphi \rightarrow \forall\beta\forall\alpha\varphi$, is fundamental to the manipulation of quantified formulas. Combined with the symmetric converse, it establishes that the order of consecutive universal quantifiers is irrelevant, simplifying reasoning about formulas with multiple quantified variables.
