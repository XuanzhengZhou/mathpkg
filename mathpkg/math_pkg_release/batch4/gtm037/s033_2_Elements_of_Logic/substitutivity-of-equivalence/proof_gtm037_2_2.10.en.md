We proceed by induction on $\chi$. We may assume that $\theta \neq \chi$. If $\chi$ is atomic, then $\chi = \varphi$ and $\psi = \theta$; this case is trivial. Suppose $\chi$ is $\neg \chi'$. Then $\theta$ is of the form $\neg \theta'$, and the induction hypothesis easily gives the desired result. The induction steps involving $\chi' \to \chi''$ are handled similarly using propositional tautologies. The quantifier steps require careful treatment of variable conditions to ensure the induction goes through without variable capture.
