The substitutivity of equivalence theorem formalizes the principle of "substitution of equals for equals" within first-order predicate logic. Given a formula $\chi$ and two equivalent formulas $\varphi$ and $\psi$ (universally quantified over a list of variables that may appear free in $\varphi$ or $\psi$ but bound in $\chi$), replacing occurrences of $\varphi$ by $\psi$ in $\chi$ yields a formula $\theta$ that is provably equivalent to $\chi$. This theorem is proved by induction on the structure of $\chi$ and handles the critical issue of variable capture through the universally quantified hypothesis $\forall\alpha_0\cdots\forall\alpha_{m-1}(\varphi\leftrightarrow\psi)$.
