Corollary 10.88 refines the Generalized Deduction Theorem for sentences by allowing a possibly infinite set $\Delta$ to be reduced to a finite subset. Specifically, $\varphi$ is deducible from $\Gamma \cup \Delta$ if and only if there exists a finite conjunction of sentences from $\Delta$ whose implication to $\varphi$ is deducible from $\Gamma$ alone. This result, combined with 10.33$(ii)$, provides a crucial bridge between infinite premise sets and finite derivations in first-order logic, and is essential in compactness arguments and the proof of the completeness theorem.
