Proposition 10.90 provides equivalent characterizations of inconsistency for a set $\Gamma$ of formulas. Condition (ii) states that $\Gamma$ proves both a formula and its negation — a direct syntactic manifestation of contradiction. Condition (iii) captures the "principle of explosion": an inconsistent set proves every formula. Together with the definition of consistency (Definition 10.89), these equivalences show that consistency, the absence of a proof of a contradiction, and the existence of at least one unprovable formula are all interchangeable notions in first-order logic. This result is fundamental for the metatheory of deductive systems.
