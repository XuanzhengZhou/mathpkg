This corollary extends existential generalization to handle simultaneous substitution of multiple terms. From a formula $\varphi(\sigma_0,\dots,\sigma_{m-1})$ obtained by simultaneously substituting terms $\sigma_0,\dots,\sigma_{m-1}$ for variables $v_0,\dots,v_{m-1}$, one may infer $\exists v_0\dots\exists v_{m-1}\varphi$. Together with Corollary 10.73 (simultaneous universal instantiation), this pair of results encapsulates the essential proof-theoretic properties of the simultaneous substitution notation introduced in 10.72, which avoids variable capture by first forming a suitable bound-variable-renamed variant.
