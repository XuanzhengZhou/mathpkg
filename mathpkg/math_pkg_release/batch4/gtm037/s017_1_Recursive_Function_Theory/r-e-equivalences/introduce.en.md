Theorem 6.2 establishes six equivalent characterizations of recursively enumerable subsets of $\omega$. An r.e. set can be described as either empty or the range of an elementary, primitive recursive, or recursive function; equivalently, as the range or domain of a partial recursive function; or as a $\Sigma_1$ relation. The equivalence $(iii) \Leftrightarrow (v)$ provides the intuitive bridge: a set is r.e. precisely when it is the domain of an algorithmic procedure.
