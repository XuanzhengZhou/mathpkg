Lemma 10.58 states that if a variable occurs bound in a formula, then there exists a subformula of the form $\forall\alpha\psi$ occurring in the formula where $\alpha$ does not occur bound in $\psi$. This decomposition lemma is used in the inductive proof of the Change of Bound Variables theorem (Theorem 10.59) to peel off an innermost binding occurrence.
