Lemma 10.53 provides a sufficient condition under which a universally quantified formula implies its free substitution instance. If the variable being quantified does not occur bound in the formula, does not appear in the substituting term, and no free occurrence falls within the scope of a quantifier on a variable occurring in the term, then the universal formula implies the result of substituting the term for the variable.
