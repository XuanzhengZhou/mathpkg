Exercise 11.60 asks to use Herbrand's theorem to prove that formulas of a specific type (the "last type" described in section 10.101) are universally valid. Herbrand's theorem provides a reduction of first-order validity to propositional satisfiability and is a fundamental tool in proof theory and automated theorem proving.
