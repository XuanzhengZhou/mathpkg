This proposition collects the elementary algebraic consequences of the cylindric algebra axioms (C1)-(C7) that are used frequently without explicit reference. The nine properties describe how cylindrifications interact with the Boolean operations: (i)-(iv) concern idempotence and the range of $c_\kappa$, (v) is a duality property linking $c_\kappa$ to its conjugate, (vi) establishes additivity, (vii) gives monotonicity, (viii) shows that the complement of a cylindrification is itself a cylindrification, and (ix) is a key diagonal-cylindrification identity used in subsequent algebraic developments of $\mathrm{CA}_\alpha$.
