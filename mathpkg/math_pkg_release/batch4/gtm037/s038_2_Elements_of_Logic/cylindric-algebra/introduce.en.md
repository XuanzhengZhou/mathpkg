A cylindric algebra of dimension $\alpha$ is the abstract algebraic counterpart to first-order logic, standing in the same relationship as Boolean algebras stand to sentential logic. The structure consists of a Boolean algebra equipped with two families of additional operations: cylindrifications $c_\kappa$ (for each $\kappa < \alpha$), which algebraically model existential quantification, and diagonal elements $d_{\kappa\lambda}$, which model equality of variables. The axioms (C1)-(C7) capture the essential algebraic identities satisfied by these operations in concrete cylindric set algebras. The theory of cylindric algebras, denoted $\mathrm{CA}_\alpha$, generalizes Boolean algebra theory to the setting of first-order logic.
