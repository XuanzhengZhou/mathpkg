The extended structure $\mathfrak{A}_G^*$ is a first-order $\mathcal{L}''$-structure constructed from an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ and a frame $G$. Its universe $A_G^*$ is the union of all $G^\tau$ over all types $\tau$, collapsing the type hierarchy into a single-sorted domain. Relation symbols $R_i$ retain their original interpretation, type predicate symbols $T_i$ pick out the subsets $G^\tau$, and the new symbol $S$ encodes the membership relation between tuples and higher-type relations. This construction reduces frame semantics for higher-order logic to ordinary first-order satisfaction, forming the technical core of the completeness proof.
