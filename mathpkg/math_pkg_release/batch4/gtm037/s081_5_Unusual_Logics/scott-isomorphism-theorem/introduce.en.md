Scott's isomorphism theorem is a landmark result for infinitary logic $L_{\omega_1\omega}$. It shows that every countable structure can be characterized up to isomorphism by a single sentence of $L_{\omega_1\omega}$. The proof constructs, via a transfinite process of length $\omega_1$, Scott sentences $\varphi_a^\alpha$ that capture the back-and-forth equivalence type of the structure, which eventually stabilizes for countable structures.
