The proof of Proposition 12.19 is not present in the extracted section text. The argument is standard: let $\{I_j\}_{j \in J}$ be a nonempty family of ideals. The intersection $\bigcap_{j \in J} I_j$ is a Boolean ideal because each $I_j$ is a Boolean ideal and the intersection of Boolean ideals is a Boolean ideal. For the cylindrification condition: if $x \in \bigcap_{j \in J} I_j$, then $x \in I_j$ for each $j$, hence $c_\kappa x \in I_j$ for each $j$ (since each $I_j$ is a CA-ideal), so $c_\kappa x \in \bigcap_{j \in J} I_j$.
