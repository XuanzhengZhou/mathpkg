A homomorphism between cylindric algebras $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ of the same dimension $\alpha$ is a function that preserves all CA-operations: the Boolean operations $+$, $\cdot$, $-$, $0$, $1$, the cylindrifications $c_\kappa$, and the diagonal elements $d_{\kappa\lambda}$. This is the natural notion of structure-preserving map in the category of cylindric algebras, generalizing Boolean algebra homomorphisms.
