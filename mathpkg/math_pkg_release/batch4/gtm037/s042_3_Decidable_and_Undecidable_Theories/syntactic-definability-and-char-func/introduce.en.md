Proposition 14.2 establishes that in any theory $\Gamma$ containing $\neg(\mathbf{0} = \mathbf{1})$, a relation $R$ is syntactically definable if and only if its characteristic function $\chi_R$ is syntactically definable. This equivalence connects the definability of relations with the definability of functions, and is a key step in showing that syntactic definability in suitable theories captures the notion of recursiveness. The proof constructs a formula for $\chi_R$ from a formula defining $R$, and vice versa, using the fact that $\mathbf{0} \neq \mathbf{1}$ is provable in $\Gamma$.
