The language $\mathcal{L}_{\text{nos}}$ is the fixed first-order language used for studying syntactic definability of number-theoretic functions and relations. It contains a constant symbol $O$ (for zero), a unary operation symbol $s$ (for successor), and binary operation symbols $+$ and $\cdot$ (for addition and multiplication). The numerals $\mathbf{n}$ are defined as the $n$-fold application of $s$ to $O$, and an abbreviation $\sigma \leq \tau$ provides a definable ordering relation. This language forms the basis for formalizing arithmetic and studying implicit definability notions in number theories.
