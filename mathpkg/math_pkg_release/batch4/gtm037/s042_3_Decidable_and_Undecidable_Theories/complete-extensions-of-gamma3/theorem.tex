Let $\Gamma_3$ be the pure theory of equality. For each $m \in \omega \setminus \{1\}$, let $\Gamma_3^m$ be the set of all sentences $\varphi$ such that
$$\{\varepsilon_m, \neg \varepsilon_{m+1}\} \models \varphi,$$
and let $\Gamma_3^\infty$ be the set of all sentences $\varphi$ such that
$$\{\varepsilon_m : m \in \omega \setminus \{1\}\} \models \varphi.$$

(i) The theories $\Gamma_3^m$ and $\Gamma_3^\infty$ are each complete, consistent, and decidable.

(ii) The theories $\Gamma_3^m$ and $\Gamma_3^\infty$ constitute all the complete and consistent extensions of $\Gamma_3$.
