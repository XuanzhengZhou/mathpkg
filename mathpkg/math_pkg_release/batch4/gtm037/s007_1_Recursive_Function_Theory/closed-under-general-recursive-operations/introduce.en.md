A class $A$ of number-theoretic functions is said to be **closed under general recursive operations** if it contains the successor function $s$ and all projection functions $U_i^n$, and is closed under the three fundamental operations: composition, primitive recursion, and minimalization (applied to special functions). Equivalently, $A$ is a superset of the class of general recursive functions. This notion provides a convenient way to verify that a given collection of functions includes all general recursive functions by checking closure under these generating operations.
