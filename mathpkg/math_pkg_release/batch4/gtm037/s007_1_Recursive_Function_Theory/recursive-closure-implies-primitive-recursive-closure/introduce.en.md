**Theorem 3.2** states that closure under general recursive operations implies closure under primitive recursive operations. Since primitive recursive operations (composition and primitive recursion) are a subset of general recursive operations (which also include minimalization), this result is essentially immediate from the definitions. An important corollary is that every primitive recursive function is general recursive. This establishes the proper containment: the primitive recursive functions form a subclass of the general recursive functions, and later results will show the inclusion is strict.
