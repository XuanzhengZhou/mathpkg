A substructure of an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{B}$ is a structure $\mathfrak{A}$ whose universe is a subset of $B$ and whose operations and relations are the restrictions of those of $\mathfrak{B}$. Equivalently, $\mathfrak{A}$ is a substructure of $\mathfrak{B}$ if the inclusion map is an embedding. When there are no operation symbols, every nonempty subset yields a substructure; with operations, the subset must be closed under them.
