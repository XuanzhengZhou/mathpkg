This exercise illustrates a limitation of the ultraproduct construction: the ultraproduct of the ring of integers $\mathbb{Z}$ over a nonprincipal ultrafilter on $\omega$ is an integral domain but fails to be a unique factorization domain. While each factor $\mathbb{Z}$ is a UFD, the ultraproduct loses this property, demonstrating that the first-order theory of rings cannot axiomatize the UFD property (since ultraproducts preserve first-order properties).
