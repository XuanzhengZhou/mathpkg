**Proof.** 1. Let $\mathfrak{w}_0 \in B_2$. Then there exists a $\mathfrak{z}_0 \in B_1$ with $g(\mathfrak{z}_0) = \mathfrak{w}_0$. From Theorem 7.5, $M_g \neq 0$ on $B_1$, and therefore there are open neighborhoods $U(\mathfrak{z}_0) \subset B_1$, $V(\mathfrak{w}_0) \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ such that $g: U \to V$ is biholomorphic. But then $V = g(U) \subset g(B_1) = B_2$; that is, $\mathfrak{w}_0$ is an interior point.

2. From (1), for each $\mathfrak{w}_0 \in B_2$ there exists an open neighborhood $V(\mathfrak{w}_0) \subset B_2$ such that $g^{-1}|V$ is holomorphic. Therefore $g^{-1}$ is holomorphic on $B_2$. $\square$
