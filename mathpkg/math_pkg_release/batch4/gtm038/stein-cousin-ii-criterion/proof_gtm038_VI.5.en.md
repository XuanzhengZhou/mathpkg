**Proof.** The second Cousin problem on a Stein manifold $ is solvable if and only if a certain topological condition in ^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$ is satisfied. From the exponential sheaf sequence, the obstruction to solving the second Cousin problem for a Cousin-II distribution given by transition functions {ij} \in \mathcal{O}^*(U_i \cap U_j)$ is the image of the Čech 1-cocycle $\{g_{ij}\}$ under the connecting homomorphism $\delta: H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*) \to H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$. The Cousin-II distribution is solvable iff $\delta(\{g_{ij}\}) = 0$. $\square$
