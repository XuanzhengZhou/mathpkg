**Proof.** If $f$ is holomorphic at $\mathfrak{z}_0$, then $f$ is complex differentiable at $\mathfrak{z}_0$. By Theorem 2.2, $f$ is continuous at $\mathfrak{z}_0$. Since holomorphy at a point implies complex differentiability, continuity follows immediately. $\square$
