**Proof (Oka's solution of Levi's problem).** Levi's problem asks: is every pseudoconvex domain a domain of holomorphy? Oka proved this for =2$; the general case was solved by Oka, Bremermann, and Norguet. The proof shows that a pseudoconvex domain $ can be exhausted by analytic polyhedra:  = \bigcup_{\nu} P_\nu$ where each \nu = \{\mathfrak{z} : |f_{\nu,1}(\mathfrak{z})| < 1, \ldots, |f_{\nu,k_\nu}(\mathfrak{z})| < 1\}$ with {\nu,j}$ holomorphic in $. Each analytic polyhedron is a domain of holomorphy. The construction uses the solution of the Levi problem on strongly pseudoconvex domains and approximates a general pseudoconvex domain from inside by strongly pseudoconvex ones. $\square$
