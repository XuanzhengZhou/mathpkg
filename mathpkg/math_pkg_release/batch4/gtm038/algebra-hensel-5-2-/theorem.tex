Let $R$ be a local $\mathbb{C}$-algebra, $\rho: R[X] \rightarrow \mathbb{C}[X]$ the mapping given above. $R$ is called henselian if there exist normalized polynomials $f_1, f_2 \in R[X]$ with $\rho(f_1) = g_1, \rho(f_2) = g_2$ and $f = f_1 \cdot f_2$, whenever $f \in R[X]$ is a normalized polynomial and $\rho(f) = g_1 \cdot g_2$ is a decomposition of $\rho(f)$ into two relatively prime normalized polynomials $g_1, g_2 \in \mathbb{C}[X]$.
