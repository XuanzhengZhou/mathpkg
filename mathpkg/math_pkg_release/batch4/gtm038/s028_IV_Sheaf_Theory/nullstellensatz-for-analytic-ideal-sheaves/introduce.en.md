This proposition establishes that for the ideal sheaf $\mathcal{I}(A)$ of an analytic set $A \subset B$, taking the zero set $N(\mathcal{I}(A))$ recovers $A$, and this coincides with the support of the quotient sheaf $\mathcal{O}/\mathcal{I}(A)$.

The statement is a sheaf-theoretic formulation of the relationship between analytic sets and ideal sheaves. However, the reverse direction does not hold: there exist ideal sheaves $\mathcal{J}$ for which $\mathcal{I}(N(\mathcal{J})) \neq \mathcal{J}$, illustrating that not every ideal sheaf is the full ideal sheaf of its zero set.

This relates to the theory of coherent ideal sheaves and the distinction between radical ideal sheaves and general ideal sheaves in complex analytic geometry.
