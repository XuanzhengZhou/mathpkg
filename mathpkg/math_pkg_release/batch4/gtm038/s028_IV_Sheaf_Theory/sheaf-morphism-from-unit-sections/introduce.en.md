This proposition establishes the equivalence between analytic sheaf morphisms $\varphi: q\mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{S}$ from a free sheaf $q\mathcal{O}$ and $q$-tuples of global sections $(s_1, \ldots, s_q)$ of $\mathcal{S}$.

Given unit sections $e_i$ of $q\mathcal{O}$ (with 1 in the $i$-th component), the images $s_i = \varphi \circ e_i$ completely determine $\varphi$ via the formula $\varphi(a_1, \ldots, a_q) = \sum_i a_i s_i(\mathfrak{z})$. Conversely, any $q$ sections $s_i \in \Gamma(B, \mathcal{S})$ define a unique sheaf morphism by this formula.

This correspondence is fundamental for the definition of finitely generated and coherent sheaves, where one studies epimorphisms from free sheaves $q\mathcal{O}$ onto a given sheaf.
