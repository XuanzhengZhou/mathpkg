This proposition states that for a finite direct sum of analytic sheaves $\mathcal{S}_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathcal{S}_\ell$, each canonical projection $p_i$ onto the $i$-th summand is a sheaf epimorphism.

This is a basic structural property of the direct sum construction for analytic sheaves. It follows directly from the definition of the direct sum: at each stalk, the projection is surjective onto the $i$-th component. Combined with the dual statement about canonical injections (Proposition 6), this establishes the direct sum as both a product and coproduct in the category of analytic sheaves.
