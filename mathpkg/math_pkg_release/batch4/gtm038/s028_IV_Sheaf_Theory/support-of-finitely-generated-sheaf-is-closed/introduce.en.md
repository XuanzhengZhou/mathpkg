**Theorem 4.1** states that the support of any finitely generated analytic sheaf is a closed subset of the base region $B \subset \mathbb{C}^n$.

The proof shows that the complement $B \setminus \operatorname{Supp}(\mathcal{S})$ is open. Given a point $\mathfrak{z}_0$ outside the support (where the stalk is zero), one uses a local sheaf epimorphism $\varphi: q\mathcal{O}|W \twoheadrightarrow \mathcal{S}|W$ and the generating sections $s_i$ to show that all stalks in a neighborhood of $\mathfrak{z}_0$ must also vanish, establishing openness of the complement.

This result is used in the study of analytic sets: for an analytic set $A \subset B$, the ideal sheaf $\mathcal{I}(A)$ yields $\operatorname{Supp}(\mathcal{O}/\mathcal{I}(A)) = A$, recovering $A$ as the support of a finitely generated sheaf.
