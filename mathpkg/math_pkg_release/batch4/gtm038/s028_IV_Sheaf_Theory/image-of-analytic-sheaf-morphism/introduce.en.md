**Image of an analytic sheaf morphism** (Def. 3.2) defines the subsheaf $\operatorname{Im}\varphi \subset \mathcal{S}_2$ formed by the union over all stalks of the images of the stalk homomorphisms $\varphi_{\mathfrak{z}}$.

This is part of the general theory of sheaf morphisms developed in Chapter IV of Grauert and Fritzsche's *Several Complex Variables*. The image sheaf inherits an analytic structure from the target sheaf $\mathcal{S}_2$, and the induced mapping $\varphi: \mathcal{S}_1 \to \operatorname{Im}\varphi$ is a sheaf epimorphism onto its image.

The definition is fundamental for the first isomorphism theorem for sheaves (Theorem 3.2) and for studying exact sequences of analytic sheaves.
