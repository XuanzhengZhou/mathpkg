**Theorem 4.9** extends the coherence of $\mathcal{R}/\mathcal{I}$ as an $\mathcal{R}$-module (which follows from Theorem 4.6) to coherence as a sheaf of $\mathbb{C}$-algebras.

The additional verification needed is that for any $(\mathcal{R}/\mathcal{I})$-module homomorphism from a free $(\mathcal{R}/\mathcal{I})$-sheaf, its kernel is finitely generated over $\mathcal{R}/\mathcal{I}$. The proof lifts such a homomorphism to an $\mathcal{R}$-module homomorphism via the canonical projection $\pi: \mathcal{R} \to \mathcal{R}/\mathcal{I}$, uses the coherence of $\mathcal{R}$ to obtain $\mathcal{R}$-generators for the lifted kernel, and then projects back to obtain $(\mathcal{R}/\mathcal{I})$-generators.

This result is important for the structure theory of coherent sheaves of algebras, particularly in applications to analytic geometry where $\mathcal{R} = \mathcal{O}$ and $\mathcal{I}$ is a coherent ideal sheaf.
