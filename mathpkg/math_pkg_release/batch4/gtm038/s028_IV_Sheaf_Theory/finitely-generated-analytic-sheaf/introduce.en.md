A **finitely generated analytic sheaf** (Def. 4.1) is an analytic sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ that is locally a quotient of a free sheaf $q\mathcal{O}$ of finite rank. At each point $\mathfrak{z} \in B$, the stalk $\mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}}$ is generated as an $\mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{z}}$-module by $q$ sections $s_1, \ldots, s_q$ defined in a neighborhood $W(\mathfrak{z})$.

This definition is the sheaf-theoretic analog of a finitely generated module over a ring. Finite generation is a necessary condition for coherence: every coherent sheaf is finitely generated, but the converse is false. Coherence additionally requires that the kernel of any homomorphism from a free sheaf is also finitely generated.

The concept is central to Oka's Coherence Theorem and the theory of Stein manifolds developed later in the book.
