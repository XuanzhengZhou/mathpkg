**Theorem 4.4** establishes that coherence passes to quotients: in a right-exact sequence $\mathcal{S}^* \to \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{S}^{**} \to 0$, if the first two terms are coherent, so is the third.

The proof is in two parts: (1) surjectivity of $p$ immediately implies $\mathcal{S}^{**}$ is finitely generated; (2) for an arbitrary homomorphism $\varepsilon^{**}: q^{**}\mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{S}^{**}$, one lifts it to $\varepsilon: q^{**}\mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{S}$ and uses the coherence of $\mathcal{S}^*$ to show that $\operatorname{Ker}\varepsilon^{**}$ is finitely generated.

This theorem, together with Theorem 4.3, shows that coherence is preserved under extensions and quotients in the category of analytic sheaves.
