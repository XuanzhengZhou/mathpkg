**Theorem 4.3** is one of the fundamental stability properties of coherent sheaves: in a short exact sequence $0 \to \mathcal{S}^* \to \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{S}^{**} \to 0$, if the outer terms $\mathcal{S}^*$ and $\mathcal{S}^{**}$ are coherent, then the middle term $\mathcal{S}$ is also coherent.

The proof establishes that $\mathcal{S}$ is finitely generated and that the kernel of any homomorphism from a free sheaf $q\mathcal{O}$ to $\mathcal{S}$ is finitely generated. This uses the coherence of $\mathcal{S}^*$ and $\mathcal{S}^{**}$ together with diagram chasing.

Together with Theorems 4.4, 4.6, and 4.7, this establishes that the category of coherent sheaves is an abelian subcategory of the category of analytic sheaves.
