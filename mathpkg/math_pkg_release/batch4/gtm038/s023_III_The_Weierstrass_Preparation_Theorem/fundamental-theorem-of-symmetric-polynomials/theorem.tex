Let $p(X_1, \ldots, X_s)$ be a symmetric polynomial with integer coefficients. Then there is exactly one polynomial $Q(Y_1, \ldots, Y_s)$ with integer coefficients such that
$$p(X_1, \ldots, X_s) = Q(\sigma_1(X_1, \ldots, X_s), \ldots, \sigma_s(X_1, \ldots, X_s))$$
holds. The quantity $\Delta(f) := Q(a_1, \ldots, a_s)$ is called the discriminant of $f(X)$.
