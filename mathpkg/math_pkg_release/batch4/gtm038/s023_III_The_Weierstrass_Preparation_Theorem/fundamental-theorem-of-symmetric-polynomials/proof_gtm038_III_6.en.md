The text notes: "In algebra (see van der Waerden I) one proves:" and refers the reader to the standard algebraic proof. The proof proceeds by induction on the degree and the number of variables, using the lexicographic ordering of monomials. For any symmetric polynomial $p$, one considers its leading monomial $X_1^{k_1} \cdots X_s^{k_s}$ with $k_1 \geq k_2 \geq \cdots \geq k_s$ (by symmetry). Then $p - \sigma_1^{k_1 - k_2} \sigma_2^{k_2 - k_3} \cdots \sigma_s^{k_s}$ has a strictly smaller leading monomial. By induction, the process terminates, yielding the desired representation $Q$. Uniqueness follows from the algebraic independence of the elementary symmetric polynomials.
