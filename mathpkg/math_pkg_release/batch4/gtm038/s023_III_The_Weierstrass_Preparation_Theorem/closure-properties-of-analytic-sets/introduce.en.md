Theorem 6.14 establishes the basic algebraic closure properties of analytic subsets of a domain $G \subset \mathbb{C}^n$. The empty set and the whole domain are trivially analytic. The class of analytic sets is closed under finite unions, finite intersections, and arbitrary intersections. Finite unions are proved using products of defining functions, while intersections are handled by collecting all defining functions. Arbitrary intersections follow from the local nature of the definition of analytic sets.
