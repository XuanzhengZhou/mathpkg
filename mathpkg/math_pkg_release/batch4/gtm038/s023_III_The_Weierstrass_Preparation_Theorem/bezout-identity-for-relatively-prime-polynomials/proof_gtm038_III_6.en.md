The proof proceeds in the same manner as the analogous statement for polynomials over a field; the text notes only "In the same way one proves" before stating Theorem 6.8. A full proof would use the fact that $A$ is a Henselian ring to construct the Bezout coefficients $q_1, q_2$ via the Euclidean algorithm applied to the relatively prime polynomials $\omega_1, \omega_2 \in A[u]$, leveraging that the resultant of $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$ is a nonzero element of $A$.
