A symmetric polynomial in $s$ variables is a polynomial with integer coefficients that remains unchanged when any two variables are swapped. The most important examples are the elementary symmetric polynomials $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_s$, defined by
$$\sigma_v(X_1, \ldots, X_s) := \sum_{1 \leq i_1 < \cdots < i_v \leq s} X_{i_1} \cdots X_{i_v}.$$
Symmetric polynomials play a central role in the theory of algebraic equations and the construction of the discriminant.
