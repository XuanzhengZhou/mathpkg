Theorem 6.19 provides a fundamental lower bound: an analytic set defined by $n-k$ holomorphic equations in $\mathbb{C}^n$ has every irreducible component of complex dimension at least $k$. In other words, each equation can reduce the dimension by at most one. This follows immediately from Theorem 6.18 by induction: starting from $G$ itself (dimension $n$), each successive intersection with the zero set of $f_i$ drops the dimension by at most one, so after $n-k$ equations, the resulting set has dimension at least $k$.
