Let $\omega(u, \mathfrak{z})$ be a pseudopolynomial of degree $s$, and set
$$M_{\omega} := \{(u, \mathfrak{z}) : \omega(u, \mathfrak{z}) = 0\}, \quad D_{\omega} := \{\mathfrak{z} : \Delta_{\omega}(\mathfrak{z}) = 0\}.$$
Then $M_{\omega}$ and $D_{\omega}$ are analytic sets and:

1. For $\mathfrak{z}_0 \in G - D_{\omega}$ there exists an open neighborhood $U(\mathfrak{z}_0) \subset G - D_{\omega}$ and holomorphic functions $f_1, \ldots, f_s$ on $U$ with $f_v(\mathfrak{z}) \neq f_\mu(\mathfrak{z})$ for $v \neq \mu$ and $\mathfrak{z} \in U$, such that $\omega(u, \mathfrak{z}) = (u - f_1(\mathfrak{z})) \cdots (u - f_s(\mathfrak{z}))$ for all $\mathfrak{z} \in U$.

2. The points of $D_{\omega}$ are branch points, i.e., above a point $\mathfrak{z} \in D_{\omega}$ there always lie fewer than $s$ points of the set $M_{\omega}$.
