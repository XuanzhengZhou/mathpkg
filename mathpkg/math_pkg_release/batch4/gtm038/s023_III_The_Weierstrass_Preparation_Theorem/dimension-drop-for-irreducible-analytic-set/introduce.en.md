Theorem 6.18 is a fundamental result about the dimension of analytic sets: intersecting an irreducible analytic set $M$ with the zero set of a holomorphic function $f$ that does not vanish identically on $M$ always reduces the complex dimension by exactly one. Moreover, every irreducible component of the intersection has the same dimension $\dim_{\mathbb{C}}(M) - 1$. This is an exact analogue of the algebraic geometry principle that a hypersurface section of an irreducible variety has codimension one, and is essential for inductive arguments in the theory of analytic sets.
