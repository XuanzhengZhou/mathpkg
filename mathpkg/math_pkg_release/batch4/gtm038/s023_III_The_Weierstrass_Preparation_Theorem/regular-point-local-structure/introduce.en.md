Theorem 6.15 states that at a regular (smooth) point of real dimension $2k$, an analytic set is locally biholomorphically equivalent to an open subset of $\mathbb{C}^k$. The proof constructs a biholomorphic map $F$ by completing a set of $n-k$ defining holomorphic functions with $k$ additional coordinate functions, forming a map with non-vanishing Jacobian determinant. This shows that the regular locus of an analytic set carries a natural complex manifold structure of complex dimension $k$.
