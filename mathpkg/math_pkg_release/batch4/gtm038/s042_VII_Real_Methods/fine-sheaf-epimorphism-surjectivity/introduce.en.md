This theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of fine sheaves: any epimorphism of fine sheaves of $\mathcal{T}$-modules induces a surjective map on global sections. In other words, every global section of the target sheaf lifts to a global section of the source sheaf. The proof uses the existence of partitions of unity subordinate to a locally finite covering, which is guaranteed by the paracompactness of $X$ and the fineness of the sheaves. This result underpins the de Rham and Dolbeault theorems by showing that the corresponding sheaf sequences are exact at the level of global sections.
