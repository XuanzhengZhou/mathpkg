Theorem 5.5 is a global $d''$-Poincaré lemma on Stein manifolds: every $d''$-closed $(p,q)$-form with $q \geqslant 1$ is globally $d''$-exact. Equivalently, the Dolbeault cohomology groups vanish: $H^{p,q}(X) = 0$ for all $p$ and all $q \geqslant 1$ when $X$ is Stein. The proof is a direct consequence of Cartan's Theorem B (which gives $H^q(X, \Omega^p) = 0$ for $q \geqslant 1$) combined with the Dolbeault isomorphism $H^{p,q}(X) \simeq H^q(X, \Omega^p)$.
