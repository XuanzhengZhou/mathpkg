**Proof.** By Cartan's Theorem B, $H^q(X, \Omega^p) = 0$ for all $q \geqslant 1$ (since $\Omega^p$ is a coherent analytic sheaf on the Stein manifold $X$). By the Dolbeault isomorphism theorem (Theorem 5.4), $H^{p,q}(X) \simeq H^q(X, \Omega^p)$. Therefore $H^{p,q}(X) = 0$ for all $q \geqslant 1$. By definition of the Dolbeault groups, this means that for every $d''$-closed $(p,q)$-form $\varphi$ with $q \geqslant 1$, there exists a $(p,q-1)$-form $\psi$ such that $d''\psi = \varphi$.
