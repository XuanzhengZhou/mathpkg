Theorem 5.3 states that the sequence of sheaves
$$0 \rightarrow \Omega^p \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} \mathcal{A}^{p,0} \xrightarrow{d''} \mathcal{A}^{p,1} \xrightarrow{d''} \mathcal{A}^{p,2} \xrightarrow{d''} \cdots$$
is exact on any complex manifold $X$. Here $\Omega^p$ is the sheaf of holomorphic $(p,0)$-forms, $\mathcal{A}^{p,q}$ is the sheaf of smooth $(p,q)$-forms, $\varepsilon$ is the canonical inclusion, and $d''$ is the Dolbeault operator. Exactness at $\mathcal{A}^{p,0}$ means a smooth $(p,0)$-form is $d''$-closed iff it is holomorphic; exactness at $\mathcal{A}^{p,q}$ for $q \geqslant 1$ is the local $d''$-Poincaré lemma (Dolbeault-Grothendieck lemma).
