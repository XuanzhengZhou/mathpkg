The Dolbeault isomorphism theorem (Theorem 5.4) states that the Dolbeault cohomology groups $H^{p,q}(X)$ are naturally isomorphic to the sheaf cohomology groups $H^q(X, \Omega^p)$ for all $q \geqslant 0$. This result follows from the fact that the sheaves $\mathcal{A}^{p,q}$ of smooth $(p,q)$-forms are fine (hence acyclic for the global section functor), and the sequence $0 \to \Omega^p \to \mathcal{A}^{p,0} \to \mathcal{A}^{p,1} \to \cdots$ is an exact resolution of $\Omega^p$. The isomorphism is obtained by applying the general theory of acyclic resolutions to this Dolbeault resolution.
