**Proof.** Let $(\tilde{P}, \tilde{H}) = (g(P), g(H))$, $g: P \to \mathbb{C}^n$ be biholomorphic. Then $f \circ g$ is holomorphic in $H$ and by Theorem 5.5 of Chapter I there is exactly one holomorphic function $F^*$ on $P$ with $F^*|H = f \circ g$. Let $F = F^* \circ g^{-1}$. Then $F$ is holomorphic in $\tilde{P}$, $F|\tilde{H} = f$, and the uniqueness of the continuation follows from the uniqueness of $F^*$. $\square$
