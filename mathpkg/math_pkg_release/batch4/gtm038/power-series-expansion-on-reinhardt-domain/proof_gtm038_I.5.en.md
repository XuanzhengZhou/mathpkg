**Proof.** If $\mathfrak{z}_0 \in G$ then there is a $\mathfrak{z}_1 \in G$ with $|z_j^{(0)}| < |z_j^{(1)}|$ for $j = 1, \ldots, n$; therefore $\mathfrak{z}_0 \in P_{\mathfrak{z}_1}$. Let $\operatorname{ch}(f|T_{\mathfrak{z}_1})(\mathfrak{z}) = \sum_{v=0}^{\infty} a_v \mathfrak{z}^v$ for $\mathfrak{z} \in P_{\mathfrak{z}_1}$. The coefficients $a_v$ are those of the Taylor series about $0$; they do not depend on $\mathfrak{z}_1$. Since $\mathfrak{z}_0$ was arbitrary it follows that the Taylor series of $f$ about $0$ converges in all of $G$. It defines a holomorphic function $g$, which coincides with $f$ near the origin. By the uniqueness theorem, $f = g$ on $G$. $\square$
