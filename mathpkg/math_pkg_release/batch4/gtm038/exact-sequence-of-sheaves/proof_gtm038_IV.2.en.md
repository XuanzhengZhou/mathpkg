**Proof.** A sequence $\mathcal{F} \xrightarrow{\alpha} \mathcal{G} \xrightarrow{\beta} \mathcal{H}$ of sheaf morphisms is exact at $\mathcal{G}$ if $\operatorname{im} \alpha = \ker \beta$ as sheaves. Exactness can be checked on stalks: the sequence is exact if and only if the induced sequence of stalks $\mathcal{F}_x \to \mathcal{G}_x \to \mathcal{H}_x$ is exact for every  \in X$. This follows from the fact that the stalk functor is exact and faithful. $\square$
