**Proof (sketch).** Oka's Coherence Theorem states that the sheaf of ideals of an analytic set is coherent. The proof proceeds by induction on the dimension. Locally, an analytic set is given as the zero set of finitely many holomorphic functions. By the Weierstrass preparation theorem, one can represent the local ideal as generated by a Weierstrass polynomial. The coherence follows from the fact that the ring of convergent power series is Noetherian (a consequence of the Weierstrass preparation theorem), so local ideals are finitely generated, and the syzygy relations are also finitely generated. $\square$
