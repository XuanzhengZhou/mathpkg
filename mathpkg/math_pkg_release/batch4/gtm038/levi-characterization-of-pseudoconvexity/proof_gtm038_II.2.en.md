**Proof.** A domain  \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ with ^2$ boundary is pseudoconvex if and only if the Levi form (\rho)(\mathfrak{z}_0)(t) = \sum \frac{\partial^2 \rho}{\partial z_j \partial \bar{z}_k}(\mathfrak{z}_0) t_j \bar{t}_k$ is positive semidefinite on the complex tangent space {\mathfrak{z}_0}^{\mathbb{C}}(\partial G)$ for every $\mathfrak{z}_0 \in \partial G$, where $\rho$ is a defining function for $ (i.e.,  = \{\rho < 0\}$, $\nabla\rho \neq 0$ on $\partial G$). The proof shows that pseudoconvexity (which is a geometric property about Hartogs figures) is equivalent to this differential condition. The forward direction uses the fact that if the Levi form has a negative eigenvalue, local biholomorphic changes of coordinates produce a Hartogs figure that violates the continuity theorem. The converse uses the fact that a positive semidefinite Levi form guarantees that the boundary is locally biholomorphically convex. $\square$
