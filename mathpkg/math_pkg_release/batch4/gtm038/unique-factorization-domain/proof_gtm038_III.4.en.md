**Proof.** The ring $\mathbb{C}\{z_1,\ldots,z_n\}$ is a unique factorization domain (UFD). Since it is Noetherian, every non-unit can be factored into indecomposable elements. The uniqueness of the factorization follows from the fact that $\mathbb{C}\{z\}$ is a regular local ring (hence a UFD by the Auslander-Buchsbaum theorem), or more directly by induction on $ using the Weierstrass preparation theorem and Gauss's lemma. $\square$
