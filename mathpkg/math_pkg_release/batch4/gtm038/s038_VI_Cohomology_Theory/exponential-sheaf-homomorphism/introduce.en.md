The exponential sheaf homomorphism $\exp: \mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{O}^*$ is
a fundamental map in the theory of several complex variables. It is induced by
the local $\mathbb{Z}$-module homomorphisms $f \mapsto e^{2\pi i f}$ from the
additive group of holomorphic functions to the multiplicative group of
non-vanishing holomorphic functions (written additively). The exponential sheaf
sequence $0 \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathcal{O} \xrightarrow{\exp}
\mathcal{O}^* \to 0$ is a central tool in cohomology theory, linking the
Cousin II problem to $H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$ via the Chern class homomorphism.
