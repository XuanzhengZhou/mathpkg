Let $X$ be an $n$-dimensional complex manifold with structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}$.
For every open set $U \subset X$ there is an associated multiplicative abelian group
$$M_U = \{f : f \text{ is holomorphic on } U \text{ and } f(x) \neq 0 \text{ for } x \in U\}.$$
$M_U$ becomes a $\mathbb{Z}$-module (with $n \cdot f := f^n$), and together with the
usual restriction mappings $r_V^U: M_U \rightarrow M_V$ yields a pre-sheaf of
$\mathbb{Z}$-modules.

The corresponding sheaf of $\mathbb{Z}$-modules $\mathcal{O}^*$ is called the
\textbf{sheaf of germs of non-vanishing holomorphic functions}.
The group operation in $\mathcal{O}^*$ and in the derived modules is written additively.
