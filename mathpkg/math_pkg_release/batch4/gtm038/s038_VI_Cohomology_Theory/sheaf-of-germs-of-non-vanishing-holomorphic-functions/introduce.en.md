On a complex manifold $X$ with structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}$, the sheaf
$\mathcal{O}^*$ of germs of non-vanishing holomorphic functions is the
sheafification of the presheaf $M_U$ of nowhere-zero holomorphic functions on
open sets $U \subset X$. Each $M_U$ is a multiplicative abelian group, made into
a $\mathbb{Z}$-module via $n \cdot f := f^n$, and the group operation in
$\mathcal{O}^*$ is written additively. This sheaf plays a fundamental role in
the cohomological formulation of the Cousin II problem, as its first cohomology
group classifies Cousin II distributions modulo solvability.
