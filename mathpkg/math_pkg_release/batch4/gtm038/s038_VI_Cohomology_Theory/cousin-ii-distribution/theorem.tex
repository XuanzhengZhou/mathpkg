\textbf{Definition 4.1.} A system $(U_t, f_t)_{t \in I}$ is called a
\textbf{Cousin II distribution} on $X$ if:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{U} = (U_t)_{t \in I}$ is an open covering of $X$.
\item $f_t$ is holomorphic on $U_t$ and vanishes identically nowhere.
\item On $U_{t_0 t_1}$ there is a nowhere vanishing holomorphic function
$h_{t_0 t_1}$ such that
$$f_{t_0} = h_{t_0 t_1} \cdot f_{t_1}$$
on $U_{t_0 t_1}$.
\end{enumerate}

A \textbf{solution} of this Cousin II distribution is a holomorphic function
$f$ on $X$ such that
$$f_t = h_t \cdot f$$
with nowhere vanishing holomorphic functions $h_t$ on $U_t$.

The functions $h_{t_0 t_1}$ are uniquely determined by the distribution
$(U_t, f_t)_{t \in I}$.
