A Cousin II distribution on a complex manifold $X$ is the multiplicative
analogue of the classical Cousin I problem. While Cousin I concerns the
construction of a meromorphic function with prescribed principal parts (additive
problem), Cousin II asks for a holomorphic function with prescribed zeroes (up
to nowhere-vanishing factors). The data consist of an open covering
$\mathfrak{U} = (U_t)$ and local nowhere-vanishing holomorphic functions $f_t$
on $U_t$, compatible up to transition functions $h_{t_0 t_1}$. These transition
functions form a $1$-cocycle in $Z^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$, whose
cohomology class controls solvability.
