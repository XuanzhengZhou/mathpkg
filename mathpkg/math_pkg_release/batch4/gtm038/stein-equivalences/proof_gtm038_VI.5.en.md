**Proof.** The following are equivalent for a complex manifold $: (i) $ is Stein; (ii) ^q(X, \mathcal{F}) = 0$ for all coherent sheaves $\mathcal{F}$ and  \geqslant 1$ (Theorem B); (iii) $ is holomorphically convex and holomorphically separable; (iv) $ is a closed complex submanifold of some $\mathbb{C}^N$. The proof of (i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) is Theorem B. (ii) $\Rightarrow$ (iii): Cohomology vanishing implies the existence of enough holomorphic functions. (iii) $\Rightarrow$ (iv): By the embedding theorem (Remmert, Bishop, Narasimhan), a Stein manifold of dimension $ embeds as a closed submanifold of $\mathbb{C}^{2n+1}$. (iv) $\Rightarrow$ (i): A closed submanifold of $\mathbb{C}^N$ is Stein. $\square$
