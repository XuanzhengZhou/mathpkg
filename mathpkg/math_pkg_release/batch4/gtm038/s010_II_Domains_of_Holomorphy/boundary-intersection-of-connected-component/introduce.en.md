Theorem 2.1 describes a fundamental boundary property for regions and domains in $\mathbb{C}^n$. If a region $B$ and a domain $G$ intersect but neither contains the other's complement, then the intersection of $G$ with the boundary of any connected component $Q$ of $B \cap G$ must meet the boundary of $B$. This result plays a key role in the theory of domains of holomorphy and pseudoconvexity.
