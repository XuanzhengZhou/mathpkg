Let $M \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ be an arbitrary subset. Then:
1. $M \subset \hat{M}_e$.
2. $\hat{M}_e$ is closed and geometrically convex.
3. $\widehat{\hat{M}_e}_e = \hat{M}_e$.
4. Let $M_1 \subset M_2 \subset \mathbb{R}^2$. Then $(\hat{M}_1)_e \subset (\hat{M}_2)_e$.
5. If $M$ is closed and geometrically convex, then $M = \hat{M}_e$.
6. If $M$ is bounded, then $\hat{M}_e$ is also bounded.
