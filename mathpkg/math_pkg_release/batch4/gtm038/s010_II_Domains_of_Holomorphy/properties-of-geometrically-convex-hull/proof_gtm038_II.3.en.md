1. Let $x \in M$. Then for each $\ell \in L$, $\ell(x) \leq \sup \ell(M)$. Therefore $x \in \hat{M}_e$.
2. Let $x_0 \notin \hat{M}_e$. Then there exists an $\ell \in L$ with $\ell(x_0) > \sup \ell(M)$. Since $\ell$ is continuous, these inequalities hold on an entire neighborhood, so $\mathbb{R}^2 - \hat{M}_e$ is open. Hence $\hat{M}_e$ is closed. Geometric convexity follows since each point outside $\hat{M}_e$ is separated by some $\ell$.
