Let $G \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ be a domain with $C^2$ boundary, $\zeta_0 \in \partial G$, and let $\varphi, \psi: U \to \mathbb{R}$ be twice differentiable defining functions for $G$ on a neighborhood $U = U(\zeta_0)$ with $(d\varphi)_{\zeta_0} \neq 0$, $(d\psi)_{\zeta_0} \neq 0$. If $\varphi = h \cdot \psi$ for a positive $C^2$ function $h$ on $U$, then the Levi forms satisfy

$$L_{\psi}(\mathfrak{w}) = h \cdot L_{\varphi}(\mathfrak{w})$$

for all $\mathfrak{w} \in T_{\zeta_0}^{1,0}(\partial G)$.
