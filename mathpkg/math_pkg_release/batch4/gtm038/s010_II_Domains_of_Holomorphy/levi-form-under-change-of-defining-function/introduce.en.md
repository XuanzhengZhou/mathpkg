This proposition establishes that the Levi form transforms by a positive factor when the defining function of a domain boundary is changed. If two defining functions $\varphi$ and $\psi$ differ by a positive factor $h$, then their Levi forms $L_\psi$ and $L_\varphi$ satisfy $L_\psi = h \cdot L_\varphi$. This is essential for the well-definedness of the Levi condition (Def. 2.6) and the pseudoconvexity criterion (Theorem 2.5).
