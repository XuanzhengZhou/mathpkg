On a Stein manifold $X$, a Cousin II distribution (the multiplicative Cousin problem) is solvable precisely when the associated Cech 1-cocycle $h \in Z^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$ has vanishing Chern class $c(h) = 0$. This provides a cohomological obstruction criterion: the Cousin II problem is solvable if and only if the obstruction class in $H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$ is trivial. The result follows from Theorem B and the exponential exact sequence.
