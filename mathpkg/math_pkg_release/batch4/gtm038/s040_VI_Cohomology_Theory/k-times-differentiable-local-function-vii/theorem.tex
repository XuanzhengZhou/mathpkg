Let $k \in \mathbb{N}_0$. A $k$-times differentiable local function at $x_0 \in X$ is a pair $(U, f)$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item $U$ is an open neighborhood of $x_0$ in $X$;
  \item $f$ is a real-valued function on $U$ continuous at $x_0$; and
  \item there exist a neighborhood $V(x_0) \subset U$ and a biholomorphic mapping $\psi: V \to G \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ such that $f \circ \psi^{-1}$ at $\psi(x_0)$ is $k$-times differentiable.
\end{enumerate}

The set of all $k$-times differentiable functions at $x_0$ is denoted by $\mathcal{D}_{x_0}^k$.
