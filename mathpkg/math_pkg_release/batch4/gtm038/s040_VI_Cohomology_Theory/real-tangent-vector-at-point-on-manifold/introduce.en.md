A real tangent vector at a point $x_0$ on an $n$-dimensional complex manifold $X$ is an $\mathbb{R}$-linear derivation on the algebra $\mathcal{D}_{x_0}^1$ of once-differentiable local functions. The derivation property expresses the product rule: $D(f \cdot g) = f(x_0)D(g) + g(x_0)D(f)$. In local holomorphic coordinates $z_v = x_v + i y_v$, the partial derivatives with respect to the real coordinates $x_1, \ldots, x_n, y_1, \ldots, y_n$ form a basis of the real tangent space $T_{x_0}$, which therefore has real dimension $2n$.
