A real tangent vector $D$ on a complex manifold is completely determined by its action on holomorphic functions. Given arbitrary complex coefficients $c_1, \ldots, c_n$, there exists a unique tangent vector satisfying $D(f) = \sum c_v \frac{\partial}{\partial x_v}(f)$ for all holomorphic $f$. Moreover, in local holomorphic coordinates, any tangent vector can be expressed in terms of its values on the coordinate functions $z_v$ as $D = \sum \operatorname{Re}(D(z_v)) \frac{\partial}{\partial x_v} + \sum \operatorname{Im}(D(z_v)) \frac{\partial}{\partial y_v}$. This uses the Cauchy-Riemann equations $f_{y_v} = i f_{x_v}$ which hold for holomorphic functions.
