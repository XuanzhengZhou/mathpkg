On a Stein manifold $X$, the first Cech cohomology group $H^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{S})$ vanishes for any coherent analytic sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ and any open covering $\mathcal{U}$. This theorem is a cornerstone of Stein theory, implying that every additive Cousin problem (Cousin I distribution) on a Stein manifold has a global solution. The result follows from Cartan's Theorem B, which ensures $H^1(X, \mathcal{S}) = 0$, combined with the injectivity of the canonical map $\varphi_1: H^1(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{S}) \to H^1(X, \mathcal{S})$.
