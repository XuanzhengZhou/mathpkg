The $2n$-dimensional real tangent space $T_{x_0}$ of an $n$-dimensional complex manifold can be canonically equipped with the structure of an $n$-dimensional complex vector space. The complex scalar multiplication is defined by $i \cdot \partial/\partial x_v = \partial/\partial y_v$ and $i \cdot \partial/\partial y_v = -\partial/\partial x_v$, which is consistent with the Cauchy-Riemann equations. Under this complex structure, $\{\partial/\partial x_1, \ldots, \partial/\partial x_n\}$ forms a $\mathbb{C}$-basis, giving $\dim_{\mathbb{C}} T_{x_0} = n$, half the real dimension.
