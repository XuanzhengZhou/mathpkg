Theorem 1.7 asserts that the global sections functor $\Gamma(X, -)$ is left-exact on the category of sheaves of $\mathcal{O}$-modules. Given a short exact sequence of sheaves, applying $\Gamma$ yields an exact sequence at the left two positions, but right-exactness generally fails. The failure is measured by sheaf cohomology groups $H^q(X, -)$. The proof uses exactness of the generalized sections functor $\hat{\Gamma}$ (Theorem 1.1) to handle the kernel condition, and a continuity argument to lift global sections through an injective sheaf morphism.
