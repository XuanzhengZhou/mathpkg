The canonical homomorphism $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*) \to H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*)$ is injective for every covering $\mathfrak{U}$ (See Theorem 3.3). If $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*) = 0$, then the cohomology class of the cocycle $h \in Z^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$ associated to a Cousin II distribution $(U_i, f_i)_{i \in I}$ subordinate to $\mathfrak{U}$ must be zero in $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$. Hence $h$ is a coboundary, which means there exist $g_i \in \mathcal{O}^*(U_i)$ such that $h_{ij} = g_i g_j^{-1}$ on $U_i \cap U_j$. Then the functions $g_i$ provide a global solution to the Cousin II distribution.
