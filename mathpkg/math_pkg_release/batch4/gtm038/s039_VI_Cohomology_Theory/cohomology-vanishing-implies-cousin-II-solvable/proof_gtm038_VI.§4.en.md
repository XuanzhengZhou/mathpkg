[The statement of Theorem 4.9 is truncated in the source text. The proof would follow from the long exact cohomology sequence associated to the exponential sheaf sequence $0 \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{O}^* \to 0$. Since $H^\ell(X, \mathcal{O}) = 0$ for all $\ell \geqslant 1$, the connecting homomorphisms $\partial: H^\ell(X, \mathcal{O}^*) \to H^{\ell+1}(X, \mathbb{Z})$ are isomorphisms for $\ell \geqslant 1$. In particular, $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*) \cong H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$ via the Chern class map. The solvability of Cousin II distributions then follows from the vanishing criterion expressed in terms of the Chern class.]
