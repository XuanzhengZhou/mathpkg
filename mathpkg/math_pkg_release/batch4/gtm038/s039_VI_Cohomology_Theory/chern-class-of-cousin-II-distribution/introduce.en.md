The Chern class of a Cousin II distribution is a cohomological invariant taking values in $H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$, defined via the boundary homomorphism $\partial$ from the long exact sequence associated to the exponential sheaf sequence $0 \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{O}^* \to 0$. Given a Cousin II distribution with cocycle $h \in Z^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$, its Chern class $c(h) = \partial(h) \in H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$ provides an obstruction to solvability: the distribution is solvable precisely when its Chern class vanishes. This construction links the classical Cousin II problem to the theory of characteristic classes on complex manifolds.
