The canonical homomorphisms $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}) \to H^1(X, \mathcal{O})$ and $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*) \to H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*)$ are injective for every covering $\mathfrak{U}$ (See Theorem 3.3). 

For part 1: If $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}) = 0$, then by injectivity $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}) = 0$ for every covering $\mathfrak{U}$. A Cousin I distribution subordinate to $\mathfrak{U}$ corresponds to a cocycle in $Z^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O})$. Since $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}) = 0$, this cocycle is a coboundary, which means the Cousin I distribution is solvable.

For part 2: If $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*) = 0$, then by injectivity $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*) = 0$ for every covering $\mathfrak{U}$. A Cousin II distribution subordinate to $\mathfrak{U}$ corresponds to a cocycle in $Z^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$. Since $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*) = 0$, this cocycle is a coboundary, which yields a global solution to the Cousin II distribution.
