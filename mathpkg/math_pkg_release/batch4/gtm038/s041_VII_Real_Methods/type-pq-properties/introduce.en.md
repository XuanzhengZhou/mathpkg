This theorem describes how the type $(p,q)$ of complex differential forms behaves under three fundamental operations: complex conjugation (which swaps $p$ and $q$), addition and scalar multiplication (which preserve the type), and the wedge product (which adds the bi-degrees componentwise). These properties establish the bigraded algebra structure of complex differential forms.
