1. $d'$ and $d''$ are $\mathbb{C}$-linear operators with $d' + d'' = d$.
2. $d' d' = 0$, $d'' d'' = 0$ and $d' d'' + d'' d' = 0$.
3. $d'$ and $d''$ are not real operators. Moreover $\overline{d' \varphi} = d'' \overline{\varphi}$ and $\overline{d'' \varphi} = d' \overline{\varphi}$.
4. If $\varphi$ is an $\ell$-form and $\psi$ arbitrary, then
$$d'(\varphi \wedge \psi) = d'\varphi \wedge \psi + (-1)^{\ell} \varphi \wedge d'\psi,$$
$$d''(\varphi \wedge \psi) = d''\varphi \wedge \psi + (-1)^{\ell} \varphi \wedge d''\psi.$$
