This theorem establishes the fundamental algebraic properties of the Dolbeault operators $d' = \partial$ and $d'' = \bar{\partial}$ on a complex manifold: they are $\mathbb{C}$-linear, their sum recovers the exterior derivative, each is a differential (square-zero), they anti-commute, and they satisfy a graded Leibniz rule with respect to the wedge product. These properties make $(A^{(\bullet)}, d', d'')$ a double complex, the foundation of Dolbeault cohomology.
