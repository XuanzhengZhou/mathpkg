Let $h \in Z^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$ be the cocycle of a Cousin II distribution $(U_i, f_i)_{i \in I}$, $h$ the corresponding cohomology class in $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*)$, and $\partial: H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*) \rightarrow H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$ the “boundary homomorphism” of the long exact cohomology sequence of $\mathfrak{O} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathcal{O} \rightarrow \mathcal{O}^* \rightarrow \mathfrak{O}$. Then $c(h) := \partial(h) \in H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$ is called the Chern class of $h$ (resp. of $(U_i, f_i)_{i \in I})$.
