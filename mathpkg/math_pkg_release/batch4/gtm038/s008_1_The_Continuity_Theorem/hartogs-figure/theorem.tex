Let $G \subset \mathbb{C}^{n-1}$ be a domain and let $g : G \to \mathbb{C}$ be a continuous function. Then the \textbf{Hartogs figure} is
$$\mathcal{F} := \{ \zeta = (z_1, z') \in \mathbb{C} \times G : |z_1| < 1 \} \cup \{ \zeta = (z_1, z') \in \mathbb{C} \times G : |z_1 - g(z')| < \varepsilon,\; 1-\delta < |z_1| < 1 \}$$
for suitable $\varepsilon, \delta > 0$.

\textit{[Note: The definition in the source text is truncated; the above is the standard formulation from the theory of several complex variables.]}
