The Hartogs figure is a fundamental construction in several complex variables. It is a domain in $\mathbb{C}^n$ shaped like a "figure" that has the remarkable property that every holomorphic function on it extends uniquely to a larger polydisc. The Hartogs figure serves as the prototypical subset $\tilde{H}$ in the Continuity Theorem, providing the mechanism by which holomorphic functions are forced to extend across boundaries in dimension $n \geq 2$.
