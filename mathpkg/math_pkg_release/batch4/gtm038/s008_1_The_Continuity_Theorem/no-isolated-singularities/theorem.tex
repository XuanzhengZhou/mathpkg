Let $n \geqslant 2$ and let $B \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ be a region. Let $\zeta_0 \in B$ and let $f$ be holomorphic in $B' := B \setminus \{\zeta_0\}$. Then $f$ has a unique holomorphic extension to $B$.

In other words, for $n \geqslant 2$, there are no isolated singularities of holomorphic functions.
