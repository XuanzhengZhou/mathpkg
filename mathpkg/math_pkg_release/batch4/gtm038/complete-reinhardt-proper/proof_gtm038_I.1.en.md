**Proof.** Let $G$ be a complete Reinhardt domain. There exists a point $\mathfrak{z}_1 \in G \cap \mathbb{C}^{*n}$ (i.e., a point with all coordinates non-zero), and by definition $0 \in P_{\mathfrak{z}_1} \subset G$. It remains to show that $G$ is connected.

a. Let $\mathfrak{z}_1 \in G$ be a point in general position (i.e., $\mathfrak{z}_1 \in G \cap \mathbb{C}^{*n}$). Then the connecting line segment between $\mathfrak{z}_1$ and $0$ lies entirely within $P_{\mathfrak{z}_1}$ and hence within $G$.

b. Now suppose $\mathfrak{z}_1$ lies on one of the "axes." Since $G$ is open there exists a neighborhood $U_\varepsilon(\mathfrak{z}_1) \subset G$, and we can find a point $\mathfrak{z}_2 \in U_\varepsilon(\mathfrak{z}_1) \cap \mathbb{C}^{*n}$. Hence there is a path in $U_\varepsilon$ which connects $\mathfrak{z}_1$ and $\mathfrak{z}_2$, and a path in $G$ which connects $\mathfrak{z}_2$ and $0$. Together they give a path in $G$ which joins $\mathfrak{z}_1$ and $0$.

From (a) and (b) it follows that $G$ is connected. $\square$
