**Proof.** Let /usr/bin/zsh \to \mathcal{F}' \to \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{F}''$ be an exact sequence of sheaves. For any open set $, the induced sequence /usr/bin/zsh \to \mathcal{F}'(U) \to \mathcal{F}(U) \to \mathcal{F}''(U)$ is exact. Injectivity at $\mathcal{F}'(U)$ follows from the sheaf axiom: if a section  \in \mathcal{F}'(U)$ maps to /usr/bin/zsh$ in $\mathcal{F}(U)$, its image in each stalk is /usr/bin/zsh$, so  = 0$. Exactness at $\mathcal{F}(U)$: a section  \in \mathcal{F}(U)$ maps to /usr/bin/zsh$ in $\mathcal{F}''(U)$ iff it comes locally from $\mathcal{F}'$, and by the sheaf property the local preimages glue. $\square$
