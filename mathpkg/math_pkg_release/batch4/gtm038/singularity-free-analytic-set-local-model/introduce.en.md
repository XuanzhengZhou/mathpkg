This lemma characterizes when an analytic set $A \subset X$ is free of singularities of codimension $d$ in terms of a local model. Near any point $x_0 \in A$, after applying a suitable isomorphism $\varphi$, the intersection $A \cap U$ corresponds to a regular analytic set in $\hat{B}$ of dimension $n-d$. Moreover, this is equivalent to the existence of a further local isomorphism $\psi$ that straightens the set into the simplest possible form -- the vanishing of the first $d$ coordinate functions in a polydisc $B$. The composition $\varphi_1 = \psi \circ \varphi$ then serves as the canonical local isomorphism exhibiting the manifold structure of $A$.
