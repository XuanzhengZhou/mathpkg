A generalized section of a sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ over an open set $U$ is any set-theoretic right inverse of the sheaf projection $\pi: \mathcal{S} \to X$, with no continuity requirement. This relaxes the usual notion of a section by dropping the continuity condition, yielding a potentially much larger $R$-module $\hat{\Gamma}(U, \mathcal{S})$ that contains the ordinary module $\Gamma(U, \mathcal{S})$ of continuous sections as a submodule. Generalized sections are the fundamental building blocks for constructing flabby sheaves via the functor $W$.
