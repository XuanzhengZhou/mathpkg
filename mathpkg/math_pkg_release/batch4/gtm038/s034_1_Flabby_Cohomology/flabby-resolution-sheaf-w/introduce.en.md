The sheaf $W(\mathcal{S})$ is constructed from the pre-sheaf of generalized sections $\{M_U, r_V^U\}$ by the standard sheafification process. For each open set $U$, $M_U = \hat{\Gamma}(U, \mathcal{S})$ is the $R$-module of all (not necessarily continuous) sections, and the restriction maps are ordinary restriction of functions. The resulting sheaf $W(\mathcal{S})$ is flabby and serves as the first step in constructing a flabby resolution of $\mathcal{S}$.
