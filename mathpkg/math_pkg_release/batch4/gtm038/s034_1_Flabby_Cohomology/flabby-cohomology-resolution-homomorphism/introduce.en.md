Definition 1 constructs the system of homomorphisms $\mathfrak{W}(\varphi): \mathfrak{W}(\mathcal{S}_1) \to \mathfrak{W}(\mathcal{S}_2)$ that extends the action of the functor $W$ to the iterated flabby resolution. Starting from a sheaf homomorphism $\varphi$, the maps $W_i\varphi$ are defined inductively on the successive quotients $W_{\ell}(\mathcal{S})/\text{Im}(d_{\ell-1})$. The well-definedness follows from the exactness of $W$: if an element lies in the image of $d_{\ell-1}$, its image under $W_{\ell}\varphi$ also lies in the corresponding image, so the induced map on quotients is well-defined. The resulting $\mathfrak{W}(\varphi)$ is a morphism of complexes that commutes with all differentials.
