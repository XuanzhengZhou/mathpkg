Theorem 1.2(1) states that the canonical map $r$ from the $R$-module of generalized sections $M_U = \hat{\Gamma}(U,\mathcal{S})$ to the $R$-module of continuous sections $\Gamma(U, W(\mathcal{S}))$ of the associated sheaf is an isomorphism. This means that every continuous section of $W(\mathcal{S})$ over $U$ corresponds uniquely to a generalized section of $\mathcal{S}$, providing a bridge between the pointwise-defined generalized sections and the genuine sheaf sections of $W(\mathcal{S})$.
