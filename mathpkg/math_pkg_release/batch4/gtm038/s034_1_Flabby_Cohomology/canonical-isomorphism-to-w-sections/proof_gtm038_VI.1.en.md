This is proved exactly as Theorem 2.3 in Chapter IV. The argument uses the standard correspondence between a pre-sheaf and its associated sheaf: for any pre-sheaf $\{M_U, r_V^U\}$, the canonical map from $M_U$ to the sections of the associated sheaf over $U$ is bijective when the pre-sheaf satisfies the sheaf conditions. Since generalized sections are defined as arbitrary set-theoretic sections without continuity constraints, the pre-sheaf $\{M_U, r_V^U\}$ is in fact already a sheaf (it trivially satisfies the gluing condition because no continuity is required). Therefore the canonical map $r$ is an isomorphism.
