An isomorphism of complex ringed spaces is a pair consisting of a homeomorphism of the underlying topological spaces and a sheaf isomorphism that is stalk-preserving and induces $\mathbb{C}$-algebra isomorphisms on each stalk. This definition captures the notion of equivalence of geometric spaces equipped with sheaves of local $\mathbb{C}$-algebras, ensuring that both the topology and the algebraic structure at every point are preserved.
