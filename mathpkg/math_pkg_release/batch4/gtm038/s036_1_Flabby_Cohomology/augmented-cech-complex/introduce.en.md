This theorem establishes that the Čech complex augmented by the global sections functor forms an augmented cochain complex. Concretely, the augmentation map $\varepsilon$ is injective, and a $0$-cochain is a coboundary if and only if its restricted values agree on all pairwise intersections. This means $\operatorname{Ker}(\delta|_{C^0}) = \operatorname{Im}(\varepsilon)$, identifying the $0$-th cohomology of the Čech complex with the global sections $\Gamma(X, \mathcal{S})$.
