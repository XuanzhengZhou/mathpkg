$(\Gamma(X, \mathcal{S}), \varepsilon, C^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S}))$ is an augmented cochain complex. That is, the sequence
$$0 \rightarrow \Gamma(X, \mathcal{S}) \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} C^0(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S}) \xrightarrow{\delta} C^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S}) \xrightarrow{\delta} C^2(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S}) \rightarrow \cdots$$
is exact at $\Gamma(X, \mathcal{S})$ and at $C^0(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S})$.
