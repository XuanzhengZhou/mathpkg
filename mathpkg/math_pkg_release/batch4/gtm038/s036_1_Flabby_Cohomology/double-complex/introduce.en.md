A double complex is an $R$-module bigraded by two indices with two differentials $d'$ and $d''$ that anticommute. This structure encodes two commuting (up to sign) cochain complex structures, one horizontal and one vertical. The total complex is obtained by summing along diagonals $i+j=n$ with total differential $d = d' + d''$. Double complexes are fundamental in homological algebra, appearing in the construction of spectral sequences, Künneth formulas, and the comparison of Čech cohomology with derived functor cohomology of sheaves.
