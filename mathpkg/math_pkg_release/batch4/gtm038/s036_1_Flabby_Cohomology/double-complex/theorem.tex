A **double complex** is a system $(C_{ij})$ of $R$-modules (with $i, j \in \mathbb{N}_0$) and $R$-module homomorphisms
$$d' : C_{ij} \rightarrow C_{i+1,j}, \quad d'' : C_{ij} \rightarrow C_{i,j+1}$$
such that

1. $d' \circ d' = 0$
2. $d'' \circ d'' = 0$
3. $d' \circ d'' = -d'' \circ d'$

Thus $d := d' + d'' : \bigoplus_{i+j=n} C_{ij} \rightarrow \bigoplus_{i+j=n+1} C_{ij}$ satisfies $d \circ d = 0$. A double complex is therefore an anticommutative diagram:

$$\begin{array}{cccccc}
C_{00} & \xrightarrow{d''} & C_{01} & \xrightarrow{d''} & C_{02} & \xrightarrow{d''} & \cdots \\
\downarrow d' & & \downarrow d' & & \downarrow d' \\
C_{10} & \xrightarrow{d''} & C_{11} & \xrightarrow{d''} & C_{12} & \xrightarrow{d''} & \cdots \\
\downarrow d' & & \downarrow d' & & \downarrow d' \\
C_{20} & \xrightarrow{d''} & C_{21} & \xrightarrow{d''} & C_{22} & \xrightarrow{d''} & \cdots \\
\downarrow d' & & \downarrow d' & & \downarrow d' \\
\vdots & & \vdots & & \vdots
\end{array}$$
