A Cousin I distribution encodes the data of the additive (or Mittag-Leffler) Cousin problem: given meromorphic "local data" $f_t$ on an open covering, the differences $f_{t_0} - f_{t_1}$ are holomorphic, so a solution is a global meromorphic function whose "principal parts" match the given local data modulo holomorphic functions. This is the sheaf-theoretic formulation that connects Cousin problems to Čech cohomology: the differences form a $1$-cocycle with values in the structure sheaf $\mathfrak{H}$.
