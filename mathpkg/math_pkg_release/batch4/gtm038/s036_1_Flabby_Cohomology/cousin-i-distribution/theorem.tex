A system $(U_t, f_t)_{t \in I}$ is called a **Cousin I distribution** on $X$ if

1. $\mathfrak{U} := (U_t)_{t \in I}$ is an open covering of $X$;
2. $f_t$ is meromorphic on $U_t$;
3. $f_{t_0} - f_{t_1}$ is holomorphic on $U_{t_0t_1}$ for all $t_0, t_1$.

A **solution** of a Cousin I distribution is a meromorphic function $f$ on $X$ such that $f_t - f$ is holomorphic on $U_t$.
