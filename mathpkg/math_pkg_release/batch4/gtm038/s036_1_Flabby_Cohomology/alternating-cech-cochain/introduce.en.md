An $\ell$-dimensional alternating cochain over an open covering $\mathfrak{U} = (U_{\iota})_{\iota \in I}$ with values in a sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ is the fundamental building block of Čech cohomology. It assigns to each $(\ell+1)$-tuple of indices a local section over the corresponding intersection, with the alternating property ensuring compatibility with the simplicial structure. The set $C^{\ell}(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S})$ forms an $R$-module, serving as the $\ell$-th term of the Čech complex.
