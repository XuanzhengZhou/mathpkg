In homological algebra, exactness of a sequence of morphisms encodes the property that the image of each map equals the kernel of the next. Lemma 2 provides a composition rule: if one has a five-term sequence with a surjective first map, injective last map, and exactness at the middle term, then the appropriately composed three-term sequence is also exact. This lemma is a technical tool used in the proof that the flabby sheaf functor $\mathfrak{W}$ is exact (Theorem 1.6).
