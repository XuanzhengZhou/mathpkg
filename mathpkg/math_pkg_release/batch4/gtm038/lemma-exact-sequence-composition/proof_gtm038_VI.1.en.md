$$\operatorname{Im}(\varphi_2 \circ \varphi_1) = \operatorname{Im} \varphi_2 = \operatorname{Ker} \varphi_3 = \operatorname{Ker}(\varphi_4 \circ \varphi_3).$$

Indeed, surjectivity of $\varphi_1$ gives $\operatorname{Im}(\varphi_2 \circ \varphi_1) = \operatorname{Im} \varphi_2$. Injectivity of $\varphi_4$ gives $\operatorname{Ker}(\varphi_4 \circ \varphi_3) = \operatorname{Ker} \varphi_3$. The hypothesis $\operatorname{Ker} \varphi_3 = \operatorname{Im} \varphi_2$ then yields the equality, establishing exactness of the composed sequence.
