The long cohomology sequence exactness theorem is a fundamental result in sheaf cohomology on complex manifolds. It states that a short exact sequence of sheaves of $R$-modules $O \to \mathcal{S}^* \to \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{S}^{**} \to O$ induces a long exact sequence in cohomology, beginning with the global sections functor $\Gamma(X, -)$ and continuing through all cohomology groups $H^i$. The connecting homomorphisms $\partial$ are constructed via the canonical flabby resolution $\mathfrak{W}$ and the properties established in Theorem 4.3. This theorem is the sheaf-theoretic analog of the classical long exact sequence in derived functor cohomology and serves as the central tool for computing cohomology groups and establishing vanishing theorems.
