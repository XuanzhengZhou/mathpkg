Let $B \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ be a region, $f$ a complex function on $B$, and $\mathfrak{z}_0 \in B$. $f$ is called complex differentiable at $\mathfrak{z}_0$ if there exist functions $\Delta_1(z), \ldots, \Delta_n(z)$ on $B$, continuous at $\mathfrak{z}_0$, such that
$$f(z) = f(\mathfrak{z}_0) + \sum_{v=1}^{n} (z_v - z_v^{(0)}) \Delta_v(z)$$
for all $z$ in a neighborhood of $\mathfrak{z}_0$.
