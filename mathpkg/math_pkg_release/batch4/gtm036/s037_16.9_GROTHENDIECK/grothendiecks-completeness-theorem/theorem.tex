Let $E$ and $F$ be paired linear spaces, and let $\mathcal{A}$ be a non-void family of $w(F, E)$-bounded, $w(F, E)$-closed, convex and circled subsets of $F$ such that $\mathcal{A}$ is directed by $\supset$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The canonical image $T[E]$ of $E$ in $F'$ is dense relative to $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ in the space of all linear functionals on $F$ which are $w(F, E)$-continuous on each member of $\mathcal{A}$.

\item[(ii)] If the linear extension of the union of the members of $\mathcal{A}$ is $F$, then $E$ is complete relative to $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ if and only if each linear functional on $F$ which is $w(F, E)$-continuous on each member of $\mathcal{A}$ is $w(F, E)$-continuous on $F$; equivalently, this is the case if and only if each hyperplane which intersects every member of $\mathcal{A}$ in a $w(F, E)$-closed set is $w(F, E)$-closed.
\end{enumerate}
