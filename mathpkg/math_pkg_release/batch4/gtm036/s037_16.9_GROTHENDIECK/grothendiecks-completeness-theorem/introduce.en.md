Grothendieck's Completeness Theorem characterizes when a paired linear space $E$ is complete in the polar topology $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ determined by a directed family $\mathcal{A}$ of weakly bounded, weakly closed, convex, circled subsets of $F$. Part (i) establishes that the canonical image $T[E]$ is dense in the space of functionals that are weak-star continuous on each member of $\mathcal{A}$. Part (ii) provides an exact criterion: when the union of $\mathcal{A}$ spans $F$, completeness of $E$ is equivalent to every $\mathcal{A}$-continuous functional being globally weakly continuous---equivalently, to every hyperplane meeting each $A \in \mathcal{A}$ in a weakly closed set being itself weakly closed.
