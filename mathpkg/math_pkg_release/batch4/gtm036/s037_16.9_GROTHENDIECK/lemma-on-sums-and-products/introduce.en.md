This lemma describes how polars behave with respect to direct sums and products of paired linear spaces. Part (i) states that the polar of a union of subsets $B_t \subset E_t$ (embedded in the direct sum) equals the Cartesian product of their individual polars in the dual components $F_t$. Part (ii) provides a sandwich: the polar of a product of circled sets $C_t \subset F_t$ contains the convex extension of the union of the polars $(C_t)_0$ (viewed in the direct sum) and is contained in a scalar multiple $aB$ for any $a > 1$.
