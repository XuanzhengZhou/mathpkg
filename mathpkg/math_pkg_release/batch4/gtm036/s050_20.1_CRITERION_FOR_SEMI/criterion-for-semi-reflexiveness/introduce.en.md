The criterion for semi-reflexiveness characterizes when a locally convex space $E$ is semi-reflexive purely in terms of its weak topology and the pairing between $E$ and its dual $E^*$. A locally convex space $E$ is semi-reflexive precisely when every bounded weakly closed subset of $E$ is weakly compact. Equivalently, semi-reflexivity holds if and only if the Mackey topology $m(E^*, E)$ coincides with the strong topology $s(E^*, E)$ on the dual space. This result implies that semi-reflexivity depends only on the dual pairing and not on the particular compatible topology, so any topology between the weak and Mackey topologies on a semi-reflexive space also yields a semi-reflexive space.
