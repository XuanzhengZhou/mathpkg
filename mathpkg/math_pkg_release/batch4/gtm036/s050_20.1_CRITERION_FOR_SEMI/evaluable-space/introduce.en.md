An evaluable space (also known as symmetric, infratonnelé, quasi-tonnelé, or quasi-barrelled) is a locally convex space for which the canonical evaluation map from $E$ into its bidual $E^{**}$ is continuous. This property admits two useful equivalent formulations: every bound absorbing barrel in $E$ is a neighborhood of $0$, and every strongly bounded subset of the dual $E^*$ is equicontinuous. The class of evaluable spaces strictly contains both barrelled spaces and bound spaces, and every evaluable space is a Mackey space.
