This theorem characterizes evaluable spaces through several equivalent conditions involving the evaluation map, strongly bounded subsets of the dual, and bound absorbing barrels. It establishes that evaluable spaces form a class intermediate between barrelled/bound spaces and Mackey spaces. The theorem further demonstrates that evaluability is preserved under the formation of quotients, products, and direct sums, and that taking the sequential completion of an evaluable space yields a barrelled space — a notable strengthening of the property.
