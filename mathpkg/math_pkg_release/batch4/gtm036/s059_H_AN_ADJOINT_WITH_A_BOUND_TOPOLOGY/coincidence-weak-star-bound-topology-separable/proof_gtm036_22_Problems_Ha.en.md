The proof uses a method similar to Theorem 22.17. One considers the strongly separable subset $A \subseteq E^*$ and analyzes the restriction of the two topologies to $A$. Since $A$ is strongly separable, it is contained in the strongly closed linear span of a countable set. By applying the techniques of 22.17, which deals with the coincidence of the weak* and strong topologies on equicontinuous sets, one shows that the relative topologies $s|_A$ and $s^b|_A$ are identical. The statement about sequences follows immediately: a sequence is a particular case of a strongly separable set (its terms form a countable set whose strong closure is separable), so $s$-convergence and $s^b$-convergence coincide.
