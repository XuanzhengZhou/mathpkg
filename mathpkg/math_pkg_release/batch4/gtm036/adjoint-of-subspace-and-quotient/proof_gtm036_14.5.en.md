Let $E$ be a linear topological space, let $F$ be a subspace, and let $F^\perp$ be the annihilator of $F$ in $E^*$. Then each member of $(E/F)^*$ corresponds uniquely to a member of $F^\perp$ by composition with the quotient map, giving a canonical isomorphism $(E/F)^* \cong F^\perp$. The space $F^\perp$ is also the null space of the restriction map from $E^*$ to $F^*$, inducing an isomorphism $E^*/F^\perp \to F^*$. In general this is not onto, but if $E$ is locally convex, then by the extension theorem 14.1(iii), each continuous linear functional on $F$ extends to one on $E$, so $E^*/F^\perp \cong F^*$.
