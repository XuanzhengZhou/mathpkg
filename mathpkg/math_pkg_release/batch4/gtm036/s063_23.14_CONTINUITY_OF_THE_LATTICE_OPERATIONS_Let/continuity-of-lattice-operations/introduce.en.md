This theorem establishes that the lattice operations of meet ($\wedge$) and join ($\vee$) are jointly continuous on a vector lattice $E$ when $E$ is equipped with any locally convex topology generated by a base of lattice pseudo-norms. The result reduces to proving continuity of the map $x \mapsto x^+$, which follows from the inequality $|x^+ - y^+| \leq |x-y|$ derived in the preceding remark. As a special case, the lattice operations are always continuous with respect to the Mackey topology $m(E, E^\star)$, making this a fundamental structural fact about the interplay between order and topology in vector lattices.
