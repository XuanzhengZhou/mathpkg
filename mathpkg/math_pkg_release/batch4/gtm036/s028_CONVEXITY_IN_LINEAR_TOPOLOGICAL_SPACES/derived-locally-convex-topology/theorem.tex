Let $\mathcal{T}$ be an arbitrary vector topology for a linear space $E$. The family of all convex circled $\mathcal{T}$-neighborhoods of $0$ is a local base for a locally convex topology $\mathcal{U}$ on $E$, called the \emph{locally convex topology derived from} $\mathcal{T}$. The topology $\mathcal{U}$ is the strongest locally convex topology that is weaker than $\mathcal{T}$. A linear functional on $E$ is $\mathcal{T}$-continuous if and only if it is $\mathcal{U}$-continuous.
