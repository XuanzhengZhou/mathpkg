Let $E$ be a linear topological space, let $A$ and $B$ be subsets of $E$, and let $a$ and $b$ be scalars. Then:
(i) If $A$ is convex, so are $A^{-}$ and $A^{i}$.
(ii) The smallest closed convex set containing $B$ is the closure of the convex extension $\langle B \rangle$ of $B$.
(iii) If $\langle A \rangle^{-}$ and $\langle B \rangle^{-}$ are compact, then $\langle A \cup B \rangle^{-}$ and $\langle aA + bB \rangle^{-}$ are compact.
(iv) If $\langle A \rangle$ is compact and $\langle B \rangle$ is closed, then $\langle aA + bB \rangle$ is closed; if, further, $E$ is Hausdorff and $\langle B \rangle$ is bounded, then $\langle A \cup B \rangle$ is closed.
