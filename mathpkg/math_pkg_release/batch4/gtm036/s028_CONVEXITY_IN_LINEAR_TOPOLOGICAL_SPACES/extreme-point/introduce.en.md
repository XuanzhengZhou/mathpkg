An extreme point of a convex set $A$ is a point that cannot be expressed as a strict convex combination of two distinct points of $A$; equivalently, it is not an interior point of any line segment contained in $A$. Extreme points are the "corners" or "vertices" of a convex set. The Krein--Milman theorem asserts that every compact convex subset of a locally convex Hausdorff space is the closed convex hull of its extreme points. This concept is fundamental in functional analysis, Choquet theory, and the representation theory of locally compact groups.
