A convex body is a convex subset of a linear topological space whose interior is non-empty. This notion marks a key structural feature: the presence of interior points allows the application of topological arguments — such as radiality at interior points — together with algebraic convexity. Convex bodies are the natural setting for the Minkowski functional and for separation theorems, and they underpin much of the duality theory in locally convex spaces.
