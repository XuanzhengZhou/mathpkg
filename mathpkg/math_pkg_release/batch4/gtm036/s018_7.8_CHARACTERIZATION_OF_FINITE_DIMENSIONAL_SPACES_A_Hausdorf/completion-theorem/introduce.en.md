Every linear topological space admits a completion: it can be topologically isomorphically embedded as a dense subspace of a complete linear topological space $E^\wedge$. This fundamental theorem generalizes the classical construction of completing a metric space. The proof proceeds by first using Theorem 6.9 to embed $E$ into a product of pseudo-metrizable linear spaces, then completing each factor to obtain $E^\wedge$.
