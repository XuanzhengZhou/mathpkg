Every finite dimensional subspace $F$ of a linear topological space $E$ can be decomposed as a product of a Euclidean space $G$ and a finite dimensional space $H$ carrying the trivial (indiscrete) topology. This exercise exercise highlights the fact that finite dimensional subspaces need not be Hausdorff in general; the Euclidean factor $G$ captures the Hausdorff part while $H$ accounts for the non-Hausdorff part.
