The completion of a linear topological Hausdorff space is unique up to topological isomorphism. Given two completions $E^\wedge$ and $E^\sim$ of the same space $E$ with embeddings $T$ and $S$, the map $T \circ S^{-1}$ defined on the dense subspace $S[E]$ extends uniquely to a topological isomorphism between $E^\sim$ and $E^\wedge$. The proof leverages Theorem 5.5 on unique continuous extensions and the fact that the composition of the extensions is the identity on a dense subspace.
