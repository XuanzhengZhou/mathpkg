By 5.5 both $T \circ S^{-1}$ and $S \circ T^{-1}$ have unique continuous extensions to all of $E^\sim$ and $E^\wedge$ respectively. Since $(T \circ S^{-1}) \circ (S \circ T^{-1})$ is the identity, the composition of the extensions of these two maps is the identity map on a dense subspace of $E^\wedge$, and it is therefore the identity on $E^\wedge$. It follows that the continuous extension of $T \circ S^{-1}$ is a topological isomorphism of $E^\sim$ onto $E^\wedge$.
