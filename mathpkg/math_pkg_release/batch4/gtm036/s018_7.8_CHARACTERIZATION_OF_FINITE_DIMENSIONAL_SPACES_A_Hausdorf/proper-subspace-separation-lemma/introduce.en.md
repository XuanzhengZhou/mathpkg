This lemma provides a crucial separation property for proper closed subspaces of a linear topological space. Given a bounded neighborhood $U$ of the origin and a circled neighborhood $V$ with $V + V \subset U$, one can find a point $x$ in $U$ whose $V$-translate avoids a given proper closed subspace $Y$. The result is proved by contradiction: if every $V$-translate intersected $Y$, then the boundedness of $V$ and closedness of $Y$ would force $V \subset Y$, contradicting that $Y$ is proper. This lemma is the key technical tool in the proof of the finite dimensional characterization theorem (7.8).
