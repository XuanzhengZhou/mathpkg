A topological space is locally compact if every point possesses a compact neighborhood. Every locally compact Hausdorff space is regular. Euclidean spaces $\mathbb{R}^n$ are locally compact, as are all topological manifolds. Locally compact Hausdorff spaces admit a one-point compactification (Alexandroff compactification), making them dense open subspaces of compact Hausdorff spaces.
