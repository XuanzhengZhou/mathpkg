Given a family of maps $g: X_g \to Y$ from topological spaces $X_g$ into a set $Y$, the induced (or final, strong) topology on $Y$ is the finest topology making all $g$ continuous. A set $U \subseteq Y$ is open iff $g^{-1}[U]$ is open in $X_g$ for every $g$. This is dual to the projective topology: a map out of $(Y, \text{induced})$ is continuous iff its pre-composition with each $g$ is continuous. The quotient topology is the special case of a single surjective map.
