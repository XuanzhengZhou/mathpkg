A topological space whose topology is induced by a pseudo-metric is Hausdorff precisely when the pseudo-metric is actually a metric (i.e., satisfies $d(x,y)=0 \implies x=y$). In a pseudo-metric space, distinct points $x \neq y$ with $d(x,y)=0$ cannot be separated by open sets, violating the Hausdorff condition. Thus, for pseudo-metrizable spaces, the Hausdorff separation axiom is equivalent to the metrizability of the space.
