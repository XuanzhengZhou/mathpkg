When all factor spaces in a product are identical, the product space becomes the set of all functions from the index set to the common factor, and the product topology is called the topology of pointwise convergence (or simple topology). In this topology, a net of functions $f_\alpha$ converges to $f$ iff $f_\alpha(x) \to f(x)$ for each point $x$ in the domain. This is the weakest topology for which all evaluation maps $f \mapsto f(x)$ are continuous.
