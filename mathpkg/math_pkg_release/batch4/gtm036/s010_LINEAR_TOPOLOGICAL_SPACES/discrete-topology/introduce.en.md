The discrete topology on a set $X$ is the topology in which every subset is open (equivalently, every singleton $\{x\}$ is open). It is the maximal element in the lattice of all topologies on $X$. In the discrete topology, every function from $X$ to any topological space is continuous, every subset is both open and closed, and convergence of nets is trivial: a net converges to $x$ iff it is eventually constantly $x$.
