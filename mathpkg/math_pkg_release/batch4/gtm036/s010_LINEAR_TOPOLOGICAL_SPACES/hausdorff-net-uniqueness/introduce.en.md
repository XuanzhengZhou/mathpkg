A topological space is Hausdorff precisely when limits of nets are unique: no net can converge to two distinct points. This provides a net-theoretic characterization of the $T_2$ separation axiom, generalizing the familiar uniqueness of limits for sequences in metric spaces. In non-Hausdorff spaces, a net may converge to multiple points simultaneously.
