A topological space is regular if the closed neighborhoods of each point form a local base. Equivalently, for any closed set $C$ and point $x \notin C$, there exist disjoint open sets separating them. Regularity is a separation axiom intermediate between Hausdorff and completely regular. Kelley's definition here emphasizes the local structure of the space.
