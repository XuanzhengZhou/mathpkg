Suppose $F$ is a family of functions, each member $f$ of $F$ being on a fixed set $X$ to a topological space $Y_f$. Then the $F$-projective topology for $X$ is the weakest topology such that each map $f$ in $F$ is continuous. Equivalently, the $F$-projective topology has as a subbase the family of all sets $f^{-1}[U]$ where $f \in F$ and $U$ is open in $Y_f$.

An arbitrary function $g$ on a topological space $Z$ into $X$ is continuous relative to the projective topology for $X$ if and only if the composition $f \circ g$ is a continuous map of $Z$ into $Y_f$ for each $f$ in $F$.
