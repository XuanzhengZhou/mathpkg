Given a family of maps $f: X \to Y_f$ from a set $X$ to topological spaces $Y_f$, the projective (or weak, initial) topology on $X$ is the coarsest topology making all $f$ continuous. A subbase consists of all sets $f^{-1}[U]$ with $U$ open in $Y_f$. A map into $(X, \text{projective})$ is continuous iff its composition with each $f$ is continuous. The product topology is the special case where $F$ is the family of all coordinate projections.
