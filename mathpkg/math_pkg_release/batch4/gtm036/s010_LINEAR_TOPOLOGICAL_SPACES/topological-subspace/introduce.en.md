A subset $Z$ of a topological space $X$, equipped with the relative topology, is called a topological subspace of $X$. Subspaces inherit many properties from the ambient space; for instance, subspaces of Hausdorff spaces are Hausdorff, and subspaces of compact spaces need not be compact unless closed in $X$.
