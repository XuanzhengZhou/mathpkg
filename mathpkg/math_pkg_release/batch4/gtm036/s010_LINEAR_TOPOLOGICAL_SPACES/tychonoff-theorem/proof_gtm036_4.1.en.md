The Tychonoff theorem is stated without proof in this section. Kelley references it as a known result from general topology, noting that the proof relies on the Axiom of Choice (in the form of Zorn's Lemma or the well-ordering principle). A standard proof proceeds by showing that every net in the product has a cluster point: given a net $\{x_\alpha\}$ in $\prod X_t$, one extracts a convergent subnet coordinatewise using the compactness of each factor, then verifies convergence in the product topology.
