The product topology on a Cartesian product of topological spaces is the coarsest topology making all coordinate projections continuous. A basic open set restricts only finitely many coordinates, leaving the others free. Convergence is pointwise (coordinatewise): a net converges in the product iff each coordinate net converges. This is often called the topology of pointwise convergence when all factor spaces are identical. The product topology is fundamental to the Tychonoff theorem and to the theory of topological vector spaces.
