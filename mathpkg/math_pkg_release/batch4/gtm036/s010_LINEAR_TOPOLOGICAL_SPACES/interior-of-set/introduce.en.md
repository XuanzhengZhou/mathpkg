The interior $A^i$ of a subset $A$ is the largest open set contained in $A$, obtained as the union of all open subsets of $A$. Equivalently, a point $x$ belongs to $A^i$ if and only if $A$ is a neighborhood of $x$. The interior is the dual of the closure under complementation: $X \setminus A^i = \overline{X \setminus A}$.
