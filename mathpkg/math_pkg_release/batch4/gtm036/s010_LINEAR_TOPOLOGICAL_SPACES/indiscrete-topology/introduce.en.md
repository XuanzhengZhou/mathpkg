The indiscrete (or trivial) topology on $X$ has only the empty set and $X$ itself as open sets. It is the minimal element in the lattice of all topologies on $X$. In this topology, every net converges to every point (so limits are not unique unless $X$ is a singleton), and every function from any topological space into an indiscrete space is continuous. The indiscrete topology is the coarsest possible topology.
