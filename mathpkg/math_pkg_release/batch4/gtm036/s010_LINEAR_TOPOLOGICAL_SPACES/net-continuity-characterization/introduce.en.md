In a general topological space (not necessarily first-countable), continuity cannot be characterized by sequences alone. Nets provide the correct generalization: a function $f$ is continuous at a point $x'$ if and only if it preserves the convergence of all nets converging to $x'$. This net characterization of continuity is one of the principal motivations for introducing Moore-Smith convergence.
