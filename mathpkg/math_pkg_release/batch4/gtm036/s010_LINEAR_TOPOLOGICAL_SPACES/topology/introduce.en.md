A topology on a set $X$ is a collection of subsets called open sets, required to contain the empty set and $X$, and to be closed under arbitrary unions and finite intersections. The pair $(X,\mathcal{T})$ is called a topological space. This foundational concept abstracts the notion of nearness and continuity from metric spaces, replacing distance with set-theoretic axioms about openness.
