A homeomorphism is a bijective continuous function $f: X \to Y$ whose inverse $f^{-1}: Y \to X$ is also continuous. Equivalently, a homeomorphism is a bijection that is both continuous and open (or relatively open onto its image). Homeomorphisms are the isomorphisms in the category of topological spaces; two spaces related by a homeomorphism are topologically indistinguishable.
