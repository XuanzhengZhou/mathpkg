A Hausdorff (or $T_2$) space is one in which any two distinct points can be separated by disjoint open neighborhoods. This key separation axiom guarantees uniqueness of limits: in a Hausdorff space, no net converges to more than one point. Most spaces in analysis and geometry are Hausdorff; the property is preserved under products, subspaces, and continuous injective images.
