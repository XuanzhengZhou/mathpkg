The space of all functions $A \to X$ is canonically identified with the product $\prod_{a \in A} X$, where each factor is the compact space $X$. The topology of pointwise convergence coincides exactly with the product topology on this product. By the Tychonoff theorem (Theorem 4.1), the product of compact spaces is compact, hence the function space is compact.
