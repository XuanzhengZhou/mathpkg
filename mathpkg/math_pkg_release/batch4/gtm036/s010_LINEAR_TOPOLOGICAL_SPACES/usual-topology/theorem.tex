If $X$ is the set of real or complex numbers and $d(x,y) = |x - y|$ for $x$ and $y$ in $X$, then $d$ is a metric on $X$. Unless specifically stated otherwise, it will be assumed that the field of complex numbers and the field of real numbers are assigned the topology of the metric $d$. It is called the usual topology for the complex (respectively, real) numbers.
