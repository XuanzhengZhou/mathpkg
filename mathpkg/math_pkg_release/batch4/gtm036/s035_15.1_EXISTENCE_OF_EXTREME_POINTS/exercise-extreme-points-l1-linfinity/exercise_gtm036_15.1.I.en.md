Let $X$ be the real line and let $\mu$ be the Lebesgue measure on $X$.

(a) The unit sphere of $L^1(X, \mu)$ has no extreme points.

(b) A member $f$ of $L^\infty(X, \mu)$ is an extreme point of the unit sphere if and only if $f^2 = 1$.

(In these problems regard $f = g$ to mean that $f(x) = g(x)$ almost everywhere.)
