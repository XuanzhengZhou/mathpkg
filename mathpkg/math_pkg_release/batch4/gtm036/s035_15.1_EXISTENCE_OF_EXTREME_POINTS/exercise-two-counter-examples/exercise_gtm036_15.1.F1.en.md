In a finite dimensional space, a convex compact set is the convex extension of the set of its extreme points, this extension being closed. This fails in general, even in a Banach space. In the space $l^\infty$ (6K), let $e_n$ be the sequence with $n$-th term $1$ and other terms $0$, and let $A$ be the closed convex extension of the set $\{e_n/n : n = 1, 2, \cdots\}$ together with $0$. Then $A$ is compact but is not the convex extension of the set of its extreme points.
