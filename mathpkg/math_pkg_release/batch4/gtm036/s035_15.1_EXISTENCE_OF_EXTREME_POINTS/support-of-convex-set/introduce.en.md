A support (or face, or extreme subset) of a convex set is a convex subset that contains the endpoints of any line segment whose interior intersects it. Supports form a fundamental notion in convex analysis: intersections of supports remain supports, and the inverse image of a support under a linear transformation intersected with the original set yields a support of the latter. A singleton is a support precisely when its element is an extreme point.
