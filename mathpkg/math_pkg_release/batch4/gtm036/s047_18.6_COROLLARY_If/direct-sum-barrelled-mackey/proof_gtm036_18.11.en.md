It is convenient to treat each space $E_t$ as a subspace of $\sum\{E_t : t \in A\}$, ignoring the injection map of $E_t$ into the sum. A barrel in $\sum\{E_t : t \in A\}$ intersects each subspace $E_t$ in a barrel in $E_t$, and if each summand is a barrelled space, then it follows that $\sum\{E_t : t \in A\}$ is, with the direct sum topology, a barrelled space. To establish the fact that the direct sum of Mackey spaces is a Mackey space, we recall (theorem 17.14) that the adjoint of $\sum\{E_t : t \in A\}$, with the weak* topology, is topologically isomorphic to $\times\{E_t^* : t \in A\}$ with the product of the weak* topologies for the factors. Each weak* compact subset of the adjoint is then a product of weak* compact sets in each factor, whence the result follows.
