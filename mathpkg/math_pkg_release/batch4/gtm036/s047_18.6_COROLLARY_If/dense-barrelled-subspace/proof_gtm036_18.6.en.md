Suppose that $F$ is a barrelled space which is a dense subspace of $E$. If $A$ is a barrel in $E$, then $A \cap F$ is a barrel in $F$ and is hence a neighborhood of $0$ in $F$. But then the closure of $A \cap F$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$, because $F$ is dense in $E$, and since $A$ contains this closure, $A$ is itself a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$. Consequently, $E$ is a barrelled space.
