Let $E$ be a barrelled space, let $F$ be a subspace, and let $Q$ be the quotient map of $E$ onto $E/F$. If $A$ is a barrel in $E/F$, then the inverse image of $A$ under $Q$ is a barrel in $E$, is hence a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$, and its image in $E/F$ is therefore a neighborhood of $0$ because $Q$ is open. Hence $E/F$ is a barrelled space.
