The computation 16.10 of the polar of such a union shows that each member of $\mathcal{A}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{A}$ which is of the form $\times \{B_t^\circ : t \in A\}$, where $B_t$ is a bounded subset of $E_t$ for each $t$ and (since $B_t = \{0\}$ except for finitely many $t$) $B_t^\circ = E_t$ except for finitely many $t$. In brief, $\mathcal{A}$ is a local base for the product of the strong topologies, and part (i) is established. The proof of (ii) is omitted; it proceeds along similar lines, via identification of the adjoint 14.6, description of bounded subsets 14.8, and the computation on polars, 16.10.
