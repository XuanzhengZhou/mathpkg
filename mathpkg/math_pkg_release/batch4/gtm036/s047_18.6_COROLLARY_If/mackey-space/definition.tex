A linear topological space $(E,\mathcal{T})$ is a \textit{Mackey space} if and only if $\mathcal{T} = m(E,E^*)$. Thus a Mackey space is a locally convex space such that each weak$^*$ compact convex circled subset of $E^*$ is equicontinuous. The foregoing theorem characterizes a Mackey space as a locally convex space $E$ whose topology $\mathcal{T}$ is the strongest locally convex topology yielding $(E,\mathcal{T})^*$ as adjoint.
