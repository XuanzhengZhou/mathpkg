If $U$ is a circled neighborhood of $0$ in the range space $E$ and $s$ is a point of $A^{-}$ then, by equicontinuity, there is a neighborhood $V$ of $s$ such that $g(t) \in g(s) + U$ for all members $g$ of $M$ and all $t$ in $V$. If $t$ is a member of $A \cap V$, and if $f$ and $g$ are members of $M$ such that $g(t) - f(t) \in U$, then a simple computation shows that $g(s) - f(s) \in U + U + U$. Hence

$$\{g: g(t) \in f(t) + U\} \subset \{g: g(s) \in f(s) + U + U + U\}.$$

It follows that each of the neighborhoods of $f$ relative to the topology of pointwise convergence on $A^{-}$ contains a neighborhood of $f$ relative to the topology of pointwise convergence on $A$.
