Consider the family $G$ of all continuous functions from a compact space $S$ to a compact metric space $Z$, endowed with the topology of pointwise convergence. If $F \subseteq G$ has the property that every sequence in $F$ possesses a cluster point in $G$, then every function in the closure of $F$ is itself a cluster point of some sequence in $F$. Moreover, every cluster point of a sequence in $F$ is actually the limit of a subsequence. This theorem bridges sequential compactness properties with closure in function spaces.
