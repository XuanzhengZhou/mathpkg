This theorem is an immediate consequence of Theorems 8.18 and 8.20. It states that for the family $G$ of all continuous functions from a compact space $S$ to a compact metric space $Z$ (with the topology of pointwise convergence), any subfamily $F$ whose sequences all have cluster points in $G$ has compact closure. Moreover, every function in this closure is the limit of a sequence from $F$. As a corollary, every countably compact family of continuous functions on $S$ to $Z$ is both compact and sequentially compact.
