In a Hausdorff linear topological space, any closed subset that is both connected and locally convex must be globally convex. This result links topological properties (connectedness, local convexity) with the algebraic-geometric notion of convexity. The proof proceeds by showing that for any two points in $A$, one can construct a chain of line segments within $A$ connecting them, and then using a maximality argument to extend a segment all the way between the points.
