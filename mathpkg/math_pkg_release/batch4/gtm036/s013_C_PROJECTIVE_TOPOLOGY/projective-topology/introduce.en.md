The projective topology on a linear space $X$ with respect to a family $\mathcal{F}$ of linear maps into linear topological spaces is the coarsest (weakest) topology that makes every $f \in \mathcal{F}$ continuous. It generalizes the notion of the initial topology from general topology to the setting of linear topological spaces. A key property is that the projective topology is always a vector topology, making it a fundamental construction for building new linear topological spaces from given ones.
