A norm is a real-valued function on a linear space that generalizes the notion of length or magnitude. It satisfies three axioms: subadditivity (the triangle inequality), absolute homogeneity (scaling linearly with the absolute value of the scalar), and positive definiteness (the only vector with zero norm is the zero vector). Every norm induces a metric via $d(x,y) = p(x-y)$, which in turn yields a metric topology. Normed spaces are the simplest and most fundamental class of linear topological spaces.
