A pseudo-norm relaxes the positive definiteness condition of a norm: it still satisfies subadditivity and absolute homogeneity, but nonzero vectors may have pseudo-norm zero. Pseudo-norms induce pseudo-metric topologies that are vector topologies but not necessarily Hausdorff. A linear topological space is pseudo-normable precisely when its topology can be generated by some pseudo-norm. The quotient of a pseudo-normable space by the subspace of pseudo-norm-zero vectors yields a normed space.
