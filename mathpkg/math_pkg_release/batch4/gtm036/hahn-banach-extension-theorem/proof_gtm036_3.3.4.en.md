Consider the linear space $E \times K$, where $K$ is the real field, and let $P$ be the set of all vectors $(x,t)$ which are "above" the graph of $p$; explicitly, $P = \{(x,t) : t \geq p(x)\}$. It is easy to see that $P$ is a cone and that $P$ is radial at $(0,1)$. For $(x,t)$ in $F \times K$ let $g(x,t) = t - f(x)$. Then $g$ restricted to $(F \times K) \cap P$ is non-negative. By applying the previous results on extension of linear functionals defined on cones, one obtains an extension of $f$ to all of $E$ satisfying the required inequality.
