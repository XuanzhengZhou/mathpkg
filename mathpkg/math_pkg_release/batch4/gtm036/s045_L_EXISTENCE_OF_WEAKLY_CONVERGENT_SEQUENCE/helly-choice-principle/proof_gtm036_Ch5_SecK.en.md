Regard $\{\mu_n\}$ as a sequence of elements of a sphere in the adjoint of $C_0(X)$, the normed space of continuous real valued functions on $X$ vanishing at infinity, and use part (a) of Theorem 17J. Specifically, the measures $\mu_n$ correspond via the Riesz representation theorem to bounded linear functionals on $C_0(X)$ with $\|\mu_n\| = \mu_n(X)$. The boundedness condition on $\{\mu_n(X)\}$ implies that $\{\mu_n\}$ lies in some closed ball of $C_0(X)^*$, which is weak* compact by the Banach-Alaoglu theorem. Since $X$ has a countable base, $C_0(X)$ is separable, and consequently the weak* topology on bounded subsets of $C_0(X)^*$ is metrizable. One may then extract a weak* convergent subsequence $\{\mu_{n_k}\}$, whose limit $\mu$ satisfies the required convergence property.
