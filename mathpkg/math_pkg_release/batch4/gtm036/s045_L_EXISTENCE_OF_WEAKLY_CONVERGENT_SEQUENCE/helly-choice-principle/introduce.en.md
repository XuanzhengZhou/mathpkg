Helly's choice principle (also known as the Helly selection theorem or Helly-Bray theorem) is a compactness result for sequences of Borel measures on a locally compact Hausdorff space with a countable base. When the total masses $\mu_n(X)$ are uniformly bounded, one can extract a subsequence converging in the weak* sense to a limiting Borel measure $\mu$, where convergence is tested against continuous functions vanishing at infinity. This principle is fundamental to the theory of Radon measures and provides a functional-analytic perspective via the dual of $C_0(X)$.
