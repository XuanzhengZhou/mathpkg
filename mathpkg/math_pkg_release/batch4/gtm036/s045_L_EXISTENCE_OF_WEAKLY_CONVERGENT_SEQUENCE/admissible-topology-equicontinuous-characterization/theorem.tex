If $\mathcal{T}$ is an arbitrary locally convex topology for a linear space $E$, then $\mathcal{T}$ is admissible relative to the natural pairing of $E$ and $E^*$. In fact, $\mathcal{T}$ is the topology of uniform convergence on $\mathcal{T}$-equicontinuous subsets of $E^*$, or equivalently, on the family of all polars of $\mathcal{T}$-neighborhoods of $0$.
