This theorem provides criteria for the continuity of a linear functional restricted to a convex subset of a linear topological space. Part (i) gives a characterization in terms of closedness of half-space intersections with the convex set. Parts (ii) and (iii) relate the level sets of a linear functional and its real part. The result is motivated by the study of completeness of spaces of linear functions under uniform convergence on convex sets: the space of linear functions bounded and continuous on a convex set $A$ is complete under this topology, while the space of linear functions continuous on the whole space $E$ and bounded on $A$ may fail to be complete.
