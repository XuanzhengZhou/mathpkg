This result demonstrates that local convexity is essential for the preservation of boundedness and total boundedness under the convex extension operation. In the space $l^{1/2}$, which is a complete metric linear space that is not locally convex, both the unit ball and a carefully constructed countable set provide counterexamples: the convex extension of a bounded set need not be bounded, and the convex extension of a totally bounded set need not be totally bounded. These examples highlight the fundamental role of local convexity in the theory developed in this chapter.
