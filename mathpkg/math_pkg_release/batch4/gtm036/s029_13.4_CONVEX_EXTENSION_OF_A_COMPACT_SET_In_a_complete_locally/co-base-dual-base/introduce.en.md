A co-base (also called a dual base) of a family of sets $\mathcal{A}$ is a subfamily $\mathcal{B} \subseteq \mathcal{A}$ such that every member of $\mathcal{A}$ is contained in some member of $\mathcal{B}$. This notion is dual to that of a base: while a base consists of sets small enough to generate the family by containment, a co-base consists of sets large enough to absorb every member of the family. In the context of topological vector spaces, co-bases are useful for describing classes of sets such as bounded sets and totally bounded sets.
