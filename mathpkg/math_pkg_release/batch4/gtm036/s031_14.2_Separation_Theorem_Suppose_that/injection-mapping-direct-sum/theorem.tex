For each $t$ in an index set $A$, there is an algebraic isomorphism, called an \textit{injection}, of $E_t$ into the direct sum $\sum \{E_s : s \in A\}$. The injection $I_t$ is defined by letting $I_t(z)$, where $z \in E_t$, be the member of the direct sum which is $0$ for $s \neq t$ and $z$ for $s = t$.
