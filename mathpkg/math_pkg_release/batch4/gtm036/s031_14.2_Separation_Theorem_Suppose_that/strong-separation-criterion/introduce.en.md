The Strong Separation Criterion characterizes when two convex sets can be strongly separated by a continuous linear functional. In a locally convex space, strong separation is equivalent to the condition that $0$ does not belong to the closure of the difference set $B - A$. The forward direction holds generally: if $f$ strongly separates $A$ and $B$, then $\sup\{r(x): x \in A\} < \inf\{r(y): y \in B\}$, which forces $0 \notin (B - A)^-$.
