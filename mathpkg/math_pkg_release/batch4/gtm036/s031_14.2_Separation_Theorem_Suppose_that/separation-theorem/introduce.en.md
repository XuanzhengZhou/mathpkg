The Separation Theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of linear topological spaces. It provides a necessary and sufficient condition for the existence of a continuous linear functional separating two non-void convex sets: such a functional exists precisely when the second set is disjoint from the interior of the first. The theorem generalizes the geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem and relies on the identification of the interior of a convex set with its radial kernel.
