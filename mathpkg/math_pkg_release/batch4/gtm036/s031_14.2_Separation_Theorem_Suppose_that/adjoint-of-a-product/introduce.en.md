This theorem characterizes the continuous dual of a product of linear topological spaces. A linear functional on the product is continuous with respect to the product topology exactly when it can be expressed as a finite sum of functionals, each acting on a single coordinate space. In other words, the continuous dual of a product is the direct sum of the continuous duals of the factors.
