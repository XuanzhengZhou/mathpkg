Recall that a local base for the product topology is the family of sets $U$ of the following form: $U$ is the set of all $x$ in the product such that, for each $t$ in a fixed finite set $B$, the coordinate $x_t$ belongs to a prescribed neighborhood of $0$ in $E_t$. Consequently, if $\phi$ is continuous, its kernel contains a set of this form, and it follows that $\phi(x)$ depends only on the coordinates indexed by the finite set $B$. Hence $\phi$ can be written as a finite sum $\sum \{g_t(x_t): t \in B\}$ where $g_t \in E_t^*$. Conversely, any functional of this form is clearly continuous relative to the product topology.
