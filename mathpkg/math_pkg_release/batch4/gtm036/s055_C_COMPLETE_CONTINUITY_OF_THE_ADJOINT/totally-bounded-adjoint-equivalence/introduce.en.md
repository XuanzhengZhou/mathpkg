Under the additional hypothesis that $F$ is evaluable and $F^{*}$ carries the strong topology, the property of being a totally bounded linear mapping is symmetric: $T$ is totally bounded exactly when its adjoint $T^{*}$ is totally bounded. This result forms the general locally convex version of what becomes the classical Schauder theorem when specialized to Banach spaces.
