The Stone-Čech compactification $\beta(X)$ of a completely regular space $X$ is constructed by embedding $X$ into the dual space $M(X)$ of $B(X)$ via the evaluation map $e(x)(f) = f(x)$. Under the weak* topology, $e$ is a homeomorphism onto its image, which lies on the surface of the unit sphere. The closure $\beta(X) = \overline{e[X]}$ is then a compact Hausdorff space containing a dense homeomorphic copy of $X$. This is the maximal compactification of $X$.
