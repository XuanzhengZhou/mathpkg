For locally convex Hausdorff spaces $E$ and $F$ and a weakly continuous linear map $T: E \to F$, equipping $E^{*}$ with the strong topology yields a duality between the images of bounded sets and equicontinuous sets. Specifically, $T$ maps bounded sets to totally bounded sets if and only if the adjoint $T^{*}$ maps equicontinuous sets to totally bounded sets. The proof applies 16A to the pairing $\langle F, F^{*} \rangle$, with $\mathcal{A}$ the family of images of bounded sets and $\mathcal{B}$ the family of equicontinuous sets, together with the identity $\langle T(x), y^{*} \rangle = \langle x, T^{*}(y^{*}) \rangle$.
