A linear mapping $T$ between topological vector spaces is called totally bounded if it sends bounded subsets of the domain to totally bounded subsets of the codomain. This concept generalizes the notion of compact mappings in the context of locally convex spaces, and it plays a central role in Schauder-type theorems concerning the adjoint operator.
