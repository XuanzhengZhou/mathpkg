In the special case where $E$ and $F$ are Banach spaces, the notion of "totally bounded" coincides with "completely continuous" (compact). Schauder's theorem then takes its classical form: a bounded linear operator $T$ between Banach spaces is compact if and only if its adjoint $T^{*}$ is compact. This is a cornerstone result in functional analysis, obtained as a corollary of the general locally convex version.
