*This is a problem statement; no full proof is provided in the source text.* The construction proceeds as follows: let $T: B(Y) \to B(X)$ be the linear mapping induced by $t$ via $(T(g))(x) = g(t(x))$ for $g \in B(Y)$. Then $T^{*}: M(X) \to M(Y)$ maps the unit ball of $M(X)$ into that of $M(Y)$. Restricting $T^{*}$ to $\beta(X) = \overline{e[X]} \subset M(X)$ gives the required extension $F$, since for $x \in X$, one has $T^{*}(e(x)) = e(t(x))$ by duality. Uniqueness follows from continuity and the density of $e[X]$ in $\beta(X)$.
