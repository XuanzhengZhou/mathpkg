For a completely regular topological space $X$, $B(X)$ denotes the set of all bounded continuous real-valued functions on $X$, equipped with the supremum norm $\|f\| = \sup_{x \in X} |f(x)|$. This is a Banach space (in fact, a commutative Banach algebra under pointwise multiplication) and serves as the starting point for the construction of the Stone-Čech compactification of $X$.
