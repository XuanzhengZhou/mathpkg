In a linear topological space, the sum of two closed subspaces is not generally closed. However, a basic result (Theorem 7.4 of Kelley--Namioka) guarantees closure when one of the summands is finite dimensional. Specifically, if $F$ and $G$ are closed subspaces and $G$ is finite dimensional, then $F + G$ remains a closed subspace. This provides a simple sufficient condition and is often used as a building block for more refined criteria, such as the angle condition in Hilbert spaces.
