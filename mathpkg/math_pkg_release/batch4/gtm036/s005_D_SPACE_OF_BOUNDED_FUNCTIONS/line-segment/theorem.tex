Let $E$ be a linear space over $\mathbb{R}$. The \textbf{line segment} joining two points $x, y \in E$, denoted by $[x:y]$, is the set of all points of the form $a x + b y$ where $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$ are non-negative and $a + b = 1$. Equivalently,
$$
[x : y] = \{ a x + (1 - a) y \mid a \in \mathbb{R},\ 0 \leq a \leq 1 \}.
$$
