The line segment $[x:y]$ is the set of all convex combinations of two points $x$ and $y$ in a real linear space. It represents the "straight line" between $x$ and $y$, including both endpoints. This is a foundational geometric notion in linear spaces: a set $A$ is convex precisely when $[x:y] \subseteq A$ for all $x, y \in A$. The notation $[x:y]$ (with a colon) is used in Kelley-Namioka to distinguish segments from intervals on the real line.
