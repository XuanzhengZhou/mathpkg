Every linear functional defined on a subspace $F$ of a linear space $E$ can be extended to a linear functional on the whole space $E$. This is a purely algebraic fact, proved by extending a Hamel basis of $F$ to a Hamel basis of $E$ (using the Axiom of Choice) and defining the extension arbitrarily on the new basis vectors. This algebraic extension theorem is a precursor to the Hahn-Banach theorem, which additionally guarantees that the extension preserves a given seminorm bound.
