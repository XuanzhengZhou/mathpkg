The First Isomorphism Theorem for linear spaces states that for any linear map $T : E \to F$, the quotient space $E / \ker T$ is isomorphic to the image $T(E)$. The isomorphism is given by the induced map $\tilde{T}(x + \ker T) = T(x)$, which is well-defined, injective, and surjective onto the range. This is a fundamental structural result in linear algebra, completely analogous to the First Isomorphism Theorem for groups, rings, and modules.
