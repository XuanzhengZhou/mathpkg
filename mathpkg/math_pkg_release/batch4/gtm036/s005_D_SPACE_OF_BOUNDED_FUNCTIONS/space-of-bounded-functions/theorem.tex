Let $S$ be an infinite set and let $K$ be the scalar field (either $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$). The \textbf{space of bounded functions} $B(S)$ is the linear space of all bounded scalar valued functions on $S$, with pointwise addition and scalar multiplication:
$$
B(S) = \{ f : S \to K \mid f \text{ is bounded} \}.
$$
