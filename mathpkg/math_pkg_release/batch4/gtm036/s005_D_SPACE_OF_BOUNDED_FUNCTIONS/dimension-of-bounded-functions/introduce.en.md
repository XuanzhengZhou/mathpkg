This theorem determines the algebraic (Hamel) dimension of the space $B(S)$ of all bounded scalar-valued functions on an infinite set $S$. The dimension equals $c^s$, where $s = |S|$ is the cardinality of the domain and $c$ is the cardinality of the scalar field. The result demonstrates that infinite-dimensional function spaces can have very large dimension: for example, $B(\mathbb{N})$ over $\mathbb{R}$ has dimension $c^{\aleph_0} = \mathfrak{c}$, the cardinality of the continuum. The proof combines an upper bound from counting all functions with a lower bound from the linearly independent family $\{u_\alpha\}$.
