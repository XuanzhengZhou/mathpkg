This lemma provides a convenient algebraic characterization of extreme points in the context of ordered linear topological spaces equipped with a positive linear functional. Given a compact convex subset $A$ of the positive cone satisfying certain order-theoretic and functional conditions, an element $x \in A$ is extreme precisely when every $y$ between $0$ and $x$ is a scalar multiple of $x$, specifically $y = f(y)x$. This result serves as a key technical tool in the proof of the Krein-Milman-type theorem (Theorem 15.1) on the existence of extreme points.
