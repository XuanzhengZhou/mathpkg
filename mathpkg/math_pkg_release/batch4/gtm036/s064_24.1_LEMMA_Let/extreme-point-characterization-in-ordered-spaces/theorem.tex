Let $E$ be an ordered linear topological space, let $f$ be a positive linear functional on $E$, and let $A$ be a subset of the positive cone of $E$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] the set $A$ is compact and convex, and $f(x) \leq 1$ for each $x$ in $A$;
\item[(ii)] if $x \in A$ and $x \geq y \geq 0$, then $y \in A$; and
\item[(iii)] if $x \in A$ and $x \neq 0$, then $f(x) \neq 0$ and $x/f(x)$ is a member of $A$.
\end{enumerate}
Then a member $x$ of $A$ is an extreme point of $A$ if and only if for each $y$ such that $x \geq y \geq 0$ it is true that $y = f(y)x$.
