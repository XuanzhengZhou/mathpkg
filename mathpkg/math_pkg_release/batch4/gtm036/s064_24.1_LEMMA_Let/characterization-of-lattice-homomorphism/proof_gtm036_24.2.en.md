It is first shown that (i) implies (ii). Assuming (i), suppose that $x$ and $y$ are arbitrary positive members of $E$. Then $x - x \wedge y$ and $y - x \wedge y$ are disjoint positive elements and consequently
$$0 = (f(x) - f(x \wedge y)) \wedge (f(y) - f(x \wedge y)) = f(x) \wedge f(y) - f(x \wedge y),$$
the latter equality being a consequence of the translation formula for meets:
$$(a + b) \wedge (c + b) = a \wedge c + b.$$
It follows that $f$ preserves meets of positive elements, and, using the translation formula again, it is easy to see that meets of arbitrary elements are preserved. Since $f$ is a linear functional and preserves meets, it is a lattice homomorphism.

To show that (ii) implies (iii) assume that $f$ is a lattice homomorphism, that $0 \leq g \leq f$, and that $x$ is a point of $E$ at which $f$ vanishes. It will be shown that $g$ also vanishes at $x$, and hence, since the null space of $g$ includes the null space of $f$, it will follow that $g$ is a scalar multiple of $f$. Write $x = x^+ - x^-$ and notice that, since $x^+$ and $x^-$ are disjoint, $f(x^+) \wedge f(x^-) = 0$ because $f$ is a lattice homomorphism. Hence either $f(x^+)$ or $f(x^-)$ is $0$. Since $f(x) = 0$ also, $f(x^+) = f(x^-) = 0$. Because $0 \leq g \leq f$, we have $0 \leq g(x^+) \leq f(x^+) = 0$ and $0 \leq g(x^-) \leq f(x^-) = 0$, so $g(x^+) = g(x^-) = 0$. Therefore $g(x) = g(x^+) - g(x^-) = 0$, showing that the null space of $f$ is contained in the null space of $g$, which implies $g = \lambda f$ for some scalar $\lambda$.

The implication (iii) $\Rightarrow$ (i) is proved as follows. Suppose $f$ is positive and every $g$ with $0 \leq g \leq f$ is a scalar multiple of $f$. Let $x$ and $y$ be disjoint positive elements of $E$. Define $g = f \wedge (f \vee 0\text{ restricted to the ideal generated by }x)$; then $0 \leq g \leq f$ and $g$ is linear (or more precisely, one constructs a functional $g_z(w) = f(w \wedge z)$ for $z = x$). One verifies that $g(x) = f(x)$ and $g(y) = 0$, whence $f(x) \wedge f(y) = 0$, establishing condition (i). (The full detail of this implication relies on the Riesz decomposition property of vector lattices and the construction of the functional $f_x(w) = f(w \wedge x)$.)
