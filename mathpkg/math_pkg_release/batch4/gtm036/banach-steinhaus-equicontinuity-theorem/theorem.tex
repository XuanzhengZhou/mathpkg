Let $E$ be a linear topological space of the second category, let $F$ be a linear topological space, and let $\mathcal{F}$ be a family of continuous linear maps from $E$ to $F$ such that for each $x$ in $E$, the set $\{T(x) : T \in \mathcal{F}\}$ is bounded in $F$. Then $\mathcal{F}$ is equicontinuous.
