This lemma establishes the order completeness of $L$-spaces and their adjoints. Every subset with an upper bound possesses a least upper bound, and every monotonically increasing bounded net is Cauchy (hence convergent, since $L$-spaces are Banach spaces). The proof uses the additivity of the norm on the positive cone to show that the net of distances forms a convergent real sequence, which forces the net itself to be Cauchy.
