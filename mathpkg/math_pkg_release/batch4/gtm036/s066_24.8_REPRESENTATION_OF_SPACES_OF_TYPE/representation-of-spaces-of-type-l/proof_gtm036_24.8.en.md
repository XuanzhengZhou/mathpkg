The proof of this theorem follows from the construction developed in the preceding sections of Chapter 24. The map which carries a member $x \in E$ into the functional on $C(X)$ whose value at $f$ is $\int f\,dm$ is an isometry and order preserving. Specifically, there is a compact subset $C$ of $B$ with the property $|m(B) - m(C)| < \varepsilon$, and the variation $\|m\|$ of $m$ is $\sup\{m(A) - m(B) : A \text{ and } B \text{ disjoint members of } \mathcal{B}\}$. For details, see Halmos [4].
