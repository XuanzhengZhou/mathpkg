This lemma is the crucial step in the characterization of $L$-spaces. Given any nonzero positive regular Borel measure $n$ that vanishes on first-category Borel sets, there exists a nonzero element $x \in E$ whose representing measure $m_x$ is strictly dominated by $n$. The proof uses a set-theoretic lemma to approximate Borel sets by clopen sets up to first category, constructs an open-closed set $A$ using Lemma 24.11, and then applies the Radon-Nikodym theorem to find a density function whose level sets yield the desired dominating measure.
