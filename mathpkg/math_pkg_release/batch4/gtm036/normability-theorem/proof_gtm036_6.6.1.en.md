If there is a bounded convex neighborhood of $0$ in a linear topological space $E$, then there is a bounded circled convex neighborhood $U$ of $0$. Let $p$ be the Minkowski functional of $U$: $p(x) = \inf \{t : t > 0 \text{ and } x \in tU\}$. Then $p$ is a pseudo-norm, and the open unit $p$-sphere about $0$ contains $(1/2)U$ and is contained in $U$. Hence the pseudo-norm topology coincides with the given topology.
