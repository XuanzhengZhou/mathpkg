The projective topology on a linear space $F$ is the coarsest (weakest) topology that renders every map in a given family of linear mappings $g_t: F \to F_t$ continuous, where each codomain $F_t$ is a locally convex space. It is the topological vector space analogue of the initial topology in general topology. When all $g_t$ separate points (i.e., $\bigcap_t g_t^{-1}(0) = \{0\}$), the projective topology is Hausdorff and $F$ can be identified with a projective limit of its images $g_t[F]$.
