The common null space $N = \bigcap_t g_t^{-1}(0)$ captures all directions in $F$ that are annihilated by every map $g_t$. This proposition shows that the projective topology decomposes $F$ as the product of $F/N$ (carrying the quotient of the projective topology) and $N$ (carrying the trivial, indiscrete topology). Consequently, assuming $N = \{0\}$ involves no loss of generality for Hausdorff projective limits: the non-Hausdorff part is entirely confined to the trivial factor $N$.
