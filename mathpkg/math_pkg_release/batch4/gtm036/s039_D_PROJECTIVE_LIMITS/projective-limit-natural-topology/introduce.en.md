A projective limit of locally convex spaces carries a natural topology obtained by restricting the product topology to the limit subspace. This relative product topology is itself a projective topology in the sense defined above, with the projections onto the coordinate spaces serving as the defining family of linear maps. In other words, every projective limit is a concrete instance where the projective topology appears as the subspace topology inherited from the ambient product.
