A family $\mathcal{U}$ of subsets of a linear space $E$ is a local base for a locally convex vector topology for $E$ if the following conditions are satisfied.

(i) Each $U \in \mathcal{U}$ is midpoint convex $(U + U \subset 2U)$, circled and radial at 0;

(ii) the intersection of any two members of $\mathcal{U}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{U}$;

(iii) for each $U \in \mathcal{U}$ there is some $V \in \mathcal{U}$ with $2V \subset U$.
