This exercise identifies the strongest vector topology on a linear space $E$ as the one induced by the family of all pseudo-norms on $E$ (i.e., all functions satisfying axioms (i)--(iv) from part (a)). This topology is the finest among all vector topologies on $E$. It provides a concrete description of the maximal vector topology, which plays an important role in the study of bornological and ultrabornological spaces.
