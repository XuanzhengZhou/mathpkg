Given any family $\{q_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$ of functions on a linear space $E$, each satisfying (i)--(iv), the finite intersections of sets of the type $\{x : q_\alpha(x) < \varepsilon\}$, with $\alpha \in A$ and $\varepsilon > 0$, form a local base for a vector topology on $E$. This is the topology determined by the family $\{q_\alpha\}$. Conversely, every linear topological space has a topology which can be determined in this way.
