This exercise characterizes when a product of linear topological spaces is pseudo-normable. The necessary and sufficient condition is that only finitely many factors are non-trivial and pseudo-normable, while all remaining factors carry the trivial (indiscrete) topology. This reflects the fact that pseudo-metrizability (first countability) does not survive infinite products in the category of topological vector spaces.
