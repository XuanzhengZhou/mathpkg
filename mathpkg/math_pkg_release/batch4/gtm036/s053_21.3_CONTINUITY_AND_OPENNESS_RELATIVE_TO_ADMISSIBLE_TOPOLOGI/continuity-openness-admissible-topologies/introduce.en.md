Theorem 21.3 is the principal result of section 21.3, providing necessary and sufficient conditions for a linear transformation between paired linear spaces to be continuous or relatively open with respect to admissible topologies. An admissible topology $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ on $E$ is a topology of uniform convergence on members of an admissible family $\mathcal{A}$ of weakly bounded, closed, convex, circled subsets. The conditions involve inclusions of closures of images under the dual transformation $T'$, establishing a fundamental duality between continuity properties of $T$ and topological properties of the dual map.
