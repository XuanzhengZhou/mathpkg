Theorem 21.5 specializes the dual transformation theory of 21.1 to the natural pairings of locally convex spaces. It establishes that a continuous linear transformation is always weakly continuous, and that the adjoint exists precisely under weak continuity. The adjoint is unique and automatically weak$^*$ continuous. Conversely, any weak$^*$ continuous map $S: F^* \to E^*$ is the adjoint of a uniquely determined weakly continuous transformation $T: E \to F$.
