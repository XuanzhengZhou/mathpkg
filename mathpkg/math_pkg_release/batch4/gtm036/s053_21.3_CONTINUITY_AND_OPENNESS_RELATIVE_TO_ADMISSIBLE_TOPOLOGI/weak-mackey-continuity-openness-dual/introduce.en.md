Theorem 21.4 establishes equivalences between the weak and Mackey continuity and openness properties of a linear transformation $T$ between paired linear spaces, in terms of properties of its dual transformation $T'$. When pairings are separated, weak continuity and openness of $T$ correspond to $T'$ being a weak topological isomorphism onto a weakly closed subspace. Under mild separation conditions, Mackey continuity and openness reduce to weak continuity and openness. The dual of $T$, when it exists, is always strongly continuous.
