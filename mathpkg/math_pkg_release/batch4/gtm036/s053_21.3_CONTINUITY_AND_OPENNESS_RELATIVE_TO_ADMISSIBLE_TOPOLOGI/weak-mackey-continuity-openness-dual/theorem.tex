\begin{theorem}[Weak and Mackey Continuity and Openness of the Dual Transformation]
\label{thm:21.4}
Let $(E,F)$ and $(G,H)$ be paired linear spaces and let $T$ be a linear transformation of $E$ into $G$.

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $T$ is Mackey continuous if and only if $T$ is weakly continuous.

\item[(ii)] $T$ possesses a dual transformation $T'$ with weakly closed range in $F$ if and only if $T$ is weakly continuous and weakly relatively open.

\item[(iii)] In case the pairings are separated, $T$ is weakly continuous and open if and only if $T'$ is a weakly topological isomorphism of $H$ onto a weakly closed subspace of $F$.

\item[(iv)] In case $E$ distinguishes points of $F$ and the pairing $\langle G,H\rangle$ is separated, $T$ is Mackey continuous and open if and only if $T$ is weakly continuous and open.

\item[(v)] If $T'$ is a dual of $T$, then $T'$ is strongly continuous.
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}
