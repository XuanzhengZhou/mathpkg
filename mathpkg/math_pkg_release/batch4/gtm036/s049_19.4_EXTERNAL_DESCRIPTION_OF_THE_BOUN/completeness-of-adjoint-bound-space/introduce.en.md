This problem (Part C of the Chapter 19 exercises) sharpens the completeness results for the adjoint $E^*$ of a bound space $E$. The adjoint is always complete under the strong topology. Under mild additional hypotheses (such as every convergent sequence being absorbed by an admissible set, or $E$ itself being complete), $E^*$ is complete under a wide range of admissible topologies, including the Mackey topology $m(E^*, E)$. This shows that bound spaces possess strong completeness properties in their duals.
