Each $w(E^b, E)$-compact circled convex subset of $E^b$ is strongly bounded, by 18.5, and is hence $\mathcal{T}^b$-equicontinuous by 19.3(ii). Consequently each $m(E, E^b)$-neighborhood of $0$ is a $\mathcal{T}^b$-neighborhood of $0$, and hence $\mathcal{T}^b$ is stronger than $m(E, E^b)$. But $E^b$ is the space of all $\mathcal{T}^b$-continuous linear functionals, and by 18.8, $m(E, E^b)$ is the strongest locally convex topology which yields $E^b$ as adjoint. Hence $\mathcal{T}^b = m(E, E^b)$.
