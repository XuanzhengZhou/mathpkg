For a locally convex space $(E, \mathcal{T})$, the bound extension $\mathcal{T}^b$ is a topology defined internally (without reference to the dual space). This theorem gives an external characterization: $\mathcal{T}^b$ coincides with the Mackey topology $m(E, E^b)$, where $E^b$ is the space of all bounded linear functionals on $E$. This connects the notion of bound extension to the Mackey-Arens theory of compatible topologies.
