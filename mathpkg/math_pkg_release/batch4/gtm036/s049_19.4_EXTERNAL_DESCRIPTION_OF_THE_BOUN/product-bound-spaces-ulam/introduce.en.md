This remarkable theorem connects the purely functional-analytic property of being a bound space with the set-theoretic notion of measurable cardinals. The product of an arbitrary family of bound spaces is again a bound space precisely when the index set's cardinal does not carry an Ulam measure. Since the existence of Ulam measures is independent of ZFC, whether all products of bound spaces are bound is similarly undecidable in standard set theory.
