If Ulam measures exist at all, the smallest cardinal $\aleph'$ admitting one is a measurable cardinal with strong closure properties: it is strongly inaccessible and exceeds the powerset cardinal $2^{\aleph}$ of every smaller cardinal $\aleph$. This result of Ulam shows that the existence of such a cardinal cannot be proved in ZFC alone and has profound consequences for the consistency strength of set theory.
