The intersection of an arbitrary family of cones is again a cone. If the family is directed by $\subset$, then the union of the members is also a cone.

Any finite linear combination of cones is a cone.

For any set $A$, let $C(A)$ temporarily denote the conical extension of $A$. Then:
$$
C(A) = \bigcup \{aA : a \geq 0\}.
$$
For any two sets $A$ and $B$ and scalars $a$ and $b$,
$$
C(aA + bB) \subset a C(A) + b C(B).
$$
For any family $\{A_t : t \in B\}$ of sets,
$$
C\left(\bigcup \{A_t : t \in B\}\right) = C\left(\bigcup \{C(A_t) : t \in B\}\right).
$$
