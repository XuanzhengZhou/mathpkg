For a real-valued function $p$ on a linear space $E$ with $p(0)=0$, the properties of non-negative homogeneity, subadditivity, and convexity are pairwise equivalent. That is, any two of these three fundamental properties imply the third. This theorem provides a key structural link between the geometric notion of convexity and the algebraic properties of homogeneity and subadditivity for functionals that vanish at the origin.
