A vector ordering on a real linear space is a partial order compatible with the linear structure (i.e., $x \geq y$ implies $x+z \geq y+z$ and $\lambda x \geq \lambda y$ for $\lambda \geq 0$). On the two-dimensional plane $\mathbb{R}^2$, there are exactly ten isomorphism classes of such orderings. This classification reveals the finite yet nontrivial diversity of compatible order structures even in the simplest nontrivial real vector space.
