The text states this classification result without proof. A vector ordering on $\mathbb{R}^2$ is determined by its positive cone $P = \{x \in \mathbb{R}^2 : x \geq 0\}$, which must be a proper convex cone such that $P \cap (-P) = \{0\}$ and $P \cup (-P) = \mathbb{R}^2$ (for a total ordering) or weaker conditions for a partial ordering. The ten equivalence classes arise from the combinatorial classification of such cones in the plane, distinguished by the angular width and boundary structure of $P$. Specifically, the positive cone is always a convex cone with apex at the origin; in $\mathbb{R}^2$ the boundary of such a cone consists of two rays from the origin, and the classification depends on whether these boundary rays are included in $P$ and the angle between them.
