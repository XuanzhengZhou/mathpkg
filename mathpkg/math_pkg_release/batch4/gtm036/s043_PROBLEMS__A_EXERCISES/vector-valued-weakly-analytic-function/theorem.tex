Let $D$ be a domain in the complex plane and let $E$ be a sequentially complete locally convex Hausdorff space. A mapping $x : D \to E$ is called \textit{weakly analytic} in $D$ if and only if it is analytic in $D$ relative to the weak topology on $E$.

Equivalently, $x$ is weakly analytic in $D$ if and only if for each $f \in E^*$, the complex-valued function
$$x_f(z) = f(x(z))$$
is analytic in $D$ in the usual sense.
