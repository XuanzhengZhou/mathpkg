A total subset of a linear topological space is one whose continuous linear functionals determine the zero functional uniquely. This concept generalizes the idea of a set whose linear span is weakly dense. The three equivalent characterizations given here connect the vanishing of functionals, the intersection of annihilator kernels, and density in the weak topology, forming a cornerstone of duality theory in functional analysis.
