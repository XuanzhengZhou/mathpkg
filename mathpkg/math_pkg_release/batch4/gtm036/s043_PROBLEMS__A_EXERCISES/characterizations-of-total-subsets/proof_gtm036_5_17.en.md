This proposition is stated as Problem B(a) in the text; the proof is left to the reader. The equivalence of the three conditions follows from the Hahn-Banach theorem and the definition of the weak topology $w(E, E^*)$.

(i) $\Leftrightarrow$ (ii): The condition $\bigcap_{x \in A} N_x = \{0\}$ means precisely that there is no nonzero $f \in E^*$ such that $f(x) = 0$ for all $x \in A$, which is exactly condition (i).

(i) $\Leftrightarrow$ (iii): By the Hahn-Banach theorem, the weak closure of the subspace generated by $A$ equals the double polar $(A^\circ)^\circ$. Condition (i) states that $A^\circ = \{0\}$, so the double polar is the whole space $E$, which means the subspace generated by $A$ is weakly dense.
