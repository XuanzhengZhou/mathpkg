This theorem describes the behavior of adjoint spaces under basic linear-topological constructions: quotients, subspaces, and direct sums, each equipped with the weak* topology. It establishes that the adjoint operation interchanges quotients with polars, subspaces with quotient duals, and direct sums with products in a topologically isomorphic manner. These dualities are fundamental in the theory of locally convex spaces and are applied throughout duality theory, particularly in the study of reflexive spaces and the Mackey-Arens theorem.
