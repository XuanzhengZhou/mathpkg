**Proof sketch (left to the reader in the text).** The proof may be accomplished by identifying the adjoint via 14.5 and 14.7, then applying 16.11 to establish parts (i) and (ii), and making a simple direct verification to establish part (iii).

For (i) and (ii), one identifies the relevant adjoint map via properties of the transpose of a continuous linear map (14.5) and the fact that the adjoint of a quotient (resp. subspace) embedding corresponds to an embedding into the polar (resp. quotient onto the dual of the subspace). Theorem 16.11 provides the topological isomorphism between the polar with the relativized weak* topology and the dual of the quotient with its weak* topology.

For (iii), the isomorphism $(\bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha)^* \cong \prod_\alpha E_\alpha^*$ as vector spaces follows from the universal property of the direct sum. One then verifies directly that this algebraic isomorphism is also a homeomorphism when both sides carry the weak* topology.
