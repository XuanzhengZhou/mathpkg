This exercise reveals a fundamental property of weak neighborhoods: the subspace $N = \bigcap_{\varepsilon > 0} \varepsilon U$ (the "core" of the weak neighborhood $U$) has finite codimension. This means that weak neighborhoods, despite being large in the sense of the original topology, have a finite-dimensional obstruction. The result is key to understanding the coarse nature of the weak topology and its relationship to finite-dimensional subspaces.
