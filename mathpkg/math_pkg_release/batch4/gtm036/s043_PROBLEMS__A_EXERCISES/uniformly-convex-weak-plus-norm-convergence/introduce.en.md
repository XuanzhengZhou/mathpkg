This result characterizes norm convergence in uniformly convex spaces: weak convergence together with convergence of the norms implies convergence in norm. This is the Radon-Riesz property (or Kadets-Klee property) of uniformly convex spaces. The proof relies on the geometric property that the unit ball is "strictly round" — the midpoint of two distinct points on the unit sphere lies strictly inside the ball.
