A vector-valued analytic function generalizes the classical notion of a complex-analytic function to mappings taking values in a sequentially complete locally convex Hausdorff space. The definition mimics the standard complex derivative: the limit of the difference quotient must exist in the topology of the target space. Sequential completeness of the target space is required to ensure that Cauchy criteria can be applied. This concept is fundamental in the theory of vector-valued holomorphic functions and finds applications in spectral theory and the functional calculus for operators on Banach spaces.
