This is stated as Problem C(b) in the text; the proof is left to the reader. One approach is to define the norm on the product $E \times F$ by $\|(x,y)\|_p = (\|x\|_E^p + \|y\|_F^p)^{1/p}$ for $1 < p < \infty$. Since $\ell^p$-sums of uniformly convex spaces are uniformly convex (a consequence of Clarkson's inequalities or the fact that $L^p$ spaces are uniformly convex for $1 < p < \infty$), this yields an equivalent uniformly convex norm on the product.
