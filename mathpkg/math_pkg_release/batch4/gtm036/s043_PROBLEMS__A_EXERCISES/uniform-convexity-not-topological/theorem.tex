Neither uniform convexity nor the convergence property that $x_\alpha \to x_0$ in norm if and only if $x_\alpha \to x_0$ weakly and $\|x_\alpha\| \to \|x_0\|$ is a topological property. That is, these properties depend on the particular norm chosen and are not invariant under arbitrary equivalent renormings.

\textit{Counterexamples:} For uniform convexity, the Euclidean plane provides a counterexample (it is uniformly convex under the Euclidean norm, but can be equivalently renormed with a non-uniformly-convex norm such as the $\ell^1$ norm). For the convergence property, consider the product of a uniformly convex infinite-dimensional normed space and the real numbers, equipped with a suitable equivalent norm.
