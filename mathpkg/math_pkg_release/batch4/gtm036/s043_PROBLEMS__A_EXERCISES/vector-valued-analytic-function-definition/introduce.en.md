This definition extends the notion of analyticity from scalar-valued to vector-valued functions. A mapping from a complex domain into a sequentially complete locally convex Hausdorff space is analytic if the complex derivative exists as a limit in the topology of $E$. A weaker notion, weak analyticity, requires only that each scalar function $f \circ x$ ($f \in E^*$) be analytic. Remarkably, these two notions coincide for sequentially complete locally convex spaces — a vector-valued version of the classical result that separate analyticity implies joint analyticity.
