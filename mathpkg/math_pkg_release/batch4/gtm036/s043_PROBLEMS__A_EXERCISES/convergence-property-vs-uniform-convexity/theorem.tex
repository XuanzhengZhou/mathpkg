The convergence property (that $x_\alpha \to x_0$ in norm if and only if $x_\alpha \to x_0$ weakly and $\|x_\alpha\| \to \|x_0\|$) does not imply uniform convexity.

\textit{Counterexample:} Consider the space $\ell^1$ of absolutely summable sequences $x = \{\xi_n\}$ with norm $\|x\| = \sum_{n=1}^\infty |\xi_n|$. This space has the Schur property (weak sequential convergence implies norm convergence), hence it satisfies the convergence property. However, $\ell^1$ is not uniformly convex (its unit ball has flat faces).

\textit{Reference:} Banach [1] Ch. IX, §3, p. 140.
