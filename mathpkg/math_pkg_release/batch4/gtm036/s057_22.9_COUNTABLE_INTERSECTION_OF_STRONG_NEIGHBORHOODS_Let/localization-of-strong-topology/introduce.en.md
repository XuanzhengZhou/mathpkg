This theorem provides a localization property of the strong topology on the adjoint $E^*$ of a pseudo-metrizable locally convex space. It characterizes strong neighborhoods of zero by their behavior on strongly bounded subsets: a convex circled set is a strong neighborhood if and only if its intersection with each strongly bounded convex circled set is a neighborhood in that bounded set. The result implies that the strong topology is the strongest locally convex topology that agrees with the strong topology on strongly bounded (equivalently, equicontinuous) subsets of $E^*$.
