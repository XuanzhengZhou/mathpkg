The **Banach condensation theorem** (Theorem 9.1) is a fundamental result in Baire category theory. It states that if $A$ is a subset of a topological space $X$, and $W$ is the union of all open sets whose intersection with $A$ is of the first category, then the intersection of $A$ with the closure of $W$ is also of the first category. A key consequence: if $A$ is of the second category in $X$, then there exists a nonempty open set $W$ such that every nonempty open subset of $W$ meets $A$ in a set of the second category — that is, $A$ is "locally of the second category" at the points of $W$.
