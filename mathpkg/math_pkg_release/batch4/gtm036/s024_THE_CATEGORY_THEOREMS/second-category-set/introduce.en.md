A subset $A$ of a topological space $X$ is said to be **of the second category** in $X$ (also called *inexhaustible*) if it is not of the first category. In other words, $A$ cannot be expressed as a countable union of nowhere dense subsets of $X$. Sets of the second category are the "large" or "thick" sets in the sense of Baire category. This notion is the foundation for the Baire category theorem, which asserts that complete metric spaces and locally compact Hausdorff spaces are of the second category in themselves.
