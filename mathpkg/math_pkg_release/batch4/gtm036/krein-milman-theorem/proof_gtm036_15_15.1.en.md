Let R be any closed (hence compact) support of A; then by the maximal principle there exists a family of closed supports of A which is maximal with respect to: (i) R is in the family; and (ii) the family has the finite intersection property. Let S be the intersection of all members of the family. Then S is a non-void closed support of A since A is compact. Furthermore, S is a minimal closed support of A. Let S be any minimal closed support of A, and assume that a line segment [x:y] with x not equal to y is contained in S. Then there exists a continuous linear functional f on the real restriction of E such that f(x) is not equal to f(y). Since S is compact, f is bounded on S, and the set where f attains its supremum on S is a proper closed support of S, contradicting minimality. Hence S is a singleton, which is an extreme point of A.
