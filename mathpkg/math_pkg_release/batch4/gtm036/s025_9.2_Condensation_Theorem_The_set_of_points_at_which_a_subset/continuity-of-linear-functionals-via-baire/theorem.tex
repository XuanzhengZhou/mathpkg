Let $f$ be a linear functional on a linear topological space $E$, let $r$ be the real part of $f$, and let $A$ be a set which is of the second category in $E$ and satisfies the condition of Baire. Then $f$ is continuous if

(i) $r$ is bounded from above on $A$; or

(ii) $r$ is upper semi-continuous on $A$.
