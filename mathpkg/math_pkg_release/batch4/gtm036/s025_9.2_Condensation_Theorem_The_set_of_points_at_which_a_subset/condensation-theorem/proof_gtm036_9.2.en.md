Let $W$ be the set of points at which $A$ is of the first category. By definition, $W$ is open, and by theorem 9.1, $W$ is identical with the interior of $W^{-}$. Consequently, $A$ is of the second category precisely at the points of the complement of the interior of $W^{-}$, which is the closure of $X \setminus W^{-}$. Setting $V = X \setminus W^{-}$, we obtain that the set of points of second category for $A$ is $V^{-}$. Moreover, $A \cap (X \setminus V) = A \cap W^{-}$ is of the first category since $W \cap A$ is of the first category.
