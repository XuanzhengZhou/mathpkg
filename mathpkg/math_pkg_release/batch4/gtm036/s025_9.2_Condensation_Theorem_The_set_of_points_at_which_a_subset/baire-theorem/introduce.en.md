The Baire theorem is the fundamental result guaranteeing that certain sets are of the second category. It deduces a topological conclusion (second category) from a metric premise (completeness with respect to a pseudo-metric). The proof constructs a nested sequence of closed spheres with radii tending to zero, whose intersection yields a point in the complement of any given countable union of nowhere dense sets.
