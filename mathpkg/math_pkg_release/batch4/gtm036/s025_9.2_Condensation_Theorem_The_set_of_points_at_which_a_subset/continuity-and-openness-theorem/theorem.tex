\textbf{10.7 Continuity and Openness.} Let $E$ and $F$ be linear topological spaces and $T$ an additive map of $E$ into $F$. If for each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $F$ the set $T^{-1}[V]$ satisfies the condition of Baire, then $T$ is continuous provided $E$ is of the second category. Dually, if for each neighborhood $U$ of $0$ in $E$ the set $T[U]$ is radial at $0$ and satisfies the condition of Baire, then $T$ is an open mapping onto $F$ provided $F$ is of the second category.
