**Sequential Convergence in $L^1(X, \mu)$.** Let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence of elements of $L^1(X, \mu)$ such that $\{\int_A f_n d\mu\}$ converges for each measurable subset $A$ of $X$.

(a) Show that there exists $f \in L^1(X, \mu)$ with $\lim \int_A f_n d\mu = \int_A f d\mu$ for each measurable $A \subset X$.

*Hint:* Use the previous problem (H) with $\phi_n(A) = \int_A f_n d\mu$ and the Radon-Nikodym theorem.

(b) Suppose also that $\{f_n\}$ is convergent in measure on every subset of finite measure. Show that $\{f_n\}$ is convergent in norm.
