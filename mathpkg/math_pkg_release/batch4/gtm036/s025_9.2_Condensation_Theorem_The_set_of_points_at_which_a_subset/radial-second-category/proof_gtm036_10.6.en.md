The first assertion was proved earlier: each set $A$ which is radial at $0$ is of the second category in $E$ because $E \subset \bigcup \{nA : n = 1, 2, \cdots\}$; hence $nA$ is of the second category in $E$ for some $n$, and therefore $A$ itself is of the second category in $E$. By a translation argument, every set radial at some point is of the second category. The second assertion is a direct consequence of the difference theorem.
