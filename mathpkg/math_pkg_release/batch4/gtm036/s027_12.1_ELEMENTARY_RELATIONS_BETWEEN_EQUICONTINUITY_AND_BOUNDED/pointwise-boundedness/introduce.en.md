Pointwise boundedness is a localized boundedness condition for a family $F$ of linear functions from $E$ to $H$. It requires that for each individual point $x \in A$, the image set $\{f(x) : f \in F\}$ is bounded in $H$. This is the weakest boundedness condition commonly considered for families of linear maps: it is implied by uniform boundedness on $A$, and also by equicontinuity of $F$ on bounded subsets. Pointwise boundedness is equivalent to boundedness in the topology of pointwise convergence on $A$, making it the natural boundedness notion for that topology.
