The Banach-Steinhaus theorem (also known as the Uniform Boundedness Principle) states that a pointwise bounded family of continuous linear maps on a second-category set is automatically equicontinuous. This is one of the fundamental principles of functional analysis, along with the Hahn-Banach theorem and the Open Mapping theorem.
