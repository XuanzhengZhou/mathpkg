A barrel (also called a disk) is a fundamental concept in the theory of locally convex spaces. It is a closed, circled, convex set that is radial at the origin. In second-category spaces, every barrel is a neighborhood of 0, which leads to the notion of barrelled spaces. This simple property underlies much of the duality theory.
