This proposition is a simple but deep fact about linear topological spaces: in any space of second category, every barrel must be a neighborhood of $0$. The proof is geometric: a barrel $D$, being radial at $0$, satisfies $E = \bigcup_n nD$, so some $nD$ (and hence $D$) is of second category; since $D$ is closed, its interior is nonempty, and $D - D = 2D$ is a neighborhood of $0$ by convexity and circledness. This fact underlies the geometric proof of the Banach-Steinhaus theorem and motivates the definition of barrelled spaces.
