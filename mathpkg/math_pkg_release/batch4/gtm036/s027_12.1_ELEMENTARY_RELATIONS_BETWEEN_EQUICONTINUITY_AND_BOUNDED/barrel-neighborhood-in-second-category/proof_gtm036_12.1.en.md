Let $D$ be a barrel in $E$, so $D$ is closed, circled, convex, and radial at $0$. Since $D$ is radial at $0$, for each $x \in E$ there exists some scalar $t$ such that $x \in tD$. Hence $E = \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} nD$.

Since $E$ is of the second category in itself, the union $\bigcup_n nD$ cannot be all of first category. Therefore some integer multiple $nD$ is of the second category in $E$. Since multiplication by a fixed nonzero scalar is a homeomorphism, $D$ itself (being homeomorphic to $nD$) is also of the second category in $E$.

The set $D$ is closed by definition of a barrel. A closed set of second category must have non-void interior. Hence the interior $D^{\circ}$ is non-empty.

Since $D$ is convex and circled, and $0 \in D$, we have $D + D = 2D$. Moreover, because $D$ is circled, $-D = D$. Therefore $D - D = D + D = 2D$. As $D$ has non-empty interior, $D - D = D + D$ contains a neighborhood of $0$. In particular, $2D$ and hence $D$ itself is a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$.
