**B. THE PRINCIPLE OF CONDENSATION OF SINGULARITIES**

Let $\{T_{m,n} : m,n = 1, 2, \cdots\}$ be a double sequence of continuous linear maps of a pseudo-normable space $E$ [of the second category into a pseudo-normed space $F$. If for each $m$ there exists $x_m \in E$ such that $\sup_n \|T_{m,n}(x_m)\| = \infty$, then there exists $x \in E$ such that $\sup_n \|T_{m,n}(x)\| = \infty$ for every $m$.]
