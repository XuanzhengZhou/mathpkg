Theorem 12.4 establishes uniform boundedness from pointwise boundedness in a situation where equicontinuity may not hold, provided the target space is locally convex and the set $A$ satisfies certain completeness conditions. The proof uses the absorption theorem (Corollary 10.3): the intersection $\bigcap\{f^{-1}[V] : f \in F\}$ absorbs each point of $A$ by pointwise boundedness (Theorem 12.1(iv)), and under the stated hypotheses on $A$, pointwise absorption implies absorption of the whole set $A$, which yields uniform boundedness. This result bridges the gap between the Banach-Steinhaus theorem (which yields equicontinuity under second category hypotheses) and the general relationship (iii)$\Rightarrow$(iv) in Theorem 12.1.
