Uniform boundedness on a set $A$ is a stronger condition than pointwise boundedness: it requires that the entire image $F[A]$ is a bounded subset of $H$, rather than requiring only that each fiber $F[x]$ is bounded. This condition sits between equicontinuity and pointwise boundedness in the hierarchy of boundedness notions for families of linear maps. Uniform boundedness on $A$ is equivalent to being bounded in the topology of uniform convergence on $A$, and it implies that for each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $H$, the intersection $\bigcap\{f^{-1}[V] : f \in F\}$ absorbs the set $A$.
