Theorem 12.1 establishes the fundamental hierarchy relating equicontinuity and various boundedness notions for a family $F$ of linear functions between linear topological spaces. The strongest condition is equicontinuity (i), which implies uniform boundedness on every bounded set (ii). Uniform boundedness on a fixed set $A$ (iii) is characterized by several equivalent conditions, including the boundedness of $F[A]$, boundedness in the topology of uniform convergence on $A$, and absorption of $A$ by inverse images of neighborhoods. Finally, pointwise boundedness (iv) is the weakest condition, characterized by absorption at individual points. The theorem captures the logical relationships: (i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) and (iii) $\Rightarrow$ (iv).
