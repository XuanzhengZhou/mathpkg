The Krein-Milman theorem is one of the central results in functional analysis. It states that every convex compact subset of a locally convex Hausdorff space is the closed convex hull of its extreme points. In particular, such sets must possess extreme points. The theorem has profound applications: it shows that the unit ball of the dual of a Banach space has many extreme points (by Alaoglu's theorem and Krein-Milman), and is fundamental to Choquet theory and the representation of states on C*-algebras.
