Every continuous linear functional on $K(X)$ admits a representation as the difference of two positive linear functionals, which in turn correspond to two regular Borel measures via the Riesz representation theorem. Thus any $\phi \in (K(X), \mathcal{T})^*$ can be written as $\phi(f) = \int f d\mu^+ - \int f d\mu^-$. For complex scalars, the decomposition uses four measures, corresponding to the positive and negative parts of the real and imaginary components. A warning applies: if neither $\mu^+$ nor $\mu^-$ is finite, the formal difference $\mu^+ - \mu^-$ is not a signed measure.
