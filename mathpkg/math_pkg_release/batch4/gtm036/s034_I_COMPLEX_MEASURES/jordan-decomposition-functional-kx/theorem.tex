If $\phi \in (K(X), \mathcal{T})^*$ and the scalars are real, then $\phi$ can be written as $\phi = \phi^+ - \phi^-$, where both $\phi^+$ and $\phi^-$ are positive linear functionals on $K(X)$. Therefore there are two regular Borel measures $\mu^+$ and $\mu^-$ on $X$ such that
$$\phi(f) = \int f \, d\mu^+ - \int f \, d\mu^-$$
for all $f \in K(X)$. If the scalars are complex, then there is a similar representation using four regular Borel measures on $X$.
