A property is said to hold locally almost everywhere if its failure set has zero measure when intersected with any set of finite measure. This is a weakening of "almost everywhere" and is used in the definition of the essential supremum norm for $L^\infty$ on measure spaces that are not totally finite. On a $\sigma$-finite space, locally almost everywhere is equivalent to almost everywhere.
