Since the inductive limit topology on $K(X)$ is stronger than the uniform topology, to each $\phi \in (C_0(X))^*$ corresponds a unique regular Borel measure $\mu$ via the representation for $K(X)$ from part (a). Specifically, the restriction of $\phi$ to $K(X)$ yields a continuous linear functional with respect to the inductive limit topology (since uniform continuity implies $\mathcal{T}$-continuity on order-bounded subsets). By part (a), there exist regular Borel measures such that $\phi(f) = \int f d\mu$ for all $f \in K(X)$.

The density of $K(X)$ in $C_0(X)$ under the uniform norm extends the representation to all of $C_0(X)$. The total variation norm satisfies $\|\phi\| = \|\mu\| = |\mu|(X)$, establishing the isometric isomorphism $(C_0(X))^* \cong M(X)$.

For complex scalars, the same argument applies using the four-measure representation from part (a), which combine into a single complex regular Borel measure.
