The dual of $L^1$ is $L^\infty$ in the sense that every continuous linear functional $\phi$ on $L^1$ has the form $\phi(f) = \int f g d\mu$ for some $g \in L^\infty$. When the measure space is not $\sigma$-finite, $L^\infty$ must be defined using locally measurable functions that are bounded locally almost everywhere. The proof proceeds by constructing $g_A$ on sets of finite measure and then taking a limit over an increasing exhausting sequence.
