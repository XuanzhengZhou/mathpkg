The adjoint of $L^1(X, \mathcal{S}, \mu)$ is isometrically isomorphic to $L^\infty(X, \mu)$, where $L^\infty(X, \mu)$ is the space of locally measurable functions bounded locally almost everywhere. For each continuous linear functional $\phi$ on $L^1$, there exists a unique $g \in L^\infty$ such that
$$\phi(f) = \int_X f g \, d\mu$$
for all $f \in L^1$, and $\|\phi\| = \|g\|_\infty$.
