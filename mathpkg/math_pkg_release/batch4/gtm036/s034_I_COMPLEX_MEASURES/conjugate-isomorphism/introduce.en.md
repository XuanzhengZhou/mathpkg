A conjugate isomorphism (or anti-linear isometric isomorphism) between two complex normed spaces is a bijective mapping that is conjugate-linear: $T(ax + by) = \overline{a}T(x) + \overline{b}T(y)$, and isometric: $\|T(x)\| = \|x\|$. This notion naturally arises in the Riesz representation theorem for Hilbert spaces, where the identification $H^* \to H$ given by $f \mapsto y$ (with $f(x) = (x,y)$) is conjugate-linear because the inner product is conjugate-linear in its second argument.
