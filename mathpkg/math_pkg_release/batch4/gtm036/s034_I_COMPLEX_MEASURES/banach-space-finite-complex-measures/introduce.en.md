The collection of all finite complex measures on a measurable space $(X, \mathcal{S})$ forms a Banach space when equipped with the total variation norm $\|\mu\| = \sup_{A \in \mathcal{S}} |\mu|(A)$. Completeness follows from the fact that the total variation is a positive measure and pointwise limits of Cauchy sequences of measures preserve finite additivity. This space is fundamental in the Riesz representation theory, as it is the dual of $C_0(X)$.
