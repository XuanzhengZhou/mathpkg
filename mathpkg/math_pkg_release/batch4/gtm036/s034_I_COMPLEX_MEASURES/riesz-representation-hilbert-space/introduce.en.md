The Riesz representation theorem for Hilbert spaces states that every continuous linear functional $f$ on a Hilbert space $H$ can be represented uniquely as an inner product $f(x) = (x, y)$ for some $y \in H$. The map $f \mapsto y$ is a conjugate-linear isometric isomorphism between $H^*$ and $H$. This self-duality is a fundamental property distinguishing Hilbert spaces from general Banach spaces.
