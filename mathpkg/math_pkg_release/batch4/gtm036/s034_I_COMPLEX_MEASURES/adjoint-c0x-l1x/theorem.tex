If $\mu$ is a regular Borel measure on the discrete space $X$, there is a function $g$ on $X$ such that
$$\mu(A) = \sum \{g(x): x \in A\}$$
for each subset $A$ of $X$. Consequently, the adjoint of $c_0(X)$ is $l^1(X)$ and the adjoint of $c(Y)$ is $l^1(Y)$, where $c_0(X) = C_0(X)$ for discrete $X$, and $c(Y) = C(Y)$ for the one-point compactification $Y$ of a discrete space $X$. The general form for continuous linear functionals is
$$f \mapsto \sum \{f(x) g(x): x \in X\}.$$
