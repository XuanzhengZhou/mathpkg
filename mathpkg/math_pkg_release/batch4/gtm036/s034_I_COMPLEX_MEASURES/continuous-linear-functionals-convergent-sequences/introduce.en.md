The dual of the space $c$ of convergent sequences is isometrically isomorphic to $l^1$, with the general continuous linear functional taking the form $\{x_n\} \mapsto \sum x_n y_n + (\lim x_n) y_0$ where $\{y_n\}_{n=0}^\infty \in l^1$. The limit term $y_0 \cdot \lim x_n$ accounts for the fact that $c$ is a one-codimensional extension of $c_0$, with the limit functional providing the extra dimension. This result is a special case of the Riesz representation theorem for $C(Y)$ on the one-point compactification of a discrete space.
