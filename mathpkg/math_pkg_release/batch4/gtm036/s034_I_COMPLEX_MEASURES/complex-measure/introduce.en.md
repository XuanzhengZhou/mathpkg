A complex measure generalizes the notion of a signed measure to complex values. It is constructed by taking two signed measures defined on the same $\sigma$-ring and combining them as the real and imaginary parts: $\mu = \mu_1 + i\mu_2$. Complex measures arise naturally when studying the adjoints of spaces of complex-valued continuous functions, as the representing measures are generally complex valued.
