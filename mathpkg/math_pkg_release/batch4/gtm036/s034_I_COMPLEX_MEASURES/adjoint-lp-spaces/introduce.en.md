The dual spaces of the sequence spaces $l^p(X)$ follow the same pattern as the general $L^p$ spaces: $(l^p)^* \cong l^q$ for $1 \leq p < \infty$ with $1/p + 1/q = 1$ (and $q = \infty$ for $p = 1$). The discrete nature of $X$ ensures localizability. However, when $X$ is uncountable and one uses the $\sigma$-ring of countable subsets, the $L^\infty$ functions are only locally measurable, not fully measurable — illustrating the subtlety of the general $L^1$-$L^\infty$ duality.
