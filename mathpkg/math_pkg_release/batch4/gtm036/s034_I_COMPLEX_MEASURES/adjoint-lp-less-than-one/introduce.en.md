When $0 < p < 1$, the $L^p$ spaces are not locally convex and their duality theory is dramatically different. For sequence spaces $l^p(X)$, the dual is $l^\infty(X)$ (using the result from 6N(d)). However, for $L^p$ on a Lebesgue interval, the only continuous linear functional is the zero functional — the space has a trivial dual. This striking contrast highlights the fundamental difference between discrete and continuous measure spaces for quasi-Banach spaces.
