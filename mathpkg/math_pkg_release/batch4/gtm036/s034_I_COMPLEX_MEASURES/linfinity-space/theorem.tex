The functions on $X$ which are locally measurable and bounded locally almost everywhere form a linear space, denoted by $L^\infty(X, \mu)$. For such a function $f$, the $L^\infty$ norm is defined by
$$\|f\|_\infty = \inf \left\{k : |f(x)| \leq k \text{ locally almost everywhere}\right\}.$$
Then $|f(x)| \leq \|f\|_\infty$ locally almost everywhere.
