This theorem establishes the existence of a fixed point for a continuous affine self-map $T$ on a nonempty compact convex subset $A$ of a linear topological space $E$, under the additional hypothesis that $A - A$ admits sufficiently many continuous linear functionals to separate points (namely, each nonzero $x \in A - A$ has some continuous linear functional $f$ with $f(x) \neq 0$). The map $T$ is required to preserve convex combinations, i.e., $T$ is affine. This is a variant of the Markov-Kakutani fixed point theorem adapted to the setting of compact convex sets in linear topological spaces where the topology is not necessarily locally convex.
