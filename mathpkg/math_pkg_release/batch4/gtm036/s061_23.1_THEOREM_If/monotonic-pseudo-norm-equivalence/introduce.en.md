This theorem establishes the full equivalence between two fundamental properties of ordered topological vector spaces. On the functional-analytic side, every continuous linear functional decomposes as a difference of positive functionals. On the geometric side, the space admits an equivalent pseudo-norm that respects the order (i.e., larger elements in the cone have larger norm). The result follows immediately from Corollary 23.3 and Theorem 23.5, combining the forward and reverse implications.
