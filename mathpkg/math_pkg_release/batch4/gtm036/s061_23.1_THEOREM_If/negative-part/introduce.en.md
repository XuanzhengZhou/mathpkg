The negative part $x^- = (-x) \vee 0 = -(x \wedge 0)$ of an element $x$ in a vector lattice is the positive part of $-x$. It satisfies $x^- \geq 0$ and represents the "negative contribution" of $x$, measured positively. Together with the positive part $x^+$, it yields the fundamental decomposition $x = x^+ - x^-$, which shows every element is the difference of two positive elements.
