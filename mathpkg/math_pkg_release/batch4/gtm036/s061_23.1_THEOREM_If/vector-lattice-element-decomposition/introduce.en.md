A fundamental identity in any vector lattice: every element $x$ is the difference of its positive and negative parts, $x = x^+ - x^-$. This follows from the translation invariance of the lattice operations and the relation $x + y = x \vee y + x \wedge y$. A particular consequence is that the positive cone $C$ generates the entire space $E$, since every element is a difference of two elements of $C$.
