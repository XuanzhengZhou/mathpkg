This theorem characterizes when every continuous linear functional on a pseudo-normed space $E$ can be expressed as the difference of two bounded positive linear functionals. Let $B$ denote the set of all positive functionals of norm at most one. Then the decomposition property holds precisely when the Minkowski difference $B - B$ forms a neighborhood of zero in the dual space $E^*$. The proof relies on weak* compactness of $B$ and the absorption theorem for locally convex spaces.
