This proposition establishes that for any element $x$ in a vector lattice, its positive and negative parts are disjoint: $x^+ \wedge x^- = 0$. The proof uses translation invariance of the lattice operations. The converse is also partially true: if $x$ and $y$ are arbitrary disjoint positive elements, then $(x - y)^+ = x$ and $(x - y)^- = y$, meaning the decomposition $x - y = x^+ - x^-$ with $x^+ = x$ and $x^- = y$ is exactly the positive/negative part decomposition.
