A vector lattice (also called a Riesz space) is an ordered vector space in which every pair of elements $x, y$ has a unique least upper bound (supremum). This additional structure greatly enriches the theory of ordered spaces. The supremum is called the join and denoted $x \vee y$; the infimum (meet) is denoted $x \wedge y$. Geometrically, the ordering is a lattice ordering if and only if the intersection of two translates $x + C$ and $y + C$ of the positive cone $C$ is itself a translate $x \vee y + C$ of the cone. The lattice ordering is necessarily antisymmetric due to uniqueness of suprema.
