In a vector lattice, two positive elements $x$ and $y$ are called disjoint (or mutually singular) if their meet is zero: $x \wedge y = 0$. This concept generalizes the notion of disjoint support for functions. Importantly, the positive and negative parts of any element are always disjoint: $x^+ \wedge x^- = 0$. Disjointness plays a key role in the structure theory of vector lattices, particularly in decomposition theorems and the theory of bands.
