The positive part $x^+ = x \vee 0$ of an element $x$ in a vector lattice is the join of $x$ with the zero element. It satisfies $x^+ \geq 0$ and represents the "positive contribution" of $x$. Every element can be decomposed as the difference of its positive and negative parts: $x = x^+ - x^-$. This decomposition is fundamental to the theory of vector lattices and implies that the positive cone generates the entire space.
