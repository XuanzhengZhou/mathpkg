For a weakly closed subset $B$ of a paired linear space $E$, compactness can be characterized by whether the image $T[B]$ under the canonical map into the algebraic dual $F'$ is bounded and closed in the weak topology. Moreover, the representability of linear functionals bounded on the polar $B^{o}$ is equivalent to the closedness of $T[B]$. This theorem connects three fundamental notions -- compactness, boundedness of polars, and representation of linear functionals -- in the framework of paired linear spaces.
