Let $E$ and $F$ be paired linear spaces, let $T$ be the canonical map of $E$ into the algebraic dual $F'$ of $F$, let $E$ have the topology $w(E,F)$, and let $F'$ have the topology $w(F',F)$. If $B$ is a $w(E,F)$-closed subset of $E$, then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $B$ is $w(E,F)$-compact if and only if $T[B]$ is bounded and closed in $(F', w(F',F))$;
\item[(b)] every linear functional on $F$ which is bounded on the polar $B^{o}$ is representable by a member of $E$ if and only if $T[B]$ is closed in $(F', w(F',F))$.
\end{enumerate}
