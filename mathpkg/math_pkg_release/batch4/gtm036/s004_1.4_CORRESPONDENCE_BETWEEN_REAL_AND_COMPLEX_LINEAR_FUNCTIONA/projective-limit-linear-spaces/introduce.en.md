The projective limit (or inverse limit) of a projective system of linear spaces is the subspace of the direct product consisting of all threads that are compatible with the connecting maps $P_{st}$. Concretely, an element of the projective limit is a family $(x_t)_{t \in A}$ with $x_t \in E_t$ such that $P_{st}(x_t) = x_s$ whenever $t \geq s$. The projective limit captures the notion of a limit of successive approximations, and is denoted by $\varprojlim E_t$.
