This theorem establishes a natural bijection between complex linear functionals on a complex linear space $E$ and real linear functionals on the real restriction of $E$. The correspondence maps a complex linear functional $f$ to its real part $\Re(f)$. Conversely, any real linear functional $r$ on the real restriction can be uniquely extended to a complex linear functional $f$ on $E$ via the formula $f(x) = r(x) - i r(ix)$. This result is fundamental for transferring results between real and complex theories of linear functionals and is used extensively in the duality theory of topological vector spaces.
