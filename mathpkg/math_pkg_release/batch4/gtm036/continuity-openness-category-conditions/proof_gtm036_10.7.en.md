Let $E$ and $F$ be linear topological spaces and $T$ an additive map of $E$ into $F$. If for each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $F$ the set $T^{-1}[V]$ satisfies the condition of Baire, and $T^{-1}[V]$ is radial at $0$, then by the difference theorem $T^{-1}[V] - T^{-1}[V]$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$. Since $T$ is additive, $T^{-1}[V] - T^{-1}[V] \subset T^{-1}[V - V]$, and standard arguments using the continuity of scalar multiplication and addition then yield continuity of $T$. The dual statement for openness follows similarly by considering images rather than preimages.
