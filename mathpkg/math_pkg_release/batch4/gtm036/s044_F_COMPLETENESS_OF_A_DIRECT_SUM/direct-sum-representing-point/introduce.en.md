The final step of the completeness proof constructs a point $x$ in the finite partial direct sum $\sum_{t \in B} E_t$ that represents the functional $\phi$ via evaluation. For each $t \in B$, the restricted functional $\phi \circ I_t$ on $E_t^*$ is weak* continuous and thus, by Grothendieck's theorem applied to the complete space $E_t$, corresponds to evaluation at some $x_t \in E_t$. The sum $x = \sum_{t \in B} x_t$ then represents $\phi$.
