Corollary 3.7 restates the preceding extension result in terms of a pseudo-norm. Since the Minkowski functional of any convex circled set that is radial at $0$ is a pseudo-norm, Corollary 3.6 directly implies this formulation. The result asserts that any linear functional $f$ on a subspace $F$ that is dominated by a pseudo-norm $p$ can be extended to a linear functional $\overline{f}$ on the whole space, still dominated by $p$. This is one of the most frequently applied forms of the Hahn-Banach theorem in functional analysis.
