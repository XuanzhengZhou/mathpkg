Theorem 3.2 establishes a fundamental extension property for cones in real linear spaces: every proper cone that has a point of radiality (i.e., a point from which the cone radiates) can be embedded in a conical half-space. The proof uses the maximal principle to extend the given cone to a maximal cone not containing a specific vector, then shows this maximal cone must be a conical half-space. This result serves as a geometric bridge between cones and linear functionals, and is used repeatedly in the subsequent separation and extension theorems of this chapter.
