Let $C$ be a proper cone which is radial at a point $x$. Then the vector $-x$ does not belong to $C$, for in this case $0$ would be in the radial kernel of $C$ and $C$ would not be a proper cone. The cone $C$ is contained in a cone $P$ which is maximal with respect to the property of not containing $-x$, in view of the maximal principle. It will be shown that $P$ is a conical half-space.

Suppose $y \notin P$. The set of all points $a y + p$, for $a \geq 0$ and $p \in P$, is a cone which properly contains $P$, and therefore contains $-x$ by maximality of $P$. Hence $-x = a y + p$ for some $a \geq 0$ and $p \in P$. Since $-x \notin P$, we have $a > 0$, so that $y = -\frac{1}{a}(x + p)$. Thus every point not in $P$ is of the form $-\alpha(x + p)$ with $\alpha > 0$ and $p \in P$, which implies that $P$ is a conical half-space.
