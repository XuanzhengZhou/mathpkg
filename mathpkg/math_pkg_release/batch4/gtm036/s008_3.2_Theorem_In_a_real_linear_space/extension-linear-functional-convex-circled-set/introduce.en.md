Corollary 3.6 gives a geometric form of the Hahn-Banach extension theorem. Instead of being dominated by a subadditive functional, the given linear functional $f$ on $F$ is bounded by $1$ on the intersection of $F$ with a convex circled set $A$ that is radial at $0$. The corollary guarantees a linear extension $\overline{f}$ to the whole space that remains bounded by $1$ on $A$. The proof relies on the observation that for a linear functional, the supremum of $|f(x)|$ on a convex circled set equals the supremum of its real part, together with Theorem 1.4.
