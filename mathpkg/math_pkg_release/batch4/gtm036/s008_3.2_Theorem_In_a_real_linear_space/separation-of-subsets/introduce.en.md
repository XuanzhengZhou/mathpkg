This definition formalizes the geometric notion of separating two subsets of a real linear space by complementary half-spaces. Equivalently, a non-zero linear functional $f$ separates $A$ and $B$ when the values of $f$ on $A$ are all less than or equal to the values of $f$ on $B$. Strong separation demands a strict inequality, which is a stronger condition that plays a key role in the separation theorems for convex sets (e.g., Theorem 3.9 on strong separation).
