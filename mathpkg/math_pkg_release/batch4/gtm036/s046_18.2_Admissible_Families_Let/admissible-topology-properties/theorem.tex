Let $E$ and $F$ be paired linear spaces.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $\mathcal{A}$ is an admissible family of subsets of $F$, then $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ is an admissible topology for $E$; the family of polars of members of $\mathcal{A}$ is a local base for $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$; and $\mathcal{A}$ is a co-base for the family of $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$-equicontinuous sets.

\item[(ii)] If $\mathcal{A}$ is a family of $w(F, E)$-bounded subsets of $F$ such that the linear extension of $\bigcup \{A : A \in \mathcal{A}\}$ is $F$, and if $\mathcal{B}$ is the admissible family generated by $\mathcal{A}$, then $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}} = \mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{B}}$.

\item[(iii)] If $\mathcal{A}$ is an admissible family of subsets of $F$, then the topology $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ is pseudo-metrizable if and only if there is a countable co-base for $\mathcal{A}$, and $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ is pseudo-normable if and only if the family of scalar multiples of some member of $\mathcal{A}$ is a co-base for $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{enumerate}
