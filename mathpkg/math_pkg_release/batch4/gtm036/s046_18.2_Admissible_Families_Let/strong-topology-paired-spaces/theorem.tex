Let $E$ and $F$ be paired linear spaces. The topology $s(E, F)$ is defined as the topology of uniform convergence on all $w(F, E)$-bounded subsets of $F$. Equivalently, $s(E, F)$ is the admissible topology for $E$ determined by the family of all $w(F, E)$-bounded subsets of $F$; its local base can be taken to be the family of all $w(E, F)$-barrels (see 18.1(ii)).

The strong topology $s(F, E)$ for $F$ is described dually: it is the topology of uniform convergence on all $w(E, F)$-bounded subsets of $E$, and the family of $w(F, E)$-barrels is a local base for $s(F, E)$.

If $E$ is a locally convex space with topology $\mathcal{T}$ and adjoint $E^*$, then the strong topology for $E^*$ is the topology of uniform convergence on $\mathcal{T}$-bounded subsets of $E$. Since a subset of a locally convex space is $\mathcal{T}$-bounded if and only if it is weakly bounded (see 17.5), the strong topology on $E^*$ is identical with $s(E^*, E)$. It is the strongest admissible topology for $E^*$, and the family of weak* barrels is a base for this strong topology. The adjoint $E^*$ is a subspace of the space $E^b$ of all bounded linear functionals on $E$ (see 6.2); the term \textit{strong} also applies to $s(E^b, E)$.
