This theorem establishes the precise relationships between three fundamental notions for subsets of the adjoint of a locally convex space: equicontinuity, weak* compactness, and strong boundedness. Part (i) shows that equicontinuity implies weak* compactness of the closed convex circled extension, via the Banach-Alaoglu theorem. Part (ii) is an absorption argument using strong barrels. Part (iii) connects strong and weak* boundedness, with sequential completeness providing the reverse implication from weak* to strong boundedness via the uniform boundedness theorem.
