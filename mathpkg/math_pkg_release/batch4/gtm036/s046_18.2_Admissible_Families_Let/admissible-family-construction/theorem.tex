Let $E$ and $F$ be paired linear spaces. Given an arbitrary family $\mathcal{A}$ of $w(F, E)$-bounded sets which covers $F$, an admissible family $\mathcal{B}$ is constructed as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Let $\mathcal{A}_1$ consist of all finite unions of members of $\mathcal{A}$.
\item Let $\mathcal{A}_2$ consist of all $w(F, E)$-closed circled convex extensions of members of $\mathcal{A}_1$.
\item Let $\mathcal{B}$ consist of scalar multiples of members of $\mathcal{A}_2$.
\end{enumerate}

The family $\mathcal{B}$ is said to be \textit{generated by} $\mathcal{A}$, and it is an admissible family of subsets of $F$.
