An admissible family of subsets in a paired space can be systematically constructed from any covering family of weakly bounded sets. The construction proceeds in three steps: first take all finite unions, then take all weakly closed circled convex extensions, and finally take all scalar multiples. This process yields the smallest admissible family containing the original family, and it is central to generating admissible topologies from arbitrary weakly bounded covers.
