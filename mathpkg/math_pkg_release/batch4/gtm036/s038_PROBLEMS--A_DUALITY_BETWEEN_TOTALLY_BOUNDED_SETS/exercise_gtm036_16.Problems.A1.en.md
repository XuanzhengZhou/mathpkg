**A. Duality between totally bounded sets.** Suppose that $E$ and $F$ are paired linear spaces, that $A$ is a $w(E,F)$-bounded subset of $E$ and that $B$ is a $w(F,E)$-bounded subset of $F$. Then $A$ is totally bounded in the topology on $E$ of uniform convergence on $B$ if and only if $B$ is totally bounded in the topology on $F$ of uniform convergence on $A$. (Use 8.17 and 16.1(vi).)
