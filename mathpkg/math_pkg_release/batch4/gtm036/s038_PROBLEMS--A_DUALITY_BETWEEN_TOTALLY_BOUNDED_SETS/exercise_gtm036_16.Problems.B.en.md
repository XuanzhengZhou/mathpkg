**B. Polar of a sum.** Let $\langle E,F \rangle$ be a pairing and let $A$ and $B$ be non-void subsets of $E$. If $A$ and $B$ are convex and circled, then
$$\frac{1}{2}(A^0 \cap B^0) \subset (A + B)^0 \subset A^0 \cap B^0.$$
To obtain a precise expression for $(A + B)^0$, valid more generally, define the following operation $\triangle$: if $X$ and $Y$ are subsets of $F$, let
$$X \triangle Y = \bigcup \{ rX \cap sY : r \geq 0,\; s \geq 0,\; r + s \leq 1 \}.$$
Then $X \cap Y \subset 2(X \triangle Y)$.
