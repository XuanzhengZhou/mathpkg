If $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ are families of $w(E,F)$- and $w(F,E)$-bounded subsets of $E$ and $F$, then each $A \in \mathcal{A}$ is totally bounded in the topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{B}$ if and only if each $B \in \mathcal{B}$ is totally bounded in the topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{A}$.
