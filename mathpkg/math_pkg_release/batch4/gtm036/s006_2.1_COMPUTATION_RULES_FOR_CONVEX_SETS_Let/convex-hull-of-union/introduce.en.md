The convex hull of the union of two non-void sets $A$ and $B$ equals the union of all line segments joining a point in the convex hull of $A$ to a point in the convex hull of $B$. This elegant formula describes how the convex-hull operator distributes over unions when mediated by line segments. It follows that the join of two non-void convex sets is convex and coincides with the convex hull of their union.
