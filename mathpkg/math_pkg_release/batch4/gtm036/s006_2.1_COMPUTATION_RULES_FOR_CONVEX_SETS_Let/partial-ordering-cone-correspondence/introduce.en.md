The final theorem of Section 2.2 establishes a fundamental correspondence between algebraic order structures and geometric objects: translation-invariant partial orderings on a real linear space are in one-to-one correspondence with subsets $P$ (the positive cone) satisfying $0 \in P$ and $P + P \subset P$. If the ordering is additionally compatible with scalar multiplication by non-negative scalars, then $P$ is a cone. This correspondence is foundational for the theory of ordered vector spaces and provides a geometric interpretation of partial orderings.
