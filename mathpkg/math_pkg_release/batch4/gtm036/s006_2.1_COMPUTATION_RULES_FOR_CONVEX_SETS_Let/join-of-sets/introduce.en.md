The join of two subsets $A$ and $B$ of a linear space is the set-theoretic union of all line segments having one endpoint in $A$ and the other in $B$. This geometric operation plays a key role in the computation rules for convex hulls: it provides a direct description of the convex hull of the union of two sets. When both $A$ and $B$ are convex, their join is also convex.
