Stone's theorem asserts that any two disjoint convex subsets of a linear space can be separated by complementary convex sets: there exist disjoint convex sets $C$ and $D$ that partition the entire space, with $A \subset C$ and $B \subset D$. This is a purely algebraic separation theorem (requiring no topology), and is proved via Zorn's lemma using Kakutani's lemma as the key inductive step. It is a fundamental result in the geometric theory of convex sets.
