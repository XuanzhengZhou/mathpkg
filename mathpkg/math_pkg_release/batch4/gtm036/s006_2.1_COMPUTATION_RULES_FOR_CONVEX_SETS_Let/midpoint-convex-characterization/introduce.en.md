Midpoint convexity of a set $A$ is equivalent to the condition $A + A = 2A$, and more generally to the stability of $A$ under any finite combination with non-negative dyadic rational coefficients summing to one. This shows that midpoint convex sets are closed under convex combinations with dyadic rational weights, which is the key step in showing that a closed midpoint convex set in a topological vector space is fully convex.
