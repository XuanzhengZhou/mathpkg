A cone in a real linear space is a subset closed under addition and under multiplication by non-negative scalars. Cones serve as the sets of "non-negative" elements in ordered vector spaces: given a cone $P$, one defines $x \geq y$ if and only if $x - y \in P$. The intersection $P \cap (-P)$ is always a linear subspace and is the set of elements that are both "non-negative" and "non-positive", i.e., equal to zero in the induced partial order.
