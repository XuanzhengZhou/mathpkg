The Minkowski functional (also called the gauge) of a set $U$ radial at the origin assigns to each vector $x$ the infimum of all positive scalars $a$ such that $x/a$ belongs to $U$. It is a fundamental tool that translates geometric information about the set $U$ into a real-valued function on the space. When $U$ is additionally convex and circled, the Minkowski functional becomes a seminorm. This construction provides the bridge between convex geometry and functional analysis — every locally convex topology is generated by a family of Minkowski functionals.
