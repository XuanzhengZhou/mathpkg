The join operation preserves convexity: the join of two non-void convex subsets of a linear space is again convex. Moreover, the join coincides with the convex hull of the union. This fact follows directly from computation rule (v) for convex sets and reinforces the geometric interpretation of the convex hull of a union as the join of the individual convex hulls.
