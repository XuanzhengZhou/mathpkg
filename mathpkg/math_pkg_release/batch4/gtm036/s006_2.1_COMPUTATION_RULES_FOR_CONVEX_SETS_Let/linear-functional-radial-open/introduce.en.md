For a non-zero linear functional $f$, the image of a set radial at $x$ is radial at $f(x)$. Furthermore, when $A$ is both convex and radial at every point, the image $f[A]$ is an open subset of the scalar field. This observation connects the purely algebraic notion of radial kernel to the topological notion of openness via linear functionals, and is a precursor to the Open Mapping Theorem in functional analysis.
