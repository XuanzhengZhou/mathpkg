A pseudo-norm (commonly called a seminorm in modern terminology) is a real-valued functional on a linear space that is non-negatively homogeneous, subadditive, and always non-negative. Pseudo-norms arise naturally as Minkowski functionals of convex, circled, radial sets, and they provide the analytic structure underlying locally convex spaces. Unlike a norm, a pseudo-norm may vanish on non-zero vectors.
