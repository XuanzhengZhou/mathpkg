The property that the intersection of any family of convex sets is again convex is one of the most fundamental facts about convexity. It guarantees that the collection of convex subsets of a linear space forms a closure system, which in turn implies that every set has a convex hull — the smallest convex set containing it. This stability under arbitrary intersections is essential for many constructions in functional analysis and convex geometry.
