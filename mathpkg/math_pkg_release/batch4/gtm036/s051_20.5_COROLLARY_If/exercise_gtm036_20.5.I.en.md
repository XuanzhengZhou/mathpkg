\textbf{I. DISTRIBUTION SPACES II (see 8J).}

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The space $\mathcal{E}$ is a Montel space. (Sketch of proof: first, it is sufficient to prove that any bounded subset $B$ of $\mathcal{E}^{(m+1)}$ is totally bounded as a subset of $\mathcal{E}^{(m)}$, by 16D(j) and (k). Now this will follow, from 8J(a) and 16D(j) again, if each $D^p[B]$, for $|p| \leq m$, is a totally bounded subset of $C(\mathbb{R}^n)$. But this is a consequence of Ascoli's theorem, $D^p[B]$ being equicontinuous because $B$ is a subset of $\mathcal{E}^{(m+1)}$.)

\item[(b)] The space $\mathcal{D}$ is the strict inductive limit (17G) of the sequence $\{\mathcal{D}_{K(r)}: r = 1, 2, \cdots\}$ (see 16C(k)); it follows in particular that $\mathcal{D}$ is complete and that each bounded subset of $\mathcal{D}$ [is contained in some $\mathcal{D}_{K(r)}$ and is relatively compact therein, so $\mathcal{D}$ is also a Montel space.]
\end{enumerate}
