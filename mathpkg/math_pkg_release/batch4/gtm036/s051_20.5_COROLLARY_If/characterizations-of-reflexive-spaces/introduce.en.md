A locally convex Hausdorff space $E$ is reflexive exactly when two conditions hold simultaneously: it is semi-reflexive (every bounded weakly closed set is weakly compact) and evaluable (every strongly bounded subset of the adjoint $E^*$ is equicontinuous). An equivalent formulation states that $E$ is reflexive if and only if it is a Mackey space and both $E$ and its strong dual $E^*$ are semi-reflexive. These characterizations synthesize earlier results (Propositions 20.1, 20.4, and 20.5) together with the fact that the evaluation map is injective if and only if $E$ is Hausdorff.
