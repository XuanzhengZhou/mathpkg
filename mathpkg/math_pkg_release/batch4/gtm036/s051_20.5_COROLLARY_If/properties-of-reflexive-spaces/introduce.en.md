Theorem 20.7 collects fundamental structural properties of reflexive locally convex spaces. Part (i) asserts that reflexivity is inherited by the strong dual: if $E$ is reflexive then $E^*$ is also reflexive. Part (ii) states that every reflexive space is barrelled and that every closed bounded subset of a reflexive space is complete. Part (iii) establishes that arbitrary products and direct sums of reflexive spaces remain reflexive. These properties are complemented by counterexamples showing that closed subspaces and quotient spaces of reflexive spaces need not be reflexive, and that the converse of (i) fails in general.
