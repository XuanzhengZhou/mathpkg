Let $\{x_\alpha, \alpha \in B, \geq\}$ be a net in the product $\prod_{t \in A} E_t$. Then $x_\alpha - x_\beta$ converges to $0$ relative to the product topology if and only if, for each coordinate $t \in A$, the net $\{x_\alpha(t), \alpha \in B, \geq\}$ is a Cauchy net in $E_t$. It follows that, if each $E_t$ is complete, then each Cauchy net in the product converges to a point. The converse is easily established by using the injection map.
