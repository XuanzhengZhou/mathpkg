For a linear topological space whose topology is induced by an invariant pseudo-metric $d$, topological completeness (every Cauchy net converges) is equivalent to metric completeness (every $d$-Cauchy sequence converges). The invariance property $d(x,y) = d(x+z, y+z)$ is crucial: without it, as shown by the example $d(x,y) = |\arctan x - \arctan y|$ on $\mathbb{R}$, metric completeness can fail even though the space is topologically complete.
