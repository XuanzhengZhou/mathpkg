In the topology of convergence in measure, a sequence $\{f_n\}$ converges to $0$ precisely when the measure of the set where $|f_n|$ exceeds any positive threshold $r$ tends to zero. This characterization justifies the name "topology of convergence in measure" and shows that sequential convergence in this topology coincides with the classical notion of convergence in measure.
