For $0 < p < 1$, the $L^p$ spaces are not locally convex and have a trivial continuous dual when the underlying measure space has no atoms. The proof constructs, from any non-zero linear functional $\phi$, a sequence of functions $\{f_n\}$ whose $L^p$ norms tend to zero while $|\phi(f_n)| \geq 1$, contradicting continuity. This highlights the striking difference between $L^p$ for $p \geq 1$ (rich dual) and $0 < p < 1$ (trivial dual).
