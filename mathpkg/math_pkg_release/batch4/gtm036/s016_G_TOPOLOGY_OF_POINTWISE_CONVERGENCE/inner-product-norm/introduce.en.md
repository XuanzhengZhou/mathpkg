The function $\|x\| = \sqrt{(x,x)}$ defines a norm on any inner product space, called the inner product norm. The triangle inequality follows from the CBS inequality. Moreover, the inner product can be uniquely recovered from this norm via the polarization identity, which expresses $(x,y)$ in terms of norms of linear combinations of $x$ and $y$.
