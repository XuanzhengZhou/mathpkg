An inner product on a complex linear space $E$ is a positive definite Hermitian sesquilinear form $( \cdot, \cdot ) : E \times E \to \mathbb{C}$. It is conjugate-linear in the first argument and linear in the second, with $(x,x) > 0$ for all $x \neq 0$. A complex linear space equipped with an inner product is called an inner product space, which serves as the foundation for Hilbert space theory.
