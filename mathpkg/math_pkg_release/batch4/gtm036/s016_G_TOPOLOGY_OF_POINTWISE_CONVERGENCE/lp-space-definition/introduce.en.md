For a measure space $(X, \mathcal{S}, \mu)$ and $p > 0$, the space $L^p(X, \mu)$ consists of all measurable functions $f$ for which $|f|^p$ is integrable. For $p \geq 1$, $L^p$ is a Banach space under the norm $\|f\|_p = (\int |f|^p d\mu)^{1/p}$. For $0 < p < 1$, $L^p$ is a complete metric space but not locally convex in general.
