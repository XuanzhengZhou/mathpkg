The Cauchy-Bunyakovsky-Schwarz (CBS) inequality states that the absolute value of the inner product of two vectors is bounded by the product of their norms. Equality holds precisely when the two vectors are linearly dependent. The proof follows by noting that the inner product of $(y,y)x - (x,y)y$ with itself is non-negative, which yields the inequality after expanding.
