This theorem generalizes the classical Hahn-Banach extension theorem by replacing the sublinear functional with a convex functional $p$ defined only on a convex subset $A$, rather than on the entire space. The additional hypothesis $A \subset F - C$, where $C$ is the conical extension of $A$, ensures that the convex set is sufficiently aligned with the subspace $F$ to permit extension. The dual statement, with the inequalities reversed, holds for concave functionals. This result is a key tool for extending linear functionals dominated by convex functionals in the setting of linear topological spaces.
