This remark extends Theorem 3.2 on the separation of convex sets by relaxing the cone hypothesis. In the original formulation, the cone generated by $B - A$ must be a proper subset of the entire space. The weakened version requires only that the cone be radial at some point with respect to its linear extension. This refinement broadens the applicability of the separation theorem to cases where the cone may be large but still admits a radial point in the appropriate sense.
