The Strong Separation Theorem gives a necessary and sufficient condition for two convex subsets of a linear space to be strongly separated by a linear functional. Strong separation means that the supremum of the functional over one set is strictly less than its infimum over the other set. The condition involves the existence of a convex set $U$, radial at the origin, whose translate $A+U$ is disjoint from $B$. This result refines the standard separation theorems by characterizing exactly when a positive gap can be achieved between the images of the two sets under a linear functional.
