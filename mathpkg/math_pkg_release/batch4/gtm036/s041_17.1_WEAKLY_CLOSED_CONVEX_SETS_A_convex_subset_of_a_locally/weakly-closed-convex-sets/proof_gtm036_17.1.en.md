Let $A$ be convex and $\mathcal{T}$-closed, and let $x$ be an element which is not in $A$. Then there is by theorem 14.4 a $\mathcal{T}$-continuous linear functional $f$, which strongly separates $A$ and $x$. Since $f$ is weakly continuous, no net in $A$ can converge weakly to $x$; hence, $A$ is weakly closed. The converse is obvious since $\mathcal{T}$ is stronger than $w$.
