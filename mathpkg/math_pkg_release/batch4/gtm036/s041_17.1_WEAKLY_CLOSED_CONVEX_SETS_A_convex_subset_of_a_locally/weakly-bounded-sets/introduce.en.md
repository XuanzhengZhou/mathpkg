In a locally convex space, the notions of boundedness with respect to the original topology and the weak topology coincide. Every $\mathcal{T}$-bounded set is trivially weakly bounded since the original topology is stronger. The converse is nontrivial and uses a chain of polar computations together with the Banach-Alaoglu theorem and the absorption theorem: given a weakly bounded set $B$ and a closed convex circled neighborhood $U$ of zero, $U$ is the polar of $U^0$; the Banach-Alaoglu theorem gives weak* compactness of $U^0$; then the polar of $B$ absorbs $U^0$ by the absorption theorem, and by taking polars again, $U$ absorbs $B$, establishing $\mathcal{T}$-boundedness.
