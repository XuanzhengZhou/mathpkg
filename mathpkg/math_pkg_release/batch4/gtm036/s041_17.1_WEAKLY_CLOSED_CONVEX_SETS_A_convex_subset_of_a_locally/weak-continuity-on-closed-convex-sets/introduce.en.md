For a closed convex subset of a locally convex space, a linear functional is continuous in the weak topology on that subset exactly when it is continuous in the original topology. This corollary shows that on closed convex sets, the notions of weak continuity and $\mathcal{T}$-continuity coincide for linear functionals. The proof reduces to the case of a real functional on a real space and uses the characterization of weak continuity via the property that inverse images of closed sets (relative to $A$) are weakly closed.
