It may be supposed without loss of generality that $f$ is a real linear functional on a real space, for $f$ is continuous if and only if its real part is continuous. By theorem 13.5, $f$ is $w$-continuous on $A$ if and only if $f^{-1}[a] \cap A$ is $w$-closed relative to $A$ for all real $a$. The set $f^{-1}[a] \cap A$ is a closed convex subset of $A$, hence it is $\mathcal{T}$-closed relative to $A$. Consequently, $f$ is $\mathcal{T}$-continuous on $A$ by the same theorem 13.5.
