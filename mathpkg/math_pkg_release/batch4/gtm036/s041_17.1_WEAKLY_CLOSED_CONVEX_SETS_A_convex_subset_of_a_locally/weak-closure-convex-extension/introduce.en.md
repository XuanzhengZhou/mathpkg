This corollary establishes a relationship between the weak closure of an arbitrary subset and the closure (in the original topology) of its convex hull. It states that the weak closure is contained in the $\mathcal{T}$-closure of the convex extension. When the locally convex space is metrizable, a stronger sequential characterization holds: every point in the weak closure can be approximated by a sequence of convex combinations of points from the original set, with the number of terms growing with the sequence index.
