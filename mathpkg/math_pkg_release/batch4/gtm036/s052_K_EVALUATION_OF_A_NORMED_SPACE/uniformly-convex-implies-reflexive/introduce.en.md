This is the Milman–Pettis theorem: every uniformly convex normed space is necessarily reflexive. Uniform convexity is a geometric property of the norm that requires the midpoint of any two nearly-antipodal unit vectors to lie strictly inside the unit ball. The theorem establishes a deep link between the geometry of the norm (uniform rotundity) and the duality structure of the space (coincidence with the bidual). This result is used extensively in the theory of Banach spaces.
