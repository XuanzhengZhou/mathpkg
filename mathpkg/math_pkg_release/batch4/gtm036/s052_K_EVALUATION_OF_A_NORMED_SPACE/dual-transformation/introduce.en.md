The dual (or adjoint) transformation is the natural map induced on dual spaces by a continuous linear map between linear topological spaces. Given a continuous linear map T from E to F, its adjoint T* maps the dual of F to the dual of E by precomposition: a functional on F is pulled back to a functional on E via T. This construction is fundamental to the duality theory of linear topological spaces and underlies the study of weak topologies, reflexivity, and the relationship between continuity of a map and continuity of its adjoint.
