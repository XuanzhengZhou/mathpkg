The converse of the Milman–Pettis theorem is false: reflexivity does not force the existence of a uniformly convex renorming. There exist reflexive Banach spaces whose norm topology cannot be generated by any uniformly convex norm, even after passing to an equivalent norm. This shows that uniform convexity is a strictly stronger geometric property than reflexivity. Examples are given in Bourbaki's Espaces Vectoriels Topologiques.
