James' space is a celebrated counterexample in Banach space theory, showing that a space can be isomorphic to its bidual yet fail to be reflexive. The canonical embedding of the space into its bidual has codimension one — the image is a proper subspace, but the space is still linearly homeomorphic to its bidual via a non-canonical isomorphism. This distinguishes the notion of reflexivity (coincidence with the bidual under the canonical map) from mere isomorphism to the bidual.
