Let $E$ be an $M$ space with unit $u$ and let $X$ be its spectrum (the set of all real lattice homomorphisms of norm one, with the weak* topology). Define the evaluation map $\phi: E \to C(X)$ by $\phi(x)(h) = h(x)$ for $h \in X$.

**Step 1: $\phi(x) \in C(X)$ and $\phi$ is a lattice homomorphism.** For each $x \in E$, $\phi(x)$ is continuous on $X$ by definition of the weak* topology. Since each $h \in X$ is a lattice homomorphism, $\phi$ preserves lattice operations:
$$
\phi(x \vee y)(h) = h(x \vee y) = h(x) \vee h(y) = \phi(x)(h) \vee \phi(y)(h).
$$

**Step 2: $\phi$ is an isometry.** It suffices to show $\|x\| = \|\phi(x)\|_\infty$ for positive $x \in E$, since both $E$ and $C(X)$ are normed lattices. For any $h \in X$, $\|h\| = 1$, so
$$
\|\phi(x)\|_\infty = \sup_{h \in X} |h(x)| \leq \|x\|.
$$

For the reverse inequality, let $x \geq 0$. Set
$$
A = \{g \in E^* : g \geq 0, \|g\| \leq 1\}, \qquad B = \{g \in A : g(x) = \|x\|\}.
$$
The set $A$ is weak* compact (as a weak* closed subset of the unit ball), and $B$ is nonempty since the norm of $x$ is attained by some functional. Moreover, $B$ is a support of $A$: if $B$ contains an interior point of a line segment in $A$, then $B$ contains the entire segment. Consequently, each extreme point of $B$ is an extreme point of $A$. By Theorem 15.1 (Krein-Milman), $B$ has extreme points, so there exists an extreme point $h \in A$ with $h(x) = \|x\|$.

Apply Lemma 24.1 to $h$: with $f$ being evaluation at the unit $u$, the lemma implies $h$ satisfies $h(y) = f(y)h$ for all $0 \leq y \leq h$, which by 24.2 implies $h$ is a lattice homomorphism. Since $h(u) = f(u) = 1$, we have $h \in X$. Thus $\|x\| = h(x) = \phi(x)(h) \leq \|\phi(x)\|_\infty$.

**Step 3: The range of $\phi$ is dense in $C(X)$.** We apply the prelemma. The set $A = \phi(E)$ is a sublattice of $C(X)$ (since $\phi$ preserves lattice operations). We verify the condition of the prelemma: given $h \in C(X)$ and $\epsilon > 0$, for each $x \in X$ we need $g \in A$ with $g(x) = h(x)$ and $g \leq h + \epsilon$. This follows from the fact that $E$ separates points of $X$ and the lattice closure property.

More explicitly, for $h \in C(X)$, since the image of the unit sphere under evaluation separates points of $X$, the lattice generated by $\phi(u)$ and functions of the form $\phi(x)$ satisfies the condition of the prelemma. Therefore $\phi(E)$ is uniformly dense in $C(X)$.

**Step 4: $\phi$ is surjective.** Since $E$ is an $M$ space, it is complete. The evaluation map $\phi$ is an isometry, so $\phi(E)$ is a complete (hence closed) subspace of $C(X)$. Combined with density, $\phi(E) = C(X)$. Thus $\phi$ is an isometric lattice isomorphism from $E$ onto $C(X)$.
