This theorem provides a complete representation of $M$ spaces with unit as spaces of continuous real-valued functions on a compact Hausdorff space (the spectrum). The proof proceeds by showing that the evaluation map $\phi: E \to C(X)$ defined by $\phi(x)(h) = h(x)$ is an isometric lattice isomorphism with dense range; since $C(X)$ is complete and $E$ is an $M$ space, the map is surjective. This is analogous to the Gelfand representation for commutative $C^*$-algebras, with lattice homomorphisms playing the role of multiplicative linear functionals.
