The evaluation map from a normed lattice into its second adjoint (bidual) not only preserves the linear and norm structure, as is true for any normed space, but also preserves the lattice operations. This means that the canonical embedding $e \colon E \to E^{**}$ satisfies $e(x \vee y) = e(x) \vee e(y)$ and $e(x \wedge y) = e(x) \wedge e(y)$ for all $x, y \in E$. Consequently, every normed lattice can be viewed as a sublattice of a Banach lattice, namely its bidual.
