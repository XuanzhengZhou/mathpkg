Let $E$ and $F$ be linear spaces. Any element $z$ of the algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$ may be written as a finite sum of elementary tensors:
$$z = \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i \otimes y_i, \qquad x_i \in E,\; y_i \in F,\; 1 \leq i \leq n.$$
Consequently, $z$ is the bilinear form on $E' \times F'$ given by
$$z(x', y') = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \langle x_i, x' \rangle \langle y_i, y' \rangle.$$
