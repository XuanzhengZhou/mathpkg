Given two linear spaces $E$ and $F$, the elementary tensor $x \otimes y$ is defined concretely as a bilinear form on the product of the algebraic duals $E' \times F'$. Its value at a pair of functionals $(x', y')$ is simply the product of the two canonical pairings $\langle x, x' \rangle \langle y, y' \rangle$. This construction embeds the abstract notion of a tensor product of vectors into the concrete space $B(E', F')$ of bilinear forms, avoiding any quotient construction. The mapping $(x, y) \mapsto x \otimes y$ is bilinear and serves as the universal bilinear map from $E \times F$ into the algebraic tensor product.
