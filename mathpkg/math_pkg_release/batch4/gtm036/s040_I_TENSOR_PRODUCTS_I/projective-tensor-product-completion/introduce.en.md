Once the algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$ is equipped with the projective tensor product topology $\mathcal{T}$ (the finest locally convex topology for which the canonical bilinear map $E \times F \to E \otimes F$ is continuous), one may take its completion to obtain a complete locally convex space $E \hat{\otimes} F$. This completed projective tensor product is the central object for studying tensor products of topological vector spaces. It inherits many properties from the factors $E$ and $F$; for instance, metrizability and barrelledness are preserved.
