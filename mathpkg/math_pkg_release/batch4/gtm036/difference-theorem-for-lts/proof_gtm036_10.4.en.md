Let $A$ be a subset of a linear topological space $E$ which is of the second category in $E$ and satisfies the condition of Baire. Then $A$ can be written as $A = U \triangle M$ where $U$ is open and $M$ is of the first category. Since $A$ is of the second category, $U$ is non-void. The set $A - A$ contains $(U - U) \setminus N$ where $N$ is of the first category. More precisely, $A - A$ contains the open neighborhood $U - U$ of $0$ whenever $A$ fills the non-void open set $U$, and the theorem follows.
