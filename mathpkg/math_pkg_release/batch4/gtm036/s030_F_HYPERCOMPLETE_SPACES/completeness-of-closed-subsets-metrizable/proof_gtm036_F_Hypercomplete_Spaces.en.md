When $E$ is complete and metrizable, the Hausdorff uniformity on non-void subsets is precisely the uniformity of the Hausdorff metric (as established in Section 4D of GTM036). It is a classical result that the space of non-void closed subsets of a complete metric space, equipped with the Hausdorff metric, is itself complete: given a Cauchy sequence $\{A_n\}$ of closed sets, one defines the limit as the set of all limit points of sequences $\{a_n\}$ with $a_n \in A_n$, and verifies that this limit is closed and that $A_n$ converges to it in the Hausdorff metric. Since $\mathcal{E}$ consists precisely of the non-void closed subsets with this uniformity, the completeness of $\mathcal{E}$ follows directly.
