A linear functional on a vector lattice is the difference of two positive linear functionals precisely when it is bounded on order-bounded sets. This characterizes exactly which functionals admit a decomposition into positive parts. On a vector lattice (as opposed to a general ordered linear space), the "bounded on order-bounded sets" condition takes the place of continuity with respect to an equivalent monotonic norm.
