This result is stated in the introductory summary of Section 23 without proof. The full proof appears later in the chapter (see Section 23.1 and subsequent sections). The theorem establishes an equivalence: the existence of an equivalent monotonic norm is both necessary and sufficient for every continuous linear functional to decompose as the difference of positive functionals. The "in particular" clause about Krein's normal cone provides a geometric sufficient condition: when the positive cone is normal, one can explicitly construct the equivalent monotonic norm.
