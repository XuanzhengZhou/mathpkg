On an ordered normed linear space, every continuous linear functional can be expressed as the difference of two positive linear functionals precisely when the space admits an equivalent monotonic norm. A sufficient condition is that the positive cone is normal (in the sense of Krein). This result connects the order structure with the duality theory: it tells us exactly when the positive cone is "large enough" to decompose arbitrary functionals.
