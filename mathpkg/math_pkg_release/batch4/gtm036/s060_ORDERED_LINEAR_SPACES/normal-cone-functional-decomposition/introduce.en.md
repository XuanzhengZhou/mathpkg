Normality of the positive cone (in the sense of Krein) provides a sufficient geometric condition ensuring that every continuous linear functional decomposes into the difference of two positive functionals. A normal cone is one for which the order intervals are bounded in the given norm topology. This property allows the construction of an equivalent monotonic norm, which then forces the decomposition via the preceding theorem.
