For any cone $C$ in a real linear space $E$, the difference set $C - C$ is a linear subspace of $E$. This fact is geometrically clear: for any $u_1 - v_1, u_2 - v_2 \in C - C$ and scalars $a, b$, we have $a(u_1 - v_1) + b(u_2 - v_2) = (au_1 + bu_2) - (av_1 + bv_2)$, and the convexity and scale-invariance of $C$ ensure the result remains in $C - C$. The subspace $C - C$ measures how much of $E$ is "covered" by the cone.
