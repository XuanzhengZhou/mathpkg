This result is stated in the introductory summary without proof. The full proof appears later in the chapter (see Section 23.14). The continuity of lattice operations follows from the fact that the Mackey topology is generated by lattice pseudo-norms, and lattice operations are naturally compatible with such pseudo-norms. Specifically, for any lattice pseudo-norm $p$, one has $p(x \vee y) \leq p(x) + p(y)$ and $p(x \wedge y) \leq p(x) + p(y)$, which implies continuity.
