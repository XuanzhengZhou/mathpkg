This lemma provides a crucial bridge between pointwise convergence and uniform convergence for nets of functions taking values in a linear topological space $E$. It states that if a net $\{f_{\gamma}\}$ of functions converges pointwise to $f$ on a set $A$, and the net is also Cauchy with respect to the topology of uniform convergence on $A$, then the convergence is actually uniform on $A$. The proof relies on the simple but powerful observation that in a linear topological space, if differences $f_{\alpha}(t)-f_{\beta}(t)$ eventually lie in a closed neighborhood of zero for all $t \in A$, then the limit $f_{\alpha}(t)-f(t)$ must also lie in that neighborhood.
