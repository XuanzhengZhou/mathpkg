This corollary establishes that the subspace $T_{\mathcal{A}}(S,E)$ consisting of functions that are totally bounded on each member of the family $\mathcal{A}$ is closed in the larger function space $B_{\mathcal{A}}(S,E)$ under the topology $T_{\mathcal{A}}$ of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{A}$. In other words, a limit (in the $T_{\mathcal{A}}$ topology) of functions that are each totally bounded on every $A \in \mathcal{A}$ must itself be totally bounded on every $A \in \mathcal{A}$. This is a fundamental stability result for the class of totally bounded functions in the theory of linear topological spaces.
