This theorem states that the property "every closed bounded (or totally bounded) set is complete" is inherited from the target space $E$ to the function space $B_{\mathcal{A}}(S,E)$ equipped with the topology $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{A}$. The key observation in the proof is that for any $t$ belonging to a member $A$ of $\mathcal{A}$, the evaluation map $f \mapsto f(t)$ is continuous and linear, so boundedness (or total boundedness) of a set of functions is preserved pointwise. Combined with Lemma 8.5, this yields the desired completeness property.
