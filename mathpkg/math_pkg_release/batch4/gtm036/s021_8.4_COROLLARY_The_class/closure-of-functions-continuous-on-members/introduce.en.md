This proposition establishes that the family $C_A(S, E)$ of all functions from a topological space $S$ to a linear topological space $E$ that are continuous on each member of a family $A$ of subsets is closed in the space $F(S, E)$ of all functions under the topology $T_A$ of uniform convergence on members of $A$. The result follows from the observation that if a net of functions, each continuous on a set $A \in A$, converges uniformly on $A$ to a limit function $f$, then $f$ is also continuous on $A$. A consequence is that the family of functions which are both bounded and continuous on each member of $A$ is closed in $B_A(S, E)$ and is complete whenever $E$ is complete.
