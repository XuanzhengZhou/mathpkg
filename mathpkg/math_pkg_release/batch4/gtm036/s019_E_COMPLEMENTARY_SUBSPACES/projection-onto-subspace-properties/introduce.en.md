When $F$ is a closed linear subspace, the projection $P$ is a continuous linear operator that is idempotent ($P^2 = P$), self-adjoint, and has $F$ as its fixed-point set. These three properties characterize orthogonal projections in Hilbert space and establish the fundamental connection between closed subspaces and orthogonal projection operators.
