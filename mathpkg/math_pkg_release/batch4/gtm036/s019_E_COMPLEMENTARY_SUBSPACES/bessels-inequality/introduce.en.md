Bessel's inequality states that for any finite orthonormal set, the sum of the squared absolute values of the Fourier coefficients $(x, x_r)$ is bounded by the squared norm of $x$. Geometrically, the projection of $x$ onto the subspace spanned by the orthonormal set has norm equal to the square root of this sum, and the inequality reflects that the projection cannot have larger norm than $x$ itself.
