In a Hilbert space, any summable family can have at most countably many non-zero terms. Moreover, the unordered sum coincides with the sum of the series obtained by any enumeration of the non-zero terms. This result connects the theory of unordered sums with the classical theory of convergent series in Hilbert spaces.
