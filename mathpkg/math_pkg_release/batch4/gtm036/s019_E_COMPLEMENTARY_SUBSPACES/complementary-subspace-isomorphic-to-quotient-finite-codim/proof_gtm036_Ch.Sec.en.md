Let $G$ be any algebraic complement of $F$ in $E$, so that $E = F \oplus G$ algebraically. Since $F$ has finite co-dimension, $G$ is finite-dimensional. Consider the restriction of the quotient map $\pi: E \to E/F$ to $G$. This restriction $\pi|_G: G \to E/F$ is a continuous linear bijection between finite-dimensional Hausdorff linear topological spaces. By the uniqueness theorem for finite-dimensional Hausdorff linear topological spaces, any continuous linear bijection between such spaces is a topological isomorphism. Therefore $G$ is isomorphic to $E/F$.
