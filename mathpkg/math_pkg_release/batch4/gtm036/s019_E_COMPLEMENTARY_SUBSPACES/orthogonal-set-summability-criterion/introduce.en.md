For orthogonal families in a Hilbert space, summability is equivalent to the summability of the squared norms. This is a powerful criterion that reduces the question of vector summability to the summability of non-negative real numbers. The Pythagorean identity extends to the infinite setting: the squared norm of the sum equals the sum of the squared norms.
