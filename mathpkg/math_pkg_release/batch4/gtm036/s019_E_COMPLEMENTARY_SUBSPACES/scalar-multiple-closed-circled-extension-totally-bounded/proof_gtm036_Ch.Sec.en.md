For a scalar $\lambda$, the map $x \mapsto \lambda x$ is continuous and linear, so the image of a totally bounded set under it is totally bounded.

For the closed circled extension: let $B$ be totally bounded. For any neighborhood $U$ of $0$, choose a circled neighborhood $V$ of $0$ with $V + V \subset U$. Since $B$ is totally bounded, $B \subset N + V$ for some finite $N$. The circled hull of $N$ is compact (as $N$ is finite) and therefore totally bounded. Then the closed circled hull of $B$ is contained in the sum of the circled hull of $N$ and the closed circled hull of $V$, from which total boundedness follows.
