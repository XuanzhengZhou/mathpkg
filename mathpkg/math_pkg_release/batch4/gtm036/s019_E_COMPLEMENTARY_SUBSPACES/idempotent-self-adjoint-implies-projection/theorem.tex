If $P$ is any continuous linear transformation from a Hilbert space $H$ into itself such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $P \circ P = P$,
\item[(ii)] $(P(x), y) = (x, P(y))$ for all $x, y \in H$,
\end{enumerate}
then there is a closed linear subspace $F$ of $H$ such that $P$ is the projection onto $F$.
