The converse of the previous theorem: any continuous linear operator on a Hilbert space that is both idempotent and self-adjoint is necessarily the orthogonal projection onto some closed linear subspace. Thus orthogonal projections are precisely the idempotent self-adjoint operators, providing an algebraic characterization of a geometric concept.
