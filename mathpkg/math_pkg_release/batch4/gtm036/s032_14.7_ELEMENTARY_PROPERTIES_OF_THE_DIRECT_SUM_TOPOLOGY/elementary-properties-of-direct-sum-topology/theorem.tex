Let $\sum \{E_t : t \in A\}$ be the direct sum of locally convex spaces $E_t$, equipped with the direct sum topology.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The direct sum topology is the strongest locally convex topology with the property that each injection is continuous.

\item[(ii)] Each injection is a topological isomorphism of a factor onto a subspace of the direct sum.

\item[(iii)] A linear function on a direct sum to a locally convex space is continuous if and only if its composition with each injection is continuous.

\item[(iv)] The direct sum of complete locally convex spaces is complete relative to the direct sum topology.

\item[(v)] A linear functional $\phi$ on a direct sum $\sum \{E_t : t \in A\}$ of locally convex spaces is continuous relative to the direct sum topology if and only if there is a member $f$ of $\bigcup \{E_t^* : t \in A\}$ such that $\phi(x) = \sum \{f_t(x_t) : t \in A\}$ for all $x$ in the direct sum.
\end{enumerate}
