This proposition collects five elementary but essential properties of the direct sum topology on locally convex spaces. Part (i) characterizes it as the strongest locally convex topology making all injections continuous. Part (ii) states that each factor embeds as a topological subspace. Part (iii) gives a universal mapping property for linear functions out of the direct sum. Part (iv) establishes that completeness is preserved under direct sums. Part (v) describes the continuous dual of a direct sum: a linear functional is continuous precisely when it is given by a finitely-supported family of continuous linear functionals on the factors.
