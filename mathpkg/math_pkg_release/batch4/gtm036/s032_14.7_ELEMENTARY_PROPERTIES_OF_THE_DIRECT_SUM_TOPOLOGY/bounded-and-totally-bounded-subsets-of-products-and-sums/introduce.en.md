This proposition characterizes the bounded and totally bounded subsets of two fundamental constructions on locally convex spaces: the product and the direct sum. For products, a subset is bounded (or totally bounded) exactly when its projection onto each factor is bounded (or totally bounded). For direct sums, the bounded subsets are those contained in the convex extension of a finite union of bounded subsets of the factors, injected into the direct sum. The proof is left as an exercise to the reader.
