This theorem establishes a natural one-to-one correspondence between complex linear functionals on a complex linear space $E$ and real linear functionals on the real restriction of $E$. The real part of a complex linear functional uniquely determines it, and conversely, every real linear functional on the real restriction gives rise to a complex linear functional via the formula $f(x) = r(x) - i r(ix)$.
