The limit of a scalar net which converges in the Hausdorff uniformity is a subspace of $E$. A proof analogous to that sketched in 18G shows that $E$ is fully complete if and only if every decreasing Cauchy scalar net converges. If $E$ is fully complete, the space of all closed subspaces is complete, with the Hausdorff uniformity; the converse is not true.
