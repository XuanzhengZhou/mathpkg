A linear mapping $T$ of a locally convex Hausdorff space $E$ into a fully complete space $F$ is continuous, provided that

(i) the graph of $T$ in $E \times F$ is closed;

(ii) for each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $F$, the closure of $T^{-1}[V]$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$.

Moreover, if $F$ is a locally convex Hausdorff space, the following statements are equivalent:
(a) $F$ is fully complete;
(b) any linear mapping of any locally convex Hausdorff space into a Hausdorff quotient of $F$ is continuous, provided that its graph is closed and its inverse is somewhere dense;
(c) any continuous somewhere dense linear mapping of $F$ onto any locally convex Hausdorff space is open.
