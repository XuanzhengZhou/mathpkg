Let $E$ be the set of sequences $x = \{\xi_n\}$ with at most a finite number of non-zero terms, with the norm $\|x\| = \sum |\xi_n|$. Let $f_n(x) = \xi_n$. Then the set consisting of the points $2^{2n}f_n$ together with $0$ is weak* compact but is not strongly bounded. Thus its weak* closed convex extension is not weak* compact. In fact, if $g_n = \sum \{2^r f_r : 1 \leq r \leq n\}$, then each $g_n$ lies in this extension, and $\{g_n\}$ is a weak* Cauchy sequence which is not weak* convergent. The weak* topology coincides with the Mackey topology $m(E^*, E)$ (see 17I). Thus $E^*$ with the Mackey topology is not even sequentially complete.
