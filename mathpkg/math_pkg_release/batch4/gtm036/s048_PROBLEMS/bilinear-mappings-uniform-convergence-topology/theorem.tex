Let $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ be families of bounded subsets of locally convex spaces $E$ and $F$ such that $E = \cup \mathcal{A}$ and $F = \cup \mathcal{B}$, and let $H$ be a linear space of bilinear mappings of $E \times F$ into a locally convex space $G$.

(a) If for each $f \in H$, $f[A \times B]$ is bounded for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$ and $B \in \mathcal{B}$, then $H$ may be given the topology $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B}}$ of uniform convergence on the sets $A \times B$, making it a locally convex linear topological space.

(b) If the sets of either $\mathcal{A}$ or $\mathcal{B}$ are convex, circled, and complete, the set of all separately continuous bilinear mappings of $E \times F$ into $G$ can be topologized in this way.

(c) Under condition (b), the space of all separately continuous bilinear functionals on $E \times F$ is $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B}}$-complete if and only if the adjoint of $E$ is $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$-complete, and the adjoint of $F$ is $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{B}}$-complete.

(d) More generally, if $G$ is complete, then (c) holds with functionals replaced by mappings into $G$, provided $E$ and $F$ are Mackey spaces.
