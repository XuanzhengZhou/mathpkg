A linear space (or vector space) is a set equipped with two operations — addition and scalar multiplication — satisfying ten axioms. These axioms ensure that addition forms an abelian group and that scalar multiplication is compatible with the field operations. Linear spaces are the fundamental algebraic structure underlying all of linear analysis and topology. The definition works over both the real and complex fields, and the choice of field determines whether the space is a real or complex linear space.
