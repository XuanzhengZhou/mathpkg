A real (complex) linear space (also called a vector space or a linear space over the real (respectively, complex) field) is a non-void set $E$ and two operations called addition and scalar multiplication.

Addition is an operation $\oplus$ which satisfies the following axioms:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] For every pair of elements $x$ and $y$ in $E$, $x \oplus y$, called the sum of $x$ and $y$, is an element of $E$;
\item[(ii)] addition is commutative: $x \oplus y = y \oplus x$;
\item[(iii)] addition is associative: $x \oplus (y \oplus z) = (x \oplus y) \oplus z$;
\item[(iv)] there exists in $E$ a unique element, $\theta$, called the origin or the (additive) zero element, such that for all $x$ in $E$, $x \oplus \theta = x$; and
\item[(v)] to every $x$ in $E$ there corresponds a unique element, denoted by $-x$, such that $x \oplus (-x) = \theta$.
\end{enumerate}

Scalar multiplication is an operation $\cdot$ which satisfies the following axioms:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(vi)] For every pair consisting of a real (complex) number $a$ and an element $x$ in $E$, $a \cdot x$, called the product of $a$ and $x$, is an element of $E$;
\item[(vii)] multiplication is distributive with respect to addition in $E$: $a \cdot (x \oplus y) = a \cdot x \oplus a \cdot y$;
\item[(viii)] multiplication is distributive with respect to the addition of real (complex) numbers: $(a + b) \cdot x = a \cdot x \oplus b \cdot x$;
\item[(ix)] multiplication is associative: $a \cdot (b \cdot x) = (ab) \cdot x$;
\item[(x)] $1 \cdot x = x$ for all $x$ in $E$.
\end{enumerate}

From the axioms it follows that the set $E$ with the operation addition is an abelian group and that multiplication by a fixed scalar is an endomorphism of this group.
