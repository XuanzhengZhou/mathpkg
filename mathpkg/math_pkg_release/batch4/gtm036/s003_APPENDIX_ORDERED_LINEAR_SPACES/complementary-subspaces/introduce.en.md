Two subspaces are complementary if every vector in the ambient space can be written uniquely as the sum of a vector from each subspace. This condition is equivalent to the sum spanning the whole space and the intersection being trivial. Complementary subspaces provide the algebraic notion of direct sum decomposition and are closely related to projections and the concept of codimension.
