This problem establishes a formula for the polar of the sum of two convex, circled sets in a pairing. While $(A+B)^\circ$ is always contained in $A^\circ \cap B^\circ$, the reverse inclusion only holds up to a factor of $1/2$. The exact formula requires the $\triangle$ operation, which combines intersections of scaled copies of the individual polars. The $\triangle$ operation satisfies several algebraic properties making it a useful tool in duality theory.
