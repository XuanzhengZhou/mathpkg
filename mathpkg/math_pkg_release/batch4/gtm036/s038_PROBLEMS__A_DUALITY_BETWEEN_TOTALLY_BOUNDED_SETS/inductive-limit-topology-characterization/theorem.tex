Let $E$ have the inductive topology determined by $\{E_t, f_t : t \in A\}$, and let $E_0$ be the linear span of $\bigcup \{f_t[E_t] : t \in A\}$.

\begin{itemize}
\item[(c)] A local base is formed by all convex circled subsets $U$ of $E$ radial at $0$ such that $f_t^{-1}[U]$ is a neighborhood of zero in $E_t$ for each $t \in A$. If $\mathcal{U}_t$ is a local base in $E_t$ for each $t \in A$ and $E_0 = E$, then the family of convex circled extensions of $\bigcup \{f_t[U_t] : t \in A\}$ ($U_t \in \mathcal{U}_t$) is also a local base.

\item[(d)] A linear mapping $T$ of $E$ into a locally convex space is continuous if and only if each $T \circ f_t$ is continuous.

\item[(e)] The quotient topology of any locally convex space by a subspace and the direct sum topology are special cases of inductive topologies.

\item[(f)] When $E_0 = E$, the inductive topology is a quotient of the direct sum topology for $\sum \{E_t : t \in A\}$.
\end{itemize}
