This duality theorem establishes a symmetric relationship between total boundedness in paired linear spaces. For two weakly bounded sets $A \subset E$ and $B \subset F$ in a pairing $\langle E, F \rangle$, the total boundedness of $A$ in the topology of uniform convergence on $B$ is equivalent to the total boundedness of $B$ in the topology of uniform convergence on $A$. The result extends to families of bounded sets and follows from Theorem 8.17 together with the polar computation 16.1(vi).
