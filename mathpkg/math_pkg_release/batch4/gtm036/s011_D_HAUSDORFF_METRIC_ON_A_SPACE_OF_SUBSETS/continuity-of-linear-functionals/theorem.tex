If $f$ is a linear functional on the linear topological space $E$ and $f$ is not identically zero, then the following conditions on $f$ are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $f$ is continuous;
\item[(ii)] the null space of $f$ is closed;
\item[(iii)] the null space of $f$ is not dense in $E$;
\item[(iv)] $f$ is bounded on some neighborhood of $0$;
\item[(v)] the image under $f$ of some non-void open set is a proper subset of the scalar field;
\item[(vi)] the real part of $f$ is bounded on some neighborhood of $0$.
\end{enumerate}
