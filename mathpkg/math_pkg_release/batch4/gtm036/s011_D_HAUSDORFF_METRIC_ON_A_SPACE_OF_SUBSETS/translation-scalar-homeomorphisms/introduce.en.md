In any linear topological space, the algebraic operations induce homeomorphisms. Translation by a fixed vector is continuous with a continuous inverse (translation by the negative of that vector), hence a homeomorphism. Likewise, multiplication by any non-zero scalar is a homeomorphism. A direct consequence is that the topology is translation-invariant: a set is open or closed if and only if all its translates are, and the neighborhood system at any point is simply a translate of the neighborhood system at the origin.
