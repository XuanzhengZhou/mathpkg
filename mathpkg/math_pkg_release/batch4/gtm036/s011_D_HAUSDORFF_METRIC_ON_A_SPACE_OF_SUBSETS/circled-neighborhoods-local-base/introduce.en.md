A direct consequence of Theorem 5.1 is that in any linear topological space, the circled (balanced) neighborhoods of the origin suffice to describe the entire topology. This means one can always work with a local base consisting exclusively of sets that are closed under multiplication by scalars of modulus at most one. This property is fundamental for the theory of locally convex spaces and for arguments involving continuity of linear maps.
