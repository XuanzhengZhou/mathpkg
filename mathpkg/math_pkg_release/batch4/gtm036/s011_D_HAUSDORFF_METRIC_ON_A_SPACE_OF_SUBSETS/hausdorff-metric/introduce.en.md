The Hausdorff metric measures the distance between two closed non-void subsets of a metric space. For a point $x$ and a set $A$, the distance is defined as the infimum of distances from $x$ to points in $A$. The distance between two sets $A$ and $B$ is then defined as the maximum of the one-sided supremum distances $\sup_{a \in A} d(a, B)$ and $\sup_{b \in B} d(A, b)$. This construction yields a metric on the hyperspace of closed non-void sets, except that infinite values may occur when the underlying metric is unbounded. Infinite values can be avoided by replacing the metric with an equivalent bounded one.
