Proposition 5.2 collects seven fundamental computational facts about linear topological spaces that are used frequently throughout the theory. These include the behavior of closures under translation and scalar multiplication, the relationship between sums of closures and closures of sums, the openness of translates of open sets, compactness of sums of compact sets, an intersection formula for the closure, a separation property for compact sets from open supersets, and the fact that the sum of a compact set and a closed set is closed. These results follow directly from the continuity of the algebraic operations and the translation-invariance of the topology.
