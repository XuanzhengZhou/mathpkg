Let $E$ be a linear topological space and let $\mathcal{U}$ be the family of neighborhoods of $0$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The closure of $x + A$ is $x + A^-$ for each vector $x$ and each subset $A$ of $E$; the closure of $aA$ is $aA^-$ for each non-zero scalar $a$ and each set $A$.
\item[(ii)] If $A$ and $B$ are subsets of $E$, then $A^- + B^- \subset (A + B)^-$.
\item[(iii)] $A + U$ is open for every set $A$ and every open set $U$. In particular, $A + U$ is a neighborhood of $A$.
\item[(iv)] If $C$ and $D$ are compact subsets of $E$, then $C + D$ is compact.
\item[(v)] $A^- = \bigcap \{A + V : V \in \mathcal{U}\} = \bigcap \{A - V : V \in \mathcal{U}\}$.
\item[(vi)] If $C$ is a compact subset of $E$ and $U$ is an open set containing $C$, then there is a neighborhood $V$ of $0$ such that $C + V \subset U$.
\item[(vii)] If $C$ is compact and $F$ is closed, and $C \cap F = \emptyset$, then $C + V$ is disjoint from $F$ for some neighborhood $V$ of $0$. Consequently, $C + F$ is closed.
\end{enumerate}
