Let $E$ be a linear space and let $\mathcal{U}$ be a family of subsets of $E$ satisfying conditions (i)--(iv) [of the preceding discussion]. Define a set $W$ to be open if for each $x \in W$ there is $U \in \mathcal{U}$ such that $x + U \subset W$. Then the resulting family $\mathcal{T}$ is a vector topology for $E$, and $\mathcal{U}$ is a local base for this topology. If, further, condition (vi) holds, then $\mathcal{T}$ is a Hausdorff topology.

The proof of this proposition is straightforward and will be omitted.
