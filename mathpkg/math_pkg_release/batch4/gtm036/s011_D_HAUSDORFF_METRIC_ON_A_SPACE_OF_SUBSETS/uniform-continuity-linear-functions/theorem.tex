A continuous linear function $T$ from a linear topological space $E$ to a linear topological space $F$ is uniformly continuous in the following sense: for each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $F$ there is a neighborhood $U$ of $0$ in $E$ such that for every $x$ and $y$ in $E$, $T(x) - T(y) \in V$ whenever $x - y \in U$.

This property implies that continuous linear functions have an important extension property, which is a direct consequence of the general principle applying to arbitrary uniformly continuous functions.
