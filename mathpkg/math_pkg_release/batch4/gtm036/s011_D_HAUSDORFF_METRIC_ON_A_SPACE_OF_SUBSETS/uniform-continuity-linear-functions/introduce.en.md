Continuous linear maps between linear topological spaces are automatically uniformly continuous in a translation-invariant sense. Specifically, the preimage of any neighborhood of zero in the codomain contains a neighborhood of zero in the domain such that differences lying in the latter map into the former. Intuitively, two points are close together precisely when their difference is near zero, so there is a uniform notion of nearness. This uniform continuity property underlies the extension theorem for continuous linear maps: such maps can be extended to completions, just as uniformly continuous functions between metric spaces extend to completions.
