This exercise establishes that weak compactness together with convexity implies boundedness in any admissible topology. In a pairing $(E,F)$, a set that is compact in the weak topology $w(E,F)$ and convex is necessarily bounded for every admissible topology $\mathcal{T}$. This illustrates the fundamental role of weak compactness in guaranteeing boundedness across all compatible topologies.
