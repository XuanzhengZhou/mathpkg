Let $E$ be a linear topological space with topology $\mathcal{T}$ such that $(P)$ holds: the only vector topologies which have local bases consisting of $\mathcal{T}$-closed sets are those weaker than $\mathcal{T}$. Then the Banach-Steinhaus theorem (12.2) holds for $E$: every pointwise bounded family of continuous linear mappings from $E$ into any locally convex space is equicontinuous.
