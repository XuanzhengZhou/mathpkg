Let $T$ be a linear mapping of a locally convex Hausdorff space $E$ into a fully complete space $F$. Then $T$ is continuous provided that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] the graph of $T$ in $E \times F$ is closed;
\item[(ii)] for each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $F$, the closure of $T^{-1}[V]$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$.
\end{itemize}
