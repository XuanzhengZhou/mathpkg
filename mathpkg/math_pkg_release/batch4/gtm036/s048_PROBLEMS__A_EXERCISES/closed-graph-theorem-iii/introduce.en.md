This closed graph theorem generalizes the result of 13G by replacing the hypothesis that $F$ is complete metric with the weaker condition that $F$ is fully complete. The additional condition (ii) -- that the inverse image of neighborhoods has dense interior -- is called "$T^{-1}$ is somewhere dense." The theorem is sharp: full completeness of $F$ is also necessary if both this closed graph theorem and the corresponding open mapping theorem are to hold. Equivalent formulations relate full completeness to continuity of somewhere-dense mappings and openness of continuous somewhere-dense surjections.
