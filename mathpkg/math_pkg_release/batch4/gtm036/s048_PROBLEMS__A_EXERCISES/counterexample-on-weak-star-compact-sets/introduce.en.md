This counter-example demonstrates that the weak* closed convex extension of a weak* compact set need not be weak* compact, in contrast to the Banach-Alaoglu theorem which guarantees weak* compactness of polars of neighborhoods. The space is the finitely supported sequences with the $\ell^1$ norm. The set $\{2^{2n}f_n\}$ is a discrete sequence converging to 0 in the weak* topology, hence weak* compact, but its convex hull is unbounded and its weak* closed convex extension fails to be compact. This also shows that the Mackey topology on this dual space is not sequentially complete.
