This lemma provides the fundamental criterion for embedding a linear topological space into a product of such spaces via a family of coordinate maps. The coordinatewise-defined map $R$ is continuous precisely when each coordinate map is continuous. A relative-openness condition — that neighborhoods of zero in the domain contain inverse images of neighborhoods in some coordinate space — guarantees that $R$ is relatively open, and together with injectivity yields a topological isomorphism onto a subspace of the product.
