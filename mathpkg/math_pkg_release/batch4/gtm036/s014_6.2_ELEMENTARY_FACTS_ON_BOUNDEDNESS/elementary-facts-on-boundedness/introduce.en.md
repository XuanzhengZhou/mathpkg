This proposition collects the elementary algebraic and topological closure properties of bounded sets in a linear topological space. Part (i) shows that the collection of bounded sets is closed under scalar multiplication, closure, Minkowski sum, and finite unions. Parts (ii) and (iii) relate boundedness of a linear map to continuity: continuity implies boundedness in general, and the converse holds when the domain is pseudo-normable. A sufficient condition for continuity is that some neighborhood of zero has bounded image.
