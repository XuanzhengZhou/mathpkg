This proposition establishes that the natural restriction maps $I_{UV}: A(V) \to A(U)$ for $U \subset V$ are continuous with respect to the compact-open topologies. Moreover, since the directed family $\mathcal{U}$ of open neighborhoods of $C$ contains a countable cofinal subfamily, the inductive limit $F(C)$ can be expressed as a sequential inductive limit of Fréchet spaces, making it a generalized $LF$ space. This structural result is important for applying general theorems about $LF$ spaces to spaces of analytic germs.
