This duality theorem establishes a fundamental relationship in the theory of spaces of analytic functions: the strong dual of $A(U)$, the space of analytic functions on an open set $U$ with the compact-open topology, is isomorphic (as a locally convex space) to $F(\sim U)$, the space of germs of analytic functions near the complement of $U$. Since both spaces are generalized $LF$ spaces, the proof reduces to showing continuity of the natural algebraic isomorphism, which relies on the continuity of the mapping from $A(V)$ into $A(U)^*$ for each open set $V$ containing $\sim U$.
