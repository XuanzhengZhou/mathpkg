$F(C)$ is the space of germs of analytic functions defined near a closed proper subset $C$ of the Riemann sphere. It is constructed as the inductive limit of Fréchet spaces $A(U)$ of analytic functions on open sets $U$ containing $C$, where each $A(U)$ carries the compact-open topology. Two functions are identified if they agree on some neighborhood of $C$. This construction is fundamental in the theory of spaces of analytic functions and provides a natural locally convex topology for spaces of germs.
