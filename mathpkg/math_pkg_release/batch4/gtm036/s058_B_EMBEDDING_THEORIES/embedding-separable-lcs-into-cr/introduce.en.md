This theorem establishes that every locally convex, metrizable, separable topological vector space $E$ can be embedded as a subspace of $C(R)$, the space of continuous scalar-valued functions on the real line equipped with the compact-open topology. The result is a universal embedding theorem showing that $C(R)$ serves as a "universal space" for a large class of locally convex spaces. The proof proceeds via a lemma that constructs a continuous surjection from $R$ onto the weak* dual $E^*$, whose compact images form a co-base for equicontinuous sets.
