The Hausdorff condition follows from the fact that $E^p(\Omega)$ is the projective limit of the spaces $l^p(\omega)$, and the projective limit is Hausdorff iff the canonical mappings separate points, which happens exactly when for each $t \in A$ there exists $\omega \in \Omega$ with $\omega(t) > 0$. Completeness follows from the completeness of each $l^p(\omega)$ and the fact that projective limits of complete spaces are complete. The description of the adjoint follows from the duality $l^p(\omega)^* \cong l^q(\omega)$ and the fact that the algebraic dual of a projective limit is the inductive limit of the duals.
