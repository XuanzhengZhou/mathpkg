This proposition describes the topological structure of the dual space $E^p(\Omega)^+$. The family of duals $\{l^p(\omega)^+\}$ forms an inductive system under the natural inclusions when $\omega \geq \sigma$, and $E^p(\Omega)^+$ is precisely its inductive limit. The inductive limit topology is always stronger than the strong dual topology. Crucially, for $1 < p < \infty$, these two topologies coincide, implying that $E^p(\Omega)$ is semi-reflexive. The case $p = 1$ is a counterexample: $l^1$ itself shows the result can fail.
