If $\omega \geq \sigma$, then $l^p(\sigma)^+ \subset l^p(\omega)^+$ and so, with injections for the canonical mappings, the family $\{l^p(\omega)^+ : \omega \in \Omega\}$ forms an inductive system, for which $E^p(\Omega)^+$ is the inductive limit. The inductive limit topology on $E^p(\Omega)^+$ is stronger than the strong topology, but if $1 < p < \infty$ they coincide and $E^p(\Omega)$ is semi-reflexive.
