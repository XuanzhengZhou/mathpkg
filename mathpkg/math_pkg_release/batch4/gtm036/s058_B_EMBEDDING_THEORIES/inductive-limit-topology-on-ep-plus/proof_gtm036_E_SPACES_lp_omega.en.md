The inclusion $l^p(\sigma)^+ \subset l^p(\omega)^+$ for $\omega \geq \sigma$ follows from the fact that a larger weight gives a larger dual space. The fact that $E^p(\Omega)^+$ is the inductive limit follows from the duality between projective and inductive limits. To prove that the inductive limit topology coincides with the strong topology when $1 < p < \infty$, one proves that any linear functional continuous relative to the inductive limit topology on $E^p(\Omega)^+$ is the evaluation at a member of $E^p(\Omega)$. Semi-reflexivity then follows. The case $p = 1$ provides a counterexample.
