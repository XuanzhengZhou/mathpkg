If $f$ is a linear functional on the linear topological space $E$ and $f$ is not identically zero, then the following conditions on $f$ are equivalent: (i) $f$ is continuous; (ii) the null space of $f$ is closed; (iii) the null space of $f$ is not dense in $E$; (iv) $f$ is bounded on some neighborhood of $0$; (v) the image under $f$ of some non-void open set is a proper subset of the scalar field.
