The Lemma on Projections states that any linear topological space $E$ with a continuous projection $T$ decomposes as the topological product $R \times N$, where $R$ is the range and $N$ is the null space of $T$. The isomorphism is given explicitly by $x \mapsto (T(x), x - T(x))$. This lemma is a key structural result used to show that arbitrary linear topological spaces differ little from Hausdorff spaces (Theorem 5.11).
