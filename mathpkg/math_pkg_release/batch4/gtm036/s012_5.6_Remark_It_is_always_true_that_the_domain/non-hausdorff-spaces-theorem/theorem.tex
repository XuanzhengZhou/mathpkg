Let $E$ be a linear topological space, let $F = \{0\}^{-}$ be the closure of $\{0\}$, and let $G$ be an arbitrary subspace of $E$ which is complementary to $F$. Then

(i) The relative topology for $G$ is Hausdorff;
(ii) $E$ is topologically isomorphic to $G \times F$, where $F$ has the trivial topology.
