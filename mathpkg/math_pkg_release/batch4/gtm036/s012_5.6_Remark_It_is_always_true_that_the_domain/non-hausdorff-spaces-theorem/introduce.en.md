Every linear topological space $E$ decomposes as the topological product of a Hausdorff space $G$ and a space $F$ with the trivial topology. Here $F = \{0\}^{-}$ is the closure of the origin, which is always a linear subspace. The complementary subspace $G$ inherits a Hausdorff topology because $\{0\}$ is closed in $G$ (since $\{0\} = G \cap \{0\}^{-}$ is closed in the relative topology). The result follows from the Lemma on Projections (5.10) together with the fact that the projection onto $F$ is continuous.
