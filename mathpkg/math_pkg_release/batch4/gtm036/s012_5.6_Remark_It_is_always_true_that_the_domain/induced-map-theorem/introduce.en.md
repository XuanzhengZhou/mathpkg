The Induced Map Theorem relates the continuity and openness of a linear map $T$ to those of its induced map $S$ on the quotient by the null space. It follows from the fact that the quotient map $Q: E \to E/F$ is both open and continuous. Specifically, $S \circ Q = T$, so $T^{-1}[W]$ is open iff $Q^{-1}[S^{-1}[W]]$ is open, giving the continuity equivalence; similarly for openness. The theorem shows that $T$ being both continuous and open characterizes when $S$ is a topological isomorphism.
