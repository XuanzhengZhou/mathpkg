The domain of the closure $\bar{T}$ of a continuous linear operator $T$ is always contained in the closure of the domain of $T$. When the target space $H$ is complete, equality holds: $D_{\bar{T}} = \bar{D}_T$. The proof uses Cauchy nets: if $x \in \bar{D}_T$, a net in $D_T$ converging to $x$ is Cauchy, its image under $T$ is Cauchy, and completeness of $H$ gives a limit $z$ such that $(x,z) \in \bar{G}_T = G_{\bar{T}}$, hence $x \in D_{\bar{T}}$.
