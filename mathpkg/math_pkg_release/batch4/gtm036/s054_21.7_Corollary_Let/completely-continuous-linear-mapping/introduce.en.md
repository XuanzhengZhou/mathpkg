A linear mapping between Hausdorff linear topological spaces is completely continuous if some neighborhood of zero is mapped into a compact set. This definition generalizes the classical notion for Banach spaces (where a map is completely continuous if it sends bounded sets to totally bounded sets). Completely continuous mappings are automatically continuous and form the foundation for the Riesz theory of compact operators on linear topological spaces.
