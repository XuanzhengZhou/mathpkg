Let $E$ be a linear topological space and $T$ a completely continuous linear mapping of $E$ into itself. Let $\lambda$ be a non-zero scalar, and write $\lambda I - T = S$. Let $G$ be a closed proper subspace of a subspace $F$ of $E$ such that $S[F] \subset G$. If $U$ is a convex circled neighborhood of $0$ mapped by $T$ into a compact set, then there is a point $x \in F \cap (2U)$ but $x \notin G + U$.
