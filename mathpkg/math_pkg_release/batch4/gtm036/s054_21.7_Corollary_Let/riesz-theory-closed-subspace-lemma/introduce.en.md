This lemma provides the key geometric estimate for the Riesz theory of completely continuous operators. Given a closed proper subspace $G$ properly contained in $F$, with $S = \lambda I - T$ mapping $F$ into $G$, and a convex circled neighborhood $U$ mapped by $T$ into a compact set, the lemma guarantees the existence of a point in $F \cap (2U)$ that is at least distance-like away from $G$ (namely, not in $G + U$). This separation property drives the stabilization arguments for the ascending chain of nullspaces $S^{-r}[0]$ and the descending chain of ranges $S^r[E]$.
