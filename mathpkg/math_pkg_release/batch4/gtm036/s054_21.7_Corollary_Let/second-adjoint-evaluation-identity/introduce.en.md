For a continuous linear transformation $T \colon E \to F$ between locally convex spaces, the adjoint $T^* \colon F^* \to E^*$ is always strongly continuous (by Theorem 21.6), so its adjoint $T^{**} \colon E^{**} \to F^{**}$ exists. This proposition shows that $T^{**}$ extends $T$ in the natural sense: the diagram formed by the evaluation maps $I_E$, $I_F$ and the transformation and its second adjoint commutes. In other words, after embedding $E$ and $F$ into their biduals via evaluation, $T^{**}$ coincides with $T$ on the images.
