Let $E$ be a linear topological space and $T$ a completely continuous linear mapping of $E$ into itself. Let $\lambda$ be a non-zero scalar and write $\lambda I - T = S$. Then the sequence $\{S^{-r}[0]\}$ of subspaces increases; there is an integer $n \geq 0$ such that $S^{-r}[0] \subsetneq S^{-(r+1)}[0]$ properly for $0 \leq r < n$ and $S^{-r}[0] = S^{-(r+1)}[0]$ for $r \geq n$.
