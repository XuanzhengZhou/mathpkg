For a continuous linear transformation $T$ between locally convex spaces, if $T$ is (relatively) open with respect to the original topologies, then it is also (relatively) open with respect to the weak topologies. This corollary follows from the fact that when $T$ is relatively open, the range of its adjoint $T^*$ is weak$^*$ closed (by Theorem 21.3(ii)), and then Theorem 21.6(ii) yields the conclusion. The result bridges the gap between the original topology and the weak topology for properties of openness.
