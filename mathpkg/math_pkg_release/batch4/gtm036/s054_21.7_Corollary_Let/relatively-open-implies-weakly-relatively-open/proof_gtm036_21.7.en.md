If $T$ is a continuous linear transformation on a locally convex space $E$ into a locally convex Hausdorff space $F$ and if $T$ is relatively open, then Theorem 21.3(ii) implies that the range of $T^*$ is weak$^*$ closed, and hence, using part (ii) of Theorem 21.6, $T$ is weakly relatively open. The statement for $T$ open follows similarly by the same reasoning applied to the case where $T$ is an open map onto its range.
