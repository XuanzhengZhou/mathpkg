Let $\Re$ be a vector space over a field $\Phi$ and let $\Re^*$ be the conjugate (dual) space of $\Re$. Any Kronecker product space

$$\underbrace{\Re \times \cdots \times \Re}_{r\text{ factors}} \times \underbrace{\Re^* \times \cdots \times \Re^*}_{s\text{ factors}}$$

is called a \text{tensor space} based on the space $\Re$. Its elements are called tensors contravariant of rank $r$ and covariant of rank $s$. The space is denoted by the abbreviated notation $\Re_s^r$. By convention, $\Phi$ is regarded as the tensor space $\Re_0^0$ of ranks $0$.

If $(e_1, e_2, \cdots, e_n)$ is a basis in $\Re$ and $(e^1, e^2, \cdots, e^n)$ is the complementary basis in $\Re^*$ satisfying $e^j(e_i) = \delta_i^j$, then the vectors

$$e_{i_1} \times e_{i_2} \times \cdots \times e_{i_r} \times e^{j_1} \times \cdots \times e^{j_s}$$

constitute a basis in the tensor space $\Re_s^r$. Any element of this space can be written as

$$\sum \xi_{j_1 \cdots j_s}^{i_1 \cdots i_r} e_{i_1} \times \cdots \times e_{i_r} \times e^{j_1} \times \cdots \times e^{j_s}.$$
