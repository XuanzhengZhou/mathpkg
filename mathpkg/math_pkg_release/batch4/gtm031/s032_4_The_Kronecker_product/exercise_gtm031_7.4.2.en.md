Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be the free algebra based on an $n$ dimensional vector space $\Re$ and let $\mathfrak{B}$ be the (two-sided) ideal in $\mathfrak{F}$ generated by the vectors $x \times y - y \times x$, $x$ and $y$ in $\Re$. Show that $\mathfrak{F}/\mathfrak{B}$ is essentially the polynomial algebra in $n$ algebraically independent transcendental elements.
