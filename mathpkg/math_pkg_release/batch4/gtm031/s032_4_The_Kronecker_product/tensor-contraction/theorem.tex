Let $\Re$ be a vector space over a field $\Phi$ and let $\Re_s^r$ be a tensor space based on $\Re$. Choose $k \in \{1, 2, \cdots, r\}$ and $l \in \{1, \cdots, s\}$. The linear mapping

$$\sum x_1 \times \cdots \times x_r \times y_1^* \times \cdots \times y_s^* \;\longmapsto\; \sum y_l^*(x_k) \, x_1 \times \cdots \times x_{k-1} \times x_{k+1} \times \cdots \times x_r \times y_1^* \times \cdots \times y_{l-1}^* \times y_{l+1}^* \times \cdots \times y_s^*$$

is a linear transformation of $\Re_s^r$ onto $\Re_{s-1}^{r-1}$. This mapping is called the \text{contraction} of $\Re_s^r$ with respect to the $k$th contravariant index and the $l$th covariant index.

The contraction is obtained by composing the bilinear evaluation map $(x, y^*) \mapsto y^*(x)$ on $\Re \times \Re^*$ with the identity on the remaining factors, after reordering the Kronecker factors to bring the $k$th contravariant and $l$th covariant factors together.
