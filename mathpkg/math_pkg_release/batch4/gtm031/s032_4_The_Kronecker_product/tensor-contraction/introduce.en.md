Contraction is a fundamental operation on tensor spaces that reduces both the contravariant and covariant rank by one. It is defined by selecting a contravariant index $k$ and a covariant index $l$, then applying the natural evaluation pairing $y^*(x)$ to the corresponding factors while leaving all other factors unchanged. The construction relies on the fact that the bilinear form $(x, y^*) \mapsto y^*(x)$ on $\Re \times \Re^*$ induces a linear map from $\Re_1^1 = \Re \times \Re^*$ onto $\Phi = \Re_0^0$, which is then extended via reordering equivalences to arbitrary tensor spaces $\Re_s^r$.
