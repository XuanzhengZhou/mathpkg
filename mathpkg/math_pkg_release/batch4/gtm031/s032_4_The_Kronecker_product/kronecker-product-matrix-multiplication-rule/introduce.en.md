This proposition states the fundamental multiplication rule for Kronecker products of matrices: the product of two Kronecker products equals the Kronecker product of the individual matrix products. This follows directly from the corresponding identity $(A \times B)(C \times D) = AC \times BD$ for linear transformations. The rule is essential for computations involving Kronecker products and underpins many properties of tensor product constructions in linear algebra.
