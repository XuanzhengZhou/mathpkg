Show that the matrix of $A \times B$ relative to the basis

$$(e_1 \times f_1, e_2 \times f_1, \cdots, e_n \times f_1; e_1 \times f_2, e_2 \times f_2, \cdots, e_n \times f_2; \cdots; e_1 \times f_m, e_2 \times f_m, \cdots, e_n \times f_m)$$

is $(\beta) \times (\alpha)$. Hence prove that $(\beta) \times (\alpha)$ is similar to $(\alpha) \times (\beta)$.
