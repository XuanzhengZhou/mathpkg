The Kronecker product of two matrices $(\alpha)$ (of size $n \times n$) and $(\beta)$ (of size $m \times m$) is the $nm \times nm$ matrix representing the Kronecker product of the corresponding linear transformations $A$ and $B$ with respect to the lexicographically ordered basis $e_i \times f_j$ of the Kronecker product space. The space of $nm \times nm$ matrices is itself the Kronecker product of the spaces of $n \times n$ and $m \times m$ matrices, and the basic rules for Kronecker multiplication of linear transformations carry over to matrices.
