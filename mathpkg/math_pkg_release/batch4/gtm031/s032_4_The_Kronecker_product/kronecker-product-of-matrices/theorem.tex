Let $(\alpha)$ be an $n \times n$ matrix and $(\beta)$ be an $m \times m$ matrix over a field $\Phi$. Let $\Re$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ be vector spaces of dimensions $n$ and $m$ respectively, with bases $(e_1, \cdots, e_n)$ and $(f_1, \cdots, f_m)$. Let $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\Re, \Re)$ have matrix $(\alpha)$ relative to $(e_i)$ and $B \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{S})$ have matrix $(\beta)$ relative to $(f_j)$. The \text{Kronecker product of matrices} $(\alpha)$ and $(\beta)$, denoted $(\alpha) \times (\beta)$, is defined as the matrix of the linear transformation $A \times B$ relative to the basis

$$(e_1 \times f_1, e_2 \times f_1, \cdots, e_n \times f_1; e_1 \times f_2, e_2 \times f_2, \cdots, e_n \times f_2; \cdots; e_1 \times f_m, e_2 \times f_m, \cdots, e_n \times f_m)$$

of $\Re \times \mathfrak{S}$, ordered lexicographically.
