The free associative algebra $\mathfrak{F}$ based on a vector space $\Re$ over a field $\Phi$ is constructed as the direct sum of all Kronecker powers $\Re_0^i = \Re \times \cdots \times \Re$ ($i$ factors), with the convention $\Re_0^0 = \Phi$ and $\Re_0^1 = \Re$. Multiplication is defined by convolution: the degree-$k$ component of the product is the sum of Kronecker products of degree-$i$ and degree-$(k-i)$ components. This construction yields the most general associative algebra generated by $\Re$ without imposing any relations, analogous to the polynomial algebra for a one-dimensional space.
