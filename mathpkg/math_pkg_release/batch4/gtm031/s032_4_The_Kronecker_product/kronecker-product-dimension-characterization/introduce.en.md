Theorem 3 provides a purely dimension-based characterization of the Kronecker product. Given three vector spaces $\Re$, $\mathfrak{S}$, $\mathfrak{P}$ over a field $\Phi$ with a bilinear product $x \times y$ satisfying the standard product conditions ($1'$, $2'$, $3'$), the space $\mathfrak{P}$ is a Kronecker product of $\Re$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ if and only if its dimension equals the product of the dimensions of the factor spaces. This result shows that the dimension condition can serve as a substitute for the independence conditions, simplifying the verification that a given construction is indeed a Kronecker product.
