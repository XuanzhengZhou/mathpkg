Let $\Re$, $\mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{P}$ be vector spaces over a field $\Phi$ and suppose that there is defined a product $x \times y$, $x \in \Re$, $y \in \mathfrak{S}$, $x \times y \in \mathfrak{P}$, such that $1'$, $2'$, and $3'$ hold. Then $\mathfrak{P}$ is a Kronecker product of $\Re$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ if and only if

$$\dim \mathfrak{P} = \dim \Re \cdot \dim \mathfrak{S}.$$
