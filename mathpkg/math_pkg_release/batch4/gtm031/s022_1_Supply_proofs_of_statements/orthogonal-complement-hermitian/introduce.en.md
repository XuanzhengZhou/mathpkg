The orthogonal complement $\mathfrak{S}^{\perp}$ of a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ with respect to a hermitian scalar product $g$ consists of all vectors orthogonal to every vector in $\mathfrak{S}$. The operation $\mathfrak{S} \mapsto \mathfrak{S}^{\perp}$ is a fundamental construction in scalar product geometry. For non-degenerate hermitian forms, it defines an involutorial anti-automorphism of the lattice of subspaces, satisfying $\dim \mathfrak{S}^{\perp} = n - \dim \mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{S}^{\perp\perp} = \mathfrak{S}$.
