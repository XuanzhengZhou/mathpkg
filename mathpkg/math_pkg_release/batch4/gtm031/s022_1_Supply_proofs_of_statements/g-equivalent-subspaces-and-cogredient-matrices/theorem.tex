Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ with anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$, and let $g$ be a scalar product on $\mathfrak{R}$. Let $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ be subspaces of $\mathfrak{R}$ of equal dimension.

If arbitrary bases are chosen in $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$, and if $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ are $g$-equivalent (i.e., there exists a linear isomorphism $T : \mathfrak{S}_1 \to \mathfrak{S}_2$ preserving the scalar product), then the matrices of the contractions of $g$ to these subspaces relative to the chosen bases are cogredient.

Conversely, if the matrices of the contractions of $g$ to $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ are cogredient, then $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ are $g$-equivalent.
