The matrix of a scalar product $g$ on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ relative to a basis $\{e_i\}$ has entries $\beta_{ij} = g(e_i, e_j)$. Together with the anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ on the underlying division ring, this matrix encodes the entire scalar product via $g(x, y) = \sum \xi_i \beta_{ij} \bar{\eta}_j$, generalizing the familiar formula $x^T G \bar{y}$ for hermitian forms over the complex numbers.
