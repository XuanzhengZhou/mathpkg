Every linear transformation between vector spaces equipped with dual pairings by non-degenerate bilinear forms admits a canonical decomposition as a sum of elementary "product" transformations $u_i' \times v_i$. This representation is the abstract counterpart of writing a matrix as a sum of rank-one matrices. The minimal number $r$ of terms in such a representation equals the rank of the transformation, and this decomposition is unique when the sets $\{u_i'\}$ and $\{v_i\}$ are chosen to be linearly independent.
