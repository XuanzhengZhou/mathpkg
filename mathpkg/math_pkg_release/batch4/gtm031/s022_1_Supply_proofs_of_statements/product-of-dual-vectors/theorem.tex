Let $\mathfrak{R}_i$ and $\mathfrak{R}_i'$, $i = 1, 2$, be dual vector spaces relative to non-degenerate bilinear forms $g_i : \mathfrak{R}_i \times \mathfrak{R}_i' \to \Delta$. Let $u' \in \mathfrak{R}_1'$ and $v \in \mathfrak{R}_2$ be fixed vectors. The **product** $u' \times v$ is the linear transformation of $\mathfrak{R}_1$ into $\mathfrak{R}_2$ defined by

$$x \mapsto g_1(x, u')\, v$$

for all $x \in \mathfrak{R}_1$.

More generally, if $u_1', u_2', \dots, u_m' \in \mathfrak{R}_1'$ and $v_1, v_2, \dots, v_m \in \mathfrak{R}_2$, the expression $\sum_i u_i' \times v_i$ denotes the linear transformation

$$x \mapsto \sum_{i=1}^m g_1(x, u_i')\, v_i.$$

The product satisfies the following bilinearity properties:
$$(u_1' + u_2') \times v = u_1' \times v + u_2' \times v,$$
$$u' \times (v_1 + v_2) = u' \times v_1 + u' \times v_2,$$
$$u' \alpha \times v = u' \times \alpha v \quad (\alpha \in \Delta).$$
