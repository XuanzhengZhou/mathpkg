Given dual vector spaces $\mathfrak{R}_i$ and $\mathfrak{R}_i'$ paired by non-degenerate bilinear forms $g_i$, one can construct linear transformations $\mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$ directly from pairs of vectors. For fixed $u' \in \mathfrak{R}_1'$ and $v \in \mathfrak{R}_2$, the product $u' \times v$ sends $x \in \mathfrak{R}_1$ to $g_1(x, u') v$. This bilinear construction is fundamental: every linear transformation between the spaces can be expressed as a sum of such elementary products, similar to expressing a matrix as a sum of elementary matrices.
