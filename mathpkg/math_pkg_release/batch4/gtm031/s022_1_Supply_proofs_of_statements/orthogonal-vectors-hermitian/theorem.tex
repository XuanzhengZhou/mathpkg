Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ with an involutorial anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$, and let $g$ be a hermitian scalar product on $\mathfrak{R}$. Two vectors $u, v \in \mathfrak{R}$ are said to be **orthogonal** (relative to $g$) if

$$g(u, v) = 0.$$

Since $g$ is hermitian, the orthogonality relation is **symmetric**: $g(u, v) = 0$ if and only if $g(v, u) = 0$, because $g(v, u) = \overline{g(u, v)} = \bar{0} = 0$.
