For a hermitian scalar product $g$, two vectors $u$ and $v$ are orthogonal if $g(u, v) = 0$. The hermitian symmetry $g(v, u) = \overline{g(u, v)}$ guarantees that orthogonality is a symmetric relation -- if $u$ is orthogonal to $v$, then $v$ is orthogonal to $u$. This symmetry is essential for building a meaningful geometry of orthogonal complements and for the spectral theory of hermitian operators.
