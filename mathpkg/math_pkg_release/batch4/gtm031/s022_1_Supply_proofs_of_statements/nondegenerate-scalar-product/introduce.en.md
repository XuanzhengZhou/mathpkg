A scalar product is non-degenerate if no non-zero vector is orthogonal to the entire space. This condition is the scalar-product analogue of non-singularity for bilinear forms and ensures that the mapping $x \mapsto g(x, \cdot)$ from $\mathfrak{R}$ to its dual is injective. Non-degeneracy is the key hypothesis for the rich structural theory of orthogonal complements: for a non-degenerate hermitian scalar product on a finite-dimensional space, the map $\mathfrak{S} \mapsto \mathfrak{S}^{\perp}$ is an involutorial lattice anti-automorphism with $\dim \mathfrak{S}^{\perp} = n - \dim \mathfrak{S}$.
