Let $\Delta$ be a division ring with an anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$. For any matrix $(\mu)$ with entries in $\Delta$, its **conjugate transpose** is the matrix

$$(\nu) = (\bar{\mu})',$$

that is, $\nu_{ji} = \bar{\mu}_{ij}$ for all $i, j$. In other words, the conjugate transpose is obtained by applying the anti-automorphism to each entry and then transposing: the $(j,i)$-entry of $(\nu)$ is the image under the anti-automorphism of the $(i,j)$-entry of $(\mu)$.

When relating left and right bases $f_i = \sum \mu_{ij} e_j$ and $f_i = \sum e_j \nu_{ji}$, the connecting matrices satisfy $(\nu) = (\bar{\mu})'$.
