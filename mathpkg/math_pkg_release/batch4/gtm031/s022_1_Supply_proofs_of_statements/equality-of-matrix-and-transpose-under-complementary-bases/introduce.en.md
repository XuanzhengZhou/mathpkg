When dual spaces $\mathfrak{R}_i$ and $\mathfrak{R}_i'$ are equipped with complementary bases derived from non-degenerate bilinear forms $g_i$, the matrix of a linear transformation $A : \mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$ and the matrix of its transpose $A' : \mathfrak{R}_2' \to \mathfrak{R}_1'$ coincide. This follows directly from the adjoint relation $g_2(e_i A, f_l') = g_1(e_i, f_l' A')$, which expresses the fundamental duality between a transformation and its transpose. The result generalizes the familiar situation of a vector space and its dual space of linear functions.
