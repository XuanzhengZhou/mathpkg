Let $\Delta$ be a division ring equipped with an anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$, and let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a left vector space over $\Delta$. Define right multiplication by scalars via

$$x \bar{\alpha} = \alpha x \quad (x \in \mathfrak{R},\; \alpha \in \Delta).$$

Equivalently, if $\alpha \mapsto \alpha^A$ denotes the inverse of the anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$, then

$$x \alpha = \alpha^A x.$$

With this definition, $\mathfrak{R}$ becomes a right vector space over $\Delta$. The left and right dimensionalities of $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Delta$ are equal. Moreover, if $(e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n)$ is a left basis for $\mathfrak{R}$, then it is also a right basis, with the coordinate relation $x = \sum \xi_i e_i = \sum e_i \bar{\xi}_i$.
