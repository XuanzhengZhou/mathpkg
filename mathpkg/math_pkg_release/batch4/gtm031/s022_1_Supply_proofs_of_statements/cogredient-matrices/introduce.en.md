Cogredience is the matrix equivalence relation appropriate for scalar products: two matrices $(\beta)$ and $(\gamma)$ are cogredient if $(\gamma) = (\mu)(\beta)(\bar{\mu})'$ for some non-singular $(\mu)$. This is the scalar-product analogue of similarity for linear transformations. Under a change of left basis given by $(\mu)$, the matrix of a scalar product transforms by cogredience, with the conjugate transpose $(\bar{\mu})'$ appearing because the scalar product is conjugate-linear in the second argument.
