A scalar product $g$ on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ is hermitian if it satisfies the conjugate-symmetry condition $g(y, x) = \overline{g(x, y)}$, where the bar denotes an involutorial anti-automorphism on the underlying division ring $\Delta$. This generalizes both symmetric bilinear forms (when $\bar{\alpha} = \alpha$) and complex Hermitian forms (when $\Delta = \mathbb{C}$ with complex conjugation). Hermitian scalar products are the algebraic foundation for orthogonal geometry, allowing the definition of orthogonality, orthogonal complements, and the study of isometries.
