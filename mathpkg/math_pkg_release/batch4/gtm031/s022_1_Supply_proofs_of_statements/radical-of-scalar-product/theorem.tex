Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space with a hermitian scalar product $g$. The **radical** of $g$ is the subspace

$$\mathfrak{R}^{\perp} = \{\, z \in \mathfrak{R} \mid g(x, z) = 0 \text{ for all } x \in \mathfrak{R} \,\}.$$

That is, the radical is the orthogonal complement of the whole space $\mathfrak{R}$. It consists of all vectors that are orthogonal to every vector in $\mathfrak{R}$.

The scalar product $g$ is called **non-degenerate** if its radical is trivial: $\mathfrak{R}^{\perp} = \{0\}$.
