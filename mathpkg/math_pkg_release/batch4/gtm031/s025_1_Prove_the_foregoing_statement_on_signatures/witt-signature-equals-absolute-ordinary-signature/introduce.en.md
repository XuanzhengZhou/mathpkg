The Witt signature (or Witt index) is a non-negative integer invariant of the cogredience class of a scalar product, measuring the maximal dimensionality of totally isotropic subspaces. For the classical cases of symmetric bilinear forms over the real numbers, hermitian forms over the complex numbers, and hermitian forms over the real quaternions, this purely algebraic invariant coincides with the absolute value of the ordinary (analytic) signature defined by counting positive and negative eigenvalues. The proof of this statement is left as an exercise.
