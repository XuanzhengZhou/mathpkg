When $\mathfrak{R}$ is a two-sided vector space and $\mathfrak{S}$ is a left vector space over the same division ring $\Delta$, the direct product $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$ (formed by treating $\mathfrak{R}$ as a right space) can be given a left vector space structure. The left scalar multiplication is defined by acting only on the $\mathfrak{R}$-component. This construction ensures that the direct product again has a vector space structure, resolving the issue that the direct product of two one-sided spaces only yields a group.
