Given an $n$-dimensional left vector space over a field $\Phi$, one can endow it with a right vector space structure by choosing $n$ automorphisms $S_1,\dots,S_n$ of $\Phi$ and defining right scalar multiplication via the diagonal matrix of the automorphism images. The key verification is that the right multiplications are linear transformations in the left space, ensuring the commutation condition holds. This construction demonstrates that two-sided vector spaces arise naturally from any left vector space together with a choice of field automorphisms.
