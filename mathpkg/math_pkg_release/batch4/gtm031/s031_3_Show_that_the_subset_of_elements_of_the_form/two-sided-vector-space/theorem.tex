A two-sided vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ is a set $\mathfrak{R}$ which is simultaneously a left vector space and a right vector space over $\Delta$ such that the left and right scalar multiplications commute, i.e.,

$$(\alpha x)\beta = \alpha(x\beta)$$

for all $\alpha, \beta \in \Delta$ and $x \in \mathfrak{R}$.
