Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a two-sided vector space over $\Delta$ with left basis $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a left vector space over $\Delta$ with basis $(f_1, \dots, f_m)$. Then the vectors $e_i \times f_j$ ($i = 1,\dots,n$, $j = 1,\dots,m$) form a basis for the left vector space $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$.
