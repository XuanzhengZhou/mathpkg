Two two-sided vector spaces are equivalent if there exists a bijective linear transformation between them that respects both the left and right vector space structures. This is the natural notion of isomorphism in the category of two-sided vector spaces. Just as one-sided vector spaces are classified up to isomorphism by their dimension, two-sided vector spaces are classified up to equivalence by their left and right dimensions together with the structural relationship between them encoded in the automorphisms defining the right multiplication.
