A linear transformation of a two-sided vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ into a two-sided vector space $\mathfrak{S}$ (both over the same division ring $\Delta$) is a mapping $L: \mathfrak{R} \to \mathfrak{S}$ which is linear for the left vector space structures of $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ and is linear also for the right vector space structures of $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{S}$. That is, for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{R}$ and $\alpha \in \Delta$,

$$L(x + y) = L(x) + L(y),$$
$$L(\alpha x) = \alpha L(x),$$
$$L(x \alpha) = L(x) \alpha.$$
