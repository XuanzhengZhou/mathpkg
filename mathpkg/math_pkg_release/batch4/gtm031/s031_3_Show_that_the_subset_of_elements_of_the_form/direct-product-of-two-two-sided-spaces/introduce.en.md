When both $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ are two-sided vector spaces over the same division ring $\Delta$, their direct product $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$ inherits both a left structure via formula (9) and a right structure via formula (10). The crucial property is that left and right scalar multiplications defined in this way commute with each other, which follows from the fact that in a two-sided space, left multiplications are linear transformations in the right space (and vice versa). Thus the category of two-sided vector spaces is closed under direct products, unlike the category of one-sided spaces.
