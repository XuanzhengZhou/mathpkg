This exercise asks the reader to verify that under the identification of the group of linear mappings $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{S})$ with the direct product $\mathfrak{R}' \times \mathfrak{S}$, the tensor-like product $A' \times B$ of a linear transformation on $\mathfrak{R}'$ and one on $\mathfrak{S}$ corresponds to the mapping $L \mapsto ALB$, where $A$ is the transpose of $A'$ on $\mathfrak{R}$. This establishes the relationship between the action of the direct product of transformations and the composition action on the space of linear mappings.
