The element $\gamma_{i\lambda}$ of the matrix of $AB$ is given by
$$\gamma_{i\lambda} = \sum_k \alpha_{ik}\beta_{k\lambda}$$
where $A \to (\alpha)$ and $B \to (\beta)$ under the correspondence. Since $(\alpha)$ and $(\beta)$ are row-finite, for each fixed $i$ only finitely many $\alpha_{ik}$ are non-zero, and for each such $k$ only finitely many $\beta_{k\lambda}$ are non-zero. Thus the sum is finite, and the product matrix $(\gamma) = (\alpha)(\beta)$ is again row-finite. It follows now that the set $\Delta_I$ of $I \times I$ row-finite matrices together with the indicated addition and multiplication is a ring isomorphic to the ring $\mathfrak{L}$.
