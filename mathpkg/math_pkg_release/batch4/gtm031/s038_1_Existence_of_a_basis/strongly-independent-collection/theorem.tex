A collection $F$ of sequences $(\gamma_1, \gamma_2, \cdots)$ with entries in a division ring $\Delta$ is \textit{strongly independent} if every finite square matrix chosen from the collection is non-singular. We choose a finite square matrix from $F$ by selecting first a finite set of sequences
$$(\gamma_1^{(1)}, \gamma_2^{(1)}, \cdots), (\gamma_1^{(2)}, \gamma_2^{(2)}, \cdots), \cdots, (\gamma_1^{(n)}, \gamma_2^{(n)}, \cdots)$$
and then a set $i_1, i_2, \cdots, i_n$ of indices, obtaining the matrix
$$\begin{bmatrix}
\gamma_{i_1}^{(1)} & \gamma_{i_2}^{(1)} & \cdots & \gamma_{i_n}^{(1)} \\
\cdot & \cdot & \cdots & \cdot \\
\cdot & \cdot & \cdots & \cdot \\
\gamma_{i_1}^{(n)} & \gamma_{i_2}^{(n)} & \cdots & \gamma_{i_n}^{(n)}
\end{bmatrix}$$
and requiring this matrix to be non-singular.
