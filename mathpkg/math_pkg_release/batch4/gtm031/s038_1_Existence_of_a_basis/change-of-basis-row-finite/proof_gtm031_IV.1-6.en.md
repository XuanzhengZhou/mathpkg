If we have a second basis for $\mathfrak{R}$, we can suppose that it has been put into 1-1 correspondence with the same set of indices $I$. Denote the vectors in this basis by $f_i$ and write
$$f_k = \sum_{\mu} \mu_{ki} e_i, \quad e_i = \sum_{\nu} \nu_{ik} f_k$$
where $(\mu)$ and $(\nu)$ are row-finite $I \times I$ matrices (by the definition of basis, each $f_k$ is a finite linear combination of the $e_i$, hence only finitely many $\mu_{ki}$ are non-zero for fixed $k$). It follows easily from substituting one expression into the other that $(\mu)(\nu) = 1 = (\nu)(\mu)$ where $1$ is the identity matrix with elements $\delta_{ik}$. Thus $(\mu)$ is a unit in the ring $\Delta_I$ of row-finite matrices.
