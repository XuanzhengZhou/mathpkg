To construct a vector space with a basis of arbitrary cardinality, one can use the function space model: take all finitely-supported functions from a set $I$ to the division ring $\Delta$. The "delta functions" $e_i(j) = \delta_{ij}$ serve as a standard basis, and the cardinality of the basis equals $|I|$. This construction shows that vector spaces of any dimension exist and that any vector space is equivalent to such a function space. In particular, it establishes that the dimension is a complete invariant: two vector spaces over the same division ring are isomorphic if and only if they have bases of the same cardinality.
