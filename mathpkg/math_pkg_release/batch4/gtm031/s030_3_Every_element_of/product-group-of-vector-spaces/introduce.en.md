A product group is a commutative group $\mathfrak{P}$ built from a right vector space $\mathfrak{R}'$ and a left vector space $\mathfrak{S}$ over the same division ring $\Delta$, together with a bilinear product operation $\times: \mathfrak{R}' \times \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{P}$. The product is distributive over addition in both arguments, homogeneous in the sense that scaling the right argument on the right equals scaling the left argument on the left, and every element of $\mathfrak{P}$ is expressible as a finite sum of pure products $x_i' \times y_i$. This construction generalizes the notion of a bilinear form and serves as the foundation for the direct product (tensor product) of vector spaces.
