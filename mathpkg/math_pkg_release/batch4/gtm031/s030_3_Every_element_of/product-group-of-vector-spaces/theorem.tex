Let $\mathfrak{R}'$ be a right vector space and $\mathfrak{S}$ a left vector space over the same division ring $\Delta$. Let $\mathfrak{P}$ be a commutative group (operation written as $+$) and suppose that, for each pair of vectors $x' \in \mathfrak{R}', y \in \mathfrak{S}$, there is associated a unique element $x' \times y \in \mathfrak{P}$. Then $\mathfrak{P}$ is a \textbf{product group} of $\mathfrak{R}'$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ relative to the product $\times$ if

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{1.}&\quad (x_1' + x_2') \times y = x_1' \times y + x_2' \times y,\\
&\quad x' \times (y_1 + y_2) = x' \times y_1 + x' \times y_2.\\[4pt]
\text{2.}&\quad x' \alpha \times y = x' \times \alpha y.\\[4pt]
\text{3.}&\quad \text{Every element of } \mathfrak{P} \text{ has the form } \sum_i x_i' \times y_i.
\end{aligned}
$$
