A product group $\mathfrak{P}$ of $\mathfrak{R}'$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ relative to $\times$ is a \textbf{direct product} of $\mathfrak{R}'$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ if the following uniqueness conditions hold:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{4. (a)}&\quad \sum_i x_i' \times y_i = 0 \text{ implies that either the } y_i \text{ are linearly dependent or that all } x_i' = 0;\\[4pt]
\text{(b)}&\quad \sum_i x_i' \times y_i = 0 \text{ implies that either the } x_i' \text{ are linearly dependent or that all } y_i = 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
