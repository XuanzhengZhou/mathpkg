Theorem 2 gives a clean algebraic characterization of non-degenerate bilinear forms. The conditions are both necessary and sufficient: the two spaces must have equal dimension, and there must exist a pair of bases with respect to which the matrix of the form is the identity matrix. Such bases naturally generalize the concept of dual bases for a vector space and its dual, and they provide a concrete computational handle for working with non-degenerate forms.
