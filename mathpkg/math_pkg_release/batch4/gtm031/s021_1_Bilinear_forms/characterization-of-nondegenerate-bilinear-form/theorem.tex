\textbf{Theorem 2.} Let $g(x, y')$ be a bilinear form connecting vector spaces $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$. Then $g$ is non-degenerate if and only if:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\dim \mathfrak{R} = \dim \mathfrak{R}' = n$ (the spaces have the same dimension);
\item There exist bases $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ of $\mathfrak{R}$ and $(e_1', \dots, e_n')$ of $\mathfrak{R}'$ such that
$$g(e_i, e_j') = \delta_{ij}, \quad i, j = 1, 2, \dots, n.$$
\end{enumerate}

The bases $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ and $(e_1', \dots, e_n')$ satisfying condition (2) are called \textbf{complementary bases} (or dual bases) relative to $g$.
