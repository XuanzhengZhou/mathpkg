The correspondence $A \mapsto A'$ is a 1-1 mapping of the set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ of linear transformations from $\mathfrak{R}_1$ into $\mathfrak{R}_2$ onto the set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_2', \mathfrak{R}_1')$ of linear transformations from $\mathfrak{R}_2'$ into $\mathfrak{R}_1'$.

Moreover, if $A'$ is the transpose of $A$, then $A$ is the transpose of $A'$ (relative to the same bilinear forms). In particular, the double transpose $A''$ defined via $L_1 A'^* L_2^{-1}$ (where $L_i$ are the equivalences of $\mathfrak{R}_i$ onto $\mathfrak{R}_i'^*$) satisfies

$$g_2(x_1 A'', y_2') = g_1(x_1, y_2' A')$$

for all $x_1 \in \mathfrak{R}_1, y_2' \in \mathfrak{R}_2'$, and the correspondences $A \to A'$ and $A' \to A''$ are inverses of each other.
