Once bases are chosen for the vector spaces $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$, every bilinear form $g(x, y')$ is completely determined by the $n \times n'$ matrix of values $g(e_i, f_j')$ on the basis vectors. Conversely, any matrix with entries in $\Delta$ defines a bilinear form via the formula $g(x, y') = \sum \xi_i \beta_{ij} \eta_j$. This establishes a one-to-one correspondence between bilinear forms on finite-dimensional spaces and matrices over $\Delta$, allowing matrix techniques to be applied to the study of bilinear forms.
