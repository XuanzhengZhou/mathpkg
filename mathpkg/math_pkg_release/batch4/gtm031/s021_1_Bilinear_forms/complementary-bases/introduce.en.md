Complementary bases are the generalization of dual bases from the special case $\mathfrak{R}' = \mathfrak{R}^*$ to an arbitrary non-degenerate bilinear pairing. They provide a coordinate system in which the bilinear form takes the simplest possible shape -- the identity matrix. The existence and essential uniqueness of complementary bases is guaranteed by Theorem 2 and the fact that the transition matrix is uniquely determined as the inverse of the matrix of the form.
