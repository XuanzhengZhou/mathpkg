The left and right radicals of a bilinear form measure the extent to which the form is degenerate. The left radical consists of all vectors in $\mathfrak{R}$ that are "orthogonal" (via the bilinear form) to every vector in $\mathfrak{R}'$, and similarly for the right radical. These radicals are precisely the kernels of the induced linear transformations $L$ and $R$, providing a direct link between the degeneracy of the bilinear form and the injectivity of the associated mappings.
