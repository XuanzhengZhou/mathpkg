\textbf{Theorem 1.} If $g(x, y')$ is a bilinear form connecting the vector spaces $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$, then there exist bases $(u_1, u_2, \dots, u_n)$ for $\mathfrak{R}$ and $(v_1', v_2', \dots, v_{n'}')$ for $\mathfrak{R}'$ such that

$$g(u_i, v_j') = \delta_{ij} \quad \text{for} \quad i, j = 1, 2, \dots, r,$$

$$g(u_i, v_j') = 0 \quad \text{if} \quad i > r \;\text{or}\; j > r.$$

The number $r$ is the rank (row or column) of the matrix $(\beta)$ of $g(x, y')$.
