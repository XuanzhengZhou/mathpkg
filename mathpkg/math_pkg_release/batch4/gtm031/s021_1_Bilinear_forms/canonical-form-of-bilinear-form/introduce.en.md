Theorem 1 establishes that every bilinear form can be brought to a simple canonical form by a suitable choice of bases in both vector spaces. The matrix of the bilinear form becomes a diagonal-like matrix with $r$ ones on the diagonal and zeros elsewhere, where $r$ is the rank of the form. This theorem is the bilinear form analogue of the result that any matrix can be reduced to the form $\begin{pmatrix} I_r & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ by elementary row and column operations, and it provides a purely algebraic definition of the rank of a bilinear form.
