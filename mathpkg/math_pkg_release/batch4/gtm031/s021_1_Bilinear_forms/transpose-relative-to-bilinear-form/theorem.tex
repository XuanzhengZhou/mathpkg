Let $\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2$ be left vector spaces over $\Delta$ and $\mathfrak{R}_1', \mathfrak{R}_2'$ be right vector spaces. Suppose $\mathfrak{R}_i$ is dual to $\mathfrak{R}_i'$ via a non-degenerate bilinear form $g_i(x_i, y_i')$ for $i = 1, 2$. Let $R_i: \mathfrak{R}_i' \to \mathfrak{R}_i^*$ be the corresponding isomorphisms.

For a linear transformation $A: \mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$, its \textbf{transpose} (relative to $g_1, g_2$) is the linear transformation $A': \mathfrak{R}_2' \to \mathfrak{R}_1'$ defined by

$$A' = R_2 \, A^* \, R_1^{-1}$$

where $A^*: \mathfrak{R}_2^* \to \mathfrak{R}_1^*$ is the usual transpose defined by $(fA^*)(x_1) = f(A x_1)$ for $f \in \mathfrak{R}_2^*$.
