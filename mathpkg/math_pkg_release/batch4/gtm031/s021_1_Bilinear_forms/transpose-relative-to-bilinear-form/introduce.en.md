The transpose of a linear transformation between spaces equipped with non-degenerate bilinear forms is defined by conjugating the usual transpose $A^*$ (between dual spaces) with the isomorphisms $R_i$ that identify each space with the dual of its paired space. This construction ensures that the transpose $A'$ maps between the paired spaces $\mathfrak{R}_2'$ and $\mathfrak{R}_1'$ in a way that is compatible with the bilinear forms, satisfying the fundamental adjoint relation $g_1(x_1, y_2' A') = g_2(x_1 A, y_2')$.
