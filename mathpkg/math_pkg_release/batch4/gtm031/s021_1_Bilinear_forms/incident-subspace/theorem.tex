Let $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$ be dual relative to a non-degenerate bilinear form $g(x, y')$. For a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ of $\mathfrak{R}$, the \textbf{incident subspace} $j(\mathfrak{S})$ is the subspace of $\mathfrak{R}'$ defined by

$$j(\mathfrak{S}) = \{ y' \in \mathfrak{R}' \mid g(x, y') = 0 \text{ for all } x \in \mathfrak{S} \}.$$

Similarly, for a subspace $\mathfrak{S}'$ of $\mathfrak{R}'$, its incident subspace $j(\mathfrak{S}')$ is the subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$ defined by

$$j(\mathfrak{S}') = \{ x \in \mathfrak{R} \mid g(x, y') = 0 \text{ for all } y' \in \mathfrak{S}' \}.$$

The mapping $j$ establishes a dimension-reversing, inclusion-reversing bijection between subspaces of $\mathfrak{R}$ and subspaces of $\mathfrak{R}'$, with $\dim j(\mathfrak{S}) = \dim \mathfrak{R} - \dim \mathfrak{S}$.
