The incident subspace generalizes the notion of the annihilator from the duality between a vector space and its algebraic dual to the setting of an arbitrary non-degenerate bilinear form. Just as the annihilator of a subspace $\mathfrak{S} \subseteq \mathfrak{R}$ consists of all linear functionals vanishing on $\mathfrak{S}$, the incident subspace $j(\mathfrak{S})$ consists of all vectors in $\mathfrak{R}'$ that pair trivially with every element of $\mathfrak{S}$ via the bilinear form. This concept is fundamental for studying orthogonality and duality in bilinear algebra.
