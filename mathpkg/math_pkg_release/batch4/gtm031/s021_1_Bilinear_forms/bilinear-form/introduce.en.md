A bilinear form is a generalization of the pairing $(x, f) \mapsto f(x)$ between a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ and its dual space $\mathfrak{R}^*$. It is a function of two variables, linear in the first argument (with respect to a left vector space structure) and linear in the second argument (with respect to a right vector space structure), taking values in the division ring $\Delta$. Bilinear forms provide the foundation for the study of duality between vector spaces, symmetric and skew-symmetric forms, and the geometry of quadratic forms.
