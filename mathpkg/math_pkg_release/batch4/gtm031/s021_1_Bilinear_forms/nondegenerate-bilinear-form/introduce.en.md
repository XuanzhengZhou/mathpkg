A bilinear form is non-degenerate if it pairs the two vector spaces together in a maximally non-trivial way: no non-zero vector in either space is orthogonal to the entire other space. This is the natural generalization of the non-degenerate pairing between a finite-dimensional vector space and its dual given by $f(x)$. When a bilinear form is non-degenerate, it establishes a perfect duality between the two spaces, making them isomorphic via the induced linear transformations $L$ and $R$.
