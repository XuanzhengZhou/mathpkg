A bilinear form on $\mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{R}'$ is equivalent to a linear transformation of the right vector space $\mathfrak{R}'$ into the conjugate space $\mathfrak{R}^*$ of $\mathfrak{R}$. Equivalently, a bilinear form is a linear transformation of $\mathfrak{R}$ into the conjugate space $(\mathfrak{R}')^*$ of $\mathfrak{R}'$.

Explicitly, given a linear transformation $R: \mathfrak{R}' \to \mathfrak{R}^*$, define $g(x, y') = (R(y'))(x)$. Then $g$ is a bilinear form. Conversely, given a bilinear form $g$, the mapping $R: y' \mapsto g(\cdot, y')$ is a linear transformation $\mathfrak{R}' \to \mathfrak{R}^*$.
