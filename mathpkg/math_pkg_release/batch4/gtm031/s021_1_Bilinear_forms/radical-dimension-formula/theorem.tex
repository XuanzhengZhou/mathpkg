Let $g(x, y')$ be a bilinear form on $\mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{R}'$ with rank $r$, and let $\mathfrak{N}_L$, $\mathfrak{N}_R$ denote the left and right radicals respectively. Let $L: \mathfrak{R} \to (\mathfrak{R}')^*$ and $R: \mathfrak{R}' \to \mathfrak{R}^*$ be the induced linear transformations. Then

$$\dim \operatorname{im} L + \dim \mathfrak{N}_L = \dim \mathfrak{R}, \quad \dim \operatorname{im} R + \dim \mathfrak{N}_R = \dim \mathfrak{R}'.$$

Moreover, $\dim \operatorname{im} L = r = \dim \operatorname{im} R$, so that

$$\dim \mathfrak{N}_L = \dim \mathfrak{R} - r, \quad \dim \mathfrak{N}_R = \dim \mathfrak{R}' - r.$$
