When the base field of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$ is extended to a larger field $P$, the original space embeds naturally into the extended space $\mathfrak{R}_P$ via the map $x \mapsto \bar{x} = 1 \times x$. This correspondence is an equivalence (a $\Phi$-linear isomorphism) between $\mathfrak{R}$ and the subspace $\bar{\mathfrak{R}} \subseteq \mathfrak{R}_P$. This embedding allows one to identify the original space with a subset of the extended space, replacing $\mathfrak{R}$ by $\bar{\mathfrak{R}}$ within $\mathfrak{R}_P$ if desired.
