If $P$ is a field which contains $\Phi$ as a subfield, then $P$ can be regarded as a vector space over $\Phi$. The addition in the vector space is taken to be the field addition, and scalar multiplication $\alpha \xi$ for $\alpha \in \Phi$ and $\xi \in P$ is defined to be the field product. The axioms of a vector space are immediate consequences of the associative and distributive laws and the fact that $\Phi$ contains the identity of $P$. The vector space $P$ over $\Phi$ may be infinite dimensional or finite dimensional.
