When extending the base field of a vector space from $\Phi$ to a larger field $P$, the dimension of the space is preserved. If $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ is a basis for the original space $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$, then the corresponding elements $\bar{e}_i = 1 \times e_i$ in the extended space $\mathfrak{R}_P$ form a basis over $P$. This fact is essential for comparing properties of linear transformations before and after field extension, as it ensures that the extended space has the same "size" as the original.
