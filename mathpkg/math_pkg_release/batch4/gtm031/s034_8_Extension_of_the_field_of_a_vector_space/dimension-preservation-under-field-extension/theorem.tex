Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over $\Phi$ and let $\mathfrak{R}_P = P \times \mathfrak{R}$ be its extension to the field $P \supseteq \Phi$. Then the dimensionality of $\mathfrak{R}_P$ over $P$ equals the dimensionality of $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$:

$$\dim_P(\mathfrak{R}_P) = \dim_\Phi(\mathfrak{R}).$$

More precisely, if $(e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$ and $\bar{e}_i = 1 \times e_i$, then $(\bar{e}_1, \bar{e}_2, \dots, \bar{e}_n)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{R}_P$ over $P$.
