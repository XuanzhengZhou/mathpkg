Theorem 5 addresses a fundamental question in linear algebra over field extensions: if two sets of matrices with entries in a base field $\Phi$ become similar after extending to a larger field $P$, can the similarity be realized by a matrix already having entries in $\Phi$? The theorem answers affirmatively under the assumption that $\Phi$ is infinite. The proof uses a linear algebra argument: the similarity condition translates to a system of linear equations with coefficients in $\Phi$, and by comparing ranks and solution spaces over $\Phi$ and $P$, one shows that a solution with entries in $\Phi$ exists. This result is important in representation theory and algebraic groups, where one often extends scalars to an algebraically closed field for convenience and then needs to descend results back to the original field.
