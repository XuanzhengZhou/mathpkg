Let $\Phi$ be an infinite field and let $\omega$ be a set of $n \times n$ matrices with elements in $\Phi$. Let $P \supseteq \Phi$ be an extension field and let $\mathfrak{R}_P$ be the extension of the vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$ to $P$. Suppose there exists a basis $(u_1, \dots, u_n)$ of $\mathfrak{R}_P$ over $P$ such that the matrices of the linear transformations in $\Omega$ (the set of extensions of the transformations corresponding to $\omega$) relative to this basis belong to $\Phi_n$. Then there exists a matrix $(\gamma) \in \mathrm{GL}(n, \Phi)$ such that for every $(\alpha) \in \omega$,

$$(\gamma)(\alpha)(\gamma)^{-1} = (\mu)(\alpha)(\mu)^{-1},$$

where $(\mu)$ is the change-of-basis matrix from the canonical basis $(\bar{e}_1, \dots, \bar{e}_n)$ to $(u_1, \dots, u_n)$.
