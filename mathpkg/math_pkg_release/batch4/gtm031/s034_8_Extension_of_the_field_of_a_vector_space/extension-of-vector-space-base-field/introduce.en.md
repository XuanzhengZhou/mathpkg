Given a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a field $\Phi$ and an extension field $P \supseteq \Phi$, one can construct a new vector space $\mathfrak{R}_P$ over the larger field $P$ by forming the Kronecker product $P \times \mathfrak{R}$. The scalar multiplication by $P$ is defined naturally on the product, making $\mathfrak{R}_P$ into a vector space over $P$. This process of extending the base field is a standard technique in linear algebra and representation theory, allowing results proved over algebraically closed or sufficiently large fields to be transferred back to the original setting.
