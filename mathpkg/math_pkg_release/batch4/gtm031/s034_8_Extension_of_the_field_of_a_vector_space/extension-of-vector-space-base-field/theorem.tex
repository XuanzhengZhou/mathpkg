Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over $\Phi$ and let $\mathfrak{P} = P \times \mathfrak{R}$ be the Kronecker product of $P$ regarded as a vector space over $\Phi$ with $\mathfrak{R}$. Then $\mathfrak{P}$ can be regarded as a vector space over $P$, where scalar multiplication by elements of $P$ is defined on the Kronecker product. We denote this space as $\mathfrak{R}_P$ and call it the space obtained from $\mathfrak{R}$ by extending the base field $\Phi$ to the field $P$.
