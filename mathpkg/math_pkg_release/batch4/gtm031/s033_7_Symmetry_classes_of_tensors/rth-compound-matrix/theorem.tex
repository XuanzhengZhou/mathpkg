Let $(\alpha)$ be the matrix of a linear transformation $A$ relative to a basis $(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ of $\Re$. The matrix relative to the lexicographically ordered basis $[e_{i_1} e_{i_2} \cdots e_{i_r}]$ (with $i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_r$) of the linear transformation induced by $A$ in the space of skew-symmetric tensors of rank $r$ is called the \textit{$r$th compound} of $(\alpha)$, denoted $C_r(\alpha)$.
