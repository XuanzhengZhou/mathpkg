Exercise 3 from Section 7. Prove the Sylvester--Franke theorem: the determinant of the $r$th compound of an $n \times n$ matrix $\alpha$ equals $(\det \alpha)^{\binom{n-1}{r-1}}$. This is a classical result connecting compound matrices with determinants and binomial coefficients, and can be proved by considering the action on the top exterior power of the space of skew-symmetric tensors.
