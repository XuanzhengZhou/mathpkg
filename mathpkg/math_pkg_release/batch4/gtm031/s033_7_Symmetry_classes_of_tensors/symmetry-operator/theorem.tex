For a permutation $\sigma \in \mathfrak{S}_r$ sending $i \mapsto i'$, the associated linear transformation $P(\sigma)$ on the space $\Re_0^r$ of contravariant tensors of rank $r$ is defined by
$$\Sigma x_1 \times x_2 \times \cdots \times x_r \mapsto \Sigma x_{1'} \times x_{2'} \times \cdots \times x_{r'}.$$
This mapping is a $(1\text{-}1)$ linear transformation in $\Re_0^r$, called a \textit{symmetry}. We set $P(1) = 1$ where $1$ denotes the identity permutation.
