A tensor $\Sigma x_1 \times x_2 \times \cdots \times x_r \in \Re_0^r$ is \textit{skew symmetric} (or \textit{alternating}) if for any permutation $\sigma: i \mapsto i'$,
$$\Sigma x_{1'} \times x_{2'} \times \cdots \times x_{r'} = \epsilon_\sigma \Sigma x_1 \times x_2 \times \cdots \times x_r,$$
where $\epsilon_\sigma = 1$ if $\sigma$ is even and $\epsilon_\sigma = -1$ if $\sigma$ is odd.
Equivalently, $P(\sigma)x = \epsilon_\sigma x$ for all $\sigma \in \mathfrak{S}_r$.
