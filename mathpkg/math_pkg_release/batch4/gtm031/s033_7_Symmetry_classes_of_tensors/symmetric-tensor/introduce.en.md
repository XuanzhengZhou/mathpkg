A symmetric tensor is a contravariant tensor of rank $r$ that remains unchanged when its factors are permuted in any way. This is the natural generalization of symmetry from bilinear forms to multilinear tensors. Symmetric tensors form a subspace of $\Re_0^r$ determined by the symmetry operators $P(\sigma) - 1$.
