In an $n$-dimensional vector space, any $r$-tuple of indices $(i_1, \ldots, i_r)$ with $r > n$ must contain at least one pair of equal indices by the pigeonhole principle. Since any component with repeated indices is zero for a skew-symmetric tensor, all components vanish, and the tensor itself is zero. This explains why the exterior power $\bigwedge^r V$ is non-trivial only for $0 \leq r \leq \dim V$.
