If $r > n$, then by the pigeonhole principle, any $r$-tuple $(i_1, i_2, \ldots, i_r)$ with each $i_j \in \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}$ must have at least two indices equal. By the corollary that $\xi^{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r} = 0$ whenever two indices are equal, every component $\xi^{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r}$ is zero. Hence the tensor is identically zero.
