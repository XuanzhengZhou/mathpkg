For a skew-symmetric tensor $\Sigma \xi^{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r} e_{i_1} \times \cdots \times e_{i_r}$, terms with repeated indices have zero coefficients and can be dropped. Moreover, for indices with $i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_r$, all permutations contribute to the same basis element, and the coefficient is $r! \xi^{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r}$. Hence the elements
$$[e_{i_1} e_{i_2} \cdots e_{i_r}] = \sum_{\sigma \in \mathfrak{S}_r} \epsilon_{\sigma} e_{i_{1'}} \times e_{i_{2'}} \times \cdots \times e_{i_{r'}}$$
with $i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_r$ form a basis for the space of skew-symmetric tensors of rank $r$.
