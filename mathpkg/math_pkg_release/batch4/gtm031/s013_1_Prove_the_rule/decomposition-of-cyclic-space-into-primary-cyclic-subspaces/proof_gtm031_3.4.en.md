*Proof.* Let $\Re = \{e\}$ be cyclic with cyclic generator $e$, and let $\mu(\lambda) = \mu_1(\lambda)\mu_2(\lambda) \cdots \mu_s(\lambda)$ where the $\mu_i(\lambda)$ are pairwise coprime. For each $i$, define

$$\nu_i(\lambda) = \frac{\mu(\lambda)}{\mu_i(\lambda)} = \prod_{j \neq i} \mu_j(\lambda).$$

Set $f_i = e \nu_i(A)$. Then $f_i \mu_i(A) = e \nu_i(A)\mu_i(A) = e \mu(A) = 0$, so the order of $f_i$ divides $\mu_i(\lambda)$. In fact, the order of $f_i$ is exactly $\mu_i(\lambda)$ (otherwise a smaller degree polynomial would annihilate $e$, contradicting that $\mu(\lambda)$ is the minimum polynomial).

Now consider the sum $\{f_1\} + \{f_2\} + \cdots + \{f_s\}$. We claim this sum is direct and equals $\Re$. The orders $\mu_i(\lambda)$ are pairwise coprime, so by the lemma on sums of vectors with independent cyclic subspaces, the order of $f_1 + f_2 + \cdots + f_s$ is $\prod \mu_i(\lambda) = \mu(\lambda)$. Since the dimension of the cyclic subspace generated by this sum is $\deg \mu(\lambda) = n = \dim \Re$, it must be the whole space. Moreover, since the dimensions of the $\{f_i\}$ sum to $n$, the sum is direct.

To obtain the actual decomposition, we construct $e_i$ from $f_i$. Since the $\nu_i(\lambda)$ are coprime in aggregate (their greatest common divisor is $1$), there exist polynomials $\psi_i(\lambda)$ such that $\sum \psi_i(\lambda)\nu_i(\lambda) = 1$. Setting $e_i = e \psi_i(A)\nu_i(A)$, one verifies that $e_i \in \{f_i\}$ and that the $e_i$ decompose the cyclic generator: $e = \sum e_i$. The order of $e_i$ is $\mu_i(\lambda)$, and $\Re = \{e_1\} \oplus \{e_2\} \oplus \cdots \oplus \{e_s\}$ as required. $\square$
