Theorem 3 is the primary decomposition theorem for cyclic linear transformations. It states that if the minimum polynomial factors into pairwise coprime factors, the cyclic space decomposes into a direct sum of cyclic subspaces whose orders are precisely those coprime factors. This theorem is the key step toward the rational canonical form and the classical canonical form: it reduces the study of a cyclic transformation to the case where the minimum polynomial is a power of an irreducible polynomial.
