\textbf{Theorem 3.} \text{Let $\Re = \{e\}$ be a cyclic space relative to $A$ and let the minimum polynomial factor as $\mu(\lambda) = \mu_1(\lambda) \cdots \mu_s(\lambda)$ where the $\mu_i(\lambda)$ are relatively prime in pairs. Then}

$$\Re = \{e_1\} \oplus \{e_2\} \oplus \cdots \oplus \{e_s\}$$

\text{where $\mu_{e_i}(\lambda) = \mu_i(\lambda)$ for each $i$. That is, $\Re$ decomposes into a direct sum of cyclic subspaces whose orders are the pairwise coprime factors of $\mu(\lambda)$.}
