This proposition shows that the minimum polynomial of a linear transformation $A$ can be computed as the least common multiple of the orders of any set of generators. In particular, taking a basis $(e_1,\dots,e_n)$, the minimum polynomial is the lcm of the orders of the basis vectors. This generalizes the earlier result for the cyclic case and provides a computational method: find the orders of a convenient basis and take their least common multiple.
