Let $(e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n)$ be any basis for $\Re$ over $\Phi$, and let $\mu_{e_i}(\lambda)$ be the order of $e_i$ relative to $A$. Then the minimum polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ of $A$ is the least common multiple of the orders $\mu_{e_i}(\lambda)$:

$$\mu(\lambda) = [\mu_{e_1}(\lambda), \mu_{e_2}(\lambda), \dots, \mu_{e_n}(\lambda)].$$

More generally, if $\{e_1, \dots, e_r\}$ is any set of generators for $\Re$ relative to $A$ (i.e., every $x \in \Re$ can be written as $x = \sum e_i \phi_i(A)$), then $\mu(\lambda) = [\mu_{e_1}(\lambda), \dots, \mu_{e_r}(\lambda)]$.
