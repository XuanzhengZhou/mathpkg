The companion matrix of a monic polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ is an $n \times n$ matrix whose characteristic polynomial and minimum polynomial both equal $\mu(\lambda)$. It has $1$'s on the superdiagonal and the coefficients of $\mu(\lambda)$ (with signs reversed) in the last row. Companion matrices are the fundamental building blocks of rational canonical forms: every linear transformation can be represented by a block diagonal matrix whose blocks are companion matrices of the invariant factors.
