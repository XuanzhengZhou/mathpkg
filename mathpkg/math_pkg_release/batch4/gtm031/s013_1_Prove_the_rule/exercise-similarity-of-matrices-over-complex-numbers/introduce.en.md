This exercise applies the result of Exercise 2 to the specific case of the $n$-th roots of unity. Since the roots are distinct, the diagonal matrix of roots of unity is cyclic with minimum polynomial $\lambda^n - 1$. The companion matrix of $\lambda^n - 1$ is also cyclic with the same minimum polynomial and dimension. Two cyclic transformations with the same minimum polynomial and dimension are similar, giving the desired conclusion.
