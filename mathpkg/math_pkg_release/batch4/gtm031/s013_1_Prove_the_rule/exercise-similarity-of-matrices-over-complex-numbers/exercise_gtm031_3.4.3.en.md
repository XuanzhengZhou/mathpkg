Use Exercise 2 to prove that, if $\mathbb{C}$ is the field of complex numbers and $\zeta_1, \zeta_2, \dots, \zeta_n$ are the $n$ distinct $n$-th roots of unity, then the diagonal matrix $\operatorname{diag}\{\zeta_1, \zeta_2, \dots, \zeta_n\}$ and the companion matrix of $\lambda^n - 1$ are similar in $\mathbb{C}_n$.
