A cyclic subspace is the smallest invariant subspace generated by a single vector $u$ under the action of a linear transformation $A$. It consists of all linear combinations of $u, uA, uA^2, \dots$ and its dimension equals the degree of the order of $u$. Cyclic subspaces are the atomic building blocks for decomposing vector spaces under the action of a linear transformation, leading to rational and Jordan canonical forms.
