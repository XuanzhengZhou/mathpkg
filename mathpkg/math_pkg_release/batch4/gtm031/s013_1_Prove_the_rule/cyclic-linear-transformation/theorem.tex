A linear transformation $A$ on a finite-dimensional vector space $\Re$ is called \text{cyclic} (sometimes \text{non-derogatory}) if there exists a single vector $e \in \Re$ such that the cyclic subspace generated by $e$ is the whole space:

$$\Re = \{e\} = [e, eA, eA^2, \dots, eA^{n-1}],$$

where $n = \dim \Re$. In this case, $(e, eA, \dots, eA^{n-1})$ is a basis for $\Re$, and the minimum polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ of $A$ has degree $n$.
