A linear transformation is cyclic if the entire vector space is generated as a cyclic subspace by a single vector. For such transformations, the minimum polynomial has maximal possible degree (equal to the dimension), and the structure of $A$ is completely captured by its minimum polynomial. Cyclic transformations are the simplest type: their matrix relative to the cyclic basis is the companion matrix of the minimum polynomial.
