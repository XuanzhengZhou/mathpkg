Let $A$ be a linear transformation in a finite-dimensional vector space $\Re$ over a field $\Phi$. The powers $A^k$ of $A$ belong to the ring $\mathfrak{A}$ of linear transformations on $\Re$. Since $\mathfrak{A}$ is $n^2$-dimensional over $\Phi$, the $n^2+1$ elements $A^0=1, A, A^2, \dots, A^{n^2}$ are linearly dependent. Hence there exists a non-zero polynomial $\mu(\lambda) \in \Phi[\lambda]$ of least degree with leading coefficient $1$ such that $\mu(A)=0$. This polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ is called the \text{minimum polynomial} of $A$. It satisfies

$$\mu(A) = A^m - \mu_{m-1}A^{m-1} - \cdots - \mu_0 1 = 0.$$

If $(\alpha)$ is the matrix of $A$ relative to a basis, then the minimum polynomial of $A$ is also called the minimum polynomial of the matrix $(\alpha)$. The polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ is the polynomial of least degree that has $(\alpha)$ as a root.
