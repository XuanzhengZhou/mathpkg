The minimum polynomial of a linear transformation $A$ on a finite-dimensional vector space is the monic polynomial $\mu(\lambda) \in \Phi[\lambda]$ of least degree such that $\mu(A) = 0$. It encodes the algebraic relations satisfied by $A$ and governs the structure of invariant subspaces. The degree of $\mu(\lambda)$ is at most the dimension of the space, and the minimum polynomial is the least common multiple of the orders of any set of generators of the space relative to $A$.
