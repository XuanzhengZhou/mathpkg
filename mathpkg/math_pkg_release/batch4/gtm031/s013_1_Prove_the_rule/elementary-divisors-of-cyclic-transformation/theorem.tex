Let $A$ be a cyclic linear transformation with minimum polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$. Factor $\mu(\lambda)$ into powers of distinct monic irreducible polynomials:

$$\mu(\lambda) = \pi_1(\lambda)^{k_1} \pi_2(\lambda)^{k_2} \cdots \pi_s(\lambda)^{k_s}.$$

The prime power factors $\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$ are called the \text{elementary divisors} of the cyclic linear transformation $A$. Each elementary divisor $\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$ corresponds to a diagonal block $B_i$ in the classical canonical matrix of $A$. The set of elementary divisors (together with their multiplicities) completely determines $A$ up to similarity.
