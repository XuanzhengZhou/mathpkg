The classical canonical matrix is the finest block diagonal matrix representation of a cyclic linear transformation. When the minimum polynomial is factored into powers of distinct irreducible polynomials (the elementary divisors), each elementary divisor $\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$ contributes a block $B_i$ that is built from companion matrices of $\pi_i(\lambda)$ arranged with connecting $N$ blocks. This form directly exhibits all the elementary divisors and is the precursor to the general rational canonical form for arbitrary (non-cyclic) transformations.
