Theorem 4 is a cornerstone of module theory over principal ideal domains. It states that submodules of finitely generated free modules are themselves free, with rank bounded by the rank of the ambient module. This generalizes the fact that subgroups of free abelian groups are free abelian. The theorem is proved by induction on the rank and uses the property that every ideal in a PID is principal. It underpins the structure theorem for finitely generated modules over PIDs and is essential for the development of canonical forms (Smith normal form, rational canonical form, Jordan canonical form) via the module-theoretic approach.
