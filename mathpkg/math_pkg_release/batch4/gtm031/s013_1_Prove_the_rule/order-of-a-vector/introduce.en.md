The order of a vector $u$ with respect to a linear transformation $A$ is the monic polynomial $\mu_u(\lambda)$ of least degree such that $u\mu_u(A) = 0$. It generalizes the notion of the minimum polynomial to individual vectors: while the minimum polynomial annihilates the entire transformation, the order annihilates only the given vector. The ideal of all polynomials sending $u$ to zero is principal, generated by $\mu_u(\lambda)$, and the order equals the minimum polynomial of $A$ restricted to the cyclic subspace $\{u\}$.
