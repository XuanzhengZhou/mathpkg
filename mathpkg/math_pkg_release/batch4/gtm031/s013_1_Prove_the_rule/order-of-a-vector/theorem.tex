Let $A$ be a linear transformation on $\Re$ and $u \in \Re$, $u \neq 0$. Let $uA^r$ ($r \geq 1$) be the first vector in the sequence $u, uA, uA^2, \dots$ that is linearly dependent on the preceding ones. Then there exists a relation of the form

$$uA^r = \nu_0 u + \nu_1 uA + \cdots + \nu_{r-1} uA^{r-1},$$

or equivalently, $u\mu_u(A) = 0$ where

$$\mu_u(\lambda) = \lambda^r - \nu_{r-1}\lambda^{r-1} - \cdots - \nu_0.$$

The polynomial $\mu_u(\lambda)$ is called the \text{order} of the vector $u$. It is the non-zero polynomial of least degree with leading coefficient $1$ having the property $u\mu_u(A) = 0$. The totality $\mathfrak{J}_u$ of polynomials $\nu(\lambda)$ such that $u\nu(A) = 0$ is the principal ideal $(\mu_u(\lambda))$ in $\Phi[\lambda]$. Moreover, $\mu_u(\lambda)$ is the minimum polynomial of the linear transformation induced by $A$ in the cyclic subspace $\{u\}$.
