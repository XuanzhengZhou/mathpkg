Let $\pi(\lambda)$ be an irreducible factor of the minimum polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ of $A \in \Omega$, and consider the space

$$\mathfrak{S} = \{ y \in \Re \mid y\pi(A) = 0 \}.$$

Since $\pi(\lambda)$ divides $\mu(\lambda)$ and $\mu(A) = 0$, the space $\mathfrak{S}$ is non-zero. For any $B \in \Omega$, since $B$ commutes with $A$, it also commutes with $\pi(A)$. Hence for $y \in \mathfrak{S}$ we have

$$(yB)\pi(A) = y\pi(A)B = 0,$$

so $yB \in \mathfrak{S}$. Thus $\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant relative to the whole set $\Omega$. Since $\Re$ is assumed irreducible relative to $\Omega$ and $\mathfrak{S} \neq 0$, we must have $\mathfrak{S} = \Re$. Therefore $\pi(A) = 0$, which means $\pi(\lambda) = \mu(\lambda)$, i.e., the minimum polynomial is irreducible.
