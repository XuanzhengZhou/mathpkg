Theorem 4 characterizes when a single linear transformation $A$ over a field $\Phi$ is indecomposable: exactly when $A$ is cyclic and its minimum polynomial is a power of an irreducible polynomial. The proof uses the theory of cyclic subspaces: any non-zero vector generates an invariant cyclic subspace, and if $A$ is indecomposable the whole space must be cyclic with a primary minimum polynomial. Conversely, a cyclic transformation with minimum polynomial $\pi(\lambda)^k$ (with $\pi$ irreducible) cannot admit a proper invariant direct sum decomposition.
