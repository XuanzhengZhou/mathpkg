The Jordan-Hölder theorem for sets of linear transformations states that any two composition series (maximal chains of $\Omega$-invariant subspaces) have the same length, and their composition factors are unique up to $\Omega$-isomorphism and permutation. This is the analogue for $\Omega$-modules of the classical Jordan-Hölder theorem from group theory. The result is fundamental because it shows that the irreducible constituents of a representation (the simple quotients in any composition series) are uniquely determined by the set $\Omega$, independent of the choice of series.
