A proper decomposition of a vector space relative to a set $\Omega$ of linear transformations is a direct sum decomposition into at least two non-zero invariant subspaces. This notion captures when the action of $\Omega$ can be split nontrivially across a direct sum. If no such proper decomposition exists, the space is called indecomposable relative to $\Omega$. Decomposability is a stronger condition than reducibility: every irreducible set is indecomposable, but there exist reducible sets that are indecomposable.
