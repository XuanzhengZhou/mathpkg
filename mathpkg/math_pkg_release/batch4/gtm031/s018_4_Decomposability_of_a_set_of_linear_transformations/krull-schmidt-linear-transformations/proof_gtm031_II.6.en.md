The proof follows the standard Krull-Schmidt argument adapted to $\Omega$-modules. Given two decompositions $\mathfrak{R} = \bigoplus_{i=1}^s \mathfrak{R}_i = \bigoplus_{j=1}^t \mathfrak{S}_j$ into indecomposable $\Omega$-invariant subspaces, one considers the projection endomorphisms associated with each decomposition. Using the fact that the endomorphism ring of an indecomposable $\Omega$-module is local (a consequence of Fitting's lemma for $\Omega$-linear transformations), one demonstrates that the identity map on $\mathfrak{R}$ can be factored through the projections, yielding a 1-1 correspondence between the sets of indecomposable summands. The $\Omega$-isomorphism of corresponding summands follows from the exchange property. The proof is identical in structure to the classical Krull-Schmidt theorem for modules of finite length, translated to the language of $\Omega$-invariant subspaces.
