The Krull-Schmidt theorem for sets of linear transformations asserts the uniqueness (up to $\Omega$-isomorphism and permutation) of a direct sum decomposition into indecomposable $\Omega$-invariant subspaces. When combined with the Jordan-Hölder theorem, it provides a complete structural picture: any $\Omega$-module can be decomposed into a direct sum of indecomposables, and although such a decomposition is not unique, the indecomposable summands are determined up to $\Omega$-isomorphism. This is the module-theoretic analogue of the classical Krull-Schmidt theorem.
