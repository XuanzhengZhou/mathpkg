Theorem 2 provides the fundamental characterization of complete reducibility: a set $\Omega$ of linear transformations is completely reducible precisely when the space decomposes as a direct sum of $\Omega$-irreducible invariant subspaces. This reduces the apparently infinite family of complementation conditions (one for each invariant subspace) to a single structural decomposition condition. The sufficiency proof proceeds by showing that any invariant subspace either equals the whole space or can be augmented by an irreducible summand to obtain a complement.
