Two invariant subspaces $\mathfrak{R}_1$ and $\mathfrak{R}_2$ relative to a set $\Omega$ of linear transformations are called \text{$\Omega$-isomorphic} (or \text{equivalent}) if there exists a 1-1 $\Omega$-linear transformation $\theta$ of $\mathfrak{R}_1$ onto $\mathfrak{R}_2$, i.e., a vector space isomorphism such that $(xA)\theta = (x\theta)A$ for all $x \in \mathfrak{R}_1$ and $A \in \Omega$.

If $(e_1, e_2, \cdots, e_r)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{R}_1$, then $(e_1\theta, e_2\theta, \cdots, e_r\theta)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{R}_2$, and every $A \in \Omega$ has the same matrix relative to $(e_1, \cdots, e_r)$ and to $(e_1\theta, \cdots, e_r\theta)$. Consequently, for arbitrary choice of bases in $\mathfrak{R}_1$ and $\mathfrak{R}_2$, the matrices $(\alpha_1)$ and $(\alpha_2)$ of any $A \in \Omega$ are related by $(\alpha_2) = (\mu)(\alpha_1)(\mu)^{-1}$ where the non-singular matrix $(\mu)$ is independent of $A$.
