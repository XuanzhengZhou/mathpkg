The standard model of a finite dimensional vector space is the space $\Delta^{(n)}$ of $n$-tuples with entries in a division ring $\Delta$. Addition is component-wise and scalar multiplication is performed component-wise on the left. This construction directly generalizes the familiar coordinate representation of vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$. It serves as the prototype for all finite dimensional vector spaces: as Jacobson shows, any abstract finite dimensional vector space over $\Delta$ is isomorphic to $\Delta^{(n)}$ for some $n$, making this the universal concrete model.
