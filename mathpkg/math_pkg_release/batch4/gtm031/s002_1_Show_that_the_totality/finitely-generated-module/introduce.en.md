A module $\mathfrak{R}$ is finitely generated if there exists a finite subset $\{e_1, \ldots, e_n\}$ such that every element of $\mathfrak{R}$ is a linear combination of these elements with coefficients from the ring. This generalizes the notion of a finite-dimensional vector space to modules over arbitrary rings.
