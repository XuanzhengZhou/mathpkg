Lemma 2 (the Replacement Lemma) states that replacing a vector by the sum of itself and a scalar multiple of another vector preserves linear independence. This is a key technical tool used in the proof of Theorem 2 (the basis cardinality bound), as it allows systematic elimination of basis coordinates to reduce the dimensionality in an induction argument.
