Theorem 3 establishes that the number of elements in a basis of a finite-dimensional vector space is an invariant---every basis has exactly the same number of vectors. This number is the dimension of the space. The proof follows directly from Theorem 2: if two bases have $n$ and $m$ elements respectively, then $m \leq n$ and $n \leq m$, hence $m = n$. Equivalent vector spaces have the same dimensionality.
