Theorem 2 is the crucial stepping stone toward proving that all bases of a finite-dimensional vector space have the same number of elements. It states that in a space with an $n$-element basis, you cannot have more than $n$ linearly independent vectors. The proof proceeds by induction on $n$, using the Replacement Lemma (Lemma 2) to eliminate one basis vector at a time.
