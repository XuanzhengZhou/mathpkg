A \textbf{(left) $\mathfrak{o}$-module} is a system consisting of a commutative group $\mathfrak{R}$, a ring $\mathfrak{o}$ with an identity, and a function of pairs $(\rho, x)$, $\rho \in \mathfrak{o}$, $x \in \mathfrak{R}$, with values $\rho x \in \mathfrak{R}$ satisfying:

$$
\text{S}1 \quad \alpha(x + y) = \alpha x + \alpha y,
$$
$$
\text{S}2 \quad (\alpha + \beta)x = \alpha x + \beta x,
$$
$$
\text{S}3 \quad (\alpha\beta)x = \alpha(\beta x),
$$
$$
\text{S}4 \quad 1x = x.
$$

A vector space is a $\Delta$-module where $\Delta$ is a division ring.
