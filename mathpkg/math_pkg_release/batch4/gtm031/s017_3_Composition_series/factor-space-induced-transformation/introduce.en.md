When a linear transformation $A$ preserves an invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, it descends naturally to a linear transformation $\bar{A}$ on the quotient space $\bar{\mathfrak{R}} = \mathfrak{R}/\mathfrak{S}$, defined by $\bar{x}\bar{A} = \overline{xA}$. This induced transformation is well-defined precisely because $\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant. In the context of composition series, the induced transformations on successive factor spaces $\mathfrak{S}_i/\mathfrak{S}_{i-1}$ are the fundamental objects whose irreducibility determines whether the filtration is maximal.
