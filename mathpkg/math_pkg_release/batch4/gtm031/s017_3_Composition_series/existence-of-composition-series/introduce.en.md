For any finite-dimensional vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ and any set $\Omega$ of linear transformations, a composition series always exists. The proof proceeds by induction on the dimension: if $\mathfrak{R}$ is already irreducible, the chain $0 \subset \mathfrak{R}$ is a composition series. Otherwise, take a proper invariant subspace, refine it recursively, and use the quotient correspondence to lift irreducibility from the factor spaces. This ensures that every linear representation admits a finest invariant filtration.
