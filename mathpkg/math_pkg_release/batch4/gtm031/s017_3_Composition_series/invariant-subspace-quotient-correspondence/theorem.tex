Let $\Omega$ be a set of linear transformations on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$, and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be an invariant subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$ relative to $\Omega$. Denote by $\bar{\mathfrak{R}} = \mathfrak{R}/\mathfrak{S}$ the quotient space and by $\bar{\Omega}$ the set of induced linear transformations $\bar{A}$ on $\bar{\mathfrak{R}}$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item If $\mathfrak{U}$ is an invariant subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$ relative to $\Omega$ such that $\mathfrak{R} \supseteq \mathfrak{U} \supseteq \mathfrak{S}$, then $\bar{\mathfrak{U}} = \mathfrak{U}/\mathfrak{S}$ is an invariant subspace of $\bar{\mathfrak{R}}$ relative to $\bar{\Omega}$.

\item Conversely, every invariant subspace of $\bar{\mathfrak{R}}$ relative to $\bar{\Omega}$ has the form $\bar{\mathfrak{U}} = \mathfrak{U}/\mathfrak{S}$, where $\mathfrak{U}$ is an invariant subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$ relative to $\Omega$ containing $\mathfrak{S}$.
\end{enumerate}
