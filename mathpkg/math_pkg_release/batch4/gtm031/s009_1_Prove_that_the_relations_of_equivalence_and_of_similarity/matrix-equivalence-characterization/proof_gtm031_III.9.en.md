If $(\alpha)$ and $(\tilde{\alpha})$ are equivalent matrices, then these can be taken to be matrices of the same linear transformation $A$. The row ranks of $(\alpha)$ and of $(\tilde{\alpha})$ coincide with the rank of $A$. Hence equivalent matrices have the same row rank.

Conversely, if $(\alpha)$ and $(\tilde{\alpha})$ have the same row rank $\rho$, then by Theorem 5 both of these matrices are equivalent to the same canonical matrix (18) (the matrix with $\rho$ ones on the diagonal and zeros elsewhere). Hence they are equivalent to each other, since equivalence is an equivalence relation.
