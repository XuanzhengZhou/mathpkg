Theorem 6 provides a complete characterization of matrix equivalence over a division ring: two matrices of the same dimensions are equivalent precisely when they share the same row rank. The forward direction follows from the fact that equivalent matrices represent the same linear transformation. The converse uses Theorem 5: both matrices, having the same row rank, are equivalent to the same canonical form and hence to each other.
