The rank-nullity theorem is a fundamental result in linear algebra relating the dimensions of the kernel (null space) and image (rank space) of a linear transformation. It states that for a linear transformation $A$ from an $n_1$-dimensional space, the sum of the rank and the nullity equals $n_1$. The proof constructs a basis by extending a basis of the null space, and shows the images of the extending vectors form a basis for the rank space.
