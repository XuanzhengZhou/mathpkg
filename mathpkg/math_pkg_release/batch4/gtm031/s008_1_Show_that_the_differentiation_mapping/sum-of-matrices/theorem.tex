If $A \mapsto (\alpha) = (\alpha_{ij})$ and $B \mapsto (\beta) = (\beta_{ij})$ under the correspondence between linear transformations and matrices, then $A + B \mapsto (\alpha) + (\beta)$, where the $(i,j)$-entry of $(\alpha) + (\beta)$ is $\alpha_{ij} + \beta_{ij}$.

Thus the sum of two $n_1 \times n_2$ matrices $(\alpha)$ and $(\beta)$ is defined as the matrix whose $(i,j)$-element is $\alpha_{ij} + \beta_{ij}$.
