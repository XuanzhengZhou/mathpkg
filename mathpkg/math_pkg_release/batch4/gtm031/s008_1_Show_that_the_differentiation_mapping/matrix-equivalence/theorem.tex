Two $n_1 \times n_2$ matrices $(\alpha)$ and $(\tilde{\alpha})$ over a division ring $\Delta$ are called \text{equivalent} (or associates) if there exist invertible matrices $(\mu) \in \mathrm{GL}(n_1, \Delta)$ and $(\nu) \in \mathrm{GL}(n_2, \Delta)$ such that
$$(\tilde{\alpha}) = (\mu)(\alpha)(\nu)^{-1}.$$
Two matrices are equivalent if and only if they represent the same linear transformation $A: \mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$ relative to (possibly different) bases in the two spaces. The matrix $(\mu)$ encodes the change of basis in $\mathfrak{R}_1$ and $(\nu)$ the change of basis in $\mathfrak{R}_2$.
