Two matrices are equivalent if they represent the same linear transformation with respect to different choices of bases in the domain and codomain. The equivalence relation is given by $(\tilde{\alpha}) = (\mu)(\alpha)(\nu)^{-1}$ where $(\mu)$ and $(\nu)$ are invertible matrices encoding the basis changes. Unlike similarity (which uses a single basis for both domain and codomain), equivalence applies to rectangular matrices and is the appropriate notion for linear transformations between different spaces. Jacobson notes that necessary and sufficient conditions for equivalence are easy to give (via rank), whereas similarity requires deeper analysis.
