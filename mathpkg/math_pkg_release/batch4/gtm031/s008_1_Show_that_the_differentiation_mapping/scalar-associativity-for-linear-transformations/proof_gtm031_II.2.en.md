For any $x \in \mathfrak{R}$,
$$x(\gamma(AB)) = \gamma\,x(AB) = \gamma((xA)B).$$

Since $\gamma$ is in the center $\Phi$ of $\Delta$, we have $\gamma((xA)B) = ((\gamma(xA)))B$ (scalar multiplication commutes through linear transformations for center elements).

Also,
$$x((\gamma A)B) = (x(\gamma A))B = (\gamma(xA))B = \gamma((xA)B).$$

And
$$x(A(\gamma B)) = (xA)(\gamma B) = \gamma((xA)B).$$

All three expressions equal $\gamma((xA)B)$, hence the equality holds.
