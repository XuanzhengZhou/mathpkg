Let $\mathfrak{R}_1$ and $\mathfrak{R}_2$ be $n_1$-dimensional and $n_2$-dimensional vector spaces over a field $\Phi$, respectively. Then the space $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ of all linear transformations from $\mathfrak{R}_1$ to $\mathfrak{R}_2$, with addition $x(A + B) = xA + xB$ and scalar multiplication $\gamma A = \gamma_l A$ for $\gamma \in \Phi$, is an $n_1 n_2$-dimensional vector space over $\Phi$.
