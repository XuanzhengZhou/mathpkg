The space of all linear transformations between finite-dimensional vector spaces is itself a finite-dimensional vector space. If the domain has dimension $n_1$ and the codomain has dimension $n_2$, then the space of linear transformations has dimension $n_1 n_2$. This follows from the 1-1 correspondence between linear transformations and $n_1 \times n_2$ matrices: fixing bases gives an isomorphism onto the matrix space, which is clearly $n_1 n_2$-dimensional. The result is fundamental for understanding the algebraic structure of operator spaces in linear algebra.
