Matrix multiplication is defined so that it corresponds to composition of linear transformations. The $(i,k)$-entry of the product matrix is the sum of products of entries from row $i$ of the first factor and column $k$ of the second. This definition makes the set of $n \times n$ matrices into a ring isomorphic to the ring of linear transformations of an $n$-dimensional vector space. The associative law for matrix multiplication follows from the associativity of function composition.
