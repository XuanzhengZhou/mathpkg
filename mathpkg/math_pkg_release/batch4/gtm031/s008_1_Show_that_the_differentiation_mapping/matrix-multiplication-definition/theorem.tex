Let $(\alpha)$ be an $n_1 \times n_2$ matrix and $(\beta)$ an $n_2 \times n_3$ matrix over a division ring $\Delta$. Their product $(\gamma) = (\alpha)(\beta)$ is the $n_1 \times n_3$ matrix defined by
$$\gamma_{ik} = \sum_{j=1}^{n_2} \alpha_{ij} \beta_{jk}.$$
If $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ has matrix $(\alpha)$ and $B \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_2, \mathfrak{R}_3)$ has matrix $(\beta)$ relative to chosen bases, then the composite $AB$ has matrix $(\alpha)(\beta)$.
