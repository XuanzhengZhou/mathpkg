Let $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ and $B \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_2, \mathfrak{R}_3)$ be linear transformations. The composition (or resultant) $AB$ is the mapping of $\mathfrak{R}_1$ into $\mathfrak{R}_3$ defined by

$$x(AB) = (xA)B$$

for all $x \in \mathfrak{R}_1$. That is, $AB$ is the resultant of $A$ followed by $B$.
