The sum of two linear transformations $A$ and $B$ from $\mathfrak{R}_1$ to $\mathfrak{R}_2$ is defined pointwise: the image of any vector $x$ under $A+B$ is the sum of the images $xA$ and $xB$ in $\mathfrak{R}_2$. This operation makes the set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ of all linear transformations into a commutative group. The sum is associative, commutative, and has the zero mapping as identity element.
