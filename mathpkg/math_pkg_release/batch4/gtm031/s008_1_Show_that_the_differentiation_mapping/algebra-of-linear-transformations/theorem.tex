A system $\mathfrak{A}$ over a field $\Phi$ is called an **algebra** (or **hyper-complex number system**) over $\Phi$ if:
1. $\mathfrak{A}$ is a ring,
2. $\mathfrak{A}$ is a vector space over $\Phi$,
3. The ring addition coincides with the vector space addition,
4. For all $\gamma \in \Phi$ and $A, B \in \mathfrak{A}$,
$$\gamma(AB) = (\gamma A)B = A(\gamma B).$$

The set $\mathfrak{L} = \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ with addition $+$, composition $\cdot$, and scalar multiplication by elements of $\Phi$ satisfies these conditions and is called the **algebra of linear transformations** in $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$.
