When the division ring $\Delta$ is non-commutative, only elements of its center $\Phi$ define scalar multiplication maps that are linear transformations. This is because $\gamma(\alpha x) = \alpha(\gamma x)$ holds for all $\alpha \in \Delta$ only when $\gamma$ commutes with every element of $\Delta$. This observation is key to viewing $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ as a vector space over $\Phi$.
