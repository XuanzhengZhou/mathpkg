The set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ of all linear transformations from a vector space to itself forms a ring under pointwise addition and composition. Addition makes it a commutative group, composition is associative and distributes over addition, and the identity mapping serves as the multiplicative identity. This ring of endomorphisms is the fundamental algebraic object for studying linear operators on a vector space.
