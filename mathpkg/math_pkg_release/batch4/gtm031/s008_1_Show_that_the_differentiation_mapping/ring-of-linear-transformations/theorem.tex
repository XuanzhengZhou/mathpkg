Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over a division ring $\Delta$. Then $\mathfrak{L} = \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ with addition $+$ and composition $\cdot$ is a ring. The identity mapping $x \to x$, denoted as $1$, is the multiplicative identity in $\mathfrak{L}$, satisfying $A1 = A = 1A$ for all $A \in \mathfrak{L}$.
