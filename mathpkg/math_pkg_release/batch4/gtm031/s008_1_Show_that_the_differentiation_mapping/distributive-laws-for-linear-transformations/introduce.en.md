The composition of linear transformations distributes over addition on both the left and the right. That is, $A(B+C) = AB + AC$ and $(B+C)D = BD + CD$, whenever the compositions are defined. These distributive laws, together with associativity of composition, make the set of linear transformations on a single vector space into a ring, and ultimately into an algebra over the center field.
