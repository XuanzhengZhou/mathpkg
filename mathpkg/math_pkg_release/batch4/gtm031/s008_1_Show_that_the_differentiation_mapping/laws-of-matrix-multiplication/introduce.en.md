Matrix multiplication satisfies the associative and distributive laws, making square matrices into an associative ring. These algebraic properties are inherited from the corresponding properties of linear transformations under composition. One can verify them directly using the summation formula $\gamma_{ik} = \sum_j \alpha_{ij} \beta_{jk}$, or deduce them from the isomorphism between matrices and linear transformations relative to fixed bases.
