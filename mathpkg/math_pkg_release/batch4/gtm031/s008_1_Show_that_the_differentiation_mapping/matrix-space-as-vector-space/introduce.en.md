The set of all $n_1 \times n_2$ matrices over a division ring forms a vector space of dimension $n_1 n_2$ under entrywise addition and scalar multiplication. Over a field, this matrix space is isomorphic to the space $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ of linear transformations via the correspondence that assigns to each linear transformation its matrix relative to fixed bases. This isomorphism preserves both addition and scalar multiplication. However, over a non-commutative division ring, the scalar multiplication obtained from the matrix correspondence depends on the choice of bases.
