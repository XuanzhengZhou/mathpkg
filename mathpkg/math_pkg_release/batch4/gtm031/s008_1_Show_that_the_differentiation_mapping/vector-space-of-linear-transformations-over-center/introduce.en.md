Even when the underlying division ring $\Delta$ is non-commutative, $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ can be made into a vector space over the field $\Phi = Z(\Delta)$, the center of $\Delta$. Scalar multiplication by $\gamma \in \Phi$ is defined via composition with the left-multiplication map $\gamma_l$. This structure is independent of the choice of bases and does not require $\Delta$ to be commutative.
