A linear transformation $A$ between finite-dimensional vector spaces can be represented by a matrix once bases are chosen for both spaces. The columns of the matrix encode where each basis vector of the domain is mapped. For any vector expressed as a linear combination of basis elements, its image under $A$ is computed via the matrix. This representation makes concrete the abstract notion of a linear transformation.
