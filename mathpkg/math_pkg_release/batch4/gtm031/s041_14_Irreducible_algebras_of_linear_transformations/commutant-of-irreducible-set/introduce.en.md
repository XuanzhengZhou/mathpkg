Theorem 10 characterizes the commutant (centralizer) of an irreducible set of linear transformations on a finite-dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field. It states that the only linear transformations commuting with every element of such an irreducible set are the scalar multiples of the identity. This is a powerful special case of Schur's lemma and a key ingredient in the proof of Burnside's theorem on irreducible algebras of linear transformations.
