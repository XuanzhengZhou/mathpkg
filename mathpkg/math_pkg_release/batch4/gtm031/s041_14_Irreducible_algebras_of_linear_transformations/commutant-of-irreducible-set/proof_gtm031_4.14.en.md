Theorem 10 follows as an immediate consequence of Schur's lemma combined with the General Density Theorem. More specifically, let $\mathfrak{B}$ be the set of linear transformations which commute with every $A \in \Omega$. By Schur's lemma, $\mathfrak{B}$ is a division algebra over $\Phi$. Since $\Phi$ is algebraically closed and $\mathfrak{R}$ is finite-dimensional, the only division algebra of linear transformations over $\Phi$ that contains $\Phi_l$ (the scalar multiplications) is $\Phi_l$ itself. Hence every element of $\mathfrak{B}$ is a scalar multiplication.
