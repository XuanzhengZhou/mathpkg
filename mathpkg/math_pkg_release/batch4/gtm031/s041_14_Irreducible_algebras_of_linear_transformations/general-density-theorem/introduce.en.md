The General Density Theorem is a cornerstone of Jacobson's structure theory for rings. It states that any irreducible ring of endomorphisms (nonzero) on a commutative group becomes a dense ring of linear transformations when the group is viewed as a left vector space over a suitable division ring. This division ring is anti-isomorphic to the centralizer of the ring of endomorphisms. The theorem generalizes Burnside's classical theorem on algebras of linear transformations and provides the foundation for the Jacobson density theorem in abstract algebra.
