Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be any irreducible ring of endomorphisms $\neq 0$ in a commutative group $\mathfrak{R}$ and regard $\mathfrak{R}$ as a left vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ anti-isomorphic to the division ring of endomorphisms which commute with every $A$ in $\mathfrak{A}$. Then $\mathfrak{A}$ is a dense ring of linear transformations in $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Delta$.
