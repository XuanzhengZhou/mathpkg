The proof of the General Density Theorem is completed in the preceding section (Section 13, on dense rings of linear transformations). The final step, given at the opening of Section 14, is:

Since $\mathfrak{A}$ is two-fold transitive, there exists a $B \in \mathfrak{A}$ such that $x_{k+1}E_iB \neq 0$ but $x_iB = 0$. If we set $F = E_iB$, we find that $x_jF = x_jE_iB = 0$ for $j \neq i$ and $\leq k$ and $x_iF = 0$ but $x_{k+1}F \neq 0$.

This proves the transitivity assertion and completes the proof.
