\textbf{Definition.} Let $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$ be dual relative to $g(x, y')$. Let $\phi(\mathfrak{R}')$ be the total subspace of $\mathfrak{R}^*$ corresponding to $\mathfrak{R}'$. Then $\phi(\mathfrak{R}')$ inherits the finite topology from $\mathfrak{R}^*$. Via the 1-1 correspondence between $\phi(\mathfrak{R}')$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$, we transfer this topology to $\mathfrak{R}'$. The open sets of $\mathfrak{R}'$ are the unions of basic open sets $O(x_i; \beta_i)$, $i = 1, \ldots, r$, where $O(x_i; \beta_i)$ is the set of $y' \in \mathfrak{R}'$ such that $g(x_i, y') = \beta_i$. This is called the $\mathfrak{R}$-topology on $\mathfrak{R}'$. Similarly, we define an $\mathfrak{R}'$-topology on $\mathfrak{R}$.
