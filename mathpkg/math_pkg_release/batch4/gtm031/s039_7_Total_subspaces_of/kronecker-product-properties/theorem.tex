\textbf{Proposition.} The proofs of the elementary properties such as commutativity and associativity of Kronecker multiplication made no use of finiteness; hence these hold in the general case. In particular, the Kronecker product of arbitrary vector spaces over a field, the Kronecker product of arbitrary (non-associative) algebras, and the extension space $\mathfrak{R}_\Sigma$ for $\mathfrak{R}$ arbitrary and $\Sigma$ any extension field of the base field $\Phi$ of $\mathfrak{R}$ can all be defined using this construction.
