\textbf{Definition.} Let $\mathfrak{R}'$ be a total subspace of $\mathfrak{R}^*$ and let $x \in \mathfrak{R}$. Then the mapping $f \mapsto x(f) \equiv f(x)$ is a linear function on $\mathfrak{R}'$, that is, it is an element of the conjugate space $\mathfrak{R}'^*$ of $\mathfrak{R}'$. The mapping $x \mapsto x(f)$ is a linear transformation of $\mathfrak{R}$ into $\mathfrak{R}'^*$ and, since $\mathfrak{R}'$ is total, this linear transformation is 1-1. Moreover, the image space is a total subspace of $\mathfrak{R}'^*$.
