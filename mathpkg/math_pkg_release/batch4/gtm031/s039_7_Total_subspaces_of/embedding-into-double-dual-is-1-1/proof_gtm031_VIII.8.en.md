**Proof.** Linearity of $x \mapsto (f \mapsto f(x))$ follows from the linearity of each $f \in \mathfrak{R}'$. Injectivity follows from totality: if $x \neq 0$, there exists $f \in \mathfrak{R}'$ with $f(x) \neq 0$, so the functional $f \mapsto f(x)$ is non-zero on $\mathfrak{R}'$. The image is total in $\mathfrak{R}'^*$ because for any non-zero $f \in \mathfrak{R}'$, there exists $x$ with $f(x) \neq 0$ (since $f$ is a non-zero linear functional), so the evaluation at $x$ separates points of $\mathfrak{R}'$.
