\textbf{Corollary.} If $\mathfrak{R}$ is finite dimensional, the finite topology in $\mathfrak{R}^*$ is discrete. Hence $\operatorname{Cl} \mathfrak{R}' = \mathfrak{R}'$ for every subspace $\mathfrak{R}'$ of $\mathfrak{R}^*$. It follows that the only total subspace of $\mathfrak{R}^*$ is $\mathfrak{R}^*$ itself. On the other hand, for infinite dimensional $\mathfrak{R}$, $\mathfrak{R}^*$ contains proper total subspaces (for example, $\mathfrak{R}_0^*$).
