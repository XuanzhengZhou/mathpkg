Let $\mathfrak{U}$ be the set of normalized matrices and $\mathfrak{B}$ the ring of $\mathfrak{o}$-endomorphisms of $\mathfrak{R}$. The correspondence $(\beta) \mapsto B$ defined by $f_i B = \sum_j \beta_{ij} f_j$ restricts to a bijection $\mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{B}$. That is, every $B \in \mathfrak{B}$ is represented by a unique normalized matrix in $\mathfrak{U}$, and distinct normalized matrices determine distinct endomorphisms.
