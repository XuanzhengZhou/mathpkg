While the general homomorphism $\mathfrak{M} \to \mathfrak{B}$ has a nontrivial kernel consisting of matrices $(\beta)$ for which $\sum_j \beta_{ij} f_j = 0$ for all $i$, restricting to the normalized matrices $\mathfrak{U}$ eliminates this non-uniqueness. Two normalized matrices produce the same endomorphism if and only if they are identical, so the correspondence $\mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{B}$ is a bijection. This gives a canonical parametrization of the endomorphism ring.
