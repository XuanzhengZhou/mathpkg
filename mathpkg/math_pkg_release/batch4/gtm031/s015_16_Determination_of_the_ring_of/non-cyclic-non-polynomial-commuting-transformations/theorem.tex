Let $A$ be a linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space over a field $\Phi$. If $A$ is not cyclic, then there exist linear transformations that commute with $A$ which are not polynomials in $A$. Equivalently, $\mathfrak{B} \supsetneq \mathfrak{A} = \Phi[A]$ whenever $t > 1$, where $t$ is the number of invariant factors of $A$.
