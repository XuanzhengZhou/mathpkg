The converse to the necessity of the order ideal divisibility condition: given any choice of elements $g_i \in \mathfrak{R}$ whose order ideals divide the corresponding $\delta_i$, the linear extension of the assignment $f_i \mapsto g_i$ defines a well-defined $\mathfrak{o}$-endomorphism of $\mathfrak{R}$. This establishes that the order ideal condition is both necessary and sufficient for a collection of images to determine an endomorphism.
