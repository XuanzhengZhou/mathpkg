For a finitely generated module over a principal ideal domain, any $\mathfrak{o}$-endomorphism is uniquely determined by where it sends a fixed set of generators. This follows directly from the linearity of the endomorphism: writing an arbitrary element as a linear combination of generators and applying $B$ distributes the map across the sum, yielding the image in terms of the images of the generators alone.
