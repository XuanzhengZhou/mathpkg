When $\mathfrak{o} = \Phi$ is a field, the module $\mathfrak{R}$ is a vector space over $\Phi$. In this case, the ring $\mathfrak{B}$ of $\mathfrak{o}$-endomorphisms is the full ring $\mathfrak{L}$ of all linear transformations (since every linear map is an endomorphism of the vector space as a $\Phi$-module). By Theorem 20, the center of $\mathfrak{B}$ consists of the scalar multiplications $x \mapsto \alpha x$ with $\alpha \in \Phi$. Since a linear transformation is expressible as a polynomial in the identity $1$ if and only if it is a scalar multiplication, this gives the result that the center of $\mathfrak{L}$ is $\Phi_1$.
