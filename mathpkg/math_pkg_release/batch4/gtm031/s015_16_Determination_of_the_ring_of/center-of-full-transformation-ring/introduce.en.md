As a special case of Theorem 20 applied to a vector space over a field (where $\mathfrak{o} = \Phi$ is a field, hence a PID, and the module is the vector space itself), the center of the full ring $\mathfrak{L}$ of all linear transformations is the set of scalar multiplications $\Phi_1$. This is a fundamental result in linear algebra, sometimes stated as: the only matrices that commute with every matrix are the scalar matrices.
