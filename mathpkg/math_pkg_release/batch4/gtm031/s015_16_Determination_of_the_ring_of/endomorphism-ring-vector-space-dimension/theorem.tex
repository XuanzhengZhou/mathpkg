Let $A$ be a linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space over a field $\Phi$, with invariant factors $\delta_1, \ldots, \delta_t$ and $u = \#\{i : \delta_i \neq 0\}$. Let $\mathfrak{B}$ be the ring of linear transformations commuting with $A$, and let $\mathfrak{A} = \Phi[A]$ be the polynomials in $A$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{B}$ is a subspace of the vector space $\mathfrak{L}$ of all linear transformations over $\Phi$.
\item The dimension of $\mathfrak{B}$ over $\Phi$ is $N = \sum_{j=1}^{t} (2t - 2j + 1) n_j$, where $n_j = \deg \delta_j$.
\item If $t > 1$, then $\dim_\Phi \mathfrak{B} > \dim_\Phi \mathfrak{A} = n_t = \sum_{j=1}^{t} n_j$, so $\mathfrak{B} \supsetneq \mathfrak{A}$.
\end{enumerate}
