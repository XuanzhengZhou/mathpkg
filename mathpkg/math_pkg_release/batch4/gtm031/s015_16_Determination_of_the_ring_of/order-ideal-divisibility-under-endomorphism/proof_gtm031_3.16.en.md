By definition of the order ideal, $\delta_i f_i = 0$ for each generator $f_i$. Applying the $\mathfrak{o}$-endomorphism $B$ to this relation gives

$$\delta_i g_i = \delta_i (f_i B) = (\delta_i f_i) B = 0 \cdot B = 0.$$

Thus $\delta_i$ belongs to the annihilator of $g_i$. If $(\epsilon_i)$ denotes the order ideal of $g_i$, then $\epsilon_i$ is a generator of this annihilator, so $\epsilon_i \mid \delta_i$.
