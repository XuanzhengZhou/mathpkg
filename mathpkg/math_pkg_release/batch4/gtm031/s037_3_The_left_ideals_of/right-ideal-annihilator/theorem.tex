Every right ideal $\mathfrak{J}$ in the ring of linear transformations $\mathfrak{L}$ has the form $\mathfrak{Z}(\mathfrak{S})$, the set of linear transformations which annihilate a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ of the underlying vector space $\mathfrak{R}$. The subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ is precisely the totality of vectors annihilated by every $B \in \mathfrak{J}$.
