Let $\mathfrak{L}$ be the ring of linear transformations on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring. This theorem characterizes every right ideal $\mathfrak{J}$ of $\mathfrak{L}$ as the set $\mathfrak{Z}(\mathfrak{S})$ of all linear transformations that annihilate a fixed subspace $\mathfrak{S} \subseteq \mathfrak{R}$. Conversely, the subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ is precisely the set of all vectors annihilated by every element of $\mathfrak{J}$. This establishes a dual correspondence between right ideals of $\mathfrak{L}$ and subspaces of $\mathfrak{R}$.
