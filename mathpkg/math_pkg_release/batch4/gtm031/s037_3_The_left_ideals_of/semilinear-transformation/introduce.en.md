A semilinear transformation generalizes the concept of a linear transformation to the case where the vector spaces are defined over possibly different division rings. It consists of an additive group homomorphism together with an associated isomorphism of the underlying division rings. Semilinear transformations arise naturally in the study of isomorphisms between rings of linear transformations: every isomorphism of such rings is induced by a semilinear transformation.
