Viewing the ring of linear transformations $\mathfrak{L}$ as a direct product $\mathfrak{R}' \times \mathfrak{R}$ (where $\mathfrak{R}'$ is the dual space), one can classify all left ideals. Every nonzero left ideal $\mathfrak{J}'$ of $\mathfrak{L}$ determines a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ (the join of all rank subspaces $\mathfrak{R}B$ for $B \in \mathfrak{J}'$), and the left ideal is contained in $\mathfrak{R}' \times \mathfrak{S}$. The correspondence $\mathfrak{S} \mapsto \mathfrak{R}' \times \mathfrak{S}$ is an order-preserving lattice isomorphism, providing a complete structural description of left ideals in terms of subspaces.
