This follows immediately from Theorem 8 by taking $\mathfrak{L}_1 = \mathfrak{L}_2 = \mathfrak{L}$, $\mathfrak{R}_1 = \mathfrak{R}_2 = \mathfrak{R}$, and $\Delta_1 = \Delta_2 = \Delta$. Theorem 8 states that any isomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{L}_1 \to \mathfrak{L}_2$ is of the form $A_1\phi = U^{-1} A_1 U$ for a 1-1 semilinear transformation $U: \mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$. Specializing to the case of an automorphism ($\mathfrak{L}_1 = \mathfrak{L}_2$) gives $U: \mathfrak{R} \to \mathfrak{R}$ as a semilinear bijection of $\mathfrak{R}$ onto itself, and the formula becomes $A\phi = U^{-1}AU$ for all $A \in \mathfrak{L}$.
