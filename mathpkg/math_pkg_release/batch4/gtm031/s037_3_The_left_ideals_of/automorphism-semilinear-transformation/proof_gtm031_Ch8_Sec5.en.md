This follows immediately from Theorem 8 by taking $\mathfrak{L}_1 = \mathfrak{L}_2 = \mathfrak{L}$ and $\phi$ to be an automorphism of $\mathfrak{L}$. Theorem 8 states that any isomorphism $A_1 \mapsto A_1\phi$ of $\mathfrak{L}_1$ onto $\mathfrak{L}_2$ is given by a 1-1 semilinear transformation $U$ of $\mathfrak{R}_1$ onto $\mathfrak{R}_2$ in the sense that $A_1\phi = U^{-1} A_1 U$. Applying this with $\mathfrak{R}_1 = \mathfrak{R}_2 = \mathfrak{R}$ yields the result.
