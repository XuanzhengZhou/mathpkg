Let $\mathfrak{L}$ be the ring of linear transformations on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ and let $\mathfrak{R}$ (denoted as a right ideal of $\mathfrak{L}$) be a nonzero right ideal. This lemma states that if a vector $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ is not annihilated by the entire right ideal (i.e., $x\mathfrak{R} \neq 0$), then the orbit $x\mathfrak{R}$ is actually the whole space $\mathfrak{R}$. The proof uses the fact that $x\mathfrak{R}$ is a nonzero subspace invariant under $\mathfrak{R}$, and the only such subspace of the simple module $\mathfrak{R}$ is $\mathfrak{R}$ itself.
