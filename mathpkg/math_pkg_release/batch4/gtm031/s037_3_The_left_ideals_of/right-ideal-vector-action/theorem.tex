If $\mathfrak{R}$ is a right ideal $\neq 0$ in the ring of linear transformations $\mathfrak{L}$ and $x$ is a vector in the underlying vector space such that $x\mathfrak{R} \neq 0$, then $x\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}$, i.e., the orbit of $x$ under $\mathfrak{R}$ equals the entire vector space $\mathfrak{R}$.
