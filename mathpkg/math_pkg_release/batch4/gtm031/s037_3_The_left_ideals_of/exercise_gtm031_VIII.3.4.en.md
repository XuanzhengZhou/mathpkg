Let $(e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n)$ be a basis for $\mathfrak{R}$ and let $U$ be a semi-linear transformation of $\mathfrak{R}$ into itself with associated automorphism $u$ of $\Delta$. If $e_i U = \sum_{j} \alpha_{ij} e_j$, then $(\alpha_{ij})$ is the matrix of $U$ relative to the given basis. Show that $U$ is completely determined by the matrix $(\alpha_{ij})$ and the associated automorphism $u$. Find the transformation formula giving the matrix of $U$ relative to a second basis. Show that to any pair $((\alpha), u)$, where $(\alpha)$ is an invertible $n \times n$ matrix over $\Delta$ and $u$ is an automorphism of $\Delta$, there corresponds a semilinear transformation $U$.
