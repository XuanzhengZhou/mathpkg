The Primary Decomposition Theorem (Theorem 6 in Jacobson) is one of the fundamental structure theorems for a single linear transformation. It asserts that a vector space $\Re$ decomposes as a direct sum of $A$-invariant subspaces $\Re_i$, each associated with a distinct irreducible factor $\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$ of the minimum polynomial of $A$. The subspace $\Re_i$ consists precisely of those vectors annihilated by $\pi_i(A)^{k_i}$, and the restriction of $A$ to $\Re_i$ has minimum polynomial $\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$. Unlike the finer decomposition into indecomposable (cyclic) subspaces, this primary decomposition is unique and forms the foundation for the rational canonical form and the Jordan decomposition.
