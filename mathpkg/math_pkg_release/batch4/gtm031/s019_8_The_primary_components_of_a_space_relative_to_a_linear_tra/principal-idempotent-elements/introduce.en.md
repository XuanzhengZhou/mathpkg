The principal idempotent elements $E_i$ of a linear transformation $A$ are a distinguished family of pairwise orthogonal idempotent operators built from the minimum polynomial of $A$. Constructed as $E_i = \phi_i(A)\mu_i(A)$ using the Bezout relation among the complementary factors $\mu_i(\lambda) = \mu(\lambda)/\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$, they satisfy $E_iE_j = 0$ for $i \neq j$, $\sum E_i = 1$, and $E_i^2 = E_i$. The ranges $\Re E_i$ coincide with the primary components $\Re_i$, providing a polynomial-based construction of the canonical projections onto the primary decomposition.
