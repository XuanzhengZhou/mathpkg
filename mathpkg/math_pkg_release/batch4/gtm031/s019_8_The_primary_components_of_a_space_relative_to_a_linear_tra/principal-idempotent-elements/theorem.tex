Let $A$ be a linear transformation in $\Re$ with minimum polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ factored as in (13). Define
$$
\mu_i(\lambda) = \mu(\lambda) / \pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}, \qquad i = 1, 2, \dots, s,
$$
and let $\phi_1(\lambda), \dots, \phi_s(\lambda)$ be polynomials such that $\sum_{i=1}^s \phi_i(\lambda)\mu_i(\lambda) = 1$. The \textbf{principal idempotent elements} of $A$ are the linear transformations
$$
E_i = \phi_i(A)\mu_i(A), \qquad i = 1, 2, \dots, s,
$$
which satisfy the orthogonal idempotent relations
$$
E_iE_j = 0 \;\; (i \neq j), \qquad \sum_{i=1}^s E_i = 1, \qquad E_i^2 = E_i.
$$
