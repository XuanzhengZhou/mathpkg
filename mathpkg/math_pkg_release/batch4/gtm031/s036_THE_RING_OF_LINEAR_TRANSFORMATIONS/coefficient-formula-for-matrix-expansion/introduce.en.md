The coefficients $\bar{\alpha}_{ij}$ in the matrix expansion $A = \sum \bar{\alpha}_{ij} E_{ij}$ can be recovered from $A$ via the formula $\bar{\alpha}_{ij} = \sum_k E_{ki} A E_{jk}$. This is an important computational identity: it expresses the $(i,j)$-th coefficient of $A$ directly in terms of $A$ and the matrix units, without requiring prior knowledge of the expansion. This formula plays a key role in the proof that the endomorphism ring $\mathfrak{L}$ is simple.
