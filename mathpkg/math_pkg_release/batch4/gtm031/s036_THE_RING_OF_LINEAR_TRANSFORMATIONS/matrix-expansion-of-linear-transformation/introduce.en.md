Every linear transformation $A$ on a finite dimensional vector space can be expressed as a linear combination $A = \sum \bar{\alpha}_{ij} E_{ij}$ where the $E_{ij}$ are matrix units and the coefficients $\bar{\alpha}_{ij}$ are scalar transformations. This expansion mirrors the matrix representation of $A$: the coefficients $\alpha_{ij}$ are exactly the entries of the matrix of $A$ relative to the chosen basis. Since $\bar{\alpha}_{ij}$ commutes with the $E_{ij}$, the coefficients can be written on either side of the matrix units.
