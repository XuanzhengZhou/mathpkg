The endomorphism ring $\mathfrak{L}$ of a finite dimensional vector space over a division ring is a simple ring -- it possesses no non-trivial two-sided ideals. This is a fundamental structural result that underlies the entire theory of the ring of linear transformations. The chapter presents two proofs: one using matrix units and the coefficient formula $\bar{\alpha}_{ij} = \sum_k E_{ki} A E_{jk}$, and a second using operator methods that constructs projections within any non-zero ideal.
