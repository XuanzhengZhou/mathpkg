The map $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ defines an isomorphism from the division ring $\Delta$ onto the set $\bar{\Delta}$ of scalar transformations. This means that $\bar{\Delta}$ forms a subring of the endomorphism ring $\mathfrak{L}$ that is structurally identical to $\Delta$. In the matrix representation, $\bar{\Delta}$ corresponds to the set of scalar matrices $\operatorname{diag}\{\alpha, \alpha, \dots, \alpha\}$, which is a subring of the matrix ring $\Delta_n$ isomorphic to $\Delta$.
