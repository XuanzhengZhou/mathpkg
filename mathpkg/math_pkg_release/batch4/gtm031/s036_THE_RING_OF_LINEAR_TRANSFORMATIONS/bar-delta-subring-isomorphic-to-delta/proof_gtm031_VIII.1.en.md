The fundamental correspondence between linear transformations and matrices associates with $\bar{\alpha}$ the matrix $\operatorname{diag}\{\alpha, \alpha, \cdots, \alpha\}$. The totality of these diagonal matrices is a subring of the full matrix ring $\Delta_n$ which is clearly isomorphic to $\Delta$ via the map $\alpha \mapsto \operatorname{diag}\{\alpha, \alpha, \cdots, \alpha\}$. Since this map factors through $\bar{\alpha}$, the correspondence $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ is an isomorphism from $\Delta$ onto $\bar{\Delta}$. Hence $\bar{\Delta}$ is a subring of $\mathfrak{L}$ isomorphic to $\Delta$.
