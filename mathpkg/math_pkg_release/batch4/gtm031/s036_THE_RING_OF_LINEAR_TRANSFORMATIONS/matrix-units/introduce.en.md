Matrix units $E_{ij}$ are linear mappings defined relative to a fixed basis $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ of a finite dimensional vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$. Each $E_{ij}$ sends the basis vector $e_i$ to $e_j$ and annihilates all other basis vectors. Together with the scalar transformations $\bar{\alpha}$, the matrix units provide a complete decomposition of any linear transformation $A \in \mathfrak{L}$ as $A = \sum \bar{\alpha}_{ij} E_{ij}$, establishing the correspondence between linear transformations and matrices with entries in $\Delta$.
