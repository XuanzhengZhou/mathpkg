Although $\bar{\alpha}$ maps each basis vector $e_i$ to $\alpha e_i$, it does not generally coincide with left scalar multiplication $\alpha_l$. For a general vector $x = \sum \xi_i e_i$, scalar multiplication gives $\alpha x = \sum (\alpha \xi_i) e_i$, while $\bar{\alpha}$ yields $x \bar{\alpha} = \sum (\xi_i \alpha) e_i$. The two coincide precisely when $\alpha$ commutes with all elements of $\Delta$, i.e., when $\alpha$ lies in the center. This distinction is fundamental when $\Delta$ is non-commutative.
