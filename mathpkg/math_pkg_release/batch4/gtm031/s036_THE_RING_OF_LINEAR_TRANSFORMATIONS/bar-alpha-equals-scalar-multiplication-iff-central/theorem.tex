Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a finite dimensional vector space over a division ring $\Delta$, and let $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ be a basis. For $\alpha \in \Delta$, let $\bar{\alpha}$ be the scalar transformation defined by $e_i \bar{\alpha} = \alpha e_i$ and let $\alpha_l$ denote left scalar multiplication $x \mapsto \alpha x$. Then

$$\bar{\alpha} = \alpha_l$$

if and only if $\alpha$ belongs to the center $Z(\Delta)$ of the division ring $\Delta$.
