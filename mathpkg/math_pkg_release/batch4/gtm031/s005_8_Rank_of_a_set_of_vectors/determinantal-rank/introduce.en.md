The determinantal rank of a matrix is defined in terms of its minors: it is the largest integer $\rho$ such that there exists a non-vanishing $\rho$-rowed minor, while every $(\rho+1)$-rowed minor vanishes. This purely algebraic definition provides an alternative characterization of the rank of a matrix that is independent of any choice of basis of the underlying vector space.
