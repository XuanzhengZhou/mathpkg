A square matrix belongs to the general linear group $L(\Phi, n)$ if and only if its determinant is non-zero. This follows from the fact that the row vectors form a basis for the space precisely when the row rank equals $n$, which by the equality of row rank and determinantal rank is equivalent to the non-vanishing of the $n$-rowed minor—the determinant itself. The author notes this can also be proved directly (cf. Volume I, p. 59).
