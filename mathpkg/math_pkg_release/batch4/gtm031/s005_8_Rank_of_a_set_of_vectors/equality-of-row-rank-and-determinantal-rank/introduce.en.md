As an immediate consequence of Theorem 9, the determinantal rank of a matrix coincides with its row rank. Taking the row vectors $x_1, \dots, x_\rho$ that form a basis for the set of all row vectors, Theorem 9 guarantees a non-vanishing $\rho$-rowed minor in the first $\rho$ rows, while every $(\rho+1)$-rowed minor vanishes. The same reasoning applied to right vector spaces yields the equality of column rank and determinantal rank as well.
