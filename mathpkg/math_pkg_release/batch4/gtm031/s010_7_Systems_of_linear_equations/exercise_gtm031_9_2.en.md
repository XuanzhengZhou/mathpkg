Let $f$ be a linear function on $\mathfrak{R}$ and let $\bar{f}$ denote its contraction on the subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ of $\mathfrak{R}$, that is, $\bar{f}$ is the mapping $y \to f(y)$ for $y$ in $\mathfrak{S}$. Show that the mapping $f \to \bar{f}$ is a linear transformation of $\mathfrak{R}^*$ into the space $\mathfrak{S}^*$ of linear functions on $\mathfrak{S}$. Show that $j(\mathfrak{S})$ is the null-space of this transformation. Prove that $f \to \bar{f}$ is a mapping of $\mathfrak{R}^*$ onto $\mathfrak{S}^*$.
