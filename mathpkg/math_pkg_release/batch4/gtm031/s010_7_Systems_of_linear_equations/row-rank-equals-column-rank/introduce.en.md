Theorem 9 establishes that for any matrix (over a division ring), the row rank equals the column rank. The proof uses the fact that every matrix is equivalent to a normal form (18) which visibly has equal row and column rank, and that equivalent matrices share both invariants. A second, more geometric proof is given later in §12.
