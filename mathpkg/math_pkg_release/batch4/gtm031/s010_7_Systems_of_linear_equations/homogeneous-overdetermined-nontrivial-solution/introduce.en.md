This is an immediate consequence of Theorem 8. If there are $m$ equations in $n > m$ unknowns, then the row rank $\rho \leq m < n$, so $n - \rho \geq n - m > 0$, implying the existence of a non-trivial solution. This result is fundamental in linear algebra, ensuring that underdetermined homogeneous systems always have nonzero solutions.
