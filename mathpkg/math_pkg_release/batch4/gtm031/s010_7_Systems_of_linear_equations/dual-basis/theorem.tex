If $(e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Delta$, the linear functions $e_1^*, e_2^*, \dots, e_n^*$ defined by
$$
e_i^*(e_j) = \delta_{ij}, \quad i, j = 1, 2, \dots, n,
$$
where $\delta_{ij} = 0$ if $i \neq j$ and $\delta_{ij} = 1$ if $i = j$, form a basis for the conjugate space $\mathfrak{R}^*$ over $\Delta$.
