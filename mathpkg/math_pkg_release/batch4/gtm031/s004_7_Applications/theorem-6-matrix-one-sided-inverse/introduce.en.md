In the ring $\Delta_n$ of $n \times n$ matrices over a division ring $\Delta$, a left inverse automatically implies a right inverse, and vice versa. That is, if $(\beta)(\alpha) = 1$ for matrices $(\alpha), (\beta) \in \Delta_n$, then $(\alpha)(\beta) = 1$ must also hold, so both matrices belong to the general linear group $L(\Delta, n)$. This property generalizes the familiar fact for matrices over fields and relies on the absence of zero divisors in the underlying division ring.
