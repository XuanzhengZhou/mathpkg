Given $r$ left linearly independent row vectors of length $n$ over a division ring $\Delta$, one can always extend them to an $n \times n$ invertible matrix by adding $n-r$ additional rows. This exercise reinforces the connection between linear independence of vectors and the existence of a basis containing them, translated into matrix language via the correspondence between ordered bases and elements of the general linear group $L(\Delta, n)$.
