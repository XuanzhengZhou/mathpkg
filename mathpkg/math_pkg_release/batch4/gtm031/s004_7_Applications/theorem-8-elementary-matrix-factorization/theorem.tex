Any matrix $(\alpha)$ in $L(\Delta, n)$ is a product of elementary matrices of types I, II, and III, where:

Type I: $T_{pq}(\beta)$ has $1$'s on the diagonal and $\beta$ in the $(p,q)$-position ($q \neq p$), with all other entries $0$.

Type II: $D_p(\gamma)$, $\gamma \neq 0$, has $1$'s on the diagonal except at the $p$-th position where $\gamma$ appears, with all other entries $0$.

Type III: $P_{pq}$ is the permutation matrix exchanging rows $p$ and $q$.
