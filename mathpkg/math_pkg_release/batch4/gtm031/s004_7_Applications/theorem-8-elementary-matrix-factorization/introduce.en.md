The general linear group $L(\Delta, n)$ over a division ring $\Delta$ is generated by elementary matrices of three types: transvections $T_{pq}(\beta)$ (adding a multiple of one row to another), dilations $D_p(\gamma)$ (multiplying a row by a non-zero scalar), and row permutations $P_{pq}$. This is the division-ring analogue of the classical result that every invertible matrix over a field factors into elementary row operations. The proof proceeds by induction on the dimension $n$, showing that any ordered basis can be transformed to the standard basis via elementary replacements corresponding to these matrix types.
