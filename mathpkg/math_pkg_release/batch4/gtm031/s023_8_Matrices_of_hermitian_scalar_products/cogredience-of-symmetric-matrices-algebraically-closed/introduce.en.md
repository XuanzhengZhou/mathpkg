Theorem 4 classifies symmetric matrices over algebraically closed fields of characteristic not equal to two. Since every element in such a field is a square, the diagonal entries $\beta_i$ obtained from Theorem 3 can be scaled to $1$ by replacing $u_i$ with $\beta_i^{-1/2} u_i$. Consequently, the matrix of any symmetric scalar product can be reduced to the form $\operatorname{diag}\{1, \cdots, 1, 0, \cdots, 0\}$, which is completely determined by the rank. Thus two symmetric matrices are cogredient precisely when their ranks coincide.
