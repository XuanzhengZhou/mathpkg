**Theorem 3.** If $g(x, y)$ is a hermitian scalar product on a finite-dimensional vector space $\Re$ over $\Delta$, then there exists a basis $(u_1, u_2, \cdots, u_r, z_1, z_2, \cdots, z_{n-r})$ such that

$$g(u_i, u_i) = \beta_i \neq 0, \quad i = 1, 2, \cdots, r,$$

and all other products are $0$.
