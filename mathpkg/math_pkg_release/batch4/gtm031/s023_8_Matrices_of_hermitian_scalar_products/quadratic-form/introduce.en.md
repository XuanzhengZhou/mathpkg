A quadratic form is the function obtained by evaluating a symmetric scalar product $g(x, y)$ on the same vector argument in both positions, yielding $g(x, x)$. This concept is fundamental to the study of symmetric bilinear forms over fields of characteristic not equal to two. The quadratic form captures the norm-like behavior of a vector under the given symmetric pairing.
