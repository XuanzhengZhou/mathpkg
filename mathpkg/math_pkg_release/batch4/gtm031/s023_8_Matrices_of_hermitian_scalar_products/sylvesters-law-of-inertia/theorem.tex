**Theorem 5 (Sylvester).** If the diagonal matrices

$$\{\beta_1, \beta_2, \cdots, \beta_n\}, \quad \{\beta_1', \beta_2', \cdots, \beta_n'\}$$

are cogredient in $\Phi_n$, where $\Phi$ is the field of real numbers, then the number $p$ of positive $\beta_i$ is the same as the number $p'$ of positive $\beta_i'$.
