This lemma establishes that a non-zero hermitian scalar product always possesses at least one non-isotropic vector, i.e., a vector $u$ for which $g(u, u) \neq 0$. This is the critical induction base for the diagonalization theorem (Theorem 3). Without it, the entire space could be totally isotropic even though the scalar product is not identically zero.
