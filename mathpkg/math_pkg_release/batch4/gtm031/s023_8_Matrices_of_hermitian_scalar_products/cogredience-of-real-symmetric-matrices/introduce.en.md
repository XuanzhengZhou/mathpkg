Theorem 6 gives the complete classification of real symmetric matrices under cogredience. Over the real numbers, a symmetric matrix can be diagonalized to the form $\operatorname{diag}\{1, \cdots, 1, -1, \cdots, -1, 0, \cdots, 0\}$, and by Sylvester's law of inertia (Theorem 5), the numbers of positive and negative entries are invariants. Thus two real symmetric matrices are cogredient precisely when they share both the rank and the signature $(p, r-p)$.
