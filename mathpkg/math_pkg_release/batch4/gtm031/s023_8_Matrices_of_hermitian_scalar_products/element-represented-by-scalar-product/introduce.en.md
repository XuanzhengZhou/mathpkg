An element $\beta$ of the base ring $\Delta$ is said to be represented by a scalar product $g$ if it arises as $g(u, u)$ for some non-zero vector $u$. The condition $g(u, u) = \overline{g(u, u)}$ forces every represented element to be invariant under the anti-automorphism of $\Delta$. This notion sets up the key question: does a given hermitian scalar product represent any non-zero elements at all?
