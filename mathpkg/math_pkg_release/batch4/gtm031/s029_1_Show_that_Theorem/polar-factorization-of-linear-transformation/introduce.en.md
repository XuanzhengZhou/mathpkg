The polar factorization theorem states that every linear transformation $A$ can be decomposed as $A = P \exp(iH)$, where $P$ is a positive semi-definite hermitian transformation and $H$ is hermitian. This is the matrix generalization of the polar representation of a complex number $\alpha = |\alpha| e^{i\eta}$. The factorization combines the polar decomposition of $A$ (which gives $P$ and a unitary factor) with the representation of the unitary factor as $\exp(iH)$ established earlier.
