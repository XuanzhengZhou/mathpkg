If $\{(\alpha^{(k)})\}$, $k = 1, 2, 3, \cdots$, is an infinite sequence of matrices in $\Phi_n$, $\Phi$ the field of complex numbers, we say that $\{(\alpha^{(k)})\}$ converges to $(\alpha)$, $(\alpha^{(k)}) \rightarrow (\alpha)$, if the sequence of complex numbers $\alpha_{ij}^{(k)} \rightarrow \alpha_{ij}$ for every $i, j = 1, 2, \cdots, n$. The limit matrix $(\alpha)$ is unique since this is the case for sequences of complex numbers.
