A sequence of $n \times n$ matrices $\{(\alpha^{(k)})\}$ over the complex numbers converges to a matrix $(\alpha)$ if and only if each entry converges as a sequence of complex numbers: $\alpha_{ij}^{(k)} \rightarrow \alpha_{ij}$ for every $i, j = 1, 2, \dots, n$. This entry-wise definition makes the space of matrices $\Phi_n$ a complete metric space and ensures that the limit matrix, when it exists, is unique.
