Every unitary transformation $U$ can be written as $\exp(iH)$ for a hermitian transformation $H$. This follows from the spectral theorem: a unitary matrix can be diagonalized by a unitary basis, and its eigenvalues lie on the unit circle, so each $\rho_i = e^{i\theta_i}$ for real $\theta_i$. Taking $H = \operatorname{diag}\{\theta_1, \dots, \theta_n\}$ in that basis yields a hermitian matrix satisfying $U = \exp(iH)$. This is the matrix analogue of the polar representation $e^{i\theta}$ of complex numbers on the unit circle.
