If $U$ is a unitary linear transformation, then the characteristic roots $\rho_i$ of $U$ satisfy $|\rho_i| = 1$. Hence $\rho_i = \exp(\sqrt{-1}\,\theta_i)$ with $\theta_i$ real. Let $H$ be the linear transformation whose matrix relative to a unitary basis diagonalizing $U$ is

$$\operatorname{diag}\{\theta_1, \theta_2, \cdots, \theta_n\}.$$

Then $H$ is hermitian and

$$U = \exp(iH).$$
