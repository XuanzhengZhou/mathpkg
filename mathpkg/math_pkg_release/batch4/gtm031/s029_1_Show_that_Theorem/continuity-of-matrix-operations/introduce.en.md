Under the entry-wise definition of matrix convergence, the operations of matrix addition and matrix multiplication are continuous functions of their arguments. This follows because the $(i,j)$-entry of the sum or product of two matrices is itself a continuous function of the $2n^2$ coordinate entries. An important consequence is that similarity transformations by a fixed non-singular matrix $(\mu)$ also preserve convergence.
