The exponential $\exp A$ of a linear transformation $A$ is defined as the infinite power series $\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} A^k/k!$. Since the scalar exponential function $e^\lambda = \sum \lambda^k/k!$ has infinite radius of convergence, Theorem 15 guarantees that $\exp A$ converges for every linear transformation $A$, regardless of the magnitude of its characteristic roots. Using the classical canonical form, the matrix of $\exp A$ has a block structure where each diagonal block corresponding to a characteristic root $\rho$ is lower triangular with entries $\exp \rho / j!$ on the $j$-th subdiagonal.
