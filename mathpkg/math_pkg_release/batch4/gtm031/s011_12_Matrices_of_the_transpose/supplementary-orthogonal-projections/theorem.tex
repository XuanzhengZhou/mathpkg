Let $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}_1 \oplus \mathfrak{R}_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{R}_r$ be a direct sum decomposition and let $E_i$ be the projection mappings determined by this decomposition. Then the $E_i$ satisfy:

$$E_i^2 = E_i, \quad E_i E_j = 0 \text{ for } i \neq j, \quad \sum_{i=1}^r E_i = 1.$$

Conversely, every finite set $\{E_1, E_2, \ldots, E_r\}$ of linear transformations satisfying these three conditions arises from the direct sum decomposition $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}E_1 \oplus \mathfrak{R}E_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{R}E_r$.
