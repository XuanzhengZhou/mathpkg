Given a direct sum decomposition $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{R}_r$ of a vector space, each vector $x$ has a unique representation as a sum of components $x_i \in \mathfrak{R}_i$. The mapping $E_i$ that extracts the $i$-th component is a linear transformation called a projection. The projections satisfy $E_i^2 = E_i$ (idempotence) and $E_i E_j = 0$ for $i \neq j$ (orthogonality).
