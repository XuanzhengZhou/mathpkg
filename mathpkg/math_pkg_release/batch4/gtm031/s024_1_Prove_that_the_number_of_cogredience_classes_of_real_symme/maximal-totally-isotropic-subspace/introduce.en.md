A maximal totally isotropic subspace is a subspace on which the hermitian scalar product vanishes identically and which is not properly contained in any larger subspace with this property. These subspaces play a crucial role in the reduction theory of cogredience of hermitian matrices: any two maximal totally isotropic subspaces have the same dimension, and the cogredience problem for general hermitian matrices can be reduced to the totally regular case by factoring out a maximal totally isotropic subspace.
