A consequence of Witt's Theorem is that any $g$-equivalence between arbitrary subspaces (not necessarily non-isotropic) can be extended to a $g$-unitary transformation of the whole space. The proof reduces the isotropic case to the non-isotropic case by embedding the given subspace into a larger non-isotropic subspace through the addition of a suitable complementary space, then applying Witt's theorem to the extension.
