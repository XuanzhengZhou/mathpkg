This exercise asks to prove that real symmetric $n \times n$ matrices fall into $(n+1)(n+2)/2$ cogredience classes. The result follows from Sylvester's law of inertia: every real symmetric matrix is cogredient to a diagonal matrix with entries $1, -1, 0$, and the signature $(p, q)$ with $p + q \leq n$ determines the cogredience class. Counting the number of possible pairs $(p, q)$ with $p, q \geq 0$, $p + q \leq n$ yields exactly $(n+1)(n+2)/2$.
