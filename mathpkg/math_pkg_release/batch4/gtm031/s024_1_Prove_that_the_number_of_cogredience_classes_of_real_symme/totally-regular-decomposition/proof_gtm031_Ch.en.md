The condition $\mathfrak{S} \cap \mathfrak{S}^\perp = 0$ (total regularity of the restriction) is necessary for the decomposition. It is also sufficient: since $g$ is totally regular on $\mathfrak{R}$, any vector $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ defines a linear functional $y \mapsto g(y, x)$ on $\mathfrak{S}$. By the non-degeneracy of $g$ restricted to $\mathfrak{S}$, there exists a unique vector $x_{\mathfrak{S}} \in \mathfrak{S}$ such that $g(y, x) = g(y, x_{\mathfrak{S}})$ for all $y \in \mathfrak{S}$. Then $x - x_{\mathfrak{S}} \in \mathfrak{S}^\perp$, giving the decomposition $x = x_{\mathfrak{S}} + (x - x_{\mathfrak{S}})$ with $x_{\mathfrak{S}} \in \mathfrak{S}$ and $x - x_{\mathfrak{S}} \in \mathfrak{S}^\perp$. The uniqueness of this representation follows from $\mathfrak{S} \cap \mathfrak{S}^\perp = 0$.
