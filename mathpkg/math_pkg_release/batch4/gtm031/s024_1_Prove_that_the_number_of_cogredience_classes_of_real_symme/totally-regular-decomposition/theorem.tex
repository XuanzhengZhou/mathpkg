If the hermitian scalar product $g$ on $\mathfrak{R}$ is totally regular and $\mathfrak{S}$ is any subspace, then

$$\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{S} \oplus \mathfrak{S}^\perp$$

if and only if the restriction of $g$ to $\mathfrak{S}$ is totally regular (i.e., $\mathfrak{S} \cap \mathfrak{S}^\perp = 0$).
