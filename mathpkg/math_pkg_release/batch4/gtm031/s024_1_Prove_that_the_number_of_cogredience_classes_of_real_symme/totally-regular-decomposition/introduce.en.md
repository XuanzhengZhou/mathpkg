For a totally regular hermitian scalar product, a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ admits an orthogonal complement decomposition $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{S} \oplus \mathfrak{S}^\perp$ precisely when the restriction of the scalar product to $\mathfrak{S}$ is itself totally regular. This is a key structural result used in the reduction theory of cogredience, as it allows one to decompose the space into orthogonal components and study the scalar product componentwise.
