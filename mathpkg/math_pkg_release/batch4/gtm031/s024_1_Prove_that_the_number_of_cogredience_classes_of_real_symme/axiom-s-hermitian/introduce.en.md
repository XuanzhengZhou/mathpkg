Axiom S is the solvability condition required for Witt's Theorem in the theory of hermitian scalar products over a division ring. It asserts that for every hermitian element $\beta$, the equation $\xi + \bar{\xi} = \beta$ has a solution. This axiom is automatically satisfied when the characteristic is not two (take $\xi = \frac{1}{2}\beta$), and also holds in characteristic two provided there exists an element $\gamma$ in the center with $\bar{\gamma} \neq \gamma$. The axiom rules out the exceptional case $\Delta = \Phi$, a field of characteristic two with trivial involution $\bar{\alpha} \equiv \alpha$.
