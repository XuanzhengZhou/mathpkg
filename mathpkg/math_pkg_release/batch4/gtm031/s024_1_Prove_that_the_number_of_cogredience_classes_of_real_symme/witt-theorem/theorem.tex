Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ with a hermitian scalar product $g(x, y)$ satisfying Axiom S. If $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ are non-isotropic subspaces of $\mathfrak{R}$ and $U$ is a $g$-equivalence of $\mathfrak{S}_1$ onto $\mathfrak{S}_2$, then $U$ can be extended to a $g$-unitary transformation of $\mathfrak{R}$.

More generally, if $\mathfrak{S}$ is any subspace (possibly isotropic) and $U$ is a $g$-equivalence of $\mathfrak{S}$ onto $\mathfrak{S}U$, then $U$ can be extended to a $g$-unitary transformation of $\mathfrak{R}$.
