Witt's Theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of hermitian scalar products. It states that any $g$-equivalence between two non-isotropic subspaces of a vector space equipped with a hermitian scalar product can be extended to a $g$-unitary transformation of the whole space. The theorem relies on Axiom S (solvability of $\xi + \bar{\xi} = \beta$) and can be proved by induction on the dimension of the subspaces, reducing to the two-dimensional case via a careful analysis of isotropic vectors and hyperbolic planes.
