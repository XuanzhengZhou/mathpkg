Let $g(x, y)$ be an alternate (skew symmetric) scalar product on a finite-dimensional vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a commutative field $\Phi$, with $g \neq 0$. Then there exists a basis

$$(u_1, v_1, u_2, v_2, \cdots, u_r, v_r, z_1, z_2, \cdots, z_{n-2r})$$

such that

$$g(u_i, v_i) = 1, \quad g(v_i, u_i) = -1$$

with all other products $0$, and $g(z_j, x) = 0$ for all $x \in \mathfrak{R}$. The matrix of $g$ relative to this basis is the direct sum of $r$ blocks $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$ and an $(n-2r) \times (n-2r)$ zero matrix.
