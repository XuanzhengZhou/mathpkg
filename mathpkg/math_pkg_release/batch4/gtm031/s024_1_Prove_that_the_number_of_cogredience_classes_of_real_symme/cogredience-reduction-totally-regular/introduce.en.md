The problem of cogredience of hermitian matrices can be reduced to the case where the associated scalar product is totally regular. Given any vector space with a hermitian scalar product, one can choose a maximal totally isotropic subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, construct a complementary totally isotropic subspace $\mathfrak{V}$ of the same dimension, and obtain an orthogonal decomposition where the scalar product is totally regular on the complement of $\mathfrak{S} \oplus \mathfrak{V}$. This yields a block matrix form where the non-regular part consists of hyperbolic plane blocks.
