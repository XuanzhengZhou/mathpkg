Let $L$ be the set of all subspaces of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$. Define the relation $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_2$ (inclusion) on $L$. Then $(L, \supseteq)$ is a partially ordered set, meaning:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{S} \supseteq \mathfrak{S}$ (reflexive),
\item If $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_2$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_1$, then $\mathfrak{S}_1 = \mathfrak{S}_2$ (antisymmetric),
\item If $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_2$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_3$, then $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_3$ (transitive).
\end{enumerate}

Thus $L$ forms a lattice with respect to the operations of intersection (meet) and join (sum of subspaces).
