A vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ is a direct sum of independent subspaces $\mathfrak{R}_1, \ldots, \mathfrak{R}_r$, denoted $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{R}_r$, if these subspaces span $\mathfrak{R}$ and are independent. The key property of a direct sum is that every vector admits a unique representation as a sum of components from each summand, making direct sums the vector-space analogue of disjoint union.
