Let $\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2, \cdots, \mathfrak{R}_r$ be independent subspaces of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ such that
$$
\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}_1 + \mathfrak{R}_2 + \cdots + \mathfrak{R}_r.
$$
Then $\mathfrak{R}$ is said to be the \textbf{direct sum} of the subspaces $\mathfrak{R}_i$, denoted by
$$
\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}_1 \oplus \mathfrak{R}_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{R}_r.
$$
In this case, every vector of $\mathfrak{R}$ can be written in one and only one way as a sum of vectors from the subspaces $\mathfrak{R}_i$.
