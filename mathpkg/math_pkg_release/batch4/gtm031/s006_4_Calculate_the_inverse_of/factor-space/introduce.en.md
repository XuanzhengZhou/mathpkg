The factor space (also called quotient vector space) $\bar{\mathfrak{R}} = \mathfrak{R} / \mathfrak{S}$ is constructed from a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ and a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ by forming cosets $x + \mathfrak{S}$. Scalar multiplication descends to the quotient because $\mathfrak{S}$ is closed under scalar multiplication, making the coset $\overline{\alpha x}$ independent of the coset representative. The resulting structure inherits all vector space axioms, giving a natural way to "mod out" a subspace.
