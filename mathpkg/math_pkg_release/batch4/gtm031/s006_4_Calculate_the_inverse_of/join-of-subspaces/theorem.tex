The set-theoretic union $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cup \mathfrak{S}_2$ of two subspaces need not be a subspace. As a substitute, we take the subspace spanned by $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cup \mathfrak{S}_2$, denoted by $[\mathfrak{S}_1 \cup \mathfrak{S}_2]$. This subspace is denoted by $\mathfrak{S}_1 + \mathfrak{S}_2$ and is called the \textbf{join} of $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$:
$$
\mathfrak{S}_1 + \mathfrak{S}_2 = [\mathfrak{S}_1 \cup \mathfrak{S}_2] = \{x + y \mid x \in \mathfrak{S}_1,\; y \in \mathfrak{S}_2\}.
$$
The join $\mathfrak{S}_1 + \mathfrak{S}_2$ acts as the least upper bound in the lattice $L$ of subspaces with respect to the inclusion relation.
