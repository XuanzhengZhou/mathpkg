The union of two subspaces is generally not a subspace. The join (or sum) $\mathfrak{S}_1 + \mathfrak{S}_2$ is defined as the subspace spanned by their union, which coincides with the set of all sums $x + y$ where $x \in \mathfrak{S}_1$ and $y \in \mathfrak{S}_2$. In the lattice of subspaces ordered by inclusion, the join serves as the least upper bound.
