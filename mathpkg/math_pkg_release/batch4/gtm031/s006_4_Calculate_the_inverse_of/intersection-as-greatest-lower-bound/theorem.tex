In the lattice $L$ of all subspaces of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$, the intersection of two subspaces $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2$ acts as the greatest lower bound (meet) with respect to the inclusion relation $\supseteq$. This means:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2$ is contained in (i.e., $\supseteq$) both $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$,
\item If any subspace $\mathfrak{S}'$ is contained in both $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$, then $\mathfrak{S}'$ is contained in $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2$.
\end{enumerate}
