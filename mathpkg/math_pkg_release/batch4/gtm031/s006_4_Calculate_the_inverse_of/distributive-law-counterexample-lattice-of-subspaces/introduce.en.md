The distributive law familiar from set theory does not hold in the lattice of subspaces of a vector space. A concrete counterexample uses three 1-dimensional subspaces in a 2-dimensional space: $\mathfrak{S}_1 = [x_1]$, $\mathfrak{S}_2 = [x_2]$, and $\mathfrak{S}_3 = [x_1 + x_2]$. While $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap (\mathfrak{S}_2 + \mathfrak{S}_3) = \mathfrak{S}_1$, the right-hand side $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2 + \mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_3$ evaluates to the zero subspace, demonstrating the failure of distributivity. A weakened version, the modular law, does hold.
