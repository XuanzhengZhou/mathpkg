For any subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ of $\mathfrak{R}$:

1. $\mathfrak{R} + \mathfrak{S} = [\mathfrak{R} \cup \mathfrak{S}] = \mathfrak{R}$, since $\mathfrak{R} \cup \mathfrak{S} \subseteq \mathfrak{R}$ and the span of $\mathfrak{R}$ is $\mathfrak{R}$ itself (any $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ can be written as $x = x + 0$ with $x \in \mathfrak{R}, 0 \in \mathfrak{S}$). Similarly $\mathfrak{S} + \mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}$.

2. $\mathfrak{R} \cap \mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{S}$, since $\mathfrak{S} \subseteq \mathfrak{R}$, the intersection of $\mathfrak{R}$ with $\mathfrak{S}$ is exactly $\mathfrak{S}$. Similarly $\mathfrak{S} \cap \mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{S}$.
