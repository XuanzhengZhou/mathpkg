A complement $\mathfrak{S}^*$ of a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ in a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ is a subspace satisfying $\mathfrak{S} \cap \mathfrak{S}^* = 0$ and $\mathfrak{S} + \mathfrak{S}^* = \mathfrak{R}$. This means every vector in $\mathfrak{R}$ can be uniquely expressed as the sum of a vector in $\mathfrak{S}$ and a vector in $\mathfrak{S}^*$, making $\mathfrak{R}$ the direct sum $\mathfrak{S} \oplus \mathfrak{S}^*$.
