When a set of subspaces $\mathfrak{S}_1, \ldots, \mathfrak{S}_r$ of a vector space are independent (i.e., each $\mathfrak{S}_i$ intersects the sum of the remaining subspaces only trivially), every vector in their sum space admits a unique decomposition as a sum of vectors from the individual subspaces. This uniqueness property is equivalent to independence of the subspaces and forms the foundation of the direct sum decomposition. It generalizes the familiar idea that vectors in a direct sum have unique coordinate representations.
