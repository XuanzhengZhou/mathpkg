Let $L$ be the lattice of all subspaces of a finite-dimensional vector space $\mathfrak{R}$.

\textbf{Descending Chain Condition (DCC):} If $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_2 \supseteq \cdots$ is an infinite descending chain of subspaces, then there exists an integer $r$ such that
$$
\mathfrak{S}_r = \mathfrak{S}_{r+1} = \cdots.
$$

\textbf{Ascending Chain Condition (ACC):} If $\mathfrak{S}_1 \subseteq \mathfrak{S}_2 \subseteq \cdots$ is an infinite ascending chain of subspaces, then there exists an integer $r$ such that
$$
\mathfrak{S}_r = \mathfrak{S}_{r+1} = \cdots.
$$

These follow immediately since the dimension $\dim \mathfrak{S}$ is a non-negative integer and $\mathfrak{S} \supset \mathfrak{S}'$ implies $\dim \mathfrak{S} > \dim \mathfrak{S}'$.
