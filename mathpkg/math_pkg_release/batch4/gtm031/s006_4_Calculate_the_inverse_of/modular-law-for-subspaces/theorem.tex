Let $L$ be the lattice of subspaces of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$. Then the following weakening of the distributive law holds in $L$:

\textbf{Modular Law:} If $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_2$, then
$$
\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap (\mathfrak{S}_2 + \mathfrak{S}_3) = \mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2 + \mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_3
$$
for any subspaces $\mathfrak{S}_1, \mathfrak{S}_2, \mathfrak{S}_3 \in L$.

Equivalently, since $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_2$, we have $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2 = \mathfrak{S}_2$, so the modular law takes the more familiar form:
$$
\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap (\mathfrak{S}_2 + \mathfrak{S}_3) = \mathfrak{S}_2 + (\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_3).
$$
