The subspace consisting only of the zero vector serves as the null element (bottom element) in the lattice of all subspaces of a vector space. For any subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, both the join $\mathfrak{S} + 0$ and the meet $\mathfrak{S} \cap 0$ behave exactly as expected: the join returns $\mathfrak{S}$ and the meet returns the zero subspace.
