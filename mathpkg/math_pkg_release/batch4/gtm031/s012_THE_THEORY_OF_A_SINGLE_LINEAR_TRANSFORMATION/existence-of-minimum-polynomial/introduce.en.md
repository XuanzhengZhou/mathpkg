This theorem establishes the explicit form of the minimum polynomial of a linear transformation $A$ on a finite-dimensional vector space. The coefficients of the minimum polynomial come directly from the first linear dependence relation among the powers $1, A, A^2, \dots$. Since the algebra $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ is $n^2$-dimensional, the sequence of powers cannot remain independent beyond $n^2$ steps, guaranteeing the existence of such an $m \le n^2$. The monic polynomial obtained is the unique polynomial of minimal degree annihilating $A$.
