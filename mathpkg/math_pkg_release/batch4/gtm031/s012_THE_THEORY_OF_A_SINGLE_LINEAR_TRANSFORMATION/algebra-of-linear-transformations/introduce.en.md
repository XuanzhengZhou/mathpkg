The set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ of all linear transformations from a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ to itself forms an algebra over the base field $\Phi$. This algebra structure combines the ring operations (addition and composition of linear transformations) with scalar multiplication by field elements. The compatibility condition $\gamma(AB) = (\gamma A)B = A(\gamma B)$ ensures that scalar multiplication interacts properly with composition. When $\mathfrak{R}$ is finite-dimensional with $\dim_\Phi \mathfrak{R} = n$, the algebra $\mathfrak{L}$ has dimension $n^2$ over $\Phi$. This algebraic framework is the natural setting for studying a single linear transformation $A \in \mathfrak{L}$ and its powers.
