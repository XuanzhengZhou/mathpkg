The set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ of all linear transformations from a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ to itself forms an associative algebra over the field $\Phi$. This algebra structure combines the ring structure (with composition as multiplication) and the vector space structure (with scalar multiplication defined pointwise). When $\mathfrak{R}$ is finite-dimensional of dimension $n$, the algebra $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ has dimension $n^2$ over $\Phi$, corresponding to the $n 	imes n$ matrix representation.
