Let $\Phi[\lambda]$ be the integral domain of polynomials in the indeterminate $\lambda$ with coefficients in the field $\Phi$. Let $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ be a linear transformation on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$. Since $A$ commutes with every scalar multiple $\alpha 1 = \alpha_l$, the mapping
$$f(\lambda) = \alpha_0 + \alpha_1 \lambda + \alpha_2 \lambda^2 + \cdots \mapsto f(A) \equiv \alpha_0 1 + \alpha_1 A + \alpha_2 A^2 + \cdots$$
is a homomorphism of $\Phi[\lambda]$ into $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$. This homomorphism is an extension of the isomorphism $\Phi \cong \Phi 1$ and is characterized by the property that it maps $\lambda$ into $A$.
