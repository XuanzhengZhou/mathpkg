Given a linear transformation $A$ on a vector space over a field $\Phi$, there is a natural homomorphism from the polynomial ring $\Phi[\lambda]$ into the algebra of linear transformations. This homomorphism sends a polynomial $f(\lambda)$ to the linear transformation obtained by formally substituting $A$ for $\lambda$. It extends the scalar multiplication isomorphism $\Phi \cong \Phi 1$ and maps the indeterminate $\lambda$ to $A$ itself. The kernel of this homomorphism is precisely the ideal generated by the minimum polynomial of $A$.
