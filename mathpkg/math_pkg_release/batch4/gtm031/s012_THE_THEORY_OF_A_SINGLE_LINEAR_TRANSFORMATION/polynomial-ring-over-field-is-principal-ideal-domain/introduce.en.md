The polynomial ring $\Phi[\lambda]$ over a field $\Phi$ is a principal ideal domain (PID). This fundamental result from ring theory follows from the existence of the division algorithm (Euclidean algorithm) for polynomials over a field: given any two polynomials $f, g$ with $g 
eq 0$, there exist polynomials $q, r$ such that $f = qg + r$ with $\deg r < \deg g$. The PID property is essential for defining the minimum polynomial of a linear transformation, as it guarantees that the kernel of the evaluation homomorphism is generated by a single polynomial.
