Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be an $n$-dimensional vector space over a field $\Phi$, and let $\mathfrak{L} = \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ be the algebra of linear transformations on $\mathfrak{R}$. For any $A \in \mathfrak{L}$, the sequence $(1, A, A^2, \ldots)$ spans a finite-dimensional subspace of $\mathfrak{L}$. Since $\dim \mathfrak{L} = n^2$, there exists a smallest integer $m \geq 1$ such that $A^m$ is linearly dependent on $1, A, \ldots, A^{m-1}$. Hence there exist $\mu_0, \mu_1, \ldots, \mu_{m-1} \in \Phi$ such that
$$A^m = \mu_0 1 + \mu_1 A + \cdots + \mu_{m-1} A^{m-1}.$$
Moreover, all higher powers $A^{m+1}, A^{m+2}, \ldots$ also lie in $[1, A, \ldots, A^{m-1}]$.
