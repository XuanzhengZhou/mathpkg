The set $\Delta_n$ of all $n \times n$ matrices over a division ring $\Delta$, equipped with matrix multiplication, forms a semigroup: the product of two $n \times n$ matrices is again an $n \times n$ matrix, and the associative law holds. The identity matrix $1$ acts as a two-sided identity, making $\Delta_n$ a monoid (a semigroup with identity).
