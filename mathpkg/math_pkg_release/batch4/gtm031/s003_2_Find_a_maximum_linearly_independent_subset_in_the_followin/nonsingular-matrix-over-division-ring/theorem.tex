A matrix $(\alpha) \in \Delta_n$ (an $n \times n$ matrix with entries in a division ring $\Delta$) is called a \text{unit}, \text{non-singular}, or \text{regular} if there exists a matrix $(\beta) \in \Delta_n$ such that

$$(\alpha)(\beta) = 1 = (\beta)(\alpha),$$

where $1$ denotes the $n \times n$ identity matrix.
