In the semigroup $\Delta_n$ of $n \times n$ matrices over a division ring, a matrix $(\alpha)$ is called non-singular (or regular, or a unit) if it possesses a two-sided inverse $(\beta)$ satisfying $(\alpha)(\beta) = 1 = (\beta)(\alpha)$. Non-singular matrices form the group of units of the monoid $\Delta_n$ and play a central role in the theory of change of basis and linear transformations.
