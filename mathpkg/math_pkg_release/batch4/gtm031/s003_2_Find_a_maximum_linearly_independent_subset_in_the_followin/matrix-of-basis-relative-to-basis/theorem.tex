Let $(e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n)$ and $(f_1, f_2, \ldots, f_n)$ be two ordered bases of an $n$-dimensional vector space $\Re$ over a division ring $\Delta$. The \text{matrix of } $(f_1, f_2, \ldots, f_n)$ \text{ relative to } $(e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n)$ is the $n \times n$ matrix $(\alpha) = (\alpha_{ij})$ defined by

$$f_j = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha_{ij} e_i \qquad (j = 1, 2, \ldots, n).$$

Equivalently, the $j$-th column of $(\alpha)$ gives the coordinates of $f_j$ in the basis $(e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n)$.
