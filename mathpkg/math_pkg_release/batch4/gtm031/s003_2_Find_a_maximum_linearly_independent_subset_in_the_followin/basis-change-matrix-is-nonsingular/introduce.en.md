Theorem 5 establishes that the matrix $(\alpha)$ relating two ordered bases of a vector space is always non-singular: the matrix $(\beta)$ of the reverse change of basis satisfies $(\beta)(\alpha) = (\alpha)(\beta) = 1$. This is a direct consequence of the composition rule for change-of-basis matrices and the fact that the matrix of a basis relative to itself is the identity.
