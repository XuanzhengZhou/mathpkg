**Theorem 5.** The matrix of any ordered basis $(f_1, f_2, \ldots, f_n)$ relative to another ordered basis $(e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n)$ of an $n$-dimensional vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ is non-singular. More precisely, if $(\alpha)$ is the matrix of $(f_i)$ relative to $(e_i)$ and $(\beta)$ is the matrix of $(e_i)$ relative to $(f_i)$, then

$$(\beta)(\alpha) = 1 = (\alpha)(\beta).$$
