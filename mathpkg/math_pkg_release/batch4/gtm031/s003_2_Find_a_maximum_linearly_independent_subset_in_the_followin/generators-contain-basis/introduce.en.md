In an $n$-dimensional vector space, any set of generators must contain at least $n$ elements, and one can always extract a subset of exactly $n$ elements that forms a basis. This is derived by selecting a maximal linearly independent subset from the generators; the maximality forces every other generator (and hence the whole space) to be spanned by this subset, making it a basis. By the invariance of dimension (Theorem 3), the size of this maximal independent subset must equal $n$.
