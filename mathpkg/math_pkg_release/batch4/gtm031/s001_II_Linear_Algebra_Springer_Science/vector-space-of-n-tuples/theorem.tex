Let $\Delta$ be a division ring and $n$ a fixed positive integer. The set $\Delta^{(n)}$ of all $n$-tuples $(\xi_1, \xi_2, \ldots, \xi_n)$ with $\xi_i \in \Delta$ is called the vector space of $n$-tuples over $\Delta$. Addition and scalar multiplication are defined componentwise:
$$x + y = (\xi_1 + \eta_1, \xi_2 + \eta_2, \ldots, \xi_n + \eta_n),$$
$$\alpha x = (\alpha \xi_1, \alpha \xi_2, \ldots, \alpha \xi_n).$$
The zero vector is $(0, 0, \ldots, 0)$ and the standard basis vectors are $e_i = (0, \ldots, 0, 1, 0, \ldots, 0)$ with $1$ in the $i$-th position.
