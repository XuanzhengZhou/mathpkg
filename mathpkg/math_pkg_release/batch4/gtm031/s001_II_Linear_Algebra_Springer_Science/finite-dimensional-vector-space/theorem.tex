A vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$ is called \emph{finite dimensional} (or said to \emph{possess a finite basis over $\Delta$}) if the following axiom holds in addition to axioms A1--A4, S1--S4:

\textbf{F.} There exist a finite number of vectors $e_1, e_2, \cdots, e_n \in \mathfrak{R}$ such that every vector $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ can be written in one and only one way in the form

$$x = \xi_1 e_1 + \xi_2 e_2 + \cdots + \xi_n e_n, \qquad \xi_i \in \Delta.$$
