The standard basis of $\Delta^{(n)}$ provides the simplest example of a basis in a finite dimensional vector space. Each basis vector $e_i$ has the multiplicative identity $1$ of the division ring $\Delta$ in the $i$-th coordinate position and zeros elsewhere. The fact that every $n$-tuple expands uniquely as $\sum \xi_i e_i$ is the computational manifestation of axiom F and establishes that $\Delta^{(n)}$ is indeed finite dimensional. These basis vectors are the direct analog of the unit coordinate vectors $\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{j}, \mathbf{k}$ in elementary three-dimensional vector algebra.
