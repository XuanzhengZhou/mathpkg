An abstract vector space generalizes the familiar geometric notion of vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$ to $n$-tuples over an arbitrary division ring $\Delta$. The definition is axiomatic: it postulates that the vectors form an abelian group under addition (A1--A4) and that scalar multiplication by elements of $\Delta$ satisfies four compatibility axioms (S1--S4). This abstraction captures the essential algebraic structure needed for the theory of linear dependence and linear transformations, which constitute the main topic of Jacobson's Lectures. Notably, the scalars are not required to form a field -- the definition works over any division ring, accommodating non-commutative examples such as the real quaternions.
