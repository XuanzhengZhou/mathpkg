A linear combination is a fundamental operation in vector spaces. Given a finite set of vectors $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ and scalars $\xi_i$ from the underlying division ring $\Delta$, the linear combination $\sum \xi_i e_i$ produces a new vector. The notion of linear combination underlies the definitions of spanning sets, linear independence, and bases. In $\Delta^{(n)}$, every vector can be expressed as a unique linear combination of the standard basis vectors.
