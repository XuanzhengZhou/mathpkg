Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be an arbitrary ring with an identity element $1$ and suppose that $\mathfrak{R}$ contains a division subring $\Delta$ that contains $1$. For the scalar multiplication $\rho x$, $\rho \in \Delta$, $x \in \mathfrak{R}$, take the ring product $\rho x$. Then the additive group of $\mathfrak{R}$, the division ring $\Delta$, and this scalar multiplication satisfy axioms S1--S4, making $\mathfrak{R}$ a vector space over $\Delta$.
