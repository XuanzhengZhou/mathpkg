A linear transformation is a structure-preserving mapping between two vector spaces over the same division ring. It generalizes the familiar operations of differentiation and linear combination, requiring only that the mapping respects vector addition and scalar multiplication. The concept is a special case of an $\mathfrak{o}$-homomorphism of modules, with the division ring $\Delta$ playing the role of the operator domain. Linear transformations form the foundational objects of study throughout this chapter, including their algebraic structure as a ring (when the domain and codomain coincide) and as a right vector space (in the case of linear functions into the one-dimensional space).
