The orthogonal matrices form a subgroup $O(n)$ of the general linear group, closed under multiplication and inversion. Within this group, the proper orthogonal matrices (determinant $+1$) form a normal subgroup $SO(n)$ of index 2. The two cosets correspond to the two orientation classes: proper (rotations) and improper (reflections). This structure is fundamental to the theory of Euclidean transformations and the geometry of rotations.
