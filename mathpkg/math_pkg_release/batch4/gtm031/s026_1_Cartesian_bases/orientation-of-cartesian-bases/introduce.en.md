The orientation of a Cartesian basis classifies orthonormal bases into two equivalence classes based on the determinant of the orthogonal transition matrix relating them. Two bases share the same orientation if the transition matrix is proper (determinant $+1$), and have opposite orientation if it is improper (determinant $-1$). Reversing one basis vector or applying an odd permutation flips the orientation, giving exactly two orientation classes in any dimension.
