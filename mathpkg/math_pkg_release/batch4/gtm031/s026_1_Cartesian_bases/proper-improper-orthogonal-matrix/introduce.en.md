Proper and improper orthogonal matrices are distinguished by the sign of their determinant. For any orthogonal matrix $(\sigma)$, the determinant must be either $+1$ or $-1$ since $(\det(\sigma))^2 = \det((\sigma)(\sigma)') = \det(1) = 1$. Proper orthogonal matrices (determinant $+1$) correspond to rotations and preserve orientation. Improper orthogonal matrices (determinant $-1$) correspond to reflections or rotoreflections and reverse orientation.
