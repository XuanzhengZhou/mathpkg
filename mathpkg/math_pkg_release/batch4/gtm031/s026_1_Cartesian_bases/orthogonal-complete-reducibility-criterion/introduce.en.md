This corollary to Theorem 3 provides a concise criterion for orthogonal complete reducibility: a set of linear transformations is orthogonally completely reducible precisely when it shares the same invariant subspaces as its set of adjoints. This connects the geometric concept of orthogonal decomposition with the algebraic condition of self-adjointness (up to closure under transpose), and is fundamental to the study of symmetric, skew-symmetric, and orthogonal transformations in Euclidean spaces.
