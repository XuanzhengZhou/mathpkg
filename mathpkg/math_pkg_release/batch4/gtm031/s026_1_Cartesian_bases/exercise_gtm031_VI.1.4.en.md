Prove that, if $(x, y)$ is a positive definite symmetric scalar product, then

$$(u, v)^2 \leq (u, u)(v, v)$$

for any $u$ and $v$, and that equality holds only if $u$ and $v$ are linearly dependent. (This is the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.)
