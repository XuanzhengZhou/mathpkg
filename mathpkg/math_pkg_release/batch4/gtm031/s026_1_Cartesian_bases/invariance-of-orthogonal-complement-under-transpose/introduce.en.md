Theorem 3 states that if a subspace is invariant under a set of linear transformations, its orthogonal complement is invariant under the set of adjoint transformations. This is a fundamental duality result: invariance under $\Omega$ for a subspace implies invariance under $\Omega'$ for the orthogonal complement. The proof relies on the defining property of the adjoint $(xA, y) = (x, yA')$ and is the key step toward the criterion for orthogonal complete reducibility.
