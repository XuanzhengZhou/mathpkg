\textbf{Theorem 3.} Let $\Re$ be a Euclidean space and let $\Omega$ be any set of linear transformations on $\Re$. If a subspace $\mathfrak{S} \subseteq \Re$ is invariant under $\Omega$, then the orthogonal complement $\mathfrak{S}^\perp$ is invariant under $\Omega'$, the set of transposes (adjoints) of the linear transformations in $\Omega$.
