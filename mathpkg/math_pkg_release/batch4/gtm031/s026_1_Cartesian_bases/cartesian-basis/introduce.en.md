A Cartesian basis is an orthonormal basis for a Euclidean space: a set of $n$ vectors that are mutually orthogonal ($(u_i, u_j) = 0$ for $i \neq j$) and of unit length ($(u_i, u_i) = 1$). In such a basis, the scalar product simplifies to the familiar dot product of coordinate vectors. The existence of Cartesian bases follows from the existence of orthonormal bases for positive definite symmetric scalar products.
