A basis $(u_1, u_2, \cdots, u_n)$ for a Euclidean space $\Re$ is called a \textbf{Cartesian basis} if it satisfies

$$(u_i, u_j) = \delta_{ij}$$

for all $i, j = 1, \ldots, n$. Then for $x = \sum \xi_i u_i$ and $y = \sum \eta_i u_i$, the scalar product takes the standard form

$$(x, y) = \sum_{i=1}^n \xi_i \eta_i.$$
