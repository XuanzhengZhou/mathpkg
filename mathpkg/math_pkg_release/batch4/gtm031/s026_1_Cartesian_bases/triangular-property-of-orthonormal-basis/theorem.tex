Let $(e_1, e_2, \cdots, e_n)$ be any basis for a Euclidean space $\Re$ and let $(u_1, u_2, \cdots, u_n)$ be the Cartesian basis obtained by the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process. Then the matrix $(\tau)$ of $(u_1, \ldots, u_n)$ relative to $(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ is triangular. Specifically,

$$u_i = \sum_{j=1}^{i} \tau_{ij} e_j$$

with $\tau_{ij} = 0$ for $j > i$. The matrix has the form

$$(\tau) = \begin{bmatrix}
\tau_{11} & & 0 \\
\tau_{21} & \tau_{22} & \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots \\
\tau_{n1} & \tau_{n2} & \cdots & \tau_{nn}
\end{bmatrix}.$$
