A Euclidean space is a finite-dimensional real vector space equipped with a positive definite symmetric scalar product (inner product). This structure allows one to measure lengths, angles, and orthogonality, forming the algebraic foundation for Euclidean geometry. The scalar product is required to be bilinear, symmetric, and strictly positive on nonzero vectors.
