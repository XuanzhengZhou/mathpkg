This theorem establishes a bijection between Cartesian (orthonormal) bases of a Euclidean space and orthogonal matrices. Fixing one Cartesian basis as reference, every other Cartesian basis corresponds to an orthogonal transition matrix, and every orthogonal matrix produces a Cartesian basis via change of basis. This gives a complete algebraic description of all orthonormal bases and connects the geometry of Euclidean spaces to the matrix group $O(n)$.
