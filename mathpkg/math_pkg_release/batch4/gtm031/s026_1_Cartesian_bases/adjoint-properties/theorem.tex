For linear transformations $A, B$ on a Euclidean space $\Re$, the adjoint operation $A \mapsto A'$ satisfies:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $(A + B)' = A' + B'$
\item $(AB)' = B' A'$
\item $A'' = A$
\end{enumerate}

These follow directly from the defining relation $(xA, y) = (x, yA')$ and the symmetry of the scalar product $(x, y) = (y, x)$.
