The adjoint operation satisfies the same fundamental algebraic identities as the matrix transpose: it is additive, it reverses the order of multiplication (anti-homomorphism), and it is an involution ($A'' = A$). These properties make the adjoint a canonical example of an involution on the algebra of linear transformations, and they are essential for the study of symmetric, skew-symmetric, and orthogonal transformations in Euclidean geometry.
