The adjoint $A'$ of a linear transformation $A$ on a Euclidean space is defined by the relation $(xA, y) = (x, yA')$ for all vectors $x, y$. This generalizes the matrix transpose to the setting of abstract vector spaces equipped with a scalar product. The mapping $A \mapsto A'$ establishes a correspondence between scalar products and linear transformations, making the theory of scalar products equivalent to the theory of linear transformations in a Euclidean space.
