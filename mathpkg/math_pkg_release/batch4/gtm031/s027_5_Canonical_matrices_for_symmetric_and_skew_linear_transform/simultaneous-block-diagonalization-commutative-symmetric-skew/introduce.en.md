Any commuting family of real matrices, each of which is either symmetric or skew symmetric, can be simultaneously brought to a common block diagonal form by a single orthogonal change of basis. The diagonal blocks are either one-dimensional (scalar) or two-dimensional skew blocks $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & \epsilon_i \\ -\epsilon_i & 0 \end{bmatrix}$. This is the generalization of the spectral theorem to commuting mixed symmetric/skew families, and it serves as the foundation for the canonical forms of normal and orthogonal matrices.
