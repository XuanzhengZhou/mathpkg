Let $\omega$ be a set of commutative real matrices which are either symmetric or skew symmetric. Then there exists a real orthogonal matrix $(\sigma)$ such that, for each $(\alpha) \in \omega$, $(\sigma)(\alpha)(\sigma)^{-1}$ has the block diagonal form
$$\begin{bmatrix}
(\beta_1) & & \\
& (\beta_2) & \\
& & \ddots \\
& & & (\beta_n)
\end{bmatrix}$$
where each $(\beta_i)$ is either a one-rowed scalar matrix or a two-rowed matrix of the form
$$(\beta_i) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & \epsilon_i \\ -\epsilon_i & 0 \end{bmatrix}.$$
