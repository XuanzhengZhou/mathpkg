A symmetric linear transformation $A$ on a Euclidean space is positive definite (respectively, semi-definite) if and only if all its characteristic roots $\rho_i$ satisfy $\rho_i > 0$ (respectively, $\rho_i \geq 0$).

In particular, if $A$ is semi-definite and 1-1, then all its characteristic roots are positive, so $A$ is positive definite.
