For a symmetric or skew linear transformation on a Euclidean space, the characteristic space corresponding to an irreducible factor of the characteristic polynomial is the subspace annihilated by that polynomial factor evaluated at the transformation. For a symmetric transformation with root $\rho$, this is the usual eigenspace $\{y : yA = \rho y\}$. For a skew transformation, the linear factor $\lambda$ gives the kernel, while irreducible quadratic factors $\lambda^2 + \delta^2$ give two-dimensional invariant subspaces where the transformation acts as a $2 \times 2$ skew block.
