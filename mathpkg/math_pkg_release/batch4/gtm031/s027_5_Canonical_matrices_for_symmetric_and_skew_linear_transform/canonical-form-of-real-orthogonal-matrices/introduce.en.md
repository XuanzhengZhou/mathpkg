Any real orthogonal matrix can be reduced by orthogonal conjugation to a block diagonal form whose blocks are either $\pm 1$ entries (corresponding to eigenvectors with eigenvalue $\pm 1$) or $2 \times 2$ rotation matrices through angles $\theta_i$. This means every orthogonal transformation on a Euclidean space is, in a suitable orthonormal basis, a composition of independent rotations in mutually orthogonal planes together with possible reflections.
