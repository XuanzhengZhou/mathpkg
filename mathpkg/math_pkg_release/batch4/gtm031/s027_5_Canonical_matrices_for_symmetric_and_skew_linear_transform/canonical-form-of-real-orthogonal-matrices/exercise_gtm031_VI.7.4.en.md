Show that, if $O$ is orthogonal and does not have $-1$ as a characteristic root, then $S = (O - 1)(O + 1)^{-1}$ is skew and $O = (1 + S)(1 - S)^{-1}$. Show that, if $O$ is orthogonal and $1$ is not a characteristic root, then $S = (O + 1)(O - 1)^{-1}$ is skew and $O = (S + 1)(S - 1)^{-1}$.
