Every real skew symmetric linear transformation admits a canonical block diagonal form under orthogonal similarity, with $2 \times 2$ blocks $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & \delta_i \\ -\delta_i & 0 \end{bmatrix}$ for each pair of purely imaginary characteristic roots $\pm i\delta_i$, and zeros on the diagonal for any remaining dimensions where the transformation vanishes. The proof decomposes the space into a direct sum of mutually orthogonal cyclic subspaces, each corresponding to an irreducible factor of the minimum polynomial, which for a skew transformation can only be $\lambda$ or $\lambda^2 + \delta_i^2$ with $\delta_i \neq 0$.
