A normal linear transformation is one that commutes with its transpose. Every linear transformation decomposes uniquely into a symmetric part and a skew part, and normality is equivalent to these two parts commuting with each other. This characterization allows the theory of commutative symmetric and skew transformations to be applied to normal transformations. Symmetric, skew, and orthogonal transformations are all special cases of normal transformations.
