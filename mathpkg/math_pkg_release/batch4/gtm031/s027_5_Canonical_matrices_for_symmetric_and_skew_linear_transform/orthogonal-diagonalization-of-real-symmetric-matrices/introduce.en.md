A real symmetric matrix can be diagonalized by an orthogonal change of basis. The proof proceeds by repeatedly isolating eigenvectors of unit length: starting from the symmetric linear transformation, one finds a one-dimensional invariant subspace spanned by an eigenvector, replaces it by a unit-length multiple, and restricts to the orthogonal complement. Iterating this process yields an orthonormal basis of eigenvectors, giving the diagonal canonical form $\operatorname{diag}\{\rho_1,\ldots,\rho_n\}$ where each $\rho_i$ is a characteristic root. This is the spectral theorem for real symmetric matrices.
