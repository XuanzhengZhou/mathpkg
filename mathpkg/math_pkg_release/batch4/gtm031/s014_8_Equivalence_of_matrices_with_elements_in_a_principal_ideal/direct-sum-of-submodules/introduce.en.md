A module is the internal direct sum of a family of submodules if every element can be expressed uniquely as a sum of elements from the submodules. This generalizes the notion of a vector space being a direct sum of subspaces. The uniqueness of representation is equivalent to the conditions that the submodules generate the whole module and that each submodule intersects the sum of the others trivially.
