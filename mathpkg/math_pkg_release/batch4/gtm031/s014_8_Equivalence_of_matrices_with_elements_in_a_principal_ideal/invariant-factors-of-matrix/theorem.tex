The diagonal elements of a normal form of a matrix over a principal ideal domain are called the \text{invariant factors} of the matrix. If

$$\operatorname{diag}\{\delta_1, \delta_2, \cdots, \delta_r, 0, \cdots, 0\}$$

is a normal form with $\delta_1 \mid \delta_2 \mid \cdots \mid \delta_r$, then $\delta_1, \delta_2, \cdots, \delta_r$ are the invariant factors of $(\sigma)$. It will be shown in \S 10 that they are, in fact, invariants of the given matrix (unique up to multiplication by units).
