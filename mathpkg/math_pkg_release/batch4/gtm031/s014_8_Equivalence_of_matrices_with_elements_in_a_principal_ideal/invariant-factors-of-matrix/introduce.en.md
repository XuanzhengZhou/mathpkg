The invariant factors of a matrix over a principal ideal domain are the non-zero diagonal entries in its Smith normal form, arranged so that each divides the next. Despite being obtained via a constructive reduction process that is not unique, these factors are genuine invariants of the equivalence class -- any two equivalent matrices share the same invariant factors up to unit multiples. The invariant factors completely determine the structure of the cokernel of the matrix regarded as a linear map between free modules, and their product (up to units) equals the greatest common divisor of the maximal minors.
