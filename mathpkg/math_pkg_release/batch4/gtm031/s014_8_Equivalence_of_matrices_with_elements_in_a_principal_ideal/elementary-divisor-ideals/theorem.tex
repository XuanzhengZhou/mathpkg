Let $\delta_1, \delta_2, \ldots, \delta_r$ be the invariant factors of a matrix $(\sigma)$ over a principal ideal domain $\mathfrak{o}$. Factor each $\delta_i$ into prime powers:
$$\delta_i = \pi_1^{e_{i1}} \pi_2^{e_{i2}} \cdots \pi_s^{e_{is}}$$
where the $\pi_j$ are distinct primes. The non-unit prime power factors $\pi_j^{e_{ij}}$ (with $e_{ij} \geq 1$) are called the **elementary divisor ideals** of $(\sigma)$. The matrix is determined up to equivalence by its invariant factors, and hence by its elementary divisor ideals together with its rank.
