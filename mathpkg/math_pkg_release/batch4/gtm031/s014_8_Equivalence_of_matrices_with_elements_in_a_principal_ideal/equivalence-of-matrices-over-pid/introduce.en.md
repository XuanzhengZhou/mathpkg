Two matrices over a principal ideal domain $\mathfrak{o}$ are equivalent if one can be obtained from the other by multiplying on the left and right by unimodular matrices (units in the matrix rings $\mathfrak{o}_m$ and $\mathfrak{o}_n$). This generalizes the notion of equivalence over a field, where matrices are equivalent precisely when they have the same determinantal rank. Over a principal ideal domain, a necessary condition for equivalence is equality of ranks, as one can embed $\mathfrak{o}$ into its quotient field and apply the field-case result. The central problem is to find a simple normal form for each equivalence class.
