If $A$ is a cyclic linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space, then every linear transformation $B$ that commutes with $A$ (i.e., $AB = BA$) is of the form $B = f(A)$ for some polynomial $f(\lambda) \in \Phi[\lambda]$. In other words, the centralizer of a cyclic transformation is precisely the algebra $\Phi[A]$ of polynomials in $A$.
