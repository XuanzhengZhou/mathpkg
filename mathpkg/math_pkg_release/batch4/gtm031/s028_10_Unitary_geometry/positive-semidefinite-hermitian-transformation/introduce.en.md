A positive semi-definite hermitian transformation generalizes the notion of positive definiteness by allowing the quadratic form $(xA, x)$ to vanish for some nonzero vectors. Such transformations occur naturally in the polar decomposition of arbitrary linear transformations, where the semi-definite factor $P = \sqrt{A'A}$ is positive semi-definite and may fail to be definite if $A$ is not invertible.
