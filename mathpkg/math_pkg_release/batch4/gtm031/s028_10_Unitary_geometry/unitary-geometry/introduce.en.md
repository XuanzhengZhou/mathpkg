Unitary geometry is the complex analogue of Euclidean geometry. It studies a vector space over the field of complex numbers equipped with a positive definite hermitian scalar product. Since the underlying field is algebraically closed, the theory of canonical matrices is simpler than in the real case, and many results from Euclidean geometry carry over with appropriate modifications.
