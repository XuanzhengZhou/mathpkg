This lemma establishes that the study of skew-hermitian transformations can be reduced to that of hermitian transformations. The key observation is that scalar multiplication by $i$ converts a skew-hermitian transformation into a hermitian one, because the transpose of the scalar map $x \mapsto \mu x$ is $x \mapsto \bar{\mu} x$, and $x \mapsto ix$ is itself skew-hermitian.
