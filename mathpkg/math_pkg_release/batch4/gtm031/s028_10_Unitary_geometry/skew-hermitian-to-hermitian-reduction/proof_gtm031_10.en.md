Let $A$ be skew-hermitian, so $A' = -A$. The transpose of scalar multiplication $x \mapsto \mu x$ is $x \mapsto \bar{\mu} x$. Hence the scalar multiplication $x \mapsto ix$ is skew-hermitian since $\bar{i} = -i$. Now consider $(iA)'$. Since $iA$ is the composition of $A$ followed by multiplication by $i$, and since scalar multiplication by $i$ commutes with $A$, we have $(iA)' = A' \cdot i' = A' \cdot (-i) = (-A)(-i) = iA$. Thus $(iA)' = iA$, so $iA$ is hermitian.
