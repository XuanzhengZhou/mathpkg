A normal linear transformation is one that commutes with its own adjoint: $AA' = A'A$. Both hermitian and unitary transformations are normal, as are skew-hermitian transformations. The spectral theorem extends to normal matrices: a matrix is unitarily diagonalizable if and only if it is normal (with the word "real" omitted from the hermitian case).
