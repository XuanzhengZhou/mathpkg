Theorem 13 is the spectral theorem for complex hermitian matrices. It states that every hermitian matrix can be unitarily diagonalized to a real diagonal matrix. The proof uses the fact that the characteristic roots of a hermitian transformation are real, and one can find a unitary basis relative to which the matrix takes diagonal form. This is the complex analogue of the principal axis theorem for real symmetric matrices.
