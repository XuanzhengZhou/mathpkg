This proposition shows that when $A$ is hermitian, the form $(xA, y)$ is a hermitian sesquilinear form. This justifies the definition of positive definiteness for hermitian transformations via the condition $(xA, x) > 0$, since $(xA, x)$ is guaranteed to be real. The proof follows directly from the hermitian property $A' = A$ and the defining properties of the hermitian scalar product.
