We compute:
$$
(yA, x) = (yA, x) = (y, xA') = (y, xA)
$$
since $A' = A$. Now using the hermitian property of the scalar product,
$$
(y, xA) = \overline{(xA, y)}.
$$
Thus $(yA, x) = \overline{(xA, y)}$, which is exactly the condition for $(xA, y)$ to be a hermitian form. Setting $y = x$ gives $(xA, x) = \overline{(xA, x)}$, so $(xA, x)$ is real.
