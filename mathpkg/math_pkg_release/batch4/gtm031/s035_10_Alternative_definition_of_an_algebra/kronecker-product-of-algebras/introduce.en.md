The Kronecker product of two algebras generalizes the tensor product construction. Given algebras $\mathfrak{A}_1$ and $\mathfrak{A}_2$ over the same field, their Kronecker product $\mathfrak{A}_1 \times \mathfrak{A}_2$ is an algebra whose underlying vector space is the Kronecker product of the underlying spaces, with multiplication defined componentwise: $(x_1 \times x_2)(y_1 \times y_2) = x_1y_1 \times x_2y_2$. As an immediate consequence, if both $\mathfrak{A}_1$ and $\mathfrak{A}_2$ are associative, then their Kronecker product is associative.
