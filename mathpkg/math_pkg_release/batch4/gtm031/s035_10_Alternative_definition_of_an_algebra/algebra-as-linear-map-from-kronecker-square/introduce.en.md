This definition characterizes an algebra (not necessarily associative) as a vector space $\mathfrak{A}$ together with a single linear map $P: \mathfrak{A} \times \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}$. The binary product is recovered via $xy = (x \times y)P$. By the universal property of the Kronecker product, this is equivalent to the standard definition requiring bilinearity of multiplication: $(x_1 + x_2)y = x_1y + x_2y$, $x(y_1 + y_2) = xy_1 + xy_2$, and $\alpha(xy) = (\alpha x)y = x(\alpha y)$. This reformulation highlights the structural role of the Kronecker product in algebra theory.
