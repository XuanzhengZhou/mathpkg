A set $\mathfrak{A}$ of linear transformations in a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$ is called \textbf{$k$-fold transitive} if, given any two ordered sets of $l \leq k$ vectors $(x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_l)$, $(y_1, y_2, \cdots, y_l)$ such that the $x_i$ are linearly independent, there exists an $A \in \mathfrak{A}$ such that
$$x_i A = y_i, \quad i = 1, 2, \cdots, l.$$
