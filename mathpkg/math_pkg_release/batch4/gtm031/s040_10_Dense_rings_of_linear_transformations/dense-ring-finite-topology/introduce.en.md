A ring $\mathfrak{A}$ of linear transformations of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ is called dense in the finite topology if it can interpolate any finite number of linearly independent vectors to arbitrary targets. This concept generalizes the idea that the ring of all linear transformations acts transitively on finite linearly independent sets. Density in the finite topology is the fundamental property studied in the structure theory of infinite-dimensional linear transformation rings, serving as the correct generalization of the full ring $\mathfrak{L}$ of all linear transformations in the finite-dimensional case.
