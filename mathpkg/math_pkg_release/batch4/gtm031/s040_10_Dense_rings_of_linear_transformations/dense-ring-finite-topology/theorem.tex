A set $\mathfrak{A}$ of linear transformations of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$ is \textbf{dense in the finite topology} if and only if for any two ordered finite sets of vectors $(x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_m)$, $(y_1, y_2, \cdots, y_m)$ such that the $x_i$ are linearly independent, there exists an $A \in \mathfrak{A}$ such that
$$x_i A = y_i, \quad i = 1, 2, \cdots, m.$$
