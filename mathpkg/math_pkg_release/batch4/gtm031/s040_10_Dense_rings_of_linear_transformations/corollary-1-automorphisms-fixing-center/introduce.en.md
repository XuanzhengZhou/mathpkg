Corollary 1 is a direct consequence of the Isomorphism Theorem 7 specialized to the case $\mathfrak{A}_1 = \mathfrak{A}_2 = \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$. When the base ring is a field $\Phi$, any automorphism fixing the center pointwise must be inner, i.e., of the form $A \mapsto U^{-1}AU$ for a semi-linear transformation $U$. Over a field, the condition that the automorphism fixes the center forces the associated semi-linear transformation to be linear (the automorphism of $\Phi$ is the identity), so $U$ is actually a linear transformation.
