Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a dense ring of linear transformations in $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Delta$, and let $\mathfrak{J}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R}) = \mathfrak{J} \cap \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$ be the set of finite rank transformations whose transposes map the total subspace $\mathfrak{R}'$ into itself. Let $F$ be any transformation of finite rank such that $\mathfrak{R}F = [u_1, u_2, \cdots, u_m]$ where the $u_i$ are linearly independent, and suppose $F = \sum_{i=1}^m \phi_i \times u_i$ with $\phi_i \in \mathfrak{R}^*$. If $\mathfrak{A}$ contains transformations $F_j = \sum_k \psi_{jk} \times v_{jk}$ where the $v_{jk}$ are linearly independent for each fixed $j$, then there exist $A_{ji} \in \mathfrak{A}$ such that $\sum_{j,i} F_j A_{ji} = F$. Consequently, $F \in \mathfrak{J} \cap \mathfrak{A}$.
