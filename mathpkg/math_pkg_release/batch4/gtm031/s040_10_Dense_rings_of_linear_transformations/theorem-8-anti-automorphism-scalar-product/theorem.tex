Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a dense ring of linear transformations in $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$ containing non-zero transformations of finite rank. Assume that $\mathfrak{A}$ possesses an anti-automorphism $A \mapsto A\Psi$. Then $\Delta$ has an anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ and there exists a non-degenerate weakly hermitian scalar product $h$ in $\mathfrak{R}$ (relative to this anti-automorphism) such that $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R} \mid \mathfrak{R}) \supseteq \mathfrak{A} \supseteq \mathfrak{J}(\mathfrak{R} \mid \mathfrak{R})$ and $A \mapsto A\Psi$ coincides with the transpose mapping relative to $h$:
$$h(xA, y) = h(x, yA\Psi)$$
for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{R}$ and $A \in \mathfrak{A}$.
