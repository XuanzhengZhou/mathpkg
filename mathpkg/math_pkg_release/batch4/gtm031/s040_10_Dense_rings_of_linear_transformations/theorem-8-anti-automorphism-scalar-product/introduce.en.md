Theorem 8 establishes the fundamental connection between anti-automorphisms of dense rings and scalar products on the underlying vector space. If a dense ring $\mathfrak{A}$ admits an anti-automorphism $\Psi$, then there exists a non-degenerate weakly hermitian scalar product $h$ on $\mathfrak{R}$ such that the anti-automorphism is precisely the adjoint (transpose) mapping with respect to $h$. Moreover, the ring is sandwiched between $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R} \mid \mathfrak{R})$ and $\mathfrak{J}(\mathfrak{R} \mid \mathfrak{R})$ with respect to this scalar product, i.e., $\mathfrak{R}$ becomes self-dual via $h$.
