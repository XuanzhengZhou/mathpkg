A total subspace of the conjugate space is a subspace of linear functionals rich enough to separate points and to interpolate arbitrary finite linearly independent sets via the Kronecker delta condition. This property is essential in the structure theory of dense rings, as it characterizes the dual pairing between a vector space and the space of linear functions that arise from finite rank transformations in a dense ring. The totality condition ensures that the finite rank transformations in $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$ are dense.
