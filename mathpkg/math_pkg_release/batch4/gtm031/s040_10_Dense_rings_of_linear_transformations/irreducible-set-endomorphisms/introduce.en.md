A set of endomorphisms of a commutative group is irreducible if the group has no proper non-trivial subgroups invariant under all endomorphisms. This is the abstract algebraic formulation of irreducibility that does not assume a vector space structure. Every dense ring of linear transformations is an irreducible set of endomorphisms, and conversely, via Schur's Lemma and the Jacobson Density Theorem, every irreducible ring of endomorphisms can be viewed as a dense ring of linear transformations over a suitable division ring.
