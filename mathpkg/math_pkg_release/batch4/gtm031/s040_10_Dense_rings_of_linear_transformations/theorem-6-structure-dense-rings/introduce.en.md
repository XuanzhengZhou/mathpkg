Theorem 6 is the fundamental structure theorem for dense rings of linear transformations. It gives a complete characterization: a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is dense and contains non-zero finite rank transformations if and only if it can be "sandwiched" as $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R}) \supseteq \mathfrak{A} \supseteq \mathfrak{J}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$ for some total subspace $\mathfrak{R}'$ of the conjugate space. The total subspace $\mathfrak{R}'$ is constructed as the set of all linear functions of the form $fF^*$ with $F \in \mathfrak{J} \cap \mathfrak{A}$. This sandwich property is the cornerstone for all subsequent isomorphism and anti-automorphism theorems.
