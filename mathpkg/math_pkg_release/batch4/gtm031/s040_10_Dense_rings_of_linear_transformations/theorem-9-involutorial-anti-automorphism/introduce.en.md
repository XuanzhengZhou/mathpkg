Theorem 9 specializes Theorem 8 to the case where the anti-automorphism $\Psi$ is an involution ($\Psi^2 = 1$). In this case, the weakly hermitian scalar product can be replaced by either a hermitian or a skew hermitian scalar product. The proof involves a rescaling argument: if the form is not already hermitian or skew hermitian, one can multiply by a suitable scalar $\tau$ to transform it into a hermitian form. The converse direction is straightforward: any non-degenerate hermitian or skew hermitian scalar product gives rise to an involutorial anti-automorphism via the transpose.
