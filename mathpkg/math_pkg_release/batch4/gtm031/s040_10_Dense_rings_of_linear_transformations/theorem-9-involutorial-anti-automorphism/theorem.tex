Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a dense ring of linear transformations in $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$ containing non-zero transformations of finite rank, and let $\Psi$ be an anti-automorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ such that $\Psi^2 = 1$. Then $\Delta$ possesses an involutorial anti-automorphism and there exists a non-degenerate hermitian or skew hermitian scalar product $s$ in $\mathfrak{R}$ such that $A \mapsto A\Psi$ coincides with the transpose mapping relative to $s$. Conversely, if $\mathfrak{R}$ has a non-degenerate hermitian or skew hermitian scalar product, then the transpose mapping in $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R} \mid \mathfrak{R})$ is an anti-automorphism.
