Let $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$ be dual vector spaces over a division ring $\Delta$ relative to a non-degenerate bilinear form $g$, and let $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$ denote the totality of linear transformations $A$ in $\mathfrak{R}$ which have transposes $A'$ in $\mathfrak{R}'$, i.e., transformations for which there exists $A' \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}')$ such that
$$g(xA, y') = g(x, y'A')$$
for all $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ and all $y' \in \mathfrak{R}'$. Then any subring $\mathfrak{A}$ of $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$ which contains $\mathfrak{J}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R}) = \mathfrak{J} \cap \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$ is a dense ring of linear transformations containing non-zero transformations of finite rank. Conversely, every dense ring containing non-zero finite rank transformations has this form.
