Theorem 6' reformulates the structure theorem in the language of dual pairs $(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R}')$ with a non-degenerate bilinear form $g$. In this formulation, a linear transformation $A$ belongs to $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}' \mid \mathfrak{R})$ precisely when it admits a transpose $A'$ on $\mathfrak{R}'$ satisfying the adjoint relation $g(xA, y') = g(x, y'A')$. This dual space perspective is particularly convenient for studying anti-automorphisms, as the transpose mapping $A \mapsto A'$ provides a natural anti-isomorphism between the ring and its transpose ring.
