Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an irreducible ring of endomorphisms of a commutative group $\mathfrak{R}$ with $\mathfrak{A} \neq 0$. Let $\mathfrak{B}$ be the division ring of endomorphisms that commute with every $A \in \mathfrak{A}$ (given by Schur's Lemma). Then $\mathfrak{R}$ is a vector space over $\mathfrak{B}$ and $\mathfrak{A}$ is a dense ring of linear transformations in $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\mathfrak{B}$. Consequently, the two concepts---dense ring of linear transformations and irreducible ring of endomorphisms---are fully equivalent.
