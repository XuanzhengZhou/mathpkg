A weakly hermitian scalar product generalizes the notion of a hermitian form to division rings with an arbitrary anti-automorphism. It is characterized by the existence of a semi-linear automorphism $Q$ that relates the form to its conjugate transpose. This concept is the natural algebraic structure that emerges when a dense ring of linear transformations admits an anti-automorphism: the anti-automorphism corresponds precisely to the transpose mapping with respect to a weakly hermitian scalar product.
