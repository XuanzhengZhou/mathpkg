Schur's Lemma states that the commutant (centralizer) of an irreducible set of endomorphisms of a commutative group is a division ring. This is the fundamental result that converts an abstract irreducible ring of endomorphisms into a ring of linear transformations over a division ring: the division ring $\Delta$ is precisely $\mathfrak{B}$, and $\mathfrak{R}$ becomes a vector space over $\Delta$ with $\mathfrak{A}$ acting as linear transformations. Schur's Lemma is the essential first step in the Jacobson Density Theorem.
