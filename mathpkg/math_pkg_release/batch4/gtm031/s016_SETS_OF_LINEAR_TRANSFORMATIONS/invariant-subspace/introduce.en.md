Let $\Re$ be a finite dimensional vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ and let $\Omega$ be a set of linear transformations in $\Re$. A subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ of $\Re$ is said to be invariant under $\Omega$ if the image of $\mathfrak{S}$ under every transformation $A \in \Omega$ remains within $\mathfrak{S}$. This generalizes the notion of an invariant subspace of a single linear transformation to an arbitrary set of transformations. The problem of determining all invariant subspaces relative to a given set $\Omega$ is fundamental in the geometric study of linear transformations.
