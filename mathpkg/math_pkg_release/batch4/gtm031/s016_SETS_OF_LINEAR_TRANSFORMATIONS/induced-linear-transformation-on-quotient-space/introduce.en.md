When a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant under a linear transformation $A$, the action of $A$ descends to the quotient space $\Re/\mathfrak{S}$ in a natural way. The induced transformation $\bar{A}$ maps the coset $x + \mathfrak{S}$ to $xA + \mathfrak{S}$. This construction allows one to study the action of $A$ "modulo" the invariant subspace, reducing the problem to a lower-dimensional space. The induced transformation plays a central role in the analysis of reducible sets of linear transformations.
