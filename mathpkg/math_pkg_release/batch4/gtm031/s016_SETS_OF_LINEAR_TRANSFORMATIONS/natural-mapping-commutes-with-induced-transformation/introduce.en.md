When a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant under a linear transformation $A$, the induced transformation $\bar{A}$ on the quotient space is related to $A$ and the natural projection $P$ by the simple commutativity relation $P\bar{A} = AP$. This equation expresses precisely how $A$ descends to the quotient: applying $\bar{A}$ after projecting is the same as projecting after applying $A$. This relation is fundamental in the theory of representations and plays a role analogous to that of equivariance.
