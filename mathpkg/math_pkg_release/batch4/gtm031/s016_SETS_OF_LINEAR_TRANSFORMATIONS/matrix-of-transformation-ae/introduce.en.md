The reduced matrix form of a linear transformation $A$ relative to an invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ consists of three blocks. The upper-left block represents the restriction $A|_{\mathfrak{S}}$; the lower-right block represents the induced transformation $\bar{A}$ on $\bar{\Re}$; and the lower-left block represents the linking transformation $A_E$, which maps elements of the quotient space back into the invariant subspace. This decomposition provides a complete matrix-theoretic picture of how $A$ acts with respect to the subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ and its complement.
