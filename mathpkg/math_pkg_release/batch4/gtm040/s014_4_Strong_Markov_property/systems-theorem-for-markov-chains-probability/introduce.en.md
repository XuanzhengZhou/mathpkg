Theorem 4-10 is the first of two "systems theorems" that follow directly from the strong Markov property. It provides a practical computational tool: the probability of an event $p$ that depends only on outcomes beyond a random time $t$ can be expressed as a weighted sum over the possible states $k$ at time $t$. Each term is the product of the probability that the chain is in state $k$ at time $t$ and the probability of the corresponding shifted event $p'$ when the chain is started from state $k$. This theorem underpins many calculations in the theory of denumerable Markov chains.
