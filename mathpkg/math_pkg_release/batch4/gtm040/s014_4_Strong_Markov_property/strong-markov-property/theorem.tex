Let $\{x_n\}$ be a Markov chain with starting distribution $\pi$, let $t$ be a random time, let $q$ be a statement relative to $\mathcal{F}_t$, let $r$ be a statement relative to $\mathcal{T}_t$, and suppose $\Pr_\pi[q \land x_t = i] > 0$. Then

$$\Pr_\pi[r \mid q \land (x_t = i)] = \Pr_\pi[r \mid x_t = i] = \Pr_i[r']$$

where $\omega \in R$ if and only if $\omega_t \in R'$.

An equivalent way of stating the first equality of the conclusion is

$$\Pr_{\pi}[q \land r \mid x_t = i] = \Pr_{\pi}[q \mid x_t = i] \Pr_{\pi}[r \mid x_t = i].$$

This is the form in which the theorem asserts that if the present is known, then the past and future are independent.
