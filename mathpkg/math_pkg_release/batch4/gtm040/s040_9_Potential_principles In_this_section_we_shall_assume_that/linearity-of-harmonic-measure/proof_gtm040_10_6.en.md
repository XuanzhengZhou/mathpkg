For a typical cylinder statement $x_0 = i \land x_1 = j \land x_2 = k$, Definition 10-23 gives
$$\Pr^h[x_0 = i \land x_1 = j \land x_2 = k] = \pi_i P_{ij} P_{jk} h_k$$
$$= c_1 \pi_i P_{ij} P_{jk} h_k^{(1)} + c_2 \pi_i P_{ij} P_{jk} h_k^{(2)}$$
$$= c_1 \Pr^{h^{(1)}}[\ldots] + c_2 \Pr^{h^{(2)}}[\ldots].$$
Taking the statement $x_v \in E$ for a Borel set $E$ and noting that $x_v = x_v^h$, the result follows.
