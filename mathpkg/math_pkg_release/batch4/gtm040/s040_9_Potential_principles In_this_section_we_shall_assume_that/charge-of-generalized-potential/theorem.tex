Let $P$ be a nondegenerate normal chain, and let $0,1$ be chosen according to Proposition 9-102. Let $\mathcal{L}$ denote the collection of all finite sets containing both $0$ and $1$. Let $f$ be any function whose non-zero components are on a finite set $E$. Then $f$ is the \text{charge} of the generalized potential $g = -Kf$, with support $E$ and total charge $\alpha f$. If $f \geq 0$, then $g$ is a \text{pure potential}. Let $\mu$ be any signed measure whose non-zero components are on $E$. Then $\mu$ is the \text{charge} of the generalized potential measure $\nu = -\mu K$, with support $E$ and total charge $\mu 1$.
