We have $\nu^E P_S = \mu^E N P_S = \mu^E (N - I) = \nu^E - \mu^E$. Along any increasing sequence of sets $E_n$ with union $S$, $\nu^E$ increases to $\nu$. Hence by monotone convergence,
$$\nu P_S = \nu - \lim_n \mu^{E_n}.$$
This shows $\nu$ is superregular (1) and gives the decomposition (2). For (3), $\nu^E$ is the potential of $\mu^E$ since $\nu^E = \mu^E N$, and $\nu^E$ agrees with $\nu$ on $E$, so it is the balayage potential.
