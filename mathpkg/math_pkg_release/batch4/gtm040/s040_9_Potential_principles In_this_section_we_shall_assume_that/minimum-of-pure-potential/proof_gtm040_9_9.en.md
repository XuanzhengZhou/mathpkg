Since $g$ has total charge 1, by Proposition 9-109 we have $\lambda^E g = -k(E)$. The vector $\lambda^E$ is a probability vector with support in $E$, so $-k(E)$ is a convex combination of the values $g_i$ for $i \in E$. Hence it lies between the minimum and maximum of $g$ on $E$.
