It suffices to consider the function $B^E_j$ where $E$ is a finite set. If $\{E_k\}$ is an increasing sequence of finite sets with union $S$, and $E$ is contained in each $E_k$ (which can be arranged), then $B^{E_k}(x, \cdot)B^E_j = B^E_j$ for all $k$ sufficiently large, since $B^{E_k}$ acts as the identity on columns of $B^E$ when $E \subseteq E_k$. By Proposition 11-9, the left side converges to $T B^E_j$, giving $T B^E_j = B^E_j$. The result for general elementary continuous functions follows by linearity of $T$.
