Lemma 11-11 is a key technical step toward characterizing $T$-continuous functions. It shows that any $T$-continuous function $h$ can be approximated in the strong operator topology by convex combinations of the taboo probability operators $B^{E_k}$ applied to $h$. The proof uses Proposition 11-9 to get pointwise convergence, then dominated convergence for weak convergence, and finally Mazur's theorem (Dunford-Schwartz, p. 422) to upgrade to strong closure of the convex hull.
