The ladder process is a construction that extracts a new Markov chain from a sums of independent random variables Markov chain on the integers. It records the outcomes of the original chain only at those times when the process reaches a new maximum value, called positive step times. By the strong Markov property (Theorem 4-9), the resulting ladder process is itself a Markov chain whose transition probabilities depend only on the difference of states, inheriting the spatial homogeneity of the original chain.
