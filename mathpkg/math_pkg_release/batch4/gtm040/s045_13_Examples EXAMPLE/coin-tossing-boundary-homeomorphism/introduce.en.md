The coin tossing process records the number of heads obtained in $m$ tosses of a fair coin. Its Martin boundary is remarkably rich: it is homeomorphic to the entire unit interval $[0,1]$, with each $t \in [0,1]$ corresponding to a limiting frequency of heads. The kernel $K((m,i), t) = 2^m t^i(1-t)^{m-i}$ is the binomial probability of $i$ heads in $m$ tosses of a biased coin with heads probability $t$, scaled by $2^m$. That every boundary point is extreme follows from the Weierstrass Approximation Theorem: the moments $\int t^i d\nu(t)$ uniquely determine the measure $\nu$.
