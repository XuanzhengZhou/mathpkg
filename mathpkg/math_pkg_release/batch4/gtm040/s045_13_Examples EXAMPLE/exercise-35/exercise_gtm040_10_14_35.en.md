Problems 35 to 38 refer to a transient chain with absorbing states. As usual, we write the transition matrix in the form

$$P = \begin{pmatrix} I & 0 \\ R & Q \end{pmatrix},$$

where $I$ corresponds to the absorbing states and $Q$ to the transient states.

Put

$$P' = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ R & Q \end{pmatrix}.$$

Show that $P'$ has only transient states. Let $\pi$ be any starting distribution for $P'$ with $\pi N' > 0$ (where $N'$ is the potential kernel of $P'$). Compute the Martin kernel for $P'$ and relate it to that of $Q$.
