To apply transient entrance boundary theory to a recurrent chain $P$, one constructs an auxiliary transient chain $Q$ by making the process disappear after it reaches a distinguished state $0$. Since $Q$ is transient, its Martin entrance boundary can be analyzed using the methods of Chapter 10. The resulting boundary is shown to be independent of the choice of state $0$ (Proposition 11-1), making it a canonical object associated to the recurrent chain $P$.
