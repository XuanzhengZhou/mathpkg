For the $p$-$q$ random walk on the integers with $0 < q < p < 1$ and $p+q=1$, the hitting probabilities are

$$H_{ij} = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } i \leq j \\ (q/p)^{i-j} & \text{if } i \geq j, \end{cases}$$

and the potential kernel is

$$N_{ij} = \begin{cases} (p - q)^{-1} & \text{if } i \leq j \\ (q/p)^{i-j}(p-q)^{-1} & \text{if } i \geq j. \end{cases}$$

The exit boundary contains exactly two points, both extreme. The harmonic measure $\mu$ assigns unit mass to $+\infty$.
