For recurrent Markov chains, the usual boundary theory of Chapter 10 does not apply directly because every non-negative superregular function is constant. Instead, one constructs an associated transient chain $Q$ by forcing the process to disappear at a distinguished state $0$, then applies transient entrance boundary theory to $Q$. The resulting compact metric space $*S$ and its boundary $*S \setminus S$ are called the completed space and recurrent entrance boundary of $P$. This construction is canonical: Proposition 11-1 shows that it does not depend on the choice of state $0$.
