Proposition 11-1 establishes that the recurrent entrance boundary construction is canonical: although the associated transient chain $Q$ depends on the choice of the distinguished state $0$, the resulting completed space $*S$, the boundary $*S \setminus S$, and the extreme boundary points $B^e$ do not. This justifies speaking unambiguously of "the" completed space and entrance boundary of a recurrent chain $P$. The proof establishes an identity relating the potential kernels relative to two different distinguished states and shows that Cauchy sequences are preserved.
