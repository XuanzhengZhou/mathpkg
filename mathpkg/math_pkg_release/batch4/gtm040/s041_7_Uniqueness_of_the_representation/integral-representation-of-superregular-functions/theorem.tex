Every nonnegative superregular function $h$ has a unique representation
$$h_i = \int_{\bar{S}} K(i, x) \, d\nu(x)$$
with $\nu$ a finite Borel measure on $\bar{S}$. Moreover, $\bar{S}$ decomposes disjointly as $\bar{S} = S \cup B_e$, and any nonnegative superregular function $h$ corresponds to a unique finite measure $\nu$ on $\bar{S}$. If $h$ is normalized ($\pi h = 1$), then $\nu = \mu^h$.
