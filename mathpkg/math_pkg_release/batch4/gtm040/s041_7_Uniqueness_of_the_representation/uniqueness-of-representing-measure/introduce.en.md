If a normalized superregular function $h$ admits an integral representation $h_i = \int_S K(i,x) d\nu(x)$ with a probability measure $\nu$ concentrated on the exit space $\bar{S}$, then $\nu$ is uniquely determined: it must equal the harmonic measure $\mu^h$ of the $h$-process. This is the central uniqueness result of the section, establishing that the Martin boundary representation is canonical. The proof uses Lemma 10-39 to relate $\mu^h$ to an integral of $K$-process probabilities, then applies Proposition 10-35 to evaluate the inner integral as a characteristic function.
