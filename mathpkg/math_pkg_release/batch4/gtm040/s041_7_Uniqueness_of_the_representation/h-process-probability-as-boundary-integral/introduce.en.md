If a nonnegative superregular function $h$ is represented as $h_i = \int_S K(i, x) d\nu(x)$ for a probability measure $\nu$ concentrated on the exit space $\bar{S}$, then the $h$-process probability of any event $A$ equals the integral of the $K(\cdot, x)$-process probability of $A$ with respect to $\nu$. The proof uses Fubini's theorem to verify that $\pi h = 1$ (so the $h$-process is well defined), then constructs a finite measure $\sigma$ on $\mathcal{G}$ that agrees with $\Pr^h$ on cylinder sets, extending to all of $\mathcal{G}$ by the measure extension theorem.
