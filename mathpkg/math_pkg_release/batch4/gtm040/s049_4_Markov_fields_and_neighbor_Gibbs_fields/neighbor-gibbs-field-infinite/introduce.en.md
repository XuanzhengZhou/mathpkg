When the index set $T$ is countably infinite, a neighbor Gibbs field cannot be defined by an explicit probability measure as in the finite case. Instead, the definition relies on the local characteristics $\mu_a^{\Lambda}(\iota)$, which specify the conditional distribution at site $a$ given the configuration on the boundary $\Lambda \setminus \{a\}$. The potential $U_B$ contributes only when $a \in B \subset \bar{a}$, and the normalizing constant $Z$ sums over all possible local states at $a$.
