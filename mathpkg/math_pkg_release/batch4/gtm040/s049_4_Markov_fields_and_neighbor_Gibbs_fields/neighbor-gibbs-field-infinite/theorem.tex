An infinite random field $\{x_t\}_{t \in T}$ with state space $S$ is a \emph{neighbor Gibbs field} with neighbor potential $U$ if, for every $a \in T$ and every finite $\Lambda \supset \bar{a}$,

$$
\mu_a^{\Lambda}(\iota) = Z^{-1} \exp\left\{ \sum_{a \in B \subset \bar{a}} U_B(\iota) \right\},
$$

where $Z$ is the normalizing constant given by

$$
Z = \sum_{s \in S} \exp\left\{ \sum_{a \in B \subset \bar{a}} U_B(s' \iota) \right\},
$$

and $s' \iota$ is the modification of $\iota$ obtained by replacing $i_a$ with $s$.
