When the index set $T$ is infinite, the set $\mathcal{G}_V$ of Gibbs fields with a given neighbor potential $V$ may be empty, a singleton, or infinite. This contrasts with the finite case where $\mathcal{G}_V$ always contains exactly one field. Phase multiplicity (or phase transition) occurs when $|\mathcal{G}_V| = \infty$, modeled by the convex structure of $\mathcal{G}_V$. The extreme points $\mathcal{E}_V$ are the indecomposable Gibbs measures, and one seeks an integral representation of arbitrary Gibbs measures in terms of these extremes.
