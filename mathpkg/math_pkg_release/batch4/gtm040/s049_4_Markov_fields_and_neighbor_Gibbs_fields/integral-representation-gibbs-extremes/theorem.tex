There is a one-to-one correspondence $x \leftrightarrow \mu^x$ between the extreme Martin boundary $B_e$ and the class $\mathcal{E}_V$ of extreme Gibbs measures. For every $\mu \in \mathcal{G}_V$,

$$
\frac{\mu([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])}{\nu([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])}
= \int_{x \in B_e} \frac{\mu^x([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])}{\nu([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])} \; d\lambda^{\rho(\mu)}(x),
$$

where $\lambda^{\rho(\mu)}$ is harmonic measure for the $h$-process with $h = \rho(\mu)$, and

$$
\lambda^{\rho(\mu)}(E) = \mu\left(\left\{ \omega \mid t\text{-}\lim_{n \to \infty} \mu^{\kappa^n(\omega)} = \mu^x \text{ for some } x \in E \right\}\right)
$$

for every Borel set $E \subset B_e$.
