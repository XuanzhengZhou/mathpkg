For given $\mu \in \mathcal{G}_V$, $m > 0$, and fixed $(\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m) \in S^{\Lambda(m)}$, define

$$
\mu_n^+ = \max_{\kappa^n \in S^{K(n)}} \mu^{\kappa^n}([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m]),
\qquad
\mu_n^- = \min_{\kappa^n \in S^{K(n)}} \mu^{\kappa^n}([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m]).
$$

Then $\{\mu_n^+\}_{n \geq 1}$ is non-increasing and $\{\mu_n^-\}_{n \geq 1}$ is non-decreasing. Hence both limits $\lim_n \mu_n^+$ and $\lim_n \mu_n^-$ exist.
