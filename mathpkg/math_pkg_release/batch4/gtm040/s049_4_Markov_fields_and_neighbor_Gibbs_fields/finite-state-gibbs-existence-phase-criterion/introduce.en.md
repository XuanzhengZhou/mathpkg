When the state space $S$ is finite, the existence of at least one Gibbs field is guaranteed. The theorem provides a practical criterion for detecting phase multiplicity: compute the extremal conditional probabilities $\mu^{\kappa^n}([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])$ for configurations extending $\kappa^n$ on $K(n)$, then take the $\max$ and $\min$ limits as $n \to \infty$. Phase multiplicity occurs precisely when these upper and lower limits differ for some cylinder set. The limits exist because the sequences are monotone -- $\mu_n^-$ increases and $\mu_n^+$ decreases with $n$.
