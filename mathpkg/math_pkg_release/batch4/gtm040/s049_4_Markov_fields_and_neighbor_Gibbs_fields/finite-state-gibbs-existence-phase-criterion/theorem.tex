If $S$ is finite, then $\mathcal{G}_V \neq \varnothing$. Moreover, the limits

$$
\mu^+([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m]) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \max_{\kappa^n \in S^{K(n)}} \mu^{\kappa^n}([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])
$$

and

$$
\mu^-([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m]) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \min_{\kappa^n \in S^{K(n)}} \mu^{\kappa^n}([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])
$$

exist for all $m \geq 0$ and $(\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m) \in S^{\Lambda(m)}$. There is phase multiplicity for $V$ if and only if

$$
\mu^+([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m]) \neq \mu^-([\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m])
$$

for some $m$ and $(\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^m)$.
