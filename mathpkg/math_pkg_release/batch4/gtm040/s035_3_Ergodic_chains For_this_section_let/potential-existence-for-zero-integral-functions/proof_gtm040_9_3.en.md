We shall prove the dual statements. Observe that

$$[\mu N^{(n)}]_i = \sum_k \mu_k N^{(n)}_{ki} = -\sum_k \mu_k [N^{(n)}_{ii} - N^{(n)}_{ki}]$$

since $\sum_k \mu_k N^{(n)}_{ii} = N^{(n)}_{ii}(\mu 1) = 0$. Now $N^{(n)}_{ii} - N^{(n)}_{ki} \leq M_{ki}$ by Lemma 9-48, and $\alpha_i \sum_k \mu_k M_{ki} = (\mu C)_i$ is finite by hypothesis. Therefore $\mu N^{(n)}$ is bounded in $n$, and the limit $\nu = \lim \mu N^{(n)} = -\mu C$ exists. The condition $\mu \hat{G}$ finite-valued ensures additional boundedness of $\nu$.
