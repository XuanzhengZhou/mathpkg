If $f$ is a function with $\alpha f = 0$ and $Gf$ finite-valued, then $f$ is a charge and its potential $g$ is such that $g = -Gf$ and $\alpha g$ is finite. If, in addition, $\hat{C}f$ is finite-valued, then $g$ is bounded.

Dually, if $\mu$ is a signed measure with $\mu 1 = 0$ and $\mu C$ finite-valued, then $\mu$ is a charge and its potential $\nu$ is such that $\nu = -\mu C$ and $\nu 1$ is finite; if, in addition, $\mu \hat{G}$ is finite-valued, then $\nu$ is bounded by a multiple of $\alpha$.
