This proposition establishes fundamental identities linking the mean first passage time matrix $M$ with the taboo occupation time matrix $^{k}\!N$ and the stationary distribution $\alpha$. The first identity relates the difference $M_{ik} + M_{kj} - M_{ij}$ to the taboo expected occupation time $^{k}\!N_{ij}$ divided by $\alpha_j$. The second identity connects $M_{ik} + M_{ki}$ with the diagonal taboo occupation time. These formulas are derived by combining Proposition 9-45 with the expression $C_{ij} = M_{ij}\alpha_j$.
