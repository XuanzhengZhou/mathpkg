This proposition is the Principle of Balayage for total-charge-zero potentials in noncyclic ergodic chains. Given a function $h$ with finite stationary mean and finite equilibrium measure on $E$, there exists a unique potential $g$ supported on $E$ that differs from $h$ by only a constant on $E$. The construction $g = B^E h - (\lambda^E h)1$ shows that $g$ is obtained by sweeping $h$ onto $E$ and then subtracting the equilibrium mean.
