This definition introduces the concept of ergodic degree, which classifies recurrent Markov chains according to the finiteness of the moments of their return times. A chain has ergodic degree $r$ if the $r$-th moment of the return time to a reference state is finite but the $(r+1)$-st moment is infinite. Chains for which all moments are finite have infinite ergodic degree. The definition is well-posed because Proposition 9-68 guarantees the classification does not depend on the choice of the reference state $0$, and $b_0^{(0)} = 1$ ensures every recurrent chain has at least degree $0$.
