By Proposition 1-57, $P^n B^E \rightarrow AB^E$. Hence $\lambda^E = \alpha B^E$ and $\lambda^E 1 = \alpha B^E 1 = \alpha 1 = 1$, so that every set $E$ is small. The formula for $^{E}\!\nu$ follows dually. For the set $S$ of all states, $B^S = I$ (since no states are avoided), so $\lambda^S = \alpha I = \alpha$.
