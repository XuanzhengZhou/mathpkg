In a noncyclic ergodic chain, the difference between the expected occupation time of state $j$ starting from $j$ and starting from $i$ is nonnegative and bounded above by the mean first passage time $M_{ij}$. As $n$ tends to infinity, this difference converges to the product $M_{ij}\alpha_j$, where $\alpha_j$ is the stationary distribution weight at state $j$. This lemma provides a fundamental inequality used throughout the theory of ergodic chains.
