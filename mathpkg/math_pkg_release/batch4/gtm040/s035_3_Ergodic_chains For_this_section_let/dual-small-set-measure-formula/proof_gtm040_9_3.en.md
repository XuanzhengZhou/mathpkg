By Proposition 6-16,

$$\alpha_j \hat{B}_{ji}^E = \alpha_i \,^{E}\!\bar{N}_{ij} \quad \text{for } i \text{ in } E.$$

Summing on $j$ gives

$$\hat{\lambda}_i^E = \sum_j \alpha_j \hat{B}_{ji}^E = \alpha_i \sum_j \,^{E}\!\bar{N}_{ij} = \alpha_i \bar{M}_{iE},$$

where the last equality uses the fact that the sum of taboo expected occupation times from $i$ avoiding $E$ before the first step equals the taboo mean time to reach $E$.
