This proposition provides a concrete formula for the dual equilibrium measure $\hat{\lambda}_i^E$ in an ergodic chain: for a state $i$ inside the set $E$, it equals the stationary weight $\alpha_i$ multiplied by the taboo mean time $\bar{M}_{iE}$ to hit $E$ (avoiding $i$ initially). The proof uses the duality relation between taboo quantities in $P$ and $\hat{P}$. This result shows that Proposition 6-24 (which deals with a related identity) is essentially the assertion that all sets are small in ergodic chains.
