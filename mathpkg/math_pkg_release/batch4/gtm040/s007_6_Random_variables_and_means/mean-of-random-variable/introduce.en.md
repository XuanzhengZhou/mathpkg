The mean (or expectation) of a random variable is defined simply as its Lebesgue integral with respect to the underlying probability measure. This definition elegantly unifies probability theory with measure theory: all properties of Lebesgue integrals---linearity, monotone convergence, dominated convergence---carry over immediately to means. Notable consequences include the interchange of infinite sum and mean for nonnegative random variables, and the convergence of means under bounded convergence.
