Let $X$ be the unit interval. Let $\mathcal{F}$ consist of all finite unions of finite intervals (with or without endpoints). We classify a point as an interval of length $0$. The measure to be constructed in this problem is called Lebesgue measure, and the Borel field is the class of all Borel sets.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] Show that $\mathcal{F}$ is a field.
\item[(b)] Show that every set $A$ in $\mathcal{F}$ can be written as a finite disjoint union of intervals $A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_n$.
\item[(c)] If $A$ is decomposed as in part (b), we define
$$\nu(A) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \ell(A_k),$$
where $\ell$ denotes length. Show that $\nu$ is consistently defined and that $\nu$ is a non-negative additive set function.
\item[(d)] Show that if $A$ is a finite union of intervals and if $\epsilon > 0$ is given, then there is a finite union $K$ of closed intervals and a finite union $G$ of open intervals such that $K \subset A$, $A \subset G$, and $\nu(G) - \nu(K) < \epsilon$.
\end{enumerate}
