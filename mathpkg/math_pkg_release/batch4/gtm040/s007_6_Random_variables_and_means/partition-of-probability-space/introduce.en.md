A partition of a probability space is a denumerable collection of disjoint, exhaustive, measurable subsets (called cells). Partitions are the natural structure for defining conditional probabilities and conditional means relative to a family of alternatives. The cells generate a sub-Borel-field $\mathcal{R}^*$ contained in the full Borel field $\mathcal{B}$, making them compatible with the measure-theoretic framework. Typical examples arise from grouping outcomes according to the value of a denumerable-valued random variable or according to events in a fixed $\mathcal{B}_n$.
