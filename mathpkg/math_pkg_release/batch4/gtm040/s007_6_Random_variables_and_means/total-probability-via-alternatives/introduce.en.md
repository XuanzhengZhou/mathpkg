Corollary 2-12 is the total probability formula for a denumerable set of alternatives. It states that the probability of any statement $p$ can be decomposed as a weighted sum of conditional probabilities $\Pr[p \mid q_i]$, with weights equal to the probabilities of the alternatives $\Pr[q_i]$. Equivalently, it says that the probability of $p$ is the sum of probabilities of its conjunctions $\Pr[p \wedge q_i]$ over the partition. The proof follows immediately by applying Proposition 2-11 (the conditional mean expansion) to the characteristic function of the set $P$.
