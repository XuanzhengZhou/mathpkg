In Problem 2, let $\mathcal{B}_n = \mathcal{F}_n$. Which of the following $f_n$ form a denumerable stochastic process $(f_n, \mathcal{B}_n)$?
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $f_n =$ the $n$th outcome.
\item[(b)] $f_n =$ time at which $n$th "$3$" is selected.
\item[(c)] $f_n = s_n =$ sum of the first $n$ numbers selected.
\item[(d)] $f_n = \frac{s_n - a}{\sqrt{cn}}$, where $a$ and $c$ are constants.
\end{enumerate}
