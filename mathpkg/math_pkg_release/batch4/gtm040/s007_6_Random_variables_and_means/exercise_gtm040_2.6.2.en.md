Consider the experiment of selecting "$1$" with probability $\frac{1}{2}$, "$2$" with probability $\frac{1}{3}$, or "$3$" with probability $\frac{1}{6}$. If this experiment is repeated infinitely often, show that the probability of selecting "$1$" only finitely often is $0$.
