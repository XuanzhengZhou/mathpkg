For coin tossing, let $f =$ the number of "heads" in the first three tosses. Let $q_i$ be the statement that there were exactly $i$ "heads" in the first two tosses ($i = 0, 1, 2$). Find $M[f]$, $M[f \mid q_0]$, $M[f \mid q_1]$, and $M[f \mid q_2]$. Verify that the first of these is a linear combination of the last three with appropriate probabilities as weights.
