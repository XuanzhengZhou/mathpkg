A set of alternatives is a denumerable family of mutually exclusive and exhaustive statements about a stochastic process---equivalently, their truth sets form a partition of the sample space. This concept provides the linguistic bridge between partitions of the probability space and logical statements about outcomes. The complete additivity of the integral immediately yields the decomposition $M[f] = \sum_i \int_{Q_i} f \, d\mu$, a foundational identity for conditioning on alternatives.
