Proposition 2-11 is the law of total expectation (also called the law of total mean) for a denumerable set of alternatives. It expresses the overall mean $M[f]$ as a probability-weighted average of the conditional means $M[f \mid q_i]$. The proof follows directly from the definition of conditional mean, the complete additivity of the Lebesgue integral over the partition $\{Q_i\}$, and the normalization factor $1/\Pr[q_i]$ for each cell of positive measure.
