This proposition gives a simple and elegant formula for the mean return time to a state in a recurrent chain: $\bar{M}_{ii} = 1/\alpha_i$, where $\alpha$ is the unique (up to scale) positive regular measure. Combined with Corollary 6-21, this shows that the regular measure $\alpha$ has direct probabilistic meaning -- the weight $\alpha_i$ is inversely proportional to the expected time to return to state $i$. States with larger measure are visited more frequently in the long run.
