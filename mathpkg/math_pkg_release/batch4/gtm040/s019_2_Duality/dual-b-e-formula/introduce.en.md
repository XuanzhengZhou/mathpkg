This proposition gives an explicit formula for the dual of the matrix $B^E$, which records the expected number of visits to states before entering the set $E$. The dual $\text{dual } B^E$ has a simple probabilistic interpretation: for states $i$ in $E$, its $(i,j)$ entry equals the mean number of times the dual process visits $j$ before returning to $E$. This result is key to deriving probabilistic interpretations for the ratio $\alpha_j / \alpha_i$ via duality.
