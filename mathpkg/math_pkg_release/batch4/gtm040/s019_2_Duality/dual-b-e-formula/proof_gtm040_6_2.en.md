Let $N = \sum Q^n$. Then

$$B^E = \begin{pmatrix} I & 0 \\ NR & 0 \end{pmatrix},$$

so that

$$\text{dual } B^E = \begin{pmatrix} I & \hat{U} \hat{N} \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} {}^E \hat{N} \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}.$$

If we define ${}^E \bar{N}$ to be a matrix indexed by $E$ and $S$ whose $i$-$j$th entry is the mean number of times starting in $i$ that the process is in $j$ before returning to $E$, then we may rewrite the result as

$$\text{dual } B^E = \begin{pmatrix} {}^E \hat{N} \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}.$$
