This proposition catalogs the fundamental algebraic properties of the duality operator. It establishes that duality is an involution (applying it twice returns the original object), respects linearity (addition and scalar multiplication), reverses the order of multiplication, and preserves the identity and zero matrices. Duality is order-preserving: if $X \geq Y$ entrywise, then the same holds for their duals. Crucially, duality interchanges the roles of $P$-superregular row and column vectors with their $\hat{P}$-counterparts, and relates the unit vector to the superregular measure via $\text{dual } 1 = \alpha$.
