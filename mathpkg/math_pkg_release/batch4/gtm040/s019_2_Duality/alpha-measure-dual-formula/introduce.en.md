This proposition establishes a fundamental relationship between the superregular measure $\alpha$, the expected number of visits ${}^E \bar{N}_{ij}$ to state $j$ before entering set $E$, and the dual hitting probability $\hat{h}_j^E$. The weighted sum $\sum_{i \in E} \alpha_i {}^E \bar{N}_{ij}$ equals $\alpha_j \hat{h}_j^E$, linking three central objects: the measure $\alpha$, the visit counts of the original chain, and the hitting probabilities of the dual chain. This identity is the key to obtaining probabilistic interpretations for the ratio $\alpha_j/\alpha_i$.
