The proof of this variational property is not given in the present section, as this statement appears as a carryover summary item (9) from the preceding section on potential theory. The result follows from the standard theory of equilibrium potentials: the equilibrium potential $u_E$ of an equilibrium set $E$ has total charge equal to the capacity $\text{cap}(E)$, and its energy $\|u_E\|^2$ equals $\text{cap}(E)$. For any other potential $g$ whose charge $\mu$ satisfies $\text{supp}(\mu) \subseteq E$ and $\mu(E) = \text{cap}(E)$, one shows via the Gauss quadratic form that $\|g\|^2 \geq \|u_E\|^2$, with equality only when $g = u_E$.
