Proposition 7-4 establishes the first half of the equivalence between Brownian motion and potential theory. It shows that the potential $g$ of any charge $\mu$ can be recovered from the Brownian transition densities $f(x,t)$ by integrating over time and letting the upper limit tend to infinity. Formally, the potential operator is $\lim_{T \to \infty} \int_0^T P^t dt$, expressed as a time integral of the semigroup of transition operators. The proof uses Fubini's theorem, a change of variables, and the monotone convergence theorem to identify the Newtonian potential kernel $\frac{1}{2\pi|x-y|}$.
