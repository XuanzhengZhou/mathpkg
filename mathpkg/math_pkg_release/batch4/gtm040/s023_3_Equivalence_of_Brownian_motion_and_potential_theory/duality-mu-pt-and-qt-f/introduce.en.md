This lemma states a fundamental duality between the action of the transition semigroup $P^t$ on measures and the action of the dual semigroup $Q^t$ on continuous functions vanishing at infinity. The relation $\int f\, d(\mu P^t) = \int (Q^t f)\, d\mu$ holds for all finite signed measures $\mu \in M$ and all $f \in C_0$. Combined with the fact that a set function in $M$ is completely determined by its effect on $C_0$, this duality shows that $Q^t$ together with the integral equation completely determine $P^t$. This provides the key to recovering the semigroup from the potential operator.
