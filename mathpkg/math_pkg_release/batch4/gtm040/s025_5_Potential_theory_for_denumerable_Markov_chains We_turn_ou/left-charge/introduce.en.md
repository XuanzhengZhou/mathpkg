In potential theory for denumerable Markov chains, a left charge is a row vector $\mu$ whose total mass $\mu\mathbf{1}$ is well-defined and finite, and for which the sequence of partial sums $\mu(I + P + \cdots + P^n)$ converges to a finite-valued row vector $\nu$. The limiting row vector $\nu$ is called the potential measure of the charge $\mu$. This notion is the Markov chain analog of a charge in classical potential theory — a signed measure that is the difference of two finite measures — and serves as the source term whose potential is obtained by summing the iterates of $P$ against $\mu$.
