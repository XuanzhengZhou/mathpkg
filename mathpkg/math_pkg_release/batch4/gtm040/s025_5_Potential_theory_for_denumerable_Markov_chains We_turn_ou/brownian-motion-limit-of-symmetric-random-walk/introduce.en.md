This proposition establishes that Brownian motion in one dimension arises as a scaling limit of the symmetric random walk. Consider a random walk on the line whose states are points of the form $j 2^{-k}$ ($j \in \mathbb{Z}$), with transition probability $\frac{1}{2}$ to each neighbor. When space is scaled by $2^{-k}$ and time by $4^{-k}$, the distribution of the walk at a diadic rational time $t$ converges, as $k \to \infty$, to the distribution of Brownian motion at the same time $t$. The proof relies on the Central Limit Theorem.
