\textbf{Lemma 12-39.} Let $Q' > 0$ be a strictly positive matrix on a finite set $S$. Then there exist a strictly positive vector $\bar{h} = (\bar{h}_i)_{i \in S} > 0$ and a constant $\bar{c} > 0$ such that
$$
Q' \bar{h} = \bar{c} \bar{h}.
$$
Moreover, the matrix $Q = (Q_{ij})_{i,j \in S}$ defined by
$$
Q_{ij} = \frac{Q'_{ij} \bar{h}_j}{\bar{c} \bar{h}_i} \quad \text{for all } i, j \in S
$$
is a stochastic matrix (i.e., $\sum_j Q_{ij} = 1$ for all $i$).
