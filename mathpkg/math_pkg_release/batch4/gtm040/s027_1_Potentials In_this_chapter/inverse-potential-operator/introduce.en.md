Theorem 8-4 establishes that the operator $I - P$ is the left-inverse of the potential operator $N$: applying $I-P$ to a potential recovers its charge. Together with Theorem 8-3, this yields a one-to-one correspondence between charges and potentials. A potential is regular at all states where its charge vanishes.
