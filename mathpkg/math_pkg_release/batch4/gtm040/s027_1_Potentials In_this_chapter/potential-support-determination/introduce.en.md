Proposition 8-19 is a crucial reduction: if the charge $f$ of a potential $g$ is supported on $E$, then the potential is completely determined by its values on $E$ via $g = B^E g$. Moreover, the charge on $E$ can be recovered as $f_E = (I - P^E)g_E$, and the potential satisfies the reduced equation $g_E = N_E f_E$. This allows potential-theoretic problems to be analyzed on the support of the charge.
