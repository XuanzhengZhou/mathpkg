Corollary 8-9 states that every potential $g$ is eventually annihilated by the iterated transition operator: $P^n g \to 0$ as $n \to \infty$. This is the key asymptotic property distinguishing potentials from general superregular functions and is an immediate specialization of Corollary 8-8. It also underlies the intuitive interpretation that the $h$-process for a potential $h$ tends to follow paths that eventually disappear.
