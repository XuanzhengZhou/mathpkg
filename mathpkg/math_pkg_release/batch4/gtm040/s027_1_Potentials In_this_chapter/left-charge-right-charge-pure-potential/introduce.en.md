For a transient Markov chain $P$ with distinguished positive superregular measure $\alpha$, a signed measure $\mu$ with finite $\mu 1$ is a left charge if its Cesàro sum under $P$ converges to a finite-valued potential measure $\nu$. Dually, a function $f$ with finite $\alpha f$ is a right charge if its Cesàro sum converges to a potential function $g$. A pure potential is simply the potential of a non-negative charge. The finiteness conditions involving $\alpha$ and $1$ serve as the natural analogs of integrability in the classical potential theory of Brownian motion.
