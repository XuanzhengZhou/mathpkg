Under the finiteness condition $\mu 1 < \infty$ (or its dual $\alpha f < \infty$), the product with the fundamental matrix $N$ remains finite-valued. This follows from the inequality $N_{ij} = H_{ij} N_{jj} \leq N_{jj}$, which bounds each entry of $N$ by a diagonal term. Lemma 8-2 ensures that the potential operators $\mu N$ and $Nf$ are well-defined for charges, a prerequisite for the subsequent theory.
