We have
$$N_{ij} = H_{ij} N_{jj} \leq N_{jj}.$$
Thus
$$|(\mu N)_j| \leq \sum_i |\mu_i| N_{ij} \leq N_{jj} \sum_i |\mu_i|.$$
Since $\mu 1 = \sum_i \mu_i$ is finite, $\sum_i |\mu_i|$ is finite, and $\mu N$ is finite-valued. The second statement follows by duality: apply the first result to $\hat{P}$, the $\alpha$-dual of $P$, noting that all states are transient in $\hat{P}$ and $\hat{P} = P$.
