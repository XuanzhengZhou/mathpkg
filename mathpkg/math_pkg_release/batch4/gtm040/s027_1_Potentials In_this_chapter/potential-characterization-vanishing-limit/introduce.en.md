Corollary 8-8 gives a clean necessary and sufficient condition for a function $h$ satisfying $(I-P)h = f$ (with $\alpha f$ finite) to be a potential: the iterates $P^n h$ must tend to zero. This follows from the Riesz decomposition $h = Nf + \lim P^n h$: if the regular part vanishes, $h$ is exactly the potential of its charge $f$. Conversely, if $\lim P^n h \neq 0$, then $h$ cannot be a potential because its charge would still be $f$ by Theorem 8-4.
