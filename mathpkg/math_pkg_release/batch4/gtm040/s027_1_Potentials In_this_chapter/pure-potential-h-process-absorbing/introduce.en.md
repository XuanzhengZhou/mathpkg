Proposition 8-14 reveals the key geometric insight behind the $h$-process: when $h = g$ is a strictly positive pure potential with support $E$, the transformed chain $P^*$ has the constant function $1$ as a potential and its enlarged chain is absorbing. This means the $h$-process reaches the support $E$ with probability one. The underlying intuition is that the $h$-process follows paths where $h$ is large, but since $P^n h \to 0$ for a potential, these paths eventually disappear.
