Proposition 8-16 catalogs the essential properties of the balayage operator $B^E$ (the hitting matrix of $E$) applied to a non-negative function $h$. The result $\bar{h} = B^E h$ coincides with $h$ on $E$, is dominated by $h$ everywhere, and is superregular. Moreover, it is the minimal non-negative superregular function that dominates $h$ on $E$ — the "réduite" (reduced function) of classical potential theory. Off $E$, $\bar{h}$ is regular, meaning its charge $\bar{f}$ vanishes on the complement.
