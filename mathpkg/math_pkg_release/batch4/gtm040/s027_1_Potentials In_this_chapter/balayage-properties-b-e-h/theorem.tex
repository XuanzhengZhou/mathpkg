Let $h$ be non-negative and finite-valued. Define $\bar{h} = B^E h$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\bar{h} = h$ on $E$ and $\bar{h} \leq h$ everywhere.
\item $\bar{h}$ is the pointwise infimum of all non-negative superregular functions which dominate $h$ on $E$.
\item If $\bar{f} = (I - P)\bar{h}$, then $\bar{f}_E = (I - P^E)h_E \geq 0$ and $\bar{f}_{\tilde{E}} = 0$. Thus $\bar{h}$ is regular on $\tilde{E}$ and is superregular everywhere.
\item If $E \subset F$, then $B^E h \leq B^F h$.
\end{enumerate}
