A general potential $g = Nf$ with signed charge $f$ decomposes as $g = Nf^+ - Nf^-$, the difference of two pure potentials. This reduction is fundamental: it implies that for many purposes one may study only pure potentials (those with non-negative charges). Moreover, a potential is superregular precisely when it is a pure potential (by Theorem 8-4).
