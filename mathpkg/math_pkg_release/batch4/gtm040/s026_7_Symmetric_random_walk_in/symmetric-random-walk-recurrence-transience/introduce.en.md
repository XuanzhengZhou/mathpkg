The symmetric random walk in $n$ dimensions is a fundamental model in probability theory, serving as a discrete analogue of Brownian motion. This proposition provides a complete classification of its recurrence and transience behavior based on the spatial dimension: the walk returns to its starting point infinitely often with probability one in dimensions one and two, but escapes to infinity almost surely in all higher dimensions. The result mirrors the well-known behavior of Brownian motion and relies on a detailed analysis of return probabilities $P_{00}^{(2n)}$ and their asymptotic estimates.
