For a Markov chain that represents sums of independent random variables on the integers, if all pairs of states belong to a single communicating class, then the probability of ever reaching any subset $E$ of states is either $0$ or $1$, regardless of the starting state $i$. This dichotomy follows from the independence structure and the fact that all states communicate, forcing tail-event-like behavior on the absorbing probabilities.
