A Markov chain on the integers is said to represent sums of independent random variables when its transition probabilities $p_k = P(i, i+k)$ depend only on the increment $k$ and not on the starting state $i$. The set of $k$ for which $p_k > 0$ forms the $k$-values, and under the assumption that their greatest common divisor is one, the chain is aperiodic in the spatial sense. This framework captures the classical one-dimensional random walk as a Markov chain with i.i.d. steps.
