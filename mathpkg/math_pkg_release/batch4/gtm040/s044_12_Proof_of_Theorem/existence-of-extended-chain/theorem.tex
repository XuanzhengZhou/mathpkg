Let $P$ be a substochastic matrix on the countable state space $S$, and let $\bar{P} = P^*_{S \cup \{b\}}$ be the enlarged transition matrix with absorbing state $b$. Let $\nu$ be a positive $P$-superregular measure on $S$. Then there exists an extended stochastic process $\{x_n\}_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ with state space $S \cup \{a\} \cup \{b\}$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] For each finite set $E \subseteq S$, the set of paths on which $E$ is ever entered has positive measure.
\item[(2)] $\displaystyle\lim_{n \to -\infty} \Pr[x_{-l} \in E \text{ for some } l \geq n] = 0$ for every finite $E \subseteq S$.
\item[(3)] For each finite $E \subseteq S$, the process watched starting in $E$ is a Markov chain with transition matrix $\bar{P}$ and starting distribution $\mu^E$, the balayage charge of $\nu$ on $E$.
\item[(4)] $\nu_j$ equals the expected number of times the process is in state $j \in S$.
\end{enumerate}

Moreover, the total measure of the process satisfies the Kolmogorov consistency conditions on cylinder sets, and the vector $\nu$ of mean times determines the process uniquely up to measure-theoretic isomorphism.
