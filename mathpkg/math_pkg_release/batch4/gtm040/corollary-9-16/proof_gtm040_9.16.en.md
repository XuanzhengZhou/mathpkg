**Proof:** If $g$ is a potential, then $P^n g \rightarrow 0$ by conclusion (3) of Theorem 9-15 and $f$ is the charge by conclusion (2). Hence $\alpha f$ is finite by definition. Conversely, if $(I - P) g = f$, we have by induction
$$g = P^n g + (I + \cdots + P^{n-1}) f.$$
If $P^n g \rightarrow 0$, then $g = \lim [(I + P + \cdots + P^{n-1}) f]$; if $\alpha f$ is finite, then $g$ is a potential by definition.
