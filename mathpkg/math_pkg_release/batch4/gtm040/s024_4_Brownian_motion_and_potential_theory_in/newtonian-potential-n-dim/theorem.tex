Let $\mu = \mu^+ - \mu^-$ be the difference of two finite measures on $\mathbb{R}^n$. The potential $g(x)$ of $\mu$ is defined by
$$g(x) = \begin{cases}
\displaystyle -\int_{\mathbb{R}^1} |x-y| \, d\mu(y) & \text{in dimension } 1 \\[12pt]
\displaystyle 2 \int_{\mathbb{R}^2} \log |x-y| \, d\mu(y) & \text{in dimension } 2 \\[12pt]
\displaystyle c_n \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} \frac{1}{|x-y|^{n-2}} \, d\mu(y) & \text{in dimension } n \geq 3,
\end{cases}$$
where
$$c_n = \frac{1}{2} \pi^{-n/2} \, \Gamma\!\left(\frac{1}{2}(n-2)\right).$$
In dimension $n \geq 3$, $g(x)$ is necessarily finite a.e.; in dimensions 1 and 2, $g$ is assumed to be finite a.e.
