The $n$-dimensional Brownian motion operator $P^t$ generalizes the transition operator for Brownian motion to arbitrary dimension $n$. For each $t > 0$, the operator maps a finite measure $\mu$ on $\mathbb{R}^n$ to the measure $\mu P^t$, whose density at $x$ is given by the convolution of $\mu$ with the Gaussian heat kernel $(2\pi t)^{-n/2} e^{-|x-y|^2/2t}$. This operator encodes the probability distribution of a Brownian particle after time $t$ when the initial distribution is $\mu$.
