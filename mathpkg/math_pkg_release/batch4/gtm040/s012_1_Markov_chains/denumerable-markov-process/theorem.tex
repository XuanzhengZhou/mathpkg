Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{B}, \mu)$ be a sequence space with a denumerable stochastic process $\{x_n\}$ from $\Omega$ to a denumerable state space $S$. The process is called a \textbf{denumerable Markov process} if
$$\Pr[x_{n+1} = c_{n+1} \mid x_0 = c_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_n = c_n] = \Pr[x_{n+1} = c_{n+1} \mid x_n = c_n]$$
for any $n$ and for any $c_0, \ldots, c_{n+1}$ such that $\Pr[x_0 = c_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_n = c_n] > 0$. This condition is known as the \textbf{Markov property}.
