The starting distribution $\pi$ (a row vector with $\pi_i = \Pr[x_0 = i]$) and the transition matrix $P$ (with $P_{ij} = \Pr[x_{n+1} = j \mid x_n = i]$) completely specify a denumerable Markov chain. The matrix $P$ is sub-stochastic: entries are nonnegative and row sums are at most 1, allowing for the possibility that the process may terminate.
