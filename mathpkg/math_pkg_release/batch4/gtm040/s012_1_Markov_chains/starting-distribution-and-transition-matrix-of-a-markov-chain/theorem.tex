For any Markov chain we define on the state space $S$ a row vector $\pi$ and a square matrix $P$ by
$$\pi_i = \Pr[x_0 = i], \qquad P_{ij} = \Pr[x_{n+1} = j \mid x_n = i] \text{ where } \Pr[x_n = i] > 0.$$
The vector $\pi$ is the \textbf{starting distribution} and the matrix $P$ is the \textbf{transition matrix}. They satisfy $\pi \geq 0$, $\pi 1 = 1$, and $P \geq 0$, $P1 \leq 1$.
