For the basic example $P$, the only $P$-regular functions are the constant functions. Dually, only multiples of $\beta$ are regular signed measures for $\hat{P}$. Since $P$ has no regular signed measures, $\hat{P}$ has no regular functions. Consequently, the non-negative superregular functions of $P$ are pure potentials plus non-negative constants, while for $\hat{P}$ only pure potentials are non-negative superregular functions.
