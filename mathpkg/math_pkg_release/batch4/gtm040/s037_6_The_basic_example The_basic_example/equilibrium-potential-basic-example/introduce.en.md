The equilibrium potential formulas for the basic example are remarkably explicit. For the forward chain $P$, a finite set's equilibrium charge is concentrated entirely at its rightmost element $m$, with magnitude $\beta_\infty/\beta_m$, and the hitting probability decays geometrically for states beyond $m$. For the reverse chain $\hat{P}$, the equilibrium charge is concentrated at the leftmost element, reflecting the deterministic leftward motion of the reverse process.
