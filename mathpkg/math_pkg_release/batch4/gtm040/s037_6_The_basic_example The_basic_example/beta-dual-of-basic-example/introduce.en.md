The beta-dual (or reverse chain) of the basic example reveals a striking asymmetry: while the forward chain $P$ can only move right or jump back to $0$, the reverse chain $\hat{P}$ marches deterministically leftward until hitting $0$, from which it redistributes according to the differences $\beta_j - \beta_{j+1}$. This structural difference explains many dualities in the potential theory, including why $P$ and $\hat{P}$ have fundamentally different equilibrium sets and regular functions.
