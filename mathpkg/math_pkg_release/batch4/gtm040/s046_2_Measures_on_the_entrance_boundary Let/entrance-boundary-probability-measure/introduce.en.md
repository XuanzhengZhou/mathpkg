Proposition 11-2 establishes a fundamental correspondence between admissible row vectors and probability measures on the entrance (Martin) boundary. For any row vector $\nu$ satisfying the three admissibility conditions, there exists a unique probability measure $\beta^\nu$ on the Borel sets of the augmented state space $S \cup B^e$ such that $\nu$ is recovered as the integral of the Martin kernel ${}_0N(x,\cdot)$ against $\beta^\nu$. This result extends the Riesz-type representation already established for superregular vectors via the $J$-kernel, exploiting the relationship $\bar{\nu}_0 = 0$ to convert the $J$-kernel integral into a ${}_0N$-kernel integral.
