The harmonic measure $\beta$ is the canonical probability measure on the entrance (Martin) boundary of a normal Markov chain. It is defined by applying Proposition 11-2 to the particular admissible row vector $\nu = G_{0\cdot}$, which Lemma 11-6 guarantees is admissible. The name "harmonic measure" reflects the fact that $\beta$ encodes the asymptotic entrance distribution of the chain: it is the weak-star limit of the normalized entrance measures $\lambda^{E_k}$ as finite sets $E_k$ expand to the full state space. The harmonic measure plays a role analogous to that of the Poisson boundary measure for random walks, providing the representing measure for the integral representation of the potential kernel.
