For a normal Markov chain $P$, the **harmonic measure** $\beta$ is defined to be the probability measure $\beta = \beta^\nu$ of Proposition 11-2 corresponding to the admissible row vector $\nu = G_{0\cdot}$, the $0$th row of the potential matrix. Thus $\beta$ is the unique probability measure on the Borel sets of $S \cup B^e$ such that
$$G_{0j} = \int_{S \cup B^e} {}_0N(x, j) \, d\beta(x)$$
for all states $j$. Theorem 11-7 below shows that $\beta$ is independent of the choice of the distinguished state $0$.
