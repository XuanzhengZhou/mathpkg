Given an admissible row vector $\nu$, the $Q^\nu$-process is a Markov chain constructed on the set $S^\nu \cup \{0\}$ that serves as the $\bar{\nu}$-dual of the original substochastic kernel $Q$. Its transition probabilities are defined by a Doob $h$-transform-like weighting: $Q_{ij}^\nu = \nu_j P_{ji} / (\nu_i + \delta_{0i})$. The fundamental matrix entries satisfy $N_{0j}^\nu = \nu_j + \delta_{0j}$, linking the potential theory of $Q^\nu$ directly to $\nu$. This dual process is the key tool for studying escape probabilities $\theta^E$ and for proving the integral representation of $\nu$ via measures on the entrance boundary.
