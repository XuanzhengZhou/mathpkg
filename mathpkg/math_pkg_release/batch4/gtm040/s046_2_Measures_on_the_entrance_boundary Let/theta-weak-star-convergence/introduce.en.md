Proposition 11-5 establishes that the escape probability measures $\theta^{E_k}$ serve as finite-state approximations to the entrance boundary measure $\beta^\nu$. As the finite sets $E_k$ grow to exhaust the state space $S$, the sequence $\theta^{E_k}$ converges in the weak-star topology on ${}^*S$ to the unique probability measure $\beta^\nu$ guaranteed by Proposition 11-2. This result provides a constructive method for computing or approximating $\beta^\nu$ through finite truncations, and it parallels the analogous convergence theorem for the harmonic measure $\beta$ of a normal chain (Theorem 11-7).
