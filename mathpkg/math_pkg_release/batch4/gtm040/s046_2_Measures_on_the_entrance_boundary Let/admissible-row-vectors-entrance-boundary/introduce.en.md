An admissible row vector $\nu$ for the entrance boundary is a finite-valued non-negative row vector indexed by the state space of a Markov chain $P$, with the additional requirements that $\nu_0 = 0$ and $\nu P \leq \nu + \delta_0$. These conditions ensure that $\bar{\nu}_j = \nu_j + \delta_{0j}$ defines a non-negative $Q$-superregular row vector. Each such $\nu$ determines a probability measure $\beta^\nu$ on the entrance boundary that integrates the Martin kernel to recover $\nu$. The class of admissible vectors includes in particular the row $G_{0\cdot}$ of the potential matrix when the chain is normal.
