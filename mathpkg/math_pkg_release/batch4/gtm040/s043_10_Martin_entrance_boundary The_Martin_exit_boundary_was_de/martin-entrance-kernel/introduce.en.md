The Martin entrance kernel $J(i,j)$ and the associated metric $d'$ provide the formalism for constructing the entrance boundary $*S$ of a denumerable Markov chain $P$, in analogy with the Martin exit boundary. The kernel is normalized so that $J(i,\cdot)f = 1$, and the convergence of $J(\cdot, j)$ as $j$ varies through $S$ defines the boundary points. This construction serves to represent $P$-superregular measures via integrals over the completed space.
