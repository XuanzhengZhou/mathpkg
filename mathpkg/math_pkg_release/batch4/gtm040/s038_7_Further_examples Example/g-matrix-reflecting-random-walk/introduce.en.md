The potential matrix $G = (G_{ij})$ captures the expected number of visits to state $j$ starting from state $i$ before absorption, and is a fundamental object in the potential theory of Markov chains. For the symmetric reflecting random walk ($p = q = 1/2$) on the nonnegative integers, the $G$-matrix takes a particularly simple form: it is strictly upper-triangular with entries growing linearly in $(j-i)$. This reflects the fact that the chain can only move upward in expected visit counts when $j > i$, and the proportionality to the distance $j-i$ comes from the unbiased nature of the walk.
