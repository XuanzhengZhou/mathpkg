If $(h_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$ is a non-negative supermartingale, then there is a unique representation

$$\left(h_n, \mathcal{R}_n\right) = \left(r_n, \mathcal{R}_n\right) + \left(g_n, \mathcal{R}_n\right)$$

of $(h_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$ as the sum of a martingale and a potential. In the representation the martingale is a non-negative martingale, the potential is a pure potential, and $r_n$ satisfies $r_n = \lim_k M\left[h_{n+k} \mid \mathcal{R}_n\right]$. Moreover, $(g_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$ is the difference between a martingale and a process consisting of an increasing sequence of random variables.
