Let $T = \mathbb{Z}$. Prove that $\mathcal{G}_Q = \varnothing$ if
$$
\lim_{n \to \infty} \sup_{t,k \in S} \frac{(Q^n)_{ij}(Q^n)_{jk}}{(Q^{2n})_{ik}} = 0 \quad \text{for some } j \in S.
$$
[Hint: Show that this condition forces $\Pr[x_0 = j] = 0$.]
