Define a $K$-neighbor potential to be a potential $U$ such that $U_A = 0$ whenever $A$ contains two sites which are not $K$-neighbors. Show that $\{x_i\}$ is $K$-Markov if and only if the canonical potential $V$ for $\{x_i\}$ is $K$-neighbor. What does this say when $K = 0$? [Hint: Define a new neighbor system.]
