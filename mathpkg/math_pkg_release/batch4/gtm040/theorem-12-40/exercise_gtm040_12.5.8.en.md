Let $S = \{0, 1, \ldots\}$, $T = \mathbb{Z}$. Define $Q_{ij} = 0$ unless $|i - j| = 1$, and for $|i - j| = 1$ let $Q_{ij} > 0$ be chosen such that $\sum_j Q_{ij}$ converges for each $i$. Determine conditions under which $\mathcal{G}_Q \neq \varnothing$ and study the structure of the resulting Markov fields on $\mathbb{Z}$ with nearest-neighbor interactions on the countable state space.
