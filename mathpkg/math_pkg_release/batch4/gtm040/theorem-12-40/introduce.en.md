When the state space $S$ is finite, every Gibbs field class $\mathcal{G}_{Q'}$ corresponding to a strictly positive matrix $Q'$ contains exactly one Markov field. This unique field is a stationary Markov chain on $\mathbb{Z}$ whose transition matrix $Q$ is the canonical stochastic representative obtained from $Q'$ via Lemma 12-39, and whose one-dimensional marginal is the unique stationary distribution $\alpha$ of $Q$. This theorem resolves the classification of homogeneous Markov fields on $\mathbb{Z}$ with finite state space, showing that the rich variety of Gibbs fields collapses to a single stationary Markov chain when $S$ is finite.
