A simple function is one that takes only finitely many values, represented canonically as a finite linear combination of characteristic functions of disjoint sets. Simple functions are the bridge between sets and general measurable functions: the Lebesgue integral is first defined for simple functions, then extended to non-negative measurable functions via supremum, and finally to signed functions by decomposition.
