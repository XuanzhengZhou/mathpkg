A completely additive set function (also called countably additive or $\sigma$-additive) extends the finite additivity property to countably infinite disjoint unions, provided the union remains in the field. This property is crucial for the development of measure theory and Lebesgue integration, as it allows passage to limits under the integral sign. The text assumes that no two sets have values $+\infty$ and $-\infty$ respectively, and that $\rho$ is not identically infinite.
