The family of all subsets of $X$ forms a Borel field containing $\mathcal{C}$. Form the intersection of all Borel fields containing $\mathcal{C}$. This intersection is non-empty (since the power set is one such Borel field), and it can be verified that the intersection of any family of Borel fields is again a Borel field. This intersection is therefore the unique smallest Borel field containing $\mathcal{C}$.
