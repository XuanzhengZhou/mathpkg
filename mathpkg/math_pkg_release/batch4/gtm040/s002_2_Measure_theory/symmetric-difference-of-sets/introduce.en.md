The symmetric difference of two sets consists of elements that belong to exactly one of the two sets. It plays a crucial role in the Carathéodory extension theorem proof, where it is used to define a metric $d(A,B) = \mu(A \oplus B)$ on sets. This metric then allows approximation of measurable sets by sets in the original field.
