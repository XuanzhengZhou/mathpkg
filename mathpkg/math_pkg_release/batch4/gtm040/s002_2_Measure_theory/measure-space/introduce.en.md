A measure space consists of a set $X$, a Borel field (sigma-algebra) $\mathcal{B}$ of subsets of $X$, and a non-negative countably additive set function $\mu$ defined on $\mathcal{B}$. When the total measure of the space is 1, it is called a probability space. The text provides four concrete examples: the trivial measure space, Lebesgue measure on Euclidean space, a finite probability space (die toss), and a denumerable measure space defined by a non-negative row vector.
