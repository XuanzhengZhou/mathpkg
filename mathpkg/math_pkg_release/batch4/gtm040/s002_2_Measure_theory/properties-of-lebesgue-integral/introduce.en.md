This proposition catalogs the fundamental properties of the Lebesgue integral: linearity with respect to constant scaling, monotonicity (integration preserves order), sets of measure zero contribute nothing, integrals are additive over disjoint domains, and the triangle inequality for integrals holds. These properties form the operational foundation for all subsequent work in measure-theoretic integration and probability theory.
