Countable subadditivity is a fundamental property of measures: the measure of a set contained in a countable union is bounded by the sum of the measures. The proof uses a standard technique of making the covering sets disjoint by subtracting off earlier sets, then applying complete additivity and monotonicity. This inequality is essential for establishing outer measure properties.
