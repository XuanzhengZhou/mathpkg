A field of sets (also called an algebra of sets) is a non-empty collection of subsets of a given set $X$ that is closed under complementation and finite unions. It provides the basic structure on which additive set functions and measures are defined. Every field automatically contains the empty set and the whole space $X$, and is closed under finite intersections and differences.
