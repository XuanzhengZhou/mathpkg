Let $X$ be an arbitrary non-empty set of points and let $\mathcal{F}$ be a family of subsets of $X$. We say that $\mathcal{F}$ is a \textit{field of sets} if
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] the empty set $\varnothing$ is in $\mathcal{F}$,
\item[(2)] whenever $A$ is a set of $\mathcal{F}$, the complement of $A$, denoted $\tilde{A}$, is in $\mathcal{F}$, and
\item[(3)] whenever $A$ and $B$ are sets of $\mathcal{F}$, so is their union, denoted $A \cup B$.
\end{enumerate}
