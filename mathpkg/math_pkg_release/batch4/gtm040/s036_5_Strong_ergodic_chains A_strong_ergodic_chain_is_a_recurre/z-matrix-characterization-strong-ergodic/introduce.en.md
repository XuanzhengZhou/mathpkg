Proposition 9-75 provides a clean characterization: the fundamental matrix $Z$ exists precisely when the chain $P$ is strong ergodic. Moreover, when $P$ is strong ergodic, $Z$ admits the explicit formula $Z = A - G(I - A)$, expressing $Z$ in terms of the stationary matrix $A$ and the potential operator $G$. This establishes $Z$ as a key object linking stationary and potential-theoretic aspects of the chain.
