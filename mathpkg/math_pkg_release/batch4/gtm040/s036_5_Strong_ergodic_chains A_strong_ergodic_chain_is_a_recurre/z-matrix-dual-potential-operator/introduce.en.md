Proposition 9-78 establishes that the fundamental matrix $Z$ serves as a unified potential operator in strong ergodic chains, replacing both $-C$ (for right potentials) and $-G$ (for left potentials). If $g = -Gf$ is the potential of a charge $f$ (with $\alpha f = 0$), then $g = Zf$ by Proposition 9-75. The result for signed measures follows by duality, since $(\text{dual } Z) = \hat{Z}$. This unification is a major advantage of $Z$ over using separate operators.
