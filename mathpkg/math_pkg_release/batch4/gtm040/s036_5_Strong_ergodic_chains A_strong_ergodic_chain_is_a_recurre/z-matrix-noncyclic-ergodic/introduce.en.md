Definition 9-74 introduces the fundamental matrix $Z$ for a noncyclic ergodic chain $P$. The matrix $Z$ is defined as the sum of the series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (P - A)^n$, where $A$ is the stationary matrix, whenever this series converges to a finite-valued matrix. The existence of $Z$ turns out to be equivalent to the chain being strong ergodic (Proposition 9-75), making $Z$ a central object in the potential theory of recurrent chains.
