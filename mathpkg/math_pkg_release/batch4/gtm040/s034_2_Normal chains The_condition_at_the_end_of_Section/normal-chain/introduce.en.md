A normal chain is a recurrent Markov chain that satisfies a key regularity condition: for some (and hence any) fixed reference state $0$, the long-run entrance probabilities ${}^0\lambda_j$ and their dual counterparts ${}^0\hat{\lambda}_j$ exist for all states $j$. The class of normal chains is quite extensive and includes all noncyclic ergodic chains. The significance of normality is that it guarantees that all functions of finite support with total charge zero are potential charges, enabling a robust potential theory analogous to that of transient chains.
