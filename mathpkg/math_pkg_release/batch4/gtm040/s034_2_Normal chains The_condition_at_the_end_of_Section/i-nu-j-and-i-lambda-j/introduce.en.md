For a recurrent Markov chain with fundamental matrix ${}^iN$ relative to state $i$, Definition 9-19 introduces two important long-run quantities: ${}^i\nu_j$ and ${}^i\lambda_j$. The quantity ${}^i\nu_j$ is the limiting mean number of visits to state $j$ (averaged over starting positions) before reaching $i$, while ${}^i\lambda_j$ is the corresponding probability that $j$ is entered in the long run before $i$. These quantities serve as the building blocks for defining normal chains and understanding the asymptotic behavior of recurrent Markov chains.
