Proposition 9-34 establishes the basic properties of the limiting matrix $\lambda^E = \lim_n P^n B^E$, where $B^E$ is the hitting matrix for a set $E$. When the limit exists, each row of $\lambda^E$ is non-negative, the columns indexed by $E$ are identically zero, and each row sum is at most $1$. These properties reflect that $\lambda^E_{ij}$ represents the probability of being at $j$ in the long run before hitting $E$.
