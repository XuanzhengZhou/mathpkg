Theorem 9-31 removes the finite-support restriction from the potential theory of normal chains: it states that any weak charge (a function $f$ with $\alpha f = 0$ for which the sums $\sum_k (P^n f)_k$ converge in a certain sense) is automatically a full charge in a normal chain. The potential is then given by the same formula $g = -Gf$ and is bounded. The dual version provides the corresponding statement for signed measures. For symmetric random walks in one and two dimensions, where $C = G$, this theorem yields the discrete analog of the classical Brownian motion potential formula.
