Proposition 9-36 shows that the property of being a small set is hereditary: any subset of a small set is itself small. Moreover, the corresponding $\lambda^E$ matrix is obtained from $\lambda^F$ by right-multiplication with the hitting matrix $B^E$. This follows from the factorization $B^E = B^F B^E$ (Proposition 5-8) and the convergence of $P^n B^F$ to $1 \lambda^F$.
