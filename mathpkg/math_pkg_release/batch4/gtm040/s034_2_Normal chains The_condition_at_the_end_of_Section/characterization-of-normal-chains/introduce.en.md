Theorem 9-26 is the central result of Section 2, providing three equivalent characterizations of normal chains. A chain is normal if and only if for some reference state $0$ and all states $j$: (1) the long-run entrance probabilities ${}^0\lambda_j$ and ${}^0\hat{\lambda}_j$ exist; equivalently, (2) the matrix entries $G_{0j}$ and $C_{j0}$ exist; equivalently, (3) every two-point function and signed measure with zero total charge is a potential charge. Moreover, in a normal chain the matrices $C$ and $G$ are completely determined by the $\nu$ and $\lambda$ quantities, and charges exist for all finitely supported zero-total-charge functions.
