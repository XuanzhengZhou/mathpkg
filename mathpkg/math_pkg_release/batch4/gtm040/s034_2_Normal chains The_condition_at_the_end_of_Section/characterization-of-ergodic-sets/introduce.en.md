Proposition 9-41 gives a simple characterization of ergodic sets: $E$ is ergodic if and only if its total $\alpha$-measure $\alpha_E 1 = \sum_{i \in E} \alpha_i$ is finite. This follows from the identity $\alpha_E P^E = \alpha_E$ and the criterion for ergodicity. Consequently, all finite sets are ergodic, ergodicity is hereditary (subsets of ergodic sets are ergodic), and the class of ergodic sets is closed under finite unions.
