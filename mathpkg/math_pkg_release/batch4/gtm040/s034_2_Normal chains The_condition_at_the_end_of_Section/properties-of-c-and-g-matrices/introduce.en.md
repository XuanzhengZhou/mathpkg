Lemma 9-25 summarizes the basic algebraic properties of the matrices $C$ and $G$ introduced in Definition 9-24. By Lemma 9-2, the quantities defining each entry are bounded and non-negative, so every existing entry is finite and non-negative. The diagonal entries vanish identically, and the dual relationship $\hat{G}_{ij} = \alpha_j C_{ji} / \alpha_i$ connects the two matrices. Moreover, the existence of $G_{0j}$ and $C_{j0}$ implies the existence of the long-run mean occupation numbers ${}^0\nu_j$ and their duals.
