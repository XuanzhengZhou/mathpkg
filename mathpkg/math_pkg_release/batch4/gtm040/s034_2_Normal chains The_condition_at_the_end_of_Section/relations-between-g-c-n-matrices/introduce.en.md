Proposition 9-45 establishes a set of algebraic identities linking the three fundamental matrices $G$, $C$, and ${}^kN$ (the fundamental matrix relative to state $k$) in a normal chain. These identities express ${}^kN_{ij}$ in terms of $G$ and $C$ with appropriate $\alpha$-weighting, and also give the diagonal entries ${}^kN_{ii}$ in a symmetric form. The first two formulas follow from Theorem 9-7 and the definitions of $G$ and $C$; setting $i = j$ yields the diagonal identities. These relations are essential for computational manipulations in the potential theory of normal chains.
