By Proposition 9-21, for a function $f$ with $\alpha f = 0$ and finite $\sum_k {}^0N_{kk} f_k$, the potential is $g = [{}^0N - 1\,{}^0\nu]f$. Choosing $f$ to be the two-point function supported on $\{0, j\}$ with $f_0 = -\alpha_j/\alpha_0$ and $f_j = 1$ (which satisfies $\alpha f = 0$), the $i$-th component gives
$$({}^0N f)_i - (1\,{}^0\nu f)_i = {}^0N_{ij} - \frac{\alpha_j}{\alpha_0}{}^0N_{i0} - {}^0\nu_j.$$

Since ${}^0N_{i0} = 0$ for all $i$, this simplifies to ${}^0N_{ij} - {}^0\nu_j$. On the other hand, by Theorem 9-26, $G_{ij} = {}^i\nu_j$, and the relationship gives
$${}^0N_{ij} - {}^0\nu_j = -\left[G_{ij} - G_{i0}\frac{\alpha_j}{\alpha_0}\right].$$
