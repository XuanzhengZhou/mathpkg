Corollary 9-29 applies the general theory of normal chains to an important class of examples: symmetric sums of independent random variables. For such processes (whether null or noncyclic ergodic), the symmetry condition $P = P^T$ forces the chain to be normal, with $C = G = \frac{1}{2}\,{}^jN_{ii}$ and all entrance probabilities ${}^i\lambda_j = 1/2$. This result covers the classical one-dimensional and two-dimensional symmetric random walks, providing a bridge between the abstract potential theory and concrete examples.
