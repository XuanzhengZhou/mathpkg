Consider the $j$-th column of $B^E$, denoted $b_j$, for $j \in E$. Since $B^E$ satisfies $(I - P) B^E = 0$ outside $E$, the column $b_j$ is superharmonic outside $E$. The finiteness condition $\sum_{k \in E} {}^0N_{kk} P^E_{kj} < \infty$ ensures that the quantities involved in the potential representation of Proposition 9-13 are well-behaved. Specifically, it guarantees that $b_j$ is a potential (up to an additive constant), and hence $\lim_n P^n b_j$ exists.

Since this holds for each $j \in E$, the matrix limit $\lambda^E = \lim_n P^n B^E$ exists. The remaining assertion about $B^E - 1 \lambda^E$ having potential columns follows from the fact that each column of $B^E - 1 \lambda^E$ has total charge zero (since $\lambda^E 1 = 1$) and satisfies the conditions of Corollary 9-28.
