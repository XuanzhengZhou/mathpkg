Proposition 9-44 constructs a unique function $x$ with prescribed boundary values on two disjoint small ergodic sets $E$ and $F$, representing the equilibrium between the two sets. The function takes value 1 on $E$ and 0 on $F$, and its values on $E$ represent the probability of returning to $F$ before $E$, while on $F$ its negative gives the probability of returning to $E$ before $F$. Moreover, $x$ differs from a potential by a constant, making it useful for studying transitions between ergodic components.
