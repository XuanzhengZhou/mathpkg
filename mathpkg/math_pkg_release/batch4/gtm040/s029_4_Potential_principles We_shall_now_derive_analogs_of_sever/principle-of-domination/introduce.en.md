The Principle of Domination is a fundamental result in potential theory for denumerable Markov chains. It states that if a non-negative superregular function $h$ dominates a potential $g = Nf$ on the support of the positive part of its charge $f^+$, then $h$ dominates $g$ everywhere. Moreover, when $h$ itself is a potential, the total charge of $g$ is bounded by that of $h$. This is the discrete analog of the classical domination principle in Newtonian potential theory.
