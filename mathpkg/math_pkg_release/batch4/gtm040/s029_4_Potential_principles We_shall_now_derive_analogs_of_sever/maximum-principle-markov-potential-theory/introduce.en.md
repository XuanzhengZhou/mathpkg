The maximum principle states that for a function $h$ satisfying $h = B^E h$, the global supremum equals the supremum over $E$. Furthermore, if the transient states $\tilde{E}$ communicate in the absorbing chain $^E P$ and $h$ attains its maximum somewhere in $\tilde{E}$, then $h$ must be constant on $\tilde{E}$. This is the Markov chain analog of the classical maximum principle for harmonic functions, where a harmonic function attains its extreme values on the boundary.
