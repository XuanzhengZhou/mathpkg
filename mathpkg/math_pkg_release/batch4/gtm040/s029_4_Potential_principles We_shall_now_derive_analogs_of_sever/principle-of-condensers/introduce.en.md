The Principle of Condensers constructs a potential $g$ that acts as the voltage between two disjoint sets $E$ and $F$, taking the value 1 on the "positive plate" $E$ and 0 on the "negative plate" $F$. The potential is realized as $g = Nf$ where the positive charge $f^+$ is supported on $E$ and the negative charge $f^-$ is supported on $F$, with non-negative total charge. The construction uses the hitting probability $^F H_{iE}$ and requires $E$ to be an equilibrium set with finite boundary.
