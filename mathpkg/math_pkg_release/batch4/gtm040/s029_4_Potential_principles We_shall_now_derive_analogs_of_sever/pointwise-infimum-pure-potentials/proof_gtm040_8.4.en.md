Apply Lemma 8-49 and Proposition 8-25. By Lemma 8-49, the pointwise infimum of a family of pure potentials is superregular (since each pure potential is superregular). By Proposition 8-25, a superregular function is a pure potential if and only if it satisfies the condition that its iterates under $P$ converge pointwise to zero. Since each pure potential in the family satisfies this condition, their pointwise infimum also satisfies it (being dominated by each member of the family). Hence the infimum is a pure potential.
