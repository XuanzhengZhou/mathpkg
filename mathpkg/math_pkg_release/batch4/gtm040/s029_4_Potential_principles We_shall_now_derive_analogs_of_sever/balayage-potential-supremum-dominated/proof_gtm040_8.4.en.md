Certainly the balayage potential does have the stated property—it is a pure potential with support in $E$ and is dominated by $g$ on $E$ (in fact, everywhere). Let $\bar{g} = B^E g$ and let $h$ be a potential with support in $E$ and with $h_E \leq g_E$. Then by Proposition 8-19, $h = B^E h$. Since $h_E \leq g_E$ and $B^E$ preserves order, we have $h = B^E h \leq B^E g = \bar{g}$. Thus $\bar{g}$ dominates every such potential, and is therefore their supremum.
