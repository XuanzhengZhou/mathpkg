**Existence:** Let $\bar{g} = B^E g$. Then $\bar{g} \leq g$, $\bar{g}_E = g_E$, and $\bar{g}$ is superregular by conclusions (1) and (3) of Proposition 8-16. By Proposition 8-25, $\bar{g}$ is a potential, and the total charge of $\bar{g}$ is less than or equal to that of $g$.

**Uniqueness:** If $h$ were another such potential, we would have
$$\bar{g} = B^E \bar{g} = B^E h = h$$
by Proposition 8-15 and the fact that $\bar{g}_E = g_E = h_E$.
