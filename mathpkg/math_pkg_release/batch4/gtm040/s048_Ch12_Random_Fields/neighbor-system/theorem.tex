A \textbf{neighbor system} on a countable parameter set $T$ is a collection $\partial = \{\partial a; a \in T\}$ of finite subsets of $T$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $a \notin \partial a$,
\item[(ii)] $a \in \partial b$ if and only if $b \in \partial a$, for all $a, b \in T$.
\end{enumerate}
The sites $b \in \partial a$ are called the \textbf{neighbors} of $a$. Denote $\bar{a} = \{a\} \cup \partial a$, and for $A \subset T$,
$$\partial A = \{b \in T - A : b \in \partial a \text{ for some } a \in A\}, \quad \bar{A} = A \cup \partial A.$$
