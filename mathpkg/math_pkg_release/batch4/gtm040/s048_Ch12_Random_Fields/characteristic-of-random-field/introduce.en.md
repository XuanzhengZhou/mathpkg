The $(A,\Lambda)$-characteristic replaces the role of transition probabilities in the general setting of random fields. It encodes the conditional probability of a configuration on a set $A$ given the configuration on the surrounding set $\Lambda - A$. The collection of all single-site characteristics $\mu_a^\Lambda$ forms the local characteristics, which are the fundamental data determining a random field. This concept is essential for defining Markov fields, where the dependence on the conditioning set is localized to neighbors.
