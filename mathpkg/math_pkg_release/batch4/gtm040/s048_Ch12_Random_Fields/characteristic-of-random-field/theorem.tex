Given a random field $\{x_t\}$, and finite (non-empty) sets $A$ and $\Lambda$ such that $A \subset \Lambda$, the \textbf{$(A, \Lambda)$-characteristic} is the real-valued function on $\Omega$ given by
$$\mu_A^\Lambda(\iota) = \Pr[x_t = i_t \text{ for all } t \in A \mid x_t = i_t \text{ for all } t \in \Lambda - A]$$
when evaluated at the configuration $\iota = \{i_t; t \in T\}$. For $a \in T$, $\mu_a^\Lambda = \mu_{\{a\}}^\Lambda$; the collection $\{\mu_a^\Lambda; a \in \Lambda \subset T\}$ is called the \textbf{local characteristics} of the random field.
