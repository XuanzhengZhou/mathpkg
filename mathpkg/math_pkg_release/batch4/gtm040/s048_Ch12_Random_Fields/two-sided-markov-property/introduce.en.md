The two-sided Markov property extends the classical Markov property by conditioning on both past and future values. For a denumerable process, the conditional distribution at time $n$ given all other times depends only on the immediate neighbors $n-1$ and $n+1$. This property serves as the motivating example for the general definition of a Markov field: it shows how the Markovian dependence structure can be encoded by a neighbor system. Unlike the one-sided Markov property, the two-sided version generalizes naturally to arbitrary parameter sets.
