A Markov field (or Markov random field) is a random field whose local characteristics at each site depend only on the configuration values at that site and its neighbors. This definition captures the intuitive idea of spatial Markovian dependence: given the neighbors, the value at a site is conditionally independent of values at all other sites. Markov fields are the central objects of study in Chapter 12. The celebrated Hammersley-Clifford theorem (Theorem 12-16 in this text) establishes their equivalence with Gibbs fields on finite parameter sets.
