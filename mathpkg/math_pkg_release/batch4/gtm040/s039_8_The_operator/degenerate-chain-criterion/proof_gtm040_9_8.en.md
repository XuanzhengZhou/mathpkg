If $0'$ is any other reference point, then
$$k'(E) = k(E) + k(\{0'\}).$$
Hence it suffices to show that $k(E) = 0$. If $0 \in E$, $k(E) = 0$ by hypothesis. If $0 \notin E$, let $0'$ be any point of $E$, and
$$0 = k'(\{0'\}) \leq k'(E) = k(E) \leq k(E \cup \{0\}) = 0.$$
Both inequalities follow by the monotonicity proved in Proposition 9-98.
