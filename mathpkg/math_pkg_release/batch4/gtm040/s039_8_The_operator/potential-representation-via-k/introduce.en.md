This theorem establishes that in a normal chain, potentials of weak charges are represented through the $K$-matrix. Specifically, row potentials $\nu$ are given by $\nu = -\mu K$ and column potentials $g$ by $g = -Kf$. Furthermore, any matrix $Y$ that represents potentials of finite support must be of the form $Y = -(K + k(1\alpha))$ for some scalar $k$. This is the central result that ties the $K$-operator into the potential theory framework.
