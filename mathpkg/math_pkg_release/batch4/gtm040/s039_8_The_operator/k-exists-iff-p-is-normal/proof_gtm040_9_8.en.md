If $P$ is normal, then $C$ and $G$ exist, and hence $K$ exists by Lemma 9-81. Conversely, suppose $K$ exists. Since $K_{i0} = C_{i0}$ and $K_{0j} = G_{0j}$, $C_{i0}$ and $G_{0j}$ exist for all $i$ and $j$. Thus $P$ is normal by Theorem 9-26.
