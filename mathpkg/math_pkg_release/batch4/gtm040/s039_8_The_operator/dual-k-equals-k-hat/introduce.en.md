This lemma states the crucial duality property of the $K$-matrix: $\hat{K}$ equals the dual of $K$. This property sets $K$ apart from $C$ and $G$, which do not satisfy individual duality in general. The fact that $\hat{K} = \operatorname{dual} K$ is fundamental for proving that potentials can be represented via $K$, and it underpins the main results of this section.
