This proposition guarantees that outside the degenerate case, we can always find two distinguished states such that any finite set containing both has positive capacity. The proof constructs these states by considering three cases based on the existence of one-point sets with non-zero capacity or of larger positive-capacity sets in $\mathcal{L}_0$, and selecting a minimal such set.
