If $k(E) = 0$, then $\lambda_E^E K_E = 0$ and $K_E^{-1}$ does not exist. If $k(E) > 0$, then by Corollary 9-86,
\begin{align*}
K_E \left( P^E + \frac{1}{k(E)} l_E^E \lambda_E^E - I \right)
&= I - 1\lambda_E^E + K_E \left( \frac{1}{k(E)} l_E^E \lambda_E^E \right) \\
&= I - 1\lambda_E^E + 1\lambda_E^E \quad \text{(by Proposition 9-87)} \\
&= I.
\end{align*}
