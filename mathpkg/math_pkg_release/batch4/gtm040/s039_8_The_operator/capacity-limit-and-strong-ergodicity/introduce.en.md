This proposition links the limiting behavior of capacity to strong ergodicity. As finite sets exhaust the state space, the capacity $k(E_n)$ approaches a finite limit exactly when the chain is strong ergodic. In that case, the formula $k(E) = M_{\alpha 0} - M_{\alpha E}$ expresses capacity as the difference between the $\alpha$-weighted mean first passage time to $0$ and to $E$. As a bonus, strong ergodicity implies $\hat{M}_{\alpha E} = M_{\alpha E}$.
