This lemma provides a transparent characterization of degenerate chains in terms of hitting probabilities: a normal chain is degenerate precisely when for any two states $i$ and $j$, at least one of the forward hitting probability $^i\lambda_j$ or the backward hitting probability $^i\hat{\lambda}_j$ vanishes. In other words, any two states cannot both reach each other. The forward direction follows from Proposition 9-89; the converse uses the formula for one-point capacities.
