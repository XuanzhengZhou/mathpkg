A normal chain is called degenerate when all finite sets have capacity zero, regardless of which reference state is chosen. This is a pathological case where potential-theoretic concepts break down. Degenerate chains are essentially of a type similar to the basic example (a chain where for every pair of states, either the forward or backward hitting probability vanishes).
