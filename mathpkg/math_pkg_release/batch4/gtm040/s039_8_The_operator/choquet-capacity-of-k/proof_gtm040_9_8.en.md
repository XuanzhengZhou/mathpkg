The capacity inequalities follow from the fact that the time to enter all of $n$ sets is no greater than the time to enter the intersection; hence the time to reach $0$ from the intersection is no greater than the time to reach $0$ after all $n$ sets are entered. We also see why $k(E) > 0$, if $E$ has more than one state.
