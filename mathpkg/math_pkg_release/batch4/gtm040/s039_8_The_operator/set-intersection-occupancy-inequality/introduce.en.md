This lemma establishes an inequality relating the mean occupation time before hitting the intersection of several sets to an alternating sum of occupation times before hitting unions of those sets. It is a probabilistic analog of the inclusion-exclusion principle. The proof uses a counting argument on sample paths: the number of visits to $j$ before entering all $r$ sets is bounded below by the number of visits before entering their intersection.
