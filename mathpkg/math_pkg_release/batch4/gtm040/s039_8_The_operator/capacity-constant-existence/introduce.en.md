This proposition establishes the existence of a constant $k(E)$ associated with each finite set $E$, which will later be defined as the capacity of $E$. The constant satisfies two product identities involving $K_E$, the equilibrium distribution $\lambda_E^E$, and the escape vector $l_E^E$. Importantly, $k(E)$ is self-dual: $k(E) = \hat{k}(E)$. The proposition is proved by equating the two products obtained from Corollary 9-86.
