Multiplying the equation of Lemma 9-95 through by $\lambda^L$, we obtain
$$\lambda^L K l^E = k(E) + \lambda^L {}^E d$$
and similarly
$$\lambda^L K l^F = k(F) + \lambda^L {}^F d.$$
Subtracting, we see that it is sufficient to prove that $\lambda^L K l^E = \lambda^L K l^F$. But
$$\lambda^L K l^E = (\lambda_L^L K_L)_L l^E = k(L) \alpha_L l^E = k(L)$$
since the support of $l^E$ is in $L$. Similarly, $\lambda^L K l^F = k(L)$. The other equality is dual.
