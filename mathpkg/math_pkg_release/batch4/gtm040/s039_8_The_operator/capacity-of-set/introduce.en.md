The capacity $k(E)$ of a finite set $E$ is the constant identified in Proposition 9-87. It generalizes the classical notion of capacity to recurrent Markov chains. Like the $K$-matrix, capacity depends on the choice of the distinguished reference state $0$. For a one-point set $\{i\}$, the capacity is given by $k(\{i\}) = (G_{0i} - C_{0i})/\alpha_i$, with $k(\{0\}) = 0$. When $0 \in E$, the capacity is nonnegative: $k(E) = (Kl^E)_0 \geq 0$.
