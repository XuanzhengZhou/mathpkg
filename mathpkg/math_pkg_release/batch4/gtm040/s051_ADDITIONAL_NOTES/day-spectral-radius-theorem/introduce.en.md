Day [1964] generalized Kesten's theorem by removing the symmetry hypothesis on the transition matrix $P$. The theorem establishes that amenability of a countable group remains equivalent to the existence of a (not necessarily symmetric) random walk with spectral radius one. Day's work provided additional equivalent characterizations of amenability and has been reproved several times by other authors.
