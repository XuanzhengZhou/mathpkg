Kesten [1959] established a fundamental connection between the spectral theory of symmetric random walks on groups and the algebraic property of amenability. The theorem characterizes amenable groups as precisely those admitting a symmetric transition matrix with spectral radius one on $\ell^2$. When the spectral radius is strictly less than one, the fundamental matrix $N$ is a bounded operator, implying the walk is transient. Day [1964] later removed the symmetry hypothesis and found further equivalent conditions for amenability.
