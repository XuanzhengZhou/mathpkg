The section only cites the result; the full proof is in Kesten [1959]. The key idea is that spectral radius one of a symmetric $P$ on $\ell^2$ is equivalent to the existence of almost-invariant vectors, which in turn characterizes amenability via F\o lner's criterion. If the spectral radius is less than one, the Neumann series $\sum P^n$ converges in operator norm, giving boundedness of $N$ and hence transience.
