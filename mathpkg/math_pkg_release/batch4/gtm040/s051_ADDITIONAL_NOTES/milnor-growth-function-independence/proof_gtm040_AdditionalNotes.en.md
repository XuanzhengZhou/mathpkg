The section only cites the results; full proofs appear in Milnor [1968a, 1968b] and Wolf [1968]. The independence of the growth function from the generating set follows from the fact that any two finite generating sets can express each other's elements as words of bounded length, so the growth functions are quasi-equivalent. The implication that spectral radius less than one forces exponential growth follows from the relationship between the $n$-step return probability and the volume growth.
