Milnor [1968a] established that the asymptotic growth rate of a finitely generated group is essentially independent of the choice of generators. He further showed that exponential growth is necessary for the existence of a symmetric random walk with spectral radius less than one. However, the converse fails: Milnor constructed a solvable group with exponential growth that nevertheless admits a symmetric walk with spectral radius one, separating the notions of growth type and spectral behavior. Milnor [1968b] and Wolf [1968] studied conditions under which the growth function is of polynomial or exponential size.
