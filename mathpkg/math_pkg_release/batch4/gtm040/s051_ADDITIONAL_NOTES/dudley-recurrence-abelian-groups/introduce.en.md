Dudley [1962] gave a complete classification of which countable abelian groups admit a recurrent random walk with a single communicating class. The condition is purely algebraic: the group must have rational rank (maximum number of linearly independent elements) at most 2. This generalizes Pólya's classical result that simple random walk on $\mathbb{Z}^d$ is recurrent if and only if $d \leq 2$. In the non-abelian case, Kesten [1967] conjectured the existence of a recurrent walk is related to the growth rate of the group.
