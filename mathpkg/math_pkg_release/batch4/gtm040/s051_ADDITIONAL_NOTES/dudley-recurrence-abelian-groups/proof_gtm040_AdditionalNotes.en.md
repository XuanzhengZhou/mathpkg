The section only cites the result; the full proof is in Dudley [1962]. The proof shows that for abelian groups of rank $\geq 3$, every random walk with a single class is transient, using potential-theoretic estimates. For rank $\leq 2$, one can construct a recurrent walk by adapting the simple random walk on $\mathbb{Z}$ or $\mathbb{Z}^2$ to the group structure.
