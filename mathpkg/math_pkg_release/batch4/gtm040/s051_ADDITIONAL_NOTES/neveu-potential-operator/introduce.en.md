Neveu introduced a one-parameter family of potential operators $U_h$ that interpolate between the transition matrix $P$ (when $h = 1$) and the standard potential $P + P^2 + \cdots$ (when $h = 0$). The operator $U_h$ is defined via a Neumann series involving the perturbed matrix $P D_{1-h}$, where $D_{1-h}$ is the diagonal matrix of the function $1-h$. The probabilistic interpretation expresses $(U_h f)_i$ as the expected sum of $f$ evaluated along the chain trajectory, with each step's weight reduced by a factor of $1-h$ at the previously visited state.
