**Proof:** By Proposition 5-7, $s^E$ is regular and it is clearly bounded; therefore $s^E = c1$. On the other hand, by Proposition 5-8,

$$s^E = B^E s^E$$

so that

$$c1 = cB^E 1 = ch^E.$$

Therefore, either $c = 0$ or $h^E = 1$. In the latter case, $s^E = 1$ by Proposition 5-8.

As in Example 6 of Section 4-6, we let $p_k = P_{i,i+k}$, which, by assumption, is independent of $i$.
