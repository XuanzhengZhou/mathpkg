Proposition 4-20 establishes the fundamental equivalence between transience and the finiteness of the expected number of visits $N_{ii}$. The expected number of visits equals $1/(1 - \bar{H}_{ii})$, providing a direct quantitative link between the first-return probability and the visit count. This formula shows that as $\bar{H}_{ii}$ approaches 1 (recurrence), the expected number of visits diverges to infinity, while a transient state with $\bar{H}_{ii} < 1$ is visited only finitely many times in expectation.
