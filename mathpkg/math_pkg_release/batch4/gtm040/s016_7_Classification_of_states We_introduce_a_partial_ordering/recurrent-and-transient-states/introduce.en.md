Definition 4-18 classifies each state of a Markov chain as either recurrent or transient based on the first-return probability $\bar{H}_{ii}$. A recurrent state is one that is almost surely revisited, while a transient state has a positive probability of never being revisited. This fundamental dichotomy underpins the entire classification theory of Markov chain states and determines the long-run behavior of the process.
