Let $p_m$ be the Poisson distribution with mean $m$ on the non-negative integers. A game is played as follows: A random integer $n_1$ is selected with probabilities determined by $p_1$. A second random integer $n_2$ is selected with probabilities determined by $p_{n_1}$. The $i$th random integer is selected with probabilities determined by $p_{n_i-1}$. Prove that with probability one the integer 0 is eventually selected.
