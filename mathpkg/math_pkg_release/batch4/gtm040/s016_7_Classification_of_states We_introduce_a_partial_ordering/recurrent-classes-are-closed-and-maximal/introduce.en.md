Proposition 4-26 establishes two key structural properties of recurrent classes. First, they are closed: once the process enters a recurrent class, it never leaves. Second, they are maximal with respect to the $R$-ordering: no state outside the class can be reached from within it. The proof is simple but elegant: if a recurrent class could be left from some state $j$, then the probability of returning to $j$ would be bounded away from 1, contradicting recurrence.
