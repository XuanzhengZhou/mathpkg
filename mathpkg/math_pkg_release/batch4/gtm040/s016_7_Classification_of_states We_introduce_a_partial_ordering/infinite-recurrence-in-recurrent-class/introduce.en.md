Proposition 4-27 describes the behavior of a Markov chain once it enters a recurrent class: with probability one, every state in the class is visited infinitely often. This is the defining characteristic of recurrent behavior — the process cycles through all states of the class endlessly. The proof uses the identity from Lemma 4-19(5) showing that for communicating recurrent states, the probability of at least $n$ visits equals 1 for every $n$, hence infinitely many visits occur almost surely.
