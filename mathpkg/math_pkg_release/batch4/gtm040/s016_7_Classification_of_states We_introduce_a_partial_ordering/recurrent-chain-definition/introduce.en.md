A recurrent Markov chain is one whose state space consists of exactly one equivalence class under the communication relation $\sim$, and that class is recurrent. Equivalently, every pair of states communicates and each state is recurrent. Every absorbing state forms a one-element recurrent class. The class of recurrent chains is of central importance because any Markov chain can be decomposed into transient and recurrent components, and within recurrent components the process is almost surely trapped forever.
