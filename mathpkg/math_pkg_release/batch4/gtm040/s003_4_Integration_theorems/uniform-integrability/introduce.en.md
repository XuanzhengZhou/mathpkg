Uniform integrability is a key condition that prevents mass from escaping to infinity. It requires that the tails of the integrals $\int_{\{f_n > k\}} f_n d\mu$ can be made uniformly small across all $n$ by choosing $k$ sufficiently large. Together with convergence in measure, uniform integrability characterizes $L^1$ convergence (Proposition 1-52).
