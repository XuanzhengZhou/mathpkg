The Lebesgue Dominated Convergence Theorem is the most powerful and widely used convergence theorem in integration theory. It states that if a sequence of measurable functions is dominated by an integrable function, then pointwise convergence implies convergence of the integrals. The proof applies Fatou's Lemma twice: once to $f_n + g \geq 0$ and once to $g - f_n \geq 0$.
