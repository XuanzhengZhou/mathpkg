The Monotone Convergence Theorem, due to B. Levi, is one of the cornerstones of Lebesgue integration theory. It states that for a monotonically increasing sequence of non-negative measurable functions, the integral of the pointwise limit equals the limit of the integrals. The proof uses a clever truncation argument with a constant $c \in (0,1)$ and simple function approximations to establish both inequalities.
