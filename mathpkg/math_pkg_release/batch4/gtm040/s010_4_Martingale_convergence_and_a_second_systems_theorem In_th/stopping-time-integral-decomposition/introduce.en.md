This technical lemma provides an integral identity used in the proof of the Optional Stopping Theorem. It decomposes the integral of the stopped process $f_t$ into two parts: the contribution up to time $n$ (where the process has already stopped) and the contribution after time $n$ (where the process has not yet stopped). The proof uses the martingale property to replace $f_k$ by $f_n$ on the sets $\{t = k\}$ for $k \leq n$.
