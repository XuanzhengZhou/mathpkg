Let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence of random variables defined on points $\omega$, and let $r$ and $s$ be numbers with $r < s$. An \emph{upcrossing} of $[r, s]$ is said to occur between $n - k$ and $n$ for the point $\omega$ if these conditions are satisfied:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $f_{n-k}(\omega) \leq r$
  \item $r < f_{n-k+m}(\omega) < s$ for $0 < m < k$
  \item $f_n(\omega) \geq s$.
\end{enumerate}
No two upcrossings overlap.
