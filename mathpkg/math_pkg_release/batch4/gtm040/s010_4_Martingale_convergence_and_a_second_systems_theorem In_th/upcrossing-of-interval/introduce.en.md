An upcrossing of an interval $[r, s]$ by a sequence of random variables $\{f_n\}$ is a crossing from below $r$ to above $s$, with all intermediate values strictly between $r$ and $s$. This concept is central to the proof of the Martingale Convergence Theorem, as one bounds the expected number of upcrossings to show that oscillations cannot occur on a set of positive measure. No two upcrossings overlap, so they can be counted independently.
