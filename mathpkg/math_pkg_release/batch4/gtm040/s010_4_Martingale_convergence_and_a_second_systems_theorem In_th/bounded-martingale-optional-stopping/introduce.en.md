This corollary simplifies the Optional Stopping Theorem when the martingale is uniformly bounded and the underlying measure space has finite total measure. Under these conditions, the two hypotheses of Theorem 3-15 are automatically satisfied: $f_t$ is bounded hence integrable, and the tail integral $\int_{\{t > n\}} f_n \, d\mu$ tends to zero because it is bounded by $K \cdot \mu(\{t > n\})$, which vanishes as $n 	o \infty$ since $t$ is almost surely finite. The result formalizes that no gambling system can alter the fairness of a bounded fair game on a finite space.
