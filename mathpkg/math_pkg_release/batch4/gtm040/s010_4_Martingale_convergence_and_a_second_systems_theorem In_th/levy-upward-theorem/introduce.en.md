Levy's upward theorem (also called the martingale convergence theorem for conditional expectations) states that for an integrable random variable $f$ measurable with respect to the limit sigma-field $\mathcal{R}^*$, the sequence of conditional expectations $g_n = M[f \mid \mathcal{R}_n]$ with respect to an increasing sequence of partitions converges almost everywhere to $f$. The proof establishes that the $g_n$ form a uniformly integrable martingale, then uses the Martingale Convergence Theorem to obtain an almost-everywhere limit $g$, and finally shows $f = g$ a.e. by comparing integrals over sets in the generating field.
