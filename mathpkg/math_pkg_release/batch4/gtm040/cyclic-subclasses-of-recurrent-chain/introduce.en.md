For a recurrent Markov chain with period $d > 1$, the state space partitions into exactly $d$ cyclic subclasses via the equivalence relation $R$: two states are related if one can reach the other in a number of steps that is a multiple of $d$. The process visits these subclasses in a fixed cyclic order, moving from one to the next at each step. The key structural insight is that the $d$-step chain $P^d$ (obtained by watching the process only at times that are multiples of $d$) decomposes as $d$ independent recurrent non-cyclic chains, one for each cyclic subclass. This reduction from cyclic to non-cyclic chains via the skeleton $P^d$ is a fundamental technique in the theory of denumerable Markov chains.
