Let
$$S_k^{(n)} = \left[ (N_{00}^{(n)} - N_{k0}^{(n)}) \frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_0} + (N_{ki}^{(n)} - N_{0i}^{(n)}) \right].$$
Since $\mu \mathbf{1} = 0$, we have
$$\sum_k \mu_k \left[ N_{ki}^{(n)} - \frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_0} N_{k0}^{(n)} \right] = \sum_k \mu_k S_k^{(n)}.$$
By Theorem 9-7, $S_k^{(n)} \to {}_0 N_{ki}$ as $n \to \infty$, and by Lemma 9-10 the sequence is uniformly bounded by ${}_0 N_{ii} + {}_0 N_{00}$. The result then follows by dominated convergence. The dual statement is proved analogously.
