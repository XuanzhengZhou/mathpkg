Corollary 9-17 reveals a striking property of null recurrent chains: every function $g$ with $\alpha g$ finite is automatically a potential. This contrasts sharply with the transient case, where potentials form a proper subclass of functions. The proof uses Corollary 9-16 and the fact that in a null chain, $P^n \to 0$ (since $L = 0$).
