Theorem 9-15 is the central result of recurrent potential theory, analogous to the characterization of charges in the transient case. It states that if a function $f$ has zero total charge ($\alpha f = 0$) and the potential at a single state exists, then $f$ is automatically a charge. The theorem then establishes five fundamental properties: (1) the potential $g$ differs from ${}_0 N f$ only by a constant, (2) $f$ is recovered from $g$ via $I-P$, (3) $P^n g$ vanishes in the limit, (4)-(5) balayage and restriction properties with respect to the support. Conclusion (2) uniquely determines the charge from its potential.
