The Doeblin Ratio Limit Theorem (Theorem 9-4) is a cornerstone of recurrent potential theory. It asserts that for any four communicating states, the limit of the ratio of their occupation time counts $N_{ij}^{(n)} / N_{i'j'}^{(n)}$ always exists. In the recurrent case the limit depends only on the ratio of the regular measure values $\alpha_j / \alpha_{j'}$, while in the transient case it involves hitting probabilities and the dual chain.
