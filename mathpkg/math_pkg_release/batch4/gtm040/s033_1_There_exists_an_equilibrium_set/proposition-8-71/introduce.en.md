Proposition 8-71 establishes the fundamental property of the space-time chain: it is always transient regardless of the original stochastic process. Moreover, the Green's function $N_{0i}$, the hitting probability $H_{0i}$, and the $n$-step transition probability $P_{0i}^{(n)}$ from the initial state all equal the measure $\mu(U)$ of the cell $U$ corresponding to state $i$. This transience is what makes potential-theoretic methods applicable to general stochastic processes.
