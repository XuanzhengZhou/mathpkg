Proposition 9-13 gives a practical criterion for determining whether a function $f$ with $\alpha f = 0$ is a charge: one computes ${}_0 N f$ and checks whether $\lim_n P^n({}_0 N f)$ exists and is finite-valued. The potential $g$ is then expressed as the difference between ${}_0 N f$ and this limit. The proof relies on the explicit computation of $(I-P){}_0 N$, which reveals that ${}_0 N$ acts as a right inverse to $I-P$ up to a constant.
