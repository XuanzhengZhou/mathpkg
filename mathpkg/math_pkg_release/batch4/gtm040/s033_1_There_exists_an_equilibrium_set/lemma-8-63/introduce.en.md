Lemma 8-63 provides a sharp criterion for when a superregular function $h$ defined as the expectation of a random variable $z$ equals the potential $Nf$ of its charge $f = M[z - z^{(1)}]$. The condition is that the iterated shifts $z^{(n)}$ converge almost everywhere to zero. This lemma is a key step in the generalization from bounded to unbounded random variables in the potential theory of denumerable Markov chains.
