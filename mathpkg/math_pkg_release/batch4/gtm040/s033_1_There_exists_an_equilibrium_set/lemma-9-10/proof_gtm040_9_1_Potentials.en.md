If $i = 0$, both sides are zero. Otherwise, by duality,
$$\hat{N}_{ik}^{(n)} \leq {}_0 \hat{N}_{ik} + \hat{N}_{0k}^{(n)}.$$
Multiplying through by $\alpha_i/\alpha_k$, we obtain
$$N_{ki}^{(n)} \leq {}_0 N_{ki} + \frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_0} N_{k0}^{(n)}.$$
Similarly, the reverse inequality holds. Therefore,
$$\left| N_{ki}^{(n)} - \frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_0} N_{k0}^{(n)} \right| \leq {}_0 N_{ki} + {}_i N_{k0} \frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_0} = {}_0 N_{ii},$$
where the last equality follows from Lemma 9-9.
