**Proof:** For $E \in \mathcal{L}_0$,

$$k(E) = \sum_{j \in E} G_{0j} l_j^E$$

$$= \sum_{j \in E} \frac{^0\nu_j \hat{\lambda}_j^E}{\alpha_j}$$

$$= \sum_{j \in E} ^0\lambda_j \left( \frac{^0N_{jj}}{\alpha_j} \right) \hat{\lambda}_j^E.$$

Thus $k(E) > 0$ if and only if there is a non-zero term. Since $^0N_{jj}/\alpha_j > 0$, the first assertion of the proposition follows. The equivalent condition follows by duality.
