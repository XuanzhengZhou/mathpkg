Suppose $E$ is an equilibrium set. If $x$ is an equilibrium potential for $E$, then $B^E x = x$ by Proposition 8-19 and $x_E = 1$ by definition. By Theorem 8-4 and Proposition 5-8, $E$ is an equilibrium set if and only if $h^E$ is a potential, or equivalently $\alpha e^E < \infty$ and $\lim P^n h^E = 0$. But $s^E = \lim P^n h^E$ by conclusion (7) of Proposition 5-8.
