A superregular measure is a non-negative row vector $\pi$ satisfying $\pi \geq \pi P$, meaning the measure of each state is at least the sum of transition-probability-weighted measures from all states. This is the measure-theoretic analogue of superregular functions. In the potential theory of Markov chains, superregular measures are also known as excessive measures.
