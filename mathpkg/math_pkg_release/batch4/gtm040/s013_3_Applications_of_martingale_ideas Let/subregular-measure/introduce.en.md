A subregular measure is a non-negative row vector $\pi$ satisfying $\pi \leq \pi P$, meaning the measure of each state is at most the sum of transition-probability-weighted measures from all states. This is the measure-theoretic analogue of subregular functions. Together with regular and superregular measures, subregular measures form the triplet of inequalities that generalize the invariant measure equation $\pi = \pi P$ in Markov chain potential theory.
