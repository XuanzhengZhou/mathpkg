A superregular function is a column vector $f$ on the state space of a Markov chain with transition matrix $P$ satisfying $f \geq Pf$. This means the function value at each state is at least the $P$-weighted average of its values at neighboring states. Superregular functions are the discrete analogue of superharmonic functions and correspond naturally to supermartingales when evaluated along sample paths of the Markov chain.
