This theorem establishes the fundamental connection between $P$-regular functions and martingale theory for Markov chains. If $h$ is a $P$-regular function (satisfying $h = Ph$) with finite absolute expectation, then the process obtained by evaluating $h$ along the sample paths $\{x_n\}$ of the Markov chain forms a martingale with respect to the natural filtration $\mathcal{R}_n$ generated by the process history. This result bridges the potential-theoretic notion of regularity with the probabilistic notion of martingales, enabling the application of martingale convergence theorems to the study of regular functions on Markov chains.
