Theorem 6-38 is the central convergence theorem for denumerable Markov chains. It asserts that for any noncyclic recurrent chain, the $n$-step transition probabilities $P_{ij}^{(n)}$ converge as $n 	o \infty$. In the ergodic (positive recurrent) case, the limit is the matrix $1lpha$ whose rows are all equal to the unique normalized regular measure $lpha$. In the null recurrent case, all entries converge to zero. Two proofs are given in the text: the first uses Lemma 6-37 and properties of regular measures; the second uses the discrete Renewal Theorem (Theorem 1-67) via the first-passage decomposition.
