Proposition 6-33 is the converse to Proposition 6-32: every sums of independent random variables ladder process (a random walk on the non-negative integers) can be realized as the ladder process of some recurrent Markov chain. The construction uses the "basic example" (birth-and-death chain) with state $s = 0$. The step probabilities $p'_k$ of the ladder process determine the parameters $\{p_i, q_i\}$ of the basic example through the relations $p'_n = eta_{n-1} q_n = eta_{n-1} - eta_n$, and the resulting chain is guaranteed to be recurrent.
