Lemma 6-36 is a zero-one law for noncyclic recurrent Markov chains. It states that for any partition of the non-negative integers into two sets $E$ and $F$, the set of times at which the chain visits a fixed state $s$ must, with probability 1, be infinite in at least one of the two partitions. Moreover, which partition achieves probability 1 is independent of the starting distribution. This lemma is a consequence of Proposition 6-32 (the ladder process construction) and the zero-one law for sums of independent random variables, and it serves as a crucial ingredient in Lemma 6-37 and the proof of the convergence theorem.
