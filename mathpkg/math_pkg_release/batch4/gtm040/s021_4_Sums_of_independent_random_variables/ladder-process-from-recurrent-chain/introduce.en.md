Proposition 6-32 establishes a fundamental connection between general recurrent Markov chains and sums of independent random variables processes. For any recurrent chain $P$ and a fixed state $s$, the successive return times to $s$ (called the ladder times) form themselves the state variables of a sums of independent random variables chain $P^*$ on the integers. The transition probabilities of $P^*$ are given by the first return probabilities of the original chain: $P^*_{ij} = ar{F}_{ss}^{(j-i)}$. This construction is essential for extending results from sums of independent random variables (where zero-one laws and renewal theory apply) to general recurrent chains.
