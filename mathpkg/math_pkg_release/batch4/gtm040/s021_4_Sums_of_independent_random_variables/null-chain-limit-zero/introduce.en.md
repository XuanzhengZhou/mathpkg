Corollary 6-39 extends the null-chain conclusion of Theorem 6-38 by removing the noncyclicity hypothesis. It states that for any null (recurrent but not positive recurrent) chain, every entry of $P^n$ tends to zero as $n 	o \infty$. The proof uses the cyclic decomposition: a cyclic null chain with period $d$ decomposes into $d$ cyclically moving classes, and on each class the $d$-step transition submatrix $P^d$ is noncyclic and null, so its powers tend to zero by Theorem 6-38. Intermediate powers also tend to zero because they are products of a matrix tending to zero with a bounded matrix.
