Lemma 6-37 states that for any noncyclic recurrent Markov chain, the difference between two initial probability distributions washes out as the chain evolves: the total variation distance $\|(eta - \gamma)P^n\|$ between the $n$-step distributions tends to zero. This "mixing" property is the essential probabilistic content of the convergence theorem. Its proof constructs auxiliary vectors $eta^{(n)}$ and $\gamma^{(n)}$ and uses the zero-one law of Lemma 6-36 to control the mass that separates the two evolutions.
