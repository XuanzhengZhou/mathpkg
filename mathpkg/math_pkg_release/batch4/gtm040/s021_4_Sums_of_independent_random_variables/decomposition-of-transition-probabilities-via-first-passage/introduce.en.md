Lemma 6-34 provides the fundamental decomposition identity that links $n$-step transition probabilities $P_{ij}^{(n)}$ with first passage probabilities $\bar{F}_{ij}^{(k)}$. The identity states that to go from $i$ to $j$ in $n$ steps, one must first reach $j$ for the first time at some step $k \leq n$, and then go from $j$ to $j$ in the remaining $n-k$ steps. This convolution relation is the discrete renewal equation for Markov chains and serves as the basis for the renewal-theoretic proof of the convergence theorem in this section.
