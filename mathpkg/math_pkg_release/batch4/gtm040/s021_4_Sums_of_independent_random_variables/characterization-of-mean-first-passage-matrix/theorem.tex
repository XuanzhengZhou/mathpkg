If $P$ is an ergodic chain, the mean first passage time matrix $M$ is uniquely characterized by the following three properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $M_{ii} = 0$ for all $i$ and $M \geq 0$.
\item[(2)] $M$ is finite-valued.
\item[(3)] $(I - P)M = E - D$, where $E$ is the matrix of all ones and $D$ is the diagonal matrix with $D_{ii} = 1/\alpha_i$.
\end{enumerate}
