Definition 6-30 formalizes two fundamental properties of sequences of random variables taking values in a denumerable set. The random variables $\{y_n\}$ are called **independent** if the joint probability of any finite collection of them falling into specified sets factors as the product of the individual probabilities. They are called **identically distributed** if the marginal distribution $\Pr[y_n \in E]$ does not depend on the index $n$. These two properties together --- independence and identical distribution (i.i.d.) --- underpin the theory of sums of independent random variables processes introduced in this section.
