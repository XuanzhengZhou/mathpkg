We have
$$2(g, h^E) = \mu h^E + \eta^E g$$
$$= \mu h^E + \eta^E Nf.$$

Since $P = \hat{P}$, we have $\mu \hat{N} = (\mu N)\hat{\phantom{x}} = \nu \hat{\phantom{x}}$. Also $h^E = N \eta^E$ (by definition of equilibrium potential) and $\eta^E$ has support in $E$. Therefore

$$\mu h^E = \mu N \eta^E = \nu \eta^E.$$

On the other hand, $\eta^E Nf = \eta^E g$. Since $g$ has support in $E$ and $\eta^E$ is supported on $E$, we have $\eta^E g = \eta^E_E g_E$. Using the symmetry $P = \hat{P}$ and the dual relations, one obtains

$$2(g, h^E) = \nu \eta^E + \eta^E Nf = 2 \alpha f,$$

hence $(g, h^E) = \alpha f$.
