The non-negativity of energy extends to all potentials of finite energy, not just pure potentials. By decomposing $g = Nf = Nf^+ - Nf^-$ into its positive and negative parts, and applying Schwarz's inequality to the cross term, one obtains $\mathbf{I}(g) \geq (\sqrt{\mathbf{I}(Nf^+)} - \sqrt{\mathbf{I}(Nf^-)})^2 \geq 0$.
