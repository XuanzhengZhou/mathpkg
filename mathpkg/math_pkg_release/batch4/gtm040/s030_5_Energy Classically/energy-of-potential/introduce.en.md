Classically, energy is the integral of the potential with respect to the charge. In the discrete Markov chain setting, if $g = Nf$ is a potential with associated charge $\mu = \text{dual } f$ and $\nu = \text{dual } g$, the energy is defined as the pairing $\mathbf{I}(g) = \mu g = \nu f$, provided $|\mu| N |f| < \infty$. This definition generalizes the classical notion to denumerable state spaces.
