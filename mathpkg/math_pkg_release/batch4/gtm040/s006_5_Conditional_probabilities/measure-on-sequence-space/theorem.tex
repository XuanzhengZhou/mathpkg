The measure on a sequence space is completely determined by
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] the starting probabilities, $\Pr[x_0 = c_0]$, and
\item[(2)] the transition probabilities,
$$\Pr[x_n = c_n \mid x_0 = c_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_{n-1} = c_{n-1}].$$
\end{enumerate}

Equivalently, for any $n$ and any $c_0, \ldots, c_n$,
\begin{align*}
\Pr[x_0 = c_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_n = c_n]
&= \Pr[x_0 = c_0] \\
&\quad \times \Pr[x_1 = c_1 \mid x_0 = c_0] \\
&\quad \times \Pr[x_2 = c_2 \mid x_0 = c_0 \wedge x_1 = c_1] \\
&\quad \times \cdots \\
&\quad \times \Pr[x_n = c_n \mid x_0 = c_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_{n-1} = c_{n-1}].
\end{align*}
