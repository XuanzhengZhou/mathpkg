Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{B}, \mu)$ be a probability space. If $p$ and $q$ are statements such that $\Pr[q] \neq 0$, the \textbf{conditional probability} of $p$ given $q$, written $\Pr[p \mid q]$, is defined by
$$\Pr[p \mid q] = \frac{\Pr[p \wedge q]}{\Pr[q]}.$$
If $\Pr[q] = 0$, we normally agree that $\Pr[p \mid q] = 0$. (Alternatively, we might leave $\Pr[p \mid q]$ undefined if $\Pr[q] = 0$.)
