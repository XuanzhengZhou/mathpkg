If $T = \mathbb{N}$ and $\mu \in \mathcal{G}_V$, then $\{x_n\}$ is a Markov process. In particular, there are probability measures $\pi_n$ and transition matrices $P_n$, $n \geq 0$, such that

$$\pi_{n,i} = \Pr[x_n = i], \quad P_{n,ij} = \Pr[x_{n+1} = j \mid x_n = i],$$

and $\pi_n P_n = \pi_{n+1}$. Similarly, if $T = -\mathbb{N}$ and $\mu \in \mathcal{G}_V$, then $\{x_n\}$ is a Markov process on $-\mathbb{N}$, and there are probability measures $\pi_n$ and transition matrices $P_n$, $n < 0$, satisfying the above relations.
