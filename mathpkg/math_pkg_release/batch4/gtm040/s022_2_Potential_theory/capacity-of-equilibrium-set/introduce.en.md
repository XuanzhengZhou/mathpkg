Capacity is the potential-theoretic analog of the electrostatic capacitance of a conductor. For an equilibrium set $E$, its capacity is the total amount of charge required to raise the potential on $E$ to a constant value of 1. Dimensional analysis shows capacity has units of $q/V$ (charge per unit potential), consistent with the physical notion of capacitance. Capacity is a fundamental set function in potential theory and serves as a measure of the "size" of a set from the perspective of the Newtonian potential.
