This theorem gives a variational characterization of the equilibrium potential as the minimal potential among all those generated by non-negative charges that are at least 1 on $E$. It shows that the equilibrium potential is the solution to an obstacle problem: the smallest non-negative-charge potential that majorizes the indicator of $E$. This infimum characterization connects equilibrium potentials to the Dirichlet principle and capacity theory, and illustrates the minimizing nature of equilibrium configurations.
