In potential theory, the Green's function is the kernel $G(x,y)=1/(2\pi|x-y|)$ of the potential operator, which maps a charge distribution to its potential via $g(x)=\int G(x,y)d\mu(y)$. This function is the fundamental solution of Laplace's equation in $\mathbb{R}^3$ and encodes the geometric interaction between points. It generalizes the Coulomb potential $q/|x-y|$ to the continuum setting and serves as the central analytic object through which charges and potentials are related.
