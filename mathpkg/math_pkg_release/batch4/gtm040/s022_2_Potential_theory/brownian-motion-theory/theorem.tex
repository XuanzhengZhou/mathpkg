Let $P^t$ be the operator defined on measures $\mu$ on $\mathbb{R}^3$ by

$$(\mu P^t)(A) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^3} \left[ \int_A \frac{1}{(2\pi t)^{3/2}} e^{-|x-y|^2/2t} dy \right] d\mu(x).$$

The totality of theorems about the operators $P^t$, the measures $\mu$ on $\mathbb{R}^3$ with $\mu(\mathbb{R}^3) = 1$, and quantities definable in terms of them and properties of $\mathbb{R}^3$ is called Brownian motion theory.

We immediately extend $P^t$ by linearity to be defined on all finite measures on $\mathbb{R}^3$ and all differences of two finite measures.
