The principle of domination is a central result in classical potential theory. It states that if one potential from a non-negative charge dominates another on the support of the latter's charge, then the domination extends globally to all of $\mathbb{R}^3$, and the total charge of the dominating potential is at least as large. This principle captures a maximum-type property of Newtonian potentials and is essential for proving uniqueness results and comparison theorems throughout potential theory.
