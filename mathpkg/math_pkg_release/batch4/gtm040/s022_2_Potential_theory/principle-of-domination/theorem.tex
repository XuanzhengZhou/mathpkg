Let $h$ and $g$ be potentials arising from non-negative charges $\bar{\mu}$ and $\mu$ respectively on $\mathbb{R}^3$. If $h$ dominates $g$ on the support of $\mu$, i.e.,

$$h(x) \geq g(x) \quad \text{for all } x \in \operatorname{supp}(\mu),$$

then $h$ dominates $g$ everywhere on $\mathbb{R}^3$ and, moreover, $\bar{\mu}(\mathbb{R}^3) \geq \mu(\mathbb{R}^3)$.
