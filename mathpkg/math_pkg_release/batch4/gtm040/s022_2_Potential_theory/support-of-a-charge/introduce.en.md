The support of a charge distribution is the smallest closed set outside of which the charge vanishes identically. Formally, it is the complement of the largest open set on which the charge is zero on every measurable subset. The support plays a crucial role in potential theory: the principle of domination relates the behavior of potentials on the support of one charge to global domination, and the principle of balayage (sweeping) constructs potentials whose charges are supported on prescribed closed sets.
