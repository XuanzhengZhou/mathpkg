If $g$ is a potential on $\mathbb{R}^3$ arising from a non-negative charge and $E \subset \mathbb{R}^3$ is a closed set, then there exists a unique potential $\bar{g}$ arising from a non-negative charge with support contained in $E$ such that

$$\bar{g}(x) = g(x) \quad \text{for all } x \in E.$$

The potential $\bar{g}$ is called the balayage (or swept potential) of $g$ onto $E$.
