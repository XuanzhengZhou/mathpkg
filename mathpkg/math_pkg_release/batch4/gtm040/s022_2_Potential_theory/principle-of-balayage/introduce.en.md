The principle of balayage (French for "sweeping") asserts that given any potential $g$ from a non-negative charge and any closed set $E$, there is a unique potential $\bar{g}$ whose charge is entirely supported on $E$ and which agrees with $g$ on $E$. Physically, this corresponds to "sweeping" the charge onto the set $E$ in such a way that the potential remains unchanged on $E$. This principle is fundamental to the solution of the Dirichlet problem and to the relationship between potentials and their equilibrium counterparts.
