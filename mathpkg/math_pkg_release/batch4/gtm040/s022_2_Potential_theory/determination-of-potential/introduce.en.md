This theorem strengthens the uniqueness of charge by showing that a potential is determined by its restriction to the support of its charge distribution. This is a remarkable fact: the values of the potential on the support alone---a possibly very thin set---are sufficient to reconstruct the potential everywhere. This property underlies the principle of balayage and is fundamental to the Dirichlet problem in potential theory.
