The potential function generalizes the classical Coulomb potential from a finite collection of point charges to an arbitrary charge distribution $\mu$ on $\mathbb{R}^3$. For point charges $q_1,\ldots,q_n$ at positions $x_1,\ldots,x_n$, the superposition principle gives the potential as $\frac{1}{2\pi}\sum_i q_i/|x-x_i|$. Passing to the limit yields the integral representation $g(x)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int |x-y|^{-1}d\mu(y)$, which is the central object of study in classical potential theory. Lemma 7-2 guarantees this integral is finite almost everywhere.
