Classical potential theory is the mathematical discipline that studies the relationship between charge distributions on $\mathbb{R}^3$ and their associated potential functions. Originating from Coulomb's law of electrostatic attraction, it abstracts the physical notions of charge, distance, and potential into a self-contained mathematical framework. The theory encompasses electrostatics as a special case, with all electrostatic quantities---capacity, energy, field, force---expressible in terms of the three fundamental notions of distance, charge, and potential.
