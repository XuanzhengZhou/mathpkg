A potential function $g$ uniquely determines the charge distribution $\mu$ from which it arises. That is, if

$$\frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{\mathbb{R}^3} \frac{d\mu_1(y)}{|x - y|} = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{\mathbb{R}^3} \frac{d\mu_2(y)}{|x - y|}$$

for almost every $x \in \mathbb{R}^3$, then $\mu_1 = \mu_2$ as measures.
