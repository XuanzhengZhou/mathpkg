This theorem asserts that the mapping from charge distributions to their potential functions is injective: a potential function uniquely determines the charge that produces it. In physical terms, this means one cannot have two different charge configurations that produce exactly the same electrostatic potential everywhere. This result is foundational because it justifies working with potentials as faithful representations of the underlying charge distributions.
