An equilibrium set is a closed subset of $\mathbb{R}^3$ for which an equilibrium potential exists---that is, there is a potential function that is identically 1 on the set and generated by a charge distribution supported on the set. Not every closed set is an equilibrium set; the existence of an equilibrium potential imposes regularity conditions. Equilibrium sets are the natural domains for defining capacity, and the theory of such sets is a major topic in classical potential theory.
