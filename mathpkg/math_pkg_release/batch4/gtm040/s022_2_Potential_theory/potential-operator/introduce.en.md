The potential operator is the linear integral operator that maps a charge distribution $\mu$ on $\mathbb{R}^3$ to its potential function $g$. It is a convolution-type operator whose kernel is $1/(2\pi|x-y|)$, the Newtonian potential kernel (also called the Green's function for the Laplacian in $\mathbb{R}^3$). This operator is fundamental to potential theory as it establishes the correspondence between charges and potentials that the entire theory studies.
