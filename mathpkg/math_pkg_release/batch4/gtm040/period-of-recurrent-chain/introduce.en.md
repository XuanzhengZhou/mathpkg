For a recurrent Markov chain, the set $T$ of positive integers $k$ for which the $k$-step return probability $(P^k)_{ii}$ is positive forms an additive semigroup (Lemma 6-26). The greatest common divisor $d$ of all elements of $T$ is defined as the period of the chain with respect to state $i$. Intuitively, the period measures the cyclic behavior of the chain: if $d > 1$, returns to $i$ can only occur at times that are multiples of $d$. Lemma 6-27 guarantees that after a sufficiently long time, returns are possible at every multiple of $d$. Proposition 6-28 then shows that $d$ is independent of the choice of state $i$.
