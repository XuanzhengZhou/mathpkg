If $P$ is strongly ergodic with stationary distribution $\alpha$, then the following identities hold relating the mean passage time matrix $M$, the fundamental matrix $Z$, and their duals for the reverse chain $\hat{P}$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $M_{\alpha j} = \dfrac{Z_{jj}}{\alpha_j} - 1$,
\item $Z_{jj} = \bar{M}_{\alpha j} \, \alpha_j$,
\item $M_{\alpha j} - M_{ij} = \hat{M}_{\alpha i} - \hat{M}_{ji}$.
\end{enumerate}

Here $M_{\alpha j} = \sum_i \alpha_i M_{ij}$ is the mean first passage time to state $j$ starting from the stationary distribution, $\bar{M}_{\alpha j}$ is the corresponding quantity for the reverse chain, and $Z$ is the fundamental matrix $Z = (I - P + A)^{-1}$ where $A = 1\alpha$ is the limiting matrix.
