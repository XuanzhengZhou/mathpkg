For the basic example Markov chain $P$, the energy of a charge $f$ (with $\beta|f| < \infty$) is given by
$$\mathbf{I}(g) = \frac{1}{\beta_\infty} (\beta f)^2 - \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} f_i \sum_{j=0}^{i-1} \beta_j f_j,$$
where $g = Nf$ is the potential of $f$ given by formula (1), and $\beta f = \sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \beta_j f_j$.

This expression is obtained from the potential representation formula
$$g_i = \frac{1}{\beta_\infty} \sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \beta_j f_j - \frac{1}{\beta_i} \sum_{j=0}^{i-1} \beta_j f_j$$
by computing $\mathbf{I}(g) = (f, g) = \sum_i f_i g_i$. Formula (2) (for $\hat{P}$) yields the same value of energy for the same charge $f$, consistent with the duality between $P$ and $\hat{P}$.
