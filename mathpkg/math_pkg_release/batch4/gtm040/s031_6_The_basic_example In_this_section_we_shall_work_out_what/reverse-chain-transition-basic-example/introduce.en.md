For the basic example of a denumerable Markov chain on the non-negative integers, the $\beta$-dual (reverse) chain $\hat{P}$ is computed explicitly from the transition probabilities of the forward chain $P$. The reverse process moves deterministically leftward until reaching state $0$, from which it may jump to any state with probability proportional to the decrease in the $\beta$ sequence. In the transient case, the extended chain for $\hat{P}$ is absorbing because the total probability of leaving $0$ is strictly less than $1$.
