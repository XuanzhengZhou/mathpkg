**Proof:** Let $A$ and $B$ be disjoint sets in $\mathcal{F}^*$ and pick $\{A_n\}$ and $\{B_n\}$ in $\mathcal{F}$ such that $A_n \rightarrow A$ and $B_n \rightarrow B$. Then since $\nu$ is additive on $\mathcal{F}$ and since $\mu$ agrees with $\nu$ on sets of $\mathcal{F}$, we have

$$\mu(A_n \cup B_n) + \mu(A_n \cap B_n) = \mu(A_n) + \mu(B_n).$$

By Lemma 1-25,

$$\mu(A \cup B) + \mu(A \cap B) = \mu(A) + \mu(B)$$

or

$$\mu(A \cup B) = \mu(A) + \mu(B).$$
