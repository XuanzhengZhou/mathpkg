This proposition establishes that matrix multiplication distributes over matrix addition, provided the relevant products and sums are well defined. The proof reduces to the entrywise distributivity of extended real numbers, using the representation of matrix products as products of row vectors with column vectors. Distributivity is one of the fundamental algebraic properties required for a workable theory of denumerable matrix multiplication.
