Under either condition (1) or (2), all the products involved are well defined and finite. By distributivity (Proposition 1-2),

$$(A(B - C))D = (AB - AC)D = (AB)D - (AC)D,$$

and similarly

$$A((B - C)D) = A(BD - CD) = A(BD) - A(CD).$$

Since $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$ are non-negative, Proposition 1-4 gives $(AB)D = A(BD)$ and $(AC)D = A(CD)$. Therefore the two expressions are equal.
