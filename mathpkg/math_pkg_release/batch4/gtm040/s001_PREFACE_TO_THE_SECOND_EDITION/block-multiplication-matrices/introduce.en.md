This proposition establishes that matrix multiplication can be performed blockwise, exactly as in the finite-dimensional case, provided the index sets are suitably partitioned. The blocks of the product are computed by multiplying and adding blocks of the factors just as if the blocks were scalar entries. The proof relies on the fact that matrix multiplication is defined independently of any ordering on the index sets, and the result holds even when some index sets are infinite.
