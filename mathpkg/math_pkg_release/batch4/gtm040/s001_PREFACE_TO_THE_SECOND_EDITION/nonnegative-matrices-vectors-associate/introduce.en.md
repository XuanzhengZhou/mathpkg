This corollary extends associativity to the case where one factor is a column vector (which is a matrix with a singleton second index set). The proof handles the positive and negative parts of $f$ separately. The second assertion is particularly important for Markov chain theory: it guarantees that the $n$-step transition operator $C^n$ applied to a function $f$ can be computed iteratively, which is the basis for the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations.
