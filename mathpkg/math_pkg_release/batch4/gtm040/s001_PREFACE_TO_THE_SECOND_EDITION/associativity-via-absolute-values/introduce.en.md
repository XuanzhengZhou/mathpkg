This corollary extends associativity from non-negative matrices to arbitrary (signed) matrices, provided that the products of their absolute values remain finite. The proof decomposes each matrix into its positive and negative parts, applies distributivity (Proposition 1-2), and then uses the associativity of non-negative matrices (Proposition 1-4) on the resulting terms. This provides a practical sufficient condition for associativity.
