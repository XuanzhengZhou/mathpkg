This corollary provides three practical sufficient conditions under which matrix multiplication is associative: (1) $A$ is row-finite, meaning each row has finitely many non-zero entries; (2) $C$ is column-finite, meaning each column has finitely many non-zero entries; or (3) $B$ has finite entries and both $A$ and $C$ are bounded. These conditions are often satisfied in applications, particularly in Markov chain theory where transition matrices are row-stochastic (hence row-finite with non-negative entries summing to 1).
