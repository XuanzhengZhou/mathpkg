A \textbf{denumerable stochastic process} is defined on a sequence space $\Omega$ with a denumerable state space $S$ and consists of:
(1) An increasing sequence of Borel fields $\mathcal{B}_0 \subset \mathcal{B}_1 \subset \cdots \subset \mathcal{B}$ such that each $\mathcal{B}_n$ contains exactly the sets determined by conditions on $x_0, \ldots, x_n$.
(2) A probability measure $\mu$ on $\mathcal{B}$ such that the future does not influence the present: for any set $A \in \mathcal{B}_n$, $\Pr[A \mid x_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_n] = \Pr[A \mid x_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_{n+1}]$.
