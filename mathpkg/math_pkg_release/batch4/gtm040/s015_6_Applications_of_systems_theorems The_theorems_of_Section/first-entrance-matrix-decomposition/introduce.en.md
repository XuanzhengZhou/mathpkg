Proposition 4-14 expresses the hitting probability matrices $H$ and $\bar{H}$ as sums of the first-time entrance distribution matrices $F^{(k)}$ and $\bar{F}^{(k)}$. The first two identities follow from the disjoint decomposition of the event "eventually reach $j$" into "first reach $j$ at time $k$." The third identity $\bar{H} = PH$ relates the shifted hitting probability to the original via a one-step transition, obtained by applying the systems theorem for statements (Theorem 4-10).
