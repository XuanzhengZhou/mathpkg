In the notation of Theorem 4-10, if $t_j$ is taken as the random time, then $p'$ is the statement $h_k$. The theorem applies and

$$\Pr\nolimits_i[p] = \sum_m \Pr\nolimits_i[x_{t_j} = m] \, \Pr\nolimits_m[p'] = \Pr\nolimits_i[x_{t_j} = j] \, \Pr\nolimits_j[h_k] = H_{ij} H_{jk}.$$
