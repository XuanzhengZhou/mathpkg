Proposition 4-15 establishes a fundamental factorization: the expected number of visits to state $j$ starting from $i$ equals the probability of ever hitting $j$ from $i$ multiplied by the expected number of visits to $j$ starting from $j$. The same holds for the shifted quantities. For the diagonal case, $N_{ii} = 1 + \bar{H}_{ii} N_{ii}$ accounts for the initial visit at time zero separately from subsequent returns to state $i$.
