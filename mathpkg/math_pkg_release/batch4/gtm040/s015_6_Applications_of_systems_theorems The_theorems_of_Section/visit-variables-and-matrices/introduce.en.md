The visit variables $h_j, n_j, t_j, f_j^{(k)}$ provide a systematic language for describing when and how often a Markov chain visits a given state $j$. The shifted versions $\bar{h}_j, \bar{n}_j, \bar{t}_j, \bar{f}_j^{(k)}$ refer to the same quantities evaluated on the shifted path $\omega_1$, which starts one step later.

The matrices $H, N, F^{(k)}, \bar{H}, \bar{N}$ collect these probabilities and expectations across all pairs of initial and target states. They form the algebraic backbone for the classification of states in denumerable Markov chains and are the primary objects manipulated by the systems theorems of Section 5.
