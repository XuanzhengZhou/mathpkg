The second identity follows from the first by adding $I$ to both sides. To obtain the first one, we apply Theorem 4-11 with $f = \bar{n}_j$ and the random time identically one. Then $f' = n_j$ since $\bar{n}_j(\omega) = n_j(\omega_1)$ by definition, and thus

$$M_i[\bar{n}_j] = \sum_k \Pr\nolimits_i[x_1 = k] \, M_k[n_j] = \sum_k P_{ik} \, M_k[n_j]$$

or $\bar{N} = PN$.
