**Proof:** Write $f = f^+ - f^-$. Then $Nf^+$ and $Nf^-$ are both finite-valued by hypothesis. Since $PN + I = N$, we have $PN \leq N$ and hence $PNf^+ \leq Nf^+$ and $PNf^- \leq Nf^-$. Therefore, by Corollary 1-5,

$$\begin{align*}
(I - P)(Nf) &= Nf - P(Nf) = Nf - (PN)f \\
&= Nf - (N - I)f \\
&= f.
\end{align*}$$
