A set $A$ is called thin at a boundary point $x \in B_e$ if it is the complement of a fine neighborhood of $x$. By Proposition 10-47's zero-one law, this is equivalent to the condition that the $K(\cdot, x)$-process visits $A$ infinitely often with probability $0$. The concept of thinness is the probabilistic analog of the classical potential-theoretic notion: a set is thin at a point if the process starting from that point almost surely eventually avoids the set. This is a core concept in the fine topology of denumerable Markov chains.
