Proposition 10-47 establishes a fundamental zero-one law for the fine topology on $S^* = S \cup B_e$. For any boundary point $x \in B_e$, the probability that the $h$-process with $h = K(\cdot, x)$ eventually remains in a given set $A$ can only be $0$ or $1$. Consequently, fine neighborhoods of $x$ are precisely those sets $A$ containing $x$ for which this probability is positive (hence equal to $1$). This zero-one law follows from Lemma 10-46 and Proposition 5-19, and it is the basis for the entire fine topology framework.
