Proposition 10-45 establishes that any non-negative $\pi$-integrable function of the form $g = Nf$ (where $N$ is the potential kernel and $f \geq 0$) satisfies $\lim_{n \to \infty} g(x_n) = 0$ almost surely under both the standard probability measure $\Pr$ and the $h$-process measure $\Pr^{K(\cdot, x)}$ for almost every boundary point $x$. This result extends the limit properties of Section 8 from regular functions to the broader class of superregular functions, using the fact that $\{g(x_n)\}$ forms a non-negative supermartingale.
