Let $\omega \in \Omega$ and let $r_j$ be the statement that $\omega$ is in a cell $R_j$ of $\mathcal{R}$. If $f$ is a random variable, then the \textbf{conditional mean of $f$ given $\mathcal{R}$}, written $M[f \mid \mathcal{R}]$, is defined to be a function of $\omega$ whose value at every point in the cell $R_j$ is the constant $M[f \mid r_j]$.
