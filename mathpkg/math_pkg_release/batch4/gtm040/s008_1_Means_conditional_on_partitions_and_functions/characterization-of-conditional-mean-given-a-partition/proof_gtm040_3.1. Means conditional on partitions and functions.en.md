Set $g = M[f \mid \mathcal{R}]$. If $g$ is the conditional mean, then $g$ satisfies (1) by definition. If $\mu(R_j) = 0$, both sides of (2) are 0. If $\mu(R_j) > 0$, equality follows from the definition of $M[f \mid \mathcal{R}]$.

Conversely, if a function $g$ satisfies (1) and (2), then it agrees with $M[f \mid \mathcal{R}]$ on all paths in cells of positive measure; that is, it agrees with $M[f \mid \mathcal{R}]$ a.e.
