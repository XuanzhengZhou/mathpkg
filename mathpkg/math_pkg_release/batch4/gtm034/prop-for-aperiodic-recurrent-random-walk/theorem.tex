For aperiodic recurrent random walk

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{G_n(0,x)}{G_n(0,0)} = 1, \quad x \in R.$$

Proof: By P1.5, the limit is $F(0,x)$. But by P5, $F(0,x) = 1$ for all $x$ in $R^+ = \bar{R}$, and as the random walk is aperiodic this gives $F(0,x) = 1$ in all of $R$.
