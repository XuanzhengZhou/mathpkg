When $P(x,y)$ is the transition function of aperiodic recurrent two-dimensional random walk, and $B \subset R$, $1 \leq |B| < \infty$, we call a non-negative function $\rho(x)$ such that $\rho(x) = 0$ on $R - B$ a charge distribution on $B$. A function $f(x)$ on $R$ is called a potential due to the charge $\rho$ on $B$ if

(i) $Pf(x) - f(x) = 0$ for $x \in R - B$,
(ii) $Pf(x) - f(x) = \rho(x)$ for $x \in B$,
(iii) $f(x) \geq 0$ for $x \in R$.
