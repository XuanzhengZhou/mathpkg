Kesten's theorem establishes that the ratio of consecutive return probabilities $F_{n+1}/F_n$ tends to $1$ for strongly aperiodic recurrent random walk in one dimension. This result, originally proved by Harry Kesten in 1963, is the key technical ingredient needed to handle the one-dimensional recurrent case with infinite variance in the proof of T1. The statement also holds in two dimensions.
