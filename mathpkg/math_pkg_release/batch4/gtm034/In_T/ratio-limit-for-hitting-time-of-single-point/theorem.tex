Let $\{x_n\}$ be an arbitrary aperiodic random walk, recurrent or transient, of dimension $d \geq 1$. Let $T = \min\{n \mid 1 \leq n \leq \infty; x_n = 0\}$ be the time of first visit to the origin after time $0$. Then for every $x \neq 0$,

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{P_x[T > n]}{P_0[T > n]} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{k=0}^{n} \left[P_k(0,0) - P_k(x,0)\right].$$

Both limits are finite and non-negative.
