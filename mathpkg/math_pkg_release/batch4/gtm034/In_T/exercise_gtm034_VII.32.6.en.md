Let $x \neq 0$ be an arbitrary point in $R$ (but not the origin). Show that either $f(x) \equiv 0$ for $x \neq 0$ or $f(x) > 0$ for all $x \neq 0$, and characterize those random walks which give $f(x) \equiv 0$. (Here $f(x)$ is a limit defined in the preceding discussion.)
