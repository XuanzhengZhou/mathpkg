Proposition P3 gives the precise asymptotic behavior of the survival probability $P_0[T > n]$ for one-dimensional recurrent random walk with finite variance $\sigma^2$. The proof combines the characteristic function estimate $\sqrt{1-t}\,U(t) \to 1/(\sqrt{2}\sigma)$ as $t \to 1$, the identity $\sum R_n t^n = [(1-t)U(t)]^{-1}$, and Karamata's theorem (strong form from P20.2) to deduce $\sqrt{n}R_n \to \sigma\sqrt{2/\pi}$.
