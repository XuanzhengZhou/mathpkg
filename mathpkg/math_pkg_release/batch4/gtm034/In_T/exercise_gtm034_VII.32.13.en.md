Continuation. Prove that the limit in Problem 12 also holds in one dimension when $\sigma^2 < \infty$. However, when $\sigma^2 = \infty$, show that either (i) holds, or

$$\text{(ii)} \quad \lim_{x \to +\infty} a(x) = M < \infty, \quad \lim_{x \to -\infty} a(x) = +\infty,$$

or

$$\text{(iii)} \quad \lim_{x \to +\infty} a(x) = +\infty, \quad \lim_{x \to -\infty} a(x) = M < \infty.$$

Note: The exact criteria for this classification are still unknown.
