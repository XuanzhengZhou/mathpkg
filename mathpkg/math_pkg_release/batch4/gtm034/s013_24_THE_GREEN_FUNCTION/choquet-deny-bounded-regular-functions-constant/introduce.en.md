The Choquet-Deny theorem states that for aperiodic transient random walk, any bounded regular (harmonic) function must be constant. This is a fundamental result in potential theory, establishing that the bounded harmonic functions on a transient random walk are trivial, in contrast to recurrent random walks which may admit non-constant bounded harmonic functions.
