For aperiodic random walk with mean 0 and finite variance $\sigma^2$, the hitting probability of a half-line has a limit at infinity. If $B = [x | -\infty < x \leq -1]$, then

$$\lim_{x \to +\infty} H_B(x,y) = \sqrt{2c} \frac{e^\alpha}{\sigma} \sum_{t=0}^{\infty} [v(0) + v(1) + \cdots + v(t)]P(t,y), \quad y \in B.$$
