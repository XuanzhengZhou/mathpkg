In one dimension, when $m_k < \infty$, the $k^{\text{th}}$ derivative $\phi^{(k)}(\theta)$ is a continuous function, and $\phi^{(k)}(0) = (i)^k \mu_k$. The converse is false, i.e. $\phi^{(1)}(0)$ may exist without $m_1$ being finite, but if $\phi^{(2)}(0)$ exists, then $m_2 = -\phi^{(2)}(0) < \infty$.
