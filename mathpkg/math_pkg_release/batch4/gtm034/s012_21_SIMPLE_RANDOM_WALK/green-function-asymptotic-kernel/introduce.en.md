This is the main theorem of Section 23 and one of the central results of the absorption problem. It proves rigorously that the interval Green function $g_N$, scaled by $\sigma^2/(2N)$, converges uniformly to the kernel $R(s,t) = \min(s,t) - st$, which is the Green function of the second derivative operator on $[0,1]$. This connects the discrete potential theory of random walk with the classical potential theory of the Laplacian. The proof is a careful estimation combining the subtraction formula (P1), the factorization of the half-line Green function (P22.4), the uniform convergence of $R_N(x)$ (T22.1), and bounds on error terms using properties of a null sequence $\epsilon(n)$.
