This proposition provides exact closed-form expressions for all absorption problem quantities in the case of simple symmetric random walk. The Green function $g_N(x,y)$ is proportional to the kernel $R$ evaluated at appropriately scaled points, revealing that $g_N$ is (up to scaling) the discrete Green function. The hitting probabilities $R_N(x)$ and $L_N(x)$ are linear functions of $x$, showing that for simple random walk the probability of exiting on the right is proportional to the distance from the left boundary. These formulas follow from the spectral decomposition of $Q_N$ and represent the rare case where all absorption problem quantities can be computed in elementary closed form.
