This proposition introduces Gábor Szegő's theory of orthogonal polynomials associated with symmetric Toeplitz matrices, applied to the transition matrix of a symmetric random walk. The polynomials $p_n(z)$ are constructed via the Gram-Schmidt process with respect to the weight $1 - \phi(\theta)$ on the unit circle, and they serve as a functional calculus for the restricted transition matrix $Q_N$. The representation of the Green function in terms of these polynomials generalizes the eigenfunction expansion for simple random walk to arbitrary symmetric random walks, for which eigenvalues cannot be computed explicitly. The limit theorem for the coefficients $p_{n,n-k}$ connects the algebraic structure of the difference operator to the potential theory of Chapter IV.
