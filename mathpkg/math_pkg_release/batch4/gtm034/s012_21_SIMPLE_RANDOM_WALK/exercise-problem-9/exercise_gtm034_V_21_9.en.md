**Continuation.** Using the theorem of Weierstrass on polynomial approximation, show that for any interval \(I\), letting \(N_n(I)\) denote the number of eigenvalues of \(Q_n\) which fall in \(I\), and \(L_\phi(I)\) the Lebesgue measure of the subset of \([-\pi,\pi]\) where \(\phi(\theta) \in I\),
\[
\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n+1} N_n(I) = \frac{L_\phi(I)}{2\pi}.
\]
