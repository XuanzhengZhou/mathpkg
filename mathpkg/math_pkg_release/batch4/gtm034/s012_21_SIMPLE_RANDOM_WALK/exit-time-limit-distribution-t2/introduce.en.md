This theorem generalizes P21.5 from simple random walk to all random walks with zero mean and finite variance. The normalized exit time \(T_N\) from the interval \([-N,N]\) converges to the same universal distribution — the first-passage time distribution of Brownian motion from an interval. This is a remarkable invariance principle: the detailed step distribution washes out in the limit, leaving only the variance \(\sigma^2\).
