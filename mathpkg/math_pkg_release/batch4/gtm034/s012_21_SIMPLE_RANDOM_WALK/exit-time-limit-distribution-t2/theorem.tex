For arbitrary one-dimensional random walk with \(\mu = 0\) and \(0 < \sigma^2 < \infty\), starting at \(0\),
\[
\lim_{N \to \infty} P_0\!\left[T_N > x \frac{N^2}{\sigma^2}\right] = 1 - F(x), \quad x > 0,
\]
where \(F(x)\) has Laplace transform \((\cosh\sqrt{2s})^{-1}\) and density
\[
f(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi}} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{2n+1}{x^{3/2}} e^{-(2n+1)^2/(2x)}.
\]
