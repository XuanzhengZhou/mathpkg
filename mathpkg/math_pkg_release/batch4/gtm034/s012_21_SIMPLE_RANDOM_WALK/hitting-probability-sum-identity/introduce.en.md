These two identities are the discrete analogues of properties of Brownian motion stopped at the boundary of an interval: the exit distribution has total mass one, and the expected exit position equals the starting point (a consequence of the martingale property when $\mu = 0$). The first identity states that the random walk must eventually exit the interval on either the right or the left. The second identity expresses the martingale property: the random walk stopped at $T_N$ satisfies $E[x_{T_N}] = x$. Both are derived from the convolution relations between $g_N$, $R_N$, $L_N$, and $P$, using the key identity $\sum_y g_N(x,y) P(y,s) = g_N(x,s) - \delta(x,s)$.
