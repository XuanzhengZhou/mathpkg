The right hitting probability $R_N(x,k)$ is the probability that a random walk starting at $x$ in $[0,N]$ first exits the interval by jumping to the point $N+k$ on the right side. It is expressed as a convolution of the Green function $g_N$ (which accounts for all possible paths within the interval) with the unrestricted transition function $P$ (which accounts for the final exit step). The total probability $R_N(x)$ of exiting on the right aggregates over all possible overshoot distances $k$.
