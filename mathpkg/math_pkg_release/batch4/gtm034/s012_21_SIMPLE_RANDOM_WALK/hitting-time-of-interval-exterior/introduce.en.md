\(T_N\) is the absorption time — the first epoch at which the random walk exits the interval \([0,N]\). Its distribution is fundamental to the study of the absorption problem, and its asymptotic behavior as \(N 	o \infty\) connects discrete random walk to the Brownian motion first-passage time distribution.
