This proposition formalizes the reduction of Theorem 23.T2 to a moment convergence problem. It invokes a general result from the theory of distribution functions: if a sequence of distributions has moments converging to those of a unique limit distribution (i.e., the moment problem is determinate), then the distributions converge weakly. Combined with Proposition 23.P3 (which establishes the uniqueness of the moment problem for $F$), this criterion reduces the proof of T2 to the purely analytical task of computing the asymptotic behavior of the moments of $\mathbf{T}_N$.
