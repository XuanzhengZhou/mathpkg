This proposition establishes a simple but crucial uniform bound for the interval Green function: $g_N(x,y)$ grows at most linearly with the distance from $x$ or $y$ to the nearest boundary of the interval. The proof combines the monotonicity of $g_N$ in $N$ with the factorization from Proposition 22.P4 and the symmetry $g_N(x,y) = g_N(N-y, N-x)$. This bound is an essential technical tool in the proof of Theorem 22.T1 and in the estimates needed for the asymptotic analysis of hitting probabilities.
