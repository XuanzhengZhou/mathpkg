As in problem 10 of Chapter I, the integers in $\mathbb{R}$ are assumed to be "red" with probability $\alpha$ and "green" with probability $(1 - \alpha)$. Let $E_{N,\alpha}$ denote the expected number of red points in the interval $[-N,N]$ and $P_{N,\alpha}$ the probability that the random walk of problem 5 above will visit a red point before leaving the interval $[-N,N]$. Prove that
$$\lim_{N \to \infty} \lim_{\alpha \to 0} \frac{P_{N,\alpha}}{E_{N,\alpha}} = \lim_{\alpha \to 0} \lim_{N \to \infty} \frac{P_{N,\alpha}}{E_{N,\alpha}} = \ln 2.$$
Does the double limit exist?
