Expand \(f(k/N)\) in a Taylor series around \(z = [Nt]/N\): \(f(k/N) = f(z) + (k/N - z)f'(z) + \frac{1}{2}(k/N - z)^2 f''(z) + \rho(z, k/N)\). Substitute into the sum and decompose into four terms \(I_1, I_2, I_3, I_4\). Using \(\mu = 0\) and \(\sigma^2 < \infty\), show \(I_1 \to 0\), \(I_2 \to 0\), \(I_3 \to -f''(t)\), and \(I_4 \to 0\) as \(N \to \infty\). The convergence is uniform because \(\rho\) is uniformly controlled on \([0,1]\).
