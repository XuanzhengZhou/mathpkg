Let $D_N$ denote the number of distinct points visited before leaving the interval $[-N,N]$, by a one-dimensional random walk with mean $0$ and variance $0 < \sigma^2 < \infty$. Calculate
$$\lim_{N \to \infty} E_0\left[ \frac{D_N}{2N} \right],$$
the "fraction of points visited." (How should one expect that the answer will depend on $\sigma^2$?)
