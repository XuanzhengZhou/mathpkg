The restricted transition function $Q_N(x,y)$ is the truncation of the transition function $P(x,y)$ of a one-dimensional random walk to the finite integer interval $[0,N]$. It is an $(N+1) \times (N+1)$ substochastic matrix that captures the behavior of the random walk when confined to the interval, yielding zero probability for transitions that would exit the interval. The iterates $Q_N^n$ are defined via matrix multiplication and represent $n$-step transition probabilities conditioned on the walk staying within $[0,N]$ at all intermediate steps.
