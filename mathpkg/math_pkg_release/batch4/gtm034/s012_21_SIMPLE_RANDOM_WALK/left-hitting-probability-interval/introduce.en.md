The left hitting probability $L_N(x,k)$ is the probability that a random walk starting at $x$ in $[0,N]$ first exits the interval by jumping to the point $-k$ on the left side. Together with the right hitting probability $R_N$, these quantities describe the complete exit distribution of the random walk from the finite interval. The sum $R_N(x) + L_N(x) = 1$ expresses the certainty that the random walk eventually leaves any finite interval (for non-degenerate random walks).
