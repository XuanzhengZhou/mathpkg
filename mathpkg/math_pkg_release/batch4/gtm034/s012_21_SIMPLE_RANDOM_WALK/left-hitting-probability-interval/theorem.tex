For a random walk with Green function $g_N$ on $[0,N]$, the \textbf{left hitting probability} $L_N(x,k)$ of first exiting the interval at the point $-k$ (with $k \geq 1$) is
$$L_N(x,k) = \sum_{y=0}^{N} g_N(x,y) P(y, -k) \quad \text{for } x \in [0,N], \quad k \geq 1.$$

The total probability of exiting on the left is
$$L_N(x) = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} L_N(x,k) \quad \text{for } x \in [0,N].$$

In the earlier terminology of hitting probabilities,
$$L_N(x,k) = H_{R-[0,N]}(x, -k).$$
