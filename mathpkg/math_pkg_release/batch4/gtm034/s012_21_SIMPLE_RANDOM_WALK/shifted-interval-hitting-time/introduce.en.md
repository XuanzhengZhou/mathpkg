This definition shifts the perspective from the interval $[0,N]$ to the symmetric interval $[-N,N]$ with the walk starting at the midpoint $0$. This is natural for studying occupation time problems and the distribution of the exit time, as the starting point is now fixed independently of $N$. The occupation count $\mathbf{N}_N(y)$ records how many times the point $y$ is visited before the walk exits the interval, a quantity of fundamental interest in the study of the range and coverage of random walk.
