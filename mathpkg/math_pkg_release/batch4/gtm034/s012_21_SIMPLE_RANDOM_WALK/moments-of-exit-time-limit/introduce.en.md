The moments of the limit distribution can be computed explicitly. Moreover, these moments satisfy Carleman's condition (\(\sum m_p^{-1/(2p)} = \infty\) is not needed; instead the exponential generating function converges), which guarantees that the distribution is uniquely determined by its moments. This uniqueness is essential for the method-of-moments proof of T23.2.
