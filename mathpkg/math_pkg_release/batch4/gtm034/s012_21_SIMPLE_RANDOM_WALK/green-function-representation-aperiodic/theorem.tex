For an aperiodic one-dimensional random walk with mean $\mu = 0$ and variance $\sigma^2 < \infty$, the Green function $g(x,y)$ of the half-line $B = \{x \mid -\infty < x \leq -1\}$ satisfies
$$g(x,y) = \sum_{k=0}^{\min(x,y)} u(x-k) v(y-k), \quad x \geq 0, \quad y \geq 0,$$
where the sequences $u(x)$ and $v(x)$ have the limits
$$\lim_{x \to +\infty} u(x) = u > 0, \quad \lim_{x \to +\infty} v(x) = v > 0,$$
satisfying
$$u v = \frac{2}{\sigma^2}.$$
