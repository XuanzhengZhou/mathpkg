The Green function $g_N(x,y)$ of the interval $[0,N]$ sums all iterates of the restricted transition function $Q_N$, representing the expected number of visits to $y$ starting from $x$ before the random walk first exits the interval. It is a fundamental object in the potential theory of random walk, playing the role of the inverse of $I_N - Q_N$. In the absorption problem, $g_N$ encodes all hitting and occupation time statistics, and its asymptotic behavior as $N \to \infty$ reveals the connection between discrete random walk and the continuous Laplace operator.
