This proposition gives the complete spectral decomposition of the restricted transition matrix $Q_N$ for simple symmetric random walk. Since $Q_N = \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tridiag}(1,0,1)$ is a symmetric tridiagonal matrix, its eigenvalues and eigenvectors can be computed explicitly using the theory of difference equations. The eigenvalues are cosines of equally spaced angles, and the eigenvectors are discretized sine functions. This spectral decomposition enables explicit formulas for the Green function $g_N$, hitting probabilities, and the distribution of exit times, making simple random walk the prototype for which all absorption problem quantities are computable in closed form.
