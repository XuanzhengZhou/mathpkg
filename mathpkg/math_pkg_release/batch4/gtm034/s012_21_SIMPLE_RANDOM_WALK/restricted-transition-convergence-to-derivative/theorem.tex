Let $P(x,y)$ be the transition function of a random walk with mean $\mu = 0$ and variance $0 < \sigma^2 < \infty$. Let $f(x)$ be a function with two continuous derivatives on $[0,1]$, and suppose that the error term in the Taylor expansion satisfies $(x-y)^{-2} \cdot \rho(x,y) \to 0$ uniformly in any interval $0 < a \leq x, y \leq b < 1$. Then for $0 < t < 1$,
$$\lim_{N \to \infty} \frac{2N^2}{\sigma^2} \sum_{k=0}^{N} \left[ \delta([Nt],k) - Q_N([Nt],k) \right] f\left(\frac{k}{N}\right) = -f''(t),$$
where the convergence is uniform on compact subsets of $(0,1)$.
