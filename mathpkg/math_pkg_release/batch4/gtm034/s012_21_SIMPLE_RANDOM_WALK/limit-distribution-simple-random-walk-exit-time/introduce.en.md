The hitting time \(T_{2N}\) of the exterior of \([0,2N]\), properly normalized by \(N^2\), converges in distribution to a limit law expressible as an infinite series of exponentials. This distribution is the first-passage time distribution for Brownian motion on an interval and was one of Bachelier's early discoveries.
