This theorem is one of the central results of the absorption problem for general random walk. It states that the probability of exiting the interval $[0,N]$ on the right, starting from $x$, is asymptotically $x/N$ — the uniform distribution. This generalizes the exact formula $R_N(x) = (x+1)/(N+2)$ for simple random walk, showing that the same linear behavior holds asymptotically for any random walk with mean zero and finite variance. The proof combines the bound on $g_N$ from P5, the identities from P6, and a key estimate involving the tail of the transition function controlled by the finite variance via Kronecker's lemma.
