Continuation. Using the theorem of Weierstrass on polynomial approximation, show that the results of problems 7 and 8 are valid for real functions
$$\phi(\theta) = \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} c_k e^{ik\theta}$$
whose Fourier series converge absolutely. By approximating step functions by polynomials, rephrase the results as follows: For any interval $I$, let $N_n(I)$ denote the number of eigenvalues of $Q_n$ which fall in the interval $I$, and let $L_\phi(I)$ be the Lebesgue measure of that subset of $[-\pi, \pi]$ where $\phi(\theta) \in I$. Then
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n+1} N_n(I) = \frac{L_\phi(I)}{2\pi}.$$
