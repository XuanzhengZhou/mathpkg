This proposition establishes the continuous analogue of the discrete eigenvalue problem for simple random walk. The discrete equation $Q_N v = \lambda v$ with zero boundary conditions $v(-1) = v(N+1) = 0$ corresponds, in the limit $N \to \infty$, to the differential equation $f'' + \mu f = 0$ on $[0,1]$ with Dirichlet boundary conditions. The kernel $R(x,y) = \min(x,y) - xy$ is the Green function of the second derivative operator, and its eigenvalues and eigenfunctions (sines) are the continuous counterparts of the discrete cosines and sines from the restricted transition matrix. Mercer's theorem guarantees that the series expansion $R(x,y) = \sum \phi_k(x)\phi_k(y)/\mu_k$ converges uniformly.
