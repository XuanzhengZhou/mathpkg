As \(N 	o \infty\), the discrete operator \(I_N - Q_N\) approximates the second derivative with zero boundary conditions. This proposition records the classical spectral theory of \(d^2/dx^2\) on \([0,1]\), which serves as the continuous analogue and limiting case of the discrete absorption problem.
