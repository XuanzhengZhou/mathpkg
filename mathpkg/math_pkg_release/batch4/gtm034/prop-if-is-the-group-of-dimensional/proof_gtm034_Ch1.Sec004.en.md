$$\theta \cdot A\theta = \sum_{i=1}^{d} \sum_{j=1}^{d} a_{ij}\theta_{i}\theta_{j}$$

is called a quadratic form if $a_{ij} = a_{ij}$, i.e., if the matrix $A = (a_{ij})$, $i, j = 1, 2, \ldots, d$ is symmetric. Such a form is called positive definite if

$$\theta \cdot A\theta \geq 0 \text{ for all } \theta \text{ in } E,$$
