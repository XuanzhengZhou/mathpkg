Proposition P3 gives the precise asymptotic decay of $P_0[T > n]$ for a one-dimensional recurrent random walk with finite variance. The $\sqrt{n}$ normalization and constant $\sigma\sqrt{2/\pi}$ emerge from Fourier analysis of $U(t) = \sum t^n P_n(0,0)$, the renewal identity $\sum R_n t^n = [(1-t)U(t)]^{-1}$, and Karamata's Tauberian theorem applied to the monotone sequence $R_n$.
