Prove that the potential kernel $A(x,y)$ of a recurrent random walk determines the transition function uniquely.

Hint: In the spirit of problem 13, Chapter VI, show that for every subset $B \subset R$, $\Pi_B(x,y)$ is determined by $A(x,y)$. This follows from T11.1 and T30.2 in all cases except for left-, or right-continuous random walk. (But even then $A(x,y)$ does determine $\Pi_B(x,y)$, according to the discussion.)
