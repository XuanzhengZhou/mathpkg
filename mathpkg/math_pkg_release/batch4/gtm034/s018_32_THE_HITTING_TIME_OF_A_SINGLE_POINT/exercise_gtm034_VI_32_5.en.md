Here and in the next problem the random walk is one dimensional and aperiodic, with mean $0$ and $\sigma^2 < \infty$. Use P32.3 to conclude that the ratios $R_n/R_{2n}$ are bounded in $n$. Then use the discussion at the end of Chapter III to prove that
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{P_x[T_B > n]}{P_0[T > n]} = \lim_{y \to +\infty} g_B(x,y) = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \lim_{y \to +\infty} g_B(x,y) + \lim_{y \to -\infty} g_B(x,y) \right]$$
for every finite set $B$.
