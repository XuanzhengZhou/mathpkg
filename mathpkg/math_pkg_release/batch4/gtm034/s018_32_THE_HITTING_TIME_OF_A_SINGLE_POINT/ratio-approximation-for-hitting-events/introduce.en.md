Proposition P7 constructs an auxiliary probability $q_n$ that approximates $F_n$ up to an arbitrarily small relative error $\epsilon_1$. The event defining $q_n$ requires that at time $k$ the walk is far from the origin yet revisits that position within $m$ to $M$ steps, with first return at time $n$. P6 guarantees the existence of suitable indices $k$, and T5.1 provides the estimate relating $q_n$ to $F_n$.
