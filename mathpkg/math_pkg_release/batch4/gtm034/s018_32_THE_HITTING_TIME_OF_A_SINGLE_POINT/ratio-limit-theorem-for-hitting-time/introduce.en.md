For an aperiodic random walk in any dimension $d \geq 1$, whether transient or recurrent, the asymptotic ratio of the tail probability that the hitting time of the origin exceeds $n$ starting from $x$ to that starting from $0$ converges to the limit of the truncated sum of differences of $n$-step transition probabilities. This fundamental ratio limit theorem (T1) unifies the transient and recurrent cases and expresses the limit in terms of the potential kernel $a(x) = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{k=0}^{n} [P_k(0,0) - P_k(x,0)]$.
