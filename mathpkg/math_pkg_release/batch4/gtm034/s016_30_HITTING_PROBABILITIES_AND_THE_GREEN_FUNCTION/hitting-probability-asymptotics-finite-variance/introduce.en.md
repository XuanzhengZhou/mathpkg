The hitting probability of a finite set \(B\) from a distant starting point is asymptotically proportional to the potential kernel \(a(x)\), with a proportionality constant given by the reciprocal of the total equilibrium measure of \(B\). This result connects the local properties of hitting probabilities to the global asymptotic behavior of the potential kernel.
