For arbitrary one-dimensional random walk (recurrent or transient), the two classifications are equivalent, that is (i) holds whenever (1) does, and so on. These are the only possibilities. Furthermore, in cases (ii) = (2) and (iii) = (3), one has

$$\lim_{x \to -\infty} H_A(x,y) = \gamma_A(y) = \frac{1}{E_0[Z]} P_0[Z \geq y]$$

for $y \in A$. The limit is interpreted as zero in case (ii) = (2).
