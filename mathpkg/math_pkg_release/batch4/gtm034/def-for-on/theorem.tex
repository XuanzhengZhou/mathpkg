$Q_n(x,y)$ for $n \geq 0$ on $(R-A) \times (R-A)$,

$\Pi_A(x,y)$ on $A \times A$,
$g_A(x,y)$ on $R \times R$.

The definitions are, in terms of the time $T = T_A$ of the first visit to $A$,

$Q_n(x,y) = P_x[x_n = y; T > n]$;
$H_A^{(n)}(x,y) = P_x[x_T = y; T = n]$ for $x \in R-A$,
$= 0$ for $x \in A, n \geq 1$,
$= \delta(x,y)$ for $x \in A, n = 0$;
$H_A(x,y) = P_x[x_T = y; T < \infty]$ for $x \in R-A$,
$= \delta(x,y)$ for $x \in A$;
$\Pi_A(x,y) = P_x[x_T = y; T < \infty]$;
$g_A(x,y) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} Q_n(x,y)$ for $x \in R-A, y \in R-A$,
$= 0$ otherwise.

It should be clear how to cast these definitions in a purely analytic form. For $Q_n(x,y)$ we define $Q_0(x,y) = \delta(x,y)$; then $Q(x,y) = Q_1(x,y) = P(x,y)$ when $(x,y)$ is in $(R-A) \times (R-A)$. Finally, just as in P1.1

$$Q_{n+1}(x,y) = \sum_{t \in R-A} Q_n(x,t)Q(t,y).$$
