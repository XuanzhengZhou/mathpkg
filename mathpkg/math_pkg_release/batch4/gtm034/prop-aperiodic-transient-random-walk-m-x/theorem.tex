Aperiodic transient random walk, $d = 1$, $m = \sum_{x \in R} |x| P(0,x) < \infty$, $\mu = \sum_{x \in R} xP(0,x)$. If $\mu > 0$, then

$$\lim_{x \to +\infty} G(0,x) = \frac{1}{\mu}, \quad \lim_{x \to -\infty} G(0,x) = 0.$$

If $\mu < 0$, the limits are reversed, and $(-\mu)^{-1}$ replaces $\mu^{-1}$.

Finally, let us introduce some simplifying notation. Let

$$f(a,b) = \sum_{z=a}^{b} P(0,x)$$

where $a$ may be $-\infty$ and $b$ may be $+\infty$;

$$\mu^{+} = \sum_{z=1}^{\infty} xP(0,x) = \sum_{a=1}^{\infty} f(a, +\infty),$$

$$\mu^{-} = -\sum_{z=-\infty}^{-1} xP(0,x) = \sum_{b=-\infty}^{-1} f(-\infty,b),$$

$$\mu = \mu^{+} - \mu^{-}.$$

Now we define $u(x) = G(0,x)$, so that

$$u(x) = \sum_{t=-\infty}^{\infty} u(t)P(0,x-t) + \delta(0,x), \quad x \in R.$$

Taking $M$ and $N$ to be two positive integers, one obtains

$$\sum_{z=-M}^{N} u(x) = \sum_{t=-\infty}^{\infty} u(t)f(-M-t,N-t) + 1.$$
