If $P(x,y)$ is the transition function of any aperiodic recurrent random walk (either of dimension one or two), then the limit

(a) $A(x,y) = a(x - y) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{k=0}^{n} [P_k(0,0) - P_k(x,y)] < \infty$

exists for all pairs $x$ and $y$ in $R$. Moreover $A(x,y)$ satisfies the equation

(b) $$\sum_{t \in R} P(x,t)A(t,y) - A(x,y) = \delta(x,y), \quad x,y \in R.$$
