\textbf{T1 (Riesz Decomposition).} Let $Q$ be a transient kernel on $S \times S$ and let $f$ be a $Q$-excessive function. Then
$$f(x) = h(x) + u(x), \quad x \in S,$$
where
$$h(x) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{y \in S} Q_n(x,y)f(y) < \infty, \quad x \in S,$$
is $Q$-regular, and
$$u(x) = \sum_{t \in S} g(x,t)[f(t) - Qf(t)], \quad x \in S,$$
is a $Q$-potential. Moreover, this decomposition is unique: if $f = h' + u'$ with $h'$ $Q$-regular and $u'$ a $Q$-potential, then $h = h'$ and $u = u'$.
