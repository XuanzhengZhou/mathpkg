\textbf{E2.} Consider aperiodic random walk in three dimensions satisfying hypotheses (a) through (c) of P26.1 (mean zero, finite second moments). Then the only $P$-regular functions are the positive constants. Equivalently,
$$\lim_{|y| \to \infty} \frac{G(x,y)}{G(0,y)} = 1, \quad x \in R,$$
and the constant function $f(x) \equiv 1$ is the unique $P$-regular function up to positive scalar multiples.
