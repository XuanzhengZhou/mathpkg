A transient kernel formalizes the notion of a sub-Markovian transition mechanism on a countable set $S$ whose associated Green function is everywhere finite. Unlike a full Markov transition function, a transient kernel need not sum to one at each point, nor need the space $S$ carry an additive structure. The defining properties include non-negativity, row sums bounded by one, convolution-like iteration of powers, and convergence of the Neumann series $\sum Q_n(x,y)$ to the Green function $g(x,y)$. This abstraction unifies the study of transient random walks, potential theory, and harmonic analysis on discrete spaces.
