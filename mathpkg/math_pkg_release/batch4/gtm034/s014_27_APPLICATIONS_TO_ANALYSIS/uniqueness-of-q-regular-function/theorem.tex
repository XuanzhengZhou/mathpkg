\textbf{P3.} Let $Q(x,y)$ be a transient kernel on $S$ such that $g(x,y) > 0$ for all $x,y \in S$, and suppose that for some point $\xi \in S$,
$$\lim_{|y| \to \infty} \frac{g(x,y)}{g(\xi,y)} = f(x) < \infty$$
exists for all $x \in S$. Suppose further that $f(x)$ is $Q$-regular. Then the positive multiples of $f(x)$ are the only $Q$-regular functions on $S$.
