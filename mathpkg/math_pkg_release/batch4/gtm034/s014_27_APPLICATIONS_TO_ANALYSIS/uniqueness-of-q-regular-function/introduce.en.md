Proposition P3 provides sufficient conditions for a transient kernel to possess a one-dimensional cone of regular functions. The hypothesis requires that the Martin kernel $g(x,y)/g(\xi,y)$ converges to a finite limit $f(x)$ as $|y| \to \infty$, and that this limit itself be $Q$-regular. The proof is a tour de force: it uses the $h$-transform to reduce to the constant function, approximates the constant by potentials via the Riesz decomposition T1, and extracts the limit function from the boundary behavior of Green function ratios. This result is the discrete analogue of the Martin boundary method for classifying positive harmonic functions, and is the central theoretical result of Section 27.
