Associated with every transient kernel $Q$ are three nested classes of non-negative functions on $S$. $Q$-regular functions are eigenfunctions of the operator $Q$ with eigenvalue one; they generalize harmonic functions. $Q$-excessive functions satisfy the sub-mean-value inequality $Qf \leq f$, forming an analogue of superharmonic functions in classical analysis. $Q$-potentials are those excessive functions representable as the Green potential $g\psi$ of some non-negative charge $\psi$. These three classes provide the function-theoretic foundation for the Riesz decomposition theorem.
