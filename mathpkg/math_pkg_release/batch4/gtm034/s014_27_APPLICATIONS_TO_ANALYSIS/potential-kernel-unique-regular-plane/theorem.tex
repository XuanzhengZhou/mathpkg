\textbf{E4 (Theorem).} Let $A(x,y) = A(0,y-x)$ be the potential kernel and $a(x) = A(x,0)$ the potential function of a recurrent aperiodic random walk in the plane. Then $a(x)$ is the unique (up to multiplicative constant) non-negative solution of
$$\sum_{y \neq 0} P(x,y)a(y) - a(x) = 0, \quad x \neq 0.$$

Equivalently, if $S = R \setminus \{0\}$ and $Q(x,y) = P(x,y)$ is restricted to $S$, then $Q$ is a transient kernel and $a(x)$ is, up to a positive constant, the only $Q$-regular function on $S$.
