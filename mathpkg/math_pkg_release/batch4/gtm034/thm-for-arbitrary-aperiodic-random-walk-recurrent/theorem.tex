For arbitrary aperiodic random walk, recurrent or transient, of dimension $d \geq 1$

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{P_x[T > n]}{P_0[T > n]} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{k=0}^{n} [P_k(0,0) - P_k(x,0)]$$

for every $x \neq 0$. Both limits are finite and non-negative.

First (in P1) the proof will be given for transient random walk. The next item on the agenda will be the proof of T1 for one-dimensional recurrent random walk with finite variance $\sigma^2$. In this case we shall in fact obtain a result which is stronger than T1, as P4 will show that

$$P_x[T > n] \sim a(x)P_0[T > n] \sim a(x)\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi n}} \sigma, \quad \text{as } n \to \infty, \text{ for } x \neq 0.$$

Since T1 was proved in Chapter III for two-dimensional recurrent random walk, the only case then remaining after P4 will be the one-dimensional recurrent case with $\sigma^2 = \infty$.
