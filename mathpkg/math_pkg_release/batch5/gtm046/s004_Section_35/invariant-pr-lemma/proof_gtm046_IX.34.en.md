(i)$\Rightarrow$(ii): Condition (i) is uniform absolute continuity; the Vitali-Hahn-Saks theorem yields $\overline{P}=\lim\overline{P^n}$ as a $\sigma$-additive probability. (ii)$\Rightarrow$(iii): $\overline{P}(TA)=\lim\frac{1}{n}\sum PT^{k-1}(TA)=\lim\frac{1}{n}\sum PT^kA=\overline{P}(A)$, so $\overline{P}$ is invariant. For $C\in\mathfrak{C}$, $T^{k-1}C=C$ so $\overline{P^n}(C)=P(C)$ and $\overline{P}(C)=P(C)$. (iii)$\Rightarrow$(i): If invariant $\overline{P}$ exists and equals $P$ on $\mathfrak{C}$, then $\overline{P^n}\to\overline{P}$, implying (i).
