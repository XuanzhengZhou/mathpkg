For every $x$ with $\bar{P}_s(x,R)=0$ and every Borel $S$,
$$\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n P^k(x,S)\to\bar{P}(x,S)$$
where $\bar{P}(x,S)$ is $\bar{P}$-continuous and $\bar{P}(x,S)=\int\bar{P}(x,dy)P(y,S)$. If $\bar{P}_s(x,R)=0$ except on a $\bar{P}$-null set, then $\bar{P}(x,S)$ is idempotent:
$$\bar{P}(x,S)=\int\bar{P}(x,dy)\bar{P}(y,S).$$
