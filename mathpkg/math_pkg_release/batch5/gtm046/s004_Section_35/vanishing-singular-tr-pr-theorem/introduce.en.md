When the singular transition probability vanishes, Cesaro averages of $n$-step transition probabilities converge to a $\bar{P}$-continuous limit $\bar{P}(x,S)$. If vanishing occurs $\bar{P}$-almost everywhere, the limit is idempotent, satisfying the Chapman-Kolmogorov equation with itself. This characterizes the asymptotic behavior of stationary Markov chains.
