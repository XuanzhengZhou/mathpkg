The extension of a translation from events to all measurable functions is unique: it is linear, commutes with suprema (hence continuous for pointwise limits), and preserves the algebraic structure. The constant function 1 maps to 1, and the indicator of an intersection maps to the product of translated indicators. These properties ensure the translation is a well-behaved operator fundamental to the ergodic theorems.
