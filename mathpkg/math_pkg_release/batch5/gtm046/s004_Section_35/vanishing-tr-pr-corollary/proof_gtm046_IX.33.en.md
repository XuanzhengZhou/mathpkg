From the limit assertion in the Vanishing Singular Transition Probability Theorem, let $n\to\infty$ in
$$\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1+m}^{n+m}P^k(x,S)=\int P^m(x,dy)\left\{\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n P^k(y,S)\right\}.$$
By stationarity, the left side converges to $\bar{P}(x,S)$ and the Cesaro average inside converges to $\bar{P}(y,S)$. Dominated convergence yields the result.
