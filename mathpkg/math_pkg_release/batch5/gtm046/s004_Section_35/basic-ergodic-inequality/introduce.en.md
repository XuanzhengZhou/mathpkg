The Basic Ergodic Inequality is the principal tool for proving almost sure convergence of ratios of partial sums in ergodic theory. It bounds integrals involving translates of the event where the supremum of partial ratios exceeds a threshold, evaluated against invariant events. The proof relies on Riesz's lemma on m-positive terms applied to $X_k-bY_k$.
