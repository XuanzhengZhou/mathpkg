Every transition probability $P(x,S)$ decomposes into invariant ergodic components. Every probability $\bar{P}'(S)=\int\bar{P}_t(S)\mu(dt)$ with $\mu$ a probability on $T$ is invariant. Conversely, if an invariant probability is $\bar{P}$-continuous, it can be represented as such a mixture of ergodic probabilities $\bar{P}_t$, each satisfying
$$\bar{P}_t(S)=\int\bar{P}_t(dx)P(x,S).$$
