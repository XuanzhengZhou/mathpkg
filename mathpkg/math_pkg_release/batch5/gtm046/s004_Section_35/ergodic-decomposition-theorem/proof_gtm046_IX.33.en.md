Since the Borel field is generated by a countable field $\{S(n)\}$ and the conditional probability $\bar{P}(x,S)$ given the invariant $\sigma$-field is regularized, decomposition theorem 26.2A applies, yielding invariant atoms $S_t$. By the Vanishing Singular Transition Probability Theorem, for each $S$: $\bar{P}(x,S)=\int\bar{P}(x,dy)P(y,S)$ except on a $\bar{P}$-null $N_S$. It follows that $\bar{P}_t(S)=\int\bar{P}_t(dx)P(x,S)$ except for $t$ in a null set. Adding these exceptional sets to a $P$-null set $N$, the equality holds for all remaining $t$ and all $S(n)$, extending to all Borel $S$ since the $S(n)$ form a field. Integrating with respect to $\mu$ shows any mixture $\bar{P}'$ is invariant. The converse follows from the decomposition.
