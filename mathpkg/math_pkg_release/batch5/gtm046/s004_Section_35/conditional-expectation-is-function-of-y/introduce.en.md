This proposition establishes that $E^Y X$ is always a measurable function of $Y$. The proof pushes the indefinite integral of $X$ forward through $Y^{-1}$ to obtain a $\sigma$-additive measure $\varphi'$ on the range $\sigma$-field, absolutely continuous with respect to the distribution $P'_Y$. The Radon-Nikodym derivative $g=d\varphi'/dP'_Y$ provides the function with $E^Y X=g(Y)$ almost surely.
