Let $\lim \sup \overline{P^n} A \to 0$ as $A \downarrow \emptyset$. Then $\overline{T^n} X \xrightarrow{a.s.} \overline{T} X$ invariant, for every r.v. $X$ such that $\frac{1}{n} \int |T^{n-1} X| \to 0$ and $\frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \int_{T^{k-1} A} T^{k-1} X \to 0$ as $n \to \infty$ and then $A \downarrow \emptyset$. In particular, $\overline{T^n} X \xrightarrow{a.s.} \overline{T} X$ bounded, for every bounded r.v. $X$.
