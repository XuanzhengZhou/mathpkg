Upon setting $Y_n = 1$ in the basic ergodic theorem, the integrability conditions reduce to those stated. The particular case for bounded $X$ follows from $|X| \leq c < \infty$ implying $|T^k X| \leq c$, so that the first integral is bounded by $c/n$ while the sum of integrals is bounded by $c \overline{P^n} A$. The convergence then follows from the condition $\lim \sup \overline{P^n} A \to 0$ as $A \downarrow \emptyset$.
