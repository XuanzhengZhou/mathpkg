The Vanishing Singular Transition Probability Theorem establishes that when the singular component of the transition probability vanishes (i.e., $\bar{P}_s(x, R) = 0$), the Cesàro averages of the $n$-step transition probabilities converge to a limit $\bar{P}(x, S)$ that is $\bar{P}$-continuous. If the singular component vanishes almost everywhere with respect to $\bar{P}$, then the limit is idempotent, meaning it satisfies the Chapman-Kolmogorov equation with itself in place of the one-step transition probability. This theorem characterizes the asymptotic behavior of stationary Markov chains.
