Given a translation $T$ on $I_\alpha$ (indicators of events), the translate by 1 of a simple function $\sum_{k=1}^n x_k I_{A_k}$ is defined as $\sum_{k=1}^n x_k I_{T A_k}$ by linear extension. For an arbitrary measurable function $\xi$, take a sequence of simple functions $\xi^{(n)}$ converging to $\xi$ pointwise. Then $T \xi = \lim_n T \xi^{(n)}$, since $T$ commutes with $\limsup$ and $\liminf$ (as shown by the continuity relations $T \sup \xi^{(n)} = \sup T \xi^{(n)}$).

This extension is unique: any two extensions that agree on simple functions and respect pointwise limits must agree on all measurable functions. The linearity, continuity, and the properties $T 1 = 1$ and $T I_{AB} = T I_A \cdot T I_B$ follow directly from the definition on indicators and the extension by limits.
