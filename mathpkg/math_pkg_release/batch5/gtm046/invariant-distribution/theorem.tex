A distribution $P_1(S)$ is invariant under a transition probability function $P(x; S)$ if
$$P_1(S) = \int P_1(dx) P(x; S).$$
It suffices that this holds for $n = 1$. Equivalently, for every $n$, $P_1(S) = \int P_1(dx) P^n(x; S)$. A chain whose law is determined by an invariant $P_1(S)$ and $P(x; S)$ is stationary.
