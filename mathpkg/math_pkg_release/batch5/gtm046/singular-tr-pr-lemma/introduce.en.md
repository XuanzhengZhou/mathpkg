The Singular Transition Probability Lemma establishes that for any fixed state $x$, the sequence of $n$-step singular transition probabilities $P_s^n(x, R)$ is nonincreasing and therefore converges to a limit $\bar{P}_s(x, R)$. The proof uses the Markov property: for $m < n$, the $n$-step probability factors through an intermediate $m$-step integration, and the singular set $S_0$ of points where positive mass can transfer introduces an inequality, yielding $P_s^n(x, R) \leq P_s^m(x, R)$. This monotonicity is fundamental for the analysis of stationary Markov chains in ergodic theory.
