This property states that conditional expectation $E^{\mathcal{B}}$ commutes with multiplication by $\mathcal{B}$-measurable random variables. That is, if $X$ is already measurable with respect to the conditioning $\sigma$-field $\mathcal{B}$, then it can be factored out of the conditional expectation: $E^{\mathcal{B}}(XY) = X E^{\mathcal{B}}Y$ almost surely. This generalizes the fact that $E^{\mathcal{B}}X = X$ a.s. when $X$ is $\mathcal{B}$-measurable.
