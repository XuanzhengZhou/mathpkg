The conditional expectation $E^{\mathcal{B}}X$ acts as a $\mathcal{B}$-smoothing operator on the random variable $X$. On each nonnull atom $B$ of the sub-$\sigma$-field $\mathcal{B}$, the conditional expectation is constant and equals the average value of $X$ over $B$ with respect to the probability measure $P$. This means that $E^{\mathcal{B}}X$ has "fewer values" than $X$, replacing the original values on each atom by their $P$-average.
