On every nonnull atom $B \in \mathcal{B}$, the conditional expectation $E^{\mathcal{B}}X$ is constant and its value $E_B X$ is the average of values of $X$ on $B$ with respect to $P$. More precisely, for every atom $B$ of $\mathcal{B}$,

$$
E_B X \cdot P B = \int_B (E^{\mathcal{B}}X) \, dP_{\mathcal{B}} = \int_B X \, dP
$$

and, for $P B > 0$,

$$
E_B X = \frac{1}{P B} \int_B X \, dP.
$$
