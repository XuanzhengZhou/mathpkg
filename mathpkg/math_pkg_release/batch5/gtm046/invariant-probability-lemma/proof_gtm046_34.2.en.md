The equivalence follows from standard measure-theoretic arguments: (iii) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) by the ergodic lemma applied to indicators; (ii) $\Rightarrow$ (i) by properties of probability measures; and (i) $\Rightarrow$ (iii) by constructing $\overline{P}$ as the limit of $\overline{P^n}$ on a determining class and extending. The invariance of $\overline{P}$ under $T$ follows from the fact that $\overline{P^n}$ and $\overline{P^n} \circ T$ differ by at most $2/n$ in total variation.
