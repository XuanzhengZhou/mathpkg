Let $\{Z_t\}$ be a family of probability densities with respect to a common measure. Write every $Z_t$ in the form $Z_t = g_t Z_0$ a.s., with $Z_0$ such that $Z_0 = 0$ a.s. $\Rightarrow$ every $Z_t = 0$ a.s.

Then the least fine sufficient $\sigma$-field with $Z_0$ is the minimal sufficient $\sigma$-field for the family $\{Z_t\}$.

Equivalently:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\mu_0 A = 0 \iff$ every $\mu_t A = 0$;
\item[(i')] up to $P$-null subsets, $Z_0 = 0$ on $A \iff$ every $Z_t = 0$ on $A$.
\end{enumerate}
