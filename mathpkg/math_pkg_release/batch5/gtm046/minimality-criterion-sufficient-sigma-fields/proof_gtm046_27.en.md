According to Chapter II, Complements and Details 23, there exists a $Z_0$ such that
$$\mu_0 A = 0 \iff \text{every } \mu_t A = 0$$
or, equivalently, up to $P$-null subsets,
$$Z_0 = 0 \text{ on } A \iff \text{every } Z_t = 0 \text{ on } A.$$

On account of this equivalence, every $E_Z^{\beta} X$ common to all the equivalence classes $E_{Z_t}^{\beta} X$ also belongs to the equivalence class of $E_{Z_0}^{\beta} X$. Consequently, every sufficient $\sigma$-field $\beta$ with $Z$ is also sufficient with $Z_0$.

Therefore, the least fine sufficient $\sigma$-field with $Z_0$ is the minimal sufficient $\sigma$-field. The corresponding factorization — every $Z_t = g_t Z_0$ a.s. — satisfies the property that $Z_0 = 0$ a.s. implies every $Z_t = 0$ a.s.
