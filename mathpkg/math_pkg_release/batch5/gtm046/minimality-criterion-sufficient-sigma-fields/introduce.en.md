The minimality criterion characterizes the minimal sufficient $\sigma$-field for a family of probability densities $\{Z_t\}$. It states the existence of a dominating density $Z_0$ with the property that every $Z_t$ vanishes almost surely precisely when $Z_0$ does. The least fine $\sigma$-field sufficient with $Z_0$ then serves as the minimal sufficient $\sigma$-field, and the factorization $Z_t = g_t Z_0$ holds almost surely.
