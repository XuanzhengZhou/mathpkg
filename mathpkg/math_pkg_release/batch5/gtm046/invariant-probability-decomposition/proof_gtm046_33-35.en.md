Since the Borel field $\mathcal{B}$ is generated by a denumerable field $\{S(n)\}$ of Borel sets and the conditional probability $\bar{P}(x, S)$ given the $\sigma$-field of invariant Borel sets is regularized, the decomposition theorem applies. This yields the asserted decomposition with invariant atoms $S_t$. By the previous theorem, for every fixed $S$, $\bar{P}(x, S) = \int \bar{P}(x, dy) P(y, S)$ for $x \notin N_S$ with $\bar{P}N_S = 0$, hence $\bar{P}_t S = \int \bar{P}_t(dx) P(x, S)$ for all non-exceptional $t$, proving invariance of each $\bar{P}_t$. By integrating with respect to $\mu$, any mixture $\bar{P}'$ of the $\bar{P}_t$ is invariant.
