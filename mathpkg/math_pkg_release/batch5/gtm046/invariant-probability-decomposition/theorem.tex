The conditional probability $\bar{P}(x, S)$ given the $\sigma$-field of invariant Borel sets decomposes over invariant atoms $S_t$: the state space partitions into indecomposable invariant components. Every $\bar{P}$-continuous invariant probability is representable as a mixture $\bar{P}'S = \int \bar{P}_t S \mu(dt)$ of invariant probabilities $\bar{P}_t$ concentrated on the atoms $S_t$, and each component chain $\{P_t, P(x, S)\}$ is stationary and indecomposable.
