The Regularity Theorem establishes that on the sample probability space of a countable family of random variables, conditional probabilities can always be chosen to be regular. This means they behave as proper probability measures for almost every conditioning value, rather than merely as equivalence classes. The theorem is fundamental for the rigorous treatment of conditional distributions, Markov chains, and Bayesian statistics, ensuring that the intuitive manipulation of conditional laws is mathematically justified.
