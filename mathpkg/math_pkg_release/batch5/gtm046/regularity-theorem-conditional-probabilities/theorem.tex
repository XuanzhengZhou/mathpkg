Conditional probabilities in sample probability spaces of countable families of random variables can be regularized and conditional distributions of their subfamilies always exist.

More precisely, let $X = (X_1, X_2, \ldots)$ be a countable family of random variables on its sample probability space $(\Omega, \alpha, P)$. For every $\sigma$-field $\mathcal{B} \subset \alpha$, there exists a conditional distribution of any subfamily of $X$ given $\mathcal{B} \subset \alpha$. In fact, there exists a conditional distribution of the countable family $X$ given $\mathcal{B}$, that is, a regular conditional probability $P^{\mathcal{B}}$.
