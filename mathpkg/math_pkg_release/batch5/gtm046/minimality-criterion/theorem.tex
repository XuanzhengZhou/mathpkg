Let $\{Z_t\}$ be a family of random variables and let $\mu_t$ be the probability measures they induce. There exists a $Z_0$ such that:

(i) $\mu_0 A = 0 \iff$ every $\mu_t A = 0$;

or, equivalently,

(i') up to $P$-null subsets, $Z_0 = 0$ on $A \iff$ every $Z_t = 0$ on $A$.

Every sufficient $\sigma$-field $\beta$ with any $Z$ is also sufficient with $Z_0$. Therefore, the least fine sufficient $\sigma$-field with $Z_0$ is the minimal sufficient $\sigma$-field for the family $\{Z_t\}$.

The corresponding factorization is: every $Z_t = g_t Z_0$ a.s., with $Z_0$ such that every $Z_t = 0$ a.s. $\Rightarrow Z_0 = 0$ a.s.
