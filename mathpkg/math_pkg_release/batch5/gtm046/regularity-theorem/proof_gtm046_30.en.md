Take the sample probability space of $X = (X_1, X_2, \dots)$. Then $X(x_1, x_2, \dots) = (x_1, x_2, \dots)$ and the range of any $X_n$ coincides with its range space $R_n$. Therefore, the existence theorem applies and, for every $\sigma$-field $\mathcal{B} \subset \mathcal{A}$, there exists a conditional distribution of any subfamily of $X$ given $\mathcal{B}$; in fact, there exists a conditional distribution of the countable family $X$ given $\mathcal{B}$, that is, a regular conditional probability $P^{\mathcal{B}}$.
