This corollary to the Vanishing Singular Transition Probability Theorem states that when the singular transition probability vanishes for every state $x$, the limit $\bar{P}(x, S)$ satisfies a symmetry relation: integrating the $m$-step transition probability $P^m(x, dy)$ against $\bar{P}(y, S)$ recovers $\bar{P}(x, S)$. This follows from the limit assertion of the theorem by shifting the summation index and letting $n \to \infty$.
