On account of the almost-sure properties of conditional expectations (linearity and monotonicity), the classical proofs of the $c_r$, Minkowski, Hölder, and convex function inequalities remain valid up to $P^{\mathfrak{B}}$-equivalence. For Hölder's inequality, one also uses the commutation of $E^{\mathfrak{B}}$ with $\mathfrak{B}$-measurable functions (property 25.2, 3). The key observation is that the non-conditional proofs of these inequalities depend only on linearity, monotonicity, and basic integral properties, all of which are inherited by the conditional expectation operation when interpreted almost surely.
