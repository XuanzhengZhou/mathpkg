F. Riesz's combinatorial lemma about $m$-positive terms is the key technical ingredient in the proof of the basic ergodic inequality. It asserts that if some terms in a sequence are $m$-positive (their partial sums from that term up to some later term within $m$ steps are all positive), then these terms can be grouped into disjoint stretches with positive sums.
