The Karhunen-Loève theorem is the foundation of spectral analysis for random functions. It decomposes a random process into a sum of deterministic spatial modes (eigenfunctions) multiplied by random temporal coefficients. This is the analog of Mercer's theorem for random processes and is fundamental in signal processing, functional data analysis, and stochastic PDEs.
