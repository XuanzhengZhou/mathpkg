If $\mathfrak{B} \subset \mathfrak{B}'$, then for every $B \in \mathfrak{B}$ we have $B \in \mathfrak{B}'$ and hence
$$\int_B E^{\mathfrak{B}}(E^{\mathfrak{B}'} X) \, dP = \int_B E^{\mathfrak{B}'} X \, dP = \int_B X \, dP = \int_B E^{\mathfrak{B}} X \, dP.$$
Since the indefinite integrals of the two $\mathfrak{B}$-measurable functions $E^{\mathfrak{B}}(E^{\mathfrak{B}'} X)$ and $E^{\mathfrak{B}} X$ coincide on $\mathfrak{B}$, they are $P_{\mathfrak{B}}$-equivalent. Therefore
$$E^{\mathfrak{B}}(E^{\mathfrak{B}'} X) = E^{\mathfrak{B}} X \text{ a.s.}$$
