A distribution is \textit{stable} if for independent r.v.'s $X_1, X_2$ with this distribution and any $a, b > 0$, there exist $c > 0$ and $d \in \mathbb{R}$ such that

$$a X_1 + b X_2 \overset{d}{=} c X + d.$$

Stable laws are precisely the possible nondegenerate limit distributions of normalized sums $(S_n - a_n)/b_n$ of i.i.d. random variables.
