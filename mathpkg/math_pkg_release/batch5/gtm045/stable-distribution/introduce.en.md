Stable distributions are those closed under linear combinations of independent copies (up to scaling and shift). They are the only possible nondegenerate limits of normalized sums of i.i.d. random variables. The class includes the normal ($\alpha = 2$), Cauchy ($\alpha = 1$), and Lévy distributions, characterized by their characteristic exponent $\alpha \in (0, 2]$.
