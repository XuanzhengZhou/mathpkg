The Lindeberg-Feller theorem provides necessary and sufficient conditions for the central limit theorem for triangular arrays: the uan condition plus the Lindeberg condition characterize convergence to the normal distribution. This is the definitive solution to the "classical" central limit problem (convergence to normality), complementing the general central convergence criterion for arbitrary infinitely divisible limits.
