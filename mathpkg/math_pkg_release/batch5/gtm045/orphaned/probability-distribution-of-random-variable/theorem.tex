The \textit{probability distribution} $P_X$ of a r.v. $X$ is defined on the Borel field $\mathcal{R}$ in $\mathbb{R}$ by

$$P_X(S) = P[X \in S], \quad S \in \mathcal{R}.$$

$P_X$ is a probability on $(\mathbb{R}, \mathcal{R})$, and $(R, \mathcal{R}, P_X)$ is the induced pr. space (sample pr. space) of $X$.

The distribution of $g(X)$ for any finite Borel function $g$ satisfies $P_{g(X)}(S) = P_X(g^{-1}(S))$.
