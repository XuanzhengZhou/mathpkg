The distribution of a random variable $X$ is the push-forward (image measure) of the probability $P$ under $X$. It captures all probabilistic information about $X$ while forgetting the underlying sample space. The sample probability space $(R, \mathcal{R}, P_X)$ is where the actual values of $X$ "live."
