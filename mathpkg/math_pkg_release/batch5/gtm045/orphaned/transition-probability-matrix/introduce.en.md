The transition probability matrix encodes the evolution law of a Markov chain. Each row is a probability distribution over states. The Chapman-Kolmogorov equation expresses the semigroup property: the probability of transitioning in $n+n'$ steps equals the matrix product of the $n$-step and $n'$-step transition matrices. A constant (time-homogeneous) chain has transition probabilities independent of the starting time.
