Poisson's theorem (1832) describes the limiting distribution of a sum of independent Bernoulli random variables when the number of trials grows but the expected number of successes remains constant. The binomial distribution with parameters $(n, \lambda/n)$ converges to the Poisson distribution with parameter $\lambda$. This is the law of rare events: when each trial has small success probability but there are many trials.
