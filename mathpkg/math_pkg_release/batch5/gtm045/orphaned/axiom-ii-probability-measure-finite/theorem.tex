Let $(\Omega, \alpha)$ be as in Axiom I. Let $P$ be a numerical function defined on $\alpha$, called \textit{probability}.

\textbf{Axiom II.} $P$ on $\alpha$ is \textit{normed}, \textit{nonnegative}, and \textit{finitely additive}:

$$P\Omega = 1, \quad PA \geq 0, \quad P \sum_{k=1}^{n} A_k = \sum_{k=1}^{n} PA_k.$$

It suffices to assume additivity for two arbitrary disjoint events.
