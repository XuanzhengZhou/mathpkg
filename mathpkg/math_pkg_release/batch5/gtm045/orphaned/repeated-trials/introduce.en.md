Repeated trials are the mathematical formalization of performing the same random experiment independently many times. In the Bernoulli case, each trial has the same success probability $p$ and trials are independent. The number of successes $S_n$ follows the binomial distribution. This model underlies classical limit theorems such as the laws of large numbers and the central limit theorem.
