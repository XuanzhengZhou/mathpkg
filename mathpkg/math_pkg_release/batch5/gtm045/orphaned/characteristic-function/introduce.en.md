The characteristic function is the Fourier-Stieltjes transform of the distribution. It uniquely determines the distribution (inversion theorem) and converts convolution (sums of independent variables) into pointwise multiplication: $\varphi_{X+Y} = \varphi_X \varphi_Y$. This makes characteristic functions the primary analytical tool for studying sums of independent random variables and limit theorems.
