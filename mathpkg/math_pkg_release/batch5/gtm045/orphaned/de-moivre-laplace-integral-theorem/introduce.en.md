Laplace (1801) extended de Moivre's local result to the integral form: the probability that the standardized binomial sum falls in an interval converges to the corresponding integral of the normal density. This is the prototype of the Central Limit Theorem, establishing the normal distribution as the universal limiting distribution of sums of independent random variables.
