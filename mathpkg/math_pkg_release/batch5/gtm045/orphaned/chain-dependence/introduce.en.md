Chain dependence (Markov dependence) is the stochastic analogue of the Huygens principle in nonhereditary physical systems: the future depends on the past only through the present state. A sequence of events or random variables is chained if the conditional probability given any finite history depends only on the most recent observation. This generalizes independence in a direction fundamental to applications.
