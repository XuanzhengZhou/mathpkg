Let $X_n$ be independent random variables. The series $\sum X_n$ converges almost surely if and only if for some $c > 0$ (or, equivalently, for every $c > 0$) the following three series converge:

$$\sum P[|X_n| \geq c], \quad \sum E X_n^c, \quad \sum \sigma^2 X_n^c,$$

where $X_n^c = X_n$ if $|X_n| < c$, and $X_n^c = 0$ otherwise.
