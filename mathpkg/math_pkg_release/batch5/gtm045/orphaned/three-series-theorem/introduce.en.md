The Kolmogorov Three-Series Theorem provides a complete criterion for the almost sure convergence of a series of independent random variables. The three conditions check: (1) tail probabilities are summable, (2) truncated expectations converge, (3) truncated variances converge. This is one of the deepest and most useful results on sums of independent random variables.
