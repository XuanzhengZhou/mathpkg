A random variable is precisely a measurable function from the probability space to the real line equipped with the Borel $\sigma$-field. Two fundamental modes of convergence are defined: convergence in probability (weakest) and almost sure convergence (strongest among standard modes). Almost sure convergence implies convergence in probability, and convergence in probability implies existence of an almost surely convergent subsequence.
