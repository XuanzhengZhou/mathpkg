A \textit{random variable} (r.v.) $X$ is a function on $\Omega$ to $R = (-\infty, +\infty)$ such that the inverse images under $X$ of all Borel sets in $R$ are events. It suffices that $[X < x] \in \alpha$ for all $x \in R$.

$X_n$ \textit{converges in probability} to $X$ ($X_n \xrightarrow{P} X$) if for every $\epsilon > 0$,
$$P[|X_n - X| \geq \epsilon] \to 0.$$

$X_n$ \textit{converges almost surely} to $X$ ($X_n \xrightarrow{\text{a.s.}} X$) if $X_n \to X$ except on a null event.
