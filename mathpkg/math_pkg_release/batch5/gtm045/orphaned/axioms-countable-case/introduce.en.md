To handle countable combinations of events and to obtain more precise limit theorems, the axioms are upgraded from the finite to the countable case. Axiom I' requires events to form a $\sigma$-field (closed under countable set operations), and Axiom II' requires $\sigma$-additivity of probability. These are the standard Kolmogorov axioms of modern probability theory.
