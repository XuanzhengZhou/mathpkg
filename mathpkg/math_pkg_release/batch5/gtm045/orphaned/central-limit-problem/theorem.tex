The \textit{central limit problem} asks: given a sequence $X_n$ of independent random variables with partial sums $S_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} X_k$, under what conditions on the distributions of the $X_n$ does there exist a sequence of constants $a_n, b_n > 0$ such that the normalized sums $(S_n - a_n)/b_n$ converge in distribution to a nondegenerate limit?
