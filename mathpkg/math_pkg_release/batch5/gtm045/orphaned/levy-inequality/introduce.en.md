Lévy's inequality bounds the probability that the maximum of partial sums of symmetric independent random variables exceeds a threshold by twice the probability that the final sum exceeds it. This is a remarkably strong result: the worst-case behavior of the entire random walk path is controlled by its terminal value alone. It is a key tool for proving strong laws and convergence of series.
