Let $X_1, X_2, \cdots$ be a sequence of elementary random variables. The condition $X_n \to 0$ determines the event

$$[X_n \to 0] = \bigcap_{m=1}^{\infty} \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} \bigcap_{\nu=1}^{\infty} \left[|X_{n+\nu}| < \frac{1}{m}\right].$$

Equivalently, the contrary case $X_n \not\to 0$ determines the event

$$[X_n \not\to 0] = \bigcup_{m=1}^{\infty} \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \bigcup_{\nu=1}^{\infty} \left[|X_{n+\nu}| \geq \frac{1}{m}\right].$$
