Borel's strong law of large numbers (1909) strengthens Bernoulli's weak law: the frequency $S_n/n$ converges not only in probability but almost surely to $p$. The proof uses the summability criterion applied to the subsequence $X_{k^2}$ and then fills the gaps using simple bounds. This was one of the first applications of measure theory to probability.
