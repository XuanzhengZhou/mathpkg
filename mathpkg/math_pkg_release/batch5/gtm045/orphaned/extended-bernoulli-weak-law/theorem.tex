Let $A_n$ be an arbitrary sequence of events, $X_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n} I_{A_k}$, and

$$p_1(n) = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n} PA_k, \quad p_2(n) = \frac{2}{n(n-1)}\sum_{1 \leq j < k \leq n} PA_j A_k.$$

Then $P[|X_n - EX_n| \geq \epsilon] \to 0$ for every $\epsilon > 0$ if, and only if,

$$d_n = p_2(n) - p_1^2(n) \to 0.$$
