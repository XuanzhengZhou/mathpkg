The extended Bernoulli weak law of large numbers gives a necessary and sufficient condition for the frequency of arbitrary (not necessarily independent) events to converge in probability to its expectation. The condition $d_n \to 0$ is an asymptotic uncorrelatedness requirement: the average pairwise joint probability must approach the square of the average marginal probability.
