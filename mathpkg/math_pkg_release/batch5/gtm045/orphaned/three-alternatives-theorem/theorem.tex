For a sequence of independent random variables $X_n$, the normalized sums $(S_n - a_n)/b_n$ exhibit one of three alternatives as $n \to \infty$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textit{Convergence}: $(S_n - a_n)/b_n$ converges in distribution to some law;
\item \textit{Divergence}: $(S_n - a_n)/b_n \to \pm\infty$ in probability;
\item \textit{Oscillation}: the sequence has at least two distinct limit points in distribution.
\end{enumerate}
