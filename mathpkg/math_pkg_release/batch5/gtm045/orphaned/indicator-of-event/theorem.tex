To every event $A$ we assign a function $I_A$ on $\Omega$ with values $I_A(\omega)$, such that $I_A(\omega) = 1$ or $0$ according as $\omega$ belongs or does not belong to $A$. $I_A$ is called the \textit{indicator} of $A$.

Properties: $I_A^2 = I_A$, $I_\emptyset = 0$, $I_\Omega = 1$. If $A \subset B$, then $I_A \leq I_B$, and conversely.
