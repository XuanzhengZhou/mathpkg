A probability space $(\Omega, \alpha, P)$ is the fundamental structure in probability theory. It consists of the sample space $\Omega$, a field $\alpha$ of events, and a probability measure $P$. In modern terminology (countable case), $\alpha$ is required to be a $\sigma$-field and $P$ is $\sigma$-additive.
