An infinitely divisible distribution can be expressed as the distribution of a sum of $n$ i.i.d. random variables for every $n$. The Lévy-Khintchine formula provides a complete characterization via a canonical representation of the characteristic function. Stable laws are a subclass, but the full class includes the Poisson, gamma, and normal distributions.
