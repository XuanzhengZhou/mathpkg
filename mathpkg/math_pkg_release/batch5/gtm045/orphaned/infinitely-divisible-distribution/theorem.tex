A distribution $F$ is \textit{infinitely divisible} if for every $n$ there exists a distribution $F_n$ such that $F = F_n^{*n}$ (the $n$-fold convolution of $F_n$ with itself). Equivalently, its characteristic function satisfies $\varphi = (\varphi_n)^n$ for some ch.f. $\varphi_n$.

The class of infinitely divisible laws coincides with the class of possible limit distributions of sums of uan triangular arrays.
