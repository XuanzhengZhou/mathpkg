For i.i.d. random variables $X_n$ with d.f. $F$, the normalized sums converge to a stable law of exponent $\alpha \in (0,2)$ if and only if the tail probabilities are regularly varying:

$$P[|X| \geq x] = x^{-\alpha} L(x),$$

where $L$ is slowly varying at $\infty$, and the tail balancing condition

$$\frac{P[X \geq x]}{P[|X| \geq x]} \to p, \quad \frac{P[X \leq -x]}{P[|X| \geq x]} \to q$$

holds for some $p, q \geq 0$ with $p + q = 1$.
