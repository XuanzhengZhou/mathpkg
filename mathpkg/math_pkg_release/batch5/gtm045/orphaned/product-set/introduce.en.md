Product sets and cylinder sets are the building blocks for constructing product $\sigma$-fields and product measures. A cylinder is specified by conditions on only finitely many coordinates, leaving the rest unrestricted. Product cylinders (intervals) play the role of rectangles in defining the product $\sigma$-field, analogous to how intervals generate the Borel $\sigma$-field on $\mathbb{R}$.
