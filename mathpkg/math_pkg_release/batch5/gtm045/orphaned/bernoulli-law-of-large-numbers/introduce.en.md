The Bernoulli law of large numbers (1713) is the first mathematical justification of the frequency interpretation of probability: in repeated independent trials, the observed frequency $S_n/n$ of an outcome converges in probability to its true probability $p$. The proof uses the Tchebichev inequality and requires only pairwise independence.
