Every function induces a partition of its domain (points mapping to the same value), and this partition generates a $\sigma$-field. In probability, the $\sigma$-field $X^{-1}(\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}))$ generated by a random variable $X$ represents the information carried by $X$: events whose occurrence is determined by observing $X$.
