Let $X: \Omega \to \Omega'$ be a function. The \textit{partition} of $\Omega$ induced by $X$ is formed by the inverse images $X^{-1}(\omega')$ of points $\omega' \in \Omega'$. The class $X^{-1}(S(\Omega'))$ of inverse images of all subsets of $\Omega'$ is a $\sigma$-field, called the $\sigma$-field induced by $X$.

If $X$ is measurable with respect to $\alpha'$, then $X^{-1}(\alpha')$ is a sub-$\sigma$-field of $\alpha$.
