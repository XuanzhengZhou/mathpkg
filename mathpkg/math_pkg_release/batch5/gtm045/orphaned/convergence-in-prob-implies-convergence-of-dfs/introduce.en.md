Convergence in probability is stronger than convergence in distribution (weak convergence): if $X_n \to X$ in probability, then the distribution functions converge at all continuity points of the limit. The proof uses simple set inclusion arguments. This establishes the hierarchy: almost sure convergence $\implies$ convergence in probability $\implies$ convergence in distribution.
