A distribution $F$ belongs to the domain of \textit{partial attraction} of a stable law $G$ if there exists a subsequence $n_k$ and constants $a_{n_k}, b_{n_k} > 0$ such that normalized sums $S_{n_k}$ of i.i.d. $F$-distributed variables converge in distribution to $G$. This is a weaker notion than full attraction, allowing convergence only along a subsequence.
