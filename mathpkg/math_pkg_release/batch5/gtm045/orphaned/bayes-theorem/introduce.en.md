Bayes' theorem provides a formula for "inverse" probability: the conditional probability of a hypothesis $A_k$ given observed evidence $B$. It expresses the posterior probability $P_B A_k$ in terms of the prior $PA_k$, the likelihood $P_{A_k}B$, and the total probability of the evidence. This theorem is the foundation of Bayesian statistical inference.
