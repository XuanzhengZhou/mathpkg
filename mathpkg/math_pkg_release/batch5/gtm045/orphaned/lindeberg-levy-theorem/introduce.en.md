The Lindeberg-Lévy Central Limit Theorem is the classical CLT: standardized sums of i.i.d. random variables with finite variance converge in distribution to the standard normal. The proof uses characteristic functions and the expansion $\varphi(u) = 1 + iu\mu - u^2\sigma^2/2 + o(u^2)$. This is the most famous result in probability theory.
