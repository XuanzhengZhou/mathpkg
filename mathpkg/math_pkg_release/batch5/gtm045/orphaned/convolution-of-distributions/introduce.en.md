Convolution is the operation on distributions corresponding to summing independent random variables. The distribution of a sum is the convolution of the individual distributions. Characteristic functions convert convolution into pointwise multiplication, making them indispensable for studying sums: $\varphi_{S_n} = \prod \varphi_{X_k}$.
