Weak convergence (convergence in distribution) is the weakest standard mode of stochastic convergence, defined by pointwise convergence of distribution functions at continuity points. The Helly-Bray selection theorem provides a compactness principle: every sequence of distribution functions has a subsequence converging to a possibly defective distribution, enabling compactness arguments in limit theory.
