Let $\{X_{nk}\}$ be a uan triangular array with row sums $S_n = \sum_k X_{nk}$. Then $S_n - a_n \xrightarrow{d} F$ (infinitely divisible with characteristic $\varphi$) if and only if

$$\sum_k F_{nk}(x) \to M(x) \text{ at continuity points, and } \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} \limsup_n \sum_k \left(\int_{|x|<\epsilon} x^2 dF_{nk} - \left(\int_{|x|<\epsilon} x dF_{nk}\right)^2\right) = \sigma^2,$$

where $F_{nk}$ is the d.f. of $X_{nk}$ and $M$ determines the Lévy-Khintchine representation of $\varphi$.
