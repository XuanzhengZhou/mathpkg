A nonsingular 2-cell is a singular 2-cell $D: \Delta \to X$ that is a homeomorphism onto its image. In the PL category of 3-manifold topology, this means $D$ embeds the polyhedral disk $\Delta$ into $X$ without self-intersections. The main conclusion of the Stallings loop theorem is that, under suitable hypotheses, a singular 2-cell whose boundary is a loop in the boundary of a 3-manifold can be replaced by a nonsingular 2-cell with the same boundary properties.
