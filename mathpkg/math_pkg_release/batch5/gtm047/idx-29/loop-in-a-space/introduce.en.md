A loop in a topological space $X$ is a closed path without a designated base point. Formally, it is a continuous mapping $L: S^1 \to X$ from the circle into $X$. This concept generalizes the notion of a closed path (which carries a base point) and is fundamental to the study of the loop theorem and Dehn's lemma in 3-manifold topology. Unlike an element of the fundamental group, a loop determines only a conjugacy class in $\pi(X, P_0)$ rather than a specific element.
