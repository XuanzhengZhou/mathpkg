The Stallings loop theorem (Theorem 1) is a powerful generalization of the classical loop theorem of Papakyriakopoulos. It states that if a triangulated 3-manifold $M$ with boundary admits a PL singular 2-cell whose boundary loop $L$ lies in a boundary component $B$ and whose loop conjugacy class avoids a normal subgroup $N$ of $\pi(B)$, then there exists a nonsingular (embedded) PL 2-cell with the same properties. The theorem uses the flexibility of an arbitrary normal subgroup $N$ to strengthen the conclusion; the classical loop theorem corresponds to the special case where $M$ is orientable and $N = \{1\}$.
