Suppose that $D: \Delta \rightarrow M$ is PL, is locally a homeomorphism, and is at most two-to-one at each point of $\Delta$. Let $B'$ be a connected 2-manifold with boundary which forms a neighborhood of $|L|$ in $B$, suppose that $P_0 \in \operatorname{Int} B'$, and let $N'$ be a normal subgroup of $\pi(B', P_0)$, such that $L(B') \cap N' = \emptyset$. Then there is a nonsingular 2-cell $D_1: \Delta \rightarrow M$ such that for $L_1 = \operatorname{Bd} D_1$ we have $|L_1| = |D_1| \cap \operatorname{Bd} M$ and $L_1(B') \cap N' = \emptyset$.
