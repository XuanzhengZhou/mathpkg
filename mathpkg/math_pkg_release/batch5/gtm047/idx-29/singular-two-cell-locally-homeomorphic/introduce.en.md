Lemma 2 in the Stallings proof of the loop theorem provides the crucial technical reduction step. If a PL singular 2-cell $D: \Delta \to M$ is locally a homeomorphism and at most two-to-one, and its boundary loop $L$ satisfies the hypothesis that its loop conjugacy class avoids a normal subgroup $N'$ of $\pi(B', P_0)$, then $D$ can be replaced by a nonsingular 2-cell $D_1$ with the same boundary properties. The proof proceeds by a complexity analysis of the singular set, considering four cases of self-intersections (polygonal curves $\Gamma_i$ and broken lines $A_j$) and showing that each case either cannot occur or would allow reduction of complexity, ultimately forcing the singular set to be empty.
