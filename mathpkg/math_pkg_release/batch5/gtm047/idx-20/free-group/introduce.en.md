The free group $F(A)$ on an alphabet $A$ is constructed from the monoid $W(A)$ of words by introducing an equivalence relation that formalizes the group operations. Two words are equivalent if one can be transformed into the other by inserting or deleting syllables of the form $a^0$, or by merging/splitting consecutive syllables with the same alphabet element via exponent addition. The equivalence classes form a group under concatenation-induced multiplication. This construction, based on Crowell and Fox, provides the algebraic framework for presenting the fundamental group of a link complement.
