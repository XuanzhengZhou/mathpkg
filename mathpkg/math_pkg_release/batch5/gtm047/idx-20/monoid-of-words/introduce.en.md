The set $W(A)$ of all words over an alphabet $A$, equipped with the concatenation product, forms a monoid. The multiplication is associative, and the empty word $e$ serves as the identity element. However, $W(A)$ is not a group because nonempty words lack inverses under concatenation alone. The free group $F(A)$ is constructed by passing to an equivalence relation on $W(A)$ that formally introduces inverses.
