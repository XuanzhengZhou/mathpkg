Let $K_1$ and $K_2$ be knots in $\mathbf{R}^3$. Let

$$\phi: K_1 \times [0, 1] \rightarrow \mathbf{R}^3$$

be an isotopy, such that (1) for each point $P$ of $K_1$, $\phi(P, 0) = P$ and (2) $\phi(K_1 \times \{1\}) = K_2$. Prove or disprove: if $K_1$ is unknotted, then so also is $K_2$.
