An alphabet is simply a nonempty set $A$ whose elements serve as the basic symbols from which words are built in the construction of a free group. The construction follows the treatment of Crowell and Fox. The alphabet plays the role of the generating set, and each element $a \in A$ will correspond to a generator of the resulting free group $F(A)$.
