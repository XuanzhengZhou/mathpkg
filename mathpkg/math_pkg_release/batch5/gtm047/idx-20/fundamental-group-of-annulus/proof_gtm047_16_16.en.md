**Proof.** A brute-force computation of $\pi(A)$ is easy. We may assume that $A$ is a polyhedron in $\mathbf{R}^2$. Here $g_1$ generates $\pi(A)$: given a closed path in $\text{CP}(A, P_0)$, we can reduce it to a product $g_1^{\pm 1} g_1^{\pm 1} \ldots g_1^{\pm 1}$, as in the case of a closed path in the complement of a link in $\mathbf{R}^3$. There are no relations, because when a path is moved across a triangle, the most that we do to the word for the path is to insert or delete $g_1^{\pm 1} g_1^{\mp 1}$.
