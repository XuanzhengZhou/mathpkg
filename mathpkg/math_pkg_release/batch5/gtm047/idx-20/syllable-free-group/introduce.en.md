A syllable is the basic building block of a word in the free group construction. Given an alphabet $A$, a syllable pairs an element $a \in A$ with an integer exponent $\alpha$. The notation $a^\alpha$ is chosen to fit the algebraic pattern of group theory, anticipating that words will multiply in a way consistent with exponent addition.
