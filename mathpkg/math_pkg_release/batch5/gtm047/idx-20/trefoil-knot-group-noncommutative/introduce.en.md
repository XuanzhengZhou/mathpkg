This is the first computable example showing that a knot group detects nontrivial knottedness. The trefoil knot, being nontrivial, has a noncommutative fundamental group, in contrast to the unknot (whose group is $\mathbb{Z}$). The proof constructs an explicit surjective homomorphism from the knot group $F(g_1,g_2,g_3)/N(R)$ onto the symmetric group $S_3$ by mapping generators to transpositions. Since $S_3$ is noncommutative, the quotient is noncommutative, hence the knot group itself is noncommutative. Consequently, no two generators commute.
