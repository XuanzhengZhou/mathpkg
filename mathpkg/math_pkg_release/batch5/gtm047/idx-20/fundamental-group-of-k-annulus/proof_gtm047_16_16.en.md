**Proof.** We recall, from the beginning of Section 13, that a $k$-annulus is a compact connected 2-manifold $A$ with boundary, imbeddable in $\mathbb{R}^2$, such that $\text{Bd } A$ has $k + 1$ components. Since $\pi(A)$ is a topological invariant of $A$, we may assume that $A \subset \mathbb{R}^2$. By the Tame imbedding theorem (Theorem 6.2), together with repeated applications of Theorem 3.7, we may suppose also that the inner components $J_i$ of $\text{Bd } A$ can be joined to the outer component $J_0$ by disjoint linear intervals. Each of these linear intervals then gives a generator which crosses it exactly once. These paths generate the group, and as in the case of a 1-annulus, there are no relations. Therefore

$$\pi(A) \approx F(g_1, g_2, \ldots, g_k).$$
