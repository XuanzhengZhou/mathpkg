**Theorem 6.** Let $p: \tilde{X} \to X$ be a covering space, let $f: Y \to X$ be a mapping, and suppose $Y$ is pathwise connected and locally pathwise connected. Then there exists a lifting $\tilde{f}: Y \to \tilde{X}$ (i.e., $p \circ \tilde{f} = f$) if and only if $f^*(\pi(Y)) \subset p^*(\pi(\tilde{X}))$. If the lifting exists, it is unique once the image of a single base point is specified.
