Every polygon in the plane can be mapped to the boundary of a 2-simplex by a homeomorphism of the whole plane. The proof proceeds by triangulating the interior of the polygon, then repeatedly removing free 2-simplexes via local homeomorphisms that are identities outside a neighborhood, until only one 2-simplex remains. This theorem is the geometric heart of the Schönflies theorem for polygons.
