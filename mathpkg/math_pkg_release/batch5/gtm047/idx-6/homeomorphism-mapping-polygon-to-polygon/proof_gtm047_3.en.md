By Theorem 4 there are homeomorphisms

$$f_1: \mathbb{R}^2 \leftrightarrow \mathbb{R}^2, \quad J \leftrightarrow \operatorname{Fr} \sigma^2,$$

$$f_2: \mathbb{R}^2 \leftrightarrow \mathbb{R}^2, \quad J' \leftrightarrow \operatorname{Fr} \tau^2.$$

By Theorem 2 there is a homeomorphism

$$f_3: \mathbb{R}^2 \leftrightarrow \mathbb{R}^2, \quad \sigma^2 \leftrightarrow \tau^2.$$

Let $h = f_2^{-1} f_3 f_1$. Then $h$ is a homeomorphism of $\mathbb{R}^2$ onto itself, and $h(J) = J'$.
