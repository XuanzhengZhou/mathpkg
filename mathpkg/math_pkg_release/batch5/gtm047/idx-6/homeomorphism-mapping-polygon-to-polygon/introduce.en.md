Any two polygons in the plane are situated in exactly the same way topologically: there is a homeomorphism of the whole plane sending one polygon onto the other. This is the Schönflies theorem for polygons, which shows that all polygonal simple closed curves are ambiently equivalent. The proof composes three homeomorphisms: first mapping one polygon to the frontier of a 2-simplex, then mapping that simplex to another via a linear extension, and finally sending the second simplex's frontier back to the second polygon.
