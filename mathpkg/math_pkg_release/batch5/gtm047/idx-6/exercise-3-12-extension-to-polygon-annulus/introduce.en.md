Prove that any homeomorphism of the inner polygonal boundary of an annular region can be extended to a homeomorphism of the entire annulus onto itself. This is analogous to Exercise 1 but for the annular region between two nested polygons. The result shows that boundary homeomorphisms on the inner component can always be "filled in" across the annulus without disturbing the outer boundary.
