A polygon $J$ has the push property at a broken line $B_1$ if there exist homeomorphisms of the plane mapping $B_1$ onto its complementary broken line $B_2 = \operatorname{Cl}(J - B_1)$, with support arbitrarily close to the region bounded by $B_1$ and including its interior. In other words, one can "push" $B_1$ across the interior to the opposite arc $B_2$ via a homeomorphism that is the identity outside any prescribed neighborhood of the affected region. This technical condition on polygons is used in the proof of the full Jordan-Schönflies theorem.
