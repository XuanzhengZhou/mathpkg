By Theorem 4, there is a homeomorphism $h: \mathbb{R}^2 \leftrightarrow \mathbb{R}^2$ such that $h(J)$ is the frontier of a 2-simplex $\sigma^2$. The 2-simplex $\sigma^2$ is a 2-cell. Then $h^{-1}(\sigma^2)$ is a 2-cell in $\mathbb{R}^2$ whose frontier is $J$. Therefore $J$ is the frontier of a 2-cell.
