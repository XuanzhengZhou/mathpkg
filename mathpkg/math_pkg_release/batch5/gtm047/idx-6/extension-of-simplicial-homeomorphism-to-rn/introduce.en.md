When two $n$-simplexes lie in $\mathbb{R}^n$, the simplicial homeomorphism between them can be extended to a homeomorphism of the whole ambient space $\mathbb{R}^n$. The construction uses translations to place both simplexes with a vertex at the origin, then defines a linear extension that restricts to the original simplicial map. This shows that simplexes of the same dimension are not only homeomorphic but sit in $\mathbb{R}^n$ in equivalent ways.
