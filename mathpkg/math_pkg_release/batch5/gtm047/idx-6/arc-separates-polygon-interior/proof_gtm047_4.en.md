Let $Q'$ and $S'$ be points of $I$, near $Q$ and $S$, as in the figure. If $Q'$ and $S'$ are in the same component of $I - A$, then there is a broken line in $I - A$ connecting $Q'$ to $S'$. This broken line, together with a suitable arc near the boundary, would produce a contradiction with the cyclic ordering of $P, Q, R, S$ on $\operatorname{Fr} I$. Therefore $Q'$ and $S'$ lie in different components of $I - A$. Let $U_Q$ be the component containing $Q'$ and $U_S$ the component containing $S'$; these are disjoint open sets with $Q$ and $S$ in their respective frontiers.

(Note that by Theorem 3.5 there is no loss of generality in supposing that $\bar{I}$ is a rectangular region. Similarly, by the result of Problem 3.5, we can put $P, Q, R$, and $S$ into the positions shown in Figure 4.1.)
