An arc that lies inside the closure of a polygon's interior and meets the boundary only at its two endpoints separates the interior into two disjoint open regions. Given four cyclically ordered boundary points, an arc connecting opposite points splits the interior so that the other two points lie in the frontiers of distinct components. This separation lemma is essential for the proof of the Jordan curve theorem, as it establishes how arcs interact with polygonal boundaries in the plane.
