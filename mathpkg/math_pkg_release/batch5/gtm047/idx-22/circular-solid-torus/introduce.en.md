A circular solid torus is the solid of revolution obtained by rotating a circular closed plane region about a coplanar line that does not intersect the region. It is the canonical model for a solid torus in $\mathbf{R}^3$, and forms the basic building block in Antoine's construction of a wild Cantor set. Every set homeomorphic to a circular solid torus is called a solid torus.
