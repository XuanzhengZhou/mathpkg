Let $\{A_i\}$ and $\{A_i'\}$ be families of polyhedral disks such that $A_i$ and $A_i'$ are $k_i$-annuli for the same $k_i$, and $A_i \cap A_j \neq \emptyset$ if and only if $A_i' \cap A_j' \neq \emptyset$, with each nonempty intersection being a polygon. Suppose further that the intersection pattern corresponds to the edges of a finite 1-complex $K$: $A_i \cap A_j \neq \emptyset \iff v_i$ and $v_j$ are endpoints of an edge of $K$.

Then there exists a piecewise linear homeomorphism
$$f : \bigcup_i A_i \leftrightarrow \bigcup_i A_i'$$
such that for each $i$, $f(A_i) = A_i'$, and for each $i,j$,
$$f(A_i \cap A_j) = A_i' \cap A_j'.$$
