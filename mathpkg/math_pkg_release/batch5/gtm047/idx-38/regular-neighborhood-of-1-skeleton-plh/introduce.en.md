Lemma 1 provides the first major step in proving that homeomorphisms of polyhedral 3-cells can be approximated by PLH maps. It constructs a regular neighborhood $N$ of the 1-skeleton and a piecewise linear homeomorphism $f_1$ from $N$ onto a neighborhood $N''$ of the image of the 1-skeleton under $h$. The crucial technical conditions (2)-(4) ensure that the images of splitting disks, dual cells, and torus neighborhoods $N_\sigma''$ intersect the images of simplexes only in the combinatorially correct way — that is, only when the corresponding simplex is incident to the relevant edge, vertex, or boundary. The proof follows from Theorem 33.1 by taking $f_1$ as a sufficiently close PLH approximation.
