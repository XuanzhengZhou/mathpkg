Let $K$ be a triangulated polyhedral $3$-cell in $\mathbb{R}^3$, let $K^1$ denote its $1$-skeleton, and let $h: K \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a homeomorphism extendable to a neighborhood $U$ of $K$. Let $N$ be a regular neighborhood of $K^1$ lying in $U$, with splitting disks $D_e$ (containing the midpoints of edges $e$ of $K$) that decompose $N$ into dual cells $C_v$ (each containing exactly one vertex $v$ of $K$). For each $2$-simplex $\sigma$ of $K$, let $N_{\sigma}$ be the union of the dual cells containing vertices of $\sigma$, so that $N_{\sigma}$ is a solid torus.

Then for any $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists a regular neighborhood $N$ of $K^1$ (chosen sufficiently small) and a piecewise linear homeomorphism
$$f_1 : N \leftrightarrow N'' \subset \mathbb{R}^3$$
satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $N''$ is a neighborhood of $h(K^1)$;
\item $D_e'' \cap \sigma' \neq \emptyset$ only if $e$ is an edge of $\sigma$;
\item $C_v'' \cap \sigma' \neq \emptyset$ only if $v$ is a vertex of $\sigma$;
\item $N_{\sigma}''$ is a neighborhood of $J' = \operatorname{Bd} \sigma'$;
\item $f_1$ is an $(\varepsilon/3)$-approximation of $h|_N$.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover, $N$ can be chosen to lie in any prescribed neighborhood of $K^1$, and $f_1$ can be chosen so that $N''$ lies in any prescribed neighborhood of $h(K^1)$.
