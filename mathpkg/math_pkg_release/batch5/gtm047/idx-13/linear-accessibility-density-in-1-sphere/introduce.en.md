For any 1-sphere $J \subset \mathbf{R}^2$ and any arc $A$ on $J$, there exists a point $v$ in the interior of $A$ from which a straight line segment can be drawn into the interior $I$ of $J$, touching $J$ only at $v$. As a consequence, the set of points on $J$ that are linearly accessible from $I$ is dense in $J$. This density result is critical for constructing polygonal approximations of the sphere from its interior, which is an essential step in the proof of the Schönflies theorem.
