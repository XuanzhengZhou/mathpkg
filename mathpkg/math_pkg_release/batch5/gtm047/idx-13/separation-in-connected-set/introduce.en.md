In a connected set $C$, a subset $D$ is said to separate two points $P$ and $Q$ (both not in $D$) if the complement $C - D$ can be written as the union of two separated sets that contain $P$ and $Q$ respectively. This definition formalizes the intuitive notion of a set "cutting" a connected space into two disconnected pieces. It is a standard point-set topological concept used throughout the study of separation properties in Euclidean spaces and manifolds.
