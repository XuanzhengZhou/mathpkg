A point $v$ on a 1-sphere $J \subset \mathbf{R}^2$ is linearly accessible from the interior $I$ of $J$ if there is a straight line segment from $v$ into $I$ whose only intersection with $J$ is the endpoint $v$. This property is used to build polygonal approximations of $J$ from the inside, a key step in proving the Schönflies theorem. The set of linearly accessible points is shown to be dense in $J$.
