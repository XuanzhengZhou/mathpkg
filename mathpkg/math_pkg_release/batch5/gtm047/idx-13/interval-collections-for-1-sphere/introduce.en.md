Given a sequence of finite collections $G_i$ of arcs on a 1-sphere $J$, there exists a nested sequence of collections $H_i$ of linear intervals radiating from $J$ into its interior $I$, such that each interval attaches at an endpoint of some arc in $G_i$, the intervals within each $H_i$ are disjoint, and the nesting satisfies a natural inclusion property: when an interval from a later stage intersects one from an earlier stage, it must be a subinterval sharing the same base point. This construction turns the interior of $J$ into the "inside of an infinitely furry animal," with increasingly fine linear "hairs" providing a framework for the polygonal approximation used in the Schönflies theorem.
