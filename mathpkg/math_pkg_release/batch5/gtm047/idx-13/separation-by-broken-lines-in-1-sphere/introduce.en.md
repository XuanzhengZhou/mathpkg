Consider a 1-sphere $J \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ formed by an arc $A$ and a broken line $B$ meeting at their endpoints $P$ and $Q$. If a finite collection $M$ of disjoint broken lines lying in the interior $I$ (with endpoints on $\text{Int } B$) separates a point $R \in \text{Int } A$ from $S \in \text{Int } B$ in the closure $\bar{I}$, then at least one of the broken lines $M_i$ must have endpoints that already separate $R$ from $S$ on the boundary $J$. This combinatorial lemma underpins the geometric construction used in the proof of the Schönflies theorem.
