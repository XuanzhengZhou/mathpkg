Let $J$ be a 1-sphere in $\mathbb{R}^2$ which is the union of an arc $A$ and a broken line $B$, intersecting in their end-points $P$ and $Q$. Let $I$ be the interior of $J$. Let $R$ and $S$ be points of $\text{Int } A$ and $\text{Int } B$ respectively. Let $M$ be the union of a finite collection of disjoint broken lines $M_1, M_2, \ldots, M_n$, lying in $I$ except for their end-points, which lie in $\text{Int } B - \{S\}$. Suppose that $M$ separates $R$ from $S$ in $\bar{I}$. Then some $M_i$ has end-points which separate $R$ from $S$ in $J$.
