Let $\mathbf{B}^2$ be the unit disk in $\mathbf{R}^2$, let $J = \text{Bd } \mathbf{B}^2$, and let $A$ be an arc such that $\text{Bd } A \subset J$ and $\text{Int } A \subset \text{Int } \mathbf{B}^2$. Prove or disprove: $\mathbf{B}^2 - A$ has exactly two components, and each of these contains a component of $J - \text{Bd } A$.
