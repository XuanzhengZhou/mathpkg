The proof (incomplete in the source text) proceeds as follows. By Theorem 3, there is a sequence of finite collections of arcs $G_i$ approximating $J$, and by Theorem 4 there are corresponding collections $H_i$ of linear intervals. The broken line $b_A$ is constructed lying in $\bar{I}'$ (the closure of a modified interior) with its endpoints separating $\text{Int } A$ from $\text{Int } b$ in a modified 1-sphere $J'$. The endpoints of $b_A$ lie in $v_0 v_0'$ and $v_1 v_1'$. Since the auxiliary 1-sphere $J_i$ lies in the $\varepsilon$-neighborhood $N(A, \varepsilon)$ and $b_A \subset J_i$, the approximation property follows.
