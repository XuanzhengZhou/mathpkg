For $i = 1, 2$, let $g_i$ be a PLH $\sigma^2 \leftrightarrow |K_i|$.

Then $g_2^{-1}fg_1$ is a PLH $\text{Bd}\,\sigma^2 \leftrightarrow \text{Bd}\,\sigma^2$. This has a PLH extension $g' : \sigma^2 \leftrightarrow \sigma^2$. Let $f' = g_2 g' g_1^{-1}$. Then $f'$ is a PLH $|K_1| \leftrightarrow |K_2|$, and

$$f'| \text{Bd}\,K_1 = g_2 g_2^{-1} f g_1 g_1^{-1} = f,$$

which is what we wanted.
