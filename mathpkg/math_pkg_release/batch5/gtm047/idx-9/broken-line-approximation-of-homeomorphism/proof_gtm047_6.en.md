We recall that $N(A, \varepsilon) = \{Q \mid d(P, Q) < \varepsilon \text{ for some } P \in A\}$. Obviously $N(A, \varepsilon)$ is open, and it is easy to show that $N(A, \varepsilon)$ is connected, because $A = h(vv')$ is connected. Since $N(A, \varepsilon)$ is open and connected, it is path-connected. Therefore there exists a broken line $B$ from $P = h(v)$ to $Q = h(v')$ lying entirely in $N(A, \varepsilon)$. This broken line $B$ is the desired approximation.
