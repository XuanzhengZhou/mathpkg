The implication $(2) \Rightarrow (1)$ is trivial, since any subdivision of $K_1$ is a subdivision of $K$. It remains to show that $(1) \Rightarrow (2)$.

Given $K_2 < K$, such that for each $\sigma \in K_2$, $f|\sigma$ is linear. Let $K_{12}$ be a common subdivision of $K_1$ and $K_2$. Then for each $\sigma \in K_{12}$, $f|\sigma$ is linear. $\square$
