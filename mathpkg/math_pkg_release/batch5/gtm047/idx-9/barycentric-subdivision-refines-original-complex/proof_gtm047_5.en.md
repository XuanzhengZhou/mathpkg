By construction, each simplex of $bK$ is contained in a simplex of $K$: at step $i+1$, each new simplex $v\sigma^i$ (where $v$ is the barycenter of $\sigma^{i+1}$ and $\sigma^i \in bK^i$ with $\sigma^i \subset \sigma^{i+1}$) lies entirely within $\sigma^{i+1} \in K$. Moreover, the vertices of $bK$ are barycenters of simplices of $K$, all lying in $|K|$, so $|bK| = |K|$. Thus $bK$ satisfies the definition of a subdivision of $K$.
