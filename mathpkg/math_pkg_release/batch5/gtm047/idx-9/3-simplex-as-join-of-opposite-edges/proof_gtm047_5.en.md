The join $J(v_0 v_1, v_2 v_3)$ is the union of all segments joining points of the edge $v_0 v_1$ to points of the opposite edge $v_2 v_3$. Any point in $\sigma^3 = v_0 v_1 v_2 v_3$ can be expressed as a convex combination $\lambda_0 v_0 + \lambda_1 v_1 + \lambda_2 v_2 + \lambda_3 v_3$ with $\lambda_i \geqslant 0$, $\sum \lambda_i = 1$. Let $s = \lambda_0 + \lambda_1$ and $t = \lambda_2 + \lambda_3$, so $s + t = 1$. Then the point equals $s \cdot (\frac{\lambda_0}{s} v_0 + \frac{\lambda_1}{s} v_1) + t \cdot (\frac{\lambda_2}{t} v_2 + \frac{\lambda_3}{t} v_3)$, which lies on the segment between a point of $v_0 v_1$ and a point of $v_2 v_3$. Hence $\sigma^3 = J(v_0 v_1, v_2 v_3)$.
