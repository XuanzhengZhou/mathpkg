Given three polygons $B_1, B_2, B_3$ meeting at a single point with pairwise disjoint interiors such that $\operatorname{Int} B_2$ lies in the interior $I_{13}$ of $B_1 \cup B_3$, this theorem describes how $I_{13}$ decomposes when $\operatorname{Int} B_2$ is removed. The two resulting components are precisely the interiors $I_{12}$ and $I_{23}$ of the unions $B_1 \cup B_2$ and $B_2 \cup B_3$, and the closure relations among these interior regions are completely characterized.
