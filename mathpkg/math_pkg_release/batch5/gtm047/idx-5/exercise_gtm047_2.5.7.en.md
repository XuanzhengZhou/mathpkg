Let $A$ and $B$ be broken lines in $\mathbf{R}^2$, with $A \cap B = \operatorname{Int} A \cap \operatorname{Int} B = \{P\}$. Suppose that there is a connected neighborhood $N$ of $P$ such that $N \cap A$ separates two points of $N \cap B$ from one another in $N$. Then $B$ crosses $A$ at $P$.
