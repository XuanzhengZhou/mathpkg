A $k$-sheeted covering (also called a $k$-fold covering) is a covering $g: \tilde{M} \to M$ in which each fiber $g^{-1}(P)$ consists of exactly $k$ elements. Because $M$ is connected, the cardinality of the fiber is constant across all points of the base space. Finite-sheeted coverings play a crucial role in relating the topology of the covering space to the algebraic structure of the fundamental group of the base, as the number of sheets equals the index of the associated subgroup.
