Given a covering $g: \tilde{M} \to M$ and basepoints $P_0 \in M$, $\tilde{P}_0 \in g^{-1}(P_0)$, the induced homomorphism $g_0^*: \pi(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0) \to \pi(M, P_0)$ maps the homotopy class of a loop $\tilde{p}$ to the class of its image $g \circ \tilde{p}$. The image $\pi_0 = g_0^*(\pi(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0))$ is called the group associated with the covering. This subgroup of $\pi(M, P_0)$ characterizes the covering up to equivalence, and the standard construction of covering spaces from subgroups of the fundamental group reverses this correspondence.
