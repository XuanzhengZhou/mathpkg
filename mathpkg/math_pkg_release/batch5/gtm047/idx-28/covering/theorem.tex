Let $\tilde{M}$ and $M$ be connected spaces, compact or not, such that $M$ is a polyhedron. Let
$$g: \tilde{M} \rightarrow M$$
be a surjective mapping. Suppose that the sets $g^{-1}(P)$ for $P \in M$ are at most countable, and that none of them have any limit points. For each $P \in M$, let
$$g^{-1}(P) = \{ \tilde{P}_i \}.$$
Suppose that for each point $P$ of $M$ there is an open set $U$, containing $P$, such that
$$g^{-1}(U) = \bigcup_i \tilde{U}_i,$$
where (1) the sets $\tilde{U}_i$ are disjoint, (2) $\tilde{P}_i \in \tilde{U}_i$ for each $i$, and (3) for each $i$, $g|\tilde{U}_i$ is a homeomorphism $\tilde{U}_i \leftrightarrow U$. Then $g: \tilde{M} \rightarrow M$ is a \emph{covering}.
