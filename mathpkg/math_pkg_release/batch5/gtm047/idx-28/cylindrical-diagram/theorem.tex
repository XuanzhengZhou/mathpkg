If $\phi$ is as in Theorem 9, then $\phi$ is called a \emph{cylindrical diagram} for the combinatorial solid torus $S$. Namely, $\phi: \sigma^2 \times [0, 1] \rightarrow S$ is a PL mapping such that $\sigma^2 \times \{0\}$ and $\sigma^2 \times \{1\}$ are mapped onto the same 2-cell in $S$, and $\phi$ is a homeomorphism elsewhere.
