A cylindrical diagram for a combinatorial solid torus $S$ is a piecewise-linear mapping $\phi: \sigma^2 \times [0,1] \to S$ from a prism to $S$ that maps the two ends of the prism onto the same 2-cell in $S$, while being a homeomorphism elsewhere. The cylindrical diagram provides a concrete parametrization of the solid torus as a twisted product, decomposing it into a finite sequence of combinatorial 3-cells stacked end-to-end. This structure is used to establish combinatorial equivalence results for solid tori in 3-manifolds.
