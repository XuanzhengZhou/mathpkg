Every non-orientable connected polyhedral 3-manifold with boundary admits a 2-fold covering. The proof constructs this covering using the orientation data of the triangulation: equivalence classes of paths from a basepoint determine whether they induce the same local orientation at the endpoint. This 2-fold covering is analogous to the orientable double cover familiar from manifold theory, providing a way to "orient" a non-orientable 3-manifold by passing to a covering space.
