Given a covering $g: \tilde{M} \to M$ and a continuous map $f: \Delta \to M$ from a 2-cell, for any choice of basepoint $\tilde{P}_0$ in the fiber over $f(Q_0)$, there exists a unique lift $\tilde{f}: \Delta \to \tilde{M}$ such that $g \circ \tilde{f} = f$ and $\tilde{f}(Q_0) = \tilde{P}_0$. The same uniqueness holds for paths. This theorem is the fundamental lifting property that makes covering spaces work: it allows one to "pull back" maps from the base to the total space, with the lift uniquely determined by the choice of a single point in the fiber.
