This theorem gives a precise algebraic criterion for lifting paths through a covering: a closed path $p$ in the base $M$ (based at $P_0$) lifts to a closed path $\tilde{p}$ in the covering space $\tilde{M}$ (based at $\tilde{P}_0$) if and only if the homotopy class $\overline{p}$ belongs to the associated subgroup $\pi_0 = g_0^*(\pi(\tilde{M}, \tilde{P}_0))$. This connects the geometric lifting property to the algebraic structure of fundamental groups, and is the key to the classification of covering spaces by subgroups of the fundamental group.
