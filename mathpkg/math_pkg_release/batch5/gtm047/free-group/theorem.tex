Let $A$ be an alphabet. A word is a finite sequence $w = a_{j_1}^{\alpha_1}, a_{j_2}^{\alpha_2}, \ldots, a_{j_m}^{\alpha_m}$ ($\alpha_i \in \mathbb{Z}$). Two words are equivalent if one can be obtained from the other by inserting/deleting $a^0$ or replacing $a^\alpha, a^\beta$ by $a^{\alpha+\beta}$. The set $F(A)$ of equivalence classes with concatenation multiplication forms a group, called the free group with alphabet $A$. If $A$ has $n$ elements, $F(A)$ is a free group on $n$ generators.
