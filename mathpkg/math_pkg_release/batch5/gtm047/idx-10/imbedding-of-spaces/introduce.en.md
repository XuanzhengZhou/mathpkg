An imbedding (or topological embedding) of one topological space into another is a homeomorphism from the first space onto a subspace of the second. This concept captures the idea that the first space can be placed inside the second in a way that preserves all topological properties. If such a homeomorphism exists, we say the first space is imbeddable in the second. This definition is used in the context of geometric topology to discuss whether certain manifolds (such as the 2-sphere) can be realized as subspaces of Euclidean space.
