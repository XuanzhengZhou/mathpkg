This theorem generalizes Theorem 3 by replacing the target space $\mathbb{R}^2$ with an arbitrary combinatorial $2$-manifold $K_2$. It states that any homeomorphism from a combinatorial $2$-manifold with boundary into another combinatorial $2$-manifold can be approximated arbitrarily closely by a piecewise linear homeomorphism, where the approximation tolerance is again controlled by a strongly positive function $\phi$. The proof is described as a fairly immediate generalization: Theorem 2 (which provides PL approximations for mappings into $\mathbb{R}^2$) still holds when $\mathbb{R}^2$ is replaced by an arbitrary combinatorial $2$-manifold, because within each stellar neighborhood of $K_2$ one can work locally as in the Euclidean plane.
