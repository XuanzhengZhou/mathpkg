First we observe that Theorem 2 still holds when $\mathbb{R}^2$ is replaced by an arbitrary combinatorial $2$-manifold $K_2$. The reason is that in $K_2$, each complex star $\operatorname{St}(v)$ (where $v$ is a vertex of $K_2$) is a combinatorial $2$-cell, and within each such star the situation is essentially the same as in $\mathbb{R}^2$. Using a partition of unity or local coordinate charts adapted to the simplicial structure, the PL approximation constructed locally in each star can be pieced together to yield a global PL homeomorphism. The rest of the proof then follows the same pattern as Theorem 3: one first obtains a PL approximation on the $1$-skeleton, then extends to $2$-simplices using the combinatorial Schönflies theorem, and verifies that the resulting map is a homeomorphism by checking that interiors of images of distinct $2$-simplices are disjoint.
