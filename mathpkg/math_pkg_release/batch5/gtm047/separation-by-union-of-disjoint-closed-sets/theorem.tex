**Theorem 1.** Let $X$ be a simply connected and locally connected topological space in which every connected open set is pathwise connected. Let $H, K, C,$ and $D$ be disjoint closed sets, and suppose that both $H$ and $K$ are connected. If $C \cup D$ separates $H$ from $K$ (in $X$), then either $C$ or $D$ separates $H$ from $K$.
