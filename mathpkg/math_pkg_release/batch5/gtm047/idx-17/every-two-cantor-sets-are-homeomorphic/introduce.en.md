Theorem 8 of Chapter 12 in Moise's "Geometric Topology in Dimensions 2 and 3" states that any two Cantor sets are homeomorphic. This is a fundamental topological classification result: there is essentially only one Cantor set up to homeomorphism. The proof constructs a homeomorphism using sequences of finite open coverings and the machinery of the preceding Theorem 7, which provides conditions under which a limit of bijections between coverings yields a homeomorphism.
