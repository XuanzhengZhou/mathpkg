(1) $h(B)$ and $p^1(N)$ are additive over the components of $N$. Therefore we may suppose, with no loss of generality, that $L$, $N$, and $B$ are connected.

(2) Suppose that $L$ is acyclic. By induction, based on Theorem 11, it follows that $N$ is a 3-cell. Then $B$ is a 2-sphere, so $h(B) = 0$, and $p^1(N) = 0$ because $N$ is contractible.

(3) In the general case, $L$ is a connected graph with $p^1(L) = p^1(N)$ edges not belonging to a maximal tree. Each such edge contributes one handle to $B$, giving $h(B) = p^1(N)$.
