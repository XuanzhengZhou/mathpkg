This theorem establishes a fundamental identity in the topology of orientable 3-manifolds. For a subcomplex $L$ of dimension at most 1 in an orientable triangulated 3-manifold $K$, the total number of handles $h(B)$ of the 2-manifold boundary $B = \operatorname{Bd} N(L)$ of the regular neighborhood equals the first Betti number $p^1(N)$ of that regular neighborhood. The proof uses additivity over components and the fact that when $L$ is acyclic, its regular neighborhood $N$ is a combinatorial 3-cell (by Theorem 11), so both sides vanish.
