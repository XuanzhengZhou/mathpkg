This theorem establishes the "doubling" operation for 3-manifolds with boundary. Given two homeomorphic copies $M_1, M_2$ of a 3-manifold $M$ with boundary, the identification space obtained by gluing along corresponding boundary points via the homeomorphisms $h_1, h_2$ is again a 3-manifold (without boundary). The key to the proof is constructing Cartesian neighborhoods along the glued boundary using the combinatorial structure provided by Theorems 2 and 4.
