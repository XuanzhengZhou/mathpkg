To get a Cartesian neighborhood $U$ of a point $f_1(P) = f_2(P)$ in $M'$, we take $g_i: \sigma_i^3 \leftrightarrow C_i^3 \subset f_i(M)$, $\sigma_i^2 \leftrightarrow d$ ($i = 1, 2$), as in Theorems 2 and 4, such that $d$ is a 2-cell neighborhood of $f_1(P) = f_2(P)$ in $\operatorname{Bd} M_1 = \operatorname{Bd} M_2$. Let

$$U = g_1 \left[ I \left( \sigma_1^3 \right) \right] \cup \operatorname{Int} d \cup g_2 \left[ I \left( \sigma_2^3 \right) \right].$$

Then $U$ is a neighborhood of $f_1(P) = f_2(P)$ in $M'$, and $U \approx \mathbf{R}^3$.
