For a triangulated 3-manifold $K$ with boundary, the boundary complex $\partial K$ consists of those 2-simplexes (and their faces) that lie in exactly one 3-simplex of $K$. The interior $I(K)$ is then defined as the set-theoretic difference $|K| - |\partial K|$, i.e., the complement of the geometric realization of the boundary complex within the geometric realization of $K$. It will later be shown that $|\partial K|$ coincides with the topological boundary $\operatorname{Bd}|K|$ and $I(K)$ with the topological interior $\operatorname{Int}|K|$.
