A combinatorial 3-manifold is a triangulated 3-manifold $K$ with the property that for each vertex $v$, the star $\operatorname{St} v$ is combinatorially equivalent (i.e., has an isomorphic subdivision) to a standard 3-simplex. This local condition ensures the triangulation is "nice" at every vertex. The same definition applies to triangulated 3-manifolds with boundary. Theorem 1 below shows that every triangulated 3-manifold automatically satisfies this condition, making the class of combinatorial 3-manifolds coincide with all triangulated 3-manifolds.
