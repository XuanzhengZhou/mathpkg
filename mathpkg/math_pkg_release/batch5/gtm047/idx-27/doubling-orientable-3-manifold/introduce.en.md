This theorem states that the doubling construction preserves orientability. If $K$ is an orientable triangulated 3-manifold with boundary, then the closed 3-manifold $K'$ obtained by gluing two copies of $K$ along their boundaries (via Theorem 5) is also orientable. The note following the theorem observes that while every 3-manifold with boundary can be topologically doubled, not every triangulation of such a space admits this construction — though the barycentric subdivision $bK$ always can.
