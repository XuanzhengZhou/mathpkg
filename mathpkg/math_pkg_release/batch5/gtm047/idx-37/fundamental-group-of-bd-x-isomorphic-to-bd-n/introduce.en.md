This lemma establishes that the fundamental groups of the boundaries $\operatorname{Bd} X$ and $\operatorname{Bd} N$ are isomorphic. The proof uses the product structure $\phi: \operatorname{Bd} N \times (0,1) \leftrightarrow \operatorname{Int} N' - K'$ (which exists because $K'$ is a 1-dimensional polyhedron) to show that any PL path in the complement of $K'$ can be deformed onto $\operatorname{Bd} X$.
