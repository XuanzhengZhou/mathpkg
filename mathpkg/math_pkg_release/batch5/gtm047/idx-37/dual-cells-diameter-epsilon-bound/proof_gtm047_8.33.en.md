First choose $N$ as a sufficiently small regular neighborhood of $K$ so that $\delta C_v' < \varepsilon/4$ for each $v$. This can be done, because if $N$ is a sufficiently small neighborhood of $K$, then the sets $C_v' = h(C_v)$ will lie in any preassigned neighborhoods of the sets $h(|\operatorname{St} v|)$. Since $K$ is compact, $h|K$ is uniformly continuous, and a suitable subdivision makes the diameters $\delta|\operatorname{St} v|$ as small as desired, guaranteeing $\delta h(|\operatorname{St} v|) < \varepsilon/4$.

For each splitting disk $D = D_e$ of $N$, choose $E = E_e$ as in Theorems 32.1--32.3. The sets $E$ decompose $N' = h(N)$ into a finite collection of sets $C_v''$, each containing exactly one vertex $v'$ of $K' = h(K)$. By (8) of Theorem 32.3, the sets $C_v''$ can be chosen so as to lie in arbitrarily small neighborhoods of the sets $C_v'$. Therefore $\delta C_v'' < \varepsilon/4$ can be achieved.
