This lemma is a crucial step in proving that $\operatorname{Int} N'$ contains no locally tame disks (LTDs). It states that any polyhedral disk $\Delta$ satisfying certain intersection conditions with the pseudocells must intersect the image $K'$ of the polyhedron. The proof relies on the hypothesis that $K$ has no endpoints and uses a path-lifting argument to derive a contradiction if $\Delta$ were disjoint from $K'$.
