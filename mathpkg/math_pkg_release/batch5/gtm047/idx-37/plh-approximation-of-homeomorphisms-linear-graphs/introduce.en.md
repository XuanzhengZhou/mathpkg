This theorem provides a foundational approximation result in geometric topology: any homeomorphism defined on a neighborhood of a 1-dimensional polyhedron (a linear graph) without endpoints in $\mathbf{R}^3$ can be $\varepsilon$-approximated by a piecewise-linear (PL) homeomorphism on a suitably chosen regular neighborhood. The result is a key step in building PL approximations of homeomorphisms in 3-manifolds. Although stated for $\mathbf{R}^3$, it generalizes to triangulated 3-manifolds by working within the star of a vertex.
