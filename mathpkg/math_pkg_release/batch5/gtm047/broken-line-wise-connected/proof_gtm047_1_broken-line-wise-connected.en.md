Let $P \in U$, and let $V$ the union of $\{P\}$ and the set of all points of $U$ that can be joined to $P$ by broken lines lying in $U$. It is then easy to show that both $U$ and $U - V$ are open. If $U - V \neq \emptyset$, then $U$ is the union of two disjoint nonempty open sets, which is false.
