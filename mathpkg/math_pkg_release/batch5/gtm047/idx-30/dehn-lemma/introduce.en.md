The Dehn Lemma is a fundamental result in 3-manifold topology, originally formulated by Max Dehn in 1910. It states that if a PL singular 2-cell has no singularities on its boundary (i.e., it is a homeomorphism near the boundary and the interior does not touch the boundary image), then its boundary curve bounds a non-singular polyhedral 2-cell. Dehn's original proof was later found to be erroneous, and the lemma remained a famous open problem until it was proved by Papakyriakopoulos in 1957. The Dehn Lemma is a straightforward consequence of the Loop Theorem.
