Let $D: \Delta \to M$ be a PL singular $2$-cell in a triangulated $3$-manifold $M = |K|$. Suppose that there is an open set $U$ in $\Delta$, containing $\operatorname{Bd} \Delta$, such that

\begin{enumerate}
  \item $D|U$ is a homeomorphism, and
  \item $D(U) \cap D(\Delta - U) = \varnothing$.
\end{enumerate}

Thus $D$ has no singularities on its boundary, and $J = D(\operatorname{Bd} \Delta)$ is a polygon. Then $D(\operatorname{Bd} \Delta)$ is the boundary of a polyhedral $2$-cell.
