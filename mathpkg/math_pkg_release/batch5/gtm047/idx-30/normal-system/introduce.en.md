A normal system is a sextuple $[M, K, D, K(\Delta), B, N]$ that packages the combinatorial data needed for the inductive proof of the Loop Theorem. It consists of a triangulated 3-manifold $M$ with boundary, a PL singular 2-cell $D$ whose boundary $L$ lies in $\operatorname{Bd} M$, a subcomplex $B \subset \operatorname{Bd} M$ forming a neighborhood of $|L|$, and a normal subgroup $N$ of $\pi(B, P_0)$ disjoint from the homotopy classes of the loops in $L$. The concept was introduced as a technical device to control the complexity of singular 2-cells when lifting to covering spaces during the proof.
