A sextuple $[M, K, D, K(\Delta), B, N]$ is called a \textbf{normal system} if the following conditions hold:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item $M = |K|$ is a finite triangulated $3$-manifold with boundary; $D: \Delta \to M$ is a PL singular $2$-cell with $\operatorname{Bd} D = L$; $D$ is simplicial relative to $K$ and a subdivision $K(\Delta)$ of $\Delta$; $M$ is a regular neighborhood of $|D|$ in a triangulated $3$-manifold with boundary, so that the inclusion $i: |D| \to M$ induces a surjective isomorphism $\pi(|D|) \leftrightarrow \pi(M)$; and $|D| \cap \operatorname{Bd} M = |L|$.

  \item $B$ is a subcomplex of $K$, and is a neighborhood of $|L|$ in $\operatorname{Bd} M$.

  \item $N$ is a normal subgroup of $\pi(B, P_0)$ for some basepoint $P_0 \in |L|$.

  \item $L(B) \cap N = \varnothing$, where $L(B)$ denotes the set of homotopy classes of the loops $L$ in $B$.
\end{enumerate}
