This theorem is the 3-dimensional analog of the Jordan Curve Theorem: every 2-sphere embedded in $\mathbf{R}^3$ (whether polyhedral or merely topological) separates $\mathbf{R}^3$ into two components. This is a special case of the Jordan-Brouwer Separation Theorem and is fundamental for the study of 3-manifolds.
