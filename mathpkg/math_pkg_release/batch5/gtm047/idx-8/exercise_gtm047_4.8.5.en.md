Let $M = |K|$, as in Problems 3 and 4, and suppose that $M$ is compact. (It follows that $K$ is finite; see Problem 0.34.) Let $N$ be a compact subset of $M$. Then there is a subset $M'$ of $M$ such that (1) $M'$ is a tree, (2) $N \subset M'$, and (3) no proper subset of $M'$ has properties (1) and (2).
