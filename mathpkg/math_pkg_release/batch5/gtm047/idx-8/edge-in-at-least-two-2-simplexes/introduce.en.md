This lemma strengthens the previous result: in a 2-manifold without boundary, every edge belongs to at least two 2-simplexes. If an edge belonged to only one 2-simplex, that simplex would contain a semicircle separating a neighborhood of an interior point of the edge, which contradicts the fact that no arc separates $\mathbf{R}^2$.
