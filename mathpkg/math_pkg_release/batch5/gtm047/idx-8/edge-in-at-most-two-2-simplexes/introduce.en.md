This lemma establishes that in a 2-manifold, an edge cannot belong to three or more 2-simplexes. If an edge were the intersection of three distinct 2-simplexes, one could construct a 1-sphere in a planar neighborhood that would separate it, contradicting the Jordan Curve Theorem. Together with Lemma 3, this implies that every interior edge of a triangulated 2-manifold without boundary lies in exactly two 2-simplexes.
