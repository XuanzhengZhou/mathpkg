Suppose that $e$ is the intersection of three distinct simplexes, $e = \sigma_1^2 \cap \sigma_2^2 \cap \sigma_3^2$, and let $v$ be an interior point of $e$. Let $U$ be a neighborhood of $v$ in $K$, homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^2$, and let $\varepsilon > 0$ be sufficiently small so that $N(v, \varepsilon) \cap |K| \subset U$. Let $C_1$ and $C_2$ be semicircles in $\sigma_1^2$ and $\sigma_2^2$, with $v$ as center, and the same radius, sufficiently small so as to lie in $N(v, \varepsilon)$. Then $J = C_1 \cup C_2$ is a 1-sphere in $U$, not separating $U$; and this contradicts the Jordan curve theorem.
