Let $e$ be an edge of $K$. As in the proof of the preceding theorem, we show that the number of 2-simplexes of $K$ that contain $e$ is either 1 or 2. It follows that every component of every set $|L(v)|$ is either a broken line or a 1-sphere. As before, $|L(v)|$ is connected, because $v$ does not separate $|\operatorname{St} v|$. Therefore $|L(v)|$ is a broken line or a 1-sphere, and $\operatorname{St} v$ is a combinatorial 2-cell, by much the same proof as before.

Let $\partial K$ be the set of all edges of $K$ that lie in only one 2-simplex of $K$. Thus Theorem 9 asserts that $\operatorname{Bd}|K| = |\partial K|$.
