(1) Since $\operatorname{Fr} M$ is closed, every point of $M - \operatorname{Fr} M$ has a locally Euclidean open neighborhood in $M$. Thus $M - \operatorname{Fr} M \subset \operatorname{Int} M = M - \operatorname{Bd} M$, and $\operatorname{Bd} M \subset \operatorname{Fr} M$.

(2) Since $M$ is closed, $\operatorname{Fr} M \subset M$. If $P \in \operatorname{Fr} M$, then $P$ does not have a locally Euclidean open neighborhood in $M$, since this would contradict Invariance of domain (Theorem 0.4). Therefore $\operatorname{Fr} M \subset \operatorname{Bd} M$, and the theorem follows.
