The Loop theorem, proved by Christos Papakyriakopoulos, is one of the foundational results in 3-manifold topology. It states that if a two-sided surface $M^2$ embedded in a 3-manifold $M^3$ carries an essential loop that becomes null-homotopic in $M^3$, then that loop bounds an embedded disk whose interior lies off the surface. The theorem is a key tool for analyzing incompressible surfaces and the structure of 3-manifolds.
