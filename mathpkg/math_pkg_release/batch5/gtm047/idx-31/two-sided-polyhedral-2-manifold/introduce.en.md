A polyhedral 2-manifold $M^2$ embedded in the interior of a triangulated 3-manifold $M^3$ is said to be two-sided if it locally separates $M^3$. More precisely, $M^2$ separates every sufficiently small connected neighborhood of $M^2$ into two components. This property is fundamental for the Loop theorem and for the study of surfaces in 3-manifolds. For disconnected $M^2$, the condition is applied componentwise.
