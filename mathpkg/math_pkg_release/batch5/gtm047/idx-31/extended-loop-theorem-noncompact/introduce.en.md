This theorem extends Papakyriakopoulos's Loop theorem to the case where the embedded 2-manifold $M^2$ is only assumed to be closed (rather than compact) in $M^3$. The key observation is that the bicollar neighborhood construction in Theorem 2 works via a locally finite process even without compactness, and the set $|D| \cap M^2$ has a compact polyhedral neighborhood within $M^2$ because $|D|$ is compact. The proof then reduces to the compact case.
