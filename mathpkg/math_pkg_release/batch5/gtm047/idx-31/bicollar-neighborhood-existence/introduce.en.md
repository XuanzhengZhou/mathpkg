This theorem constructs a piecewise-linear homeomorphism $\rho$ from the product $B \times [0,1]$ onto a neighborhood $W$ of the compact boundary $B$ of a triangulated 3-manifold $M^3$. The mapping provides a bicollar (or product) neighborhood, so that $B$ sits as the "bottom" face $\rho(B,0) = B$ inside $M^3$. The proof proceeds inductively by decomposing $B$ into polyhedral 2-cells and extending the PLH cell by cell, using the existence of polyhedral 3-cells adjacent to the boundary.
