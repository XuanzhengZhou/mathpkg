Let $P$ be a point in the unbounded component $E$ of $\mathbf{R}^3 - M^2$, and let $B$ be a broken line $PQ$ such that $B \cap M^2$ is a point which lies in the interior of an edge of $B$ and in the interior of a 2-simplex of $M^2$. We shall show that $Q$ lies in a bounded component $I$ of $\mathbf{R}^3 - M^2$.

As in the proof of Theorem 3, we see that for each regular neighborhood $N$ of $M^2$, $N - M^2$ is the union of at most two connected sets, and therefore of exactly two. From this it follows that $M^2 = \text{Fr } E = \text{Fr } I$, and that there is no third component of $\mathbf{R}^3 - M^2$.
