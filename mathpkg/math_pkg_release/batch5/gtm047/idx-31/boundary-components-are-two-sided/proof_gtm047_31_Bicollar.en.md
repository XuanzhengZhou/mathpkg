We need to show that every component $B$ of $M^2$ is two sided. Let $W$ be any connected neighborhood of $B$ which intersects no other component of $\text{Bd } N$. Then

$$W - B = W - \text{Bd } N = (W \cap \text{Int } N) \cup (W - N),$$

where the two sets on the right are nonempty and separated. Thus $W - B$ is disconnected, verifying that $B$ separates every sufficiently small connected neighborhood, and therefore $B$ (and hence $M^2$) is two sided.
