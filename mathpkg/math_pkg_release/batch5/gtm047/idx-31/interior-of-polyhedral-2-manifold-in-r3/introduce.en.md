For a compact connected polyhedral 2-manifold $M^2$ in $\mathbf{R}^3$, the complement has exactly two components by Theorem 6. The bounded component is called the interior of $M^2$ in $\mathbf{R}^3$, and the unbounded component is called the exterior. This definition parallels the classical definition of the interior and exterior of a polygon in $\mathbf{R}^2$ via the Jordan curve theorem.
