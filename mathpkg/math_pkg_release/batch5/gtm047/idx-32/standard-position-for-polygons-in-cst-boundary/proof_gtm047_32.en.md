First move $J_1$ into standard position, as in the preceding proof, by a PLH $h_1$. Then move $h_1(J_2)$ into standard position, by a PLH $h_2$, chosen so that $h_2 h_1(J_1)$ is in standard position. (Note that $h_1(J_1)$ may bound a 2-cell in $T - J_x$, and this 2-cell may be moved across $J_x$ by $h_2$. If $J_1$ does not bound a 2-cell in $T$, then $h_2$ can be chosen so that $h_2|_{h_1(J_1)}$ is the identity.) In $n$ such steps, we get the desired $h = h_n h_{n-1} \dots h_2 h_1$.
