This theorem establishes that the polyhedral and combinatorial definitions of solid torus coincide in $\mathbb{R}^3$. A polyhedral solid torus is defined via a decomposition into four 3-cells arranged cyclically, while a combinatorial solid torus is defined as the image of a product $\sigma^2 \times [0,1]$ under a PL identification mapping identifying the two ends. The proof uses the rectilinear triangulation theorem, simple connectivity of the components, the Loop theorem, and properties of bicollar neighborhoods to construct the required PL identification mapping $\phi$.
