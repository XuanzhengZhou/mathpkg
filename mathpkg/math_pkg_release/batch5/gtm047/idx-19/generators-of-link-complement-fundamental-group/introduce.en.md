This theorem establishes the generator half of the Wirtinger presentation for the fundamental group of a link complement in $\mathbf{R}^3$. For a link $L$ with $n$ arcs in its regular planar diagram, one constructs $n$ geometric triangular paths $g_i$, each looping around a single arc with a right-handed crossing. The theorem asserts that the homotopy classes of these $n$ paths generate the entire fundamental group $\pi(\mathbf{R}^3 - L, P_0)$, so that every closed path is homotopic to a finite product of these generators raised to powers $\pm 1$. This reduces the computation of the link group to the combinatorial data of the link diagram.
