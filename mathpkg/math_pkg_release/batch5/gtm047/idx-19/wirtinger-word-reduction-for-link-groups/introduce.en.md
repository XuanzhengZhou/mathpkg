This theorem completes the Wirtinger presentation of the fundamental group of a link complement by characterizing when a generator word represents the trivial element. For each crossing in the link diagram, one associates a crossing word $r_i$ of the form $g_i g_k g_j^{-1} g_k^{-1}$ or $g_i g_k^{-1} g_j^{-1} g_k$, which is geometrically null-homotopic. The theorem states that any generator word representing the identity can be reduced to the empty word by repeatedly inserting or deleting trivial pairs $g_i g_i^{-1}$ or conjugates of the crossing words $g_i r_j^{\pm 1} g_i^{-1}$. This gives a complete set of relations $R = \{r_i\}$ for the link group.
