Let $N$ be a strip neighborhood of $J$, formed by small convex polyhedral neighborhoods of the edges and vertices of $J$.

**Lemma 1.** $\mathbb{R}^2 - J$ has at most two components.
**Lemma 2.** $\mathbb{R}^2 - J$ has at least two components.

The bounded component $I$ of $\mathbb{R}^2 - J$ is called the interior of $J$, and the unbounded component $E$ is called the exterior.
