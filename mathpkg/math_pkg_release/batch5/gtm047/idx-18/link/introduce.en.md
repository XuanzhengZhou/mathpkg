A link generalizes a knot: it is a finite collection of pairwise disjoint polygonal simple closed curves in $\mathbb{R}^3$. Topologically, a link is a compact polyhedral $1$-manifold (without boundary) embedded in $\mathbb{R}^3$. Links are the fundamental objects of study in classical knot theory, and the complement $\mathbb{R}^3 \setminus L$ carries rich topological information encoded in its fundamental group.
