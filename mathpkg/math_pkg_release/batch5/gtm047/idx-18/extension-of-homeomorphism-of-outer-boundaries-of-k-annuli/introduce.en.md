Theorem 1 states that a homeomorphism between the outer boundaries of two $k$-annuli in the plane can be extended to a homeomorphism of the entire annuli, the whole plane $\mathbb{R}^2$, and all boundary components. The proof proceeds by induction on $k$: first reducing to the case where the annuli are disjoint (via translation), then decomposing each annulus into $2$-cells using broken lines, and extending the homeomorphism through a sequence of intermediate maps. The induction step shows that if the theorem holds for $k$-annuli then it holds for $(k+1)$-annuli.
