In Moise's treatment, a knot is defined as a polygonal simple closed curve in $\mathbb{R}^3$. This piecewise linear definition avoids the topological pathologies that can arise with wild embeddings and is well-suited to the combinatorial and PL methods used throughout the book. Every tame knot (in the usual topological sense) is equivalent to such a polygonal knot.
