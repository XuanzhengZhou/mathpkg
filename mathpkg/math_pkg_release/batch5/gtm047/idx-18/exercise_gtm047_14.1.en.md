In $\mathbb{R}^2$, let $A$ be the closure of the graph of $f(x) = \sin(1/x)$ ($0 < x \leq 1/\pi$). Let $B$ be an arc in $\mathbb{R}^2$ from $(0, -1)$ to $(1/\pi, 0)$, such that $A \cap \operatorname{Int} B = \varnothing$. Let $M = A \cup B$. Then $M$ is pathwise connected and simply connected. (This is typical of various cases in which the fundamental group gives "wrong answers.")
