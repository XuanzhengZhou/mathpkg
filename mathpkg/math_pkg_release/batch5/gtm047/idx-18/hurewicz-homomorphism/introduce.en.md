The Hurewicz homomorphism $h: \pi(|K|, P_0) \to H_1(K)$ maps each based homotopy class of a loop to its corresponding $1$-dimensional homology class. A simplicial representative of the loop is decomposed into oriented $1$-simplexes, summing contributions to form a $1$-cycle. The kernel of $h$ is exactly the commutator subgroup, so the abelianization of the fundamental group is isomorphic to the first homology group with integer coefficients. Problems 4-13 at the end of the section outline a proof of this theorem.
