Given a link $L$, choose the coordinate axes so that: (i) if $v$ is a vertex of $L$, the vertical line through $v$ contains no other point of $L$; (ii) no three points of $L$ lie on the same vertical line. (This is a general position condition, satisfied by almost all directions for the $z$-axis.)

Under this condition, the orthogonal projection of $L$ onto the $xy$-plane $\mathbb{R}^2$ is called the diagram of $L$.
