Path multiplication (or concatenation) is the operation that runs two loops end-to-end: first traverse $p$ at double speed on $[0, \frac{1}{2}]$, then traverse $q$ at double speed on $[\frac{1}{2}, 1]$. This operation is associative only up to homotopy (not strictly), which is why one passes to equivalence classes to obtain a group structure.
