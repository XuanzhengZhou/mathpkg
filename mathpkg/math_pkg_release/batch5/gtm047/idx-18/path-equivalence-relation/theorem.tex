Let $p, q \in \operatorname{CP}(X, P_0)$ and let $D = [0,1]^2$ be the unit square. Suppose there exists a continuous mapping

$$f: D \to X$$

such that

$$f(t, 0) = p(t), \quad f(t, 1) = q(t),$$

$$f(0, y) = f(1, y) = P_0 \quad \text{for every } y \in [0, 1].$$

Then $p$ and $q$ are called equivalent, written

$$p \cong q.$$

This relation is stronger than ordinary homotopy: in the definition of homotopy we do not require that the vertical edges of the mapping cylinder be mapped onto the base point.
