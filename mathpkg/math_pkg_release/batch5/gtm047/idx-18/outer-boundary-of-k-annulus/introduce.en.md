When a $k$-annulus $A$ is embedded in $\mathbb{R}^2$, its boundary $\operatorname{Bd} A$ decomposes into $k+1$ disjoint simple closed curves. The notation $J_0$ is reserved for the outermost component -- the one that serves as the frontier of the unbounded component of the complement $\mathbb{R}^2 \setminus A$. Throughout Section 13, this convention is maintained consistently.
