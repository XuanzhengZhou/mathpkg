Theorem 4 shows that the algebraic structure of $\pi(X, P_0)$ is independent of the choice of base point: any path $p$ from $P_0$ to $P_1$ induces an isomorphism $p^*$ by conjugation. Thus for a pathwise connected space, one often writes simply $\pi(X)$ when the base point is understood or irrelevant.
