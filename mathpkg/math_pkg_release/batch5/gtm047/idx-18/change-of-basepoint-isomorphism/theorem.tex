Let $P_0$ and $P_1$ be points of $X$, and let $p$ be a path from $P_0$ to $P_1$. Then $p$ induces an isomorphism

$$p^*: \pi(X, P_0) \xrightarrow{\cong} \pi(X, P_1),$$

such that for each $\bar{q} \in \pi(X, P_0)$ we have

$$p^*(\bar{q}) = \overline{p^{-1}qp}.$$

Here $p^{-1}$ is the path $t \mapsto p(1-t)$, and the indicated multiplication is end-to-end concatenation.
