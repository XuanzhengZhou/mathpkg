**Proof.** The proof is the same as that of Theorem 10.9. The essential argument is that if $Q$ and $S$ were in different components of $C^2 - (M_1 \cup M_2)$, then there would exist a separation of $C^2$ contradicting the fact that a $2$-cell is connected and cannot be separated by two disjoint closed sets that each only intersect the interior of one boundary arc without touching the endpoints.
