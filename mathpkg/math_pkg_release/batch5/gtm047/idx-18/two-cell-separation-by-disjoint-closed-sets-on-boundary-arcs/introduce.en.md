Theorem 3 concerns the connectivity inside a $2$-cell whose boundary is split into two arcs $B_1$ and $B_2$ sharing endpoints $Q$ and $S$. If disjoint closed sets $M_1$ and $M_2$ intersect the boundary only inside the interiors of $B_1$ and $B_2$ respectively, then the endpoints $Q$ and $S$ remain in the same connected component after removing $M_1 \cup M_2$. The proof is analogous to that of Theorem 10.9, using the fact that a $2$-cell cannot be separated by two disjoint closed sets each touching only one boundary arc.
