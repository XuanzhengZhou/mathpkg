A topological space is homogeneous if its homeomorphism group acts transitively: for any two points $P$ and $Q$, there exists a homeomorphism of the space sending $P$ to $Q$. Equivalently, every trivial homeomorphism of singletons $\{P\} \leftrightarrow \{Q\}$ can be extended to a homeomorphism of the whole space. Homogeneity is a strong symmetry property; for example, $\mathbf{R}^2$ is homogeneous with respect to Cantor sets.
