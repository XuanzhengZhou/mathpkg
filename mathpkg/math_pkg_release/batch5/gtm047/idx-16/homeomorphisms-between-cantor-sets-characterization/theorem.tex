Let $C$ be a Cantor set, and let $C'$ be a compact metrizable space. Let $G_1, G_2, \ldots$ be a sequence of finite coverings of $C$ by disjoint nonempty open (and therefore closed) sets, such that (1) $G_{i+1} \leqslant G_i$ for each $i$ (i.e., $G_{i+1}$ refines $G_i$) and (2) $\|G_i\| \to 0$ as $i \to \infty$. Let $G'_1, G'_2, \ldots$ be a sequence of finite coverings of $C'$ by nonempty open sets, satisfying (1) and (2). For each $i$, let $\phi_i: G_i \to G'_i$ be a bijection such that whenever $g \in G_{i+1}$ and $g \subset h \in G_i$, then $\phi_{i+1}(g) \subset \phi_i(h)$. Then there is a unique homeomorphism $f: C \leftrightarrow C'$ such that for each $g$ in each $G_i$, $f(g) \subset \phi_i(g)$.
