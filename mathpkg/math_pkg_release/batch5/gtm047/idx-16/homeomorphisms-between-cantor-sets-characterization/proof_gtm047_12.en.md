For each $P \in C$ and each $i$, there is a unique $g_{iP} \in G_i$ containing $P$. The sets $\phi_i(g_{iP})$ form a descending sequence of closed sets in $C'$, and since $\|G'_i\| \to 0$, their diameters tend to zero. Since $C'$ is compact and metrizable, $\bigcap_{i=1}^{\infty} \phi_i(g_{iP})$ is a single point. Define $f(P)$ to be this point. The mapping $f: C \to C'$ so defined is bijective (by symmetry of the construction) and continuous (by the refinement condition). By Theorem 6, $f$ is a homeomorphism. Uniqueness follows from the fact that the coverings determine the topology.
