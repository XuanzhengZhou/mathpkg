A Cantor set is a compact, metrizable, totally disconnected space with no isolated points (every point is a limit point). The prototype is the classical middle-third Cantor set in $\mathbf{R}$. Cantor sets play a central role in geometric topology, particularly in the study of homeomorphism extension problems in Euclidean spaces.
