Let $M$ be a closed set in a metrizable space $[X, \emptyset]$, and let $A$ and $B$ be disjoint closed sets in $X$. If $M$ is the union of two disjoint closed sets, containing $M \cap A$ and $M \cap B$ respectively, then $A$ and $B$ are separable in $M$. If not, $A$ and $B$ are inseparable in $M$. (In the latter case, it follows trivially that both $A$ and $B$ intersect $M$.)
