Theorem 6 states that a continuous bijection from a compact metrizable space to another metrizable space is automatically a homeomorphism (i.e., its inverse is also continuous). This is a fundamental result in point-set topology: compactness ensures that the inverse image of a closed set under $f^{-1}$ is closed, since $f$ maps closed subsets of a compact space to compact (hence closed) subsets. The proof given uses a sequential argument in metric spaces.
