J. W. Alexander's theorem states that any homeomorphism of the closed unit ball $\mathbf{B}^n$ in $\mathbf{R}^n$ that fixes the boundary sphere $\mathbf{S}^{n-1}$ pointwise is isotopic to the identity. The proof constructs an explicit isotopy $\phi$ that pushes the homeomorphism inward, interpolating between the given homeomorphism $f_1$ and the identity map. This is a foundational result in geometric topology.
