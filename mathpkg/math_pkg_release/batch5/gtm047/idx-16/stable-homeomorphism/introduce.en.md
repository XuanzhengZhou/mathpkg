A stable homeomorphism of a topological space $X$ is a homeomorphism $f: X \to X$ that restricts to the identity on some nonempty open set $U \subset X$. On an orientable manifold, stable homeomorphisms necessarily preserve orientation. Stability is a useful intermediate notion for proving that certain homeomorphisms are isotopic to the identity.
