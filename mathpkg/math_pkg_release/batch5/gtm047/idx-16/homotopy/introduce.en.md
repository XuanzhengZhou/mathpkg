A homotopy is a continuous deformation of one mapping into another. Given two mappings $f_0, f_1: A \to B$, a homotopy $\phi: A \times [0, 1] \to B$ interpolates between them, with $\phi(\cdot, 0) = f_0$ and $\phi(\cdot, 1) = f_1$. This is one of the most fundamental concepts in topology, providing the basis for classifying maps up to continuous deformation.
