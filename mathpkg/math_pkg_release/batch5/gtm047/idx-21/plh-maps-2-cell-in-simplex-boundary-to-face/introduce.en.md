Given a 3-simplex $\sigma^3$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with a polyhedral 2-cell $D$ in its boundary, and any open neighborhood $W$ of $\sigma^3$, there exists a piecewise-linear homeomorphism of $\mathbb{R}^3$ that maps $D$ onto a 2-face of $\sigma^3$ while keeping $\sigma^3$ invariant and acting as the identity outside $W$. This technical lemma is a key step in proving that every 3-simplex has the push property.
