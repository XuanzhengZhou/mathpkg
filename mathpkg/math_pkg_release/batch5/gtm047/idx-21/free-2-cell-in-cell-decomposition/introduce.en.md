In a cell-decomposition $K$ of a 2-cell, a 2-cell $C$ is called free if the intersection of its boundary with the boundary of the total space $|K|$ is an arc. This notion is analogous to that of a free 2-simplex in a triangulation, and is essential for inductive reduction arguments in piecewise-linear topology, such as proving that cell decompositions can be simplified by removing free cells.
