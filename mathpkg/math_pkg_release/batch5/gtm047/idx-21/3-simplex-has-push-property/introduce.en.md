Every 3-simplex embedded in $\mathbb{R}^3$ possesses the push property: given a polyhedral 2-cell $D_1$ in its boundary, there is a PLH of $\mathbb{R}^3$ that interchanges $D_1$ with the complementary 2-cell $D_2 = \operatorname{Cl}(\operatorname{Bd} \sigma^3 - D_1)$ while fixing the common boundary $J = \operatorname{Bd} D_1 = \operatorname{Bd} D_2$. The proof uses Theorem 5 to map $D_1$ to a face, then applies a push across that face, and finally pulls back.
