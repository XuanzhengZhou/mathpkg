For a closed 3-manifold $M$ with boundary lying in $\mathbb{R}^3$, the boundary $\operatorname{Bd} M$ and the frontier $\operatorname{Fr} M$ coincide. This result allows us to use the notation $\operatorname{Int} M$ unambiguously for the topological interior $M - \operatorname{Fr} M$ of $M$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$. The proof follows from the definition of manifold interior and the Invariance of Domain theorem.
