If a 2-cell is decomposed into more than one 2-cell by a cell-decomposition $K$, then at least two of the 2-cells in $K$ must be free. This combinatorial fact mirrors the analogous result for simplicial triangulations (Theorem 3.3) and is used in induction arguments that reduce a cell-decomposition step by step via removal of free cells.
