Wilder's arc $A$ is a wild arc in $\mathbb{R}^3$ whose complement $\mathbb{R}^3 - A$ has trivial fundamental group (it is homeomorphic to the complement of a point). This shows that wildness of an arc cannot be detected solely by the fundamental group of its complement. The proof uses the concept of local commutativity of the fundamental group: for a tame arc (whose image under a homeomorphism is a linear interval), the fundamental group of the complement is locally commutative at each point of the arc, and this property is a topological invariant not satisfied by $A$.
