The Fox-Artin arc $A$ is constructed by replicating a knotted configuration and shrinking toward limit points $P$ and $Q$. A cross-section $S$ decomposes $A$ into two subarcs $A_1$ and $A_2$. While $\mathbb{R}^3 - A_2$ is homeomorphic to the complement of a point (by Theorem 2), $A_2$ is nevertheless wild because $\mathbb{R}^3 - A_2$ is not locally simply connected at $Q$. This provides a key example for constructing a wild 2-sphere with simply connected complement.
