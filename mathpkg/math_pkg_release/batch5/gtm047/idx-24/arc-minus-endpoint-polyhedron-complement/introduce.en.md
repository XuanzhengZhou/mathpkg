If an arc $A$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ has the property that removing one endpoint $Q$ leaves an (infinite) polyhedron, then the complement $\mathbb{R}^3 - A$ is homeomorphic to the complement of that endpoint. The proof constructs a homeomorphism via a decomposition into 3-cells and uses the mapping from Theorem 1 that collapses an interval to a point. This result is used to analyze the Fox-Artin wild arc example.
