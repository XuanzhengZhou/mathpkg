Although Wilder's arc $A$ is wild, its complement $\mathbb{R}^3 - A$ is homeomorphic to the complement of a point. This surprising result shows that the total fundamental group of the complement does not detect wildness: $\mathbb{R}^3 - A$ has trivial topology despite $A$ being wild. The proof constructs $\mathbb{R}^3 - A$ as the intersection of a nested sequence of polyhedral 3-cells and applies a result about such intersections.
