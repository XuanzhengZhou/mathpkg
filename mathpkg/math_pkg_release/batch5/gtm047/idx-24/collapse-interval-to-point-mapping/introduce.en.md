This theorem constructs a mapping $f$ on a solid of revolution $D^3$ that collapses the interval $P_0P_1$ to the point $P_0$, while being a homeomorphism on the complement of $P_0P_1$. The construction uses the geometry of the 2-simplex $T$ rotated about the $z$-axis. This mapping is a key technical tool used in Section 20 to show that certain arc complements are homeomorphic to the complement of a point.
