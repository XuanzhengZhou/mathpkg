**Definition.** Let $[X, \emptyset]$ be a pair where $X$ is a nonempty set and $\emptyset$ is a collection of subsets of $X$ called open sets, satisfying:

(O.1) $\varnothing \in \emptyset$.
(O.2) $X \in \emptyset$.
(O.3) $\emptyset$ contains every union of elements of $\emptyset$.
(O.4) $\emptyset$ contains every finite intersection of elements of $\emptyset$.

Then $[X, \emptyset]$ is a topological space.
