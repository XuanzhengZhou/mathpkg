This theorem strengthens the Jordan Curve Theorem by showing that the polygon $J$ is exactly the common boundary between its interior $I$ and exterior $E$. Every point on the polygon is a limit point of both components, meaning that $J$ is contained in the frontier of $I$ and in the frontier of $E$. The proof uses Theorem 4 on the frontier of an open set and a broken-line argument to show that any proper closed subset of $J$ cannot separate the plane.
