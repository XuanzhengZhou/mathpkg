This theorem states that the closed interior of any polygon is a finite polyhedron -- it can be triangulated by a finite simplicial complex. The proof decomposes the plane using the lines containing the edges of $J$, which partition $\mathbf{R}^2$ into finitely many closed convex regions. Each region is then triangulated by joining an interior point to the vertices on its boundary, yielding a global triangulation of the closed interior.
