The Jordan Curve Theorem for polygons states that any simple closed polygonal curve $J$ in the plane separates $\mathbf{R}^2$ into exactly two connected components. This piecewise-linear version of the classic Jordan Curve Theorem is proved using strip neighborhoods and an index argument modulo 2. The two components are the bounded interior and the unbounded exterior of the polygon.
