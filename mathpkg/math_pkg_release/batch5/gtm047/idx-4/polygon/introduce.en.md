A polygon is defined as a polyhedral 1-sphere -- a simple closed curve built from finitely many straight line segments (edges) meeting only at their endpoints (vertices). In plane separation theory, the polygon plays the role of a piecewise-linear Jordan curve, for which the fundamental theorem states that its complement in the plane consists of exactly two connected components.
