This theorem gives an alternative characterization of the frontier (boundary) of an open set: it is simply the closure minus the set itself. By definition, $\operatorname{Fr} U = \overline{U} \cap \overline{X - U}$. When $U$ is open, points in $\overline{U} - U$ are in $X - U$ and hence in its closure, so they belong to the intersection. This elementary result is used in the proof of Theorem 5 to analyze the frontier of the interior of a polygon.
