An \textbf{arc} $A$ is a $1$-cell, that is, the image of a $1$-simplex $[0,1] \subset \mathbf{R}$ under a homeomorphism $f$. The boundary of an arc $A$, denoted $\operatorname{Bd} A$, consists of its two endpoints: if $A = f([0,1])$ with $P = f(0)$ and $Q = f(1)$, then $\operatorname{Bd} A = \{P, Q\}$ and $\operatorname{Int} A = A - \{P, Q\}$.
