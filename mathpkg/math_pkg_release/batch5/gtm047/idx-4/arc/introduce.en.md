An arc is the topological image of the closed unit interval, i.e., a 1-cell. It is a 1-manifold with boundary: the interior points have Euclidean neighborhoods (homeomorphic to open intervals), while the two endpoints form the boundary. Unlike a polygon, an arc is not closed; it is used to construct broken lines and serves as the building block for non-self-intersecting curves.
