The standard $n$-ball $B^n$ is the closed unit ball in $n$-dimensional Euclidean space $\mathbf{R}^n$, centered at the origin $P_0$. It consists of all points whose Euclidean distance from the origin is at most 1. This is the fundamental model for an $n$-dimensional cell and serves as the building block for constructing polyhedra and complexes in geometric topology.
