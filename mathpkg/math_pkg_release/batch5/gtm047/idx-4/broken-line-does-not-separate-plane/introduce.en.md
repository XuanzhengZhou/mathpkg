This theorem establishes a crucial contrast with the Jordan Curve Theorem: while a simple closed polygon separates the plane into two components, a broken line (an unclosed polyhedral arc) does not separate $\mathbf{R}^2$. The proof constructs a strip neighborhood and uses a path-connectedness argument similar to Lemma 1, showing that all points in the complement can be joined.
