The Euler characteristic of a compact connected $2$-manifold classifies surfaces up to the invariant $2h + m$. From the open cell-decomposition with one vertex, $2h + m$ edges (the curves forming handles and cross-caps), and one face, the Euler characteristic is computed as $\chi = V - E + F = 1 - (2h + m) + 1 = 2 - (2h + m)$. This formula unifies the standard values: $\chi(S^2) = 2$ ($h=m=0$), $\chi(T^2) = 0$ ($h=1,m=0$), $\chi(\mathbb{R}P^2) = 1$ ($h=0,m=1$), and $\chi(K) = 0$ ($h=0,m=2$, Klein bottle).
