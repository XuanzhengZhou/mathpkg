The Euler characteristic and the first Betti number of a compact connected surface satisfy a simple linear relationship that depends on orientability. For orientable surfaces, $\chi = 2 - p^1$, while for non-orientable surfaces, $\chi = 1 - p^1$. The difference of $1$ between the two formulas reflects the presence of $\mathbf{Z}_2$ torsion in the non-orientable case. This theorem provides a quick way to compute one invariant from the other and to test orientability.
