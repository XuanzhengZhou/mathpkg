For each edge $e$ of the triangulation $K$, define
$$N'(e) = \operatorname{Cl}\left[ N(e) - N(\operatorname{Bd} e) \right].$$
The sets $N(v)$ for $v \in K^0$ and the sets $N'(e)$ are polyhedral $2$-cells; if two such sets intersect, then their intersection is an arc lying in the boundary of each. For each edge $e = vv' \in K$, the set
$$N(e) = N(v) \cup N'(e) \cup N(v')$$
is a $2$-cell consisting of three $2$-cells "laid end to end."
