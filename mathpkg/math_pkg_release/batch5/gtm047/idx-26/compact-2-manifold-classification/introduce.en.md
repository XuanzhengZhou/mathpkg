Every compact connected $2$-manifold without boundary is homeomorphic to a standard surface: a $2$-sphere with some number $h \geqslant 0$ of handles (orientable genus $h$ surface) and $m \in \{0,1,2\}$ cross-caps (real projective plane or Klein bottle contributions). The classification has two parts: this existence theorem guarantees that every such manifold can be reduced to one of these standard forms; the uniqueness part (Theorem 8) states that $(h,m)$ uniquely characterizes the homeomorphism type. The fact that $m \leqslant 2$ reflects that three cross-caps can be traded for a handle plus one cross-cap.
