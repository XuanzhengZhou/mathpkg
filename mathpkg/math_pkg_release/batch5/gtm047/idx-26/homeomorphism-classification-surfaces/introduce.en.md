The classification of compact connected $2$-manifolds is complete: the ordered pair $(h, m)$, where $h$ is the number of handles and $m \in \{0,1,2\}$ is the number of cross-caps, is a complete homeomorphism invariant. Two such surfaces are homeomorphic exactly when they have the same $h$ and the same $m$. The proof that equal invariants implies homeomorphism uses an explicit construction identifying the two standard models. The converse follows from the topological invariance of orientability ($m=0$ vs $m>0$), the Euler characteristic $\chi = 2 - (2h+m)$, and the Betti number, which together force $h_1 = h_2$ and $m_1 = m_2$.
