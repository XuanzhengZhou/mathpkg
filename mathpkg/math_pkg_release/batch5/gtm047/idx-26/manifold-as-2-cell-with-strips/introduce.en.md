Every compact connected $2$-manifold with a triangulation admits a decomposition into two distinguished polyhedral $2$-cells $D$ and $D'$ together with $n$ interpolating strips $S_i$, all with disjoint interiors. Each strip $S_i$ attaches to both $D$ and $D'$ along two disjoint arcs each, and $D$ and $D'$ meet along $2n$ disjoint arcs total. This "$2$-cell with strips" representation is the geometric starting point for simplifying the manifold down to its standard form as a sphere with handles and cross-caps.
