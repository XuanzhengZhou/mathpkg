Let $M$ be a compact connected $2$-manifold with triangulation $K$. Then $M$ can be expressed as a union
$$M = D \cup D' \cup \bigcup_{i=1}^{n} S_i$$
of polyhedral $2$-cells with disjoint interiors, such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item for each $i$, each of the sets $S_i \cap D$ and $S_i \cap D'$ is the union of two disjoint arcs, and
\item $D \cap D'$ is the union of $2n$ disjoint arcs.
\end{enumerate}
The sets $S_i$ are called \emph{strips}, and such a representation is called a \emph{$2$-cell with strips}.
