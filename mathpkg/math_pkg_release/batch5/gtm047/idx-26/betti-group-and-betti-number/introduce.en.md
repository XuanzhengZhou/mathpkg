For a compact connected $2$-manifold $M$, the first homology group with integer coefficients $H_1(M; \mathbf{Z})$ splits into a free part $B^1$ (the Betti group, isomorphic to $\mathbf{Z}^{p^1}$) and a finite torsion part $T^1$. The rank $p^1(M)$ of the free part is the first Betti number. Together, $p^1(M)$ and $T^1$ provide homology invariants that help distinguish non-homeomorphic surfaces and complement the Euler characteristic in the classification.
