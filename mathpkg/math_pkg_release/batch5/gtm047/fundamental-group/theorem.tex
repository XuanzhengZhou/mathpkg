Let $[X, \emptyset]$ be a topological space, and suppose that $X$ is pathwise connected. Let $P_0 \in X$, and let $\operatorname{CP}(X, P_0)$ be the set of all closed paths $p: [0, 1] \rightarrow X$ with $p(0) = p(1) = P_0$. Two paths $p, q \in \operatorname{CP}(X, P_0)$ are equivalent ($p \cong q$) if there is a mapping $f: [0, 1]^2 \rightarrow X$ such that $f(t,0) = p(t)$, $f(t,1) = q(t)$, and $f(0,y) = f(1,y) = P_0$ for every $y$. The set of equivalence classes $\pi(X, P_0) = \{\bar{p} \mid p \in \operatorname{CP}(X, P_0)\}$ with multiplication $\bar{p}\bar{q} = \overline{pq}$ forms a group, called the fundamental group of $X$ at $P_0$.
