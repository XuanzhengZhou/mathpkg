Prove or disprove: No polyhedral 2-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^3 - \mathcal{Q}$ separates two points of $\mathcal{Q}$ from one another in $\mathbf{R}^3$. (That is, if $S$ is a polyhedral 2-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^3 - \mathcal{Q}$, then $\mathcal{Q}$ lies in a single component of $\mathbf{R}^3 - S$.) (In fact, this holds for all 2-spheres, polyhedral or not; but the proof is unreasonably difficult, on the basis of the methods developed in this book; see $[A_4]$.)
