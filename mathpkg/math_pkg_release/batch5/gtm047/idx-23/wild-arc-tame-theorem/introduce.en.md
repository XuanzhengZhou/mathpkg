This theorem establishes that the arc $A_1$ -- constructed as a wild arc with simply connected complement from an ascending sequence of concentric cubes -- is nevertheless tame. The proof constructs a homeomorphism $\phi: \mathbf{R}^3 \leftrightarrow \mathbf{R}^3$ in stages: first the identity outside the outermost cube, then inductively on the concentric shells, mapping the wild arc $A_1$ onto a straight line segment $A_1'$. The construction uses the fact that in each spherical shell, the portion of the arc is unknotted in a carefully chosen polyhedral 3-cell.
