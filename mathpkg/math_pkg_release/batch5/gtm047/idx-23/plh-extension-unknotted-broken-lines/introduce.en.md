This theorem extends the unknottedness property of broken lines to a uniqueness result: given two pairs $(C_i, B_i)$ of polyhedral 3-cells with unknotted broken lines, any piecewise-linear homeomorphism between their boundaries that respects the endpoints of the broken lines can be extended to a PLH between the entire cells mapping one broken line onto the other. The proof reduces to the standard model $C = \sigma^2 \times [0,1]$ and $B = R \times [0,1]$, then uses PL 2-cells bounded by polygons on the boundary to decompose the cells and extend the homeomorphism successively.
