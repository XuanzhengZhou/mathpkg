**Theorem 4.** Let $I, P, Q, R, S$ be as in the preceding theorems, and let $A_1$ and $A_2$ be disjoint arcs in $\overline{I}$, such that $A_1 \cap \operatorname{Fr} I = \{P\}$ and $A_2 \cap \operatorname{Fr} I = \{R\}$. Then $S$ and $Q$ are in the frontier of the same component of $I - (A_1 \cup A_2)$.
