By a cell-complex we mean a finite collection $K$ of topological cells, such that (1) different elements of $K$ have disjoint interiors, (2) for each cell $C$ in $K$, $\operatorname{Bd} C$ is a union of elements of $K$, and (3) if $C, C' \in K$ and $C \cap C' \neq \varnothing$, then $C \cap C'$ is a cell, and is a union of elements of $K$. If the elements of $K$ are polyhedra, then $K$ is a PL cell-complex.
