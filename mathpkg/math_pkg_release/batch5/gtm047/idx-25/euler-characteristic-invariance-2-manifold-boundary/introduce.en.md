Theorem 9 proves that the Euler characteristic is well-defined for compact $2$-manifolds with boundary: every triangulation of the same manifold yields the same value of $\chi = V - E + F$. The proof caps off boundary components with $2$-cells to obtain a closed $2$-manifold, whose Euler characteristic is a topological invariant by the Hauptvermutung, and then subtracts the contribution of the added cells.
