Theorem 5 constructs a wild $2$-sphere $S^2$ in $\mathbf{R}^3$ that is locally polyhedral everywhere except at a single wild point $Q$, yet its complement $\mathbf{R}^3 - S^2$ is homeomorphic to the complement of the standard $2$-sphere. This shows that the property of having a complement homeomorphic to the complement of a standard sphere does not characterize tameness. The construction uses the wild arc $A_2$ from Theorem 4 and a carefully chosen $3$-cell neighborhood.
