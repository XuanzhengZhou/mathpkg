Let $K$ be a finite complex, of dimension $\leqslant 2$, and let $\mathcal{C}$ be a finite collection of subsets of $|K|$. For each $C \in \mathcal{C}$, $\operatorname{Fr} C$ is defined relative to $|K|$. Suppose that
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(1)] the elements of $\mathcal{C}$ are disjoint,
  \item[(2)] for each $C \in \mathcal{C}$, $\overline{C}$ is a finite polyhedron,
  \item[(3)] each set $\operatorname{Fr} C$ is the union of a collection of elements of $\mathcal{C}$, and
  \item[(4)] each $C \in \mathcal{C}$ either is a point or is homeomorphic to the interior of a Euclidean simplex.
\end{enumerate}
Then $\mathcal{C}$ is called an \textbf{open cell-complex}. $|\mathcal{C}|$ is the union of the elements of $\mathcal{C}$, and $\mathcal{C}$ is called an \textbf{open cell-decomposition} of $|\mathcal{C}|$. The points $v \in \mathcal{C}$ are called \textbf{vertices} of $\mathcal{C}$; similarly for the \textbf{edges} and \textbf{faces} ($=2$-faces) of $\mathcal{C}$.
