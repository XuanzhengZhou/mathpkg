In an open cell-complex $\mathcal{C}$, the frontier of every $2$-dimensional face $C^2$ is connected. This property follows from the fact that each face is homeomorphic to the interior of a $2$-simplex, and its closure is a finite polyhedron whose boundary forms a connected $1$-dimensional subcomplex.
