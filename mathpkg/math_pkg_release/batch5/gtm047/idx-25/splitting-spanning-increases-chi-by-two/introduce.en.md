Theorem 11 quantifies how the Euler characteristic changes under a specific geometric operation: splitting a $2$-manifold along a separating $1$-sphere and then capping the new boundary components with $2$-cells increases the Euler characteristic by exactly $2$. The attached $2$-cells are disjoint and intersect the original manifold only along their boundaries.
