Since Theorem 9 guarantees that all triangulations of a compact $2$-manifold with boundary share the same Euler characteristic, we can define the Euler characteristic of the manifold itself as this common value. This makes $\chi(M)$ a well-defined topological invariant of $M$, independent of any particular triangulation.
