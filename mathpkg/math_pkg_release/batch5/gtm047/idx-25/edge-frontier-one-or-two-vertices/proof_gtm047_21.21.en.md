Let $C^1$ be an edge of $\mathcal{C}$. Since $\overline{C^1}$ is a finite polyhedron and $C^1$ is homeomorphic to the interior of a $1$-simplex, its frontier $\operatorname{Fr} C^1 = \overline{C^1} - C^1$ must consist of the endpoints. By condition (3) of the definition of an open cell-complex, $\operatorname{Fr} C^1$ is a union of elements of $\mathcal{C}$. These can only be vertices, and a $1$-simplex has at most two endpoints. The frontier may consist of one vertex if the two endpoints are identified (a loop edge).
