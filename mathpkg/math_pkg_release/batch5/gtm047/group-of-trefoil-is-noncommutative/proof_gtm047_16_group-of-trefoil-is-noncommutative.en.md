From the diagram, the relations are $r_1 = g_1 g_3^{-1} g_2^{-1} g_3$, $r_2 = g_2 g_1^{-1} g_3^{-1} g_1$, $r_3 = g_3 g_2^{-1} g_1^{-1} g_2$. Mapping $g_1 \mapsto (23)$, $g_2 \mapsto (13)$, $g_3 \mapsto (12)$ in the symmetric group $S_3$ gives a surjective homomorphism. Since $S_3$ is noncommutative, so is $\pi(\mathbb{R}^3 - K)$.
