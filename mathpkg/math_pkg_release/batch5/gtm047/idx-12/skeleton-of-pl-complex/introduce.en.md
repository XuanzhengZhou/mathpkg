The $i$-skeleton $\mathcal{K}^i$ of a PL complex $\mathcal{K}$ consists of all PL simplexes in $\mathcal{K}$ whose dimension is at most $i$. The skeleta form an increasing filtration $\mathcal{K}^0 \subset \mathcal{K}^1 \subset \cdots \subset \mathcal{K}$ that captures the combinatorial structure of the complex by dimension. This is the PL analog of the $i$-skeleton of a Euclidean simplicial complex.
