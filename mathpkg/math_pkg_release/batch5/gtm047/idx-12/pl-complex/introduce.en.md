A PL (piecewise-linear) complex $\mathcal{K}$ is a countable collection of PL simplexes in a space $[X, \emptyset]$ that satisfies three axioms. (K.1) is the face-closure condition: every face of a simplex in $\mathcal{K}$ also belongs to $\mathcal{K}$. (K.2) is the intersection condition: any two simplexes intersect in a common face, and crucially, they must induce the same barycentric coordinate system on that intersection — a condition that is automatic for Euclidean simplexes but not for abstract PL simplexes. (K.3) is a local-finiteness condition ensuring the topology is well-behaved. PL complexes generalize Euclidean complexes by freeing them from an embedding in $\mathbf{R}^m$, which is essential for proofs like Rado's triangulation theorem.
