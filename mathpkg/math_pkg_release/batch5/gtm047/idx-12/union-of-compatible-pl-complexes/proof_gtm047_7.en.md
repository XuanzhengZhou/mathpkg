Conditions (K.1) and (K.2) for $\mathcal{K}_1 \cup \mathcal{K}_2$ follow immediately from the hypothesis: for any two simplexes, if they both lie in $\mathcal{K}_1$ or both in $\mathcal{K}_2$, the conditions hold because each $\mathcal{K}_i$ is a PL complex; if one lies in $\mathcal{K}_1$ and the other in $\mathcal{K}_2$, the hypothesis directly provides the required faces and equality of restricted equivalence classes. The verification of condition (K.3) is trivial because each set $|[h]|$ in $\mathcal{K}_1 \cup \mathcal{K}_2$ belongs to one of the two complexes, and local finiteness in that complex implies local finiteness in the union.
