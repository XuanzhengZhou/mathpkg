This theorem provides a gluing condition for PL complexes: if two PL complexes $\mathcal{K}_1$ and $\mathcal{K}_2$ in the same PL space $[X, \emptyset]$ have the property that any intersection of a simplex from one with a simplex from the other is a common face with matching barycentric coordinate structures, then their union $\mathcal{K}_1 \cup \mathcal{K}_2$ is again a PL complex. The proof is straightforward: conditions (K.1) and (K.2) hold by the hypothesis on intersections, and condition (K.3) (local finiteness) is trivially verified since both complexes individually satisfy it.
