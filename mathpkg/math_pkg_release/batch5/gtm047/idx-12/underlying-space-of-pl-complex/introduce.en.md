The underlying space $|\mathcal{K}|$ of a PL complex $\mathcal{K}$ is the set-theoretic union of the images $|[h]|$ of all PL simplexes $[h]$ belonging to $\mathcal{K}$. This space inherits a topology from the ambient PL space $[X, \emptyset]$ in which the complex lives. The notation $|\mathcal{K}|$ parallels the notation $|K|$ for the geometric realization of a Euclidean complex $K$.
