The star $\operatorname{St} v$ of a vertex $v$ in a PL complex $\mathcal{K}$ is the set of all PL simplexes whose image contains $v$, together with all faces of those simplexes. This definition mirrors the star of a vertex in a simplicial complex, but uses the PL simplex equivalence classes $[h]$ instead of Euclidean simplexes. The star of a vertex provides a combinatorial neighborhood of $v$ within the complex and is fundamental for defining concepts like isomorphism, diagram, and combinatorial equivalence in the PL category.
