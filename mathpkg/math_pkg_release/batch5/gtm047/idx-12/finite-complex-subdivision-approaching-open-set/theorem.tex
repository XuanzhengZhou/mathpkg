Let $K$ be a finite complex, and let $U$ be an open set in $|K|$. Then there exists a complex $K_U$ such that $|K_U| = U$ and every simplex of $K_U$ is a subsimplex of a simplex of $K$.

More generally, there is a sequence of finite complexes $K_1, K_2, \ldots$ such that:
(1) $K_1 = K$,
(2) $K_{i+1} < K_i$ for each $i$,
(3) $\lim_{i \to \infty} \|K_i\| = 0$,
and a sequence of subcomplexes $L_1, L_2, \ldots, L_r$ such that each $L_i$ is a subcomplex of $K_{n_i}$ with $\bigcup |L_i| \subset U$ forming a neighborhood of its predecessors.
