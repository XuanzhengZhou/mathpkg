This theorem establishes that if two coordinate mappings $g$ and $h$ are equivalent ($g \sim h$), then for any point $w$ in their common image $|g| = |h|$, the barycentric coordinates of $w$ with respect to the vertices of $\sigma_g$ and $\sigma_h$ are the same. Specifically, if $w = g(\sum \alpha_i v_i)$ and $h^{-1} \circ g$ is the simplicial isomorphism mapping $v_i$ to $x_i$, then $w = h(\sum \alpha_i x_i)$ with the same coefficients $\alpha_i$. This is a crucial consistency result: it means barycentric coordinates are well-defined on PL simplexes (equivalence classes $[h]$), not just on individual coordinate mappings.
