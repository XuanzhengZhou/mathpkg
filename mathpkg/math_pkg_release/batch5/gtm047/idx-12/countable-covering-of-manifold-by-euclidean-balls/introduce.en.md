This theorem provides a countable cover of an $n$-manifold $M$ by pairs of nested open sets $(N_i, N_i')$, each pair homeomorphic to the standard unit ball $D$ and its half-radius sub-ball $D'$ in $\mathbf{R}^n$. The proof uses local Euclidean neighborhoods at each point, together with the separability of $M$ (as a metric space) to extract a countable subcover. This is the first preparatory step in the proof of Radó's triangulation theorem for 2-manifolds.
