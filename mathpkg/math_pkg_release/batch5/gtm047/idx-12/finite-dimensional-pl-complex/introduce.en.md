A PL complex $\mathcal{K}$ is called finite-dimensional if the set of dimensions of its constituent PL simplexes is bounded above. The dimension $\dim \mathcal{K}$ is then defined as the maximum of these dimensions. This parallels the definition for Euclidean complexes and is essential for working with complexes such as combinatorial manifolds, where the dimension is uniform.
