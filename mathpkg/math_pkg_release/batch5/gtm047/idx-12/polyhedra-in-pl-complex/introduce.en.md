A subset $M$ of the underlying space $|\mathcal{K}|$ of a PL complex is called a polyhedron if there exists a PL complex $\mathcal{K}'$ whose underlying space equals $M$ and every simplex of $\mathcal{K}'$ is a rectilinear subsimplex of some simplex of $\mathcal{K}$. In the compact case, this means $M$ forms a subcomplex of some subdivision of $\mathcal{K}$. Polyhedra generalize the concept of subcomplexes by allowing subdivisions, which is crucial for PL topology where subdivisions are used to achieve compatibility between different triangulations.
