This theorem is the natural extension of the Schönflies theorem from the plane to the $2$-sphere. While the classical Schönflies theorem asserts that a $1$-sphere in $\mathbf{R}^2$ bounds a $2$-cell, this version states that a $1$-sphere in a $2$-sphere partitions the sphere into two complementary $2$-cells sharing the $1$-sphere as their common frontier. It follows from Theorem 1 (the Jordan curve theorem on $S^2$) together with the classical Schönflies theorem.
