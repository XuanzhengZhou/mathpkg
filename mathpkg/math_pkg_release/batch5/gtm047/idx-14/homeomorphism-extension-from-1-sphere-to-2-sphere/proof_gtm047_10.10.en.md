Let $C_1$ and $C_2$ be the $2$-cells whose common boundary is $J$, as guaranteed by Theorem 2. Similarly, let $C'_1, C'_2$ be the $2$-cells whose common boundary is $J'$. By Theorem 5.6, the homeomorphism $h: J \to J'$ can be extended to give homeomorphisms $C_i \leftrightarrow C'_i$ for $i = 1, 2$. These extensions agree on the common boundary $J$ and together define a homeomorphism $S^2 = C_1 \cup C_2 \leftrightarrow C'_1 \cup C'_2 = S^2$.
