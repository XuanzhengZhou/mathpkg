Theorem 10 states that there is no continuous map from a 2-cell onto its boundary that fixes the boundary pointwise. The proof relies on Theorem 9: assuming a retraction $r: C^2 \to J$ exists, the preimages $M_1 = r^{-1}(P)$ and $M_2 = r^{-1}(R)$ of two opposite points on $J$ would be disjoint closed sets. Theorem 9 then forces $Q$ and $S$ to lie in the same component of the complement, contradicting the retraction property. This result is foundational to the Brouwer fixed-point theorem for the disk.
