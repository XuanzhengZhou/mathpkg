Theorem 7 is the fundamental taming theorem for 1-dimensional polyhedra in the plane. It states that any homeomorphic image $M$ of a linear graph without end-points can be straightened to a polyhedron by a homeomorphism of the whole plane that is arbitrarily close to the identity on a prescribed open set $U$ containing $M$. The proof constructs the homeomorphism $h$ by composing local straightening maps around each edge (via Lemma 2 / contracting collections) and around each vertex (via the decomposition from Lemmas 3 and 4). The $h$ is a $\phi$-approximation, meaning $d(P, h(P)) < \phi(P)$ for all $P \in U$.
