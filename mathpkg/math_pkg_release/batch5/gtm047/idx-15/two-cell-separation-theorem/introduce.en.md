Theorem 9 generalizes the separation property established in Theorem 4.4 from arcs to arbitrary disjoint closed sets. Given a 2-cell $C^2$ with four cyclically ordered points $P, Q, R, S$ on its boundary, if $P$ and $R$ lie in one closed set $M_1$ and $Q$ and $S$ lie in another closed set $M_2$ (both closed in $C^2$ and intersecting the boundary in closed sets), then $Q$ and $S$ belong to the same connected component of the complement $C^2 - (M_1 \cup M_2)$. This is the key topological input for proving that the boundary of a 2-cell is not a retract.
