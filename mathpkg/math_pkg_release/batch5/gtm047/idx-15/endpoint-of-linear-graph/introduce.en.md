An end-point of a linear graph (1-complex) is a vertex that belongs to exactly one edge. In the context of taming theorems for polyhedra in the plane, linear graphs with end-points present technical difficulties. Moore introduces this concept to isolate the problematic case and handle it separately via an extension lemma that embeds the graph into a larger complex without end-points.
