A retraction is a continuous map from a topological space $A$ onto a subspace $B$ that fixes $B$ pointwise. The subspace $B$ is then called a retract of $A$. This concept is central to fixed-point theory and is used in Moise's proof that the boundary of a 2-cell is not a retract of the 2-cell, which in turn relies on a separation argument.
