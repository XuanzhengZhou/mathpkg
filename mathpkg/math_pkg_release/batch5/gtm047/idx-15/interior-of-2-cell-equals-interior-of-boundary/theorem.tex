Let $C^2$ be a 2-cell in $\mathbf{R}^2$. Then
$$\operatorname{Int} C^2 = \operatorname{Int} (\operatorname{Bd} C^2),$$
where $\operatorname{Int} (\operatorname{Bd} C^2)$ denotes the bounded component of $\mathbf{R}^2 - \operatorname{Bd} C^2$ (the interior of the Jordan curve $\operatorname{Bd} C^2$).
