Theorem 12 reconciles two notions of "interior" for a 2-cell in the plane: the topological interior of the 2-cell as a space, and the bounded complementary component of its boundary Jordan curve. The result follows from Theorem 11 (which states that the interior of a 2-cell is the image of the interior of its boundary under a suitable homeomorphism) together with the Schönflies theorem. This is essential for the proof of Theorem 13, where triangulated 2-cells are shown to be polyhedral.
