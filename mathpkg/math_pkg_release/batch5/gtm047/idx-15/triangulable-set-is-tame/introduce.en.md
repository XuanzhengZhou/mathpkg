Theorem 13 is the culminating result of the chapter: every triangulable set in the plane is tame. The proof first applies Theorem 8 (which extends Theorem 7 to all linear graphs, with or without end-points) to straighten the 1-skeleton $|K^1|$ into a polyhedron. Then, for each 2-simplex $\sigma^2$, its image under $h \circ f$ is a 2-cell whose boundary is already a polygon. Theorem 12 shows the interior of this 2-cell equals the interior of its polygonal boundary, so the 2-cell itself is polyhedral (via Theorem 2.2). This establishes that any homeomorphic image of a finite simplicial complex in the plane can be straightened to a polyhedron.
