The diagram $\Phi(K)$ of a Euclidean complex $K$ captures its combinatorial structure by recording which sets of vertices span simplexes. This abstraction strips away the geometric embedding in $\mathbf{R}^m$ and retains only the incidence relations. The five properties (1)-(5) of $\Phi(K)$ motivate the definition of an abstract complex.
