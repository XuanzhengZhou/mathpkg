An abstract complex is finite-dimensional when the cardinality of its simplexes is uniformly bounded. The dimension is the smallest integer $n$ such that no simplex has more than $n+1$ vertices. This condition, corresponding to property (5) of the diagram of a Euclidean complex, guarantees that the complex can be realized in a Euclidean space of dimension $2n+1$.
