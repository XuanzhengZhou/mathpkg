A function from a subset $M$ of a topological space $X$ into a subset $N$ of a topological space $Y$ is called a mapping if it is continuous when $M$ and $N$ are equipped with their respective subspace topologies. This definition is needed to state theorems about connectivity being preserved by continuous maps between subspaces.
