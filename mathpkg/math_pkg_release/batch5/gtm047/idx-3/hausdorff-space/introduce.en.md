A topological space is called Hausdorff (or $T_2$) if any two distinct points can be separated by disjoint open neighborhoods. This separation axiom is one of the most fundamental properties in topology and ensures the uniqueness of limits of convergent sequences or nets. Most spaces encountered in geometric topology, including metric spaces and manifolds, are Hausdorff.
