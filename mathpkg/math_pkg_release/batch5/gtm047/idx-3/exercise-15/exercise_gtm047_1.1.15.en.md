Let $M$ be a compact set, in a metric space. Let $P$ and $Q$ be points of $M$. Suppose that $M$ is not the union of any two disjoint closed sets $H$ and $K$, containing $P$ and $Q$ respectively. Then $M$ contains a compact connected set which contains $P$ and $Q$.
