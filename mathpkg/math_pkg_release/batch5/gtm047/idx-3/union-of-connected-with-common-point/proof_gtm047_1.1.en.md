Suppose that $G^{*} = H \cup K$ (separated and nonempty), with $P \in H$. Since each $g \in G$ is connected, each $g$ lies in $H$ or in $K$ (by Theorem 10). Therefore $g \subset H$, $G^{*} \subset H$, and $K = \emptyset$, which contradicts the hypothesis for $K$.
