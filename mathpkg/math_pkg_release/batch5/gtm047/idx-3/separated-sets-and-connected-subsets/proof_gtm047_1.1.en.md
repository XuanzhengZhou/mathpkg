If not, $M = (M \cap H) \cup (M \cap K)$, where the two sets on the right are separated and nonempty. (Evidently, if $H$ and $K$ are separated, and $H' \subset H$ and $K' \subset K$, then $H'$ and $K'$ are separated.) This contradicts the connectedness of $M$.
