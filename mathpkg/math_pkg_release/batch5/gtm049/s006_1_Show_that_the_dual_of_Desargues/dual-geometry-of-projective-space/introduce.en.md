The dual geometry $\mathcal{P}^*(V)$ of a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is defined as the set of all subspaces of $V$ together with the incidence mapping $\circ$. In $\mathcal{P}^*(V)$, the projective dimension of a subspace $M$ is $n-1-r$, where $r$ is its projective dimension in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $n = \operatorname{pdim} V$. This construction formalizes the principle of projective duality: a correlation of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ can be interpreted as a projectivity from $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto $\mathcal{P}^*(V)$, and a duality from $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto $\mathcal{P}(W)$ becomes a projectivity from $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto $\mathcal{P}^*(W)$.
