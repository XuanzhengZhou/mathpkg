When a linear map $f$ from a vector space $V$ into its dual space $V^*$ is represented by matrices with respect to two different ordered bases, the matrices $A$ and $B$ are related by congruence: $B = S^t A S$ for some invertible matrix $S$. This result is the dual-space analogue of the similarity relation $B = S^{-1} A S$ for linear operators on $V$, with the transpose replacing the inverse because the dual basis transforms contravariantly. This proposition motivates the study of congruence classes of matrices as a means of classifying bilinear forms and linear maps into the dual space.
