Two square matrices $A$ and $B$ over a field $F$ are congruent if $B = S^t A S$ for some invertible matrix $S$. Congruence is an equivalence relation on $F^{n \times n}$, distinct from similarity (where $B = S^{-1} A S$). The congruence relation arises naturally from the study of bilinear forms and linear maps from a vector space into its dual, since a change of basis transforms the representing matrix by congruence rather than similarity.
