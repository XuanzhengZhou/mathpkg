This exercise explores the dual of Desargues' Theorem. In the projective plane, the dual statement swaps points and lines: the dual of "if two triangles are perspective from a point, they are perspective from a line" becomes "if two triangles are perspective from a line, they are perspective from a point"---which is precisely the converse. In three dimensions, Desargues' Theorem is self-dual.
