Orthogonality generalizes perpendicularity from Euclidean geometry to arbitrary bilinear forms. Given a bilinear form $\sigma$ on a vector space $V$, two vectors $a$ and $b$ are orthogonal if $\sigma(a,b) = 0$. The definition distinguishes left and right orthogonal complements: $M^{\perp}(\sigma)$ consists of all $b$ such that $\sigma(M,b)=0$ (right orthogonal complement), while $M^{\top}(\sigma)$ consists of all $a$ such that $\sigma(a,M)=0$ (left orthogonal complement). For symmetric bilinear forms the two notions coincide, but in general they are distinct.
