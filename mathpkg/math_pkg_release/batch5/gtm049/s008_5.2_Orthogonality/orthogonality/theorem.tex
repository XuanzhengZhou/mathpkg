If $\sigma$ is a bilinear form on a vector space $V$ and if $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ then we say that $a, b$ (in that order) are \emph{orthogonal}, or that $a$ is \emph{orthogonal to $b$} (with respect to $\sigma$). Two subsets $A, B$ of $V$ are \emph{orthogonal} if $a, b$ are orthogonal for every $a \in A$, $b \in B$.

If $M$ is any subspace of $V$ then we denote by $M^{\perp}(\sigma)$ the set of all vectors $b \in V$ such that $\sigma(M, b) = 0$ (i.e., for which $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a \in M$); and by $M^{\top}(\sigma)$ the set of all vectors $a \in V$ such that $\sigma(a, M) = 0$.

If the bilinear form $\sigma$ is understood we shall often write $\perp$ for $\perp(\sigma)$ and $\top$ for $\top(\sigma)$.
