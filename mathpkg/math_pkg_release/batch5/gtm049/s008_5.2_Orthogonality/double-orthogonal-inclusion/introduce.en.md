Lemma 2 states the elementary inclusion $M \subset M^{\perp\top}$ and $M \subset M^{\top\perp}$ for any subspace $M$. The proof follows directly from the definition of orthogonality: if $a \in M$ and $b \in M^{\perp}$, then $\sigma(a,b)=0$, which means $a \in (M^{\perp})^{\top}$, hence $M \subset M^{\perp\top}$. Under non-degeneracy (Theorem 1), these inclusions become equalities, establishing $\perp$ and $\top$ as mutually inverse bijections on the lattice of subspaces.
