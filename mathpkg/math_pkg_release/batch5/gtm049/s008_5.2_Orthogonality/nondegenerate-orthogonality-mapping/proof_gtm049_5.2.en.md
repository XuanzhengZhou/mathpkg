(1) Non-degeneracy of $\sigma$ implies $V^{\top} = 0 = V^{\perp}$. Then Proposition 4 gives
$$\dim M^{\perp} = \dim V - \dim M + \dim(M \cap V^{\top}) = \dim V - \dim M.$$
The same formula holds for $\dim M^{\top}$ by symmetry.

(2) By Lemma 2, $M \subset M^{\perp\top}$. By (1), $\dim M^{\perp\top} = \dim V - \dim M^{\perp} = \dim V - (\dim V - \dim M) = \dim M$. Hence the inclusion is an equality: $M = M^{\perp\top}$. The same argument gives $M = M^{\top\perp}$.

(3)-(5) The rest of the proof is entirely analogous to the proof of Theorem 6 of Chapter IV (p. 81), which establishes the corresponding properties for the annihilator mapping $\circ$ on the dual space. The mapping $\perp$ (and $\top$) under non-degeneracy behaves exactly as the annihilator mapping does in the finite-dimensional dual space setting.
