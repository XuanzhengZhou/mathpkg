A vector $b$ lies in $(M\tilde{\sigma})^{\circ}$ if and only if $b f = 0$ for all $f \in M\tilde{\sigma}$, i.e., for all $f = a \tilde{\sigma}$ with $a \in M$. By the definition of $\tilde{\sigma}$, the condition $b(a\tilde{\sigma}) = 0$ is equivalent to $\sigma(a, b) = 0$. Thus $b \in (M\tilde{\sigma})^{\circ}$ if and only if $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a \in M$, which is exactly the definition of $b \in M^{\perp}(\sigma)$. Hence $M^{\perp}(\sigma) = (M\tilde{\sigma})^{\circ}$.

The second equality $M^{\top}(\sigma) = (M\tilde{\sigma})^{\circ}$ is proved in exactly the same way, using the left correspondence $\tilde{\sigma} \colon V \to V^{*}$.
