Let $\sigma \in \mathcal{B}(V)$ and let $\varrho, \tilde{\sigma}: V \to V^*$ be the linear maps defined by
$$a\varrho(b) = \sigma(a, b), \quad b\tilde{\sigma}(a) = \sigma(a, b).$$

For any subspace $M \subseteq V$,
$$M^{\perp}(\sigma) = (M\varrho)^{\circ}, \qquad M^{\top}(\sigma) = (M\tilde{\sigma})^{\circ},$$
where $(\cdot)^{\circ}$ denotes the annihilator in $V$.
