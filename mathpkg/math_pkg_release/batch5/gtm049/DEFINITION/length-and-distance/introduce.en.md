The positive definite bilinear form on a Euclidean space induces a norm via $\|a\| = \sqrt{\sigma(a, a)}$, which generalizes the Euclidean length in $\mathbf{R}^n$. The distance between two vectors is then defined as the length of their difference. These definitions turn the Euclidean space into a metric space with particularly nice geometric properties, including the triangle inequality and the parallelogram law.
