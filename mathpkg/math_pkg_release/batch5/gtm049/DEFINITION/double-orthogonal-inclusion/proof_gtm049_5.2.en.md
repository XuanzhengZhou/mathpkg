**Proof.** If $a$ is any vector in $M$ and $b$ is any vector in $M^{\perp}$, then by the definition of $M^{\perp}$, $\sigma(a, b) = 0$. But this means that $a$ is orthogonal (in the $\top$ sense) to every element of $M^{\perp}$, hence $a \in (M^{\perp})^{\top}$. Thus $M \subset M^{\perp \top}$. The other inclusion $M \subset M^{\top \perp}$ is proved similarly.
