If $M$ is any subspace of $V$, we denote by $M^{\perp}(\sigma)$ the set of all vectors $b \in V$ such that $\sigma(M, b) = 0$, i.e., $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a \in M$; and by $M^{\top}(\sigma)$ the set of all vectors $a \in V$ such that $\sigma(a, M) = 0$.

If the bilinear form $\sigma$ is understood, we often write $\perp$ for $\perp(\sigma)$ and $\top$ for $\top(\sigma)$.

From the definitions, $M \subset N$ implies $M^{\perp} \supset N^{\perp}$ and $M^{\top} \supset N^{\top}$. Both $M^{\perp}$ and $M^{\top}$ are subspaces of $V$, and $0^{\perp} = 0^{\top} = V$.
