If $\sigma$ is a bilinear form on a vector space $V$ and if $\sigma(a, b) = 0$, then we say that $a, b$ (in that order) are \textbf{orthogonal}, or that $a$ is orthogonal to $b$ (with respect to $\sigma$). Two subsets $A, B$ of $V$ are \textbf{orthogonal} if $a, b$ are orthogonal for every $a \in A$, $b \in B$.
