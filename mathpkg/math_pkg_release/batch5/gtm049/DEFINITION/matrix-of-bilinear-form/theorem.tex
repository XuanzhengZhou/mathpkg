Let $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ be an ordered basis of $V$ over $F$. Let $\mathcal{B}(V)$ denote the vector space of all bilinear forms on $V$.

(1) The mapping $\sigma \rightarrow (\sigma; (a_i))$ is a linear isomorphism of $\mathcal{B}(V)$ onto $F^{n \times n}$, where $(\sigma; (a_i)) = (s_{ij})$ with $s_{ij} = \sigma(a_i, a_j)$.

(2) The mapping $\sigma \rightarrow \sum \sigma(a_i, a_j) X_i Y_j$ is a linear isomorphism of $\mathcal{B}(V)$ onto the vector space of all bilinear polynomials in $X_1, \ldots, X_n, Y_1, \ldots, Y_n$ over $F$.

Moreover, if $a, b$ have coordinate rows $x = (x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, $y = (y_1, \ldots, y_n)$ with respect to this basis, then

$$\sigma(a, b) = x S y^t,$$

where $S = (\sigma; (a_i))$.
