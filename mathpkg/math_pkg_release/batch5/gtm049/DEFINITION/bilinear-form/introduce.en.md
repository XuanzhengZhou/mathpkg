A bilinear form on a vector space $V$ over a field $F$ is a function of two vector arguments that is linear in each argument separately. Condition B.1 expresses linearity in the first variable, and condition B.2 expresses linearity in the second variable. The pair $(V, \sigma)$ is then called a bilinear space. The structure theory of vector spaces equipped with a bilinear form is the central topic of Chapter V.
