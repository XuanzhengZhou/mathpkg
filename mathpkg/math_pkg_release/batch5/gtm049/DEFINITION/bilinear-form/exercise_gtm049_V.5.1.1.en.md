Consider the following properties of a bilinear form $\sigma$ on a vector space $V$ (not assumed finite dimensional):

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $b$ implies $a = 0$;
\item[(ii)] $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a$ implies $b = 0$.
\end{itemize}

Prove that (i) is equivalent to the map $\varrho: V \to V^*$ (defined by $a\varrho(b) = \sigma(a,b)$) being one-to-one, and that (ii) is equivalent to $\tilde{\sigma}$ being one-to-one.

If $V$ is finite dimensional, show that (ii) is also equivalent to $\varrho$ being onto $V^*$. Thus in the finite dimensional case, (i) and (ii) are equivalent.
