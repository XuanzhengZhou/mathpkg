A Euclidean space is a real vector space equipped with a positive definite bilinear form. This generalizes the familiar dot product on $\mathbf{R}^n$ to an abstract setting. The positive definite bilinear form allows the definition of length, distance, and angle, making Euclidean spaces the foundational objects of Euclidean geometry.
