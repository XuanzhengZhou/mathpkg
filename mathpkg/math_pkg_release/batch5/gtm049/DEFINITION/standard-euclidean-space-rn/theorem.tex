There is a unique bilinear form $\sigma_0$ on $\mathbf{R}^n$ for which the standard basis $(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ is a cartesian basis of $(\mathbf{R}^n, \sigma_0)$. Explicitly,

$$\sigma_0((x_1, \ldots, x_n), (y_1, \ldots, y_n)) = x_1 y_1 + \cdots + x_n y_n.$$

The corresponding distance is denoted by $d_0$.
