A distance on a real vector space is an axiomatic notion abstracting the properties of the Euclidean distance. It must be symmetric (D.1), positive definite (D.2), satisfy the triangle inequality (D.3), be translation-invariant (D.4), be homogeneous with respect to scalar multiplication (D.5), and satisfy the parallelogram law (D.6). The parallelogram law is the key condition that distinguishes distances coming from bilinear forms from general norms.
