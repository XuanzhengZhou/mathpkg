Since $\sigma$ is linear in the second variable, the mapping $b \mapsto \sigma(a, b)$ is a linear form on $V$. Denote this form by $a\varrho$. Since $\sigma$ is linear in the first variable, $\varrho$ is a linear mapping of $V$ into the dual space $V^*$. Conversely, given any linear map $f: V \to V^*$, define $\sigma$ by $\sigma(a, b) = (af)(b)$. The mapping $f \mapsto \sigma$ is the inverse of $\sigma \mapsto \varrho$, so the correspondence is bijective. Linearity follows immediately from the definitions of $\sigma + \tau$ and $x\sigma$.
