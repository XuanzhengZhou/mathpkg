Let $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ and $(b_1, \ldots, b_n)$ be ordered bases of $V$ and let $P$ be the transition matrix from $(a_i)$ to $(b_i)$, i.e., $b_i = \sum_j p_{ij} a_j$. Then

$$(\sigma; (b_i)) = P (\sigma; (a_i)) P^t.$$

Consequently, the matrices of a bilinear form $\sigma$ with respect to all ordered bases of $V$ lie in a single congruence class, and every matrix congruent to $(\sigma; (a_i))$ is the matrix of $\sigma$ with respect to some basis.
