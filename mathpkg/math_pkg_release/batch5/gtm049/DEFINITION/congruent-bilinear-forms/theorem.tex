If $f$ is a linear mapping of $V$ into $V$, we denote by $\sigma^f$ the bilinear form obtained from $\sigma$ by the rule

$$\sigma^f(a, b) = \sigma(af, bf)$$

for all $a, b$ in $V$. Two bilinear forms $\sigma$ and $\sigma'$ on $V$ are \textbf{congruent} if, and only if, there exists an automorphism $f$ of $V$ such that $\sigma' = \sigma^f$.
