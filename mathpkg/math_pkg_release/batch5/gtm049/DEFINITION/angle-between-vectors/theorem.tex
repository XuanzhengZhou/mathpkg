Let $(V, \sigma)$ be a euclidean space and let $\cos \theta$ be defined for all real $\theta$ as the sum of the series

$$1 - \frac{\theta^2}{2!} + \frac{\theta^4}{4!} - \cdots.$$

Recall that $\cos \theta$ is a strictly decreasing mapping of $[0, \pi]$ onto $[-1, 1]$.

For non-zero vectors $a, b \in V$, the unique $\theta \in [0, \pi]$ such that

$$\cos \theta = \frac{\sigma(a, b)}{\|a\| \|b\|}$$

is called the \textbf{angle} between $a$ and $b$.
