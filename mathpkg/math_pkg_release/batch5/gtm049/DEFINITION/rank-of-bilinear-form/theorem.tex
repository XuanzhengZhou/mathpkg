Let $\sigma$ be a bilinear form on a vector space $V$. If $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ is any ordered basis of $V$, then the \textbf{rank} of $\sigma$ is the rank of the matrix $(\sigma; (a_i))$. If $\operatorname{rank} \sigma < n$, we say that $\sigma$ is \textbf{degenerate}; if $\operatorname{rank} \sigma = n$, we say that $\sigma$ is \textbf{non-degenerate}.

The rank is independent of the choice of basis.
