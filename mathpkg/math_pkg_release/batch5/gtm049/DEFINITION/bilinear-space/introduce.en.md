A **bilinear space** $(V, \sigma)$ consists of a vector space $V$ over a field $F$ together with a bilinear form $\sigma$ on $V$. The notation $(V, \sigma)$ emphasizes that the vector space is equipped with the additional structure of the bilinear form. The study of bilinear spaces is the central theme of Chapter V; properties of $\sigma$ such as orthogonality, rank, and congruence are naturally expressed in terms of the bilinear space it defines.
