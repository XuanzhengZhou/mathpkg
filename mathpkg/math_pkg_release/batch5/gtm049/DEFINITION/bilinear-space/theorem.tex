A pair $(V, \sigma)$ where $V$ is a vector space over a field $F$ and $\sigma$ is a bilinear form on $V$ is called a \textbf{bilinear space}. An isomorphism of the bilinear space $(V, \sigma)$ onto the bilinear space $(V', \sigma')$ is a linear isomorphism $f: V \to V'$ which satisfies

$$\sigma(a, b) = \sigma'(af, bf)$$

for all $a, b \in V$. If such an isomorphism exists, $(V, \sigma)$ and $(V', \sigma')$ are \textbf{isomorphic} bilinear spaces.
