With respect to a given bilinear form $\sigma$ on a vector space $V$, two vectors are called orthogonal if the value of $\sigma$ on the ordered pair is zero. This generalizes the Euclidean notion of perpendicular vectors. Note that orthogonality with respect to a bilinear form is not necessarily symmetric unless the form itself is symmetric or alternating.
