A euclidean geometry is an affine space (a coset of a real vector space) equipped with a distance function that arises from a positive definite bilinear form on the underlying vector space. This unifies the synthetic approach to geometry (points, lines, incidence) with the metric structure (distances and angles), allowing the full development of classical euclidean geometry in a rigorous algebraic framework.
