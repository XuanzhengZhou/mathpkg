When the bilinear form is non-degenerate, the orthogonal complement operations $\perp$ and $\top$ are bijective inclusion-reversing maps on the lattice of subspaces. They satisfy dimension complementarity, double complement equals identity, and distribute over sums and intersections. This theorem establishes that $\perp$ and $\top$ form a Galois correspondence with particularly strong properties analogous to those of annihilators in duality theory.
