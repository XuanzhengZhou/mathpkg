Every bilinear form on a vector space $V$ gives rise to two linear maps from $V$ to its dual $V^*$: one obtained by fixing the first argument and varying the second, and the other by fixing the second argument and varying the first. The assignment is a linear isomorphism between the space of bilinear forms and the space of linear maps into the dual. This fundamental connection allows one to translate problems about bilinear forms into problems about linear transformations and vice versa.
