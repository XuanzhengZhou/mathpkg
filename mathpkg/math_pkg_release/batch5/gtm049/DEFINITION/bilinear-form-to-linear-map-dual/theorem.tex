Let $V$ be a vector space over $F$ and let $\mathcal{B}(V)$ be the vector space of all bilinear forms on $V$. Let $\mathcal{L}(V, V^*)$ be the vector space of all linear mappings of $V$ into its dual $V^*$.

For each $\sigma \in \mathcal{B}(V)$, define $\varrho_\sigma: V \to V^*$ by

$$a\varrho_\sigma(b) = \sigma(a, b)$$

for all $a, b \in V$. Then $\sigma \mapsto \varrho_\sigma$ is a linear isomorphism of $\mathcal{B}(V)$ onto $\mathcal{L}(V, V^*)$.

Similarly, define $\tilde{\sigma}: V \to V^*$ by

$$b\tilde{\sigma}(a) = \sigma(a, b).$$

If $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ is an ordered basis of $V$ with dual basis $(a^1, \ldots, a^n)$, then

$$(\varrho_\sigma; (a_i), (a^j)) = (\sigma; (a_i)) = (s_{ij})$$

and

$$(\tilde{\sigma}; (a_i), (a^j)) = (s_{ij})^t.$$

In particular, the ranks of $\varrho_\sigma$ and $\tilde{\sigma}$ are both equal to the rank of $\sigma$.
