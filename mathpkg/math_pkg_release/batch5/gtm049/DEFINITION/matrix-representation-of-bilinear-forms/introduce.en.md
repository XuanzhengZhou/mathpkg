Given an ordered basis, every bilinear form corresponds uniquely to an $n \times n$ matrix $S = (s_{ij})$ where $s_{ij} = \sigma(a_i, a_j)$, and the form evaluates as $\sigma(a, b) = x S y^t$ where $x, y$ are the coordinate rows. This correspondence is a linear isomorphism between $\mathcal{B}(V)$ and $F^{n \times n}$. Equivalently, one may associate a bilinear polynomial $\sum s_{ij} X_i Y_j$.
