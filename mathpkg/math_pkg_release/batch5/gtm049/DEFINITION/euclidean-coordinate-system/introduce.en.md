A Euclidean coordinate system is a distance-preserving affine isomorphism from a Euclidean geometry to the standard model $(\mathbf{R}^n, d_0)$. It provides a way to coordinatize any Euclidean geometry so that distances are given by the standard Pythagorean formula. This is the Euclidean analog of affine coordinate systems studied earlier in the book.
