If $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ is a euclidean geometry of dimension $n$, then a \textbf{euclidean coordinate system} for $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ is a euclidean isomorphism $\alpha$ of $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ onto $\mathcal{A}(\mathbf{R}^n, d_0)$, where $(\mathbf{R}^n, d_0)$ is the standard $n$-dimensional euclidean space.
