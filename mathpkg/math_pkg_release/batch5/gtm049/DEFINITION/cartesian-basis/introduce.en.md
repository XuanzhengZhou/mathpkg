A Cartesian basis is an orthonormal basis for a Euclidean space: the basis vectors are mutually orthogonal and have unit length. With respect to such a basis, the distance takes the familiar Pythagorean form, and the angle between vectors is given by the classical formula involving sums of products of coordinates. The existence of Cartesian bases follows from the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process.
