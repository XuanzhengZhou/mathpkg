An isomorphism $\alpha$ of a euclidean geometry $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ onto a euclidean geometry $\mathcal{A}(S', d')$ is an isomorphism of affine geometries that preserves distance, i.e.,

$$d(u, v) = d'(u\alpha, v\alpha)$$

for all $u, v \in S$.

Such an isomorphism is an affinity whose underlying linear isomorphism is an isomorphism of euclidean spaces.
