Suppose $\dim S \geq 2$ and that $M, M'$ are the subspaces belonging to the cosets $S, S'$. By Theorem 5 of the affine geometry chapter, an affine isomorphism between $S$ and $S'$ induces a linear isomorphism between $M$ and $M'$. Since $\alpha$ preserves distance, for any $a, b \in S$ we have $d(a,b) = d'(a\alpha, b\alpha)$. Writing $a = p + m_1$, $b = p + m_2$, the translation invariance of distance (D.4) gives $d(m_1, m_2) = d'(m_1 f, m_2 f)$ where $f$ is the linear part of $\alpha$. The condition $\sigma(a,b) = \sigma'(af, bf)$ for the bilinear forms is equivalent to the distance-preserving condition, so $f$ is an isomorphism of the euclidean spaces $(M, \sigma)$ and $(M', \sigma')$.
