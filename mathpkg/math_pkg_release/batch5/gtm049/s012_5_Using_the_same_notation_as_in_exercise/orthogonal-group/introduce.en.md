The orthogonal group consists of all linear automorphisms of a Euclidean space that preserve the inner product (bilinear form). It is a subgroup of the general linear group. The determinant homomorphism maps the orthogonal group onto $$\{+1, -1\}$$, with the kernel being the rotation group (special orthogonal group).
