Lemma 2 establishes that every $\sigma$-symmetric linear mapping $f$ on a finite-dimensional real vector space $V$, with $\sigma$ a positive definite symmetric bilinear form, possesses a real eigenvalue. The proof extends $f$ to the complexification $V_{(C)}$ and uses the $\sigma$-symmetric property together with conjugation symmetry to force the eigenvalue to equal its complex conjugate, hence to be real. This lemma is the crucial first step toward the simultaneous diagonalization of two symmetric bilinear forms.
