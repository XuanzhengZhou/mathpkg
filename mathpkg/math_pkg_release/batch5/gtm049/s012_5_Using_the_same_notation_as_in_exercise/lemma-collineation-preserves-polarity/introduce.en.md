Lemma 7 establishes the correspondence between collineations preserving a definite polarity and linear automorphisms preserving the associated distance class. A collineation $\mathcal{P}(g)$ of $\mathcal{P}(H)$ preserves the polarity $\perp(\sigma)$ if and only if $g$ preserves the distance class $D$ determined by $\sigma$. The proof uses the formula $\perp(\sigma^g)\mathcal{P}(g) = \mathcal{P}(g)\perp(\sigma)$ relating the transformed bilinear form to the transformed polarity. This lemma is central to the projective characterization of similarity euclidean isomorphisms.
