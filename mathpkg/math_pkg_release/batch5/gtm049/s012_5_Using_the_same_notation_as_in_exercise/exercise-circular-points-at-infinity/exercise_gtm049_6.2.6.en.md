Let $H$ be a line in a real projective plane $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\perp(\sigma)$ a definite polarity in $\mathcal{P}(H)$. Complexify $V$ and extend $\sigma$ to $\sigma'$ as in $\S 5.6$ (p. 113). Then the quadric $Q(\sigma')$ consists of two points $I$ and $J$. (These are called the "circular points at infinity" for the similarity euclidean plane $(A, \perp)$.)

Given a point $A$ in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ but not in $H$, show that a coordinate system of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ may be chosen so that $A$ has coordinates $(1, 0, 0)$ and in the corresponding coordinate system of $\mathcal{P}(V_{(\mathbb{C})})$, $I$ and $J$ have coordinates $(0, 1, i)$, $(0, 1, -i)$, respectively.

Using this coordinate system, prove the following result: If $B$ and $C$ are points of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ distinct from each other and from $A$, let $B' = AB \cap H$ and $C' = AC \cap H$. Then the real part of $\operatorname{cr}(I, J; B', C')$ is $\cos 2\theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle $\widehat{BAC}$ in the similarity euclidean plane $(A, \perp)$.
