Let $(A, \bot)$ be a similarity euclidean geometry. Show that the orthogonal reflections correspond to involutory collineations of the projective geometry at infinity $\mathcal{P}(H)$ preserving the polarity $\bot$. Prove that the set of points at infinity fixed by such an involution forms a non-degenerate quadric, called the absolute quadric or the circular points at infinity.
