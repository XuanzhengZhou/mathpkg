Let $$H$$ be a line in a real projective plane $$\mathcal{P}(V)$$ and $$\perp(\sigma)$$ a definite polarity in $$\mathcal{P}(H)$$. Complexify $$V$$ and extend $$\sigma$$ to $$\sigma$$. The quadric $$Q(\sigma)$$ consists of two points $$I$$ and $$J$$, called the **circular points at infinity** for the similarity Euclidean plane $$(A, \perp)$$.
