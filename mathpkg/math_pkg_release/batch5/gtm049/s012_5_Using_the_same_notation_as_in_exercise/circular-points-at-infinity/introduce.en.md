The circular points at infinity are a pair of complex conjugate points on the line at infinity of a real projective plane, obtained by complexifying the definite polarity that defines the Euclidean structure. These points are fundamental to interpreting Euclidean geometry projectively: angles can be expressed in terms of cross-ratios involving the circular points, and circles are conics passing through both circular points.
