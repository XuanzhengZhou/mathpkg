This theorem gives the complete structure of orthogonal transformations on a Euclidean space. Every such automorphism decomposes the space into: (1) two-dimensional invariant subspaces where it acts as a rotation by angle $$\theta_k$$, (2) a subspace where it acts as the identity ($$+1$$), and (3) a subspace where it acts as $$-1$$. The proof uses induction based on Lemmas 4-6.
