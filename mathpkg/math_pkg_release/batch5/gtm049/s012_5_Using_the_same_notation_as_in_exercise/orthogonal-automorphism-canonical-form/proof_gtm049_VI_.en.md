Let $$c$$ be an eigenvector of $$f$$ (extended to $$V_{(C)}$$) with eigenvalue $$e^{i\theta}$$.

If $$e^{i\theta}$$ is real ($$\theta = 0$$ or $$\pi$$), set $$M = [c]$$ (a one-dimensional invariant subspace of $$V$$).

If $$e^{i\theta}$$ is not real, set $$M = T$$ as in Lemma 5 (a two-dimensional invariant subspace).

In either case, by Proposition 5 of Chapter V (p. 96), $$V = M \oplus M^\perp$$ since $$M$$ is non-degenerate. By Lemma 6, $$Mf = M$$ and $$M^\perp f = M^\perp$$. The existence of a Cartesian basis of the required form now follows by induction on $$\dim V$$.

The invariants $$r, s, t, |\theta_1|, \ldots, |\theta_r|$$ are determined by $$f$$ (they correspond to the Segre symbol and eigenvalue data of the complexified mapping).
