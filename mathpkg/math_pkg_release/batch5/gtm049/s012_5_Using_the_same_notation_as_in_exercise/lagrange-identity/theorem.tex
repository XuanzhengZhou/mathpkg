In a euclidean space $(V, \sigma)$ with norm $\|a\| = \sqrt{\sigma(a,a)}$, the Lagrange identity holds:
$$\|u\|^2 \|v\|^2 - \sigma(u,v)^2 = \sum_{i<j} (u_i v_j - u_j v_i)^2$$
where $(u_i), (v_i)$ are coordinates with respect to a cartesian basis. In particular, $\sigma(u,v)^2 \leq \|u\|^2 \|v\|^2$ (Schwarz inequality), with equality iff $u$ and $v$ are linearly dependent.
