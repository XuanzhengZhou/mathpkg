The Lagrange identity expresses the difference $\|u\|^2\|v\|^2 - \sigma(u,v)^2$ as a sum of squares, providing an algebraic proof of the Schwarz inequality. In geometric terms, this difference equals the squared area of the parallelogram spanned by $u$ and $v$, connecting the algebraic identity to the geometric concept of area.
