The spectral theorem for normal mappings on a finite-dimensional Hilbert space states that every normal mapping can be diagonalized by a Cartesian (orthonormal) basis. Normal mappings include Hermitian (self-adjoint) mappings and unitary mappings as special cases. The theorem generalizes the real symmetric case and is proved by induction using the properties of the adjoint $$f^*$$.
