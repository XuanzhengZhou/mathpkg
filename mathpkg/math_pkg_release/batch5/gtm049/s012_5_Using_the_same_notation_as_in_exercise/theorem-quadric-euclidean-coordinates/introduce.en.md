Theorem 2 concerns the classification of quadrics in euclidean geometry. Given a real projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ with a distinguished hyperplane $H$ at infinity, a positive definite bilinear form $\sigma$ on $H$ determining a euclidean geometry, and a projective quadric $Q(\tau)$, there exists a euclidean coordinate system such that the cross-section of $Q(\tau)$ by the affine space $\mathcal{A}(c+H)$ is expressed in diagonal form. This result refines the projective classification by requiring the coordinate system to be euclidean, and relies on Theorem 1 for simultaneous orthogonalization.
