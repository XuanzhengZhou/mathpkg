Let $\mathcal{P}(V)$ be an $n$-dimensional real projective geometry, $H$ a hyperplane in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\sigma$ a positive definite bilinear form on $H$. Let the euclidean geometry be $\mathcal{A}(c + H, d)$ for some $c$ not in $H$, where $d$ is the distance determined by $\sigma$. Let $Q(\tau)$ be a projective quadric in $\mathcal{P}(V)$.

Then there exists a euclidean frame of reference for $\mathcal{A}(c + H, d)$ with respect to which the cross-section $R$ of $Q(\tau)$ by $\mathcal{A}(c + H, d)$ has its equation in diagonal form.
