An eigenvector of a linear mapping is a non-zero vector that is mapped to a scalar multiple of itself. The scalar is the associated eigenvalue. In projective geometry, eigenvectors correspond to fixed points of the induced collineation. The concept is fundamental to the simultaneous diagonalization of symmetric bilinear forms and the structure theory of orthogonal transformations.
