Let $$V$$ be a vector space over a field $$F$$ and $$f$$ a linear mapping of $$V$$ into $$V$$. A non-zero vector $$a$$ of $$V$$ is an **eigenvector** of $$f$$ if $$[a]f \subset [a]$$; equivalently, if there exists a scalar $$x$$ in $$F$$ such that $$af = xa$$. The scalar $$x$$ is called the **eigenvalue** of $$f$$ corresponding to $$a$$.
