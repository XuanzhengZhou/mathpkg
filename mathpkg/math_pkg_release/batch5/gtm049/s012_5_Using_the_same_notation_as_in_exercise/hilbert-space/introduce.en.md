A finite-dimensional Hilbert space is a complex vector space equipped with a positive definite Hermitian inner product. It is the complex analogue of a Euclidean space. Hilbert spaces arise naturally from Euclidean spaces via complexification. Length (norm) and distance are defined exactly as in the Euclidean case, using the Hermitian form.
