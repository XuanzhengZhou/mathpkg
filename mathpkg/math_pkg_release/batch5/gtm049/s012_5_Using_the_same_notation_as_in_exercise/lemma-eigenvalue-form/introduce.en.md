Lemma 4 characterizes the eigenvalues of an automorphism $f$ of a euclidean space $(V, \sigma)$. Extending $f$ to the complexification $V_{(C)}$ (which becomes a Hilbert space with the hermitian form $\sigma'$), every eigenvalue of the extended $f$ lies on the unit circle: $x = e^{i\theta}$ for some real $\theta$ with $-\pi < \theta \leq \pi$. The proof uses the fact that $f$ preserves the hermitian form $\sigma'$: substituting $cf = xc$ into $\sigma'(\bar{c}f, cf) = \sigma'(\bar{c}, c)$ forces $\bar{x}x = 1$.
