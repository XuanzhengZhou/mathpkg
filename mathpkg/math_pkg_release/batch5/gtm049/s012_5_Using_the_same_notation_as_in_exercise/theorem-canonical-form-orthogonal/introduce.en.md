Theorem 3 gives the canonical form of an automorphism $f$ of a euclidean space $(V, \sigma)$. There exists a cartesian basis of $(V, \sigma)$ with respect to which the matrix of $f$ is block diagonal, consisting of $2 \times 2$ rotation blocks (for non-real eigenvalues $e^{\pm i\theta_k}$), $+1$ blocks (for eigenvalue $1$), and $-1$ blocks (for eigenvalue $-1$). The integers $r, s, t$ counting the numbers of each block type, together with the absolute rotation angles $|\theta_k|$, are invariants of $f$. The proof proceeds by induction on dimension using Lemmas 4-6.
