Proposition 4 states that for a $\sigma$-symmetric linear mapping $f$ on a euclidean space $(V, \sigma)$, one can find an eigenvector $a_1$ normalized so that $\sigma(a_1, a_1) = 1$, and then decompose $V$ as the orthogonal direct sum of the line $[a_1]$ and its orthogonal complement. By Lemma 3 (which states that the restriction of a $\sigma$-symmetric mapping to an invariant non-degenerate subspace remains $\sigma$-symmetric) and induction on dimension, this yields a cartesian basis of eigenvectors for $f$.
