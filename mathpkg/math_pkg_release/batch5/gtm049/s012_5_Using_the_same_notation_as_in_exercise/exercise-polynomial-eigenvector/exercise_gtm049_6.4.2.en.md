If $a$ is a given non-zero vector in a complex vector space $V$ and $f$ is a linear mapping of $V$ into $V$, prove that there exists a polynomial $p(X)$ such that $ap(f)$ is an eigenvector of $f$.

(Consider the vectors $af^i$, $i = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$ and proceed as in the proof of Proposition 3.)
