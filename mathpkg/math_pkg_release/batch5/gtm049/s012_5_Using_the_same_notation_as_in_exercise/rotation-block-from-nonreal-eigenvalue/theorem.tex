To each eigenvector $$c$$ of an orthogonal automorphism $$f$$ with non-real eigenvalue $$e^{i\theta}$$ there corresponds a two-dimensional subspace $$T$$ of $$V$$ with $$Tf = T$$ and a Cartesian basis $$(u, v)$$ of $$(T, \sigma_T)$$ such that
$$(f; (u, v)) = \begin{pmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{pmatrix}.$$
