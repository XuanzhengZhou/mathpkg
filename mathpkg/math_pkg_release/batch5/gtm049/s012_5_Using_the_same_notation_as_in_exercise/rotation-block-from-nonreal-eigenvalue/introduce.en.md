Non-real eigenvalues of an orthogonal automorphism come in conjugate pairs and produce two-dimensional invariant subspaces on which the automorphism acts as a rotation. The proof decomposes the complex eigenvector $$c = a + ib$$ into real and imaginary parts to construct the real two-dimensional invariant subspace and the Cartesian basis yielding the rotation matrix.
