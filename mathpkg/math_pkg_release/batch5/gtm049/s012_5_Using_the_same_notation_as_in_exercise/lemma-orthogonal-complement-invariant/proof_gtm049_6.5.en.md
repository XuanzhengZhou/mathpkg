For any $a$ in $M$ and $b$ in $M^\perp$ we have $af^{-1} \in M$ (since $Mf = M$ implies $Mf^{-1} = M$) and so

$$\sigma(a, bf) = \sigma(af^{-1}, b) = 0.$$

Thus $(M^\perp)f \subset M^\perp$. Equality follows because $f$ is one-one, so the restriction of $f$ to $M^\perp$ has image of the same dimension as $M^\perp$.
