Two distance functions on the same affine geometry are similar if one is a positive scalar multiple of the other. This defines an equivalence relation whose classes capture the notion of "same shape up to scaling." Similarity classes are the foundation for similarity Euclidean geometry, generalizing Euclidean geometry by replacing a fixed distance with an equivalence class of distances.
