Let $$A_0, A_1, A_2, A_3$$ be distinct collinear points in a projective geometry $$\mathcal{P}(V)$$ over a field $$F$$. Choose homogeneous vectors such that $$[a_0] = A_0, [a_1] = A_1, [a_0 + a_1] = A_2$$ and $$[x a_0 + a_1] = A_3$$. The scalar $$x$$ is uniquely determined and is called the **cross-ratio** of the quadruple, written $$\operatorname{cr}(A_0, A_1; A_2, A_3)$$.
