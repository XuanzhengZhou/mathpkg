Let $$H$$ be a line in a real projective plane $$\mathcal{P}(V)$$ and $$\bot(\sigma)$$ a definite polarity in $$\mathcal{P}(H)$$. Complexify $$V$$ and extend $$\sigma$$ to $$\sigma'$$ as in §5.6 (p. 113). Then the quadric $$Q(\sigma')$$ consists of two points $$I$$ and $$J$$ (the circular points at infinity).

Given a point $$A$$ in $$\mathcal{P}(V)$$ but not in $$H$$, show that a coordinate system of $$\mathcal{P}(V)$$ may be chosen so that $$A$$ has coordinates $$(1, 0, 0)$$ and in the corresponding coordinate system of $$\mathcal{P}(V_{(C)})$$, $$I$$ and $$J$$ have coordinates $$(0, 1, i)$$, $$(0, 1, -i)$$, respectively.

Using this coordinate system, prove: If $$B$$ and $$C$$ are points of $$\mathcal{P}(V)$$ distinct from each other and from $$A$$, let $$B' = AB \cap H$$ and $$C' = AC \cap H$$. Then the real part of $$\operatorname{cr}(I, J; B', C')$$ is $$\cos 2\theta$$, where $$\theta$$ is the angle $$\widehat{BAC}$$ in the similarity Euclidean plane $$(A, \bot)$$.
