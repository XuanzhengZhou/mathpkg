In similarity euclidean geometry, perpendicularity is defined projectively via the polarity at infinity. Two affine subspaces are perpendicular if their intersections with the hyperplane at infinity are orthogonal with respect to the definite polarity $\bot$. This definition is independent of the choice of representative distance in the similarity class, making perpendicularity a similarity-invariant notion.
