This corollary restates Theorem 3 in matrix language: every orthogonal matrix $A$ can be brought to the canonical block-diagonal form by an orthogonal change of basis. The transforming matrix $P$ is the change-of-basis matrix between two cartesian bases. This is the classical result that every orthogonal transformation can be expressed, in suitable orthonormal coordinates, as a direct sum of planar rotations, identity transformations, and reflections.
