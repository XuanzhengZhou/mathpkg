A $$\sigma$$-symmetric linear mapping is one that is self-adjoint with respect to the bilinear form $$\sigma$$. Such mappings arise naturally when studying pairs of symmetric bilinear forms: if $$\sigma$$ and $$\tau$$ are given, then the mapping $$f = \tau\sigma^{-1}$$ is $$\sigma$$-symmetric. In a Euclidean space, $$\sigma$$-symmetric mappings correspond to symmetric matrices in any Cartesian basis.
