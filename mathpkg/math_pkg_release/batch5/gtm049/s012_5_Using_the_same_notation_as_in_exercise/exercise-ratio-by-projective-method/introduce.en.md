This exercise asks for a purely projective method to compute the ratio of distances between two pairs of points in a similarity euclidean geometry. The key insight is that while absolute distances are not projective invariants, ratios of distances can be expressed via cross-ratios and the polarity structure at infinity, using the results of previous exercises on orthogonal reflections and the relation between bilinear forms and distance classes.
