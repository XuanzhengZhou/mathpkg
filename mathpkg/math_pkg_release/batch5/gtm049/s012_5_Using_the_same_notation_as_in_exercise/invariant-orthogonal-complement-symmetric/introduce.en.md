For a $$\sigma$$-symmetric mapping on a Euclidean space, the orthogonal complement of an invariant subspace is also invariant. This lemma, combined with the existence of a real eigenvector, enables an inductive construction of a Cartesian basis consisting entirely of eigenvectors, which is the key to the simultaneous diagonalization theorem.
