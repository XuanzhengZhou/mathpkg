Let $(\mathsf{A}, \perp)$ be the similarity euclidean geometry in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ determined by $H$ and the definite polarity $\perp$ of $\mathcal{P}(H)$. Denote by $(\Pr \mathcal{P}(V))_{H,\perp}$ the subgroup of the projective group $\Pr \mathcal{P}(V)$ whose elements restrict to collineations of $\mathcal{P}(H)$ preserving $\perp$. Then the isomorphism of $(\Pr \mathcal{P}(V))_{H}$ onto $\mathsf{A}$ (defined in Theorem 7 of Chapter III, p. 61) restricts to an isomorphism

$$(\Pr \mathcal{P}(V))_{H,\perp} \cong \operatorname{Aut}(\mathsf{A}, \perp).$$
