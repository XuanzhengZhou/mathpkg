Every $$\sigma$$-symmetric linear mapping on a Euclidean space can be diagonalized by a Cartesian basis. The proof proceeds by induction: find one real eigenvector, normalize it, take its orthogonal complement (which is invariant), and apply the induction hypothesis. This result is the linear algebra foundation for the principal axis theorem.
