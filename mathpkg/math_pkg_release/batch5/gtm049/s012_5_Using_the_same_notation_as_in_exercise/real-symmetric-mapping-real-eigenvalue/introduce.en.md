For a $$\sigma$$-symmetric mapping on a Euclidean space, every eigenvalue must be real. The proof extends $$f$$ to the complexification and uses the $$\sigma$$-symmetry condition to show that any complex eigenvalue $$x$$ satisfies $$\bar{x} = x$$, forcing $$x$$ to be real. This lemma is the crucial step toward simultaneous diagonalization of symmetric forms.
