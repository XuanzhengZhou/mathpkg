For sesquilinear forms, the property of orthosymmetry -- that the left and right orthogonality relations coincide -- imposes strong structural constraints. When the rank is at least two, the only possibilities are alternating forms (where the automorphism must be the identity, so the form is actually bilinear) or scalar multiples of hermitian forms. This parallels the classification of orthosymmetric bilinear forms as either symmetric or alternating, but with the richer structure that non-trivial field automorphisms allow.
