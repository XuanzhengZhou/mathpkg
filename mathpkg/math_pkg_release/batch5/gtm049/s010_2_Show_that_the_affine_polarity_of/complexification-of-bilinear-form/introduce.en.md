Given a real bilinear form on a real vector space, there is a natural way to extend it to a sesquilinear form on the complexified vector space. The extension respects complex conjugation and preserves the key property of positive definiteness. This complexification bridges the gap between the purely bilinear theory over the reals and the richer sesquilinear theory over the complex numbers, where hermitian forms can be studied.
