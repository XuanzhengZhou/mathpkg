Let $F$ be a field, $\zeta$ an automorphism of $F$, and $f \colon V \to V^*$ a semi-linear mapping with respect to $\zeta$. Define $\tau \colon V \times V \to F$ by
$$\tau(a, b) = a(bf)$$
for all $a, b \in V$. Then $\tau$ is called a \text{right sesquilinear form} with respect to $\zeta$. It is semi-linear in the second variable and linear in the first; explicitly, for all $a, b, c \in V$ and all $x, y \in F$:
\begin{align*}
\tau(a, xb + yc) &= (x\zeta)\tau(a, b) + (y\zeta)\tau(a, c), \\
\tau(xa + yb, c) &= x\tau(a, c) + y\tau(b, c).
\end{align*}
