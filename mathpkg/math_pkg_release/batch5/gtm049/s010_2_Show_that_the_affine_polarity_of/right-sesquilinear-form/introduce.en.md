A right sesquilinear form is the mirror of a left sesquilinear form, obtained by interchanging which variable carries the field automorphism. The theory of right sesquilinear forms is exactly analogous to that of left sesquilinear forms, so one typically studies only the left variety. Both reduce to ordinary bilinear forms when the automorphism is the identity.
