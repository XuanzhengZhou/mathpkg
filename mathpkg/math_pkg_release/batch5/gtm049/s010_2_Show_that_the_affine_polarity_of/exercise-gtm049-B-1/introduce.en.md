This exercise extends the matrix representation of bilinear forms to sesquilinear forms. For a sesquilinear form $\sigma$ with respect to a field automorphism $\zeta$, the matrix $S$ satisfies $\sigma(a,b) = (x\zeta) S y^t$, where $x\zeta$ means the automorphism is applied componentwise to the coordinate row. This generalizes the bilinear formula $\sigma(a,b) = x S y^t$.
