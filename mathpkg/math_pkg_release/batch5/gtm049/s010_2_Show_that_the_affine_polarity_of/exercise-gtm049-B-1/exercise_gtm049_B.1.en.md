**Notation.** If $A = (a_{ij})$ is a matrix with elements $a_{ij}$ in $F$ and if $\zeta$ is an automorphism of $F$, then $A\zeta$ denotes the matrix $(a_{ij}\zeta)$.

Let $\sigma$ be a sesquilinear form with respect to $\zeta$ on $V$ and $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ an ordered basis of $V$. Define the matrix $S$ of $\sigma$ with respect to $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ as for bilinear forms. Prove that if

$$x = (x_1, \ldots, x_n), \quad y = (y_1, \ldots, y_n)$$

are the coordinate rows of $a, b$ with respect to $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$, then

$$\sigma(a, b) = (x\zeta)Sy^t.$$
