In a Hilbert space $(W, \sigma)$, the nondegenerate hermitian form $\sigma$ allows one to represent any sesquilinear form $\tau$ by a linear endomorphism $f$. The mapping $f$ is defined implicitly by the identity $\sigma(af, b) = \tau(a, b)$ for all vectors $a, b$. This correspondence between sesquilinear forms and linear operators is fundamental to the study of adjoints and normal mappings on Hilbert spaces.
