Given a sesquilinear form $\tau$ on a finite dimensional complex Hilbert space, one can find a cartesian basis with respect to which $\tau$ assumes triangular (upper or lower) matrix form. This corollary follows from representing $\tau$ by a linear operator via the hermitian form and then applying the fact that every linear operator on a finite dimensional complex vector space admits a triangular matrix in some orthonormal (cartesian) basis.
