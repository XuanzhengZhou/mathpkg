The spectral theorem for normal mappings on a finite-dimensional Hilbert space generalizes both the Hermitian (self-adjoint) and unitary cases. A normal mapping is one that commutes with its adjoint. The theorem asserts the existence of a Cartesian basis of eigenvectors, providing the complete diagonalization of normal mappings.
