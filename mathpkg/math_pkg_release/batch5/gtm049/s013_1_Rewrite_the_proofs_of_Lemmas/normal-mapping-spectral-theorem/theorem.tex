If $$f$$ is a normal mapping on the Hilbert space $$(W, \sigma)$$ (i.e., $$ff^* = f^*f$$ where $$f^*$$ is the adjoint with respect to $$\sigma$$), then there exists a Cartesian basis of $$(W, \sigma)$$ all of whose vectors are eigenvectors of $$f$$.

A mapping $$f$$ is:
- $$\sigma$$-symmetric (Hermitian) if, and only if, $$f^* = f$$;
- an automorphism of $$(W, \sigma)$$ (unitary) if, and only if, $$ff^* = 1$$.

Both are special cases of normal mappings.
