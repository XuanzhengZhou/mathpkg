For any linear mapping $f$ on a Hilbert space, its adjoint $f^*$ (defined by $\sigma(af, b) = \sigma(a, bf^*)$) preserves the norm: $\|wf\| = \|wf^*\|$ for every vector $w$. This fundamental property reflects the symmetry between $f$ and $f^*$ and is a key step toward understanding normal and unitary operators on Hilbert spaces.
