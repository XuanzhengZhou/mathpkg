A Hilbert space $(W, \sigma)$ is called \emph{complete} if it satisfies the following property: whenever $\{a_n\}$ is a sequence of vectors in $W$ such that
$$
\|a_m - a_n\| \to 0 \quad \text{as } m, n \to \infty,
$$
then there exists a vector $a \in W$ such that
$$
\|a - a_n\| \to 0 \quad \text{as } n \to \infty.
$$
