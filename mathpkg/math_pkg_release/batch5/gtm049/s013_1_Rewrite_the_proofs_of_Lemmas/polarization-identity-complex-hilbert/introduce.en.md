The polarization identity expresses the hermitian inner product $\sigma(a, b)$ of a complex Hilbert space entirely in terms of the norm induced by $\sigma$. This identity generalizes the real polarization identity and involves four squared norms evaluated at $a \pm b$ and $a \pm ib$. It shows that the inner product structure is fully determined by the norm geometry, and conversely, if a distance function satisfies certain axioms, this equation can be used to define a positive definite hermitian form on the space.
