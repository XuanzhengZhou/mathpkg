If a one-dimensional subspace $[w]$ spanned by a vector $w$ is invariant under a linear mapping $f$ (so $wf \in [w]$), then its orthogonal complement $[w]^\perp$ is also invariant under $f$. This lemma is a crucial tool for proofs by induction on the dimension of a Hilbert space, allowing one to reduce the study of an operator to an invariant subspace and its orthogonal complement.
