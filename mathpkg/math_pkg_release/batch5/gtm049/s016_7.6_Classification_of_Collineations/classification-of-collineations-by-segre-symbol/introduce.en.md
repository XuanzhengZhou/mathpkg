Theorem 5 establishes that the Segre symbol is a complete invariant for the projective equivalence class of a collineation (when the minimum polynomial splits). Two collineations share the same Segre symbol precisely when some projective collineation sends the full lattice of invariant subspaces of the first onto that of the second. The proof decomposes the module $V_f$ into its primary components $K_i$ (the generalized eigenspaces), constructs module isomorphisms $e_i: K_i \to L_i$ using the equality of elementary divisors, and shows the combined isomorphism $e$ respects the invariant configuration of each primary component, hence the whole invariant configuration.
