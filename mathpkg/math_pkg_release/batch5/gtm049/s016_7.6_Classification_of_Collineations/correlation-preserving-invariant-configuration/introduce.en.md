Theorem 6 shows that for any collineation $\mathcal{P}(f)$ there exists a correlation (a duality of the projective space) that leaves the invariant configuration of $\mathcal{P}(f)$ globally fixed. The proof uses the module isomorphism $g: V_f \to (V^*)_{f^t}$ supplied by Proposition 10 and verifies, via the defining relation $vfg = vgf^t$, that the associated correlation $\mathcal{P}(g)_\circ$ maps $f$-invariant subspaces to $f$-invariant subspaces and conversely. This result is especially useful when the Segre symbol is not defined, providing an alternative approach to analyzing collineations without invoking elementary divisor theory.
