This theorem establishes that the Segre symbol is a complete invariant for the projective equivalence class of a collineation. Two collineations of a projective space have projectively equivalent invariant configurations (meaning one can be mapped onto the other by a projective collineation) precisely when they share the same Segre symbol. The proof constructs an explicit module isomorphism from the equality of elementary divisors, then verifies that it respects the invariant subspace lattice, yielding the required collineation via the fundamental theorem of projective geometry.
