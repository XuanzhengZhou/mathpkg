Let $F$ be a finite field with $p^{n+1}$ elements and let $a$ be a generator of the cyclic group $F^*$. (See exercise 11 of § 7.3.) Prove that

$$f \colon x \to xa$$

is an automorphism of $F$, viewed as a vector space over $F_p$, and that $f$ has order $p^{n+1}-1$. Deduce that $\mathcal{P}(f)$ is a collineation of $\mathcal{P}(F)$ of order $1+p+\cdots+p^n$.

(Observe that $1+p+\cdots+p^n$ is the number of points in $\mathcal{P}(F)$ (cf. exercise 5 of § 2.5, p. 32). Now, by Theorem 6 of Chapter II, p. 32, any projective geometry $P$ of dimension $n$ over $F_p$ is isomorphic to $\mathcal{P}(F)$. Hence this exercise implies that there exists a collineation that permutes all the points of $P$ cyclically. In fact this result is true for all projective geometries over $F_p$ because there exist fields with $p^{n+1}$ elements for all $n \geq 0$.)
