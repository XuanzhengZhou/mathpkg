If $f$ is an element of $\mathcal{L}(V, V^*)$ with non-zero kernel, then $\mathcal{P}(f)$ is a degenerate projectivity and $\mathcal{P}(f) \circ$ is called a \textbf{degenerate correlation}. Any degenerate correlation is of the form $\bot(\sigma)$ (or $\top(\sigma)$), where $\sigma$ is a degenerate bilinear form, and is called a \textbf{degenerate polarity} when $\sigma$ is respectively symmetric or alternating.
