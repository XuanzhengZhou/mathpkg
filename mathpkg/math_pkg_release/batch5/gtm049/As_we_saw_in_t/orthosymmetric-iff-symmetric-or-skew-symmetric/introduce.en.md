Proposition 6 provides the complete classification of orthosymmetric bilinear forms: a bilinear form is orthosymmetric precisely when it is either symmetric ($\sigma(a,b) = \sigma(b,a)$) or skew-symmetric ($\sigma(a,b) = -\sigma(b,a)$). This fundamental result establishes that the only bilinear forms for which orthogonality is a symmetric relation are the symmetric and skew-symmetric ones.
