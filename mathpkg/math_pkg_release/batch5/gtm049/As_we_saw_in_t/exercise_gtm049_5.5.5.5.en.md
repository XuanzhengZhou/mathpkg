Let $A$ be the affine geometry in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ determined by a hyperplane $H$ and let $\perp$ be a polarity of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. We consider the subset $B$ of $A$ obtained by omitting all elements of $A$ which contain the point $H^\perp$. Let $\perp'$ be the restriction of $\perp$ to $B$. We call $\perp'$ the 	extbf{affine polarity} (of $A$) determined by $\perp$.

Prove that $\perp'$ is a one-to-one mapping of $B$ onto $B$ and that $\perp$ is the only polarity of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ which determines $\perp'$.

(We call $H^\perp$ the 	extbf{center} of $\perp'$. Note that the center need not lie in $A$.)
