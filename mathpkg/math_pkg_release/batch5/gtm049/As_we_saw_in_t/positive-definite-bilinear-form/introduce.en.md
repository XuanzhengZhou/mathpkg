A real symmetric bilinear form is positive definite if the associated quadratic form takes only positive values on non-zero vectors. Positive definiteness is the key property that distinguishes Euclidean geometry from general metric geometry — it ensures that "lengths" are positive and that the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality holds. The concepts of positive and negative (semi-)definiteness provide the vocabulary for Sylvester's Law of Inertia.
