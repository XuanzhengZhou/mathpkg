An affine quadric $Q'$ not contained in a hyperplane has a unique projective completion $Q$; the points of $Q$ in the hyperplane at infinity are determined by $Q'$.

More precisely: a point $P$ of the hyperplane at infinity $H$ lies on the completing projective quadric $Q$ if, and only if, there is a line through $P$ meeting $Q'$ in exactly one point (rather than two).
