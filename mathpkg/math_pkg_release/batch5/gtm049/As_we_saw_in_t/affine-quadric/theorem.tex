Let $M$ be a subspace of a vector space $V$ over a field $F$. If $q$ is a quadratic form on $M$, $l$ a linear form on $M$, and $z$ a scalar in $F$, then the set of all points $\{a\}$ in the affine geometry $\mathcal{A}(M)$ for which

$$q(a) + l(a) + z = 0$$

is called an \textbf{affine quadric} in $\mathcal{A}(M)$.
