The special cases where $\operatorname{pdim} V$ equals 0 or 1 are obvious. For $\operatorname{pdim} V \geq 2$, let $\pi: \mathcal{P}(V) 	o \mathcal{P}(V')$ be a projective isomorphism. By the Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry, $\pi$ is induced by a semilinear bijection $f: V 	o V'$. If $Q(\sigma)$ is a quadric in $\mathcal{P}(V)$, then $\pi(Q(\sigma)) = Q(\sigma')$ where $\sigma'(f(a), f(b)) = \sigma(a,b)$ (up to the field automorphism). The rank is preserved because $f$ is a bijection and maps bases to bases.
