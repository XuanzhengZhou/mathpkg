Let $\perp(\sigma)$ be a polarity of an $n$-dimensional projective geometry $\mathcal{P}$. A simplex of reference $(A_0, \ldots, A_n)$ is called 	extbf{self-polar} if, for each $i$, the polar hyperplane $A_i^\perp$ of $A_i$ is the join of all the other vertices $A_j$, $j 
eq i$.

Interpret the proof of Theorem 3 (in the case where $\sigma$ is a non-degenerate symmetric bilinear form and $F$ is not of characteristic 2) as the construction of a self-polar simplex of reference.
