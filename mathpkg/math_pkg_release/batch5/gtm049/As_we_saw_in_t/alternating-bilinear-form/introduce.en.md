An alternating bilinear form is one for which every vector is self-orthogonal: $\sigma(a,a) = 0$ for all vectors $a$. Every alternating form is necessarily skew-symmetric, and over fields of characteristic not equal to 2, the converse also holds. Alternating spaces (also called symplectic spaces) form the foundation of symplectic geometry and are characterized by the existence of a standard hyperbolic basis.
