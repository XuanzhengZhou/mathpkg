For a real symmetric bilinear form $\sigma$ decomposed as $V = P \oplus Q \oplus V^\perp$ where $\sigma|_P$ is positive definite and $\sigma|_Q$ is negative definite, the integer

$$p - q$$

where $p = \dim P$ and $q = \dim Q$, is called the \textbf{signature} of $\sigma$ (or of the associated quadratic form).

Sylvester's Law implies that there are essentially (i.e., to within isomorphism) $n+1$ distinct non-degenerate real symmetric spaces of dimension $n$.
