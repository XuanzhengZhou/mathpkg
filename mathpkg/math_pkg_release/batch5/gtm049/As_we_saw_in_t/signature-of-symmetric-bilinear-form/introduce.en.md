The signature $p - q$ of a real symmetric bilinear form is the difference between the number of positive and negative squares in any diagonalization. By Sylvester's Law of Inertia, the signature is well-defined and is a complete invariant for non-degenerate real symmetric bilinear forms. For example, the "spacetime" of special relativity has rank 4 and signature 2 (corresponding to $p=3$, $q=1$).
