Let $\sigma$ be a symmetric bilinear form on a vector space $V$ over a field $F$ not of characteristic 2. The set of all points $[a]$ of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ for which $\sigma(a, a) = 0$ is called a \textbf{(projective) quadric} and is denoted by $Q(\sigma)$.

If $S = (\sigma; (a_i))$ is the matrix of $\sigma$ with respect to an ordered basis, then the equation of the quadric in the corresponding projective coordinate system is

$$X S X^t = 0$$

where $X = (X_0, \ldots, X_n)$.
