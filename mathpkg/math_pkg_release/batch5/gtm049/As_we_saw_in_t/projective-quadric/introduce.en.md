A projective quadric is the set of isotropic points (points $[a]$ with $\sigma(a,a) = 0$) of a symmetric bilinear form in a projective space. Quadrics generalize conics to higher dimensions and are fundamental objects in projective geometry. They are defined by homogeneous quadratic equations. Over fields of characteristic not 2, there is a one-to-one correspondence between symmetric bilinear forms (up to scalar) and quadrics (that are not contained in a hyperplane).
