A polarity is a correlation (an incidence-reversing bijection of the projective space) that comes from a symmetric bilinear form. The image $M^\perp$ of a subspace under a polarity is called its polar. There are no null-polarities in even-dimensional projective geometries and essentially only one in each odd-dimensional projective geometry. The geometry of polarities is considerably richer than that of null-polarities and leads to the theory of projective quadrics.
