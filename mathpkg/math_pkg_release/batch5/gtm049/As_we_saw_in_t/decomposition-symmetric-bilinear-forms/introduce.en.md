Theorem 3 establishes the fundamental structure theorem for symmetric bilinear forms that are not alternating: the vector space decomposes as an orthogonal direct sum of one-dimensional non-degenerate subspaces plus the radical. Over fields of characteristic not 2, this means any symmetric bilinear form can be diagonalized. This theorem is the foundation for the classification of symmetric bilinear forms, Sylvester's Law of Inertia, and the study of quadratic forms.
