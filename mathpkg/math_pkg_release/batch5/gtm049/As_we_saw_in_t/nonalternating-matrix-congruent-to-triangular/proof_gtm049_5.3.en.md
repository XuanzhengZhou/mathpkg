By Theorem 4, there exists a basis with respect to which the bilinear form $\sigma$ corresponding to $A$ satisfies $\sigma(A_i, A_j) = 0$ for all $i < j$. In this basis, the matrix of $\sigma$ has zeros above the diagonal, i.e., it is lower triangular. Since this matrix is congruent to $A$, the result follows.
