Prove that $q: x \mapsto xar{x}$ is a quadratic form on $\mathbf{C}$, viewed as a two-dimensional real vector space (where the bar denotes complex conjugation). Show further that, when $q$ is restricted to the multiplicative group $\mathbf{C}^*$, it is a group homomorphism.
