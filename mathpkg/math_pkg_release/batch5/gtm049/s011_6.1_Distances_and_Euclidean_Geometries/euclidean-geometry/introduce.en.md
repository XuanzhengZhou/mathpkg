A euclidean geometry is an affine space whose underlying vector space carries a euclidean distance. The distance on the coset $S = a + M$ is defined by transporting the distance on the subspace $M$ via translation invariance. This construction extends the metric structure from the vector space level to the affine geometric setting, forming the proper domain for Euclidean geometry.
