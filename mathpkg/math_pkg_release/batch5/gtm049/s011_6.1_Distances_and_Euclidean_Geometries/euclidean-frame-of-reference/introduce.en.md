A Euclidean frame of reference is the geometric counterpart of a coordinate system, consisting of $n+1$ points: an origin $Q$ and $n$ points $P_i$ whose position vectors relative to $Q$ form a cartesian basis. This provides a concrete geometric description of the coordinate axes. The condition $(p_1 - q, \ldots, p_n - q)$ being a cartesian basis is both necessary and sufficient.
