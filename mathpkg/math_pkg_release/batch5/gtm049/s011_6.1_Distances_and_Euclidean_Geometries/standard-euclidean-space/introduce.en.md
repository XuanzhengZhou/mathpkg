The standard Euclidean space $(\mathbf{R}^n, \sigma_0)$ is the canonical $n$-dimensional Euclidean space, where $\sigma_0$ is the usual dot product. Its standard basis $(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ forms a cartesian basis, and the induced distance $d_0$ is the familiar Euclidean distance. Every $n$-dimensional Euclidean geometry is isomorphic to this model.
