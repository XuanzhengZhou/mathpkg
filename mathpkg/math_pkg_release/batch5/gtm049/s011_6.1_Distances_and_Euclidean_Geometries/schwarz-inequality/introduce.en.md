Schwarz's Inequality (also known as the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality) bounds the absolute value of the inner product by the product of the norms. It is the cornerstone inequality of Euclidean geometry, used to prove the triangle inequality for the induced distance and to define angles between vectors. Equality characterizes linear dependence of the two vectors.
