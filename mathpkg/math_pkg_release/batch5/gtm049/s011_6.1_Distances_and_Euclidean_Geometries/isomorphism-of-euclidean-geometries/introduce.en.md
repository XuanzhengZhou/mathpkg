An isomorphism of Euclidean geometries is an affine isomorphism that additionally preserves the distance function. This means it respects both the affine structure (preserving lines and parallelism) and the metric structure (preserving lengths and distances). Such maps are the natural notion of "sameness" for Euclidean geometries.
