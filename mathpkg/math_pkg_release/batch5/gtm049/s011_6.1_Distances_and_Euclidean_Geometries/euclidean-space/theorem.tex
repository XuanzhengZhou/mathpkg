If $V$ is a real vector space and $\sigma$ is a positive definite bilinear form on $V$, then $(V, \sigma)$ is called a \text{euclidean space}.

For any vectors $a, b$ in a euclidean space $(V, \sigma)$ we define
$$\|a\| = +\sqrt{\sigma(a, a)}, \quad d(a, b) = \|a - b\|$$
and call $\|a\|$ the length (or norm) of $a$, $d(a, b)$ the distance from $a$ to $b$.
