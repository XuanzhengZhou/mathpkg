The angle between two lines in Euclidean geometry is defined by parallel-transporting one line to intersect the other, then measuring the angle between direction vectors. The definition is independent of the choices made and always yields a value in $[0, \pi/2]$. The cosine formula involves the absolute value of the inner product of direction vectors, normalized by their lengths.
