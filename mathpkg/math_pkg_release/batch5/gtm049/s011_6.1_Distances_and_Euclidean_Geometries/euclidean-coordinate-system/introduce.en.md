A Euclidean coordinate system is a choice of isomorphism from a given Euclidean geometry to the standard model $\mathcal{A}(\mathbf{R}^n, d_0)$. It provides a global chart in which points are represented by $n$-tuples of real numbers and distances are computed by the standard Euclidean formula. This is the Euclidean analogue of affine coordinate systems.
