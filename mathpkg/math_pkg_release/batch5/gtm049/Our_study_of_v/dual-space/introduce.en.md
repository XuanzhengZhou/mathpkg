The dual space $V^*$ of a vector space $V$ is the space of all linear mappings from $V$ to the one-dimensional vector space $F$ (the ground field itself). Elements of $V^*$ are called linear forms or linear functionals. Since $F$ is itself a vector space, $V^* = \mathcal{L}(V, F)$ inherits the vector space structure of the hom-space. For finite-dimensional $V$, the dual space has the same dimension as $V$, and the dual of the dual is canonically isomorphic to $V$.
