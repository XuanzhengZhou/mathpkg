Once ordered bases are fixed for the domain and codomain, every linear mapping between finite-dimensional vector spaces can be uniquely represented by a matrix. The $i$-th row of the matrix records the coordinates of the image of the $i$-th basis vector of the domain, expressed in terms of the codomain basis. This correspondence $f \mapsto (f; (v_i), (v_j'))$ is a one-to-one mapping from $\mathcal{L}(V, V')$ onto $F^{m \times n}$, enabling all computations with linear maps to be carried out via matrix arithmetic.
