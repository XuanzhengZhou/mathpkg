If $f: V \rightarrow V'$ is a linear mapping, $(v_1, \ldots, v_m)$ is an ordered basis of $V$, and $(v_1', \ldots, v_n')$ is an ordered basis of $V'$, then for each $i$,
$$
v_i f = \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} v_j'.
$$
The $i$-th row of the matrix $(a_{ij})$ is the coordinate row of $v_i f$ with respect to the ordered basis $(v_1', \ldots, v_n')$. We say that $(a_{ij})$ is the \text{matrix of $f$ with respect to the ordered bases} $(v_i), (v_j')$ and write
$$
(a_{ij}) = (f; (v_i), (v_j')).
$$
