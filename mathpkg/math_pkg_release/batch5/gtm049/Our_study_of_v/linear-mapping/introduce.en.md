A linear mapping (or homomorphism) is a structure-preserving map between two vector spaces over the same field. It generalizes the notion of isomorphism by dropping the requirements of injectivity and surjectivity. The two defining conditions ensure that the mapping respects vector addition and scalar multiplication, making it the natural notion of morphism in the category of vector spaces. Linear mappings are the central objects of study when analyzing relationships between different vector spaces, possibly of different dimensions.
