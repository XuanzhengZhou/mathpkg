Since $(a+b)\iota: f \mapsto (a+b)f = af + bf$ and $(xa)\iota: f \mapsto (xa)f = x(af)$, the mapping $\iota$ is linear. It is one-one: if $v\iota = 0$, then $vf = 0$ for all $f \in V^*$, which implies $v = 0$ (since for any non-zero $v$ we can construct a linear form not vanishing on $v$). Finally, $\dim V = \dim V^* = \dim V^{**}$ (by Corollary 1 to Theorem 4), and so by the corollary to Theorem 1, $V\iota = V^{**}$, i.e., $\iota$ is onto.
