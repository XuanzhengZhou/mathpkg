If $F$ is a field which is not of characteristic $2$ and if $A \in F^{n \times n}$, show that the linear mapping $x \rightarrow xA$ has the property $(xA)(xA)^t = xx^t$ for all $x$ in $F^n$ if, and only if, $AA^t = I$. What can be deduced about $A$ if $F$ is a field of characteristic $2$? (A square matrix $A$ for which $AA^t = I$ is called an *orthogonal matrix*.)
