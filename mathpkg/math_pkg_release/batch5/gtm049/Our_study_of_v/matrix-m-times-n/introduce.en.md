An $m \times n$ matrix over a field $F$ is a rectangular array of field elements with $m$ rows and $n$ columns. Matrices serve as the concrete computational representation of linear mappings between finite-dimensional vector spaces: once bases are chosen, every linear map corresponds uniquely to a matrix. The set $F^{m \times n}$ of all such matrices itself forms a vector space under entrywise addition and scalar multiplication.
