We denote by $I_n$, or by $I$ if no confusion is possible, the $n \times n$ matrix $(\delta_{ij})$ (where $\delta_{ij}$ is the Kronecker delta), i.e., $I$ has $1$'s down the diagonal $i=j$ and $0$'s elsewhere. If $V$ has ordered basis $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ then $I$ is the matrix $(1_V; (a_i), (a_j))$ of the identity mapping $1_V$ on $V$.
