The annihilator $M^\circ$ of a subspace $M$ of $V$ is the set of all linear forms on $V$ that vanish identically on $M$. It is a subspace of the dual space $V^*$. The annihilator operation establishes a one-to-one, inclusion-reversing correspondence between subspaces of $V$ and subspaces of $V^*$, with $\dim M^\circ = \dim V - \dim M$. This correspondence underpins the principle of duality in projective geometry.
