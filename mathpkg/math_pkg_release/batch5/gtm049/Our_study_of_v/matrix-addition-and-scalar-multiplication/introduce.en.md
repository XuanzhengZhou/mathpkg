Matrix addition and scalar multiplication are defined entrywise, making $F^{m \times n}$ into a vector space over $F$. These operations are precisely those that make the correspondence between linear mappings and their matrices into a vector space isomorphism: the matrix of a sum of linear maps is the sum of their matrices, and the matrix of a scalar multiple is the scalar multiple of the matrix. Thus $\mathcal{L}(V, V') \cong F^{m \times n}$ as vector spaces.
