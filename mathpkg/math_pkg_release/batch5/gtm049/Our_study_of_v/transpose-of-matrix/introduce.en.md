The transpose operation flips a matrix over its main diagonal, turning rows into columns and vice versa. It satisfies $(A + B)^t = A^t + B^t$ and $(xA)^t = xA^t$, making it a linear involution. When a square matrix equals its own transpose, it is called symmetric. The transpose plays a crucial role in duality theory: the matrix of the transpose (dual) of a linear mapping is the transpose of the original matrix.
