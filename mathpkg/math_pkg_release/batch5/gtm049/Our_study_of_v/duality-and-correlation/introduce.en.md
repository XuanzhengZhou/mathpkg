A duality between projective geometries is an isomorphism between one projective space and the dual of another, composed with the annihilator correspondence. When the domain and codomain coincide, the duality is called a correlation -- an incidence-reversing automorphism of the projective geometry. In dimension 3, a correlation interchanges points and planes while preserving lines. When $\dim V \geq 3$, every duality is induced by a semi-linear isomorphism $V \to W^*$, a consequence of the Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry.
