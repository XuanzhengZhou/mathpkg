If $f$ is a linear isomorphism of $V$ onto $W^*$, then $\mathcal{P}(f) \circ$ (where $\circ$ denotes the annihilator mapping) is called a \text{duality} of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto $\mathcal{P}(W)$. A duality of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto itself is called a \text{correlation} of $\mathcal{P}(V)$.

Thus a correlation of a $3$-dimensional projective geometry maps points to planes and planes to points and maps lines to lines.
