This theorem establishes the annihilator mapping as a dimension-reversing, inclusion-reversing bijection between the subspace lattices of $V$ and $V^*$. Property (2) shows the operation is involutive when the double dual is identified with $V$. Properties (4) and (5) show that the annihilator interchanges sums with intersections, making it an order-reversing lattice isomorphism (a Galois connection). These properties are the algebraic foundation for the principle of duality in projective geometry.
