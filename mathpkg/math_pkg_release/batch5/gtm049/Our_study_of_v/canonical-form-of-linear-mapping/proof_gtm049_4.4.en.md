Let $r = \dim Vf$. Choose a basis $(a_1', \ldots, a_r')$ of $Vf$ and extend it to a basis $(a_1', \ldots, a_n')$ of $V'$. By Theorem 1, $V \cong Vf \oplus \operatorname{Ker} f$. Let $M$ be a subspace of $V$ complementary to $\operatorname{Ker} f$ such that the restriction of $f$ to $M$ is an isomorphism onto $Vf$. Choose $a_i \in M$ such that $a_i f = a_i'$ for $i = 1, \ldots, r$, and choose a basis $(a_{r+1}, \ldots, a_m)$ of $\operatorname{Ker} f$. Then $(a_1, \ldots, a_m)$ is a basis of $V$, and with respect to the bases $(a_i)$ and $(a_j')$, the matrix of $f$ has the required block form.
