If $f \in \mathcal{L}(U, V)$ and $h \in \mathcal{L}(V, F)$, then $fh \in \mathcal{L}(U, F)$. Hence the equation $h f^t = fh$ defines a mapping $f^t$ of $V^*$ into $U^*$. Show that $f^t \in \mathcal{L}(V^*, U^*)$ ($f^t$ is called the *transpose* of $f$). Show that if $U^*$, $V^*$ are identified with $U$, $V$ by the isomorphism of Theorem 5, then $(f^t)^t$ is identified with $f$. Hence prove that the mapping $f \rightarrow f^t$ is a one-one mapping of $\mathcal{L}(U, V)$ onto $\mathcal{L}(V^*, U^*)$. Show also that $(f + g)^t = f^t + g^t$, $(xf)^t = xf^t$, and $(fg)^t = g^t f^t$.
