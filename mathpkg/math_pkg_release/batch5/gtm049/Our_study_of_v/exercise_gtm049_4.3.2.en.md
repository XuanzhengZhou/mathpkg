Let $E_{ij}$ denote the $m \times n$ matrix with all terms zero except the $(i, j)$-term, which is $1$ ($i = 1, \ldots, m$; $j = 1, \ldots, n$). Prove that these $E_{ij}$'s form a basis of $F^{m \times n}$. We call this the *standard basis* of $F^{m \times n}$.
