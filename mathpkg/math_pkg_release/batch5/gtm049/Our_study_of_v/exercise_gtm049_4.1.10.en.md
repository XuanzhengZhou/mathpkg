Let $\pi$ be a non-identity central collineation of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ with hyperplane $H$ and center $A$. Suppose that $\pi = \mathcal{P}(f)$, where the automorphism $f$ is chosen so that $f$ restricts to the identity mapping on $H$. Prove that $H = \operatorname{Ker}(f - 1)$ and $A = V(f - 1)$.

(An automorphism $f$ of $V$ which restricts to the identity mapping on a hyperplane is called an \textit{elementary transformation} of $V$. If $f \neq 1$, then the hyperplane of fixed vectors of $f$ is necessarily $\operatorname{Ker}(f - 1)$.)
