Matrix equivalence is the relation that identifies matrices representing the same linear mapping under different choices of bases. Two matrices are equivalent precisely when they have the same rank, making rank a complete invariant for this equivalence relation. Thus the classification of linear mappings between finite-dimensional vector spaces up to change of basis reduces to a single integer invariant: the rank. This is a much coarser relation than similarity, which is reserved for endomorphisms.
