Similarity is the matrix relation corresponding to a single linear operator represented with respect to two different bases of the same space. Unlike equivalence (which allows different bases in domain and codomain), similarity uses the same change-of-basis matrix on both sides, reflecting that domain and codomain are the same space. The classification of matrices up to similarity — finding the simplest matrix similar to a given one — is the central problem of linear algebra and leads to canonical forms such as the Jordan and rational canonical forms.
