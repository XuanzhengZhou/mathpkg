\textbf{(The Principle of Duality.)} If $\mathfrak{P}$ is a proposition which is true in all $n$-dimensional projective geometries over a given field $F$, then the dual proposition $\mathfrak{P}^*$ is also true in all $n$-dimensional projective geometries over $F$.

Here the dual proposition $\mathfrak{P}^*$ is obtained from $\mathfrak{P}$ by changing $\subset$ to $\supset$ throughout and hence replacing intersection, join, and dimension $r$ by join, intersection, and dimension $n-1-r$, respectively.
