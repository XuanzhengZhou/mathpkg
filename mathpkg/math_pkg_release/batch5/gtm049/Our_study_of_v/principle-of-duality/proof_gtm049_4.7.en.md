The annihilator mapping $M \mapsto M^\circ$ establishes an inclusion-reversing bijection between the subspaces of $V$ and those of $V^*$ (Theorem 6). Passing to the projective geometries $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\mathcal{P}(V^*)$, this yields a one-to-one correspondence that reverses inclusion, interchanges intersections with joins, and maps a subspace of projective dimension $r$ to one of projective dimension $n-1-r$. Since this correspondence is valid for any $n$-dimensional vector space $V$ over $F$, any proposition $\mathfrak{P}$ true in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ yields the dual proposition $\mathfrak{P}^*$ true in $\mathcal{P}(V^*)$. But $\mathcal{P}(V^*) \cong \mathcal{P}(V)$ as projective geometries, so $\mathfrak{P}^*$ holds in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ as well.
