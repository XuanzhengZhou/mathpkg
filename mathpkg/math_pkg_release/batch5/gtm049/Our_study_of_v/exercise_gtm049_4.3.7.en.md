Let $D$ be the subset of $\mathbb{C}^{2 \times 2}$ consisting of all matrices of the form
$$
\begin{pmatrix} x & y \\ -\bar{y} & \bar{x} \end{pmatrix}
$$
where $x, y \in \mathbb{C}$ and the bar denotes complex conjugation. Prove that $D$ is a linear algebra over $\mathbb{R}$ but not over $\mathbb{C}$. Show further that $D$ is a division ring but is not a field. ($D$ is called the *real quaternion algebra*.)
