If $A \in F^{m \times n}$ and if $x A y^t = 0$ for all $x$ in $F^{1 \times m}$ and $y$ in $F^{1 \times n}$, show that $A = 0$.

If $S \in F^{n \times n}$ and if $x S x^t = 0$ for all $x$ in $F^{1 \times n}$, show that $S = -S^t$. (Such a matrix $S$ is called *skew-symmetric*.)
