If $D$ denotes the operation of differentiation and $J$ denotes the operation of "integration from $0$ to $X$", show that $D$ and $J$ are linear mappings of $\mathbb{Q}[X]$ into $\mathbb{Q}[X]$. Prove that $D$ is onto $\mathbb{Q}[X]$ but is not one-one, and $J$ is one-one but not onto $\mathbb{Q}[X]$. (This shows that the corollary to Theorem 1 is false for infinite dimensional spaces.) Find $JD - DJ$.
