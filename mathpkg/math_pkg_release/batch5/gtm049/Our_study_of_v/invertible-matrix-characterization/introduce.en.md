This proposition provides the conceptual characterization of matrix invertibility: a square matrix is invertible precisely when it represents an invertible linear transformation (an isomorphism). This connects the purely algebraic notion of having a two-sided matrix inverse with the geometric notion of being a bijective linear map. The "if" direction follows from the fact that the inverse mapping yields the inverse matrix, while the converse is left as an exercise.
