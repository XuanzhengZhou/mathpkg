This proposition establishes the fundamental correspondence between composition of linear mappings and multiplication of their representing matrices. The matrix of a composite $fg$ is the product of the matrices of $f$ and $g$. This justifies the definition of matrix multiplication: it is precisely the operation that makes the correspondence $f \mapsto (f)$ a contravariant functor from the category of based vector spaces to matrices. Consequently, all algebraic properties of matrix multiplication (such as associativity) follow from the corresponding properties of function composition.
