When a linear mapping is represented by different matrices with respect to different choices of bases, those matrices are related by multiplication by invertible change-of-basis matrices on both sides. Specifically, $B = PAQ$ where $P$ is the change-of-basis matrix in the domain and $Q$ is the change-of-basis matrix in the codomain. This relation is the matrix-level expression of the naturality of the representation of linear maps, and it motivates the definition of matrix equivalence.
