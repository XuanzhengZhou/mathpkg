The invariant factors and elementary divisors of a linear mapping $f \colon V \to V$ are defined to be those of the associated $F[X]$-module $V_f$. This terminology transfers the module-theoretic invariants from the structure theory of finitely generated modules over a PID to the language of linear algebra. The invariant factors uniquely determine the similarity class of $f$, making them fundamental to the classification of linear transformations up to change of basis.
