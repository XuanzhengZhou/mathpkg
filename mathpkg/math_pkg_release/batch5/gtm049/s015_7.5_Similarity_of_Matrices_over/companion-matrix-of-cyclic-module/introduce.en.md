This lemma shows that when the $F[X]$-module $V_f$ is cyclic with generator $a$, there is a natural ordered basis $(a, af, \dots, af^{s-1})$ for $V$ in which the matrix of $f$ takes the companion matrix form $B$. The last row of $B$ contains the coefficients of the minimal polynomial $m_a(X)$, making explicit the connection between the module structure and the matrix representation. This companion matrix is the fundamental building block for the rational canonical form.
