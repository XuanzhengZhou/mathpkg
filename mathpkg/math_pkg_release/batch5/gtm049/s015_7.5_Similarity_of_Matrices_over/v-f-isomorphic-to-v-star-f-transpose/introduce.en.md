Proposition 10 establishes a fundamental duality: the $F[X]$-module $V_f$ associated to a linear map $f$ is isomorphic to the module $(V^*)_{f^t}$ associated to its transpose $f^t$. The proof uses the invariant factor decomposition of $V_f$ and constructs a dual decomposition of $V^*$ via annihilators, showing that the cyclic summands of $V_f$ correspond naturally to those of $(V^*)_{f^t}$. This implies $f$ and $f^t$ share the same invariant factors and elementary divisors, which is used later in the study of bilinear forms.
