The rational canonical form theorem states that for any linear transformation $f \colon V \to V$ over a field $F$, there exists a basis of $V$ in which the matrix of $f$ is block diagonal, with each block being a companion matrix. This follows directly from the invariant factor decomposition of the $F[X]$-module $V_f$ (Theorem 1), combined with Lemma 1 which gives the companion matrix form for each cyclic summand. The rational canonical form works over any field and is uniquely determined by the invariant factors of $f$.
