This definition extends the notions of minimum polynomial, invariant factors, and elementary divisors from linear transformations to square matrices. Given a matrix $A \in F^{n \times n}$, one constructs a linear mapping $f$ on a vector space $V$ using an arbitrary basis. Since any two $F[X]$-modules $V_f$ obtained this way are isomorphic, the invariants of $f$ are well-defined and independent of the choice of $V$ and basis. Two square matrices are similar over $F$ if and only if they share the same invariant factors (or elementary divisors).
