Let $A = (a_{ij}) \in F^{n \times n}$ be a square matrix. For any $n$-dimensional vector space $V$ over $F$ and any ordered basis $(v_1, \ldots, v_n)$ of $V$, $A$ determines a linear mapping $f$ by
$$v_i f = \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} v_j, \quad i = 1, \ldots, n.$$
Any two $F[X]$-modules $V_f$ obtained in this way from $A$ are isomorphic. The \textbf{minimum polynomial}, \textbf{invariant factors}, and \textbf{elementary divisors} of the matrix $A$ relative to $F$ are defined to be those of the linear mapping $f$.
