This exercise from the classification of collineations uses complexification to relate real invariant lines to complex invariant points. When a real vector space $V$ is complexified to $V_{(C)}$, a real invariant line under a collineation corresponds to a pair of conjugate complex invariant points in the complexified space, a key observation for the canonical form theory.
