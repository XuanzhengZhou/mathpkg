Let $V$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{C}$, and let $g$ be a semi-linear automorphism of $V$ with respect to complex conjugation. For a linear mapping $f$ of $V$, the \textit{adjoint} $f^*$ of $f$ (with respect to $g$) is the linear mapping defined by
$$af^* = (f(ag))g^{-1} \quad \text{for all } a \in V.$$
Equivalently, $f^*$ satisfies
$$b(af^*g) = bf(ag) \quad \text{for all } a, b \in V.$$
In terms of the transpose $f^t$, we have
$$f^* = g f^t g^{-1}.$$
