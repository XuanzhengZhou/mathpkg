This definition introduces the adjoint $f^*$ of a linear mapping $f$ on a complex vector space, relative to a semi-linear automorphism $g$ that incorporates complex conjugation. The defining equation $b(af^*g) = bf(ag)$ for all $a,b$ characterizes $f^*$ uniquely. In the language of transposes, $f^* = g f^t g^{-1}$, which generalizes the usual Hermitian adjoint in the case where $g$ is the identity map composed with complex conjugation.
