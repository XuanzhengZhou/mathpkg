This proposition establishes that the adjoint operation $f \mapsto f^*$ defined relative to a semi-linear automorphism is an involution: applying it twice recovers the original mapping $f$. The proof uses the defining sesquilinear form $\sigma$ and the property that $\sigma$ is non-degenerate. The result is a direct consequence of the Hermitian symmetry of $\sigma$ together with the fact that $g$ is semi-linear with respect to complex conjugation.
