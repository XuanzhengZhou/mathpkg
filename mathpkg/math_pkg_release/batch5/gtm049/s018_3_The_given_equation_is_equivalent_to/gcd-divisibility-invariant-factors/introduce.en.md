This exercise fills in the details of the greatest common divisor argument used in the proof of the invariant factor theorem for modules over a principal ideal domain. It shows how basis changes using Bezout coefficients establish the divisibility chain $m_1 \mid m_2 \mid \cdots \mid m_t$ that characterizes invariant factors.
