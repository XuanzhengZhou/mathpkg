Let $f, g$ be linear mappings of a vector space $V$ over a field $F$. Let $c$ be a common eigenvector of $f$ and $g$, and let $M$ be a subspace of $V$ such that
$$V = M \oplus [c].$$
For any $m \in M$, express $mf$ uniquely as $mf = mf' + xc$ where $mf' \in M$ and $x \in F$. Then $f'$ is a linear mapping of $M$ into $M$, and
$$(fg)' = f'g'.$$
