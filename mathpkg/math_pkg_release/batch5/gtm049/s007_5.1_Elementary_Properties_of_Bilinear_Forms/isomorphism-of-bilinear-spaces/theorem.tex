An isomorphism of the bilinear space $(V, \sigma)$ onto the bilinear space $(V', \sigma')$ is a linear isomorphism $f$ of $V$ onto $V'$ which satisfies

$$\sigma(a, b) = \sigma'(af, bf)$$

for all $a, b$ in $V$. If such an isomorphism $f$ exists, $(V, \sigma)$ and $(V', \sigma')$ are said to be isomorphic. The bilinear forms $\sigma$ and $\sigma'$ are then said to be congruent.
