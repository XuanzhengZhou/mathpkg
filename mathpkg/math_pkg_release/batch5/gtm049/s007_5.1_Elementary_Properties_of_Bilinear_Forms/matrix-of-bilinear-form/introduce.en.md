Once an ordered basis is chosen, every bilinear form on an $n$-dimensional vector space is represented by an $n \times n$ matrix. The value $\sigma(a, b)$ is then computed as $x S y^t$, where $x$ and $y$ are the coordinate row vectors of $a$ and $b$. The bilinear polynomial $\sum s_{ij} X_i Y_j$ encodes the same information in polynomial form. This matrix representation reduces the study of bilinear forms to matrix algebra.
