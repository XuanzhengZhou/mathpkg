A bilinear form on a vector space $V$ over a field $F$ is a scalar-valued function of two vector arguments that is linear in each argument separately. Condition B.1 expresses linearity in the first variable, while condition B.2 expresses linearity in the second variable. Bilinear forms generalize the dot product and provide the algebraic foundation for studying quadratic forms, symmetric forms, and the geometry of vector spaces equipped with a pairing.
