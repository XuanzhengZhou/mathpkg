Given a bilinear form $\sigma$ and a linear map $f$ on $V$, the transformed bilinear form $\sigma^f$ is defined by pulling back $\sigma$ along $f$. This operation is fundamental to the study of congruence: two bilinear forms $\sigma$ and $\sigma'$ on $V$ are congruent if and only if there exists an automorphism $f$ of $V$ such that $\sigma' = \sigma^f$.
