A bilinear space is simply a vector space equipped with a specified bilinear form. The notation $(V, \sigma)$ emphasizes that the bilinear form $\sigma$ is part of the structure. This abstraction allows one to study vector spaces not just by their linear structure but also by the additional geometric or algebraic relations encoded in the bilinear form.
