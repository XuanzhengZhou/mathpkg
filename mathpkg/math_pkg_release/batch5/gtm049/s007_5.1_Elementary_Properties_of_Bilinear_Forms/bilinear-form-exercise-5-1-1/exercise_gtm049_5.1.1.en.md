Consider the following properties of a bilinear form $\sigma$ on a vector space $V$ (the vector space is not assumed to be finite dimensional; the definitions of a bilinear form $\sigma$ and of the mappings $\varrho$, $\tilde{\sigma}$ are exactly as given in the text):

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $b$ implies $a = 0$;
\item[(ii)] $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a$ implies $b = 0$.
\end{enumerate}

Prove that (i) is equivalent to $\varrho$ being one-to-one and (ii) is equivalent to $\tilde{\sigma}$ being one-to-one.

If $V$ is finite dimensional, show that (ii) is also equivalent to $\varrho$ being onto $V^*$.

(Thus in the case of a finite dimensional vector space, the conditions (i) and (ii) are each equivalent to the non-degeneracy of $\sigma$.)
