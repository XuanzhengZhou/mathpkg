Every quadratic space admits an orthogonal decomposition $V = M \oplus V^\perp$ where $M$ is a subspace complementary to the radical $V^\perp$. The dimension of $M$ equals the rank $r$ of the bilinear form $\sigma$, and the restriction $\sigma_M$ is non-degenerate. By choosing coordinates appropriately, the quadric $Q$ can be expressed in the diagonal form $a_0 X_0^2 + \cdots + a_{r-1} X_{r-1}^2 = 0$ with all coefficients non-zero. This decomposition is fundamental for classifying quadrics by rank and for studying when $Q(\sigma_M)$ is empty.
