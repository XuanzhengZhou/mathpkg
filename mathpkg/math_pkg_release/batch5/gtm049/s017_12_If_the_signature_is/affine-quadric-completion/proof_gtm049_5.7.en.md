Let $A$ be the affine space determined by the hyperplane at infinity $H$. Let $Q' \subseteq K$ be an affine quadric and $Q$ its projective completion. Since $Q' = Q \setminus H$ and $Q' \subseteq K$, we have $Q \subseteq H \cup K$. Apply Exercise 6 to classify $Q$: $Q$ is either contained in $H$, contained in $K$, equal to $H \cup K$, or is the union of four subspaces. Translating each case back to the affine space yields the stated classification of $Q'$. (i) If $Q \subseteq H$, then $Q$ has no affine points, so $Q' = \emptyset$. (ii) If $Q \subseteq K$, then $Q' = Q$, and by Exercise 3, $Q'$ is a subspace of $K$. (iii) If $Q = H \cup K$, then $Q' = K \setminus H$, which consists of all affine points of $K$. (iv) The exceptional case from Exercise 6, occurring only over $\mathbf{F}_3$, yields $Q'$ as all affine points in a pair of hyperplanes of $K$.
