Let $A$ be an affine space determined by a hyperplane $H$ in $\mathcal{P}(V)$. Let $Q'$ be an affine quadric contained in a hyperplane $K$ of $A$, and let $Q$ be a projective quadric completing $Q'$. Then $Q \subseteq H \cup K$. Furthermore:

\begin{enumerate}
\item If $Q \subseteq H$, then $Q'$ is empty.
\item If $Q \subseteq K$, then $Q'$ consists of all the affine points in some subspace $N$ of $K$.
\item If $Q$ consists of all the points of $H \cup K$, then $Q'$ consists of all the affine points of $K$. (Note that $Q = H \cup K$ and $Q = K$ give the same $Q'$.)
\item There is one further case: $Q'$ is the set of all affine points in a pair of hyperplanes of $K$, and the ground field is isomorphic to $\mathbf{F}_3$.
\end{enumerate}
