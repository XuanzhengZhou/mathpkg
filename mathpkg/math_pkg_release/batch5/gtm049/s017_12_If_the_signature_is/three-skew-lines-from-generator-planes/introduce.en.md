If one takes any three distinct planes through a given generator $L$ of a quadric, their intersections with the quadric (other than $L$) produce three new lines. These three lines are generators of the quadric and are pairwise skew -- they do not intersect and are not coplanar. This construction provides a standard way to produce triples of skew generators on a ruled quadric, which can then be used as a frame of reference for further geometric analysis.
