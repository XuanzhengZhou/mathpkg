The possible ranks of a quadratic form whose zero-set is empty (i.e., the quadric has no real points) depend on the ground field. Over an ordered field such as $\mathbb{R}$, any rank $r \geq 1$ can occur if all coefficients have the same sign. Over an algebraically closed field, only rank 1 is possible. Over the real numbers, the rank can be at most 2: either $r = 1$, or $r = 2$ with coefficients of equal sign. These bounds classify when a quadric can be "pointless" over the given field.
