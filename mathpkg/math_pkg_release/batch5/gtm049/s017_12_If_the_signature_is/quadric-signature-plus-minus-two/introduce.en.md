A non-degenerate quadric in projective 3-space whose associated quadratic form has signature $\pm 2$ can be brought to the canonical form $X_0^2 + X_1^2 + X_2^2 - X_3^2 = 0$ by a suitable choice of projective coordinates. This is a classification result for real quadrics: the signature determines the equation up to coordinate change. Furthermore, a quadric of this signature admits no generators, because the intersection with the plane $X_3 = 0$ would require a non-trivial real solution to $X_0^2 + X_1^2 + X_2^2 = 0$.
