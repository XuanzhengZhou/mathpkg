By the classification of quadratic forms in projective geometry, a non-degenerate quadratic form on a 4-dimensional vector space with signature $\pm 2$ is equivalent, up to a change of basis, to the form $X_0^2 + X_1^2 + X_2^2 - X_3^2$. This determines the equation of the corresponding quadric in $\mathbb{P}^3$. To see that there is no generator: if $L$ were a generator of the quadric, then the plane $X_3 = 0$ would intersect $L$ in points satisfying $X_0^2 + X_1^2 + X_2^2 = 0$. Over the real numbers, this equation has only the trivial solution $X_0 = X_1 = X_2 = 0$, which does not correspond to a point in $\mathbb{P}^2$. Hence no such generator can exist.
