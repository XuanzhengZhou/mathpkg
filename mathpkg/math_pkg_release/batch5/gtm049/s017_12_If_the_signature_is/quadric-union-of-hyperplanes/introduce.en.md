When a non-degenerate, non-empty quadric $Q$ is contained in the union of two distinct hyperplanes $H \cup K$, a striking classification emerges. The triple intersection $Q \cap H \cap K$ must be empty, and the quadric can only take one of two forms: either it coincides with the set of all points of $H$ and $K$ (a degenerate union of hyperplanes), or it consists of four linear subspaces -- two hyperplanes within $H$ and two within $K$. This classification is used in the study of affine quadrics and their projective completions.
