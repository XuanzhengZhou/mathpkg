Let $Q \subseteq H \cup K$ where $H$ and $K$ are two distinct hyperplanes, and assume $Q$ is non-empty and non-degenerate. Then $Q \not\subseteq H$ and $Q \not\subseteq K$. Moreover:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $Q \cap H \cap K$ is empty.
\item[(ii)] Either $Q$ consists of all the points on the hyperplanes $H$ and $K$, or $Q$ consists of the points on four subspaces: two being hyperplanes in $H$ and two being hyperplanes in $K$.
\end{enumerate}
