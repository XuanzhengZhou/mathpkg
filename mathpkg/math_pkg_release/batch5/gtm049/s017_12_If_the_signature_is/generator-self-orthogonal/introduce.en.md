A generator of a quadric is a maximal isotropic subspace contained in the quadric. A fundamental property is that a generator is equal to its own orthogonal complement: $L^\perp = L$. This self-orthogonality characterizes the role of generators in the geometry of quadrics, and is the starting point for understanding how planes through a generator intersect the quadric.
