When the zero-set $Q(\sigma)$ of a quadratic form is contained in a hyperplane $H$, a strong orthogonality relation emerges. Any point $A$ on the quadric is orthogonal to every point $B$ outside the hyperplane $H$, because the line $AB$ intersects $Q$ only at $A$, forcing the restriction of $\sigma$ to the line to be degenerate. Since the points outside $H$ span the whole ambient vector space, this implies that $Q(\sigma)$ is contained in the radical $V^\perp$ of the bilinear form.
