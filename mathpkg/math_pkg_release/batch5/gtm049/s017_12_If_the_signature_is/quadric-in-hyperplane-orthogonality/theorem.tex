Let $Q(\sigma)$ lie in a hyperplane $H$ of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. If $A \in Q(\sigma)$ and $B$ is any point not in $H$, then the line $AB$ meets $Q$ only at $A$, so $\sigma_{AB}$ is degenerate. Consequently, $A$ is orthogonal to $B$. Since $V$ is spanned by the points $B$ not in $H$, it follows that $Q(\sigma) \subseteq V^\perp$.
