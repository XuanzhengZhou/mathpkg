$$Q(\sigma)$$ lies in the hyperplane $$H$$. Let $$A \in Q(\sigma)$$ and $$B$$ be any point not in $$H$$. The line $$AB$$ meets $$Q$$ only at $$A$$ and so $$\sigma_{AB}$$ is degenerate. Thus $$A$$ is orthogonal to $$B$$. But $$V$$ is spanned by the points $$B$$ not in $$H$$ and so $$A \subset V^\perp$$.
