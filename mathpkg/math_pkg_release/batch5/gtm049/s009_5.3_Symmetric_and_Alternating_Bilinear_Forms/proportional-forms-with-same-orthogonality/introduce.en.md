Lemma 4 states that when two bilinear forms $\sigma$ and $\tau$ share the same left-orthogonality relation $\bot(\sigma) = \bot(\tau)$, they are proportional: $\sigma = z\tau$ for some nonzero scalar $z$. This result shows that the orthogonality structure of a bilinear form determines the form uniquely up to a scalar factor. The proof uses the existence of an ordered basis adapted to the rank of the forms and relies on the linear independence of the first $r$ rows of the representing matrices.
