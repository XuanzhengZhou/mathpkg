A real symmetric bilinear form is positive (semi-)definite if the associated quadratic form $q(a) = \sigma(a,a)$ takes only non-negative values. Positive definiteness further requires that $q(a)=0$ only for $a=0$. These notions are fundamental for the study of Euclidean geometry and the classification of real symmetric forms via Sylvester's Law of Inertia.
