Proposition 6 establishes the fundamental classification of orthosymmetric bilinear forms: a bilinear form is orthosymmetric exactly when it is either symmetric ($\sigma(a,b) = \sigma(b,a)$ for all $a,b$) or skew-symmetric ($\sigma(a,b) = -\sigma(b,a)$ for all $a,b$). This result connects the geometric property of symmetric orthogonality to the algebraic properties of the form itself.
