An orthosymmetric bilinear form is one for which orthogonality becomes a symmetric relation: $\sigma(a,b)=0$ holds if and only if $\sigma(b,a)=0$. This condition is equivalent to the equality of the left and right orthogonal complements, $\bot(\sigma) = \top(\sigma)$. Orthosymmetry captures the fundamental property that makes orthogonality a geometrically meaningful concept in the study of bilinear forms.
