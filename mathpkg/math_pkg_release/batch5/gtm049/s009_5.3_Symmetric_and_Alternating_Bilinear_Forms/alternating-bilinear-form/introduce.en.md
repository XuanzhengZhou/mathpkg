An alternating bilinear form satisfies $\sigma(a,a)=0$ for all vectors $a$, which forces $\sigma(a,b)=-\sigma(b,a)$ -- i.e., every alternating form is skew-symmetric. Over fields of characteristic not equal to 2, the converse also holds. However, in characteristic 2, alternating forms form a proper subclass of symmetric (and simultaneously skew-symmetric) forms, making the distinction algebraically significant.
