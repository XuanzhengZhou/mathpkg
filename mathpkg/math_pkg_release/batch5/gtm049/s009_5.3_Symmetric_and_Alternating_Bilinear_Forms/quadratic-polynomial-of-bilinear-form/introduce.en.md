Given a symmetric bilinear form $\sigma$ with matrix $(s_{ij})$ relative to an ordered basis, the associated quadratic function is $q(a) = \sigma(a,a)$, which expands to the homogeneous quadratic polynomial $f(X_1,\ldots,X_n) = \sum s_{ij} X_i X_j$. The quadratic polynomial is obtained from the bilinear polynomial $\sum s_{ij}X_i Y_j$ by setting $Y_i = X_i$. Over fields of characteristic $\neq 2$, the quadratic form $q$ uniquely determines the symmetric bilinear form $\sigma$ via the polarization identity.
