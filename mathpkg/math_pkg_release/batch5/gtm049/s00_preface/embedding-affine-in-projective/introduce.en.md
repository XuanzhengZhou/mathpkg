Theorem 7 establishes the precise relationship between affine and projective geometry. An affine geometry $\mathcal{A}(c+H)$ (cosets in the complement of a hyperplane $H$) embeds one-to-one into the projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ via the mapping $S \mapsto [S]$. This embedding preserves inclusion, intersections, joins, and dimensions (with the projective dimension being exactly the affine dimension). Parallelism in the affine geometry corresponds to the relative position of the "hyperplane at infinity" $H$.
