The join of a family $(S_i)_{i \in I}$ of cosets in $V$ is the intersection of all the cosets in $V$ which contain every $S_i$. We denote this join by $\mathcal{J}(S_i: i \in I)$, or simply by $\mathcal{J}(S_i)$.

For two cosets we more often use the notation $S_1 \cup S_2$. Then clearly $(S_1 \cup S_2) \cup S_3 = S_1 \cup (S_2 \cup S_3) = \mathcal{J}(S_1, S_2, S_3)$.

It follows from Proposition 1 that $\mathcal{J}(S_i)$ is itself a coset in $V$ and $\mathcal{J}(S_i)$ is the least coset in $V$ containing every $S_i$.
