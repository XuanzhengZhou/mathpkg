A generalized affine geometry is a set $A$ together with a bijection $\varphi$ from the affine geometry $\mathcal{A}(S)$ of a coset $S$. Any geometrical statement about $A$ is interpreted by pulling back through $\varphi$ to the concrete affine geometry $\mathcal{A}(S)$. This generalization allows us to treat subsets of projective space (such as the complement of a hyperplane) as affine geometries in their own right.
