Put $M = \bigcap (M_i : i \in I)$. Since $0_V \in M_i$ for each $i$, $M$ is not empty. If $a, b \in M$ and $x \in F$, then $a + b \in M_i$ and $xa \in M_i$ for each $i$; thus $a + b \in M$ and $xa \in M$.
