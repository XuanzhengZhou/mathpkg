The projective space associated with a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is the set of all projective points (1-dimensional subspaces of $V$). Higher-dimensional elements (lines, planes, etc.) are viewed as sets of points they contain. This perspective is natural when discussing curves, surfaces, and incidence in projective geometry.
