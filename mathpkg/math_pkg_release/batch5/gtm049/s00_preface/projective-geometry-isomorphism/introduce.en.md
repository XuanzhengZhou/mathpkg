Theorem 6 states that projective geometries of the same dimension over the same field are isomorphic. The proof is straightforward: a vector space isomorphism $f: V \to V'$ induces a mapping on subspaces $M \mapsto Mf$ which preserves all projective incidence relations. It follows that we may speak of "the projective geometry of dimension $n$ over $F$".
