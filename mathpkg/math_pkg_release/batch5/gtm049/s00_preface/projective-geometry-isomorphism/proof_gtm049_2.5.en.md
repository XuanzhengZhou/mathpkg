If $f$ is an isomorphism of $V$ onto $V'$, then the mapping $\pi: M \rightarrow Mf$ is clearly an isomorphism of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto $\mathcal{P}(V')$, since $f$ preserves subspace inclusion, sums, and intersections, and these correspond to projective incidence, join, and intersection respectively.
