A vector space over a field $F$ is a set $V$ equipped with vector addition (forming a commutative group) and scalar multiplication by elements of $F$, satisfying four distributivity/compatibility axioms. The five axioms V.1--V.5 ensure that linear combinations of vectors behave as expected. This structure is the central object of linear algebra and the foundation for all geometries studied in this book.
