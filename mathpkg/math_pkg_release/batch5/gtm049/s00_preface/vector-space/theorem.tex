Let $F$ be a given field and $V$ a set together with rules of addition and multiplication which associate to any two elements $a, b$ in $V$ a sum $a+b$ in $V$, and to any two elements $x$ in $F$, $a$ in $V$ a product $xa$ in $V$. Then $V$ is called a vector space over the field $F$ if the following axioms hold:

\begin{itemize}
\item[V.1.] $V$ is a commutative group with respect to addition;
\item[V.2.] $x(a+b) = xa+xb$,
\item[V.3.] $(x+y)a = xa+ya$,
\item[V.4.] $(xy)a = x(ya)$,
\item[V.5.] $1a=a$ where $1$ is the identity element of $F$,
\end{itemize}

for all $x, y$ in $F$ and all $a, b$ in $V$.

The elements of $V$ are called vectors and the elements of $F$ are called scalars.
