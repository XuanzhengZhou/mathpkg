The harmonic conjugate $H$ of a point $G$ with respect to points $A, B$ on a line is the unique point such that $(A, B; G, H)$ forms a harmonic range. When $A = \{a\}$, $B = \{b\}$ and $G = \{xa + yb\}$ with $x+y=1$, we have $H = \{(xa - yb)/(x-y)\}$ provided $x \neq y$. In classical terminology, $G$ and $H$ divide $A, B$ internally and externally in the same ratio.
