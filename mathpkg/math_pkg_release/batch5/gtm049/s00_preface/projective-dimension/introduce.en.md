Projective dimension is defined as vector space dimension minus one: a $k$-dimensional subspace of $V$ has projective dimension $k-1$. Thus points correspond to 1-dimensional subspaces (pdim 0), lines to 2-dimensional subspaces (pdim 1), planes to 3-dimensional subspaces (pdim 2), and hyperplanes to codimension-1 subspaces. This shift by one aligns projective geometry with our geometric intuition.
