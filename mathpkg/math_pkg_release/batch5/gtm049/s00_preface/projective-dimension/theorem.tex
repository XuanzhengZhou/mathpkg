If $M$ is a subspace of a vector space $V$, the projective dimension of $M$ is defined to be $\dim M - 1$ and will be denoted by $\operatorname{pdim} M$. The dimension of the geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$, written $\operatorname{pdim} \mathcal{P}(V)$, will be $\operatorname{pdim} V$. The elements of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of projective dimensions $0, 1, 2$ are called projective points, projective lines, projective planes, respectively. An element of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of projective dimension $\operatorname{pdim} V - 1$ is called a hyperplane in $\mathcal{P}(V)$.
