Theorem 2 gives the fundamental dimension formula for subspaces: the sum of the dimensions of $M+N$ and $M \cap N$ equals the sum of the dimensions of $M$ and $N$. It also shows that a proper inclusion of subspaces forces a strict inequality of dimensions, making dimension a faithful measure of subspace containment.
