The orthogonal complement $M^\perp$ of a subspace $M \subseteq F^n$ collects all vectors that are orthogonal (under the standard dot product) to every vector in $M$. It is a subspace whose dimension complements that of $M$: $\dim M^\perp = n - \dim M$. This construction is self-dual in the sense that $(M^\perp)^\perp = M$, making it a fundamental tool for translating between subspaces and the systems of linear equations that define them.
