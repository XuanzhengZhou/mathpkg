Using exercise 3 of §3.1, prove that an affinity $\alpha$ of $\mathcal{A}(a+M)$ onto $\mathcal{A}(a+M)$ is a dilatation if, and only if, the uniquely determined vector space isomorphism associated with $\alpha$ is $x 1_M$, for some non-zero scalar $x$. Show, further, that a dilatation (other than the identity) is a translation if, and only if, it does not fix any point. (Cf. also exercise 1 of §3.4.)
