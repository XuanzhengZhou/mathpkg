This proposition provides the complete isomorphism classification of finitely generated modules over $F[X]$ (equivalently, over any principal ideal domain). The invariant factors (for general finitely generated modules) and the elementary divisors (for torsion modules) are complete invariants: two modules are isomorphic precisely when they share the same set of invariant factors or elementary divisors. This result is an immediate consequence of Theorems 1 and 2 together with the fact that every cyclic module is isomorphic to $R/\mathfrak{a}$ (Proposition 4). In linear algebra, this translates to the statement that two linear transformations are similar if and only if they have the same invariant factors (or elementary divisors).
