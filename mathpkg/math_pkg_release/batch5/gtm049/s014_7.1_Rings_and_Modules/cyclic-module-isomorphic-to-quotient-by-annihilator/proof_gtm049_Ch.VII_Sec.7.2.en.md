Let $M = [a]$ be a cyclic $R$-module generated by $a$. Consider the mapping
$$f: R \to M, \quad x \mapsto xa.$$
For any $x, y \in R$ and $r \in R$,
$$f(x+y) = (x+y)a = xa + ya = f(x) + f(y),$$
$$f(rx) = (rx)a = r(xa) = r f(x).$$
Thus $f$ is an $R$-homomorphism. Since $M = [a]$, every element of $M$ is of the form $xa$ for some $x \in R$, so $f$ is surjective.

The kernel of $f$ is $\{x \in R \mid xa = 0\} = \mathfrak{a}(a)$, the annihilator of $a$. But $\mathfrak{a}(a) = \mathfrak{a}(M)$ since $a$ generates $M$. By the first isomorphism theorem for modules (the module version of Proposition 1 of \S 4.1), we have
$$M \cong R/\mathfrak{a}(M).$$
