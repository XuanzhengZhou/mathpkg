Every cyclic $R$-module arises as a quotient of the base ring $R$ by an ideal. If $M = [a]$ is generated by a single element $a$, then the map $f: R \to M$ given by $x \mapsto xa$ is a surjective $R$-homomorphism whose kernel is $\mathfrak{a}(a) = \mathfrak{a}(M)$. By the first isomorphism theorem, $M \cong R/\mathfrak{a}(M)$. This characterization is fundamental: it shows that cyclic modules are classified by ideals of $R$, and it forms the basis for the invariant factor decomposition of finitely generated modules over a PID.
