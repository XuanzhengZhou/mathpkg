A principal ideal is an ideal generated by a single element: it consists of all multiples of a fixed element $x$ by arbitrary ring elements. In the integers $\mathbb{Z}$, every ideal is principal (e.g., $n\mathbb{Z}$ is the principal ideal generated by $n$). Rings in which every ideal is principal are called principal ideal rings; when such a ring is also an integral domain, it is a principal ideal domain (PID). Polynomial rings $F[X]$ over a field and the integers $\mathbb{Z}$ are the primary examples of PIDs, and the structure theory of finitely generated modules over PIDs is a cornerstone of linear algebra.
