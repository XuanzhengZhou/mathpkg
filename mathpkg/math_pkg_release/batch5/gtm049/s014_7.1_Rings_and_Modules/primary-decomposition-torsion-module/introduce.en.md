The Primary Decomposition Theorem (Theorem 2) refines the structure theorem by decomposing a finitely generated torsion module into its $p$-primary components. For each distinct irreducible factor $p_i$ of the minimal polynomial $m$, the submodule $K_i$ consists of all elements annihilated by some power of $p_i$. Each $K_i$ further decomposes into cyclic submodules whose annihilators are powers $p_i^{n_{ij}}$ of that irreducible factor. These powers, arranged in non-increasing order, are the elementary divisors of the module. This decomposition is as fine as possible: each cyclic summand is indecomposable. The elementary divisors, like the invariant factors, are complete isomorphism invariants for finitely generated torsion modules over a PID.
