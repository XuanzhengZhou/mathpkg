This proposition uses the Chinese Remainder Theorem for the polynomial ring $F[X]$ to decompose a cyclic module whose annihilator is generated by a polynomial $p$ into a direct sum of cyclic submodules, each annihilated by a power of a distinct irreducible factor of $p$. If $p = s t$ with $s, t$ relatively prime, then setting $b = s a$ (where $M = [a]$) gives $\mathfrak{a}(b) = [t]$, and similarly one obtains a component with annihilator $[s]$. Iterating this process yields the primary decomposition. This result is the crucial step in passing from the invariant factor decomposition to the elementary divisor decomposition.
