A torsion element in an $R$-module is an element that is annihilated by some non-zero scalar from the ring $R$. This represents a fundamental departure from vector space theory, where $xa = 0$ with $x \neq 0$ always implies $a = 0$. In module theory, non-zero elements can be "killed" by non-zero scalars---for instance, every element of the finite field $\mathbb{F}_p$ regarded as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module satisfies $p \cdot 1 = 0$. Torsion elements play a central role in the classification of finitely generated modules over principal ideal domains.
