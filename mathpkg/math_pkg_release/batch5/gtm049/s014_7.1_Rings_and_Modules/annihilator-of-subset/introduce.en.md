The annihilator of a subset $S$ of an $R$-module $M$ is the set of all ring elements that send every element of $S$ to zero under scalar multiplication. The annihilator is always an ideal of the ring $R$. For a single element $a \in M$, the annihilator $\mathfrak{a}(a)$ measures how far $a$ is from being "freely generated"---if $\mathfrak{a}(a) \neq 0$, then $a$ is a torsion element. The concept of annihilator is fundamental in the structure theory of finitely generated modules over principal ideal domains.
