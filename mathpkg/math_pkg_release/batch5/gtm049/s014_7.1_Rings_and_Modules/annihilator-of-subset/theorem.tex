Let $R$ be a commutative ring and $M$ an $R$-module. Given a non-empty subset $S$ of $M$, the set
$$\mathfrak{a}(S) = \{x \in R \mid xa = 0 \text{ for all } a \in S\}$$
is called the \emph{annihilator} of $S$. If $S = \{a\}$, we write $\mathfrak{a}(a)$ for $\mathfrak{a}(S)$ and call this the \emph{annihilator} of $a$.
