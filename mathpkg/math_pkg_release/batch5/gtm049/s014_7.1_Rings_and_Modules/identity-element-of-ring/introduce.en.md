The identity element (also called unity or multiplicative identity) of a ring is the unique element that acts as a neutral element for multiplication, satisfying $1 \cdot x = x \cdot 1 = x$ for all elements $x$ in the ring. Not every ring possesses an identity element; for example, $2\mathbb{Z}$ (even integers) is a ring without identity. The presence of an identity is essential for module theory, as the module axiom M.5 requires the identity to act as the identity map on module elements.
