Let $f$ belong to $\mathcal{L}(V, V)$ and let $m(X)$ be the minimum polynomial of $f$. If $c$ is a non-zero scalar, show that the minimum polynomial of $cf$ is $c^r m(X/c)$, where $r = \text{degree } m(X)$. If the elementary divisors of $f$ are $(X - x_i)^{n_{ij}}$, $i = 1, \ldots, t$; $j = 1, \ldots, k_i$, prove that the elementary divisors of $cf$ are $(X - cx_i)^{n_{ij}}$ with the same range of $i$ and of $j$.
