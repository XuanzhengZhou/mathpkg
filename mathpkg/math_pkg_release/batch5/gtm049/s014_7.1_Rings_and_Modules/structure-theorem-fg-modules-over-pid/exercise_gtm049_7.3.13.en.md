If $G$ is a finite group of order $p_1^{n_1} \ldots p_t^{n_t}$ where $p_1, \ldots, p_t$ are distinct prime numbers, then a subgroup of $G$ of order $p_i^{n_i}$ is called a Sylow $p_i$-subgroup of $G$. Show that a finite commutative group has exactly one Sylow $p$-subgroup for each prime $p$ dividing its order, and that the group is the direct sum of its Sylow subgroups.
