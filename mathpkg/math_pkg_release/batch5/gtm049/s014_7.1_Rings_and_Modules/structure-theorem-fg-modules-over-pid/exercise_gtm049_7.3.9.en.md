Let $B, C$ be commutative groups with respect to addition and let $A$ be the set of all ordered pairs $(b, c)$ where $b \in B, c \in C$. Define
$$(b, c) + (b', c') = (b + b', c + c')$$
and show that $A$ is a commutative group with respect to this addition. If $B = \mathbb{Z}$, $C = \mathbb{F}_2$ and $a_1 = (1, 0), a_2 = (0, 1), a_1' = (1, 1)$, show that
$$A = [a_1] \oplus [a_2] \text{ and } A = [a_1'] \oplus [a_2].$$
(This gives an example where the direct decomposition of Theorem 1 is not unique.)
