Given $s, t$ in a principal ideal domain $R$, show that $[d] = [s] + [t]$ if, and only if, $d$ satisfies the following two conditions:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $d$ divides $s$ and $t$;
\item[(ii)] any element which divides $s$ and $t$ must also divide $d$.
\end{itemize}
An element $d$ satisfying (i) and (ii) is called a greatest common divisor of $s$ and $t$.
