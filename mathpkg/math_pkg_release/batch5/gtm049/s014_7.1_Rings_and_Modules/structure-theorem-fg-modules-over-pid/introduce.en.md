The Structure Theorem for finitely generated modules over $F[X]$ (equivalently, over any principal ideal domain) is the central result of module theory in linear algebra. It states that every finitely generated module decomposes uniquely as a direct sum of cyclic modules, where the annihilator ideals form an ascending chain. The torsion-free part (with zero annihilators) is a free module whose rank $r$ is an invariant, while the torsion part is governed by the invariant factors---the monic generators of the non-zero annihilator ideals. This theorem generalizes the classification of finitely generated abelian groups and provides the algebraic foundation for the rational and Jordan canonical forms of a linear transformation.
