A cyclic module is an $R$-module generated by a single element $a$, denoted $[a]$. Every cyclic module is isomorphic to the quotient $R/\mathfrak{a}(a)$ of the base ring by the annihilator ideal of the generator. In the $F[X]$-module $V_f$ associated with a linear transformation $f$, the cyclic submodule generated by a vector $a$ is the $f$-cyclic subspace $[a]_f$, whose dimension over $F$ equals the degree of the minimal polynomial $m_a(X)$ of $a$ with respect to $f$.
