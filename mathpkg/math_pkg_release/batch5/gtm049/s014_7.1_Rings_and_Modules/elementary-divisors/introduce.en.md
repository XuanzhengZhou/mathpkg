Elementary divisors are the powers of irreducible polynomials that appear as the annihilator generators in the finest possible cyclic decomposition of a torsion module. They are derived from the invariant factors by further decomposing each cyclic summand via the Chinese Remainder Theorem. The decomposition into elementary divisors yields as many cyclic summands as possible—each being indecomposable. Together with the rank of the torsion-free part, the elementary divisors form a complete set of isomorphism invariants for finitely generated modules over a PID. In the context of a linear transformation $f$, the elementary divisors correspond to the invariant factors of the primary components of $V_f$.
