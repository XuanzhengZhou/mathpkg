Elementary moves are the basic operations used to transform bases of torsion-free $F[X]$-modules while preserving the module structure. They consist of swapping two generators or adding a polynomial multiple of one generator to another. These moves generalize the elementary row and column operations from linear algebra over a field to operations over the polynomial ring $F[X]$. When applied to the matrix of a homomorphism between torsion-free modules, elementary moves on the domain basis effect row operations, while moves on the codomain basis effect column operations. By successive elementary moves, any matrix over $F[X]$ can be brought to Smith normal form.
