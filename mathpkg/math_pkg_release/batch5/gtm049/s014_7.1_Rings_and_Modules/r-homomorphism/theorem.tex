For any commutative ring $R$ with identity, a mapping $f: M \to N$ between $R$-modules is called an \emph{$R$-homomorphism} (or \emph{homomorphism}) if it is additive and $R$-linear:
$$f(a+b) = f(a) + f(b), \quad f(xa) = xf(a)$$
for all $a, b \in M$ and $x \in R$. Isomorphisms, automorphisms, and the kernel of a homomorphism are defined as for linear mappings.
