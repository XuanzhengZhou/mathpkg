An $R$-homomorphism (or $R$-linear map) between $R$-modules generalizes the notion of a linear transformation between vector spaces. It preserves both addition and scalar multiplication by elements of the ring $R$. The kernel of a homomorphism is a submodule of the domain, and the image is a submodule of the codomain. The isomorphism theorems for modules carry over verbatim from vector space theory. Homomorphisms form the morphisms of the category $R$-Mod, enabling module-theoretic constructions such as exact sequences, direct sums, and quotient modules.
