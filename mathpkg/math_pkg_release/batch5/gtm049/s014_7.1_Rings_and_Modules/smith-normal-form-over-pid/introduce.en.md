The Smith normal form theorem for matrices over $F[X]$ (Proposition 8) states that every matrix with polynomial entries can be diagonalized via elementary row and column operations over $F[X]$, yielding a diagonal matrix where each diagonal entry divides the next. The non-unit diagonal entries are precisely the invariant factors of the quotient module $E'/Eg$, providing a computable method to determine invariant factors. This theorem solves the equivalence problem for matrices over $F[X]$: two matrices are equivalent (represent the same homomorphism up to change of bases) if and only if they have the same Smith normal form. Applied to $XI - A$, the Smith normal form yields the invariant factors and rational canonical form of the matrix $A$ over $F$.
