Let $(v_i), (v_j')$ be ordered bases of the torsion-free $F[X]$-modules $E, E'$ of ranks $m, n$, respectively. If $g: E \to E'$ is a homomorphism, then there exist ordered bases $(e_i), (e_j')$ obtained from $(v_i), (v_j')$, respectively, by elementary moves such that the matrix of $g$ has the form
$$\begin{pmatrix} D & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$
where $D$ is the diagonal matrix $\operatorname{diag}(d_1, \ldots, d_t)$ and
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $d_i$ divides $d_{i+1}$ for $i = 1, \ldots, t-1$,
\item[(ii)] $d_t \neq 0$.
\end{itemize}
Moreover, if $s$ is the largest index $i$ for which $[d_i] = F[X]$ (i.e., $d_i$ is a non-zero constant), then
$$[d_{s+1}] \supset \cdots \supset [d_t] \supset \underbrace{0 = \cdots = 0}_{n-t}$$
are the invariant factors of $E'/Eg$.
