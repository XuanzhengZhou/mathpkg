If $A$ is a commutative group and $a$ is an element of finite order $k$, prove that $\mathbb{Z}k = \mathfrak{a}(a)$, where $A$ is viewed as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module. If $\mathfrak{a}(A) = \mathbb{Z}t$ and $A$ is finite of order $s$, prove that $t$ divides $s$. (One calls $t$ the exponent of $A$.)
