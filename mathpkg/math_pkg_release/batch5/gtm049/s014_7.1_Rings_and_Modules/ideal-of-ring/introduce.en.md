An ideal of a commutative ring $R$ is an additive subgroup closed under multiplication by arbitrary ring elements. Ideals are the ring-theoretic analogue of normal subgroups in group theory: they are precisely the submodules of $R$ when $R$ is regarded as a module over itself. Ideals are essential for constructing quotient rings $R/\mathfrak{a}$ and for the study of ring homomorphisms (where kernels are ideals). The annihilator $\mathfrak{a}(S)$ of any subset $S$ of a module is always an ideal of the coefficient ring.
