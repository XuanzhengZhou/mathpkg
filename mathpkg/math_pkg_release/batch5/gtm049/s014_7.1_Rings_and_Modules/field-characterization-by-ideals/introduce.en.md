This proposition gives a purely ideal-theoretic characterization of fields: a commutative ring with identity is a field exactly when it has no non-trivial proper ideals. The forward direction follows because any non-zero element of an ideal in a field is invertible, forcing the ideal to contain 1 and hence be the whole field. The converse uses the fact that a non-zero element generates a principal ideal, which must be the whole ring, guaranteeing the existence of a multiplicative inverse. This result highlights how the ideal structure controls the arithmetic of the ring.
