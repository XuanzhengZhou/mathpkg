Suppose that $R$ is a field and that $x$ is a non-zero element in an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ of $R$. For any $r \in R$, the element $r x^{-1} x = r$ lies in $\mathfrak{a}$ (since $x \in \mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{a}$ is closed under multiplication by ring elements). Hence $\mathfrak{a} = R$. Thus the only ideals are $\{0\}$ and $R$.

Conversely, assume $\{0\}$ and $R$ are the only ideals of $R$. For any non-zero $x \in R$, the principal ideal $Rx$ is non-zero (it contains $x$), so $Rx = R$. Hence there exists $y \in R$ such that $yx = 1$, so $x$ is invertible. Therefore $R$ is a field.
