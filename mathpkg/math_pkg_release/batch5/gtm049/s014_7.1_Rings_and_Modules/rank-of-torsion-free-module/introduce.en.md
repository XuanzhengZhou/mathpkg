The rank of a finitely generated torsion-free module over $F[X]$ generalizes the concept of dimension from vector spaces. A torsion-free module of rank $n$ is isomorphic to the free module $F[X]^n$, and the rank is the maximal number of linearly independent elements over $F[X]$. Every minimal set of generators is a maximal linearly independent set (though the converse need not hold). The rank plays a role analogous to dimension in the study of linear transformations: given a homomorphism $g: E \to E'$ between torsion-free modules of ranks $m$ and $n$, its matrix with respect to chosen bases provides the setting for the Smith normal form.
