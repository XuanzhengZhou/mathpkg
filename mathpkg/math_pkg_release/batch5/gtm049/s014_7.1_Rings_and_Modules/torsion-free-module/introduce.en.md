A torsion-free module is an $R$-module in which the zero element is the only element annihilated by any non-zero scalar. Every vector space over a field is torsion-free, as is the free module $F[X]^n$. In the structure theory of finitely generated modules over a principal ideal domain, the torsion-free part of a module is a free module whose rank is an invariant. Torsion-free modules of finite rank share many properties with vector spaces, including the existence of bases.
