The proof follows exactly the same pattern as that of Theorem 4 (in the affine setting), adapted to the projective context. The argument uses homogeneous coordinates and proceeds by choosing appropriate vectors for the six points and computing the intersections algebraically. Unlike Desargues' theorem, the proof of Pappus' theorem requires the commutative law of multiplication $$xy = yx$$ in the ground field $$F$$.
