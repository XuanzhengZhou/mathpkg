Given two coplanar triangles $$ABC$$, $$A'B'C'$$ in perspective from a point $$P$$. Take any line through $$P$$ not in the plane of the triangles and any points $$Q, R$$ on this line so that $$P, Q, R$$ are distinct. Find a triangle $$A''B''C''$$ in perspective with $$ABC$$ from $$Q$$ and in perspective with $$A'B'C'$$ from $$R$$. Hence deduce Desargues' Theorem for the given coplanar triangles by using Desargues' Theorem for non-coplanar triangles (cf. § 2.5, exercise 6).
