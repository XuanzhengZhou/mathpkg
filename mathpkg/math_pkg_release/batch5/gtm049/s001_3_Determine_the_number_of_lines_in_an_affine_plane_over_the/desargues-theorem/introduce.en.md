Desargues' theorem is one of the most fundamental incidence theorems in projective geometry. It states that two triangles have a center of perspective (the three lines joining corresponding vertices are concurrent) if and only if they have an axis of perspective (the three intersections of corresponding sides are collinear). The theorem holds in any projective space of dimension at least three, but there exist non-Desarguesian projective planes where it fails. The theorem plays a centrally important role in the synthetic foundations of projective geometry.
