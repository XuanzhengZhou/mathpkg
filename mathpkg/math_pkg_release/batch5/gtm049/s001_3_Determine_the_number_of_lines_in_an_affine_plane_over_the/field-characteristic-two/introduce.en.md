A field is said to have characteristic 2 if the equation 2 = 0 holds, where 2 denotes the element 1 + 1. The simplest example is the finite field with two elements, F_2. In projective geometry over a field of characteristic 2, the harmonic construction exhibits degenerate behavior: a point G coincides with its own harmonic conjugate if and only if the ground field has characteristic 2. This affects the validity of certain incidence configurations, such as the plane form of Desargues' theorem.
