Let $$ABC, A'B'C'$$ be two triangles whose six sides are distinct. If
$$L = BC \cap B'C',\quad M = CA \cap C'A',\quad N = AB \cap A'B'$$
are distinct collinear points and the line $$LMN$$ is distinct from the sides of the triangles, we shall say that the triangles have $$LMN$$ as \textit{axis of perspective}.
