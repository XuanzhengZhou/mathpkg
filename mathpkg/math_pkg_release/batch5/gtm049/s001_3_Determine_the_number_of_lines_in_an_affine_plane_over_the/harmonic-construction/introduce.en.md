The harmonic construction is a purely incidence-based construction in projective geometry that, given two points A, B and a point G on the line AB, produces a fourth point H on AB called the harmonic conjugate of G with respect to A and B. The construction is remarkable because H is independent of the auxiliary choices made (the points C and D). In the projective setting, the construction is always valid without exceptional cases, unlike in affine geometry where midpoints may fail to exist.
