Given two distinct points A, B and a point G on the line AB in a projective plane, the harmonic conjugate of G with respect to A, B is the unique point H on AB constructed by the harmonic construction (Theorem 10). The quadruple (A, B; G, H) is called a harmonic range. This notion generalizes the affine concept of midpoint: when H is taken as the point at infinity on the line AB, the harmonic conjugate of H with respect to A, B is the midpoint of A and B (if it exists in the affine setting).
