We may suppose that $A = \mathcal{A}(a + M)$ and $A' = \mathcal{A}(a' + M')$ where $a + M, a' + M'$ are cosets in vector spaces $V, V'$, respectively, over the same field. Let the points $P_1, \ldots, P_n, Q, P_1', \ldots, P_n', Q'$ have vectors $p_1, \ldots, p_n, q, p_1', \ldots, p_n', q'$, respectively.

Any affinity $\alpha$ which satisfies $Q\alpha = Q'$ is expressible in the form

$$\alpha = \mathcal{A}(t_{-q} g t_{q'})$$

where $g$ is an isomorphism of $M$ onto $M'$. If also $P_i\alpha = P_i'$ for $i = 1, \ldots, n$ then $g$ takes $p_i - q$ into $p_i' - q'$ for $i = 1, \ldots, n$. By the definition of frames of reference, the vectors $p_i - q$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$) form a basis of $M$ and the vectors $p_i' - q'$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$) form a basis of $M'$. Thus by Theorem 3 of Chapter I (p. 12) there is one and only one such isomorphism $g$ and so there is one and only one such affinity $\alpha$.
