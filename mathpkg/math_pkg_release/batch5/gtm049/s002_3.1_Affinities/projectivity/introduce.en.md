A projectivity is the projective analog of an affinity: it is the mapping $\mathcal{P}(f)$ on subspaces induced by a vector space isomorphism $f \colon V \to V'$. When $V = V'$, the projectivity is called a collineation of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. If $f$ and $g$ are two isomorphisms for which the product $fg$ is defined, then $\mathcal{P}(fg) = \mathcal{P}(f)\mathcal{P}(g)$. A projectivity is clearly an isomorphism of the corresponding projective geometries.
