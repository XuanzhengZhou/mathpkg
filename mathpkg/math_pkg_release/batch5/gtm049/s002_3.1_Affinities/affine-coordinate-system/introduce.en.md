An affine coordinate system for an $n$-dimensional affine geometry $A$ over a field $F$ is an affinity mapping $A$ onto the standard affine geometry $\mathcal{A}(F^n)$. By Theorem 1, there is a one-to-one correspondence between frames of reference $(P_1, \ldots, P_n, Q)$ for $A$ and affine coordinate systems $\alpha$ for $A$, given by sending each $P_i$ to the standard basis point $E_i$ and $Q$ to the origin. This provides a concrete coordinatization of any affine geometry.
