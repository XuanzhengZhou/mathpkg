This exercise shows how to obtain an affine coordinate system from a projective one. Given a projective coordinate system where the hyperplane at infinity is $X_0 = 0$, the mapping $(x_0, \ldots, x_n) \mapsto (x_1/x_0, \ldots, x_n/x_0)$ yields an affine coordinate system on the affine geometry $A = \mathcal{P}(V) \setminus H$. The origin corresponds to the projective point $(1, 0, \ldots, 0)$.
