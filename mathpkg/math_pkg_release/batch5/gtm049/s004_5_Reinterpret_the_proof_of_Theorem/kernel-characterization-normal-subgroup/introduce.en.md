This exercise demonstrates that kernels of group homomorphisms are precisely the normal subgroups. The subgroup $\{1, \pi\}$ of the symmetric group $S_3$ is not normal (it is not closed under conjugation), hence cannot be the kernel of any homomorphism. This is a key structural result: every kernel is normal, but the converse is also true---every normal subgroup is the kernel of the canonical quotient map.
