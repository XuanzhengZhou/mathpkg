Let $G$ be the group of permutations of the set $\{1, 2, 3\}$. If $\pi$ is the permutation defined by $1\pi = 2$, $2\pi = 1$, $3\pi = 3$, show that the subgroup $\{1, \pi\}$ of $G$ cannot be the kernel of a homomorphism of $G$ into another group. (If $K$ is a kernel in a group $G$ then $x^{-1}yx \in K$ for all $y$ in $K$ and all $x$ in $G$).
