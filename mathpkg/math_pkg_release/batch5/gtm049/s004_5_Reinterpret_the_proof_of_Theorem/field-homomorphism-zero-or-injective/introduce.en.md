This exercise establishes a fundamental property of fields: any ring homomorphism from a field to another ring is either injective or the zero map. This is because the kernel of a ring homomorphism is an ideal, and a field has only the trivial ideals $\{0\}$ and the whole field. This fact is central to the theory of semi-linear isomorphisms developed in Chapter 3.
