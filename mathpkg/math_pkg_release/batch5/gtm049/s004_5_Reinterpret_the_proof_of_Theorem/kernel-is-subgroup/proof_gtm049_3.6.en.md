The proof is left as an exercise. One must verify that the kernel contains the identity, is closed under multiplication, and is closed under taking inverses. If $a\theta = 1$ and $b\theta = 1$, then $(ab)\theta = (a\theta)(b\theta) = 1 \cdot 1 = 1$, so the kernel is closed under multiplication. Also $(a^{-1})\theta = (a\theta)^{-1} = 1^{-1} = 1$, so the kernel is closed under inverses.
