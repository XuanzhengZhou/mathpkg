Proposition 6 gives the structural decomposition of the affine automorphism group as a split product (semidirect product). The full affine automorphism group $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{A}(V)$ splits as the semi-linear group $S(V)$ and the translation group $T \cong V$. The affine group $\operatorname{Af} \mathcal{A}(V)$ (consisting of affinities) splits as the general linear group $\operatorname{Aut} V$ and the translation group. This generalizes the familiar fact that every affine transformation is the composition of a linear transformation and a translation.
