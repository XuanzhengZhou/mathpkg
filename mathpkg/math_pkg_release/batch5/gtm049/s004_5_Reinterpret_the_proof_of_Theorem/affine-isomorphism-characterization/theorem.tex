Let $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{A}(a+M), \mathcal{A}' = \mathcal{A}(a'+M')$ be affine geometries over fields $F, F'$, respectively, and let $\alpha: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{A}'$ be an isomorphism. If $\dim \mathcal{A} = 1$ then $\alpha$ determines a one--one mapping $\zeta$ of $F$ onto $F'$. If $\dim \mathcal{A} \geq 2$ then $\alpha$ determines an isomorphism $\zeta$ of $F$ onto $F'$ and a semi-linear isomorphism $g$ of $M$ onto $M'$ with respect to $\zeta$, such that the affine isomorphism induced by $g$ is $\alpha$ itself.
