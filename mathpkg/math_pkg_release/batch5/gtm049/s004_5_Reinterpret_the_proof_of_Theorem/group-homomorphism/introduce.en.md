A group homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between groups that respects the group operation. Homomorphisms are the fundamental tool for relating different groups and for studying the internal structure of groups through kernels and images. In the context of linear geometry, homomorphisms describe how automorphism groups of vector spaces, affine spaces, and projective spaces are related to each other.
