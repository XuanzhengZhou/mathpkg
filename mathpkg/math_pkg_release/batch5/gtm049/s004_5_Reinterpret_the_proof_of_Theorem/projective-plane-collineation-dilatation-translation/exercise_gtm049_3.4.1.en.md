Let $P$ be a projective plane and $A$ the affine plane determined by $H$ as line at infinity. If $\pi$ is a collineation of $P$ leaving $H$ invariant (i.e., $H\pi = H$) and $\alpha$ is the affinity of $A$ obtained from $\pi$ by restriction (according to Proposition 3), prove that $\alpha$ is a dilatation if, and only if, $\pi$ is a central collineation with $H$ as axis. Prove, further, that $\alpha$ is a translation if, and only if, $\pi$ in addition has its center on $H$. (Cf. exercises 3 and 9 of §3.2.)
