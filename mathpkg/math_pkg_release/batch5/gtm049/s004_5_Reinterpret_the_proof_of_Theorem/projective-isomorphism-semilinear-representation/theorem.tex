Let $\mathcal{P} = \mathcal{P}(V)$, $\mathcal{P}' = \mathcal{P}(V')$ be projective geometries over fields $F$, $F'$, respectively and let $\pi$ be an isomorphism of $\mathcal{P}$ onto $\mathcal{P}'$.

If $\operatorname{pdim} \mathcal{P} = 1$ then there is a one--one mapping of $F$ onto $F'$.

If $\operatorname{pdim} \mathcal{P} \geq 2$ then there is an isomorphism $\zeta$ of $F$ onto $F'$ and a semi-linear isomorphism $f$ of $V$ onto $V'$ with respect to $\zeta$ such that $\pi = \mathcal{P}(f)$.
