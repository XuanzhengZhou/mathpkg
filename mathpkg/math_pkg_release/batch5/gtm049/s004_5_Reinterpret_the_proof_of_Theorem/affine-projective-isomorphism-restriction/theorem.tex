Let $V, V'$ be vector spaces over fields $F, F'$, respectively, and let $H, H'$ be hyperplanes in $\mathcal{P}(V), \mathcal{P}(V')$ determining affine geometries $A, A'$, respectively. Then any projective isomorphism $\pi$ of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto $\mathcal{P}(V')$ for which $H\pi = H'$ restricts to an affine isomorphism $\alpha$ of $A$ onto $A'$. Conversely, any affine isomorphism $\alpha$ of $A$ onto $A'$ is the restriction of just one such projective isomorphism $\pi$.

If $F = F'$ then $\pi$ is a projectivity if, and only if, $\alpha$ is an affinity.
