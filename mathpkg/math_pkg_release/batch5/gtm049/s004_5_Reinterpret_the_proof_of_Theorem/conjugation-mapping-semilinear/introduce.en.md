This exercise shows that complex conjugation, applied componentwise to $\mathbf{C}^n$, induces a semi-linear automorphism of $\mathcal{P}(\mathbf{C}^n)$. The invariant points of this projective automorphism are precisely those that can be represented by vectors with real coordinates, providing a geometric interpretation of the real points inside the complex projective space.
