This exercise computes the classical formula for the order of the general linear group $\operatorname{GL}(n, \mathbf{F}_p)$. The formula counts the number of ways to choose an ordered basis: $p^n-1$ choices for the first basis vector (non-zero), $p^n-p$ for the second (not in the span of the first), and so on. The orders of the projective and affine automorphism groups are then derived from this via the exact sequences in Proposition 5 and 6.
