Proposition 7 establishes the uniqueness of the center for a non-identity central collineation. A central collineation is a projective transformation that fixes every point of some hyperplane (its axis). The center is a point such that every line through it is invariant. This proposition shows that such a center exists and is unique, and is itself an invariant point.
