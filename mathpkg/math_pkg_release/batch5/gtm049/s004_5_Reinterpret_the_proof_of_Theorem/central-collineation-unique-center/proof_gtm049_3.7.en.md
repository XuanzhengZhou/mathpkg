Suppose that $\pi = \mathcal{P}(f)$ where $f$ is an element of $\operatorname{Aut} V$ and that $P\pi = P$ for every point $P$ of the hyperplane $H$ of $V$. By multiplying $f$ by a suitable non-zero scalar we may assume that the restriction of $f$ to $H$ is the identity mapping $1_H$. Let $c$ be a vector of $V$ not in $H$; then any vector of $V$ can be expressed uniquely in the form $xc + h$ where $x \in F$ and $h \in H$. By our assumption

$$(xc + h)f - (xc + h) = x(cf - c).$$

If $cf = c$ then of course $f$ is the identity mapping on all of $V$. If $cf \neq c$ we may put $a = cf - c \neq 0$. The point $A = [a]$ is then a center as required.

If $A$ and $B$ are two distinct centers then $P\pi = P$ for all points $P$ not on $AB$, which forces $\pi$ to be the identity. Hence the center is unique.
