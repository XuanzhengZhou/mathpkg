Suppose $\pi$ is a projective isomorphism and there exists a line $\ell$ such that the restriction $\pi|_\ell$ is a projectivity. Then $\pi|_\ell = \mathcal{P}(g)$ for some linear isomorphism $g$ between the 2-dimensional subspaces corresponding to $\ell$ and $\ell\pi$.

To show $\pi$ is globally a projectivity, one uses Theorem 6: if $\operatorname{pdim} \mathcal{P} \geq 2$, then $\pi = \mathcal{P}(f)$ for some semi-linear isomorphism $f$. The condition that $\pi|_\ell$ is already a projectivity forces the associated field automorphism $\zeta$ to be the identity, because the restriction of a semi-linear isomorphism with respect to $\zeta$ to a line yields a projectivity on that line only if $\zeta$ acts trivially. Hence $f$ is in fact linear, and $\pi$ is a projectivity.
