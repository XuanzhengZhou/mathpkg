The coefficient $m_j$ is a normalized linear combination of the conjugates $\sigma^k \alpha$ of the primitive $\lambda$th root of unity, weighted by powers of a primitive $(\lambda-1)$st root of unity $\beta$. Explicitly,

$$m_j = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \beta^{jk} \sigma^k \alpha,$$

where $\sigma : \alpha \mapsto \alpha^\gamma$ is the conjugation corresponding to a primitive root $\gamma$ modulo $\lambda$. The $m_j$ arise naturally when expressing $L(1, \chi_j)$ in the compact form $L(1, \chi_j) = (-1)^j m_j \sum \beta^{-jk} \log(1/(1-\sigma^k \alpha))$. For the even and odd character cases, $m_{2\nu}$ and $m_{2\nu+1}$ appear as prefactors in the explicit formulas.
