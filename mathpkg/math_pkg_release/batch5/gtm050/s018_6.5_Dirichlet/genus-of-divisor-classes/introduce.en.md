In Gauss's theory of binary quadratic forms, divisor classes are partitioned into genera according to their character values. A genus is the set of all divisor classes that share a common character, where the character is a tuple of signs determined by the Legendre symbols of the primes dividing the discriminant. This concept plays a central role in Gauss's second proof of quadratic reciprocity, where the number of genera that actually occur is at most half of the number of possible characters.
