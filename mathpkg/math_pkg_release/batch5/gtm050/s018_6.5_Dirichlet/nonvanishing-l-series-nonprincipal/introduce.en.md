The nonvanishing of $L(1, \chi)$ for nonprincipal Dirichlet characters is a crucial result in both Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions and Kummer's derivation of the class number formula. For a prime modulus $\lambda$, the proof is particularly simple: if $\chi$ is not real (i.e., $\chi(\gamma) \neq \pm 1$), then $L(1, \chi) = 0$ would imply the vanishing for two distinct characters, which is impossible. For the real nonprincipal character $\chi_{\mu}$ with $\chi_{\mu}(\gamma) = -1$, the explicit formula for $L(1, \chi_{\mu})$ shows directly that $L(1, \chi_{\mu}) \neq 0$.
