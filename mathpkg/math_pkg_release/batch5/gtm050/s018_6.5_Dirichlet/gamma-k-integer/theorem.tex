Let $\lambda$ be a prime and let $\gamma$ be a primitive root modulo $\lambda$. For each $k = 0, 1, \dots, \lambda-2$, define $\gamma_k$ to be the unique integer satisfying

$$0 < \gamma_k < \lambda, \qquad \gamma_k \equiv \gamma^k \pmod{\lambda}.$$

When $\alpha = e^{2\pi i/\lambda}$, the conjugate $\sigma^k \alpha$ equals $e^{2\pi i \gamma^k/\lambda}$, and the corresponding angle $\theta$ in $(0, 2\pi)$ is $\theta = 2\pi \gamma_k / \lambda$.
