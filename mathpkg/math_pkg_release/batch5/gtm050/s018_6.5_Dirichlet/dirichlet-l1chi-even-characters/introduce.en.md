Dirichlet derived explicit formulas for $L(1,\chi)$ at $s=1$ in terms of cyclotomic units. These formulas are fundamental to Kummer's class number formula for cyclotomic fields. For even characters $\chi_{2\nu}$ (where $\chi(\gamma) = \beta^{2\nu}$ with $\beta$ a primitive $(\lambda-1)$-st root of unity), the value $L(1,\chi_{2\nu})$ is expressed as a product of the cyclotomic "period" $m_{2\nu}$ and a sum of logarithms of absolute values $|1-\sigma^k\alpha|$. The derivation uses the fact that terms come in complex-conjugate pairs, converting complex logarithms to real logarithms of absolute values.
