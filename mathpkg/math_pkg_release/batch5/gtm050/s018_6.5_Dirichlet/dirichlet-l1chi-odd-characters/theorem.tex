For $j$ odd, let $\chi_{2\nu+1}$ be the character modulo $\lambda$ for which $\chi_{2\nu+1}(\gamma) = \beta^{2\nu+1}$. Let $\gamma_k$ be the integer determined by $0 < \gamma_k < \lambda$, $\gamma_k \equiv \gamma^k \pmod{\lambda}$. Then with $\alpha = e^{2\pi i/\lambda}$,

$$L(1, \chi_{2\nu+1}) = \frac{i\pi}{\lambda} m_{2\nu+1} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \gamma_k \beta^{-(2\nu+1)k}$$

where

$$m_{2\nu+1} = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \beta^{(2\nu+1)k} \sigma^k \alpha.$$
