For odd characters $\chi_{2\nu+1}$, the conjugate pairing that simplified the even case no longer applies, and the formula takes a different form involving the integers $\gamma_k$. Choosing $\alpha = e^{2\pi i/\lambda}$, the logarithm splits as

$$\log \frac{1}{1-\sigma^k \alpha} = -\log\left(2\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\right) + \frac{i}{2}(\pi - \theta),$$

where $\theta = 2\pi \gamma_k / \lambda$. The real parts cancel in the sum over the full set of $\lambda-1$ terms, leaving only the imaginary part. The result,

$$L(1, \chi_{2\nu+1}) = \frac{i\pi}{\lambda} m_{2\nu+1} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \gamma_k \beta^{-(2\nu+1)k},$$

expresses $L(1, \chi_{2\nu+1})$ as a pure imaginary multiple of a character sum weighted by the residues $\gamma_k$. This formula is essential for evaluating the class number formula for the full cyclotomic field.
