The $c_\nu$ coefficients arise in Dirichlet's formula for $L(1,\chi)$, expanding the L-series into a linear combination of logarithms of cyclotomic units. For a Dirichlet character $\chi$ modulo a prime $\lambda$, each $c_\nu$ is defined as

$$c_\nu = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{j=1}^{\lambda-1} \chi(j) \alpha^{-j\nu}$$

where $\alpha$ is a primitive $\lambda$th root of unity. These coefficients encapsulate the Fourier-like transform of the character with respect to powers of $\alpha$. A useful rewriting orders the terms by powers of a primitive root $\gamma$ modulo $\lambda$, yielding $b_k = c_\nu$ with $\nu \equiv \gamma^k \bmod \lambda$.
