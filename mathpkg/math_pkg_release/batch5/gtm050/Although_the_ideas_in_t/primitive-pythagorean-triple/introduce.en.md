A primitive Pythagorean triple is a Pythagorean triple $(x, y, z)$ of positive integers satisfying $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$ whose entries share no common divisor greater than $1$. Every Pythagorean triple can be reduced to a primitive one by dividing through by the greatest common divisor of its three entries. Conversely, any primitive Pythagorean triple can be scaled by an arbitrary positive integer $d$ to produce a (generally non-primitive) Pythagorean triple $(dx, dy, dz)$. This notion is fundamental to the analytic method for constructing all Pythagorean triples, as it suffices to determine all primitive triples.
