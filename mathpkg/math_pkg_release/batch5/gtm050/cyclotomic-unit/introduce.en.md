A unit in the ring of cyclotomic integers is an invertible element, i.e., a divisor of $1$. Units are analogous to $\pm 1$ in the ordinary integers. In cyclotomic integers, there are infinitely many units, including powers of $\alpha$ and the cyclotomic units constructed from quotients $(1-\alpha^k)/(1-\alpha)$. Kummer showed that units congruent to integers modulo $\lambda$ are $\lambda$-th powers, a crucial fact for proving FLT for regular primes.
