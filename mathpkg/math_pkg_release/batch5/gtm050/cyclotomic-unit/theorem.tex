A cyclotomic integer $e(\alpha)$ is called a \text{unit} if it divides $1$, i.e., if there exists another cyclotomic integer $f(\alpha)$ such that $e(\alpha)f(\alpha) = 1$. The units form a multiplicative group. The cyclotomic units include $\pm 1, \pm \alpha, \pm \alpha^2, \ldots, \pm \alpha^{\lambda-1}$ and their products.
