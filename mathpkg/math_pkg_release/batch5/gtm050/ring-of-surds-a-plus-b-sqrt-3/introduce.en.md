The ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ consists of numbers of the form $a + b\sqrt{-3}$ with integer coefficients. Euler used this ring in his attempted proof of FLT for $n=3$, exploiting the factorization $p^2 + 3q^2 = (p + q\sqrt{-3})(p - q\sqrt{-3})$. While unique factorization fails in this ring, the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$ (where $\omega = (-1+\sqrt{-3})/2$) does have unique factorization.
