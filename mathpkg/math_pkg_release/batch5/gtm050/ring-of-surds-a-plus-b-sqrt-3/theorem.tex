Consider numbers of the form $a + b\sqrt{-3}$ where $a, b$ are integers. Addition, subtraction, and multiplication are defined by:
$$(a + b\sqrt{-3}) \pm (c + d\sqrt{-3}) = (a \pm c) + (b \pm d)\sqrt{-3},$$
$$(a + b\sqrt{-3})(c + d\sqrt{-3}) = (ac - 3bd) + (ad + bc)\sqrt{-3}.$$
These "numbers" form a commutative ring with unit $1 = 1 + 0\sqrt{-3}$.
