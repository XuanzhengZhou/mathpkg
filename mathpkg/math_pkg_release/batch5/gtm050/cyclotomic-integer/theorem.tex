Let $\lambda$ be a prime and let $\alpha \neq 1$ be a complex root of $\alpha^\lambda = 1$. A \textbf{cyclotomic integer} is a number of the form
$$f(\alpha) = a_0 + a_1\alpha + a_2\alpha^2 + \cdots + a_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$$
where $a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_{\lambda-1} \in \mathbb{Z}$, subject to the relation
$$1 + \alpha + \alpha^2 + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1} = 0.$$

The \textbf{conjugates} of $f(\alpha)$ are $f(\alpha^2), f(\alpha^3), \ldots, f(\alpha^{\lambda-1})$.

The \textbf{norm} of $f(\alpha)$ is
$$Nf(\alpha) = f(\alpha)f(\alpha^2)f(\alpha^3)\cdots f(\alpha^{\lambda-1}) \in \mathbb{Z}.$$

A cyclotomic integer with norm $\pm 1$ is called a \textbf{unit}.
