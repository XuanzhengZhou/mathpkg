If $p$ is a prime and $a$ is an integer not divisible by $p$, then
$$a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}.$$

More generally, let $d$ be the least positive integer such that $a^d \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$ (called the \textit{order} of $a$ modulo $p$). Then $d$ divides $p - 1$, and the powers $a^0, a^1, \ldots, a^{d-1}$ leave $d$ distinct remainders modulo $p$, which occur in a cyclic pattern.
