No Pythagorean triangle (i.e., a right triangle with integer sides) can have area equal to a square. Equivalently, there do not exist positive integers $x, y, z$ such that $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$ and $\frac{1}{2}xy$ is a perfect square.

In the language of Fermat's Last Theorem, this implies that $z^4 - x^4$ cannot be a square, much less a fourth power, thereby proving the $n = 4$ case of Fermat's Last Theorem.
