Mersenne primes are primes of the form $2^p - 1$ with $p$ prime. They are named after Marin Mersenne, a contemporary and frequent correspondent of Fermat. The observation that $n$ must be prime for $2^n - 1$ to be prime (since $2^{km} - 1$ factors) reduces the search to prime exponents. This reduction was central to Fermat's discovery of his famous theorem.
