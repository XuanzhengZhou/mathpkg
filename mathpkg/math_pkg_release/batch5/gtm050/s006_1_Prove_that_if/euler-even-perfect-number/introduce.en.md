Euler proved the converse of Euclid's theorem: every even perfect number is of the form $2^{p-1}(2^p - 1)$ with $2^p - 1$ prime. This completes the Euclid-Euler characterization of even perfect numbers. The existence of odd perfect numbers remains an open problem to this day.
