Let $p$ be a prime and let $a$ be an integer not divisible by $p$. Let $d$ be the \textit{order} of $a$ modulo $p$, i.e., the least positive integer such that $a^d \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The powers $a^0, a^1, \ldots, a^{d-1}$ leave $d$ distinct remainders modulo $p$.
\item $a^m \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$ if and only if $d \mid m$.
\item $d$ divides $p - 1$.
\end{enumerate}

Furthermore, the $p-1$ nonzero remainders modulo $p$ can be partitioned into sets of size $d$ by considering the remainders of $k, ak, a^2k, \ldots, a^{d-1}k$ for each remainder $k$ not yet included.
