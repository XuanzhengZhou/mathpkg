A perfect number is one that equals the sum of its proper divisors. The study of perfect numbers dates back to the prehistory of number theory and held great fascination for scholars over many centuries, from the mysticism of numerology to more scientific attempts by Euclid, Euler, and others. The concept leads naturally to the study of Mersenne primes and to Fermat's theorem.
