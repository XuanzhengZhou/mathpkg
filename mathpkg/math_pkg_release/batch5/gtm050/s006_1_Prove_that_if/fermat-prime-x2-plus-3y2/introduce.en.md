Fermat discovered that a prime other than 3 is representable in the form $x^2 + 3y^2$ if and only if it is of the form $6n+1$. This is closely related to the fact that every prime other than 3 is of one of the forms $3n+1$ or $3n+2$, and those of the form $3n+1$ must be of the form $6n+1$ (since oddness forces $n$ even). The sufficiency half reduces, via the product identity, to proving every prime $3n+1$ is of form $x^2+3y^2$, which is proved in the next chapter.
