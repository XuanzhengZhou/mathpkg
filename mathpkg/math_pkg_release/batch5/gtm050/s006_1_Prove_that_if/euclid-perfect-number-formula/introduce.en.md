Euclid proved that if $2^p - 1$ is prime, the formula $2^{p-1}(2^p - 1)$ yields a perfect number. Euler later proved the converse: every even perfect number must be of Euclid's form. Whether any odd perfect numbers exist remains a famous unsolved problem. This result reduces the search for even perfect numbers to finding Mersenne primes.
