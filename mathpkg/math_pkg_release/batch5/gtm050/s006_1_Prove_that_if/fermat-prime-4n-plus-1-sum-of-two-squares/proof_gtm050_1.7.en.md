Fermat stated this theorem many times and claimed he could prove it rigorously, though he never put the proof in writing. Complete proofs, which may or may not be the proofs Fermat had in mind, are given in Sections 2.4 and 2.6 of the book. The proof uses the method of infinite descent and properties of the Gaussian integers $\mathbb{Z}[i]$.

The uniqueness of the representation (up to order and signs) is also proved in those sections.
