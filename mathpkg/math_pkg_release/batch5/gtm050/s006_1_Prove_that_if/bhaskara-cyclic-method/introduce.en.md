The cyclic method (Sanskrit: chakravala) is an ancient Indian algorithm for solving Pell's equation, developed by Bhaskara Acharya (born 1114 AD) and earlier Indian mathematicians. The method iteratively constructs successively better approximations, using the key identity $(a^2 - Ab^2)(c^2 - Ad^2) = (ac + Abd)^2 - A(ad + bc)^2$. For $A = 67$, the method produces the solution $48842^2 - 67 \cdot 5967^2 = 1$ after 8 steps. The English method of Wallis and Brouncker is conceptually different but computationally similar.
