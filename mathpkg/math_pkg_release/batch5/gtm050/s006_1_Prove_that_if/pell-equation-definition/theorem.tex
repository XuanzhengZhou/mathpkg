\textit{Pell's equation} is the Diophantine equation
$$y^2 - Ax^2 = 1$$
where $A$ is a given positive integer that is not a perfect square. The problem is to find all integer solutions $(x, y)$.

The name is due to a historical mistake by Euler, who attributed the method of solution to John Pell, an English mathematician who had nothing to do with it. The problem was first posed by Fermat in 1657.
