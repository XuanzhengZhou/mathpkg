This identity is the analog of the Brahmagupta-Fibonacci identity for the quadratic form $x^2 + 2y^2$. It shows that the product of two numbers representable in this form is again representable in this form. Fermat discovered that rules similar to those for sums of two squares apply to representations by $x^2 + 2y^2$.
