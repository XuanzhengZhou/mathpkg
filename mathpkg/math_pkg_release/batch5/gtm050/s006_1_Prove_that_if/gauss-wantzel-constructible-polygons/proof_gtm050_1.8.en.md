Gauss proved the sufficiency: if $n = 2^k p_1 p_2 \ldots p_m$ where the $p_i$ are distinct Fermat primes, then a regular $n$-gon is constructible with ruler and compass. The proof uses the fact that a regular $p$-gon for a Fermat prime $p$ can be constructed because $p-1 = 2^{2^j}$ is a power of 2, making the construction reducible to a sequence of quadratic extensions. Gauss demonstrated this concretely for the 17-gon.

Wantzel proved the necessity: if a regular $n$-gon is constructible, then $\varphi(n)$ must be a power of 2, which forces $n$ to have the stated form. The proof uses the algebraic characterization of constructible numbers as those lying in an extension obtained by adjoining a sequence of square roots.
