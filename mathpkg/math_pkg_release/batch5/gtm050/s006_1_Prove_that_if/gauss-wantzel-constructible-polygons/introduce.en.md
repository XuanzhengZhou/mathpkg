The Gauss-Wantzel theorem classifies exactly which regular $n$-gons are constructible with ruler and compass. Gauss proved the sufficiency (showing that a regular 17-gon is constructible because 17 is a Fermat prime), and Wantzel later proved the necessity. This theorem provides one of the most striking applications of Fermat primes, connecting ancient Greek construction problems to modern number theory.
