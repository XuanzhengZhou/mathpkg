A positive integer $n$ can be written as a sum of two squares (including $0^2 = 0$) if and only if $n$ is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A square, or
\item A prime number that is itself a sum of two squares (i.e., a prime of the form $2$ or $4k+1$), or
\item A product of such numbers.
\end{enumerate}

Equivalently, a number can be written as a sum of two squares if and only if, in its prime factorization, every prime of the form $4n+3$ appears with an even exponent.
