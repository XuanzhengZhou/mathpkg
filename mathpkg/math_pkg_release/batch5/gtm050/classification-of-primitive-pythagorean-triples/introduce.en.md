This theorem gives a complete parametrization of all primitive Pythagorean triples. It is proved by factoring $x^2 = z^2 - y^2 = (z+y)(z-y)$ and using the fact that if a product of two relatively prime integers is a square, then each factor must be a square. This factorization technique, based on unique factorization in $\mathbb{Z}$, is the prototype for the more sophisticated arguments involving unique factorization in rings of cyclotomic integers that appear throughout the book.
