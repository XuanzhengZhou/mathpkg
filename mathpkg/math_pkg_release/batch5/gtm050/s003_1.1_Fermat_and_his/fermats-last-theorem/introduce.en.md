Fermat's Last Theorem is the statement, originally asserted by Pierre de Fermat in a marginal note around 1637, that for any integer $n > 2$ the Diophantine equation $x^n + y^n = z^n$ has no solutions in positive integers. Fermat claimed to have a "truly marvelous demonstration" but never published it. The problem remained open for over three centuries until it was proved by Andrew Wiles in 1994. In Edwards's formulation, only positive whole numbers $x, y, z$ are considered, tacitly excluding the trivial zero case. The theorem is the namesake and central subject of the entire book.
