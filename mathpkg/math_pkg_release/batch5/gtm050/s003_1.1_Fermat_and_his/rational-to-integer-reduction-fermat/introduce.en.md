This elementary lemma shows that for the Diophantine equation $x^n + y^n = z^n$, the existence of rational solutions implies the existence of integer solutions. The argument uses the simple technique of clearing denominators: if $x, y, z$ are rational numbers with common denominator $d$, multiplying through by $d$ produces an integer triple satisfying the same equation. This reduces Fermat's Last Theorem to the statement about positive integers.
