Let $x, y, z$ be rational numbers satisfying $x^n + y^n = z^n$, and let $d$ be a common denominator of $x, y, z$ (for instance, the least common denominator). Then $xd, yd, zd$ are integers, and

$$(xd)^n + (yd)^n = d^n x^n + d^n y^n = d^n(x^n + y^n) = d^n z^n = (zd)^n.$$

Thus $(xd, yd, zd)$ is a triple of integers satisfying the same equation $X^n + Y^n = Z^n$. Therefore, if there exists a rational solution to $x^n + y^n = z^n$, there also exists an integer solution.
