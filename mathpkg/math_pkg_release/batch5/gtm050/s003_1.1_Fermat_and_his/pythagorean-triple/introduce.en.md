A Pythagorean triple is a triple of positive integers $(x, y, z)$ satisfying the Pythagorean equation $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$. Geometrically, such a triple corresponds to a right triangle with integer side lengths. The simplest example is $(3, 4, 5)$, and triples that are scalar multiples of each other (such as $(6, 8, 10)$ and $(9, 12, 15)$) are regarded as essentially the same. The problem of finding all Pythagorean triples was studied by Euclid and, over a thousand years earlier, by the ancient Babylonians.
