If two positive integers are coprime and their product is a perfect square, then each factor must itself be a perfect square. This is a fundamental lemma in elementary number theory, proved by the method of infinite descent. It plays a crucial role in Fermat's proof that the equation $x^4 + y^4 = z^4$ has no nontrivial integer solutions.
