This is an equivalent reformulation of Fermat's theorem that the area of a right triangle cannot be a square. By clearing denominators, the geometric statement about rational right triangles translates to the arithmetic statement that no primitive Pythagorean triple can have an area that is a perfect square. The area of a right triangle with legs $x$ and $y$ is $\frac{1}{2}xy$, and since $x, y$ cannot both be odd in a primitive triple, this is always an integer.
