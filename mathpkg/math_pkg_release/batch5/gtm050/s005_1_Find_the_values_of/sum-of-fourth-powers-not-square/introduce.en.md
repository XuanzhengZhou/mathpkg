A stronger statement than Fermat's Last Theorem for $n = 4$: the sum of two fourth powers of positive integers can never be a perfect square. This is what the infinite descent argument actually proves. Since every fourth power is a square, this immediately implies that $x^4 + y^4 = z^4$ has no positive integer solutions.
