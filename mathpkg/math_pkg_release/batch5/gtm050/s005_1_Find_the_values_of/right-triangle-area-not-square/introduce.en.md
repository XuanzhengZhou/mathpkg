Fermat proved that the area of a right triangle can never be a square. This is historically significant as one of the few proofs Fermat actually wrote out in detail. The problem originates from Diophantus' Arithmetic, Book VI, which deals with finding Pythagorean triangles satisfying various area conditions. Fermat's proof uses the method of infinite descent applied to the parametrization of Pythagorean triples.
