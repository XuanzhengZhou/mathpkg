The method of infinite descent is a proof technique invented by Fermat. It establishes the impossibility of a property for positive integers by showing that the existence of any integer with that property would imply the existence of a strictly smaller positive integer with the same property, leading to an infinite descending sequence which cannot exist by the well-ordering principle. Fermat considered this his signature method and stated that all his number-theoretic proofs used it.
