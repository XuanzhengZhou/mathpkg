The integer expression $p^2 + 3q^2$ can be factored as a product of conjugate surds in the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$: namely, $(p + q\sqrt{-3})(p - q\sqrt{-3})$. This factorization is the key insight that allows Euler to analyze when $p^2 + 3q^2$ is a cube, replacing the more cumbersome identity $(a^2 + 3b^2)(c^2 + 3d^2) = (ac - 3bd)^2 + 3(ad + bc)^2$ with simple multiplication of surds.
