This theorem gives necessary and sufficient conditions for a number represented by the quadratic form $ax^2 + by^2$ to be prime, under the hypothesis that each genus for determinant $D = -ab$ contains a single class. Numbers $n = ab$ satisfying these hypotheses are Euler's "convenient numbers" (numeri idonei). The conditions generalize Euler's criterion for primality of numbers of the form $x^2 + ny^2$ when the class group has one class per genus. The proof uses divisor theory for quadratic integers with determinant $D = -ab$.
