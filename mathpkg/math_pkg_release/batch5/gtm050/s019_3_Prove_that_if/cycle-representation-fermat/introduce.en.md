This theorem connects the cyclic method of quadratic integers to Fermat's classical theorem on representing primes congruent to 1 mod 4 as sums of two squares. When $D$ is a positive prime $\equiv 1 \pmod 4$, the cycle of the principal class contains consecutive divisors whose norms are negatives of each other. This property provides an effective algorithm for finding integers $u, v$ such that $D = u^2 + v^2$, a result that goes back to Gauss (Disquisitiones Arithmeticae, Art. 265). The method is illustrated for $D = 13$ and $D = 233$.
