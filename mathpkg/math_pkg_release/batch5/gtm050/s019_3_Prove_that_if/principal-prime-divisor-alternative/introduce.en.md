This theorem describes the structure of the divisor class group when the determinant $D$ is the product of two primes both congruent to $3$ modulo $4$. In this case, both $p$ and $q$ ramify, producing two nontrivial ambiguous classes. The theorem asserts that exactly one of the corresponding prime divisors $(p,*)$ or $(q,*)$ is principal, meaning it represents the identity in the class group. The other is non-principal, giving a class group with exactly two elements (one nontrivial ambiguous class).
