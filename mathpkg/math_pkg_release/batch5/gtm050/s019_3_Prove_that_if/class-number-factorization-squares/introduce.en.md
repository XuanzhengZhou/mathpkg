This theorem expresses the class number $h$ of quadratic integers in two complementary factorizations. The first, $h = gn$, factors $h$ by genus classification: $g$ genera each containing $n$ classes. The second, $h = sa$, factors $h$ by the squaring homomorphism: $a$ ambiguous classes (the kernel of squaring) and $s$ distinct squares (the image). In group-theoretic terms, squaring is a homomorphism of the class group whose kernel has $a$ elements and whose image has $s$ elements, giving $h = sa$ immediately.
