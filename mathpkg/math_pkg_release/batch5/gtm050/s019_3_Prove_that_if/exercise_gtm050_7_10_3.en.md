Prove that if $n = 165$ then the necessary conditions of Exercise 2 are sufficient.

[Hint: If $k$ is not prime then the divisor of $x + y\sqrt{-165}$ is either of the form $A_1A_2$ where $A_1$ and $A_2$ are relatively prime, or it is a power of a prime divisor $(p, u)^n$ where $n > 1$. In the first case $\bar{A}_1A_2$ is principal but distinct from both $A_1A_2$ and $\bar{A}_1A_2$, which contradicts (3). In the second case, there is a principal divisor which has norm $k$ and is divisible by $p$, and this too contradicts (3).]
