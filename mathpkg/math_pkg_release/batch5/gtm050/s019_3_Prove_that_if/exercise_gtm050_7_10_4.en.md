Prove that if $a$ and $b$ are positive integers with the properties that $ab$ is squarefree, $ab \neq 3$ mod 4, and each genus for the determinant $D = -ab$ contains only one class then the following are both necessary and sufficient conditions for $k = ax^2 + by^2$ to be prime:

(1) $ax$ and $by$ are relatively prime.

(2) $ax$ and $by$ have opposite parity.

(3) The only other representations $k = au^2 + bv^2$ are those in which $u = \pm x$, $v = \pm y$.

[Hint: Assume without loss of generality that $a = p_1p_2 \cdots p_n$ is odd. Then $ak$ is the norm of a principal divisor of the form $(p_1, *)(p_2, *) \cdots (p_n, *)A$.]

A number $n = ab$ of this sort---such as 165---is a case of what Euler called a convenient number (*numerus idoneus*). A full discussion of convenient numbers must deal with the cases $ab \equiv 3$ mod 4 and/or $ab$ not squarefree. See Exercises 8--12 of Section 8.1.
