This corollary is the crucial step in Gauss's second proof of quadratic reciprocity. Since the class number factors as $h = gn = sa$, and since the square of any class is in the principal genus, we have $s \leqslant n$, which implies $g \leqslant a$. Combined with the bound on $a$ from the previous section, this shows that at most half of the theoretically possible genus characters actually occur. Gauss deduced the law of quadratic reciprocity (and its supplementary laws) from this restriction on character occurrence.
