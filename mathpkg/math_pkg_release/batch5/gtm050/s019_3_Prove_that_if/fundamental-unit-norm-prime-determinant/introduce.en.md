This theorem characterizes the norm of the fundamental unit of a real quadratic order whose determinant $D > 0$ is an odd prime. The norm is $-1$ exactly when $D \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, and $+1$ when $D \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$. This result is significant because the sign of the norm determines whether the Pell equation $x^2 - Dy^2 = -1$ has integer solutions. The proof uses the theory of ambiguous classes developed in Section 7.10.
