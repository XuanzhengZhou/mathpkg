This theorem identifies a fundamental mechanism for producing ambiguous divisor classes in the theory of quadratic integers. When a prime $p$ ramifies in the quadratic order, the corresponding prime divisor $(p,*)$ squares to the principal divisor. Consequently, the class of $(p,*)$, as well as any product of such classes and the class of $(-1,*)$ when $D > 0$, is ambiguous. This construction accounts for all ambiguous classes in every example examined.
