This structure theorem states that every ambiguous divisor class in the divisor class group of quadratic integers is represented by a product of ramified prime divisors. The proof proceeds by analyzing the cyclic method: if $A_0 \sim \bar{A}_0$ is ambiguous, one shows that either $A_0$ itself or some divisor equivalent to it divides $4D$ (or $D$ when $D \equiv 1 \pmod 4$), and all prime divisors of $4D$ are ramified primes. This characterization is the key to counting ambiguous classes.
