For a quadratic field with discriminant $D$, the number $a$ of ambiguous divisor classes (classes whose square is the principal class) is at most $2^{m+\varepsilon-1}$, where $m$ is the number of distinct odd prime factors of $D$ and $\varepsilon = 0$ or $1$ depending on $D \pmod{4}$.

Every ambiguous class contains a divisor of the form $(p_1, *)(p_2, *) \cdots (p_k, *)$ where $p_i$ are primes that ramify in the field (or $-1$ when $D > 0$).
