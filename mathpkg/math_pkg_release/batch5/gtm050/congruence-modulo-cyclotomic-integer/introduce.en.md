Congruence modulo a cyclotomic integer generalizes ordinary modular arithmetic to the cyclotomic setting. The relation satisfies the usual properties (reflexive, symmetric, transitive, compatible with addition and multiplication). This concept is fundamental to Kummer's analysis of prime factors of binomials $x + \alpha^j y$, where studying congruences modulo an unknown prime factor $h(\alpha)$ yields enough information to determine its properties.
