Fermat's Last Theorem for an odd prime exponent $n$ is traditionally divided into two cases. Case I (also called the first case) asserts that the equation $x^n + y^n = z^n$ has no nontrivial integer solutions in which none of $x, y, z$ is divisible by $n$. Equivalently, reformulated symmetrically as $x^n + y^n + z^n = 0$, it states that this equation has no solution in integers all prime to $n$. Case II (the second case) allows one of the variables to be divisible by $n$. Sophie Germain's Theorem addresses Case I specifically, providing sufficient conditions for its truth.
