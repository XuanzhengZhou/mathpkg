The case $n=4$ of Fermat's Last Theorem was proved by Fermat himself using the method of infinite descent combined with the classification of Pythagorean triples. The proof shows that if $x^4 + y^4 = z^4$, then one can construct a strictly smaller solution, leading to an infinite descent. This is the simplest nontrivial case, and it implies FLT for all $n$ divisible by $4$.
