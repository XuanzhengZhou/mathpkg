Pell's equation $x^2 - Ay^2 = 1$, despite its name, was studied long before Pell by ancient Indian mathematicians (Brahmagupta, Bhaskara II) who developed the cyclic method (chakravala) for its solution. Fermat posed it as a challenge to English mathematicians. The misattribution to Pell is due to Euler. The equation has deep connections to units in real quadratic fields and to the theory of binary quadratic forms.
