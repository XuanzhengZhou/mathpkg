This simple but crucial observation reduces the infinite number of possible exponents to just the odd primes (and the case $n=4$). The argument is: if $n = mk$ and $x^n + y^n = z^n$, then $(x^k)^m + (y^k)^m = (z^k)^m$. Since every $n > 2$ is divisible either by 4 or by some odd prime, proving the impossibility for $n=4$ and all odd primes suffices. This reduction focuses all subsequent effort in the book on prime exponents.
