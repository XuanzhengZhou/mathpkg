Euler sought to characterize when an integer of the form $p^2 + 3q^2$ is a cube. His approach, using the factorization $p^2 + 3q^2 = (p + q\sqrt{-3})(p - q\sqrt{-3})$, led to a sufficient condition: if the factor $p + q\sqrt{-3}$ itself is a cube in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$, say $p + q\sqrt{-3} = (a + b\sqrt{-3})^3$, then multiplying by its conjugate gives $p^2 + 3q^2 = (a^2 + 3b^2)^3$, a perfect integer cube. This reduces the problem to finding integer solutions $(a,b)$ such that $(a+b\sqrt{-3})^3$ matches a given surd.
