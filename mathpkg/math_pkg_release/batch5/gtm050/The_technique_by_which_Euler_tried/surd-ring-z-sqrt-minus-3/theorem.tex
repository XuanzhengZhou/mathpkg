Let $a, b \in \mathbb{Z}$ be integers. A \textbf{surd} (or number of the form $a + b\sqrt{-3}$) is an expression

$$
a + b\sqrt{-3}
$$

where addition, subtraction, and multiplication are defined by the usual rules of arithmetic together with the identity $(\sqrt{-3})^2 = -3$. The set of all such numbers is denoted $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ and forms a commutative ring with unit $1 = 1 + 0\sqrt{-3}$.
