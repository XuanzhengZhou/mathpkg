If a surd $\alpha = a + b\sqrt{-3} \in \mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ satisfies $\alpha^3 = p + q\sqrt{-3}$ for some integers $p, q$, then its conjugate $\overline{\alpha} = a - b\sqrt{-3}$ satisfies

$$
\overline{\alpha}^3 = p - q\sqrt{-3}.
$$

In general, for any $\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$, the conjugate of $\alpha\beta$ is $\overline{\alpha}\,\overline{\beta}$, and consequently $\overline{\alpha^3} = (\overline{\alpha})^3$.
