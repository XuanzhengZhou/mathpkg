The conjugation map $a + b\sqrt{-3} \mapsto a - b\sqrt{-3}$ in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ is a ring automorphism. It follows that the conjugate of a product is the product of conjugates, and in particular, the conjugate of a cube is the cube of the conjugate. This simple observation, used by Euler, allows one to conclude from $p + q\sqrt{-3} = (a + b\sqrt{-3})^3$ that $p - q\sqrt{-3} = (a - b\sqrt{-3})^3$, completing the factorization $p^2 + 3q^2 = (a^2 + 3b^2)^3$.
