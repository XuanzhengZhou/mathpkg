Let $c = 5$. The equation

$$
49 = 2^2 + 5 \cdot 3^2
$$

expresses $49 = 7^2$ (a square) in the form $x^2 + 5y^2$. Euler's method would require solving the system

$$
\begin{cases}
2 = 5q^2 - p^2, \\
3 = 2pq
\end{cases}
$$

for integers $p, q$. This system has no rational solutions with $p, q \in \mathbb{Z}$, as the hyperbolas intersect only at $p = \sqrt{5/2}$, $q = \sqrt{9/10}$ (and their negatives), showing that Euler's conclusion $x = cq^2 - p^2$, $y = 2pq$ is not universally valid.
