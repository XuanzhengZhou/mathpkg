For integers $a, b, c, d \in \mathbb{Z}$, the product of two surds $a + b\sqrt{-3}$ and $c + d\sqrt{-3}$ is given by

$$
(a + b\sqrt{-3})(c + d\sqrt{-3}) = (ac - 3bd) + (ad + bc)\sqrt{-3}.
$$

The usual laws of commutativity, associativity, and distributivity apply, and $1 \cdot (a + b\sqrt{-3}) = a + b\sqrt{-3}$.
