The multiplication of surds $a + b\sqrt{-3}$ follows from expanding the product $(a + b\sqrt{-3})(c + d\sqrt{-3})$ using distributivity and the defining relation $(\sqrt{-3})^2 = -3$. This yields the formula $(ac - 3bd) + (ad + bc)\sqrt{-3}$. The operation satisfies all the usual ring axioms: commutativity, associativity, distributivity, and the existence of a multiplicative identity $1 = 1 + 0\sqrt{-3}$. This multiplication rule is the foundation for Euler's algebraic manipulations with surds.
