The multiplication follows directly from the distributive law and the defining relation $(\sqrt{-3})^2 = -3$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
(a + b\sqrt{-3})(c + d\sqrt{-3})
&= ac + ad\sqrt{-3} + bc\sqrt{-3} + bd(\sqrt{-3})^2 \\
&= ac + (ad + bc)\sqrt{-3} + bd(-3) \\
&= (ac - 3bd) + (ad + bc)\sqrt{-3}.
\end{aligned}
$$

The commutativity, associativity, and distributivity properties are inherited from the usual arithmetic of integers extended by the formal symbol $\sqrt{-3}$. The identity element is $1 = 1 + 0\sqrt{-3}$, since $1 \cdot (a + b\sqrt{-3}) = a + b\sqrt{-3}$.
