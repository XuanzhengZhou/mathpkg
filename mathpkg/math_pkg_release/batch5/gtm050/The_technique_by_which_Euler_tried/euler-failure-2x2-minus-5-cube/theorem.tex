Euler considered the equation $2x^2 - 5 = \text{cube}$ and attempted to solve it by his method of factoring in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{10}]$. Setting

$$
x\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{5} = (a\sqrt{2} + b\sqrt{5})^3,
$$

and expanding, one obtains the system

$$
\begin{cases}
x = 2a^3 + 15ab^2, \\
1 = 6a^2b + 5b^3.
\end{cases}
$$

The second equation implies $b = \pm 1$ and $6a^2 + 5b^2 = \pm 1$, which is clearly impossible. Therefore Euler's method indicates that $2x^2 - 5$ cannot be a cube. This is Euler's own observation of a failure case, noted in his work (§195) and discussed by Dickson.
