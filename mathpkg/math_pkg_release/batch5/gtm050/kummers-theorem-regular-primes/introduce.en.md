This is the central theorem of Kummer's theory and the main result of the book. Kummer proved in the 1840s that Fermat's Last Theorem is true for all "regular" prime exponents. Remarkably, all primes less than 100 except 37, 59, and 67 are regular, so the theorem was proved at one stroke for most small primes. The condition on Bernoulli numbers provides a computable criterion for regularity. Kummer's theorem represents the culmination of the "genetic" approach: it grew out of Euler's work on $n=3$, Dirichlet and Legendre's work on $n=5$, and Kummer's own theory of ideal factorization. The proof combines the theory of cyclotomic integers, divisors, the class number formula, and deep analysis of units.
