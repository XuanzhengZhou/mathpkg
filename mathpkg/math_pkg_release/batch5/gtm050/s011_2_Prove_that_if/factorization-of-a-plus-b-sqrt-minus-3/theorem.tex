Let $a$ and $b$ be relatively prime integers. Then $a + b\sqrt{-3}$ can be written in the form

$$a + b\sqrt{-3} = \pm (p_1 \pm q_1\sqrt{-3})(p_2 \pm q_2\sqrt{-3}) \cdots (p_n \pm q_n\sqrt{-3})$$

where the $p_i$ and $q_i$ are positive integers and each factor $p_i \pm q_i\sqrt{-3}$ corresponds to a prime (or the power of 4) dividing $a^2 + 3b^2$, the sign being uniquely determined.
