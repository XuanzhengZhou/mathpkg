This proposition, Statement (3) in Edwards' exposition, establishes a factorization theorem for elements of the quadratic integer ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$. When $a$ and $b$ are coprime, the "number" $a+b\sqrt{-3}$ factors uniquely (up to signs) into linear factors $p_i \pm q_i\sqrt{-3}$, where each factor corresponds to a prime divisor of the norm $a^2+3b^2$. The theorem is proved by iteratively applying the identity $P = p^2+3q^2$ from Statement (5') (the odd factor theorem) and the divisibility argument showing that either $pb+aq$ or $pb-aq$ is divisible by $P$. This factorization is the crucial bridge between the arithmetic in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ and the integer norm form, enabling Euler's proof of the cube lemma for the $n=3$ case.
