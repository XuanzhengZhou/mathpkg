Only a slight modification of the proof of this section is needed to prove that if $a$ and $b$ are relatively prime and if $a^2 + 2b^2$ is a cube then there exist integers $p$ and $q$ such that $a + b\sqrt{-2} = (p + q\sqrt{-2})^3$. Use this fact to prove Fermat's Last Theorem for $n=3$ in the case where $x^3 + y^3 = z^3$ is rewritten in the form $x^3 = z^3 - y^3 = (z-y)(z^2 + zy + y^2)$ and $z-y$ is assumed to be of the form $2u^2$ rather than $3u^2$.
