Fermat stated in a 1654 letter to Pascal that every prime of the form $4n+1$ is a sum of two squares. This is one of Fermat's most famous theorems in number theory, concerning the representation of primes as sums of two perfect squares. The theorem is closely connected to the study of the Gaussian integers and the factorization of primes in the ring $\mathbb{Z}[i]$.
