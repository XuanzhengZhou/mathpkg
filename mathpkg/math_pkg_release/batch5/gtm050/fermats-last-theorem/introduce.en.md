Fermat's Last Theorem asserts that the Diophantine equation $x^n + y^n = z^n$ has no solutions in positive integers when $n > 2$. Fermat famously claimed to have a "truly marvelous demonstration" in a marginal note of his copy of Diophantus' Arithmetic. The theorem remained unproven for over 350 years until Andrew Wiles's proof in 1994.
