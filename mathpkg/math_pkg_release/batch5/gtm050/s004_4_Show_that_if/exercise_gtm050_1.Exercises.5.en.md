Note that the simplification of Exercise 4 makes it possible to compute the sequence of $k$'s and $r$'s without ever computing any $p$'s or $q$'s. For example, show that the cyclic method in Fermat's case $A = 149$ in which lines 1 and 2 are $1^2 - 149 \cdot 0^2 = 1$ and $12^2 - 149 \cdot 1^2 = -5$, respectively, will arrive at a solution $p^2 - 149q^2 = 1$ on line 15. (Do not compute the solution.) Show that the English method will arrive at a solution on line 19.
