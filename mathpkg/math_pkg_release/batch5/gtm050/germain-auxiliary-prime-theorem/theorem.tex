If $n$ is an odd prime and $2n+1$ is also prime, then $x^n + y^n = z^n$ implies that one of $x, y, z$ is divisible by $n$.

In other words, for such primes $n$, Case I of Fermat's Last Theorem is impossible without further analysis; only Case II (where $n$ divides one of $x, y, z$) needs to be considered.
