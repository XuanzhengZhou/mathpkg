Kummer's Theorem provides a sufficient condition for Fermat's Last Theorem to hold for a given odd prime exponent $p$: the prime $p$ must be regular, meaning it does not divide the numerator of any Bernoulli number $B_2, B_4, \ldots, B_{p-3}$. Kummer proved this result in the 1840s using his theory of ideal factorization in cyclotomic fields. The theorem settled FLT for all prime exponents less than 100 except 37, 59, and 67, and modern computations have verified the condition for all primes up to 100,000.
