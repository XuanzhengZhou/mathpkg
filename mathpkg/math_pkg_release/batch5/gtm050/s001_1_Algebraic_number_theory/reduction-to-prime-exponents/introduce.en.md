Since every integer $n > 2$ is divisible either by $4$ or by an odd prime, the divisibility reduction lemma implies that proving Fermat's Last Theorem reduces to two base cases: the exponent $n = 4$ (proved by Fermat via infinite descent) and all odd prime exponents $n = p$. This reduction is the standard starting point for all serious attacks on FLT, including Kummer's theory of ideal factorization for regular primes.
