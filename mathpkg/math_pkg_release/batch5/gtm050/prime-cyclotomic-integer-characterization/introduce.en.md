This theorem provides a complete characterization of prime elements in the ring of cyclotomic integers. It is the foundation of Kummer's factorization theory. The key insight is that prime cyclotomic integers correspond to ordinary prime numbers as their norms, and the behavior of a prime cyclotomic integer is governed by a congruence condition modulo an ordinary integer $k$. This allows computations with prime factors to be reduced to ordinary modular arithmetic. The theorem also shows that prime cyclotomic integers always divide binomials of the form $x + \alpha^j y$, which is why they are the natural objects to study in the context of FLT.
