The ring of quadratic integers extends ordinary integer arithmetic by adjoining $\sqrt{-c}$. This construction was introduced by Euler in his work on Fermat's Last Theorem for $n=3$, where he used numbers of the form $a + b\sqrt{-3}$. The key formula $(a^2+cb^2)(p^2+cq^2) = (ap-cbq)^2 + c(aq+bp)^2$ is the norm multiplicativity property. The idea of working with these extended "numbers" is the historical precursor to modern algebraic number theory. However, unique factorization into primes does not always hold in these rings, which motivated Kummer's theory of ideal factors.
