Let $p$ be an odd prime. If there exists an auxiliary prime $q$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $x^p + y^p + z^p \equiv 0 \pmod{q}$ implies $q \mid xyz$,
\item $x^p \equiv p \pmod{q}$ has no solution,
\end{enumerate}
then Case I of Fermat's Last Theorem holds for the exponent $p$. (Case I means $p \nmid xyz$.)
