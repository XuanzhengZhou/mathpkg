Sophie Germain's theorem provides a sufficient condition for proving Case I of Fermat's Last Theorem (where the exponent does not divide any of $x$, $y$, or $z$). Using this theorem, Germain was able to prove Case I for all primes less than 100. Legendre later extended this to all odd primes less than 197. This was the first general result about FLT beyond the specific small exponents.
