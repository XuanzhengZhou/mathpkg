Fermat claimed that every positive integer is the sum of at most $n$ $n$-gonal numbers. The case of four squares is Lagrange's celebrated theorem (1770); the triangular number case was proved by Gauss (1796, his famous "Eureka" entry in his diary); the general case was proved by Cauchy (1813). The theorem is a landmark in additive number theory.
