This lemma is the key to proving the necessity of Girard's condition: if a number is a sum of two squares, then every prime factor $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$ must appear with even exponent. The proof uses Fermat's theorem: if $p \mid x^2 + y^2$ and $p \nmid x$, then $(y/x)^2 \equiv -1 \pmod{p}$, which would imply $(-1)^{(p-1)/2} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$, contradicting $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$.
