The Euler product formula expresses the zeta function of a quadratic field as a product over prime divisors. This is the starting point for Dirichlet's derivation of the class number formula. By relating the product over prime divisors to the ordinary Riemann zeta function and the Dirichlet $L$-series, one obtains an analytic expression for the class number. The Euler product is the quadratic analog of the product formula used in Kummer's derivation of the class number for cyclotomic fields in Chapter 6.
