For an odd prime exponent $n$, Fermat's Last Theorem splits into two cases. Case I (also called the first case) asserts that the equation $x^n + y^n = z^n$ has no nonzero integer solutions in which none of $x, y, z$ is divisible by $n$. Equivalently, reformulated symmetrically as $x^n + y^n + z^n = 0$, Case I states that there are no solutions with all three variables prime to $n$. This case is the one addressed by Sophie Germain's Theorem, which gives a sufficient criterion in terms of an auxiliary prime $p$.
