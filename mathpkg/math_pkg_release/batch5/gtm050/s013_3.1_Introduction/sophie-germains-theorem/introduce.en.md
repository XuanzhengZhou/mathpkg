Sophie Germain's Theorem provides a sufficient condition for Case I of Fermat's Last Theorem for an odd prime exponent $n$. If there exists an auxiliary prime $p$ satisfying two congruence conditions -- (1) any nontrivial solution to $x^n + y^n + z^n \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$ must have one variable congruent to $0$ modulo $p$, and (2) the congruence $x^n \equiv n \pmod{p}$ has no solution -- then the equation $x^n + y^n + z^n = 0$ has no nonzero integer solutions with $x, y, z$ all prime to $n$. This theorem, attributed to Sophie Germain by Legendre, was a major breakthrough in the study of Fermat's Last Theorem before Kummer's theory of ideal factors.
