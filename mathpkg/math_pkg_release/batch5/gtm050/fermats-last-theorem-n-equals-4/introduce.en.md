The case $n=4$ of Fermat's Last Theorem is the simplest to prove and was known to Fermat himself. The proof uses the method of infinite descent combined with the classification of Pythagorean triples. A crucial consequence is the reduction: since every integer $n > 2$ is divisible by either 4 or an odd prime, proving the theorem for $n=4$ and for all odd primes suffices to prove the general case.
