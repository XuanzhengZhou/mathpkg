A Pythagorean triple is a triple $(x,y,z)$ of positive integers satisfying the Diophantine equation $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$. The name derives from the Pythagorean theorem, which states that a triangle with side lengths in such ratios is a right triangle. The simplest example is $(3,4,5)$, known since antiquity.
