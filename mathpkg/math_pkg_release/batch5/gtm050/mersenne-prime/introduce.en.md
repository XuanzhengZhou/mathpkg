Mersenne primes are named after Father Marin Mersenne (1588-1648), a French monk and mathematician who corresponded with Fermat and compiled a list of such primes. The study of Mersenne primes is intimately connected to perfect numbers via Euclid's formula. Fermat discovered his "Little Theorem" while investigating the factorization of Mersenne numbers $2^n - 1$. The search for Mersenne primes continues today, with the largest known primes typically being Mersenne primes discovered by the GIMPS project.
