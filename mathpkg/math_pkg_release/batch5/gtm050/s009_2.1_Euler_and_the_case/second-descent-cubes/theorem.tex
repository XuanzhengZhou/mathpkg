Let $a, b$ be relatively prime integers of opposite parity and assume $3 \nmid a$. Then the three integers $2a$, $a - 3b$, and $a + 3b$ are pairwise relatively prime. If their product $2a(a - 3b)(a + 3b)$ is a cube, then each factor is itself a cube. Hence there exist integers $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ such that
$$2a = \alpha^3, \qquad a - 3b = \beta^3, \qquad a + 3b = \gamma^3.$$
Consequently,
$$\beta^3 + \gamma^3 = (a - 3b) + (a + 3b) = 2a = \alpha^3,$$
yielding a new solution of $x^3 + y^3 = z^3$.
