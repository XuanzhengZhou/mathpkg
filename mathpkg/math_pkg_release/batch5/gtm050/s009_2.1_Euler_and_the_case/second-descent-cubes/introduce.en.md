From the factorization $2p = 2a(a - 3b)(a + 3b)$ being a cube, this lemma shows that the three factors $2a$, $a - 3b$, and $a + 3b$ are pairwise coprime (assuming $3 \nmid a$). Since their product is a cube and they are pairwise coprime, each factor must itself be a cube. This yields $2a = \alpha^3$, $a - 3b = \beta^3$, $a + 3b = \gamma^3$, and consequently $\beta^3 + \gamma^3 = \alpha^3$ -- a new solution of the original cubic equation.
