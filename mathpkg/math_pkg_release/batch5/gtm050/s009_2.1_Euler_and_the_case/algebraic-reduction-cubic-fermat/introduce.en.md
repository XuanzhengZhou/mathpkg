This lemma performs the key algebraic manipulation in Euler's proof. Assuming $x, y$ are odd and $z$ is even, setting $x + y = 2p$ and $x - y = 2q$ transforms the cubic equation $x^3 + y^3 = z^3$ into the condition that $2p(p^2 + 3q^2)$ is a perfect cube, where $p, q$ are relatively prime integers of opposite parity. This reduces the problem from three variables to an equation involving the quadratic form $p^2 + 3q^2$.
