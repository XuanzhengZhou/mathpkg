This corollary establishes the strict inequality $\alpha^3 \beta^3 \gamma^3 = 2p < z^3$ (or $< x^3$), showing that the new solution $(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)$ obtained from the descent step is strictly smaller than the original solution $(x, y, z)$. Together with the infinite descent principle, this completes the proof for the case where 3 does not divide $p$. Euler then treats the complementary case ($3 \mid p$) as a modification of this argument.
