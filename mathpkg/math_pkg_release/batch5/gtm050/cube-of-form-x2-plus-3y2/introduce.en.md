This proposition characterizes when an integer of the form $p^2 + 3q^2$ is a perfect cube. Euler used it in his proof of FLT for $n=3$, but his argument implicitly assumed unique factorization in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$, which does not hold. However, the statement itself is true—it can be proved using unique factorization in the larger ring $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$ where $\omega = (-1+\sqrt{-3})/2$.
