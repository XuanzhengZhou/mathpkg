A triple $(x, y, z)$ of positive integers is a primitive Pythagorean triple (i.e., $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$ with $\gcd(x, y, z) = 1$) if and only if there exist relatively prime positive integers $p > q$ of opposite parity such that
$$x = 2pq, \quad y = p^2 - q^2, \quad z = p^2 + q^2$$
or with $x$ and $y$ interchanged. Moreover, every Pythagorean triple is an integer multiple of a primitive one.
