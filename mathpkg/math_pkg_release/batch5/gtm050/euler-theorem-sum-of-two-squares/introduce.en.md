Fermat stated, and Euler proved in 1747, that every prime $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ is expressible as a sum of two squares. This theorem, together with the product formula and the fact that $2 = 1^2 + 1^2$, completely characterizes which integers are sums of two squares (Girard's condition). Euler's proof uses infinite descent and is a masterpiece of elementary number theory.
