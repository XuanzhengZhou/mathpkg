The product formula for sums of two squares is a classical identity, known to Diophantus, Fibonacci, and ancient Indian mathematicians. It is the algebraic expression of the multiplicativity of the norm in $\mathbb{Z}[i]$: $|(a+bi)(c+di)|^2 = |a+bi|^2|c+di|^2$. This identity is the foundation for the theory of representations of integers as sums of two squares.
