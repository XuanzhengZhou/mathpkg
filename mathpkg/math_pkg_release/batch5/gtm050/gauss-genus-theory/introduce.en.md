Gauss's genus theory classifies binary quadratic forms of a given determinant into genera based on their behavior modulo the prime factors of the determinant. Each genus consists of those forms whose values have the same quadratic character with respect to each prime dividing the determinant. Gauss proved that exactly half of the theoretically possible characters actually occur, a result that is equivalent to quadratic reciprocity. In the language of divisors, a genus is an equivalence class under the subgroup of squares in the class group. The theory of genera was the historical precursor to class field theory.
