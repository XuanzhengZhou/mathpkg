For a determinant $D$, the number of genera (equivalence classes of the principal genus under the character group) is exactly $2^{m+\varepsilon-1}$, where $m$ is the number of distinct odd prime factors of $D$ and $\varepsilon = 0$ or $1$ depending on $D \pmod{4}$.

Equivalently, at most half of the possible characters of divisor classes actually occur, and exactly half occur when $D$ is a fundamental discriminant. The occurring characters are precisely those whose product over all assigned signs is $+1$.
