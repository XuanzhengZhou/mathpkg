Girard's condition gives a complete characterization of which integers can be expressed as a sum of two squares. The "only if" direction (necessity) is elementary: if $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$ divides $x^2 + y^2$, then $p$ must divide both $x$ and $y$, so $p^2$ divides the sum. The "if" direction (sufficiency) follows from Euler's theorem that every prime $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ is a sum of two squares, together with the product formula and $2 = 1^2 + 1^2$.
