Prove that a number which does not satisfy Girard's conditions cannot be written as a sum of two squares. More specifically, prove that if $p$ divides $x^2 + y^2$ and $p$ is a prime of the form $4n + 3$ then $p$ divides both $x$ and $y$. [If $p$ divides $x^2 + y^2$ then it divides $(x^2)^{2n+1} + (y^2)^{2n+1}$. Unless $p$ divides both $x$ and $y$ this number is either 1 or 2 greater than a multiple of $p$ and therefore not divisible by $p$.]
