Kummer's theorem, proved in the 1840s, establishes Fermat's Last Theorem for all regular prime exponents at once. Using his theory of ideal factorization in cyclotomic integers, Kummer reduced the problem to showing that certain units are $\lambda$-th powers, which follows from the regularity condition. Combined with the fact that all primes less than 100 except 37, 59, and 67 are regular, Kummer proved FLT for all primes less than 100.
