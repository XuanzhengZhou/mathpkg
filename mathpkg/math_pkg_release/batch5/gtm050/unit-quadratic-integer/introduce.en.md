Units are the invertible elements in the ring of quadratic integers. They correspond to solutions of the norm equation $x^2 + cy^2 = \pm 1$. Units play a crucial role in factorization theory because multiplying a factor by a unit does not change the essential factorization. In the context of FLT for $n=3$, the classification of units in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ is essential. For real quadratic fields, the infinite group of units (given by Pell's equation solutions) introduces complications that Dirichlet had to handle in his proof for $n=5$.
