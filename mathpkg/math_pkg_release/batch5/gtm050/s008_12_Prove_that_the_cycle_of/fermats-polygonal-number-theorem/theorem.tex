Every natural number can be written as a sum of at most $n$ $n$-gonal numbers.

Equivalently:
\begin{itemize}
\item Every number is a sum of at most three triangular numbers.
\item Every number is a sum of at most four squares (Lagrange's four-square theorem).
\item Every number is a sum of at most five pentagonal numbers.
\item Every number is a sum of at most six hexagonal numbers.
\end{itemize}
And so forth \textit{ad infinitum} for all $n \geq 3$.
