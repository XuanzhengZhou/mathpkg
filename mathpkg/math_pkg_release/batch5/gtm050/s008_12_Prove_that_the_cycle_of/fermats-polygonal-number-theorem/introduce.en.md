Fermat's polygonal number theorem asserts that every natural number can be expressed as a sum of at most $n$ $n$-gonal numbers, for every $n \geq 3$. This generalizes the classical result that every number is a sum of at most three triangular numbers, four squares, five pentagonal numbers, and so on. Fermat claimed to have a proof, but the theorem engaged the greatest mathematicians of subsequent generations: Lagrange first proved the case $n=4$ (every number is a sum of four squares), and Gauss first proved the case $n=3$ (every number is a sum of three triangular numbers). The full theorem was eventually proved by Cauchy in 1813.
