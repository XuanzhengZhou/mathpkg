Prove Fermat's statement that the only natural numbers $x$ for which $1 + x + x^2 + x^3$ is a square are $x = 1$ and $x = 7$. The only integers with this property are these and $x = 0, -1$.

[This is an excellent but very difficult problem. Use the fact that $x^4 + y^4$ can never be a square (Section 1.5) and that $x^4 - y^4 = z^2$ only in the trivial cases $y = 0$ or $z = 0$ (Section 1.6, Exercise 2).]
