The nonvanishing of $L(1,\chi)$ is the crucial analytic fact needed to derive Kummer's class number formula and Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions. If $L(1,\chi) = 0$ for some nonprincipal character, the class number formula would give zero, which is impossible. The proof for prime modulus $\lambda$ is simpler than the general case and uses the explicit formula for $L(1,\chi)$ developed in Section 6.5. Specifically, the character with $\chi(\gamma) = -1$ (where $\gamma$ is a primitive root mod $\lambda$) is shown to have $L(1,\chi) \neq 0$ by direct evaluation, and the other characters are handled by showing at most one nonprincipal character can have $L(1,\chi) = 0$.
