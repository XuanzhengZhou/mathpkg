Sophie Germain (1776-1831) was one of the few women mathematicians of her era to overcome the prejudice against women in higher mathematics. Her theorem provides a sufficient condition for Case I of Fermat's Last Theorem (where none of $x, y, z$ is divisible by the exponent). Using this theorem, she proved Case I for all primes less than 100, and Legendre extended this to all primes less than 197. The theorem splits FLT into two cases: Case I (none of $x, y, z$ divisible by $n$) and Case II (one of them is divisible by $n$). Sophie Germain's theorem handles Case I for many small primes.
