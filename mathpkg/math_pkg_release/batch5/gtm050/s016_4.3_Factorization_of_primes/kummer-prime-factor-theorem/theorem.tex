Let $h(\alpha)$ be a cyclotomic integer. If $Nh(\alpha)$ is a prime integer $p$, then $h(\alpha)$ itself is prime. Moreover, $p \equiv 0$ or $1 \pmod{\lambda}$, and there exists an integer $k$ such that $h(\alpha)$ divides $\alpha - k$.

When $p = \lambda$, the integer $k$ must satisfy $k \equiv 1 \pmod{\lambda}$, and $h(\alpha)$ is a unit multiple of $\alpha - 1$.

When $p \equiv 1 \pmod{\lambda}$, the possible values of $k$ are the $\lambda$ distinct solutions modulo $p$ of the congruence $k^{\lambda} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$.
