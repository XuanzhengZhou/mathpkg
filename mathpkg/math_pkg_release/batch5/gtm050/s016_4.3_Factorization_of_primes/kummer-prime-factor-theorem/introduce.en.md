This theorem is the central result of Section 4.3 and one of the cornerstones of Kummer's theory. It establishes that the norm provides a reliable criterion for primality in the ring of cyclotomic integers: if the norm of $h(\alpha)$ is a rational prime $p$, then $h(\alpha)$ itself is prime. Moreover, the theorem shows that every such prime cyclotomic integer must divide a binomial of the particularly simple form $\alpha - k$, where $k$ is an integer satisfying $k^\lambda \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$. The two cases $p = \lambda$ and $p \equiv 1 \pmod{\lambda}$ are treated separately, corresponding to whether the prime is ramified or splits completely in the cyclotomic field.
