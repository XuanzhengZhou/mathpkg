Prove that if $h(\alpha)$ is irreducible but not prime then there is a cyclotomic integer which can be written in two distinct ways as a product of irreducibles. That is, if "irreducible" does not imply "prime" then unique factorization fails. [Assume that every cyclotomic integer can be written as a product of irreducibles.]
