Kummer's key insight was that congruences modulo a prime cyclotomic divisor $h(\alpha)$ of a binomial can be translated into ordinary integer congruences. If one knows an integer $k$ such that $\alpha \equiv k \pmod{h(\alpha)}$, then the truth of $f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \bmod h(\alpha)$ can be decided by evaluating both sides at $\alpha=k$ and checking whether the resulting integers are congruent modulo $p = Nh(\alpha)$. This equivalence is what makes the "method of analysis" possible: one can deduce necessary conditions on $h(\alpha)$ without actually knowing $h(\alpha)$ explicitly, by working entirely in the integers modulo $p$.
