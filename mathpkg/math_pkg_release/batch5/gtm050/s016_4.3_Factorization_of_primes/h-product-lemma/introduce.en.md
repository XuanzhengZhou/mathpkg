This lemma is a key technical step in Kummer's proof that every prime cyclotomic integer dividing a binomial must divide some $\alpha - k$. Given a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity modulo $p$, represented by $m$, the lemma asserts that the product of the evaluations $h(m^j)$ for $j = 1, \ldots, \lambda-1$ is divisible by $p$. This non-vanishing condition forces at least one factor $h(m^j)$ to be $\equiv 0 \pmod{p}$, establishing the existence of the required integer $k = m^j$. The proof uses the norm formula $N(\alpha - k) = (k^\lambda - 1)/(k - 1)$.
