Let $p \equiv 1 \pmod{\lambda}$ be a prime, let $m$ be an integer such that $m^\lambda \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$ and the powers $m, m^2, \ldots, m^\lambda \equiv 1$ represent all $\lambda$ solutions of $k^\lambda \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$. If $h(\alpha)$ is a cyclotomic integer such that $h(m) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$ implies $h(\alpha)$ divides $\alpha - m$, then

$$h(m)h(m^2) \cdots h(m^{\lambda - 1}) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}.$$
