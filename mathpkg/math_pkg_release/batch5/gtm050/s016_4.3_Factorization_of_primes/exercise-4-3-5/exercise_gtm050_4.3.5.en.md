It was shown in the text that if $p$ divides $N(x + \alpha y)$ where $x$ and $y$ are relatively prime and if $p$ has a prime divisor $h(\alpha)$ then $p \equiv 0$ or $1 \pmod{\lambda}$. Prove that the assumption about a prime divisor $h(\alpha)$ is unnecessary. [Use the formula for $N(x + \alpha y)$.]
