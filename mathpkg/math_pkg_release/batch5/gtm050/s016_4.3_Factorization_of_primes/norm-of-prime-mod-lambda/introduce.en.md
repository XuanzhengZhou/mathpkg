This corollary sharply restricts which rational primes can appear as norms of prime cyclotomic integers. The only possibilities are $\lambda$ itself (the ramified case) and primes congruent to $1$ modulo $\lambda$ (the split case). This is a concrete arithmetic consequence of Kummer's theorem and gives a simple necessary condition: if a rational prime $p$ is not congruent to $0$ or $1$ modulo $\lambda$, then no prime cyclotomic integer can have norm $p$. Such primes remain inert or have more complicated factorization behavior.
