This is the simplest application of Kummer's prime factor theorem. Since the norm of $\alpha - 1$ is $N(\alpha - 1) = \lambda$, which is a rational prime, the theorem implies that $\alpha - 1$ itself is prime. This makes $\alpha - 1$ the canonical prime dividing $\lambda$ in the cyclotomic integers — it is the unique prime (up to unit multiples) lying above the rational prime $\lambda$. The corollary also confirms that any unit times $\alpha - 1$ is likewise prime, since multiplication by a unit does not change the norm.
