Two cyclotomic integers $f(\alpha)$ and $g(\alpha)$ are said to be \textbf{congruent modulo} a third cyclotomic integer $h(\alpha)$, written

$$f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha),$$

if $h(\alpha)$ divides their difference $f(\alpha) - g(\alpha)$. This relation is reflexive, symmetric, transitive, and consistent with addition and multiplication:

\begin{itemize}
\item $f(\alpha) \equiv f(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ \quad (reflexive)
\item $f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ implies $g(\alpha) \equiv f(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ \quad (symmetric)
\item $f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ and $g(\alpha) \equiv \phi(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ imply $f(\alpha) \equiv \phi(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ \quad (transitive)
\item $f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ implies $f(\alpha) + \phi(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) + \phi(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ for all $\phi(\alpha)$ \quad (consistent with addition)
\item $f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ implies $f(\alpha)\phi(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha)\phi(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha)$ for all $\phi(\alpha)$ \quad (consistent with multiplication)
\end{itemize}
