If $u$, $v$, and $w$ are pairwise relatively prime positive integers such that $w$ is divisible by $10$, then
$$u^5 \pm v^5 \neq w^5.$$
In other words, the first case of Fermat's Last Theorem for exponent $n = 5$ is impossible: there are no solutions in which the term divisible by $5$ is also divisible by $2$.
