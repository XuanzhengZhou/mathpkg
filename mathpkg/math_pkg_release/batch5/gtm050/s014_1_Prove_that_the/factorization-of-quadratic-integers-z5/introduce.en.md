This factorization theorem describes how an element $a + b\sqrt{5}$ in the quadratic integer ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$ decomposes according to the prime factorization of its norm $a^2 - 5b^2$. Given relatively prime integers $a, b$ of opposite parity with $a^2 - 5b^2 = \prod P_i^{n_i}$, one can find primes $P_i$ each expressed as a norm $p_i^2 - 5q_i^2$, and the element factors as a product of the corresponding quadratic integers $(p_i + q_i\sqrt{5})^{n_i}$ times a unit $t + u\sqrt{5}$ satisfying $t^2 - 5u^2 = 1$. This result is a consequence of Dirichlet's Lemma and provides a form of unique factorization in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$ for the restricted class of elements arising in the proof of Fermat's Last Theorem for $n = 5$.
