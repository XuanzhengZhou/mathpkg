In his 1825 proof that the first case of Fermat's Last Theorem for exponent $n=5$ is impossible, Dirichlet needed to extract fifth roots in the quadratic ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$. The ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{5}]$ does not enjoy unique factorization, so knowing that $P^2-5Q^2$ is a fifth power does not automatically imply that $P+Q\sqrt{5}$ is a fifth power. Dirichlet discovered that the extra condition $5 \mid Q$ (along with relative primality and opposite parity) is sufficient to guarantee this unique factorization. This lemma was the critical technical innovation that made his proof work.
