Kummer's class number formula is one of the deepest results in the book. It expresses the class number of the cyclotomic field in terms of values of $L$-functions at $s=1$, which in turn can be expressed in terms of Bernoulli numbers. The factorization $h = h_1 \cdot h_2$ separates the contributions from the "imaginary" and "real" parts of the field. The connection to Bernoulli numbers is crucial: it gives a computable criterion for determining whether $\lambda$ is regular (i.e., $h_1$ is not divisible by $\lambda$). The proof occupies most of Chapter 6 and involves the Euler product formula, Dirichlet's evaluation of $L(1,\chi)$, and sophisticated analytic arguments.
