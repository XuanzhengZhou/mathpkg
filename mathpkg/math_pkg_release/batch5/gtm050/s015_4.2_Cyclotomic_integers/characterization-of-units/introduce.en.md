This theorem characterizes the invertible elements in the ring of cyclotomic integers. If a cyclotomic integer has a multiplicative inverse that is also a cyclotomic integer, then its norm must be $1$, making it a unit. Moreover, the inverse is uniquely determined as the product of all non-trivial conjugates. The proof uses the multiplicativity of the norm and the fact that norms are non-negative integers.
