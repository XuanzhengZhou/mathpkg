A unit in the ring of cyclotomic integers is an element whose norm equals $1$. Since the norm is multiplicative, a unit divides every cyclotomic integer (multiplying by the inverse of the unit recovers the original element). Consequently, units are considered trivial factors and are disregarded in the study of unique factorization, just as $\pm 1$ are disregarded in the factorization of ordinary integers. The inverse of a unit is simply the product of its non-trivial conjugates.
