Let $\lambda = 5$ and let
$$f(\alpha) = 2 + \alpha + 3\alpha^2 - 2\alpha^3, \quad g(\alpha) = -\alpha + \alpha^2 - 4\alpha^3 - 2\alpha^4.$$
The text states that $f(\alpha) = g(\alpha)$. Prove that $f(\alpha^3) = g(\alpha^3)$. Is $f(\alpha^5) = g(\alpha^5)$?
