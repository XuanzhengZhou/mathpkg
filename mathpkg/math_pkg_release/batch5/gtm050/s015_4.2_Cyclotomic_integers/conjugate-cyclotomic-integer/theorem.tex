Let $f(\alpha)$ be a cyclotomic integer expressed as a polynomial in a primitive $\lambda$th root of unity $\alpha$. The \textbf{conjugates} of $f(\alpha)$ are the $\lambda-1$ cyclotomic integers
$$f(\alpha), \; f(\alpha^2), \; f(\alpha^3), \; \ldots, \; f(\alpha^{\lambda-1})$$
obtained by replacing $\alpha$ with $\alpha^j$ for $j = 1, 2, \ldots, \lambda-1$. The substitution $f(\alpha^j)$ is well-defined for $j = 2, 3, \ldots, \lambda-1$, but $f(\alpha^\lambda)$ is not meaningful because $\alpha^\lambda = 1$ does not behave like a primitive root under substitution.
