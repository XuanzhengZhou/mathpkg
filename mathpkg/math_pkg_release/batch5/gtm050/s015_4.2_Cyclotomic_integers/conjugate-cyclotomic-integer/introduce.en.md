For a cyclotomic integer $f(\alpha)$, its conjugates are obtained by replacing the primitive $\lambda$th root of unity $\alpha$ with each of its Galois conjugates $\alpha^2, \alpha^3, \ldots, \alpha^{\lambda-1}$. These $\lambda-1$ numbers are the algebraic conjugates of $f(\alpha)$ in the cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\alpha)$. The notion of conjugates is essential for defining the norm, which is the product of all conjugates and plays a central role in Kummer's factorization theory.
