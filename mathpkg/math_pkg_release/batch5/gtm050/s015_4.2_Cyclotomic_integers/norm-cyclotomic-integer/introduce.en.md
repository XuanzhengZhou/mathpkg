The norm of a cyclotomic integer $f(\alpha)$ is the product of all its $\lambda-1$ conjugates. As a symmetric function of the conjugates, the norm is invariant under the Galois group of $\mathbb{Q}(\alpha)/\mathbb{Q}$ and is therefore an ordinary integer. The norm is the central tool in Kummer's theory because it converts the problem of factoring cyclotomic integers into the problem of factoring ordinary integers: a factorization $h(\alpha) = f(\alpha)g(\alpha)$ implies $Nh(\alpha) = Nf(\alpha) \cdot Ng(\alpha)$.
