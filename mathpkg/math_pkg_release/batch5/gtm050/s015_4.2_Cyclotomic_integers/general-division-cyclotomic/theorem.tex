Let $f(\alpha)$ and $h(\alpha)$ be cyclotomic integers with $f(\alpha) \neq 0$. The problem of finding a cyclotomic integer $g(\alpha)$ such that
$$f(\alpha)g(\alpha) = h(\alpha)$$
can be reduced to the case of division by an ordinary integer via the norm. Specifically, $g(\alpha)$ exists if and only if the product
$$h(\alpha) \cdot f(\alpha^2)f(\alpha^3)\cdots f(\alpha^{\lambda-1})$$
is divisible (in the sense of the ordinary integer criterion) by the integer $Nf(\alpha)$.
