Kummer solved the general division problem for cyclotomic integers by reducing it to the special case of division by an ordinary integer. Multiplying the equation $f(\alpha)g(\alpha) = h(\alpha)$ by the product of the non-trivial conjugates of $f(\alpha)$ transforms the left side into $Nf(\alpha) \cdot g(\alpha)$, turning the problem into division by the ordinary integer $Nf(\alpha)$. The resulting divisibility condition can then be checked using the criterion that all coefficients must be congruent modulo $Nf(\alpha)$.
