Since cyclotomic integers admit non-unique representations in the form $a_0 + a_1\alpha + \cdots + a_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$, one must verify that the operation of replacing $\alpha$ by $\alpha^j$ yields a well-defined result. This lemma states that if two expressions represent the same cyclotomic integer, then their images under the substitution $\alpha \mapsto \alpha^j$ also coincide. The proof uses the fact that the only relation among powers of $\alpha$ is the fundamental relation $1 + \alpha + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1} = 0$.
