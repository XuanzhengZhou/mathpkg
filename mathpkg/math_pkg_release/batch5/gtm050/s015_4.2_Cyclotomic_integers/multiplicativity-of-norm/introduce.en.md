The norm is multiplicative: the norm of a product equals the product of the norms. This property is the crucial bridge between factorization in the ring of cyclotomic integers and factorization in the ordinary integers. If a cyclotomic integer $h(\alpha)$ factors as $f(\alpha)g(\alpha)$, then the ordinary integer $Nh(\alpha)$ factors as $Nf(\alpha) \cdot Ng(\alpha)$, so possible factorizations of $h(\alpha)$ correspond to factorizations of its norm.
