Since $\alpha^\lambda = 1$, we have $\alpha^\lambda - 1 = 0$. Factor the left side:
$$\alpha^\lambda - 1 = (\alpha - 1)(\alpha^{\lambda-1} + \alpha^{\lambda-2} + \cdots + \alpha + 1) = 0.$$
By assumption $\alpha \neq 1$, so $\alpha - 1 \neq 0$. Since we are working in the field of complex numbers, we may cancel the non-zero factor $\alpha - 1$ to obtain
$$\alpha^{\lambda-1} + \alpha^{\lambda-2} + \cdots + \alpha + 1 = 0.$$
Rearranging terms yields the stated relation.
