For a primitive $\lambda$th root of unity $\alpha \neq 1$, the sum of all $\lambda$ powers from $1$ to $\alpha^{\lambda-1}$ equals zero. This relation is an immediate consequence of the identity $\alpha^\lambda = 1$ and the factorization $(\alpha - 1)(1 + \alpha + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1}) = \alpha^\lambda - 1 = 0$ with $\alpha \neq 1$. This fundamental relation implies that representations of cyclotomic integers in the form $a_0 + a_1\alpha + \cdots + a_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$ are not unique.
