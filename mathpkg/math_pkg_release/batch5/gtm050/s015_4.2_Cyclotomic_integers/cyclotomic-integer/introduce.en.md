A cyclotomic integer is an integer linear combination of powers of a primitive $\lambda$th root of unity $\alpha$, where $\lambda$ is a prime. These numbers generalize ordinary integers and were introduced by Kummer in his study of Fermat's Last Theorem. Cyclotomic integers form a ring under the usual addition and multiplication of complex numbers, subject to the relation $\alpha^\lambda = 1$. They serve as the fundamental objects in Kummer's theory of ideal factorization.
