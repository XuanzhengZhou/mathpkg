The norm $Nf(\alpha) = f(\alpha)f(\alpha^2)\cdots f(\alpha^{\lambda-1})$ is, by construction, a symmetric polynomial in the $\lambda-1$ conjugates $\alpha, \alpha^2, \ldots, \alpha^{\lambda-1}$. Any symmetric polynomial in all primitive $\lambda$th roots of unity can be expressed as a polynomial with integer coefficients in the elementary symmetric functions of these roots. The elementary symmetric functions are, up to sign, the coefficients of the cyclotomic polynomial
$$\Phi_\lambda(X) = X^{\lambda-1} + X^{\lambda-2} + \cdots + X + 1,$$
which are all integers (specifically, all equal to $1$). Therefore $Nf(\alpha)$ is an integer combination of integers, hence an integer. Moreover, since $f(\alpha)$ has integer coefficients, its norm is in fact an ordinary (rational) integer.
