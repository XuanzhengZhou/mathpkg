This theorem provides a necessary and sufficient condition for a cyclotomic integer to be divisible by an ordinary integer $a_0$. The condition is representation-independent: a cyclotomic integer $h(\alpha)$ is divisible by $a_0$ precisely when all coefficients in any representation are congruent modulo $a_0$. This criterion is the key step in Kummer's solution of the general division problem for cyclotomic integers, as it reduces the general case to this special case via the norm.
