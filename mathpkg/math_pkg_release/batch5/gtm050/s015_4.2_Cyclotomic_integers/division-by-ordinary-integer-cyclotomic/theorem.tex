Let $a_0 \neq 0$ be an ordinary integer and let $h(\alpha) = b_0 + b_1\alpha + \cdots + b_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$ be a cyclotomic integer. Then there exists a cyclotomic integer $g(\alpha)$ such that
$$h(\alpha) = a_0 \cdot g(\alpha)$$
if and only if all coefficients of $h(\alpha)$ are congruent to one another modulo $a_0$, i.e.,
$$b_i \equiv b_j \pmod{a_0} \quad \text{for all } i, j \in \{0, 1, \ldots, \lambda-1\}.$$
