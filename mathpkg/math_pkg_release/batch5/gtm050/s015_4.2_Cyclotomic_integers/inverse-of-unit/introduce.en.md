For a unit $f(\alpha)$ in the ring of cyclotomic integers (an element of norm $1$), its inverse is simply the product of all its non-trivial conjugates. This follows directly from the definition of the norm: since $Nf(\alpha) = f(\alpha) \cdot f(\alpha^2) \cdots f(\alpha^{\lambda-1}) = 1$, the factor $f(\alpha^2)\cdots f(\alpha^{\lambda-1})$ must be the multiplicative inverse of $f(\alpha)$. This shows that the inverse of a unit is also a cyclotomic integer.
