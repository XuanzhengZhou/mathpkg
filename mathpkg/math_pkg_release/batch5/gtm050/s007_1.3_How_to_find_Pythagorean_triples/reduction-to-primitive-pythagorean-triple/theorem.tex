Every Pythagorean triple $(x, y, z)$ can be reduced to a primitive Pythagorean triple by dividing $x$, $y$, and $z$ by their greatest common divisor $d = \gcd(x, y, z)$. The resulting triple $(x/d, y/d, z/d)$ is primitive and satisfies the same equation
$$\left(\frac{x}{d}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{y}{d}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{z}{d}\right)^2.$$
Conversely, every Pythagorean triple can be obtained from a primitive one by multiplying by an integer.
