Every Pythagorean triple can be reduced to a primitive one by canceling the square of the greatest common divisor from all three numbers. This reduction theorem is fundamental to the classification of Pythagorean triples: it suffices to find all primitive triples, since every non-primitive triple is an integer multiple of a primitive one. The proof follows directly from the fact that if $d$ divides $x$, $y$, and $z$, then $d^2$ can be canceled from the equation $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$.
