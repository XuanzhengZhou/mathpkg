A Pythagorean triple is a triple of positive integers $(x, y, z)$ that satisfies the equation $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$. These triples correspond to right triangles with integer side lengths, named after the ancient Greek mathematician Pythagoras. The problem of finding all Pythagorean triples dates back to antiquity and was studied by Diophantus and Euclid. Every Pythagorean triple can be reduced to a primitive Pythagorean triple by dividing through by the greatest common divisor of the three numbers.
