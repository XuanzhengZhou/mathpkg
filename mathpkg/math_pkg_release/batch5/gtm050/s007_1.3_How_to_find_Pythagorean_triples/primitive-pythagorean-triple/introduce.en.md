A primitive Pythagorean triple is a Pythagorean triple $(x, y, z)$ in which the three integers have no common divisor other than $1$. This means $\gcd(x, y, z) = 1$, so the triple cannot be reduced further by canceling a common square factor. The classification of primitive Pythagorean triples is the essential step toward finding all Pythagorean triples, since any non-primitive triple can be obtained by multiplying a primitive one by an integer square factor.
