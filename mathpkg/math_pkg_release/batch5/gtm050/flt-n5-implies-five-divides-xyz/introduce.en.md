This theorem is the first step in the proof of Fermat's Last Theorem for exponent 5. It reduces the problem to two subcases depending on which variable is divisible by 5. The proof uses the factorization $x^5+y^5 = (x+y)(x^4 - x^3y + x^2y^2 - xy^3 + y^4)$ and an analysis of the possible fifth powers modulo 11. The same technique generalizes: if $n$ is an odd prime and $2n+1$ is also prime, then $x^n + y^n = z^n$ implies $n \mid xyz$. This observation is the starting point for Sophie Germain's more general theorem.
