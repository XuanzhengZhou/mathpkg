The norm is multiplicative: the norm of a product equals the product of the norms. This fundamental property makes the norm a powerful algebraic tool. It implies that any factorization of a cyclotomic integer into two cyclotomic integers yields a corresponding factorization of ordinary integers via the norm. Consequently, constraints on the possible ordinary integer factorizations of the norm constrain the possible factorizations of the cyclotomic integer itself.
