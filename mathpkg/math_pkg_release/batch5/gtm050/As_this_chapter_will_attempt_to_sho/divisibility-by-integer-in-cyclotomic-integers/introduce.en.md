This theorem solves the problem of simple division by an ordinary integer in the ring of cyclotomic integers. It provides a necessary and sufficient condition: a cyclotomic integer is divisible by an integer $a_0$ precisely when all its coefficients are congruent modulo $a_0$. This criterion is independent of the particular representation, because if all coefficients are congruent modulo $a_0$, the cyclotomic integer can be rewritten with all coefficients divisible by $a_0$, making the factor $a_0$ explicit. This reduces the general division problem to the integer case via the norm.
