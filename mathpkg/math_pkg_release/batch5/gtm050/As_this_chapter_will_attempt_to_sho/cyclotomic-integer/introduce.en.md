A cyclotomic integer is an integer linear combination of powers of a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity $\alpha$, where $\lambda$ is a prime. Formally, it is an expression $a_0 + a_1\alpha + \cdots + a_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$ with integer coefficients. These numbers generalize ordinary integers and are the central objects of study in Kummer's theory of ideal factorization. Computations with cyclotomic integers follow the usual commutative, associative, and distributive laws, together with the relation $\alpha^\lambda = 1$. Kummer, following Jacobi, was led to study these numbers by their role in Gauss's theory of the division of the circle.
