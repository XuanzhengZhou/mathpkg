The norm of a cyclotomic integer $f(\alpha)$ is the product of all $\lambda-1$ of its conjugates. Crucially, the norm is always an ordinary integer, providing a bridge between factorization in the cyclotomic integers and factorization in the ordinary integers. If $f(\alpha) = g(\alpha)h(\alpha)$ is a factorization into cyclotomic integers, then taking norms gives $Nf(\alpha) = Ng(\alpha) \cdot Nh(\alpha)$, a factorization of ordinary integers. Thus the norm is an essential tool for studying divisibility in the ring of cyclotomic integers.
