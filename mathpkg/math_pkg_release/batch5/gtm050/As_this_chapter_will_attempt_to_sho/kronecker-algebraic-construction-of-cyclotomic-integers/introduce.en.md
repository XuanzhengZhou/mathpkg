Kronecker, a student and close associate of Kummer, proposed an abstract algebraic approach to cyclotomic integers that avoids explicit reference to complex numbers. In this formulation, cyclotomic integers are formal expressions $a_0 + a_1\alpha + \cdots + a_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$ with integer coefficients, subject to the single polynomial relation $1 + \alpha + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1} = 0$. This construction corresponds to the quotient of the polynomial ring $\mathbb{Z}[X]$ by the ideal generated by the cyclotomic polynomial $X^{\lambda-1} + X^{\lambda-2} + \cdots + X + 1$. The approach emphasizes the purely algebraic computational rules and is more in the spirit of contemporary algebra.
