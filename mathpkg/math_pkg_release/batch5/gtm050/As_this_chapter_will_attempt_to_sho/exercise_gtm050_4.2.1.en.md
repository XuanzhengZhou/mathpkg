For $\lambda = 5$, the equality
$$2 + \alpha + 3\alpha^2 - 2\alpha^3 = -\alpha + \alpha^2 - 4\alpha^3 - 2\alpha^4$$
holds. Let $f(\alpha)$ be the left side of this equation and $g(\alpha)$ the right side. Prove that $f(\alpha^3) = g(\alpha^3)$. Is $f(\alpha^5) = g(\alpha^5)$?
