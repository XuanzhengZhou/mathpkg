If a product of two cyclotomic integers equals $1$, then each factor is a unit and the inverse of one is precisely the product of all conjugates of the other (excluding the original element itself). This elegant characterization follows from the multiplicativity of the norm: taking norms of $f(\alpha)g(\alpha) = 1$ yields $Nf(\alpha) \cdot Ng(\alpha) = 1$, so both norms are $\pm 1$ (actually $1$ since norms are nonnegative). The explicit formula for the inverse shows that the inverse of a cyclotomic unit is always a cyclotomic integer.
