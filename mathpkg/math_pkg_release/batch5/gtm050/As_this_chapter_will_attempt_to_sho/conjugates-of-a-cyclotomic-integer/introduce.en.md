The conjugates of a cyclotomic integer $f(\alpha)$ are obtained by systematically replacing the primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity $\alpha$ with each of its nontrivial powers $\alpha^2, \alpha^3, \ldots, \alpha^{\lambda-1}$ in the polynomial expression. There are $\lambda-1$ conjugates in total, forming a complete set of Galois conjugates over the rational numbers. The substitution $f(\alpha) \mapsto f(\alpha^j)$ is well-defined despite the non-uniqueness of representation, because equivalent representations are intertwined by the substitution.
