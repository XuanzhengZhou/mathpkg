The norm of a binomial $x\alpha + y$ in the ring of cyclotomic integers $\mathbb{Z}[\alpha]$ for a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity $\alpha$ simplifies to a rational expression $\frac{x^\lambda + y^\lambda}{x + y}$. This formula follows from the factorization $x^\lambda + y^\lambda = (x + y)\prod_{j=1}^{\lambda-1}(x\alpha^j + y)$, where the product runs over the conjugates of $x\alpha + y$. It is a critical computational tool in Kummer's method for determining the prime factorization of rational primes $p \equiv 1 \pmod{\lambda}$ in cyclotomic integer rings.
