The logarithmic series identity states that the Taylor expansion $-\log(1-x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} x^n/n$ converges for all complex $x$ in the closed unit disk $|x| \leqslant 1$ except at $x = 1$, where the series diverges. The proof uses summation by parts (Abel summation) rather than a simple ratio test, since convergence on the boundary $|x| = 1$ is conditional. In the context of Kummer's work on Fermat's Last Theorem, this identity validates the use of cyclotomic conditionally convergent series $\sum \alpha^{nj}/n$ appearing in logarithmic derivative formulas for the class number.
