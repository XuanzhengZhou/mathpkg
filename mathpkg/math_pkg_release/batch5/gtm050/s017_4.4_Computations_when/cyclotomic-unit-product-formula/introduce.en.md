The product formula for cyclotomic units relates the product $\prod_{j=2}^{\lambda-1} (\alpha^j - 1)$ of all nontrivial $\alpha^j - 1$ to a simple polynomial expression in $\alpha$ with descending integer coefficients. This identity holds for arbitrary $\lambda$ and is used in Kummer's computational method for working with cyclotomic integers—specifically for computing inverses such as $(\alpha + 6)(\alpha - 1)^{-1}$ and for division by cyclotomic integers in the course of prime factorization. The formula appears as Exercise 11 in Edwards' exposition of Kummer's theory.
