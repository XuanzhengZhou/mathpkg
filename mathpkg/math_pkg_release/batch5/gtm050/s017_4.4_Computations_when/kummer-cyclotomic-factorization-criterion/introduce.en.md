Kummer's divisibility criterion provides a practical method for testing whether a cyclotomic integer $g(\alpha)$ is divisible by a particular prime divisor of a rational prime $p \equiv 1 \pmod{\lambda}$. By evaluating $g$ at an integer $k$ arising from a primitive root modulo $p$, one reduces the problem of divisibility in the cyclotomic integer ring $\mathbb{Z}[\alpha]$ to a simple congruence condition modulo $p$. Each of the $\lambda-1$ prime factors of $p$ corresponds to a different power $k^j$, enabling the complete factorization of $p$ to be determined by testing $g(k), g(k^2), \ldots, g(k^{\lambda-1})$. This criterion was the computational engine behind Kummer's extensive factorization tables for $\lambda \leq 19$, $p < 1000$.
