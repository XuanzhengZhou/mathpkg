Let $p \equiv 1 \pmod{\lambda}$ be a prime. Let $h$ be a primitive root modulo $p$ and set $k \equiv h^{(p-1)/\lambda} \pmod{p}$. Then a cyclotomic integer $g(\alpha) \in \mathbb{Z}[\alpha]$ is divisible by a given prime divisor $h(\alpha)$ of $p$ if and only if

$$
g(k) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}.
$$

The $\lambda-1$ distinct prime factors of $p$ in $\mathbb{Z}[\alpha]$ correspond to the $\lambda-1$ distinct residue classes $k, k^2, \ldots, k^{\lambda-1} \pmod{p}$, with the factor corresponding to $k^j$ dividing $g(\alpha)$ exactly when $g(k^j) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$.
