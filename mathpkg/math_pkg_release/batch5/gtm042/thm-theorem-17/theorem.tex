Let $X$ be a family of subgroups of a finite group $G$. Let Ind: $\bigoplus_{H \in X} R(H) \rightarrow R(G)$ be the homomorphism defined by the family of Ind$^G_H$, $H \in X$. Then the following properties are equivalent:

(i) $G$ is the union of the conjugates of the subgroups belonging to $X$.

(ii) The cokernel of Ind: $\bigoplus_{H \in X} R(H) \rightarrow R(G)$ is finite.

Since $R(G)$ is finitely generated as a group, we can rephrase (ii) in the following way:

(ii') For each character $\chi$ of $G$, there exist virtual characters $\chi_H \in R(H)$, $H \in X$, and an integer $d \geqslant 1$ such that

$$d\chi = \sum_{H \in X} \text{Ind}^G_H(\chi_H).$$
