Let $r = \operatorname{Card}(S_k)$ be the number of isomorphism classes of simple $k[G]$-modules. Then $P_A(G)$ can be identified with $\mathbb{Z}^r$ and $P_A^+(G)$ with $\mathbb{N}^r$ (cf. 14.3 and 14.4). Under this identification, an element $x \in P_A(G)$ corresponds to an $r$-tuple of integers, and the condition $nx \in P_A^+(G)$ means that each coordinate of $nx$ is nonnegative. Since $n \geq 1$, this implies that each coordinate of $x$ is nonnegative, hence $x \in P_A^+(G)$.
