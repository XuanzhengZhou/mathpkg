Let $E$ be a simple $K[G]$-module, and let $P$ be a lattice in $E$ stable under $G$. Assume that the dimension $N$ of $E$ is divisible by the largest power $p^n$ of $p$ dividing the order $g$ of $G$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $P$ is a projective $A[G]$-module.
\item[(b)] The canonical map $A[G] \to \operatorname{End}_A(P)$ is surjective, and its kernel is a direct factor in $A[G]$ (as a two-sided ideal).
\item[(c)] The reduction $\bar{P} = P/\mathfrak{m}P$ of $P$ is a simple and projective $k[G]$-module.
\end{enumerate}
