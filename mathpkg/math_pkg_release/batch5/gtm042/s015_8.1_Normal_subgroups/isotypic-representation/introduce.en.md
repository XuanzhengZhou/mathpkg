An isotypic representation is one that decomposes as a direct sum of mutually isomorphic irreducible subrepresentations. In other words, only one isomorphism type of irreducible representation appears in the decomposition. This notion is central to the canonical decomposition of a representation into isotypic components and appears in the classification of irreducible representations relative to normal subgroups (Proposition 24).
