Let $H$ be a normal subgroup of a group $G$ and let $R_H$ be a Sylow $p$-subgroup of $G/H$.

(a) Show that there exists a Sylow $p$-subgroup $P$ of $G$ whose image in $G/H$ is $R_H$ [use the conjugacy of Sylow subgroups].

(b) Show that $P$ is unique if $H$ is a $p$-group or if $H$ is in the center of $G$ [reduce to the case where $H$ has order prime to $p$, and use the fact that each homomorphism from $R_H$ into $H$ is trivial].
