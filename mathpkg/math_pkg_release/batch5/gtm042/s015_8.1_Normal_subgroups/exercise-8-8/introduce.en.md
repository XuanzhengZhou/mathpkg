Exercise 8.8 establishes a key structural property of finite nilpotent groups: they are exactly the direct products of their Sylow $p$-subgroups. For each prime $p$, a nilpotent group has exactly one Sylow $p$-subgroup, which is necessarily normal. The proof uses induction and the fact that the quotient by the center inherits nilpotency.
