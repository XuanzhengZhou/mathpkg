Exercise 8.4 provides a concrete counterexample showing that solvability does not imply supersolvability. The alternating group $\mathfrak{A}_4$ has order 12 and is solvable (its derived subgroup is the Klein four-group, which is abelian), but it has no cyclic normal series, so it fails to be supersolvable. The symmetric group $\mathfrak{S}_4$ is likewise solvable but not supersolvable.
