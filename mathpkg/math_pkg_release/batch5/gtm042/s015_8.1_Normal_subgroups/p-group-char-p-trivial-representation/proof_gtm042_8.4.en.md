This follows from Lemma 3 of 8.3: for a $p$-group $G$ acting on a vector space $V$ in characteristic $p$, the set $V^G$ of fixed vectors is non-trivial whenever $V$ is non-trivial (since $\dim V \equiv \dim V^G \pmod{p}$ by the lemma applied to the underlying set). If $V$ is irreducible, then $V^G = V$, so $G$ acts trivially.
