In view of the preceding discussion it suffices to show that the center of every nontrivial $p$-group $G$ is nontrivial. This follows from Lemma 3 applied to $X = G$ with $G$ acting by inner automorphisms. The set $X^G$ is the center $C$ of $G$. Lemma 3 gives

$$\operatorname{Card}(C) \equiv \operatorname{Card}(G) \equiv 0 \pmod{p},$$

whence $C \neq \{1\}$, which proves the theorem.
