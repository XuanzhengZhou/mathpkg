Let $A$ be a normal subgroup of a group $G$, and let $\rho: G \rightarrow GL(V)$ be an irreducible representation of $G$. Then:

(a) either there exists a subgroup $H$ of $G$, unequal to $G$ and containing $A$, and an irreducible representation $\sigma$ of $H$ such that $\rho$ is induced by $\sigma$;

(b) or else the restriction of $\rho$ to $A$ is isotypic.
