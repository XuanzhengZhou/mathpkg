Proposition 24 describes the possible structure of an irreducible representation $\rho$ of a group $G$ relative to a normal subgroup $A$. The theorem presents a dichotomy: either $\rho$ is induced from a proper subgroup $H$ containing $A$, or the restriction of $\rho$ to $A$ decomposes into copies of a single irreducible representation (i.e., is isotypic). The proof uses the canonical decomposition of $\operatorname{Res}_A \rho$ into isotypic components and the fact that $G$ permutes these components transitively by irreducibility.
