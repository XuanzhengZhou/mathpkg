A solvable group is one that admits a finite normal series with abelian successive quotients $G_{i-1}/G_i$. Equivalently, a solvable group is built up from the trivial group by a finite sequence of extensions by abelian groups. Solvability is a fundamental finiteness condition in finite group theory; for example, Burnside's theorem asserts that every group of order $p^a q^b$ (with $p$, $q$ primes) is solvable.
