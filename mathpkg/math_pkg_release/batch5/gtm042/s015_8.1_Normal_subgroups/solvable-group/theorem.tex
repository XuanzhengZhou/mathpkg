A group $G$ is \textbf{solvable} if there exist subgroups $G = G_0 \supset G_1 \supset \dots \supset G_n = \{1\}$ such that $G_i$ is normal in $G_{i-1}$ and $G_{i-1}/G_i$ is abelian for $1 \leq i \leq n$. (Equivalent definition: $G$ is obtained from the group $\{1\}$ by a finite number of extensions with abelian kernels.)
