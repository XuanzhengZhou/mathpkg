A nilpotent group is one that admits a finite central series: a chain of subgroups each normal in $G$ such that each successive quotient is contained in the center of the corresponding quotient group. Equivalently, a nilpotent group is built from the trivial group by a finite sequence of central extensions. Nilpotent implies supersolvable, which in turn implies solvable. Every $p$-group is nilpotent (Theorem 14).
