A group $G$ is \textbf{nilpotent} if there exist subgroups $G = G_0 \supset G_1 \supset \dots \supset G_n = \{1\}$ such that $G_i$ is normal in $G$ and $G_i/G_{i-1}$ is contained in the center of $G/G_{i-1}$ for $1 \leq i \leq n$. (Equivalent definition: $G$ is obtained from the group $\{1\}$ by a finite number of central extensions.)
