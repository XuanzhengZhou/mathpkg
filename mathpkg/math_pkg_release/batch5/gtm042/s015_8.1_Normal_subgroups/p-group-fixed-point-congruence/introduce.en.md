Lemma 3 is a fundamental counting principle for actions of $p$-groups on finite sets: the total cardinality of the set is congruent modulo $p$ to the number of points fixed by the entire group. The proof decomposes $X \setminus X^G$ into nontrivial orbits, each of which has cardinality a positive power of $p$ (by the orbit-stabilizer theorem), hence is divisible by $p$. This lemma underlies the proof that nontrivial $p$-groups have nontrivial centers and is central to Sylow theory.
