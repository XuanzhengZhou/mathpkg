The proof is by induction on the order of $G$. In case (a) of the preceding proposition the induction hypothesis shows that the degree of $\sigma$ divides $(H : A)$, and by multiplying this relation by $(G : H)$ we see that the degree of $\rho$ divides $(G : A)$. In case (b) let $G' = \rho(G)$ and $A' = \rho(A)$; since the canonical map $G/A \rightarrow G'/A'$ is surjective, $(G' : A')$ divides $(G : A)$. The fact that $A$ is abelian and the restriction of $\rho$ to $A$ is isotypic implies that the elements of $A'$ are homotheties, thus are contained in the center of $G'$. By Proposition 17 of 6.5, it follows that the degree of $\rho$ divides $(G' : A')$ and a fortiori $(G : A)$.
