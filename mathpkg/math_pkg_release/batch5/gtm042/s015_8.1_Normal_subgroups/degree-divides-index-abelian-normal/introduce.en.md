This corollary of Proposition 24 gives an arithmetic restriction on the degrees of irreducible representations when the group $G$ contains an abelian normal subgroup $A$. Specifically, $\deg(\rho)$ must divide the index $(G : A)$. The proof proceeds by induction on the order of $G$, applying the dichotomy of Proposition 24: in the induced case one uses the induction hypothesis on $H$ and multiplies by $(G : H)$, while in the isotypic case one passes to the quotient modulo homotheties.
