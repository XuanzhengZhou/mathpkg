Exercise 8.6 walks through a proof of Burnside's famous $p^a q^b$ theorem: every finite group whose order is divisible by at most two distinct primes is solvable. The proof uses conjugacy class sizes and an inductive argument. The exercise also notes that such groups need not be supersolvable (e.g., $\mathfrak{A}_4$), and that three primes suffice for nonsolvability (e.g., $\mathfrak{S}_5$ has order $120 = 2^3 \cdot 3 \cdot 5$).
