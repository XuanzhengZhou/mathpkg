A supersolvable group is a group that admits a finite normal series in which every subgroup $G_i$ is normal in the whole group $G$ and each successive quotient $G_i/G_{i-1}$ is cyclic. This is a stronger condition than solvability: supersolvable implies solvable, but the converse fails (e.g., the alternating group $\mathfrak{A}_4$ is solvable but not supersolvable). Nilpotent groups are always supersolvable.
