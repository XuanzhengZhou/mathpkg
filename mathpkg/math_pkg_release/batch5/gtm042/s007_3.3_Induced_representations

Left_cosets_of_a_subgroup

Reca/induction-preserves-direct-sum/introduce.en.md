The induction functor $\operatorname{Ind}_H^G$ is additive: it preserves direct sums of representations. This property is essential in the proof of Theorem 11, where it allows reduction from arbitrary representations to irreducible ones. It also implies that the character of an induced representation is additive with respect to the inducing representation.
