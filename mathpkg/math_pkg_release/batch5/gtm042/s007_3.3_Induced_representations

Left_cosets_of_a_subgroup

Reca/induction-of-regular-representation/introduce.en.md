The regular representation of a finite group $G$ is induced from the regular representation of any subgroup $H$. This is seen by taking the group algebra basis $(e_t)_{t \in G}$ and restricting to the $H$-invariant subspace spanned by $(e_t)_{t \in H}$, which carries the regular representation of $H$. This fact is used in the proof of Theorem 11 to reduce the existence of induced representations to the regular case.
