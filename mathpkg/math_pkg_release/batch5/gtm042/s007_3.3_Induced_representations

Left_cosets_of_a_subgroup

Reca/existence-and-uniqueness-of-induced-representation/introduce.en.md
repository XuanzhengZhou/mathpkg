Theorem 11 guarantees that for any representation of a subgroup $H$, an induced representation of the ambient group $G$ always exists and is unique up to isomorphism. The existence proof reduces to the case of irreducible representations by embedding $\theta$ into the regular representation of $H$, then inducing to the regular representation of $G$. Uniqueness follows from a dimension argument using the universal property (Lemma 1).
