A left coset $sH$ of a subgroup $H$ in a group $G$ is the set obtained by multiplying a fixed element $s \in G$ with every element of $H$. Left cosets partition the group $G$ into disjoint subsets of equal size. This notion is fundamental for constructing induced representations, where the representation space decomposes according to a system of coset representatives.
