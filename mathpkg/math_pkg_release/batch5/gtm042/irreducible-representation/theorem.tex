Let $\rho: G \to \text{GL}(V)$ be a linear representation of $G$. It is called \textbf{irreducible} (or \textbf{simple}) if $V \neq 0$ and if no vector subspace of $V$ is stable under $G$, except $0$ and $V$. By Theorem 1, this is equivalent to saying that $V$ is not the direct sum of two nontrivial representations. A representation of degree 1 is always irreducible.
