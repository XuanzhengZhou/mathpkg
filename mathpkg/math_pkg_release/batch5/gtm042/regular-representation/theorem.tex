Let $V$ be a vector space having a basis $(e_t)_{t \in G}$ indexed by the elements of $G$. For $s \in G$, let $\rho_s$ be the linear map of $V$ into $V$ which sends $e_t$ to $e_{st}$. This defines a linear representation of $G$, called the \textbf{regular representation} of $G$. Its degree is equal to the order $g$ of $G$.

Conversely, let $W$ be a representation of $G$ containing a vector $w$ such that the vectors $\rho_s(w)$, $s \in G$, form a basis of $W$. Then $W$ is isomorphic to the regular representation via $\tau(e_s) = \rho_s(w)$.
