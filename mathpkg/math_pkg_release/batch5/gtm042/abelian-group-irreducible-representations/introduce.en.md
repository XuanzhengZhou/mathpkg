Theorem 9 characterizes abelian groups in terms of their representation theory: a finite group is abelian if and only if every irreducible representation is one-dimensional. This follows from the fact that the number of conjugacy classes equals the group order exactly when the group is abelian, combined with $\sum n_i^2 = g$. The result extends to infinite groups via Schur's Lemma.
