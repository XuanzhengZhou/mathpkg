Let $R$ be the free $\mathbb{Z}$-module with basis $S_L$. The family of the various $[E]$, $E \in S_L$, defines a homomorphism $\alpha: R \to R_L(G)$.

On the other hand, if $F$ is an $L[G]$-module, and if $E \in S_L$, let $\ell_E(F)$ denote the number of times which $E$ appears in a composition series of $F$. It is clear that $\ell_E$ is an additive function of $F$. Thus there exists a homomorphism $\beta_E: R_L(G) \to \mathbb{Z}$ such that $\beta_E([F]) = \ell_E(F)$ for all $F$.

The $\beta_E$'s define a homomorphism

$$\beta: R_L(G) \to R,$$

and it is immediate that $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are inverses of one another. The proposition follows.

More generally, the same argument applies to the category of modules of finite length over an arbitrary ring.
