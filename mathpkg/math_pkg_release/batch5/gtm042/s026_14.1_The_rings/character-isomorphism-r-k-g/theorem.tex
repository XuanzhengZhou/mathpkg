Let $G$ be a finite group and $K$ a field of characteristic zero. The map $x \mapsto \chi_x$, defined by extending the character map $E \mapsto \chi_E$ linearly from $R_K^+(G)$ to $R_K(G)$, is an isomorphism from $R_K(G)$ onto the group of virtual characters of $G$ over $K$.

We say that $\chi_x$ is the character (or the virtual character) of an element $x \in R_K(G)$.
