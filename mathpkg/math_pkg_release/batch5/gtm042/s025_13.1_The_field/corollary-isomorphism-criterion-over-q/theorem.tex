Let $V$ and $V'$ be two linear representations of $G$ over $\mathbb{Q}$. In order that $V$ be isomorphic to $V'$, it is necessary and sufficient that, for each cyclic subgroup $C$ of $G$, we have

$$\dim V^C = \dim V'^{C},$$

where $V^C$ (resp. $V'^{C}$) denotes the subspace of $V$ (resp. $V'$) consisting of the elements invariant under $C$.
