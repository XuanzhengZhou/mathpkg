This corollary of Theorem 30 provides a practical criterion for testing whether two representations of $G$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ are isomorphic: it suffices to compare the dimensions of the subspaces fixed by each cyclic subgroup. The necessity is obvious (isomorphic representations have equal fixed-point dimensions), while sufficiency follows by applying Theorem 30 to the difference of the characters, using the fact that $\dim V^C = \langle \operatorname{Res}_C^G \chi, 1_C \rangle_C$.
