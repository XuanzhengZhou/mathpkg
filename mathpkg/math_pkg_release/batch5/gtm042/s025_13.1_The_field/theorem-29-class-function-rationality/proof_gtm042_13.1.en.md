The theorem follows by applying the results of Section 12.4, which characterizes the action of the Galois group $\Gamma_Q$ on the representation ring, together with Gauss's theorem identifying $\Gamma_Q = (\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z})^*$. The compatibility condition $\sigma_t(f) = \Psi^t(f)$ for all $t$ prime to $m$ encodes the $\Gamma_Q$-invariance required for rationality over $Q$.
