Theorem 29 provides a precise criterion for determining when a class function on a finite group $G$ lies in the rational representation ring $Q \otimes R(G)$ or its subspace $Q \otimes R_Q(G)$. The condition involves compatibility between the Galois automorphisms $\sigma_t$ of the cyclotomic field $Q(m)$ and the Adams operations $\Psi^t$ acting on class functions. Part (b) notably implies that for $f$ to belong to $Q \otimes R_Q(G)$, its values must be rational and constant on each cyclic subgroup of $G$.
