Corollary 2 establishes a surprising equivalence: all irreducible characters of a finite group $G$ take rational values exactly when any two elements generating the same cyclic subgroup are conjugate in $G$. Moreover, rationality of character values automatically implies integrality, since character values are algebraic integers and the only rational algebraic integers are ordinary integers. This provides a purely group-theoretic criterion for rationality of all characters.
