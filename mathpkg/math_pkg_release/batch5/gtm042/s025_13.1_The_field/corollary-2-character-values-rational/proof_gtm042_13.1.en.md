The equivalence of (i) and (i') follows from the fact that character values are algebraic integers, hence are elements of $\mathbb{Z}$ whenever they belong to $\mathbb{Q}$. The equivalence of (i) and (ii) follows from Theorem 29(b): a class function $f$ belongs to $Q \otimes R_Q(G)$ if and only if $f$ has values in $Q$ and $\Psi^t(f) = f$ for all $t$ prime to $m$. The condition $\Psi^t(f) = f$ means $f(x) = f(x^t)$ for all $x$ and all $t$ coprime to $m$, which is equivalent to $f$ being constant on each cyclic subgroup. Applying this to all irreducible characters (which span $Q \otimes R_Q(G)$) yields the equivalent condition (ii).
