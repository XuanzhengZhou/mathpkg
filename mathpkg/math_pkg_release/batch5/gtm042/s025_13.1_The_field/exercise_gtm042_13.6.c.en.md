Show that the center of $\mathbb{Q}[G]$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Q} \otimes R(G)$ in each of the following cases:

$(c_1)$ $G$ is abelian (use an isomorphism from $G$ onto its dual $\hat{G}$, and observe that $\mathbb{Q}[G] = R(\hat{G})$).

$(c_2)$ $G$ is a $p$-group and $p \neq 2$ (use the fact that $\Gamma_Q$ is cyclic).

(For an example of a group $G$ such that $X$ and $Y$ are not $\Gamma_Q$-isomorphic, see J. Thompson, J. of Algebra, 14, 1970, pp. 1-4.)
