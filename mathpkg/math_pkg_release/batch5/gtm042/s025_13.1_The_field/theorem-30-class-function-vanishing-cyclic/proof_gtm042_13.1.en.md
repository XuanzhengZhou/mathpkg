We prove this result by induction on $\operatorname{Card}(G)$. Let $s \in G$, and let $C(s)$ be the cyclic subgroup of $G$ generated by $s$. Let $x \in C(s)$; if $x$ generates $C(s)$, we have $\theta(x) = \theta(s)$ since $x$ and $s$ are $\Gamma_Q$-conjugate (by the criterion that two elements are $\Gamma_Q$-conjugate iff they generate conjugate cyclic subgroups); if $x$ generates a proper subgroup of $C(s)$, the induction hypothesis (applied to the restriction of $\theta$ to this proper subgroup) shows that $\theta(x) = 0$. Therefore

$$\sum_{x \in C(s)} \theta(x) = a \cdot \theta(s),$$

where $a$ is the number of generators of $C(s)$. But by hypothesis we have

$$\sum_{x \in C(s)} \theta(x) = 0,$$

and therefore $\theta(s) = 0$. Since $s \in G$ was arbitrary, $\theta = 0$.
