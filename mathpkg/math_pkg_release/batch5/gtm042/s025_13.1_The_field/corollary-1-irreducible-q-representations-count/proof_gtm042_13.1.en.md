This follows from Corollary 2 to Theorem 25 (in Section 12.4), which states that the number of isomorphism classes of irreducible representations of $G$ over $Q$ equals the number of $\Gamma_Q$-conjugacy classes of $G$. By the earlier corollary (Gauss), two elements are $\Gamma_Q$-conjugate if and only if the cyclic subgroups they generate are conjugate. Hence the $\Gamma_Q$-conjugacy classes correspond bijectively to conjugacy classes of cyclic subgroups of $G$.
