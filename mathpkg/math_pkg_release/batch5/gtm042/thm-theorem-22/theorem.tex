Let $\varphi$ be a class function on $G$ such that, for each elementary subgroup $H$ of $G$, and each character $\chi$ of degree 1 of $H$, the number

$$\langle \chi, Res_H \varphi \rangle_H = \frac{1}{\text{Card}(H)} \sum_{s \in H} \chi(s^{-1}) \varphi(s)$$

belongs to $B$. Then $\varphi$ belongs to $B \otimes R(G)$.

Let $H$ be an elementary subgroup of $G$. Let

$$Res_H^G \varphi = \sum_\omega c_\omega \omega, \quad \text{where } c_\omega = \langle \omega, Res_H \varphi \rangle_H,$$

be the decomposition of $Res_H^G \varphi$ into irreducible characters $\omega$ of $H$. By th. 16, each character $\omega$ is induced by a character $\chi_\omega$ of degree 1 of a subgroup $H_\omega$ of $H$. By Frobenius reciprocity, we have

$$c_\omega = \langle \chi_\omega, Res_H^G \varphi \rangle_H_\omega.$$

Since $H_\omega$ is an elementary group, the hypothesis on $\varphi$ insures that $c_\omega$ belongs to $B$. Consequently, $Res_H \varphi = \sum_\omega c_\omega \omega$ belongs to $B \otimes R(H)$, and the result follows by th. 21.
