Identify $C[G]$ with the product of the $\text{End}(W_i)$ via the isomorphism $\tilde{\rho}: C[G] \xrightarrow{\sim} \prod_{i=1}^h \text{End}(W_i)$ given by the extended algebra homomorphisms of all irreducible representations (Wedderburn decomposition). Under this identification, the center of $C[G]$ becomes the product of the centers of the $\text{End}(W_i)$. But the center of $\text{End}(W_i)$ consists of homotheties, i.e., scalar multiples of the identity, which is isomorphic to $\mathbb{C}$. Thus we obtain an isomorphism of $\text{Cent. } C[G]$ onto $\mathbb{C}^h$. The map is given explicitly by $u \mapsto (\omega_1(u), \ldots, \omega_h(u))$.
