The collection of homomorphisms $\omega_i: \text{Cent. } C[G] \to \mathbb{C}$ indexed by the $h$ irreducible representations of $G$ together give an algebra isomorphism from the center of the group algebra to the product algebra $\mathbb{C}^h$. This follows from identifying $C[G]$ with $\prod_i \text{End}(W_i)$ via the Wedderburn decomposition; the center of $C[G]$ then corresponds to the product of the centers of the $\text{End}(W_i)$, each of which consists of scalar matrices.
