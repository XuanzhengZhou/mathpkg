An algebraic integer is a complex number that satisfies a monic polynomial equation with integer coefficients. This generalizes ordinary integers: every $n \in \mathbb{Z}$ satisfies $x - n = 0$. Roots of unity are fundamental examples, since $\zeta^m = 1$ for some $m$ yields $x^m - 1 = 0$. The concept plays a crucial role in the integrality theory of group characters.
