A rational number that is an algebraic integer must in fact be an ordinary integer. Writing $x = p/q$ in lowest terms and substituting into the monic polynomial yields $p^n + a_1 q p^{n-1} + \cdots + a_n q^n = 0$, which forces $q \mid p^n$ and thus $q = 1$. This simple fact is essential for bounding degrees of irreducible representations.
