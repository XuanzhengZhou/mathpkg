By Proposition 12, the number in question is the image of $u$ under the homomorphism
$$\omega: \text{Cent. } C[G] \to \mathbb{C}$$
associated with the irreducible representation $\rho$, namely
$$\omega(u) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{s \in G} u(s)\chi(s).$$

By Proposition 16, $u$ is integral over $\mathbb{Z}$. Since $\omega$ is an algebra homomorphism (in particular a ring homomorphism), the image of an integral element under a ring homomorphism remains integral. Therefore $\omega(u)$ is an algebraic integer.
