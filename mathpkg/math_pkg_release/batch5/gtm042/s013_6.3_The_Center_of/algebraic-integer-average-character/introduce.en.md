For an irreducible representation $\rho$ with character $\chi$ and degree $n$, the normalized sum $\frac{1}{n} \sum_{s \in G} u(s)\chi(s)$ is an algebraic integer whenever all coefficients $u(s)$ of the central element $u$ are algebraic integers. This follows because this number equals $\omega(u)$, the image of $u$ under the homomorphism $\omega: \text{Cent. } C[G] \to \mathbb{C}$ associated with $\rho$. Since $u$ is integral over $\mathbb{Z}$ by Proposition 16, its image under the algebra homomorphism is also integral.
