The center of the group algebra $C[G]$ consists of all elements that commute with every element of $G$ under the convolution product. A natural basis for the center is given by the class sums $e_c = \sum_{s \in c} s$, where $c$ runs over the conjugacy classes of $G$. The dimension of the center equals the number $h$ of conjugacy classes, linking the ring-theoretic structure of $C[G]$ to the conjugacy geometry of the group.
