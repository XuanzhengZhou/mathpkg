For each irreducible representation $\rho_i$ of $G$, the extended algebra homomorphism $\tilde{\rho}_i$ sends every central element of the group algebra $C[G]$ to a scalar multiple of the identity on the representation space $W_i$. This induces an algebra homomorphism $\omega_i$ from the center of $C[G]$ to $\mathbb{C}$, whose value on a central element $u$ is given by the normalized trace formula. This reformulates Schur's lemma in the context of the algebra homomorphism associated with a representation.
