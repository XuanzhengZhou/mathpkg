A deep result in representation theory: the degree $n$ of any irreducible representation of a finite group $G$ divides the index $(G:C)$ of the center $C$ in $G$. The proof, due to J. Tate, considers the $m$-fold tensor product representation of $G^m$, restricts to a subgroup $H \subset C^m$ that acts trivially, and uses the integrality results from Propositions 14-16. The divisibility follows from the fact that $(g/cn)^m \in c^{-1}\mathbb{Z}$ for all $m$, forcing $g/cn$ to be an integer.
