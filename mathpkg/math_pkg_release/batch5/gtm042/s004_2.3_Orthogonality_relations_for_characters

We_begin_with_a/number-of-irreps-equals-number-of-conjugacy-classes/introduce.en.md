This corollary is one of the most striking consequences of the completeness of irreducible characters. It establishes a beautiful bijection between the two fundamental invariants of a finite group: the number of irreducible representations equals the number of conjugacy classes. Practically, this means the character table is always a square matrix, and theoretically it ties the representation theory tightly to the internal conjugacy structure of the group.
