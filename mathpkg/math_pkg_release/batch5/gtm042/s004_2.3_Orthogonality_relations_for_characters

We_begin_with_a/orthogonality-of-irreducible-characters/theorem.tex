Let $G$ be a finite group and let $(\cdot|\cdot)$ denote the scalar product on the space of complex-valued functions on $G$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $\chi$ is the character of an irreducible representation of $G$, then $(\chi|\chi) = 1$ (i.e., $\chi$ is of norm 1).
\item[(ii)] If $\chi$ and $\chi'$ are the characters of two nonisomorphic irreducible representations of $G$, then $(\chi|\chi') = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
