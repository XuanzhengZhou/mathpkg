Theorem 3 establishes the first orthogonality relations for irreducible characters with respect to the scalar product on class functions. Irreducible characters are orthonormal: each has norm one, and distinct irreducible characters are orthogonal. This result follows from Schur's lemma (Proposition 4 of Chapter 2) and is the cornerstone of character theory, allowing one to decompose arbitrary characters into irreducible components by computing scalar products.
