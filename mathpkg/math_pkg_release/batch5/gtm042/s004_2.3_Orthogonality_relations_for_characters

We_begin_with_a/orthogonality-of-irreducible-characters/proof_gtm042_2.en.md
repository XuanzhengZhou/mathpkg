The theorem follows from the orthogonality relations for matrix coefficients (Corollaries 2 and 3 to Theorem 2 of Section 2.2). Suppose that the matrices $(r_{ij}(t))$ of the irreducible representations are chosen to be unitary (this can be realized by a suitable choice of basis, cf. Section 1.3). Then $r_{ij}(t^{-1}) = r_{ji}(t)^*$, and the orthogonality relations for matrix coefficients translate directly into the stated orthogonality relations for characters under the scalar product $(\phi|\psi)$.

More explicitly, if $\chi$ is the character of an irreducible representation $\rho$ of degree $n$, then $\chi(t) = \sum_i r_{ii}(t)$. Applying the orthogonality of matrix coefficients yields $(\chi|\chi) = 1$. If $\chi$ and $\chi'$ arise from nonisomorphic irreducible representations, the corresponding matrix coefficient orthogonality gives $(\chi|\chi') = 0$.
