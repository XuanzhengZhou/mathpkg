This corollary reveals a deep structural relationship: the regular representation contains every irreducible representation, and the number of times each appears is exactly its dimension. This is the representation-theoretic analogue of the fact that the group algebra decomposes as a direct sum of matrix algebras. The result leads directly to the fundamental identity $\sum n_i^2 = g$.
