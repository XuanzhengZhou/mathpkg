Proposition 5 computes the character of the regular representation of a finite group. Its character is highly concentrated at the identity element, taking the value of the group order there, and vanishing everywhere else. This simple but powerful formula is the starting point for deriving the fundamental numerical relations among irreducible characters, including the degree-sum relation $\sum n_i^2 = g$.
