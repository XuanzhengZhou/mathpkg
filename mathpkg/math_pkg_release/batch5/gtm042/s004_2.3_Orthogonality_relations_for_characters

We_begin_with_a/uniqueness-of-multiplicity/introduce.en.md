This corollary asserts that the decomposition of a representation into irreducible components is unique in the sense of multiplicities: however one decomposes $V$ into a direct sum of irreducibles, each isomorphism type of irreducible representation appears the same number of times. The multiplicity is intrinsic to the representation and equals $(\phi|\chi)$, which manifestly does not depend on any particular decomposition.
