Let $V$ and $V'$ be two representations of $G$ with the same character $\phi$. Decompose each into irreducible representations. By Corollary 1 (uniqueness of multiplicity), the multiplicity of any irreducible representation $W$ (with character $\chi$) in $V$ is $(\phi|\chi)$. The same computation for $V'$ yields the same multiplicity, since $V'$ also has character $\phi$. Thus $V$ and $V'$ contain each irreducible representation the same number of times, and are therefore isomorphic.

If $\chi_1, \ldots, \chi_h$ are the distinct irreducible characters of $G$, and $W_1, \ldots, W_h$ denote corresponding representations, each representation $V$ with character $\phi$ is isomorphic to a direct sum
$$V = m_1 W_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus m_h W_h,$$
where $m_i = (\phi|\chi_i)$ are uniquely determined integers. $\square$
