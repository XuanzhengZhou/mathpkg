These relations are among the most important numerical identities in character theory. Part (a) states that the sum of the squares of the degrees of all irreducible representations equals the order of the group---a powerful constraint that often allows one to check whether a complete set of irreducible representations has been found. Part (b) gives a weighted sum of character values that vanishes at every non-identity element, generalizing the column orthogonality relations.
