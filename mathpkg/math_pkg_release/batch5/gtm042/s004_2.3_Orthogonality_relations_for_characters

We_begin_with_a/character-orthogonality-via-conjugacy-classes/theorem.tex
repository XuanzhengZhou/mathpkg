Let $G$ be a finite group, let $s \in G$, and let $c(s)$ be the number of elements in the conjugacy class of $s$. Let $\chi_1, \ldots, \chi_h$ be the distinct irreducible characters of $G$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{h} \chi_i(s)^* \chi_i(s) = \frac{g}{c(s)}$.
\item[(b)] For $t \in G$ not conjugate to $s$, $\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{h} \chi_i(s)^* \chi_i(t) = 0$.
\end{enumerate}

(For $s = 1$, this yields Corollary 2 to Proposition 5.)
