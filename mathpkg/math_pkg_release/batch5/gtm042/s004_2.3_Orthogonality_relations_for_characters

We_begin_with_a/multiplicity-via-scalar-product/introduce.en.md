Theorem 4 provides the practical mechanism for decomposing representations: the multiplicity of any irreducible representation $W$ inside a given representation $V$ is exactly the scalar product of their characters. Combined with Theorem 3, this means one can read off the complete decomposition of a character into irreducibles by computing scalar products against the irreducible characters. This is the computational heart of character theory.
