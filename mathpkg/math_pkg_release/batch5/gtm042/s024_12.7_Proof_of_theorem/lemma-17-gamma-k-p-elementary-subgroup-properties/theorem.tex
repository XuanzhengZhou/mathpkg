Let $x$ be a $p'$-element of $G$, let $C$ be the cyclic subgroup generated by $x$, let $N(x)$ be the set of $y \in G$ such that there exists $t \in \Gamma_K$ with $yxy^{-1} = x^t$, and let $P$ be a Sylow $p$-subgroup of $N(x)$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $H = C \cdot P$ is a $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary subgroup of $G$.
\item[(b)] Each linear representation of $C$ over $K$ extends to $H$.
\item[(c)] The map $\operatorname{Res}: R_K(H) \to R_K(G)$ is surjective.
\end{enumerate}
