Lemma 17 is a key structural result used in the proof of Brauer's theorem (Theorem 28). Given a $p'$-element $x$ of a finite group $G$, it constructs an associated $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary subgroup $H = C \cdot P$, where $C = \langle x \rangle$ and $P$ is a Sylow $p$-subgroup of the $\Gamma_K$-normalizer $N(x)$. The lemma establishes three crucial properties: (a) $H$ is indeed $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary; (b) every linear representation of the cyclic subgroup $C$ over $K$ extends to a representation of $H$; and (c) the restriction map from $R_K(H)$ to $R_K(G)$ is surjective. These properties enable the construction of class functions with prescribed congruence conditions modulo prime ideals.
