Assertion (a) is clear.

To prove (b), it suffices to consider the case of an irreducible representation of $C$ over $K$. Such a representation can be obtained by choosing a homomorphism $\chi: C \to L^*$, taking as vector space the subfield $K_\chi$ of $L$ generated by $\chi(C)$, and defining $\rho: C \to \operatorname{GL}(K_\chi)$ by the formula
$$\rho(s)\omega = \chi(s)\omega \quad \text{if } s \in C \text{ and } \omega \in K_\chi.$$

The group $\Gamma_K = \operatorname{Gal}(L/K)$ acts $K$-linearly on $K_\chi$. If $y \in P$, let $t \in \Gamma_K$ be such that $yxy^{-1} = x^t$, and define $\rho(y)$ as the restriction of $\sigma_t$ to $K_\chi$. One checks that $\rho(y)$ does not depend upon the choice of $t$, and that
$$\rho(y)\rho(x)\rho(y)^{-1} = \rho(x^t).$$

It follows that the homomorphisms of $C$ and of $P$ into $\operatorname{GL}(K_\chi)$ thus defined extend to a homomorphism of $H$ into $\operatorname{GL}(K_\chi)$, which proves (b).

For (c), let $Z$ be the set $\{x^t \mid t \in \Gamma_K\}$, i.e., the $\Gamma_K$-conjugacy class of $x$. Define the class function $\psi'$ on $G$ by
$$\psi'(x) = \frac{1}{|H|} \sum_{\substack{y \in G \\ yxy^{-1} \in Z}} 1 = \frac{|N(x)|}{|P|}.$$

Since $P$ is a Sylow $p$-subgroup of $N(x)$, the index $|N(x)|/|P|$ is an integer prime to $p$. Hence $\psi'(x)$ is an integer prime to $p$, which implies the surjectivity of the restriction map $\operatorname{Res}: R_K(H) \to R_K(G)$, proving (c).
