Exercise 12.7 from Serre's "Linear Representations of Finite Groups" asks to prove that the induction map from the direct sum of the reduced Grothendieck groups $\overline{R}_K(H)$ over all $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups $H \in X_K$ to $\overline{R}_K(G)$ is surjective. This result is a key step in the proof of Brauer's theorem on rationality, and the hint suggests adapting the argument used in the proof of Proposition 36.
