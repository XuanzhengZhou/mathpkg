Lemma 15 provides a decomposition result for class functions on a finite group $G$ that are constant on $\Gamma_K$-classes and take values in $gA$ (where $g = |G|$ and $A$ is the ring generated by the $m$th roots of unity). Any such function $f$ can be expressed as a sum of induced functions $\operatorname{Ind}_C^G(\varphi_C)$ from cyclic subgroups $C$ of $G$, with each $\varphi_C$ belonging to $A \otimes R_K(C)$. This lemma is a crucial technical step in the proof of Brauer's theorem (Theorem 28).
