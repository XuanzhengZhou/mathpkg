Let $p$ be a prime ideal of $A$ such that $p \cap Z = pZ$ (which is equivalent to saying that $A/p$ is a finite field of characteristic $p$). Let $\chi \in A \otimes R(G)$, let $x \in G$, and let $x_r$ be the $p'$-component of $x$. Show that $\chi(x) \equiv \chi(x_r)$ (mod. $p$) (same proof as for lemma 7) but that we no longer always have $\chi(x) \equiv \chi(x_r)$ (mod. $pA$).
