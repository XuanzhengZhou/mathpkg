Let $V$ be a vector space over the field $\mathbb{C}$ of complex numbers and let $\text{GL}(V)$ be the group of isomorphisms of $V$ onto itself. Suppose $G$ is a finite group. A \textbf{linear representation} of $G$ in $V$ is a homomorphism $\rho$ from the group $G$ into the group $\text{GL}(V)$, i.e., an association of each element $s \in G$ with an element $\rho(s) \in \text{GL}(V)$ such that

$$\rho(st) = \rho(s) \cdot \rho(t) \quad \text{for } s, t \in G.$$

This implies $\rho(1) = 1$ and $\rho(s^{-1}) = \rho(s)^{-1}$. The space $V$ is called a \textbf{representation space} of $G$ (or simply a representation of $G$).
