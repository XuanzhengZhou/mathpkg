The alternating square $\text{Alt}^2(V)$ is the subspace of $V \otimes V$ consisting of antisymmetric tensors (those negated by swapping factors). It is a subrepresentation of the tensor square, with dimension $n(n-1)/2$. Together with the symmetric square, it gives $V \otimes V = \text{Sym}^2(V) \oplus \text{Alt}^2(V)$.
