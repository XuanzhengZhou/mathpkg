Let $V$ be a representation of $G$, and let $\theta$ be the automorphism of $V \otimes V$ such that $\theta(x \cdot y) = y \cdot x$. The \textbf{alternating square} $\text{Alt}^2(V)$ is the subspace of elements $z \in V \otimes V$ such that $\theta(z) = -z$.

If $(e_i)$ is a basis of $V$, the elements $(e_i \cdot e_j - e_j \cdot e_i)_{i < j}$ form a basis of $\text{Alt}^2(V)$. We have

$$\dim \text{Alt}^2(V) = \frac{n(n-1)}{2}$$

where $n = \dim V$.
