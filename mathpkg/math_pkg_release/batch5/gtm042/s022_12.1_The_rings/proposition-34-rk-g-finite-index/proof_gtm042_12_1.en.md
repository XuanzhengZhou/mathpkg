First, observe that each irreducible representation of $G$ over $\mathbb{C}$ can be realized over a finite extension of $K$ (namely, the extension generated by the coefficients of a corresponding matrix representation). Hence there exists a finite extension $L$ of $K$ such that $R_L(G) = R(G)$. Let $d = [L : K]$ be the degree of this extension. The proposition then follows from Lemma 12, which states that $d \cdot \overline{R}_K(G) \subset R_K(G)$.

Since $R_K(G)$ and $\overline{R}_K(G)$ are finitely generated free $\mathbb{Z}$-modules (by Proposition 32 and the analogous statement for $\overline{R}_K(G)$ in Proposition 35), the chain of inclusions

$$d \cdot \overline{R}_K(G) \subset R_K(G) \subset \overline{R}_K(G)$$

implies that $R_K(G)$ has finite index in $\overline{R}_K(G)$.
