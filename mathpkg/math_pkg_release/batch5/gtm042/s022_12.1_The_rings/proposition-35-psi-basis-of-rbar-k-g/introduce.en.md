Proposition 35 provides the precise structure of $\overline{R}_K(G)$ using the theory of semisimple algebras. The characters $\psi_i = \chi_i/m_i$ (where $m_i$ is the Schur index) form a $\mathbb{Z}$-basis. This refines Proposition 34: the index $[\overline{R}_K(G) : R_K(G)]$ is exactly $\prod_i m_i$, and the Schur indices measure the obstruction for a $K$-valued virtual character to come from an actual $K$-representation.
