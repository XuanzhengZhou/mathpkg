Let $K$ be a field of characteristic zero, $G$ a finite group, and $V_i$ an irreducible linear representation of $G$ over $K$. Decompose $K[G] = \prod_i A_i$ into simple $K$-algebras. Let $D_i = \operatorname{Hom}^G(V_i, V_i)$ be the commuting division algebra and $K_i = Z(D_i)$ its center. For each $K$-embedding $\sigma: K_i \hookrightarrow \mathbb{C}$, we have

$$D_i \otimes_{K_i,\sigma} \mathbb{C} \cong M_{m_i}(\mathbb{C})$$

for an integer $m_i$. The **Schur index** of $V_i$ is $m_i$. The corresponding complex irreducible representation has degree $n_i m_i$, and the $K$-character decomposes as $\chi_i = m_i \sum_{\sigma \in \Sigma_i} \psi_{i,\sigma}$ where the $\psi_{i,\sigma}$ are Galois-conjugate irreducible complex characters.
