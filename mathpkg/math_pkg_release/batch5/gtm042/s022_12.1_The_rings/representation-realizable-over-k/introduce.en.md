A complex representation of a finite group $G$ is realizable over a subfield $K \subset \mathbb{C}$ if it comes from a $K$-representation by extending scalars. Concretely, this means the representation can be given by matrices with entries in $K$. This notion is fundamental to rationality questions: which representations require the full complex numbers, and which can be defined over smaller fields such as $\mathbb{Q}$ or cyclotomic extensions?
