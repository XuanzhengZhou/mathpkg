Let $K$ be a field of characteristic zero and $\mathbb{C}$ an algebraic closure of $K$. A linear representation of $G$ over $\mathbb{C}$ is said to be **realizable over $K$** (or **rational over $K$**) if it is isomorphic to a representation $\rho_{\mathbb{C}}$ obtained from a $K$-representation $\rho: G \to \operatorname{GL}(V)$ by extending scalars:

$$\rho_{\mathbb{C}}: G \to \operatorname{GL}(V_{\mathbb{C}}), \quad V_{\mathbb{C}} = \mathbb{C} \otimes_K V.$$

This amounts to saying that the representation can be realized by matrices having coefficients in $K$.
