First, let $V$ be a linear representation of $G$ over $L$ with character $\chi$. By restricting scalars from $L$ to $K$, we can consider $V$ as a $K$-vector space (of dimension $d$ times as large, where $d = [L:K]$) and even as a linear representation of $G$ over $K$. We see immediately that the character of this representation is equal to $\operatorname{Tr}_{L/K}(\chi)$, where $\operatorname{Tr}_{L/K}$ denotes the trace associated with the extension $L/K$.

It follows by linearity that $\operatorname{Tr}_{L/K}(\chi) \in R_K(G)$ for each element $\chi$ of $R_L(G)$.

In particular, take $\chi \in \overline{R}_K(G)$, i.e., suppose that the values of $\chi$ belong to $K$. Then $\operatorname{Tr}_{L/K}(\chi) = d \cdot \chi$, since the field trace of an element already in $K$ is $d$ times that element. Hence $d \cdot \chi \in R_K(G)$, and the proof is complete.
