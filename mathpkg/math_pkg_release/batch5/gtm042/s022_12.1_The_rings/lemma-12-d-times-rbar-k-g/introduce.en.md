Lemma 12 is the key technical step in proving that $R_K(G)$ has finite index in $\overline{R}_K(G)$. Given a finite extension $L/K$, any $L$-representation can be viewed as a $K$-representation by scalar restriction, and its $K$-character is obtained by applying the field trace $\operatorname{Tr}_{L/K}$ to the original $L$-character. When the original character already takes values in $K$, the trace simply multiplies by the degree $d = [L:K]$, yielding the inclusion $d \cdot \overline{R}_K(G) \subset R_K(G)$.
