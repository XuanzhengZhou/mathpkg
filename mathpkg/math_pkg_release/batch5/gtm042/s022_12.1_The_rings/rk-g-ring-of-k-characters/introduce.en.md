$R_K(G)$ is the ring (or additive group) generated by the characters of all finite-dimensional linear representations of a finite group $G$ over a field $K$ of characteristic zero. It is a subring of the classical complex representation ring $R(G) = R_{\mathbb{C}}(G)$. The study of $R_K(G)$ leads to rationality questions: which complex representations can be realized over the smaller field $K$, and how the ring $R_K(G)$ sits inside $R(G)$.
