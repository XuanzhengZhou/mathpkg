Let $K$ be a field of characteristic zero and $G$ a finite group. We denote by $R_K(G)$ the group generated by the characters of the linear representations of $G$ over $K$; it is a subring of the ring $R(G) = R_{\mathbb{C}}(G)$. Equivalently, $R_K(G)$ may be defined as the Grothendieck group of the category of $K[G]$-modules of finite type.
