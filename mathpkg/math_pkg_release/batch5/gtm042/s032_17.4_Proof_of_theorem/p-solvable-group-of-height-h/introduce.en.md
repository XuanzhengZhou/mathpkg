A finite group $G$ is $p$-solvable of height $h$ if it admits a normal series of length $h$ whose successive quotients are each either a $p$-group or a group of order prime to $p$. This definition provides the inductive framework for the Fong–Swan theorem (Theorem 38): the proof proceeds by induction on the $p$-height $h$, with the base case $h = 0$ corresponding to the trivial group. Groups of $p$-height 1 are precisely those that are either $p$-groups or groups of order prime to $p$.
