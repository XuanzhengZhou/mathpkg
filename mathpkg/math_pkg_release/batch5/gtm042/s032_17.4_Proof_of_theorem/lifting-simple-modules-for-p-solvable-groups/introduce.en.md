Theorem 38 (the Fong–Swan theorem) asserts that for a $p$-solvable group $G$, every simple $k[G]$-module in characteristic $p$ can be lifted to a projective $A[G]$-module over the valuation ring $A$, provided the coefficient field $K$ is sufficiently large. The proof proceeds by double induction on the $p$-height $h$ of $G$ and on the group order, using Clifford theory to relate the module to a normal subgroup $I$ of order prime to $p$, constructing a central extension $G_1$ by $A^*$, and reducing to a quotient of smaller $p$-height. This result is the modular analogue of the fact that ordinary irreducible characters of $p$-solvable groups lift Brauer characters.
