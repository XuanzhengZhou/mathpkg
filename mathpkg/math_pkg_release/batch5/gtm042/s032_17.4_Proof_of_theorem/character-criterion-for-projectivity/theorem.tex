Let $G$ be a finite group, let $p$ be a prime, and let $K$ be a complete discrete valuation ring with residue field $k$ of characteristic $p$. Let $K'$ be a finite sufficiently large extension of $K$, with residue field $k'$ and valuation ring $A'$. Let $y \in R_{k'}(G)$ be an element of the Grothendieck group, and let $\chi$ be the corresponding virtual character. Then:

(i) If $y$ belongs to the image of the Cartan map $c': P_{A'}(G) \to R_{k'}(G)$ (i.e., $y \in P_{A'}(G)$), then $\chi(s) = 0$ for every $p$-singular element $s \in G$.

(ii) Conversely, if $\chi(s) = 0$ for every $p$-singular element $s \in G$, then $y \in P_{A'}(G)$.

(iii) Moreover, if $\chi$ has values in $K$ (not merely in $K'$), then $y$ belongs to $P_A(G)$.
