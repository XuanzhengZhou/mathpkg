The Artin–Brauer theorem (Theorem 35) bounds the obstruction to lifting projective modules to ordinary representations. It states that the cokernel of the Cartan map $c: P_k(G) \to R_k(G)$, which sends a projective $k[G]$-module to its class in the Grothendieck group, is annihilated by $p^n$, the largest $p$-power dividing $|G|$. The proof splits into two cases: when the coefficient field $K$ is sufficiently large, one reduces to elementary subgroups where the Cartan matrix is the scalar matrix $p^n$; the general case follows by scalar extension to a sufficiently large field.
