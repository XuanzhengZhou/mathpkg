Suppose that $G$ acts on a finite set $X$, so that for each $s \in G$ there is a permutation $x \mapsto sx$ of $X$ satisfying $1x = x$ and $s(tx) = (st)x$. Let $V$ be a vector space with a basis $(e_x)_{x \in X}$ indexed by $X$. For $s \in G$, let $\rho_s$ be the linear map of $V$ into $V$ which sends $e_x$ to $e_{sx}$. The linear representation of $G$ thus obtained is called the \textbf{permutation representation} associated with $X$.
