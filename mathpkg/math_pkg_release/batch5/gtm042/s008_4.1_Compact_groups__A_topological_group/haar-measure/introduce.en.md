The Haar measure, also called the invariant measure, is the unique probability measure on a compact group that is invariant under both left and right translations. It generalizes the normalized counting measure on a finite group (each element has mass $1/g$) and the normalized angular measure $(1/2\pi)\,d\alpha$ on the circle group. The existence of the Haar measure is the crucial tool that allows the theory of linear representations to extend from finite groups to arbitrary compact groups, with integrals replacing finite sums.
