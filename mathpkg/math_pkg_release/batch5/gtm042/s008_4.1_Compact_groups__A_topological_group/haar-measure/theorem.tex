The measure $dt$ on a compact group $G$ satisfying

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $\displaystyle\int_G f(t)\,dt = \int_G f(ts)\,dt$ for each continuous function $f$ and each $s \in G$ (invariance under right translation),
\item[(ii)] $\displaystyle\int_G dt = 1$ (total mass equals $1$),
\end{itemize}

and also

\[
\text{(i')}\qquad \int_G f(t)\,dt = \int_G f(st)\,dt
\]

(invariance under left translation) is called the \text{invariant measure} (or \text{Haar measure}) of the group $G$.
