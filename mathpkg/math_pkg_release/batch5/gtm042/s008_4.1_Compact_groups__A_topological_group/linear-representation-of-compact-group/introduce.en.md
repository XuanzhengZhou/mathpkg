A linear representation of a compact group $G$ on a finite-dimensional complex vector space $V$ is a continuous group homomorphism $\rho: G \to \operatorname{GL}(V)$. The continuity condition means that the action $(s, x) \mapsto \rho_s x$ is jointly continuous in both variables. This definition extends to Hilbert space representations, and a fundamental result (the Peter--Weyl theorem) shows that every such representation decomposes as a Hilbert direct sum of finite-dimensional unitary representations. Consequently, the study of representations of compact groups reduces to the finite-dimensional unitary case, where most results from finite group representation theory carry over with the Haar measure replacing finite sums.
