On any compact group $G$, there exists a unique probability measure $dt$, called the Haar measure, that is invariant under right translations. This means that for every continuous function $f$ on $G$ and every group element $s \in G$, the integral of $f(t)$ equals the integral of $f(ts)$. The measure is normalized so that the total mass of the group is $1$. Moreover, the Haar measure is also left-invariant. This theorem generalizes the finite-group averaging operation $(1/g)\sum_{t\in G} f(t)$ to the compact setting, replacing the finite sum with an integral. The existence and uniqueness of the Haar measure is a cornerstone of harmonic analysis on compact groups and is essential for extending representation theory from finite to compact groups.
