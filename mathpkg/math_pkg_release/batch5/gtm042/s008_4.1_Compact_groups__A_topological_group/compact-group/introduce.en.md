A compact group is a topological group whose underlying topological space is compact, meaning it satisfies the Borel--Lebesgue covering property (every open cover has a finite subcover). Compact groups generalize finite groups: a finite group with the discrete topology is a compact group. Important examples include the group of rotations in Euclidean space and its closed subgroups. In contrast, the translation group and the Lorentz group are noncompact. The theory of linear representations extends naturally from finite groups to compact groups, with the Haar measure replacing the averaging operation over the finite group.
