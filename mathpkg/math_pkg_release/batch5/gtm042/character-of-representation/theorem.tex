Let $\rho: G \to \text{GL}(V)$ be a linear representation of a finite group $G$ in the vector space $V$. For each $s \in G$, the \textbf{character} of $\rho$ is the complex-valued function $\chi_\rho$ on $G$ defined by:

$$\chi_\rho(s) = \text{Tr}(\rho_s).$$

The character is the sum of the eigenvalues of $\rho_s$ (counted with multiplicities) and does not depend on the choice of basis.
