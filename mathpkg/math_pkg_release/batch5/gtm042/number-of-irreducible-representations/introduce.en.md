This fundamental corollary states that a finite group has exactly as many irreducible representations as it has conjugacy classes. This follows because the dimension of the space of class functions equals $k$ (the number of classes), and by Theorem 6, the $h$ irreducible characters form a basis, so $h = k$. This is an essential tool for classifying all representations of a group.
