Theorem 1, often called Maschke's Theorem in the context of complex representations, states that every subrepresentation of a finite group admits a stable complement. This is the foundational result establishing complete reducibility: every finite-dimensional complex representation of a finite group decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible representations. The proof uses an averaging argument over the group to construct a $G$-invariant projection onto the complement, exploiting that $|G|$ is invertible in $\mathbb{C}$.
