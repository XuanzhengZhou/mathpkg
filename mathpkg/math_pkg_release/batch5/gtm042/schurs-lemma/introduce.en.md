Schur's Lemma is one of the most important results in representation theory. It states that an intertwining operator between irreducible representations is either zero (if the representations are non-isomorphic) or a scalar multiple of the identity (if they are the same). This simple result has profound consequences, including the orthogonality relations for characters and the commutativity of the endomorphism algebra of an irreducible representation.
