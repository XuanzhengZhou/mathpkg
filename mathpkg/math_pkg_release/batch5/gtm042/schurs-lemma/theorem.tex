Let $\rho^1: G \to \text{GL}(V_1)$ and $\rho^2: G \to \text{GL}(V_2)$ be two irreducible representations of $G$, and let $f$ be a linear mapping of $V_1$ into $V_2$ such that $\rho_s^2 \circ f = f \circ \rho_s^1$ for all $s \in G$. Then:

(1) If $\rho^1$ and $\rho^2$ are not isomorphic, we have $f = 0$.

(2) If $V_1 = V_2$ and $\rho^1 = \rho^2$, then $f$ is a homothety (i.e., a scalar multiple of the identity).
