The homomorphism $e: P_k(G) \to R_K(G)$ is a key component of the cde triangle in modular representation theory. It is constructed by composing the tensor-product-with-$K$ functor (from $P_A(G)$ to $R_K(G)$) with the inverse of the canonical isomorphism $P_A(G) \to P_k(G)$. Intuitively, $e$ sends the class of a projective $k[G]$-module (or equivalently an $A[G]$-module that is $A$-free) to its lift in characteristic zero, viewed as a virtual representation over $K$. The matrix $E$ of $e$ has dimensions $|S_K| \times |S_k|$, where $S_K$ and $S_k$ index the simple modules over $K$ and $k$ respectively.
