Assume that $K$ is sufficiently large, and let $C_{ST}$ (resp. $C_{TS}$) be the multiplicity of $S$ (resp. $T$) in a composition series of $P_T$ (resp. $P_S$), cf. 15.1.

(a) Show that $C_{ST} d_S = \dim \operatorname{Hom}^G(P_S, P_T)$.

(b) Show that $C_{ST} d_S = C_{TS} d_T$ [apply ex. 14.5]. When $K$ is sufficiently large, the $d_S$ are equal to 1, and we obtain again the fact that the matrix $C = (C_{ST})$ is symmetric.
