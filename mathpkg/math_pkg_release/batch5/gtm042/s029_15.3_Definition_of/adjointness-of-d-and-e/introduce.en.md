This proposition establishes that the decomposition homomorphism $d: R_K(G) \to R_k(G)$ and the homomorphism $e: P_k(G) \to R_K(G)$ are adjoint with respect to the canonical bilinear forms $\langle-,-\rangle_k$ on $P_k(G) \times R_k(G)$ and $\langle-,-\rangle_K$ on $R_K(G) \times R_K(G)$. The adjointness relation $\langle x, d(y) \rangle_k = \langle e(x), y \rangle_K$ for $x \in P_k(G), y \in R_K(G)$ is a fundamental structural property of the cde triangle. The proof uses the fact that $\operatorname{Hom}^G(X, Y)$ is a free $A$-module when $X$ is a projective $A[G]$-module and $Y$ is $A$-free, and compares ranks under extension of scalars to $K$ and $k$.
