Corollary 2 reformulates the injectivity of $e$ in concrete module-theoretic terms. It states that if two projective $A[G]$-modules become isomorphic after extending scalars to $K$ (i.e., after "lifting to characteristic zero"), then they were already isomorphic over $A$. This follows from the equality $[P] = [P']$ in $R_A(G) \simeq R_k(G)$ being equivalent to $P \simeq P'$ for projective $A[G]$-modules, combined with the injectivity of $e$ from Theorem 34.
