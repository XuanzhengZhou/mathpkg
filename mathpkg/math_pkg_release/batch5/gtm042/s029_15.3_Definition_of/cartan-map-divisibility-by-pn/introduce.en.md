Theorem 35 gives a precise description of the image of the Cartan homomorphism $c: P_k(G) \to R_k(G)$. It states that an element of $R_k(G)$ is in the image of $c$ if and only if it is divisible by $p^n$, the largest power of $p$ dividing $|G|$. Equivalently, there exists a homomorphism $c': R_k(G) \to P_k(G)$ such that $c \circ c' = p^n$ (which then implies $c' \circ c = p^n$). The exponent $n$ is best possible (cf. Exercise 16.3). The proof is given in 17.4.
