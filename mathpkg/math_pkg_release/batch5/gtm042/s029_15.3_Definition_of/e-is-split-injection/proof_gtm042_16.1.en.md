When $K$ is sufficiently large, $e$ is the transpose of $d$ (cf. 15.4), and the fact that $d$ is surjective (Theorem 33) implies that $e$ is a split injection.

In the general case, let $K'$ be a finite sufficiently large extension of $K$, and let $k'$ be its residue field. Consider the commutative diagram:

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
P_k(G) & \xrightarrow{e} & R_K(G) \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow \\
P_{k'}(G) & \xrightarrow{e'} & R_{K'}(G).
\end{array}$$

As we have just seen, $e'$ is a split injection. In view of 14.6, the same is true for $P_k(G) \to P_{k'}(G)$. Their composition is a split injection as well, hence the same holds for $e$.
