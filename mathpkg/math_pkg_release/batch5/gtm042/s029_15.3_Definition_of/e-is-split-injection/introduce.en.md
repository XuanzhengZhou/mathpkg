Theorem 34 establishes that the homomorphism $e: P_k(G) \to R_K(G)$ is a split injection. When $K$ is sufficiently large, $e$ is the transpose of $d$ with respect to the bilinear forms (by 15.4), so the surjectivity of $d$ (Theorem 33) implies that $e$ is a split injection. For the general case, one passes to a sufficiently large finite extension $K'$ of $K$ and uses the commutative diagram relating $P_k(G)$, $P_{k'}(G)$, $R_K(G)$, and $R_{K'}(G)$ together with the result from 14.6.
