Proposition 43 describes the remarkably simple structure of modular representation theory for groups whose order is prime to the characteristic $p$ (so-called $p'$-groups). In this case, every $k[G]$-module is projective, reduction mod $\mathfrak{m}$ gives a bijection between the sets of simple $K[G]$-modules and simple $k[G]$-modules, and the matrices $C$, $D$, $E$ of the cde triangle are identity matrices. This means that the representation theory over the field $k$ of characteristic $p$ is essentially identical to the representation theory over the characteristic-zero field $K$.
