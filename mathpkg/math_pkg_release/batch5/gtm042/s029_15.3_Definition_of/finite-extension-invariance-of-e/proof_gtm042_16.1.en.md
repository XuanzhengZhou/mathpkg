This is an immediate consequence of the proof of Theorem 34. In that proof, we showed that $e: P_k(G) \to R_K(G)$ is a split injection by factoring it through a sufficiently large extension $K'$ where $e'$ is a split injection. The same argument shows that for any finite extension $K'$, the composition $P_k(G) \xrightarrow{e} R_K(G) \to R_{K'}(G)$ remains a split injection, since we can always pass to a common sufficiently large extension of both $K$ and $K'$.
