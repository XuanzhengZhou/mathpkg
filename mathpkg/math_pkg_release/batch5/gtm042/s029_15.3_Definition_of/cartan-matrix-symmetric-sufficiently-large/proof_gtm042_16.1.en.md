When $K$ is sufficiently large, the bilinear form $\langle -, - \rangle_K$ on $R_K(G)$ is symmetric (cf. 14.5). Since $d$ and $e$ are adjoints with respect to the bilinear forms (15.4b), and the bilinear form on $P_k(G) \times R_k(G)$ is also symmetric in an appropriate sense, we have:

$$C = \langle c(-), - \rangle_k = \langle d(e(-)), - \rangle_k = \langle e(-), d(-) \rangle_K.$$

Since the bilinear form on $R_K(G)$ is symmetric when $K$ is sufficiently large, and $e$ is the transpose of $d$, the matrix $C = D \cdot E = D \cdot D^T$ is symmetric. More directly, from the adjointness relation and the identification of $P_k(G)$ with the dual of $R_k(G)$ when $K$ is sufficiently large, the symmetry of $C$ follows from the properties established in 15.4.
