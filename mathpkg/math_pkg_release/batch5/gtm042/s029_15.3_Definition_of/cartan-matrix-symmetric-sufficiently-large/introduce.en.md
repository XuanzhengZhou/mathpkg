Corollary 3 to Theorem 35 states that when the characteristic-zero field $K$ is sufficiently large (i.e., contains enough roots of unity so that all simple $K[G]$-modules are absolutely simple), the Cartan matrix $C$ is symmetric. This is a fundamental structural property of the Cartan matrix in modular representation theory. The symmetry can also be derived from the adjointness of $d$ and $e$ (15.4b) and the fact that $e$ is the transpose of $d$ when $K$ is sufficiently large, together with the commutativity $C = D \cdot E$.
