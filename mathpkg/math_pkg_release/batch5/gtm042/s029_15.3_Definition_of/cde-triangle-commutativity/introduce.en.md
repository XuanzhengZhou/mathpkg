The cde triangle consists of three homomorphisms relating the Grothendieck groups that appear in modular representation theory: the Cartan homomorphism $c: P_k(G) \to R_k(G)$, the decomposition homomorphism $d: R_K(G) \to R_k(G)$, and the homomorphism $e: P_k(G) \to R_K(G)$ defined in 15.3. The commutativity relation $c = d \circ e$ means that applying $e$ to lift a projective $k[G]$-module to characteristic zero and then decomposing via $d$ recovers the original Cartan map. In matrix form, this reads $C = D \cdot E$, where $C$, $D$, $E$ are the matrices of $c$, $d$, $e$ respectively.
