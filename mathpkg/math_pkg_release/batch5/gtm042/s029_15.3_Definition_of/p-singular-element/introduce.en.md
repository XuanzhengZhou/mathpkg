A $p$-singular element of a finite group $G$ is an element whose order is divisible by the prime $p$. This is the complement of the notion of $p$-regular (or $p'$-) elements, whose orders are prime to $p$. The distinction between $p$-regular and $p$-singular elements is fundamental in modular representation theory: by a theorem of Brauer, the character of a representation over a field of characteristic $p$ is determined by its values on $p$-regular elements. In Section 16.2, $p$-singular elements are used to characterize the image of the homomorphism $e$ in terms of class functions vanishing on $p$-singular elements.
