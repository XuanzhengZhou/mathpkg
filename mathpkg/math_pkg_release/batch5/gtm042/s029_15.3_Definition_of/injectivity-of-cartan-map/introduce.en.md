Corollary 1 to Theorem 35 establishes two key properties of the Cartan map $c$: it is injective, and its cokernel is a finite $p$-group. The second assertion follows immediately from Theorem 35, since every element of $R_k(G)$ becomes divisible by $p^n$ after multiplying by a sufficiently large power of $p$. The injectivity then follows because $P_k(G)$ and $R_k(G)$ are free $\mathbb{Z}$-modules of the same rank (namely $|S_k|$, the number of simple $k[G]$-modules). The injectivity of $c$ can also be seen as a consequence of the injectivity of $e$ from Theorem 34.
