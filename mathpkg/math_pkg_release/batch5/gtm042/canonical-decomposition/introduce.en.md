The canonical decomposition groups together all irreducible subrepresentations of the same isomorphism type into isotypic components $V_i$. Unlike the decomposition into irreducible summands (which is not unique), this coarser decomposition is unique and independent of choices. It is the finest decomposition that is canonically determined by the representation.
