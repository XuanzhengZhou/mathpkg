The restriction homomorphism $\text{Res}_H^G$ maps representations (or projective modules) of a group $G$ to those of a subgroup $H$ by simply viewing a $k[G]$-module as a $k[H]$-module via the inclusion $H \hookrightarrow G$. This definition, already established for the representation ring $R_K(G)$, extends naturally to the Grothendieck groups $R_k(G)$ and $P_k(G)$ of finitely generated $k[G]$-modules and projective $k[G]$-modules respectively. The key property is that projectivity is preserved under restriction, so the homomorphism is well-defined on $P_k(G)$ as well.
