Theorem 40 provides a modular analogue of Artin's theorem (cf. Theorem 26). After tensoring with the rational numbers $Q$, every element of $Q \otimes R_k(G)$ or $Q \otimes P_k(G)$ can be expressed as a rational linear combination of characters induced from cyclic subgroups. The proof uses the same argument as Theorem 39, relying on the fact that the argument in the proof of Theorem 39 applies to many other situations (cf. Swan [21], Sections 3-4).
