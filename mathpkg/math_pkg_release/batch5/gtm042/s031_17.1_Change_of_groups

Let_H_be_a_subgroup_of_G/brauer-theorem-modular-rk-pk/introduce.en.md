Theorem 39 extends Brauer's classical theorem (Theorem 27) to the modular setting. It states that the induction map from the direct sum of the Grothendieck groups $R_k(H)$ (respectively $P_k(H)$) over all $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups $H$ of $G$ is surjective onto $R_k(G)$ (respectively $P_k(G)$). In other words, every element of $R_k(G)$ or $P_k(G)$ can be expressed as a sum of induced characters from $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups. The proof uses Theorem 27 together with the fact that the map $d: R_K(G) \to R_k(G)$ commutes with induction.
