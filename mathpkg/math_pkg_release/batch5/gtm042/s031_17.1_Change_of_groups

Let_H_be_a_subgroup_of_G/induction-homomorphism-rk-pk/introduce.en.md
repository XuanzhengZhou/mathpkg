The induction homomorphism $\text{Ind}_H^G$ constructs a $k[G]$-module from a $k[H]$-module $E$ via the tensor product $\text{Ind}\,E = k[G] \otimes_{k[H]} E$. When $E$ is projective over $k[H]$, the induced module is projective over $k[G]$, so induction descends to well-defined homomorphisms on the Grothendieck groups $R_k(H) \to R_k(G)$ and $P_k(H) \to P_k(G)$. This is the modular analogue of the classical induction map on $R_K(G)$, obtained by the same algebraic construction.
