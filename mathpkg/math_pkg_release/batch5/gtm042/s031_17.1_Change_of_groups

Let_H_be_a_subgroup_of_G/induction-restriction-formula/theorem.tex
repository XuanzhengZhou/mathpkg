$$\text{Ind}_H^G(x \cdot \text{Res}_H^G y) = \text{Ind}_H^G(x) \cdot y$$

holds in each of the following situations:

(a) $x \in R_K(H), y \in R_K(G)$ and $\text{Ind}_H^G(x) \cdot y \in R_K(G)$,

(b) $x \in R_k(H), y \in R_k(G)$ and $\text{Ind}_H^G(x) \cdot y \in R_k(G)$,

(c) $x \in R_k(H), y \in P_k(G)$ and $\text{Ind}_H^G(x) \cdot y \in P_k(G).$
