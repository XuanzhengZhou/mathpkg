The induction-restriction formula (also known as the projection formula) states that $\text{Ind}_H^G(x \cdot \text{Res}_H^G y) = \text{Ind}_H^G(x) \cdot y$. This identity relates the induction and restriction homomorphisms on Grothendieck groups and holds in three contexts: for the ordinary representation ring $R_K$, for the modular representation Grothendieck group $R_k$, and for the projective Grothendieck group $P_k$ (where $P_k(G)$ is viewed as an $R_k(G)$-module). The proof follows from associativity of the tensor product.
