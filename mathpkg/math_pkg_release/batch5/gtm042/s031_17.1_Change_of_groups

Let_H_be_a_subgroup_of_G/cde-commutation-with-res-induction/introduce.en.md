The $c$, $d$, $e$ homomorphisms introduced in Chapter 15 (relating $R_K(G)$, $R_k(G)$, and $P_k(G)$) are natural with respect to change of groups: they commute with both restriction $\text{Res}_H^G$ and induction $\text{Ind}_H^G$. This functoriality ensures that the entire diagram of Grothendieck groups and cde-maps is compatible with subgroup operations, which is essential for the proofs of Brauer's theorem in the modular case and the surjectivity of $d$.
