Theorem 41 is a key step in proving that the decomposition map $d: R_K(G) \to R_k(G)$ is surjective (Theorem 33). It states that for a $\Gamma_K$-elementary group $G$ -- a semidirect product of an $l$-group $P$ by a cyclic normal subgroup $C$ of order prime to $l$ -- every simple $k[G]$-module lifts to characteristic zero. The proof proceeds by induction on $|G|$, using Clifford theory and the fact that $p$-groups have non-trivial fixed points.
