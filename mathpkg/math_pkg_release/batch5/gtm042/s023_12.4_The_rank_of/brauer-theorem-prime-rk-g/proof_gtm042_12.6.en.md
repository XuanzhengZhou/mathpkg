The proof of this theorem is completely analogous to that of Theorem 18' (Section 10.5), to which it reduces when $K$ is algebraically closed (i.e., when $\Gamma_K = \{1\}$). The argument is given in the next section (12.7).

In outline, one constructs for each $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary subgroup $H = C \cdot P$ (where $C$ is cyclic of order prime to $p$ and $P$ is a $p$-group) a suitable character $\theta_H \in R_K(H)$ such that

$$l = \sum_{H \in X_K(p)} \operatorname{Ind}_H^G(\theta_H).$$

The existence of such $\theta_H$ relies on the compatibility condition in the definition of $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary subgroups: for each $t \in \Gamma_K$, the automorphism $c \mapsto c^t$ of $C$ lies in the subgroup generated by the Frobenius $c \mapsto c^p$. This ensures that the characters constructed over the algebraic closure actually descend to characters over $K$.
