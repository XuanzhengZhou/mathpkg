This theorem is the key $p$-primary ingredient in the proof of Brauer's induction theorem for $R_K(G)$. For a fixed prime $p$, it shows that the constant function $l$ (the $p'$-part of the group order $g = p^n l$) can be expressed as a sum of characters induced from $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary subgroups. This implies that the submodule $V_{K,p}$ generated by such induced characters has index coprime to $p$ in $R_K(G)$. Taking all primes $p$ dividing $g$ together yields the full Brauer theorem (Theorem 27). The proof is analogous to that of Theorem 18' in the classical (algebraically closed) case.
