This proposition provides a practical criterion for testing whether a class function on $G$ comes from a virtual representation over $K$: one only needs to check its restrictions to $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups. This is a direct consequence of Brauer's induction theorem (Theorem 27), which expresses the constant function $1$ as a sum of characters induced from $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups. Multiplying by $\varphi$ and using Frobenius reciprocity then reduces the question to checking the restrictions. The criterion is useful both theoretically and computationally.
