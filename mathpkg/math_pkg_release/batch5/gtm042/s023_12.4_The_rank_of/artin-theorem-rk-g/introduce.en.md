Artin's classical theorem states that every character of a finite group is a rational linear combination of characters induced from cyclic subgroups. Theorem 26 extends this result to representations over an arbitrary field $K$ of characteristic zero, asserting that the analogous map $\mathbb{Q} \otimes \operatorname{Ind}$ from cyclic subgroups to $G$ remains surjective when $R(G)$ is replaced by $R_K(G)$. The proof carries over without essential change: one either uses a duality argument (showing injectivity of the restriction map) or the explicit formula $g = \sum_{H \in T} \operatorname{Ind}_H^G(\theta_H)$ with $\theta_H \in R_K(H)$.
