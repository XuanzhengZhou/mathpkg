This corollary gives a concrete criterion for determining when a $K$-valued class function on $G$ can be expressed as a $K$-linear combination of characters of representations over $K$. The condition is that the function must be constant on $\Gamma_K$-classes, which are the equivalence classes under the combined action of ordinary conjugacy and the $\Gamma_K$-exponentiation $s \mapsto s^t$. This generalizes the well-known fact for algebraically closed fields, where class functions in $R(G) \otimes K$ are precisely those constant on ordinary conjugacy classes.
