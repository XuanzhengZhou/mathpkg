This theorem determines the rank of the Grothendieck group $R_K(G)$ of representations of a finite group $G$ over an arbitrary field $K$ of characteristic zero. The key insight is that the number of irreducible representations over $K$ equals not the number of ordinary conjugacy classes, but the number of $\Gamma_K$-classes, which incorporate the Galois action of the field $K$. When $K$ is algebraically closed ($\Gamma_K = \{1\}$), $\Gamma_K$-classes reduce to ordinary conjugacy classes, recovering the classical result. For arbitrary $K$, the $\Gamma_K$-invariant class functions with values in $K$ are precisely those expressible as $K$-linear combinations of irreducible characters over $K$.
