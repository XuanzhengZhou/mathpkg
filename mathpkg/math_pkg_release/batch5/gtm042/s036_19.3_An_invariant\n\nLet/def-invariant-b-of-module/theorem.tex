Let $l$ be a prime number unequal to the residual characteristic of $E$. Put $k = \mathbb{Z}/l\mathbb{Z}$ and let $M$ be a $k[G]$-module. The invariant $b(M)$ of $M$ is defined by the formula

$$b(M) = \langle \overline{Sw_G}, M \rangle_k = \dim \operatorname{Hom}_{k[G]}(\overline{Sw_G}, M) = \dim \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}_l[G]}(Sw_G, M),$$

where $\overline{Sw_G} = Sw_G/l \cdot Sw_G$ denotes the reduction mod $l$ of the $\mathbb{Z}_l[G]$-module $Sw_G$ defined by Theorem 44. The scalar product $\langle \overline{Sw_G}, M \rangle_k$ makes sense since $\overline{Sw_G}$ is projective (cf. 14.5).
