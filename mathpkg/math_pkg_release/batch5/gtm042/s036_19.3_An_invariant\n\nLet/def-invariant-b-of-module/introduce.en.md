The invariant $b(M)$ is a numerical invariant associated to any $k[G]$-module $M$, where $k = \mathbb{Z}/l\mathbb{Z}$ for a prime $l$ distinct from the residual characteristic of the ground field $E$. It is defined via the scalar product with the reduction modulo $l$ of the Swan representation $Sw_G$. This invariant plays a role analogous to the Artin conductor in the modular representation theory of finite groups, capturing information about wild ramification.
