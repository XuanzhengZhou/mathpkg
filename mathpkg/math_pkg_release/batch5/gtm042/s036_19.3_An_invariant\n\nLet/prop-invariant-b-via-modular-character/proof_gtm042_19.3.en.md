This follows from the relationship between the Swan representation and the modular character. By definition, $b(M) = \langle \overline{Sw_G}, M \rangle_k$. Since $\overline{Sw_G}$ is the reduction modulo $l$ of the $\mathbb{Z}_l[G]$-module $Sw_G$, and using the compatibility between the scalar products in characteristic $0$ and characteristic $l$ (cf. 18.1, formula (viii)), we obtain

$$b(M) = \langle sw_G, \phi_M \rangle = \frac{1}{g} \sum_{s \in G_{\text{reg}}} sw_G(s^{-1}) \phi_M(s).$$
