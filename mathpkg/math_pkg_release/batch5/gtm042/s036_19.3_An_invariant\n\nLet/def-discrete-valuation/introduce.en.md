A discrete valuation on a field $K$ is a surjective group homomorphism from the multiplicative group $K^*$ to the integers, satisfying the ultrametric inequality $v(x+y) \geq \min(v(x), v(y))$. Discrete valuations are the algebraic foundation of local field theory, providing the link between number theory and the representation theory of Galois groups through ramification.
