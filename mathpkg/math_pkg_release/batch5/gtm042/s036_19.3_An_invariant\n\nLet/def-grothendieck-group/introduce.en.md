The Grothendieck group $K(\mathcal{F})$ of a category $\mathcal{F}$ of modules is the universal abelian group that encodes the additive structure of short exact sequences. Each module $E$ contributes a generator $[E]$, and every short exact sequence $0 \to E' \to E \to E'' \to 0$ imposes the relation $[E] = [E'] + [E'']$. This construction is fundamental in $K$-theory and representation theory.
