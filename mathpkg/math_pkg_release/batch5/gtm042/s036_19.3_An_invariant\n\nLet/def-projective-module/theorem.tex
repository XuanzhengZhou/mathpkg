Let $A$ be a ring and $P$ be a left $A$-module. $P$ is said to be \emph{projective} if it satisfies the following equivalent conditions (cf. Bourbaki, Alg., Ch. II, \S 2):

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] There exists a free $A$-module of which $P$ is a direct factor.
\item[(b)] For every surjective homomorphism $f: E \to E''$ of left $A$-modules, and for every homomorphism $g': P \to E''$, there exists a homomorphism $g: P \to E$ such that $g' = f \circ g$.
\item[(c)] The functor $E \mapsto \operatorname{Hom}_A(P, E)$ is exact.
\end{enumerate}
