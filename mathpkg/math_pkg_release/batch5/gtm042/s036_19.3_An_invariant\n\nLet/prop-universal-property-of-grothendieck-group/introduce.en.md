The Grothendieck group $K(\mathcal{F})$ satisfies a universal property: any additive map from the category $\mathcal{F}$ to an abelian group $H$ (i.e., a map compatible with short exact sequences) factors uniquely through the canonical map $E \mapsto [E]$. This characterizes $K(\mathcal{F})$ as the universal target for additive invariants on $\mathcal{F}$.
