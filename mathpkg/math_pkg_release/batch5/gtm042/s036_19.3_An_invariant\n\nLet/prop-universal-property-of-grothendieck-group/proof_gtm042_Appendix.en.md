Given a homomorphism $f: K(\mathcal{F}) \to H$, the composition $\phi(E) = f([E])$ is additive because $[E] = [E'] + [E'']$ in $K(\mathcal{F})$ for each exact sequence, and $f$ is a group homomorphism. Conversely, given an additive map $\phi: \mathcal{F} \to H$, define $f([E]) = \phi(E)$ on generators. Additivity of $\phi$ ensures that the relations $[E] = [E'] + [E'']$ are respected, so $f$ extends uniquely to a well-defined homomorphism on $K(\mathcal{F})$.
