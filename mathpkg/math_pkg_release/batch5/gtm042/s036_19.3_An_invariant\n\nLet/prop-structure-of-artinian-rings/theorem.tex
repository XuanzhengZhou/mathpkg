If $A$ is artinian, its radical $\mathfrak{r}$ is nilpotent, and the ring $S = A/\mathfrak{r}$ is semisimple. The ring $S$ can be decomposed as a product $\prod S_i$ of simple rings; each $S_i$ is isomorphic to a matrix algebra $M_{n_i}(D_i)$ over a (skew) field $D_i$, and possesses a unique simple module $E_i$, which is a $D_i$-vector space of dimension $n_i$. Every semisimple $A$-module is annihilated by $\mathfrak{r}$ and thus may be viewed as an $S$-module; if the module is simple, it is isomorphic to one of the $E_i$.
