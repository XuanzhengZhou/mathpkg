See Bourbaki, Alg. Ch. VIII, \S 2 for a complete treatment. The key steps are: (1) In an artinian ring, the Jacobson radical $\mathfrak{r}$ is the intersection of all maximal left ideals and is the largest nilpotent ideal; nilpotence follows from the descending chain condition. (2) The quotient $S = A/\mathfrak{r}$ has radical zero, hence is semisimple. (3) By the Wedderburn-Artin structure theory, a semisimple ring is a finite product of simple artinian rings. (4) Each simple artinian ring is isomorphic to $M_{n_i}(D_i)$ for some division ring $D_i$, with a unique simple module $E_i \cong D_i^{n_i}$ up to isomorphism. (5) Since $\mathfrak{r}$ annihilates every semisimple $A$-module, such modules lift to $S$-modules, completing the classification.
