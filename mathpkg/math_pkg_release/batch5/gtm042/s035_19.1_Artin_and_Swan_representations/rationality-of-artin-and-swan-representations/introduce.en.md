Theorem 44 establishes that although Artin and Swan representations are not always realizable over $\mathbb{Q}$ or $\mathbb{R}$, they are realizable over the $l$-adic numbers $\mathbb{Q}_l$ for any prime $l$ different from the residue characteristic. Moreover, there exists a projective $\mathbb{Z}_l[G]$-module $\mathrm{Sw}_G$, unique up to isomorphism, whose extension of scalars to $\mathbb{Q}_l$ yields the Swan representation. The Artin case follows since $a_G = sw_G + u_G$ and $u_G$ is realizable over any field.
