The Cartan homomorphism $c: P_k(G) \to R_k(G)$ is a fundamental map in modular representation theory of finite groups. It sends each projective $k[G]$-module $P$ to its class in the Grothendieck group $R_k(G)$ of finitely generated $k[G]$-modules. Since the class $[P]$ in $R_k(G)$ is an additive function of $P$, this assignment induces a well-defined group homomorphism from the Grothendieck group $P_k(G)$ of projective $k[G]$-modules to $R_k(G)$. The Cartan homomorphism encodes how projective modules are built from simple modules via composition series.
