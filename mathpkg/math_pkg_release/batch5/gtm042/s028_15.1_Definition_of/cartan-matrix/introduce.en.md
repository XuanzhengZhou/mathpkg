The Cartan matrix $C$ is the matrix representation of the Cartan homomorphism $c: P_k(G) \to R_k(G)$ with respect to the canonical bases $[P_S]$ and $[S]$ of the Grothendieck groups $P_k(G)$ and $R_k(G)$, respectively. It is a square matrix of size $|S_k| \times |S_k|$ with nonnegative integer entries. Each entry $C_{ST}$ counts the multiplicity of the simple $k[G]$-module $S$ in a composition series of the projective envelope $P_T$ of $T$, so that $[P_T] = \sum_{S \in S_k} C_{ST}[S]$ holds in $R_k(G)$.
