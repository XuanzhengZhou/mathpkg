When a $K[G]$-module with ordinary character $\chi$ is reduced modulo $\mathfrak{m}$ (by choosing a $G$-stable $A$-lattice), the resulting modular character equals the restriction of $\chi$ to the $p$-regular elements $G_{\text{reg}}$. This is the fundamental link between ordinary and modular representation theory: the Brauer character is "what remains" of the ordinary character after reduction modulo $p$. Theorem 32 guarantees independence from the choice of stable lattice.
