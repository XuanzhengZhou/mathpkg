The set $G_{\text{reg}}$ of $p$-regular elements of a finite group $G$ consists of all group elements whose order is coprime to the prime $p$. This set is the natural domain for defining modular (Brauer) characters in characteristic $p$. Since $p'$-elements are diagonalizable in representations over a field of characteristic $p$, their eigenvalues belong to the group of $m'$-th roots of unity, which lifts isomorphically from the residue field $k$ to the valuation ring $K$ via reduction modulo the maximal ideal.
