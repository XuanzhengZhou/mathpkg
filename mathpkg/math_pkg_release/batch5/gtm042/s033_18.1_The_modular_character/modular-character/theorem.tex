Let $G$ be a finite group and let $p$ be a prime number (the characteristic of the residue field $k$). Let $K$ be a sufficiently large field of characteristic $0$ with a discrete valuation, with valuation ring $A$, maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$, and residue field $k = A/\mathfrak{m}$ such that $K$ contains the group $\mu_K$ of $m'$-th roots of unity, where $m'$ is the l.c.m. of the orders of the $p$-regular elements of $G$.

Let $E$ be a $k[G]$-module of dimension $n$, let $s \in G_{\text{reg}}$, and let $s_E$ be the endomorphism of $E$ defined by $s$. Since the order of $s$ is prime to $p$, $s_E$ is diagonalizable, and its eigenvalues $(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n)$ belong to the group $\mu_k$ of $m'$-th roots of unity of $k$. Let $\tilde{\lambda}_i$ denote the element of $\mu_K$ whose reduction modulo $\mathfrak{m}$ is $\lambda_i$.

The \textbf{modular character} (or \textbf{Brauer character}) of $E$ is the function $\phi_E: G_{\text{reg}} \to A$ defined by
$$\phi_E(s) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \tilde{\lambda}_i.$$
