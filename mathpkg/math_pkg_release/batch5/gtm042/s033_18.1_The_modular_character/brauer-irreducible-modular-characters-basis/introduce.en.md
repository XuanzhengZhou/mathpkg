Brauer's Theorem 42 is a cornerstone of modular representation theory. It states that the irreducible Brauer characters $\phi_E$ (for simple $k[G]$-modules $E$) form a basis of the $K$-vector space of class functions on the $p$-regular elements $G_{\text{reg}}$. This is the modular analogue of the fact that irreducible ordinary characters form an orthonormal basis for class functions on $G$. The proof uses the density theorem: the map $k[G] \to \bigoplus_{E \in S_k} \operatorname{End}_k(E)$ is surjective (since $K$ is sufficiently large), which forces linear independence of the $\phi_E$ after reduction modulo $\mathfrak{m}$.
