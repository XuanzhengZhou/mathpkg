The homomorphism $e: \mathbb{P}_k(\mathbb{G}) \to \mathbb{R}_K(\mathbb{G})$ is a split injection.

When $K$ is sufficiently large, $e$ is the transpose of $d$ (cf. 15.4), and the fact that $d$ is surjective implies that $e$ is a split injection. In the general case, let $K'$ be a finite sufficiently large extension of $K$, and let $k'$ be its residue field. Consider the diagram:

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
\mathbb{P}_k(\mathbb{G}) & \xrightarrow{e} & \mathbb{R}_K(\mathbb{G}) \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow \\
\mathbb{P}_{k'}(\mathbb{G}) & \xrightarrow{e'} & \mathbb{R}_{k'}(\mathbb{G}).
\end{array}$$
