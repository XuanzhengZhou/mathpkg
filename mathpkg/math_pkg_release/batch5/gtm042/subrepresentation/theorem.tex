Let $\rho: G \to \text{GL}(V)$ be a linear representation and let $W$ be a vector subspace of $V$. Suppose that $W$ is \textbf{stable} under the action of $G$ (or \textbf{invariant}), i.e., $x \in W$ implies $\rho_s x \in W$ for all $s \in G$. Then the restriction $\rho_s^W$ of $\rho_s$ to $W$ is an isomorphism of $W$ onto itself, and $\rho_{st}^W = \rho_s^W \cdot \rho_t^W$. Thus $\rho^W: G \to \text{GL}(W)$ is a linear representation of $G$ in $W$, called a \textbf{subrepresentation} of $V$.
