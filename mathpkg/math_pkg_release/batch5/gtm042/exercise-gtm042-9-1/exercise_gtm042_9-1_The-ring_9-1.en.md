Let $\varphi$ be a real-valued class function on $G$. Assume that $\langle \varphi, 1 \rangle = 0$ and that $\varphi(s) \leqslant 0$ for each $s \neq 1$. Show that for each character $\chi$ the real part of $\langle \varphi, \chi \rangle$ is $\geqslant 0$ [use the fact that the real part of $\varphi(s^{-1})\chi(s)$ is greater than or equal to that of $\varphi(s^{-1})\chi(1)$ for all $s$]. Conclude that, if $\varphi$ belongs to $R(G)$, $\varphi$ is a character.
