Proposition 8 provides a constructive method for explicitly decomposing a representation into irreducible summands. Using the matrix coefficients $r_{\beta\alpha}(t^{-1})$ of an irreducible representation $W_i$, one defines operators $p_{\alpha\beta}$ that project onto and identify copies of $W_i$ within $V$. This method, while computationally intensive, makes the abstract decomposition theorem concrete and algorithmically applicable.
