Let $W_i$ be given in matrix form $(r_{\alpha\beta}(s))$ with respect to a basis $(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$. For $\alpha, \beta \in \{1, \ldots, n\}$, define

$$p_{\alpha\beta} = \frac{n}{g} \sum_{t \in G} r_{\beta\alpha}(t^{-1}) \rho_t.$$

Then:

(a) $p_{\alpha\alpha}$ is a projection, zero on $V_j$ ($j \neq i$). Its image $V_{i,\alpha} \subseteq V_i$ and $V_i = \bigoplus_{\alpha} V_{i,\alpha}$. Moreover $p_i = \sum_\alpha p_{\alpha\alpha}$.

(b) $p_{\alpha\beta}$ is zero on $V_j$ ($j \neq i$) and on $V_{i,\gamma}$ for $\gamma \neq \beta$; it defines an isomorphism $V_{i,\beta} \to V_{i,\alpha}$.

(c) If $x_1 \neq 0$ in $V_{i,1}$ and $x_\alpha = p_{\alpha1}(x_1)$, then the $x_\alpha$ are linearly independent and generate a subrepresentation isomorphic to $W_i$.

(d) Choosing a basis of $V_{i,1}$ and applying (c) yields an explicit decomposition of $V_i$ into a direct sum of subrepresentations isomorphic to $W_i$.
