Let $\rho^1: G \to \text{GL}(V_1)$ and $\rho^2: G \to \text{GL}(V_2)$ be two linear representations of $G$. For $s \in G$, define $\rho_s \in \text{GL}(V_1 \otimes V_2)$ by:

$$\rho_s(x_1 \cdot x_2) = \rho_s^1(x_1) \cdot \rho_s^2(x_2) \quad \text{for } x_1 \in V_1, x_2 \in V_2.$$

Then $\rho_s = \rho_s^1 \otimes \rho_s^2$ defines a linear representation of $G$ in $V_1 \otimes V_2$, called the \textbf{tensor product} of the given representations.

The dimension satisfies $\dim(V_1 \otimes V_2) = \dim(V_1) \cdot \dim(V_2)$.
