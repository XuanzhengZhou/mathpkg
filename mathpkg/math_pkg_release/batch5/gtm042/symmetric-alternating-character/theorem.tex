Let $\rho: G \to \text{GL}(V)$ be a linear representation with character $\chi$, and let $\chi_\sigma^2$ and $\chi_\alpha^2$ be the characters of $\text{Sym}^2(V)$ and $\text{Alt}^2(V)$ respectively. For each $s \in G$:

$$\chi_\sigma^2(s) = \frac{1}{2}(\chi(s)^2 + \chi(s^2)),$$
$$\chi_\alpha^2(s) = \frac{1}{2}(\chi(s)^2 - \chi(s^2)).$$

We have $\chi_\sigma^2 + \chi_\alpha^2 = \chi^2$, reflecting $V \otimes V = \text{Sym}^2(V) \oplus \text{Alt}^2(V)$.
