Show that $\chi_h \cdot \chi_{h'} = \chi_{h+h'} + \chi_{h-h'}$. In particular, we have

$$\chi_n \cdot \chi_h = \chi_{2h} + \chi_0 = \chi_{2h} + \psi_1 + \psi_2.$$

Show that $\psi_2$ is the character of the alternating square of $\rho^h$, and that $\chi_{2h} + \psi_1$ is the character of its symmetric square (cf. 1.5 and prop. 3).
