Let $\rho: G \to \text{GL}(V)$ and $\rho': G \to \text{GL}(V')$ be two linear representations of the same group $G$. They are said to be \textbf{similar} (or \textbf{isomorphic}) if there exists a linear isomorphism $\tau: V \to V'$ such that

$$\tau \circ \rho(s) = \rho'(s) \circ \tau \quad \text{for all } s \in G.$$

In matrix form, with matrices $R_s$ and $R_s'$, this means there exists an invertible matrix $T$ such that $T \cdot R_s = R_s' \cdot T$ for all $s \in G$.
