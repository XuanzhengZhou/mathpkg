Theorem 5 provides an extremely useful numerical criterion for irreducibility: a representation is irreducible if and only if the inner product of its character with itself equals 1. More generally, $(\phi|\phi) = \sum m_i^2$ where $m_i$ are the multiplicities of the irreducible components, so $(\phi|\phi)$ is always a positive integer. This makes checking irreducibility a simple computation.
