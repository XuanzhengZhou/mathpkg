Lemma 21(a) shows that reduction mod $\mathfrak{m}$ gives a well-defined homomorphism $\rho: P_A(G) \to P_k(G)$ sending $[P]$ to $[\bar{P}]$. Lemma 21(b) shows that $\rho$ is injective on isomorphism classes, hence on the Grothendieck group. Surjectivity follows from the fact that every projective $k[G]$-module can be lifted to a projective $A[G]$-module (by lifting the projective envelope of each simple $k[G]$-module). The map preserves the positive cone because reduction of a module preserves the class of an actual module (as opposed to a virtual difference).
