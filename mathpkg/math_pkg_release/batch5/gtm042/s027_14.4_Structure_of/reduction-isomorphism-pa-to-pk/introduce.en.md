Corollary 3 of Section 14.4 establishes a canonical isomorphism between the Grothendieck group $P_A(G)$ of projective $A[G]$-modules and $P_k(G)$ of projective $k[G]$-modules, induced by reduction modulo the maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ of the complete discrete valuation ring $A$. This isomorphism also identifies the positive cones $P_A^+(G)$ and $P_k^+(G)$. Consequently, for the purposes of the projective Grothendieck group, one may work entirely over the residue field $k$ without loss of information.
