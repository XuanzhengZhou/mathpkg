If $P$ is projective over $\Lambda[G]$, it is projective over $\Lambda$: this follows from the fact that $\Lambda[G]$ is a free $\Lambda$-module, hence any projective $\Lambda[G]$-module restricts to a projective $\Lambda$-module. For the converse direction, the existence of the $\Lambda$-endomorphism $u$ satisfying $\sum_{s \in G} s \cdot u(s^{-1}x) = x$ allows one to split the canonical surjection from a free $\Lambda[G]$-module onto $P$, establishing $\Lambda[G]$-projectivity.
