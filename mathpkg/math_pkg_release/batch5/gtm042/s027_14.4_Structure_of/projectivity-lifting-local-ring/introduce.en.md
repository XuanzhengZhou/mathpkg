Lemma 21 of Chapter 14 provides a reduction-to-the-residue-field principle for projective modules over group rings with a local coefficient ring $\Lambda$. Part (a) states that $\Lambda[G]$-projectivity of a $\Lambda$-free module $P$ can be detected after base change to the residue field $k_\Lambda$. Part (b) extends this to isomorphisms: two projective $\Lambda[G]$-modules are isomorphic exactly when their reductions modulo the maximal ideal are isomorphic as $k_\Lambda[G]$-modules. This lemma is the key technical tool for relating $P_A(G)$ to $P_k(G)$.
