This follows from the preceding proposition (Lemma 21) and known results for projective $k[G]$-modules. By Lemma 21(b), the reduction mod $\mathfrak{m}$ gives a bijection between isomorphism classes of projective $A[G]$-modules and projective $k[G]$-modules. The decomposition and uniqueness for $k[G]$-modules (Krull–Schmidt theorem) then lifts to $A[G]$-modules. Moreover, a projective indecomposable $A[G]$-module reduces to a projective indecomposable $k[G]$-module, which is precisely the projective envelope of some simple $k[G]$-module.
