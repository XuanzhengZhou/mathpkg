Proof of Theorem 42.

(a) We prove first that $\phi_{\text{E}}(\text{E} \in \text{S}_k)$ are linearly independent over $\text{K}$. Indeed, suppose that we had a relation $\sum a_{\text{E}} \phi_{\text{E}} = 0$, with $a_{\text{E}} \in \text{K}$, not all zero. Multiplying the $a_{\text{E}}$ by some element of $\text{K}$, we can assume that they all belong to the ring $\text{A}$, and that at least one does not belong to $\mathfrak{m}$. By reduction mod. $\mathfrak{m}$, we then have

$$\sum_{\text{E} \in \text{S}_k} \bar{a}_{\text{E}} \overline{\phi_{\text{E}}(s)} = 0 \quad \text{for all } s \in \text{G}_{\text{reg}}$$

where the bar denotes reduction mod. $\mathfrak{m}$, and one of the $\bar{a}_{\text{E}}$ is not zero. From formula (v) of the preceding section, we get

$$\sum \bar{a}_{\text{E}} \text{Tr}(t_{\text{E}}) = 0 \quad \text{for all } t \in \text{G},$$

thus also for all $t \in k[\text{G}]$.
