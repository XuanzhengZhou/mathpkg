This classification theorem states that the representations constructed in Section 5.5 -- the two degree-1 characters $\psi_{1}, \psi_{2}$ and the infinite family of degree-2 representations $\rho^{h}$ ($h \geq 1$) -- exhaust all irreducible unitary representations of the infinite dihedral group $D_{\infty}$. The result follows from the Peter-Weyl theorem and the fact that the characters $\{1, \psi_{2}, \chi_{h}\}$ form a complete orthonormal system for the space of square-integrable class functions on $D_{\infty}$, which is the continuous analogue of the corresponding result for finite dihedral groups.
