The degree-1 irreducible representations of $D_{\infty}$ are constructed analogously to those of the finite dihedral group $D_{n}$. The rotation subgroup $C_{\infty}$ is a normal subgroup of index $2$ in $D_{\infty}$, with quotient $D_{\infty}/C_{\infty} \cong \{\pm 1\}$. Any homomorphism $D_{\infty} \to \mathbb{C}^{\times}$ that factors through this quotient yields a degree-1 representation.

The quotient has exactly two irreducible representations: the trivial representation sending both cosets to $1$, and the sign representation sending $C_{\infty}$ to $1$ and the nontrivial coset to $-1$. Lifting these to $D_{\infty}$ gives the two characters:
$$
\psi_{1}(r_{\alpha}) = 1, \quad \psi_{1}(s r_{\alpha}) = 1,
$$
$$
\psi_{2}(r_{\alpha}) = 1, \quad \psi_{2}(s r_{\alpha}) = -1.
$$
These are clearly irreducible since they are one-dimensional.
