The character $\chi_{h}$ of the degree-2 irreducible representation $\rho^{h}$ is obtained by taking the trace of the defining matrices. On a rotation $r_{\alpha}$, the trace is $e^{i h \alpha} + e^{-i h \alpha} = 2 \cos(h \alpha)$, while on a reflection $s r_{\alpha}$ the off-diagonal matrix has trace zero. This is the continuous analogue of the formula $\chi_{h}(r^{k}) = 2 \cos(2 \pi h k / n)$ for the finite dihedral group $D_n$.
