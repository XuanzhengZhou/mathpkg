The character $\chi_{h}$ is the trace of the representation $\rho^{h}$. Computing the trace of the defining matrices:
$$
\rho^{h}(r_{\alpha}) = \begin{pmatrix} e^{i h \alpha} & 0 \\ 0 & e^{-i h \alpha} \end{pmatrix} \implies \chi_{h}(r_{\alpha}) = \operatorname{Tr} \rho^{h}(r_{\alpha}) = e^{i h \alpha} + e^{-i h \alpha} = 2 \cos(h \alpha).
$$
$$
\rho^{h}(s r_{\alpha}) = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & e^{-i h \alpha} \\ e^{i h \alpha} & 0 \end{pmatrix} \implies \chi_{h}(s r_{\alpha}) = \operatorname{Tr} \rho^{h}(s r_{\alpha}) = 0 + 0 = 0.
$$
Thus the character values are as stated.
