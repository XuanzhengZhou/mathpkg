By Theorem 1, every representation $V$ has a complement $W^0$ for any stable subspace $W$.
Apply this repeatedly: if $V$ is not irreducible, it has a proper nonzero stable subspace $W$, and $V = W \oplus W^0$.
By induction on dimension, $W$ and $W^0$ decompose into irreducibles, hence so does $V$.
