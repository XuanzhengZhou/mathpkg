Theorem 1 guarantees that every invariant subspace of a representation of a finite group admits an invariant complement. This is proved by averaging an arbitrary projection over the group using the formula $p^0 = \frac{1}{g} \sum_{t \in G} \rho_t \cdot p \cdot \rho_t^{-1}$. This result is the foundation of complete reducibility for finite group representations over $\mathbb{C}$.
