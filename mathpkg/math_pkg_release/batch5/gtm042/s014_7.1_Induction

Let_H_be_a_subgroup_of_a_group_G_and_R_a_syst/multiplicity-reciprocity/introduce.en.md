Proposition 21 is a direct consequence of Frobenius Reciprocity applied to irreducible characters. It states that the multiplicity of an irreducible representation $W$ of a subgroup $H$ in the restriction of an irreducible $E$ of $G$ equals the multiplicity of $E$ in the induced representation of $W$. This symmetric relationship is extremely useful in practice: it allows one to compute restriction multiplicities via induction, or vice versa, whichever is more convenient for the specific groups involved.
