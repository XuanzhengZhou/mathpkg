This is clear: by definition of the stabilizer $H$, the subspace $W$ is stable under $H$, so $W$ is a $\mathbb{C}[H]$-module. The transitivity of the $G$-action on the set $\{W_i\}_{i \in I}$ implies that the $W_i$ are precisely the translates $sW$ for $s$ ranging over a set of coset representatives of $G/H$, and the direct sum decomposition $V = \bigoplus_{i \in I} W_i$ corresponds exactly to $V = \bigoplus_{s \in G/H} sW$. Hence $V$ is induced by $W$ as a $\mathbb{C}[H]$-module.
