Mackey's Irreducibility Criterion provides necessary and sufficient conditions for an induced representation to be irreducible. The criterion reduces the global question of irreducibility of $\operatorname{Ind}_H^G(W)$ to two local checks: the irreducibility of the original representation $W$ of $H$, and the disjointness of certain pairs of representations of the subgroups $H_s = sHs^{-1} \cap H$ for double coset representatives $s \notin H$. This is one of the most practically useful results in representation theory, as it gives a computable criterion that avoids constructing the entire induced representation.
