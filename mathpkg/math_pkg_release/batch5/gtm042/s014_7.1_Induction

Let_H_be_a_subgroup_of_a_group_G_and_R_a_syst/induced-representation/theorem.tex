Let $H$ be a subgroup of a group $G$ and let $R$ be a system of left coset representatives for $H$. Let $V$ be a $\mathbb{C}[G]$-module and let $W$ be a sub-$\mathbb{C}[H]$-module of $V$. The module $V$ (or the representation $V$) is said to be \textbf{induced} by $W$ if

$$V = \bigoplus_{s \in R} sW,$$

i.e., if $V$ is a direct sum of the images $sW$, $s \in R$ (a condition which is independent of the choice of $R$).
