This is a consequence of the fact that the elements of $R$ form a basis of $\mathbb{C}[G]$ considered as a right $\mathbb{C}[H]$-module. Indeed, the tensor product $\mathbb{C}[G] \otimes_{\mathbb{C}[H]} W$ decomposes as $\bigoplus_{s \in R} (s \otimes W)$, and the map $i$ sends $s \otimes w$ to $sw$. Since the elements of $R$ form a basis, the map $i$ is an isomorphism if and only if the images $sW$ for $s \in R$ span $V$ and are linearly independent — which is exactly the condition that $V = \bigoplus_{s \in R} sW$.
