\textbf{Exercise 7.4.} Let $k$ be a finite field, let $G = \operatorname{SL}_2(k)$ and let $H$ be the subgroup of $G$ consisting of matrices $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ such that $c = 0$. Let $\omega$ be a homomorphism of $k^*$ into $\mathbb{C}^*$ and let $\chi_\omega$ be the character of degree $1$ of $H$ defined by

$$\chi_\omega \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ 0 & d \end{pmatrix} = \omega(a).$$

Show that the representation of $G$ induced by $\chi_\omega$ is irreducible if $\omega^2 \neq 1$.
