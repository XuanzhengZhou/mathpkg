Lemma 2 establishes the crucial identity that the inner product of two characters equals the dimension of the space of $G$-homomorphisms between the corresponding representations. This bridges the character-theoretic and module-theoretic perspectives on representations. The identity is a direct consequence of the orthogonality relations for irreducible characters (Theorem 3 of Section 2.3) via decomposition into irreducibles. It serves as the foundation for Frobenius reciprocity and the multiplicity formulas that govern induction and restriction.
