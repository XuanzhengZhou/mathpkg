Decompose $V_1$ and $V_2$ into direct sums of irreducible representations. By the orthogonality formulas for characters (Section 2.3, Theorem 3), the inner product $\langle \varphi_1, \varphi_2 \rangle_G$ counts the number of common irreducible constituents (with multiplicities) of $V_1$ and $V_2$. On the other hand, by Schur's Lemma, $\dim \operatorname{Hom}^G(V_1, V_2)$ also equals exactly this number. More precisely, if $V_1 \cong \bigoplus_i n_i U_i$ and $V_2 \cong \bigoplus_i m_i U_i$ where the $U_i$ are pairwise non-isomorphic irreducible representations, then both quantities equal $\sum_i n_i m_i$.
