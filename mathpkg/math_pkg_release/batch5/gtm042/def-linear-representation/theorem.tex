Let $G$ be a finite group and $V$ a vector space over the field $\mathbb{C}$ of complex numbers.
A linear representation of $G$ in $V$ is a homomorphism $\rho: G \to \mathrm{GL}(V)$.
In other words, we associate with each element $s \in G$ an element $\rho(s)$ of $\mathrm{GL}(V)$ such that
$$\rho(st) = \rho(s) \cdot \rho(t) \quad \text{for } s, t \in G.$$
When $\rho$ is given, $V$ is called a representation space of $G$.
The dimension $n$ of $V$ is called the degree of the representation.
