If $k < 12$ there is at most one term in the sum and the theorem can be verified directly. If $f$ has weight $k < 12$ the weight formula (5) implies $N = N(i\infty) = 0$ so the only possible zeros of $f$ are at the vertices $\rho$ and $i$.

For $k \geq 12$, we proceed by induction. Since $\Delta$ has a first-order zero at $i\infty$, the quotient $f/\Delta$ (if $f$ vanishes at $i\infty$) or a suitable linear combination reducing the order of vanishing at $i\infty$ yields an entire form of lower weight. Therefore $f = cG_k + \Delta h$ is a sum of the type shown in (7). This proves, by induction, that every entire form of even weight $k$ has at least one representation of the type in (7).

To show there is at most one such representation we need only verify that the products $G_{k-12r}\Delta^r$ are linearly independent. This follows easily from the fact that $\Delta(i\infty) = 0$ but $G_{2r}(i\infty) \neq 0$. Details are left as an exercise for the reader.
