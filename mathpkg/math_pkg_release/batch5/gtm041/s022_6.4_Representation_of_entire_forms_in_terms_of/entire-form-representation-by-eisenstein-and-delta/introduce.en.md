Theorem 6.3 provides a canonical basis for the vector space $M_k$ of entire modular forms of even weight $k$. It states that every such form can be written uniquely as a linear combination of the products $G_{k-12r} \Delta^r$ for $r = 0, 1, \ldots, [k/12]$, where $G_{k-12r}$ are Eisenstein series and $\Delta$ is the modular discriminant. The cusp forms are precisely those combinations where the coefficient $a_0$ of the pure Eisenstein term vanishes, giving a clean characterization of the subspace $M_{k,0}$.
