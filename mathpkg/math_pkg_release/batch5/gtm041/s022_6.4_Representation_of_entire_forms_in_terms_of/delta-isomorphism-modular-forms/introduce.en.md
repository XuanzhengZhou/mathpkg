This proposition shows that for $k \geq 12$, multiplication by the discriminant $\Delta$ gives a linear isomorphism $T: M_{k-12} \to M_{k,0}$ between the space of entire modular forms of weight $k-12$ and the subspace of cusp forms of weight $k$. The isomorphism follows from the fact that $\Delta$ has a first-order zero at $i\infty$ and no other zeros, so multiplying by $\Delta$ exactly encodes the cusp condition. As a corollary, $\dim M_{k,0} = \dim M_{k-12}$, which together with $\dim M_{k,0} = \dim M_k - 1$ yields the fundamental recursion $\dim M_k = 1 + \dim M_{k-12}$.
