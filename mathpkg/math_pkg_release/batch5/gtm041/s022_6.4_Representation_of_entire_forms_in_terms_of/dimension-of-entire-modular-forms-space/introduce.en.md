The dimension formula for $M_k$ follows directly from the canonical basis provided by Theorem 6.3. The number of basis elements $G_{k-12r} \Delta^r$ is $[k/12] + 1$ in general, but when $k \equiv 2 \pmod{12}$ the term with $r = [k/12]$ corresponds to $G_2$, which is not an entire modular form, reducing the count by one. This formula is fundamental for understanding the structure of spaces of modular forms and their graded ring.
