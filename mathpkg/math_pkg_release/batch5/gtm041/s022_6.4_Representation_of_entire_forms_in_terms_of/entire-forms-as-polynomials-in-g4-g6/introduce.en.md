Theorem 6.4 refines the result of Theorem 6.3 by showing that every entire modular form of weight $k$ is a homogeneous polynomial in the two Eisenstein series $G_4$ (weight 4) and $G_6$ (weight 6). The polynomial is constrained by the weight condition $4a + 6b = k$, which restricts the possible monomials $G_4^a G_6^b$. This gives a concrete algebraic description of the space $M_k$ and shows that the graded ring of entire modular forms is generated by $G_4$ and $G_6$.
