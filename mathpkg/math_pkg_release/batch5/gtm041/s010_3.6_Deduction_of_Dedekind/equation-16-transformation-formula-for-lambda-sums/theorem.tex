Let $h$ and $k$ be integers with $k > 0$ and $(h, k) = 1$. Let $H$ be an integer such that $hH \equiv -1 \pmod{k}$. Then for $\operatorname{Re}(z) > 0$ we have

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda\!\left(\frac{n}{k}(z - ih)\right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda\!\left(\frac{n}{k}\!\left(\frac{1}{z} - iH\right)\right) + \frac{\pi}{12}\!\left(z - \frac{1}{z}\right)\!\left(1 - \frac{1}{k}\right) + \pi i\, s(h, k),$$

where $\lambda(x) = -\log(1 - e^{-2\pi i x})$ for $\operatorname{Im}(x) > 0$ and $s(h, k)$ is the Dedekind sum

$$s(h, k) = \sum_{\mu=1}^{k-1} \frac{\mu}{k}\!\left(\frac{h\mu}{k} - \left\lfloor\frac{h\mu}{k}\right\rfloor - \frac{1}{2}\right).$$
