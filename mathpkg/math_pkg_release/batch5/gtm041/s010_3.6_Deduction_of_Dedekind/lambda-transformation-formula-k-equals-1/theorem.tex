Let $h$ be an integer. For $\operatorname{Re}(z) > 0$ we have

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda\!\left(n(z - ih)\right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda\!\left(n\!\left(\frac{1}{z} - iH\right)\right) + \frac{1}{2}\log z - \frac{\pi}{12}\!\left(z - \frac{1}{z}\right),$$

where $H$ is any integer such that $hH \equiv -1 \pmod{1}$ (i.e., any integer) and $\lambda(x) = -\log(1 - e^{-2\pi i x})$ for $\operatorname{Im}(x) > 0$. Since $\lambda(x + i) = \lambda(x)$, the shifts by $ih$ and $iH$ drop out, giving the equivalent form

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda(nz) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda\!\left(\frac{n}{z}\right) + \frac{1}{2}\log z - \frac{\pi}{12}\!\left(z - \frac{1}{z}\right).$$
