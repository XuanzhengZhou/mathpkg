Theorem 5.4 shows that when two reduced fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$ satisfy the unimodular condition $bc - ad = 1$, their mediant $h/k = (a+c)/(b+d)$ lies strictly between them and also satisfies unimodular relations with both parent fractions. This property is essential for the inductive construction of Farey sequences, as it guarantees that inserting mediants preserves the unimodular condition between consecutive fractions.
