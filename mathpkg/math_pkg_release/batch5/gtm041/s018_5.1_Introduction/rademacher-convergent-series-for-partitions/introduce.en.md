Hans Rademacher, while preparing lecture notes in 1937 on the work of Hardy and Ramanujan, made a small change in the analysis which resulted in slightly different terms $R_k(n)$ in place of the divergent $P_k(n)$. This had a profound effect: instead of a divergent asymptotic series, Rademacher obtained a convergent series for $p(n)$. This formula is both asymptotic and exact -- since $p(n)$ is an integer, the finite sum gives $p(n)$ exactly when enough terms are taken.
