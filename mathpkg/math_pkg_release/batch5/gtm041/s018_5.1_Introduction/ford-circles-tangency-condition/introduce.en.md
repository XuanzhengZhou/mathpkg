Theorem 5.6 establishes the fundamental geometric property of Ford circles: two such circles are either disjoint or tangent, with tangency occurring exactly when the corresponding fractions satisfy the unimodular condition. Since consecutive Farey fractions always satisfy $bc - ad = 1$, their Ford circles are tangent. This geometric structure is the basis for Rademacher's modification of the circle method contour.
