Euler's product formula establishes the fundamental generating function for the unrestricted partition function $p(n)$. The infinite product $\prod_{m=1}^{\infty} (1-x^m)^{-1}$ expands as a power series whose coefficients are precisely the partition numbers. This identity is the starting point for the circle method and all subsequent analytic study of $p(n)$. A proof can be found in Chapter 14 of Apostol's Introduction to Analytic Number Theory.
