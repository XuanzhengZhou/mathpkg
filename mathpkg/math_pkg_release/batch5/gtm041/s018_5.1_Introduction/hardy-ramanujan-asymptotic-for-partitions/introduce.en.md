The Hardy-Ramanujan asymptotic formula describes the growth of $p(n)$ for large $n$, showing that it grows like $\exp(K\sqrt{n})$ divided by a polynomial factor. Discovered independently by Hardy and Ramanujan (1918) and Uspensky (1920), this result was a triumph of the circle method. The dominant term $e^{K\sqrt{n}}/(4n\sqrt{3})$ was later refined into Rademacher's exact convergent series.
