Theorem 2.1 establishes that the modular group $\Gamma$ is generated by two elementary transformations: the translation $T\tau = \tau + 1$ and the inversion $S\tau = -1/\tau$. The proof uses an inductive argument on the absolute value of the lower-left matrix entry, reducing any element to a product of powers of $T$ and $S$ via the Euclidean algorithm. This generation result is essential for constructing fundamental domains and for the analytic theory of modular forms.
