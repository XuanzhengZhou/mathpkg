Lemma 1 guarantees that given any pair of complex numbers whose ratio is not real, the lattice they generate possesses a fundamental pair $(\omega_1, \omega_2)$ that satisfies the geometric inequalities $|\omega_2| \geq |\omega_1|$ and $|\omega_1 \pm \omega_2| \geq |\omega_2|$. The proof constructs such a pair by ordering the lattice points by increasing distance from the origin and selecting the first non-collinear vector. This lemma is the crucial step in proving that every point in the upper half-plane is equivalent under $\Gamma$ to a point in the standard fundamental region $R_{\Gamma}$.
