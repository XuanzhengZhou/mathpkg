The modular group $\Gamma$ is the group of M\"obius transformations with integer coefficients and determinant one, equivalently realized as the projective special linear group $\operatorname{PSL}(2, \mathbb{Z}) = \operatorname{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z})/\{\pm I\}$. Each element acts on the upper half-plane $H = \{\tau \in \mathbb{C} : \operatorname{Im}(\tau) > 0\}$ by fractional linear transformations $\tau \mapsto (a\tau+b)/(c\tau+d)$. The modular group is the fundamental symmetry group underlying the theory of modular functions and modular forms.
