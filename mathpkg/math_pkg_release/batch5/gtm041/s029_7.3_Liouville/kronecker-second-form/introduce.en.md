Kronecker's approximation theorem in its second (stronger) form states that under linear independence conditions, one can simultaneously approximate arbitrary real numbers $x_1, \ldots, x_k$ by integer combinations $q\alpha_j - p_j$. This is a strengthening of the first form (which only handles $x_j = 0$) and is fundamental to Bohr's theory of equivalence of Dirichlet series. The theorem shows that the sequence $\{q\alpha\}$ is uniformly distributed modulo 1 when $\alpha$ is irrational.
