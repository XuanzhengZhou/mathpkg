This lemma is a key step in the proof of Kronecker's theorem (first form). It reduces the Diophantine approximation problem to an analytic statement about the supremum of an exponential sum $F(t)$. If the approximation property holds, each exponential term is nearly 1, making $|F(t)|$ close to $n+1$. Conversely, if $L = n+1$, the approximation property must hold; otherwise a geometric argument places points outside a shaded sector, yielding $L \leq n+1-\delta < n+1$, a contradiction.
