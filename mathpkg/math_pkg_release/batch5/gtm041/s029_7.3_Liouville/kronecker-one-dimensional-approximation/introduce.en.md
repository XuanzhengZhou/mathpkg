This theorem removes the restriction $0 \leq \alpha \leq 1$ from the one-dimensional Kronecker approximation theorem. For any real $\alpha$, irrational $\theta$, and $\varepsilon > 0$, there exist integers $h$ and $k > 0$ such that $|k\theta - h - \alpha| < \varepsilon$. The proof is a simple reduction to the unit interval case: write $\alpha = [\alpha] + \{\alpha\}$, apply Theorem 7.7 to approximate $\{\alpha\}$, and absorb the integer part.
