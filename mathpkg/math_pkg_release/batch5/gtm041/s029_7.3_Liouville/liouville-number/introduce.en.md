A Liouville number is a real number that can be approximated by rational numbers to an extraordinarily high degree of accuracy. Formally, $\theta$ is a Liouville number if for every positive integer $r$, there exist integers $h_r$ and $k_r > 0$ such that $0 < |\theta - h_r/k_r| < 1/k_r^r$. Liouville numbers are the classic examples of transcendental numbers; Liouville's approximation theorem shows that any algebraic number of degree $n$ cannot be approximated this well, so Liouville numbers must be transcendental.
