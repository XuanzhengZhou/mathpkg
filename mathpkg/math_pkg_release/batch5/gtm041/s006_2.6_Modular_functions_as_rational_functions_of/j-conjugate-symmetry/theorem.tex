The $j$-invariant satisfies the conjugate symmetry

$$J(\tau) = J(-\bar{\tau})$$

for all $\tau$ in the upper half-plane $H$. In particular, on the circular arc $\tau \bar{\tau} = 1$ (the right boundary of the fundamental region $R_{\Gamma}$), we have $-\bar{\tau} = -1/\tau$, hence $J(-\bar{\tau}) = J(-1/\tau) = J(\tau)$, so $J$ is real on this arc.
