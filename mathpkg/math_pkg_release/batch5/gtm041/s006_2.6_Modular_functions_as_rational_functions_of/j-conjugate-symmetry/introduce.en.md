This lemma establishes a conjugate symmetry property of the modular invariant $J$. It states that $J$ takes conjugate values at points that are symmetric with respect to the imaginary axis, i.e., $J(\tau) = J(-\bar{\tau})$. An important consequence is that on the circular arc $\tau \bar{\tau} = 1$ forming the right boundary of the standard fundamental region $R_{\Gamma}$, $J$ takes real values. This plays a key role in understanding the mapping properties of $J$ from the upper half-plane to the complex plane.
