Theorem 2.8 characterizes modular functions in terms of the modular invariant $J$. The forward direction is immediate: any rational function of $J$ inherits modular invariance from $J$ itself. The converse direction is the deep part: given an arbitrary modular function $f$, one can construct a rational function of $J$ that has exactly the same zeros and poles as $f$, implying their quotient is a constant. Thus every modular function is, up to a multiplicative constant, a rational function of $J$.
