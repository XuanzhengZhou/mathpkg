Theorem 2.9 solves the inversion problem for Eisenstein series in the Weierstrass theory of elliptic functions. The problem asks: given two complex numbers $a_2, a_3$ satisfying the non-degeneracy condition $a_2^3 - 27a_3^2 \neq 0$ (the discriminant of the cubic $x^3 - g_2 x - g_3$ must be non-zero), can we find periods $\omega_1, \omega_2$ with $\operatorname{Im}(\omega_2/\omega_1) > 0$ such that the Eisenstein series $g_2$ and $g_3$ evaluated at these periods take the prescribed values? The theorem answers affirmatively using the mapping properties of the $j$-invariant.
