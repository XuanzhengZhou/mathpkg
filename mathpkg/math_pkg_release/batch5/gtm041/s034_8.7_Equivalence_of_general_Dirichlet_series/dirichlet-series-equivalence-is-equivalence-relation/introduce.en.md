Theorem 8.11 verifies that the relation $\sim$ defined as equivalence of general Dirichlet series relative to a basis $B$ satisfies all three axioms of an equivalence relation: reflexivity (every series is equivalent to itself by taking all $y_n = 0$), symmetry (if $b(n) = a(n)e^{ix_n}$ then $a(n) = b(n)e^{-ix_n}$, with the negative sequence giving the required real parameters), and transitivity (the sum of the real parameter sequences serves for the composed equivalence). This legitimizes the term "equivalence" and makes the quotient space of equivalence classes a well-defined object for further study.
