Theorem 8.12 specializes Bohr's general equivalence relation to ordinary Dirichlet series $\sum a(n)n^{-s}$. For ordinary Dirichlet series, equivalence admits a concrete characterization: two such series are equivalent if and only if their coefficient sequences are related by multiplication by a completely multiplicative function $f$ that has absolute value $1$ on all primes dividing any $n$ for which $a(n) \neq 0$. This connects the abstract Bohr equivalence framework to the concrete arithmetic of completely multiplicative functions, making the theory applicable to classical Dirichlet series in number theory.
