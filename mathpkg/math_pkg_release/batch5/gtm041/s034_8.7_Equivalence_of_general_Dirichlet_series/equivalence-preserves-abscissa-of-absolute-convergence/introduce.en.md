Theorem 8.10 establishes two important properties of Bohr's equivalence relation for general Dirichlet series. First, equivalent Dirichlet series share the same abscissa of absolute convergence, which follows immediately from the fact that the moduli of the coefficients are preserved under equivalence. Second, the equivalence relation is independent of the choice of basis $B$ used to represent the exponent sequence $\Lambda$; if two series are equivalent with respect to one basis, they remain equivalent with respect to any other basis, via the Bohr matrix relating the two bases.
