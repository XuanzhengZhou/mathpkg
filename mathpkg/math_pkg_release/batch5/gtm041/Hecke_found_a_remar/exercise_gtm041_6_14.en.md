A quadratic polynomial $1 - Ax + Bx^2$ with real coefficients $A$ and $B$ can be factored as follows:
$$1 - Ax + Bx^2 = (1 - r_1 x)(1 - r_2 x).$$
Assume that $|r_1| = |r_2| = \sqrt{B}$. Show that the Euler product
$$\prod_p \frac{1}{1 - c(p)p^{-s} + p^{2k-1-2s}}$$
can be expressed in terms of the roots of the quadratic factor at each prime, and relate this factorization to the symmetric square $L$-function.
