Hecke proved that the Dirichlet series $\varphi(s) = \sum c(n)n^{-s}$ formed from the Fourier coefficients of a modular form $f \in M_k$ possesses a meromorphic continuation to the whole complex plane, with at most a simple pole at $s = k$ when the constant term $c(0)$ is nonzero. The proof uses the Mellin transform representation $(2\pi)^{-s}\Gamma(s)\varphi(s) = \int_0^\infty (f(iy)-c(0))y^{s-1}dy$ and exploits the modular transformation $f(i/y) = (iy)^k f(iy)$ to obtain a symmetric integral expression valid for all $s$. This yields the functional equation relating $\varphi(s)$ and $\varphi(k-s)$, which is the prototypical example of the functional equations satisfied by $L$-functions of modular forms and automorphic representations.
