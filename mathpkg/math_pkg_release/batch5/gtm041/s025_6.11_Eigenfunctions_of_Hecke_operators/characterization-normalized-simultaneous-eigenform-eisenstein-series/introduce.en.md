Theorem 6.16 characterizes all normalized simultaneous eigenforms in $M_{2k}$ ($k \geq 2$) that are not cusp forms: they are uniquely determined by the divisor function $\sigma_{2k-1}(n)$ as their Fourier coefficients. These forms are constant multiples of the Eisenstein series $G_{2k}$. The constant term involves $\zeta(2k)$, which is related to Bernoulli numbers. This theorem, together with Theorem 6.15, provides a complete classification of simultaneous eigenforms.
