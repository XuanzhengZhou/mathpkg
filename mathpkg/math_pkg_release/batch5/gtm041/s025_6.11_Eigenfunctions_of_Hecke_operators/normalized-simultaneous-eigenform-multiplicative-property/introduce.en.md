Theorem 6.15 gives a concrete arithmetic characterization of normalized simultaneous eigenforms among cusp forms: a normalized cusp form ($c(1) = 1$) is an eigenform for all Hecke operators precisely when its Fourier coefficients satisfy a multiplicative convolution identity. This beautifully connects the Hecke operator formalism with the multiplicative structure of arithmetic functions like Ramanujan's $\tau(n)$. The proof works by equating coefficients in the Fourier expansions of $T_n f$ and $\lambda(n) f$.
