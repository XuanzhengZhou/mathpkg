For an integer $k \geq 2$, the \textit{Eisenstein series} $G_{2k}(\tau)$ is defined by
$$G_{2k}(\tau) = \sum_{(m,n) \neq (0,0)} \frac{1}{(m + n\tau)^{2k}},$$
where the sum runs over all pairs of integers $(m,n)$ except $(0,0)$. It is a modular form of weight $2k$ satisfying
$$G_{2k}\left(\frac{a\tau + b}{c\tau + d}\right) = (c\tau + d)^{2k} G_{2k}(\tau)$$
for every $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$.
