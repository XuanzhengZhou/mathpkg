A modular form is a holomorphic function on the upper half-plane that transforms in a specific way under the action of the modular group $\Gamma$. If $f$ satisfies $f((a\tau + b)/(c\tau + d)) = (c\tau + d)^r f(\tau)$ for all $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$, then $f$ is said to have weight $r$. Modular forms must also be periodic with period $1$ (since $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$) and therefore possess Fourier expansions. The theory of modular forms connects number theory, complex analysis, and representation theory, with important examples including the discriminant function $\Delta(\tau)$ and Eisenstein series $G_{2k}(\tau)$.
