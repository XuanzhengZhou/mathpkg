A \textit{modular form} of weight $r$ (where $r$ is an integer) is a function $f$ defined on the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H}$ that satisfies the transformation law
$$f\left(\frac{a\tau + b}{c\tau + d}\right) = (c\tau + d)^r f(\tau)$$
for every $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ in the modular group $\Gamma$, together with certain analyticity conditions (including holomorphy on $\mathbb{H}$ and at the cusp $i\infty$).
