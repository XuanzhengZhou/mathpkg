The Fourier expansion (or $q$-expansion) of the discriminant $\Delta(\tau)$ expresses this modular form as an infinite series in $q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$. Since $\Delta$ is a cusp form, its $q$-expansion has no constant term, beginning with $q^1$. The coefficients $\tau(n)$ are the values of Ramanujan's tau function, an integer-valued multiplicative arithmetic function with deep connections to the theory of partitions, elliptic curves, and the Langlands program. The factor $(2\pi)^{12}$ is a normalization constant that makes the coefficients $\tau(n)$ integers.
