The modular transformation property is stated in this section as an introductory motivation for the definition of modular forms. The author notes that the discriminant $\Delta(\tau)$ satisfies the transformation law with $r = 12$, and the Eisenstein series $G_{2k}(\tau)$ satisfies it with $r = 2k$. The full proofs of these transformation properties are deferred to later sections of the book. In particular:

- For $\Delta(\tau)$, the transformation property follows from its definition as a product involving the Dedekind eta function, or as a cusp form constructed from Eisenstein series $G_4$ and $G_6$.

- For $G_{2k}(\tau)$, the transformation property is verified by reindexing the lattice sum and using the fact that $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$ maps the integer lattice $\mathbb{Z}^2$ onto itself.

This section serves as an introduction, motivating the general definition of modular forms to be given precisely in the next section.
