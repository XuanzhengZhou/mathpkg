The divisor sum function $\sigma_{\alpha}(n)$ is a fundamental arithmetic function that sums the $\alpha$-th powers of all positive divisors of $n$. It is multiplicative in $n$ for each fixed $\alpha$. The case $\alpha = 0$ gives the divisor counting function $d(n)$, while $\alpha = 1$ yields the classical sum-of-divisors function $\sigma(n)$. Divisor sum functions appear naturally in the Fourier expansions of Eisenstein series, where $\sigma_{2k-1}(n)$ provides the arithmetic content of the Fourier coefficients of $G_{2k}(\tau)$.
