For an integer $\alpha$, the \textit{divisor sum function} $\sigma_{\alpha}(n)$ is defined for $n \geq 1$ as the sum of the $\alpha$-th powers of the positive divisors of $n$:
$$\sigma_{\alpha}(n) = \sum_{d \mid n} d^{\alpha}.$$
In particular, $\sigma_0(n) = d(n)$ is the number of divisors, and $\sigma_1(n) = \sigma(n)$ is the sum of divisors.
