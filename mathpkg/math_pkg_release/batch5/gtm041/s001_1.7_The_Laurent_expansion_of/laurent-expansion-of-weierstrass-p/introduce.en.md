The Weierstrass $\wp$ function has its only singularity at lattice points, where it has a double pole. Near the origin $z=0$, it admits a Laurent expansion whose coefficients are expressed in terms of the Eisenstein series $G_{2n+2}$. The radius of convergence $r$ is the distance from the origin to the nearest nonzero lattice point. This expansion reveals the intimate connection between $\wp$ and the lattice invariants and is fundamental for deriving the differential equation satisfied by $\wp$.
