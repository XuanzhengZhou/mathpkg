A basis for the exponents of a Dirichlet series generalizes the notion of a vector space basis to the setting of sequences of real numbers, with rational coefficients replacing scalar multiplication. Introduced by Harald Bohr in his theory of general Dirichlet series, a basis captures the minimal linearly independent (over $\mathbb{Q}$) subset from which all exponents can be expressed as finite rational linear combinations, and vice versa. This concept is foundational for the subsequent definition of the Bohr function and the study of equivalence classes of Dirichlet series.
