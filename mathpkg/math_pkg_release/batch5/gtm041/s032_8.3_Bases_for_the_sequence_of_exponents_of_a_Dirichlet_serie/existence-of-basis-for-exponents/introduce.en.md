Theorem 8.4 guarantees that every sequence of distinct real numbers $\Lambda$, regardless of its complexity, contains a subsequence that serves as a basis. The proof is constructive: one iteratively picks the first remaining element of $\Lambda$ as a new basis element and deletes all elements that are rational linear combinations of the basis elements chosen so far. This provides the foundational existence result needed to develop the theory of Bohr functions for arbitrary Dirichlet series.
