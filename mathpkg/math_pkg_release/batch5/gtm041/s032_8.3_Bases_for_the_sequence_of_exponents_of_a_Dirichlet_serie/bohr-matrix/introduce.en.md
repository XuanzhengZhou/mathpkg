A Bohr matrix is a rational square matrix (finite or infinite) in which each row has only finitely many nonzero entries. Named after Harald Bohr, these matrices provide a convenient algebraic formalism for expressing how the exponents $\Lambda = \{\lambda(n)\}$ of a Dirichlet series relate to a chosen basis $B = \{\beta(n)\}$ via $\Lambda = RB$. The finiteness condition on rows ensures that every $\lambda(n)$ is a finite rational linear combination of the basis elements, consistent with condition (b) in the definition of a basis.
