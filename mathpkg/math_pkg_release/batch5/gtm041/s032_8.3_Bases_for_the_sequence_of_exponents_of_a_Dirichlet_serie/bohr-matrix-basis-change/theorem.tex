Let $B$ and $\Gamma$ be two bases for $\Lambda$, and write $\Gamma = AB$, $\Lambda = R_B B$, $\Lambda = R_\Gamma \Gamma$, where $A$, $R_B$, $R_\Gamma$ are Bohr matrices. Then
$$R_B = R_\Gamma A.$$

If we write $\Lambda/B$ for $R_B$, $\Lambda/\Gamma$ for $R_\Gamma$ and $\Gamma/B$ for $A$, this may be expressed as
$$\frac{\Lambda}{B} = \frac{\Lambda}{\Gamma} \cdot \frac{\Gamma}{B}.$$
