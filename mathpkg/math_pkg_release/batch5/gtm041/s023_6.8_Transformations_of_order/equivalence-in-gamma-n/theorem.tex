Two transformations $A_1$ and $A_2$ in $\Gamma(n)$ are called \emph{equivalent}, and we write $A_1 \sim A_2$, if there is a transformation $V$ in $\Gamma$ such that
$$A_1 = V A_2.$$
The relation $\sim$ is obviously reflexive, symmetric, and transitive, and hence is an equivalence relation. Consequently, the set $\Gamma(n)$ can be partitioned into equivalence classes such that two elements of $\Gamma(n)$ are in the same class if, and only if, they are equivalent.
