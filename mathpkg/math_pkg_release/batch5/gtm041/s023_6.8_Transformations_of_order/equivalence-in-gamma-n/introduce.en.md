Two transformations of order $n$ are equivalent if one can be obtained from the other by left multiplication by an element of the modular group $\Gamma$. This equivalence relation partitions $\Gamma(n)$ into orbits under the left action of $\Gamma$, and the resulting equivalence classes are fundamental for constructing Hecke operators. The next theorem (Theorem 6.7) shows that each equivalence class contains a convenient triangular representative.
