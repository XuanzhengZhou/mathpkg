The set $\Gamma(n)$ consists of all $2 \times 2$ integer matrices with determinant $n$, modulo the identification $A \sim -A$. This generalizes the classical modular group $\Gamma = \Gamma(1)$ to arbitrary positive determinant. The structure of $\Gamma(n)$ under left multiplication by elements of $\Gamma$ forms the algebraic foundation for defining Hecke operators $T_n$ acting on modular forms.
