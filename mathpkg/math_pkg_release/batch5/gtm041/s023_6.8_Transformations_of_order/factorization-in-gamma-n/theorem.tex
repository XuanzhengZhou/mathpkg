If $A_1 \in \Gamma(n)$ and $V_1 \in \Gamma$, then there exist matrices $A_2$ in $\Gamma(n)$ and $V_2$ in $\Gamma$ such that
$$A_1 V_1 = V_2 A_2.$$
Moreover, if
$$A_i = \begin{pmatrix} a_i & b_i \\ 0 & d_i \end{pmatrix} \quad \text{and} \quad V_i = \begin{pmatrix} \alpha_i & \beta_i \\ \gamma_i & \delta_i \end{pmatrix}$$
for $i = 1, 2$, then we have
$$a_1 (\gamma_2 A_2 \tau + \delta_2) = a_2 (\gamma_1 \tau + \delta_1).$$
