Since $\det(A_1 V_1) = \det A_1 \det V_1 = n$, the matrix $A_1 V_1$ is in $\Gamma(n)$, so by Theorem 6.7, there exists $A_2$ in $\Gamma(n)$ and $V_2$ in $\Gamma$ such that
$$A_1 V_1 = V_2 A_2.$$
