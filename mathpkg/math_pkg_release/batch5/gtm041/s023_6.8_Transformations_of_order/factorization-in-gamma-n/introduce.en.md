Theorem 6.9 shows that the product of a transformation of order $n$ (on the left) with a modular transformation (on the right) can be refactored as a modular transformation (on the left) times a transformation of order $n$ (on the right). This "commutation up to equivalence" is the crucial algebraic lemma for proving that the Hecke operator $T_n$ maps modular forms to modular forms. The supplementary formula (23) relates the entries of the two factorizations under a modular change of variables.
