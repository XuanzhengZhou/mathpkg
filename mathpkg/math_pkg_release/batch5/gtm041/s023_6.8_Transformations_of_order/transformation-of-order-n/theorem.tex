Let $n$ be a fixed positive integer. A transformation of the form
$$A\tau = \frac{a\tau + b}{c\tau + d},$$
where $a, b, c, d$ are integers with $ad - bc = n$, is called a \emph{transformation of order $n$}. It can be represented by the $2 \times 2$ matrix
$$A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix},$$
where, as usual, we identify each matrix with its negative.
