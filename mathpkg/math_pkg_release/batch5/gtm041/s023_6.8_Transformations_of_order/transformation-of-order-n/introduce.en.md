A transformation of order $n$ generalizes the notion of a modular transformation (which has determinant $1$) by allowing the determinant to be an arbitrary positive integer $n$. These transformations, represented by $2 \times 2$ integer matrices with determinant $n$, are the basic building blocks for constructing Hecke operators $T_n$ on modular forms. The set of all such transformations is denoted $\Gamma(n)$, and understanding its structure under the action of the modular group $\Gamma = \Gamma(1)$ is essential for the theory of modular forms.
