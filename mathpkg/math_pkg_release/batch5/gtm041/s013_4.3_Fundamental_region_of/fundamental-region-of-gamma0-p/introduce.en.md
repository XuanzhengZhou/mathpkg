Theorem 4.2 gives an explicit description of a fundamental region for the congruence subgroup $\Gamma_0(p)$ of the full modular group $\Gamma$, where $p$ is any prime. The fundamental region is constructed by taking the standard fundamental region $R_\Gamma$ of $\Gamma$ together with its $p$ translates under the maps $ST^k$ for $k = 0, 1, \ldots, p-1$, where $S\tau = -1/\tau$ and $T\tau = \tau + 1$. This construction provides a concrete geometric picture of how $\Gamma_0(p)$ tessellates the upper half-plane $H$, and it underlies many subsequent computations involving modular forms for congruence subgroups.
