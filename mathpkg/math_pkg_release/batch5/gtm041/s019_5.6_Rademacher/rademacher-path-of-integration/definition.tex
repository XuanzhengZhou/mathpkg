For each integer $N$, let $F_N$ be the Farey series of order $N$. Consider the Ford circles for the fractions in $F_N$. If $h_1/k_1 < h/k < h_2/k_2$ are consecutive in $F_N$, the points of tangency of $C(h_1, k_1)$, $C(h, k)$, and $C(h_2, k_2)$ divide $C(h, k)$ into two arcs, an upper arc and a lower arc. The \textbf{Rademacher path} $P(N)$ is the union of the upper arcs so obtained. For the fractions $0/1$ and $1/1$ we use only the part of the upper arcs lying above the unit interval $[0, 1]$. The path $P(N)$ joins the points $i$ and $i+1$.
