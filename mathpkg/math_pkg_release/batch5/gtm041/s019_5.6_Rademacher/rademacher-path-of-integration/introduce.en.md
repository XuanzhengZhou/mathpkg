Rademacher's path $P(N)$ is a carefully constructed integration contour built from the upper arcs of Ford circles associated with the Farey series $F_N$. It was introduced by Hans Rademacher to refine the Hardy-Ramanujan circle method and obtain an exact convergent series for the partition function $p(n)$. The path joins $i$ to $i+1$ and, by Theorem 5.7, always lies above a certain horizontal row in the upper half-plane. Each arc forming $P(N)$ is the upper portion of a Ford circle $C(h,k)$ between its points of tangency with neighboring Ford circles.
