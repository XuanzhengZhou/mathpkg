Theorem 5.9 provides essential estimates for the images $z_1(h,k)$ and $z_2(h,k)$ of the Ford circle tangency points under the mapping $z = -ik^2(\tau - h/k)$. The moduli of $z_1$ and $z_2$ are expressed in terms of $k$, $k_1$, and $k_2$, where $h_1/k_1 < h/k < h_2/k_2$ are consecutive Farey fractions. When $z$ lies on the chord joining $z_1$ and $z_2$, its modulus is bounded by $\sqrt{2}k/N$, and the chord length itself is at most $2\sqrt{2}k/N$. These bounds are crucial for controlling the error terms in Rademacher's asymptotic analysis of the partition function.
