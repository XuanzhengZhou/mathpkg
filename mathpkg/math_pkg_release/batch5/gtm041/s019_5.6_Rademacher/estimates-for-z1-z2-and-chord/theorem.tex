For the points $z_1$ and $z_2$ defined in Theorem 5.8, we have
$$
|z_1(h, k)| = \frac{k}{\sqrt{k^2 + k_1^2}}, \qquad |z_2(h, k)| = \frac{k}{\sqrt{k^2 + k_2^2}}.
$$
Moreover, if $h_1/k_1 < h/k < h_2/k_2$ are consecutive in the Farey series $F_N$ and $z$ is on the chord joining $z_1$ and $z_2$, then
$$
|z| < \frac{\sqrt{2}\, k}{N}.
$$
The length of this chord does not exceed $2\sqrt{2}\, k / N$.
