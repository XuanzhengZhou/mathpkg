This theorem describes how a specific Möbius-type transformation $z = -ik^2(\tau - h/k)$ maps the Ford circle $C(h,k)$ to a standard circle $K$ of radius $1/2$ centered at $z = 1/2$. The transformation consists of a translation followed by a rotation and dilation. The images $z_1(h,k)$ and $z_2(h,k)$ of the tangency points $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ lie on $K$ and play a crucial role in Rademacher's contour integration, as they define the endpoints of the arc along which the partition generating function is integrated.
