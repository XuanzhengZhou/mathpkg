The discriminant $\Delta(\tau) = g_2^3 - 27g_3^2$ is not invariant under the full modular group $\Gamma$, but is a
modular form of weight 12. Under a general modular transformation $\tau \mapsto (a\tau+b)/(c\tau+d)$, it transforms by
the factor $(c\tau+d)^{12}$. In particular, $\Delta$ is periodic with period 1 and satisfies
$\Delta(-1/\tau) = \tau^{12}\Delta(\tau)$. This transformation law is fundamental to constructing the univalent function
$\varphi(\tau) = \Delta(q\tau)/\Delta(\tau)$ on $\Gamma_0(q)$.
