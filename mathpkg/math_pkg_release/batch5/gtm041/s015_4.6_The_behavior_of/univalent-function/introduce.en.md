A univalent function on a subgroup $G$ of the modular group is an automorphic function under $G$ with valence exactly 1.
Such functions are the analog of the modular invariant $J$ for the full modular group $\Gamma$; they generate
the field of all automorphic functions on $G$. The existence of univalent functions is tied to the genus of the
fundamental region $R_G$ being zero.
