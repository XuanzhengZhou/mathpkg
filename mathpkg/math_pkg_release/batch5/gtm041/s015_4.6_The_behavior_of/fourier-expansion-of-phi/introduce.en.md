This proposition gives the $q$-expansion of $\varphi(\tau) = \Delta(q\tau)/\Delta(\tau)$, where $\Delta$ is the
discriminant modular form of weight 12. Writing $x = e^{2\pi i\tau}$, the expansion is $\varphi(\tau) = x^{q-1}(1 + \sum b_n x^n)$
with integer coefficients $b_n$. This follows from the Fourier expansion $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} \sum \tau(n) x^n$,
and shows that $\varphi$ has a zero of order $q-1$ at $\infty$ and no poles in the upper half-plane.
