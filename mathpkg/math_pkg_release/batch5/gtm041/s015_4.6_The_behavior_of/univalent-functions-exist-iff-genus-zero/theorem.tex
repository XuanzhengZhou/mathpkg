It can be shown (using Riemann surfaces) that univalent functions exist on $G$ if and only if the genus of the fundamental region $R_G$ is zero. \text{[This is the topological genus of the surface obtained by identifying congruent edges of $R_G$. For example, the genus of $R_\Gamma$ is zero because $R_\Gamma$ is topologically equivalent to a sphere when its congruent edges are identified.]}
