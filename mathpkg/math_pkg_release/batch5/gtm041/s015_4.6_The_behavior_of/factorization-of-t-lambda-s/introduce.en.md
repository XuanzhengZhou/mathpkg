Lemma 2 provides the algebraic core of the proof of Theorem 4.6. It asserts that for each $\lambda \in \{1,\ldots,p-1\}$,
the composition $T_\lambda S$ (where $T_\lambda\tau = (\tau+\lambda)/p$ and $S\tau = -1/\tau$) can be refactored as $V T_\mu$
with $V \in \Gamma_0(p)$ and $\mu$ also in $\{1,\ldots,p-1\}$. The mapping $\lambda \mapsto \mu$ is a permutation of
$\{1,\ldots,p-1\}$. This lemma allows the sum over $\lambda$ in the definition of $f_p$ to be re-indexed after applying $S$.
