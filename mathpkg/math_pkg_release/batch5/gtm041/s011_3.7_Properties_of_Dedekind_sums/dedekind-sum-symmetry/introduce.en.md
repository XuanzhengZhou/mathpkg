Theorem 3.6 establishes the fundamental symmetry properties of Dedekind sums $s(h,k)$. Part (a) shows that $s(h,k)$ depends only on $h$ modulo $k$ up to sign, part (b) relates the Dedekind sum of $h$ to that of its modular inverse $\bar{h}$, and part (c) provides a sufficient condition for the vanishing of $s(h,k)$ in terms of the quadratic congruence $h^2 + 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{k}$.
