Let $G$ denote the subgroup of $\Gamma$ generated by the transformations $S$ and $T^2$, where $S\tau = -1/\tau$ and $T\tau = \tau + 1$.

(a) If $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in G$, prove that $a \equiv d \pmod{2}$ and $b \equiv c \pmod{2}$.

(b) If $V \in G$, prove that there exist elements $A$ and $B$ of $\Gamma$ such that

$$\frac{V\tau + 1}{2} = A\left(\frac{\tau + 1}{2}\right) \quad \text{and} \quad V\tau + 1 = B(\tau + 1).$$

(c) If $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in G$ and $c > 0$, prove that

$$\vartheta\left(\frac{a\tau + b}{c\tau + d}\right) = \varepsilon(a, b, c, d)\{-i(c\tau + d)\}^{1/2}\vartheta(\tau),$$

where $|\varepsilon(a, b, c, d)| = 1$. Express $\varepsilon(a, b, c, d)$ in terms of Dedekind sums.
