This congruence was proved by Morris Newman [30] in 1958. The proof is not reproduced in this text; the result is cited in a note preceding the Chapter 4 exercises. For the full proof, see Newman, M. (1958), "Congruences for the coefficients of modular forms and some new congruences for the partition function", *Canad. J. Math.*, 10, 49--66.

The method involves studying the action of Hecke operators on the space of modular forms and exploiting the fact that $j(\tau)$ is invariant under the full modular group $\Gamma = \text{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$. The appearance of the modular inverse $p^{-1}$ modulo $13$ reflects the transformation behavior of certain modular functions under the Fricke involution. Atkin and O'Brien [5] later generalized these congruences using the theory of modular forms modulo $p$ and the notion of "admissible" forms.
