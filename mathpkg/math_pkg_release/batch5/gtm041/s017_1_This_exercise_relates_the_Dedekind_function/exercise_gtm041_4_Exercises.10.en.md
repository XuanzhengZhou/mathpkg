Use Exercise 9 together with Exercise 10 of Chapter 6 to prove that

$$\frac{65520}{691} \left\{\sigma_{11}(n) - \tau(n)\right\} = \tau(n+1) + 24\tau(n) + \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} c(k)\tau(n-k).$$

This formula, due to Lehmer [20], can be used to determine the coefficients $c(n)$ recursively in terms of $\tau(n)$. Since the right member is an integer, the formula also implies Ramanujan's remarkable congruence

$$\tau(n) \equiv \sigma_{11}(n) \pmod{691}.$$
