These congruences were derived by Lehner [24] through repeated application of the modular transformation properties of $j(\tau)$ and the action of Hecke operators. The proof is not reproduced in this text; the result is cited in a note preceding the Chapter 4 exercises. For the full proof, see Lehner, J. (1949), "Further congruence properties of the Fourier coefficients of the modular invariant $j(\tau)$", *Amer. J. Math.*, 71, 373--386.

The method relies on the fact that $j(\tau)$ is a modular function of weight $0$ for the full modular group $\Gamma = \text{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$, and the coefficients $c(n)$ can be expressed in terms of singular moduli. The congruence properties are obtained by analyzing the $q$-expansion of $j(\tau)$ modulo prime powers and applying induction on $\alpha$.
