Ramanujan's congruence modulo 691 is one of the most famous congruences in the theory of modular forms. It states that the Ramanujan tau function $\tau(n)$ and the sum-of-11th-powers divisor function $\sigma_{11}(n)$ are congruent modulo 691 for all $n \geq 1$. The congruence was originally conjectured by Ramanujan and proved by Mordell using the theory of Hecke operators. It also follows from Lehmer's formula relating the coefficients $c(n)$ of the modular $j$-function to $\tau(n)$. The prime 691 appears as the numerator of the Bernoulli number $B_{12} = -691/2730$, reflecting deep connections between modular forms and Bernoulli numbers.
