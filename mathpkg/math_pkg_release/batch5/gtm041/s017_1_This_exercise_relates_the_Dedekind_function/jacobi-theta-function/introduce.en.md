The Jacobi theta function $\vartheta(\tau)$ is a holomorphic function on the upper half-plane $H$ that is periodic with period $2$. It is one of the classical Jacobi theta functions and plays a fundamental role in the theory of elliptic functions, modular forms, and number theory. The function can be expressed either as a bilateral series $\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} e^{\pi i n^2 \tau}$ or as $1 + 2\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} e^{\pi i n^2 \tau}$, highlighting its symmetry. It is closely related to the Dedekind eta function via Jacobi's triple product identity.
