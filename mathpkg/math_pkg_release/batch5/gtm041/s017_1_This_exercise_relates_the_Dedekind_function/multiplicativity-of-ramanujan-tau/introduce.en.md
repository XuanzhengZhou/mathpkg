Ramanujan's tau function $\tau(n)$ is a fundamental arithmetic function arising as the Fourier coefficients of the modular discriminant $\Delta(\tau)$. One of its most important properties is multiplicativity: $\tau(mn) = \tau(m)\tau(n)$ whenever $m$ and $n$ are coprime. More generally, the function satisfies a convolution identity involving the divisor sum over $d^{11}$. This theorem was first proved by Mordell in 1917, and it provided strong evidence for the broader theory of Hecke operators, which systematically explains why Fourier coefficients of certain modular forms possess multiplicative properties.
