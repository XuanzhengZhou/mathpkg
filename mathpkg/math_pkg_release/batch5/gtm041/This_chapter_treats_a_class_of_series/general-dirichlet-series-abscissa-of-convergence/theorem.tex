Assume the series $\sum a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$ converges for some $s$ with $\sigma > 0$ but diverges for all $s$ with $\sigma < 0$. Then the number
$$L = \limsup_{n \to \infty} \frac{\log \left|\sum_{k=1}^{n} a(k)\right|}{\lambda(n)}$$
is the abscissa of convergence of the series. In other words, the series converges for all $s$ with $\sigma > L$ and diverges for all $s$ with $\sigma < L$.
