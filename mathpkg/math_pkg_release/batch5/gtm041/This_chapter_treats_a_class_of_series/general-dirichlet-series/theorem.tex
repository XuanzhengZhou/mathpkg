Let $\{\lambda(n)\}$ be a strictly increasing sequence of real numbers such that $\lambda(n) \to +\infty$ as $n \to \infty$. A series of the form
$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$$
is called a general Dirichlet series. The numbers $\lambda(n)$ are called the exponents of the series, and the numbers $a(n)$ are called its coefficients.

As usual, we write $s = \sigma + it$ where $\sigma$ and $t$ are real.

\textit{Note.} When $\lambda(n) = \log n$ then $e^{-s\lambda(n)} = n^{-s}$ and we obtain the ordinary Dirichlet series $\sum a(n)n^{-s}$. When $\lambda(n) = n$ the series becomes a power series in $x$, where $x = e^{-s}$.

A general Dirichlet series is analogous to the Laplace transform of a function, $\int_0^\infty f(t)e^{-st}\,dt$. Both Dirichlet series and Laplace transforms are special cases of the Laplace-Stieltjes transform, $\int_0^\infty e^{-st}\,d\alpha(t)$. When $\alpha(t)$ has a continuous derivative $\alpha'(t) = f(t)$ this gives the Laplace transform of $f$. When $\alpha$ is a step function with jump $a(n)$ at the point $\lambda(n)$ the integral becomes the general Dirichlet series $\sum a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$.
