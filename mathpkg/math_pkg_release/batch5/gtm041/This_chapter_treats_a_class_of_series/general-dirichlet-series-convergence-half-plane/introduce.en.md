Theorem 8.1 of Apostol establishes the fundamental convergence criterion for general Dirichlet series. Given a series $\sum a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$ that converges at some $s_0$ with positive real part $\sigma_0 > 0$, the value $L = \limsup_{n \to \infty} (\log|\sum_{k=1}^n a(k)|)/\lambda(n)$ satisfies $L \leq \sigma_0$. The theorem further guarantees that the series converges for all $s$ with real part $\sigma > L$, with uniform convergence on every compact subset of that half-plane. This result expresses the abscissa of convergence directly in terms of the partial sums of the coefficients and the exponents $\lambda(n)$.
