A function $f$ is said to be an \textbf{entire modular form of weight $k$} if it satisfies the following conditions:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $f$ is analytic in the upper half-plane $H = \{\tau : \operatorname{Im}(\tau) > 0\}$.
\item[(b)] $$f\left(\frac{a\tau + b}{c\tau + d}\right) = (c\tau + d)^k f(\tau)$$ whenever $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$, where $\Gamma$ denotes the modular group.
\item[(c)] The Fourier expansion of $f$ has the form $$f(\tau) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c(n)e^{2\pi i n\tau}.$$
\end{enumerate}
