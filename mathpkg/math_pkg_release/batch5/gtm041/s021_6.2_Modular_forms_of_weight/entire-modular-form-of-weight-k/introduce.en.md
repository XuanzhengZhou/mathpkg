An entire modular form of weight $k$ is a function on the upper half-plane that is analytic everywhere (including at $i\infty$), transforms under the modular group $\Gamma$ with the factor $(c\tau+d)^k$, and admits a Fourier expansion in powers of $e^{2\pi i\tau}$ with nonnegative indices. The term "entire" distinguishes such forms from more general modular forms that may have poles in $H$ or at $i\infty$. Entire modular forms of weight 0 are necessarily constant, and nonconstant entire modular forms exist only for even $k \geq 4$.
