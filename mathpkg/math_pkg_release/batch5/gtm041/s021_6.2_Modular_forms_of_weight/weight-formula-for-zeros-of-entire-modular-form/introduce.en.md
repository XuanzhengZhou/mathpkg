Theorem 6.1 gives a fundamental relation between the weight $k$ of an entire modular form and the multiplicities of its zeros at the three special points of the fundamental region: $i$, $\rho = e^{2\pi i/3}$, and $i\infty$. The formula shows that the total number of zeros $N$ in the closure of $R_\Gamma$ is $\frac{k}{12}$ minus weighted contributions from the elliptic fixed points $i$ and $\rho$ and the cusp $i\infty$. This explains why nonconstant entire modular forms can exist only for even $k \geq 4$: for $k=2$ the right-hand side would be non-integral or negative. The term $\frac{k}{12}$ arises from the $(c\tau+d)^k$ factor in the functional equation, which contributes an extra logarithmic integral absent in the weight-0 case (Theorem 2.4).
