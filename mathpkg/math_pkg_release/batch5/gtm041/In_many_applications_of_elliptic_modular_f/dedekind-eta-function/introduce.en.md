The Dedekind eta function, introduced by Dedekind in 1877, is a fundamental modular form of weight 1/2 defined on the upper half-plane. It is given by $\eta(\tau) = e^{\pi i \tau/12} \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} (1 - e^{2\pi i n\tau})$. The eta function is analytic on $H$ and never vanishes there, as the infinite product converges absolutely and uniformly on compact subsets. It is closely related to the discriminant modular form via $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} \eta^{24}(\tau)$. The eta function satisfies simple transformation formulas under the generators of the modular group: under translation it picks up a 12th root of unity, and under inversion it transforms with a square-root factor.
