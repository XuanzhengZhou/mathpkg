The \textbf{Dedekind eta function} is defined in the upper half-plane $H = \{\tau : \text{Im}(\tau) > 0\}$ by the equation
$$\eta(\tau) = e^{\pi i \tau / 12} \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} (1 - e^{2 \pi i n \tau}).$$
The infinite product converges absolutely and is nonzero for all $\tau \in H$ since $|e^{2\pi i \tau}| < 1$. Moreover, $\eta(\tau)$ is analytic on $H$ because the convergence is uniform on compact subsets of $H$.
