Theorem 3.1 gives the transformation formula for the Dedekind eta function $\eta(\tau)$ under the modular inversion $S\tau = -1/\tau$, showing that $\eta(-1/\tau) = (-i\tau)^{1/2} \eta(\tau)$ for all $\tau$ in the upper half-plane $H$. This functional equation, together with the simpler periodicity relation $\eta(\tau+1) = e^{\pi i/12} \eta(\tau)$, completely describes the behavior of $\eta(\tau)$ under the generators $S$ and $T$ of the modular group $\Gamma$. The theorem is a special case of a more general functional equation (Theorem 3.4) for $\eta((a\tau+b)/(c\tau+d))$. The book presents two proofs: a short proof by C. L. Siegel using residue calculus (Section 3.2), and a second proof via a general functional equation. A third proof using conditionally convergent iterated series is outlined in the exercises.
