The Dedekind eta function $\eta(\tau)$ is defined on the upper half-plane $H = \{\tau \in \mathbb{C} : \text{Im}(\tau) > 0\}$ as an infinite product $\eta(\tau) = e^{\pi i \tau / 12} \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} (1 - e^{2 \pi i n \tau})$. Introduced by Dedekind in 1877, it plays a central role in the theory of elliptic modular functions and their applications to number theory. The infinite product converges absolutely and is nonzero for $\tau \in H$, and since the convergence is uniform on compact subsets of $H$, $\eta(\tau)$ is an analytic function on $H$. The eta function is closely related to the discriminant $\Delta(\tau)$ via $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} \eta^{24}(\tau)$.
