Theorem 8.19 provides an integral representation linking the Riemann zeta function to Liouville's function $\lambda(n)$. The formula expresses $\zeta(2s)/((s-1)\zeta(s))$ as a Mellin-type integral of the summatory function $C(x) = \sum_{n \leq x} \lambda(n)/n$. This representation is crucial for Turán's proof that $\zeta(s) \neq 0$ for $\sigma > 1/2$ under the assumption that $C(x) \geq 0$ for large $x$. The proof uses Abel's summation formula to relate the Dirichlet series $\sum \lambda(n) n^{-s}$ to the integral of $C(x)$, then replaces $s$ by $s-1$.
