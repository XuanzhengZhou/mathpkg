For any Dirichlet series $\sum a(n) n^{-s}$ with $a(n) \neq 0 \implies (n, k) = 1$, the twist by a Dirichlet character $\chi$ modulo $k$ preserves Bohr equivalence:
$$
\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{a(n)}{n^s} \sim \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{a(n)\chi(n)}{n^s}.
$$
In particular, for a Dirichlet $L$-series $L(s,\chi_1)$, twisting by another character $\chi_2$ modulo $k$ yields
$$
L(s,\chi_1) \sim L(s,\chi_1\chi_2).
$$
