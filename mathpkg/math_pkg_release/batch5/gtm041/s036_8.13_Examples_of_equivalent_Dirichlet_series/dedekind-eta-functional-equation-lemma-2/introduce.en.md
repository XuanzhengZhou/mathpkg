Lemma 2 handles the multiplier transformation under right multiplication by the inversion generator $S$. The proof is more involved than Lemma 1 because $S$ exchanges $c$ and $-d$, requiring the use of the Dedekind sum reciprocity law. The case split on the sign of $d$ arises from the branch of the square root in the transformation formula, leading to different phase factors $e^{\mp \pi i/4}$.
