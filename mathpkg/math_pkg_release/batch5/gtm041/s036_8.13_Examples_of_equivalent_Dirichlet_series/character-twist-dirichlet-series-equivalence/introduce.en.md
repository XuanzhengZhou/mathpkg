Theorem 8.17 shows that multiplying the coefficients of a Dirichlet series by a Dirichlet character $\chi(n)$ does not change its Bohr equivalence class, provided the original series has coefficients supported only on integers coprime to the modulus $k$ of $\chi$. This is a natural application of Theorem 8.12 (the characterization of equivalent ordinary Dirichlet series via completely multiplicative functions). The condition $(n,k)=1$ whenever $a(n)\neq 0$ guarantees that $|\chi(p)|=1$ for all primes $p$ dividing any $n$ with $a(n)\neq 0$, which is the key requirement of Theorem 8.12(b).
