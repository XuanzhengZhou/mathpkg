Hardy and Ramanujan refined their first-order asymptotic into a finite sum of oscillatory terms $P_k(n)$, each contributing successively smaller exponential corrections. Remarkably, since $p(n)$ is an integer and the error is $O(n^{-1/4})$, the finite sum yields the exact value of $p(n)$ for sufficiently large $n$ — a rare phenomenon where an asymptotic expansion delivers an exact result. However, Lehmer showed in 1937 that the corresponding infinite series diverges, which motivated Rademacher's convergent modification.
