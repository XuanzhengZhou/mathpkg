Theorem 5.6 characterizes when two Ford circles intersect. Each Ford circle $C(h,k)$ is centered at $(h/k, 1/(2k^2))$ with radius $1/(2k^2)$. The theorem shows that the geometric condition for tangency -- the circles touching at exactly one point or being disjoint -- is algebraically equivalent to the unimodular condition $|bc - ad| = 1$. Combined with Theorem 5.5, this implies Ford circles of consecutive Farey fractions are always tangent, forming a chain that defines Rademacher's optimal integration contour.
