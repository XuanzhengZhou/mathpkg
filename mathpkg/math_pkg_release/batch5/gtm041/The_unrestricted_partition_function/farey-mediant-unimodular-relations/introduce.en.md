Theorem 5.4 formalizes a fundamental algebraic property of consecutive Farey fractions: their mediant preserves the unimodular condition $bc - ad = 1$. Geometrically, this implies that the Ford circles corresponding to $a/b$ and $h/k$, and likewise $h/k$ and $c/d$, are tangent. This theorem provides the induction step for constructing all Farey sequences and directly connects Farey fractions to the geometry of Ford circles used in Rademacher's contour integration.
