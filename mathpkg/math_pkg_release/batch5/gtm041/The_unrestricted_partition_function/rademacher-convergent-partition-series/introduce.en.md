Rademacher's breakthrough was to slightly modify the Hardy-Ramanujan analysis so that the now-divergent sum of $P_k(n)$ terms is replaced by a convergent series of terms $R_k(n)$. This yields a genuinely exact formula for $p(n)$ valid for all $n$, not just asymptotically large values. The terms $R_k(n)$ involve Kloosterman sums $A_k(n)$ and modified Bessel functions. Rademacher's proof represents one of the crowning achievements of the circle method and elegantly exploits the modular properties of Dedekind's eta function.
