Theorem 5.7 provides the explicit complex coordinates of the tangency points between consecutive Ford circles. These coordinates are essential for Rademacher's refinement of the circle method: instead of integrating along a circular contour of radius nearly 1, Rademacher's path goes from $\alpha_1(h,k)$ to $\alpha_2(h,k)$ along the arc of $C(h,k)$ for each Farey fraction $h/k$, producing the convergent terms $R_k(n)$. The geometric fact that $\alpha_1(h,k)$ lies on a semicircle above the real axis connects the complex integration to the modular transformation properties of the Dedekind eta function.
