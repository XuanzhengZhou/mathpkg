Every Möbius transformation is a bijection of the extended complex plane (Riemann sphere) $C^* = C \cup \{\infty\}$ onto itself. Injectivity follows directly from the difference formula $f(w)-f(z) = (ad-bc)(w-z)/((cw+d)(cz+d))$, and surjectivity is established by solving $f(z) = w$ for $z$, yielding the inverse transformation $z = (dw-b)/(-cw+a)$ which is itself a Möbius transformation. This property makes Möbius transformations the automorphisms of the Riemann sphere.
