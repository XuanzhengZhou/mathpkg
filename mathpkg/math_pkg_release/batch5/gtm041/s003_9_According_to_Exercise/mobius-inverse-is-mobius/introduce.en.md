Solving the equation $w = (az+b)/(cz+d)$ for $z$ yields $z = (dw-b)/(-cw+a)$, showing that the inverse of a Möbius transformation is again a Möbius transformation. Together with the fact that the composition of two Möbius transformations is also one, this implies that Möbius transformations form a group under composition — the Möbius group, isomorphic to $\text{PGL}(2,\mathbb{C})$.
