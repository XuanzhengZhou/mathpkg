Since multiplying all four coefficients $a,b,c,d$ by the same nonzero constant leaves the fraction $(az+b)/(cz+d)$ unchanged, a Möbius transformation only depends on the ratios of its coefficients. This allows us to normalize the transformation so that $ad-bc = 1$, which is the standard convention when studying the modular group. Under this normalization, Möbius transformations correspond to elements of $\text{SL}(2,\mathbb{C})$.
