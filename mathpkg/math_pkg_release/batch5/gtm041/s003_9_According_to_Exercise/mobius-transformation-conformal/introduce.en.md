A Möbius transformation is conformal (angle-preserving) at every point of the extended complex plane except its pole. This follows directly from the fact that $f'(z) = (ad-bc)/(cz+d)^2$ never vanishes on the domain of analyticity, which is a necessary and sufficient condition for conformality of a holomorphic function. Together with bijectivity, this makes Möbius transformations the conformal automorphisms of the Riemann sphere.
