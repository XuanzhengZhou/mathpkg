A Möbius transformation is a fractional linear transformation $f(z) = (az+b)/(cz+d)$ with $ad-bc \neq 0$, defined on the extended complex plane $C^* = C \cup \{\infty\}$. These transformations are fundamental in complex analysis and appear naturally in the study of the modular group, where the coefficients $a,b,c,d$ are integers with $ad-bc=1$. Möbius transformations are analytic everywhere on $C^*$ except at a simple pole, and they form a group under composition.
