Theorem 6.20 of Apostol is a landmark result in Hecke's theory of modular forms. Given a modular form $f \in M_k$ ($k \geq 4$ even) with Fourier coefficients $c(n)$, the associated Dirichlet series $\varphi(s) = \sum c(n)/n^s$ enjoys an analytic continuation beyond its initial half-plane of convergence. When $c(0) = 0$ (the cusp form case), $\varphi(s)$ extends to an entire function; otherwise it is meromorphic with a single simple pole at $s = k$. Moreover, the completed $L$-function $(2\pi)^{-s}\Gamma(s)\varphi(s)$ satisfies the symmetric functional equation relating $s$ and $k-s$, generalizing the classical functional equation of the Riemann zeta function.
