Theorem 6.19 of Apostol establishes that if the Fourier coefficients $c(n)$ of a modular form satisfy a certain multiplicative convolution identity, then the associated Dirichlet series factors into an Euler product over primes. The key step uses the prime-power recurrence $c(p)c(p^n) = c(p^{n+1}) + p^{2k-1}c(p^{n-1})$ to derive a formal power series identity which, after substituting $x = p^{-s}$, yields the Euler product formula $\varphi(s) = \prod_p (1 - c(p)p^{-s} + p^{2k-1-2s})^{-1}$. This result is a central link between the arithmetic of Fourier coefficients and the analytic structure of Dirichlet series.
