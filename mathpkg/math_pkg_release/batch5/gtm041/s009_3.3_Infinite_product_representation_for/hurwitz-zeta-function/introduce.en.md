The Hurwitz zeta function $\zeta(s,\alpha)$ generalizes the Riemann zeta function by introducing a shift parameter $\alpha$: $\zeta(s,\alpha) = \sum_{r=0}^{\infty} (r+\alpha)^{-s}$ for $\operatorname{Re}(s) > 1$ and $0 < \alpha \leq 1$. It admits meromorphic continuation to the whole complex $s$-plane with a simple pole at $s=1$ of residue $1$, and satisfies a functional equation relating $\zeta(s,\alpha)$ to the periodic zeta function. The Hurwitz zeta function plays a central role in the analytic proof of Iseki's transformation formula, where it arises naturally from the Mellin transform of the exponential series.
