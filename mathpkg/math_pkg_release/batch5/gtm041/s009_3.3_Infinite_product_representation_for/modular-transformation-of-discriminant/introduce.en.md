The discriminant $\Delta(\tau)$ is a modular form of weight $12$ for the full modular group $\Gamma = \operatorname{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$. Theorem 3.2 states that under any modular transformation $\tau \mapsto (a\tau+b)/(c\tau+d)$ with $\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$, the discriminant transforms with the automorphy factor $(c\tau+d)^{12}$. This property is essential for expressing $\Delta(\tau)$ as a constant multiple of the $24$th power of the Dedekind eta function $\eta(\tau)$.
