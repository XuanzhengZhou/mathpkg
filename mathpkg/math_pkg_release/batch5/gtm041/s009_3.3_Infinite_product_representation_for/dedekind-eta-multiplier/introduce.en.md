The multiplier $\varepsilon(a,b,c,d)$ is a $24$th root of unity that appears in Dedekind's functional equation for $\eta(\tau)$. It is defined in terms of the matrix entries $a,d,c$ and the Dedekind sum $s(-d,c)$. The expression $\frac{a+d}{12c}$ reflects the arithmetic of the modular group, and together with $s(-d,c)$ it encodes the precise transformation behavior of the eta function under $\operatorname{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$. This multiplier system is a fundamental object in the theory of modular forms of half-integral weight.
