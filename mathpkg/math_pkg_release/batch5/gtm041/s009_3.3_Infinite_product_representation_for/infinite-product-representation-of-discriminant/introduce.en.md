Theorem 3.3 establishes the fundamental identity linking the discriminant $\Delta(\tau)$ to the Dedekind eta function: $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} \eta^{24}(\tau)$. Written in terms of $x = e^{2\pi i\tau}$, this gives the infinite product $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} x \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} (1-x^n)^{24}$. As an immediate corollary, the generating function of Ramanujan's tau function $\tau(n)$ admits the same product representation.
