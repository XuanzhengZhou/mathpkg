The Dedekind sum $s(h,k)$ is an arithmetic function of two coprime integers $h$ and $k$ (with $k > 0$) that appears in the multiplier system of the Dedekind eta function. It is defined as a finite sum involving fractional parts: $s(h,k) = \sum_{r=1}^{k-1} ((r/k))((hr/k))$, where $((x)) = x - \lfloor x \rfloor - 1/2$ is the sawtooth function. Dedekind sums satisfy a remarkable reciprocity law and play a role in number theory, modular forms, and the geometry of numbers.
