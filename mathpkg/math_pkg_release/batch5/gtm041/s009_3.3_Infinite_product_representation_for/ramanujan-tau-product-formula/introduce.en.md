As an immediate consequence of the identity $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} \eta^{24}(\tau)$ and the Fourier expansion $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} \sum \tau(n) x^n$, the generating function of Ramanujan's tau function admits an elegant product representation: $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \tau(n) x^n = x \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} (1-x^n)^{24}$ for $|x| < 1$. This formula plays a central role in the arithmetic theory of modular forms and underpins many conjectures about the tau function, including the celebrated Ramanujan conjectures.
