The periodic zeta function $F(x,s) = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} e^{2\pi i m x} / m^s$ is the Fourier series analogue of the Hurwitz zeta function, convergent for $\operatorname{Re}(s) > 1$ and real $x$. When $x$ is an integer it reduces to the Riemann zeta function $\zeta(s)$; for non-integer $x$ it is an entire function of $s$. Together with the Hurwitz zeta function, $F(x,s)$ forms a dual pair related by the Hurwitz functional equation and appears in the contour integral representation of $\Lambda(\alpha,\beta,z)$ in the proof of Iseki's formula.
