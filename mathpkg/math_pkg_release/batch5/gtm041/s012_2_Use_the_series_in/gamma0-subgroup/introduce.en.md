The congruence subgroup $\Gamma_0(q)$ consists of all modular group matrices whose lower-left entry $c$ is divisible by $q$. It is a subgroup of the full modular group $\Gamma = \text{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z})$ and plays a fundamental role in the theory of modular forms. For a prime $p$, $\Gamma_0(p)$ has finite index in $\Gamma$, which allows one to express every element of $\Gamma$ as a product of elements of $\Gamma_0(p)$ and translations. This subgroup is crucial for studying congruences of modular form coefficients because functions invariant under $\Gamma_0(q)$ can be expressed as rational functions of certain basic modular functions.
