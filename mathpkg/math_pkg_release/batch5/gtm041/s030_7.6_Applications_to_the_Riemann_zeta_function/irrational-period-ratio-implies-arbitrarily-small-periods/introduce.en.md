When a meromorphic function $f$ possesses two periods $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$ whose ratio is irrational, the additive group generated by these periods is dense in a line (if the ratio is real) or in the plane (if the ratio is complex). Applying Kronecker's theorem or Dirichlet's approximation theorem, one can construct integer combinations $k\omega_2 - h\omega_1$ that are arbitrarily small but nonzero, yielding periods of arbitrarily small modulus.
