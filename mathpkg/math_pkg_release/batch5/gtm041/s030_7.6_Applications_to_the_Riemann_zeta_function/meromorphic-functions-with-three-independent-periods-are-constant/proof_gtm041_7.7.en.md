By Theorem 7.13, if $f$ has three periods $\omega_1, \omega_2, \omega_3$ which are linearly independent over the integers, then $f$ has arbitrarily small nonzero periods. In Chapter 1 it was proved that a meromorphic function having arbitrarily small nonzero periods is constant on every open connected subset in which it is analytic. Hence $f$ must be constant on each such component. In particular, any non-constant meromorphic function can have at most two periods that are linearly independent over the integers.
