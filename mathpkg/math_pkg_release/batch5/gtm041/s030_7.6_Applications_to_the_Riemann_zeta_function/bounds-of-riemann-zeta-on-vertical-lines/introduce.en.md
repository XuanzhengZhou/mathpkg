For any fixed $\sigma > 1$, the supremum $M(\sigma)$ of $|\zeta(\sigma + it)|$ over all real $t$ is attained on the real axis and equals $\zeta(\sigma)$. The infimum $m(\sigma)$ is given by the ratio $\zeta(2\sigma)/\zeta(\sigma)$, and its proof relies on Kronecker's approximation theorem to construct values of $t$ for which the Euler factors $|1 - p_k^{-s}|$ simultaneously approximate $1 + p_k^{-\sigma}$ from below.
