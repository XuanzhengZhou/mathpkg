This result establishes the optimality of the constant $\sqrt{5}$ in Hurwitz's approximation theorem. The golden ratio $\alpha = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ is the irrational number most resistant to rational approximation: for any constant $A > \sqrt{5}$, the inequality $|\alpha - h/k| < 1/(Ak^2)$ has only finitely many solutions. This connects to the theory of continued fractions, where $\alpha = [1; 1, 1, 1, \ldots]$ has the slowest-converging continued fraction expansion, and to the Markov spectrum in Diophantine approximation.
