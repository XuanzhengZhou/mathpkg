Let $\alpha = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}$. For any $A > \sqrt{5}$, there exist only finitely many rational numbers $h/k$ with $k > 0$ such that

$$\left|\alpha - \frac{h}{k}\right| < \frac{1}{A k^2}.$$

Equivalently, the constant $\sqrt{5}$ in Hurwitz's theorem

$$\left|\theta - \frac{h}{k}\right| < \frac{1}{\sqrt{5} k^2}$$

is best possible, with $\alpha = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ being extremal.
