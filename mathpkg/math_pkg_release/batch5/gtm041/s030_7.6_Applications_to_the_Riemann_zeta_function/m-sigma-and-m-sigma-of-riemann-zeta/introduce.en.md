For a fixed real value $\sigma$, the quantities $m(\sigma)$ and $M(\sigma)$ represent the infimum and supremum, respectively, of the absolute value $|\zeta(\sigma + it)|$ of the Riemann zeta function along the vertical line $\sigma = \text{constant}$, taken over all real values of $t$. These functions capture the extremal behavior of the zeta function on vertical lines and are fundamental to understanding the growth and oscillation of $\zeta(s)$ in the critical strip and beyond.
