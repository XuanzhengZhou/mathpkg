Let $\theta$ be irrational, $0 < \theta < 1$. Then $\theta$ lies between two consecutive Farey fractions, say
$$\frac{a}{b} < \theta < \frac{c}{d}.$$

(a) Prove that either $\theta - a/b < 1/(2b^2)$ or $c/d - \theta < 1/(2d^2)$.

(b) Deduce that there exist infinitely many fractions $h/k$ with $(h, k) = 1$ and $k > 0$ such that
$$\left| \theta - \frac{h}{k} \right| < \frac{1}{2k^2}.$$
