This is an extension of Dirichlet's classical approximation theorem from one real number to $n$ real numbers simultaneously. Using the pigeonhole principle on the $n$-dimensional unit cube, one finds an integer $k \leq N^n$ such that all fractional parts $\{k\theta_i\}$ are within $1/N$ of 0. This result is a quantitative precursor to Kronecker's theorem and is essential for understanding simultaneous Diophantine approximation.
