When a meromorphic function $f$ has three periods $\omega_1, \omega_2, \omega_3$ that are linearly independent over the integers, one can express $\omega_3$ as a real linear combination of $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$ and consider three cases based on the rationality of the coefficients. In each nontrivial case, Kronecker's theorem (or its one-dimensional variant) yields integer combinations of the periods with arbitrarily small nonzero modulus. This result implies that such a function must be constant on any open connected subset where it is analytic.
