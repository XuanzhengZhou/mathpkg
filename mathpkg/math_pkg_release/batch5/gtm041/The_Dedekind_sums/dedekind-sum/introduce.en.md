The Dedekind sum $s(h,k)$ is an arithmetic function of two coprime positive integers that arises in the transformation formula for the Dedekind eta function $\eta(\tau)$. It is defined as a finite sum involving the fractional part function. Dedekind sums appear in many areas of number theory, including the theory of modular forms, quadratic reciprocity, and the geometry of lattice points.
