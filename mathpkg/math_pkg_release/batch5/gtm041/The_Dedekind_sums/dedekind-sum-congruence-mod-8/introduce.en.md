Theorem 3.9 provides an explicit congruence for $12k\,s(h,k)$ modulo $8$ in terms of the integer part of $2hr/k$. When $k$ is odd, the congruence simplifies significantly because $4h(k-1) \equiv 0 \pmod{8}$ and $(k-1)(k+2) \equiv k-1 \pmod{8}$. This result is a key step in determining the exact power of $2$ dividing expressions involving Dedekind sums, which is needed for studying the multiplier system of the Dedekind eta function.
