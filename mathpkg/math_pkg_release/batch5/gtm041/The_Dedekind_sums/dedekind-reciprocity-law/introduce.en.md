The Dedekind reciprocity law is the most important identity satisfied by Dedekind sums. It relates $s(h,k)$ and $s(k,h)$ through a simple rational function of $h$ and $k$, analogous to the quadratic reciprocity law for Legendre symbols. The reciprocity law was discovered by Dedekind in his study of the transformation properties of the eta function $\eta(\tau)$. A congruence version of the law (part (b)) is also essential for studying the multiplier system of modular forms.
