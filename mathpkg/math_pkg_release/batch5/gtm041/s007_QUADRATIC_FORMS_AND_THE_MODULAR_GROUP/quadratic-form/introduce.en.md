A quadratic form in two variables is a homogeneous polynomial of degree two. The general binary quadratic form is expressed as $Q(x, y) = ax^2 + bxy + cy^2$ with real coefficients $a, b, c$. Such forms arise naturally in number theory when studying representations of integers and their behavior under unimodular transformations.
