The discriminant $d = 4ac - b^2$ is a fundamental invariant of a binary quadratic form $Q(x,y) = ax^2 + bxy + cy^2$. It determines many properties of the form, including whether it is definite or indefinite, and remains invariant under unimodular transformations. The discriminant plays a central role in the classification of quadratic forms and their connection to the modular group.
