For a binary quadratic form with positive discriminant and positive leading and trailing coefficients, the associated quadratic polynomial $f(z) = az^2 + bz + c$ has two complex roots. The root lying in the upper half-plane is called the representative of the quadratic form. This establishes a connection between quadratic forms and points in the upper half-plane, which is fundamental for linking quadratic forms to the modular group.
