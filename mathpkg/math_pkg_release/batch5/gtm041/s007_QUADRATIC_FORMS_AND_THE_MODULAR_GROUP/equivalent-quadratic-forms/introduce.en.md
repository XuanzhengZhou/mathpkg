Two quadratic forms are equivalent if one can be transformed into the other by a unimodular change of variables from the modular group $\Gamma$. This equivalence relation partitions all quadratic forms into equivalence classes. Forms in the same class share the same discriminant and represent the same integers, making this concept fundamental for the arithmetic theory of quadratic forms.
