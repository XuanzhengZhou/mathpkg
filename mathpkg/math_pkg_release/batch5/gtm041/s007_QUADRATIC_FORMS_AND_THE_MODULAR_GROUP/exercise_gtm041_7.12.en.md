Prove that the quotient group $\Gamma/\Gamma^{(n)}$ is finite. That is, there exist a finite number of elements of $\Gamma$, say $A_1, \ldots, A_k$, such that every $B$ in $\Gamma$ is representable in the form
$$B = A_i B^{(n)}$$
where $1 \leq i \leq k$ and $B^{(n)} \in \Gamma^{(n)}$.

The smallest such $k$ is called the index of $\Gamma^{(n)}$ in $\Gamma$.
