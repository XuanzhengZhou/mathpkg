The equivalence relation $A \equiv B \pmod{n}$ partitions $\Gamma$ into equivalence classes. Since
$$A \equiv B \pmod{n} \iff AB^{-1} \equiv I \pmod{n} \iff AB^{-1} \in \Gamma^{(n)},$$
two matrices are congruent modulo $n$ if and only if they lie in the same left coset of $\Gamma^{(n)}$. Equivalently, by normality, they lie in the same right coset.

Thus the equivalence classes of matrices modulo $n$ are precisely the cosets of $\Gamma^{(n)}$ in $\Gamma$. By definition, the index $[\Gamma : \Gamma^{(n)}]$ is the number of distinct cosets. Therefore the index equals the number of equivalence classes of matrices in $\Gamma$ modulo $n$.
