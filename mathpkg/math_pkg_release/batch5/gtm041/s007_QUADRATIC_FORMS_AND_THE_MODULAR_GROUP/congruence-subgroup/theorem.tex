Let $\Gamma = \text{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z})$ be the modular group and let $n$ be a positive integer. The congruence subgroup of level $n$ (German: Stufe $n$), denoted $\Gamma^{(n)}$, is the set of all matrices $A \in \Gamma$ such that $A \equiv I \pmod{n}$, i.e.,
$$\Gamma^{(n)} = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma : a \equiv d \equiv 1,\; b \equiv c \equiv 0 \pmod{n} \right\}.$$
