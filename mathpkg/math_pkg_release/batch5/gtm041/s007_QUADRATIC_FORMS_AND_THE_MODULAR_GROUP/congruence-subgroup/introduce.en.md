The congruence subgroup $\Gamma^{(n)}$ of level $n$ consists of all matrices in the modular group $\Gamma = \text{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z})$ that are congruent to the identity matrix modulo $n$. This is the principal congruence subgroup and is the kernel of the natural reduction homomorphism $\text{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z}) \to \text{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})$. Congruence subgroups are of central importance in the theory of modular forms.
