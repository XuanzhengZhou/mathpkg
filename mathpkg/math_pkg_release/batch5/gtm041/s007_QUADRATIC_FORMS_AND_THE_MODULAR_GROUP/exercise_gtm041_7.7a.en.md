Consider forms $ax^2 + bxy + cy^2$ with $d > 0$, $a > 0$, and $c > 0$. The associated quadratic polynomial $f(z) = az^2 + bz + c$ has two complex roots. The root $\tau$ with positive imaginary part is called the representative of the quadratic form $Q(x, y) = ax^2 + bxy + cy^2$.

If $d$ is fixed, prove that there is a one-to-one correspondence between the set of forms with discriminant $d$ and the set of complex numbers $\tau$ with $\text{Im}(\tau) > 0$.
