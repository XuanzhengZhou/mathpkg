The quotient group $\Gamma/\Gamma^{(n)}$ of the modular group by a principal congruence subgroup is finite. This follows from the fact that $\Gamma/\Gamma^{(n)}$ embeds into the finite group $\text{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})$ via the reduction homomorphism modulo $n$. The finiteness of this quotient is essential for defining modular forms via finite group actions.
