Let $A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ and $B = \begin{pmatrix} \alpha & \beta \\ \gamma & \delta \end{pmatrix}$ be two unimodular matrices (elements of $\Gamma$, not identifying a matrix with its negative). For a positive integer $n$, write
$$A \equiv B \pmod{n}$$
whenever $a \equiv \alpha$, $b \equiv \beta$, $c \equiv \gamma$, and $d \equiv \delta \pmod{n}$. This defines an equivalence relation with the property that if $A_1 \equiv A_2 \pmod{n}$ and $B_1 \equiv B_2 \pmod{n}$, then
$$A_1B_1 \equiv A_2B_2 \pmod{n} \quad\text{and}\quad A_1^{-1} \equiv A_2^{-1} \pmod{n}.$$
Consequently, $A \equiv B \pmod{n}$ if and only if $AB^{-1} \equiv I \pmod{n}$, where $I$ is the identity matrix.
