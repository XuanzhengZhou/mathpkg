Prove that $\Gamma^{(n)}$ is a subgroup of $\Gamma$. Moreover, if $B \in \Gamma^{(n)}$ then $A^{-1}BA \in \Gamma^{(n)}$ for every $A$ in $\Gamma$. That is, $\Gamma^{(n)}$ is a normal subgroup of $\Gamma$.
