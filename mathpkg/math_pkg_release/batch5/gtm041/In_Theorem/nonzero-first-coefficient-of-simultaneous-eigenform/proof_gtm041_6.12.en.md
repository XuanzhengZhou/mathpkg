The coefficient of $x$ in the Fourier expansion of $T_n f$ is $\gamma_n(1) = c(n)$, as shown by formula (36). Since $f$ is a simultaneous eigenform, we have $T_n f = \lambda(n) f$ for all $n \geq 1$. Equating the coefficients of $x^m$ in the Fourier expansions, we obtain

$$c(n) = \lambda(n) c(1)$$

for all $n \geq 1$. If $c(1) = 0$, then $c(n) = 0$ for all $n \geq 1$, implying that the constant term $c(0)$ is the only possibly nonzero coefficient. But if $f$ is a cusp form, then $c(0) = 0$ as well, which would force $f = 0$, contradicting the definition of an eigenform. Even if $f$ is not a cusp form, the vanishing of all $c(n)$ for $n \geq 1$ would mean $f$ is constant, and one can verify that no nonzero constant function in $M_k$ can be a simultaneous eigenform for all $T_n$. Therefore $c(1) \neq 0$.
