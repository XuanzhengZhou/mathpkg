Let $f \in M_{k,0}$ be a cusp form with Fourier expansion
$$f(\tau) = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} c(m)x^m, \quad x = e^{2\pi i\tau},$$
and suppose $c(1) = 1$ (so $f$ is \textit{normalized}). Then $f$ is a normalized simultaneous eigenform (i.e., $T_n f = c(n) f$ for all $n \geq 1$) if and only if the Fourier coefficients satisfy the multiplicative relation
$$c(m)c(n) = \sum_{d \mid (m,n)} d^{\,k-1} \, c\!\left(\frac{mn}{d^2}\right)$$
for all $m \geq 1$, $n \geq 1$.
