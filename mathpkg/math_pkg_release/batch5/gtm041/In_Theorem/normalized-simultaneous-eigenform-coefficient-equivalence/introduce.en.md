Theorem 6.15 gives the precise equivalence between being a normalized simultaneous eigenform and having multiplicatively related Fourier coefficients. For a cusp form $f$ with $c(1) = 1$, the condition $T_n f = c(n) f$ for all $n$ is equivalent to the identity $c(m)c(n) = \sum_{d|(m,n)} d^{k-1} c(mn/d^2)$. This explains why Ramanujan's $\tau$ function and the divisor functions $\sigma_\alpha(n)$ exhibit multiplicative behavior -- they arise as eigenvalues of Hecke operators acting on normalized simultaneous eigenforms such as the discriminant $\Delta$ and Eisenstein series.
