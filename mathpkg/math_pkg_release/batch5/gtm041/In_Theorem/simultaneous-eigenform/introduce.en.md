A simultaneous eigenform is a modular form that is an eigenfunction of every Hecke operator $T_n$ simultaneously. All the one-dimensional space examples mentioned above (Eisenstein series $G_4, G_6, G_8, G_{10}, G_{14}$ and cusp forms $\Delta, \Delta G_4, \Delta G_6, \Delta G_8, \Delta G_{10}, \Delta G_{14}$) are simultaneous eigenforms. The theory of simultaneous eigenforms is fundamental to the connection between modular forms and Dirichlet series with Euler products.
