Assume that $f \in M_{2k}$, where $k \geq 2$, and that $f$ is not a cusp form (i.e., the constant term $c(0) \neq 0$). Then $f$ is a normalized simultaneous eigenform if and only if
$$f(\tau) = \frac{(2k-1)!}{2(2\pi i)^{2k}} \, G_{2k}(\tau) = \frac{(2k-1)!}{(2\pi i)^{2k}} \,\zeta(2k) + \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \sigma_{2k-1}(m)\,x^m,$$
where $G_{2k}$ is the Eisenstein series of weight $2k$ and $\sigma_{2k-1}(m) = \sum_{d \mid m} d^{2k-1}$. Equivalently, the Fourier coefficients are given by
$$c(n) = \sigma_{2k-1}(n)$$
for all $n \geq 1$, and the constant term is
$$c(0) = \frac{(2k-1)!}{(2\pi i)^{2k}} \,\zeta(2k) = -\frac{B_{2k}}{4k},$$
where $B_{2k}$ is the $2k$-th Bernoulli number.
