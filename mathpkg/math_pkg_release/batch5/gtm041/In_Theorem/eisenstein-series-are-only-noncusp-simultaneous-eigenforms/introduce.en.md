Theorem 6.16 completely classifies the simultaneous eigenforms in $M_{2k}$ ($k \geq 2$) that are not cusp forms: they are precisely the constant multiples of the Eisenstein series $G_{2k}$. The normalized such form has Fourier coefficients $c(n) = \sigma_{2k-1}(n)$, the sum of $(2k-1)$-st powers of divisors of $n$. This explains why the divisor functions $\sigma_\alpha(n)$ are multiplicative and arise naturally in the theory of modular forms. Combined with the earlier results on cusp forms (e.g., the discriminant $\Delta$), this theorem shows that the space of simultaneous eigenforms decomposes into the Eisenstein series and cusp forms such as $\Delta$.
