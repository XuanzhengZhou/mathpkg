A general Dirichlet series is a series of the form $\sum a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$ where $\{\lambda(n)\}$ is a strictly increasing sequence of real numbers diverging to $+\infty$. This construction unifies ordinary Dirichlet series (when $\lambda(n) = \log n$) and power series (when $\lambda(n) = n$). The theory is developed by analogy with the Laplace--Stieltjes transform, where the coefficients $a(n)$ correspond to jumps of a step function $\alpha(t)$ at the points $\lambda(n)$. Each general Dirichlet series has an associated abscissa of convergence $\sigma_c$ and abscissa of absolute convergence $\sigma_a$, which are studied in the theorems that follow.
