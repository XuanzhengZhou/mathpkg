A modular function is a meromorphic function on the upper half-plane that is invariant under the action of the modular group and possesses a Fourier expansion with at worst a pole at infinity. This definition is the central object of study in the theory of elliptic modular functions. The modular invariant $J$ is the prototypical example: it is analytic in the upper half-plane with a first order pole at $i\infty$.
