Theorem 4.10 provides a functional equation relating the Hecke-averaged $j_p$ at reciprocally related cusps: $j_p(-1/(p\tau)) = j_p(p\tau) + \frac{1}{p}j(p^2\tau) - \frac{1}{p}j(\tau)$. The Fourier expansion reveals that $p j_p(-1/(p\tau))$ has leading term $x^{-p^2}$ and constant term $-x^{-1}$ plus integer-coefficient power series. By comparing this with the behavior of $\psi(\tau) = p^{r/2}\Phi(-1/(p\tau))$, whose reciprocal has expansion $x^{-1}+I(x)$, one constructs a sequence of integer subtractions that eventually cancels all polar terms, expressing $p j_p$ as a monic polynomial in $\psi$ of degree $p^2$ with integer coefficients.
