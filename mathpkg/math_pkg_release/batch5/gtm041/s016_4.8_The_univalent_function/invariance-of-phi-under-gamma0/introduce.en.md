Theorem 4.9 establishes that for the primes $q=2,3,5,7,13$, the univalent function $\Phi(\tau)=(\eta(q\tau)/\eta(\tau))^r$ with $r=24/(q-1)$ is invariant under the action of the congruence subgroup $\Gamma_0(q)$. The case $q=2$ reduces to Theorem 4.7. For $q\ge 3$, the proof computes the automorphy factor arising from the Dedekind eta transformation formula and shows that the resulting phase factor $e^{-\pi i r\delta}$ equals $1$ because $r\delta$ is an even integer — a consequence of the Dedekind sum reciprocity law (Theorem 3.11) and the fact that $ad\equiv 1$ modulo both $c$ and $c_1$.
