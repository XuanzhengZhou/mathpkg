Theorem 4.5 provides a fundamental construction for producing modular forms for the congruence subgroup $\Gamma_0(p)$ from modular forms for the full modular group $\Gamma$. The operator $f \mapsto f_p$ defined by averaging over the $p$ translations $\tau \mapsto (\tau + \lambda)/p$ can be viewed as a Hecke-type trace operator. The resulting function $f_p$ is automorphic under $\Gamma_0(p)$, and its $q$-expansion ($q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$) is obtained by extracting every $p$-th coefficient from the original expansion of $f$.
