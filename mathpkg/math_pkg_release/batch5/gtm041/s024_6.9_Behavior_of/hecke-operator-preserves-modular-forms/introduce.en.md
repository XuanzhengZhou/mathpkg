Theorem 6.11 establishes that each Hecke operator $T_n$ maps the space $M_k$ of modular forms of weight $k$ into itself, and also preserves the subspace $M_{k,0}$ of cusp forms. The proof combines three ingredients: analyticity from the definition of $T_n$, the Fourier expansion from Theorem 6.6 showing the correct behavior at $i\infty$, and the modular transformation property from Theorem 6.10. This result confirms that Hecke operators are endomorphisms of the relevant spaces of modular forms.
