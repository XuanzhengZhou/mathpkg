Theorem 6.12 states that Hecke operators compose multiplicatively when their indices are coprime: $T_m T_n = T_{mn}$ whenever $\gcd(m,n)=1$. This is the first step in establishing the full multiplicative structure of the Hecke algebra. The proof uses matrix representatives and the fact that when $m$ and $n$ are relatively prime, the Chinese remainder theorem implies that the product of complete residue systems yields a complete residue system for the product index $mn$.
