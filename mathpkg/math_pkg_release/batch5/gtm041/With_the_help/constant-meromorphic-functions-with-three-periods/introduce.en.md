In Chapter 1 it was shown that a function with arbitrarily small nonzero periods is constant on every open connected subset in which it is analytic. Combining this with Theorem 7.13 -- which states that any three periods linearly independent over the integers force the existence of arbitrarily small nonzero periods -- yields the fundamental result: the only meromorphic functions possessing three independent periods are constant functions. This explains why elliptic functions, which are doubly periodic, admit at most two independent periods.
