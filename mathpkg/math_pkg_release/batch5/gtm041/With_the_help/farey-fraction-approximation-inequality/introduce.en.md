Exercise 4 of Chapter 7 uses Farey fractions to obtain a rational approximation bound for irrational numbers. Part (a) shows that any irrational $\theta$ in $(0,1)$ is within $1/(2b^2)$ of one of its adjacent Farey fractions. Part (b) then deduces the existence of infinitely many reduced fractions $h/k$ satisfying $|\theta - h/k| < 1/(2k^2)$, a result closely related to Hurwitz's theorem on Diophantine approximation.
