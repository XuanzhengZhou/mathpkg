Theorem 6.17, often called the Hecke bound for cusp forms, provides an asymptotic upper bound on the Fourier coefficients of cusp forms. For a cusp form $f \in M_{2k,0}$ of weight $2k$, the $n$-th Fourier coefficient grows at most like $n^k$. The proof exploits the modular invariance of the quantity $|f(\tau)| (\operatorname{Im} \tau)^k$, which is bounded on the upper half-plane because it is $\Gamma$-invariant and vanishes at the cusp. Inserting this bound into the Cauchy integral representation of $c(n)$ and optimizing the choice of imaginary part yields the estimate $|c(n)| \leq M n^k e^{2\pi}$.
