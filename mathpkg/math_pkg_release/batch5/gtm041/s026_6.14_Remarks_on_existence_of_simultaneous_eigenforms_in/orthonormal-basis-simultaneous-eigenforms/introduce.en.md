This theorem is the spectral theorem for Hecke operators acting on the space $M_{2k,0}$ of cusp forms. It guarantees the existence of a full orthonormal basis of simultaneous eigenforms, meaning every cusp form can be expressed as a linear combination of forms that are eigenvectors for all Hecke operators $T_n$ simultaneously. Since the $T_n$ are Hermitian with respect to the Petersson inner product, the eigenvalues are real. By scaling, one obtains a basis of normalized eigenforms (leading coefficient $1$) that remains orthogonal but is no longer necessarily orthonormal. The theorem implies $e(k) = \kappa$, i.e., the number of linearly independent simultaneous eigenforms equals the full dimension of the space.
