When $\dim M_{2k,0} = 1$, the corresponding normalized eigenforms are given by
$$
(2\pi)^{-12} \Delta(\tau) \cdot \frac{G_{2k-12}(\tau)}{2\zeta(2k-12)} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \tau(n)x^n \left\{ 1 - \frac{2(2k-12)}{B_{2k-12}} \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \sigma_{2k-13}(m)x^m \right\}.
$$
Define $\tau(0) = 0$ and $\sigma_{2k-1}(0) = -\frac{B_{2k}}{4k}$. Then the coefficients $c(n)$ of these eigenforms are given by the Cauchy product
$$
c(n) = -\frac{4k-24}{B_{2k-12}} \sum_{m=0}^{n} \tau(m) \, \sigma_{2k-13}(n-m).
$$
They satisfy the multiplicative property
$$
c(m) c(n) = \sum_{d \mid (m,n)} d^{2k-1} \, c\!\left( \frac{mn}{d^2} \right)
$$
for all $m \geq 1$, $n \geq 1$.
