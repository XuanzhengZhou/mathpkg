This proposition gives an explicit formula for the Fourier coefficients of the normalized Hecke eigenform in the space $M_{2k,0}$ of cusp forms when the dimension of this space equals one. The eigenform is constructed as a product of the Ramanujan Delta function $\Delta(\tau)$ and the Eisenstein series $G_{2k-12}(\tau)$, normalized by a zeta factor. Its coefficients $c(n)$ are then expressed as a Cauchy product of the Ramanujan tau function $\tau(n)$ and generalized divisor sums $\sigma_{2k-13}(n)$, and the resulting sequence satisfies the fundamental Hecke eigenvalue multiplicative property $c(m)c(n) = \sum_{d \mid (m,n)} d^{2k-1} c(mn/d^2)$.
