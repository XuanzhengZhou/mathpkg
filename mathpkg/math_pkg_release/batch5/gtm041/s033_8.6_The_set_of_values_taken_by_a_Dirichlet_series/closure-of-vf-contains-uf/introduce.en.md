Theorem 8.9 establishes a fundamental inclusion relation between the set of values $V_f(\sigma_0)$ of a Dirichlet series on a vertical line and its Bohr equivalence class $U_f(\sigma_0)$.

The theorem states that whenever $\sigma_0$ exceeds the abscissa of absolute convergence, the closure of $V_f(\sigma_0)$ contains $U_f(\sigma_0)$, yielding $V_f(\sigma_0) \subseteq U_f(\sigma_0) \subseteq \overline{V_f(\sigma_0)}$.

Consequently, the closures of these two sets coincide: $\overline{U_f(\sigma_0)} = \overline{V_f(\sigma_0)}$.

The proof splits the series into a finite initial sum (controlled by Kronecker's theorem on simultaneous Diophantine approximation) and a tail made small by absolute convergence.
