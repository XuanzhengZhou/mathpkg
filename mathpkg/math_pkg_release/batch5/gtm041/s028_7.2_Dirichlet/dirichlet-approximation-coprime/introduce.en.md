This theorem strengthens Dirichlet's Approximation Theorem by additionally guaranteeing that the integers $h$ and $k$ can be chosen to be coprime. Given any real $\theta$ and positive integer $N$, there exist coprime integers $h, k$ with $0 < k \leq N$ such that $|k\theta - h| < 1/N$. The proof reduces to the original theorem by dividing out the greatest common divisor $d = (h', k')$ from a pair satisfying the original inequality, yielding a coprime pair that still satisfies the bound.
