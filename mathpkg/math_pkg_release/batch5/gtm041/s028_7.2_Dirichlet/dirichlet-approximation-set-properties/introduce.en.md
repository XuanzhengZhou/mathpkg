Theorem 7.4 establishes four fundamental properties of the Dirichlet approximation set $S(\theta)$. Part (a) follows from Theorem 7.3. Part (b) proves that for irrational $\theta$, there are infinitely many coprime pairs satisfying $|\theta - h/k| < 1/k^2$, a proof by contradiction using the minimal distance $\alpha$. Part (c) shows that when $S(\theta)$ is infinite, the denominators $k$ grow without bound. Part (d) proves that for rational $\theta = a/b$, $S(\theta)$ is finite, since any $(h,k)$ with $k > b$ would force $0 < |ak - bh| < 1$, impossible for integers. The combined result yields a clean irrationality criterion.
