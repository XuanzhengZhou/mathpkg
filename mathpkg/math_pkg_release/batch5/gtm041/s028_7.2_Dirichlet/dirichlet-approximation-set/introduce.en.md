For a real number $\theta$, the set $S(\theta)$ consists of all coprime integer pairs $(h, k)$ with $k > 0$ satisfying the Dirichlet inequality $|\theta - h/k| < 1/k^2$. This set captures all "good" rational approximations to $\theta$ in the sense of Dirichlet's theorem. Theorem 7.4 characterizes $S(\theta)$ by four properties: nonemptiness, infiniteness for irrational $\theta$, unbounded denominators when infinite, and finiteness for rational $\theta$, thereby providing an irrationality criterion.
