Dirichlet's Approximation Theorem is a foundational result in Diophantine approximation. It states that for any real number $\theta$ and any positive integer $N$, there exist integers $h$ and $k$ with $0 < k \leq N$ such that $|k\theta - h| < 1/N$. This shows that every real number can be approximated arbitrarily closely by rational numbers $h/k$, with the error bounded by $1/(kN)$. The proof uses the pigeonhole principle applied to the fractional parts $\{m\theta\}$ for $m = 0, 1, \ldots, N$.
