Theorem 8.13 establishes that if two general Dirichlet series $f(s)$ and $g(s)$ are equivalent (in the sense of Bohr) and both converge absolutely on the line $\sigma = \sigma_0$, then their sets of values $U_f(\sigma_0)$ and $U_g(\sigma_0)$ on that line are identical. The proof relies on the representation of the coefficients $b(n)$ in terms of $a(n)$ via the equivalence relation, and the transition to Bohr series $F$ and $G$ in several complex variables. This result is a crucial step toward proving Bohr's equivalence theorem, which relates the value sets of equivalent Dirichlet series in half-planes.
