In 1942, D. H. Lehmer proved that the Fourier coefficients $c(n)$ of the normalized modular invariant $12^3 J(\tau)$ satisfy the congruence $(n+1)c(n) \equiv 0 \pmod{24}$ for all $n \geq 1$. This is one of the earliest results revealing the rich arithmetic structure hidden in the coefficients of modular forms, predating the modern theory of congruences for modular forms developed by Serre, Swinnerton-Dyer, and others.
