Theorem 1.15 establishes that the four fundamental modular functions -- the Eisenstein series $g_2$ and $g_3$, the discriminant $\Delta$, and Klein's modular invariant $J$ -- are all analytic on the upper half-plane $H$. Since $\Delta(\tau) \neq 0$ in $H$, it suffices to prove analyticity of $g_2$ and $g_3$, which are given by absolutely convergent double series of the form $\sum (m+n\tau)^{-\alpha}$ with $\alpha > 2$. The proof demonstrates uniform convergence in strips $|x| \leq A, y \geq \delta > 0$, guaranteeing analyticity.
