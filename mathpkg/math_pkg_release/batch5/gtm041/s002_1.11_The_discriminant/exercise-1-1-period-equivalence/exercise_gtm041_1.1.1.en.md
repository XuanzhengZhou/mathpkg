Given two pairs of complex numbers $(\omega_1, \omega_2)$ and $(\omega_1', \omega_2')$ with nonreal ratios $\omega_2/\omega_1$ and $\omega_2'/\omega_1'$. Prove that they generate the same set of periods if, and only if, there is a $2 \times 2$ matrix $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ with integer entries and determinant $\pm 1$ such that

$$\begin{pmatrix} \omega_2' \\ \omega_1' \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} \omega_2 \\ \omega_1 \end{pmatrix}.$$
