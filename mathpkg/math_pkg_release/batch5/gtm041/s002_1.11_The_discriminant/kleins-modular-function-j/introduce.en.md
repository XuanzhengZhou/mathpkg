Klein's modular function $J(\tau)$ is defined as the quotient $J = g_2^3 / \Delta$, where $g_2$ and $\Delta$ are the Eisenstein invariant and discriminant associated with a lattice. The numerator and denominator are both homogeneous of the same degree, which makes $J$ homogeneous of degree 0 and therefore a function of the period ratio $\tau = \omega_2/\omega_1$ alone. The $J$-function is one of the most important modular functions, generating the field of modular functions on the full modular group and playing a central role in the theory of elliptic curves and complex multiplication.
