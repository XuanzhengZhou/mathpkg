The Ramanujan tau function $\tau(n)$ is defined as the $n$-th Fourier coefficient of the normalized discriminant modular form $\Delta(\tau)/(2\pi)^{12}$. It is a multiplicative arithmetic function (as later proved in the book) with $\tau(1) = 1$ and $\tau(2) = -24$. Ramanujan discovered and conjectured many remarkable properties of $\tau(n)$, including the multiplicativity $\tau(mn) = \tau(m)\tau(n)$ for coprime $m, n$ and the bound $|\tau(p)| \leq 2p^{11/2}$ for primes $p$ (the Ramanujan conjecture, later proved by Deligne as a consequence of the Weil conjectures).
