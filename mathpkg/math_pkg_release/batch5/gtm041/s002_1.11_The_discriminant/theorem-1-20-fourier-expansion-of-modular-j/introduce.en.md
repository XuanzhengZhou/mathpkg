Theorem 1.20 gives the Fourier expansion of Klein's modular invariant $J(\tau)$. After multiplying by $12^3$, the expansion has a simple pole at infinity (the $e^{-2\pi i\tau}$ term), a constant term $744$, and nonnegative powers with integer coefficients $c(n)$. These integers grow rapidly and have deep connections to the representation theory of the monster group (Moonshine), as the first coefficient $c(1) = 196884 = 196883 + 1$ is one more than the dimension of the smallest nontrivial irreducible representation of the monster.
