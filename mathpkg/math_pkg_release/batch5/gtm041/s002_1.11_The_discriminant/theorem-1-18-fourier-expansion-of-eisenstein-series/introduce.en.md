Theorem 1.18 provides the full Fourier expansions (also called $q$-expansions) of the Eisenstein series $g_2(\tau)$ and $g_3(\tau)$. The coefficients are expressed in terms of the divisor sum functions $\sigma_3(k) = \sum_{d|k} d^3$ and $\sigma_5(k) = \sum_{d|k} d^5$, revealing deep connections between modular forms and arithmetic. The constant terms involve the special values $\zeta(4) = \pi^4/90$ and $\zeta(6) = \pi^6/945$ of the Riemann zeta function. These expansions are the starting point for the theory of Eisenstein series as modular forms.
