This definition introduces an equivalence relation on general Dirichlet series sharing the same sequence of exponents $\Lambda$. Two such series $\sum a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$ and $\sum b(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$ are called equivalent if their coefficients differ only by a unimodular factor $e^{ix_n}$, where the sequence $X = \{x_n\}$ is obtained by applying a Bohr matrix $R$ to a real sequence $Y = \{y_n\}$. The relation is defined relative to a chosen basis $B$ for $\Lambda$, but Theorem 8.10 shows that it is in fact independent of the choice of basis.
