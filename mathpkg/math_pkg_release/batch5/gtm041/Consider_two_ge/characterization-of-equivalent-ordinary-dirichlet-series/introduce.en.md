Theorem 8.12 provides a concrete characterization of the abstract equivalence relation for the special case of ordinary Dirichlet series $\sum a(n)n^{-s}$. In this setting, the sequence of exponents is $\{\log n\}$ and a natural basis can be constructed from prime powers. The theorem shows that two ordinary Dirichlet series are equivalent precisely when their coefficients differ by a completely multiplicative function $f$ of absolute value $1$ (at primes dividing $n$ whenever $a(n) \neq 0$). This result connects the algebraic notion of equivalence to the multiplicative structure of the coefficient sequences.
