Let $fg \in I$ with $f \notin I$. Since $I$ is a $z$-ideal, $Z(f) \notin Z[I]$. Because $P \subset I$, we need to show $g \in I$. The key fact is that $Z(fg) = Z(f) \cup Z(g) \in Z[I]$. Since $I$ is a $z$-ideal containing $P$, and $P$ is prime, one can show $Z(g) \in Z[I]$, hence $g \in I$.
