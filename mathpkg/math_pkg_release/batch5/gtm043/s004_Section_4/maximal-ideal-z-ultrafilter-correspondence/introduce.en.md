Theorem 2.5 establishes the fundamental one-to-one correspondence between maximal ideals in $C(X)$ and $z$-ultrafilters on $X$. Under the zero-set mapping $Z$, maximal ideals correspond precisely to maximal $z$-filters. This correspondence is the central structural result of Chapter 2 and underpins the Gelfand-Kolmogoroff theorem, which identifies maximal ideals with points of the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$.
