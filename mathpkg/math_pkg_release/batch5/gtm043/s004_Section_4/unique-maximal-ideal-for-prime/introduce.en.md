Theorem 2.11 is a fundamental structural result: every prime ideal in $C(X)$ is contained in exactly one maximal ideal. This is proved using the fact that the intersection of two distinct maximal ideals is a $z$-ideal that is not prime (by 2.10), and therefore, by Theorem 2.9, contains no prime ideal.
