Every ideal is contained in at least one maximal ideal. If $M$ and $M'$ are distinct maximal ideals, their intersection is a $z$-ideal (since $M$ and $M'$ are $z$-ideals), but it is not prime (2.10). By Theorem 2.9, $M \cap M'$ contains no prime ideal. Therefore, a prime ideal cannot be contained in two distinct maximal ideals.
