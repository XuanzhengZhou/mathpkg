Theorem 2.3 establishes the fundamental correspondence between ideals in $C(X)$ and $z$-filters on $X$. The zero-set mapping $Z$ sends ideals to $z$-filters, while its inverse $Z^{\leftarrow}$ sends $z$-filters back to ideals. This bi-directional correspondence is the cornerstone that connects the algebraic structure of the ring $C(X)$ with the topology of $X$, enabling the translation of algebraic problems into topological ones and vice versa.
