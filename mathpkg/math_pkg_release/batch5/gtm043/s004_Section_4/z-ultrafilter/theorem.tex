A \text{$z$-ultrafilter} on $X$ is a maximal $z$-filter, i.e., a $z$-filter not properly contained in any other $z$-filter. Equivalently, a $z$-filter $\mathcal{A}$ is a $z$-ultrafilter if and only if, whenever a zero-set $Z$ meets every member of $\mathcal{A}$, then $Z \in \mathcal{A}$ (Theorem 2.6(b)).

Every $z$-filter is contained in a $z$-ultrafilter (by the maximal principle). In a discrete space, $z$-ultrafilters coincide with ultrafilters.
