Let $\mathcal{F}$ be a prime $z$-filter. The ideal $P = Z^{\leftarrow}[\mathcal{F}]$ is a $z$-ideal. Suppose $fg \in P$. Then $Z(fg) = Z(f) \cup Z(g) \in Z[P] = \mathcal{F}$. By hypothesis, $Z(f)$ or $Z(g)$ belongs to $Z[P]$. Then $f$ or $g$ belongs to the $z$-ideal $P$. Thus $P$ is prime. The converse follows similarly from the definitions.
