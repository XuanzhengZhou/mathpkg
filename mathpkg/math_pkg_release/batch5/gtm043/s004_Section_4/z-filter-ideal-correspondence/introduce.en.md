Theorem 2.3 establishes the fundamental correspondence between algebraic ideals in the ring $C(X)$ and topological $z$-filters on the space $X$. Part (a) shows that the zero-set family $Z[I]$ of any ideal $I$ forms a $z$-filter, while part (b) shows that the preimage $Z^{\leftarrow}[\mathcal{F}]$ of any $z$-filter $\mathcal{F}$ is an ideal. This bidirectional mapping is the cornerstone of the duality between the algebraic properties of $C(X)$ and the topological properties of $X$ that permeates the entire book.
