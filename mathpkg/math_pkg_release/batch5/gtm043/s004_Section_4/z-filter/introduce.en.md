A $z$-filter on a topological space $X$ is a nonempty family of zero-sets closed under finite intersections and supersets, and not containing the empty set. It is the topological analogue of a set-theoretic filter, restricted to the lattice of zero-sets $Z(X)$. $z$-filters provide the crucial bridge between ideals in $C(X)$ and the topology of $X$, as the zero-set mapping $Z$ sends ideals to $z$-filters and vice versa.
