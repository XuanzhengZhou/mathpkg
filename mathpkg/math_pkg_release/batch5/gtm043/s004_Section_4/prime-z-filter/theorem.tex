A $z$-filter $\mathcal{F}$ is called a \text{prime $z$-filter} if, whenever the union of two zero-sets belongs to $\mathcal{F}$, at least one of them belongs to $\mathcal{F}$. Equivalently, $\mathcal{F}$ is prime if $Z(f) \cup Z(g) \in \mathcal{F}$ implies $Z(f) \in \mathcal{F}$ or $Z(g) \in \mathcal{F}$.

Every $z$-ultrafilter is a prime $z$-filter. A prime $z$-filter is contained in a unique $z$-ultrafilter (2.13).
