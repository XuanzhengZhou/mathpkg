A prime $z$-filter is a $z$-filter with the property that if the union of two zero-sets belongs to it, then at least one of them does. This property mirrors that of prime ideals in ring theory. Every $z$-ultrafilter is prime, and a prime $z$-filter is contained in a unique $z$-ultrafilter. The concept provides the filter-theoretic analogue of prime ideals in $C(X)$.
