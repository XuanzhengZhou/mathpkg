(1) implies (2): Given a countable set $\{f_k\}$, apply Lemma 16.24 to get covers $\mathcal{U}_m$ and then use Theorem 16.28 to find $g \in C_n(X)$ in the intersection of the dense open sets $G(\mathcal{U}_m)$ (via the Baire category theorem). The analytic subring $A_g$ with base $\{g_1, \dots, g_n\}$ contains every $f_k$. (2) implies (3) is trivial. (3) implies (1): Conversely, if every finite family is in some analytic subring with $n$ generators, one shows $\dim X \leq n$ using the geometric theory of envelopment.
