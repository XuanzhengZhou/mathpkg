As a special case of Katětov's theorem, a space $X$ is zero-dimensional precisely when its function ring $C^*(X)$ has no proper analytic subring. For compact $X$, this is equivalent to $X$ being totally disconnected, since the set of functions constant on a nontrivial connected set forms a proper analytic subring.
