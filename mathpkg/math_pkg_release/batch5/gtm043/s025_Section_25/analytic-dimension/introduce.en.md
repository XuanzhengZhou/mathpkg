The analytic dimension $\operatorname{ad} C^*(X)$ is an algebraic invariant of the ring $C^*(X)$. It is defined as the minimal cardinal $m$ such that every countable family of functions is contained in an analytic subring generated by at most $m$ functions. The main theorem of the chapter (Katětov's theorem) states that $\dim X = \operatorname{ad} C^*(X)$ for every completely regular space $X$, establishing an algebraic characterization of topological dimension.
