Theorem 16.32 restates the Stone-Weierstrass theorem in the language of analytic subrings. Given a family $B$ of functions on a compact space $X$, the smallest analytic subring containing $B$ (its analytic envelope) consists exactly of those functions constant on every connected stationary set of $B$. This formulation is essential for the algebraic characterization of dimension.
