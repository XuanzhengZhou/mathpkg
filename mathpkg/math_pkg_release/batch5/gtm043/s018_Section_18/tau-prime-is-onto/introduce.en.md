Theorem 10.6 addresses the inverse problem: given a homomorphism $t: C(Y) \to C(X)$ that preserves the constant function $1$, when is it induced by a continuous map $\tau: X \to Y$? The answer is that if $Y$ is realcompact, such a $\tau$ always exists and is unique. The construction proceeds pointwise: for each $x \in X$, the evaluation $g \mapsto (tg)(x)$ is a nonzero homomorphism into $\mathbf{R}$, and realcompactness of $Y$ guarantees a unique point $\tau x \in Y$ representing this evaluation functional via $g(\tau x) = (tg)(x)$.
