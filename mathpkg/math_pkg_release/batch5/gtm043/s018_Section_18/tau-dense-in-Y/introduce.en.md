Theorem 10.7 provides an alternative proof that $C$-embedding implies the extension property for mappings into realcompact spaces. Given a dense $C$-embedded subspace $X \subseteq T$ and a continuous map $\varphi: X \to Y$ with $Y$ realcompact, one constructs an extension $\overline{\varphi}: T \to Y$ by applying Theorem 10.6 to the homomorphism $t: C(Y) \to C(T)$ defined by $t(g) = (g \circ \varphi)_0$, where $(g \circ \varphi)_0$ is the unique continuous extension of $g \circ \varphi$ from $X$ to $T$.
