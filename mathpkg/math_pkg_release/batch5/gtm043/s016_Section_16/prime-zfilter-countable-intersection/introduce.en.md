A space $$X$$ is realcompact precisely when every prime $$z$$-filter possessing the countable intersection property is fixed. Sufficiency is immediate from the definition of realcompactness. The necessity direction uses the relationship between free prime $$z$$-filters and $$z$$-ultrafilters corresponding to maximal ideals, providing an algebraic characterization of realcompactness that is useful in proving the product theorem.
