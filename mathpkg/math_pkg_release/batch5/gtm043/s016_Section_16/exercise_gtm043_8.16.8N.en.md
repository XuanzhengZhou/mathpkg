**Discrete Subspaces of Pseudocompact Spaces.** Let $$m$$ be a cardinal $$> \aleph_0$$. Define $$Y = W^* \times W(\alpha + 1)$$, where $$\alpha$$ is of power $$m$$, and form $$X$$ by removing from $$Y$$ all points $$(\omega_1, \lambda)$$ for which $$\lambda$$ is a limit ordinal. Then $$X$$ is pseudocompact, although it contains a closed, discrete subspace of cardinal $$m$$. [If $$f$$ is unbounded on a sequence of points $$(\alpha_n, \sigma_n)$$, then $$f$$ is unbounded on a set that is homeomorphic with a subspace of $$T$$ containing $$W \times N^*$$.]
