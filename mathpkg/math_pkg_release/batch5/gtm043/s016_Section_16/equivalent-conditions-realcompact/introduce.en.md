Theorem 8.17 provides four equivalent characterizations of spaces $$Y$$ for which every subspace is realcompact. Condition (1) states that the preimage of $$Y$$ under a continuous map with compact fibers is realcompact whenever the domain is; condition (2) says every one-one continuous image is realcompact; condition (3) explicitly says every subspace is realcompact; and condition (4) says removing any single point yields a realcompact space. The implications establish that hereditarily realcompact spaces are closed under certain operations, with the key step using an enlargement of the topology argument.
