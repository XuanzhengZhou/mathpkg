Every metric space is completely regular, which can be seen by taking $f(x) = \min(1, d(x, F)/d(x, F) + \dots)$ or more directly via Urysohn's lemma (metric spaces are normal, and normal spaces are completely regular). In particular, the real line $\mathbf{R}$ and all its subspaces are completely regular, ensuring that the most important spaces in analysis fall within the scope of the theory of rings of continuous functions.
