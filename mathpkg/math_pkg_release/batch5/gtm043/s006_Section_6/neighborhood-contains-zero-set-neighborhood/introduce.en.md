Corollary (b) to Theorem 3.2 states that in a completely regular space, the zero-set-neighborhoods of any point form a neighborhood base at that point. This means that the topology can be described entirely using zero-sets, making them the natural building blocks for topological arguments in the study of $C(X)$.
