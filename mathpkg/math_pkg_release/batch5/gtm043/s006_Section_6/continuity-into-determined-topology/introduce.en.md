Theorem 3.8 provides a convenient criterion for continuity: when the topology of the target space $Y$ is the weak topology induced by a family $C'$, a mapping $\sigma: S \to Y$ is continuous precisely when its composition with every function in $C'$ yields a continuous function on $S$. This is the defining property of the weak (initial) topology and generalizes the fact that continuity into a product is coordinatewise continuity.
