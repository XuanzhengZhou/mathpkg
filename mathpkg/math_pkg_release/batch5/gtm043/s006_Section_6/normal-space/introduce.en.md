A normal space is a Hausdorff space in which any two disjoint closed sets can be separated by disjoint open neighborhoods. Normality is a stronger separation axiom than complete regularity (every normal space is completely regular, by Urysohn's lemma). In the theory of rings of continuous functions, the authors emphasize that remarkably little is gained by assuming normality over complete regularity; most results proved for normal spaces also hold for completely regular spaces through the use of zero-set techniques.
