In a completely regular space, the ring $C(X)$ of all continuous real-valued functions separates points: distinct points of $X$ are distinguished by the values some continuous function takes on them. This property is fundamental because it ensures that the algebraic structure of $C(X)$ faithfully reflects the topology of $X$, allowing the space to be recovered from its function ring.
