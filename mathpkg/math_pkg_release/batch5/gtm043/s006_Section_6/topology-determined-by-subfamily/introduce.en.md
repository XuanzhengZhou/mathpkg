Theorem 3.7 provides a convenient sufficient condition for complete regularity: if the topology of a Hausdorff space $X$ is the weak topology induced by some family $C'$ of real-valued functions, then $X$ is automatically completely regular. This is because every function in $C'$ is continuous (so $C' \subset C(X)$), and the weak topology from $C'$ is contained in that from $C(X)$, which is always contained in the given topology---forcing equality and hence complete regularity by Theorem 3.6.
