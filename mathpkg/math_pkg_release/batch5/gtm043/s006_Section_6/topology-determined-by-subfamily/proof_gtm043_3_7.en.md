Clearly, every function in $C'$ is continuous, i.e., $C' \subset C(X)$. Hence the weak topology induced by $C'$ is contained in the weak topology induced by $C$. But the latter topology is always contained in the given topology on the space $X$. The hypothesis now implies that the two coincide, and so, by Theorem 3.6, $X$ is completely regular.
