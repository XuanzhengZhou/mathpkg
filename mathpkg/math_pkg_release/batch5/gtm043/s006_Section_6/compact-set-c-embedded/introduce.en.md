Theorem 3.11(c) is a crucial result in the theory of rings of continuous functions: every compact subset of a completely regular space is $C$-embedded, meaning every continuous real-valued function on the compact set extends continuously to the whole space. This is proved using Theorem 3.11(a) (disjoint compact sets are completely separated) together with Urysohn's extension theorem (1.17). It plays a role analogous to the Tietze extension theorem for normal spaces but applies in the broader setting of completely regular spaces.
