Compact is an absolute topological concept, not relative (like closed): a compact space is compact in any embedding. Let $S$ be a compact subspace of a completely regular space $X$. Completely separated sets in $S$ have disjoint closures in $S$. As these closures are compact, they are, by Theorem 3.11(a), completely separated in $X$. Urysohn's extension theorem (1.17) now yields that every continuous function on $S$ extends continuously to $X$, i.e., $S$ is $C$-embedded in $X$.
