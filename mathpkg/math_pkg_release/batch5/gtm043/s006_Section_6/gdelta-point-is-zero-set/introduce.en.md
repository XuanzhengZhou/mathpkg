A $G_{\delta}$-point is a singleton that can be written as a countable intersection of open sets. In a completely regular space, every $G_{\delta}$-point is a zero-set (since points are compact). This is a special case of the more general fact that compact $G_{\delta}$ sets are zero-sets. In first-countable spaces, every point is a $G_{\delta}$-point, hence every point is a zero-set.
