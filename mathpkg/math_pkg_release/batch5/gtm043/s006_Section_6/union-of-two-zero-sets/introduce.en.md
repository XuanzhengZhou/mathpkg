The family $Z(X)$ of all zero-sets is closed under finite unions because $Z(f) \cup Z(g) = Z(fg)$. This elementary algebraic fact is of fundamental importance: it means that the subbase consisting of zero-sets is already a base (since finite unions of zero-sets remain zero-sets). This property is what makes zero-sets, rather than arbitrary closed sets, the natural building blocks for topological arguments in $C(X)$.
