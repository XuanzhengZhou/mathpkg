Theorem 3.6 consolidates the relationship between topology and continuous functions. It states that the bounded and all continuous functions induce the same weak topology, that $Z(X)$ is a base for the closed sets of this topology, and provides an explicit neighborhood base. Most importantly, it characterizes completely regular Hausdorff spaces as precisely those whose given topology coincides with the weak topology from $C(X)$---the topology is determined by its continuous functions.
