A completely regular space is a Hausdorff space in which every closed set $F$ and point $x$ not in $F$ can be completely separated by a continuous real-valued function, meaning there exists $f \in C(X)$ with $f(x) = 1$ and $f[F] = \{0\}$. This class of spaces is precisely the class of subspaces of compact Hausdorff spaces, and it is the natural setting for the theory of rings of continuous functions. Every metric space is completely regular, and the property is hereditary (every subspace of a completely regular space is completely regular).
