Theorem 3.10(b) generalizes the continuity criterion to arbitrary weak topologies: a map into a space whose topology is determined by a family $\Phi$ is continuous precisely when its composition with each map in $\Phi$ is continuous. This is the universal property of the initial topology. It does not depend on special properties of $\mathbf{R}$ and holds for families of mappings into arbitrary spaces.
