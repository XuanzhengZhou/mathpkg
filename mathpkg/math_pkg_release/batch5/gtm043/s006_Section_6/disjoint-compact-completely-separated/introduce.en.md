Theorem 3.11(a) states that in a completely regular space, disjoint compact sets can be completely separated by a continuous function---in fact, they are contained in disjoint zero-sets. The proof uses the complete regularity to separate each point of one compact set from the other, then extracts a finite subcover by compactness. Forming the union (a zero-set) and intersection of the corresponding zero-sets yields the desired separating zero-sets.
