For a product of completely regular spaces, the closed sets have an explicit base consisting of finite unions of preimages of zero-sets under coordinate projections. The key observation---that each such finite union is itself a zero-set (since preimages of zero-sets under projections are zero-sets, and finite unions of zero-sets are zero-sets)---is what makes the product again completely regular. This result is the technical foundation for the proof that arbitrary products preserve complete regularity.
