A compact set that is also a $G_{\delta}$ (a countable intersection of open sets) in a completely regular space is automatically a zero-set. This follows directly from Theorem 3.11(b) by taking $S = A$ (the compact $G_{\delta}$ itself). This corollary is useful because it identifies a large class of zero-sets that arise naturally in analysis, where compact $G_{\delta}$ sets appear frequently (e.g., in locally compact, $\sigma$-compact spaces).
