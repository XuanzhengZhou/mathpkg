Corollary (a) to Theorem 3.2 strengthens the characterization of closed sets in completely regular spaces: not only is every closed set an intersection of zero-sets, but it is an intersection of zero-set-neighborhoods of itself. A zero-set-neighborhood of $F$ is a zero-set that contains $F$ in its interior, providing a stronger separation property that is frequently useful in the theory.
