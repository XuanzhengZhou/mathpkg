Complete regularity is a hereditary property: any subspace of a completely regular space, endowed with the subspace topology, is again completely regular. This follows directly from the definition by restricting the separating continuous function from the ambient space to the subspace. The hereditary nature makes completely regular spaces a particularly well-behaved class of topological spaces.
