When the target spaces $Y_{\alpha}$ are completely regular, the weak topology induced by $\Phi$ has a particularly convenient subbase for its closed sets: the preimages of zero-sets in the target spaces. This is because in a completely regular space, the zero-sets form a base for the closed sets, and pulling them back under the mappings in $\Phi$ yields a subbase. This observation is essential for analyzing subspaces and products of completely regular spaces.
