For each $x \in X$, the neighborhood $X$ of $x$ contains a compact neighborhood of $x$ (since $T$ is locally compact and $X$ is open in $T$). This compact neighborhood serves as a compact neighborhood of $x$ in the subspace $X$. Hence $X$ is locally compact.
