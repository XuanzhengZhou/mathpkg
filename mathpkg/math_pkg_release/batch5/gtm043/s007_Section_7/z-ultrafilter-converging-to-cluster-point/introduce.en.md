If a point $p$ is a cluster point of a $z$-filter $\mathcal{F}$, then there exists at least one $z$-ultrafilter extending $\mathcal{F}$ that actually converges to $p$. This proposition bridges the gap between clustering (belonging to all closures) and convergence (every neighborhood meets the filter). The proof uses the maximal principle to extend $\mathcal{F}$ to a $z$-ultrafilter, and then shows that this $z$-ultrafilter must contain all zero-set-neighborhoods of $p$.
