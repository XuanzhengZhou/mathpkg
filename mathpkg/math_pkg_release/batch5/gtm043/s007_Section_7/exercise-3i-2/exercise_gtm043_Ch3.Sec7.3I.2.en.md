Let $X$ be the set of real numbers, under the discrete topology, and take $A = \{\sigma\}$, where $\sigma$ is the identity map of $X$ onto $R$. Then $\tau = \sigma$, and $Y = R$. Hence $\tau$ is not a quotient mapping.
