Let $X$ be a completely regular space. Any two countable sets, neither of which meets the closure of the other, are contained in disjoint cozero-sets. [Inductively, choose a suitable closed neighborhood of each point, alternating between the two sets.] (But the given sets need not be completely separated, even if they are closed; see 8J.4.)
