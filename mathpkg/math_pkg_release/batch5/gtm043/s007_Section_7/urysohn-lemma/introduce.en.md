Urysohn's Lemma is a fundamental result in general topology, named after Pavel Urysohn. It states that in any normal topological space, any two disjoint closed sets can be separated by a continuous real-valued function. In other words, there exists a continuous function $f: X \to [0,1]$ such that $f(A) = \{0\}$ and $f(B) = \{1\}$. As an immediate consequence, every normal space is completely regular, establishing the hierarchy of separation axioms: normal implies completely regular.
