Let $\mathcal{F}$ be a $z$-filter on a completely regular space $X$. A point $p \in X$ is called a cluster point of $\mathcal{F}$ if $p$ belongs to the closure of every member of $\mathcal{F}$. Thus, since the members of $\mathcal{F}$ are closed sets, $p$ is a cluster point of $\mathcal{F}$ if and only if $p \in \bigcap \mathcal{F}$.
