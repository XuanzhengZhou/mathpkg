A cluster point of a $z$-filter $\mathcal{F}$ on a completely regular space $X$ is a point $p \in X$ that lies in the closure of every member of $\mathcal{F}$. Since $z$-filters consist of zero-sets (which are closed), this is equivalent to $p$ belonging to the intersection of all members of $\mathcal{F}$. The notion generalizes the concept of accumulation point from sequences and nets to the language of $z$-filters, which plays a central role in the theory of rings of continuous functions.
