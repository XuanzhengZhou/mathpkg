In a topological space $X$, define $x \equiv x'$ to mean that $\operatorname{cl}\{x\} = \operatorname{cl}\{x'\}$. Let $Y$ be the set of all equivalence classes thus defined, let $\tau$ map each point of $X$ into its equivalence class, and provide $Y$ with the quotient topology relative to $\tau$. For $E$ closed in $X$, if $x \in E$ and $x \equiv x'$, then $x' \in E$; hence $\tau[X - E] = Y - \tau[E]$.
