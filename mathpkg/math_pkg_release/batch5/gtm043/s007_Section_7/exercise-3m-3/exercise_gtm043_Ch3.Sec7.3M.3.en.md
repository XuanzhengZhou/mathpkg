Let $(f_\alpha)$ be a family of functions in $C$, and for $r \in \mathbf{R}$, define $U^r = \operatorname{cl} \bigcup_\alpha \{x : f_\alpha(x) > r\}$. If $g \in C$, and $g \geq f_\alpha$ for every $\alpha$, then, for each $x$, $g(x) \geq \sup \{r : x \in U^r\}$. [If $g(x) < s$, then $g(y) < s$ on a neighborhood of $x$, whence $x \notin U^s$, so that $\sup \{r : x \in U^r\} \leq s$.]
