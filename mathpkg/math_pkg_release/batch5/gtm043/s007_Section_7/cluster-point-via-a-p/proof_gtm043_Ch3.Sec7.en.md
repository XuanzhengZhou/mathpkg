$p$ is a cluster point of $\mathcal{F}$ if and only if $p$ belongs to the closure of every member of $\mathcal{F}$. Since the members of $\mathcal{F}$ are closed sets (they are zero-sets), this is equivalent to $p$ belonging to every member of $\mathcal{F}$. That is, every $Z \in \mathcal{F}$ satisfies $p \in Z$, which means $\mathcal{F} \subset A_p$.
