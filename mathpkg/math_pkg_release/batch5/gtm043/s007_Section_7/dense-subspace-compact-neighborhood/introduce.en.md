This proposition relates the topologies of a dense subspace and its ambient space. If $X$ is dense in a Hausdorff space $T$, then any compact neighborhood of a point $p$ in the subspace $X$ is also a neighborhood of $p$ in the larger space $T$. The proof uses the fact that a compact subset of a Hausdorff space is closed, and density forces the interior of the compact neighborhood to be the same in both $X$ and $T$.
