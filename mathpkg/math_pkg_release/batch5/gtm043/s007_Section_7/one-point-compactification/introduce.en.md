The one-point compactification (also known as the Alexandroff compactification) is a construction that adds a single "point at infinity" to a locally compact, noncompact Hausdorff space $X$ to produce a compact Hausdorff space $X^*$. The original space $X$ embeds as an open subspace, and the complements of compact subsets of $X$ form a neighborhood base for the new point. This construction shows that every locally compact space is a subspace of a compact space, and hence is completely regular.
