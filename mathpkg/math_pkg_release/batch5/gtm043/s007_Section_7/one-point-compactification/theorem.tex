Every locally compact, noncompact space $X$ has a so-called one-point compactification $X^*$, defined as follows: one new point is adjoined to $X$, $X$ is an open subspace of $X^*$, and the complements of compact subsets of $X$ form a base of neighborhoods for the adjoined point. One verifies without difficulty that $X^*$ is indeed a compactification of $X$. Thus, every locally compact space is completely regular.
