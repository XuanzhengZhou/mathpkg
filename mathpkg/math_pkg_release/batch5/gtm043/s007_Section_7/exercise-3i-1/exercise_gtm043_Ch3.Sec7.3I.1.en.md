Let $A$ be a nonempty subfamily of $C(X)$. Define $Y$ and $\tau$ as in 3.9, except that now $f$ ranges only over $A$, instead of over all of $C(X)$. Then $Y$ is completely regular, $\tau$ is continuous, and the mapping $g \rightarrow g \circ \tau$ is an isomorphism from $C(Y)$ into $C(X)$.
