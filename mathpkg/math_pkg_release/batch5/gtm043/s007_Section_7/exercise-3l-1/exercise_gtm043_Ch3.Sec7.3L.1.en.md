Let $X$ be a completely regular space. Let $(V_\alpha)$ be a family of disjoint sets in $X$ with nonempty interiors, and such that for each index $\alpha$, the set $\bigcup_{\sigma \neq \alpha} V_\sigma$ is closed. Any set $D$ formed by selecting one element from the interior of each $V_\alpha$ is $C$-embedded in $X$. (Any attempt to weaken the hypothesis in a serious way runs into the pathology of the Tychonoff plank, $T$, described in detail in 8.20. $T$ contains a countable, closed, discrete set that is not even $C^*$-embedded.)
