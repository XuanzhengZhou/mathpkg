$A_p$ denotes the collection of all zero-sets in $X$ that contain the point $p$. This is always a $z$-filter. Moreover, it is actually a $z$-ultrafilter: any zero-set not containing $p$ is completely separated from $\{p\}$, and this property forces maximality. The family of $z$-ultrafilters of the form $A_p$ (for $p \in X$) are precisely the convergent $z$-ultrafilters on $X$. Note that $\{p\}$ itself need not be a zero-set, and hence need not belong to $A_p$.
