Recall that in the completely regular space $X$, every neighborhood of $p$ contains a zero-set-neighborhood of $p$ (by 3.2(b)). If $\mathcal{F}$ converges to $p$, then every neighborhood of $p$ contains a member of $\mathcal{F}$. In particular, every zero-set-neighborhood of $p$ contains a member of $\mathcal{F}$, and since $\mathcal{F}$ is a $z$-filter, each zero-set-neighborhood of $p$ belongs to $\mathcal{F}$. Thus $\mathcal{F}$ contains the $z$-filter of all zero-set-neighborhoods of $p$. Conversely, if $\mathcal{F}$ contains the $z$-filter of all zero-set-neighborhoods of $p$, then for any neighborhood $U$ of $p$, there exists a zero-set-neighborhood $Z$ of $p$ contained in $U$. Since $Z \in \mathcal{F}$, it follows that $U$ contains a member of $\mathcal{F}$, so $\mathcal{F}$ converges to $p$.
