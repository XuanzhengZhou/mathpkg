This theorem states that in a completely regular space, prime $z$-filters (and therefore $z$-ultrafilters, which are a special case) can have at most one cluster point. A prime $z$-filter need not have any cluster point at all: for example, the family of all zero-sets in $\mathbf{R}$ whose complements are bounded is a $z$-filter without any cluster point, and any $z$-ultrafilter containing it also lacks cluster points.
