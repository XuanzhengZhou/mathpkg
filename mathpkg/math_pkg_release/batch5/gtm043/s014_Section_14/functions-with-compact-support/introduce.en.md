The set $C_K(X)$ of continuous functions with compact support on a topological space $X$ admits a concise description in terms of the Stone-Čech compactification: a bounded continuous function has compact support precisely when the zero-set of its Stone extension is a neighborhood of the remainder $\beta X \setminus X$. This yields the representation of $C_K(X)$ as the intersection of all $O^p$ for points $p$ in the remainder, and consequently as the intersection of all free ideals in both $C(X)$ and $C^*(X)$.
