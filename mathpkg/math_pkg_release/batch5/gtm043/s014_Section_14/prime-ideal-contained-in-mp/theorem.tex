Let $P$ be a prime ideal contained in $M^p$. For each $f \in C(X)$:

(a) $|P(f)|$ is infinitely large if and only if $|M^p(f)|$ is infinitely large---or, equivalently, $f^*(p) = \infty$.

(b) $|P(f) - r|$ is infinitely small or zero (where $r \in \mathbf{R}$) if and only if $|M^p(f) - r|$ is infinitely small or zero---or, equivalently, $f^*(p) = r$.
