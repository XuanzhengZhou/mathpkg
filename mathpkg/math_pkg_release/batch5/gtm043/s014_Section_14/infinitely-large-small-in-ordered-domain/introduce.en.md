In the context of totally ordered integral domains, the notions of infinitely large and infinitely small elements generalize the familiar concepts from non-standard analysis. An element is infinitely large if it exceeds every natural number, and infinitely small if it is positive but its multiples by natural numbers never reach 1. These concepts are essential for analyzing the order structure of residue class fields $C/P$ and $C/M^p$ in the theory of rings of continuous functions.
