**Necessity.** Let $I$ be an ideal containing $O^p$ that is contained in $M^p$. Suppose $f \in M^p$ and the cozero-set of $f$ meets every zero-set in $Z[I]$. Then $Z(f)$ meets every member of $Z[I]$. Since $M^p$ is the only maximal ideal containing $I$, we must have $(I, g) = C(X)$ for any $g \notin I$. Consequently, there exist $h \in I$ and $s \in C(X)$ such that $1 = h + sg$. Hence $f = hf \in I$.

**Sufficiency.** We show that $M^p$ is the only maximal ideal containing $O^p$. If $q \neq p$, there exists $f \in C^*(X)$ such that $f^\beta$ vanishes on a neighborhood of $p$, while $f^\beta(q) \neq 0$; then $f \in O^p \setminus M^q$.
