The ideal $O^p$ is a $z$-ideal in $C(X)$ associated with each point $p$ of the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$. It consists of all continuous functions whose zero-set closure in $\beta X$ is a neighborhood of $p$. The ideal $O^p$ is always contained in the corresponding maximal ideal $M^p$, and for points $p$ in $X$ itself, $O^p$ reduces to the familiar ideal $O_p$. The $z$-filter $Z[O^p]$ plays a crucial role in describing the neighborhood structure of $p$ within $\beta X$.
