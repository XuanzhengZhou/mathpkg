The condition is sufficient: if $Z(f) \supset V \cap X$ for some neighborhood $V$ of $p$, then $\mathrm{cl}_{\beta X} Z(f) \supset \mathrm{cl}_{\beta X}(V \cap X) \supset V$, so $\mathrm{cl}_{\beta X} Z(f)$ is a neighborhood of $p$, hence $f \in O^p$.

Conversely, if $f \in O^p$, then $\mathrm{cl}_{\beta X} Z(f)$ is a neighborhood of $p$. Taking $V$ to be the interior of $\mathrm{cl}_{\beta X} Z(f)$, we have $Z(f) = \mathrm{cl}_{\beta X} Z(f) \cap X \supset V \cap X$, which yields the stated condition.
