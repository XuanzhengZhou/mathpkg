$C_\infty(X)$ is the ring of continuous functions that vanish at infinity, meaning the sets where $|f(x)| \geq 1/n$ are compact for every $n$. This ring contains all functions with compact support and can be characterized as the intersection of all free maximal ideals in $C^*(X)$. On locally compact, $\sigma$-compact non-compact spaces, the intersection of free maximal ideals in $C(X)$ is strictly smaller, revealing a subtle distinction between the bounded and unbounded cases.
