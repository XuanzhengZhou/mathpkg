Let $C_\infty(X)$ denote the family of all functions $f \in C(X)$ for which the set
$$\{x \in X : |f(x)| \geq 1/n\}$$
is compact for every $n \in \mathbf{N}$. Such functions are said to \textbf{vanish at infinity}. One always has $C_\infty(X) \supset C_K(X)$.

The set $C_\infty(X)$ equals the intersection of all the free maximal ideals in $C^*(X)$. The intersection of all the free maximal ideals in $C(X)$ is always contained in the intersection of all the free maximal ideals in $C^*(X)$. When $X$ is locally compact and $\sigma$-compact but not compact, the inclusion is proper.
