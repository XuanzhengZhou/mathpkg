This theorem gives the fundamental characterization of convex ideals in partially ordered rings: the residue class ring $A/I$ inherits a natural partial order from $A$ precisely when $I$ is convex. The order is defined by declaring a residue class nonnegative when it contains a nonnegative representative. Under this condition, the canonical projection $A \to A/I$ becomes an order-preserving homomorphism.
