A convex ideal in a partially ordered ring generalizes the geometric notion of convexity to the algebraic setting. An ideal $I$ is convex if it is "downward closed" with respect to the partial order: whenever an element $y$ belongs to $I$, every element $x$ between $0$ and $y$ also belongs to $I$. This property is equivalent to the condition that the residue class ring $A/I$ inherits a natural partial order from $A$, making convexity the decisive notion for constructing ordered quotient rings.
