(a) $|M(f)|$ is infinitely large for some maximal ideal $M$ in $C$ if and only if $f$ is unbounded on $X$.

(b) $|M(f)|$ is infinitely large for every free maximal ideal $M$ in $C$ if and only if $f$ is unbounded on every noncompact zero-set in $X$.

(c) $|M(f)|$ is infinitely large for some maximal ideal $M$ in $C$ if and only if $f$ is unbounded on $X$. (Necessity follows from (a).) Conversely, if $f$ is unbounded, then the family of sets $Z_n = \{x : |f(x)| \geq n\}$ has the finite intersection property, and hence is embeddable in a $z$-ultrafilter $\mathcal{A}$. The ideal $M = Z^{\leftarrow}[\mathcal{A}]$ in $C$ is maximal, and by (a), $|M(f)|$ is infinitely large.
