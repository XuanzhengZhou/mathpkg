A completely regular space $X$ is said to be \emph{realcompact} if every real maximal ideal in $C(X)$ is fixed.

The problem of describing the residue class fields of $C^*$ is now settled: by Theorem 5.8, every maximal ideal in $C^*$ is real. For $C$, the description of residue class fields leads naturally to the study of realcompact spaces.
