A realcompact space is a completely regular space in which every real maximal ideal of $C(X)$ is fixed -- that is, corresponds to evaluation at some point. Realcompactness generalizes compactness: in a compact space every maximal ideal is fixed, while in a realcompact space only the real maximal ideals must be fixed. The concept arises naturally from the study of residue class fields of $C(X)$, as the description of when all maximal ideals are real (Theorem 5.8) leads directly to realcompactness.
