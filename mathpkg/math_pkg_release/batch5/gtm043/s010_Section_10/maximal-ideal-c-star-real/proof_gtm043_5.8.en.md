**(a)** Let $M$ be a maximal ideal in $C^*(X)$. For any $f \in C^*(X)$, there exists $n \in \mathbf{N}$ such that $|f| \leq n$. Then in $C^*/M$, we have $|M(f)| \leq n$. Therefore $C^*/M$ contains no infinitely large elements, and hence is archimedean. Since every archimedean ordered field embeds in $\mathbf{R}$, and $C^*/M$ already contains a canonical copy of $\mathbf{R}$, it follows that $C^*/M = \mathbf{R}$. Thus $M$ is real.

**(b)** This is immediate from 5.7(b): $|M(f)|$ is infinitely large for some maximal ideal $M$ if and only if $f$ is unbounded. Hence all maximal ideals are real (no infinitely large elements exist) if and only if every $f \in C(X)$ is bounded, i.e., $X$ is pseudocompact.
