Since $I$ is absolutely convex, $I(f) \geq 0$ if and only if $f \equiv |f| \pmod{I}$ (by Theorem 5.3(5)). This means $f - |f| \in I$. Since $I$ is a $z$-ideal, $f - |f|$ belongs to $I$ precisely when there exists a zero-set $Z \in Z[I]$ on which $f - |f|$ vanishes. On such a zero-set, $f = |f|$, i.e., $f$ is nonnegative on $Z$. Conversely, if $f$ is nonnegative on some $Z \in Z[I]$, then $f - |f| = 0$ on $Z$, so $f - |f| \in I$, giving $I(f) = I(|f|) \geq 0$.
