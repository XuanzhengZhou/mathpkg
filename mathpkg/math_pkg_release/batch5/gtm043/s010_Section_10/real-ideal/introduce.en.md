A real ideal is a maximal ideal $M$ in $C(X)$ or $C^*(X)$ whose residue class field is precisely the canonical copy of the real numbers $\mathbf{R}$. In other words, the quotient $C/M$ contains no "non-standard" elements beyond the images of constant functions. Every fixed maximal ideal $M_p$ is a real ideal, since the evaluation map $f \mapsto f(p)$ provides an isomorphism $C/M_p \cong \mathbf{R}$.
