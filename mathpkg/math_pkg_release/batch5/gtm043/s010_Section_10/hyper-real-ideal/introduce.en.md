A hyper-real ideal is a maximal ideal whose residue class field strictly contains the canonical copy of $\mathbf{R}$, containing non-standard (infinitely large or infinitely small) elements. Such fields are necessarily nonarchimedean: they contain elements larger than every natural number. This contrasts with real ideals, where the quotient field is exactly $\mathbf{R}$. The residue class field is archimedean if and only if it is real.
