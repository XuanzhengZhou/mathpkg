This theorem establishes a fundamental structural result for rings of continuous functions: every prime ideal in $C(X)$ or $C^*(X)$ is absolutely convex, implying that the quotient ring is a totally ordered integral domain. Moreover, the constant functions embed as an order-isomorphic copy of the real numbers into the residue class ring. In particular, every residue class field modulo a maximal ideal contains a canonical copy of $\mathbf{R}$.
