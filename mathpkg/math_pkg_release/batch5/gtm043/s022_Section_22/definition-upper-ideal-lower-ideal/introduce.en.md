In Section 14.2, the authors define upper and lower ideals within a chain of prime ideals. An upper ideal possesses an immediate predecessor (its set of predecessors has a maximal element), while a lower ideal possesses an immediate successor. These concepts are central to analyzing the structure of prime ideal chains in $C(X)$ and $C/P$, as they partition the chain into "jump" points and "limit" points, analogous to successor and limit ordinals.
