Theorem 13.20 establishes that the set $Q$ of upper elements of $s$ has cardinality exactly $c = 2^{\aleph_0}$, the cardinality of the continuum. The proof uses the chain $Q_{\omega+1} \subset Q = \bigcup_{\sigma < \omega_1} Q_\sigma$ together with the bound $|Q_\sigma| \leq 2^{|\sigma|}$ to obtain $c \leq |Q| \leq \aleph_1 \cdot c = c$. Combined with later results, this shows $Q$ is an $\eta_1$-set of cardinality $\aleph_1$ under the Continuum Hypothesis.
