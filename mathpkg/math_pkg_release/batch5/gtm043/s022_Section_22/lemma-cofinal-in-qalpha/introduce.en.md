Lemma 13.21 is a technical result asserting that for any $\alpha < \omega_1$, subsets of $Q_\alpha$ have countable cofinal and coinitial subsets. The proof constructs the countable cofinal subset by considering the supremum $x$ of a set $A \subset Q_\alpha$: if $x \notin A$, then by 13.18(a) there is a limit ordinal $\tau \leq \alpha$ such that $x_\xi = 0$ for $\xi \geq \tau$, and truncations at indices $\sigma < \tau$ where $x_\sigma = 1$ yield countably many approximating elements whose associated elements of $A$ form a countable cofinal subset.
