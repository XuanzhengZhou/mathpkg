Theorem 14.7 establishes that minimal prime ideals over $z$-ideals are themselves $z$-ideals. The proof constructs a multiplicatively closed set $S = (C - P) \cup \{hf^n : h \notin P, n \in \mathbf{N}\}$ and shows that if $P$ is a non-$z$-ideal prime containing $I$, then $S$ is disjoint from some multiplicatively closed set, yielding a smaller prime $z$-ideal containing $I$ -- contradicting minimality of $Q$ unless $Q$ itself is a $z$-ideal. This result connects the $z$-ideal structure with prime ideal chains in $C(X)$.
