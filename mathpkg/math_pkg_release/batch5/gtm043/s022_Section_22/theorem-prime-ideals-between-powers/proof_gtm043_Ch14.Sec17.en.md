The hypothesis implies that $a_i^{1/m} < b_j^n$ for all $i, j, m, n$. By Lemma 14.15(a), there exists $c$ such that
$$a_i^{1/m} < c < b_j^n$$
for all $i, j, m, n$. Hence
$$a_i < c^m \leq c < b_j^n$$
for all $i, j, m, n$, and the result follows from Theorem 14.6.
