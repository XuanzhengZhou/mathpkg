Lemma 14.8 establishes that the sum of two $z$-ideals is again a $z$-ideal (or the whole ring $C$). The proof uses the Stone-Čech compactification: given $Z(h) \supset Z(g_1 + g_2)$ with $g_1 \in I$, $g_2 \in J$, one constructs a continuous function $\varphi$ on $\operatorname{cl}_{\beta X} Z_1 \cup \operatorname{cl}_{\beta X} Z_2$ that matches the extension $h^\beta$ on one part and vanishes on the other; extending $\varphi$ to $\beta X$ yields a function $f \in C^*$ such that $f \in I$ (from the zero-set condition) and $h - f \in J$, so $h \in (I,J)$.
