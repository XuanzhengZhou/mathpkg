Theorem 14.3(c) establishes that the prime ideals in $C/P$ are totally ordered by inclusion, forming a chain. This follows because every prime ideal is convex (14.3a), every ideal in a ring is symmetric, and convex ideals in a totally ordered ring are symmetric intervals -- and of any two symmetric intervals, one contains the other. As a corollary, $C/P$ has exactly one maximal ideal, providing a third proof of the uniqueness of the maximal ideal containing $P$ in $C$.
