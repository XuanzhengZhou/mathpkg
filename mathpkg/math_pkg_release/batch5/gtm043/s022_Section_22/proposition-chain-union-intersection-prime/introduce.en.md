Section 14.2 opens the study of prime ideals in $C(X)$ with a purely algebraic observation: the union and intersection of a chain of prime ideals are again prime ideals. The union being prime is clear; the intersection being prime follows from the fact that if $a \notin \bigcap \mathfrak{A}$ and $b \notin \bigcap \mathfrak{A}$, there exist members of the chain excluding $a$ and $b$, and chain comparability yields a member excluding $ab$. This result is fundamental for analyzing the structure of prime ideal chains in $C/P$.
