Every nonzero prime ideal in $C/P$ is a union of upper ideals. Every nonmaximal prime ideal in $C/P$ is an intersection of lower ideals.

Explicitly, if $J$ is a nonzero prime ideal:
$$J = \bigcup_{0 < a \in J} P^a.$$

The corresponding result for lower ideals holds similarly. When a nonzero prime ideal is not itself an upper ideal, it is also a union of the corresponding lower ideals. Similarly, any nonmaximal prime ideal that is not a lower ideal is an intersection of upper ideals.
