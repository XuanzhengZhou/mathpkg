Let $J$ be a nonzero prime ideal in $C/P$. If $a$ is a positive element of $J$, then $P^a \subset J$ (since $P_a$ is the largest prime ideal not containing $a$, and $P^a$ is its immediate successor). Therefore
$$J = \bigcup_{0 < a \in J} P^a.$$

This proves the first statement. The proof of the second is similar, using lower ideals and intersections.
