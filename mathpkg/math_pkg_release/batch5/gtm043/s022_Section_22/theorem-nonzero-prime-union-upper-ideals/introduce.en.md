Theorem 14.4 provides a fundamental decomposition of prime ideals in $C/P$ in terms of upper and lower ideals. Every nonzero prime ideal is the union of all upper ideals $P^a$ for positive $a$ in the ideal, while every nonmaximal prime ideal is the intersection of all lower ideals $P_a$ properly containing it. This shows that the upper and lower ideals are the "building blocks" of the prime ideal chain, analogous to how successor ordinals generate limit ordinals.
