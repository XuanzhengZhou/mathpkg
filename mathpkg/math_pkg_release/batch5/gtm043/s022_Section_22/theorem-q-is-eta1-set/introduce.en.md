Theorem 13.22 is the culmination of the construction: the set $Q$ of all upper elements of $s$ is an $\eta_1$-set. The proof proceeds by induction on $\alpha$, showing each $Q_\alpha$ is an $\eta_1$-set, and since $Q_{\omega_1} = Q$, the result follows. This establishes $Q$ as the canonical example of an $\eta_1$-set of cardinality $\aleph_1$ (under CH), playing a role analogous to the rationals for $\eta_0$-sets.
