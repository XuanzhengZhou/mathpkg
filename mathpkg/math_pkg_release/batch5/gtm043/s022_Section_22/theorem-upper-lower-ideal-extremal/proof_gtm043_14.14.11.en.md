The first assertion was noted earlier (the successor of $P_a$ is $P^a$). For an upper ideal $J$, let $I$ be its immediate predecessor in some maximal chain. Using Theorem 14.9, intersecting with another maximal chain containing any predecessor $H$ of $J$, the intersection gives a $z$-ideal $P$ properly contained in $J$. This forces $H \subset I$, so $I$ is the largest predecessor.
