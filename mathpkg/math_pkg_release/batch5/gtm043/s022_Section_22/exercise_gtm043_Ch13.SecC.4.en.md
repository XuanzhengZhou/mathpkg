If $s$ and $t$ are independent transcendentals, there exists no isomorphism from $\mathcal{R}(\mathbf{Q}(s))$ into $\mathcal{R}(\mathbf{Q}(t))$. Thus, the analogue of Theorem 13.13 for $\eta_0$-sets does not hold, even when the fields have the same transcendence degree over $\mathbf{Q}$.
