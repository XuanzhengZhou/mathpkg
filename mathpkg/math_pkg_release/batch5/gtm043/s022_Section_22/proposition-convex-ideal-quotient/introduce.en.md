Proposition 14.3(b) is a general algebraic fact: convexity of ideals is preserved under quotient by a smaller convex ideal. Given $0 \leq I(a) \leq I(b)$ with $I(b) \in J/I$, one constructs representatives to show $I(a) \in J/I$ using the convexity of $J$ in $A$. This proposition underpins the proof that prime ideals in $C/P$ are convex.
