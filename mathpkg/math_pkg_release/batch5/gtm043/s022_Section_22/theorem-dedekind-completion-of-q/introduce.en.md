Theorem 13.23 identifies the Dedekind completion of the $\eta_1$-set $Q$: it is the set $R$ obtained from $s$ by removing all lower elements and the endpoints. The proof shows $R$ is Dedekind-complete, contains $Q$, and no proper subset containing $Q$ is Dedekind-complete (using Lemma 13.19(a) to guarantee $Q$ is dense in $R$). The cardinality computation yields $|R| = 2^{\aleph_1}$, showing the Dedekind completion can dramatically increase cardinality.
