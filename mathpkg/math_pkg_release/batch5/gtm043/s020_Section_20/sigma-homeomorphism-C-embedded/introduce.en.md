Theorem 11.3 establishes the fundamental embedding of a completely regular space $X$ into the product $P = \mathbf{R}^{C(X)}$. The map $\sigma$ sends each point $x \in X$ to the vector of all its function values $(f(x))_{f \in C(X)}$. Continuity follows from the weak topology and the identity $\pi_f \circ \sigma = f$, while the fact that $\sigma[X]$ is $C$-embedded is proved by showing that the induced homomorphism $\sigma'$ maps $C(P)$ onto $C(X)$ via $\sigma'\pi_f = f$, then applying Theorem 10.3(b).
