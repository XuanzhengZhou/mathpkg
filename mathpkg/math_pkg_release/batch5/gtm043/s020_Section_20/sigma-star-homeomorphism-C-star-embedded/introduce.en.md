The bounded-function analogue of Theorem 11.3(a). The map $\sigma_*$ embeds $X$ into the product $P_* = \mathbf{R}^{C^*(X)}$ by evaluating at each bounded continuous function. The proof is exactly analogous to the unbounded case: $\pi_{*f} \circ \sigma_* = f$ for each $f \in C^*(X)$ gives continuity, and the induced homomorphism argument yields that $\sigma_*[X]$ is $C^*$-embedded. This embedding provides the foundation for constructing the Stone-Čech compactification as the closure of $\sigma_*[X]$ in $P_*$.
