Certainly, $\tau$ is continuous, and $\tau \circ \sigma = \sigma_*$. The latter states that the restriction of $\tau$ to $\sigma[X]$ is the homeomorphism $\sigma_* \circ \sigma^{-1}$. Since $\sigma[X]$ is dense in $H$, and $H_*$ is Hausdorff, $\tau$ is the unique continuous extension of this homeomorphism to $H$. Therefore $\tau$ is a homeomorphism of $H$ onto its image, which is contained in $H_*$.
