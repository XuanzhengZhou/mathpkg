Since $\sigma$ is a homeomorphism with $\sigma[X]$ $C$-embedded in $P$, the ring $C(X)$ is isomorphic to $C(\sigma[X])$. Passing to the closure, this isomorphism extends naturally to $C(\operatorname{cl} \sigma[X])$ via the restriction of the projection maps $\pi_f$ from $P$ to $\operatorname{cl} \sigma[X]$. This explicit identification is essential for the proof that the closure is realcompact.
