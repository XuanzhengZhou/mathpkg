Theorem 11.9 provides the bounded-function analogue of the $H$ characterization. The proof of (a) differs from that of Theorem 11.7 because zero-sets of ideals in $C^*(X)$ need not have the finite intersection property; instead one works with $e$-filters. Part (b) states that every maximal ideal in $C^*(H_*)$ is fixed -- the analogue of realcompactness. To conclude $H_*$ is compact from (b) requires Theorem 4.11 (embedding arbitrary ideals in maximal ideals), which involves the axiom of choice.
