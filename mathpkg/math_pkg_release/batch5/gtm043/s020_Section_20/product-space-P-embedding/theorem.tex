Let

$$P = \mathbf{R}^{C(X)} = \prod_{f \in C(X)} \mathbf{R}_f,$$

where each $\mathbf{R}_f$ is a copy of the real line $\mathbf{R}$. The points of $P$ are precisely the real-valued functions whose domain is $C(X)$: each point $p$ of $P$ has the form

$$p = (p_f)_{f \in C(X)},$$

where the real number $p_f$ is the value of $p$ at $f$.

For each $f \in C(X)$, $\pi_f$ denotes the projection of $P$ onto $\mathbf{R}$ defined by

$$\pi_f(p) = p_f.$$

The topology of the product space $P$ is the weak topology induced by the family of all functions $\pi_f$.

The product $P_*$, the projections $\pi_{*f}$, and the topology of $P_*$ are described in an analogous manner, with $C(X)$ replaced by $C^*(X)$:

$$P_* = \mathbf{R}^{C^*(X)} = \prod_{f \in C^*(X)} \mathbf{R}_f.$$
