The embedding of a completely regular space into a product of real lines is the core construction behind both the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$ and the realcompactification $v X$. Two product spaces are defined: $P = \mathbf{R}^{C(X)}$, indexed by all continuous real-valued functions on $X$, and $P_* = \mathbf{R}^{C^*(X)}$, indexed by all bounded continuous functions. Each point of $P$ is simply a real-valued function on $C(X)$, and the topology is the weak topology induced by the projection maps $\pi_f(p) = p_f$. This construction generalizes Tychonoff's original embedding of $X$ into a cube and provides the ambient space for the subsequent homeomorphic embeddings $\sigma$ and $\sigma_*$.
