Theorem 11.7 gives an algebraic characterization of the closure of $\sigma[X]$ in $P$. The set $H$ consists exactly of those points $p \in P$ that act as ring homomorphisms from $C(X)$ onto $\mathbf{R}$. The proof shows $H$ is closed (as an intersection of closed sets defined by the homomorphism conditions) and that every basic neighborhood of a homomorphism $p$ contains some $\sigma x$, using the fact that the kernel of $p$ is a real maximal ideal whose zero-sets have the finite intersection property.
