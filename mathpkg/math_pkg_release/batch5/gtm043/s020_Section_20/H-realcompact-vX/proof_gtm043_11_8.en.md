Let $M$ be any real maximal ideal in $C(H)$. Now, as was noted in 11.4(a), $C(X)$ is isomorphic with $C(H)$, under the mapping $f \mapsto \pi_f|H$. Therefore the set

$$K = \{f \in C(X) : \pi_f|H \in M\}$$

is a real maximal ideal in $C(X)$. This implies that $K$ is the kernel of some homomorphism $p \in H$, so that $f \in K$ if and only if $\pi_f(p) = 0$. Hence, $M$ is the fixed ideal $M_p$ in $C(H)$. Thus, every real maximal ideal in $C(H)$ is fixed, which means $H$ is realcompact.

Since $\sigma[X]$ is homeomorphic with $X$ and is $C$-embedded in $H$, $H = v(\sigma[X])$.
