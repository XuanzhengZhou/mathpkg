The spaces $W$ and $W^*$ are fundamental examples in the study of rings of continuous functions. Here $W = W(\omega_1)$ is the set of all countable ordinals equipped with the order topology, and $W^* = W(\omega_1 + 1)$ adds the first uncountable ordinal $\omega_1$ as the maximum point. The space $W^*$ is compact, while $W$ is not; indeed $W^*$ is precisely the one-point compactification of $W$. These spaces serve as key examples for pseudocompactness, realcompactness, and the behavior of maximal ideals in $C(X)$.
