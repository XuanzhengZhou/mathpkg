Under the canonical isomorphism $C(W^*) \cong C(W)$, the fixed maximal ideal $M_{\omega_1}$ in $C(W^*)$ (consisting of functions vanishing at $\omega_1$) corresponds to the ideal $M^{\omega_1}$ in $C(W)$. This ideal consists precisely of all functions in $C(W)$ that vanish on a tail $W - W(\alpha)$ for some $\alpha < \omega_1$. The quotient $C(W)/M^{\omega_1} \cong \mathbb{R}$ evaluates a function at its final constant value. The zero-set filter $Z[M^{\omega_1}]$ has the countable intersection property and is closed under countable intersections.
