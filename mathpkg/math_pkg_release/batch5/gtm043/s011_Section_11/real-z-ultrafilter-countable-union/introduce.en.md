In a real $z$-ultrafilter $\mathcal{A}$, the countable intersection property dualizes to a countable union property: if a countable union $\bigcup_{n} Z_n$ of zero-sets belongs to $\mathcal{A}$, then $Z_n \in \mathcal{A}$ for at least one index $n$. The proof is an exact analogue of the set-theoretic proof (see 2.13) that any $z$-ultrafilter is prime. This property further distinguishes real $z$-ultrafilters from general $z$-ultrafilters.
