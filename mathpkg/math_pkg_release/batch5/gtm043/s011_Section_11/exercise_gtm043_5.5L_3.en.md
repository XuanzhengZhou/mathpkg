Prove directly from the fact that $\mathcal{A}$ is real that for every $f \in C(W)$, there exists $r \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $f^{\leftarrow}(r)$ is cofinal in $W$. As in 5.12, this implies that $f$ is constant on a tail.
