Every continuous real-valued function $f$ on $W = W(\omega_1)$ is eventually constant: there exists a countable ordinal $\alpha$ such that $f$ takes the same value $r$ on the entire tail $W - W(\alpha)$. The proof uses the countable compactness of tails together with a nested intersection argument on the image sets $f[W - W(\sigma)]$. This property is fundamental to the structure of $C(W)$ and its maximal ideals.
