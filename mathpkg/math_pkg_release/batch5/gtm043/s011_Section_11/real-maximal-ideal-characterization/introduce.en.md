A maximal ideal $M$ in $C(X)$ is real (i.e., $C(X)/M \cong \mathbb{R}$) if and only if its associated $z$-filter $Z[M]$ is closed under countable intersections. Equivalently, $Z[M]$ has the countable intersection property. The proof that (1) implies (2) constructs a continuous function $g(x) = \sum (|f_n(x)| \wedge 2^{-n})$ using uniform convergence, then argues via the realness of $M$ that $Z(g) = \bigcap Z(f_n)$ must belong to $Z[M]$. The remaining implications link the algebraic property of being real to a purely set-theoretic intersection property of zero-sets.
