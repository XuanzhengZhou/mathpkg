For every ordinal $\alpha$, the well-ordered space $W(\alpha) = \{\sigma : \sigma < \alpha\}$ equipped with the order topology is a normal space. The Hausdorff axiom is trivially verified. To establish normality, given disjoint closed sets $H$ and $K$, one constructs for each $\tau \in H$ an open interval of the form $[\sigma, \tau]$ that does not meet $K$, and symmetrically for each $\tau \in K$. The unions of these intervals provide separating open sets, demonstrating that disjoint closed sets can be separated by disjoint open neighborhoods in $W(\alpha)$.
