For every ordinal $\alpha$, the well-ordered space $W(\alpha)$ is always locally compact. This follows from the compactness of initial segments: each point $\sigma \in W(\alpha)$ has a compact neighborhood of the form $W(\sigma + 1)$. Furthermore, if $W(\alpha)$ itself is not compact, then the successor space $W(\alpha + 1)$ serves as its one-point compactification, adding a single point at infinity that compactifies the space.
