A space $X$ is realcompact if and only if every real $z$-ultrafilter on $X$ is fixed. Now suppose $X$ is countable and $\mathcal{A}$ is a free $z$-filter on $X$. Since $\mathcal{A}$ is free, $\bigcap \mathcal{A} = \varnothing$. But $\mathcal{A}$ consists of subsets of the countable set $X$, so by taking a countable subfamily whose intersection is already empty, we see that $\mathcal{A}$ cannot have the countable intersection property. Therefore no free $z$-filter on a countable space can have the countable intersection property. In particular, there are no free real $z$-ultrafilters. Hence every real $z$-ultrafilter on $X$ is fixed, and $X$ is realcompact.
