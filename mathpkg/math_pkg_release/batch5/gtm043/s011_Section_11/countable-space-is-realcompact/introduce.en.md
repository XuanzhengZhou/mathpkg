Every countable space is realcompact. This follows immediately from the characterization of realcompactness: $X$ is realcompact iff every real $z$-ultrafilter on $X$ is fixed. On a countable space, any free $z$-filter would consist of subsets of a countable set; by the pigeonhole principle, no free $z$-filter on a countable set can have the countable intersection property, hence there are no free real $z$-ultrafilters. Therefore all real $z$-ultrafilters are fixed, and $X$ is realcompact.
