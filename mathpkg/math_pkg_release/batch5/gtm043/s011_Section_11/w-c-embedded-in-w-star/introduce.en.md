The space $W = W(\omega_1)$ is $C$-embedded in its one-point compactification $W^* = W(\omega_1 + 1)$. By 5.12(c), every $f \in C(W)$ is constant on a tail; letting $r$ be that final constant value, one extends $f$ to $f^\beta \in C(W^*)$ by setting $f^\beta(\omega_1) = r$. This extension is unique and continuous, so the restriction map $C(W^*) \to C(W)$ is an isomorphism. This contrasts with the fact that $W(\omega)$ is not $C^*$-embedded in $W(\omega + 1)$.
