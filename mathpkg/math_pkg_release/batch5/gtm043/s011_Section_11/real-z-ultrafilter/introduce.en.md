A $z$-ultrafilter $Z[M]$ on $X$ is called a \emph{real $z$-ultrafilter} when it comes from a real maximal ideal $M$ in $C(X)$. By Theorem 5.14, these are exactly the $z$-ultrafilters possessing the countable intersection property. A space $X$ is realcompact if and only if every real $z$-ultrafilter on $X$ is fixed. Note that not every $z$-filter with the countable intersection property is contained in a real $z$-ultrafilter (see 8H.5 for a counterexample).
