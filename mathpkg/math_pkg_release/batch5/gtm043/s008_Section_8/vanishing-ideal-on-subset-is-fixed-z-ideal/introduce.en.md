For any nonempty subset $S$ of $X$, the set of all continuous functions vanishing on $S$ forms a fixed ideal and, more specifically, a $z$-ideal. This construction provides a natural bridge between the topology of $X$ (via subsets) and the algebra of $C(X)$ (via ideals). The ideal is nonzero precisely when $S$ is not dense, and its analogue in $C^*(X)$ is obtained by intersecting with the bounded functions.
