A fixed ideal in $C(X)$ or $C^*(X)$ is an ideal whose member functions share a common zero. Precisely, the intersection of the zero-sets of all functions in the ideal is nonempty, meaning there exists at least one point $p \in X$ at which every function in the ideal vanishes. The fixed/free dichotomy is fundamental to the theory of rings of continuous functions: fixed maximal ideals have a straightforward description in terms of points of $X$, while free maximal ideals require deeper analysis and constitute the core challenge of the theory.
