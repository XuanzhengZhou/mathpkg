The set $C_K(\mathbf{N})$ of finitely supported functions on $\mathbf{N}$ -- those vanishing outside some finite set -- is a natural example of a free ideal in both $C(\mathbf{N})$ and $C^*(\mathbf{N})$. No single point of $\mathbf{N}$ is a common zero for all finitely supported functions, making the ideal free. This ideal turns out to be the intersection of all free ideals in these rings, making it a canonical and important object in the theory.
