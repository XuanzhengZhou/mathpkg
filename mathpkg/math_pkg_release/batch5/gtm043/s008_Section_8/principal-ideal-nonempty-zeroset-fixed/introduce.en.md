If a continuous function $f$ has a nonempty zero-set, then the principal ideal generated by $f$ is automatically a fixed ideal. This follows because the intersection of the zero-sets of all multiples $fg$ is exactly $Z(f)$, the zero-set of $f$ itself. Thus the fixed nature of a principal ideal is directly determined by whether the generator has a zero.
