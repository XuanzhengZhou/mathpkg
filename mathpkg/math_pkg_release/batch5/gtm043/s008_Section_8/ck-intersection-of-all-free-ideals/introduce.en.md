This theorem reveals a striking structural property: the ideal $C_K(\mathbf{N})$ of finitely supported functions is precisely the intersection of all free ideals in $C(\mathbf{N})$ and $C^*(\mathbf{N})$. The proof uses the characteristic functions $\chi_{\{n\}}$ as building blocks: any free ideal must contain every such function (otherwise a common zero would exist), and the ideal generated by all $\chi_{\{n\}}$ is exactly $C_K(\mathbf{N})$. This makes $C_K(\mathbf{N})$ a canonical minimal free ideal -- the "core" contained in every free ideal.
