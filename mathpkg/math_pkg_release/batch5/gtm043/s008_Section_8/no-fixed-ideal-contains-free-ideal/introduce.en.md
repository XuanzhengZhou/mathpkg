The fixed and free classes of ideals are separated by containment: a fixed ideal can never contain a free ideal. This is an immediate consequence of the definitions -- if a fixed ideal $I$ contained a free ideal $J$, then every point in $\bigcap Z[I]$ would be a common zero for all functions in $J$, contradicting freeness. This simple fact underscores the fundamentally different nature of fixed and free ideals.
