The function $j(n) = 1/n$ on $\mathbf{N}$ provides a canonical example of a free ideal generator in $C^*(\mathbf{N})$. Since $j$ tends to zero at infinity but never vanishes on $\mathbf{N}$, any ideal containing $j$ cannot have a common zero and is thus free. Since $j$ is not a unit (it is not bounded away from zero), such ideals exist, and maximal ideals containing $j$ give concrete examples of free maximal ideals.
