title: "Almost disjoint subsets"
---
Any set of cardinal $\mathfrak{m} \geq \aleph_0$ has a family of more than $\mathfrak{m}$ subsets, each of power $\mathfrak{m}$, such that the intersection of any two is of power $< \mathfrak{m}$.

[Hint: In Lemma 12.8, $X \times Y$ has such a family.] (For the special case $\mathfrak{m} = \aleph_0$, a stronger result is given in 6Q.1.)
