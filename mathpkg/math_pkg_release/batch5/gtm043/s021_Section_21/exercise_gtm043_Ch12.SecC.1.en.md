title: "Zero-sets of large cardinal"
---
Let $\mathfrak{m} \geq \mathfrak{n} \geq \aleph_0$, and let $X$ be the discrete space of power $\mathfrak{m}$.

1. There exists an ultrafilter $\mathcal{U}$ on $X$ such that $|Z| \geq \mathfrak{n}$ for every $Z \in \mathcal{U}$, and for each subset $S$ of $X$, the family
$$\{Z_y : y \in S\} \cup \{X - Z_y : y \notin S\}$$
has the finite intersection property, and so is contained in an ultrafilter $\mathcal{U}_S$;

2. If $S \neq S'$, then $\mathcal{U}_S \neq \mathcal{U}_{S'}$.
