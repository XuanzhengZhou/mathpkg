This strengthens Theorem 12.7: not only is $|C/M| > \mathfrak{m}$, but no subset of cardinality $\leq \mathfrak{m}$ can be cofinal. Combined with the upper bound $|C/M| \leq 2^{\mathfrak{m}}$ (from the fact that $|C(X)| \leq 2^{\mathfrak{m}}$), the cardinality is pinned down precisely when no intermediate cardinal exists.
