A cardinal $\mathfrak{m}$ is measurable if there exists a nontrivial $\{0,1\}$-valued measure on a set of cardinality $\mathfrak{m}$. Such a measure must assign measure $1$ to the whole set but $0$ to every singleton. The existence of measurable cardinals is a celebrated unsolved problem in set theory; they are closely tied to the theory of realcompactness of discrete spaces.
