A proper subfield of an ordered field cannot contain any interval. The proof: subtracting $a$ shows $E$ contains all positive elements below $b-a$; taking reciprocals gives all elements above $1/(b-a)$; and every element of $F$ can be expressed using these. This fact helps construct transcendence bases by showing certain subfields are proper.
