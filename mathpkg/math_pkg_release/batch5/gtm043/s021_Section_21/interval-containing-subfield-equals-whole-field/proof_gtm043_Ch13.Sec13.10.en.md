If $E$ contains $[a, b)$, then subtracting $a$ yields $\{x \in F : 0 \leq x < b-a\} \subset E$. Taking reciprocals, every element $y \in F$ with $y > 1/(b-a)$ belongs to $E$.

Now, any $w \in F$ can be written as $w = u - v$ where $u, v > 1/(b-a)$ (e.g., by adding a sufficiently large element of $E$ to $w$ and separating). Since $E$ is a field closed under subtraction, $w \in E$. Therefore $E = F$.
