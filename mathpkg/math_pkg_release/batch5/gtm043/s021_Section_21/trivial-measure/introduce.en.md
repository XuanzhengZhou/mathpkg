Every set $X$ admits certain trivial $\{0,1\}$-valued measures: the zero measure assigning $0$ to all subsets, and the point-mass measures (Dirac measures) that assign $1$ to sets containing a fixed point $x$ and $0$ otherwise. A cardinal is nonmeasurable precisely when every $\{0,1\}$-valued measure is trivial.
