A $\{0,1\}$-valued measure $\mu$ is called \emph{$\mathfrak{m}$-additive} if $\mu(\bigcup_{s \in S} A_s) = 0$ whenever $(A_s)_{s \in S}$ is a family of disjoint sets of measure zero, with $|S| = \mathfrak{m}$.

Equivalently, $\mu$ is $\mathfrak{m}$-additive if and only if the intersection of any $\mathfrak{m}$ sets of measure $1$ is of measure $1$. An $\mathfrak{m}$-additive measure is automatically $\mathfrak{n}$-additive for every $\mathfrak{n} \leq \mathfrak{m}$.
