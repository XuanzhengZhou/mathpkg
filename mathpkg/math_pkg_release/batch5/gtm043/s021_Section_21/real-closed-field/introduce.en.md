A real-closed field is an ordered field admitting no proper algebraic ordered extension, equivalently one for which adjoining $\sqrt{-1}$ yields an algebraically closed field. $\mathbb{R}$ and the real algebraic numbers are examples. The unique ordering is definable from the field structure. The Artin-Schreier theorem guarantees every ordered field has a unique real-closed algebraic extension.
