Let $I$ be an absolutely convex ideal in a lattice-ordered ring $A$. Every countable family $E$ in $A/I$ has a family $F$ of preimages in $A$ — that is, $E = \{I(a) : a \in F\}$ — such that for all $a, b \in F$,
$$I(a) \leq I(b) \ \text{in} \ E \ \text{implies} \ a \leq b \ \text{in} \ A.$$
Furthermore, $F$ can include a specified preimage of any one element of $E$.
