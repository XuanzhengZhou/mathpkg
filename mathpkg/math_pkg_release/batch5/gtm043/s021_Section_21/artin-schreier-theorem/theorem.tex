Every ordered field $E$ has an algebraic extension to a real-closed field $\mathcal{R}E$ whose order is an extension of the order on $E$. Furthermore, $\mathcal{R}E$ is unique up to an isomorphism that leaves the elements of $E$ fixed. Since it is an algebraic extension, $\mathcal{R}E$ is equipotent with $E$ whenever $E$ is infinite.
