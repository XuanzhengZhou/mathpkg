The first assertion is a special case of 6.9(c): an isolated point $\{x\}$ is open-and-closed in $X$, so its closure in $\beta X$ is open-and-closed, hence $\{x\}$ is isolated in $\beta X$.

The second assertion follows from 3.15: $X$ is open in $\beta X$ if and only if $X$ is locally compact.
