Let $X$ be dense in a space $T$, and let $\mathcal{F}$ be a $z$-filter (or filter) on $X$. A point $p \in T$ is a \text{cluster point} of $\mathcal{F}$ if every neighborhood (in $T$) of $p$ meets every member of $\mathcal{F}$; in other words,

$$p \in \bigcap_{Z \in \mathcal{F}} \operatorname{cl}_T Z.$$
