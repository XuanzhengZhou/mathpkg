The Stone-Čech Compactification Theorem asserts that every completely regular space $X$ admits a unique compactification $\beta X$ characterized by five equivalent properties. The most important of these is Stone's theorem (I): every continuous map from $X$ into a compact space extends uniquely to $\beta X$. Equivalently (II), $X$ is $C^*$-embedded in $\beta X$, meaning every bounded continuous function on $X$ extends to $\beta X$. The construction adjoins one new point for each free $z$-ultrafilter on $X$ to serve as its limit, and the topology is defined by taking $\{\operatorname{cl}_{\beta X} Z : Z \in Z(X)\}$ as a closed base. The theorem links the maximal ideal theory of $C(X)$ and $C^*(X)$ to the topology of compactifications.
