Every (completely regular) space $X$ has a compactification $\beta X$, with the following equivalent properties.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(I)] (Stone) Every continuous mapping $\tau$ from $X$ into any compact space $Y$ has a continuous extension $\bar{\tau}$ from $\beta X$ into $Y$.
\item[(II)] (Stone--\v{C}ech) Every function $f$ in $C^*(X)$ has an extension to a function $f^\beta$ in $C(\beta X)$.
\item[(III)] (\v{C}ech) Any two disjoint zero-sets in $X$ have disjoint closures in $\beta X$.
\item[(IV)] For any two zero-sets $Z_1$ and $Z_2$ in $X$,
$$\operatorname{cl}_{\beta X}(Z_1 \cap Z_2) = \operatorname{cl}_{\beta X} Z_1 \cap \operatorname{cl}_{\beta X} Z_2.$$
\item[(V)] Distinct $z$-ultrafilters on $X$ have distinct limits in $\beta X$.
\end{enumerate}

Furthermore, $\beta X$ is unique, in the following sense: if a compactification $T$ of $X$ satisfies any one of the listed conditions, then there exists a homeomorphism of $\beta X$ onto $T$ that leaves $X$ pointwise fixed.
