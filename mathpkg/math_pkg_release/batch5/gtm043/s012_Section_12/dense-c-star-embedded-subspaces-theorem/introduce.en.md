Theorem 6.7 specifies all spaces in which $X$ is dense and $C^*$-embedded: they are precisely the subspaces of $\beta X$ that contain $X$. The only compact one among them is $\beta X$ itself. Equivalently, $T$ has the same Stone-Čech compactification as $X$ ($\beta T = \beta X$). This result crystallizes the relationship between $C^*$-embedding, denseness, and the Stone-Čech compactification.
