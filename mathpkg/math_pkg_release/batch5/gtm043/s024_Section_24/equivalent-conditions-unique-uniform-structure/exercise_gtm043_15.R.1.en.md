The following conditions are equivalent for any completely regular space $X$. [Successive implications are suggested in the hints.] By (7) and 6J, another equivalent condition is $|\beta X - X| \leq 1$. In particular, $W$, $T$, and $\Omega$ (8L) are noncompact spaces having unique structures. Recall that $T$ is not even countably compact.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $X$ admits a unique structure.
\item[(2)] Every continuous mapping from $X$ into any uniform space is uniformly continuous in every admissible structure on $X$. [15G.5.]
\item[(3)] Every function in $C(X)$ is uniformly continuous in every admissible structure on $X$.
\item[(4)] Every function in $C^*(X)$ is uniformly continuous in every admissible structure on $X$.
\item[(5)] Every function in $C^*(X)$ is uniformly continuous in every admissible precompact structure on $X$.
\item[(6)] $X$ admits only one precompact structure. [$\mathcal{C}^*$ is the smallest precompact structure. By 15I.3, it is also the largest.]
\item[(7)] $X$ has a unique compactification. [(7) implies (1). By 6J, $X$ is pseudo-compact. Apply 15J.4 and 15Q.1.]
\end{enumerate}
