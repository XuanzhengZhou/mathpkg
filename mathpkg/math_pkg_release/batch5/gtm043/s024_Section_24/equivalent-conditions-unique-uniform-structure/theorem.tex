For a completely regular space $X$, the following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $X$ admits a unique admissible uniform structure.
\item[(2)] Every continuous mapping from $X$ into any uniform space is uniformly continuous in every admissible structure on $X$.
\item[(3)] Every function in $C(X)$ is uniformly continuous in every admissible structure on $X$.
\item[(4)] Every function in $C^*(X)$ is uniformly continuous in every admissible structure on $X$.
\item[(5)] Every function in $C^*(X)$ is uniformly continuous in every admissible precompact structure on $X$.
\item[(6)] $X$ admits only one precompact structure.
\item[(7)] $X$ has a unique compactification.
\end{enumerate}
Another equivalent condition is $|\beta X - X| \leq 1$.
