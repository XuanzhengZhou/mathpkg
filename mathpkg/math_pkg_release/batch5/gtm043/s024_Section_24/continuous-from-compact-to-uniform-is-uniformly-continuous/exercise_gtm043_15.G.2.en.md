Prove that every continuous mapping from a compact space into a uniform space is uniformly continuous. [Let $f: X \to [Y; \mathcal{U}]$ be continuous, where $X$ is compact. For $\epsilon > 0$, for each $x$, there exists $d_x \in \mathcal{D}$ such that $d_x(x, y) \leq 1$ implies $u(f(x), f(y)) \leq \epsilon$. Apply compactness of $X$, and (i) and (ii) of 15.3.]
