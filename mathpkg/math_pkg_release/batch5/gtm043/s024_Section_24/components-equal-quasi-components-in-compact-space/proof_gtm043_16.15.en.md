Let $\mathcal{H}$ denote the family of open-and-closed sets containing a point $x$. We show first that $S = \bigcap \mathcal{H}$ is connected. Let $\{K_1, K_2\}$ be a partition of $S$, with $x \in K_1$, and let $H_1$ and $H_2$ be as in Lemma 16.13. Since $H_1 \cup H_2$ is the intersection of a finite family of open-and-closed sets, $H_1$ is open-and-closed in $X$. Now, $x \in K_1 \subset H_1$, and so $S \subset H_1$. Therefore $S = S \cap H_1 = K_1$; thus, $S$ is connected.

If $A$ is any set containing $S$, and $y \in A - S$, then there exists an open-and-closed set containing $S$ but not $y$. Consequently, $A$ is not connected, and, therefore, $S$ is a maximal connected set.
