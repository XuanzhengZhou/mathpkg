A sequence $(x_n)_{n \in \mathbf{N}}$ in a metric space $X$ is a Cauchy sequence if and only if the family of sets $\{x_n\}_{n \geq k}$, for $k \in \mathbf{N}$, contains arbitrarily small sets. $X$ is sequentially complete if every Cauchy sequence converges. Prove that a metric space $X$ is complete if and only if it is sequentially complete. [Sufficiency. From a given Cauchy $z$-filter, select $Z_n$ of diameter $\leq 1/n$, and choose $x_n \in Z_n$.]
