A Lindelöf space is a topological space in which every open cover admits a countable subcover. Equivalently, every family of closed subsets with the countable intersection property has nonempty intersection. This covering property is weaker than compactness but still strong enough to guarantee realcompactness: every Lindelöf space is realcompact. Important examples include $\sigma$-compact spaces (countable unions of compact subspaces), spaces with a countable base, and all separable metric spaces.
