This theorem establishes that the Lindelöf covering property is a sufficient condition for realcompactness. The proof is brief: in a Lindelöf space, every $z$-filter with the countable intersection property is fixed, which in turn implies that every real maximal ideal is fixed. Consequently, all $\sigma$-compact spaces, all second-countable spaces, and all separable metric spaces are realcompact. In particular, every subspace of a Euclidean space --- including $\mathbb{R}$ itself --- is realcompact.
