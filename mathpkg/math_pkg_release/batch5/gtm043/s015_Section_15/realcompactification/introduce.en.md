A realcompactification of a completely regular space $X$ is a realcompact space containing $X$ as a dense subspace. Every compactification is a realcompactification (since compact spaces are realcompact). The key question is to find a realcompactification in which $X$ is $C$-embedded --- this leads to the Hewitt realcompactification $vX$, which is the maximal such space contained in the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$.
