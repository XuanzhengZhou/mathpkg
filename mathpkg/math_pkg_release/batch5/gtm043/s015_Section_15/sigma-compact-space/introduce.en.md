A $\sigma$-compact space is a topological space that can be expressed as a countable union of compact subspaces. This property lies between compactness and the Lindelöf property: every $\sigma$-compact space is Lindelöf, and hence realcompact. In particular, every countable space is $\sigma$-compact (as each singleton is compact), giving another proof that countable spaces are realcompact.
