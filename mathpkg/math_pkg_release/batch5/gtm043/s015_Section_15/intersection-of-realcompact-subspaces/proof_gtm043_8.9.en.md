Let $(Y_\alpha)$ be a family of realcompact subspaces of a space $Y$, and let $X = \bigcap_\alpha Y_\alpha$. For each $\alpha$, the identity mapping $\tau$ from $X$ into $Y$ has a continuous extension from $vX$ into the realcompact space $Y_\alpha$ (Theorem 8.7). As $\tau$ can have only one continuous extension from $vX$ into $Y$, these extensions all coincide; hence this common extension carries $vX$ into $X$. Therefore $vX \subset X$, which implies $vX = X$ and $X$ is realcompact.
