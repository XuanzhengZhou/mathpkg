This theorem establishes that the ring $C(X)$ of all continuous real-valued functions is a complete algebraic invariant for realcompact spaces: two realcompact spaces are homeomorphic precisely when their function rings are isomorphic. The correspondence $p \mapsto M_p$ maps points of a realcompact space bijectively onto the set of all real maximal ideals of $C(X)$, and the property of being a real maximal ideal is algebraically determined. Thus the topological structure of a realcompact space can be recovered entirely from the algebraic structure of $C(X)$.
