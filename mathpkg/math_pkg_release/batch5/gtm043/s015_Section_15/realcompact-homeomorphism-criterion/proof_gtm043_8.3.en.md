Necessity is obvious: if $X$ and $Y$ are homeomorphic, then $C(X)$ and $C(Y)$ are isomorphic.

For sufficiency, suppose $C(X) \cong C(Y)$. The correspondence $p \mapsto M_p$ is one-to-one from the realcompact space $X$ onto the set of all real maximal ideals in $C(X)$; and the property of being a real maximal ideal is an algebraic invariant. This means that the points of $X$ can be recovered from the algebraic structure of the ring $C(X)$. Furthermore, the purely algebraic relation between ideals determines the topology, and for realcompact $X$, this topological space of ideals is homeomorphic with $X$. Since $C(X) \cong C(Y)$ algebraically, the corresponding spaces of real maximal ideals are homeomorphic, and therefore $X$ and $Y$ themselves are homeomorphic.
