This is the central theorem of Chapter 8, establishing the existence and universal properties of the Hewitt realcompactification $vX$. Every completely regular space $X$ admits a unique realcompactification $vX \subset \beta X$ characterized by any of five equivalent properties: (I) the extension property for maps into arbitrary realcompact spaces, (II) $C$-embedding of $X$ in $vX$, (III)-(IV) preservation of countable intersections of zero-sets, and (V) convergence of real $z$-ultrafilters. The space $vX$ is the realcompact analogue of the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$.
