Every (completely regular) space $X$ has a realcompactification $vX$, contained in $\beta X$, with the following equivalent properties.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(I)] Every continuous mapping $\tau$ from $X$ into any realcompact space $Y$ has a continuous extension $\tau^\circ$ from $vX$ into $Y$. (Necessarily, $\tau^\circ = \bar{\tau}|_{vX}$, where $\bar{\tau}$ is the Stone extension into $\beta Y$.)
\item[(II)] Every function $f \in C(X)$ has an extension to a function $f^\nu \in C(vX)$. (Necessarily, $f^\nu = f^*|_{vX}$.)
\item[(III)] If a countable family of zero-sets in $X$ has empty intersection, then their closures in $vX$ have empty intersection.
\item[(IV)] For any countable family of zero-sets $Z_n$ in $X$,
  $$\mathrm{cl}_{vX} \bigcap_n Z_n = \bigcap_n \mathrm{cl}_{vX} Z_n.$$
\item[(V)] Every point of $vX$ is the limit of a unique $z$-ultrafilter on $X$, and it is a real $z$-ultrafilter.
\end{enumerate}
Furthermore, the space $vX$ is unique, in the following sense: if a realcompactification $T$ of $X$ satisfies any one of the listed conditions, then there exists a homeomorphism of $vX$ onto $T$ that leaves $X$ pointwise fixed.
