The Hewitt realcompactification $vX$ of a completely regular space $X$ is defined as the subspace of $\beta X$ consisting of all points whose associated $z$-ultrafilter is real (i.e., has the countable intersection property). It satisfies $X \subset vX \subset \beta X$ and $\beta(vX) = \beta X$. The space $vX$ is the unique maximal subspace of $\beta X$ in which $X$ is $C$-embedded, and it serves as the universal realcompactification of $X$.
