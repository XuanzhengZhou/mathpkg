The multiplicative inverse $f^{-1}$ of a function $f$ is defined pointwise as the reciprocal $1/f(x)$ at each point $x \in X$, provided $f(x) \neq 0$ for all $x$. Not every nonzero function has a multiplicative inverse in $\mathbf{R}^X$; the inverse exists exactly when $f$ never vanishes on $X$.
