This theorem confirms that the set of continuous functions is closed under the ring operations of pointwise addition and multiplication, and contains the constant function $1$ as multiplicative identity. The continuity of sums, products, and negatives of continuous functions is a standard result in topology, and it ensures that $C(X)$ inherits the full commutative ring structure from $\mathbf{R}^X$.
