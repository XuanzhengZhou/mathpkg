This theorem establishes an important implication: every countably compact space is pseudocompact. The proof uses the countable compactness property — that every countable open cover has a finite subcover — applied to the sets $\{x : |f(x)| < n\}$ for a continuous function $f$. The converse is false: there exist pseudocompact spaces that are not countably compact (see 5I in the book).
