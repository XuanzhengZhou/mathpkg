The meet operation $f \wedge g$ is the dual of the join operation, providing the greatest lower bound (infimum) of two functions in $\mathbf{R}^X$. Defined pointwise as the minimum of the two real values at each point, it satisfies the universal property of greatest lower bounds. Together with the join operation, this equips $\mathbf{R}^X$ with the structure of a lattice.
