The additive inverse of a function $f \in \mathbf{R}^X$ is defined pointwise: at each $x \in X$, the value of $-f$ is the negation $-(f(x))$. This follows naturally from the ring structure of $\mathbf{R}$ and ensures that $f + (-f) = 0$, the constant zero function. Together with the additive identity, this completes the additive group structure of $\mathbf{R}^X$.
