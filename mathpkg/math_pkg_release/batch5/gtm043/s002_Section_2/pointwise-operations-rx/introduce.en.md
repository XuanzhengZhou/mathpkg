Pointwise operations define the algebraic structure on $\mathbf{R}^X$ by performing addition and multiplication at each point $x \in X$ individually. These operations inherit associativity, commutativity, and distributivity directly from the field $\mathbf{R}$ of real numbers. The definitions are natural and universal: they do not reference any topology on $X$, which is why they are introduced before restricting attention to continuous functions.
