The fact that $\mathbf{R}^X$ is a commutative ring with unity follows directly from the pointwise definitions of addition and multiplication, which inherit the ring properties of $\mathbf{R}$. The constant zero function serves as the additive identity and the constant one function as the multiplicative identity. This proposition establishes the algebraic foundation on which all subsequent study of function rings is built.
