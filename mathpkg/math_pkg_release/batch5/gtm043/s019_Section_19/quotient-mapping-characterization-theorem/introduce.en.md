This theorem gives a function-algebraic criterion for a continuous surjection $\tau: X \to Y$ between completely regular spaces to be a quotient mapping. The condition is that every continuous function on $X$ which is constant on each fiber $\tau^{-1}(y)$ must factor through $\tau$, i.e., belong to the image of the induced homomorphism $\tau': C(Y) \to C(X)$. The hypothesis that $Y_{\tau}$ is completely regular is needed because quotient spaces of completely regular spaces need not be completely regular.
