Let $\tau$ be a continuous mapping from $X$ onto a completely regular space $Y$, and suppose that the quotient space $Y_{\tau}$ is also completely regular. Then $\tau$ is a quotient mapping if and only if $\tau'[C(Y)]$ contains (hence is) the set $A$ of all functions in $C(X)$ that are constant on all sets $\tau^{\leftarrow}(y)$, for $y \in Y$.
