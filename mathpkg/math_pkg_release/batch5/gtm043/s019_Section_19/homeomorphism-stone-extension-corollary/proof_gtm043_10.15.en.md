When $\tau$ is a homeomorphism, Lemma 6.11 can be applied to strengthen Theorem 10.13. The conclusion that $\tau_0$ carries $X_0 - X$ to $Y - \tau[X]$ follows from the properties established in Theorem 10.13 together with the injectivity of $\tau$. The particular case about compactifications follows by taking $Y$ to be a compactification of $X$ and $\tau$ to be the inclusion $X \hookrightarrow Y$, so that $\bar{\tau}: \beta X \to Y$ is the canonical map given by the universal property of $\beta X$.
