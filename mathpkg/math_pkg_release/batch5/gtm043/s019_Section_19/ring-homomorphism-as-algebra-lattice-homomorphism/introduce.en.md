This observation notes that every ring homomorphism $\mathfrak{s}: C(Y) \to C(X)$ is automatically an algebra homomorphism (preserving scalar multiplication by real constants, 1I) and a lattice homomorphism (preserving pointwise suprema and infima, Theorem 1.6). This remarkable fact is made transparent by the representation theorem: on the open-and-closed set $E$ the homomorphism acts by composition with a continuous map, which clearly preserves both algebraic and lattice operations.
