Given a quotient mapping $\tau: X \to Y$, the quotient space $Y_{\tau}$ is the set $Y$ equipped with the quotient topology: a subset is closed (resp. open) precisely when its preimage under $\tau$ is closed (resp. open) in $X$. This is the finest topology on $Y$ making $\tau$ continuous. The notation $Y_{\tau}$ emphasizes that the topology depends on the mapping $\tau$, not just the set $Y$.
