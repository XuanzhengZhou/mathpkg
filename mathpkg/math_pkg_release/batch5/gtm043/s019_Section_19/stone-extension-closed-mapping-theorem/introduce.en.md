Theorem 10.13 describes how a continuous map $\tau: X \to Y$ (where $\tau[X]$ is dense in $Y$) can be extended to a closed map $\tau_0: X_0 \to Y$ with compact fibers, where $X_0$ is the preimage of $Y$ under the Stone extension $\bar{\tau}: \beta X \to \beta Y$. The subspace $X_0$ of $\beta X$ is maximal for such extensions. This theorem is fundamental for understanding how spaces relate to their compactifications and provides a general framework for constructing spaces in which $X$ is dense.
