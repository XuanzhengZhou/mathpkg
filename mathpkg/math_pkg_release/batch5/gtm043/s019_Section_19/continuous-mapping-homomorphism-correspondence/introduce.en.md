This fundamental correspondence states that every continuous map $\tau: X \to vY$ into the Hewitt realcompactification of $Y$ induces a homomorphism $\mathfrak{s}: C(Y) \to C(X)$ by $\mathfrak{s}g = g^v \circ \tau$, and conversely, every homomorphism whose image contains the constant functions arises uniquely in this way. When the image contains the constants, the open-and-closed set $E$ in the representation theorem is all of $X$, so the homomorphism is globally induced by a continuous map into $vY$.
