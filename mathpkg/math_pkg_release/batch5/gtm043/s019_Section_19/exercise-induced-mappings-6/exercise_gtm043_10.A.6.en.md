Let $\sigma$ be a continuous mapping from $X$ into $Y$, and $\sigma': C^*(Y) \to C^*(X)$ the induced homomorphism. Define a homomorphism $\hat{s}: C(\beta Y) \to C(\beta X)$ by replacing the functions in $C^*(Y)$ and $C^*(X)$ by their extensions to $\beta Y$ and $\beta X$, respectively. Then $\hat{s}' = \bar{\sigma}$. (This contains the essence of the proof of Theorem 10.7.)
