This theorem provides an explicit structural description of an arbitrary ring homomorphism $\mathfrak{s}: C(Y) \to C(X)$. It shows that $\mathfrak{s}$ is determined by an open-and-closed subset $E$ of $X$ (the support of the idempotent $\mathfrak{s}\mathbf{1}$) together with a continuous map $\tau: E \to vY$ into the Hewitt realcompactification of $Y$. On $E$ the homomorphism acts by composition with $g^v \circ \tau$, while on the complement $X - E$ it vanishes identically. This representation simultaneously exhibits why every such homomorphism is automatically an algebra homomorphism and a lattice homomorphism.
