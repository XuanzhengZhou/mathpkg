For compact spaces $X$ and $Y$, the existence of a topological embedding of $X$ into $Y$ is equivalent to the purely algebraic condition that $C(X)$ is a homomorphic image of $C(Y)$. This theorem bridges topology and algebra, showing that the ring of continuous functions completely captures the embedding structure for compact spaces.
