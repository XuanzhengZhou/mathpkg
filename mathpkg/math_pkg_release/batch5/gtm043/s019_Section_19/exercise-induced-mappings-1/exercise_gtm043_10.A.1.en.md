Let $Y$ be realcompact, let $t$ be a homomorphism from $C(Y)$ into $C(X)$ such that $t\mathbf{1} = \mathbf{1}$, and let $t'$ denote the mapping $\tau$ of $X$ into $Y$ described in Theorem 10.6. If each point $x$ of $X$ is identified with the mapping $f \mapsto f(x)$ of $C(X)$ into $\mathbb{R}$, and similarly for $Y$, then $t'$ may be interpreted as a mapping induced by $t$ in the sense of 10.2.
