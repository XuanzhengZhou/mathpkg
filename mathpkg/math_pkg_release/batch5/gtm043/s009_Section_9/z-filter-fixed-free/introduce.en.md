A \(z\)-filter is classified as fixed if the intersection of all its member zero-sets is nonempty, and free otherwise. This terminology mirrors the classification of ideals in \(C(X)\): an ideal \(I\) is fixed precisely when its associated \(z\)-filter \(Z[I]\) is fixed. The correspondence between fixed ideals and fixed \(z\)-filters is fundamental to the Gillman–Jerison theory.
