This theorem gives a purely algebraic characterization of compactness for completely regular spaces: \(X\) is compact if and only if every ideal in \(C(X)\) is fixed. Equivalently, compactness means that every \(z\)-filter (or \(z\)-ultrafilter) on \(X\) is fixed. The same equivalences hold for the ring \(C^*(X)\) of bounded continuous functions, establishing a fundamental link between the topology of \(X\) and the ideal structure of its function rings.
