The equivalence of (1) with (2) is the special case \(Z = X\) of Lemma 4.10 (compact zero-set lemma). Likewise, (1) implies \((2^*)\) because \(C = C^*\) when \(X\) is compact (every continuous function on a compact space is bounded).

Next, if \(I\) is a free ideal in \(C\), then \(I \cap C^*\) is a free ideal in \(C^*\); therefore \((2^*)\) implies (2).

Finally, (2) is equivalent with (3), and \((2^*)\) with \((3^*)\), because every free ideal is contained in a free maximal ideal (by Zorn's lemma, every proper ideal extends to a maximal ideal, and if the original ideal is free, any maximal ideal containing it must also be free).
