Let \(\mathfrak{M} = \mathfrak{M}(X)\) denote the set of all maximal ideals in \(C(X)\). The Stone topology on \(\mathfrak{M}\) is defined by taking, as a base for the closed sets, all sets of the form

\[
\{ M \in \mathfrak{M} : f \in M \} \quad (f \in C(X)).
\]

For given \(f\), \(M_p\) belongs to this set if and only if \(f(p) = 0\). The one-one correspondence \(p \mapsto M_p\) carries the zero-sets in \(X\) onto the family of all such basic closed sets. Thus \(\mathfrak{M}\) is well defined as a topological space and is homeomorphic with \(X\) when \(X\) is compact.
