Let \(\mathcal{U}\) be a free ultrafilter on \(\mathbf{N}\), let \(\Sigma = \mathbf{N} \cup \{\sigma\}\) (where \(\sigma \notin \mathbf{N}\)), and define a topology on \(\Sigma\) as follows: all points of \(\mathbf{N}\) are isolated, and the neighborhoods of \(\sigma\) are the sets \(U \cup \{\sigma\}\) for \(U \in \mathcal{U}\).

\(\mathbf{N}\) is a dense subspace of \(\Sigma\). Every set containing \(\sigma\) is closed; hence every subset of \(\Sigma\) is open or closed. \(\Sigma\) is a normal space (cf. 3B.5 and 3D.5); in fact, every closed set is a zero-set.
