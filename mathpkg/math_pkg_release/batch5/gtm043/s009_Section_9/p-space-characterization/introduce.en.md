A \(P\)-space is a completely regular space in which every \(G_\delta\)-set is open. This theorem collects eleven equivalent characterizations, linking the topological property (every zero-set is open) with deep algebraic properties of the ring \(C(X)\): it is a regular ring (every principal ideal is generated by an idempotent), every ideal is a \(z\)-ideal and an intersection of maximal ideals, and every cozero-set is \(C\)-embedded. These equivalences showcase the Gillman–Jerison philosophy that topological properties of \(X\) are faithfully reflected in algebraic properties of \(C(X)\).
