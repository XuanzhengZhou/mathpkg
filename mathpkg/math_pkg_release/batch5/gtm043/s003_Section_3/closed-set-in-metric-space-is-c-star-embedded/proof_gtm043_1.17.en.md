In a metric space, every closed set is a zero-set, so that any two disjoint closed sets are completely separated. Now, if $S$ is closed, then closed sets in $S$ are also closed in $X$; therefore completely separated sets in $S$ have disjoint closures in $X$, and hence are completely separated in $X$. By Urysohn's Extension Theorem, $S$ is $C^*$-embedded in $X$.
