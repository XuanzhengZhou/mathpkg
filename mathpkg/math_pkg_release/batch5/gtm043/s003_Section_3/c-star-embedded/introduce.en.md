A subspace $S$ of a topological space $X$ is $C^*$-embedded if every bounded continuous real-valued function on $S$ admits a continuous extension to all of $X$. This is a fundamental extension property that bridges the topology of $S$ with that of $X$. The property is transitive: if $S \subset X \subset Y$ and $X$ is $C^*$-embedded in $Y$, then $S$ is $C^*$-embedded in $Y$ exactly when it is $C^*$-embedded in $X$.
