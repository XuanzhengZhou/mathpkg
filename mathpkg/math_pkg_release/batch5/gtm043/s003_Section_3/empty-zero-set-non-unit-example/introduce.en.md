The simplest example of a bounded continuous function with empty zero-set that fails to be a unit in $C^*$ is the sequence $j = (1/n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ on the discrete space $\mathbb{N}$. Its reciprocal is the unbounded identity function $i(n) = n$, which does not belong to $C^*(\mathbb{N})$. This illustrates the essential difference between $C$ and $C^*$ regarding units.
