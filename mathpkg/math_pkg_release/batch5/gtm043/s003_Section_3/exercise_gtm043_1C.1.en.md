Consider the subspaces $\mathbf{R}$, $\mathbf{Q}$, $\mathbf{N}$, and $\mathbf{N}^* = \{1, \frac{1}{2}, \cdots, 1/n, \cdots, 0\}$ of $\mathbf{R}$, and the rings $C$ and $C^*$ for each of these spaces. Each of these rings is of cardinal $\mathfrak{c}$.

For each $m \leq \aleph_0$, each ring on $\mathbf{R}$, $\mathbf{N}$, or $\mathbf{N}^*$ contains a function having exactly $2^m$ square roots. If a member of $C(\mathbf{Q})$ has more than one square root, it has $\mathfrak{c}$ of them.
