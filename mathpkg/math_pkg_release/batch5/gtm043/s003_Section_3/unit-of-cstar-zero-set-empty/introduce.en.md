For a function to be a unit (have a multiplicative inverse) in the ring $C^*(X)$ of bounded continuous functions, its zero-set must be empty. But unlike the situation in $C(X)$, this condition is not sufficient in $C^*(X)$: a bounded function may never vanish yet its reciprocal may be unbounded, hence not belong to $C^*(X)$. The classic example is the sequence $j = (1/n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ on $\mathbb{N}$, which has empty zero-set but its inverse $i(n) = n$ is unbounded.
