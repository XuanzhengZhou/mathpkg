For finite $m$, $X$ has $m$ components if and only if $1$ has $2^m$ square roots in $C(X)$. For infinite $m$, the statement is false. [Consider the subspace $\{1, \frac{1}{2}, \cdots, 1/n, \cdots, 0\}$ of $\mathbf{R}$.]
