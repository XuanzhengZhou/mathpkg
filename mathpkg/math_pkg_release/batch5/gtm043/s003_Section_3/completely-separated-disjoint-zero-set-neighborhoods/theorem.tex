If $A$ and $A'$ are completely separated, then they have disjoint zero-set-neighborhoods. Explicitly, there exists $f \in C(X)$ equal to $0$ on $A$ and $1$ on $A'$, for which
$$F = \{x: f(x) \leq \tfrac{1}{3}\}, \quad F' = \{x: f(x) \geq \tfrac{2}{3}\}$$
are disjoint zero-set-neighborhoods of $A$ and $A'$ respectively.
