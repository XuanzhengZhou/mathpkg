The family $Z(X)$ consists of all zero-sets of continuous functions on $X$. This notation is consistent with the image-of-a-set convention $Z[C'] = \{Z(f) : f \in C'\}$. An important observation is that $C(X)$ and $C^*(X)$ produce exactly the same family of zero-sets, because for any $f \in C$, the truncated function $g = |f| \wedge 1$ belongs to $C^*$ and has the same zero-set.
