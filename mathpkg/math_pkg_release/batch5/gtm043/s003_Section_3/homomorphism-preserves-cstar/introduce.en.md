If a ring homomorphism $t \colon C(Y) \to C(X)$ between rings of continuous functions has an image that contains all bounded continuous functions $C^*(X)$, then $t$ automatically maps the bounded functions of $Y$ onto the bounded functions of $X$. This result shows that the property of having the full ring of bounded functions in the image forces a homomorphism to preserve boundedness. The proof uses the lattice operations $\lor$ and $\land$ (join and meet) to truncate a preimage into a bounded one that still maps correctly.
