The zero-set $Z(f)$ of a continuous function $f$ on a topological space $X$ is the preimage of $\{0\}$ under $f$. Zero-sets are always closed subsets of $X$, and they form a fundamental bridge between the topological structure of $X$ and the algebraic structure of the ring $C(X)$. The collection of all zero-sets in $X$, denoted $Z(X)$, is closed under finite unions and finite intersections. Importantly, $C(X)$ and $C^*(X)$ yield exactly the same family of zero-sets.
