The Stone-Čech compactification $\beta \mathbf{N}$ of the natural numbers has the remarkable property that its remainder $\beta \mathbf{N} - \mathbf{N}$ contains a homeomorphic copy of $\beta \mathbf{N}$ itself. This is proved by constructing a discrete $C^*$-embedded subspace $D$ of $\beta \mathbf{N}$ contained entirely within the remainder, and observing that the closure of $D$ in $\beta \mathbf{N}$ is $\beta D \cong \beta \mathbf{N}$, all of which lies in $\beta \mathbf{N} - \mathbf{N}$.
