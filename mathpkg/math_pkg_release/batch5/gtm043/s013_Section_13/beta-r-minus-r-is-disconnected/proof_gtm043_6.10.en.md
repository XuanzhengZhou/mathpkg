Obviously, $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ \cup \operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^- = \beta \mathbf{R}$. The arctangent function in $C(\mathbf{R})$ has a continuous extension to all of $\beta \mathbf{R}$; clearly, this extension assumes the sole value $\pi/2$ on $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$, and the sole value $-\pi/2$ on $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^- - \mathbf{R}^-$. Therefore $\beta \mathbf{R} - \mathbf{R}$ is disconnected: $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ is disjoint from $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^- - \mathbf{R}^-$, and their union is $\beta \mathbf{R} - \mathbf{R}$.
