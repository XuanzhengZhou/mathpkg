The remainder $\beta \mathbf{R} - \mathbf{R}$ of the Stone-Čech compactification of the real line is disconnected. It decomposes into two disjoint compact sets: the remainders of the positive reals $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ and the negative reals $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^- - \mathbf{R}^-$. The arctangent function provides a continuous extension that separates these two sets by taking the constant value $\pi/2$ on one and $-\pi/2$ on the other.
