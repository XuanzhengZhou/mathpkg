The Stone-Čech compactification $\beta \mathbf{N}$ of the natural numbers has cardinality at least that of the continuum $\mathfrak{c}$. This is shown by considering a bounded function $f$ on $\mathbf{N}$ that attains all rational values in $[0,1]$, whose Stone extension $f^\beta$ maps $\beta \mathbf{N}$ onto $[0,1]$, forcing $|\beta \mathbf{N}| \geq |[0,1]| = \mathfrak{c}$. In fact, the cardinal of $\beta \mathbf{N}$ is $2^{\mathfrak{c}}$.
