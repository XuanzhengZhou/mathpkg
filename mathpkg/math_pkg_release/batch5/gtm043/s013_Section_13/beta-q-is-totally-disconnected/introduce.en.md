The Stone-Čech compactification $\beta \mathbf{Q}$ of the rational numbers is totally disconnected. For any two distinct points $p$ and $q$ in $\beta \mathbf{Q}$, one can find disjoint closed neighborhoods and then an open-and-closed set in $\mathbf{Q}$ separating the traces; the closure of this set in $\beta \mathbf{Q}$ yields an open-and-closed set separating $p$ and $q$ in $\beta \mathbf{Q}$.
