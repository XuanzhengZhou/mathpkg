For distinct points $p$ and $q$ are contained in disjoint closed neighborhoods $U$ and $V$, respectively. Then $U \cap \mathbf{Q}$ and $V \cap \mathbf{Q}$ are disjoint closed sets in $\mathbf{Q}$. Consequently, there is an open-and-closed set $E$ in $\mathbf{Q}$ containing $U \cap \mathbf{Q}$ and disjoint from $V \cap \mathbf{Q}$. (This is proved in 16.16, to which the reader may refer directly.) The open-and-closed set $\operatorname{cl}_{\beta \mathbf{Q}} E$ in $\beta \mathbf{Q}$ (6.9(c)) then contains $p$ but not $q$.
