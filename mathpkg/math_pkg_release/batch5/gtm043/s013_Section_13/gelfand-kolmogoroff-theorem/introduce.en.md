The Gelfand-Kolmogoroff theorem establishes an explicit one-to-one correspondence between the maximal ideals in the ring $C(X)$ of all continuous real-valued functions on a Tychonoff space $X$ and the points of the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$. The maximal ideal $M^p$ corresponding to a point $p \in \beta X$ consists of all functions $f \in C(X)$ whose zero-set closure in $\beta X$ contains $p$. The bridge between maximal ideals and points of $\beta X$ is provided by $z$-ultrafilters: each point $p \in \beta X$ corresponds to a unique $z$-ultrafilter $A^p$, and $Z[M^p] = A^p$.
