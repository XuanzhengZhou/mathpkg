Theorem 7.2 gives a complete description of the maximal ideals in the ring $C^*(X)$ of bounded continuous real-valued functions on a Tychonoff space $X$. Each maximal ideal corresponds to a unique point $p$ of the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$, and consists of all functions whose Stone extension vanishes at $p$. This follows from the isomorphism $C^*(X) \cong C(\beta X)$ together with the fact that maximal ideals in $C(\beta X)$ are the fixed ideals at points of the compact space $\beta X$.
