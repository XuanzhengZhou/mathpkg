The mapping $f \mapsto f^{\beta}$ is an isomorphism from $C^*(X)$ onto $C(\beta X)$; and since $\beta X$ is compact, the maximal ideals in $C(\beta X)$ are precisely the fixed ideals

$$\{ f^{\beta} \in C(\beta X) : f^{\beta}(p) = 0 \}.$$

Evidently, $M^{*p}$ is free or fixed according as $p \in \beta X - X$ or $p \in X$; and, in the latter case, $M^{*p}$ is the same as $M^{*}_p$.

We recall (5.6) that each residue class field of $C$ or $C^*$ (modulo a maximal ideal $M$) contains a canonical copy of $\mathbf{R}$.

As another application of the theorem, consider the result (established in 4.7 and 5.10) that $j$ belongs to every free maximal ideal in $C^*(\mathbf{N})$. Third proof: obviously, $j^{\beta}(p) = 0$ for every $p \in \beta \mathbf{N} - \mathbf{N}$ (since $\mathbf{N}$ is dense in $\beta \mathbf{N}$); hence $j \in M^{*p}$ for all such $p$.
