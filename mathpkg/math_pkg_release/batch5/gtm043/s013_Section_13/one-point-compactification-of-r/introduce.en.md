The one-point compactification (also called the Alexandroff compactification) of the real line $\mathbf{R}$ is denoted $\mathbf{R}^* = \mathbf{R} \cup \{\infty\}$. It is obtained by adding a single point at infinity to $\mathbf{R}$, with neighborhoods of $\infty$ being complements of compact subsets of $\mathbf{R}$. In the theory of rings of continuous functions, $\mathbf{R}^*$ serves as the codomain for Stone extensions of possibly unbounded functions: each $f \in C(X)$ can be regarded as a continuous mapping of $X$ into $\mathbf{R}^*$.
