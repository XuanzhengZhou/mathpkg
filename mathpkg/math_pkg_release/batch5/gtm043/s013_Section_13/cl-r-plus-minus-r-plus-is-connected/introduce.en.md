The remainder of the positive real line in its Stone-Čech compactification, $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$, is a connected space. Although $\beta \mathbf{R} - \mathbf{R}$ as a whole is disconnected (split into the positive and negative remainders), each piece is individually connected. The proof uses the intermediate value property on the connected space $\mathbf{R}^+$ to show that no continuous two-valued function can exist on the remainder.
