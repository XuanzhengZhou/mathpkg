If $X$ admits a structure $\mathcal{D}$, then for any closed set $A$ and point $x \notin A$, the closure formula gives $d(x, A) > 0$ for some $d \in \mathcal{D}$. The function $y \mapsto d(y, A)$ is continuous and separates $x$ from $A$, establishing complete regularity.

Conversely, if $X$ is completely regular, then the functions in $C(X)$ separate points from closed sets, and one verifies that the uniform topology induced by $\mathcal{C}(X)$ coincides with the given topology. The same holds for $\mathcal{C}^*(X)$.
