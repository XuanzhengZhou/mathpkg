Let $X$ be a subset of a uniform space $[T; \mathcal{U}]$. The \textit{relative uniform structure} $\mathcal{D}$ on $X$ is the structure generated by the restrictions to $X \times X$ of all $u \in \mathcal{U}$. The space $[X; \mathcal{D}]$ is called a \textit{subspace} of $[T; \mathcal{U}]$. The uniform topology on $[X; \mathcal{D}]$ coincides with the topology induced by the uniform topology of $[T; \mathcal{U}]$.

A \textit{completion} of a uniform space $X$ is a complete Hausdorff uniform space in which $X$ is dense.
