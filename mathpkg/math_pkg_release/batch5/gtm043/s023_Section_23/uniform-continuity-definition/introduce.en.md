Uniform continuity generalizes the familiar notion from metric spaces to uniform spaces: a mapping is uniformly continuous if it respects the uniform structures in the sense that the pullback of each pseudometric on the target belongs to the structure on the domain. Every uniformly continuous mapping is continuous with respect to the uniform topologies. A uniform isomorphism is a bijection that is uniformly continuous in both directions.
