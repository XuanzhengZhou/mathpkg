The condition that all finitely generated ideals be principal is preserved under homomorphism. Therefore, if $C(S)$ or $C^*(S)$ are homomorphic images of $C(X)$ or $C^*(X)$, and if $X$ is an $F$-space, then $S$ is an $F$-space.

If a subspace $S$ is $C^*$-embedded in $X$, then $C^*(S)$ is a homomorphic image of $C^*(X)$. Consequently, every $C^*$-embedded subspace of an $F$-space is an $F$-space. In particular, every cozero-set in an $F$-space is an $F$-space.
