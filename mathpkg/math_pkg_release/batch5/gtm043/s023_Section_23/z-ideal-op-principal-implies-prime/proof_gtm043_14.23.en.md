By the preceding lemma, there exists $Z \in Z[I]$ such that $Z \cap \operatorname{pos} f$ and $Z \cap \operatorname{neg} f$ are completely separated. These sets, therefore, have disjoint closures in $\beta X$. Hence there exists a zero-set $Z' \in Z[O^p]$ disjoint from, say, $Z \cap \operatorname{neg} f$. Evidently, $f$ is nonnegative on the zero-set $Z \cap Z'$ — which belongs to $Z[I]$, because $Z' \in Z[O^p] \subset Z[I]$. So $I(f) \geq 0$ (5.4(a)). This shows that $C/I$ is a totally ordered ring; and since $I$ is a $z$-ideal, it is prime (Theorem 2.9).
