An admissible uniform structure is one whose uniform topology coincides with the original topology of the space. Two canonical uniform structures are associated with any completely regular space: $\mathcal{C}(X)$, generated by all continuous functions, and $\mathcal{C}^*(X)$, generated by all bounded continuous functions. These structures play a fundamental role in connecting uniform space theory to rings of continuous functions.
