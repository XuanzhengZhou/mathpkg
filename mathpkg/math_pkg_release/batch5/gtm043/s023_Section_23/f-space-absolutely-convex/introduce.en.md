This equivalence, part of Theorem 14.25, characterizes $F$-spaces in terms of the convexity of ideals: $X$ is an $F$-space precisely when every ideal in $C(X)$ is absolutely convex. An ideal $I$ is absolutely convex if whenever $|f| \leq |g|$ and $g \in I$, then $f \in I$. This algebraic condition captures a fundamental order-theoretic property of the function ring on $F$-spaces.
