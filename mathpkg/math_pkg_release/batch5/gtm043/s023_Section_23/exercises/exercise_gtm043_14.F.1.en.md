A $z$-filter $\mathcal{Z}$ on $X$ is prime if and only if there exists an ultrafilter $\mathcal{U}$ such that $\mathcal{Z}$ is the family of all zero-sets belonging to $\mathcal{U}$—i.e., in the notation of 10.17, $\mathcal{Z} = \delta^{\#}\mathcal{U}$. [Adjoin the complement of every zero-set not in $\mathcal{Z}$.]
