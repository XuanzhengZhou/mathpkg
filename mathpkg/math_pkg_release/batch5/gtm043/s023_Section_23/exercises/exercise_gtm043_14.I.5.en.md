Given $\mathcal{V}$, let $I$ denote the set of all $f \in C(\mathbf{R})$ such that $\operatorname{int} Z(f)$ contains a member of $\mathcal{V}$. Then $I$ is a $z$-ideal, but is not prime. [If $s \in C(\mathbf{R})$ is as in 2G.2, then neither $s \vee 0$ nor $-(s \wedge 0)$ belongs to $I$, but their product does.]
