By a uniform structure on $X$, we shall mean a nonempty family $\mathcal{D}$ of pseudometrics on $X$, with the properties:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] if $d_1, d_2 \in \mathcal{D}$, then $d_1 \vee d_2 \in \mathcal{D}$; and
\item[(ii)] if $e$ is a pseudometric, and if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exist $d \in \mathcal{D}$ and $\delta > 0$ such that
$$d(x, y) \leq \delta \text{ implies } e(x, y) \leq \epsilon$$
for all $x, y \in X$, then $e \in \mathcal{D}$.
\end{itemize}
