Theorem 14.27 provides a rich source of compact $F$-spaces: the Stone-Cech remainder $\beta X \setminus X$ of any locally compact, $\sigma$-compact space $X$ is a compact $F$-space. As an example, $\beta \mathbb{R}^+ \setminus \mathbb{R}^+$ (where $\mathbb{R}^+$ is the space of non-negative reals) is a compact, connected $F$-space of cardinality $2^{\mathfrak{c}}$.
