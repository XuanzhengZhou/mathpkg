Shirota's Theorem is one of the crowning achievements of the theory of rings of continuous functions. It states that, modulo measurable cardinals, the spaces admitting a complete uniform structure are precisely the realcompact spaces. The forward direction uses the hypothesis on closed discrete subspaces to ensure every real $z$-ultrafilter is a Cauchy $z$-filter. Conversely, if $X$ is realcompact, then $\mathcal{C}(X)$ provides an admissible complete structure.
