The condition that every ideal $O^p$ be prime characterizes $X$ as an $F$-space. In the extreme case, every such ideal is maximal. This is evidently equivalent to the condition that every prime ideal be maximal, i.e., that $X$ be a $P$-space.

If $X$ is an infinite $P$-space, then $\beta X$ is an $F$-space; but it is not a $P$-space, as every compact $P$-space is finite.
