This theorem characterizes the quotient ring $C/I$ when $I$ is a $z$-ideal containing $O^p$ satisfying the principal ideal condition. The result shows that $C/I$ becomes a valuation ring — a commutative ring where the principal ideals form a chain — and moreover satisfies the stronger property that the smaller of two positive elements is always a multiple of the larger. This property is central to the theory of $F$-spaces.
