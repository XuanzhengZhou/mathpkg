Let $I$ be a $z$-ideal containing $O^p$ such that $(I(f), I(|f|))$ is principal in $C/I$ for every $f \in C$. Then the quotient ring $C/I$ is a totally ordered integral domain, and the following conditions are equivalent:

(1) For any $0 < a < b$ in $C/I$, $a$ is a multiple of $b$.

(2) $C/I$ is a valuation ring (its principal ideals form a chain).

(3) For any $a, b \in C/I$, either $(a) \subset (b)$ or $(b) \subset (a)$.

(4) Every finitely generated ideal in $C/I$ is principal.
