A uniform space is complete if every family of closed sets with the finite intersection property that contains arbitrarily small sets has nonempty intersection. Equivalent characterizations are that every Cauchy $z$-filter (respectively, every Cauchy $z$-ultrafilter) is fixed. This generalizes the familiar notion of completeness from metric spaces: a metric space is complete exactly when every Cauchy sequence converges.
