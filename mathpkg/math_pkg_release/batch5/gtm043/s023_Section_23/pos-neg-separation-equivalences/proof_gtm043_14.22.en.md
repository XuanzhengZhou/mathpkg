Both (1) and (2) are simply reformulations of the statement that there exists $k \in C$ that is equal to $1$ on $\operatorname{pos} f$ and to $-1$ on $\operatorname{neg} f$. Trivially, (2) implies (3). Finally, if (3) holds, then the lemma (principal-quotient-zero-set-separation), with $I = (0)$ (whence $Z = X$), yields (1).

Notice that if $f = k|f|$, then $|f| = kf$ — for the same $k$. Since the validity of these equations is not affected by the values of $k$ on $Z(f)$, it may always be assumed that $|k| \leq 1$.
