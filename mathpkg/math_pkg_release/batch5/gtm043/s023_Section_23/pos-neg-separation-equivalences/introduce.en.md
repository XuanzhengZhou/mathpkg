This corollary establishes three equivalent formulations for the topological separation of the positive and negative parts of a continuous function. Condition (2) expresses the existence of a "sign function" $k$ that recovers $f$ from its absolute value, while condition (3) translates this into an algebraic statement about the principal ideal generated by $f$ and $|f|$. The equivalence provides a crucial bridge between topological separation properties and algebraic ideal structure in rings of continuous functions.
