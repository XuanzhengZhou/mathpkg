The Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$ of an infinite discrete space $X$ has cardinality exactly $2^{2^{|X|}}$. This is the sharpest possible evaluation: the upper bound $|\beta X| \leq 2^{2^{|X|}}$ follows from the fact that each ultrafilter on $X$ is a subset of the power set of $X$, while the lower bound requires constructing at least $2^{2^{|X|}}$ distinct ultrafilters. The proof proceeds by constructing families of subsets of $F \times \Phi$ (where $F$ is the set of finite subsets of $X$ and $\Phi$ is the set of finite subsets of $F$) and embedding each family into a distinct ultrafilter via the finite intersection property.
