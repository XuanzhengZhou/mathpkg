Theorem 9.11 asserts that any closed subset of $\beta X \setminus vX$ that is not discrete must contain a topological copy of the entire Stone-Čech compactification $\beta N$ of the natural numbers. Consequently, every such set has cardinality at least $2^{\mathfrak{c}}$. This result generalizes earlier cardinality estimates for the growth of Stone-Čech compactifications and shows that the "non-realcompact growth" is always massive. The proof (whose beginning is present in the text) invokes Lemma 9.10 to extract a countable discrete subset whose closure yields the desired copy.
