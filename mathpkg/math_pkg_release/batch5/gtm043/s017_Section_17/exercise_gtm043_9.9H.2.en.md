If $X$ is basically disconnected, then every infinite closed set $E$ in $\beta X$ contains a copy of $\beta\mathbf{N}$, and so its cardinal is at least $2^{\mathfrak{c}}$. [By 0.13, $E$ contains a countably infinite discrete subset $D$. By 6M.1, $\beta X$ is basically disconnected.] In particular, this holds for discrete $X$.
