Let $X$ and $Y$ be spaces in which every nonisolated point is both a $G_\delta$ and the limit of a sequence of distinct points. If $C^*(X)$ is isomorphic with $C^*(Y)$, then $X$ is homeomorphic with $Y$. [Identify $\beta X$ with $\beta Y$. If $y \in Y - X$, then by 9N.1, $Y - \{y\}$ is $C^*$-embedded in $Y$, leading to a contradiction.]
