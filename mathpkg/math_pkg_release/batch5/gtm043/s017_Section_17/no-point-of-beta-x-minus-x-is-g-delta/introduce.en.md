Corollary 9.6 states that no point of the growth $\beta X \setminus X$ can be expressed as a countable intersection of open sets in $\beta X$. This follows immediately from Theorem 9.5 together with the fact (3.11(b)) that every $G_\delta$-point is a zero-set. A direct consequence is that a compact metric space cannot be the Stone-Čech compactification of any proper dense subspace.
