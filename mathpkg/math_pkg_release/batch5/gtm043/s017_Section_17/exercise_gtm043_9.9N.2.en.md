If $S$ is dense in $Y$, and a point $y \in Y - S$ is both a $G_\delta$ in $S \cup \{y\}$ and the limit of a sequence $(y_n)_{n \in \mathbf{N}}$ of distinct points of $Y$, then $S$ is not $C^*$-embedded in $S \cup \{y\}$. [Note that $y$ is a $G_\delta$-point in the space
$$X = S \cup \{y\} \cup \{y_n : n \in \mathbf{N}\},$$
so that $\{y\} = \mathbf{Z}(f)$ for some $f \in C^*(X)$. Find $h \in C^*(\mathbf{R} - \{0\})$ such that $h \circ (f \mid S)$ has no continuous extension to $y$.]
