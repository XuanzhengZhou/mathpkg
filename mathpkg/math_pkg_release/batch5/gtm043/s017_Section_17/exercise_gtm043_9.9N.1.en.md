If $Y$ is $C^*$-embedded in $T$, $V$ is open in $T$, and $V \cap Y$ is dense in $V$, then $V \cap Y$ is $C^*$-embedded in $V$. [Given $t \in V - Y$, there exists $h \in C^*(T)$ that vanishes outside $V$ and is equal to $1$ on a neighborhood of $t$. Given $g \in C^*(V \cap Y)$, the function $g \cdot (h \mid V \cap Y)$ has a continuous extension to all of $T$. This supplies an extension of $g$ to a neighborhood of $t$.]
