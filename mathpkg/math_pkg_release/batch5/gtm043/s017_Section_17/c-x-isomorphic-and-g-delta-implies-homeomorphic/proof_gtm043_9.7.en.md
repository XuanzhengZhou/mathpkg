The proof uses the structure of $\beta X$ and the fact that the isomorphism between $C(X)$ and $C(Y)$ induces a homeomorphism between $\beta X$ and $\beta Y$. Under the hypothesis that all points are $G_\delta$'s, it can be shown that the homeomorphism carries $X$ onto $Y$. The argument relies on the preceding discussion: every $G_\delta$-point of $X$ is a $G_\delta$ in $vX$, and no point of $vX - X$ is a $G_\delta$ in $vX$ (8.8(b)). A $G_\delta$-point of $X$ need not be a $G_\delta$ in $\beta X$, but if a point has a countable base of neighborhoods in $X$, then it also has a countable base in $\beta X$. No point of $\beta X - X$ has a countable base of neighborhoods in $\beta X$ (Corollary 9.6).
