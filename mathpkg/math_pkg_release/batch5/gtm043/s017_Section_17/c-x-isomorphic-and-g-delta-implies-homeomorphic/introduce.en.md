Theorem 9.7(a) provides a situation where algebraic isomorphism of the rings of continuous functions $C(X) \cong C(Y)$ forces the underlying spaces $X$ and $Y$ to be homeomorphic. The hypothesis is that every point in both spaces is a $G_\delta$-set (a countable intersection of open sets). Together with the discussion in 9.7, this shows that while $G_\delta$-points in $X$ remain $G_\delta$ in the Hewitt realcompactification $vX$, they need not remain $G_\delta$ in the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$, unless the point has a countable neighborhood base.
