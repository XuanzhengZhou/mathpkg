A lattice homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between lattices that commutes with both the join and meet operations. This is the natural notion of morphism in the category of lattices: it preserves the algebraic essence of the lattice structure. A lattice homomorphism is necessarily order-preserving, but the converse is false — an order-preserving bijection between lattices need not be a lattice isomorphism.
