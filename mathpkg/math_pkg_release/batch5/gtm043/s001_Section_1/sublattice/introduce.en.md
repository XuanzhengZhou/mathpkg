A sublattice is a subset of a lattice that is itself a lattice under the same join and meet operations. The critical condition is that the join and meet of two elements, computed in the ambient lattice, must lie in the subset. It is not sufficient that the subset merely have some supremum and infimum relative to its own induced order; the operations must agree with those of the parent lattice.
