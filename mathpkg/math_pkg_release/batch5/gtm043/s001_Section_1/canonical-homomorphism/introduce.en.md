The canonical homomorphism (also called the natural projection or quotient map) sends each element of a ring $A$ to its coset modulo an ideal $I$. It is always surjective with kernel $I$, establishing that every ideal is the kernel of some homomorphism. This construction is fundamental: the residue class ring $A/I$ inherits the ring structure from $A$, and the properties of $A/I$ reflect those of $I$.
