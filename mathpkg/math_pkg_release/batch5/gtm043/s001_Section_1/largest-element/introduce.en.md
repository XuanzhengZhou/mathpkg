The largest element (also called the greatest element or maximum) of a partially ordered set is an element that dominates every element in the set. Unlike a maximal element, a largest element is comparable with and greater than or equal to all elements. If a largest element exists, it is necessarily unique and is also the unique maximal element. The dual concept is the smallest (or least) element.
