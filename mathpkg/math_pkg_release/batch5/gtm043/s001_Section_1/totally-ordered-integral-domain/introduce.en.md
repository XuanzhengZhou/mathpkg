A totally ordered integral domain is an integral domain with a total order compatible with the ring operations. Key properties include: nonzero squares are positive, so $1 > 0$ and $-1$ has no square root; the map $n \mapsto n \cdot 1$ embeds the integers; and in the field case, the rational numbers embed via $m/n \mapsto (m \cdot 1)/(n \cdot 1)$. These facts underpin the theory of ordered fields and the characterization of archimedean ordered fields.
