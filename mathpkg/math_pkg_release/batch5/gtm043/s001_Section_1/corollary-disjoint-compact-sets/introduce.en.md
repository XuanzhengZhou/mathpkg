In a Hausdorff space, any two disjoint compact subsets can be separated by disjoint open neighborhoods. This follows from the fact that in a Hausdorff space, a compact set and a point outside it have disjoint neighborhoods; applying the Lemma on disjoint neighborhoods of a set and a compact set then yields the full separation of the two compact sets. This property is essential for establishing that compact subsets of a Hausdorff space are closed.
