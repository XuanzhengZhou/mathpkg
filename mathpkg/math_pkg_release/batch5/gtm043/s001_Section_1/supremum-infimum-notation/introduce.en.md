The notation $a \vee b$ (join) and $a \wedge b$ (meet) denotes the supremum and infimum of a two-element set in a partially ordered set. The join is the least upper bound, while the meet is the greatest lower bound. These binary operations form the foundation of lattice theory, where they are required to exist for all pairs. The symbols derive from the logical OR ($\vee$) and AND ($\wedge$) by analogy: $a \vee b$ is the weakest element stronger than both, and $a \wedge b$ is the strongest element weaker than both.
