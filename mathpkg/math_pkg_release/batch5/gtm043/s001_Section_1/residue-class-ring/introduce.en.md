The residue class ring (or quotient ring) $A/I$ is constructed from a ring $A$ modulo an ideal $I$ by taking cosets $I + a$ as elements. The ring operations are well-defined precisely because $I$ is an ideal. This construction satisfies the universal property of quotients: any homomorphism whose kernel contains $I$ factors uniquely through $A/I$. The First Isomorphism Theorem states that the image of any homomorphism is isomorphic to the quotient of the domain by its kernel.
