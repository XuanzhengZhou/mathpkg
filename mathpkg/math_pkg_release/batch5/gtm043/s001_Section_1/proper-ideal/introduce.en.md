A proper ideal is a subring of a ring that is closed under multiplication by arbitrary ring elements and is not the whole ring. In this text, the term "ideal" always means proper ideal. The defining absorption property ($a \in I \implies xa \in I$ for all $x$) makes ideals the natural kernels of ring homomorphisms. Since a proper ideal cannot contain a unit, it cannot contain $1$, which ensures the quotient ring is nontrivial.
