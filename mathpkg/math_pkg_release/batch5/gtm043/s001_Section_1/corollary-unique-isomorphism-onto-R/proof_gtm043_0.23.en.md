If $u$ and $v$ are isomorphisms from the same ring onto $\mathbf{R}$, then $u \circ v^{\leftarrow}$ is an automorphism of $\mathbf{R}$, and hence is the identity (since the only ring automorphism of $\mathbf{R}$ is the identity). Therefore $u = v$.

Given homomorphisms $s$ and $t$ from a ring $A$ onto $\mathbf{R}$, with common kernel $I$, we consider the associated isomorphisms $\bar{s}$ and $\bar{t}$ from $A/I$ onto $\mathbf{R}$ (i.e., such that $s = \bar{s} \circ h$ and $t = \bar{t} \circ h$, where $h$ is the canonical homomorphism of $A$ onto $A/I$). Since $\bar{s} = \bar{t}$ (by the first part), we have $s = t$.
