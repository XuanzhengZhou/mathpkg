This corollary establishes two uniqueness results about homomorphisms onto $\mathbb{R}$. First, there is at most one ring isomorphism from a given ring onto $\mathbb{R}$ — this follows from the fact that the only ring automorphism of $\mathbb{R}$ is the identity (itself a consequence of the order-preserving nature of ring isomorphisms of $\mathbb{R}$). Second, any homomorphism onto $\mathbb{R}$ is uniquely determined by its kernel, because two homomorphisms with the same kernel factor through the same quotient and the uniqueness of the isomorphism from that quotient onto $\mathbb{R}$ forces them to agree.
