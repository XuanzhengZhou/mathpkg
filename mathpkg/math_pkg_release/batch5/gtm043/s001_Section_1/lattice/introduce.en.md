A lattice is a partially ordered set in which every pair of elements has both a least upper bound (join, $\vee$) and a greatest lower bound (meet, $\wedge$). Lattices bridge order theory and algebra: the join and meet operations satisfy idempotent, commutative, associative, and absorption laws. Lattices appear throughout mathematics — the ideals of a ring form a lattice under inclusion, as do the closed sets of a topological space, and the collection of all subsets of any set.
