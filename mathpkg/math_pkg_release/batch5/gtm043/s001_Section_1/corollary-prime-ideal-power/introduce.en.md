This corollary provides a criterion for the existence of a prime ideal containing a given ideal $I$ while excluding a specific element $a$: it suffices that no power of $a$ lies in $I$. The proof applies Theorem 0.16 to the multiplicatively closed set $S = \{1, a, a^2, \ldots\}$, which is disjoint from $I$ by hypothesis. This result is fundamental to the relationship between prime ideals and the nilradical.
