A partially ordered ring is a ring equipped with a partial order that is compatible with the ring operations: the order is translation-invariant (adding a fixed element preserves inequalities) and the product of nonnegative elements is nonnegative. This concept is central to the study of rings of continuous functions, where the natural pointwise order ($f \geq g$ iff $f(x) \geq g(x)$ for all $x$) makes the ring partially ordered. The condition $a \geq b \iff a - b \geq 0$ shows the order is determined by the set of nonnegative elements.
