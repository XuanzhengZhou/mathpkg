The finite intersection property is a condition on a family of sets requiring that every finite subfamily has nonempty intersection. It is a fundamental concept in set theory and topology, playing a key role in characterizations of compactness. A compact space can be defined as one in which every family of closed sets with the finite intersection property has nonempty total intersection. The property is strictly stronger than requiring that any two members intersect; a family may have pairwise nonempty intersections yet fail the finite intersection property.
