A ring homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between rings that respects addition and multiplication. Throughout this text, the term "homomorphism" without qualification always means ring homomorphism. The kernel of a homomorphism is the set of elements mapping to zero, which is always an ideal. Conversely, every ideal is the kernel of some homomorphism — specifically, the canonical homomorphism onto the residue class ring.
