An ordered field is called archimedean if its natural integers (the elements $n \cdot 1$) are cofinal — that is, for every element $x$ there exists an integer $n$ with $n > x$. This property captures the intuitive notion that there are no "infinitely large" or "infinitesimally small" elements. The fundamental characterization theorem states that an ordered field is archimedean if and only if it is isomorphic to a subfield of $\mathbb{R}$.
