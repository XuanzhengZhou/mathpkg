A maximal ideal is a proper ideal that is not strictly contained in any other proper ideal. Equivalently, an ideal $M$ is maximal if and only if the quotient ring $A/M$ is a field. Every maximal ideal is prime. In the theory of rings of continuous functions on a compact space, the maximal ideals correspond bijectively to the points of the space, with each point $p$ associated to the maximal ideal of functions vanishing at $p$.
