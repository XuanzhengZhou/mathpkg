Freely falling photons in normal Schwarzschild spacetime that maintain direction ($J = 0$) admit a complete classification. Photons can go directly in toward the central mass, go directly out, or circle at a constant height $H \geq 3\mu$. The circling photon at $H = 3\mu$ is a purely relativistic effect with no Newtonian analogue. This classification follows from the constants of motion given by Proposition 7.3.7.
