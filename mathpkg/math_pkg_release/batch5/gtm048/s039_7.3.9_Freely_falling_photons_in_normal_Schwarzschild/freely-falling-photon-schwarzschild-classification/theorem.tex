For a freely falling photon $\lambda: \hat{\mathcal{E}} \to N$ in a normal Schwarzschild spacetime maintaining direction ($J = 0$), the motion is equivalent to a standard form. The table summarizes the classification for particles with total angular momentum $J$, total energy $E$:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c|c|c|c|c}
$\mathcal{E}$ & $r \circ \gamma(s)$ & Property & $m$ & $J$ & $E$ \\
\hline
$(2\mu, \infty)$ & $s$ & Directly in & $0$ & $0$ & $1$ \\
$(-\infty, -2\mu)$ & $-s$ & Directly out & $0$ & $0$ & $1$ \\
$(-\infty, \infty)$ & $H$ (constant) & Circles at $H \in [3\mu, \infty)$ & $|(1-3\Delta)^{1/2}|$ & $(\mu H)^{1/2}$ & $1-2\Delta$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
where $\Delta = \mu/H \in (0, 1/3]$. When $H = 3\mu$, a photon can circle the central mass.
