Verify the details in the following table which summarizes the freely falling particles $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow N$ in normal Schwarzschild spacetime with rest-mass $m$, total angular momentum $J$, total energy $E$ and the indicated properties. For the notation, $\beta: (-\infty, \infty) \rightarrow \mathcal{S}^2$ is a unit speed great circle, while $H \in [3\mu, \infty)$ and $\Delta = \mu/H \in (0, \frac{1}{3}]$ are constants.

| $\mathcal{E}$ | $(r \circ \gamma)s$ | $(P \circ \gamma)s$ | $(t \circ \gamma)s$ | $m$ | $J$ | $E$ | Property |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| $(2\mu, \infty)$ | $s$ | $q \in \mathcal{S}^2$ | $s + 2\mu \ln s$ | $0$ | $0$ | $1$ | Directly in |
| $(-\infty, -2\mu)$ | $-s$ | $q \in \mathcal{S}^2$ | $s - 2\mu \ln (-s)$ | $0$ | $0$ | $1$ | Directly out |
| $(-\infty, \infty)$ | $H$ | $\beta(Js/H^2)$ | $s$ | $\lvert(1 - 3\Delta)^{1/2}\rvert$ | $(\mu H)^{1/2}$ | $1 - 2\Delta$ | Circles |

Newtonian analogue. For a particle with inertial mass unity in a circular orbit at height $|\vec{x}|$ above the center of the sun, the third row of the table gives very similar results when $|\vec{x}| = H \gg 3\mu$. But when $H = 3\mu$, we have $m = 0$, corresponding to a photon circling; Newtonian physics cannot handle such situations.
