The motion of a freely falling particle $\alpha$ in Schwarzschild spacetime is classified according to the sign of $dr(\alpha_*)$ and whether the particle maintains direction. A particle goes in if $dr(\alpha_*) < 0$, maintains height if $dr(\alpha_*) = 0$, and goes out if $dr(\alpha_*) > 0$. Combining with the condition $J = 0$ (maintaining direction), one obtains the notions of going directly in, hovering, and going directly out. A particle circles if it maintains height and its spatial projection traces an arc of a great circle on $\mathcal{S}^2$.
