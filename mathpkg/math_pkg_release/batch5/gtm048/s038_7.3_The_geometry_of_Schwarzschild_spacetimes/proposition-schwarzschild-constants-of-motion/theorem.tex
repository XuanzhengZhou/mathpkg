Let $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to U$ be a future-pointing causal geodesic in a Schwarzschild spacetime. Then there exist unique numbers $E \in \mathbb{R}$, $J \in [0, \infty)$, $J_x \in [-J, J]$ such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $g(\gamma_*, \partial) = -E$, where $\partial = (\partial/\partial t) \circ \gamma$,
\item[(b)] $\rho^4 h(\delta_*, \delta_*) = J^2$, where $\delta = P \circ \gamma$ and $\rho = r \circ \gamma$,
\item[(c)] $\rho^2 h(\delta_*, K_x) = J_x$, where $K_x$ is the generator of rotations about the $x$-axis on $\mathcal{S}^2$.
\end{itemize}
$E$ is the total energy, $J$ the total angular momentum, and $J_x$ the angular momentum in the $x$-direction.
