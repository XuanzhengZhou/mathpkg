These results establish fundamental global properties of spacetimes. Every spacetime admits a reference frame (a unit timelike vector field), which follows from the fact that any Lorentzian manifold admits a nowhere-zero timelike vector field. A compact spacetime cannot be simply connected (via Poincaré duality), which is why compact spacetimes are rarely considered in physics. The construction $g + 2\omega \otimes \omega$ provides a canonical way to obtain a Riemannian metric from a Lorentzian one.
