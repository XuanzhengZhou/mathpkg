Let $(V, g)$ be an $N$-dimensional Lorentzian vector space and let $W \subset V$ be a subspace. The causal character of $W$ is:
\begin{itemize}
  \item[(a)] spacelike iff $g$ is positive definite on $W$;
  \item[(b)] lightlike iff $g$ is positive semi-definite but not positive definite on $W$;
  \item[(c)] timelike otherwise.
\end{itemize}
For $v \in V$, the causal character of $v$ is that of $\operatorname{span}\{v\}$. A vector $v \in V$ is called causal iff $v$ is not spacelike.
