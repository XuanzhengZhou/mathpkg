Let $(M, g)$ be a time-orientable Lorentzian manifold. Then $(M, g)$ is time oriented iff one connected component of $\mathcal{T}$ is labelled $\mathcal{T}^+$ and called the future. The complement of $\mathcal{T}^+$ in $\mathcal{T}$, denoted $\mathcal{T}^-$, is then called the past.

If there exists a causal vector field $X$ on $(M, g)$, then the function $\psi: \mathcal{T} \to (-\infty, 0) \cup (0, \infty)$ defined by $(x, W) \mapsto g(W, X_x)$ is a smooth surjection, so $\mathcal{T}$ is disconnected and $(M, g)$ is time orientable. Designating $\psi^{-1}(-\infty, 0)$ as $\mathcal{T}^+$ means $(M, g)$ is time oriented by $X$; in this case the future is the component of $\mathcal{T}$ whose closure contains $X_x$ for all $x \in M$.
