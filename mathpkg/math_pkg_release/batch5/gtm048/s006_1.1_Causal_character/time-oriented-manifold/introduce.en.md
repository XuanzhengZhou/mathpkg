A time-oriented Lorentzian manifold is a time-orientable manifold together with a choice of which connected component of the timelike bundle $\mathcal{T}$ is designated as the "future" ($\mathcal{T}^+$) and which as the "past" ($\mathcal{T}^-$). A convenient way to specify a time orientation is via a global causal vector field $X$: the future is then the component of $\mathcal{T}$ whose vectors satisfy $g(\cdot, X) < 0$, and the closure of this component contains $X_x$ at each point. Time orientation is essential for formulating causality, distinguishing future-directed from past-directed curves, and defining the future/past lightcones in general relativity.
