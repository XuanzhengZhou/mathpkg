For a connected Lorentzian manifold $(M, g)$, the set $\mathcal{T}$ of all timelike tangent vectors forms an open subset of the tangent bundle $TM$. This proposition establishes that $\mathcal{T}$ decomposes as the union of a connected component $\mathcal{A}$ and its antipodal image $\psi(\mathcal{A})$. The two possibilities — $\mathcal{A} = \psi(\mathcal{A})$ (one component) or $\mathcal{A} \cap \psi(\mathcal{A}) = \varnothing$ (two components) — correspond precisely to whether the manifold is time orientable. The proof uses the fact that the bundle projection maps of the two open subsets cannot intersect when $M$ is connected, forcing one of the two cases.
