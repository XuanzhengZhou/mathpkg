(a) Let $v, w \in V$ be causal vectors in an $N$-dimensional Lorentzian vector space $(V, g)$. Show that the Schwarz inequality now "goes the wrong way":
$$|g(v, w)| \geq |v|\,|w|,$$
and equality holds iff $v$ and $w$ are proportional.

(b) Let $(M, g)$ be a Lorentzian manifold, $X$ a timelike vector in $M_x$, and $\omega \in M_x^*$ a timelike $1$-form. Show that
$$|\omega X| \geq |\omega|\,|X|,$$
and equality holds iff $\omega$ and $aX$ are physically equivalent for some $a \in \mathbb{R}$. (The norm $|\omega|$ is taken with respect to the Lorentzian inner product $\hat{g}_x$ on $M_x^*$ from Exercise 0.0.15.)
