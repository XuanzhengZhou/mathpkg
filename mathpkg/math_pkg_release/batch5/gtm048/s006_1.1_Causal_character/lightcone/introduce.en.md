In a Lorentzian vector space $(V, g)$, vectors $v$ satisfying $g(v, v) = 0$ are called lightlike (or null). The set of all such nonzero vectors forms the lightcone $\mathcal{L}_0 \subset V$, which is a fundamental geometric object in special and general relativity. The lightcone separates timelike vectors (those with $g(v,v) < 0$) from spacelike vectors ($g(v,v) > 0$). One can also regard $(V, g)$ itself as a Lorentzian manifold and investigate the causal character of $\mathcal{L}_0$ as a submanifold.
