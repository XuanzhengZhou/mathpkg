Let $(V, g)$ be an $N$-dimensional Lorentzian vector space and let $W \subset V$ be a subspace. The \textbf{causal character} of $W$ is:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] \textbf{spacelike} iff $g$ is positive definite on $W$;
\item[(b)] \textbf{lightlike} iff $g$ is positive semi-definite but not positive definite on $W$;
\item[(c)] \textbf{timelike} otherwise.
\end{itemize}
For $v \in V$, the causal character of $v$ is that of $\operatorname{span}\{v\}$; $v$ is called \textbf{causal} iff $v$ is not spacelike.
