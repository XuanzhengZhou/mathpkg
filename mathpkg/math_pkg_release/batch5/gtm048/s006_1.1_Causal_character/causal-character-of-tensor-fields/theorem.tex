Let $(M, g)$ be a Lorentzian manifold.
\begin{itemize}
  \item Let $\phi: N \to M$ be an immersion of a manifold $N$. If $\phi_* N_x \subset M_{\phi(x)}$ has the same causal character for all $x \in N$, that causal character is assigned to the immersion $\phi$ and to its image $\phi(N)$.
  \item A vector field $X$ defined on $U \subset M$ is timelike (respectively, spacelike, lightlike) iff for all $x \in U$, the vector $X_x$ is timelike (respectively, spacelike, lightlike).
  \item Let $\omega$ be a $1$-form defined on $U \subset M$ and let $X$ be the vector field physically equivalent to $\omega$. If $X$ possesses a causal character, this causal character is assigned to $\omega$.
\end{itemize}
The corresponding definitions are used for curves into $M$ and so on.
