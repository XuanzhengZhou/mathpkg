The notion of causal character, originally defined for vectors and subspaces of a Lorentzian vector space, extends naturally to geometric objects on a Lorentzian manifold. An immersion receives the causal character of its tangent spaces (when uniform), a vector field inherits the pointwise causal character of its values, and a 1-form is assigned the causal character of its physically equivalent vector field via the metric $g$. This extension allows one to speak of timelike curves, spacelike hypersurfaces, lightlike 1-forms, and similar objects throughout Lorentzian geometry and general relativity.
