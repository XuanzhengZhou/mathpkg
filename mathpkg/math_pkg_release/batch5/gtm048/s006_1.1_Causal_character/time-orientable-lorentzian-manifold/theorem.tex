Let $(M, g)$ be a Lorentzian manifold and let $\mathcal{T} \subset TM$ be the set of all timelike tangent vectors. Then $(M, g)$ is called \textbf{time orientable} iff $\mathcal{T}$ is not connected (i.e., has at least two components). If $(M, g)$ is time orientable, it is \textbf{time oriented} iff one component of $\mathcal{T}$ is designated $\mathcal{T}^+$ and called the \textbf{future}; the complement $\mathcal{T}^- = \mathcal{T} \setminus \mathcal{T}^+$ is then called the \textbf{past}. For each $x \in M$, define $\mathcal{T}_x^+ = \mathcal{T}^+ \cap \pi^{-1}(x) \subset M_x$ and similarly $\mathcal{L}_x^+$ as the corresponding component of the lightcone in $M_x$.

A causal vector field $X$ on $(M, g)$ defines a time orientation by designating $\{ (x, W) \in \mathcal{T} \mid g(W, X_x) < 0 \}$ as $\mathcal{T}^+$. In this case, the future is the component whose closure contains $X_x$ for all $x \in M$.
