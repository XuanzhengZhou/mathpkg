Time orientability is a global property of a Lorentzian manifold $(M, g)$ that determines whether one can consistently distinguish past from future across the entire manifold. The manifold is time orientable if the set $\mathcal{T}$ of all timelike tangent vectors is disconnected; choosing one component as the future then gives a time orientation. A causal vector field provides a natural way to time-orient the manifold. Time orientability is a physically essential condition in general relativity, as it allows a globally consistent notion of causal ordering and the distinction between forward and backward time directions.
