Let $(V, g)$ be an $N$-dimensional Lorentzian vector space. The \textbf{lightcone} $\mathcal{L}_0 \subset V$ is the set of all lightlike vectors in $V$:
$$\mathcal{L}_0 = \{v \in V \mid g(v, v) = 0,\; v \neq 0\}.$$
Then $\mathcal{L}_0$ is a lightlike submanifold of $V$ (when $V$ is regarded as a Lorentzian manifold). Moreover, the set $\mathcal{T}_0$ of timelike vectors is an open submanifold with two connected components $\mathcal{T}_0^+$ and $\mathcal{T}_0^-$, each diffeomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^N$. For $N \geq 3$, $\mathcal{L}_0$ also splits into two connected components $\mathcal{L}_0^+$ and $\mathcal{L}_0^-$, each diffeomorphic to $\mathbb{R} \times S^{N-2}$.
