Let $\phi: \mathbb{R}^3 \times T \to \mathbb{R}$ be a $C^\infty$ time-dependent scalar field and $\vec{j} = j^\alpha \vec{e}_\alpha$ a time-dependent vector field, with $\alpha \in (1,2,3)$. Then $\phi$ is regarded as a function on the naive spacetime $\mathbb{R}^4$ via
$$
\phi(w) = \phi(u^1 w, u^2 w, u^3 w, u^4 w) \quad \forall w \in \mathbb{R}^4,
$$
and similarly for each $j^\alpha$. Thus $\vec{j} = j^\alpha \partial_\alpha$ is regarded as a vector field on $\mathbb{R}^4$ satisfying $du^4(j) = 0$.

Conversely, let $J = J^i \partial_i$ be a vector field on $\mathbb{R}^4$, where $J^i: \mathbb{R}^4 \to \mathbb{R}$ for $i \in (1,\dots,4)$. Then $J^4$ is regarded as a time-dependent scalar field $J^4(\vec{x}, t)$, and $\vec{j} = (J^1, J^2, J^3)$ as a time-dependent vector field. A stress-energy tensor $\hat{T} = T^{ij} \partial_i \otimes \partial_j$ accordingly becomes a time-dependent scalar field $T^{44}(\vec{x},t)$, two equal time-dependent vector fields $T^{\alpha 4} \vec{e}_\alpha = T^{4\alpha} \vec{e}_\alpha$, and a time-dependent tensor field $T^{\alpha\beta} \vec{e}_\alpha \otimes \vec{e}_\beta$.
