Suppose two Newtonian point particles $(\vec{r}, m)$ and $(\vec{r}', m')$ collide. Suppose two particles are outgoing, each having inertial mass $(m + m')/2$. Assume the RC terms in Section 9.2.2 are negligible. State what else needs to be known about the outgoing particles in order to determine both outgoing momenta uniquely from the collision conservation laws.

[*Hints: two appropriately chosen angles can be used; it is simplest to work in the center of mass frame (Exercise 3.8.8).*]
