Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a consistent particle set (cf. 3.8). Suppose each particle in $\mathcal{P}$ has non-zero restmass. Consider a collision event such that $\sum_{\text{in}} m = \sum_{\text{out}} m$ (Newtonian conservation of inertial mass). Express the consistency condition (Definition 3.8.4) in the form:

$$\frac{1}{2} \sum_{\text{in}} m |\dot{\vec{r}}|^2 = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{\text{out}} m |\dot{\vec{r}}|^2 + \text{RC} \quad (\text{Newtonian energy conservation})$$

$$\sum_{\text{in}} m \dot{\vec{r}} = \sum_{\text{out}} m \dot{\vec{r}} + \text{RC} \quad (\text{Newtonian momentum conservation})$$

where "RC" denotes "relativistic correction terms," small if $|\dot{\vec{r}}| \ll 1$ for each particle involved.
