This exercise generalizes the integral form of Maxwell's equations to the case where the spatial integration region is not static but expands (at half the speed of light). It provides the bridge between the relativistic formulation using causal boxes and the Newtonian formulation using time-dependent spatial regions. The correspondence shows how the relativistic stress-energy tensor of the electromagnetic field decomposes into the familiar Newtonian quantities: energy density $U = (|\vec{E}|^2 + |\vec{B}|^2)/8\pi$, Poynting vector $\vec{S} = (\vec{E} \times \vec{B})/4\pi$, and Maxwell stress tensor.
