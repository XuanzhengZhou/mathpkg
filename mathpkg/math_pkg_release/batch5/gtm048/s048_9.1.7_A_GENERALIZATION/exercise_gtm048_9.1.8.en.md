Show directly that the Newtonian analogues of the plane waves (Example 3.7.3) — namely, $\vec{E} = f(z - t)\vec{e}_x$ and $\vec{B} = f(z - t)\vec{e}_y$ — obey the four classical Maxwell equations (Sections 9.1.2 to 9.1.3) with $\sigma = 0 = \vec{j}$.
