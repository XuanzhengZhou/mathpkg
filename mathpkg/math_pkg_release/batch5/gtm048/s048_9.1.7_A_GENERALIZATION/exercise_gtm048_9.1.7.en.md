Find a causal box $\phi: \mathcal{N}_0 \to \mathbb{R}^4$ such that $\phi\mathcal{N}_0$ corresponds to part of the history of a solid ball in $\mathbb{R}^3$ which expands at half the speed of light. State, prove, and heuristically interpret a Newtonian analogue of Proposition 3.7.5c in this case.
