(d) Let $\lambda: \mathcal{E} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a freely falling photon. Define a function $e$ over $\lambda$ by the following condition: $\forall u \in \mathcal{E}$, $eu =$ photon energy observed by the instantaneous observer $(\lambda u, Z\lambda u)$, $Z$ as in Section 9.3.1. Show

$$e(1 + \phi \circ \vec{r}) = \text{constant},$$

the "Newtonian" equation for how the energy (and thus the frequency) of a photon changes as it climbs up or falls down in a gravitational field.
