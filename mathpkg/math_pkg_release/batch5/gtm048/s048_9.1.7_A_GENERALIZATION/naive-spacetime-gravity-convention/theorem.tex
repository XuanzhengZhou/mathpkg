On naive spacetime $(\mathbb{R}^4, g)$, take
$$g = -(1 + 2\phi) du^4 \otimes du^4 + (1 - 2\phi) \sum_{\alpha=1}^{3} du^\alpha \otimes du^\alpha,$$
where $\phi: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}$ is a time-independent Newtonian gravitational potential, with $\phi \ll 1$ so that all quadratic terms ($\phi^2$, $\phi \partial \phi$, $\phi \partial^2 \phi$) are negligible.
