Throughout this section the conventions in Section 9.0 hold. We will use a naive spacetime $(\mathbb{R}^4, g)$ (Section 9.0.2) with

$$g = -(1 + 2\phi) du^4 \otimes du^4 + (1 - 2\phi) \sum_{\alpha=1}^3 du^\alpha \otimes du^\alpha,$$

where $\phi: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}$ will be interpreted as a time-independent Newtonian gravitational potential (cf. Section 0.1.7 and the fine-print remarks in Section 1.3). We shall regard $\phi$ as so small compared to unity that all quadratic terms, such as $\phi^2$, $\phi(\partial\phi/\partial x^\alpha)$, $\phi(\partial^2\phi/\partial x^\alpha \partial x^\beta)$, and so on, are negligible.

(a) Show $\partial_4$ is a timelike Killing vector field and $(\mathbb{R}^4, g)$ is static.
