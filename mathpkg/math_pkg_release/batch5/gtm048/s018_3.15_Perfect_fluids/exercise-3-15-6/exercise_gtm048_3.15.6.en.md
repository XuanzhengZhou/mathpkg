Let $M$ be a quasi-gas on $M$ whose stress-energy tensor is spatially isotropic at each $z \in M$. Let $Z$ be the reference frame guaranteed by Proposition 3.14.1. The random 3-velocity $V_A$ of the $A^{\text{th}}$ particle flow at $z \in M$ is by definition the Newtonian velocity of $\gamma_A$ observed by $(z, Z_z)$; the energy $e_A$ of the $A^{\text{th}}$ particle flow is the energy of $\gamma_A$ observed by $(z, Z_z)$ (Sections 2.1 and 3.2). Note that $e_A > 0$ because of the wrong-way Schwarz inequality (Exercise 1.1.10). Denote the world density of the $A^{\text{th}}$ component by $\eta_A$, and the pressure and energy-density of the quasi-gas by $p$ and $\rho$, respectively. Show:

$$\frac{\sum_{A=1}^{N} \eta_A(z) e_A^2 |V_A|^2}{\sum_{A=1}^{N} \eta_A(z) e_A^2} \leq 1.$$

Roughly speaking, this exercise says:

$$3p = \rho \cdot |\text{average random 3-velocity}|^2,$$

which corresponds to the intuitive notion that random velocity creates pressure. In cosmology, the random 3-velocity of the galaxies can be observed and typically $|V_A| < 10^{-2}$. Thus $p \ll \rho$. This exercise should be compared with Exercise 3.8.8.
