This proposition characterizes the stress-energy tensor of a quasi-gas whose stress-energy is everywhere spatially isotropic. It shows that such a tensor admits a canonical decomposition into energy density $\rho$ and pressure $p$ terms, with the reference frame $Z$ serving as a distinguished eigenvector field. The result also establishes fundamental inequalities $\rho > 0$ and $\rho \geq 3p \geq 0$, and characterizes the equality case $\rho = 3p$ as equivalent to all components having zero rest-mass — a condition relevant to radiation-dominated regimes.
