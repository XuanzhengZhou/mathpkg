A photon beam is defined as a particle flow of type $(0, 0)$, meaning both the rest-mass $m$ and the electric charge $e$ are zero. Photons are massless and electrically neutral, so a photon beam represents a collection of light rays propagating through spacetime. This is the limiting case of the general charged particle flow $(m, e, P, \eta)$ introduced earlier in Section 3.2.4, and it plays a fundamental role in modeling electromagnetic radiation within the stress-energy tensor formalism.
