Corollary 7.5.4 establishes three fundamental properties of the Kruskal spacetime. Part (a) computes the Kretschmann scalar $f = R_{abcd}R^{abcd}$ as $144\mu^2/r^6$ everywhere on $K$, showing the curvature singularity at $r = 0$. Part (b) states that $K$ is vacuum (Ricci-flat, satisfying the vacuum Einstein equations), not flat (since $f \neq 0$), and not stationary (possesses no timelike Killing vector field). The proof uses analytic continuation from $M_I$, where the results are known from the Schwarzschild solution, together with the fact that $dr$ is timelike on $M_{II}$.
