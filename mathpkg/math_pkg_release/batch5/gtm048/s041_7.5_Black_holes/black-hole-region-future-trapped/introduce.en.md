Proposition 7.5.5 formalizes the defining property of a black hole: once a particle or observer enters the black hole region $M_{II}$ of the Kruskal spacetime, it can never escape. The future-directed trajectory from the entry point onward remains entirely within $M_{II}$. This is the geometric statement that the black hole region is a future set — its causal future is contained within itself. The proof uses the lightlike vector field $V$ (physically equivalent to $-dv$) to show that $r \circ \gamma$ is strictly decreasing along any trajectory entering $M_{II}$.
