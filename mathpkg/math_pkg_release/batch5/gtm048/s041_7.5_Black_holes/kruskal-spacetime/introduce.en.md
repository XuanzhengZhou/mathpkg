The Kruskal spacetime $(K, g)$ is the maximal analytic extension of the Schwarzschild solution. It is constructed as $K = \mathcal{Q} \times \mathcal{S}^2$ where $\mathcal{Q}$ is a region in the $(u,v)$-plane bounded by the hyperbola $uv = 2\mu/e$. The spacetime contains four regions: two isometric to the normal (exterior) Schwarzschild spacetime and two representing black hole and white hole interiors. The submanifold $M_I$ (where $u > 0, v > 0$) corresponds to the normal Schwarzschild exterior, while $M_{II}$ (where $u > 0, v < 0$) is the black hole interior from which no particle or photon can escape to infinity.
