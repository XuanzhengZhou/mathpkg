For $\mu \in (0, \infty)$, let $\mathcal{Q} \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ be the region determined by
$$uv < \frac{2\mu}{e} \quad \forall (u,v) \in \mathcal{Q},$$
where $e$ is the base of natural logarithms. Define
$$K = \mathcal{Q} \times \mathcal{S}^2.$$
The Kruskal spacetime $(K, g)$ is the maximal, vacuum spacetime whose metric $g$ extends the Schwarzschild metric and contains two normal Schwarzschild spacetimes and two Schwarzschild black holes.

The open submanifolds of $K$ are:
$$M_I = \{(u,v,p) \in K : u > 0,\; v > 0\} \quad \text{(normal Schwarzschild region)},$$
$$M_{II} = \{(u,v,p) \in K : u > 0,\; v < 0\} \quad \text{(black hole region)}.$$
The closure satisfies $\mathcal{A} = \overline{M_I \cup M_{II}}$.
