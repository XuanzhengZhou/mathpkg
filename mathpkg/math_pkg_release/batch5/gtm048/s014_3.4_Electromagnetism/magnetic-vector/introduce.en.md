The magnetic vector $B$ is the magnetic component of an electromagnetic field $F$ measured by an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$. It is defined implicitly via exterior algebra: for any two vectors $X, Y$ in the observer's rest space $Z^\perp$, the value $F(X,Y)$ equals $4!\,\Omega(X,Y,B,Z)$, where $\Omega$ is the metric volume element. This construction is essentially the Hodge dual of $F$ contracted with $Z$, isolating the magnetic degrees of freedom in the observer's frame.
