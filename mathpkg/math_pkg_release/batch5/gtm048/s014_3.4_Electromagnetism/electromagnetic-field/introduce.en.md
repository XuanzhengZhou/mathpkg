An electromagnetic field on a spacetime $M$ is defined as an antisymmetric $(0,2)$-tensor field $F$, i.e., a 2-form. This geometric definition unifies the electric and magnetic fields of pre-relativistic physics into a single covariant object. Given an instantaneous observer, $F$ decomposes into an electric vector and a magnetic vector in the observer's rest space, recovering the familiar three-dimensional fields as observer-dependent projections.
