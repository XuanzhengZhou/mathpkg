Let $(K,g)$ be a Kruskal spacetime. The open submanifold $M_I$ is determined by $u > 0, v > 0$ in Kruskal null coordinates. Then $(M_I, g|_{M_I})$ is isometric to the normal Schwarzschild spacetime of active mass $8\pi\mu$.

The isometry $\psi: M_I \rightarrow (-\infty,\infty) \times (2\mu,\infty) \times \mathcal{S}^2$ is given by $\psi(n) = (t(n), r(n), P(n))$ for every $n \in M_I$, where $t = 2\mu[\ln u - \ln(-v)]|_{M_I}$ and $r$ is the unique number in $(2\mu,\infty)$ determined by the Kruskal coordinate relation.
