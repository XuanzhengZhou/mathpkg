A gravitational plane wave spacetime is a model for gravitational radiation in regions far from the source. It is constructed by perturbing the Minkowski metric with a term involving two amplitude functions $f$ and $g$ depending on the phase coordinate $\phi$. The wave travels in a lightlike direction (given by the Killing vector field $Y$), and its wavefronts are lightlike 3-surfaces of constant phase. These spacetimes are the gravitational analogue of electromagnetic plane waves, with the key difference that a graviton has spin 2 (manifested by two independent amplitude functions and transverse relative accelerations) rather than spin 1.
