(Hard) One of the most sensitive tests of general relativity is the measurement of what is called planetary perihelion precession, particularly for Mercury. Let $(\gamma, m)$ be a freely falling particle with rest-mass $m \neq 0$, total angular momentum $J \neq 0$, and total energy $E < m$. Assume the domain $\mathcal{E}$ of $\gamma$ is $\mathcal{E} = \mathbb{R}$ and that $(\gamma, m)$ does not circle (Section 7.3.8). Let $\tilde{\gamma}$ be the orbit function. Show that $\tilde{\gamma}$ is periodic. Write the period $P$ as $P = 2\pi + \Delta\Phi$; $\Delta\Phi$ is called the perihelion precession. Compute $\Delta\Phi$ for parameters appropriate to Mercury using appropriate approximations. If $N$ is the number of times Mercury goes around the sun in one earth century, show $N\Delta\Phi \cong 43$ seconds of arc.
