A gravitational plane wave is linearly polarized when its two amplitude functions $f$ and $g$ are linearly dependent over $\mathbb{R}$. In this case, the relative acceleration transformation $\psi_u$ measured by any geodesic observer has a simplified structure, and the spacetime polarization plane has a well-defined constant direction. This corresponds physically to a gravitational wave with a single, fixed polarization state.
