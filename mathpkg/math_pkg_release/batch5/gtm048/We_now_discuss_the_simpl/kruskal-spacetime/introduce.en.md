The Kruskal spacetime is the maximal analytic extension of the Schwarzschild solution in general relativity. It is constructed as $(K,g) = (\mathcal{Q} \times \mathcal{S}^2, g)$ where $\mathcal{Q} \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ is a region in the Kruskal null coordinate plane, and the metric $g$ is expressed in terms of Kruskal null coordinates $u,v$. The spacetime contains four distinct regions: two normal Schwarzschild spacetimes (regions $I$ and $III$), one black hole region (region $II$), and one white hole region (region $IV$). It provides the simplest complete model for gravitational collapse and is a maximal, vacuum spacetime.
