Let $\mu \in (0,\infty)$, let $e$ be the base of natural logarithms, and let $\mathcal{Q} \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ be the region determined by $(u^1 q)(u^2 q) < 2\mu/e$ for all $q \in \mathcal{Q}$. Define
$$K = \mathcal{Q} \times \mathcal{S}^2$$
with the Lorentz metric
$$g = 2\mu r^{-1} \exp(1 - r/2\mu) du^1 \otimes du^2 + r^2 h \quad \text{on } K$$
where $r = r(q)$ is the unique number in $(0,\infty)$ satisfying $(2\mu/r - 1) \exp(r/2\mu) = -u^1 u^2$, and $h$ is the usual Riemannian metric for the unit 2-sphere $\mathcal{S}^2$. The coordinate functions $u = u^1$ and $v = u^2$ are called Kruskal null coordinates. Then $(K,g)$ is a spacetime called a Kruskal spacetime. $K$ is diffeomorphic to $\mathcal{S}^2 \times \{\text{the two-dimensional region}\}$ and is a maximal, vacuum spacetime that contains two normal Schwarzschild spacetimes (regions $M_I$, $M_{III}$) and two Schwarzschild black holes (regions $M_{II}$, $M_{IV}$).
