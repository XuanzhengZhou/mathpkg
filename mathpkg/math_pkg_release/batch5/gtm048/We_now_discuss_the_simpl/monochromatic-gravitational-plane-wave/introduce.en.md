A gravitational plane wave is monochromatic when its amplitude functions $f$ and $g$ are sinusoidal with a single frequency $\omega$. In this case the wave has a characteristic frequency, but it is important to note that the frequency is observer-dependent: different instantaneous observers will measure different frequencies due to the lightlike nature of the propagation direction. The frequency $\omega$ is determined only up to a constant factor depending on the observer's energy relative to the Killing vector field $Y$.
