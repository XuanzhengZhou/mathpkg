An instantaneous observer captures the state of an observer at a single point in spacetime, as opposed to a full observer which is an entire world line. It is an ordered pair consisting of a spacetime point and a future-pointing timelike unit vector at that point. This abstraction is sufficient for many purposes where only the tangent vector matters, such as Newtonian interpretations of relativistic concepts.
