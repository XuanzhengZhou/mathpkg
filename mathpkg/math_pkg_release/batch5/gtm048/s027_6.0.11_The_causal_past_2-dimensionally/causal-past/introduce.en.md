The causal past $J^-(z)$ of a point $z$ in a spacetime $M$ is the set of all events from which a signal (such as a freely falling particle or light ray) can reach $z$. Physically, if $z$ models "here-now," then $J^-(z)$ is precisely the observable universe -- the collection of all spacetime events that could have influenced the present observation. The causal past is defined via the causal relation $x \leq z$, meaning there exists a future-directed causal curve from $x$ to $z$.
