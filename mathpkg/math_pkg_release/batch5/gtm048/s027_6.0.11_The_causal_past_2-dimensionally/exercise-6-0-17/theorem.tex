Let $(N, h)$ be a simple cosmological spacetime (Example 1.4.3).
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] Each Euclidean isometry $\bar{\psi}$ gives rise to an isometry $\psi: N \to N$ just as in Proposition 6.0.5f.
  \item[(b)] Find an example where $G N$ (the isometry group of $N$) is larger than the Euclidean isometry group of $\mathbb{R}^3$.
  \item[(c)] Show from (a) and Section 3.6.3a that $\partial_\mu$ is a Killing vector field.
\end{enumerate}
