Let $(N, h)$ be a simple cosmological spacetime (Example 1.4.3).

(a) Show that each Euclidean isometry $\bar{\psi}$ gives rise to an isometry $\psi: N \to N$ just as in Proposition 6.0.5f.

(b) Find an example where $G N$ is larger than the Euclidean isometry group of $\mathbb{R}^3$.

(c) Show from (a) and Section 3.6.3a that $\partial_\mu$ is a Killing vector field.
