This exercise studies the isometry group of simple cosmological spacetimes. Part (a) shows that each Euclidean isometry of the spatial slices lifts to a spacetime isometry, generalizing the construction from Einstein-de Sitter spacetime. Part (b) asks for an example where the spacetime isometry group is strictly larger than the Euclidean isometry group, illustrating that spatial symmetry is not the whole story. Part (c) deduces that the coordinate vector fields $\partial_\mu$ are Killing vector fields.
