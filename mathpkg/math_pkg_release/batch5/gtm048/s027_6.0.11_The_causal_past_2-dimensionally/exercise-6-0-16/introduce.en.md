This exercise studies the orbit $S$ of a point $x$ under the group $O_z^3$ of spatial rotations about $z$ in Einstein-de Sitter spacetime. When $\delta(x,z) \neq 0$, the orbit is shown to be a spacelike 2-dimensional submanifold diffeomorphic to the 2-sphere $S^2$, explicitly characterized by the constancy of $u^4$ and $\delta(\cdot, z)$. Moreover, the induced Riemannian metric on $S$ is exactly that of a round sphere in $\mathbb{R}^3$ whose radius depends on both the cosmic time $u^4 x$ and the spatial distance $\delta(x,z)$.
