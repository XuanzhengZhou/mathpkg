In Einstein-de Sitter spacetime $M$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] $M$ is spatially isotropic for an instantaneous observer $(x, X)$ iff $X = \partial_4 x$.
  \item[(b)] The Einstein tensor $Gx$ is spatially isotropic for $(x, X)$ iff $X = \partial_4 x$.
  \item[(c)] $u^4$ is the unique time function for $\partial_4$ whose image is $(0, \infty)$.
  \item[(d)] For $z \in M$, $u^4 z$ is the ``longest timelike distance to the big bang'': no future-pointing timelike curve with future endpoint $z$ has arclength less than $u^4 z$, but the past-endless $\partial_4$ integral curve with future endpoint $z$ has arclength $u^4 z$.
\end{enumerate}
