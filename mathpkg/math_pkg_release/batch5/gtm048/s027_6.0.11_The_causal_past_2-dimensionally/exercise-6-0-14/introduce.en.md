This exercise gives characterizations of Einstein-de Sitter spacetime that are closer to observational quantities than the geometric characterizations introduced earlier. Part (a) extends Exercise 2.1.11 by characterizing spatial isotropy in terms of the instantaneous observer. Part (b) relates spatial isotropy of the Einstein tensor to the same condition. Part (c) establishes the uniqueness of the cosmic time function $u^4$. Part (d) gives a geometric interpretation of $u^4 z$ as the longest possible proper time from the big bang to $z$.
