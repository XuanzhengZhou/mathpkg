In a relativistic model $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$, the matter content $\mathcal{M}$ is assigned a stress-energy tensor that encodes its energy, momentum, and stress distributions on spacetime. When the matter model consists of a finite collection of particle flows $\{(m_A, e_A, P_A, \eta_A)\}$, each flow contributes a term $\eta_A P_A \otimes P_A$ to the total stress-energy tensor. The resulting $(0,2)$-tensor field $\hat{T} = \sum_{A=1}^{N} \eta_A P_A \otimes P_A$ satisfies the defining properties of a stress-energy tensor as introduced in Definition 3.3.5.
