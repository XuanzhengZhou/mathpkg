\textbf{Proposition 3.7.5.} Let $J$ be a vector field on $M$, let $\Omega$ be the metric volume element on $M$, and let $F$ be a 2-form on $M$. Then $(M, F, J)$ obeys Maxwell's equations if and only if:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\displaystyle \int_{\partial \mathcal{D}} i(\hat{F})\Omega = 4\pi \int_{\mathcal{D}} i(J)\Omega$ for every space-section $\mathcal{D}$; and
\item[(b)] $\displaystyle \int_{\partial \mathcal{C}} F = 0$ for every 3-dimensional compact submanifold-with-boundary $\mathcal{C} \subset M$.
\end{enumerate}

Here $i(\cdot)$ denotes the interior product, and $\hat{F}$ is the $(2,0)$-tensor field physically equivalent to $F$.
