Proposition 3.7.5 provides the integral formulation of Maxwell's equations in general relativity. Condition (a) states that the flux of $\hat{F}$ through the boundary of any space-section $\mathcal{D}$ equals $4\pi$ times the total charge enclosed, generalizing Gauss's law. Condition (b) asserts that the integral of the 2-form $F$ over the boundary of any 3-dimensional compact submanifold-with-boundary vanishes, which, via Stokes' theorem, is equivalent to the closure condition $dF = 0$. These integral forms reveal the geometric nature of Maxwell's theory as a statement about conservation of electromagnetic flux and charge.
