Maxwell's equations in general relativity unify the four classical Maxwell equations into two elegant geometric conditions. The first condition, $dF = 0$, states that the electromagnetic 2-form $F$ is closed, which is equivalent to the absence of magnetic monopoles and Faraday's law of induction. The second condition, $\operatorname{div} \hat{F} = 4\pi J$, relates the divergence of the physically equivalent $(2,0)$-tensor $\hat{F}$ to the charge-current density $J$, encoding the Ampere-Maxwell law and Gauss's law. Together, these equations describe how matter and spacetime influence the electromagnetic field.
