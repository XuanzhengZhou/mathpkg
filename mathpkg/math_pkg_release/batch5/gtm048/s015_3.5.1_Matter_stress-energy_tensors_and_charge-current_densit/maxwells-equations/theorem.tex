\textbf{Definition 3.7.1.} Let $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$ be a relativistic model. Then $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$ \textbf{obeys Maxwell's equations} if and only if:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $F$ is closed --- that is, $dF = 0$; and
\item[(b)] $\operatorname{div} \hat{F} = 4\pi J$,
\end{enumerate}

where $J$ is the charge-current density of $\mathcal{M}$ and $\hat{F}$ is the $(2,0)$-tensor field physically equivalent to $F$. One then calls $J$ the \textbf{source} of $F$ and also says the triple $(M, F, J)$ obeys Maxwell's equations.
