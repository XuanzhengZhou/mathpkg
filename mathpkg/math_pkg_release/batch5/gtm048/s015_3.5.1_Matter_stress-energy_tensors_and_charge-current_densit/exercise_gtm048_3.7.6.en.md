Let $(M, g)$ be Minkowski space with the $u^4$-axis deleted. Let $r: M \to (0, \infty)$ be defined by $r = \left( \sum_{v=1}^{3} u^v u^v \right)^{1/2}$, and, for $e \in \mathbb{R}$, define $F = (4\pi)^{-1} e r^{-2} dr \wedge du^4$.

**(a)** Show that $(M, F, 0)$ obeys Maxwell's equations.

**(b)** Let $\mathcal{C} \subset M$ be an imbedded submanifold homeomorphic to the 2-sphere. Show that if $\mathcal{C}$ is contractible, $\int_{\mathcal{C}} i(\hat{F})\Omega = 0$.

**(c)** Give an example of a $\mathcal{C}$ such that $\int_{\mathcal{C}} i(\hat{F})\Omega = e$.

**(d)** Give an example of an instantaneous observer who observes a nonzero magnetic vector.

**(e)** Show that no instantaneous observer observes zero electric vector.

*Remark:* $F$ in this exercise is the electric field of a point charge at rest; the deleted $u^4$-axis corresponds to the history of the point charge. Part (d) shows that "an observer moving in an electric field observes a magnetic field."
