For any $p$-form $\eta$ on a semi-Riemannian manifold, the exterior derivative can be expressed in terms of the covariant derivative along a basis of vector fields: $d\eta = \omega^i \wedge D_{X_i} \eta$, where $\{X_i\}$ is any basis of vector fields and $\{\omega^i\}$ its dual basis. The right-hand side is independent of the choice of basis, making it globally well-defined. This formula provides a convenient computational link between the exterior derivative and the covariant derivative, and is proven by verifying equality at each point using normal coordinates.
