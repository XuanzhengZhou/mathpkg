The stress-energy tensor $E$ of an electromagnetic field $F$ encodes the energy density, momentum density, and stress associated with the electromagnetic field. Its components are defined as $E_{ij} = (1/4\pi)[F_{im}F_j^{\,m} - \frac{1}{4}g_{ij}F_{mn}F^{mn}]$, which unifies the classical electromagnetic energy density $(1/8\pi)(\vec{E}^2 + \vec{B}^2)$, the Poynting vector $(1/4\pi)\vec{E} \times \vec{B}$, and the Maxwell stress tensor into a single covariant object. The tensor $E$ has vanishing trace and its physically equivalent $(2,0)$-tensor $\hat{E}$ satisfies the dominant energy condition, making it a valid stress-energy tensor in the sense of Section 3.3.1.
