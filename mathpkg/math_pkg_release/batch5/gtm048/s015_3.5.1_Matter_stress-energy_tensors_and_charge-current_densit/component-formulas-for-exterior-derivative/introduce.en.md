The exterior derivative of a differential form can be expressed in components using covariant derivative notation. For a 1-form, $(d\eta)_{ij} = \frac{1}{2}(\eta_{j|i} - \eta_{i|j})$; for a 2-form, the formula involves a cyclic sum with factor $\frac{1}{3}$; and for a 3-form, an alternating sum with factor $\frac{1}{4}$. These formulas generalize the familiar pattern: the exterior derivative is the antisymmetrized covariant derivative, with the vertical bar $|$ denoting covariant differentiation. The formulas are valid in any coordinate system when the covariant derivative is torsion-free.
