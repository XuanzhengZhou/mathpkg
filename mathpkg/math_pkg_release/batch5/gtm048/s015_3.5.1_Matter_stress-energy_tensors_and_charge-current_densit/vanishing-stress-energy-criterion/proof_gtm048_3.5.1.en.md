From the definition of the stress-energy tensor, $\hat{T} = \sum_{A=1}^{N} \eta_A P_A \otimes P_A$, we have

$$T(Z, Z) = \sum_{A=1}^{N} (\eta_A z) [g(P_A, Z)]^2.$$

For each $A$, the world density $\eta_A z$ is nonnegative by definition. Moreover, for any instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$, we have $e_A = -g(P_A, Z) > 0$ (as established in Section 3.3.1 for particle flows). Since $[g(P_A, Z)]^2 > 0$ for all $A$, the sum $\sum (\eta_A z)[g(P_A, Z)]^2$ is a sum of nonnegative terms. Hence $T(Z, Z) = 0$ if and only if each term vanishes, i.e., $\eta_A z = 0$ for all $A$. This in turn is equivalent to $\hat{T}z = 0$ by the definition of $\hat{T}$ as a sum of tensor products.
