Take a rotating reference frame on Minkowski spacetime and show explicitly how the radar-based synchronization procedure of Section 5.3 fails to produce a consistent global time function. Identify where the consistency condition $t_1 = \frac{1}{2}(t_0 + t_2)$ breaks down for nearby radar measurements, and explain how this failure corresponds to the fact that a rotating reference frame is not synchronizable (its physically equivalent 1-form $\omega$ satisfies $\omega \wedge d\omega \neq 0$).
