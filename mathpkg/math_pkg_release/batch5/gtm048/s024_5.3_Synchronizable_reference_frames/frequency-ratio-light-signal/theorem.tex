Let $\lambda: [a, b] \to M$ be a freely falling photon and let $(\lambda_a, Z_a)$, $(\lambda_b, Z_b)$ be instantaneous observers at emission and reception respectively. Let $[\lambda]$ denote the light signal containing $\lambda$. The **frequency ratio** for $([\lambda], Z_a, Z_b)$ is defined as

$$r = \frac{g(\lambda_* a, Z_a)}{g(\lambda_* b, Z_b)} = \frac{f_a}{f_b},$$

where $f_A = -g(\lambda_* A, Z_A)/h$ are the measured frequencies ($A = a, b$). The value $r$ is independent of the choice of representative photon $\lambda$ within the light signal $[\lambda]$.
