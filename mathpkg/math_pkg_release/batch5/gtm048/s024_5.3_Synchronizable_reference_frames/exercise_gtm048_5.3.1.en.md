Generalize the radar measurement discussion in Section 5.3 to the case that $Z$ is synchronizable but not proper time synchronizable. Assume one autocrat $\gamma$ and other observers who regard the consistency condition

$$t_1 = \frac{1}{2}(t_0 + t_2)$$

for every nearby radar measurement as more important than insisting on their own proper time. Show that the observers, by accepting this consistency condition as fundamental, can still establish a common time function $t$ on (part of) $M$, and that at worst they need only rescale their own proper time to get agreement with $t$.
