Let $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer on spacetime $M$ and let $P_z = \mathcal{S}_Z \times (0, \infty)$ be its direction-energy space. A **photon distribution function** for $(z, Z)$ is a function

$$F_Z: P_z \to [0, \infty).$$

Physically, $e^2 F_Z(U, e)$ models the number of photons per unit spatial volume per unit solid angle per unit energy interval at direction $U \in \mathcal{S}_Z$ and energy $e \in (0, \infty)$. The factor $e^3 F_Z(U, e)$ gives the perceived brightness in direction $U$.
