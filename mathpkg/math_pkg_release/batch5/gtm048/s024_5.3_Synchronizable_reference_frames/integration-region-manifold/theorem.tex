Let $N$ be a $C^\infty$ manifold and let $\Lambda$ be a volume element on $N$, so that $N$ is orientable. Take $N$ and open submanifolds of $N$ as oriented via $\Lambda$. An **integration region** in $N$ is a compact subset $\mathcal{K} \subset N$ such that the interior $\mathcal{K}^0$ is nonempty and both $\mathcal{K}$ and $\partial\mathcal{K}$ are piecewise $C^\infty$. This condition guarantees that $\int_{\mathcal{K}} f\Lambda$ is a well-defined real number whenever $f: N \to \mathbb{R}$ is suitably regular.
