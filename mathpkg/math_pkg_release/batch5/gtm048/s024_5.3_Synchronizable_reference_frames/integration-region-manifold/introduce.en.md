An integration region provides the minimal geometric conditions needed to define integrals of functions on a manifold equipped with a volume element. The requirements — compactness, nonempty interior, and piecewise smooth boundary — are sufficient to guarantee that the Riemann-type integral $\int_{\mathcal{K}} f\Lambda$ exists for continuous integrands $f$. This notion is used throughout general relativity to integrate quantities such as energy density over spatial regions, where the volume element is induced by the spacetime metric and the integration takes place on spacelike hypersurfaces.
