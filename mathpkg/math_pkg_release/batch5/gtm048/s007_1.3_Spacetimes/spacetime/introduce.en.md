A spacetime is the fundamental mathematical object studied in general relativity. It consists of a connected 4-dimensional Lorentzian manifold that is both oriented and time-oriented, equipped with its Levi-Civita connection. The 4 dimensions model the three spatial dimensions and one time dimension of the physical universe. Connectedness reflects the intuition that the universe is not "disconnected" across time, and time-orientability is motivated by the second law of thermodynamics, which allows distinguishing past from future directions. The inclusion of the Levi-Civita connection is a choice — many alternative formulations using metric connections with torsion have been proposed, but these are not treated in the standard framework.
