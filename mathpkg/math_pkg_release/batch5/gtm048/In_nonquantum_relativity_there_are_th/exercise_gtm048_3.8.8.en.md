To analyze collisions, one often uses a "center of mass frame." Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a consistent particle set and $x$ a collision event. Show that there exists an instantaneous observer $(x, Z)$ at $x$ such that the total 3-momentum vanishes:

$$\sum_{\gamma \in \operatorname{in}(x)} p_\gamma = 0,$$

where $p_\gamma$ is the 3-momentum of $\gamma$ measured by $(x, Z)$. Equivalently, $Z$ is proportional to the total 4-momentum $\sum_{\operatorname{in}(x)} \gamma_*$.
