A consistent particle set formalizes the collision laws of relativistic particle physics directly on spacetime. A finite collection of particles $\mathcal{P}$ is consistent if, at every collision event, both the total 4-momentum and the total electric charge are conserved between incoming and outgoing particles. Each particle is assumed to contain its endpoints to avoid physically irrelevant "wiggling" pathologies. The conservation laws operate at a macroscopic level and are powerful enough to yield predictions even when the detailed microphysics of a collision is unknown—the historical discovery of the neutrino being a prime example, where apparent violation of 4-momentum conservation led Pauli to postulate a new, unseen outgoing particle.
