Let $(M, g, D)$ be a spacetime, $(m, e)$ a particle type, $F$ an electromagnetic field on $M$, $x \in M$, and $W \in M_x$ future-pointing with $g(W, W) = -m^2$.

\textbf{(a)} There is precisely one inextendible curve $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow M$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\gamma 0 = x$,
  \item $\gamma_* 0 = W$,
  \item $\gamma$ obeys the Lorentz world-force law with respect to $F$ (Definition 3.8.1).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{(b)} $g(\gamma_*, \gamma_*) = g(W, W)$ everywhere on $\mathcal{E}$, hence $(\gamma, m, e)$ is a particle on $M$ with rest mass $m$.
