This theorem is the "present determines the future" result for charged particles in general relativity, analogous to the ODE existence-and-uniqueness theorem for Newtonian mechanics. Given initial position $x$ and initial 4-velocity $W$ (with $g(W,W) = -m^2$), the Lorentz world-force law determines exactly one inextendible worldline $\gamma$ starting from those data. Part (b) confirms that the rest mass is conserved along the trajectory—the length of the 4-velocity remains constant, so $(\gamma, m, e)$ is indeed a particle. The proof reduces the geometric equation to a system of second-order ODEs in local coordinates and invokes the basic existence theorem, then extends to an inextendible curve via Zorn's lemma.
