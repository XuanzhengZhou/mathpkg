In Minkowski space, every light signal admits a canonical parametrization by a spatial direction vector $w$ (unit length) and an offset vector $v$ perpendicular to $w$. Two such light signals are defined to meet at infinity if they share the same spatial direction $w$ and have offset vectors differing only along that direction. This notion, introduced by Hawking and Ellis, captures the idea that parallel light rays converge at future null infinity, and it generalizes to certain classes of nonflat spacetimes.
