Let $(\mathbb{R}^4, g)$ be Minkowski space. Each inextendible light signal has exactly one representative $\lambda: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^4$ of the form $\lambda(u) = (v, 0) + u(w, 1)$ where $v, w \in \mathbb{R}^3$ and $w \cdot w = 1$ (with the $\mathbb{R}^3$ Euclidean inner product). Conversely, for every pair $(v, w) \in \mathbb{R}^3 \times \mathbb{R}^3$ with $w \cdot w = 1$ there is a unique light signal, denoted $[\lambda]_{(v, w)}$.

Two light signals $[\lambda]_{(v, w)}$ and $[\lambda]_{(v', w')}$ are said to \textbf{meet at infinity}, denoted
$$[\lambda]_{(v, w)} \sim [\lambda]_{(v', w')},$$
if and only if $w = w'$ and $(v - v') \cdot w = 0$.
