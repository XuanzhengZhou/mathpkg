Let $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to M$ be an observer with worldline $\beta = \gamma(\mathcal{E})$. Suppose $u_1 \in \mathcal{E}$ is given. Then there exists an open interval $\mathcal{F} \subset \mathcal{E}$ containing $u_1$ and an open neighborhood $\mathcal{W}$ of $\beta(u_1)$ such that, for all $x \in \mathcal{W} \setminus \beta(\mathcal{F})$, the following holds:

There exist $u_0, u_2 \in \mathcal{F}$, a light signal $[\lambda]$ from $x$ to $\beta(u_2)$, and a light signal $[\lambda']$ from $\beta(u_0)$ to $x$. Moreover, $u_0$, $u_2$, $[\lambda]$, and $[\lambda']$ are unique.
