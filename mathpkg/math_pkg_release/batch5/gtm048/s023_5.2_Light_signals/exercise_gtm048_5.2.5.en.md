Let $(M, g)$ be Einstein-de Sitter spacetime; thus $M = \mathbb{R}^3 \times (0, \infty)$.

(a) If $\tilde{\psi}: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is a Euclidean isometry, then $\psi: M \to M$ defined by $\psi(v, t) = (\tilde{\psi} v, t)$ is an isometry of $(M, g)$.

(b) If $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to M$ is a geodesic, there exists an isometry $\psi: M \to M$ such that $\gamma \circ \psi$ lies in the plane $u^1 = 0 = u^2$.

(c) If $\gamma$ in (b) is an inextendible, freely falling photon, there exists an isometry $\psi$ such that $[\gamma \circ \psi] = [\lambda]$, where $\lambda$ is as in Example 5.2.2. (Hint: See Section 6.0.5 if you get stuck.)
