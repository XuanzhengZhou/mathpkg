A curve in a manifold $M$ is a smooth map $\gamma: E \to M$ where $E \subset \mathbb{R}$ is an interval. Smoothness is understood in the sense that $\gamma$ extends to a $C^\infty$ map on an open superset of $E$. This definition allows curves defined on closed or half-open intervals while retaining smoothness at endpoints.
