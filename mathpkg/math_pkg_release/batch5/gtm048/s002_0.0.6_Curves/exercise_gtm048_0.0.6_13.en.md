(c) Show that the map $\hat{g}: V^* \times V^* \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ defined by

$$\hat{g}(\omega, \omega') = g(\phi^{-1}\omega, \phi^{-1}\omega'), \quad \forall \omega, \omega' \in V^*,$$

is an inner product on $V^*$, where $\phi: V \to V^{**}$ is the canonical isomorphism.

(d) Show that $\operatorname{index} g = \operatorname{index} \hat{g}$. (In particular, $\hat{g}$ is Lorentzian iff $g$ is.)

(e) Let $\{e_1, \ldots, e_N\}$ be an orthonormal basis of $V$ with respect to $g$, and let $\{e^1, \ldots, e^N\}$ be its dual basis. Show that $\{e^1, \ldots, e^N\}$ is orthonormal with respect to $\hat{g}$. (This justifies the terminology of ``orthonormal basis of 1-forms'' introduced in Section 0.0.9.)

(f) Show that $\hat{g}$, considered as a $(2,0)$ tensor, is $\phi$-equivalent to $g$ in the sense of Exercise 0.0.14(c).

(g) Show that the element of $V$ $\phi$-equivalent to an $\omega \in V^*$ is given by $\hat{g}(\omega, \cdot)$.
