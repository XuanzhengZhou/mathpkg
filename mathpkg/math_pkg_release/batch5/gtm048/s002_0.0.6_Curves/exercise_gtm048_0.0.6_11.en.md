Let $V$ be an $N$-dimensional vector space, $g$ be an inner product on $V$, and $W \subset V$ be a $K$-dimensional subspace. We define

$$W^\perp = \{v \in V \mid g(v, w) = 0\ \forall w \in W\};$$

if $w$ spans $W$, we shall also write $w^\perp \equiv W^\perp$.

Show:

(a) $W^\perp$ is an $(N - K)$-dimensional subspace.

(b) $W^{\perp\perp} = W$.

(c) $V = W \oplus W^\perp$ iff $g|_W$ is nondegenerate.
