Let $\gamma$ be an observer on a spacetime $(M, g, D)$. Let $p_u: M_{\gamma u} \to R_u$ and $q_u: M_{\gamma u} \to T_u$ be the orthogonal projections onto the local rest space and local time axis of $(\gamma u, \gamma_* u)$, and let $pX$, $qX$ be the corresponding vector fields over $\gamma$. There exists precisely one connection $F$ over $\gamma$ such that for every vector field $Y$ on $\mathcal{E}$ and every vector field $X$ over $\gamma$,
$$F_Y X = \bigl[p(\gamma^* D)_Y p + q(\gamma^* D)_Y q\bigr] X.$$
Moreover, if $f$ is a $C^\infty$ function over $\gamma$, then $F_Y(fX) = f F_Y X + (Yf)X$. Then $F$ extends uniquely to a derivation on all tensor fields over $\gamma$ that commutes with contractions and satisfies $F_Y f = Yf$.
