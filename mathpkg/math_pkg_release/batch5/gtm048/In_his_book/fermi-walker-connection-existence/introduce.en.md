Proposition 2.2.1 establishes the existence and uniqueness of the Fermi-Walker connection along an observer's worldline. Given an observer $\gamma$, the orthogonal projections $p$ and $q$ onto the local rest space and local time axis are used to define $F$ from the ambient Levi-Civita connection $D$. The Fermi-Walker connection is the unique derivation on tensor fields over $\gamma$ extending this definition and is the mathematical tool for determining when a spatial direction remains "the same" over proper time, which underpins the operational definition of a gyroscope axis.
