$F_Y X$ is $\mathbb{R}$-linear in $X$ and linear (with respect to $C^\infty$ functions over $\gamma$) in $Y$. Moreover, if $f$ is a $C^\infty$ function over $\gamma$, then
$$F_Y(fX) = [p(\gamma^* D)_Y p + q(\gamma^* D)_Y q](fX)$$
$$= f \cdot [p(\gamma^* D)_Y p + q(\gamma^* D)_Y q]X + (Yf) \cdot (p^2 + q^2)X$$
$$= f F_Y X + (Yf)X,$$
since $p^2 = p$, $q^2 = q$, and $p + q = \text{identity}$ on $M_{\gamma u}$ (the direct sum decomposition), so $p^2 + q^2 = p + q = \text{id}$. If we demand $F_Y f = Yf$ for functions $f$ over $\gamma$, then there is precisely one derivation of tensor fields over $\gamma$ that commutes with contractions and agrees with $F_Y$ on vector fields and functions over $\gamma$ (by the standard theory of tensor derivations, cf. Section 2.0.1).
