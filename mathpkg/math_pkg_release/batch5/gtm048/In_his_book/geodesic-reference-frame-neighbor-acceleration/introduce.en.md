Proposition 2.3.3 is the general-relativistic analogue of the Newtonian tidal force equation. In a geodesic reference frame (one where all observers are freely falling, $D_Q Q = 0$), the relative 3-acceleration between neighboring observers is governed directly by the Riemann curvature tensor via $\psi_Z X = R_{X Z} Z$. This is the equation of geodesic deviation expressed in the Fermi-Walker formalism: curvature acts as a linear operator on the spatial separation vector, producing relative acceleration. The self-adjointness of $\psi_Z$ reflects the symmetries of the Riemann tensor. This proposition provides the mathematical foundation for understanding how spacetime curvature manifests as tidal forces.
