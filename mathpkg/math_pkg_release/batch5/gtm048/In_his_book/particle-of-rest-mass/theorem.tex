A **particle of rest-mass $m$** (where $m \geq 0$) on a spacetime $(M, g, D)$ is a future-pointing curve $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to M$ such that
$$g(\gamma_*, \gamma_*) = -m^2.$$
We sometimes write $(\gamma, m)$ for a particle $\gamma$ of rest-mass $m$. If $m \neq 0$, there exists a positive affine reparametrization making $\gamma$ an observer (so the terminology for observers carries over: arc-length is proper time, etc.). A particle $\gamma$ is **freely falling** iff $\gamma$ is geodesic, i.e., $D_{\gamma_*} \gamma_* = 0$.
