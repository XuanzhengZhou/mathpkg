The Fermi-Walker connection $F$ along an observer's worldline provides the mathematical framework for defining "non-rotation" in general relativity. An observer detects whether a spatial direction remains the same over proper time by $F$-parallel transport: a vector field $X$ over $\gamma$ represents a non-rotating direction if $F_{\gamma_*} X = 0$. Physically, the axis of an ideal gyroscope traces out an $F$-parallel vector field, making the Fermi-Walker connection the operational tool for measuring rotation without reference to distant matter.
