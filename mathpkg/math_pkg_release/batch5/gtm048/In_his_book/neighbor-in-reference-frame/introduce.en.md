Definition 2.3.2 formalizes the notion of "neighboring observers" within a reference frame $Q$. A neighbor of an observer $\gamma$ in $Q$ is a spatial vector field $W$ along $\gamma$ that arises as the spatial projection of a vector field $W'$ which is Lie-invariant along $Q$. Physically, $W$ represents the infinitesimal separation between $\gamma$ and a one-parameter family of nearby observers in $Q$. The condition $L_Q W' = 0$ ensures the family of observers is "comoving" with $Q$, while $pW' = W$ extracts the purely spatial component.
