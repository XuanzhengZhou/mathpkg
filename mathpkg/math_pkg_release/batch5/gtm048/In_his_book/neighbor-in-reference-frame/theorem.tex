Let $Q$ be a reference frame on a spacetime $(M, g, D)$, and let $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to M$ be an observer in $Q$. Let $p$ and $q$ be the orthogonal projection operators onto the local rest space and local time axis of $(\gamma u, \gamma_* u)$. A vector field $W$ over $\gamma$ is called a **neighbor of $\gamma$ in $Q$** iff there exists a vector field $W'$ over $\gamma$ such that
$$pW' = W \quad\text{and}\quad L_Q W' = 0,$$
where $L_Q$ is the Lie derivative along $Q$ (restricted to $\gamma$).
