In 2-dimensional Minkowski space let $\gamma_1$ and $\gamma_2$ be freely falling observers such that $\gamma_1 0 = 0$ and $\gamma_2 0 = 0$. Let $\gamma_1$ be the observer at rest: $\gamma_1 u = (0, u)$. Assume $\gamma_2$ is another freely falling observer. (a) Show $L_2 < L_1$. (b) If $|v|$ is the Newtonian speed of $\gamma_2$ observed by $(0, (\gamma_1)_* 0)$, compute $L_1/L_2$ in terms of $|v|$.

$\mathcal{S}$ represents the complete history of a rigid rod. $\gamma_1$ is an observer at rest with respect to the rod and $\gamma_2$ is moving relative to the rod. Then the length of the rod measured by $\gamma_1$ is strictly greater than that for $\gamma_2$.
