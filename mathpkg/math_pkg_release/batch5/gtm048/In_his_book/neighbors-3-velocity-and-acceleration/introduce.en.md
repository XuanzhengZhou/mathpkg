Given a neighbor $W$ of an observer $\gamma$, its Fermi-Walker derivatives define the relative kinematics. The first derivative $F_{\gamma_*} W$ is the neighbor's 3-velocity, describing the rate of change of the spatial separation vector as measured by $\gamma$. The second derivative $F_{\gamma_*}^2 W$ is the neighbor's 3-acceleration, capturing the relative acceleration. Both quantities lie entirely in the local rest space $R_u$ (hence the "3" in their names, referring to the 3 spatial dimensions), as a consequence of Proposition 2.2.2 which guarantees that the Fermi-Walker connection preserves the spatial/temporal decomposition.
