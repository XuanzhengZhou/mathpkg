Let $W$ be a neighbor of an observer $\gamma$ in a reference frame $Q$, and let $F$ be the Fermi-Walker connection over $\gamma$.

The vector field $F_{\gamma_*} W$ is called the **neighbor's 3-velocity** relative to $\gamma$.

The vector field $F_{\gamma_*}^2 W \equiv F_{\gamma_*} (F_{\gamma_*} W)$ is called the **neighbor's 3-acceleration** relative to $\gamma$.

Both $(F_{\gamma_*} W)u$ and $(F_{\gamma_*}^2 W)u$ lie in the local rest space $R_u$ of $(\gamma u, \gamma_* u)$ for all $u \in \mathcal{E}$.
