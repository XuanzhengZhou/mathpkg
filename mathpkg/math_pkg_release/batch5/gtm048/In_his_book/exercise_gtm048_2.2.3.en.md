Suppose $\omega \in (0, 1)$. In Minkowski space $(\mathbb{R}^4, g, D)$, let $\gamma: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^4$ be defined by $\gamma u = (1 - \omega^2)^{-1/2}(\cos \omega u, \sin \omega u, 0, u)$.

(a) Show that $\gamma$ is an observer.

(b) For $\omega \ll 1$, use the first two terms of the binomial expansion of $\gamma u$ to find the approximate solution $V$ of the equations $F_{\gamma_*} V = 0$ and $g(\gamma_*, V) = 0$, where $F$ is the Fermi–Walker connection on $\gamma$.

(c) Use the isomorphism of Exercise 0.0.10 to compare $V(2\pi/\omega)$ with $V0$ and show that the two vectors differ slightly.

Physical interpretation: $\gamma$ is the complete history of an orbiting electron around the nucleus of a hydrogen atom. The axis of the electron (represented by $V$) precesses.
