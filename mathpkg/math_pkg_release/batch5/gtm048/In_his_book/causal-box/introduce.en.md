A causal box is a spacetime region modelled on a standard cylinder in Minkowski space, designed to provide a compact domain for applying Stokes' theorem in integral conservation laws. It consists of spacelike "top" and "bottom" faces ($B_1$, $B_2$), a timelike lateral boundary ($B_3$), and corner edges ($C_1$, $C_2$). The Newtonian analogue: $B_1$ is like an open solid ball in Euclidean 3-space at one instant, and the whole causal box $A$ is like the history of such a ball evolving at sub-light speed. Space-sections (defined as $D = B_2 \cup C_2$ with induced orientation) provide the spatial slices for integration, analogous to Cauchy surfaces.
