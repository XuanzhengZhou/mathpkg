Proposition 2.3.5 establishes the equivalence between the geometric condition of a reference frame being irrotational and the Frobenius integrability condition $\omega \wedge d\omega = 0$ for local synchronizability. This connects the kinematic description ($A_Q$ self-adjoint) with the causal structure (existence of local time hypersurfaces). In a sufficiently small neighborhood, an irrotational reference frame admits a time function $t$ such that $\omega = -h\, dt$, meaning the observers in $Q$ can synchronize their clocks locally. This result has an important practical consequence: high-precision astronomy on the rotating Earth is complicated precisely because the terrestrial reference frame is not irrotational, hence not locally synchronizable.
