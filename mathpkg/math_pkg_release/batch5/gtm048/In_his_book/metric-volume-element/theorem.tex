Let $(M, g, D)$ be an oriented spacetime with $\dim M = 4$. The **metric volume element** $\Omega$ is the unique nowhere-zero 4-form on $M$ such that for every positively oriented orthonormal basis $\{X_1, X_2, X_3, X_4\}$ of $M_x$,
$$\Omega(X_1, X_2, X_3, X_4) = \frac{1}{4!}.$$
If $\{X_1, \ldots, X_4\}$ is a positively oriented basis of vector fields on $U \subset M$ with dual basis $\{\omega^1, \ldots, \omega^4\}$ and $g_{ij} = g(X_i, X_j)$, let $g = |\det\{g_{ij}\}|$. Then in $U$:
$$\Omega = \sqrt{g}\; \omega^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \omega^4.$$
If $\{\omega^1, \ldots, \omega^4\}$ is an orthonormal basis of 1-forms (consistent with the orientation), then $\Omega = \omega^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \omega^4$.
