Let $\gamma$ be an observer on a spacetime $(M, g, D)$, and let $F$ be the Fermi-Walker connection over $\gamma$ with orthogonal projections $p$ and $q$ onto the local rest space $R_u$ and local time axis $T_u$. Then for all vector fields $X$ over $\gamma$:

(a) $F_{\gamma_*} \gamma_* = 0$.

(b) If $qX = 0$ (i.e., $X$ is purely spatial), then $F_{\gamma_*} X = p(D_{\gamma_*} X)$, and consequently $q(F_{\gamma_*} X) = 0$.

(c) If $pX = 0$ (i.e., $X$ is purely temporal), then $F_{\gamma_*} X = q(D_{\gamma_*} X)$, and consequently $p(F_{\gamma_*} X) = 0$.
