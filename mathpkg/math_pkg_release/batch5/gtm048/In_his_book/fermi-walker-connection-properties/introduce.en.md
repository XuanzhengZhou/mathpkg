Proposition 2.2.2 summarizes the main properties of the Fermi-Walker connection $F$ along an observer $\gamma$. Part (a) states that the observer's own 4-velocity is $F$-parallel, i.e., $\gamma$ itself is non-rotating by definition. Parts (b) and (c) show that $F$ respects the orthogonal decomposition into spatial and temporal components: $F$-differentiation of a purely spatial (resp. temporal) vector field yields another purely spatial (resp. temporal) vector field. These properties are essential for the interpretation of $F$-parallel transport as preserving the "same spatial direction" over proper time.
