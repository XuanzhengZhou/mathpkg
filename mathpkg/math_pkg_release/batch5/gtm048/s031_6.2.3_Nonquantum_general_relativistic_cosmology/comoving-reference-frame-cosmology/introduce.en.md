In cosmological models with a normal stress-energy tensor, there exists a unique comoving reference frame determined by the matter distribution. Integral curves of this reference frame model the centers of galaxies, which are approximately at rest relative to the cosmic expansion. The condition $\operatorname{div} Z(z) > 0$ at here-now corresponds to the empirical Hubble law indicating cosmic expansion.
