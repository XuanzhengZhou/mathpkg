A cosmological model is the formal structure used in general relativity to describe the universe as a whole. It consists of a maximal spacetime, a matter model, and a distinguished point designated as "here-now." The matter model's stress-energy tensor is required to be normal and obey the timelike convergence condition, reflecting the empirical observation that there is abundant matter in the universe and that gravity is attractive on average.
