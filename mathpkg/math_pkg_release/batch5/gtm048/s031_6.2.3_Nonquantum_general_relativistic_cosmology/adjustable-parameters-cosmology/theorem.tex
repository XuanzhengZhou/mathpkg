By \textbf{adjustable parameters} of a cosmological model, we mean the indeterminate constants that appear in the model. For example, in the Einstein--de Sitter model the numerical value of the cosmological time $u^4 z$ at here-now is not predetermined---it is an adjustable parameter, conventionally chosen as $10^{10}$ years. More sophisticated models (e.g., Robertson--Walker spacetimes of nonzero spatial curvature) possess additional adjustable parameters such as the curvature constant $A \in (0, \infty)$.
