Under the assumptions of Proposition 6.3.5a, let $\Omega$ be the area (solid angle) subtended on the celestial sphere $S_z$ by the galaxy at $x$, and let $A$ be the intrinsic area of the equal-redshift surface $S$ through $x$. Let $\tau = \tau(x, z)$ be the cosmological frequency ratio and $d = d(\tau)$ the distance from Proposition 6.3.4a. Then:
$$\frac{\Omega}{A} = \left(\frac{\tau}{d}\right)^2,$$
equivalently, the \textbf{area-distance} is
$$d_A = \frac{d}{\tau}.$$
