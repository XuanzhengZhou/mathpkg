Let $(\mathcal{M}, M, z)$ be the Einstein--de Sitter model. Suppose $x \in M$ and there exists a light signal from $x$ to $z = \text{here-now}$. Define
$$S = \{\, y \in M \mid \tau(y, z) = \tau(x, z) \text{ and there is a light signal from } y \text{ to } z \,\},$$
where $\tau$ is the cosmological frequency ratio. Then:
$$y \in S \iff \text{there exists a spatial rotation around } z,\; \psi: M \to M,\; \text{such that } \psi x = y.$$
