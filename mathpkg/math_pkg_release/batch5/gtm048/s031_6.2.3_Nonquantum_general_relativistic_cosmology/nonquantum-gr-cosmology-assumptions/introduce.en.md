The four formal assumptions (a)--(d) codify the foundational framework of nonquantum general relativistic cosmology. Assumption (a) requires the universe to be a relativistic model obeying Einstein's and Maxwell's equations; (b) demands maximality of the spacetime; (c) imposes physical reasonableness on the matter model through normality of the stress-energy tensor and the timelike convergence condition. Assumption (d) singles out a distinguished actual observer at here-now whose comoving frame matches the eigenvector reference frame of the stress-energy tensor and for whom the divergence of the frame vector field is positive (expansion).
