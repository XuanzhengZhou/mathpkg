By Proposition 6.3.5a, the set $S$ is invariant under spatial rotations about $z$, so $S$ is a sphere-like surface of constant cosmological time and redshift. The intrinsic area $A$ of such a surface is given by the $4\pi r^2$ formula where the effective radius is $r = (u^4 x)^{2/3} \delta(x, z)$. This follows from the metric of the simple cosmological spacetime restricted to the level surface $u^4 = \text{constant}$, which is (up to the scale factor) the Euclidean metric on $\mathbb{R}^3$. Hence
$$A = 4\pi r^2 = 4\pi (u^4 x)^{4/3} \delta^2(x, z).$$

The ratio $\Omega / A = 4\pi / A$ follows from the spherical symmetry: the total solid angle $4\pi$ is uniformly distributed over the surface $S$, and the fraction subtended by the galaxy is $\Omega / 4\pi$. Since the galaxy's intrinsic area element maps to the solid angle $\Omega$, we have $\Omega / 4\pi = (\text{area element}) / A$, so $\Omega / A = 4\pi / A$.
