Corollary 6.3.5 gives the intrinsic area $A$ of the redshift surface $S$ (the set of all galaxies at the same observed redshift) in the Einstein--de Sitter model. The formula $A = 4\pi (u^4 x)^{4/3} \delta^2(x, z)$ expresses the area in terms of the cosmological time coordinate $u^4 x$ and the separation function $\delta$. The corollary follows from the spatial rotation characterization of Proposition 6.3.5a: the spherical symmetry of $S$ about here-now reduces the area calculation to a standard $4\pi r^2$ form, where the effective radius is $(u^4 x)^{2/3} \delta(x, z)$.
