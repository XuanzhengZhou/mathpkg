Under the assumptions of Proposition 6.3.5a, the set $\Delta S_z = \chi^{-1}(\Delta S)$ of points on our celestial sphere at which we see the galaxy has a well-defined area $\Omega \in (0, 4\pi)$. By Proposition 6.3.5a and the symmetry it provides,
$$\frac{\Omega}{A} = \frac{4\pi}{A},$$
where $A$ is the intrinsic area of $S$, given by
$$A = 4\pi (u^4 x)^{4/3} \delta^2(x, z).$$
