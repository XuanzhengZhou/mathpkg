Let $M$ be a simple cosmological spacetime with Einstein tensor $G$. Suppose $G = T$, where $\hat{T}$ is a stress-energy tensor which is normal and obeys the timelike convergence condition. Suppose $\operatorname{div} \partial_4$ is somewhere nonnegative. Show the following generalizations:

(a) $\partial_4$ is canonically determined as the eigenvector reference frame of $\hat{T}$. The results of Section 6.0.2 on expansion, synchronizability, etc., of $\partial_4$ remain valid verbatim. $R$ satisfies $\dot{R} > 0$.

(b) $\mathcal{F}$ has a finite lower bound, which can be taken as $0$ without loss of generality.

Now replace assumption (b) of Proposition 6.2.7 by the requirement that $M$ be a Robertson--Walker spacetime of negative spatial curvature. Show from the Einstein field equation that one may take $\mathcal{F} = (0, \infty)$ and $R(u) = A(\cosh t - 1)$ where $u = A(\sinh t - t)$ and $A \in (0, \infty)$.

(c) Derive the analogous result for positive spatial curvature.

(d) For a Robertson--Walker spacetime with $\dot{R} \neq 0$, define the deceleration parameter
$$q = -\frac{\ddot{R} R}{\dot{R}^2}.$$
Show: in Einstein--de Sitter spacetime $q = 1/2$, interpretable as $q = |a|/|v|^2$ (relative acceleration/velocity of Proposition 2.3.7); in case (b) $0 < q < 1/2$; in case (c) $1/2 < q$.
