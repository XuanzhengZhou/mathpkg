Exercise 6.2.13 generalizes the results of Section 6.0 to the setting where the Einstein tensor $G$ of a simple cosmological spacetime equals a normal stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ obeying the timelike convergence condition (rather than being specifically a dust). Part (a) shows $\partial_4$ remains canonically determined and $\dot{R} > 0$. Parts (b)--(d) explore Robertson--Walker generalizations: negative and positive spatial curvature solutions, and the deceleration parameter $q$ which distinguishes between models ($q = 1/2$ for Einstein--de Sitter, $0 < q < 1/2$ for negative curvature, $1/2 < q$ for positive curvature). This exercise introduces the key observable parameter $q$ used to discriminate between cosmological models.
