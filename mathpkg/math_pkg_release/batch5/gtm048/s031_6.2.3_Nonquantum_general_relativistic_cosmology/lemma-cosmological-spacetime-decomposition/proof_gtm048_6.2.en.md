**Proof.** The Einstein tensor of a simple cosmological spacetime was computed in Proposition 1.4.4. The decomposition $G = \rho_0 \, du^4 \otimes du^4 + p_0(g + du^4 \otimes du^4)$ follows directly from that computation. Specifically, from Proposition 1.4.4, the (1,1) tensor field physically equivalent to $G$ is $(2/3)(u^4)^{-2}[-X_4 \otimes \omega^4 + \sum_{\rho=1}^3 X_\rho \otimes \omega^\rho]$. Converting to the $(0,2)$ form and expressing in terms of the basis $\{du^i\}$ yields the stated formulas. Part (c) follows from Lemma 4.3.3: $\operatorname{div} \partial_4 = 3(\dot{R}/R) \circ u^4$ by direct computation using the metric form $g = R^2(u^4)\sum_{\mu=1}^3 du^\mu \otimes du^\mu - du^4 \otimes du^4$.
