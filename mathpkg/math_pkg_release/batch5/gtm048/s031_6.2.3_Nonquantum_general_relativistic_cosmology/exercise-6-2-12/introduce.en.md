Exercise 6.2.12 asks the reader to establish three structural properties of composite stress-energy tensors. Part (a) verifies that the timelike convergence condition holds for the total stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ of a finite superposition satisfying the generality conditions of Proposition 6.2.2. Part (b) extends this to $\hat{T} + \hat{E}$ (including the electromagnetic contribution). Part (c) shows that if the total stress-energy tensor has the form of a dust ($\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z$), then all constituent matter components must be compatible with that dust structure: no photon gas components, perfect fluids must be dust aligned with $Z$, and particle flows must be massive and comoving with $Z$.
