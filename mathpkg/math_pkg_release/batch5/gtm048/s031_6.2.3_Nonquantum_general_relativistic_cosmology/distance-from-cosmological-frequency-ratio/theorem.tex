Let $(\mathcal{M}, M, z)$ be the Einstein--de Sitter model, with predicted Hubble time $t = \frac{3}{2} u^4 z$. Suppose a galaxy at $x \in M$ can send a photon to $z = \text{here-now}$. Let $d$ be the distance, within the level surface $u^4 = u^4 z$, between $z$ and the point $y$ where the inextendible $\partial_4$ integral curve through $x$ intersects the level surface. Let $\iota = \iota(x, z)$ be the cosmological frequency ratio of Proposition 6.0.8. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $d = 2t\bigl(1 - \iota^{-1/2}\bigr)$, with $d$ strictly increasing in $\iota$ and $\operatorname{range}(d) = (0, 2t)$.
\item[(b)] $u^4 z - u^4 x = \frac{2t}{3}\bigl(1 - \iota^{-3/2}\bigr)$.
\item[(c)] For $\iota - 1 \ll 1$ (i.e., $x$ close to $z$), both $d$ and $u^4 z - u^4 x$ are directly proportional to $\iota - 1$, with the proportionality constants obtainable from the Taylor series of (a) and (b).
\end{enumerate}
