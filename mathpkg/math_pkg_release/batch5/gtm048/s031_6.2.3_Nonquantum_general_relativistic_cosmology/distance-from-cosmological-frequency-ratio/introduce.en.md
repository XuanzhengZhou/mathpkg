Proposition 6.3.4a connects the observable cosmological frequency ratio $\iota$ (redshift) to the unobservable geometric distance $d$ and the cosmological time difference $u^4 z - u^4 x$ in the Einstein--de Sitter model. The formula $d = 2t(1 - \iota^{-1/2})$ shows that the distance is an increasing function of redshift, ranging from $0$ to $2t$. For nearby galaxies ($\iota \approx 1$), distance and time difference are both proportional to the redshift $x = \iota - 1$, recovering the Hubble law. This proposition enables the replacement of the unobservable distance $d$ with the measurable frequency ratio $\iota$ in further analyses.
