A stationary observer is one who belongs to a stationary reference frame. If $Z$ is a stationary reference frame with associated Killing vector field $X = fZ$, and $\gamma$ is an observer in $Z$, then $\gamma_* = Z \circ \gamma = (1/f \circ \gamma)(X \circ \gamma)$. For any two events $u, \hat{u}$ on the observer's worldline, a local isometry coming from the flow of $X$ maps a neighborhood of $\gamma u$ to a neighborhood of $\gamma \hat{u}$, formalizing the idea that the observer experiences no change in local geometry over time.
