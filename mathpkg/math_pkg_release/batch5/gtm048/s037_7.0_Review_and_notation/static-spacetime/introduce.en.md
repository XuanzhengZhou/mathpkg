A static spacetime is one that admits a static reference frame---a family of observers who experience both time independence and no local rotation of the geometry. While a stationary spacetime corresponds to a gravitational field generated by a time-independent source, the additional requirement of being static means the source also does not rotate. The existence of a static reference frame is an extreme idealization, but it provides the appropriate setting for models of non-rotating stars and the exterior Schwarzschild solution.
