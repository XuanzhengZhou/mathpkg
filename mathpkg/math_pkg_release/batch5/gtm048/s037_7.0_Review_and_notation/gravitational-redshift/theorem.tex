Let $Z$ be an absolute stationary reference frame on $M$. Suppose $x, y \in M$ and there exists at least one freely falling photon $\lambda$ such that $\lambda a = x$ and $\lambda b = y$. Then the frequency ratio $\tau = g(\lambda_*a, Z\lambda a)/g(\lambda_*b, Z\lambda b)$ is uniquely determined by $(x, y)$. The quantity $\tau - 1$ is defined as the \textbf{gravitational redshift} for $x$ observed at $y$.
