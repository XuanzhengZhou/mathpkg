On the unit 2-sphere $\mathcal{S}^2$ with its standard metric $h$ and volume form $\zeta$, there is a distinguished family of Killing vector fields parametrized by points of the sphere. For each point $x \in \mathcal{S}^2$, the generator of rotations $K_x$ is the unique Killing field vanishing at $x$, whose dual 1-form has exterior derivative aligned with the orientation, and whose squared norm attains a maximum value of 1. Up to sign, $K_x$ corresponds to rotations of $\mathbb{R}^3$ about the axis determined by $x$ and the origin. This family provides the standard model for infinitesimal rotational symmetries on the sphere and is used throughout the analysis of spherical symmetry in general relativity.
