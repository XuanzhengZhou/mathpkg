The pressure $p$ of a quasi-gas is the companion function to the energy density $\rho$ in the perfect fluid stress-energy tensor decomposition $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z + p(\hat{g} + Z \otimes Z)$. Both $\rho$ and $p$ are smooth functions on the spacetime $M$, derived from the quasi-gas abstraction of a collection of particle flows. The pressure satisfies the physical inequality $\rho \geq 3p \geq 0$, and its magnitude is related to the average random 3-velocity of the constituent particles, providing a bridge between the continuum description and kinetic theory.
