Proposition 3.15.3 analyzes the matter equation $\operatorname{div} \hat{T} = 0$ for a perfect fluid stress-energy tensor of the form $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z + p(\hat{g} + Z \otimes Z)$. The divergence-free condition, which encodes local energy-momentum conservation in general relativity, decomposes into two coupled equations: a continuity-like equation $\operatorname{div}(\rho Z) = -p \operatorname{div} Z$ relating the energy flux to the pressure work through volume expansion, and a geodesic-deviation equation $D_Z Z = -(\rho + p)^{-1}\{(Zp)Z + \operatorname{grad} p\}$ that governs the acceleration of the reference frame $Z$ in terms of pressure gradients.
