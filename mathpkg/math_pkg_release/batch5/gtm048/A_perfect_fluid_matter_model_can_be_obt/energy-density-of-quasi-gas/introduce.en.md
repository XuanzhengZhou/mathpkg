The energy density $\rho$ of a quasi-gas is the physical quantity that an instantaneous observer $(z, Z_z)$ measures as the local energy concentration of the stress-energy tensor. Starting from the canonical perfect fluid representation $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z + p(\hat{g} + Z \otimes Z)$, the function $\rho$ is recovered by evaluating the physically equivalent $(0,2)$-tensor $T$ on the observer's 4-velocity: $\rho(z) = T(Z_z, Z_z)$. The smoothness of $\rho$ follows from the eigenfunction property $\hat{T}Z = -\rho Z$.
