Proposition 3.15.1 provides the canonical representation of the stress-energy tensor for a perfect fluid matter model, derived from a quasi-gas whose stress-energy is everywhere spatially isotropic. The tensor takes the form $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z + p(\hat{g} + Z \otimes Z)$, where $\rho$ is the energy density and $p$ is the pressure, both smooth functions satisfying the physical inequalities $\rho > 0$ and $\rho \geq 3p \geq 0$. The proposition further characterizes the physically significant case of zero rest-mass, which occurs precisely when $\rho = 3p$ everywhere.
