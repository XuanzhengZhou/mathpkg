The notion of spatial isotropy for a stress-energy tensor captures the idea that the matter model looks the same in all spatial directions from the perspective of a particular instantaneous observer. A stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ on a spacetime $M$ is called spatially isotropic at a point $z \in M$ precisely when there exists some instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ for whom the tensor $\hat{T}$ exhibits spatial isotropy in the sense defined in Section 2.1.7. This definition serves as the foundational geometric condition for modeling perfect fluids in general relativity.
