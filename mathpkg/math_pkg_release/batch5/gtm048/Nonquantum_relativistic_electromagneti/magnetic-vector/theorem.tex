Let $F$ be an electromagnetic field on spacetime $M$, and let $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer. Let $\Omega$ be the metric volume element on $M$. The \text{magnetic vector} $B$ that $(z, Z)$ measures for $F$ is the unique vector $B \in Z^\perp$ satisfying
$$4! \, \Omega(X, Y, B, Z) = F(X, Y) \quad \forall X, Y \in Z^\perp.$$
Equivalently, $B$ is obtained from $F$ via Hodge duality restricted to the observer's rest space $Z^\perp$.
