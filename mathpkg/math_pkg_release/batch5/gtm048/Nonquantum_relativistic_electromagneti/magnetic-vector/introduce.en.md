For an electromagnetic field $F$ and an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$, the magnetic vector $B$ is the unique vector in the observer's rest space $Z^\perp$ such that $4! \Omega(X, Y, B, Z) = F(X, Y)$ for all $X, Y \in Z^\perp$. Here $\Omega$ is the metric volume element; the construction uses Hodge duality (cf. Bishop–Goldberg) to extract the magnetic part of $F$ relative to the observer's 4-velocity $Z$.
