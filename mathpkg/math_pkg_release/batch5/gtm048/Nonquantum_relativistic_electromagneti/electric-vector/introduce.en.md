Given an electromagnetic field $F$ on $M$ and an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$, the electric vector $E$ that $(z, Z)$ measures is defined as $E = \tilde{F}Z$. Since $F$ is antisymmetric, $\tilde{F}Z$ lies in $Z^\perp$, the observer's rest space. This definition provides the relativistic formulation of the electric field, generalizing the Newtonian notion of measuring electric force with a test charge.
