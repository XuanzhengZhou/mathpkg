An electromagnetic field on spacetime $M$ is an antisymmetric $(0,2)$ tensor field $F$ on $M$, i.e., a 2-form. This definition (3.4.2) unifies the prerelativistic electric and magnetic fields into a single geometric object on spacetime. The antisymmetry $F(X,Y) = -F(Y,X)$ is essential; together with an instantaneous observer, $F$ determines both the measured electric vector and the measured magnetic vector.
