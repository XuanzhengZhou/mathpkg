Suppose any instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ actually measures electric charge per unit 3-volume of $Z^\perp$. The result is supposed to be $-g(Z, J)$, where $J$ is a charge-current density on $M$. This prescription can be regarded both as an operational definition of $J$ and as a physical law, analogous to the stress-energy measurement prescription of Section 3.3.
