For a particle flow $(m, e, P, \eta)$, the charge-current density $J$ is defined as $J = e\eta$, where $e$ is the charge and $\eta$ is the number-flux vector field of the particle flow. This generalizes the stress-energy tensor definition of a particle flow (Definition 3.3.5) to the electromagnetic setting, expressing the charge-current density directly from the particle flow data.
