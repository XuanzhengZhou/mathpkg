The proof is left as Exercise 8.2.1(b) to the reader. One can argue that a connected Lie group is generated by any neighborhood of the identity, which together with local compactness implies second countability. Then by Exercise 8.2.1(a), paracompactness follows, which is equivalent to the existence of a Riemannian metric. Alternatively, one can construct a left-invariant Riemannian metric directly by choosing an inner product on the Lie algebra and translating it via left multiplication.
