Let $F$ be an electromagnetic field on $M$, $\hat{E}$ its stress-energy tensor, and $E$ (respectively, $B$) the electric (respectively, magnetic) field measured by an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$. Suppose $F \neq 0$. Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $g(E, B) = 0$ iff $F \wedge F = 0$.
\item[(b)] $g(E, E) = g(B, B)$ iff $F^{ij}F_{ij} = 0$.
\item[(c)] $\hat{T} = \hat{E}_z$ is diagonalizable iff $\hat{T}$ obeys the dominant energy condition iff either $g(E, B) \neq 0$ or $g(E, E) \neq g(B, B)$.
\item[(d)] $\hat{T}$ is not normal but obeys the timelike convergence condition and the strong energy condition.
\end{itemize}
