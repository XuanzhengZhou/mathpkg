The proof is left as Exercise 8.1.8(c) to the reader. One computes the matrix representation of $\hat{T}$ in a basis adapted to the instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ and analyzes its characteristic polynomial. The diagonalizability condition reduces to the discriminant of the characteristic polynomial being nonzero, which is equivalent to either $g(E, B) \neq 0$ or $g(E, E) \neq g(B, B)$. The dominant energy condition for $\hat{T}$ is then shown to be equivalent to the same algebraic condition.
