The proof is left as Exercise 8.1.9(a) to the reader. One proves existence by projecting $S$ onto the decomposition $T_0^2(M_z) \cong \mathbb{R} \oplus R \oplus R \oplus (R \otimes_s R)_0 \oplus (R \otimes_a R) \oplus \mathbb{R}$, where the first summand corresponds to $a Z \otimes Z$, the next two to $X$ and $Y$, the fourth to the trace-free symmetric part $\sigma$, the fifth to the skew-symmetric part $\omega$, and the last to the trace term $\frac{1}{2}\theta \hat{h}$. Uniqueness follows because this is a direct sum decomposition.
