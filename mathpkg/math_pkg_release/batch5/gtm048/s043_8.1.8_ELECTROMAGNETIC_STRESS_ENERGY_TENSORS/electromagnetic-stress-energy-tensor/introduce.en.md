The electromagnetic stress-energy tensor $\hat{E}$ encodes the energy, momentum, and stress carried by an electromagnetic field $F$ on a Lorentzian manifold. For a given instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$, the tensor $\hat{T} = \hat{E}_z$ decomposes into electric and magnetic field components $E$ and $B$ measured in the observer's rest frame. This concept is central to the Einstein-Maxwell system, where the stress-energy of the electromagnetic field acts as the source of spacetime curvature via Einstein's field equation.
