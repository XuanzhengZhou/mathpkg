Let $K \subset \mathcal{S}_Z$ be the smallest integration region such that an observed photon comes from a particular astronomical object only if the corresponding spatial direction $U$ is in $K$. Suppose the photon distribution $F_Z$ vanishes outside $K \times (0, \infty)$. Then the apparent luminosity $\ell$ of the object for $(z, Z)$ is

$$\ell = \int_{K \times (0, \infty)} eF_z \pi_Z = u,$$

where $u$ is the total energy density contributed by photons from that object.
