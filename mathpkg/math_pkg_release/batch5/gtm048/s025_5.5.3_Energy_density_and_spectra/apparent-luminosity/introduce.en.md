Apparent luminosity $\ell$ is the energy per unit spatial volume that an instantaneous observer $(z,Z)$ receives from a specific astronomical object. It is computed by integrating the photon distribution function restricted to the solid angle $K$ subtended by the object, weighted by photon energy. Unlike absolute luminosity, apparent luminosity depends on the observer's position and is simply the total energy density $u$ contributed by that object's photons.
