The Planck function $P(u) = 2h^{-3}[(\exp u)-1]^{-1}$ is a fundamental function in quantum statistical mechanics that describes the energy distribution of photons in thermal equilibrium. It depends on Planck's constant $h$ and takes a dimensionless argument $u = e/kT$, where $e$ is photon energy, $k$ is Boltzmann's constant, and $T$ is temperature. When multiplied by $e^3$, it gives the specific intensity of blackbody radiation.
