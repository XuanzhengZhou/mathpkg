The energy density functional $u[a,b]$ quantifies how much electromagnetic energy (per unit spatial volume) is carried by photons whose measured energies fall within a specified interval $[a,b]$. It is obtained by integrating the photon distribution function $F_z$ weighted by the photon energy $e$ over the celestial sphere $\mathcal{S}_Z$ and the energy interval. The total energy density $u$ is the limit as the energy interval expands to cover all positive energies, and it may be finite or infinite depending on the distribution.
