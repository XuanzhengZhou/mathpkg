For each interval $[a, b] \subset (0, \infty)$, define a nonnegative number $u[a, b]$ by

$$u[a, b] = \int_{\mathcal{S}_Z \times [a, b]} eF_z \pi_z = \int_{\mathcal{S}_Z} \zeta \int_a^b e^3F_z \, d\epsilon,$$

where the second equality comes from Fubini's theorem. Then $u[a, b]$ is interpreted as the measured energy density of those photons described by $F_z$ that have measured energies in the range $[a, b]$. Since $F_z$ is nonnegative, the double limit $\lim_{a \to 0, b \to \infty} u[a, b]$ exists and is either $\infty$ or a nonnegative number; call this limit $u$. $u$ is then interpreted as the total energy density $(z, Z)$ measures for the photons modelled by $F_z$.
