The apparent size of an astronomical object is the solid angle it subtends on the celestial sphere $\mathcal{S}_Z$ of an instantaneous observer $(z,Z)$. It is the measure of the smallest region $K$ of spatial directions from which photons from that object can arrive. For distant stars, $K$ is so small that it cannot be distinguished from a point by the human eye.
