We may assume $f$ is nonnegative (the nonpositive case follows by considering $-f$). Let $\{\mathcal{K}_i\}$ be an exhaustive sequence. The sequence of integrals $\int_{\mathcal{K}_i} fA$ is non-decreasing because $\mathcal{K}_i \subset \mathcal{K}_{i+1}$ and $f \geq 0$. A non-decreasing sequence of extended real numbers always has a limit (finite or $+\infty$). It remains to show that this limit is independent of the choice of exhaustive sequence, which holds for sign-keeping functions by standard comparison arguments.
