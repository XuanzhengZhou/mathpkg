Proposition 5.6.3 guarantees that the improper integral of a continuous sign-keeping function (everywhere nonnegative or everywhere nonpositive) over a manifold with a volume element always exists as an extended real number. This is a fundamental result that avoids the need to check independence of the choice of exhaustive sequence for such functions, and it generalizes the familiar fact that integrals of nonnegative measurable functions always exist in Lebesgue theory.
