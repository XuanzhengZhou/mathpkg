For a perfect fluid obeying the Einstein field equation, the mean relative-acceleration $\alpha$ of any geodesic reference frame $Q$ is strictly negative, bounded above by $-\frac{1}{6}(\rho + 3p) - \frac{1}{3}E(Q,Q)$. This provides the precise sense in which gravity "attracts": freely falling observers experience a negative mean relative-acceleration, meaning they tend to converge rather than diverge. The bound is sharp — equality holds exactly when $Q$ coincides with the comoving frame $Z$ — and the presence of pressure $p$ amplifies the attractive effect, a purely general-relativistic phenomenon with no Newtonian counterpart.
