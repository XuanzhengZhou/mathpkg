Let $(M, \mathbf{M}, F)$ be a perfect fluid spacetime obeying the Einstein field equation, with energy density $\rho$, pressure $p$, and comoving reference frame $Z$, satisfying $\rho > 0$ and $\rho \geq 3p \geq 0$. Let $\hat{G} = (\rho + p)Z \otimes Z + p\hat{g} + \hat{E}$ be the $(2,0)$ stress-energy tensor, where $\hat{E}$ is the electromagnetic contribution with $\operatorname{trace} \hat{E} = 0$.

Let $Q$ be any geodesic reference frame defined on an open subset $U \subset M$, and let $\alpha: U \to \mathbb{R}$ be the mean relative-acceleration of $Q$. Then

$$
\alpha \leq -\frac{1}{6}(\rho + 3p) - \frac{1}{3}E(Q, Q) < 0.
$$

Moreover, at $z \in U$, the first inequality becomes an equality if and only if $Q_z = Z_z$.
