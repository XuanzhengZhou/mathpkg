Let $\hat{T}$ be a stress-energy tensor on a spacetime $M$. Define $\hat{W} = \hat{T} - \frac{1}{2}(\operatorname{trace} \hat{T})\hat{g}$, where $\hat{g}$ is the $(2,0)$-tensor field physically equivalent to $g$; $\hat{W}$ is then a symmetric $(2,0)$-tensor field. We say $\hat{T}$ obeys the \emph{timelike convergence condition} iff $\hat{W}(\omega, \omega) > 0$ for all timelike $1$-forms $\omega$.

\textbf{(a)} Show that the stress-energy tensor of a quasigas (Example 3.13.4) obeys the timelike convergence condition.

\textbf{(b)} Let $(M, \mathbf{M}, F)$ be a relativistic model which obeys the Einstein field equation, and let $\hat{T}$ obey the timelike convergence condition. Show that $\operatorname{Ric}(Z, Z) > 0$ for all timelike vector fields $Z$.

\textbf{(c)} Under the assumptions of (b), let $Z$ be a geodesic irrotational reference frame on $M$. Show that no observer in $Z$ can have all of $\mathbb{R}$ as domain.

\textit{Remark.} Part (c) accounts for the nomenclature "timelike convergence": from a geometric standpoint, it holds because the neighboring geodesics of each timelike geodesic $\gamma$ tangent to $Z$ must converge on $\gamma$ sooner or later.
