Define a tensor field $T: U \to T_4^0 U$ as follows. For all $x \in U$ and for all $X_1, X_2, X_3, X_4 \in U_x$:
$$T(X_1, X_2, X_3, X_4) = \frac{1}{4}\Big\{[j(X_1, X_3)(P^* h)(X_2, X_4) - (1 \leftrightarrow 2)] - [3 \leftrightarrow 4]\Big\},$$
where $(1 \leftrightarrow 2)$ and $[3 \leftrightarrow 4]$ denote interchange of indices. For example, if $F \in T_2^0(U_x)$, then $F(X_1, X_2) - (1 \leftrightarrow 2) \equiv F(X_1, X_2) - F(X_2, X_1)$.

Then $T$ has all the symmetry properties of a curvature-like $(4,0)$-tensor as stated in Section 1.0.2a-c.
