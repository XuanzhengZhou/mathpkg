Corollary 7.3.5 characterizes Killing vector fields on the normal Schwarzschild region $N$. It states that any future-pointing Killing field on $N$ must be a positive constant multiple of the coordinate vector field $\partial/\partial t$, establishing the uniqueness of the time-translation symmetry on $N$. Furthermore, for the canonical choice $X = \partial/\partial t$, the normalization $g(X, X) \to -1$ as $r \to \infty$ holds for all observers escaping to infinity, confirming that $t$ asymptotically approaches proper time at large distances. This lemma is crucial for establishing the static reference frame on $N$ in Corollary 7.3.6.
