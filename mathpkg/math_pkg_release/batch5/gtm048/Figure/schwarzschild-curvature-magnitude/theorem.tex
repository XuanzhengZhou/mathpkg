Let $f: U \to [0,\infty)$ be the curvature magnitude function on a Schwarzschild spacetime $U = N \cup B$, defined as the scalar measuring the overall magnitude of the gravitational field (observable locally via relative acceleration measurements, Proposition 2.3.3). Then

$$f = \frac{144\mu^2}{r^6},$$

where $\mu$ is the mass parameter and $r: U \to (0,\infty)$ is the radial coordinate.

In particular, a particle or observer $\gamma: (a,b) \to U$ escapes to infinity iff $\lim_{u \to b} f \circ \gamma(u) = 0$, which is equivalent to $\lim_{u \to b} r \circ \gamma(u) = \infty$.
