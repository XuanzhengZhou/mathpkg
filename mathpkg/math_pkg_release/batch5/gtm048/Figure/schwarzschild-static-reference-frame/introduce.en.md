Corollary 7.3.6 constructs a canonical static reference frame on the normal Schwarzschild region $N$ using the normalized vector field $Z = (1 - 2\mu/r)^{-1/2}(\partial/\partial t)$. This frame is static (its integral curves are orthogonal to the spatial slices) and absolute (it is determined purely by the geometry). The time function $\hat{t}$ for $Z$ is uniquely characterized by the conditions $\hat{Z}\hat{t} \to 1$ as $r \to \infty$ and $\hat{d}\hat{t} = dt|_N$, meaning $t$ on $N$ is geometrically singled out as proper time at infinity. Observers at rest in this frame are precisely those with constant radial coordinate $r$. This provides the geometric interpretation that $t$ is a time function and $r$ is a radial coordinate on $N$, but not on the black hole region $B$.
