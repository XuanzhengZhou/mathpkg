The energy of a photon measured by an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ is defined as $e = -g(Y, Z) > 0$, where $Y$ is the photon's energy-momentum vector and $Z$ is the observer's time axis. The existence of a distinguished local time axis gives rise to a decomposition $Y = e(Z + U)$, where $U \in Z^\perp$ is the spatial direction measured by the observer.
