Electromagnetic radiation is classified into frequency ranges measured by an instantaneous observer: radio waves ($f < 10^9$ s$^{-1}$), microwaves through infrared ($10^9$ to $3 	imes 10^{14}$ s$^{-1}$), visible light ($3 	imes 10^{14}$ to $10^{15}$ s$^{-1}$), ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays at successively higher frequencies. Red light has a smaller frequency and larger wavelength than blue or violet light.
