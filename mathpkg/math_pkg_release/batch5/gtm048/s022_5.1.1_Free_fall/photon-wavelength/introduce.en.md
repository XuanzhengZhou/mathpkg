The wavelength of a photon measured by an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ is defined as the reciprocal of the frequency: $\lambda = f^{-1}$. The relation $f\lambda = 1$ (equal to the speed of light in geometric units) corresponds to the usual relation between frequency, wavelength, and wave speed. This definition is motivated by the wave model for light.
