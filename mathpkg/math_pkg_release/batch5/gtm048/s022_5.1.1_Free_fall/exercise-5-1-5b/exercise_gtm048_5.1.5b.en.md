Let $M$ be Minkowski space, $\gamma$ be an inextendible geodesic observer, and $x$ a point not on the world line of $\gamma$. Show there is exactly one freely falling photon $\lambda: [0, 1] ightarrow M$ from $x$ to a point $\lambda 1$ on the world line of $\gamma$. Moreover, $u^4$ is smaller at emission than at reception.
