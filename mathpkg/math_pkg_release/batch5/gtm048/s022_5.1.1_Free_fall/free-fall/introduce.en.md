A freely falling photon is a photon that travels along a geodesic in spacetime. Since photons have zero electric charge, they do not interact directly with electromagnetic fields; other matter can influence a photon only by destroying it. Thus each photon of interest in general relativity is subject only to gravity and moves along a lightlike geodesic.
