Photon emission refers to the creation of a photon within spacetime at a specific past endpoint. If a photon curve $\lambda: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow M$ has a past endpoint $x \in M$, one says the photon is emitted or sent out at $x$. The photon may be emitted by an instantaneous observer, a star, or any physical process at $x$.
