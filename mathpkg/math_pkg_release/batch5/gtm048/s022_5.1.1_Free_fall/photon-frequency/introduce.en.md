The frequency of a photon measured by an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ is defined as $f = e/h$, where $e$ is the measured energy and $h$ is Planck's constant. Historically, Einstein postulated the relation $e = hf$ in 1905, but the present treatment reverses the order by defining frequency in terms of energy. The terms "frequency" and "wavelength" are motivated by alternate wave models for light.
