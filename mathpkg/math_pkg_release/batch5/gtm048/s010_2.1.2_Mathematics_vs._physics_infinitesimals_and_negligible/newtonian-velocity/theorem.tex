Let $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer on a spacetime $(M, g)$, so that $Z \in M_z$ is a future-pointing unit timelike vector, $Z^\perp \subset M_z$ is the orthogonal complement, and $M_z = Z^\perp \oplus \operatorname{span} Z$.

Let $X \in M_z$ be a future-pointing causal vector. Then there exists a unique orthogonal decomposition
$$X = eZ + p, \qquad e \in \mathbb{R}, \quad p \in Z^\perp.$$
Moreover, $e = -g(X, Z) > 0$.

The \textbf{Newtonian velocity} of $X$ (or of an observer $\gamma$ with $\gamma_* u = X$) observed by $(z, Z)$ is defined to be
$$\frac{p}{e}.$$
