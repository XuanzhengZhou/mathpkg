The Newtonian velocity is the spatial component of a causal tangent vector as measured by an instantaneous observer, normalized by the time component. It arises from the orthogonal decomposition $X = eZ + p$ of a future-pointing causal vector $X$ relative to the observer's time axis $Z$. The quotient $p/e$ generalizes the Newtonian notion of velocity (displacement divided by time) to the Lorentzian setting, where $e$ plays the role of the relativistic energy- or time-component. The concept is fundamental for interpreting kinematic quantities in general relativity in a way that reduces to familiar Newtonian mechanics in the appropriate limit.
