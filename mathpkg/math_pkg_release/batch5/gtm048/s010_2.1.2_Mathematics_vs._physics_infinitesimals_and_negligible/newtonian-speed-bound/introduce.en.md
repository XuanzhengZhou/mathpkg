The Newtonian speed bound is a fundamental result that connects the causal character of a tangent vector (timelike, lightlike, or spacelike) to its Newtonian speed as measured by an instantaneous observer. The bound $|p|/e \le 1$ follows directly from the causal condition $g(X,X) \le 0$ and the orthogonal decomposition $X = eZ + p$. The strict inequality $|p|/e < 1$ characterizes timelike vectors, establishing that the speed of light ($=1$ in geometric units) is the universal speed limit, consistent with special relativity.
