Let $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer on a spacetime $(M, g)$. Let $p: M_z \to Z^\perp$ denote the orthogonal projection onto the local rest space. Define the positive-semidefinite bilinear form $h$ on $M_z$ by
$$h(X, Y) = g(pX, pY), \qquad X, Y \in M_z.$$
Equivalently, $h$ is the pullback of the positive-definite metric $g|_{Z^\perp}$ via the projection $p$.

For tangent vectors $X, Y \in M_z$, the \textbf{Newtonian angle} $\theta$ between $X$ and $Y$ as observed by $(z, Z)$ is defined by
$$\cos \theta = \frac{h(X, Y)}{[h(X, X)\,h(Y, Y)]^{1/2}},$$
provided $pX \neq 0$ and $pY \neq 0$.

\textbf{Remark.} The angle $\theta$ depends on the observer $Z$, not merely on $X$ and $Y$. When $X$ and $Y$ are lightlike, this dependence on $Z$ corresponds to the astronomical phenomenon of \textit{aberration}.
