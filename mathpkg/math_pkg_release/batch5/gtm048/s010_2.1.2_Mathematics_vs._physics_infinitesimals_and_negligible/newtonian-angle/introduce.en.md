The Newtonian angle is the angle between two tangent vectors in spacetime as measured by an instantaneous observer. It is defined by first projecting the vectors onto the observer's local rest space $Z^\perp$ (using the Riemannian metric $g|_{Z^\perp}$, which is Euclidean 3-space) and then taking the ordinary Euclidean angle between the projections. The resulting angle depends on the observer's 4-velocity $Z$, not just on the vectors $X$ and $Y$; for lightlike vectors this observer-dependence is precisely the relativistic aberration of light. This concept is fundamental for translating geometric spacetime relations into observable quantities.
