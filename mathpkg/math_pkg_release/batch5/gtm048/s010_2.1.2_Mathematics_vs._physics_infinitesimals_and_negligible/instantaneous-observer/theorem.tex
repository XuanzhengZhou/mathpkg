Let $(M, g)$ be a spacetime and $z \in M$. An \textbf{instantaneous observer} at $z$ is a pair $(z, Z)$ where $Z \in M_z$ is a future-pointing unit timelike vector, i.e., $g(Z, Z) = -1$ and $Z$ is future-directed.

The \textbf{local time axis} of $(z, Z)$ is the 1-dimensional timelike subspace $\operatorname{span} Z \subset M_z$.

The \textbf{local rest space} of $(z, Z)$ is the orthogonal complement $Z^\perp \subset M_z$ with respect to $g$. Since $Z$ is timelike, $g|_{Z^\perp}$ is positive definite, making $Z^\perp$ a Euclidean 3-space.

The tangent space decomposes as the orthogonal direct sum
$$M_z = Z^\perp \oplus \operatorname{span} Z.$$
