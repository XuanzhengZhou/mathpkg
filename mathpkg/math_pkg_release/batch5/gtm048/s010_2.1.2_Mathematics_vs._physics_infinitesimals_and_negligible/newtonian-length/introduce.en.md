The Newtonian length is the spatial length of a tangent vector as measured by an instantaneous observer. It is defined as the Euclidean norm of the vector's projection onto the observer's local rest space $Z^\perp$, using the induced Riemannian metric $h(X,Y) = g(pX, pY)$. Together with the Newtonian angle, it provides a complete Euclidean geometric structure on the observer's rest space, allowing the observer to interpret spacetime vectors in familiar Newtonian terms of lengths and angles.
