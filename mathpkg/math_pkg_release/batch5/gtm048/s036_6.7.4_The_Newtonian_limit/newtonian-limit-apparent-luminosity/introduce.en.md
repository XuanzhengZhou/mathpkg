In the Einstein--de Sitter cosmological model, the Newtonian limit describes the regime where an instantaneous comoving observer is very near the source $\gamma$. In this limit, the stress-energy tensor of a particle flow $(P,\eta)$ takes an explicit form involving the absolute luminosity $L$, the temperature $T$, and the spatial distance $d$. From this, the apparent luminosity $\ell = T(\partial_4 x, \partial_4 x)$ measured by a moving instantaneous observer $(x, \partial_4 x)$ on $\mathcal{U}$ is obtained as $\ell = (4\pi)^{-1}(L \circ T^3) \times [(u^4)^{-1/3} T]^4 d^{-2}$. This formula connects the intrinsic properties of the source (absolute luminosity, temperature) with the observable apparent luminosity through the inverse-square law modified by the Einstein--de Sitter geometry.
