In the Newtonian limit of the Einstein--de Sitter model, for the particle flow $(P,\eta)$, the stress-energy tensor is
$$\hat{T} = \eta P \otimes P = (4\pi)^{-1}(L \circ T^{3}) T^{4} (u^{4})^{-4/3} d \cdot [(\partial_{4} + \partial) \otimes (\partial_{4} + \partial)],$$
where $d = (u^{4})^{2/3} r$ is the spatial distance. Consequently, the energy density (apparent luminosity) is
$$\ell = T(\partial_{4} x, \partial_{4} x) = (4\pi)^{-1}(L \circ T^{3}) \times [(u^{4})^{-1/3} T]^{4} \, d^{-2},$$
measured by any moving instantaneous observer $(x, \partial_{4} x)$ on $\mathcal{U}$.
