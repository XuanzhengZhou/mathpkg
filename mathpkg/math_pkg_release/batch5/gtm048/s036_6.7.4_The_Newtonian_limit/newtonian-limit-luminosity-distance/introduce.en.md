In the Newtonian limit of the Einstein-de Sitter model, the relativistic luminosity distance formula reduces to the familiar inverse-square law. This shows how general relativity recovers Newtonian physics in the appropriate limit, with relativistic corrections becoming negligible when the observer is very near the source and spacetime curvature effects are small.
