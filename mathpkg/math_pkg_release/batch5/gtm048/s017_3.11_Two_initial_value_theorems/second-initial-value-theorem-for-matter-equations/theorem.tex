\textbf{Theorem 3.11.2 (Second Initial Value Theorem for Matter Equations).}
Let $F$ be a given electromagnetic field on a spacetime $M$, and let $(m, e)$ be a particle type. Suppose also given an imbedded $3$-dimensional spacelike submanifold $\phi: W \to M$, a $C^\infty$ vector field $P_0$ over $\phi$ such that $P_0 y$ is future-pointing for all $y \in W$ and $g(P_0, P_0) = -m^2$, and a positive $C^\infty$ function $\eta_0$ over $\phi$. Fix $w \in \phi W$ and let $U$ be an open neighborhood of $M$ containing $w$.

For $U$ sufficiently small, there exists a unique $C^\infty$ vector field $P$ on $U$ and a unique $C^\infty$ function $\eta: U \to (0, \infty)$ such that:
\begin{itemize}
  \item[(a)] $P \circ \phi = P_0$ and $\eta \circ \phi = \eta_0$;
  \item[(b)] $M = (m, e, P, \eta)$ is a particle flow on $(U, g|_U)$;
  \item[(c)] $(U, M, F|_U)$ obeys the simple matter equations.
\end{itemize}
