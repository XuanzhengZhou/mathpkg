The First Initial Value Theorem for Maxwell's Equations guarantees that the electromagnetic field on a spacetime can be uniquely recovered from its values on a spacelike hypersurface, provided the charge-current density is prescribed and satisfies the continuity equation. The necessary and sufficient initial conditions, expressed in terms of the pullback of the field 2-form, encode the homogeneous and inhomogeneous Maxwell constraints on the initial surface. The proof uses the exponential map of the normal bundle to construct a foliation near the surface, then reformulates the problem as a symmetric hyperbolic system of PDEs to which the theory of Friedrichs and Lax applies.
