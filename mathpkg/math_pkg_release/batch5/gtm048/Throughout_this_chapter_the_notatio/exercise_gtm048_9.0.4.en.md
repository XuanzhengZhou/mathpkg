Show that Newton's law $\vec{F} = m\ddot{r}$ (Section 0.1.6) is invariant under the Galilean velocity transformations
$$\vec{x} \rightarrow \vec{x} + \vec{v} t, \quad \vec{F} \rightarrow \vec{F}, \quad t \rightarrow t, \quad \vec{r} \rightarrow \vec{r} + \vec{v} t, \quad m \rightarrow m; \quad \vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^3.$$
