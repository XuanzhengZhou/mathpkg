Let $N_0 = A \cup_{i=1}^{3} B_i \cup_{j=1}^{2} C_j$ be the standard cylinder (Section 3.0.1), $D \subset \mathbb{R}^4$ be a space-section, $C \subset B_3$ be topologically trivial. In this exercise you will not need to give proofs, just to draw some $\mathbb{R}^3$ and $\mathbb{R}^4$ pictures. "Show":

(a) $B_1$ is the open unit ball $\subset \mathbb{R}^3$ at $t = -1$.

(b) $C_1$ is the unit sphere $\subset \mathbb{R}^3$ at $t = -1$.

(c) $A$ is the history of $B_1$ during $(-1, 1)$.

(d) $B_3$ is the history of $C_1$ during $(-1, 1)$.

(e) If $D$ is contained in a level hypersurface of $u^4$ then $D$ is an oriented, topologically trivial, relatively compact, open region $D \subset \mathbb{R}^3$ with smooth oriented boundary $\partial D$ diffeomorphic to $S^2$.

(f) If $C$ is chosen appropriately it is the history during $(a, b) \subset T$ of an oriented, imbedded, relatively compact, 2-submanifold $A \subset \mathbb{R}^3$ with smooth oriented boundary $\partial A$ diffeomorphic to $S^1$.
