A spacetime is the fundamental geometric object in general relativity. It consists of a connected, 4-dimensional, oriented, and time-oriented Lorentzian manifold equipped with its Levi–Civita connection. The dimension four accounts for three spatial dimensions and one time dimension. The Levi–Civita connection is the unique torsion-free metric-compatible connection, used to define parallel transport and geodesics. A general relativistic gravitational field is defined as an equivalence class of spacetimes under orientation- and time-orientation-preserving isometries.
