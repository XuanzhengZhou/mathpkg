This exercise explores the Lorentzian distance function on Minkowski space. Part (a) establishes that $k(x, \hat{x})$, defined as the proper time/length along a connecting geodesic, is well-defined — it does not depend on which geodesic is chosen between $x$ and $\hat{x}$. Part (b) constructs two disjoint lightlike geodesics (light rays) that nevertheless maintain a constant "distance" $A$ between any point on one geodesic and any point on the other. This is a striking feature of Lorentzian geometry with no Riemannian analogue, highlighting the degenerate nature of the induced metric on lightlike submanifolds.
