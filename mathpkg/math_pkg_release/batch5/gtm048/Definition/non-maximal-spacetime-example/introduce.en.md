This exercise provides two concrete 2-dimensional Lorentzian manifolds on $\mathbb{R}^2$ and asks the reader to determine which one is maximal. The first metric $g$ is the flat 2D Minkowski metric, which is geodesically complete and hence maximal. The second metric $h$ has an exponential factor $e^{u^2}$ multiplying the time component, which causes geodesic incompleteness along certain directions, making it non-maximal. This exercise illustrates that maximality depends on the global geometric structure, not just the underlying manifold.
