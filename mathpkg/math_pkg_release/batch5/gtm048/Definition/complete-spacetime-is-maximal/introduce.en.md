This exercise asks the reader to prove that every geodesically complete spacetime is maximal. Since a maximal spacetime is one that cannot be properly extended (it is not a proper subset of any larger spacetime), completeness ensures that geodesics cannot be continued beyond the manifold, providing a sufficient condition for maximality. The converse is false: there exist maximal but incomplete spacetimes (e.g., the Einstein–de Sitter spacetime).
