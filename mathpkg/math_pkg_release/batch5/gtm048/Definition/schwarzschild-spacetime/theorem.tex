Let $(\mathcal{S}^2, h, \zeta)$ be the unit $2$-sphere. Let $\mu \in (0, \infty)$ be given. Define $\mathcal{A} \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ by $\mathcal{A} = (u^1)^{-1}[(0, 2\mu) \cup (2\mu, \infty)]$. Then $\mathcal{A}$ is open and has two connected components. Define $M = \mathcal{S}^2 \times \mathcal{A}$, with $P: M \rightarrow \mathcal{S}^2$ and $Q: M \rightarrow \mathcal{A}$ as the natural projections. Define $r = u^1 \circ Q: M \rightarrow (0, 2\mu) \cup (2\mu, \infty)$ and $t = u^2 \circ Q: M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$. We define a Lorentzian metric $g$ on $M$ by

$$g = \left(1 - \frac{2\mu}{r}\right)^{-1} dr \otimes dr + r^2 P^* h - \left(1 - \frac{2\mu}{r}\right) dt \otimes dt.$$

The manifold $M$ together with this metric $g$ and the Levi–Civita connection $D$ constitutes the Schwarzschild spacetime.
