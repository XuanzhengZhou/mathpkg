The Schwarzschild spacetime is the most important exact solution in general relativity after Minkowski space, found by Schwarzschild in 1916. It models the gravitational field outside a spherically symmetric body of mass $\mu$. The manifold is a product of the unit 2-sphere with an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^2$, and the metric depends on a radial coordinate $r$ and is singular at $r = 2\mu$ (the Schwarzschild radius). Schwarzschild spacetimes are geodesically incomplete, Ricci flat, but not flat. They exhibit a rich causal structure including black hole regions, which will be studied in Chapter 7.
