This exercise establishes that on a time-oriented Lorentzian manifold, every causal vector field and every causal curve has a globally consistent time direction. Part (a) shows that a causal (timelike or lightlike) vector field cannot change its time orientation — it must be either everywhere past pointing or everywhere future pointing. Part (b) extends this result to causal curves, showing that the tangent vectors along a causal curve all share the same time orientation. This consistency is essential for defining past-pointing and future-pointing curves, which underpin the causal structure of spacetime.
