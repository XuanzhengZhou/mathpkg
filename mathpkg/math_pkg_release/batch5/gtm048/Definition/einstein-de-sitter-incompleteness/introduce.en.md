This corollary proves that the Einstein–de Sitter spacetime is geodesically incomplete: there exist inextendible incomplete geodesics whose affine parameter is bounded from below. Physically, this means particles cannot be traced arbitrarily far into the past — they "enter the universe" at a Big Bang singularity where the scalar curvature diverges. The proof uses the explicit curvature computation (Proposition 1.4.4) together with the properties of integral curves and geodesics under isometries.
