The proper time function $t(p, q)$ is a fundamental concept in Lorentzian geometry and general relativity. It assigns to any two causally related points $p \ll q$ the supremum of arc lengths over all future-pointing timelike curves joining them, representing the maximal possible proper time experienced by an observer traveling from $p$ to $q$. This function takes values in $(0, \infty]$ and plays a crucial role in the study of global hyperbolicity and the existence of maximal geodesics in spacetimes.
