Global hyperbolicity is a strong causality condition that ensures the spacetime admits a Cauchy surface and has good predictability properties (the "present determines the future"). The standard spacetimes used in general relativity---Minkowski, Einstein-de Sitter, and Kruskal---are all globally hyperbolic, which is one reason they are physically well-behaved models. However, global hyperbolicity is not automatic: there exist complete spacetimes that fail to be globally hyperbolic.
