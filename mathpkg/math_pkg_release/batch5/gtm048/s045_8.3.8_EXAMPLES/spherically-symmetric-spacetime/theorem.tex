A spacetime $(M, g)$ is called \textbf{spherically symmetric} if and only if its isometry group $\mathcal{G}(M)$ contains a subgroup $H$ with the following two properties:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $H$ is isomorphic to the group $SO(3)$ of proper rotations of Euclidean $3$-space, and
  \item each orbit of $H$ is spacelike and at most $2$-dimensional.
\end{enumerate}
