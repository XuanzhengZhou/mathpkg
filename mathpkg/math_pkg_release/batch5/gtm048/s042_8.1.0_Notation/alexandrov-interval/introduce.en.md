# Alexandrov Interval

An Alexandrov interval $\langle X, Y \rangle$ in a Lorentzian vector space $M_z$ is the set of all vectors $V$ that are *chronologically between* $X$ and $Y$. Specifically, $V$ belongs to $\langle X, Y \rangle$ if $V - X$ is future-directed timelike (i.e., lies in the time-orientation cone $\mathcal{T}_z^+$) and $Y - V$ is also future-directed timelike. These intervals form the Alexandrov basis for the standard topology on $M_z$, a fundamental construction in the causal theory of Lorentzian manifolds. The concept is named after A. D. Alexandrov and provides a topology defined purely in terms of the causal structure.
