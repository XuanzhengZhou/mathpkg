Throughout Section 8.1, the following notation is fixed: $Z$ is a reference frame on a spacetime $M$; $z \in M$; $Z = Zz$; $\zeta \in M_z^*$ is physically equivalent to $-Z$; $g = gz \in T_2^0(M_z)$; and $\mathcal{T}_z^+ = \{X \in M_z \mid X \text{ is timelike and } \zeta(X) > 0\}$. Thus $(M_z, g)$ is a Lorentzian vector space and $Z$ is a unit vector in $\mathcal{T}_z^+$. This section studies algebraic properties of $(M_z, g, \mathcal{T}_z^+, Z)$ including: wider/narrower Lorentzian inner products, Alexandrov basis for the topology, normal forms for stress-energy tensors, decompositions of $(2,0)$ tensors, and averages over directions.
