In general relativity, each particle carries an electric charge $e \in \mathbb{R}$, assigned in essentially the same way as in Newtonian physics. This charge is a real number that forms part of the particle's fundamental description alongside its rest-mass and energy-momentum. The notation $(\gamma, m, e)$ is used to denote a particle $\gamma$ with rest-mass $m$ and electric charge $e$.
