A photon is formally defined as a particle with zero rest-mass. In nature, photons are "particles of light" and carry zero electric charge, consistent with the ad hoc axiom that $e = 0$ whenever $m = 0$. Two other kinds of zero rest-mass particles have been observed (neutrinos and the hypothetical graviton), but photons are the primary example treated in this book; all of Chapter 5 is devoted to their study.
