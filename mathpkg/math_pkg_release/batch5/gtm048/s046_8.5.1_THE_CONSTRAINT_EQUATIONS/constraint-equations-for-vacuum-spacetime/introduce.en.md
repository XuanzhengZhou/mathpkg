The constraint equations are fundamental to the initial value formulation of general relativity. They are the conditions that initial data (a Riemannian 3-manifold with a second fundamental form) must satisfy in order to be embeddable as a spacelike hypersurface in a Ricci-flat spacetime. The first equation is the Hamiltonian constraint (scalar equation) and the second is the momentum constraint (vector equation). Together they form an underdetermined elliptic system, analogous to the constraint equations $\operatorname{div} E = \operatorname{div} B = 0$ for electromagnetism.
