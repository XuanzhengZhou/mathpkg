Let $I: TM \rightarrow TM$ be the identity map, regarded as a vector field $I$ over the projection $\Pi$ (cf. Section 2.0). Let $I_0$ be the restriction of $I$ to $\mathcal{L}^+$, regarded as a vector field over $\Xi$. A vector field $H$ on $TM$ is defined as horizontal iff $(\Pi^*D)_H I = 0$ (cf. Bishop-Goldberg [1], Section 5.12). Similarly a vector field $H: \mathcal{L}^+ \rightarrow T\mathcal{L}^+$ is horizontal iff $(\Xi^*D)_H I_0 = 0$. Let $H$ be a horizontal vector field on $TM$.

**(a)** Show that $dK(H) = 0$.

**(b)** Suppose $v \in \mathcal{L}^+$. Show $H(v)$ is tangent to $\mathcal{L}^+$.

**(c)** Show there exists a unique horizontal vector field $G$ on $TM$ such that $\Pi_*G = I$.
