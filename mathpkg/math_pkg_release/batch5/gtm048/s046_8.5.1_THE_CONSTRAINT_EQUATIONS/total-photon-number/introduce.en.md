The total photon number in a photon gas is defined by integrating the distribution function $F$ against the 6-form $i(L) \omega$ over a 6-dimensional submanifold $\mathcal{N}$ of the light cone. The condition that $\mathcal{N}$ intersects each integral curve of $L$ at most once ensures a consistent counting of photons. This definition is consistent with the operational definitions from Section 5.5 and the integration on light cones developed in Section 5.6.
