A horizontal vector field on the tangent bundle $TM$ is defined by the condition that the covariant derivative of the identity vector field $I$ along $H$ vanishes. This definition uses the pullback connection $\Pi^* D$ from the base manifold. Horizontal vector fields are fundamental in the study of sprays, geodesic flows, and the geometry of tangent bundles over Lorentzian manifolds. They provide a natural notion of "constancy" for vectors along curves lifted to $TM$.
