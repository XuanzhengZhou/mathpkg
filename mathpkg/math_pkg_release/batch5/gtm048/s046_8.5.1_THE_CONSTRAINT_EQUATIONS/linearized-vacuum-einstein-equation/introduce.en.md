The linearized vacuum Einstein equation governs first-order perturbations $h$ of a Ricci-flat Lorentzian metric $g$. Given a smooth one-parameter family of Ricci-flat metrics $g_u$ with $g_b = g$, the derivative $h = \partial g_u/\partial u|_{u=b}$ necessarily satisfies this linear PDE. The equation involves the second covariant derivatives of $h$ contracted with the inverse background metric $g^{ij}$, and it is the defining condition for a symmetric $(0,2)$-tensor field to be a linearized Ricci-flat perturbation. Solutions to this equation describe infinitesimal gravitational waves propagating on the background spacetime.
