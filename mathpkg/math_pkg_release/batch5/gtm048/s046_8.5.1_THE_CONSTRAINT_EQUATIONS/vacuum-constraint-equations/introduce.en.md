The vacuum constraint equations are a pair of partial differential equations that a spacelike hypersurface $(B, b, \tilde{k})$ must satisfy when it is embedded as a Cauchy surface in a Ricci-flat spacetime $(M, g)$. The first equation is a scalar condition relating the intrinsic scalar curvature $s$ of the Riemannian 3-manifold $(B, b)$ to the trace and squared trace of the extrinsic curvature $\tilde{k}$. The second equation is a vector condition equating the divergence of $\tilde{k}$ to the exterior derivative of its trace. These equations form the foundation of the initial value formulation of the vacuum Einstein field equation, analogous to how the Gauss-law constraint and the vanishing of the magnetic divergence serve as constraints for Maxwell's equations.
