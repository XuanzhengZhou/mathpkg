This theorem establishes the fundamental equivalence between Ricci flatness of a spacetime $(M,g)$ and the satisfaction of the vacuum constraint equations on every spacelike hypersurface. The forward direction (Ricci flat implies constraint equations) follows from the Gauss-Codazzi equations, which relate the intrinsic and extrinsic curvature of a submanifold to the ambient curvature. The converse direction (constraint equations on every spacelike hypersurface imply Ricci flatness) shows that the constraint equations fully encode the vacuum Einstein field equation when considered across all possible spacelike slicings. This equivalence is the geometric foundation of the initial value formulation of general relativity.
