The Einstein field equation is the fundamental postulate of general relativity, relating the geometry of spacetime to the distribution of matter and electromagnetic fields. It states that the Einstein tensor $G$ of the spacetime equals the sum of the stress-energy tensors $T$ (matter) and $E$ (electromagnetic field). This equation is a system of second-order quasilinear partial differential equations for the components of the metric tensor, encoding how matter and energy curve spacetime.
