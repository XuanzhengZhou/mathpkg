In a spacetime, the metric $g$ is often evaluated along a curve $\gamma$. The notation $g(\gamma_*, X)$ abbreviates the evaluation of the restricted metric $g \circ \gamma$ on the tangent vector $\gamma_*$ and the restricted vector field $X \circ \gamma$. Similarly, $|\gamma_*|^2$ denotes the absolute value of the squared norm of the tangent vector, using the restricted metric. These abbreviations are used throughout the remainder of the book.
