An $(r,s)$ tensor field over a smooth map $\phi: N \to M$ generalizes the ordinary notion of a tensor field on a manifold. Instead of assigning a tensor to each point of $M$, it assigns to each point of the domain $N$ a tensor based at the image point $\phi(y) \in M$. This concept is particularly useful when $\phi$ is a curve with self-intersections, where an ordinary vector field tangent to the curve would be ill-defined.
