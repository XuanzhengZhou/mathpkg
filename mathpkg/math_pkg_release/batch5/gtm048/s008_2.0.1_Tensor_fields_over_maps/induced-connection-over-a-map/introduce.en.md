Given a connection $\bar{D}$ on the target manifold $M$, one can pull it back along a smooth map $\phi: N \to M$ to obtain a connection $\phi^* \bar{D}$ over $\phi$. This induced connection is uniquely characterized by how it acts on restricted tensor fields: differentiating $A \circ \phi$ along $X$ yields the same result as differentiating $A$ on $M$ along the pushforward $\phi_* X$. In the special case where $\phi$ is a curve, this gives the covariant derivative along the curve.
