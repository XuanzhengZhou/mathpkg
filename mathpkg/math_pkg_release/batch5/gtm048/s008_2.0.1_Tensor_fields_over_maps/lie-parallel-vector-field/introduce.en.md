A vector field $W$ defined over an integral curve $\gamma$ of $X$ is called Lie parallel if it is invariant under the flow of $X$: pushing $W(u)$ forward by the time-$s$ flow $\mu_s$ yields $W(u+s)$. This is the Lie-transport analogue of parallel transport, expressing that $W$ is the linearized (infinitesimal) version of a one-parameter family of integral curves of $X$ near $\gamma$. Equivalently, the Lie derivative satisfies $L_X W = 0$.
