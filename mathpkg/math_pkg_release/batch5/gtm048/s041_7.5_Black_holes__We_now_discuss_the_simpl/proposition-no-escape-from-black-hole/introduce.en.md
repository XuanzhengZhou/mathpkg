This proposition formalizes the defining property of a black hole: nothing, not even light, can escape from inside the event horizon. The proof uses the fact that $r$ is a decreasing function along any future-pointing causal curve in $M_{II}$, forcing the curve to eventually hit the singularity at $r = 0$. The event horizon at $r = 2\mu$ acts as a one-way membrane.
