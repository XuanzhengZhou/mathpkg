This corollary establishes the fundamental geometric properties of the Kruskal spacetime. It is Ricci flat (vacuum) like the Schwarzschild solution, but it is not stationary---unlike the normal Schwarzschild exterior, which has a timelike Killing field $\partial/\partial t$. The loss of stationarity in the black hole region $M_{II}$ reflects the fact that inside the event horizon, the $r$ coordinate becomes timelike, forcing everything to move toward the singularity at $r = 0$.
