Let $(\mathbb{R}^4, h)$ be Minkowski space and let $f, g: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ be $C^\infty$ functions. On $\mathbb{R}^4$ define a Lorentzian metric
$$g = h + 2F d\phi \otimes d\phi,$$
where $\phi = (u^4 - u^3)/\sqrt{2}$ and $F = f \circ \phi \cdot [(u^1)^2 - (u^2)^2]/2 + g \circ \phi \cdot u^1 u^2$. Orient $\mathbb{R}^4$ as usual and time orient by $Y = (\partial_3 + \partial_4)/\sqrt{2}$. Then $(\mathbb{R}^4, g)$ is a spacetime, called a \textbf{gravitational plane wave spacetime}. It is \textbf{linearly polarized} iff $f$ and $g$ are linearly dependent over $\mathbb{R}$, and \textbf{monochromatic} iff $f(t) = a\cos\omega t + b\sin\omega t$ and $g(t) = c\cos\omega t + d\sin\omega t$ for some constants.
