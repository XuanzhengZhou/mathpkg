Gravitational plane wave spacetimes model gravitational radiation in regions far from the source. They are the simplest exact solutions describing gravitational waves, analogous to electromagnetic plane waves in Minkowski space. The metric depends on two arbitrary functions $f$ and $g$ that encode the two polarization modes of the gravitational wave. A linearly polarized wave has only one independent amplitude function, while a monochromatic wave oscillates at a single frequency $\omega$.
