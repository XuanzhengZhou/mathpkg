Let $\mu \in (0, \infty)$. Define $K = \mathcal{S}^2 \times \mathcal{Q}$ where $\mathcal{Q} \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ is the region determined by $(u^1 q)(u^2 q) < 2\mu/e$ for all $q \in \mathcal{Q}$, with $e = 2.7\cdots$ the base of natural logarithms. Equip $K$ with the metric
$$g = r^2 P^* h + 2\mu\left(1 - \frac{2\mu}{r}\right)(dv \otimes dv + dv \otimes du),$$
where $r: K \to (0, \infty)$ is determined by $(r - 2\mu)\exp(r/2\mu) = 2\mu uv/e$. Then $(K, g)$ is a maximal, vacuum spacetime called a \textbf{Kruskal spacetime}. It contains two normal Schwarzschild spacetimes and two Schwarzschild black holes as open submanifolds.
