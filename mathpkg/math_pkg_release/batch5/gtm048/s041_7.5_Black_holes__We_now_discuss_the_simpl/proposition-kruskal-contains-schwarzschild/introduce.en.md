This proposition establishes that the Kruskal spacetime is a proper extension of the normal Schwarzschild spacetime. The region $M_I$ (where $u > 0, v > 0$) corresponds exactly to the normal Schwarzschild exterior, while the other regions ($M_{II}$ etc.) represent the black hole interior and a second asymptotically flat universe. The coordinates $(u, v)$ cover the entire manifold without the coordinate singularity at $r = 2\mu$ present in Schwarzschild coordinates.
