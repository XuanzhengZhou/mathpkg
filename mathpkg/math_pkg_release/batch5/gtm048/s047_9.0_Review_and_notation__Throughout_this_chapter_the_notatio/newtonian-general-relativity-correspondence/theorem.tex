A framework for translating between Newtonian physics and general relativity is established. The naive spacetime $(\mathbb{R}^4, g)$ uses the metric
$$g = -(1 + 2\phi)du^4 \otimes du^4 + (1 - 2\phi)\sum_{\alpha=1}^3 du^\alpha \otimes du^\alpha,$$
where $\phi: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}$ is interpreted as a time-independent Newtonian gravitational potential, assumed small ($|\phi| \ll 1$). In this framework:
\begin{itemize}
\item A time-dependent scalar field $\phi(\vec{x}, t)$ corresponds to a function on $\mathbb{R}^4$.
\item A time-dependent vector field $\vec{j}(\vec{x}, t)$ corresponds to a vector field with $du^4(\vec{j}) = 0$.
\item A stress-energy tensor $\hat{T} = T^{ij}\partial_i \otimes \partial_j$ decomposes into energy density $T^{44}$, energy flux $T^{4\alpha}$, and 3-stress $T^{\alpha\beta}$.
\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
