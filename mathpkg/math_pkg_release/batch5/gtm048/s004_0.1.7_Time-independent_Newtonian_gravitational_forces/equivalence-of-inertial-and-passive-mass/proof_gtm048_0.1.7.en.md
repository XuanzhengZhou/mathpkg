# Experimental Evidence for the Equivalence of Inertial and Passive Mass

This is an empirical proposition, not a mathematical theorem. The experimental bound
$$\left|\frac{\tilde{m}}{m} - 1\right| \leq 10^{-11}$$
was established by Dicke using a torsion balance experiment (Dicke, R. H., "Experimental Relativity", in *Relativity, Groups and Topology*, 1964). The experiment compared the gravitational acceleration of different materials and found no composition-dependent deviation to within the stated precision. This null result is considered one of the most precisely tested principles in physics and forms the empirical basis for the weak equivalence principle. Sachs and Wu adopt the idealization $\tilde{m} = m$ throughout the remainder of the text.
