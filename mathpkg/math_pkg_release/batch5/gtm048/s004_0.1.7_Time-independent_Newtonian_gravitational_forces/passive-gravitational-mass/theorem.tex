Every point particle $(\vec{r}, m)$, where $\vec{r}$ is its position and $m$ its inertial mass, can be assigned a \textbf{passive gravitational mass}
$$\tilde{m} \in (0, \infty)$$
such that the Newtonian gravitational force on the particle is
$$\vec{F} = -\tilde{m}\,\vec{\nabla}\phi,$$
where $\phi: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}$ is the gravitational potential. The passive mass $\tilde{m}$ is measured operationally by comparing, in a given time-independent gravitational field, the weight of $(\vec{r}, m)$ with the weight of a standard particle.
