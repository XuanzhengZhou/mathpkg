# Passive Gravitational Mass

In Newtonian physics, a point particle possesses both an inertial mass $m$ (resistance to acceleration) and a passive gravitational mass $\tilde{m}$ (sensitivity to gravitational fields). The gravitational force experienced by the particle is $\vec{F} = -\tilde{m}\,\vec{\nabla}\phi$, proportional to its passive mass and the local gravitational field. The passive mass is an operational quantity: it is determined by weighing the particle in a known gravitational field against a standard reference. The near-perfect proportionality $\tilde{m} \approx m$ observed experimentally motivates the equivalence principle that underlies general relativity.
