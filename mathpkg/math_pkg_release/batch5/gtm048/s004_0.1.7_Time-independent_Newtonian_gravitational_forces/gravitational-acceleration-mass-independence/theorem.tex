Under the assumption $\tilde{m} = m$ (equality of passive gravitational mass and inertial mass), Newton's second law together with the gravitational force law yields
$$m\ddot{\vec{r}} = \vec{F} = -\tilde{m}\vec{\nabla}\phi = -m\vec{\nabla}\phi,$$
which simplifies to
$$\ddot{\vec{r}} = -\vec{\nabla}\phi.$$
Thus the gravitational acceleration $\ddot{\vec{r}}$ depends only on the gravitational potential $\phi$ and is independent of both the inertial mass $m$ and the passive gravitational mass $\tilde{m}$ of the particle.
