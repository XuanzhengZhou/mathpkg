# Proof of Mass Independence of Gravitational Acceleration

Starting from Newton's second law, the gravitational force law, and the equivalence $\tilde{m} = m$:

$$m\ddot{\vec{r}} = \vec{F} \quad \text{(Newton's second law)}$$
$$\vec{F} = -\tilde{m}\vec{\nabla}\phi \quad \text{(gravitational force law)}$$
$$\tilde{m} = m \quad \text{(equivalence of inertial and passive mass)}$$

Substituting the second and third equations into the first:

$$m\ddot{\vec{r}} = -\tilde{m}\vec{\nabla}\phi = -m\vec{\nabla}\phi.$$

Since $m > 0$, we may divide both sides by $m$ to obtain:

$$\ddot{\vec{r}} = -\vec{\nabla}\phi.$$

The right-hand side depends only on the gravitational potential $\phi$ and the position $\vec{r}$, not on the mass $m$ or $\tilde{m}$. Hence all particles experience the same gravitational acceleration at a given point, confirming that $\tilde{m}$ is physically irrelevant for determining gravitational motion.
