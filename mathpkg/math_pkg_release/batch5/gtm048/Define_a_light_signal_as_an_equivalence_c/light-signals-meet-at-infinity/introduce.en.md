Two light signals are said to meet at infinity if they are equivalent under a specific equivalence relation defined on light signals. In Minkowski space, this relation identifies two light signals if their spatial direction vectors are equal and the difference of their initial spatial positions is orthogonal to that direction. This natural geometric condition captures when two parallel light rays asymptotically converge, and the concept extends usefully to certain nonflat spacetimes.
