A light signal is defined as an equivalence class of freely falling photons in a spacetime. This concept formalizes the idea that a light ray is characterized not by a single photon trajectory but by the entire equivalence class of all freely falling photon representatives that share the same path. The motivation for this definition comes from the physical observation that individual photons are not directly measurable; what is observed is the entire signal carried by the light.
