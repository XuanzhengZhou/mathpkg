**Proof.** The intrinsic area is $A = 4\pi(u^4 x)^{4/3}\delta^2(x, z)$. Using the results from Proposition 6.3.4:

$$A = 4\pi\varsigma^{-2}\left(\frac{2t}{3}\right)^{4/3} \cdot 9 \left[ \left(\frac{2t}{3}\right)^{1/3} - \varsigma^{-1/2}\left(\frac{2t}{3}\right)^{1/3} \right]^2$$

$$= 16\pi\varsigma^{-2}[t(1 - \varsigma^{-1/2})]^2 = 4\pi\varsigma^{-2}d^2(\varsigma).$$

Thus $\Omega/A = 4\pi/A = (\varsigma/d)^2$, and by the operational definition of area-distance (Section 6.1.6b), $d_A = (A/\Omega)^{1/2} = d/\varsigma$.
