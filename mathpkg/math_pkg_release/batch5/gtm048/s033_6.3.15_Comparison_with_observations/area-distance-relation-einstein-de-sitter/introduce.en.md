This proposition establishes how the apparent angular size of a galaxy relates to its intrinsic area and cosmological redshift in the Einstein-de Sitter model. The area-distance $d_A = d/\varsigma$ shows that for a given intrinsic size, more distant galaxies (larger redshift) appear smaller, but with corrections due to spacetime curvature that differ from Euclidean expectations.
