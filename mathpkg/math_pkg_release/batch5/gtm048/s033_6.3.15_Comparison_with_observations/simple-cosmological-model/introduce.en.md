Definition 6.4.4 formalizes the notion of a simple cosmological model as a specialization of the general cosmological model introduced in Section 6.2.5. It requires the spacetime to be simple (i.e., of the form $M = \mathbb{R}^3 \times (0,\infty)$ with a Robertson-Walker metric), demands that the scale factor $R \to 0$ as cosmological time $u^4 \to 0$ (encoding a big bang singularity), and fixes the present age of the universe at $u^4 z = 10^{10}$ years. This definition replaces the specific dust assumption of the Einstein-de Sitter model with a more general framework that still captures the essential behavior near the big bang.
