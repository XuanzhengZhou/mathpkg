**THE BIG BANG**

Let $(M, \mathbf{M}, z)$ be a simple cosmological model such that $\mathbf{M}$ is a superposition of perfect fluids $\{(\rho_A, p_A, \partial_4) \mid A = 1, \ldots, N\}$.

**(a)** Generalize Lemma 6.4.3 by showing $\sum_{A=1}^{N} \rho_A \to \infty$ as $u^4 \to 0$. (Intuitively: "overall, matter gets denser as we approach the big bang".)

**(b)** Use Example 3.12.1 to construct a case where $N = 2$ and $\rho_2 \to 0$ as $u^4 \to 0$.
