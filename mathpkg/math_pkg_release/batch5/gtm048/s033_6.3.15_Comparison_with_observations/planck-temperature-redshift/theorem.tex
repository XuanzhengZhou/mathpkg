Suppose there exist constants $a \in \mathcal{E}$ and $T_a \in (0, \infty)$ such that $F$ is Planck with temperature $T_a$ for $(x, \partial_4 x)$ whenever $x \in M$ and $u^4 x = a$. Then $F$ is Planck with temperature
$$
T = \frac{R(a)}{R(u^4 y)} T_a
$$
for $(y, \partial_4 y)$ whenever $y \in \mathbb{R}^3 \times \mathcal{E}$.
