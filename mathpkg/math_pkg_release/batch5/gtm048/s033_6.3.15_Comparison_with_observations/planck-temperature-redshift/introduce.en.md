Proposition 6.5.1 establishes the fundamental temperature-redshift relation in Robertson-Walker cosmology: if a photon distribution function $F$ is Planckian at some cosmological time $a$ with temperature $T_a$, then at any later time it remains Planckian with temperature scaled inversely to the scale factor $R$, i.e., $T = R(a)/R(u^4 y) \cdot T_a$. The proof relies on the conservation of the distribution function along photon geodesics, the invariance of the Planck form, and the energy-redshift relation $g(\lambda_* b, \partial_4 \lambda b) = [R(u^4 y)/R(a)] g(\partial_4 y, Y)$ from Exercise 6.0.17d. This result is the relativistic generalization of the classical $T \propto 1/R$ cooling law for blackbody radiation in an expanding universe.
