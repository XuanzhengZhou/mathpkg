Suppose $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$ is a finite superposition of relativistic models and each component is either a particle flow, or a perfect fluid, or a photon gas. Suppose at least one of the following generality conditions holds for the superposition:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] One component is a perfect fluid; or
\item[(b)] $\forall x \in M$ one component is a photon gas not identically zero on $L_x^+$; or
\item[(c)] $\forall x \in M$ there is a component that is a particle flow $(m, e, P, \eta)$ with $m\eta(x) \neq 0$.
\end{itemize}
Then the stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ of the matter model $\mathcal{M}$ is normal and obeys the timelike convergence condition.
