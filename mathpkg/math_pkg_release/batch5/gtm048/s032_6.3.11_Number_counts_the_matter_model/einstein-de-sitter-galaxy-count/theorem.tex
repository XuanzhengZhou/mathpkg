Suppose $d_0 \in (0, \infty)$ and let $\tau_0$ be the unique cosmological frequency ratio such that $\tau_0 d(\tau_0) = d_0$ (notation as in Proposition 6.3.4). Then

$$N = \frac{4\pi}{3} n(u^4 z) \, d(\tau_0)^3 = \frac{4\pi n(u^4 z)}{3} \frac{d_0^3}{\tau_0^3},$$

where $d(\tau_0) = d_0 / \tau_0$ is the angular-diameter distance corresponding to the luminosity distance $d_0$, and $n(u^4 z)$ is the number density of galaxies per unit 3-volume at cosmological time $u^4 z$.
