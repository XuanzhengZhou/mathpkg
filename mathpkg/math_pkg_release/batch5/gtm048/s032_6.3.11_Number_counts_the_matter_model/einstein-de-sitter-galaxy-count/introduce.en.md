In the Einstein--de Sitter cosmological model, one counts galaxies whose luminosity distance $d_L$ does not exceed a given bound $d_0$. Proposition 6.3.13 gives the exact formula for this number $N$, replacing the naive Euclidean guess $N = (4\pi/3) n d_0^3$. The result shows that galaxy counts are suppressed by a factor of $1/\tau_0^3$ relative to the Euclidean expectation, where $\tau_0$ is the cosmological frequency ratio satisfying $\tau_0 d(\tau_0) = d_0$. The formula is derived under the assumptions that galaxies are modelled as a conserved, spatially homogeneous particle flow in the dust matter model.
