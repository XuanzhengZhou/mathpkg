This theorem is the "present determines the future" result for relativistic charged particles. Given the initial spacetime position $x$, the initial 4-velocity $W$ (satisfying the mass-shell condition), and the Lorentz world-force law, there exists exactly one inextendible world-line $\gamma$ satisfying these initial conditions. Moreover, the squared 4-velocity $g(\gamma_*, \gamma_*)$ is conserved along the entire trajectory, meaning the particle's rest mass $m$ is preserved. The proof reduces the Lorentz world-force law to a system of second-order ordinary differential equations for the coordinate functions $\gamma^i = x^i \circ \gamma$, to which the standard existence and uniqueness theorem for ODEs applies. Zorn's lemma then extends the local solution to an inextendible one.
