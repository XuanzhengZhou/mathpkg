Let $(M, g, D)$ be a spacetime, let $(m, e)$ be a particle type, and let $F$ be an electromagnetic field on $M$. Fix $x \in M$ and a future-pointing $W \in M_x$ such that $g(W, W) = -m^2$.

(a) There is precisely one inextendible curve $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow M$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $\gamma 0 = x$,
\item[(2)] $\gamma_* 0 = W$,
\item[(3)] $\gamma$ obeys the Lorentz world-force law $e\tilde{F}\gamma_* = D_{\gamma_*}\gamma_*$.
\end{enumerate}

(b) $g(\gamma_*, \gamma_*) = -m^2$ holds on the entire domain $\mathcal{E}$.
