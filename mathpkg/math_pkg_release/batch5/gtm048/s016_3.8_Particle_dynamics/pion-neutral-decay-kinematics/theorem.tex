Consider the decay of a neutral pion into two photons. One particle $(\pi, m, 0)$ is incoming, with $m = 273$ electron rest-masses. Two photons $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are outgoing. Let $z \in M$ be the collision event and take all vectors in $M_z$.

The collision conservation laws (Definition 3.8.4a and 3.8.4b) are satisfied if and only if there exists a spacelike unit vector $W \in M_z$ such that
$$g(\pi_*, W) = 0, \qquad \alpha_* = \frac{\pi_*}{2} + \frac{mW}{2}, \qquad \beta_* = \frac{\pi_*}{2} - \frac{mW}{2}.$$
