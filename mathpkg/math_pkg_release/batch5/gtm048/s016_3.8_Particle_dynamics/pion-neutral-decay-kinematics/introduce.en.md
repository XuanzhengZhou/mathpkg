This proposition analyzes the kinematics of the simplest nontrivial decay: a neutral pion ($\pi^0$) of rest mass $m = 273 m_e$ decaying into two photons. The collision conservation laws of Definition 3.8.4 determine the outgoing photon 4-momenta $\alpha_*$ and $\beta_*$ up to a single spacelike unit vector $W$, which encodes the direction of one photon in the pion's rest frame. The result shows that the two photons share the pion's 4-momentum equally ($\pi_*/2$ each) plus equal and opposite spacelike components ($\pm mW/2$) orthogonal to $\pi_*$, ensuring that the photons, being massless, have lightlike 4-momenta.
