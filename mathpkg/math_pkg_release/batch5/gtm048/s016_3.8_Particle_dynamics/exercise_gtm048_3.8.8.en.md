To analyze collisions, one often uses a "center of mass frame." Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a finite set of particles. For a given collision event $x$, define the total 4-momentum $P = \sum_{\operatorname{in}(x)} \gamma_*$. Assume $P$ is timelike and future-pointing at $x$. An instantaneous observer $(x, Z)$ is a center-of-mass observer at $x$ iff $Z$ is parallel to $P$. Show that if $\mathcal{P}$ satisfies the conservation laws of Definition 3.8.4, then the total 3-momentum observed by $(x, Z)$ is zero for both incoming and outgoing particles.
