Definition 3.1.1 formalizes a relativistic particle as a future-pointing curve $\gamma$ whose tangent has constant squared norm $-m^2$. The parameter $m \geq 0$ is the rest-mass (allowing massless particles, $m = 0$). The tangent vector $\gamma_*$ unifies the Newtonian concepts of energy and momentum into a single 4-vector called the energy-momentum. Freely falling particles are those whose world line is a geodesic.
