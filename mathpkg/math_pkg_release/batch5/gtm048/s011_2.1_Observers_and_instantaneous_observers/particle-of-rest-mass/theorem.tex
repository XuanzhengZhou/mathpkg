A \textbf{particle of rest-mass} $m$ is a future-pointing curve $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow M$ such that
$$g(\gamma_*, \gamma_*) = -m^2.$$
We sometimes write $(\gamma, m)$ for a particle $\gamma$ of rest-mass $m$. The tangent $\gamma_*$ is defined as the \textbf{energy-momentum} of $\gamma$. If $\gamma$ is a particle of nonzero rest-mass, there exists a positive affine reparametrization $\gamma'$ such that $\gamma'$ is an observer.
