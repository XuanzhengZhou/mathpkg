A space-section $D$ is the oriented "top" boundary of a causal box. It plays the role of a spacelike slice in integral formulations: a 2-form is closed iff its integral over the boundary of every space-section vanishes. This is the general relativistic analogue of saying that the flux through any closed spatial surface is zero.
