For a neighbor $W$ of an observer $\gamma$ in a reference frame $Q$, the Fermi-Walker derivatives $F_{\gamma_*}W$ and $F_{\gamma_*}^2 W$ define the neighbor's 3-velocity and 3-acceleration relative to $\gamma$. Both quantities lie in the local rest space $R_u$ (hence the "3" in their names). This parallels the Newtonian notion of relative velocity and relative acceleration between nearby particles.
