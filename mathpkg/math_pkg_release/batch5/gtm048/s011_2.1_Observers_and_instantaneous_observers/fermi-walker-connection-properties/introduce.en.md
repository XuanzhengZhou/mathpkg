Proposition 2.2.2 summarizes the key properties of the Fermi-Walker connection $F$. It decomposes the covariant derivative along $\gamma_*$ into spatial and temporal projections, preserves the metric, and commutes with the projection operators. These properties justify the interpretation that $F$-parallel transport defines "same spatial direction" for an observer at different proper times.
