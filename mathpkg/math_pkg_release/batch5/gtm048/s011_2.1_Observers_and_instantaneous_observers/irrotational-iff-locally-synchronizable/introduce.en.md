Proposition 2.3.5 establishes that a reference frame is irrotational precisely when it is locally synchronizable. This connects a geometric property (self-adjointness of the spatial expansion tensor $A_Q$) with an operational property (the ability to synchronize clocks via the Frobenius condition $\omega \wedge d\omega = 0$). A direct consequence: on a rotating earth, one cannot synchronize clocks globally.
