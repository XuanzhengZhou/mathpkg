Let $A$ and $B$ be two tensor fields defined on an open set $\mathcal{U} \subset M$. Then $A$ is \textit{physically equivalent} to $B$ iff
$$A_x \sim B_x \quad \forall\, x \in \mathcal{U},$$
where $\sim$ on the right-hand side denotes the physical equivalence relation on tensors at a point. The generic symbol for a tensor field physically equivalent to a given tensor field $A$ is $\tilde{A}$ or $\hat{A}$.

In classical terminology, two tensor fields are physically equivalent iff one is obtained from the other by raising and/or lowering indices. All tensor fields in a physical equivalence class have the same physical interpretation.
