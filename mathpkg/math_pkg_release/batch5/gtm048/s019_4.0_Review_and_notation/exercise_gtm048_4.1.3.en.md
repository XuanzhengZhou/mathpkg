In special relativity, one often uses models of the following kind: $M$ in $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$ is Minkowski space, $F \neq 0$, and $T = 0$. Show that then the model does not obey the Einstein field equation.

(Note: Generalizing from this and similar cases, it is sometimes asserted that Einstein's and Maxwell's equations "imply the matter equations." This is very misleading. In more complicated cases, some of the matter equations are independent of Einstein's and Maxwell's equations. This occurs, for example, in the models of Section 3.9.1 when $N$ exceeds one.)
