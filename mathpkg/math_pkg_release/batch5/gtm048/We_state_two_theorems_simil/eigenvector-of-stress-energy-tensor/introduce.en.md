An eigenvector of a stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ at a spacetime point $x$ is a nonzero tangent vector $X$ satisfying $\tilde{T}X = aX$ for some real scalar $a$. Here $\tilde{T}$ denotes the $(1,1)$-tensor field physically equivalent to $\hat{T}$. The study of eigenvectors of stress-energy tensors is fundamental for understanding the local structure of matter in general relativity, as demonstrated by Proposition 3.14.1 and Corollary 3.14.2.
