Let $F$ be a given electromagnetic field on a spacetime $M$. Let $(m, e)$ be a particle type. Suppose given an imbedded 3-dimensional spacelike submanifold $\phi: W \rightarrow M$, a $C^\infty$ vector field $P_0$ over $\phi$ such that $P_0 y$ is future pointing for all $y \in W$ and $g(P_0, P_0) = -m^2$, and a positive $C^\infty$ function $\eta_0$ over $\phi$. Fix $w \in \phi W$ and let $U$ be an open neighborhood of $M$ containing $w$.

Then for $U$ sufficiently small, there exists a unique $C^\infty$ vector field $P$ on $U$ and a unique $C^\infty$ function $\eta: U \rightarrow (0, \infty)$ such that: (a) $P \circ \phi = P_0$ and $\eta \circ \phi = \eta_0$; (b) $M = (m, e, P, \eta)$ is a particle flow on $(U, g|_U)$; and (c) $(U, M, F|_U)$ obeys the simple matter equations.
