In general relativity, there is no universal matter model; the phrase "appropriate matter equations" is defined on a case-by-case basis. However, two universal criteria apply: (a) matter equations combined with Maxwell's equations must imply the differential energy-momentum conservation law $\operatorname{div} \hat{D} = 0$, and (b) physically positive quantities such as energy density must remain positive and must not be forced negative by the equations. These criteria are formulated in Section 3.12 of GTM048.
