Superposition of relativistic models allows combining two or more matter models on the same spacetime by adding their stress-energy tensors and charge-current densities. The components retain their individual matter equations, and the superposition is called collision-free when there is no direct interaction between components. This construction is motivated by the collision conservation laws of energy-momentum and charge, and can be extended to any finite number $N$ of component models.
