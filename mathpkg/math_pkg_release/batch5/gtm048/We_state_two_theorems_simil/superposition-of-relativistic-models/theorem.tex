If $(M, M_1, F)$ and $(M, M_2, F)$ are relativistic models on the same spacetime $M$, their superposition $(M, M, F)$ is defined as follows: $M$ is the pair $\{M_1, M_2\}$; the stress-energy tensor is $\hat{T} = \hat{T}_1 + \hat{T}_2$; and the charge-current density is $J = J_1 + J_2$. The components $M_1$ and $M_2$ obey the same matter equations individually. The superposition is called collision-free, corresponding to the assumption of no direct interaction between $M_1$ and $M_2$.

The addition of stress-energy tensors and charge-current densities is motivated by the collision conservation laws of energy-momentum and charge. The construction generalizes to finite superposition of $N \geq 1$ relativistic models with $M$ and $F$ fixed.
