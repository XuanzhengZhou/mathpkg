A complete matter vacuum model $(M, M, F)$ on a spacetime $M$ is defined by: $M$ is the empty set; the stress-energy tensor $\hat{T} = 0$ and the charge-current density $J = 0$; and the matter "equations" consist of the statement that no matter is created from gravity or electromagnetism. Complete matter vacuum is interpreted as absence of all matter, including "test matter."

If additionally $F = 0$, one has a complete vacuum.
