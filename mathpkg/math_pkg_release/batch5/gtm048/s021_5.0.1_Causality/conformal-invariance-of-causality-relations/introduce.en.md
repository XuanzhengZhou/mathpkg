A fundamental property of causal structure is that it depends only on the conformal class of the metric, not on the metric itself. If $g'$ is conformally equivalent to $g$, the notions of timelike, lightlike, and causal curves are preserved, and hence the chronology relation $\ll$ and causality relation $\leq$ are identical for both metrics. This invariance is computationally useful: causal relations can often be computed by passing to a conformally simpler metric, as illustrated in Section 6.0.13.
