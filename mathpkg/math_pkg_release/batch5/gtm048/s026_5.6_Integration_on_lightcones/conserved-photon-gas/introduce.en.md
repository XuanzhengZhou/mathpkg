A photon gas $F$ is defined to be conserved on a subset $\mathcal{A}$ of spacetime if its value remains constant when transported along any lightlike geodesic that stays entirely within $\mathcal{A}$. This condition models the physical situation in which collisions---emission, absorption, or scattering---do not alter the photon distribution along light rays. On the entire manifold $M$, conservation admits an equivalent differential formulation: $F$ is conserved on $M$ if and only if its Lie derivative along the geodesic spray vector field $L$ on the future lightcone bundle $\mathcal{L}^+$ vanishes, i.e., $LF = 0$. This is the fundamental matter equation for a collisionless photon gas in general relativity.
