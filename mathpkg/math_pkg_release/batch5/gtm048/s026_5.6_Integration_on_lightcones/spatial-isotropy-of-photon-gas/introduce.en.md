A photon gas $F$ on a spacetime $M$ is said to be spatially isotropic for an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ if its value at the point $z$ is invariant under all spatial rotations about the observer's 4-velocity direction. Concretely, for every rotation $\psi \in \mathcal{O}^3$ (the orthogonal group in three dimensions) and every future-directed lightlike vector $Y$ at $z$, the value $F(z, \psi Y)$ equals $F(z, Y)$. An important example is provided by a Planck-distributed photon gas: if $F$ is Planck for $(z, Z)$, then $F$ is automatically spatially isotropic for $(z, Z)$.
