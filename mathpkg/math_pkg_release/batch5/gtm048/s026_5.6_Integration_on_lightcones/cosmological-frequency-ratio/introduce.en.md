Proposition 6.0.8 gives the explicit formula for the cosmological frequency ratio $\iota(x,z)$ in Einstein--de Sitter spacetime. When a light signal travels from an emission event $x$ to a reception event $z$, the ratio of the observed frequency at $z$ to the emitted frequency at $x$ depends only on the ratio of the cosmological times $u^4 z$ and $u^4 x$, raised to the power $2/3$. Since $u^4 z > u^4 x$ for a signal propagating forward in time, $\iota > 1$, corresponding to a cosmological redshift. The proof relies on two lemmas: Lemma 6.0.9 which states that isometries preserve the frequency ratio, and Lemma 6.0.10 which computes the ratio for the standard photon.
