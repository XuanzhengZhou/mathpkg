*Proof.* The idea is to use an isometry to simplify the calculation. Two lemmas are isolated for this purpose.

*Lemma 6.0.9.* If $\psi: M 	o M$ is an isometry, then $\iota(\psi x, \psi z) = \iota(x, z)$.

*Lemma 6.0.10.* If $\lambda$ is the standard photon and $a \in (0, \infty)$, then the frequency ratio along $\lambda$ is given by the ratio of $R \circ u^4$ at the endpoints.

Using these lemmas, given arbitrary $x, z \in M$ connected by a light signal, one applies an isometry to map the light signal to the standard photon, computes the ratio via Lemma 6.0.10, and concludes via Lemma 6.0.9 that the original ratio is unchanged. The result $\iota(x, z) = R(u^4 z)/R(u^4 x) = (u^4 z / u^4 x)^{2/3}$ follows because $R(u) = u^{2/3}$ in Einstein--de Sitter spacetime.
