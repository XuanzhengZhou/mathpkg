*Proof.* The standard photon $\lambda$ is defined as a lightlike geodesic in Einstein--de Sitter spacetime with specific coordinate parametrization. The frequency ratio is computed by evaluating the energy of the photon relative to the comoving observers $\partial_4$ at the emission and reception events. Using the explicit form of the metric $g = -du^4 \otimes du^4 + (R^2 \circ u^4) \sum du^\mu \otimes du^\mu$ and the fact that $\partial_4$ is geodesic and proper-time synchronizable, the ratio reduces to $R(u^4(\lambda a))/R(u^4(\lambda u))$, where $R(u) = u^{2/3}$.
