Proposition 5.7.2 establishes the existence and uniqueness of the stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ for a photon gas $F$ on a spacetime $M$. The tensor is defined pointwise at each $x \in M$ by integrating the squared extension $	ilde{\omega}^2$ of a covector $\omega$ against $F$ over the future lightcone $\mathcal{L}_x^+$, equipped with its natural volume element $\Lambda_x$. The proposition further records four key properties: that $\hat{T}$ is indeed a stress-energy tensor, that it is normal (i.e., maps causal vectors to timelike vectors) whenever $F$ is not identically zero on the lightcone, that it is trace-free, and that its energy density relative to any instantaneous observer $(x,X)$ equals the total energy density $u$ obtained by integrating the photon energy over the photon momentum space $P_X$.
