An integration region in a smooth orientable manifold $N$ is a compact subset $\mathcal{K}$ with nonempty interior and piecewise $C^\infty$ boundary. This definition guarantees that $\int_{\mathcal{K}} f \Lambda$ is a well-defined real number whenever $f: N \to \mathbb{R}$ is a sufficiently regular function and $\Lambda$ is a volume element on $N$. Integration regions provide the domains over which photon distribution functions are integrated to obtain macroscopic observables such as energy density.
