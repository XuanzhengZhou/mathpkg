**EXERCISE 5.2.6**

For all $s \in (0, a)$:

(a) Suppose $M$ is Minkowski spacetime and $\gamma, \alpha$ are geodesics. Show $f = 0$ if and only if $\gamma$ and $\alpha$ are orthogonal.

(b) Dropping the geodesic restriction on both, show that $\lim_{s \to 0} f(s) = 0$ and show that $\lim_{s \to 0} f'(s) = 0$ if and only if $g(\gamma_* 1, \alpha_* 0) = 0$.

(c) Show that (b) remains valid when $M$ is an arbitrary spacetime.
