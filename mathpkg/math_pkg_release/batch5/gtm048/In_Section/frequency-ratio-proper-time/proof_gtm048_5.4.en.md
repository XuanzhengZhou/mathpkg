**Proof of Proposition 5.4.1.**

The frequency ratio is defined as
$$
r = \frac{g(\dot{\sigma}_0 a, \gamma_a \cdot 0)}{g(\dot{\sigma}_0 b, \gamma_b \cdot 0)} \cdot \frac{|\dot{\gamma}_b 0|}{|\dot{\gamma}_a 0|}.
$$
Here $Z_a = \dot{\gamma}_a 0 / |\dot{\gamma}_a 0|$ and $Z_b = \dot{\gamma}_b 0 / |\dot{\gamma}_b 0|$, so the first factor is precisely
$$
\frac{g(\dot{\sigma}_0 a, Z_a)}{g(\dot{\sigma}_0 b, Z_b)}.
$$

Gauss' lemma (Section 5.0.3) implies that the first square bracket equals one, since $\sigma_0$ is a geodesic photon and the rectangle structure ensures the relevant orthogonality conditions. Therefore
$$
r = \frac{|\dot{\gamma}_b 0|}{|\dot{\gamma}_a 0|},
$$
which is exactly the inverse of the proper time ratio for the rectangle $\sigma$ at $\sigma_0$.
