Let $\sigma: [-\varepsilon, \varepsilon] \times [a, b] \to M$ be a rectangle on a spacetime $M$, with $\sigma_0 = \sigma(\cdot, 0)$ a geodesic photon from $\sigma(a, 0)$ to $\sigma(b, 0)$. Define the observer curves $\gamma_A: [-\varepsilon, \varepsilon] \to M$ by $\gamma_A v = \sigma(A, v)$ for $A = a, b$, which are future-pointing timelike. Let $Z_A = \dot{\gamma}_A 0 / |\dot{\gamma}_A 0|$ be the instantaneous observer at $\sigma(A, 0)$. Then the frequency ratio $r$ for the light signal $[\sigma_0]$ with respect to the observers $Z_a$ and $Z_b$, given by
$$
r = \frac{g(\dot{\sigma}_0 a, Z_a)}{g(\dot{\sigma}_0 b, Z_b)},
$$
equals the inverse of the proper time ratio for $\sigma$ at $\sigma_0$, that is,
$$
r = \frac{|\dot{\gamma}_b 0|}{|\dot{\gamma}_a 0|}.
$$
