Let $M$ be a spacetime, let $\lambda: [a, b] \to M$ be a freely falling photon, and let $(\lambda a, Z_a)$ and $(\lambda b, Z_b)$ be instantaneous observers at the emission and reception events respectively. Let $[\lambda]$ be the light signal containing $\lambda$. The \textbf{frequency ratio} for $([\lambda], Z_a, Z_b)$ is defined as
$$
r = \frac{g(\lambda_* a, Z_a)}{g(\lambda_* b, Z_b)} = \frac{f_a}{f_b},
$$
where $f_A = -g(\lambda_* A, Z_A)/h$ is the measured frequency at emission ($A = a$) and reception ($A = b$). The ratio is independent of the choice of representative photon in the light signal $[\lambda]$.
