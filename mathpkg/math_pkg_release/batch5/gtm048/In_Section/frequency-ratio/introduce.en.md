The frequency ratio $r = f_a / f_b$ compares the measured frequency of a photon at emission and reception, using instantaneous observers at both events. It is well-defined for a light signal $[\lambda]$ because any two photons in the same signal are reparametrizations of each other, making the ratio independent of the representative chosen. In special cases, $r$ can be interpreted as a Doppler shift (Minkowski spacetime) or cosmological redshift (e.g., Einstein-de Sitter spacetime), but such additional interpretations require extra geometric structure such as distant parallelism.
