The direction-energy space $P_z = \mathcal{S}_Z \times (0,\infty)$ is introduced for an instantaneous observer $(z,Z)$ on a spacetime. It parametrizes all possible photon energy-momenta that $(z,Z)$ can measure: the first component gives the spatial direction on the celestial sphere $\mathcal{S}_Z$, and the second component $e \in (0,\infty)$ gives the measured energy. This space serves as the domain for the photon distribution function $F_Z$, enabling an observer-independent continuum description of many photons.
