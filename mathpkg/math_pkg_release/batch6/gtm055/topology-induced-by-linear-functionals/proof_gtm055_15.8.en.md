There is no loss of generality in assuming $\mathcal{M}$ to be a linear manifold; if $\mathcal{M}$ is an arbitrary set of linear functionals, let $\bar{\mathcal{M}}$ denote the linear submanifold of the full algebraic dual generated by $\mathcal{M}$. Then every functional in $\bar{\mathcal{M}}$ is continuous with respect to the topology inversely induced by $\mathcal{M}$ (Prob. 12U), so the latter topology coincides with the topology induced by $\bar{\mathcal{M}}$. Using Lemma 15.1, the topology induced on $\mathcal{E}$ by $\mathcal{M}$ coincides with the linear topology induced by the family of pseudonorms $\{\sigma_f\}_{f \in \mathcal{M}}$ where $\sigma_f(x) = |f(x)|$. The proof is then virtually a repetition of the proofs of Propositions 15.2 and 15.3 and Corollary 15.4.
