A monotone mapping between partially ordered sets is a function that preserves or reverses the order relation. Monotone increasing (also called order-preserving or isotone) maps satisfy $x \leq y \implies f(x) \leq f(y)$. Monotone decreasing (order-reversing or antitone) maps reverse the inequality. The strict variants require strict inequality preservation. Monotone mappings play a central role in fixed-point theorems and order theory.
