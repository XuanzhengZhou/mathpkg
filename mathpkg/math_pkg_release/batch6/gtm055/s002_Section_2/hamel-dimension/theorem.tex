If $X$ and $Y$ are any two Hamel bases for a linear space $\mathcal{E}$, then $\operatorname{card} X = \operatorname{card} Y$. The common cardinal number of all the Hamel bases for $\mathcal{E}$ is called the \textbf{Hamel dimension} of $\mathcal{E}$. A linear space with finite Hamel dimension is \textbf{finite dimensional}; otherwise it is \textbf{infinite dimensional}. For finite dimensional $\mathcal{E}$, the Hamel dimension is called the \textbf{dimension} of $\mathcal{E}$ and is denoted $\dim \mathcal{E}$.
