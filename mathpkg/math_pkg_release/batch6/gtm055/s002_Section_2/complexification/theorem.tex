Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a real vector space. The \textbf{complexification} $\mathcal{E}^+$ of $\mathcal{E}$ is the complex vector space consisting of $\mathcal{E} \times \mathcal{E}$ with:
$$(x_1, y_1) + (x_2, y_2) = (x_1 + x_2, y_1 + y_2)$$
and for $\alpha = s + it \in \mathbb{C}$:
$$\alpha(x, y) = (sx - ty, tx + sy).$$
Identifying $x \mapsto (x, 0)$ embeds $\mathcal{E}$ as a real linear manifold in $\mathcal{E}^+$ (regarded as a real space), and every vector in $\mathcal{E}^+$ has unique expression $x + iy$ with $x, y \in \mathcal{E}$.
