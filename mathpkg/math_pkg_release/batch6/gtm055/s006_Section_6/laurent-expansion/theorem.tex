Let $f$ be an analytic function on an annular domain $A = \{\lambda \in \mathbb{C} : r_0 < |\lambda - \alpha| < r_1\}$ where $0 \leq r_0 < r_1 \leq +\infty$. Then there exist uniquely determined coefficients $\{\alpha_n\}_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty}$ such that
$$f(\lambda) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} \alpha_n (\lambda - \alpha)^n, \quad \lambda \in A.$$
The convergence is absolute in $A$ and uniform on compact subsets of $A$.
