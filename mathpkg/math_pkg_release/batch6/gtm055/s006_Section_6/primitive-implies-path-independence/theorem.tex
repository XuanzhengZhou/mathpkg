Let $f$ be a function defined and continuous on a domain $\Delta$ and suppose $f$ possesses a primitive $F$ on $\Delta$. In other words, suppose there is an analytic function $F$ on $\Delta$ whose derivative is $f$. Then for an arbitrary rectifiable arc $\alpha$ in $\Delta$ we have
$$\int_{\alpha} f(\zeta) \, d\zeta = F(\lambda_1) - F(\lambda_0),$$
where $\lambda_0$ and $\lambda_1$ denote the initial and terminal points of $\alpha$, respectively. In particular, if $\gamma$ is a closed rectifiable arc in $\Delta$, then
$$\int_{\gamma} f(\zeta) \, d\zeta = 0.$$
