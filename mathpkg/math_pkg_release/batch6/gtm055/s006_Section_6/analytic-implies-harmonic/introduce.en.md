This corollary follows from the Cauchy-Riemann equations and the fact that analytic functions are infinitely differentiable (by Taylor's theorem). Differentiating $u_x = v_y$ with respect to $x$ and $u_y = -v_x$ with respect to $y$, then adding, yields $u_{xx} + u_{yy} = 0$ since mixed partials are equal. Similarly for $v$. Conversely, every harmonic function on a simply connected domain is the real part of some analytic function.
