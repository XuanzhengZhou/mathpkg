(Abel's Theorem) Let
$$f(\lambda) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \alpha_n \lambda^n,$$
where the power series has radius of convergence $r_0$, $0 < r_0 < +\infty$, and suppose that for some point $\lambda_0 = r_0 e^{it_0}$ on the circle of convergence the series $\sum_{n} \alpha_n \lambda_0^n$ converges, say to $\sigma$. Show that
$$\lim_{r \uparrow r_0} f(r e^{it_0}) = \sigma.$$
Thus $f$ converges "radially" to the sum of the power series representing $f$ at each point on the circle of convergence at which the series converges.
