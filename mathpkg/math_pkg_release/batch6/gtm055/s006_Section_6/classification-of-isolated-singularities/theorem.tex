Let $\alpha$ be an isolated singularity of an analytic function $f$ with Laurent expansion
$$f(\lambda) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} \alpha_n (\lambda - \alpha)^n.$$
If $\alpha_n = 0$ for all $n < 0$, then $\alpha$ is a **removable singularity**; $f$ extends analytically to $\alpha$. If there are infinitely many nonzero $\alpha_{-n}$ ($n > 0$), $\alpha$ is an **essential singularity**. If there are only finitely many nonzero $\alpha_{-n}$ and $N$ is the largest positive integer with $\alpha_{-N} \neq 0$, then $\alpha$ is a **pole of order $N$**, and $f(\lambda) = g(\lambda)/(\lambda - \alpha)^N$ with $g$ analytic and $g(\alpha) \neq 0$.
