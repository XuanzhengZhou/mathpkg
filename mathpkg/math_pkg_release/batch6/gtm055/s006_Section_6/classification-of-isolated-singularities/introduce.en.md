Isolated singularities are classified into three types based on Laurent expansion. A removable singularity has no negative powers -- the function can be extended analytically. A pole of order $N$ has finitely many negative powers, with the function behaving like $(\lambda - \alpha)^{-N}$ near the singularity. An essential singularity has infinitely many negative powers and exhibits the most complicated behavior (Picard's theorem).
