Let $\Delta$ be a domain in $\mathbb{C}$, and let $f$ be an analytic function on $\Delta$. For each point $\alpha$ in $\Delta$, there exist complex coefficients $\{\alpha_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ and a positive number $R$ (depending on $\alpha$) such that the disc $D_R(\alpha)$ is contained in $\Delta$ and
$$f(\lambda) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \alpha_n (\lambda - \alpha)^n$$
for every $\lambda$ in $D_R(\alpha)$. The coefficients are uniquely determined and satisfy $\alpha_n = f^{(n)}(\alpha)/n!$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N}_0$.
