If $f$ is an analytic function on a domain $\Delta$ and $f(\alpha) = 0$, then $\alpha$ is called a **zero** of $f$. By Taylor's theorem, there exist a unique positive integer $N$ and an analytic function $g$ on $\Delta$ such that $g(\alpha) \neq 0$ and $f(\lambda) = (\lambda - \alpha)^N g(\lambda)$. The integer $N$ is called the **order** (or multiplicity) of the zero.
