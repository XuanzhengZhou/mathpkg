Morera's Theorem provides a converse to Cauchy's theorem: a continuous function with zero integral over every triangle is necessarily analytic. The proof constructs a local primitive using line integrals, shows the primitive is analytic, and concludes that its derivative $f$ is also analytic. One only needs to verify the condition for arbitrarily small triangles contained in discs within the domain.
