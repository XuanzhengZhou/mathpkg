The Cauchy-Riemann equations characterize analytic functions in terms of their real and imaginary parts. They are necessary conditions for analyticity, and when combined with $\mathcal{C}^{(1)}$ regularity, they become sufficient. These equations establish the fundamental link between complex analysis and the theory of harmonic functions.
