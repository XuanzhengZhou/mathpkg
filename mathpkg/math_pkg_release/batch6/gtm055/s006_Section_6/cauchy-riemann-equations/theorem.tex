Let $f$ be a complex-valued function on a complex domain $\Delta$, and let $u$ and $v$ be the real and imaginary parts of $f$ on the corresponding real domain $\Delta'$. If $f$ is analytic on $\Delta$ then $u$ and $v$ satisfy the Cauchy--Riemann equations
$$u_x = v_y, \quad u_y = -v_x,$$
identically on $\Delta'$. Conversely, if $u$ and $v$ belong to $\mathcal{C}^{(1)}(\Delta')$ and satisfy the Cauchy--Riemann equations identically on $\Delta'$, then $f$ is analytic on $\Delta$.
