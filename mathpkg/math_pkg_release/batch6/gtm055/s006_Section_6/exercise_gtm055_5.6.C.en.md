In real analysis the hyperbolic functions $\sinh t$ and $\cosh t$ are closely related to the circular functions. This relation becomes particularly lucid in complex analysis. Show that, in fact,
$$\cosh t = \cos(it) \quad \text{and} \quad \sinh t = -i \sin(it)$$
for every real number $t$. (These relations are used to define the entire functions $\cosh \zeta$ and $\sinh \zeta$ on $\mathbb{C}$.)
