Proof. The collection $S$ of all characteristic functions of sets of finite measure is a subset of $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$. Consequently, if $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$ is separable for some one value of $p$, then $S$ is also separable in the relative metric. (Recall (Prop. 4.1) that a metric space is separable if and only if it satisfies the second axiom of countability.) Let $S_0$ be a countable subset of $S$ that is dense in $S$, and let $\mathcal{C}_0$ denote the corresponding collection of measurable sets. For any set $F$ having finite measure there is a sequence $\{E_n\}$ in

spaces of Banach space valued mappings. In this connection we recall (cf. Chapter 6) that if $(X, \mathbf{S})$ is a measurable space and $\mathcal{E}$ a normed space, then an $\mathcal{E}$-valued mapping $\Phi$ defined on $X$ is measurable if $\Phi^{-1}(M) \in \mathbf{S}$ for every Borel set $M$ in $\mathcal{E}$.
