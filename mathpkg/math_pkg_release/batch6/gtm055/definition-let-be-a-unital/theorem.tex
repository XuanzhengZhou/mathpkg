Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a unital Banach algebra and let $x$ be an element of $\mathcal{A}$. A complex number $\lambda$ is said to belong to the left spectrum of $x$ with respect to $\mathcal{A}$ (notation: $\lambda \in \sigma_{l, \mathcal{A}}(x)$) if $\lambda - x(= \lambda 1_{\mathcal{A}} - x)$ is not left invertible in $\mathcal{A}$, i.e., if there does not exist any element $y$ of $\mathcal{A}$ such that $y(\lambda - x) = 1$.
