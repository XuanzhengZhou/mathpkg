A hyperplane in a topological vector space $\mathcal{E}$ is a level set of a continuous nonzero real linear functional. For complex spaces, one regards $\mathcal{E}$ as a real space to apply the definition. Hyperplanes serve as geometric dividing surfaces in separation theorems for convex sets.
