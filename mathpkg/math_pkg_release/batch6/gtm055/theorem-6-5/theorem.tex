Let $X$ be a set and let $\mathcal{A}$ be a real algebra of real-valued functions on $X$ that contains the constant functions and is closed with respect to the formation of limits of pointwise convergent sequences. Then there is a unique $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ of subsets of $X$ such that $X$ is a measurable space

if it assumes each of its values on a measurable set, or, equivalently, if and only if the sets $E_i$ in the representation (2) are measurable. The following useful result shows how measurable real-valued functions can be approximated by measurable simple functions; for a sketch of a proof as well as a discussion of the complex case see Problem S.
