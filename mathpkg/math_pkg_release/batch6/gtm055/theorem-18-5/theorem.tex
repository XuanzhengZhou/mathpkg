(Stone–Weierstrass Theorem). Let $X$ be a compact Hausdorff space. Then the only (sup norm) closed, self-conjugate, strongly separating subalgebra of $\mathcal{C}(X)$ is the algebra $\mathcal{C}(X)$ itself. Similarly, the only closed, strongly separating subalgebra of the real algebra $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ is $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ itself.

The proof of this theorem is fairly lengthy, and will be given in part as a sequence of preparatory propositions. Our first step is to elucidate the role of the hypothesis of self-conjugacy.
