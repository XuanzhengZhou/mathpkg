Let $\{\sigma_{\gamma}\}$ be a separating indexed family of pseudonorms on a linear space $\mathcal{E}$ and let $\mathcal{T}$ denote the topology induced on $\mathcal{E}$ by $\{\sigma_{\gamma}\}$. If the family $\{\sigma_{\gamma}\}$ is countably determined (Prob. 1P), then $\mathcal{T}$ is metrizable, and is, in fact, induced by a (suitably constructed) quasinorm. (A topological vector space whose topology is induced by a quasinorm is said to be quasinormable.) Thus every neighborhood of 0 with respect to $\mathcal{T}$ contains a neighborhood of 0 in the topology $\mathcal{T}$, and we have proved that $\mathcal{T}$ is the topology induced by $\mathcal{T}$.

To go the other way, suppose $\mathcal{T}$ is metrizable, and let $\{U_n\}$ be a countable neighborhood base at 0 with respect to $\mathcal{T}$. For each $n$ there exists a finite subset $D_n$ of $\Gamma$ and a positive number $\varepsilon_n$ such that

$$\{x \in \mathcal{E} : \sigma_\gamma(x) < \varepsilon_n, \gamma \in D_n\} \subset U_n,$$

and if we set $\Gamma_0 = \bigcup_n D_n$, then the countable subfamily $\{\sigma_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma_0}$ induces $\mathcal{T}$.
