$$\varphi \left( \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n \right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \varphi(A_n).$$

If $\varphi$ takes on only ordinary complex values the sum appearing in (4) is simply a convergent infinite series of complex numbers. Note that since the left member of (4) is not changed if an arbitrary permutation is applied to the sequence $\{A_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$, the series appearing in (4) is unconditionally convergent, and therefore

$$\varphi \left( \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n \right) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \varphi(A_n) = \sum_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \varphi(A_n),$$

where the latter expression represents, of course, the sum of the indexed family $\{\varphi(A_n)\}_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ of complex numbers (see Example 3Q).
