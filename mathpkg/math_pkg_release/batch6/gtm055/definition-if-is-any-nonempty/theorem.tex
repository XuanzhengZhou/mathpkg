If $M$ is any nonempty subset of a locally convex topological linear space $\mathcal{E}$, then the annihilator of $M$ is the set

$$M^a = \{ f \in \mathcal{E}^* : f(M) = (0) \}$$

consisting of all the continuous linear functionals on $\mathcal{E}$ that vanish identically on $M$. Dually, if $M$ is a nonempty subset of $\mathcal{E}^*$, then the preannihilator of $M$ is the set $^a M$ of all those vectors $x$ in $\mathcal{E}$ such that $f(x) = 0$ for every $f$ in $M$. (By convention the empty subset of $\mathcal{E}$ is assigned $\mathcal{E}^*$ as its annihilator; similarly, the preannihilator of the empty subset of $\mathcal{E}^*$ is taken to be the entire space $\mathcal{E}$.)

The following two propositions and accompanying corollary give a summary of the elementary properties of annihilators and preannihilators. Proposition 16.1 is self-evident and requires no proof; it is recorded here solely for convenience of reference. The lack of symmetry in the formulation of Proposition 16.2 stems from the fact that every closed convex subset of a locally convex space $\mathcal{E}$ is also weakly closed; see Problem 15L.
