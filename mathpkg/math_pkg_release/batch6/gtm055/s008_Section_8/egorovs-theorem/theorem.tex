Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space, let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence of measurable functions on $X$ converging pointwise almost everywhere $[\mu]$ to a measurable function $f$, and suppose $\mu(X) < +\infty$. Then for every $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists a measurable set $E \subseteq X$ such that $\mu(X \setminus E) < \varepsilon$ and $\{f_n\}$ converges uniformly to $f$ on $E$.
