Let $L$ be a (real or complex) Lebesgue integral on a measurable space $X$, and let $\{f_n\}$ be a monotone increasing sequence of nonnegative functions on $X$, each integrable with respect to $L$. If $f = \lim_n f_n$ pointwise, then $f$ is integrable iff $\{L(f_n)\}$ is bounded above in $\mathbb{R}$, and in this case $L(f) = \lim_n L(f_n)$.
