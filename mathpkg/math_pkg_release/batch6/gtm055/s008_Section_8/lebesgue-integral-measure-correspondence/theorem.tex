Let $(X, \mathbf{S})$ be a measurable space, and suppose given a Lebesgue integral (or real Lebesgue integral) $(X, \mathcal{L}, L)$ on $X$. For each measurable set $E$ in $X$, define

$$\mu(E) = \begin{cases} L(\chi_E), & \chi_E \in \mathcal{L}, \\ +\infty, & \chi_E \notin \mathcal{L}. \end{cases}$$

Then $\mu$ is a measure on $(X, \mathbf{S})$. Conversely, if $\mu$ is any measure on $(X, \mathbf{S})$, then there exists a unique Lebesgue integral (or real Lebesgue integral) $(X, \mathcal{L}, L)$ on $X$ that is related to $\mu$ as in (6). Thus (6) gives rise to a one-to-one correspondence between the set of all Lebesgue integrals (or real Lebesgue integrals) on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ and the set of all measures on $(X, \mathbf{S})$.
