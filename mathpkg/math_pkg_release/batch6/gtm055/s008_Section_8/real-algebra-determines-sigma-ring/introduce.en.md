Theorem 6.5 is a converse to Theorem 6.4. Given a real algebra $\mathcal{A}$ of real-valued functions on a set $X$ that contains constant functions and is closed under pointwise limits, there exists a unique $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ on $X$ for which the measurable real-valued functions are exactly the members of $\mathcal{A}$. The proof uses the Weierstrass approximation theorem (or Stone-Weierstrass for the complex case) to generate characteristic functions.
