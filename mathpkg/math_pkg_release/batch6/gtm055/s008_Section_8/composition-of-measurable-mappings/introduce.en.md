Proposition 6.1 establishes that measurability of mappings between measurable spaces is preserved under composition. If $\Phi: X \to Y$ and $\Psi: Y \to Z$ are both measurable with respect to the given $\sigma$-rings, then their composition $\Psi \circ \Phi: X \to Z$ is also measurable. This follows directly from the fact that $(\Psi \circ \Phi)^{-1}(U) = \Phi^{-1}(\Psi^{-1}(U))$, and both inverse images are measurable by the hypotheses. As a corollary, the composition of Borel measurable mappings between topological spaces remains Borel measurable.
