Proposition 7.3 records basic properties of a finitely additive measure $\nu$ on a ring: monotonicity ($A \subset B$ implies $\nu(A) \le \nu(B)$) and subtractivity ($\nu(B \setminus A) = \nu(B) - \nu(A)$ when $\nu(A)$ is finite). These follow directly from finite additivity and nonnegativity.
