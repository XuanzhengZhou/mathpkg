Let $X$ and $Y$ be sets, and let $\Phi: X \rightarrow Y$ be a mapping of $X$ into $Y$. Then the induced mapping $\Phi^{-1}$ of the power class on $Y$ into the power class on $X$ preserves arbitrary unions and intersections as well as complements, differences, and symmetric differences. (Thus, for example, $\Phi^{-1}(A \nabla B) = \Phi^{-1}(A) \nabla \Phi^{-1}(B)$.)
