Proposition 6.6 provides three equivalent characterizations of measurability for real-valued functions. The most important are that $f$ is measurable iff it is the pointwise limit of measurable simple functions (ii). Condition (iii) strengthens this to a monotone sequence dominated by $|f|$ that converges uniformly on sets where $f$ is bounded. This approximation is fundamental for the Lebesgue integral.
