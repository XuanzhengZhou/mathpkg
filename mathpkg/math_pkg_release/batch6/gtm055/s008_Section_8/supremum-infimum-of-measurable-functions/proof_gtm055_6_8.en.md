For $\sup_n f_n$: $\{x : \sup_n f_n(x) > a\} = \bigcup_{n} \{x : f_n(x) > a\}$, a countable union of measurable sets. Similarly for $\inf_n f_n$. For $\limsup_n f_n = \inf_m \sup_{n \ge m} f_n$, each $\sup_{n \ge m} f_n$ is measurable, and then the infimum over $m$ is measurable. The bounded-above set equals $\{x : \limsup_n f_n(x) < +\infty\}$ and is thus measurable.
