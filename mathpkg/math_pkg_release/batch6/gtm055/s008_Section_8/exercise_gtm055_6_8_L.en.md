Let $X$ be a topological space and let $A$ be an arbitrary subset of $X$. If $\mathcal{T}$ denotes the topology of $X$, then the trace $\mathcal{T}_A$ of $\mathcal{T}$ on $A$ is the relative topology on the subspace $A$. Use this fact to show that the ring of Borel sets in the subspace $A$ is the trace $\mathbf{B}_A$ of $\mathbf{B}$ on $A$, where $\mathbf{B}$ denotes the ring of Borel sets in $X$ (see Problem D).
