Theorem 6.4 summarizes the structural properties of the collection of all measurable complex-valued functions on a measurable space. This collection forms a unital algebra over $\mathbb{C}$ that is self-conjugate (closed under complex conjugation) and contains all constant functions. Crucially, it is closed under the formation of pointwise limits of convergent sequences. The real-valued subcollection inherits the algebra structure and is additionally a function lattice.
