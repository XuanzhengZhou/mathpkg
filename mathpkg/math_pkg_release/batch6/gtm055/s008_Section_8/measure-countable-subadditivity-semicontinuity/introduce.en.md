Proposition 7.4 establishes that every countably additive measure $\mu$ is countably subadditive and semicontinuous from below: for any monotone increasing sequence $\{E_n\}$ of measurable sets, $\mu(\bigcup_n E_n) = \lim_n \mu(E_n)$. Conversely, for a finitely additive measure defined on a $\sigma$-ring, either countable subadditivity or semicontinuity from below implies countable additivity, providing a useful criterion for checking whether a finitely additive set function is in fact a measure.
