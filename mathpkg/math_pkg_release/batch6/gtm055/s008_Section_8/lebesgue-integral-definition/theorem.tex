Let $X = (X, \mathbf{S})$ be a measurable space. A linear functional $L$ defined on a linear space $\mathcal{L}$ of complex-valued measurable functions on $X$ is a (complex) Lebesgue integral on $X$ provided:
$(L_1)$ If $f \in \mathcal{L}$ and $g$ is measurable with $|g| \leq |f|$, then (a) $g \in \mathcal{L}$, and (b) $|L(g)| \leq L(|f|)$.
$(L_2)$ If $\{f_n\} \subset \mathcal{L}$, $\lim_{m,n} L(|f_m - f_n|) = 0$, and $f_n \to g$ pointwise, then $g \in \mathcal{L}$ and $\lim_n L(f_n) = L(g)$.
A real linear functional on a real linear space of real-valued measurable functions satisfying $(L_1)$ and $(L_2)$ is a real Lebesgue integral.
