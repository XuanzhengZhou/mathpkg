Proposition 7.1 shows that the Lebesgue integral induces a natural pseudometric $\sigma(f,g) = L(|f-g|)$ on the space $\mathcal{L}$ of integrable functions. Convergence in this pseudometric is exactly convergence in the mean. The proposition also establishes positivity: $L(f) \ge 0$ for nonnegative integrable $f$, and $L(f)$ is real whenever $f$ is real-valued.
