Let $L$ be a (real or complex) Lebesgue integral on a measurable space $X$, and let $\mathcal{L}$ denote the vector space of integrable functions with respect to $L$. Then $\sigma(f,g) = L(|f-g|)$ defines a pseudometric on $\mathcal{L}$ with the property that convergence in the mean is convergence with respect to $\sigma$. Moreover, if $f$ is nonnegative and belongs to $\mathcal{L}$, then $L(f) \ge 0$, and if $f$ is real-valued and belongs to $\mathcal{L}$, then $L(f)$ is real.
