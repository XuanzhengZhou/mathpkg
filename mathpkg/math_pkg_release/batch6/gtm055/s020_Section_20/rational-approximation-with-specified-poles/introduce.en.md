Proposition 18.14 states that if $K$ is a nonempty compact subset of $\mathbb{C}$ and $P$ contains at least one point from each connected component of $\hat{\mathbb{C}} \setminus K$, then any function locally analytic on a neighborhood of $K$ can be uniformly approximated on $K$ by rational functions whose poles lie only in $P$. The proof relies on Proposition 14.10 (a consequence of the Hahn-Banach theorem) and the identity theorem for analytic functions. This result is a key precursor to Runge's theorem.
