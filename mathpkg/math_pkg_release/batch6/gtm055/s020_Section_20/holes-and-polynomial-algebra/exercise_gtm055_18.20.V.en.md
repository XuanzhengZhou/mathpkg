Let $K$ be a compact subset of the complex plane, and let $V$ denote the set of all those complex numbers $\alpha$ such that the function $1/(\lambda - \alpha)$ belongs to the algebra $\mathcal{P}(K)$.

(i) Show that each hole in $K$ is either disjoint from $V$ or wholly contained in $V$. Show also that the unbounded component $U_{\infty}$ of $\mathbb{C} \setminus K$ is always contained in $V$, and conclude that $\mathcal{P}(\hat{K}) = \mathcal{R}(\hat{K})$. (Hint: Show that $V$ is both open and closed relative to $\mathbb{C} \setminus K$. To verify that $U_{\infty} \subset V$ use the fact that the series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \lambda^n/\alpha^{n+1}$ converges uniformly in $\lambda$ on $K$ for $|\alpha|$ sufficiently large. Recall that $\hat{K} = \mathbb{C} \setminus U_{\infty}$.)

(ii) Use the fact that $U_{\infty} \subset V$ to give a different proof of Runge's theorem on a simply connected domain (see Example K). (Hint: Recall the remark following Corollary 18.15.)
