(i) Develop the theory of indexed biorthogonal systems for a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$, that is, pairs of similarly indexed families $X = \{x_{\gamma}\}$ and $A = \{\alpha_{\gamma}\}$ in $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{E}^*$, respectively, satisfying $\alpha_{\gamma'}(x_{\gamma''}) = \delta_{\gamma' \gamma''}$ for all $\gamma', \gamma'' \in \Gamma$. (Hint: Let $\mathcal{L}$ be the linear space of scalar families $\{\alpha_{\gamma}\}$ with $\{\alpha_{\gamma} x_{\gamma}\}$ summable in $\mathcal{E}$. Use Problem 4N to show $\sum_{\gamma} |\alpha_{\gamma} f(x_{\gamma})| < \infty$ for each $f \in \mathcal{E}^*$. Then show $\|a\| = \sup_D \|\sum_{\gamma \in D} \alpha_{\gamma} x_{\gamma}\|$ is a complete norm on $\mathcal{L}$.)

(ii) Let $(X, A)$ be an indexed biorthogonal system for $\mathcal{E}$, and set $E_D x = \sum_{\gamma \in D} \alpha_{\gamma}(x) x_{\gamma}$. Show that $X$ is an indexed basis for $\mathcal{E}$ if and only if $X$ spans $\mathcal{E}$ and the family of expansion operators $\{E_D\}$ is uniformly bounded.
