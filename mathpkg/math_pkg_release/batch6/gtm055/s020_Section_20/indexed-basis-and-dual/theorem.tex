Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space and let $\{x_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ be an indexed family of vectors. The family is an indexed basis for $\mathcal{E}$ if for every $x \in \mathcal{E}$ there exists a unique family of scalars $\{\alpha_\gamma\}$ such that $x = \sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} \alpha_\gamma x_\gamma$ (the sum converging unconditionally). The coefficient functionals $f_\gamma: x \mapsto \alpha_\gamma$ are continuous linear functionals, and $\{(x_\gamma, f_\gamma)\}$ forms a biorthogonal system. The dual family $\{f_\gamma\}$ is an indexed basis for its closed linear span in $\mathcal{E}^*$.
