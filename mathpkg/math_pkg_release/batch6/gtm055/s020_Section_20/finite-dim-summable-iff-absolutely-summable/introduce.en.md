In finite-dimensional spaces, unconditional (unordered) summability coincides with absolute summability. This is the vector generalization of the fact that rearrangements of a conditionally convergent scalar series can yield any sum. In infinite dimensions, the Dvoretzky--Rogers theorem shows that summability does not imply absolute summability, a crucial distinction in Banach space theory.
