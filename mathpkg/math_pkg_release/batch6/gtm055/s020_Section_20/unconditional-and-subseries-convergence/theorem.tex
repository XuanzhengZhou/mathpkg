A family of vectors $\{x_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ in a Banach space is summable (or unconditionally summable) if the net of finite partial sums converges. Equivalent conditions: (i) every subseries converges; (ii) for every permutation (bijection) $\sigma: \mathbb{N} \to \Gamma$ (when $\Gamma$ is countable), the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} x_{\sigma(n)}$ converges; (iii) the family satisfies the Cauchy criterion: for every $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists a finite set $F_0 \subset \Gamma$ such that $\|\sum_{\gamma \in F} x_\gamma\| < \varepsilon$ for every finite $F$ disjoint from $F_0$.
