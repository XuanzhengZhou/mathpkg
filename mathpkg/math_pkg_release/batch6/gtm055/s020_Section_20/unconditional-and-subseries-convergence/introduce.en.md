Unconditional convergence (also called summability or unordered convergence) is the strongest form of convergence for families of vectors. A family is unconditionally summable precisely when every subfamily is summable, or equivalently when all rearrangements converge to the same sum. This notion is central to the theory of bases, where unconditional bases provide the most useful coordinate representations.
