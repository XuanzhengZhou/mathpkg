The Orlicz--Pettis theorem is a cornerstone of Banach space theory: weak summability implies norm summability. Equivalently, a series that converges for every rearrangement in the weak topology also converges unconditionally in norm. This theorem bridges weak and norm convergence properties, and is essential in the study of vector measures, bases, and the geometry of Banach spaces.
