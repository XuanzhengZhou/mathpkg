These facts follow immediately from the definition of convergence of an infinite series of vectors. For the scalar combination, note that the sequence of partial sums $\{\sum_{n=0}^{N} (\alpha x_n + \beta y_n)\}$ equals $\{\alpha \sum_{n=0}^{N} x_n + \beta \sum_{n=0}^{N} y_n\}$, which converges to $\alpha x + \beta y$ by continuity of vector addition and scalar multiplication. For the bounded linear transformation $T$, the sequence $\{\sum_{n=0}^{N} T x_n\} = \{T(\sum_{n=0}^{N} x_n)\}$ converges to $Tx$ by continuity of $T$. The same reasoning applies with appropriate notational changes for other index systems.
