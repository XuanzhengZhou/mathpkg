Let $\{x_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ be a family of vectors in a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$ such that the coefficient functionals $f_\gamma$ (defined by the biorthogonal expansion) are continuous. If the family is a weak basis (i.e., every $x \in \mathcal{E}$ has a unique weak expansion $x = \sum_{\gamma} f_\gamma(x) x_\gamma$), then it is already a Schauder basis: the expansion converges unconditionally in norm. This follows from the Orlicz--Pettis theorem applied to the family $\{f_\gamma(x) x_\gamma\}$.
