Let $U$ be an open subset of $\mathbb{C}$ and let $P$ be a subset of $\hat{\mathbb{C}} \setminus U$ with the property that $P$ contains at least one point of each connected component of $\hat{\mathbb{C}} \setminus U$. Then for any locally analytic function $f$ on $U$ there exists a sequence $\{r_n\}$ of rational functions, each with poles confined to $P$, such that $\{r_n\}$ converges to $f$ uniformly on compact subsets of $U$.
