A sequence $X = \{x_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ in a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$ is a (Schauder) basis for $\mathcal{E}$ if for each vector $x$ in $\mathcal{E}$ there exists a unique sequence $\{\alpha_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ of complex numbers such that
$$x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \alpha_n x_n.$$

The scalars $\alpha_n$ are the coefficients of $x$ with respect to the basis $X$. A two-way infinite sequence $X = \{x_n\}_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty}$ is a basis for $\mathcal{E}$ in the primary sense [Cauchy sense] if for each $x \in \mathcal{E}$ there exists a unique sequence $\{\alpha_n\}_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty}$ such that
$$x = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} \alpha_n x_n$$
in the primary sense [Cauchy sense].
