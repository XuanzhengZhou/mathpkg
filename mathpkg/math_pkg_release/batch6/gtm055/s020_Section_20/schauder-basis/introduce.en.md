A Schauder basis for a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$ is a sequence $\{x_n\}$ such that every vector $x \in \mathcal{E}$ admits a unique convergent series expansion $x = \sum \alpha_n x_n$. The uniqueness of coefficients ensures linear independence. Bases are fundamental tools for representing vectors and studying the structure of Banach spaces. The concept extends to two-way infinite sequences with primary and Cauchy senses of convergence.
