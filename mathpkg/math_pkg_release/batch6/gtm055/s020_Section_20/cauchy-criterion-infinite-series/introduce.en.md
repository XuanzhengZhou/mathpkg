Proposition 19.2 gives the Cauchy criterion for convergence of infinite series in Banach spaces: a series $\sum x_n$ converges if and only if its tail sums are arbitrarily small in norm, uniformly in the number of terms. This is the direct analog of the Cauchy criterion for sequences, adapted to series via the partial sum formulation. Suitably worded versions also hold for series indexed by $\mathbb{N}$ and $\mathbb{Z}$.
