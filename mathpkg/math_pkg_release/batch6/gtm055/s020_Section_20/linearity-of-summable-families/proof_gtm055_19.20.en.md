These facts follow immediately from the definition of summability. For any finite subset $D$ of $\Gamma$, let $s_D = \sum_{\gamma \in D} x_{\gamma}$ and $t_D = \sum_{\gamma \in D} y_{\gamma}$. Then $\sum_{\gamma \in D} (\alpha x_{\gamma} + \beta y_{\gamma}) = \alpha s_D + \beta t_D$. By the definition of summability, $s_D \to x$ and $t_D \to y$ along the directed set of finite subsets, so the linear combination converges to $\alpha x + \beta y$. Similarly, $T(s_D) \to Tx$ by continuity of $T$.
