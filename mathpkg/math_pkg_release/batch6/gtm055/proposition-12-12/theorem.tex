Let $A$ be a unital Banach algebra, let $x$ be an element of $A$, let $\lambda_0$ be a complex number in the resolvent set of $x$, and let $d = \| R_x(\lambda_0) \|$. Then every scalar $\lambda$ in the disc $D = \{\lambda \in \mathbb{C}: |\lambda - \lambda_0| < 1/d\}$ belongs to the resolvent set of $x$, and for each $\lambda$ in $D$,

$$R_x(\lambda) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (\lambda_0 - \lambda)^n R_x(\lambda_0)^{n+1}. \tag{9}$$

In particular, no point of $\sigma_A(x)$ lies in $D$.
