The strong topology on $\mathcal{F}$ is the polar topology of uniform convergence on all weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$. It is the finest admissible polar topology in any dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$. In the Banach space setting, the strong topology on $\mathcal{E}$ with respect to the dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}^*, \mathcal{E} \rangle$ recovers the norm topology, showing how the abstract framework of polar topologies unifies the classical topologies of functional analysis.
