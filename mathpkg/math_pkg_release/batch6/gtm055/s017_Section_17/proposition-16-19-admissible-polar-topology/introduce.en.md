Proposition 16.19 establishes that every admissible collection $\mathcal{A}$ of weakly bounded sets yields a separated locally convex topology on $\mathcal{F}$ via uniform convergence, with polars $M^0$ ($M \in \mathcal{A}$) forming a neighborhood base at the origin. The admissibility conditions (closed under finite unions, scalar multiples, and algebraic generation of $\mathcal{E}$) are precisely what guarantee separatedness and compatibility with the dual pair structure.
