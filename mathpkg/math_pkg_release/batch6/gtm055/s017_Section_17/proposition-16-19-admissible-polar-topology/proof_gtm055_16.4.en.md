The topology $\mathcal{T}$ is generated by the family of pseudonorms $\{\sigma_M\}_{M \in \mathcal{A}}$ where $\sigma_M(y) = \sup_{x \in M} |\langle x, y \rangle|$. Since each $M \in \mathcal{A}$ is weakly bounded, each $\sigma_M$ is finite-valued, so this is a locally convex topology.

Condition (i) of admissibility (closed under finite unions) ensures that the collection $\{M^0 : M \in \mathcal{A}\}$ forms a neighborhood base at $0$: the intersection of two polars $M_1^0 \cap M_2^0 = (M_1 \cup M_2)^0$ corresponds to a set in $\mathcal{A}$.

Separatedness follows from condition (iii): $\bigcup \mathcal{A}$ generates $\mathcal{E}$ algebraically. If $y \neq 0$ in $\mathcal{F}$, then by the dual pair property there exists $x \in \mathcal{E}$ with $\langle x, y \rangle \neq 0$. Since $\bigcup \mathcal{A}$ spans $\mathcal{E}$, there exists $M \in \mathcal{A}$ and a scalar multiple of some element of $M$ that also pairs nontrivially with $y$, so $\sigma_M(y) > 0$.

Each $x \in \mathcal{E}$ corresponds to a functional $F_x(y) = \langle x, y \rangle$ on $\mathcal{F}$. By condition (ii) (closure under scalar multiples), for any $x \in \mathcal{E}$ there exists $M \in \mathcal{A}$ with $x \in M$, and then $|F_x(y)| \leq \sigma_M(y)$, showing $F_x$ is $\mathcal{T}$-continuous.
