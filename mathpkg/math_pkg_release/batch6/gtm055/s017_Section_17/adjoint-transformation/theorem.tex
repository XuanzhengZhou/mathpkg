Let $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{F}$ be locally convex topological linear spaces, and let $T$ be a continuous linear transformation of $\mathcal{E}$ into $\mathcal{F}$. Then for each functional $f$ in the dual $\mathcal{F}^*$ the composition $f \circ T$ is an element of $\mathcal{E}^*$. Write $f^*$ for this functional, so that
$$f^*(x) = f(Tx), \quad x \in \mathcal{E}, \quad f \in \mathcal{F}^*.$$

Setting $T^*f = f^*$, $f \in \mathcal{F}^*$, defines a mapping $T^*$ of $\mathcal{F}^*$ into $\mathcal{E}^*$, called the \textbf{adjoint} (or \textbf{dual transformation}) of $T$.
