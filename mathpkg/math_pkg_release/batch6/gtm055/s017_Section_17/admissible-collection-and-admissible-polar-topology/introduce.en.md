An admissible collection of weakly bounded sets must be closed under finite unions, closed under nonzero scalar multiplication, and must algebraically generate the entire space $\mathcal{E}$. These conditions ensure that the associated polar topology is a separated locally convex topology. Admissible polar topologies interpolate between the weak topology (coarsest admissible) and the strong topology (finest admissible), providing a flexible framework for studying duality.
