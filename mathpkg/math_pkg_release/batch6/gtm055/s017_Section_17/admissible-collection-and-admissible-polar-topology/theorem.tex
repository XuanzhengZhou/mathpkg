If $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ is a dual pair, then a collection $\mathcal{A}$ of weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$ is \textbf{admissible} if the following conditions are satisfied:

(i) If $M_1$ and $M_2$ belong to $\mathcal{A}$, then there is a set $N \in \mathcal{A}$ such that $M_1 \cup M_2 \subset N$,

(ii) If $M \in \mathcal{A}$ and $\lambda \neq 0$, then $\lambda M \in \mathcal{A}$,

(iii) $\bigcup \mathcal{A}$ generates $\mathcal{E}$ algebraically.

A polar topology is said to be \textbf{admissible} if there exists an admissible collection $\mathcal{A}$ of weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$ that induces it.
