A dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ consists of two vector spaces equipped with a separating bilinear form $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$, meaning that for each nonzero element in one space there exists an element in the other that pairs nontrivially with it. This abstract framework generalizes the relationship between a locally convex space and its continuous dual, allowing $\mathcal{F}$ to be identified with a subspace of the algebraic dual $\mathcal{E}'$. The canonical example is $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{E}^* \rangle$ where $\langle x, f \rangle = f(x)$.
