\textbf{Problem G.} Show that for any element $x$ of a Banach algebra $\mathcal{A}$, the sequence $\{\|x^n\|^{1/n}\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is always convergent and that the spectral radius $r_{\mathcal{A}}(x)$ is given by the formula
$$r_{\mathcal{A}}(x) = \lim_{n} \|x^n\|^{1/n}.$$
Thus, in particular, if $\mathcal{E}$ is a Banach space and $T$ is a bounded operator on $\mathcal{E}$, then
$$r(T) = \lim_{n} \|T^n\|^{1/n}.$$
(Hint: Review Problems 12M and 12P, and use Problem F.)
