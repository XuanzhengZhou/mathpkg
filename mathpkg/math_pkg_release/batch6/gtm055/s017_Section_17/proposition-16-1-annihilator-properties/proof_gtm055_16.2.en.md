The statement is self-evident from the definition of annihilators and requires no proof. It is recorded solely for convenience of reference.

(1) If $M \subset N$ and $f \in N^a$, then $f(N) = (0)$, so in particular $f(M) = (0)$, hence $f \in M^a$. Thus $N^a \subset M^a$, i.e., $M^a \supset N^a$.

(2) For the union-intersection formula: $f \in (\bigcup_\gamma M_\gamma)^a$ if and only if $f$ vanishes on $\bigcup_\gamma M_\gamma$, which happens if and only if $f$ vanishes on each $M_\gamma$, i.e., $f \in M_\gamma^a$ for all $\gamma$, which means $f \in \bigcap_\gamma M_\gamma^a$.

(3) The dual statements for preannihilators follow by symmetry.
