Proposition 16.1 records the elementary set-theoretic properties of annihilators: the annihilator operation reverses inclusions, and the annihilator of a union equals the intersection of annihilators. These follow directly from the definition and require no proof. The dual statements for preannihilators are equally straightforward and establish a Galois connection between subsets of $\mathcal{E}$ and subsets of $\mathcal{E}^*$.
