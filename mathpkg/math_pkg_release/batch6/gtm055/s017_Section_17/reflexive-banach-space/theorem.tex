Let $$\mathcal{E}$$ be a normed space with dual space $$\mathcal{E}^*$$, and let $$j$$ denote the natural embedding of $$\mathcal{E}$$ in the second dual $$\mathcal{E}^{**}$$. The space $$\mathcal{E}$$ is said to be \textbf{reflexive} if $$j$$ maps $$\mathcal{E}$$ onto $$\mathcal{E}^{**}$$, i.e., if the only bounded linear functionals on $$\mathcal{E}^*$$ are those defined by elements $$x$$ of $$\mathcal{E}$$ according to the formula
$$f \rightarrow f(x), \quad f \in \mathcal{E}^*.$$

If $$\mathcal{E}$$ is reflexive then $$j$$ is an isometric isomorphism between $$\mathcal{E}$$ and $$\mathcal{E}^{**}$$, and it follows that $$\mathcal{E}$$ is necessarily complete. A reflexive Banach space $$\mathcal{E}$$ may be viewed as being identical with its own second dual so that $$\mathcal{E}$$ and $$\mathcal{E}^*$$ become the dual spaces of one another.
