Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space, and let $\{a_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ be a sequence of coefficients in $\mathcal{E}$. Show that if the sequence $\{r^n a_n\}$ is bounded in $\mathcal{E}$ for some positive number $r$, then for an arbitrary complex number $\lambda_0$ the series
$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (\lambda - \lambda_0)^n a_n$$
is absolutely convergent in $\mathcal{E}$ for all $|\lambda - \lambda_0| < r$. Conclude that every $\mathcal{E}$-valued power series possesses a radius of convergence $R$, $0 \leq R \leq +\infty$, such that the series converges absolutely for $|\lambda - \lambda_0| < R$ and defines an $\mathcal{E}$-valued mapping on the disc $D_R(\lambda_0)$.
