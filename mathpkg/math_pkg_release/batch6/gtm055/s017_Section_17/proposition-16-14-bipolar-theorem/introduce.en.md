The Bipolar Theorem (Proposition 16.14) is one of the cornerstones of duality theory. It states that for an absolutely convex subset $A$ of $\mathcal{E}$, the bipolar $^0(A^0)$ equals the weak closure of $A$. The proof relies on the Hahn–Banach separation theorem: if $x_0 \notin \overline{A}^w$, there exists $f \in \mathcal{F}$ separating $x_0$ from $A$, which after rescaling yields an element of $A^0$ witnessing $x_0 \notin \,^0(A^0)$.
