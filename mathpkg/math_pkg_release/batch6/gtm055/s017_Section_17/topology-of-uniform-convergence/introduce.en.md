The topology of uniform convergence on a weakly bounded set $M$ is induced by the pseudonorm $\sigma(y) = \sup_{x \in M} |\langle x, y \rangle|$, so that convergence corresponds to uniform convergence on $M$. More generally, a polar topology on $\mathcal{F}$ is generated by a family of such pseudonorms indexed by a collection $\mathcal{C}$ of weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$. All important topologies in duality theory — the weak topology, the strong topology, and the norm topology — are polar topologies.
