Let $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ be a dual pair and let $M$ be a weakly bounded subset of $\mathcal{E}$. Then the \textbf{topology induced on $\mathcal{F}$ by the pseudonorm associated with $M$} is called the \textbf{topology of uniform convergence on $M$}.

The pseudonorm $\sigma$ associated with $M$ (the Minkowski function of $M^0$) is given by
$$\sigma(y) = \sup_{x \in M} |\langle x, y \rangle|, \quad y \in \mathcal{F}.$$

$M^0$ is the closed unit ball with respect to $\sigma$, and a net $\{y_\lambda\}$ in $\mathcal{F}$ converges to $y$ with respect to $\sigma$ if and only if $\{y_\lambda\}$ converges uniformly to $y$ on $M$.

More generally, if $\mathcal{C}$ is a nonempty collection of weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$, then the locally convex linear topology $\mathcal{T}$ induced on $\mathcal{F}$ by the family of pseudonorms associated with the sets in $\mathcal{C}$ is called a \textbf{polar topology}.
