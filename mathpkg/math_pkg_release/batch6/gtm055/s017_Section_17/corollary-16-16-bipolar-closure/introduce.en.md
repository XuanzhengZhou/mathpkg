Corollary 16.16 extracts two consequences of the Bipolar Theorem. First, the threefold polar operation stabilizes: $(^0(M^0))^0 = M^0$ for any $M$, analogous to the annihilator identity $(^a(M^a))^a = M^a$. Second, for absolutely convex sets, the bipolar $^0(A^0)$ is precisely the weak closure of $A$, and when $A$ is already weakly closed, the bipolar recovers $A$ exactly. This establishes a bijection between weakly closed absolutely convex sets in $\mathcal{E}$ and their polars in $\mathcal{F}$.
