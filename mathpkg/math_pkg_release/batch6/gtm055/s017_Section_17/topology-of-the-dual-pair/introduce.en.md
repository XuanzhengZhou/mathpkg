A topology of the dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ is any locally convex topology on $\mathcal{E}$ whose continuous dual is precisely $\mathcal{F}$ (identified as a subspace of the algebraic dual). This compatibility condition ensures the topology respects the given pairing. The Mackey–Arens theorem characterizes all such topologies as precisely those lying between the weak topology and the Mackey topology, providing a complete description of all compatible locally convex topologies.
