Proposition 16.13 records the elementary set-theoretic properties of polars in a dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$: the polar operation reverses inclusions, and the polar of a union equals the intersection of the individual polars. These properties mirror those of annihilators (Proposition 16.1) and follow directly from the definition of the polar.
