The assertions are self-evident from the definition and require no proof; they are recorded solely for convenience of reference.

(1) If $M \subset N$ and $y \in N^0$, then $\sup_{x \in N} |\langle x, y \rangle| \leq 1$, so in particular $\sup_{x \in M} |\langle x, y \rangle| \leq 1$, hence $y \in M^0$. Thus $N^0 \subset M^0$, i.e., $M^0 \supset N^0$.

(2) $y \in (\bigcup_\gamma M_\gamma)^0$ iff $\sup_{x \in \bigcup_\gamma M_\gamma} |\langle x, y \rangle| \leq 1$ iff $\sup_{x \in M_\gamma} |\langle x, y \rangle| \leq 1$ for all $\gamma$ iff $y \in \bigcap_\gamma M_\gamma^0$.

The dual statements for prepolars follow by symmetry.
