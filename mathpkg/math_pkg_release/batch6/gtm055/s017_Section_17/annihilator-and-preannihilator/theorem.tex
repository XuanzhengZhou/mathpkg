If $$M$$ is any nonempty subset of a locally convex topological linear space $$\mathcal{E}$$, then the \textbf{annihilator} of $$M$$ is the set
$$M^a = \{ f \in \mathcal{E}^* : f(M) = (0) \}$$
consisting of all the continuous linear functionals on $$\mathcal{E}$$ that vanish identically on $$M$$.

Dually, if $$M$$ is a nonempty subset of $$\mathcal{E}^*$$, then the \textbf{preannihilator} of $$M$$ is the set $${}^a M$$ of all those vectors $$x$$ in $$\mathcal{E}$$ such that $$f(x) = 0$$ for every $$f$$ in $$M$$.

By convention the empty subset of $$\mathcal{E}$$ is assigned $$\mathcal{E}^*$$ as its annihilator; similarly, the preannihilator of the empty subset of $$\mathcal{E}^*$$ is taken to be the entire space $$\mathcal{E}$$.
