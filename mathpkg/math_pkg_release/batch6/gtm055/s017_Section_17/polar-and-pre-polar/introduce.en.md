The polar $M^0$ of a subset $M$ in a dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ consists of all elements of $\mathcal{F}$ whose absolute pairing with every element of $M$ is bounded by $1$. Polars generalize both annihilators (for subspaces) and closed balls centered at the origin (in normed spaces). The bipolar theorem, a cornerstone of duality theory, states that the bipolar $^0(M^0)$ of an absolutely convex set $M$ is its weak closure.
