Let $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ be a dual pair and let $M$ be a nonempty subset of $\mathcal{E}$. Then the \textbf{polar} of $M$ is the set
$$M^0 = \left\{y \in \mathcal{F} : \sup_{x \in M} |\langle x, y \rangle| \leq 1\right\}.$$

Dually, if $M$ is a nonempty subset of $\mathcal{F}$, then the \textbf{prepolar} of $M$ is the set $^0 M$ of all those vectors $x \in \mathcal{E}$ such that $|\langle x, y \rangle| \leq 1$ for every $y \in M$.

By convention the empty subset of $\mathcal{E}$ is assigned $\mathcal{F}$ as its polar; similarly the prepolar of the empty subset of $\mathcal{F}$ is taken to be $\mathcal{E}$.
