Lemma 9.1 establishes the fundamental relationship between measurability on a product space and measurability of sections. Given a function measurable on the product $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S} 	imes \mathbf{T}$, its $x$-sections $f(x, \cdot)$ are measurable on $(Y, \mathbf{T})$. When $
u$ is $\sigma$-finite, the set of $x$ where $f(x, \cdot)$ is integrable with respect to $
u$ is measurable, and the integral $g(x) = \int_Y f(x, y) d
u(y)$ defines a measurable function on that set. This lemma provides the technical foundation for the Fubini and Tonelli theorems.
