The proof proceeds by establishing the result first for characteristic functions of measurable rectangles, then extending through simple functions to arbitrary measurable functions via standard approximation arguments. For a measurable rectangle $E 	imes F$ with $E \in \mathbf{S}$ and $F \in \mathbf{T}$, the $x$-section is either $F$ (if $x \in E$) or $\emptyset$ (if $x 
otin E$), hence measurable. The linearity of the integral extends the result to simple functions, and monotone convergence arguments handle the general case of nonnegative measurable functions. For complex-valued $f$, the result follows by decomposing into real and imaginary parts, and then into positive and negative parts.
