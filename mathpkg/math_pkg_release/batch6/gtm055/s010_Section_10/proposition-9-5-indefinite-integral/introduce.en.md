Proposition 9.5 formalizes the construction of new measures from old via integration against a nonnegative density function. Given a nonnegative measurable function $f$ on a measure space $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$, the set function $v_f(E) = \int_E f \, d\mu$ defines a measure on $(X, \mathbf{S})$, called the indefinite integral of $f$. Moreover, integration with respect to $v_f$ is equivalent to integration against the product $gf$ with respect to the original measure $\mu$, establishing a change-of-measure formula that is fundamental to the Radon--Nikodym theorem.
