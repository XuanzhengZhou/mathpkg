Let $W$ denote the topological space $W(\Omega)$ of countable ordinal numbers in the order topology, and let $\mathbf{B}$ denote the $\sigma$-ring of Borel subsets of $W$. Show that the diagonal $\Delta = \{(\alpha, \alpha) : \alpha \in W\}$ is a Borel set in the topological space $W \times W$, but that $\Delta$ does not belong to the $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{B}$. (Hint: Every set $A$ in the $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{B}$ has the property that either $A$ or its complement $(W \times W) \setminus A$ contains a set of the form $F \times F$, where $F$ is a closed unbounded subset of $W$.)
