Let $\alpha$ be a complex-valued function defined on a real interval $[a, b]$. Show that $\alpha$ is Lipschitzian on $[a, b]$ if and only if there exists a bounded, Lebesgue measurable, complex-valued function $\beta$ on $[a, b]$ such that

$$\alpha(t) - \alpha(a) = \int_a^t \beta(u) du, \quad a \leq t \leq b.$$
