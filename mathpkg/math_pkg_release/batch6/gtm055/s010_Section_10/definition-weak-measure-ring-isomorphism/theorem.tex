Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ and $(Y, \mathbf{T}, \nu)$ be measure spaces, and let $(\dot{\mathbf{S}}, \dot{\mu})$ and $(\dot{\mathbf{T}}, \dot{\nu})$, respectively, be their measure rings. A one-to-one mapping $\Phi$ of $\dot{\mathbf{S}}$ onto $\dot{\mathbf{T}}$ is said to be a weak measure ring isomorphism if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\Phi([E] \setminus [F]) = \Phi([E]) \setminus \Phi([F])$ for every pair $[E], [F]$ in $\dot{\mathbf{S}}$, and
\item[(ii)] $\Phi\left(\bigvee_{n=1}^{\infty} [E_n]\right) = \bigvee_{n=1}^{\infty} \Phi([E_n])$ for every sequence $\{[E_n]\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ in $\dot{\mathbf{S}}$.
\end{enumerate}

If $\Phi$ is a weak measure ring isomorphism satisfying the further condition

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(iii)] $\dot{\nu}(\Phi([E])) = \dot{\mu}([E])$, \quad $[E] \in \dot{\mathbf{S}}$,
\end{enumerate}

then $\Phi$ is said to be a measure ring isomorphism.
