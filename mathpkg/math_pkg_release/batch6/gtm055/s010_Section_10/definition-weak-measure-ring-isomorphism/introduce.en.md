A weak measure ring isomorphism is a bijection between the measure rings of two measure spaces that preserves the Boolean operations of set difference and countable supremum (join). This algebraic notion captures the structural similarity of two measure spaces at the level of equivalence classes of measurable sets modulo null sets. When the isomorphism additionally preserves the measure (condition iii), it is called a measure ring isomorphism. These concepts are fundamental to the classification of measure spaces: the Halmos--von Neumann theorem shows that, up to measure ring isomorphism, there is a unique separable atom-free measure space of each total mass.
