Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a $\sigma$-finite measure space, and suppose given a measure $\nu$ on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ that is absolutely continuous with respect to $\mu$. Then there exists a measurable nonnegative extended real-valued function $f$ on $X$ such that

$$\nu(E) = \int_E f \, d\mu, \quad E \in \mathbf{S}.$$

In other words, $\nu = \nu_f$ is the indefinite integral of $f$ with respect to $\mu$. The function $f$, which is unique in the sense that if $f_0$ is any other function satisfying the above, then $f = f_0$ a.e. $[\mu]$, is called the Radon--Nikodym derivative of $\nu$ with respect to $\mu$, and is frequently denoted by $d\nu/d\mu$.
