Two measures $\mu$ and $
u$ on the same measurable space are equivalent, denoted $\mu \equiv 
u$, when each is absolutely continuous with respect to the other. Equivalently, they share exactly the same collection of null sets. This notion refines absolute continuity into an equivalence relation that partitions measures into mutual absolute continuity classes. Equivalent measures arise naturally via the Radon--Nikodym theorem: if $d
u/d\mu$ is strictly positive almost everywhere, then $\mu$ and $
u$ are equivalent.
