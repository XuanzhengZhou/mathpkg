Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space, and suppose that $\nu$ is a measure on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ with the property that $\mu(E) = 0$ implies $\nu(E) = 0$ for every set $E$ in $\mathbf{S}$. Then we say that $\nu$ is absolutely continuous with respect to $\mu$, or absolutely continuous $[\mu]$, and we write

$$\nu \ll \mu.$$
