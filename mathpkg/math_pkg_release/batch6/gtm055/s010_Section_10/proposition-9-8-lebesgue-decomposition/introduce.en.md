The Lebesgue decomposition theorem states that any $\sigma$-finite measure $
u$ can be uniquely expressed as the sum $
u = 
u_1 + 
u_2$, where $
u_1$ is absolutely continuous with respect to a reference measure $\mu$ and $
u_2$ is singular with respect to $\mu$. This decomposition is fundamental: the absolutely continuous part $
u_1$ is characterized by the Radon--Nikodym theorem as having a density $d
u_1/d\mu$, while the singular part $
u_2$ is supported on a $\mu$-null set. Together, these results provide a complete structural picture of the relationship between two $\sigma$-finite measures.
