The Halmos--von Neumann theorem is a landmark classification result in measure theory. It asserts that, up to measure ring isomorphism, there is exactly one separable atom-free measure space for each possible total mass $\mu(X) \in [0, +\infty]$, namely the standard Lebesgue--Borel measure on the interval $[0, \mu(X))$. This theorem unifies the structural theory of measure spaces: every separable non-atomic measure space is essentially an interval with Lebesgue measure. A striking consequence is that, for example, the unit square and the unit interval, both equipped with Lebesgue measure, are isomorphic as measure spaces.
