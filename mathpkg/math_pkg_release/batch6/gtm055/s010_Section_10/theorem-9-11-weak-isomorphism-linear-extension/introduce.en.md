Theorem 9.11 extends a weak measure ring isomorphism $\Phi$ between the measure rings of two $\sigma$-finite measure spaces to a linear isomorphism $T_\Phi$ between their spaces of (equivalence classes of) integrable functions. The extension is mediated by a nonnegative measurable density function $h$ on the target space. On characteristic functions of finite-measure sets, $T_\Phi$ acts by composition with the density $h$, and this determines a unique linear isomorphism between the $L^1$ spaces. This theorem, together with its counterpart Theorem 9.10 for full measure ring isomorphisms, establishes the deep connection between the algebraic structure of measure rings and the functional-analytic structure of integrable functions.
