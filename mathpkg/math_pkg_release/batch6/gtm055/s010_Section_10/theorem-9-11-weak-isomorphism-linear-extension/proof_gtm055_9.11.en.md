The proof proceeds by first establishing the result for the dense subspace $\dot{\mathcal{S}}_\mu$ of equivalence classes of integrable simple functions, then extending by continuity. The density function $h$ is constructed using the Radon--Nikodym theorem applied to the measure $\dot{
u} \circ \Phi$ on the measure ring, which is absolutely continuous with respect to $\dot{\mu}$. For a simple function $s = \sum_i c_i \chi_{E_i}$ with disjoint $E_i$ of finite measure, if $\Phi([E_i]) = [F_i]$, one defines $T_0([s]) = [\sum_i c_i h \chi_{F_i}]$. The key step is verifying that $T_0$ is an isometry between the dense subspaces $\dot{\mathcal{S}}_\mu$ and $\dot{\mathcal{S}}_
u$, which follows from the properties of $\Phi$ and $h$ together with the definition of the integral. Since $\dot{\mathcal{S}}_\mu$ is dense in $\dot{\mathcal{L}}_\mu$, the isometry extends uniquely to a linear isomorphism $T_\Phi$ between the completions. Uniqueness follows from the fact that any linear transformation satisfying (i) must agree with $T_0$ on simple functions, and condition (ii) forces agreement on the whole space $\dot{\mathcal{L}}_\mu$.
