Let $\Phi$ be a weak measure ring isomorphism of the measure ring $(\dot{\mathbf{S}}, \dot{\mu})$ of a $\sigma$-finite measure space $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ onto the measure ring $(\dot{\mathbf{T}}, \dot{\nu})$ of a $\sigma$-finite measure space $(Y, \mathbf{T}, \nu)$. Then there exists a nonnegative measurable function $h$ on $Y$ and a linear isomorphism $T_\Phi$ of $\dot{\mathcal{L}}_\mu$ onto $\dot{\mathcal{L}}_\nu$ satisfying the following conditions:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $E$ is a set in $\mathbf{S}$ such that $\mu(E) < +\infty$, and if $\Phi([E]) = [F]$, then $T_\Phi([\chi_E]) = [h\chi_F]$,
\item[(ii)] If $[f] \in \dot{\mathcal{L}}_\mu$ and $T_\Phi([f]) = [g]$, then $[|g|] = [h \cdot (|f| \circ \Phi^{-1})]$.
\end{enumerate}

Moreover, $T_\Phi$ is uniquely determined by conditions (i) and (ii).
