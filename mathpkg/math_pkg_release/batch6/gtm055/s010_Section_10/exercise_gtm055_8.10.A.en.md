Let $(X, \mathbf{S}_1)$ and $(X, \mathbf{S}_2)$ be two measurable spaces with the same carrier $X$, and let $\mathcal{D}$ be a collection of sets belonging to both $\mathbf{S}_1$ and $\mathbf{S}_2$ that is closed with respect to the formation of finite intersections. Suppose $\varphi_1$ and $\varphi_2$ are signed [complex] measures on $(X, \mathbf{S}_1)$ and $(X, \mathbf{S}_2)$, respectively, such that $\varphi_1(E)$ and $\varphi_2(E)$ are finite and equal for every set $E$ in $\mathcal{D}$. Show that $\varphi_1 = \varphi_2$ on the entire $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}(\mathcal{D})$. (Hint: If $E_0$ is any one fixed set in $\mathcal{D}$, and if $\mathcal{Q}$ denotes the collection of all those subsets $F$ of $E_0$ such that $\varphi_1(F) = \varphi_2(F)$, then $\mathcal{Q}$ is a $\sigma$-quasiring containing the trace $\mathcal{D}_{E_0}$. Recall Problems 6D and 6K.)
