The Riesz Representation Theorem (Theorem 18.4) identifies the dual space of continuous real-valued functions $C_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ on a compact Hausdorff space $X$ with the space of regular signed Borel measures. The proof proceeds by extending a positive linear functional defined on nonnegative functions to all of $C_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ and showing homogeneity and additivity. The correspondence $\mu \mapsto \varphi_\mu$ is an isometric isomorphism of Banach spaces, establishing the fundamental duality between measures and continuous functions.
