Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space and let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space. A mapping $\Phi: X \to \mathcal{E}$ is measurable if it is the pointwise limit (in norm) of a sequence of $\mathcal{E}$-valued simple functions. The mapping $\Phi$ is weakly measurable if $x \mapsto f(\Phi(x))$ is measurable for every $f \in \mathcal{E}^*$. If $\mathcal{E}$ is separable, then every weakly measurable mapping is measurable. Moreover, if $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ are measurable mappings into $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{E}^*$ respectively, then $x \mapsto \Psi(x)(\Phi(x))$ is measurable.
