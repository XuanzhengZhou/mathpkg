The polynomially convex hull $\hat{K}$ of a compact set $K \subset \mathbb{C}$ is obtained by filling in all the "holes" of $K$ -- equivalently, it is the complement of the unbounded component of $\mathbb{C} \setminus K$. This concept is fundamental in polynomial approximation theory because polynomials cannot distinguish between a point inside a hole and points on the boundary. The hull $\hat{K}$ plays a role analogous to the convex hull in real approximation theory.
