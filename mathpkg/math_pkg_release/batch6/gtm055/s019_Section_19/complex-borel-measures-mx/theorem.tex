Let $X$ be a locally compact Hausdorff space, and let $\mathcal{B}$ denote the $\sigma$-ring of Borel subsets of $X$. If $\xi$ and $\zeta$ are complex Borel measures on $X$ the sum $\xi + \zeta$ is defined setwise:
$$(\xi + \zeta)(E) = \xi(E) + \zeta(E), \quad E \in \mathcal{B}.$$
Similarly, if $\alpha$ is a complex number, then $\alpha\xi$ is defined setwise:
$$(\alpha\xi)(E) = \alpha\xi(E), \quad E \in \mathcal{B}.$$
The collection of all complex Borel measures on $X$, which is clearly a linear space with respect to these setwise linear operations, will be denoted by $\mathcal{M}(X)$.
