If $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ is a finite measure space, and if $p$ and $p'$ are extended real numbers such that $1 \leq p < p' \leq +\infty$, then $\mathcal{L}_{p'}(X)$ is contained (as a subset) in $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$ (Ex. A). Show that the inclusion mapping $i$ of $\mathcal{L}_{p'}(X)$ into $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$ is bounded. Conclude that $\mathcal{L}_{p'}(X)$ is a dense, first category, linear submanifold of $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$ (except when $X$ consists entirely of a finite number of atoms, in which case $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$ and $\mathcal{L}_{p'}(X)$ are simply $\mathbb{C}^n$ in two different norms). (Hint: Use the Hölder inequality to show that $\| i \| = \mu(X)^{(p' - p)/pp'}$ and recall Problem 8S.)
