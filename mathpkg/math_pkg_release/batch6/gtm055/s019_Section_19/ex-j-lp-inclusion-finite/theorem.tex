Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a finite measure space and let $1 \leq p < p' \leq +\infty$. Then $\mathcal{L}_{p'}(X)$ is contained (as a subset) in $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$, and the inclusion mapping $i: \mathcal{L}_{p'}(X) \to \mathcal{L}_p(X)$ is bounded with norm
$$\|i\| \leq \mu(X)^{(p' - p)/pp'}.$$
Consequently, $\mathcal{L}_{p'}(X)$ is a dense, first category linear submanifold of $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$ unless $X$ consists of finitely many atoms (in which case both spaces are $\mathbb{C}^n$ in different norms).
