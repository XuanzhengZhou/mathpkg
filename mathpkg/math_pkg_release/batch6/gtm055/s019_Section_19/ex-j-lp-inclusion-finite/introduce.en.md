On a finite measure space, higher $L_p$ exponents give smaller spaces: $\mathcal{L}_{p'} \subset \mathcal{L}_p$ for $p < p'$. The inclusion is bounded with norm controlled by the total measure. Unless the space consists of finitely many atoms, the smaller space is a dense, first-category subset of the larger one. This result follows from the Holder inequality and characterizes the relative topological sizes of $L_p$ spaces.
