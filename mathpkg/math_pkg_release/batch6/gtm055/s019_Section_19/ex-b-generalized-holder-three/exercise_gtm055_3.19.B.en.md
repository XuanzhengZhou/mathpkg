Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space, and suppose $p, q,$ and $r$ are positive real numbers such that $1/p + 1/q + 1/r = 1$. Show that if $f, g,$ and $h$ are elements of $\mathcal{L}_p(X), \mathcal{L}_q(X),$ and $\mathcal{L}_r(X),$ respectively, then

$$\| fgh \|_1 \leq \| f \|_p \| g \|_q \| h \|_r.$$

(Hint: If $s = 1 - 1/r$, then $ps$ and $qs$ are Hölder conjugates, as are $r$ and $1/s,$ and $|f|^{1/s}$ and $|g|^{1/s}$ belong to $\mathcal{L}_{ps}(X)$ and $\mathcal{L}_{qs}(X),$ respectively.) Show, in general, that if $f_i \in \mathcal{L}_{p_i}(X), i = 1, \ldots, k,$ where $1/p_1 + \cdots + 1/p_k = 1,$ then $\| f_1 \cdots f_k \|_1 \leq \| f_1 \|_{p_1} \cdots \| f_k \|_{p_k}.$
