Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a finite measure space and suppose that $1 \leq p < +\infty$. Let $g$ be a measurable function on $X$ with the property that the product $fg$ is integrable $[\mu]$ whenever $f \in L_p(X)$. Then $g$ must be an element of $L_q(X)$, where $1/p + 1/q = 1$ (if $p = 1$, then $q = +\infty$; if $p = +\infty$, then $q = 1$). This extends to $\sigma$-finite measure spaces.
