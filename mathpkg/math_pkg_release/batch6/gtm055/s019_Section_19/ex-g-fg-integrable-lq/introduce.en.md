Exercise G proves a partial converse to the Hölder inequality: if multiplication by a fixed measurable function $g$ maps all of $L_p$ into $L_1$ (i.e., $fg$ is integrable for every $f \in L_p$), then $g$ necessarily belongs to $L_q$. The proof uses truncation to reduce to the bounded case, where the norm of the functional $f \mapsto \int fg \, d\mu$ on $L_p(X)$ is precisely $\|g\|_q$, as established in Theorem 17.5.
