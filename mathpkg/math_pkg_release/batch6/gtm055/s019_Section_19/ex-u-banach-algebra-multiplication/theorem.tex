Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a Banach algebra. The multiplication operation $(a, b) \mapsto ab$ is a bounded bilinear mapping from $\mathcal{A} \times \mathcal{A}$ to $\mathcal{A}$ satisfying $\|ab\| \leq \|a\| \|b\|$ for all $a, b \in \mathcal{A}$. Consequently, multiplication is jointly continuous and extends to a bounded bilinear mapping on the completion. If $\mathcal{A}$ is unital with identity $1$, then $\|1\| \geq 1$, and $\mathcal{A}$ can be renormed equivalently so that $\|1\| = 1$ while preserving the Banach algebra structure.
