In a Banach algebra, multiplication is a bounded bilinear operation satisfying the norm inequality $\|ab\| \leq \|a\| \|b\|$. This joint continuity of multiplication is a defining feature that distinguishes Banach algebras from mere Banach spaces. The submultiplicativity of the norm can always be arranged so that the identity (if present) has norm one.
