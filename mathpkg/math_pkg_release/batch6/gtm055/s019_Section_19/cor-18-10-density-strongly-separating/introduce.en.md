Corollary 18.10 is the practical form of the Stone-Weierstrass Theorem. It says that the algebra generated by a strongly separating family of functions (and their complex conjugates, in the complex case) is always dense in $C(X)$. This directly yields the classical Weierstrass theorem by taking $\mathcal{F} = \{t, 1\}$ on $[a, b]$, showing polynomials are dense in $C([a, b])$.
