In both cases the system described is a self-conjugate strongly separating subalgebra. Taking its closure yields a closed, self-conjugate, strongly separating subalgebra, which by the Stone-Weierstrass Theorem (Theorem 18.5) must equal $\mathcal{C}(X)$ (respectively $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$). Hence the original system is dense.
