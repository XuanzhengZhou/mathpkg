Corollary 18.12 gives a clean criterion for when polynomials (or locally analytic functions) are dense in all continuous functions on a compact set $K \subset \mathbb{C}$. The condition is simply that the complex conjugate function $\bar{\lambda}$ be approximable by polynomials on $K$. This follows from the Stone-Weierstrass theorem, since the algebra generated by polynomials and $\bar{\lambda}$ is self-conjugate and strongly separating.
