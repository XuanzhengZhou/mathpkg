Proposition 18.13 reveals a fundamental fact about uniform limits of polynomials on compact sets: any such limit automatically extends analytically across the holes of $K$. This follows from the maximum modulus principle: a sequence of polynomials converging uniformly on $K$ also converges uniformly on the outer boundary, and hence on the entire polynomially convex hull $\hat{K}$. This explains why polynomial approximation fails when $K$ has holes -- the limit would need to be analytic inside the holes.
