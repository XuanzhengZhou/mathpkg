(Theorem of Beppo–Levi). Let $(X, S, \mu)$ be a measure space, let $\{p_n\}$ be a sequence of extended real-valued functions defined and nonnegative a.e. $[\mu]$ and measurable $[\mu]$ on $X$, and let $p$ denote the (almost everywhere defined) pointwise sum $p(x) = \sum_n p_n(x)$. Then

$$\int_X p d\mu = \sum_n \int_X p_n d\mu.$$

In particular, $p$ is integrable $[\mu]$ if and only if all of the functions $p_n$ are integrable and the numerical series $\sum_n \int p_n d\mu$ is convergent in $\mathbb{R}$. Hence $\sum_n \int p_n d\mu < +\infty$ implies $\sum_n p_n(x) < +\infty$ at almost every point $x$.

Proof. Just as in the preceding proof it is easy to see that it is enough to treat the case in which the functions $p_n$ are defined and nonnegative everywhere on $X$. Set $f_n = p_1 + p_2 + \cdots + p_n, n = 1, 2, \ldots$, and apply the monotone convergence theorem. $\square$
