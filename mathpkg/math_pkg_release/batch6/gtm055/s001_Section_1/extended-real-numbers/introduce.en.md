The extended real number system adjoins $+\infty$ and $-\infty$ to $\mathbb{R}$, extending the order and (partially) the arithmetic operations. Some expressions such as $(+\infty) + (-\infty)$ remain undefined. The extended reals are widely used in measure and integration theory to handle suprema of unbounded sets and limits of monotone sequences.
