The positive and negative variations decompose the total variation of a function into its upward and downward contributions. For each partition, $v_+(\mathcal{P})$ sums the positive increments and $v_-(\mathcal{P})$ sums the negative increments (taken positively). These nets are monotone increasing under partition refinement, and their boundedness characterizes functions of bounded variation. The resulting decomposition $f(b) - f(a) = V_+(f; a, b) - V_-(f; a, b)$ is the Jordan decomposition of the net change of $f$.
