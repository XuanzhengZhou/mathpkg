The positive part $t^+ = t \vee 0$ and negative part $t^- = -(t \wedge 0)$ of a real number $t$ are nonnegative numbers satisfying $t^+ + t^- = |t|$ and $t^+ - t^- = t$. This decomposition of a real number into its positive and negative parts is a fundamental tool in real analysis, and the same notation generalizes to functions and measures.
