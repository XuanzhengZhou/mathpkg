For every pair of real numbers $s$ and $t$,
$$s \vee t = \frac{1}{2}[s + t + |s - t|], \quad s \wedge t = \frac{1}{2}[s + t - |s - t|].$$
If $t$ is a real number, the numbers $t \vee 0$ and $-(t \wedge 0)$ are called the \textit{positive} and \textit{negative parts} of $t$, and are denoted by $t^+$ and $t^-$, respectively. They are nonnegative and satisfy
$$t^+ + t^- = |t|, \quad t^+ - t^- = t.$$
