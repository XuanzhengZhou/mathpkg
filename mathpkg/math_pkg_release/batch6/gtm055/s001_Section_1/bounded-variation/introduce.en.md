A real-valued function on $[a, b]$ is of bounded variation if the sums of positive and negative increments over any partition remain bounded. The total variation $V(f; a, b)$ decomposes as $V_+ + V_-$, and the net change $f(b) - f(a)$ equals $V_+ - V_-$. Functions of bounded variation are also called rectifiable; their total variation is the "length" of the function as a curve.
