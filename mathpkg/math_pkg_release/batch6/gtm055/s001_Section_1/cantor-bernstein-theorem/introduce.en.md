The Cantor–Bernstein theorem (also called the Schröder–Bernstein theorem) states that if each of two sets can be injected into the other, then there exists a bijection between them. In cardinal arithmetic, this implies antisymmetry of the cardinal ordering. The proof uses the Banach–Knaster–Tarski fixed-point lemma applied to the mapping $\Phi(A) = X \setminus \psi(Y \setminus \varphi(A))$ on the power set of $X$.
