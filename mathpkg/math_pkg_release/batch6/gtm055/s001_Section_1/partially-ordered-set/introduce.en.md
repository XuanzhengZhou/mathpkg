The authors review standard terminology for partially ordered sets (posets). An element is maximal (minimal) if no strictly larger (smaller) element exists. A subset has an upper bound if some element dominates it; the least such upper bound is the supremum ($\sup$). The dual notions are lower bound and infimum ($\inf$). A poset is bounded when it has both upper and lower bounds. For finite subsets the notations $x_1 \vee \cdots \vee x_n$ and $x_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_n$ abbreviate supremum and infimum respectively.
