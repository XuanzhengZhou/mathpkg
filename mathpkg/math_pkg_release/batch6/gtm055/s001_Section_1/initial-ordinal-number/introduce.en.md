The initial ordinal number of an infinite cardinal $c$ is the smallest ordinal having that cardinality. Thus $\omega$ is the initial ordinal of $\aleph_0$, and $\Omega$ is the initial ordinal of $\aleph_1$ (the first uncountable cardinal). A crucial property is that every countable subset of $W(\Omega)$ is bounded, which follows because otherwise $W(\Omega)$ would be a countable union of countable sets and therefore countable. This boundedness property is fundamental in transfinite induction arguments throughout analysis.
