The Banach--Knaster--Tarski lemma states that every monotone increasing self-map of a complete lattice admits a fixed point. The proof constructs the fixed point as the infimum of the set $A = \{x \in X : \varphi(x) \leq x\}$, showing that this infimum belongs to $A$ and is fixed by $\varphi$. The result is a cornerstone of order-theoretic fixed point theory.
