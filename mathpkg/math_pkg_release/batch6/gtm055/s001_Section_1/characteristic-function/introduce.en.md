The characteristic function $\chi_A$ of a subset $A \subset X$ takes the value $1$ on $A$ and $0$ on $X \setminus A$. Characteristic functions translate set-theoretic operations into algebraic ones: intersection becomes pointwise product or minimum, union becomes maximum, and inclusion corresponds to pointwise inequality. This correspondence is fundamental in measure theory and functional analysis.
