If $M$ is bounded, then $M$ is clearly weakly bounded. Conversely, if $M$ is weakly bounded, and if $j$ denotes the natural embedding of $\mathcal{E}$ in $\mathcal{E}^{**}$, then $j(M)$ is, by hypothesis, pointwise bounded on $\mathcal{E}^{*}$, and is therefore bounded in $\mathcal{E}^{**}$ by the uniform boundedness theorem (Th. 12.15). Since $j$ is an isometry, the proposition follows. (Note that $\mathcal{E}^{*}$ is always complete, whether $\mathcal{E}$ itself is complete or not; see Proposition 12.2.)
