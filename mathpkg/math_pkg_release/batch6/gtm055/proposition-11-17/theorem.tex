Let $\sigma$ be a pseudonorm on a linear space $\mathcal{E}$, and let $\mathcal{Z}$ denote the zero space with respect to $\sigma$. Then, in the topology induced on $\mathcal{E}$ by $\sigma$, $\mathcal{Z}$ is a closed linear manifold in $\mathcal{E}$ coinciding with the closure $(0)^-$ of the trivial linear manifold $(0)$. If $x$ and $y$ are congruent vectors modulo $\mathcal{Z}$ (that is, if $x - y \in \mathcal{Z}$), then $\sigma(x) = \sigma(y)$. Hence, if for each vector $x$ in $\mathcal{E}$ we denote by $\dot{x}$ the coset $x + \mathcal{Z}$, then $\|\dot{x}\| = \sigma(x)$ defines unambiguously a nonnegative function on the quotient space $\mathcal{E} = \mathcal{E}/\mathcal{Z}$, and this function is a norm on $\mathcal{E}$. (The norm $\|\cdot\|$ is called the norm associated with $\sigma$, and $\dot{\mathcal{E}}$ equipped with the associated norm $\|\cdot\|$ is called the normed space associated with the given space $\mathcal{E}$ and pseudonorm $\sigma$.) Finally, the metric defined by the associated norm $\|\cdot\|$ coincides with the metric associated with the invariant pseudometric defined by $\sigma$ (Prob. 4G).
