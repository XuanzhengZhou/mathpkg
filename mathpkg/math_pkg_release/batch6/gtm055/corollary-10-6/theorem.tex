If $X$ is a locally compact Hausdorff space that is metrizable, then the sets belonging to the $\sigma$-ring $S(\mathcal{H})$ generated by the collection $\mathcal{H}$ of compact sets in $X$ are all regular with respect to every Borel measure on

Borel set $E$ in $W$, either $E$ or $W \setminus E$ contains some closed unbounded subset of $W$ (Prob. 6Q), and the collection $J$ of those Borel sets $E$ in $W$ such that $W \setminus E$ contains a closed unbounded subset of $W$ forms a $\sigma$-ideal in the $\sigma$-ring $B$ of Borel subsets of $W$. Hence, if $a$ denotes an arbitrary nonnegative extended real number, and if we define

$$\mu_a(E) = \begin{cases} 0, & E \in J, \\ a, & E \in B \setminus J, \end{cases}$$

then $\mu_a$ is a measure on $(W, B)$ with the property that $\mu_a(K) = 0$ for every compact set $K$ in $W$. Thus $\mu_a$ is a Borel measure on $W$, but $\mu_a$ is not regular unless $a = 0$ (in which case $\mu_a = 0$).

Whether a Borel measure is regular or not has profound consequences for the Lebesgue integral with respect to that measure.
