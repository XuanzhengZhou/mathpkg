Zermelo's Well-Ordering Theorem asserts that every set can be well-ordered, i.e., equipped with a total order such that every nonempty subset has a least element. This statement is equivalent to the Axiom of Choice. The theorem implies that every cardinal number is the cardinality of some ordinal number, providing a canonical representative (the initial ordinal) for each cardinal. It is a fundamental tool in transfinite induction and set-theoretic constructions throughout analysis and topology.
