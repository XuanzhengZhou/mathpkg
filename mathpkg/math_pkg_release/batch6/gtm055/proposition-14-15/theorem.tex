Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a locally convex topological linear space, let $C$ be a closed convex set in $\mathcal{E}$, and let $x_0$ be a vector in $\mathcal{E}$ that does not belong to $C$. Then there exists a linear functional $g$ in $\mathcal{E}^*$ and a real number $c$ such that $\text{Re } g(x) \leq c$ for all $x$ in $C$, while $\text{Re } g(x_0) > c$. Thus $C$ is the intersection of all of the closed half-spaces of $\mathcal{E}$ that contain $C$.
