Example H. Let $\{t_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ be a strictly increasing sequence of nonnegative numbers such that

$$t_0 = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{n} t_n = +\infty.$$

Let $B$ denote the $\sigma$-ring of Borel subsets of the half-line $[0, +\infty)$, and for each positive integer $n$ let $B_n$ denote the trace of $B$ on the interval $[t_{n-1}, t_n)$ (Prob. 6D). Then

$$([0, +\infty), B, \mu) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \oplus ([t_{n-1}, t_n), B_n, \mu | [t_{n-1}, t_n)),$$

where $\mu$ denotes Lebesgue–Borel measure on $[0, +\infty)$. More generally, if $(X, S, \mu)$ is an arbitrary measure space and if $\{E_n\}$ is any partition of $X$ into a countable collection of measurable sets, then

$$(X, S, \mu) = \sum_{n} \oplus (E_n, S_{E_n}, \mu | E_n).$$

Example I. Let $X$ be an arbitrary set, let $S$ denote the power class on $X$, and let $\kappa$ be the counting measure on $(X, S)$ (Ex. 7J). Then $(X, S, \kappa)$ is, in an obvious sense, the direct sum of the (self-indexed) family of all the singletons in $X$.
