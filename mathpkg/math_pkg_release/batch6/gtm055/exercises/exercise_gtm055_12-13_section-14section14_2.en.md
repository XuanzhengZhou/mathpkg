B. If a linear transformation $T$ of a normed space $\mathcal{E}$ into another normed space $\mathcal{F}$ is bounded on any nonempty open set, then $T$ is continuous. Hence, in particular, if the function $\| Tx \|$ is continuous (or even upper semi-continuous; Example 3K) at a single point $x_0$ of $\mathcal{E}$, then $T$ is bounded.
