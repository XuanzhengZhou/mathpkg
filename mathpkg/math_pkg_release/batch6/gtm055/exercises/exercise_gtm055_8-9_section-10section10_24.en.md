X. Let $\alpha$ be a complex-valued function of bounded variation on a real interval $[a, b]$, and let $\varphi$ be a bounded, complex-valued, Borel measurable function on $[a, b]$. Show that if the Riemann–Stieltjes integral $\int_a^b \varphi(t)d\alpha(t)$ exists (see Problem L), then

$$\int_a^b \varphi(t)d\alpha(t) = \int_{[a, b]} \varphi d\zeta_\alpha,$$

where $\zeta_\alpha$ is the Stieltjes–Borel measure associated with $\alpha$ (Ex. K). Using this fact, show that if the Riemann–Stieltjes integral of $\varphi$ with respect to $\alpha$ over $[a, b]$ exists, then

$$\left| \int_a^b \varphi(t)d\alpha(t) \right| \leq \int_{[a, b]} |\varphi(t)| d\mu_L \leq L(b) \max_{a \leq t \leq b} |\varphi(t)|,$$

where $L(t)$ denotes the total variation of $\alpha$ on the interval $[a, t]$.
