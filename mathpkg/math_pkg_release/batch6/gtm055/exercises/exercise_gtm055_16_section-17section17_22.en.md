In this chapter we continue our investigation of the interplay between a separated locally convex topological linear space $\mathcal{E}$ and its dual $\mathcal{E}^*$. The matters to be discussed here, however, are most useful and most easily understood in the context of Banach spaces (where a natural topology, viz., the norm topology, exists on $\mathcal{E}^*$); accordingly, we shall focus our attention principally on that important special case. However, in order not to restrict unnecessarily our treatment of duality theory, we shall formulate both theorems and definitions in the general context of locally convex spaces whenever that is convenient. The following concept is of basic importance.

Definition. Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a normed space with dual space $\mathcal{E}^*$, and let $j$ denote the natural embedding of $\mathcal{E}$ in the second dual $\mathcal{E}^{**}$ (Prop. 15.6). The space $\mathcal{E}$ is said to be reflexive if $j$ maps $\mathcal{E}$ onto $\mathcal{E}^{**}$, i.e., if the only bounded linear functionals on $\mathcal{E}^*$ are those defined by elements $x$ of $\mathcal{E}$ according to the formula

$$f \rightarrow f(x), \quad f \in \mathcal{E}^*.$$
