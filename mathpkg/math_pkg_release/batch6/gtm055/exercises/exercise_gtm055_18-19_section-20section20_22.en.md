V. Let $K$ be a compact subset of the complex plane, and let $V$ denote the set of all those complex numbers $\alpha$ such that the function $1/(\lambda - \alpha)$ belongs to the algebra $\mathcal{P}(K)$.

(i) Show that each hole in $K$ is either disjoint from $V$ or wholly contained in $V$. Show also that the unbounded component $U_\infty$ of $\mathbb{C} \setminus K$ is always contained in $V$, and conclude that $\mathcal{P}(\hat{K}) = \mathcal{R}(\hat{K})$. (Hint: Show that $V$ is both open and closed relative to $\mathbb{C} \setminus K$. To verify that $U_\infty \subset V$ use the fact that the series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \lambda^n/\alpha^{n+1}$ converges uniformly in $\lambda$ on $K$ for $|\alpha|$ sufficiently large. Recall that $\hat{K} = \mathbb{C} \setminus U_\infty$.) (ii) Use the fact that $U_\infty \subset V$ to give a different proof of Runge’s theorem on a simply connected domain (see Example K). (Hint: Recall the remark following Corollary 18.15.)

The following problem outlines an alternate proof of the Stone–Weierstrass theorem (Th. 18.5) based on Theorem 18.4 and the Krein–Mil’man theorem (Prob. 14T). This proof is due to L. de Branges [14].
