For every compact subset $K$ of $\mathbb{C}$, $\mathcal{P}(K)$ and $\mathcal{A}(K)$ are closed subalgebras of $\mathcal{C}(K)$ such that $\mathcal{P}(K) \subset \mathcal{A}(K)$.

Proof. Since every polynomial function clearly belongs to $\mathcal{A}(K)$, it suffices to prove that $\mathcal{P}(K)$ is a subalgebra of $\mathcal{C}(K)$ and that $\mathcal{A}(K)$ is closed. To show the former, let $f$ and $g$ be functions in $\mathcal{P}(K)$, and let $\{p_n\}$ and $\{q_n\}$ be sequences of polynomials such that $p_n \rightarrow f$ and $q_n \rightarrow g$ uniformly on $K$. Then $p_n q_n \rightarrow fg$ uniformly on $K$, so $fg \in \mathcal{P}(K)$. To show the latter, let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence in $\mathcal{A}(K)$ that converges in $\mathcal{C}(K)$ to a limit $g$. If $D = D_r(\alpha)$ is an open disc contained in $K^\circ$, then $\{f_n\}$ also converges uniformly to $g$ on $D$, and since each of the functions $f_n$ is analytic on $D$, it follows that $g$ is analytic on $D$ (see Problem 11U). But this shows that $g$ is locally analytic on $K^\circ$, and hence that $g \in \mathcal{A}(K)$.
