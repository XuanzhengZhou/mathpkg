Given a compact subset $K$ of an open set $U$ in the complex plane, one can find finitely many pairwise disjoint Jordan domains whose closures (Jordan regions) cover $K$ and remain within $U$. This proposition provides a fundamental approximation of compact planar sets by well-behaved Jordan domains, essential for constructing integration contours in complex analysis.
