A subspace $A$ of a compact Hausdorff space $X$ inherits the Hausdorff property automatically, but compactness of $A$ requires $A$ to be closed in $X$. This proposition gives a clean characterization: the compact subspaces of a compact Hausdorff space are exactly its closed subsets. The result is frequently used in analysis to pass between closedness and compactness in Hausdorff settings.
