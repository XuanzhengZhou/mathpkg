If $A$ is closed in the compact space $X$, then $A$ is compact (a closed subset of a compact space is compact). Since $X$ is Hausdorff, the subspace $A$ is also Hausdorff. Conversely, if $A$ is a compact subspace of the Hausdorff space $X$, then $A$ must be closed in $X$; this is a basic property of Hausdorff spaces (compact subsets of Hausdorff spaces are closed).
