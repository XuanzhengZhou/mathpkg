If $X$ is a topological space, then a net $\{x_\lambda\}_{\lambda \in \Lambda}$ in $X$ is said to \textbf{converge} to a point $x_0$ in $X$ and $x_0$ is said to be the \textbf{limit} of the net $\{x_\lambda\}$ (notation: $\lim_\lambda x_\lambda = x_0$ or $x_\lambda \rightarrow x_0$) if for every neighborhood $V$ of $x_0$ there exists an index $\lambda_V$, depending on $V$, such that $x_\lambda \in V$ for every $\lambda$ in $\Lambda$ such that $\lambda \geq \lambda_V$.
