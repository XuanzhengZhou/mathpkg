A net $\{x_\lambda\}$ in a topological space $X$ converges to $x_0$ if it eventually enters every neighborhood of $x_0$. Nets generalize sequences (which correspond to the directed set $\mathbb{N}$) and provide the correct notion of convergence for arbitrary topological spaces. Unlike sequences, nets can characterize continuity, closedness, and compactness in all topological spaces, not just those satisfying the first axiom of countability.
