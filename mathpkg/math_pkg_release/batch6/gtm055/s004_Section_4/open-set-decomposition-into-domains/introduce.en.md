Every open subset of the complex plane $\mathbb{C}$ decomposes uniquely into a countable collection of pairwise disjoint domains (nonempty connected open sets), which are exactly its connected components. The proof uses the second axiom of countability and the fact that discs are connected. This is the analogue for the plane of the decomposition of open subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ into open intervals.
