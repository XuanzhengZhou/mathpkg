Let $X$ be a set, and for each $\gamma$ in an index set $\Gamma$ let $f_{\gamma}$ be a mapping of $X$ into a topological space $Y_{\gamma}$. Then the \textbf{coarsest topology} on $X$ making all of the mappings $f_{\gamma}$ continuous is the \textbf{topology inversely induced on $X$ by the family} $\{f_{\gamma}\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$.

A subbase for this topology is given by the collection of all sets of the form $f_{\gamma}^{-1}(U)$, where $U$ is an open set in $Y_{\gamma}$ and $\gamma$ runs through $\Gamma$. Consequently a base is given by the collection of all sets of the form
$$f_{\gamma_{1}}^{-1}(U_{1}) \cap \cdots \cap f_{\gamma_{n}}^{-1}(U_{n})$$
where each $U_{i}$ is an open set in $Y_{\gamma_{i}}$ and $\{\gamma_{1}, \ldots, \gamma_{n}\}$ is an arbitrary finite subset of $\Gamma$.
