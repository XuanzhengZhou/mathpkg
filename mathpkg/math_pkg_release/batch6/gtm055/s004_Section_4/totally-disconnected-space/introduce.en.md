A totally disconnected space is one in which the only connected subsets are singletons. Every discrete space is totally disconnected, but the converse is false -- the Cantor set is a classic example of a totally disconnected space that is not discrete. These notions are important in the study of topological dimension theory and in the structure theory of compact Hausdorff spaces.
