Every open subset $U$ of the real line $\mathbb{R}$ decomposes uniquely into a countable collection of pairwise disjoint open intervals, which are precisely the connected components of $U$. This structural result relies on the fact that $\mathbb{R}$ satisfies the second axiom of countability, ensuring that an uncountable disjoint collection of nonempty open intervals cannot exist.
