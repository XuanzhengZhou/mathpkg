An arc in a topological space $Y$ is the image of a closed interval $[a, b]$ under a continuous map. Since $[a, b]$ is connected, the range of an arc is a connected subset of $Y$. Arcs generalize the intuitive notion of a continuous path and play a fundamental role in defining arcwise (path) connectedness.
