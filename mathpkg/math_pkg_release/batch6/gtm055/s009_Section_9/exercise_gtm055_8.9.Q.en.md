Show that an indexed family $\{\lambda_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ of complex numbers is summable (Ex. 3Q) when and only when the function $f(\gamma) = \lambda_\gamma$ is an integrable function on the index set $\Gamma$ with respect to the counting measure $\kappa$ on $\Gamma$ (Ex. J), and that if this is the case, then $\int_\Gamma f d\kappa = \sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} \lambda_\gamma$. Thus the summation of (summable) indexed families of complex numbers is a special case of Lebesgue integration. (Hint: It suffices to treat the case $\lambda_\gamma \geq 0$; use Proposition 7.2.)
