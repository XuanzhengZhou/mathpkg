Suppose $\mu(E) = +\infty$ and $\mu(F) = -\infty$. Then $-\infty < \mu(E \cap F) < +\infty$ by what has already been said, so $\mu(E \setminus F) = +\infty$ and $\mu(F \setminus E) = -\infty$, which is impossible because these are disjoint sets whose signed measures would be opposite infinities. The remaining assertions follow from similar set-theoretic arguments and countable additivity.
