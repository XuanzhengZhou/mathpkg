From a Hahn decomposition $X = A \cup B$ of a signed measure $\mu$, the positive variation $\mu_+$ and negative variation $\mu_-$ are genuine (nonnegative) measures that decompose $\mu = \mu_+ - \mu_-$. The positive variation assigns to $E$ the $\mu$-measure of $E \cap A$, while the negative variation assigns the negative of the $\mu$-measure of $E \cap B$. The independence from the choice of Hahn decomposition makes these variations well-defined invariants of the signed measure.
