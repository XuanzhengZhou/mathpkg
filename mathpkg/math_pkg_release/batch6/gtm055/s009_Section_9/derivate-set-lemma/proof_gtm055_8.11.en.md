For points $t$ where $D_+(t) > M$, we have $t \in R_M(f; U)$ whenever $t \in U$. Similarly, if $d_-(t) < m$, then $t \in L_m(f; U)$. Using Proposition 8.9, the sets $R_M(f; U)$ and $L_m(f; U)$ are open, and on each component $(c_k, d_k)$ of $R_M(f; U)$, we have $(f(d_k) - f(c_k))/(d_k - c_k) \geq M$. Since $f$ is increasing, summing over components and using the relationship with $L_m$ yields the measure estimate $\mu_1(V) \leq (m/M)\mu_1(U)$.
