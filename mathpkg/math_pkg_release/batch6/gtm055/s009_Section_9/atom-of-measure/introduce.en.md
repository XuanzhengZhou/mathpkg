An atom of a measure is a measurable set of positive measure that cannot be partitioned into two measurable subsets both of positive measure. Every measurable subset of an atom either has full measure or measure zero. Atoms represent indivisible units of measure and are central to understanding the structure of measure spaces, particularly in the characterization of locally finite measures.
