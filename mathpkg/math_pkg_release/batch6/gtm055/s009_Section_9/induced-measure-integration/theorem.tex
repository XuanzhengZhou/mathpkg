Let $(Y, \mathbf{T})$ be a measurable space, let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space, and let $\Phi$ be a mapping of $X$ into $Y$ that is measurable $[\mathbf{S}, \mathbf{T}]$. If $\nu$ denotes the measure induced on $Y$ by $\Phi$ and $\mu$, and if $f$ is a measurable function on $Y$ that is either integrable $[\nu]$ or nonnegative, then
$$\int_Y f d\nu = \int_X (f \circ \Phi) d\mu.$$
In particular, $f$ is integrable $[\nu]$ if and only if $f \circ \Phi$ is integrable $[\mu]$, and
$$\nu(E) = \mu(\Phi^{-1}(E)), \quad E \in \mathbf{T}.$$
