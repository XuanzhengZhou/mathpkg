Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space and let $f$ be a measurable complex-valued function on $X$.

(i) Show that there exists a radius $R \geq 0$ such that $\mu(f^{-1}(\mathbb{C} \setminus D_R(0)^{-})) = 0$ if and only if $f$ is essentially bounded, and that if $f$ is essentially bounded, then $\operatorname{ess sup}_X |f|$ is the smallest such radius. Show, in general, that the essential range of $f$ is the smallest closed set $F$ in $\mathbb{C}$ such that $\mu(f^{-1}(\mathbb{C} \setminus F)) = 0$.

(ii) Let $f$ be essentially bounded, and let $\dot{\mathcal{L}}_\mu$ denote the linear space of all equivalence classes $[g]$ of integrable functions $g$ on $X$.
