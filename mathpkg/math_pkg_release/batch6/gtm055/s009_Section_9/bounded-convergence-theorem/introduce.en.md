The Bounded Convergence Theorem (Theorem 7.13) is a special case of the Dominated Convergence Theorem for finite measure spaces. If a uniformly bounded sequence of measurable functions converges almost everywhere on a finite measure space, then the limit function is integrable and limits may be passed under the integral sign. The constant bound $M$ serves as the dominating function, which is integrable precisely because the measure is finite.
