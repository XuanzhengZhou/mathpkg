Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a finite measure space, and let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence of complex-valued functions measurable $[\mu]$ on $X$. Suppose that $\{f_n\}$ converges a.e. to a function $f$, and that there exists a real number $M$ such that $|f_n| \leq M$ a.e. for every $n$. Then the functions $f_n$ and $f$ are integrable $[\mu]$,
$$\lim_n \int_X |f_n - f| d\mu = 0,$$
and
$$\int_X f d\mu = \lim_n \int_X f_n d\mu.$$
