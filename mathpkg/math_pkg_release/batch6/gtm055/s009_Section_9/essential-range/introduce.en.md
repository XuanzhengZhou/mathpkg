The essential range of a measurable function $f$ on a measure space $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ captures the set of complex numbers $\lambda$ that $f$ cannot avoid on any set of positive measure. A number $\lambda$ belongs to the essential range if every $\varepsilon$-neighborhood of $\lambda$ has a preimage of positive $\mu$-measure. This refines the ordinary range by ignoring behavior on null sets.
