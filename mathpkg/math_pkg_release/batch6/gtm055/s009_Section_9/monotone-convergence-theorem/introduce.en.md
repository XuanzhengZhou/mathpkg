The Monotone Convergence Theorem (Theorem 7.9) is a cornerstone of Lebesgue integration. For a monotone increasing sequence of nonnegative measurable functions, the integral of the pointwise limit equals the limit of the integrals. It serves as the foundation for Fatou's Lemma and the Dominated Convergence Theorem, providing the primary mechanism for passing limits under the integral sign when integrands increase monotonically.
