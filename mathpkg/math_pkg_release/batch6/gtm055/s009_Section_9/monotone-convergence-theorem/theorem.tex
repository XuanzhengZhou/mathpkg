Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space, and let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence of extended real-valued functions that are defined and nonnegative a.e. $[\mu]$ and measurable $[\mu]$ on $X$. Suppose also that the sequence $\{f_n\}$ is monotone increasing a.e. $[\mu]$. Then
$$\int_X (\lim_n f_n) d\mu = \lim_n \int_X f_n d\mu.$$
In particular, $\lim_n f_n$ is integrable if and only if the numerical sequence $\{\int f_n d\mu\}$ is bounded above in $\mathbb{R}$.
