The Theorem of Beppo-Levi (Corollary 7.10) generalizes the Monotone Convergence Theorem to series of nonnegative measurable functions. The Lebesgue integral of a pointwise sum of nonnegative functions equals the sum of the individual integrals, so term-by-term integration is always valid for nonnegative series. The result also yields a key implication: if the sum of the integrals is finite, then the pointwise series converges almost everywhere.
