The direct sum construction combines a family of pairwise disjoint measurable spaces into one measurable space. The $\sigma$-ring consists of sets whose intersection with each component is measurable in that component. The direct sum measure is defined componentwise by summing the component measures of intersections. This construction allows measure spaces to be assembled from simpler pieces, such as partitioning the real half-line into intervals.
