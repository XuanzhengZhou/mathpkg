Signed measures and complex measures generalize ordinary (nonnegative) measures by allowing negative or complex values. A signed measure is an extended real-valued countably additive set function, while a complex measure is complex-valued. A crucial constraint: a signed measure cannot simultaneously attain both $+\infty$ and $-\infty$, ensuring countable sums remain well-defined. These generalizations are fundamental to the Hahn-Jordan decomposition and the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
