That $|\mu| = \mu_+ + \mu_-$ and $|\nu| = \nu_+ + \nu_-$ follows from the definitions of total variation for signed measures. The supremal definition of $|\zeta|(E)$ and the triangle inequality yield the stated inequalities. Since both $|\mu|$ and $|\nu|$ are finite measures (as $\mu$ and $\nu$ are finite signed measures), the inequalities imply $|\zeta|$ is also a finite measure. The detailed verification is left as an exercise (Problem U(ii)).
