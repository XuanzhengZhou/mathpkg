It is clear that $\mu_+$ and $\mu_-$ are measures, one necessarily finite (by Lemma 8.4), both finite ($\sigma$-finite) if $\mu$ is, and $\mu = \mu_+ - \mu_-$. For minimality: given measures $\nu_1, \nu_2$ with $\mu = \nu_1 - \nu_2$, let $\{A, B\}$ be a Hahn decomposition. For measurable $E \subset A$, $\mu(E) \geq 0$ yields $\nu_1(E) \geq \nu_2(E)$. For $E \subset B$, $\mu(E) \leq 0$ yields $\nu_1(E) \leq \nu_2(E)$. Define $\delta(E) = \nu_1(E) - \mu_+(E)$; one verifies $\delta$ is a finite measure satisfying the stated identities.
