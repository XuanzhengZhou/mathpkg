Theorem 8.6 establishes the Jordan decomposition theorem for signed measures. The positive and negative variations derived from a Hahn decomposition are genuine measures providing the unique minimal decomposition $\mu = \mu_+ - \mu_-$. Minimality means any other difference-of-measures representation contains $\mu_+$ and $\mu_-$ as summands plus a common finite measure $\delta$. This is the measure-theoretic analog of the Jordan decomposition of a function of bounded variation.
