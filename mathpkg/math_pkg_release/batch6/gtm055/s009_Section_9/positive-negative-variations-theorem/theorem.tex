The positive and negative variations $\mu_+$ and $\mu_-$ of a signed measure $\mu$ on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ are measures on $(X, \mathbf{S})$, one of which at least is finite, and $\mu = \mu_+ - \mu_-$ setwise. Moreover, $\mu_+$ and $\mu_-$ are minimal: if $\nu_1, \nu_2$ are any measures with $\mu = \nu_1 - \nu_2$, then there exists a finite measure $\delta$ on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ such that $\nu_1 = \mu_+ + \delta$ and $\nu_2 = \mu_- + \delta$. If $\mu$ is finite or $\sigma$-finite, then so are $\mu_+$ and $\mu_-$.
