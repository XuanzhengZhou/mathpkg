Suppose now that $M$ is a subset of $\mathcal{E}$ and that $\mathcal{M}$ is the smallest closed linear manifold in $\mathcal{E}$ that contains $M$. From what has already been shown it is clear that $M \subset a(M^a)$. On the other hand, if $x_0$ is any vector in $\mathcal{E}$ that does not belong to $\mathcal{M}$, then there is an element $f_0$ of $M^a$ such that $f_0(x_0) \neq 0$ (Prop. 14.13). But then $x_0 \notin a(M^a)$, and it follows that $\mathcal{M}$ and $a(M^a)$ coincide.

Similarly, if $M \subset \mathcal{E}^*$ and $\mathcal{M}$ denotes the smallest weak* closed linear submanifold of $\mathcal{E}^*$ that contains $M$, then it is clear that $M \subset (aM)^a$. But again, if $f_0$ is any element of $\mathcal{E}^*$ that does not belong to $\mathcal{M}$, then there exists a vector $x_0$ in $aM$ such that $f_0(x_0) \neq 0$. Thus $f_0 \notin (aM)^a$, and it follows that $\mathcal{M} = (aM)^a$. (Recall (Prob. 15J) that the weak* continuous linear functionals on $\mathcal{E}^*$ are precisely the ones of the form $F_x$, $x \in \mathcal{E}$.)
