If $\mathcal{L}$ is a linear manifold in a topological linear space $\mathcal{E}$, then $\mathcal{L}^-$ is also a linear manifold in $\mathcal{E}$.

Proof. Let $x$ and $y$ be vectors in $\mathcal{L}^-$ and suppose $V$ is a neighborhood of $x + y$. There exist neighborhoods $W_1$ and $W_2$ of $x$ and $y$, respectively, such that $W_1 + W_2 \subset V$. Since both $W_1$ and $W_2$ contain vectors belonging to $\mathcal{L}$, this shows that $V$ contains a vector belonging to $\mathcal{L}$, and it follows that $x + y \in \mathcal{L}^-$. Similarly, one shows that $\mathcal{L}^-$ is closed with respect to multiplication by scalars.
