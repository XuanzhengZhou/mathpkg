The Open Mapping Theorem is one of the three fundamental principles of functional analysis (along with the Uniform Boundedness Principle and the Hahn-Banach Theorem). It states that every continuous linear surjection between $F$-spaces is an open mapping. An immediate and important consequence is that a continuous linear bijection between $F$-spaces automatically has a continuous inverse, i.e., it is a topological isomorphism.
