Proposition 14.12 extends the separation results of Lemma 14.10 and Corollary 14.11 from normed spaces to spaces equipped with pseudonorms. It guarantees that even when the topology is induced by a pseudonorm (rather than a norm), one can still separate a point from a closed subspace using a linear functional that is bounded with respect to that pseudonorm. This is essential for the duality theory of locally convex spaces where topologies are defined by families of pseudonorms.
