Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a linear space equipped with a pseudonorm $\sigma$, let $\mathcal{M}$ be a linear submanifold of $\mathcal{E}$, and let $x_0$ be a vector in $\mathcal{E}$ not belonging to the $\sigma$-closure of $\mathcal{M}$. Then there exists a linear functional $f$ on $\mathcal{E}$, bounded with respect to $\sigma$, such that $f(\mathcal{M}) = (0)$, $f(x_0) = 1$, and the norm of $f$ with respect to $\sigma$ equals $1/d_\sigma(x_0, \mathcal{M})$, where $d_\sigma$ denotes the distance measured via $\sigma$.
