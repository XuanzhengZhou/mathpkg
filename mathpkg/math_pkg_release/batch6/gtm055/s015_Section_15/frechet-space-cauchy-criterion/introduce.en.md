Proposition 14.9 provides a practical criterion for verifying that a space with a separating sequence of pseudonorms is a Frechet space. Rather than working with the constructed quasinorm, one checks completeness pseudonorm by pseudonorm: a sequence is Cauchy/convergent in the space topology iff it is Cauchy/convergent with respect to each individual pseudonorm. This is frequently used in analysis, e.g., for spaces of smooth functions with topologies of uniform convergence of all derivatives on compact sets.
