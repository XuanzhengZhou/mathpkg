The Closed Graph Theorem is one of the cornerstones of functional analysis, alongside the Open Mapping Theorem and the Uniform Boundedness Principle. It provides a remarkably simple criterion for continuity: a linear transformation between $F$-spaces is continuous if and only if its graph is a closed subset of the direct sum $\mathcal{E} \oplus_1 \mathcal{F}$. This is especially useful because verifying that an operator has a closed graph is often simpler than directly establishing continuity.
