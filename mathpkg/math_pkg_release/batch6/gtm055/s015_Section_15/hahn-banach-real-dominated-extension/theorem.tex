Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a real linear space, and let $p$ be a real-valued function on $\mathcal{E}$ satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $p(x + y) \leq p(x) + p(y)$ for all $x, y \in \mathcal{E}$,
\item[(ii)] $p(tx) = tp(x)$ for all $x \in \mathcal{E}$ and $t > 0$.
\end{enumerate}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a linear manifold in $\mathcal{E}$ and $f$ a linear functional on $\mathcal{M}$ satisfying $f(x) \leq p(x)$ for all $x \in \mathcal{M}$. Then there exists a linear extension $f_0$ of $f$ to $\mathcal{E}$ satisfying $f_0(x) \leq p(x)$ for all $x \in \mathcal{E}$.
