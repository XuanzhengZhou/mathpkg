Assume $\mathcal{M} \neq \mathcal{E}$. Let $x_0 \in \mathcal{E} \setminus \mathcal{M}$ and let $\mathcal{M}_0$ be the linear submanifold generated by $\mathcal{M}$ and $x_0$. Every $z \in \mathcal{M}_0$ can be uniquely expressed as $z = y + t x_0$. If $g$ extends $f$ to $\mathcal{M}_0$, then $g(z) = f(y) + tr$ where $r = g(x_0)$. The condition $g(z) \leq p(z)$ is equivalent to $f(y) + tr \leq p(y + t x_0)$. For $t > 0$ this gives $r \leq p(y/t + x_0) - f(y/t)$; for $t < 0$ (set $t = -s$, $s > 0$) this gives $r \geq -p(y/s - x_0) + f(y/s)$. Setting $r_1 = \sup_{y \in \mathcal{M}} \{-p(y - x_0) + f(y)\}$ and $r_2 = \inf_{y \in \mathcal{M}} \{p(y + x_0) - f(y)\}$, one shows $r_1 \leq r_2$ using subadditivity of $p$. Any $r \in [r_1, r_2]$ works. The extension to the whole space is completed by Zorn's lemma.
