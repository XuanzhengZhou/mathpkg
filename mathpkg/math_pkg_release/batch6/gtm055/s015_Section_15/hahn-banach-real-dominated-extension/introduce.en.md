Proposition 14.6 is a powerful formulation of the Hahn-Banach theorem for real vector spaces. Instead of requiring a norm, it only needs a sublinear functional $p$ (positively homogeneous and subadditive) that dominates the given linear functional on the subspace. This version does not assume any topology, making it purely algebraic. It serves as the foundation for separation theorems for convex sets.
