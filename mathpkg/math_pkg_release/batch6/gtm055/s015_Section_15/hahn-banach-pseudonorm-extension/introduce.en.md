Proposition 14.4 generalizes the classical Hahn-Banach theorem from normed spaces to spaces equipped with pseudonorms. It guarantees that any linear functional bounded with respect to a pseudonorm on a subspace can be extended to the whole space while preserving the pseudonorm-bound. This is essential for the study of locally convex spaces where topologies are induced by families of pseudonorms rather than a single norm.
