Let $\mathcal{F}$ be an arbitrary infinite dimensional Banach space, and let $\{x_{\gamma}\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ be an indexed Hamel basis for $\mathcal{F}$ consisting of unit vectors. Consider the normed space $\mathcal{L}$ consisting of all similarly indexed families of complex numbers $\{\lambda_{\gamma}\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ with the property that $\lambda_{\gamma} = 0$ except for a finite number of indices $\gamma$, equipped with the norm $\|\{\lambda_{\gamma}\}\| = \sum_{\gamma} |\lambda_{\gamma}|$. The linear transformation $T$ of $\mathcal{L}$ onto $\mathcal{F}$ defined by

$$T(\{\lambda_{\gamma}\}) = \sum_{\gamma} \lambda_{\gamma} x_{\gamma}, \quad \{\lambda_{\gamma}\} \in \mathcal{L},$$

is bounded but is not an open mapping.
