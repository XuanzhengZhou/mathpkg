For each nonzero vector $x_0$ in a normed space $\mathcal{E}$ there exists a linear functional $f$ in $\mathcal{E}^*$ such that $\|f\| = 1$ and $f(x_0) = \|x_0\|$. Consequently, if $x$ and $y$ are distinct vectors in $\mathcal{E}$, there exists a functional $f$ in $\mathcal{E}^*$ such that $f(x) \neq f(y)$.
