A lattice is a partially ordered set in which every pair of elements possesses both a supremum (join, denoted $x \vee y$) and an infimum (meet, denoted $x \wedge y$). The operations $\vee$ and $\wedge$ are binary operations on the lattice satisfying idempotence, commutativity, associativity, and the absorption laws. Lattices generalize the structure of subsets under union and intersection, and real numbers under max and min.
