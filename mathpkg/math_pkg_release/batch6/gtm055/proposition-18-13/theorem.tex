If $K$ is a compact subset of $\mathbb{C}$ then the algebra $\mathcal{P}(K)$ coincides with the restriction to $K$ of the subalgebra $\mathcal{P}(\hat{K})$ of $\mathcal{C}(\hat{K})$. Thus the functions in $\mathcal{P}(K)$ are not only locally analytic on $K^{\circ}$, but can be extended so as to be locally analytic on the interior of $\hat{K}$.
