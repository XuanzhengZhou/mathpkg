The intersection of an arbitrary nonempty collection of subspaces of a normed space $\mathcal{E}$ is again a subspace of $\mathcal{E}$. Consequently, given an arbitrary subset $M$ of $\mathcal{E}$, there exists a smallest subspace $M$ of $\mathcal{E}$ that contains $M$.

Proof. An intersection of linear manifolds in a linear space is a linear manifold, and an intersection of closed sets in a topological space is a closed set. Hence an intersection of subspaces of $\mathcal{E}$ is a subspace of $\mathcal{E}$.
