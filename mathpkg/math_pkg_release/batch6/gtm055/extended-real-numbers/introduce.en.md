The extended real number system augments the real numbers with two symbols $+\infty$ and $-\infty$, providing a compact framework for handling suprema, infima, and limits in analysis and measure theory. Arithmetic operations are extended partially: sums and products involving like-signed infinities are defined, while indeterminate forms such as $\infty - \infty$ remain undefined. The extended reals form a complete lattice, ensuring that every subset has a supremum and infimum, which is essential for the theory of integration and measure.
