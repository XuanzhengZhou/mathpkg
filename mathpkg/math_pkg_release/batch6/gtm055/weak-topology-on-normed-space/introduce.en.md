The weak topology on a normed space $\mathcal{E}$ is the coarsest topology that makes every norm-continuous linear functional continuous. It is refined by the norm topology (hence the term "weak") and is induced by the family of seminorms $\sigma_f(x) = |f(x)|$ for $f \in \mathcal{E}^*$. The weak topology turns $\mathcal{E}$ into a locally convex space.
