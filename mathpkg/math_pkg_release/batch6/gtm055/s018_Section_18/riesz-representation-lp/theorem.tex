Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space, let $p$ and $q$ be Holder conjugates with $1 < p < +\infty$, and suppose that $g$ is an element of $\mathcal{L}_q(X)$. Then the formula

$$\varphi_g(f) = \int_X f g \, d\mu, \quad f \in \mathcal{L}_p(X),$$

defines a bounded linear functional $\varphi_g$ on $\mathcal{L}_p(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$, and $\|\varphi_g\| = \|g\|_q$. Conversely, every bounded linear functional on $\mathcal{L}_p(X)$ is of the form $\varphi_g$ for some uniquely determined $g$ in $\mathcal{L}_q(X)$.
