The Cauchy--Goursat theorem generalizes to Banach-space-valued functions: if $\Phi: U \to \mathcal{E}$ is locally analytic (in the sense of norm-differentiability), then its contour integral around any triangle in $U$ vanishes. This implies the existence of local primitives and infinite differentiability of the function, extending the classical scalar theory to the vector-valued setting.
