Let $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ and $\langle \mathcal{E}', \mathcal{F}' \rangle$ be dual pairs, and let $T$ be a linear transformation of $\mathcal{E}$ into $\mathcal{E}'$. Show that there exists a mapping $T^*: \mathcal{F}' \rightarrow \mathcal{F}$ satisfying the condition

$$\langle Tx, y' \rangle = \langle x, T^*y' \rangle, \quad x \in \mathcal{E}, \quad y' \in \mathcal{F}'$$

if and only if $T$ is weakly continuous, i.e., continuous when $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{E}'$ are given the topologies induced by $\mathcal{F}$ and $\mathcal{F}'$, respectively. Show also that if $T$ is weakly continuous then $T^*$ is a uniquely determined linear transformation. (The mapping $T^*$ is called the adjoint of $T$.)

(i) Verify that the adjoint $T^*$ is in turn weakly continuous with respect to the reversed dual pairs $\langle \mathcal{F}', \mathcal{E}' \rangle$ and $\langle \mathcal{F}, \mathcal{E} \rangle$.
