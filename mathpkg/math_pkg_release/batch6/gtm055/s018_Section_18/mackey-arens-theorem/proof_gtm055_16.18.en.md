Suppose first that the topology $\mathcal{T}$ is induced by such a collection of sets, and let $\varphi$ be a linear functional on $\mathcal{E}$ that is continuous with respect to $\mathcal{T}$. Then, since $\mathcal{A}$ is admissible, there exists a set $A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ such that $\varphi$ is bounded on $0^0 A$, and, by the preceding lemma (applied to the space $\mathcal{F}$ in the topology induced by $\mathcal{E}$), the functional $\varphi$ is one of those defined on $\mathcal{E}$ by some vector in $\mathcal{F}$. Thus $\mathcal{T}$ is a topology of the dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$, and one half of the theorem is proved. In view of Proposition 16.20, to prove the other half it suffices to establish the converse.
