Let $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ be a dual pair. The Mackey topology on $\mathcal{E}$ with respect to this dual pair is defined as the topology of uniform convergence on the collection of all those absolutely convex subsets of $\mathcal{F}$ that are compact in the topology induced by $\mathcal{E}$. Verify that if $\mathcal{E}$ is a separated locally convex space, then the Mackey topology on $\mathcal{E}$ with respect to the dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{E}^* \rangle$ agrees with the Mackey topology as defined in Example 15K.
