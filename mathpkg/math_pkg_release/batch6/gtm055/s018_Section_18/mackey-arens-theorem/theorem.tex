A locally convex linear topology $\mathcal{T}$ on the space $\mathcal{E}$ of a dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ is a topology of that dual pair if and only if it is the topology of uniform convergence on the sets of some admissible collection of absolutely convex subsets of $\mathcal{F}$ each of which is compact in the topology induced on $\mathcal{F}$ by $\mathcal{E}$.
