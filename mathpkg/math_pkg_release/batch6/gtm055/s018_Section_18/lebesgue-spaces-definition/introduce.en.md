The Lebesgue spaces $\mathcal{L}_p(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ for $1 \leq p \leq +\infty$ are the normed spaces obtained from measurable functions by identifying functions that agree almost everywhere. They generalize the classical sequence spaces $\ell_p$ by replacing the counting measure on $\mathbb{N}_0$ with an arbitrary measure $\mu$. These spaces play a central role in functional analysis and integration theory.
