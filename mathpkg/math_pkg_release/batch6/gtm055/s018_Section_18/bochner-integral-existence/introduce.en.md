Theorem 17.14 establishes the existence of the Bochner integral for separable Banach space valued mappings. It constructs a unique bounded linear operator $L: \mathcal{L}_1(X; \mathcal{E}) \to \mathcal{E}$ extending the natural definition on simple functions. The construction first defines $L_0$ on integrable simple mappings by $L_0(\sum \chi_{E_i} v_i) = \sum \mu(E_i) v_i$, verifies its boundedness with norm at most $1$, and then extends to the whole space by density.
