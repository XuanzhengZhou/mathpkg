Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space and let $\mathcal{E}$ be a normed space. The collection $\mathcal{L}_{\infty}(X; \mathcal{E}) = \mathcal{L}_{\infty}(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu; \mathcal{E})$ of all those measurable mappings $\Phi$ of $X$ into $\mathcal{E}$ such that

$$\|\Phi\|_{\infty} = \|N_{\Phi}\|_{\infty} = \operatorname{ess sup}_{X} \|\Phi(x)\| < +\infty$$

is a linear space with respect to pointwise linear operations, and $\|\Phi\|_{\infty}$ is a pseudonorm on $\mathcal{L}_{\infty}(X; \mathcal{E})$.
