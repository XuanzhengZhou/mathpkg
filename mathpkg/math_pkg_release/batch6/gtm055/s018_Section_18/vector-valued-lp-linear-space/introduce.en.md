Proposition 17.9 extends the definition of Lebesgue spaces to Banach space valued mappings. For a normed space $\mathcal{E}$, the space $\mathcal{L}_p(X; \mathcal{E})$ consists of measurable $\mathcal{E}$-valued mappings $\Phi$ for which $\|\Phi(\cdot)\|^p$ is integrable. This construction generalizes the scalar-valued $\mathcal{L}_p$ spaces and is fundamental for the Bochner integral theory.
