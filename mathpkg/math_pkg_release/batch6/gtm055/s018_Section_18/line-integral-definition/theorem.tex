Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space and let $\gamma: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ be a rectifiable curve. For a continuous $\mathcal{E}$-valued function $\Phi$ defined on the trace of $\gamma$, the line integral (or contour integral) of $\Phi$ along $\gamma$ is defined as the limit of Riemann--Stieltjes sums:
$$\int_{\gamma} \Phi(\zeta) d\zeta = \lim_{\text{mesh}(\mathcal{P}) \to 0} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \Phi(\gamma(t_i^*)) (\gamma(t_i) - \gamma(t_{i-1})),$$
where $\mathcal{P} = \{a = t_0 < t_1 < \cdots < t_n = b\}$ is a partition of $[a, b]$ and $t_i^* \in [t_{i-1}, t_i]$.
