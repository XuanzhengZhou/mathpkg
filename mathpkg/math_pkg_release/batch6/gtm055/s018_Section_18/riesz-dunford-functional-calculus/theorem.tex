Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a unital Banach algebra and let $a \in \mathcal{A}$. For a complex-valued function $f$ that is locally analytic on an open neighborhood of the spectrum $\sigma(a)$, the Riesz--Dunford functional calculus defines $f(a) \in \mathcal{A}$ by the operator-valued contour integral
$$f(a) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\gamma} f(\zeta) (\zeta 1 - a)^{-1} d\zeta,$$
where $\gamma$ is an oriented envelope of $\sigma(a)$ contained in the domain of analyticity of $f$. This mapping $f \mapsto f(a)$ is a continuous unital algebra homomorphism from the algebra of locally analytic functions on neighborhoods of $\sigma(a)$ into $\mathcal{A}$.
