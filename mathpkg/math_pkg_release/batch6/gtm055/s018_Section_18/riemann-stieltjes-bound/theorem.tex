Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space, let $[a, b]$ be a closed interval, and suppose that $\Phi: [a, b] \to \mathcal{E}$ is continuous and $\alpha: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is of bounded variation. Then $\Phi$ is integrable over $[a, b]$ with respect to $\alpha$, and

$$\left\| \int_a^b \Phi(t) d\alpha(t) \right\| \leq \int_a^b \| \Phi(t) \| dL(t) \leq \max_{a \leq t \leq b} \| \Phi(t) \| \, L(b)$$

where $L(t)$ denotes the total variation of $\alpha$ on the interval $[a, t]$, $a \leq t \leq b$.
