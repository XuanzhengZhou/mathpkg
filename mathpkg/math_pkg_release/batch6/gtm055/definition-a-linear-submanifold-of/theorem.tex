A linear submanifold $M$ of a normed space $\mathcal{E}$ is called a subspace of $\mathcal{E}$ (sometimes, for special emphasis, a closed subspace of $\mathcal{E}$) if and only if $M$ is closed in $\mathcal{E}$.

In any normed space $\mathcal{E}$ the linear submanifolds (0) and $\mathcal{E}$ are subspaces. Moreover, as will be seen below (Prob. P), unless $\mathcal{E}$ is one-dimensional, there are many other subspaces as well. The following results, while easily proved, are nonetheless of basic importance.
