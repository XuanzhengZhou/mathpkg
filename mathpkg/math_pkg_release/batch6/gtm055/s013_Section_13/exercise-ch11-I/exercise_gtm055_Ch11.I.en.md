(Generalization and converse of Theorem 11.10) Let $v$ be a value on a linear space $\mathcal{E}$, let $\mathcal{M}$ be a linear submanifold of $\mathcal{E}$ that is closed with respect to the invariant metric defined by $v$, and let $[x]$ denote the coset in $\mathcal{E}/\mathcal{M}$ of a vector $x$ in $\mathcal{E}$. Show that

$$v'([x]) = \inf_{z \in \mathcal{M}} v(x + z), \quad x \in \mathcal{E},$$

defines a value on $\mathcal{E}/\mathcal{M}$, and verify that $\mathcal{E}$ is complete if and only if $\mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{E}/\mathcal{M}$ are both complete (with respect to the metrics defined by $v$ and $v'$, respectively).
