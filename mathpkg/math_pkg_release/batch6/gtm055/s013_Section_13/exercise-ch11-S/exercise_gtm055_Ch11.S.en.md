Let $\{\mathcal{E}_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ be a nonempty indexed family of normed spaces. The full algebraic direct sum $\mathcal{E} = \sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} + \mathcal{E}_\gamma$ is a topological linear space in the product topology. The bounded families $\{x_\gamma\}$ in $\mathcal{E}$, i.e., those such that $\sup_\gamma \|x_\gamma\| < +\infty$, form a linear manifold $\mathcal{B}$ in $\mathcal{E}$, and $\|\{x_\gamma\}\|_\infty = \sup_\gamma \|x_\gamma\|$ defines a norm on $\mathcal{B}$. Show that $\mathcal{B}$ is complete in the norm $\|\cdot\|_\infty$ if and only if all the spaces $\mathcal{E}_\gamma$ are complete.
