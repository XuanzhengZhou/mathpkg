If $\mathcal{E}$ is a Banach space, then the open ball of radius one centered at $1_{\mathcal{E}}$ in $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E})$ consists entirely of invertible operators. More precisely, if $T \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E})$ and $\|1_{\mathcal{E}} - T\| < 1$, then $T$ is invertible and

$$T^{-1} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (1_{\mathcal{E}} - T)^n.$$
