Let $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{F}$ be Banach spaces, let $T$ be a left [right] invertible element of $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F})$, let $R$ be a left [right] inverse of $T$, and let $d = \| R \|$. Then every linear transformation $S$ in $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F})$ such that $\| S - T \| < 1/d$ is also left [right] invertible. Thus the set of left [right] invertible elements of $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F})$ is open. Hence the set $\mathcal{U}$ of invertible elements of $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F})$ is also open. Moreover, $T \rightarrow T^{-1}$ is a continuous mapping of $\mathcal{U}$ into $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{F}, \mathcal{E})$.
