Proposition 12.6 improves upon Proposition 12.5 in the complete setting: if $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{F}$ are Banach spaces, then any bounded-below operator $T \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E},\mathcal{F})$ automatically has closed range. Combined with Proposition 12.5, this yields that a bounded-below operator between Banach spaces is invertible onto its (closed) range, i.e., it is an equivalence onto a closed subspace of $\mathcal{F}$.
