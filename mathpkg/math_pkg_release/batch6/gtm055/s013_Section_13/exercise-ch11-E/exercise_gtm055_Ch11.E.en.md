If $X$ is an arbitrary nonempty set, then the space $\mathcal{B}(X)$ of all bounded complex-valued functions on $X$ is a Banach space in the sup norm. If $X$ is a topological space, then the space $\mathcal{C}_b(X)$ is a subspace of $\mathcal{B}(X)$. Suppose now that $X$ is a metric space. Show that the uniformly continuous functions in $\mathcal{B}(X)$ form a linear manifold in $\mathcal{B}(X)$. Show, likewise, that the same is true of the Lipschitzian functions in $\mathcal{B}(X)$. Are these linear manifolds closed?
