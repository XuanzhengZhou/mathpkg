If $\mathcal{A}$ is a unital Banach algebra and $x$ is an arbitrary element of $\mathcal{A}$, then the series

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{\lambda^{n+1}}$$

converges to $(\lambda - x)^{-1}$ for every complex number $\lambda$ such that $|\lambda| > \|x\|$. Hence the spectrum $\sigma_{\mathcal{A}}(x)$ is bounded, being a subset of the disc $\{\lambda \in \mathbb{C}: |\lambda| \leq \|x\|\}$, and the resolvent set of $x$ contains the complement of this disc.
