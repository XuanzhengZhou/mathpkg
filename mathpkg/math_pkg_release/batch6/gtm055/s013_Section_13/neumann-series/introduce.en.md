Proposition 12.11 establishes that for any element $x$ of a unital Banach algebra $\mathcal{A}$, the resolvent $(\lambda - x)^{-1}$ can be expanded as a convergent Neumann series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n/\lambda^{n+1}$ whenever $|\lambda| > \|x\|$. This immediately implies that the spectrum $\sigma(x)$ is bounded and is contained in the closed disc of radius $\|x\|$ centered at the origin. The resolvent set always contains the exterior of this disc, so the spectrum is never empty in infinite dimensions.
