The spectrum $\sigma(T)$ of a bounded operator $T$ on a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$ is the set of complex numbers $\lambda$ for which $\lambda - T$ fails to be invertible. The resolvent set $\mathbb{C} \setminus \sigma(T)$ consists of those $\lambda$ for which the resolvent $R_T(\lambda) = (\lambda - T)^{-1}$ exists as a bounded operator. The left and right spectra refine this notion by considering one-sided invertibility. The resolvent $R_T$ is a continuous $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E})$-valued function on the resolvent set.
