Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space and let $T$ be a bounded operator on $\mathcal{E}$. Then the \text{spectrum} $\sigma(T)$ of $T$ is the set of those complex numbers $\lambda$ such that the operator $\lambda - T$ is not invertible. The set of those complex numbers $\lambda$ such that $\lambda - T$ is not left [right] invertible is called the \text{left [right] spectrum} of $T$ and is denoted by $\sigma_l(T)$ [$\sigma_r(T)$]. The complement $\mathbb{C} \setminus \sigma(T)$, consisting of those scalars $\lambda$ such that $\lambda - T$ is invertible, is called the \text{resolvent set} of $T$, and the $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E})$-valued mapping $R_T$ defined by

$$R_T(\lambda) = (\lambda - T)^{-1}, \quad \lambda \notin \sigma(T),$$

is called the \text{resolvent} of $T$.
