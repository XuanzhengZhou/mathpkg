A bounded linear transformation between normed spaces is one for which the image of the unit ball is bounded. This concept provides the fundamental link between the algebraic structure of linear maps and the topological structure of normed spaces. The operator norm $\|T\|$ quantifies the maximum stretching factor of the transformation, and it can be equivalently computed as the supremum of $\|Tx\|$ over the unit ball, the unit sphere, or as the supremum of the ratio $\|Tx\|/\|x\|$ over all nonzero vectors.
