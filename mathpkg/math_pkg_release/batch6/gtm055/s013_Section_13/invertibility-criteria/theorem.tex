Let $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{F}$ be normed spaces and let $T \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F})$. If $T$ is left invertible, then $T$ is bounded below. If $T$ is right invertible, then $\mathcal{R}(T) = \mathcal{F}$. Moreover, $T$ is invertible if and only if $T$ is both onto and bounded below.
