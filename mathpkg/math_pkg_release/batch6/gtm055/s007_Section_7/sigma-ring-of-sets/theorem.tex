A ring of sets that is closed with respect to the formation of countable unions is a \textit{$\sigma$-ring} of sets. Every $\sigma$-ring $\mathcal{S}$ of sets is closed under the formation of countable intersections. Moreover, $\mathcal{S}$ is closed under the formation of complements if and only if the entire space $X$ belongs to $\mathcal{S}$.
