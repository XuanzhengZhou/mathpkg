A $\sigma$-ring is a ring of sets that is additionally closed under countable unions (and consequently under countable intersections). This closure property is essential for measure theory because measures are required to be countably additive. A $\sigma$-ring that contains the whole space $X$ is closed under complements, making it a $\sigma$-algebra. The distinction between rings and $\sigma$-rings parallels the distinction between finite additivity and countable additivity of measures.
