A domain $\Delta$ in the complex plane is simply connected if every closed rectifiable arc in $\Delta$ has winding number zero around every point outside $\Delta$. Intuitively, this means $\Delta$ has no holes. Simply connected domains are the natural setting for global versions of the Cauchy theorem, the existence of primitives for analytic functions, and analytic branches of the logarithm.
