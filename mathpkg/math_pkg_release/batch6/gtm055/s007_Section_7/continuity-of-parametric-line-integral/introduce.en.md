If $g(\zeta, \lambda)$ is continuous on the product of the ranges of two rectifiable arcs, then the parametric integrals obtained by integrating out one variable define continuous functions of the remaining variable. The proof exploits the uniform continuity of $g$ on the compact parameter rectangle to bound integral differences by the arc length times $\varepsilon$.
