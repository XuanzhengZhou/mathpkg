If $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n$ are arbitrary arcs in $\mathbb{C}$, the \textit{formal sum} $\alpha = \alpha_1 + \cdots + \alpha_n$ is introduced with the understanding that two formal sums $\alpha = \alpha_1 + \cdots + \alpha_n$ and $\beta = \beta_1 + \cdots + \beta_m$ are equal if (and only if) each $\alpha_i$ and $\beta_i$ can be partitioned into a chained sum $\alpha_i = \sum_{j=1}^{n_i} \alpha_{ij}$, $\beta_i = \sum_{j=1}^{m_i} \beta_{ij}$, in such a way that the two systems of arcs $\{\alpha_{ij}\}$ and $\{\beta_{ij}\}$ are pairwise equivalent except for order.

If $\alpha = \alpha_1 + \cdots + \alpha_n$ is a formal sum of rectifiable arcs and $W$ denotes the union of the ranges of the arcs $\alpha_i$, then for each continuous function $f$ on $W$,
$$\int_\alpha f(\zeta) \, d\zeta = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \int_{\alpha_i} f(\zeta) \, d\zeta.$$
