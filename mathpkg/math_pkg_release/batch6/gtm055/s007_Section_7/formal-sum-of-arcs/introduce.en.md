A formal sum of arcs generalizes the chained sum of arcs. Arbitrary arcs $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n$ in the complex plane are combined into a formal sum $\alpha = \alpha_1 + \cdots + \alpha_n$, with equality determined by existence of common chained partitions whose component arcs are pairwise equivalent up to order. Integration over a formal sum of rectifiable arcs is defined as the sum of integrals over each individual arc.
