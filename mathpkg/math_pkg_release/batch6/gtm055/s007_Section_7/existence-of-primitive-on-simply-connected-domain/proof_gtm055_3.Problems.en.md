Fix $\lambda_0 \in \Delta$. For any $\lambda \in \Delta$, let $\alpha$ be a rectifiable arc in $\Delta$ from $\lambda_0$ to $\lambda$, and define $F(\lambda) = \int_{\alpha} f(\zeta) d\zeta$. If $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ are two such arcs, $\alpha_1 + \tilde{\alpha}_2$ is a closed rectifiable arc in $\Delta$. Since $\Delta$ is simply connected, $\alpha_1 + \tilde{\alpha}_2 \sim 0$ in $\Delta$, so by the general Cauchy-Goursat theorem $\int_{\alpha_1} f(\zeta) d\zeta = \int_{\alpha_2} f(\zeta) d\zeta$. Thus $F$ is well-defined. A standard argument shows $F'(\lambda) = f(\lambda)$ for all $\lambda \in \Delta$.
