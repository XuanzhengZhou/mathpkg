Let $C = C_r(\lambda_0)$ be a circle in $\Delta$ centered at an arbitrary point $\lambda_0 \in \Delta$. One constructs two auxiliary arcs $\kappa_1$ and $\kappa_2$ lying in $\Delta \cap \operatorname{Ext}(C)$ except for their endpoints, with initial points on $C$ and terminal points on the range $J$ of $\gamma$, and with disjoint ranges.

Chain subarcs of $\gamma$ and $\tilde{C}$ together with $\kappa_1$ and $\tilde{\kappa}_2$ (and $\tilde{\kappa}_1$ and $\kappa_2$) to form two simply closed arcs $\gamma_1$ and $\gamma_2$ such that $w_{\gamma_1}(\lambda_0) = w_{\gamma_2}(\lambda_0) = 0$, while
$$w_{\gamma_1 + \gamma_2}(\lambda_0) = w_{\gamma + \tilde{C}}(\lambda_0).$$

Since $w_{\gamma_1}(\lambda_0) = w_{\gamma_2}(\lambda_0) = 0$, we have $w_{\gamma + \tilde{C}}(\lambda_0) = 0$, which gives $w_{\gamma}(\lambda_0) = w_{C}(\lambda_0) = 1$. As $\lambda_0$ was arbitrary in $\Delta$, the result follows.
