Let $\Delta$ be a simply connected domain and let $f$ be an analytic function on $\Delta$ with no zeros in $\Delta$. Then there exists an analytic determination of $\log f(\lambda)$ on $\Delta$, that is, an analytic function $\log f(\lambda)$ on $\Delta$ such that
$$\exp\{\log f(\lambda)\} \equiv f(\lambda) \quad \text{on } \Delta.$$

In particular, if $0 \notin \Delta$, then there is an analytic determination of $\log \lambda$ on $\Delta$, unique up to an additive constant of the form $2k\pi i$, $k \in \mathbb{Z}$.

Consequently, if $\Delta$ is a simply connected domain not containing $0$, then there exists a real harmonic function $\arg \lambda$ on $\Delta$ (unique up to an additive constant of the form $2k\pi$, $k \in \mathbb{Z}$) such that $\lambda = |\lambda| e^{i \arg \lambda}$.
