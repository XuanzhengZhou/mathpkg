On a simply connected domain, every zero-free analytic function admits an analytic branch of the logarithm. This is a fundamental result in complex analysis, obtained by integrating the logarithmic derivative $f'/f$ (which is analytic since $f$ has no zeros) and using the existence of primitives on simply connected domains. As a special case, if the domain does not contain the origin, one obtains an analytic branch of $\log \lambda$ and a harmonic argument function $\arg \lambda$, both unique up to integer multiples of $2\pi i$ and $2\pi$ respectively.
