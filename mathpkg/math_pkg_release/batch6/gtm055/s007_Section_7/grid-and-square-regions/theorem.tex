Let $\mathcal{G}_m$ denote the grid of lines parallel to the real axis and to the imaginary axis by amounts $km$, $k \in \mathbb{Z}$. A \textit{horizontal strip} of $\mathcal{G}_m$ is the closed strip bounded by two adjacent horizontal lines $\mathbb{R} + kmi$ and $\mathbb{R} + (k + 1)mi$. A \textit{vertical strip} of $\mathcal{G}_m$ is a closed strip bounded by two adjacent vertical lines in $\mathcal{G}_m$. A \textit{square region} $Q$ of $\mathcal{G}_m$ is the intersection of a horizontal strip and a vertical strip of $\mathcal{G}_m$. A complex number $\lambda_0$ is a \textit{vertex} of $\mathcal{G}_m$ if it is a vertex of some square region of $\mathcal{G}_m$.
