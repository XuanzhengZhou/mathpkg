A grid $\mathcal{G}_m$ partitions the complex plane by equally spaced horizontal and vertical lines at intervals of $m$. The basic building blocks are horizontal strips, vertical strips, and square regions (intersections of adjacent strips). Vertices are the intersection points of grid lines. This geometric structure supports the study of boundary parametrizations and winding numbers for finite unions of square regions.
