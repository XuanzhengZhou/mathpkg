Let $\gamma$ be a smooth closed arc and let $\alpha_i$ be one of the smooth arcs of which $\gamma$ is the chained sum. For each $t$ with $t_{i-1} < t < t_i$, let $\lambda_0 = \lambda_0(t) = \alpha_i(t)$. Let $\lambda_1 = \lambda_1(t)$ denote the point on the inward tending normal to $\alpha_i$ at $\lambda_0(t)$ such that $|\lambda_1 - \lambda_0| = 1$. Let $\lambda_2 = \lambda_2(t)$ denote the point on the tangent line to $\alpha_i$ at $\lambda_0$ such that $\lambda_2 - \lambda_0$ is a positive multiple of $\alpha_i'(t)$ and $|\lambda_2 - \lambda_0| = 1$.

Write $\lambda_j - \lambda_0 = x_j + i y_j$, where $x_j, y_j$ are real, $j = 1, 2$. Then the determinant
$$\delta(t) = \begin{vmatrix} x_2 & y_2 \\ x_1 & y_1 \end{vmatrix}$$
is either $+1$ for every $t$ or $-1$ for every $t$, $t_{i-1} < t < t_i$.

When $\delta(t) \equiv 1$, we say that $\alpha_i$ is \textit{oriented positively} with respect to $\Delta$.
