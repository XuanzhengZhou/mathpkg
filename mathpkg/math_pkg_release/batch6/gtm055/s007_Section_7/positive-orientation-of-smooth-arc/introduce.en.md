The positive orientation of a smooth arc with respect to a domain is defined via a determinant test involving the tangent vector and the inward normal vector at each point. The determinant $\delta(t)$ formed by the coordinates of these two unit vectors is constant (either $+1$ or $-1$) along the entire smooth arc. When $\delta(t) \equiv 1$, the arc is positively oriented, meaning the domain lies to the left of the direction of travel. This orientation is consistent across consecutive smooth arcs in a chained sum, and positively oriented closed curves have winding number identically one on the enclosed domain.
