Dixon's theorem constructs a locally analytic extension of a Cauchy-type integral using the divided difference kernel $g(\zeta, \lambda) = (f(\zeta) - f(\lambda))/(\zeta - \lambda)$. The function $h$ coincides with the standard Cauchy integral formula on $V$ (where $w_\gamma = 0$) and analytically continues to $U \cup V$. The proof combines parametric integral continuity, interchange of iterated integrals, and Morera's theorem.
