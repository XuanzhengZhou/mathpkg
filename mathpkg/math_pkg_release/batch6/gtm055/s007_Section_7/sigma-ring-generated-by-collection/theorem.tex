The $\sigma$-ring $\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{C})$ generated by a collection $\mathcal{C}$ of subsets is defined as the intersection of all $\sigma$-rings containing $\mathcal{C}$. Unlike the generated ring $\mathcal{R}(\mathcal{C})$, the general description of $\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{C})$ requires a transfinite procedure (see Problem G).
