The $\sigma$-ring generated by a collection of sets is the smallest $\sigma$-ring containing that collection. While the generated ring $\mathcal{R}(\mathcal{C})$ admits an explicit finite construction using Boolean operations, the generated $\sigma$-ring $\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{C})$ generally requires a transfinite induction, building up through $\omega_1$ stages by alternately adding countable unions and then closing under ring operations. The Borel sets in a topological space are a fundamental example: they form the $\sigma$-ring generated by the open sets.
