Let $P$ be a simple closed polygon in $\mathbb{C}$. Show that $\text{Int}(P)$ can be expressed as the union of a finite number of closed triangular regions $T_1, \ldots, T_k$ such that all the vertices of these triangular regions are also vertices of $P$, and such that any two triangular regions $T_i$ and $T_j$, $i \neq j$, that are not disjoint meet only in a common edge (which must then be a line segment lying entirely in $\text{Int}(P)$ except for its endpoints, which are vertices of $P$). Use (i) and an induction on the number of edges in $P$.
