A domain $\Delta$ in $\mathbb{C}$ is said to be simply connected if every closed rectifiable arc $\gamma$ in $\Delta$ satisfies the condition $\gamma \sim 0$ in $\Delta$.

(i) Use the results of Problem K to prove that a domain $\Delta$ in $\mathbb{C}$ is simply connected if and only if the complement of $\Delta$ in the Riemann sphere $\hat{\mathbb{C}}$ is connected (cf. Problem 3W).

(ii) Show that every analytic function on a simply connected domain $\Delta$ possesses a primitive on $\Delta$.

(Hint: Fix a point $\lambda_0$ in $\Delta$. If $f$ is analytic on $\Delta$ and if $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ are any two rectifiable arcs in $\Delta$ joining $\lambda_0$ to a point $\lambda$, then $\int_{\alpha_1} f(\zeta) \, d\zeta = \int_{\alpha_2} f(\zeta) \, d\zeta$ by the Cauchy-Goursat theorem.)
