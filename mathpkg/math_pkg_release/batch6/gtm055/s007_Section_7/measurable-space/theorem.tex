A set $X$ together with a $\sigma$-ring $\mathcal{S}$ of subsets of $X$ such that $X$ belongs to $\mathcal{S}$ is a \textit{measurable space}. Thus a measurable space is a pair $(X, \mathcal{S})$ where $\mathcal{S}$ is a $\sigma$-ring of subsets of $X$ containing $X$ (equivalently, $\mathcal{S}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra of subsets of $X$). The sets in $\mathcal{S}$ are called the \textit{measurable sets}.
