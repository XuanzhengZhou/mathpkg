If $\Delta$ is simply connected, the condition $\gamma \sim 0$ in $\Delta$ for every closed rectifiable arc $\gamma$ implies that no closed curve in $\Delta$ can separate points of the complement, forcing $\hat{\mathbb{C}} \setminus \Delta$ to be connected. Conversely, if $\hat{\mathbb{C}} \setminus \Delta$ is connected, any closed rectifiable arc $\gamma$ in $\Delta$ cannot wind around isolated components of the complement. By the topological properties of winding numbers established in Problem K, this forces $w_\gamma(\lambda) = 0$ for all $\lambda \notin \Delta$, so $\Delta$ is simply connected. Compare with Problem 3W.
