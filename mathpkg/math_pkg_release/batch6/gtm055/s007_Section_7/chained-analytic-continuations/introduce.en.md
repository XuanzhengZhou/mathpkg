A sequence of analytic continuations along multiple circle chains is called chained when consecutive continuations share a common function element at the junction point -- the terminal element of one chain coincides with the initial element of the next. This chaining mechanism is the basic building block for analytic continuation along longer paths, particularly polygonal arcs, and is essential for the statement and proof of the monodromy principles.
