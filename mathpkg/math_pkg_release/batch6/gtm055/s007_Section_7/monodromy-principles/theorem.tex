Let $\Delta$ be a simply connected domain, let $D_0$ be an open disc contained in $\Delta$, and let $f_0$ be an analytic function defined on $D_0$. Suppose $f_0$ can be analytically continued along every polygonal arc in $\Delta$ (starting at the center $\lambda_0$ of $D_0$).

If $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$ are any polygonal arcs in $\Delta$ joining $\lambda_0$ to the same point $\lambda$, then the last function elements in the analytic continuations of $f_0$ along $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$, respectively, agree in a neighborhood of $\lambda$.

Consequently, there exists a (unique) analytic function $f$ on $\Delta$ that agrees with $f_0$ on $D_0$.
