The general Cauchy-Goursat theorem states that for a formal sum $\gamma$ of closed rectifiable arcs that does not wind around points outside $U$ (written $\gamma \sim 0$ in $U$), the integral of any locally analytic function $f$ over $\gamma$ vanishes. The associated Cauchy integral formula expresses $w_\gamma(\lambda) f(\lambda)$ as a contour integral. This formulation unifies the classical local theory with the global language of winding numbers and formal sums of arcs.
