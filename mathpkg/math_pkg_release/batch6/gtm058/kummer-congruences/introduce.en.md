The Kummer congruences are classical congruences among Bernoulli numbers that were discovered in the context of Kummer's work on Fermat's Last Theorem. They are equivalent to the existence of a continuous hBadic zeta function, and they were one of the first indications that a hBadic interpolation of zeta values should exist.
