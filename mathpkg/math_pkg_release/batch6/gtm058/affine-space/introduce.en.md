Affine space $\mathbb{A}_F^n$ is the most basic geometric object: the set of $n$-tuples with coordinates in $F$. Over finite fields $\mathbb{F}_q$, counting points on affine hypersurfaces (solution sets of polynomial equations) leads to the definition of the zeta function of a hypersurface.
