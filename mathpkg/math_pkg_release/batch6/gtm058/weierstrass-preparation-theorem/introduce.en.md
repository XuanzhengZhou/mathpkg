The $p$-adic Weierstrass Preparation Theorem is the fundamental structure theorem for $p$-adic power series. It states that any convergent power series can be factored as a polynomial (capturing the zeros) times an invertible power series (nowhere vanishing). This is the $p$-adic analogue of the Weierstrass preparation theorem in complex analysis. A consequence is that an everywhere-convergent power series with no zeros must be constant, contrasting sharply with the complex case where $e^z$ is a non-constant everywhere-convergent zero-free function.
