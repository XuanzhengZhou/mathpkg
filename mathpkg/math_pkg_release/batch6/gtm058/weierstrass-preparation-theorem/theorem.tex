(Theorem 14) Let $f(X) = 1 + \sum_{i=1}^\infty a_i X^i \in 1 + X\Omega[[X]]$ converge on $D(p^\lambda)$. Let $N$ be the total horizontal length of all segments of the Newton polygon having slope $\leq \lambda$ (if finite), or the greatest $i$ with $(i,\operatorname{ord}_p a_i)$ on the last segment. Then there exists a polynomial $h(X) \in 1 + X\Omega[X]$ of degree $N$ and a power series $g(X)$ which converges and is nonzero on $D(p^\lambda)$, such that $h(X) = f(X) \cdot g(X)$. The polynomial $h(X)$ is uniquely determined, and its Newton polygon coincides with that of $f(X)$ out to $(N,\operatorname{ord}_p a_N)$.
