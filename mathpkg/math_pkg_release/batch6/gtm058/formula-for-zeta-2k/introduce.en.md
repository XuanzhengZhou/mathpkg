Euler's famous formula expresses $\zeta(2k)$ as a rational multiple of $\pi^{2k} B_{2k}$. This formula is the starting point for hBadic interpolation: once we remove the transcendental factor $\pi^{2k}$, the value $\zeta(2k)$ times an appropriate factor becomes a rational number depending on Bernoulli numbers, which can be interpolated hBadically.
