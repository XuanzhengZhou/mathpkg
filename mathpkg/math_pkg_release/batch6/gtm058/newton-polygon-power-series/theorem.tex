For a power series $f(X) = 1 + \sum_{i=1}^\infty a_i X^i$, plot the points $(0,0), (1,\operatorname{ord}_p a_1), \ldots, (i,\operatorname{ord}_p a_i), \ldots$. The Newton polygon is the limit of the Newton polygons of the truncations $f_n(X)$, obtained by rotating the vertical line through $(0,0)$ until it hits a point, and continuing. Three cases arise: (1) infinitely many finite segments; (2) a finite number of segments, the last infinitely long; (3) a last segment whose slope is the least upper bound of possible slopes of lines that don't pass above any point.
