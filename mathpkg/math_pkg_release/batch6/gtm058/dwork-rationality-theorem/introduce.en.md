Dwork's 1960 proof of the rationality of the zeta function was the first major breakthrough on the Weil Conjectures. His proof uses $p$-adic analysis in an ingenious way: the zeta function is expressed as an alternating product of characteristic polynomials of a certain $p$-adic completely continuous operator acting on a space of power series. The rationality follows from the fact that such operators have a well-defined Fredholm determinant.
