A hBadic distribution is the hBadic analogue of a measure, defined on compact-open subsets rather than measurable sets. Distributions can be integrated against locally constant functions. Key examples include the Haar distribution, the Dirac distribution, and the Mazur distribution, the latter being fundamental to the construction of the hBadic zeta function.
