Unlike the classical exponential which converges everywhere, the $p$-adic exponential has a finite radius of convergence because $n!$ is highly divisible by $p$, making $|1/n!|_p$ large. The convergence region $D(p^{-1/(p-1)})$ is the maximal disc on which the exponential defines an isomorphism to its image. This restricted convergence is both a limitation and, as seen in the Weierstrass preparation theorem, an advantage.
