A \text{norm} on a field $F$ is a map denoted $\|\cdot\|$ from $F$ to the nonnegative real numbers such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\|x\| = 0$ if and only if $x = 0$.
\item $\|x \cdot y\| = \|x\| \cdot \|y\|$.
\item $\|x + y\| \leq \|x\| + \|y\|$.
\end{enumerate}
A metric $d$ is said to \text{come from} (or be \text{induced by}) a norm $\|\cdot\|$ if $d(x, y) = \|x - y\|$.
