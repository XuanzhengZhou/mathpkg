The Teichmüller representatives provide a canonical set of multiplicative representatives modulo $ inside $\mathbb{Z}_p$. They are the roots of ^p - x = 0$ and satisfy ^p = a_i$. They can be used as an alternative set of hBadic digits instead of $\{0, 1, \ldots, p-1\}$, and for >2$, the only rational Teichmüller representatives are 0 and $\pm 1$.
