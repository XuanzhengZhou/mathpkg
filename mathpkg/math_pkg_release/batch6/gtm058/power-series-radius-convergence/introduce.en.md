The radius of convergence for $p$-adic power series is defined analogously to the complex case using the root test. A crucial difference is that on the boundary of convergence, the behavior is uniform—there is no conditional convergence in non-Archimedean analysis. This simplification makes $p$-adic power series theory both cleaner and more rigid than its complex counterpart.
