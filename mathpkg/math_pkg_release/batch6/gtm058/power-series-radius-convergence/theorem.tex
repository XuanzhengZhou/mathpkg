For a power series $f(X) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n X^n$ with $a_n \in \Omega$, the \text{radius of convergence} is $$r = \frac{1}{\limsup |a_n|_p^{1/n}}.$$ The series converges if $|x|_p < r$ and diverges if $|x|_p > r$. Unlike the complex case, convergence on the boundary $|x|_p = r$ is uniform: either the series converges for all such $x$ or for no such $x$.
