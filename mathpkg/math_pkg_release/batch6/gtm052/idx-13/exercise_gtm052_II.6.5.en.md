Let $X$ be the nonsingular cubic curve $y^2z = x^3 - xz^2$ in $\mathbf{P}_k^2$ with $\text{char } k \neq 2$, and let $P_0 = (0,1,0)$.

(a) Show that the group operation on $X$ is commutative.

(b) A point $P \in X$ has order 2 in the group law on $X$ if and only if the tangent line at $P$ passes through $P_0$.

(c) A point $P \in X$ has order 3 in the group law on $X$ if and only if $P$ is an inflection point.

(d) Let $k = \mathbb{C}$. Show that the points of $X$ with coordinates in $\mathbb{Q}$ form a subgroup of the group $X$. Can you determine the structure of this subgroup explicitly?
