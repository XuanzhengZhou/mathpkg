(i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) follows from (4.9).
(ii) $\Rightarrow$ (iii). If $X$ is complete, then every discrete valuation ring of $K/k$ has a unique center on $X$ (Ex. 4.5). Since the local rings of $X$ at the closed points are all discrete valuation rings, this implies that the closed points of $X$ are in 1-1 correspondence with the discrete valuation rings of $K/k$, namely the points of $C_K$. Thus it is clear that $X \cong t(C_K)$.
