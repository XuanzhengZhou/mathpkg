Proposition 6.7 gives a fundamental characterization of complete nonsingular curves. A curve $X$ is complete if and only if every discrete valuation ring of its function field $K/k$ has a unique center on $X$, which is also equivalent to $X$ being isomorphic to the abstract curve $t(C_K)$ constructed from the set of all DVRs of $K/k$.
