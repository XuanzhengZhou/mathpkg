The prime ideal of $Y$ is $\mathfrak{p} = (y,z)$. Let $\mathfrak{m} = (x,y,z)$. The vector space $\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2$ is 3-dimensional over $k$ generated by $\bar{x}, \bar{y}, \bar{z}$. Since $\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}$, the image of $\mathfrak{p}$ in $\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2$ contains $\bar{y}$ and $\bar{z}$, so $\mathfrak{p}$ cannot be principal. Thus $\mathfrak{p}A_{\mathfrak{m}}$ is not principal even in the local ring $A_{\mathfrak{m}}$, so $Y$ is not locally principal at the vertex and hence not Cartier. On the other hand, $2Y$ corresponds to the divisor of $y$, which is principal since $y = 0 \Rightarrow z^2 = 0$.
