Let $P_0 = (0,1,0)$. To each closed point $P \in X - Z$, associate the Cartier divisor $D_P$ which is $1$ in a neighborhood of $Z$ and corresponds to $P-P_0$ on $X-Z$. Injectivity: if $P \neq Q$ and $D_P \sim D_Q$, then there exists $f \in K^*$ invertible at $Z$ with $(f) = P-Q$ on $X-Z$, giving a birational morphism $X \to \mathbf{P}^1$. But the local ring of $Z$ on $X$ would dominate some DVR of $\mathbf{P}^1$, impossible since $Z$ is singular.

Surjectivity follows the same inductive procedure as the nonsingular case, noting that the geometric constructions remain within $X-Z$. The group variety is $\mathbf{G}_a$: the parametrization $t \mapsto (t, 1, t^3)$ gives $X-Z \cong \mathbf{A}^1$, and the group law corresponds to addition of parameters.
