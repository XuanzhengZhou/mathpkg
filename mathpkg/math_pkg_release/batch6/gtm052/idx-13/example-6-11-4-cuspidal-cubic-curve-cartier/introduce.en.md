Example 6.11.4 extends the group law construction to the singular cuspidal cubic curve, using Cartier divisors instead of Weil divisors. The nonsingular locus $X - Z \cong \mathbf{A}^1$ acquires the additive group structure $\mathbf{G}_a$, contrasting with the nonsingular case where the group is an elliptic curve.
