A Cartier divisor on a scheme $X$ is a global section of the sheaf $\mathcal{K}^*/\mathcal{O}^*$, where $\mathcal{K}$ is the sheaf of total quotient rings of $\mathcal{O}_X$. Equivalently, a Cartier divisor is given by an open cover $\{U_i\}$ of $X$ and elements $f_i \in \Gamma(U_i, \mathcal{K}^*)$ such that $f_i/f_j \in \Gamma(U_i \cap U_j, \mathcal{O}^*)$ for all $i,j$. A Cartier divisor is principal if it is in the image of $\Gamma(X, \mathcal{K}^*) \to \Gamma(X, \mathcal{K}^*/\mathcal{O}^*)$. Two Cartier divisors are linearly equivalent if their difference is principal.
