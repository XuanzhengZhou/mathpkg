This example computes the divisor class group of the nonsingular quadric surface $Q: xy = zw$ in $\mathbf{P}^3$. Using the isomorphism $Q \cong \mathbf{P}^1 \times \mathbf{P}^1$, one finds that $\text{Cl} Q \cong \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}$, generated by the two families of lines. This is one of the first nontrivial computations of a divisor class group for a surface.
