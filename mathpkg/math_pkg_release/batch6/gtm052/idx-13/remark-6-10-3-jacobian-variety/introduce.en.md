Remark 6.10.3 generalizes the cubic curve example to arbitrary complete nonsingular curves. The degree-zero part of the divisor class group $\text{Cl}^0 X$ is isomorphic to the Jacobian variety, an abelian variety whose dimension equals the genus of $X$. This connects the algebraic study of divisors with the geometric theory of abelian varieties.
