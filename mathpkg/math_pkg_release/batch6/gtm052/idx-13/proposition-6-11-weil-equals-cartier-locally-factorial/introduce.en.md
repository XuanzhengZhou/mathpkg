Proposition 6.11 establishes that on a sufficiently nice scheme (integral, separated, noetherian, with locally factorial local rings), the notions of Weil divisor and Cartier divisor coincide. This is a crucial bridge between the two theories and applies in particular to regular schemes, since regular local rings are UFDs.
