This example continues the study of the quadric surface $Q \cong \mathbf{P}^1 \times \mathbf{P}^1$. The twisted cubic curve $C$ lies on $Q$, and one computes its divisor type as $(1,2)$ using the quadric cone $Y: yz = w^2$. An interesting consequence is that no surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$ (other than $Q$ itself) can intersect $Q$ exactly in the twisted cubic curve.
