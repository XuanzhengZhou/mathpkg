Let $C$ be the twisted cubic curve $x = t^3, y = u^3, z = t^2 u, w = tu^2$ which lies on the quadric $Q: xy = zw$. If $Y$ is the quadric cone $yz = w^2$, then $Y \cap Q = C \cup L$ where $L$ is the line $y = w = 0$. Since $Y \sim 2H$ on $\mathbf{P}^3$, $Y \cap Q$ is a divisor of type $(2,2)$ on $Q$. The line $L$ has type $(1,0)$, so $C$ is of type $(1,2)$. It follows that there does not exist any surface $Y \subseteq \mathbf{P}^3$, not containing $Q$, such that $Y \cap Q = C$, even set-theoretically.
