Remark 7.4.3 clarifies the relationship between "very ample" and "ample" invertible sheaves. Serre's theorem shows that very ample implies ample. The converse is not true in general, but ampleness is a stable property: some sufficiently high tensor power of an ample sheaf becomes very ample (proved later in Proposition 7.6).
