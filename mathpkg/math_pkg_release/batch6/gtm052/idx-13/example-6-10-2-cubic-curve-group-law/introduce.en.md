Example 6.10.2 exhibits the celebrated group law on a nonsingular cubic curve. By identifying closed points of $X$ with elements of $\text{Cl}^0 X$ via $P \mapsto P - P_0$ (where $P_0$ is an inflection point), the curve acquires the structure of a group variety. This is the foundation of the theory of elliptic curves.
