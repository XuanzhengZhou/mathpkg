Let $X$ be a complete nonsingular curve over $k$, let $Y$ be any curve over $k$, and let $f: X \rightarrow Y$ be a morphism. Then either:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $f(X) = \text{a point}$, or
\item[(2)] $f(X) = Y$, $K(X)$ is a finite extension field of $K(Y)$, $f$ is a finite morphism, and $Y$ is also complete.
\end{enumerate}
