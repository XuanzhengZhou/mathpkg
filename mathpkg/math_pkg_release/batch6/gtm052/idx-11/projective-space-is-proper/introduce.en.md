Theorem 4.9 establishes that projective space $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbb{Z}}^n$ is proper over $\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}$. By base change, it follows that $\mathbf{P}_Y^n$ is proper over $Y$ for any scheme $Y$, and consequently every projective morphism is proper. The proof uses the valuative criterion of properness (Theorem 4.7) together with a careful valuation-theoretic argument that chooses coordinates to minimize the valuation, generalizing the classical proof that projective varieties are complete.
