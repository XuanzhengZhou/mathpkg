For the following statements, $X_0$ will denote a separated scheme of finite type over $\mathbb{R}$, and $X, \sigma$ will denote the corresponding scheme with involution over $\mathbb{C}$.

(b) Show that $X_0$ is affine if and only if $X$ is.
(c) If $X_0, Y_0$ are two such schemes over $\mathbb{R}$, then to give a morphism $f_0: X_0 \to Y_0$ is equivalent to giving a morphism $f: X \to Y$ which commutes with the involutions, i.e., $f \circ \sigma_X = \sigma_Y \circ f$.
(d) If $X \cong \mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{C}}^1$, then $X_0 \cong \mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{R}}^1$.
(e) If $X \cong \mathbb{P}_{\mathbb{C}}^1$, then either $X_0 \cong \mathbb{P}_{\mathbb{R}}^1$, or $X_0$ is isomorphic to the conic in $\mathbb{P}_{\mathbb{R}}^2$ given by the homogeneous equation $x_0^2 + x_1^2 + x_2^2 = 0$.
