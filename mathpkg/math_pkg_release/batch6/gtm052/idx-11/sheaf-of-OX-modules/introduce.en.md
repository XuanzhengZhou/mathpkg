Section 5 of Chapter II introduces sheaves of modules on a ringed space, the fundamental objects for developing cohomology of schemes. An $\mathcal{O}_X$-module $\mathcal{F}$ assigns an $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$-module $\mathcal{F}(U)$ to each open set $U$, with restriction maps compatible with the ring structure. The category of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules is abelian (kernels, cokernels, images all exist), providing the setting for quasi-coherent and coherent sheaves that are central to modern algebraic geometry.
