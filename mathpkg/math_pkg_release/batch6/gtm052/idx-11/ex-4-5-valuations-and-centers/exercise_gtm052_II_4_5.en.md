Let $X$ be an integral scheme of finite type over a field $k$, having function field $K$. We say that a valuation of $K/k$ has center $x$ on $X$ if its valuation ring $R$ dominates the local ring $\mathcal{O}_{x,X}$.

(a) If $X$ is separated over $k$, then the center of any valuation of $K/k$ on $X$ (if it exists) is unique.
(b) If $X$ is proper over $k$, then every valuation of $K/k$ has a unique center on $X$.
*(c) Prove the converses of (a) and (b).
(d) If $X$ is proper over $k$, and if $k$ is algebraically closed, show that $\Gamma(X, \mathcal{O}_X) \cong k$.
