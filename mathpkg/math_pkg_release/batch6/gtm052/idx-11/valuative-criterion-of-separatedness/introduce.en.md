Theorem 4.3 (Valuative Criterion of Separatedness) provides a practical way to check separatedness using valuation rings. Given a commutative square with $T = \operatorname{Spec} R$ (for a valuation ring $R$) and $U = \operatorname{Spec} K$ (its fraction field), if $f$ is separated then any two extensions $T \to X$ agreeing on $U$ must be equal. The converse holds under the noetherian hypothesis on $X$, reducing the geometric notion of separatedness to an existence-and-uniqueness property of liftings through valuation rings.
