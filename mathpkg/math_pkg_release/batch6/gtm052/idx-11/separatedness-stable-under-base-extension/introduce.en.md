Corollary 4.6 establishes that separatedness is stable under base extension: if $f: X \to Y$ is separated, then for any morphism $Y' \to Y$, the induced morphism $f': X \times_Y Y' \to Y'$ remains separated. The proof uses the valuative criterion: given two lifts $T \to X'$ over $Y'$, composing with the projection $X' \to X$ yields two lifts to $X$ over $Y$, which must agree since $f$ is separated; the universal property of the fiber product then forces the original lifts to agree.
