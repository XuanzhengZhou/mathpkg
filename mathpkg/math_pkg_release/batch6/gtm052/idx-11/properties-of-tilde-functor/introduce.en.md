Proposition 5.1 establishes the fundamental properties of the tilde functor $M \mapsto \widetilde{M}$. It is exact (because localization is exact), fully faithful (so $\operatorname{Hom}_A(M,N) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathcal{O}_X}(\widetilde{M}, \widetilde{N})$), and commutes with direct sums and tensor products. Moreover, it is compatible with direct and inverse image along morphisms of affine schemes: $f_*(\widetilde{N})$ is the sheaf associated to $N$ viewed as an $A$-module, and $f^*(\widetilde{M})$ is the sheaf associated to $M \otimes_A B$.
