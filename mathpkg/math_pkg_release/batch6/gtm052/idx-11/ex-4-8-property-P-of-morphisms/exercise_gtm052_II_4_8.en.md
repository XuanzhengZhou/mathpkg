Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a property of morphisms of schemes such that:
(a) a closed immersion has $\mathcal{P}$;
(b) a composition of two morphisms having $\mathcal{P}$ has $\mathcal{P}$;
(c) $\mathcal{P}$ is stable under base extension.

Then show that:
(d) a product of morphisms having $\mathcal{P}$ has $\mathcal{P}$;
(e) if $f: X \to Y$ and $g: Y \to Z$ are two morphisms, and if $g \circ f$ has $\mathcal{P}$ and $g$ is separated, then $f$ has $\mathcal{P}$;
(f) If $f: X \to Y$ has $\mathcal{P}$, then $f_{\text{red}}: X_{\text{red}} \to Y_{\text{red}}$ has $\mathcal{P}$.

[Hint: For (e), consider the graph morphism $\Gamma_f: X \to X \times_Z Y$ and note that it is obtained by base extension from the diagonal morphism $\Delta: Y \to Y \times_Z Y$.]
