*Dimension of the Fibres of a Morphism. Let $f: X \to Y$ be a dominant morphism of integral schemes of finite type over a field $k$.

(a) Let $Y'$ be a closed irreducible subset of $Y$, whose generic point $\eta'$ is contained in $f(X)$. Let $Z$ be any irreducible component of $f^{-1}(Y')$, such that $\eta' \in f(Z)$, and show that $\operatorname{codim}(Z, X) \leqslant \operatorname{codim}(Y', Y)$.

(b) Let $e = \dim X - \dim Y$ be the relative dimension of $X$ over $Y$. For any point $y \in f(X)$, show that every irreducible component of the fibre $X_y$ has dimension $\geqslant e$.
