Given a commutative diagram with $U \to X' = X \times_Y Y'$ and $T \to Y'$, we compose with the projections to get morphisms $U \to X$ and $T \to Y$. By the valuative criterion for $f$ (which is proper), there exists a unique morphism $T \to X$ making the diagram commute. Since $X'$ is the fiber product of $X$ and $Y'$ over $Y$, the universal property yields a unique morphism $T \to X'$ compatible with all maps. Hence $f'$ satisfies the valuative criterion of properness and is therefore proper.
