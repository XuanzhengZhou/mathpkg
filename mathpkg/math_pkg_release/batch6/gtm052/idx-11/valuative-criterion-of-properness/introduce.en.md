Theorem 4.7 (Valuative Criterion of Properness) characterizes proper morphisms using valuation rings. Unlike the separatedness criterion which only requires at most one lift, properness demands exactly one lift $T \to X$ for every commutative diagram involving a valuation ring $R$. This is the algebro-geometric analogue of the topological fact that a continuous map between compact Hausdorff spaces is proper if and only if it is closed and has compact fibers.
