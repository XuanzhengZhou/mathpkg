Let $f: X \to Y$ be a morphism of finite type, with $X$ noetherian. Then $f$ is proper if and only if for every valuation ring $R$ and for every morphism of $U$ to $X$ and $T$ to $Y$ forming a commutative diagram

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
U & \to & X \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow \\
T & \to & Y
\end{array}
$$

(using the notation $T = \operatorname{Spec} R$, $U = \operatorname{Spec} K$ where $K$ is the quotient field of $R$), there exists a unique morphism $T \to X$ making the whole diagram commutative.
