A morphism of schemes $f: X \to Y$ is called separated if its diagonal morphism $\Delta: X \to X \times_Y X$ is a closed immersion. This notion, defined in Chapter II Section 4 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry, captures the idea that a scheme should not have "doubled points" — the prototypical non-separated example is the affine line with doubled origin. Separatedness is the scheme-theoretic analogue of the Hausdorff property in topology, expressed through the functorial behavior of the diagonal.
