Lemma 4.5 states that for a quasi-compact morphism $f: X \to Y$, the image $f(X)$ is closed in $Y$ exactly when it is stable under specialization (i.e., if $y_1 \in f(X)$ and $y_0$ is a specialization of $y_1$, then $y_0 \in f(X)$). One direction is trivial (closed subsets are always stable under specialization); the content lies in the converse, which uses the quasi-compactness hypothesis to show that stability under specialization forces closedness.
