The tilde construction $M \mapsto \widetilde{M}$ associates to each $A$-module $M$ a sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_{\operatorname{Spec} A}$-modules on the affine scheme $\operatorname{Spec} A$. It is defined by localizing $M$ at each prime ideal and assembling the local data into a sheaf. This construction is the fundamental bridge between commutative algebra and algebraic geometry: every quasi-coherent sheaf on an affine scheme arises as $\widetilde{M}$ for some $A$-module $M$, and the functor $M \mapsto \widetilde{M}$ is an equivalence of categories.
