An abstract variety that is proper over its base field $k$ is called a complete variety. Completeness is the algebraic analogue of compactness in topology. While every projective variety is complete (by Theorem 4.9), Nagata showed there exist complete non-projective varieties. The status of complete varieties is summarized: every complete curve is projective, every nonsingular complete surface is projective (Zariski), but there exist singular nonprojective complete surfaces and nonsingular complete nonprojective threefolds.
