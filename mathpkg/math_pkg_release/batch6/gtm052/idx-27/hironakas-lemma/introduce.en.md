Hironaka's Lemma is a technical result in commutative algebra that provides conditions under which a local noetherian domain is normal. It states that if an element $t$ in a local noetherian domain $A$ generates an ideal with a unique minimal associated prime $\mathfrak{p}$, and $t$ generates the maximal ideal of the localization $A_{\mathfrak{p}}$, and the quotient $A/\mathfrak{p}$ is normal, then in fact $\mathfrak{p} = tA$ and $A$ itself is normal. This lemma plays a crucial role in algebraic geometry, particularly in the study of flat families of varieties, where it is used to show that algebraic families of normal varieties are flat.
