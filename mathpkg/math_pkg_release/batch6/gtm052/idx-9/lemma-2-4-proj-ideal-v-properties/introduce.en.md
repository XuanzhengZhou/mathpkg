Lemma 2.4 establishes the fundamental properties of $V(\mathfrak{a})$ for homogeneous ideals in a graded ring $S$, paralleling Lemma 2.1 for $\text{Spec } A$. The set $\text{Proj } S$ consists of homogeneous prime ideals not containing the irrelevant ideal $S_+$. The $V(\mathfrak{a})$ sets form the closed sets of the Zariski topology on $\text{Proj } S$. The key property used in the proof is that a homogeneous ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ is prime if and only if $ab \in \mathfrak{p}$ implies $a \in \mathfrak{p}$ or $b \in \mathfrak{p}$ for all homogeneous $a,b \in S$.
