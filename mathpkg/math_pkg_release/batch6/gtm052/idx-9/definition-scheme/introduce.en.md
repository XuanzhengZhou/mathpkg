A scheme is the central object of study in modern algebraic geometry, generalizing the notion of an algebraic variety. It is a locally ringed space locally isomorphic to the spectrum of a commutative ring. Schemes can be constructed by gluing affine schemes along open subsets, allowing for spaces that are not globally affine (such as projective space). The definition unifies number theory and geometry by allowing rings with nilpotent elements and rings of integers from algebraic number theory to be studied geometrically.
