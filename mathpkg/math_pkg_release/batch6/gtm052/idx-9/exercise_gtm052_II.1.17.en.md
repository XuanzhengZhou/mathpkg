Let $X$ be a topological space, let $P$ be a point, and let $A$ be an abelian group. Define a sheaf $i_P(A)$ on $X$ as follows: $i_P(A)(U) = A$ if $P \in U$, and $0$ otherwise. 

Verify that the stalk of $i_P(A)$ is $A$ at every point $Q \in \overline{\{P\}}$, and $0$ elsewhere, where $\overline{\{P\}}$ denotes the closure of the set consisting of the point $P$. Hence the name "skyscraper sheaf."

Show that this sheaf could also be described as $i_*(A)$, where $A$ denotes the constant sheaf $A$ on the closed subspace $\overline{\{P\}}$, and $i: \overline{\{P\}} \to X$ is the inclusion.
