Proposition 2.2 describes the structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}$ on the spectrum $\text{Spec } A$ of a ring $A$. It states that the stalk at a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ recovers the local ring $A_\mathfrak{p}$, the sections over a basic open set $D(f)$ recover the localized ring $A_f$, and the global sections recover the original ring $A$ itself. This shows that the spectrum construction fully encodes the ring as the ring of global sections.
