Proposition 2.3 shows that $\text{Spec } A$ is a locally ringed space and establishes the functorial relationship between ring homomorphisms and morphisms of locally ringed spaces. Part (b) constructs a morphism $(f, f^\#)$ from any ring homomorphism $\varphi: A \to B$ by taking $f(\mathfrak{p}) = \varphi^{-1}(\mathfrak{p})$. Part (c) is the key result: every morphism between affine spectra arises uniquely from a ring homomorphism, making $\text{Spec}$ a contravariant equivalence between the category of rings and the category of affine schemes.
