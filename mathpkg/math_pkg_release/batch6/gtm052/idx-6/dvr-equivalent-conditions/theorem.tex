Let $A$ be a noetherian local domain of dimension one, with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$. Then the following conditions are equivalent:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] $A$ is a discrete valuation ring.
  \item[(ii)] $A$ is integrally closed.
  \item[(iii)] $A$ is a principal ideal domain.
  \item[(iv)] $\mathfrak{m}$ is a principal ideal.
  \item[(v)] Every nonzero proper ideal of $A$ is a power of $\mathfrak{m}$.
  \item[(vi)] There exists $t \in A$ such that every nonzero ideal of $A$ is of the form $(t^n)$ for some $n \geq 0$.
\end{enumerate}
