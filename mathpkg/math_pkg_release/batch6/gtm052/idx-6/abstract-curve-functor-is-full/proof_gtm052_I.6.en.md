**Proof.** Given a $k$-homomorphism $\theta: K(Y_2) \to K(Y_1)$ of function fields, one constructs the corresponding morphism $Y_1 \to Y_2$ using the abstract curve $C_K$ and the valuation criterion. Each point $P \in Y_1$ corresponds to a DVR $R_P$ of $K(Y_1)/k$, and its image under $\theta$ yields a valuation of $K(Y_2)/k$. By Theorem 6.1A, this valuation is dominated by a DVR $R_Q$ of $K(Y_2)/k$, corresponding to a unique point $Q \in Y_2$ by Theorem 6.7. This defines the morphism set-theoretically; one then checks it is a morphism of varieties. The uniqueness follows from the faithfulness established in Theorem 6.7 and the fact that the corresponding morphisms on $C_K$ are compatible.
