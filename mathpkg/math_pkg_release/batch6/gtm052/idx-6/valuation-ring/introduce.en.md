A valuation ring is the subring of a field $K$ consisting of elements with nonnegative valuation under a given valuation $v$. Its maximal ideal consists of elements with strictly positive valuation. Valuation rings generalize the notion of discrete valuation rings and are fundamental in algebraic geometry for studying local properties of varieties. Unlike discrete valuation rings, general valuation rings need not be noetherian.
