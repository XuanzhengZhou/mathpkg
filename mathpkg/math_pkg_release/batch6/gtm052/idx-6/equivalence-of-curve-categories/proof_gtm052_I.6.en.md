**Proof.** The functor $Y \mapsto K(Y)$ from (i) to (iii) is fully faithful by Theorems 6.7 and 6.8. The functor $K \mapsto C_K$ from (iii) to (i) maps a function field to its abstract nonsingular curve, which is projective by Theorem 6.9. If two homomorphisms of function fields $K_1 \to K_2 \to K_3$ are given, it follows from the uniqueness part of (6.8) that the corresponding morphisms $C_1 \to C_2 \to C_3$ are compatible. Hence $K \mapsto C_K$ is a functor from (iii) to (i). It is clearly inverse to the given functor (i) $\to$ (ii) $\to$ (iii), so we have an equivalence of categories.
