Let $K$ be a function field of dimension $1$ over $k$ (i.e., a finitely generated extension field of transcendence degree $1$). Let $C_K$ be the set of all discrete valuation rings of $K/k$. The elements of $C_K$ are called \text{points}, and we write $P \in C_K$ where $P$ stands for the valuation ring $R_P$.

The set $C_K$ is infinite. It is made into a topological space by taking the closed sets to be the finite subsets and the whole space (the cofinite topology).

If $U \subseteq C_K$ is an open subset, the \text{ring of regular functions} on $U$ is defined as
$$\mathcal{O}(U) = \bigcap_{P \in U} R_P.$$
An element $f \in \mathcal{O}(U)$ defines a function $U \to k$ by taking $f(P)$ to be the residue of $f$ modulo the maximal ideal of $R_P$. The residue field of any $R \in C_K$ is $k$.
