Let $X$ be a curve of genus $g$ over an algebraically closed field $k$, and let $D$ be an effective special divisor on $X$. Then

$$\dim|D| \leqslant \frac{1}{2} \deg D.$$

Equality holds if and only if $D = 0$, or $D = K$ (the canonical divisor), or $X$ is hyperelliptic and $|D|$ is a multiple of the unique $g_2^1$. In the latter case, $\dim|D| = \frac{1}{2} \deg D$ implies $|D| = r \cdot g_2^1$ for some $r$.
