Clifford's theorem (Theorem 5.4) from Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry is a fundamental result bounding the dimension of a special linear system on a curve. It states that for any effective special divisor, the dimension of the complete linear system is at most half the degree. The equality cases characterize hyperelliptic curves and the canonical divisor. The theorem is closely related to the canonical embedding, providing a key tool for classifying curves by their special linear systems.
