Castelnuovo's bound (Theorem 6.4) from Chapter IV, Section 6 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry provides a sharp upper bound for the genus of a curve in $\mathbf{P}^3$ in terms of its degree, assuming the curve is not contained in any plane. The bound is quadratic in the degree and is attained for every degree $d \geqslant 3$, with equality precisely when the curve lies on a quadric surface. This theorem is a cornerstone of the classical classification of space curves initiated by Noether and Halphen.
