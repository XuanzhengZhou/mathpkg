The coarse moduli variety $\mathfrak{M}_g$ parametrizes isomorphism classes of curves of genus $g$. Introduced in Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry (following Mumford), it provides a weaker but existent alternative to the fine moduli variety. While a fine moduli variety (with a universal family) does not exist for curves of genus $g \geqslant 2$, Mumford proved the existence of a coarse moduli variety. For $g=1$, the $j$-line serves as the coarse moduli space for elliptic curves.
