[The proof of Theorem 5.2 is partially truncated in the source OCR. For a nonhyperelliptic curve, one shows that $|K|$ separates points and tangent vectors using Riemann-Roch. If $X$ is hyperelliptic, then for any $P+Q$ in the unique $g_2^1$, one has $f(P)=f(Q)$, so $\varphi_K$ is not injective. The converse uses the fact that if $\varphi_K$ is not an embedding, then there exist points $P \neq Q$ with $\dim|K-P-Q|=\dim|K|-1$, which forces $\dim|P+Q|=1$ by Riemann-Roch, giving a $g_2^1$.]

For a nonhyperelliptic curve of genus 3, the canonical embedding is a quartic curve in $\mathbf{P}^2$. For genus 4, the canonical embedding is a curve of degree 6 in $\mathbf{P}^3$, which is the complete intersection of a unique irreducible quadric surface $Q$ and an irreducible cubic surface $F$.
