Theorem 5.2 from Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry provides the fundamental dichotomy for curves of genus at least 2: the canonical morphism is a closed embedding precisely when the curve is nonhyperelliptic. For nonhyperelliptic curves, the canonical embedding realizes the curve as a subvariety of degree 2g-2 in projective space of dimension g-1, enabling detailed study via projective geometry. Hyperelliptic curves instead map 2-to-1 onto a rational normal curve.
