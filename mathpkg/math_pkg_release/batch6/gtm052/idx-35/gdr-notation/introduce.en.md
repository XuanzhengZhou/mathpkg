The $g_d^r$ notation is classical shorthand used in the theory of algebraic curves to denote a linear system of dimension $r$ and degree $d$. Introduced in Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry, it streamlines the discussion of special linear systems. For instance, a hyperelliptic curve is characterized by possessing a $g_2^1$, and much of the classification theory of curves is expressed in this language.
