The fine moduli variety is the ideal universal parameter space for algebraic curves of a given genus, introduced conceptually in Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. It would carry a universal flat family of curves from which all other families could be obtained by pullback via a unique morphism. Unfortunately, such a fine moduli variety does not exist for curves of genus $g \geqslant 2$ due to the existence of nontrivial isotrivial families. This necessitates the more subtle notion of a coarse moduli variety.
