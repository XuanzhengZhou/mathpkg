A \text{fine moduli variety} $\mathfrak{M}_g$ for curves of genus $g$ would be a universal parameter variety: there exists a flat family $\mathfrak{X} \to \mathfrak{M}_g$ of curves of genus $g$ such that for any other flat family $X \to T$ of curves of genus $g$, there is a unique morphism $T \to \mathfrak{M}_g$ making $X$ the pullback of $\mathfrak{X}$. Such a universal family does not exist for $g \geqslant 2$, because there are nontrivial families of curves all of whose fibres are isomorphic to each other.
