The canonical morphism is the map defined by the complete canonical linear system on a curve of genus at least 2. Introduced in Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry, it provides the fundamental link between the intrinsic geometry of a curve (its canonical divisor) and its extrinsic realization as a subvariety of projective space. The behavior of the canonical morphism distinguishes hyperelliptic from nonhyperelliptic curves.
