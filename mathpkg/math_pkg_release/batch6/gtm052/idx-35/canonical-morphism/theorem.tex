Let $X$ be a curve of genus $g \geqslant 2$ over an algebraically closed field $k$. The canonical linear system $|K|$ is effective and base-point-free, hence it determines a morphism

$$\varphi_K: X \to \mathbf{P}^{g-1}$$

called the \text{canonical morphism} of $X$. If $g = 0$, $|K|$ is empty; if $g = 1$, $|K| = 0$ gives the constant map to a point.
