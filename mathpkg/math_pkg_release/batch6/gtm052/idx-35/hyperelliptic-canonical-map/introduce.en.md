Theorem 5.3 from Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry describes the structure of the canonical morphism for hyperelliptic curves. Unlike nonhyperelliptic curves where the canonical map is an embedding, the canonical map of a hyperelliptic curve factors through the 2-to-1 cover of the projective line, with image a rational normal curve. This theorem completes the geometric picture of how the canonical linear system reflects the distinction between hyperelliptic and nonhyperelliptic curves.
