Lemma 6.1 ensures that the divisor of a rational function is well-defined as a finite formal sum. For a nonzero rational function $f$ on a noetherian integral separated scheme that is regular in codimension one, the valuation $v_Y(f)$ along a prime divisor $Y$ vanishes for all but finitely many $Y$. The proof reduces to the affine case where $f$ is regular on $U = \operatorname{Spec} A$, and observes that $v_Y(f) > 0$ only when $Y$ is contained in the zero locus of $f$, which is a proper closed subset and can contain only finitely many prime divisors.
