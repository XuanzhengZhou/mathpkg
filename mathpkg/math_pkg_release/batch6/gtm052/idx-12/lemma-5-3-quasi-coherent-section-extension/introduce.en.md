Lemma 5.3 describes the behavior of sections of a quasi-coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on an affine scheme $X = \operatorname{Spec} A$ with respect to the basic open set $D(f)$. Part (a) states that if a global section vanishes upon restriction to $D(f)$, then it is annihilated by some power of $f$. Part (b) is a local-to-global extension result: any section over $D(f)$ can be multiplied by a sufficiently high power of $f$ to extend to a global section over all of $X$. This lemma is fundamental for proving that quasi-coherent sheaves on affine schemes correspond to modules over the coordinate ring.
