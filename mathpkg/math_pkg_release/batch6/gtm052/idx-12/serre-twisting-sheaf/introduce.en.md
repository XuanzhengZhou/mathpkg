The Serre twisting sheaf $\mathcal{O}_X(n)$ is defined on $X = \operatorname{Proj} S$, where $S$ is a graded ring, as the sheaf associated to the shifted graded module $S(n)$. The case $n = 1$ gives the twisting sheaf of Serre $\mathcal{O}_X(1)$, which plays a central role in projective algebraic geometry. For any sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules $\mathcal{F}$, the twisted sheaf $\mathcal{F}(n)$ is defined as $\mathcal{F} \otimes_{\mathcal{O}_X} \mathcal{O}_X(n)$. This operation allows one to adjust the "degree" of sheaves and is essential for Serre's theorems on coherent sheaves over projective schemes.
