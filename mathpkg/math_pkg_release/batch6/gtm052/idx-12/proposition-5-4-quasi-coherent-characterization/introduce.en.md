Proposition 5.4 provides the fundamental local characterization of quasi-coherent and coherent sheaves on a scheme. A sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules is quasi-coherent exactly when it is isomorphic to the sheaf $\tilde{M}$ associated to an $A$-module $M$ on every open affine subset $U = \operatorname{Spec} A$. In the noetherian case, coherence is equivalent to the additional requirement that each $M$ be finitely generated over $A$. This result establishes the link between the algebraic notion of modules and the geometric notion of sheaves, forming the backbone of scheme theory.
