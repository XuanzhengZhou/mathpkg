The functor $\Gamma_*$ associates to every sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules on $X = \operatorname{Proj} S$ a graded $S$-module $\Gamma_*(\mathcal{F}) = igoplus_{n \in \mathbf{Z}} \Gamma(X, \mathcal{F}(n))$. The module structure is defined using the natural map $\mathcal{F}(n) \otimes \mathcal{O}_X(d) 	o \mathcal{F}(n+d)$ together with the fact that homogeneous elements $s \in S_d$ give global sections of $\mathcal{O}_X(d)$. This construction is the right adjoint to the tilde operation $\sim$ and plays a crucial role in Serre's theorems comparing sheaves on $\operatorname{Proj} S$ with graded $S$-modules.
