(a) For any $p \in X$, the stalk $(	ilde{M})_p = M_{(p)}$ (where $M_{(p)}$ denotes the degree zero part of the localization at the homogeneous prime ideal $p$). For any homogeneous $f \in S_+$, we have $	ilde{M}|_{D_+(f)} \cong (M_{(f)})^{\sim}$ via the isomorphism of $D_+(f)$ with $\operatorname{Spec} S_{(f)}$, where $M_{(f)}$ denotes the group of elements of degree 0 in the localized module $M_f$. Applying this to $M = S(n)$, we see that $\mathcal{O}_X(n)$ is locally free of rank 1, hence invertible.

(b) follows from the same localization isomorphism, noting that $(M(n))_{(f)} = M_{(f)}$ with degrees shifted appropriately, and the compatibility with tensor products follows from $S(n) \otimes_S S(m) \cong S(n+m)$.
