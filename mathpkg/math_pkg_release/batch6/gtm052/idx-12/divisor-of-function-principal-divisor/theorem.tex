Let $X$ be a noetherian integral separated scheme which is regular in codimension one, and let $f \in K^*$. We define the divisor of $f$, denoted $(f)$, by
$$(f) = \sum_Y v_Y(f) \cdot Y,$$
where the sum is taken over all prime divisors $Y$ of $X$. By Lemma 6.1, this is a finite sum, hence it is a divisor. Any divisor which is equal to the divisor of a function is called a principal divisor.

Note that if $f, g \in K^*$, then $(f/g) = (f) - (g)$ and $(fg) = (f) + (g)$.
