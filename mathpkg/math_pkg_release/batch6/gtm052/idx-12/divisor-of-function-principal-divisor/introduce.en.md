The divisor of a rational function (also called a principal divisor) is a formal $\mathbf{Z}$-linear combination of prime divisors, where the coefficient of each prime divisor $Y$ is the valuation $v_Y(f)$ of $f$ along $Y$. Lemma 6.1 guarantees that only finitely many prime divisors appear with nonzero coefficient. Principal divisors form a subgroup of the divisor group, and two divisors that differ by a principal divisor are said to be linearly equivalent. This notion is fundamental to the theory of divisors and linear systems on algebraic varieties.
