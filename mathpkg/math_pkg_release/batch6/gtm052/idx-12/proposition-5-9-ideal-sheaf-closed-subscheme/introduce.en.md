Proposition 5.9 establishes the fundamental correspondence between closed subschemes of a scheme $X$ and quasi-coherent sheaves of ideals on $X$. For any closed subscheme $Y$, its ideal sheaf $\mathcal{I}_Y$ is quasi-coherent (and coherent when $X$ is noetherian). Conversely, every quasi-coherent sheaf of ideals arises uniquely as the ideal sheaf of some closed subscheme. This bijection generalizes the classical correspondence between ideals in a ring and closed subsets of its spectrum.
