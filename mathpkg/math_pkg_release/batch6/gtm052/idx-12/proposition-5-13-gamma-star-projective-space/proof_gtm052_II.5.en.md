We cover $X$ with the open sets $D_+(x_i)$. Then to give a section $t \in \Gamma(X, \mathcal{O}_X(n))$ is the same as giving sections $t_i \in \mathcal{O}_X(n)(D_+(x_i))$ for each $i$, which agree on the intersections $D_+(x_i x_j)$. Now $t_i$ is just a homogeneous element of degree $n$ in the localization $S_{x_i}$, and its restriction to $D_+(x_i x_j)$ is just the image of that element in $S_{x_i x_j}$. Summing over all $n$, we see that $\Gamma_*(\mathcal{O}_X)$ can be identified with the set of $(r+1)$-tuples of elements in the localizations that agree on the intersections, which is precisely $S$ itself.
