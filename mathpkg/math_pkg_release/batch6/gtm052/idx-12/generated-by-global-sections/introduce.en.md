A sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules is said to be generated by global sections if its stalks $\mathcal{F}_x$ are spanned by the images of global sections $s_i \in \Gamma(X, \mathcal{F})$. Equivalently, $\mathcal{F}$ admits a surjection from a (possibly infinite) free sheaf $igoplus_i \mathcal{O}_X 	woheadrightarrow \mathcal{F}$. This property is a central technical condition in algebraic geometry: it is the sheaf-theoretic analogue of a module being generated by its elements, and it is the key hypothesis in Serre's theorem on embedding schemes into projective space.
