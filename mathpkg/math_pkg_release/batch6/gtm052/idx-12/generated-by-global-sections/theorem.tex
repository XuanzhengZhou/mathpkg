Let $X$ be a scheme, and let $\mathcal{F}$ be a sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules. We say that $\mathcal{F}$ is generated by global sections if there is a family of global sections $\{s_i\}_{i \in I}$, $s_i \in \Gamma(X, \mathcal{F})$, such that for each $x \in X$, the images of $s_i$ in the stalk $\mathcal{F}_x$ generate that stalk as an $\mathcal{O}_x$-module.

Note that $\mathcal{F}$ is generated by global sections if and only if $\mathcal{F}$ can be written as a quotient of a free sheaf. Indeed, the generating sections $\{s_i\}_{i \in I}$ define a surjective morphism of sheaves $\bigoplus_{i \in I} \mathcal{O}_X \rightarrow \mathcal{F}$, and conversely.
