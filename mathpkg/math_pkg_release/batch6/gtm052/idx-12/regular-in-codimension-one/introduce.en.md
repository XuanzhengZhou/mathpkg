A scheme $X$ is said to be regular in codimension one if all its local rings of Krull dimension one are regular local rings. The most important examples are nonsingular varieties over a field and noetherian normal schemes. This condition is the minimal regularity hypothesis needed to develop the theory of Weil divisors: it ensures that the local ring at the generic point of each prime divisor is a discrete valuation ring, whose valuation can be used to assign order of vanishing to rational functions.
