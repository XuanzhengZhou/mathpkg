Theorem 5.17, due to Serre, is one of the foundational results of projective algebraic geometry. It states that for any coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on a projective scheme $X$ over a noetherian ring, twisting $\mathcal{F}$ by a sufficiently high power of a very ample invertible sheaf $\mathcal{O}(1)$ yields a sheaf $\mathcal{F}(n)$ that is generated by finitely many global sections. This theorem bridges the gap between the affine case (where quasi-coherent sheaves are always generated by global sections) and the projective case, and is the key technical tool for proving finiteness theorems in cohomology of coherent sheaves.
