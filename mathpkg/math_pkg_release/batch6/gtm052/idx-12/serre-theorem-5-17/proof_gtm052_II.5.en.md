Let $i: X ightarrow \mathbf{P}_A^r$ be a closed immersion of $X$ into projective space such that $\mathcal{O}_X(1) \cong i^*\mathcal{O}_{\mathbf{P}^r}(1)$. Then $i_*\mathcal{F}$ is a coherent sheaf on $\mathbf{P}_A^r$ (by (5.8) and (5.4)), so it suffices to prove the theorem for $X = \mathbf{P}_A^r$. In this case, $\mathcal{F}$ corresponds to a finitely generated graded $S$-module $M$ (where $S = A[x_0, \ldots, x_r]$). Then for $n$ sufficiently large, $M_n$ (the degree $n$ part of $M$) generates $\mathcal{F}(n)$ as an $\mathcal{O}_X$-module, because the sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ is obtained from $M$ by the tilde construction, and the twisting operation shifts degrees. The finite generation of $M$ ensures that for large $n$, the generators of $M$ in various degrees produce enough global sections to generate all stalks.
