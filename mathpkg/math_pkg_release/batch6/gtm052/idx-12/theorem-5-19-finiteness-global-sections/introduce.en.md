Theorem 5.19 is a fundamental finiteness result: for a projective scheme $X$ over a finitely generated $k$-algebra $A$, the space of global sections $\Gamma(X, \mathcal{F})$ of any coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ is a finitely generated $A$-module. When $A = k$ is a field, this says that $\Gamma(X, \mathcal{F})$ is a finite-dimensional $k$-vector space. This generalizes the classical fact that the space of global regular functions on a projective variety is finite-dimensional (in fact, just the constants). The theorem is a direct consequence of Serre's Theorem 5.17 and is the starting point for the cohomological study of coherent sheaves on projective varieties.
