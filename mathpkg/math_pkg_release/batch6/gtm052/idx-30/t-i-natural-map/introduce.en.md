Proposition 12.5 constructs a natural map $\varphi: T^i(A) \otimes M \to T^i(M)$ for any $A$-module $M$, arising from the functoriality of $T^i$. The proposition then establishes that right exactness of $T^i$ is equivalent to $\varphi$ being an isomorphism (or even just surjective) for all $M$. This result, combined with Proposition 12.4, is a key step toward the base change theorem, as it relates the abstract functor $T^i$ to the simpler operation of tensoring with $T^i(A)$.
