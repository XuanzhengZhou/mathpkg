Proposition 12.1 establishes that each $T^i$ is an additive, covariant functor from $A$-modules to $A$-modules that is exact in the middle, and the collection $(T^i)_{i \geqslant 0}$ forms a $\delta$-functor. This follows from the flatness of $\mathcal{F}$ over $Y$ and the fact that cohomology can be computed using a Cech resolution. The key algebraic ingredient is that $T^i(M)$ can be expressed as $h^i(C^\bullet \otimes_A M)$ for a suitable complex $C^\bullet$ of $A$-modules.
