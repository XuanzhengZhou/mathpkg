Proposition 12.4 gives equivalent characterizations of when the functor $T^i$ is left exact. Using the complex $L^\bullet$ of finitely generated free $A$-modules from Lemma 12.3, one defines $W^i = \operatorname{coker}(d^{i-1})$. Then $T^i$ is left exact if and only if $W^i$ is a flat $A$-module, which is equivalent to $W^i$ being projective (since it is finitely generated over a noetherian ring). A third equivalent condition is that $T^i$ is representable by a finitely generated $A$-module $Q$, i.e., $T^i(M) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_A(Q, M)$. The finite generation of $Q$ is a nontrivial consequence of the geometric origin of $T^i$.
