Theorem 12.8, the Semicontinuity Theorem, is one of the central results in the cohomology of projective morphisms. It states that for a projective morphism $f: X \to Y$ and a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$ flat over $Y$, the dimension of the cohomology of the fibres $h^i(y, \mathcal{F}) = \dim_{k(y)} H^i(X_y, \mathcal{F}_y)$ varies upper semicontinuously as a function of $y \in Y$. This means that the cohomology dimension can jump up on closed subsets but never jump down. The proof reduces to the local affine case $Y = \operatorname{Spec} A$ and uses the $T^i$ functors together with the criterion for left exactness (Proposition 12.4).
