The $T^i$ functors are a family of additive covariant functors from the category of $A$-modules to itself, defined by $T^i(M) = H^i(X, \mathcal{F} \otimes_A M)$, where $f: X \to Y = \operatorname{Spec} A$ is a projective morphism and $\mathcal{F}$ is a coherent sheaf on $X$ flat over $Y$. These functors interpolate between the cohomology of the total space $H^i(X, \mathcal{F})$ and the cohomology of the fibres $H^i(X_y, \mathcal{F}_y)$, with $T^i(A) = H^i(X, \mathcal{F})$ and $T^i(k(y)) = H^i(X_y, \mathcal{F}_y)$. Grothendieck's insight is to study $T^i$ as a functor on all $A$-modules, enabling the use of homological algebra to prove semicontinuity and base change theorems for cohomology.
