Theorem 5.4 from Chapter V, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry states that any birational morphism $f: X' \to X$ between nonsingular projective surfaces factors as a composition of exactly $n(f)$ monoidal transformations, where $n(f)$ counts the number of irreducible curves in $X'$ that are contracted to points by $f$. This theorem is the key structural result linking birational geometry of surfaces with the explicit operation of blowing up points. The proof proceeds by induction on $n(f)$: as long as $n(f) > 0$, one factors $f$ through the monoidal transformation centered at a fundamental point of $f^{-1}$, reducing $n(f)$ by one each step until an isomorphism is reached.
