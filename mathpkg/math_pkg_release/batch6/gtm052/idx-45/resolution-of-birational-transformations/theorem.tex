Let $T: X \dashrightarrow X'$ be a birational transformation of nonsingular projective surfaces. Then there exists a nonsingular projective surface $X''$ and birational morphisms $f: X'' \to X$ and $g: X'' \to X'$ such that $T = g \circ f^{-1}$. In other words, any birational transformation can be resolved by a finite sequence of monoidal transformations and their inverses.
