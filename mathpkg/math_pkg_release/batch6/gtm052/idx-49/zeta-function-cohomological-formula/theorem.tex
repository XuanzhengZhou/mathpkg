Let $X$ be projective and smooth over $k = \mathbf{F}_q$, of dimension $n$. Then

$$Z(X,t) = \frac{P_1(t) \cdots P_{2n-1}(t)}{P_0(t) P_2(t) \cdots P_{2n}(t)}$$

where $P_i(t) = \det(1 - f^* t; H^i(\bar{X}, \mathbf{Q}_l))$ and $f^*$ is the endomorphism induced by the Frobenius morphism $f: \bar{X} \rightarrow \bar{X}$.

Furthermore, $P_0(t) = 1 - t$. The Frobenius morphism is a finite morphism of degree $q^n$, hence it must act as multiplication by $q^n$ on a generator of $H^{2n}(\bar{X}, \mathbf{Q}_l)$. So $P_{2n}(t) = 1 - q^n t$. If we provisionally define the $i$th Betti number $B_i$ as $\dim H^i(\bar{X}, \mathbf{Q}_l)$, then $B_i = \text{degree } P_i(t)$, and one can show easily that the invariant $E$ of $X$ is given by $E = \sum (-1)^i B_i$.
