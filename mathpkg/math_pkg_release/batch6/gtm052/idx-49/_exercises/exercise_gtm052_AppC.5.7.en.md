Use (V, Ex. 1.10) to prove the analogue of the Riemann hypothesis (1.3) for any curve $C$ of genus $g$ defined over $\mathbf{F}_q$. Write $N_r = 1 - a_r + q^r$. Then according to (V, Ex. 1.10),

$$|a_r| \leqslant 2g\sqrt{q^r}.$$

On the other hand, by (4.2) the zeta function of $C$ can be written

$$Z(t) = \frac{P_1(t)}{(1 - t)(1 - qt)}$$

where $P_1(t) = \prod_{i=1}^{2g} (1 - \alpha_i t)$. Show that $|a_r| \leqslant 2g\sqrt{q^r}$ for all $r$ implies $|\alpha_i| = \sqrt{q}$ for all $i$.
