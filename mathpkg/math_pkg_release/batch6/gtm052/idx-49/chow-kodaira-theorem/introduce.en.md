The Chow-Kodaira theorem characterizes projective algebraic surfaces among compact complex manifolds of dimension 2: a compact complex surface with two algebraically independent meromorphic functions is necessarily projective algebraic. This provides a concrete criterion for algebraicity in dimension 2, complementing the more general results about Moishezon manifolds.
