Chow's Theorem states that any compact analytic subspace of complex projective space $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{C}}^n$ is necessarily algebraic, i.e., it arises as the associated analytic space $X_h$ of some projective subscheme $X$. Serre obtained a new proof of this result as a corollary of his GAGA theorem, answering affirmatively the question of whether projective analytic objects come from algebraic geometry.
