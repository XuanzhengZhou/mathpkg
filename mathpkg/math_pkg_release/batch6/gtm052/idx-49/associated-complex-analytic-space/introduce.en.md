Given a scheme $X$ of finite type over $\mathbf{C}$, one constructs its associated complex analytic space $X_h$ by writing $X$ as a gluing of affine schemes $\operatorname{Spec} \mathbf{C}[x_1,\ldots,x_n]/(f_1,\ldots,f_q)$, interpreting the polynomials $f_i$ as holomorphic functions, and gluing the resulting analytic zero sets. This defines a functor $h$ from the category of $\mathbf{C}$-schemes of finite type to the category of complex analytic spaces, forming the basis of Serre's GAGA correspondence.
