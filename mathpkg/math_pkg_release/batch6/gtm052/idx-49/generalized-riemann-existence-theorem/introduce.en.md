The Generalized Riemann Existence Theorem, proved by Grauert-Remmert and reproved by Grothendieck using Hironaka's resolution of singularities, states that any finite morphism of analytic spaces over $X_h$ comes from an algebraic finite morphism. A key corollary is that the algebraic fundamental group $\pi_1^{\text{alg}}(X)$ is isomorphic to the profinite completion of the topological fundamental group $\pi_1^{\text{top}}(X_h)$.
