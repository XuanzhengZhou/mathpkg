Let $X$ be a normal scheme of finite type over $\mathbf{C}$. Let $\mathfrak{X}'$ be a normal complex analytic space, together with a finite morphism $\mathfrak{f}: \mathfrak{X}' \rightarrow X_h$. (We define a finite morphism of analytic spaces to be a proper morphism with finite fibres.) Then there is a unique normal scheme $X'$ and a finite morphism $g: X' \rightarrow X$ such that $X_h' \cong \mathfrak{X}'$ and $g_h = \mathfrak{f}$.
