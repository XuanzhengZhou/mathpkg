Assuming (1.3), we can define the $i$th Betti number $B_i = B_i(X)$ to be the degree of the polynomial $P_i(t)$. Then we have $E = \sum (-1)^i B_i$. Furthermore, suppose that $X$ is obtained from a variety $Y$ defined over an algebraic number ring $R$, by reduction modulo a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of $R$. Then $B_i(X)$ is equal to the $i$th Betti number of the topological space $Y_h = (Y \times_R \mathbf{C})_h$, i.e., $B_i(X)$ is the rank of the ordinary cohomology group $H^i(Y_h, \mathbf{Z})$.
