The first Weil conjecture states that the zeta function of a smooth projective variety over a finite field is a rational function. This was proven by Dwork using $p$-adic methods, and later by Grothendieck using $l$-adic cohomology, which expresses the zeta function as an alternating product of characteristic polynomials of the Frobenius endomorphism acting on cohomology.
