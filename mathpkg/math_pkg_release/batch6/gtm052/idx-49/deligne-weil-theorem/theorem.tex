With the hypotheses of (4.2) (i.e., $X$ is projective and smooth over $k = \mathbf{F}_q$ of dimension $n$), the polynomials $P_i(t)$ have integer coefficients, independent of $l$, and they can be written

$$P_i(t) = \prod_j (1 - \alpha_{ij} t)$$

where the $\alpha_{ij}$ are algebraic integers with $|\alpha_{ij}| = q^{i/2}$.

This result completes the solution of the Weil conjectures. Note that it implies that the polynomials $P_i(t)$ of (4.2) are the same as those of (1.3), and hence the two definitions of the Betti numbers agree.
