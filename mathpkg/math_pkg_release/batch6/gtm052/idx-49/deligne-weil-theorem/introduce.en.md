Deligne's 1973 theorem, proving the analogue of the Riemann hypothesis for varieties over finite fields, was the culmination of the $l$-adic cohomology program initiated by Grothendieck. The proof uses the deeper properties of $l$-adic cohomology developed in SGA 4, SGA 5, and SGA 7, particularly Lefschetz pencils and the monodromy action on cohomology near singular fibers. This established that the complex absolute values of the reciprocal roots of $P_i(t)$ are precisely $q^{i/2}$.
