The $l$-adic cohomology groups satisfy a list of formal properties analogous to those of singular cohomology for complex manifolds. These include finite-dimensionality, functoriality, cup-products, a comparison theorem with classical cohomology for varieties over $\mathbf{C}$, and a cycle class map from the Chow ring to cohomology. These properties make $l$-adic cohomology a Weil cohomology theory capable of proving the Weil conjectures.
