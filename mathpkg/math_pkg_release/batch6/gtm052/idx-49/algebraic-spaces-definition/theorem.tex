Over any field $k$, an algebraic space is defined to be something which is locally a quotient of a scheme by an etale equivalence relation. The category of algebraic spaces contains the category of schemes. If $X$ is an algebraic space of finite type over $\mathbf{C}$, one can define its associated complex analytic space $X_h$. Artin shows that the category of smooth proper algebraic spaces over $\mathbf{C}$ is equivalent, via the functor $h$, to the category of Moishezon manifolds.
