Algebraic spaces, introduced by Artin and Knutson, generalize schemes by allowing quotients by etale equivalence relations. They form a category containing schemes as a full subcategory. Remarkably, Artin proved that smooth proper algebraic spaces over $\mathbf{C}$ correspond exactly, via the analytification functor $h$, to Moishezon manifolds, providing an algebraic framework for studying these analytic objects.
