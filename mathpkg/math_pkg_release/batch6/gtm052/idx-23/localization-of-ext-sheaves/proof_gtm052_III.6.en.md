Both sides give $\delta$-functors in $\mathcal{G}$ from $\operatorname{Mod}(X)$ to $\operatorname{Mod}(U)$. For $i=0$, both sides yield $\mathcal{H}om_X(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G})|_U \cong \mathcal{H}om_U(\mathcal{F}|_U,\mathcal{G}|_U)$, which holds by the local nature of the sheaf Hom (II, §5). For $i > 0$ and $\mathcal{G}$ injective, both sides vanish — the left side because $\mathcal{G}$ is injective hence $\mathcal{E}xt^i_X(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}) = 0$ by (6.1), and the right side because the restriction of an injective sheaf to an open subset is injective in $\operatorname{Mod}(U)$, so $\mathcal{E}xt^i_U(\mathcal{F}|_U,\mathcal{G}|_U) = 0$. By the universal property of $\delta$-functors (1.3A), the two $\delta$-functors are isomorphic.
