For a flat morphism $f: X \to Y$ of finite type over a field, the local dimension of the fibre $X_y$ at a point $x$ obeys the simple formula $\dim_x(X_y) = \dim_x X - \dim_y Y$. This shows that flat morphisms have well-behaved fibre dimensions: the dimension of the total space at $x$ equals the sum of the fibre dimension and the base dimension. The proof relies on base-changing to the local ring at $y$ and applying properties of flat local homomorphisms.
