For a projective morphism $f: X \to Y$ of noetherian schemes and a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$, the higher direct images $R^i f_*(\mathcal{F})$ are coherent sheaves on $Y$ for all $i \geq 0$. Furthermore, for sufficiently large twists $n \gg 0$, the natural adjunction map $f^* f_*(\mathcal{F}(n)) \to \mathcal{F}(n)$ is surjective. This is a fundamental finiteness theorem in algebraic geometry, generalizing the fact that cohomology of coherent sheaves on projective varieties is finite-dimensional.
