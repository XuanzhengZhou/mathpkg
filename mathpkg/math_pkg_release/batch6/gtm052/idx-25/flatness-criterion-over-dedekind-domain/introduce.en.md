Over a regular one-dimensional base scheme $Y$ (e.g., a nonsingular curve), a morphism $f: X \to Y$ is flat precisely when no associated point of $X$ maps to a closed point of $Y$. Equivalently, every associated (or generic, in the reduced case) point of $X$ maps to the generic point of $Y$. This criterion is extremely useful for checking flatness in concrete geometric situations and is proved by reducing to discrete valuation rings, where flatness is equivalent to torsion-freeness.
