Flatness satisfies a two-of-three property for short exact sequences of modules. If the outer two terms of a short exact sequence are flat, the middle term is flat. Conversely, if the middle and right terms are flat, the left term is flat. This follows from the long exact sequence of Tor and is a fundamental stability property used in deformation theory and flat descent.
