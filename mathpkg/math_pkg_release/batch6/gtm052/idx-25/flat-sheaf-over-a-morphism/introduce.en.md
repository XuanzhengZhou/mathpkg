An $\mathcal{O}_X$-module $\mathcal{F}$ is flat over $Y$ via a morphism $f: X \to Y$ if all its stalks are flat modules over the corresponding stalks of $Y$. This generalizes the notion of flat modules to the geometric setting: flatness is checked stalk-locally. When $\mathcal{F} = \mathcal{O}_X$, this defines a flat morphism $f$. Flatness ensures that the sheaf-theoretic operations behave well under base change, and it is the key condition for defining a good notion of algebraic families.
