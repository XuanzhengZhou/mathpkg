For a flat morphism $f: X \to Y$ of finite type over a field with irreducible base $Y$, the equidimensionality of the total space (all components of dimension $\dim Y + n$) is equivalent to all fibres $X_y$ being equidimensional of dimension $n$. This gives a clean geometric characterization: flat families have fibres of constant dimension precisely when the total space is equidimensional.
