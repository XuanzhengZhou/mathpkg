Cohomology of quasi-coherent sheaves commutes with flat base change. Given a cartesian square where the base morphism $u: Y' \to Y$ is flat, pulling back higher direct images along $u$ yields the same result as taking higher direct images of the pulled-back sheaf along $g$. This is proven by reducing to the affine case and using Čech cohomology, where flatness guarantees that the functor $-\otimes_A A'$ is exact and thus commutes with taking cohomology of the Čech complex.
