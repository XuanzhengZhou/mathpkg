Corollary 1.4 establishes that the right derived functors $(R^i F)$ are the unique (up to canonical isomorphism) universal $\delta$-functor extending $F$. This provides an alternative characterization: rather than constructing derived functors via injective resolutions, one can verify that a candidate $\delta$-functor $T$ with $T^0 \cong F$ satisfies the universal property (e.g., by effaceability), and conclude $T^i \cong R^i F$. This is how one proves that Cech cohomology agrees with derived functor cohomology for schemes.
