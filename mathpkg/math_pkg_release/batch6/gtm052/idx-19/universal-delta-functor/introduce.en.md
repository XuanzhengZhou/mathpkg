A universal $\delta$-functor is a $\delta$-functor $T$ satisfying a universal property: any morphism of functors from $T^0$ to the degree-zero part of another $\delta$-functor extends uniquely to a morphism of $\delta$-functors. This property characterizes derived functors abstractly: when $\mathfrak{A}$ has enough injectives, the derived functors $(R^i F)_{i \geqslant 0}$ form a universal $\delta$-functor with $R^0 F \cong F$. The uniqueness part implies that any two $\delta$-functor structures extending $F$ with the universal property are canonically isomorphic.
