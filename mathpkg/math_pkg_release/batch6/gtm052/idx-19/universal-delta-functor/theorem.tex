A \text{$\delta$-functor} between abelian categories $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ is a sequence of additive functors $T = (T^i)_{i \geqslant 0}$ together with morphisms $\delta^i: T^i(A'') \to T^{i+1}(A')$ for each short exact sequence $0 \to A' \to A \to A'' \to 0$, making the associated long sequence exact.

A $\delta$-functor $T$ is \text{universal} if, given any other $\delta$-functor $T'$ and a morphism of functors $f^0: T^0 \to T'^0$, there exists a unique sequence of morphisms $f^i: T^i \to T'^i$ for each $i \geqslant 0$, starting with the given $f^0$, which commute with the $\delta^i$ for each short exact sequence.

If a covariant additive functor $F: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ admits a universal $\delta$-functor $T$ with $T^0 = F$, then such $T$ is unique up to unique isomorphism. The functors $T^i$ are then called the \text{right satellite functors} of $F$.
