An object $J$ is acyclic for a left exact functor $F$ if all its higher derived functors vanish: $R^i F(J) = 0$ for $i > 0$. Injective objects are always acyclic (by Theorem 1.1A(e)), but acyclicity is a weaker condition. Acyclic objects are important because their resolutions---called $F$-acyclic resolutions---can be used to compute derived functors instead of injective resolutions, which are often harder to construct in practice.
