A left exact functor between abelian categories preserves exactness at the leftmost nontrivial position: it sends a short exact sequence to a sequence that is exact at the first two terms but may fail exactness at the last. Left exactness is the essential property of the global sections functor on sheaves, the Hom functor, and many other naturally occurring functors. Right derived functors provide a systematic way to measure and extend the failure of right exactness.
