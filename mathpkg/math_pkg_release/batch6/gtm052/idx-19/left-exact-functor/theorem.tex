Let $F: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ be an additive covariant functor between abelian categories. $F$ is \text{left exact} if for every short exact sequence
$$0 \to A' \to A \to A'' \to 0$$
in $\mathfrak{A}$, the sequence
$$0 \to F A' \to F A \to F A''$$
is exact in $\mathfrak{B}$. $F$ is \text{right exact} if instead
$$F A' \to F A \to F A'' \to 0$$
is exact. $F$ is \text{exact} if it is both left and right exact. If only $F A' \to F A \to F A''$ is exact, we say $F$ is \text{exact in the middle}.

For a contravariant functor, the analogous definitions are made with the order reversed: e.g., $F$ is left exact if $0 \to F A'' \to F A \to F A'$ is exact for every short exact sequence as above.
