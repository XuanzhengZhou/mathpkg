Theorem 1.1A is the fundamental existence theorem for right derived functors, stating that for any left exact functor $F$ from an abelian category with enough injectives, there exist additive functors $R^i F$ satisfying the defining properties of a $\delta$-functor. The theorem establishes the long exact cohomology sequence (property b), naturality of connecting morphisms (c-d), and the vanishing of higher derived functors on injectives (e). This theorem is the foundation for all sheaf cohomology: one applies it to the global sections functor $\Gamma(X, \cdot)$ on the category of sheaves of abelian groups.
