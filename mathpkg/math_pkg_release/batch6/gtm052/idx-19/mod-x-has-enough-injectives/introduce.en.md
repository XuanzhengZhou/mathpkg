Proposition 2.2 is the foundational result that makes sheaf cohomology via derived functors possible for any ringed space. For each sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ and each point $x$, one embeds the stalk $\mathcal{F}_x$ into an injective $\mathcal{O}_{x,X}$-module $I_x$, then forms the product sheaf $\mathcal{I} = \prod_x j_*(I_x)$ where $j$ is the inclusion of $\{x\}$. This sheaf is injective, and the natural map $\mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{I}$ is a monomorphism. Consequently, the category $\operatorname{Mod}(X)$ has enough injectives, and Theorem 1.1A applies to any left exact functor defined on it.
