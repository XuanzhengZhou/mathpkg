A complex in an abelian category is a sequence of objects connected by morphisms (called differentials) such that the composition of any two consecutive differentials vanishes. This condition, $d^{i+1} \circ d^i = 0$, is equivalent to the inclusion $\operatorname{im} d^i \subseteq \ker d^{i+1}$ and is the fundamental algebraic structure underlying homology and cohomology theories. Complexes form the input data for derived functors and spectral sequences.
