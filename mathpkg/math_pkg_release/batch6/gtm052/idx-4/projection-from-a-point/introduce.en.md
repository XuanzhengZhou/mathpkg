Projection from a point is a fundamental construction in projective geometry. Given a hyperplane $\mathbf{P}^n \subset \mathbf{P}^{n+1}$ and a point $P$ not on it, each point $Q \neq P$ determines a unique line through $P$ and $Q$; the projection maps $Q$ to the intersection of this line with the hyperplane. This map is a morphism of varieties, and its restriction to any projective subvariety $Y$ not containing $P$ yields a morphism $Y \to \mathbf{P}^n$, which is useful for studying projective varieties by projecting them to lower-dimensional spaces.
