To blow up a closed subvariety $Y \subseteq \mathbf{A}^n$ at the origin, take the strict transform $\tilde{Y}$, defined as the closure of $\varphi^{-1}(Y - O)$ in the blow-up $X$ of $\mathbf{A}^n$ at $O$. The resulting morphism $\varphi: \tilde{Y} \to Y$ is birational: it is an isomorphism away from the exceptional set $\varphi^{-1}(O)$. Geometrically, blowing up replaces the point $O$ by the projectivized tangent directions to $Y$ at $O$, "separating" branches that intersect at the point. This construction generalizes to blowing up arbitrary subvarieties and is the primary tool for resolution of singularities.
