This result states that the Krull dimension of the local ring $\mathcal{O}_P$ of a variety $X$ at a point $P$ equals the dimension of the variety itself. This is a fundamental compatibility between the algebraic notion of dimension (Krull dimension of the local ring) and the geometric notion of dimension (transcendence degree of the function field, or topological dimension). The proof proceeds by reducing to the affine case and using the relationship between the height of prime ideals and the dimension of their corresponding subvarieties.
