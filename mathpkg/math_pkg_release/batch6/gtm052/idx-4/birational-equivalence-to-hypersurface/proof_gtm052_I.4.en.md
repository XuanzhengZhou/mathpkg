Let $X$ be a variety of dimension $r$. By Theorem 4.4, it suffices to show its function field $K(X)$ is the function field of a hypersurface. Since $k$ is algebraically closed (hence perfect), $K(X)/k$ is separably generated by Theorem 4.8A. By definition there exists a separating transcendence base $\{t_1, \ldots, t_r\}$ such that $K(X)$ is a finite separable extension of $k(t_1, \ldots, t_r)$. By the Theorem of the Primitive Element (4.6A), there exists $\alpha \in K(X)$ such that $K(X) = k(t_1, \ldots, t_r)(\alpha)$. Let $f(T) \in k(t_1, \ldots, t_r)[T]$ be the minimal polynomial of $\alpha$. Clearing denominators gives an irreducible polynomial $F(t_1, \ldots, t_r, T) \in k[t_1, \ldots, t_r, T]$. The hypersurface $H \subset \mathbf{A}^{r+1}$ defined by $F = 0$ has function field $K(H) = k(t_1, \ldots, t_r)[T]/(F) \cong K(X)$. By Theorem 4.4, $X$ is birationally equivalent to $H$.
