This proposition states that any $r$-dimensional variety over an algebraically closed field is birationally equivalent to a hypersurface in $\mathbf{A}^{r+1}$. The proof combines the category equivalence of Theorem 4.4 with field-theoretic results: the function field $K(X)$ is separably generated (Theorem 4.8A), contains a separating transcendence base of size $r$ (Theorem 4.7A), and is a finite separable extension thereof, hence generated by a single primitive element (Theorem 4.6A). This yields an embedding of $K(X)$ as a finite separable extension of $k(t_1, \ldots, t_r)$, corresponding to a hypersurface.
