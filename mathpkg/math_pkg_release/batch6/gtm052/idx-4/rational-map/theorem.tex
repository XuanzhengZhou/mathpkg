Let $X, Y$ be varieties. A rational map $\varphi: X \dashrightarrow Y$ is an equivalence class of pairs $\langle U, \varphi_U \rangle$ where $U \subseteq X$ is a nonempty open subset and $\varphi_U: U \to Y$ is a morphism. Two pairs $\langle U, \varphi_U \rangle$ and $\langle V, \varphi_V \rangle$ are equivalent if $\varphi_U = \varphi_V$ on $U \cap V$.

The rational map is dominant if the image of some (equivalently, any) representative $\varphi_U$ is dense in $Y$.
