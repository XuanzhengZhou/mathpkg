We must show for any point $P \in Y$ and any open set $U$ containing $P$, that there exists an open affine set $V$ with $P \in V \subseteq U$. Since $Y$ is covered by open affine subsets, we may assume $Y$ is affine. Then consider the closed subset $Y - U$. Since $Y$ is affine, its topology has a basis of principal open sets $D(f)$, so we can find $f \in A(Y)$ such that $P \in D(f) \subseteq U$. The principal open set $D(f)$ is precisely the complement of the hypersurface $V(f)$, which by Lemma 4.2 is isomorphic to an affine variety.
