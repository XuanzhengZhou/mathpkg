This proposition asserts that every variety can be covered by open affine subsets, and moreover these open affines form a basis for the Zariski topology. This means any point has arbitrarily small affine neighborhoods. The proof uses Lemma 4.2 to show that removing a hypersurface from an affine open yields another affine open, allowing one to shrink affine neighborhoods as needed. This result is essential for reducing local questions about varieties to the affine case.
