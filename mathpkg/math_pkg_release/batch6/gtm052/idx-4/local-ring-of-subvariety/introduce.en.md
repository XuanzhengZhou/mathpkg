The local ring $\mathcal{O}_{Y,X}$ of a subvariety $Y$ on a variety $X$ generalizes the local ring of a point $\mathcal{O}_P$. It is defined via equivalence classes of regular functions on open subsets meeting $Y$, with equivalence given by agreement on the intersection. This ring captures the local behavior of $X$ along the entire subvariety $Y$, has residue field $K(Y)$ (the function field of $Y$), and dimension $\dim X - \dim Y$. When $Y$ is not a point, the residue field $K(Y)$ is not algebraically closed, yielding local rings with non-algebraically-closed residue fields.
