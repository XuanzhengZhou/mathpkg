A projective variety $Y \subseteq \mathbf{P}^n$ is projectively normal if its homogeneous coordinate ring $S(Y)$ is integrally closed. This is a stronger condition than ordinary normality: every projectively normal variety is normal, but the converse fails -- there exist normal projective varieties that are not projectively normal, such as the twisted quartic curve in $\mathbf{P}^3$. Projective normality depends on the specific embedding of the variety in projective space, not just on the abstract variety itself.
