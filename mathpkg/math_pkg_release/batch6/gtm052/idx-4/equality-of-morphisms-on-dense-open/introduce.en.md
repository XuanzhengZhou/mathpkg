This lemma establishes that two morphisms from a variety $X$ to a variety $Y$ are identical if they agree on any nonempty open subset $U \subseteq X$. Since nonempty open subsets of irreducible varieties are dense, this means the behavior of a morphism on a dense open set determines it uniquely. The proof uses the Segre embedding to reduce to the case $Y = \mathbf{P}^n$ and exploits the fact that the diagonal $\Delta \subseteq \mathbf{P}^n \times \mathbf{P}^n$ is closed, which reflects the separatedness of projective varieties.
