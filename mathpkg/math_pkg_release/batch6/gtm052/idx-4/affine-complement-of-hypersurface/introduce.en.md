This lemma shows that removing a hypersurface from affine space yields an affine variety. Specifically, $\mathbf{A}^n - Y$ is isomorphic to the hypersurface $H$ in $\mathbf{A}^{n+1}$ defined by $x_{n+1}f = 1$, whose affine coordinate ring is the localization $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]_f$. This result is fundamental for constructing open affine subsets on arbitrary varieties and is a key step in proving that every variety has a base of open affine subsets.
