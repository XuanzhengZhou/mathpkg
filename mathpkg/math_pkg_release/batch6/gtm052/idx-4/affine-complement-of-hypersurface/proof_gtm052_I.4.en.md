For $P = (a_1, \ldots, a_{n+1}) \in H$, let $\varphi(P) = (a_1, \ldots, a_n)$. Then clearly $\varphi$ is a morphism from $H$ to $\mathbf{A}^n$, corresponding to the homomorphism of rings $A \to A_f$, where $A = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$. It is also clear that $\varphi$ gives a bijective mapping of $H$ onto its image, which is $\mathbf{A}^n - Y$. To show that $\varphi$ is an isomorphism, it is sufficient to show that $\varphi^{-1}$ is a morphism. But $\varphi^{-1}(a_1, \ldots, a_n) = (a_1, \ldots, a_n, 1/f(a_1, \ldots, a_n))$, so the fact that $\varphi^{-1}$ is a morphism on $\mathbf{A}^n - Y$ follows from (3.6).
