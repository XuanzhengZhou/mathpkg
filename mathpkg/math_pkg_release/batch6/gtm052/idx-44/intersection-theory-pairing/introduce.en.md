An intersection theory on a class of varieties $\mathfrak{V}$ is a system of bilinear pairings $A^r(X) \times A^s(X) \to A^{r+s}(X)$ on the Chow groups of each $X \in \mathfrak{V}$, making the Chow ring $A(X)$ into a commutative graded ring. The pairings must satisfy a set of functoriality and normalization axioms (A1--A7) that ensure compatibility with pullback, pushforward, and the geometry of intersections.
