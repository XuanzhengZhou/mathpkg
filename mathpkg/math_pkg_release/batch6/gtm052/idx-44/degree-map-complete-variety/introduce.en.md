For a complete variety $X$, the degree map $\deg: A^n(X) \to \mathbf{Z}$ sends a zero-cycle $\sum n_i P_i$ to the sum of its coefficients $\sum n_i$. This homomorphism is well-defined on rational equivalence classes, providing a numerical invariant of zero-dimensional cycles. The degree map generalizes the degree of a divisor on a complete curve.
