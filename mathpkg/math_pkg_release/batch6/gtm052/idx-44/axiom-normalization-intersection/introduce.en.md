Axiom A7 of intersection theory provides the normalization condition. When a subvariety $Y$ is intersected with an effective Cartier divisor $Z$ that meets $Y$ properly, the intersection product is the cycle associated to the restricted Cartier divisor on $Y$. This axiom pins down the intersection multiplicities in the simplest geometric situation and implies that transversal intersections of nonsingular subvarieties always have multiplicity 1.
