Rational equivalence is the fundamental equivalence relation on algebraic cycles used in intersection theory. It generalizes linear equivalence of divisors to cycles of arbitrary codimension. For a subvariety $V$, two cycles pushed forward from linearly equivalent Weil divisors on the normalization $\tilde{V}$ are declared rationally equivalent. The quotient of the group of cycles by rational equivalence yields the Chow groups $A^r(X)$.
