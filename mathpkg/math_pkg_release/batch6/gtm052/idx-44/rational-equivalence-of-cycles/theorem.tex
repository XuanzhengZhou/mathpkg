Let $X$ be an algebraic variety. For any subvariety $V$ of $X$, let $f: \tilde{V} \to V$ be the normalization of $V$. Whenever $D$ and $D'$ are linearly equivalent Weil divisors on $\tilde{V}$, we say that $f_* D$ and $f_* D'$ are \text{rationally equivalent} as cycles on $X$. Rational equivalence of cycles on $X$ in general is defined by dividing out by the subgroup generated by all such $f_* D \sim f_* D'$ for all subvarieties $V$ and all linearly equivalent Weil divisors $D, D'$ on $\tilde{V}$.
