Axiom A6 of intersection theory expresses the local nature of intersection multiplicities. When two subvarieties $Y$ and $Z$ intersect properly, their intersection product is a sum over the irreducible components $W_j$ of $Y \cap Z$, weighted by local intersection multiplicities $i(Y,Z; W_j)$. The integer $i(Y,Z; W_j)$ is determined solely by the local geometry near the generic point of $W_j$.
