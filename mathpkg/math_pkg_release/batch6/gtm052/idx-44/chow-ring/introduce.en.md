The Chow ring $A(X)$ is the graded group formed by taking the direct sum of the Chow groups $A^r(X)$ over all codimensions $r = 0, \ldots, \dim X$. In the presence of an intersection product (as in intersection theory on nonsingular varieties), $A(X)$ becomes a graded commutative ring. The degree-zero part is always $A^0(X) = \mathbf{Z}$, and $A^r(X) = 0$ for $r$ exceeding the dimension of $X$.
