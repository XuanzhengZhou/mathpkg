Serre's intersection multiplicity formula defines the local intersection multiplicity $i(Y,Z; W)$ using the alternating sum of lengths of the Tor functors over the local ring $A$ at the generic point of $W$. This algebraic definition is compact and elegant, though it is historically the most recent approach. The higher Tor terms are necessary for correct multiplicities, as illustrated by examples where the naive quotient $A/(\mathfrak{a} + \mathfrak{b})$ gives the wrong length.
