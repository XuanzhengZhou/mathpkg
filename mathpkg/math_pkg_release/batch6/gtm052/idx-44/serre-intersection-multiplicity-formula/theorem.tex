Let $Y$ and $Z$ intersect properly, and let $W$ be an irreducible component of $Y \cap Z$. Let $A$ be the local ring of the generic point of $W$ on $X$, and let $\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{b}$ be the ideals of $Y$ and $Z$ in $A$. Then the local intersection multiplicity is defined by Serre's formula:
$$i(Y,Z; W) = \sum_{i \geq 0} (-1)^i \operatorname{length}_A \operatorname{Tor}_i^A(A/\mathfrak{a}, A/\mathfrak{b}).$$
