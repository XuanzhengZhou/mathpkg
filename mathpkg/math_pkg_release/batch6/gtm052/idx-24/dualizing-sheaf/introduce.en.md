A dualizing sheaf $\omega_X^\circ$ on a proper scheme $X$ of dimension $n$ over a field $k$ is a coherent sheaf equipped with a trace morphism $t: H^n(X, \omega_X^\circ) \to k$ that induces an isomorphism $\operatorname{Hom}(\mathcal{F}, \omega_X^\circ) \cong H^n(X, \mathcal{F})^\vee$ for all coherent sheaves $\mathcal{F}$. This axiomatizes the essential properties of the canonical sheaf on a nonsingular variety and generalizes Serre duality.
