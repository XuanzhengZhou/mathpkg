Over a regular local ring $A$, the projective dimension of every module is bounded by the Krull dimension of $A$. The residue field $k = A/\mathfrak{m}$ achieves the maximum possible projective dimension, namely $\operatorname{pd}(k) = \dim A$. This result, due to Serre, characterizes regular local rings in homological terms and is foundational for the homological study of commutative algebra.
