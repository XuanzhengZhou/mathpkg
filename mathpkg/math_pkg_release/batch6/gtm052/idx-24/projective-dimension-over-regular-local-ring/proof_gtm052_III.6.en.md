See Matsumura [2, Th. 42, p. 131].

The proof uses the characterization of regular local rings via finite global dimension (Serre's theorem). Since $A$ is regular local of dimension $d = \dim A$, the global dimension of $A$ equals $d$. The global dimension is the supremum of projective dimensions of all $A$-modules, hence $\operatorname{pd}(M) \leq d$ for every $M$. For the residue field $k = A/\mathfrak{m}$, one proves $\operatorname{pd}(k) = d$ using the Koszul complex on a minimal set of generators of $\mathfrak{m}$, which forms a regular sequence of length $d$.
