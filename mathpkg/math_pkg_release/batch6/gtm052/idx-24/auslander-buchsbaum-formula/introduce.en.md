The Auslander--Buchsbaum formula states that for a finitely generated module $M$ over a regular local ring $A$ of dimension $n$, the sum of the projective dimension and the depth of $M$ equals $n$: $\operatorname{pd} M + \operatorname{depth} M = n$. This elegant identity links projective dimension, a homological invariant, with depth, a measure of regular sequences, and is one of the fundamental theorems of commutative algebra.
