Let $M$ be an $A$-module. A projective resolution of $M$ is an exact sequence

$$\cdots \to L_i \to L_{i-1} \to \cdots \to L_1 \to L_0 \to M \to 0$$

where each $L_i$ is projective. If $L_i = 0$ for $i > n$ and $L_n \neq 0$, the resolution has \textbf{length} $n$. The \textbf{projective dimension} of $M$, denoted $\operatorname{pd}(M)$, is the least length of a projective resolution of $M$ (or $+\infty$ if there is no finite projective resolution).
