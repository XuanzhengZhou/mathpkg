The duality theorem for projective space $\mathbf{P}_k^n$ establishes that the top cohomology of the canonical sheaf $\omega_X$ is one-dimensional over $k$, the natural pairing between $\operatorname{Hom}(\mathcal{F}, \omega)$ and $H^n(X, \mathcal{F})$ is perfect, and there are functorial isomorphisms $\operatorname{Ext}^i(\mathcal{F}, \omega) \cong H^{n-i}(X, \mathcal{F})^\vee$ for all coherent sheaves $\mathcal{F}$. This is the prototype of Serre duality.
