The residue theorem for algebraic curves states that the sum of the residues of a rational differential form $\tau$ over all closed points of a nonsingular projective curve $X$ is zero: $\sum_{P \in X} \operatorname{res}_P \tau = 0$. This is the algebro-geometric analogue of the classical residue theorem from complex analysis and is foundational for the Riemann--Roch theorem and the theory of algebraic curves.
