This corollary of Serre duality states that for a nonsingular projective variety $X$ of dimension $n$, the cohomology of differential $p$-forms satisfies $H^q(X, \Omega^p) \cong H^{n-q}(X, \Omega^{n-p})^\vee$. The numbers $h^{p,q} = \dim H^q(X, \Omega^p)$ are important birational invariants called Hodge numbers. This symmetry is a fundamental structural result in algebraic geometry.
