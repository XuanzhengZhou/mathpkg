Let $X$ be a noetherian scheme with enough locally frees. Define $K(X)$, the Grothendieck group of coherent sheaves, and $K_1(X)$, the Grothendieck group of locally free sheaves. There is a natural group homomorphism $\varepsilon: K_1(X) \to K(X)$ induced by the inclusion. Show that $\varepsilon$ is an isomorphism (Borel and Serre [1, \S4]) as follows:

(a) Given a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$, use (Ex. 6.8) to show that it has a locally free resolution $\mathcal{E}_\bullet \to \mathcal{F} \to 0$. Then use (6.11A) and (Ex. 6.5) to show that it has a finite locally free resolution
$$0 \to \mathcal{E}_n \to \cdots \to \mathcal{E}_1 \to \mathcal{E}_0 \to \mathcal{F} \to 0.$$

(b) For each $\mathcal{F}$, choose a finite locally free resolution $\mathcal{E}_\bullet \to \mathcal{F} \to 0$, and let $\delta(\mathcal{F}) = \sum (-1)^i \gamma(\mathcal{E}_i)$ in $K_1(X)$. Show that $\delta(\mathcal{F})$ is independent of the resolution chosen, that it defines a homomorphism of $K(X)$ to $K_1(X)$, and finally, that it is an inverse to $\varepsilon$.
