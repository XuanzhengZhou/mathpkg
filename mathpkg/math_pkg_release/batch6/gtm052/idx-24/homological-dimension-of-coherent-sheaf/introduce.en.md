This exercise defines the homological dimension $\operatorname{hd}(\mathcal{F})$ of a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ as the minimum length of a locally free resolution, and establishes its characterization via vanishing of Ext sheaves. The result parallels the module-theoretic notion of projective dimension: $\mathcal{F}$ is locally free iff $\mathcal{E}xt^1(\mathcal{F}, \mathcal{G}) = 0$ for all $\mathcal{G}$, and $\operatorname{hd}(\mathcal{F}) \leq n$ iff all higher Ext sheaves vanish.
