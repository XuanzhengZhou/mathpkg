Theorem 4.11 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry characterizes ample and very ample divisors on a smooth cubic surface $X \subseteq \mathbf{P}^3$. On a cubic surface, the Nakai-Moishezon criterion simplifies dramatically: a divisor $D$ is ample if and only if it intersects every one of the 27 lines positively. Furthermore, on the cubic surface, ampleness and very ampleness are equivalent, so the single condition $D.L > 0$ for all 27 lines suffices.
