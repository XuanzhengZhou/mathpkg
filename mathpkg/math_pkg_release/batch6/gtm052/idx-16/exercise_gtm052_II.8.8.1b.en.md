Generalize (8.8) as follows. With $B, k$ as in part (a), assume furthermore that $k$ is perfect, and that $B$ is a localization of an algebra of finite type over $k$. Then show that $B$ is a regular local ring if and only if $\Omega_{B/k}$ is free of rank equal to $\dim B + \operatorname{tr.deg.} k(B)/k$.
