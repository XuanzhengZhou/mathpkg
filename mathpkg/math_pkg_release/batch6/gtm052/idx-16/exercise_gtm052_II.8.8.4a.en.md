Complete Intersections in $\mathbf{P}^n$. A closed subscheme $Y$ of $\mathbf{P}_k^n$ is called a (strict, global) complete intersection if the homogeneous ideal $I$ of $Y$ in $S = k[x_0, \ldots, x_n]$ can be generated by $r = \operatorname{codim}(Y, \mathbf{P}^n)$ elements (I, Ex. 2.17).

Let $Y$ be a closed subscheme of codimension $r$ in $\mathbf{P}^n$. Then $Y$ is a complete intersection if and only if there are hypersurfaces (i.e., locally principal subschemes of codimension $1$) $H_1, \ldots, H_r$, such that $Y = H_1 \cap \cdots \cap H_r$ as schemes, i.e., $\mathcal{I}_Y = \mathcal{I}_{H_1} + \cdots + \mathcal{I}_{H_r}$. [Hint: Use the fact that the unmixedness theorem holds in $S$ (Matsumura [2, p. 107]).]
