A \textbf{complete intersection} in projective space $\mathbf{P}_k^n$ is a closed subscheme $Y$ whose homogeneous ideal can be generated by the minimal possible number of elements, namely its codimension $r$. Equivalently (as shown in Exercise 8.4(a)), $Y$ is a scheme-theoretic intersection of $r$ hypersurfaces. Complete intersections form an important class of varieties that share many good properties with hypersurfaces: they are Cohen--Macaulay, and normal complete intersections of dimension at least one are projectively normal (Exercise 8.4(b)).
