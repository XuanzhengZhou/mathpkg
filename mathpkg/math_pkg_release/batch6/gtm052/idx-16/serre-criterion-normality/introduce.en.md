\textbf{Serre's criterion} characterizes normal noetherian rings by two local conditions. Condition (1) requires regularity in codimension one: localizations at height-one primes are discrete valuation rings (or fields), which is equivalent to the ring being regular at all primes of height at most one. Condition (2) imposes a depth condition at primes of height at least two, ensuring the ring satisfies the Serre condition $S_2$. Together, these two conditions are equivalent to normality, making the criterion a fundamental tool for checking normality in algebraic geometry and commutative algebra.
