\textbf{Proposition 8.23} establishes two fundamental properties of locally complete intersection subschemes $Y$ inside a nonsingular variety $X$. Part (a) states that such $Y$ are always Cohen--Macaulay, which follows because $X$ is nonsingular (hence Cohen--Macaulay) and the ideal sheaf is locally generated by a regular sequence. Part (b) gives a simple criterion for normality: a locally complete intersection is normal precisely when it is regular in codimension one. This uses Serre's criterion (8.22A): the depth condition is automatically satisfied by the Cohen--Macaulay property from (a).
