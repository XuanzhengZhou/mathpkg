Let $A$ be a complete local ring containing a field $k$. Assume that the residue field $k(A) = A/\mathfrak{m}$ is a separably generated extension of $k$. Then there exists a subfield $K \subseteq A$, containing $k$, such that the natural map $K \to A/\mathfrak{m}$ is an isomorphism. (The subfield $K$ is called a \emph{field of representatives} for $A$.)
