\textbf{Theorem 8.25A (I. S. Cohen)} provides a field of representatives for a complete local ring $A$ containing a field $k$. Under the assumption that the residue field $k(A) = A/\mathfrak{m}$ is a separably generated extension of $k$, there exists a subfield $K \subseteq A$ that maps isomorphically onto the residue field. This result is a key ingredient in Cohen's structure theory of complete local rings. In the context of Hartshorne, it is used in the exercises to extend properties of the sheaf of differentials to non-closed points.
