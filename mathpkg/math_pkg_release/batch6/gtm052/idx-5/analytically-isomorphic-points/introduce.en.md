Two points on algebraic varieties are analytically isomorphic when the completions of their local rings are isomorphic. This means that the formal neighborhoods of the two points are indistinguishable algebraically. Unlike the local ring $\mathcal{O}_P$ itself, which determines the birational equivalence class of the variety, the completion $\hat{\mathcal{O}}_P$ carries finer local information, capturing the "formal geometry" near the point. For example, the origin on the nodal cubic $y^2 = x^2 + x^3$ is analytically isomorphic to the origin on $xy = 0$ (two crossing lines), reflecting that the node looks like two smooth branches crossing.
