A regular local ring is a noetherian local ring whose embedding dimension (the $k$-dimension of $\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2$) equals its Krull dimension. This notion, introduced by Zariski, provides the intrinsic algebraic characterization of nonsingularity: a point on an algebraic variety is nonsingular if and only if its local ring is regular. The condition $\dim_k \mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2 = \dim A$ can be interpreted as the tangent space having the same dimension as the variety itself.
