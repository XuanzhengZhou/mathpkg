The Cohen Structure Theorem completely classifies complete regular local rings that contain a field: they are isomorphic to formal power series rings over their residue field. This is the algebraic analogue of the fact that a nonsingular point on an algebraic variety looks formally like affine space. The theorem is fundamental for understanding the local structure of smooth varieties; it implies that the completion of the local ring at a nonsingular point of an $n$-dimensional variety over $k$ is isomorphic to $k[[x_1, \ldots, x_n]]$.
