Proposition 1.1 of Hartshorne's Chapter IV establishes the fundamental fact that for a nonsingular projective curve, three a priori distinct notions of genus coincide: the arithmetic genus $p_a$ defined via the Hilbert polynomial, the geometric genus $p_g$ defined as the dimension of global sections of the canonical sheaf, and the cohomological invariant $\dim H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$. This unification justifies referring to a single numerical invariant $g$, the genus of the curve, and underpins the Riemann--Roch theorem and the entire classification theory of algebraic curves.
