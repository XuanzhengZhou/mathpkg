Let $X$ be a curve over an algebraically closed field $k$. Let $p_a(X)$ be the arithmetic genus, defined as $p_a(X) = 1 - P_X(0)$ where $P_X$ is the Hilbert polynomial of $X$. Let $p_g(X)$ be the geometric genus, defined as $p_g(X) = \dim_k \Gamma(X, \omega_X)$, where $\omega_X$ is the canonical sheaf on $X$. Then

$$p_a(X) = p_g(X) = \dim_k H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X).$$

This common value is called the \textbf{genus} of $X$ and is denoted by $g$.
