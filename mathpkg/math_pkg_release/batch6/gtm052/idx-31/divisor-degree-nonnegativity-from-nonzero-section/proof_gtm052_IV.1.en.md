**Proof.** If $l(D) \neq 0$, then the complete linear system $|D|$ is nonempty. Hence $D$ is linearly equivalent to some effective divisor. Since the degree depends only on the linear equivalence class, and the degree of an effective divisor is nonnegative, we find $\deg D \geq 0$. If $\deg D = 0$, then $D$ is linearly equivalent to an effective divisor of degree $0$. But there is only one such, namely the zero divisor.
