Lemma 1.2 is a basic but essential observation about divisors on a curve. If $l(D) \neq 0$, the complete linear system $|D|$ is nonempty, so $D$ is linearly equivalent to an effective divisor. Since degree is invariant under linear equivalence and effective divisors have nonnegative degree, we obtain $\deg D \geq 0$. The second part is even stronger: if the degree is zero and $l(D) \neq 0$, then $D$ must be linearly equivalent to the zero divisor, so its associated invertible sheaf is trivial. This lemma is used repeatedly in the study of curves, particularly in the proof of the Riemann--Roch theorem.
