The Cohomology and Base Change Theorem (Theorem 12.11 in Hartshorne) is a fundamental result in algebraic geometry relating higher direct images to cohomology of fibers. It states that for a flat, coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on a projective morphism $f: X \to Y$, the natural base change map $\varphi^i(y): R^i f_*(\mathcal{F}) \otimes k(y) \to H^i(X_y, \mathcal{F}_y)$ has strong surjectivity properties. If $\varphi^i(y)$ is surjective at a point $y$, then it is an isomorphism and remains so in a neighborhood. Furthermore, the surjectivity of $\varphi^{i-1}(y)$ is equivalent to $R^i f_*(\mathcal{F})$ being locally free near $y$.
