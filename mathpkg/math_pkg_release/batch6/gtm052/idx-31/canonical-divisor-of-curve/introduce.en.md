The canonical divisor $K$ on a curve $X$ is any divisor representing the invertible sheaf of relative differentials $\Omega_{X/k}$. Since $X$ has dimension $1$, the sheaf of differentials coincides with the canonical sheaf $\omega_X$. The canonical divisor plays a central role in the Riemann--Roch theorem, Serre duality, and the classification of curves. For example, on a curve of genus $g$, the degree of the canonical divisor is $2g - 2$, and the complete linear system $|K|$ determines whether the curve is hyperelliptic.
