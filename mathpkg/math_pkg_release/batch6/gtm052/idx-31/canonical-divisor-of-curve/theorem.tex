Let $X$ be a curve over an algebraically closed field $k$. Denote by $\Omega_X = \Omega_{X/k}$ the sheaf of relative differentials of $X$ over $k$. Since $X$ has dimension $1$, $\Omega_X$ is an invertible sheaf on $X$ and is equal to the canonical sheaf $\omega_X$ on $X$. Any divisor in the linear equivalence class corresponding to $\Omega_X$ is called a \textbf{canonical divisor} and is denoted by $K$.
