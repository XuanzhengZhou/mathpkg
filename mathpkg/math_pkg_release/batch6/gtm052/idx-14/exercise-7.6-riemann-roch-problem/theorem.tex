Let $X$ be a nonsingular projective variety over an algebraically closed field, and let $D$ be a divisor on $X$. For any $n > 0$, consider the complete linear system $|nD|$. The Riemann-Roch problem is to determine $\dim|nD|$ as a function of $n$, and in particular its behavior for large $n$.

(a) If $D$ is very ample and $X \hookrightarrow \mathbf{P}_k^N$ is the corresponding embedding, show that for $n$ sufficiently large, $\dim|nD| = P_X(n) - 1$, where $P_X$ is the Hilbert polynomial.

(b) If $D$ corresponds to a torsion element of $\operatorname{Pic} X$ of order $r$, show that $\dim|nD| = 0$ if $r \nmid n$, and $\dim|nD|$ is some other computable number if $r \mid n$.
