This exercise asks to prove that any surjective morphism between invertible sheaves on a locally ringed space must actually be an isomorphism. The key idea is that invertible sheaves are locally free of rank one, so a surjective map between them is locally a surjective map from a rank-one free module to itself, which is necessarily injective. This fact is used in the proof of the universal property of blowing up.
