Proposition 7.7 establishes the precise relationship between global sections of an invertible sheaf and effective divisors in the corresponding linear equivalence class. Part (a) shows that every nonzero section cuts out an effective divisor linearly equivalent to the reference divisor. Part (b) states the converse: every effective divisor in the linear system arises from some section. Part (c) characterizes when two sections give the same divisor, namely when they differ by a nonzero scalar multiple.
