Lemma 7.8 translates the geometric notion of a base point into sheaf-theoretic language: a point is a base point precisely when every section in the corresponding subspace vanishes at the point in the sense of belonging to the maximal ideal times the stalk. This characterization is fundamental because it connects the geometry of linear systems to the algebraic properties of invertible sheaves and their global sections.
