Exercise 7.11 explores the non-uniqueness of the ideal sheaf used to define a blowing up. Part (a) shows that replacing an ideal by a power yields the same blowup (via the Veronese embedding). Part (b) demonstrates that twisting by an invertible ideal does not change the blowup. These facts are important because they show that the blowup depends only on the "birational class" of the ideal sheaf, not on its precise embedding.
