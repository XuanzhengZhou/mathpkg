Proposition 7.16 specializes the blowing-up construction to the case of varieties over an algebraically closed field. It establishes that blowing up a variety produces another variety, that the blowup morphism is birational, proper, and surjective, and that quasi-projectivity and projectivity are preserved. These properties make blowing up an indispensable tool in the birational classification of varieties.
