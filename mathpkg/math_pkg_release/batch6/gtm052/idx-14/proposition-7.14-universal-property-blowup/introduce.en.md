Proposition 7.14 states the universal property of blowing up: any morphism to $X$ for which the pullback of the ideal sheaf becomes invertible factors uniquely through the blowup. This universal property characterizes the blowup up to unique isomorphism and makes it the "minimal" modification of $X$ on which the given ideal becomes invertible. It is the foundational result that justifies the use of blowing up throughout algebraic geometry.
