Lemma 7.9 shows that twisting the graded algebra by an invertible sheaf produces an isomorphic Proj, with the twisting sheaf $\mathcal{O}(1)$ adjusted by the pullback of the invertible sheaf. This is an essential technical tool because it allows one to replace a given graded algebra with a twist that has better generation properties without changing the underlying Proj scheme, a technique used crucially in the proof of Theorem 7.10 on the properties of Proj.
