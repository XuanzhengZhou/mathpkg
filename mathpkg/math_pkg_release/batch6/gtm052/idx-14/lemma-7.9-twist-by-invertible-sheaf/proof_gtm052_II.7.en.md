**Proof.** Let $\theta: \mathcal{O}_U \xrightarrow{\cong} \mathcal{L}|_U$ be a local isomorphism of $\mathcal{O}_U$ with $\mathcal{L}|_U$ over a small open affine subset $U$ of $X$. Then $\theta$ induces an isomorphism of graded rings $\mathcal{S}(U) \cong \mathcal{S}'(U)$ and hence an isomorphism $\theta^*: \operatorname{Proj} \mathcal{S}'(U) \cong \operatorname{Proj} \mathcal{S}(U)$. If $\theta_1: \mathcal{O}_U \cong \mathcal{L}|_U$ is a different local isomorphism, then $\theta$ and $\theta_1$ differ by an element $f \in \Gamma(U, \mathcal{O}_U^*)$. The two isomorphisms $\theta^*$ and $\theta_1^*$ differ by the automorphism of $\operatorname{Proj} \mathcal{S}(U)$ induced by multiplying the homogeneous coordinates by $f$, which is the identity. Thus the local isomorphisms glue to give a global isomorphism $\varphi: P' \cong P$. The relation between the twisting sheaves follows by tracking the local construction of $\mathcal{O}(1)$. $\square$
