**Proof.** Apply Proposition 7.14 to the composition $\tilde{Y} \xrightarrow{\pi_Y} Y \xrightarrow{f} X$, noting that $\pi_Y^{-1}(f^{-1}\mathcal{I} \cdot \mathcal{O}_Y) \cdot \mathcal{O}_{\tilde{Y}}$ is invertible on $\tilde{Y}$ by Proposition 7.13(a). The universal property then provides the unique morphism $\tilde{f}: \tilde{Y} \to \tilde{X}$ factoring $f \circ \pi_Y$ through $\pi_X$. $\square$
