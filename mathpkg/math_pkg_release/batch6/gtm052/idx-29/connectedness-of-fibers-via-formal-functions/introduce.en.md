This corollary of the Theorem on Formal Functions gives a cohomological criterion for the connectedness of fibres of a projective morphism. The key step in the proof uses the case $i = 0$ of the theorem, identifying the completed stalk of $f_*\mathcal{O}_X$ with the inverse limit $\varprojlim H^0(X_n, \mathcal{O}_{X_n})$. If the fibre were disconnected, this completed local ring would split as a direct sum of two nonzero rings, contradicting the fact that a local ring is indecomposable. This result is the essential technical input for Zariski's Main Theorem.
