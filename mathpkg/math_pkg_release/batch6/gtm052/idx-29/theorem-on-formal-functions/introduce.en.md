The Theorem on Formal Functions (Theorem III.11.1) is one of the cornerstones of the cohomological approach to algebraic geometry developed in EGA and Hartshorne. It relates the stalk of the $i$-th higher direct image sheaf $R^i f_*(\mathcal{F})$, completed at a point $y \in Y$, to the inverse limit of the cohomology groups of the thickened fibres $X_n$ with coefficients in the pulled-back sheaves $\mathcal{F}_n$. The proof proceeds by descending induction on $i$, reducing to the case of sheaves of the form $\mathcal{O}(q)$, and uses the Artin–Rees lemma (via Krull's theorem) to control the kernels that arise when tensoring is not exact. The theorem is the key ingredient for Zariski's Main Theorem and Stein factorization.
