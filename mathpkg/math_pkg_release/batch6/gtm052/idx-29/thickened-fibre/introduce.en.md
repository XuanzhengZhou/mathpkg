The thickened fibre $X_n$ is a fundamental construction in the study of formal completions along fibres of a morphism. For $n = 1$, it recovers the ordinary fibre $X_y$, while for $n > 1$, nilpotent elements encode infinitesimal information about the neighbourhood of the fibre. The sheaves $\mathcal{F}_n$ obtained by pullback allow one to study the behaviour of a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ near the fibre via the cohomology groups $H^i(X_n, \mathcal{F}_n)$. As $n$ varies, these groups form an inverse system whose limit is related to the higher direct image sheaves $R^i f_*(\mathcal{F})$ via the Theorem on Formal Functions.
