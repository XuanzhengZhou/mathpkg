Zariski's Main Theorem (ZMT) is one of the most important structural results in algebraic geometry. This version, Corollary III.11.4 in Hartshorne, states that the fibres of a birational projective morphism onto a normal integral scheme are always connected. The proof reduces to verifying that $f_*\mathcal{O}_X \cong \mathcal{O}_Y$ (which holds for birational morphisms onto normal schemes) and then applies the connectedness criterion from the formal functions theorem. ZMT explains why resolving singularities of a normal variety does not disconnect the exceptional locus.
