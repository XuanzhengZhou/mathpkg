Castelnuovo's contraction theorem is a foundational result in the birational geometry of algebraic surfaces. It states that any curve on a surface that is isomorphic to the projective line $\mathbf{P}^1$ and has self-intersection $-1$ (an exceptional curve of the first kind) can be contracted to a nonsingular point via a monoidal transformation. Conversely, the exceptional curve of any monoidal transformation centered at a point is an exceptional curve of the first kind. The theorem thus gives a complete geometric characterization of the blowup of a surface at a point, and plays a central role in the classification of algebraic surfaces and the minimal model program in dimension two.
