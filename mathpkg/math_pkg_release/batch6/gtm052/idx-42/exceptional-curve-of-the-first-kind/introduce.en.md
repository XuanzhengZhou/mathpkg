An exceptional curve of the first kind is a curve on an algebraic surface that is isomorphic to the projective line $\mathbf{P}^1$ and has self-intersection number $-1$. Such curves arise naturally as the exceptional divisors of monoidal transformations (blowups) of a surface at a point. Castelnuovo's contraction theorem shows the converse: any exceptional curve of the first kind can be contracted to a nonsingular point by a monoidal transformation.
