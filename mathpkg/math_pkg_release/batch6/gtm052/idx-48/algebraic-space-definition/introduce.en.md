Algebraic spaces, introduced by Artin and Knutson, generalize schemes by allowing objects that are locally quotients of schemes by étale equivalence relations. They form a category containing the category of schemes. A remarkable theorem of Artin shows that smooth proper algebraic spaces over $\mathbf{C}$ correspond exactly to Moishezon manifolds via the analytification functor. This means Hironaka's example of a non-algebraic Moishezon manifold provides an example of an algebraic space that is not a scheme.
