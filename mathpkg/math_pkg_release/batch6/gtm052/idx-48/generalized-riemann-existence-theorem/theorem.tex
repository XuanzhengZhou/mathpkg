Let $X$ be a normal scheme of finite type over $\mathbf{C}$. Let $\mathfrak{X}$ be a normal complex analytic space, together with a finite morphism $\mathfrak{f}: \mathfrak{X} \rightarrow X_h$. (A finite morphism of analytic spaces is a proper morphism with finite fibres.) Then there is a unique normal scheme $X$ and a finite morphism $g: X \rightarrow X$ such that $X_h \cong \mathfrak{X}$ and $g_h = \mathfrak{f}$.
