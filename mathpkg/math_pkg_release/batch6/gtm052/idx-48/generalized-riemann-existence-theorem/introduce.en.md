The Generalized Riemann Existence Theorem, proved by Grauert and Remmert with an elegant proof by Grothendieck using Hironaka's resolution of singularities, extends Riemann's classical result to higher dimensions. It states that any finite (proper with finite fibers) analytic cover of a normal algebraic scheme over $\mathbf{C}$ is itself algebraic. A key corollary is that the algebraic fundamental group $\pi_1^{\text{alg}}(X)$ is isomorphic to the profinite completion of the topological fundamental group $\pi_1^{\text{top}}(X_h)$.
