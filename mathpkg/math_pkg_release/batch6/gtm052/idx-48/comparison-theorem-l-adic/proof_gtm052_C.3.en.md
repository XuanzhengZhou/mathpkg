This is a deep theorem relating étale cohomology and singular cohomology. The proof uses the fact that for smooth proper varieties over $\mathbf{C}$, both theories satisfy the same formal properties, and one can reduce to the case of $\mathbf{P}^n$ where both sides can be computed explicitly and agree. See [SGA 4] for the corresponding statement with torsion coefficients, and [SGA 5] for the passage to the limit of $\mathbf{Z}_l$ or $\mathbf{Q}_l$ coefficients.
