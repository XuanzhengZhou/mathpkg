A complex analytic space, in the sense of Grauert, is the transcendental analogue of a scheme. It is a locally ringed space locally modeled on the common zero set of finitely many holomorphic functions on a polydisc in $\mathbf{C}^n$, with a structure sheaf that may have nilpotent elements. This generalizes the notion of a complex manifold by allowing singularities and non-reduced structure. The theory of coherent analytic sheaves and their cohomology on complex analytic spaces parallels the algebraic theory developed in Chapter III.
