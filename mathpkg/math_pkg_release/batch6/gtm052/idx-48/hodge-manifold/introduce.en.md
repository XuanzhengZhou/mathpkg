A Hodge manifold is a compact Kähler manifold whose Kähler class is integral (lies in the image of integral cohomology). This condition is precisely what is needed for the Kodaira embedding theorem to produce a projective embedding. The notion of Hodge manifold thus provides a differential-geometric characterization of projective algebraic manifolds over $\mathbf{C}$.
