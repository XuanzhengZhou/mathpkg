The Kodaira embedding theorem provides a complete differential-geometric characterization of projective algebraic manifolds over $\mathbf{C}$: a compact complex manifold is projective algebraic if and only if it admits a Hodge metric (a Kähler metric with integral Kähler class). This theorem, together with its vanishing theorem corollaries, is one of the most powerful tools for applying differential-geometric methods to algebraic geometry.
