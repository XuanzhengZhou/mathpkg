The proof is due to Kodaira [1]. Given a Hodge manifold $\mathfrak{X}$ with integral Kähler class $\omega \in H^2(\mathfrak{X}, \mathbf{Z})$, one uses the Kodaira vanishing theorem to show that a sufficiently high power of the corresponding holomorphic line bundle $L$ (with $c_1(L) = \omega$) has enough global sections to give an embedding into projective space. The integrality of $\omega$ is essential for $L$ to exist as a holomorphic line bundle. The converse direction (projective algebraic $\implies$ Hodge) follows by pulling back the Fubini-Study metric from $\mathbf{P}^n$.
