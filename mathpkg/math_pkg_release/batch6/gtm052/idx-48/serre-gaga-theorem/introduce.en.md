Serre's GAGA theorem (Géométrie Algébrique et Géométrie Analytique) establishes that for projective varieties over $\mathbf{C}$, the algebraic and analytic categories are essentially equivalent at the level of coherent sheaves. The functor $h$ gives an equivalence of categories of coherent sheaves, and the natural maps on cohomology are isomorphisms. This is one of the most important results connecting algebraic and complex analytic geometry, and it answers questions Q3, Q4, and Q5 affirmatively in the projective case.
