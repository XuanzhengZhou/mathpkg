The exactness of the sequence of groups $0 \rightarrow \mathbf{Z} \rightarrow \mathbf{C} \xrightarrow{f} \mathbf{C}^* \rightarrow 0$ follows from the fact that $f(x) = e^{2\pi i x}$ is a surjective homomorphism from the additive group $\mathbf{C}$ to the multiplicative group $\mathbf{C}^*$, with kernel $\mathbf{Z}$. For any reduced complex analytic space $\mathfrak{X}$, the sheaf version is obtained by taking holomorphic functions with values in these groups. The surjectivity of $\mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{X}} \rightarrow \mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{X}}^*$ holds locally because on a polydisc, one can take a branch of the logarithm, so every nonvanishing holomorphic function has a holomorphic logarithm.
