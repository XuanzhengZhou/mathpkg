The construction of the cycle class map uses the Gysin homomorphism in $l$-adic cohomology. For a closed immersion $i: Z \hookrightarrow X$ of codimension $q$, one has a Gysin map $i_*: H^{i}(Z, \mathbf{Q}_l) \rightarrow H^{i+2q}(X, \mathbf{Q}_l)$. The cohomology class $\eta(Z)$ is defined as the image of the fundamental class $1 \in H^0(Z, \mathbf{Q}_l)$ under this map. The compatibility with rational equivalence and intersection products follows from the functoriality of the Gysin map and the deformation to the normal cone technique. See [SGA 4] and [SGA 5] for details.
