If $X$ is smooth and proper over $k$, and if $Z$ is a subvariety of codimension $q$, then there is associated to $Z$ a cohomology class $\eta(Z) \in H^{2q}(X, \mathbf{Q}_l)$. This map extends by linearity to cycles. Rationally equivalent cycles have the same cohomology class. Intersection of cycles becomes cup-product of cohomology classes. In other words, $\eta$ is a homomorphism from the Chow ring $A(X)$ to the cohomology ring $H^*(X, \mathbf{Q}_l)$. Finally, it is non-trivial: if $P \in X$ is a closed point, then $\eta(P) \in H^{2n}(X, \mathbf{Q}_l)$ is nonzero.
