Let $V \times W \rightarrow K$ be a perfect pairing of vector spaces $V, W$ of dimension $r$ over $K$. Let $\lambda \in K$, and let $\varphi: V \rightarrow V$ and $\psi: W \rightarrow W$ be endomorphisms such that

$$\langle \varphi v, \psi w \rangle = \lambda \langle v, w \rangle$$

for all $v \in V, w \in W$. Then

$$\det(1 - \psi t, W) = \frac{(-1)^r \lambda^r t^r}{\det(\varphi; V)} \det\left(1 - \frac{\varphi}{\lambda t}; V\right).$$
