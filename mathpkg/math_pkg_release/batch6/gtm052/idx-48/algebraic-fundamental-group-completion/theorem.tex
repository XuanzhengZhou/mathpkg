The algebraic fundamental group of $X$, $\pi_1^{\text{alg}}(X)$, defined as the inverse limit of the Galois groups of finite étale covers of $X$ (IV, Ex. 4.8), is isomorphic to the completion $\pi_1^{\text{top}}(X_h)^*$ of the usual fundamental group of $X_h$ with respect to subgroups of finite index. Indeed, if $\mathfrak{Y}$ is any finite unramified topological covering space of $X_h$, then $\mathfrak{Y}$ has a natural structure of normal complex analytic space, and the map $\mathfrak{Y} \rightarrow X_h$ is a finite morphism.
