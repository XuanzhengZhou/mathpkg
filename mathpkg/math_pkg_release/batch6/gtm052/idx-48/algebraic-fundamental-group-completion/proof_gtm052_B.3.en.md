If $\mathfrak{Y}$ is any finite unramified topological covering space of $X_h$, then $\mathfrak{Y}$ has a natural structure of normal complex analytic space, and the map $\mathfrak{Y} \rightarrow X_h$ is a finite morphism. By the Generalized Riemann Existence Theorem (3.2), there exists a unique normal scheme $Y$ and a finite morphism $Y \rightarrow X$ such that $Y_h \cong \mathfrak{Y}$. Since the covering is unramified, this morphism is étale. This establishes a correspondence between finite topological covering spaces of $X_h$ and finite étale covers of $X$, from which the isomorphism of fundamental groups follows by taking inverse limits.
