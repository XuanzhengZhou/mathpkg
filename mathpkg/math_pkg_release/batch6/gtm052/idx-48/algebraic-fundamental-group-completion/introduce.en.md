This corollary of the Generalized Riemann Existence Theorem establishes a deep connection between topology and algebraic geometry: the algebraic fundamental group of a scheme over $\mathbf{C}$ (defined via finite étale covers) is isomorphic to the profinite completion of the topological fundamental group of its associated analytic space. This means that the algebraic fundamental group captures precisely the finite-index subgroup structure of the topological fundamental group, providing a crucial link between étale topology and classical topology.
