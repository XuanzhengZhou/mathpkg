From the long exact cohomology sequence of the exponential sequence:

$$0 \rightarrow H^1(X_h, \mathbf{Z}) \rightarrow H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X) \rightarrow \operatorname{Pic} X \rightarrow H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z}) \rightarrow H^2(X, \mathcal{O}_X) \rightarrow \dots$$

Algebraically equivalent Cartier divisors give the same image in $H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$, so the Néron–Severi group $\operatorname{NS}(X)$ injects into $H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$. Since $X$ is triangulable (Hironaka [5]), $H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$ is a finitely generated abelian group, hence its subgroup $\operatorname{NS}(X)$ is also finitely generated.
