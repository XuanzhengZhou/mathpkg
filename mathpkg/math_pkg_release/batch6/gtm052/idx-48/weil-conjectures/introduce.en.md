The Weil conjectures, formulated by André Weil in 1949, are among the most influential statements in 20th-century mathematics. They predict deep properties of the zeta function of a smooth projective variety over a finite field: rationality, a functional equation, an analogue of the Riemann hypothesis (the absolute values of reciprocal roots of the $P_i$ are $q^{i/2}$), and a connection between the Betti numbers in characteristic $p$ and the ordinary topological Betti numbers in characteristic zero. The rationality and functional equation were proved by Grothendieck using $l$-adic cohomology. The Riemann hypothesis analogue was proved by Deligne in 1973, completing the solution of the Weil conjectures.
