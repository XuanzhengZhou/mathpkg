The Weil conjectures were proven over several decades. The rationality (1.1) and functional equation (1.2) were proven by Grothendieck using $l$-adic cohomology and the Lefschetz trace formula (see Section 4 of Appendix C). The analogue of the Riemann hypothesis (1.3) was proven by Deligne [3] in 1973, using deep properties of $l$-adic cohomology, Lefschetz pencils, and monodromy. The statement about Betti numbers (1.4) follows from the comparison theorem (3.7) and the smooth base change theorem (3.6).
