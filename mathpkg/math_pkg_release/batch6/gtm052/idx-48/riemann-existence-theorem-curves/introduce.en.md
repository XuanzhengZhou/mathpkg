Riemann's existence theorem asserts that every compact Riemann surface (compact complex manifold of dimension 1) is algebraic — it is isomorphic to the analytification of a nonsingular projective algebraic curve. The proof proceeds in two steps: first, using harmonic analysis to show the existence of nonconstant meromorphic functions; second, using a nonconstant meromorphic function to embed the surface as a finite cover of $\mathbf{P}^1$, thereby exhibiting it as an algebraic curve.
