The proof has two steps. (a) The first step is to show the existence of nonconstant meromorphic functions on $\mathfrak{X}$. This uses analysis — either the theory of harmonic integrals (Hodge, Weil) or the theory of distributions (Gunning) — to first prove finite-dimensionality of cohomology of coherent analytic sheaves, and then deduce the existence of meromorphic functions.

(b) The second step (the "Riemann existence theorem" proper) is to take a nonconstant meromorphic function $f$ on $\mathfrak{X}$, regard it as a finite morphism of $\mathfrak{X}$ to $\mathbf{P}^1$, and then show that $\mathfrak{X}$ is a nonsingular algebraic curve, hence projective. Part (b) is elementary by comparison with part (a).
