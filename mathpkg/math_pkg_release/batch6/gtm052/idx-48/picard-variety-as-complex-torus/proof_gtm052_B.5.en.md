From the long exact cohomology sequence of the exponential sequence, the map $\operatorname{Pic} X \cong H^1(X_h, \mathcal{O}_{X_h}^*) \rightarrow H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$ sends a line bundle to its Chern class. The kernel of this map is the group of line bundles with trivial Chern class, which correspond to divisors algebraically equivalent to zero modulo linear equivalence. This kernel is the image of $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$ in $\operatorname{Pic} X$. Since $H^1(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$ is a discrete subgroup of the finite-dimensional complex vector space $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$ (by Serre's GAGA theorem and the finite-dimensionality of coherent cohomology), the quotient $\operatorname{Pic}^\circ X = H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)/H^1(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$ is a compact complex torus. That it is actually an abelian variety (i.e., projective) is a deeper result requiring the existence of a positive line bundle on the Picard variety.
