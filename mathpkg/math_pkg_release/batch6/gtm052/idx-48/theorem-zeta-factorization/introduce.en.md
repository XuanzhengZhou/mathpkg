This theorem gives the cohomological interpretation of the Weil zeta function. The Lefschetz trace formula expresses the number of fixed points of the Frobenius morphism (i.e., the number $N_r$ of $\mathbf{F}_{q^r}$-rational points) as an alternating sum of traces of $f^{*r}$ on $l$-adic cohomology. Substituting this into the definition of the zeta function and applying Lemma 4.1 (the trace-determinant identity) factors the zeta function into an alternating product of characteristic polynomials $P_i(t) = \det(1 - f^* t; H^i)$. This immediately implies the rationality (1.1) of the Weil conjectures and provides the framework for the remaining conjectures.
