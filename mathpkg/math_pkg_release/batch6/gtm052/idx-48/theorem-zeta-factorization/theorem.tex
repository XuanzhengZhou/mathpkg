Let $X$ be projective and smooth over $k = \mathbf{F}_q$, of dimension $n$. Then

$$Z(X,t) = \frac{P_1(t) \cdots P_{2n-1}(t)}{P_0(t) P_2(t) \cdots P_{2n}(t)}$$

where $P_i(t) = \det(1 - f^* t; H^i(\bar{X}, \mathbf{Q}_l))$, and $f^*$ is the endomorphism of $H^i(\bar{X}, \mathbf{Q}_l)$ induced by the Frobenius morphism $f: \bar{X} \rightarrow \bar{X}$ relative to $k$.

Furthermore, $P_0(t) = 1-t$, $P_{2n}(t) = 1 - q^n t$, and $B_i = \dim H^i(\bar{X}, \mathbf{Q}_l) = \deg P_i(t)$. The invariant $E$ of $X$ is given by $E = \sum (-1)^i B_i$, so $E$ is the "topological Euler-PoincarĂŠ characteristic" of $X$.
