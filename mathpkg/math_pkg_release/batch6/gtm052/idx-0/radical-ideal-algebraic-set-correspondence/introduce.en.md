This is Corollary 1.4 from Chapter I, Section §1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. It establishes that the operations $Z$ and $I$ define an inclusion-reversing bijection between the set of algebraic subsets of $\mathbf{A}^n$ and radical ideals of $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$. In particular, irreducible algebraic sets correspond to prime ideals, which is the algebraic justification for studying affine varieties through their coordinate rings.
