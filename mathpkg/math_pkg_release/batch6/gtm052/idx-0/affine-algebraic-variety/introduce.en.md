This is a definition from Chapter I, Section §1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. An affine algebraic variety is the fundamental object of study in classical algebraic geometry -- it is an irreducible closed subset of affine $n$-space under the Zariski topology. Every affine variety has an associated coordinate ring $A(Y) = k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]/I(Y)$.
