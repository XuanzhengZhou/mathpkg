Hartshorne refers the reader to Matsumura [2, Ch. 5, §14] or, in the case $k$ is algebraically closed, Atiyah--Macdonald [1, Ch. 11]. The proof uses Noether normalization: after a suitable change of variables, $B$ is a finitely generated module over a polynomial subring $k[y_1,\ldots,y_d]$ where $d = \operatorname{trdeg}_k K(B)$. Then $\dim B = \dim k[y_1,\ldots,y_d] = d$. Part (b) follows from the fact that for an integrally closed domain finitely generated over a field, the dimension formula holds. This can be proven by induction using Krull's principal ideal theorem.
