This is a definition from Chapter I, Section §1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. A quasi-affine variety is an open subset of an affine variety, equipped with the induced topology. Quasi-affine varieties generalize affine varieties and arise naturally when studying local properties such as regular functions on open sets.
