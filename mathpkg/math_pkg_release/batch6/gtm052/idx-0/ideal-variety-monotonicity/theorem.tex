(a) If $T_1 \subseteq T_2$ are subsets of $A = k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$, then $Z(T_1) \supseteq Z(T_2)$.

(b) If $Y_1 \subseteq Y_2$ are subsets of $\mathbf{A}^n$, then $I(Y_1) \supseteq I(Y_2)$.

(c) For any two subsets $Y_1, Y_2$ of $\mathbf{A}^n$, we have $I(Y_1 \cup Y_2) = I(Y_1) \cap I(Y_2)$.

(d) For any ideal $\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A$, we have $I(Z(\mathfrak{a})) = \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$, the radical of $\mathfrak{a}$.
