This is a definition from Chapter I, Section §1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. An irreducible subset of a topological space cannot be decomposed into a union of two proper closed subsets. This concept is essential for defining algebraic varieties, which are precisely the irreducible closed subsets of affine or projective space under the Zariski topology.
