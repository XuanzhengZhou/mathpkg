This is Proposition 1.9 from Chapter I, Section §1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. It confirms that affine $n$-space $\mathbf{A}^n$ over an algebraically closed field $k$ has dimension $n$, which follows from the fact that the polynomial ring $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$ has Krull dimension $n$, or equivalently that its fraction field has transcendence degree $n$ over $k$.
