By Proposition 1.7, the dimension of $\mathbf{A}^n$ equals the Krull dimension of its coordinate ring $A(\mathbf{A}^n) = k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$. By Theorem 1.8A(a), the Krull dimension of $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$ equals the transcendence degree over $k$ of its fraction field $k(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$, which is $n$ since $x_1,\ldots,x_n$ are algebraically independent over $k$.
