In a ring $A$, the \textbf{height} of a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ is the supremum of all integers $n$ such that there exists a chain $\mathfrak{p}_0 \subset \mathfrak{p}_1 \subset \ldots \subset \mathfrak{p}_n = \mathfrak{p}$ of distinct prime ideals. The \textbf{dimension} (or \textbf{Krull dimension}) of $A$ is the supremum of the heights of all prime ideals.
