This is a definition from Chapter I, Section §1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. The Krull dimension of a commutative ring is the maximum length of a chain of prime ideals. This algebraic notion is the key to defining the dimension of algebraic varieties, as the dimension of an affine algebraic set equals the Krull dimension of its coordinate ring.
