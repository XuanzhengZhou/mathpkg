Let $f$ be a rational function on the surface $X$. Show that it is possible to "resolve the singularities of $f$" in the following sense: there is a birational morphism $g: X' \rightarrow X$ so that $f$ induces a morphism of $X'$ to $\mathbf{P}^1$. Hints: Write the divisor of $f$ as $(f) = \sum n_i C_i$. Then apply embedded resolution (3.9) to the curve $Y = \bigcup C_i$. Then blow up further as necessary whenever a curve of zeros meets a curve of poles until the zeros and poles of $f$ are disjoint.
