This definition from Appendix A, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry introduces the concept of a relatively minimal model for surfaces. A nonsingular projective surface is called a relatively minimal model if it cannot be birationally contracted to another nonsingular projective surface except via an isomorphism. The idea is to find, within each birational equivalence class of surfaces, a model that is as canonical as possible.
