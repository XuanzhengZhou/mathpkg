A curious consequence of the implication $\text{(iv)} \Rightarrow \text{(iii)}$ of (4.11) is the following numerical fact: Given integers $a, b_1, \ldots, b_6$ such that $b_i > 0$ for each $i$, $a - b_i - b_j > 0$ for each $i, j$ and $2a - \sum_{i \neq j} b_i > 0$ for each $j$, we must necessarily have $a^2 - \sum b_i^2 > 0$. Prove this directly (for $a, b_1, \ldots, b_6 \in \mathbb{R}$) using methods of freshman calculus.
