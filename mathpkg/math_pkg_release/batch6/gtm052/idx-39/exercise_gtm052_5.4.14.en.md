Using the method of (4.13.1), verify that there are nonsingular curves in $\mathbf{P}^3$ with $d = 8, g = 6, 7$; $d = 9, g = 7, 8, 9$; $d = 10, g = 8, 9, 10, 11$. Combining with (IV, §6), this completes the determination of all possible $g$ for curves of degree $d \leqslant 10$ in $\mathbf{P}^3$.
