Let $X$ and $Y$ be projective varieties. A \text{birational transformation} $T$ from $X$ to $Y$ is given by an open subset $U \subseteq X$ and a morphism $\varphi: U \rightarrow Y$ which induces an isomorphism of function fields $K(Y) \cong K(X)$. Two such representations $(U, \varphi)$ and $(V, \psi)$ represent the same birational transformation if $\varphi$ and $\psi$ agree on $U \cap V$. There exists a largest open set $U \subseteq X$ on which $T$ is represented by a morphism $\varphi: U \rightarrow Y$. We say $T$ is \text{defined} at the points of $U$, and call the points of $X - U$ the \text{fundamental points} of $T$.

The \text{graph} $\Gamma$ of $T$ is the closure in $X \times Y$ of the graph $\Gamma_0 \subseteq U \times Y$ of $\varphi$. For any subset $Z \subseteq X$, the \text{total transform} $T(Z)$ is defined as $p_2(p_1^{-1}(Z))$, where $p_1$ and $p_2$ are the projections of $\Gamma$ to $X$ and $Y$.
