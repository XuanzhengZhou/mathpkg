Let $S$ be a scheme, and let $X, Y$ be schemes over $S$, i.e., schemes with morphisms to $S$. We define the fibred product of $X$ and $Y$ over $S$, denoted $X \times_S Y$, to be a scheme, together with morphisms $p_1: X \times_S Y \rightarrow X$ and $p_2: X \times_S Y \rightarrow Y$, which make a commutative diagram with the given morphisms $X \rightarrow S$ and $Y \rightarrow S$, such that given any scheme $Z$ over $S$, and given morphisms $f: Z \rightarrow X$ and $g: Z \rightarrow Y$ which make a commutative diagram with the given morphisms $X \rightarrow S$ and $Y \rightarrow S$, then there exists a unique morphism $\theta: Z \rightarrow X \times_S Y$ such that $f = p_1 \circ \theta$, and $g = p_2 \circ \theta$. The morphisms $p_1$ and $p_2$ are called the projection morphisms of the fibred product onto its factors.
