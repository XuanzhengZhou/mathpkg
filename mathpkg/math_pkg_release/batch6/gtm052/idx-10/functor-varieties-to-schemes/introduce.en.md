This proposition establishes the fundamental relationship between classical algebraic varieties and the modern language of schemes. The functor $t$ embeds the category of varieties over an algebraically closed field $k$ as a full subcategory of schemes over $k$. The topological space of a variety $V$ is naturally homeomorphic to the closed points of the associated scheme $t(V)$, and the regular functions on $V$ correspond to restrictions of the structure sheaf of $t(V)$.
