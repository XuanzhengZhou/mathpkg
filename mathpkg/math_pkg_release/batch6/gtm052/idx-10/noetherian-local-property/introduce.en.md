Proposition 3.2 establishes that the noetherian property for schemes is truly local: if $X$ is locally noetherian, then every open affine subset $\operatorname{Spec} A$ of $X$ has a noetherian coordinate ring $A$. In particular, an affine scheme $\operatorname{Spec} A$ is noetherian exactly when $A$ is a noetherian ring. The proof uses a base of open affines $\operatorname{Spec} B_f$ for noetherian $B$, together with a lemma about extending ideal relations from localizations.
