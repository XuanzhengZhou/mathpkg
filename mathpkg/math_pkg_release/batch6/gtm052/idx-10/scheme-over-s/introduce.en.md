A scheme over $S$ is a scheme $X$ equipped with a morphism $X \to S$, called the structure morphism. This relativizes the notion of a scheme to work over a fixed base $S$. Morphisms between schemes over $S$ must be compatible with the structure morphisms, forming the category $\mathfrak{Sch}(S)$. When $S = \operatorname{Spec} A$, the category is denoted $\mathfrak{Sch}(A)$, which is fundamental to the functorial approach to algebraic geometry.
