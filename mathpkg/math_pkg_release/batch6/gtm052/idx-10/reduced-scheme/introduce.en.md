A scheme $X$ is reduced if its structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}_X$ has no nilpotent elements in any section. Equivalently, all local rings $\mathcal{O}_P$ at points $P \in X$ are reduced rings (no nonzero nilpotents). Reducedness is a local property that distinguishes varieties and integral schemes from more general schemes with infinitesimal structure.
