The fibre of a morphism $f: X \to Y$ at a point $y \in Y$ is the scheme $X_y = X \times_Y \operatorname{Spec} k(y)$, obtained by base-changing along the inclusion of the residue field. Its underlying topological space is homeomorphic to the set-theoretic fibre $f^{-1}(y)$, but the scheme structure can be non-reduced, capturing infinitesimal information about the family. This construction enables viewing a morphism as a family of schemes parametrized by $Y$.
