A scheme $X$ is integral if the ring of sections $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$ over every open set $U$ is an integral domain. An integral scheme is both reduced and irreducible; these three properties form an important hierarchy of scheme-theoretic notions generalizing the algebraic properties of rings to the geometry of schemes.
