Exercise 3.5 shows that removing a hypersurface from projective space yields an affine variety. The proof uses the Veronese embedding: if $H$ has degree $d$, the $d$-uple embedding sends $H$ to a hyperplane section, and the complement of a hyperplane in projective space is affine space. This is a fundamental result showing projective varieties are "compactifications" of affine varieties.
