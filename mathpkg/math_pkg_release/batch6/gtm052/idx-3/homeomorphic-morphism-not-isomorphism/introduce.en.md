Exercise 3.2(a) illustrates the subtlety in the definition of isomorphism. The map $t \mapsto (t^2, t^3)$ gives a bijective, bicontinuous morphism from the affine line to the cuspidal cubic $y^2 = x^3$. Despite being a homeomorphism in the Zariski topology, it is not an isomorphism because the inverse map $x \mapsto \sqrt{x}$ (or more precisely, $y/x$) is not a regular function at the cusp.
