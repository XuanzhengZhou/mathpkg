Theorem 3.4 is the projective analogue of Theorem 3.2. Unlike the affine case, the ring of global regular functions on a projective variety is just the ground field $k$ (there are no non-constant global regular functions). The local ring at a point is the degree-zero part of the localization of the homogeneous coordinate ring at the corresponding homogeneous prime ideal. The function field is the degree-zero part of the localization at the zero ideal.
