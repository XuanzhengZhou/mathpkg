Exercise 2.17(d) mentions a classical open problem in algebraic geometry (at the time of Hartshorne's writing): whether every irreducible curve in $\mathbf{P}^3$ can be expressed as the set-theoretic intersection of two surfaces. This problem highlights the subtle distinction between ideal-theoretic and set-theoretic complete intersections.
