A function $f: Y \to k$ is \text{regular at a point} $P \in Y$ if there is an open neighborhood $U$ with $P \in U \subseteq Y$, and polynomials $g, h \in A = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$, such that $h$ is nowhere zero on $U$, and $f = g/h$ on $U$. We say that $f$ is \text{regular on} $Y$ if it is regular at every point of $Y$.
