Exercise 3.2(b) provides another counterexample in characteristic $p$. The Frobenius morphism $t \mapsto t^p$ on $\mathbf{A}^1$ is a bijective, bicontinuous morphism that is not an isomorphism. The inverse would require extracting $p$-th roots, which is not a regular function because the derivative of $t^p$ is identically zero in characteristic $p$, reflecting inseparability.
