The ring $\mathcal{O}(Y)$ consists of all global regular functions on a variety $Y$. For an affine variety, $\mathcal{O}(Y)$ is isomorphic to the affine coordinate ring $A(Y)$, making it an intrinsic invariant. For a projective variety, $\mathcal{O}(Y)$ reduces to the ground field $k$, reflecting the fact that projective varieties admit no non-constant global regular functions.
