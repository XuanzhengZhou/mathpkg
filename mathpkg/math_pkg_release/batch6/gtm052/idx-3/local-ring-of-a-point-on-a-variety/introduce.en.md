The local ring $\mathcal{O}_{P,Y}$ captures the behavior of regular functions in arbitrarily small neighborhoods of a point $P$. Its elements are equivalence classes (germs) of regular functions defined near $P$. This ring is always a local ring: its unique maximal ideal consists of germs vanishing at $P$, and the residue field is canonically isomorphic to the base field $k$.
