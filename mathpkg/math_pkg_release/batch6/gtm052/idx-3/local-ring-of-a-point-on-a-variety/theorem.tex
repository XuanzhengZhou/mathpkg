If $P$ is a point of a variety $Y$, we define the \text{local ring of $P$ on $Y$}, $\mathcal{O}_{P,Y}$ (or simply $\mathcal{O}_P$), to be the ring of germs of regular functions on $Y$ near $P$. An element of $\mathcal{O}_P$ is a pair $\langle U, f \rangle$ where $U$ is an open subset of $Y$ containing $P$, and $f$ is a regular function on $U$, where we identify two such pairs $\langle U, f \rangle$ and $\langle V, g \rangle$ if $f = g$ on $U \cap V$.
\end{definition}

Note that $\mathcal{O}_P$ is indeed a local ring: its maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ is the set of germs of regular functions which vanish at $P$. For if $f(P) \neq 0$, then $1/f$ is regular in some neighborhood of $P$. The residue field $\mathcal{O}_P/\mathfrak{m}$ is isomorphic to $k$.
