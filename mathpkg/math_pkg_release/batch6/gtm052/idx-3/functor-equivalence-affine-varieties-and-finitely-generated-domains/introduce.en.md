Corollary 3.8 expresses the algebra-geometry correspondence in categorical language. The functor assigning to each affine variety its coordinate ring is a contravariant equivalence of categories. This means that studying affine varieties over $k$ is essentially the same as studying finitely generated integral $k$-algebras, but with arrows reversed. This principle is one of the cornerstones of modern algebraic geometry.
