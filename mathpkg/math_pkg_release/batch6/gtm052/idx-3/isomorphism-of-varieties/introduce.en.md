An isomorphism of varieties is a morphism with a two-sided inverse that is also a morphism. While every isomorphism is bijective and bicontinuous (a homeomorphism), the converse is false: a bijective bicontinuous morphism may fail to be an isomorphism. This subtlety distinguishes the category of varieties from the category of topological spaces.
