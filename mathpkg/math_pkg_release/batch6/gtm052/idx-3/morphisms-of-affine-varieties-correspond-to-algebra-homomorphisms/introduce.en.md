Theorem 3.5 establishes the fundamental anti-equivalence between affine varieties and finitely generated integral domains over $k$. Every morphism of affine varieties induces a $k$-algebra homomorphism between their coordinate rings by pullback of regular functions, and conversely every such homomorphism comes from a unique morphism. This is the algebraic foundation of modern algebraic geometry.
