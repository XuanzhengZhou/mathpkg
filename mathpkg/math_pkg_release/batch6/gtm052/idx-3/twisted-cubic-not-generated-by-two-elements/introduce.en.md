Exercise 2.17(c) provides a concrete counterexample to the converse of 2.17(b). The twisted cubic curve in $\mathbf{P}^3$ is a set-theoretic complete intersection (it is the intersection of a quadric and a cubic surface), but its homogeneous ideal requires three generators, so it is not a strict complete intersection.
