Castelnuovo's theorem provides a sharp upper bound for the genus of a non-degenerate space curve (i.e., a curve in $\mathbf{P}^3$ not lying in any plane) in terms of its degree. The bound is roughly $\frac{1}{4}d^2$, and equality is achieved by curves lying on quadric surfaces. The proof estimates the dimensions of the linear systems $|nD|$ where $D$ is a hyperplane section, carefully choosing the section to avoid trisecants.
