Theorem 5.2 in Hartshorne's Chapter IV on curves describes the canonical morphism $\varphi_K: X \to \mathbf{P}^{g-1}$ for a curve of genus $g \geqslant 2$. It establishes a fundamental dichotomy: if $X$ is nonhyperelliptic, this morphism is a closed embedding, realizing $X$ as a subvariety of projective space. If $X$ is hyperelliptic, the morphism is $2$-to-$1$ onto a rational normal curve, factoring through the unique $g_2^1$ on $X$.
