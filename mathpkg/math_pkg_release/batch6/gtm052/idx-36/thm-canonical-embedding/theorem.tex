Let $X$ be a curve of genus $g \geqslant 2$ over an algebraically closed field $k$, and let $f: X \to \mathbf{P}^{g-1}$ be the canonical morphism determined by the canonical linear system $|K|$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item If $X$ is not hyperelliptic, then $|K|$ is very ample, so the canonical morphism $f$ is an embedding (called the \text{canonical embedding}).
\item If $X$ is hyperelliptic, then the canonical morphism $f$ factors as $X \xrightarrow{g_2^1} \mathbf{P}^1 \xrightarrow{\nu_{g-1}} \mathbf{P}^{g-1}$, where the first map is the unique $g_2^1$ on $X$, and the second map $\nu_{g-1}$ is the $(g-1)$-uple embedding of $\mathbf{P}^1$ in $\mathbf{P}^{g-1}$. In particular, the image $X' = f(X)$ is a rational normal curve of degree $g-1$, and $f$ is a morphism of degree $2$ onto $X'$. Moreover, every effective canonical divisor on $X$ is a sum of $g-1$ divisors in the unique $g_2^1$, so we write $|K| = \sum_{1}^{g-1} g_2^1$.
\end{enumerate}
