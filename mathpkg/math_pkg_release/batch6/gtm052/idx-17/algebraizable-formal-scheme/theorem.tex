A formal scheme $\mathfrak{X}$ is called \text{algebraizable} if it can be obtained as the formal completion of some noetherian scheme $X$ along a closed subscheme $Y \subseteq X$. Not every noetherian formal scheme is algebraizable; counterexamples were constructed by Hironaka and Matsumura [1, §5] and by Hartshorne [5, V, 3.3, p. 205].
