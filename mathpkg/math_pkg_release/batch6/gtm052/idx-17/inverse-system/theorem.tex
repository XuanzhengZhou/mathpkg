A collection of sets or groups $A_n$ for $n \in \mathbb{N}$, together with maps $\varphi_{n'n}: A_{n'} \to A_n$ for each $n' \geq n$, such that for each $n'' \geq n' \geq n$,
$$\varphi_{n''n} = \varphi_{n'n} \circ \varphi_{n''n'},$$
is called an \text{inverse system}, denoted $(A_n, \varphi_{n'n})$ or simply $(A_n)$. The definition extends naturally to objects in any category; in particular, if the $A_n$ are abelian groups, ringed spaces, or sheaves, the maps are required to be morphisms in the respective category.
