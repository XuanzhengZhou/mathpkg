A formal scheme is a generalization of an ordinary scheme, obtained by gluing formal completions of noetherian schemes along closed subschemes. While a scheme is locally modeled on the spectrum of a ring, a formal scheme is locally modeled on the formal spectrum (the formal completion of an affine scheme). The structure sheaf of a formal scheme can have nilpotent elements of arbitrarily high order, allowing it to capture infinitesimal deformations and formal neighborhoods that ordinary schemes cannot. Formal schemes play an essential role in deformation theory, the study of Zariski's theorem on formal functions, and Grothendieck's existence theorem.
