An \text{affine (noetherian) formal scheme} is a formal scheme obtained by completing a single affine noetherian scheme along a closed subscheme. Explicitly, if $X = \operatorname{Spec} A$ is an affine noetherian scheme, $Y = V(I)$ is the closed subscheme defined by an ideal $I \subseteq A$, and $\mathfrak{X} = \hat{X}$ is the formal completion of $X$ along $Y$, then $\mathfrak{X}$ is an affine formal scheme. Its structure sheaf is the sheaf associated to the $I$-adic completion $\hat{A} = \varprojlim A/I^n$.
