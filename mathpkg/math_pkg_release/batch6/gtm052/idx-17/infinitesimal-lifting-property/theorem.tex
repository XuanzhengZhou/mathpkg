Let $k$ be an algebraically closed field, let $A$ be a finitely generated $k$-algebra such that $\operatorname{Spec} A$ is a nonsingular variety over $k$. Let $B' \to B$ be a surjective homomorphism of $k$-algebras whose kernel $I$ satisfies $I^2 = 0$, and let $f: A \to B$ be a $k$-algebra homomorphism. Then there exists a $k$-algebra homomorphism $g: A \to B'$ lifting $f$ (i.e., making the diagram commute), and the set of all such liftings is a principal homogeneous space under $\operatorname{Hom}_A(\Omega_{A/k}, I)$, where $I$ is regarded as an $A$-module via $f$.
