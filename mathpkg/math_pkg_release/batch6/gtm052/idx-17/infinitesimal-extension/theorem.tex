Let $X$ be a scheme and let $\mathcal{F}$ be a coherent sheaf on $X$. An \text{infinitesimal extension} of $X$ by $\mathcal{F}$ is a scheme $X'$ together with a closed immersion $X \hookrightarrow X'$ defined by a sheaf of ideals $\mathcal{I}$ such that $\mathcal{I}^2 = 0$ and $\mathcal{I} \cong \mathcal{F}$ as $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules. The \text{trivial extension} is obtained by taking the ringed space $(X, \mathcal{O}_X \oplus \mathcal{F})$ with multiplication $(a \oplus f)(a' \oplus f') = aa' \oplus (af' + a'f)$, where $(X, \mathcal{O}_X \oplus \mathcal{F})$ is a scheme because $\mathcal{F}$ is coherent.
