**Exercise 8.7.** Let $X$ be a scheme and let $\mathcal{F}$ be a coherent sheaf on $X$. An *infinitesimal extension* of $X$ by $\mathcal{F}$ is a scheme $X'$ with a closed immersion $X \hookrightarrow X'$ defined by a sheaf of ideals $\mathcal{I}$ such that $\mathcal{I}^2 = 0$ and $\mathcal{I} \cong \mathcal{F}$ as $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules. The *trivial extension* is $(X, \mathcal{O}_X \oplus \mathcal{F})$ with multiplication $(a \oplus f)(a' \oplus f') = aa' \oplus (af' + a'f)$.

(a) Show that the trivial extension is indeed a scheme (use coherence of $\mathcal{F}$).

(b) Prove: if $X$ is affine and nonsingular, then every infinitesimal extension of $X$ by a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ is isomorphic to the trivial one.

*(Hint: Use the infinitesimal lifting property from Exercise 8.6. See also (III, Ex. 4.10) for another case.)*
