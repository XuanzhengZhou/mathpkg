An infinitesimal extension of a scheme $X$ by a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ is a first-order thickening $X \hookrightarrow X'$ where the ideal of the closed immersion squares to zero and is isomorphic to $\mathcal{F}$. The trivial extension is constructed by adjoining $\mathcal{F}$ as a square-zero ideal to the structure sheaf. The classification of all extensions of $X$ by $\mathcal{F}$ is generally complicated, but in the special case where $X$ is affine and nonsingular, every extension is isomorphic to the trivial one. This result is a geometric incarnation of the infinitesimal lifting property (Exercise 8.6) and is closely related to the vanishing of certain cohomology groups.
