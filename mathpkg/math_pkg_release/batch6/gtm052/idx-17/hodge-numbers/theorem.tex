Let $X$ be a projective nonsingular variety over a field $k$. For any $q$ with $0 \leq q \leq \dim X$, the \text{Hodge number} $h^{q,0}(X)$ is defined as
$$h^{q,0}(X) = \dim_k \Gamma(X, \Omega_X^{q/k}),$$
where $\Omega_X^{q/k} = \bigwedge^q \Omega_X^{k}$ is the sheaf of regular $q$-forms on $X$. For $q = \dim X$, one recovers the geometric genus $p_g = h^{\dim X, 0}$. The numbers $h^{q,0}$ are birational invariants of $X$.
