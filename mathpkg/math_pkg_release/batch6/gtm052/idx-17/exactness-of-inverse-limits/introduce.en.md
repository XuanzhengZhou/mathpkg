Proposition 9.1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry states that the inverse limit functor is exact on short exact sequences of inverse systems, provided the first system satisfies the Mittag-Leffler condition. Part (a) shows that the ML condition passes from the middle system to the cokernel system. Part (b) gives the exactness result: the only nontrivial part is surjectivity on the right, which uses a clever reduction to the ML condition for sets. This result is essential for studying cohomology of formal schemes, though one must beware that the analogous statement fails for sheaves on a topological space.
