**Exercise 8.8 (Plurigenera and Hodge Numbers).** Let $X$ be a projective nonsingular variety over a field $k$.

(a) For any $n > 0$, define the $n$th *plurigenus* $P_n = \dim_k \Gamma(X, \omega_X^{\otimes n})$, where $\omega_X$ is the canonical sheaf. Note that $P_1 = p_g$ is the geometric genus.

(b) For $0 \leq q \leq \dim X$, define the *Hodge number* $h^{q,0} = \dim_k \Gamma(X, \Omega_X^{q/k})$, where $\Omega_X^{q/k} = \bigwedge^q \Omega_X^{k}$ is the sheaf of regular $q$-forms. In particular, $h^{\dim X, 0}$ recovers the geometric genus $p_g$.

(c) Using the method of (8.19), prove that $P_n$ and $h^{q,0}$ are birational invariants: if $X$ and $X'$ are birationally equivalent nonsingular projective varieties, then $P_n(X) = P_n(X')$ and $h^{q,0}(X) = h^{q,0}(X')$ for all $n$ and $q$.
