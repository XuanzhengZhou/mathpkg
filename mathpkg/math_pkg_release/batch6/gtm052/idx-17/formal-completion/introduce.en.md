The formal completion of a noetherian scheme $X$ along a closed subscheme $Y$ is the geometric analog of completing a ring with respect to an ideal. The construction produces a ringed space $(\hat{X}, \mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}})$ whose underlying topological space is simply $Y$, but whose structure sheaf is the inverse limit $\varprojlim \mathcal{O}_X / \mathcal{I}^n$. This encodes the infinitesimal neighborhood of $Y$ inside $X$, capturing all orders of thickening along $Y$. The stalks of $\mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}}$ are local rings, making $\hat{X}$ an example of a formal scheme.
