Let $X$ be a noetherian scheme and let $Y \subseteq X$ be a closed subset. If $\mathcal{I}$ and $\mathcal{J}$ are two sheaves of ideals on $X$ defining (possibly different) closed subscheme structures on $Y$, then the inverse systems $(\mathcal{O}_X / \mathcal{I}^n)$ and $(\mathcal{O}_X / \mathcal{J}^n)$ are cofinal with each other, and therefore have the same inverse limit. Consequently, the structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}}$ of the formal completion depends only on the closed subset $Y$, not on the particular choice of sheaf of ideals defining a subscheme structure on $Y$.
