Since $X$ is noetherian and both $\mathcal{I}$ and $\mathcal{J}$ define $Y$ as a closed subset, there exist integers $m, n$ such that $\mathcal{J} \supseteq \mathcal{I}^m$ and $\mathcal{I} \supseteq \mathcal{J}^n$ (this follows from the fact that on a noetherian scheme, any sheaf of ideals is finitely generated, and the inclusion of closed subsets yields an inclusion-of-radicals relation). Raising to powers gives $\mathcal{J}^k \supseteq \mathcal{I}^{mk}$ and $\mathcal{I}^k \supseteq \mathcal{J}^{nk}$ for all $k$. Thus the two inverse systems $(\mathcal{O}_X / \mathcal{I}^n)$ and $(\mathcal{O}_X / \mathcal{J}^n)$ are cofinal with each other, which implies that their inverse limits are canonically isomorphic. Hence $\mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}}$ depends only on the closed subset $Y$.
