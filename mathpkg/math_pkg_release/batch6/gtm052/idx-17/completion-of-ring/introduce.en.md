The completion of a ring $A$ with respect to an ideal $I$ is a fundamental construction in commutative algebra that generalizes the notion of completing a local ring introduced earlier in Chapter I, Section 5. It is defined as the inverse limit $\hat{A} = \varprojlim A/I^n$, which captures the behavior of elements modulo arbitrarily high powers of $I$. This algebraic completion serves as the model for the geometric notion of completing a scheme along a closed subscheme, which is central to the theory of formal schemes.
