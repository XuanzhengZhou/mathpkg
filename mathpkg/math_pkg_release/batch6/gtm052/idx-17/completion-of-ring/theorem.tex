Let $A$ be a commutative ring with identity and let $I \subseteq A$ be an ideal. For each $n \geq 1$, consider the quotient ring $A/I^n$. The natural surjections $\varphi_{n'n}: A/I^{n'} \to A/I^n$ for $n' \geq n$ make $(A/I^n)$ into an inverse system of rings. The \text{completion of $A$ with respect to $I$} is the inverse limit
$$\hat{A} = \varprojlim_{n} A/I^n.$$
The ring $\hat{A}$ comes equipped with a natural homomorphism $A \to \hat{A}$ whose kernel is $\bigcap_{n \geq 1} I^n$. If this map is an isomorphism, $A$ is called \text{$I$-adically complete}.
