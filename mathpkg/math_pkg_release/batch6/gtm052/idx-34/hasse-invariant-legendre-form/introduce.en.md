This corollary specializes the Hasse invariant criterion to elliptic curves in Legendre form $y^2 = x(x-1)(x-\lambda)$. The Hasse invariant vanishes exactly when the Deuring polynomial $h_p(\lambda) = \sum_{i=0}^k \binom{k}{i}^2 \lambda^i$ evaluates to zero, where $k = (p-1)/2$. Igusa proved that the roots of $h_p$ are always distinct, which allows an exact count of supersingular $j$-invariants: $[p/12]$ ordinary ones besides the special cases $j=0$ and $j=1728$.
