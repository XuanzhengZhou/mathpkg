For any curve $X$, the algebraic fundamental group $\pi_1(X)$ is defined as $\varprojlim \operatorname{Gal}(K'/K)$, where $K$ is the function field of $X$, and $K'$ runs over all Galois extensions of $K$ such that the corresponding curve $X'$ is etale over $X$ (III, Ex. 10.3). Thus, for example, $\pi_1(\mathbf{P}^1) = 1$ (2.5.3). Show that for an elliptic curve $X$,

$$\pi_1(X) = \prod_{l \neq p} \mathbf{Z}_l \times \mathbf{Z}_l \quad \text{if } \operatorname{char} k = 0;$$

$$\pi_1(X) = \prod_{l \neq p} \mathbf{Z}_l \times \mathbf{Z}_l \quad \text{if } \operatorname{char} k = p \text{ and Hasse } X = 0;$$

$$\pi_1(X) = \prod_{l \neq p} \mathbf{Z}_l \times \mathbf{Z}_l \times \prod_{p \neq \operatorname{char} k} T_p(X)$$

(up to the truncation of the text).
