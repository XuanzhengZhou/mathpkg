This proposition gives a concrete algebraic criterion for computing the Hasse invariant of an elliptic curve embedded as a cubic in $\mathbf{P}^2$. The Hasse invariant vanishes precisely when the coefficient of $(xyz)^{p-1}$ in $f^{p-1}$ is zero, where $f$ is the homogeneous cubic equation defining the curve. The proof uses the Frobenius morphism on cohomology and the Čech cohomology of $\mathbf{P}^2$ with coefficients in $\mathcal{O}(-3)$.
