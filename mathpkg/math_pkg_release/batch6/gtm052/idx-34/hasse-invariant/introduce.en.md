The Hasse invariant is a fundamental invariant of elliptic curves defined over fields of positive characteristic. It classifies elliptic curves into two types: those with Hasse invariant $1$ (ordinary) and those with Hasse invariant $0$ (supersingular). The invariant is defined via the action of the Frobenius morphism on the one-dimensional cohomology group $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$, which must be either zero or bijective because the ground field is perfect.
