Let $X$ be an elliptic curve over a perfect field $k$ of characteristic $p > 0$, and let $F: X \to X$ be the Frobenius morphism. Then $F$ induces a $p$-linear map

$$F^*: H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X) \to H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$$

on cohomology, satisfying $F^*(\lambda a) = \lambda^p F^*(a)$ for all $\lambda \in k$, $a \in H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$. Since $X$ is elliptic, $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$ is a one-dimensional vector space over $k$.

\textbf{Definition.} If $F^* = 0$, we say that $X$ has \textbf{Hasse invariant} $0$ or that $X$ is \textbf{supersingular}; otherwise we say that $X$ has \textbf{Hasse invariant} $1$.
