We apply Theorem 7.7 with $H = Z$ (a hypersurface of degree $e$ in $\mathbf{P}^2$). The irreducible components of $Y \cap Z$ are the points $P_1, \ldots, P_s$. The degree of a point $P_j$ in $\mathbf{P}^2$ is 1, since its homogeneous coordinate ring $S/\mathfrak{p}_j$ has Hilbert polynomial $P_{P_j}(l) = 1$ for all $l \geqslant 0$. Indeed, a point corresponds to a maximal homogeneous ideal, and the homogeneous coordinate ring is isomorphic to $k[t]$ with the usual grading shifted, so $\dim_k (S/\mathfrak{p}_j)_l = 1$ for all $l \gg 0$. Thus $\deg P_j = 0! \cdot 1 = 1$. Substituting into the formula from Theorem 7.7 gives $\sum i(Y, Z; P_j) \cdot 1 = (\deg Y)(\deg Z) = d e$.
