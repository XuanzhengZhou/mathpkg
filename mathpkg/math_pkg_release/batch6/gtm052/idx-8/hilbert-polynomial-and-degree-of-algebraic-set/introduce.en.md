For an algebraic set $Y$ in projective space $\mathbf{P}^n$, the Hilbert polynomial $P_Y$ is defined as the Hilbert polynomial of its homogeneous coordinate ring $S(Y) = S/I_Y$. The degree of $P_Y$ equals the dimension of $Y$, and the degree of $Y$ is $r!$ times the leading coefficient of $P_Y$. This generalizes the notion of degree of a hypersurface to arbitrary algebraic sets, providing a fundamental numerical invariant.
