Proposition 1.1 establishes the fundamental principle that a morphism of sheaves is an isomorphism if and only if it induces isomorphisms on all stalks. This reflects the local nature of sheaves: properties of sheaves can be checked stalk by stalk. The proof shows injectivity by noting that if a section vanishes on every stalk, it vanishes locally and hence globally. Surjectivity uses the gluing axiom to lift sections from stalks back to open sets.
