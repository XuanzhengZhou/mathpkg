Theorem 7.7 generalizes Bézout's classical theorem to intersections of projective varieties with hypersurfaces in $\mathbf{P}^n$. The intersection multiplicity $i(Y,H; Z_j)$ is defined algebraically via the multiplicity of the module $S/(I_Y + I_H)$ at the minimal prime corresponding to each irreducible component $Z_j$. The weighted sum of these multiplicities by the degrees of the components equals the product of the degrees of $Y$ and $H$.
