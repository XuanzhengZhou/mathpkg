The Hilbert polynomial is the fundamental numerical invariant of a finitely generated graded module over a polynomial ring. For large degrees $l$, the dimension of the $l$-th graded piece $M_l$ is given by a polynomial $P_M(l)$. The degree of $P_M$ equals the dimension of the projective variety defined by the annihilator of $M$, making it a crucial tool for studying dimensions and degrees in projective algebraic geometry.
