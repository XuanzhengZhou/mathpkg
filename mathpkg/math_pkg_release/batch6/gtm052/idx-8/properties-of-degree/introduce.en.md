Proposition 7.6 establishes the fundamental properties of the degree of an algebraic set in projective space. The degree is always a positive integer for nonempty sets. It is additive over unions of equidimensional components whose intersection has strictly lower dimension. The projective space $\mathbf{P}^n$ itself has degree 1, and a hypersurface defined by a single homogeneous polynomial of degree $d$ has degree $d$, confirming consistency with the earlier definition.
