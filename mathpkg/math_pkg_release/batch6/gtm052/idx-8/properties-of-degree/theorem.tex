Let $Y \subseteq \mathbf{P}^n$ be an algebraic set.

(a) If $Y \neq \emptyset$, then $\deg Y$ is a positive integer.

(b) Let $Y = Y_1 \cup Y_2$, where $Y_1$ and $Y_2$ have the same dimension $r$, and $\dim(Y_1 \cap Y_2) < r$. Then $\deg Y = \deg Y_1 + \deg Y_2$.

(c) $\deg \mathbf{P}^n = 1$.

(d) If $H \subseteq \mathbf{P}^n$ is a hypersurface whose ideal is generated by a homogeneous polynomial of degree $d$, then $\deg H = d$. In other words, this definition of degree is consistent with the degree of a hypersurface as defined earlier (1.4.2).
