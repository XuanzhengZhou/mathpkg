The constant sheaf $\underline{A}$ on a topological space $X$ is constructed from an abelian group $A$ by taking continuous maps into $A$ with the discrete topology. On a connected open set, the sections are isomorphic to $A$ itself. Unlike the constant presheaf (which would assign $A$ to every open set), the constant sheaf properly accounts for disconnected open sets by assigning a product of copies of $A$, one per connected component. It is a fundamental example in sheaf theory and cohomology.
