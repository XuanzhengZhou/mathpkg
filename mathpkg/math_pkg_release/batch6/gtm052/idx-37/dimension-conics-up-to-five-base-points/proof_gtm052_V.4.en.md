(a) Every time we prescribe a new base point on a linear system without unassigned base points, the dimension drops by exactly one. Since the linear system of all conics in $\mathbf{P}^2$ has dimension 5, and by Lemma 4.1 the system has no unassigned base points for up to 4 points (and the same argument extends), the result follows.

(b) For $r = 5$, $\dim \mathfrak{d} = 0$, so there is a unique conic containing $P_1, \ldots, P_5$. It must be irreducible since no three of the $P_i$ are collinear — if it were reducible, it would be a union of two lines, and one of the lines would contain three of the points, contradicting the hypothesis.
