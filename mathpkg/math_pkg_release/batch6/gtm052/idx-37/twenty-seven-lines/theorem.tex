Let $X$ be a nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$, realized as the blowup of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at six points $P_1, \ldots, P_6$ in general position. Then $X$ contains exactly 27 lines. Each line has self-intersection $-1$, and they are the only irreducible curves on $X$ with negative self-intersection. The 27 lines consist of:

(a) the six exceptional curves $E_i$ ($i = 1, \ldots, 6$);

(b) the strict transform $F_{ij}$ of the line in $\mathbf{P}^2$ through $P_i$ and $P_j$, for each of the $\binom{6}{2} = 15$ pairs $\{i,j\}$ with $i < j$;

(c) the strict transform $G_j$ of the conic in $\mathbf{P}^2$ through the five points $\{P_1, \ldots, P_6\} \setminus \{P_j\}$, for each $j = 1, \ldots, 6$.
