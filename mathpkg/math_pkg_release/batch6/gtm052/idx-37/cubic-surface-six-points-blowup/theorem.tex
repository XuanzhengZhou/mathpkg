Let $P_1, \ldots, P_6$ be six points in $\mathbf{P}^2$ such that no three are collinear and not all six lie on a conic. Let $X$ be the blowup of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at $P_1, \ldots, P_6$, with exceptional divisors $E_1, \ldots, E_6$. Then the complete linear system $|3l - E_1 - \ldots - E_6|$ on $X$ (where $l$ is the pullback of a line in $\mathbf{P}^2$) is very ample and embeds $X$ as a nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$. Conversely, every nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$ can be obtained in this way.
