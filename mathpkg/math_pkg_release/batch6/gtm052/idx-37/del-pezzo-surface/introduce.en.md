A Del Pezzo surface is defined as a surface $X \subset \mathbf{P}^d$ of degree $d$ with anticanonical embedding, i.e., $\omega_X \cong \mathcal{O}_X(-1)$. Every Del Pezzo surface is either obtained by blowing up $r$ points in $\mathbf{P}^2$ ($0 \leqslant r \leqslant 8$) or is the 2-uple embedding of a quadric surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$ (degree 8 in $\mathbf{P}^8$). In particular, every nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$ is a Del Pezzo surface of degree 3, obtained by blowing up six points.
