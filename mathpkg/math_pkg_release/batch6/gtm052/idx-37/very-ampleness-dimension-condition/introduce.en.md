This criterion reformulates very ampleness in terms of dimension drop: a complete linear system $|D|$ on a surface is very ample if and only if prescribing any two base points $P, Q$ (possibly with $Q$ infinitely near $P$) causes the dimension to drop by exactly 2. This is a convenient numerical reformulation of the classical very ampleness criterion, reminiscent of the Riemann-Roch style dimension counting in (IV, 3.1).
