The quadratic transformation $\varphi: \mathbf{P}^2 \dashrightarrow \mathbf{P}^2$ associated to three non-collinear points admits a clean birational description: after blowing up the three points, the resulting surface $X'$ can be mapped isomorphically to a second $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at three points, with the exceptional curves and strict transforms of the coordinate lines interchanged. Thus the quadratic transformation is equivalently "blow up three points, then blow down the three lines joining them."
