Let $P_1, P_2, P_3$ be three non-collinear points in $\mathbf{P}^2$, and let $\varphi: \mathbf{P}^2 \dashrightarrow \mathbf{P}^2$ be the quadratic transformation defined by the linear system of conics through $P_1, P_2, P_3$. Let $X'$ be $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at $P_1, P_2, P_3$, with exceptional curves $E_i$. Then $\varphi$ factors as $\psi \circ \pi^{-1}$, where $\pi: X' \to \mathbf{P}^2$ is the blowup, and $\psi: X' \to \mathbf{P}^2$ identifies $X'$ with the second $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at the three points $Q_1, Q_2, Q_3$, in such a way that:
\begin{itemize}
\item the exceptional curve $\psi^{-1}(Q_i)$ is the strict transform of the line $L_{jk}$ joining $P_j, P_k$ on the first $\mathbf{P}^2$, for each $\{i,j,k\} = \{1,2,3\}$;
\item $\psi(E_i)$ is the line $M_{jk}$ joining $Q_j$ and $Q_k$, for each $\{i,j,k\} = \{1,2,3\}$.
\end{itemize}
Thus the quadratic transformation is "blowing up $P_1, P_2, P_3$ and blowing down the lines $\tilde{L}_{12}, \tilde{L}_{13}, \tilde{L}_{23}$."
