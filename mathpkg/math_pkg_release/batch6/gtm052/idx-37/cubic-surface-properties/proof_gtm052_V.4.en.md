All properties follow from earlier results. (a) and (b) follow from (3.2), the intersection pairing on a blowup. (c) comes from the definition of the embedding in $\mathbf{P}^3$ via the linear system $|h|$. (d) The canonical class of a blowup of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at six points is $K_X = -3l + \sum e_i$ by the blowup formula for the canonical sheaf. Since $h = 3l - \sum e_i$, we have $K_X = -h$. (e) follows from (d) and linearity of intersection pairing. (f) is the adjunction formula for curves on a surface: $2p_a(D) - 2 = D.(D + K_X)$. Substituting $D.K_X = -d$ gives the stated formula. (g) follows by expanding $D^2 = a^2 - \sum b_i^2$ and $d = 3a - \sum b_i$ in the formula from (f), yielding $\frac{1}{2}(a^2 - 2a) - \sum \frac{1}{2}(b_i^2 - b_i) = \frac{1}{2}(a-1)(a-2) - \sum \frac{1}{2} b_i(b_i-1)$. The statement about irreducible curves follows from the description of the strict transform (3.6).
