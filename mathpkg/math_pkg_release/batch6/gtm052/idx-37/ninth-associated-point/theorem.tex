Let $P_1, \ldots, P_8$ be eight points in $\mathbf{P}^2$, no four collinear, and no seven lying on a conic. Then there exists a uniquely determined point $P_9$ (possibly an infinitely near point) such that every cubic curve passing through $P_1, \ldots, P_8$ also passes through $P_9$. This remains true if $P_2$ is infinitely near $P_1$, and $P_8$ is infinitely near any one of $P_1, \ldots, P_7$.
