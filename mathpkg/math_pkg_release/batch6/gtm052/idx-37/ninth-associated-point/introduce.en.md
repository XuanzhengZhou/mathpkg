This classical result (Corollary 4.5) asserts that given eight points in $\mathbf{P}^2$ in sufficiently general position, there is a uniquely determined ninth point through which every cubic passing through the eight points must also pass. The proof uses Bezout's theorem: the linear system of cubics through eight points has dimension at least 1, so it contains two distinct irreducible cubics, which intersect in nine points by Bezout -- eight of which are the given points, forcing the ninth intersection point to be a common base point.
