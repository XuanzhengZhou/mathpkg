Let $\mathfrak{d} = |3H - P_1 - \ldots - P_r|$ be the linear system of plane cubic curves through points $P_1, \ldots, P_r$ in $\mathbf{P}^2$, with no 4 collinear and no 7 on a conic. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] if $r \leqslant 7$, then $\dim \mathfrak{d} = 9 - r$;
\item[(b)] if $r = 8$, then $\dim \mathfrak{d} \geqslant 1$, and $\mathfrak{d}$ has finitely many unassigned base points.
\end{enumerate}
