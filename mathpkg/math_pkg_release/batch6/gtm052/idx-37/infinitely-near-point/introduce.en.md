An infinitely near point of a curve $C$ on a surface $X$ is a point lying on the strict transform of $C$ after blowing up $X$ at a singular point $P \in C$. This concept, introduced by Zariski, allows us to analyze curve singularities by successively blowing up until the curve becomes nonsingular. The configuration of infinitely near singular points and their multiplicities provides discrete invariants that can be used to classify singularities.
