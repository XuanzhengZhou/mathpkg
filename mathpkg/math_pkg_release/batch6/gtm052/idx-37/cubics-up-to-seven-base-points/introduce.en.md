Proposition 4.3 establishes that the linear system of plane cubic curves passing through up to seven points in sufficiently general position (no four collinear, no seven on a conic) has no unintended base points. This result is critical for the construction of the cubic surface as the image of $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at six points under the linear system of cubics through those points, ensuring that the resulting rational map is a morphism.
