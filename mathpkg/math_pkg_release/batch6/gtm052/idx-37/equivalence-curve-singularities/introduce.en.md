This definition introduces a classification of curve singularities on surfaces via an equivalence relation based on the resolution process: two singularities are equivalent if after successive blowups the behavior of the strict transform and exceptional curves are isomorphic. The first discrete invariants are the multiplicity and the configuration of infinitely near singular points. For double points, the equivalence class is determined by the number of blowups needed and whether the singularity is a node or cusp at the last step, and in this case the classification coincides with the analytic one ($y^2 = x^r$).
