Proposition 4.10 asserts that on a nonsingular cubic surface, any six mutually skew lines among the 27 lines can serve as a set of exceptional curves to be blown down, yielding $\mathbf{P}^2$. Thus the representation of the cubic surface as $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at six points is not unique -- each choice of six mutually skew lines among the 27 gives a different representation, reflecting the high symmetry of the 27-line configuration.
