Let $X$ be a nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$, and let $E'_1, \ldots, E'_6$ be any subset of six mutually skew lines chosen from among the 27 lines on $X$. Then the $E'_i$ can be simultaneously blown down, and the resulting surface is isomorphic to $\mathbf{P}^2$.

Consequently, any ordered set of six mutually skew lines on $X$ gives a representation of $X$ as $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at six points, and the 15 lines $F'_{ij}$ and the 6 lines $G'_j$ in the 27-line configuration are the strict transforms of the lines and conics with respect to this representation.
