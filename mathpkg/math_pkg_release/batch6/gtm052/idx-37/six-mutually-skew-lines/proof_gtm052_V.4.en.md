Since each $E'_i$ has self-intersection $-1$ by Theorem 4.9 and they are mutually skew (disjoint), Castelnuovo's criterion allows us to blow down each $E'_i$ to a smooth point. Blowing down all six yields a smooth surface $X_0$. By computing the canonical class and intersection numbers, one verifies that $X_0 \cong \mathbf{P}^2$. The remaining 21 lines on $X$ become the strict transforms of lines through pairs of the blown-down points and conics through five of them, matching the standard description from Theorem 4.9.
