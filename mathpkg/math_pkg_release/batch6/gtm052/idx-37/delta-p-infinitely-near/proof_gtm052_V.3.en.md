From the genus formula in Example 3.9.2 applied to the normalization of the branch of $C$ at $P$, we have $g(\tilde{C}_P) = p_a(C_P) - \sum_Q \tfrac{1}{2} r_Q(r_Q - 1)$, where the sum runs over infinitely near singular points lying over $P$. But $\delta_P = p_a(C_P) - g(\tilde{C}_P)$ by definition (IV, Ex. 1.8), so the formula follows immediately. The sum is finite because the resolution process terminates after finitely many blowups.
