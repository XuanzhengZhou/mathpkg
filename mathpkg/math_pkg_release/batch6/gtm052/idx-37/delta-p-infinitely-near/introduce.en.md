The invariant $\delta_P$ measures the contribution of a singular point $P$ on a curve to the difference between arithmetic and geometric genus. By working with the infinitesimal neighborhood of one point at a time, $\delta_P$ can be expressed as a sum over all infinitely near singular points $Q$ lying over $P$ (including $P$ itself) of $\tfrac{1}{2}r_Q(r_Q-1)$, where each term represents the drop in arithmetic genus when blowing up that point.
