The functor (i) $\to$ (ii) is the inclusion. The functor (ii) $\to$ (iii) sends a quasi-projective curve to its function field. The functor (iii) $\to$ (i) sends a function field $K$ to the abstract nonsingular curve $C_K$, which is projective by Theorem 6.9.

Given two homomorphisms of function fields, it follows from the uniqueness part of (6.8) that the corresponding morphisms $C_1 \to C_2 \to C_3$ and $C_1 \to C_3$ are compatible. Hence $K \mapsto C_K$ is a functor from (iii) $\to$ (i). It is clearly inverse to the given functor (i) $\to$ (ii) $\to$ (iii), so we have an equivalence of categories.
