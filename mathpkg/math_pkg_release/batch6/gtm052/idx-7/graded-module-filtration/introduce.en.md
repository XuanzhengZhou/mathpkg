This proposition is the graded analogue of the existence of a composition series for finitely generated modules over a noetherian ring. For a finitely generated graded $S$-module $M$, it provides a filtration whose successive quotients are isomorphic to shifts of $S/\mathfrak{p}_i$ for homogeneous prime ideals $\mathfrak{p}_i$. This filtration is essential for the definition of the Hilbert polynomial: since the Hilbert polynomial is additive in short exact sequences, it reduces the computation for general $M$ to the case of $S/\mathfrak{p}$, which corresponds to a projective variety.
