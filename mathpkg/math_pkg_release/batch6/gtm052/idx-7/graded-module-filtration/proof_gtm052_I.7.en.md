Consider the set of graded submodules of $M$ which admit such a filtration. This set is nonempty (the zero submodule qualifies). Let $M'$ be a maximal element in this set under inclusion. Suppose $M' \neq M$. Then consider $M'' = M/M'$. Let $I_m = \operatorname{Ann}(m)$ for a nonzero homogeneous element $m \in M''$. Choose $m$ such that $I_m$ is maximal among such annihilators.

We claim $I_m$ is prime. Suppose $ab \in I_m$ with $a \notin I_m$. Then $am \neq 0$, and $I_m \subseteq I_{am}$. By maximality, $I_m = I_{am}$. Since $ab \in I_m$, we have $abm = 0$, so $a \in I_{am} = I_m$, showing $b \in I_m$. Thus $I_m$ is a homogeneous prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$.

Let $m$ have degree $l$. Then the submodule $N \subseteq M''$ generated by $m$ is isomorphic to $(S/\mathfrak{p})(-l)$. Lifting to $M$ gives $M' \subsetneq N' \subseteq M$ with $N'/M' \simeq (S/\mathfrak{p})(-l)$, contradicting the maximality of $M'$. Hence $M' = M$, proving the existence of the filtration.

For (a), $\mathfrak{p} \supseteq \operatorname{Ann} M \iff \mathfrak{p} \supseteq \operatorname{Ann}(M^i/M^{i-1})$ for some $i$. But $\operatorname{Ann}((S/\mathfrak{p}_i)(l)) = \mathfrak{p}_i$, giving the result.

For (b), localize at a minimal prime $\mathfrak{p}$. After localization, $M^i_{\mathfrak{p}} = M^{i-1}_{\mathfrak{p}}$ except when $\mathfrak{p}_i = \mathfrak{p}$, in which case $M^i_{\mathfrak{p}}/M^{i-1}_{\mathfrak{p}} \simeq (S/\mathfrak{p})_{\mathfrak{p}} = k(\mathfrak{p})$, the residue field. Thus the length of $M_{\mathfrak{p}}$ equals the number of $i$ with $\mathfrak{p}_i = \mathfrak{p}$.
