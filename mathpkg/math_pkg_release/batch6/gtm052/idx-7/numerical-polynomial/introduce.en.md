A numerical polynomial is a polynomial with rational coefficients that takes integer values for all sufficiently large integer inputs. This concept is fundamental for the definition of the Hilbert polynomial of a graded module. Key examples include the binomial coefficient polynomials $\binom{z}{r} = \frac{1}{r!}z(z-1)\cdots(z-r+1)$, which form an integral basis for the space of numerical polynomials. Numerical polynomials are used to encode dimension and degree information of projective varieties.
