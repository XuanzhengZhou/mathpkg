The Affine Dimension Theorem provides a lower bound on the dimension of the intersection of two varieties in affine space. If $Y$ and $Z$ have dimensions $r$ and $s$ respectively, then each irreducible component of their intersection has dimension at least $r + s - n$. This is analogous to the linear algebra result that the intersection of subspaces of dimensions $r$ and $s$ in an $n$-dimensional vector space has dimension at least $r + s - n$. The proof uses Krull's Hauptidealsatz for the hypersurface case and reduction to the affine cone for the general case.
