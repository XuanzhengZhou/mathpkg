Every algebraic curve, regardless of its singularities, is birationally equivalent to a nonsingular projective curve. This follows immediately from Theorem 6.9: if $Y$ is any curve with function field $K$, then $Y$ is birationally equivalent to $C_K$, the abstract nonsingular curve associated to $K$, which by Theorem 6.9 is isomorphic to a nonsingular projective curve. This provides a resolution of singularities for curves in characteristic zero.
