Given a function field $K$ of dimension $1$ over $k$, one can construct the abstract nonsingular curve $C_K$ whose points are the discrete valuation rings of $K/k$. This theorem shows that $C_K$ is always isomorphic to a nonsingular projective curve, establishing that every abstract nonsingular curve (in the sense of having function field of dimension $1$) can be realized as a projective variety. The proof proceeds by covering $C_K$ with affine open subsets, taking projective closures, and using the product construction to build a projective model.
