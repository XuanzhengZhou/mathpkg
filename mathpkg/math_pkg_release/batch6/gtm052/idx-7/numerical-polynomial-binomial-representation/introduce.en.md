This proposition shows that every numerical polynomial can be uniquely expressed as an integer linear combination of binomial coefficient polynomials $\binom{z}{r}$. The binomial coefficients form a $\mathbf{Z}$-basis for the additive group of numerical polynomials. This representation is crucial for extracting geometric invariants: the leading coefficient of the Hilbert polynomial, expressed in this basis, gives the degree of the projective variety.
