If $P \in \mathbf{Q}[z]$ is a numerical polynomial, then there are integers $c_0, c_1, \ldots, c_r$ such that
$$P(z) = c_0 \binom{z}{r} + c_1 \binom{z}{r-1} + \ldots + c_r,$$
where
$$\binom{z}{r} = \frac{1}{r!} z(z-1) \cdots (z-r+1).$$
In particular, $P(n) \in \mathbf{Z}$ for all $n \in \mathbf{Z}$.
