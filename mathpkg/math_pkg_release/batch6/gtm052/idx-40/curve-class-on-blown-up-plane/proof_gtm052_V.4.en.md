If $C$ is any irreducible curve in $X$, other than $E_1, \ldots, E_6$, then $\pi(C)$ is an irreducible plane curve $C_0$, and $C$ in turn is the strict transform of $C_0$. Let $C_0$ have degree $a$, and suppose that $C_0$ has a point of multiplicity $b_i$ at each $P_i$. Then $\pi^*C_0 = C + \sum b_i E_i$, by (3.6). Since $C_0 \sim a \cdot \text{line}$, we conclude that $C \sim al - \sum b_i e_i$.
