This result establishes the correspondence between divisor classes on a cubic surface (realized as a blow-up of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at six points) and plane curves with prescribed multiplicities at the blown-up points. Any divisor class $al - \sum b_i e_i$ corresponds to the strict transform of a plane curve of degree $a$ with multiplicity $b_i$ at $P_i$. This reduces the study of curves on the cubic surface to the study of plane curves with assigned singularities.
