Let $P_1, \ldots, P_6$ be six points in $\mathbf{P}^2$, no three collinear, not all on a conic. Let $\pi: X' \to \mathbf{P}^2$ be the blowing-up of $P_1, \ldots, P_6$, with exceptional divisors $E_1, \ldots, E_6$. Let $l$ be the pullback of a line in $\mathbf{P}^2$, and let $e_i = [E_i] \in \operatorname{Pic} X'$. Let $D = 3l - \sum e_i$. Then the complete linear system $|D|$ is very ample, giving an embedding of $X'$ as a nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$, and

$$\omega_{X'} \cong \mathcal{O}_{X'}(-D).$$

In particular, $\omega_{X'} \cong \mathcal{O}_{X'}(-1)$ in the given projective embedding.
