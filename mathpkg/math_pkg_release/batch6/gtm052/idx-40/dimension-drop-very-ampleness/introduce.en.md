This reformulation of the very ampleness condition states that the dimension of a complete linear system drops by exactly 2 when two base points are assigned (including the case where one is infinitely near the other). This captures both point separation and tangent vector separation in a single dimension condition, reminiscent of the Riemann-Roch approach (IV, 3.1).
