Let $X$ be a nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$, realized as the blow-up of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at six points $P_1, \ldots, P_6$. Let $h$ be the hyperplane class, $l$ the pullback of a line, and $e_1, \ldots, e_6$ the exceptional divisor classes. Then for any divisor $D$ on $X$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $h \sim 3l - \sum e_i$;
\item[(b)] the intersection form on $\operatorname{Pic} X$ is given by $l^2 = 1$, $e_i^2 = -1$, $l.e_i = 0$, $e_i.e_j = 0$ $(i \neq j)$;
\item[(c)] $h^2 = 3$ (the degree);
\item[(d)] $D.K = -D.h$, where $K$ is the canonical divisor;
\item[(e)] the degree of $D$ is $D.h$;
\item[(f)] the arithmetic genus is given by $2p_a(D) - 2 = D^2 - D.h$;
\item[(g)] $p_a(D) = \frac{1}{2}(D^2 - D.h) + 1$.
\end{enumerate}
