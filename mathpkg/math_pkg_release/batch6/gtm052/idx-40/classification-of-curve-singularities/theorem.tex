Let $C$ be a (reduced) curve on a surface $X$, and let $P \in C$ be a singular point. The classification of the singularity $(C,P)$ is determined by:

1. The multiplicity $r_P$.
2. The multiplicities of all infinitely near singular points lying over $P$, and their configuration around $P$.

This data determines $\delta_P = \sum \frac{1}{2} r_Q(r_Q - 1)$ (3.9.3). For double points ($r = 2$), blowing up repeated $n$ times until the strict transform $\tilde{C}$ becomes nonsingular yields:
- (a) $\tilde{C}$ meeting the exceptional curve transversally in two distinct points (node), or
- (b) $\tilde{C}$ meeting the exceptional curve in one point and tangent to it (cusp).

For double points, this classification by equivalence coincides with classification by analytic isomorphism; up to analytic isomorphism, any double point is given by $y^2 = x^r$ for some $r \geqslant 2$.
