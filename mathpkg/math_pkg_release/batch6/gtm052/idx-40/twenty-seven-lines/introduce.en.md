This is one of the most famous results in classical algebraic geometry: every nonsingular cubic surface contains exactly 27 lines. The proof classifies all irreducible curves with negative self-intersection on $X$, showing they must lie in classes $al - \sum b_i e_i$ satisfying $a \leqslant 2$ and specific multiplicity conditions. The 27 lines consist of the 6 exceptional curves, 15 lines through pairs of blown-up points, and 6 conics through five of the six points. This result connects to rich classical geometry, including Schläfli's double-six configuration.
