An infinitely near point of a curve $C$ on a surface $X$ is a point that appears on the strict transform of $C$ after a monoidal transformation (blow-up) centered at a point of $C$. This concept is fundamental for studying curve singularities, as the configuration of infinitely near singular points determines invariants such as $\delta_P$. Infinitely near points allow successive resolution of singularities, where multiplicities at these points contribute to the drop in arithmetic genus.
