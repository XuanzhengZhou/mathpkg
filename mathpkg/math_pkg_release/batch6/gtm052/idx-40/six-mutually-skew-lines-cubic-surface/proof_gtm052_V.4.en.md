[The proof in the text is truncated. The standard argument uses the fact that six mutually skew $(-1)$-curves on $X$ can be contracted to a nonsingular surface. By Castelnuovo's criterion, each $(-1)$-curve can be blown down, and since they are mutually disjoint, they can be blown down simultaneously, yielding $\mathbf{P}^2$.]
