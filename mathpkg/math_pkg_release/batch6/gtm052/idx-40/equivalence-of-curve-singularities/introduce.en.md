Two curve singularities are equivalent if they share the same discrete data: multiplicities at all singular points (including infinitely near ones) and their configuration around the original singular point. This equivalence relation is coarser than analytic isomorphism — any analytically isomorphic singularities are equivalent, but the converse does not hold. The data of infinitely near multiplicities and their configuration suffices to determine $\delta_P$ and provides a useful classification framework.
