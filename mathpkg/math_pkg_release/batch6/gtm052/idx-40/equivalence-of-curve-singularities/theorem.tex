Let $C$ and $C'$ be (reduced) curves lying on surfaces $X$ and $X'$, with singular points $P \in C$ and $P' \in C'$. The singularities $(C,P)$ and $(C',P')$ are \text{equivalent} if they have the same sequence of multiplicities at infinitely near singular points and the same configuration of these points around $P$ and $P'$ respectively. This classification is weaker than the classification by analytic isomorphism (I, 5.6.1).
