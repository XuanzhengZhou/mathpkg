This result describes the standard quadratic transformation of $\mathbf{P}^2$ as a composition of blowing up the three fundamental points and then blowing down the strict transforms of the three lines joining them. The isomorphism is established by considering the incidence variety $V \subset \mathbf{P}^2 \times \mathbf{P}^2$ defined by $x_0 y_0 = x_1 y_1 = x_2 y_2$, whose first projection identifies $V$ with the blown-up $\mathbf{P}^2$.
