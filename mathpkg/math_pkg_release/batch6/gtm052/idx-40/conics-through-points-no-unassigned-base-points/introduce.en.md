This proposition states that the linear system of conics in $\mathbf{P}^2$ passing through up to 4 points (with no three collinear) has no unassigned base points. This means every point not among the assigned base points is avoided by some conic in the system. The proof constructs explicit pairs of reducible conics (lines through the points) whose intersection is precisely the set of assigned base points.
