Let $X$ be a surface, let $|D|$ be a complete linear system of curves on $X$, and let $P_1, \ldots, P_r$ be points of $X$. The sublinear system

$$\mathfrak{d} = |D - P_1 - \ldots - P_r|$$

consists of divisors $D \in |D|$ which pass through the points $P_1, \ldots, P_r$. These are called the \text{assigned base points}.

If we assign base points $Q_1, \ldots, Q_s$ infinitely near a point $P$, then every divisor containing $Q_1, \ldots, Q_s$ will automatically have at least an $s$-fold point at $P$. By convention, every point $P$ must be assigned with a multiplicity at least equal to the sum of the multiplicities of the assigned base points infinitely near $P$.
