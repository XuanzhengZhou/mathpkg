(a) Every time we prescribe a new base point on a linear system without unassigned base points, the dimension drops by one. Since the linear system of all conics in $\mathbf{P}^2$ has dimension 5, this follows from (4.1).

(b) For $r = 5$, $\dim \mathfrak{d} = 0$, so there is a unique conic containing $P_1, \ldots, P_5$. It must be irreducible since no three of the $P_i$ are collinear.
