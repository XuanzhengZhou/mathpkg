This classical result states that any 8 points in the plane in sufficiently general position determine a unique ninth point through which every cubic passing through the 8 points must also pass. The proof uses Bézout's theorem: choosing two distinct irreducible cubics through the 8 points, their intersection consists of 9 points, determining $P_9$. Since the linear system has dimension 1, any other cubic in the system is a linear combination and thus also passes through $P_9$.
