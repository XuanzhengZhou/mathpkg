By (4.4) the linear system $\mathfrak{d}$ of all cubics through $P_1, \ldots, P_8$ has dimension one, and we can choose two distinct irreducible ones $C, C' \in \mathfrak{d}$. Then by Bézout's theorem (1.4.2) (cf. Ex. 3.2), $C$ and $C'$ meet in 9 points, 8 of which are $P_1, \ldots, P_8$. So this determines a ninth point $P_9$, possibly an infinitely near point. Now since $\dim \mathfrak{d} = 1$, any other curve $C'' \in \mathfrak{d}$, irreducible or not, is a linear combination of $C$ and $C'$, so it must also pass through $P_9$. Thus $P_9$ is an unassigned base point of $\mathfrak{d}$.
