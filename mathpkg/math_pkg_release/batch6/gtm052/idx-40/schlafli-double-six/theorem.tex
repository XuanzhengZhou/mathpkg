A \text{Schl\"afli double-six} is a configuration of 12 lines $E_1, \ldots, E_6, G_1, \ldots, G_6$ in $\mathbf{P}^3$ with the property that:
\begin{itemize}
\item the $E_i$ are mutually skew (disjoint),
\item the $G_j$ are mutually skew,
\item $E_i$ meets $G_j$ if and only if $i \neq j$.
\end{itemize}

Given a line $E_1$ and five lines $G_2, \ldots, G_6$ meeting it, but otherwise in sufficiently general position, the other lines $E_2, \ldots, E_6$ and $G_1$ are uniquely determined so as to form a double-six. Furthermore, each double-six is contained in a unique nonsingular cubic surface, and thus forms part of a set of 27 lines on a cubic surface.
