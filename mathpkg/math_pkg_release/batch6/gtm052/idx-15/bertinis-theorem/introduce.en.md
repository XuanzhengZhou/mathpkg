Bertini's theorem asserts that for a nonsingular projective variety $X \subset \mathbf{P}^n_k$, a general hyperplane section $H \cap X$ is again nonsingular (and connected when $\dim X \geq 2$). The proof uses the surjectivity of a natural map $\varphi_x: V \to \mathcal{O}_{x,X}/\mathfrak{m}_x^2$ at each closed point $x \in X$, where $V = \Gamma(\mathbf{P}^n, \mathcal{O}(1))$. The "bad" hyperplanes at each point form a proper linear subspace of the complete linear system, so their union cannot cover it.
