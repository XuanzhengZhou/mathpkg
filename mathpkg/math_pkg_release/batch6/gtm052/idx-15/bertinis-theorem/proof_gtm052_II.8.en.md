A hyperplane $H$ is determined by a nonzero global section $f \in V = \Gamma(\mathbf{P}^n, \mathcal{O}_{\mathbf{P}^n}(1))$. Fix $f_0 \in V$ such that $x \notin H_0$, the hyperplane defined by $f_0$. For each closed point $x \in X$, define a map of $k$-vector spaces

$$\varphi_x: V \to \mathcal{O}_{x,X}/\mathfrak{m}_x^2$$

by sending $f \in V$ to the image of $f/f_0$ in $\mathcal{O}_{x,X}/\mathfrak{m}_x^2$ (note that $f/f_0$ is a regular function on $\mathbf{P}^n - H_0$). The scheme $H \cap X$ is defined at $x$ by the ideal generated by $f/f_0$, so $x \in H \cap X$ iff $\varphi_x(f) \in \mathfrak{m}_x$, and $x$ is nonregular on $H \cap X$ iff $\varphi_x(f) \in \mathfrak{m}_x^2$.

Since $x$ is a closed point and $k$ is algebraically closed, $\mathfrak{m}_x/\mathfrak{m}_x^2$ is generated by linear forms, so $\varphi_x$ is surjective. If $\dim X = r$, then $\dim_k \mathcal{O}_{x,X}/\mathfrak{m}_x^2 = r+1$. With $\dim V = n+1$, we have $\dim \ker \varphi_x = n-r$. The set $B_x$ of hyperplanes singular at $x$ is a linear system of dimension $n-r-1$ in the complete linear system $|H| \cong \mathbf{P}^n$. The union of $B_x$ over all $x \in X$ cannot cover $\mathbf{P}^n$ since it has dimension at most $n-1$ (when $\dim X \geq 1$). Thus a general hyperplane is nonsingular everywhere on $X$. The connectedness claim for $\dim X \geq 2$ follows from (III, 7.9.1).
