A variety $X$ over an algebraically closed field $k$ is nonsingular (or smooth) if every local ring $\mathcal{O}_{X,x}$ is a regular local ring. This extends the definition from Chapter I for quasi-projective varieties to abstract varieties. Nonsingularity is equivalent to the sheaf of differentials $\Omega_{X/k}$ being locally free of rank equal to the dimension of $X$, making differentials a powerful tool for detecting smoothness.
