The module of relative differentials $\Omega_{B/A}$ can be constructed explicitly using the diagonal homomorphism $f: B \otimes_A B \to B$. The kernel $I = \ker f$ encodes infinitesimal information, and the quotient $I/I^2$ — with the universal derivation $d: B \to I/I^2$ sending $b \mapsto 1 \otimes b - b \otimes 1$ — satisfies the universal property of Kähler differentials. This construction shows that $\Omega_{B/A}$ is generated as a $B$-module by the set $\{db \mid b \in B\}$ and provides a concrete model for proving structural properties such as base change and exact sequences.
