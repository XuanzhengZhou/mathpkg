Since $\dim_k M \otimes k = r$, Nakayama's lemma tells us that $M$ can be generated by $r$ elements. So there is a surjective map $\varphi: A^r \to M \to 0$. Let $R$ be its kernel. Then tensoring with the quotient field $K$ we obtain an exact sequence

$$0 \to R \otimes_A K \to K^r \to M \otimes_A K \to 0.$$

Since $\dim_K M \otimes_A K = \dim_k M \otimes_A k = r$ (because $M$ is torsion-free, the rank is constant), we have $R \otimes_A K = 0$. But $R$ is a submodule of $A^r$, hence torsion-free. Therefore $R = 0$, and $\varphi$ is an isomorphism $M \cong A^r$.
