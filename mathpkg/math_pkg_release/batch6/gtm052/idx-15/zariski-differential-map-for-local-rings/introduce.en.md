For a local ring $B$ containing a field $k$ isomorphic to its residue field, the canonical map $\delta: \mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2 \to \Omega_{B/k} \otimes_B k$ from the Zariski cotangent space to the module of differentials is an isomorphism. This result identifies the Zariski tangent space $\operatorname{Hom}_k(\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2, k)$ with the space of $k$-derivations $\operatorname{Der}_k(B,k)$, establishing a fundamental link between differentials and tangent spaces in algebraic geometry.
