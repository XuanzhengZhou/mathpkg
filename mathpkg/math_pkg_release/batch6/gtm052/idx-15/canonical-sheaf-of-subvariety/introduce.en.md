The adjunction formula relates the canonical sheaf of a nonsingular subvariety $Y \subset X$ to that of the ambient variety: $\omega_Y \cong \omega_X \otimes \bigwedge^r \mathcal{N}_{Y/X}$. This follows from the exact sequence $0 \to \mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2 \to \Omega_X \otimes \mathcal{O}_Y \to \Omega_Y \to 0$ by taking determinants. For hypersurfaces in projective space, this yields the well-known formula $\omega_Y \cong \mathcal{O}_Y(d - n - 1)$ where $d$ is the degree and $n$ the dimension of the ambient projective space.
