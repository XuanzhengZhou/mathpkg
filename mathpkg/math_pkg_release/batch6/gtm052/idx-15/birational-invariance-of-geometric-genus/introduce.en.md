The geometric genus $p_g$ is a birational invariant of nonsingular projective varieties — birationally equivalent varieties have equal geometric genus. The proof uses the rational map $f: V \to X'$ defined on an open set $V \subset X$ and the pullback map on differentials $f^* \Omega_{X'/k} \to \Omega_{V/k}$, which induces a map on canonical sheaves. Since the complement of $V$ has codimension at least 2 and $X$ is normal, global sections extend, yielding $p_g(X) \geq p_g(X')$. Symmetry gives equality.
