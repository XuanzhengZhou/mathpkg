This theorem links the vector space dimension of the module of differentials $\Omega_{K/k}$ with the transcendence degree of a finitely generated field extension. The inequality $\dim_K \Omega_{K/k} \geq \operatorname{tr.deg.} K/k$ always holds, and equality is equivalent to $K$ being separably generated over $k$. A key corollary: for a finite algebraic extension, $\Omega_{K/k} = 0$ precisely when the extension is separable, making differentials a powerful tool for detecting separability.
