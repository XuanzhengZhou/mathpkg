The sheaf of relative differentials $\Omega_{X/Y}$ globalizes the algebraic notion of Kähler differentials to schemes. For a morphism $f: X \to Y$, it is defined via the diagonal $\Delta: X \to X \times_Y X$ as the pullback $\Delta^*(\mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2)$, where $\mathcal{I}$ is the ideal sheaf of the diagonal. This construction yields a quasi-coherent $\mathcal{O}_X$-module (coherent when $Y$ is noetherian and $f$ is of finite type) that locally recovers the module of relative differentials of the corresponding ring homomorphisms.
