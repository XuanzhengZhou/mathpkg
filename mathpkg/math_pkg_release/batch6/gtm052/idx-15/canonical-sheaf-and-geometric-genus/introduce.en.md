The canonical sheaf $\omega_X = \bigwedge^n \Omega_{X/k}$ is an invertible sheaf intrinsically defined from the sheaf of differentials. For a projective nonsingular variety, its space of global sections has finite dimension $p_g = \dim_k \Gamma(X, \omega_X)$, called the geometric genus. This invariant is birational (Theorem 8.19), making it a crucial tool in the classification of algebraic varieties. For curves, $p_g$ coincides with the arithmetic genus (by Serre duality); for higher-dimensional varieties they may differ.
