Let $X$ be a nonsingular variety over $k$ of dimension $n$. The **canonical sheaf** of $X$ is

$$\omega_X = \bigwedge^n \Omega_{X/k},$$

the $n$-th exterior power of the sheaf of differentials. It is an invertible sheaf on $X$.

If $X$ is projective and nonsingular, the **geometric genus** of $X$ is

$$p_g = \dim_k \Gamma(X, \omega_X),$$

a nonnegative integer. The geometric genus is a birational invariant of nonsingular projective varieties.
