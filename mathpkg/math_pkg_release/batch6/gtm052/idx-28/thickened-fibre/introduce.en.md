The thickened fibre (or formal neighbourhood) is a fundamental construction used to probe the infinitesimal structure of a morphism $f: X \to Y$ near a point $y \in Y$. By pulling back along the nilpotent thickening $\operatorname{Spec} \mathcal{O}_y / \mathfrak{m}_y^n \to Y$, one obtains schemes $X_n$ that share the same underlying space as the fibre $X_y$ but carry additional nilpotent structure capturing higher-order information. These $X_n$ form an inverse system whose limit relates the formal completion of higher direct images $R^i f_* \mathcal{F}$ to cohomology groups on the infinitesimal neighbourhoods.
