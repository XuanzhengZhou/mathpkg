Let $f: X \to Y$ be a morphism of schemes, let $y \in Y$, and let $\mathcal{O}_y$ be the local ring of $y$ on $Y$ with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}_y$. For each integer $n \ge 1$, define
$$X_n = X \times_Y \operatorname{Spec} \mathcal{O}_y / \mathfrak{m}_y^n.$$
For $n = 1$, $X_1 = X_y$ is the fibre over $y$. For $n > 1$, $X_n$ is a scheme with nilpotent elements having the same underlying topological space as $X_y$. It is a "thickened fibre" (infinitesimal neighbourhood) of $X$ over $y$.

Let $v: X_n \to X$ be the natural projection and define $\mathcal{F}_n = v^*\mathcal{F}$ for any sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$. As $n$ varies, the schemes $X_n$ and the sheaves $\mathcal{F}_n$ form inverse systems.
