A \textbf{geometrically ruled surface}, or simply \textbf{ruled surface}, is a surface $X$, together with a surjective morphism $\pi: X \rightarrow C$ to a nonsingular curve $C$, such that the fibre $X_y$ is isomorphic to $\mathbf{P}^1$ for every point $y \in C$, and such that $\pi$ admits a section (i.e., a morphism $\sigma: C \rightarrow X$ such that $\pi \circ \sigma = \text{id}_C$).

\textit{Note:} In fact, one can show using Tsen's theorem that the existence of a section is a consequence of the other provisions of the definition.
