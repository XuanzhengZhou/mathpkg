Clearly $C_0.f = 1$, because $C_0$ and $f$ meet at only one point, and are transversal there. We have $f^2 = 0$ because two distinct fibres don't meet.

Now if $D \in \operatorname{Pic} X$, let $n = D.f$, and let $D' = D - nC_0$. Then $D'.f = 0$. Therefore by (2.1), $\pi_*(\mathcal{L}(D'))$ is an invertible sheaf on $C$, and clearly $\mathcal{L}(D') \cong \pi^*\pi_*(\mathcal{L}(D'))$. Since $\pi^*: \operatorname{Pic} C \rightarrow \operatorname{Pic} X$ is clearly injective, we see that $\operatorname{Pic} X \cong \mathbf{Z} \oplus \pi^*\operatorname{Pic} C$. The generator of the $\mathbf{Z}$ summand is $C_0$.

For the numerical equivalence group: since any two fibres are numerically equivalent, $\operatorname{Num} X \cong \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z}$, generated by $C_0$ and $f$. See also (II, Ex. 7.9) and (III, Ex. 12.5).
