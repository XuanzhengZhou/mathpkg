We have $\deg K_C = 2g - 2$ (IV, 1.3.3) and $\deg \mathfrak{e} = -e$. The first formula follows from Proposition 2.10 by replacing linear equivalence with numerical equivalence, noting that $\deg K_C = 2g - 2$.

For $K^2$, we compute using (2.3) and (2.9):

$$K^2 = (-2C_0 + (2g - 2 - e)f)^2.$$

Using the intersection numbers $C_0^2 = -e$, $C_0.f = 1$, $f^2 = 0$, we get:

$$K^2 = 4C_0^2 - 4(2g - 2 - e)C_0.f + (2g - 2 - e)^2 f^2$$
$$= 4(-e) - 4(2g - 2 - e) \cdot 1 + 0$$
$$= -4e - 8g + 8 + 4e$$
$$= 8(1 - g).$$
