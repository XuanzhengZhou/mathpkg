Passing to numerical equivalence, the canonical divisor of a ruled surface satisfies $K \equiv -2C_0 + (2g - 2 - e)f$. Crucially, the self-intersection $K^2 = 8(1 - g)$ depends only on the genus $g$ of the base curve $C$, and is independent of the invariant $e$. This means all ruled surfaces over a fixed curve $C$ have the same $K^2$.
