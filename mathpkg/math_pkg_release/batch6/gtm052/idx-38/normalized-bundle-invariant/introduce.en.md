Every ruled surface $X$ can be written as $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ with a "normalized" locally free sheaf $\mathcal{E}$ of rank $2$ on $C$. The normalization condition makes $\mathcal{E}$ as negative as possible while retaining a global section. The integer $e = -\deg \mathcal{E}$ is then an invariant of the ruled surface, independent of the choice of normalized $\mathcal{E}$. The distinguished section $C_0$ corresponds to $\mathcal{O}_{\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})}(1)$.
