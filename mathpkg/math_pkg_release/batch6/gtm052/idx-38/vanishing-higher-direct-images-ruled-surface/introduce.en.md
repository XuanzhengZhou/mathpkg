If $D$ is a divisor on a ruled surface $X \to C$ with nonnegative intersection against a fibre, then all higher direct images $R^i\pi_*\mathcal{L}(D)$ vanish for $i > 0$. Consequently, the cohomology of $\mathcal{L}(D)$ on $X$ coincides with the cohomology of its direct image on the base curve $C$. This simplifies cohomological computations on ruled surfaces dramatically.
