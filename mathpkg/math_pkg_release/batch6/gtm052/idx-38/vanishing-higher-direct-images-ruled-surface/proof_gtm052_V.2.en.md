Since $\mathcal{L}(D)_y$ is an invertible sheaf of degree $D.f \geq 0$ on $X_y \cong \mathbf{P}^1$, we have $H^i(X_y,\mathcal{L}(D)_y) = 0$ for all $i > 0$. Therefore $R^i\pi_*\mathcal{L}(D) = 0$ for $i > 0$, by the theorem on cohomology and base change (III, Ex. 11.8) or (III, 12.9). The second statement follows from the Leray spectral sequence (III, Ex. 8.1) or directly from (III, 12.9), since the vanishing of higher direct images implies that the natural map $H^i(C, \pi_*\mathcal{L}(D)) \to H^i(X, \mathcal{L}(D))$ is an isomorphism for all $i$.
