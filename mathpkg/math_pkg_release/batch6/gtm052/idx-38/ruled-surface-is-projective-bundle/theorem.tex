If $\pi: X \rightarrow C$ is a ruled surface, then there exists a locally free sheaf $\mathcal{E}$ of rank $2$ on $C$ such that $X \cong \mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ over $C$. Conversely, every such $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ is a ruled surface over $C$. If $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{E}'$ are two locally free sheaves of rank $2$ on $C$, then $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ and $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E}')$ are isomorphic as ruled surfaces over $C$ if and only if there is an invertible sheaf $\mathcal{L}$ on $C$ such that $\mathcal{E}' \cong \mathcal{E} \otimes \mathcal{L}$.
