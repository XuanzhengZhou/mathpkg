Every geometrically ruled surface $X$ over a curve $C$ is isomorphic to the projectivization $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ of a rank-$2$ locally free sheaf $\mathcal{E}$ on $C$. The sheaf $\mathcal{E}$ is not unique: twisting by an invertible sheaf $\mathcal{L}$ on $C$ yields the same ruled surface. Conversely, any $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ with $\mathcal{E}$ locally free of rank $2$ on a curve $C$ is automatically a ruled surface, so the two notions coincide.
