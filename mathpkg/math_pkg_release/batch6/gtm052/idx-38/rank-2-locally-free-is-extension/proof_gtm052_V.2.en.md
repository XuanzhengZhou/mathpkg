Since $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ has a section by Proposition 2.2, we get an exact sequence $0 \rightarrow \mathcal{N} \rightarrow \mathcal{E} \rightarrow \mathcal{L} \rightarrow 0$ where $\mathcal{N}$ and $\mathcal{L}$ are invertible sheaves. This also follows from (II, Ex. 8.2).

More explicitly: Let $\sigma: C \to \mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ be a section. Then $\sigma$ corresponds to a surjection $\mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{L} \to 0$ for some invertible sheaf $\mathcal{L}$ on $C$, by (II, 7.12). Let $\mathcal{N}$ be the kernel of this surjection. Then $\mathcal{N}$ is locally free since it is a subsheaf of a locally free sheaf over a nonsingular curve, and its rank is $1$ by additivity of rank in exact sequences.
