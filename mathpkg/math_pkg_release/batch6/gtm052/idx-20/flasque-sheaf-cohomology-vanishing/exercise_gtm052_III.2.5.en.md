Let $X$ be a Zariski space (II, Ex. 3.17). Let $P \in X$ be a closed point, and let $X_P$ be the subset of $X$ consisting of all points $Q \in X$ such that $P \in \overline{\{Q\}}$. We call $X_P$ the local space of $X$ at $P$, and give it the induced topology. Let $j: X_P \rightarrow X$ be the inclusion, and for any sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$, let $\mathcal{F}_P = j^*\mathcal{F}$. Show that for all $i$, and all $\mathcal{F}$, we have

$$H_P^i(X, \mathcal{F}) = H_P^i(X_P, \mathcal{F}_P).$$
