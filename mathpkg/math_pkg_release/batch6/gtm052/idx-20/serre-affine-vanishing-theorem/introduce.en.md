Serre's vanishing theorem is a cornerstone of algebraic geometry. It states that for an affine scheme $X = \operatorname{Spec} A$ with $A$ noetherian, the higher cohomology groups $H^i(X, \mathcal{F})$ vanish for all $i > 0$ and all quasi-coherent sheaves $\mathcal{F}$. The proof first shows that the sheaf associated to an injective $A$-module is flasque on $\operatorname{Spec} A$, using Lemma 3.3 on the surjectivity of localization maps. Then for any quasi-coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F} = \tilde{M}$, taking an injective resolution of $M$ and applying the associated sheaf functor yields a flasque resolution of $\mathcal{F}$, from which the vanishing follows. This theorem is essential because it implies that Cech cohomology with respect to an affine open cover computes the correct derived functor cohomology for separated noetherian schemes.
