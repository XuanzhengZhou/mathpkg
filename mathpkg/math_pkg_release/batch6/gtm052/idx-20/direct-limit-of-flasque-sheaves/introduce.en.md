Flasque sheaves are characterized by the property that all restriction maps are surjective. On a noetherian topological space, this property is stable under direct limits. Lemma 2.8 of Hartshorne uses the exactness of the direct limit functor together with the fact that on a noetherian space, the direct limit of sections over an open set equals the sections of the direct limit sheaf. This lemma is a key ingredient for proving that sheaf cohomology commutes with direct limits.
