A fundamental compatibility result in sheaf cohomology is that on a noetherian topological space, cohomology commutes with direct limits. Proposition 2.9 of Hartshorne establishes natural isomorphisms between the direct limit of cohomology groups and the cohomology of the direct limit sheaf. The proof constructs a natural transformation of $\delta$-functors using the category of directed systems and relies on the fact that both sides are effaceable (hence universal), together with the result that direct limits of flasque sheaves remain flasque on noetherian spaces.
