Given a closed subset $Y$ of a topological space $X$ with inclusion $j: Y \hookrightarrow X$, the pushforward sheaf $j_*\mathcal{F}$ extends a sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $Y$ to all of $X$. Lemma 2.10 of Hartshorne asserts that the cohomology groups are unchanged by this operation: $H^i(Y, \mathcal{F}) \cong H^i(X, j_*\mathcal{F})$. The proof uses flasque resolutions and the fact that the direct image of a flasque sheaf under a closed immersion remains flasque. This lemma justifies the common practice of writing $\mathcal{F}$ in place of $j_*\mathcal{F}$ when computing cohomology.
