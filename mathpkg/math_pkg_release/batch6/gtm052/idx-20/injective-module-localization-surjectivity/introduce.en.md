For an injective module $I$ over a noetherian ring $A$, localization with respect to any element $f \in A$ yields a surjective map $I \to I_f$. Lemma 3.3 of Hartshorne is a crucial technical step in the proof that the sheaf $\tilde{I}$ on $\operatorname{Spec} A$ is flasque. The proof uses the ascending chain of annihilators of powers of $f$, which must stabilize by the noetherian property, to construct preimages of elements in $I_f$ from $I$.
