An affine variety is the fundamental geometric object of classical algebraic geometry: an irreducible closed subset of affine $n$-space equipped with the Zariski topology. The irreducibility condition ensures the variety is not the union of two proper closed subsets, which corresponds algebraically to the coordinate ring being an integral domain. Together with quasi-affine varieties (open subsets of affine varieties), these form the first class of spaces studied in the book.
