Proposition 1.5 establishes that in a noetherian topological space, every closed subset admits a unique decomposition into finitely many maximal irreducible closed subsets, called its irreducible components. Applied to algebraic sets in $\mathbf{A}^n$, this gives a canonical decomposition of any algebraic set into a finite union of affine varieties. This is the geometric counterpart of primary decomposition in commutative algebra.
