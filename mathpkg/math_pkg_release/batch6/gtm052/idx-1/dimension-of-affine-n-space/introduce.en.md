Proposition 1.9 confirms that the algebraic definition of dimension (via Krull dimension of the coordinate ring) yields the expected answer for affine $n$-space: $\dim \mathbf{A}^n = n$. This follows from Theorem 1.8A, since the coordinate ring $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ has transcendence degree $n$ over $k$. It is a reassuring sanity check that the abstract notion of dimension aligns with geometric intuition.
