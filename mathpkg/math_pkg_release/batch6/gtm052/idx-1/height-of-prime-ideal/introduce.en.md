The height (or codimension) of a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ measures the maximum length of a chain of distinct prime ideals ending at $\mathfrak{p}$. This is the algebraic counterpart of the codimension of an irreducible subvariety. In algebraic geometry, the height of the prime ideal corresponding to a subvariety equals its codimension. The dimension (Krull dimension) of the ring is the supremum of heights of all its prime ideals.
