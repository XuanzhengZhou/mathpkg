Hilbert's Nullstellensatz (Theorem 1.3A) is the cornerstone of algebraic geometry over algebraically closed fields. It states that if a polynomial $f$ vanishes on the zero set of an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$, then some power of $f$ lies in $\mathfrak{a}$. Equivalently, the ideal of the zero set of $\mathfrak{a}$ is the radical $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$. This establishes the fundamental correspondence between algebraic sets and radical ideals, and between irreducible algebraic sets and prime ideals.
