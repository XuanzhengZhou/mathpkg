A quasi-affine variety is an open subset of an affine variety, endowed with the induced Zariski topology. These varieties generalize affine varieties and appear naturally when studying local properties. For example, the complement of a hypersurface in an affine variety is a quasi-affine variety. Together with affine varieties, they constitute the class of objects studied in the first chapter of Hartshorne.
