A noetherian topological space satisfies the descending chain condition on closed subsets, meaning there are no infinite strictly descending chains. Affine space $\mathbf{A}^n$ with the Zariski topology is noetherian because $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is a noetherian ring. This property ensures that every closed subset can be uniquely expressed as a finite union of irreducible closed subsets (its irreducible components), which is fundamental for decomposing algebraic sets into varieties.
