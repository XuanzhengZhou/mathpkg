Affine $n$-space $\mathbf{A}^n$ is the fundamental ambient space for affine algebraic geometry. It consists of all $n$-tuples of elements from a fixed algebraically closed field $k$. The polynomial ring $A = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ can be interpreted as the ring of functions on $\mathbf{A}^n$, with each polynomial $f \in A$ defining a function $f: \mathbf{A}^n \to k$ by evaluation. This allows us to study geometric objects as zero sets of polynomials.
