Proposition 1.2 summarizes the fundamental properties of the two operations $Z$ (zero set) and $I$ (ideal of a subset). Together they form an order-reversing Galois connection between subsets of $\mathbf{A}^n$ and ideals of $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$. The key facts are that $Z(I(Y))$ recovers the Zariski closure of $Y$, and $I(Z(\mathfrak{a}))$ recovers the radical of the ideal $\mathfrak{a}$, which is the content of Hilbert's Nullstellensatz (Theorem 1.3A).
