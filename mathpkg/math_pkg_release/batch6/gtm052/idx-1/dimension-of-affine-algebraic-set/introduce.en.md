Proposition 1.7 establishes the fundamental link between geometry and algebra: the geometric dimension of an affine algebraic set (the maximal length of a chain of irreducible closed subsets) equals the Krull dimension of its affine coordinate ring. This allows the powerful dimension theory of noetherian rings from commutative algebra to be applied directly to algebraic varieties, reducing geometric questions to algebraic computations.
