Corollary 1.4 of Hilbert's Nullstellensatz establishes the fundamental dictionary of algebraic geometry: there is an inclusion-reversing bijection between algebraic sets in $\mathbf{A}^n$ and radical ideals of the polynomial ring $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$. Moreover, irreducible algebraic sets correspond to prime ideals, connecting the geometric notion of irreducibility to the algebraic notion of primality. This correspondence reduces geometric questions about varieties to algebraic questions about ideals.
