The Krull dimension of a ring is the supremum of the heights (lengths of maximal chains of prime ideals) of all its prime ideals. For affine coordinate rings of algebraic varieties, this algebraic dimension coincides with the geometric dimension (the length of the longest chain of irreducible closed subsets). This connection, formalized in Proposition 1.7, allows dimension theory from commutative algebra to be applied to algebraic geometry.
