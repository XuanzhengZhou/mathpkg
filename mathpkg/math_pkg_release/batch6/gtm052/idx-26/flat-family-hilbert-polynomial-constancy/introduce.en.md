Theorem 9.9 is the fundamental result on the constancy of the Hilbert polynomial in flat projective families. It establishes the equivalence of three key conditions: freeness of the module of global sections, constancy of the dimension of cohomology of the fibres, and invariance of the Hilbert polynomial across the family. This theorem is the essential technical tool needed to prove the properness of connected components of the Hilbert scheme via the valuative criterion.
