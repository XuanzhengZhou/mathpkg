This definition formalizes what it means for a family of varieties to be "well-behaved" over the parameter space. The two conditions ensure that each scheme-theoretic fibre coincides with the reduced induced structure on the set-theoretic fibre. Condition (1) guarantees that all fibres have the expected dimension, while condition (2) is a local regularity condition at the generic point of each fibre. Families satisfying these conditions are automatically flat by Theorem 9.7, making this a geometric characterisation of flatness.
