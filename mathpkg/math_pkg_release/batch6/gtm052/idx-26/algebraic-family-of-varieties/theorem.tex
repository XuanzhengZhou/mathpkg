Let $k$ be an algebraically closed field, let $f: X \to T$ be a surjective map of varieties over $k$, and assume that for each closed point $t \in T$, we have

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(1)] $f^{-1}(t)$ is irreducible of dimension equal to $\dim X - \dim T$, and
  \item[(2)] if $\mathfrak{m}_t \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{t,T}$ is the maximal ideal, and if $\zeta \in f^{-1}(t)$ is the generic point, then $f^\# \mathfrak{m}_t$ generates the maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}_\zeta \subseteq \mathcal{O}_{\zeta,X}$.
\end{enumerate}

Under these circumstances, we let $X_{(t)}$ be the variety $f^{-1}(t)$ (with the reduced induced structure) and we say that the varieties $\{X_{(t)} \mid t \in T\}$ form an algebraic family of varieties parametrized by $T$.
