Proof. By base extension to the irreducible components of $T$ with their reduced induced structure, we reduce to the case $T$ integral. Now the result follows from Theorem 9.9 and the facts (I, §7) and (Ex. 5.3) that

$$\dim X_t = \deg P_t,$$
$$\deg X_t = (r!) \cdot (\text{leading coefficient of } P_t),$$

where $r = \dim X$, and

$$p_a(X_t) = (-1)^r(P_t(0) - 1).$$

Since Theorem 9.9 guarantees that the Hilbert polynomial $P_t$ is independent of $t$, all three quantities derived from $P_t$ are also independent of $t$.
