Castelnuovo's criterion (or Castelnuovo's contraction theorem) is a foundational result in the birational geometry of algebraic surfaces. It provides the converse to the observation that the exceptional divisor of a monoidal transformation (blow-up at a point) is a smooth rational curve with self-intersection \(-1\). Castelnuovo proved that every such \((-1)\)-curve can be smoothly contracted: there exists a morphism to a nonsingular projective surface that acts as the inverse of blowing up, contracting the curve to a point and leaving the rest of the surface unchanged. The theorem is a cornerstone of the classification theory of surfaces, as it enables the systematic removal of exceptional curves to obtain minimal models.
