Let $K_\infty / K$ be a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. We say that $K_\infty / K$ satisfies **condition IW** (Iwasawa's condition) if $K_\infty$ is totally ramified over $K$ at a finite non-empty set of prime ideals in $K$, and unramified at all other prime ideals of $K$.

Equivalently, there exists a non-empty finite set $S$ of primes of $K$ such that:
- Every prime $\mathfrak{p} \in S$ is totally ramified in $K_\infty / K$ (its ramification index is infinite).
- Every prime $\mathfrak{q} \notin S$ is unramified in $K_\infty / K$.

The number $s = |S|$ is the number of ramified primes. In many applications, one studies the case $s = 1$ (a single totally ramified prime).
