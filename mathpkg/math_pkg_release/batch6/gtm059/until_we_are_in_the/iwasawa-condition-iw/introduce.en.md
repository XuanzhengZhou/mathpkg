Iwasawa's condition (IW) is a simplifying hypothesis on the ramification in a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. It requires that the extension be totally ramified at only finitely many primes (typically one) and unramified everywhere else. While not all $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions satisfy this condition, the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension does (with $s = 1$ when the base field is $\mathbb{Q}$), and many key theorems in Iwasawa theory are first proved under condition IW before being extended to the general case.
