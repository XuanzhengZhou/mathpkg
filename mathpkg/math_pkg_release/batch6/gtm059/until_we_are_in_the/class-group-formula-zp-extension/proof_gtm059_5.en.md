By the Iwasawa structure theorem (Theorem 3.1), $X$ is pseudo-isomorphic to a direct sum

$$
X \sim \bigoplus_{i=1}^r \Lambda/(f_i) \oplus \bigoplus_{j=1}^t \Lambda/(p^{\mu_j})
$$

where each $f_i$ is a distinguished polynomial. Define the characteristic ideal of $X$ as the product $\prod f_i \cdot \prod p^{\mu_j}$, and let $f_X(T) = \prod f_i(T)$ be the characteristic polynomial.

By class field theory, for each $n$ we have an isomorphism $A_n \cong X / (\gamma_n - 1)X$ where $\gamma_n$ is a topological generator of $\Gamma^{p^n}$. Computing the order of this quotient using the structure theorem and the Weierstrass preparation theorem yields the asymptotic formula.

Specifically, for each cyclic summand $\Lambda/(f)$ with $f$ a distinguished polynomial of degree $\lambda$, the order of $\Lambda/(f, \gamma_n-1)$ grows as $p^{\lambda n}$. For a summand $\Lambda/(p^\mu)$, the order grows as $p^{\mu p^n}$. Summing these contributions gives the formula with $\lambda = \sum \deg f_i$ and $\mu = \sum \mu_j$.

The bound on the number of generators by $s$ follows from Nakayama's lemma applied to the $\Lambda$-module $X$: since $X/(\gamma-1)X$ is a quotient of $A_0$, which has at most $s$ generators as an abelian group (one per ramified prime), $X$ is generated by at most $s$ elements over $\Lambda$.
