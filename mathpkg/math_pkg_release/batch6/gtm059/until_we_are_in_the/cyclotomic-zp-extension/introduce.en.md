The cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension is the fundamental example of a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. It is obtained by adjoining all $p$-power roots of unity to $\mathbb{Q}$ (or to a general number field $K$). For $p$ odd, the Galois group $\operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty})/\mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ contains a finite torsion subgroup, and the fixed field of this torsion subgroup yields the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. Every number field admits at least one $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension via this cyclotomic construction.
