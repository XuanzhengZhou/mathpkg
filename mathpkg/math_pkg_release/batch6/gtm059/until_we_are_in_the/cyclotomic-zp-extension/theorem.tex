Let $p$ be a prime number. The cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ is

$$
\mathbb{Q}_\infty = \mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty}) = \bigcup_{n \geq 1} \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{p^n})
$$

where $\zeta_{p^n}$ is a primitive $p^n$-th root of unity (or a primitive $2^{n+2}$-th root of unity when $p = 2$, using $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{2^{n+2}})$ for $n \geq 0$). The Galois group is

$$
\operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}_\infty / \mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p^\times \cong \mathbb{Z}_p \times (\text{finite cyclic group})
$$

and the fixed field of the torsion subgroup of $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ is the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension (the maximal $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension contained in $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty})$).

More generally, let $K$ be any number field. The extension

$$
K_\infty = K(\mu_{p^\infty})
$$

obtained by adjoining all $p$-power roots of unity is abelian over $K$, and its maximal $\mathbb{Z}_p$-quotient gives a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of $K$ called the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of $K$.
