Let $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ be the Iwasawa algebra. Let $M$ be a finitely generated $\Lambda$-module. Then $M$ is pseudo-isomorphic to a direct sum

$$
M \sim \bigoplus_{i=1}^{r} \Lambda / (f_i) \oplus \bigoplus_{j=1}^{s} \Lambda / (p^{\mu_j})
$$

where each $f_i \in \mathbb{Z}_p[X]$ is a distinguished polynomial and each $\mu_j \geq 1$ is an integer. The elementary module $\Lambda/(f_i)$ corresponds to the case where $f_i \neq 0$, and the factors $\Lambda/(p^{\mu_j})$ are torsion modules of $p$-power order. If $f, g$ are distinguished and relatively prime, the natural map

$$
\Lambda/(fg) \to \Lambda/(f) \oplus \Lambda/(g)
$$

is a pseudo-isomorphism with finite kernel and cokernel, allowing further decomposition into factors where each $f_i$ is a power of an irreducible distinguished polynomial.
