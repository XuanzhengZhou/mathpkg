The Iwasawa structure theorem in module form classifies all finitely generated modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ up to pseudo-isomorphism. Every such module is pseudo-isomorphic to a direct sum of cyclic modules $\Lambda/(f_i)$ where the $f_i$ are distinguished polynomials (or powers of $p$). This is the $\Lambda$-adic analogue of the structure theorem for finitely generated modules over a principal ideal domain, and it is fundamental to Iwasawa's study of class groups in $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions.
