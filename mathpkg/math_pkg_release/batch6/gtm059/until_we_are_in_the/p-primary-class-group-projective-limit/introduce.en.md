The central object of Iwasawa theory is the projective limit $X = \varprojlim A_n$ of the $p$-primary parts of the ideal class groups in the tower of a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. This is a module over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]]$, and the Iwasawa structure theorem classifies it up to pseudo-isomorphism. The growth of $\#A_n$ as $n \to \infty$ is governed by the characteristic polynomial of $X$, leading to Iwasawa's famous asymptotic formula for class numbers.
