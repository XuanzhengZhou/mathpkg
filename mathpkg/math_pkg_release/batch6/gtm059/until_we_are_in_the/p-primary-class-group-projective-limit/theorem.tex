Let $K_\infty / K$ be a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension with intermediate fields $K = K_0 \subset K_1 \subset \cdots \subset K_n \subset \cdots$. For each $n \geq 0$, let $A_n$ be the $p$-primary part of the ideal class group of $K_n$:

$$
A_n = \operatorname{Cl}(K_n)[p^\infty].
$$

Let $C_n = A_n$ (or sometimes the $p$-Sylow subgroup of the class group). The norm maps $N_{K_{n+1}/K_n} : A_{n+1} \to A_n$ form a projective (inverse) system. Define

$$
X = \varprojlim_n A_n
$$

as the projective limit with respect to the norm maps. This is a compact $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]]$-module where $\Gamma = \operatorname{Gal}(K_\infty/K) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$.

There is a canonical isomorphism via class field theory:

$$
A_n \cong \operatorname{Gal}(L_n / K_n)
$$

where $L_n$ is the maximal unramified abelian $p$-extension of $K_n$ (the $p$-Hilbert class field of $K_n$). Furthermore, letting $M_n$ be the maximal abelian $p$-extension of $K_n$ unramified outside the ramified primes, the projective limit

$$
Y = \varprojlim_n \operatorname{Gal}(M_n / K_n)
$$

plays a central role in Iwasawa theory.
