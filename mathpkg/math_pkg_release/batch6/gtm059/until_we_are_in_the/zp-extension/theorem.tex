Let $K$ be a number field. A Galois extension $K_\infty$ of $K$ is called a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension if its Galois group is isomorphic to the additive group of $p$-adic integers:

$$
\operatorname{Gal}(K_\infty / K) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p.
$$

Equivalently, to give such an extension is to give a tower of fields

$$
K = K_0 \subset K_1 \subset K_2 \subset \cdots \subset K_n \subset \cdots \subset K_\infty = \bigcup_{n \geq 0} K_n
$$

such that for each $n \geq 1$, the extension $K_n / K$ is cyclic of degree $p^n$, and $\operatorname{Gal}(K_\infty/K) = \varprojlim \operatorname{Gal}(K_n/K) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$.
