Let $x = \sum \theta(a)\sigma_a \in R$ be an element of the Stickelberger ideal, with $\theta(a) \in \mathbb{Z}$ and only finitely many nonzero coefficients. Then $\sum \theta(a) = 0 \bmod m$.

By Theorem 2.2 we have the factorization
$$\prod \theta(a)^{\sigma_a^{-1}} = \psi^{\mathfrak{p}}$$
and it is immediately verified that the left-hand side lies in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$ by using the Gauss sum properties of the preceding section. The product on the left is a principal ideal, and the condition $\sum \theta(a) \equiv 0 \pmod{m}$ ensures that the exponent is an integer. Thus any element of the Stickelberger ideal factors as a principal ideal, proving that it annihilates the ideal class group.
