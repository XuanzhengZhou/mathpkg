The Stickelberger ideal annihilates the ideal class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$.

More precisely, let $R = \mathbb{Z}[G]$ where $G = \operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)/\mathbb{Q})$, and let $I$ be the Stickelberger ideal. Then for any element $x = \sum \theta(a)\sigma_a \in I$ and any ideal class $[\mathfrak{a}]$ in the class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$, we have $x \cdot [\mathfrak{a}] = 0$.
