In the setting of FAC 1, the group ring element $\theta(\mathfrak{n}) = \sigma_b$ has the $n$-th power factoring as

$$(b\sigma_b)^{n} = \mathfrak{p}^{\mathfrak{n}-1} + \mathfrak{p}^{\mathfrak{n}-2}$$

where the left-hand side lies in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$ and gives the ideal factorization of the $(b\sigma_b)^n$-power of the Gauss sum.
