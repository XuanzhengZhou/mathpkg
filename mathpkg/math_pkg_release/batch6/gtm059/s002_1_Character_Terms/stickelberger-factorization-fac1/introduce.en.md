A direct consequence of Theorem 2.2 giving the prime ideal factorization of Gauss sums for special characters. The factorization takes the particularly simple form $S_{\chi}(\mathfrak{n}) = \mathfrak{p}^{\mathfrak{n}-1}$, showing that the Gauss sum generates a power of the prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$. This yields an explicit principal ideal, demonstrating the Stickelberger element's action on the ideal class group in concrete cases.
