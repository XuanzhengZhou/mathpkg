We note that case (i) of Theorem 1.2 is unchanged; only the derivation in case (i) needs modification to account for the finite torsion submodule. In this case, $V$ is a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-free module tensored with a finite group, and an argument similar to that of Theorem 1.2 shows that

$$p^{e_n} = p^{b n} + v_n$$

for all $n \geq n_0$, where $v_n$ accounts for the finite part. Let $W = W_{n_0}$ denote the free part at level $n_0$. Then the $p$-adic order of $|V_n|$ satisfies

$$\operatorname{ord}_p(|V_n|) = \operatorname{ord}_p(|W|) + b(n - n_0) + c$$

for some constant $c$, since $W$ has the same $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank as $V$. The finite torsion contributes a bounded additive constant, which does not affect the asymptotic growth given by the Iwasawa invariants $\lambda$ and $\mu$. This proves the theorem.
