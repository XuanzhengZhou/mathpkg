Let $A$ be a complete local ring with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$. Let

$$f(X) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_k X^k \in A[[X]]$$

be a power series that is $\mathfrak{m}$-regular of order $n$, i.e., $a_0, \dots, a_{n-1} \in \mathfrak{m}$ and $a_n$ is a unit in $A$. Then $f$ can be written uniquely in the form

$$f(X) = (X^n + b_{n-1} X^{n-1} + \dots + b_0) \cdot u(X)$$

where $b_0, \dots, b_{n-1} \in \mathfrak{m}$ and $u(X)$ is a unit in $A[[X]]$ (i.e., $u(0)$ is a unit in $A$). The polynomial $P(X) = X^n + b_{n-1} X^{n-1} + \dots + b_0$ is called the Weierstrass polynomial (or distinguished polynomial) associated to $f$.
