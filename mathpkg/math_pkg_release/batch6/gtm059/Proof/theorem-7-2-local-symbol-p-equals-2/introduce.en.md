Theorem 7.2 handles the special case $p = 2$ for the local symbol in explicit reciprocity laws. The prime $2$ requires separate treatment because the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_2$-extension has different ramification behavior (the extension is wildly ramified). The theorem shows that the local symbol, defined via the trace of a logarithmic derivative expression, is well-defined modulo $\mathbb{Z}_2$ and relates the symbol of $x$ and $a$ to the symbol of $x$ and the logarithmic derivative $\lambda(a)$. This result is essential for completing the proof of the main explicit reciprocity law for all primes $p$.
