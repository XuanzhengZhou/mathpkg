Assume $p = 2$. Let $x \in K_n^{\times}$ and $a \in K_n^{\times}$, where $K_n = \mathbb{Q}_2(\zeta_{2^n})$ is the $2^n$-th cyclotomic extension of $\mathbb{Q}_2$. Then the local symbol

$$[x, a] = \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{Tr}_{K_n/\mathbb{Q}_2}\left( \lambda(a) \cdot \frac{d\log x}{d\pi} \right)$$

is well-defined modulo $\mathbb{Z}_2$ (i.e., takes values in $\frac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}_2/\mathbb{Z}_2$), lies in the conductor $c(x)$, and satisfies

$$[x, a] \equiv [x, \lambda(a)] \pmod{\mathbb{Z}_2}$$

where $\lambda$ denotes the logarithmic derivative map and $\frac{d\log x}{d\pi}$ is the logarithmic derivative with respect to a uniformizer $\pi$.
