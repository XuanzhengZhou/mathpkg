Let $K$ be a number field with maximal real subfield $K^+$. Then the quotient of Dedekind zeta functions admits a product decomposition
$$\frac{\zeta_K(s)}{\zeta_{K^+}(s)} = \prod_{\chi} L(s, \chi)$$
where the product is taken over all non-trivial primitive Dirichlet characters $\chi$ associated to the extension $K/K^+$. Moreover, the functional equation of $\zeta_K(s)$ factorizes accordingly, giving the following product expressions for the analytic class number formula:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\displaystyle \kappa_K = \frac{2^{r_1}(2\pi)^{r_2} h_K R_K}{w_K \sqrt{|d_K|}}$
\item[(ii)] If $K$ is totally real ($r_2 = 0$), then $\kappa_K = \prod_{\chi \text{ even}} L(0, \chi)$
\item[(iii)] If $K$ is totally imaginary, then $\kappa_K = \prod_{\chi \text{ odd}} L(0, \chi)$
\end{enumerate}
where $h_K$ is the class number, $R_K$ is the regulator, $w_K$ is the number of roots of unity in $K$, $d_K$ is the discriminant, and $r_1, r_2$ are the numbers of real and complex places.
