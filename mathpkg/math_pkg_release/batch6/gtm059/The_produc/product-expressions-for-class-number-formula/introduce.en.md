This theorem provides a product decomposition of the Dedekind zeta function of a number field $K$ in terms of $L$-series of Dirichlet characters associated to the abelian extension $K/\mathbb{Q}$. The factorization is obtained by expressing $\zeta_K(s)$ as a product of $L$-functions over all primitive characters induced by the characters of the Galois group. When combined with the functional equation of the zeta function, this yields explicit product expressions for the analytic class number formula, separating contributions from even and odd characters according to whether $K$ is totally real or totally imaginary. The result is fundamental for connecting the arithmetic invariants (class number, regulator, discriminant) to values of $L$-functions at $s = 0$.
