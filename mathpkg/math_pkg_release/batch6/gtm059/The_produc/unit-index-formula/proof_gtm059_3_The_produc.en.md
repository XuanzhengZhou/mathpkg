The proof follows from computing the regulator of the units in $K$ with respect to $K^+$. Consider the logarithmic embedding of the unit groups. By Dirichlet's unit theorem, the ranks of $E$ and $E^+$ are $r_1 + r_2 - 1$ and $r_1 - 1$ respectively (where $r_1$ is the number of real places of $K^+$, which equals the number of complex conjugate pairs in $K$).

The index $(E : \mu_K \cdot E^+)$ appears as a factor when comparing the volumes of the fundamental domains in the logarithmic space. Specifically, the regulator $R$ of $K$ is (up to a power of $2$) the volume of the lattice generated by the logarithms of a system of fundamental units in $K$, and similarly $R^+$ for $K^+$. The embedding $E^+ \hookrightarrow E$ induces an inclusion of the corresponding lattices, and the index of this inclusion is precisely the quotient of the covolumes multiplied by the appropriate power of $2$ accounting for the real embeddings.

More explicitly, computing the regulator determinant for $E$ with respect to the basis formed from $E^+$ together with a set of representatives of $E / \mu_K \cdot E^+$, each row of the determinant picks up a local factor of $2$ from the complex conjugation action. The resulting factor is $2^{r_2}$, and the index formula follows from comparing the two regulator volumes together with the class number factors that arise from the functional equation of the zeta function.
