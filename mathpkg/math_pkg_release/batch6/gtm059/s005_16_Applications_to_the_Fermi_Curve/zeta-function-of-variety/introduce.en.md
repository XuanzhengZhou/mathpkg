The zeta function $Z(V,T)$ of a variety over a finite field encodes the sequence of point counts $N_\nu$ over all finite extensions of the base field. Taking the logarithmic derivative recovers the counting function $\sum N_\nu T^{\nu-1}$. For the Fermat curve, the zeta function factorizes in terms of Jacobi sums and Hecke characters, providing an explicit realization of the Weil conjectures in this special case.
