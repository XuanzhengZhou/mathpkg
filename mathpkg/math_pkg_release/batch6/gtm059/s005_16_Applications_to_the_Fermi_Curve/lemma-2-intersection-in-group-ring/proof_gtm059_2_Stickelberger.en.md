The Stickelberger ideal $S$ is generated by elements of the form $\theta(a) = \sum_{\substack{(b,m)=1 \\ 1 \leq b < m}} \left\langle \frac{ab}{m} \right\rangle \sigma_b^{-1}$ where $\langle x \rangle$ denotes the fractional part. Applying complex conjugation $c \in G$ to such an element sends $\sigma_b^{-1}$ to $\sigma_{-b}^{-1}$, and using the property $\langle -x \rangle = 1 - \langle x \rangle$ for $x \notin \mathbf{Z}$, one finds that $c \cdot \theta(a) = -\theta(a)$ modulo certain relations. Hence $S \subset R^- = (1-e)R$, and the intersection relations follow.
