The Fermat curve $X^n + Y^n = Z^n$ is a central object in arithmetic geometry, intimately related to Fermat's Last Theorem. Its study via cyclotomic methods reveals deep connections between algebraic number theory and the arithmetic of algebraic curves. The zeta function of the Fermat curve can be expressed in terms of Jacobi sums and Hecke $L$-series, providing one of the earliest examples of the Weil conjectures.
