Diagonal equations are natural generalizations of the Fermat equation to higher dimension. The methods used to study the Fermat curve's zeta function -- Jacobi sums, Gauss sums, and Hecke $L$-series -- carry over directly to diagonal hypersurfaces. The composition of solutions in finite fields works in essentially the same way, with the zeta function factorizing according to the characters determined by the coefficients $a_i$.
