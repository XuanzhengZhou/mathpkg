This corollary converts the affine point count of Theorem 6.1 into a projective point count. The relation $\overline{N} = 1 + (q-1)N$ between projective and affine points follows from the fact that the Fermat curve is a plane curve: the projective completion adds points at infinity corresponding to solutions of the homogeneous equation $X^d + Y^d = Z^d$ with $Z = 0$. Combined with the Davenport-Hasse theorem (Theorem 5.1), this result yields the zeta function of the Fermat curve as a product of linear factors involving Jacobi sums.
