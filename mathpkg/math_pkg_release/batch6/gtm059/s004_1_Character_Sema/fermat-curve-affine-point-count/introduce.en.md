This theorem expresses the number of affine points on the Fermat curve $x^d + y^d = 1$ over a finite field $\mathbb{F}_q$ as a sum of Jacobi sums involving characters of order $d$. The assumption $d \mid (q-1)$ ensures that $\mathbb{F}_q$ contains the $d$-th roots of unity. The theorem is a classic application of character sums to algebraic geometry: the point count is reduced to an exponential sum that factors into Jacobi sums via the relation $u + v + w = 0$ for points on the curve. This result, combined with the Davenport-Hasse theorem (Theorem 5.1), yields the zeta function of the Fermat curve.
