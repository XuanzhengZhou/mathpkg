The proof proceeds by analyzing the sequence of groups and subgroups given by the inclusion relations shown in the following diagram:

$$\begin{array}{c c c c c}
R & \supset & R_1 & \supset & R_2 \\
\cup & & \cup & & \cup \\
R' & \supset & R'_1 & \supset & R'_2
\end{array}$$

The inclusion relations have been established, and the indices of successive subgroups are indicated on the right-hand side. The index $(R^* : \mathcal{P})$ is computed by multiplying the successive indices along the chain:

$$(R^* : \mathcal{P}) = \prod (R_i : R_{i+1}) \cdot \prod (R'_j : R'_{j+1}).$$

Using properties of the Stickelberger elements and the group ring structure, each factor simplifies to a determinant $R_{i,i}$, yielding the product formula.

The proof is due to [Wu 2] and was generalized to higher-order Stickelberger ideals where one must work with the module generated by Stickelberger elements rather than a single element.
