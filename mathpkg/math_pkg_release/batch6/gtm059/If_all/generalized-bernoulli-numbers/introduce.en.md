# Generalized Bernoulli Numbers (Leopoldt)

The generalized Bernoulli numbers $B_{k,f}$ extend the classical Bernoulli numbers to incorporate a twisting function $f$ (typically a Dirichlet character). When $f = \chi$ is a primitive Dirichlet character, these numbers interpolate the values of the Dirichlet $L$-function $L(s,\chi)$ at negative integers via $L(1-k, \chi) = -B_{k,\chi}/k$. In the context of cyclotomic fields, the generalized Bernoulli numbers appear in the Stickelberger ideal and in the formula for the minus part of the class number, making them a crucial link between analytic and algebraic number theory. They also admit an integral representation with respect to the Bernoulli distribution.
