Assume $u \in \ker(T)$. For the case $m = p^n$ a prime power, we consider the character $z$ on $R^n$ and its associated positive character $y$ with conductor $m$. By the properties of the distribution relation discussed in the next section, one verifies that replacing $m$ by $m+1$ still yields $y$ on $R^n$. Using the fact that $y(0) = R_n = 0$ for odd character $x$ (a consequence of the relation between $R_n$ and the Stickelberger elements, to be developed fully in Chapter 3), it follows that $u$ must lie in $R$, proving the claim.
