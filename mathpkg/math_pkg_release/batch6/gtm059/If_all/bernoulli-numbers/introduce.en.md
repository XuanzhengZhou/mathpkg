# Bernoulli Numbers

The Bernoulli numbers are a sequence of rational numbers that appear throughout number theory, particularly in formulas for sums of powers, the Riemann zeta function at integers, and the theory of cyclotomic fields. They are defined via the exponential generating function $t/(e^t-1)$. The odd-indexed Bernoulli numbers vanish for $k > 1$, and the even-indexed ones alternate in sign. In the context of cyclotomic fields, Bernoulli numbers appear in the Stickelberger ideal, in formulas for class numbers, and in the construction of $p$-adic $L$-functions via the Kubota-Leopoldt approach.
