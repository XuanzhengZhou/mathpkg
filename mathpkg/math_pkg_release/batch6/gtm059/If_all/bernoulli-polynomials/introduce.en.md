# Bernoulli Polynomials

The Bernoulli polynomials $B_k(X)$ are the polynomial extension of the Bernoulli numbers, defined by the generating function $te^{tX}/(e^t-1)$. They satisfy $B_k(0) = B_k$, the $k$-th Bernoulli number, and obey the fundamental distribution relation $B_k(X) = N^{k-1} \sum_{a=0}^{N-1} B_k(\frac{X+a}{N})$. This distribution property makes them the foundation for constructing distributions (in the sense of ordinary and Bernoulli distributions) on projective systems, which in turn lead to $p$-adic $L$-functions.
