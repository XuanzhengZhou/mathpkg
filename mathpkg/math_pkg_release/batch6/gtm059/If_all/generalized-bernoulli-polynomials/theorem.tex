Let $N$ be a positive integer and let $f$ be a function on $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$ with values in an abelian group. The \textbf{generalized Bernoulli polynomials} (relative to $f$) are defined by
$$B_{k,f}(X) = \sum_{a=1}^{N} f(a) \, B_k\!\left(\frac{X + a}{N}\right) \cdot N^{k-1},$$
where $B_k(X)$ are the classical Bernoulli polynomials. In particular, $f$ may be a Dirichlet character on $(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^{\times}$, extended to $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$ by setting $f(a) = 0$ when $\gcd(a,N) > 1$.
