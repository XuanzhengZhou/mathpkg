Consider the diagram of fields for the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension $K_{\infty}/K$:

$$\begin{array}{c}
K_{\infty} \\
\vdots \\
K_{n+1} \\
K_n \\
\vdots \\
K
\end{array}$$

The unique prime above $p$ in $K_n$ is principal. Indeed, in a cyclotomic extension the prime above $p$ is generated by the element $1 - \zeta_n$, where $\zeta_n$ is a primitive $p^{n+1}$-th root of unity (after a suitable normalization).

The degree $[K_n : \mathbb{Q}]$ is bounded independently of $n$. Let $e_n$ be the ramification index of $p$ in $K_n$, which is bounded by this degree. Let $e$ be the least common multiple of the integers $e_n$ for all $n$. This shows that the ideal above $p$ is principal for all $n$.

As ideals in $K_n$, for $n$ sufficiently large, the ideal above $p$ is divisible by arbitrarily large powers of $p$ as $n \to \infty$, reflecting the total ramification at $p$ in the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension.

By Kummer theory and class field theory, the maximal $p$-abelian extension $Q_n$ of $K_n$ is generated by the $p$-primary ideal class group and the unit group. The assumption that there is only one prime above $p$, together with the fact that this prime is principal, implies that the contribution from the ideal class group is trivialized in the limit.

Consequently, the Kummer extension is generated solely by adjoining $p$-power roots of units. Specifically, taking the $p^{n+1}$-th powers of the unit group $E_n$, we obtain
$$Q_n = Q(E_n^{(p^{n+1})})$$
as claimed. Thus under the unique prime hypothesis, the maximal $p$-abelian extension admits a purely Kummer-theoretic description via units.
