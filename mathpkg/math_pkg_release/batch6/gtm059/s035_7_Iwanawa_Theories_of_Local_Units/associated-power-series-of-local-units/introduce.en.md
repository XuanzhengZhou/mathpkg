This theorem provides a fundamental representation of local units via power series. Every element $u$ in the projective limit $U$ of local unit groups corresponds uniquely to a power series $f_u(X)$ with coefficients in $\mathbb{Z}_p$. The correspondence is a group homomorphism into the multiplicative group of power series congruent to $1$ modulo $p(X)$. Uniqueness follows from the fact that a nonzero power series has only finitely many roots, while existence is proved using the formal properties CW 1 and CW 2 along with continuity arguments.
