This theorem establishes the convergence region for power series in the Leopold space $\mathcal{L}$. Any $f \in \mathcal{L}$ converges on the open disc of radius $|p|^{1/(p-1)}$ in $\mathbb{C}_p$, and the supremum bound $|f(x)|_p \le \|f\|_p$ holds on this disc. This result is fundamental because it guarantees that the $p$-adic Leopoldt transform of such power series yields well-defined $p$-adic functions on an appropriate domain.
