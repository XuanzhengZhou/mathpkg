This theorem establishes the existence and uniqueness of a linear map $\Gamma$ from the polynomial ring $K[X]$ to the space of functions from $\mathbb{Z}$ to $K$, satisfying natural conditions that characterize the Leopoldt transform. The map $\Gamma$ satisfies $\Gamma(1) = 1$ and $\Gamma(X/n)(k) = k/n$, and more generally realizes the Leopoldt transform $f \mapsto f^*$. It also satisfies the homogeneity property $\Gamma(X) f^*(k) = k f(k)$.
