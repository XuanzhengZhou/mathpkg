The Leopold norm $\|\cdot\|_p$ on $\mathcal{L}$ is the supremum (maximum, by the limit condition) of the $p$-adic absolute values of the coefficients. This norm makes $\mathcal{L}$ into a $p$-adic Banach space and, crucially, a Banach algebra. The submultiplicativity $\|fg\|_p \le \|f\|_p \|g\|_p$ enables the use of functional-analytic methods in the study of the $p$-adic Leopoldt transform.
