The gamma distribution is constructed from the classical gamma function $\Gamma(t)$ by a suitable normalization. The functional equation (multiplication formula) of the gamma function translates into the distribution relation, making it an odd distribution on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$. Rohrlich conjectured that this distribution is universal for odd distributions, making it a fundamental object in transcendental number theory. The study of algebraic relations among gamma values is connected to deep questions about periods and motives.
