The Lobachevsky distribution arises from the Lobachevsky function $\Lambda(\theta) = -\int_0^\theta \log|2\sin t|\,dt$, which appears naturally in the computation of volumes of ideal tetrahedra in hyperbolic 3-space. The function satisfies a distribution relation analogous to that of the Bernoulli polynomials. Milnor and others used this connection to study relations among volumes of hyperbolic manifolds and to understand the algebraic structure of the scissors congruence group. Kubert's work on universal distributions provided a general framework for these phenomena.
