The \text{Lobachevsky function} is defined by
$$
\Lambda(\theta) = -\int_0^\theta \log |2 \sin t| \, dt.
$$
Since the function $t \mapsto \log|2 \sin t|$ satisfies an additive distribution-like relation, $\Lambda$ satisfies a distribution relation on $[0, \infty)$. 

In hyperbolic geometry, let $H$ be hyperbolic 3-space (upper half-space model). For a tetrahedron $T$ in $H$ with dihedral angles $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$, the volume is given by
$$
\text{Vol}(T) = \frac{1}{2}\bigl(\Lambda(\alpha) + \Lambda(\beta) + \Lambda(\gamma)\bigr).
$$
The search for relations among such volumes led Milnor to consider the distribution properties of the Lobachevsky function.
