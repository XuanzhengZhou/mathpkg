The von Staudt-Clausen theorem determines the denominators of the Bernoulli numbers. It states that for even $k \geq 2$, the denominator of $B_k$ (in lowest terms) is exactly the product of all primes $p$ such that $p-1$ divides $k$. In particular, $B_k$ is $p$-integral unless $p-1 \mid k$, in which case $p B_k \equiv -1 \pmod{p}$. This result is fundamental in $p$-adic analysis of Bernoulli numbers and can be proved using the integral representation with respect to the Bernoulli distribution.
