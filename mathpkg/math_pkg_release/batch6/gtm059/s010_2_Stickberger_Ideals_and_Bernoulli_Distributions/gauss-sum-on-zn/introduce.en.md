Gauss sums on $\mathbb{Z}(m)$ generalize the classical Gauss sums over finite fields to the ring $\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z}$, which may have zero divisors. They are defined as character sums and factorize according to the prime power decomposition of $m$. The theory closely parallels that of Gauss sums over finite fields, with the additional feature that characters may not be primitive. Gauss sums are fundamental tools in the study of cyclotomic fields, $L$-functions, and the Stickelberger relation.
