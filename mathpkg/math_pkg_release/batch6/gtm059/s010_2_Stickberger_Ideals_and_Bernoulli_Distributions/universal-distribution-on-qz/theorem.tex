Let $N$ be a positive integer. The \text{universal ordinary distribution} on $\frac{1}{N}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z}$ is the abelian group generated by symbols $g(x)$ for $x \in \frac{1}{N}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z}$ subject to the distribution relations. 

For $N = p^n$ a prime power, let $T_N$ be the set of elements in $\frac{1}{N}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z}$ having exact period $N$. Then the elements $g(t)$ for $t \in T_N$ form a basis of the universal distribution, subject to the single relation
$$
\sum_{t \in T_N} g(t) = 0.
$$
The rank of the universal distribution is $\varphi(N) - 1$ for prime power $N$.

For composite $N = \prod p_i^{n_i}$, the universal distribution decomposes according to the partial fraction decomposition, with generators corresponding to elements whose components are primitive at each prime power factor.
