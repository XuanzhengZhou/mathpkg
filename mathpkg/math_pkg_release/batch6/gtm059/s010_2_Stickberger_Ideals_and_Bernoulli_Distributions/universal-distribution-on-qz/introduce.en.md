The universal ordinary distribution on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ is the free abelian group generated by symbols satisfying the distribution relations. It was studied systematically by Kubert and Lang. The theorem describes its structure: for a prime power level, the primitive elements (those of exact period) form a generating set with one linear relation. This universal object classifies all ordinary distributions and provides a framework for understanding the Bernoulli distribution, cyclotomic units, and other arithmetic distributions.
