For any function $f$ on $\mathbb{Z}(m)$, define its \text{Fourier transform} by
$$
\widehat{f}(\chi) = \sum_{x \in \mathbb{Z}(m)^\times} f(x) \chi(x).
$$
If $\chi$ is a primitive character, then
$$
|S(\chi, \lambda)|^2 = m
$$
for any primitive additive character $\lambda$, and the Fourier inversion formula holds:
$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{\varphi(m)} \sum_{\chi} \widehat{f}(\chi) \overline{\chi}(x).
$$
For primitive $\chi$, the normalized Gauss sum $\tau(\chi) = S(\chi, \lambda)/\sqrt{m}$ has absolute value $1$.
