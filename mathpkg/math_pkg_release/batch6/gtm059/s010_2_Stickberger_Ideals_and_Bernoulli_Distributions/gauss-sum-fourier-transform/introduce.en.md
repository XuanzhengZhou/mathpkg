This theorem establishes the Fourier inversion formula on $\mathbb{Z}(m)$ using Gauss sums. It generalizes the classical theory of Fourier analysis on finite abelian groups to the ring $\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z}$ with its group of units. The key identity is $|S(\chi, \lambda)|^2 = m$ for primitive characters, which parallels the classical result for Gauss sums over finite fields. This provides the foundation for the analytic theory of Dirichlet characters and $L$-functions on cyclotomic fields.
