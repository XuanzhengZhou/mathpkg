Let $k$ be an even positive integer such that $k \not\equiv 0 \pmod{p-1}$. Then for any $a \equiv k \pmod{p-1}$, the values
$$
\frac{B_k}{k} \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{B_a}{a}
$$
are congruent modulo $p$. More precisely, if $c$ is a primitive root modulo $p$, then
$$
(1 - c^k)\frac{B_k}{k} \equiv (1 - c^a)\frac{B_a}{a} \pmod{p}.
$$
