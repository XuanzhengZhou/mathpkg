Modular units are units in the ring of modular functions on modular curves, forming an important class of arithmetic objects. They can be described via their $q$-expansions and satisfy distribution relations analogous to those of the Bernoulli distribution. The theory was developed by Ramachandra, Robert, and others, and provides a geometric interpretation of distributions: the modular units on $X(N)$ give rise to a universal even distribution on $\mathbb{Q}^2/\mathbb{Z}^2$.
