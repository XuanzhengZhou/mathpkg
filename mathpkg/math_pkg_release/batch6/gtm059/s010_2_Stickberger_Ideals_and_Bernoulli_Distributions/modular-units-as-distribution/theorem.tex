The \text{modular units} are defined by their $q$-expansions. For a suitable modular function $g(\tau)$ on the modular curve $X(N)$, the association
$$
a \mapsto g(a/N)
$$
defines an even ordinary distribution on $\mathbb{Q}^2/\mathbb{Z}^2 \setminus \{(0,0)\}$, with values in the multiplicative group of modular functions modulo constants, following the work of Ramachandra and Robert. The ordinary Bernoulli distribution with $k = 2$ appears as a homomorphic image of this modular unit distribution.
