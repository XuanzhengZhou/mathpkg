This theorem characterizes when a Gauss sum on $\mathbb{Z}(m)$ vanishes. When a character is primitive (its conductor equals the full modulus) but the additive character is not, the orthogonality relations force the sum to be zero. This extends the well-known fact for Gauss sums over finite fields. The proof uses the prime power decomposition and a coset decomposition of the unit group to exhibit cancellation.
