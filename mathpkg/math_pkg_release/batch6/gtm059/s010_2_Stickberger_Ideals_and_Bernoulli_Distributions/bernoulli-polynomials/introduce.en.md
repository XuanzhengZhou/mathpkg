Bernoulli polynomials are a sequence of polynomials that generalize the Bernoulli numbers. They are defined by the generating function $t e^{tX}/(e^t - 1)$. The constant term of $B_k(X)$ is the $k$-th Bernoulli number. Bernoulli polynomials satisfy the distribution relation $B_k(NX) = N^{k-1} \sum_{a=0}^{N-1} B_k(X + a/N)$, which makes them a fundamental example of an ordinary distribution on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.
