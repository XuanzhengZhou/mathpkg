This theorem establishes the fundamental distribution relation satisfied by the Bernoulli polynomials. It states that $B_k(NX)$ can be expressed as an average over the $N$-th roots of translation of $B_k$. This identity is the prototype for the concept of an ordinary distribution on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ and underlies the construction of the Bernoulli distribution, which is central to the theory of Stickelberger ideals and $p$-adic $L$-functions.
