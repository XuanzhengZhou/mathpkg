Assume that $K = \mathbb{Q}_p$ and that $K(A_1)$ does not contain the $p$-th roots of unity. Then the pairing

$$A_1(A_1) \times K^\times \to A_1(A_1)$$

has the property that the kernel on the left is invariant under the natural action. Moreover,

$$\operatorname{order of } K^\times / K^{\times p^n} = (d(A_1)) \cdot [\mathfrak{m}^{p^n} A_1] / p^n,$$

where $d(A_1)$ is related to the group of $p$-power roots of unity in $K$.

Consequently, the kernel on the right of the pairing is identical to the kernel on the left, yielding an isomorphism between the relevant quotients by duality of finite abelian groups.
