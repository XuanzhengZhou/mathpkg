*Proof.* Let $f_k(X)$ be the power series associated to the Lubin-Tate formal group. The proof proceeds by examining the valuations of the series coefficients. For (i), when $p$ is odd, the series expansion of $(v_k v_k)^2$ contains a factor divisible by $p$, forcing the product to vanish in the relevant quotient. For (ii), the identity $(v_k - v_k)^2 = 0$ is immediate. For (iii) and (iv), the condition $\operatorname{ord}_\pi x \geq e + 1$ ensures sufficient $\pi$-adic divisibility so that all cross-terms in the expansion vanish modulo the appropriate power of $\pi$. The details follow from the formal group addition law and the definition of the Lubin-Tate endomorphism $[\pi]$.
