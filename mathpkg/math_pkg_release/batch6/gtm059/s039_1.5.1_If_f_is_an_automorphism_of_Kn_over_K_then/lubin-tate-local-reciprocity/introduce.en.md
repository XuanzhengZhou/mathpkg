The Lubin-Tate local reciprocity law establishes that every totally ramified abelian extension of a local field can be obtained from the torsion points of a Lubin-Tate formal group. The Artin reciprocity map identifies the unit group $\mathcal{O}_K^{\times}$ with the Galois group of the Lubin-Tate extension. This generalizes the classical fact that the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension is obtained by adjoining $p$-power roots of unity (torsion points of the multiplicative group). In the context of cyclotomic fields, this theory is essential for understanding the action of the Galois group on local units.
