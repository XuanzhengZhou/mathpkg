The proof proceeds in several steps: (1) Show that $[\pi^n]_F(X)$ is an Eisenstein polynomial of degree $q^n$, so that adjoining the $\pi^n$-torsion points gives a totally ramified extension of degree $q^{n-1}(q-1)$. (2) Construct the norm-compatible action of $\mathcal{O}_K^{\times}$ on the torsion points. (3) Verify that this action factors through the quotient $\mathcal{O}_K^{\times}/(1+\pi^n\mathcal{O}_K)$ at level $n$. (4) Use the compatibility under the norm map to pass to the limit, obtaining an isomorphism in the projective limit.
