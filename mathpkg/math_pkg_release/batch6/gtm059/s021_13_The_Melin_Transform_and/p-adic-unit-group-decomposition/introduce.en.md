In a $p$-adic field, the group of units $U$ splits as a direct product $U = U_0 \times U_1$, where $U_0$ is the finite subgroup consisting of all roots of unity whose orders are coprime to $p$, and $U_1$ is the subgroup of principal units (those congruent to $1$ modulo the maximal ideal). This decomposition is fundamental for extending the $p$-adic logarithm from $U_1$ to the full unit group and ultimately to the entire multiplicative group $C_p^\times$, since the logarithm vanishes on $U_0$ and the extension to $C_p^\times$ is obtained by setting $\log p = 0$.
