Let $K$ be a finite extension of $\mathbb{Q}_p$ and let $\varepsilon_1, \dots, \varepsilon_r$ be a basis for the units of $K$ modulo roots of unity. The $p$-adic regulator $R_p(K)$ (up to sign) is defined by
$$
R_p(K) = R_p(\varepsilon_1, \dots, \varepsilon_r) = \det\!\bigl( \log_p \varepsilon_i^{(j)} \bigr)_{1 \leq i,j \leq r},
$$
where $\varepsilon_i^{(j)}$ denote the $j$-th conjugates of $\varepsilon_i$ in the algebraic closure of $\mathbb{Q}_p$ and $\log_p$ is the $p$-adic logarithm extended to $C_p^\times$ with $\log_p p = 0$.

More generally, for a set of units $\eta_1, \dots, \eta_r$ (not necessarily a basis), the $p$-adic regulator is
$$
R_p(\eta_1, \dots, \eta_r) = \det\!\bigl( \log_p \eta_i^{(j)} \bigr).
$$
If $\eta_1, \dots, \eta_r$ is a basis for the units modulo roots of unity, this coincides with $R_p(K)$.
