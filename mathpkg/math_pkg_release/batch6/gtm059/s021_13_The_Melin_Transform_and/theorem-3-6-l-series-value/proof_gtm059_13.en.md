By Theorem 3.3, Proposition 3.5, and the definition of the $p$-adic $L$-function $L_p(1,\chi)$, we find:

$$
\left(1 - \chi(p) p^{-1}\right) L_p(1,\chi) = \frac{\tau(\chi)}{N} \sum_{a=1}^{N} \chi^{-1}(a) \log_p(1 - \zeta_N^a).
$$

Using the fact that $N$ is the conductor of $\chi$ and that the sum of a non-trivial character over a group vanishes, we verify that

$$
\sum_{a=1}^{N} \chi^{-1}(a) = 0.
$$

It follows that the formula simplifies as stated.
