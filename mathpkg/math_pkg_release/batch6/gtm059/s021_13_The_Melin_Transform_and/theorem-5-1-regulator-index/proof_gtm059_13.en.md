The result follows directly from the definition of the $p$-adic regulator as a determinant of $p$-adic logarithms and the relationship between the group of all units and the subgroup of cyclotomic units. If $\eta_1, \dots, \eta_r$ form a basis for $\mathcal{C}$ modulo $\pm 1$, then they also generate a subgroup of $\mathcal{E}$ of index $(\mathcal{E}:\mathcal{C})$. Writing a basis $\varepsilon_1, \dots, \varepsilon_r$ of $\mathcal{E}$ modulo roots of unity in terms of the cyclotomic units, the change-of-basis matrix has determinant $(\mathcal{E}:\mathcal{C})$, giving
$$
R_p(\mathcal{E}) = \det(\log_p \varepsilon_i^{(j)}) = (\mathcal{E}:\mathcal{C}) \cdot \det(\log_p \eta_i^{(j)}) = (\mathcal{E}:\mathcal{C}) \cdot R_p(\mathcal{C}).
$$
The equality $h^+ = (\mathcal{E}:\mathcal{C})$ is established in Theorem 5.1 of the preceding chapter via the class number formula.
