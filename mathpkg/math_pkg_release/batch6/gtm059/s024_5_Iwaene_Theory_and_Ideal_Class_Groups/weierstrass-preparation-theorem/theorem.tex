Let $A$ be a complete local ring with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$. Let

$$f(X) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_k X^k \in A[[X]]$$

be a power series such that not all coefficients $a_k$ lie in $\mathfrak{m}$. Let $n$ be the smallest integer such that $a_n$ is a unit in $A$. Then $f$ can be written uniquely in the form

$$f(X) = P(X) \cdot u(X)$$

where

$$P(X) = X^n + b_{n-1} X^{n-1} + \cdots + b_0$$

is a distinguished polynomial (i.e., $b_i \in \mathfrak{m}$ for all $i$) and $u(X)$ is a unit in $A[[X]]$.

The integer $n$ is called the Weierstrass degree of $f$, denoted $\deg_W(f)$. A power series not all of whose coefficients lie in $\mathfrak{m}$ can thus be expressed as a distinguished polynomial of its Weierstrass degree times a unit in the power series ring.
