The Weierstrass Division Theorem provides a Euclidean-style division algorithm for power series over a complete local ring $A$. Given a power series $f(X)$ whose first unit coefficient occurs at degree $n$, any other power series $g(X)$ can be uniquely expressed as $g = qf + r$ where $r$ is a polynomial of degree less than $n$. This theorem is the foundation for the Weierstrass Preparation Theorem and plays a central role in the structure theory of modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$, where $A = \mathbb{Z}_p$ is the $p$-adic integers.
