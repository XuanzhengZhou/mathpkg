This lemma establishes that the projective limit $U = \varprojlim U_n$ of local principal unit groups has no nontrivial $\mathbb{Z}_p$-torsion. The proof proceeds by contradiction: if there existed a $p$-power torsion element $u$ in $U$, then by descending to a finite level one would obtain arbitrarily large roots of unity in the unit group of some $K_n$, which is impossible. This tors ion-freeness property is essential for applying structure theorems for finitely generated modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda$, since it ensures that $U$ is a torsion-free $\Lambda$-module (in the sense of having no $\mathbb{Z}_p$-torsion).
