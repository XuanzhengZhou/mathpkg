This is a special case of Theorem 1.2 (the integral representation formula) with $z = 0$. Theorem 1.2 states that for any $z \in \mathbb{C}_p$,
$$
f(z-1) = \int z^x \, d\mu(x).
$$
Setting $z = 1$ (i.e., $X = 0$ in the power series variable $X = z-1$), we obtain
$$
f(0) = \int 1^x \, d\mu(x) = \int d\mu(x),
$$
which is precisely the Mean Zero formula.
