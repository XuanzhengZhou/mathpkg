This theorem expresses the value $L(1, \chi)$ of a Dirichlet $L$-function at $s = 1$ as a finite sum involving the Gauss sum $\tau(\chi)$ and logarithms of cyclotomic numbers $1 - \zeta^a$. It is the fundamental bridge between analytic $L$-functions and algebraic number theory, as $L(1, \chi)$ appears in the class number formula for cyclotomic fields. The proof uses Abel summation to express $L(1, \chi)$ as a limit, then decomposes the character sum using the orthogonality relations and the fact that $\chi$ is primitive.
