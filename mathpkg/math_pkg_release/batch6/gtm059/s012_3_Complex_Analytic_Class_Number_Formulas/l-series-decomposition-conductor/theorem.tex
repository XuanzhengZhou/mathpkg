Let $\chi$ be a Dirichlet character modulo $m$, and let $f \mid m$ be the conductor of $\chi$, so that $\chi$ is induced from a primitive character $\psi$ modulo $f$. Then for $\Re(s) > 1$,

$$L(s, \chi) = L(s, \psi) \prod_{\substack{p \mid m \\ p \nmid f}} \left(1 - \frac{\psi(p)}{p^s}\right).$$

In particular, $L(s, \chi)$ and $L(s, \psi)$ differ only by a finite Euler product over the primes dividing $m$ but not $f$. The value $L(1, \chi)$ is therefore a non-zero multiple of $L(1, \psi)$, and the class number formula reduces to the evaluation of $L(1, \psi)$ for primitive characters.
