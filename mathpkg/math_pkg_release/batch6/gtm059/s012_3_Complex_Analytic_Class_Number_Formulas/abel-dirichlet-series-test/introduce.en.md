The Abel-Dirichlet test provides a criterion for convergence of series of the form $\sum a_n b_n$ where $a_n$ decreases to zero and the partial sums of $b_n$ are bounded. This lemma is a direct consequence of summation by parts (Abel summation) and is the fundamental tool used to establish convergence of Dirichlet $L$-series $L(s, \chi) = \sum \chi(n) n^{-s}$ in the region $\Re(s) > 0$ (except possibly at $s = 1$). In the context of complex analytic class number formulas, it is applied to the logarithm series $\sum \chi(a) \log(1 - \zeta^a)$ to justify convergence and termwise manipulation.
