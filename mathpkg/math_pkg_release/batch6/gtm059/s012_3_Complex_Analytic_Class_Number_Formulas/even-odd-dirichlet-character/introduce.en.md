The parity of a Dirichlet character $\chi$ is determined by its value at $-1$. Even characters satisfy $\chi(-1) = 1$ and correspond to characters of the real subfield; odd characters satisfy $\chi(-1) = -1$ and correspond to characters of the imaginary part. This distinction is essential in the evaluation of $L(1, \chi)$, as the formula simplifies differently in the two cases: for even characters the sum involves $\log|1 - \zeta^a|$, while for odd characters it simplifies to a weighted sum of the residues $a$.
