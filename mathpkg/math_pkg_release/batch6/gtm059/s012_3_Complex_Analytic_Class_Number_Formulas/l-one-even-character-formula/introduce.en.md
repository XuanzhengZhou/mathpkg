When $\chi$ is an even primitive character ($\chi(-1) = 1$), the general formula for $L(1, \chi)$ simplifies because the complex logarithms combine to give real quantities. Specifically, pairing the terms $\overline{\chi}(a) \log(1 - \zeta^a)$ and $\overline{\chi}(m-a) \log(1 - \zeta^{m-a})$ and using $\chi(m-a) = \chi(-a) = \chi(a)$ (since $\chi$ is even), the imaginary parts cancel, leaving a sum of logarithms of absolute values $|1 - \zeta^a|$. This yields a purely real expression for $L(1, \chi)$.
