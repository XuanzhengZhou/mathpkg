The integralized Bernoulli distribution $E_{k,c}^{(N)}$ is a modification of the standard Bernoulli distribution $E_k^{(N)}$ obtained by subtracting the distribution composed with multiplication by $c^{-1}$ scaled by $c^k$. This construction, due to Mazur and extensively used by Iwasawa for the case $k=1$ and by Coates-Sinnott for $k>1$, makes the distribution $p$-adically integral. The integralization process plays a fundamental role in the theory of Stickelberger ideals and in the study of $p$-adic $L$-functions.
