Let $c$ be a rational number. For $N$ prime to $c$ (i.e., prime to the numerator and denominator of $c$) we define

$$E_{k,c}^{(N)}(x) = E_k^{(N)}(x) - c^k E_k^{(N)}(c^{-1}x),$$

for $x \in \mathbb{Z}(N)$. Multiplication by $c$ or $c^{-1}$ is well defined on $\mathbb{Z}(N)$. If $N$ is a power of a prime $p$, then we may also take $c$ to be a $p$-adic unit. We write symbolically

$$E_{k,c} = E_k - c^k E_k \circ c^{-1}.$$
