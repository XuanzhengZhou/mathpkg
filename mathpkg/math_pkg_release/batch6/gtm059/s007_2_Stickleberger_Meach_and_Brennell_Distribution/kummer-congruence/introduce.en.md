Kummer's congruence is a fundamental result in the arithmetic of Bernoulli numbers. It states that for even positive integers $k$ belonging to the same non-zero residue class modulo $p-1$, the rational numbers $\frac{1}{k}B_k$ are $p$-integral and mutually congruent modulo $p$. This congruence is the foundation for the construction of $p$-adic $L$-functions and plays a central role in Iwasawa theory and the proof of the Herbrand-Ribet theorem.
