This congruence, which appears at the beginning of §3 on Integral Stickelberger Ideals, connects generalized Bernoulli-Leopoldt numbers (with the Teichmuller character twist) to ordinary Bernoulli numbers modulo $p$. It is obtained by combining Theorem 2.3 (integral representation of $B_k$) with Theorem 2.4 (character formula for Bernoulli-Leopoldt numbers), and provides a crucial link between the classical and generalized Bernoulli numbers in the context of the Stickelberger ideal theory.
