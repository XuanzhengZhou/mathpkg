The von Staudt congruence determines the fractional part of the Bernoulli number $B_k$ when $k$ is even and divisible by $p-1$: the denominator is exactly $p$, and $B_k \equiv -1/p$ modulo $\mathbf{Z}_p$. Together with Kummer's congruence, this result completely characterizes the $p$-adic behavior of Bernoulli numbers. This classical 19th-century result is elegantly reproved using the $p$-adic distribution theory of Stickelberger.
