Theorem 2.3 expresses the Bernoulli number $B_k$, divided by $k$, as an integral of $x^{k-1}$ against the integralized distribution $E_{1,c}$ over the $p$-adic units $\mathbf{Z}_p^*$. This integral representation is a cornerstone of the $p$-adic approach to Bernoulli numbers, showing how the Bernoulli numbers can be recovered from the distribution theory. The formula requires choosing a rational integer $c$ with $c^k \not\equiv 1 \pmod{p}$ to ensure the denominator $1-c^k$ is a $p$-adic unit.
