By Property E2 we have

$$E_{k,c}^{(p^n)}(x) \equiv x^{k-1}E_{1,c}^{(p^n)}(x) \pmod{\frac{p^n}{D(k)}\mathbf{Z}[c, 1/c]}.$$

For the rest of the section, we let $N = p^n$ with $p$ a fixed prime, so the distributions are defined on the projective limit of $\mathbf{Z}(p^n)$, which is $\mathbf{Z}_p$. The values of the distributions are viewed in $\mathbf{C}_p$, the completion of the algebraic closure of the $p$-adic numbers.

Passing to the projective limit as $n \to \infty$, the modulus $\frac{p^n}{D(k)}\mathbf{Z}[c, 1/c]$ tends to $0$ in the $p$-adic topology. Hence the congruence becomes the exact equality

$$E_{k,c}(x) = x^{k-1}E_{1,c}(x)$$

in the limit, as stated.
