This theorem, originally proved by Iwasawa for prime level and extended by W. W. Beilstein for general level, describes how the Galois group of the cyclotomic field acts on certain algebraic integers arising from character sums. The condition $u \equiv 1 \pmod{m}$ ensures that the corresponding Galois automorphism acts trivially on the values $w(\alpha, u)$. This result is a key step in proving that the Stickelberger ideal annihilates the ideal class group, as it shows that the algebraic numbers constructed from Gauss and Jacobi sums are invariant under a sufficiently small congruence subgroup of the Galois group.
