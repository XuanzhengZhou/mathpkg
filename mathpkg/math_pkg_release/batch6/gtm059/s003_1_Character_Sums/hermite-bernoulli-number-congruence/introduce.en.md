Hermite's theorem, discovered in the 19th century, establishes a fundamental congruence relation among Bernoulli numbers modulo a prime $p$. It states that the normalized Bernoulli numbers $B_k/k$ satisfy a periodicity relation modulo $p$ with period $p-1$. This theorem is a precursor to Kummer's congruences and plays an important role in the study of regular primes and Fermat's Last Theorem. In the context of cyclotomic fields, Hermite's theorem connects Bernoulli numbers to the structure of the ideal class group via the Stickelberger ideal.
