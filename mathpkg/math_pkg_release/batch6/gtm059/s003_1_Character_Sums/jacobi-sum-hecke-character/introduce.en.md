Jacobi sums, introduced by C.G.J. Jacobi, are fundamental objects in number theory that relate Gauss sums to each other. In the context of cyclotomic fields, Jacobi sums can be interpreted as Hecke characters (also called Grossencharaktere), which are continuous homomorphisms from the idele class group to $\mathbb{C}^\times$. This interpretation allows Jacobi sums to be used in the construction of $p$-adic $L$-functions and in the proof of the analytic class number formula for cyclotomic fields. The Fourier transform formalism provides a convenient framework for studying their properties.
