This theorem, a classical result of Kronecker, characterizes which algebraic integers are roots of unity: an algebraic integer is a root of unity if and only if all its Galois conjugates lie on the unit circle. In the context of cyclotomic fields and character sums, this theorem is applied to show that certain algebraic numbers constructed from Gauss sums and Jacobi sums are roots of unity. This fact is crucial for establishing the integrality properties of the Stickelberger ideal and for relating character sums to ideal class groups.
