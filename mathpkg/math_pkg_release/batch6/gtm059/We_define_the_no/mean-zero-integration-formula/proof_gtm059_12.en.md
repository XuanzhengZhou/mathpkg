This is a special case of Theorem 1.2 (the general integration formula for the measure associated with a power series) with $z = 0$. By Theorem 1.2, for any $z \in \mathbb{Z}_p$,

$$\int (1 + z)^x d\mu(x) = f(z).$$

Setting $z = 0$ gives $(1 + z)^x = 1$ for all $x$, and thus

$$\int_{\mathbb{Z}_p} d\mu(x) = f(0),$$

as required.
