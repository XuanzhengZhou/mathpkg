The Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ is a complete Noetherian local ring that serves as the fundamental algebraic object in Iwasawa theory. It arises as the projective limit of the group rings $\mathbb{Z}_p[\Gamma/\Gamma^{p^n}]$, where $\Gamma \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ is the Galois group of a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. Modules over the Iwasawa algebra encode the asymptotic behavior of arithmetic objects such as ideal class groups along the tower of intermediate fields.
