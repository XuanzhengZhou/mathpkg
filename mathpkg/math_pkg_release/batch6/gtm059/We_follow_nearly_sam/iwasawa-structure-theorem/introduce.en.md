The structure theorem for finitely generated torsion modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ is the cornerstone of Iwasawa theory. It states that up to pseudo-isomorphism, every such module decomposes into a free part, elementary $p$-torsion modules $\Lambda/(p^{\mu_i})$, and modules of the form $\Lambda/(f_j^{m_j})$ where $f_j$ are irreducible distinguished polynomials. The asymptotic growth of the $p$-adic orders $s_n(V)$ is governed by the Iwasawa invariants $\mu$ and $\lambda$, which are fundamental arithmetic invariants.
