A polynomial $f(X) \in \mathbb{Z}_p[X]$ is called \textbf{distinguished} if

$$f(X) = X^d + a_{d-1}X^{d-1} + \cdots + a_1 X + a_0$$

with $d \geq 0$ and $p \mid a_i$ for all $i = 0, 1, \ldots, d-1$. In other words, $f$ is monic and all its coefficients except the leading one are divisible by $p$. Equivalently, $f(X) \equiv X^d \pmod{p}$.

In the context of Iwasawa theory, distinguished polynomials play a role analogous to irreducible polynomials in the structure theory of finitely generated modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$, via the Weierstrass Preparation Theorem.
