Nakayama's Lemma for the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ is the topological analogue of the classical Nakayama lemma in commutative algebra. It states that for a compact $\Lambda$-module $M$, a closed submodule $N$ satisfying $M = N + \mathfrak{m}M$ must equal $M$ itself, where $\mathfrak{m} = (p, X)$ is the maximal ideal. This lemma is a crucial tool for studying the structure of finitely generated $\Lambda$-modules and is used repeatedly in Iwasawa theory to prove generation results.
