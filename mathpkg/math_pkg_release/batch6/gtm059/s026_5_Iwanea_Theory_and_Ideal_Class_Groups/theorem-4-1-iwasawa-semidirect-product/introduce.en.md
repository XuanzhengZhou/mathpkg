Theorem 4.1 is a fundamental structural result in Iwasawa theory. When a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension has exactly one ramified prime, the Galois group $G$ of the maximal $p$-abelian unramified extension decomposes as a semidirect product of the inertia group $I \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ and the Galois group $G_1$ of the top field over $K_1$. The inertia group provides a canonical splitting of the exact sequence, and $G_1$ is identified as the commutator subgroup of $G$.
