Let $K$ be a number field with $r_1$ real embeddings and $r_2$ pairs of complex embeddings. Let $E$ be the group of global units of $K$. Embed $E$ into $\prod_{\mathfrak{p} \mid p} K_\mathfrak{p}^*$ via the diagonal embedding into the completions at primes dividing $p$. Let $\overline{E}$ be the $p$-adic closure of $E$ in this product.

**Leopoldt's Conjecture.** The $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank of $\overline{E}$ equals $r_1 + r_2 - 1$, i.e., the closure of the global units in the $p$-adic topology has the same rank as predicted by Dirichlet's unit theorem. Equivalently, the $p$-adic regulator $R_p(K)$ is non-zero.

Consequently, the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank of $G_M(K)$ (the defect) satisfies

$$\operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{Z}_p} G_M(K) = r_2 + 1 + \delta_K,$$

where $\delta_K \geq 0$ is the Leopoldt defect, and the conjecture asserts $\delta_K = 0$.
