Let $p$ be a prime number. The **cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension** of $\mathbb{Q}$ is

$$\mathbb{Q}_\infty = \mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty}) = \bigcup_{n \geq 1} \mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^n}),$$

where $\mu_{p^n}$ denotes the $p^n$-th roots of unity. Its Galois group is

$$\operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}_\infty / \mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p^\times \cong \mathbb{Z}_p \times (\text{finite cyclic}),$$

and the fixed field of the torsion subgroup is the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension, denoted $\mathbb{Q}_{\text{cyc}}$.

More generally, for any number field $K$, the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of $K$ is $K_{\text{cyc}} = K \cdot \mathbb{Q}_{\text{cyc}}$, obtained by composing $K$ with the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of $\mathbb{Q}$.
