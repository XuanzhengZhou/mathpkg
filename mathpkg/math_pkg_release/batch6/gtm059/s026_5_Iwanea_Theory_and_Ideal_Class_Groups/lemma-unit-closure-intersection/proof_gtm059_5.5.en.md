*Proof.* Let $U^{(n)}_p$ be the group of units in $U_p$ that are congruent to $1$ modulo $p^n$. Then the groups $U^{(n)}_p \cdot U_p^\perp K^*$ form a fundamental system of neighborhoods for the closure $\overline{U_p^\perp K^*}$ in the idele topology. 

An element of the intersection $\overline{U_p E} \cap \overline{U_p^\perp K^*}$ can be approximated by elements of the form $u \cdot e$ (with $u \in U_p$, $e \in E$) and also by elements of the form $u' \cdot k$ (with $u' \in U_p^\perp$, $k \in K^*$). Since the components at primes dividing $p$ and at primes not dividing $p$ are independent in the idele topology, the intersection reduces to studying when a global unit can be approximated by local units at primes dividing $p$, which by the unit theorem happens only for roots of unity. The precise statement follows from a careful analysis of the idele group quotients.
