Let $K$ be a number field. A Galois extension $K_\infty / K$ is called a **$\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension** if its Galois group is isomorphic to the additive group of $p$-adic integers:

$$\operatorname{Gal}(K_\infty / K) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p.$$

Equivalently, a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension is given by a tower of fields

$$K = K_0 \subset K_1 \subset K_2 \subset \cdots \subset K_\infty = \bigcup_{n \geq 0} K_n$$

such that $K_n / K$ is cyclic of degree $p^n$ for each $n \geq 1$.
