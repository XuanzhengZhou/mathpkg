Let $K_\infty / K$ be a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension with intermediate fields $K_n$ ($[K_n : K] = p^n$). Then:

(i) Only finitely many prime ideals of $K$ ramify in $K_\infty / K$.

(ii) If a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of $K$ ramifies in $K_\infty / K$, then $\mathfrak{p}$ is totally ramified in $K_n / K$ for all sufficiently large $n$. In particular, the ramification index of $\mathfrak{p}$ in $K_n / K$ is $p^{n - n_0}$ for large $n$.

(iii) At least one prime ramifies in $K_\infty / K$ (a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension cannot be everywhere unramified).
