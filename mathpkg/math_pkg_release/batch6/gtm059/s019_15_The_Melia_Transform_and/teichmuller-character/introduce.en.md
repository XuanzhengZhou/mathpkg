The Teichmüller character $\omega$ is a multiplicative character on the group of $p$-adic units $\mathbb{Z}_p^{\times}$. For odd $p$, it maps $\mathbb{Z}_p^{\times}$ onto the group $\mu_{p-1}$ of $(p-1)$-st roots of unity in $\mathbb{Z}_p$, characterized by the congruence $\omega(a) \equiv a \pmod{p}$. For $p = 2$, the character takes values $\pm 1$ with the congruence $\omega(a) \equiv a \pmod{4}$. The decomposition $a = \omega(a)\langle a \rangle$ separates any $p$-adic unit into its Teichmüller (torsion) part and its principal unit part, a fundamental tool in the construction of $p$-adic $L$-functions.
