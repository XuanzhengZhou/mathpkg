The Mellin transform (also called the Gauss transform in this context) of a $p$-adic measure $\mu$ is defined by integrating the character $\langle x \rangle^{s}$ against $\mu$ over the group of $p$-adic units $\mathbb{Z}_p^{\times}$. Here $\langle x \rangle$ denotes the principal unit part of $x$ under the Teichmüller decomposition. This transform converts measures into analytic functions on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ and serves as the bridge between $p$-adic measures and $p$-adic $L$-functions; indeed, the $p$-adic $L$-function is realized as the Mellin transform of a suitably defined measure.
