The integral can be written as a sum of integrals over cosets of $1 + p\mathbb{Z}_p$ (or $1 + 4\mathbb{Z}_2$ for $p = 2$). Changing the measure appropriately with respect to $p$ reduces the proof to showing, for odd $p$, that for any measure $\mu$, the integral

$$\int_{\mathbb{Z}_p^{\times}} \Omega^k(t) \, d\mu(t)$$

has the desired analyticity property. We note that the integral

$$\int_{\mathbb{Z}_p^{\times}} \Omega^k(t) \, d\mu(t)$$

can be expressed as a power series in $t$. By decomposing the domain of integration into the cosets $1 + p^n\mathbb{Z}_p$ and using the structure of the measure, one obtains the power series expansion with the required properties.
