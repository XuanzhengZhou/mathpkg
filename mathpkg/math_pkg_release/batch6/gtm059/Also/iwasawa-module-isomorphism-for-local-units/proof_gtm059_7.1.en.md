The proof occupies the rest of this section and results from the construction of a canonical $\Lambda$-basis element for $U(\chi)$ over $\Lambda$. The argument proceeds as follows.

Let $K_n^0 = K_n$ and let $\widehat{K}_n$ be the maximal $p$-abelian extension of $K_n$. We have a tower of fields
$$K_n = K_n^0 \subset K_n^1 \subset \cdots \subset \widehat{K}_n.$$
Recall that the Galois group $\Gamma = \mathrm{Gal}(K_\infty/K)$ operates by conjugation on $\mathrm{Gal}(\widehat{K}_n/K_n)$.

The strategy uses Galois cohomology and local class field theory in a manner analogous to the global case. One first shows that $U(\chi)$ is a finitely generated torsion-free $\Lambda$-module. The fact that $U$ has no $\mathbb{Z}_p$-torsion (established in Lemma 2) implies that $U$ is $\Lambda$-torsion-free. Combined with the rank computation from Lemma 1, one obtains that the $\Lambda$-rank of $U(\chi)$ is $1$.

The construction of the explicit $\Lambda$-basis element for $U(\chi)$ is completed in the next section, where a norm-coherent sequence of local units is produced using the theory of the logarithm and the explicit reciprocity law. The isomorphism $U(\chi) \cong \Lambda$ then follows.
