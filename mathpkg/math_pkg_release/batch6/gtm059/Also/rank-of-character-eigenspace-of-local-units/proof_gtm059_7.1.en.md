The integers of $K_n$ contain a free submodule over the group ring $\mathbb{Z}_p[G_n]$, and for large $n$ with $1 + p^n$, this submodule is Galois-compatible with the above submodule under the exponential map, and is contained in $U_n$ with finite index. This establishes the first part.

For the second part, the only torsion in $U_n$ consists of the roots of unity $\mu_{p^{n+1}}$ which lie in the $1$-eigenspace (the trivial character component). Hence if $\chi \neq 1$, the eigenspace $U_n(\chi)$ has no torsion, so it is a free $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module. Moreover, $U_n(\chi)$ is isomorphic to a submodule of $\mathbb{Z}_p^{p^n}$, which gives the rank assertion.
