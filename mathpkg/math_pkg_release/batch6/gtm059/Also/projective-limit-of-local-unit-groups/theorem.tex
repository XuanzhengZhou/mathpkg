Let $K_n$ be the $n$-th layer of the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of a finite extension $K$ of $\mathbb{Q}_p$, and let $U_n$ be the group of principal units in $K_n$. The groups $U_n$ form a projective system under the norm maps $N_{m,n}: U_m \to U_n$ for $m \ge n$. The projective limit
$$U = \varprojlim_{n \to \infty} U_n$$
is a topological compact $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module, and also a $\Lambda$-module where $\Lambda = \varprojlim_n \mathbb{Z}_p[\mathrm{Gal}(K_n/K)]$ is the Iwasawa algebra. An element $u \in U$ is viewed as a vector $u = (\dots, u_n, \dots)$ with components $u_n \in U_n$ such that $N_{m,n}(u_m) = u_n$ for $m \ge n$, written as $u = \lim_{n \to \infty} u_n$.
