For an odd prime $p$, let $R_{ij}^+$ denote the least positive integer in the residue class of $ij$ modulo $p$, where $i,j$ range from $1$ to $(p-1)/2$. Similarly, let $R_{ij}^-$ denote the least positive integer in the residue class of $-ij$ modulo $p$. In other words,

$$1 \leq R_{ij}^+ \leq p-1, \quad R_{ij}^+ \equiv ij \pmod{p},$$
$$1 \leq R_{ij}^- \leq p-1, \quad R_{ij}^- \equiv -ij \pmod{p}.$$

These quantities are used to form the matrices $(R_{ij}^+)$ and $(R_{ij}^-)$ of size $\frac{p-1}{2} \times \frac{p-1}{2}$, whose determinants appear in Theorem 7.1.
