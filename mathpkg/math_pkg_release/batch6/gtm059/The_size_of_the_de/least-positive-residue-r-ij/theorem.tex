Let $p$ be an odd prime. For integers $i,j$, let $R_{ij}$ denote the least positive integer in the residue class of $ij$ modulo $p$. That is, $R_{ij}$ is the unique integer satisfying

$$1 \leq R_{ij} \leq p-1, \qquad R_{ij} \equiv ij \pmod{p}.$$

Equivalently, $R_{ij}$ is the remainder of $ij$ upon division by $p$, except that when $p \mid ij$ the remainder is taken as $p$ rather than $0$.
