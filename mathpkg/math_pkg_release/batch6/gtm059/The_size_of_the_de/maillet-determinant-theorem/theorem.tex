Let $p$ be an odd prime. Let $R_{ij}^+$ be the least positive integer in the residue class of $ij$ modulo $p$, and let $R_{ij}^-$ be the least positive integer in the residue class of $-ij$ modulo $p$, for $i,j = 1,\dots,\frac{p-1}{2}$. Define the Maillet determinant

$$D_p = \det\left( R_{ij}^+ - R_{ij}^- \right)_{i,j=1,\dots,\frac{p-1}{2}}.$$

Then $\pm D_p$ is equal to $p^{(p-3)/2}$ times the relative class number $h^-$ of the $p$-th cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_p)$, up to a power of $2$. In particular, each entry $R_{ij}^+ - R_{ij}^-$ is $\pm p$ or $0$, and the scaled determinant $D_p / p^{(p-1)/2}$ has integer entries of absolute value at most $1$.
