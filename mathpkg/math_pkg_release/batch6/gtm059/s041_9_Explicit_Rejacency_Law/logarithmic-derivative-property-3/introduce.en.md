This lemma (designated DL 3 in the text) describes the behavior of the logarithmic derivative $\lambda_i$ on units in the Galois group of a local field extension. For $u$ in the Galois group $\operatorname{Gal}(K^2/K)$ and simultaneously a unit in $K\mathbb{Z}$, the logarithmic derivative satisfies the functional equation $\lambda_i(u) = u(\lambda_i(u)^{\alpha})$. This property is fundamental for comparing logarithmic derivatives of different elements and is used to establish explicit reciprocity laws in the Lubin-Tate framework.
