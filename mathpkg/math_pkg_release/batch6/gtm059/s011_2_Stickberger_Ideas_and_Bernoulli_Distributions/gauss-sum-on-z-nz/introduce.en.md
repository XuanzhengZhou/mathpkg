The Gauss sum $S(\chi)$ on the finite cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ is defined as the sum of $\chi(a)\zeta^a$ over $a \in \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, where $\zeta$ is a primitive $n$-th root of unity and $\chi$ is a character. This generalizes the classical Gauss sum over finite fields to the ring $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, which has zero divisors when $n$ is composite. The product decomposition of $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ and the conductor of $\chi$ play essential roles in understanding the multiplicative properties of these sums.
