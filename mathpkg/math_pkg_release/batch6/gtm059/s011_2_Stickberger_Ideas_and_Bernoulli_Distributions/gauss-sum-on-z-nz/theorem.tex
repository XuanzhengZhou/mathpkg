Let $\zeta$ be a primitive $n$-th root of unity. For a character $\chi$ on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, the \textbf{Gauss sum} $S(\chi)$ is defined by
$$S(\chi) = \sum_{x \in (\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times} \chi(x) \, \zeta^x.$$
More generally, for the additive group $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, the Gauss sum with respect to a primitive character $\chi$ is
$$S(\chi) = \sum_{a \in \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}} \chi(a) \, \zeta^a.$$
For the non-primitive case, if $\chi$ factors through $\mathbb{Z}/d\mathbb{Z}$ with $d \mid n$, the Gauss sum over $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ involves a summation over the full group, and it vanishes when conditions on the conductor are not met.
