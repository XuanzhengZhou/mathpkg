Given a character $\chi$ on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, its conductor is the smallest modulus $d \mid n$ through which $\chi$ factors. This generalizes the classical notion of conductor for Dirichlet characters. The conductor measures how far $\chi$ is from being primitive: $\chi$ is primitive precisely when its conductor equals $n$. In the prime power case $n = p^v$, the conductor is $p^g$ where $g$ is minimal with $\chi(1 + p^g \mathbb{Z}/p^v\mathbb{Z}) = 1$.
