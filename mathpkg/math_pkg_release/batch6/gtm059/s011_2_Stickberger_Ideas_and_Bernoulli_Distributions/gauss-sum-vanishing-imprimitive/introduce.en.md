Theorem 1.1 of Lang's *Cyclotomic Fields* states that if a character $\chi$ on $\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z}$ is primitive of conductor $d$ and $n$ is an integer not prime to $m$, then the generalized Gauss sum $S(\chi, n)$ vanishes. This is one of the fundamental properties distinguishing primitive characters from imprimitive ones. The vanishing occurs because the sum over cosets of the subgroup on which $\chi$ is non-trivial evaluates to zero by orthogonality.
