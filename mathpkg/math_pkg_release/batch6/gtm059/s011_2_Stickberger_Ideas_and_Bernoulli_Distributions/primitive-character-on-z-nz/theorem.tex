A character $\chi$ on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ is called \textbf{primitive} if it does not factor through $\mathbb{Z}/d\mathbb{Z}$ for any proper divisor $d$ of $n$. Equivalently, in the prime power case $n = p^v$, the character $\chi$ is primitive if and only if $\chi$ is non-trivial on the subgroup $1 + p^{v-1}\mathbb{Z}/p^v\mathbb{Z}$.
