Theorem 1.2 establishes the fundamental relationship between the Fourier transform of a primitive character and its Gauss sum. For a primitive character $\chi$ on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, the Fourier transform $T\chi$ evaluated at $x$ equals the Gauss sum $\tau(\chi)$ multiplied by the conjugate character value $\overline{\chi(x)}$. As an immediate corollary, the absolute value of the Gauss sum is $|\tau(\chi)| = \sqrt{n}$, extending the classical result for Gauss sums over finite prime fields to the case of composite moduli with primitive characters.
