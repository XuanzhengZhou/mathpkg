Let $\chi$ be a primitive character on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ and let $\tau(\chi) = S(\chi) = \sum_{a \in \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}} \chi(a) \zeta^a$ be its Gauss sum, where $\zeta$ is a primitive $n$-th root of unity. Then for the Fourier transform $T\chi$ defined by
$$(T\chi)(x) = \sum_{a \in \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}} \chi(a) \zeta^{ax},$$
we have:
$$(T\chi)(x) = \tau(\chi) \, \overline{\chi(x)}.$$
In particular,
$$|\tau(\chi)|^2 = n.$$
