The Fourier transform on the finite cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ maps a function $f$ to its transform $\widehat{f}$ defined on the dual group of characters. Using the self-duality pairing $(x, y) \mapsto \zeta^{xy}$ with a primitive $n$-th root of unity $\zeta$, the Fourier transform takes the form $\widehat{f}(y) = \sum_x f(x) \zeta^{-xy}$. This transform is a fundamental tool for analyzing Gauss sums, as the Gauss sum $S(\chi)$ is essentially the Fourier transform of the character $\chi$ itself, evaluated at a suitable point.
