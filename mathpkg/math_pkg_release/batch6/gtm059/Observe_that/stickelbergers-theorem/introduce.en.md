Stickelberger's Theorem is a central result in the arithmetic of cyclotomic fields. It determines the exact power of a prime ideal $\mathfrak{P}$ above $p$ that divides a Gauss sum $S(\chi)$. The valuation is expressed in terms of the $p$-adic digit sum $s(\chi)$, a combinatorial invariant computed from the $p$-adic expansion of integers associated to the character. This theorem provides the essential link between Gauss sums and $p$-adic $L$-functions, and it is the starting point for Stickelberger's construction of elements in the group ring that annihilate the ideal class group of cyclotomic fields — the Stickelberger ideal.
