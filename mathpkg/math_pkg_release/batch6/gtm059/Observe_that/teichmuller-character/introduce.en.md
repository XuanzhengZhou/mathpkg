The Teichmüller character is a fundamental construction in $p$-adic number theory and algebraic number theory. It provides a canonical multiplicative section of the reduction map $\mathbb{Z}_p^* \to \mathbb{F}_p^*$, lifting each non-zero residue class to a unique $(p-1)$-th root of unity in the $p$-adic integers. This character generates the character group of the multiplicative group of the finite field, so any multiplicative character of $\mathbb{F}_p^*$ (or more generally of $\mathbb{F}_q^*$) can be expressed as a power of the Teichmüller character. It plays a central role in the theory of Gauss sums, Stickelberger's theorem, and Iwasawa theory.
