Let $\chi$ be a multiplicative character of the finite field $F_q^*$ of order $m$. The Gauss sum $S(\chi) = \sum_{x \in F_q^*} \chi(x) \zeta_p^{\operatorname{Tr}(x)}$ is an algebraic integer in the compositum $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{q-1}, \zeta_p)$. Theorem 1.3 determines the smallest cyclotomic field containing $S(\chi)^m$: it lies in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$, the field generated by the $m$-th roots of unity. This result is fundamental for understanding the Galois action on Gauss sums and underpins the proof of Stickelberger's theorem on the prime ideal factorization of Gauss sums.
