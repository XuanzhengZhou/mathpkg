The $p$-adic digit sum $s(k)$ is a combinatorial function that sums the digits in the base-$p$ expansion of an integer $k$. It appears naturally in Stickelberger's theorem, where the prime ideal factorization of Gauss sums is governed by $s(\chi)$, the digit sum computed from the $p$-adic expansion of the integer associated to a Dirichlet character. The related quantity $\gamma(k) = \prod k_i!$, the product of factorials of the $p$-adic digits, also appears in the refined congruence for Gauss sums modulo higher powers of the prime ideal. These functions are made $(q-1)$-periodic for use with characters of $F_q^*$.
