The logarithmic derivative map

$$
D_n : K_n^\times \longrightarrow \mathfrak{m}_n^{\,-1} \mathfrak{D}_n^{-1}
$$

is a homomorphism whose image on the unit group lies in $\mathfrak{m}_n^{\,-1} \mathfrak{D}_n^{-1}$, where $\mathfrak{D}_n$ is the different of $K_n$ over $K$ and $\mathfrak{m}_n$ is the maximal ideal.

Let $x \in K_n$ and write $x = g(\pi_n)$ as a power series with $g(X) = X^m + \text{higher terms}$ and leading coefficient a unit. Then

$$
g'(\pi_n) = \frac{1}{2} \pi_n^m + \frac{1}{4} f(\pi_n) \quad \text{or} \quad 0 \pmod{\varpi_1}
$$

where $f(X)$ is the irreducible polynomial of $\pi_n$ over $K$. In particular, $g'(\pi_n)$ is well-defined modulo the different.
