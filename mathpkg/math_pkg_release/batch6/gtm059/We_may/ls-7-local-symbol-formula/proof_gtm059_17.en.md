The proof proceeds by applying the logarithm map to the local symbol pairing. Starting from the pairing

$$
A(b_0)^{g+1}A(b_1) \times K_2 \longrightarrow A_{\mathfrak{a}}
$$

we apply the logarithm of the Lubin-Tate formal group. Since the logarithm is an isomorphism near the origin, the kernel is precisely the torsion subgroup. It follows that the kernel of the induced map is trivial on the torsion-free part, yielding a well-defined pairing

$$
A(b_0)^{g+1}A(b_1) \times A_{\mathfrak{a}} \longrightarrow A_{\mathfrak{a}}.
$$

The exact duality sequence

$$
\mathfrak{a}^{g+1}\mathfrak{a}^* \times \mathfrak{a}^* \longrightarrow \mathfrak{a}^* \longrightarrow \mathfrak{a}^* \times \mathfrak{a}^*
$$

then provides the unique group homomorphism

$$
A_{\mathfrak{a}} \longrightarrow A(b_0)^{g+1}A(b_1)
$$

with the stated property. The formula LS 7 is the explicit expression of this homomorphism in terms of the local symbol.
