This is a special case of Theorem 1.1 applied to the formal multiplicative group $\mathbb{G}_m$. For $\mathbb{G}_m$, the Lubin-Tate group law is $X + Y + XY$, and the logarithm is $\log(1+X)$. The points of $\mathbb{G}_m(\mathfrak{p}^n)$ correspond to elements $c \equiv 1 \pmod{\mathfrak{p}}$. Using the explicit reciprocity formula of Theorem 1.1 and specializing to the formal multiplicative group, one obtains the description

$$
\mathbb{G}_m(\mathfrak{p}^n) = \left\{ c \;\middle|\; c = \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} a_i b_i u^i \right\}
$$

with $u \equiv 1 \pmod{\mathfrak{p}}$ and coefficients determined by the group law. This is the content of Corollary 2 obtained by Wiles as a special case of the Lubin-Tate formula.
