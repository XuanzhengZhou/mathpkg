The unit index $Q = (E : \mu_K E^+)$ is a crucial invariant in the arithmetic of CM fields $K$. It appears in the class number formula relating the class number $h$ of $K$ to the class number $h^+$ of its maximal real subfield $K^+$. Following Hasse, the unit index enters the decomposition $h = h^+ \cdot h^-$ and determines the relationship between regulators. For cyclotomic fields, Hasse proved that $Q = 1$ when the conductor is a prime power, and $Q = 2$ otherwise, a result that plays a fundamental role in the analytic determination of relative class numbers.
