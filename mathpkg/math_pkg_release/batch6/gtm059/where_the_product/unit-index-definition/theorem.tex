Let $K$ be a CM field (totally imaginary quadratic extension of a totally real field $K^+$). Let $E = \mathcal{O}_K^\times$ be the group of units of $K$, and let $E^+ = \mathcal{O}_{K^+}^\times$ be the group of units of the maximal real subfield $K^+$. Let $\mu_K$ denote the group of roots of unity in $K$. The \textbf{unit index} (also called Hasse's unit index) is defined as

$$Q = Q_K = \left( E : \mu_K E^+ \right),$$

the index of the subgroup generated by the roots of unity and the units of the maximal real subfield within the full unit group of $K$. Equivalently, $Q$ measures the extent to which $K$ has units that are not products of roots of unity and units coming from the real subfield.
