The decomposition of $L$-series expresses the Dedekind zeta function of a number field as a product of Dirichlet $L$-functions over primitive characters. This factorization is fundamental to the analytic theory of cyclotomic fields, as it relates arithmetic invariants of the field (like the class number and discriminant) to values of $L$-functions. The decomposition is established by comparing Euler products at each prime: for a prime $p$ not dividing the conductor, the local Euler factor of $\zeta_K(s)$ factors according to the decomposition of $p$ into prime ideals, which in turn corresponds to a product over the characters of the associated Galois group. This result underpins the analytic class number formula and the proof of Leopoldt's $p$-adic analogue.
