Theorem 3.2 gives the class number formula for a totally imaginary abelian extension $K$ of $\mathbb{Q}$ in terms of the class number and regulator of its maximal real subfield $K^+$, the unit index $Q$, and the number of roots of unity $w$. This decomposition $h = h^+ \cdot h^-$ (where $h^- = h/h^+$ is the relative class number) is fundamental in Iwasawa theory, as the minus part $h^-$ is governed by $p$-adic $L$-functions while $h^+$ is related to cyclotomic units. The formula is obtained by comparing the analytic class number formulas for $K$ and $K^+$, noting that the product over odd characters contributes the relative factor.
