The analytic class number formula expresses the residue at $s=1$ of the Dedekind zeta function in terms of arithmetic invariants: the class number $h$, the regulator $R$, the number of roots of unity $w$, and the discriminant $d$. This deep connection between analysis and arithmetic is one of the crown jewels of algebraic number theory. For abelian extensions of $\mathbb{Q}$ (cyclotomic fields), the zeta function factors into Dirichlet $L$-functions, yielding an explicit expression for the class number as a product of $L$-function values at $s=1$. This serves as the foundation for Iwasawa theory and the construction of $p$-adic $L$-functions.
