Let $K$ be a number field of degree $N = [K : \mathbb{Q}]$. Let

$$r_1 = \text{number of real embeddings of } K,$$
$$r_2 = \text{number of pairs of complex conjugate embeddings},$$

so that $N = r_1 + 2r_2$. Define the following invariants:

$$w = w_K = \text{number of roots of unity in } K,$$
$$h = h_K = \text{class number of } K,$$
$$R = R_K = \text{regulator of } K,$$
$$d = d_K = \text{discriminant of } K.$$

Then the residue of the Dedekind zeta function $\zeta_K(s)$ at $s = 1$ is given by the analytic class number formula:

$$\lim_{s \to 1} (s - 1) \zeta_K(s) = \frac{2^{r_1} (2\pi)^{r_2} h R}{w \sqrt{|d|}}.$$

If $K$ is totally real ($r_2 = 0$), then $w = 2$ and the formula reads

$$\frac{2^{r_1} h R}{w \sqrt{|d|}} = \prod_{\chi \neq 1} L(1, \chi)$$

where the product is taken over all non-trivial primitive characters of the Galois group of $K/\mathbb{Q}$. For the maximal real subfield $K^+$ of a CM field $K$, the corresponding formula involves the product over even characters only.
