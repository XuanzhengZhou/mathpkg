Let $\chi$ run over all primitive characters induced by the characters of the Galois group of the number field $K$. Then the following product expressions hold:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\displaystyle \prod_{\chi} \tau(\chi) = \sqrt{d_K}^{\,w_K}$ where $\tau(\chi)$ is the Gauss sum of $\chi$ and $d_K$ is the discriminant.

\item[(ii)] If $K$ is totally real, then
$$\prod_{\chi \text{ even}} \tau(\chi) = \sqrt{d_K}.$$

\item[(iii)] If $K$ is a CM field (totally imaginary), then the product over even characters yields
$$\prod_{\chi \text{ even}} L(1, \chi) = \frac{2^{r_1} h R}{w \sqrt{|d_K|}}$$
where $h$ is the class number, $R$ the regulator, $w$ the number of roots of unity, and $d_K$ the discriminant.
\end{enumerate}

Equivalently, in terms of $L$-series values at $s=1$, the factorization of $\zeta_K(s)$ as a product of $L(s,\chi)$ evaluated at $s=1$ gives the class number formula.
