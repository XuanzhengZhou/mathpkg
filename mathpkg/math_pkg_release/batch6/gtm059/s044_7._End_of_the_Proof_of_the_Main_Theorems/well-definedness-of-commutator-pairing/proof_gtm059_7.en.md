Since $A_n(1) = 0$, it follows that $A_n(1) = a(1) = g$. This shows that $[x, a]$ is well defined and defined in $a(1)$, well defined in $n^2$, requiring $A_n(1)$. The proof shows that $g$ suffices to prove the generality of $A_n(1)$ in the special Lie algebra $L^2$.

We may therefore assume that $n$ is a unit in $n^2$. In this case we follow the same arguments as in the proof of Theorem 6.1, using Lemma 6.3 instead of Theorem 6.2. The reader can check that each step is valid, to conclude the proof.
