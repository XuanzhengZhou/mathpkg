This theorem establishes the well-definedness of a commutator-type pairing $[x, a]$ under the assumptions $p = 2$ and $n = 2$, showing that the expression $\frac{1}{2} T(\lambda(a)(a(1)))$ yields a well-defined element modulo $n$ lying in the subspace $c(x)$. The result appears in the concluding section of the proof of the main theorems of cyclotomic field theory, and its proof relies on the vanishing property $A_n(1) = 0$ together with the Lie algebra framework developed in earlier sections (Theorem 6.1, Lemma 6.3, and Theorem 6.2).
