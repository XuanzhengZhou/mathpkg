Let $c \in \mathbb{Z}$ be a primitive root modulo $p^k$, and for each $n$ define
$$v_n = u(\zeta_n)\left(\frac{\zeta_n^{c} - 1}{\zeta_n - 1}\right),$$
where $\zeta_n$ is a primitive $p^{\,n}$-th root of unity. Then $v_n \equiv 1 \pmod{\mathfrak{m}_n}$, where $\mathfrak{m}_n$ is the maximal ideal of the $n$-th layer. The elements $v_n$ form a norm-compatible system in the cyclotomic tower:
$$N_{m,n} \, v_m = v_n \quad \text{for all } m \ge n.$$
The projective limit $v = \varprojlim_n v_n$ is a well-defined element of $V = \varprojlim_n V_n$, and its associated power series satisfies
$$f_v(X) = \lim_{n \to \infty} v_n(\zeta_n) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \zeta_n^{\,c^{\,n}}.$$
