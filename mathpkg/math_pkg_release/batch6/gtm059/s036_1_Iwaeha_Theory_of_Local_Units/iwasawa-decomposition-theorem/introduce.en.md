Theorem 5.1 provides the fundamental structural decomposition of the Iwasawa module $V = \varprojlim V_n$ formed by the projective limit of closures of cyclotomic units. By decomposing according to even characters $\chi \neq 1$ of the torsion subgroup of $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$, one obtains that each isotypic component $V(\chi)$ is a cyclic module over the Iwasawa algebra $\mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]]$. This cyclicity result is essential for comparing $V(\chi)$ with the full unit module $U(\chi)$, as the ratio is measured by the $p$-adic $L$-function via the Iwasawa main conjecture. The proof follows from Theorem 3.2 of Chapter 6 (see also Theorem 3.2 of Chapter 15), which establishes the direct relationship between the structure of cyclotomic units and the Iwasawa algebra.
