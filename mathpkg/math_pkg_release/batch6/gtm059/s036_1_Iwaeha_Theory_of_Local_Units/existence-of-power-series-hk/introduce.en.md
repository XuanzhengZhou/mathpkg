Theorem 4.2 in the Iwasawa theory of local units establishes the existence of power series $h_k$ parametrized by congruence classes modulo $p-1$. These power series arise from the decomposition of the logarithm of the Coleman power series according to the characters of the multiplicative group $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^\times$. The construction uses the formal logarithm $D\log$ and integration against $p$-adic measures to produce $h_k$ as a sum of contributions from each component of the character decomposition. The result that $h_k$ is a unit in $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ for even congruence classes is crucial for establishing the structure of the Iwasawa module of local units as a module over the Iwasawa algebra.
