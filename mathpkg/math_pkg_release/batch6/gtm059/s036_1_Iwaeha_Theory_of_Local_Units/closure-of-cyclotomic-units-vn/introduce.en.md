For each level $n$ of the cyclotomic tower, the group of cyclotomic units $E_n$ is a subgroup of the local principal unit group $U_n$. Taking the $p$-adic closure yields $V_n = \overline{E_n}$, which captures the $p$-adic limit behavior of cyclotomic units. Passing to the projective limit $V = \varprojlim V_n$ yields a compact $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module with a continuous action of the Galois group $\Gamma \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$, making it a module over the Iwasawa algebra $\mathbb{A} = \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]]$. The Iwasawa main conjecture relates the characteristic ideal of $U/V$ (where $U = \varprojlim U_n$) to the $p$-adic $L$-function, thus linking algebraic and analytic objects.
