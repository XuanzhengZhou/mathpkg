Theorem 3.2 provides the complete factorization of the class number $h_K$ for an imaginary abelian extension $K/\mathbb{Q}$. It expresses $h_K$ as the product of the class number $h^+$ of the maximal real subfield, the unit index $Q$, the number of roots of unity $w$, and a product of generalized Bernoulli numbers over odd characters. This decomposition is fundamental in Iwasawa theory: the odd part $h^-$ is well-understood via $p$-adic $L$-functions and the Ferrero-Washington theorem, while the even part $h^+$ remains mysterious and is the subject of Vandiver's conjecture.
