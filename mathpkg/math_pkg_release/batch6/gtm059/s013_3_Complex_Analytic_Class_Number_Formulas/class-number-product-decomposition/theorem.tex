Let $K$ be an abelian extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ with Galois group $G$, $N = [K : \mathbb{Q}]$, and let $K^+$ denote the maximal real subfield. Let $h = h_K$, $h^+ = h_{K^+}$, and similarly $R$, $R^+$, $w$, $w^+$, $d$, $d^+$. Define:

$$h^- = h / h^+, \quad R^- = R / R^+, \quad Q = (E : W E^+)$$

where $E$ is the unit group of $K$, $E^+$ the unit group of $K^+$, and $W$ the group of roots of unity in $K$.

Then the following product expressions hold:

1. If $K$ is real:
   $$h R = \prod_{\chi \neq 1} \left( -\frac{1}{2} B_{1,\chi} \right)$$

2. If $K$ is imaginary, the decomposition involves the partition into even and odd characters:
   $$h^- = Q \cdot w \cdot \prod_{\chi \text{ odd}} \left( -\frac{1}{2} B_{1,\chi} \right)$$

where $B_{1,\chi}$ denotes the generalized Bernoulli number attached to the character $\chi$, and the products run over non-trivial primitive characters of $G$ (or over odd characters, respectively).
