The Analytic Class Number Formula expresses the residue of the Dedekind zeta function $\zeta_K(s)$ at $s = 1$ in terms of fundamental arithmetic invariants of the number field $K$: the class number $h_K$, the regulator $R_K$, the discriminant $d_K$, and the number of roots of unity $w_K$. For an abelian extension $K/\mathbb{Q}$, the zeta function factorizes as a product of Dirichlet $L$-functions, yielding the classical form that relates $L(1, \chi)$ for non-trivial characters $\chi$ to these arithmetic quantities. This formula is the foundation for $p$-adic analogues developed by Leopoldt and Iwasawa.
