Stickelberger's theorem provides the fundamental congruence governing the prime ideal factorization of Gauss sums in cyclotomic fields. It expresses the Gauss sum $S(k) = \tau(\omega^{-k})$ modulo a prime ideal $\mathfrak{P}$ above $p$ in terms of the Gauss sum of the trivial exponent, with the exponent controlled by the $p$-adic digit sum $s_p(k)$. This result is the cornerstone for studying the Stickelberger ideal, which annihilates the ideal class group of cyclotomic fields, and it leads to deep connections with Bernoulli numbers and $p$-adic $L$-functions.
