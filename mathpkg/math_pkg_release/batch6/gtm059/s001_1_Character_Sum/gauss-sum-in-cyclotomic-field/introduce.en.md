The Gauss sum $\tau(\chi)$ of a multiplicative character $\chi$ on a finite field, although defined using complex $p$-th roots of unity, lies in a much smaller cyclotomic field when raised to an appropriate power. This theorem shows that if $\chi$ has order $m$, then $\tau(\chi)^m$ belongs to $\mathbf{Q}(\zeta_m)$, the $m$-th cyclotomic field, rather than the larger field $\mathbf{Q}(\zeta_p, \zeta_m)$. The proof proceeds by checking Galois invariance under automorphisms $\sigma_{ij}$ that act on the roots of unity.
