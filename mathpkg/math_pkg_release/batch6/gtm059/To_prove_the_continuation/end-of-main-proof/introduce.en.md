This theorem concludes the proof of the explicit reciprocity law for the symbol $(x, w_n)$ in the Lubin-Tate theory of local class field theory. Under sufficient lower bounds on the parameter $k$, the generalized bracket symbol $[x, w_n]$ is shown to lie in a high power of the ideal $i$, and the Hilbert symbol equals $[x_n, y_n]_B(x)$. The proof reduces the general case to previously established cases: when $x$ is a power of $n$, and when $x$ is a unit. The collection of Theorems 5.1 through 7.1 together constitute the full explicit reciprocity law for the Lubin-Tate formal group, generalizing the classical Artin-Hasse reciprocity law for the multiplicative group.
