This theorem establishes the equality between the Hilbert symbol $(x, n_i)$ and the universal norm symbol $[x, n_i]_{\kappa}(x)$ in the context of Lubin-Tate formal groups. The equality holds under two separate sufficient conditions on the element $x$ and the parameter $m$, which governs the level of the Lubin-Tate tower. The first condition requires $x$ to lie in the field $\kappa(n_i)$ obtained by adjoining $n_i$-torsion points, with a bound $m \ge 2n+1$. The second relaxes the field condition to $\kappa(2n^2 + n_i)$ but imposes only $m \ge n$. This is one of the central results in the explicit reciprocity law for Lubin-Tate formal groups.
