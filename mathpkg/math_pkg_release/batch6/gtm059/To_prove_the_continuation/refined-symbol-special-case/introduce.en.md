This theorem provides a refinement of Theorem 5.1 for the special case where the Lubin-Tate formal group law $F(X)$ is a polynomial of degree $n$ with leading coefficient $1$. In this situation, the symbol $[x_n]$ can be defined more precisely using the derivative $F_X$ and the universal norm power series $Y_n(X)$. The result is the equality $(x - x_n) = [x - x_n]_a$ for all $x$ in the domain of definition $d(X)$. This refined formulation leads to a more precise reciprocity law, illustrated by the example of the formal multiplicative group where one recovers the classical Artin-Hasse reciprocity law.
