The identity $J(\chi, \psi) = S(\chi)S(\psi)/S(\chi\psi)$ (for $\chi\psi \neq 1$) is the fundamental bridge between Jacobi sums and Gauss sums. It allows algebraic properties of Jacobi sums (such as their prime ideal factorization) to be deduced from those of Gauss sums, and is essential for Stickelberger's theorem on the ideal class group of cyclotomic fields.
