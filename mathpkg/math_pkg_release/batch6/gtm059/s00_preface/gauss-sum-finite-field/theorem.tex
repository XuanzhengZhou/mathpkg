Let $F = \mathbb{F}_q$ be a finite field with $q = p^n$ elements. Fix a non-trivial additive character $\lambda: F \to \mathbb{C}^\times$. For a multiplicative character $\chi: F^* \to \mathbb{C}^\times$ extended to $F$ by setting $\chi(0) = 0$ (if $\chi \neq 1$) or $\chi(0) = 1$ (if $\chi = 1$), the Gauss sum $S(\chi)$ is defined by

$$S(\chi) = \sum_{x \in F^*} \chi(x) \lambda(x).$$

Equivalently, $S(\chi) = \sum_{x \in F} \chi(x) \lambda(x)$ under the extension convention.
