Property GS4 states that Gauss sums are invariant under the Frobenius automorphism: $S(\chi^p) = S(\chi)$. This follows because raising to the $p$-th power is an automorphism of the finite field $F$, preserving the trace: $\operatorname{Tr}(x^p) = \operatorname{Tr}(x)$. Hence $S(\chi^p)$ is obtained from $S(\chi)$ by merely permuting the elements of $F$ under $x \mapsto x^p$, leaving the sum unchanged.
