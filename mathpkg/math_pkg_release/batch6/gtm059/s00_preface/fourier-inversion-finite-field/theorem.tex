Let $F = \mathbb{F}_q$ be a finite field and $\lambda$ a fixed non-trivial additive character. Let $T$ be the Fourier transform defined by $Tf(y) = \sum_{x \in F} f(x)\lambda(-xy)$. Define $f^{-}(x) = f(-x)$. Then

$$TTf = f^{-}.$$

Explicitly, for any $x \in F$,

$$\sum_{y \in F} \sum_{z \in F} f(z) \lambda(-yz) \lambda(-xy) = f(-x).$$
