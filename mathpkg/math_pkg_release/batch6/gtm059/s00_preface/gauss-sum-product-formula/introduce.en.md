The product formula $S(\chi)S(\chi^{-1}) = \chi(-1)q$ is one of the most important identities for Gauss sums. It implies that over the complex numbers, $|S(\chi)| = \sqrt{q}$, showing that Gauss sums have a well-defined absolute value. The proof uses the Fourier transform of a multiplicative character: evaluating $T\chi \cdot T\chi^{-1} = T(\chi * \chi^{-1})$ at $0$ yields the result.
