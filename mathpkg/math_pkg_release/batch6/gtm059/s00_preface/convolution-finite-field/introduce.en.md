The convolution of two functions $f, g: F \to \mathbb{C}$ on a finite field $F$ is defined by $(f * g)(x) = \sum_{y \in F} f(y)g(x-y)$. Convolution is commutative and serves as the multiplicative structure that the Fourier transform converts to pointwise multiplication, a key property used throughout the theory of character sums.
