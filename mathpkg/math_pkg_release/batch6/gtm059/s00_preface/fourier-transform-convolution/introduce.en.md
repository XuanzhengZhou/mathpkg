Theorem 1.2 establishes that the Fourier transform on a finite field converts convolution to pointwise multiplication: $T(f * g) = Tf \cdot Tg$. This is the discrete analogue of the classical Fourier transform property on $\mathbb{R}$. A dual formula $T(fg) = \frac{1}{q} Tf * Tg$ follows by applying the inversion theorem since $T$ is an isomorphism.
