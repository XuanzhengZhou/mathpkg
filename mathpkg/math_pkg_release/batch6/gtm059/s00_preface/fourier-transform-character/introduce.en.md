This proposition shows that the Fourier transform of a multiplicative character $\chi$ (extended by $\chi(0)=0$ for non-trivial $\chi$) evaluates to $T\chi(y) = \chi(y^{-1})S(\chi)$ for $y \neq 0$ and vanishes at $y=0$. It is a direct computation using the change of variables $x \mapsto -y^{-1}x$ in the definition of $T\chi$, and provides a key link between the Fourier transform and Gauss sums.
