The Kummer pairing for Lubin-Tate formal groups generalizes the classical Kummer pairing from the multiplicative group to arbitrary one-dimensional formal groups over local fields. Given a Lubin-Tate formal group $A$ and a uniformizer $\pi$, the pairing associates to an element $x$ in the formal group evaluated on the maximal ideal and an element $a \in K^{*}$ an element in the torsion subgroup $A[\pi^n]$. The construction uses the reciprocity automorphism $r_a$ (defined earlier in this section) to measure the failure of $t$ (a $\pi^n$-th root of $x$ in the formal group) to be fixed by $r_a$. When specialized to the formal multiplicative group, this pairing recovers the classical norm residue (Hilbert) symbol, establishing that the Lubin-Tate formalism provides a unified framework for local class field theory.
