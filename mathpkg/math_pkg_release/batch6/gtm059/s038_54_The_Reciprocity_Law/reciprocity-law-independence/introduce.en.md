This theorem, often called the Reciprocity Law in local class field theory, establishes that the maximal abelian extension of a local field $K$ constructed via Lubin-Tate formal groups is independent of the choice of uniformizer $\pi$. The theorem defines the reciprocity automorphism $r_a$ for each $a \in K^*$ by specifying its action on the unramified part (via the Frobenius automorphism $\varphi$) and on the totally ramified Lubin-Tate extension (via the formal group endomorphism $[u^{-1}]_f$). The key assertion is that the map $a \mapsto r_a$ is canonical, i.e., it does not depend on the auxiliary choice of uniformizer used in the Lubin-Tate construction. This independence is the cornerstone of the local reciprocity map and provides an explicit description of the Galois group of the maximal abelian extension.
