The proof parallels that of Theorem 5.5 with the sign character incorporated. One writes $R = R^+ \oplus R^-$ (decomposition into even and odd parts under $\sigma_{-1}$). On the odd part $R^-$, the Stickelberger element acts via the twisted Bernoulli distribution, and the index factors through the odd characters.

Key steps: (1) Lemma establishing $R = 2R^- \cap R$ and index relations. (2) Reduction to determinant computations on the odd component. (3) Factorization via character decomposition, yielding the product over odd $\chi$ of $B_{k,\chi}/k$.

The additional factor of $2^{\varphi(N)/2}$ from the lemma is absorbed into the normalization, and the result follows from linear algebra over the group ring as in the even case.
