For the Bernoulli polynomials $B_k(X)$, the following distribution relation holds for
any positive integer $N$:

$$B_k(X) = N^{k-1} \sum_{a=0}^{N-1} B_k\!\left(\frac{X + a}{N}\right).$$

Equivalently, for $y \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$,

$$N^{k-1} \sum_{a=0}^{N-1} B_k\!\left(\left\langle y + \frac{a}{N} \right\rangle\right) = B_k(\langle N y \rangle),$$

where $\langle x \rangle$ denotes the fractional part of $x$.
