The universal distribution theorem constructs the "free" distribution for a prime level $N$. The primitive elements (those of exact period $N$) generate an abelian group with a single linear relation, and this group carries the universal distribution. This result provides a lower bound for the rank of spaces of distributions and shows that the Bernoulli distribution attains the maximal possible rank, establishing it as a "universal" odd distribution.
