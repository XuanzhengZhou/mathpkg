The first statement follows from analyzing the distribution relation on the projective system. For prime level $N$, the only non-trivial distribution relation is that the sum over all elements of level $N$ equals the element at level $1$, giving the single relation $\sum g(a) = 0$.

For (ii), one constructs $\varphi$ by mapping an element $x \in \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ of exact order $M$ to $\sum_{b} g(b)$ where $b$ ranges over the primitive elements above $x$ at level $N$. The distribution relation ensures well-definedness. The universality follows from the fact that any distribution $\psi$ on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ gives rise to a homomorphism from $A$ by sending $g(a)$ to $\psi(a)$.
