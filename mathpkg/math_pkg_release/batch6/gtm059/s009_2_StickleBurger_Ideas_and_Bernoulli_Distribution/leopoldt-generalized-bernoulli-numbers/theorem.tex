Let $\chi$ be an even Dirichlet character of conductor $f_\chi$. The \textbf{Leopoldt
generalized Bernoulli number} $B_{k,\chi}$ is defined by:

$$B_{k,\chi} = f_\chi^{k-1} \sum_{a=1}^{f_\chi} \chi(a) B_k\!\left(\frac{a}{f_\chi}\right),$$

where $B_k(X)$ is the $k$-th Bernoulli polynomial. Equivalently, using the Stickelberger
element notation:

$$\frac{1}{k} B_{k,\chi} = \chi(\theta) = \frac{1}{f_\chi} \sum_{a=1}^{f_\chi} \chi(a) a.$$

For the trivial character $\chi = 1$, one has $B_{k,1} = B_k$ (with the Euler factor at
primes dividing the conductor removed).
