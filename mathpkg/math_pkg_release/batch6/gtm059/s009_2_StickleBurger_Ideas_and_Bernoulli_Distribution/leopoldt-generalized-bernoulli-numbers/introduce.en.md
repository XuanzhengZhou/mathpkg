Leopoldt's generalized Bernoulli numbers $B_{k,\chi}$ for even Dirichlet characters $\chi$ are the algebraic counterpart of special values of Dirichlet $L$-functions via the formula $L(1-k, \chi) = -B_{k,\chi}/k$. When applied to the characters of the Galois group of a cyclotomic field, the numbers $B_{1,\chi}$ appear in the Stickelberger element and govern the annihilation of ideal class groups. The first generalized Bernoulli numbers $B_{1,\chi}$ are particularly important, as the Stickelberger ideal index theorem expresses $(R:S)$ as their product.
