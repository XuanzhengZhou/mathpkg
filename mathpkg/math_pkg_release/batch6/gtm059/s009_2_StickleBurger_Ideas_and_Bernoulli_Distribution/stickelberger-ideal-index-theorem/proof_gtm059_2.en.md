The proof proceeds by analyzing a sequence of groups and subgroups relating the Stickelberger ideal to the group ring. One considers the determinant of multiplication maps on the group ring, decomposing according to characters. The computation reduces to evaluating determinants which, by standard linear algebra over group rings, yield the product of the generalized Bernoulli numbers $B_{1,\chi}$. The index is then given by the absolute value of this determinant.

More precisely, one considers the $\mathbb{Z}[G]$-module structure and uses the fact that the Stickelberger ideal $S$ is defined as $R \cap \theta R$, where $\theta$ is the Stickelberger element. By decomposing the group ring according to idempotents associated to characters, the index factors as a product over characters, and each factor equals the corresponding generalized Bernoulli number $B_{1,\chi}$.
