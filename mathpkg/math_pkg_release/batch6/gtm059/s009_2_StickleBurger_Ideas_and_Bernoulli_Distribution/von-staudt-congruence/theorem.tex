Let $k$ be an even positive integer with $k \equiv 0 \pmod{p-1}$. Then

$$B_k \equiv -\frac{1}{p} \pmod{\mathbb{Z}_p},$$

i.e., $p B_k \equiv -1 \pmod{p \mathbb{Z}_p}$. More generally, for any even $k \geq 2$,

$$B_k + \sum_{(p-1) \mid k} \frac{1}{p} \in \mathbb{Z},$$

where the sum is taken over all primes $p$ such that $p-1$ divides $k$.
