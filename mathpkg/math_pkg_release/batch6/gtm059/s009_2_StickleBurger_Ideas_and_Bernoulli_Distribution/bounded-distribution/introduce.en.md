A bounded distribution is one whose values are uniformly bounded in the non-Archimedean sense. This boundedness condition ensures that the integral of any continuous function against the distribution converges. Bounded distributions are also called Bernoulli distributions in the context of cyclotomic fields, as the Bernoulli polynomials give rise to the fundamental examples of such distributions.
