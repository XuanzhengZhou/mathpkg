Let $K$ be a complete non-Archimedean valued field, and let $\varphi$ be a distribution
on the projective system $\{X_n\}$ with values in $K$. Then $\varphi$ is called
\textbf{bounded} (or a \textbf{Bernoulli distribution}) if the values
$|\varphi_n(x)|$ are bounded independently of $n$ and $x \in X_n$.

For a bounded distribution $\varphi$ and a continuous function $f$ on
$X = \varprojlim X_n$, the integral

$$\int_X f \, d\varphi = \lim_{n \to \infty} \int_X f_n \, d\varphi$$

converges, where $f_n$ is a sequence of locally constant functions approximating $f$
uniformly.
