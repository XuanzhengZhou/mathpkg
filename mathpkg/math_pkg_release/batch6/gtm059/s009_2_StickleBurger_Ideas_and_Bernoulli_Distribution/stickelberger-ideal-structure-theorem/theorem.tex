Let $p$ be a prime and $N = p^n$. Then:

(i) $S \cap R = J^{(p)}$, where $J^{(p)}$ is the $p$-component of the integral
Stickelberger ideal.

(ii) $S + R = R$ (the Stickelberger ideal together with the group ring generates
the full ring).

More precisely, if an element $\alpha \in R$ satisfies $\theta \alpha \in R$ (i.e.,
$\alpha \in S$), then $\alpha \in J^{(p)}$. Conversely, $J^{(p)} \subseteq S$.
