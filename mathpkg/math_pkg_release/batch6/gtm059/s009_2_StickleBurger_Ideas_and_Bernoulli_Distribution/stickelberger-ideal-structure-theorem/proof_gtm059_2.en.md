The proof proceeds in several steps using properties of Bernoulli polynomials:

Step 1: Show that $J^{(p)} \subseteq S$. This uses the explicit formula for the Stickelberger element and the integrality properties of the regularized Bernoulli distribution. For any element $\sum x_a \sigma_a \in J^{(p)}$, one verifies that multiplication by $\theta$ yields an element of $R$ by analyzing the $p$-adic valuations.

Step 2: Show that $S \subseteq J^{(p)}$. If $\alpha \in S$, then $\theta \alpha \in R$. By examining the action on characters and using the distribution relation, one deduces the congruence condition $\sum x_a a \equiv 0 \pmod{N}$ that characterizes $J^{(p)}$.

The proof relies on the lemmas establishing that the Bernoulli distribution, after regularization, takes $p$-integral values, and that the indices can be computed via determinants of multiplication maps on the group ring.
