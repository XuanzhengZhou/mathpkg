Generalized Bernoulli polynomials extend the classical Bernoulli polynomials by incorporating a twisting function $f$ on $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$. When $f$ is a Dirichlet character, the resulting generalized Bernoulli numbers $B_{k,\chi}$ are the Leopoldt generalized Bernoulli numbers, which play a central role in Iwasawa theory and the study of $L$-functions. They express special values of Dirichlet $L$-functions and appear in the Stickelberger element.
