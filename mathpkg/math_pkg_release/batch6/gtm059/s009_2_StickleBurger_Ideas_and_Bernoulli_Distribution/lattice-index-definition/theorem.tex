Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional vector space over $\mathbb{Q}$, and let $A, B$ be two
lattices in $V$ (finitely generated $\mathbb{Z}$-submodules that span $V$ over $\mathbb{Q}$).
Let $C$ be a lattice containing both $A$ and $B$. The \textbf{index} $(A : B)$ is defined as

$$(A : B) = \frac{|C/B|}{|C/A|}.$$

This is independent of the choice of $C$ and satisfies:

$$(A : B)(B : C) = (A : C), \quad (A : B) = \frac{(A : D)}{(B : D)}$$

for any lattice $D$ contained in both $A$ and $B$. If $A \subseteq B$, then $(A : B) = |B/A|$.
If $\theta \in \mathbb{Q}[G]$ acts on $R = \mathbb{Z}[G]$, then $(R : \theta R) = |\det(\theta)|$,
where the determinant is taken with respect to a $\mathbb{Z}$-basis of $R$.
