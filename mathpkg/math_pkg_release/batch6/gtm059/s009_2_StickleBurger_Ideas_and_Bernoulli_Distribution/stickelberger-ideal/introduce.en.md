The Stickelberger ideal is a fundamental object in the study of cyclotomic fields. It is an ideal in the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}[G]$ of the Galois group $G = \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)/\mathbb{Q})$, constructed from the Stickelberger element, which encodes fractional parts of rational numbers. The Stickelberger ideal annihilates the ideal class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$, making it a central tool in proving results about class numbers and the structure of cyclotomic units.
