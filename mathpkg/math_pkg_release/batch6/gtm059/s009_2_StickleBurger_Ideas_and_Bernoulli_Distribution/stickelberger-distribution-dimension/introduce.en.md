This theorem computes the dimension of the space of Stickelberger distributions as a module over the group ring. The dimension formula relates the structure of distributions to the primitive elements at each level. A crucial consequence is the characterization of when a character $\chi$ yields a non-trivial distribution: this happens precisely when the associated Stickelberger sum $S(\chi)$ vanishes. This vanishing criterion determines the rank of the universal distribution.
