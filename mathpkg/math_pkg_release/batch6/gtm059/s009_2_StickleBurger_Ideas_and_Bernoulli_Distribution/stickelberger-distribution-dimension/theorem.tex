Let $N$ be a positive integer and let $G = (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^\times$. Let
$V$ be the space of distributions on the projective system of level $N$ with values
in a field $F$ of characteristic $0$. Then the dimension of $V$ as an $F[G]$-module
is equal to the number of connected components of the set of primitive elements, or
equivalently,

$$\dim_F V = \sum_{d \mid N} \varphi(d),$$

where $\varphi$ is Euler's totient function. Moreover, the eigenspace for a character
$\chi$ of $G$ has dimension $1$ if the conductor of $\chi$ equals $N$, and dimension
depending on the number of prime factors of $N$ relative to the conductor otherwise.
