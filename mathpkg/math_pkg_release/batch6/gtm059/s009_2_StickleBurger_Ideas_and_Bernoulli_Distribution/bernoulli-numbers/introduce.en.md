Bernoulli numbers are a sequence of rational numbers that appear throughout number theory, particularly in formulas for sums of powers, the Riemann zeta function at integer arguments, and the theory of cyclotomic fields. They are defined via the exponential generating function $t/(e^t - 1)$. Their arithmetic properties, such as the von Staudt-Clausen theorem and Kummer congruences, are fundamental to the study of Stickelberger ideals and class numbers of cyclotomic fields.
