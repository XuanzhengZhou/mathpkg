This theorem establishes that the Bernoulli distribution achieves the theoretically maximal rank for an odd distribution. The nonvanishing of the Stickelberger sums $S_k(\chi)$ for odd characters implies that the Stickelberger distribution is "universal" in the sense of providing a full-rank lattice in the space of all distributions. This result is the culmination of the distribution-theoretic approach to Stickelberger ideals.
