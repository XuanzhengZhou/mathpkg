Let $g: \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \to A$ be an ordinary distribution with values in an
abelian group $A$. For each level $N$, define $g_N: (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^k \to A$
by

$$g_N(a_1, \ldots, a_k) = \sum_{x \in \mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}} g(x) \prod_{j=1}^{k} \delta_{x \equiv a_j \pmod{N}},$$

where $\delta$ is the Kronecker delta. Then the family $\{g_N\}$ defines a distribution
on the projective system $\{(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^k\}$, called the
\textbf{Stickelberger distribution} associated to $g$.
