This theorem shows how to construct a Stickelberger distribution on the $k$-fold product $(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^k$ starting from an ordinary distribution $g$ on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$. The construction uses the Bernoulli polynomial as a kernel. The resulting distribution encodes Stickelberger elements and provides a flexible framework for studying the action of these elements on various modules arising in cyclotomic fields.
