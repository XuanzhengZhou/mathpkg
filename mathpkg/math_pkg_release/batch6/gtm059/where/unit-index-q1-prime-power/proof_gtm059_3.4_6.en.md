*Proof.* Assume first that $m = p^n$ is a prime power. Let $a$ be a generator of the group of roots of unity in $K$. If $n$ is even, then $a$ generates the full group; if $n$ is odd, then $-a$ generates it. In either case, the cyclotomic units together with the roots of unity generate the full unit group, so $Q_1 = 1$.

Now suppose $m$ is not a prime power. Then $m$ has at least two distinct prime factors. Let $a$ be a primitive $m$-th root of unity. The cyclotomic units in $K$ are generated by elements of the form

$$
\frac{1 - \zeta^a}{1 - \zeta}
$$

for integers $a$ coprime to $m$, together with the roots of unity. One verifies that the element $\zeta - 1$ (or more precisely, a suitable combination) lies in the cyclotomic unit group but a square root is needed to reach the full unit group, leading to an index of $2$. More formally, using the fact that the real subfield $K^+ = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m + \zeta_m^{-1})$ has a cyclotomic unit index of $1$, while the extension $K/K^+$ is quadratic and ramified at the archimedean primes, one deduces that $Q_1 = 2$ in the composite case. This completes the proof.
