For an imaginary abelian extension $K$ of $\mathbb{Q}$, the norm map from the ideal class group $C_K$ to the class group of the maximal real subfield $C_{K^+}$ is injective. This is a fundamental result in Iwasawa theory, proved using class field theory. The proof relies on the fact that the extension $K/K^+$ is ramified at the archimedean primes, which prevents the Hilbert class field of $K^+$ from intersecting $K$ non-trivially. The theorem implies that the minus part $C_K^-$ of the ideal class group embeds faithfully into $C_K$.
