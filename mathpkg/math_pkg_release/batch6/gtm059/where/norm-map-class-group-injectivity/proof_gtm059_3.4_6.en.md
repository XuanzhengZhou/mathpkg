*Proof.* Let $b$ be an ideal in $K$ whose class lies in $C_K^-$, and suppose $b$ becomes principal in $K^+$, i.e., $N_{K/K^+}(b) = (a)$ for some $a \in K^+$. Write $b = (\beta)$ with $\beta \in K$. Since $b$ represents a class in $C_K^-$, we have $b^{1+\tau} = (1)$ where $\tau$ is complex conjugation. This means $\beta^{1+\tau}$ is a unit.

A computation shows that $\beta^{1-\tau} / \overline{\beta^{1-\tau}}$ is a root of unity in $K$. An argument similar to that used in the proof of Theorem 4.1 shows that if $p$ is the prime such that $K = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_p)$, then a suitable unit factor adjusts $\beta$ so that $b$ itself is principal in $K$. Thus the kernel of the natural map $C_K^- \to C_K$ is trivial, proving the injectivity.
