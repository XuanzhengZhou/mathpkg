This theorem states that for an imaginary abelian extension $K$ of $\mathbb{Q}$, the norm map from the ideal class group of the maximal real subfield $K^+$ to the ideal class group of $K$ is injective. The proof uses class field theory: the Hilbert class field of $K^+$ cannot intersect $K$ nontrivially because $K/K^+$ is ramified at the archimedean primes. This result is fundamental for understanding the structure of ideal class groups in cyclotomic fields, particularly the relationship between the plus and minus eigenspaces under complex conjugation.
