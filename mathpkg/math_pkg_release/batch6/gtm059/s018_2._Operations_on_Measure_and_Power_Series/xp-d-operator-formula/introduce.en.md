This lemma relates the function $x_p(X)$ to the action of the D-operator $D = (1+X)D_p$ on power series. The key identity expresses $x_p(x)$ in terms of the limit $x = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{(1+X)^n - 1}{n}$, which is the p-adic logarithm. The proof uses a limiting argument together with Taylor's formula, reducing the integral of a step function against $x_p$ to an evaluation of the D-operator at the origin. This result bridges the p-adic L-function theory with the differential calculus of Iwasawa power series.
