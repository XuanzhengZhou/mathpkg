We compute trivially the Fourier expansion of $g$:
$$\phi(X) = \frac{1}{p} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \zeta^{n} = \left[ \frac{-1}{p} \right] \frac{1}{1-p} = 1.$$

Then Lemma 3 gives
$$g(X) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \phi(G(I+1) - X) = U(X),$$
as was to be shown.

**Remark.** Let $A$ be the formal multiplicative group (of Chapter 8). In the notation of each group, we can write the unitization operation in the form
$$U(X) = f(X) - \frac{1}{p} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} f(X+1).$$
