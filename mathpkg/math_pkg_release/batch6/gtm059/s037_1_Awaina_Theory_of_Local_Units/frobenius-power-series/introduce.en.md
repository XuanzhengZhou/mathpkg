A Frobenius power series over a complete discrete valuation ring is a power series that begins with the uniformizer $\pi$ as its linear term and whose higher coefficients lie in the maximal ideal. These series serve as the input data for the Lubin-Tate construction: each Frobenius power series determines a unique formal group law for which it is an endomorphism. The prototypical example over $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is $f(X) = (1+X)^p - 1$, which corresponds to the multiplicative group and yields the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension.
