Let $\mathfrak{o}$ be a complete discrete valuation ring with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$, uniformizer $\pi$, and residue field of cardinality $q = |\mathfrak{o}/\mathfrak{m}|$. A \textbf{Frobenius power series} (or \textbf{special power series}) is a power series $f(X) \in \mathfrak{o}[[X]]$ satisfying
$$
f(X) = \pi X + \text{higher degree terms}, \qquad f(X) \equiv X^q \pmod{\pi}.
$$
Equivalently, $f(X)$ is an element of the set $\mathcal{F}_\pi$ of power series with leading coefficient $\pi$ and all non-leading coefficients in $\mathfrak{m}$. The standard example is $f(X) = (1+X)^p - 1$ when $\mathfrak{o} = \mathbb{Z}_p$ and $\pi = p$.
