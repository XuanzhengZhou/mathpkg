The formal multiplicative group is the simplest and most important example of a formal group. Its group law $X+Y+XY$ is the formal analog of the ordinary multiplicative group law $(1+x)(1+y)-1$, so that the shift $x \mapsto 1+x$ converts the formal addition into ordinary multiplication. Over the $p$-adic integers $\mathbb{Z}_p$, the Frobenius power series $f(X) = (1+X)^p - 1$ is an endomorphism of $G_m$, giving the Lubin-Tate formal group that produces the cyclotomic $p$-extensions.
