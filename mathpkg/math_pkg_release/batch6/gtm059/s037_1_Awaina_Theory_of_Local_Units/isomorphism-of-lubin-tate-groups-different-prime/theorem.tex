Let $\mathfrak{o}$ be a complete discrete valuation ring, and let $f_1 \in \mathcal{F}_{\pi_1}$, $f_2 \in \mathcal{F}_{\pi_2}$ be Frobenius power series associated with uniformizers $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$ such that $\pi_1 / \pi_2$ is a unit in $\mathfrak{o}^*$. Then there exists a \textbf{unique} isomorphism of formal groups
$$
\theta: F_{f_1} \xrightarrow{\cong} F_{f_2}
$$
defined over $\mathfrak{o}$ with $\theta(X) \equiv \varepsilon X \pmod{\deg 2}$ where $\varepsilon$ is a unit satisfying $\varepsilon^{q-1} = \pi_1/\pi_2$, and such that $\theta$ commutes with the endomorphism actions:
$$
\theta([a]_{f_1}(X)) = [a]_{f_2}(\theta(X)) \qquad \text{for all } a \in \mathfrak{o}.
$$
In particular, $[a]_{f_2}(\theta(X)) = \theta([a]_{f_1}(X))$ for all $a \in \mathfrak{o}$.
