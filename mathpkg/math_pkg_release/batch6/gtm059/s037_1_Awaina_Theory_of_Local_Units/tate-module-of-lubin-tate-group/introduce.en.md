The Tate module $T_\pi(A)$ packages all the finite-level torsion information into a single $\mathfrak{o}$-module via the projective limit. An element of $T_\pi(A)$ is an infinite compatible sequence $(w_n)$ with $w_n \in A_n$ and $[\pi](w_{n+1}) = w_n$. As an $\mathfrak{o}$-module, $T_\pi(A)$ is free of rank $1$, and the Galois group acts through a character $\chi$ into $\mathfrak{o}^*$. This is the Lubin-Tate analog of the $p$-adic Tate module of the multiplicative group $T_p(G_m) = \varprojlim \mu_{p^n}$, whose Galois action gives the $p$-adic cyclotomic character.
