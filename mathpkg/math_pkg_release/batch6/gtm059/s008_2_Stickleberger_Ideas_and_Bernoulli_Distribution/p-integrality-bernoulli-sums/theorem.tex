Let $p^e$ be the smallest power of $p$ such that $p^e N^e$ is $p$-integral. Then

$$
p \sum_{i=1}^{k-1} \binom{n}{i} \binom{n}{i-1} p^{i-1} = p\text{-integral}.
$$

Moreover, the Bernoulli numbers $B_i$ are $p$-integral for $i < p$ (by the Kummer congruences), and for $i \geq p$, the factor $N^{i-1}$ in front of the Bernoulli polynomial term makes

$$
\frac{N^{i-1}}{N} p^{i-1}
$$

$p$-integral. Consequently, $I \cap \mathbb{Z} = (p^e)$, where $I$ denotes the Stickelberger ideal.
