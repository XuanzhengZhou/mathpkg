Let $R(X)$ be the operator defined on the polynomial ring $\mathbb{Q}[X]$ by the Bernoulli polynomial action. For an element $\zeta = \sum_{i=0}^{n} a_i R(X)^i$ with rational coefficients $a_i$, the sum

$$
S^n = N^n - \sum_{i=0}^{n} a_i R(X) \frac{1}{k}[R_X]_{i=0}^{n-1} r_i^n
$$

is integral. Moreover, for an integer $b$,

$$
\frac{N^{n-1}}{k} R_X\!\left(\frac{1}{k}\right) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \binom{n}{i} B_i b^{n-i}.
$$

Here $R_X$ denotes the operator evaluated at $X$, and $r_i$ are the coefficients arising from the expansion.
