The space generated by the elements $u(\chi)$ with $\chi \in \mathcal{G}_Z(N)$ is clearly a $G(N)$-module. The idempotent associated with $\chi$ is the usual $e_\chi = e(\chi)$; then $G(N) \sum_{i=1}^{n} h(i)v(\chi)$ spans this space.

If $M$ is the conductor of $\chi$, then

$$
\frac{1}{M} \left(\frac{1}{u(\chi)}\right) v_\chi = S_Z(\chi) \frac{1}{h(\chi)} v_\chi
$$

as one sees from the fact that the value depends only on the residue class modulo $M$. Hence $S_Z(\chi) = 0$ characterizes exactly when the action is non-trivial. The connectivity claim follows by analyzing the kernel of the reduction map $G(N) \to G(M)$.
