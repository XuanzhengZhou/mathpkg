This theorem establishes that the Stickelberger distribution is non-degenerate: it does not vanish identically and in fact has maximal rank among all odd distributions. This non-vanishing is crucial for applications to class groups, as it guarantees that the Stickelberger ideal is non-trivial and contains enough information to control the class group.
