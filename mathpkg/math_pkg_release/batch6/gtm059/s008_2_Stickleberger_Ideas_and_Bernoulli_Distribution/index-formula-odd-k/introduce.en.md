This theorem handles the odd (twisted) case of the Stickelberger ideal index. Unlike the even case, the index formula involves an additional factor of $p^{n+1}$ and a different combination of Bernoulli numbers. The proof requires a more delicate analysis using the structure of the group ring at the prime 2.
