Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional vector space over $\mathbb{Q}$. Let $A$ and $B$ be two lattices in $V$ (i.e., finitely generated $\mathbb{Z}$-submodules spanning $V$ over $\mathbb{Q}$). Let $C$ be a lattice containing both $A$ and $B$. The **index** $(A : B)$ is defined by

$$
(A : B) = \frac{[C : A]}{[C : B]},
$$

where $[C : A]$ denotes the usual group index. This is independent of the choice of $C$ and satisfies the multiplicative property

$$
(A : B)(B : D) = (A : D).
$$

If $E$ is a lattice contained in both $A$ and $B$, then

$$
(A : B) = \frac{(A : E)}{(B : E)}.
$$

When $A$ is an algebra over $\mathbb{Z}$ and $\theta \in \mathbb{Q}A$, then

$$
(A : A\theta) = \lvert\det(\theta)\rvert,
$$

where the determinant is that of the linear transformation of $\mathbb{Q}A$ given by multiplication by $\theta$.
