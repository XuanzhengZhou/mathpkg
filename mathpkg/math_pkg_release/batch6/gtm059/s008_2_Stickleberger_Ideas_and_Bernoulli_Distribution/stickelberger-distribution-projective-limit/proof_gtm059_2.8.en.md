One passes to the projective limit by defining, for each level $N$, the value $g_N(a)$ and verifying the distribution relation. The transition maps from level $M$ to level $N$ (when $N \mid M$) are given by the trace map in the group algebra, which sums over the fibers of the natural surjection $G(M) \to G(N)$. The distribution relation ensures that these maps are compatible, yielding the projective limit. The isomorphism $A_h(A_h) = A_h(A_h)(A_h)$ follows by passing to the limit and using the universal property of the projective limit.
