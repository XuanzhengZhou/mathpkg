The Stickelberger distribution $g$ on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ can be expressed as a projective limit:

$$
g : \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z} \longrightarrow \varprojlim_{N} A[G(N)],
$$

where the inverse limit is taken over the group algebras $A[G(N)]$ ordered by divisibility, with the transition maps given by the trace (sum over fibers) under the canonical projection $G(M) \to G(N)$ for $N \mid M$.

Consequently, the Stickelberger distribution gives rise to an isomorphism

$$
A_h(A_h) = A_h(A_h)(A_h)
$$

compatible with the action of the Stickelberger element.
