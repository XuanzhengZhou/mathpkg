The first statement is immediate from the distribution relation. Let us prove (ii). We have
$$\sum_{u \neq p} g(p, u)(a) = \sum_{u \neq p} g(p, u)\left(\frac{1}{a}\right).$$

By the distribution relation, we know that
$$\frac{n(u)}{u} = \sum_{u \neq p} \left(\frac{1}{a}\right) = \sum_{u \neq p} \left(\frac{1}{a}\right) + \sum_{u \neq p} \left(\frac{1}{a}\right).$$

The elements $a, p \in \mathbb{Z}(N)$ that are not primitive but are in bijection with the element $a$ are simply called the condition $a = p$ and $e = p^a \bmod M$, under the map $c \mapsto pc$ which sends $\mathbb{Z}(N)p$ into $p\mathbb{Z}(N)\mathbb{Z}(N)$. By the distribution relation, plugging this in and making a change of variable $w = k_0$, we find
$$S_{k_0} h_0 = S_{k_0} h_0 - \sum_{i=0}^{k_0} h_i f\left(\frac{k}{k_0}\right) = (1 - z) f\left(\frac{k}{k_0}\right) S_{k_0} h_0.$$

This concludes the proof of the lemma.
