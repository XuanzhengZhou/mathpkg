A distribution can be decomposed as the sum of an odd and an even part, provided that multiplication by 2 is invertible in the module of values.

The distribution arising from the Bernoulli polynomial $B_k(X)$ is shown to be non-vanishing for all $k$, and $h$ has the maximal attainable rank for an odd distribution. The proof proceeds by analyzing the distribution relation and the rank of the value space on the cyclotomic character group, establishing maximality through the structure of the universal distribution.

In this theorem $k$ has been proved only for distributions with values in the appropriate module, with the arguments based on the Kubert-Lang theory of universal distributions.
