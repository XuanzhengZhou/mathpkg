Let $V$ be a finite dimensional vector space over the rationals, and let $A, B$ be two lattices in $V$. Let $C$ be a lattice containing both. The generalized index is defined by:
$$(A : B) = \frac{|C/B|}{|C/A|}.$$
It is independent of the choice of $C$ and satisfies the multiplicative property:
$$(A : D : B) = (A : B).$$
Furthermore, if $E$ is a lattice contained in both $A$ and $B$, then
$$(A : B) = \frac{(A : E)}{(B : E)}.$$
