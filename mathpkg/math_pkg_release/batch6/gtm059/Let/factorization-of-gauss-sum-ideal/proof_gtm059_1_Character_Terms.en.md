We have

$$\operatorname{ord}_{\mathfrak{p}}(S_{\omega(\mathfrak{a})}) = \operatorname{ord}_{\mathfrak{p}} e_{S_{\omega(\mathfrak{a})}} = \operatorname{ord}_{\mathfrak{p}} S_{\omega(\mathfrak{a})} = \operatorname{ord}_{\mathfrak{p}}(\omega(\mathfrak{a}))$$

by Theorem 2.1. On the other hand, the isotropy group of $\omega$ in the Galois group $G$ consists of the powers $\{\omega_k\}$ for $k = 0, \dots, n-1$. Hence in the ideal $\mathfrak{p}^{\rho(\omega)}$ the prime $\omega(\mathfrak{a})$ occurs with multiplicity

$$\sum_{k=0}^{n-1} \frac{\omega_k}{q^{k-1}}.$$

The proof is completed by Lemma 1, which establishes the required formula for $t(k)$.
