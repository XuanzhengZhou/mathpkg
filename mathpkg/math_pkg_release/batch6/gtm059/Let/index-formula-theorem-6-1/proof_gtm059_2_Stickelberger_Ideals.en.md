The Stickelberger ideal in this case is $R \cap R = R$ and is odd. The rest of the section is devoted to the proof.

First we write
$$(R : R) = \left( \frac{r: R}{r: R} \right) B_{n,i}$$
and then
$$(r: R: x: R) = (r: R: x: R) B_{n,i} B_{n,i} B_{n,i} B_{n,i} = \prod_{i=1}^{n} B_{n,i} R : r : R)$$
because $R$ is invertible in $R[G]$. The proof proceeds by analyzing the index step by step as in the even case, with modifications for the odd character situation.
