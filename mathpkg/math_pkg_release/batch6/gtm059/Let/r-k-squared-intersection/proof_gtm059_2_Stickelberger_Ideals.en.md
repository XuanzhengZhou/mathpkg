First we prove that for any prime $j \geq 2$, we have
$$R^2 = R_c \quad \text{and} \quad J^2 = J_c.$$

A similar property is due to Macza and Couture-Smortt, as mentioned in the text. Indeed, we have
$$e^2(c^2) - c^2 p^2 = \sum_{j=1}^{k} E_{k^2}(J^2).$$

This establishes the claimed intersection relations.
