We may assume that $1 \le k < n$. Both sides are $(q-1)$-periodic in $k$, and the relation is divisible for $k = 0$. We show $q = 1 \pmod{q-1}$ we find:

$$k = k_0 + k_1 q + k_2 q^2 + \dots + k_{n-1} q^{n-1}$$

where $k_i$ are the $q$-adic digits of $k$. Then

$$q^{n-1} k_0 + k_1 q + k_2 q^2 + \dots + k_{n-1} q^{n-1} \equiv k \pmod{q-1}.$$

The periodicity and the representation in terms of $q$-adic digits establish the formula.
