This follows directly from Theorem 2.2 as a special case.

Therefore, if $b$ is an integer prime to $m$, and $\omega = \omega_{2(2)}$ then the group ring element $\Omega(2) = \omega$ has the $n$-th power in $\mathbb{Q}_{2(2)}$.

Thus we have the ideal factorization of the $(b - \omega)^n$-power of the Gauss sum. In every ideal class there exists an ideal prime to $m$. Since the ideal $\mathfrak{p}^{n-1} - \omega$ is principal for every prime $p$, we find that $\Omega(2) = \omega$ annihilates the ideal class group.
