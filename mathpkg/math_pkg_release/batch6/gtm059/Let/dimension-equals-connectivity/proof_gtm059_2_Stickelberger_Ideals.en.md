The space generated by the elements $u(x)$ with $r = z_X$ is clearly a $G(N)$ possible space, namely $G_Z(N)$ for $b = G(N)$.

Let the idempotent associated with $x$ be the usual $r_X = r(x)$, then
$$G(N) \sum_{i=1}^{n} h(i)v(x).$$

If $M$ is the conductor of $x$, then
$$\frac{1}{M} \left( \frac{1}{u(x)} \right) v_X = S_Z(x) \frac{1}{h(x)} v_X$$
as one sees from the fact that $d$ depends only on the residue class of $A$. That is, $A \neq G(N)$. Hence it has a non-trivial coexistence if $S_Z(x) = 0$.
