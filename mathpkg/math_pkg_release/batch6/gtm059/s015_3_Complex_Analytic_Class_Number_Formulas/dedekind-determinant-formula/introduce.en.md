The Dedekind determinant formula (also known as the Frobenius determinant theorem) factorizes the group determinant $\det(f(ab^{-1}))$ as a product over all characters $\chi$ of the group $G$. Each factor is the Fourier transform $\sum_{a \in G} \chi(a) f(a)$ of the function $f$ at the character $\chi$. This formula is a crucial tool in the analytic derivation of class number formulas, as it allows the class number to be expressed as a product of character sums.
