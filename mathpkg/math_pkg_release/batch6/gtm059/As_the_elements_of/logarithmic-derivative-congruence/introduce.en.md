Lemma DL 4 establishes a congruence for the logarithmic derivative of a unit $u$ in the formal group. For $m \geq 2$, the values $\lambda_i(u)$ are congruent modulo a certain modulus $\tau_{u_m}$ derived from the structure of the Lubin-Tate formal group. The proof relies on the chain rule and the representation of $a = g(s)$ as a power series with a unit coefficient, together with the properties established in DL 3.
