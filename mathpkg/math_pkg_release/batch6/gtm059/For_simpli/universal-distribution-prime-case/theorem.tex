Let $N$ be a prime. Let $U(N)$ be the universal distribution on $\frac{1}{N}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z}$, generated by symbols $[a/N]$ for $a = 0, 1, \dots, N-1$ modulo the distribution relations. Let $T_N$ be the set of primitive elements, i.e., elements of exact order $N$ in $\frac{1}{N}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z}$.

\textbf{Theorem 9.1.}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] The elements $[a/N]$ for $a$ prime to $N$ (i.e., $a = 1, \dots, N-1$) generate the abelian group $U(N)$.
  \item[(ii)] The elements $[a/N]$ with $a$ prime to $N$ are free generators of $U(N)$; equivalently, the natural map from the free abelian group on these generators to $U(N)$ is an isomorphism.
  \item[(iii)] The natural distribution on cyclotomic units maps onto a subgroup of finite index in $U(N)$, realizing $U(N)$ as the universal object in the category of distributions.
\end{enumerate}
