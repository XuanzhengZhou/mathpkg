Let $Q(N)$ denote the set of rational numbers with denominator dividing $N$, modulo $\mathbb{Z}$. Let $A$ be an abelian group. An \textbf{ordinary distribution} (or simply a \text{distribution}) is a function
$$g: Q(N) \to A$$
satisfying the distribution relation: for every $r = a/N \in Q(N)$ and every positive integer $d$,
$$\sum_{dy \equiv r \pmod{\mathbb{Z}}} g(y) = g(r),$$
where the sum is taken over all $y \in Q(dN)$ such that $dy \equiv r \pmod{\mathbb{Z}}$.
