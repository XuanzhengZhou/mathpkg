An ordinary distribution is a map from the torsion subgroup $Q(N)$ of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ into an abelian group, satisfying a natural compatibility condition under multiplication. This definition encodes the distribution-like behavior observed in Bernoulli polynomials and Stickelberger elements. Distributions form a category, and the universal distribution is the initial object in this category, playing a central role in the study of cyclotomic fields and $L$-functions.
