Let $p$ be a prime. For a positive integer $k$, write $k = q_0 + q_1 p + \cdots + q_r p^r$ with $0 \leq q_i < p$ (the base-$p$ expansion). Let $s(k) = \sum_{i=0}^r q_i$ denote the sum of the $p$-adic digits of $k$. Let $v_p(k!) = \frac{k - s(k)}{p-1}$ denote the $p$-adic valuation of $k!$.

\textbf{Lemma 1.} For $1 \leq k \leq p-1$, we have the congruence
$$k! \equiv (-1)^{k+1} \frac{1}{k} \pmod{p}.$$
More generally, for any positive integer $k$,
$$\frac{k!}{p^{v_p(k!)}} \equiv (-1)^{v_p(k!)} \prod_{i=0}^r (q_i!) \pmod{p}.$$
