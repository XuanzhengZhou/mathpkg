The universal distribution is the initial object in the category of ordinary distributions on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$. It is constructed as the free abelian group generated by symbols for all torsion elements, modulo the distribution relations. For a fixed level $N$, the universal distribution $U(N)$ has rank $\varphi(N) - 1$, where the single relation reduces the rank from the number of primitive elements. This construction is central to the proof of Stickelberger's theorem and the study of cyclotomic units.
