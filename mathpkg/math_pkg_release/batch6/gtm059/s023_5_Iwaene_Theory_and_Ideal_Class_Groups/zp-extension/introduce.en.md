A $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of a number field $K$ is an infinite Galois extension whose Galois group is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_p$. Every number field admits at least one $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension, the cyclotomic one, and typically admits several independent ones. Iwasawa theory studies the growth of arithmetic invariants (such as the $p$-primary part of the ideal class group) in the intermediate layers $K_n$ of a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension.
