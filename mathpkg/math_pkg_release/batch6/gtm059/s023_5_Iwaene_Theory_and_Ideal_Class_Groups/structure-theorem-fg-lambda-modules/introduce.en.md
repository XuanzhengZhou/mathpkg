Theorem 3.1 is the fundamental structure theorem for finitely generated modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$. It states that, up to quasi-isomorphism, every finitely generated $\Lambda$-module decomposes as a direct sum of a free part $\Lambda^r$, $p$-power torsion parts $\Lambda/(p^{\mu_i})$, and parts coming from irreducible distinguished polynomials $\Lambda/(f_j^{e_j})$. This is the analogue for $\Lambda$ of the structure theorem for finitely generated modules over a PID, adapted to the $2$-dimensional local ring $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$.
