Let $K$ be a number field. Define:
$$G(K) = \operatorname{Gal}(M(K)/K)$$
where $M(K)$ is the maximal $p$-abelian unramified extension of $K$. By class field theory,
$$G(K) \cong \left(\prod_{\mathfrak{p}} U_{\mathfrak{p}}\right) \cdot K^{\times} \Big/ \overline{E} \cdot K^{\times}$$
where $U_{\mathfrak{p}}$ are the local units at each prime $\mathfrak{p}$, $E$ is the global unit group of $K$, and the bar denotes closure in the idele topology. The group $G(K)$ is a finitely generated $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module.

The Leopoldt conjecture asserts that the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank of $G(K)$ is $r_2 + 1$, where $r_2$ is the number of complex places of $K$.
