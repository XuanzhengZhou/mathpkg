Nakayama's Lemma for the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ states that a compact $\Lambda$-module $M$ satisfying $M = \mathfrak{m}M$ (where $\mathfrak{m} = (p,X)$ is the maximal ideal) must be zero. This is one of the fundamental tools for studying finitely generated modules over the Iwasawa algebra, and plays a role analogous to Nakayama's Lemma in commutative algebra for local rings.
