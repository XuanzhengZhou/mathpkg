Let $K_{\infty}/K$ be a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Only finitely many prime ideals of $K$ can ramify in $K_{\infty}/K$, and those that do ramify are totally ramified.
\item[(ii)] At least one prime of $K$ ramifies in any $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension that is not the trivial extension.
\end{enumerate}
