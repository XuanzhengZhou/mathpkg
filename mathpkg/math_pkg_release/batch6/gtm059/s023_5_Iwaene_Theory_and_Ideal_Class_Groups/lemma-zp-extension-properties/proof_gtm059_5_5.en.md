(i) Since $K_{\infty}/K$ is a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension, each finite layer $K_n/K$ is cyclic of degree $p^n$. The ramification index of any prime in a $p$-extension is a power of $p$. Over the completion, a totally ramified extension of degree $p^n$ would have ramification index $p^n$. The finiteness of ramified primes follows from the fact that only primes dividing the discriminant of each finite layer can ramify, and the tower's discriminant grows in a controlled way.

(ii) If no prime ramified, then $K_{\infty}$ would be an unramified $p$-extension of $K$ of infinite degree, contradicting the finiteness of the class number (since the maximal unramified abelian $p$-extension has finite degree over $K$, bounded by the $p$-part of the class group).
