A routine induction shows that $h_n = (1+X)^{p^n} - 1$ lies in $(p, X)^{p^{n-1}+1}$, where $(p, X)$ is the maximal ideal of $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$. It follows that the intersection of the ideals $(h_n)$ is zero. The kernel of $\varphi$ is this intersection, so $\varphi$ is injective. For surjectivity, the Euclidean algorithm in $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ shows that the natural map $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]] \to \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]/(h_n)$ is surjective, giving the required projective limit identification.
