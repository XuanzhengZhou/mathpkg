Theorem 3.2 provides the computational backbone for the structure theorem of $\Lambda$-modules: any relation matrix can be reduced to a diagonal normal form using three types of admissible operations (modifying rows, extending the module by a finite index, and surjecting with finite kernel). The diagonal entries are distinguished polynomials or powers of $p$, ordered by Weierstrass degree. This is the analogue of the Smith normal form over a PID.
