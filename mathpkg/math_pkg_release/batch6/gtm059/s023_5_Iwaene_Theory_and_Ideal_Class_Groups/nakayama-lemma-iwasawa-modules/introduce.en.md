Nakayama's lemma, adapted to the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$, is a fundamental tool for proving that certain projective limits (such as the projective limit of $p$-parts of ideal class groups) are finitely generated $\Lambda$-modules. The compact version states that if a compact $\Lambda$-module $M$ satisfies $M/\mathfrak{m}M$ is finite, then $M$ is finitely generated over $\Lambda$. This is the starting point for showing that the Iwasawa modules $X = \varprojlim A_n$ (where $A_n$ is the $p$-Sylow of the class group of the $n$-th layer) are finitely generated torsion $\Lambda$-modules.
