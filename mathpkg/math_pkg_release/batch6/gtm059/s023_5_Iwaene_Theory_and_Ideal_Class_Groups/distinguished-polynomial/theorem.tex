A polynomial $P(X) = X^n + b_{n-1} X^{n-1} + \dots + b_0 \in A[X]$ where $A$ is a local ring with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ is called \textbf{distinguished} (or a Weierstrass polynomial) if all coefficients $b_0, \dots, b_{n-1}$ lie in $\mathfrak{m}$.

In the context of the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$, a distinguished polynomial is a monic polynomial whose non-leading coefficients are divisible by $p$.
