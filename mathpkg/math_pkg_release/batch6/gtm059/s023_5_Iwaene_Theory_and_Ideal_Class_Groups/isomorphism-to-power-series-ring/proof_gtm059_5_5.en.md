A direct induction shows that
$$h_n = (1+X)^{p^n} - 1 \in (p, X)^{n+1}$$
where $(p,X)$ denotes the maximal ideal of $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ generated by $p$ and $X$. It follows that the intersection of the ideals $(h_n)$ is $0$, from which the kernel of $e$ is trivial, so $e$ is injective. The map is also surjective because $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ is complete with respect to the $(p,X)$-adic topology and the projective limit is taken over the quotients $\mathbb{Z}_p[X]/(h_n)$. Note that the isomorphism $e$ depends on the original choice of generator $\gamma$.
