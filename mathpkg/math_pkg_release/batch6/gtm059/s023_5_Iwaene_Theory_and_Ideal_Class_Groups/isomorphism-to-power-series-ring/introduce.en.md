Theorem 1.1 establishes that the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda$ is canonically isomorphic to the formal power series ring $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ upon choosing a topological generator $\gamma$ of $\Gamma \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$, with $X = \gamma - 1$. The proof uses the fact that $h_n = (1+X)^{p^n} - 1$ lies in $(p,X)^{n+1}$, so the intersection of the ideals $(h_n)$ is zero, making the map injective; surjectivity follows from completeness of $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$.
