By class field theory, the Galois group $G(K)$ is isomorphic to the $p$-primary part of the quotient
$$\left(\prod_{\mathfrak{p}} U_{\mathfrak{p}}\right) \cdot K^{\times} \Big/ \overline{E} \cdot K^{\times}.$$
At primes not dividing $p$, the local unit groups $U_{\mathfrak{p}}$ are finite, so their contribution is finite. At primes $\mathfrak{p} \mid p$, the principal units $U_{\mathfrak{p}}^{(1)}$ form a pro-$p$ group isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_p^{[K_{\mathfrak{p}}:\mathbb{Q}_p]}$. The closure $\overline{E}$ of the global units is embedded diagonally, and the quotient yields the stated quasi-isomorphism. The Leopoldt conjecture concerns the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank of $\overline{E}$ and hence determines the rank $r_p$.
