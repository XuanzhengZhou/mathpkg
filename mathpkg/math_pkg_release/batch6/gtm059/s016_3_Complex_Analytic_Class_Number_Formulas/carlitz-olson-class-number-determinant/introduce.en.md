The Carlitz--Olson determinant formula expresses the class number $h_p^+$ of the maximal real subfield of a cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_p)$ as a determinant whose entries are differences of smallest positive residues modulo $p$. Each entry is $0$, $1$, or $-1$, so the determinant is an integer. This formulation, originating from work of Carlitz and Olson and presented by Lang, connects the algebraic invariant $h_p^+$ to an explicit combinatorial matrix, making it amenable to bounds via Hadamard's inequality and to computational verification for small primes.
