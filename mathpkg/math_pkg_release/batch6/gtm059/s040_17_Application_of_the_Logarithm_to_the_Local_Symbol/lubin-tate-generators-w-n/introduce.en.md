The Lubin-Tate generators $$w_n$$ form a compatible sequence satisfying the formal group law relations. Their importance lies in the computation of the different ideal $$B_n = \mathfrak{D}_{K_n/K}$$, which controls the ramification of the Lubin-Tate extension. The derivative of the power series representing field elements, evaluated at the generators, determines whether the element is integral and to what power of the maximal ideal it belongs, providing the foundation for the logarithmic derivative theory.
