If $$\alpha \in \operatorname{Gal}(K, \mathbb{R})$$ and $$\alpha \mapsto a(\alpha)$$ is the representation of $$a^*$$ on $$T_{\alpha}(R)$$, then

$$\psi_a(\alpha) = \psi(a) \psi_a(0)^T$$

and

$$\varepsilon(\sigma^{-1} a, \alpha) = (\lambda, \alpha) = \left( \sum_i \lambda_i \, d_i(\phi_a)(\alpha) \right)_{a_i}.$$

Using $$[ab] = [a][b]$$ and $$T_{\alpha}(R) = T_{\alpha}(R)$$, the formula follows.
