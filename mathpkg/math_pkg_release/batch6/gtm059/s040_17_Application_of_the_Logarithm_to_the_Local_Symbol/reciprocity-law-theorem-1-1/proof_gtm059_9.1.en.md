By the formalism of the norm residue symbol, we know that

$$1 = (N_1^* K_2) (N_1^* K_1) (N_1^* K_0).$$

Hence $$[N_1^* \mathfrak{u}_i] = \mathfrak{u}_i$$ by the Lubin-Tate theory, establishing the first assertion. We choose $$t = s_0$$, so that $$[\pi^{n+1}] = \mathfrak{u}_0$$. Then

$$\langle \mathfrak{u}_i, \mathfrak{u}_j \rangle = \pi^* t - t.$$

Since $$(N_1^* K_2)(N_1^* K_1)(N_1^* K_0)$$ yields by Lubin-Tate theory

$$\langle \mathfrak{u}_i, \mathfrak{u}_j \rangle = (N_1^* \mathfrak{u}_i) t - t = (N_1^{n+1} - 1) t,$$

and the result follows.
