For $$x \in A(\mathfrak{b}_0)^{g+1} A(\mathfrak{b}_1)$$ and $$y \in A_{\mathfrak{a}_0}$$, there exists a unique group homomorphism

$$A_{\mathfrak{a}_0} \to A(\mathfrak{b}_0)^{g+1} A(\mathfrak{b}_1)$$

such that for the local symbol pairing

$$\langle x, y \rangle_{LS}$$

the orthogonality with respect to the trace pairing and the local symbol coincides, i.e.,

$$\langle x, y \rangle_{LS} = T_i(xy)$$

under the identification given by the logarithm map.
