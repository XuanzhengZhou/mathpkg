Theorem 1.2 is the central explicit reciprocity law of the chapter, equating the classical Hilbert norm residue symbol $$\langle x, n \rangle$$ with the Lubin-Tate symbol $$[x, N_n]_{A_f(n)}$$ constructed from the logarithmic derivative. The theorem holds under technical conditions ensuring the symbols are well-defined, and establishes that the value is independent of the choice of sufficiently large level $$n$$. This result, due to Wiles, provides a computable expression for the local symbol in terms of the Lubin-Tate logarithm.
