The proof builds on the explicit reciprocity law (Theorem 1.2) and the properties of the logarithmic derivative established in DI 1. The key idea is to relate the symbol $$(x, n_0)$$ — defined via the classical norm residue symbol — to the Lubin-Tate symbol $$[x, n_1]$$ using the formalism of the Lubin-Tate group. The factor $$|u_n|$$ arises from the normalization of the logarithm map.

For the formal multiplicative group case with $$K = \mathbb{Q}_p$$, the formula reduces to Iwasawa's classical formula. The general case follows from the compatibility of the Lubin-Tate logarithm with the norm map, as established in the preceding sections, and the fact that the value is independent of the choice of $$n \geq 2n+1$$.
