The logarithmic derivative operator $$D_n$$ is defined by choosing a power series $$g$$ representing an element $$a \in K$$ when evaluated at the uniformizer $$\pi$$, and then taking the formal derivative $$g'(\pi)$$. Although the definition depends on the choice of power series and the Lubin-Tate generator sequence, it turns out that under suitable conditions the resulting symbol is independent of these choices. This operator is central to the formulation of Wiles' explicit reciprocity law.
