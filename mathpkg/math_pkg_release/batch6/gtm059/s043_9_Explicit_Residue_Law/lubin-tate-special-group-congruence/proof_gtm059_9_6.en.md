**Proof.** By Theorem 3.20 of Chapter 8, we know that the group law on $B$ satisfies

$$F(X, Y) = X + Y \bmod \text{terms of degree } \ge n.$$

If $n, y \in \mathfrak{p}_n$, it follows that

$$x +_B y = p(x) + y \bmod \mathfrak{p}^n,$$

where $+_B$ denotes addition in the formal group $B$ and $+$ denotes ordinary addition. But $\mathfrak{p}^n \in [x] B_n$. Hence addition on $B_n$ and addition on the multiplicative group $\mathbb{G}_m$ are interchangeable on the left of the symbol $(x, w_n)$ under the stated conditions. The rest of the proof is identical with that of Theorem 6.2.
