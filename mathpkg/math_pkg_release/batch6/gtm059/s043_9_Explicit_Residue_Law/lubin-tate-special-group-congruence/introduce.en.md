Lemma 6.3 verifies that the special Lubin-Tate group $B$, whose Frobenius power series is a polynomial, satisfies the same addition law congruence as the general case. Specifically, by Theorem 3.20 of Chapter 8, the group law on $B$ satisfies $F(X, Y) = X + Y \bmod \text{terms of degree } \ge n$, which guarantees that addition on $B_n$ and addition on the multiplicative group $\mathbb{G}_m$ are interchangeable on the left of the symbol $(x, w_n)$ under the stated order conditions.
