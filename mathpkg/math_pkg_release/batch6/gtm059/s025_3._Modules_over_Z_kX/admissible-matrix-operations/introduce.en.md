Admissible operations are transformations on the relation matrix of a finitely generated module $M$ over the Iwasawa algebra $A = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$. Each admissible operation corresponds to either embedding $M$ into a larger module with finite quotient, or taking a surjection with finite kernel -- in other words, a pseudo-isomorphism. The operations are designed to diagonalize the relation matrix through a sequence of row and column manipulations while preserving the pseudo-isomorphism class of $M$. Operations (O.1), (O.2), and (O.3) are specific to the Iwasawa algebra and exploit the properties of distinguished polynomials and $p$-divisibility.
