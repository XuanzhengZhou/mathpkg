The cyclotomic units satisfy two fundamental types of distribution relations, denoted CU 1 and CU 2 in Lang's treatment. CU 1 expresses compatibility with the norm map between successive layers of the cyclotomic tower: taking the norm from $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^n})$ down to $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^{n-1}})$ sends $1 - \zeta_{p^n}$ to $1 - \zeta_{p^{n-1}}$. CU 2 states that the product of all nontrivial $p$-th roots of unity (shifted by 1) equals $p$. These relations are the defining properties that make cyclotomic units a "universal distribution" in the sense of Kubert-Lang.
