Theorem 3.4 establishes that the cyclotomic unit group $E_n$ generates the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}[E_n]$ as a module over the Galois group ring. Writing an element of degree 0 in the form $x = k\lambda(kN - 1)$, one observes that $n - 1$ is divisible by $n - 1$ in the integral group ring because $n$ is a generator of the cyclic group $N$. This structural result connects cyclotomic units to the Stickelberger ideal and forms the algebraic foundation for the theory of circular (cyclotomic) units.
