Theorem 3.2 of Lang's Chapter 3 establishes that the Galois group $Z_k^* = \operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^k})/\mathbb{Q})$ acts simply transitively on the set of primitive elements in the cyclotomic unit group. The corresponding homomorphism from $Z_k^*$ into the automorphism group is surjective by construction, and injectivity follows from the fact that the cyclotomic unit group is torsion-free (proved in Theorem 3.2.3), which forces the ranks of the two groups to be equal.
