Cyclotomic units are a fundamental construction in algebraic number theory, introduced by Kummer in his work on Fermat's Last Theorem. They form a subgroup of the full unit group of a cyclotomic field, generated by roots of unity and the elements $\zeta - 1$ for nontrivial roots of unity $\zeta$. These units satisfy remarkable distribution relations that make them a universal distribution in the sense of Kubert and Lang, linking them to the Stickelberger ideal and class number formulas.
