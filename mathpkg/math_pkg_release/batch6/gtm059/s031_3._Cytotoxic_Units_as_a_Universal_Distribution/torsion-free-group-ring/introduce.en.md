Theorem 3.3 (numbered 3.2.3 in some editions) establishes that the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}[Z_k]$ of the cyclic Galois group $Z_k$ is torsion-free. The proof uses the embedding of the cyclotomic unit group $V_k$ into its dual $V_k^*$, which corresponds to an embedding of group rings. An element in $\mathbb{Z}[Z_k]$ that is a torsion element must have equal coefficients under the projection maps, forcing it to already lie in the base ring, which is torsion-free.
