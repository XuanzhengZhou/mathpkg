Let $v_0$ be an element of $R_0$ such that
$$v_0 + \pi = 0$$
where $\pi$ is a uniformizer. The field
$$K_0 = K(v_0)$$
is totally ramified. For $u \in K_0^\times$ a unit, let $g(W) \in \mathcal{O}_K[[W]]$ be a power series such that
$$u = g(v_0).$$
If $\tilde{g}$ is another such power series, then $\tilde{g}'(W) \equiv g'(W) \pmod{\varpi(W)}$ where $\varpi(W)$ is the irreducible polynomial of $v_0$ over $K$. Consequently,
$$g'\bigl(g(v_0)\bigr)$$
is well defined modulo $\mathfrak{p}$, and lies in the residue field. We define
$$\varphi_k(v_0) = v_0, \quad \text{for } 1 \leq k \leq q - 2.$$
Then
$$\varphi_k: U_1^{(k)} \to \bar{K}$$
is a homomorphism, which we call the \textbf{Kummer homomorphism} of degree $k$.
