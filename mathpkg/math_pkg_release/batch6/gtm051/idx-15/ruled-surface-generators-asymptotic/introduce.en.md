This proposition establishes the fundamental relationship between the geometry of ruled surfaces and their Gaussian curvature. Part (i) shows that the generators (lines of constant $t$) of a ruled surface are asymptotic curves, which immediately implies non-positive Gaussian curvature $K \leq 0$. Part (ii) provides equivalent characterizations of developability: the mixed partial derivative condition $f_{st} \in \operatorname{span}\{f_s, f_t\}$ and the vanishing of Gaussian curvature $K = 0$. These results are central to the classification of flat surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$.
