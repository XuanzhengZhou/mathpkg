This proposition constructs a developable surface that osculates a given surface along a prescribed curve. Given a curve $c(t)$ on a surface $f$ and a tangent vector field $Y(t)$ along $c(t)$ that is orthogonal to the normal curvature in the sense $II(\dot{c}, Y) = 0$, the ruled surface $g(s, t) = sY(t) + c(t)$ is developable. The resulting surface shares the curve $c$ and its tangent planes with the original surface $f$ along $c$. This construction is essential for defining parallel translation in Riemannian geometry.
