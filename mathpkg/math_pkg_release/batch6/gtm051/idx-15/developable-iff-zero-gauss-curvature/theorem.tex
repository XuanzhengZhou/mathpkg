A surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ without planar points is developable if and only if its Gaussian curvature $K = 0$ everywhere.

\textbf{Remark.} A surface $f$ is \text{planar} at $u_0 \in U$ if $II_{u_0} \equiv 0$. The theorem fails without the hypothesis of "no planar points." An explicit counterexample is constructed in (3.9.4).
