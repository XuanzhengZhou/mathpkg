A tangential developable is a ruled surface constructed from a space curve $c(t)$ by taking the tangent lines at each point as generators. The surface is parametrized as $f(s, t) = s\dot{c}(t) + c(t)$, where $c(t)$ serves as the directrix. This is a canonical example of a developable surface---it has zero Gaussian curvature wherever defined. Tangential developables arise naturally in the study of curves in $\mathbb{R}^3$ and provide the geometric foundation for understanding the relationship between space curves and flat surfaces.
