A developable surface is a ruled surface whose normal vector field is constant along each generator. This means the tangent plane does not vary as one moves along a generator. Developable surfaces are characterized by having zero Gaussian curvature (in the absence of planar points), making them locally isometric to the plane. They play a fundamental role in differential geometry, particularly in the study of surfaces that can be flattened onto a plane without stretching or tearing.
