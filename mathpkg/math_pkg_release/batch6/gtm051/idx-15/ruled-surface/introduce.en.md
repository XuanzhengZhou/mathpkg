A ruled surface is a surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$ swept out by a family of straight lines. The surface is parametrized as $f(s, t) = s X(t) + c(t)$, where $c(t)$ is a curve (the directrix) and $X(t)$ is a vector field along it. The lines of constant $t$ are called generators, and curves of constant $s$ are directrices. Ruled surfaces form the foundation for the study of developable surfaces, which are ruled surfaces with the additional property that the normal vector is constant along generators.
