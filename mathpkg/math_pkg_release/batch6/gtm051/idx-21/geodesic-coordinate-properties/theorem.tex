Let $c(s)$ be a unit-speed geodesic basis curve. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(ii)] $g_{22}(0, u^2) = 1$, $g_{22,1}(0, u^2) = 0$, and $\Gamma_{ij}^k(0, u^2) = 0$ for all $i, j, k$.
\item[(iii)] $g_{11} = 1$, $g_{12} = 0$, and $g_{22} > 0$, so that the curves $u^1 = \text{constant}$ are unit-speed geodesics cutting the curves $u^2 = \text{constant}$ orthogonally, and the curves $u^1 = \text{constant}$ and $u^2 = \text{constant}$ are parallel curves.
\end{enumerate}
