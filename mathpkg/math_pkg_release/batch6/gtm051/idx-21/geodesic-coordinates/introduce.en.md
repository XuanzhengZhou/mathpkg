Geodesic coordinates are a local coordinate system on a surface parameterized such that the coordinate curves $u^1 = \text{constant}$ are parallel curves and the curves $u^2 = \text{constant}$ are unit-speed geodesics intersecting them orthogonally. When the basis curve $u^1 = 0$ is itself a geodesic parameterized by arc length, these are called Fermi coordinates. Geodesic coordinates provide a canonical local frame for analyzing the intrinsic geometry of surfaces, generalizing the notion of normal coordinates.
