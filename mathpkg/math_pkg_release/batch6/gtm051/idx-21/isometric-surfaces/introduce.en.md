Isometric surfaces are surfaces between which there exists a diffeomorphism preserving the first fundamental form, i.e., lengths of tangent vectors and angles between them. The cylinder and a strip in the plane are isometric but not congruent in $\mathbb{R}^3$ — this distinction separates intrinsic geometry (preserved under isometry) from extrinsic geometry (preserved under rigid motions in the ambient space). Isometry is an equivalence relation on surfaces and is the foundational notion for studying intrinsic curvature properties.
