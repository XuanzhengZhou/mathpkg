This theorem gives four equivalent characterizations of surfaces with everywhere vanishing Gauss curvature. Condition (i) is the intrinsic curvature condition $K \equiv 0$. Condition (ii) says the surface admits coordinates where the first fundamental form is the identity matrix — a Euclidean metric in coordinates. Condition (iii) states that parallel translation of tangent vectors depends only on the endpoints, not the path taken. Condition (iv) characterizes such surfaces as locally isometric to the plane; they are the developable surfaces. This theorem is the foundational result linking intrinsic flatness to the existence of Cartesian coordinates.
