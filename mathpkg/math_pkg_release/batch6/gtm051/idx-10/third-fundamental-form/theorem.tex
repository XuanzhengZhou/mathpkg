The \text{third fundamental form} of $f$ at $u$ is the symmetric bilinear form given by
$$(X, Y) \in T_u \mathbb{R}^2 \times T_u \mathbb{R}^2 \mapsto dn_u X \cdot dn_u Y \in \mathbb{R}.$$

The third fundamental form is denoted by $III_u$, $III$, or $dn \cdot dn$. If we want to consider $III$ as a form on $T_u f$, it is given by $L_u X \cdot L_u Y$.
