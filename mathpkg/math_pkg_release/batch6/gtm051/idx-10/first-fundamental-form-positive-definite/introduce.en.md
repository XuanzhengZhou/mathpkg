The first fundamental form $I$ of a surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is a symmetric bilinear form on each tangent space that encodes the metric induced by the ambient Euclidean inner product. This proposition asserts two basic but essential properties: $I$ is positive definite, so it genuinely defines a Riemannian metric on the parameter domain $U$, and it is differentiable, meaning the metric varies smoothly with the point on the surface. Together these properties guarantee that the surface inherits a smooth Riemannian structure from $\mathbb{R}^3$.
