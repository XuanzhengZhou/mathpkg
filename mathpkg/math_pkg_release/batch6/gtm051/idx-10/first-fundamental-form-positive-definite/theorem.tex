Let $f: U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[i)] The first fundamental form $I$ of $f$ is positive definite.
\item[ii)] $I$ is differentiable, i.e., the coefficients of the matrix $g_{ik}: U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ are differentiable. This is equivalent to the following condition: For any $X: U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$, $Y: U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$, tangential vector fields along $f$, the map $u \mapsto g_u(X(u), Y(u))$ is differentiable.
\end{enumerate}
