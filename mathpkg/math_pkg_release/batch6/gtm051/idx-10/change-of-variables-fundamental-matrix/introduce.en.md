When a surface is reparametrized by a change of variables $\phi$, the first fundamental form transforms naturally. This corollary gives the explicit coordinate formula: the metric coefficients $\tilde{g}_{ij}$ in the new coordinates are obtained from the original coefficients $g_{kl}$ by contracting with the Jacobian matrix of the change of variables. This is the classical transformation law for a Riemannian metric under a change of coordinates, confirming that $(g_{ij})$ is a tensor of type $(0,2)$.
