The length of a curve on a Riemannian surface generalizes the arc length formula to manifolds. Given a curve $c: I \to M$ and a Riemannian metric $g$, the length is the integral of the norm of the velocity vector $\dot{c}(t)$ measured with respect to the inner product $g_{c(t)}$ on the tangent space $T_{c(t)} M$. This definition is independent of the choice of coordinate chart.
