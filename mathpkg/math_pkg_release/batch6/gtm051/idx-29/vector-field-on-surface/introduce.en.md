A vector field on a differentiable surface $M$ is a differentiable cross-section of the tangent bundle, assigning to each point $p \in M$ a tangent vector $X(p) \in T_p M$. The condition $\pi \circ X = \operatorname{id}_M$ ensures that the vector assigned to $p$ lies in the tangent space at $p$. Locally, in a coordinate chart, a vector field is represented by two differentiable component functions.
