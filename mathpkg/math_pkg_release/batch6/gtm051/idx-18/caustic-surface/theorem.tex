Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface whose principal curvatures $\kappa_1$ and $\kappa_2$ are nonzero and unequal, and let $(u^1, u^2)$ be principal curvature coordinates. The **caustic surfaces** (or focal surfaces) of $f$ are defined by
$$b_i(u) = f(u) + \frac{n(u)}{\kappa_i(u)}, \quad i = 1, 2.$$

These are (immersed) surfaces if and only if $\kappa_{1,1}\kappa_{2,2} \neq 0$.
