A Weingarten surface, or $W$-surface, is a surface whose two principal curvatures satisfy a non-trivial functional relation. This class generalizes surfaces of constant mean curvature and surfaces of constant Gauss curvature. The study of Weingarten surfaces was initiated by Julius Weingarten in the 19th century and has produced many beautiful results about the interplay between intrinsic and extrinsic geometry. Notable contributions have been made by Hilbert, Chern, Hopf, and Voss.
