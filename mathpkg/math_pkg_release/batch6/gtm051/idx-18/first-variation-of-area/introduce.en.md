The first variation of area formula computes how the area of a surface changes under a normal deformation. Given a surface $f$ and a variation $f^\epsilon = f + \epsilon a n$ with compactly supported function $a$, the change in the area element to first order in $\epsilon$ is proportional to $-2aH$, where $H$ is the mean curvature. Integrating this yields the first variation of the area functional. This formula shows that a surface has vanishing first variation of area for all such variations precisely when its mean curvature is identically zero, characterizing minimal surfaces as critical points of the area functional.
