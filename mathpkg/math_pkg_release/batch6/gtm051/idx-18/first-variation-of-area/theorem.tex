Let $f^\epsilon(u) = f(u) + \epsilon a(u)n(u)$ be a variation of a surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$, where $a: U \to \mathbb{R}$ is a smooth function and $n$ is the unit normal. Then, up to terms of second order and higher in $\epsilon$,
$$g_{ik}^\epsilon = g_{ik} - 2\epsilon a h_{ik},$$
and the area element satisfies
$$g^\epsilon = \det(g_{ik}^\epsilon) = g(1 - \epsilon \cdot 4aH).$$
Consequently,
$$\left. \frac{\partial \sqrt{g^\epsilon}}{\partial \epsilon} \right|_{\epsilon=0} = -2aH\sqrt{g}.$$

A surface is minimal ($H \equiv 0$) if and only if for every variation $f^\epsilon$ with $a$ having compact support, the area function
$$A(\epsilon) = \int_U \sqrt{g^\epsilon} \, du^1 du^2$$
has a critical point at $\epsilon = 0$.
