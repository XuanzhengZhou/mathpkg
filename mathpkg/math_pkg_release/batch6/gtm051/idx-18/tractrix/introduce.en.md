The tractrix is a classical curve with the property that the segment of any tangent line from the point of tangency to its intersection with a fixed line (the $z$-axis) has constant length. When revolved about this axis, it generates the pseudosphere, a surface of constant negative Gauss curvature. The tractrix was studied as early as the 17th century and played an important role in the early development of non-Euclidean geometry as a model surface where hyperbolic geometry is realized.
