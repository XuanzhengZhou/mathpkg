This example, due to E. Heintze, demonstrates that vanishing Gauss curvature ($K \equiv 0$) does not imply a surface is developable (ruled by straight lines with constant tangent plane along each generator). While every developable surface has $K = 0$, the converse is false. The construction uses smooth fundamental forms that satisfy the Gauss-Codazzi equations but whose asymptotic lines behave pathologically near the origin, preventing the existence of a ruling parametrization. The example illustrates a subtle distinction between local intrinsic flatness and the extrinsic property of being ruled.
