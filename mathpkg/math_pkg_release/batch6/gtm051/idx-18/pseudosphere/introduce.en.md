The pseudosphere is a surface of revolution with constant Gauss curvature $K = -1$, generated by rotating the tractrix about its asymptote. It was an important example in the early history of non-Euclidean geometry, demonstrating that a surface with constant negative curvature could be realized in $\mathbb{R}^3$. The pseudosphere provides a local model for the hyperbolic plane, though unlike the sphere (which globally realizes $K = +1$), no complete, smooth surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$ can have constant negative curvature everywhere (a consequence of Hilbert's theorem).
