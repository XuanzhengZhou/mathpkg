The Fundamental Theorem of Surface Theory, sometimes called Bonnet's theorem, establishes that the first and second fundamental forms of a surface are not independent: they must satisfy the Gauss and Codazzi-Mainardi equations as compatibility conditions. Conversely, given any such compatible pair of quadratic forms on a simply-connected domain, there exists a surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$ realizing them as its fundamental forms, and this surface is unique up to rigid motion. This theorem is the surface-theoretic analogue of the fundamental theorem of curves, completing the local theory of surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$.
