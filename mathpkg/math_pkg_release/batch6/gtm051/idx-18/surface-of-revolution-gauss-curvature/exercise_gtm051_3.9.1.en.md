**3.9.1 Surfaces of revolution with constant Gauss curvature.** Consider a surface of revolution given as in (3.3.7) by
$$f(u, v) = (h(u) \cos v, h(u) \sin v, k(u)).$$
Assume that $h'^2 + k'^2 = 1$ and hence that $k'k'' = -h''h'$.

(a) Prove: $g_{11} = 1$, $g_{12} = 0$, $g_{22} = h^2$, $h_{11} = -k'h'' + h'k''$, $h_{12} = 0$, $h_{22} = hk'$.

(b) Show that the Gauss curvature simplifies to
$$K = \frac{(h'k'k'' - k'k'h'')}{h} = -\frac{h''}{h}.$$

(c) Show that $f$ has constant Gauss curvature $K_0$ if and only if $h$ satisfies
$$h''(u) + K_0 h(u) = 0,$$
with the constraint $h'^2 \leq 1$.

(d) Classify the surfaces of revolution of constant Gauss curvature for the cases $K_0 = 0$, $K_0 > 0$, and $K_0 < 0$. Describe the tractrix and the pseudosphere.
