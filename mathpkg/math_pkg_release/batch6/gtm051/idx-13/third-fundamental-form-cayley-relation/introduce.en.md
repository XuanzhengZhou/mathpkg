This proposition establishes that the third fundamental form $III$ of a surface is linearly dependent on the first and second fundamental forms $I$ and $II$, with coefficients given by the mean curvature $H$ and Gauss curvature $K$: $III - 2H\,II + K\,I = 0$. This relation is a special case of the Cayley-Hamilton theorem applied to the Weingarten map $L$, since $III(X,Y) = I(LX, LY)$ and the characteristic polynomial $\chi(L) = L^2 - 2H L + K \cdot \mathrm{id}$ vanishes on $L$. Consequently, the third fundamental form carries no independent curvature information beyond what is already encoded in $I$, $II$, $K$, and $H$.
