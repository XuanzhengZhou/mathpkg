This theorem establishes the invariance properties of the principal curvatures, Gauss curvature $K$, and mean curvature $H$ under two fundamental types of transformations: rigid motions (isometries) of the ambient $\mathbb{R}^3$ and changes of parametrization (diffeomorphisms of the parameter domain). Under a congruence (orientation-preserving isometry), $K$ and the absolute values of $\kappa$ and $H$ are preserved, while a symmetry (orientation-reversing isometry) flips the signs of $\kappa$ and $H$ but leaves $K$ unchanged. Under a change of variables, all curvature quantities transform by composition with the coordinate change, confirming that the curvatures are geometric invariants of the surface itself.
