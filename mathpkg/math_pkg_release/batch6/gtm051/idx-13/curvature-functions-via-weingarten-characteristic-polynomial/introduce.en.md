This proposition establishes the fundamental algebraic link between the Gauss and mean curvatures and the Weingarten map $L_u = -dn \circ df^{-1}$. The characteristic polynomial of $L_u$ is $\kappa^2 - 2H\kappa + K$, which immediately yields $2H = \operatorname{Tr}(L)$ and $K = \det(L)$. The proposition also provides the classical formulas for $K$ and $H$ in terms of the coefficients of the first and second fundamental forms: $K = \det(h_{ik}) / \det(g_{ik})$ and $2H = \sum h_{ik} g^{ik}$, making these curvatures computable directly from the metric and the shape operator.
