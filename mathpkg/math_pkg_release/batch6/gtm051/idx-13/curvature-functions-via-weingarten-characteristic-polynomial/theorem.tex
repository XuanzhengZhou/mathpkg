The curvature functions $K$ and $H$ are determined by the equation

$$\det(\kappa \, \mathrm{id} + dn \circ df^{-1}) = \kappa^2 - 2H\kappa + K,$$

where the left-hand side is the characteristic polynomial $\chi(L_u)$ of the Weingarten map $L_u = -dn \circ df^{-1}$ in the variable $\kappa$. Consequently,

$$2H(u) = \operatorname{Tr} L(u), \quad K(u) = \det L(u).$$

Moreover, if $(h_{ik})$ is the matrix representation of the second fundamental form $II$ and $(g_{ik})$ that of the first fundamental form $I$, then $K = \det(h_{ik}) / \det(g_{ik})$ and $2H = \sum_{i,k} h_{ik} g^{ik}$, where $(g^{ik})$ is the inverse matrix of $(g_{ik})$.
