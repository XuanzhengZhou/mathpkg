In geodesic coordinates $(u^1, u^2)$ on an oriented surface $M$ with Riemannian metric
$$ds^2 = (du^1)^2 + g_{22}(du^2)^2,$$
let $E_1, E_2$ be the orthonormal frame with $E_1 = \partial/\partial u^1$, $E_2 = (1/\sqrt{g_{22}}) \partial/\partial u^2$, and let $\theta(t)$ be the angle between $E_1$ and the tangent vector $\dot{u}(t)$ of a curve. Then the geodesic curvature $\kappa_g$ of the curve is given by
$$\kappa_g(t) = \dot{\theta}(t) + g(\nabla E_1/dt, E_2)(t).$$
Since in geodesic coordinates $\Gamma_{11}^1 = \Gamma_{11}^2 = \Gamma_{21}^1 = 0$ and $\Gamma_{12}^2 = (\sqrt{g_{22}})_{,1}/\sqrt{g_{22}}$, this simplifies to
$$\kappa_g(t) = \dot{\theta}(t) - (\sqrt{g_{22}})_{,1} \cdot \dot{u}^2(t).$$
