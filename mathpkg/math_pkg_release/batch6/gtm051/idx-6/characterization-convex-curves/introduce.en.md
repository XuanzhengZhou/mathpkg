A simple closed regular plane curve is convex exactly when its curvature does not change sign. More precisely, Theorem 2.3.2 of Klingenberg's text establishes that a simple closed plane curve is convex (lies entirely on one side of every tangent) if and only if its curvature $\kappa(t)$ is everywhere nonnegative or everywhere nonpositive. The two conditions are geometrically equivalent via an orientation-reversing parameter change. Examples show the "simple" hypothesis is necessary: a trefoil (pretzel curve) satisfies $\kappa \geq 0$ but is not convex.
