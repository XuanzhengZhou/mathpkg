A convex plane curve is a regular curve in $\mathbb{R}^2$ that lies entirely on one side of every tangent line. Equivalently, for any point $c(t_0)$ on the curve, the inner product of the displacement vector $c(t) - c(t_0)$ with the unit normal $e_2(t_0)$ is either always nonnegative or always nonpositive across the entire parameter domain. Convexity is a key global property of plane curves used in the four vertex theorem and the isoperimetric inequality.
