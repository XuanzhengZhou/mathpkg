A Riemannian metric on an open subset $U \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ is a smooth assignment of a positive definite symmetric bilinear form to each point of $U$. Concretely, it is a differentiable map $g$ from $U$ into the space $S(2)$ of $2 \times 2$ symmetric positive definite matrices. The notation $(U, g)$ denotes an open set equipped with a Riemannian metric, and this structure serves as the local coordinate representation of a Riemannian surface.
