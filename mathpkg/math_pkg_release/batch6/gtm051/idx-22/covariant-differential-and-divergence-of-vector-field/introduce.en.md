The covariant differential $\nabla X$ of a vector field $X$ on a Riemannian surface generalizes the ordinary Jacobian matrix by incorporating the Christoffel symbols, ensuring coordinate-invariance under isometries. Its $(k, j)$-entry is the sum of the partial derivative of the component $\xi^k$ and a correction term involving the metric connection. The divergence $\operatorname{div} X$ is then defined as the trace of this linear transformation, providing a coordinate-invariant measure of the "outflow" of the vector field on the surface.
