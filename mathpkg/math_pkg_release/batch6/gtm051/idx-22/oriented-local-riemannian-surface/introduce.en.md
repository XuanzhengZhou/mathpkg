An oriented local Riemannian surface is a class of isometric Riemannian metrics on open subsets of $\mathbb{R}^2$ that are positively equivalent, i.e., related by an orientation-preserving diffeomorphism. This definition incorporates the choice of orientation into the very notion of the surface, so that orientation becomes part of the intrinsic geometric data of $M$.
