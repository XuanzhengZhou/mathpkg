A local Riemannian surface is defined intrinsically as an isometry class of local coordinate patches $(U, g)$ equipped with Riemannian metrics. Rather than fixing a specific parametrization, a surface is identified by the equivalence class of all isometric parametrizations. The notation $M$ is used to denote such an equivalence class, and in practice $M$ is described by choosing one representative $(U, g)$.
