Two isometric Riemannian metrics are positively equivalent if the isometry can be realized by an orientation-preserving diffeomorphism, meaning the Jacobian determinant satisfies $\det d\phi > 0$ everywhere. This stronger equivalence relation is used to define oriented local Riemannian surfaces, where the orientation structure is part of the data.
