Let $c$ be a curve on a Riemannian surface $M$, represented in local coordinates $(U, g)$ by $u(t)$, $t_0 \leq t \leq t_1$. The length $L(c)$ and the energy $E(c)$ are defined by

$$L(c) := \int_{t_0}^{t_1} \sqrt{\sum_{i,k} g_{ik} \circ u(t) \, \dot{u}^i(t) \, \dot{u}^k(t)} \, dt,$$

$$E(c) := \frac{1}{2} \int_{t_0}^{t_1} \sum_{i,k} g_{ik} \circ u(t) \, \dot{u}^i(t) \, \dot{u}^k(t) \, dt,$$

and are invariantly defined (independent of the choice of representative within the isometry class).
