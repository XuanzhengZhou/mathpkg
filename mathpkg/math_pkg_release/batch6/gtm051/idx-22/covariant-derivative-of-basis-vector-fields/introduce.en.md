The covariant derivative of a coordinate basis vector field $e_i$ along the direction $\partial / \partial u^j$ is expressed as a linear combination of the basis fields $e_k$ with coefficients given by the Christoffel symbols $\Gamma_{ij}^k$. This defines the Levi-Civita connection on the local Riemannian surface in coordinates, and it extends to arbitrary vector fields by the Leibniz rule, enabling the computation of covariant derivatives of tensor fields, parallel transport, and geodesics.
