A 2-form is an alternating bilinear form on a real vector space. It is a mapping from pairs of vectors to real numbers that is linear in each argument and changes sign when the arguments are swapped. The alternating property implies that a 2-form vanishes whenever its two arguments are equal. On a vector space of dimension 2, the space of all 2-forms is one-dimensional, spanned by the determinant-like wedge product $e^1 \wedge e^2$.
