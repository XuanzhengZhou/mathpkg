The set $\Lambda^2 T^*$ of all 2-forms on a vector space $T$ forms a vector space under pointwise addition and scalar multiplication. This space is called the second exterior power (or exterior square) of the dual space $T^*$. When the underlying space $T$ has dimension $n$, the dimension of $\Lambda^2 T^*$ is $\binom{n}{2}$. In the special case $n = 2$, the space is one-dimensional and provides the algebraic foundation for defining area elements and differential 2-forms on surfaces.
