When the underlying vector space $T$ has dimension 2, the space $\Lambda^2 T^*$ of all alternating bilinear forms is one-dimensional. Any 2-form $\Omega$ can be expressed as a scalar multiple of the determinant form $e^1 \wedge e^2$. This fact is fundamental for differential geometry on surfaces: it means that at each point of a surface, there is essentially only one non-trivial area 2-form up to a scalar factor, which encodes the notion of oriented area.
