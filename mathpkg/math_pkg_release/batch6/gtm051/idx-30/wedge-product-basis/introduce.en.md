Given a basis $e_1, e_2$ of a 2-dimensional vector space $T$, the wedge product $e^1 \wedge e^2$ is the 2-form that assigns to a pair of vectors the determinant of their coordinate representations. In coordinates, $e^1 \wedge e^2(X, Y) = x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1$, which measures the signed area of the parallelogram spanned by $X$ and $Y$. This element spans the one-dimensional space $\Lambda^2 T^*$ and serves as the fundamental building block for defining area 2-forms on surfaces and, more generally, differential forms on manifolds.
