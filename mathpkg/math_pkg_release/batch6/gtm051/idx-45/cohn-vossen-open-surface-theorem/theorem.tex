Let $M$ be a complete open (i.e., non-compact) surface on which the Gauss curvature is everywhere positive, $K > 0$. Then:

(i) $M$ is diffeomorphic to the plane $\mathbb{R}^2$;

(ii) $\int_M K\,dM \leq 2\pi$;

(iii) There are no closed geodesics on $M$;

(iv) Every geodesic has at most one self-intersection from which it runs off to infinity in both directions (it leaves every compact subset of $M$);

(v) Any two complete geodesics must intersect;

(vi) Through each $p \in M$ there passes at least one complete geodesic without self-intersection.
