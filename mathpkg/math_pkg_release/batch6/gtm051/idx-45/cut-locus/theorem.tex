Let $M$ be a compact Riemannian surface. For any $p \in M$, the cut locus $C(p)$ of $p$ is defined as follows. Associated to each tangent vector $X \in S^1_p M \subset T_p M$ there exists a well-defined extended real number $t(X) > 0$ for which:

(a) the unique geodesic $c_X(t) = \exp_p tX$ with initial condition $X$ is length-minimizing on $[0, t(X)]$;

(b) for every $t > t(X)$, $d(p, c_X(t)) < t$.

The map $c_X(t) \mapsto t(X)X \in T_p M$ is continuous. The image of this map is a non-self-intersecting closed curve in $T_p M$. The image of this curve under $\exp_p$ is
$$C(p) = \{c_X(t(X)) \mid X \in S^1_p M\}.$$
The complement of $C(p)$ in $M$ may be contracted radially onto $p$: each such point $q$ is of the form $\exp_p(t_0 X_0)$ with $t_0 < t(X_0)$.
