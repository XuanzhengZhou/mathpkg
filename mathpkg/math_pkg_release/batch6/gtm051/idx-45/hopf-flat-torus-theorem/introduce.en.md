E. Hopf proved a rigidity result for the torus: any Riemannian metric on a topological torus that has no conjugate points along any geodesic must be flat. This is a converse to the fact that the flat torus, which satisfies $K \equiv 0$, is free of conjugate points (by the Jacobi field criterion). The result connects the global topological constraint of being homeomorphic to a torus (which forces $\int_M K\,dM = 0$ via Gauss-Bonnet) with the local curvature-conjugate point relationship.
