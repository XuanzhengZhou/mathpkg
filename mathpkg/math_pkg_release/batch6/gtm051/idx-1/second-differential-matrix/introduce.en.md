The second differential $d^2F_{x_0}$ is the differential of the map $dF$, which takes values in the space $L(\mathbb{R}^n, \mathbb{R}^m)$ of linear transformations (an $n \cdot m$-dimensional vector space). Its matrix representation involves all second-order partial derivatives $\partial^2 F^i / \partial x^j \partial x^k$. Unlike the first differential whose matrix is the familiar $m \times n$ Jacobian, the second differential is represented by an $m \times (n \cdot m)$ matrix where the column index runs over all ordered pairs $(j, k)$. This object encodes the local quadratic behavior of $F$ and is essential for studying curvature in differential geometry.
