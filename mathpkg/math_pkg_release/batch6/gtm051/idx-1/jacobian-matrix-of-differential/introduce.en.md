The abstract definition of the differential $dF_{x_0}$ as a linear map is concretely realized by the Jacobian matrix of first-order partial derivatives. Each entry $\partial F^i / \partial x^j$ measures the instantaneous rate of change of the $i$-th component of $F$ along the $j$-th coordinate direction. This representation makes the differential computable and is fundamental for the chain rule, inverse function theorem, and change of variables in multiple integrals.
