Differentiability of a mapping $F$ is typically defined only on open domains where limits make sense. To handle mappings defined on arbitrary subsets $A \subset \mathbb{R}^n$, one requires the existence of a differentiable extension $G$ to some open neighborhood $U$ of $A$. This definition ensures that derivatives can still be discussed when the domain is, for instance, a closed subset or a manifold with boundary.
