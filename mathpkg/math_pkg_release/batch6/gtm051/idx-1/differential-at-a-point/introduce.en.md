The differential of a mapping between Euclidean spaces generalizes the derivative from single-variable calculus to higher dimensions. At a point $x_0$, the differential $dF_{x_0}$ is the unique linear transformation that best approximates $F$ near $x_0$, with the error being $o(|x - x_0|)$. This linear map serves as the foundation for all differential-geometric constructions on manifolds, where tangent maps between tangent spaces play the role of derivatives.
