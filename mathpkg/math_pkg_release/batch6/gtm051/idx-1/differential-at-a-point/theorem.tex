Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be an open set and $F: U \to \mathbb{R}^m$ a mapping. Suppose $F$ is differentiable at $x_0 \in U$, i.e., there exists a linear map $L \in L(\mathbb{R}^n, \mathbb{R}^m)$ such that

$$F(x) = F(x_0) + L(x - x_0) + o(|x - x_0|) \quad \text{as } x \to x_0.$$

The unique linear map $L = L(F, x_0)$ is called the \text{differential} of $F$ at $x_0$, denoted by $dF_{x_0}$ or simply $dF$.
