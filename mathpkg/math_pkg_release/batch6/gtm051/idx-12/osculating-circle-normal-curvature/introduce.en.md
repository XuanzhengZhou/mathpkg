This proposition, a typical result of classical surface theory, relates the osculating circle of a curve on a surface to the normal curvature. For a curve $c$ on a surface $f$ whose tangent direction at $t_0$ is aligned with a unit vector $X$ with nonzero normal curvature, the osculating circle is obtained by intersecting the osculating plane with a sphere of radius $1/|\kappa(X)|$ centered at the point $f(u_0) + n(u_0)/\kappa(X)$. This provides a geometric interpretation of normal curvature through the osculating circle.
