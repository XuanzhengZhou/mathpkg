The normal curvature $\kappa(X)$ of a surface $f$ at a point in the direction of a unit tangent vector $X$ is defined as the value of the second fundamental form $II$ evaluated on $(X,X)$. It measures how much the surface bends in the direction $X$, and satisfies $\kappa(X) = \kappa(-X)$ by symmetry of the second fundamental form. When $II(X,X) \neq 0$, the absolute value $|\kappa(X)|$ equals the curvature of a curve through the point that lies in a normal section at that point.
