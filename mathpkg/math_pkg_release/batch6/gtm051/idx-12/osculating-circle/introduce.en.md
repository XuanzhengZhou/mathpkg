The osculating circle of a space curve at a point is the circle that best approximates the curve at that point, lying in the osculating plane spanned by the unit tangent $e_1(t)$ and the principal normal $e_2(t)$. Its radius is the reciprocal of the curvature $1/\kappa(t)$, and its center is located at $c(t) + e_2(t)/\kappa(t)$. The osculating circle can be characterized as the limit of circles passing through three points on the curve as those points approach the given point.
