A curve on a surface lies in a normal section at a point if its principal normal vector coincides (up to sign) with the surface normal at that point. This condition means the osculating plane of the curve at that point is perpendicular to the tangent plane of the surface. Normal sections are important because they relate the curvature of curves on the surface to the normal curvature: for a curve in a normal section, the magnitude of the normal curvature equals the ordinary curvature of the curve.
