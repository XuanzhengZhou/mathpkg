This definition constructs the tangent space of an abstract differentiable surface (manifold of dimension $2$) without reference to an ambient Euclidean space. The key idea is to use chart maps to pull back the tangent space of $\mathbb{R}^2$: each chart $(u_\alpha, M_\alpha)$ provides a representative $X_\alpha \in T_{u_\alpha(p)} \mathbb{R}^2$ for a prospective tangent vector. When two charts overlap, their representatives are identified via the differential of the transition map $d(u_\beta \circ u_\alpha^{-1})$, which is a linear isomorphism. The equivalence classes under this relation form the tangent space $T_p M$, a $2$-dimensional real vector space whose structure is chart-independent. This definition generalizes directly to manifolds of arbitrary dimension.
