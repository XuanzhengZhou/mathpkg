This proposition gives the Taylor series expansion of the scalar coefficient $y(t)$ of a Jacobi field $Y(t) = y(t)e_2(t)$ along a geodesic $c(t)$, normalized so that $y(0) = 0$ and $\dot{y}(0) = 1$. The expansion shows that the Jacobi field initially grows linearly in $t$, with a cubic correction proportional to the Gauss curvature $K$ at $c(0)$. This result is a direct consequence of the Jacobi differential equation $\ddot{y} + K \circ c(t) \cdot y(t) = 0$, and it plays a crucial role in the geometric interpretation of curvature as controlling the deviation of nearby geodesics.
