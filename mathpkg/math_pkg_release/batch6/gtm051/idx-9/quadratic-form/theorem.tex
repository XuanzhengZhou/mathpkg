Let $T$ be a real vector space. A symmetric bilinear form or a quadratic form is a map $\beta: T \times T \to \mathbb{R}$ satisfying
$$\beta(X, Y) = \beta(Y, X) \quad \text{(symmetry)}$$
$$\beta(aX + bY, Z) = a\beta(X, Z) + b\beta(Y, Z) \quad \text{(bilinearity)}$$
for all $X, Y, Z \in T$ and $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$.
