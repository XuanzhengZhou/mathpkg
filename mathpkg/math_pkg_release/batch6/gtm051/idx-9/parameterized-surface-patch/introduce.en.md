A parameterized surface patch is a differentiable map from an open subset of the plane into three-dimensional Euclidean space whose differential is everywhere injective. This injectivity condition ensures the image is locally a smooth two-dimensional manifold without singularities. The definition provides the fundamental local model for surfaces in classical differential geometry, generalizing the notion of a regular curve to two parameters.
