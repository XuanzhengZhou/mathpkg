Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface. A change of variables of $f$ is a diffeomorphism
$$\phi: V \subset \mathbb{R}^2 \to U \subset \mathbb{R}^2,$$
where $V$ is an open set in $\mathbb{R}^2$, such that $d\phi$ always has rank $= 2$. If $\det(d\phi) > 0$, $\phi$ is called orientation preserving. The surface $\tilde{f} := f \circ \phi: V \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is said to be related to $f$ by the change of variables $\phi$.
