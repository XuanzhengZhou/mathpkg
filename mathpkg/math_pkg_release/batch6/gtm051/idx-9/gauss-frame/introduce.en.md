The Gauss frame is the natural moving frame attached to a parameterized surface, consisting of the two tangent basis vectors (partial derivatives of the parametrization) together with the unit normal vector. Although not orthonormal in general, this frame provides the fundamental reference system for expressing geometric quantities such as the first and second fundamental forms and the Christoffel symbols.
