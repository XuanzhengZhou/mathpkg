The matrix representation of a quadratic form with respect to a basis encodes the form's values on basis vectors. Given a choice of basis, the form's evaluation on arbitrary vectors reduces to a bilinear expression in coordinates with the matrix entries as coefficients. The transformation law $G = A H {}^t\!A$ describes how the matrix changes under a change of basis, a fundamental fact used to ensure geometric quantities are well-defined independent of coordinates.
