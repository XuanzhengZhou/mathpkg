Let $T$ be a real vector space and $\beta: T \times T \to \mathbb{R}$ a quadratic form. The matrix representation of $\beta$ with respect to a basis $\{e_i\}$, $1 \leq i \leq n$, of $T$ is the matrix
$$(g_{ij}) := (\beta(e_i, e_j)).$$
If $X = \sum_i \xi_i e_i$ and $Y = \sum_j \eta_j e_j$, then
$$\beta(X, Y) = \sum_{i,j} \xi_i \eta_j g_{ij}.$$
Under a change of basis $e_i = \sum_k a_i^k f_k$, the matrix transforms as
$$G = A \cdot H \cdot {}^t\!A,$$
where $G = (g_{ij})$, $A = (a_i^j)$, $H = (h_i^j)$, and the dot denotes matrix multiplication.
