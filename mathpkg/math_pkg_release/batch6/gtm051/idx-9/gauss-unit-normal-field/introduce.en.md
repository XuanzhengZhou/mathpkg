The Gauss unit normal field assigns to each point of a parameterized surface a unit vector orthogonal to the tangent space, obtained by normalizing the cross product of the partial derivatives. The Gauss map is the corresponding mapping from the parameter domain to the unit sphere $S^2$, and it encodes the bending of the surface in space. This map is the central object for defining the second fundamental form and Gaussian curvature.
