An induced quadratic form is obtained by pulling back a quadratic form along a linear map. When the linear map is injective and the original form is positive definite, the induced form inherits positive definiteness. This construction is the fundamental mechanism by which the Euclidean inner product on $\mathbb{R}^3$ induces the first fundamental form on the tangent space of a surface.
