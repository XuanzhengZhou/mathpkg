Let $L: S \to T$ be a linear mapping between vector spaces $S$ and $T$. Suppose $\beta$ is a quadratic form on $T$. Then, for $X, Y \in S$,
$$\alpha(X, Y) := \beta(LX, LY)$$
defines a quadratic form on $S$. The form $\alpha$ is said to be induced by $\beta$ via $L$. If $L$ is injective and $\beta$ is positive definite, then $\alpha$ is also positive definite.
