Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface. For each $u \in U$, the inner product on $\mathbb{R}^3 \cong T_{f(u)} \mathbb{R}^3$ induces a quadratic form on the tangent space $T_u f \subset T_{f(u)} \mathbb{R}^3$ via the inclusion map. This quadratic form is called the first fundamental form of $f$ at $u$ and is denoted by $I_u$ (or $I$). Explicitly, for $X, Y \in T_u f$,
$$I_u(X, Y) := X \cdot Y.$$
The matrix representation of $I_u$ with respect to the basis $\{f_u^1, f_u^2\}$ of $T_u f$ is
$$(g_{ik}(u)) := (f_u^i(u) \cdot f_u^k(u)).$$
