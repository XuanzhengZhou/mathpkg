The tangent space of a parameterized surface at a point is the image of the differential map, forming a two-dimensional linear subspace of the ambient tangent space to $\mathbb{R}^3$. Since the differential is injective, the tangent space is indeed two-dimensional and provides the linear approximation to the surface at each parameter point. Tangent vectors are the elements of this subspace, representing infinitesimal directions along the surface.
