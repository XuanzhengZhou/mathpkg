A conformal mapping between Riemannian surfaces scales the metric tensor by a positive scalar function at each point. Because the angle between two tangent vectors is computed as a ratio involving the metric, the scaling factor cancels out, leaving the angle unchanged. Thus conformal mappings are precisely the angle-preserving diffeomorphisms in Riemannian geometry. This property makes them essential in complex analysis and the geometry of surfaces, where conformal coordinates simplify the study of curvature and geodesics.
