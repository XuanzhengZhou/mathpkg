A Jacobi field is a vector field along a geodesic that satisfies the Jacobi equation $\nabla^2 Y/dt^2 + (K \circ c) Y = 0$, where $K$ is the Gauss curvature and $\nabla$ is the covariant derivative. Jacobi fields are always orthogonal to the geodesic and describe the infinitesimal variation of geodesics -- they measure how nearby geodesics spread apart or converge. When expressed in the Frenet frame $e_1(t), e_2(t)$ along $c$, the Jacobi equation reduces to the scalar ODE $\ddot{y}(t) + K(c(t)) y(t) = 0$, making it an ordinary differential equation whose coefficient is the Gauss curvature along the geodesic.
