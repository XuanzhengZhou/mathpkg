This lemma provides a geometric construction of Jacobi fields via geodesic polar coordinates. By considering the variation $\phi(t,\theta) = \exp_p(t\cos\theta\,e_1 + t\sin\theta\,e_2)$ of geodesics emanating from $p$, the derivative $Y(t) = \partial\phi/\partial\theta(t,0)$ yields a Jacobi field along the central geodesic $c(t)$. Crucially, this connects the metric coefficient $g_{22}$ in geodesic polar coordinates to the norm of the Jacobi field: $g_{22}(t,0) = |Y(t)|^2$. This relationship allows the Gauss curvature to be expressed as $K = -(\sqrt{g_{22}})_{11}/\sqrt{g_{22}}$ in geodesic coordinates.
