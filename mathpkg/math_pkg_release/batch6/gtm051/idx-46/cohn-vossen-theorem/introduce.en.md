The Cohn-Vossen theorem characterizes the global geometry of complete open (non-compact) surfaces with everywhere strictly positive Gauss curvature. Such surfaces are necessarily diffeomorphic to the Euclidean plane, and their total Gaussian curvature is bounded above by $2\pi$. The theorem further gives detailed qualitative information about geodesic behavior: closed geodesics cannot exist, every geodesic has at most one self-intersection, any two complete geodesics intersect, and through every point there passes a complete self-intersection-free geodesic. The paraboloid of revolution in $\mathbb{R}^3$ serves as the canonical example.
