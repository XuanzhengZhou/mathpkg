Suppose $M$ is a compact Riemannian surface. For any $p \in M$, the cut locus $C(p)$ of $p$ is defined as follows.

Associated to each tangent vector $X \in S_p^1 M \subset T_p M$ there exists a well-defined extended real number $t(X) > 0$ for which

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] the unique geodesic $c_X(t) = \exp_p tX$ with initial condition $X$ is length-measuring on $[0, t(X)]$;
  \item[(b)] for every $t > t(X)$, $d(p, c_X(t)) < t$.
\end{enumerate}

The map $X \mapsto t(X)X \in T_p M$ is continuous. The image of this map is a non-self-intersecting closed curve in $T_p M$. The image of this curve under $\exp_p$ is
$$C(p) = \{ c_X(t(X)) \mid X \in S_p^1 M \}.$$
