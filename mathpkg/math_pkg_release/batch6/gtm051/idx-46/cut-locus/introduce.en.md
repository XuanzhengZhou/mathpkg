The cut locus $C(p)$ of a point $p$ on a compact Riemannian surface $M$ is the set of points where geodesics emanating from $p$ cease to be globally length-minimizing. For each unit tangent direction $X \in S_p^1 M$, the quantity $t(X)$ is the supremum of times for which the radial geodesic $\exp_p tX$ remains a shortest path. The cut locus is the image under the exponential map of the closed curve formed by the vectors $t(X)X$ in $T_p M$, forming a fundamental geometric object for analyzing the global structure of the surface.
