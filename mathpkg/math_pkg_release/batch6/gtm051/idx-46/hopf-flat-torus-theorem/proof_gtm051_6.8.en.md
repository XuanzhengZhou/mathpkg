The proof of Hopf's theorem is not included in the source text. The text merely cites the result as proved by E. Hopf. The flat torus satisfies $K \equiv 0$ (see the second example in (6.7)), and by (6.5.6 (i)) the flat torus is free of conjugate points. Hopf proved the converse: the absence of conjugate points forces the metric to be flat.
