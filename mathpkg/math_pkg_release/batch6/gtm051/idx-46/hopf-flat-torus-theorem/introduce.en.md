Hopf's flat torus theorem provides a converse to the observation that the flat torus has no conjugate points. It states that if a surface homeomorphic to a torus admits a Riemannian metric in which no geodesic possesses a conjugate point, then the metric must be flat everywhere. This characterizes the flat torus uniquely among metrics on the torus by the absence of conjugate points along geodesics.
