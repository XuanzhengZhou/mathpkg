The Riemann curvature tensor $R_{ijkl}$ of a surface is defined in coordinates using the Christoffel symbols and their first derivatives. The components $R_{ijk}^m$ measure the non-commutativity of covariant differentiation on the surface. Despite being defined through the extrinsic Christoffel symbols, the curvature tensor is actually an intrinsic quantity — it can be expressed entirely in terms of the first fundamental form $g_{ij}$ and its derivatives. For a surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$, the curvature tensor is completely determined by the single independent component $R_{1212}$, which equals $\det(h_{ij})$, the determinant of the second fundamental form.
