The Christoffel symbols are the coefficients that appear when expressing the second partial derivatives of a surface parametrization in terms of the tangent vectors and the normal vector. The Christoffel symbols of the first kind $\Gamma_{kij}$ are symmetric in the first two indices while those of the second kind $\Gamma_{ik}^l$ are symmetric in the lower indices. Both are determined entirely by the first fundamental form $g_{ij}$ and its first partial derivatives, making them intrinsic quantities of the surface. They serve as the connection coefficients that define the Levi-Civita connection on the surface.
