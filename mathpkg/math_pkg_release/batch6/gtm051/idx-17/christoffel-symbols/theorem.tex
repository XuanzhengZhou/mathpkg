The six functions $\Gamma_{ik}^l(u) = \Gamma_{ki}^l(u)$ appearing in the Gauss formula

$$
f_{ik}(u) = \sum_l \Gamma_{ik}^l(u) f_l(u) + h_{ik}(u) n(u)
$$

are called the **Christoffel symbols of the second kind**. The functions

$$
\Gamma_{kij} = \frac{1}{2}(g_{ij,k} + g_{jk,i} - g_{ki,j})
$$

are called the **Christoffel symbols of the first kind**, where $g_{ij,k} = \partial g_{ij} / \partial u^k$ and $(g_{ij})$ is the matrix representation of the first fundamental form. The two kinds are related by

$$
\Gamma_{ik}^l = \sum_j g^{lj} \Gamma_{j,ik},
$$

where $(g^{ij})$ is the inverse matrix of $(g_{ij})$.
