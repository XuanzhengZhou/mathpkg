The Gauss-Codazzi-Mainardi equations are the integrability conditions for the existence of a surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with prescribed first and second fundamental forms. The Gauss equation (i) relates the intrinsic curvature of the surface (expressed through Christoffel symbols and their derivatives) to the second fundamental form — it is the coordinate expression of Gauss's Theorema Egregium. The Codazzi-Mainardi equations (ii) provide compatibility conditions between the Christoffel symbols and the second fundamental form. Together with the Gauss-Weingarten equations, these integrability conditions constitute the fundamental equations of classical surface theory and are essential for the Bonnet theorem on the existence and uniqueness of surfaces.
