The equations $f_{ijk} = f_{ikj}$ and $n_{ij} = n_{ji}$ (equality of mixed partial derivatives) are equivalent to the following relations between $g_{ik}$, $h_{ik}$, $g_{ik,l}$, $h_{ik,l}$, and the Christoffel symbols $\Gamma_{ij}^k$:

**(i) The Gauss equation:**

$$
\Gamma_{ij,k}^m - \Gamma_{ik,j}^m + \sum_l (\Gamma_{ij}^l \Gamma_{lk}^m - \Gamma_{ik}^l \Gamma_{lj}^m) = \sum_l (h_{ij} h_{kl} - h_{ik} h_{jl}) g^{lm}.
$$

**(ii) The Codazzi-Mainardi equations:**

$$
\sum_l \Gamma_{ij}^l h_{lk} - \sum_l \Gamma_{ik}^l h_{lj} + h_{ij,k} - h_{ik,j} = 0.
$$
