Lemma 3.8.5 provides the explicit algebraic relationship between the Riemann curvature tensor $R_{ijkl}$ and the second fundamental form $h_{ij}$ for a surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$. The formula $R_{ijkl} = h_{ij}h_{kl} - h_{ik}h_{jl}$ shows that the curvature tensor is completely determined by the determinant of $(h_{ij})$, with only one independent component $R_{1212}$. This result is a direct consequence of the Gauss equation (Theorem 3.8.3, i) and leads immediately to Gauss's Theorema Egregium: since the curvature tensor is expressible solely through the Christoffel symbols (Definition 3.8.4), and the Christoffel symbols depend only on the first fundamental form, the Gauss curvature $K = \det(h_{ij}) / \det(g_{ij})$ is an intrinsic quantity of the surface.
