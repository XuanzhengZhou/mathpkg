Given a ruled surface parametrized as $f(s, t) = s X(t) + c(t)$, the curves obtained by fixing $t$ and varying $s$ are straight line segments in $\mathbb{R}^3$. These lines are called the generators of the ruled surface. They play a central role in the local geometry: for example, Proposition 3.7.5 shows that generators are asymptotic curves, which forces the Gauss curvature $K \leq 0$ on any ruled surface.
