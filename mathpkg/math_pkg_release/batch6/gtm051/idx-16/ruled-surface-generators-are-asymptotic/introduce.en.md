Proposition 3.7.5 establishes two fundamental connections between ruling structure and curvature. Part (i) shows that the straight-line generators of any ruled surface are asymptotic curves, which directly forces the Gauss curvature to be non-positive ($K \leq 0$). Part (ii) gives three equivalent characterizations of developability: the surface is developable if and only if the mixed second derivative $f_{st}$ lies in the span of $f_s$ and $f_t$, which in turn is equivalent to the vanishing of the Gauss curvature $K = 0$.
