A ruled surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$ is a surface that admits a local parametrization $f(s, t) = s X(t) + c(t)$, where $X(t)$ is a vector field along a base curve $c(t)$. The name comes from the fact that through each point of the surface passes a straight line (a "rule"), namely the $t = \text{constant}$ curves, which are called generators. The curves $s = \text{constant}$ trace out a directrix along the surface.
