**Proof.**

We compute:

$$g_{st} = \dot{Y}, \qquad n \cdot g_{st} = n \cdot \dot{Y} = -\dot{n} \cdot Y = II(\dot{c}, Y) = 0.$$

Since $n \cdot g_{st} = 0$, the second fundamental form $h_{12} = 0$, which implies $K = 0$ by the formula for Gauss curvature in $(s, t)$-parameters. By Proposition 3.7.5 (ii), a ruled surface with $K = 0$ is developable, so $g(s, t)$ is developable. $\square$
