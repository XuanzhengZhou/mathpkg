Proposition 3.7.7 constructs a developable surface $g(s, t)$ that osculates a given curve $c(t)$ on a surface $f$. Given any vector field $Y(t)$ tangential to $f$ and satisfying $II(\dot{c}(t), Y(t)) = 0$ (i.e., orthogonal in the second fundamental form to the tangent direction), the surface $g(s, t) = s Y(t) + c(t)$ is developable. This construction is used later in the book as a key step in defining parallel translation.
