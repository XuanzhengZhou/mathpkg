\begin{proposition}[3.7.7]
Let $c(t) = f \circ u(t)$ be a regular curve on a surface $f$. Suppose $Y(t)$ is a vector field along $c(t)$ tangential to $f$, satisfying $II(\dot{c}(t), Y(t)) = 0$, and linearly independent of $\dot{c}(t)$. Then $g(s, t) = s Y(t) + c(t)$ is a developable surface.
\end{proposition}
