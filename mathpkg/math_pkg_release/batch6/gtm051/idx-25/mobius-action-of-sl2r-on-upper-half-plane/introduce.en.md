The group $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ acts on the Poincar\'e upper half-plane $H_r^2$ via M\"obius (fractional linear) transformations $z \mapsto (az+b)/(cz+d)$. This action is well-defined because elements of $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ preserve the upper half-plane: the imaginary part of $(az+b)/(cz+d)$ remains positive whenever $v > 0$. This action is fundamental to hyperbolic geometry, as $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ serves as the isometry group of $H_r^2$.
