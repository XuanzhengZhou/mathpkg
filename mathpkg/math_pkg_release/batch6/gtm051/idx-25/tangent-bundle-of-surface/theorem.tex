The tangent bundle of a surface $M$, denoted by $\mathbb{T}_M$, is the set of all tangent vectors of $M$, together with the projection map $\pi: \mathbb{T}_M \rightarrow M$ which sends each $X \in \mathbb{T}_M$ to its base point. If $U$ is a coordinate neighborhood representing part of $M$, the pair $(\mathbb{T}_U, \pi_U: \mathbb{T}_U \rightarrow U)$ is called a representation of $\mathbb{T}_M$. The tangent bundle $\mathbb{T}_M$ possesses a natural differentiable structure inherited from the differentiable structure of its representatives, and this structure is independent of the choice of representative.
