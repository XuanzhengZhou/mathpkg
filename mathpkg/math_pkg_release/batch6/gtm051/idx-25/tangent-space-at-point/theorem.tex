The inverse image $\pi^{-1}(p)$ of a point $p \in M$ under the bundle projection $\pi: \mathbb{T}_M \rightarrow M$ is called the tangent space of $M$ at $p$, denoted $\mathbb{T}_p M$. The space $\mathbb{T}_p M$ consists of precisely those vectors in $\mathbb{T}_M$ whose base point equals $p$. If $\mathbb{T}_M$ is represented by $\mathbb{T}_U$ and $p$ is represented by $u$, then $\mathbb{T}_p M$ is represented by $\mathbb{T}_u \mathbb{R}^2$. Via this identification, $\mathbb{T}_p M$ has the structure of a two-dimensional real vector space with a positive definite inner product $g_p$ defined by $g_u$.
