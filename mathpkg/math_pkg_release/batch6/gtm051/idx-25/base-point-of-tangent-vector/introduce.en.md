The base point of a tangent vector $X$ to a surface $M$ is the point $p \in M$ at which the vector is attached. Given a local coordinate chart $U \subset M$, if $X$ is represented by a pair $(u, X_u)$ where $u \in U$ and $X_u$ is a vector in $\mathbb{T}_u \mathbb{R}^2$, then the base point is the point corresponding to $u$. This notion is well-defined and independent of the choice of coordinate chart.
