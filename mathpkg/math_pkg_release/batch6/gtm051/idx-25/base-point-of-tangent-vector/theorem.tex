Let $M$ be a surface. Every tangent vector $X$ to $M$ determines an element of $M$. If $X$ is represented by $(u, X_u) \in \mathbb{T}_u \mathbb{R}^2 \subset \mathbb{T}_U$, the point $p \in M$ represented by $u \in U$ is called the base point of the tangent vector $X$. The base point of $X$ is defined independently of the choice of coordinate systems.
