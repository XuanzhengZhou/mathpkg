The norm $|X|$ of a tangent vector $X$ on a Riemannian surface $M$ is defined using the Riemannian metric $g$: in local coordinates, $|X| = \sqrt{g_u(X_u, X_u)}$. This norm gives the length of the tangent vector and is well-defined independent of the choice of coordinate representative. It equips each tangent space $\mathbb{T}_p M$ with the structure of an inner product space, reflecting the geometry of $M$ at the infinitesimal level.
