Let $c = f \circ u$ be a curve on a surface $f$, parameterized by arc length. The normal curvature $\kappa_n$ of $c$ is the component of the acceleration $\ddot{c}$ in the direction of the surface normal $n$. It satisfies
$$\kappa_n(t) = II_{u(t)}(\dot{u}(t), \dot{u}(t)) = \kappa(t) \cos \theta(t)$$
where $\theta(t)$ is the angle between the principal normal of $c$ and the surface normal.
