On a surface, each nontrivial element of the fundamental group can be represented by a closed geodesic. The construction proceeds by lifting the group element to a covering transformation on the universal cover and minimizing the distance between a point and its image under the transformation. The minimizing geodesic segment, when extended by the group action, yields a $\gamma$-invariant geodesic that projects to a closed geodesic on the original surface. In the negatively curved case, this invariant geodesic is unique up to parameter translation.
