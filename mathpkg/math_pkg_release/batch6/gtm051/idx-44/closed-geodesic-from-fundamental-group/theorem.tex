Let $M$ be a surface with universal covering $\pi: \tilde{M} \to M$ and fundamental group $\Gamma$. For every nontrivial element $\gamma \in \Gamma$, $\gamma \neq 1$, there exists a $\gamma$-invariant geodesic $\tilde{c}: \mathbb{R} \to \tilde{M}$ satisfying

$$\gamma \circ \tilde{c}(t) = \tilde{c}(t + \omega), \quad \omega > 0,$$

which projects to a closed geodesic $c = \pi \circ \tilde{c}$ in $M$ of length $\omega$.

Moreover:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(iii)] If the Gauss curvature $K < 0$, then for each $\gamma \in \Gamma$ there exists at most one (up to parameter translation) $\gamma$-invariant geodesic in $\tilde{M}$.
\item[(iv)] If $\gamma, \gamma' \in \Gamma$ are nontrivial commuting elements with $K < 0$, then the associated invariant geodesics $\tilde{c}$ and $\tilde{c}'$ satisfy $\tilde{c}'(t) = \tilde{c}(t + t_0)$ for some $t_0 \in \mathbb{R}$.
\end{itemize}
