A nontrivial element of the fundamental group of a surface, when lifted to act as a covering transformation on the universal cover, must be fixed-point free. This follows from the simple connectivity of the universal cover: if a covering transformation fixed a point, it would agree with the identity transformation in a neighborhood, and thus globally, contradicting nontriviality. This property is essential for the construction of closed geodesics from elements of the fundamental group.
