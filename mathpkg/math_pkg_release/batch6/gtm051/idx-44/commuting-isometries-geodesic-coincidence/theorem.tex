Let $M$ be a surface with universal covering $\tilde{M}$ and fundamental group $\Gamma$. Suppose the Gauss curvature satisfies $K < 0$. Let $\gamma, \gamma' \in \Gamma$ be nontrivial commuting elements. Then the $\gamma$-invariant geodesic $\tilde{c}$ and the $\gamma'$-invariant geodesic $\tilde{c}'$ in $\tilde{M}$ coincide up to a parameter translation:

$$\tilde{c}'(t) = \tilde{c}(t + t_0)$$

for some $t_0 \in \mathbb{R}$.
