When two nontrivial elements of the fundamental group commute and the surface has negative Gauss curvature, their associated invariant geodesics must coincide up to a parameter shift. This follows from the uniqueness result (Theorem 6.7.1 part iii) together with the fact that if $\gamma$ and $\gamma'$ commute, then $\gamma'$ maps the $\gamma$-invariant geodesic to another $\gamma$-invariant geodesic, forcing them to be the same curve.
