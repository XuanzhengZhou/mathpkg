Let $M$ be a compact surface and let $\gamma: M \to M$ be a nontrivial fixed-point free isometry. Then there exists a $\gamma$-invariant closed geodesic. More precisely, there exists a unit-speed geodesic $c: \mathbb{R} \to M$ and a constant $\omega > 0$ such that

$$\gamma \circ c(t) = c(t + \omega)$$

for all $t \in \mathbb{R}$. If $\gamma^n = \text{id}$ for some $n \in \mathbb{N}$, then $c(t)$ for $0 \leq t \leq n\omega$ is a closed geodesic.
