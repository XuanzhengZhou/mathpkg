On a compact surface, every nontrivial fixed-point free isometry admits an invariant closed geodesic. The proof uses a variational argument: the continuous function $f(p) = d(p, \gamma p)$ attains a positive minimum on the compact surface, and the minimizing geodesic segment between $p$ and $\gamma p$ extends to a $\gamma$-invariant geodesic. When the isometry has finite order, this geodesic closes after finitely many periods, yielding a genuine closed geodesic.
