The text references the proof by Chern [A5], p. 23. A standard proof uses the fact that by the Jordan curve theorem, a simple closed plane curve divides the plane into two disjoint regions, one of which is bounded. The inequality $L^2 \geq 4\pi A$ is then proved using the parametrization of the curve by arc length and the Wirtinger inequality.
