The isoperimetric inequality is a fundamental result in global differential geometry, stating that among all simple closed plane curves of a given length, the circle uniquely maximizes the enclosed area. Equivalently, for a given enclosed area, the circle minimizes the perimeter. This is a classic result bounding $L^2 - 4\pi A \geq 0$ for any simple closed curve, with equality characterizing the circle.
