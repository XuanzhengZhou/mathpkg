For a closed, strictly convex plane curve of constant width $d$, its total circumference equals $\pi d$, analogous to the familiar formula for a circle of diameter $d$. This result is a consequence of the parametrization by tangent angle and the relationship between antipodal points on a constant-width curve, following from Barbier's theorem.
