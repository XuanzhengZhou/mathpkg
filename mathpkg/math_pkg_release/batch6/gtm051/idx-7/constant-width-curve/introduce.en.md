A closed, strictly convex plane curve $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ is said to have constant width $d$ if for every point $c(t)$ there exists a unique antipodal point $c(t')$ (where the unit tangent vectors satisfy $e_1(t) = -e_1(t')$) such that the Euclidean distance $d(c(t), c(t'))$ equals the constant $d$ independent of $t$. This concept generalizes the circle, which has constant width equal to its diameter, and appears in the study of convex geometry and global differential geometry of plane curves.
