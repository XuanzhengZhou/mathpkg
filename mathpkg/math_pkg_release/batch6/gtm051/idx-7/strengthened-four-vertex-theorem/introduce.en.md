This theorem strengthens the classical four-vertex theorem by characterizing the precise structure of the curvature function of a simple closed convex curve: either the curvature is constant (yielding a circle), or it exhibits exactly two strict local maxima and two strict local minima, with the maximum values strictly exceeding the minimum values. This provides a more detailed description of the oscillatory behavior of curvature.
