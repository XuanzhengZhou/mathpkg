Let $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be a simple closed convex plane curve with curvature function $\kappa(t)$. Then either $\kappa \equiv \text{constant} \neq 0$ (so $c$ is a circle), or $\kappa$ has exactly two relative maxima and two relative minima, and the values of $\kappa$ at the relative maxima are strictly greater than the values of $\kappa$ at the relative minima.
