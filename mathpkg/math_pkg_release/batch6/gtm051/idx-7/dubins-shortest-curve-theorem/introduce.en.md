Dubins' theorem solves the problem of finding the shortest $C^1$ curve with prescribed endpoints and tangent directions, subject to a bound on the average curvature. The optimal paths are composed of at most three pieces, each being either a circular arc of the maximal allowed curvature or a straight line segment. This result has applications in robotics and motion planning, where it describes the shortest feasible path for a vehicle with a minimum turning radius.
