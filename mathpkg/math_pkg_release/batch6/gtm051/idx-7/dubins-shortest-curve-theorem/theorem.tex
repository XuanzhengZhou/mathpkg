Given $p, q \in \mathbb{R}^2$ and unit vectors $X \in T_p\mathbb{R}^2$, $Y \in T_q\mathbb{R}^2$, consider the problem of finding a curve of shortest length from $p$ to $q$ with initial direction $X$ and final direction $Y$. A solution does not always exist (e.g., when $p \neq q$ and $X \perp Y$). However, if the class of curves is restricted to those with bounded average curvature (less than or equal to $1/r$ for some $r > 0$) and $C^1$ curves are allowed, then a solution always exists. The optimal curves consist of circular arcs of radius $r$ and line segments, with at most three distinct arcs on any solution curve.
