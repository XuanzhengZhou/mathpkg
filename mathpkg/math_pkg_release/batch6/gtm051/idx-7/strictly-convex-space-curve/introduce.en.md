A closed space curve in $\mathbb{R}^3$ without self-intersections is called strictly convex if for every point on the curve there exists a supporting plane that intersects the curve only at that single point. This definition generalizes the notion of strict convexity from plane curves to space curves and is used in higher-dimensional analogues of the four-vertex theorem.
