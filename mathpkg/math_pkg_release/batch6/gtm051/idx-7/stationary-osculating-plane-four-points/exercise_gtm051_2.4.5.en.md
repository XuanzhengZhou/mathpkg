The four vertex theorem can be derived from the following result concerning closed curves $c$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with no self-intersections. Suppose $c$ is strictly convex, in the sense that through each point of $c$ there passes a plane which has no other points in common with $c$. Then $c$ has at least four points with stationary osculating plane; i.e., four points $c(t)$ where $\tau(t) = 0$. For a proof of this result, see Barner.
