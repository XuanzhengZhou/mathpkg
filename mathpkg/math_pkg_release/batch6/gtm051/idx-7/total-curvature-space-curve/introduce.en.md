For a closed space curve $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^n$ ($n \geq 3$) with positive curvature $\kappa > 0$, the total curvature $K(c)$ is the integral of the absolute curvature over the entire curve length. This global invariant generalizes the total curvature concept from plane curves (where $\int \kappa\,ds = 2\pi$ for simple closed curves by the Umlaufsatz) to higher dimensions.
