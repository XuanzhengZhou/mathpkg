Fenchel's theorem states that the total curvature of any closed curve in $\mathbb{R}^n$ ($n \geq 3$) is at least $2\pi$, generalizing the result for plane curves (where $\int \kappa\,ds = 2\pi$ for simple closed curves). Equality $K(c) = 2\pi$ holds precisely when the curve is a convex plane curve. This is a fundamental global theorem in the differential geometry of space curves.
