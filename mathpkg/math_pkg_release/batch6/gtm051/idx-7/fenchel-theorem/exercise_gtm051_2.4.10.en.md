A proof of Fenchel's theorem. i) Prove: Let $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be a closed curve lying on $S^{n-1}(r) = \{x \in \mathbb{R}^n \mid |x| = r\}$, i.e., $|c(t)| = r$, $t \in [0, \omega]$. Suppose $c$ does not lie in any open hemisphere of $S^{n-1}(r)$. Then the length of $c$ is at least $2\pi r$. (A simple proof by Horn$^{13}$.) Using this result,
ii) Prove: Fenchel's theorem (2.4.9).

$^{13}$ Horn, R. A. On Fenchel's theorem. Amer. Math. Monthly, 78, 380-381 (1971).
