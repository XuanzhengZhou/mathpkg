For closed convex curves, the classical isoperimetric inequality $L^2 \geq 4\pi A$ admits a sharpening that incorporates the radius $r$ of the maximal inscribed circle (or minimal circumscribed circle). The refined lower bound $\frac{(A - \pi r^2)^2}{r^2}$ reduces to zero exactly when the curve is a circle of radius $r$, providing a quantitative measure of deviation from circularity. For further developments, see Osserman.
