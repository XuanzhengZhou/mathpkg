Loop concatenation defines an operation on the loop space $\Omega(p)$ that composes two based loops into a single loop. Given loops $\alpha$ and $\beta$, the concatenation $\beta * \alpha$ first traverses $\alpha$ at double speed on $[0, 1/2]$, then traverses $\beta$ on $[1/2, 1]$. This operation descends to homotopy classes and provides the group structure of the fundamental group $\pi_1(M)$, where the constant loop serves as the identity element.
