Suppose $M$ is connected, simply connected, and complete with $K \leq 0$ everywhere on $M$. Then for every $p \in M$,

$$\exp_p: T_p M \to M$$

is a bijective diffeomorphism. In other words, $M$ is diffeomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^2$. Moreover, there exists a unique minimal geodesic joining any two points $p$ and $q$ in $M$.
