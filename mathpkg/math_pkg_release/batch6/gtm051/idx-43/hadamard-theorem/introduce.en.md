Hadamard's theorem characterizes complete, simply connected surfaces of non-positive Gaussian curvature. It asserts that such a surface is globally diffeomorphic to the Euclidean plane $\mathbb{R}^2$ via the exponential map at any point. The theorem further guarantees the existence and uniqueness of a minimal geodesic between any two points, strengthening the Hopf–Rinow theorem in this setting. This is a foundational result in global differential geometry, demonstrating how a purely local curvature condition ($K \leq 0$) forces strong global topological and metric conclusions.
