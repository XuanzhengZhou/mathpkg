The loop space $\Omega(p)$ at a point $p$ on a surface $M$ is the set of all continuous closed curves based at $p$. This space serves as the underlying set for defining the fundamental group $\pi_1(M)$. Two loops in $\Omega(p)$ are considered equivalent if they are homotopic with fixed endpoints, and the fundamental group is the set of homotopy classes of loops with the group operation given by concatenation.
