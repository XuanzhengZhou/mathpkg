The fundamental group $\pi_1(M)$ of a surface $M$ is the group of homotopy classes of loops based at a point, with concatenation as the group operation. For a connected surface, $\pi_1(M)$ is independent of the base point up to isomorphism. The fundamental group is a topological invariant that encodes the one-dimensional hole structure of the surface: for the sphere $\pi_1(S^2)$ is trivial, while for the torus $\pi_1(T^2) \cong \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}$. The fundamental group acts on the simply-connected covering surface $\tilde{M}$ via deck transformations, providing a crucial link between closed geodesics and the topology of the surface.
