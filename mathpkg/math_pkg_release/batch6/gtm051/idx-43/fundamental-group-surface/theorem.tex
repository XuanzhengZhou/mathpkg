Two curves $\alpha, \beta \in \Omega(p)$ are called \emph{homotopic} if there exists a continuous map $H: [0,1] \times [0,1] \to M$ such that

$$H(t,0) = \alpha(t), \quad H(t,1) = \beta(t), \quad H(0,s) = H(1,s) = p.$$

The homotopy relation is an equivalence relation on $\Omega(p)$. The set of equivalence classes is denoted by $\pi_1(M, p)$, or simply $\pi_1(M)$ when $M$ is connected (in which case the group is independent of the base point $p$ up to isomorphism).

The concatenation $\beta * \alpha$ induces a group structure on $\pi_1(M)$: the class of the constant curve $t \mapsto p$ is the identity element, and the inverse of $[\alpha]$ is $[\alpha^{-1}]$ where $\alpha^{-1}(t) = \alpha(1-t)$.
