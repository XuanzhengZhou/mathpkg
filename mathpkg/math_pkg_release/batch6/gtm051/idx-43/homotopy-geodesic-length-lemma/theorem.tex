Let $M$ be a surface with Gaussian curvature $K \leq K_1$ everywhere on $M$, where $K_1 > 0$. Let $c_0, c_1: [0,1] \to M$ be two piecewise differentiable curves from $p$ to $q$ that are homotopic with fixed endpoints. Then

$$L(c_0) + L(c_1) \geq \frac{2\pi}{\sqrt{K_1}}.$$

If $K_1 \leq 0$, the inequality is interpreted as $L(c_0) + L(c_1) = +\infty$, which implies that two distinct geodesics from $p$ to $q$ cannot be homotopic, and a closed geodesic cannot be homotopic to a constant curve.
