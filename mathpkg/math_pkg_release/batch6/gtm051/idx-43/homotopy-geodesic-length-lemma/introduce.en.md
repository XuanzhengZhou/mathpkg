This lemma provides a fundamental relationship between curvature and the lengths of homotopic curves on a surface. It states that if the Gaussian curvature is bounded above by $K_1 > 0$, then no two curves with fixed endpoints can be homotopic if the sum of their lengths is less than $2\pi/\sqrt{K_1}$. In the non-positive curvature case, this implies geodesics are unique in their homotopy class. The lemma is a key ingredient in the proof of Hadamard's theorem (6.6.4) characterizing complete simply connected surfaces of non-positive curvature.
