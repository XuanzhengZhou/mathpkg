This refined isoperimetric inequality strengthens the classical bound by incorporating the radius $r$ of an extremal inscribed or circumscribed circle. The right-hand side is non-negative and provides a sharper lower bound on the isoperimetric defect $L^2 - 4\pi A$ when the curve is far from being a circle.
