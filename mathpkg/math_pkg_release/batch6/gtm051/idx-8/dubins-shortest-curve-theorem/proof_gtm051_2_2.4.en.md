The proof uses calculus of variations and Pontryagin's maximum principle from optimal control theory. The curvature constraint $\kappa \leq 1/r$ defines a reachable set. The optimal trajectories satisfy a Hamiltonian system whose solutions are arcs of circles of radius $r$ or straight lines. A concatenation of at most three such arcs suffices to connect any initial and final configuration.
