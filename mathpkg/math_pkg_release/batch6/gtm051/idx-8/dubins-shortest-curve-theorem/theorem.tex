Given $p, q \in \mathbb{R}^2$ and unit tangent vectors $X \in T_p\mathbb{R}^2$, $Y \in T_q\mathbb{R}^2$, consider the problem of finding a $C^1$ curve of shortest length from $p$ to $q$ with initial direction $X$, final direction $Y$, and with average curvature at most $1/r$ (where $r > 0$). Then a solution always exists and consists of circular arcs of radius $r$ and line segments. Moreover, any solution curve contains at most three distinct arcs of these types.
