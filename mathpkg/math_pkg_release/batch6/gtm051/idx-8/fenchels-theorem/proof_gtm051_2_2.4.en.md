The proof proceeds by considering the tangent indicatrix $\tilde{c} = \dot{c}/|\dot{c}|: I \to S^{n-1}$, which is a closed curve on the unit sphere. The length of $\tilde{c}$ equals the total curvature $K(c)$. The key step: for a closed spherical curve not contained in any open hemisphere, the length is at least $2\pi$ (Horn's lemma). If the tangent indicatrix is contained in a hemisphere, the original curve is planar and the Umlaufsatz gives the $2\pi$ bound. Equality requires the indicatrix to be a great circle, which corresponds to a planar convex curve.
