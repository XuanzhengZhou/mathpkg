Let $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be a closed, strictly convex curve. For every point $c(t)$, let $c(t')$ be the unique point such that the tangent vectors are opposite: $e_1(t) = -e_1(t')$, as guaranteed by the property that a strictly convex curve has a unique opposite point with parallel tangent. Then $c$ is said to have \textbf{constant width} $d$ if the distance between every such pair of opposite points is the same constant:

$$d(c(t), c(t')) = d \quad \text{for all } t \in I.$$
