When a space curve is parametrized by arc length ($|\dot{c}(t)| = 1$), the formulas for curvature and torsion simplify considerably. The curvature becomes simply the norm of the acceleration vector $\ddot{c}(t)$, and the torsion is expressed as a determinant involving the first three derivatives, divided by the squared curvature. These formulas are derived directly from the definitions and the Frenet frame relationships.
