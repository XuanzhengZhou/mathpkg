For a space curve $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^3$, the curvature $\kappa(t)$ measures how much the curve deviates from a straight line, while the torsion $\tau(t)$ measures how much it deviates from a plane — that is, how it twists out of the osculating plane. Both are defined in terms of the distinguished Frenet frame $e_1(t), e_2(t), e_3(t)$ and the speed $|\dot{c}(t)|$. The Frenet equations in $3 \times 3$ skew-symmetric matrix form compactly encode how the entire frame evolves along the curve.
