Let $c: I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$ be a curve. A \text{moving $n$-frame} along $c$ is a collection of $n$ differentiable mappings $$e_i: I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n, \quad 1 \leq i \leq n,$$ such that for all $t \in I$, $e_i(t) \cdot e_j(t) = \delta_{ij}$, where $\delta_{ij} = \begin{cases} 1 & i=j \\ 0 & i \neq j \end{cases}$. Each $e_i(t)$ is a vector field along $c$, and $e_i(t)$ is considered as a vector in $T_{c(t)}\mathbb{R}^n$.
