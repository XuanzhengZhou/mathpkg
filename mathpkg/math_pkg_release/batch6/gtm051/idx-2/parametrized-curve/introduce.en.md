A parametrized curve is the fundamental object of study in the differential geometry of curves. It is defined as a smooth ($C^\infty$) map from an interval $I \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ into $\mathbb{R}^n$. The parameter $t \in I$ provides an ordering of points along the curve and enables the definition of velocity, acceleration, and higher-order derivatives. When $I$ is not open, the smoothness condition is defined via extension to an open interval containing $I$.
