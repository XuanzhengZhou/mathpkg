The distinguished Frenet frame is a canonical orthonormal frame adapted to a curve, constructed by applying the Gram-Schmidt process to the successive derivatives $\dot{c}, \ddot{c}, \ldots, c^{(n-1)}$. It is the fundamental tool for analyzing curves in $\mathbb{R}^n$: the frame vectors $e_1, \ldots, e_{n-1}$ span the same subspaces as the derivatives, and the final vector $e_n$ completes the positively oriented orthonormal basis. The differentiation of this frame yields the Frenet equations.
