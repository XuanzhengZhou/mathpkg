A local diffeomorphism is a differentiable map whose differential at a point is bijective, which by the Inverse Function Theorem implies the map is locally invertible with a differentiable inverse. This concept is fundamental in differential geometry: it characterizes when a smooth map between manifolds of the same dimension is a local diffeomorphism. The condition of having a bijective differential (i.e., being a local diffeomorphism) is the differentiable analog of a local homeomorphism.
