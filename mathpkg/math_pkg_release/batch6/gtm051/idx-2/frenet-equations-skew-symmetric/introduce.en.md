The Frenet equations express the derivatives of a moving frame in terms of the frame itself via a skew-symmetric matrix of connection forms. The skew-symmetry follows directly from differentiating the orthonormality condition $e_i \cdot e_j = \delta_{ij}$. For the distinguished Frenet frame, the connection matrix has a particularly simple tridiagonal structure, and the non-zero entries are precisely the curvature functions of the curve. This elegant algebraic structure underpins the fundamental theorem of curves, which states that curvature functions determine a curve up to rigid motion.
