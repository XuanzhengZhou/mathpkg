The length of a curve is defined as the integral of the speed $|\dot{c}(t)|$ over the parameter interval. This definition generalizes the Euclidean notion of length for straight line segments and is invariant under reparametrization. The length functional is fundamental in the calculus of variations, where geodesics are characterized as critical points of the length (or energy) functional.
