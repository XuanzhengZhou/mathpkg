The Implicit Function Theorem in its injective form describes the local structure of a differentiable map whose differential is injective. Given $F: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^m$ with $m \geq n$ and $dF_0$ injective, the theorem constructs a local diffeomorphism $g$ on $\mathbb{R}^m$ that straightens $F$ to the standard inclusion $\mathbb{R}^n \hookrightarrow \mathbb{R}^m$. This result is fundamental for the theory of submanifolds: it shows that the image of an injective immersion is locally embedded as a coordinate plane.
