This proposition establishes two fundamental invariance properties of the Frenet equations. First, the connection forms (and hence the curvature functions) are invariant under rigid motions (isometries) of $\mathbb{R}^n$, confirming that curvature is a truly geometric quantity. Second, under reparametrization, the connection forms transform by the chain rule, revealing how curvature depends on the parametrization. Together, these properties underpin the fundamental theorem of curves: curvature functions up to rigid motion determine a curve uniquely.
