The energy of a curve is defined as one-half the integral of the squared speed. The energy functional is often preferred over the length functional in the calculus of variations because its critical points (geodesics) are automatically parametrized proportionally to arc length, while the length functional is invariant under arbitrary reparametrization. The energy plays a central role in the study of geodesics on Riemannian manifolds.
