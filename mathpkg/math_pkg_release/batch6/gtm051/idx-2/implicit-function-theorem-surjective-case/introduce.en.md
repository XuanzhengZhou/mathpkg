The Implicit Function Theorem in its surjective form describes the local structure of a differentiable map whose differential is surjective (a submersion). Given $F: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^m$ with $n \geq m$ and $dF_0$ surjective, the theorem shows that $F$ is locally equivalent to a linear projection after applying a suitable local diffeomorphism. This is the foundational result for the theory of regular level sets and fiber bundles: the preimage of a regular value is a smooth submanifold of dimension $n-m$.
