Near a non-umbilic point of a surface, where the two principal curvatures are distinct, one can introduce local coordinates whose coordinate lines are precisely the lines of curvature. In these principal curvature coordinates, the Weingarten map takes a diagonal matrix form with the principal curvatures on the diagonal. This lemma follows from Theorem 3.6.4 by selecting the principal direction fields as the vector fields to which coordinates are fitted.
