By the continuity of the principal curvature functions, we can find a neighborhood of $u_0$ on which $\kappa_1(u) < \kappa_2(u)$. Let $X_1(u), X_2(u)$ denote the associated principal directions. They may be chosen to be differentiable vector fields for the following reason. Since $\kappa_1(u), \kappa_2(u)$ are solutions to $\det(dn_u + \kappa \, df_u) = 0$, they are differentiable. Since $\bar{X}_i(u) = df_u^{-1}X_i(u)$ are solutions to $dn_u \bar{X}_i(u) + \kappa_i(u) df_u \bar{X}_i(u) = 0$, they may be chosen to be differentiable. Of course, they are linearly independent. An application of Theorem 3.6.4 yields the desired change of variables.
