Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface. Then
(i) $\det(\kappa \cdot \mathrm{id} + dn \circ df^{-1}) = \kappa^2 - 2H\kappa + K$, where the left side is the characteristic polynomial $\chi(L_u)$ of the Weingarten map. Consequently, $K(u) = \det L_u$ and $2H(u) = \operatorname{Tr} L_u$.
(ii) In terms of the matrices $(g_{ik})$ and $(h_{ik})$: $K = \det(h_{ik})/\det(g_{ik})$ and $H = (h_{11}g_{22} - 2h_{12}g_{12} + h_{22}g_{11})/(2\det(g_{ik}))$.
