The Gauss curvature $K$ of a two-dimensional Riemannian manifold is an intrinsic invariant, independent of the choice of local coordinates. In any coordinate system $(U, g)$, it can be expressed as the ratio $K = R_{1212} / \det(g_{ij})$, where $R_{1212}$ is the $(1,2,1,2)$-component of the Riemann curvature tensor. The proof verifies that length, energy, and the covariant derivative are all coordinate-invariant, from which the invariance of $K$ follows.
