Given $u_0 \in U$ and a Riemannian metric $g$ on $U$, there exists a surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ such that the metric coefficients $g_{ij}(u_0)$ and the Christoffel symbols $\Gamma_{ij}^k(u_0)$, defined by the induced metric of $f$, agree with those given by the Riemannian metric $(U, g)$ at the point $u_0$.
