A Jacobi field along a geodesic $c(t)$ is a vector field $Y(t)$ satisfying the Jacobi differential equation
$$\frac{\nabla^2 Y}{dt^2} + R(Y, \dot{c})\dot{c} = 0,$$
where $R$ is the Riemann curvature tensor. In surface theory with $e_2(t)$ a unit parallel field orthogonal to $\dot{c}$, a Jacobi field can be written $Y(t) = y(t)e_2(t)$ with $\ddot{y}(t) + K(c(t))y(t) = 0$.
