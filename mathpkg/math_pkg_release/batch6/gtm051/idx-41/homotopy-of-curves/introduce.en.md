Two curves with the same endpoints are homotopic if one can be continuously deformed into the other while keeping the endpoints fixed throughout the deformation. The deformation is parameterized by a continuous map $h$ from the unit square into the surface $M$. This notion of fixed-endpoint homotopy is fundamental in differential geometry for studying the global structure of surfaces. It is used to compare closed geodesics with trivial curves and leads to lower bounds on the lengths of non-contractible geodesics when curvature is bounded above.
