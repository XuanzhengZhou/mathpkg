Bonnet's theorem gives a fundamental compactness criterion for complete Riemannian surfaces. If the Gauss curvature is bounded below by a positive constant $K_0 > 0$, then the diameter of the surface cannot exceed $\pi / \sqrt{K_0}$. Since the surface is also complete, the Hopf-Rinow theorem implies it is a complete bounded metric space, hence compact. This is the 2-dimensional analog of the Myers theorem for Riemannian manifolds.
