The proof follows from the Sturm comparison theorem (6.5.5) and the properties of conjugate points established in Section 6.5. If $K \geq K_0 > 0$, then by the Sturm comparison theorem, any geodesic of length greater than $\pi/\sqrt{K_0}$ must contain a pair of conjugate points. Such a geodesic cannot be a minimal geodesic connecting its endpoints (by 6.5.3). Therefore the distance between any two points, which is realized by a minimal geodesic segment, cannot exceed $\pi/\sqrt{K_0}$. The compactness then follows from the Hopf-Rinow theorem: a complete bounded metric space is compact.
