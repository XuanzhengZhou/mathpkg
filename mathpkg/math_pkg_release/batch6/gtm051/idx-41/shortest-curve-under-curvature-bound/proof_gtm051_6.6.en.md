This follows directly from (6.5.6) together with (6.5.3). Specifically, (6.5.6) states that if $K \leq K_1$, then any Jacobi field along a geodesic with length less than $\pi/\sqrt{K_1}$ has no zeros except possibly at the initial point. This means there are no conjugate points along geodesic segments shorter than $\pi/\sqrt{K_1}$. The result (6.5.3) then guarantees that a geodesic segment without conjugate points is the shortest curve joining its endpoints when compared with all nearby curves.
