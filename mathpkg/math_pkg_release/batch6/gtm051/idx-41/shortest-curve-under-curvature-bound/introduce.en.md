This theorem provides a local minimality result for geodesic segments under an upper curvature bound. When the Gauss curvature $K$ of a complete surface is bounded above by $K_1$, any geodesic segment shorter than $\pi / \sqrt{K_1}$ is a local distance minimizer. This follows from the conjugate point estimates (6.5.6) and the general principle that geodesics without conjugate points are local minimizers (6.5.3). The result is the converse direction of the Bonnet-Myers phenomenon: upper curvature bounds give length thresholds below which geodesics cannot have conjugate points.
