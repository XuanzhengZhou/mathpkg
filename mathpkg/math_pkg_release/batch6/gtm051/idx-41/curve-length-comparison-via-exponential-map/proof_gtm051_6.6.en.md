Since $K \leq K_1$, it follows from the Rauch comparison theorem that the exponential map $\exp_p|_{B_\rho(0)}$ is non-singular. Define a Riemannian metric $\tilde{g}$ on $B_\rho(0) \subset T_p M$ by pulling back the metric $g$ on $M$ via $\exp_p$, i.e., $\tilde{g} = \exp_p^* g$. With respect to this metric, $\exp_p|_{B_\rho(0)}$ is a local isometry. Observe that polar coordinates in $B_\rho(0)$ are geodesic polar coordinates for the surface $(B_\rho(0), \tilde{g})$. The theorem now follows from (5.3.4) applied to $(B_\rho(0), \tilde{g})$, which states that in geodesic polar coordinates, the radial geodesic from the origin minimizes length among all curves with the same endpoints contained in the coordinate domain.
