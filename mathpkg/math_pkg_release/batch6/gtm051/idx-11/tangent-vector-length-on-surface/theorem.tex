\textbf{Proposition 3.4.2.} Let $c = f \circ u: I \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a curve on a surface $f$. Then
$$\dot{c}(t) = \sum_i \dot{u}^i \, (f_{u^i} \circ u)(t)$$
is a tangent vector to $f$ at $u(t)$. The squared length of $\dot{c}(t)$ is given by
$$|\dot{c}(t)|^2 = \sum_{i,j} g_{ij}(u(t)) \, \dot{u}^i(t) \, \dot{u}^j(t)$$
where $g_{ij}$ are the coefficients of the first fundamental form of $f$.
