A curve on a surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is a curve in $\mathbb{R}^3$ that can be expressed as the composition $c = f \circ u$, where $u: I \to U$ is a parameterized curve in the parameter domain $U \subset \mathbb{R}^2$. This definition allows the study of geometric properties of curves—such as length and curvature—directly in terms of the surface $f$ and its first and second fundamental forms, without referring to the ambient Euclidean coordinates.
