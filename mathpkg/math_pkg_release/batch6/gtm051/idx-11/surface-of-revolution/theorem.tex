A \textbf{surface of revolution} is a surface parameterized by
$$f(u, v) := (h(u) \cos v, \, h(u) \sin v, \, k(u))$$
where $h'^2 + k'^2 \neq 0$ and $h \neq 0$. The surface parameterized by $f$ is the surface generated by revolving the curve $(h(u), 0, k(u))$ about the $z$-axis. The coefficients of the first fundamental form satisfy
$$f_v^2 = h'^2 + k'^2, \quad f_u \cdot f_v = 0, \quad f_u^2 = h^2.$$
