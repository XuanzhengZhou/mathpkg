A one-parameter group of isometries is a smooth family $\{\gamma_t\}_{t \in \mathbb{R}}$ of rigid motions of $\mathbb{R}^3$ indexed by a real parameter, satisfying the group law $\gamma_{t+s} = \gamma_t \circ \gamma_s$. Each $\gamma_t$ is an isometry (preserving distances and angles), and the mapping $(t, x) \mapsto \gamma_t(x)$ is differentiable in both variables. An important example is the helical motion $\gamma(t, x) = (x^1 \cos t + x^2 \sin t, -x^1 \sin t + x^2 \cos t, x^3 + bt)$, which combines rotation about the $x^3$-axis with translation along it.
