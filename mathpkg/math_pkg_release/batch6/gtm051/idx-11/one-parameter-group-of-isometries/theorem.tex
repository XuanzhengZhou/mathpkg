A \textbf{one-parameter group of isometries} of $\mathbb{R}^3$ is a differentiable mapping
$$\gamma: \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^3 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$$
with the following property: for each $t \in \mathbb{R}$, the map $\gamma_t: \mathbb{R}^3 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ given by $\gamma_t(x) = \gamma(t, x)$ is an isometry of $\mathbb{R}^3$, and $\gamma_{t+s} = \gamma_t \circ \gamma_s$ for all $t, s \in \mathbb{R}$.
