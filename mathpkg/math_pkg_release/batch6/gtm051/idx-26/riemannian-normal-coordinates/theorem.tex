Let $M$ be a surface with a Riemannian metric. Let $p \in M$ be a point and let $\rho > 0$ be such that $B_\rho(p)$ is a $\rho$-disk with center at $p$. Let $\{e_1(p), e_2(p)\}$ be an orthonormal basis of $T_p M$.

i) The coordinate system $\phi: B_\rho(0) \subset T_p M \cong \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow B_\rho(p)$, defined by

$$(v^1, v^2) \mapsto \exp_p\left(\sum_i v^i e_i(p)\right),$$

is called (Riemannian) normal coordinates.

ii) Geodesic polar (or Riemannian polar) coordinates $(\rho, \theta)$ are defined by writing points of $T_p M \setminus \{0\}$ in polar form $X = \rho (\cos\theta \, e_1(p) + \sin\theta \, e_2(p))$ and mapping via $\exp_p$. Thus

$$(\rho, \theta) \mapsto \exp_p\bigl(\rho(\cos\theta \, e_1(p) + \sin\theta \, e_2(p))\bigr).$$
