A $\rho$-disc $B_\rho(p)$ is the image under the exponential map of an open ball of radius $\rho$ in the tangent space $T_p M$, centered at the origin. It provides a natural geodesic neighborhood around $p$: every point in $B_\rho(p)$ can be joined to $p$ by a unique geodesic of length less than $\rho$. These discs are the fundamental building blocks for studying local Riemannian geometry, as they serve as domains for normal coordinates and geodesic polar coordinates.
