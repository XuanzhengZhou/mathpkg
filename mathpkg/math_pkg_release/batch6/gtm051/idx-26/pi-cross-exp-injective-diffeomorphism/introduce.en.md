This lemma states that near any point $p_0$ of a Riemannian surface $M$, the combined map $X \mapsto (\pi X, \exp X)$ from a tangent ball $B_\epsilon M_0$ into $M \times M$ is an injective diffeomorphism onto its image. The map sends a tangent vector $X$ to the ordered pair consisting of its base point and its exponential. This result is fundamental for establishing that the exponential map furnishes a local coordinate system and for proving the existence of normal neighborhoods.
