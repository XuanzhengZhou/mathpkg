Let $M$ be a two-dimensional differentiable manifold. Then $TM \oplus TM$ can be given the structure of a six-dimensional differentiable manifold: If $(u_\alpha, M_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ is an atlas of $M$, define an atlas $(Tu_\alpha \oplus Tu_\alpha, TM_\alpha \oplus TM_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ for $TM \oplus TM$ by
$$Tu_\alpha \oplus Tu_\alpha: TM_\alpha \oplus TM_\alpha \to TU_\alpha \oplus TU_\alpha = U_\alpha \times \mathbb{R}^2 \times \mathbb{R}^2$$
$$(X, Y) \in T_pM \oplus T_pM \mapsto (u_\alpha(p), X_\alpha, Y_\alpha) \in U_\alpha \times T_{u_\alpha(p)}\mathbb{R}^2 \times T_{u_\alpha(p)}\mathbb{R}^2,$$
where $X_\alpha$ and $Y_\alpha$ are the representatives of $X$ and $Y$ with respect to the chart $(u_\alpha, M_\alpha)$.
