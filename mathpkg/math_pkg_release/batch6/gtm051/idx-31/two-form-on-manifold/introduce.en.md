A differential 2-form on a manifold assigns an alternating bilinear form on each tangent space $T_pM$, varying smoothly with $p$. On a surface, the space of 2-forms at each point is one-dimensional, spanned by the wedge product $du^1 \wedge du^2$. Under coordinate changes, the coefficient $A$ transforms by the determinant of the Jacobian. Two-forms are the natural objects for area integration on surfaces, with the area element $dM$ and curvature 2-form $K\,dM$ being key examples in differential geometry.
