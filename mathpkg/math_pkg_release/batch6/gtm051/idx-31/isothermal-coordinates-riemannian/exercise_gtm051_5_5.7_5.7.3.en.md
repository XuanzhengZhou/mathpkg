Suppose $\phi: (v^1, v^2) \mapsto (u^1, u^2)$ is a change of variables between isothermal coordinate systems (a conformal mapping). Prove that the functions $u^1(v^1, v^2)$ and $u^2(v^1, v^2)$ satisfy the Cauchy-Riemann equations:
$$\frac{\partial u^1}{\partial v^1} = \frac{\partial u^2}{\partial v^2}, \quad \frac{\partial u^1}{\partial v^2} = -\frac{\partial u^2}{\partial v^1}.$$
Conclude that $\phi$ can be written as a holomorphic function between open sets in $\mathbb{C}$.
