For a regular plane curve $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ with distinguished Frenet frame $\{e_1(t), e_2(t)\}$, the curvature of $c$ is the function

$$\kappa(t) := \frac{\omega_{12}(t)}{|\dot{c}(t)|} = \frac{\dot{e}_1(t) \cdot e_2(t)}{|\dot{c}(t)|}.$$

The sign of $\kappa(t)$ indicates the direction in which the curve bends: $\kappa(t) > 0$ means the curve turns toward $e_2$, while $\kappa(t) < 0$ means it turns away from $e_2$.
