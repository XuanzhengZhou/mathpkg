Let $c, \tilde{c}: I \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be two curves satisfying condition (1.2.2). Suppose that $|\dot{c}(t)| = |\dot{\tilde{c}}(t)|$ and that the curvature functions satisfy $\kappa_i(t) = \tilde{\kappa}_i(t)$ for $i = 1, \dots, n-1$ and all $t \in I$. Then there exists a unique proper congruence $B$ (an isometry of $\mathbb{R}^n$ whose orthogonal component has determinant $+1$) such that

$$\tilde{c}(t) = B \circ c(t) \quad \text{for all } t \in I.$$

Conversely, if two curves are related by a proper congruence, then their speeds and curvature functions coincide.
