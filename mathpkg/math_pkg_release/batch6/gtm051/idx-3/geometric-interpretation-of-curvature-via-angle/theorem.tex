Let $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be a regular plane curve with distinguished Frenet frame $\{e_1(t), e_2(t)\}$. Fix a unit vector $v \in \mathbb{R}^2$ and define the angle function $\theta(t)$ locally by

$$\cos \theta(t) = e_1(t) \cdot v, \qquad \sin \theta(t) = -e_2(t) \cdot v.$$

Then $\theta(t)$ measures, up to a multiple of $2\pi$, the angle from $v$ to the unit tangent $e_1(t)$ in the positive direction. Its derivative is well-defined and satisfies

$$\dot{\theta}(t) = \omega_{12}(t) = \kappa(t) |\dot{c}(t)|.$$

In particular, if $c$ is parametrized by arc length ($|\dot{c}(t)| = 1$), then $\kappa(t) = \dot{\theta}(t)$.
