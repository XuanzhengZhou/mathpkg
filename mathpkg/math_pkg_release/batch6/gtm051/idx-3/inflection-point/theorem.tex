Let $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be a regular plane curve with curvature function $\kappa(t)$. A point $c(t)$ is called an inflection point of the curve if

$$\kappa(t) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \dot{\kappa}(t) \neq 0.$$

The condition $\dot{\kappa}(t) \neq 0$ ensures that the zero of $\kappa$ is isolated.
