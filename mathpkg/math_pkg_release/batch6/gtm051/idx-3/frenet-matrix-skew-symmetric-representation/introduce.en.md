When using the distinguished Frenet frame, the skew-symmetric matrix of connection forms $\omega$ takes a particularly simple tridiagonal form. All entries more than one position off the diagonal vanish. The superdiagonal entries are $\kappa_i |\dot{c}|$ and the subdiagonal entries are $-\kappa_i |\dot{c}|$. This sparse structure is the defining feature of the Frenet equations in $\mathbb{R}^n$ and is the matrix form of the general Frenet formulas.
