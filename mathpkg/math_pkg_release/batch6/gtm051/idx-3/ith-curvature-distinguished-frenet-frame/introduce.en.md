The $i$-th curvature $\kappa_i$ of a curve is defined by normalizing the connection form $\omega_{i,i+1}$ of the distinguished Frenet frame by the speed of the curve. Since $\omega_{ij}/|\dot{c}|$ is invariant under reparametrization, the curvature functions $\kappa_i$ are geometric invariants. For the distinguished Frenet frame, the connection form matrix reduces to a simple skew-symmetric tridiagonal matrix whose superdiagonal entries are precisely the curvatures multiplied by the speed.
