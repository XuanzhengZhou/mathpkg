On a Riemannian surface $M$, one defines the intrinsic distance $d(p,q)$ between two points as the infimum of lengths of all piecewise smooth curves joining them. This theorem establishes that $d$ is indeed a metric: it is nonnegative, symmetric, satisfies the triangle inequality, and $d(p,q)=0$ implies $p=q$. Furthermore, the open sets of the resulting metric topology coincide with the original manifold topology, so the metric faithfully captures the topological structure of $M$.
