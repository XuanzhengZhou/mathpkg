An embedded surface is a subset $M$ of $\mathbb{R}^3$ that is locally homeomorphic to open subsets of $\mathbb{R}^2$ via a family of parameterized surfaces. The inverse maps of these parameterizations serve as charts, giving $M$ the structure of a two-dimensional smooth submanifold of $\mathbb{R}^3$. This definition bridges the local theory of parameterized surfaces (Chapter 3) with the global study of surface geometry (Chapter 6).
