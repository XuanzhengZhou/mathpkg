Green's first formula relates the integral of $\psi_1 \Delta\psi_2$ and the inner product of gradients over a compact region $P$ to the boundary integral of $\psi_1$ times the normal derivative of $\psi_2$. It is the Riemannian surface analogue of the classical integration-by-parts formula in $\mathbb{R}^2$, and follows from applying Gauss's theorem (the divergence theorem) to the vector field $\psi_1 \operatorname{grad} \psi_2$.
