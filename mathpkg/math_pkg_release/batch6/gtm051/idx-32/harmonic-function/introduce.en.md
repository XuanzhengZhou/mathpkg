A differentiable function $\psi: M \to \mathbb{R}$ on a Riemannian surface $M$ is called harmonic if its Laplace–Beltrami operator vanishes identically: $\Delta\psi = 0$. Every point $p \in M$ admits a neighborhood on which a non-constant harmonic function exists satisfying $d\psi \neq 0$. The existence of such harmonic functions is intimately tied to the existence of isothermal coordinates and the complex structure on Riemann surfaces.
