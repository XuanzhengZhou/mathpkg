The Laplace–Beltrami operator is a second-order linear differential operator defined on the algebra $F(M)$ of differentiable functions on a Riemannian surface $M$. It is given by the composition $\Delta\psi = \operatorname{div} \operatorname{grad} \psi$, generalizing the classical Laplacian to curved spaces. This operator plays a fundamental role in Riemannian geometry, appearing in Green's formulae, the heat equation, and the definition of harmonic functions.
