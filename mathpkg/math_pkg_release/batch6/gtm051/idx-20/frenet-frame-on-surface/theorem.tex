Let $c(t) = f \circ u(t)$ be a curve on a surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ with $\dot{c}(t) \neq 0$. The ordered pair of tangential vector fields

$$e_1(t) := \frac{\dot{c}(t)}{|\dot{c}(t)|}, \quad e_2(t),$$

along $c$, where $e_2(t)$ satisfies $|e_2(t)| = 1$, $e_2(t) \cdot e_1(t) = 0$, and $(e_1(t), e_2(t))$ has the same orientation as $(f_{u^1}(u(t)), f_{u^2}(u(t)))$, is called the **Frenet frame** of $c$.
