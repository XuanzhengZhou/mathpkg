The Frenet frame on a surface generalizes the classical Frenet frame of a plane curve to curves lying on surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$. For a regular curve on a parametrized surface, the first frame vector $e_1(t)$ is the normalized velocity vector, and $e_2(t)$ is the unique unit tangent vector orthogonal to $e_1(t)$ such that the ordered pair $(e_1, e_2)$ matches the orientation induced by the surface parametrization. This frame is uniquely determined and is invariant under orientation-preserving reparametrizations. The Frenet frame provides the natural basis for defining the geodesic curvature of the curve, which measures the tangential component of the covariant acceleration.
