This proposition provides a fundamental characterization of geodesics on surfaces in terms of the geodesic curvature. A regular curve has vanishing geodesic curvature if and only if it can be reparametrized by arc length to become a geodesic. Such curves are called pre-geodesics. The result generalizes the familiar fact from plane geometry that a curve has zero curvature exactly when it is a straight line (parametrized by an affine function of arc length). The proof follows directly from the definition of geodesic curvature and the fact that geodesic curvature is invariant under reparametrization, reducing the condition $\kappa_g = 0$ to the geodesic equation $\nabla \dot{c}/dt = 0$ for a unit-speed curve.
