The Parallel Translation Theorem establishes two fundamental properties of parallel vector fields along curves on surfaces. First, for any tangent vector at a point, there exists a unique parallel vector field along a given curve with that vector as its initial value. Second, the parallel translation map that sends a tangent vector at the initial point to the value of its parallel extension at the terminal point is an isometry — it preserves the inner product. These properties are derived from the system of linear ordinary differential equations satisfied by the coefficients of a parallel vector field, which guarantees existence and uniqueness of solutions, while the constancy of the scalar product under parallel transport (proved earlier) ensures the map is an isometry.
