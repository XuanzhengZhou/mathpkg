The geodesic curvature $\kappa_g(t)$ generalizes the curvature of a plane curve to curves on surfaces. It is defined as the component of the covariant derivative of the unit tangent vector $e_1(t)$ along the direction of the orthogonal Frenet frame vector $e_2(t)$, normalized by the speed $|\dot{c}(t)|$. Geometrically, $\kappa_g(t)$ measures how much the curve deviates from being a geodesic: a curve has vanishing geodesic curvature exactly when it is a pre-geodesic (i.e., it can be reparametrized to a geodesic). This invariant is intrinsic to the surface metric and plays a central role in the Gauss--Bonnet theorem and the study of curves on surfaces.
