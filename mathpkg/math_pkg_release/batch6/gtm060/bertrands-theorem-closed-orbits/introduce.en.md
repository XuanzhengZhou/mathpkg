Bertrand's theorem is a remarkable classification result: among all possible central force laws, only the harmonic oscillator ($U \propto r^2$) and the Newtonian/Coulomb potential ($U \propto -1/r$) have the property that all bounded orbits are closed curves. For any other power law (or logarithmic potential), generic bounded orbits precess and densely fill an annulus. The proof proceeds by analyzing the angle $\Phi$ between successive pericenters and showing it must be independent of energy and angular momentum, which forces the specific power laws.
