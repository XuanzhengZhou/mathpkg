If a differential $k$-form $\omega$ is a relative integral invariant of a map $g$, then its exterior derivative $d\omega$ is an absolute integral invariant of $g$. The proof is a concise application of Stokes' theorem: for any $(k+1)$-chain $c$, the integral of $d\omega$ over $c$ equals the integral of $\omega$ over the boundary $\partial c$, which is preserved under $g$ by the relative invariance. This theorem links the two types of integral invariants and underpins the hierarchy of integral invariants in Hamiltonian mechanics.
