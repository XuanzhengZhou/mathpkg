A differential $k$-form $\omega$ is called a relative integral invariant of a map $g: M \to M$ if its integral over any closed $k$-chain $c$ is preserved under the action of $g$: $\int_{gc} \omega = \int_c \omega$. This differs from an absolute integral invariant, where the equality holds for every $k$-chain, not just closed ones. Relative integral invariants are central to the study of canonical transformations and Hamiltonian mechanics, where the 1-form $\mathbf{p}\,d\mathbf{q}$ serves as the prototypical example.
