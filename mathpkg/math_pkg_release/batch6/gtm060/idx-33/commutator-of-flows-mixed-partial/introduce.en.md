This lemma computes the leading-order term measuring the non-commutativity of two flows $A^t$ and $B^s$. The difference $\varphi(A^t B^s x) - \varphi(B^s A^t x)$ vanishes to first order in $s$ and $t$; its mixed second partial derivative equals the commutator $L_B L_A - L_A L_B$ of the corresponding directional derivative operators. This identity motivates the definition of the Poisson bracket of vector fields, as the vector field $C$ whose associated differential operator $L_C$ realizes this commutator.
