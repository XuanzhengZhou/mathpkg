A central field is a vector field invariant under rotations and reflections about a fixed center. Its vectors are radial, and its magnitude depends only on the distance $r$ from the center: $\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}) = \Phi(r)\mathbf{e}_r$. Central fields model gravitational and electrostatic forces and possess special conservation properties.
