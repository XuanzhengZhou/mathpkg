This corollary provides a fundamental equivalence between the group-theoretic and dynamical descriptions of Hamiltonian mechanics. A one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms of phase space evolves according to Hamilton's equations precisely when each individual transformation in the group preserves the canonical symplectic structure. This unifies the notion of canonical transformations (which preserve the form $\omega^2 = \sum dp_i \wedge dq_i$) with the time evolution of a Hamiltonian system.
