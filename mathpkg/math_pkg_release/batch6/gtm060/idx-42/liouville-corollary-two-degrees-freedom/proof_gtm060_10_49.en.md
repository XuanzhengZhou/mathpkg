**Proof.** For a system with $n = 2$ degrees of freedom, the phase space has dimension $2n = 4$. The hamiltonian $H$ is one first integral. The additional first integral $F$, which does not depend on $H$, is also in involution with $H$ since $(H, F) \equiv 0$ by definition of a first integral. Thus we have $n = 2$ independent first integrals $\{H, F\}$ in involution on a 4-dimensional symplectic manifold. By Liouville's theorem, the system is integrable by quadratures.
