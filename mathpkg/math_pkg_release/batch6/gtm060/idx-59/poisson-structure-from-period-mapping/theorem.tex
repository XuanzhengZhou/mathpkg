Suppose that the fibres of a differentiable fibre bundle are (real) oriented even-dimensional manifolds, and consider their homology in the middle dimension. When the intersection form in the middle-dimensional homology of the fibres is skew-symmetric, a Poisson structure on the base arises from the intersection form via a nondegenerate period mapping.

The local identification of the base with the cohomology of the typical fibre, given by the period mapping, provides the base with local coordinates whose Poisson brackets are constant.
