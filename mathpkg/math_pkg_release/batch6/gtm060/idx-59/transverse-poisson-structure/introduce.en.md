At a nongeneric point of a Poisson manifold where the dimension of the symplectic leaf drops below the generic rank, the local structure is still a product of the symplectic leaf and a transverse manifold. The transverse Poisson structure is the induced Poisson structure on a minimal submanifold transverse to the leaf, uniquely defined up to diffeomorphism. Its linearization at the point is given by structure constants of a finite-dimensional Lie algebra. This concept, introduced by Weinstein, is crucial for the singularity theory of Poisson structures.
