This is a direct application of the germ equivalence theorem with $\beta = -1$ and $n = 2$. The form $h dx \wedge dy / f$ is equivalent to $f^\beta h dx$ with $\beta = -1$ and $dx = dx_1 \wedge dx_2$. The Newton diagram argument gives the specific form of the resonant monomials: for $\beta = -1$, monomials $x^m$ appearing in the perturbation correspond to points $(m_1+1, m_2+1)$ in the interior of the Newton diagram of $f$. The nondegeneracy condition on $f$ ensures the Newton diagram has nonempty interior, and the quasi-homogeneity ensures the weights are well-defined. The proof is completed by applying the normal form theorem and translating back to the Poisson bivector formulation.
