This corollary specializes the quasi-homogeneous normal form theorem to the Poisson structure case ($\beta = -1$, $n = 2$). A 2D Poisson structure is given by a bivector field $f(x,y) \partial_x \wedge \partial_y$, which corresponds to the 2-form $dx \wedge dy / f$. When the singular curve $f = 0$ is quasi-homogeneous, the Poisson structure near the singular point can be reduced to a finite-parameter normal form determined by the interior integral points of the Newton diagram. The monomials in the normal form correspond to exponent vectors shifted by 1 from the Newton diagram interior. This result connects the classification of Poisson singularities with toric geometry.
