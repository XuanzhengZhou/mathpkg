Given a Poisson structure on a two-dimensional manifold, it is either reducible in a neighborhood of each point to one of the normal forms in Table 1, or it belongs to a set of codimension 8 in the space of Poisson structures.

In suitable local coordinates with origin at the singular point, the Poisson structure can be written as $\{x, y\} = f(x, y)$, where $f$ is given by:

$$\begin{array}{cccc}
A_0 & A_{2k} & A_{2k-1}^a & D_{2k}^{a,b} \\
y & x^2 + y^{2k+1} & \frac{x^2 \pm y^{2k}}{1 + ay^{k-1}} & \frac{x^2 y \pm y^{2k-1}}{1 + ax + by^k}
\end{array}$$

$$\begin{array}{cccc}
D_{2k+1}^a & E_6 & E_7^a & E_8 \\
\frac{x^2 y + y^{2k}}{1 + ax} & x^3 + y^4 & \frac{x^3 + xy^3}{1 + ay^2} & x^3 + y^5
\end{array}$$
