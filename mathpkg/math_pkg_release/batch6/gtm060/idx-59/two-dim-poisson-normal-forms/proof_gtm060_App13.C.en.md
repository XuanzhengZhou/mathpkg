The proof proceeds by analyzing the zero set $f = 0$ of the bivector field. On a two-dimensional manifold, a Poisson structure is given by $\{x, y\} = f(x, y)$ where $f$ is a smooth function. A nonsingular point corresponds to $f(0) \neq 0$, and by the inverse function theorem one can choose coordinates so that $f \equiv 1$.

At a singular point where $f(0) = 0$, one studies the singularity type of $f$. By the classification of function singularities (the ADE classification), $f$ can be reduced to one of the normal forms $A_\mu, D_\mu, E_6, E_7, E_8$ by a suitable coordinate change. However, the coordinate changes that preserve the Poisson structure (diffeomorphisms of the plane) can also affect the function, leading to additional moduli and the appearance of parameters $a, b$ in the normal forms.

The codimension 8 statement refers to the fact that these ADE normal forms cover all singularities up to codimension 7 in the space of functions; Poisson structures with more degenerate singularities (of codimension $\geq 8$) form a thin set. The classification combines the ADE classification of function germs with the action of the diffeomorphism group on the space of bivectors.
