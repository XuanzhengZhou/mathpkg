Conn's analytic linearization theorem (1984) is a fundamental result in the local theory of Poisson structures. It states that if the linearized transverse Poisson structure corresponds to a semisimple Lie algebra, then all higher-order nonlinear terms in the Taylor expansion can be eliminated by an analytic coordinate change. This means the Poisson structure is locally analytically equivalent to its linear part. The result is a Poisson-geometric analog of the Poincaré-Dulac theorem for vector fields.
