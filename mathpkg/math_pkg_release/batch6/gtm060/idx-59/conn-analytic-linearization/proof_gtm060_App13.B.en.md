The proof by Conn (Annals of Math. 119, 1984, 577-601) uses an iterative scheme to eliminate higher-order terms order by order. The key step involves solving cohomological equations on the Lie algebra of formal Poisson vector fields modulo Hamiltonian vector fields. The semisimplicity condition ensures that the Lie algebra cohomology $H^2(\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g})$ vanishes in positive degrees, which guarantees that each cohomological obstruction can be absorbed by an appropriate coordinate change at each order. The analytic convergence of the iteration is established using majorant methods (Newton's method adapted to the Lie algebra setting). The compactness or semisimplicity of the linearized Lie algebra provides the necessary estimates for convergence of the formal series to an analytic diffeomorphism.
