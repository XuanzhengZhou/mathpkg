Each leaf of a Poisson manifold inherits a natural symplectic structure defined via the Poisson bracket of Hamiltonian functions. Given two Hamiltonian vector fields at a point of the leaf, the symplectic 2-form assigns the value of the Poisson bracket of the corresponding Hamiltonian functions. Closedness follows from the Jacobi identity and nondegeneracy from the fact that a tangent vector annihilating derivatives of all functions must be zero. This structure makes every leaf an even-dimensional symplectic manifold whose symplectic form is preserved by phase flows of Hamiltonian vector fields on the ambient Poisson manifold.
