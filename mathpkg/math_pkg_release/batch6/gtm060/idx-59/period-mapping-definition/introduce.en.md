A period mapping is a fundamental construction in singularity theory and algebraic geometry. Given a locally trivial fibration $E \to B$, the cohomology groups of the fibres form a vector bundle over the base. A period mapping is a section of this cohomology bundle. The canonical example arises from a differential form on the total space that is closed on each fibre: its restriction to each fibre defines a cohomology class, and the map assigning this class to each base point is the period mapping. The Gauss-Manin connection provides a canonical way to differentiate period mappings along vector fields on the base.
