A mapping $\varphi: M \to N$ from one Poisson manifold to another is called a **Poisson mapping** if it is consistent with the Poisson structures, i.e., if for any two functions $f, g$ on $N$, the Poisson bracket of their pullbacks to $M$ coincides with the pullback of their Poisson bracket:

$$\{f \circ \varphi, g \circ \varphi\}_M = \{f, g\}_N \circ \varphi.$$
