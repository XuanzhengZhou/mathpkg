Conn's smooth linearization theorem (1985) extends his earlier analytic result to the $C^\infty$ category. If the linearized transverse Poisson structure corresponds to a Lie algebra of compact type (the Lie algebra of a compact Lie group), then the nonlinear terms can be removed by a smooth coordinate transformation. The compactness condition replaces semisimplicity and analyticity in the earlier result. This theorem, together with Weinstein's conjecture about the necessity of semisimplicity for linearization, sparked significant investigation into which Poisson structures admit normal forms.
