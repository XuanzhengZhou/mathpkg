Conn's proof (Annals of Math., 1985) adapts the iterative linearization scheme to the $C^\infty$ setting. The compact type condition implies that the adjoint representation of the linearized Lie algebra integrates to a representation of a compact Lie group. This allows averaging techniques (integration over the compact group) to be used at each iterative step to solve the relevant cohomological equations smoothly. The key technical point is that compactness provides uniform estimates that guarantee the $C^\infty$ convergence of the iterative scheme (in contrast to the analytic case where majorant methods are used). The result is that the formal power series solution constructed by the cohomological iteration converges in the $C^\infty$ sense to a genuine smooth diffeomorphism that linearizes the Poisson structure.
