The proof analyzes the action of the diffeomorphism group on the space of forms $f^\beta h \, dx$. The tangent space to the orbit at a given form corresponds to Lie derivatives $L_X (f^\beta h \, dx)$ where $X$ is a vector field. These can be expressed as $d(\iota_X f^\beta h \, dx)$ by Cartan's formula (since the form is of top degree, $d\omega = 0$ automatically).

The cokernel of the infinitesimal action map, i.e., the space of forms modulo those reachable by infinitesimal diffeomorphisms, is isomorphic to the local algebra $Q_f = \mathcal{O}_n / (f, \partial f/\partial x_1, \ldots, \partial f/\partial x_n)$. Its dimension is the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity.

For resonance $\beta$, certain weighted-homogeneous terms in the expansion of $h$ lie in the image of the Lie derivative action, reducing the number of effective moduli. The resonance condition $\beta = -p/q$ corresponds to additional relations in the cohomology of the Koszul complex, which occur exactly for those $\beta$ where $-\beta - \sigma$ is a quasi-homogeneous weight of some monomial in the Jacobian ideal.
