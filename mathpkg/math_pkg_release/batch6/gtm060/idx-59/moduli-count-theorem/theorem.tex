For almost all $\beta$, the number of moduli of the form

$$f^\beta h \, dx_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_n$$

(for fixed $\beta$ and $f$ and arbitrary $h$, $h(0) \neq 0$) is equal to the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity of the germ $f$. The exceptional (resonance) values of $\beta$ consist of a finite number of arithmetic progressions of negative rational numbers, with difference $-1$. In particular, for any $\beta \geq 0$, the number of moduli equals the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity.
