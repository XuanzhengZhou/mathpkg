This theorem determines the number of continuous moduli (invariants depending continuously on the data) in the classification of differential forms $f^\beta h \, dx$ under diffeomorphisms. For almost all exponents $\beta$, the moduli count equals the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity of the singular hypersurface $f = 0$. The exceptional resonance values of $\beta$ form finitely many arithmetic progressions of negative rational numbers. For nonnegative $\beta$ there are no resonances, and the moduli count is exactly the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity—an invariant measuring how far $f$ is from admitting quasi-homogeneous coordinates.
