A **power of a volume form** is a differential form of the type

$$f(x)(dx)^\alpha = f(x_1, \ldots, x_n)(dx_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_n)^\alpha,$$

where $\alpha$ is a fixed number (generally complex) and $f$ is a smooth (or holomorphic) function.

Equivalently, one may consider forms of the type

$$f^\beta dx, \quad \beta = 1/\alpha,$$

whose classification is equivalent. The hypersurface $f = 0$ is invariantly connected with the form.
