The concept of a power of a volume form generalizes the classification of Poisson structures on the plane. While a Poisson structure in 2D corresponds to a form $f(dx \wedge dy)^{-1}$ (the case $\alpha = -1$), one can consider forms $f (dx)^\alpha$ for arbitrary $\alpha \in \mathbb{C}$. The classification of such forms under the action of diffeomorphisms leads to a rich theory involving resonance values of $\alpha$ (certain negative rational numbers) and is related to the ADE classification of hypersurface singularities. The case $\beta = -1$, corresponding to Poisson structures, is always a resonance case.
