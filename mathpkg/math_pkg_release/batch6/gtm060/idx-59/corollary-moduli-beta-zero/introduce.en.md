This corollary specializes the moduli count theorem to the case $\beta = 0$, corresponding to ordinary volume forms $h \, dx$. The number of moduli equals the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity of the singularity $f$. In particular, this number is zero precisely when $f$ can be given quasi-homogeneous coordinates, making the classification rigid (no continuous parameters). This provides a clean geometric interpretation of quasi-homogeneity: a singularity is quasi-homogeneous if and only if every volume form with an isolated zero admits, up to diffeomorphism preserving the singular set, no continuous moduli.
