This follows directly from the moduli count theorem by setting $\beta = 0$. Since $\beta = 0 \geq 0$, there are no resonance values, and the number of moduli equals the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity. The statement about quasi-homogeneity follows from the definition: $f$ is quasi-homogeneous if and only if $f$ belongs to its Jacobian ideal $(\partial f/\partial x_1, \ldots, \partial f/\partial x_n)$, in which case the quotient space $(f, \partial f/\partial x_i)/(\partial f/\partial x_i)$ has dimension zero.
