This theorem of Varchenko and Givental reveals a deep connection between singularity theory and Poisson geometry. Starting from a versal deformation of a function singularity in two variables, one constructs a period mapping from the complement of the discriminant (where the fibres are nonsingular) to the cohomology of the Milnor fibre. The intersection form on the middle homology induces a Poisson structure on the base. Remarkably, this Poisson structure extends analytically across the discriminant locus — the set where the fibration degenerates. For the $A_3$ singularity (versal deformation of $x^4$), the discriminant is the swallowtail surface, and the resulting Poisson structure has planes as symplectic leaves with the standard symplectic form.
