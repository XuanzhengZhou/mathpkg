This theorem provides a normal form for differential forms $f^\beta h \, dx$ where $f$ is a quasi-homogeneous polynomial. The reduction eliminates the arbitrary nonvanishing factor $h$ by absorbing it into a finite sum of monomial terms weighted by the Newton diagram data. The monomials $x^m$ appearing in the normal form correspond to interior lattice points of the Newton polyhedron of $f$, and the exponent $\beta$ determines the resonance structure. When $\beta$ is not a negative rational number, the normal form simplifies dramatically: the perturbation term $\phi$ vanishes identically, yielding the trivial form $f^\beta dx$.
