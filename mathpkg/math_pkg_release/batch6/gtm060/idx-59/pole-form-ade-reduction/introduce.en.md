This corollary states that for the ADE simple singularities in three variables, any pole form $h dx \wedge dy \wedge dz / f$ with nonvanishing numerator at the origin can be simplified to the canonical form $dx \wedge dy \wedge dz / f$. In other words, the arbitrary function $h$ can be eliminated by a coordinate change when $f$ is a simple singularity. This is because for the ADE polynomials, the exponent $\beta = -1$ is non-resonant in the quasi-homogeneous classification: the quasi-homogeneous weights satisfy $-\beta - \sigma < 0$, so no resonant monomials can contribute to the perturbation term $\phi$.
