The proof follows from the quasi-homogeneous normal form theorem. For $\beta = -1$ and $n = 3$, one computes the quasi-homogeneous weight $\sigma$ of the volume form $dx \wedge dy \wedge dz$: $\sigma = w_1 + w_2 + w_3$ where $w_i$ are the weights of the variables for which $f$ has weight 1. For each ADE polynomial, one verifies that $-\beta - \sigma = 1 - \sigma < 0$. Since the perturbation monomials in the normal form must have weight $-\beta - \sigma = 0$ to contribute, and there are no monomials of negative weight, the perturbation term $\phi$ must vanish identically. Therefore $h dx \wedge dy \wedge dz / f$ is equivalent to $dx \wedge dy \wedge dz / f$ via the diffeomorphism that sends the normal form back to the original coordinates.
