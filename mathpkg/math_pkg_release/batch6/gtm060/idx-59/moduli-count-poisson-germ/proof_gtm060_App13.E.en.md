The proof combines the moduli count theorem with the specific resonance analysis for $\beta = -1$. For Poisson structures, $\beta = -1$ is always a resonance value. The resonance analysis reveals that one modulus per irreducible component of the curve $f = 0$ is lost (the residues of the form $dx \wedge dy / f$ along each component). Since the sum of residues is zero, the effective number of lost moduli is $r-1$ where $r$ is the number of components. Therefore the number of moduli equals the degree of non-quasi-homogeneity minus $(r-1)$. When the curve has only one component ($r=1$), no moduli are lost from the generic count. For more components, additional constraints reduce the moduli space dimension.
