The ADE classification of simple singularities of hypersurfaces is a cornerstone of singularity theory. The types $A_\mu$ (cusp-type), $D_\mu$ (fold-type), $E_6$, $E_7$, and $E_8$ are the simple singularities, so named because they have no continuous moduli. These normal forms arise from the classification of critical points of functions up to right-equivalence (coordinate changes in the domain). The same ADE families appear throughout mathematics—in Lie algebras, representation theory of quivers, and the classification of Poisson structures in the plane. The indices correspond to the Milnor number of the singularity.
