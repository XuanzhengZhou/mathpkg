The proof follows from Corollary 1 combined with the ADE classification of simple singularities. By Corollary 1, for any ADE polynomial $f$, the form reduces to $dx \wedge dy \wedge dz / f$. The codimension of the singularity type of $f$ determines the minimum number of parameters in a family where such a singularity appears generically. For $\ell \leq 6$, only singularities of codimension $\leq 6$ can occur. Enumerating the ADE singularities by their codimensions (with appropriate sign choices accounting for the $\pm$ in the normal forms): $A_0$ (codim 0), $A_1$ (codim 1), $A_2$ (codim 2), $A_3$ (codim 3), $D_4$ (codim 4), $A_4$ (codim 4), $D_5$ (codim 5), $A_5$ (codim 5), $D_6$ (codim 6), $A_6$ (codim 6), $E_6$ (codim 6). Counting distinct sign choices yields 24 total normal forms.
