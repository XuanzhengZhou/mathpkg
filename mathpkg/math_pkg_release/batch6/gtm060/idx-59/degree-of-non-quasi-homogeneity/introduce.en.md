The degree of non-quasi-homogeneity measures how far a function germ is from being quasi-homogeneous. If $f$ is quasi-homogeneous (with respect to some positive weights), then $f$ belongs to its Jacobian ideal, so the quotient space is zero. More generally, this invariant equals the number of continuous moduli in the classification of differential forms $f^\beta h \, dx$ (for fixed $f$ and generic $\beta$). It appears as the dimension of the space of infinitesimal deformations that cannot be absorbed by coordinate changes preserving the singularity hypersurface $f = 0$.
