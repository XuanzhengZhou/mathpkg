A stationary rotation of a rigid body is a motion in which the angular velocity vector remains constant in both magnitude and direction, i.e., $\omega = \text{const}$ in space and $\Omega = \text{const}$ in the body frame. Such motions correspond to equilibrium points (fixed points) of the Euler equations. They occur precisely when the body rotates about one of its principal axes of inertia.
