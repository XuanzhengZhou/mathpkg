The stationary solutions of Euler's equations corresponding to rotation about the largest and smallest principal axes of inertia are stable in the sense of Liapunov, while the solution corresponding to the middle principal axis is unstable. This is the "intermediate axis theorem" or "tennis racket theorem": for a small deviation of initial conditions from rotation about the largest or smallest axis, the trajectory remains a small closed curve; for a small deviation from the middle axis, the trajectory undergoes large excursions.
