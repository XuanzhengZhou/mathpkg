The motion of $M$ is confined to the intersection of an ellipsoid $2E = M_1^2/I_1 + M_2^2/I_2 + M_3^2/I_3$ and a sphere $M^2 = M_1^2 + M_2^2 + M_3^2$. The semiaxes of the ellipsoid are $\sqrt{2EI_1} > \sqrt{2EI_2} > \sqrt{2EI_3}$.

For a small deviation of the initial condition from $M = M_1 e_1$ (rotation about the largest axis), the trajectory lies on the intersection of the ellipsoid with a sphere whose radius is close to $\sqrt{2EI_1}$. The intersection consists of small closed curves encircling the ends of the largest semiaxis. Similarly, for a small deviation from $M = M_3 e_3$, the trajectory is a small closed curve encircling the ends of the smallest semiaxis.

For a small deviation from $M = M_2 e_2$ (rotation about the middle axis), the sphere and ellipsoid intersect along curves that are not small — they extend across a significant portion of the ellipsoid. Near the middle semiaxis endpoints, the intersection degenerates to two circles at $M = \sqrt{2EI_2}$, and a small perturbation sends the trajectory into a large closed curve. Therefore rotation about the middle axis is unstable.
