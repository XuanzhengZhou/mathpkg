The Euler equations for a rigid body possess two independent first integrals: the kinetic energy $E$ and the squared magnitude of the angular momentum $M^2$. These are quadratic forms in the components of the angular momentum vector $M$ expressed in the principal axes. Their conservation follows directly from the laws of conservation of energy and of angular momentum, and they determine the phase portrait of the rotational motion as the intersection of an ellipsoid with a sphere.
