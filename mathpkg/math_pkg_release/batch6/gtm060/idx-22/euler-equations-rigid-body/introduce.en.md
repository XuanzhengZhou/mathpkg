Euler's equations describe the time evolution of the angular momentum vector of a rigid body rotating about a fixed point, expressed in a coordinate system attached to the moving body. The derivation uses the fact that the angular momentum vector in space is conserved, combined with the kinematic formula for the velocity of a vector in a rotating frame. When expressed in the basis of the principal axes of inertia, the equations take the form of a three-dimensional quadratic dynamical system.
