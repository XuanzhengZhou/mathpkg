By Poinsot's theorem, the motion is described by the inertia ellipsoid rolling without slipping on the fixed invariable plane $\pi$. For initial conditions close to rotation about the largest (or smallest) principal axis, the point of tangency on the ellipsoid lies near the corresponding semiaxis endpoint. Since the instantaneous axis of rotation passes through the point of tangency, and the ellipsoid is rolling on a fixed plane, the angular velocity vector in space $\omega = \sqrt{2T}\,\xi$ (where $\xi$ is the tangency point on $B_t E$) remains close to its initial direction. The stability in the body frame $\Omega$ is already established by the Liapunov stability of the Euler equation solutions near the largest and smallest axes.
