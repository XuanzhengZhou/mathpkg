A rigid body fixed at a point admits a stationary rotation about each of its three principal axes of inertia. When the principal moments of inertia are distinct ($I_1 > I_2 > I_3$), these are the only stationary rotations of the body, since the right-hand side of the Euler equations vanishes only when two components of the angular momentum are zero, i.e., when the rotation axis coincides with a principal axis.
