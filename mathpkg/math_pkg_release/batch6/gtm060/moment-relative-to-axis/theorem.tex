Let $z$ be an axis oriented by the unit vector $e_z$ in $E^3$, $F$ a vector in $\mathbb{R}^3$, $0$ a point on the $z$ axis, and $r = x - 0 \in \mathbb{R}^3$ the radius vector relative to $0$.

The moment $M_z$ relative to the $z$ axis of the vector $F$ applied at the point $r$ is the projection onto the $z$ axis of the moment of $F$ relative to some point on this axis:
$$M_z = (e_z, [r, F]).$$

The number $M_z$ does not depend on the choice of the point $0$ on the $z$ axis, but depends on the direction of the axis.
