The mapping at a period (also called the Poincare map or monodromy operator) is defined for a system of differential equations with periodic coefficients. It sends an initial condition $x(0)$ to the value of the solution after one full period $T$, i.e., $A = g^T$. This discrete dynamical system encodes all stability properties of the original continuous-time periodic system, reducing the analysis of periodic solutions to the study of fixed points of $A$.
