This theorem provides a complete stability classification for area-preserving linear maps of $\mathbb{R}^2$ based solely on the trace. The condition $\det A = 1$ forces the product of eigenvalues to be $1$, so when $|\operatorname{tr} A| < 2$ the eigenvalues are complex conjugates on the unit circle (elliptic case, stable rotation), while for $|\operatorname{tr} A| > 2$ one eigenvalue has modulus greater than $1$ (hyperbolic case, unstable). This simple trace criterion is foundational for the analysis of parametric resonance in Hamiltonian systems.
