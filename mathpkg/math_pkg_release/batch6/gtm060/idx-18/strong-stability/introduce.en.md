Strong stability strengthens ordinary Liapunov stability by requiring that the stability property persists under small perturbations of the linear Hamiltonian system. This concept is essential in parametric resonance theory because mere stability (which can be destroyed by arbitrarily small changes in parameters) provides no guarantee for real physical systems where parameters are never known exactly. Strong stability ensures the stability is structurally robust.
