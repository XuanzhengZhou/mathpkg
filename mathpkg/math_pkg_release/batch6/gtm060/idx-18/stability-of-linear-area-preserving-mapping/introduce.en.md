This corollary applies the general properties of the mapping at a period to the specific equation $\ddot{x} = -\omega^2(1 + \varepsilon a(t))x$, which models a pendulum with oscillating frequency. Since the corresponding system is both linear and Hamiltonian, the mapping $A$ inherits linearity and area preservation, reducing the stability analysis of the trivial solution to the study of the discrete map $A$ on the phase plane.
