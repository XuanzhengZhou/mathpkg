When a mechanical system has axial symmetry, the component of angular momentum along the symmetry axis is conserved. The proof follows from the fact that the force vector lies in the plane spanned by the position vector and the symmetry axis, so the cross product $[r, \ddot{r}]$ is perpendicular to the axis. This is an instance of Noether's theorem: continuous symmetries imply conservation laws.
