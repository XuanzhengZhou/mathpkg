These properties can be attained by an arbitrarily small perturbation of the Lagrangian manifold, under which it remains Lagrangian.

Consider a simple singular point on the Lagrangian manifold. We take a system of coordinates $q_1, \ldots, q_n$ in a neighborhood of the projection of this point onto the configuration space. Let $p_1, \ldots, p_n$ be corresponding coordinates in the fiber of the cotangent bundle. In a neighborhood of a simple singular point, the Lagrangian manifold can be represented as the graph of a vector function $(q_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n)$ of the variables $(p_1, q_2, \ldots, q_n)$ (or an analogous form with a different distinguished coordinate).

The simple singular points near the given one are defined by the condition $\partial q_1 / \partial p_1 = 0$. For Lagrangian manifolds in general position, this derivative changes sign upon passing from one side of the manifold of singular points to the other.
