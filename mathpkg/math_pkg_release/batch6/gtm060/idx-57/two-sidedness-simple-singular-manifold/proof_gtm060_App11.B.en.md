Consider a simple singular point on the Lagrangian manifold. Take a system of coordinates $q_1, \ldots, q_n$ in a neighborhood of the projection of this point onto the configuration space. Let $p_1, \ldots, p_n$ be corresponding coordinates in the fiber of the cotangent bundle. In a neighborhood of a simple singular point, the Lagrangian manifold can be considered as the graph of the vector function $(q_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n)$ of the variables $(p_1, q_2, \ldots, q_n)$ (or a vector function of an analogous form in which the role of the distinguished coordinate is played by any of the remaining coordinates).

Singular points near the given one are defined by the condition $\partial q_1 / \partial p_1 = 0$. For Lagrangian manifolds in general position, this derivative changes sign upon passing from one side of the manifold of singular points to the other. We call the side where this derivative is positive the positive side.

It remains to prove that the definitions of positive direction near different points agree with one another, and that the positive direction near one point is well defined, i.e., does not depend on the coordinate system.
