This proposition asserts that within an $n$-dimensional Lagrangian manifold, the locus of the simplest (corank 1) singular points -- which forms an $(n-1)$-dimensional submanifold -- is two-sided. This means there exists a globally consistent way to distinguish the two sides of this singular hypersurface, analogous to the notion of co-orientability. The two-sidedness is demonstrated by showing that the partial derivative $\partial q_1 / \partial p_1$ changes sign when crossing the singular locus, allowing a consistent definition of a positive side independent of the choice of local coordinates.
