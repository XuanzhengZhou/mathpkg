Conjugate points are a special case of focal points that arise when the Lagrangian manifold $M$ is taken to be the fiber of the cotangent bundle over a point $q_0$ (i.e., all momenta at fixed position). A point conjugate to $q_0$ along an extremal is the projection onto configuration space of a focal point along the corresponding phase curve. In Riemannian geometry, where extremals are geodesics, conjugate points mark the locations where neighboring geodesics from the same initial point intersect. The classical example is antipodal points on a sphere, which are conjugate along any meridian geodesic.
