We first consider the case when the time interval $t$ is small. In this case, the sum is reduced to a single term, since the Lagrangian manifold obtained from the original Lagrangian manifold by the phase flow transformation after small time projects diffeomorphically onto the configuration space. In other words, of the family of particles corresponding to the initial condition for Schrödinger's equation, only one arrives at $Q$ after the small time $t$.

For small $t$, the Morse index is equal to zero. In this way the function $\psi(Q, t)$ has, like the initial condition, a rapidly oscillating form. Thus, the function $S$ defining the wave fronts at time $t$ is none other than the value at time $t$ of the action functional.

For larger $t$, the Lagrangian manifold $g^t M$ may have singularities when projected onto the configuration space, and multiple trajectories may arrive at $Q$. The Morse index $\mu_j$ counts the focal points encountered, which contributes the Maslov phase correction $e^{-(i\pi/2)\mu_j}$. The Jacobian determinant $\left| \frac{DQ}{Dq_j} \right|^{-1/2}$ appears from the stationary phase method applied to the oscillatory integral representation of the solution.
