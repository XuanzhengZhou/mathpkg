The momentum of a system of particles is the vector sum of the individual momenta $m_i \dot{r}_i$. It is the total linear momentum of the system. For a closed system (no external forces), momentum is conserved. The momentum of the system equals the momentum of a single particle of mass $\sum m_i$ located at the center of mass.
