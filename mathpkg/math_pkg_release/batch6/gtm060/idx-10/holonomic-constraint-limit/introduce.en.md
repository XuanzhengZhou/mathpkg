This theorem provides the physical motivation for the definition of holonomic constraints in Lagrangian mechanics. It shows that a system confined by an infinitely strong potential well near a submanifold of configuration space behaves exactly as a Lagrangian system on that submanifold with the restricted Lagrangian $L_*$. The potential barrier is modeled as $U_N = U_0 + N q_2^2$, and taking $N \to \infty$ produces the constrained dynamics as a limit of unconstrained motions. This justifies the standard practice of treating constraints as geometric restrictions on the configuration space rather than as large forces.
