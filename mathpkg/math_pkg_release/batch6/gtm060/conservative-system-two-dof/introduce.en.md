A two-degree-of-freedom system is conservative if the force field is the negative gradient of a scalar potential. Unlike the one-dimensional case where every system admits a potential, in two dimensions this imposes a nontrivial condition: the work must be path-independent. When the condition holds, the total energy is conserved and the motion is confined to the region where $U(x) \leq E$.
