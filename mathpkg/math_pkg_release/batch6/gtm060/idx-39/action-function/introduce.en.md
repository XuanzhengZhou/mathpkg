The action function \(S(q, t)\) is the integral of the Lagrangian along the unique extremal connecting two points in the extended configuration space. It is the mechanical analogue of the optical path length in geometric optics. For the definition to be well-posed, one requires that the extremals from the initial point form a central field — meaning the mapping from initial velocities and time to the final point is nondegenerate. Under this condition, the action function depends differentiably on its endpoint, and its differential is the fundamental object that leads to the Hamilton-Jacobi equation.
