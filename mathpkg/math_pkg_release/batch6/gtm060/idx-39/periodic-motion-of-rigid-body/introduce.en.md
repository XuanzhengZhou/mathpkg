This theorem guarantees the existence of periodic motions for a rigid body with a fixed point in an arbitrary potential field. The key observation is that the configuration space \(\mathrm{SO}(3)\) is not simply connected -- it contains non-contractible loops. By applying the Jacobi metric construction together with the fact that on a non-simply-connected compact Riemannian manifold every nontrivial free homotopy class contains a closed geodesic, one obtains at least one periodic orbit. The result holds for arbitrarily large total energy \(h\).
