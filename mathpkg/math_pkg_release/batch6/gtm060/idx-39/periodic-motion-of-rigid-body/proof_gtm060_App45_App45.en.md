The configuration space $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ of a rigid body fastened at a stationary point is not simply connected: there exist non-contractible curves in it. Applying the Jacobi metric theorem, for sufficiently large $h$ (larger than the maximum potential energy) the metric $d\rho = \sqrt{h - U} ds$ has no singularities. On a non-simply-connected compact Riemannian manifold, every nontrivial free homotopy class contains a closed geodesic. Translating this geodesic back to the original mechanical system via the Jacobi metric yields a periodic motion. The energy $h$ can be taken arbitrarily large, so periodic motions exist for arbitrarily high energies.
