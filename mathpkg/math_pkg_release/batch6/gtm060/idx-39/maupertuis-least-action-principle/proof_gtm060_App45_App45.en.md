Parametrize $\gamma$ by $\tau$, $a \leq \tau \leq b$, $\gamma(a) = q_0$, $\gamma(b) = q_1$. Then at every point $q$ of $\gamma$ there is a velocity vector $\dot{q} = d\gamma(\tau)/d\tau$, and the corresponding momentum $p = \partial L/\partial \dot{q}$. If the parameter $\tau$ is chosen so that $H(p, q) = h$, then we obtain a curve $\hat{\gamma}: q = \gamma(\tau)$, $p = \partial L/\partial \dot{q}$ on the surface $M^{2n-1}$. Applying the theorem above to the curve $\hat{\gamma}$ on $M^{2n-1}$, we obtain the corollary.
