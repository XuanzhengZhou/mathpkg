This theorem, a direct application of Maupertuis' principle of least action, states that the trajectories of a mechanical system with kinetic energy \(T = \frac{1}{2}(ds/d\tau)^2\) and potential energy \(U(\mathbf{q})\) at fixed total energy \(h\) are geodesics of the Jacobi metric \(d\rho = \sqrt{h - U(\mathbf{q})} ds\). The Jacobi metric is conformally equivalent to the original Riemannian metric \(ds\), with a conformal factor that depends only on position through the potential energy. This reduces the problem of finding mechanical trajectories to finding geodesics in a modified Riemannian geometry.
