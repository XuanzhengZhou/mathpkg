Huygens' principle describes the propagation of wave fronts in geometric optics. It states that the wave front at time \(t + s\) from a source point \(q_0\) is precisely the envelope of the wave fronts at time \(s\) emanating from each point on the wave front at time \(t\). This principle, which follows from Fermat's principle of least time, reduces the description of wave propagation to two complementary pictures: tracing individual rays (shortest-time paths) or tracing entire wave fronts. The principle generalizes from point sources to arbitrary initial wave fronts and from three-dimensional space to any smooth manifold.
