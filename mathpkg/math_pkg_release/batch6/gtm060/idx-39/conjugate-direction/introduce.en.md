In an anisotropic optical medium, the propagation of light is described at each point by the indicatrix -- a surface of velocity vectors. The direction conjugate to a given velocity vector \(v\) is defined as the direction of the hyperplane tangent to the indicatrix at the point \(v\). This geometric notion encodes how the direction of wave front motion (normal slowness vector \(p\)) relates to the direction of the ray (velocity vector \(\dot{q}\)): the two directions are conjugate to each other with respect to the indicatrix.
