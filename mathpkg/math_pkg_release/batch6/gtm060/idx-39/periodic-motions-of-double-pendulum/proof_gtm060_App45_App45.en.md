The configuration space of the planar double pendulum is the torus $T^2$. If $h$ is larger than the maximum value of $U$ on the configuration space, then the metric $d\rho = \sqrt{h - U(\mathbf{q})} ds$ has no singularities. Therefore, we can apply topological theorems about geodesics on riemannian manifolds. Among all closed curves on $T^2$ making $m$ rotations around the parallel and $n$ around the meridian, there exists a curve of shortest length. This curve is a closed geodesic. By the Jacobi metric theorem, this geodesic corresponds to a trajectory of the mechanical system, i.e., a periodic motion of the double pendulum with the prescribed rotation numbers.
