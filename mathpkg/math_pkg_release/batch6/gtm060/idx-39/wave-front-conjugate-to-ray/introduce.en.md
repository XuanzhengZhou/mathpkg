This theorem establishes the fundamental geometric relationship between wave fronts and rays in geometric optics. At any point along a ray, the tangent plane to the wave front passing through that point is conjugate (in the sense of the indicatrix) to the direction of the ray. The proof follows directly from Huygens' principle by considering an infinitesimal neighborhood: for very small \(\varepsilon\), the wave front emanating from a nearby point on the ray approximates the indicatrix scaled by \(\varepsilon\), and by Huygens' principle this front is tangent to the original wave front, yielding the conjugacy relation in the limit.
