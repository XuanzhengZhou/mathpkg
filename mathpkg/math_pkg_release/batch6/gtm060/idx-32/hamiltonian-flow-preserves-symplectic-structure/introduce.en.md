This fundamental theorem states that the Hamiltonian phase flow $g^t$ preserves the symplectic form $\omega^2$ under pullback: $(g^t)^* \omega^2 = \omega^2$. In the special case of a 2-dimensional phase space ($n=1$), this reduces to Liouville's theorem on the conservation of phase area. The proof uses the notion of the track $Jc$ of a chain under the homotopy $g^t$ and the key lemma relating the derivative of the integral of $\omega^2$ over the track to the integral of $dH$.
