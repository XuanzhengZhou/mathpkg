This corollary extends the preservation of the symplectic form to all its exterior powers: $(\omega^2)^2, (\omega^2)^3, \ldots$ are integral invariants of the Hamiltonian phase flow. The top exterior power $(\omega^2)^n$ is a nondegenerate volume form on the $2n$-dimensional symplectic manifold, and its invariance implies Liouville's theorem on volume preservation in phase space. The fact that $(\omega^2)^k = 0$ for $k > n$ follows from dimension considerations and reflects Poincaré's discovery of a whole series of preserved differential forms.
