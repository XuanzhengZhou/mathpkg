A symplectic structure on a manifold establishes a natural isomorphism between tangent vectors and 1-forms, analogous to the isomorphism provided by a Riemannian metric. Given a symplectic manifold $(M^{2n}, \omega^2)$, each tangent vector $\xi$ defines a 1-form $\omega^1_\xi$ via contraction with the symplectic form: $\omega^1_\xi(\eta) = \omega^2(\eta, \xi)$. This correspondence is a linear isomorphism $I: T^*M_x \to TM_x$, which plays a fundamental role in defining Hamiltonian vector fields.
