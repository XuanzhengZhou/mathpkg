This theorem reformulates the preservation of the symplectic structure in the language of integral invariants: the symplectic form $\omega^2$ is an integral invariant of the Hamiltonian phase flow $g^t$. This means the integral of $\omega^2$ over any $2$-chain equals the integral over the transported chain $g^t c$. It is an equivalent restatement of $(g^t)^* \omega^2 = \omega^2$, which for $n=1$ reduces to Liouville's area-preservation theorem.
