Let $g: M \to M$ be a differentiable map. A differential $k$-form $\omega$ is called an \textbf{integral invariant} of the map $g$ if the integrals of $\omega$ on any $k$-chain $c$ and on its image under $g$ are the same:
$$
\int_{gc} \omega = \int_c \omega.
$$
Equivalently, $\omega$ is an integral invariant of $g$ if and only if $g^*\omega = \omega$.

If $\omega^k$ and $\omega^\ell$ are integral invariants of $g$, then their exterior product $\omega^k \wedge \omega^\ell$ is also an integral invariant of $g$.
