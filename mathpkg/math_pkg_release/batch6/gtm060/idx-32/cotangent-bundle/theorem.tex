Let $V$ be an $n$-dimensional differentiable manifold. A $1$-form on the tangent space to $V$ at a point $x$ is called a \textbf{cotangent vector} to $V$ at $x$. The set of all cotangent vectors to $V$ at $x$ forms an $n$-dimensional vector space, dual to the tangent space $TV_x$. This vector space of cotangent vectors is denoted by $T^*V_x$ and called the \textbf{cotangent space} to $V$ at $x$.

The union of the cotangent spaces to the manifold at all of its points is called the \textbf{cotangent bundle} of $V$ and is denoted by $T^*V$. The set $T^*V$ has a natural structure of a differentiable manifold of dimension $2n$. A point of $T^*V$ is a $1$-form on the tangent space to $V$ at some point of $V$. If $q$ is a choice of $n$ local coordinates for points in $V$, then such a form is given by its $n$ components $p$. Together, the $2n$ numbers $p, q$ form a collection of local coordinates for points in $T^*V$.

There is a natural projection $f: T^*V \to V$ sending every $1$-form on $TV_x$ to the point $x$. The projection $f$ is differentiable and surjective. The pre-image of a point $x \in V$ under $f$ is the cotangent space $T^*V_x$.
