Using the effective potential reduction, the central field problem is solved by two quadratures: $\dot{r} = \sqrt{2(E-V(r))}$ gives $t(r)$, and $d\varphi/dr = M/(r^2\sqrt{2(E-V(r))})$ gives the orbit shape $\varphi(r)$. These formulas reduce the problem to evaluating definite integrals.
