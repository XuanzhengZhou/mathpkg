In Newtonian mechanics, a point mass constrained to a surface would require an additional force to keep it on the surface instead of following a straight-line trajectory. This additional force is the constraint force $\mathbf{R} = m\ddot{\mathbf{x}} + \partial U/\partial \mathbf{x}$. Physically, it can be understood as the limit of a very strong potential $N U_1$ (with $N \to \infty$) that confines the particle to the constraint surface, where the constraint force is orthogonal to the surface.
