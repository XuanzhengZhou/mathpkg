If a Lagrangian system $(M, L)$ admits the one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms $h^s : M \to M$, $s \in \mathbb{R}$, then the Lagrangian system of equations of motion has a first integral $I : TM \to \mathbb{R}$. In local coordinates $\mathbf{q}$ on $M$ the integral $I$ is written in the form

$$I(\mathbf{q}, \dot{\mathbf{q}}) = \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\mathbf{q}}} \left. \frac{d}{ds} \right|_{s=0} h^s(\mathbf{q}).$$
