Noether's theorem is a fundamental result in mathematical physics that establishes a deep connection between continuous symmetries of a physical system and conserved quantities. For a Lagrangian system $(M, L)$, every one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms $h^s : M \to M$ that preserves the Lagrangian yields a first integral $I : TM \to \mathbb{R}$ of the equations of motion. In local coordinates, the conserved quantity takes the form $I = (\partial L / \partial \dot{\mathbf{q}})(d/ds)h^s(\mathbf{q})|_{s=0}$. The theorem generalizes familiar conservation laws such as conservation of momentum (from translation symmetry) and conservation of angular momentum (from rotational symmetry).
