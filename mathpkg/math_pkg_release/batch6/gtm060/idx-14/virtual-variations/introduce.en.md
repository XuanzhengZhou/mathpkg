Virtual variations are the infinitesimal displacements compatible with the constraints of a mechanical system. They are formalized as tangent vectors to the configuration manifold $M$, representing directions in which the system can move without violating the constraints at a given instant. This geometric interpretation unifies the treatment of holonomic constraints and forms the foundation for the D'Alembert-Lagrange principle.
