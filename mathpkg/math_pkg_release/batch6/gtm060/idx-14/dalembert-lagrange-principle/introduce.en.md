The D'Alembert-Lagrange principle provides a variational formulation of constrained mechanical systems. It states that the sum of the works of the constraint forces on any virtual variation (tangent vector to the configuration manifold) is identically zero. This principle can be derived as a theorem when constraints are treated as limits of strong potentials, or it can be taken as the definition of ideal holonomic constraints. It is equivalent to the condition that the constrained motion be a conditional extremal of the action functional.
