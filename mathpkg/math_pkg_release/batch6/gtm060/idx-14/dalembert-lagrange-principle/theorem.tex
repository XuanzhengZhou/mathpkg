For a system of point masses $\mathbf{x}_i$ with masses $m_i$ subject to holonomic constraints, the D'Alembert-Lagrange principle states:

$$\sum_i \left( m_i \ddot{\mathbf{x}}_i + \frac{\partial U}{\partial \mathbf{x}_i}, \xi_i \right) = 0$$

for any virtual variation $\{\xi_i\} \in TM_{\mathbf{x}}$. Equivalently,

$$\left( m \ddot{\mathbf{x}} + \frac{\partial U}{\partial \mathbf{x}}, \xi \right) = 0$$

for any virtual variation $\xi$. The work of the constraint forces on any virtual variation is zero.
