The first integral $I$ arising from a one-parameter symmetry group $h^s$ quantifies the conserved quantity predicted by Noether's theorem. In local coordinates, it is computed as the partial derivative of the Lagrangian with respect to velocity, contracted with the infinitesimal generator of the symmetry $(d/ds)h^s(\mathbf{q})|_{s=0}$. For a system of point masses with translation symmetry, $I$ reduces to the total linear momentum; for rotational symmetry, it yields angular momentum. Crucially, $I$ is a well-defined function on $TM$ independent of coordinate choices, since it represents the rate of change of $L(v)$ as $v$ varies within a fixed tangent space $TM_x$.
