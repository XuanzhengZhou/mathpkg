For a Lagrangian system with configuration manifold $M$ and Lagrangian $L : TM \to \mathbb{R}$, if $h^s : M \to M$ is a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms preserving $L$, then the quantity

$$I(\mathbf{q}, \dot{\mathbf{q}}) = \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\mathbf{q}}} \left. \frac{d}{ds} \right|_{s=0} h^s(\mathbf{q})$$

is called the \textit{first integral} corresponding to the symmetry $h^s$. The function $I : TM \to \mathbb{R}$ is constant on solutions of Lagrange's equations and does not depend on the choice of local coordinates $\mathbf{q}$.
