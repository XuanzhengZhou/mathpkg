An ideal constraint is a holonomic constraint for which the constraint forces perform no work on virtual variations. This idealized model captures smooth, frictionless constraints such as a bead on a wire or a particle sliding on a smooth surface. Ideal constraints can be defined in three equivalent ways: as limits of systems with increasingly strong confining potentials, as pairs $(M, L)$ where $M$ is a submanifold of the unconstrained configuration space, or directly through the D'Alembert-Lagrange principle.
