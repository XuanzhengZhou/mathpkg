The normal field lemma is a fundamental result in the calculus of variations that connects integral orthogonality conditions to pointwise perpendicularity. It states that if a continuous vector field $\mathbf{f}(t)$ is orthogonal (in the $L^2$ sense) to all tangent vector fields along a curve that vanish at the endpoints, then $\mathbf{f}$ is pointwise normal to the constraint manifold. This lemma generalizes the argument used to derive the Euler-Lagrange equations and serves as a crucial step in proving the equivalence between D'Alembert's principle and the variational formulation.
