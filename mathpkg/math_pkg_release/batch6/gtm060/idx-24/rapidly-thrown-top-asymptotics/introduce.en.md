This theorem describes the asymptotic behavior of a Lagrange top that is released from rest with a rapidly spinning axis. As the axial angular velocity $\omega_3$ tends to infinity, the nutation frequency grows linearly with $\omega_3$, the nutation amplitude decays quadratically, and the precession frequency decays inversely. The proof uses a similarity argument to reinterpret the small-gravity ($g \to 0$) asymptotics in terms of the large-$\omega_3$ limit. The key step is applying the perturbed-minimum lemma to locate the new equilibrium inclination $\theta_g$ and then computing the nutation amplitude from the initial conditions $\theta = \theta_0$, $\dot{\theta} = 0$.
