For a system with one degree of freedom governed by $\ddot{x} = f(x)$, the total energy $E = T + U = \frac{1}{2}\dot{x}^2 - \int f(x)dx$ is conserved along solutions. The proof follows directly from the chain rule and the equation of motion, showing that $dE/dt = \dot{x}(\ddot{x} - f(x)) = 0$.
