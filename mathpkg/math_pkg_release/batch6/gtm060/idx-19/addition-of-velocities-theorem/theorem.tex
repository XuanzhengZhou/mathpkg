If the moving system $K$ has a translational motion relative to $k$, then the absolute velocity is equal to the sum of the relative velocity and the velocity of the motion of the system $K$:

$$v = v' + v_0,$$

where

$$v = \dot{q} \in k \quad \text{is the absolute velocity},$$

$$v' = B\dot{Q} \in k \quad \text{is the relative velocity (distinct from } \dot{Q} \in K\text{)},$$

$$v_0 = \dot{r} \in k \quad \text{is the velocity of motion of the moving coordinate system.}$$
