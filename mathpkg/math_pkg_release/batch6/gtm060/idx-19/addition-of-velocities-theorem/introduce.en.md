This theorem expresses the classical (Galilean) law of velocity addition for translational motion of coordinate systems. The absolute velocity $v$ of a point as measured in the fixed frame $k$ is the vector sum of its relative velocity $v'$ (as measured in the moving frame $K$, after mapping to $k$ via $B$) and the frame's own velocity $v_0 = \dot{r}$. The distinction between $\dot{Q} \in K$ and $v' = B\dot{Q} \in k$ is important because these vectors belong to different vector spaces.
