Starting from the general formula for the position of a point:

$$q(t) = D_t Q(t) = B_t Q(t) + r(t).$$

Differentiating with respect to $t$ gives the general velocity addition formula:

$$\dot{q} = \dot{B}Q + B\dot{Q} + \dot{r}.$$

For translational motion, $\dot{B} = 0$, so the first term vanishes. Substituting this yields:

$$\dot{q} = B\dot{Q} + \dot{r}.$$

The absolute velocity is $v = \dot{q} \in k$. The relative velocity is $v' = B\dot{Q} \in k$, which is the velocity relative to the moving system $K$ mapped into $k$ via the constant rotation $B$. The frame velocity is $v_0 = \dot{r} \in k$. Therefore:

$$v = v' + v_0.$$
