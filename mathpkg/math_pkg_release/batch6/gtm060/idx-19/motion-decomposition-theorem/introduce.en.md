This theorem states that any motion $D_t$ of one oriented Euclidean space relative to another can be uniquely expressed as a rotation $B_t$ followed by a translation $C_t$. The rotation $B_t$ maps the moving frame $K$ to the fixed frame $k$ while preserving the origin, and the translation shifts by the vector $r(t) = D_t(0)$. This decomposition is fundamental for separating the rotational and translational components of rigid body motion.
