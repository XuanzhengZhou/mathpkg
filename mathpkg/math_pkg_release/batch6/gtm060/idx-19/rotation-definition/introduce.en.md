A rotation $B_t: K \to k$ is a special type of motion between oriented Euclidean spaces that fixes the origin, making it a linear orthogonal transformation. Rotations form the orthogonal part of the decomposition of any general motion into a rotation followed by a translation. The time dependence of $B_t$ captures how the orientation of the moving frame changes relative to the fixed frame.
