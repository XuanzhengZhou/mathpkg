A translational motion is a motion between two coordinate frames where the rotational part $B_t$ is constant in time. Only the translation vector $r(t)$ varies. In this case, the moving frame $K$ does not change its orientation relative to the fixed frame $k$; every axis of $K$ remains parallel to its initial direction. Translational motion simplifies velocity addition, as the relative and absolute velocities differ only by the translational velocity of the moving frame.
