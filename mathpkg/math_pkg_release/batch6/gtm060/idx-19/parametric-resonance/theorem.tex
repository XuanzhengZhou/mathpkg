For $\omega \approx k/2$, $k = 1, 2, \ldots$, the lowest equilibrium position of the idealized swing

$$\ddot{x} = -\omega^2(1 + \varepsilon a(t))x$$

is unstable and it swings under an arbitrarily small periodic change of length. This phenomenon is called \text{parametric resonance}. A characteristic property of parametric resonance is that it is strongest when the frequency of the variation of the parameter $v$ (in the equation, $v = 1$) is twice the characteristic frequency $\omega$. Theoretically, parametric resonance can be observed for the infinite collection of cases $\omega/v \approx k/2$, $k = 1, 2, \ldots$.
