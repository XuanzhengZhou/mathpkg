Parametric resonance is a phenomenon in oscillatory systems where a periodic variation of a system parameter (such as the length of a pendulum) induces instability at certain resonant frequencies. The resonance is strongest when the frequency of the parameter variation is approximately twice the natural frequency of the system. For the idealized swing modeled by a Hill-type equation, the unstable region appears as narrow "tongues" near $\omega \approx k/2$ for $k = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$. In practice, parametric resonance is usually observed only for small values of $k$ because the instability region narrows rapidly and friction suppresses higher-order resonances.
