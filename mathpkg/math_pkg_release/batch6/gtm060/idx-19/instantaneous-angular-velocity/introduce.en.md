This theorem establishes that for any rotation of a rigid body, there exists a unique instantaneous angular velocity vector $\omega(t)$ such that the velocity of a point fixed in the rotating frame is given by the cross product $\dot{q} = [\omega, q]$. The proof uses the fact that the operator $A = \dot{B}B^{-1}$ is skew-symmetric and that every skew-symmetric operator on $\mathbb{R}^3$ corresponds to vector multiplication by some $\omega$. The instantaneous angular velocity describes the axis and rate of rotation at each moment, which can change over time.
