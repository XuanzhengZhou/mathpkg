\textbf{Hamilton's Principle of Least Action.} The actual motion of a mechanical system between times $t_0$ and $t_1$ with fixed initial and final configurations $q(t_0)$ and $q(t_1)$ is an extremal of the action functional
$$\Phi(\gamma) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} L(q, \dot{q}, t) \, dt.$$

Equivalently, the Euler-Lagrange equations for this functional give the equations of motion:
$$\frac{d}{dt}\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} - \frac{\partial L}{\partial q} = 0.$$
