The index of a closed curve on a Lagrangian submanifold is an integer invariant computed via the winding number of the complex determinant of a unitary transformation taking the tangent plane to a reference plane. To each point on the curve one associates a complex number of modulus one representing the position of the tangent Lagrangian plane relative to a fixed reference. After traversing the closed curve, the square of the determinant of this transformation executes a number of full rotations, and this integer count is the Maslov index of the curve. This index appears in asymptotic quantization conditions and relates the Morse index of a phase curve to the Maslov index of a lifted curve in an extended phase space.
