The index of a closed curve on a Lagrangian submanifold of the linear phase space $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ can be calculated using the complex structure. Introduce a euclidean structure (with scalar square $p^2 + q^2$) and a complex structure $I(p,q) = (-q,p)$, related by $[x,y] = (Ix, y)$.

For each point of the closed curve lying on the Lagrangian submanifold, consider the tangent plane to the Lagrangian submanifold. The image of this tangent plane under the unitary transformation taking it to a reference Lagrangian plane (e.g., the $q$-plane $\{(0,q)\}$) yields a complex number of modulus one. As the point moves along the closed curve, this complex number changes. After one full circuit of the curve, the square of the determinant makes an integral number of rotations around the origin in the complex plane (oriented from $1$ to $i$). This integer is the **index** of the closed curve.
