The complex structure on a linear phase space is a linear map $I$ that squares to minus the identity, making the real vector space $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ into a complex vector space $\mathbb{C}^n$. Arnold introduces this structure in the context of calculating indices of closed curves on Lagrangian submanifolds. The complex structure is compatible with both the symplectic form (via $[x,y] = (Ix, y)$) and the euclidean metric, forming a Kähler triple on the phase space. Transformations that preserve any two of these three structures automatically preserve the third and are called unitary transformations.
