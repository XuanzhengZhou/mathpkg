A Poisson manifold is the natural setting for Hamiltonian mechanics when the Poisson bracket may be degenerate — something that occurs frequently in applications, such as the bracket of angular momentum components. The manifold foliates into symplectic leaves of varying dimensions, each carrying a nondegenerate symplectic structure. These leaves are the "elementary" phase spaces; the full Poisson manifold is a union of such symplectic manifolds fitting together smoothly. Arnold points out that in applications to the mathematical physics of continuous media, one often encounters Poisson structures on infinite-dimensional manifolds, where the symplectic leaves frequently (but not always) have finite dimension or codimension.
