Gardner's theorem extends Lax's insight by showing that the stationary points of the higher KdV conserved quantities correspond to finite-dimensional completely integrable Hamiltonian systems. These "finite-gap" or "multiphase" solutions of the KdV equation are the natural generalization of the single-soliton: they are quasi-periodic functions of $x$ and $t$ determined by a Riemann surface of finite genus. Novikov later showed that these solutions give potentials $u(x)$ for which the Schrödinger operator $-X'' + u(x)X = \lambda X$ has a finite number of zones of parametric resonance (finite-gap potentials). This beautiful connection links the KdV equation, algebraic geometry (Riemann surfaces), and spectral theory of periodic differential operators.
