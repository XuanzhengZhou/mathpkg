A **Lagrangian mapping** is the projection mapping of an $n$-dimensional Lagrangian submanifold of the $2n$-dimensional phase space (the cotangent bundle of an $n$-dimensional configuration manifold) onto the configuration space.

At most points, this mapping is a local diffeomorphism. At singular points of the Lagrangian manifold where the rank of the differential drops, the image forms a **caustic** in the configuration space.

More formally: let $M$ be an $n$-dimensional configuration manifold, and let $L \subset T^*M$ be an $n$-dimensional Lagrangian submanifold. The Lagrangian mapping is the restriction to $L$ of the cotangent bundle projection $\pi: T^*M \to M$, i.e., $\pi|_L: L \to M$.
