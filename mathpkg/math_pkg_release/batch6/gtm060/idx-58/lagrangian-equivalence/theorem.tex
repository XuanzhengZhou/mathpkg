Two Lagrangian mappings $\pi|_L: L \to M$ and $\pi'|_{L'}: L' \to M'$ of manifolds of the same dimension $n$ are **Lagrangian equivalent** if there exists a **Lagrangian equivalence mapping**, i.e., a symplectic diffeomorphism $\Phi$ of the first phase space $T^*M$ to the second $T^*M'$ such that:
1. $\Phi$ takes fibers of the first cotangent bundle to fibers of the second,
2. $\Phi(L) = L'$, i.e., $\Phi$ maps the first Lagrangian submanifold onto the second.

Equivalently, two Lagrangian mappings are Lagrangian equivalent if they are related by diffeomorphisms of the pre-image and image induced by a symplectic diffeomorphism of the ambient phase spaces preserving the fibration structure.

In particular, the caustics of two Lagrangian equivalent mappings are diffeomorphic. However, the classification up to Lagrangian equivalence is finer than the classification of caustics alone, and finer than the classification up to arbitrary diffeomorphisms of pre-image and image.
