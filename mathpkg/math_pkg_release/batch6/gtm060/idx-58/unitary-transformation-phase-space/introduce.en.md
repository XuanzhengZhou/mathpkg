A unitary transformation of the linear phase space $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ is a linear transformation that preserves any two of the three compatible structures: symplectic, euclidean, and complex. The compatibility condition $[x,y] = (Ix, y)$ implies that preserving any two automatically preserves the third. These transformations form the unitary group $\mathrm{U}(n)$, realized as $\mathrm{Sp}(2n,\mathbb{R}) \cap \mathrm{O}(2n,\mathbb{R})$. Their key geometric property is that they map Lagrangian planes to Lagrangian planes, making them essential for computing the Maslov index of closed curves via the complex determinant.
