Linear transformations of the phase space $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ preserving any two (and, therefore, all three) of the symplectic structure $dp \wedge dq$, the euclidean structure (with scalar square $p^2 + q^2$), and the complex structure $I(p,q) = (-q,p)$ are called **unitary transformations**.

Such transformations take Lagrangian planes to Lagrangian planes. The group of unitary transformations is the intersection of the symplectic group $\mathrm{Sp}(2n, \mathbb{R})$ and the orthogonal group $\mathrm{O}(2n, \mathbb{R})$, which is isomorphic to the unitary group $\mathrm{U}(n)$.
