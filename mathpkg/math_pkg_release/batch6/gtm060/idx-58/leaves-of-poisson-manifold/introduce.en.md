The symplectic leaves are the fundamental building blocks of a Poisson manifold. Just as a manifold can be foliated by submanifolds, a Poisson manifold decomposes into symplectic leaves — each leaf is a symplectic manifold where the Poisson bracket is nondegenerate. The foliation is typically singular: leaves can have different dimensions, and they "fit together" smoothly in a way governed by the Poisson structure. The classic example is the dual of a Lie algebra with the Lie-Poisson bracket, where the leaves are the coadjoint orbits. This decomposition is essential for understanding how degenerate Poisson structures arise from families of ordinary symplectic manifolds, and it underpins the method of symplectic reduction.
