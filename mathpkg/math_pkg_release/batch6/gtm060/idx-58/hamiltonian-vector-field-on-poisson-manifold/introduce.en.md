The Hamiltonian vector field is the geometric object that generates the dynamics on a Poisson manifold, just as in classical symplectic mechanics. The key difference is that on a degenerate Poisson manifold, the map from functions to vector fields has a nontrivial kernel: the Casimir functions. These are constants of motion for any Hamiltonian flow, playing a crucial role in the theory of integrable systems and symplectic reduction. The fact that $\theta_{\{a,b\}} = [\theta_a, \theta_b]$ encodes the compatibility between the Poisson bracket and the dynamics it generates.
