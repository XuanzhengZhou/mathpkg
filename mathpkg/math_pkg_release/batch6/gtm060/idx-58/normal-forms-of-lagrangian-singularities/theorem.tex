Let $(q_1, \ldots, q_n)$ be coordinates on the configuration space and $(p_1, \ldots, p_n)$ the corresponding momenta, forming a symplectic coordinate system on phase space. A Lagrangian manifold is given via a generating function $F$ by

$$q_i = \frac{\partial F}{\partial p_i}, \quad p_j = -\frac{\partial F}{\partial q_j},$$

where $i$ runs over some subset of $\{1,\ldots,n\}$ and $j$ over the complement ($i=1$, $j>1$ for $A_k$ singularities; $i=1,2$, $j>2$ for $D_k$ and $E_k$).

The **normal forms of typical singularities** of projections of Lagrangian manifolds of dimension $n \leq 5$ are given by the generating functions:

For $n = 1$:
$$A_1: \; F = p_1^2 \qquad A_2: \; F = \pm p_1^3$$

For $n = 2$ (in addition to the above):
$$A_3: \; F = \pm p_1^4 + q_2 p_1^2$$

For $n = 3$ (in addition to the preceding):
$$A_4: \; F = \pm p_1^5 + q_3 p_1^3 + q_2 p_1^2$$
$$D_4: \; F = \pm p_1^2 p_2 \pm p_2^3 + q_3 p_2^2$$

These continue through $n=5$ with further $A_k$, $D_k$, and $E_k$ types. The types are:
- $A_1$: nonsingular point
- $A_2$: fold singularity
- $A_3$: tuck (cusp) — semi-cubical cusp on the caustic
- $A_4$: swallowtail singularity
- $D_4$: three cuspidal edges tangent at one point (two variants, depending on whether the additional edges are real or imaginary)
