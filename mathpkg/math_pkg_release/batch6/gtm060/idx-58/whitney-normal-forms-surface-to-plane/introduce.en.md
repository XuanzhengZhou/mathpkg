Whitney's theorem classifies all stable singularities of smooth maps from a surface to a plane. After a small perturbation, every point falls into one of three types: regular points (local diffeomorphism), fold points (modelled on the projection of a sphere onto a plane, giving a smooth apparent contour), and cusp points (giving a semi-cubical parabola $27x^2 = 4y^3$ on the apparent contour). The normal forms $y_1=x_1^2, y_2=x_2$ (fold) and $y_1=x_1x_2-x_1^3, y_2=x_2$ (cusp/tuck) provide the local models. This theorem is foundational for singularity theory and serves as the template for the more refined classification of Lagrangian singularities.
