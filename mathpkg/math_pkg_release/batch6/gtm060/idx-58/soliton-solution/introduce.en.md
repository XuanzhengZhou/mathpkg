The single-soliton solution of the KdV equation is obtained by the traveling wave reduction $u = \varphi(x-ct)$, which transforms the nonlinear PDE into Newton's equation with a cubic potential $\varphi'' = 3\varphi^2 + c\varphi + d$. The soliton corresponds to a separatrix orbit in the phase plane — a homoclinic connection from the saddle at $\varphi = 0$ back to itself. This mechanical analogy reveals the soliton as a localized coherent structure whose stability is rooted in the integrability of the underlying infinite-dimensional Hamiltonian system. Remarkably, when two solitons collide, they emerge with unchanged shapes and velocities, exhibiting a purely elastic interaction characteristic of completely integrable systems.
