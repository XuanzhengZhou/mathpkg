Assume that the phase flow corresponding to the potential $U$ has an invariant Lagrangian manifold lying on the energy level $H = E$. Then the equation

$$\frac{1}{2} \Delta \psi = \lambda^2 (U(q) - E) \psi$$

has a series of eigenvalues $\lambda_N \rightarrow \infty$ with asymptotic form $\lambda_N = \mu_N + O(\mu_N^{-1})$ if, for every closed contour $\gamma$ on the Lagrangian manifold, we have the congruence

$$\frac{2 \mu_N}{\pi} \oint_{\gamma} p \, dq \equiv \operatorname{ind} \gamma \pmod{4}.$$

In the one-dimensional case, the Lagrangian manifold is a circle, its index is equal to $2$, and the formula above reduces to the quantization condition

$$\mu_N \oint_{\gamma} p \, dq = 2\pi\left(N + \frac{1}{2}\right).$$
