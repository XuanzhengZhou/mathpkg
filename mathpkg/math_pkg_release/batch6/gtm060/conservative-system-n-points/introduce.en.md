A system of $n$ points is conservative if the total force in configuration space is the negative gradient of a potential energy function. Equivalently, the work done by all forces between two configurations depends only on the endpoints. When this holds, the total energy $E = T + U$ is conserved. For pairwise central interactions, the potential energy decomposes as $U = \sum_{i>j} U_{ij}(|r_i - r_j|)$, reflecting the additive nature of interaction energies.
