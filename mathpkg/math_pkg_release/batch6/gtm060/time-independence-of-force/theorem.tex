Invariance with respect to time translations means that "the laws of nature remain constant," i.e., if $\mathbf{x} = \varphi(t)$ is a solution to Newton's equation $\ddot{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{x}, \dot{\mathbf{x}}, t)$, then for any $s \in \mathbb{R}$, $\mathbf{x} = \varphi(t + s)$ is also a solution.

From this it follows that the right-hand side of Newton's equation in an inertial coordinate system does not depend on the time:

$$\ddot{\mathbf{x}} = \boldsymbol{\Phi}(\mathbf{x}, \dot{\mathbf{x}}).$$
