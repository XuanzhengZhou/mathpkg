As a consequence of Galilean invariance under time translations, the right-hand side of Newton's equation cannot depend explicitly on time in an inertial frame. The equation reduces to $\ddot{\mathbf{x}} = \boldsymbol{\Phi}(\mathbf{x}, \dot{\mathbf{x}})$, meaning the laws of mechanics are autonomous — the future depends only on the current state, not on the absolute time.
