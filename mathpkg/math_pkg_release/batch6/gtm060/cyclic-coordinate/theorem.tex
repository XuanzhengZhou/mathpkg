\textbf{Definition.} If a coordinate $q_1$ does not enter into the hamiltonian function $H(p_1, \ldots, p_n; q_1, \ldots, q_n; t)$, i.e., $\partial H/\partial q_1 = 0$, then it is called cyclic.

\textbf{Corollary 2.} For a cyclic coordinate $q_1$, the corresponding generalized momentum is conserved: $p_1 = \text{const}$. After solving the reduced $2n-2$ dimensional system, the equation for $q_1$ reduces to $\dot{q}_1 = f(t)$ and is easily integrated.

\textbf{Corollary 3.} Every closed system with two degrees of freedom ($n = 2$) which has a cyclic coordinate is integrable.
