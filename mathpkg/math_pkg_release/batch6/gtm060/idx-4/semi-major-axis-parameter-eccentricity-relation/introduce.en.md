The semi-major axis $a$ of an ellipse can be expressed in terms of the parameter $p$ and eccentricity $e$ by the formula $a = p/(1 - e^2)$. This relation is obtained by adding the pericenter distance $r_{\min} = p/(1 + e)$ and the apocenter distance $r_{\max} = p/(1 - e)$, yielding $2a = p/(1 - e) + p/(1 + e) = 2p/(1 - e^2)$. In the context of the Kepler problem, combining this with $p = M^2/k$ and $e^2 = 1 + 2EM^2/k^2$ gives $a = k/(2|E|)$ for elliptic orbits.
