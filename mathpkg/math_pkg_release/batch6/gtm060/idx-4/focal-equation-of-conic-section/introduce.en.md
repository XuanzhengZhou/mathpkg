The focal equation of a conic section describes the orbit of a particle moving under an inverse-square central force in polar coordinates $(r, \varphi)$ with origin at one focus. It is obtained by integrating the equations of motion and expresses $r$ as a function of the polar angle $\varphi$. The shape of the orbit is determined by the eccentricity $e$: the orbit is an ellipse for $e < 1$, a parabola for $e = 1$, and a hyperbola for $e > 1$.
