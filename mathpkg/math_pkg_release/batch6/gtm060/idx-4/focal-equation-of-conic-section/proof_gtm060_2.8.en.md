Integrating the equation of motion for a central field with potential $U = -k/r$, we obtain

$$\varphi = \arccos \frac{\frac{M}{r} - \frac{k}{M}}{\sqrt{2E + \frac{k^2}{M^2}}}.$$

An arbitrary constant of integration is set to zero, which corresponds to choosing the origin of the angle $\varphi$ at the pericenter. Introducing the notation

$$\frac{M^2}{k} = p, \quad \sqrt{1 + \frac{2EM^2}{k^2}} = e,$$

we obtain $\varphi = \arccos ((p/r) - 1)/e$, which after rearrangement yields

$$r = \frac{p}{1 + e \cos \varphi}.$$

This is the focal equation of a conic section. The motion is bounded when $E < 0$, in which case $e < 1$ and the conic section is an ellipse.
