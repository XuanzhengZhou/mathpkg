The eccentricity $e$ of a conic section measures the deviation of the orbit from a perfect circle. In the context of central field motion, $e$ is defined in terms of the total energy $E$, angular momentum $M$, and gravitational constant $k$ as $e = \sqrt{1 + 2EM^2/k^2}$. The sign of the energy $E$ determines the type of conic section: negative energy yields an ellipse ($e < 1$), zero energy yields a parabola ($e = 1$), and positive energy yields a hyperbola ($e > 1$).
