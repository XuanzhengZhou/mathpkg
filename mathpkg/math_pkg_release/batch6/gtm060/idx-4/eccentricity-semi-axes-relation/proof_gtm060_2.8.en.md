By definition, the eccentricity is the ratio of the focal distance $c = ae$ to the semi-major axis $a$, so $e = c/a$. For an ellipse, the foci, center, and vertices satisfy the Pythagorean relation $a^2 = b^2 + c^2$, where $b$ is the semi-minor axis. Solving for $c$ gives $c = \sqrt{a^2 - b^2}$. Substituting into the definition:

$$e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{\sqrt{a^2 - b^2}}{a}.$$
