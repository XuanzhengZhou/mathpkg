Kepler's First Law states that each planet moves around the Sun in an elliptical orbit, with the Sun located at one focus of the ellipse. Kepler discovered this law empirically by analyzing Tycho Brahe's observations of Mars. From the perspective of classical mechanics, this law follows from Newton's law of universal gravitation $U = -k/r$, which implies that planetary motion in a central gravitational field yields conic section trajectories — ellipses when the total energy is negative.
