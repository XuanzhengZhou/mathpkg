The Hamilton-Jacobi equation is a nonlinear first-order partial differential equation that the action function $S(\mathbf{q}, t)$ of a mechanical system must satisfy. It arises from the relation $\partial S / \partial t = -H$ and $\mathbf{p} = \partial S / \partial \mathbf{q}$ established by the differential of the action function. This equation establishes a deep connection between the trajectories of mechanical systems ("rays") and the solutions of partial differential equations ("wave fronts"), forming the basis of the optical-mechanical analogy and Jacobi's method for integrating Hamilton's canonical equations.
