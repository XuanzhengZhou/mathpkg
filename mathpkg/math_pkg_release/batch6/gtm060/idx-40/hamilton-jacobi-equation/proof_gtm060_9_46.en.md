It is sufficient to notice that, by the previous theorem (differential of the action function),

$$\frac{\partial S}{\partial t} = -H(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}, t), \quad \mathbf{p} = \frac{\partial S}{\partial \mathbf{q}}.$$

Substituting $\mathbf{p} = \partial S / \partial \mathbf{q}$ into the expression for $\partial S / \partial t$ yields

$$\frac{\partial S}{\partial t} + H\left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial \mathbf{q}}, \mathbf{q}, t \right) = 0,$$

which is the Hamilton-Jacobi equation. $\square$
