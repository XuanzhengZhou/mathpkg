Galilean invariance under uniform motion (boosts) forces the equations of motion to depend only on relative velocities $\dot{\mathbf{x}}_j - \dot{\mathbf{x}}_k$ rather than absolute velocities. This is the mathematical expression of the fact that absolute uniform motion cannot be detected by mechanical experiments.
