From invariance under passage to a uniformly moving coordinate system (which does not change $\ddot{\mathbf{x}}_i$ or $\mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_k$, but adds to each $\dot{\mathbf{x}}_j$ a fixed vector $\mathbf{v}$) it follows that the right-hand side of Newton's equation in an inertial system of coordinates can depend only on the relative velocities:

$$\ddot{\mathbf{x}}_i = \mathbf{f}_i(\{\mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_k, \dot{\mathbf{x}}_j - \dot{\mathbf{x}}_k\}), \quad i, j, k = 1, \ldots, n.$$
