Kepler's first law states that planetary orbits are ellipses with the Sun at one focus. This is a direct mathematical consequence of Newton's inverse-square law of gravitation. Solving the orbit equation by quadrature for $U = -k/r$ yields the focal equation $r = p/(1 + e\cos\varphi)$, which represents a conic section. The type of conic (ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola) depends on the sign of the total energy. Kepler discovered this empirically from Tycho Brahe's observations; Newton showed it follows from his law of universal gravitation.
