\textbf{Kepler's First Law.} The planets describe ellipses, with the sun at one focus.

For the Newtonian potential $U = -k/r$, the orbit equation in polar coordinates is
$$r = \frac{p}{1 + e \cos \varphi},$$
where $p = M^2/k$ is the parameter and $e = \sqrt{1 + 2EM^2/k^2}$ is the eccentricity. This is the focal equation of a conic section:
\begin{itemize}
\item $E < 0$, $e < 1$: ellipse (bounded motion)
\item $E = 0$, $e = 1$: parabola
\item $E > 0$, $e > 1$: hyperbola
\end{itemize}
