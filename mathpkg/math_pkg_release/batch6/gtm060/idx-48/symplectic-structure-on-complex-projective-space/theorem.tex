The symplectic structure $\Omega$ on $\mathbb{C}P^n$ is defined as the imaginary part of the hermitian form on the tangent space, taken with coefficient $-1/\pi$:

$$\Omega = -\frac{1}{\pi} \operatorname{Im} \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle,$$

where $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ is the hermitian scalar product (3) on $T(\mathbb{C}P^n)$. The coefficient is chosen so that the integral of $\Omega$ over the projective line $\mathbb{C}P^1$ equals $1$, giving the usual orientation of the complex line.
