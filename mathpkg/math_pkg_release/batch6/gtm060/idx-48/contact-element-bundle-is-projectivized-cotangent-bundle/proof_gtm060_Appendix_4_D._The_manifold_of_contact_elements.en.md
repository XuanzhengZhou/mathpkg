A contact element is given by a $1$-form on the tangent space for which this element is a zero level set. This form is not zero and is determined up to multiplication by a nonzero number. But a form on the tangent space is a vector of the cotangent space. Therefore, a nonzero form on the tangent space, defined up to multiplication by a nonzero number, is precisely a nonzero vector of the cotangent space defined up to scalar multiplication, i.e., a point of the projectivized cotangent space.
