This theorem states that the integral of a closed differential form over a cycle depends only on the homology class of the cycle. If two cycles $a$ and $b$ differ by a boundary ($a - b = \partial c$), then by Stokes' theorem $\int_a \omega - \int_b \omega = \int_{\partial c} \omega = \int_c d\omega = 0$, since $\omega$ is closed. This invariance is the essential reason why integration induces a well-defined pairing between de Rham cohomology classes and homology classes, and is the foundation of de Rham's theorem.
