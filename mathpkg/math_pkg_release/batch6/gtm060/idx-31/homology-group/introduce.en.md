The $k$-th homology group $H_k(M)$ of a manifold $M$ is the quotient of the group of $k$-cycles by the subgroup of $k$-boundaries. It is the fundamental algebraic invariant in singular homology theory, capturing the "$k$-dimensional holes" in $M$. Elements of $H_k(M)$ are homology classes of cycles. By de Rham's theorem, the rank of $H_k(M)$ equals the $k$-th Betti number, and integration provides a nondegenerate pairing $H^k(M, \mathbb{R}) \times H_k(M) \to \mathbb{R}$.
