A symplectic manifold $(M^{2n}, \omega^2)$ is an even-dimensional smooth manifold equipped with a closed, nondegenerate 2-form. These manifolds are the natural geometric arena for Hamiltonian dynamics: on a symplectic manifold there is a natural isomorphism between vector fields and 1-forms, under which Hamiltonian vector fields correspond to exact 1-forms $dH$. The phase flow of a Hamiltonian vector field preserves the symplectic structure. The cotangent bundle of any manifold carries a canonical symplectic structure, making it the standard model for phase space in mechanics.
