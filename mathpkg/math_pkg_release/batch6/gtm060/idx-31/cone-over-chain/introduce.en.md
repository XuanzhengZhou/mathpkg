The cone over a chain $c$ with vertex at the origin $0$ is a fundamental geometric construction in algebraic topology. Given a $k$-chain $c$, its cone $pc$ is the $(k+1)$-chain formed by taking the union of all line segments from the origin to points of $c$. The resulting operator $p$ is a chain homotopy between the identity and the zero map, satisfying $\partial p + p \partial = \text{id}$. This identity is the algebraic foundation for the proof of Poincaré's lemma.
