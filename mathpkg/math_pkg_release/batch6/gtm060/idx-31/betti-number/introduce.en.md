The $k$-th Betti number $\beta_k(M)$ is a fundamental topological invariant of a manifold $M$, defined as the dimension of the $k$-th de Rham cohomology group $H^k(M, \mathbb{R})$. Intuitively, $\beta_0$ counts connected components, $\beta_1$ counts independent non-bounding 1-cycles (loops), and $\beta_2$ counts independent non-bounding 2-cycles (voids). For the circle $S^1$, $\beta_1 = 1$; for the torus $T^2$, $\beta_1 = 2$. By de Rham's theorem, the Betti numbers computed from differential forms coincide with those from singular homology.
