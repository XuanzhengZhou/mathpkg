De Rham's theorem establishes the fundamental relationship between differential-form cohomology and singular homology on a smooth manifold $M$. It asserts that the de Rham cohomology groups $H^k(M, \mathbb{R})$ and the singular cohomology groups (with real coefficients) are isomorphic, and in particular that the $k$-th Betti number equals both $\dim H^k(M, \mathbb{R})$ and the rank of $H_k(M)$. The isomorphism is realized by the integration pairing $([\omega], [c]) \mapsto \int_c \omega$, demonstrating the deep geometric connection between analysis (differential forms) and topology (cycles).
