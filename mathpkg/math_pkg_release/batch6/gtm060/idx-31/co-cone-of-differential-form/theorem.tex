Let $\omega^k$ be a differential $k$-form on $\mathbb{R}^n$. The \textit{co-cone} over $\omega^k$ is the $(k-1)$-form $p\omega^k$ defined by duality with the cone over chains: for any chain $c_{k-1}$,
$$\int_{c_{k-1}} p\omega^k = \int_{p c_{k-1}} \omega^k.$$
Explicitly, its value on tangent vectors $\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_{k-1}$ at $x$ is
$$(p\omega)_{\mathbf{x}}(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_{k-1}) = \int_0^1 \omega_{t\mathbf{x}}(\mathbf{x}, t\xi_1, \ldots, t\xi_{k-1})\, dt.$$
