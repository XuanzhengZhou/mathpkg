The co-cone operator $p$ on differential forms is the dual of the geometric cone construction on chains. It maps a $k$-form $\omega^k$ to a $(k-1)$-form $p\omega^k$ by integration along rays from the origin. Together with the exterior derivative $d$, it satisfies the algebraic identity $d \circ p + p \circ d = \text{id}$, which provides an explicit homotopy between the identity map and the zero map on the de Rham complex, and hence a proof of Poincaré's lemma.
