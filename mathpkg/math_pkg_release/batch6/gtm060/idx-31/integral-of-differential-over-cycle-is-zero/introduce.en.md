This theorem is an immediate consequence of Stokes' formula $\int_{c} d\omega = \int_{\partial c} \omega$. When $c$ is a cycle ($\partial c = 0$), the right-hand side vanishes, yielding $\int_c d\omega = 0$. It shows that exact forms (differentials of other forms) always integrate to zero over cycles. Together with the converse direction, this leads to the definition of cohomology: closed forms modulo exact forms.
