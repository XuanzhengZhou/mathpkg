In a system with one degree of freedom, the kinetic energy is $T = \frac{1}{2}\dot{x}^2$, a positive quadratic form in the velocity. More generally for $n$ degrees of freedom with unit masses, $T = \frac{1}{2}\sum \dot{x}_i^2$. The kinetic energy measures the energy of motion.
