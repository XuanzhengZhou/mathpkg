A system performing small oscillations is called *more rigid* than another if both share the same kinetic energy but the former has pointwise larger potential energy. This definition allows the comparison of two oscillatory systems by directly comparing their potential energy quadratic forms. It plays a key role in understanding how characteristic frequencies change when the stiffness of couplings is increased.
