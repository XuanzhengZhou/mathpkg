By Lemma 2 this theorem is equivalent to the geometric proposition (Theorem 4) on the semi-axes of a cross-section of an ellipsoid. Theorem 4 asserts that if an $n$-dimensional ellipsoid $E$ with semi-axes $a_1 \geq a_2 \geq \cdots \geq a_n$ is cut by a hyperplane through its center, the resulting $(n-1)$-dimensional ellipsoid $E'$ has semi-axes $a'_1 \geq a'_2 \geq \cdots \geq a'_{n-1}$ satisfying
$$a_1 \geq a'_1 \geq a_2 \geq a'_2 \geq \cdots \geq a_{n-1} \geq a'_{n-1} \geq a_n.$$

Since the characteristic frequencies are the reciprocals of the semi-axes (after normalizing the kinetic energy to the Euclidean inner product), taking reciprocals reverses the inequalities, giving the desired interlacing property for frequencies. $\square$
