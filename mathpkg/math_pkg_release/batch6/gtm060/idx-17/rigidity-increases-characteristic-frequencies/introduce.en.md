This theorem, a form of the Rayleigh-Courant-Fisher theorem, asserts that making a system stiffer can only raise its characteristic oscillation frequencies. The proof reduces to a geometric statement about nested ellipsoids: the semi-axes of an inner ellipsoid are componentwise bounded above by those of the containing ellipsoid. The result follows by identifying characteristic frequencies with the reciprocals of the semi-axes of the potential energy ellipsoid.
