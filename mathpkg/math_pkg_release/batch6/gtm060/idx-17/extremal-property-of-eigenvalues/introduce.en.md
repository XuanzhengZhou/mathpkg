This theorem gives a variational (minimax) characterization of the semi-axes of an ellipsoid: the $k$-th largest semi-axis equals the maximum, over all $k$-dimensional subspaces, of the minimum norm on the intersection of the subspace with the ellipsoid. This is the geometric analogue of the Courant-Fischer minimax principle for eigenvalues of a symmetric matrix. It is the key tool used to prove all the comparison and separation theorems of this section.
