Let $\mathbb{R}^{n-1} \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be an $(n-1)$-dimensional subspace in $\mathbb{R}^n$. Consider the system with $n-1$ degrees of freedom ($q \in \mathbb{R}^{n-1}$) whose kinetic and potential energies are the restrictions of $T = \frac{1}{2}(\dot{q}, \dot{q})$ and $U = \frac{1}{2}(Bq, q)$ to $\mathbb{R}^{n-1}$. This system is said to be obtained from the original by \text{imposition of a linear constraint}.
