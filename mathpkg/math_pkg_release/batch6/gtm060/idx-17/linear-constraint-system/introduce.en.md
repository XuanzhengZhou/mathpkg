A system with one fewer degree of freedom can be obtained from an $n$-degree-of-freedom oscillatory system by restricting the kinetic and potential energy quadratic forms to an $(n-1)$-dimensional linear subspace. This construction is called *imposition of a linear constraint*. It provides the setting for studying how characteristic frequencies behave when degrees of freedom are removed, leading to the interlacing (separation) theorem.
