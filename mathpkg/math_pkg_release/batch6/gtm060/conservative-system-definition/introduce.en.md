A conservative mechanical system is one where all forces can be derived from a single scalar potential energy function $U$. The force on each particle is the negative gradient of $U$ with respect to that particle's coordinates. This formulation unifies a vast range of mechanical systems -- from falling stones and springs to planetary motion and electrical oscillations -- under a common mathematical framework. The potential energy encodes all the interactions within the system.
