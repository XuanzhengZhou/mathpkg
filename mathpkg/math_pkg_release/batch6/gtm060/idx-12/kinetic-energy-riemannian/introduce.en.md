On a Riemannian manifold $M$, the kinetic energy of a mechanical system is defined at each tangent space $TM_x$ as half the squared norm of the velocity vector $v$ under the Riemannian metric. This definition generalizes the classical kinetic energy $\frac{1}{2} m |\dot{\mathbf{r}}|^2$ to curved configuration spaces, where the metric encodes the mass distribution and geometric constraints of the system.
