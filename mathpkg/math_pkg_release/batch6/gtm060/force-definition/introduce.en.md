Force, in Newton's formulation, is defined as the product of mass and acceleration: $F = m\ddot{x}$. This definition unifies the concepts of inertia (mass) and change of motion (acceleration). A force of 1 newton accelerates a 1 kg mass at 1 m/s^2. At the Earth's surface, gravity exerts approximately 9.8 newtons on each kilogram of mass.
