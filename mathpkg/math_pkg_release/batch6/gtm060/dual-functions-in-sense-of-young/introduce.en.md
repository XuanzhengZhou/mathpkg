Two convex functions are dual in the sense of Young if each is the Legendre transform of the other. This duality is symmetric (by involutivity) and satisfies Young's inequality. Examples include $x^2/2 \leftrightarrow p^2/2$ and $x^\alpha/\alpha \leftrightarrow p^\beta/\beta$ where $1/\alpha + 1/\beta = 1$. In mechanics, the Lagrangian $L(\dot{q})$ and Hamiltonian $H(p)$ are Young duals with respect to the velocity/momentum variables.
