Lissajous figures are the orbits in the $(x_1, x_2)$ plane of a system consisting of two independent harmonic oscillators with frequencies $1$ and $\omega$.
The shape of a Lissajous figure depends sensitively on the frequency ratio $\omega$ and the phase difference $\varphi_2 - \varphi_1$.
When $\omega = 1$, the figures are ellipses; when $\omega \approx 1$, the figure slowly deforms through all possible ellipses; when $\omega = 2$, the figure can become a doubly-traversed arc.
Lissajous figures are commonly visualized on oscilloscopes displaying independent harmonic oscillations on horizontal and vertical axes.
