The equation $\ddot{x} = f(x)$ is equivalent to the system
$$\dot{x} = y, \quad \dot{y} = f(x).$$
The plane with coordinates $x$ and $y$ is called the \textit{phase plane} of the equation.
The right-hand side of the system determines a vector field on the phase plane, called the \textit{phase velocity vector field}.
