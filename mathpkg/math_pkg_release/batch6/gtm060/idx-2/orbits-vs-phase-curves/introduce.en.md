Orbits are the projections of four-dimensional phase curves onto the two-dimensional configuration plane $(x_1, x_2)$.
Unlike phase curves, which never intersect in phase space (by the uniqueness theorem for ODEs), orbits in the configuration plane can and do intersect.
This distinction highlights the importance of working in the full phase space rather than just the configuration space.
