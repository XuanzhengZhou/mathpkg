The points of the phase plane are called \textit{phase points}.
A solution of the system $\dot{x} = y,\; \dot{y} = f(x)$ is a motion $\varphi: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^2$ of a phase point in the phase plane, such that the velocity of the moving point at each moment equals the phase velocity vector at the location of the phase point at that moment.
