The potential energy $U(x)$ of a one-degree-of-freedom system is defined as the negative integral of the force function $f(x)$.
This definition recovers the familiar relation $f = -dU/dx$, so that forces drive the system toward lower potential energy.
The constant of integration $x_0$ corresponds to an arbitrary additive constant in the potential energy, which does not affect the equations of motion.
Given the potential energy, the force law and thus the entire dynamics are determined.
