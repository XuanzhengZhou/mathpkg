A system with two degrees of freedom is a mechanical system whose configuration is described by a point $x = (x_1, x_2)$ in the Euclidean plane $E^2$.
The equations of motion are $\ddot{x} = f(x)$ where $f$ is a vector field on the plane.
Unlike the one-degree-of-freedom case, analyzing general potential systems with two degrees of freedom is beyond the capability of modern science, and only the simplest examples can be treated.
