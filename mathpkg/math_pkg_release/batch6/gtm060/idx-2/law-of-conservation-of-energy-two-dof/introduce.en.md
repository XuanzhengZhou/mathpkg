The law of conservation of energy extends to conservative systems with two degrees of freedom: the total energy $E = T + U$ is constant along every trajectory.
The proof uses the chain rule and the fact that $f = -\partial U / \partial x$, giving $dE/dt = (\dot{x}, \ddot{x} + \partial U/\partial x) = 0$.
This conservation law constrains the motion to a three-dimensional energy level hypersurface in the four-dimensional phase space.
