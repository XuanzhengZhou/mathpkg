The total energy $E(x, \dot{x}) = T + U$ of a one-degree-of-freedom system is the sum of its kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2}\dot{x}^2$ and its potential energy $U(x)$.
It is a function on the phase plane that assigns a real number to each state $(x, \dot{x})$.
The conservation of total energy is the most fundamental property of such systems, implying that motions are constrained to energy level sets.
