Every central field is conservative, and its potential energy depends only on the distance to the center of the field, $U = U(r)$.

Proof. We may set $\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}) = \Phi(r)\mathbf{e}_r$, where $\mathbf{r}$ is the radius vector, $r$ its length and $\mathbf{e}_r = \mathbf{r}/|\mathbf{r}|$ its direction. Then

$$\int_{M_1}^{M_2} (\mathbf{F}, d\mathbf{S}) = \int_{r(M_1)}^{r(M_2)} \Phi(r)dr,$$

and this integral is obviously independent of the path.
