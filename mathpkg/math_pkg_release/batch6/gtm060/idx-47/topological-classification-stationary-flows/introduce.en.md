Theorem 11 provides a topological classification of stationary flows of an ideal incompressible fluid on a three-dimensional Riemannian manifold. When the velocity and vorticity fields commute (as they do for stationary flows), the flow lines lie on Bernoulli surfaces. The theorem states that closed Bernoulli surfaces are necessarily tori, while non-closed ones consist of annuli. This result relies on the fact that commuting velocity and vorticity fields generate an $\mathbb{R}^2$ action, and uses the topological constraints of Liouville's theorem. Arnold notes that the analyticity assumption is not essential, but the non-collinearity of velocity and vorticity is crucial; computer experiments by Henon show more complicated behavior when the fields are collinear.
