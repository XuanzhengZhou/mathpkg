This proposition establishes the fundamental relationship between isovorticial velocity fields and their vorticity. It states that a volume-preserving diffeomorphism relating two isovorticial fields necessarily pushes forward the curl of one field to the curl of the other. In the simply connected case, the converse also holds, making isovorticity equivalent to the equivalence of vorticity fields under volume-preserving diffeomorphisms. This connection reveals why the problem of classifying coadjoint orbits in three dimensions is intimately tied to the difficult problem of classifying divergence-free vector fields up to volume-preserving diffeomorphisms.
