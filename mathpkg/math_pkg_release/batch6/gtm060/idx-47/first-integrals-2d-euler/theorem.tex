Euler's equations of motion of a two-dimensional ideal fluid
$$\frac{\partial v}{\partial t} + v \nabla v = -\operatorname{grad} p, \quad \operatorname{div} v = 0,$$
have an infinite collection of first integrals. For each integer $k \geq 1$, the integral of the $k$-th power of the vorticity is a first integral:
$$I_k = \iint_D \left( \frac{\partial v_2}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial v_1}{\partial y} \right)^k dx \wedge dy = \iint_D r^k \, dS.$$

More generally, for any function $\Phi$, the integral
$$\int_D \Phi(r) \, dS$$
is a first integral of the two-dimensional Euler equations.
