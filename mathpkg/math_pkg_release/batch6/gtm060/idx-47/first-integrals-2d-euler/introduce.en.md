The two-dimensional Euler equations for an ideal incompressible fluid possess an infinite family of first integrals, given by the moments of the scalar vorticity $r$. This remarkable fact follows from the simple structure of coadjoint orbits in two dimensions: since isovorticity is equivalent to having the same vorticity distribution function, any functional of the vorticity that is constant on distribution-preserving rearrangements is conserved. In particular, the integrals $I_k = \int r^k dS$ of all powers of the vorticity are constants of motion, yielding infinitely many conservation laws.
