A stationary flow of an ideal fluid is distinguished from all flows isovorticial to it by the fact that it is a conditional extremum (or critical point) of the kinetic energy. Formally, if $v_0$ is a stationary flow, then $v_0$ is a critical point of the functional
$$H(v) = \frac{1}{2} \int_D \langle v, v \rangle \, d\mu$$
restricted to the manifold of divergence-free vector fields isovorticial to $v_0$.
