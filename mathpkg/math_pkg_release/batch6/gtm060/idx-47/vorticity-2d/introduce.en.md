In two-dimensional hydrodynamics, the vorticity simplifies to a scalar function $r$ defined on the flow region, in contrast to the three-dimensional case where it is a vector field. The scalar vorticity is characterized by the property that its integral over any oriented region equals the circulation of the velocity field around the boundary of that region, via Stokes' theorem. This scalar nature of 2D vorticity makes the analysis of two-dimensional ideal fluids significantly more tractable than the three-dimensional case.
