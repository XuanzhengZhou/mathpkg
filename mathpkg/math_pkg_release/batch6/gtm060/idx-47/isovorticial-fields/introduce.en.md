Isovorticial fields are a central concept in the geometric theory of ideal hydrodynamics, defining the equivalence classes that form the coadjoint orbits of the group of volume-preserving diffeomorphisms. The classification of these orbits differs dramatically between two and three dimensions: in two dimensions the orbits are relatively simple and admit infinitely many first integrals, while in three dimensions the orbit structure is far more complex and includes the problem of classifying divergence-free vector fields up to volume-preserving diffeomorphism.
