This proposition characterizes isovorticity in two-dimensional simply connected flows in terms of the distribution function of the scalar vorticity. Two velocity fields are isovorticial precisely when their vorticity functions have the same measure-theoretic distribution. This implies that all moments of the vorticity are invariants of coadjoint orbits, which in turn yields an infinite family of first integrals for the two-dimensional Euler equations -- a dramatic simplification compared to the three-dimensional case.
