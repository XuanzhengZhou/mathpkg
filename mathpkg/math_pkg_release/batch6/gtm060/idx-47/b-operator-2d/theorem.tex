In the two-dimensional case, the vector field $B(c, a)$ defined by $\langle [a, b], c \rangle \equiv \langle B(c, a), b \rangle$ is given by the formula
$$B(c, a) = -(\Delta \psi_c) \operatorname{grad} \psi_a + \operatorname{grad} \alpha,$$
where $\psi_a$ and $\psi_c$ are the stream functions of the vector fields $a$ and $c$, $\Delta = \operatorname{div} \operatorname{grad}$ is the Laplacian, and $\alpha$ is a single-valued function uniquely determined (up to an additive constant) by the condition that $B \in \mathfrak{g}$ (i.e., $\operatorname{div} B = 0$ and $B$ is tangent to the boundary of $D$).
