In two-dimensional hydrodynamics, the stream function $\psi$ provides a powerful scalar representation of the velocity field of an incompressible fluid. Since the metric and orientation induce a symplectic structure on the flow region, a divergence-free velocity field is necessarily Hamiltonian, and its stream function is the Hamiltonian function. The velocity field is obtained by applying the clockwise $90^\circ$ rotation operator to the gradient of the stream function. If the flow region is not simply connected, the stream function may be multi-valued.
