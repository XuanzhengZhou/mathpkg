We say that $M$ is an embedded $k$-dimensional submanifold of euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^n$ if in a neighborhood $U$ of every point $x \in M$ there are $n - k$ functions

$$f_1: U \to \mathbb{R},\ f_2: U \to \mathbb{R},\ \ldots,\ f_{n-k}: U \to \mathbb{R}$$

such that the intersection of $U$ with $M$ is given by the equations

$$f_1 = 0,\ \ldots,\ f_{n-k} = 0,$$

and the vectors $\operatorname{grad} f_1, \ldots, \operatorname{grad} f_{n-k}$ at $x$ are linearly independent.
