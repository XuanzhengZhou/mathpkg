A system of $n$ point masses with $3n - m$ ideal holonomic constraints is defined by restricting the configuration space to an $m$-dimensional surface $\gamma$, parameterized by generalized coordinates $\mathbf{q} = (q_1, \ldots, q_m)$. The motion on this constrained configuration space is governed by the standard Euler-Lagrange equations, with the Lagrangian expressed in terms of the constrained coordinates. Holonomic constraints can also be understood as the limiting case of a system with a very large potential energy that confines motion to the constraint surface.
