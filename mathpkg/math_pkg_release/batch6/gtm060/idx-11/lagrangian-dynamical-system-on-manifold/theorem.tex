Let $M$ be a differentiable manifold, $TM$ its tangent bundle, and $L: TM \to \mathbb{R}$ a differentiable function. A map $\gamma: \mathbb{R} \to M$ is called a motion in the lagrangian system with configuration manifold $M$ and lagrangian function $L$ if $\gamma$ is an extremal of the functional

$$\Phi(\gamma) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} L(\dot{\gamma})\, dt,$$

where $\dot{\gamma}$ is the velocity vector $\dot{\gamma}(t) \in TM_{\gamma(t)}$.

In local coordinates $q = (q_1, \ldots, q_n)$, the Lagrangian $L: TM \to \mathbb{R}$ is written as a function $L(q, \dot{q})$ of $2n$ coordinates, and the evolution satisfies Lagrange's equations:

$$\frac{d}{dt} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} = \frac{\partial L}{\partial q}.$$
