A Lagrangian dynamical system on a manifold $M$ is defined by a Lagrangian function $L: TM \to \mathbb{R}$ on the tangent bundle. Motions are curves $\gamma: \mathbb{R} \to M$ that extremize the action functional $\Phi(\gamma) = \int L(\dot{\gamma})\, dt$. This geometric formulation unifies both unconstrained systems (where $M$ is a region in $\mathbb{R}^n$) and constrained systems (where $M$ is the constraint surface). In local coordinates, the extremality condition reduces to the standard Euler-Lagrange equations.
