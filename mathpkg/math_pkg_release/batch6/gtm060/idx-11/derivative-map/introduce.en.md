The derivative (or pushforward) $f_{*x}: TM_x \to TN_{f(x)}$ of a differentiable map $f: M \to N$ between manifolds is a linear map between tangent spaces. It is defined by taking a tangent vector $v \in TM_x$ represented by a curve $\varphi(t)$ through $x$, and mapping it to the velocity vector of the image curve $f \circ \varphi$ at $f(x)$. In local coordinates, the derivative is given by the Jacobian matrix of the coordinate representation of $f$.
