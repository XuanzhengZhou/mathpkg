A tangent vector to a manifold $M$ at the point $x$ is an equivalence class of curves $\varphi(t)$, with $\varphi(0) = x$.

Two curves $x = \varphi(t)$ and $x = \psi(t)$ are called equivalent if $\varphi(0) = \psi(0) = x$ and

$$\lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\varphi(t) - \psi(t)}{t} = 0$$

in some chart. This tangent relationship is true in any chart.

The operations of multiplication of a tangent vector by a number and addition of tangent vectors are defined. The set of tangent vectors to $M$ at $x$ forms a vector space $TM_x$, called the tangent space to $M$ at $x$.

For embedded manifolds, $TM_x$ is the orthogonal complement to $\{\operatorname{grad} f_1, \ldots, \operatorname{grad} f_{n-k}\}$, and tangent vectors can also be defined directly as velocity vectors of curves in $M$:

$$\dot{x} = \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{\varphi(t) - \varphi(0)}{t}.$$
