The union of the tangent spaces to $M$ at the various points,

$$TM = \bigcup_{x \in M} TM_x,$$

has a natural differentiable manifold structure, the dimension of which is twice the dimension of $M$.

This manifold is called the tangent bundle of $M$ and is denoted by $TM$. A point of $TM$ is a vector $\xi$, tangent to $M$ at some point $x$. Local coordinates on $TM$ are constructed as follows. Let $q_1, \ldots, q_n$ be local coordinates on $M$, and $\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_n$ components of a tangent vector in this coordinate system. Then the $2n$ numbers $(q_1, \ldots, q_n, \xi_1, \ldots, \xi_n)$ give local coordinates on $TM$.
