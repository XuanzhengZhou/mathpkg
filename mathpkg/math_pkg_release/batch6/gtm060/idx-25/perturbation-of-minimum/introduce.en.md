This lemma describes how the location and curvature of a minimum of a smooth function change when a small perturbation $\varepsilon h(x)$ is added. It states that if $f$ has a non-degenerate minimum at $x=0$ with second derivative $A > 0$, then for sufficiently small $\varepsilon$, the perturbed function $f_\varepsilon(x) = f(x) + \varepsilon h(x)$ has a minimum at $x_\varepsilon = -C\varepsilon/A + O(\varepsilon^2)$, where $C = h'(0)$. The second derivative at the new minimum remains $A + O(\varepsilon)$, meaning the curvature is only slightly affected. The proof follows from expanding $f'_\varepsilon(x)$ and applying the implicit function theorem.
