The rate of change of total angular momentum equals the total external torque. Internal forces contribute zero net torque because they are central and obey Newton's third law: $[r_i, F_{ij}] + [r_j, F_{ji}] = [(r_i - r_j), F_{ij}] = 0$. For closed systems, angular momentum is conserved -- a fundamental consequence of the isotropy of space.
