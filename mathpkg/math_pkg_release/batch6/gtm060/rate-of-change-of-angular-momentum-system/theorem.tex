\textbf{Theorem.} The rate of change of the total angular momentum of a system is equal to the sum of the moments of the external forces acting on the points of the system:
$$\frac{dM}{dt} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} [r_i, F'_i] = N.$$

\textbf{Corollary 1 (Law of conservation of angular momentum).} If the system is closed, then $M = \text{const}$.

\textbf{Corollary 2.} If the moment of the external forces relative to the $z$ axis is zero, then $M_z$ is constant.
