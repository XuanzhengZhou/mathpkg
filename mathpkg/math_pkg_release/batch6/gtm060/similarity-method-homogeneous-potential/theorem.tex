Suppose that the potential energy of a central field is a homogeneous function of degree $\nu$:
$$U(\alpha r) = \alpha^\nu U(r) \quad \text{for any } \alpha > 0.$$

Then if a curve $\gamma$ is the orbit of a motion, the homothetic curve $\alpha \gamma$ is also an orbit (under appropriate initial conditions). The ratio of the circulation times along these orbits is determined by the degree of homogeneity.

\textbf{Consequences:}
\begin{itemize}
\item $\nu = 2$ (harmonic oscillator): isochronicity of oscillations (period independent of amplitude)
\item $\nu = -1$ (Newtonian/Coulomb): Kepler's third law $T^2 \propto a^3$
\end{itemize}
