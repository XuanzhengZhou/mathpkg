The principal axes $\mathbf{e}_1, \mathbf{e}_2, \mathbf{e}_3$ of a rigid body at a point $O$ are the three mutually orthogonal eigenvectors of the symmetric inertia operator $A$. In the principal axis basis, the relationship between angular velocity and angular momentum diagonalizes: $M_i = I_i \Omega_i$, and the kinetic energy takes the simple diagonal form $T = \frac{1}{2}(I_1\Omega_1^2 + I_2\Omega_2^2 + I_3\Omega_3^2)$. When the eigenvalues $I_1, I_2, I_3$ are distinct, the principal axes are unique; otherwise there is a continuous family of choices.
