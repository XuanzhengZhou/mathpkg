A corollary of the uniform linear motion of the center of mass: in an inertial coordinate system where the center of mass is stationary, the rigid body rotates about its center of mass exactly as if that point were fixed. This reduction is fundamental -- the six-degree-of-freedom problem of a free rigid body separates into uniform translation of the center of mass (3 degrees) and rotation about the center of mass as a fixed point (3 degrees).
