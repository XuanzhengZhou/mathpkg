The system of a free rigid body admits all translational displacements, which do not change the Lagrangian function. By Noether's theorem, there exist three first integrals corresponding to translational invariance: the three components of the vector of momentum. Therefore, the total momentum is conserved. Since the total momentum equals the total mass times the velocity of the center of mass, the center of mass moves with constant velocity. Hence its motion is uniform and linear:

$$\frac{d}{dt}(m \dot{x}_c) = 0 \implies \dot{x}_c = \text{const}.$$
