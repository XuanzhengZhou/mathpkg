A fundamental result in rigid body dynamics: under free motion (no external forces), the center of mass of a rigid body moves uniformly and linearly. This follows from Noether's theorem, which guarantees conservation of the total linear momentum. The translational symmetry of the system implies the existence of three first integrals: the three components of the momentum vector. Consequently, the motion separates into uniform translation of the center of mass and rotation about the center of mass as if it were fixed at a stationary point.
