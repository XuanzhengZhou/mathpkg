This corollary identifies the eigenvalues $I_i$ of the inertia operator $A$ as the physical moments of inertia of the body about the corresponding principal axes $\mathbf{e}_i$. By comparing the two expressions for kinetic energy -- the general quadratic form $T = \frac{1}{2} \sum_i I_i \Omega_i^2$ and the axis-specific form $T = \frac{1}{2} I_{\mathbf{e}_i} \Omega^2$ when $\Omega$ is directed along $\mathbf{e}_i$ -- one obtains $I_i = I_{\mathbf{e}_i}$. Thus the spectral decomposition of $A$ has a direct mechanical interpretation.
