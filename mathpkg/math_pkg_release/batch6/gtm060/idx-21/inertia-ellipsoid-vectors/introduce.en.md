This theorem provides the geometric construction of the inertia ellipsoid. Consider the map $\mathbf{e} \mapsto \mathbf{e}/\sqrt{I_e}$ sending each unit direction $\mathbf{e}$ to a vector whose length is $1/\sqrt{I_e}$. The resulting locus is a level set $\{\Omega : (A\Omega, \Omega) = 1\}$ of the quadratic form defined by the inertia operator, and therefore is an ellipsoid. The directions of its principal axes coincide with the principal axes of inertia, and the semi-axis lengths are $1/\sqrt{I_i}$.
