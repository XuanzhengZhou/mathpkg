This theorem generalizes the familiar formula $T = \frac{1}{2} I \omega^2$ for rotation of a rigid body about a fixed axis. The scalar moment of inertia $I_e$ about the axis $\mathbf{e}$ is defined as the sum $\sum_i m_i r_i^2$ where $r_i$ is the perpendicular distance from the $i$-th mass point to the rotation axis. The proof follows directly from the definition of kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2} \sum_i m_i v_i^2$ and the relation $\mathbf{v}_i = [\Omega\mathbf{e}, \mathbf{q}_i]$, giving $v_i = \Omega r_i$.
