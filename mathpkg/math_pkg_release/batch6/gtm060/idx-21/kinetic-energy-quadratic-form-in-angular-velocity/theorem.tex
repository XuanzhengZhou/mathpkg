The kinetic energy of a point of a body is a quadratic form with respect to the vector of angular velocity $\Omega$:

$$T = \frac{1}{2}(A\Omega, \Omega) = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{M}, \Omega).$$

For a body consisting of many points $\mathbf{Q}_i$ with masses $m_i$:

$$T = \sum_i T_i = \sum_i \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{M}_i, \Omega) = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{M}, \Omega) = \frac{1}{2}(A\Omega, \Omega),$$

where $A = \sum_i A_i$ is the total inertia operator.
