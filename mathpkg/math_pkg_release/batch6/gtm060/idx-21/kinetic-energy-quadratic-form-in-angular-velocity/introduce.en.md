A direct consequence of the symmetry of the inertia operator: substituting the angular velocity vector $\Omega$ for $\mathbf{X} = \mathbf{Y}$ in the bilinear form $(A\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y})$, and noting that $[\Omega, \mathbf{Q}]^2 = \mathbf{V}^2 = \mathbf{v}^2$, the kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2} m v^2$ simplifies to $T = \frac{1}{2}(A\Omega, \Omega) = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{M}, \Omega)$. For an extended body, the additivity of both kinetic energy and angular momentum yields the same expression for the total kinetic energy, with the total inertia operator $A$ being the sum of the point inertia operators $A_i$.
