For a rigid body rotating about a fixed point $O$ in the absence of external forces, Noether's theorem provides four independent first integrals: the three components of angular momentum $M_x, M_y, M_z$ (from rotational invariance) and the total energy $E = T$ (from time-translation invariance). These four conserved quantities define a two-dimensional invariant submanifold $V_c \subset T\mathrm{SO}(3)$ in the six-dimensional phase space. Topological arguments show $V_c$ is a two-dimensional torus, implying the motion is conditionally periodic with two frequencies $\omega_1, \omega_2$.
