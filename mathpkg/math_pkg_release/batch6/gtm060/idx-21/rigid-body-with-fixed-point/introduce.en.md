A rigid body with a fixed point $O$ is defined as a system of point masses whose positions are constrained so that one designated point $x_1$ coincides with $O$, in addition to the usual rigidity constraints that preserve all pairwise distances. The configuration manifold of such a system is the three-dimensional rotation group $\mathrm{SO}(3)$, reflecting the fact that the only allowed motions are rotations about the fixed point. This reduces the degrees of freedom from the six of a free rigid body to three.
