For pairwise central forces (forces along the line connecting two particles, with magnitude depending only on separation), a potential energy can be constructed by integrating the force magnitude. The total interaction energy is the sum over all unordered pairs. This additive structure is fundamental to gravitational, electrostatic, and molecular mechanics, where the $N$-body potential decomposes into two-body terms.
