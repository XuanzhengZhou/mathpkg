Invariance with respect to translations in three-dimensional space means that space is homogeneous. If $\mathbf{x}_i = \varphi_i(t)$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$) is a motion of a system of $n$ points satisfying Newton's equation, then for any $\mathbf{r} \in \mathbb{R}^3$ the motion $\varphi_i(t) + \mathbf{r}$ also satisfies the equation.

From this it follows that the right-hand side of Newton's equation in the inertial coordinate system can depend only on the relative coordinates $\mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_k$.
