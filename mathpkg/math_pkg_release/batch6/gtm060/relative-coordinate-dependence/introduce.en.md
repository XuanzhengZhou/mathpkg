The homogeneity of space — invariance under spatial translations — implies that the force on a system of particles can only depend on the relative positions $\mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_k$ of the particles, not on their absolute positions. This is a direct consequence of Galileo's principle of relativity.
