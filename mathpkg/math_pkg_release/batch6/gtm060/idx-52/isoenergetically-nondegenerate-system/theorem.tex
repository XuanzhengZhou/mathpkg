A Hamiltonian system $H_0(I)$ with $n$ degrees of freedom is called **isoenergetically nondegenerate** if the ratio of frequencies varies from torus to torus on each fixed energy level set $H_0 = \text{const}$. For $n = 2$, the condition is that the derivative of the ratio of frequencies with respect to the action variable numbering the tori on the given level set of $H_0$ is non-zero.

The analytic condition for isoenergetic nondegeneracy is
$$\det \begin{vmatrix}
\frac{\partial^2 H_0}{\partial I^2} & \frac{\partial H_0}{\partial I} \\[4pt]
\frac{\partial H_0}{\partial I} & 0
\end{vmatrix} \neq 0.$$

For $n > 2$, isoenergetic nondegeneracy means nondegeneracy of the frequency-ratio map from the $(n-1)$-dimensional level manifold of $H_0$ to the $(n-1)$-dimensional projective space:
$$I \mapsto (\omega_1(I) : \omega_2(I) : \cdots : \omega_n(I)).$$

The conditions for nondegeneracy and isoenergetic nondegeneracy are independent: a nondegenerate system may be isoenergetically degenerate, and vice versa.
