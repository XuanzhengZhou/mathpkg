Isoenergetic nondegeneracy is a condition on an integrable Hamiltonian system requiring that the frequency ratios vary across different invariant tori on a fixed energy surface. It is independent of ordinary nondegeneracy and is used in the isoenergetic version of Kolmogorov's theorem, which guarantees the survival of most invariant tori on a given energy level under small perturbations. For two degrees of freedom, the condition reduces to a single determinant; for higher dimensions, it requires the frequency-ratio map to projective space to be nondegenerate.
