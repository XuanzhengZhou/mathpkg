Small denominators are linear combinations of characteristic frequencies that appear as divisors when constructing canonical transformations to Birkhoff normal form. They become arbitrarily small near resonance, causing two fundamental difficulties: the normalizing transformation depends discontinuously on parameters (with poles at exact resonance), and the domain where the normal form accurately describes the system shrinks to zero at resonance. Small denominators are the essential reason why perturbation series in Hamiltonian mechanics generally diverge, a phenomenon first recognized by Poincare.
