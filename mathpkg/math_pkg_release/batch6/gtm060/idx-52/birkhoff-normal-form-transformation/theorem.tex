A Birkhoff normal form of degree $s$ for a canonical transformation is a canonical transformation of the plane to itself which is a rotation by a variable angle that is a polynomial of degree not more than $m = [s/2] - 1$ in the action variable $\tau$ of the canonical polar coordinate system:

$$(\tau, \varphi) \to (\tau, \varphi + \alpha_0 + \alpha_1 \tau + \cdots + \alpha_m \tau^m),$$

where the canonical polar coordinates are defined by
$$p = \sqrt{2\tau} \cos \varphi, \quad q = \sqrt{2\tau} \sin \varphi.$$

The multi-dimensional generalization of an elliptic transformation is the direct product of $n$ elliptic rotations of the planes $(p_l, q_l)$ with eigenvalues $\lambda_l = e^{\pm i\alpha_l}$. A Birkhoff normal form of degree $s$ in the multi-dimensional case is given by
$$(\tau, \varphi) \to \left( \tau, \varphi + \frac{\partial S}{\partial \tau} \right),$$
where $S$ is a polynomial of degree not more than $[s/2]$ in the action variables $\tau_1, \ldots, \tau_n$.
