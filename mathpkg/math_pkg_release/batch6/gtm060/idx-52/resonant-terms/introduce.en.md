Resonant terms are those monomials in the Hamiltonian expansion that survive the normalization procedure because the corresponding frequency combination vanishes at resonance. While non-resonant terms can be eliminated by canonical coordinate changes, resonant terms must be kept in the normal form. This is necessary to ensure the normal form depends continuously on parameters, avoiding the poles that appear when one insists on eliminating terms whose small denominators vanish at nearby resonant parameter values.
