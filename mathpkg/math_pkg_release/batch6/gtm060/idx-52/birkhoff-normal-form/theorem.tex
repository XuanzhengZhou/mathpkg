For a system with one degree of freedom, the Birkhoff normal form of degree $2m$ (or $2m+1$) is
$$H_{2m} = H_{2m+1} = a_1 \tau + a_2 \tau^2 + \cdots + a_m \tau^m, \quad \tau = \frac{P^2 + Q^2}{2}.$$

For a system with two degrees of freedom, the Birkhoff normal form of degree $4$ is
$$H_4 = a_1 \tau_1 + a_2 \tau_2 + a_{11} \tau_1^2 + a_{12} \tau_1 \tau_2 + a_{22} \tau_2^2,$$
where $\tau_k = (P_k^2 + Q_k^2)/2$. The coefficients $a_1, a_2$ are the characteristic frequencies, and the coefficients $a_{ij}$ describe the dependence of the frequencies on the amplitude.
