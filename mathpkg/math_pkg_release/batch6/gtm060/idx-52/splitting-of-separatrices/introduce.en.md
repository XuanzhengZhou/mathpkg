Splitting of separatrices is the phenomenon that the stable and unstable invariant manifolds of neighboring saddle fixed points of a Poincaré map do not coincide, unlike in the truncated (averaged) system. Discovered by Poincaré, this splitting is exponentially small in the perturbation parameter but fundamentally important: it implies that the Birkhoff normal form series generally diverge and that the dynamics near resonance is genuinely chaotic. The intersection points of the split separatrices form an intricate homoclinic tangle, which Poincaré described as a "lattice with infinitely fine mesh."
