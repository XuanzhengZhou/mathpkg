Theorem 2 states that an elliptic canonical transformation whose linear part is a rotation by an angle $\alpha$ with $\lambda = e^{i\alpha}$ not a root of unity of low order can be brought to Birkhoff normal form. The normal form is a rotation by a variable angle depending polynomially on the action. The condition that $\lambda$ is not a root of unity of degree $\leq s$ is the non-resonance condition for maps: it ensures that the small denominators appearing in the normalization procedure do not vanish.
