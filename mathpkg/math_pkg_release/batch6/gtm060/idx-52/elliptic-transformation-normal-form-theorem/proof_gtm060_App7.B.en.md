The proof proceeds by induction on the degree of the normal form, analogous to the proof for Hamiltonian systems (Section A). In a suitable complex coordinate system, the transformation is expanded in a power series. At each step, a canonical coordinate change with a homogeneous polynomial generating function eliminates non-resonant terms of a given degree. The resonance condition is that $\lambda^{\alpha - \beta} = 1$ for terms of the form $z^\alpha \bar{z}^\beta$ of degree $|\alpha| + |\beta| = N$. Since $\lambda$ is not a root of unity of degree $s$ or less, all such denominators are non-zero for $N \leq s$, and the reduction to normal form can be carried out up to degree $s$.
