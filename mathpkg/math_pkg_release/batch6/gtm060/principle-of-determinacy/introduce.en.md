Newton's principle of determinacy states that the future motion of a mechanical system is completely determined by its initial positions and velocities. This principle is the physical foundation for the mathematical fact that Newton's second-order differential equation, together with initial conditions, has a unique solution. It reflects our empirical experience that knowing where things are and how fast they are moving is sufficient to predict their future trajectories.
