An extremal is a curve at which the first variation of the functional vanishes for all admissible variations. This is the infinite-dimensional analogue of a critical point in finite dimensions. Finding extremals is the central problem of the calculus of variations. Under suitable convexity conditions, extremals correspond to minima, maxima, or saddle points of the functional.
