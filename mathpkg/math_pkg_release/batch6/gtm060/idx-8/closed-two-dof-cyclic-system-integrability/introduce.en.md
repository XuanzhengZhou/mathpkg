This corollary states that any closed Hamiltonian system with two degrees of freedom that possesses a cyclic coordinate is integrable by quadrature. When $n = 2$, after removing the cyclic coordinate via momentum conservation, the remaining system for the non-cyclic degree of freedom is one-dimensional and can be integrated directly using the conservation of the Hamiltonian $H(p', q') = c$. This result explains why many important mechanical problems (central force motion, rigid body rotation) are exactly solvable.
