For a closed system with $n = 2$, let $q_1$ be a cyclic coordinate. Then $p_1 = \text{const}$ by Corollary 2, and the cyclic coordinate can be eliminated. The remaining system for the non-cyclic variables $p'$ and $q'$ is one-dimensional, governed by a Hamiltonian $H(p_1; p', q')$ where $p_1$ enters only as a constant parameter.

Since the system is closed, the Hamiltonian is conserved: $H(p_1; p', q') = c$. This single equation determines the trajectory in the $(p', q')$-plane and can be immediately integrated. Thus the entire system is integrable by quadrature.
