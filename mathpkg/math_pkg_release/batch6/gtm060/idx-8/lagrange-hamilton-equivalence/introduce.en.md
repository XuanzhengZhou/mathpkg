This fundamental theorem establishes the equivalence between the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations of mechanics. By applying the Legendre transformation to the Lagrangian function with respect to the generalized velocities, the system of $n$ second-order Lagrange equations is converted into an equivalent system of $2n$ first-order Hamilton equations. The resulting Hamiltonian system is remarkably symmetric: the time derivatives of positions and momenta are given by the partial derivatives of a single scalar function $H$, the Hamiltonian.
