Young's inequality bounds the product $px$ by the sum of a convex function $f(x)$ and its Legendre conjugate $g(p)$. It is a direct consequence of the definition of the Legendre transform. The classical case $f(x) = x^\alpha/\alpha$ yields the well-known inequality $px \leq x^\alpha/\alpha + p^\beta/\beta$ for conjugate exponents $\alpha, \beta > 1$ with $1/\alpha + 1/\beta = 1$, which is fundamental in the theory of $L^p$ spaces and Hölder's inequality.
