This corollary states that the total time derivative of the Hamiltonian along a solution of Hamilton's equations equals its partial derivative with respect to time. Consequently, when the Hamiltonian does not depend explicitly on time (i.e., $\partial H/\partial t = 0$), the Hamiltonian is a conserved quantity along trajectories. In mechanical systems where $H = T + U$, this reduces to the law of conservation of energy, one of the most fundamental principles of mechanics.
