This corollary formalizes the crucial connection between symmetry and conservation in Hamiltonian mechanics: a coordinate absent from the Hamiltonian yields a conserved conjugate momentum. This is a precursor to Noether's theorem. The practical consequence is dimensional reduction -- the conserved momentum $p_1$ becomes a constant parameter, and the remaining $2n-2$ variables can be solved independently, after which $q_1$ is recovered by a simple quadrature.
