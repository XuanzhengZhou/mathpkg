Let $f$ be the quadratic form $f(\mathbf{x}) = \sum f_{ij} x_i x_j$. Show that its Legendre transform is again a quadratic form $g(\mathbf{p}) = \sum g_{ij} p_i p_j$, and that the values of both forms at corresponding points coincide:

$$f(\mathbf{x}(\mathbf{p})) = g(\mathbf{p}) \quad \text{and} \quad g(\mathbf{p}(\mathbf{x})) = f(\mathbf{x}).$$
