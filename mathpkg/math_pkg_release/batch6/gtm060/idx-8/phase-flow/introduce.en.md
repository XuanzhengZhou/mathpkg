The phase flow is the one-parameter group of transformations of phase space generated by Hamilton's equations. It maps an initial state $(\mathbf{p}(0), \mathbf{q}(0))$ to the state $(\mathbf{p}(t), \mathbf{q}(t))$ after time $t$. Liouville's theorem states that this flow preserves phase volume, a property with profound consequences: Hamiltonian systems cannot be asymptotically stable. The phase flow is the central object of study in the qualitative theory of dynamical systems.
