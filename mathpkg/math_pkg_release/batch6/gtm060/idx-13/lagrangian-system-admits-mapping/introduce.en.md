A Lagrangian system $(M, L)$ is said to admit a mapping $h: M \to M$ if the Lagrangian $L$ is invariant under the pushforward action of $h$ on the tangent bundle. This means that evaluating $L$ on any tangent vector $\mathbf{v}$ gives the same result as evaluating it on the pushed-forward vector $h_*\mathbf{v}$. This notion captures the idea of a symmetry of the Lagrangian. For example, a Lagrangian that does not depend explicitly on a coordinate $x_1$ admits translations along the $x_1$-axis. The concept is fundamental to Noether's theorem, which links continuous symmetries of a Lagrangian system to conserved quantities.
