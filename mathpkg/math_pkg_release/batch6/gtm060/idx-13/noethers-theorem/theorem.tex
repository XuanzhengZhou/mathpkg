Let $M$ be a smooth manifold and $L: TM \to \mathbb{R}$ a smooth Lagrangian function. If the Lagrangian system $(M, L)$ admits a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms $h^s: M \to M$, $s \in \mathbb{R}$, then the Lagrangian equations of motion possess a first integral $I: TM \to \mathbb{R}$.

In local coordinates $\mathbf{q}$ on $M$, the first integral is given by

$$I(\mathbf{q}, \dot{\mathbf{q}}) = \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\mathbf{q}}} \cdot \left. \frac{d}{ds} \right|_{s=0} h^s(\mathbf{q}).$$

Invariantly, $I(v)$ equals the rate of change of $L(v)$ when the tangent vector $v \in TM_x$ varies within $TM_x$ with velocity $\frac{d}{ds}\big|_{s=0} h^s x$.
