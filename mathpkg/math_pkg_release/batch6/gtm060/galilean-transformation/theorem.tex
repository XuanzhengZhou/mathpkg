The galilean group is the group of all transformations of a galilean space which preserve its structure. The elements of this group are called galilean transformations. Thus, galilean transformations are affine transformations of $A^4$ which preserve intervals of time and the distance between simultaneous events.

Every galilean transformation of the galilean coordinate space $R \times R^3$ can be written in a unique way as the composition of a rotation, a translation, and a uniform motion:
\begin{align*}
g_1(t, x) &= (t, x + vt) \quad \text{(uniform motion with velocity $v$)}\\
g_2(t, x) &= (t + s, x + s) \quad \text{(translation of the origin)}\\
g_3(t, x) &= (t, Gx) \quad \text{(rotation, $G: R^3 \to R^3$ orthogonal)}
\end{align*}
Thus $g = g_1 \circ g_2 \circ g_3$ and the dimension of the galilean group is $3 + 4 + 3 = 10$.
