Liouville's theorem states that the Hamiltonian phase flow preserves volume in phase space. This follows immediately from the divergence-free nature of the Hamiltonian vector field. The theorem has profound consequences: it implies the impossibility of asymptotic stability in Hamiltonian systems, underpins the foundations of statistical mechanics (equal a priori probability for equal phase volumes), and is the classical precursor to the unitary evolution of quantum mechanics.
