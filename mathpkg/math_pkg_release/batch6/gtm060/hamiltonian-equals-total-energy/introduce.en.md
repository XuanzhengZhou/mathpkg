For standard mechanical systems with quadratic kinetic energy, the Hamiltonian equals the total energy $H = T + U$. This follows from Euler's theorem on homogeneous functions: for a quadratic form, $(\partial f/\partial x)x = 2f$, so $g(p) = px - f(x) = 2f - f = f$. Thus $H = p\dot{q} - L = 2T - (T - U) = T + U$. The Hamiltonian is conserved when it does not depend explicitly on time.
