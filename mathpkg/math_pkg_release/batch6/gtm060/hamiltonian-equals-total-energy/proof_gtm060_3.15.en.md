\textbf{Lemma:} By Euler's theorem on homogeneous functions, $(\partial f/\partial x)x = 2f$ for a quadratic form. Therefore $g(p(x)) = px - f(x) = (\partial f/\partial x)x - f = 2f(x) - f(x) = f(x)$.

\textbf{Theorem:} $H = p\dot{q} - L = p\dot{q} - (T - U)$. Since $T$ is quadratic in $\dot{q}$, by the lemma, $p\dot{q} = 2T$ (as $p = \partial T/\partial \dot{q}$). Hence $H = 2T - T + U = T + U$.
