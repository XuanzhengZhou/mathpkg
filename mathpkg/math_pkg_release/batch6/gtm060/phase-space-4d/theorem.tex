The equation of motion $\ddot{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x})$ can be written as the system:

$$\dot{x}_1 = y_1 \quad \dot{x}_2 = y_2$$
$$\dot{y}_1 = f_1(x_1, x_2) \quad \dot{y}_2 = f_2(x_1, x_2).$$

The phase space of a system with two degrees of freedom is the four-dimensional space with coordinates $x_1, x_2, y_1,$ and $y_2$.

The system defines the phase velocity vector field in four space as well as the phase flow of the system (a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms of four-dimensional phase space). The phase curves are subsets of four-dimensional phase space. All of phase space is partitioned into phase curves.
