If an unperturbed hamiltonian system
$$H_0(I), \qquad I \in G \subset \mathbb{R}^n, \quad \varphi \bmod 2\pi \in T^n$$
is nondegenerate, i.e.
$$\det \left| \frac{\partial^2 H_0}{\partial I^2} \right| \neq 0,$$
then for sufficiently small conservative hamiltonian perturbations
$$H = H_0(I) + \varepsilon H_1(I, \varphi),$$
most non-resonant invariant tori do not vanish, but are only slightly deformed, so that in the phase space of the perturbed system there are also invariant tori densely filled with phase curves winding around them conditionally-periodically, with a number of independent frequencies equal to the number of degrees of freedom. These invariant tori form a majority in the sense that the measure of the complement of their union is small when the perturbation is small.
