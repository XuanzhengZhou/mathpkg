This KAM variant addresses equilibrium positions in periodically-forced Hamiltonian systems. In the extended phase space (including time as an additional variable), the equilibrium becomes a periodic orbit, and the preserved objects are $(n+1)$-dimensional invariant tori. For one degree of freedom ($n=1$), the nondegeneracy condition has a concrete mechanical interpretation: the period of small oscillations must genuinely depend on the amplitude. When satisfied, this condition guarantees Liapunov stability of the equilibrium -- a result that requires the full KAM machinery, since linear stability alone is insufficient to prevent Arnold diffusion.
