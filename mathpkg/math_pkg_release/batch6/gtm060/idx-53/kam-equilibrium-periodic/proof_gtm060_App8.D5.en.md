The equilibrium position of the periodically-forced system corresponds to a periodic orbit (a circle $\tau = 0$) in the extended $(2n+1)$-dimensional phase space. After reduction to Birkhoff normal form in a neighborhood of this circle (analogous to the autonomous case but accounting for time-periodic terms), the truncated integrable part depends on action variables $\tau_k$ and has an additional trivial angle variable (time $t$ with frequency 1).

The Kolmogorov iteration is applied to find $(n+1)$-dimensional invariant tori near the circle. The nondegeneracy condition involves the dependence of the internal frequencies on the actions. For $n = 1$, this condition reduces to $dT/dA \neq 0$, where $T$ is the period of small oscillations and $A \propto \tau$ is the amplitude squared. When nondegenerate, the preserved 2-dimensional tori divide the 3-dimensional extended energy manifold, ensuring Liapunov stability.
