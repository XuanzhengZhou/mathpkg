The Diophantine non-resonance condition is a quantitative strengthening of the requirement that frequencies are rationally independent. It forbids not only exact linear relations among frequencies with integer coefficients, but also approximate relations, by imposing a lower bound $C|k|^{-\nu}$ on $|(\omega, k)|$. This condition is essential in KAM theory to control the small denominators that appear when solving the linearized equations at each iterative step. For $\nu > n-1$, the set of frequency vectors satisfying this condition has full Lebesgue measure in any bounded region.
