Consider a time-periodic hamiltonian system with $n$ degrees of freedom:
$$H = H_0(I) + \varepsilon H_1(I, \varphi, t), \qquad I \in G \subset \mathbb{R}^n, \quad \varphi \bmod 2\pi \in T^n,$$
where the perturbation $H_1$ is $2\pi$-periodic in the angle variables $\varphi$ and in time $t$. In the $(2n+1)$-dimensional extended phase space $\{(I, \varphi, t)\} = \mathbb{R}^n \times T^{n+1}$, the invariant tori of the unperturbed system have dimension $n+1$.

If the nondegeneracy condition
$$\det \left| \frac{\partial^2 H_0}{\partial I^2} \right| \neq 0$$
holds, then under a sufficiently small perturbation ($\varepsilon \ll 1$), most $(n+1)$-dimensional invariant tori are preserved.

If $n = 1$, this nondegeneracy condition also guarantees stability of the action variable for all time under sufficiently small perturbations.
