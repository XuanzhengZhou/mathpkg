This variant of the KAM theorem applies to non-autonomous Hamiltonian systems with time-periodic coefficients. By treating time $t$ as an additional angle variable, the extended phase space acquires dimension $2n+1$, and the preserved invariant tori have dimension $n+1$. The nondegeneracy condition is the standard one (non-singular Hessian of $H_0$). For systems with one degree of freedom ($n=1$), the theorem additionally guarantees that action variables remain forever close to their initial values -- a strong form of Liapunov stability.
