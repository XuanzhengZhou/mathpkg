For a nearly-integrable Hamiltonian system with $n = 2$ degrees of freedom satisfying the isoenergetic nondegeneracy condition
$$\det \begin{vmatrix}
\frac{\partial^2 H_0}{\partial I^2} & \frac{\partial H_0}{\partial I} \\[4pt]
\frac{\partial H_0}{\partial I} & 0
\end{vmatrix} \neq 0,$$
the action variables $I$ remain forever close to their initial values for all initial conditions, under sufficiently small perturbations. This holds both for non-resonant phase curves (which conditionally-periodically fill out two-dimensional tori comprising most of the phase space) and for the remaining initial conditions falling in the gaps between invariant tori.

The key geometric fact is that for $n = 2$, the phase space has dimension 4, an energy level manifold is 3-dimensional, and the invariant two-dimensional tori divide each energy level set. Therefore, a phase curve beginning in a gap between two invariant tori remains forever confined between those tori, and the corresponding action variables remain forever near their initial conditions.
