This KAM variant applies to fixed points of symplectic (canonical) mappings, which arise naturally as Poincare maps of periodic orbits. Under the assumption that all eigenvalues lie on the unit circle and low-order resonances are absent, the mapping can be transformed to Birkhoff normal form. The nondegeneracy condition $\det |\omega_{kl}| \neq 0$ then guarantees a set of invariant tori of almost full measure. For the planar case ($n=1$), this yields stability of the fixed point. The required differentiability order for the planar case was a subject of classical work by Moser and Russmann, starting from 333 derivatives and eventually reduced to 6.
