This KAM variant applies to symplectic (area-preserving) mappings rather than continuous Hamiltonian flows. Such mappings arise naturally as Poincare return maps on surfaces of section, reducing questions about periodic orbits of dynamical systems to questions about fixed points. Under a nondegeneracy condition on the unperturbed generating function $S_0(I')$, most invariant tori (or invariant circles, for $n=1$) persist under small perturbations. For the case of one degree of freedom ($n=1$), this provides stability: points near a preserved invariant circle remain in its vicinity for all iterations.
