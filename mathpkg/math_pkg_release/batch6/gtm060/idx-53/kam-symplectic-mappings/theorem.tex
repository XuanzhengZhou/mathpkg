Consider a symplectic (area-preserving) mapping of the "$2n$-dimensional annulus":
$$(I, \varphi) \to (I', \varphi'), \qquad I \in G \subset \mathbb{R}^n, \quad \varphi \bmod 2\pi \in T^n,$$
with generating function of the form
$$S(I', \varphi) = S_0(I') + \varepsilon S_1(I', \varphi),$$
where $S_0$ generates the unperturbed integrable mapping
$$I' = I, \qquad \varphi' = \varphi + \frac{\partial S_0}{\partial I}.$$

If the nondegeneracy condition
$$\det \left| \frac{\partial^2 S_0}{\partial I^2} \right| \neq 0$$
holds (equivalently, the rotation angles $\alpha(I) = \frac{\partial S_0}{\partial I}$ vary with $I$), then for sufficiently small perturbations ($\varepsilon \ll 1$), most $n$-dimensional invariant tori close to the tori $I = \text{const}$ are preserved. For $n = 1$, the invariant tori become invariant circles, and a point initially near a circle will remain near that circle for all iterations if the perturbation is sufficiently small.
