The isoenergetic nondegeneracy condition is a variant of the standard KAM nondegeneracy condition that applies when one restricts attention to a fixed energy level manifold. It involves a bordered Hessian determinant that couples the Hessian of $H_0$ with its gradient. This condition is generally weaker than full nondegeneracy and is satisfied by systems in general position. Notably, it is the appropriate nondegeneracy condition for the restricted three-body problem, where one frequency is fixed (the angular velocity of the rotating frame), making the full nondegeneracy condition fail while isoenergetic nondegeneracy still holds.
