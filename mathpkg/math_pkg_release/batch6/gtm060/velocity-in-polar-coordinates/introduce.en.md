In polar coordinates, the velocity decomposes as $\dot{\mathbf{r}} = \dot{r}\mathbf{e}_r + r\dot{\varphi}\mathbf{e}_\varphi$, where the radial component $\dot{r}$ measures change in distance and the angular component $r\dot{\varphi}$ measures tangential speed. The basis vectors themselves rotate, with $\dot{\mathbf{e}}_r = \dot{\varphi}\mathbf{e}_\varphi$ and $\dot{\mathbf{e}}_\varphi = -\dot{\varphi}\mathbf{e}_r$.
