A functional is any mapping from the space of curves to the real numbers. Functionals generalize the concept of a function to infinite-dimensional domains, namely spaces of curves. An example is the length functional \(\Phi(\gamma) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} \sqrt{1 + \dot{x}^2}\,dt\) for a plane curve \(\gamma\). The calculus of variations studies the extremals of functionals.
