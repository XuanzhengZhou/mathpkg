By the theorem above, a motion of the mechanical system is an extremal of the action functional $\int L\, dt$ with $L = T - U$. The condition for a curve to be an extremal of a functional does not depend on the choice of coordinate system (as noted in the remark on coordinate invariance of the Euler–Lagrange equation). Therefore, in any system of coordinates $\mathbf{q}$, the Euler–Lagrange equation written in that coordinate system is satisfied by the motion.
