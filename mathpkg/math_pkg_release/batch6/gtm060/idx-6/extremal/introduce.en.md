An extremal of a differentiable functional \(\Phi(\gamma)\) is a curve \(\gamma\) such that the differential vanishes, i.e., \(F(h) = 0\) for all variations \(h\). This generalizes the notion of a stationary point of a function to infinite-dimensional spaces of curves.
