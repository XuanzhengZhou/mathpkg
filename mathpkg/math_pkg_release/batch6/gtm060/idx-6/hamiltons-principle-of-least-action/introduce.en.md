Hamilton's principle of least action (also called Hamilton's form of the principle of least motion) states that the motion of a mechanical system from time $ to $ is an extremal — and in many cases a minimum — of the action functional \(\int_{t_0}^{t_1} L\, dt\). This principle unifies mechanics under a single variational framework.
