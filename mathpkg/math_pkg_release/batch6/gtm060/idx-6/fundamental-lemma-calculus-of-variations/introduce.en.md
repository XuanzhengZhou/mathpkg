If a continuous function \(f(t)\) on 0$ satisfies \(\int_{t_0}^{t_1} f(t) h(t)\, dt = 0\) for every continuous function \(h(t)\) with \(h(t_0) = h(t_1) = 0\), then \(f(t) \equiv 0\). This lemma is the key step in deriving the Euler–Lagrange equation from the vanishing of the first variation.
