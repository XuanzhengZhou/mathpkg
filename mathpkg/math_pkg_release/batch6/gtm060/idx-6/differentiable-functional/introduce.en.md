A functional \(\Phi\) is called differentiable if its increment \(\Phi(\gamma + h) - \Phi(\gamma)\) can be decomposed into a linear part \(F(h)\) and a remainder \(R(h,\gamma) = O(h^2)\). The linear part, called the differential (or variation), generalizes the Fréchet derivative to the space of curves. This definition is foundational for the calculus of variations.
