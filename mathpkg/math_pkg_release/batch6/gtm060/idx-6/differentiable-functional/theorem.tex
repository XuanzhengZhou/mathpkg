A functional $\Phi$ is called differentiable if

$$\Phi(\gamma + h) - \Phi(\gamma) = F(h) + R(h, \gamma),$$

where $F$ depends linearly on $h$ (i.e., $F(h_1 + h_2) = F(h_1) + F(h_2)$ and $F(ch) = cF(h)$ for fixed $\gamma$), and $R(h, \gamma) = O(h^2)$ in the sense that for $|h| < \varepsilon$ and $|dh/dt| < \varepsilon$, we have $|R| < C\varepsilon^2$. The linear part $F(h)$ is called the differential of $\Phi$.
