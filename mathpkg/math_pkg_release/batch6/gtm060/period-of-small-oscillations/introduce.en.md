Near a stable equilibrium (minimum of potential energy), the equation of motion can be linearized to $\ddot{x} = -U''(\xi)(x-\xi)$, giving harmonic oscillations with period $T_0 = 2\pi/\sqrt{U''(\xi)}$. The period depends only on the curvature of the potential at the minimum, not on the amplitude, in the small-amplitude limit.
