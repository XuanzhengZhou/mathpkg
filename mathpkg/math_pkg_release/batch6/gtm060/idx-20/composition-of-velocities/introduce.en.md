The composition of velocities theorem describes how the absolute velocity of a point decomposes when observed from a moving coordinate system. In the most general case, where the moving frame both translates and rotates relative to a fixed inertial frame, the absolute velocity is the sum of three components: the relative velocity (motion within the moving frame), the transferred velocity of rotation (due to the angular velocity of the moving frame), and the translational velocity of the moving frame's origin. This theorem is fundamental for analyzing motion in non-inertial reference frames and underpins the derivation of inertial forces.
