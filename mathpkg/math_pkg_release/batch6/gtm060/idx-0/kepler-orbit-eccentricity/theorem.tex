The eccentricity $e$ of a Keplerian orbit is defined by

$$e = \sqrt{1 + \frac{2EM^2}{k^2}},$$

where $E$ is the total mechanical energy, $M$ is the angular momentum, and $k$ is the gravitational constant. The orbit is an ellipse when $E < 0$ (so $e < 1$), a parabola when $E = 0$ (so $e = 1$), and a hyperbola when $E > 0$ (so $e > 1$).
