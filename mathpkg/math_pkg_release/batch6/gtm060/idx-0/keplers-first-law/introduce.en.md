Kepler's First Law characterizes planetary orbits as ellipses with the Sun occupying one focus. Derived from the conservation of angular momentum and energy in a central inverse-square force field, the orbit equation takes the polar form $r = p/(1 + e \cos \varphi)$. For bounded motion ($E < 0$), the eccentricity satisfies $e < 1$, yielding elliptic trajectories.
