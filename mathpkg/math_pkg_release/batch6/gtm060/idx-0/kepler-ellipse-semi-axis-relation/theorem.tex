For a Keplerian ellipse, the semi-major axis $a$, semi-minor axis $b$, parameter $p$, and eccentricity $e$ are related by:

$$a = \frac{p}{1 - e^2},$$

$$e = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{\sqrt{a^2 - b^2}}{a},$$

where $c = ae$ is the distance from the center to a focus. Equivalently,

$$b = a\sqrt{1 - e^2} = \frac{p}{\sqrt{1 - e^2}}.$$
