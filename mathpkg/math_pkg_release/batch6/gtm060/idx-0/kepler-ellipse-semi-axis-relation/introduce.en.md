The semi-major axis $a$ of the Kepler ellipse is related to the parameter $p$ and eccentricity $e$ by $a = p/(1 - e^2)$. The eccentricity is expressed geometrically as $e = c/a$, where $c = ae$ is the focal distance. These relations connect the orbital invariants ($p$, $e$) derived from the conservation laws to the standard geometric parameters of the ellipse.
