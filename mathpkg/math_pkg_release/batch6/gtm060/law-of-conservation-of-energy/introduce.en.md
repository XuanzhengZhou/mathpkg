The law of conservation of energy for one-degree-of-freedom systems states that the total energy $E = \frac{1}{2}\dot{x}^2 + U(x)$ remains constant along solutions. The proof follows immediately from the chain rule: differentiating $E$ and using the equation of motion $\ddot{x} = -dU/dx$ yields zero. This conservation law is the key to qualitative analysis via the phase plane.
