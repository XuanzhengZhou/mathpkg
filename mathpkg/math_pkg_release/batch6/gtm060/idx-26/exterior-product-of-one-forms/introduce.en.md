The exterior product of $k$ 1-forms generalizes the wedge product to higher degree. Given $1$-forms $\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_k$, their product $\omega_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \omega_k$ is a $k$-form whose value on $k$ vectors equals the determinant of the $k \times k$ matrix $(\omega_i(\xi_j))$. Geometrically, this is the oriented $k$-dimensional volume of the image of the parallelepiped spanned by $\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_k$ under the map $\xi \mapsto (\omega_1(\xi), \ldots, \omega_k(\xi))$ into $\mathbb{R}^k$. These products are called exterior monomials and serve as building blocks for all $k$-forms.
