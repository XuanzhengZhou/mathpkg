A $2$-form generalizes the $1$-form to act on pairs of vectors. It is bilinear (linear in each argument separately) and skew-symmetric (swapping the arguments flips the sign). Geometrically, a $2$-form can represent oriented area: for example, the oriented area of the projection of a parallelogram onto a coordinate plane in $\mathbb{R}^3$ is a $2$-form. The space of all $2$-forms on $\mathbb{R}^n$ has dimension $\binom{n}{2} = n(n-1)/2$.
