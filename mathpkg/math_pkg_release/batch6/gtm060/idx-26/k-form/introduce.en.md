A $k$-form is the general degree-$k$ exterior form on $\mathbb{R}^n$. It is a multilinear function of $k$ vectors that is totally antisymmetric: swapping any two arguments changes the sign by $-1$. The prototypical example is the oriented $k$-dimensional volume of the parallelepiped spanned by $k$ vectors. The space of all $k$-forms on $\mathbb{R}^n$ has dimension $\binom{n}{k}$. For $k > n$, every $k$-form is identically zero.
