The total energy of a conservative system is conserved, i.e.,

$$\frac{dE}{dt} = 0, \quad \text{where } E = \frac{1}{2} \dot{\mathbf{x}}^2 + U(\mathbf{x}), \quad \dot{\mathbf{x}}^2 = (\dot{\mathbf{x}}, \dot{\mathbf{x}}).$$

Proof: $dE/dt = (\dot{\mathbf{x}}, \ddot{\mathbf{x}}) + (\partial U / \partial \mathbf{x}, \dot{\mathbf{x}}) = (\ddot{\mathbf{x}} + (\partial U / \partial \mathbf{x}), \dot{\mathbf{x}}) = 0$ by the equation of motion.
