$$\frac{dH}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p}\dot{p} + \frac{\partial H}{\partial q}\dot{q} + \frac{\partial H}{\partial t}.$$
By Hamilton's equations: $\dot{p} = -\partial H/\partial q$, $\dot{q} = \partial H/\partial p$. Substituting:
$$\frac{dH}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p}\left(-\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}\right) + \frac{\partial H}{\partial q}\frac{\partial H}{\partial p} + \frac{\partial H}{\partial t} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial t}.$$
