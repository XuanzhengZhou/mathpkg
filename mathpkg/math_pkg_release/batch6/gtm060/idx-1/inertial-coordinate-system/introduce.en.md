An inertial coordinate system is a reference frame in which the laws of physics take their simplest form. In such a frame, a body not subject to external forces moves with constant velocity. The principle of Galilean relativity asserts that all inertial frames are equivalent for describing physical laws, and any frame moving uniformly with respect to an inertial frame is also inertial.
