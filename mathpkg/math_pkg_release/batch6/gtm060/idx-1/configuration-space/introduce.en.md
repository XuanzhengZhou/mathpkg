The configuration space of a mechanical system of $n$ point particles is the $3n$-dimensional Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^{3n}$, obtained as the direct product of $n$ copies of physical three-dimensional space. Each point in configuration space represents a complete specification of the positions of all $n$ particles. A motion of the system is then a single differentiable curve in this $3n$-dimensional space, parametrized by time. This construction reduces the study of many-particle systems to the geometry of curves in higher-dimensional spaces.
