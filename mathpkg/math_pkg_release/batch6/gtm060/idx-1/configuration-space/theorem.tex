The direct product of $n$ copies of $\mathbb{R}^3$ is called the \text{configuration space} of the system of $n$ points. The $n$ mappings $\mathbf{x}_i: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$) define one mapping

$$\mathbf{x}: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^N \quad N = 3n$$

of the time axis into the configuration space. Such a mapping is called a \text{motion of a system of $n$ points} in the galilean coordinate system on $\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^3$.
