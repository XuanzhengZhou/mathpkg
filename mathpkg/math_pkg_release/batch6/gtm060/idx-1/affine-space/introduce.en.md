An affine space is a geometric structure that generalizes the properties of a vector space by removing the distinguished origin. While the sum of two points is not defined, the difference of two points yields a vector in the associated vector space, and the vector space acts on the affine space by parallel displacement. Affine spaces provide the natural setting for describing positions in classical mechanics, where coordinates are relative and there is no absolute origin.
