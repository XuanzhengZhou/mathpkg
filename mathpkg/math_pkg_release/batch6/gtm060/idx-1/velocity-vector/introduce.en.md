The velocity vector is the first time derivative of a motion, representing the instantaneous rate and direction of change of position. It is the limit of the difference quotient as the time interval approaches zero. The velocity at each instant is an element of the same Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^N$ in which the motion takes place, geometrically interpreted as the tangent vector to the trajectory at that point.
