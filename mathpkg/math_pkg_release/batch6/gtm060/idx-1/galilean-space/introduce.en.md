A Galilean space is the mathematical model of space-time in classical Newtonian mechanics. It is a four-dimensional affine space $A^4$ equipped with the Galilean space-time structure: a time mapping defining simultaneity between events and a Euclidean metric on each three-dimensional slice of simultaneous events. Importantly, there is no intrinsic notion of "same place at different times" -- spatial identity across time requires a choice of inertial coordinate system (a reference frame).
