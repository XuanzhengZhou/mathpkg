A world line is the space-time trajectory of a particle in Galilean space. It is a curve in the four-dimensional affine space $A^4$ whose projection onto any Galilean coordinate system appears as the graph of a motion mapping time to three-dimensional position. A mechanical system of $n$ points is represented by $n$ world lines in Galilean space. World lines provide a coordinate-independent, geometric description of mechanical motion.
