A motion in $\mathbb{R}^N$ is a differentiable mapping from a time interval into Euclidean space, representing the position of a point as a function of time. The image of this mapping is called a trajectory or curve. This definition formalizes the intuitive notion of a particle's path through space, with differentiability ensuring that velocity and acceleration are well-defined. The concept extends naturally to systems of $n$ points by taking $N = 3n$, where the configuration space is the direct product of $n$ copies of $\mathbb{R}^3$.
