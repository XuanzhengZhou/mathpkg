Galileo's principle of relativity is the foundational postulate of classical mechanics. It states that the laws of physics are identical in all inertial reference frames, and that uniform motion relative to an inertial frame yields another inertial frame. This principle implies that no mechanical experiment conducted entirely within a closed laboratory can detect uniform rectilinear motion, establishing the equivalence of all inertial observers.
