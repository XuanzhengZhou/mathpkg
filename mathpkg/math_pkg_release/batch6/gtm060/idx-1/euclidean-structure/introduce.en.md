A Euclidean structure on a vector space is a positive definite symmetric bilinear form, also called an inner product or scalar product. This structure equips the vector space with notions of length, angle, and orthogonality. When an affine space has its associated vector space endowed with a Euclidean structure, the distance between points in the affine space is defined as the norm of their difference vector.
