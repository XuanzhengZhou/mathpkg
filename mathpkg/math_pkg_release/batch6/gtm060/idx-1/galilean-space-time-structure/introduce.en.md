The Galilean space-time structure is the mathematical formalization of space and time in classical mechanics. It consists of three ingredients: a four-dimensional affine universe whose points are events, a linear time mapping that assigns time intervals to pairs of events and defines simultaneity, and a Euclidean distance on each three-dimensional space of simultaneous events. This structure captures the Newtonian notions of absolute time and Euclidean spatial geometry while remaining independent of any choice of inertial coordinate system.
