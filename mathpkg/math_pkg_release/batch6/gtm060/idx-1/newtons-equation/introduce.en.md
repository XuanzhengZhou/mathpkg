Newton's equation is the fundamental law of classical mechanics, expressing that the acceleration of a mechanical system is a function of its positions, velocities, and time. Combined with the existence and uniqueness theorem for ordinary differential equations, it guarantees that the entire motion is uniquely determined by the initial positions and velocities. The specific form of the function $F$ characterizes each particular mechanical system and is determined experimentally.
