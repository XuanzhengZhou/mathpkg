The product of mass times acceleration $m\ddot{x}$ does not depend on the body, and is a characteristic of the extension of the spring. This value is called the \text{force} of the spring acting on the body.

As a unit of force, we take the ``newton.'' If one liter of water is suspended on a spring at the surface of the earth, the spring acts with a force of 9.8 newtons ($= 1$ kg).
