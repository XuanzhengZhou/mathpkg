Force is defined operationally through Newton's second law as the product of mass and acceleration, $F = m\ddot{x}$. The key experimental insight is that for a given interaction (such as a spring with a fixed extension), the quantity $m\ddot{x}$ is independent of the particular body being acted upon -- it characterizes the interaction itself rather than the body. The SI unit of force is the newton, defined such that a 1 kg mass experiencing gravitational acceleration at Earth's surface ($g \approx 9.8$ m/s$^2$) feels a force of 9.8 newtons.
