A Euclidean space $E^n$ is an affine space whose associated vector space is equipped with a Euclidean structure (a positive definite symmetric bilinear form). The scalar product induces a distance function between points, making $E^n$ a metric space. Euclidean spaces are the natural setting for describing the positions and motions of mechanical systems in classical mechanics, where three-dimensional Euclidean space $E^3$ models physical space.
