The total kinetic energy of a system of particles is the sum of individual kinetic energies. It can be expressed in configuration space $E^{3n}$ as $T = \frac{1}{2}\|\dot{\mathbf{r}}\|^2$ with respect to the mass-weighted inner product. The work-energy theorem relates the change in kinetic energy to the work done by all forces.
