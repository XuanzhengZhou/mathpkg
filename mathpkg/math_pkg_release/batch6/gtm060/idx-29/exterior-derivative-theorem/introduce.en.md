The theorem on the exterior derivative establishes that for any $k$-form $\omega^k$ on a manifold, there exists a unique $(k+1)$-form which is the principal $(k+1)$-linear part of the integral of $\omega^k$ over the boundary of an infinitesimal curvilinear parallelepiped. This $(k+1)$-form is precisely the exterior derivative $d\omega^k$, and it is intrinsically defined -- independent of the auxiliary coordinate choices used to construct the curvilinear cell. In local coordinates, the theorem provides the explicit computational formula: $d(\sum a_{i_1,\ldots,i_k} dx_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_{i_k}) = \sum da_{i_1,\ldots,i_k} \wedge dx_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_{i_k}$, which reduces exterior differentiation to ordinary differentiation of the coefficient functions.
