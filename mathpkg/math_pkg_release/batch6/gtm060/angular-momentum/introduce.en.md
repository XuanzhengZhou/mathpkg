The angular momentum $\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{r} \times \dot{\mathbf{r}}$ measures the rotational aspect of motion about a point. For central forces, $\mathbf{M}$ is a conserved vector, providing three constants of motion (in 3D). In planar motion, the single scalar $M = |\mathbf{M}|$ encodes the conservation and enables reduction to a 1D problem.
