For Hamilton's equations, the divergence of the vector field is

$$\text{div } \mathbf{f} = \frac{\partial}{\partial \mathbf{p}} \left( -\frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{q}} \right) + \frac{\partial}{\partial \mathbf{q}} \left( \frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{p}} \right) \equiv 0,$$

since mixed partial derivatives commute. By Liouville's theorem (Theorem 2), if $\text{div } \mathbf{f} \equiv 0$, then the phase flow $g^t$ preserves volume. Hence the phase flow of any Hamiltonian system is volume-preserving.
