In Hamiltonian mechanics, the phase flow $g^t$ maps initial conditions to their evolved states at time $t$. This theorem states that the phase flow is volume-preserving: for any region $D$ in phase space, the volume of the image $g^t D$ equals the volume of $D$ for all times $t$. This is a special case of Liouville's theorem, applicable specifically to Hamiltonian systems where the divergence of the vector field vanishes identically. The result is fundamental to statistical mechanics and ergodic theory.
