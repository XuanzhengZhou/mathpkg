This theorem describes the long-term behavior of an irrational rotation on a circle. When the rotation angle $\alpha$ is incommensurable with $2\pi$ (i.e., $\alpha/2\pi$ is irrational), the orbit of any point under repeated rotation by $\alpha$ is dense in the circle. This means that for any starting point $x$ and any target point on the circle, there are iterates $g^k x$ arbitrarily close to that target. In contrast, if $\alpha$ is a rational multiple of $2\pi$, the orbit is periodic and visits only finitely many points. The result follows from Poincaré's recurrence theorem combined with the fact that the orbit cannot be finite.
