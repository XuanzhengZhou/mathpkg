This lemma relates the instantaneous rate of change of volume under a flow to the integral of the divergence of the vector field. For a system of ordinary differential equations $\dot{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x})$ generating a flow $g^t$, the derivative of the volume $v(t)$ of an evolving region $D(t) = g^t D(0)$ at time $t = 0$ equals the integral of $\text{div } \mathbf{f}$ over the initial region $D(0)$. The proof uses the change-of-variables formula for multiple integrals and the expansion of the Jacobian determinant to first order in $t$.
