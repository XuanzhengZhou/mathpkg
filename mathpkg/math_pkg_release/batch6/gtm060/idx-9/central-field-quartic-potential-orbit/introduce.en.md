This problem explores the qualitative behavior of orbits in a central force field with potential energy proportional to $r^4$. Unlike the Kepler problem ($U \propto -1/r$) or the harmonic oscillator ($U \propto r^2$), the quartic potential produces an interesting dichotomy: every bounded orbit is either periodic (closed) or densely fills an annular region between two concentric circles. The result follows from applying Poincaré's recurrence theorem to the reduced one-dimensional effective potential problem, combined with the fact that bounded motion in one degree of freedom is either periodic or quasiperiodic with dense orbits.
