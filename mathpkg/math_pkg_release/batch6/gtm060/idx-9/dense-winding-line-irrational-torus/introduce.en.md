This theorem describes the behavior of a constant linear flow on a two-dimensional torus. When the ratio of the two angular velocities $\alpha_1/\alpha_2$ is irrational, the trajectory -- called a winding line -- is dense in the torus. This means it passes arbitrarily close to every point on the torus. The system is divergence-free, so Liouville's theorem guarantees volume preservation, and Poincaré's recurrence theorem then implies that the winding line is recurrent. The irrationality of the frequency ratio prevents the trajectory from being periodic, forcing it to densely fill the entire torus.
