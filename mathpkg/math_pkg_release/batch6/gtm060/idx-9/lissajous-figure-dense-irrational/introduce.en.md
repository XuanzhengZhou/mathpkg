This problem explores the geometric structure of Lissajous figures, which are parametric curves arising from two harmonic oscillations with different frequencies. When the frequency ratio $\omega$ is irrational, the curve $(x, y) = (\cos t, \cos \omega t)$ is dense in the square $[-1, 1] \times [-1, 1]$. This is a direct consequence of the dense winding line theorem on the torus: the map $(t, \omega t) \bmod 2\pi$ is a dense flow on the torus $T^2$, and the cosine map $(\varphi_1, \varphi_2) \mapsto (\cos \varphi_1, \cos \varphi_2)$ is continuous and surjective onto the square, so the image of a dense set is dense.
