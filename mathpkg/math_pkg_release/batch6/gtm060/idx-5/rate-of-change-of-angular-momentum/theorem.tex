The rate of change of the angular momentum of a system is equal to the sum of the moments of the external forces acting on the points of the system:
$$\frac{d\mathbf{M}}{dt} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} [\mathbf{r}_i, \mathbf{F}'_i] = \mathbf{N},$$
where $\mathbf{N}$ denotes the total moment of the external forces.
