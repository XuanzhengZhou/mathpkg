**Proof.** Denote the total moment of external forces by $\mathbf{N} = \sum_i [\mathbf{r}_i, \mathbf{F}'_i]$. The theorem on angular momentum gives $d\mathbf{M}/dt = \mathbf{N}$. Taking the $z$-component:
$$\frac{dM_z}{dt} = N_z = \sum_i (\mathbf{e}_z, [\mathbf{r}_i, \mathbf{F}'_i]).$$

If $N_z = 0$, then $dM_z/dt = 0$, so $M_z = \text{const}$.
