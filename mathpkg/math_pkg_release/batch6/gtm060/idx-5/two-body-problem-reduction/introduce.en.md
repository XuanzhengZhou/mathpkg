The two-body problem is a fundamental problem in classical mechanics. By separating the motion into the center-of-mass motion (which is uniform and linear for a closed system) and the relative motion, the problem reduces to an equivalent one-body problem with a reduced mass. This reduction is essential for analyzing planetary motion, binary star systems, and scattering problems.
