This is the most general formulation of the law of conservation of energy. It states that while mechanical energy may not be conserved in non-conservative systems (due to friction, dissipation, etc.), the sum of mechanical and non-mechanical energy (thermal, chemical, etc.) is always conserved. The value of this formulation lies in its applicability to concrete physical systems where non-mechanical energy can be expressed in terms of measurable physical quantities like temperature.
