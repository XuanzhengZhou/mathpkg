**Proof.** By definition of non-mechanical energy, $E'(t_1) - E'(t_0) = E(t_0) - E(t_1)$. Rearranging gives $E(t_1) + E'(t_1) = E(t_0) + E'(t_0)$, i.e., $H(t_1) = H(t_0)$, where $H = E + E'$ is the total energy. This is an immediate corollary of the definition of non-mechanical energy, and its value lies in the fact that in concrete physical systems, expressions for non-mechanical energy can be found in terms of other physical quantities (temperature, etc.).
