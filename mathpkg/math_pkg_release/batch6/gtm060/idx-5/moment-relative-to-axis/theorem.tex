Let $z$ be an axis oriented by the unit vector $\mathbf{e}_z$ in $E^3$, let $\mathbf{F}$ be a vector in $\mathbb{R}^3$, let $0$ be a point on the $z$ axis, and let $\mathbf{r} = x - 0 \in \mathbb{R}^3$ be the radius vector of the point $x \in E^3$ relative to $0$. The \textit{moment} $M_z$ of the vector $\mathbf{F}$ applied at the point $\mathbf{r}$ relative to the $z$ axis is the projection onto the $z$ axis of the moment of $\mathbf{F}$ relative to some point on this axis:
$$M_z = (\mathbf{e}_z, [\mathbf{r}, \mathbf{F}]).$$

The number $M_z$ does not depend on the choice of the point $0$ on the $z$ axis.
