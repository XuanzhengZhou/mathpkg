**Proof.** By properties of the scalar triple product,
$$(\mathbf{M}, \mathbf{r}) = ([\mathbf{r}, \dot{\mathbf{r}}], \mathbf{r}) = 0,$$
so $\mathbf{r}(t) \perp \mathbf{M}$ for all $t$. Since $\mathbf{M} = \text{const}$ in a central field, all orbits lie in the plane perpendicular to the constant vector $\mathbf{M}$.
