A central field in three-dimensional space has spherical symmetry around the center of force. The conservation of angular momentum $\mathbf{M}$ forces the position vector $\mathbf{r}(t)$ to remain perpendicular to $\mathbf{M}$ at all times, confining the motion to a fixed plane. This reduces the three-dimensional central force problem to the two-dimensional problem studied in earlier sections.
