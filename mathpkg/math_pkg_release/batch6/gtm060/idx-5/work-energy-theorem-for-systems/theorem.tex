The increase in the kinetic energy of a system is equal to the sum of the work of all forces acting on the points of the system:
$$\frac{dT}{dt} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (\dot{\mathbf{r}}_i, \mathbf{F}_i).$$

Equivalently, in integral form:
$$T(t_1) - T(t_0) = \int_{\mathbf{r}(t_0)}^{\mathbf{r}(t_1)} (\mathbf{F}, d\mathbf{r}) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} (\dot{\mathbf{r}}, \mathbf{F}) \, dt,$$
where $\mathbf{r} = (\mathbf{r}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{r}_n)$ is the radius vector in configuration space $E^{3n}$ and $\mathbf{F} = (\mathbf{F}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{F}_n)$ is the force vector.
