This theorem generalizes the work-energy principle from a single particle to a system of $n$ particles. The rate of change of total kinetic energy equals the total power developed by all forces (both internal and external). In configuration space $E^{3n}$, the theorem takes an elegant form: the increase in kinetic energy equals the work of the force vector along the path in configuration space.
