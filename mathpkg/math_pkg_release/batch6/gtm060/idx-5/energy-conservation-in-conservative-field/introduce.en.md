In a conservative force field, the work done by the force depends only on the endpoints of the path, not on the path itself. This property implies the existence of a potential energy function $U(\mathbf{r})$, and the total mechanical energy $E = T + U$ (kinetic plus potential) remains constant along any trajectory. This is the fundamental law of conservation of energy for a point particle moving in three-dimensional Euclidean space.
