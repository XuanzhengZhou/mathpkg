**Proof.** Since $\ddot{\mathbf{r}} = -\partial U / \partial \mathbf{r}$, we compute the time derivative of the energy:
$$\frac{dE}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{1}{2}\dot{\mathbf{r}}^2 + U(\mathbf{r})\right) = \dot{\mathbf{r}} \cdot \ddot{\mathbf{r}} + \frac{\partial U}{\partial \mathbf{r}} \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}} = \dot{\mathbf{r}} \cdot \left(-\frac{\partial U}{\partial \mathbf{r}}\right) + \frac{\partial U}{\partial \mathbf{r}} \cdot \dot{\mathbf{r}} = 0.$$

Thus $E$ is constant along trajectories.
