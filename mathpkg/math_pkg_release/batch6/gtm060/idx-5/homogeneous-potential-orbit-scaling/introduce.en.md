This powerful result uses dimensional analysis (the method of similarity) to deduce qualitative properties of orbits without solving the equations of motion. When the potential energy is a homogeneous function, scaling the spatial coordinates scales the entire orbit. By choosing the scaling factor appropriately, one can relate the periods of different orbits. Two famous consequences are Kepler's third law ($\nu = -1$) and the isochronicity of small oscillations ($\nu = 2$).
