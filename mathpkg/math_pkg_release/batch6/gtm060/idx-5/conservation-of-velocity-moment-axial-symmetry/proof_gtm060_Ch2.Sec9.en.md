**Proof.** Let $M_z = (\mathbf{e}_z, [\mathbf{r}, \dot{\mathbf{r}}])$. Since $\ddot{\mathbf{r}} = \mathbf{F}$ and the field is axially symmetric, the vectors $\mathbf{r}$ and $\ddot{\mathbf{r}}$ lie in a plane passing through the $z$ axis; therefore $[\mathbf{r}, \ddot{\mathbf{r}}]$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{e}_z$. Differentiating:
$$\dot{M}_z = (\mathbf{e}_z, [\dot{\mathbf{r}}, \dot{\mathbf{r}}]) + (\mathbf{e}_z, [\mathbf{r}, \ddot{\mathbf{r}}]) = 0 + 0 = 0.$$

Thus $M_z$ is constant. The proof works for any force field in which the force vector $\mathbf{F}$ lies in the plane spanned by $\mathbf{r}$ and $\mathbf{e}_z$.
