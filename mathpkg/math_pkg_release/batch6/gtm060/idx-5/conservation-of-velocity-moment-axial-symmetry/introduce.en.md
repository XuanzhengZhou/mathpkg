In a conservative force field that is symmetric under rotations about a fixed axis, the component of angular momentum along that axis is conserved. This is a direct consequence of Noether's theorem: axial symmetry implies conservation of the corresponding component of angular momentum. The proof uses the fact that the force vector lies in the plane spanned by the position vector and the axis direction.
