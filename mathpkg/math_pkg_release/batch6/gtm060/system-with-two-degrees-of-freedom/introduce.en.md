A system with two degrees of freedom is governed by $\ddot{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x})$ where $\mathbf{x} \in E^2$ and $\mathbf{f}$ is a vector field on the Euclidean plane. Unlike the 1-DOF case, not every such system is conservative — a potential energy $U$ with $\mathbf{f} = -\partial U/\partial \mathbf{x}$ exists only for gradient (irrotational) vector fields.
