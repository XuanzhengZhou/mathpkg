Consider the direct product $R \times R^3$ of the $t$ axis with a three-dimensional vector space $R^3$; suppose $R^3$ has a fixed euclidean structure. Such a space has a natural galilean structure. We will call this space galilean coordinate space.

Three examples of galilean transformations of this space:
First, uniform motion with velocity $v$:
$$g_1(t, x) = (t, x + vt) \quad \forall t \in R, x \in R^3.$$
Next, translation of the origin:
$$g_2(t, x) = (t + s, x + s) \quad \forall t \in R, x \in R^3.$$
Finally, rotation of the coordinate axes:
$$g_3(t, x) = (t, Gx), \quad \forall t \in R, x \in R^3,$$
where $G: R^3 \rightarrow R^3$ is an orthogonal transformation.
