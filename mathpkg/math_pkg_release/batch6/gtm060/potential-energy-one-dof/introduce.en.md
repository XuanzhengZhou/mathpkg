For a one-degree-of-freedom system $\ddot{x} = f(x)$, a potential energy $U(x)$ always exists and is defined as the negative integral of the force function $f$. The potential energy completely determines the equation of motion since $f(x) = -dU/dx$. Adding a constant to $U$ does not affect the dynamics, reflecting the fact that only differences in potential energy are physically meaningful.
