The phase plane of a one-degree-of-freedom system is the $(x, y) = (x, \dot{x})$ plane, where the second-order equation $\ddot{x} = f(x)$ becomes a first-order system. Phase curves are integral curves of the phase velocity vector field $(y, f(x))$. The phase plane provides a complete geometric picture of all possible motions.
