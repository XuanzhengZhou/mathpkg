The equation $\ddot{x} = f(x)$ is equivalent to the system of two equations:

$$\dot{x} = y \quad \dot{y} = f(x).$$

We consider the plane with coordinates $x$ and $y$, which we call the phase plane of the equation. The points of the phase plane are called phase points. The right-hand side of the system determines a vector field on the phase plane, called the phase velocity vector field.

A solution is a motion $\varphi: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^2$ of a phase point in the phase plane, such that the velocity of the moving point at each moment of time is equal to the phase velocity vector at the location of the phase point at that moment.

The image of $\varphi$ is called the phase curve. Thus the phase curve is given by the parametric equations $x = \varphi(t), y = \dot{\varphi}(t)$.
