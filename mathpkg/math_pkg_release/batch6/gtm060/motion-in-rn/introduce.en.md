A motion in $\mathbb{R}^N$ is a differentiable path parametrized by time — a smooth mapping from a time interval $I \subset \mathbb{R}$ to the space $\mathbb{R}^N$. The image of this mapping is the trajectory or curve traced by the moving point. This definition is the starting point for the kinematic description of mechanical systems.
