A $2$-cocycle of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is a bilinear skew-symmetric form $C: \mathfrak{g} \times \mathfrak{g} \to \mathbb{R}$ satisfying a cyclic sum condition derived from the Jacobi identity. This condition is precisely the $2$-cocycle condition in the Chevalley-Eilenberg cohomology of Lie algebras. In the context of symplectic geometry, the cocycle $C(a,b)$ arises naturally as the obstruction to the Poisson action defining a genuine Lie algebra homomorphism into the Poisson algebra of Hamiltonian functions.
