On a hypersurface $E^{2n}$ transverse to the contact planes, the Lie derivative of the contact form $\alpha$ along the characteristic vector field $\xi$ is proportional to $\alpha$ itself: $L_\xi \alpha = c\alpha$. This relation implies that the evolution of the $1$-form $\alpha$ applied to any transported tangent vector $\eta(t)$ satisfies a first-order linear differential equation $\frac{dy}{dt} = c(t)y(t)$. Consequently, if a vector is initially tangent to an integral manifold of the contact field, it remains so under the characteristic flow.
