A symplectic action of a Lie group $G$ with single-valued Hamiltonians defines a distinguished $2$-dimensional cohomology class in $H^2(\mathfrak{g}; \mathbb{R})$. This class is independent of the choice of additive constants in the Hamiltonian functions, since changing these constants modifies the cocycle by a coboundary. This cohomological invariant classifies Poisson actions and is the fundamental obstruction to the existence of a moment map (a genuine Lie algebra homomorphism into the Poisson algebra).
