On a hypersurface $E^{2n}$ in a contact manifold $M^{2n+1}$, the contact structure induces a distinguished vector field called the field of characteristic directions. At each point $x \in E^{2n}$, the characteristic vector $\xi$ is the unique vector in the contact plane satisfying $d\Phi(\cdot) = \omega(\xi, \cdot)$, where $\Phi = 0$ locally defines $E$ and $\omega = d\alpha$ is the symplectic form on the contact plane. This geometric construction reduces the integration of first-order nonlinear PDEs to the integration of a system of ordinary differential equations along the characteristic directions.
