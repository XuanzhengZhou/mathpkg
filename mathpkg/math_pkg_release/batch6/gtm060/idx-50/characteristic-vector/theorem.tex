Let $M^{2n+1}$ be a contact manifold with contact form $\alpha$ ($\omega = d\alpha$), and let $E^{2n}$ be a hypersurface in $M^{2n+1}$ transverse to the contact planes at all its points. Let $\Phi = 0$ be a local equation of $E^{2n}$ (so $d\Phi \neq 0$ at $x \in E^{2n}$). The restriction of $d\Phi$ to the contact plane $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ at $x$ defines a nonzero linear form on $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$. The $2$-form $\omega$ gives $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ the structure of a symplectic vector space and thus an isomorphism of this space with its dual. The nonzero $1$-form $d\Phi|_{\mathbb{R}^{2n}}$ corresponds to a nonzero vector $\xi \in \mathbb{R}^{2n}$ such that

$$d\Phi(\cdot) = \omega(\xi, \cdot).$$

The vector $\xi$ is called the \textit{characteristic vector} of the manifold $E^{2n}$ at the point $x$. The characteristic vector lies in the intersection of $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ with the tangent plane to $E^{2n}$, since $d\Phi(\xi) = \omega(\xi,\xi) = 0$.
