Two $2$-cocycles $C$ and $C'$ are cohomologous if they differ by a coboundary, i.e., $C'(a,b) = C(a,b) + \rho([a,b])$ for some linear functional $\rho$ on the Lie algebra. In the context of symplectic actions, changing the choice of additive constants in the Hamiltonian functions modifies the cocycle by exactly such a coboundary, so the cohomology class of the cocycle is an invariant of the Poisson action.
