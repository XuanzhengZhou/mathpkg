From a Poisson action of a Lie group $G$ with single-valued Hamiltonians, one obtains a linear map from the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ to the Lie algebra of Hamiltonian functions on $M$. The constants of integration can be chosen to make this dependence linear by fixing Hamiltonians on a basis of $\mathfrak{g}$ and extending by linearity. This construction is a precursor to the modern notion of the moment map, and the failure of this map to be a Lie algebra homomorphism is measured by a $2$-cocycle.
