The Hamiltonian function $H_{[a,b]}$ associated to the commutator of two elements $a,b$ of the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is equal to the Poisson bracket $(H_a, H_b)$, or else differs from this Poisson bracket by a constant:

$$H_{[a,b]} = (H_a, H_b) + C(a,b)$$

where $C(a,b)$ is a bilinear skew-symmetric function on the Lie algebra. The appearance of the constant $C$ is a consequence of the existence of a two-dimensional cohomology class of the Lie algebra of (globally) Hamiltonian fields.
