Lagrange's equations are the Euler-Lagrange equations applied to the mechanical Lagrangian $L = T - U$. They are second-order differential equations in the generalized coordinates $q$. Unlike Newton's equations, they are form-invariant under arbitrary coordinate transformations (they are covariant), which makes them particularly powerful for systems with constraints or in curvilinear coordinates. The quantities $p_i = \partial L/\partial \dot{q}_i$ are the generalized momenta.
