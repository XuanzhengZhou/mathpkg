This formulation follows directly from the normal form $$\omega = x\,dy + dz$$ of Darboux's theorem. In the coordinates $$(x, y, z)$$, the contact hyperplane at a point is given by the kernel of $$\omega = x\,dy + dz$$, which can be interpreted as the hyperplane element with coordinates $$(x, y)$$ passing through the point $$(y, z)$$. Thus the local model is precisely the manifold of contact elements of $$m$$-dimensional space, where $$m = n+1$$.
