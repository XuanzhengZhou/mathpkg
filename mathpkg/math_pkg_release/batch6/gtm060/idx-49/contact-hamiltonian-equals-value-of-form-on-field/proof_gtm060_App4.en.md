We use the expression for the increment of the ordinary Hamiltonian function over a path in terms of the vector field and the symplectic structure (Section 48C). For this we draw a vertical interval $$\{\lambda B\}$$, $$0 < \lambda \leq 1$$, through the point $$B$$ of the symplectification at which we want to calculate the Hamiltonian function. The translations of this interval over small time $$\tau$$ under the action of the symplectified flow defined by our field $$X$$, fill out a two-dimensional region $$\sigma(\tau)$$. The value of the Hamiltonian at the point $$B$$ is equal to the limit

$$H(B) = \lim_{\tau \to 0} \tau^{-1} \iint_{\sigma(\tau)} d\alpha,$$

since $$H(\lambda B) \to 0$$ as $$\lambda \to 0$$. But the integral of the form $$d\alpha$$ over the region is the integral of the 1-form $$\alpha$$ along the edge formed by the trajectory of the point $$B$$ (the other parts of the boundary give zero integrals). Therefore, the double integral is simply the integral of the 1-form $$\alpha$$ along the interval of trajectories, and the limit is the value of $$\alpha$$ on the velocity vector $$Y$$ of the symplectified field. Thus $$K(\pi B) = H(B) = \alpha(Y) = \omega(X)$$, as was to be shown.
