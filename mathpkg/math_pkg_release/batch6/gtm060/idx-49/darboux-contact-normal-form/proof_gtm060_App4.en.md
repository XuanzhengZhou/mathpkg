This is the third formulation of Darboux's theorem and implies the other two. The proof proceeds by symplectification, applying the symplectic Darboux theorem to obtain coordinates $$(p_0, q_0, \ldots, p_n, q_n)$$ with $$d\alpha = \sum dp_i \wedge dq_i$$, then restricting to the surface $$p_0 = 0$$ and projecting coordinates $$p_1, \ldots, p_n; q_0; q_1, \ldots, q_n$$ to the contact manifold, obtaining the normal form $$\omega = x\,dy + dz$$ with $$x_i = p_i$$, $$y_i = q_i$$, $$z = q_0$$.
