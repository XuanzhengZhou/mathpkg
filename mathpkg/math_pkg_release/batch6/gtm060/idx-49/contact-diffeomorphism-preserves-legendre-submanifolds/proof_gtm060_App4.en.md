Under the action of the Legendre involution, the Legendre manifold of plane elements tangent to the graph of a function is carried into a new Legendre manifold. This new manifold is called the Legendre transform of the original manifold. More generally, a diffeomorphism carrying contact planes to contact planes preserves the property of being an integral submanifold of maximal dimension (a Legendre submanifold). The proof follows from the fact that Legendre submanifolds are defined intrinsically by the contact structure: they are integral submanifolds of the contact distribution of maximal possible dimension $$n$$ in a $$(2n+1)$$-dimensional contact manifold.
