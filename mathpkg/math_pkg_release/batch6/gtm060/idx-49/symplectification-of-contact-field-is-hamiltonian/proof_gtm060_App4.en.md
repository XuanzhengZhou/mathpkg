The fact that symplectifications of contact diffeomorphisms are symplectic implies that the symplectification of a contact field is Hamiltonian. The homogeneity of the Hamiltonian follows from the homogeneity of symplectic diffeomorphisms (from commutativity with multiplication by $$\lambda$$). Thus the first assertion of the theorem follows from the theorem on symplectifications of contact diffeomorphisms. The second part follows in the same way from the theorem on homogeneous symplectic diffeomorphisms.
