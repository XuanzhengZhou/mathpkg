\textbf{Young's Inequality.} If $f$ and $g$ are dual in the sense of Young (i.e., Legendre transforms of each other), then for all $x$ and $p$:
$$px \leq f(x) + g(p).$$

\textbf{Example 1.} For $f(x) = \frac{1}{2}x^2$, $g(p) = \frac{1}{2}p^2$: $px \leq \frac{1}{2}x^2 + \frac{1}{2}p^2$.

\textbf{Example 2.} For $f(x) = x^\alpha/\alpha$, $g(p) = p^\beta/\beta$ with $1/\alpha + 1/\beta = 1$: $px \leq x^\alpha/\alpha + p^\beta/\beta$.
