\textbf{Theorem.} The total energy of a conservative system is conserved, i.e.,
$$\frac{dE}{dt} = 0, \quad \text{where } E = \frac{1}{2} \dot{x}^2 + U(x), \quad \dot{x}^2 = (\dot{x}, \dot{x}).$$

\textbf{Corollary.} If at the initial moment the total energy is equal to $E$, then all trajectories lie in the region where $U(x) \leq E$, i.e., a point remains inside the potential well $U(x_1, x_2) \leq E$ for all time.
