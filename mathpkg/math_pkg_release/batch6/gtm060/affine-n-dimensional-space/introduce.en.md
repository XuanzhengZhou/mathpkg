An affine $n$-dimensional space $A^n$ is a set on which the additive group of the vector space $\mathbb{R}^n$ acts freely and transitively by parallel displacements. Unlike $\mathbb{R}^n$, affine space has no distinguished origin; the difference of two points is a well-defined vector, but their sum is not. Affine spaces provide the natural geometric setting for classical mechanics before a coordinate system is chosen.
