A phase curve consisting of only one point is called an equilibrium position. The vector of phase velocity at an equilibrium position is zero.

Any equilibrium position of $\dot{x} = y, \dot{y} = f(x)$ must lie on the $x$ axis of the phase plane. The point $x = \xi, y = 0$ is an equilibrium position if $\xi$ is a critical point of the potential energy, i.e., if $(\partial U/\partial x)|_{x=\xi} = 0$.

The local maximum points of the potential energy are unstable, but the minimum points are stable equilibrium positions.
