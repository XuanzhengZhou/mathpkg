The effective potential $V(r) = U(r) + M^2/(2r^2)$ combines the physical potential with a centrifugal term arising from angular momentum conservation. The 1D energy $E_1 = \dot{r}^2/2 + V(r)$ equals the original total energy. Analysis of $V(r)$ reveals the qualitative features of orbits: bounded regions, circular orbits (minima of $V$), and escape conditions.
