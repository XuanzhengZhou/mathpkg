A small calculation using the discriminant of the characteristic equation shows that, to first order, the difference of the two eigenvalues behaves like the square root of a quadratic form in the parameter deviations from the singular point. Explicitly, near a point where $\lambda_1 = \lambda_2$, the two eigenvalues satisfy $(\lambda_1 - \lambda_2)^2 = Q(p_1, p_2)$ where $Q$ is a positive-definite quadratic form in the two parameter coordinates. Thus the graph $\{(\lambda_1, \lambda_2, p_1, p_2)\}$ projects to the parameter plane as a two-sheeted covering branched at the singular point, forming a two-sheeted cone.
