Near a point in a two-parameter family where two eigenvalues coincide, the eigenvalue surfaces form a double cone (like the surface $z^2 = x^2 + y^2$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$). The apex of the cone is the degenerate point where the eigenvalues are equal. This conical structure explains why a one-parameter subfamily passing near such a point sees the eigenvalues approach and then repel — the curve avoids the apex and traces a hyperbola-like path on the cone.
