Since ellipsoids of revolution form a subset of codimension at least 2 in the manifold of all ellipsoids, a generic one-parameter curve (which has dimension 1) cannot intersect this subset. Hence a typical continuous deformation depending on one parameter preserves the property that all eigenvalues are distinct. This explains why, in general, characteristic frequencies avoid crossing when a single parameter is varied.
