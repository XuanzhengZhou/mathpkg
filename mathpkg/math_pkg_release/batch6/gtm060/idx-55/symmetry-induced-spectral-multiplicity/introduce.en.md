While a generic system has simple spectrum, symmetries can force eigenvalue multiplicities that are robust under symmetric perturbations. For a system with $C_3$ rotational symmetry (three identical masses at the vertices of an equilateral triangle), the configuration space decomposes into invariant subspaces: one-dimensional subspaces of symmetric modes and two-dimensional subspaces where the rotation cyclically permutes independent eigenmodes sharing the same frequency. The multiplicity is thus forced by the representation theory of the symmetry group.
