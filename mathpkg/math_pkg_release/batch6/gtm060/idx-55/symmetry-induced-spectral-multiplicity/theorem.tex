For an ellipsoid invariant under an orthogonal transformation $g$ of order three (a $120^\circ$ rotation), one may choose the principal axes so that each axis is either fixed under $g$ or is rotated by $120^\circ$ in an invariant plane spanned by it and another axis (orthogonal to it and of the same length). Consequently, characteristic oscillations of a system with third-order rotational symmetry are of two types: those invariant under the rotation ($g\xi = \xi$) and those passing under such a rotation to independent characteristic oscillations with the same frequency ($\xi$ and $g\xi$ independent). In a configuration space of dimension $6$ (three identical particles in the plane), the representation decomposes as the orthogonal direct sum of two invariant lines (symmetric oscillations) and two invariant two-dimensional planes (non-symmetric oscillations, each pair sharing the same eigenvalue and obtained from each other by $120^\circ$ rotation).
