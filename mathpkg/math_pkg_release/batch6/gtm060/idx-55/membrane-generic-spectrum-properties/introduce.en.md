This proposition extends the finite-dimensional conclusions about ellipsoids of revolution to infinite-dimensional systems describing continuous media. The kinetic energy defines a Euclidean structure on the function space of displacements, and the potential energy (Dirichlet integral) defines a quadratic form. Assuming transversality, the codimension-2 phenomenon persists: generic membranes have simple spectra, and varying $k$ parameters is needed to create a $k$-fold multiplicity. These conclusions have been rigorously proven by K. Uhlenbeck (Amer. J. Math. 98, 1976).
