This corollary explains the phenomenon of "avoided crossing" in quantum mechanics and classical oscillation theory. Because the eigenvalue graph over a two-parameter space forms a two-sheeted cone at a multiplicity point, any one-parameter curve passing near the singular point traces a hyperbola-like path on the cone: the eigenvalues approach, reach a minimum separation, and then diverge. They cannot actually cross (collide) because the cone's vertex is at codimension 2 and a generic curve avoids it.
