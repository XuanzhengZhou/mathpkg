This proposition establishes that the ordered list of characteristic frequencies is a topological invariant under deformation, independent of the specific path taken, as long as the path avoids multiple spectra. The eigenfunctions, however, exhibit a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ monodromy: traversing a closed loop around the codimension-2 set of multiply-spectral membranes can flip the sign of a characteristic function without changing its frequency. This is an early example of what would later be understood in terms of Berry's phase.
