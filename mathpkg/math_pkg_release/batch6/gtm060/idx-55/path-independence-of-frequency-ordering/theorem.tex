If one starts from a membrane with a simple spectrum and continuously deforms it to another membrane with a simple spectrum along any path in general position, then as a result the $k$-th largest characteristic frequency of the second membrane is always obtained, independently of the deformation path, from the $k$-th largest characteristic frequency of the original membrane. The continuations of the characteristic functions, however, do generally depend on the path of deformation: by changing the path, the sign of the resulting characteristic function can be changed.

In particular, if starting from a membrane with a simple spectrum one describes a closed path in the space of membranes, bypassing the set of membranes with multiple spectra (which has codimension 2), then the $k$-th characteristic frequency returns to its original value, while the $k$-th characteristic function may change sign.
