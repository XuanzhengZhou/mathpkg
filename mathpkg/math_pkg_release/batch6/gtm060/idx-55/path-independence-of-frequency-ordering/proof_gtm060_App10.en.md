Since the set of membranes with multiple spectra has codimension 2 in the space of all membranes (by the infinite-dimensional analogue of Theorem 1, rigorously established by K. Uhlenbeck), the complement of this set (the space of membranes with simple spectra) is connected. Any two generic paths between the same endpoints can be deformed into each other while avoiding the codimension-2 subset, so the continuous assignment of the $k$-th eigenvalue along each path yields the same result.

The sign ambiguity of the characteristic function arises because the real line bundle of eigenfunctions over the space of simple-spectrum membranes may have nontrivial monodromy around the codimension-2 set. Traversing a loop encircling a multiplicity point (where two eigenvalues coincide) interchanges the two corresponding eigenfunctions or, in the real case, can reverse the sign of one eigenfunction. This is analogous to the fact that the fundamental group of the complement of a codimension-2 submanifold in a simply-connected space can be nontrivial, allowing $\mathbb{Z}_2$ holonomy.
