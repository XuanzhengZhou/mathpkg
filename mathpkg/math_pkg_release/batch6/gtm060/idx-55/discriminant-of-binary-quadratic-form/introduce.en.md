The discriminant $\Delta$ of a binary quadratic form $Ax^2 + 2Bxy + Cy^2$ factorises as a sum of two squares: $\Delta = (A-C)^2 + 4B^2$. This reveals that the vanishing of $\Delta$, which signals equal eigenvalues, imposes two independent conditions rather than one. Geometrically, the set of circles in the 3-dimensional space of ellipses is a line (1-dimensional), not a surface (2-dimensional).
