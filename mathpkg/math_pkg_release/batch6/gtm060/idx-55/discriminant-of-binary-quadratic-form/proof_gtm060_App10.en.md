Expand the discriminant:

$$\Delta = (A + C)^2 - 4(AC - B^2) = A^2 + 2AC + C^2 - 4AC + 4B^2 = A^2 - 2AC + C^2 + 4B^2 = (A - C)^2 + 4B^2.$$

Since $(A-C)^2 \ge 0$ and $4B^2 \ge 0$, the equation $\Delta = 0$ implies both $A - C = 0$ and $B = 0$. Hence $\Delta = 0$ is equivalent to the two independent conditions $A = C$ and $B = 0$, which define a line ($A = C$, $B = 0$ with $A$ free) in the three-dimensional space of coefficients $(A,B,C)$.

In the context of ellipses in the plane, this means the manifold of circles has codimension 2 in the manifold of ellipses: circles form a line in a three-dimensional space, not a surface.
