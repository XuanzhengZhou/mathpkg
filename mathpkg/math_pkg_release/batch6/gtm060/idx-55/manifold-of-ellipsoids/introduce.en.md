The manifold of ellipsoids centered at the origin in $\mathbb{R}^n$ is identified with the set of positive-definite quadratic forms. This identification endows the space of ellipsoids with a smooth manifold structure of dimension $n(n+1)/2$, covered by a single chart given by a region in the vector space of all quadratic forms. This geometric viewpoint is fundamental for studying how eigenvalues and axis multiplicities behave under deformations.
