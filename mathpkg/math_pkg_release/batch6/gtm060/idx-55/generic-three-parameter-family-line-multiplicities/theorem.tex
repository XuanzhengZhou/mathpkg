In a typical three-parameter family of ellipsoids, ellipsoids of revolution are encountered only on certain lines in the three-dimensional parameter space. The parameter space exhibits two types of singularities: transversal intersections of two branches along some curve, and conic singularities at isolated points (not lying on this curve), corresponding to ellipsoids with three equal axes. These conic singularities have the following structure: intersecting the three-dimensional manifold of ellipsoids of revolution with a small four-dimensional sphere centered at the singular point yields two copies of the projective plane $\mathbb{R}P^2$, embedded in $S^4$ via the five spherical harmonics of degree two.
