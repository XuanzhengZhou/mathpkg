By Theorem 1, the set of ellipsoids of revolution has codimension 2. A generic three-dimensional submanifold intersects a codimension-2 submanifold along a curve (dimension $3 - 2 = 1$). The conic singularities at isolated points arise from the further degeneracy where three axes coincide (codimension 5 within the full manifold). At such a point, intersecting with a small 4-sphere yields an embedding of two copies of $\mathbb{R}P^2$, diffeomorphic to the embedding given by the five spherical harmonics of degree two on $S^2$ (the five linear combinations of $x_i x_j$, orthonormal in $L^2(S^2)$ and orthogonal to the constant function, give an even mapping $S^2 \to S^4$ and hence an embedding $\mathbb{R}P^2 \to S^4$).
