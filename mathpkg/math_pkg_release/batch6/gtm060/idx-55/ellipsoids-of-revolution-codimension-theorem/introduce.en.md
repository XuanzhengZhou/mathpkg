This theorem states that in the manifold of all ellipsoids centered at the origin (of dimension $n(n+1)/2$), the subset of ellipsoids that possess at least one pair of equal axes -- the ellipsoids of revolution -- is not a hypersurface but rather has codimension at least 2. This geometric fact has profound consequences for the behavior of eigenvalues under parameter variation: generic one-parameter families avoid multiple eigenvalues entirely, and generic two-parameter families encounter them only at isolated points.
