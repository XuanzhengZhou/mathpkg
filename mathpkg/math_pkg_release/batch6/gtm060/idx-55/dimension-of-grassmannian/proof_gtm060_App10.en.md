A $k$-dimensional plane in general position in an $n$-dimensional space can be thought of as the graph of a mapping from a $k$-dimensional space to an $(n - k)$-dimensional space. Such a mapping is given by a rectangular $k \times (n - k)$ matrix, which has $k(n - k)$ independent entries. These entries serve as local coordinates on the Grassmannian near the chosen $k$-plane, establishing the dimension as $k(n - k)$.
