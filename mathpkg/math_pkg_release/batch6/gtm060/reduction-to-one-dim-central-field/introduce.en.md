Angular momentum conservation reduces two-dimensional central force motion to a one-dimensional problem for the radial coordinate. The effective potential $V(r) = U(r) + M^2/(2r^2)$ includes a centrifugal barrier term $M^2/(2r^2)$ that prevents the particle from reaching the origin (for $M \neq 0$). This reduction is the key to solving the Kepler problem and understanding the qualitative features of bounded and unbounded orbits in any central field.
