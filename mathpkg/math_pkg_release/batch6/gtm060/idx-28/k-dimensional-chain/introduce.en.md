A $k$-dimensional chain is a formal integer-linear combination of oriented singular cells. Chains generalize the notion of an integration contour: just as a 1-dimensional contour may consist of several curve segments traversed in either direction, possibly with multiplicities, a $k$-chain allows the integration domain to be built from multiple $k$-dimensional pieces. The set of all $k$-chains forms an abelian group, the chain group, which is the domain of the boundary operator in homology theory.
