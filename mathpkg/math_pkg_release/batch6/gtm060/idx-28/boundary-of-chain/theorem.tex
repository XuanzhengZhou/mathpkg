The boundary $\partial c_k$ of the $k$-dimensional chain $c_k = m_1 \sigma_1 + \cdots + m_r \sigma_r$ in $M$ is defined as the sum of the boundaries of the cells of $c_k$ with multiplicities:
$$\partial c_k = \partial(m_1 \sigma_1 + \cdots + m_r \sigma_r) = m_1 \partial \sigma_1 + \cdots + m_r \partial \sigma_r.$$
Thus $\partial c_k$ is a $(k-1)$-chain on $M$, and $\partial$ is a linear operator from the group of $k$-chains to the group of $(k-1)$-chains.
