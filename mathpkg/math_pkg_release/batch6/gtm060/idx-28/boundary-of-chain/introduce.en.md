The boundary of a $k$-chain is defined by extending the boundary operation from singular cells linearly over integer multiplicities. This makes $\partial$ a group homomorphism from the $k$-chain group to the $(k-1)$-chain group. Together with the chain groups, the boundary operator forms the chain complex that underpins singular homology theory, and it is the operator that appears in the statement of Stokes' theorem: $\int_{c} d\omega = \int_{\partial c} \omega$.
