This theorem provides the canonical coordinate expression for any differential $k$-form on $\mathbb{R}^n$. At each point $x$, the $n$ basic 1-forms $dx_1, \ldots, dx_n$ form a basis of the cotangent space, and their exterior products $dx_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_{i_k}$ (with $i_1 < \cdots < i_k$) form a basis of the space of exterior $k$-forms. The coefficients $a_{i_1,\ldots,i_k}(x)$ are smooth functions that encode the dependence of the form on the base point. Uniqueness follows from the linear independence of the basic wedge products.
