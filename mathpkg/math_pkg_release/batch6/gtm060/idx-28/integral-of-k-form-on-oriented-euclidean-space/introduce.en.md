In the special case of $\mathbb{R}^k$ with an oriented coordinate system, every $k$-form is a scalar multiple of the standard volume form $dx_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_k$. The integral of the $k$-form over a bounded convex polyhedron $D$ reduces to the ordinary Riemann integral of the coefficient function $\varphi(x)$ over $D$. This definition aligns with the general paradigm of approximating the integration domain by tangent parallelepipeds, since in this case the tangent space is naturally identified with the manifold itself.
