The integral of a $k$-form $\omega$ over a $k$-dimensional singular cell $\sigma = (D, f, \text{Or})$ is defined by pulling the form back to the Euclidean polyhedron $D$ via $f^*$ and then integrating as usual. This elegant definition reduces integration on a curved manifold to integration on a flat Euclidean domain, using the pullback to translate the geometric data. The result depends linearly on the form and changes sign when the orientation of the cell is reversed.
