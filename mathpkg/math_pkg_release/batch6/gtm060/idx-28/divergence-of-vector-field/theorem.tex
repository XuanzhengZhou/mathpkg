Let $A$ be a vector field on the oriented Euclidean three-space $\mathbb{R}^3$, and let $S$ be the boundary of a parallelepiped $\Pi$ with edges $\xi_1, \xi_2, \xi_3$ at the vertex $x$. Consider the outward flux of the field $A$ through the surface $S$:
$$F(\Pi) = \int_S (A, d\mathbf{n}).$$
The divergence, $\operatorname{div} A$, of the field $A$ at $x$ is defined as the limit
$$\operatorname{div} A = \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0} \frac{F(\varepsilon \Pi)}{\varepsilon^3 V},$$
where $\varepsilon \Pi$ is the parallelepiped with edges $\varepsilon \xi_1, \varepsilon \xi_2, \varepsilon \xi_3$ and $V = (\xi_1, \xi_2, \xi_3)$ is the volume of $\Pi$. This limit does not depend on the choice of the parallelepiped $\Pi$ but only on the point $x$.
