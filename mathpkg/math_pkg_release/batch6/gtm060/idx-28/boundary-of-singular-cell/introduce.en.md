The boundary of a $k$-dimensional singular cell $\sigma = (D, f, \text{Or})$ is a $(k-1)$-chain obtained by restricting the parametrization $f$ to each $(k-1)$-dimensional face of the polyhedron $D$, with induced orientations from the outward normal rule. This construction lifts the boundary operation from the Euclidean model $D$ to the manifold $M$ via the map $f$, and is the key step in defining the boundary operator $\partial$ on chains, which is fundamental to homology theory and Stokes' theorem.
