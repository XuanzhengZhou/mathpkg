Let $D$ be a convex oriented $k$-dimensional polyhedron in $k$-dimensional Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^k$. The boundary of $D$ is the $(k-1)$-chain $\partial D$ on $\mathbb{R}^k$ consisting of the $(k-1)$-dimensional faces $D_i$ of $D$, each taken with an orientation chosen by the following rule: construct a vector $n$ outwardly normal to the polyhedron $D$. An orienting frame for the face $D_i$ is a frame $f_1, \ldots, f_{k-1}$ on $D_i$ such that the frame $(n, f_1, \ldots, f_{k-1})$ is oriented correctly (i.e., the same way as the frame $e_1, \ldots, e_k$ of $\mathbb{R}^k$).
