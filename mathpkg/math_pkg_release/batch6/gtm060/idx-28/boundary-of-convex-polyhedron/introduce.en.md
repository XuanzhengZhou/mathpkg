The boundary of a convex oriented $k$-dimensional polyhedron $D$ is a $(k-1)$-chain whose constituent cells are the $(k-1)$-dimensional faces of $D$. Each face is assigned an induced orientation via the outward normal rule: a frame on the face is positively oriented if, when preceded by the outward normal vector, it yields the positive orientation of the ambient $\mathbb{R}^k$. This orientation convention is essential for Stokes' theorem, ensuring that adjacent faces cancel out when computing the boundary of a boundary.
