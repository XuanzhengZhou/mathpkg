The negative of a singular cell is the same geometric object with the opposite orientation. It serves as the formal inverse in the chain group: integrating a form over $-\sigma$ yields the negative of the integral over $\sigma$. This sign rule reflects the geometric fact that reversing the orientation of the domain of integration flips the sign of the resulting integral.
