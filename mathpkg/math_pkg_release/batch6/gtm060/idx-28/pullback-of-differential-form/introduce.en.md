The pullback $f^*\omega$ transports a differential $k$-form $\omega$ from a manifold $N$ backward along a smooth map $f: M \to N$ to produce a $k$-form on $M$. Its value on tangent vectors at $x$ is defined by evaluating $\omega$ on the forward images of those vectors under the derivative map $f_*$. The pullback is contravariantly functorial: $(g \circ f)^* = f^* \circ g^*$, and it preserves the algebraic operations of addition and wedge product, making it the natural way to compare forms on different manifolds.
