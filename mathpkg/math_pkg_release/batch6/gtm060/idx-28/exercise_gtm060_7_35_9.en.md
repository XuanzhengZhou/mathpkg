Show that the set of all $k$-chains on $M$ forms a commutative group if we define the addition of chains by the formula
$$(m_1 \sigma_1 + \cdots + m_r \sigma_r) + (m'_1 \sigma'_1 + \cdots + m'_r \sigma'_r) = m_1 \sigma_1 + \cdots + m_r \sigma_r + m'_1 \sigma'_1 + \cdots + m'_r \sigma'_r.$$
