A $k$-dimensional cell $\sigma$ of a manifold $M$ (also called a singular $k$-dimensional polyhedron) is represented by a triple $\sigma = (D, f, \text{Or})$ consisting of
\begin{enumerate}
\item a convex polyhedron $D \subset \mathbb{R}^k$,
\item a differentiable map $f: D \to M$, and
\item an orientation on $\mathbb{R}^k$, denoted by Or.
\end{enumerate}
