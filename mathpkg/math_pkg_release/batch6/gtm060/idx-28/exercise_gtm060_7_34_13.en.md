Find the values of the forms $dx_1, dx_2$, and $dx_3$ on the vectors $e_1, e_2$, and $e_3$.

Answer. $dx_i(e_i) = 1/\sqrt{E_i}$, the rest are zero. In particular, for cartesian coordinates $dx(e_x) = dy(e_y) = dz(e_z) = 1$; for cylindrical coordinates $dr(e_r) = dz(e_z) = 1$ and $d\varphi(e_\varphi) = 1/r$; for spherical coordinates $dR(e_R) = 1$, $d\varphi(e_\varphi) = 1/R \cos \theta$ and $d\theta(e_\theta) = 1/R$.
