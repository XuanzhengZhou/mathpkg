In an oriented Euclidean three-space, every vector $A$ corresponds to a 1-form $\omega_A^1$ and a 2-form $\omega_A^2$, defined by the conditions
$$\omega_A^1(\xi) = (A, \xi), \qquad \omega_A^2(\xi, \eta) = (A, \xi, \eta), \quad \xi, \eta \in \mathbb{R}^3,$$
where $(\cdot, \cdot)$ denotes the inner product and $(\cdot, \cdot, \cdot)$ denotes the triple scalar product. The correspondence between vector fields and forms does not depend on the system of coordinates, but only on the Euclidean structure and orientation.
