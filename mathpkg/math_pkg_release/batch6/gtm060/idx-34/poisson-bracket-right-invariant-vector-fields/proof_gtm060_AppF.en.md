The right-invariant vector fields on a Lie group $G$ are in one-to-one correspondence with tangent vectors at the identity. Let $X$ and $Y$ be right-invariant vector fields corresponding to $A, B \in TG_e$. The Poisson bracket $[X, Y]$ is a vector field on $G$. To show it is right-invariant, one uses that right translations preserve the Poisson bracket by definition. Since $X$ and $Y$ are invariant under right translations, their Poisson bracket inherits this invariance. Evaluating at the identity yields the Lie bracket: $[X, Y]_e = [A, B]$, where the right-hand side is the Lie bracket in $TG_e$ defined via one-parameter subgroups.
