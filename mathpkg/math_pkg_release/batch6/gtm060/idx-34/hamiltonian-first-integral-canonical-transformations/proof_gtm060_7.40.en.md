Since $H$ is a first integral of the flow $g_F^t$, we have $(H, F) = 0$ by definition of the Poisson bracket (the derivative of $H$ along the phase flow of $F$ vanishes). Therefore, by the skew-symmetry of the Poisson bracket (Corollary 4), $(F, H) = 0$. Hence $F$ is a first integral of the flow with hamiltonian function $H$.
