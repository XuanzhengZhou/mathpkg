A Lie group is a group that simultaneously carries the structure of a differentiable manifold, with the requirement that the group operations of multiplication and inversion are smooth maps. This definition combines algebra and differential geometry, forming the foundational object of Lie theory. Lie groups arise naturally as symmetry groups of mechanical systems, where the smooth structure captures continuous families of transformations.
