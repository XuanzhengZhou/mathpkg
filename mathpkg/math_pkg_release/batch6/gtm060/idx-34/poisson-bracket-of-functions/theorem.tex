Let $(M^{2n}, \omega^2)$ be a symplectic manifold. To a given function $H: M^{2n} \to \mathbb{R}$ on the symplectic manifold there corresponds a one-parameter group $g_H^t: M^{2n} \to M^{2n}$ of canonical transformations of $M^{2n}$---the phase flow of the hamiltonian function equal to $H$. Let $F: M^{2n} \to \mathbb{R}$ be another function on $M^{2n}$.

The Poisson bracket $(F, H)$ of functions $F$ and $H$ given on a symplectic manifold $(M^{2n}, \omega^2)$ is the derivative of the function $F$ in the direction of the phase flow with hamiltonian function $H$:

$$(F, H)(x) = \frac{d}{dt}\bigg|_{t=0} F(g_H^t x).$$
