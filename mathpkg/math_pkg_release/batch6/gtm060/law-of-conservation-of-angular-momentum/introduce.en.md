For motion in any central force field, angular momentum is conserved. The proof is immediate: $\dot{M} = [\dot{r}, \dot{r}] + [r, \ddot{r}]$, the first term vanishes identically, and the second vanishes because in a central field $\ddot{r}$ is collinear with $r$. This conservation law is a consequence of rotational symmetry (Noether's theorem) and implies both that orbits lie in a plane and that the sectorial velocity is constant (Kepler's second law).
