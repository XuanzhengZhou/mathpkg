The involutivity of the Legendre transformation means that $f$ and $g$ are dual to each other: the transformation applied twice returns the original function. This is a consequence of the convexity of $f$, which ensures the mapping $x \leftrightarrow p$ is one-to-one. Geometrically, the envelope of the family of tangent lines to the graph of $f$ reconstructs the graph of $f$. This duality is central to the equivalence between Lagrangian and Hamiltonian mechanics.
