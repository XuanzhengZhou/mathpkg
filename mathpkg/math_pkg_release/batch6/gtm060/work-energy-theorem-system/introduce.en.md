The work-energy theorem states that the change in total kinetic energy equals the total work done by all forces (internal and external). In configuration space $E^{3n}$, this takes the form of a line integral. For conservative systems, the work depends only on endpoints, leading to energy conservation. For non-conservative systems, the lost mechanical energy is converted to other forms (heat, etc.).
