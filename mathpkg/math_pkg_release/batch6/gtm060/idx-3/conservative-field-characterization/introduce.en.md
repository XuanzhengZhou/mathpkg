A vector field is conservative if it can be expressed as the (negative) gradient of a scalar potential function. This theorem establishes the fundamental equivalence between conservativity and path-independence: a vector field is conservative precisely when the work done along any curve depends only on the endpoints, not on the particular path taken between them. The proof constructs the potential energy function $U(M) = -\int_{M_0}^M (F, dS)$ when the work is path-independent, and conversely shows that if $F = -\partial U/\partial x$, then the work integral reduces to the difference $U(M_1) - U(M_0)$.
