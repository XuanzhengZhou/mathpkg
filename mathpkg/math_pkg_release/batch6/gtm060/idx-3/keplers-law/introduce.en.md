Kepler's second law (the law of areas) states that the radius vector from the sun to a planet sweeps out equal areas in equal times. In mathematical terms, the sectorial velocity $C = dS/dt$ is constant. Using polar coordinates, one finds $C = \frac{1}{2} r^2 \dot{\varphi}$, which equals one-half of the angular momentum $M$. Thus, Kepler's empirical law is a direct consequence of the conservation of angular momentum in a central gravitational field. Satellites with highly elongated orbits demonstrate this: they spend most of their time in the distant part of their orbit where $\dot{\varphi}$ is small.
