This lemma expresses the velocity vector of a point moving in the plane in terms of the polar coordinate basis $\{e_r, e_\varphi\}$. The radial component is $\dot{r} e_r$ and the angular component is $r \dot{\varphi} e_\varphi$. The derivation uses the fact that the basis vectors themselves rotate: $\dot{e}_r = \dot{\varphi} e_\varphi$ and $\dot{e}_\varphi = -\dot{\varphi} e_r$. This decomposition is essential for computing the angular momentum $M = [r, \dot{r}] = r^2 \dot{\varphi} [e_r, e_\varphi]$ and connecting it to Kepler's law of areas.
