The effective potential energy $V(r)$ combines the actual potential energy $U(r)$ with a centrifugal barrier term $M^2/(2r^2)$. When studying the radial motion of a particle in a central field, the angular momentum $M$ acts as a repulsive force that prevents the particle from approaching the origin (when $M \neq 0$). The graph of $V(r)$ determines the qualitative behavior: bounded motion occurs between $r_{\min}$ and $r_{\max}$ where $V(r) \leq E$, and the turning points satisfy $\dot{r} = 0$. The effective potential framework transforms the two-dimensional central force problem into an easily visualized one-dimensional problem.
