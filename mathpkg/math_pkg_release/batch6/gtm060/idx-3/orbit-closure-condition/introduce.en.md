This theorem characterizes when bounded orbits in a central field are closed curves. The key quantity is the apocentral angle $\Phi$, computed by integrating $M/(r^2\sqrt{2(E-V(r))})$ from $r_{\min}$ to $r_{\max}$. The orbit closes precisely when $\Phi/\pi$ is rational. Otherwise, the trajectory densely fills the annular region between the two circular boundaries. For nearly circular orbits, $\Phi \approx \pi/\sqrt{V''(r)}$ evaluated at the equilibrium. The only potentials for which all bounded orbits are closed — regardless of initial conditions — are the harmonic oscillator $U = ar^2$ and the Newtonian/Coulomb potential $U = -k/r$ (Bertrand's theorem).
