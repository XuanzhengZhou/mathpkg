The law of conservation of angular momentum is one of the fundamental conservation laws of classical mechanics. It states that for a material point moving under a central force, the angular momentum $M = [r, \dot{r}]$ is constant in time. The proof uses the product rule for vector derivatives and the fact that in a central field, the acceleration $\ddot{r}$ is collinear with the position vector $r$, causing their cross product to vanish. Historically, this law was first discovered by Kepler through observation of planetary motion and formulated as the constancy of sectorial velocity.
