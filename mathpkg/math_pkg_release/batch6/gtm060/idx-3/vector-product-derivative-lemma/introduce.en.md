This lemma provides the product rule for differentiating a vector (cross) product with respect to time in $\mathbb{R}^3$. It states that the derivative of $[a, b]$ is the sum of two terms, each obtained by differentiating one factor while holding the other constant. This is analogous to the ordinary product rule for scalar functions and follows directly from the definition of the derivative. The lemma is a crucial computational step in proving the law of conservation of angular momentum.
