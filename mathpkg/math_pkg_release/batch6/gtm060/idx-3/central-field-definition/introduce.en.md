A central vector field is one that possesses full rotational symmetry about a fixed point (the center). By definition, the field is invariant under all plane motions (rotations and reflections) that leave the center fixed. A consequence is that all field vectors lie on rays emanating from the center, and the magnitude depends only on the distance from the center. Examples include the Newtonian gravitational field $F = -k r/|r|^3$, while fields like $F = (x_2, -x_1)$ are not central.
