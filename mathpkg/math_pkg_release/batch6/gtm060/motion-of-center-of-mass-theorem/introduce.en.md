The center of mass theorem is a powerful reduction: the translational motion of any system, however complex, is described by a single point moving under the total external force. This decouples the center-of-mass motion from the internal (relative) motion. For closed systems, the center of mass undergoes uniform rectilinear motion, reflecting conservation of total momentum.
