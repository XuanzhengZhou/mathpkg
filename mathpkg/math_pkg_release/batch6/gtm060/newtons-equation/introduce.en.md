Newton's equation $\ddot{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{x}, \dot{\mathbf{x}}, t)$ is the fundamental differential equation of classical mechanics. It expresses the principle of determinacy: the acceleration of a mechanical system is a function of its position, velocity, and time. Given initial conditions, this second-order ODE uniquely determines the entire future motion.
