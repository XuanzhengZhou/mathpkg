The equation $\ddot{x} = F(x, \dot{x}, t)$ follows directly from Newton's principle of determinacy (Section 1C). Since the initial positions $x(t_0)$ and velocities $\dot{x}(t_0)$ uniquely determine the entire motion, they must in particular determine the second derivative $\ddot{x}(t_0)$. This functional dependence defines $F$. The uniqueness of the motion for given initial conditions then follows from the standard existence and uniqueness theorem for ordinary differential equations, assuming $F$ satisfies appropriate smoothness conditions (e.g., Lipschitz continuity).
