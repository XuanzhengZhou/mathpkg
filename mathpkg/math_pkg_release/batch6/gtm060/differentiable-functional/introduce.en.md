A functional is differentiable if its increment can be decomposed into a linear part (the differential or variation) plus a remainder that is of second order in the variation $h$ of the curve. This definition generalizes the notion of the differential from finite-dimensional calculus to infinite-dimensional spaces of curves. The differential, also called the first variation, plays the same role as the gradient in finite dimensions.
