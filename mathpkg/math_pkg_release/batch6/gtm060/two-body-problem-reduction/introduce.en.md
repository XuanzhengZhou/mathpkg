The two-body problem separates into the uniform motion of the center of mass and the equivalent one-body problem for the relative coordinate with reduced mass $\mu = m_1 m_2/(m_1 + m_2)$. This reduction is exact for any central interaction potential. For Newtonian gravity, the relative motion traces a conic section (ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola) with the center of mass at one focus. This solved the ancient problem of planetary motion and remains the foundation of orbital mechanics.
