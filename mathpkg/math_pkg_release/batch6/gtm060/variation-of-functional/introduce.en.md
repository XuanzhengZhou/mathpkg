The variation (or first variation) of a functional is its differential -- the linear part of the increment when the curve is varied by $h$. For integral functionals $\Phi = \int L(x, \dot{x}, t) dt$, integration by parts yields a formula with a bulk term involving the Euler-Lagrange operator and a boundary term. Setting the variation to zero for all admissible $h$ gives the Euler-Lagrange equation.
