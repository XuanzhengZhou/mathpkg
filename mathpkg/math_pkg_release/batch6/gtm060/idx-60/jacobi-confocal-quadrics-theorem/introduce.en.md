Jacobi's theorem is the foundational result of elliptic coordinate theory. It states that exactly $n$ confocal quadrics pass through any point in $n$-dimensional euclidean space, and that smooth confocal quadrics always intersect orthogonally. The proof uses duality: each point not at the origin corresponds to an affine hyperplane in the dual space, and principal axes analysis shows exactly $n$ quadrics are tangent to this hyperplane.
