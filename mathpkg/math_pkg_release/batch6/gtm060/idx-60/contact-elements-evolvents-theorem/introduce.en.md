This 1978 theorem describes the contact elements of evolvents of a plane curve near an inflection point. The surface of these contact elements, fibered over the plane, is locally equivalent to the discriminant surface in the space of cubic polynomials (polynomials with multiple roots). Together with the boundary curve, this forms a variety diffeomorphic to the singular orbit variety of the reflection group $B_3$, inaugurating the theory of boundary singularities.
