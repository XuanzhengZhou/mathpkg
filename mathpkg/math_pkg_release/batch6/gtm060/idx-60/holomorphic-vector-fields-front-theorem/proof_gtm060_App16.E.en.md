Example: A generic vector field in the neighborhood of the most singular point of a swallowtail $\{x^4 + ax^2 + bx + c = (x + d)^2 \ldots\}$ is equivalent, by a holomorphic diffeomorphism preserving the swallowtail, to the normal form $\partial/\partial c$.

The general proof uses the theory of versal deformations of simple singularities. The front is the discriminant (set of non-regular orbits) of the reflection group. Vector fields transverse to the discriminant can be studied via the action of the reflection group on the parameter space. The transversality condition ensures that the vector field can be straightened by a group-invariant diffeomorphism, which descends to a diffeomorphism of the orbit space preserving the discriminant.

The reduction of various objects to normal form, by a diffeomorphism preserving a wave front or caustic, is a basic technique for studying the geometry of systems of rays and fronts.
