This theorem describes the space-time geometry of moving wave fronts. As a wave front propagates, its cuspidal edges trace out a surface in four-dimensional space-time. Generically, near a swallowtail caustic, this swept surface is an open swallowtail -- a singularity where the cuspidal edge is retained but the self-intersection of the ordinary swallowtail disappears.
