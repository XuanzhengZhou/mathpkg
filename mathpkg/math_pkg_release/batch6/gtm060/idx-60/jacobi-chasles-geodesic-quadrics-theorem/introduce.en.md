The Jacobi-Chasles theorem connects geodesics on a quadric to confocal quadrics. For any geodesic on a quadric $Q$ in $n$-dimensional space, there exist $n-2$ confocal quadrics such that all tangent lines to the geodesic are also tangent to these quadrics. The proof uses the symplectic structure on the manifold of oriented lines and the induced line functions constructed from confocal quadrics.
