Shcherbak's theorem (1982) reveals the unexpected appearance of icosahedral symmetry in the obstacle problem. The graph of the multivalued time function for bypassing a smooth plane curve is diffeomorphic near an inflection point to the variety of singular orbits of the reflection group $H_3$ (the symmetry group of the icosahedron). The cuspidal edges of orders $3/2$ and $5/2$ on this surface correspond to the structure of the $H_3$ discriminant.
