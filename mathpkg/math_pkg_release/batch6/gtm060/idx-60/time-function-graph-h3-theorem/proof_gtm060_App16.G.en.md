The proof of this theorem uses Lyashko's theorem, which identifies the variety $\Sigma$ of singular orbits for the group $H_3$ as the variety of polynomials $x^5 + ax^4 + bx^2 + c$ having a multiple root. This variety is the union of the tangents to the curve $(t, t^3, t^5)$.

Shcherbak's theorem applies to any curve of the form $(t + o(t), t^3 + o(t^3), t^5 + o(t^5))$. The graph of the multivalued time function in the obstacle problem, near an inflection point of the boundary curve, has two cuspidal edges of orders $3/2$ and $5/2$. The local normal form of this graph, after formal diffeomorphism, matches the $\Sigma$ variety described by Lyashko.

The same singularity appears on a generic front at the point of tangency of an asymptotic ray with the bounding surface of an obstacle in $\mathbb{R}^3$.
