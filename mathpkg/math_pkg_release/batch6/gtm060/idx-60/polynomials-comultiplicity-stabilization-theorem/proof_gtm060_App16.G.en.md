Consider, in the space of monic polynomials $x^n + \lambda_1 x^{n-1} + \cdots + \lambda_{n-1}$, the set of polynomials with a root of fixed comultiplicity $k$, i.e., polynomials of the form $(x - \alpha)^{n-k}(x^k + \cdots)$. Differentiation of polynomials preserves the comultiplicity of roots.

The stabilization occurs because, once the degree $n$ is sufficiently large ($n \geq 2k+1$), adding higher-degree terms does not create new singularities in the variety of polynomials with the given comultiplicity. The self-intersections that appear for smaller $n$ are eliminated when the degree reaches $2k+1$. The proof uses the theory of versal deformations: for $n \geq 2k+1$, the deformation of the singularity $(x-\alpha)^{n-k}$ is versal, and the discriminant (variety of non-generic polynomials) stabilizes.
