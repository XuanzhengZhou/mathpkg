The manifold of tangent rays is locally diffeomorphic near a biasymptotic ray to the product of a swallowtail and a line. The proof uses the characterization of biasymptotic directions in terms of the contact of the surface with lines. Near a biasymptotic direction, the family of distance functions along lines has a singularity of type $A_3$ (which gives the swallowtail), while the transverse direction gives the linear factor.

The symplectic structure on the space of lines restricts to this product. The theorem asserts that all generic symplectic structures near such a point are formally equivalent by diffeomorphisms preserving the product structure. The proof uses the homotopy method (Moser's technique) for symplectic forms, combined with a relative version that respects the product decomposition. The formal normal form is obtained by power series expansion and induction on the degree of the jet.
