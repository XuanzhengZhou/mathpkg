A euclidean pencil of quadrics is a one-parameter family of quadratic forms or quadric surfaces in a euclidean vector space. This structure arises naturally from the passage to the dual space of the theory of principal axes. The elliptic coordinates of a point are defined as the parameter values for which confocal quadrics from a fixed family pass through that point.
