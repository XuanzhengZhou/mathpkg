A \textbf{euclidean pencil of quadrics} (resp.\ \textbf{quadratic forms}) in a euclidean vector space $V$ is a one-parameter family of surfaces of degree $2$ (resp.\ quadratic forms) of the form
$$\frac{1}{2}(A x, x) = 1$$
(resp.\ $B = \frac{1}{2}(A x, x) - \frac{1}{2}(l, x)^2$ where $(l, x) = 1$ is the equation of an affine hyperplane in the dual space).
