This lemma establishes that projecting confocal quadrics along a pencil of parallel lines produces apparent contours that themselves form a confocal family. The proof relies on duality between sections and projections: the apparent contours are dual to sections of the dual quadrics by a hyperplane through the origin, which form a euclidean pencil.
