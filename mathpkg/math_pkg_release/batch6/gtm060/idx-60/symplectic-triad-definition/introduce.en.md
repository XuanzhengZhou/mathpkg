A symplectic triad is a geometric structure that generates Lagrangian varieties. It consists of a smooth hypersurface $H$ in a symplectic manifold, a Lagrangian submanifold $L$, and a distinguished hypersurface $\ell$ in $L$ along which $L$ is tangent to $H$ to first order. The Lagrangian variety generated by the triad is the image of $L$ under projection to the manifold of characteristics of $H$.
