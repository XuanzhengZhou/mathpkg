Lyashko's theorem describes the variety of singular orbits for the group $H_3$ as the union of the tangents to the curve $(t, t^3, t^5)$. This curve is the set of polynomials $x^5 + ax^4 + bx^2 + c$ having a root of multiplicity at least $4$. The polynomials with at least a double root form a $2$-dimensional surface in the $3$-dimensional parameter space $(a, b, c)$, which is the union of all tangent lines to this curve. The variety $\Sigma$ is exactly this discriminant surface.

The identification with $H_3$ follows from the fact that the ring of invariant polynomials for the $H_3$ action on $\mathbb{C}^3$ is generated by polynomials of degrees $2, 6, 10$, and the discriminant (set of non-regular orbits) has the parametrization described above.
