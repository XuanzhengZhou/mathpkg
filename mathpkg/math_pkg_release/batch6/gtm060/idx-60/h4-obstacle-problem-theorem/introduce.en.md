Shcherbak's theorem extends the $H_3$ result to three dimensions, showing that the $H_4$ reflection group (symmetries of the regular 120-cell in $\mathbb{R}^4$) governs the singularity of the time function graph in the 3D obstacle problem. The singularity appears at the point of tangency of an asymptotic ray with the obstacle boundary. There is an associated embedding of the local algebra $D_4$ into $E_8$, reflecting deep connections between these singularity types.
