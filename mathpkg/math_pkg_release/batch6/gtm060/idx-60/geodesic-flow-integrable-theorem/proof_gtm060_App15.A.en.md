The proof follows from Theorems 2 and 3 together with Lemmas A and B. We construct $n-1$ induced line functions from the $n-1$ confocal quadrics tangent to a generic line (Theorem 2). By Lemma B and Theorem 2 (orthogonality of tangent planes), these $n-1$ functions Poisson-commute pairwise, providing $n-1$ independent integrals in involution. By Lemma A, the phase curves of the hamiltonian system generated by any one of these integrals consist of tangent lines to geodesics on the corresponding quadric. Since the geodesic flow on an $n$-dimensional quadric has $n-1$ degrees of freedom, the existence of $n-1$ independent integrals in involution establishes complete integrability in the sense of Liouville.

Theorem 4 was initially proved just for quadrics with unequal principal axes, but passage to a limit extends the result to more symmetric quadrics of revolution (as well as to noncentral paraboloids).
