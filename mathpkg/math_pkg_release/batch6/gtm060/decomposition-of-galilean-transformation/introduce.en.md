This theorem states that any Galilean transformation of $R \times R^3$ factors uniquely into a uniform motion (3 parameters), a space-time translation (4 parameters), and a spatial rotation (3 parameters). This gives the Galilean group dimension 10 and provides a complete parametrization of all inertial coordinate transformations.
