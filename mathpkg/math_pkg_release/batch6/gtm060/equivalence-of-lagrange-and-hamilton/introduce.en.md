Lagrange's and Hamilton's formulations are mathematically equivalent under the Legendre transform, provided the Lagrangian is convex in the velocity variables. The transformation is reversible (involutive). This equivalence is not limited to mechanics -- it applies to any variational problem. The Hamiltonian form has the advantage of being first-order and revealing the symplectic structure, while the Lagrangian form is more convenient for relativistic and field-theoretic generalizations.
