The Galilean space-time structure formalizes the geometry underlying Newtonian mechanics. It consists of a four-dimensional affine universe $A^4$, a linear time mapping $t$ that measures time intervals between events, and a Euclidean distance on the three-dimensional spaces of simultaneous events. This structure captures the absolute time and Euclidean space of classical physics.
