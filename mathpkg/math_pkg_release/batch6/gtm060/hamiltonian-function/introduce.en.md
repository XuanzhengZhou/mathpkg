The Hamiltonian $H(p, q, t)$ is obtained from the Lagrangian $L(q, \dot{q}, t)$ by Legendre transform with respect to the velocity variables. For standard mechanical systems where $L = T - U$ and $T$ is a quadratic form in $\dot{q}$, the Hamiltonian equals the total energy: $H = T + U$. The Hamiltonian formulation treats positions and momenta on equal footing and reveals the symplectic geometry underlying classical mechanics.
