Poincaré's geometric theorem is a foundational result in symplectic topology and celestial mechanics. It states that any area-preserving homeomorphism of a planar circular annulus that twists the boundary circles in opposite directions must possess at least two fixed points. This theorem provides a topological mechanism for the existence of periodic solutions in Hamiltonian systems with two degrees of freedom, via the method of surface of section.
