Every symplectic diffeomorphism $A$ homologous to the identity and sufficiently close to $A_0$ has, near the torus $x = x_0$, at least $2^n$ periodic points $\xi$ of period $N$ (such that $A^N \xi = \xi$), counting multiplicity.

Here $A_0$ is an integrable symplectic diffeomorphism of the form

$$(x, y) \mapsto (x, y + \alpha(x)), \quad \text{where } \alpha(x_0) = 0, \quad \det\left|\frac{\partial \alpha}{\partial x}\right|_{x_0} \neq 0.$$

By the implicit function theorem, the mapping $A_0^N$ has, near the torus $x = x_0$, a torus which is displaced only radially $(x, y) \mapsto (X, Y)$ and is given by an equation of the form $x = f(y)$; its image is also given by an equation $x = g(y)$ of the same form, with $X(f(y), y) = g(y)$, $Y(f(y), y) = y$.
