For a symplectic diffeomorphism near an integrable one, this theorem guarantees the existence of at least $2^n$ periodic points near each nondegenerate invariant torus. The proof uses the generating function $Xy + S(X, y)$ and considers the function $F(y) = S(X(f(y), y), y)$ on the torus, whose critical points correspond to period-$N$ fixed points. By Morse theory, $F$ has at least $2^n$ critical points on the $n$-torus.
