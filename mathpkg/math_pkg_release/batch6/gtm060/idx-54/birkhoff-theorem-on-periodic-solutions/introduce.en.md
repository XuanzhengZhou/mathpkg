Birkhoff's theorem asserts that any linearly stable periodic orbit in a generic Hamiltonian system is accumulated by infinitely many nearby periodic orbits. The result is proved by first approximating the symplectic return map by its Birkhoff normal form near the fixed point, then applying the generating function method to relate fixed points of iterates of the map to critical points of suitable functions. This demonstrates that periodic solutions are abundant in Hamiltonian dynamics.
