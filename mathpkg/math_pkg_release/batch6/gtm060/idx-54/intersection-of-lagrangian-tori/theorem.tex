Consider one of the $n$-dimensional tori $x = c = \text{const}$ and apply to it a symplectic diffeomorphism homologous to the identity. The original torus intersects its image in at least $2^n$ points (counting multiplicities), of which at least $n + 1$ are geometrically different, at least under the assumption that the image torus has an equation of the form $x = f(y)$, where $f$ is smooth.

More generally, any Lagrangian torus intersects its image in at least $2^n$ points (of which at least $n+1$ are geometrically different), under the assumption that both the original torus and its image project single-valued onto the $y$-space, i.e., are given by equations $y = f(x)$ and $x = g(y)$, respectively.
