This proposition extends the intersection theory of Lagrangian submanifolds to the setting of $n$-dimensional invariant tori in Hamiltonian systems. When a Liouville torus $x = c$ is mapped by a symplectic diffeomorphism homologous to the identity, the original torus and its image must intersect in at least $2^n$ points (counting multiplicity), with at least $n+1$ geometrically distinct intersections. This lower bound follows from Morse theory on the $n$-torus, which guarantees at least $2^n$ critical points for any smooth function.
