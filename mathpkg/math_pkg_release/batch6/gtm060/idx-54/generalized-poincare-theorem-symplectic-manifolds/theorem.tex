Every symplectic diffeomorphism of a compact symplectic manifold, homologous to the identity, has at least as many fixed points as a smooth function on this manifold has critical points (at least if this diffeomorphism is not too far from the identity).

The condition of the mapping being homologous to the identity is essential, as demonstrated by the example of a translation on the torus, which has no fixed points at all.

In the case of the two-dimensional torus, it is sufficient that none of the eigenvalues of the Jacobi matrix of the diffeomorphism (in any global symplectic coordinate system on $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$) be equal to $-1$.
