This theorem gives a sweeping generalization of Poincaré's geometric theorem: any symplectic diffeomorphism of a compact symplectic manifold that is homologous to the identity must possess at least as many fixed points as a smooth function on that manifold has critical points. The lower bound is given by the Lusternik-Schnirelman category (or Morse-type invariants) of the manifold. The homology-to-identity condition is necessary, as translations on the torus show. This result connects the topology of the underlying manifold to the dynamics of symplectic transformations.
