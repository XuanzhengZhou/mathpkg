A symplectic diffeomorphism is homologous to the identity if it can be deformed to the identity through a smooth path of symplectic diffeomorphisms whose velocity field at each instant admits a single-valued Hamiltonian function. This condition forms the commutator subgroup of the identity component in the symplectic diffeomorphism group. For the two-dimensional torus, this property is equivalent to preserving the center of gravity, and it serves as a necessary hypothesis for generalized Poincaré-type fixed point theorems on compact symplectic manifolds.
