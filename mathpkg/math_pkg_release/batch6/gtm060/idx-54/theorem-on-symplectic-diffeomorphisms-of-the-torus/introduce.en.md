This theorem extends Poincaré's geometric theorem from the annulus to the two-dimensional torus. For a symplectic diffeomorphism of the torus that preserves the center of gravity, the lower bound increases from two to at least four fixed points (counting multiplicity), with at least three being geometrically distinct. The proof involves constructing a function $\Phi$ on the torus and applying Morse theory, which guarantees at least four critical points for any smooth function on the torus.
