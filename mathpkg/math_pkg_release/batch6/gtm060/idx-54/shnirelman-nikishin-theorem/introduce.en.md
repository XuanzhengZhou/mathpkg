The Shnirel'man–Nikishin theorem asserts that every area-preserving diffeomorphism of the two-dimensional sphere possesses at least two geometrically distinct fixed points. This result is the spherical analogue of Poincaré's geometric theorem for the annulus. The proof relies on the topological fact that the index of the gradient vector field of any smooth function on $S^2$ equals $2$, guaranteeing at least two critical points, which translate into fixed points of the diffeomorphism.
