This principle explains why eigenvalue degeneracies (multiple spectra) are generically not observed when only a single parameter is varied. The set of symmetric matrices with a repeated eigenvalue has codimension 2 in the space of all symmetric matrices, so a generic one-parameter curve avoids it. This has practical consequences: making a rigid body's inertia ellipsoid into an ellipsoid of revolution typically requires at least two independently adjustable masses. The phenomenon is a finite-dimensional analogue of the "avoided crossing" principle in quantum mechanics.
