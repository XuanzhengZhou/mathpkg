The principle of Galilean relativity imposes strong constraints on the form of Newton's equation in inertial frames. Time-translation invariance forces the right-hand side to be autonomous (no explicit time dependence). Spatial translation invariance restricts it to relative coordinates. Invariance under boosts (uniform motion) restricts it further to relative velocities. Finally, rotational invariance (isotropy of space) requires equivariance under orthogonal transformations. Together, these constraints severely restrict the possible forms of force laws in closed mechanical systems.
