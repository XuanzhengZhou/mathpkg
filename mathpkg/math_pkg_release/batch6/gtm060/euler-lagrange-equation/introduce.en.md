The Euler-Lagrange equation is the fundamental necessary condition for a curve to be an extremal of an integral functional $\int L(x, \dot{x}, t) dt$. It is a second-order ordinary differential equation. In mechanics, with $L = T - U$, the Euler-Lagrange equations are precisely Lagrange's equations of motion. The derivation uses integration by parts and the fundamental lemma of the calculus of variations.
