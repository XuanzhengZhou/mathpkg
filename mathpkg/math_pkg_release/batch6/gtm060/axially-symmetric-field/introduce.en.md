An axially symmetric field is invariant under rotations about a fixed axis. In cylindrical coordinates, the potential energy depends only on $r$ (distance from the axis) and $z$ (coordinate along the axis), not on the azimuthal angle $\varphi$. Such fields arise naturally from solids of revolution, and the component of angular momentum along the symmetry axis is conserved.
