Angular momentum is the rotational analog of linear momentum, defined as the cross product of position and velocity vectors: $M = r \times \dot{r}$. For motion in a central field, angular momentum is conserved, which implies that the motion takes place in a plane and that the sectorial velocity (area swept per unit time) is constant. These facts are the geometric content of Kepler's second law.
