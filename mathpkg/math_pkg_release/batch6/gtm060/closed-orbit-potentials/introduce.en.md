Bertrand's theorem states that only two central potentials yield closed bounded orbits for all initial conditions: the harmonic oscillator $U = ar^2$ and the Newtonian/Coulomb potential $U = -k/r$. This profound result explains why Kepler's ellipses and Hooke's ellipses are special — any other radial dependence leads to orbit precession.
