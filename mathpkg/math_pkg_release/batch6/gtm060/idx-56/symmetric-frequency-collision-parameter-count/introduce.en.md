This proposition gives the minimal codimension (and hence the minimal number of parameters) required for collisions of characteristic frequencies to occur stably in systems with rotational symmetry of order $n \geq 3$. Symmetric and asymmetric oscillations behave differently: simple symmetric eigenvalues require two parameters to collide, while double asymmetric eigenvalues require four. This result explains why, in generic one-parameter families of symmetric membranes, one can observe collisions between a simple and a double frequency but not between two simple or two double frequencies.
