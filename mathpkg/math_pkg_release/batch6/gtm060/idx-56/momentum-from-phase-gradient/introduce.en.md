In the quasi-classical (WKB) approximation, the phase function $s(q)$ of a short-wave initial condition determines the initial classical momentum via $p = \partial s / \partial q$. This relationship is the bridge between quantum and classical mechanics: the graph of $p(q)$ in phase space is a Lagrangian manifold, and its evolution under the classical Hamiltonian flow governs the leading-order asymptotics of the quantum wave function as $h \to 0$.
