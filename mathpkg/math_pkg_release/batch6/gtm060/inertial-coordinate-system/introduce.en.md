An inertial coordinate system is the fundamental reference frame of classical mechanics, defined by Galileo's principle of relativity. In such a system, the laws of physics take the same form at all times, and any frame moving uniformly with respect to an inertial frame is also inertial. The concept formalizes the experimental observation that uniform motion is indistinguishable from rest in a closed physical system.
