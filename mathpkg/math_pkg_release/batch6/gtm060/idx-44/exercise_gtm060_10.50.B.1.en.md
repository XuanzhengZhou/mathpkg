Find the generating function $S$ and action variable $I$ for a harmonic oscillator with Hamiltonian $H = \frac{1}{2} a^2 p^2 + \frac{1}{2} b^2 q^2$.

Answer. The level curve $M_h$ is the ellipse bounding the area

$$\Pi(h) = \pi \left(\frac{\sqrt{2h}}{a}\right)\left(\frac{\sqrt{2h}}{b}\right) = \frac{2\pi h}{ab} = \frac{2\pi h}{\omega}.$$

Thus for a harmonic oscillator the action variable is the ratio of energy to frequency: $I = h/\omega$. The angle variable $\varphi$ is the phase of oscillation.
