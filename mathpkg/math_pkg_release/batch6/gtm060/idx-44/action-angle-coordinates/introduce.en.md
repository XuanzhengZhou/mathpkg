In the theory of integrable Hamiltonian systems, after applying Liouville's theorem one obtains that the compact connected level manifolds $M_f$ of the first integrals are $n$-dimensional tori. On such a torus, angular coordinates $\varphi_i$ can be chosen so that the phase flow takes a particularly simple linear form. Action variables $I_i$ are then introduced as the conjugate momenta, completing a canonical coordinate system $(I,\varphi)$ in a neighborhood of $M_f$ called the action-angle variables. These coordinates reduce the equations of motion to $\frac{dI}{dt}=0$, $\frac{d\varphi}{dt}=\omega(I)$, making the integration of the system trivial and forming the foundation for perturbation theory.
