In action-angle variables, the equations of motion are

$$\frac{dI}{dt} = 0, \quad \frac{d\varphi}{dt} = \omega(I) = \frac{\partial H}{\partial I}.$$

From the definition of the action variable for one degree of freedom, $I = \Pi(h)/(2\pi)$, so

$$\frac{dI}{dh} = \frac{1}{2\pi} \frac{d\Pi}{dh}.$$

Now the angular frequency is

$$\dot{\varphi} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial I} = \left(\frac{dI}{dh}\right)^{-1} = 2\pi \left(\frac{d\Pi}{dh}\right)^{-1}.$$

Since the period $T$ is the time for $\varphi$ to increase by $2\pi$, we have

$$T = \frac{2\pi}{\dot{\varphi}} = \frac{d\Pi}{dh}.$$
