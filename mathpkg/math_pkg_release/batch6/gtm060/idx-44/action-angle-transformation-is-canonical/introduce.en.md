In the construction of action-angle variables for a system with $n$ degrees of freedom, one must verify that the transformation $(p,q) \mapsto (I,\varphi)$ preserves the canonical symplectic structure. The proof uses the fact that the invariant torus $M_f$ is a null manifold (the $2$-form $\omega^2 = dp \wedge dq$ vanishes identically on $M_f$), which implies that the $1$-form $p \, dq$ is closed on $M_f$. A generating function $S(I,q) = \int_{q_0}^q p(I,q) dq$ is then defined, and the canonical transformation formulas $p = \partial S/\partial q$, $\varphi = \partial S/\partial I$ yield the required equality of symplectic forms. The angle variables acquire multiples of $2\pi$ as their periods, confirming their interpretation as angular coordinates.
