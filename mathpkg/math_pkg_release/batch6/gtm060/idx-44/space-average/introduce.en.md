The space average $\bar{f}$ of an integrable function $f$ on the $n$-dimensional torus $T^n$ is its normalized integral with respect to the standard Lebesgue measure. It represents the mean value of $f$ over the entire phase space $T^n$, weighted uniformly. In the context of conditionally periodic motion, the space average is compared with the time average along trajectories, and the celebrated theorem on averages (the ergodic theorem for translations on the torus) asserts their equality when the frequencies are independent and $f$ is Riemann integrable.
