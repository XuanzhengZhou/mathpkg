Uniform distribution strengthens the density result: not only does the trajectory visit every neighborhood, but it does so in proportion to the measure of that neighborhood. For a measurable region $D \subset T^n$, the fraction of time the trajectory spends in $D$ converges to $\operatorname{mes}(D)/(2\pi)^n$, the normalized Lebesgue measure of $D$. This follows directly from the theorem on averages applied to the characteristic function of $D$. The result is a prototype of the ergodic theorem and explains why conditionally periodic motion with independent frequencies explores the torus in a statistically uniform manner.
