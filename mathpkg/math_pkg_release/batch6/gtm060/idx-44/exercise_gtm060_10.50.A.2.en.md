Can the functions $\omega(I)$ in the action-angle equations

$$\frac{dI}{dt} = 0, \quad \frac{d\varphi}{dt} = \omega(I)$$

be arbitrary?

Solution. In the variables $(I, \varphi)$, the equations of the flow have the canonical form with hamiltonian function $H(I)$. Therefore, $\omega(I) = \partial H / \partial I$; thus if the number of degrees of freedom is $n \geq 2$, the functions $\omega(I)$ are not arbitrary, but satisfy the symmetry condition $\partial \omega_i / \partial I_j = \partial \omega_j / \partial I_i$.
