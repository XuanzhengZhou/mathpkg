The first lemma in the proof of the theorem on averages establishes the result for the simplest building blocks: exponential functions $e^{i(k,\varphi)}$ on the torus. When $k = 0$, the function is constant and both averages trivially equal $1$. When $k \neq 0$, the space average is zero by orthogonality, and the time average is zero because the denominator $(k, \omega)$ is nonzero due to the independence of frequencies, while the numerator $e^{i(k,\omega)T} - 1$ remains bounded as $T \to \infty$. This lemma provides the base case from which the full theorem follows by linearity (trigonometric polynomials) and approximation (Riemann integrable functions).
