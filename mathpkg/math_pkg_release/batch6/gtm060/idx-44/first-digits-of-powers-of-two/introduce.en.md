A striking application of the uniform distribution corollary is the statistical behavior of the first digits of powers of two. The first digit of $2^n$ is determined by the fractional part of $n \log_{10} 2$, and since $\log_{10} 2$ is irrational, the sequence $\{n \log_{10} 2\}$ is uniformly distributed modulo 1. This means the proportion of powers of $2$ starting with a given digit $d$ equals $\log_{10}(1 + 1/d)$, a logarithmic distribution reminiscent of Benford's law. The corollary quantifies the relative frequency of specific digits, such as the ratio of $7$'s to $8$'s in the sequence of first digits.
