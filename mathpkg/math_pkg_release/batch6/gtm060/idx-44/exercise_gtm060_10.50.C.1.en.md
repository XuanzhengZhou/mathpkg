Show that the integral defining the action variables

$$I_i(f) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \oint_{\gamma_i} p \, dq$$

does not depend on the choice of the curve $\gamma_i$ representing the homology cycle on $M_f$.

Hint. In Section 49 it was shown that the $2$-form $\omega^2 = \sum dp_i \wedge dq_i$ on the manifold $M_f$ is equal to zero. By Stokes' formula,

$$\oint_{\gamma} p \, dq - \oint_{\gamma'} p \, dq = \iint_{\sigma} dp \wedge dq = 0,$$

where $\partial \sigma = \gamma - \gamma'$.
