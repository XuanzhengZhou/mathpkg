For a Hamiltonian system with one degree of freedom, the construction of action-angle variables proceeds by seeking a generating function $S(I,q)$ of the second type. For fixed $I$, the differential $dS|_{I=\text{const}} = p \, dq$ defines a well-defined $1$-form on the invariant curve $M_{h(I)}$. Integrating along the curve yields $S(I,q) = \int_{q_0}^q p \, dq$, which is multi-valued with period equal to the enclosed area $\Pi(I) = \oint p \, dq$. Despite this multi-valuedness, the canonical transformation defined by $p = \partial S/\partial q$ and $\varphi = \partial S/\partial I$ is well-defined and gives the desired action-angle variables.
