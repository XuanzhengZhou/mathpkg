The third and final lemma in the proof of the theorem on averages provides the approximation step that extends the result from trigonometric polynomials to all Riemann integrable functions. It states that any Riemann integrable function can be sandwiched between two trigonometric polynomials whose integrals differ by an arbitrarily small amount. For continuous functions this follows directly from the Weierstrass approximation theorem; for Riemann integrable functions it requires an additional layer of approximation by continuous functions from above and below. The sandwich inequality allows one to bound the time average of $f$ between the time averages of the approximating polynomials, forcing the limit to exist and equal the space average.
