Theorem (on the averages). Let $f$ be an integrable function on the $n$-dimensional torus $T^n$, and let $\varphi(t) = \varphi_0 + \omega t$ be a conditionally periodic motion with frequencies $\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n$. If $f$ is continuous (or merely Riemann integrable) and the frequencies $\omega_i$ are independent, then the time average exists everywhere and coincides with the space average:
$$f^*(\varphi_0) = \bar{f} \quad \text{for all } \varphi_0 \in T^n.$$
