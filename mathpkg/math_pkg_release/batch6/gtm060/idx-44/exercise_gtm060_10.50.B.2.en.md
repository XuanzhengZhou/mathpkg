Show that the period $T$ of motion along the closed curve $H = h$ on the phase plane $(p, q)$ is equal to the derivative with respect to $h$ of the area bounded by this curve:

$$T = \frac{d\Pi(h)}{dh}.$$

Solution. In action-angle variables the equations of motion give

$$\dot{\varphi} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial I} = \left( \frac{dI}{dh} \right)^{-1} = 2\pi \left( \frac{d\Pi}{dh} \right)^{-1},$$

and since the period $T$ is the time for $\varphi$ to increase by $2\pi$,

$$T = \frac{2\pi}{\dot{\varphi}} = \frac{d\Pi}{dh}.$$
