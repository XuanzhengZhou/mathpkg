The notion of independent (or rationally independent) frequencies is central to the theory of conditionally periodic motion. When frequencies are independent, no nontrivial integer linear combination vanishes, which implies that no resonance relation exists among them. This property ensures that the trajectory is not confined to a subtorus of lower dimension. In the context of integrable Hamiltonian systems, the independence of frequencies determines whether the motion is ergodic on the invariant torus: independent frequencies guarantee that every trajectory is dense and uniformly distributed on $T^n$.
