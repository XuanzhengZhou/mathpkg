Suppose one of the eigenvalues satisfies $\lambda = \omega^2 > 0$. Then the system can perform a small oscillation of the form

$$q(t) = (C_1 \cos \omega t + C_2 \sin \omega t) \xi,$$

where $\xi$ is an eigenvector corresponding to $\lambda$, satisfying

$$B \xi = \lambda A \xi.$$

This oscillation is the product of the one-dimensional motion $Q_i = C_1 \cos \omega_i t + C_2 \sin \omega_i t$ and the trivial motion $Q_j = 0$ for $j \neq i$.
