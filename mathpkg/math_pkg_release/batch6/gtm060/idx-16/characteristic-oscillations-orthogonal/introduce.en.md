This theorem establishes that the $n$ characteristic oscillation directions $\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_n$ form an orthogonal basis with respect to the inner product $(A \cdot, \cdot)$ induced by the kinetic energy. The proof is direct: in the principal axis coordinates $Q$, the kinetic energy is $\frac{1}{2}\sum \dot{Q}_i^2$, which means the coordinate axes are orthogonal under the $A$-inner product. The eigenvectors $\xi_i$ correspond to these coordinate axes.
