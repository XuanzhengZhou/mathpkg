This corollary states that any small oscillation can be expressed as a linear superposition of characteristic oscillations. To decompose a given motion, one projects the initial conditions $q(0)$ and $\dot{q}(0)$ onto the characteristic directions $\xi_i$ (which form an $A$-orthogonal basis), then solves each one-dimensional problem independently. A sum of characteristic oscillations is generally not periodic, producing Lissajous figures when frequencies are incommensurable.
