For small oscillations near a stable equilibrium, the nonlinear period approaches the period of the linearized harmonic oscillator. The linearized system has kinetic energy $T_2 = \frac{1}{2}a\dot{q}^2$ and potential energy $U_2 = \frac{1}{2}bq^2$, yielding the harmonic oscillator equation $\ddot{q} = -\omega_0^2 q$ with general solution $q = c_1 \cos \omega_0 t + c_2 \sin \omega_0 t$ for any initial amplitude.
