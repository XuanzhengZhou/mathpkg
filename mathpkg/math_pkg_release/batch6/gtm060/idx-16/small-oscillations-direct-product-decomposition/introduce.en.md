This fundamental theorem states that a Lagrangian system performing small oscillations splits into $n$ independent one-dimensional harmonic oscillators. By simultaneously diagonalizing the kinetic and potential energy quadratic forms via a linear coordinate change $Q = Cq$, the coupled equations of motion decouple. The eigenvalues $\lambda_i$ of $B$ with respect to $A$ determine the character of each mode: oscillation ($\lambda > 0$), neutral equilibrium ($\lambda = 0$), or instability ($\lambda < 0$).
