Motions in a linearized Lagrangian system with Lagrangian $L_2 = T_2 - U_2$, where

$$T_2 = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} a_{ij}(0) \dot{q}_i \dot{q}_j, \quad U_2 = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} \left. \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial q_i \partial q_j} \right|_{q=0} q_i q_j,$$

are called **small oscillations** near the equilibrium position $q = q_0$. In a one-dimensional problem, the numbers $\tau_0 = 2\pi/\omega_0$ and $\omega_0$ are called the **period** and **frequency** of small oscillations.
