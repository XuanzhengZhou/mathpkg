In order to linearize the Lagrangian system

$$\frac{d}{dt} \frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q}} = \frac{\partial T}{\partial q} - \frac{\partial U}{\partial q}$$

in a neighborhood of the equilibrium position $q = 0$, it is sufficient to replace the kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2} a_{ij}(q) \dot{q}_i \dot{q}_j$ by its value at $q = 0$,

$$T_2 = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} a_{ij} \dot{q}_i \dot{q}_j, \quad a_{ij} = a_{ij}(0),$$

and replace the potential energy $U(q)$ by its quadratic part

$$U_2 = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} b_{ij} q_i q_j, \quad b_{ij} = \left. \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial q_i \partial q_j} \right|_{q=0}.$$
