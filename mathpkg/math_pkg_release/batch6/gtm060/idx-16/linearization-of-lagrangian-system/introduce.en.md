For a Lagrangian mechanical system near equilibrium, linearization simplifies to evaluating the kinetic energy at the equilibrium configuration and expanding the potential energy to second order. The resulting linearized system has Lagrangian $L_2 = T_2 - U_2$ with constant-coefficient quadratic forms. The proof passes through the Hamiltonian formulation: the quadratic part $H_2 = T_2(p) + U_2(q)$ determines the linear terms of Hamilton's equations.
