A periodic motion of the form

$$q(t) = (C_1 \cos \omega t + C_2 \sin \omega t) \xi, \quad B\xi = \lambda A\xi, \quad \lambda = \omega^2 > 0$$

is called a **characteristic oscillation** of the Lagrangian system with quadratic kinetic and potential energies $T = \frac{1}{2}(A\dot{q}, \dot{q})$ and $U = \frac{1}{2}(Bq, q)$. The number $\omega$ is called the **characteristic frequency**.

Characteristic oscillations are also called **principal oscillations** or **normal modes**. A nonpositive $\lambda$ also has eigenvectors; the corresponding motions are also called characteristic oscillations, although they are not periodic; the corresponding characteristic frequencies are imaginary.
