A characteristic oscillation (also called a principal oscillation or normal mode) is a periodic motion where all degrees of freedom oscillate with the same frequency $\omega$ along a fixed direction $\xi$ in configuration space. The direction $\xi$ is an eigenvector of the generalized eigenvalue problem $B\xi = \lambda A\xi$ with $\lambda = \omega^2 > 0$. These motions are the building blocks from which all small oscillations can be assembled by superposition.
