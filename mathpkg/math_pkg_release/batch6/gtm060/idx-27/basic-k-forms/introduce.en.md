Basic $k$-forms are the standard basis of the vector space of exterior $k$-forms on $\mathbb{R}^n$. They are the wedge products $x_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge x_{i_k}$ of coordinate 1-forms with strictly increasing indices. There are exactly $\binom{n}{k}$ such forms, and they span the space of all $k$-forms, giving the space dimension $\binom{n}{k}$. In particular, for $k=n$ there is only one basic $n$-form, $x_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_n$, which represents the oriented volume element.
