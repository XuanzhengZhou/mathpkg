The forms
$$x_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge x_{i_k}, \quad \text{where } 1 \leq i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_k \leq n,$$
are called \text{basic $k$-forms} on $\mathbb{R}^n$. They are linearly independent, and their number is $\binom{n}{k}$. Every $k$-form $\omega^k$ on $\mathbb{R}^n$ can be uniquely represented as a linear combination of basic forms:
$$\omega^k = \sum_{1 \leq i_1 < \cdots < i_k \leq n} a_{i_1, \ldots, i_k} \, x_{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge x_{i_k},$$
with coefficients $a_{i_1, \ldots, i_k} = \omega^k(\mathbf{e}_{i_1}, \ldots, \mathbf{e}_{i_k})$. Thus the dimension of the vector space of $k$-forms on $\mathbb{R}^n$ is $\binom{n}{k}$.
