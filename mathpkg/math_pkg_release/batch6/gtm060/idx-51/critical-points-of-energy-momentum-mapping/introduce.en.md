This theorem provides a variational characterization of relative equilibria: they are precisely the constrained critical points of the Hamiltonian $H$ on the momentum level set $M_p$. After reduction by $G_p$, these conditional extrema descend to ordinary critical points of the reduced Hamiltonian. This perspective is fundamental for studying the topology of invariant manifolds as energy and momentum vary, and for understanding bifurcations in symmetric Hamiltonian systems.
