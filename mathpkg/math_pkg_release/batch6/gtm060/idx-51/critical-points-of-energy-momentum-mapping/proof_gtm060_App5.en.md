The critical points of the mapping $P \times H$ are the conditional extrema of $H$ on the momentum level manifold $M_p$. This is because the level manifold is regular: for every $x \in M_p$, we have $P_* T_x M = T_p \mathfrak{g}^*$ (this follows from the fact that $p$ is a regular value of $P$).

After factorization by the stationary subgroup $G_p$, the conditional extrema of $H$ on $M_p$ define the critical points of the reduced hamiltonian function (since $H$ is invariant under $G_p$ by hypothesis). But the critical points of the reduced hamiltonian are, by definition, the equilibrium positions of the reduced system. Their preimages under the projection $\pi: M_p \to F_p$ are precisely the relative equilibria of the original system.
