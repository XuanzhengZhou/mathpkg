This corollary is the Hamiltonian formulation of Noether's theorem: every continuous symmetry of a Hamiltonian system yields a conserved quantity. If the Hamiltonian $H$ is invariant under a Poisson action of a Lie group $G$, then every component of the momentum mapping $P: M \to \mathfrak{g}^*$ is constant along the phase flow. This unifies the conservation of linear momentum (from translation invariance) and angular momentum (from rotational invariance) under a single geometric principle.
