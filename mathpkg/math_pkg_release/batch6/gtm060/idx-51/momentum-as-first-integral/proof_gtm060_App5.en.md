The derivative, in the direction of the phase flow with hamiltonian function $H$, of each component of the momentum is zero, since it is equal to the Poisson bracket of the corresponding hamiltonian function $H_a$ (a component of $P$) with $H$. By the equivariance theorem, $\{H_a, H\} = H_{[a, 0]} = 0$ because $H$ is invariant under the group action, meaning the derivative of $H$ along each one-parameter subgroup is zero. Therefore each component of $P$ is a first integral.
