The proof of this theorem is due to D.M. Galin and is based on the work of J. Williamson ("On an algebraic problem concerning the normal forms of linear dynamical systems," Amer. J. of Math. 58, (1936), 141-163). Williamson's paper gives the normal forms to which a quadratic form in a symplectic space over any field can be reduced. Galin extended this work by computing the codimension of each degenerate type, counting the number of independent parameters needed to specify a Hamiltonian with a given Jordan block configuration. The detailed computation involves the enumeration of versal deformations of each Jordan type and is beyond the scope of this appendix.
