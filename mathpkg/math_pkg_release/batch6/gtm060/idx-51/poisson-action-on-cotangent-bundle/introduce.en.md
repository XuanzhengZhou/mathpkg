When a Lie group acts on a smooth manifold, the action lifts naturally to the cotangent bundle. The hamiltonian functions of the lifted one-parameter subgroups are given by contracting the canonical 1-form with the velocity field. These hamiltonian functions are linear in the fiber (momentum) coordinates, and their Poisson brackets correspond to the Lie bracket of the algebra. This makes the lifted action a Poisson action, establishing the fundamental link between symmetries and conserved quantities in Hamiltonian mechanics.
