By definition of the 1-form $\alpha$, the hamiltonian functions $H_a$ are linear "in $p$" (i.e., on every cotangent space). Therefore, their Poisson brackets are also linear. Thus the function

$$H_{[a,b]} - \{H_a, H_b\}$$

is linear in $p$. Since it is constant, it is equal to zero. Hence $\{H_a, H_b\} = H_{[a,b]}$, which is precisely the condition for a Poisson action.
