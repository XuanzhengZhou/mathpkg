This corollary follows from the fact that a continuous function on a sphere must have at least two critical points (a maximum and a minimum). After reduction by the rotational symmetry, the reduced Hamiltonian becomes a function on the sphere $S^2$, guaranteeing at least two relative equilibria for each angular momentum value. This provides a topological lower bound on the number of stationary rotational motions.
