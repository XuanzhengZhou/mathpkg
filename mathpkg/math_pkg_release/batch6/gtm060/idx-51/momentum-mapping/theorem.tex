A Poisson action of a group $G$ on a symplectic manifold $M$ defines a mapping of $M$ into the dual space of the Lie algebra of the group:

$$P: M \rightarrow \mathfrak{g}^*.$$

We fix a point $x \in M$ and define $P(x)$ as the linear functional on $\mathfrak{g}$ whose value on $a \in \mathfrak{g}$ is the value at $x$ of the hamiltonian function $H_a$ of the one-parameter group generated by $a$:

$$P(x)(a) = H_a(x).$$

The mapping $P$ is called the momentum mapping. For the group of rotations, the values of the momentum are the vectors of angular momentum; for the group of parallel translations, the values are the vectors of linear momentum.
