For quadratic Hamiltonian systems, the linearized dynamics are governed by the eigenvalues of the infinitesimally-symplectic operator $IA$. These eigenvalues always come in symmetric groups: real pairs $(a,-a)$, purely imaginary pairs $(ib,-ib)$, complex quadruples $(\pm a \pm ib)$, and zero eigenvalues. This symmetry is forced by the symplectic structure. The classification of these eigenvalues and their associated Jordan blocks is the foundation for the normal form theory of linear Hamiltonian systems, worked out by J. Williamson in 1936.
