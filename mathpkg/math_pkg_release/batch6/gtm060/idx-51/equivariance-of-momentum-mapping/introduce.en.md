A fundamental property of the momentum mapping is its equivariance: it intertwines the Poisson action of a connected Lie group $G$ on the symplectic manifold with the co-adjoint action of $G$ on the dual of its Lie algebra. This means that applying a group element $g$ to a point in $M$ and then computing the momentum yields the same result as co-adjointly transforming the momentum of the original point. This equivariance is the geometric heart of Noether's theorem in its modern formulation.
