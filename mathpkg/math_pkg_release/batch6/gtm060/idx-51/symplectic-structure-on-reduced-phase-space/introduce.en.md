The reduced phase space $F_p$ inherits a natural symplectic structure from the original manifold $M$. The construction involves lifting tangent vectors on the quotient to representatives on the momentum level set $M_p$, then evaluating the original symplectic form. The theorem guarantees that this is well-defined: the result is independent of the choice of lift and of the point in the $G_p$-orbit. This makes symplectic reduction a fundamental tool for constructing new symplectic manifolds from ones with symmetry.
