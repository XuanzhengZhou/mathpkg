The reduced phase space is the central construction of symplectic reduction. Given a Hamiltonian system with a continuous symmetry group $G$, one fixes a value $p$ of the momentum mapping, then takes the quotient of the momentum level set $M_p$ by the residual symmetry group $G_p$. The result $F_p = M_p / G_p$ is a smaller symplectic manifold whose dynamics capture the essential "reduced" motion after factoring out the symmetry. This construction, developed by Marsden and Weinstein and by Meyer, generalizes the classical procedure of eliminating cyclic coordinates.
