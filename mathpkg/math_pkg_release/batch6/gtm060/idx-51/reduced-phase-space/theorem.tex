Let $G$ be a Lie group with a Poisson action on a symplectic manifold $M$, and let $P: M \to \mathfrak{g}^*$ be the momentum mapping. Fix a value $p \in \mathfrak{g}^*$ and consider the level set $M_p = P^{-1}(p)$. The stationary subgroup $G_p = \{g \in G : \operatorname{Ad}_{g^{-1}}^* p = p\}$ acts on $M_p$.

Assume that:
1. $p$ is a regular value, so that $M_p$ is a manifold,
2. The stationary subgroup $G_p$ is compact, and
3. The elements of $G_p$ act on $M_p$ without fixed points.

Then the orbit space $F_p = M_p / G_p$ is a smooth manifold, called the **reduced phase space** of the system with symmetry. The manifold $F_p$ is the base space of the bundle $\pi: M_p \to F_p$ with fiber diffeomorphic to $G_p$.
