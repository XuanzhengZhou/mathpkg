A symmetric (or Lagrangian) top is the only known completely integrable case of the heavy rigid body problem. The body is fixed at a point $O$; its inertia ellipsoid at $O$ is an ellipsoid of revolution, and its center of mass lies on the symmetry axis $e_3$. The rotational symmetry around $e_3$ leaves the Lagrangian invariant, and by Noether's theorem this yields a third first integral (the projection of angular momentum onto $e_3$), supplementing the total energy $E$ and the vertical angular momentum component $M_z$. With three first integrals for a system of three degrees of freedom, the symmetric top is Liouville-integrable and its motion decomposes into rotation, precession, and nutation.
