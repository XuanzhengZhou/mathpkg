In the case $I_2 = I_3 \neq I_1$, Euler's equations imply that the angular momentum vector $m$ and the axis $B_t e_1$ lie in a common plane with $\omega$ at every instant. From the conservation of $|m|$ and the kinetic energy $T$, the magnitude $|\omega|$ and the angle between $\omega$ and $m$ are preserved. The vector $m$ is fixed in the stationary space (by conservation of angular momentum), so the motion of $\omega$ and $B_t e_1$ relative to $m$ yields the precession of both axes around $m$ with the same angular velocity. The cone swept out by $\omega$ is called the space cone, and the cone swept out by $B_t e_1$ is called the body cone.
