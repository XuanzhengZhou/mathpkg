Let $I_2 = I_3 \neq I_1$ for a force-free rigid body fixed at a point $O$. Then the axis of the inertia ellipsoid $B_t e_1$, the instantaneous axis of rotation $\omega$, and the angular momentum vector $m$ always lie in one plane. The angles between them and the magnitude $|\omega|$ are preserved; the axes $\omega$ and $B_t e_1$ sweep out cones around the angular momentum vector $m$ with the same angular velocity. This motion around $m$ is called \textit{precession}.
