By the work-energy theorem: $T(t_1) - T(t_0) = \int_{r(t_0)}^{r(t_1)} (F, dr) = U(r(t_0)) - U(r(t_1))$ for a conservative field. Therefore $E(t_1) = T(t_1) + U(r(t_1)) = T(t_0) + U(r(t_0)) = E(t_0)$.

For the two-point interaction potential: $-\partial U_{ij}(r_i - r_j)/\partial r_i = -f_{ij} \cdot \partial|r_i - r_j|/\partial r_i = f_{ij} e_{ij}$, giving the correct force.
