A 2-DOF system is conservative if the force field $\mathbf{f}$ is the negative gradient of a potential energy $U$. Unlike the 1-DOF case where every system is conservative (a potential always exists via integration), in 2-DOF the condition $\partial f_1/\partial x_2 = \partial f_2/\partial x_1$ must hold for a potential to exist.
