The Lagrangian function $L = T - U$ is the fundamental scalar function from which the equations of motion are derived via Hamilton's principle of least action. Unlike the Newtonian formulation which uses forces (vectors), the Lagrangian approach uses energies (scalars), making it coordinate-independent and better suited for systems with constraints. The Lagrangian viewpoint is the gateway to modern mechanics, field theory, and Noether's theorem relating symmetries to conservation laws.
