By definition, $ad_\xi = \frac{d}{dt}|_{t=0} Ad_{e^{t\xi}}$. For any $\eta \in \mathfrak{g}$, $Ad_{e^{t\xi}}\eta$ is the derivative at $\tau = 0$ of $e^{t\xi} e^{\tau\eta} e^{-t\xi}$. Computing this gives $\frac{d}{d\tau}|_{\tau=0} e^{t\xi} e^{\tau\eta} e^{-t\xi} = L_{e^{t\xi}*} R_{e^{-t\xi}*} \eta$. Differentiating with respect to $t$ at $t = 0$ yields $[\xi, \eta]$ by definition of the Lie bracket.
