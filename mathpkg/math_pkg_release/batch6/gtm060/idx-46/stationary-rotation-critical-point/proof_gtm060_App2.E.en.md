By Theorem 4, Euler's equation on a coadjoint orbit $V$ is Hamiltonian with $H = T|_V$. A point is an equilibrium of a Hamiltonian system iff it is a critical point of the Hamiltonian. Since stationary rotations correspond exactly to equilibria of Euler's equation ($dM_c/dt = 0$), they are precisely the critical points of $H$ on $V$. The statement for $\omega_c$ follows by applying $A^{-1}$.
