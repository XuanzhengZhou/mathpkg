This theorem characterizes stationary rotations variationally: they are exactly the critical points of the kinetic energy restricted to coadjoint orbits. For the classical rigid body ($SO(3)$), this recovers the fact that steady rotations occur about the principal axes of the inertia ellipsoid, which are the critical points of the kinetic energy on the angular momentum sphere.
