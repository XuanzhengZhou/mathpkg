A geodesic variation vector field (also called a Jacobi field) describes how a one-parameter family of geodesics deviates from a reference geodesic. It is the infinitesimal separation between nearby geodesics. These fields satisfy the Jacobi equation, a second-order linear ODE along the geodesic whose coefficients involve the curvature tensor.
