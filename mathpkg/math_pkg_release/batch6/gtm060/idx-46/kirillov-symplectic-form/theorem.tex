Let $x$ be a point in $\mathfrak{g}^*$ and let $V \subset \mathfrak{g}^*$ be the orbit of $x$ under the coadjoint representation. At each point of $V$, there is a natural symplectic structure, called the \textbf{Kirillov form}. The symplectic structure is defined by: for vectors $\xi, \eta$ tangent to the orbit at $x$, which can be written as $\xi = ad^*_{f_1} x$, $\eta = ad^*_{f_2} x$ with $f_1, f_2 \in \mathfrak{g}$, the symplectic form is

$$\Omega(\xi, \eta) = (x, [f_1, f_2]).$$

The coadjoint orbits are always even-dimensional.
