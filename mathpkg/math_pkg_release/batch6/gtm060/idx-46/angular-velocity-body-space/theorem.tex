Let $G$ be a Lie group and $g = g(t)$ a curve describing the motion of a generalized rigid body. The tangent vector $\dot{g} \in TG_g$ can be translated to the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g} = TG_e$ in two ways:

$$\omega_c = L_{g^{-1}*} \dot{g} \in \mathfrak{g}, \quad \omega_s = R_{g^{-1}*} \dot{g} \in \mathfrak{g}.$$

The vector $\omega_c$ is called the \textbf{angular velocity in the body} (or "relative to the body"), and $\omega_s$ the \textbf{angular velocity in space} (or "relative to space").
