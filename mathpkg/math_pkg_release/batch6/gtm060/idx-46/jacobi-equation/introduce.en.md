The Jacobi equation governs the infinitesimal separation of nearby geodesics on a Riemannian manifold. It is a second-order linear ODE along a reference geodesic, with the curvature tensor acting as a "spring constant." When curvature is positive, nearby geodesics tend to converge (like on a sphere); when negative, they diverge exponentially. This equation is fundamental to the study of geodesic stability and conjugate points.
