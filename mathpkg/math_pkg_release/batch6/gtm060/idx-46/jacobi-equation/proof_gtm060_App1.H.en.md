The proof follows from differentiating the geodesic equation with respect to the variation parameter $\alpha$. Let $x(t,\alpha)$ satisfy the geodesic equation $\frac{D}{Dt} \frac{\partial x}{\partial t} = 0$ for each $\alpha$. Differentiating this condition with respect to $\alpha$ at $\alpha = 0$, and commuting the covariant derivatives $\frac{D}{Dt}$ and $\frac{D}{D\alpha}$ using the definition of the curvature tensor, yields the Jacobi equation. The curvature term $-\Omega(v,\xi)v$ arises from the commutation formula $\frac{D}{Dt}\frac{D}{D\alpha} - \frac{D}{D\alpha}\frac{D}{Dt} = \Omega(\frac{\partial x}{\partial t}, \frac{\partial x}{\partial \alpha})$.
