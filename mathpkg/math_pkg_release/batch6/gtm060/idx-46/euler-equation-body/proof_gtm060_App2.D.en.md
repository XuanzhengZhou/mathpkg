From the definition $M_s = R_g^* M$ and Theorem 1 ($dM_s/dt = 0$), we compute $dM_c/dt$ where $M_c = L_g^* M$. Using the relation $M_s = Ad_g^* M_c$ (where $Ad_g^*$ is the coadjoint representation of the group), and differentiating, one obtains $dM_c/dt = ad^*_{\omega_c} M_c = \{\omega_c, M_c\}$. The proof for a generalized rigid body is formally identical to the classical case ($G = SO(3)$) where $\{\omega_c, M_c\}$ corresponds to $M_c \times \omega_c$ (cross product).
