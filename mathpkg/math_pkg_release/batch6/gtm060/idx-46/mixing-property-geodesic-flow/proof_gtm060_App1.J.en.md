The mixing property of geodesic flows on compact manifolds of negative curvature was proved by D. V. Anosov and Ya. G. Sinai in the 1960s. The proof uses the Anosov (U-system) structure: the tangent bundle splits into stable, unstable, and flow directions, with exponential contraction/expansion along stable/unstable manifolds. The Hopf argument then establishes mixing.
