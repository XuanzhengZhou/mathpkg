Parallel translate any vector along the line

$$x_1 = t, \quad x_2 = 0, \quad y = 1 \quad (0 \leq t \leq \tau)$$

in a Lobachevsky space with metric

$$ds^2 = \frac{dx_1^2 + dx_2^2 + dy^2}{y^2}.$$

Answer. Vectors in the directions of the $x_1$ and $y$ axes are rotated by angle $\tau$ in the plane spanned by them (rotation is in the direction from the $y$-axis towards the $x_1$-axis); vectors in the $x_2$-direction are carried parallel to themselves in the sense of the euclidean metric.
