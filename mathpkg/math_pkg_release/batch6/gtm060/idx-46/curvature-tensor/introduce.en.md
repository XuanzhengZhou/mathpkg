The curvature tensor is the fundamental object encoding the intrinsic curvature of a Riemannian manifold. It generalizes the Gaussian curvature of surfaces to higher dimensions. For each pair of tangent vectors $\xi, \eta$ at a point $x$, the curvature tensor $\Omega(\xi,\eta)$ produces a skew-symmetric operator on the tangent space $TM_x$, representing the infinitesimal rotation experienced by a vector after parallel transport around the parallelogram spanned by $\xi$ and $\eta$. The map $\xi,\eta \mapsto \Omega(\xi,\eta)$ is bilinear and skew-symmetric in $\xi, \eta$, and takes values in the space of skew-symmetric operators.
