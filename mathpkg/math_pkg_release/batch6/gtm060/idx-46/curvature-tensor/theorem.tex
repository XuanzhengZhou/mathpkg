Let $\xi$ and $\eta$ be vectors tangent to a riemannian manifold $M$ at a point $x$. Construct a small curvilinear parallelogram $\Pi_\varepsilon$ on $M$ whose sides are obtained from the vectors $\varepsilon\xi$ and $\varepsilon\eta$ by a coordinate identification of a neighborhood of zero in $TM_x$ with a neighborhood of $x$ in $M$. The result of parallel translation along the sides of $\Pi_\varepsilon$ (starting the circuit at $\xi$) is an orthogonal transformation of $TM_x$, close to the identity. Any orthogonal operator $A$ close to the identity can be written as

$$A = e^{\Phi} = E + \Phi + \frac{\Phi^2}{2!} + \cdots,$$

where $\Phi$ is a small skew-symmetric operator. The curvature tensor $\Omega$ is defined by the relation

$$\Omega(\xi, \eta) = -\Phi,$$

describing the infinitesimal rotation caused by parallel translation around an infinitesimal parallelogram spanned by $\xi$ and $\eta$.
