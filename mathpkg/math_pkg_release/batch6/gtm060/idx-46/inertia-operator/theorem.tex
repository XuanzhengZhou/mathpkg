Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be the Lie algebra of a Lie group $G$. A left-invariant riemannian metric on $G$ is given by a symmetric positive-definite linear operator $A: \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}^*$, called the \textbf{inertia operator}, satisfying

$$\langle A\xi, \eta \rangle = \langle A\eta, \xi \rangle \quad \text{for all } \xi, \eta \in \mathfrak{g}.$$

The scalar product on $\mathfrak{g}$ is $\langle \xi, \eta \rangle = (A\xi, \eta)$. The kinetic energy is $T = \frac{1}{2}\langle \omega, \omega \rangle = \frac{1}{2}(A\omega, \omega)$.
