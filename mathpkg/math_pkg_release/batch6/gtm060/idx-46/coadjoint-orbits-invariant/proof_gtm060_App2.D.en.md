The right-hand side of Euler's equation at $M_c$ is $\{\omega_c, M_c\} = ad^*_{\omega_c} M_c$, which is precisely a tangent vector to the coadjoint orbit through $M_c$ (since the tangent space to the orbit at $M_c$ consists of all vectors of the form $ad^*_\xi M_c$ for $\xi \in \mathfrak{g}$). Therefore the Euler vector field is everywhere tangent to the coadjoint orbits, making them invariant manifolds.
