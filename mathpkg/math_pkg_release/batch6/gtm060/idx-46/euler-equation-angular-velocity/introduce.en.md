This theorem gives Euler's equation directly in terms of angular velocity rather than angular momentum. The right-hand side $B(\omega_c, \omega_c)$ is quadratic and purely algebraic (involving only the Lie bracket and the inertia operator). For $SO(3)$, this reduces to Euler's familiar equations $\dot{\omega}_1 = a_1 \omega_2 \omega_3$, etc., where the constants $a_i$ depend on the principal moments of inertia.
