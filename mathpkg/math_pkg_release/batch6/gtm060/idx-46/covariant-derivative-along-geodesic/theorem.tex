Let $\zeta(t)$ be a vector field given on the geodesic $x(t)$. Take the vector $\zeta(t + h)$, parallel translate it from $x(t + h)$ to $x(t)$ along the geodesic, differentiate the resulting vector in the tangent space $TM_{x(t)}$ with respect to $h$, and evaluate at $h = 0$. The result is a vector in $TM_{x(t)}$, called the \textbf{covariant derivative} of $\zeta(t)$ with respect to $t$, denoted by $D\zeta/Dt$.
