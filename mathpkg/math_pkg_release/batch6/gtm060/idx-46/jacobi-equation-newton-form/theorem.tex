For the normal component of the variation vector $\xi$ (perpendicular to the velocity $v$), the Jacobi equation takes the form of Newton's equation

$$\frac{D^2 \xi}{Dt^2} = -\operatorname{grad} U,$$

where the quadratic form $U$ of the vector $\xi$ is expressed in terms of the curvature tensor:

$$U(\xi) = \frac{1}{2} \langle \Omega(v, \xi)v, \xi \rangle = \frac{1}{2} K \langle \xi, \xi \rangle \langle v, v \rangle,$$

and $K$ is the curvature of $M$ in the direction of the $(\xi, v)$ plane.
