The normal component of the Jacobi equation can be recast as Newton's equation of motion for a linear oscillator with potential energy $U(\xi) = \frac{1}{2}K|\xi|^2|v|^2$. When $K > 0$, the potential is positive-definite (stable oscillator); when $K < 0$, it is negative-definite (unstable, leading to exponential divergence). This mechanical analogy makes the behavior of nearby geodesics intuitively clear.
