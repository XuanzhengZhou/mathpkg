Let $G$ be a Lie group with Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g} = TG_e$. For each $g \in G$, define the inner automorphism $A_g: G \to G$ by $A_g(h) = ghg^{-1}$. Its derivative at the identity is a linear map on the Lie algebra:

$$Ad_g = (A_g)_{*e} : \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}.$$

The map $Ad: G \to \operatorname{GL}(\mathfrak{g})$ given by $Ad(g) = Ad_g$ is called the \textbf{adjoint representation} of the group $G$.
