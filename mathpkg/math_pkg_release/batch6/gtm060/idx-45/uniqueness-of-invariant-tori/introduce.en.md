Action-angle variables for an integrable Hamiltonian system are not unique; their construction involves choices such as the selection of basis cycles on the invariant tori. Despite this arbitrariness, the nondegeneracy condition guarantees that the invariant tori themselves are intrinsic geometric objects: they can be characterized as the closures of phase trajectories with independent frequencies, independent of any coordinate choices.
