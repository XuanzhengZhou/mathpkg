Translate a vector directed along the imaginary axis at the point $z = i$ to the point $z = t + i$ along the horizontal line ($dy = 0$) on the Lobachevsky plane (upper half-plane with metric $ds^2 = \frac{dx^2 + dy^2}{y^2}$).

**Answer.** Under translation by $t$ the vector turns $t$ radians in the direction from the $y$-axis towards the $x$-axis.
