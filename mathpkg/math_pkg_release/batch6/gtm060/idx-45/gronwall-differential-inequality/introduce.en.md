This is a simple but useful Gronwall-type estimate for differential inequalities. It states that if the absolute derivative of a quantity $z$ is bounded by a linear function of $|z|$ plus a constant, then $|z(t)|$ grows at most exponentially with the constant term contributing linearly in time. The proof follows by comparing $|z(t)|$ with the exact solution of the corresponding differential equation $\dot{y} = ay + b$.
