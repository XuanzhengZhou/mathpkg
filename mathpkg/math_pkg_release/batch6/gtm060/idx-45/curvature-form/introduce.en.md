The curvature form $\Omega$ captures the intrinsic curvature of a Riemannian surface in a single differential 2-form. It is defined by the angle of rotation a tangent vector undergoes when parallel transported around the boundary of a small region. The additivity of this rotation angle under subdivision of regions ensures that $\Omega$ is a genuine 2-form. For the plane $\Omega = 0$, for a sphere of radius $R$ we have $\Omega = R^{-2} dS$, and for the Lobachevsky plane $\Omega = -dS$, where $dS$ is the area element.
