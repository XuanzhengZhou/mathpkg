Let $\Lambda$ be a matroid. Then the following are equivalent:

(a) $\Lambda$ is regular.
(b) $\Lambda$ admits a unimodular Tutte subspace (over $\mathbb{Q}$).
(c) $\Lambda$ is binary and no minor of $\Lambda$ is a Fano matroid.

M. J. Piff and D. J. A. Welsh [p.5] have shown that every gammoid, and hence every transversal matroid, is representable over all sufficiently large fields. We close by presenting in the form of an exercise an example of a matroid which is representable over no field at all. The reader may recognize part (b) as part of the classical theorem of Desargues.
