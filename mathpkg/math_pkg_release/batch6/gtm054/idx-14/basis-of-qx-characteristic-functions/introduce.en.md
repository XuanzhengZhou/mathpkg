Just as in the Boolean case $\mathbb{K}^X$, the singleton characteristic functions $\{c_x : x \in X\}$ form a basis for the vector space $\mathbb{Q}^X$. This means any function $h \in \mathbb{Q}^X$ can be uniquely expressed as a finite linear combination $h = \sum_{x \in X} h(x) c_x$. This basis property is fundamental for the development of network theory in subsequent sections, where flows and potentials are expressed as linear combinations of elementary characteristic functions corresponding to vertices of the underlying graph.
