For graphs with invariant $\iota(\Gamma) < 2$, this corollary gives sharp chromatic number bounds depending on the girth $\gamma(\Gamma)$. Specifically, if the girth is at least 4, the chromatic number is at most 4; if the girth is at least 6, it is at most 3. These bounds refine the general Heawood-type estimates for graphs with small iota invariant. Compare with results B7 and B8.
