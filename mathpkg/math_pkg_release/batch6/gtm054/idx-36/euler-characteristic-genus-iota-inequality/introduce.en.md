This proposition establishes fundamental inequalities connecting the topological invariants of a graph. The first inequality bounds the Euler characteristic from below in terms of the genus: $2 - \varepsilon(\Gamma) \leq 2\eta(\Gamma)$. For connected graphs, the second inequality bounds the invariant $\iota(\Gamma)$ by $2 - \varepsilon(\Gamma)$. Together with other results, these inequalities lead to Heawood-type upper bounds for the chromatic number.
