This corollary provides an upper bound for the chromatic number $\chi_0(\Gamma)$ of a nonplanar graph $\Gamma$ in terms of the invariant $\iota(\Gamma)$. The bound involves the Heawood function $H(3, k) = \lfloor(7 + \sqrt{1 + 24k})/2\rfloor$ evaluated at $k = \iota(\Gamma)$. This result follows from C12 and Exercise C3, and the observation that $H(3, 0) = 6$ shows that it yields no new information for planar graphs.
