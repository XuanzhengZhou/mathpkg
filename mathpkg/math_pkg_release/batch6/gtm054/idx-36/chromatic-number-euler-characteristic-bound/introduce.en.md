This corollary provides upper bounds for the chromatic number of a nonplanar graph in terms of two fundamental topological invariants: the Euler characteristic $\varepsilon(\Gamma)$ and the genus $\eta(\Gamma)$. The right-hand inequality follows from the relationship between the Euler characteristic and the genus established in D3. The left-hand inequality is proved by reducing to a component achieving the chromatic number and applying C13 and C6 together with the inequality $\iota(\Gamma) \leq 2 - \varepsilon(\Gamma)$ from D3.
