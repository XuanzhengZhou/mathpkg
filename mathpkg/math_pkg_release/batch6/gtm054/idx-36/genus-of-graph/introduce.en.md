The genus $\eta(\Gamma)$ of a graph $\Gamma$ is defined as the minimum genus among all orientable surfaces in which $\Gamma$ admits a topological imbedding. An imbedding achieving this minimum is called a minimal imbedding. The genus is monotone with respect to subgraphs: if $\Theta$ is a subgraph of $\Gamma$, then $\eta(\Theta) \leq \eta(\Gamma)$. This invariant plays a central role in topological graph theory and is closely related to the chromatic number via Heawood-type bounds.
