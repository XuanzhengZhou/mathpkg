The \textbf{Euler characteristic} $\varepsilon(\Gamma)$ of $\Gamma$ is the Euler characteristic of the surface $S$ of greatest Euler characteristic such that $\Gamma$ can be topologically imbedded in $S$. An imbedding of $\Gamma$ in a surface of Euler characteristic $\varepsilon(\Gamma)$ is called a \textbf{simplest imbedding}.

For any subgraph $\Theta$ of $\Gamma$, clearly $\varepsilon(\Theta) \geq \varepsilon(\Gamma)$.
