Show that the number of necklaces that can be made with $k_1$ orange beads and $k_2$ blue beads, where $k_1 + k_2$ is an odd prime is

$$\frac{1}{2(k_1 + k_2)} \binom{k_1 + k_2}{k_1} + \frac{1}{2} \binom{(k_1 + k_2 - 1)/2}{[k_1/2]}.$$

No doubt the reader has already surmised that as a practical counting tool, the Pólya Theorem is hardly the epitome of simplicity and efficiency. Even so simple a situation as the necklace problem can lead to a considerable quantity of tedious calculations. In the following exercise we suggest some other patterns to enumerate which are relatively simple. The reader is encouraged to attempt variations on these.
