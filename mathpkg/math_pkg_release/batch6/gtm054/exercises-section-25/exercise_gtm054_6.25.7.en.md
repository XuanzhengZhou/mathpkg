(H. J. Ryser [r.9]). Let $\mathcal{Q}_i \in \mathbb{P}_m(U)$ for $i = 1, 2$.

(a) Prove that $(U, \mathcal{Q}_1)$ and $(U, \mathcal{Q}_2)$ admit an LCR if and only if for all $\mathcal{S} \subseteq \mathcal{Q}_1 \cup \mathcal{Q}_2$,

$$\mathcal{S} \text{ covers } U \Rightarrow |\mathcal{S}| \geq m.$$

(b) Prove that if $\mathcal{Q}_1 \cup \mathcal{Q}_2 \subseteq \mathcal{P}_n(U)$ for some $n$, then $(U, \mathcal{Q}_1)$ and $(U, \mathcal{Q}_2)$ admit an LCR.
