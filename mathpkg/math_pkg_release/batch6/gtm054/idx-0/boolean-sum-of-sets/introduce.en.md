The Boolean sum (also known as symmetric difference) of two sets $S$ and $T$ consists of elements belonging to exactly one of the two sets. It serves as the primary set operation in this text, replacing the usual notation for complementation since $S + U$ equals the complement of $S$ in the universal set $U$. The Boolean sum satisfies commutativity and associativity.
