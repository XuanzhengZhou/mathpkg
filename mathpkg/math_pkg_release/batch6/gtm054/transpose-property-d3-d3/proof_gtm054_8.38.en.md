Let $\Gamma$ be bipartite and let $S$ be a set of vertices of $\Gamma$. If $S$ is independent, then $\omega(\Gamma_s) = 1 = \chi_0(\Gamma_s)$, and $\alpha_0(\Gamma_s) = |S| = \theta(\Gamma_s)$. If $S$ is not independent, then since $\Gamma_s$ is also bipartite, $\omega(\Gamma_s) = 2 = \chi_0(\Gamma_s)$. (Cf. VIIA6.) Since suppressing the isolated vertices of $\Gamma_s$ would decrease $\alpha_0(\Gamma_s)$ and $\theta(\Gamma_s)$ each by the exact number of such vertices, we may assume that $\Gamma_s$ has no isolated vertices. Hence $\theta(\Gamma_s)$ is precisely $\beta_{10}(\Gamma_s)$, and by VC11, $\beta_{10}(\Gamma_s) = \alpha_0(\Gamma_s)$.
