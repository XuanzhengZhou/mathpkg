In determining $\varepsilon(\Gamma)$ one is not restricted to consideration of orientable manifolds. Hence the first inequality follows from D1.

Now suppose that $\Gamma$ is connected. Let $Z_1, \ldots, Z_m$ be the cycles of the regions of some simplest—and hence 2-cell—imbedding of $\Gamma$. Since $\nu_{-1}(\Gamma) = 1$, C1 and D2 yield

$$\iota(\Gamma) \leq \iota(Z_1, \ldots, Z_m) = 2 - \nu_0(\Gamma) + \nu_1(\Gamma) - m = 2 - \varepsilon(\Gamma).$$
