A doubly-capacitated network generalizes the integral network $(V, k)$ by assigning both lower and upper capacity bounds to each edge. Each edge $e$ must carry a flow between $k_1(e)$ and $k_2(e)$, representing minimum and maximum permissible flow respectively. This models pipeline systems where links must maintain a certain minimum flow to prevent deterioration, in addition to respecting maximum capacity limits.
