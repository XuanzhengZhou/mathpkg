Proposition G2 establishes that any feasible flow through an edge $e_0$ is bounded between the lower and upper capacities of any cut through $e_0$. The proof follows from the orthogonality of the flow vector $h$ and the characteristic vector $g_U$ of the cut, together with the capacity constraints $k_1(e) \leq h(e) \leq k_2(e)$. This inequality is the doubly-capacitated analog of the standard max-flow min-cut bound.
