In a doubly-capacitated network $(V, k_1, k_2)$, a flow $h$ is feasible precisely when it satisfies both the lower and upper capacity constraints on every edge. This means the flow on each edge must be at least the minimum required flow $k_1(e)$ and at most the maximum allowed flow $k_2(e)$. An ordinary integral network $(V, k)$ corresponds to the special case $(V, 0, k)$ where the lower capacities are uniformly zero.
