In a doubly-capacitated network, each cut $C$ through a designated edge $e_0$ is assigned two capacity values: an upper capacity $k_2(C; e_0)$ and a lower capacity $k_1(C; e_0)$. The upper capacity sums the upper bounds on forward edges and subtracts lower bounds on backward edges, while the lower capacity uses the opposite arrangement. These quantities provide bounds on any feasible flow through $e_0$, as formalized in Proposition G2.
