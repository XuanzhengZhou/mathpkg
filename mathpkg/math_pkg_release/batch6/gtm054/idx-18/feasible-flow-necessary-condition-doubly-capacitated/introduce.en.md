Theorem G5 addresses the fundamental question of when a feasible flow exists in a doubly-capacitated network. From the inequality $k_2(C; e_0) \geq h(e_0) \geq k_1(e_0)$ and choosing $C$ appropriately, a necessary condition emerges: for every proper nonempty subset $U$ of vertices, the sum of upper capacities on forward edges minus the sum of lower capacities on backward edges must be nonnegative. This condition is also sufficient, as demonstrated in Exercise G6.
