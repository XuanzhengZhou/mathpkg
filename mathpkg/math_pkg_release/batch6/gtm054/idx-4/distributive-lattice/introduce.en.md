A distributive lattice is one in which the meet and join operations distribute over each other. By Proposition B32, it suffices to verify any one of the three equivalent distributive conditions. The canonical example is the Boolean lattice of all subsets of a set, where meet is intersection and join is union. Distributivity is a crucial structural property that distinguishes well-behaved lattices from more exotic ones and is essential for representation theorems like the bijection between complemented distributive lattices and power sets of their atoms.
