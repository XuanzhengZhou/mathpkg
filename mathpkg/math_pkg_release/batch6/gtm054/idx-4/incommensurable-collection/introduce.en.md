An incommensurable collection of subsets is one in which no member is contained in another — the sets are pairwise incomparable under inclusion. This property generalizes the notion of an antichain in the Boolean lattice $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$. Incommensurability is a key structural property used throughout combinatorial set theory, particularly in the study of Sperner families and in the characterization of elementary sets.
