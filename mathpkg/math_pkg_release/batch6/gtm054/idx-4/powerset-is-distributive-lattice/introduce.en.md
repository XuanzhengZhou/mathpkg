For any set $U$, the power set $\mathcal{P}(U)$ ordered by inclusion forms a distributive lattice where meet is intersection and join is union. This is the prototypical example of a distributive lattice and, more specifically, of a Boolean lattice. The distributivity follows directly from the set-theoretic distributive laws $A \cap (B \cup C) = (A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C)$ and its dual.
