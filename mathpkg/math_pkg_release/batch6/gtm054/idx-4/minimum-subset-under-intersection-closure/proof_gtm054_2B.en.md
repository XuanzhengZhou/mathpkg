Let $\mathcal{C} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(U)$ be nonempty and closed under $\cap$. Since $\mathcal{C}$ is finite (as a subcollection of the finite power set $\mathcal{P}(U)$ when $U$ is finite) or by properties of closure under finite intersections, the intersection of all members of $\mathcal{C}$ belongs to $\mathcal{C}$ and is the minimum element with respect to $\subseteq$. The dual statement for $\cup$ follows by the duality principle in the lattice $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$.
