A pair $(V, D)$, where $V$ is a set and $D \subseteq (V \times V) \setminus \{(v, v): v \in V\}$, is called a \textit{directed graph}. The elements of $V$ are called \textit{vertices} and the elements of $D$ are called \textit{edges}. A sequence of vertices and edges of the form

$$v_0, (v_0, v_1), v_1, (v_1, v_2), v_2, \dots, v_{k-1}, (v_{k-1}, v_k), v_k$$

is called a \textit{path} of length $k$ from $v_0$ to $v_k$. If $v_0 = v_k$ and $k > 0$, the path is called a \textit{directed circuit}. A directed graph with no directed circuits is called \textit{acyclic}.
