A directed graph (or digraph) is a fundamental combinatorial structure consisting of a set of vertices and a collection of ordered pairs (edges) representing directed relationships between vertices. Loops are excluded by definition. Paths, circuits, and acyclicity are defined in terms of edge-vertex sequences. A directed graph induces a binary relation on its vertex set, which is reflexive and transitive precisely when the graph determines a preorder.
