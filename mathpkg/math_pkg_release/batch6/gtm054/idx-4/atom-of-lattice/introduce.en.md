An atom in a lattice is a minimal non-zero element — it covers the bottom element $0$ in the order. Atoms are the fundamental building blocks of atomic lattices. In the Boolean lattice $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$, the atoms are exactly the singleton subsets. Any two distinct atoms meet to $0$, and the join of distinct atoms strictly exceeds each individual atom. The set of atoms plays a central role in the structure theory of complemented distributive lattices.
