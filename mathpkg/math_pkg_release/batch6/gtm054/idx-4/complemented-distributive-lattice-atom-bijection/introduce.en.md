Proposition B41 establishes a fundamental representation theorem: every complemented distributive lattice is isomorphic to the power set of its set of atoms. The bijection $f$ sends each subset of atoms to the join (direct sum $\oplus$) of its elements. The proof has two main parts: showing surjectivity by proving that the top element $1$ is the join of all atoms, and establishing that every element can then be expressed as a join of the atoms below it. This result generalizes the well-known fact that the Boolean lattice $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$ is isomorphic to the power set of the singletons.
