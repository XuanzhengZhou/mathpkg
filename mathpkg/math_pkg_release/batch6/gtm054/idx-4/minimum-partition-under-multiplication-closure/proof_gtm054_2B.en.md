Let $\mathcal{C} \subseteq \mathbb{P}(U)$ be nonempty and closed under partition multiplication. For any two partitions $\mathcal{Q}_1, \mathcal{Q}_2 \in \mathcal{C}$, their product $\mathcal{Q}_1 \mathcal{Q}_2$ is also in $\mathcal{C}$. The product of all partitions in $\mathcal{C}$ (or by induction on $|\mathcal{C}|$) yields a partition $\mathcal{Q}^*$ that refines every member of $\mathcal{C}$, hence is the minimum element with respect to refinement $\leq$. This follows from the fact that multiplication of partitions gives the greatest common refinement.
