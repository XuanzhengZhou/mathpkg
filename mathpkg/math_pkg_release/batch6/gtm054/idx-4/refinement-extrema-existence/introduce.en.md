Given a collection $\mathcal{S}$ of partitions of a set $U$, one can consider the family of all partitions coarser than every member of $\mathcal{S}$ (those $\mathcal{Q}$ with $\mathcal{S} \leq \mathcal{Q}$) and the family of all partitions finer than every member of $\mathcal{S}$ (those $\mathcal{Q}$ with $\mathcal{Q} \leq \mathcal{S}$). This proposition asserts that each of these two families contains a unique extremal element: a coarsest common coarsening (minimum) and a finest common refinement (maximum). The proof relies on showing that the family of coarser partitions is closed under partition multiplication, then invoking a previous corollary about minimum elements.
