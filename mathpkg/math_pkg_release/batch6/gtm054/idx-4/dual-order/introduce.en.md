Every partially ordered set admits a dual order obtained simply by reversing the direction of the order relation. When applied to a lattice, the dual order produces the dual lattice in which the roles of meet and join are interchanged. For instance, the dual of the Boolean lattice $(\mathcal{P}(U), \subseteq)$ is $(\mathcal{P}(U), \supseteq)$, where intersections become joins and unions become meets. The duality principle allows every lattice-theoretic statement to have a dual counterpart.
