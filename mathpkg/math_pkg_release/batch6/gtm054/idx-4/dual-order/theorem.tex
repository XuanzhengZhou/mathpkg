If $(U, \leq)$ is a partially ordered set, the \textit{dual order} $\geq$ on $U$ is defined by: $x \geq y$ if and only if $y \leq x$, for all $x, y \in U$. Then $(U, \geq)$ is also a partially ordered set. In particular, if $(U, \leq)$ is a lattice with meet and join denoted by $\wedge$ and $\vee$, respectively, then $(U, \geq)$ is its \textit{dual lattice}, with meet and join given by $\vee$ and $\wedge$, respectively.
