By definition, a design may be degenerate for either of two reasons: either $s^*(E) > 0$ or $\bar{s}(V) > 0$. Duality interchanges the roles of vertices and blocks, hence interchanges the two definitions of degeneracy. The preceding result (invariance of certain properties under duality) implies that if a $(t; a)$-design is degenerate for one reason (say $\bar{s}(V) > 0$), then by considering the dual design, one finds $s^*(E) = \lambda^*(a)$ and conversely. The equalities $\bar{s}(V) = \lambda(t)$ and $s^*(E) = \lambda^*(a)$ follow directly from the definition of the surplus functions and the design parameters.
