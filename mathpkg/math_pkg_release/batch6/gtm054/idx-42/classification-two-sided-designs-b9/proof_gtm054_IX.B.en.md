The proof follows from Proposition A5 and Corollary B8. Corollary B8 implies that for a nondegenerate two-sided $(t;a)$-design with $t \geq 2$ and $a \geq 2$, we must have $ta \leq 4$. The only possibilities with both parameters at least 2 are $(t,a) = (2,2)$ and $(t,a) = (1,2)$ or $(2,1)$ (but $t \geq 2$ rules out $(1,2)$).

For $(2;2)$ designs, these correspond to symmetric $\lambda$-linked designs (type (d)). For the case where one parameter is 1, we obtain types (a) and (b) as transposes of each other. The case $(1,2;1,2)$ yields nontrivial symmetric block designs (type (c)). Type (e) covers the trivial design where both the vertex design and block design contain all possible subsets.

Conversely, one checks that each of these five types indeed describes the design-type of some nondegenerate two-sided design.
