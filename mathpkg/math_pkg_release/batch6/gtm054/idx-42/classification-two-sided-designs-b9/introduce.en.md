This theorem provides a complete classification of all possible design-types for nondegenerate two-sided designs. Using Proposition A5 and Corollary B8, the result shows that only five families are possible: (a) nontrivial $t$-designs with block parameter 1, (b) their transposes, (c) nontrivial symmetric block designs of type $(1,2;1,2)$, (d) symmetric $\lambda$-linked designs of type $(2;2)$, and (e) trivial designs where both vertex and block designs are full. This classification is exhaustive and demonstrates the severe restrictions on two-sided designs.
