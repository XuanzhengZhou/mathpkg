This corollary sharply restricts the possible parameters of nondegenerate two-sided designs. If both the vertex parameter $t$ and block parameter $a$ are at least 2, and their product exceeds 4, the design must be either degenerate or trivial. The proof is concise: assuming nondegeneracy, Theorem B6(a) and its dual transpose yield $v = b$; then applying B6(b) or its dual for $ta > 4$ forces triviality. Together with Proposition A5, this result enables a complete classification of two-sided design-types in Theorem B9.
