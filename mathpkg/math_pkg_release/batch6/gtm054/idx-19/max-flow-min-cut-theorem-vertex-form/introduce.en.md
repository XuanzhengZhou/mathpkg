The Vertex Form of the Max-Flow–Min-Cut Theorem is a version where capacities are placed on vertices rather than edges. In this formulation, a flow through a designated vertex $x_0$ is bounded by the minimum total vertex capacity of a vertex-cut separating $x_0$ from itself in the directed circuit sense. The theorem is proved by constructing a transformation (F3) that maps the vertex-capacitated problem to the edge-capacitated network flow problem already solved by the Max-Flow–Min-Cut Theorem, and showing that the maximum flows coincide (F4). This vertex form is closely related to Menger's Theorem.
