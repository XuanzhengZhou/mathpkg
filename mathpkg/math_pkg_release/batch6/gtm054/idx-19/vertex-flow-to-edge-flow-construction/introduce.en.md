This construction provides a bijection between feasible flows in a vertex-capacitated directed graph and feasible flows in an associated edge-capacitated network. Each vertex $x$ is split into a pair of vertices $x^1, x^2$, with the original edges $(x,y)$ mapped to edges from $x^1$ to $y^2$, and the vertex flow value $h(x)$ accounted for by an edge from $x^2$ to $x^1$. This transformation is the key step in reducing the Vertex Form of the Max-Flow–Min-Cut Theorem to the already-proved edge-capacitated version.
