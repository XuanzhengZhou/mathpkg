This identity states that for a flow $h$ in a directed graph $(V, D)$, the total inflow at any vertex $x$ equals the total outflow at $x$. The value $h(x)$ is defined as the sum of flow entering $x$ from all other vertices. The equality follows from the flow conservation property B2, which was established earlier in the book. This formula is fundamental to the vertex-capacitated formulation of the Max-Flow–Min-Cut Theorem.
