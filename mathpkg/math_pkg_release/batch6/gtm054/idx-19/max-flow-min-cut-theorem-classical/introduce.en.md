The Max-Flow–Min-Cut Theorem in its classical form states that in a directed network with a source $x_0$, a sink $y_0$, and integer-valued capacities, the maximum value of a feasible flow from source to sink equals the minimum capacity of a cut separating the source from the sink. This is the formulation due to L. R. Ford, Jr. and D. R. Fulkerson that is most commonly encountered in the optimization literature. The theorem is equivalent to the algebraic formulation C6 proved in the same chapter for integral networks.
