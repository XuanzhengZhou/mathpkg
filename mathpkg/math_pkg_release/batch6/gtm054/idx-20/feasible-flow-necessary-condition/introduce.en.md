Proposition G5 gives a necessary condition for a doubly-capacitated network to admit a feasible flow: for every nonempty proper subset $U$ of vertices, the quantity $k(U)$ — defined as the sum of upper capacities on forward edges across the cut minus the sum of lower capacities on reverse edges — must be nonnegative. This follows directly from the bound in G2 together with the observation that $k_2(C; e_0) \geq h(e_0) \geq k_1(e_0)$. As stated in G6, this condition is also sufficient.
