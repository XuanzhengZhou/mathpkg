Proposition G6 (labeled as an exercise) gives a necessary and sufficient condition for a doubly-capacitated network to admit a feasible flow. The condition $k(U) \geq 0$ for all vertex subsets $U$ generalizes the cut-capacity condition from the singly-capacitated case. The proof is constructive: one augments the network with two new vertices $x_0, y_0$ and suitably extended capacity functions $k_1', k_2'$, then applies the Max-Flow Min-Cut Theorem G3. If all $k(U) \geq 0$, a feasible flow exists; conversely, G5 shows the condition is necessary.
