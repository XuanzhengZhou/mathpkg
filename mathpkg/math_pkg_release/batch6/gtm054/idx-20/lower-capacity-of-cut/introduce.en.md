The lower capacity $k_1(C; e_0)$ of a cut $C$ through $e_0$ generalizes the ordinary capacity of a cut to the doubly-capacitated setting. It is formed by summing the lower capacities $k_1(e)$ over edges directed in the sense of the cut, minus the upper capacities $k_2(e)$ over edges directed opposite the cut (excluding $e_0$). Together with the upper capacity $k_2(C; e_0)$, this provides a two-sided bound: $k_1(C; e_0) \leq h(e_0) \leq k_2(C; e_0)$ for any feasible flow $h$.
