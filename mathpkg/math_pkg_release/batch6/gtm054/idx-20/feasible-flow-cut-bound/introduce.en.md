Proposition G2 establishes that the flow $h(e_0)$ through any edge $e_0$ of a feasible flow is sandwiched between the lower and upper capacities of any cut through $e_0$. The lower bound follows from the feasibility condition $h(e) \geq k_1(e)$ on forward edges and $h(e) \leq k_2(e)$ on reverse edges, while the upper bound follows from $h(e) \leq k_2(e)$ on forward edges and $h(e) \geq k_1(e)$ on reverse edges. This two-sided inequality generalizes the single-sided bound $h(e_0) \leq k(C; e_0)$ from the singly-capacitated case.
