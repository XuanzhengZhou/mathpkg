In a doubly-capacitated network $(V, k_1, k_2)$, a flow must respect both lower and upper capacity bounds on every edge to be considered feasible. The lower capacity $k_1(e)$ represents a minimum throughput required on edge $e$, while the upper capacity $k_2(e)$ is the maximum allowed. An integral network $(V, k)$ in the sense of Section C corresponds to the special case where $k_1 \equiv 0$, so that every flow is automatically nonnegative and feasibility reduces to the single upper-bound condition.
