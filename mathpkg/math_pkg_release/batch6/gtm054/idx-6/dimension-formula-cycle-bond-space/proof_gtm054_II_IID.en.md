The bond space $\mathcal{Y}(\Lambda)$ is by definition the subspace of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ spanned by $f[E]$, i.e., the image of the linear map $\vec{f}: \mathcal{P}(E) \to \mathcal{P}(V)$. The cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Lambda)$ is defined as $\mathcal{Y}^\perp(\Lambda)$, the orthogonal complement of the bond space, which is precisely the kernel of $\vec{f}$. By the rank-nullity theorem for the linear map $\vec{f}$ whose domain has dimension $|E|$, we have
$$\dim(\ker \vec{f}) + \dim(\operatorname{im} \vec{f}) = |E|.$$
Since $\mathcal{Z}(\Lambda) = \ker \vec{f}$ and $\mathcal{Y}(\Lambda) = \operatorname{im} \vec{f}$, the result follows.
