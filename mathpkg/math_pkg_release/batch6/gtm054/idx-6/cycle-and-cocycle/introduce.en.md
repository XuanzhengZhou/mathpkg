A cycle of a set system $\Lambda$ is any subset of the edge set $E$ that lies in the cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Lambda)$. Dually, a cocycle is any subset of $E$ that lies in the cocycle space $\mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Lambda)$. In the context of graphs, cycles correspond to edge sets where every vertex has even degree in the induced subgraph, while cocycles correspond to edge cuts (bonds) of the graph. These concepts generalize the classical notions of cycles and cuts to arbitrary set systems.
