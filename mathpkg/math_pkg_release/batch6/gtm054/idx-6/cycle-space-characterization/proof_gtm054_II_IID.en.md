The cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Lambda)$ is defined as $\mathcal{Y}^\perp(\Lambda)$, the orthogonal complement of the bond space $\mathcal{Y}(\Lambda)$ in $\mathcal{P}(E)$. Recall that $\mathcal{Y}(\Lambda)$ is the subspace of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ spanned by $f[E]$. For $A \subseteq E$, we have $A \in \mathcal{Z}(\Lambda)$ if and only if $A$ is orthogonal to every element of $\mathcal{Y}(\Lambda)$, i.e., $A \cdot B = \varnothing$ for all $B \in \mathcal{Y}(\Lambda)$. By the definition of the orthogonal complement and the structure of the bond space, this orthogonality condition is equivalent to $\sum_{e \in A} f(e) = \varnothing$, which follows directly from the definitions of the spaces involved.
