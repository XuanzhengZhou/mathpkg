In the algebraic theory of set systems, the cospace $\mathcal{Y}^\perp(\Lambda)$ is the orthogonal complement of the bond space $\mathcal{Y}(\Lambda)$ within the power set algebra $\mathcal{P}(E)$. Likewise, the cocycle space $\mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Lambda)$ is the orthogonal complement of the cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Lambda)$. These spaces complete the dual pairings $(\mathcal{Y}, \mathcal{Y}^\perp)$ and $(\mathcal{Z}, \mathcal{Z}^\perp)$ under the symmetric difference inner product, and they play a fundamental role in the dimension-theoretic analysis of systems via the rank-nullity analogues D3-D5.
