This proposition provides constructive information about matchings in bipartite graphs. While critical subsets represent "overly large" subsets of $V_1$ that cannot be fully matched, their complements admit matchings. Specifically, if $U$ is a critical subset of $V_1$, then $V_1 \setminus U$ can be matched into $V_2 \setminus N(U)$, meaning the vertices in the neighborhood of the critical set are not required for matching the remaining vertices of $V_1$.
