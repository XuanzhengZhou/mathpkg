A 1-factor of a graph generalizes the notion of a mutual matching from bipartite graphs to arbitrary graphs. It is a set of edges that is simultaneously independent (no two edges share a vertex) and vertex-covering (every vertex is incident to some edge in the set). The existence of a 1-factor is equivalent to the condition $\nu_0(\Gamma) = 2\alpha_1(\Gamma)$ relating the vertex covering number and the edge independence number, and it forces the graph to have no isolated vertices.
