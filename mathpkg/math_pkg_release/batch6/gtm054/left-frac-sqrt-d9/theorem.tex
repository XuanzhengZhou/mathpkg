The first major advance in proving it was initiated by Gerhard Ringel in the 1950's when he divided the problem into twelve cases according to the congruence class modulo 12 of $\chi_0(S)$. A few of these cases were easily disposed of, but the last three cases (the congruence classes 2, 8, and 11) were finally resolved in 1967–68 through the joint work of Ringel and J. W. T. Youngs.
