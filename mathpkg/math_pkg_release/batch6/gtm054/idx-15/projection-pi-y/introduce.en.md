For a subspace $L \subseteq \mathbb{Q}^X$, the projection $\pi_Y$ restricts a function to a subset $Y \subseteq X$ and extends by zero elsewhere. The image $\pi_Y[L]$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{Q}^Y$, and the kernel is $L \cap \mathbb{Q}^{X \setminus Y}$. This projection is the key tool for relating properties of $L$ to those of its restrictions.
