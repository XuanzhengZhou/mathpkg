Let $L$ be a subspace of $\mathbb{Q}^X$ and let

$$\mathcal{N}(L) = \{\sigma(h): h \in L\}.$$

As a subcollection of $\mathcal{P}(X)$, $\mathcal{N}(L)$ inherits the partial order $\subseteq$. Let $\mathcal{M}(L)$ denote the collection of elementary sets in $\mathcal{N}(L)$. If $h \in L$ and $\sigma(h) \in \mathcal{M}(L)$, then $h$ is called a minimal function of $L$.
