A subspace $L \subseteq \mathbb{Q}^X$ is called unimodular if every elementary function of $L$ takes values only in $\{0, 1, -1\}$. Unimodular subspaces are important for combinatorial applications, where the functions represent incidence relations or flow values in integer networks.
