If two functions in a subspace $L \subseteq \mathbb{Q}^X$ have supports related by inclusion, and the one with the larger support is a minimal function, then the other must be a rational scalar multiple of it. This proposition is fundamental to the theory of minimal functions and is used repeatedly in the proof of the $\mathcal{M}$-decomposition theorem.
