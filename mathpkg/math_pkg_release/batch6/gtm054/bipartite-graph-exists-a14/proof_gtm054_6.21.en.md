By Proposition A11 we may let $A = (\{W_1, W_2\}, \mathcal{F})$ be a $\hat{\rho}(\text{B})$-valent bipartite graph such that $V_i \subseteq W_i$ ($i = 1, 2$) and $B = A_{V_1 \cup V_2}$. By Exercise A9, there exists a partition $\{F_1, \ldots, F_{\hat{\rho}(\text{B})}\}$ of $\mathcal{F}$, each of whose cells is a mutual matching of $W_1$. Since B contains a vertex incident with precisely $\hat{\rho}(\text{B})$ edges in $\mathcal{E}$, it is readily seen that for each $i = 1, \ldots, \hat{\rho}(\text{B})$, the set $\mathcal{E} \cap F_i$ is a nonempty matching in B. Hence $\{ \mathcal{E} \cap F_1, \ldots, \mathcal{E} \cap F_{\hat{\rho}(\text{B})} \}$ is the required partition.
