For any set $U$, the inner product on $\mathcal{P}(U)$ is the function $\mathcal{P}(U) \times \mathcal{P}(U) \rightarrow \mathbb{K}$ defined by
$$S \cdot T = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } |S \cap T| \text{ is even;} \\ 1 & \text{if } |S \cap T| \text{ is odd.} \end{cases}$$

It satisfies for all $R, S, T \in \mathcal{P}(U)$:
$$R \cdot S = S \cdot R;$$
$$R \cdot (S + T) = (R \cdot S) + (R \cdot T);$$
$$R \cdot S = 0 \quad \text{for all } S \in \mathcal{P}(U) \Leftrightarrow R = \varnothing.$$
