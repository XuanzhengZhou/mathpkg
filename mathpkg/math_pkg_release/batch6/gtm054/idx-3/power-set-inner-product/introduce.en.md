The inner product on $\mathcal{P}(U)$ assigns to each pair of subsets $S, T$ a value in the field $\mathbb{K}$ based on the parity of the size of their intersection. This bilinear form is symmetric, linear in the second argument with respect to symmetric difference addition, and nondegenerate (the only element orthogonal to all subsets is the empty set). The inner product provides the framework for defining orthogonality and orthogonal complements in the combinatorial vector space $\mathcal{P}(U)$.
