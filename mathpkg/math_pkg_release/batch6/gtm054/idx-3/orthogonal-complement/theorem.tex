If $\mathcal{S} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(U)$, the orthogonal complement of $\mathcal{S}$ is
$$\mathcal{S}^{\perp} = \{T \in \mathcal{P}(U): S \cdot T = 0 \text{ for all } S \in \mathcal{S}\}.$$

$\mathcal{S}^{\perp}$ is always a subspace of $\mathcal{P}(U)$. Moreover, $(\mathcal{S}^{\perp})^{\perp}$ is a subspace of $\mathcal{P}(U)$ containing $\mathcal{S}$, and $\mathcal{S}$ is a subspace of $\mathcal{P}(U)$ if and only if $(\mathcal{S}^{\perp})^{\perp} = \mathcal{S}$.
