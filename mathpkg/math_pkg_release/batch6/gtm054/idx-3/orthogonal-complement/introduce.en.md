The orthogonal complement $\mathcal{S}^{\perp}$ of a family $\mathcal{S}$ of subsets of $U$ consists of all subsets that are orthogonal to every member of $\mathcal{S}$. This operation always produces a subspace of $\mathcal{P}(U)$, even when $\mathcal{S}$ itself is not a subspace. The double orthogonal complement $(\mathcal{S}^{\perp})^{\perp}$ is the smallest subspace containing $\mathcal{S}$, providing a Galois connection that characterizes subspaces: a subset family is a subspace precisely when it equals its own double orthogonal complement.
