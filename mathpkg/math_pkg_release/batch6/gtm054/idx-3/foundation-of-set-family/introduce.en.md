The foundation $\operatorname{Fnd}(\mathcal{S})$ of a family $\mathcal{S}$ of subsets of $U$ is the union of all members of $\mathcal{S}$. It represents the set of all elements of $U$ that appear in at least one member of the family. In the context of subspaces of $\mathcal{P}(U)$, the foundation plays a crucial role in defining the coordinate sum operation: two subspaces admit a coordinate sum precisely when their foundations are disjoint.
