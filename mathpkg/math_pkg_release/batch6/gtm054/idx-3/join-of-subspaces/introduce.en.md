The join $\mathcal{A} \vee \mathcal{B}$ of two subspaces of $\mathcal{P}(U)$ is the set of all symmetric differences of one element from $\mathcal{A}$ and one from $\mathcal{B}$. This operation corresponds to the sum of subspaces in ordinary linear algebra, producing the smallest subspace containing both $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$. The join is commutative and associative, making the collection of subspaces of $\mathcal{P}(U)$ a join-semilattice under this operation.
