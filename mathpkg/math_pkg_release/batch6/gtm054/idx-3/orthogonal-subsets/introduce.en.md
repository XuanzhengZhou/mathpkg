Two subsets $S$ and $T$ of $U$ are orthogonal if their inner product is zero, which occurs precisely when the cardinality of their intersection is even. This natural combinatorial orthogonality relation generalizes the geometric notion of perpendicular vectors to the setting of the power set vector space. Orthogonality serves as the foundation for defining orthogonal complements of families of subsets.
