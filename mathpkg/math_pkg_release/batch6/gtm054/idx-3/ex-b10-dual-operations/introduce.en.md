Exercise B10 asks the reader to explore dual versions of the monotonicity properties established in B7 and B9. For B7, the intersection operation is replaced by union (respectively symmetric difference $+$), examining whether analogues of $S \cap T \subseteq T$ and the monotonicity of intersection hold. For B9, addition on $\mathbb{S}(U)$ is replaced by multiplication, testing whether $st \geq t$ and the order-preserving property carry over. Some of these dual statements fail, illustrating the asymmetry between intersection/union and addition/multiplication in these combinatorial structures.
