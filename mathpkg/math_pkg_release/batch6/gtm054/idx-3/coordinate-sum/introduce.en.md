The coordinate sum $\mathcal{A} \oplus \mathcal{B}$ is a special case of the join of two subspaces of $\mathcal{P}(U)$, defined when the foundations (supports) of $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ are disjoint. In this case the join is a direct sum in the sense of linear algebra: every element of $\mathcal{A} \oplus \mathcal{B}$ has a unique representation as $S + T$ with $S \in \mathcal{A}$ and $T \in \mathcal{B}$. The coordinate sum plays an essential role in the decomposition theory of subspaces of the power set.
