(a) A necessary and sufficient condition for a subspace $\mathcal{A}$ to be connected is that given $x_1, x_2 \in \operatorname{Fnd}(\mathcal{A})$, there exists $M \in \mathcal{M}(\mathcal{A})$ such that $\{x_1, x_2\} \subseteq M$.

(b) The relation $\sim$ on $\operatorname{Fnd}(\mathcal{A})$ given by
$$x \sim y \Leftrightarrow \{x, y\} \subseteq A \text{ for some } A \in \mathcal{M}(\mathcal{A})$$
is an equivalence relation. The equivalence classes are precisely the cells of the component partition.
