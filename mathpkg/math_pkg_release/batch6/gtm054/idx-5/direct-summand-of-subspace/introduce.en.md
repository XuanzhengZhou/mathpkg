A subspace $\mathcal{B}$ is called a direct summand of $\mathcal{A}$ if $\mathcal{A}$ can be expressed as the direct sum of $\mathcal{B}$ and some other subspace $\mathcal{C}$. The trivial subspaces $\{\varnothing\}$ and $\mathcal{A}$ are always direct summands of $\mathcal{A}$. Direct summands are fundamental to understanding how a subspace decomposes into independent pieces and lead to the notions of connectedness and components.
