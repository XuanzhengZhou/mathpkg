A subspace $\mathcal{A}$ is connected if it cannot be decomposed as a direct sum of two nontrivial subspaces; that is, $\{\varnothing\}$ and $\mathcal{A}$ are its only direct summands. Connectedness is the analog of indecomposability for the subspace structure, analogous to the notion of connectedness in graph theory where a graph cannot be partitioned into disconnected components.
