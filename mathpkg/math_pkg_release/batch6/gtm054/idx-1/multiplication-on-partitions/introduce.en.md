The product of two partitions $\mathcal{Q}$ and $\mathcal{R}$ is formed by taking all nonempty intersections of a cell from $\mathcal{Q}$ with a cell from $\mathcal{R}$. This operation is commutative, associative, and admits an identity (Exercise B6). If $\mathcal{Q}$ and $\mathcal{R}$ have $m$ and $n$ cells respectively, their product has at most $mn$ cells.
