Every function $f: B \rightarrow U$ has two combinatorial invariants: its partition (the collection of fibers over elements in the image) and its selection (recording the size of each fiber). The partition describes "which elements of $B$ map to the same element of $U$", while the selection describes "how many elements of $B$ map to each element of $U$".
