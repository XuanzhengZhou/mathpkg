If $\Gamma_{(x)}$ is complete, then $\kappa(\Gamma_{(x)}) = |V| - 2 \geq \kappa(\Gamma) - 1$ by C3. If $\Gamma_{(x)}$ is not complete, we let $S \in \mathcal{P}_{m-2}(V + \{x\})$. Since $|S + \{x\}| = m - 1$, $(\Gamma_{(x)})_{(S)} = \Gamma_{(S + \{x\})}$ is connected. Hence $\Gamma_{(x)}$ is $(m - 1)$-connected.

Let $E = \{x, y\}$, and suppose that $\Gamma_{(E)}$ is not $(m - 1)$-connected. Since $\Gamma$ is $m$-connected, $|V| \geq m + 1$ by C3. Hence there exists $S \in \mathcal{P}_{m-2}(V)$ such that $\Gamma_{(S \cup \{E\})}$ is not connected. Select $a$ and $z$ in different components of $\Gamma_{(S \cup \{E\})}$. We may choose $\{a, z\} \neq \{x, y\}$, for otherwise $V = S \cup \{x, y\}$, which implies that $|V| \leq |S| + 2 = m$. We may suppose $x \notin \{a, z\}$.
