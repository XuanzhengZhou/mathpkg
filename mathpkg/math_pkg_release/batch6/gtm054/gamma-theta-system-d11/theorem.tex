Thus $\Gamma' = (U, g, E)$ and $\Theta$ are system-isomorphic. By this and the hypothesis, $p[\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma')] = \mathcal{Z}(\Theta) = p[\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)]$. Since $p$ is a bijection, $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma') = \mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)$. Hence $\mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Gamma') = \mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Gamma)$, and the result follows from the proposition.

If $V$ is an $n$-set for $n > 0$, any graph which is isomorphic to the graph $(V, \mathcal{P}_2(V))$ is called a complete graph of order $n$, and the symbol $K_n$ is always used to denote such a graph. Thus, $K_n$ is connected and $\dim(\mathcal{Z}(K_n)) = \binom{n}{2} - n + 1 = \binom{n-1}{2}$.
