Given an $m$-connected multigraph $\Gamma$, this proposition generalizes Menger's theorem by allowing multiple paths to start or end at the same vertex, with multiplicities prescribed by functions $g$ and $h$. It guarantees the existence of an openly-disjoint $m$-family of $AZ$-paths where each vertex $a \in A$ is the initial vertex of exactly $g(a)$ paths and each vertex $z \in Z$ is the terminal vertex of exactly $h(z)$ paths. The proof adapts Theorem B2 by duplicating vertices according to the prescribed multiplicities.
