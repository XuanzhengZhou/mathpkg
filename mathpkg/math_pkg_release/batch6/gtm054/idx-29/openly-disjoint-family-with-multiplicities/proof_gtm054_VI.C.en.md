This result is essentially an adaptation of Theorem B2 to the terminology of the present section. The proof proceeds by ''splitting'' each vertex $a \in A$ into $g(a)$ copies and each vertex $z \in Z$ into $h(z)$ copies, then applying Menger's theorem (Theorem B2) to the resulting multigraph. Since $\Gamma$ is $m$-connected, the conditions of B2 are satisfied, yielding the required $m$-family $\mathcal{F}$ of openly-disjoint $AZ$-paths with the prescribed multiplicities.

Note that the proposition does not assert that for any particular $a_0 \in A$ and $z_0 \in Z$, the family $\mathcal{F}$ includes an $a_0z_0$-path. This stronger condition requires additional structure, as the next result (Condition $O_n$) indicates.
