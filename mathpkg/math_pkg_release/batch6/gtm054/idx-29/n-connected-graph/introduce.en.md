A multigraph $\Gamma$ is defined to be $n$-connected if it has at least $n+1$ vertices and the removal of any set of fewer than $n$ vertices leaves the graph connected. This generalizes ordinary connectedness: $1$-connected is exactly connected. The property is monotone in $n$: if $\Gamma$ is $n$-connected, then it is also $m$-connected for all $0 \leq m \leq n$.
