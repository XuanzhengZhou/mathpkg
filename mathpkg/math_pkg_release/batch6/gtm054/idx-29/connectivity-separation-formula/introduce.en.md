This theorem provides an alternative characterization of the vertex-connectivity $\kappa(\Gamma)$ for non-complete multigraphs. Rather than checking all possible separating sets, $\kappa(\Gamma)$ equals the minimum separation number $\sigma(a, z)$ over all nonincident vertex pairs $\{a, z\}$. This formula connects the global connectivity invariant to the local pairwise Menger-type separation function, making it practical for computation. It also clarifies that biconnected graphs are exactly $2$-connected graphs, and tri-connected graphs are exactly $3$-connected graphs.
