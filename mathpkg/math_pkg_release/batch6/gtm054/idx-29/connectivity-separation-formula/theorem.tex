\textbf{Theorem (C2).} Whenever $\Gamma$ is not complete,
$$\kappa(\Gamma) = \min\{\sigma(a, z): \{a, z\} \in \mathcal{P}_2(V) \setminus \mathcal{E}\}.$$
In particular, if $\kappa(\Gamma) = 1$ and $|V| \geq 3$, then $\Gamma$ has an articulation vertex. The terms ``biconnected'' and ``$2$-connected'' are equivalent for graphs. Similarly, the terms ``tri-connected'' and ``$3$-connected'' are equivalent for graphs.
