If $\Gamma$ is $m$-connected and if $a$ and $z$ are nonincident vertices of $\Gamma$, then $\sigma(a, z) \geq m$ by the definition of $m$-connectedness (any set separating $z$ from $a$ must have size at least $m$). On the other hand, if $\Gamma$ is not complete, then by definition of $\kappa(\Gamma)$ there exists a $\kappa(\Gamma)$-subset $S$ of $V$ which separates some vertex $z$ from some vertex $a$. Since $\Gamma$ is not complete, we may choose $\{a, z\} \in \mathcal{P}_2(V) \setminus \mathcal{E}$. Then $\sigma(a, z) \leq \kappa(\Gamma)$. Combining both inequalities yields the formula.
