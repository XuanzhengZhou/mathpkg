For any multigraph $\Gamma$ with at least two vertices, the vertex-connectivity $\kappa(\Gamma)$ cannot exceed $|V| - 1$. This absolute upper bound is achieved precisely by complete graphs: $\kappa(K_n) = n - 1$. In a complete graph, every vertex is adjacent to every other, so to disconnect the graph one must remove all but one vertex, which realizes the bound. This inequality is fundamental for understanding the range of possible connectivity values.
