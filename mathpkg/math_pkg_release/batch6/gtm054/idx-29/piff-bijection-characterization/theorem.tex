\textbf{Proposition (B7).} Let $\Gamma = (V, f, E)$ or $(V, D)$ and let $A, Z \in \mathcal{P}_m(V)$. There exists an $m$-family of disjoint $AZ$-paths in $\Gamma$ if and only if there exists a bijection $b: V \setminus Z \to V \setminus A$ such that for each $x \in V \setminus Z$, either $b(x) = x$ or, when $\Gamma = (V, f, E)$ then $\{x, b(x)\} \in f[E]$, and when $\Gamma = (V, D)$ then $(x, b(x)) \in D$.
