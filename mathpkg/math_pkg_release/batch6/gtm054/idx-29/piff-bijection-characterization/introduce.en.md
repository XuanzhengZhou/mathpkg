This proposition, due to M. J. Piff, characterizes the existence of an $m$-family of disjoint $AZ$-paths in a multigraph or directed graph through the existence of a bijection on the vertex sets. The bijection $b: V \setminus Z \to V \setminus A$ maps each vertex either to a neighbor (via an edge or directed edge) or to itself. This condition is equivalent to the Menger-type separation condition, providing an algebraic reformulation of a purely graph-theoretic property.
