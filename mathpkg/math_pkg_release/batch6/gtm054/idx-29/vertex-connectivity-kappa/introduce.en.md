The vertex-connectivity $\kappa(\Gamma)$ of a multigraph $\Gamma$ is the largest integer $n$ such that $\Gamma$ is $n$-connected. Alternatively, it is the minimum number of vertices whose removal disconnects the graph (or reduces it to a single vertex). For complete graphs, $\kappa(K_n) = n - 1$, which serves as the canonical example and upper bound.
