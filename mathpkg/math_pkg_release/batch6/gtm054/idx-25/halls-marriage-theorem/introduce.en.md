Philip Hall's Theorem (1935), also known as Hall's Marriage Theorem, provides a necessary and sufficient condition for the existence of a system of distinct representatives (SDR). Given a set system $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ where $f: E \to \mathcal{P}(V)$, an SDR is an injection $\lambda: E \to V$ such that $\lambda(e) \in f(e)$ for all $e \in E$. Hall's condition requires that for every subset $F$ of the index set $E$, the cardinality of $F$ does not exceed the cardinality of the union of the corresponding sets. The theorem is equivalent to the statement that a bipartite graph has a matching covering one partite set.
