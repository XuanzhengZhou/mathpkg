Gallai's Theorem establishes fundamental relationships between four key graph parameters: the vertex independence number $\alpha_0$, the edge covering number $\beta_{01}$, the edge independence (matching) number $\alpha_1$, the vertex covering number $\beta_{10}$, and the total number of vertices $\nu_0$. Part (a) states that for any graph, the sum of the vertex independence number and the edge covering number equals the total number of vertices. Part (b) states the analogous relationship for edge independence and vertex covering numbers, valid for graphs without isolated vertices.
