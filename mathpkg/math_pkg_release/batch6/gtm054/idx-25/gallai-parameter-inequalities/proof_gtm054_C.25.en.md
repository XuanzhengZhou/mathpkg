From the two parts of Gallai's theorem,

$$\beta_{10} - \alpha_0 = \beta_{01} - \alpha_1 = \nu_0 - \alpha_0 - \alpha_1,$$

and by Proposition C6b, $\beta_{10} - \alpha_0 \geq 0$. This establishes the three left-hand inequalities as well as the fact that if equality holds in any one of them, then it holds in all three. The right-hand inequality conditions follow by similar reasoning from the equality $\beta_{10} - \alpha_0 = \beta_{01} - \alpha_1$ combined with the bounds relating $\delta$ to the other parameters.
