If $V$ is a set and $\mathcal{A}$ is a subspace of $\mathbb{F}^V$, then we write $\mathcal{A}^\perp$ to indicate the orthogonal complement of $\mathcal{A}$ under the standard inner product, i.e.,

$$\mathcal{A}^\perp = \{g \in \mathcal{V} : \sum_{x \in V} f(x)g(x) = 0 \text{ for all } f \in \mathcal{A}\}.$$

The inner product of §IIA is the special case of this notion when $\mathbb{F} = \mathbb{K}$. Equation IIA6 still applies.
