A subsystem is obtained by restricting a system to a subset of vertices and a subset of blocks, with the incidence function restricted accordingly. Two important special cases are the subsystem induced by a set of blocks (taking the union of those blocks as the new vertex set) and the subsystem induced by a set of vertices (keeping only blocks whose vertices are entirely within the chosen set). These constructions generalize the familiar notions of induced subgraphs.
