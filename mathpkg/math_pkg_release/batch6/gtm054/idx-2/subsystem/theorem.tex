A system $\Omega = (W, g, F)$ is a \textbf{subsystem} of the system $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ if: $W \subseteq V$, $F \subseteq E$, and for all $e \in F$ it holds that $g(e) = f(e) \subseteq W$.

For $F \subseteq E$, the \textbf{subsystem induced by $F$} is $\Lambda_F = (W, g, F)$ where $W = \bigcup_{d \in F} f(d)$ and $g = f|_F$.

For $W \subseteq V$, the \textbf{subsystem induced by $W$} is $\Lambda_W = (W, g, F)$ where $F = \{e \in E : f(e) \subseteq W\}$ and $g = f|_F$.
