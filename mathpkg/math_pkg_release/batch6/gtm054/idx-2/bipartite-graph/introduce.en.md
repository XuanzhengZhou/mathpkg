A bipartite graph is one whose vertex set can be partitioned into two parts such that every edge has exactly one endpoint in each part. The definition includes the trivial case $|V| \leq 1$ and emphasizes that the bipartition need not be unique. This system-theoretic formulation captures the essence of bipartiteness without relying on coloring.
