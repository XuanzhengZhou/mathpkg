The bar-selection $\bar{s}$ is the cumulative version of the selection $s$: for a subset $S \subseteq V$, $\bar{s}(S)$ counts the number of blocks that contain $S$ (i.e., blocks whose image under $f$ is a superset of $S$). It is obtained from $s$ by summing over all supersets. The relationship between $s$ and $\bar{s}$ is a discrete analogue of the relationship between a function and its cumulative distribution, and the Möbius inversion formula (Proposition E6) provides the inverse transform.
