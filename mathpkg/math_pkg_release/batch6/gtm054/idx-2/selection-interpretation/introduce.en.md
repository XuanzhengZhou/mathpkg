This proposition gives the fundamental set-theoretic interpretations of the four selections associated with any system. The selection $s(S)$ counts blocks whose image is exactly $S$, its cumulative version $\bar{s}(S)$ counts blocks whose image contains $S$. The dual selection $s^*(A)$ counts vertices belonging to exactly the blocks in $A$, while $\bar{s}^*(A)$ counts vertices belonging to all blocks in $A$. These four quantities are the building blocks of the Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion.
