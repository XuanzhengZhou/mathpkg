A graph is a multigraph without parallel edges, represented as a pair $(V, \mathcal{E})$ where each edge is a distinct 2-element subset of vertices. This is the standard definition used throughout combinatorial graph theory. Graphs are the central objects studied in this book, and the system-theoretic framework generalizes naturally to hypergraphs (systems with arbitrary blocksizes).
