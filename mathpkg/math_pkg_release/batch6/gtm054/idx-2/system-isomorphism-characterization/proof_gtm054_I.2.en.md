Assume (a) holds. There exist bijections $p: E \to F$ and $q: V \to W$ such that
$$q[f(e)] = g(p(e)), \quad \text{for all } e \in E.$$
Thus $(p, q)$ is a function-isomorphism from $f$ to $g$. By B16a, $q$ is a selection-isomorphism from $s$ to $t$, establishing (b).

Conversely, assume (b) holds. By B16b there exists a bijection $p': E \to F$ such that $(p', q)$ is a function-isomorphism from $f$ to $g$. Together with the definition of system-isomorphism, this yields (a).
