Ramsey's Theorem is one of the two main results in this chapter. A special case of it yields an upper bound for $\nu_0$ in terms of the parameters $\alpha_0$ and $\omega$. The second main result, conjectured by C. Berge and proved by L. Lovász, states that $\omega(\Gamma_s) = \chi_0(\Gamma_s)$ for all $S \subseteq V$ if and only if $\alpha_0(\Gamma_s) = \theta(\Gamma_s)$ for all $S \subseteq V$.
