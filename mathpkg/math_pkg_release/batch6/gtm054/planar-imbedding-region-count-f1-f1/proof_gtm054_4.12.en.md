If $Z_1, \ldots, Z_k$ is a simple planar imbedding of $\Gamma$, then by the definition and A15b,

$$k = \dim(\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)) + 1 = \nu_1(\Gamma) - \nu_0(\Gamma) + \nu_{-1}(\Gamma) + 1.$$

The number of regions in a simple planar imbedding of $\Gamma$ is thus a parameter of $\Gamma$ and is denoted by $\nu_2(\Gamma)$, abbreviated by $\nu_2$ when there is no risk of confusion. We now state a result familiar to both graph theorists and topologists, according to how the symbols are interpreted.
