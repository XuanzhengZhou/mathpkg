This proposition gives a direct characterization of the circuits of the orthogonal (dual) matroid $\Lambda^\perp$ in terms of the circuits $\mathcal{M}$ of the original matroid $\Lambda$. It defines a family $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets of $V$ that intersect no circuit in exactly one element, and shows that the circuits of $\Lambda^\perp$ are precisely the minimal members of $\mathcal{A}$. This bypasses the intermediate steps of defining $\mathcal{I}(\Lambda)$, $\mathcal{B}(\Lambda)$, $\mathcal{B}^\perp(\Lambda)$, and $\mathcal{I}^\perp(\Lambda)$.
