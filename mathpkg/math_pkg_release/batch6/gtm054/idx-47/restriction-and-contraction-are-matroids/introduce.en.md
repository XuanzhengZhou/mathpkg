This proposition asserts that the restriction $\Lambda_U$ and the contraction $\Lambda_{[U]}$ of a matroid $\Lambda$ to a subset $U \subseteq V$ are themselves matroids. The restriction $\Lambda_U$ is defined as $(U, \mathcal{M}(\pi_U[\mathcal{M}]))$ where $\pi_U(S) = U \cap S$, and the contraction uses the system $\Lambda_{[U]} = (U, \mathcal{M}(\pi_U[\mathcal{E}]))$ for a set system $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{E})$. This closure property is fundamental to the theory of matroid minors.
