This exercise characterizes the independence, basis, and spanning systems of a direct sum of two matroids in terms of the corresponding systems of the summands. The key observation is that each such system of the direct sum consists of all unions of sets from the corresponding systems of $\Lambda_1$ and $\Lambda_2$. It also gives the rank function formula for the direct sum. The exercise shows why one cannot substitute "independence system", "basis system", or "spanning system" for "matroid" in the direct sum definition D18/D19 -- the matroid structure is essential for the direct sum to work properly.
