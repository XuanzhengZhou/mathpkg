This corollary shows that any sequence of restriction and contraction operations applied to a matroid can be reduced to a single contraction followed by a single restriction. More precisely, given a chain of matroids where each step is either a restriction or a contraction to a smaller vertex set, the final matroid $\Lambda^{(n)}$ is a minor of the original matroid $\Lambda^{(0)}$, expressible as $(\Lambda^{(0)}_{[T]})_{V_n}$ for some superset $T$ of $V_n$. This is the foundation for the definition of a matroid minor as any matroid of the form $(\Lambda_U)_{[T]}$ or $(\Lambda_{[U]})_T$ with $T \subseteq U \subseteq V$.
