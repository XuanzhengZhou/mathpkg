This lemma classifies Platonic graphs with $\rho(\Gamma) = 3$: they are precisely the three Platonic solids $K_4$ (tetrahedron), the cube, and the octahedron, each of which is a simple graph and isocovalent. The proof proceeds casewise through the three types (III, IV, V) from Table F16, using edge-counting and valence arguments to identify each type with its corresponding polyhedral graph. This result is fundamental to the classification of all Platonic graphs.
