Kuratowski's Theorem is a foundational result in topological graph theory, giving a complete characterization of planar multigraphs in terms of two forbidden subcontractions: the complete graph on five vertices $K_5$ and the complete bipartite graph $K_{3,3}$. A multigraph is planar if and only if neither of these two graphs can be obtained through a sequence of edge deletions and contractions. This result transforms the geometric problem of planarity into a purely combinatorial condition, and its proof typically proceeds by induction on the number of edges, using separating sets of size at most three.
