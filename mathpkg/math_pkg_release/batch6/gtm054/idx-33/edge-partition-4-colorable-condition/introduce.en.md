This proposition gives an algebraic reformulation of the Four-Color Problem. A planar graph with at least 4 vertices is 4-colorable if and only if its edge set can be partitioned into three subsets such that, for every region of a planar imbedding, each subset contains an even number of edges from that region's boundary. The proof uses a vertex 4-coloring with values in $\mathbb{K} \times \mathbb{K}$ to define an edge labeling whose fibers give the required partition.
