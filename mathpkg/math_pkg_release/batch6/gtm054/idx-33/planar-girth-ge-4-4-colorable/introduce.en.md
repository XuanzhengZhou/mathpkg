This corollary shows that planar graphs with girth at least 4 (i.e., no triangles) are 4-colorable. The proof uses the fact that girth at least 4 forces every subgraph with edges to have minimum face-degree at least 4, which implies that the average vertex degree is strictly less than 4. This allows constructing a vertex ordering with degree at most 3 in each step, ultimately reducing to a subgraph with at most 11 vertices, where Corollary B6 applies.
