This proposition establishes an important equivalence: the Four-Color Conjecture holds for all planar graphs precisely when it holds for the subclass of planar graphs admitting a triangular imbedding (where every region is a triangle). The proof constructs, from any 5-critical planar graph, a planar graph with a triangular imbedding that would also require 5 colors, thus reducing the general problem to the triangular case.
