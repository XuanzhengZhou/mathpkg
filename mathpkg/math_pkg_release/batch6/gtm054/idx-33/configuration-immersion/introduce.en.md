A configuration $(\Gamma, S)$ is immersed in a larger graph $\Theta$ if $\Gamma$ appears as a subgraph of $\Theta$ and the induced subgraph on the complement of $S$ within $\Gamma$ forms an entire connected component of the corresponding induced subgraph of $\Theta$. This notion captures the idea that the "interior" of the configuration is isolated from the rest of the graph except through the boundary vertices $S$.
