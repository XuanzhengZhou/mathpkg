This corollary, due to P. G. Tait (1880), provides the most famous reformulation of the Four-Color Conjecture: instead of vertex-coloring planar graphs, one can equivalently study edge-coloring of planar trivalent (3-regular) graphs without isthmuses. A Tait coloring is a proper 3-edge-coloring. The equivalence follows by combining Proposition B12 (reducing to triangular imbeddings) with Corollary B16 (linking triangular imbeddings to Tait colorings of orthogonal graphs).
