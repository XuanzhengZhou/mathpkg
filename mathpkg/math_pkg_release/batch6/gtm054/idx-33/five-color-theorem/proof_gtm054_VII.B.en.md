If $\chi_0(\Gamma) > 5$, then there exists a 5-critical subgraph $\Theta$ of $\Gamma$. By IIIE15, $\Theta$ is planar, so by IIIF12, $\rho(\Theta) \leq 5$. By IIIF13, there exists a vertex $x_0 \in V(\Theta)$ of valence exactly 5. The proof then proceeds by considering the neighbors of $x_0$ and using Kempe chain arguments to construct a 5-coloring of $\Theta$, contradicting the assumption that $\Theta$ is 5-critical. $\square$
