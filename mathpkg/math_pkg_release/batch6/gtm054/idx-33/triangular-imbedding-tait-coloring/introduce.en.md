This corollary applies Proposition B14 to the special case of triangular imbeddings. When every region is a 3-cycle, the edge partition condition translates exactly into the existence of a Tait coloring (a proper 3-edge-coloring) of the orthogonal graph. Since the orthogonal graph of a triangular imbedding is trivalent, the three edge subsets correspond precisely to the three color classes of a Tait coloring.
