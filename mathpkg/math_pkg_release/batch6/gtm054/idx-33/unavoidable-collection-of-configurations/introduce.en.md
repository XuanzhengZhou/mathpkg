An unavoidable collection of configurations is a set of configurations such that every minimal counterexample to the Four-Color Conjecture must contain at least one of them as an immersed configuration. The Appel-Haken strategy reduces to proving the existence of a nonempty unavoidable collection of reducible configurations: if every configuration in the collection is reducible, then no minimal counterexample can exist, and thus the Four-Color Theorem holds.
