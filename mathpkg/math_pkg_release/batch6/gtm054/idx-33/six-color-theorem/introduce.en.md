The Six-Color Theorem provides an elementary upper bound on the vertex chromatic number of any planar graph. It states that every planar graph can be properly vertex-colored using at most six colors. The proof relies on two key facts: every planar graph has a vertex of degree at most five, and every critical subgraph of a planar graph remains planar. This result serves as a stepping stone toward the stronger Five-Color Theorem and the historic Four-Color Theorem.
