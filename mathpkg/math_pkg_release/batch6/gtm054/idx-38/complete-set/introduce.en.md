A subset $S$ of vertices in a graph $\Gamma = (V, \mathcal{E})$ is complete if every pair of distinct vertices in $S$ is adjacent — that is, the induced subgraph on $S$ is a complete graph. In other words, $\mathcal{P}_2(S) \subseteq \mathcal{E}$, where $\mathcal{P}_2(S)$ denotes all 2-element subsets of $S$. Complete sets are the fundamental building blocks for defining the clique number $\omega(\Gamma)$ and the clique covering number $\theta(\Gamma)$.
