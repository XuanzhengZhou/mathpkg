This exercise concerns a construction of 2-cell imbeddings of complete graphs $K_n$ using cyclic groups $\mathbb{Z}_n$. The vertices are labeled by group elements, and the rotation system is defined by cyclicly shifting a base permutation $\pi_0$ by each group element. The exercise asks to prove a necessary and sufficient condition for the resulting imbedding to be triangular — meaning every face has exactly three sides — which yields a genus-minimizing triangulation of the surface.
