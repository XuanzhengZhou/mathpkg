Exercise B5 establishes two fundamental identities for Ramsey numbers. Part (a) shows that appending a color parameter equal to $s$ (the subset size) does not change the Ramsey number: $n(q_1,\ldots,q_{m-1}:s) = n(q_1,\ldots,q_{m-1},s:s)$. The special case $m=2$ gives $n(q,s:s)=q$. Part (b) establishes symmetry: Ramsey numbers are invariant under permutation of their arguments.
