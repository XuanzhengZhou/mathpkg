This multi-part exercise establishes fundamental results about the genus of complete graphs via triangular imbeddings. Part (a) proves a necessary congruence condition $n \equiv 0, 3, 4,$ or $7 \pmod{12}$ for $K_n$ to admit a triangular imbedding. Part (b) shows that under this congruence condition, $K_n$ has a triangular imbedding precisely when its genus attains the lower bound $\lceil (n-3)(n-4)/12 \rceil$. Parts (c) and (d) apply these results to compute the genus of $K_{19}$ and $K_{12}$ explicitly.
