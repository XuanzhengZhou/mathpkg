Let $\Gamma$ be topologically imbedded in $S$ and let $\Theta$ be a $\chi_0(\Gamma)$-critical subgraph of $\Gamma$. Thus $\Theta$ is topologically imbedded in $S$, and it suffices to prove the theorem for $\Theta$. Let $\nu_2$ denote the number of regions of such an imbedding, and suppose that $\Theta \neq K_{h(S)}$. We suppress the argument $\Theta$ for the remainder of the proof.

Now suppose that $\varepsilon(S) \leq -10$. One verifies by elementary operations that this assumption implies

$$\sqrt{21 - 6\varepsilon(S)} \leq \frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{49 - 24\varepsilon(S)}),$$

and so by D19,

$$\chi_0 \leq 2 + \left[ \frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{49 - 24\varepsilon(S)}) \right] = h(S) - 1,$$

contrary to hypothesis. One can dispose of the remaining cases $-9 \leq \varepsilon(S) \leq -3$ one by one in a similar fashion.
