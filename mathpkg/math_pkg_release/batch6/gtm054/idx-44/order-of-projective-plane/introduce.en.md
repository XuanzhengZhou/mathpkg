The order $n = k-1$ is the fundamental numerical invariant of a finite projective plane. It determines the key parameters: the plane has $n^2 + n + 1$ points and equally many lines, with $n+1$ points on each line and $n+1$ lines through each point. All known finite projective planes have prime power orders, though the general existence question for arbitrary orders remains one of the deep open problems in combinatorics (e.g., the non-existence of order 6 was proved, while order 10 remains open).
