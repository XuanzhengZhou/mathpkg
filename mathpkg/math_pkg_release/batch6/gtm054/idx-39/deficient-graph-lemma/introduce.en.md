The Deficient Graph Lemma (C9) is a key structural tool in the graph-theoretic study of Ramsey numbers. Given a $d$-deficient $(q_1, q_2)$-graph $\Gamma$ and a vertex $x$, the subgraph induced by the neighborhood $N(x)$ must be a $(q_1-1, q_2)$-graph (since any $(q_1-1)$-clique there would extend via $x$ to a $q_1$-clique), while the subgraph induced by the non-neighbors is a $(q_1, q_2-1)$-graph. Part (b) gives a deficiency inequality, and part (c) specializes to $0$-deficient graphs, constraining vertex degrees to $\{2, 3\}$, which is crucial for the classification of extremal Ramsey graphs.
