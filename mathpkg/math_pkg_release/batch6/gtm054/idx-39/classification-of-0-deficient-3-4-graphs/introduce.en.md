This proposition provides a complete classification of the extremal graphs for the Ramsey number $n(3, 4) = 9$. A $0$-deficient $(3,4)$-graph has $8$ vertices and contains neither a triangle ($3$-clique) nor a $4$-independent set. The classification shows there are exactly three such graphs up to isomorphism: $\Theta_8$, $\Theta_8'$, and $\Theta_8''$, all represented in Figure C11. The proof proceeds by case analysis on the existence of a vertex of valency $2$, using Lemma C9 to relate the structure to the previously classified $0$-deficient $(3,3)$-graph (the $5$-circuit) and $1$-deficient $(3,3)$-graphs.
