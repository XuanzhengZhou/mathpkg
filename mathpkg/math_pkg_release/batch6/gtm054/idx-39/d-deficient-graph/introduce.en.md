The deficiency $d$ of a $(q_1, q_2)$-graph $\Gamma$ measures how far $\Gamma$ is from being extremal for the Ramsey number $n(q_1, q_2)$. Specifically, $d = n(q_1, q_2) - \nu_0(\Gamma) - 1$, so a $0$-deficient $(q_1, q_2)$-graph has the maximum possible number of vertices, namely $n(q_1, q_2) - 1$. Deficiency is always nonnegative and plays a central role in the inductive analysis of Ramsey numbers, particularly in Lemma C9 which relates the deficiency of a graph to the deficiencies of its neighborhood subgraphs.
