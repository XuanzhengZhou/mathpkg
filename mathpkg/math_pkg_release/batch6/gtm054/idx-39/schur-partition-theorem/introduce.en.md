Schur's Partition Theorem states that any finite partition of the nonnegative integers contains a part that is not sum-free: there exist two elements in that part whose sum also belongs to the same part. Equivalently, the equation $x + y = z$ admits a monochromatic solution under any finite coloring of $\mathbb{N} \cup \{0\}$. This is one of the earliest results in Ramsey theory, predating Ramsey's theorem itself, and follows as a corollary of the finite Ramsey theorem for $s = 2$ with appropriate choice of parameters.
