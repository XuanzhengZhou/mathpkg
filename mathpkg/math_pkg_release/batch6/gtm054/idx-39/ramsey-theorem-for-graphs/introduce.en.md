The Ramsey Theorem for Graphs establishes both the existence of the graph Ramsey numbers $n(q_1, q_2)$ for all $q_1, q_2 \geq 1$ and a fundamental recurrence inequality: $n(q_1, q_2) \leq n(q_1-1, q_2) + n(q_1, q_2-1)$ for $q_1, q_2 \geq 2$. This is a special case of the general Ramsey theorem with $s = 2$, but the graph-theoretic proof via induction on $q_1 + q_2$ provides geometric insight. The recurrence yields the classical upper bound $n(q_1, q_2) \leq \binom{q_1+q_2-2}{q_1-1}$.
