A partial geometry is an incidence structure introduced by R. C. Bose that generalizes both BIB-designs and finite projective geometries. This proposition establishes the fundamental counting relations among the five parameters $b, v, r, k, t$ of a partial geometry. The relations generalize the well-known identities $bk = vr$ and $r(k-1) = \lambda(v-1)$ of BIB-designs, with the parameter $t$ (the number of lines through a point not on a given line that meet that line) playing the role analogous to $\lambda$.
