A Moore graph of type $(\rho, 2)$ is a regular graph of degree $\rho$ with diameter $2$ and girth $5$ that attains the Moore bound. This proposition shows that such graphs are strongly isovalent, meaning they correspond to two-class association schemes. The proof relies on the Bose-Clatworthy theorem (E15): it suffices to verify that the intersection numbers $p_{11}^1$ and $p_{11}^2$ are constant, which follows directly from the structural properties of Moore graphs.
