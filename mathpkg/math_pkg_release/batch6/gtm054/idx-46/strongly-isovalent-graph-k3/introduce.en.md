This proposition is a critical stepping stone toward the Friendship Theorem of Erdős, Rényi, and Sós. It states that the only strongly isovalent (i.e., distance-regular of diameter 2) graph in which two adjacent vertices have exactly one common neighbor and two non-adjacent vertices also have exactly one common neighbor is the triangle $K_3$. The proof uses the algebraic relations among the parameters $p_{jk}^i$ of a two-class association scheme to deduce $n_1 = 2$ and $n_2 = 0$, forcing the graph to be complete on three vertices.
