R. C. Bose derived integrality conditions that any set of parameters $(r, k, t)$ must satisfy for a partial geometry to exist. The first two quantities correspond to $n_1$ and $n_2$ (the numbers of first and second associates) from the associated PBIB-design, while the third is the total number $\alpha$ of lines, which must also be integral. This condition is necessary but not sufficient---Bose and Clatworthy showed that the parameters $(3, 11, 1)$ satisfy these conditions yet no such partial geometry exists.
