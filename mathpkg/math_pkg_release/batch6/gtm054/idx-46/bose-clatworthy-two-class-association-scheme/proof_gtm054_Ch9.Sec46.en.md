The proof is given in Bose and Clatworthy [b.12]. The argument shows that constancy of $p_{11}^1$ and $p_{11}^2$ forces constancy of the remaining six functions $p_{jk}^i$ through the algebraic relations among the intersection numbers of an association scheme. Since E4 is assumed, the $p_{jk}^i(x,y)$ are well-defined as functions on the associate classes; the additional hypothesis that $p_{11}^1$ and $p_{11}^2$ are constant suffices to propagate constancy to all $p_{jk}^i$, yielding a full $2$-class association scheme.
