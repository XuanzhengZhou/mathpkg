Let $L_0 \in \mathcal{L}$ and let $x_0^*$ be a dual line (i.e., a point of $\Lambda^*$) such that $L_0 \notin x_0^*$. Since $x_0 \notin L_0$ in the original geometry, by F2 there exists a $t$-set $\mathcal{T}$ of lines through $x_0$ which meet $L_0$. For each $L \in \mathcal{T}$, let $x_L \in L \cap L_0$. By F3, these points $x_L$ are all distinct. Each of the $t$ dual lines in $\{x_L^* : L \in \mathcal{T}\}$ contains $L_0$ and meets $x_0^*$. Also no other dual lines through $L_0$ meet $x_0^*$. Thus condition F2 holds in $\Lambda^*$ with the same parameter $t$. The verification of F1 and F3 for $\Lambda^*$ is immediate by symmetry, and the parameters interchange as stated.
