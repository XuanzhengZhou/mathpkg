Let $\Gamma$ be a Platonic graph and let $Z_1, \ldots, Z_k$ be the regions of an isocovalent imbedding of $\Gamma$. By the definition and D3, $Z_1, \ldots, Z_k$ form the vertex cocycles of a connected multigraph $\Theta$ orthogonal to $\Gamma$. Clearly $\Theta$ is isovalent without isthmuses or isolated vertices. The vertex cocycles of $\Gamma$ form a $\rho(\Theta)$-covalent imbedding of $\Theta$. Hence by F15, $\Theta$ is of one of the types in Table F16. Since $\rho(\Theta) = \rho^\perp(\Gamma)$ and $\rho^\perp(\Theta) = \rho(\Gamma)$, $\Theta$ is Platonic.
