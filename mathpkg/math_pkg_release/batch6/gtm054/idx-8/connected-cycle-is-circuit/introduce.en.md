Proposition A10 provides the strongest possible converse to A9: if a nonzero cycle $Z$ in the cycle space has the property that its submultigraph $\Gamma_Z$ is connected, then $\Gamma_Z$ must be a single circuit. This characterizes when a cycle comes from exactly one circuit as opposed to a disjoint union of circuits.
