A multigraph is $k$-separated if it contains a proper nonempty submultigraph that has exactly $k$ attachment vertices and carries at least $k$ edges itself while leaving at least $k$ edges outside. This definition refines the notion of graph separation for multigraphs by measuring separation in terms of both vertex attachment size and edge distribution. The condition ensures the separating set is neither trivially small nor trivial in edge count.
