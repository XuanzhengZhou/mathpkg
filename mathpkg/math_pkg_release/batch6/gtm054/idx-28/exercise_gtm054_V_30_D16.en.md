Suppose that $\Gamma$ admits the atomic partition and that $\kappa(\Gamma) < \check{\rho}(\Gamma)$.

(a) Determine the graph $\Gamma$ such that $\nu_0(\Gamma)$ is minimal, and show that it is unique.

(b) Show that $\kappa(\Gamma)$ cannot be prime and that $\check{\rho}(\Gamma)$ cannot equal $4$ or $6$.

(c) Show that if $\check{\rho} = 8$, then $\Gamma$ is a graph of the form in the proof of D14 with $n = 3$.

(d) Show that if $\check{\rho} = 7$, then $\kappa = 6$ and either $\alpha = 2$ or $\alpha = 3$; in the latter case the atoms induce paths of length $2$. Construct examples in both cases.
