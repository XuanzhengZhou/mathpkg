Let $W_1$ and $W_2$ be fragments of $\Gamma$. Show that:

(a) $W$ is a fragment of $\Gamma$ if and only if $\overline{W}$ is a fragment of $\Gamma$.

(b) $N(W_i) = N(\overline{W}_i)$;

(c) $W_i = \overline{\overline{W}}_i$;

(d) $N(W_1 \cap W_2) \subseteq (W_1 \cap N(W_2)) + (N(W_1) \cap W_2) + (N(W_1) \cap N(W_2))$;

(e) $N(W_1 \cup W_2) = (\overline{W}_1 \cap N(W_2)) + (N(W_1) \cap \overline{W}_2) + (N(W_1) \cap N(W_2))$;

(f) $W_1 \cap W_2$ and $W_1 \cup W_2$ are fragments whenever the corresponding intersections and unions are proper and nonempty.
