Writing the vertex connectivity as $\kappa = m\alpha + b$ with remainder $0 \leq b < \alpha$ yields a refined upper bound for vertex degrees in graphs admitting the atomic partition. The bound $\rho(x) \leq \frac{m+1}{m}\kappa - 1 - \frac{b}{m}$ is particularly sharp for vertex-transitive graphs where the atomic partition naturally exists.
