The Tutte connectivity $\tau(\Gamma)$ of a multigraph is the minimum $k$ such that $\Gamma$ is $k$-separated, taking the value $\infty$ if no such $k$ exists. Unlike vertex connectivity $\kappa(\Gamma)$, Tutte connectivity considers submultigraphs constrained by edge counts rather than vertex interior/exterior conditions. A multigraph is $m$-Tutte connected when its Tutte connectivity is at least $m$.
