When every vertex of a graph belongs to some atom (a fragment of minimum cardinality), the atoms form a unique partition of the vertex set called the atomic partition. Since distinct atoms are always disjoint, this partition provides a canonical decomposition of the graph. The atomic partition always has more than one cell and is particularly relevant for vertex-transitive graphs, where it corresponds to a complete system of imprimitivity.
