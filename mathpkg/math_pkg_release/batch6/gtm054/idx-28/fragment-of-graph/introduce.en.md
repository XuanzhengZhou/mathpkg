For a connected graph that is not complete, a fragment is a vertex subset $W$ whose interior and complement are both nonempty, and whose neighborhood has cardinality exactly equal to the vertex-connectivity $\kappa(\Gamma)$. Fragments are the minimal building blocks for analyzing the connectivity structure of a graph. Every fragment $W$ has the property that its complement $\overline{W}$ is also a fragment, and the neighborhood mapping satisfies symmetries studied through fragment intersection and union properties.
