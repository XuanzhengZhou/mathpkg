Proposition B28 describes three fundamental structural relationships in a matroid. Part (a) states that for any basis $B$ and any element $x$ not in $B$, there is a unique cycle (the fundamental circuit of $x$ with respect to $B$) contained in $B \cup \{x\}$ and containing $x$. Part (b) is the dual statement: for any cycle $M$ and any $x \in M$, removing $x$ yields an independent set that can be extended to a basis $B_x$ not containing $x$, with $M \subseteq B_x \cup \{x\}$. Part (c) characterizes spanning sets: $S$ spans the matroid iff every element outside $S$ belongs to some cycle contained in $S \cup \{x\}$.
