Recall that $\Gamma$ is biconnected if and only if $\mathcal{X}^{\perp}(\Gamma)$ is connected. We introduce a general concept of connectivity of a subspace in such a way that for any multigraph $\Gamma$, the connectivity of $\mathcal{X}^{\perp}(\Gamma)$ is less by 1 than $\tau(\Gamma)$. A subspace $\mathcal{A} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(E)$ is $m$-separated if there exists $\{F_1, F_2\} \in \mathbb{P}_2(E)$ such that

(a) $m < \min\{|F_1|, |F_2|\}$;
(b) $m = \dim(\mathcal{A}) - \dim(\mathcal{A} \cap \mathcal{P}(F_1)) - \dim(\mathcal{A} \cap \mathcal{P}(F_2))$.

The connectivity of the subspace $\mathcal{A}$ is defined analogously as $\tau(\mathcal{A}) = \min\{m: \mathcal{A} is m$-separated\}.
