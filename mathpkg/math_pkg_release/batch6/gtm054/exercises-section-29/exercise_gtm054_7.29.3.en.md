Suppose $\Gamma \neq K_1, K_2, K_3, B_2

PROOF. By E4, we may assume that some two vertices are not incident. By Exercise C16, there exists a submultigraph $\Theta$ of $\Gamma$ with an interior vertex $a$, an exterior vertex $z$, and $|A(\Theta)| = \kappa(\Gamma)$. By C4, $\kappa(\Gamma) \leq \rho(a) \leq \nu_1(\Theta)$, and $\kappa(\Gamma) \leq \rho(z) \leq \nu_1(\Gamma) - \nu_1(\Theta)$. So $\kappa(\Gamma) \leq \nu_1(\Theta) \leq \nu_1(\Gamma) - \kappa(\Gamma)$. Hence $\tau(\Gamma) \leq |A(\Theta)| = \kappa(\Gamma)$.

The girth of $\Gamma$ is the cardinality of a smallest nonempty cycle of $\Gamma$ and is denoted by $\gamma(\Gamma)$. We adopt the convention that $\gamma(\Gamma) = \infty$ if $\Gamma$ is a forest. Clearly $\gamma(\Gamma) \geq 2$ for any multigraph $\Gamma$, and $\gamma(\Gamma) \geq 3$ if and only if $\Gamma$ is a graph.
