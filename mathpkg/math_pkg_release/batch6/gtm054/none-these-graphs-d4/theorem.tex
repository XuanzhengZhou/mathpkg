None of these graphs is biconnected, and we may inquire whether perhaps biconnectivity will suffice. It does not; $\Gamma_4$ and $\Gamma_5$ are biconnected, non-isomorphic, and have identical cycle and cocycle spaces. We observe in this example that while $\{1, 3, 4\}$ is a vertex cocycle of $\Gamma_4$, it is not a vertex cocycle of $\Gamma_5$. We wish then to find conditions on a multigraph so that its vertex cocycles may be distinguished algebraically from the other cocycles.

Let $\Gamma$ be connected and let $C$ be an elementary cocycle. By C7, $\Gamma_{(C)}$ has two components. If $C$ is not a vertex cocycle, both of these components contain edges, and so $\mathcal{X}^{\perp}(\Gamma_{(C)})$ is not connected. (For example, let $C = \{4, 5\}$ in $\Gamma_4$ of the Figure.)
