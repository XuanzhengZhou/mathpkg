This proposition reveals a deeper symmetry among the parameters of an $m$-class association scheme: the product $n_i p_{jk}^i$ is invariant under cyclic permutation of the three indices $(i, j, k)$. Equivalently, counting the number of triples $(x, y, z) \in V^3$ such that $\{x, y\} \in Q_i$, $\{y, z\} \in Q_j$, and $\{z, x\} \in Q_k$ in three different ways (fixing each pair in turn and counting the appropriate third vertex) yields the same value. This identity further constrains the independent parameter set beyond the $p_{jk}^i = p_{kj}^i$ symmetry and the row-sum formula of Proposition E10.
