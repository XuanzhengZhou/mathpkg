A normal set in the context of matching theory is an extremal subset $U$ of vertices that satisfies two additional properties: the union of $U$ and its neighbor set $N(U)$ covers the entire vertex set $V$, and every component of the subgraph induced by $U$ is itself almost factorable. Normal sets play a crucial role in the constructive characterization of maximum independent edge sets (matchings) in graphs.
