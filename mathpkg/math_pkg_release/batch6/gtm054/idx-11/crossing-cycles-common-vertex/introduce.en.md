This lemma (presented without an explicit number in the text but following from Lemma E21) asserts a fundamental geometric property of planar imbeddings: if two cycles cross at a vertex, they must intersect at another vertex as well. Equivalently, two cycles cannot cross at exactly one vertex in a planar imbedding. The proof uses the cyclic ordering of edges around a vertex (Lemma E21) and classifies incident edges into types based on how many region cycles they belong to.
