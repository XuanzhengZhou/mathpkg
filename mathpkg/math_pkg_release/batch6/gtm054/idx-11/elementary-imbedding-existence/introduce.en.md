Let $\Gamma$ be a planar multigraph. An elementary imbedding is a planar imbedding in which every region is bounded by an elementary circuit, and a simple imbedding is one in which no region touches itself. This exercise asks to prove that every planar multigraph possesses at least one elementary planar imbedding and at least one simple planar imbedding. These are foundational existence results that ensure these special types of planar imbeddings are always available for study.
