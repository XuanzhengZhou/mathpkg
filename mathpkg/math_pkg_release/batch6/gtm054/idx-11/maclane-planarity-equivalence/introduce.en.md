MacLane's theorem is the central result of Chapter E: it establishes that the combinatorial definition of planarity — based on the existence of a planar imbedding in the cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)$ — is equivalent to the conventional topological notion of being drawable in the plane without edge crossings. The section concludes with a heuristic sketch of the proof, indicating the main ideas without full topological rigor. The forward direction (combinatorial implies topological) relies on the fact that each region cycle being elementary allows it to bound a topological disk.
