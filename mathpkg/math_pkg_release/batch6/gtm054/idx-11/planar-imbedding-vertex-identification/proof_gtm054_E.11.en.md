Let $\Gamma'$ be obtained by identification of the two vertices in $f(e)$, and let $F = \operatorname{Fnd}(\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)) \setminus f^{-1}[f(e)]$. By Lemma E14, we have

$$\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma') = \pi_F[\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)].$$

Since $Z_1, \ldots, Z_k$ span $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)$, their projections $\pi_F[Z_1], \ldots, \pi_F[Z_k]$ span $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma')$. Moreover, the planar imbedding relation $\sum_{i=1}^{k} Z_i = \varnothing$ in $\Gamma$ projects to the corresponding relation in $\Gamma'$, and each edge in $\operatorname{Fnd}(\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma'))$ remains in at most two of the projected cycles. Thus the projections of the region cycles form a planar imbedding of $\Gamma'$.
