Let $\Gamma$ be a planar multigraph. This proposition, labeled E9 in the text, establishes a clean algebraic criterion: all planar imbeddings of $\Gamma$ are simultaneously simple (no self-touching regions) and elementary (regions are elementary circuits) precisely when its cycle space $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)$ is connected. If $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)$ is not connected, there exist planar imbeddings that fail to be simple. This bridges the combinatorial geometry of planar imbeddings with the algebraic structure of the cycle space.
