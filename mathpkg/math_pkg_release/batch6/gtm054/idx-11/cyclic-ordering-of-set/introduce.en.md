A cyclic ordering of an $n$-element set $U$ is a way to arrange the elements of $U$ in a circle, formalized as a bijection from the cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}_n$ to $U$. This notion is essential in planar graph theory: around each vertex of a planar imbedding, the incident edges naturally inherit a cyclic ordering corresponding to their rotational arrangement in the plane.
