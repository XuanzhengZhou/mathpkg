A contraction of a multigraph $\Gamma = (V, f, E)$ is obtained by partitioning the vertex set $V$ into subsets (each inducing a connected submultigraph) and collapsing each subset to a single new vertex. Edges whose endpoints lie entirely within a single subset are deleted, and edges that span across partitions are retained with their incidence updated to the new vertices. This operation generalizes the topological notion of collapsing a subspace and is fundamental to graph minor theory and planarity arguments.
