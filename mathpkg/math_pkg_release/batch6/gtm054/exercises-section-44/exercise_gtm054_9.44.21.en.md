Let $V, \mathcal{Q}$, and the functions $p_{ijk}^1$ be as given in the statement of Theorem E15. Show that the conclusion of Corollary E20 also holds if instead of requiring the constancy specifically of $p_{11}^1$ and $p_{11}^2$, we merely require the constancy of some one function $p_{jk}^1$ and some one function $p_{ih}^2$.

Bose and Clatworthy [b.12] show further that to assume the constancy of some arbitrary pair of the eight functions $p_{jk}^1$ is not sufficient for a 2-class association scheme. Their counter-example, being a PBIB-design (in our sense, not theirs) of blocksize 2, can be represented by the graph in Figure E22, where the blocks coincide with the edges. The PBIB-design parameters are $v = 7$, $b = 14$, $r = 4$, $k = 2$, $\lambda_1 = 1$, $n_1 = 4$, $\lambda_2 = 0$, $n_2 = 2$. The four functions $p_{ij}^2$ are all constant, with $p_{11}^2 = 3$, $p_{12}^2 = p_{21}^2 = 1$, and $p_{22}^2 = 0$. However, $p_{11}^1(x_0, x_1) = 1$ while $p_{11}^1(x_0, x_3) = 2$. In the light of Exercise E21, none of the functions $p_{ij}^1$ is constant. Observe also that if one considers the complementary graph, then each of the functions is $p_{ij}^1$ is constant but none of the functions $p_{ij}^2$ is.
