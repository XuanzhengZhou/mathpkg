Let $m, n \in \mathbb{N}$ and $2 < m < n$. Let $v = mn$, and let $V$ be a $v$-set whose elements are displayed in an $m \times n$ array. Two elements of $V$ will be first associates if they lie in the same row of this array, second associates if they lie in the same column, and third associates otherwise. This yields a 3-class association scheme. A PBIB-design satisfying E6 can be formed letting each union of one row and one column of the array be a block (Bose and Nair [b. 14]).
