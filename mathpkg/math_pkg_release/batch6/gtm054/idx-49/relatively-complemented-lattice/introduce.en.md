A lattice is relatively complemented if every interval $[x,y]$ in the lattice is a complemented lattice. This is a stronger condition than the lattice itself being complemented. Matroid lattices are relatively complemented, which can be interpreted geometrically: in a geometry, the interval between two closed sets corresponds to a subgeometry that is itself complemented.
