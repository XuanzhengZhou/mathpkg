Let $V$ be an $n$-set and let $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ be a matroid of rank $r$. Define

$$\mathcal{B}^{\perp}(\Lambda) = \{B \in \mathcal{P}(V): V \setminus B \in \mathcal{B}(\Lambda)\}.$$

By Corollary A3, $(V, \mathcal{B}^{\perp}(\Lambda))$ is a basis system. Define

$$\mathcal{I}^{\perp}(\Lambda) = \bigcup_{B \in \mathcal{B}^{\perp}(\Lambda)} \mathcal{P}(B)$$

and

$$\mathcal{M}^{\perp} = \mathcal{M}(\mathcal{P}(V) \setminus \mathcal{I}^{\perp}(\Lambda)).$$

Then $\Lambda^{\perp} = (V, \mathcal{M}^{\perp})$ is a matroid of rank $n - r$, called the **matroid orthogonal to** $\Lambda$.
