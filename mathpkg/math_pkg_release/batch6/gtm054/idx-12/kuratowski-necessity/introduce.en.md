This proposition establishes the necessity part of the famous Kuratowski criterion for planarity: any graph that contains $K_5$ (the complete graph on 5 vertices) or $K_{3,3}$ (the complete bipartite graph on 3+3 vertices) as a subcontraction cannot be planar. The proof uses the fact that subcontractions preserve planarity (Exercise E15), so it suffices to show that neither $K_5$ nor $K_{3,3}$ is planar. The non-planarity of $K_5$ follows from Proposition E6 and D13; the non-planarity of $K_{3,3}$ is shown via Euler's formula and the average covalence relation.
