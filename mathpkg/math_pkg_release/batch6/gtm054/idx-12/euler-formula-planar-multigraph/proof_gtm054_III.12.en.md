From Proposition F1, $\nu_2(\Gamma) = \nu_1(\Gamma) - \nu_0(\Gamma) + \nu_{-1}(\Gamma) + 1$. Rearranging yields the general form:
$$\nu_2 - \nu_1 + \nu_0 - \nu_{-1} = 1.$$
For a connected planar multigraph, $\nu_{-1}(\Gamma) = 1$, and substituting this value gives the classical Euler Formula:
$$\nu_2 - \nu_1 + \nu_0 = 2.$$
