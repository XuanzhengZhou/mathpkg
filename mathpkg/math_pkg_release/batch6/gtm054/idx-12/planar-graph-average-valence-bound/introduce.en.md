This proposition establishes a fundamental numerical bound for planar multigraphs: the average valence is always strictly less than 6, which forces the existence of a vertex of valence at most 5. Part (b) gives a dual bound: if the average valence is at least 3, then the average covalence is strictly less than 6. These bounds follow from Euler's formula (via Corollary F4) and the observation that every region in a planar imbedding of a graph without isthmuses has covalence at least 3 (Corollary F7b).
