The average covalence $\rho^{\perp}(\Gamma)$ is the dual notion to average valence $\rho(\Gamma)$. While average valence measures the mean number of edges incident to a vertex, average covalence measures the mean number of edges bounding a region in a planar imbedding. It is computed as twice the number of edges divided by the number of regions. Along with average valence, the average covalence provides an elegant reformulation of Euler's Formula.
