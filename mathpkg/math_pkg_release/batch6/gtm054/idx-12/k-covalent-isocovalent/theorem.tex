Let $\Gamma$ be a planar multigraph without isthmuses and let $Z_1, \ldots, Z_{\nu_2(\Gamma)}$ be a simple planar imbedding of $\Gamma$. The covalence of the region $Z_i$ is $\rho^{\perp}(Z_i) = |Z_i|$.

\begin{itemize}
\item If for some $k \in \mathbb{N}$, $\rho^{\perp}(Z_i) = k$ for all $i = 1, \ldots, \nu_2(\Gamma)$, then the planar imbedding is said to be \textbf{$k$-covalent}.
\item If for some $k \in \mathbb{N}$, every simple planar imbedding of $\Gamma$ is $k$-covalent, then $\Gamma$ is said to be \textbf{$k$-covalent}.
\item $\Gamma$ is \textbf{isocovalent} if it is $k$-covalent for some $k \in \mathbb{N}$.
\end{itemize}
