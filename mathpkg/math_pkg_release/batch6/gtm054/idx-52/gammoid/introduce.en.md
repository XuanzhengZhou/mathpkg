A gammoid is any submatroid obtained by restricting a strict gammoid to a subset of its ground set. Equivalently, a gammoid is a matroid on a set $U$ of vertices of a directed graph $(V, D)$ (with $U \subseteq V$), determined by a target set $Z \subseteq V$, where the independent sets are those $A \subseteq U$ that can be linked to $Z$ by $|A|$ pairwise vertex-disjoint directed paths. Gammoids generalize the matroid structure arising from Menger's theorem and linking systems in directed graphs.
