Proposition F4 asserts that for a matroid $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$, the existence of a Tutte subspace over a field $\mathbb{F}$ is equivalent to the existence of a Whitney function over $\mathbb{F}$. A Whitney function is a map $w: V \to \mathbb{F}^V / \mathcal{T}$ (or, in an alternative formulation, a linear transformation encoding the matroid's cycle structure). This equivalence is fundamental to the algebraic theory of matroid representability: both a Tutte subspace (a subspace of $\mathbb{F}^V$ whose minimal nonempty supports are the cycles of $\Lambda$) and a Whitney function provide dual algebraic descriptions of the same representability property.
