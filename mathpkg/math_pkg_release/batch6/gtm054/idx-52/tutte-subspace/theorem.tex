Let $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ be a matroid and let $\mathbb{F}$ be a field. A \textbf{Tutte subspace} for $\Lambda$ over $\mathbb{F}$ is a subspace $\mathcal{T} \subseteq \mathbb{F}^V$ such that the set of minimal nonempty supports of functions in $\mathcal{T}$ is precisely $\mathcal{M}$, the set of cycles (circuits) of $\Lambda$. That is,
$$\mathcal{M} = \{\sigma(h) : h \in \mathcal{T} \setminus \{0\} \text{ and } \sigma(h) \text{ is minimal with respect to inclusion}\},$$
where $\sigma(h) = \{x \in V : h(x) \neq 0\}$ denotes the support of $h$.
