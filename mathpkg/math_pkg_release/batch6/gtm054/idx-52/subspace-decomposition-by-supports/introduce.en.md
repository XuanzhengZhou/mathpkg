Exercise F10 extends Proposition IVA6 to vector subspaces of $\mathbb{F}^V$ over arbitrary fields. It asks to prove that any function $h$ in a subspace $\mathcal{S} \subseteq \mathbb{F}^V$ can be decomposed as a sum $h = \sum_{i=1}^m h_i$ where each summand $h_i$ belongs to $\mathcal{S}$, has support contained in $\sigma(h)$, and whose support is a minimal nonempty support among all functions in $\mathcal{S}$. When $\mathbb{F}$ is an ordered field, an additional sign condition holds (as in Proposition IVA6(c)), but this is not required for the general case. The result is important for the algebraic theory of matroids where $\mathcal{S}$ is a Tutte subspace and its minimal nonempty supports are precisely the cycles of the matroid.
