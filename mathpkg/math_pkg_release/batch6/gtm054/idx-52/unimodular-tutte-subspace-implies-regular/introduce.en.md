Proposition F11 provides a sufficient condition for a matroid to be regular (representable over every field). If a matroid $\Lambda$ admits a Tutte subspace $\mathcal{T}$ over the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ that is unimodular (i.e., has a basis consisting of vectors with entries in $\{-1, 0, 1\}$ satisfying certain integrality properties), then $\Lambda$ is regular. The proof proceeds by showing that for any arbitrary field $\mathbb{F}$, one can map the unimodular Tutte subspace from $\mathbb{Q}^V$ into $\mathbb{F}^V$ by reinterpreting the integer coefficients $-1, 0, 1$ as the corresponding field elements. This yields a Tutte subspace over $\mathbb{F}$, establishing representability over every field.
