A geodetic graph is one in which, for any pair of vertices, there is exactly one geodesic (shortest path) connecting them. This property generalizes the behavior of trees (which are geodetic) and captures graphs where shortest paths behave nicely. The study of geodetic graphs connects to the theory of lobes and the dimension of the cycle space.
