It follows directly from the definitions that each of (a), (b), and (c) is equivalent to $\mathcal{L}^\perp = \mathcal{P}(E)$. Hence they are equivalent to each other.

Note that (d) is equivalent to $\nu_{-1} = 1$. Thus by A15a, any two of the conditions (c), (d), (e) imply the third. If $\Gamma$ is a tree, then (a) and (d) hold. $\square$
