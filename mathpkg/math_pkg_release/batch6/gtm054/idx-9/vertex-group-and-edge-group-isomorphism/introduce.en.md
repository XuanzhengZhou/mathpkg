This exercise explores the relationship between the vertex automorphism group and the edge automorphism group of a graph. For finite graphs, these groups are isomorphic precisely when the graph is "vertex-edge symmetric" — a condition equivalent to having at most one isolated vertex and no component consisting of a single edge (K_2). The result fails for infinite graphs, revealing a fundamental difference between finite and infinite graph symmetry.
