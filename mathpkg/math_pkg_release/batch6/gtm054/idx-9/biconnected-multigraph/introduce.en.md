A biconnected multigraph is a connected multigraph with at least two edges that contains no articulation vertex — meaning no single vertex whose removal disconnects the graph. Equivalently, a multigraph with two or more edges is biconnected precisely when it is connected and every vertex cocycle is elementary (i.e., cannot be expressed as a proper sum of nonempty cocycles). Biconnectedness is the natural two-connected analog for multigraphs and is closely related to the structure of the cycle and cocycle spaces.
