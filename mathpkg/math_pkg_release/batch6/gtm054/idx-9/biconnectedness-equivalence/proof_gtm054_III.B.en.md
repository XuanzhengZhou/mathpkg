(b) $\Rightarrow$ (c). Since $\mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Gamma)$ is connected and $|E| \geq 2$, $\Gamma$ has no isthmuses by IIC10b. By Exercise A21, $\text{Fnd}(\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)) = E = \text{Fnd}(\mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Gamma))$. By IIC12, $\mathcal{Z}(\Gamma)$ is connected.

(c) $\Rightarrow$ (b). Since $\text{Fnd}(\mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Gamma)) = E$, the result follows from IIC12.

(c) $\Leftrightarrow$ (d). This follows from IIC22a.

(b) $\Rightarrow$ (a). Assume that $\mathcal{Z}^\perp(\Gamma)$ is connected. It follows from B1 that $\Gamma$ contains no articulation vertex. Now let $x$ and $x'$ be any two vertices of $\Gamma$; we must show that there exists an $xx'$-path. Since $\Gamma$ has no isolated vertices, we may choose edges $e, e'$ incident with $x$ and $x'$, respectively. Since (b) implies (d), $e$ and $e'$ (and hence $x$ and $x'$) are in a common elementary circuit. Thus there exists an $xx'$-path, and $\Gamma$ is connected. Hence $\Gamma$ is biconnected.
