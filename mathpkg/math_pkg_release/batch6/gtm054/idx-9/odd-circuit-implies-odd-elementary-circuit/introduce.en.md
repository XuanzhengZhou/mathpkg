This exercise asks to prove that the existence of any odd circuit in a multigraph implies the existence of an odd elementary circuit. An elementary circuit is one with no repeated vertices (except start=end). The result follows from the fact that any circuit can be decomposed into elementary circuits, and the sum of their lengths mod 2 must equal the length of the original circuit mod 2.
