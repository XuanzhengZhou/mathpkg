This proposition gives multiple equivalent characterizations of edge subsets whose removal yields a spanning forest. The conditions range from dimension-based descriptions to extremal properties (minimal with respect to meeting cocycles, maximal with respect to containing no cycles) to basis-theoretic descriptions of the cocycle space. The equivalence of (a) and (b) is the core, with the remaining conditions following from the spanning forest property. Note the Exchange Property: a new spanning forest is obtained from an old one by exchanging one edge for another.
