There is no loss of generality in assuming that $\Gamma$ has no isolated vertices and hence is a hypergraph. Thus by II D11, $\Gamma$ is connected. Suppose that $x_0$ is an articulation vertex of $\Gamma$. Let $C_1$ be an elementary cocycle such that $C_1 \subset f^*(x_0)$, and let $C_2 = f^*(x_0) + C_1$. By A16, $C_1 = \sum_{x \in U_1} f^*(x)$ for some $U_1 \subset V + \{x_0\}$. Let $U_2 = V + \{x_0\} + U_1$. Then $C_2 = \sum_{x \in U_2} f^*(x)$. Thus both $C_1$ and $C_2$ are nonempty cocycles, and $f^*(x_0) = C_1 + C_2$. Since $C_1 \cap C_2 = \varnothing$, the cocycle space $\mathcal{L}^\perp(\Gamma)$ splits as a direct sum of the subspace generated by cocycles supported in $U_1 \cup \{x_0\}$ and that generated by cocycles supported in $U_2 \cup \{x_0\}$. Hence $\mathcal{L}^\perp(\Gamma)$ is not connected, contradiction.
