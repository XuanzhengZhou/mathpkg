This inequality gives a simple lower bound for the edge chromatic number of any multigraph: the edge chromatic number is at least the maximum degree $\hat{\rho}(\Theta)$. The bound is sharp; for example, a multigraph obtained by replicating each edge of an even circuit exactly $r$ times satisfies $\hat{\rho} = \rho = 2r = \chi_1$. Together with Vizing's theorem (A37), it yields the classical bounds $\hat{\rho} \leq \chi_1 \leq \hat{\rho} + 1$ for simple graphs.
