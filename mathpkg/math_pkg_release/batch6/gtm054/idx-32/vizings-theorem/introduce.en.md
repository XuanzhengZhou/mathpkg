Vizing's theorem provides a fundamental upper bound for the edge chromatic number (chromatic index) of any multigraph. The bound depends on the maximum degree $\hat{\rho}(\Theta)$ and the maximum multiplicity $s(U)$ of edges between any subset of vertices. For simple graphs, this specializes to the classical Vizing bound $\chi_1 \leq \hat{\rho} + 1$. The theorem is sharp in the sense that the complete graph $K_3$ with each edge replicated $r$ times achieves equality: $\chi_1 = 2r + r$ while $\hat{\rho} = 2r$.
