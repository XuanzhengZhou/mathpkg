Let $m \geq 4$ be given and let $\Theta$ be a graph satisfying the hypothesis. Let $\Gamma = (V, \mathcal{E})$ be a $\chi_0(\Theta)$-critical subgraph of $\Theta$. Then $\Gamma$ satisfies the hypothesis, too. By A12, $\chi_0 - 1 \leq \rho(x) \leq m - 1$ for all $x \in V$. If $\chi_0 = m$, then $\Gamma$ is an $m$-critical, $(m - 1)$-valent graph different from $K_m$. By IIIA1, $2\nu_1 = (m - 1)\nu_0$. But by Theorem A20

$$2\nu_1 \geq (m - 1)\nu_0 + m - 3 > (m - 1)\nu_0,$$

which contradicts $2\nu_1 = (m - 1)\nu_0$. Hence $\chi_0(\Gamma) \leq m - 1$.
