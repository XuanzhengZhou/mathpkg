Brooks' theorem is a classical result in chromatic graph theory that provides a sharp upper bound on the chromatic number of a connected graph. It states that for any connected graph with maximum degree $\Delta \geq 3$, the chromatic number is at most $\Delta$, with the sole exceptions of complete graphs and odd cycles. The theorem refines the trivial bound $\chi_0(\Gamma) \leq \Delta + 1$ by showing that equality can only hold for these specific families of graphs.
