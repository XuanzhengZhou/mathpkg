Let $U$ be an $n$-set and choose $x \in U$. The collection of $m$-subsets of $U$ which do not contain $x$ is precisely $\mathcal{P}_m(U + \{x\})$, while the collection of those that do is set-isomorphic to $\mathcal{P}_{m-1}(U + \{x\})$. Hence $|\mathcal{P}_{m-1}(U + \{x\})| + |\mathcal{P}_m(U + \{x\})| = |\mathcal{P}_m(U)|$.

Of course one could also have obtained this corollary from the definition by simple computation. It is, however, of interest to see a combinatorial argument as well.
