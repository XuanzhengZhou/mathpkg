A system is asymmetric if its point automorphism group $G_0(\Lambda)$ is trivial, meaning no non-identity permutation of the vertices can be extended to an automorphism of the entire system. This is the strongest form of rigidity for an incidence structure. Asymmetry plays a key role in the structure theory of fixed-point free systems.
