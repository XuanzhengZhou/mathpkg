Let $\eta: G_0(\Lambda) \rightarrow G_1(\Lambda)$ be the map defined such that $(\eta(q), q) \in G(\Lambda)$. Prove:

(a) If $\Lambda$ distinguishes vertices, then $\eta$ is an injection.

(b) If $\Lambda$ is a set system, then $\eta$ is uniquely determined and is a (group) epimorphism.

(c) For any $\Lambda$, there exists a homomorphism $\eta: G_0(\Lambda) \rightarrow G_1(\Lambda)$ such that $(\eta(q), q) \in G(\Lambda)$ for all $q \in G_0(\Lambda)$.
