A permutation group $G$ on a set $X$ is transitive if it can send any element of $X$ to any other via some group element. This is a fundamental property in the theory of group actions and is used in the analysis of automorphism groups of combinatorial systems, particularly in the wreath product decomposition of component automorphism groups.
