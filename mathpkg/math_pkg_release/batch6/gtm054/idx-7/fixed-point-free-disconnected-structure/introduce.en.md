This proposition, first proved by G. Sabidussi for the special case of graphs, describes the structure of disconnected systems that are fixed-point free. Such a system can have at most two components with nonempty vertex sets, and each of those components must be asymmetric ($G_0(\Lambda_i) = \{1_{V_i}\}$). This gives a strong constraint linking the fixed-point free property to decomposability and asymmetry.
