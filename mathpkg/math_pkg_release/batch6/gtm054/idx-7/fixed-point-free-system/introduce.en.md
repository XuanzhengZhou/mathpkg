A system is fixed-point free if it is not asymmetric (so $G_0(\Lambda)$ is nontrivial) but every non-identity automorphism in $G_0(\Lambda)$ is a derangement -- it has no fixed points on the vertex set. This concept was introduced by Sabidussi for graphs and generalizes to arbitrary incidence systems. Fixed-point free systems have a highly constrained component structure.
