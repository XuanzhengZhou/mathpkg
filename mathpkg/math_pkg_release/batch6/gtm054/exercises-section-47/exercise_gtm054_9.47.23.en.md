Determine the closed sets of the cocycle matroid of a multigraph.

A matroid $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ is called a geometry if $\mathcal{P}_0(V) + \mathcal{P}_1(V) \subseteq \mathcal{C}(\Lambda)$. (Crapo and Rota [c.5] use the term “pregeometry” for a matroid.) Both of the Fano matroids, for example, are geometries. Their closed sets are precisely the “geometric objects” such as the points, the lines, the entire plane, and of course $\varnothing$. The same is true of the matroid in Example C21.
