The transversal matroid of a set system $\Lambda = (V, f, E)$ is the matroid whose independent sets are precisely the transversals of $\Lambda$. By Lemma E1, these transversals form an independence system, and the resulting matroid structure encodes the transversal theory of the set system into the language of matroids. Transversal matroids form an important and well-studied class of matroids with applications in combinatorial optimization and matching theory.
