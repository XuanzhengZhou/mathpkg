A minor of a matroid $\Lambda = (V, \mathcal{M})$ is obtained by first taking either a restriction to a set $U$ or a projection onto $U$, and then applying the other operation with respect to a subset $T \subseteq U$. The two orders of operation produce equivalent results. Just as graph minors are fundamental to graph theory, matroid minors serve as the essential structural reductions for studying matroid connectivity, excluded-minor characterizations, and matroid representability.
