The connectivity $\tau(\Lambda)$ of a matroid $\Lambda$ is defined as the minimum integer $m$ for which $\Lambda$ is $m$-separated, with $\tau(\Lambda) = \infty$ if no such separation exists. This invariant measures how tightly the matroid is held together: a higher value indicates stronger connectivity. This is the matroid-theoretic analogue of Tutte connectivity for graphs.
