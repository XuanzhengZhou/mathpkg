From the definition of an $m \times m$ Hadamard matrix, it follows that the inner product of two distinct rows is always 0. Hence each row other than the first row of a normalized Hadamard matrix must have as many entries equal +1 as -1. It follows that if $m > 1$, then $m$ is even. In fact a stronger condition holds.
