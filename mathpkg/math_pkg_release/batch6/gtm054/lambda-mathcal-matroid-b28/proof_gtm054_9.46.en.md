(a) Let $B \in \mathcal{B}(\Lambda)$ and let $x \in V + B$. Since $B + \{x\} \notin \mathcal{I}(\Lambda)$, there exists $M_x \in \mathcal{M}$ such that $M_x \subseteq B + \{x\}$ by Proposition B19. Since $M_x \notin B$, we must have $x \in M_x$. Now suppose that $x \in M \subseteq B + \{x\}$. If $M \neq M_x$, then $(M, M_x, x)$ is an admissible triple. There exists $M' \in \mathcal{M}$ such that $M' \subseteq (M \cup M_x) + \{x\} \subseteq B$, which is impossible.

(b) Let $M \in \mathcal{M}$ and let $x \in M$. Then $M + \{x\} \in \mathcal{I}(\Lambda)$, and hence $M + \{x\} \subseteq B_x$ for some $B_x \in \mathcal{B}(\Lambda)$. Since $M \notin B_x$, we have $x \notin B_x$. Hence $M \subseteq B_x + \{x\}$.
