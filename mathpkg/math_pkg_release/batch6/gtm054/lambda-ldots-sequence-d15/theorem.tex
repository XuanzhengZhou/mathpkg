Let $\Lambda^{(i)} = (V_i, M_i)$ for $i = 0, \ldots, n$ be a sequence of matroids such that for each $i = 1, \ldots, n$, $V_i \subseteq V_{i-1}$ and either $\Lambda^{(i)} = \Lambda_{V_i}^{(i-1)}$ or $\Lambda^{(i)} = \Lambda_{[V_i]}^{(i-1)}$. Then there exist $T$, $U \in \mathcal{P}(V_0)$ containing $V_n$ such that $\Lambda^{(n)} = (\Lambda_{[T]}^{(0)})_{V_n} = (\Lambda_{[U]}^{(0)})_{V_n}.$

If $\Lambda = (V, M)$ is a matroid, then any matroid of the form $(\Lambda_U)_{[T]},$ or equivalently of the form $(\Lambda_{[U]})_{T},$ where $T \subseteq U \subseteq V,$ is called a minor of $\Lambda.$
