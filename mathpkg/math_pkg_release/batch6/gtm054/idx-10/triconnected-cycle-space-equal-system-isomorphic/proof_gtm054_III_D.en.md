Let $\Gamma = (V, f, E)$ and $\Gamma' = (U, g, E)$ satisfy the hypotheses. Then for any $x \in V$, $\Gamma_{V+\{x\}}$ is biconnected. Since the submultigraphs $\Gamma_{V+\{x\}}$ and $\Gamma_{(f*\{x\})}$ differ only insofar as the latter includes the isolated vertex $x$, the two submultigraphs have equal cocycle spaces, which by B3 must be connected. Also $\mathcal{Z}^{\perp}(\Gamma'_{(f*\{x\})})$ is connected, since by B4b it is equal to $\pi_{E+f*\{x\}}[\mathcal{Z}^{\perp}(\Gamma')] = \mathcal{Z}^{\perp}(\Gamma_{(f*\{x\})})$. Since $f^*(x)$ is an elementary cocycle of $\Gamma$, and hence of $\Gamma'$, we may apply Proposition D5 to conclude that $f^*(x)$ is a vertex cocycle of $\Gamma'$. Thus the vertex cocycles of $\Gamma$ correspond bijectively to the vertex cocycles of $\Gamma'$, which implies $\Gamma$ and $\Gamma'$ are system-isomorphic.
