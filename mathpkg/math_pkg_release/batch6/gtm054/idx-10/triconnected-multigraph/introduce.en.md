A multigraph is triconnected if, upon removing any single vertex, the resulting submultigraph remains biconnected. This is a natural strengthening of connectivity: every triconnected multigraph is biconnected and hence connected, has at least three vertices, and satisfies $\dim(\mathcal{Z}^{\perp}(\Gamma)) \geq 2$. Triconnectivity plays a crucial role in Whitney's uniqueness theorem for multigraphs with identical cycle spaces.
