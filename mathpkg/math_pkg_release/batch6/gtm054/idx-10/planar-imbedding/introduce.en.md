A planar imbedding is a combinatorial characterization of what it means for a multigraph to be "drawn in the plane." It consists of a sequence of cycles that span the cycle space and have the property that every edge belongs to exactly two of these cycles. These cycles correspond to the boundaries of the regions in a planar drawing of the multigraph, giving a purely combinatorial definition of planarity without invoking topology.
