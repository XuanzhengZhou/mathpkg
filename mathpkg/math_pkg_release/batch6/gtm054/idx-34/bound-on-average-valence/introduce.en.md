This theorem provides a sharp upper bound on the average valence $\rho$ of a graph in terms of its girth $\gamma$ and imbedding index $\iota$. The bound is expressed through the function $H(\gamma, \iota)$, which takes different forms depending on whether $\iota \leq 2$ or $\iota > 2$. For $\iota > 2$, $H(\gamma, \iota)$ is defined as the least natural number $H_0$ satisfying a certain inequality involving $G(\gamma) = 2\gamma/(\gamma-2)$. This result generalizes both Heawood's inequality (the spherical case) and Ringel's analog for the Euler characteristic, making it the central theorem of the section on cycle covers and the imbedding index.
