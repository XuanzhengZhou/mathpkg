Lemma B21 states that any 3-cycle $Z$ in a graph belonging to the class $G$ must appear as a region (face boundary) in every planar embedding of that graph. This lemma is a standard tool used in the Appel-Haken approach to the Four-Color Theorem, helping to control the structure of separating vertex sets. It connects the combinatorial property of being a 3-cycle with the topological notion of a region in a planar embedding.
