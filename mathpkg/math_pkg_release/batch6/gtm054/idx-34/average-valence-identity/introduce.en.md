The identity $\rho\nu_0 = 2\nu_1$ relates the average valence $\rho$, the number of vertices $\nu_0$, and the number of edges $\nu_1$ in a graph. This is essentially the handshaking lemma expressed in terms of the average degree: the sum of all vertex degrees equals twice the number of edges. This identity is used to convert between vertex-based and edge-based quantities in graph-theoretic inequalities involving the imbedding index.
