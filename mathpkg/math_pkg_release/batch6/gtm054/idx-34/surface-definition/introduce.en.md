A surface (or compact 2-manifold) is defined as a compact, connected topological space that is locally Euclidean of dimension 2 -- every point has a neighborhood homeomorphic to an open disk in the plane. This definition generalizes the sphere and torus and provides the natural setting for studying graph embeddings beyond the planar case. On surfaces other than the sphere, there exist circuits that are not homotopically trivial, making the embedding theory richer and more complex.
