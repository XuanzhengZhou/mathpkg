Since $\Gamma$ contains a 5-critical subgraph on the same vertex set, it is biconnected. By Lemma IIIE21, the edges in the vertex cocycle of $x$ may be denoted by $E_0, E_1, \ldots, E_{\rho(x)-1}$ so that for some triangular imbedding, $E_i$ and $E_{i+1}$ lie on a common region (where subscripts are read modulo $\rho(x)$). Let $E_i = \{x, y_i\}$ for $i = 0, 1, \ldots, \rho(x) - 1$. Since every region is a 3-cycle, $\{y_0, y_1, \ldots, y_{\rho(x)-1}\}$ forms a cycle. Hence $\Gamma_{N(x)}$ is an elementary circuit.
