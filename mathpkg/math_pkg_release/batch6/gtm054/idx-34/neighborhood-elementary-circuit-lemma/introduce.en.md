Lemma B22 asserts that for any graph $\Gamma$ in the class $G$ and any vertex $x$ of $\Gamma$, the subgraph induced by the neighbors $N(x)$ of $x$ forms an elementary circuit (a simple cycle). This structural property is crucial in the Appel-Haken proof of the Four-Color Theorem, as it constrains how 5-valent and 6-valent vertices can be arranged around a given vertex. The lemma relies on the biconnectedness of 5-critical subgraphs and the properties of triangular imbeddings.
