The imbedding index $\iota(\Gamma)$ of a graph $\Gamma$ is a combinatorial invariant defined as the difference between the minimum size $\nu_2(\Gamma)$ of a cycle cover and the dimension of the cycle space $\mathcal{L}(\Gamma)$. It serves as a combinatorial substitute for the genus of a topological embedding, allowing results about graph embeddings on surfaces to be formulated and proved without explicit topological machinery. The main theorem of this section generalizes both Heawood's inequality and Ringel's analog for the Euler characteristic as special cases of a bound involving $\iota(\Gamma)$.
