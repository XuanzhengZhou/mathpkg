Let $a, b \in \mathbb{C}^N$. Prove that if $a$ is a polynomial or if $b_0 = 0$, then $\sum_{j=0}^{\infty} a_j(b(x))^j \in \mathbb{C}^N$.

When $\sum_{j=0}^{\infty} a_j(b(x))^j$ is a formal power series, it is denoted by $a \circ b$ and is called the composition of $a$ by $b$. As in the calculus, composition is associative and a "chain rule" applies.
