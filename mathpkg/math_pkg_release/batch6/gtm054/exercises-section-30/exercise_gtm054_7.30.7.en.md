Prove: if $S \subseteq V$, then $0 \leq \chi_0(\Gamma) - \chi_0(\Gamma_{(S)}) \leq |S|$; if $\mathcal{S} \subseteq \mathcal{E}$ then $0 \leq \chi_1(\Gamma) - \chi_1(\Gamma_{(S)}) \leq |\mathcal{S}|$.

For $m \geq 2$, we say that $\Gamma$ is $m$-critical if $\chi_0(\Gamma) = m$ but $\chi_0(\Theta) < m$ for all proper subgraphs $\Theta$ of $\Gamma$. We call $\Gamma$ a critical graph if $\Gamma$ is $m$-critical for some $m \geq 2$. The complete graph $K_m$ is clearly $m$-critical, and $K_2$ is the only 2-critical graph.
