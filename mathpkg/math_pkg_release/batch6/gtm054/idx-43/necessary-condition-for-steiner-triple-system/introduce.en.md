For a Steiner triple system, the congruence condition $v \equiv 1$ or $3 \pmod{6}$ is necessary. This follows from the identities $v - 1 = 2r$ (so $v$ is odd) and $b = v(v-1)/6$ (so $b$ must be an integer). For $v > 6$, this condition is also sufficient, as first proved by Kirkman in 1847.
