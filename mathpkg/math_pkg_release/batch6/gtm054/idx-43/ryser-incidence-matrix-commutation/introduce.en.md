Ryser's Theorem (1950) states that for a $(; 1, 2)$-design with equal numbers of blocks and vertices ($b = v$) and $\lambda^*(2) < k < v$, the incidence matrix $M$ commutes with its transpose: $MM^* = M^*M$. This is a fundamental result connecting incidence matrix algebra with design theory and leads directly to the corollary that such designs are symmetric block designs.
