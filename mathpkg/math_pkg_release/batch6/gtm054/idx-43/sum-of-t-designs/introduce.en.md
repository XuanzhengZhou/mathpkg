The sum of two $t$-designs with the same blocksize and disjoint block sets is formed by taking the union of their block sets. The incidence function on each block is inherited from the original design. This operation leads naturally to the concepts of the $n$th multiple $n\Lambda$ of a design (obtained by replicating each block $n$ times) and the notions of indecomposable and decomposable designs.
