Ryser's Corollary states that any $(; 1, 2)$-design with $b = v$ and $\lambda^*(2) < k < v$ must be a symmetric block design. The proof uses the commutation theorem $MM^* = M^*M$ together with Exercise C15a to establish that the design is both $(1, 2;)$ and $(; 1, 2)$, hence a $(1, 2; 1, 2)$-design, i.e., a symmetric block design.
