The maximum deficiency $\delta_i(B)$ of a bipartite graph $B$ is the largest value of the deficiency $\delta(U) = |U| - |N(U)|$ over all subsets $U$ of the vertex part $V_i$. This parameter is always nonnegative since $\delta(\emptyset) = 0$. The values $\delta_1(B)$ and $\delta_2(B)$ capture how far the bipartite graph is from satisfying Hall's condition; they are essential to the statement and proof of the Main Matching Theorem.
