An edge-covering set is a subset $S \subseteq V \cup \mathcal{E}$ of the vertices and edges of a graph such that every edge of the graph is incident (in the extended sense) with some element of $S$. Like the vertex-covering property, this property is upward-closed under set inclusion. Edge-covering sets complement the notion of vertex-covering sets in the study of graph covering and matching theory.
