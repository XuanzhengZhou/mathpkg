An independent set in a graph is a subset $S \subseteq V \cup \mathcal{E}$ in which no two distinct elements are incident with one another under the extended incidence relation. This property is hereditary (downward-closed): any subset of an independent set is also independent. The concept unifies the notions of independent vertex sets and independent edge sets (matchings) under a single definition, and is the starting point for defining independence numbers.
