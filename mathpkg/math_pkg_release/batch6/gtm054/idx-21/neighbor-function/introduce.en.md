The neighbor function $N: \mathcal{P}(V) \to \mathcal{P}(V)$ maps each subset $U$ of vertices to the set of all vertices outside $U$ that are adjacent to at least one vertex in $U$. This function is fundamental to the study of matchings in bipartite graphs, as it leads directly to the concept of deficiency $\delta(U) = |U| - |N(U)|$, which is central to Hall's marriage theorem and the Main Matching Theorem.
