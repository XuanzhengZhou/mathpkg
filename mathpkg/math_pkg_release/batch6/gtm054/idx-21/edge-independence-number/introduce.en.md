The edge-independence number $\alpha_1(\Gamma)$ of a graph $\Gamma$ is the maximum size of a matching in $\Gamma$. It is the edge-analogue of the vertex-independence number $\alpha_0(\Gamma)$. Together these two invariants capture the independence structure of a graph from complementary perspectives, and $\alpha_1(\Gamma)$ in particular is central to the theory of matchings in bipartite graphs developed in this chapter.
