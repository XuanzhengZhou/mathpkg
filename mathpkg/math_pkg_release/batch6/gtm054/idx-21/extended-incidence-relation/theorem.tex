Let $(V, \mathcal{E})$ denote a graph. The notion of incidence is extended to a reflexive and symmetric relation on $V \cup \mathcal{E}$ as follows. For $x_1, x_2 \in V$ and $E_1, E_2 \in \mathcal{E}$:

\begin{itemize}
\item $x_1$ is incident with $E_1$ if $x_1 \in E_1$;
\item $x_1$ is incident with $x_2$ if either $x_1 = x_2$ or $\{x_1, x_2\} \in \mathcal{E}$;
\item $E_1$ is incident with $E_2$ if $E_1 \cap E_2 \neq \emptyset$.
\end{itemize}
