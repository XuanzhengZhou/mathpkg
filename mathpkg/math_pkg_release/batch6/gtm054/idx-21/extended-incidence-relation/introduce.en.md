The extended incidence relation generalizes the ordinary incidence relation on a graph to a reflexive and symmetric relation defined on the entire set $V \cup \mathcal{E}$ of vertices and edges. This unified notion allows one to speak of incidence between any two elements—vertex-vertex, vertex-edge, or edge-edge—using a single framework, and serves as the foundation for defining covering sets, independent sets, and matchings in later sections.
