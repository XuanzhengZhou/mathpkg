Woodall's Theorem (A12), proved by D. R. Woodall in 1970, establishes that for a $(;2)$-design with $s^*(E) = 0$, the number of blocks $b$ does not exceed the number of vertices $v$. This is a Fisher-type inequality in the general setting of Graver and Watkins' design theory. The proof uses determinant calculations on an extended incidence matrix.
