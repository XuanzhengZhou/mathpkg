A design is called degenerate if some vertex appears in every block ($\bar{s}(V) > 0$) or some block contains every vertex ($\bar{s}^*(E) > 0$). This notion is symmetric under duality: $\Lambda$ is degenerate exactly when its dual $\Lambda^*$ is degenerate. Degenerate designs are treated separately in the classification of design-types (Section IXB).
