Suppose that $\Lambda$ is a $(t_1, \ldots, t_p; a_1, \ldots, a_q)$-design and let $0 < t_1' < \cdots < t_m' < v$ and $0 < a_1' < \cdots < a_n' < b$. Consider the following two statements:
\begin{itemize}
\item[A1] $\{t_1', \ldots, t_m'\} \subseteq \{t_1, \ldots, t_p\}$ and $\{a_1', \ldots, a_n'\} \subseteq \{a_1, \ldots, a_q\}$.
\item[A2] $\Lambda$ is a $(t_1', \ldots, t_m'; a_1', \ldots, a_n')$-design.
\end{itemize}
Clearly A1 implies A2. Whenever for a design $\Lambda$, A2 implies A1, we say that $(t_1, \ldots, t_p; a_1, \ldots, a_q)$ is the **design-type** of $\Lambda$.
