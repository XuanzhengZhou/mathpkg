A $t$-design is a nondegenerate $(t; 1)$-design — the classic notion of a block design where every $t$-subset of vertices is contained in exactly $\lambda(t)$ blocks. This definition generalizes the familiar concepts of balanced incomplete block designs (BIBDs). An important property is that every $t$-design is also a $t'$-design for all $t' \leq t$, meaning the parameter $t$ determines a hierarchy of regularity.
