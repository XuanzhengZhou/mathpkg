The Berge Conjecture, presented as Theorem D11 in Graver and Watkins, gives four equivalent characterizations of color-perfect graphs. These equivalences link the property of being color-perfect to the presence of clumps and spreads in induced subgraphs, as well as to the color-perfection of the complement graph. The proof is a concise circular argument relying on two lemmas by Lovász: Lemma D9 and Lemma D10.
