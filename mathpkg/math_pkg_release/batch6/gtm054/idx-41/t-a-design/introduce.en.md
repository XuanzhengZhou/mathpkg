A $(t_1, \ldots, t_p; a_1, \ldots, a_q)$-design is the most general notion of a combinatorial design introduced in Chapter IX of Graver and Watkins. It generalizes the classical concept of a $t$-design by allowing the selection function $\bar{s}$ and its dual $\overline{s^*}$ to be constant on specified collections of $t$-subsets of vertices and $a$-subsets of blocks respectively. The parameters capture the regularity with which subsets appear in blocks and blocks intersect in subsets of vertices.
