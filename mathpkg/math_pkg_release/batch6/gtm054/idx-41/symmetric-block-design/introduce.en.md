A symmetric block design is a $t$-design with $t \geq 2$ that is also a $(;2)$-design — equivalently a $(1,2;1,2)$-design. These classical objects are the balanced incomplete block designs (BIBDs) where $\lambda(t)$ equals $\lambda^*(2)$, ensuring maximal symmetry between vertices and blocks. They receive detailed attention in Section IXC.
