Corollary D12 provides an immediate and elegant characterization of crucial graphs as a consequence of the Berge Conjecture (Theorem D11). A graph is crucial precisely when it contains neither a spread nor a clump, yet every proper induced subgraph contains both. This captures the notion of being "minimally imperfect" in the context of spreads and clumps.
