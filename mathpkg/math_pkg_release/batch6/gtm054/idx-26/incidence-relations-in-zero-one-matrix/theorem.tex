Let $M = [m_{ij}]$ be an $r \times s$ $\{0,1\}$-matrix. The following incidence relations are defined:

\begin{itemize}
\item The $i$th row and $j$th column of $M$ are \textbf{incident} if $m_{ij} = 1$.
\item The $i$th and $j$th rows are \textbf{incident} if their inner product satisfies
  $$\sum_{k=1}^{s} m_{ik} m_{jk} > 0,$$
  i.e., $m_{ik} = 1 = m_{jk}$ for some $k \in \{1, \ldots, s\}$.
\item Similarly, two columns are \textbf{incident} if their inner product is positive.
\item The position $(i, j)$ is \textbf{incident} with the $i$th row and with the $j$th column.
\item Two positions on a common line are \textbf{incident}.
\end{itemize}
