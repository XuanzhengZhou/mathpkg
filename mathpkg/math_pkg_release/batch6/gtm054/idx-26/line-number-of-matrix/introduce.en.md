For an $r \times s$ matrix $M$, the total number of lines is the sum of the number of rows and columns, denoted $\pi(M)$. This notation is useful when analyzing $\{0,1\}$-matrices in combinatorial contexts, where counting both rows and columns as "lines" unifies the treatment of incidence relations.
