The cartesian product of a family of sets $\{S_i\}_{i \in I}$ is the set of all choice functions $x \colon I \to \bigcup_{i \in I} S_i$ such that $x(i) \in S_i$ for each $i \in I$. Each element of the product is a function, and its value at an index is called a coordinate. This definition generalizes the familiar finite cartesian product $S_1 \times S_2 \times \cdots \times S_n$ to arbitrary (possibly infinite) index sets $I$. The notation $\prod_{i \in I} S_i$ is standard throughout the book.
