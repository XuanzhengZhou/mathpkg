The standard notation for intervals on the real line uses parentheses for open endpoints and brackets for closed endpoints. The open interval $(a,b)$ excludes both endpoints, the closed interval $[a,b]$ includes both, and the half-open interval $(a,b]$ includes the right endpoint only. These intervals are fundamental building blocks for the topology of $\mathbf{R}$ and serve as the domains for paths and homotopies in algebraic topology. The notation is fixed throughout the book.
