The standard number sets $\mathbf{Z}$ (integers), $\mathbf{Q}$ (rational numbers), $\mathbf{R}$ (real numbers), and $\mathbf{C}$ (complex numbers) are fixed notation throughout the book. These four sets form the foundational number systems upon which the algebraic and topological structures studied in algebraic topology are built. The notation is conventional in modern mathematics, with each set carrying its standard algebraic and topological structure.
