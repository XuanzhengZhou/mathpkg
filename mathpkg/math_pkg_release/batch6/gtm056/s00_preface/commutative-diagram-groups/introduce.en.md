A commutative diagram of groups is a directed graph whose vertices are groups and whose edges are homomorphisms, with the property that any two paths between the same pair of vertices compose to the same homomorphism. The commutativity condition $gf = f'g'$ in a square diagram is the fundamental local condition; larger diagrams are commutative if every constituent face commutes. This notion is central to category theory and algebraic topology, where it is used to express relationships between functors, natural transformations, and universal properties. Note that commutativity of a diagram has nothing to do with whether the groups involved are abelian.
