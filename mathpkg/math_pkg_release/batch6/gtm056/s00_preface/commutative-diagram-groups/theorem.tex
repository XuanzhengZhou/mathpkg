A diagram of groups and homomorphisms, such as

$$
\begin{CD}
A @>{f}>> B \\
@V{f'}VV @VV{g}V \\
C @>>{g'}> D
\end{CD}
$$

is said to be \textbf{commutative} if all possible homomorphisms from one group to another in the diagram are equal. In the above diagram, there are two homomorphisms from group $A$ to group $D$, namely, $gf$ (i.e., $f$ followed by $g$) and $f'g'$. Thus, requiring this diagram to be commutative is equivalent to requiring that $gf = f'g'$.
