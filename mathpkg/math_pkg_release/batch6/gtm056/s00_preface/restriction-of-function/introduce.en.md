The restriction of a function $f \colon B \to C$ to a subset $A \subseteq B$, denoted $f \mid A$, is the function $f \mid A \colon A \to C$ defined by $(f \mid A)(a) = f(a)$ for all $a \in A$. It is the composition $f \circ i$ where $i \colon A \hookrightarrow B$ is the inclusion map. Restriction is a fundamental operation in analysis, topology, and algebra, used whenever one wishes to study the behavior of a function on a smaller domain.
