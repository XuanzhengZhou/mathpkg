Two topological spaces are said to be of the same topological type if there exists a homeomorphism between them -- a continuous bijection with a continuous inverse. This notion captures the idea that two spaces are topologically indistinguishable; they have the same open sets, the same connectivity properties, the same compactness properties, and the same fundamental group. The classification of spaces up to topological type is a central goal of algebraic topology, and this phrase is used throughout the book as a synonym for homeomorphic equivalence.
