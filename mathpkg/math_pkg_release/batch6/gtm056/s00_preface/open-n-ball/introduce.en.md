The open $n$-dimensional ball (or open $n$-disc) is the set of all points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean norm is strictly less than $1$, equipped with the subspace topology. It is homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^n$ itself and serves as the standard model for an open $n$-manifold without boundary. Together with the closed $n$-ball $E^n$ and the $(n-1)$-sphere $S^{n-1}$, it forms a fundamental triple of spaces in algebraic topology.
