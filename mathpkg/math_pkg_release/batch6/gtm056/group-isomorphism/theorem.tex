A homomorphism $\varphi: G \to H$ from one group to another is called an \textbf{isomorphism} if it is both one-to-one (injective) and onto (surjective). Equivalently, $\varphi$ is an isomorphism if there exists a homomorphism $\psi: H \to G$ such that $\psi \circ \varphi = \operatorname{id}_G$ and $\varphi \circ \psi = \operatorname{id}_H$.
