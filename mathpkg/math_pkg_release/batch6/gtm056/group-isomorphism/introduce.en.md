A group isomorphism is a bijective homomorphism between two groups — it is simultaneously an epimorphism (onto) and a monomorphism (one-to-one). Isomorphic groups are algebraically indistinguishable: they have the same group-theoretic properties, the same subgroup lattice, and satisfy the same first-order sentences. The existence of an isomorphism between groups $G$ and $H$ is the formal meaning of "$G$ and $H$ are the same group up to relabeling."
