The closed $n$-dimensional ball $D^n$ is the set of points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean norm is at most 1. It is a compact, contractible topological space whose boundary is the $(n-1)$-dimensional sphere $S^{n-1}$. The closed ball is a fundamental building block in algebraic topology: CW-complexes are constructed by attaching $n$-cells (homeomorphic to $D^n$) along their boundaries, and the ball serves as the domain for homotopies and the definition of the fundamental group.
