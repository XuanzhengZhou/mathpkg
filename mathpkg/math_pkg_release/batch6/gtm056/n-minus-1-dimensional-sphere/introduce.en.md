The $(n-1)$-dimensional sphere $S^{n-1}$ is the set of points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ at unit distance from the origin. It is the boundary of the closed $n$-dimensional ball $D^n$. Spheres are central objects in algebraic topology: the fundamental group of $S^1$ is $\mathbf{Z}$, higher homotopy groups $\pi_n(S^n) \cong \mathbf{Z}$ capture higher-dimensional "holes," and spheres serve as domains for attaching maps in CW-complex constructions.
