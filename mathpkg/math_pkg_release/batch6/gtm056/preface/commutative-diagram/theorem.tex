A diagram of groups and homomorphisms is said to be \textbf{commutative} if all possible homomorphisms from one group to another in the diagram are equal. In a diagram
\[
\begin{CD}
A @>f>> B \\
@Vf'VV @VVgV \\
C @>>g'> D
\end{CD}
\]
commutativity is equivalent to $g \circ f = g' \circ f'$ (all possible paths from one group to another coincide). The requirement that a diagram be commutative has nothing to do with whether any of the groups involved is commutative or abelian.
