A commutative diagram is a diagram of objects and morphisms (here groups and homomorphisms) such that all directed paths with the same start and end point yield equal composite morphisms. This notion is fundamental throughout algebra and topology, encoding equations between composites in a visual form. The concept does not require the groups themselves to be abelian; commutativity refers to the diagram, not the group operation.
