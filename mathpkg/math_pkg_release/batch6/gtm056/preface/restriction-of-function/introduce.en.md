The restriction of a function $f : B \to C$ to a subset $A \subseteq B$ is the function $f|_A : A \to C$ that agrees with $f$ on $A$. Restriction is a fundamental operation that allows one to study the behavior of a function on a smaller domain. In topology, restrictions of continuous maps to subspaces preserve continuity, making them essential for local analysis.
