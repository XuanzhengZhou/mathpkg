The cartesian product of a family of sets, indexed by an arbitrary set $I$, is the collection of all choice functions that select one element from each set $S_i$. This generalizes the familiar finite product $S_1 \times S_2 \times \cdots \times S_n$ to arbitrary (possibly infinite) index sets. Each element of the product is determined by its "coordinates," one per index.
