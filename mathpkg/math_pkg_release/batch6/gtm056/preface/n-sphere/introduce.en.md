The $(n-1)$-dimensional sphere $S^{n-1}$ is the set of all points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean norm equals exactly 1. It is the boundary of the closed $n$-ball and a central object in algebraic topology. The fundamental group of $S^1$ is $\mathbf{Z}$, while higher spheres $S^n$ for $n \geq 2$ are simply connected but have nontrivial higher homotopy groups.
