The open interval $(a, b)$ consists of all real numbers strictly between $a$ and $b$, excluding the endpoints. In the standard topology on $\mathbf{R}$, open intervals form a basis for the topology and are homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}$ itself. Open intervals are fundamental in analysis and topology, serving as the building blocks for defining open sets, continuity, and limits on the real line.
