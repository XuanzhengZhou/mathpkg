A diagram of groups and homomorphisms

$$\begin{CD}
A @>f>> B \\
@Vf'VV @VVgV \\
C @>>g'> D
\end{CD}$$

is said to be \text{commutative} if all possible homomorphisms from one group to another in the diagram are equal. In the above diagram, there are two homomorphisms from group $A$ to group $D$, namely, $gf$ (i.e., $f$ followed by $g$) and $f'g'$. Thus, requiring that this diagram be commutative is equivalent to requiring that $gf = f'g'$.

\text{Note:} The requirement that a diagram be commutative has nothing to do with whether any of the groups involved is commutative or abelian. For example, the above diagram could be commutative even if $A, B, C$, and $D$ were non-abelian groups.
