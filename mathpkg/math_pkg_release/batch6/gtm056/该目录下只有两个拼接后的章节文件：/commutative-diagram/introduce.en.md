A commutative diagram is a diagram of objects (such as groups) and morphisms (such as homomorphisms) in which all directed paths with the same start and end points lead to the same result by composition. In the context of group theory, a commutative diagram of groups and homomorphisms requires that any two sequences of homomorphisms connecting the same source and target groups yield equal composite homomorphisms. This notion is independent of whether the groups themselves are abelian; commutativity of the diagram refers to the equality of compositions, not to the commutativity of the group operations.
