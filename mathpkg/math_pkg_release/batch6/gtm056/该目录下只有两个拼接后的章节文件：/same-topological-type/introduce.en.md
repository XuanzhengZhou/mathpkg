Two topological spaces are of the same topological type if there exists a homeomorphism between them -- a continuous bijection with a continuous inverse. This is the fundamental notion of equivalence in topology: homeomorphic spaces are indistinguishable from the perspective of topological properties alone. In algebraic topology, spaces of the same topological type have isomorphic fundamental groups, homology groups, and cohomology rings, making this concept central to the classification of topological spaces.
