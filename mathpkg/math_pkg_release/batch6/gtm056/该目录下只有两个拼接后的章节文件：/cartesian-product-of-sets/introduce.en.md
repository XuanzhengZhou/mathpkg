The cartesian product of a family of sets $\{S_i\}_{i \in I}$ indexed by a set $I$ is the set of all functions $x$ from $I$ to the union of the $S_i$ such that $x(i) \in S_i$ for each $i \in I$. Each such function is called an element of the product, and the value $x_i = x(i)$ is the coordinate of $x$ at index $i$. This generalizes the familiar finite cartesian product $S_1 \times S_2 \times \cdots \times S_n$ to arbitrary (possibly infinite) index sets.
