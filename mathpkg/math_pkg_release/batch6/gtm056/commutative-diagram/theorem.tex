A diagram of groups and homomorphisms is said to be \textbf{commutative} if all possible homomorphisms from one group to another in the diagram are equal. That is, for any two groups $A$ and $B$ in the diagram, any two composite homomorphisms $A \to B$ obtained by following directed paths in the diagram coincide.

For example, in the diagram
\[
\begin{CD}
A @>f>> B \\
@VVgV @VVhV \\
C @>k>> D
\end{CD}
\]
commutativity means $h \circ f = k \circ g$. Note that commutativity of a diagram has nothing to do with whether any of the groups involved is commutative (abelian).
