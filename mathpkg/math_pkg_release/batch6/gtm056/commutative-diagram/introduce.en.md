A commutative diagram is a diagram of objects (here, groups) and morphisms (here, group homomorphisms) such that all directed paths between any two objects yield the same composite morphism. Commutative diagrams are a fundamental notational and conceptual tool throughout modern mathematics, particularly in algebraic topology and category theory. The concept originates from the need to concisely express equality of composite maps without writing out long chains of equalities.
