The cartesian product of an arbitrary family of sets generalizes the familiar finite cartesian product $S_1 \times S_2 \times \cdots \times S_n$. Instead of ordered $n$-tuples, elements of the general product are choice functions: for each index $i$, an element is selected from the corresponding set $S_i$. This formulation does not require the Axiom of Choice to define the product set itself, though the Axiom of Choice is equivalent to the statement that the product of a nonempty family of nonempty sets is nonempty.
