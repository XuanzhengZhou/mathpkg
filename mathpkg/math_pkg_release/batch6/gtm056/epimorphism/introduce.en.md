An epimorphism is a surjective group homomorphism — a structure-preserving map between groups that covers the entire target group. In category-theoretic language, epimorphisms are the natural notion of "onto" maps. While in the category of groups every epimorphism in the categorical sense is a surjective homomorphism (and vice versa), the terminology in this book follows the classical usage: an epimorphism is simply a homomorphism that is onto.
