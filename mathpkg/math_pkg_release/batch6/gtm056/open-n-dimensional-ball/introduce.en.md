The open $n$-dimensional ball is the set of points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean norm is strictly less than 1. It is homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^n$ itself and serves as the standard model for an $n$-dimensional manifold chart. In algebraic topology, the open ball appears as the interior of the closed ball $D^n$ and is essential in the definition of CW-complexes: the interior of each $n$-cell is homeomorphic to an open $n$-ball.
