"Topological type" is synonymous with homeomorphism class — two spaces have the same topological type if they are homeomorphic. This terminology reflects the perspective of topology as the study of properties invariant under homeomorphism. In algebraic topology, a central goal is to classify spaces up to topological type (or up to homotopy type) using algebraic invariants such as the fundamental group, homology groups, and cohomology rings.
