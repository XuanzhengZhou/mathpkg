Let $K$ be the consequence of $\{[g_i, g_j]\}$. Clearly $K \subset [G,G]$. For the converse, define $l(g)$ as the minimum number of generators needed to express $g$. By induction on $l(u) + l(v)$, one shows that any commutator $[u,v]$ lies in $K$. Base case: $l(u) = l(v) = 1$ gives $[g_i^{\pm 1}, g_j^{\pm 1}]$, which is a conjugate of $[g_i, g_j]^{\pm 1}$ or its inverse, hence in $K$. The inductive step uses commutator identities to reduce the length.
