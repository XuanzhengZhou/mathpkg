By Theorem (3.2), the $k$th knot polynomial $\Delta_k$ is a GCD of all $(n-k) \times (n-k)$ subdeterminants of the Alexander matrix, meaning $(\Delta_k)$ is the smallest principal ideal containing $E_k$. Since $E_k \subset (\Delta_k)$ by definition, and $E_{k+1} \supset E_k$ (ascending chain), the principal ideal $(\Delta_{k+1})$ containing $E_{k+1}$ also contains $E_k$. By minimality of $(\Delta_k)$, we have $(\Delta_k) \subset (\Delta_{k+1})$, hence $\Delta_{k+1} \mid \Delta_k$ (Theorem 3.3). If $E_k$ is principal, then $E_k = (\Delta_k)$ (Theorem 3.2).
