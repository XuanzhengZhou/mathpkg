A \text{group presentation} is denoted $(\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r})$, where $\mathbf{x}$ is a subset of an underlying set of generators and $\mathbf{r}$ is a subset of the free group $F(\mathbf{x})$. The \text{group defined by} $(\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r})$ is $|\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}| = F(\mathbf{x})/R$, where $R$ is the \text{consequence} of $\mathbf{r}$ (the smallest normal subgroup containing $\mathbf{r}$). An element is a consequence of $\mathbf{r}$ iff it is of the form $\prod_{j=1}^{l} h_j r_{i(j)}^{n(j)} h_j^{-1}$.
