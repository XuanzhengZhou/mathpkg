Let $a$ be a unit and $b$ its inverse, so $ab = 1$. Define $\rho(a) = a t^{-\mu(a)}$, which maps $JH$ into the polynomial ring $J[t]$ (associating each element with an associate having no negative powers). Then $\rho(ab) = \rho(a)\rho(b) = \rho(1) = 1$. Since the only units in $J[t]$ are $\pm 1$, we have $\rho(a) = \pm 1$, hence $a = \pm t^{\mu(a)}$.
