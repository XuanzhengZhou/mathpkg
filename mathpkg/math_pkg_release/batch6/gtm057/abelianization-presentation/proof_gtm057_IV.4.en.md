Let $G = |\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}|$ with generators $x_1, \dots, x_n$. The abelianized group $G/[G,G]$ is obtained by adding the commutator relations $[x_i, x_j] = 1$ for all $i,j$. By Theorem (4.5), the commutator subgroup $[G,G]$ is the consequence of all commutators of generators. Thus

$$G/[G,G] = |x_1, \dots, x_n : \mathbf{r}, [x_i, x_j] \;(i,j = 1, \dots, n)|.$$

Since all generators now commute, the presentation reduces to that of an abelian group with relations $\mathbf{r}$ expressed additively.
