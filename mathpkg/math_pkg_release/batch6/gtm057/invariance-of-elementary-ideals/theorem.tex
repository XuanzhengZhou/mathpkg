If $f: (\mathbf{x}:\mathbf{r}) \to (\mathbf{y}:\mathbf{s})$ is a presentation equivalence, then the induced isomorphism $f_{**}$ of abelianized groups maps the $k$th elementary ideal of $(\mathbf{x}:\mathbf{r})$ onto that of $(\mathbf{y}:\mathbf{s})$. Consequently, the elementary ideals are invariants of group presentation type.
