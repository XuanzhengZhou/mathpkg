A \textit{presentation equivalence} is a pair of presentation maps $f: (\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}) \to (\mathbf{y} : \mathbf{s})$ and $g: (\mathbf{y} : \mathbf{s}) \to (\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r})$ such that $gf \simeq 1$ and $fg \simeq 1$. Two presentations are of the \textit{same type} if there exists a presentation equivalence between them.
