The definition of greatest common divisor in a general commutative ring extends the familiar concept from integers. The knot polynomials are defined as g.c.d.'s of determinants of submatrices of the Alexander matrix. The characterization of a g.c.d. via principal ideals (the smallest principal ideal containing the given elements) is essential for establishing the relationship between elementary ideals and knot polynomials.
