Theorem 3.8 shows that for knot groups, the first elementary ideal is always principal, generated by the Alexander polynomial. This follows from the fact that in an over presentation with $n$ generators and $n-1$ relators, all generators map to the same element in the abelianization, so one column of the Alexander matrix can be replaced by zeros, leaving only one non-trivial $(n-1)\times(n-1)$ submatrix.
