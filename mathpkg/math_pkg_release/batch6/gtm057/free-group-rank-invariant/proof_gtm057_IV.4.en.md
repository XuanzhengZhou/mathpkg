Consider free groups $F_m$ and $F_n$ of rank $m$ and $n$. If they are isomorphic, then by the word subgroup theorem, $F_m/F_m^2 \cong F_n/F_n^2$. The elements of $F_m/F_m^2$ are products $x_1^{\delta_1} \cdots x_m^{\delta_m}$ with $\delta_i \in \{0,1\}$. For finite $m$, this group has $2^m$ elements; for infinite $m$, it has $m$ elements. Equality of cardinalities forces $m=n$.
