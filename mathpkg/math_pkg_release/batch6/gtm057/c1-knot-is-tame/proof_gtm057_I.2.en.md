A proof is given in Appendix I. The assumptions on $K$ are that it is rectifiable and given as the image of a vector-valued function $p(s) = (x(s), y(s), z(s))$ of arc length $s$ with continuous first derivatives. The proof is complicated but straightforward and uses nothing beyond the standard techniques of advanced calculus.
