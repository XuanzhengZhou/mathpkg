For the trivial knot (the unknot), a regular projection with a single overpass and a single underpass can be chosen (Figure 40 of Chapter VI). The over presentation has $n = 1$ generator $x_1$ corresponding to the single overpass. Since there is only one underpass, there is exactly one relator $r_1$. However, by Theorem (1.3) of Chapter VI, any one relator in an over presentation is a consequence of the others, so for $n = 1$ the relator $r_1$ must be a consequence of the empty set — i.e., $r_1 = 1$ in the free group.

Thus the over presentation reduces to $(x :)$, which presents the infinite cyclic group. Hence $\pi(R^3 - K) \cong \mathbb{Z}$ for the trivial knot. The generators $\phi x_1$ generate the knot group, and the abelianized group is also infinite cyclic. This computation verifies that the fundamental group of the complement of the unknotted circle is indeed the free group on one generator.
