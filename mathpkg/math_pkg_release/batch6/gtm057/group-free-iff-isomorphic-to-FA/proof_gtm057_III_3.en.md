If $G$ is free with basis $E$, let $\mathcal{A}$ have the same cardinality and map $[\mathcal{A}]$ bijectively to $E$. This extends to a homomorphism $F[\mathcal{A}] \to G$ which is an isomorphism because the universal property provides an inverse. Conversely, $[\mathcal{A}]$ is a free basis of $F[\mathcal{A}]$ by construction.
