Given presentations $G_1 = |\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}|_{\phi_1}$, $G_2 = |\mathbf{y} : \mathbf{s}|_{\phi_2}$, $G_0 = |\mathbf{z} : \mathbf{t}|_{\phi_0}$, lift the inclusion-induced homomorphisms $\theta_1, \theta_2$ to free groups: $\bar{\theta}_i: F(\mathbf{z}) \to F_i$. Let $F$ be free on $\mathbf{x} \cup \mathbf{y}$ (with $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}$ disjoint). Define $\phi: F \to G$ by $\phi|F_i = \omega_i \phi_i$. The van Kampen theorem gives that the kernel of $\phi$ is the consequence of $\mathbf{r} \cup \mathbf{s} \cup \{\bar{\theta}_1 z_k \bar{\theta}_2 z_k^{-1}\}$. Hence

$$G = |\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} : \mathbf{r}, \mathbf{s}, \{\bar{\theta}_1 z_k \bar{\theta}_2 z_k^{-1}\}|_\phi.$$

This is Theorem (3.6) — the alternative formulation of the van Kampen theorem in terms of group presentations.
