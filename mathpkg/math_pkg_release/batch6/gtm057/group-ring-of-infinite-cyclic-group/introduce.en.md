The group ring of an infinite cyclic group is the ring of Laurent polynomials $J[t, t^{-1}]$. Its algebraic properties — being an integral domain, having only trivial units $\pm t^n$, and being a unique factorization domain — are essential for defining and computing knot polynomials. The function $\mu(a)$ gives the lowest degree of a Laurent polynomial, satisfying $\mu(ab) = \mu(a) + \mu(b)$. These properties extend to group rings of finitely generated free abelian groups.
