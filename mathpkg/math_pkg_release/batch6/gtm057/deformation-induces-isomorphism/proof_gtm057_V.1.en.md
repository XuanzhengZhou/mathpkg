Let $a(t) = h_t(p)$ be the path traced by the basepoint. For any loop $b$ based at $p$, define three families:
$k_s(t) = h_s(b(t))$,
$j_s(t) = a(1 - (t-s))$ for $s \leq t \leq 1$, and
$l_s(t) = a(s+t)$ for $s+t \leq 1$.
The product family $\{j_s \cdot k_s \cdot l_s\}$ is a fixed-endpoint family showing $a^{-1} \cdot b \cdot a \simeq c \cdot h_1 b \cdot c$ where $c$ is constant. Thus $[a^{-1} \cdot b \cdot a] = [h_1 b] = (h_1)_*[b]$, proving $(h_1)_*(\beta) = \alpha^{-1} \cdot \beta \cdot \alpha$, which is an isomorphism.
