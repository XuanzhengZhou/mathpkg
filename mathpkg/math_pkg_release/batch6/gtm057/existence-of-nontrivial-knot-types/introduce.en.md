The nontriviality of the trefoil is proved by showing its knot group $|x,y : xyx = yxy|$ admits a surjective homomorphism onto the symmetric group $S_3$ (via $x \mapsto (12)$, $y \mapsto (23)$), hence is nonabelian, while the trivial knot group is infinite cyclic. This is the first rigorous proof that distinct knot types exist.
