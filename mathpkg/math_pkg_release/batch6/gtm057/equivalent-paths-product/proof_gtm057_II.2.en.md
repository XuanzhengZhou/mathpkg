If $\{h_s\}$ and $\{k_s\}$ are the fixed-endpoint families exhibiting the equivalences $a \simeq a'$ and $b \simeq b'$ respectively, then the collection of paths $\{h_s \cdot k_s\}$ is a fixed-endpoint family which gives $a \cdot b \simeq a' \cdot b'$.

We observe that the products $h_s \cdot k_s$ are defined for every $s$ because
$$h_s(\|h_s\|) = h_0(\|h_0\|) = a(\|a\|) = b(0) = k_0(0) = k_s(0).$$
In particular, $a' \cdot b' = h_1 \cdot k_1$ is defined. The family $\{h_s \cdot k_s\}$ satisfies the fixed-endpoint condition and simultaneous continuity requirements.
