For a group presentation $(\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r})$, the \textit{Alexander matrix} $A = \|a_{ij}\|$ is defined by
$$a_{ij} = \alpha \gamma \left( \frac{\partial r_i}{\partial x_j} \right),$$
where $\partial/\partial x_j$ is the free derivative in $JF$, $\gamma: JF \to J|\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}|$ is induced by the canonical homomorphism, and $\alpha$ is the abelianizer to $JH$ where $H = |\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}| / [|\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}|, |\mathbf{x} : \mathbf{r}|]$.
