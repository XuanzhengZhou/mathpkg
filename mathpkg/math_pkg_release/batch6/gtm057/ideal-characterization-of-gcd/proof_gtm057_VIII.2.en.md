If smallest principal ideal $D$ containing $\{a_i\}$ exists and $d$ generates it, then $a_i \in (d)$ so $d \mid a_i$. For any $e$ with $e \mid a_i$, the ideal $(e)$ contains all $a_i$, so $(d) \subset (e)$, hence $e \mid d$. Conversely, if $d$ is a g.c.d., then $(d)$ is the smallest principal ideal containing $\{a_i\}$.
