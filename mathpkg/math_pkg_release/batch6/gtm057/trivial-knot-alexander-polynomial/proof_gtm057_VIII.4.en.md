For the trivial knot, the group is infinite cyclic with the presentation $\pi(R^3 - K) = |x :|$. Rather than work with a matrix with one column and no rows, observe that $(x :)$ is of the same presentation type as $(x : 1)$. The single relator is $r = 1$, whose derivative with respect to $x$ is $\partial 1/\partial x = 0$. Hence the Alexander matrix is $A = \| 0 \|$, a $1 \times 1$ zero matrix.

For $k \geq 1$, the ideal $E_k$ is generated by determinants of $0 \times 0$ submatrices (for $n - k \leq 0$), which are defined to be the whole ring. Therefore $\Delta_k = 1$ for all $k \geq 1$. Equivalently, the $0$th elementary ideal is the zero ideal, and all higher ideals are the unit ideal, giving trivial knot polynomials.
