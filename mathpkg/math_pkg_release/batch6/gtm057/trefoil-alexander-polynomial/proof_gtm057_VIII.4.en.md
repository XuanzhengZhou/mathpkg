For the clover-leaf (trefoil) knot, we use the simplified presentation $\pi(R^3 - K) = |x, y : xyx = yxy|$ obtained in Chapter VI, Example (4.2). The single relator is $r = xyx(yxy)^{-1} = xyx y^{-1} x^{-1} y^{-1}$. Computing the free derivatives:

$$\frac{\partial r}{\partial x} = 1 + xy - y, \quad \frac{\partial r}{\partial y} = x - 1 - yx.$$

Applying the abelianizer $\alpha\gamma$, which sends both generators to $t$, yields the $1 \times 2$ Alexander matrix

$$A = \| 1 - t + t^2 \quad -1 + t - t^2 \|.$$

Since any $(n-1) \times (n-1)$ submatrix is just one of the two entries (up to sign), the first elementary ideal is generated by $1 - t + t^2$. Normalizing (no negative powers, positive constant term), we obtain the Alexander polynomial

$$\Delta_1(t) = t^2 - t + 1,$$

and $\Delta_k = 1$ for $k \geq 2$.
