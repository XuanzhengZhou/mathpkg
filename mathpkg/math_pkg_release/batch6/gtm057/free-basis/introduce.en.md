A free basis is a generating set that satisfies the universal mapping property: any function from the basis to any group extends uniquely to a homomorphism. This is the categorical definition of freeness. The trivial group is free with empty basis. A group is free if and only if it is isomorphic to $F[\mathcal{A}]$ for some alphabet $\mathcal{A}$.
