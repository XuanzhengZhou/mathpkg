Let $W \subset F(\mathbf{x})$ be a set of words defining a word subgroup. For a homomorphism $\phi: G \to G'$, consider $\phi_*: JG \to JG'$ (the ring extension). By definition, $W(G)$ is generated by $\omega(w)$ for all $w \in W$ and all homomorphisms $\omega: F(\mathbf{x}) \to G$. For any such $\omega$, the composition $\phi \omega: F(\mathbf{x}) \to G'$ generates $\phi(\omega(w)) \in W(G')$. Thus $\phi(W(G)) \subset W(G')$. For onto $\phi$, the reverse inclusion holds by taking preimages of generating homomorphisms.
