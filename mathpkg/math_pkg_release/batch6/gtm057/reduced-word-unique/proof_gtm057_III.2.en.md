The proof shows that if $u$ and $v$ differ by one elementary operation, then their standard reductions agree. For elementary contraction of type I (removing $a^0$) and type II (merging $a^p a^q$), one shows that $\rho^{k+1}(u) = \rho^k(v)$ or $\rho^{k+2}(u) = \rho^{k+1}(v)$ where $k$ is the reduction length of the prefix. By induction on the number of elementary operations, equivalent words have the same reduced form.
