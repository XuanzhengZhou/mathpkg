Let $G$ be a knot group with an over presentation $(x_1, \dots, x_n : r_1, \dots, r_n)_\phi$. For each relator $r_i$, the exponent sum with respect to $x_j$ is the image of $\partial r_i/\partial x_j$ under the trivializer $t: JF \to J$. From the geometry of the Wirtinger presentation, for each underpass $B_i$ adjacent to overpasses $A_{\kappa(i)}$ and $A_{\lambda(i)}$, the exponent sums of $r_i$ satisfy:

$$t\left(\frac{\partial r_i}{\partial x_j}\right) = \delta_{j,\kappa(i)} - \delta_{j,\lambda(i)}.$$

Thus each $r_i$ has total exponent sum zero, i.e., $t(\partial r_i/\partial x_j)$ sums to $0$ over $j$. It follows that if $\theta$ is any abelian homomorphic image of $G$ and $t_j = \theta\phi x_j$, then $t_j = t_k$ for all $j,k$, so $\theta G$ is generated by a single element $t$.

To show the abelianized group is infinite cyclic, define $\zeta: F \to (t)$ by $\zeta x_j = t$, where $(t)$ is infinite cyclic. Since $\zeta r_i = t^{s_i}$ with $s_i = \sum_j t(\partial r_i/\partial x_j) = 0$, each relator maps to 1. Hence $\zeta$ factors through $G$, giving an epimorphism $G \to (t)$. By Theorem (4.4) of Chapter IV, this factors through the abelianizer, yielding $G/[G,G] \to (t)$. Since every abelian image of $G$ is cyclic, $G/[G,G]$ is cyclic. The existence of an epimorphism onto an infinite group forces $G/[G,G]$ to be infinite cyclic.
