Theorem 1.1 (Chapter VIII) is a fundamental structural result: the abelianization of any knot group is always infinite cyclic. This is crucial for the construction of the knot polynomials, as it means the Alexander matrix has entries in the group ring of an infinite cyclic group, i.e., Laurent polynomials $J[t,t^{-1}]$. The proof uses the Wirtinger presentation, where each relation involves $x_{i+1}$ and $x_i$, making all generators equal in the abelianization.
