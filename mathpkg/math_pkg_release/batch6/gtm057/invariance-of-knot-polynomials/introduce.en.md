Theorem 3.4 establishes that the knot polynomials are invariants of knot type. The proof goes: equivalent knots have isomorphic groups, isomorphic groups have presentation-equivalent presentations, and the polynomials are invariant under presentation equivalence. This means that if two knots have different normalized knot polynomials, they are distinct knot types.
