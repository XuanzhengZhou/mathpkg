Let $(x_1, \ldots, x_n : r_1, \ldots, r_n)_\phi$ be an over presentation of $G$. From a typical crossing, the $\kappa(i)$th and $\lambda(i)$th exponent sums of $r_i$ are $+1$ and $-1$ respectively, while all other exponent sums are 0. Hence the sum of all exponent sums of each $r_i$ is 0.

Define $\zeta: F \to (t)$ by $\zeta x_j = t$ for all $j$. Then $\zeta r_i = t^0 = 1$, so the consequence of the relators lies in $\ker \zeta$. Thus $\zeta$ induces a homomorphism $\theta: G \to (t)$ onto the infinite cyclic group. The abelianizer factors through this, forcing $G/[G,G] \cong (t)$.
