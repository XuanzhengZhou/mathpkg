Theorem (3.5): For any knot group, $E_0 = \Delta_0 = 0$. In any over presentation, one relator is a consequence of the others, so the Alexander matrix is $(n-1) \times n$. For $k=0$, we consider determinants of $n \times n$ submatrices. But the matrix has only $n-1$ rows, so $n - 0 > n-1$ (i.e., $n > m$), giving $E_0 = 0$ by definition. Consequently $\Delta_0 = 0$. This reflects the fact that the abelianized group of a knot group is infinite (hence the 0th ideal is trivial, cf. (4.1) of Chapter VII).
