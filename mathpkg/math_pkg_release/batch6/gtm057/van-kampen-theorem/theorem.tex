Let $X = X_1 \cup X_2$ where $X_1, X_2$, and $X_0 = X_1 \cap X_2$ are open, pathwise connected, and nonvoid. Let $G = \pi(X,p)$, $G_i = \pi(X_i,p)$, and $\theta_i: G_0 \to G_i$, $\omega_i: G_i \to G$ be induced by inclusions. Then the images $\omega_i G_i$ generate $G$, and the kernel of the homomorphism from the free product $G_1 * G_2$ onto $G$ is the consequence of $\{\theta_1(g) \theta_2(g)^{-1} : g \in G_0\}$.
