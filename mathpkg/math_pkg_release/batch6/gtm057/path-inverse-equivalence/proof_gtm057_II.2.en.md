The paths $e_1$ and $e_2$ are the identities corresponding to the initial and terminal points of $a$. Consider the family $\{h_s\}$ defined by a deformation that gradually shrinks the loop $a \cdot a^{-1}$ to the constant path. For $s=0$ we obtain $e_1$, and for $s=1$:
$$h_1(t) = \begin{cases} a(t), & 0 \leq t \leq \|a\|, \\ a(2\|a\| - t), & \|a\| \leq t \leq 2\|a\| \end{cases} = (a \cdot a^{-1})(t).$$
This exhibits $a \cdot a^{-1} \simeq e_1$. The other equivalence follows by symmetry or by applying the first result to $a^{-1}$.
