The proof uses the van Kampen theorem repeatedly. First, $R^3 - K_*$ (where $K_*$ includes a square $S$ below the knot) is shown to be simply-connected. Then adjoining $K_* - B_*$ (disjoint union of $n$ open topological discs) yields $R^3 - B_*$, whose fundamental group is free of rank $n$. A final application of the van Kampen theorem fills in $B_* - K$ and yields that the kernel of $\phi$ is the consequence of any $n-1$ of $r_1, \ldots, r_n$.
