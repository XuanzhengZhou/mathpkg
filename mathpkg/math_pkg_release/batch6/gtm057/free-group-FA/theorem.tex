Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a set (an \text{alphabet}). A \text{syllable} is $a^n$ for $a \in \mathcal{A}$, $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. A \text{word} is a finite sequence of syllables. $W(\mathcal{A})$ is the set of all words with multiplication by concatenation. Words are \text{equivalent} ($u \sim v$) if one can be obtained from the other by elementary contractions/expansions (removing $a^0$, combining $a^p a^q$ to $a^{p+q}$). $F[\mathcal{A}] = W(\mathcal{A})/{\sim}$ is the \text{free group} on $\mathcal{A}$.
