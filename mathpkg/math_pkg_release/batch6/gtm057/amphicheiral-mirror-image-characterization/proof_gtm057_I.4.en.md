The mirror image $\mathcal{R}K$ of $K$ is defined by $\mathcal{R}(x,y,z) = (x,y,-z)$. If $h$ is an orientation reversing homeomorphism such that $hK = K$, then $h \circ \mathcal{R}$ is orientation preserving and maps $K$ onto $\mathcal{R}K$. Conversely, if there exists an orientation preserving $g$ with $gK = \mathcal{R}K$, then $\mathcal{R} \circ g$ is orientation reversing and maps $K$ onto $K$.
