Assume free groups $F_m$ and $F_n$ are isomorphic. Then by properties of word subgroups, $F_m/F_m^2 \cong F_n/F_n^2$. Since $F_m/F_m^2$ is abelian, its elements are products $x_1^{\delta_1} \cdots x_m^{\delta_m}$ with $\delta_i \in \{0,1\}$. For finite $m$, $|F_m/F_m^2| = 2^m$; for infinite $m$, $|F_m/F_m^2| = m$. Thus $m = n$.
