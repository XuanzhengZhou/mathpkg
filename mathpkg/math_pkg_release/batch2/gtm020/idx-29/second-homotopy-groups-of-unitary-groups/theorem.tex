$$\pi_2(U_F(n)) = \pi_2(SU_F(n)) = 0 \quad \text{for all } n \geq 1,$$
where $F = \mathbf{C}$ (unitary case) or $F = \mathbf{C}$ with determinant 1 (special unitary case). In particular:
$$\pi_2(U(n)) = \pi_2(SU(n)) = 0,$$
and by the quaternionic identification $Sp(1) = SU(2)$, also $\pi_2(Sp(n)) = 0$ for $1 \leq n \leq +\infty$.
