Stable equivalence, or $s$-equivalence, is the fundamental equivalence relation used in topological K-theory. Two vector bundles are stably equivalent if they become isomorphic after adding trivial bundles of possibly different dimensions. This relation, together with Whitney sum and tensor product operations, endows the set of stable equivalence classes over a finite-dimensional CW-complex with a ring structure, known as the reduced K-ring $\tilde{K}(X)$. Chapter 9 studies the relationship between ordinary isomorphism and stable equivalence of vector bundles.
