Two vector bundles $\xi$ and $\eta$ over the same base space are called \textit{$s$-equivalent} (or \textit{stably equivalent}) provided there exist nonnegative integers $n$ and $m$ such that
$$\xi \oplus \theta^n \cong \eta \oplus \theta^m,$$
where $\theta^m$ denotes the $m$-dimensional trivial vector bundle. Stable equivalence is an equivalence relation, and the stable equivalence classes form a ring (over finite-dimensional CW-complexes), with $\oplus$ inducing the addition operation and $\otimes$ inducing the multiplication operation. These rings are the $\tilde{K}$-rings of the base space.
