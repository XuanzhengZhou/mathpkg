This theorem is the natural generalization to vector bundles of the basis theorem for vector spaces. It states that any $k$-dimensional vector bundle over an $n$-dimensional CW-complex is stably equivalent to a bundle whose dimension is bounded by a function of $n$ that depends on the field $F$. For real bundles, every bundle reduces to dimension at most $n$ after adding trivial bundles; for complex bundles, the bound is approximately $n/2$; for quaternionic bundles, approximately $n/4$. The proof is by repeated application of Proposition 1.1.
