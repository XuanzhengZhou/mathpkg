Given two monomorphisms $u, v: \theta^1 \to \xi^k$ with $n \leq ck - 2$, both determine everywhere-nonzero cross sections $s_u$ and $s_v$ of $\xi_0$, the bundle of nonzero vectors. The fibre $F^k \setminus \{0\}$ is $(ck-2)$-connected, and since $n \leq ck-2$, the space of cross sections of $\xi_0$ is path-connected.

Thus $s_u$ and $s_v$ are homotopic as cross sections of $\xi_0$. A homotopy between nowhere-zero cross sections induces an isomorphism between their cokernel bundles. More concretely, the homotopy provides a family of monomorphisms interpolating between $u$ and $v$, and the corresponding family of cokernels forms a vector bundle over $X \times I$. By the homotopy invariance of vector bundles over paracompact spaces, the cokernels at the two ends are isomorphic:
$$\operatorname{coker}(u) \cong \operatorname{coker}(v).$$
