For complex Stiefel manifolds, the first non-vanishing homotopy group occurs in dimension $2(n-k)+1$ and is always infinite cyclic. The base case follows from $V_1(\mathbf{C}^n) = S^{2n-1}$ whose first non-trivial homotopy group is $\pi_{2n-1}(S^{2n-1}) = \mathbf{Z}$, and the result extends to higher $k$ via the stability isomorphism of Proposition 11.1 with $c=2$.
