Define maps $r, s: S^3 \to SO(4)$ by
$$r(x)y = xyx^{-1}, \quad s(x)y = xy$$
for $x \in S^3 \subset \mathbf{H}$ and $y \in \mathbf{R}^4 = \mathbf{H}$.
There is a commutative diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
S^3 & \xrightarrow{r} & SO(4) \\
{\scriptstyle f}\downarrow & & \downarrow{\scriptstyle g} \\
\mathbf{R}P^3 & \xrightarrow{r'} & SO(3)
\end{array}$$
where $r'$ is a homeomorphism, $f$ is the natural projection, and $g$ is the natural inclusion given by $g(u)1 = 1 \in \mathbf{H} = \mathbf{R}^4$. The map $r'$ is uniquely determined by this diagram.
