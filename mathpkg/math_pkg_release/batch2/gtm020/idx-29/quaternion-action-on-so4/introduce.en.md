This proposition provides the quaternionic description of the classical maps relating $S^3$ and the special orthogonal groups in low dimensions. The map $r$ is conjugation by unit quaternions, giving a double cover $S^3 \to SO(3)$ whose quotient by the antipodal map yields the homeomorphism $\mathbf{R}P^3 \cong SO(3)$ established in 12.3. The map $s$ is left multiplication, embedding $S^3$ into $SO(4)$. Together these maps are fundamental for computing $\pi_3$ of orthogonal groups.
