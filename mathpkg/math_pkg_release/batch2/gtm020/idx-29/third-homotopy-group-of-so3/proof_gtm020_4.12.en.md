From Proposition 12.7, the map $r': \mathbf{R}P^3 \to SO(3)$ is a homeomorphism. Thus $\pi_3(SO(3)) \cong \pi_3(\mathbf{R}P^3)$.

The universal covering of $\mathbf{R}P^3$ is $S^3$ via the projection $f: S^3 \to \mathbf{R}P^3$, which is a fibre bundle with discrete fibre $\mathbf{Z}_2$. The homotopy exact sequence of this covering gives:
$$\cdots \to \pi_3(\mathbf{Z}_2) \to \pi_3(S^3) \xrightarrow{f_*} \pi_3(\mathbf{R}P^3) \to \pi_2(\mathbf{Z}_2) \to \cdots$$

Since $\mathbf{Z}_2$ is discrete, $\pi_i(\mathbf{Z}_2) = 0$ for $i \geq 1$. Therefore $f_*: \pi_3(S^3) \to \pi_3(\mathbf{R}P^3)$ is an isomorphism.

Now $\pi_3(S^3) = \mathbf{Z}$, generated by the identity map $[id_{S^3}]$. Under the homeomorphism $r'$, the generator of $\pi_3(SO(3))$ corresponds to $r' \circ f = r$, where $r: S^3 \to SO(3)$ is the quaternion conjugation map. Hence $\pi_3(SO(3)) = \mathbf{Z}$ is generated by $r$.
