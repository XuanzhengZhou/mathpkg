This proposition extends the computation of $\pi_3(SO(3)) = \mathbf{Z}$ to all special orthogonal groups $SO(k)$ with $k \geq 5$, using the stability properties of homotopy groups of Stiefel manifolds. The key step is analyzing the exact sequence of the fibration $SO(4) \to SO(5) \to S^4$ and showing that the boundary map's cokernel is $\mathbf{Z}$. Once stability is reached at $k=5$, all higher $SO(k)$ share the same $\pi_3$.
