Let $X$ be a $G$-space with an associated bundle $\xi = (X, p, B)$. For each map $f: B_1 \rightarrow B$ the total space $X_1$ of $f^*(\xi) = (X_1, p_1, B_1)$ has a natural $G$-space structure, and there is a homeomorphism $g: X_1 \bmod G \rightarrow B_1$ making the following diagram commutative.

$$X_1 \xrightarrow{f_\xi} X$$

Moreover, the $G$-space structure on $X_1$ can be chosen in exactly one way such that $f_\xi$ is a $G$-morphism. Finally, if $(X, p, B)$ is a principal $G$-bundle, then $(X_1, p_1, B_1)$ is a principal $G$-bundle.
