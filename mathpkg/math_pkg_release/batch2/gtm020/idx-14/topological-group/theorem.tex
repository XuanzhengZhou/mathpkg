A topological group $G$ is a set $G$ together with a group structure and topology on $G$ such that the function $(s, t) \mapsto st^{-1}$ is a map $G \times G \rightarrow G$.

The above condition of continuity on $(s, t) \mapsto st^{-1}$ is equivalent to the statement that $(s, t) \mapsto st$ is a map $G \times G \rightarrow G$ and $s \mapsto s^{-1}$ is a map $G \rightarrow G$.

Examples: The real line $\mathbf{R}$ with addition as group operation, the real line minus zero $\mathbf{R} - \{0\}$ with multiplication as group operation, the full linear groups of nonsingular matrices $GL(n, \mathbf{R})$ and $GL(n, \mathbf{C})$, and the orthogonal, unitary, and symplectic groups $O(n), SO(n), U(n), SU(n)$, and $Sp(n)$ are all topological groups.
