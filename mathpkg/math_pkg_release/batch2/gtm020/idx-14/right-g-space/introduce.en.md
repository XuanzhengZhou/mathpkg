Definition 1.2 from Chapter 4 of Husemoller's Fibre Bundles. A right $G$-space is a topological space $X$ equipped with a continuous right action of a topological group $G$, satisfying the usual associativity and identity axioms. Left and right $G$-space structures are in bijective correspondence via the inversion map $s \mapsto s^{-1}$, so it suffices to study right $G$-spaces.
