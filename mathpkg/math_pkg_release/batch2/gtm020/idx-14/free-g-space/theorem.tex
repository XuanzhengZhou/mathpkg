A $G$-space $X$ is called free provided it has the property that $xs = x$ implies $s = 1$. Let $X^*$ be the subspace of all $(x, xs) \in X \times X$, where $x \in X$, $s \in G$ for a free $G$-space $X$. There is a function $\tau: X^* \rightarrow G$ such that $x\tau(x, x') = x'$ for all $(x, x') \in X^*$. The function $\tau: X^* \rightarrow G$ such that $x\tau(x, x') = x'$ is called the translation function.

From the definition of the translation function $\tau(x, x')$, it has the following properties:

(1) $\tau(x, x) = 1$.
(2) $\tau(x, x')\tau(x', x'') = \tau(x, x'')$.
(3) $\tau(x', x) = \tau(x, x')^{-1}$ for $x, x', x'' \in X$.
