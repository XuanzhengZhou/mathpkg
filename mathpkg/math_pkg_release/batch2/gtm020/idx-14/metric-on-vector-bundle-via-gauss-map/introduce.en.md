Exercise 2 from Chapter 3, Section 9 of Husemoller's Fibre Bundles. This exercise shows that the construction of a metric on a vector bundle given in Theorem 9.5 is essentially the only way to obtain a metric when the bundle admits a Gauss map into a Grassmannian. The Gauss map pulls back the standard inner product from the universal bundle, and this exercise asserts that every metric arises this way.
