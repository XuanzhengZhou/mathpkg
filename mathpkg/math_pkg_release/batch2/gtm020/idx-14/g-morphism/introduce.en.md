Definition 1.3 from Chapter 4 of Husemoller's Fibre Bundles. A $G$-morphism is an equivariant map between $G$-spaces, i.e., a continuous map that commutes with the group action. The collection of $G$-spaces and $G$-morphisms forms the category $\mathbf{Sp}_G$, which serves as the categorical foundation for the theory of principal bundles.
