The idea of the proof is to show that $S$: $[X, Y] \rightarrow [S(X), S(Y)] = [X, \Omega S(Y)]$ is a bijection under the above hypotheses. If the above hypotheses hold for $X$ and $Y$, they hold for $S(X)$ and $S(Y)$. The bijective character of $[X, Y] \rightarrow [X, \Omega S(Y)]$ is established using a Wang type of sequence over $S(Y)$ for the fibring $ES(Y) \rightarrow S(Y)$.

A composition $\{X, Y\} \times \{Y, Z\} \rightarrow \{X, Z\}$ is defined from the composition functions

$$[S^n(X), S^n(Y)] \times [S^n(Y), S^n(Z)] \rightarrow [S^n(X), S^n(Z)]$$
