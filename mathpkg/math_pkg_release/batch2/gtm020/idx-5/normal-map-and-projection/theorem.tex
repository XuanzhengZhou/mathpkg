For each $b \in \mathbf{R}^n$, $b \neq 0$, there are two linear functions $v_b \colon \mathbf{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbf{R}^n$, the normal map, and $\pi_b \colon \mathbf{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbf{R}$, the projection on $b$, such that for each $x \in \mathbf{R}^n$:
$$x = v_b(x) + \pi_b(x) x,$$
$$\pi_b(x) = \frac{(b \mid x)}{(b \mid b)},$$
$$v_b(x) = x - \pi_b(x) b,$$
and $(b \mid v_b(x)) = 0$.
