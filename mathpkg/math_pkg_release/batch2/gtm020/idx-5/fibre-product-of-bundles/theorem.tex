The fibre product $\xi_1 \oplus \xi_2$ of two bundles
$$\xi_1 = (E_1, p_1, B) \quad \text{and} \quad \xi_2 = (E_2, p_2, B)$$
over $B$ is $(E_1 \oplus E_2, q, B)$, where $E_1 \oplus E_2$ is the subspace of all $(x, x') \in E_1 \times E_2$ with $p_1(x) = p_2(x')$ and $q(x, x') = p_1(x) = p_2(x')$.

The fibre $q^{-1}(b)$ of $(E_1 \oplus E_2, q, B)$ over $b \in B$ is $p_1^{-1}(b) \times p_2^{-1}(b) \subset E_1 \times E_2$.

The operation $\oplus \colon \text{Bun}_B \times \text{Bun}_B \rightarrow \text{Bun}_B$ is a functor: given $B$-morphisms $u_1 \colon (E_1, p_1, B) \rightarrow (E'_1, p'_1, B)$ and $u_2 \colon (E_2, p_2, B) \rightarrow (E'_2, p'_2, B)$, the $B$-morphism $u_1 \oplus u_2 \colon (E_1 \oplus E_2, q, B) \rightarrow (E'_1 \oplus E'_2, q', B)$ is defined by $(u_1 \oplus u_2)(x_1, x_2) = (u_1(x_1), u_2(x_2))$.
