The fibre product, also known as the Whitney sum, is a binary operation on fibre bundles over a common base space $B$. Given two bundles $\xi_1 = (E_1, p_1, B)$ and $\xi_2 = (E_2, p_2, B)$, their fibre product $\xi_1 \oplus \xi_2$ is a new bundle whose total space consists of pairs $(x, x')$ lying over the same basepoint. The fibre over each $b \in B$ is naturally the Cartesian product of the individual fibres. This operation extends to a functor on the category of bundles over $B$, acting on $B$-morphisms componentwise.
