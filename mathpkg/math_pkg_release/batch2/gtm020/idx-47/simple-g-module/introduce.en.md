A simple $G$-module is the representation-theoretic analog of an irreducible representation: it has no nontrivial $G$-invariant subspaces. Simple $G$-modules are the fundamental building blocks in the study of $G$-modules, as general $G$-modules may admit filtrations or decompositions into simple constituents. The simplicity condition plays a crucial role in Schur's Lemma, which classifies $G$-morphisms between simple $G$-modules.
