Schur's Lemma is a cornerstone of representation theory. It states that any $G$-morphism between two simple $G$-modules is either zero or an isomorphism — no partial identification is possible. In the special case of a self-map on a simple $G$-module over an algebraically closed field $F$, the morphism must be multiplication by a scalar. This second part follows by picking an eigenvalue $\lambda$ of $f$ and observing that the $G$-submodule $\ker(f - \lambda)$ must be the whole module.
