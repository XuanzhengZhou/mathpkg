The first statement follows from (4.9): if $f: M \rightarrow N$ is a $G$-morphism between simple $G$-modules, then by (4.9), $f$ is either zero or a monomorphism, and also $f$ is either zero or an epimorphism. If $f \neq 0$, then $f$ is simultaneously a monomorphism and an epimorphism, hence an isomorphism.

For the second statement, let $\lambda$ be an eigenvalue of $f$, and let $\ker(f - \lambda) = L$ be a $G$-submodule of $M$. Since $\lambda$ is an eigenvalue, $L \neq 0$. By simplicity of $M$, we have $L = M$, and thus $f(x) = \lambda x$ for all $x \in M$. This proves the theorem.
