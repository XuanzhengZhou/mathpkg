A $G$-module is a $G$-space with a linear action, combining the structure of a vector space over $F = \mathbf{R}, \mathbf{C}, \text{ or } \mathbf{H}$ with a continuous group action by linear automorphisms. The defining properties ensure that the action of each group element is a linear automorphism, that the group operation distributes compatibly, and that the action map $G \times M \rightarrow M$ is continuous. This concept generalizes representations of topological groups on vector spaces.
