Either $\ker f$ equals $M$ or $0$, and either $\operatorname{coker} g$ equals $M$ or $0$ in the second case. Since $M$ is simple, its only $G$-submodules are $0$ and $M$. For $f: M \rightarrow N$, the kernel $\ker f$ is a $G$-submodule of $M$, so $\ker f = 0$ (and $f$ is a monomorphism) or $\ker f = M$ (and $f$ is zero). For $g: L \rightarrow M$, the cokernel is isomorphic to $M/\operatorname{im} g$, and $\operatorname{im} g$ is a $G$-submodule of $M$. Thus either $\operatorname{im} g = M$ (and $g$ is an epimorphism) or $\operatorname{im} g = 0$ (and $g$ is zero).
