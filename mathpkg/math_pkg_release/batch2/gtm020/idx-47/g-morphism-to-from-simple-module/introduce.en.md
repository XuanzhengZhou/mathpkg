This proposition describes the fundamental restriction on $G$-morphisms involving simple $G$-modules: any $G$-morphism out of a simple $G$-module is either the zero map or injective, and any $G$-morphism into a simple $G$-module is either zero or surjective. This follows from the fact that the kernel and cokernel are $G$-submodules, and a simple module has no nontrivial proper $G$-submodules. This result directly implies Schur's Lemma as a corollary.
