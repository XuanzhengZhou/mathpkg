This definition endows the $F$-vector space $\text{Hom}_F(M, N)$ of all $F$-linear maps between two $G$-modules with a natural $G$-module structure via conjugation: for $s \in G$ and $f \in \text{Hom}_F(M, N)$, the transformed map is $s_N \circ f \circ s_M^{-1}$. An important consequence is that $f$ is a $G$-morphism precisely when it is $G$-invariant under this action. Taking $N = F$ with trivial $G$-action yields the dual $G$-module $M^+$ of conjugate linear functionals.
