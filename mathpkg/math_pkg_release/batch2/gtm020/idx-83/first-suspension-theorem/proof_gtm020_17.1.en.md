The theorem follows immediately from Theorems 1.3 and 1.4 (the Moore homology and cohomology suspension theorems). For a sphere $S^n$, which is $(n-1)$-connected, the induced morphism $\pi_i(\beta): \pi_i(S^n) \to \pi_i(\Omega S^{n+1}) = \pi_{i+1}(S^{n+1})$ is precisely the suspension homomorphism $E$, assigning to the homotopy class of $u: S^i \to S^n$ the homotopy class of its suspension $Su: S^{i+1} \to S^{n+1}$. The isomorphism ranges follow from the connectivity estimates provided by the Hopf algebra isomorphisms of Theorems 1.3 and 1.4.
