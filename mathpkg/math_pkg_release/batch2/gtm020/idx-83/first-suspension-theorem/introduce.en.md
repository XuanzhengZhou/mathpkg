The First Suspension Theorem (Freudenthal suspension theorem) is an immediate consequence of Moore's homology and cohomology suspension theorems (Theorems 1.3 and 1.4). For an $(n-1)$-connected space $X$, it describes how the suspension homomorphism $E: \pi_i(X) \to \pi_{i+1}(SX)$ behaves: it is an isomorphism in the stable range $i < 2n-1$ and an epimorphism at the boundary $i = 2n-1$. The terminology "Einhängung" (German for suspension) is the origin of the notation $E$ for this homomorphism.
