The theorem follows by putting together Proposition 1.1 and Proposition 1.2. Proposition 1.1 provides the coalgebra morphism $H_*(\beta): H_*(X) \to H_*(\Omega S(X))$ arising from the composite $X \xrightarrow{u_{-}} E^{+} \xrightarrow{g_{+}} \Omega S(X) \times S_{-}(X) \xrightarrow{\mathrm{pr}_1} \Omega S(X)$, together with the isomorphism $\phi(1 \otimes H_*(\beta))$ establishing the required factorization. Proposition 1.2 then implies that the unique Hopf algebra morphism $f: TH_*(X) \to H_*(\Omega S(X))$ induced by the coalgebra morphism $H_*(\beta)$ is an isomorphism, since the composite $A \otimes I(C) \to A \otimes I(A) \to I(A)$ is an isomorphism by the result of Proposition 1.1.
