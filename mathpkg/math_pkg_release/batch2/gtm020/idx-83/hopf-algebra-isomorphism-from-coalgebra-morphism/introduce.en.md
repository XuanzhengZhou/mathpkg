This proposition gives a criterion for when a coalgebra morphism from a connected graded coalgebra $C$ into a connected graded Hopf algebra $A$ induces an isomorphism from the tensor Hopf algebra $T(C)$ onto $A$. The condition is that the composite $A \otimes I(C) \to A \otimes I(A) \to I(A)$ is an isomorphism, where $I(\cdot)$ denotes the augmentation ideal. This result, together with Proposition 1.1, forms the algebraic core of Moore's theorem on the Hopf algebra structure of the homology of loop spaces on suspensions.
