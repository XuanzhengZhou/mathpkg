Stable equivalence (or $s$-equivalence) is the fundamental equivalence relation on vector bundles that K-theory is designed to classify. Two vector bundles $\xi$ and $\eta$ over $X$ are stably equivalent if they become isomorphic after adding trivial bundles of appropriate ranks: $\xi \oplus \theta^n \cong \eta \oplus \theta^q$.

This relation is coarser than isomorphism: a bundle that is not trivial may still be $s$-trivial, meaning it becomes trivial after adding a trivial bundle. A classic example is the tangent bundle $\tau(S^n)$ of the $n$-sphere, which is $s$-trivial for all $n$ (since $\tau(S^n) \oplus \theta^1 \cong \theta^{n+1}$) but is isomorphic to a trivial bundle only for $n = 1, 3, 7$. The set of $s$-equivalence classes forms the underlying set of the reduced K-theory group $\tilde{K}_F(X)$.
