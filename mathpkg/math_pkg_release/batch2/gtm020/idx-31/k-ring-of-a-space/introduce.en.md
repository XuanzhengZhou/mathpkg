The $K_F(X)$ ring (or group, when $F = \mathbf{H}$) is a fundamental object in topological K-theory. It is constructed as the Grothendieck completion of the semiring $\text{Vect}_F(X)$ of isomorphism classes of $F$-vector bundles over a space $X$. The completion adds formal differences $\xi - \eta$ of vector bundles, turning the additive structure into a group.

The assignment $X \mapsto K_F(X)$ is a cofunctor (contravariant functor) from spaces to rings: a continuous map $f: Y \to X$ induces a pullback homomorphism $K_F(f): K_F(X) \to K_F(Y)$. This construction reduces the classification of vector bundles up to stable equivalence to a purely algebraic object.
