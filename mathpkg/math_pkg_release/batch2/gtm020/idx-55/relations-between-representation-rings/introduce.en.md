This proposition establishes the algebraic relations between the real representation ring $RO(G)$, the complex representation ring $R(G)$, and the quaternionic (symplectic) representation ring $RSp(G)$ of a compact Lie group $G$. The morphisms $\varepsilon_U$ (complexification), $\varepsilon_O$ (realification/restriction to real), and $\varepsilon_{Sp}$ (symplectification/restriction to quaternionic) satisfy simple composition identities involving the Adams operation $\psi_{-1}$, which corresponds to taking the conjugate representation. These relations are fundamental for understanding the interplay among real, complex, and quaternionic $K$-theory.
