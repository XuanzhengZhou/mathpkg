The Euler characteristic $\chi(X)$ is a fundamental topological invariant defined as the alternating sum of the dimensions of the rational cohomology groups $H^i(X, \mathbf{Q})$. For this definition to be meaningful, one assumes that the cohomology groups vanish for sufficiently large dimension $i$ and that each $\dim H^i(X, \mathbf{Q})$ is finite. The Euler characteristic generalizes the classical formula $V - E + F$ for polyhedra and plays a central role in relating the Euler class of the tangent bundle of a closed manifold to its topology.
