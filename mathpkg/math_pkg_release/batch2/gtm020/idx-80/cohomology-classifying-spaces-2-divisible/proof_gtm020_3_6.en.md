The statements about $CP^\infty$, $CP^\infty \times CP^\infty$, and $G_2(C^\infty)$ follow from Proposition 3.1 and Theorem 3.2 applied with 2-divisible coefficients (since $\mathbb{Z} \subset R$, the integral cohomology determines the $R$-cohomology). Since $u_2$ is induced by the map corresponding to $\eta \mapsto \eta \oplus \bar{\eta}$, the formula $u_2(c_1(\gamma')) = e(\gamma_2^+)$ and $u_2(c_1(\gamma'')) = -e(\gamma_2^+)$ follow. The character of $w_2$ and $H^*(G_2^+(\mathbf{R}^\infty))$ follows from the fibration $CP^\infty \to G_2^+(\mathbf{R}^\infty) \to G_2(\mathbf{R}^\infty)$ together with the Gysin sequence. The map $v_2$ is induced by the complexification map $SO(2) \hookrightarrow U(2)$, giving $v_2(p_1(\gamma_2)) = e(\gamma_2^+)^2$, $v_1(c_1(\gamma_2)) = 0$, and $v_1(c_2(\gamma_2)) = p_1(\gamma_2)$. By induction on $n$, one shows that $H^*(BSO(n), R)$ is generated by $p_1, \dots, p_r$ and $e$, where for $n = 2r+1$ we have $e = 0$ and for $n = 2r$ we have $e^2 = p_r$.
