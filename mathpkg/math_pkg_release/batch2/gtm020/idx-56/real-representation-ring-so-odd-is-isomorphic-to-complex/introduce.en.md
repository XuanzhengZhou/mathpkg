For the odd special orthogonal group $SO(2r+1)$, the complexification map $\varepsilon_U$ from the real representation ring $RO(SO(2r+1))$ to the complex representation ring $RSO(2r+1)$ is an isomorphism. This follows because the exterior powers $\lambda^i \rho_n$ of the standard representation generate $RSO(2r+1)$, and the relation $\varepsilon_0 \varepsilon_U = 2$ together with the fact that $R(G)$ is a free abelian group implies $\varepsilon_U$ is a monomorphism.
