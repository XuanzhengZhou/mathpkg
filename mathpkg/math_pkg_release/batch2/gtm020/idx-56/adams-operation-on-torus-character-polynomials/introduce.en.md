For a compact group $G$ with maximal torus $T^r$, the representation ring $RG$ embeds as a subring of the Laurent polynomial ring $RT^r = \mathbf{Z}[\beta_1, \beta_1^{-1}, \ldots, \beta_r, \beta_r^{-1}]$ via restriction. When an element $a \in RG$ is expressed as a polynomial $P$ in the characters $\beta_i$, the Adams operation $\psi^k$ acts simply by raising each variable $\beta_i$ to the $k$-th power, providing a computationally effective formula.
