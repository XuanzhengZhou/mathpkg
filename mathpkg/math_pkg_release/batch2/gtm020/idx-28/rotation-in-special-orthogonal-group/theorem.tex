For $a, b \in S^{n-1}$ with $a \neq -b$, let $R(b, a)$ denote the unique rotation in $SO(n)$ that sends $a$ to $b$ and fixes the orthogonal complement of $\langle a, b \rangle$. This rotation acts in the plane spanned by $a$ and $b$ through the angle between $a$ and $b$, and acts as the identity on the orthogonal complement.
