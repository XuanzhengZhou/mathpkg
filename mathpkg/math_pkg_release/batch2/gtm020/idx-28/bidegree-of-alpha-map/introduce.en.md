The bidegree of the alpha map measures the topological complexity of the reflection action of $S^{n-1}$ on itself. The first component $+1 + (-1)^n$ gives the degree of the map $x \mapsto \alpha(x)\alpha(e_{n-1})y$, which is $0$ for odd $n$ and $2$ for even $n$ (as a map $S^{n-1} \to S^{n-1}$). The second component $-1$ reflects that for fixed $x$, the map $y \mapsto \alpha(x)y$ is a hyperplane reflection, which has degree $-1$.
