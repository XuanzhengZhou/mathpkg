Define the map $\phi: S^n \setminus \{-e_n\} \to SO(n+1)$ by
$$\phi(x) = R(x, e_n),$$
where $R$ is the rotation defined in (9.1) and $e_n = (0, \ldots, 0, 1)$ is the north pole of $S^n$. The map $\phi$ sends a point $x$ (different from the south pole $-e_n$) to the unique rotation in $SO(n+1)$ that sends $e_n$ to $x$.
