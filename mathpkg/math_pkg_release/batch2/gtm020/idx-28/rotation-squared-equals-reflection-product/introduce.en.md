The square of the rotation $R(x_1, x_2)$ from $x_2$ to $x_1$ in $SO(n)$ can be expressed as the product of two hyperplane reflections (or two $180^\circ$ rotations about axes). The maps $\alpha$ and $\beta$ encode reflections through the hyperplane orthogonal to a given vector and $180^\circ$ rotations about the axis defined by that vector, respectively. This identity is central to computing the characteristic map of the tangent bundle of $S^n$.
