Let $\xi$ be the principal $G$-bundle associated to $\xi[F]$. By Definition 8.5, the characteristic map $c_\xi: S^{n-1} \to G$ is the clutching map that reconstructs $\xi$ over $S^n = S(S^{n-1})$.

Consider the universal $G$-bundle $\omega_0 = (E_0, p_0, B_0)$ in dimensions $\leq n$, and let $f: S^n \to B_0$ be the classifying map of $\xi$. The boundary map $\partial: \pi_n(B_0) \to \pi_{n-1}(G)$ sends $[f]$ to the homotopy class $[c_\xi]$ of the characteristic map (this is Exercise 3 of this chapter).

Now, for the associated bundle $\xi[F] = \xi \times_G F$, the boundary map $\partial$ in its homotopy exact sequence factors through the boundary map of the principal bundle. Specifically, evaluating the characteristic map at $y_0 \in F$ yields $c_F: S^{n-1} \to F$ defined by $c_F(x) = c_\xi(x) y_0$. The homomorphism $G \to F$ given by $g \mapsto g y_0$ induces $\pi_{n-1}(G) \to \pi_{n-1}(F)$ sending $[c_\xi]$ to $[c_F]$. Since $\pi_n(S^n) \cong \mathbb{Z}$ is generated by the identity map, the image of $\partial$ is cyclic and generated by $[c_F]$. This means $\operatorname{im} \partial = \ker \alpha$ is the subgroup of $\pi_{n-1}(F)$ generated by $[c_F]$.
