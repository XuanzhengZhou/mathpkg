For Stiefel varieties $V_k(F^{k+m})$, which parametrize $k$-tuples of orthonormal vectors in $F^{k+m}$, the lower homotopy groups vanish below a threshold that depends linearly on $m$ and the real dimension $c$ of the field $F$. This theorem follows from the exact homotopy sequence of the fibration $U_F(m) \to U_F(k+m) \to V_k(F^{k+m})$, together with the known stability range for unitary group homotopy groups. It is fundamental for computing the stable homotopy groups of Stiefel varieties and underlies the classification of vector bundles.
