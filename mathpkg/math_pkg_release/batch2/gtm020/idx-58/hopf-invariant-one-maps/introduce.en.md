This corollary asserts the existence of maps $S^{2n-1} \to S^n$ with Hopf invariant $1$ precisely when $n = 2, 4$, or $8$. Moreover, by taking integer multiples, the Hopf invariant can be made equal to any integer in these dimensions. The proof uses the fact that the Clifford algebras $C_1$, $C_3$, and $C_7$ admit irreducible modules of dimensions $2$, $4$, and $8$ respectively, producing orthogonal multiplications that yield maps of bidegree $(1,1)$ on spheres. Combined with Theorem (3.5) on the Hopf construction, these give the desired Hopf invariant $1$ maps.
