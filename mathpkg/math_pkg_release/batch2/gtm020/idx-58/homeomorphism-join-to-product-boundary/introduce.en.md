This proposition provides two explicit homeomorphisms involving the join $S^{n-1} \ast S^{m-1}$. The first homeomorphism $u$ identifies the join with the boundary of the product of two balls, $\partial(B^n \times B^m)$, which is the union $(B^n \times S^{m-1}) \cup (S^{n-1} \times B^m)$. The second homeomorphism $v$ identifies the same join with the sphere $S^{n+m-1}$ using trigonometric parametrization. These homeomorphisms are essential for relating the $K$-theory of the join to cup product structures on relative $K$-groups.
