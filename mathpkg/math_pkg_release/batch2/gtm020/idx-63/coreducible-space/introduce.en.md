A based space $X$ is called coreducible if there exists a map $g: X \to S^n$ inducing isomorphisms in reduced cohomology $\tilde{H}^i$ for all $i \leq n$. This property is dual to reducibility — while reducibility relates to the top cell splitting off from an $n$-dimensional CW-complex, coreducibility relates to the bottom cell splitting off. A space is $S$-coreducible if some suspension $S^k X$ is coreducible, generalizing the notion to the stable homotopy category.
