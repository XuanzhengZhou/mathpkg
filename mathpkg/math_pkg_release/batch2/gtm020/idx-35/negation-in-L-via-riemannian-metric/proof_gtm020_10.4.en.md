The result is true for $A = \emptyset$ or $A = \text{point}$ by a direct inspection: in these cases the Riemannian metric provides an orthogonal complement bundle and the adjoint map $\beta: \xi_1 \to \xi_0$ satisfying $\beta \circ \alpha = \text{id}$ on the appropriate subbundles.

In general, we use the natural isomorphism $L(X/A, *) \rightarrow L(X, A)$ given by the commutative diagram:
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
L(X/A, *) & \xrightarrow{\Delta} & K(X/A, *) \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow \\
L(X, A) & \xrightarrow{\Delta} & K(X, A)
\end{array}$$

The result for the general case follows from the pointed case $(X/A, *)$ via this natural isomorphism.
