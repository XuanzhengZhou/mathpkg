Special case 4.8 from Relative K-Theory. When the subspace $A$ is empty, $S(X,\emptyset)$ reduces to pairs of vector bundle isomorphism classes over $X$, and the equivalence relation defining $L(X,\emptyset)$ corresponds to $s$-equivalence (stable equivalence) of vector bundles. This connects $L(X,\emptyset)$ to the usual $K$-theory construction via the Grothendieck group.
