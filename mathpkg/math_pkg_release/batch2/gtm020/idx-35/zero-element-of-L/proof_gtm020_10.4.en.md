If $\alpha: \xi_0 \to \xi_1$ is an isomorphism over $X$, then for any difference isomorphism $\beta: \eta_0 \to \eta_1$, the sum $\alpha \oplus \beta$ is isomorphic to $\beta$ in $S(X,A)$ after stabilization, so $[\alpha]$ acts as the identity. Thus the class of all isomorphisms defined over $X$ is the zero element of the commutative semigroup $L(X,A)$.
