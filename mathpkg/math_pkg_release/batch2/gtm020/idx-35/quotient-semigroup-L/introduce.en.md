The quotient semigroup $L(X,A)$ is obtained from $S(X,A)$ by identifying difference isomorphisms that become isomorphic after stabilization by Whitney sums with identity maps on auxiliary bundles. This construction is analogous to the Grothendieck construction for $K$-theory and serves as the fundamental object for defining relative $K$-theory groups via the map $\Delta: L(X,A) \to K(X,A)$.
