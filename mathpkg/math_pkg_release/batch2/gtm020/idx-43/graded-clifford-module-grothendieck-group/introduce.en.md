For a $\mathbf{Z}_2$-graded algebra $A$, two Grothendieck groups are defined to classify its module theory. $M(A)$ is the free abelian group generated by isomorphism classes of irreducible $\mathbf{Z}_2$-graded $A$-modules -- those having no proper nontrivial graded submodules. $N(A)$ is the free abelian group generated by isomorphism classes of irreducible ungraded $A$-modules, ignoring the $\mathbf{Z}_2$-grading. These groups form the basis for the classification of Clifford modules: the rank of $M(A)$ (or $N(A)$) indicates the number of distinct irreducible representations, up to Morita equivalence. For Clifford algebras $C_k$, these groups exhibit the same 8-fold periodicity as the algebras themselves.
