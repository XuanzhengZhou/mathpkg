For complexified Clifford algebras $C_k^c = C_k \otimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C}$, there is a periodicity of order 2: the isomorphism type of $C_k^c$ repeats every two dimensions up to tensoring with a full complex matrix algebra. Specifically, $C_{k+2}^c \cong C_k^c \otimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C}(2)$. This is the complex analog of Bott periodicity and reflects the fact that the complex Clifford algebra depends only on the parity of $k$. The proof uses the isomorphism $C_{k+2} \otimes \mathbf{C} \cong C'_k \otimes \mathbf{C} \otimes C_2$ combined with tensor product manipulations, reducing to the known structure of the low-dimensional complex Clifford algebras.
