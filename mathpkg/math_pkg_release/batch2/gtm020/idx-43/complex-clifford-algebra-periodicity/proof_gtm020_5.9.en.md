From (5.6) there is an isomorphism
$$
C_{k+2} \bigotimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C} = C_{k+2}^c \cong C'_k \bigotimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C} \bigotimes_{\mathbf{R}} C_2 = \left( C'_k \bigotimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C} \right) \bigotimes_{\mathbf{R}} \left( C_2 \bigotimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C} \right) = C_k^c \bigotimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C}(2).
$$
The key steps use the relation $C'_k \otimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C} \cong C_k^c$ (the complexification identifies the positive and negative definite Clifford algebras) and $C_2 \otimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C} \cong \mathbf{C}(2)$ (the complexification of the quaternion algebra $\mathbf{H} \cong C_2$ yields the $2 \times 2$ complex matrix algebra).
