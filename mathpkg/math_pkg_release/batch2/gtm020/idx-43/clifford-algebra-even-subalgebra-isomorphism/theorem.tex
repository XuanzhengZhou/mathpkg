Let $C_{k-1}$ be the Clifford algebra of a quadratic form on an $(k-1)$-dimensional vector space $M$, and let $C_k$ be the Clifford algebra of the form $-xy \oplus f$ on $\mathbf{R} \oplus M$, where $f$ is the original quadratic form on $M$. Then there is an algebra isomorphism
$$
\phi: C_{k-1} \overset{\cong}{\longrightarrow} C_k^0
$$
where $C_k^0$ denotes the even subalgebra (the degree-0 part of the $\mathbf{Z}_2$-grading) of $C_k$. Explicitly, $\phi$ is given by
$$
\phi(x_0 + x_1) = x_0 + e_k x_1
$$
for $x_0 + x_1 \in C_{k-1}^0 \oplus C_{k-1}^1$, where $e_k$ is the generator of $C(-xy)$ with $e_k^2 = -1$.
