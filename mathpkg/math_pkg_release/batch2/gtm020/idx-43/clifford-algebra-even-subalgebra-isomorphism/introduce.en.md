For any quadratic space $(M, f)$, there is a natural isomorphism between the Clifford algebra $C_{k-1} = C(f)$ and the even subalgebra $C_k^0$ of the Clifford algebra $C(-xy \oplus f)$ on the orthogonal direct sum of a hyperbolic plane with $(M, f)$. This isomorphism is fundamental to the study of Clifford algebra periodicity: it relates the full Clifford algebra in dimension $k-1$ to the even part in dimension $k$, allowing an inductive construction. The explicit formula $\phi(x_0 + x_1) = x_0 + e_k x_1$ provides a concrete vector space isomorphism whose multiplicative property follows from the relation $e_k x_1 e_k y_1 = x_1 y_1$ in the even subalgebra.
