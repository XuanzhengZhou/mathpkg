The Bott periodicity theorem for real Clifford algebras states that the Clifford algebra $C_{k+8}$ (associated to the negative definite quadratic form on $\mathbf{R}^{k+8}$) is isomorphic to the tensor product of $C_k$ with the full $16 \times 16$ real matrix algebra $\mathbf{R}(16)$. The same holds for the positive definite Clifford algebras $C'_k$. This periodicity of order 8 is fundamental to real K-theory and the classification of topological insulators: the isomorphism type of $C_k$ repeats every 8 dimensions, up to tensoring with a matrix algebra. The proof iterates the low-dimensional isomorphisms of Proposition 5.5 and uses $\mathbf{H}(2) \otimes \mathbf{H}(2) \cong \mathbf{R}(16)$.
