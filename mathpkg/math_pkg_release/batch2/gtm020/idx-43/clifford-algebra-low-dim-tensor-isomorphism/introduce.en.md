For low-dimensional Clifford algebras $C_k$ and $C'_k$ (where $C_k$ is the Clifford algebra of the negative definite quadratic form on $\mathbf{R}^k$ and $C'_k$ is the Clifford algebra of the positive definite form), there are specific tensor product isomorphisms with the complex numbers $\mathbf{C}$ and quaternions $\mathbf{H}$. For $k = 1, 2$, we have $\mathbf{C} \otimes C_k \cong \mathbf{C} \otimes C'_k$. The key computation involves constructing an explicit algebra isomorphism $w: \mathbf{H} \otimes \mathbf{H} \to \mathbf{R}(4)$ by $w(x_1 \otimes x_2)x = x_1 x \bar{x}_2$, which shows that the tensor square of the quaternions is isomorphic to the full $4 \times 4$ real matrix algebra. These isomorphisms are building blocks for the Bott periodicity theorem for Clifford algebras.
