This theorem classifies the irreducible modules over real and complex Clifford algebras $C_k$ and $C_k^c$ for $k = 1$ through $8$. The invariants $N_k$ and $M_k$ are the Grothendieck groups of irreducible ungraded and $\mathbf{Z}_2$-graded $C_k$-modules respectively, while $a_k$ is the rank of the corresponding free abelian group (essentially the number of isomorphism classes of irreducible modules up to Morita equivalence, weighted by the dimension of the underlying division algebra). The classification exhibits periodicity: $N_{k+8} \cong N_k$ and $M_{k+8} \cong M_k$ with $a_{k+8} = 16a_k$ for the real case, and $N_{k+2}^c \cong N_k^c$ with $a_{k+2}^c = 2a_k^c$ for the complex case. This table is the representation-theoretic counterpart to the Bott periodicity of Clifford algebras and is fundamental to the construction of K-theory.
