This theorem gives the complete classification of the real Clifford algebras $C_k$ (associated to the negative definite quadratic form $-\sum x_i^2$ on $\mathbf{R}^k$), $C'_k$ (associated to the positive definite form $\sum x_i^2$), and the complexification $C'_k{}^c$ for $k = 1$ through $8$. The classification shows that each Clifford algebra is isomorphic to a full matrix algebra over $\mathbf{R}$, $\mathbf{C}$, or $\mathbf{H}$, or a direct sum of two such algebras. Together with Bott periodicity ($C_{k+8} \cong C_k \otimes \mathbf{R}(16)$), this table determines all real Clifford algebras. The pattern exhibits the celebrated 8-fold periodicity fundamental to real topological K-theory.
