Let $\xi = (E,p,B)$ and $\xi' = (E',p',B')$ be two vector bundles. A \textbf{morphism of vector bundles} $(u,f): \xi \to \xi'$ is a morphism of the underlying bundles; that is, $u: E \to E'$ and $f: B \to B'$ are maps such that $p'u = fp$, and the restriction $u: p^{-1}(b) \to (p')^{-1}(f(b))$ is linear for each $b \in B$.
