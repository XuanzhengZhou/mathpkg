Let $\mathbf{VB}$ denote the category of vector bundles, whose objects are vector bundles $\xi = (E,p,B)$ and whose morphisms are vector bundle morphisms $(u,f): \xi \to \xi'$.

Let $\mathbf{VB}_k$ denote the subcategory of $k$-dimensional vector bundles.

Let $\mathbf{VB}_B$ denote the category of vector bundles over a fixed base space $B$, whose morphisms are $B$-morphisms $u: \xi \to \xi'$ of the form $(u, 1_B)$.

Finally, the subcategory $\mathbf{VB}_B^k$ of $k$-dimensional vector bundles over $B$ is the intersection $\mathbf{VB}_B \cap \mathbf{VB}_k$.

An \textbf{isomorphism of vector bundles over $B$} is a $B$-morphism $u: \xi \to \xi'$ such that there exists a $B$-morphism $v: \xi' \to \xi$ with $vu = 1_\xi$ and $uv = 1_{\xi'}$.
