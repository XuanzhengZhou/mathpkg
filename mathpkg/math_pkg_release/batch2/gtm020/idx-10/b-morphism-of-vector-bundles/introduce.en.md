A $B$-morphism of vector bundles is a morphism between two vector bundles that share the same base space $B$, where the base map is required to be the identity on $B$. This notion is fundamental for comparing vector bundles over a fixed base, and it underlies the definition of the category $\mathbf{VB}_B$ of vector bundles over $B$. The condition that the base map is $1_B$ ensures that the morphism compares fibres lying over the same base point.
