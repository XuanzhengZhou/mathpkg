The fibre of $\xi_0$ is $F^k - \{0\}$ and is $(ck - 2)$-connected. By Theorem 2(7.1), under hypothesis (H1), we have a cross section $s$ of $\xi_0$. The map $s$ can be viewed as an everywhere-nonzero cross section of $\xi$. This cross section determines a monomorphism $u: \theta^1 \rightarrow \xi$, where $u(b, a) = as(b)$ for $(b, a

$E(\theta^1)$. Let $\eta$ be the coker $u$ which is a vector bundle by 3(8.2), and since $X$ is paracompact, by 3(9.6), there is an isomorphism between $\xi$ and $\eta \oplus \theta^1$. This proves the proposition.

For $x \in \mathbf{R}$, let $\langle x \rangle$ denote the smallest integer $n$ with $x \leq n$.
