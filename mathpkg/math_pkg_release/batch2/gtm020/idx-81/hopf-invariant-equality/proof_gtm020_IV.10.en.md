The Chern character $\mathrm{ch} \colon K(X) \to H^{\mathrm{ev}}(X, \mathbf{Q})$ is a ring homomorphism. Therefore, we have
$$\mathrm{ch}(a_f^2) = \mathrm{ch}(a_f) \cdot \mathrm{ch}(a_f) = (a^* + r b^*)^2 = (a^*)^2,$$
where $r \in \mathbf{Q}$ and we use $H^*(C_f, \mathbf{Q}) = \mathbf{Q}[a^*]/((a^*)^2)$ for dimensional reasons. Then from $a_f^2 = h_f b_f$ in $K$-theory we derive the relation
$$h_f \cdot \mathrm{ch}(b_f) = \mathrm{ch}(a_f^2) = (a^*)^2 = h_f^* \cdot b^*.$$
Since $\mathrm{ch}(b_f) = b^*$, it follows that $h_f = h_f^*$.
