Clearly $\mathrm{ch}(1) = 1$, since $c_1(1) = 0$ for the trivial line bundle $1$. For the canonical line bundle $\gamma$ over $S^2 = \mathbb{CP}^1$, we have $\mathrm{ch}(\gamma - 1) = c_1(\gamma)$, which generates $H^2(S^2, \mathbf{Z})$. Since $\gamma - 1$ forms part of an additive basis for $K(S^2)$ together with $1$, and $\mathrm{ch}$ sends these basis elements to generators of $H^*(S^2, \mathbf{Z})$, the map $\mathrm{ch}$ is an isomorphism.
