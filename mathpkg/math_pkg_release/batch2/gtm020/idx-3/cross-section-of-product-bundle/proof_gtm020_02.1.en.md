Every map $s: B \rightarrow B \times F$ has the form $s(b) = (s'(b), f(b))$, where $s': B \rightarrow B$ and $f: B \rightarrow F$ are maps uniquely defined by $s$. Since $p$ is the projection on the first factor, $p(s(b)) = s'(b)$. The condition that $s$ is a cross section, i.e., $p \circ s = 1_B$, is equivalent to $s'(b) = b$ for each $b \in B$. Hence $s(b) = (b, f(b))$, and $f = \operatorname{pr}_2 \circ s$ is uniquely determined. The correspondence $s \mapsto \operatorname{pr}_2 \circ s$ is a bijection.
