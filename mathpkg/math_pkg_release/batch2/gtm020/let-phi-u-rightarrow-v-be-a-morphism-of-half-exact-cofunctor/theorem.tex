CHAPTER 11

Bott Periodicity in the Complex Case

Using elementary methods in analysis and vector bundle theory, we present the Atiyah-Bott proof of the complex periodicity theorem. In this setting we see the role of the Hopf bundle in defining the periodicity isomorphism.

1. K-Theory Interpretation of the Periodicity Result

Ket $k = 2$ for complex $K$-theory $K$ and $k = 8$ for real $K$-theory $KO$. From the sequence of spaces

$$X \vee S^k \rightarrow X \times S^k \rightarrow X \wedge S^k$$

there is the following exact sequence in either real or complex $K$-theory:

$$0 \rightarrow \tilde{K}(X \wedge S^k) \rightarrow \tilde{K}(X \times S^k) \rightarrow \tilde{K}(X \vee S^k) \rightarrow 0$$

The external cup product or tensor product yields a morphism

$$K(X) \otimes K(S^k) \rightarrow K(X \times S^k)$$

From this we have the following commutative diagram:

$$K(X) \otimes K(S^k) = (\tilde{K}(X) \otimes \tilde{K}(S^k)) \oplus \tilde{K}(X) \oplus \tilde{K}(S^k) \oplus \mathbf{Z}$$

$$K(X \times S^k) = \tilde{K}(X \wedge S^k) \oplus \tilde{K}(X) \oplus \tilde{K}(S^k) \oplus \mathbf{Z}$$
