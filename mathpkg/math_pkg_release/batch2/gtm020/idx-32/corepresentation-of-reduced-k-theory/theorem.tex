There is a natural isomorphism of group-valued cofunctors

$$\theta: [-, B_{(F)}] \xrightarrow{\cong} \tilde{K}_F(-)$$

defined on the category of finite connected CW-complexes.

For $[f], [g] \in [X, B_{(F)}]$, there exists an integer $n$ such that $f, g: X \rightarrow G_n(F^{2n})$. Then $\theta([f]) = f^*(\gamma_n^{2n}) - n$ and $\theta([g]) = g^*(\gamma_n^{2n}) - n$. Moreover, there exists a unique map $\psi: B_{(F)} \times B_{(F)} \rightarrow B_{(F)}$ making the diagram commute with the Grassmannian structure maps $\psi_n$, and $\theta$ is a group homomorphism.
