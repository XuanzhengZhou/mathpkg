The corepresentation theorem establishes that the reduced $K$-theory cofunctor $\tilde{K}_F$ is naturally isomorphic to the homotopy-class cofunctor $[-, B_{(F)}]$ on the category of finite connected CW-complexes. The isomorphism $\theta$ sends a homotopy class $[f]$ to the pullback of the universal bundle $\gamma_n^{2n}$ minus the rank $n$, and this map respects the additive group structure of $K$-theory via the $H$-space structure on $B_{(F)}$. This is the fundamental result linking topological $K$-theory to homotopy theory.
