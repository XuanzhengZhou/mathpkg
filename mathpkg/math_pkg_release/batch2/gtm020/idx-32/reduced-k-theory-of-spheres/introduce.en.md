Using the Bott periodicity isomorphism $\tilde{K}_F(S^i) \cong \pi_{i-1}(U_F)$, the reduced $K$-theory of low-dimensional spheres can be computed explicitly from the known homotopy groups of the classical groups. For spheres of dimension 0, the reduced $K$-theory recovers $\mathbb{Z}$ in all three cases (real, complex, quaternionic). For $S^1$, the real $K$-theory yields $\mathbb{Z}_2$ while the complex and quaternionic cases vanish, reflecting the connectivity properties of the corresponding structure groups $O$, $U$, and $Sp$.
