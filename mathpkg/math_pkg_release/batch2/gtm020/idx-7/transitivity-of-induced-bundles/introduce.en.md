This proposition establishes two natural properties of the induced bundle construction. First, pulling back by the identity map recovers the original bundle up to isomorphism. Second, the pullback operation is transitive with respect to composition of base maps: pulling back first by $f$ and then by $g$ yields a bundle isomorphic to the pullback by the composition $fg$. These properties are essential for the functorial behavior of the pullback construction.
