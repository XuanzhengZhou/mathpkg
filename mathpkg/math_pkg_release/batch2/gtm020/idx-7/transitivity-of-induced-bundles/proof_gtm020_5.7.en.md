*Proof.* (1) Define $u: \xi \to 1^*(\xi)$ by $u(x) = (p(x), x)$. Then $u$ is clearly an isomorphism.

(2) Define $v: (fg)^*(\xi) \to g^*(f^*(\xi))$ by $v(b_2, x) = (b_2, (g(b_2), x))$. Then $v$ is clearly an isomorphism.
