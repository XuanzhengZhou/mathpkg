Two bundles over the same base space $B$ are said to be locally isomorphic if they become isomorphic upon restriction to sufficiently small open neighborhoods of each point. This is a strictly weaker notion than global isomorphism; every pair of isomorphic bundles is locally isomorphic, but the converse does not hold. Local isomorphism captures the idea that bundles may look the same in small patches while differing in their global twisting.
