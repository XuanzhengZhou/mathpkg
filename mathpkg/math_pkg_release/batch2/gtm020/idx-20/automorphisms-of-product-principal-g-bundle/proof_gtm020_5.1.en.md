From the relation

$$h_g(b, st) = (b, g(b)st) = (b, g(b)s)t = h_g(b, s)t,$$

it follows that $h_g$ is an automorphism with inverse morphism $h_g^{-1} = h_{g'}$, where $g'(b) = g(b)^{-1}$.

Since $p h = p$, we have $h(b, s) = h(b, f(b, s))$ for some map $f: B \times G \to G$. For $g(b) = f(b, 1)$, we have

$$h(b, s) = h(b, 1)s = (b, g(b))s = (b, g(b)s) = h_g(b, s).$$

This proves the theorem.
