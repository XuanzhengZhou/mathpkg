This theorem gives a criterion for two fibre (or vector) bundles to be isomorphic over the base space $B$. Two bundles are isomorphic if and only if their transition functions are equivalent in the sense that there exist maps $r_i: V_i \to G$ conjugating one system to the other by $g'_{i,j}(b) = r_i(b)^{-1} g_{i,j}(b) r_j(b)$. The proof constructs the isomorphism explicitly from the maps $r_i$, establishing the fundamental relationship between bundle isomorphism classes and cohomology classes of transition functions.
