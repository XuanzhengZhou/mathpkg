By Theorem 1.1, fibre bundle automorphisms are quotients of $(b, s, y) \mapsto (b, g(b)s, y)$. Since

$$(b, g(b)s, y) \bmod G = (b, g(b)y),$$

the fibre bundle automorphisms are of the form $(b, y) \mapsto (b, g(b)y) = h_g(b, y)$.

Note that for $h_g(b, y) = (b, g(b)y)$ and $h_{g'}(b, y) = (b, g'(b)y)$ we have $h_{g'} h_g = h_{g' g}$, and $h_g$ is the identity if and only if $g(b) = 1$ for each $b \in B$.
