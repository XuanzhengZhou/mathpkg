The Adams Conjecture, posed by J. F. Adams in 1963, concerns the image of Adams operations $\psi^{k}$ under the $J$-homomorphism $J: \widetilde{KO}(X) \to J(X)$. It asserts that for any integer $k \geq 2$ and $x \in \widetilde{KO}(X)$, the difference $\psi^{k}(x) - x$ becomes trivial in $J(X)$ after multiplication by a sufficiently high power of $k$. The conjecture is a central result in algebraic topology linking $K$-theory to stable homotopy theory, and its resolution led to a precise determination of the image of $J$ on spheres. Four independent proofs were subsequently found: by Quillen-Friedlander using étale homotopy theory, by Sullivan also using étale methods, by Quillen via modular character theory of finite groups, and by Becker-Gottlieb using transfer maps.
