For every integer $k \geq 2$ and every element $x \in \widetilde{KO}(X)$, there exists an integer $e = e(k, x) \geq 1$ such that
$$
k^{e}(\psi^{k}(x) - x) = 0 \quad \text{in } J(X),
$$
where $\psi^{k}: \widetilde{KO}(X) \to \widetilde{KO}(X)$ denotes the $k$-th Adams operation and $J: \widetilde{KO}(X) \to J(X)$ is the $J$-homomorphism.
