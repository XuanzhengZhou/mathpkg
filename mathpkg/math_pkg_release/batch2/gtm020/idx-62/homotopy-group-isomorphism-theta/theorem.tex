Let $M_n^d$ denote the component of $\operatorname{Map}_*(S^{n-1}, S^{n-1})$ consisting of maps of degree $d$. Then for $1 \leq k \leq n-3$, there is an isomorphism
$$\theta: \pi_k(M_n^1) \xrightarrow{\cong} \pi_{n+k-1}(S^{n-1}).$$
Explicitly, for $[f] \in \pi_k(M_n^1)$, the map $f$ corresponds under $\theta$ to the map $h': S^k \times S^{n-1} \to S^{n-1}$ given by translation by the identity, where $h'(x, y) = *$ if either $x = *$ or $y = *$.
