Dold's Lemma provides conditions under which a $D^n$-morphism between two product bundles is a fibre homotopy equivalence. It requires that the restriction to each fibre $\{x\} \times Y$ be a homotopy equivalence and that the map admits a bundle morphism inverse up to fibre homotopy. This result is foundational for the theory of fibre homotopy equivalence of bundles over CW-complexes.
