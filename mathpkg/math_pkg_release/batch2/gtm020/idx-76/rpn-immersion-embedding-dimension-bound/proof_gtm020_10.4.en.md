Apply Theorem (10.3) to the inclusion $\mathbf{RP}^{2^r} \subset \mathbf{RP}^n$. Since the dual Stiefel-Whitney classes restrict naturally under inclusion, and $\tilde{w}_i(\mathbf{RP}^{2^r}) \neq 0$ for $0 \leq i \leq 2^r - 1$, the same non-vanishing holds for the corresponding classes of $\mathbf{RP}^n$. By Theorem (10.2), if $\mathbf{RP}^n$ immersed into $\mathbf{R}^m$, then $\tilde{w}_i(\mathbf{RP}^n) = 0$ for $i > m - n$. With $m = 2^{r+1} - 2$, one checks that the non-vanishing range $i \leq 2^r - 1$ violates the immersion condition, yielding the contradiction. The embedding case is analogous with the stronger bound $k = 2^{r+1} - 1$.
