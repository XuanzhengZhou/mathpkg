For the first part, the immersion yields a normal bundle $\nu$ of dimension $k$, where $\tau(M) \oplus \nu$ is trivial. Therefore, we have
$$0 = w_i(\nu) = \tilde{w}_i(M) \quad \text{for } i > k.$$

For the statement about embeddings, recall that $e(\xi) = s^* i^*(U_\xi)$ or $w^k(\xi) = s^* i^*(U_\xi) \bmod 2$ for $\dim \xi = k$, where $B \to E(\xi) \to (\cdots)$ is the Thom complex. The vanishing of the dual Stiefel-Whitney classes $\tilde{w}_i(M)$ for $i \geq k$ follows from the relation between the normal bundle of an embedding and the dual classes, together with the fact that the normal bundle has dimension $k$. When the normal bundle $\nu$ is orientable, the Euler class $e(\nu)$ satisfies the analogous vanishing condition derived from the Thom isomorphism.
