The dual Stiefel-Whitney class $\tilde{w}(\xi)$ is the multiplicative inverse of the total Stiefel-Whitney class $w(\xi)$ in the cohomology ring $H^*(B(\xi), \mathbf{Z}_2)$. For a manifold $M$, one defines $\tilde{w}(M) = \tilde{w}(\tau(M))$ in terms of the tangent bundle. This concept is particularly useful in the study of immersions and embeddings, because if $\nu$ is the normal bundle of an immersion $M \to \mathbf{R}^p$, then $w(\nu) = \tilde{w}(M)$, connecting the geometry of the immersion to the topology of the manifold.
