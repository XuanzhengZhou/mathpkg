This theorem determines precisely for which dimensions $n$ the real projective space $\mathbf{RP}^n$ admits an immersion or embedding into Euclidean space of one dimension higher. The condition is expressed in terms of powers of two: immersions exist exactly when $n = 2^r - 1$ or $n = 2^r - 2$, while embeddings require the stronger condition $n = 2^r - 1$. For $n = 2^r$, the theorem also gives stronger non-immersion and non-embedding results. The proof relies on the computation of the dual Stiefel-Whitney classes of $\mathbf{RP}^n$ and the vanishing conditions from (10.2).
