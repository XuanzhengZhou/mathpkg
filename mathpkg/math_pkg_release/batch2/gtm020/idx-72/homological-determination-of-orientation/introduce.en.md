This theorem provides a homological characterization of the orientation number $O(f)$. It shows that the induced maps $f_*$ and $f^*$ on local homology and cohomology groups are simply multiplication by $O(f)$. The proof reduces the general diffeomorphism to a linear map (via homotopy), then to an orthogonal transformation, and finally to a composition of reflections, each of which reverses orientation and acts by $-1$ on the local homology generator.
