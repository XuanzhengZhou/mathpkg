An orientation of a diffeomorphism between open connected subsets of Euclidean space is defined by the sign of its Jacobian determinant. If the determinant is everywhere positive, the diffeomorphism preserves orientation ($O(f)=+1$); if everywhere negative, it reverses orientation ($O(f)=-1$). Since the domain is connected and the diffeomorphism is of class $C^r$ ($r \geq 1$), the determinant cannot change sign without vanishing, so this notion is well-defined.
