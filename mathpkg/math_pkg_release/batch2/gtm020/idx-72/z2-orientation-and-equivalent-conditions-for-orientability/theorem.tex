Every manifold $M$ has a unique $\mathbf{Z}_2$-orientation. For a manifold $M$ the following statements are equivalent:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $M$ is orientable.
\item[(2)] There exists an atlas $\mathcal{A}$ of $M$ such that for $(U, \phi), (V, \psi) \in \mathcal{A}$ the relation
  $$\det D(\psi \circ \phi^{-1}) > 0 \quad \text{on } \phi(U \cap V)$$
  holds.
\item[(3)] The tangent bundle $\tau(M)$ has $\mathrm{SO}(n)$ as its structure group.
\end{enumerate}

Finally, if $M$ is connected, $M$ has precisely two orientations.
