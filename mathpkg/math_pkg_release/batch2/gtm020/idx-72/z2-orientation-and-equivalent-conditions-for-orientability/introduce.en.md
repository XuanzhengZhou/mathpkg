This theorem establishes the existence and uniqueness of $\mathbf{Z}_2$-orientations for all manifolds and characterizes orientability via three equivalent conditions: the existence of an atlas with orientation-preserving transition maps, the orientability of the manifold in the usual sense, and the reduction of the structure group of the tangent bundle from $\mathrm{GL}(n)$ to $\mathrm{SO}(n)$. For connected orientable manifolds, there are exactly two orientations, corresponding to the two generators of $H_n(M, M - x; \mathbf{Z})$.
