The Prolongation Theorem for cross sections is the fundamental technical result underpinning the homotopy classification of fibre bundles over CW-complexes. It states that given a locally trivial bundle $$\xi$$ over a relative CW-complex $$(B, A)$$, any cross section defined on the subspace $$A$$ can be extended (prolonged) to the whole base $$B$$, provided that either the fibre $$F$$ has sufficiently high connectivity relative to the dimension of $$B$$, or the base has a product structure $$B = Y \times I$$ compatible with the CW decomposition. The infinite-dimensional case follows by an inductive construction on the skeleta.
