The property of being locally trivial with a given fibre $$F$$ is preserved under both the pullback operation along a continuous map $$f: B_1 \to B$$ and the restriction operation to a subset $$A \subset B$$. Thus, if $$\xi$$ is a locally trivial bundle over $$B$$ with fibre $$F$$, then the pullback bundle $$f^*(\xi)$$ over $$B_1$$ and the restricted bundle $$\xi|A$$ over $$A$$ are both locally trivial bundles with the same fibre $$F$$. This ensures functorial stability of local triviality under basic bundle constructions.
