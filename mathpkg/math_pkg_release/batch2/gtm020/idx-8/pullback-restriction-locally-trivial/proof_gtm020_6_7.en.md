This follows immediately from Corollary 6.6. Since $$\xi$$ is locally trivial with fibre $$F$$, it is locally isomorphic to the trivial bundle $$B \times F$$. By Corollary 6.6, the restrictions of two locally isomorphic bundles to any subset remain locally isomorphic. Therefore $$f^*(\xi)$$ is locally isomorphic to $$f^*(B \times F) \cong B_1 \times F$$, and $$\xi|A$$ is locally isomorphic to $$(B \times F)|A \cong A \times F$$. Hence both $$f^*(\xi)$$ and $$\xi|A$$ are locally trivial with fibre $$F$$.
