For an Eilenberg-MacLane space $K(\Pi, n)$, the first nonzero reduced cohomology group appears in degree $n$ and equals $\text{Hom}(\Pi, \Pi)$. This group possesses a distinguished canonical element $\iota_n$ corresponding to the identity homomorphism on $\Pi$. The class $\iota_n$ serves as a universal cohomology class: pulling it back along maps $f: X \to K(\Pi, n)$ yields a natural isomorphism between the set of homotopy classes $[X, K(\Pi, n)]$ and the cohomology group $H^n(X, \Pi)$.
