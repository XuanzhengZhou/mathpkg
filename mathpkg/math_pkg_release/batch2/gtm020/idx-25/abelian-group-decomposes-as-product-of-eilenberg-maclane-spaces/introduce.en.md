A fundamental structural theorem of J. C. Moore states that every abelian topological group $A$ splits, up to homotopy, as a product of Eilenberg-MacLane spaces $K(\Pi, n)$. This decomposition holds not only as ordinary $H$-spaces but also at the level of higher homotopies. The result was originally proved in the context of simplicial sets and simplicial groups, and is a cornerstone for understanding the homotopy type of topological groups with abelian structure.
