For any abelian group $\Pi$ and any $n \geq 0$ there exists an abelian topological group $A$ which is a $K(\Pi, n)$ as a space. Moreover, the classifying space $B(A) = BK(\Pi, n) = K(\Pi, n + 1)$, which gives an inductive construction of $K(\Pi, n)$ as an abelian group using a classifying space construction which preserves products, and the loop space $\Omega K(\Pi, n) = K(\Pi, n - 1)$ from the homotopy exact triangle for a fibre sequence.
