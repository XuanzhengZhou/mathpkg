For every abelian group $\Pi$ and integer $n \geq 0$, one can construct a $K(\Pi, n)$ space that is simultaneously an abelian topological group. This construction is inductive: the classifying space functor $B$ shifts the Eilenberg-MacLane degree up by one, giving $B(K(\Pi, n)) = K(\Pi, n+1)$, while the loop space functor $\Omega$ shifts it down by one, giving $\Omega K(\Pi, n) = K(\Pi, n-1)$. The construction preserves products, making it compatible with the group structure.
