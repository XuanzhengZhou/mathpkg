A theorem of Rene Thom describes the homotopy type of the space of all maps from a space $X$ into an Eilenberg-MacLane space $K(\Pi, n)$. This mapping space splits as a product of Eilenberg-MacLane spaces: $\text{Map}(X, K(\Pi, n)) \simeq \prod_{0 \leq q \leq n} K(H^{n-q}(X, \Pi), q)$. The cohomology groups of $X$ thus determine the homotopy type of the mapping space. The theorem can be proved by induction on $n$ using the classifying space construction and the loop space fibration.
