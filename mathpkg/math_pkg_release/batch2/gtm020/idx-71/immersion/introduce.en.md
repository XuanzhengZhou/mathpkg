An immersion is a $C^r$-map between manifolds whose tangent map (derivative) is injective on each tangent space. This means that locally, an immersion looks like a linear inclusion. When $f: M \to N$ is an immersion, there exists a monomorphism of vector bundles $\tau(M) \to f^*(\tau(N))$ over $M$. The restriction of an immersion to a sufficiently small neighborhood of each point yields an embedding.
