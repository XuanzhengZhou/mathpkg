An embedding is a stronger condition than an immersion: it requires that the map $f$ be not only an immersion but also a homeomorphism onto its image with the subspace topology. This ensures that the image $f(M)$ is a submanifold of $N$ and that the manifold structure of $M$ is faithfully represented in $N$.
