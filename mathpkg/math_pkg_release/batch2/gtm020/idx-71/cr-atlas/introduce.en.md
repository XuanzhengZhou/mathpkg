A $C^r$-atlas is a collection of coordinate charts on a topological space whose transition maps are $C^r$-differentiable. Two atlases are equivalent if their union is again an atlas, and every atlas is contained in a unique maximal atlas. The notion of equivalence and maximality provides the foundation for defining differentiable structures on manifolds.
