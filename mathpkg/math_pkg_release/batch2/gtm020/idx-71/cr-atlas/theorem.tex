A $C^r$-atlas $A$ on a space $X$ is a family of charts on $X$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] The set $X$ is the union of all $U$ for $(U, \phi) \in A$.
\item[(2)] For $(U, \phi), (V, \psi) \in A$, the restriction $\psi \phi^{-1} : \phi(U \cap V) \to \psi(U \cap V)$ is a $C^r$-map (thus a $C^r$-isomorphism with inverse $\phi \psi^{-1}$).
\end{enumerate}
Two $C^r$-atlases $A$ and $A'$ are equivalent provided $A \cup A'$ is a $C^r$-atlas. A maximal $C^r$-atlas is an atlas such that for every $C^r$-atlas $A' \supset A$ there is the relation $A = A'$. Every $C^r$-atlas is included in a unique maximal $C^r$-atlas.
