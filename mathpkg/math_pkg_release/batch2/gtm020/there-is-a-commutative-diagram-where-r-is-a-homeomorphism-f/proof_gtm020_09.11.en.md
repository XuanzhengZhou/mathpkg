Since $r(x)1 = 1$ and $r(x) = r(-x)$, the map $r'$ exists. If $r(x)y = y$ for all $y$, then $xy = yx$ for all $y \in \mathbf{H}$. This happens if and only if $x$ is real, and therefore $x = \pm 1$. This means that $r'$ is injective. The formula $r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta) = j \cos 2\theta + k \sin 2\theta$ and the formulas arising from cyclic permutations of $i, j, k$ demonstrate that the image of $r$ includes all rotations about the three axes $i, j,$ and $k$. Therefore, $r'$ is a bijection and a homeomorphism because $RP^3$ is compact.
