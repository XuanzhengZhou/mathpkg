This proposition gives the periodicity relations for the Grothendieck groups $N_k$, $M_k$ of irreducible $Z_2$-graded modules over the Clifford algebras $C_k$, and for the rank invariant $a_k$. For real Clifford algebras, the groups $N_k$, $M_k$ exhibit 8-fold periodicity while the rank $a_k$ is multiplied by 16 every 8 steps. For complexified Clifford algebras, the group $N_k^c$ has 2-fold periodicity and $a_k^c$ doubles every 2 steps, while $M_k^c$ still obeys 8-fold periodicity. These relations are fundamental for the computation of the real and complex $K$-theory of a point via Clifford modules.
