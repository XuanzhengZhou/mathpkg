Let $M$ be a vector space equipped with a quadratic form $f$, and let $(-xy)$ denote the 1-dimensional quadratic space with generator $e_0$ satisfying $e_0^2 = -1$. Then
$$C(-xy \oplus f) = C(-xy) \,\hat{\otimes}\, C(f).$$
In particular, taking $f$ to be the standard quadratic form on $\mathbf{R}^{k-1}$, there is an isomorphism
$$\phi: C_{k-1} \xrightarrow{\cong} C_k^0,$$
where $C_k^0$ denotes the even subalgebra of $C_k$. The isomorphism is given by the formula
$$\phi(x_0 + x_1) = x_0 + e_k x_1$$
for $x_0 + x_1 \in C_{k-1}^0 \oplus C_{k-1}^1$, where $e_k$ is the $k$-th standard basis vector of $\mathbf{R}^k$.
