Let $\xi = (E, p, B)$ be an $n$-dimensional vector bundle. Let $E_0$ denote the non-zero vectors in $E$, and let $p_0$ denote the restriction $p|_{E_0}$. Let $E'$ be the quotient space of $E_0$ where two vectors in the same line in a given fibre of $\xi$ are identified, and let $q: E' \to B$ be the factorization of $p_0: E_0 \to B$ through $E'$ by the natural projection $E_0 \to E'$.

The bundle $(E', q, B)$ is called the projective bundle associated with $\xi$ and is denoted $P\xi$. The fibre of $P\xi^n$ is $\mathbb{F}P^{n-1}$, and the bundle is locally trivial. For each $b \in B$, the inclusion $\mathbb{F}^n \to p^{-1}(b) \subset E$ defines a natural inclusion $j_b: \mathbb{F}P^{n-1} \to q^{-1}(b) \subset E'$. A point in $E'$ is a line $L$ in the fibre of $\xi$ over $q(L)$.
