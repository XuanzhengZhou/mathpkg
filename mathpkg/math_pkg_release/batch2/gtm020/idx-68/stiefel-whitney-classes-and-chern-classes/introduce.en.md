Stiefel-Whitney classes $w_i(\xi) \in H^i(B, \mathbb{Z}_2)$ and Chern classes $c_i(\xi) \in H^{2i}(B, \mathbb{Z})$ are the fundamental characteristic classes of real and complex vector bundles respectively. They are defined via the relation $a_\xi^n = -\sum x_i(\xi) a_\xi^{n-i}$ in the cohomology of the projective bundle, where the uniqueness of the $x_i(\xi)$ follows from the Leray-Hirsch theorem. These classes capture essential topological information about the vector bundle and satisfy naturality, Whitney sum formula, and normalization axioms.
