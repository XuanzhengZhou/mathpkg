Define $u: \xi \rightarrow 1^*(\xi)$ by the relation $u(x) = (p(x), x)$, and $u$ is clearly an isomorphism. Next, let $v: (fg)^*(\xi) \rightarrow g^*(f^*(\xi))$ be defined by $v(b_2, x) = (b_2, (g(b_2), x))$. Then $v$ is clearly an isomorphism.
