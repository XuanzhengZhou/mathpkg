Let $B = B_1 \cup B_2$ where $B_1 = A \times [a, c]$ and $B_2 = A \times [c, b]$ are overlapping product intervals with $a < c < b$. If a principal $G$-bundle $\xi$ over $B$ restricts to a trivial bundle on each of $B_1$ and $B_2$, then $\xi$ is trivial over the whole space $B$. This pasting lemma is used in the homotopy invariance arguments for numerable bundles.
