Observe that $r^*(\xi)$ and $(\xi|(B \times 1)) \times I$ are $(B \times I)$-isomorphic principal $G$-bundles, where $r(b, t) = (b, 1)$. By (9.7), $r^*(\xi)$ and $\xi$ are isomorphic over $B \times I$. Thus $\xi$ is $G$-isomorphic to $(\xi|(B \times 1)) \times I$.

A symmetric argument shows that $\xi$ is also $G$-isomorphic to $(\xi|(B \times 0)) \times I$.

For the second statement, note that $\varepsilon_0^*(\xi)$ is isomorphic to $\varepsilon_0^*((\xi|(B \times 1)) \times I)$, and similarly for $\varepsilon_1^*(\xi)$. Since both pullbacks yield bundles isomorphic to the restriction at the respective endpoints, and the product with $I$ collapses under pullback along the endpoint inclusions, we obtain that $\varepsilon_0^*(\xi)$ and $\varepsilon_1^*(\xi)$ are $B$-isomorphic.
