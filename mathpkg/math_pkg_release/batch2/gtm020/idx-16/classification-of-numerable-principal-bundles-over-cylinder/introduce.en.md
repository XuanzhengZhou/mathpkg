For any numerable principal $G$-bundle $\xi$ over the cylinder $B \times I$, the bundle is $G$-isomorphic to the product of its restriction to either end with the interval. Consequently, the pullbacks of $\xi$ along the inclusion maps $\varepsilon_0, \varepsilon_1: B \hookrightarrow B \times I$ at the two ends yield $B$-isomorphic bundles. This is the fundamental homotopy invariance theorem for numerable principal $G$-bundles: homotopic maps induce isomorphic pullback bundles.
