The Hopf construction associates to any map $g: X \times Y \rightarrow Z$ a map $g^0$ from the join $X \ast Y$ to the suspension $SZ$. When applied to maps between spheres $g: S^{n-1} \times S^{n-1} \rightarrow S^{n-1}$, the Hopf construction produces a map $g^0: S^{2n-1} \rightarrow S^n$, which is a fundamental source of elements in the homotopy groups of spheres. The Hopf invariant of $g^0$ is related to the bidegree of $g$, providing a powerful method for constructing and analyzing nontrivial homotopy classes.
