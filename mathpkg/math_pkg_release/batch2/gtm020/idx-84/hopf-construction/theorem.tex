Let $X$, $Y$, and $Z$ be spaces and let $g: X \times Y \rightarrow Z$ be a map. The **Hopf construction** on $g$ is a map

$$g^0: X \ast Y \rightarrow SZ$$

where $X \ast Y$ is the join of $X$ and $Y$, defined as the quotient of $X \times [0,1] \times Y$ by the identifications $(x,0,y) \sim (x',0,y)$ and $(x,1,y) \sim (x,1,y')$, and $g^0$ is given by

$$g^0(x,t,y) = \langle g(x,y), t \rangle \in S(Z).$$

Note that $S^p \ast S^q$ is homeomorphic to $S^{p+q+1}$.
