Since $S^n$ is $(n-1)$-connected, the corollary follows from Theorem 2.1. The induced morphism $\pi_i(\beta): \pi_i(S^n) \rightarrow \pi_i(\Omega S^{n+1}) = \pi_{i+1}(S^{n+1})$ is the suspension morphism which assigns to the homotopy class of $u: S^i \rightarrow S^n$ the homotopy class of its suspension $Su: S^{i+1} \rightarrow S^{n+1}$. The notation $E: \pi_i(S^n) \rightarrow \pi_{i+1}(S^{n+1})$ for $\pi_i(\beta)$ comes from the German word Einhängung.

The double suspension in the statement factors as the composition of two single suspensions. By analyzing the homology of the pair $(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}, S^{2n-1})$ via Theorem 2.1 and applying the relative Hurewicz theorem in its localized form, one obtains the stated isomorphism and epimorphism range. Note that this corollary and Corollary 2.2 can also be derived from a simple application of the total space (Serre) spectral sequence, and they should be regarded as refinements of (1.6).
