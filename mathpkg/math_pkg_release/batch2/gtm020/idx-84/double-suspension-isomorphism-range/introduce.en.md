This corollary refines the classical Freudenthal suspension theorem for odd-dimensional spheres localized at a prime $p$. It states that the double suspension map on $p$-local homotopy groups of spheres is an isomorphism in degrees below $2np - 2$ and remains surjective at the boundary degree $2np - 2$. The result follows from the mod-$p$ homology calculation of $\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}$ and provides sharper connectivity bounds than the unlocalized suspension theorem.
