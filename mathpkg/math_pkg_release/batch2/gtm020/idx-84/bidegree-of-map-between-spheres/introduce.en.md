The bidegree $(d_1, d_2)$ of a map $g: S^{n-1} \times S^{n-1} \rightarrow S^{n-1}$ captures the degrees obtained by fixing each coordinate in turn. This notion is essential for computing the Hopf invariant of the Hopf construction: if $g$ has bidegree $(d_1, d_2)$, then the Hopf invariant of $g^0$ is $H([g^0]) = d_1 d_2$. Maps of specified bidegree can be constructed explicitly, providing a systematic method for producing elements in $\pi_{2n-1}(S^n)$ with prescribed Hopf invariants.
