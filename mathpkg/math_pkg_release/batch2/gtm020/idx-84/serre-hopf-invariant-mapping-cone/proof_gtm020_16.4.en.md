Let $S^{2n-1} \rightarrow M_f \rightarrow S^n$ be the mapping cylinder factorization of $f$, where $M_f/S^{2n-1} = C_f$ is the mapping cone and $S^n \rightarrow M_f$ is a homotopy equivalence. Then $H^n(C_f) = H^n(M_f, S^{2n-1}) = \mathbf{Z}v$ and $H^{2n}(C_f) = H^{2n}(M_f, S^{2n-1}) = \mathbf{Z}w$ are the only nonzero cohomology groups in this two-cell complex. Write $v^2 = hw$; we must show $h = \pm H([f])$.

Let $E \rightarrow M_f$ be the path space fibration and let $E' \rightarrow S^{2n-1}$ be its restriction to $S^{2n-1}$. The fibre can be taken to be $\Omega S^n \subset E' \subset E$ because $S^n \rightarrow M_f$ is a homotopy equivalence. The groups $H^{2n-1}(E')$ and $H^{2n}(E, E')$ are isomorphic, and one shows that $\pm h = H([f])$ by establishing that $H^{2n-1}(E') = \mathbf{Z}/H([f])$ through an analysis of the transgression in the Serre spectral sequence of the path space fibration.
