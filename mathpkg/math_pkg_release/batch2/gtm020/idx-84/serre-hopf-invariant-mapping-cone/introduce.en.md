This theorem of Serre provides a geometric interpretation of the Hopf invariant: for a map $f: S^{2n-1} \rightarrow S^n$, the Hopf invariant $H([f])$ is determined (up to sign) by the cup product structure in the cohomology of the mapping cone $C_f$. Specifically, if $v$ generates $H^n(C_f)$ and $w$ generates $H^{2n}(C_f)$, then the cup square $v^2$ equals $\pm H([f]) w$. This result connects the homotopy-theoretic Hopf invariant to the cohomology ring of a two-cell complex.
