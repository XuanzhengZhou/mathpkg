The K-theory Hopf invariant $h_f$ assigns an integer to any continuous map $f: S^{2n-1} \to S^n$ using the ring structure of the reduced $K$-theory of the mapping cone $C_f$. Two choices of the element $a_f$ differ by a multiple of $b_f$, and since $b_f^2 = 0$, the relation $a_f^2 = h_f b_f$ uniquely determines $h_f$ independently of the choice. For odd $n$, the Hopf invariant vanishes identically, so the invariant is only interesting when $n$ is even.
