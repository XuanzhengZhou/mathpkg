This proposition asserts that the Hopf invariant depends only on the homotopy class of the map. If two base point preserving maps $f_0, f_1: S^{2n-1} \to S^n$ are connected by a homotopy, their Hopf invariants coincide. This is essential for defining the Hopf invariant as a homotopy invariant on the set $[S^{2n-1}, S^n]$ of homotopy classes of maps.
