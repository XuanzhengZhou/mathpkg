For each $b \in \mathbf{R}^n$, $b \neq 0$, there are two linear functions
$$v_b: \mathbf{R}^n \to \mathbf{R}^n, \quad \pi_b: \mathbf{R}^n \to \mathbf{R}$$
the \text{normal map} and the \text{projection on $b$}, such that for each $x \in \mathbf{R}^n$:
$$x = v_b(x) + \pi_b(x)x,$$
$$\pi_b(x) = \frac{(b|x)}{(b|b)},$$
$$v_b(x) = x - \pi_b(x)b,$$
$$(b|v_b(x)) = 0.$$
