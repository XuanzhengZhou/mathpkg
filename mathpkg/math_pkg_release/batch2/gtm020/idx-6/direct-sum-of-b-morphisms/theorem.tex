Let $u_1: (E_1, p_1, B) \to (E'_1, p'_1, B)$ and $u_2: (E_2, p_2, B) \to (E'_2, p'_2, B)$ be two $B$-morphisms. Then we define the $B$-morphism
$$u_1 \oplus u_2: (E_1 \oplus E_2, q, B) \to (E'_1 \oplus E'_2, q', B)$$
by the relation $(u_1 \oplus u_2)(x_1, x_2) = (u_1(x_1), u_2(x_2))$.

Since $p'_1 u_1(x_1) = p_1(x_1) = p_2(x_2) = p'_2 u_2(x_2)$, this defines a $B$-morphism. This makes $\oplus: \text{Bun}_B \times \text{Bun}_B \to \text{Bun}_B$ into a functor.
