This definition formalizes the relationship between principal bundles with different structure groups. Given a principal $G$-bundle $\xi$ and a closed subgroup $H \subset G$, a restriction of $\xi$ is a principal $H$-bundle $\eta$ whose total space $Y$ embeds as a closed $H$-invariant subspace of the total space $X$ of $\xi$. Conversely, $\xi$ is called a prolongation of $\eta$. In essence, restriction reduces the structure group from $G$ to $H$, while prolongation enlarges the structure group from $H$ to $G$, analogous to the operations introduced in Chapter 5, Section 7.
