For a closed subgroup $H$ of $G$, let $\xi = (X, p, B)$ be a principal $G$-bundle, and $\eta = (Y, q, B)$ a principal $H$-bundle. Let $f: Y \rightarrow f(Y) \subset X$ be a homeomorphism onto a closed subset $f(Y)$ such that
$$
f(ys) = f(y)s
$$
for $y \in Y$ and $s \in H$. Then $\eta$ is called a **restriction** of $\xi$, and $\xi$ is a **prolongation** of $\eta$.
