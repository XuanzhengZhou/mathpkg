Let $H$ be a closed subgroup of $G$, let $\omega_H = (Y_0, q_0, B_H)$ be a universal bundle for $H$, and let $\omega_G = (X_0, p_0, B_G)$ be a universal bundle for $G$. By Corollary (3.2), $\omega_H[G]$ is a numerable principal $G$-bundle over $B_H$. By the classification theorem, there is a principal $G$-bundle morphism $(h_0, f_0): \omega_H[G] \rightarrow \omega_G$, where $f_0^*(\omega_G)$ and $\omega_H[G]$ are isomorphic over $B_H$.

Let $\xi = (X, p, B)$ be a numerable principal $G$-bundle over $B$ with classifying map $f: B \rightarrow B_G$; that is, $f^*(\omega_G)$ and $\xi$ are $B$-isomorphic. Then the restrictions $\eta = (Y, q, B)$ of $\xi$ to principal $H$-bundles are in bijective correspondence with homotopy classes of maps $g: B \rightarrow B_H$ such that $f_0 \circ g$ and $f$ are homotopic. This yields the following commutative diagram up to homotopy:

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
B & \xrightarrow{f} & B_G \\[4pt]
{\scriptstyle g}\downarrow & & \uparrow {\scriptstyle f_0} \\[4pt]
B_H & = & B_H
\end{array}
$$
