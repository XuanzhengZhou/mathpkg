This theorem translates the algebraic problem of reducing the structure group of a principal bundle into a homotopy-theoretic problem using classifying spaces. Given a principal $G$-bundle $\xi$ classified by a map $f: B \to B_G$, its reductions to a closed subgroup $H$ correspond exactly to homotopy classes of lifts of $f$ through the map $f_0: B_H \to B_G$ induced by the inclusion $H \hookrightarrow G$. This is a fundamental result linking the classification theorem with the theory of structure group reduction, and it underlies many applications in algebraic topology and differential geometry such as the reduction of $O(n)$-bundles to $SO(n)$-bundles (orientability) or to $U(n)$-bundles (almost complex structures).
