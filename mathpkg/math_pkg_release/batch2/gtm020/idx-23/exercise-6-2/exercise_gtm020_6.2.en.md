Using the notation of (5.1), suppose there also exists a map $g_0: B_G \rightarrow B_H$ such that $f_0 g_0 \simeq 1$. Give a homotopy criterion for the restriction to always be unique in the sense that the correspondence of Theorem 5.1 yields exactly one restriction up to isomorphism. In particular, under what condition on the homotopy classes of maps $B \rightarrow B_H$ does every principal $G$-bundle admit a unique restriction to $H$?
