This theorem provides a practical criterion for determining when a principal $G$-bundle admits a restriction to a closed subgroup $H$. The restriction exists precisely when the associated bundle with fibre the homogeneous space $G/H$ has a global cross section. The correspondence is constructive: given a cross section $\sigma: B \to \xi[G/H]$, one recovers the restricted $H$-bundle as the preimage of the basepoint coset $eH$ under the associated map $g: X \to G/H$. This generalizes the process described in Chapter 5, Section 7.
