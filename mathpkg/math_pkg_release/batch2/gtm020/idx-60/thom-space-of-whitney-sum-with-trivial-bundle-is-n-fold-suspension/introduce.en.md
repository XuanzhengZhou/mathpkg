The Thom space of the Whitney sum of a vector bundle $\xi$ with the trivial $n$-plane bundle $\theta^n$ is homeomorphic to the $n$-fold suspension of $T(\xi)$. This corollary follows immediately from Proposition 1.3 together with the fact that $\xi \oplus \theta^n \cong \xi \times \mathbf{R}^n$, where $\mathbf{R}^n$ denotes the $n$-dimensional vector bundle over a point. It provides a fundamental stability property of Thom spaces and is essential for the construction of the Thom spectrum in stable homotopy theory.
