Let $\xi$ be a real vector bundle. Let $p_1$ be the projection in the bundle $S(\xi) \rightarrow B(\xi)$, let $p_2(x)$ be the line through $0$ and $x$ for $x \in S(\xi)$, let $q_1(x)$ be the vector $((1 - \|x\|)x, 2\|x\| - 1)$ divided by its length for $x \in D(\xi)$, and let $q_2(x)$ be the line determined by $0$ and $(x, 1 - \|x\|^2)$. Then the restrictions
$$q_1: D(\xi) \setminus \operatorname{im} f_2 \rightarrow S(\xi \oplus \theta^1) \setminus \operatorname{im} f_1$$
and
$$q_2: D(\xi) \setminus \operatorname{im} f_2 \rightarrow P(\xi \oplus \theta^1) \setminus \operatorname{im} f_3$$
are homeomorphisms. These maps induce homeomorphisms
$$D(\xi)/S(\xi) \rightarrow S(\xi \oplus \theta^1)/B(\xi)$$
and
$$D(\xi)/S(\xi) \rightarrow P(\xi \oplus \theta^1).$$
