This example demonstrates that the inclusion of a subbundle into its parent bundle is always a bundle morphism. Specifically, if $(E', p', B')$ is a subbundle of $(E, p, B)$, then the inclusion maps on both total spaces $u: E' \hookrightarrow E$ and base spaces $f: B' \hookrightarrow B$ together form a bundle morphism $(u, f)$. This is an immediate consequence of the subbundle definition and the condition $p'u = fp$, which holds because restrictions of the projection commute with inclusions.
