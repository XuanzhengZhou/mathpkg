A $B$-morphism is a bundle morphism between two bundles over the same base space $B$ where the base map is the identity $1_B$. This terminology is standard in the theory of fibre bundles. The defining condition $p'u = p$ means that the map $u$ on total spaces preserves fibres: it sends the fibre over $b$ in the source bundle into the fibre over $b$ in the target bundle. $B$-morphisms are precisely the morphisms of the category of bundles over a fixed base $B$.
