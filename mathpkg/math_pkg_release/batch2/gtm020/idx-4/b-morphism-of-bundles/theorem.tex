A \text{$B$-morphism} (or bundle morphism over $B$) is a bundle morphism of the form $(u, 1_B): (E, p, B) \rightarrow (E', p', B)$, where both bundles share the same base space $B$ and the base map is the identity.

The condition $p'u = p$ can be expressed by $u(p^{-1}(b)) \subset (p')^{-1}(b)$ for each $b \in B$; that is, $u$ is fibre preserving. The bundle morphisms $u$ over $B$ are exactly the bundle morphisms $(u, 1_B)$.
