The tangent bundle $\tau(\mathbb{R}P^n)$ of real projective $n$-space is described as a quotient of the tangent bundle $\tau(S^n)$ of the $n$-sphere. Under the antipodal identification $b \sim -b$ used to define $\mathbb{R}P^n = S^n / \{\pm 1\}$, the tangent vectors $(b, x) \in \tau(S^n)$ are identified via $(b, x) \sim (-b, -x)$. This construction exhibits $\tau(\mathbb{R}P^n)$ as a natural example of a quotient vector bundle and is closely related to the canonical line bundle over $\mathbb{R}P^n$.
