A bundle morphism is a structure-preserving map between two fibre bundles. It consists of a pair of continuous maps: one between the total spaces and one between the base spaces, such that the diagram commutes. This means the map on total spaces sends fibres over a point $b$ into the fibre over the image $f(b)$ in the base space of the target bundle. Bundle morphisms form the arrows of the category of bundles.
