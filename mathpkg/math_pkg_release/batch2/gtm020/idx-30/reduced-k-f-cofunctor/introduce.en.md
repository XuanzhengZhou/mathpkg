The reduced $K_F$ cofunctor $\tilde{K}_F$ is defined as the kernel of the rank homomorphism $\operatorname{rk}: K_F(X) \to \mathbf{Z}$. This yields a natural splitting $K_F(X) = \tilde{K}_F(X) \oplus \mathbf{Z}$, where $\tilde{K}_F(X)$ encodes the stable isomorphism classes of vector bundles that differ from trivial bundles. For any map $f: Y \to X$, the induced map $\tilde{K}_F(f)$ is simply the restriction of $K_F(f)$.
