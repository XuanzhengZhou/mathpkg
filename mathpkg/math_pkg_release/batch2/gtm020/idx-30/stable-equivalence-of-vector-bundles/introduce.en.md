Stable equivalence, or $s$-equivalence, is an equivalence relation on the set of isomorphism classes of vector bundles over a fixed space $X$. Two vector bundles $\xi$ and $\eta$ are $s$-equivalent if they become isomorphic after adding trivial bundles of possibly different dimensions. In the language of $s$-equivalence, the classical theorems on vector bundles over CW-complexes state that every bundle is $s$-equivalent to one of bounded dimension, and two bundles of sufficiently low dimension are isomorphic precisely when they are $s$-equivalent.
