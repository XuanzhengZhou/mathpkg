This theorem of Atiyah and James reduces the problem of the existence of $\rho(n)$ orthonormal vector fields on the sphere $S^{n-1}$ to a problem in the nonexistence of a coreduction of a certain shunted projective space. The proof uses the construction of vector fields on higher-dimensional spheres, the cross-section criterion (Theorem 9.4), and $S$-duality theory to relate the reducibility condition for large spheres to the coreducibility of a shunted projective space of smaller dimension with the same $\rho$-invariant.
