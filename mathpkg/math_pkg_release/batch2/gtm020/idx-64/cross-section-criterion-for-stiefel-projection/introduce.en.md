This theorem provides a criterion for when the projection from the Stiefel manifold $V_{\rho(n)+1}(\mathbf{R}^n)$ onto the sphere $S^{n-1}$ admits a cross section. The condition is expressed in terms of the existence of a map from $S^{n-1}$ into a shunted real projective space whose composition with the natural projection is of degree $1$. This result is a key step in reducing the problem of the existence of vector fields on spheres (the Radon-Hurwitz problem) to a question about the reducibility of projective spaces.
