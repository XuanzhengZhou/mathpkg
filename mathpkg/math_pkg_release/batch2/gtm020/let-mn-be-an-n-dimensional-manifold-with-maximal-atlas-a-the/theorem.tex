For two $C^r$-maps $f: M \rightarrow N$ and $g: N \rightarrow V$ the relation $\tau(gf) = \tau(g)\tau(f)$ holds. Consequently, $\tau$ is a functor from $C^r$-manifolds and $C^r$-maps to vector bundles and vector bundle morphisms.

The inverse function theorem says that for a $C^r$-map $f: M \rightarrow N$ such that $\tau(f): \tau(M)_z \rightarrow \tau(N)_{f(x)}$ is an isomorphism there are open submanifolds $U$ of $M$ and $V$ of $N$ such that the restriction $f|U: U \rightarrow V$ is a $C^r$-isomorphism and $x \in U$.
