A $\lambda$-semiring is a semiring equipped with operations $\lambda^i$ that formalize the behavior of exterior powers. The axioms require $\lambda^0(x) = 1$, $\lambda^1(x) = x$, and the addition formula $\lambda^k(x+y) = \sum_{i+j=k} \lambda^i(x) \lambda^j(y)$, which reflects the decomposition of exterior powers of a direct sum. This structure appears naturally for isomorphism classes of vector bundles, where $\lambda^i([\xi]) = [\Lambda^i \xi]$. When the underlying semiring is a ring, one obtains a $\lambda$-ring, which provides the algebraic framework for studying Adams operations in K-theory and representation rings.
