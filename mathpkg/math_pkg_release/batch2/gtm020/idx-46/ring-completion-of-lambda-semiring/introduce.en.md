This proposition establishes that the ring completion (Grothendieck group) of a $\lambda$-semiring carries a canonical $\lambda$-ring structure extending the original $\lambda$-operations. The construction uses the power series map $\lambda_t(x) = \sum \lambda^i(x) t^i$, which converts the additive semigroup structure into a multiplicative group of formal power series. The uniqueness of the extension is crucial: it guarantees that the $\lambda$-ring structure on $K(X)$ obtained from $\operatorname{Vect}(X)$ is well-defined and functorial.
