Let $\tilde{R}$ denote the ring completion of the underlying semiring $R$ of the $\lambda$-semiring $(R, \lambda^i)$. There exists a $\lambda$-ring structure on $\tilde{R}$ given by operations $\tilde{\lambda}^i$ such that the natural morphism $\theta: R \to \tilde{R}$ is a $\lambda$-semiring morphism. Moreover, the operations $\tilde{\lambda}^i$ are unique with respect to this property.
