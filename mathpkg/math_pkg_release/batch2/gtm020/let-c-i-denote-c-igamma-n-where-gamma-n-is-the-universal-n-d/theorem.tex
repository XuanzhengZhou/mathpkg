4. Complex Characteristic Classes

Let $R$ denote a commutative ring with a unit. Associated with the ring $R[[t]]$ of formal series in $t$ with coefficients in $R$ are several commutative semigroups. Let $R[[t]]_+$ denote the additive group of $R[[t]]$, let $R[[t]]_x$ denote the multiplicative semigroup of nonzero elements of $R[[t]]$, and let $R[[t]]_x^*$ denote the group of units in $R[[t]]$.

4.1 Notation. Let $H$ be a ring-valued cofunctor. Let $[\text{Vect}_F, H]_\mu$ denote the set of all characteristic classes $\phi: \text{Vect}_F \rightarrow H$ which preserve the following algebraic structures:

(1) For $\mu = (+, +)$, $\phi(\xi \oplus \eta) = \phi(\xi) + \phi(\eta)$.

(2) For $\mu = (+, \times)$, $\phi(\xi \oplus \eta) = \phi(\xi)\phi(\eta)$.

(3) For $\mu = r$, each $\phi(X)$ is required to be a semiring morphism.

4.2 Remarks. The natural morphism $\text{Vect}_F \rightarrow K_F$ defines an injection $[K_F, H]_\mu \rightarrow [\text{Vect}_F, H]_\mu$. In the two sets $[\text{Vect}_F, H]_{+,+}$ and $[\text{Vect}_F, H]_{+,x}$ there is a natural semigroup structure, where $(\phi + \psi)(\xi) = \phi(\xi) + \psi(\xi)$ in the first case and $(\phi\psi)(\xi) = \phi(\xi)\psi(\xi)$ in the second case.
