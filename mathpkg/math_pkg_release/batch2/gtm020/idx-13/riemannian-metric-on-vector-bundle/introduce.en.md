A Riemannian metric (in the real case, $F = \mathbf{R}$) or Hermitian metric (in the complex case, $F = \mathbf{C}$) on a vector bundle $\xi$ is a continuous function $\beta$ defined on the total space of the Whitney sum $\xi \oplus \xi$, taking values in the scalar field $F$, whose restriction to each fibre is an inner product. The metric provides a continuous choice of inner product varying with the base point, generalizing the notion of a Riemannian metric on a smooth manifold to arbitrary vector bundles.
