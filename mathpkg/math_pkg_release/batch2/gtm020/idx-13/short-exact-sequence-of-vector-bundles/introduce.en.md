A short exact sequence of vector bundles is a three-term exact sequence of the form $0 \to \xi \to \eta \to \zeta \to 0$. The bundle at each end is the trivial zero-dimensional bundle. In such a sequence, the first term $\xi$ is isomorphic to the kernel of the second morphism $v$, and the last term $\zeta$ is isomorphic to the cokernel of $v$. Short exact sequences are the fundamental tool for studying extensions and splittings of vector bundles.
