For $s \in G$, the fact that $G$ is abelian means that $s_M: M \rightarrow M$ is a $G$-morphism. By Schur's Lemma (Theorem 4.10), $s_M$ is multiplication by a complex number $\lambda_s$. Since each one-dimensional subspace of $M$ is a $G$-submodule and since $M$ is simple, $M$ is one-dimensional. The last statement is immediate from the relation $\lambda_s \lambda_t x = \lambda_{s+t} x$ for $x \in M$.
