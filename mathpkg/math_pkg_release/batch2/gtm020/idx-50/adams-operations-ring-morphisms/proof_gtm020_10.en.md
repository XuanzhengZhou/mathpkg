Let $\xi^m$ and $\eta^n$ be two vector bundles over $X$ with principal bundles $\alpha$ and $\beta$ respectively. Using the constructions of Section 5(5.4), we have a morphism $\widetilde{\alpha \oplus \beta}: R(U(m) \oplus U(n)) \rightarrow K(X)$ such that $(\widetilde{\alpha \oplus \beta})(\rho_m \oplus 0) = \tilde{\alpha}(\rho_m) = \{\xi\}$ and $(\widetilde{\alpha \oplus \beta})(0 \oplus \rho_n) = \tilde{\beta}(\rho_n) = \{\eta\}$ in $K(X)$ for complex vector bundles. For real vector bundles the morphism $\widetilde{\alpha \oplus \beta}$ is defined from $RO(SO(m) \oplus SO(n))$ to $KO(X)$.

Observe that if $T$ is a maximal torus of $G$ and $T'$ of $G'$, then $T \oplus T'$ is a maximal torus of $G \oplus G'$.

The theorem holds for $K(X)$ and for the subgroup of $KO(X)$ generated by classes of oriented real vector bundles from the relation $\psi^k(\widetilde{\alpha \oplus \beta}) = (\widetilde{\alpha \oplus \beta})\psi^k$ and from the fact that the relations $\psi^k \psi^l = \psi^{kl}$ and the ring morphism property hold in $R(U(m) \oplus U(n))$ and in $RO(SO(m) \oplus SO(n))$ by Theorem 9.5. Finally, the theorem holds for all of $KO(X)$ by Corollary 10.2 and Proposition 10.3, which allow any class to be expressed in terms of oriented bundles.
