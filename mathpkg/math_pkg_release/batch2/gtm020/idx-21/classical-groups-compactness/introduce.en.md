As closed and bounded subsets of the space of matrices (with the operator norm topology), the classical groups $O(k)$, $U(k)$, and $Sp(k)$ are compact topological groups. Compactness follows from the fact that the inner product preserving condition forces each matrix entry to be bounded (by $1$ in the real and complex cases), and the condition defines a closed subset. This compactness is fundamental for the representation theory and the classification of principal bundles with these structure groups.
