The complex spin modules $\Delta$, $\Delta^+$, and $\Delta^-$ are the fundamental irreducible representations of the spin groups arising from the structure of complex Clifford algebras. Each $C_n^c$-module determines a complex $\text{Spin}(n+1)$-module, and the simple modules over $C_{2r}^c = \mathbf{C}(2^r)$ and $C_{2r-1}^c = \mathbf{C}(2^{r-1}) \oplus \mathbf{C}(2^{r-1})$ yield the defining representations for $\text{Spin}(2r+1)$ and $\text{Spin}(2r)$ respectively. The distinction between $\Delta^+$ and $\Delta^-$ for the even case is given by the action of the volume element $i^r e_1 \cdots e_{2r}$, which acts as multiplication by $\pm 1$.
