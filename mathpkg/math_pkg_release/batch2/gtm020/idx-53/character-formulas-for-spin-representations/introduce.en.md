This proposition expresses the fundamental spin representations $\Delta$, $\Delta^+$, and $\Delta^-$ as elements of the representation rings $R\,\text{Spin}(2r+1)$ and $R\,\text{Spin}(2r)$ via explicit character formulas in terms of the variables $\alpha_j$. For the odd case, $\Delta$ is given by the product $\prod_j (\alpha_j^{1/2} + \alpha_j^{-1/2})$, which expands as the sum over all sign choices. For the even case, the two half-spin representations $\Delta^\pm$ are distinguished by the sign of the product $\varepsilon(1) \cdots \varepsilon(r)$. Additionally, the exterior power representations $\lambda_r^\pm$ in $RSO(2r)$ are expressed as sums over ordered index sets with a parity condition.
