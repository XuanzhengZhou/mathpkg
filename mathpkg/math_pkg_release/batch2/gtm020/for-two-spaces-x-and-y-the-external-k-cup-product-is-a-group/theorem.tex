Since $rk(p_X^!(a)p_Y^!(b))$ equals $rk(a)rk(b)$, the external $K$-cup product induces an external $\tilde{K}$-cup product $\tilde{K}(X) \otimes \tilde{K}(Y) \rightarrow \tilde{K}(X \times Y)$ by restriction.

We derive a more precise form of the $\tilde{K}$-cup product by investigating $\tilde{K}$ on products and coproducts.
