Let $\zeta$ be a vector bundle over a space $X$, and let $p_0, p_\infty: \zeta \to \zeta$ be the bundle projections associated to a polynomial clutching map $p(x,z) = a(x)z + b(x)$. Define the following subbundles:
$$
\zeta_+^0 = \operatorname{im} p_0, \qquad
\zeta_+^\infty = \operatorname{im} p_\infty,
$$
$$
\zeta_-^0 = \ker p_0, \qquad
\zeta_-^\infty = \ker p_\infty.
$$
The relation $p(x,z) p_0(x) = p_\infty(x) p(x,z)$ implies that the following restrictions of $p(x,z)$ are defined:
$$
p_+(-,z): \zeta_+^0 \to \zeta_+^\infty, \qquad
p_-(-,z): \zeta_-^0 \to \zeta_-^\infty.
$$
