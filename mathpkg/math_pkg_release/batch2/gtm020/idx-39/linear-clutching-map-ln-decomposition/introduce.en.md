This notation introduces the operator $L^n$ which sends a vector bundle $\zeta$ to its $(n+1)$-fold Whitney sum $L^n(\zeta) = (n+1)\zeta$. When applied to a polynomial clutching map $p$ of degree $\leq n$, the associated linear clutching map $L^n(p)$ induces a direct sum decomposition of $L^n(\zeta)$ into positive and negative parts $L^n(\zeta, p)_+$ and $L^n(\zeta, p)_-$. Proposition 4.6 then implies that the bundle $[L^n(\zeta), L^n(p)]$ decomposes as $[L^n(\zeta, p)_+, z] \oplus [L^n(\zeta, p)_-, 1]$.
