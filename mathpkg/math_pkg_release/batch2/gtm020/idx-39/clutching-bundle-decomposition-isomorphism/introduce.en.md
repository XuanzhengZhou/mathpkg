This proposition shows that the vector bundle over $X \times S^2$ constructed from a polynomial clutching map $p$ decomposes as a direct sum of two simpler bundles. One factor, $[\zeta_+^0, z]$, is obtained by clutching the image subbundle $\zeta_+^0$ with the standard identity clutching map $z$. The other factor, $[\zeta_-^0, 1]$, is obtained by clutching the kernel subbundle $\zeta_-^0$ with the constant clutching map $1$ (which yields a trivial bundle over the second factor). The proof uses the linear homotopies $p_+^t$ and $p_-^t$ to establish the isomorphism.
