This is a general property of projections on vector bundles and is easily seen by considering local cross sections. Let $s_1, \dots, s_n$ be local cross sections of $\zeta$ such that $s_1(x), \dots, s_r(x)$ is a basis of $\ker(p_0)_x$ and $s_{r+1}(x), \dots, s_n(x)$ is a basis of $\operatorname{im}(p_0)_x$. Then $(1 - p_0)s_1(y), \dots, (1 - p_0)s_r(y)$ is a basis of $\ker(p_0)_y$ near $x$, and $p_0 s_{r+1}(y), \dots, p_0 s_n(y)$ is a basis of $\operatorname{im}(p_0)_y$ for $y$ near $x$ in the base space of $\zeta$. The same argument applies to $p_\infty$.
