Let $F$ equal $\mathbf{R}$, $\mathbf{C}$, or $\mathbf{H}$ (the quaternions). For $x = (x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, $y = (y_1, \ldots, y_n) \in F^n$, define the inner product
$$(x \mid y) = x_1 \overline{y}_1 + \cdots + x_n \overline{y}_n$$
and the norm $\|x\| = (x \mid x)^{1/2}$, where $\overline{y}_i$ denotes conjugation in $F$ (for $F = \mathbf{R}$, this is the identity).

The \textbf{orthogonal group} in $k$ dimensions, denoted $O(k)$, is the subgroup of $u \in \mathbf{GL}(k, \mathbf{R})$ such that $(u(x) \mid u(y)) = (x \mid y)$ for all $x, y \in \mathbf{R}^k$.

The \textbf{unitary group} in $k$ dimensions, denoted $U(k)$, is the subgroup of $u \in \mathbf{GL}(k, \mathbf{C})$ such that $(u(x) \mid u(y)) = (x \mid y)$ for all $x, y \in \mathbf{C}^k$.

The \textbf{symplectic group} in $k$ dimensions, denoted $Sp(k)$, is the subgroup of $u \in \mathbf{GL}(k, \mathbf{H})$ such that $(u(x) \mid u(y)) = (x \mid y)$ for all $x, y \in \mathbf{H}^k$.

In each case, $O(k)$, $U(k)$, and $Sp(k)$ are closed and bounded subsets of the space of matrices; therefore, they are compact topological groups.
