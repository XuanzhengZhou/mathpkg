The orthogonal group $O(k)$, unitary group $U(k)$, and symplectic group $Sp(k)$ are the three families of classical compact Lie groups. They consist of invertible linear transformations over the real numbers, complex numbers, and quaternions, respectively, that preserve the standard inner product on $F^k$. These groups arise naturally as the structure groups of vector bundles equipped with a metric, and their compactness follows from the fact that they are closed and bounded in the space of matrices. They play a fundamental role in the classification of vector bundles and in the theory of characteristic classes.
