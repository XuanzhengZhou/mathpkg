This proposition shows that any vector bundle $\xi$ equipped with a metric admits an atlas whose transition functions take values in the appropriate classical compact Lie group ($O(n)$, $U(n)$, or $Sp(n)$). In other words, a metric on $\xi$ provides a reduction of the structure group from the general linear group $\mathbf{GL}(n, F)$ to the corresponding orthogonal, unitary, or symplectic subgroup. This is a fundamental result in the theory of fibre bundles, as it connects Riemannian (or Hermitian) geometry with the classification of vector bundles by their structure groups.
