The conjugate bundle $\overline{\xi}$ of a complex or quaternionic vector bundle $\xi$ is obtained by replacing the scalar multiplication on each fibre by its conjugate. This construction is essential in the theory of characteristic classes, particularly for defining Pontryagin classes and for relating complex vector bundles to their underlying real bundles. The conjugate bundle shares the same transition functions as $\xi$, but conjugated; if $\xi$ has transition functions $g_{i,j}$, then $\overline{\xi}$ has transition functions $\overline{g}_{i,j}$.
