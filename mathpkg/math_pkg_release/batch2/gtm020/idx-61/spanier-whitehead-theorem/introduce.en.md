The Spanier-Whitehead theorem provides a fundamental comparison between ordinary homotopy classes $[X, Y]$ and the stabilized $S$-maps $\{X, Y\}$. Under dimension and connectivity conditions — specifically when $X$ is an $n$-dimensional CW-complex and $Y$ is $r$-connected with $n-1 < 2r-1$ — the natural map from homotopy classes to $S$-maps is a bijection. This means that within the stable range, the stabilization process does not lose any information; every $S$-map is realized by an ordinary map and two maps represent the same $S$-map only if they are already homotopic.
