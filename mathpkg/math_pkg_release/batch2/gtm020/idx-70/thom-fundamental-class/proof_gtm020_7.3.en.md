As with Theorem (1.1), a spectral sequence argument is needed if $\xi$ is not of finite type. This we leave to the reader and give an elementary argument when $\xi$ is of finite type. Statements (1) and (2) hold over open sets $V$ where $\xi|V$ is trivial. Let $U_V$ denote the resulting class.

If statements (1) and (2) hold over $V_1, V_2$, and $V_1 \cap V_2$, they hold over $V_1 \cup V_2$ by a Mayer-Vietoris sequence argument. Let $E_1 = p^{-1}(V_1)$, $E_2 = p^{-1}(V_2)$, $E_3 = p^{-1}(V_1 \cap V_2)$, $E_4 = p^{-1}(V_1 \cup V_2)$, and $E_i^0 = E_i \cap E_0$. Then we have an exact sequence relating the cohomology of these pairs, and the five-lemma implies that the Thom isomorphism holds on $V_1 \cup V_2$. By induction on a finite trivializing cover, the result follows for finite-type bundles. Statement (3) then follows from the cup product structure and the isomorphism already established.
