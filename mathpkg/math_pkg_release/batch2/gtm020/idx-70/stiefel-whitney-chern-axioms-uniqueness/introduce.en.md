This fundamental theorem asserts that the axiomatic characterizations of Stiefel-Whitney classes (for real vector bundles) and Chern classes (for complex vector bundles) uniquely define them. Given any two assignments satisfying the axioms, they must coincide. The proof uses splitting maps to reduce the computation to line bundles, where the axioms force a unique determination.
