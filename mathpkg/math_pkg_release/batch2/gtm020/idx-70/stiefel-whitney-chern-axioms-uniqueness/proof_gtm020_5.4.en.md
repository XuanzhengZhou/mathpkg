Let $w_i$ and $\bar{w}_i$ be two sets of Stiefel-Whitney classes, and let $\xi^n$ be a vector bundle with splitting map $f: B_1 \rightarrow B$. Since $w_1$ is uniquely determined for line bundles by axiom $(\text{SW}_0)$ and $(\text{SW}_1)$, and $f^*$ is a monomorphism in cohomology, it follows that $w_i(\xi)$ and $\bar{w}_i(\xi)$ coincide when pulled back along the splitting map. By the injectivity of $f^*$, they must be equal in the cohomology of $B$. The argument for Chern classes is analogous, using the identity bundle and the canonical line bundle $\lambda_\xi$ over the projective bundle $E(P\xi)$. The equation $\prod_{1 \leq i \leq n} [q^*(x_1(\lambda_i)) + x_1(\lambda_\xi^*)] = 0$ in the cohomology of $E(P\xi)$ provides the relation needed to uniquely determine the Chern classes inductively.
