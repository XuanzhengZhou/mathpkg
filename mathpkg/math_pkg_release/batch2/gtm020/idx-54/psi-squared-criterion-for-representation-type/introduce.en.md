This corollary provides a computational criterion using the Adams operation $\psi^2$ to determine the type of a simple complex $G$-module. Given a simple $G$-module $M$, one computes $\psi^2(M) = M \otimes M - 2\lambda^2(M)$ and checks the multiplicity of the trivial representation. A coefficient of $+1$ indicates $M$ is the complexification of a real representation (real type), $-1$ indicates $M$ is the restriction of a quaternionic representation (quaternionic type), and $0$ indicates $M$ is strictly complex (not isomorphic to its conjugate). This follows from the criterion in Theorem 11.4 combined with formula (11.6).
