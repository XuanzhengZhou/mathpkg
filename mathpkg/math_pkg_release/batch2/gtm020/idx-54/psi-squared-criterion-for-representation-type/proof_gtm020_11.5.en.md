If $M$ is simple, $\psi^2(M) = M \otimes M - 2\lambda^2(M)$ will contain the trivial representation $1$ with scalar multiple $+1$ if $M$ is the complexification of a real $G$-module, with scalar multiple $-1$ if $M$ is the restriction of a quaternionic module, and with scalar multiple $0$ if $M$ and its conjugate $M^*$ are nonisomorphic. This follows immediately from formula (11.6), which expresses the multiplicity of the trivial representation in $\psi^2(M)$ in terms of the invariant bilinear forms on $M$ characterized in Theorem 11.4.
