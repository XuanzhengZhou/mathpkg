This theorem provides a criterion for determining whether a complex $G$-module equipped with a $G$-invariant hermitian form arises from a real or quaternionic representation. For a compact connected Lie group $G$, a complex $G$-module $M$ with a $G$-invariant hermitian form is the complexification of a real $G$-module precisely when it admits a $G$-invariant nondegenerate symmetric bilinear form. Similarly, $M$ is the restriction of a quaternionic $G$-module precisely when it admits a $G$-invariant nondegenerate alternating (symplectic) form. The proof uses an involution $u$ on $M$ satisfying $u^2 = \pm 1$ and decomposes $M$ into eigenspaces.
