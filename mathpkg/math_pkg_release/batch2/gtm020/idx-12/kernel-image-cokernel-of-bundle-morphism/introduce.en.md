For any morphism $u: \xi \to \eta$ of vector bundles over the same base $B$, one can define the kernel, image, and cokernel as set-theoretic constructions on the total spaces. The kernel $\ker u$ consists of all vectors mapped to zero, the image $\operatorname{im} u$ is the set-theoretic image of $u$, and the cokernel $\operatorname{coker} u$ is the quotient of $\eta$ by the image. When $u$ has constant rank on fibres, these constructions yield honest vector bundles, a result established by Theorem 8.2.
