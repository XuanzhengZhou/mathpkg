The morphism $\theta'_{k,l}$ exists since $\tau_{M \oplus N} = \tau_M \oplus \tau_N$ and since
$$(\dim(M \oplus N))\xi_l/\tau_{M \oplus N} \quad \text{and} \quad ((\dim M)\xi_l/\tau_M) \oplus ((\dim N)\xi_l/\tau_N)$$
are isomorphic. This additivity ensures that $\theta'_{k,l}$ is well-defined on the Grothendieck group $N_k$.

Clearly, $\theta'_{k,l}$ is unique — any two such morphisms would have to agree on the generators of $N_k$, and the additivity condition forces them to coincide on all elements.

Finally, for a module $M \in r(N_l)$, we have $c_l \mid \dim M$, so $(\dim M)\xi_l$ is trivial. Consequently, $\theta'_{k,l}(r(N_l)) = 0$, which implies that $\theta'_{k,l}$ factors through the quotient by $r(N_l)$. Thus $\theta_{k,l}$ is uniquely defined by the commutative diagram.
