For $k \leq l$ there is a group morphism
$$\theta'_{k,l}: N_k \to \tilde{KO}(RP^l/RP^k)$$
such that for each $C_k$-module $M$,
$$\theta'_{k,l}(M) = (\dim M)\xi_l/\tau_M$$
where $\tau_M: m\xi_k \to \mathbf{R}^m$ with $m = \dim_{\mathbf{R}} M$, and the trivialization is defined by restricting the orthogonal pairing and factoring it, $E(m\xi_k) \to E(\theta^m)$. Moreover, $\theta'_{k,l}$ is unique and there is a commutative diagram
$$\xymatrix{
N_l \ar[r]^{\theta'_{l,l+1}} \ar[d]_{r} & \tilde{KO}(RP^{l+1}/RP^l) \ar[d] \\
N_k \ar[r]_{\theta'_{k,l}} & \tilde{KO}(RP^l/RP^k)
}$$
where the vertical morphism is a quotient morphism, and $r: N_l \to N_k$ is induced by the inclusion $C_k \to C_l$. The morphism $\theta_{k,l}$ is uniquely defined by factoring $\theta'_{k,l}$ through the quotient.
