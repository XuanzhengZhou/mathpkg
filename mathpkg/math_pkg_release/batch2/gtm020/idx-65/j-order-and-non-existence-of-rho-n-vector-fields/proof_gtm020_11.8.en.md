Suppose that there are $\rho(n)$ vector fields on $S^{n-1}$ for some $n$. Then
$$RP^{m+\rho(m)}/RP^{m-1} = T(m\xi_{\rho(m)})$$
is coreducible for some $m$.

Let $m$ be such that $c_m = c_{m+\rho(m)}$. By the assumption, $m\xi_{\rho(m)}$ is trivial in $J(RP^{m+\rho(m)})$, which implies that the class of $J(\xi_{m+\rho(m)})$ has order dividing $c_m$ in $J(RP^{m+\rho(m)})$. But $c_{m+\rho(m)} > c_m$ for some choice of $m$, i.e., for those $m$ where $\rho(m)$ jumps. This contradicts the hypothesis that the order of $J(\xi_k)$ equals $c_k$ for each $k$.

Therefore, the assumption that $\rho(n)$ vector fields exist on $S^{n-1}$ leads to a contradiction for those $n$ where $\rho(n)$ jumps. Hence no such $\rho(n)$ vector fields can exist for any $n$, completing the proof.
