This corollary of Theorem 3.6 states that the natural projection $q: V_{k+1}(F^n) \to V_k(F^n)$, which forgets the last vector of an orthonormal $(k+1)$-frame, is a fibre map. The fibre over an orthonormal $k$-frame is the unit sphere in the orthogonal complement of the spanned subspace. This result is fundamental to the study of homotopy groups of Stiefel varieties and classical groups.
