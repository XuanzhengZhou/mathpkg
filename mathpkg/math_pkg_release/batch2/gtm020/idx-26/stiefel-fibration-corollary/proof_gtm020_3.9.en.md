**Proof.** This follows directly from Theorem 3.6 (specifically from the local product structure described in formula (3.8)) together with Proposition 1(5.2) which states that locally trivial bundles are fibrations. The local product structure obtained in the proof of Theorem 3.6 exhibits $q^{-1}(O_H^*) \cong O_H^* \times V_1(F^{n-k})$ as a product, where $O_H^* \subset V_k(F^n)$ is an open set. Hence $q$ is locally trivial with fibre $V_1(F^{n-k})$, and therefore a fibre map (fibration).

The fibre $V_1(F^{n-k})$ is the unit sphere in $F^{n-k}$, which is homeomorphic to $S^{n-k-1}$ for $F = \mathbf{R}$, $S^{2n-2k-1}$ for $F = \mathbf{C}$, and $S^{4n-4k-1}$ for $F = \mathbf{H}$.
