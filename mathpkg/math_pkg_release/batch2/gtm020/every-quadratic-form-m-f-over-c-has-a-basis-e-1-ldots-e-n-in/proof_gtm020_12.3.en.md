For the first part, let $C(f)$ be the tensor algebra $T(M) = \sum_{0 \leq k} T^k(M)$ modulo the ideal generated by $x \otimes x - f(x,x)1$. Here $T^k(M)$ is the $k$-fold tensor product of $M$. Let $\theta$ be the composition of the injection $M \rightarrow T^1(M) \subset T(M)$ and the projection $T(M) \rightarrow C(f)$. If $u: M \rightarrow A$ is a linear function with $u(x)^2 = f(x,x)1$, then $u$ factors as $M \rightarrow T(M) \xrightarrow{u'} A$ and as $M \xrightarrow{\theta} C(f) \xrightarrow{u'} A$. Moreover, $u'$ is unique because im $\theta$ generates $C(f)$.

Finally, the uniqueness of $(C(f),\theta)$ follows from the existence of two algebra morphisms $u: C(f) \rightarrow C(f)'$ and $v: C(f)' \rightarrow C(f)$ with $\theta' = u\theta$ and $\theta = v\theta'$. This implies that $\theta = vu\theta$ and $\theta' = uv\theta'$. From the uniqueness property of factorizations, $1 = vu$ and $1 = uv$. This proves the theorem.
