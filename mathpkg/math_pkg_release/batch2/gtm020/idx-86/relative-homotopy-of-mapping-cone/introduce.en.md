This proposition compares the relative homotopy and homology groups of three pairs of spaces — $(T_n, S^{2n-1})$, $(\Omega F_n, S^{2n-1})$, and $(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}, S^{2n-1})$ — all localized at an odd prime $p$. The boundary map $\partial$ from $\pi_{2np-2}(T_n, S^{2n-1})$ being injective forces the Hurewicz homomorphisms in the diagram to be isomorphisms of cyclic $p$-groups. The conclusion is that the map $j_n \colon T_n \to \Omega F_n$ induces an isomorphism on the first nontrivial relative homotopy group, which is a crucial step in the wedge decomposition of $S\Omega F_n$.
