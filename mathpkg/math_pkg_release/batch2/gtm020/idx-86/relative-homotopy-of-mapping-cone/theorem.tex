Assume $n > 1$. Consider the comparison diagram of Hurewicz homomorphisms in degree $2np-2$:

$$H_{2np-2}(T_n) \rightsquigarrow H_{2np-2}(T_n, S^{2n-1}) \rightsquigarrow \pi_{2np-2}(T_n, S^{2n-1})_{(p)}$$

$$H_{2np-2}(\Omega F_n) \rightsquigarrow H_{2np-2}(\Omega F_n, S^{2n-1}) \rightsquigarrow \pi_{2np-2}(\Omega F_n, S^{2n-1})_{(p)}$$

$$H_{2np-2}(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}) \rightsquigarrow H_{2np-2}(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}, S^{2n-1}) \rightsquigarrow \pi_{2np-2}(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}, S^{2n-1})_{(p)}$$

Since $\partial \colon \pi_{2np-2}(T_n, S^{2n-1})_{(p)} \to \pi_{2np-3}(S^{2n-1})_{(p)}$ is a monomorphism, the Hurewicz maps are monomorphisms of cyclic groups of order $p$ and hence are isomorphisms. Consequently the induced map $j_{n*} \colon \pi_{2np-2}(T_n, S^{2n-1})_{(p)} \to \pi_{2np-2}(\Omega F_n, S^{2n-1})_{(p)}$ is an isomorphism.
