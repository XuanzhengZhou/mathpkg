The second statement, $\Delta \circ E^2 = u_p$, is immediate from the definitions. For the first statement, recall that $H_{2np-2}(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}, \mathbf{Z}_{(p)}) = \mathbf{Z}/p$ by (2.4). The following sequence of homology groups over $\mathbf{Z}_{(p)}$ is exact:
\[
\mathbf{Z}_{(p)} = H_{2np-2}(\Omega^3 S^{2np+1}) \to \mathbf{Z}_{(p)} = H_{2np-2}(\Omega F_n) \to H_{2np-2}(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}) = \mathbf{Z}/p.
\]
From this exact sequence we see that the map $H_{2np-2}(\Omega^3 S^{2np+1}) = \mathbf{Z}_{(p)} \to \mathbf{Z}_{(p)} = H_{2np-2}(\Omega F_n)$ is multiplication by $p$ up to a unit. Since $E^2$ and $g_n$ induce isomorphisms on $H_{2np-2}$ over $\mathbf{Z}_{(p)}$, it follows that $H_{2np-2}(u_p)$ is multiplication by $p$. Hence $u_p$ is of degree $p$.
