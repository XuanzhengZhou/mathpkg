The map $j_n \colon T_n \to \Omega F_n$ defines, by loop multiplication, a map
$$g_m \colon (T_n)^m \to \Omega F_n,$$
and this map is a surjection in mod $p$ homology in degrees below a certain range. Let $Sf$ denote the suspension of the commutator map
$$f \colon T_n \wedge T_n \to \Omega F_n.$$
The homotopy class of $Sf$ lies in $\pi_{4np-4}(S^{2n-1})$, and the suspension morphism from odd to even spheres is injective on the odd prime components:
$$\pi_{4np-4}(S^{2n-1})_{(p)} \hookrightarrow \pi_{4np-3}(S^{2n})_{(p)}.$$

Hence the class of $Sf$ is zero, and the space $S(T_n \wedge T_n)$ has the homotopy type of a wedge of four spheres. The map $\bar{h}$ restricts to a mod $p$ homology isomorphism
$$h \colon ST_n \vee X \to S\Omega F_n,$$
where $X$ is a subwedge of spheres of $\bigvee_{m \ge 2} S(\bigwedge_m T_n)$. After localizing at $p$, the map $h$ is the desired homotopy equivalence, which proves the proposition.

The homology of $S\Omega F_n$ determines which dimensions the spheres appear in, namely of the form $(2np-2)i+1$ for $i \ge 2$ and $(2np-2)i+(2n-1)+1$ for $i \ge 1$.
