The collapsing construction takes a vector bundle $\xi$ over $X$ together with a trivialization $t$ over a subset $A$, and produces a new vector bundle $\xi/t$ over the quotient space $X/A$. The morphism $u$ is a fibrewise isomorphism away from $A$, while over $A$ it identifies fibres via $t$. The isomorphism $r$ identifies the fibre over the basepoint $* \in X/A$ with $F^n$, encoding how the trivialization collapses all fibres above $A$ to a single fibre.
