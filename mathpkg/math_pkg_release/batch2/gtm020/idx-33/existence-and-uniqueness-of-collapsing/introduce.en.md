This proposition establishes the fundamental existence and uniqueness properties of the collapsing construction. A collapsing exists precisely when the trivialization can be extended to an open neighborhood of the subset $A$. Furthermore, any two collapsings of the same data are canonically isomorphic via a unique morphism. The final statement shows that the collapsing construction is essentially surjective: every vector bundle over the quotient space $X/A$ can be represented as a collapsing of some bundle over $X$.
