Let $\xi$ be a vector bundle over $X$, let $A \subset X$ be a closed subset, and let $t$ be a trivialization of $\xi$ over $A$. The collapsing $(\xi/t, u, r)$ of $\xi$ with respect to $t$ exists if and only if there exists a trivialization $t'$ of $\xi$ over an open set $U$ containing $A$ which extends $t$. If $((\xi/t)', u', r')$ is a second collapsing of $\xi$ with respect to $t$, there exists a morphism $v: \xi/t \rightarrow (\xi/t)'$ such that the diagram

\[
\begin{CD}
\xi @>u>> \xi/t \\
@| @VVvV \\
\xi @>u'>> (\xi/t)'
\end{CD}
\]

is commutative, and $v$ restricts to the identity on the fibre over the basepoint via the isomorphisms $r$ and $r'$. Moreover, $v$ is unique with respect to this property and $v$ is an isomorphism. Finally, every vector bundle on $X/A$ is isomorphic to $\xi/t$ for some bundle $\xi$ on $X$ with a trivialization $t$ over $A$.

This proposition applies to each CW-pair $(X, A)$, where $X$ is a CW-complex with subcomplex $A$; in this case $A$ is a neighborhood deformation retract.
