If either $\alpha: \xi_0 \rightarrow \xi_1$ is an isomorphism over $X$ or $\beta: \eta_0 \rightarrow \eta_1$ over $Y$, the sequences $(\alpha, \beta)$, $(\alpha^*, \beta)$, and $(-\alpha, \beta^*)$ above are exact over $X \times Y$, and $\alpha\beta$ is an isomorphism over $X \times Y$. Consequently, if $\alpha = 0$ in $L(X, A)$ or $\beta = 0$ in $L(Y, B)$, then $\alpha\beta = 0$ in $L(X \times Y, (X \times B) \cup (A \times Y))$. This proves the proposition.
