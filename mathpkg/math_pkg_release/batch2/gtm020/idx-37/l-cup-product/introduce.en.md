The $L$-cup product is the external product operation on the $L$-groups $L(X, A)$, defined for pairs of spaces. Given difference isomorphism classes $[\alpha: \xi_0 \to \xi_1] \in L(X, A)$ and $[\beta: \eta_0 \to \eta_1] \in L(Y, B)$, their $L$-cup product is the class of the tensor product morphism $\alpha\beta$ in $L(X \times Y, (A \times Y) \cup (X \times B))$. This operation is well-defined by Proposition 6.2, which shows compatibility with the equivalence relation on $S(X, A)$. There is an analogous $K$-cup product $K(X, A) \otimes K(Y, B) \to K(X \times Y, (A \times Y) \cup (X \times B))$.
