Let $M_G(X, Y)$ denote the subspace of $G$-morphisms $X \rightarrow Y$. Since the composition of $G$-morphisms is a $G$-morphism, the class of $G$-spaces and $G$-morphisms forms a category denoted $\mathbf{sp}_G$.

Two elements $x, x' \in X$ in a $G$-space are called $G$-equivalent provided there exists $s \in G$ with $xs = x'$.
