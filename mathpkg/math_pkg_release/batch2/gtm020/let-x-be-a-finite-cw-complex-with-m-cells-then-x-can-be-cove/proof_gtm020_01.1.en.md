We use induction on $m$. For $m = 1$, $X$ is a point, and the statement is clearly true. Let $C$ be a cell of maximal dimension. Then $X$ equals a subcomplex $A$ of $m - 1$ cells with $C$ attached by a map $u_C$. There are $V_1', \ldots, V_{m-1}'$ contractible open sets in $A$ which prolong by (2.3) and (2.4) to contractible open sets $V_1, \ldots, V_{m-1}$ of $X$ which cover $A$. If $V_m$ denotes $C = u_C(\text{int } B^n)$, then $V_1, \ldots, V_m$ forms an open contractible covering of $X$.
