A $G$-morphism is a structure-preserving map between two $G$-modules, combining $F$-linearity with $G$-equivariance. These morphisms form a category whose objects are $G$-modules, and the usual constructions of kernel, image, and cokernel are available within this category. The condition $f(sx) = sf(x)$ ensures that the map respects the group action, making $G$-morphisms the natural homomorphisms in representation theory.
