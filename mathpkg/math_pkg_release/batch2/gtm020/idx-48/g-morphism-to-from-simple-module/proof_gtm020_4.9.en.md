**Proof.** For the first statement, let $f: M \to N$ be a $G$-morphism with $M$ simple. The kernel $\ker f$ is a $G$-submodule of $M$. Since $M$ is simple, either $\ker f = M$ or $\ker f = 0$. If $\ker f = M$, then $f$ is zero. If $\ker f = 0$, then $f$ is injective, hence a monomorphism.

For the second statement, let $g: L \to M$ be a $G$-morphism with $M$ simple. The image $\operatorname{im} g$ is a $G$-submodule of $M$. By simplicity of $M$, either $\operatorname{im} g = 0$ or $\operatorname{im} g = M$. If $\operatorname{im} g = 0$, then $g$ is zero. If $\operatorname{im} g = M$, then $g$ is surjective, hence an epimorphism. $\square$
