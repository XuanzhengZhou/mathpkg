A $G$-module is a vector space equipped with a continuous linear action of a topological group $G$. This generalizes the notion of a group representation to the setting where $G$ is a topological group, with $F = \mathbf{R}$, $\mathbf{C}$, or $\mathbf{H}$ as the scalar field. The action associates to each group element $s \in G$ a linear automorphism $s_M$ of the module, satisfying the natural composition and inversion relations. G-modules form the fundamental objects of study in the representation theory of compact Lie groups.
