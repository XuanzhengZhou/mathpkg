Schur's Lemma is a cornerstone of representation theory. It states that any $G$-morphism between simple $G$-modules is either zero or an isomorphism, and that when $F$ is algebraically closed, every endomorphism of a simple $G$-module is a scalar multiple of the identity. The first part follows from the fact that kernels and images of $G$-morphisms are $G$-submodules; the second uses the existence of an eigenvalue in an algebraically closed field.
