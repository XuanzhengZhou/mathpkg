This theorem computes the reduced complex K-theory of spheres, a fundamental result that follows from the Bott periodicity theorem. The odd-dimensional spheres $S^{2n+1}$ have trivial reduced K-theory, while the even-dimensional spheres $S^{2n}$ have reduced K-theory isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}$, generated by the Bott class $\beta_{2n}$ with $\beta_{2n}^2 = 0$. The natural map from a product of 2-spheres to the $2n$-sphere induces an injection on K-theory, and the image of the generator $\beta_{2n}$ is given by a product of the reduced line bundle classes $a_i = \{\zeta_i\} - 1$.
