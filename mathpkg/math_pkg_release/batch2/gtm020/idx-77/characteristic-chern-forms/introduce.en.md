Characteristic Chern forms are differential forms obtained by evaluating the Chern polynomials (invariant polynomials) on the curvature matrix of a connection on a vector bundle. The transformation law $K' = AKA^{-1}$ for curvature under changes of local frame, together with the invariance property $c_q(AXA^{-1}) = c_q(X)$, ensures that $c_q(E, \nabla)$ is a globally well-defined $2q$-form on the base manifold. These forms are the key ingredients in the Chern-Weil construction of characteristic classes: they are closed, and their de Rham cohomology classes are independent of the choice of connection.
