Let $E$ be a smooth $n$-dimensional complex vector bundle over a smooth manifold $M$, and let $\nabla$ be a connection on $E$ with local curvature form $K$. The \textbf{characteristic Chern forms} $c_q(E, \nabla)$ are the $2q$-forms on $M$ defined by
$$
c_q(E, \nabla) = \frac{1}{(2\pi i)^q} c_q(K),
$$
where $c_q$ is the $q$-th Chern polynomial evaluated on the curvature matrix $K$.

More generally, for any invariant polynomial $\phi(x) \in \text{Inv}(n) = k[x_{1,1}, \ldots, x_{n,n}]^{\text{GL}_n(k)}$, the \textbf{characteristic form} $\phi(K)$ is obtained by substituting the curvature matrix $K$ into $\phi$. The relation $K' = AKA^{-1}$ under a change of local frame guarantees that $c_q(K)$ and $\phi(K)$ are well-defined global differential forms on $M$.
