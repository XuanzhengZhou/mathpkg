The de Rham Chern class $c_q(E)_{DR}$ is the cohomology class represented by the Chern form $c_q(E, \nabla)$ in de Rham cohomology. Since Chern forms for different connections are cohomologous (as established by homotopy invariance), $c_q(E)_{DR}$ is independent of the choice of connection. This provides the bridge between the Chern-Weil differential-geometric construction and the topological Chern classes: under the coefficient morphism $H^{2q}(M, \underline{\mathbb{Z}}) \to H^{2q}_{DR}(M, \underline{\mathbb{C}})$, the integral Chern class maps to the de Rham Chern class.
