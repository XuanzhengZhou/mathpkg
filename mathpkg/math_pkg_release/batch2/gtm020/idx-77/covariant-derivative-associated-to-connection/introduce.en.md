The covariant derivative $\nabla_\xi s$ is an equivalent way to describe a connection on a vector bundle. Instead of viewing a connection as a map $\nabla: \Gamma(E) \to \Gamma(T^*(M) \otimes E)$, the covariant derivative expresses how to differentiate a section $s$ along a vector field $\xi$. It satisfies natural $A^0(M)$-linearity in the vector field argument and a Leibniz rule in the section argument. This formulation is particularly well-suited for discussing metric compatibility and the Levi-Civita connection.
