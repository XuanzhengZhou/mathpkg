This fundamental theorem describes the structure of the algebra of invariant polynomials $\text{Inv}(n) = k[x_{1,1},\ldots,x_{n,n}]^{\text{GL}_n(k)}$ over a field $k$ of characteristic zero. It states that this algebra is freely generated as a polynomial algebra by the $n$ Chern polynomials $c_1(x),\ldots,c_n(x)$, which are defined via the characteristic polynomial $\det(I + Xt)$. When $X$ is diagonalizable, these generators reduce to the ordinary elementary symmetric functions in the eigenvalues. This result underpins the Chern-Weil construction of characteristic classes.
