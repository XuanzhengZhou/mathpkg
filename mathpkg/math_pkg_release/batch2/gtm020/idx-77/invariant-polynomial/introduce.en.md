An invariant polynomial is a polynomial in the entries of an $n \times n$ matrix that remains unchanged under conjugation by invertible matrices. These polynomials arise naturally in differential geometry when studying curvature: the local curvature form $K$ transforms by conjugation $K' = AKA^{-1}$ under changes of local frame, so evaluating an invariant polynomial on $K$ yields a globally well-defined differential form on the manifold. The algebra of invariant polynomials $\text{Inv}(n)$ is freely generated by the elementary symmetric functions (equivalently, the Chern polynomials) over a field of characteristic zero.
