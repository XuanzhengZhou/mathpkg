A homotopy of connections is a connection on the product bundle $E \times [0,1]$ over $M \times [0,1]$ that interpolates between two given connections $\nabla_0$ and $\nabla_1$ on $E$. This notion is crucial for proving that characteristic forms from different connections determine the same de Rham cohomology class: if $\phi(K)$ is a closed characteristic form on $M \times [0,1]$ from the homotopy connection, its restrictions to $M \times \{0\}$ and $M \times \{1\}$ are $\phi(K_0)$ and $\phi(K_1)$, which are therefore cohomologous.
