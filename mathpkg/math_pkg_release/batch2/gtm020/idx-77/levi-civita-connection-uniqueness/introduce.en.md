The fundamental theorem of Riemannian geometry states that on a pseudo-riemannian manifold $(M, g)$, there exists a unique connection on the tangent bundle that is both symmetric (torsion-free) and compatible with the metric. This connection is called the Levi-Civita connection. The uniqueness proof uses the Koszul formula, which expresses $g(\xi, \nabla_\zeta \eta)$ as a combination of derivatives of the metric and Lie brackets of vector fields. The six-term Koszul formula also provides an explicit construction of the connection.
