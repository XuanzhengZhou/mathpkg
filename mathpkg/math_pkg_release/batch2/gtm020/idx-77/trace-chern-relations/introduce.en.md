These are the Newton identities relating power sums $\text{Tr}(X^q)$ to the elementary symmetric functions $c_q(x)$. The generating function relation $-t \frac{d}{dt} \log(\det(I + Xt)) = \sum \text{Tr}(X^q)(-t)^q$ compactly encodes all relations. For $n=2$, these specialize to $\text{Tr}(X^2) = \text{Tr}(X)^2 - 2\det(X)$, which follows from the Hamilton-Cayley theorem. These identities are essential for expressing characteristic forms in terms of traces of powers of the curvature.
