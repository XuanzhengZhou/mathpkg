Assume $k$ is a field of characteristic zero. The traces $\text{Tr}(X^q)$ and the Chern polynomials $c_q(x)$ are related by
$$
-t \frac{d}{dt} \log(\det(I + Xt)) = \sum_{q \geq 1} \text{Tr}(X^q)(-t)^q.
$$
In particular:
$$
c_1(X) = \text{Tr}(X),
$$
$$
\text{Tr}(X^2) = c_1(X)^2 - 2c_2(X).
$$
For $n = 2$, the Hamilton-Cayley theorem gives $X^2 - \text{Tr}(X)X + \det(X)I = 0$, and taking the trace yields
$$
\text{Tr}(X^2) = \text{Tr}(X)^2 - 2\det(X),
$$
which is consistent with the above relations.
