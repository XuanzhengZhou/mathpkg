3. S-Duality and the Atiyah Duality Theorem

We consider maps $u: X \wedge X' \rightarrow S^n$ which are referred to as $n$-pairings. Such a map defines two group morphisms $u_Z: \{Z, X'\} \rightarrow \{X \wedge Z, S^n\}$ and $u_Z': \{Z, X\} \rightarrow \{Z \wedge X', S^n\}$, by the relations $u_Z(\{f\}) = \{u(1 \wedge f)\}$ and $u_Z(\{g\}) = \{u(g \wedge 1)\}$.
