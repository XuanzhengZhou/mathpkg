A fundamental result in characteristic class theory is the computation of the cohomology ring of the infinite complex Grassmannian $G_n(C^\infty)$, which classifies $n$-dimensional complex vector bundles. The theorem states that the integer cohomology ring is a polynomial ring freely generated by the Chern classes $c_1, \ldots, c_n$ of the universal bundle $\gamma_n$. This establishes the Chern classes as algebraically independent generators and justifies the study of characteristic classes as elements of a polynomial algebra.
