A splitting map for a vector bundle is a map from some space $X$ to the base of the bundle such that the pullback splits as a direct sum of line bundles. The splitting principle reduces the study of characteristic classes of general vector bundles to the study of line bundles. For the canonical $n$-plane bundle $\gamma_n$ over the complex Grassmannian $G_n(C^\infty)$, the classifying map $h_n$ of the $n$-fold product of the canonical line bundle provides a natural splitting map.
