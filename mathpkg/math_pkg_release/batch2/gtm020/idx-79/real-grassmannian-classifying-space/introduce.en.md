The infinite real Grassmannian $G_n(R^\infty)$ serves as the classifying space $BO(n)$ for $n$-dimensional real vector bundles. The product of $n$ copies of $RP^\infty$ is identified with the classifying space $B(Z_2)^n$ of the elementary abelian 2-group $(Z_2)^n$. The inclusion of diagonal matrices into the orthogonal group $O(n)$ induces the splitting map at the level of classifying spaces, providing a geometric interpretation of the splitting principle.
