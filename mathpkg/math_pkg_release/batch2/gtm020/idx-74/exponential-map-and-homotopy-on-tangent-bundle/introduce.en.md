The exponential map $\exp_x$ is defined on the tangent space at each point $x$ of a closed connected Riemannian manifold $M^n$, sending a tangent vector $v$ to the point reached at time $t=1$ along the unique geodesic through $x$ with initial velocity $v$. By rescaling the metric, one obtains a homotopy $h_t$ that maps the disk bundle $D(\tau)$ diffeomorphically onto a closed tubular neighborhood of the diagonal $\Delta$ in $M \times M$ for each $t \in [0,1]$. This construction is the key geometric input for defining the Thom class of the tangent bundle and relating cohomology of $D(\tau), S(\tau)$ to cohomology of $(M \times M, M \times M - \Delta)$.
