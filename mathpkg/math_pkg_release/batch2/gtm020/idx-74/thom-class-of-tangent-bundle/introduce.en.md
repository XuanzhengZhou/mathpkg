For a closed connected manifold $M^n$ with tangent bundle $\tau$, the Thom class $U \in H^n(D(\tau), S(\tau))$ is defined using a coefficient ring $R$ that contains $\mathbb{Z}_2$ when $M$ is non-orientable. The exponential map construction from (6.1) provides an excision isomorphism $(h_t)^*$ between the cohomology of $(M \times M, M \times M - \Delta)$ and $(D(\tau), S(\tau))$, independent of $t$. Pulling back the Thom class via this isomorphism and then applying $j^*$ yields the fundamental class $U_M \in H^n(M \times M)$, which plays a central role in the duality theory of manifolds.
