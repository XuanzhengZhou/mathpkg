Let $R$ denote a ring which we assume contains $\mathbb{Z}_2$ if $M$ is not oriented; require that all cohomology groups have their coefficients in $R$.

Let $U_r$ or $U$ denote the Thom class of $\tau$, where $U \in H^n(D(\tau), S(\tau))$. By excision,
$$(h_t)^*: H^*(M \times M, M \times M - \Delta) \to H^*(D(\tau), S(\tau))$$
is an isomorphism, which is independent of $t$.

Define $U'_r = ((h_t)^*)^{-1}(U_r) \in H^n(M \times M, M \times M - \Delta)$, and
$$U_M = j^* U'_r \in H^n(M \times M),$$
where $j: (M \times M, \emptyset) \to (M \times M, M \times M - \Delta)$ is the inclusion.
