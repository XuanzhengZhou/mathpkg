The map $u \times 1_F: X \times F \to X' \times F$ is a $G$-morphism because $u$ is a $G$-morphism and the action on $F$ is unchanged. Passing to the orbit space yields $u_F: X_F \to X'_F$, which together with $f: B \to B'$ satisfies $p'_F \circ u_F = f \circ p_F$, i.e., $(u_F, f)$ is a bundle morphism. Composition of principal bundle morphisms $(u, f)$ and $(v, g)$ yields $(v \circ u, g \circ f)$, and the associated map on quotients satisfies $(v \circ u)_F = v_F \circ u_F$. Thus the assignment preserves composition and identities, defining a functor.
