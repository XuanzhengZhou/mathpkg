A numerable covering is a covering of a topological space for which there exists a subordinate partition of unity. This notion is central to Dold's theory of local-to-global properties of bundles. A property that holds locally over a numerable covering can often be extended globally. The existence of a numerable covering imposes a paracompactness-like condition on the space.
