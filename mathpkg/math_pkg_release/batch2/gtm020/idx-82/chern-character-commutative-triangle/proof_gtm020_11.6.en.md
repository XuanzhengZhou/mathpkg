This theorem follows from (11.5) by comparing $R(G)$ with $R(T)$ and $H^*(B_G, \mathbf{Q})$ with $H^*(B_T, \mathbf{Q})$. The statement in (11.5) identifies $H^*(B_G, \mathbf{Q})$ with the Weyl-invariant subring of $H^*(B_T, \mathbf{Q})$. Similarly, $R(G)$ embeds as the Weyl-invariant elements of $R(T)$. The Chern character provides the isomorphism between the two descriptions, and the commutativity of the triangle follows from the naturality of these identifications and the definition of the Chern character on line bundles.
