This theorem establishes that the section extension property is both hereditary (it passes to open subsets) and local-to-global (if it holds over each set in a numerable covering, it holds globally). This is the foundational result in Dold's theory, used as the base case to prove that other bundle properties such as being a fibration, being a weak fibration, and being a fibre homotopy equivalence also satisfy local-to-global principles.
