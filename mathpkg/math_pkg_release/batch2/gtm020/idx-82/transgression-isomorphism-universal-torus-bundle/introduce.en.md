This proposition states that for the universal $T^r$-bundle over $(CP^\infty)^r$, the transgression map from $H^1(T^r, \mathbf{Z})$ to $H^2(B(\gamma), \mathbf{Z})$ is an isomorphism. For $r = 1$, the free generator of $H^1(S^1, \mathbf{Z})$ corresponds to the canonical line bundle over $CP^\infty$, and its first Chern class generates $H^2(CP^\infty, \mathbf{Z})$. For general $r$, the transgression is the product of $r$ copies of the $r = 1$ case, hence remains an isomorphism.
