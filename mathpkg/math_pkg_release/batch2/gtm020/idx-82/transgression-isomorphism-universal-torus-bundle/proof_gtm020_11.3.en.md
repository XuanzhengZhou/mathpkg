For $r = 1$, the free generator of $H^1(S^1, \mathbf{Z})$ corresponds to the canonical line bundle over $CP^\infty$, and its first Chern class is the free generator of $H^2(CP^\infty, \mathbf{Z})$. For general $r$, the transgression $\tau_\gamma$ is the product of $r$ transgressions, each for the case $r = 1$, and consequently $\tau_\gamma$ is an isomorphism.
