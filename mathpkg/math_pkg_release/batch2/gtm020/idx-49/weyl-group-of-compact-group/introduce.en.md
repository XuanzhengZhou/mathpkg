The Weyl group $W(G) = N_G(T)/T$ of a compact Lie group $G$ is a finite group that acts on the maximal torus $T$ by conjugation. It is a fundamental invariant governing the representation theory of $G$: the representation ring $R(G)$ is isomorphic to the ring of $W(G)$-invariant elements of $R(T)$. For the classical groups, the Weyl groups are the familiar symmetric groups and signed permutation groups. The Weyl group is independent of the choice of maximal torus up to isomorphism, since all maximal tori are conjugate.
