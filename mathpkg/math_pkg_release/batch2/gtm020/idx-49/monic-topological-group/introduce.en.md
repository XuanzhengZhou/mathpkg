A topological group is called monic if it admits a single element whose powers are dense. The term "monic" alludes to the group being generated (topologically) by one element. Theorem 8.5 establishes that every torus is monic -- a fact that plays a crucial role in the proof that maximal tori in a compact Lie group are all conjugate.
