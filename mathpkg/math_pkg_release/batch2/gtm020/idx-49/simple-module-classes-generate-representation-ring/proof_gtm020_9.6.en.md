**Proof.** By the semisimplicity theorem, every $G$-module $M$ decomposes as a finite direct sum $M = \bigoplus_i M_i$ where each $M_i$ is a simple $G$-module. Hence $[M] = \sum_i [M_i]$ in $\mathbf{M}_F(G)$. Since every element of $R_F(G)$ is of the form $[M] - [N]$ for $G$-modules $M$ and $N$, it follows that $R_F(G)$ is generated as an abelian group by the classes $[M_i]$ of simple $G$-modules. $\square$
