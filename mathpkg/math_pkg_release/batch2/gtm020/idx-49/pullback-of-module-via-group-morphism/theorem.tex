Let $u: G \rightarrow H$ be a morphism of topological groups (continuous and preserving the group structure). For an $H$-module $M$, the pullback $u^*(M)$ is the underlying vector space of $M$ with $G$-module structure given by $sx = u(s)_M(x)$ for $s \in G$ and $x \in M$.

For $H$-modules $M$ and $N$ we have:
$$u^*(M \oplus N) = u^*(M) \oplus u^*(N), \quad u^*(M \otimes N) = u^*(M) \otimes u^*(N), \quad u^*(\Lambda^i M) = \Lambda^i u^*(M).$$

Consequently, $u^*$ defines a $\lambda$-semiring morphism $\mathbf{M}_F(u): \mathbf{M}_F(H) \rightarrow \mathbf{M}_F(G)$ and a unique $\lambda$-ring morphism $R_F(u): R_F(H) \rightarrow R_F(G)$ making the natural diagram commute.
