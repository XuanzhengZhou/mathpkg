Let $M$ be a $G$-module over $F = \mathbf{R}$ or $\mathbf{C}$ where $G$ is a compact topological group. If $M$ admits a $G$-invariant hermitian form $\beta$, then every $G$-submodule $L \subset M$ has a $G$-invariant complement $L'$, given by $L' = \{y \in M : \beta(x, y) = 0 \text{ for all } x \in L\}$. Consequently, $M$ is semisimple, i.e., a direct sum of simple $G$-modules.
