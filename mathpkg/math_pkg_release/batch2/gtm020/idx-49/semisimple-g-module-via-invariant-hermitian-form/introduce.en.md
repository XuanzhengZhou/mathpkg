This is the fundamental result underlying the representation theory of compact groups. Given a $G$-invariant hermitian form on a $G$-module $M$, the orthogonal complement of any submodule is again a $G$-submodule, yielding a direct sum decomposition. By induction, every finite-dimensional $G$-module decomposes as a direct sum of simple (irreducible) submodules. This generalizes the complete reducibility of representations of finite groups to compact Lie groups.
