This theorem is a key technical result in the structure theory of compact Lie groups. It states that any element commuting with all elements of a torus can be adjoined to that torus within a larger torus. As a corollary, maximal tori are precisely the maximal abelian closed connected subgroups. The proof uses the fact that the closed subgroup generated by $T$ and $s$ has identity component a torus, and the quotient by this component is a finite group, from which a generator argument yields the desired extension.
