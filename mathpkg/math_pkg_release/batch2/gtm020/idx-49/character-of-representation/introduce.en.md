The character of a finite-dimensional representation is the trace of the representing linear transformations. Characters are central tools in representation theory because they determine representations up to isomorphism (over $\mathbf{C}$) and behave well under direct sum and tensor product. For compact groups, characters of simple modules form an orthonormal basis for the space of class functions, and the character ring $\operatorname{ch}_F G$ is isomorphic to the representation ring $R_F(G)$.
