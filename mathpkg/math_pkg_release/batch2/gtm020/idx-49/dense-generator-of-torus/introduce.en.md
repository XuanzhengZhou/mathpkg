This theorem is a classical result about torus topology, closely related to Kronecker's theorem on diophantine approximation. When the coordinates of a vector in $\mathbf{R}^n$ are linearly independent over $\mathbf{Q}$ together with $1$, its image in the torus generates a dense cyclic subgroup. This fact is crucial for the study of maximal tori, as it implies every torus is monic, which is used to prove that all maximal tori in a compact Lie group are conjugate.
