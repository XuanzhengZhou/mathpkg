Let $a_1, \ldots, a_n \in \mathbf{R}$ be real numbers such that $1, a_1, \ldots, a_n$ are $\mathbf{Q}$-linearly independent. Let $a$ denote the class of $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ in $T^n = \mathbf{R}^n / \mathbf{Z}^n$. Then the set $\{a^k : k \geq 0\}$ is dense in $T^n$. In particular, every torus is monic (has a topological generator).
