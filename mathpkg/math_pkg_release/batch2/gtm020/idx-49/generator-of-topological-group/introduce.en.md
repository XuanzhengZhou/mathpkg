A generator of a topological group is an element whose non-negative powers form a dense subset. This notion generalizes the idea of a cyclic group to the topological setting: while not every element is a finite product of the generator, every element can be approximated arbitrarily well by powers $a^k$. The existence of a generator (the group being "monic") is a useful property, satisfied in particular by all tori.
