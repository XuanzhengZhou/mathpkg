An $H$-space structure $\phi: S^{n-1} \times S^{n-1} \to S^{n-1}$ is a map of bidegree $(1, 1)$, and the Hopf construction yields a map $S^{2n-1} \to S^n$ of the Hopf invariant $1$ by Theorem (3.5). By (4.3), the Hopf invariant $1$ can exist only for $n = 1, 2, 4, 8$, which correspond to the spheres $S^1$, $S^3$, and $S^7$ (since the Hopf construction produces a map on $S^{2n-1}$ giving an $H$-space on $S^{n-1}$, we have $n-1 = 0, 1, 3, 7$, i.e. the spheres $S^0$, $S^1$, $S^3$, $S^7$; but $S^0$ is a two-point set which admits a trivial $H$-space structure, leaving the nontrivial connected cases $S^1$, $S^3$, $S^7$).
