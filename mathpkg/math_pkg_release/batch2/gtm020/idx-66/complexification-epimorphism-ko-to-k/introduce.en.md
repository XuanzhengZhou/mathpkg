The complexification map $\varepsilon_U: \widetilde{KO}(RP^n) \to \tilde{K}(RP^n)$ relates real and complex $K$-theory of real projective spaces. It is always surjective, and in the range $n \equiv 6,7,8 \pmod{8}$ it is an isomorphism. The generator $u = \xi_n(\mathbb{R}) - 1$ of the $KO$-group maps to the generator $w$ of the $K$-group, reflecting the fact that complexifying the canonical real line bundle yields the complex canonical line bundle.
