The proof follows from the property that $\varepsilon_U(u) = w$ where $u = \xi_n(\mathbb{R}) - 1$ generates $\widetilde{KO}(RP^n)$ and $w$ generates $\tilde{K}(RP^n)$, as established in 14(11.4). Since $w$ generates the target and lies in the image of $\varepsilon_U$, the map is surjective. The isomorphism statement for $n \equiv 6,7,8 \pmod{8}$ follows from comparing the orders of the source and target groups in these congruence classes; the proof uses periodicity properties of $KO$-theory. Full details are in Chapter 14, Section 11 of the book.
