The theorem is proved by a Mayer-Vietoris argument. One constructs an open cover of the base $B(\xi)$ over which the bundle $\xi$ trivializes. On each open set of the cover, the sphere bundle reduces to a product, and the problem reduces to the case of a trivial bundle, which is exactly the statement of 14(13.3) (the absolute case of Bott's theorem). The Mayer-Vietoris exact sequence in $KO$-theory is then used to glue the local statements together. The involution comes from the natural action of the Clifford algebra on the spin representations, which exchanges $\Delta^+_{8m}$ and $\Delta^-_{8m}$. See Bott [5] for the original proof and further details.
