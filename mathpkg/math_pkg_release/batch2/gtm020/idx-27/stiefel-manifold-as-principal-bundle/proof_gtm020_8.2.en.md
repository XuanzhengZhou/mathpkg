The theorem follows from the fact that homogeneous spaces of compact groups define principal bundles. The group $U_F(k)$ acts freely on $V_k(F^n)$ on the right by change of orthonormal basis: for $(v_1, \ldots, v_k) \in V_k(F^n)$ and $a = (a_{ij}) \in U_F(k)$, the action is $(v_1, \ldots, v_k) \cdot a = (\sum_i v_i a_{i1}, \ldots, \sum_i v_i a_{ik})$. This action is free because an orthonormal $k$-frame determines the basis uniquely, and the only $a \in U_F(k)$ fixing a given frame is the identity. The projection $p: V_k(F^n) \rightarrow G_k(F^n)$ is invariant under this action, and $U_F(k)$ acts transitively on each fibre. By the general theory of Lie group actions on manifolds, this yields a principal $U_F(k)$-bundle structure. For the oriented case, the subgroup $SU_F(k) \subset U_F(k)$ acts freely, and $U_F(n)/(SU_F(k) \times U_F(n-k)) \cong SG_k(F^n)$ yields the principal $SU_F(k)$-bundle structure.
