The projection $p: V_k(F^n) \to G_k(F^n)$ is locally trivial, and

$$(V_k(F^n), p, G_k(F^n))$$

is a locally trivial principal bundle with fibre $V_k(F^k) = U_F(k)$.

Moreover, for each $W \in G_k(F^n)$, there exists an open neighborhood $O(W) \subset G_k(F^n)$ and a homeomorphism

$$O(W) \times U_F(k) \xrightarrow{\cong} p^{-1}(O(W))$$

compatible with the right $U_F(k)$-action on the fibres. For an orthonormal frame $(v_1, \ldots, v_k)$ with span $W$, the local trivialization is constructed using the orthogonal projection $\pi_{W'}(v_i)$ onto $W' \in O(W)$ followed by the Gram-Schmidt map.
