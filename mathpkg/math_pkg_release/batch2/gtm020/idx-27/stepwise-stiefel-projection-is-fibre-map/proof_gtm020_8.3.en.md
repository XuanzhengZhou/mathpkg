This follows from Theorem 3.6 (or its refinement 3.8) and Proposition 1(5.2) on the behavior of principal bundles. From the local triviality of $p: V_k(F^n) \to G_k(F^n)$ established in Theorem 3.6, one obtains by restriction a fibre bundle structure for the forgetful map between consecutive Stiefel manifolds. The proof uses the local trivialization constructed in Theorem 3.6: for a given $k$-frame $(v_1,\ldots,v_k) \in V_k(F^n)$, the fibre $q^{-1}(v_1,\ldots,v_k)$ consists of all unit vectors $v_{k+1}$ orthogonal to each $v_i$, which is precisely the unit sphere in the $(n-k)$-dimensional orthogonal complement $W^\perp$. The map $q$ inherits local triviality from the local triviality of $p$, with the local product structure $O \times V_1(F^{n-k})$ over a neighborhood in $V_k(F^n)$.
