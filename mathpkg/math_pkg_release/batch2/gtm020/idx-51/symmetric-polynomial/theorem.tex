A polynomial $P(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \in R[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is called symmetric if
$$
{}^{\tau}P = P \quad \text{for each } \tau \in S_n,
$$
where $R$ is a commutative ring with $1$, $S_n$ denotes the permutation group on $n$ letters, and the action is defined by
$$
{}^{\tau}P(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = P(x_{\tau(1)}, \ldots, x_{\tau(n)}).
$$
