A symmetric polynomial is a polynomial in $n$ variables over a commutative ring that remains unchanged under any permutation of its variables. The action of the symmetric group $S_n$ on $R[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is defined by permuting the indices of the variables. This concept is fundamental to the theory of elementary symmetric functions and plays a central role in the computation of representation rings of classical groups.
