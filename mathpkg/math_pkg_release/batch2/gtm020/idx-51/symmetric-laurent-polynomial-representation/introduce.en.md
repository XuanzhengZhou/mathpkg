This proposition characterizes Laurent polynomials invariant under the involution $x \mapsto 1/x$: they are precisely polynomials in $x + x^{-1}$. The proof uses the fact that $f(x) = \sum a_m x^m$ with $a_m = a_{-m}$, allowing a grouping into terms $a_m(x^m + x^{-m})$, each of which is a polynomial in $x + x^{-1}$ by Proposition 5.1. This result is essential for analyzing the Weyl group invariants that appear in the representation ring of $Sp(n)$.
