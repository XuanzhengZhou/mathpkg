Let $f \in R[\alpha_1, \alpha_1^{-1}, \ldots, \alpha_n, \alpha_n^{-1}]$ satisfy
$$
f(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_i, \ldots, \alpha_n) = f(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_i^{-1}, \ldots, \alpha_n)
$$
for each $i = 1, \ldots, n$. Then $f$ belongs to the subring
$$
R[\alpha_1 + \alpha_1^{-1}, \ldots, \alpha_n + \alpha_n^{-1}] \subset R[\alpha_1, \alpha_1^{-1}, \ldots, \alpha_n, \alpha_n^{-1}].
$$
