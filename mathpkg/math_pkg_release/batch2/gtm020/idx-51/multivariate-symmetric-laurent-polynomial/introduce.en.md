This corollary extends Proposition 5.2 to several variables. If a Laurent polynomial in $n$ variables is invariant under the inversion of any single variable $\alpha_i \mapsto \alpha_i^{-1}$, then it can be expressed as a polynomial in the quantities $\alpha_i + \alpha_i^{-1}$. The proof applies Proposition 5.2 successively to each variable. This result is critical for describing the Weyl group invariants that characterize the representation ring $R(Sp(n))$.
