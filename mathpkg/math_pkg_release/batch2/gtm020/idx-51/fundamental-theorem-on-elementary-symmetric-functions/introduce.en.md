The fundamental theorem on elementary symmetric functions states that every symmetric polynomial in $n$ variables over a commutative ring $R$ can be expressed as a polynomial in the elementary symmetric functions $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_n$. The representation is unique. This theorem is the algebraic foundation for the computation of representation rings of classical groups, as the characters of exterior powers give rise to elementary symmetric functions in the basic characters.
