Proof. The first statement says that $\phi$ factors through the group completion $K_F$ if and only if its image is included in a subgroup of $H(X)$ for each $X$. The second statement follows from the first, because each bundle $\xi$ has an $s$-inverse $\eta$, where $\xi \oplus \eta$ is trivial. Then $\phi(\xi \oplus \eta) = \phi(\xi)\phi(\eta)$ is the neutral element of $H(X)$.

2.4 Example. Let $H(X)$ be $H^{\text{ev}}(X, Z) = \sum_{k \geq 0} H^{2k}(X, Z)$ with the cup product as the commutative semigroup operation. Then the total Chern class $c$: $\text{Vect}_C \rightarrow H^{\text{ev}}(-, Z)$ is a characteristic class. Since $c(\xi) = 1$ for a trivial bundle $\xi$, $c$ is stable by (2.3), and we can view $c$ as defined by $K \rightarrow H^{\text{ev}}(-, Z)$.
