The duality map $D_K^V$ is the central construction in the proof of Poincaré–Alexander–Lefschetz duality for manifolds. Given an oriented $n$-manifold $M$, a compact subset $K$, and an open neighborhood $V$ of $K$, the map sends a cohomology class $c \in H^q(V)$ to the cap product of $c$ with the orientation class $\omega_K^V \in H_n(V, V-K)$, pushed forward via the excision isomorphism. The theorem of Poincaré–Alexander duality asserts that under suitable conditions (e.g., when $K$ is a finite simplicial complex), the map $D_K^V$ is an isomorphism. This notation package — $\omega_K$, $\omega_K^V$, and $D_K^V$ — is used throughout Chapter 5 of Husemoller's *Fibre Bundles*.
