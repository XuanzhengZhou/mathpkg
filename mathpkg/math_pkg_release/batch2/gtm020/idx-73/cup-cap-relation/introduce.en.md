The cup-cap relation expresses a fundamental adjunction between the cup product in cohomology and the cap product between cohomology and homology. Given a canonical pairing $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ induced by the chain-level substitution pairing, the relation $\langle c_1 \cup c_2, u \rangle = \langle c_1, c_2 \cap u \rangle$ holds for all cohomology classes $c_1, c_2$ and homology class $u$ of compatible degrees. This identity is essential in the proof of Poincaré–Alexander–Lefschetz duality on manifolds, where it connects the duality map $D_K^V$ defined via the cap product with the orientation class to the evaluation pairing.
