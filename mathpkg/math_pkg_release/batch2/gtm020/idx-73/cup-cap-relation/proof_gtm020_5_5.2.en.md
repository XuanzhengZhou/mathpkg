The relation follows directly from the chain-level definitions of the cup and cap products. At the cochain level, the substitution pairing $C^*(M, A) \otimes C_*(M, A) \to \mathbf{Z}$ (or $\mathbf{Z}_2$) induces the canonical pairing on (co)homology. For cochains $c^1 \in C^k(V)$ and $c^2 \in C^{n-k}(V)$ and a chain $u \in C_n(V, V-K)$, the cup product $c^1 \cup c^2$ and cap product $c^2 \cap u$ satisfy the adjunction identity at the chain level:

$$\langle c^1 \cup c^2, u \rangle = \langle c^1, c^2 \cap u \rangle.$$

Passing to homology and cohomology classes yields the stated relation. This is a purely algebraic identity that holds for any chain complex equipped with cup and cap products satisfying the standard axioms.
