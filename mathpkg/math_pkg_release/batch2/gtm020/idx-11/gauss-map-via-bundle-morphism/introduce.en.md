This proposition establishes the equivalence between Gauss maps of a vector bundle $\xi^k$ and vector bundle morphisms from $\xi^k$ to the universal bundle $\gamma_k^m$ that are isomorphisms on each fibre. The forward direction is clear: composing such a morphism with the canonical Gauss map $q$ on $\gamma_k^m$ yields a Gauss map. Conversely, any Gauss map $g$ uniquely determines a classifying map $f: B \to G_k(F^m)$ by sending each basepoint to the image of its fibre under $g$, and a bundle map $u$ lifting $f$.
