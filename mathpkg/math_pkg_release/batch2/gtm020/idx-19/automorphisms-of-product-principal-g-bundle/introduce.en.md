This theorem characterizes all bundle automorphisms of a trivial principal $G$-bundle $(B \times G, p, B)$ that cover the identity on the base space $B$. It establishes that such $B$-automorphisms correspond bijectively to arbitrary maps from the base space $B$ into the structure group $G$, with the automorphism acting by left multiplication: $(b, s) \mapsto (b, g(b)s)$. The inverse automorphism is given by the pointwise inverse map $g'(b) = g(b)^{-1}$. This result is fundamental for understanding how trivial bundles are glued together via transition functions in the general theory of fibre bundles.
