This fundamental theorem establishes that any system of transition functions satisfying the cocycle condition gives rise to a principal $G$-bundle, and that this bundle is unique up to isomorphism. The construction proceeds by taking the disjoint union of the product spaces $V_i \times G$, imposing an equivalence relation determined by the transition functions: $(b, s, i) \sim (b', s', j)$ if $b = b'$ and $s' = g_{j,i}(b)s$. The resulting quotient space carries a natural $G$-action making it a principal $G$-bundle. This theorem provides the essential link between the abstract cocycle data and concrete geometric bundles.
