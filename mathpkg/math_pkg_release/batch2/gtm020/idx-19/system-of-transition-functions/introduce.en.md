A system of transition functions is the algebraic data encoding how local trivializations of a fibre bundle are glued together over intersections of the covering open sets. Given an open covering $\{V_i\}$ of the base space $B$ and a structure group $G$, a family of maps $g_{i,j}: V_i \cap V_j \rightarrow G$ satisfying the cocycle condition $g_{i,k} = g_{i,j} g_{j,k}$ on triple intersections constitutes such a system. From this condition, one deduces $g_{i,i} = 1$ and $g_{i,j} = g_{j,i}^{-1}$. Transition functions are the bridge between the abstract notion of a fibre bundle and its concrete local coordinate description.
