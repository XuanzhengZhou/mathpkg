This proposition gives explicit character formulas in the representation ring for the fundamental spin representations of the classical groups. In $R\,\text{Spin}(2r+1)$, the basic spinor module $\Delta$ of dimension $2^r$ is expressed as a product over the Weyl group orbit. For $R\,\text{Spin}(2r)$, the two half-spin representations $\Delta^+$ and $\Delta^-$, each of dimension $2^{r-1}$, are distinguished by the sign of the product $\varepsilon(1) \cdots \varepsilon(r)$. The formulas for $\lambda_r^\pm$ in $RSO(2r)$ correspond to the two irreducible components of the $r$-th exterior power representation.
