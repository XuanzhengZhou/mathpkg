Every $\text{Spin}(n)$-module $M$ splits canonically into two submodules distinguished by the action of the central element $-1 \in \text{Spin}(n)$. The submodule $M_1$ on which $-1$ acts trivially descends to an $SO(n)$-module via the natural homomorphism $\phi \colon \text{Spin}(n) \to SO(n)$. The submodule $M_2$ on which $-1$ acts as $-1$ carries the structure of a $C_{n-1}$-module, compatible with the action of $\text{Spin}(n)$ through the inclusion $\text{Spin}(n) \subset C_n^0$ and the isomorphism $C_n^0 \cong C_{n-1}$.
