For an irreducible nonperiodic Markov chain with persistent states:
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} p_{j,k}^{(n)} = u_k > 0,$$
where $u_k$ is independent of $j$, and $\{u_k\}$ is the unique invariant probability distribution satisfying
$$\sum_j u_j p_{j,k} = u_k, \quad \sum_k u_k = 1.$$

For transient or null states: $\lim_{n \to \infty} p_{j,k}^{(n)} = 0$ for all $j, k$.

For periodic states with period $t$: $p_{j,j}^{(st)} \to t/\mu_j$ as $s \to \infty$.
