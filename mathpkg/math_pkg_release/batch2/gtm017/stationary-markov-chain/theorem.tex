A Markov chain with stationary transition mechanism governed by one-step transition matrix $P$ is called \textbf{stationary} if there exists a left invariant probability vector $p = (p_i)$ satisfying
$$pP = p, \quad p_i \geq 0, \quad \sum_i p_i = 1.$$

With this initial distribution, the instantaneous distribution $P(X_s = i) = p_i$ is invariant under time shift, and the full probability structure of the process is invariant under time shifts.
