Let $T$ be a measure-preserving transformation on $(\Omega, \mathcal{B}, P)$ and let $f$ satisfy $E|f(w)| < \infty$. Then
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} f(T^j w)$$
exists with probability one and equals $E\{f(w) \mid \mathcal{G}\}$, where $\mathcal{G}$ is the $\sigma$-field of invariant sets. If the process is metrically transitive, $E\{f(w) \mid \mathcal{G}\} = E\{f(w)\}$, so time and space averages are equal. Metric transitivity is necessary and sufficient for ergodicity.
