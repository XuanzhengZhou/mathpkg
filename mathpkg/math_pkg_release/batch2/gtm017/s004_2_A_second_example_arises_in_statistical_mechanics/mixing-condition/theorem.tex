A stationary process satisfies the \textbf{mixing condition} if for any two measurable sets $A, B$,
$$\lim_{j \to \infty} P(B \cap T^{-j}A) = P(B)P(A).$$
This is a form of asymptotic independence. Mixing implies ergodicity via the weaker condition $\lim_n \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} P(B \cap T^{-j}A) = P(A)P(B)$.
