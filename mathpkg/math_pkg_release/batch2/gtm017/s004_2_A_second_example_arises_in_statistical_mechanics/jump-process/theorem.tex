A Markov process is a \textbf{jump process} if for small $t - \tau > 0$,
$$P(\tau, x; t, A) = \{1 - p(t, x)(t - \tau)\} \delta(x, A) + p(t, x)(t - \tau) \Pi(t, x, A) + o(t - \tau),$$
where $\delta(x, A) = 1$ if $x \in A$, $0$ otherwise. $p(t, x)$ is bounded continuous (jump intensity), $\Pi(t, x, A)$ a probability measure (jump distribution).
