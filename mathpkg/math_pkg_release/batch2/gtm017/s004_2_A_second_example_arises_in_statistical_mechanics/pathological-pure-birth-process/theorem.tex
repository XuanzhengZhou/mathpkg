A \textbf{pure birth process} has $b_{i,i} = -b_i$, $b_{i,i+1} = b_i > 0$, $b_{i,j} = 0$ otherwise. If $\sum 1/b_n < \infty$, the system can pass through all states in finite time with positive probability. The minimal solution satisfies backward but not forward equations; forward inequalities hold instead. Non-minimal solutions are constructed by returning from an adjoined state at infinity with probabilities $\alpha_i$.
