Under boundedness of $p(t, x)$, there exists a unique transition probability function $P(\tau, x; t, A) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty P_n(\tau, x; t, A)$, where $P_0 = \delta(x, A)\exp\{-\int_\tau^t p(s,x) ds\}$ (no jump) and $P_n$ is the probability of exactly $n$ jumps, constructed recursively. Moreover, $P(\tau, x; t, \Omega_X) = 1$.
