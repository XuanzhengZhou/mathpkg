Let $\{X_n\}$, $n = 0, \pm 1, \ldots$, be a strictly stationary process with finite first moment $E|X_n| < \infty$. Let $T$ be the shift transformation on the sample space $\Omega$ such that $(Tw)_n = w_{n+1}$. The process $\{X_n\}$ is called an \textbf{ergodic process} if the limiting time average
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} f(T^k w)$$
exists and is equal to the space average $Ef(w)$ for almost all $w$ and for every integrable function $f$.
