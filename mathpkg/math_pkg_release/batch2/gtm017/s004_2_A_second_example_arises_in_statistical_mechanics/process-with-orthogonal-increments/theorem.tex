A random process $Z(\lambda)$ has \textbf{orthogonal increments} if for disjoint intervals $(\lambda,\mu)$ and $(\lambda',\mu')$, $E(Z(\mu)-Z(\lambda))\overline{(Z(\mu')-Z(\lambda'))} = 0$ and $E|Z(\mu)-Z(\lambda)|^2 = F(\mu)-F(\lambda)$. In differential notation: $E dZ(\lambda) \overline{dZ(\mu)} = \delta_{\lambda,\mu} dF(\lambda)$.
