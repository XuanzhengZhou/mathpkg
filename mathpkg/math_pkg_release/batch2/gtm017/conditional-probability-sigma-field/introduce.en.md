Conditional probability given a sigma-field generalizes the elementary notion of conditioning on an event. It uses the Radon-Nikodym theorem to define $P(A|\mathfrak{B})$ as the density of the measure $P_A(B) = P(A \cap B)$ with respect to $P$ restricted to $\mathfrak{B}$. This definition handles conditioning on continuous random variables without the division-by-zero problem.
