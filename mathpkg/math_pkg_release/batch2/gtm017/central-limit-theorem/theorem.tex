Let $X_1, \ldots, X_n$ be independent, identically distributed random variables with mean $m$ and variance $\sigma^2 > 0$. Then
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} P\left(\frac{\sqrt{n}}{\sigma}\left[\frac{S_n}{n} - m\right] \leq x\right) = \Phi(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{x} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-u^2/2} \, du,$$
where $S_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$ and $\Phi(x)$ is the standard normal distribution function.
