This comparison theorem bounds eigenvalues of a complex matrix $B$ by the maximal eigenvalue of a dominating non-negative matrix $A$. Equality at the maximal eigenvalue occurs only when $B$ is essentially $A$ up to a unitary diagonal similarity transformation. It is used to analyze the cyclic structure of eigenvalues for imprimitive matrices.
