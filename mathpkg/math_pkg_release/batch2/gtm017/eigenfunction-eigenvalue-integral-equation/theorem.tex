A function $\varphi(t)$ is an \textbf{eigenfunction} of the integral equation
$$\varphi(t) = \lambda \int_0^T r(t, \tau) \varphi(\tau) d\tau$$
with kernel $r(t, \tau)$ if $\varphi$ is nontrivial, and $\lambda \neq 0$ is the corresponding \textbf{eigenvalue}.

For a symmetric, positive definite kernel:
\begin{enumerate}
\item All eigenvalues are positive
\item Eigenfunctions corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal
\item Eigenfunctions may be taken real-valued
\end{enumerate}
