Let $\{Z_n\}$ be a sequence of martingale differences with respect to $\{\mathfrak{B}_n\}$ (i.e., $E[Z_n \mid \mathfrak{B}_{n-1}] = 0$) satisfying appropriate moment and Lindeberg-type conditions. Then
$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \sum_{j=1}^{n} Z_j \xrightarrow{d} N(0, \sigma^2)$$
as $n \to \infty$, where $\sigma^2 = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} E[Z_j^2 \mid \mathfrak{B}_{j-1}]$ (assumed constant).
