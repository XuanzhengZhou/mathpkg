Let $\{X_t, -\infty < t < \infty\}$ be a weakly stationary process with $EX_t = 0$ and covariance function $r(t)$. Then there exists a unique measure $F(d\lambda)$ (the spectral measure) and a process $Z(\lambda)$ with orthogonal increments such that
$$X_t = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{it\lambda} \, dZ(\lambda),$$
with $E[dZ(\lambda)] = 0$ and $E[|dZ(\lambda)|^2] = dF(\lambda)$. The covariance function has the spectral representation
$$r(t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{it\lambda} \, dF(\lambda).$$
