The spectral representation theorem (Khintchine-Kolmogorov-Cramér) represents a weakly stationary process as a Fourier-Stieltjes integral of orthogonal increments. The covariance function is the Fourier transform of the spectral measure. This is the probabilistic analog of the fact that positive definite functions are Fourier transforms of finite measures, via Bochner's theorem.
