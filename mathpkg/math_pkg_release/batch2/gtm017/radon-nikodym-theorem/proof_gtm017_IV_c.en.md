For finite measures, the proof constructs $f$ as the supremum of functions $g \geq 0$ with $\int_A g dm_2 \leq m_1(A)$ for all $A$. Using the Hahn decomposition, one shows that the maximal function $f$ achieves equality.

For sigma-finite measures, decompose $\Omega$ into a countable union of finite-measure sets and piece together the derivatives.
