Invariant events are those unchanged (up to null sets) by the time shift. Metric transitivity means the only invariant events are trivial (probability 0 or 1). This is the key condition for the ergodic theorem, ensuring that time averages along individual sample paths equal the ensemble (space) average. A process is ergodic iff it is metrically transitive.
