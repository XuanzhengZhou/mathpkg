Suppose $X_n \to X$ a.e. For fixed $\varepsilon > 0$, define $E_n = \{\omega : |X_n(\omega) - X(\omega)| > \varepsilon\}$ and $F_N = \bigcup_{n \geq N} E_n$. Since $X_n \to X$ a.e., $\bigcap_N F_N$ has probability zero. Since $F_N \supset F_{N+1}$, by continuity of measure $P(F_N) \to 0$. As $E_n \subset F_n$, we have $P(E_n) \to 0$. Counterexample: On $([0,1], \mathcal{B}, \lambda)$, define $X_n = I_{[k/2^m, (k+1)/2^m]}$ where $n = 2^m + k$. Then $X_n$ does not converge pointwise but $P(|X_n| > \varepsilon) = 2^{-m} \to 0$.
