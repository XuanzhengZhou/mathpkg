Let $F(x)$ be a distribution function and $\varphi(t)$ its characteristic function. For any two continuity points $a, b$ of $F$ ($a < b$),
$$F(b) - F(a) = \lim_{T \to \infty} \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-T}^{T} \frac{e^{-ita} - e^{-itb}}{it} \varphi(t) \, dt.$$
If $\varphi(t)$ is integrable, then $F$ is absolutely continuous with density $f$:
$$F(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{x} f(u) \, du, \quad f(x) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-itx} \varphi(t) \, dt.$$
