Consider $I_T = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-T}^{T} \frac{e^{-ita} - e^{-itb}}{it} \varphi(t) dt = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-T}^{T} \frac{e^{-ita} - e^{-itb}}{it} \int e^{itx} dF(x) dt$. Interchange integrals (Fubini for finite $[-T,T]$). The inner integral evaluates to $2\int_0^T \frac{\sin t(x-a) - \sin t(x-b)}{t} dt$. As $T \to \infty$, using the Dirichlet integral, this converges to $\pi$ for $a < x < b$, $\pi/2$ at $x=a,b$, and 0 otherwise. At continuity points, $I_T \to F(b)-F(a)$. If $\varphi$ is integrable, dominated convergence yields the Fourier inversion formula.
