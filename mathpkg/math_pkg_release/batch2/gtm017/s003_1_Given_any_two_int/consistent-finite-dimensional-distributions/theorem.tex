For a stochastic process $\{X_t, t \in T\}$, the family of joint distribution functions $F_{(X_{t_1}, \ldots, X_{t_k})}(x_{t_1}, \ldots, x_{t_k})$ for all finite $t_1, \ldots, t_k \in T$ satisfies two consistency conditions:
(1) Permutation invariance: For any permutation $Q$, $F_{(X_{t_{Q1}}, \ldots, X_{t_{Qk}})}(x_{t_{Q1}}, \ldots) = F_{(X_{t_1}, \ldots, X_{t_k})}(x_{t_1}, \ldots)$.
(2) Marginal consistency: $\lim_{x_{t_{j+1}}, \ldots, x_{t_k} \to \infty} F_{(X_{t_1}, \ldots, X_{t_k})}(x_1, \ldots, x_j, \infty, \ldots, \infty) = F_{(X_{t_1}, \ldots, X_{t_j})}(x_1, \ldots, x_j)$.
