Show how the sample mean $\frac{1}{n}\sum f(X^{(i)})$ estimates the integral $\int f$. This is the basic Monte Carlo integration method. When the dimension $k$ is large, Monte Carlo methods avoid the curse of dimensionality that afflicts deterministic quadrature methods, whose computational cost grows exponentially with $k$.
