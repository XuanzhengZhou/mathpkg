Given a random variable $X(\omega)$, its characteristic function $\varphi(t)$ is the Fourier-Stieltjes transform of its distribution function $F(x)$:
$$\varphi(t) = E e^{itX} = E[\cos(tX) + i \sin(tX)] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{itx} \, dF(x).$$
Properties: (1) $\varphi(0) = 1$; (2) $|\varphi(t)| \leq 1$; (3) $\varphi(t)$ is uniformly continuous; (4) $\varphi$ is positive definite: $\sum_{i,j} c_i \bar{c}_j \varphi(t_i - t_j) \geq 0$.
