For each mode of convergence -- almost everywhere, in probability, and in mean square -- the Cauchy convergence criterion holds: if $X_n - X_m \to 0$ as $n, m \to \infty$ in the given sense, then there exists a random variable $X$ such that $X_n \to X$ in the corresponding sense as $n \to \infty$. For mean square convergence, this is the Riesz-Fisher theorem.
