Necessity: For any distribution $F$, $\varphi(t) = \int e^{itx} dF(x)$ satisfies continuity, $\varphi(0)=1$, and positive definiteness (since $\sum c_i \bar{c}_j \varphi(t_i-t_j) = \int |\sum c_i e^{it_ix}|^2 dF(x) \geq 0$). Sufficiency: Positive definiteness implies, via Herglotz's theorem, existence of distribution functions whose characteristic functions approximate $\varphi$. Helly's selection theorem extracts a convergent subsequence; the continuity theorem yields the limit distribution with characteristic function $\varphi$.
