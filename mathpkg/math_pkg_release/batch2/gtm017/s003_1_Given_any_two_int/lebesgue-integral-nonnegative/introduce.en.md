The Lebesgue integral of a non-negative random variable is constructed by approximating the variable from below by simple functions taking finitely many dyadic values. At each level $k$, the range is partitioned into intervals of length $1/2^k$, and the approximating function $X_k$ takes the lower endpoint on each preimage set. The integral is a finite sum of value times probability, and their nondecreasing limit defines the integral. This construction is the foundation of expectation in probability theory.
