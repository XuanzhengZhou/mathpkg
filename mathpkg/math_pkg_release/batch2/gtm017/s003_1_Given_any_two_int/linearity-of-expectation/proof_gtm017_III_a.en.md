The linearity of the integral follows from the construction of the Lebesgue integral via simple function approximations. For non-negative $X$ and $Y$, the simple approximating functions satisfy linearity at each dyadic level by the additivity of the probability measure $P$ on disjoint sets. Taking limits as $k \to \infty$ preserves the linear combination. For general integrable $X$ and $Y$, decompose into positive and negative parts and apply the non-negative case separately.
