Let $\varphi(t)$ be the common characteristic function. Since $E[X_i]=0, E[X_i^2]=1$: $\varphi'(0)=iEX=0, \varphi''(0)=-EX^2=-1$. Taylor: $\varphi(t)=1-t^2/2+o(t^2)$. The characteristic function of $S_n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}\sum X_j$ is $[\varphi(t/\sqrt{n})]^n = [1-t^2/(2n)+o(t^2/n)]^n \to e^{-t^2/2}$. This limit is the standard normal characteristic function, continuous at 0. By the continuity theorem, the distributions converge to standard normal.
