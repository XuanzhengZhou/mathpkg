Let $X_1, \ldots, X_n$ be independent, identically distributed random variables with common density function
$$f_\mu(x) = \frac{1}{\pi} \frac{1}{1 + (x - \mu)^2}.$$
Find the probability distribution of $S_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$. What does this imply about $S_n$ as an estimate of $\mu$? Can you suggest other estimates of $\mu$? The density function $f_\mu$ is sometimes called the Cauchy density function.
