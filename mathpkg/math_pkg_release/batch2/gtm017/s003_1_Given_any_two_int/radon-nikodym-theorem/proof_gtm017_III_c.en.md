For finite measures, consider the set $\mathcal{G}$ of non-negative measurable $g$ with $\int_A g dm_2 \leq m_1(A)$ for all $A$. Let $\alpha = \sup_{g \in \mathcal{G}} \int g dm_2$ and choose a maximizing sequence. Define $f = \lim \max(g_1,\ldots,g_n)$. By monotone convergence, $\int f dm_2 = \alpha$. Define $\nu(A) = m_1(A) - \int_A f dm_2 \geq 0$. If $\nu \not\equiv 0$, absolute continuity allows adding a multiple of an indicator to $f$, contradicting maximality. Hence $\nu \equiv 0$. For sigma-finite measures, apply argument piecewise.
