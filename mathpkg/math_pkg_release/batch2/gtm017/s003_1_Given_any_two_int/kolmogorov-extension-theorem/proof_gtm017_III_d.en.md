Let $\Omega = \mathbb{R}^T$ with coordinate mappings $X_t(\omega) = \omega(t)$. Define cylinder sets and the set function $P$ on the generated field using the finite-dimensional distributions. Consistency ensures $P$ is well-defined and finitely additive. Countable additivity follows from compactness/tightness arguments on $\mathbb{R}^k$. Caratheodory's theorem extends $P$ uniquely to the sigma-field generated by cylinders. The coordinate mappings serve as the stochastic process.
