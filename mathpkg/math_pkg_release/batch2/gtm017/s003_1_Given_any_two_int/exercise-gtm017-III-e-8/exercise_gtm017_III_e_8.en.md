Show that if a random variable $X$ has a finite absolute moment $E|X|^n < \infty$, then its characteristic function $\varphi(t)$ is differentiable up to $n$-th order. The $k$-th derivative at 0 is $\varphi^{(k)}(0) = i^k E[X^k]$. This follows from differentiating under the integral sign, justified by dominated convergence since $|x|^k \leq |x|^n$ for $k \leq n$ and large $|x|$.
