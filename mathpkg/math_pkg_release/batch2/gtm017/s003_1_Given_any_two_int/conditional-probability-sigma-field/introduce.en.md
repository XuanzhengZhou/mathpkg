Conditional probability with respect to a sub-sigma-field generalizes elementary conditional probability to general probability spaces. Instead of conditioning on a specific event, one conditions on all information in $\mathfrak{B}$. Defined as the Radon-Nikodym derivative of $B \mapsto P(A \cap B)$ with respect to $P$ restricted to $\mathfrak{B}$, it acts like a probability measure in $A$ for almost every $\omega$. This resolves the difficulty of conditioning on events of probability zero.
