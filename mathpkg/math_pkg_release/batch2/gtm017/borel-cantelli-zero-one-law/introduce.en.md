The Borel-Cantelli lemma (zero-one law for independent events) states that an infinite sequence of independent events occurs infinitely often with probability 0 or 1, depending on whether the sum of probabilities converges or diverges. This is a paradigmatic example of a zero-one law and is fundamental to almost-sure convergence arguments throughout probability theory.
