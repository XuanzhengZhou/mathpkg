Consider an irreducible non-periodic Markov chain. For any states $j, k$:
\begin{itemize}
\item If the states are transient or null: $p_{j,k}^{(n)} \to 0$ as $n \to \infty$ for all $j, k$.
\item If the states are persistent: $\lim_{n \to \infty} p_{j,k}^{(n)} = u_k > 0$, where $\{u_k\}$ is the unique invariant probability distribution satisfying $u_k = \sum_j u_j p_{j,k}$ and $\sum_k u_k = 1$.
\end{itemize}
