In an irreducible aperiodic Markov chain, the $n$-step transition probabilities converge. For persistent states, they converge to the unique stationary distribution, which is the left eigenvector of the transition matrix with eigenvalue 1. For transient or null states, all probabilities decay to zero. This result connects the probabilistic limit theory to the algebraic Perron-Frobenius theory.
