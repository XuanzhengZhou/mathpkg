Conditional probability $P(A_1 \mid A_2)$ is the probability of event $A_1$ occurring given that $A_2$ has occurred. It is defined as the ratio of the joint probability to the probability of the conditioning event, provided the latter is positive. Independence of events is defined through the product relation: events are independent if knowledge of one does not affect the probability of the other. This extends to collections of events through the product condition on all finite subcollections.
