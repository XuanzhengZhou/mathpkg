Let $F(x)$ be a distribution function with characteristic function $\varphi(t)$. For any two continuity points $a, b$ of $F$,
$$F(b) - F(a) = \lim_{T \to \infty} \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-T}^{T} \frac{e^{-ita} - e^{-itb}}{it} \varphi(t) \, dt.$$

If $\varphi(t)$ is integrable over $(-\infty, \infty)$, then $F$ is absolutely continuous with density
$$f(x) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-itx} \varphi(t) \, dt.$$
