This lemma constrains the eigenstructure of the covariance integral operator. Using the inequality $\int_0^T r(t,t) dt \geq \sum 1/\lambda_i$, one shows that there cannot be infinitely many eigenfunctions sharing one eigenvalue, and eigenvalues cannot accumulate in any bounded interval away from zero. Consequently, the eigenvalues form a countable set, each with finite multiplicity, justifying the subsequent Mercer expansion.
