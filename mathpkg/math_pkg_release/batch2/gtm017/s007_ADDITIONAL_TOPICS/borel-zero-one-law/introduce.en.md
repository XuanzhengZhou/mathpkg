When the events $A_i$ are independent, a much stronger statement holds: the probability that they occur infinitely often can only be $0$ or $1$. The outcome is determined entirely by whether the series of probabilities converges or diverges. This is a prototypical zero-one law. The proof uses $P(\bigcap_{i=n}^\infty \bar{A}_i) = \prod (1-P(A_i)) \leq \exp(-\sum P(A_i))$ to show that divergence forces $P(A_i \text{ f.o.}) = 0$.
