Uniform mixing is robust under transformations that depend only on a finite window of the past. If $Y_t$ is constructed from $X_t$ by any operation that looks back at most $L$ time units, then $Y_t$ inherits the uniform mixing property. This is because the $\sigma$-field of $Y$ up to time $t$ is contained in that of $X$ up to time $t$, and future events of $Y$ beyond $\tau$ depend on $X$ beyond $\tau-L$, allowing adjustment of the mixing rate function. This closure property makes uniformly mixing processes useful for applications like narrow band-pass filtering.
