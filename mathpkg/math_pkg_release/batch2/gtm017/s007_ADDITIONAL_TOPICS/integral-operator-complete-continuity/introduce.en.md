An integral operator with square-integrable kernel is completely continuous (i.e., compact). This means it maps bounded sets to precompact sets. The proof proceeds by first showing the property for separable kernels $\alpha(t)\beta(\tau)$, then for finite sums, and finally for general $L^2$ kernels via approximation with a diagonal subsequence argument. Complete continuity is essential for establishing the existence of eigenfunctions via Hilbert-Schmidt theory.
