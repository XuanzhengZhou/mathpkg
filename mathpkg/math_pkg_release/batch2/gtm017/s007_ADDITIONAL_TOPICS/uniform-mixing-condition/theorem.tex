Let $X_t = X_t(\omega)$, $-\infty < t < \infty$, be a random process (not necessarily stationary), measurable jointly in $t$ and $\omega$. Let $\mathcal{B}_t$ and $\mathcal{F}_\tau$ denote the $\sigma$-fields generated by the process before time $t$ and after time $\tau$, respectively.

The process $X_t$ satisfies a uniform mixing condition if there exists a positive function $g(s)$, defined for $0 \leq s < \infty$, with $g(s) \to 0$ as $s \to \infty$, such that for any pair of events $B \in \mathcal{B}_t$ and $F \in \mathcal{F}_\tau$ with $t < \tau$,
$$|P(B \cap F) - P(B)P(F)| < g(\tau - t).$$
