The uniform mixing condition is a strong form of asymptotic independence for stochastic processes. It requires that events in the distant future become approximately independent of events in the past, with the approximation error bounded by a function $g(\tau-t)$ that decays to zero uniformly over all pairs of past and future events. Unlike ordinary mixing, uniform mixing is strong enough to imply a central limit theorem for time averages. The condition was introduced in [66] and [70].
