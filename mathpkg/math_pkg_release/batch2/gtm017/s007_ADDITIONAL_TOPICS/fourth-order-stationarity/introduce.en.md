Fourth-order stationarity extends second-order (weak) stationarity by requiring that fourth-order product moments also depend only on time differences. Together with fourth-order mean continuity and finite fourth moments, these conditions provide sufficient regularity for establishing central limit theorems for time averages of uniformly mixing processes. The function $P$ captures the fourth-order dependence structure and, combined with the mixing rate $g(s)$, controls the asymptotic variance of normalized sums.
