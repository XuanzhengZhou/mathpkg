Eigenfunctions of the covariance integral equation enjoy an orthogonality property analogous to eigenvectors of a symmetric matrix. When two eigenfunctions correspond to distinct eigenvalues, their inner product vanishes. This follows from the symmetry of the kernel $r(t,\tau) = r(\tau,t)$ and the eigenvalue equations. Orthogonality allows the eigenfunctions to be orthonormalized via Gram-Schmidt to form a convenient basis for representing the random process.
