The notation $A_i$ i.o. (infinitely often) denotes the set of sample points that belong to infinitely many of the events $A_i$. This set is expressed as a countable intersection of countable unions: $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \bigcup_{i=n}^{\infty} A_i$. The complement $A_i$ f.o. (finitely often) consists of points belonging to only finitely many $A_i$ and is given by $\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} \bigcap_{i=n}^{\infty} \bar{A}_i$. This $\limsup$ formulation is fundamental in probability theory, particularly for the Borel-Cantelli lemmas and zero-one laws.
