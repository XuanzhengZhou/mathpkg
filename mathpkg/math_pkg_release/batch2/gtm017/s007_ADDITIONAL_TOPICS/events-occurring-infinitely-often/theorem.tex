Let $A_1, A_2, \ldots$ be a sequence of events of a probability space. The event that the $A_i$ occur infinitely often is defined as
$$A_i \text{ i.o.} = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \bigcup_{i=n}^{\infty} A_i.$$
Its complement, the event that the $A_i$ occur finitely often, is
$$A_i \text{ f.o.} = \overline{A_i \text{ i.o.}} = \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} \bigcap_{i=n}^{\infty} \bar{A}_i.$$
