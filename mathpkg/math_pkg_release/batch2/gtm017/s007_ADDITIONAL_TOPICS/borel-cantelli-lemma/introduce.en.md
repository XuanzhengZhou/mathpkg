The Borel-Cantelli lemma provides a fundamental link between the convergence of the sum of probabilities and the occurrence of events infinitely often. If the series of probabilities converges, then with probability one, only finitely many of the events occur. This is the "easy" direction and holds for arbitrary events without any independence assumption. The bound $P(A_i \text{ i.o.}) \leq \sum_{i=n}^\infty P(A_i)$ for each $n$ follows directly from countable subadditivity of the probability measure.
