Let $A_1, A_2, \ldots$ be a sequence of events. Then for every $n$,
$$P(A_i \text{ i.o.}) \leq P\left(\bigcup_{i=n}^{\infty} A_i\right) \leq \sum_{i=n}^{\infty} P(A_i).$$
Consequently, if $\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} P(A_i) < \infty$, then $P(A_i \text{ i.o.}) = 0$.
Equivalently, if $P(A_i \text{ i.o.}) > 0$, then $\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} P(A_i)$ diverges.
