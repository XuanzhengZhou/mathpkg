Let $X_t = X_t(\omega)$, $0 \leq t \leq T < \infty$, be a real-valued random process with covariance function $r(t,\tau) = E[X_t X_\tau]$. A solution $\varphi(t)$ of the integral equation
$$\varphi(t) = \lambda \int_0^T r(t,\tau) \varphi(\tau) \, d\tau$$
is called an eigenfunction of the equation, and the corresponding number $\lambda$ is called its eigenvalue. Only nontrivial solutions with $\lambda \neq 0$ and square-integrable eigenfunctions ($\int_0^T |\varphi(t)|^2 dt < \infty$) are of interest.
