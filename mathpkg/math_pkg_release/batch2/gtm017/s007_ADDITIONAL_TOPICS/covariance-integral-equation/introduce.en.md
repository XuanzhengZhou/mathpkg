The covariance integral equation is the eigenvalue problem associated with the covariance kernel $r(t,\tau)$ of a second-order random process. An eigenfunction $\varphi(t)$ satisfies $\varphi = \lambda R\varphi$, where $R$ is the integral operator with kernel $r$. This equation is central to the Karhunen-Loeve expansion, which provides a diagonal representation of the random process in terms of its eigenfunctions and eigenvalues. Since the covariance function of a mean-square continuous process on $[0,T]$ is uniformly continuous, the eigenfunctions are all continuous.
