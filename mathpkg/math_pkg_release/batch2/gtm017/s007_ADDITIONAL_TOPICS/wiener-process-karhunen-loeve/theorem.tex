For the Wiener process $X_t$ on $0 \leq t \leq 1$ with covariance $r(t,\tau) = \min(t,\tau)$, the eigenfunctions and eigenvalues of the integral equation are
$$\varphi_k(t) = \sqrt{2} \sin\left(k + \frac{1}{2}\right)\pi t, \quad \lambda_k = \left(k + \frac{1}{2}\right)^2 \pi^2, \quad k = 1, 2, \ldots$$
with $\varphi(0) = 0$. The Karhunen-Loeve expansion is
$$X_t = \sqrt{2} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} Z_k \frac{\sin (k + \frac{1}{2}) \pi t}{(k + \frac{1}{2}) \pi},$$
where $Z_k$ are independent standard normal random variables.
