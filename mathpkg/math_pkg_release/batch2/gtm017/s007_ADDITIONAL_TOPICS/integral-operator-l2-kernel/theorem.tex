Let $r(t,\tau)$ be square integrable on $0 \leq t, \tau \leq T$. The integral operator $R$ generated by $r$ is defined by
$$(Rg)(t) = \int_0^T r(t,\tau) g(\tau) \, d\tau = h(t).$$
The inner product of two square-integrable functions $f, g$ is $(f,g) = \int_0^T f(t) g(t) \, dt$, the norm is $\|f\| = [(f,f)]^{1/2}$, and the operator norm is $\|R\| = \sup_{\|f\| = 1} \|Rf\|$.
