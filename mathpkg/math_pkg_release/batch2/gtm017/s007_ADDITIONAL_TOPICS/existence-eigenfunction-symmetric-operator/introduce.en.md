This theorem guarantees at least one nontrivial eigenfunction with nonzero eigenvalue for any symmetric completely continuous operator with positive norm. The proof constructs the eigenfunction as the limit of a maximizing sequence for the quadratic form $(Rf,f)$ over the unit sphere. Complete continuity provides the compactness to extract a convergent subsequence. This result is the cornerstone of Hilbert-Schmidt spectral theory used in the Mercer expansion.
