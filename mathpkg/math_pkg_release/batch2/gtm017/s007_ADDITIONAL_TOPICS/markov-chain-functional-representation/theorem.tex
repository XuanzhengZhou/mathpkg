Let $X_n, n = 0, \pm 1, \ldots$, be a finite-state stationary Markov chain with state space $\{1, \ldots, m\}$, transition probability matrix
$\mathbf{P} = (p_{i,j})$ with $p_{i,j} = P(X_{n+1}=j \mid X_n=i)$, and invariant probability vector $\mathbf{p}$ with $\mathbf{p}\mathbf{P} = \mathbf{p}$. Assume all $p_{i,j} > 0$.

Then there exists a function $f$ and a sequence of independent uniformly distributed (on $[0,1]$) random variables $\xi_n$ such that
$$X_n = f(\xi_n, \xi_{n-1}, \ldots).$$
More generally, a necessary and sufficient condition for a denumerable-state stationary Markov chain to admit such a representation is that the chain be ergodic and have no periodic states.
