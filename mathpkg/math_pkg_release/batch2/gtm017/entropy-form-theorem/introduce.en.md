The entropy function quantifies the uncertainty of a random experiment. Under natural axioms (additivity under conditioning, maximum for equiprobable outcomes, continuity), Shannon showed the entropy must be $-\lambda \sum p_k \log p_k$. The proof proceeds by first establishing $L(n) = \lambda \log n$ for equally likely outcomes, then extending to rational probabilities via a refinement argument.
