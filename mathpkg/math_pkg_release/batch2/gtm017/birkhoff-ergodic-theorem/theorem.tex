\textbf{Birkhoff's Individual Ergodic Theorem:} Let $T$ be a measure-preserving transformation on a probability space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$ and $f(w)$ an integrable function ($E|f| < \infty$). Then
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} f(T^j w)$$
exists with probability one and equals $E\{f(w) \mid \mathcal{G}\}$, where $\mathcal{G}$ is the sigma-field of invariant sets.

If the process is metrically transitive, $E\{f(w) \mid \mathcal{G}\} = E f(w)$, so time and space averages are equal.
\end{enumerate}
