An integral operator $R$ defined by
$$(Rg)(t) = \int_0^T r(t, \tau) g(\tau) d\tau$$
with square integrable kernel $r(t, \tau)$ is \textbf{completely continuous}: every sequence $\{g_n\}$ bounded in mean square contains a subsequence $\{g_{n_k}\}$ such that $\{Rg_{n_k}\}$ converges in mean square.

If $r(t, \tau)$ is symmetric with $\|R\| > 0$, then $R$ possesses at least one eigenfunction with nonzero eigenvalue.
\end{enumerate}
