A process $\{X_t, t \in T\}$ (where $T = \mathbb{Z}$ or $\mathbb{R}$) is called \text{weakly stationary} (or \text{wide-sense stationary}) if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E[X_t] \equiv m$ is constant (independent of $t$).
\item $E[|X_t|^2] < \infty$ for all $t$.
\item The covariance $r(t, s) = E[(X_t - m)(\overline{X_s - m})]$ depends only on the difference $t - s$:
  $$r(t, s) = r(t - s).$$
\end{enumerate}
