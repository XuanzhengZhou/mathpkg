A random process $X_t$, $-\infty < t < \infty$, satisfies a \textbf{uniform mixing condition} if there exists a function $g(s) \to 0$ as $s \to \infty$ such that for any events $B \in \mathcal{B}_t$ (past before $t$) and $F \in \mathcal{F}_\tau$ (future after $\tau$), $t < \tau$,
$$|P(B \cap F) - P(B) P(F)| < g(\tau - t).$$

Processes derived from $X_t$ by operations over a finite time interval and their shifts also satisfy the uniform mixing condition.
