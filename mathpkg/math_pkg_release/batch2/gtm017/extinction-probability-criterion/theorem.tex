Let $e_t = P(X_t = 0)$ be the probability of extinction by time $t$ in a branching process with offspring generating function $\varphi(s)$. Then $e = \lim_{t \to \infty} e_t$ exists and satisfies $e = \varphi(e)$. Let $\mu = \varphi'(1)$ be the mean offspring per individual:

\begin{itemize}
\item If $\mu \leq 1$ (excluding the trivial case $q_1 = 1$), the probability of eventual extinction is $e = 1$.
\item If $\mu > 1$, there is a unique solution $\zeta$, $0 \leq \zeta < 1$, of $\zeta = \varphi(\zeta)$, and $\zeta$ is the probability of eventual extinction. When starting with $r$ individuals, the extinction probability is $\zeta^r$.
\end{itemize}
