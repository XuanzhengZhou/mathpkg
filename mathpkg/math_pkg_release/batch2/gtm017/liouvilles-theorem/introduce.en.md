Liouville's theorem states that Hamiltonian flows preserve phase space volume. The proof computes $\partial J/\partial t$ and shows it vanishes because the mixed partial derivatives of $H$ cancel. This volume preservation is the foundation for statistical mechanics: a uniform initial distribution on an energy shell remains uniform, generating a stationary process and motivating ergodic theory.
