Consider a conservative Hamiltonian system with $n$ degrees of freedom described by generalized coordinates $q_1, \ldots, q_n$ and momenta $p_1, \ldots, p_n$, with Hamiltonian $H(p,q)$. The equations of motion are
$$\frac{dq_i}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_i}, \quad \frac{dp_i}{dt} = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q_i}, \quad i = 1, \ldots, n.$$

Let $T_t$ be the flow mapping initial phase space points to their positions at time $t$. Then the Jacobian of $T_t$ satisfies $J(t) \equiv 1$; that is, the flow preserves volume in the $2n$-dimensional phase space. This is \text{Liouville's theorem}.
