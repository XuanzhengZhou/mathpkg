Given an increasing family of sigma-fields $\{\mathfrak{B}_n\}$, a random variable $N$ taking values in $\{0, 1, 2, \ldots, \infty\}$ is called a \text{stopping time} (relative to $\{\mathfrak{B}_n\}$) if
$$\{w \mid N(w) = n\} \in \mathfrak{B}_n$$
for every finite $n$. That is, the decision to stop at time $n$ depends only on information available up to time $n$.
