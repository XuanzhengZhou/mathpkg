A Markov process with continuous time parameter is a family of random variables $\{X_t, t \geq 0\}$ such that the conditional distribution of the future given the past and present depends only on the present. Two important classes are:
\begin{itemize}
\item \text{Jump processes}: the process stays in a state for an exponentially distributed time, then jumps to a new state according to a transition probability matrix.
\item \text{Diffusion processes}: continuous sample path processes characterized by drift and diffusion coefficients, locally behaving like Brownian motion with drift.
\end{itemize}
