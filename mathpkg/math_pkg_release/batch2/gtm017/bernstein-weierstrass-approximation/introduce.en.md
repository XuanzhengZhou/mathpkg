Bernstein gave an elegant probabilistic proof of the Weierstrass approximation theorem. The Bernstein polynomial $p_n(x)$ is the expected value of $f(Y/n)$ where $Y$ is a binomial random variable with $n$ trials and success probability $x$. By the weak law of large numbers, $Y/n$ concentrates near $x$, and continuity of $f$ ensures uniform convergence. This proof constructs explicit polynomial approximants and connects approximation theory to probability.
