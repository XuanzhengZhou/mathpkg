A reversible Markov chain satisfies detailed balance: the probability flux from $i$ to $j$ equals the flux from $j$ to $i$. This makes the chain statistically symmetric in time --- evolution forward and backward are indistinguishable. Reversibility is a sufficient condition for a function of a Markov chain to remain Markovian.
