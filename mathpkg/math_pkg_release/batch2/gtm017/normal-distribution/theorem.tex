A random variable is \textbf{normally distributed} with mean $m$ and variance $\sigma^2 > 0$ if its distribution function is
$$F(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} \int_{-\infty}^{x} e^{-(u-m)^2/(2\sigma^2)} du.$$

The mean is $EX = m$ and variance $E(X-m)^2 = \sigma^2$ (for $\sigma^2 = 0$, $X = m$ with probability one).

If $X$ is normal with mean 0 and variance 1, then $Y = m + \sigma X$ is normal with mean $m$ and variance $\sigma^2$.
