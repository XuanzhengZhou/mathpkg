Let ${}_R U_S$ define a Morita duality and let $M$ be $U$-reflexive. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $M$ is finitely generated (finitely cogenerated) iff $M^*$ is finitely cogenerated (finitely generated).
\item[(2)] $0 \to M_1 \xrightarrow{f} M_2 \xrightarrow{g} M_3 \to 0$ is (split) exact iff its dual $0 \to M_3^* \xrightarrow{g^*} M_2^* \xrightarrow{f^*} M_1^* \to 0$ is (split) exact.
\item[(3)] $M$ is simple, semisimple, indecomposable iff $M^*$ is.
\item[(4)] $c(M) = c(M^*)$.
\item[(5)] $\operatorname{Soc}(M^*) \cong (M/\operatorname{Rad} M)^*$ and $M^*/\operatorname{Rad} M^* \cong (\operatorname{Soc} M)^*$.
\item[(6)] $M$ is injective (projective) iff $M^*$ is projective (injective).
\item[(7)] $f: M_1 \to M_2$ is an injective envelope (projective cover) iff $f^*: M_2^* \to M_1^*$ is a projective cover (injective envelope).
\item[(8)] $M$ is a generator (cogenerator) iff $M^*$ is a cogenerator (generator).
\item[(9)] $l_R(M) = r_R(M^*)$.
\item[(10)] If $e$ is a primitive idempotent in $R$, then $Re/Je \cong (eR/eJ)^*$ where $J = J(R)$, and $E(Re/Je) \cong (eR)^*$.
\end{enumerate}
