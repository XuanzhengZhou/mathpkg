Under a duality between module categories, the contravariant functors $H'$ and $H''$ induce abelian group isomorphisms between Hom-sets, reversing the direction of morphisms. When applied to endomorphisms of a single object, this yields ring isomorphisms between the endomorphism rings. Because $H'$ is contravariant, the composition order is reversed: $H'(fg) = H'(g)H'(f)$. However, by adopting the usual operator conventions (viewing domain morphisms as right operators and codomain morphisms as left operators), one obtains a proper ring isomorphism $\operatorname{End}({}_R M) \cong \operatorname{End}(H'(M)_S)$.
