Let $(H', H'')$ be a duality between full subcategories ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}_S$ of ${}_R \mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{M}_S$. Then for each $M_1, M_2$ in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and each $N_1, N_2$ in $\mathcal{D}_S$, the restrictions of $H'$ to $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M_1, M_2)$ and of $H''$ to $\operatorname{Hom}_S(N_1, N_2)$ are abelian group isomorphisms
$$H': \operatorname{Hom}_R(M_1, M_2) \to \operatorname{Hom}_S(H'(M_2), H'(M_1)),$$
$$H'': \operatorname{Hom}_S(N_1, N_2) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(H''(N_2), H''(N_1)).$$
If $M$ in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and $N$ in $\mathcal{D}_S$ are non-zero, then these maps are ring isomorphisms
$$\operatorname{End}({}_R M) \to \operatorname{End}(H'(M)_S),$$
$$\operatorname{End}(N_S) \to \operatorname{End}({}_R H''(N)).$$
For $f, g \in \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, M)$, since $H'$ is contravariant,
$$H'(fg) = H'(g)H'(f).$$
Under the usual convention of viewing $f, g$ as right operators in $\operatorname{End}({}_R M)$ and $H'(f), H'(g)$ as left operators in $\operatorname{End}(H'(M)_S)$, the order is straightened out to give a ring isomorphism $\operatorname{End}({}_R M) \to \operatorname{End}(H'(M)_S)$.
