Let $C$ be a category. Recall (Exercise (3.4)) that a morphism $f: A \to B$ in $C$ is a monomorphism (an epimorphism) iff it is cancellable on the left (on the right). Suppose $(H', H'')$ is a duality between categories $C$ and $D$. Prove that a morphism $f: A \to B$ in $C$ is a monomorphism (an epimorphism) iff $H'(f): H'(B) \to H'(A)$ is an epimorphism (a monomorphism) in $D$. Thus in particular, the statements "$f$ is a monomorphism" and "$f$ is an epimorphism" are dual.
