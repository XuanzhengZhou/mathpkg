Let $(H', H'')$ be a duality between categories $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}$. Then a morphism $f: A \to B$ in $\mathcal{C}$ is a monomorphism (an epimorphism) if and only if $H'(f): H'(B) \to H'(A)$ is an epimorphism (a monomorphism) in $\mathcal{D}$. In particular, the statements "$f$ is a monomorphism" and "$f$ is an epimorphism" are dual.
