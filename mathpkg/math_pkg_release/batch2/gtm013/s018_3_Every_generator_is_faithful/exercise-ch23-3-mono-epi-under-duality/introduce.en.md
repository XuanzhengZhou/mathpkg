Under a duality $(H', H'')$ between categories, the contravariant functor $H'$ reverses the direction of morphisms. This exercise establishes that monomorphisms and epimorphisms are dual concepts: a morphism $f$ is monic in $\mathcal{C}$ precisely when $H'(f)$ is epic in $\mathcal{D}$, and vice versa. This is a fundamental example of how duality interchanges categorical properties.
