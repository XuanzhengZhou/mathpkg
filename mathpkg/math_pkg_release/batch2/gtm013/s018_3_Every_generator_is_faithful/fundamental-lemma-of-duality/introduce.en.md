The Fundamental Lemma of Duality establishes the key adjunction isomorphism $\mu_{MN}$ between Hom-sets for a duality $(H', H'')$. For each pair of objects $M$ in the domain category and $N$ in the codomain category, there is a natural isomorphism $\operatorname{Hom}_S(N, H'(M)) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, H''(N))$. This is the dual analog of the fundamental adjunction for equivalences (21.1), and it is the essential tool used in the proof of Morita's characterization theorem. The lemma also provides explicit formulas governing the behavior of the adjunction isomorphisms under composition with morphisms.
