This is the main theorem of the chapter, due to Morita. It characterizes all dualities between full subcategories of module categories as $U$-dualities, i.e., dualities induced by a bimodule $U$ via the Hom functors. The only requirements are that the subcategories contain the regular modules and are closed under isomorphic images. The theorem shows that every such duality is naturally isomorphic to $\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U)$ and $\operatorname{Hom}_S(-, U)$ for the bimodule $U = H''(S_S) \cong H'({}_R R)$, and that all objects in the subcategories are $U$-reflexive. This is the duality-theoretic analog of Morita's characterization of equivalences (Theorem 22.1).
