This corollary combines Theorem (26.1) with result (25.8) to give a clean characterization of left noetherian rings. A ring $R$ is left noetherian precisely when the category ${}_R\mathbf{M}$ possesses a single module $H$ whose direct sums suffice to embed every left $R$-module. The forward direction uses the fact that the class of injective modules cogenerates ${}_R\mathbf{M}$ when $R$ is left noetherian; the converse relies on the Kaplansky-Walker theorem and the structure of injective modules.
