This lemma complements Azumaya's Theorem (12.6): while Azumaya's theorem guarantees that a non-zero direct summand $K$ of a module $M$ decomposed into terms with local endomorphism rings contains a direct summand isomorphic to one of the $M_\alpha$, this lemma provides the converse direction. It shows that each $M_\alpha$ is isomorphic to a direct summand of either $K$ or $L$, effectively "distributing" the indecomposable summands of $N$ among the two complementary summands $K$ and $L$. The proof uses idempotents and the property that $f\operatorname{End}(_R M)f \cong \operatorname{End}(_R N)$ is a local ring.
