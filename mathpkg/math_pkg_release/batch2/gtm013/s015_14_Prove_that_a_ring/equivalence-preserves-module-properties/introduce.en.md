This proposition catalogs the fundamental module-theoretic properties preserved by a category equivalence. Every important structural property — simplicity, semisimplicity, finite generation/cogeneration, chain conditions, composition length, and indecomposability — is preserved. The proofs all reduce to the fact that the submodule lattice isomorphism established in 21.6/21.7 preserves these lattice-theoretic properties. This is the culmination of the structural analysis of equivalence: Morita equivalent rings have identical module theory.
