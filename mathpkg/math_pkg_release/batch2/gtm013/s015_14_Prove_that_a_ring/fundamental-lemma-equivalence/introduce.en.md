The Fundamental Lemma reveals that the inverse equivalences $F$ and $G$ actually form two adjoint pairs: $(G, F)$ and $(F, G)$. The maps $\phi$ and $\theta$ establish natural isomorphisms between the corresponding Hom sets, satisfying adjunction-like composition rules. This adjoint relationship is the key technical machinery used throughout the rest of Morita theory, allowing diagrams in ${}_R \mathbf{M}$ to be "transformed" into corresponding diagrams in ${}_S \mathbf{M}$ and vice versa.
