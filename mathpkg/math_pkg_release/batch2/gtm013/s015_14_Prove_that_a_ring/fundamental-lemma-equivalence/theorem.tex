Let $R$ and $S$ be equivalent rings with notation as in (21.1). Then the homomorphisms

$$\phi: \operatorname{Hom}_S(N, F(M)) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(G(N), M)$$
$$\theta: \operatorname{Hom}_S(F(M), N) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, G(N))$$

are isomorphisms natural in each variable $M \in {}_R \mathbf{M}$ and $N \in {}_S \mathbf{M}$. In particular, for each

$$\gamma \in \operatorname{Hom}_S(N_1, F(M_1)), \quad \delta \in \operatorname{Hom}_S(F(M_2), N_2)$$
$$\bar{\gamma} \in \operatorname{Hom}_R(G(N_1), M_1), \quad \bar{\delta} \in \operatorname{Hom}_R(M_2, G(N_2))$$

and for each $h: M_1 \to M_2$, $k: N_2 \to N_1$ we have

(1) $\phi(F(h)\gamma k) = h \phi(\gamma) G(k)$,
(2) $\theta(k \delta F(h)) = G(k) \theta(\delta) h$,
(3) $\phi^{-1}(h \bar{\gamma} G(k)) = F(h) \phi^{-1}(\bar{\gamma}) k$,
(4) $\theta^{-1}(G(k) \bar{\delta} h) = k \theta^{-1}(\bar{\delta}) F(h)$.

Moreover, $\phi(\gamma)$ is a monomorphism (epimorphism) if and only if $\gamma$ is a monomorphism (epimorphism), and similarly for $\theta(\delta)$.
