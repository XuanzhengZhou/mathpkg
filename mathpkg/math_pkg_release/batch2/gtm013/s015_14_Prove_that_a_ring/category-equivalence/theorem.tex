Let $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}$ be arbitrary categories. A covariant functor

$$F: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$$

is a **category equivalence** if there exists a covariant functor

$$G: \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}$$

and natural isomorphisms

$$GF \cong 1_{\mathcal{C}} \quad \text{and} \quad FG \cong 1_{\mathcal{D}}.$$

A functor $G$ with this property is called an **inverse equivalence** of $F$. Two categories are **equivalent**, written $\mathcal{C} \approx \mathcal{D}$, if there exists a category equivalence from one to the other. This defines an equivalence relation on the class of all categories.
