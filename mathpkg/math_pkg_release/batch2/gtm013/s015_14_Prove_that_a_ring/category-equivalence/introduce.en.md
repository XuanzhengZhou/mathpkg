A category equivalence is a covariant functor $F: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ that admits an inverse functor $G: \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}$ up to natural isomorphism. This means the compositions $GF$ and $FG$ are naturally isomorphic to the identity functors on $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}$ respectively. Category equivalence is the correct notion of "sameness" for categories, weaker than isomorphism but preserving all categorical properties. For module categories, one restricts to additive equivalences between additive categories.
