This corollary translates the module-theoretic preservation results of 21.8 into ring-theoretic statements. Semisimplicity follows from (21.8.1) and the characterization of semisimple rings. The artinian and noetherian cases use the fact that a ring is left artinian (noetherian) iff each finitely generated left module is artinian (noetherian). Primitivity and having zero Jacobson radical are characterized by the existence of a faithful simple (respectively semisimple) module, which is preserved by equivalence.
