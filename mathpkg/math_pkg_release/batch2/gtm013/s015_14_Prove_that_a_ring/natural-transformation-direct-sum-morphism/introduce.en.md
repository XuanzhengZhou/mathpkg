This exercise establishes that for additive functors preserving direct sums, a natural transformation is an isomorphism on a direct sum if and only if it is an isomorphism on each summand. The forward direction follows from the naturality of $\eta$ and the fact that direct summands are preserved. The converse uses the universal property of the direct sum to stitch together the inverse isomorphisms on each summand.
