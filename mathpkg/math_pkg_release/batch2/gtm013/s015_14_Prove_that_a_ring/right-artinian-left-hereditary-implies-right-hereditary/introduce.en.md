This exercise shows that for a right artinian ring, the property of being left hereditary implies right hereditariness. A ring is left (right) hereditary if every left (right) ideal is projective. The proof uses the fact that for a right artinian ring, the Jacobson radical is nilpotent, along with properties of injective modules and the relationships between flatness and projectivity established in earlier exercises.
