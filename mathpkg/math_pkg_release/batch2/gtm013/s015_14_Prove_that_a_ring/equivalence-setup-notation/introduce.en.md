This establishes the standard notation used throughout the study of Morita equivalence. For equivalent rings $R$ and $S$, we fix inverse additive equivalences $F$ and $G$ between their module categories, together with natural isomorphisms $\eta: GF \cong 1$ and $\zeta: FG \cong 1$. The naturality of $\eta$ ensures that the above diagram commutes, which is the fundamental coherence condition used throughout the subsequent propositions and proofs.
