Let $R$ and $S$ be a pair of equivalent rings with inverse additive equivalences

$$F: {}_R \mathbf{M} \to {}_S \mathbf{M}, \qquad G: {}_S \mathbf{M} \to {}_R \mathbf{M}$$

and natural isomorphisms

$$\eta: GF \to 1_{{}_R \mathbf{M}}, \qquad \zeta: FG \to 1_{{}_S \mathbf{M}}.$$

For each ${}_R M$ there is an isomorphism $\eta_M: GF(M) \to M$ in ${}_R \mathbf{M}$ such that for each $M, M'$ in ${}_R \mathbf{M}$ and each $f: M \to M'$, the diagram

$$\begin{CD}
GF(M) @>{GF(f)}>> GF(M')\\
@V{\eta_M}VV @VV{\eta_{M'}}V\\
M @>>{f}> M'
\end{CD}$$

commutes. The natural isomorphism $\zeta$ determines a similar structure for $S$-modules.
