Let $R$ and $S$ be rings and let ${}_R U_S$ be a fixed bimodule. For each left $R$-module $M$ and each right $S$-module $N$, set

$$M^* = H'(M) = \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, U), \qquad N^* = H''(N) = \operatorname{Hom}_S(N, U),$$

and denote the natural transformations by

$$\sigma_M: M \to M^{**}, \qquad \sigma_N: N \to N^{**}.$$

The bimodule ${}_R U_S$ is said to define a **Morita duality**, or the duality given by $\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U)$ and $\operatorname{Hom}_S(-, U)$ is called a **Morita duality**, if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] ${}_R R$ and $S_S$ are $U$-reflexive;
\item[(2)] Every submodule and every factor module of a $U$-reflexive module is $U$-reflexive.
\end{enumerate}
