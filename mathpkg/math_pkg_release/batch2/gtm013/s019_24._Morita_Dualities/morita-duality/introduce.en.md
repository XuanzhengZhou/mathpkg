A Morita duality is a contravariant category equivalence between full subcategories of left $R$-modules and right $S$-modules induced by the Hom functors $\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U)$ and $\operatorname{Hom}_S(-, U)$ for a fixed bimodule ${}_R U_S$. Unlike the more common Morita equivalence (which is covariant), a Morita duality requires the bimodule $U$ to be an injective cogenerator on both sides and imposes strong finiteness conditions on the rings involved. Morita dualities are relatively rare; they exist only when both rings are left and right artinian.
