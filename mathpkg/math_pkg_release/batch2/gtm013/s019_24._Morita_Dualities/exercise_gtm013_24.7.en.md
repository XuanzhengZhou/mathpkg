Let ${}_R U_S$ define a Morita duality. Since $U_S$ is isomorphic to $({}_R R)^*$ and ${}_R U$ is isomorphic to $(S_S)^*$, there exist lattice anti-isomorphisms between the submodule lattices of ${}_R R$ and $U_S$ and between those of $S_S$ and ${}_R U$. (See (24.5).) Prove that:

(1) For each ${}_R I \leq {}_R R$ and each $V_S \leq U_S$, $l_R(r_U(I)) = I$ and $r_U(l_R(V)) = V$.

(2) For each $K_S \leq S_S$ and each ${}_R W \leq {}_R U$, $r_S(l_U(K)) = K$ and $l_U(r_S(W)) = W$.
