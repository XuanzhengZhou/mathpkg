(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b). If there is a duality between ${}_R \mathbf{FM}$ and $\mathbf{FM}_S$, then by the general theory of dualities (Theorem 23.5), this duality is induced by a bimodule ${}_R U_S$ via the Hom functors. Since every finitely generated module is $U$-reflexive, in particular ${}_R R$ and $S_S$ are $U$-reflexive. By Osofsky's Lemma (24.7), $R$ cannot contain an infinite direct sum of non-zero left ideals. This together with the fact that the duality exchanges sums and intersections (24.5) implies that $R$ is left artinian (by (15.21)). The existence of a Morita duality follows from Theorem 24.1.

(b) $\Rightarrow$ (a). If $R$ is left artinian and a Morita duality exists, then by (15.21) every left $R$-module has a finitely generated essential socle. By the lattice anti-isomorphism of (24.5), the dual of a finitely generated module is finitely cogenerated. Since every finitely cogenerated module over an artinian ring is $U$-reflexive (24.6.3), the duality restricts to a duality between ${}_R \mathbf{FM}$ and $\mathbf{FM}_S$.

(a) $\Leftrightarrow$ (c). By symmetry. The last statement follows from (24.7), (15.21), and (24.5).
