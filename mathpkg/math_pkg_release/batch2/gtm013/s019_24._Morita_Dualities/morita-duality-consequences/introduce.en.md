Theorem 24.6 collects the far-reaching structural consequences of the existence of a Morita duality between $R$ and $S$. The rings must share the same lattice of two-sided ideals and have isomorphic centers. Moreover, every finitely generated or finitely cogenerated module is forced to be $U$-reflexive, and the duality exchanges finitely generated projective modules with finitely cogenerated injective modules.
