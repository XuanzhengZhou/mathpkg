Let $R$ and $S$ be rings, let ${}_R M$, $N_S$ and ${}_R U_S$ be modules and let $\mu: M \times N \to U$ be $(R, S)$-bilinear. Then for all submodules $M', M''$, and $M_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in A$) of $M$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $M' \leq M''$ implies $r_N(M') \geq r_N(M'')$;
\item[(2)] $M' \leq l_M r_N(M')$;
\item[(3)] $r_N(M') = r_N l_M r_N(M')$;
\item[(4)] $r_N(\sum_A M_\alpha) = \bigcap_A r_N(M_\alpha)$;
\item[(5)] $r_N(\bigcap_A M_\alpha) \geq \sum_A r_N(M_\alpha)$.
\end{enumerate}

Moreover, analogous statements hold for the $S$-submodules of $N$.
