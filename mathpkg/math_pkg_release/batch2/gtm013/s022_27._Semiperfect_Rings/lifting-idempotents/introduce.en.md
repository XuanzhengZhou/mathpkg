Lifting idempotents is a fundamental technique in ring theory where one asks whether an idempotent in a factor ring $R/I$ can be represented by an idempotent in the original ring $R$. While the natural map $R \to R/I$ always sends idempotents to idempotents, the converse is not generally true. The ability to lift idempotents modulo the Jacobson radical $J(R)$ is the defining property of semiperfect rings, and it allows the decomposition theory of the semisimple ring $R/J(R)$ to be "lifted" to $R$ itself.
