If $M \cong R e / I e$ for some idempotent $e \in R$ and left ideal $I \subseteq J(R)$, then the natural epimorphism $R e \to R e / I e$ is a projective cover since $I e \subseteq J e \ll R e$ by (15.13).

Conversely, suppose ${}_R M$ is cyclic with projective cover $p: P \to M$. Since $M$ is cyclic, $P$ can be taken to be $R e$ for some idempotent $e \in R$ (any projective cover of a cyclic module is isomorphic to a direct summand of $R$). Then $\operatorname{Ker} p \ll R e$, so $\operatorname{Ker} p \subseteq J e$. Writing $\operatorname{Ker} p = I e$ for some left ideal $I \subseteq J(R)$ yields the desired form.
