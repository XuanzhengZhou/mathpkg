Theorem 27.16 establishes that semiperfect rings admit a block decomposition — a decomposition as a ring direct sum of indecomposable two-sided ideals (blocks). A primitive idempotent belongs to exactly one block, and two primitive idempotents belong to the same block precisely when their corresponding simple modules are "linked" via non-vanishing homomorphism spaces. This generalizes the block theory of Artinian rings to the broader class of semiperfect rings.
