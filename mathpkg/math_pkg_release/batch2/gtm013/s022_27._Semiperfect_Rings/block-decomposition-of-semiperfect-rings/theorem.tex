Every semiperfect ring $R$ has a block decomposition. If $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ is a complete set of primitive idempotents in $R$, then the blocks of $R$ correspond to the equivalence classes of the relation $e_i \approx e_j$ defined by the existence of a sequence $e_i = e_{i_0}, e_{i_1}, \ldots, e_{i_k} = e_j$ such that $e_{i_{t-1}} R e_{i_t} \neq 0$ for $t = 1, \ldots, k$. Moreover, members of a pair of left modules over a semiperfect ring have a common composition factor if and only if there exists a primitive idempotent $e \in R$ that annihilates neither module.
