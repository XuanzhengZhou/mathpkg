Theorem 27.11 describes the complete structure of projective modules over semiperfect rings. Every projective module decomposes as a direct sum of primitive (indecomposable projective) submodules, each isomorphic to $R e_i$ for some primitive idempotent $e_i$ from the basic set. The decomposition is unique up to isomorphism, generalizing the Krull-Schmidt theorem. The proof relies on the fact that each endomorphism ring $e_i R e_i$ is local (by 27.6.b), together with the Crawley-Jonsson-Warfield theorem on decompositions complementing direct summands.
