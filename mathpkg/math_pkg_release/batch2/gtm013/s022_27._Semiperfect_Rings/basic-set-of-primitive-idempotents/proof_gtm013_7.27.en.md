The equivalence of (a) and (c) follows from (27.4) and (17.18). Also (c) and (d) are equivalent by (13.7). Every primitive left $R$-module is indecomposable and projective. So to prove the equivalence of (a) and (b) it will suffice to prove that every indecomposable projective $R$-module is primitive. Suppose then that ${}_R P$ is a non-zero projective. Then for some ${}_R P'$ and some set $A$,

$$P \oplus P' \cong R^{(A)}.$$

By (27.6.b) and (4.15), $R^{(A)}$ is a direct sum of primitive modules each with a local endomorphism ring. Thus by (12.6), $P$ itself is isomorphic to such a primitive module. The corresponding statements for right modules follow by symmetry.
