This proposition states that nil ideals are "small enough" to permit idempotent lifting: every idempotent in $R/I$ can be represented by an idempotent in $R$. The proof uses the binomial theorem and the nilpotency of $g - g^2$ to construct the required idempotent $e$ from a given coset representative $g$. This result is fundamental because the Jacobson radical $J(R)$ often contains nil ideals, providing one route to proving that idempotents lift modulo $J(R)$.
