A semiperfect ring combines two key properties: the factor ring $R/J(R)$ is semisimple (Artinian with zero radical), and every idempotent in this semisimple quotient can be lifted to an idempotent in $R$. Local rings and left/right Artinian rings are semiperfect. In a semiperfect ring, the Jacobson radical is the unique largest ideal containing no nonzero idempotents. This class of rings admits a rich decomposition theory where the structure of projective modules is determined by the semisimple ring $R/J(R)$.
