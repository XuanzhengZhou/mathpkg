A left $R$-module homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between two left $R$-modules. It generalizes the notion of a linear transformation from vector spaces to modules over arbitrary rings. The defining property is $R$-linearity: the map respects both addition and scalar multiplication by elements of the ring $R$. Equivalently, via the endomorphism ring perspective, an $R$-homomorphism is precisely an abelian group homomorphism that commutes with the ring action.
