Let $\phi: R \to S$ be a ring homomorphism. Then $\phi$ induces an $(R,R)$-bimodule structure ${}_R S_R$ on $S$ via
$$(r, s) \mapsto \phi(r)s \quad \text{and} \quad (s, r) \mapsto s\phi(r).$$
Moreover, $\phi$ is an $(R,R)$-bimodule homomorphism $\phi: {}_R R_R \to {}_R S_R$, and its image is both an $(R,R)$-submodule and a subring of $S$. If $\phi$ is onto $S$ with kernel $K$, then $\phi$ induces lattice isomorphisms between the left, right, and two-sided ideal lattices of $S$ and the corresponding sublattices of ideals of $R$ that contain $K$.
