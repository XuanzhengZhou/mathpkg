Let $M$ and $N$ be left $R$-modules and let $f: M \to N$ be an $R$-homomorphism. Then $f$ is an isomorphism if and only if there exist functions $g, h: N \to M$ such that
$$fg = 1_N \quad \text{and} \quad hf = 1_M.$$
When these conditions are satisfied, $g = h$ is an $R$-isomorphism (the inverse of $f$).
