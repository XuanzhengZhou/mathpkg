Right multiplication by an element $x$ of a module provides a canonical homomorphism from the ring (viewed as a module over itself) onto the cyclic submodule generated by $x$. The kernel is the left annihilator of $x$. This construction, together with the First Isomorphism Theorem, shows that every cyclic module is isomorphic to a quotient of the ring by a left ideal.
