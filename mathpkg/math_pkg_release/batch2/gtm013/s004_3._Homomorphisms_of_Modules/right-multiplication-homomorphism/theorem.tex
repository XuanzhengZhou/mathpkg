Let ${}_R M$ be a left $R$-module and $x \in M$. The \textbf{right multiplication by $x$} is the map
$$\rho_x: R \to Rx, \qquad \rho_x(r) = rx \quad (r \in R).$$
This is a left $R$-homomorphism from ${}_R R$ onto the cyclic submodule $Rx$. Its kernel is the \textbf{left annihilator} of $x$ in $R$:
$$\text{Ker}\, \rho_x = l_R(x) = \{r \in R \mid rx = 0\}.$$
Consequently, by the First Isomorphism Theorem, $R / l_R(x) \cong Rx$.
