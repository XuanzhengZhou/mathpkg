Let $f: M \to N$ be an epimorphism of left $R$-modules with kernel $K = \text{Ker}\, f$. Then there are inverse lattice isomorphisms between the lattice $\mathcal{S}(M)/K$ of submodules of $M$ that contain $K$ and the lattice $\mathcal{S}(N)$ of all submodules of $N$. These isomorphisms are induced by the First Isomorphism Theorem $M/K \cong N$ together with the lattice correspondence for quotient modules.
