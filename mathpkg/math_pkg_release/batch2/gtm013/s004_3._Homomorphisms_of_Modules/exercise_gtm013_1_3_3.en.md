Let $f: M \to N$ be an $R$-homomorphism. Prove that if $f$ is monic, then
$$l_R(M) \supseteq l_R(N),$$
whereas if $f$ is epic, then
$$l_R(M) \subseteq l_R(N).$$

Here $l_R(M) = \{r \in R \mid rM = 0\}$ denotes the left annihilator of the module $M$ in $R$.
