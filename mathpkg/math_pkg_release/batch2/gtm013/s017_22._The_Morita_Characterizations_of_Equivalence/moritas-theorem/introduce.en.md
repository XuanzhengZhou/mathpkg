Morita's Theorem provides the definitive characterization of when two rings have equivalent module categories. It establishes that ring equivalence is precisely captured by the existence of a progenerator bimodule that is balanced, with the equivalence functors being given by the tensor product and Hom functors associated to that bimodule. This theorem generalizes the classical Wedderburn--Artin theory and forms the cornerstone of Morita theory.
