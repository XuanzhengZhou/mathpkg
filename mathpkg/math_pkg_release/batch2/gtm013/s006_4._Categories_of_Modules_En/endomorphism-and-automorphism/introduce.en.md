An endomorphism of a module $M$ is a homomorphism from $M$ to itself, and an automorphism is an isomorphism from $M$ to itself. The set of all endomorphisms $\operatorname{End}(_R M)$ forms not only an abelian group under pointwise addition (by 4.1) but also a ring under composition. The endomorphisms are conventionally written as right operators: for $f \in \operatorname{End}(_R M)$ and $x \in M$, one writes $xf$ rather than $f(x)$. The ring structure makes $\operatorname{End}(_R M)$ a central object of study, as its idempotents correspond to direct summands of $M$.
