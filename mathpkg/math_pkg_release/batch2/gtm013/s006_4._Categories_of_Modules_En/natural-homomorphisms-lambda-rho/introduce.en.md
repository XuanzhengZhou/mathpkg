Given a bimodule $_R M_S$, the left multiplication by elements of $R$ gives a ring homomorphism $\lambda: R \to \operatorname{End}(M_S)$, because the left $R$-action commutes with the right $S$-action: $(rx)s = r(xs)$. Similarly, right multiplication by elements of $S$ gives $\rho: S \to \operatorname{End}(_R M)$. These natural maps encode how the ring actions are represented as endomorphisms. The faithfulness of the module actions is characterized by the injectivity of these maps. The study of when $\lambda$ and $\rho$ are surjective (or isomorphisms) leads to the concepts of balanced and faithfully balanced bimodules.
