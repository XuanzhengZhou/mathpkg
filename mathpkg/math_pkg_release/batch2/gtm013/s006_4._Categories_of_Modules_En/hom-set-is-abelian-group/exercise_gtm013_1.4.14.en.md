Let $M$ and $N$ be left $R$-modules and let $f: M \rightarrow N$ be an $R$-isomorphism. Prove that

(1) There is a ring isomorphism $\phi_1: \operatorname{End}(_R M) \rightarrow \operatorname{End}(_R N)$ such that $f(m \gamma) = f(m) \phi_1(\gamma)$ for all $m \in M$, $\gamma \in \operatorname{End}(_R M)$.

(2) There is a ring isomorphism $\phi_2: \operatorname{BiEnd}(_R M) \rightarrow \operatorname{BiEnd}(_R N)$ such that $f(bm) = \phi_2(b) f(m)$ for all $m \in M$, $b \in \operatorname{BiEnd}(_R M)$.

(3) If $_R M$ is balanced, then so is $_R N$.
