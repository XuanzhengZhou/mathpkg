Let $R$ and $S$ be rings and let $\phi: R \rightarrow S$ be a ring homomorphism. For each $_S M$ let $T_\phi(M)$ be the $R$-module $(M, \gamma)$ where $\gamma(r)(x) = \phi(r) x$ for each $r \in R$, $x \in M$. For each pair $_S M, _S N$ and each $f \in \operatorname{Hom}_S(M, N)$, let $T_\phi(f) \in \operatorname{Hom}_R(T_\phi(M), T_\phi(N))$ be $T_\phi(f) = f$. Prove that

(1) $T_\phi$ defines a covariant functor from $_S\mathbf{M}$ to $_R\mathbf{M}$.

(2) $T_\phi$ is an embedding (i.e., is faithful and injective on objects).
