A bimodule $_R M_S$ has a left $R$-action and a right $S$-action that commute. These actions induce ring homomorphisms $\lambda: R \to \operatorname{End}(M_S)$ (left multiplication) and $\rho: S \to \operatorname{End}(_R M)$ (right multiplication). The bimodule is faithful on the left (resp. right) if the corresponding map is injective. A balanced bimodule is one in which every $S$-endomorphism of $M$ comes from left multiplication by some element of $R$, and every $R$-endomorphism comes from right multiplication by some element of $S$ (i.e., both $\lambda$ and $\rho$ are surjective). If both maps are isomorphisms, the bimodule is faithfully balanced.
