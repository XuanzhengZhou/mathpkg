If $e$ is a non-zero idempotent in a ring $R$, then

$$\rho: eRe \rightarrow \operatorname{End}(_R Re) \quad \text{and} \quad \lambda: eRe \rightarrow \operatorname{End}(eR_R)$$

defined via

$$\rho(ere): ae \mapsto aere \quad \text{and} \quad \lambda(ere): ea \mapsto erea$$

are ring isomorphisms. In particular,

$$\operatorname{BiEnd}(_R Re) = \operatorname{End}(Re_{eRe}).$$
