Proposition 4.15 identifies the endomorphism ring of the left ideal $Re$ (generated by an idempotent $e$) with the corner ring $eRe$, and similarly the endomorphism ring of the right ideal $eR$ with $eRe$ (acting on the opposite side). This generalizes Proposition 4.11 (the case $e = 1$) and is a fundamental result for the Morita theory of module categories: it establishes that idempotents correspond to projective direct summands whose endomorphism rings are corner rings of $R$. The final statement $\operatorname{BiEnd}(_R Re) = \operatorname{End}(Re_{eRe})$ follows from applying (4.12) and the identification of the endomorphism ring.
