From the proof of (4.12), we have $\operatorname{End}(_B M) \leq \operatorname{End}(_R M) = T$, and the map $T \to \operatorname{End}(_B M)$ is shown to be surjective. Since $T$ acts faithfully on $M$, it is also injective, so $\operatorname{End}(_B M) \cong T$. By definition, $\operatorname{BiEnd}(M_T) = \operatorname{End}(_B M)$ (with $B = \operatorname{End}(M_T)$), so $\operatorname{BiEnd}(M_T) = T$.
