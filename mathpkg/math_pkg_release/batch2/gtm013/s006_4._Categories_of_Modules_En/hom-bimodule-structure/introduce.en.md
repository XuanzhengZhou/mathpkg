Although the basic $R$-action on $M$ and $N$ does not transfer to $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M,N)$, additional module structures on $M$ or $N$ over other rings do induce module structures on the Hom set. This proposition describes how a bimodule structure on $M$ or $N$ transfers to an action on $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M,N)$. A key mnemonic: when transferring from the first variable $M$, the sides switch (contravariance); when transferring from the second variable $N$, the sides stay the same (covariance). For example, if $M$ is an $R$-$S$-bimodule and $N$ is an $R$-$T$-bimodule, then $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M,N)$ becomes an $S$-$T$-bimodule via $(sf)(x) = f(xs)$ and $(ft)(x) = f(x)t$.
