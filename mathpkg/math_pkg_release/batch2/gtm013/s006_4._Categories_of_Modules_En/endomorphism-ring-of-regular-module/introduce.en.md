Proposition 4.11 establishes that the endomorphism ring of the regular right module $R_R$ is canonically isomorphic to $R$ itself via left multiplication, and dually, the endomorphism ring of the regular left module $_R R$ is isomorphic to $R$ via right multiplication. This is a special case of (4.5): since $\operatorname{Hom}_R(R_R, R_R) \cong R$ as right $R$-modules, composing with the ring structure of endomorphisms yields a ring isomorphism. Consequently, the regular bimodule $_R R_R$ is faithfully balanced, meaning that the natural maps $R \to \operatorname{BiEnd}(_R R)$ and $R \to \operatorname{BiEnd}(R_R)$ are isomorphisms.
