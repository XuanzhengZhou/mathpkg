Given two $S$-modules $M$ and $N$ and a ring homomorphism $\phi: R \to S$, the map $\phi$ pulls back the $S$-module structure to an $R$-module structure on both $M$ and $N$ via $r \cdot x = \phi(r)x$. Any $S$-homomorphism $f: M \to N$ then satisfies $f(rx) = f(\phi(r)x) = \phi(r)f(x) = r f(x)$, so $f$ is also an $R$-homomorphism. This yields a chain of subgroup inclusions among the Hom sets: $\operatorname{Hom}_S(M,N)$ is contained in $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M,N)$, which equals $\operatorname{Hom}_{\phi(R)}(M,N)$, and all are subgroups of the abelian group $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(M,N)$ of all additive group homomorphisms from $M$ to $N$.
