From (4.2), for $M = N$, we have the subgroup inclusions
$$\operatorname{Hom}_S(M,M) \leq \operatorname{Hom}_R(M,M) = \operatorname{Hom}_{\phi(R)}(M,M) \leq \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(M,M).$$

Since composition of endomorphisms is preserved under these inclusions (the ring structure is the same—composition of maps), each inclusion is a subring inclusion. Thus as rings,
$$\operatorname{End}(_S M) \leq \operatorname{End}(_R M) = \operatorname{End}(_{\phi(R)} M) \leq \operatorname{End}_{\mathbb{Z}}(M).$$
