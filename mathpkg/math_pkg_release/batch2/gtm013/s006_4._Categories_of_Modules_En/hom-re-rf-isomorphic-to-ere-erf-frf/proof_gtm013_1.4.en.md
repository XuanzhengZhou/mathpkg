Apply Proposition 4.6 with $M = Rf$, $S = R$, and the bimodule $_R (Rf)_R$. Then $eM = eRf$, which is an $eRe$-$fRf$-bimodule. Proposition 4.6 gives the bimodule isomorphism

$$\rho: eRf \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(Re, Rf), \quad \rho(erf)(se) = s(erf) = serf.$$

Thus $\operatorname{Hom}_R(Re, Rf) \cong {}_{eRe} eRf_{fRf}$.

For the second isomorphism, apply the right-sided version of Proposition 4.6 with $M = eR$ and the idempotent $f$. This yields $Mf = eRf$ and

$$\lambda: eRf \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(fR, eR), \quad \lambda(erf)(fr) = erfr.$$

Hence $\operatorname{Hom}_R(fR, eR) \cong {}_{eRe} eRf_{fRf}$, and the result follows.
