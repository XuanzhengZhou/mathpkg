This corollary specializes Proposition 4.6 to the case where $M = Rf$ (or $M = eR$). It shows that the abelian group of $R$-homomorphisms between the left ideals $Re$ and $Rf$ is isomorphic, as a bimodule, to the Peirce component $eRf$ with its natural $eRe$-$fRf$-bimodule structure. Moreover, it is also isomorphic to $\operatorname{Hom}_R(fR, eR)$, the Hom between the corresponding right ideals. This result is central to the Morita theory of equivalences between module categories.
