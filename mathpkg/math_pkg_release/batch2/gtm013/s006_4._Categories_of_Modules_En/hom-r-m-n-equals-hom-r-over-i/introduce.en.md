When an ideal $I$ of $R$ annihilates both modules $M$ and $N$, the $R$-module structure on each factors uniquely through the quotient ring $R/I$. Concretely, the scalar multiplication $r \cdot x$ depends only on the coset $r + I$. Consequently, every $R$-homomorphism between $M$ and $N$ is automatically an $R/I$-homomorphism, and vice versa. Thus the Hom sets with respect to $R$ and with respect to $R/I$ are identical as abelian groups. Combined with (4.2), this yields $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M,N) = \operatorname{Hom}_{R/I}(M,N)$.
