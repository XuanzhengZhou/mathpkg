For any left $R$-module $M$, there is a canonical isomorphism $\rho: M \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(R, M)$ sending each $x \in M$ to the homomorphism $r \mapsto rx$. This is a special case of the bimodule structure induced on Hom sets (4.4.1) applied to the regular bimodule $_R R_R$. The isomorphism establishes that $M$ can be recovered as the set of $R$-linear maps from the regular module $R$ into $M$. Its inverse sends a homomorphism $f: R \to M$ to $f(1)$, the image of the identity element.
