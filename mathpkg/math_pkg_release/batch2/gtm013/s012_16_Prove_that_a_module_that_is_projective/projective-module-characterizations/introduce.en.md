A left module over a ring is projective precisely when the covariant Hom functor it represents preserves epimorphisms. This proposition gives four equivalent formulations of projectivity: the standard diagram-lifting definition, the condition that $\operatorname{Hom}_R(P, -)$ sends epimorphisms to epimorphisms, exactness of the Hom functor for any compatible bimodule structure, and preservation of exactness of three-term sequences. These characterizations are fundamental tools for working with projective modules in homological algebra.
