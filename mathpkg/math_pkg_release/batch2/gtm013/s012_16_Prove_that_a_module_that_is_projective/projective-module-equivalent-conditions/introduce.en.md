A left module is projective exactly when every epimorphism onto it splits, which in turn is equivalent to being isomorphic to a direct summand of a free module. This theorem provides the most practical characterizations of projective modules: the splitting condition for epimorphisms and the structural description as a direct summand of a free module. These equivalences are central to the theory of projective modules and their applications in homological algebra.
