**Proof.** By (17.2), $P$ is isomorphic to a direct summand of a free left $R$-module $R^{(A)}$. Write $R^{(A)} = P \\oplus P'$. Then

$$J^{(A)} = J \\cdot R^{(A)} = JP \\oplus JP'.$$

Since $JP \\leq \\operatorname{Rad}(P)$ and $JP' \\leq \\operatorname{Rad}(P')$ (see (15.18)), we must have $\\operatorname{Rad}(P) = JP$.
