**Proof.** Exercise 17.5.

(1) [Hint: Exercises (13.8) and (17.4).] The equivalences follow from the fact that a minimal left ideal in a semiprime ring is generated by an idempotent, hence is projective and simple. The symmetry between left and right follows from (17.4).

(2) If $R = \operatorname{End}(V_D)$ for a vector space $V$ over a division ring $D$, then $R$ acts faithfully and transitively on $V$, making it primitive. The set of endomorphisms of finite rank contains minimal left ideals generated by idempotents of rank 1.

(3) This is the Jacobson Density Theorem: a left primitive ring is isomorphic to a dense ring of linear transformations of a vector space over a division ring. The existence of a minimal left ideal forces the transformations to have finite rank.
