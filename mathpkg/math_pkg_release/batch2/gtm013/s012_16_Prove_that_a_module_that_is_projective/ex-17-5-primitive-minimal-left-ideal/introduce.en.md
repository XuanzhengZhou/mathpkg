A primitive ring with a minimal left ideal has a very rigid structure: it is equivalent to having a simple projective left module, and symmetrically for the right side. This exercise establishes the equivalence of various primitivity conditions and connects them to the classic example: the endomorphism ring of a vector space (over a division ring) is always primitive with a minimal left ideal. More precisely, such rings embed as dense rings of finite-rank linear transformations, a fundamental result in the Jacobson density theorem.
