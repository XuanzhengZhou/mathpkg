**Proof.** Exercise 16.17.

(1) Semisimple modules: every submodule is a direct summand, so the lifting/extension properties hold automatically.

(2) The group $\bigoplus_{\mathbb{P}} \mathbb{Z}_p$: each $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is simple, and the direct sum of pairwise non-isomorphic simple modules is quasi-projective and quasi-injective. However, its annihilator is $0$, and as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module it is neither projective (not free) nor injective (not divisible).

(3) Biendomorphism ring: follows from the fact that the biendomorphism ring acts faithfully.

(4) Direct sum criterion: depends on the pairwise relative projectivity/injectivity conditions.
