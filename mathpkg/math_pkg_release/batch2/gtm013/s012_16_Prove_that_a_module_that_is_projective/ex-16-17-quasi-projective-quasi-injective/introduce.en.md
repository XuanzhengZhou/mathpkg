A module $U$ is quasi-projective if it is projective relative to itself — meaning homomorphisms from $U$ lift through epimorphisms onto $U$. Quasi-injectivity is defined dually. This exercise explores fundamental properties: semisimple modules are both quasi-projective and quasi-injective; the direct sum of $\mathbb{Z}_p$ over all primes is quasi-projective/injective but not projective/injective; and quasi-projectivity is preserved when passing to the biendomorphism ring.
