A projective cover of a module $M$ is a "minimal" projective presentation: a projective module $P$ together with an epimorphism $p: P \to M$ whose kernel is superfluous (small) in $P$. This means that no proper submodule of $P$ maps onto $M$, making $(P, p)$ the most economical projective approximation of $M$. Projective covers, when they exist, are unique up to isomorphism.
