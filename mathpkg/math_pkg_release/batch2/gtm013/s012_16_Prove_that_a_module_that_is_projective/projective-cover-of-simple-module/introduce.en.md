A projective module is a projective cover of a simple module exactly when its endomorphism ring is local. This provides a crucial link between the internal structure of the module (its radical being a maximal superfluous submodule) and the ring-theoretic property of its endomorphism ring being local. Such modules are precisely the cyclic projective modules $Re$ for primitive idempotents $e$, and they form the building blocks of module categories over semiperfect rings.
