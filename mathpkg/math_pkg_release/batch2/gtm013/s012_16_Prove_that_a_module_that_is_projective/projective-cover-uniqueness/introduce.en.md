Projective covers, when they exist, are essentially unique: any two projective covers of isomorphic modules are isomorphic via a lifting of the given isomorphism. More generally, any projective module mapping onto $M$ via an epimorphism contains a direct summand that is itself a projective cover of $M$. This proposition establishes the fundamental uniqueness property that makes projective covers a well-defined tool in module theory.
