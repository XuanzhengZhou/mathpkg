When $e$ is an idempotent in a ring $R$, the left module $Re$ and the right module $eR$ exhibit a striking symmetry: $Re$ is simple and faithful precisely when $eR$ is. Moreover, under this condition, the entire socle of $R$ (on either side) is the two-sided ideal $ReR$ generated by $e$. This exercise reveals a deep structural correspondence in the theory of primitive idempotents and the socle of a ring.
