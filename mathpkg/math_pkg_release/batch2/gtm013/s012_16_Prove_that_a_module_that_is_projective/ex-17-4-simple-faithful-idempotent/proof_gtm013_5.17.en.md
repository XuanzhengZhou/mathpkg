**Proof.** Exercise 17.4.

(1) [Hint: By (17.20), if $J(R) = 0$, then $Re$ is simple iff $eR$ is. If $Re$ is simple and faithful and $0 \neq r \in R$, then there exist $s, t \in R$ with $srte = e$.]

(2) If $Re$ is simple and faithful, then by the properties of the socle and the fact that $Re$ is a minimal left ideal, one can show that the sum of all minimal left ideals isomorphic to $Re$ equals the two-sided ideal $ReR$, which must then equal the entire left socle. The right-side equality follows symmetrically.
