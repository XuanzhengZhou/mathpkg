A natural isomorphism is a natural transformation $\eta: F \to G$ in which every component morphism $\eta_M: F(M) \to G(M)$ is an isomorphism. This means the two functors are not just related by a natural transformation but are essentially the same up to a uniform, structure-preserving identification of their values on each object.
