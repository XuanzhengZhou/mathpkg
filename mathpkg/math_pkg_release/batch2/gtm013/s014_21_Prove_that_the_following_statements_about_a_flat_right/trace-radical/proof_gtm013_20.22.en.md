(1) By definition, $\operatorname{Trad}({}_R R) = \operatorname{Ann}_R(S)$ where $S$ is the direct sum of representatives of simple right $R$-modules. By Exercise (19.18.8), $\operatorname{Ann}_R(S) = J(R)$. Hence $\operatorname{Trad}({}_R R) = J(R)$.

(2) If $R$ is commutative, then by Exercise (19.18.9), for any module $M$, $\operatorname{Ann}_M(S) = \operatorname{Rad} M$ where $\operatorname{Rad} M$ is the intersection of all maximal submodules. Combined with Exercise (15.5) which identifies this with the Jacobson radical of the module, we obtain $\operatorname{Trad} M = \operatorname{Rad} M$.

(3) If $R/J(R)$ is semisimple, then every simple right module is isomorphic to a minimal right ideal of $R/J(R)$. The annihilator of the direct sum $S$ then coincides with the radical of the module, giving $\operatorname{Trad} M = \operatorname{Rad} M$ by the same argument as in the commutative case applied to the factor ring $R/J(R)$.
