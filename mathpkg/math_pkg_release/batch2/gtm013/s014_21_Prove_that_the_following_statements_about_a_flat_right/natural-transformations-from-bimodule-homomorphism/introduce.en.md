A bimodule homomorphism $\theta: {}_R U_S \to {}_R V_S$ functorially induces natural transformations between the associated Hom and tensor functors. Pre-composition with $\theta$ gives a natural transformation $\operatorname{Hom}_R(V, -) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(U, -)$, while post-composition gives $\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(-, V)$. Similarly, tensoring with $\theta$ gives $(- \otimes_R U) \to (- \otimes_R V)$. If $\theta$ is an isomorphism, each induced transformation is also a natural isomorphism.
