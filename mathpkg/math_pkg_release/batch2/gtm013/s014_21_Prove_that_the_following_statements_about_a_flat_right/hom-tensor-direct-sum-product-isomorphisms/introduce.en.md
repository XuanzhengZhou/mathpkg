This proposition gives three natural isomorphisms of functors that describe how the Hom and tensor functors interact with direct sums and products. The Hom functor converts a direct sum in the first variable into a direct product, and preserves a direct product in the second variable. The tensor functor distributes over direct sums in the second variable. These isomorphisms are rephrasings of (16.5) and were used to prove that direct sums of projectives are projective and direct products of injectives are injective.
