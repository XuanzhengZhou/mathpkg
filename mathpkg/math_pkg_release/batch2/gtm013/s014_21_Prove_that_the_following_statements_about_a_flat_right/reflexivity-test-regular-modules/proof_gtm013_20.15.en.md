Recall that ${}_R R^* = \operatorname{Hom}_R({}_R R, {}_R U_S) \cong U_S$ via $\gamma \mapsto \gamma(1)$, and the $S$-action is given by $(\gamma s)(r) = \gamma(r)s$. Also ${}_R R^{**} \cong \operatorname{Hom}_S(U_S, U_S) = \operatorname{End}(U_S)$. Under these identifications, the evaluation map $\sigma_{{}_R R}: R \to \operatorname{End}(U_S)$ is given by

$$\sigma_{{}_R R}(r)(u) = [\sigma_{{}_R R}(r)](\gamma_u) = \gamma_u(r) = ru = \lambda(r)(u),$$

where $\gamma_u \in {}_R R^*$ corresponds to $u \in U_S$. Thus $\sigma_{{}_R R}$ coincides with the left multiplication map $\lambda: R \to \operatorname{End}(U_S)$. The conclusions about injectivity and surjectivity follow immediately, as do the characterizations of $U$-torsionlessness and $U$-reflexivity.
