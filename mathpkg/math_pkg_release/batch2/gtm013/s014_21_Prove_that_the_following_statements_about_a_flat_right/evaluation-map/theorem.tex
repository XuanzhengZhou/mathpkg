For each $M$ in ${}_R\mathcal{M}$ or $\mathcal{M}_S$, the \text{evaluation map} is defined by

$$[\sigma_M(m)](\gamma) = \gamma(m) \quad (m \in M, \gamma \in M^*).$$

This defines

$$\sigma_M: M \to M^{**}.$$

The evaluation map is an $R$-homomorphism if $M \in {}_R\mathcal{M}$ or an $S$-homomorphism if $M \in \mathcal{M}_S$. Moreover, the evaluation maps yield natural transformations $\sigma: 1 \to (-)^{**}$.
