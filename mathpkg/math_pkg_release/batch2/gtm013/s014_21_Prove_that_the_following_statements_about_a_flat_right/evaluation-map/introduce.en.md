For a module $M$ and its $U$-dual $M^* = \operatorname{Hom}(M, U)$, the evaluation map $\sigma_M: M \to M^{**}$ is defined by $[\sigma_M(m)](\gamma) = \gamma(m)$ for $m \in M$ and $\gamma \in M^*$. This map is a module homomorphism and is natural in $M$, giving a natural transformation $\sigma: 1 \to (-)^{**}$ from the identity functor to the double dual functor. The evaluation map generalizes the familiar embedding of a vector space into its double dual.
