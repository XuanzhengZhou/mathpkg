Let $R$ be a ring. Then there are natural isomorphisms:

(1) $\rho: 1_{R\mathcal{M}} \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(R, -)$ where for each ${}_R M$, each $m \in M$, and each $r \in R$,

$$\rho_M(m)(r) = rm.$$

(2) $\tau: (R \otimes_R -) \to 1_{R\mathcal{M}}$ where for each ${}_R M$, each $r \in R$, and each $m \in M$,

$$\tau_M(r \otimes m) = rm.$$
