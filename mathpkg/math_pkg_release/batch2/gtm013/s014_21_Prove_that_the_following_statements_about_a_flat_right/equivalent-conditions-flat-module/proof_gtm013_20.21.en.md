(a) ⇒ (b): If $V$ is completely faithful and ${}_R M \neq 0$, then by definition $\operatorname{Ann}_V(M) \neq V$, so there exists a non-zero homomorphic image. Since $V$ is flat, $V \otimes_R M$ detects this, giving $V \otimes_R M \neq 0$.

(b) ⇒ (c): Immediate, since every simple left $R$-module $T$ satisfies $T \neq 0$.

(c) ⇒ (d): For a maximal left ideal $I$, the simple module $T = R/I$ satisfies $V \otimes_R T \neq 0$. By Exercise (19.20), this implies $VI \neq V$.

(d) ⇒ (a): If $VI \neq V$ for every maximal left ideal $I$, then for any non-zero module $M$, taking a maximal left ideal $I$ containing the annihilator of a non-zero element gives $V \otimes_R (R/I) \neq 0$, hence $V$ is completely faithful by Exercise (19.1.1).
