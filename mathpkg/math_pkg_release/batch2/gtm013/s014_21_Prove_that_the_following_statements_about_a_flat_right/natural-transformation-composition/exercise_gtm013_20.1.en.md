Let $\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D},$ and $\mathcal{E}$ be categories, let $F, F', F'': \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ and $G, G': \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{E}$ be functors. Prove:

(1) If $\eta: F \to F'$ and $\mu: F' \to F''$ are natural transformations (isomorphisms), then so is their "composite" $\mu \circ \eta: F \to F''$ defined by $(\mu \circ \eta)_M = \mu_M \circ \eta_M$.

(2) If $\eta: F \to F'$ is a natural isomorphism, then its "inverse" $\eta^{-1}: F' \to F$ defined by $(\eta^{-1})_M = (\eta_M)^{-1}$ is also a natural isomorphism.
