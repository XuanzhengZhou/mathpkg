Given modules ${}_S P$, ${}_S U_T$ and ${}_T N$ there is a homomorphism, natural in $P$, $U$ and $N$,

$$\eta: \operatorname{Hom}_S(P, U) \otimes_T N \to \operatorname{Hom}_S(P, (U \otimes_T N))$$

defined via

$$\eta(\gamma \otimes_T n): p \mapsto \gamma(p) \otimes_T n.$$

If ${}_S P$ is finitely generated and projective, then $\eta$ is an isomorphism.
