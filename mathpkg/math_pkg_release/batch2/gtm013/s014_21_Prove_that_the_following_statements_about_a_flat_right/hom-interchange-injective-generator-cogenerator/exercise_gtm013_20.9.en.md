Given modules $N_S$ and ${}_R U_S$, use the isomorphism

$$\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, \operatorname{Hom}_S(N, {}_R U_S)) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_S(N, \operatorname{Hom}_R(-, {}_R U_S))$$

of Proposition (20.7) to prove that:
(1) $N_S$ projective and ${}_R U$ injective implies $\operatorname{Hom}_S(N, {}_R U_S)$ injective.
(2) $N_S$ a generator and $\operatorname{Hom}_S(N, {}_R U_S)$ injective implies ${}_R U$ injective.
(3) $N_S$ a generator and ${}_R U$ a cogenerator implies $\operatorname{Hom}_S(N, {}_R U_S)$ a cogenerator.
(4) $\operatorname{Hom}_S(N, {}_R U_S)$ a cogenerator implies ${}_R U$ a cogenerator.
