A natural transformation is a morphism between functors. Given two functors $F, G: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ of the same variance, a natural transformation $\eta: F \to G$ assigns to each object $M \in \mathcal{C}$ a morphism $\eta_M: F(M) \to G(M)$ such that the naturality square commutes for every morphism $f: M \to N$. This concept captures the idea of a "uniform" family of morphisms that respects the structure of the categories involved.
