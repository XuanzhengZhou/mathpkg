Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a full subcategory of ${}_R\mathcal{M}$ and let ${}_R U_S$ and ${}_R V_S$ be bimodules with ${}_R U$ and ${}_R V$ both in $\mathcal{C}$. Consider the functors $\operatorname{Hom}_R(U_S, -)$ and $\operatorname{Hom}_R(V_S, -)$ from $\mathcal{C}$ to ${}_S\mathcal{M}$, and $\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U_S)$ and $\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, V_S)$ from $\mathcal{C}$ to $\mathcal{M}_S$. Prove that:

(1) If $\phi: \operatorname{Hom}_R(U_S, -) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(V_S, -)$ is a natural isomorphism, then $\phi_U(1_U): V \to U$ and $\phi_V^{-1}(1_V): U \to V$ are inverse $(R, S)$-isomorphisms.

(2) If $\phi: \operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U_S) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(-, V_S)$ is a natural isomorphism, then $\phi_U(1_U): U \to V$ and $\phi_V^{-1}(1_V): V \to U$ are inverse $(R, S)$-isomorphisms.
