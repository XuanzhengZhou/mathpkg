This proposition states that the properties of being $U$-reflexive and $U$-torsionless are preserved under finite direct sums and direct summands. If $M \cong M_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus M_n$, then $M$ is $U$-reflexive (or $U$-torsionless) precisely when every summand $M_i$ is. This follows from Lemma 20.9 and the fact that the evaluation map on a direct sum is the direct sum of the evaluation maps.
