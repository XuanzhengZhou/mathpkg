By Lemma (20.9), the class of $U$-reflexive modules is closed under direct summands and finite direct sums. The same holds for $U$-torsionless modules since both properties are defined by the evaluation map $\sigma_M: M \to M^{**}$ being an isomorphism or monomorphism, and the natural transformation $\sigma: 1 \to (-)^{**}$ satisfies the conditions of Lemma 20.9. Specifically, if $M \cong M_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus M_n$, then $\sigma_M \cong \sigma_{M_1} \oplus \cdots \oplus \sigma_{M_n}$, so $\sigma_M$ is monic (resp. an isomorphism) iff each $\sigma_{M_i}$ is monic (resp. an isomorphism).
