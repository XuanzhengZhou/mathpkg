Given an indexed family of additive functors $(F_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ from a module category $\mathcal{C}$ to $\mathcal{D}$ (where $\mathcal{D}$ admits all direct sums and products), their direct sum $\bigoplus_A F_\alpha$ and direct product $\prod_A F_\alpha$ are defined coordinatewise. On objects, $(\bigoplus_A F_\alpha)(M) = \bigoplus_A F_\alpha(M)$ and on morphisms, $(\bigoplus_A F_\alpha)(f) = \bigoplus_A F_\alpha(f)$. These constructions produce new additive functors and are fundamental for decomposing functorial structures.
