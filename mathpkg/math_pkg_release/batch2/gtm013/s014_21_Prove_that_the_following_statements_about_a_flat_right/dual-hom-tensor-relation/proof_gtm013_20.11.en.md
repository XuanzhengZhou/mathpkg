Similar arguments to Proposition 20.10 can be used. First verify that $\eta$ is a $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism natural in all three variables.

For $P = R_R$, by (20.1) we have $\operatorname{Hom}_R(R, U) \cong U$ via evaluation at $1$. Then

$$N \otimes_T \operatorname{Hom}_R(R, U) \cong N \otimes_T U \cong \operatorname{Hom}_R(R, N \otimes_T U).$$

One checks that $\eta$ is precisely the composite of these isomorphisms for $P = R$, hence is an isomorphism in this case.

As in (20.10), by Lemma 20.9 the class of modules $P_R$ for which $\eta$ is an isomorphism is closed under direct summands and finite direct sums. Since $R_R$ belongs to this class, every finitely generated free right $R$-module does, and by (17.3) every finitely generated projective right $R$-module is a direct summand of one, completing the proof.
