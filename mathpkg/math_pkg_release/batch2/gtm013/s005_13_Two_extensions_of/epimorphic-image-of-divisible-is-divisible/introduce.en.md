Divisibility of modules is preserved under epimorphisms (surjective homomorphisms). Given any $n \in N$ and $a \neq 0$, one lifts $n$ to $M$, uses divisibility of $M$ to solve $ax = m$, and maps the solution forward. This property is analogous to the fact that quotients of divisible abelian groups are divisible.
