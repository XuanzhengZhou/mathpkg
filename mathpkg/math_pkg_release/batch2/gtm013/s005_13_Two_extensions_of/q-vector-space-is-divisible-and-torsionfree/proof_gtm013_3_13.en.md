*Proof.* This is Exercise 15(1) in the source text. For divisibility: given $0 \neq a \in R$ and $m \in M$, since $a^{-1} \in Q$, set $x = a^{-1}m \in M$; then $ax = a(a^{-1}m) = m$. For torsion-freeness: if $0 \neq a \in R$ and $m \in M$ satisfy $am = 0$, then multiplying by the inverse of $a$ in $Q$ yields $m = 0$.
