There are (at least) two non-equivalent extensions of $\mathbb{Z}_2$ by $\mathbb{Z}_4$. One is the split extension $\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_4$, and the other is $\mathbb{Z}_8$ with the natural injection $\mathbb{Z}_2 \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_8$ (mapping $1 \mapsto 4$) and the projection $\mathbb{Z}_8 \twoheadrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_4$ (modulo $2$). This illustrates that the group $\operatorname{Ext}^1_{\mathbb{Z}}(\mathbb{Z}_4, \mathbb{Z}_2) \cong \mathbb{Z}_2$ is nontrivial.
