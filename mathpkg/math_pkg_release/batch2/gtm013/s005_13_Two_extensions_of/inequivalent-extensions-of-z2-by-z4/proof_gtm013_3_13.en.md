*Proof.* This is Exercise 13(4) in the source text. The split extension $0 \to \mathbb{Z}_2 \to \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_4 \to \mathbb{Z}_4 \to 0$ and the nonsplit extension $0 \to \mathbb{Z}_2 \xrightarrow{\times 4} \mathbb{Z}_8 \xrightarrow{\bmod 2} \mathbb{Z}_4 \to 0$ are inequivalent because $\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_4$ has two elements of order $4$ while $\mathbb{Z}_8$ has four.
