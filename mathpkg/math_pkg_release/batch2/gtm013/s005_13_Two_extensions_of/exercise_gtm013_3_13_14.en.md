**Exercise 14 (Torsion submodule).**

Let $R$ be a commutative integral domain and let $M$ be a left $R$-module. The set
$$T(M) = \{x \in M \mid I_R(x) \neq 0\}$$
is a submodule (proof?) called the *torsion submodule* of $_R M$. If $T(M) = M$, then $M$ is *torsion*; if $T(M) = 0$, then $M$ is *torsion free*. Let $_R M$ and $_R N$ and let $f: M \rightarrow N$ be an $R$-homomorphism. Prove that:

1. $T(M/T(M)) = 0$.
2. If $N$ is torsion free, then $T(M) \leq \operatorname{Ker} f$.
3. If $M$ is torsion, then $\operatorname{Im} f \leq T(N)$.
