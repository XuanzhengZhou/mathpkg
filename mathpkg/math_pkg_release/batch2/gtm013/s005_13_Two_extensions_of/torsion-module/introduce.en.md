A module $M$ over a commutative integral domain is called torsion if every element has a nonzero annihilator. Examples include finite abelian groups as $\mathbb{Z}$-modules and the Prüfer $p$-group $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$. Torsion modules are the opposite extreme from torsion-free modules.
