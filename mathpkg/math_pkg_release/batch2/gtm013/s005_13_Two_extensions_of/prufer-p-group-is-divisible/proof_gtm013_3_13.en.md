*Proof.* This is Exercise 16(1) in the source text. Any element of $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ lies in some $\mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z}$. Given $m \in \mathbb{Z}$ and an element of order $p^k$, write $m = p^t u$ with $p \nmid u$. The equation $m x = y$ can be solved by moving to $\mathbb{Z}/p^{k+t}\mathbb{Z}$ where division by $p^t$ and multiplication by $u^{-1}$ are well-defined.
