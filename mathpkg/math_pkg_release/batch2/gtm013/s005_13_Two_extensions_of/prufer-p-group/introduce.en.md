The Prüfer $p$-group $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ (also denoted $\mathbb{Z}(p^\infty)$ or the quasicyclic $p$-group) is a fundamental example of a divisible, torsion abelian group. It is the $p$-primary component of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ and can be described as the group of $p^n$-th roots of unity in the complex numbers for all $n$, or as the direct limit of the cyclic groups $\mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z}$.
