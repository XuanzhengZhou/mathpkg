Two short exact sequences $0 \to K \xrightarrow{f} M \xrightarrow{g} N \to 0$ and $0 \to K \xrightarrow{f'} M' \xrightarrow{g'} N \to 0$ are said to be equivalent extensions of $K$ by $N$ if there exists a homomorphism $h: M \to M'$ making the diagram commute with the identity maps on $K$ and $N$. This defines an equivalence relation on the class of all extensions of $K$ by $N$, and equivalent extensions necessarily have $h$ as an isomorphism.
