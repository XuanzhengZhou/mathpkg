A finite semigroup $\mathcal{R}$ with $0$ such that $\mathcal{R} = \{e_1, \ldots, e_n\} \cup \mathcal{J}$ satisfying $\mathcal{R}\mathcal{J} \subseteq \mathcal{J}$, $\mathcal{J}\mathcal{R} \subseteq \mathcal{J}$, $\mathcal{J}^m = \{0\}$, and $e_i e_j = \delta_{ij} e_i$ (i.e., $\mathcal{J}$ is a nilpotent ideal and $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ are orthogonal idempotents $\notin \mathcal{J}$); and $\mathcal{R} = \bigcup_{ij} e_i \mathcal{R} e_j$ is called an algebra semigroup.

(1) Prove that if $\mathcal{R}$ is an algebra semigroup with $R = K\mathcal{R}$ (the semigroup algebra), then $J(R) = K\mathcal{J}$ and $R e_i = K\mathcal{R} e_i$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$).

(2) Complete the proof of Theorem 32.10.
