The forward direction follows from the structure theory of semiperfect rings. If $S \approx \mathbb{UTM}_n(D)$, then $S$ is a semiperfect ring whose basic ring is isomorphic to the $(1,1,\ldots,1)$ block upper triangular matrix ring over $D$, i.e., the ordinary $n \times n$ upper triangular matrix ring $\mathbb{T}_n(D)$. The idempotents of the basic ring lift to orthogonal idempotents in $S$, and the block decomposition of $S$ yields the $(m_1,\ldots,m_n)$ structure where $m_i$ is the multiplicity of the $i$-th simple module.

Conversely, if $S$ is isomorphic to an $(m_1,\ldots,m_n)$ block upper triangular matrix ring over $D$, then the basic ring of $S$ is $\mathbb{T}_n(D) = \mathbb{UTM}_n(D)$ (with all block sizes equal to $1$). By Morita's theorem, a ring is Morita equivalent to its basic ring, so $S \approx \mathbb{T}_n(D) = \mathbb{UTM}_n(D)$.
