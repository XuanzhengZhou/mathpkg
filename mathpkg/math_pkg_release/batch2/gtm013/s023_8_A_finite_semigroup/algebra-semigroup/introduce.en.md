An algebra semigroup is a finite semigroup with zero that decomposes as the disjoint union of a set of orthogonal idempotents $\{e_1, \ldots, e_n\}$ and a nilpotent ideal $\mathcal{J}$. The semigroup also satisfies the Pierce decomposition $\mathcal{R} = \bigcup_{i,j} e_i \mathcal{R} e_j$, meaning every element can be expressed in terms of its "matrix components" relative to the idempotents. When $K$ is a field, the semigroup algebra $K\mathcal{R}$ is a split basic finite-dimensional $K$-algebra, and this construction provides a combinatorial bridge between finite semigroups and finite-dimensional algebras.
