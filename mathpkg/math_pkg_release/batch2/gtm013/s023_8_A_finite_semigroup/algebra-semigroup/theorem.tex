A finite semigroup $\mathcal{R}$ with $0$ such that $\mathcal{R} = \{e_1, \ldots, e_n\} \cup \mathcal{J}$ satisfying
$$\mathcal{R}\mathcal{J} \subseteq \mathcal{J},\quad \mathcal{J}\mathcal{R} \subseteq \mathcal{J},\quad \mathcal{J}^m = \{0\},$$
and $e_i e_j = \delta_{ij} e_i$ (i.e., $\mathcal{J}$ is a nilpotent ideal and $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ are orthogonal idempotents not in $\mathcal{J}$); and
$$\mathcal{R} = \bigcup_{i,j} e_i \mathcal{R} e_j$$
is called an \textit{algebra semigroup}.
