This theorem describes the structure of indecomposable serial rings in terms of block upper triangular matrix rings over a division ring $D$. When the first Cartan invariant $c_1$ equals $1$, the ring $R$ is a proper homomorphic image of such a block upper triangular matrix ring. When $c_1 = i$ (the index of the simple module), $R$ is actually isomorphic to the block upper triangular matrix ring. This result is a fundamental structure theorem for serial rings, showing they are closely related to triangular matrix rings over division rings.
