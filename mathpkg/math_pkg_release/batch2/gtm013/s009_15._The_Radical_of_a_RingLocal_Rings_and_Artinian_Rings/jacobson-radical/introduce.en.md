The Jacobson radical $J(R)$ of a ring $R$ is defined as the radical $\operatorname{Rad}(R)$ of $R$ viewed as a ring. It is a two-sided ideal of $R$. Theorem 15.3 shows that $J(R)$ coincides with $\operatorname{Rad}(R_R)$, resolving any potential left-right ambiguity in the definition.
