Corollary 15.7 gives five equivalent characterizations of the Jacobson radical $J(R)$. An ideal $I$ equals $J(R)$ precisely when it is left quasi-regular and the factor $R/I$ is semiprimitive, or equivalently when it is superfluous as a left ideal and contained in or containing the radical in the appropriate sense.
