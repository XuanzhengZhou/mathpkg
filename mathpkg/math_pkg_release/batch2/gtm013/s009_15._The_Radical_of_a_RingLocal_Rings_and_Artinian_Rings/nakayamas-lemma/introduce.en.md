Nakayama's Lemma (Corollary 15.13) characterizes when a left ideal $I$ is contained in the Jacobson radical $J(R)$. It states that $I \leq J(R)$ if and only if $IM = M$ implies $M = 0$ for every finitely generated left $R$-module $M$, or equivalently, $IM$ is always superfluous in $M$. This is one of the most useful results in noncommutative ring theory.
