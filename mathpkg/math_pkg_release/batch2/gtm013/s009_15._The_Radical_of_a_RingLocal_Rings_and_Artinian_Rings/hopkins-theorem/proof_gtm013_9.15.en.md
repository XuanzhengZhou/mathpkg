[Note: The proof in the OCR source is partial and somewhat garbled. The following reconstructs the proof from the available text.]

Let $0 \neq x \in M$. Then $Rx$ is a factor of $R$, hence $Rx$ is artinian and $\operatorname{Soc} Rx = Rx \cap (\operatorname{Soc} M) \neq 0$ by (10.11). By (15.16), $R/J$ is semisimple, so by (15.17.e), $\operatorname{Soc} M = r_M(J)$. By (15.16) and (15.18), $\operatorname{Rad} M = JM$. By (15.19), $J$ is nilpotent, so $JM \ll M$ (see Exercise (5.18)).

\textbf{(a) $\Rightarrow$ (c).} If $M$ is finitely generated, there exists an $R$-epimorphism $R^{(n)} \to M \to 0$. Since $R$ is both artinian and noetherian (by the main statement), so is $M$ by (10.12) and (10.13). Hence $M$ has a composition series by (11.1).

\textbf{(c) $\Rightarrow$ (b) and (d).} By (11.1).

\textbf{(b) $\Rightarrow$ (a).} By (10.9).

\textbf{(d) $\Rightarrow$ (e).} If $M$ is artinian, then $M/JM$ is artinian by (10.12). Hence by (15.18) and (10.15), $M/JM$ is finitely generated.

\textbf{(e) $\Leftrightarrow$ (a).} Since $JM \ll M$, this follows from (15.18) and (10.4).
