Let $R$ be a ring with $J = J(R)$. Then $R$ is left artinian if and only if $R$ is left noetherian, $J$ is nilpotent, and $R/J$ is semisimple.

For a left $R$-module $M$ over a left artinian ring $R$, the following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $M$ is finitely generated;
\item[(b)] $M$ is noetherian;
\item[(c)] $M$ has a composition series;
\item[(d)] $M$ is artinian;
\item[(e)] $M/JM$ is finitely generated.
\end{enumerate}
