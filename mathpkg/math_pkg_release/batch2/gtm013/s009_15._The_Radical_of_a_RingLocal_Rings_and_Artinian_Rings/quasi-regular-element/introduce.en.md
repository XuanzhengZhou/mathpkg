An element $x$ in a ring $R$ is called left quasi-regular if $1 - x$ has a left inverse, and quasi-regular if $1 - x$ has a two-sided inverse. This generalizes nilpotence: every nilpotent element is quasi-regular, since $(1-x)^{-1} = 1 + x + \cdots + x^{n-1}$ when $x^n = 0$. Quasi-regularity plays a key role in characterizing the Jacobson radical.
