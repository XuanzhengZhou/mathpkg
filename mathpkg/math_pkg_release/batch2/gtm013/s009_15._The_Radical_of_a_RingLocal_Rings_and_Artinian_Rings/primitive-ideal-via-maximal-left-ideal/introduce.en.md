Proposition 15.1 characterizes primitive ideals as the annihilators of simple left $R$-modules: an ideal $P$ is left primitive if and only if $P = \ell_R(R/M)$ for some maximal left ideal $M$ of $R$. This follows from the fact that $R/P$ is primitive exactly when $P$ is the annihilator of a faithful simple module.
