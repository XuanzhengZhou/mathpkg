Proposition 15.2 establishes that for a left ideal $I$ of a ring $R$, the notions of being left quasi-regular, quasi-regular, and superfluous in $R$ are equivalent. This result is fundamental to the characterization of the Jacobson radical, as it allows the radical to be expressed equivalently in terms of quasi-regularity or superfluity.
