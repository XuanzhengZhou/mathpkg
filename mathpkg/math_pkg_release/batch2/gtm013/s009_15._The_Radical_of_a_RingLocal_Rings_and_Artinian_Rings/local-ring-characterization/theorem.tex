For a ring $R$, the following statements are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $R$ is a local ring;
\item[(b)] $R$ has a unique maximal left ideal;
\item[(c)] $J(R)$ is a maximal left ideal;
\item[(d)] The set of elements of $R$ without left inverses is closed under addition;
\item[(e)] $J(R) = \{x \in R \mid Rx \neq R\}$;
\item[(f)] $R/J(R)$ is a division ring;
\item[(g)] $J(R) = \{x \in R \mid x \text{ is not invertible}\}$;
\item[(h)] If $x \in R$, then either $x$ or $1 - x$ is invertible.
\end{enumerate}
