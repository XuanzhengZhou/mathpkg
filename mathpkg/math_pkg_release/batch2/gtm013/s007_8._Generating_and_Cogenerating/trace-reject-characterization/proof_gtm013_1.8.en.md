(1) Let $(U_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ be an indexed set in $\mathcal{U}$ and let $h: \oplus_A U_\alpha \to M$ be a homomorphism. Then $\mathrm{Im}\, h = \Sigma_A \mathrm{Im}(h \circ \iota_\alpha)$ where each $h \circ \iota_\alpha \in \mathrm{Hom}_R(U_\alpha, M)$. Thus $\mathrm{Im}\, h \leq \mathrm{Tr}_M(\mathcal{U})$. Conversely, every $\mathrm{Im}\, h$ for $h: U \to M$ ($U \in \mathcal{U}$) is contained in some submodule of $M$ generated by $\mathcal{U}$. Hence $\mathrm{Tr}_M(\mathcal{U})$ is the sum of all submodules generated by $\mathcal{U}$, so it is the unique largest such submodule.

(2) For $K \leq M$, $M/K$ is cogenerated by $\mathcal{U}$ iff for every $x \in M \setminus K$ there exists $h: M \to U$ with $U \in \mathcal{U}$ such that $x \notin \mathrm{Ker}\, h$. Thus $K$ contains $\mathrm{Rej}_M(\mathcal{U})$. And $\mathrm{Rej}_M(\mathcal{U})$ itself satisfies this property, making it the unique smallest submodule with a cogenerated factor.
