(1) If $\mathcal{V} \subseteq \mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$, then for each $h: V \to M$ with $V \in \mathcal{V}$, the image $\mathrm{Im}\, h$ is a submodule of $M$ that is generated by $V$, hence by Corollary 8.6 generated by $\mathcal{U}$. Therefore $\mathrm{Im}\, h \leq \mathrm{Tr}_M(\mathcal{U})$, and taking sums yields $\mathrm{Tr}_M(\mathcal{V}) \leq \mathrm{Tr}_M(\mathcal{U})$.

(2) Suppose $x \notin \mathrm{Rej}_M(\mathcal{V})$. Then there exists a homomorphism $f: M \to V$ with $V \in \mathcal{V}$ and $x \notin \mathrm{Ker}\, f$. Since $V \in \mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$, there exists a homomorphism $h: V \to U$ with $U \in \mathcal{U}$ such that $f(x) \notin \mathrm{Ker}\, h$. Now $h \circ f: M \to U$ is a homomorphism with $x \notin \mathrm{Ker}(h \circ f)$. Hence $x \notin \mathrm{Rej}_M(\mathcal{U})$. This proves $\mathrm{Rej}_M(\mathcal{U}) \leq \mathrm{Rej}_M(\mathcal{V})$.
