A module $M$ is (finitely) cogenerated by a class $\mathcal{U}$ if there exists a (finite) monomorphism from $M$ into a direct product of modules from $\mathcal{U}$. This is the categorical dual of the notion of generation: whereas generation involves an epimorphism from a direct sum onto the module, cogeneration involves embedding the module into a direct product. In the category of abelian groups, a classic example is that every torsion-free abelian group can be embedded into a power of $\mathbb{Q}$, so $\mathbb{Q}$ cogenerates the class of torsion-free groups.
