Proposition 8.9 provides a concrete way to construct a generator (resp. cogenerator) for the class of modules generated (resp. cogenerated) by a class $\mathcal{U}$. If $\mathcal{U}$ has a set of isomorphism representatives $\{U_\alpha\}$, then $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha$ serves as a generator for $\mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$, and both the direct sum and direct product serve as cogenerators for $\mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$. This is a nearly trivial consequence of Corollary 8.6.
