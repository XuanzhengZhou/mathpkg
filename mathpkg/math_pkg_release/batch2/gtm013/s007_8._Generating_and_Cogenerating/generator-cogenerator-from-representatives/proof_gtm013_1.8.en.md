(1) Trivially, $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha \in \mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$. For each $\alpha$, the natural projection $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha \to U_\alpha$ is an epimorphism, so $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha$ generates each $U_\alpha$. By Corollary 8.6, $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha$ generates every module in $\mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$, hence is a generator for $\mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$.

(2) By Proposition 8.5, both $\Pi_A U_\alpha$ and its submodule $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha$ are in $\mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$. The injection maps $\iota_\alpha: U_\alpha \to \bigoplus_A U_\alpha$ are monic, so $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha$ cogenerates each $U_\alpha$; by the dual of (1) it cogenerates $\mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$. Trivially $\Pi_A U_\alpha$ cogenerates $\bigoplus_A U_\alpha$, so by Corollary 8.6 both are cogenerators for $\mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$.
