(1) Suppose $\mathcal{V} \subseteq \mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$. If $M \in \mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{V})$, there exists an indexed set $(V_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ in $\mathcal{V}$ and an epimorphism $\oplus_A V_\alpha \to M \to 0$. Since each $V_\alpha \in \mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$, there is an epimorphism from a direct sum of modules in $\mathcal{U}$ onto each $V_\alpha$, hence onto $\oplus_A V_\alpha$. Composing yields an epimorphism from a direct sum of $\mathcal{U}$-modules onto $M$, so $M \in \mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$.

(2) The dual argument: if $\mathcal{V} \subseteq \mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$ and $M \in \mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{V})$, then $M$ embeds in a product of modules from $\mathcal{V}$. Each embeds in a product from $\mathcal{U}$, so $M$ embeds in a product from $\mathcal{U}$.
