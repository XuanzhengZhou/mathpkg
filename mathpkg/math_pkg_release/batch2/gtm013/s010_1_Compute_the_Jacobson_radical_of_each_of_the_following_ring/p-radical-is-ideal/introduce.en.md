For any class $\mathcal{P}$ of rings, the $\mathcal{P}$-radical $\operatorname{rad}_{\mathcal{P}}(R)$ is defined as the intersection of all kernels of ring homomorphisms from $R$ into rings in $\mathcal{P}$. This general construction includes the Jacobson radical as the special case where $\mathcal{P}$ is the class of primitive rings. A fundamental property is that the $\mathcal{P}$-radical is always an ideal, which follows directly from the fact that it is an intersection of ideals (kernels of homomorphisms).
