An additive functor is a functor between categories of modules that respects the additive structure of the Hom-sets. Since $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M,N)$ is an abelian group in module categories, we require that the induced map on Hom-sets be a group homomorphism, i.e., $T(f+g) = T(f) + T(g)$. This property is fundamental to the study of Hom and tensor functors, and to the theory of projective, injective, and flat modules.
