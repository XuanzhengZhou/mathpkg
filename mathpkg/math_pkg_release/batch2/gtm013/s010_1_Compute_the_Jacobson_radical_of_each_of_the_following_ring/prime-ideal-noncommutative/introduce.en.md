In non-commutative ring theory, a prime ideal is defined as an ideal whose factor ring is a prime ring. The defining criterion is $xRy \subseteq P \implies x \in P$ or $y \in P$, which involves the two-sided multiplication $xRy$ rather than just the product $xy$. This reduces to the familiar commutative definition when the ring is commutative. Prime ideals generalize the notion of primeness from integers to arbitrary rings and play a key role in the structure theory of non-commutative rings.
