Proposition 2.2 states that the set $RX$ of all $R$-linear combinations of a non-empty subset $X$ of a module $M$ is always a submodule of $M$. This is the fundamental closure property that makes the notation $RX$ meaningful: the $R$-multiples of $X$ are closed under addition and further scalar multiplication. The result justifies calling $RX$ the submodule generated by $X$.
