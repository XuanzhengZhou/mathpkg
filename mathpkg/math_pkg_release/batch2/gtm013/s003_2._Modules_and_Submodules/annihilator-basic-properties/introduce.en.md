Proposition 2.14 establishes the fundamental order-reversing properties of annihilator maps. The left annihilator $l_R$ and right annihilator $r_M$ form a Galois connection between subsets of $R$ and subsets of $M$, yielding the triple-annihilator identities $l_R = l_R r_M l_R$ and $r_M = r_M l_R r_M$. These properties are analogous to the closure operations in Galois correspondences.
