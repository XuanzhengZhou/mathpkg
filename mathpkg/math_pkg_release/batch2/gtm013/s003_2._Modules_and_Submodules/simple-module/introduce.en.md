A simple module is a non-zero module with no non-trivial proper submodules. Simple modules are the irreducible building blocks of module theory, analogous to primes in arithmetic. Every simple module is cyclic, and an abelian group is simple if and only if it is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_p$ for some prime $p$.
