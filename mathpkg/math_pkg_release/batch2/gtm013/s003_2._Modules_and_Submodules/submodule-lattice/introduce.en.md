Proposition 2.5 establishes that the collection $\mathcal{S}(M)$ of all submodules of a module $M$, ordered by inclusion, forms a complete modular lattice. The join (least upper bound) of a family of submodules is their sum $\Sigma \mathcal{A}$, while the meet (greatest lower bound) is their intersection $\bigcap \mathcal{A}$. The modular law is a key structural property of submodule lattices, though they need not be distributive.
