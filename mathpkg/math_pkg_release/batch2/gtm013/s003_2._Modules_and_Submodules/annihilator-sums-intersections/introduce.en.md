Proposition 2.16 shows that left annihilators convert sums into intersections: the annihilator of a sum equals the intersection of the individual annihilators. The dual statement for intersections only yields an inclusion (not equality in general), reflecting the fact that the annihilator maps $l_R$ and $r_M$ are order-reversing maps that preserve meets but not necessarily joins.
