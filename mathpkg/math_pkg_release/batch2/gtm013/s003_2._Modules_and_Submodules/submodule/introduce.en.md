A submodule $N$ of a left $R$-module $M$ is a subgroup of the additive group of $M$ that is closed under scalar multiplication by elements of $R$. In other words, $N \leq M$ means $N$ is an $R$-module in its own right and the inclusion map is an $R$-module homomorphism. The condition that a subgroup be closed under scalar multiplication is equivalent to the requirement that all $R$-linear combinations of elements of $N$ remain in $N$.
