Proposition 2.9 establishes the fundamental correspondence theorem for modules: the submodule lattice of a factor module $M/K$ is lattice-isomorphic to the interval $[K, M]$ in the submodule lattice of $M$. The mutually inverse maps are $n_K(H) = H/K$ and $n_K^+(T) = \{x \in M : x+K \in T\}$. This generalizes the analogous correspondence theorem for groups to the module setting.
