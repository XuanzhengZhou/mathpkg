(1) Let $I$ be a left ideal and $J$ a two-sided ideal in $R$. Prove that if $I$ and $J$ are nil (nilpotent), then so is the left ideal $I + J$. [Hint: Consider $(I + J)/J$ in $R/J$. Note that $I$ nilpotent means $I^n = 0$ for some $n \in \mathbb{N}$.]

(2) Prove that if $I$ is a left ideal in $R$, then $I$ is nilpotent if and only if $I R$ is nilpotent. Conclude that if $I$ is nilpotent, then the two-sided ideal generated by $I$ is nilpotent.
