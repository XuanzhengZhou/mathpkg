Let $M$ be a left $R$-module, let $A, B \subseteq R$, and let $X \subseteq M$. Prove that:

(1) $AX \leq M$ whenever $A$ is a left ideal of $R$;

(2) $A(BX) = (AB)X$;

(3) $A(\sum_{C} X_\gamma) = \sum_{C} A X_\gamma$ whenever each $X_\gamma$ is a subgroup of ${}_{\mathbb{Z}} M$ ($\gamma \in C$);

(4) $(\sum_{C} A_\gamma)X = \sum_{C} A_\gamma X$ whenever each $A_\gamma$ is a subgroup of ${}_{\mathbb{Z}} R$.
