Let $R$ be a ring and $x \in R$. Prove that the ideal (i.e., $(R, R)$-submodule of $R$) generated by $x$ is the set of all finite sums

$$r_1 x s_1 + r_2 x s_2 + \cdots + r_n x s_n$$

for $n \in \mathbb{N}$, $r_1, \ldots, r_n, s_1, \ldots, s_n \in R$. Give an example showing that $\{r x s \mid r, s \in R\}$ need not be an ideal of $R$. [Hint: Consider $\mathbb{M}_2(\mathbb{Q})$.]
