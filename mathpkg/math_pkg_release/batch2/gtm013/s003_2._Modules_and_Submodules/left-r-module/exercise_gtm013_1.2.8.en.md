Let $p$ be a prime and let $M$ be an additive subgroup of $\mathbb{Q}$ with subgroup $\mathbb{Z}$. Denote the factor group $M/\mathbb{Z}$ by $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$.

(1) Prove that for each $x \in \mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ and each $m \neq 0$ in $\mathbb{Z}$, there is a $y \in \mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ with $x = m y$.

(2) Prove that every proper subgroup of $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ is cyclic and spanned by $1/p^n$ for some $n$. Then deduce that the lattice of subgroups of $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ is a well-ordered chain and that $\mathbb{Z}_{p^\infty}$ has no maximal subgroup.
