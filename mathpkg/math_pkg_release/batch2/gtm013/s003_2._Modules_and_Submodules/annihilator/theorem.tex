Let $M$ be a left $R$-module. For each $X \subseteq M$, the \textbf{left annihilator} of $X$ in $R$ is

$$l_R(X) = \{r \in R \mid rx = 0 \text{ for all } x \in X\}.$$

For each $A \subseteq R$, the \textbf{right annihilator} of $A$ in $M$ is

$$r_M(A) = \{x \in M \mid ax = 0 \text{ for all } a \in A\}.$$

For singletons $\{x\}$ and $\{a\}$, we abbreviate to $l_R(x)$ and $r_M(a)$. When no ambiguity arises, subscripts $R$ and $M$ may be omitted. If $A \subseteq l_R(X)$, we say that $A$ \textbf{annihilates} $X$.

For a right $R$-module $M$, the dual notions are the right annihilator $r_R(X)$ in $R$ and the left annihilator $l_M(A)$ in $M$.
