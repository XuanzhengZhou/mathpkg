Let $R$ and $S$ be two rings. An abelian group $M$ is a \textbf{left $R$-right $S$-bimodule} if $M$ is both a left $R$-module and a right $S$-module for which the two scalar multiplications jointly satisfy

$$r(xs) = (rx)s \qquad (r \in R,\; s \in S,\; x \in M).$$

There are other styles of bimodules depending on the sides on which $R$ and $S$ operate. The crucial identity for, say, a left $R$-left $S$-bimodule is

$$r(sx) = s(rx) \qquad (r \in R,\; s \in S,\; x \in M).$$
