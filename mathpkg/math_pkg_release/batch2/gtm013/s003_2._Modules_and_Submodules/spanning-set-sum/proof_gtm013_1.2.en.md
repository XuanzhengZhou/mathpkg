Since $X$ is a spanning set, $RX = M$. By definition, $RX$ consists of all finite $R$-linear combinations of elements of $X$, i.e., all finite sums $\sum_i r_i x_i$ with $r_i \in R$, $x_i \in X$. Each summand $r_i x_i$ lies in the cyclic submodule $R x_i$. Hence the entire sum belongs to $\sum_{x \in X} R x$. Conversely, each element of $\sum_{x \in X} R x$ is a finite sum of elements from the cyclic submodules $R x$, thus an $R$-linear combination of elements of $X$, i.e., an element of $RX = M$.
