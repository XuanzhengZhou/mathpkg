A non-zero module $H$ is uniform if every non-zero submodule is essential in $H$, and co-uniform if every proper submodule is superfluous in $H$. These dual notions capture indecomposability at the level of submodule structure. The exercise then connects them to structural properties: $H$ is uniform iff its injective envelope $E(H)$ is indecomposable, and $H$ is co-uniform iff the endomorphism ring of its projective cover is local.
