A non-zero module $H$ is \textit{uniform} in case each of its non-zero submodules is essential in $H$. It is \textit{co-uniform} in case each of its proper submodules is superfluous in $H$.

(1) Prove that a non-zero module $H$ is uniform iff $E(H)$ is indecomposable.

(2) Suppose that $p: P \to H$ is a projective cover. Prove that $H$ is co-uniform iff $\operatorname{End}({}_R P)$ is local.
