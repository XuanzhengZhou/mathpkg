This theorem gives an economical criterion for a ring $R$ to be left noetherian: it suffices to check that the countable direct sum $C_0^{(\mathbb{N})}$ of the minimal left $R$-cogenerator $C_0$ is injective. Since $C_0$ is built as the direct sum of the injective envelopes of the simple left $R$-modules, this test is much cheaper to verify than checking injectivity of direct sums of all injective modules. The theorem follows from Lemma 25.2 and Faith's theorem (25.1).
