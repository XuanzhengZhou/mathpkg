For a left $R$-module $M$, the left ideals of $R$ that arise as annihilators $\ell_R(X)$ of subsets $X \subseteq M$ are called $M$-annihilator left ideals. The collection $\mathcal{L}_R(M)$ of all such ideals forms a complete lattice under inclusion, with meet given by intersection and join by $\ell_R(r_M(\sum \mathcal{A}))$. This lattice structure is central to Faith's characterization of when direct sums of an injective module remain injective, as the ascending chain condition on $\mathcal{L}_R(E)$ controls the injectivity of $E^{(\mathbb{N})}$.
