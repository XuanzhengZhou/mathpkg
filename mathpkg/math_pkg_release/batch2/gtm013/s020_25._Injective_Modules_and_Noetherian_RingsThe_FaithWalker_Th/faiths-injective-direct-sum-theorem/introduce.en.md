Faith's theorem gives a precise criterion for when arbitrary direct sums of a given injective left $R$-module $E$ remain injective. The theorem establishes that the injectivity of $E^{(\mathbb{N})}$ is already sufficient to guarantee injectivity of all direct sums $E^{(A)}$, and this happens exactly when the $E$-annihilator left ideals satisfy the ascending chain condition. This result sharpens the classical characterization (Theorem 18.13) that $R$ is left noetherian iff all direct sums of injectives are injective, by focusing on a single injective module $E$.
