The Goldie dimension of a module $M$, denoted $\operatorname{G.dim}(M)$, is defined as the infimum of cardinal numbers $c$ such that $M$ contains an essential submodule that is a direct sum of $c$ uniform submodules. For the zero module, the Goldie dimension is $0$. This notion generalizes the concept of uniform dimension and is fundamental in the study of injective modules and Noetherian rings.
