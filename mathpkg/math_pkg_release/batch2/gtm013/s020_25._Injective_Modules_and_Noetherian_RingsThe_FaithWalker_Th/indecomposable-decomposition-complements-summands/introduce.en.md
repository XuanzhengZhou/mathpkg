This proposition establishes that any indecomposable decomposition of an injective module automatically has the strong property of complementing direct summands. Given an indecomposable decomposition $E = \bigoplus_A E_\alpha$ and any direct summand $K$ of $E$, one can find a subset $B \subseteq A$ such that $E = (\bigoplus_B E_\beta) \oplus K$. The proof uses Azumaya's Theorem (12.6) and relies critically on the fact that the module is injective, so that direct sums of indecomposable summands remain injective.
