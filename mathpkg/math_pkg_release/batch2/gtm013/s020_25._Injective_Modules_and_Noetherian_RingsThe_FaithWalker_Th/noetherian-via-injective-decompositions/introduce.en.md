This theorem characterizes left noetherian rings purely in terms of the decomposition theory of injective modules. A ring $R$ is left noetherian precisely when every injective left $R$-module decomposes as a direct sum of indecomposable submodules, or equivalently when every injective module admits a decomposition that complements (maximal) direct summands. The proof uses Azumaya's Theorem, Proposition 25.5, and the minimal cogenerator test (Theorem 25.3) to connect the decomposition properties back to the ascending chain condition.
