The Faith-Walker theorem is the culminating result of section 25, giving a powerful characterization of left noetherian rings: $R$ is left noetherian precisely when there is a single cardinal $c$ such that every injective left $R$-module can be expressed as a direct sum of modules each generated by at most $c$ elements. The forward direction follows from Theorem 25.6 and the fact that indecomposable injectives form a set; the converse uses a cardinality argument involving the direct product $E^B$ to produce enough independent copies of an injective module to force noetherianity via Theorem 25.3.
