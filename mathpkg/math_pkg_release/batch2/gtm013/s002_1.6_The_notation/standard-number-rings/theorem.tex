The notation $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathbb{Q}$, $\mathbb{R}$, and $\mathbb{C}$ for the sets of integers, rational numbers, real numbers, and complex numbers will also be used to denote these sets with their usual ring structures. As rings they are all commutative integral domains, and $\mathbb{Q}$, $\mathbb{R}$, and $\mathbb{C}$ are fields. As an abelian group $\mathbb{Z}$ is cyclic, so every subgroup is cyclic. Thus every ideal of the ring $\mathbb{Z}$ is principal; i.e., is of the form $\mathbb{Z}n = \{an \mid a \in \mathbb{Z}\}$ for some unique $n \geq 0$.
