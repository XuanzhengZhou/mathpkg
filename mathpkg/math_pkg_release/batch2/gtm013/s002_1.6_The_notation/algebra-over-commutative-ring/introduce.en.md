A $K$-algebra is a ring $R$ equipped with a distinguished ring homomorphism from a commutative ring $K$ into the center of $R$. This structure generalizes the notion of an algebra over a field by allowing $K$ to be any commutative ring. The map $\phi$ provides a way to multiply elements of $R$ by scalars from $K$ in a manner compatible with the ring structure of $R$.
