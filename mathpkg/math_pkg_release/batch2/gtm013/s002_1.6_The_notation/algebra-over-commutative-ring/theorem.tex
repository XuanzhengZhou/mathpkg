Let $R$ be a ring, $K$ a commutative ring, and $\phi: K \rightarrow \operatorname{Cen} R$ a ring homomorphism. The resulting system $(R, K, \phi)$ is called a $K$-algebra. In practice we suppress $\phi$ and speak of $R$ as a $K$-algebra or as an algebra over $K$.

By the First Isomorphism Theorem, $R$ is a $K$-algebra (with respect to some $\phi$) if and only if there is an ideal $I$ of $K$ with $K/I$ isomorphic to a subring of $\operatorname{Cen} R$.
