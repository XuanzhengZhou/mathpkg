A nilpotent element is one whose some power vanishes. The nilpotency index is the smallest exponent achieving this. Only $0$ is both idempotent and nilpotent. Matrix rings provide rich sources of nilpotent elements: strictly triangular matrices (zero diagonal) in $\mathbb{M}_n(R)$ are nilpotent of index at most $n$.
