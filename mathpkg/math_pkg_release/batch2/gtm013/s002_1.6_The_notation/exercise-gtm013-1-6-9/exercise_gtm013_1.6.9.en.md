Let $R$ be a ring and $A \subseteq R$. The $R$-centralizer of $A$ is

$$\operatorname{Cen}_R(A) = \{x \in R \mid ax = xa \; (a \in A)\}.$$

Prove that:
(1) $\operatorname{Cen}_R(A)$ is a subring of $R$.
(2) $A$ is a maximal commutative subring of $R$ iff $A = \operatorname{Cen}_R(A)$.
(3) If $x \in \operatorname{Cen}_R(A)$ is invertible in $R$, then its inverse is in $\operatorname{Cen}_R(A)$.
(4) Infer that the center of a simple ring is a field. [Hint: If $x \in \operatorname{Cen} R$, then $\{rx \mid r \in R\}$ is an ideal of $R$.]
