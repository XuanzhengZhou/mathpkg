A subset $A$ of a ring is nilpotent if there exists a uniform bound $n$ such that the product of any $n$ elements from $A$ (in any order) is zero. This generalizes elementwise nilpotence: an element $x$ is nilpotent precisely when the singleton $\{x\}$ is a nilpotent subset.
