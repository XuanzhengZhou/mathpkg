Let $(R_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ be an indexed family of rings. Let

$$R = \prod_A R_\alpha$$

be the cartesian product of this indexed set of sets. Then the ring structures on the factors $R_\alpha$ induce a ring structure, defined coordinatewise, on the product $R$. That is, with respect to the operations

$$(r + s)(\alpha) = r(\alpha) + s(\alpha), \quad (rs)(\alpha) = r(\alpha)s(\alpha) \quad (\alpha \in A),$$

for all $r, s \in R$, $R$ is a ring with additive and multiplicative identities $0$ and $1$ defined by

$$0(\alpha) = 0_\alpha \quad \text{and} \quad 1(\alpha) = 1_\alpha \quad (\alpha \in A).$$

Using $A$-tuple notation, the operations are

$$(r_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A} + (s_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A} = (r_\alpha + s_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}, \quad (r_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}(s_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A} = (r_\alpha s_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$$

and the identities by $(0_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ and $(1_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$.
