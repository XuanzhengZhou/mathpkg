A fundamental property of nilpotent elements: $1 - x$ is always a unit (invertible) when $x$ is nilpotent. The inverse is given by the finite geometric series $1 + x + \cdots + x^{n-1}$, mirroring the formula $\frac{1}{1-x} = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} x^k$ in analysis, which is valid here because the series terminates.
