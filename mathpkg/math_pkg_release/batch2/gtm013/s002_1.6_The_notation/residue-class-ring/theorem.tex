For each $n > 1$ and each $a \in \mathbb{Z}$, denote by $[a]_n$ the least positive remainder of $a$ divided by $n$; that is, $[a]_n$ is the unique element of

$$\mathbb{Z}_n = \{0, 1, \ldots, n - 1\}$$

in the coset $a + \mathbb{Z}n$. Now $\mathbb{Z}_n$ is a ring under the usual operations of residues modulo $n$.
