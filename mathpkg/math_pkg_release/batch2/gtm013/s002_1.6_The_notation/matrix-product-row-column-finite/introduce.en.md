Matrix multiplication for infinite matrices is well-defined when one of the factors satisfies a finiteness condition: $A$ is row finite or $B$ is column finite. Under these conditions, each entry of the product involves only finitely many nonzero summands. The product inherits column (or row) finiteness when both factors have the property.
