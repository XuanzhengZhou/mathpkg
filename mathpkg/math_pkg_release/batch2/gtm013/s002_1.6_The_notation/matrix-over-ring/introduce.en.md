A $\Gamma \times \Lambda$-matrix over a ring $R$ is a function from the Cartesian product $\Gamma \times \Lambda$ to $R$. This generalizes the familiar finite matrix concept to allow arbitrary (possibly infinite) index sets. The entry at position $(\alpha, \beta)$ is denoted $a_{\alpha\beta}$ and the matrix is written $\llbracket a_{\alpha\beta} \rrbracket$.
