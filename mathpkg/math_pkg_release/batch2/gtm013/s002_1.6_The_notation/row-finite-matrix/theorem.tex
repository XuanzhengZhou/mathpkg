A matrix $A = \llbracket a_{\alpha\beta} \rrbracket$ over a ring $R$ is row finite (column finite) in case each row (column) of $A$ has at most finitely many non-zero entries.

For non-empty sets $\Gamma$ and $\Lambda$, let $\mathbb{M}_{\Gamma \times \Lambda}(R)$ denote the set of all $\Gamma \times \Lambda$-matrices over $R$. Then $\mathbb{M}_{\Gamma \times \Lambda}(R)$ is an abelian group under entrywise addition:

$$(A + B)(\alpha, \beta) = a_{\alpha\beta} + b_{\alpha\beta}.$$

The identity is the zero matrix $0 = \llbracket 0_{\alpha\beta} \rrbracket$, and the inverse of $A$ is $-A = \llbracket -a_{\alpha\beta} \rrbracket$.
