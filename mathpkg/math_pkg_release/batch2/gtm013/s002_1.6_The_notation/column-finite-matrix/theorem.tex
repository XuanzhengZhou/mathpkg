For a non-empty set $\Gamma$, the collection $\mathbb{CFM}_{\Gamma}(R)$ of all column finite $\Gamma \times \Gamma$-matrices over $R$ is a ring with respect to the operations of addition and multiplication. Likewise, the collection $\mathbb{RFM}_{\Gamma}(R)$ of all row finite $\Gamma \times \Gamma$-matrices over $R$ is a ring.

A matrix is a scalar matrix in case it is of the form $\llbracket \delta_{\alpha\beta} r \rrbracket$ for some $r \in R$, where $\delta_{\alpha\beta}$ is the Kronecker delta.
