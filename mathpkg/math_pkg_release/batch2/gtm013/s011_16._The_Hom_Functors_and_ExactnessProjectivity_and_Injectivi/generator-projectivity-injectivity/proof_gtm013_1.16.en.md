Since $G$ is a generator, every left $R$-module is an epimorphic image of a direct sum of copies of $G$. Thus:

(1) If $U$ is $G$-injective, then by Proposition 16.13(2), $U$ is injective relative to any direct sum of copies of $G$. By Proposition 16.13(1), $U$ is then injective relative to any epimorphic image of such a direct sum. Since every module is such an image, $U$ is injective relative to every module, i.e., $U$ is injective.

(2) If $U$ is $G$-projective and finitely generated, then by Proposition 16.12(2), $U$ is projective relative to any direct sum of copies of $G$. For any module $M$, write $M$ as an epimorphic image of a direct sum of copies of $G$. Using the finite generation of $U$ and Proposition 16.12(1), we conclude $U$ is $M$-projective for all $M$, i.e., $U$ is projective.
