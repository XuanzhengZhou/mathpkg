Let $(U_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ be an indexed set of left $R$-modules. If $M$ and $N$ are left $R$-modules, then there exist $\mathbb{Z}$-isomorphisms $\eta_M, \eta_N, v_N$ and $v_M$ such that for all $f: M \to N$ the following diagrams commute:

(1)
$$\begin{CD}
Hom_R(\oplus_A U_\alpha, M) @>{Hom(\oplus_A U_\alpha, f)}>> Hom_R(\oplus_A U_\alpha, N) \\
@V{\eta_M}VV @VV{\eta_N}V \\
\prod_A Hom_R(U_\alpha, M) @>{\prod_A Hom(U_\alpha, f)}>> \prod_A Hom_R(U_\alpha, N)
\end{CD}$$

(2)
$$\begin{CD}
Hom_R(N, \prod_A U_\alpha) @>{Hom(f, \prod_A U_\alpha)}>> Hom_R(M, \prod_A U_\alpha) \\
@V{v_N}VV @VV{v_M}V \\
\prod_A Hom_R(N, U_\alpha) @>{\prod_A Hom(f, U_\alpha)}>> \prod_A Hom_R(M, U_\alpha)
\end{CD}$$

That is, the isomorphisms $Hom_R(\oplus_A U_\alpha, M) \cong \prod_A Hom_R(U_\alpha, M)$ and $Hom_R(M, \prod_A U_\alpha) \cong \prod_A Hom_R(M, U_\alpha)$ are natural.
