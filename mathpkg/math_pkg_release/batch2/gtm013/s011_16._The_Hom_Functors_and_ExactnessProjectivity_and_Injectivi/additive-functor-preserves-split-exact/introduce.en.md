Proposition 16.2 states that additive functors preserve split exact sequences. For a covariant additive functor $F$, the image of a split exact sequence $0 \to K \to M \to N \to 0$ remains split exact. For a contravariant additive functor $G$, the image is also split exact but with the arrows reversed. As a corollary, additive functors preserve isomorphisms.
