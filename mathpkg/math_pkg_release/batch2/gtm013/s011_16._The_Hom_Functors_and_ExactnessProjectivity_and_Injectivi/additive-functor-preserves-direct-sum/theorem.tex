Let $C$, $D$, $F$ (covariant), and $G$ (contravariant) be as in (16.2). If $M, M_1, \ldots, M_n$ are modules in $C$ and $M = M_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus M_n$ is a direct sum with injections $\iota_1, \ldots, \iota_n$ and projections $\pi_1, \ldots, \pi_n$, then:

(1) $F(M)$ is a direct sum of $F(M_1), \ldots, F(M_n)$ with injections $F(\iota_1), \ldots, F(\iota_n)$ and projections $F(\pi_1), \ldots, F(\pi_n)$;

(2) $G(M)$ is a direct sum of $G(M_1), \ldots, G(M_n)$ with injections $G(\pi_1), \ldots, G(\pi_n)$ and projections $G(\iota_1), \ldots, G(\iota_n)$.

Moreover,

$$F(\oplus_{i=1}^{n} f_i) = \oplus_{i=1}^{n} F(f_i), \quad F(\prod_{i=1}^{n} g_i) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} F(g_i),$$

$$G(\oplus_{i=1}^{n} f_i) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} G(f_i), \quad G(\prod_{i=1}^{n} g_i) = \oplus_{i=1}^{n} G(g_i).$$
