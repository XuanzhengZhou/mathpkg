Given a bimodule ${}_R U_S$, the constructions $M \mapsto Hom_R(U, M)$ and $M \mapsto Hom_R(M, U)$ define two fundamental functors from the category of left $R$-modules. The first, $Hom_R(U, -)$, is covariant and lands in the category of left $S$-modules. The second, $Hom_R(-, U)$, is contravariant and lands in the category of right $S$-modules. These Hom functors are additive and form the backbone of homological algebra.
