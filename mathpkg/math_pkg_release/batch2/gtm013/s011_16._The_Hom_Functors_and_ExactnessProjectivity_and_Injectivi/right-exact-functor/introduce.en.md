A right exact functor preserves exactness of short exact sequences at the rightmost (and middle) terms. For a covariant functor $F$, this means $F(K) \to F(M) \to F(N) \to 0$ is exact. For a contravariant functor $G$, the sequence becomes $G(N) \to G(M) \to G(K) \to 0$. The tensor product functor $M \otimes_R -$ is the canonical example of a right exact functor.
