Let $U_R$ and ${}_R M$ be modules. Define the annihilator in $M$ of $U$ to be
$$\operatorname{Ann}_M(U) = \{m \in M \mid u \otimes m = 0 \text{ in } U \otimes_R M \text{ for all } u \in U\}.$$
Say that $U_R$ is ${}_R M$-faithful in case $\operatorname{Ann}_M(U) = 0$. Prove that:
(1) $\operatorname{Ann}_M(U)$ is the unique smallest submodule $K$ of $M$ such that $U$ is $M/K$-faithful.
(2) If $f: {}_R M \to {}_R N$, then $f(\operatorname{Ann}_M(U)) \subseteq \operatorname{Ann}_N(U)$. In particular, $\operatorname{Ann}_M(U)$ is stable under endomorphisms of $M$.
