Proposition 19.13 states that tensor functors are right exact but not necessarily left exact. This is a fundamental fact in homological algebra: given a short exact sequence, tensoring preserves exactness at the right two positions but may fail at the left. For example, $\mathbb{Z}_n \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} \mathbb{Z} \cong \mathbb{Z}_n$ but $\mathbb{Z}_n \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} \mathbb{Q} = 0$, showing that the inclusion $\mathbb{Z} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Q}$ is not preserved by tensoring with $\mathbb{Z}_n$. This failure motivates the definition of flat modules and the derived functor $\operatorname{Tor}$.
