Theorem 19.10 establishes the tensor product as an additive covariant bifunctor that preserves arbitrary direct sums. Given a bimodule ${}_S U_R$, the functor $U \otimes_R -$ takes a left $R$-module $M$ to the left $S$-module $U \otimes_R M$, and sends a homomorphism $f$ to $1_U \otimes f$. Preservation of direct sums follows from Lemma 19.9 and is a crucial property used in homological algebra, particularly in proving that tensor functors are right exact and that free modules are flat.
