Let $R$ be a ring, $L \leq_R R$ and $I \leq_R R$. Prove that:
(1) For each ${}_R M$ there is a $\mathbb{Z}$-isomorphism $f: R/I \otimes_R M \to M/IM$ such that $f((r+I) \otimes m) = rm + IM$.
(2) For each $M_R$ there is a $\mathbb{Z}$-isomorphism $g: M \otimes_R R/L \to M/ML$ such that $g(m \otimes (r+L)) = mr + ML$.
