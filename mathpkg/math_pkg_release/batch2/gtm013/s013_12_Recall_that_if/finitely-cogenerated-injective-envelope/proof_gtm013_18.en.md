If $E(M) \cong E(T_1) \oplus \cdots \oplus E(T_n)$, then $E(M) \cong E(T_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus T_n)$ has a finitely generated essential socle (namely $T_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus T_n$). Thus each of its submodules (including $M$) is finitely cogenerated.

Conversely, if $M$ is finitely cogenerated, then $\operatorname{Soc} M = T_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus T_n$ is a finite direct sum of simple modules. Since $\operatorname{Soc} M \trianglelefteq M$ (by definition of finite cogeneration), we have $E(M) = E(\operatorname{Soc} M) \cong E(T_1) \oplus \cdots \oplus E(T_n)$ by (18.12)(4).
