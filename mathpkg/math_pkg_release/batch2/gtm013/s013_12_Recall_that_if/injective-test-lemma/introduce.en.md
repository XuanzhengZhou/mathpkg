The Injective Test Lemma, also known as the Baer Criterion, provides a highly practical characterization of injective modules. Instead of checking the lifting property for all monomorphisms between arbitrary modules, it suffices to check that every homomorphism from a left ideal $I \leq R$ into $E$ extends to $R$. This reduces the verification of injectivity to the behavior of $E$ with respect to the inclusion maps of left ideals, making it the standard tool for proving modules are injective.
