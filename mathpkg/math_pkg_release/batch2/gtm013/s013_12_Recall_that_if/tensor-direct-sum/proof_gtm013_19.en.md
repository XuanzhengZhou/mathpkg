Let $(p_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ and $(q_\beta)_{\beta \in B}$ be the canonical projections satisfying $p_\alpha i_{\alpha} = \delta_{\alpha \alpha} 1_{M_\alpha}$, $q_\beta j_{\beta} = \delta_{\beta \beta} 1_{N_\beta}$, and $\sum_A i_\alpha p_\alpha(m) = m$, $\sum_B j_\beta q_\beta(n) = n$ for all $m \in M$, $n \in N$. Then $(p_\alpha \otimes q_\beta)(i_{\alpha} \otimes j_{\beta}) = \delta_{\alpha\alpha}\delta_{\beta\beta} 1_{M_\alpha \otimes N_\beta}$ and $\sum_{A \times B} (i_\alpha \otimes j_\beta)(p_\alpha \otimes q_\beta)(m \otimes n) = m \otimes n$ for all generators. Hence $(i_\alpha \otimes j_\beta)_{\alpha,\beta}$ and $(p_\alpha \otimes q_\beta)_{\alpha,\beta}$ form a direct sum decomposition.
