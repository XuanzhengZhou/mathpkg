(1) Prove that if $p_i: P_i \to M_i$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$) are projective covers, then $(\bigoplus_i p_i): \bigoplus_i P_i \to \bigoplus_i M_i$ is a projective cover.
(2) Let $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ be a complete set of pairwise orthogonal primitive idempotents. Prove that every simple left $R$-module has a projective cover.
(3) Prove that every semisimple module has a projective cover.
(4) Prove that for each left $R$-module $M$, there is an indexed set $(f_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ in $\{e_1, \ldots, e_n\}$ and a projective cover $\bigoplus_A R f_\alpha \to M$.
(5) Prove that if ${}_R P$ is projective, then there exists an indexed set $(f_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ in $\{e_1, \ldots, e_n\}$ such that $P \cong \bigoplus_A R f_\alpha$.
