An $R$-balanced map is a bilinear map from the Cartesian product $M \times N$ into an abelian group that respects the right $R$-module structure on $M$ and the left $R$-module structure on $N$ via condition (3): $\beta(mr, n) = \beta(m, rn)$. The most familiar examples are inner products in linear algebra (where $R$ is a field) and ring multiplications $R \times R \to R$. $R$-balanced maps are the morphisms that the tensor product is designed to linearize.
