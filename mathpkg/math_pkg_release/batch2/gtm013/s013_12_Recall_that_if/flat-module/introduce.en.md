A flat module is one for which the tensor functor $(V \otimes_R -)$ preserves monomorphisms, hence is exact. Since tensor functors are always right exact, flatness is precisely the condition that tensoring with $V$ preserves kernels. Flat modules generalize projective modules (every projective module is flat, by Proposition 19.16) and free modules, and they play a fundamental role in homological algebra. The derived functor $\operatorname{Tor}^R$ measures the failure of a module to be flat.
