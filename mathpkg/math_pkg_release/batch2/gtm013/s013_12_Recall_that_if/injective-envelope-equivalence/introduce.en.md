This corollary provides three equivalent ways to characterize the injective envelope $E(M)$. Condition (a) is the definition. Condition (b) says $E(M)$ is the unique minimal injective extension: it embeds as a submodule of every injective module containing $M$. Condition (c) says $E(M)$ is the unique maximal essential extension: it contains a copy of every essential extension of $M$. Thus $E(M)$ is simultaneously the minimal injective extension and the maximal essential extension of $M$.
