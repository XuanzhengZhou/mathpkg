Lemma 19.18 gives a criterion for when a factor module of a flat module is flat: one needs the condition $KI = K \cap VI$ for all (finitely generated) left ideals $I$. This condition can be interpreted as saying that the submodule $K$ is "pure" in $V$. The proof uses a commutative diagram involving $K \otimes_R I$, $V \otimes_R I$, and $V \otimes_R I$, exploiting the Flat Test Lemma (19.17) and the right exactness of tensor products. This lemma is essential for studying flatness in exact sequences and for the Chase Theorem (19.20).
