Suppose $\beta: M \times N \to A$ is an $R$-balanced map. Since $F$ is free on $M \times N$, there exists a unique $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism $h: F \to A$ such that $h((m,n)) = \beta(m,n)$ for all generators. Since $\beta$ is $R$-balanced, $K \leq \operatorname{Ker} h$. Thus $h$ factors uniquely through $T = F/K$, giving a $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism $f: T \to A$ with $f \circ \tau = \beta$. Since $\tau(M \times N)$ spans $T$, $f$ is uniquely determined by this condition.
