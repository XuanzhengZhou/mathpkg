Proposition 19.2 constructs the tensor product explicitly as a quotient of a free abelian group. Starting from the free abelian group $F$ on the set $M \times N$, one imposes the relations that encode $R$-balancedness (bi-additivity and the $R$-balancing condition). The resulting quotient $T = F/K$ together with the natural map $\tau$ satisfies the universal property of the tensor product, proving that tensor products always exist for any pair of modules over any ring.
