The fact that each element of $M \otimes_R N$ can be expressed as a finite sum $\sum_i (m_i \otimes n_i)$ follows directly from the construction: $F = \bigoplus_{M \times N} \mathbb{Z}(m,n)$ is generated by the symbols $(m,n)$, and $T = F/K$ inherits this property. Every element of $T$ is a finite $\mathbb{Z}$-linear combination of cosets $(m,n) + K = m \otimes n$.

The arithmetic properties follow from the defining relations of $K$:
(1) $(m_1 + m_2) \otimes n - (m_1 \otimes n) - (m_2 \otimes n) = ((m_1+m_2, n) - (m_1, n) - (m_2, n)) + K = 0 + K$, since the generator lies in $K$.
(2) $m \otimes (n_1 + n_2) - (m \otimes n_1) - (m \otimes n_2) = 0$ similarly.
(3) $mr \otimes n - m \otimes rn = 0$ because $(mr, n) - (m, rn) \in K$.

These identities completely characterize the bilinearity of the tensor product.
