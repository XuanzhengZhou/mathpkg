This proposition provides a practical test for when an injective module is a cogenerator: it suffices to check that every simple module has a non-zero homomorphism into $E$. This is much easier to verify than the full definition of a cogenerator, since one only needs to consider simple modules. The proof uses the fact that every non-zero cyclic module has a simple factor module, and injectivity allows lifting homomorphisms from the simple factor to the cyclic submodule and then to the whole module.
