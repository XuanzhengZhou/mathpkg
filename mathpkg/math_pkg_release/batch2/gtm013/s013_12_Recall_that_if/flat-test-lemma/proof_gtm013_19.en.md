($\Rightarrow$) If $V$ is flat, then for any left ideal $I \leq {}_R R$, the inclusion $I \hookrightarrow R$ is monic, so $V \otimes_R I \to V \otimes_R R$ is monic by flatness.

($\Leftarrow$) Suppose $V \otimes_R I \to V \otimes_R R$ is monic for every finitely generated left ideal $I$. Let $K \leq {}_R M$ be any submodule. To show $V \otimes_R K \to V \otimes_R M$ is monic, suppose $\sum_i (v_i \otimes k_i) \in V \otimes_R K$ maps to $0$ in $V \otimes_R M$. Then $\sum_i v_i \otimes k_i = 0$ in $V \otimes_R M$.

Let $I = \sum_i R k_i$ be the left ideal generated by the $k_i$. Consider the exact sequence $0 \to I \to M$. Since $V \otimes_R I \to V \otimes_R R$ is monic (by hypothesis, reducing to the finitely generated case), and the argument $\sum_i v_i \otimes k_i$ lies in the image of $V \otimes_R I$, its image being zero in $V \otimes_R M$ forces it to be zero in $V \otimes_R I$, hence zero in $V \otimes_R K$. Thus $V$ is flat.

The equivalence $V \otimes_R I \cong VI$ follows from identifying $V \otimes_R R \cong V$ and noting that the image of $V \otimes_R I$ under this isomorphism is exactly $VI$.
