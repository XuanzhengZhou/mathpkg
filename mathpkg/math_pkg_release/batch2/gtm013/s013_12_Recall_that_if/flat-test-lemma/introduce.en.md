The Flat Test Lemma is the analog of the Baer Criterion for flatness. Just as injectivity can be tested on inclusions of left ideals, flatness can be tested by checking that $V \otimes_R I \to V \otimes_R R \cong V$ is monic for every (finitely generated) left ideal $I$. This reduces the verification of flatness from arbitrary monomorphisms to the behavior on ideals, making it the primary practical tool for proving modules are flat. The "finitely generated" refinement, due to Villamayor, is an important strengthening.
