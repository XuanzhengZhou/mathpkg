Theorem 18.10 asserts that every module possesses an injective envelope (injective hull). The proof uses Zorn's Lemma to find a maximal essential extension of $M$ within a fixed injective module containing $M$ (whose existence is guaranteed by Proposition 18.6). The maximality argument shows that any essential extension beyond this maximal one would force a decomposition contradicting maximality. Uniqueness up to isomorphism follows from the Fundamental Lemma (18.9). This theorem is dual to the existence of projective covers for modules over perfect rings.
