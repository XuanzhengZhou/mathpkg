Let $M$ be a left $R$-module and suppose that $i: M \to E$ is an injective envelope of $M$. If ${}_R Q$ is injective and $q: M \to Q$ is a monomorphism, then $Q$ has a decomposition
$$Q = E \oplus E$$
such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $E \cong E$;
\item[(2)] $\operatorname{Im} q \leq E$;
\item[(3)] $q: M \to E$ is an injective envelope of $M$.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover, if $f: M_1 \to M_2$ is an isomorphism and $i_1: M_1 \to E_1$, $i_2: M_2 \to E_2$ are injective envelopes, then there exists an isomorphism $\bar{f}: E_1 \to E_2$ such that $\bar{f} i_1 = i_2 f$.
