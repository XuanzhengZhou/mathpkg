An inductive poset (also called a chain-complete poset or a poset satisfying the ascending chain condition in the sense of Zorn) is one in which every chain has an upper bound. This condition is the hypothesis of the Maximal Principle (Zorn's Lemma), which guarantees the existence of maximal elements.
