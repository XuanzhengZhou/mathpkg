A modular lattice satisfies a weakened form of the distributive law, requiring distributivity only when elements are comparable. The lattice of ideals of a ring and the lattice of subspaces of a vector space are modular. Modularity is a key property in the Jordan-Holder theory and Dedekind's study of ideal lattices.
