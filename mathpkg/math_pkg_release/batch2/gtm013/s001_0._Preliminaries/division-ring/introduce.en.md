A division ring (or skew field) is a ring where every non-zero element has a two-sided multiplicative inverse. Division rings are the "best-behaved" non-commutative rings: they are simple rings and integral domains. The quaternions form a non-commutative division ring. Every finite division ring is a field (Wedderburn's theorem, 1905).
