A natural isomorphism is a natural transformation in which every component is an isomorphism. It provides the correct notion of "sameness" for functors: two naturally isomorphic functors are indistinguishable from the categorical perspective. Many fundamental theorems in algebra assert natural isomorphisms (e.g., the hom-tensor adjunction).
