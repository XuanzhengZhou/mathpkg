A poset $(P, \leq)$ is a lattice if every pair $a, b \in P$ has both a join $a \vee b$ and a meet $a \wedge b$ in $P$. The maps $\vee: P \times P \to P$ and $\wedge: P \times P \to P$ defined by $(a, b) \mapsto a \vee b$ and $(a, b) \mapsto a \wedge b$ are binary operations satisfying $a \vee a = a = a \wedge a$ for all $a \in P$, and both $(P, \vee)$ and $(P, \wedge)$ are commutative semigroups.
