Let $(P, \leq)$ be a poset and let $a, b \in P$. An element $c \in P$ is the join (or supremum) of $a$ and $b$, denoted $a \vee b$, if $c$ is the least upper bound of $\{a, b\}$: $a \leq c$, $b \leq c$, and if $d \in P$ satisfies $a \leq d$ and $b \leq d$, then $c \leq d$. Dually, $c$ is the meet (or infimum) of $a$ and $b$, denoted $a \wedge b$, if $c$ is the greatest lower bound of $\{a, b\}$.
