Let $C$ and $D$ be categories. A contravariant functor $F = (F', F'')$ from $C$ to $D$ consists of an object map $F'$ and a morphism map $F''$ satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(F.1)*] $F''(f): F'(B) \to F'(A)$ in $D$ for each $f: A \to B$ in $C$;
\item[(F.2)*] $F''(g \circ f) = F''(f) \circ F''(g)$;
\item[(F.3)] $F''(1_A) = 1_{F'(A)}$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus a contravariant functor reverses the direction of arrows and the order of composition.
