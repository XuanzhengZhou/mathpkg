A contravariant functor reverses the direction of morphisms: a morphism $f: A \to B$ is sent to $F(f): F(B) \to F(A)$. The composition rule is also reversed. The prototypical example is the $\operatorname{Hom}(-, X)$ functor, which sends $f: A \to B$ to precomposition $f^*: \operatorname{Hom}(B, X) \to \operatorname{Hom}(A, X)$.
