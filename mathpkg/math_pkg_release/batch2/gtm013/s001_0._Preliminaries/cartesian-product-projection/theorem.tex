Let $(X_{\alpha})_{\alpha \in A}$ be an indexed family of sets. The Cartesian product $\prod_{\alpha \in A} X_{\alpha}$ is the set of all functions $\sigma: A \to \bigcup_{\alpha \in A} X_{\alpha}$ such that $\sigma(\alpha) \in X_{\alpha}$ for each $\alpha \in A$. For each $\alpha \in A$, the projection map
$$\pi_{\alpha}: \prod_{\alpha \in A} X_{\alpha} \to X_{\alpha}$$
is defined by $\pi_{\alpha}(\sigma) = \sigma(\alpha)$. In tuple notation: $\pi_{\alpha}((x_{\beta})_{\beta \in A}) = x_{\alpha}$. Each $\pi_{\alpha}$ is surjective (by the Axiom of Choice).
