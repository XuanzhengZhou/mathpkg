This is the fundamental factorization theorem for ring homomorphisms. Given a surjective homomorphism $\phi'$ and another homomorphism $\phi$, a factorization $\psi$ exists through $\phi'$ precisely when the kernel of $\phi'$ is contained in the kernel of $\phi$. The induced map $\psi$ is injective exactly when the kernels coincide. This theorem is the ring-theoretic analogue of the first isomorphism theorem.
