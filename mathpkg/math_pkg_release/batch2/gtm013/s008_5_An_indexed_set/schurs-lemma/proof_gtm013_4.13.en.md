Every non-zero endomorphism $T \to T$ of a simple module is an isomorphism, since its kernel is a proper submodule (hence zero) and its image is a non-zero submodule (hence all of $T$). Therefore every non-zero element of $\operatorname{End}({}_R T)$ is invertible, so $\operatorname{End}({}_R T)$ is a division ring. $\square$
