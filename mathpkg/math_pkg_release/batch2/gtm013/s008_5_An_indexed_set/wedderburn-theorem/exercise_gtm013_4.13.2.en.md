Define $C(k) = \{ [\alpha_{ij}] \in \mathbb{M}_n(D) \mid \alpha_{ij} = \delta_{jk} \alpha_{ij} \}$ and $R(k) = \{ [\alpha_{ij}] \in \mathbb{M}_n(D) \mid \alpha_{ij} = \delta_{ik} \alpha_{ij} \}$.

(1) Prove that $C(k)$ is a simple left ideal of $\mathbb{M}_n(D)$ and $R(k)$ is a simple right ideal of $\mathbb{M}_n(D)$. [Hint: Exercise (13.2).]

(2) Prove that as left $\mathbb{M}_n(D)$ modules $C(k) \cong \mathbb{C}_n(D)$ (see (13.1)) and as right $\mathbb{M}_n(D)$ modules, $R(k) \cong \mathbb{R}_n(D)$.

(3) Prove that the left (right) regular module over $\mathbb{M}_n(D)$ is the direct sum of $C(1), \ldots, C(n)$ (resp. $R(1), \ldots, R(n)$).
