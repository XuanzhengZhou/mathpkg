Wedderburn's Theorem characterizes rings possessing a simple left generator: they are precisely the full matrix rings over division rings. If ${}_R T$ is such a simple generator, then $R \cong \mathbb{M}_n(D)$ where $D = \operatorname{End}({}_R T)$ is a division ring by Schur's Lemma and $n$ is the composition length of $T$. Equivalently, $T_D$ is a finite-dimensional vector space over $D$ and $R \cong \operatorname{End}(T_D)$.
