(1) Using the fact that every finite division ring is a field, prove

**Theorem.** Let $R$ be a simple ring of $m$ elements and let $K$ be its center. Then: $K = GF(p^n)$ for some prime $p$ and some $n$; $m = (p^n)^{k^2}$ for some $k$; and
$$R \cong \mathbb{M}_k(GF(p^n)).$$
[Here $GF(p^n)$ is the unique (to within isomorphism) finite field of $p^n$ elements.]

(2) Deduce that there is a natural bijection between the finite semisimple rings (to within isomorphism) and the set of all finite sequences
$$(p_1, n_1, k_1), \ldots, (p_l, n_l, k_l)$$
of triples of natural numbers with $p_1, \ldots, p_l$ primes.
