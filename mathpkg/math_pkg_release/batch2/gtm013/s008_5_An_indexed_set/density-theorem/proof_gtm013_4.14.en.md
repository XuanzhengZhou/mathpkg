Since $M$ is semisimple, $M^{(n)}$ is also semisimple. Thus the cyclic submodule $R(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ of $M^{(n)}$ is a direct summand of $M^{(n)}$; so $R(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is also a $\operatorname{BiEnd}({}_R M^{(n)})$ submodule of $M^{(n)}$. Then by (14.2), $R(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is a $\operatorname{BiEnd}({}_R M)$-submodule of $M^{(n)}$; in particular,
$$\operatorname{BiEnd}({}_R M)(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = R(x_1, \ldots, x_n).$$
Thus, for $b \in \operatorname{BiEnd}({}_R M)$ there is an $r \in R$ such that
$$(b x_1, \ldots, b x_n) = b(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = r(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = (r x_1, \ldots, r x_n).$$
This proves the density property. $\square$
