A family of left $R$-modules $(M_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ is called homologically independent if there are no nonzero $R$-linear maps between distinct members of the family. This condition forces the family to be "mutually orthogonal" with respect to the Hom functor. When combined with indecomposability, homological independence yields strong uniqueness properties for direct sum decompositions of the direct sum $M = \bigoplus_A M_\alpha$.
