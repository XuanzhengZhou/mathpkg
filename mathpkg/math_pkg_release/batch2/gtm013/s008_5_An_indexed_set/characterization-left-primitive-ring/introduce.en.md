This corollary of the Density Theorem gives Jacobson's characterization of left primitive rings: they are exactly the dense rings of linear transformations of vector spaces over division rings. Concretely, a ring $R$ is left primitive if and only if it admits a faithful simple left module ${}_R T$ such that, letting $D = \operatorname{End}({}_R T)$ (a division ring by Schur's Lemma), $R$ acts densely on the $D$-vector space $T$: given any finite $D$-linearly independent set and any target values, some element of $R$ realizes the transformation.
