The Wedderburn–Artin Theorem gives the complete structure of semisimple rings: every semisimple ring decomposes uniquely as a finite direct sum of simple artinian rings. Combined with Wedderburn's Theorem (each simple artinian ring is a matrix ring over a division ring), this yields the definitive classification: every semisimple ring is isomorphic to a finite product of matrix rings over division rings.
