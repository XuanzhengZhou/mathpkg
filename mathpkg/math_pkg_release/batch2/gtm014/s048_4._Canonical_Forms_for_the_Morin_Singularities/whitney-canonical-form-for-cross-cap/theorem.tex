Let $X$ be an $n$-dimensional manifold, $Y$ a $(2n-1)$-dimensional manifold, and $f: X \to Y$ a smooth map with a cross-cap at $x_0$. Then there exist coordinates $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ centered at $x_0$ and $y_1, \ldots, y_{2n-1}$ centered at $f(x_0)$ such that

$$
f^* y_i = x_i \quad \text{for } i = 2, \ldots, n.
$$

The set $S_1(f)$ is the locus of points for which

$$
\frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1} = \frac{\partial f_{n+1}}{\partial x_1} = \cdots = \frac{\partial f_{2n-1}}{\partial x_1} = 0
$$

where $f_i = f^* y_i$. The transversality condition $j^1 f \pitchfork S_1$ is equivalent to the assertion that the differentials

$$
d\!\left(\frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1}\right),\; d\!\left(\frac{\partial f_{n+1}}{\partial x_1}\right),\; \ldots,\; d\!\left(\frac{\partial f_{2n-1}}{\partial x_1}\right)
$$

are linearly independent.
