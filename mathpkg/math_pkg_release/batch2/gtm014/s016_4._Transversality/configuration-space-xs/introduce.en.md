The configuration space $X^{(s)}$ is the set of ordered $s$-tuples of pairwise distinct points in a smooth manifold $X$. It is obtained by removing all diagonals $\{x_i = x_j\}$ from the $s$-fold Cartesian product $X^s$, making it an open subset and therefore a smooth manifold. This space serves as the natural domain for the $s$-fold $k$-jet extension $j_s^k f$, enabling the study of multijet transversality and the self-intersection properties of smooth maps.
