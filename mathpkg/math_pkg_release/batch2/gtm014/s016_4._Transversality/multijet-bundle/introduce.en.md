The $s$-fold $k$-jet bundle $J_s^k(X, Y)$ generalizes the ordinary jet bundle by considering $s$-tuples of $k$-jets whose source points are distinct. It is defined as the preimage of the configuration space $X^{(s)}$ under the $s$-fold source map $\alpha^s$. Since $X^{(s)}$ is open in $X^s$, the multijet bundle is an open submanifold of the $s$-fold product $J^k(X, Y)^s$. This construction is essential for the multijet transversality theorem, which characterizes when a smooth map has desirable transversality properties with respect to submanifolds of multijet space.
