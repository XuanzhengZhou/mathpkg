Mather's computable criterion states that stability can be checked pointwise: $f$ is stable exactly when a certain finite-dimensionality condition holds at every point. The quotient module measures the obstruction to solving $T_f(\xi, \eta) = \tau$ modulo higher-order terms. This criterion, combined with the preparation theorem, makes stability a verifiable local condition.
