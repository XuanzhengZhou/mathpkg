This lemma extends Proposition 1.10 from local to global: if a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ is infinitesimally stable, then there is an entire Whitney-open neighborhood $W$ of $f$ such that every map $g$ in $W$ satisfies the multijet condition $(\dagger)$ for all finite subsets of $X$ having at most $m+1$ points (where $m = \dim Y$). The proof constructs neighborhoods $W_r$ for each subset size $r$ separately, using Theorem 1.6, and then intersects them. The $r=1$ case reduces to Proposition 1.10.
