Condition $(Z)$ is an algebraic criterion for a smooth map germ $f: (\mathbf{R}^n, 0) \to (\mathbf{R}^m, 0)$ involving the solvability of a matrix equation. Given a germ $\phi \in C_0^\infty(\mathbf{R}^n)$, the condition asks for matrices $H$ and $K$ of germs such that $\phi I_m = (df)H + K \circ f$. This condition is equivalent to the infinitesimal stability equations $(**)$ (solvability to order $m$), and it is closed under multiplication: if it holds for $\phi_1$ and $\phi_2$, it holds for $\phi_1 \phi_2$.
