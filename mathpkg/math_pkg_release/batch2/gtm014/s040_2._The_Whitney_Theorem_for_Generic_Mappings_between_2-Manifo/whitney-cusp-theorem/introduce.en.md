Whitney's second theorem for generic maps between 2-manifolds gives the local normal form for simple cusp points. When an $S_1$ singularity has the property that the tangent space of the singular set coincides with the kernel of the differential, and the kernel vector field has contact of exactly order two with the singular set, the map can be expressed locally as $(x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_1, x_1x_2 + x_2^3)$. The proof uses the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem and is due to Morin. Together with the fold normal form, this theorem completes Whitney's classification of generic singularities of maps between 2-manifolds.
