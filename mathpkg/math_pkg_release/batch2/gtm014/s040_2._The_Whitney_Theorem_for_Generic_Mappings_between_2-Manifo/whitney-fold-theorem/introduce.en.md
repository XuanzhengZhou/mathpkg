Whitney's first theorem for generic maps between 2-manifolds gives the local normal form for fold points. A fold point is an $S_1$ singularity where the tangent space of the singular set and the kernel of the differential are complementary subspaces of the tangent space. In suitable local coordinates centered at the point and its image, the map takes the simple quadratic form $(x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_1, x_2^2)$. This theorem is a special case of the normal form derived for submersions with folds (see III, Theorem 4.5).
