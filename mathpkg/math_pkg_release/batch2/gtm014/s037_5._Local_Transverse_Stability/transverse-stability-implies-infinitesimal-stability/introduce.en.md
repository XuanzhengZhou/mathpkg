Theorem 5.13 establishes that local transverse stability implies local infinitesimal stability for a smooth map $f: X \to Y$. This is a key implication in Mather's stability theory, connecting the geometric condition of transversality of the jet extension to the algebraic infinitesimal criterion. The proof reduces to showing that $f$ satisfies the infinitesimal stability conditions to order $m = \dim Y$ by analyzing the action of diffeomorphisms on multijet spaces.
