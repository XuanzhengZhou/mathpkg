Let $\eta$ be a vector field on $Y$ with compact support representing $[\eta]_{q} \in C^{\infty}(TY)_{q}$, and let $\phi_{t}$ be the one-parameter group generated by $\eta$. Consider the curve $c(t) = j^{k}\phi_{t}(q) \cdot \sigma$ in $\mathcal{D}_{\sigma}$. By definition, $(d\gamma_{1})(\eta) = \left.\frac{dc}{dt}\right|_{t=0}$.

Now apply the natural restriction map $T_{\sigma}\mathcal{D}_{\sigma} \to J^{k}(f^{*}TY)_{p}$ (induced by the splitting $T_{\sigma}J^{k}(X,Y) \cong T_{p}X \oplus J^{k}(f^{*}TY)_{p}$ from Proposition 5.8). On one hand, the image of $(d\gamma_{1})(\eta)$ under this map is precisely $f^{*}(\pi_{k}^{\infty}(\eta))$, since $\pi_{k}^{\infty}(\eta)$ is the $k$-jet of $\eta$ at $q$ and $f^{*}$ pulls it back to a $k$-jet of a vector field along $f$ at $p$. On the other hand, going around the diagram via $J^{k}(TY)_{q} \xrightarrow{f^{*}} J^{k}(f^{*}TY)_{p}$ also yields $f^{*}(\pi_{k}^{\infty}(\eta))$. Hence the diagram commutes.
