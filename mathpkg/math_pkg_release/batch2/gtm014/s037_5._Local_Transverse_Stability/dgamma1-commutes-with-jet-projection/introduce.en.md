Proposition 5.10 establishes the compatibility between the infinitesimal action of the diffeomorphism group $\operatorname{Diff}(Y)$ on the space of smooth mappings $C^{\infty}(X,Y)$ and the passage to finite-order jets. The map $(d\gamma_{1})$ encodes how a vector field on $Y$ generates a tangent vector to the orbit $\mathcal{D}_{\sigma}$ in the jet space $J^{k}(X,Y)$. The proposition states that taking $k$-jets commutes with pulling back along $f$, meaning that the following two operations yield the same result: first applying the diffeomorphism action then restricting to $J^{k}(f^{*}TY)_{p}$, or first projecting to $k$-jets then pulling back by $f$. This commutativity is essential for reducing computations about infinitesimal deformations of $f$ to purely jet-theoretic calculations.
