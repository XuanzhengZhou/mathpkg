Let $\sigma$ be in $J^1(X, Y)$; then $\sigma$ defines a unique linear mapping of $T_p X \to T_q Y$ where $p$ is the source of $\sigma$ and $q$ is the target of $\sigma$. Let $f$ be a representative of $\sigma$ in $C^\infty(X, Y)$. Then $(df)_p$ is that linear mapping. Define

$$\operatorname{rank} \sigma = \operatorname{rank}(df)_p$$

and

$$\operatorname{corank} \sigma = q - \operatorname{rank} \sigma$$

where $q = \min(\dim X, \dim Y)$.
