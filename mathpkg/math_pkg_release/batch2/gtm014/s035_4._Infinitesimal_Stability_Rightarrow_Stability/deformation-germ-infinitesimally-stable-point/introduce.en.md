This proposition is a key local step in the proof that infinitesimal stability implies stability (the Mather–Malgrange theorem). It asserts that if a smooth map $f \colon X \to Y$ is infinitesimally stable at a point $p$, then every $k$-deformation $F$ of $f$ can be infinitesimally trivialized near $(p,0)$ — meaning the deformation vector field $\tau_F$ can be expressed as $(dF)(\zeta) + F^*\eta$ with vector fields $\zeta, \eta$ whose $\mathbf{R}^k$-components vanish. The proof reduces to a module-theoretic claim and invokes the Malgrange Preparation Theorem (IV, Theorem 3.6).
