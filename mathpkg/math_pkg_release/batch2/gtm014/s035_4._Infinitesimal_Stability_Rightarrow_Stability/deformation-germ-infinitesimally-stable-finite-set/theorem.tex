Let $f \colon X \to Y$ be a smooth map which is infinitesimally stable at a finite set
$S = \{p_1, \ldots, p_k\} \subset X$.
Let $F \colon X \times \mathbf{R}^k \to Y \times \mathbf{R}^k$ be a $k$-deformation of $f$.
Then there exist vector fields $\zeta$ on $X \times \mathbf{R}^k$ and $\eta$ on $Y \times \mathbf{R}^k$
whose $\mathbf{R}^k$-components are zero, such that
$$\tau_F = (dF)(\zeta) + F^*\eta$$
in a neighborhood of $S \times \{0\}$.
