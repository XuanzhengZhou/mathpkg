This proposition extends the local deformation trivialization results (Proposition 4.3 and Theorem 4.4) from a finite set of points to a full open neighborhood $B$ of $\Sigma \times \{0\}$, where $\Sigma$ is the critical point set of $f$. The compactness of $X$ ensures that $\Sigma$ is covered by finitely many coordinate charts on which the pointwise results apply, and a patching argument using the finite generation property of the relevant modules yields the desired vector fields $\zeta$ and $\eta$ whose $\mathbf{R}^k$-components vanish on $B$. This is a key step toward proving the global statement that infinitesimal stability implies stability.
