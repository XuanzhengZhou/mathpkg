The proof that infinitesimal stability implies stability is the deepest part of Mather's theory. It uses the characterization of trivial deformations: given a deformation of $f$, the infinitesimal stability condition guarantees the deformation is trivial, so all nearby mappings lie in the orbit of $f$. The proof relies on the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem, Nakayama's Lemma, and an inverse function theorem for Frechet spaces.
