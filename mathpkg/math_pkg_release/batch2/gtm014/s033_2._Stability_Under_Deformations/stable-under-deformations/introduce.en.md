A smooth map is **stable under deformations** (or **homotopically stable**) if every deformation of it is trivial, meaning the deformation factors through the original map up to smooth families of diffeomorphisms on the domain and codomain. This concept generalizes the intuitive idea that a stable map retains its essential form under small perturbations. Homotopic stability is a key bridge between the geometric notion of stability and the infinitesimal (linearized) stability criterion developed in the preceding section, forming the foundation for Mather's theory of stable mappings.
