A deformation is trivial if every member $f_u$ is equivalent to the original $f$ via diffeomorphisms depending smoothly on the parameter $u$. This means the family is "constant up to coordinate changes" and captures the idea that no new singularities appear. The characterization of trivial deformations via infinitesimal conditions is a key step in proving stability.
