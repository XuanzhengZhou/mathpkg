A deformation $F(x, u) = (f_u(x), u)$ of $f$ is trivial if there exist diffeomorphisms $G: X \times \mathbf{R}^s \to X \times \mathbf{R}^s$ and $H: Y \times \mathbf{R}^s \to Y \times \mathbf{R}^s$ of the form $G(x, u) = (g_u(x), u)$, $H(y, u) = (h_u(y), u)$ such that $H \circ F \circ G^{-1}(y, u) = (f(y), u)$. That is, each $f_u$ is equivalent to $f$ via diffeomorphisms depending smoothly on $u$.
