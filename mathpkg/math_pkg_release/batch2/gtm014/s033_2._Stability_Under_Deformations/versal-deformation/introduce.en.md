A versal deformation is a "universal" family containing all possible deformations up to equivalence and pullback of parameters. If $f$ is stable, then the constant deformation is versal. The theory of versal deformations, developed by Thom and Mather, provides the mathematical framework for understanding how singularities bifurcate under perturbation.
