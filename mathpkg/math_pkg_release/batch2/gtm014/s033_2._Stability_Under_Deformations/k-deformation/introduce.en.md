A **$k$-deformation** generalizes the notion of a 1-parameter deformation of a smooth map $f$ to a $k$-parameter family. Instead of a single real interval parameter, the deformation $F$ is defined over a neighborhood $U$ of the origin in $\mathbf{R}^k$, with the requirement that each fiber $F_v$ maps $X$ to $Y$ and $F_0 = f$. This generalization is essential for studying stability in higher-dimensional parameter spaces, where deformations may arise from multiple independent directions in the space of smooth maps.
