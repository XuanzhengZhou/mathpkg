Let $f: X \to Y$ be a smooth map and let $U$ be a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R}^k$. A smooth map $F: X \times U \to Y \times U$ is a \textbf{$k$-deformation} of $f$ if

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] For each $v \in U$, $F$ maps $X \times \{v\}$ to $Y \times \{v\}$. Denote by $F_v: X \to Y$ the map defined by $F(x, v) = (F_v(x), v)$.
  \item[(ii)] $F_0 = f$.
\end{enumerate}
