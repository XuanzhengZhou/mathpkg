This theorem establishes that infinitesimal stability is locally a condition of finite order: the germ $[f]_p$ is infinitesimally stable precisely when the stability PDEs $(**)$ can be solved modulo terms vanishing to order $m+1$, where $m = \dim Y$. The proof relies on the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem applied to the finitely generated $C_p^\infty(X)$-module $M_f^p = C^\infty(f^*TY)_p / (df)C^\infty(TX)_p$. This finite-order characterization enables passage from formal power series solutions to genuine smooth solutions.
