Let $G = \operatorname{Diff}(X) \times \operatorname{Diff}(Y)$ act on $C^\infty(X, Y)$. Then $f$ is infinitesimally stable iff the derivative of the orbit map $\alpha_f: G \to C^\infty(X, Y)$ given by $(g, h) \mapsto h \circ f \circ g^{-1}$ is surjective at the identity. This derivative is precisely the map $T_f(\xi, \eta) = \eta \circ f - df \circ \xi$.
