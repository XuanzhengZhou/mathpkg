Infinitesimal stability can be reinterpreted geometrically on the Frechet manifold $C^\infty(X, Y)$: $f$ is infinitesimally stable precisely when the tangent map to the orbit of $f$ under $\operatorname{Diff}(X) \times \operatorname{Diff}(Y)$ is surjective. This formulation connects stability to the orbit structure of the diffeomorphism group action and motivates Mather's criterion.
