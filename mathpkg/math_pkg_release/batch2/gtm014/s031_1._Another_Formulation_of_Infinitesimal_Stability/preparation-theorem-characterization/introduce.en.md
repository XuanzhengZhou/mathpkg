Using the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem, infinitesimal stability can be reformulated purely algebraically: the module $C^\infty_f(X, TY)$ of vector fields along $f$ must be finitely generated over $C^\infty(Y)$. This connects the geometric/analytic condition of infinitesimal stability to the algebraic condition of finite generation, making the problem amenable to local algebraic techniques.
