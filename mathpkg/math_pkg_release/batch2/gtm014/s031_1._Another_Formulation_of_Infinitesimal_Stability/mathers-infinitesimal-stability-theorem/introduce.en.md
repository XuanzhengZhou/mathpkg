Mather's Infinitesimal Stability Theorem is the central result of the theory: for proper mappings, the geometric notion of stability (every nearby mapping is equivalent under diffeomorphisms) is equivalent to the algebraic/computable condition of infinitesimal stability (surjectivity of $T_f$). This reduces the problem of determining stability to checking whether a certain linear map between Frechet spaces is surjective.
