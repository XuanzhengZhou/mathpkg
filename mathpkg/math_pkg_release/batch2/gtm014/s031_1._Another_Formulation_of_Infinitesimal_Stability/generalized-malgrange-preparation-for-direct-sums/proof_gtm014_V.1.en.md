**Proof of Lemma 1.4.** [Note: The original OCR text for this proof was truncated. The following is reconstructed from mathematical context and the usage in Proposition 1.5.]

The forward direction is immediate: if $A = \bigoplus A_i$ is finitely generated over $C_q^\infty(Y)$, then each $A_i$ (as a direct summand) is also finitely generated over $C_q^\infty(Y)$.

For the converse, suppose each $A_i$ is finitely generated over $C_q^\infty(Y)$ via $f^*$. Then each $A_i$ has a finite set of generators $\{a_{i,1},\ldots,a_{i,r_i}\}$ over $C_q^\infty(Y)$. The collection $\{(0,\ldots,a_{i,j},\ldots,0)\}$ across all $i,j$ forms a finite generating set for $A$ over $C_q^\infty(Y)$, since any element of $A$ is a $k$-tuple and each coordinate can be expressed as a $C_q^\infty(Y)$-linear combination of the generators of the corresponding $A_i$. The coordinatewise action of $C_q^\infty(Y)$ on $A$ ensures that these linear combinations combine to give the desired representation. $\square$
