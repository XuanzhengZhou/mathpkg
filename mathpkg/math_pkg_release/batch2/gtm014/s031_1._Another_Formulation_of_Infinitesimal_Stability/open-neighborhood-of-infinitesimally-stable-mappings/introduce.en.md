This proposition addresses the third objective of the section: showing that infinitesimally stable mappings form an open set in the $C^\infty$ topology. The proof uses the jet-space criterion of Corollary 1.3: since the condition $J^m(f^*TY)_p = (df)J^m(TX)_p + f^*J^m(TY)_q$ is an equality of linear maps between finite-dimensional vector spaces, it is an open condition. By compactness of $X$, this local openness can be promoted to a full neighborhood of $f$ in which every map is locally infinitesimally stable.
