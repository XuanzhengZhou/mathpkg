The germ of a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ at $p$ is infinitesimally stable if every infinitesimal deformation $\tau$ of $f$ (represented by a vector field along $f$) can be expressed as the sum of a deformation induced by a vector field $\zeta$ on the source and one induced by a vector field $\eta$ on the target. In local coordinates this reduces to a system of first-order PDEs $(**)$. The finite-order solvability condition (part (b)) asks that these PDEs be solvable modulo terms vanishing to order $k+1$, which connects infinitesimal stability to jet transversality conditions.
