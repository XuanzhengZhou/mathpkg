The proof is straightforward but a little tedious. The text does not include the full proof; the reader is directed to the exercises.

The essential idea is as follows. One must verify that $\pi^*$ is injective on $L^0(Q_p, W)$, that its differential is everywhere injective (immersion property), and that the preimage of any compact set in $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W) - M$ is compact in $L^0(Q_p, W)$ (properness). The injectivity follows from the fact that $\pi_p: V \to Q_p$ is surjective, so $\pi^*$ composed with pullback by $\pi_p$ yields the identity on $\operatorname{Hom}(Q_p, W)$. The immersion property follows from differentiating this composition. The properness relies on the fact that $M$ (the higher-corank locus) has been removed, ensuring that limits of full-rank maps remain full-rank under the identification.
