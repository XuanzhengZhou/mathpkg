Proposition 1.4 is the key technical step in establishing that $L^r(V, W)$ is a submanifold of $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W)$. It asserts that the transpose map $\pi^*$, induced by the projection $\pi_p: V \to Q_p = V/K_p$, restricts to an injective proper immersion when its domain is taken to be the full-rank linear maps $L^0(Q_p, W)$ and its codomain is the complement of the higher-corank locus $M$ in $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W)$. Once this proposition is established, it follows that $S_r$ is a submanifold of $J^1(X, Y)$, and by the Thom Transversality Theorem, generically $S_r(f)$ is a submanifold of $X$ with $\operatorname{codim} S_r(f) = r^2 + er$ where $e = |\dim X - \dim Y|$.
