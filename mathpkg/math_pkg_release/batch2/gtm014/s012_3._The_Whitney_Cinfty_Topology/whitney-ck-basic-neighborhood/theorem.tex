Let $X$ and $Y$ be smooth manifolds. Choose a metric $d$ on $J^k(X, Y)$ compatible with its topology (possible since all manifolds are metrizable). For $f \in C^\infty(X, Y)$, define the basic open neighborhood

$$B_\delta(f) \equiv \{\, g \in C^\infty(X, Y) \mid \forall x \in X,\ d(j^k f(x), j^k g(x)) < \delta(x) \,\}$$

where $\delta: X \to \mathbf{R}^+$ is a continuous positive-valued function. The collection of all such sets $B_\delta(f)$ as $\delta$ ranges over all continuous positive functions forms a neighborhood basis for the Whitney $C^k$ topology on $C^\infty(X, Y)$.
