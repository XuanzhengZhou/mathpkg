This exercise asks the reader to prove that every smooth compact manifold admits a 1:1 (injective) immersion into some Euclidean space $\mathbf{R}^N$, establishing that compact manifolds can always be realized as submanifolds of Euclidean space. This result is closely related to the Whitney Embedding Theorem and uses techniques from the theory of immersions and submersions developed in the preceding sections.
