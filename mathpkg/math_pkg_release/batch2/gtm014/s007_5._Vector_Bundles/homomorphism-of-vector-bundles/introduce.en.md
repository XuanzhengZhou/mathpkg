A homomorphism between two families of vector spaces $\pi_E: E \to X$ and $\pi_F: F \to X$ over the same base $X$ is a smooth, fiber-preserving map $\phi: E \to F$ that restricts to a linear map on each fiber. An isomorphism is a diffeomorphism that is also a homomorphism, providing the natural notion of equivalence between families of vector spaces.
