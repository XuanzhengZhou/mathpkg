Let $\bar{s} = \sum_{i,j=1}^{n} a_{ij} \, dx_i \, dx_j$ be a Riemannian metric on $\mathbf{R}^n$, and let $\bar{d}$ be the induced pseudo-metric on $\mathbf{R}^n$. Then on any given compact set $K \subset \mathbf{R}^n$, there exist positive constants $L$ and $M$ such that for every $p, q \in K$,

$$M \cdot d(p, q) \geq \bar{d}(p, q) \geq L \cdot d(p, q),$$

where $d$ denotes the standard Euclidean metric on $\mathbf{R}^n$.
