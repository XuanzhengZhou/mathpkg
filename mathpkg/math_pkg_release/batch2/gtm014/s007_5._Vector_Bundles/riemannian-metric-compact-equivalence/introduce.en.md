Lemma 5.7 establishes that on any compact subset $K$ of $\mathbf{R}^n$, an arbitrary Riemannian metric $\bar{s}$ induces a pseudometric $\bar{d}$ that is uniformly comparable to the standard Euclidean distance $d$. Specifically, there exist constants $L, M > 0$ such that $L \cdot d(p,q) \leq \bar{d}(p,q) \leq M \cdot d(p,q)$ for all $p, q \in K$. This lemma is used in the proof that the pseudometric induced by a Riemannian metric is in fact a genuine metric.
