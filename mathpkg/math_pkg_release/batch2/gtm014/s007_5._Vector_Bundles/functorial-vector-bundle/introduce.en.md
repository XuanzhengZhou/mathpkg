Given a vector bundle $E$ over $X$ and a smooth covariant functor $T$ defined on finite-dimensional vector spaces, one can form a new set $T(E) = \bigcup_{p \in X} T(E_p)$ (disjoint union of the fibers). Proposition 5.4 asserts that $T(E)$ inherits a unique vector bundle structure over $X$, with local trivializations induced by applying $T$ to the transition functions of $E$. This construction produces associated bundles such as the dual bundle, symmetric powers, and tensor powers.
