Let $E$ be a vector bundle over $X$ and $T$ be a smooth covariant functor defined on finite-dimensional vector spaces. Then

$$T(E) = \bigcup_{p \in X} T(E_p) \quad \text{(disjoint union)}$$

can be given a unique structure of a vector bundle over $X$ such that for any local trivialization $\phi: E_U \to U \times V$ of $E$, the induced map $T(\phi): T(E_U) \to U \times T(V)$ is a local trivialization of $T(E)$.

Moreover, if $T$ is contravariant, or a functor of several variables with any mixture of variance, an analogous statement holds.
