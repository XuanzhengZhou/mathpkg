Let $T$ be a covariant functor which takes finite-dimensional vector spaces into finite-dimensional vector spaces, i.e., $T: \mathbf{Vect} \rightarrow \mathbf{Vect}$, and for any vector spaces $V$ and $W$,

$$T: \operatorname{Hom}(V, W) \rightarrow \operatorname{Hom}(T(V), T(W)).$$

This latter map has the property that if $f: V \rightarrow W$ and $g: W \rightarrow Z$, then $T(g \circ f) = T(g) \circ T(f)$.

$T$ is \textbf{smooth} if for every pair of vector spaces $V$ and $W$, the mapping

$$T: \operatorname{Hom}(V, W) \rightarrow \operatorname{Hom}(T(V), T(W))$$

is smooth. (Here $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W)$ is isomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^{m \cdot n}$ where $n = \dim V$ and $m = \dim W$, giving it the structure of a smooth manifold.)
