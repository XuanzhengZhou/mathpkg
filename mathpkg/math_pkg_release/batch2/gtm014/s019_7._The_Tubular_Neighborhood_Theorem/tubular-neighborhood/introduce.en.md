A tubular neighborhood generalizes the notion of a normal bundle to an abstract manifold setting. Given a submanifold $X \subset Y$, a tubular neighborhood is an open set $Z$ around $X$ equipped with a projection $\pi: Z \to X$ that makes $Z$ a vector bundle over $X$, with $X$ itself sitting as the zero section. This structure is fundamental in differential topology for reducing local properties near $X$ to bundle-theoretic arguments.
