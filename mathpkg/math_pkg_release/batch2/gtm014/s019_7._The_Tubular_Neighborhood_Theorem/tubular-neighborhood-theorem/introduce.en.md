The Tubular Neighborhood Theorem asserts that every submanifold $X$ of a smooth manifold $Y$ possesses a tubular neighborhood — an open neighborhood of $X$ in $Y$ that is diffeomorphic to the normal bundle of $X$ in $Y$. The proof first embeds $Y$ into a Euclidean space via the Whitney Embedding Theorem, constructs a tubular neighborhood in the ambient Euclidean space, and then restricts to $Y$ using the normal bundle structure. This theorem is a cornerstone of differential topology, allowing local-to-global arguments near submanifolds.
