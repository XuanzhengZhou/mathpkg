This lemma shows that the total space $E$ of any vector bundle can be diffeomorphically mapped into any prescribed open neighborhood $V$ of the zero section, while preserving the fibration ($\pi \circ h = \pi$). The construction uses a fiberwise metric to define ball bundles $B_x(a)$, a smooth positive function $\delta: X \to (0,1)$ obtained by a partition of unity, and a radial rescaling map $h(v) = \delta(x)v/(1 + t(x)(v,v))^{1/2}$. This shrinking of fibers into arbitrarily small neighborhoods is a key tool in constructing tubular neighborhoods.
