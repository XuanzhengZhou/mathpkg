For any chart $\phi$ around a point $p$ on a smooth $n$-manifold $X$, there is a natural map from $\mathbf{R}^n$ to the tangent space $T_p X$ sending a vector $v$ to the equivalence class of the curve $t \mapsto \phi^{-1}(\phi(p) + tv)$. This map is a bijection: injectivity follows because distinct vectors produce curves with distinct derivatives in the chart, and surjectivity holds because any curve class $[c]$ is hit by the vector $v = (d\phi \cdot c)_0$. This lemma gives the first concrete identification of the abstract tangent space $T_p X$ with a Euclidean space.
