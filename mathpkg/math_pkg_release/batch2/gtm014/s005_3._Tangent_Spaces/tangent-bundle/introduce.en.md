The tangent bundle $TX$ of a smooth manifold $X$ is formed by taking the disjoint union of all tangent spaces $T_p X$ as $p$ ranges over $X$. The natural projection $\pi \colon TX \to X$ sends each tangent vector back to its base point. As the next proposition shows, $TX$ itself carries the structure of a smooth manifold of dimension $2 \dim X$, making it the prototypical example of a vector bundle over $X$.
