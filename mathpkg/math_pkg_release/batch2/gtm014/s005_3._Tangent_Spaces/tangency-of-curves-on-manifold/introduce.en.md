Two curves on a smooth manifold $X$ based at the same point $p$ are said to be tangent at $p$ if their images under any local chart $\phi$ around $p$ have the same derivative at $0$. Charts translate the manifold setting into Euclidean space where ordinary differentiation makes sense. This definition is independent of the choice of chart, making it a well-defined equivalence relation on the set of curves based at $p$. The equivalence classes are precisely the tangent vectors to $X$ at $p$.
