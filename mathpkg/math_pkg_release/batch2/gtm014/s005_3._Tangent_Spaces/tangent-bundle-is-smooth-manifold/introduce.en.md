This proposition shows that the tangent bundle $TX$ of a $C^k$ $n$-manifold is itself a smooth manifold of dimension $2n$, but only of class $C^{k-1}$ due to the differentiation involved in transition maps. The manifold structure is constructed by lifting an atlas $\{\phi_\beta\}$ of $X$ to charts $\tilde{\phi}_\beta$ on $TX$: for $\alpha \in T_p X$ with $p \in \operatorname{dom} \phi_\beta$, set $\tilde{\phi}_\beta(\alpha) = (\phi_\beta(p), (\lambda_{\phi_\beta}^p)^{-1}(\alpha)) \in \mathbf{R}^{2n}$. The transition maps between these lifted charts involve derivatives of the original chart transitions, hence the drop in differentiability class.
