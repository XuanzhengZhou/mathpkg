A generic property of smooth mappings is one that holds on a topologically large (residual) subset of the mapping space and is invariant under the natural action of diffeomorphisms on source and target. This definition formalizes the intuitive notion of a "typical" or "general position" property in differential topology. With this definition, any stable mapping automatically satisfies every generic property, linking the theory of stable mappings to the study of generic singularities.
