\begin{proof}
The equivalences are established by the following chain of implications, each relying on a result proved earlier in the chapter:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b)] By Lemma 6.3, stability implies transverse stability.

\item[(b) $\Rightarrow$ (c)] By Theorem 6.4, transverse stability implies infinitesimal stability.

\item[(c) $\Leftrightarrow$ (d) $\Leftrightarrow$ (e)] By Theorem 4.2 and Proposition 4.1, infinitesimal stability, homotopic stability, and stability under $k$-parameter families of deformations are all pairwise equivalent.

\item[(d) $\Rightarrow$ (a)] By Proposition 2.6, homotopic stability implies stability.
\end{itemize}

Thus all five conditions are equivalent when $f: X \to Y$ is smooth and $X$ is compact.
\end{proof}
