Proposition 1.4 is the key technical step in showing that $L^r(V, W)$ — the set of linear maps of corank $r$ — is a submanifold of $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W)$. The map $\pi^*$ takes a point in the Grassmannian (a kernel subspace $K$) together with an injective map from the quotient $V/K$ to $W$, and produces the corresponding linear map from $V$ to $W$ with kernel exactly $K$. The proposition asserts that this map is injective, immersive, and proper when the target is restricted away from the higher-corank locus $M$. The proof is described as straightforward but tedious and is left as an exercise. Once established, the same statement holds for vector bundles, yielding the submanifold property of $S_r$ in $J^1(X,Y)$.
