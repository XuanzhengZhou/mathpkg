The map $\pi^*$ is the transpose (pullback) of the natural projection $\pi: V \to V/K$ from a vector space onto a quotient. By varying the subspace $K$ over the Grassmannian $G(s, V)$, this defines a map from the total space of the Hom-bundle $\operatorname{Hom}(Q, W)$ to $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W)$. The key property is that the image of $\pi^*$ consists exactly of linear maps whose kernel contains $K$, i.e., maps of corank at least $r = \dim V - s$. Restricted to the injective maps $L^0(Q_p, W)$, $\pi^*$ provides a bijection onto the set of linear maps of corank precisely $r$, giving the identification (1.2) that endows $L^r(V, W)$ with a manifold structure.
