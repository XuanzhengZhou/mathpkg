The statement follows immediately from the Thom Transversality Theorem and Chapter II, Theorem 4.4. By the Thom Transversality Theorem, the set of mappings $f$ for which $j^1 f \pitchfork S_r$ is residual. When $j^1 f$ is transverse to $S_r$, the preimage $S_r(f) = (j^1 f)^{-1}(S_r)$ is a submanifold of $X$ with $\operatorname{codim} S_r(f) = \operatorname{codim} S_r$. Chapter II, Theorem 4.4 establishes that $S_r$ is a submanifold of $J^1(X,Y)$ of codimension $r^2 + er$, where $e = |\dim X - \dim Y|$. Hence for a residual set of mappings, $S_r(f)$ is a submanifold of the stated codimension. The main technical ingredient (besides transversality) is the fact that $S_r$ is a submanifold of $J^1(X,Y)$; a sketch of an alternative proof of this fact is given in order to motivate the material in §4.
