Let $G(t, x)$ be a complex-valued function defined and smooth on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^n$. Let $P_k(t, \lambda) = t^k + \lambda_{k-1} t^{k-1} + \cdots + \lambda_0$ be the monic polynomial of degree $k$ in $t$ with coefficients $\lambda = (\lambda_0, \ldots, \lambda_{k-1})$. Then there exist smooth, complex-valued functions $q(t, x, \lambda)$ and $r(t, x, \lambda)$ defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^n \times \mathbb{R}^k$ satisfying:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $G(t, x) = q(t, x, \lambda) P_k(t, \lambda) + r(t, x, \lambda)$, and
\item[(ii)] $\displaystyle r(t, x, \lambda) = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} r_i(x, \lambda) t^i$ where each $r_i$ is a smooth function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbb{R}^n \times \mathbb{R}^k$.
\end{enumerate}

Moreover, if $G$ is real-valued then $q$ and $r$ may be chosen to be real-valued.
