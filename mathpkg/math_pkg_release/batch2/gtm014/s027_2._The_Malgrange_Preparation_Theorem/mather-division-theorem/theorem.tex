Let $F$ be a smooth real-valued function defined on a nbhd of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$ such that $F(t, 0) = g(t)t^k$ where $g(0) \neq 0$ and $g$ is smooth near $0 \in \mathbf{R}$. Then given any smooth real-valued function $G$ defined near $0 \in \mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$, there exist smooth functions $q$ and $r$ such that
$$G = qF + r$$
near $0$, where $r(t, x) = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} r_i(x) t^i$.
