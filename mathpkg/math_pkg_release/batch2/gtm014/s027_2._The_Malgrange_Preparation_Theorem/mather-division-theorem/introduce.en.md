The Mather Division Theorem is the smooth ($C^\infty$) analogue of the Weierstrass Division Theorem. Given a smooth function $F(t, x)$ whose restriction to $x=0$ vanishes to order $k$ in $t$, any smooth $G$ can be divided by $F$ with a remainder that is a polynomial in $t$ of degree $< k$. Unlike the complex case, uniqueness does not hold. This theorem, together with the Malgrange Preparation Theorem, is the technical cornerstone for Mather's theory of stable mappings.
