Let $F$ be a smooth real-valued function near $0 \in \mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$ with $F(t, 0) = g(t)t^k$, $g(0) \neq 0$. Then there exists a smooth function $q$ with $q(0) \neq 0$ and smooth functions $\lambda_0, \ldots, \lambda_{k-1}$ near $0 \in \mathbf{R}^n$ such that
$$(qF)(t, x) = t^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \lambda_i(x) t^i$$
near $0 \in \mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$.
