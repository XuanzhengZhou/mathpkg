The Malgrange Preparation Theorem is the smooth ($C^\infty$) version of the Weierstrass Preparation Theorem. It states that a smooth function $F(t, x)$ vanishing to finite order $k$ in $t$ at $x=0$ can, after multiplication by a unit $q$, be expressed as a monic polynomial in $t$ with smooth coefficients $\lambda_i(x)$. This follows from the Mather Division Theorem exactly as the Weierstrass Preparation Theorem follows from the Weierstrass Division Theorem.
