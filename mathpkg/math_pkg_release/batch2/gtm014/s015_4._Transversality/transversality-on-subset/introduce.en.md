Definition 4.8 extends the notion of transversality of a smooth mapping to a submanifold by localizing it to a prescribed subset $W'$ of the target submanifold $W$. A smooth map $f: X \to Y$ is transverse to $W$ on $W'$ if transversality holds at every preimage point of $W'$. This localized definition is essential when one needs transversality only on a portion of the target submanifold, a refinement used in perturbation arguments.
