source_section: "§3. Immersions with Normal Crossings"
---

Any mapping equivalent to an immersion with normal crossings is an immersion with normal crossings, since the condition $f^{(s)} \pitchfork \Delta Y^s$ is invariant under diffeomorphisms of $X$ and $Y$. Since $f$ is stable, every $f'$ in a neighborhood of $f$ is equivalent to $f$. By Proposition 3.2, there exist mappings with normal crossings arbitrarily close to $f$. Picking one such $f'$ in the stability neighborhood, $f$ is equivalent to $f'$, and thus $f$ has normal crossings.
