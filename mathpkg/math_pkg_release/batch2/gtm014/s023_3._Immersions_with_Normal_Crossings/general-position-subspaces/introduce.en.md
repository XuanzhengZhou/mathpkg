A collection of subspaces $H_1, \ldots, H_r$ of a vector space $V$ is in general position if codimensions add under intersection: the codimension of any intersection equals the sum of the individual codimensions. For two subspaces, this is equivalent to $H_1 + H_2 = V$, i.e., the subspaces span the ambient space. This concept is used to analyze the local geometry of normal crossing preimages.
