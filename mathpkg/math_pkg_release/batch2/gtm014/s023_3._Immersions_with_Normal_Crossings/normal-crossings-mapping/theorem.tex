Let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth and let $f^{(s)}: X^{(s)} \to Y^s$ be the restriction of $f \times \cdots \times f: X^s \to Y^s$ to $X^{(s)} = \{ (x_1, \ldots, x_s) \in X^s \mid x_i \neq x_j \text{ for } i \neq j \}$. Let $\Delta Y^s = \{ (y, \ldots, y) \in Y^s \mid y \in Y \}$. Then $f$ is a mapping with normal crossings if for every $s > 1$, $f^{(s)} \pitchfork \Delta Y^s$.
