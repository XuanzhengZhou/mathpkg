A smooth mapping $f: X \to Y$ has normal crossings if the restriction of the $s$-fold product $f \times \cdots \times f$ to the configuration space $X^{(s)}$ (distinct $s$-tuples) is transverse to the diagonal $\Delta Y^s$ for all $s > 1$. This condition controls how the image of $X$ sits inside $Y$, ensuring that all multiple points of the immersion are transversal self-intersections.
