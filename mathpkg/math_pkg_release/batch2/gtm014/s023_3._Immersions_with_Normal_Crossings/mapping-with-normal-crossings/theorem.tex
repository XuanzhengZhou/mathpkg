Let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth and let $f^{(s)}: X^{(s)} \to Y^{s}$ be the restriction of $f \times \cdots \times f: X^{s} \to Y^{s}$ to $X^{(s)}$. Let $\Delta Y^{s} = \{(y, \ldots, y) \in Y^{s} \mid y \in Y\}$. Then $f$ is a mapping with normal crossings if for every $s > 1$, $f^{(s)} \pitchfork \Delta Y^{s}$.
