Let $U_1 \subset \mathbf{R}^k$ and $U_2 \subset \mathbf{R}^l$ be open sets. Let $\phi: U_1 \times U_2 \to \mathbf{R}^l$ be $C^s$-differentiable. Define $(d_y \phi)_{(x_0, y_0)} = (d \phi_{x_0})_{y_0}$ where $x_0 \in U_1$, $y_0 \in U_2$ and $\phi_{x_0}: U_2 \to \mathbf{R}^l$ is given by $\phi_{x_0}(y) = \phi(x_0, y)$ for all $y \in U_2$.

Suppose $\phi(x_0, y_0) = y_0$. If $(d_y \phi)_{(x_0, y_0)}$ is of rank $l$, then there exists a neighborhood of $(x_0, y_0)$ and a $C^s$-differentiable function $g$ such that $\phi(x, g(x)) = y_0$.
