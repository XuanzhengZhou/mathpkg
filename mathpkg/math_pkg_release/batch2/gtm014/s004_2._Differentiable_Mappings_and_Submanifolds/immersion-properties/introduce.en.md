Corollary 2.11 gives two fundamental properties of immersions. First, the preimage of any point is a discrete set -- an immersion cannot collapse a continuum. Second, the image $\phi(X)$ is a (regular) submanifold precisely when the subspace topology inherited from $Y$ coincides with the manifold topology of the immersed submanifold; this is the key criterion distinguishing embeddings from mere 1-1 immersions.
