Definition 2.1 introduces the fundamental notion of differentiability for functions and mappings between $C^k$-differentiable manifolds. A real-valued function $f$ on a manifold $Y$ is $C^k$-differentiable if its coordinate representation $f \circ \phi^{-1}$ is $C^k$-differentiable for every chart $\phi$. A mapping $\phi: X \to Y$ between manifolds is $C^k$-differentiable if pulling back any $C^k$-differentiable function on $Y$ yields a $C^k$-differentiable function on $X$. The term "differentiable" is used throughout the text to mean $C^k$-differentiable for $k \geq 1$.
