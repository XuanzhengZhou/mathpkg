A diffeomorphism is a bijective $C^k$-differentiable map $\phi: X \to Y$ between manifolds of the same dimension $n$ whose derivative has maximal rank $n$ at every point. The inverse $\phi^{-1}$ is then automatically as differentiable as $\phi$ itself, a consequence of the inverse function theorem. Diffeomorphisms provide the natural notion of isomorphism in the category of differentiable manifolds.
