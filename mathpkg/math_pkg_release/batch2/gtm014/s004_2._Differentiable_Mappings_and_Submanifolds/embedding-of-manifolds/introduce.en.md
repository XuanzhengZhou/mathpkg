An embedding is a differentiable map $\phi: X \to Y$ that is both an immersion and a homeomorphism onto its image (with the subspace topology from $Y$). Embeddings are the "nicest" kind of map between manifolds: the image $\phi(X)$ inherits a submanifold structure from $Y$ that is compatible with the original differentiable structure on $X$.
