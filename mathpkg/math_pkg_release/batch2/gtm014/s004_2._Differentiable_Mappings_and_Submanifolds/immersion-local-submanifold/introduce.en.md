Proposition 2.10 states that every immersion $\phi: X \to Y$ is locally well-behaved: near any point $p \in X$, there is a neighborhood $U$ such that $\phi$ restricts to a homeomorphism onto its image $\phi(U)$, and moreover $\phi(U)$ is a genuine submanifold of $Y$. This is a key result bridging the local theory of immersions (via the rank theorem / Corollary 2.5) with the global definition of submanifolds.
