Definition 2.9 introduces the immersed submanifold: given a 1-1 immersion $f: X \to Y$, one can endow the image $f(X)$ with a differentiable structure by pulling back the charts from $X$ via the bijection. Crucially, this manifold topology may differ from the subspace topology inherited from $Y$. This distinction is what separates immersed submanifolds from regular submanifolds (Definition 2.7); the immersed submanifold carries the "source" topology, not the "target" subspace topology.
