Theorem 2.13 provides a fundamental criterion: every 1-1 proper immersion is an embedding, meaning its image is a regular submanifold of the target. The proof uses Corollary 2.11(2): one must show that $\phi: X \to \phi(X)$ is a homeomorphism when $\phi(X)$ carries the subspace topology. Since a continuous bijection from a compactly-generated space is a homeomorphism if it is proper, the conclusion follows. This theorem is essential for the study of stable mappings.
