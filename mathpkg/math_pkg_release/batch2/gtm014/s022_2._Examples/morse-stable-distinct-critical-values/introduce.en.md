This proposition gives a complete characterization of stable real-valued smooth functions on a compact manifold. The stable functions are precisely the Morse functions whose critical values are all pairwise distinct. The forward direction uses the fact that Morse functions with distinct critical values are dense in $C^\infty(X, \mathbb{R})$ and that stable functions are open under equivalence. The converse constructs explicit local vector fields at regular and critical points to show infinitesimal stability.
