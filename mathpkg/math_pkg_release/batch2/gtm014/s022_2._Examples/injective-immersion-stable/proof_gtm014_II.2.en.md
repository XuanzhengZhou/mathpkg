We show that $f$ is infinitesimally stable. With the given assumptions, $\operatorname{Im} f$ is a submanifold of $Y$. A vector field $w$ along $f$ can be identified with a vector field $\bar{w}$ on $\operatorname{Im} f$, since $f: X \to f(X)$ is a diffeomorphism. Let $t$ be any smooth extension of $\bar{w}$ to all of $Y$. (To see that $\bar{w}$ has a smooth extension, construct it locally and use a partition of unity argument on $f(X)$.) Then $t \circ f = \bar{w} \circ f = w$. So $w = (df)(0) + t \circ f$ and hence $f$ is infinitesimally stable.
