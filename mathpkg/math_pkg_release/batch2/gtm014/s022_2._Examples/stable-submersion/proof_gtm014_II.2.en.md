We show that $C^\infty(TX) \to C_f^\infty(X, TY)$ given by $s \mapsto (df) \cdot s$ is onto, which trivially implies that $f$ is infinitesimally stable. Since $f$ is a submersion, $(df)_x : T_xX \to T_{f(x)}Y$ is onto for every $x$ in $X$. Hence $\operatorname{Ker}(df)_x$ has constant dimension and forms a subbundle of $TX$ by I, Proposition 5.11. By I, Proposition 5.12 there exists a subbundle $H$ complementary to $\operatorname{Ker}(df)$ in $TX$. Clearly $(df)_x : H_x \to T_{f(x)}Y$ is an isomorphism (onto) and thus induces an isomorphism on sections, i.e., $C^\infty(H) \to C^\infty(TY)$. Consequently, the map $C^\infty(TX) \to C_f^\infty(X, TY)$ is surjective.
