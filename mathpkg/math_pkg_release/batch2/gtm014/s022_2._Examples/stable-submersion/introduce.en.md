A submersion $f: X \to Y$ between smooth manifolds has the property that its differential $(df)_x$ is surjective at every point $x \in X$. This proposition shows that every submersion is infinitesimally stable, meaning the map $C^\infty(TX) \to C_f^\infty(X, TY)$ sending a vector field $s$ to $(df) \cdot s$ is surjective. The proof uses the constant rank of the kernel bundle and a choice of complementary horizontal subbundle to lift vector fields from $Y$ to $X$.
