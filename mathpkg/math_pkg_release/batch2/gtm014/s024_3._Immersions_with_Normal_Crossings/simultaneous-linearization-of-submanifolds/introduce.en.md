Lemma 3.10 states that when submanifolds $Y_1, \ldots, Y_r$ of a manifold $Y$ are in general position at a common point $q$, they can be simultaneously linearized by a single coordinate chart around $q$. Concretely, there exists a neighborhood $W$ of $q$ and a chart $\phi: W \to \mathbb{R}^n$ such that each $Y_i \cap W$ is the preimage of a linear subspace $H_i$ under $\phi$. This lemma is the fundamental local model result underpinning the theory of immersions with normal crossings.
