With the identifications established in Lemma 1.12, let $\beta_f: T_{\mathrm{id}_Y} \operatorname{Diff}(Y) \to T_f C^\infty(X, Y)$ be the differential of the map $h \mapsto h \circ f$ at the identity. Show that for a tangent vector $t \in C^\infty(TY)$ represented by a smooth curve $s \mapsto h_s$ in $\operatorname{Diff}(Y)$ based at $\mathrm{id}_Y$,
$$
\beta_f(t) = t \circ f.
$$
That is, $\beta_f(t)$ is the vector field along $f$ obtained by composing the vector field $t$ on $Y$ with $f$. Use the chain rule, Lemma 1.12, and the method described in the text for computing $\alpha_f(s) = -(df) \cdot s$.
