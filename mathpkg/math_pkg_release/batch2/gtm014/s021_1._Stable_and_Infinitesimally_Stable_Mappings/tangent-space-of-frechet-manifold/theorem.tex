Let $X$ be a Fr\'{e}chet manifold and let $p \in X$. Define
$$
S_p(X) = \{ c: \mathbf{R} \to X \mid c \text{ is smooth and } c(0) = p \}.
$$
For $c_1, c_2 \in S_p(X)$, say $c_1$ is tangent to $c_2$ at $p$ if for every chart $h_\alpha$,
$$
(dh_\alpha \cdot c_1)_0 = (dh_\alpha \cdot c_2)_0.
$$
Then ``is tangent to'' is a well-defined equivalence relation, and the tangent space $T_p X$ is the set of equivalence classes
$$
T_p X = S_p(X) / \sim.
$$
$T_p X$ is a vector space. A smooth map $f: X \to Y$ between Fr\'{e}chet manifolds induces a well-defined linear mapping
$$
(df)_p: T_p X \to T_{f(p)} Y.
$$
