Let $X$ be a compact finite dimensional manifold. Then
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $C^\infty(X, \mathbf{R})$ is a Fr\'{e}chet space,
\item[(b)] if $E$ is a vector bundle over $X$, then $C^\infty(E)$ is a Fr\'{e}chet space, and
\item[(c)] let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth, then $C_f^\infty(X, TY)$ --- the set of vector fields along $f$ --- is a Fr\'{e}chet space.
\end{enumerate}
In each case the topology induced by the Fr\'{e}chet ``norm'' is the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology.
