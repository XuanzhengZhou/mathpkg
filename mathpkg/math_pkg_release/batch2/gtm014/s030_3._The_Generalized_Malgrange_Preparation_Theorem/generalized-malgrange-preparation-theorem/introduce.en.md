The Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem extends the classical preparation theorem to the setting of finitely generated modules over rings of smooth function germs. Given a smooth map $\phi: X \to Y$ and a finitely generated $C_p^\infty(X)$-module $A$, the theorem asserts that $A$ becomes a finitely generated $C_q^\infty(Y)$-module via $\phi^*$ whenever the quotient $A / \mathcal{M}_q(Y)A$ is finite dimensional over $\mathbb{R}$. The proof factorizes $\phi$ into an immersion followed by a sequence of submersions and applies separate lemmas for the immersion case (Lemma 3.8) and the submersion case (Lemma 3.7). This result is fundamental in singularity theory for reducing questions about modules over $C_p^\infty(X)$ to finite-dimensional linear algebra.
