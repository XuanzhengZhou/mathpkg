The map $\phi^*: C_q^\infty(Y) \to C_p^\infty(X)$ is onto, since there is a neighborhood $U$ of $p$ in $X$ such that $\phi|U$ is a one-to-one immersion and $\phi(U)$ is a submanifold of $Y$. Using the definition of submanifold, every smooth function on $\phi(U)$ can be extended to a smooth function on a neighborhood of $\phi(U)$ in $Y$. (Since we are working locally we can assume that $\phi(U)$ is small enough to be made a $k$-plane in $\mathbf{R}^m$ by one chart on $Y$.) The surjectivity of $\phi^*$ implies that any finitely generated $C_p^\infty(X)$-module $A$ is, via restriction of scalars along $\phi^*$, a finitely generated $C_q^\infty(Y)$-module.
