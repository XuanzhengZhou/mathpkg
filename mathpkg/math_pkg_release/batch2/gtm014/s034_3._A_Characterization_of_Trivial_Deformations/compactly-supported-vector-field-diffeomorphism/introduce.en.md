Lemma 3.4 is a technical construction used in the sufficiency proof of the Thom-Levine theorem (Theorem 3.3). Given a compactly supported vector field $\zeta$ on $X \times \mathbf{R}^k$ whose $\mathbf{R}^k$-component vanishes, the lemma produces a diffeomorphism $g$ that is a deformation of the identity on $X$ and whose derivative pushes forward $\partial/\partial t_k$ by precisely $\zeta + \partial/\partial t_k$. The proof uses the existence of a global one-parameter group for $\zeta + \partial/\partial t_k$, guaranteed by the compact support of $\zeta$. This lemma, together with its counterpart Lemma 3.5, provides the iterative construction that conjugates a deformation satisfying the infinitesimal condition to the trivial deformation.
