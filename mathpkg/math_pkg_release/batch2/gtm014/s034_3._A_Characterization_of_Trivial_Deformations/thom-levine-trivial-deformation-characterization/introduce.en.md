The Thom-Levine theorem (Theorem 3.3 in GTM014) gives a fundamental characterization of trivial deformations of smooth mappings. A $k$-deformation $F: X \times V \to Y \times V$ of a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ is trivial precisely when there exist vector fields $\zeta^i$ on $X \times U$ and $\eta^i$ on $Y \times U$ with vanishing $\mathbf{R}^k$-components satisfying the infinitesimal equation $\tau_F^i = (dF)(\zeta^i) + F^*(\eta^i)$. Here $\tau_F^i$ measures the infinitesimal variation of $F$ in the $i$-th deformation direction. The theorem reduces the geometric question of triviality to an algebraic condition on vector fields, playing a central role in the theory of stable mappings and singularities.
