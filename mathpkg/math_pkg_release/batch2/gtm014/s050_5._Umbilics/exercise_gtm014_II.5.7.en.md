Let $Q$ be the vector space consisting of all pairs $(\alpha_1, \alpha_2)$ where $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ are homogeneous quadratic polynomials in $(x_1, x_2)$. Let $P$ be the subset of $Q$ consisting of all $(\alpha_1, \alpha_2)$ for which $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ have a common linear factor. Let $U$ be the subset of $P$ consisting of all $(\alpha_1, \alpha_2)$ for which $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ have a common quadratic factor (i.e., are constant multiples of each other). Let $W$ be the subset of $U$ consisting of all $(\alpha_1, \alpha_2)$ for which
$$\alpha_i = c_i (s_1 x_1 + s_2 x_2)^2, \quad i = 1, 2.$$

Show that $f$, given by
$$(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \mapsto (\alpha_1 + \cdots,\; \alpha_2 + \cdots,\; x_3, \ldots, x_n),$$
has an $S_{2,0}$ singularity at the origin and satisfies the transversality condition $j^1 f \pitchfork S_2$. Show it is not an umbilic. (Hint: Use Exercise 7, VI, \S 1.)

Also show by an example that a small perturbation of $f$ will yield an umbilic at $0$.
