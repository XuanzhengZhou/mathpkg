Lemma 5.22 is the elliptic analogue of Lemma 5.13, providing a smooth diagonalization result for a perturbed difference-of-squares quadratic form. Given a quadratic form $a(x_1^2 - x_2^2) + bx_2^2$ depending smoothly on parameters $z$ and reducing to $x_1^2 - x_2^2$ when $z = 0$, the lemma guarantees a smooth rescaling of coordinates by a factor $\lambda$ (with reciprocal scaling on the other coordinate) that brings the form back to a pure difference-of-squares $\bar{a}(\bar{x}_1^2 - \bar{x}_2^2)$. This lemma replaces Lemma 5.13 in the proof of the elliptic case of Theorem 5.10, which establishes the canonical normal form for elliptic umbilics.
