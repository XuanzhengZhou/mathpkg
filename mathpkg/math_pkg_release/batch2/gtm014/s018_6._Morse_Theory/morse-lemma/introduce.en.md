The Morse Lemma is the foundational result of Morse theory. It states that near a nondegenerate critical point $p$ of a smooth function $f$, there exist local coordinates in which $f$ takes the standard quadratic form $f(p) - x_1^2 - \cdots - x_k^2 + x_{k+1}^2 + \cdots + x_n^2$. The integer $k$, called the Morse index, is the number of negative eigenvalues of the Hessian and is an invariant of the critical point. This normal form completely determines the local behavior of the function, reducing the study of critical points to the study of nondegenerate quadratic forms.
