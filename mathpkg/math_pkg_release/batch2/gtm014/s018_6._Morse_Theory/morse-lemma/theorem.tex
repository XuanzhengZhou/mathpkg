\textbf{Morse Lemma.} Let $f: X \to \mathbb{R}$ be a smooth function on a smooth manifold $X$ and let $p \in X$ be a nondegenerate critical point of $f$. Then there exists a neighborhood $U$ of $p$ and a coordinate chart $\alpha: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ centered at $p$ such that for all $x \in U$,
$$f(x) = f(p) - (\alpha_1^2(x) + \cdots + \alpha_k^2(x)) + \alpha_{k+1}^2(x) + \cdots + \alpha_n^2(x),$$
where $k$ is the index of the Hessian $(d^2 f)_p$ (the number of negative eigenvalues).
