Lemma (B) describes how the intrinsic Hessian transforms under pullback by a smooth map. If $\phi: X \to Y$ and $f: Y \to \mathbb{R}$ with $q$ a critical point of $f$, then any preimage $p \in \phi^{-1}(q)$ is a critical point of the pullback $\phi^* f$, and the Hessian of $\phi^* f$ at $p$ is the pullback of the Hessian of $f$ at $q$. The proof follows from the chain rule in local coordinates.
