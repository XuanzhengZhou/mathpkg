A smooth function $f$ has a nondegenerate critical point at $p$ precisely when its Hessian matrix at $p$ is invertible. This equivalence is proved using jet bundle transversality: the 1-jet $j^1 f$ is transverse to the submanifold $S_1 \subset J^1(U, \mathbf{R})$ (the set of 1-jets with zero derivative) exactly when the Hessian is nonsingular. Nondegenerate critical points are isolated and play a central role in Morse theory.
