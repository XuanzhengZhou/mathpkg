Lemma 6.11 extends the first-order Taylor expansion to second order, under the assumption that the first-order coefficients vanish at the basepoint $a$ (which holds at a critical point). The function $f(x) - f(a)$ can be expressed as a quadratic form $\sum g_{ij}(x)(x_i - a_i)(x_j - a_j)$ with smooth, symmetric coefficient functions $g_{ij}$. The values $g_{ij}(a)$ recover half the Hessian matrix entries at $a$. This lemma is a key step in proving the Morse Lemma, providing the quadratic representation needed for the inductive diagonalization in Lemma 6.12.
