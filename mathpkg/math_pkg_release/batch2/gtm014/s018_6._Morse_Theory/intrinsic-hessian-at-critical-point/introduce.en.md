The intrinsic (coordinate-free) Hessian $(d^2 f)_p$ at a critical point $p$ of a smooth function $f: X \to \mathbf{R}$ is a well-defined symmetric bilinear form on the tangent space $T_p X$. It is nondegenerate precisely when $p$ is a nondegenerate critical point. This invariant definition is constructed using the intrinsic derivative and pullback along coordinate charts, with symmetry derived from local coordinate computations.
