Proposition 6.4 of GTM014 characterizes nondegenerate critical points in terms of the Hessian matrix: a critical point $p$ is nondegenerate precisely when the Hessian $(\partial^2 f/\partial x_i \partial x_j)(p)$ is nonsingular. The proof uses jet bundle machinery: viewing $j^1 f$ as a section of the 1-jet bundle, the condition $j^1 f \pitchfork S_1$ (transversality to the zero section) translates via Lemma 4.3 to the submersion condition on $\pi \circ j^1 f$, which is equivalent to the Hessian being nonsingular.
