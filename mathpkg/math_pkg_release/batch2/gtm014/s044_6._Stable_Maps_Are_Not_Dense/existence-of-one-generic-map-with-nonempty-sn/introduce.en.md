Proposition 6.1 establishes that when the source and target manifolds both have dimension $n^2$, there exists at least one one-generic map whose $S_n$ singularity locus is nonempty. This is a crucial existence result in the proof that stable maps are not dense in $C^\infty(X,Y)$ for these dimensions. The proof strategy reduces the global problem to a local one by constructing a map germ at a point that takes on an $S_n$ singularity transversely, then perturbing to obtain a one-generic map with the desired property.
