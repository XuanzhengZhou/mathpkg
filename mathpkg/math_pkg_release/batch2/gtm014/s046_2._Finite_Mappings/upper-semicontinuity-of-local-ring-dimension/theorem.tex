Let $f: X \to Y$ be a smooth map between manifolds, and for $x \in X$ let $\mathcal{R}_f(x) = C_x^\infty(X) / f^*(\mathcal{M}_{f(x)}(Y))$ be the local ring of $f$ at $x$. Then the function
$$x \mapsto \dim_{\mathbf{R}} \mathcal{R}_f(x)$$
is upper semi-continuous on $X$. In particular, if $f$ is finite at a point $x \in X$, then $f$ is finite at all points in some neighborhood of $x$.
