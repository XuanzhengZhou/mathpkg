Call $\dim_{\mathbf{R}} \mathcal{R}_f(x)$ the *multiplicity* of the point $x$ with respect to the mapping $f: X \to Y$. Prove Proposition 2.4: for a finite smooth map $f$, the sum of multiplicities over the pre-image points of a regular value is locally constant. That is, there exists an open neighborhood $U$ of $x$ such that for every $y$ sufficiently close to $f(x)$,
$$\sum_{z \in f^{-1}(y) \cap U} \dim_{\mathbf{R}} \mathcal{R}_f(z)$$
is constant.
