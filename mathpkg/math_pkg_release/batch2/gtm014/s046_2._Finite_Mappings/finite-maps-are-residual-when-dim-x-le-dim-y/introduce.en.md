Theorem 2.6, due to Tougeron, states that when the source manifold has dimension no greater than the target, finite maps are generic in the sense of Baire category: they form a residual (dense $G_\delta$) subset of $C^\infty(X, Y)$ in the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology. The theorem goes further -- the complement of the finite maps has "infinite codimension," meaning the non-finite maps are exceptionally rare. The proof appears in Tougeron's work [51].
