**Proof.** The proof of Theorem 2.6 is not given in the text. The authors refer to Tougeron's original work:

> See [51].

The reference [51] is: J. Cl. Tougeron, *Ideaux de fonctions differentiables*, Ergebnisse der Mathematik, Band 71, Springer-Verlag, 1972.

The argument proceeds by showing that the set of non-finite maps is a countable union of closed nowhere-dense subsets of $C^\infty(X, Y)$ (in the Whitney topology), making the finite maps a residual set. The "infinite codimension" statement refers to the fact that the non-finite maps fail to satisfy an infinite number of generically satisfied transversality conditions.
