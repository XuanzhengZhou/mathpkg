This proposition establishes that composition of smooth maps is a continuous operation in the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology when the domain $X$ is compact. The proof uses Lemma 3.7 to relate the jet composition map $\gamma: J^k(X, Y) \times_Y J^k(Y, Z) \to J^k(X, Z)$ to the fiber product of jet bundles over $Y$. A key remark notes that the proposition fails for non-compact $X$, unless one restricts to the subset of proper mappings from $X$ to $Y$. This result is essential for treating $C^\infty$ mapping spaces as topological categories under the Whitney topology.
