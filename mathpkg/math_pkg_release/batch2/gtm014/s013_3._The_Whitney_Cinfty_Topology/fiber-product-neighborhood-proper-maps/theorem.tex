Let $A$, $B$, and $P$ be Hausdorff spaces. Suppose that $P$ is locally compact and paracompact. Let $\pi_A: A \to P$ and $\pi_B: B \to P$ be continuous. Set
$$A \times_P B = \{(a, b) \in A \times B \mid \pi_A(a) = \pi_B(b)\}$$
and give $A \times_P B$ the topology induced from $A \times B$. Let $K \subset A$ and $L \subset B$ be subsets such that $\pi_A|_K$ and $\pi_B|_L$ are proper. Let $U$ be an open neighborhood of $K \times_P L$ in $A \times_P B$. Then there exists a neighborhood $V$ of $K$ in $A$ and a neighborhood $W$ of $L$ in $B$ such that $V \times_P W \subseteq U$.
