Let $Z$ be a manifold with $p \in Z$, and let $A, B$ be submanifold germs of $Z$ near $p$. Define
$$
\mathcal{I}_p(B) = \{ g \in C_p^\infty(Z) \mid g(B) = 0 \}.
$$
Let $i_A: A \to Z$ be the canonical inclusion map. Define $\mathcal{I}_p(A, B)$ to be the ideal $i_A^*(\mathcal{I}_p(B))$ where $i_A^*: C_p^\infty(Z) \to C_p^\infty(A)$ is the ring homomorphism induced by $i_A$. Then
$$
\mathcal{R}_{A,B} = C_p^\infty(A) / \mathcal{I}_p(A, B)
$$
is called the \textit{local ring of the contact} of $B$ with $A$. It is an invariant of the contact of $B$ with $A$.
