The local ring $\mathcal{R}_{A,B}$ of the contact of $B$ with $A$ is an algebraic invariant constructed from the ideal of smooth functions vanishing on $B$, pulled back to $A$ via the inclusion map, and then quotienting the ring of smooth functions on $A$ by this ideal. This construction transforms the geometric notion of contact of submanifold germs into an algebraic object — a local ring — that can be studied with commutative algebra. It is clear from the definitions that if $B_1$ and $B_2$ have the same contact with $A$, then $\mathcal{R}_{A,B_1} = \mathcal{R}_{A,B_2}$.
