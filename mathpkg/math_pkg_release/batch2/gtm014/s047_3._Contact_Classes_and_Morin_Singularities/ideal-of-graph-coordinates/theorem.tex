Let $b: (\mathbf{R}^k, 0) \to (\mathbf{R}^l, 0)$ be a map germ where $b(x) = (b_1(x), \ldots, b_l(x))$, with coordinates $x$ on $\mathbf{R}^k$ and $y$ on $\mathbf{R}^l$. Then
$$
\mathcal{I}_{(0,0)}(\mathbf{R}^k \times \{0\}, \operatorname{graph} b) = (b_1, \ldots, b_l)
$$
as an ideal in $C_0^\infty(\mathbf{R}^k)$, where $\mathbf{R}^k \times \{0\}$ and $\operatorname{graph} b$ are submanifold germs of $\mathbf{R}^k \times \mathbf{R}^l$ near $(0, 0)$.
