Two submanifold germs $B_1$ and $B_2$ through a point $p$ in a manifold $Z$ have the same contact with a reference submanifold germ $A$ if there exists a local diffeomorphism of $Z$ fixing $A$ pointwise that sends $B_1$ to $B_2$. This notion captures the idea that $B_1$ and $B_2$ are geometrically indistinguishable relative to $A$ near $p$. Contact of submanifold germs is the foundational geometric concept underlying the classification of map singularities via local rings.
