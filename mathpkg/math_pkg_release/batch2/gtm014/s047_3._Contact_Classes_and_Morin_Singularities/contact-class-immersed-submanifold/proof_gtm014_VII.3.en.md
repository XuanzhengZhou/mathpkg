As in previous theorems of this type, the crucial part of the proof is in showing that $S_{\mathcal{R}} \cap J^k(X, Y)_{p,q}$ is a submanifold, since $S_{\mathcal{R}}$ is clearly a subfiber bundle of $J^k(X, Y)$ over $X \times Y$. For this purpose, we may choose coordinates so that $X = \mathbf{R}^n$, $Y = \mathbf{R}^m$, and $p = q = 0$.

There is a natural action of the Lie group $G^k(\mathbf{R}^n)_0$ (invertible $k$-jets on $\mathbf{R}^n$ at $0$) on $H_{k,l}^n$, the space of $k$-th order contact elements. Let $(j^k \phi)_0$ be in $G^k(\mathbf{R}^n)_0$ and let $A$ be in $H_{k,l}^n$. Then $\phi(A)$ is a submanifold germ of $\mathbf{R}^n$ at $0$ in $H_{k,l}^n$. This gives a well-defined smooth action. Similarly, the action of $G^k(\mathbf{R}^m)_0$ on the target side combines with the source action. The contact class $S_{\mathcal{R}}$ is an orbit of this combined group action, and by the general theory of Lie group actions (Appendix A), orbits are immersed submanifolds.
