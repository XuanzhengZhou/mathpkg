As in previous theorems of this type, the crucial part of the proof is showing that $S_{\mathcal{R}} \cap J^k(X, Y)_{p,q}$ is a submanifold, since $S_{\mathcal{R}}$ is clearly a subfiber bundle of $J^k(X, Y)$ over $X \times Y$. For this purpose, we may choose coordinates so that $X = \mathbf{R}^n$, $Y = \mathbf{R}^m$, and $p = q = 0$.

Now there is a natural action of the Lie group $G^k(\mathbf{R}^n)_0$ (the group of invertible $k$-jets on $\mathbf{R}^n$ at $0$) on $H_{k,l}^n$. Let $(j^k\phi)_0 \in G^k(\mathbf{R}^n)_0$ and let $A \in H_{k,l}^n$. Then $\phi(A)$ is a submanifold germ of $\mathbf{R}^n$ at $0$ in $H_{k,l}^n$, giving a well-defined smooth action. Similarly, the action of $G^k(\mathbf{R}^m)_0$ on the target side and the combined action on the jet space produce a transitive group action whose orbits are precisely the fibers of the contact class map.

The key observation is that the contact class $S_{\mathcal{R}} \cap J^k(\mathbf{R}^n, \mathbf{R}^m)_{0,0}$ is an orbit of the combined action of $G^k(\mathbf{R}^n)_0 \times G^k(\mathbf{R}^m)_0$ on $J^k(\mathbf{R}^n, \mathbf{R}^m)_{0,0}$. Since Lie group actions on manifolds produce immersed submanifolds as orbits, the result follows.

More precisely, the local ring $\mathcal{R}_\sigma$ of a $k$-jet $\sigma$ at $0$ is invariant under the contact group action. The orbit of a given $k$-jet under the contact group is exactly the set of all $k$-jets with isomorphic local rings. By the general theory of Lie group actions, each orbit is an immersed submanifold, establishing the theorem.
