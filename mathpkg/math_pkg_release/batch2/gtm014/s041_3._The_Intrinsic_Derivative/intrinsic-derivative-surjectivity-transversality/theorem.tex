Suppose $\sigma = \rho_x$ is of corank $r$. Then the following two assertions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $(D\rho)_x : T_x X \to \operatorname{Hom}(K_\sigma, L_\sigma)$ is surjective.
\item[(b)] $\rho$, viewed as a map $X \to \operatorname{Hom}(E, F)$, is transverse to $L^r(E, F)$ at $x$.
\end{enumerate}
