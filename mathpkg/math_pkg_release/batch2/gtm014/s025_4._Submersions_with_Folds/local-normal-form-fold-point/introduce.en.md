Near a fold point, a smooth mapping admits local coordinates in which it takes the standard quadratic form $(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \mapsto (x_1, \ldots, x_{m-1}, \sum \pm x_i^2)$. The quadratic part in the last coordinate involves only the variables $x_m, \ldots, x_n$, with a nondegenerate (Morse-type) Hessian. This normal form is the fold analogue of the Morse Lemma and explains the geometric structure: folding occurs along the singular set $S_1(f) = \{x_m = \cdots = x_n = 0\}$.
