Lemma 4.7 is the main technical step in proving the sufficiency part of the infinitesimal stability criterion for submersions with folds (Theorem 4.4). It states that any vector field $\tau$ along $f$ that vanishes on the singular set $S_1(f)$ near a fold point $p$ can be lifted through the differential $df$ to a vector field $\zeta$ on $X$. This local lifting property is crucial for establishing that $f|_{S_1(f)}$ is infinitesimally stable, using the direct sum decomposition $T X|_{S_1(f)} = T S_1(f) \oplus \operatorname{Ker}(dg)$ at fold points.
