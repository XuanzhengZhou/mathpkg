This exercise establishes a general upper bound for the cardinality of fibers of a stable immersion $f: X \to Y$. The bound is $\dim Y / (\dim Y - \dim X)$, which follows from the normal crossings condition: at any point $q \in Y$, the preimages lie on transversely intersecting sheets of the immersed submanifold $f(X)$, and dimension counting restricts how many such sheets can meet at a single point. For the special case $\dim Y = 2 \dim X$, the bound reduces to $2$, confirming that stable immersions have at most double points.
