For compact $X$ with $\dim X \geq \dim Y$, a smooth mapping $f: X \to Y$ is stable precisely when: (1) its only singularities are fold points, and (2) the restriction of $f$ to the singular set $S_1(f)$ is an immersion with normal crossings. This theorem, due to Mather, provides a complete geometric characterization of stable mappings in terms of elementary singularity types and their mutual intersections.
