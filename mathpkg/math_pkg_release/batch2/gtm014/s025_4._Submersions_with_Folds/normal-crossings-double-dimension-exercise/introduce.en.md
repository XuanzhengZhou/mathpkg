This exercise asks to interpret the normal crossings condition for an immersion $f: X \to Y$ in the special case where the target manifold has twice the dimension of the source, i.e., $\dim Y = 2 \dim X$. In this situation, the immersion has no triple self-intersections; normal crossings means that $f(X)$ intersects itself transversely in at most isolated double points.
