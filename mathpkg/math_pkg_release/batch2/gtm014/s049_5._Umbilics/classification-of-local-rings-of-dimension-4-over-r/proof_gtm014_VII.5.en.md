Let $\mathcal{M}$ be the maximal ideal of $\mathcal{R}$. 

Suppose first that $\dim_{\mathbf{R}} \mathcal{M}/\mathcal{M}^2 = 1$. Let $t$ be an element of $\mathcal{M} \setminus \mathcal{M}^2$. Then $\mathcal{M}^i = \{c t^i\} + \mathcal{M}^{i+1}$, so the mapping of $\mathbf{R}[t]$ into $\mathcal{R}$ is surjective. The kernel is generated by a polynomial $p(t)$, which we can write as $t^k q(t)$ with $q(t)$ having a nonzero constant term. The image of $q(t)$ in $\mathcal{R}$ is invertible, so the kernel is also generated by $t^k$. Hence $\mathcal{R} \cong \mathbf{R}[t]/(t^4)$ (since $\dim \mathcal{R} = 4$, the only possibility is $k = 4$).

Now suppose $\dim \mathcal{M}/\mathcal{M}^2 = 2$. Let $x, y$ be a basis of $\mathcal{M}/\mathcal{M}^2$. Then $\mathcal{M}^2/\mathcal{M}^3$ is spanned by $x^2, xy, y^2$, giving the quadratic form (5.8). If this form is nondegenerate and definite, then after a linear change we have $x^2 = y^2$ and $xy = 0$, yielding $\mathcal{R} \cong \mathbf{R}[x, y]/(x^2 - y^2, xy)$. If nondegenerate and indefinite, we get $x^2 = y^2 = 0$ with $xy \neq 0$, yielding $\mathcal{R} \cong \mathbf{R}[x, y]/(x^2, y^2)$. If degenerate, we get $\mathcal{R} \cong \mathbf{R}[x, y]/(x^2, xy)$.

Finally, if $\dim \mathcal{M}/\mathcal{M}^2 = 3$, then by dimension count $\mathcal{M}^2 = 0$ and $\mathcal{R} \cong \mathbf{R}[x, y, z]/(x^2, y^2, z^2, xy, xz, yz)$.
