Proposition 5.6 gives a complete algebraic classification of local $\mathbf{R}$-algebras of vector space dimension 4. The proof proceeds by case analysis on $\dim \mathcal{M}/\mathcal{M}^2$, where $\mathcal{M}$ is the maximal ideal. For dimension 1, one obtains the truncated polynomial ring $\mathbf{R}[t]/(t^4)$. For dimension 2, the isomorphism type is determined by whether the natural quadratic form on $\mathcal{M}/\mathcal{M}^2$ is nondegenerate definite, nondegenerate indefinite, or degenerate. For dimension 3, necessarily $\mathcal{M}^2 = 0$.
