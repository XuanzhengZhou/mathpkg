If a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ is stable (every nearby map is equivalent to $f$ under diffeomorphisms of source and target), then at every point $p \in X$, the $m$-jet extension $j^m f$ is transverse to the $\mathrm{Diff}(X) \times \mathrm{Diff}(Y)$-orbit through $j^m f(p)$. The proof uses the Thom Transversality Theorem: since $f$ has a neighborhood $U$ of equivalent maps, one can find $g \in U$ whose $m$-jet is transverse to the orbit; conjugating back to $f$ preserves the transversality because the diffeomorphism action maps orbits to orbits.
