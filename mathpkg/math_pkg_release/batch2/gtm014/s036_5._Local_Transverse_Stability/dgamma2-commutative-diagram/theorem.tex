Let $(d\gamma_2): C^\infty(TX)_p \to T_\sigma \mathcal{D}_\sigma$ be the map induced by the action of $\mathrm{Diff}(X)$ on $J^k(X,Y)$, defined by $(d\gamma_2)(\xi) = (dc/dt)|_{t=0}$ where $c(t) = \sigma \cdot j^k \psi_t^{-1}(\psi_t(p))$ and $\psi_t$ is the one-parameter group of $\xi$. Then the diagram
\[
\begin{CD}
C^\infty(TX)_p @>{(d\gamma_2)}>> T_\sigma \mathcal{D}_\sigma \\
@V{\pi_k^\infty}VV @A{\lambda}AA \\
J^k(TX)_p @>{-(df) + (dj^k f)_p \cdot \pi_0^k}>> J^k(f^*TY)_p
\end{CD}
\]
commutes, where $\pi_0^k: J^k(TX)_p \to J^0(TX)_p = T_p X$ is the obvious projection and $\pi_k^\infty$ is the $k$-jet projection.
