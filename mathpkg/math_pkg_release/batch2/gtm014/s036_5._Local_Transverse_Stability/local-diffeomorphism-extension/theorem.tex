Let $\phi$ be a local diffeomorphism on $\mathbb{R}^n$ defined near $0$ satisfying $\phi(0) = 0$ and $\det(d\phi)_0 > 0$. Then there is a diffeomorphism $\eta: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ such that $\eta = \mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{R}^n}$ outside of some compact set $K$ and $\eta = \phi$ on some neighborhood of $0$.
