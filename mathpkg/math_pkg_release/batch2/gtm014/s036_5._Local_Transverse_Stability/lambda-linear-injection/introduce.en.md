This proposition constructs a canonical linear map $\lambda$ from the $k$-jet of sections of the pullback bundle $f^*TY$ at $p$ into the tangent space of the jet bundle $J^k(X,Y)$ at $\sigma = j^k f(p)$. Given a vector field $\tau$ along $f$ (representing an element of $J^k(f^*TY)_p$), one takes a deformation $F$ of $f$ with initial velocity $\tau$, and differentiates the path $t \mapsto j^k F_t(p)$. The map is well-defined because the derivative depends only on the $k$-jet of $\tau$, and injectivity follows from a coordinate computation using Taylor expansions. This map is the jet-level analogue of the identification $T_f C^\infty(X,Y) \cong C^\infty(f^*TY)$.
