Let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth and let $p$ be in $X$. Let $m = \dim Y$ and let $\sigma = j^m f(p)$. Then $f$ is \textit{locally transverse stable at $p$} if $j^m f \pitchfork \mathcal{D}_\sigma$ at $p$, where $\mathcal{D}_\sigma$ is the orbit of the action of $\mathrm{Diff}(X) \times \mathrm{Diff}(Y)$ on $J^m(X, Y)$ through $\sigma$.
