A smooth map $f: X \to Y$ is locally transverse stable at a point $p$ if its $m$-jet extension $j^m f: X \to J^m(X,Y)$ (where $m = \dim Y$) is transverse to the orbit $\mathcal{D}_\sigma$ of the natural $\mathrm{Diff}(X) \times \mathrm{Diff}(Y)$ action through $\sigma = j^m f(p)$. Since $\mathcal{D}_\sigma$ is merely an immersed submanifold (Theorem 5.1), transversality to an immersed submanifold is understood in the natural way: either the image misses the submanifold, or the tangent space of the ambient manifold is spanned by the image of the derivative and the tangent space of the submanifold. This definition is the geometric bridge between the algebraic notion of infinitesimal stability and the geometric notion of stability under deformations.
