This proposition shows that the tangent space $T_\sigma J^k(X,Y)$ at a jet $\sigma = j^k f(p)$ fits into a natural short exact sequence. The kernel of the derivative of the source map $(d\alpha)_\sigma$ is precisely the image of the injection $\lambda: J^k(f^*TY)_p \to T_\sigma J^k(X,Y)$. The proof verifies that $\mathrm{Im}\,\lambda \subseteq \mathrm{Ker}(d\alpha)_\sigma$ by noting that the source point $p$ is constant along the deformation defining $\lambda$, and then proves equality by a dimension count: both $\dim J^k(f^*TY)$ and $\dim \mathrm{Ker}(d\alpha)_\sigma$ equal the dimension of the space of polynomial mappings of degree $\leq k$ from $\mathbb{R}^n$ to $\mathbb{R}^m$.
