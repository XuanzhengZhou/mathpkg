A Lie group is a set that carries both the structure of a smooth manifold and an algebraic group, with the two structures compatible: the map $(a, b) \mapsto ab^{-1}$ is required to be smooth. Examples include $\mathbf{R}^n$ under addition, the circle $S^1$, tori $T^n = S^1 \times \cdots \times S^1$, and matrix groups such as $GL(n, \mathbf{R})$, $O(n)$, and $SL(n, \mathbf{R})$. The only abelian connected Lie groups are $\mathbf{R}^n \times T^m$. Lie groups are essential in singularity theory for studying group actions on jet spaces and defining contact equivalence.
