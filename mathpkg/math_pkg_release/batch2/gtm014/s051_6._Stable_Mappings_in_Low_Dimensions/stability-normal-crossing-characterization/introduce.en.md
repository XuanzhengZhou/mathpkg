Theorem 6.3 provides a complete global characterization of stable equidimensional maps in dimensions at most 4. Once a map $f: X \to Y$ (with $\dim X = \dim Y \leq 4$) exhibits only the permissible Boardman singularities and does so transversely, stability reduces entirely to the normal crossing condition: the tangent spaces of different singularity strata pushed forward to the target must be in general position at every multiply-covered image point. This theorem is deduced from a slightly more general result (Theorem 6.4) and builds on the stratification framework developed in Chapter VI.
