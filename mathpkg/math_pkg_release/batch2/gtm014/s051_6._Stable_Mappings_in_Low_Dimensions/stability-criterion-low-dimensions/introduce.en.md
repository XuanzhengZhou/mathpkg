In dimensions at most 4, the possible "nonremovable" singularities of stable equidimensional maps form a short, explicit list: folds in dimension 1, folds and cusps in dimension 2, and progressively more types up to dimension 4 where elliptic and hyperbolic umbilics appear alongside $S_{1_k}$ singularities. Proposition 6.2 states that a map germ in these low dimensions is stable precisely when it exhibits only singularities from this list and does so transversely. This characterization follows from Mather's general theory and gives a complete local picture of stable mappings in low dimensions.
