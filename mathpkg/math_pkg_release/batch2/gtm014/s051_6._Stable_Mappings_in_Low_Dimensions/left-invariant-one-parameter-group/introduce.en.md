Lemma A.3 is the key step in establishing the exponential map for Lie groups. It states that any left-invariant vector field $\zeta$ on a Lie group $G$ admits a globally defined flow (one-parameter group), not just a local one. The proof uses left invariance to extend a locally defined flow $\psi_t$ near the identity to the whole group via the relation $g \psi_t(a) = \psi_t(ga)$, which follows from $(dL_g)\zeta = \zeta$. Since $\psi_t$ is globally defined for small $|t|$, it generates a full one-parameter group on all of $G$.
