This theorem is the culmination of Section 2, establishing that the space of $k$-jets $J^k(X, Y)$ is a smooth manifold and a fiber bundle over $X \times Y$ with fiber diffeomorphic to $B_{n,m}^k$. The source and target maps are smooth submersions, making $J^k(X, Y)$ a well-behaved geometric object. The fiber over $(p,q)$ parametrizes all possible Taylor polynomials of degree $k$ (without constant term) of maps sending $p$ to $q$. Notably, $J^k(X, Y)$ is a fiber bundle with Euclidean fiber but is not in general a vector bundle, because the transition functions involve nonlinear changes of coordinates (chain rule for higher derivatives).
