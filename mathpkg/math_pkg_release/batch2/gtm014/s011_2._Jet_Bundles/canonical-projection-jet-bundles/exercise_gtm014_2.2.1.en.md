There is an obvious canonical projection $\pi_{k,l}: J^k(X, Y) \to J^l(X, Y)$ for $k > l$ defined by forgetting the jet information of order $> l$. Show that $J^k(X, Y)$ is a fiber bundle over $J^l(X, Y)$ with projection $\pi_{k,l}$ and identify the fiber.
