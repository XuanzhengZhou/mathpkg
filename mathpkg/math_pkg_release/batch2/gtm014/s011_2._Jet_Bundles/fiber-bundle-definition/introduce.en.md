A fiber bundle is a smooth surjection $\pi: E \to X$ that is locally a product $U \times F$. The total space $E$ is globally assembled from these local products via transition functions. Fiber bundles generalize product spaces and include vector bundles, principal bundles, and sphere bundles as special cases. In the context of jet theory, $J^k(X,Y)$ is a fiber bundle over $X \times Y$ with fiber $B_{n,m}^k$, but it is notably not a vector bundle for $k > 1$ because the transition functions for higher derivatives are polynomial rather than linear.
