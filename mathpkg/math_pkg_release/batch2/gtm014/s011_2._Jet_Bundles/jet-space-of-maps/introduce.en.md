The space $J^k(X, Y)$ of $k$-jets collects all equivalence classes of smooth map germs under the relation of $k$-th order contact. Each $k$-jet $\sigma$ has a well-defined source $\alpha(\sigma) \in X$ and target $\beta(\sigma) \in Y$, giving two natural projections. For any smooth map $f: X \to Y$, its $k$-jet extension $j^k f: X \to J^k(X, Y)$ records the Taylor polynomial of degree $k$ of $f$ at each point in a coordinate-invariant manner, making $J^k(X, Y)$ the universal space for studying singularities of smooth maps.
