Boardman proved in his I.H.E.S. paper [6] that for every sequence of integers $r_1 \geq r_2 \geq \cdots \geq r_k \geq 0$, one can define a fiber subbundle $S_{r_1, \ldots, r_k}$ of $J^k(X, Y)$ with the property that if $j^k f$ is transverse to $S_{r_1, \ldots, r_k}$, then the intrinsic derivative gives a map from the pre-image to a bundle of homomorphisms. The construction proceeds by induction on $k$: given the subbundle $S_{r_1, \ldots, r_{k-1}}$ of $J^{k-1}(X, Y)$, one defines $S_{r_1, \ldots, r_k}$ as the subset where the intrinsic $(k-1)$-st derivative drops rank by $r_k$. The transversality and algebraic properties ensure that each step produces a well-defined fiber subbundle. The proof is a systematic generalization of the construction for $S_{r,s}$ given in Section 4.
