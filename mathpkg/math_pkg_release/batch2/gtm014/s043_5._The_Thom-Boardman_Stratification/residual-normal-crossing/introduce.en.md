Proposition 5.6 establishes that the generalized normal crossing condition (5.5) is generic: for any finite family of submanifolds $T_j$ in $J^k(X, Y)$ whose target maps are submersions, a residual set of smooth maps $f: X \to Y$ satisfies the normal crossing condition relative to the $T_j$. The proof uses the target map on the product $T_1 \times \cdots \times T_s$ and the parametrized transversality theorem. As an immediate corollary (Theorem 5.2), taking $T_j = S_{I_j}$ yields that the NC condition for Boardman maps holds generically.
