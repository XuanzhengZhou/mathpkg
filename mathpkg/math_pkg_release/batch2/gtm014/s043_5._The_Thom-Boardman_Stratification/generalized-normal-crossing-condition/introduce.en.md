The Generalized Normal Crossing Condition (5.5) reformulates Condition NC in a more flexible setting that does not require the $T_j$ to be the universal Boardman subbundles $S_{I_j}$. Instead, one takes arbitrary submanifolds $T_j$ of $J^k(X, Y)$ whose target projections are submersions, and considers maps $f$ whose $k$-jets are transversal to each $T_j$. The preimages $T_j(f) = (j^k f)^{-1}(T_j)$ are then submanifolds of $X$, and the normal crossing condition (5.5) requires their tangent images under $df$ to be in general position in $T_y Y$ whenever they map to the same point. This generalization is the setting for Proposition 5.6, and Theorem 5.2 follows as the special case $T_j = S_{I_j}$.
