Let $T_1,\ldots,T_s$ be submanifolds of $J^k(X, Y)$ (not necessarily distinct). Suppose each $T_j$ has the property that the target map $T_j \to Y$ is a submersion. By the transversality theorem there is a residual set of maps $f: X \to Y$ such that $j^k f \pitchfork T_j$ for $j = 1,\ldots,s$. For such a map $f$, let $T_j(f) = (j^k f)^{-1}(T_j)$. (The transversality assumption ensures each $T_j(f)$ is a submanifold of $X$.)

Let $x_1,\ldots,x_s$ be distinct points of $X$ with $x_j \in T_j(f)$ and
$$f(x_1) = \cdots = f(x_s) = y.$$
Let $H_j$ be the tangent space to $T_j(f)$ at $x_j$. Then the \textbf{generalized normal crossing condition} (5.5) requires that
$$(df)_{x_1}H_1,\; \ldots,\; (df)_{x_s}H_s$$
are in general position in $T_y Y$.
