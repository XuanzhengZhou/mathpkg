The Thom-Boardman stratification partitions the domain manifold $X$ into a disjoint union of submanifolds according to the singularity type of a Boardman map $f$. For maps with $\dim X \leq \dim Y$, the strata consist of the nonsingular points $X \setminus \bigcup S_i(f)$ (where $f$ is an immersion) and the Boardman sets $S_{r_1, \ldots, r_k}(f)$ with $r_k = 0$ (where $f$ restricted to each stratum is an immersion). This stratification captures the hierarchical structure of singularities and is the foundation for the classification of stable mappings.
