A Boardman map is a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ whose $k$-jet extension $j^k f$ is transversal to every universal Boardman subbundle $S_{r_1,\ldots,r_k}$ in $J^k(X, Y)$, for all $k$. By the Thom transversality theorem, Boardman maps form a residual (dense $G_\delta$) subset of $C^\infty(X, Y)$, so they are "generic" among smooth maps. In particular, every stable map is a Boardman map, though the converse fails both globally (due to normal crossing requirements) and locally (for subtle reasons explored in subsequent sections).
