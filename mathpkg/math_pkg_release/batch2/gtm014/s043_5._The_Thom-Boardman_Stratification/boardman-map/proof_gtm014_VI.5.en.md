A map $f: X \to Y$ is called a Boardman map if its $k$-jet extension $j^k f$ is, for all $k$, transversal to all the $S_{r_1, \ldots, r_k}$ subbundles. By the Thom Transversality Theorem, the set of Boardman maps is a residual subset of $C^\infty(X, Y)$. In particular, every stable map is a Boardman map, since stability implies transversality to all Boardman subbundles. The converse is generally false: being a Boardman map does not guarantee stability, as additional normal crossing conditions on the images of the Boardman strata are required.
