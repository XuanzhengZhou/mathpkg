Higher-order Boardman symbols generalize the $S_r$ and $S_{i,j}$ singularity classifications to arbitrary finite depth. Given a 2-generic map $f: X \to Y$, the symbol $S_{i,j,k}(f)$ records points in the Boardman submanifold $S_{i,j}(f)$ where the restriction of $f$ drops rank by $k$. When each successive restriction yields a submanifold, this inductive process defines $S_{r_1,\ldots,r_k}(f)$ for any non-increasing sequence of nonnegative integers, providing a complete singularity-type tree for generic smooth maps.
