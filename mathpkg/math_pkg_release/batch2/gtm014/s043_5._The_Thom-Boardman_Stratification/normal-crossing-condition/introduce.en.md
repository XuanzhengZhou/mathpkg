Condition NC is the normal crossing requirement that a Boardman map must satisfy in order to be globally stable. Given a Boardman map $f: X \to Y$, consider distinct points $x_1,\ldots,x_s$ lying in (possibly different) Boardman strata $S_{I_j}(f)$ but mapping to the same point $y \in Y$. The condition demands that the images of the tangent spaces of these strata under the differential $df$ are in general position in $T_y Y$. This is analogous to the normal crossing condition for immersions described in Chapter III, and it ensures that the branches of the singular set meeting at $y$ intersect transversally in the target.
