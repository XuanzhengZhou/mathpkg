The embedding $i_a: \mathbb{R}^{n-1} \to \mathbb{R}^n$ defined by $i_a(x) = (a, x)$ identifies $\mathbb{R}^{n-1}$ with the slice $\{a\} \times \mathbb{R}^{n-1}$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$. This map is used in Theorem 1.7 to formulate a Fubini-type criterion: if all slices $i_a^{-1}(A)$ of a compact set $A$ have measure zero, then $A$ itself has measure zero.
