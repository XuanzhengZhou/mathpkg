The volume of an open cube $C(a,b)$ is the product of the lengths of its sides: $\prod_{i=1}^n (b_i - a_i)$. This is the natural $n$-dimensional generalization of the length of an interval. The volume of open cubes is used to define measure zero: a set has measure zero if it can be covered by countably many open cubes whose total volume is arbitrarily small.
