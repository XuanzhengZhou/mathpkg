A minimal covering of a closed interval $I \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ by subintervals $[a_1, b_1], \ldots, [a_m, b_m]$ is one where removing any single subinterval causes the remaining collection to no longer cover $I$. This irredundancy property is used in Lemma 1.8 to bound the total length of such coverings, which in turn plays a role in the proof of Theorem 1.7.
