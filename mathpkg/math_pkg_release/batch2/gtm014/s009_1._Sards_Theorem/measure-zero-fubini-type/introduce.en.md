Theorem 1.7 is a Fubini-type result for measure zero sets. If $A$ is a compact subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$ and for every $a \in \mathbb{R}$ the slice $i_a^{-1}(A)$ has measure zero in $\mathbb{R}^{n-1}$, then $A$ itself has measure zero in $\mathbb{R}^n$. This theorem is the key technical tool in the induction proof of Sard's Theorem (Proposition 1.13/Theorem 1.12), allowing the reduction from dimension $n$ to $n-1$.
