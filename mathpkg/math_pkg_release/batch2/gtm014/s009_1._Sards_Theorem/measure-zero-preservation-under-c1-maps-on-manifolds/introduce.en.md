Corollary 1.4 generalizes Proposition 1.3 to the manifold setting: if $f: X \to Y$ is a $C^1$ map between differentiable $n$-manifolds and $Z \subseteq X$ has measure zero, then $f(Z)$ has measure zero in $Y$. The proof reduces to the Euclidean case via charts, applying Proposition 1.3 to the local representative $\psi \circ f \circ \phi_i^{-1}$.
