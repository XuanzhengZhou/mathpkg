A smooth map $\phi: X \to Y$ induces a pullback homomorphism $\phi^*$ on germs: $[f]_q \mapsto [f \circ \phi]_p$. This is a ring homomorphism, and it is an isomorphism precisely when $\phi$ is a local diffeomorphism. This algebraic characterization of local diffeomorphisms is a key step in applying algebraic methods (like Nakayama's Lemma) to geometric problems.
