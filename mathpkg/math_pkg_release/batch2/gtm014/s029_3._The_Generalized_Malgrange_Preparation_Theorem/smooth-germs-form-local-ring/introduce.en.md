$C_p^\infty(X)$, the ring of germs of smooth functions at a point $p$ on a smooth manifold $X$, is a local ring. The unique maximal ideal $M_p(X)$ consists of all germs of functions that vanish at $p$. This algebraic characterization is the starting point for applying commutative algebra to differential topology, particularly in the study of singularities of smooth maps via the Malgrange Preparation Theorem.
