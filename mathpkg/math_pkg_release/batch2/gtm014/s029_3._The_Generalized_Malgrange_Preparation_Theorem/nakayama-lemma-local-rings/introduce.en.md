Nakayama's Lemma is a fundamental result in commutative algebra: for a finitely generated module $A$ over a local ring, if $A = \mathcal{M}A$ then $A = 0$. The key insight for its proof is that $1 + s$ is invertible for any $s \in \mathcal{M}$. In the context of singularity theory, this lemma allows one to lift generators from the finite-dimensional vector space $A/\mathcal{M}_q(Y)A$ to module generators over $C_q^\infty(Y)$.
