Lemma B handles the first layer of the critical set: points that are critical but at which not all first-order partial derivatives vanish. At such a point, some first partial derivative is nonzero, so the Implicit Function Theorem can be applied to locally represent the map in a simpler form. The proof reduces the dimension by 1 and applies the induction hypothesis, showing that the image of $C - C_1$ under $f$ has measure zero.
