This is the local Euclidean version of Sard's Theorem, from which the global manifold version (Theorem 1.12) follows by a standard chart argument. The proof proceeds by induction on the source dimension $n$. The base case $n = 0$ is trivial ($\mathbf{R}^0$ is a point). The induction step uses an analysis of the critical set $C[f]$ stratified by the order of vanishing partial derivatives, via three lemmas (Lemmas B, C, D), together with Fubini's Theorem and Taylor's Theorem.
