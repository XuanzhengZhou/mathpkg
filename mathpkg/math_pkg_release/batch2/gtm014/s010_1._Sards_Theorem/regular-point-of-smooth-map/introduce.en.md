A regular point of a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ between smooth manifolds is a point where the differential is surjective. By the Implicit Function Theorem, a regular point has a neighborhood on which $f$ is locally a submersion — i.e., it looks like a projection map. Points that are not regular are called critical points, and their images are the critical values whose measure is controlled by Sard's Theorem.
