A regular value of a smooth map is any point in the target manifold that is not a critical value. Importantly, points not even in the image of $f$ count as regular values by definition. Sard's Theorem guarantees that almost every point in $Y$ is a regular value of $f$, which is the foundation for transversality theory and the Preimage Theorem.
