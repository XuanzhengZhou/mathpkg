A critical value of a smooth map between smooth manifolds is the image of a critical point. In other words, $q \in Y$ is a critical value of $f: X \to Y$ precisely when there exists some $p \in X$ with $f(p) = q$ such that the differential $df_p$ fails to be surjective. Sard's Theorem states that the set of critical values is always small — it has measure zero in the target manifold $Y$.
