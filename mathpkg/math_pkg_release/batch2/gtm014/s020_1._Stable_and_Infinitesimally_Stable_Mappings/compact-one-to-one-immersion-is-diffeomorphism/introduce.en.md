For a compact manifold, every injective immersion into itself is automatically a diffeomorphism. The proof uses the fact that on a compact manifold, a one-to-one immersion is also a submersion (by dimensional considerations), so it restricts to a diffeomorphism on each connected component. This lemma is a key step in establishing that the diffeomorphism group of a compact manifold is open in the space of all smooth self-maps.
