Let $V_1$ and $V_2$ be Frechet spaces and $U$ an open subset of $V_1$. Let $f: U \to V_2$ be continuous and $p \in U$. Then $f$ is differentiable at $p$ iff there exists a continuous linear mapping $A_p: V_1 \to V_2$ such that
$$\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{|f(p+h) - f(p) - A_p(h)|}{|h|} = 0.$$
The map $A_p$ is called the Frechet derivative of $f$ at $p$.
