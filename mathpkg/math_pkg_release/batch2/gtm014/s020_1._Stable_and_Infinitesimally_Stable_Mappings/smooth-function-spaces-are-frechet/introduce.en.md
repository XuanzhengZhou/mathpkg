When $X$ is a compact manifold, the space of smooth real-valued functions on $X$ forms a Frechet space under the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology. This generalizes to smooth sections of any vector bundle over $X$ and, crucially, to the space of vector fields along a fixed mapping $f$. These examples form the foundation for the proof that the entire mapping space $C^\infty(X, Y)$ is a Frechet manifold, which is the geometric setting for Mather's theory of stability.
