The tangent space to a point on a Frechet manifold is defined by the same equivalence-class-of-curves construction used for finite-dimensional manifolds. Since the chain rule remains valid in Frechet spaces, tangency of curves is a well-defined equivalence relation independent of the choice of chart. This construction is crucial because the mapping space $C^\infty(X, Y)$ carries the structure of a Frechet manifold when $X$ is compact, allowing us to differentiate the action of diffeomorphism groups and study infinitesimal stability.
