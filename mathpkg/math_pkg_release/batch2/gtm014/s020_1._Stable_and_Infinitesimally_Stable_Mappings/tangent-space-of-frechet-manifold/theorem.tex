Let $X$ be a Frechet manifold and let $p \in X$. Let $S_p(X)$ be the set of smooth curves $c: \mathbf{R} \to X$ such that $c(0) = p$.

Two curves $c_1, c_2 \in S_p(X)$ are \text{tangent at $p$} if for every chart $h_\alpha$ around $p$,

$$
(d(h_\alpha \circ c_1))_0 = (d(h_\alpha \circ c_2))_0.
$$

The \text{tangent space} $T_p X$ is the set of equivalence classes of $S_p(X)$ under the equivalence relation "is tangent at $p$." As in the finite-dimensional case, $T_p X$ is a vector space, and a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ between Frechet manifolds induces a well-defined linear mapping $(df)_p: T_p X \to T_{f(p)} Y$.
