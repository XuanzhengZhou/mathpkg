Let $X$ and $Y$ be smooth manifolds, and let $G = \operatorname{Diff}(X) \times \operatorname{Diff}(Y)$ act on $C^\infty(X, Y)$ by the natural action

$$
(g, h) \cdot f = h \circ f \circ g^{-1}, \qquad (g, h) \in \operatorname{Diff}(X) \times \operatorname{Diff}(Y).
$$

Then a mapping $f \in C^\infty(X, Y)$ is stable if and only if its orbit $G \cdot f$ is open in $C^\infty(X, Y)$ (with respect to the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology).
