Infinitesimal stability is the linearized version of stability, obtained by differentiating the action of the diffeomorphism groups at the identity. A mapping is infinitesimally stable if every infinitesimal deformation (represented by a vector field along the mapping) can be realized by infinitesimal diffeomorphisms of the domain and codomain. Mather's theorem establishes that, under suitable compactness conditions, infinitesimal stability is equivalent to stability, providing a computable criterion via the tangent map.
