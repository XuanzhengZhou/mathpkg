When the domain $X$ is compact, the space of all smooth mappings from $X$ to $Y$ carries the structure of a Frechet manifold. The local charts are constructed using tubular neighborhoods of graphs: near a given mapping $f$, other mappings are identified with sections of the normal bundle to the graph of $f$ in $X \times Y$, which is a Frechet space by Proposition 1.9. This theorem provides the geometric foundation for applying differential calculus to the diffeomorphism group action and defining infinitesimal stability.
