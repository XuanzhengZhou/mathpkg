Let $X$ and $Y$ be smooth manifolds, and let $f \in C^\infty(X, Y)$. Then $f$ is \text{stable} if there exists a neighborhood $W_f$ of $f$ in $C^\infty(X, Y)$ (with respect to the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology) such that every $f' \in W_f$ is equivalent to $f$.

In other words, $f$ is stable if every nearby mapping $f'$ is identical to $f$ after suitable changes of coordinates in both the domain and the range.
