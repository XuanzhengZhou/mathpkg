Two smooth mappings between manifolds are equivalent if one can be transformed into the other by applying diffeomorphisms to both the domain and the codomain. This relation captures the idea that the mappings have the same qualitative behavior up to smooth changes of coordinates. Equivalence of mappings is the fundamental notion that underlies the definition of stability in singularity theory.
