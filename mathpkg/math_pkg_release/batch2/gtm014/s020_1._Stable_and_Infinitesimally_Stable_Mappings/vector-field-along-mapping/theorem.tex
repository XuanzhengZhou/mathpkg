Let $X$ and $Y$ be smooth manifolds, and let $f: X \to Y$ be a smooth mapping. Let $\pi_Y: TY \to Y$ be the canonical projection of the tangent bundle. A smooth map $w: X \to TY$ is called a \text{vector field along $f$} if the diagram

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
TY & \xleftarrow{w} & X \\
{\scriptstyle \pi_Y}\downarrow & & \downarrow{\scriptstyle f} \\
Y & = & Y
\end{array}
$$

commutes, i.e., $\pi_Y \circ w = f$.

The set of all vector fields along $f$ is denoted by $C_f^\infty(X, TY)$.
