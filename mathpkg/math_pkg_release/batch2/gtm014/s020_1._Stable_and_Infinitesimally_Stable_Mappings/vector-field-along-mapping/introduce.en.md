A vector field along a smooth mapping $f: X \to Y$ is a smooth lift of $f$ to the tangent bundle $TY$. Unlike an ordinary vector field on $Y$, which assigns a tangent vector to each point of $Y$, a vector field along $f$ assigns to each point $x \in X$ a tangent vector at $f(x) \in Y$. This concept generalizes the notion of a variation of a mapping and is fundamental for defining infinitesimal stability.
