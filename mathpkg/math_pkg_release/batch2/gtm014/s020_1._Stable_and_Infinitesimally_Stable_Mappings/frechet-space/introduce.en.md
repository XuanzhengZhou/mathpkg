A Frechet space is a complete topological vector space whose topology is induced by a translation-invariant metric satisfying the usual properties of a norm except homogeneity. Frechet spaces generalize Banach spaces and provide the natural analytic setting for spaces of smooth functions. The space $C^\infty(X, \mathbf{R})$ of smooth functions on a compact manifold is a fundamental example of a Frechet space that is not a Banach space.
