The Malgrange Preparation Theorem is the smooth ($C^\infty$) analogue of the Weierstrass Preparation Theorem. It states that any smooth function $F(t, x)$ which, when restricted to $x = 0$, vanishes to order $k$ in $t$ with non-degenerate leading coefficient, can be expressed locally as the product of an invertible smooth factor and a monic polynomial in $t$ of degree $k$ whose coefficients are smooth functions of $x$. This theorem follows from the Mather Division Theorem and is fundamental to the local study of smooth mappings and singularities.
