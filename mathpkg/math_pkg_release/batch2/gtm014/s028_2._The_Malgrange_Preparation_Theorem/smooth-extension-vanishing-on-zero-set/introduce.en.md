Proposition 2.4 is the central technical result underpinning the proof of the Polynomial Division Theorem. It constructs a smooth extension of $G$ from $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$ into the complex domain $\mathbf{C} \times \mathbf{R}^n \times \mathbf{C}^k$ whose $\bar{\partial}$-derivative vanishes to infinite order on the zero set of $P_k$. This ensures that the area integral in the generalized Cauchy formula (Lemma 2.3) is well-defined (no singularities from the zeroes of $P_k$ in the denominator) and yields smooth functions. The proof proceeds by induction on $k$, using Lemma 2.7 to solve a $\bar{\partial}$-equation along the zero set.
