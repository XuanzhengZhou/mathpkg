Let $G(t, x)$ be a complex-valued function defined and smooth on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$. Then there exist smooth, complex-valued functions $q(t, x, \lambda)$ and $r(t, x, \lambda)$ defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n \times \mathbf{R}^k$ satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G(t, x) = q(t, x, \lambda) P_k(t, \lambda) + r(t, x, \lambda)$, and
\item $r(t, x, \lambda) = t^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} r_i(x, \lambda) t^i$
\end{enumerate}
where each $r_i$ is a smooth function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R}^n \times \mathbf{R}^k$, and
$$P_k(t, \lambda) = t^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \lambda_i t^i.$$
Moreover, if $G$ is real-valued then $q$ and $r$ may be chosen to be real-valued.
