A 2-generic mapping is one whose second jet extension is transverse to all the universal $S_{r,s}$ singularity sets. This is the second-order analogue of 1-genericity (transversality to the $S_r$ sets). The Transversality Theorem implies that 2-generic mappings form a residual set in the $C^\infty$ topology, meaning they are "generic" in the sense of Baire category. For 2-generic maps, the Thom-Boardman stratification $S_{r,s}(f)$ is well-behaved and each stratum is a smooth submanifold of the source manifold.
