Proposition 4.3 establishes that the set $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)_s$, consisting of those symmetric bilinear maps whose associated linear map $V \to \text{Hom}(V, W)$ has rank at most $s$, is a smooth submanifold of the vector space $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)$. The dimension is computed by identifying it with $\text{Hom}(Q \circ Q, W)_0$ where $Q$ is a quotient of dimension $k-s$, giving a formula involving the binomial coefficient for symmetric tensors plus the Grassmannian dimension. This is the vector-space analogue of the $S_r$ classification from Chapter III, adapted to symmetric second derivatives.
