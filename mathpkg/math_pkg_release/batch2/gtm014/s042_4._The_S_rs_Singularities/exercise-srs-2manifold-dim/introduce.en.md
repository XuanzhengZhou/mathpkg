This exercise applies the dimension formula for $S_{r,s}(f)$ to the special case where both source and target are 2-dimensional manifolds. For a 2-generic map $f: X^2 \to Y^2$, the formula yields that the singular set $S_1(f)$ (where $df$ has rank 1) is a 1-dimensional submanifold, and the second-order singularities $S_{1,1}(f)$ (where the intrinsic second derivative also has rank 1) form a discrete set of points (dimension 0).
