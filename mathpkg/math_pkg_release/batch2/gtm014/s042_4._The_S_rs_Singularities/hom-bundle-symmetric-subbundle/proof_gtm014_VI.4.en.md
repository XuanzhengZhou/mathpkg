This follows directly from Proposition 4.3 (the vector space case) by applying it fiberwise. For each $p \in X$, the fiber $\text{Hom}(E_p \circ E_p, F_p)_s$ is a submanifold of $\text{Hom}(E_p \circ E_p, F_p)$ of the stated codimension. Since the construction is natural with respect to bundle transition functions, the fiberwise submanifolds assemble into a fiber subbundle of $\text{Hom}(E \circ E, F)$. The codimension is constant across fibers and given by the same formula (4.4) as in the vector space case.
