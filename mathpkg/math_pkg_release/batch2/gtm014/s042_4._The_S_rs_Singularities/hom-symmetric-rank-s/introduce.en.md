The space $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)_s$ consists of those linear maps from the symmetric tensor product $V \circ V$ to $W$ whose image under the natural composition (4.2) lands in $L^s(V, \text{Hom}(V, W))$, the set of linear maps of rank at most $s$. This definition is central to constructing the universal $S_{r,s}$ singularities: it generalizes the $S_r$ classification to the second derivative by capturing the rank of the associated map from $V$ to $\text{Hom}(V, W)$. The space is not simply $L^s(V \circ V, W)$; the rank condition is imposed after passing through the isomorphism $\text{Hom}(V \otimes V, W) \cong \text{Hom}(V, \text{Hom}(V, W))$.
