The set $S_{r,s}(f)$ is defined via the $2$-jet extension of $f$. The construction uses the universal bundle $\operatorname{Hom}(E \circ E, F)_s$ associated to the vector bundles $E = \ker(df)$ and $F = \operatorname{coker}(df)$. Since $\operatorname{Hom}(E \circ E, F)_s$ is a fiber subbundle of $\operatorname{Hom}(E \circ E, F)$ with codimension given by Proposition 4.3, the transversality of $j^2 f$ to this subbundle ensures that $S_{r,s}(f)$ is a submanifold of $X$ whose codimension equals that of $\operatorname{Hom}(E \circ E, F)_s$ in $\operatorname{Hom}(E \circ E, F)$. The dimension formula follows directly from the codimension computation of Proposition 4.3.
