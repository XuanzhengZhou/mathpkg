Proposition 4.6 is a technical lemma needed to prove that $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)_s$ is a submanifold of $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)$. It defines $M_s$ as the union of all higher-rank strata (those with $t > s$) and constructs a map $\pi^*$ from the zero-rank symmetric homomorphisms on a suitable quotient $Q$ into the complement of $M_s$. The map is shown to be a one-to-one proper immersion, which provides the local model needed to verify the submanifold property. The proof is essentially the same as that of Proposition 1.2 (Chapter III, Section 1).
