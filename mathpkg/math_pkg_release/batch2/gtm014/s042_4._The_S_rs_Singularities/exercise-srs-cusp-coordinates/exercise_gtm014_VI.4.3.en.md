Let $X$ and $Y$ be two manifolds and let $f: X \to Y$ be one-generic. Let $x \in X$ be a cusp point. Show that if $x$ is not a simple cusp there exist coordinates $(x_1, x_2)$ centered at $x$, coordinates $(y_1, y_2)$ centered at $y = f(x)$, and a mapping of the form

$$(4.10) \qquad (x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_1, x_1 \alpha(x_1, x_2))$$

with the same 3-jet at $0$ as $f$.

*Hint:* Use the coordinates in the proof of Theorem 2.2. In these coordinates $f$ is of the form $(x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_1, h(x_1, x_2))$; and at a cusp point which is not simple,

$$h = \frac{\partial h}{\partial x_2} = \frac{\partial^2 h}{\partial x_2^2} = 0.$$

Hence $h(x_1, x_2) = x_2^2 \gamma(x_1, x_2)$ for some smooth function $\gamma$.
