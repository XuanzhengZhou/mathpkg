This corollary refines the Thom Transversality Theorem to allow localized perturbations. Given a submanifold $W$ of a jet bundle whose source projection lies in an open set $U \subset X$, any smooth map $f$ can be perturbed slightly (within any given $C^\infty$ neighborhood) to a map $g$ that is transversal to $W$, while keeping $g$ identical to $f$ outside $U$. This follows directly from the local polynomial perturbation construction used in the proof of Theorem 4.9, where the perturbation domain $U_r$ can be chosen to lie inside $U$.
