This corollary formalizes the basic principle of transversality: if a parametrized family of mappings, viewed as a single map via its $k$-jet extension, intersects a submanifold $W$ of the jet bundle transversely, then for a dense set of parameters the individual mappings also intersect $W$ transversely. The proof applies Lemma 4.6 to the natural map $j: B \to C^\infty(X, J^k(X, Y))$ sending each parameter to the $k$-jet of the corresponding mapping.
