The Multijet Transversality Theorem generalizes the Thom Transversality Theorem from single-jet to multijet bundles. The $s$-fold $k$-jet bundle $J_s^k(X, Y)$ is defined as the set of $s$-tuples of $k$-jets at pairwise distinct source points, and it is an open subset of $J^k(X, Y)^s$. This theorem is crucial for studying the injectivity and self-intersection properties of smooth mappings, as transversality of the $s$-fold $k$-jet extension $j_s^k f$ to a submanifold $W \subset J_s^k(X, Y)$ encodes conditions like immersions with normal crossings. The proof adapts the local polynomial perturbation argument from Theorem 4.9, requiring disjoint coordinate neighborhoods for the $s$ source points.
