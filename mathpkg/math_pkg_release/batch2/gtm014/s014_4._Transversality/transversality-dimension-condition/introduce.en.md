This proposition captures the simplest consequence of transversality: when the sum of the dimensions of $X$ and $W$ is strictly less than the dimension of the ambient manifold $Y$, transversality forces the image of $X$ to be disjoint from $W$. The proof is a direct dimension-counting argument: if $f(x) \in W$, then the sum of tangent spaces cannot span all of $T_{f(x)}Y$, contradicting transversality.
