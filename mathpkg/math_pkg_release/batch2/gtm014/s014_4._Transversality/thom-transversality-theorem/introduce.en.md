The Thom Transversality Theorem is the fundamental result that justifies the use of transversality in differential topology. It states that for any submanifold $W$ of a jet bundle $J^k(X, Y)$, the set of smooth maps whose $k$-jet extension is transversal to $W$ forms a residual (dense $G_\delta$) subset of $C^\infty(X, Y)$ in the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology. When $W$ is closed, this set is also open. The proof decomposes $W$ into a countable union of compact pieces, shows transversality to each is open and dense via polynomial perturbations, then applies the Baire category theorem. This theorem implies that "generic" smooth maps satisfy any finite-order transversality condition with respect to closed submanifolds of jet space.
