Let $\Gamma$ be a pseudogroup on $\mathbf{R}^n$ and let $X$ be a Hausdorff topological space which satisfies the second axiom of countability. Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a subset of all local homeomorphisms of $X$ into $\mathbf{R}^n$ (i.e., homeomorphisms which are defined on an open subset of $X$ and whose range is an open subset of $\mathbf{R}^n$). Then $\mathcal{A}$ is a $\Gamma$-atlas on $X$ if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\displaystyle X = \bigcup_{\phi \in \mathcal{A}} \operatorname{dom} \phi$,
\item[(b)] for every pair $\phi, \psi \in \mathcal{A}$, the transition map $\psi \circ \phi^{-1}$ belongs to $\Gamma$.
\end{enumerate}
