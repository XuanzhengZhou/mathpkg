Real projective $n$-space $\mathbf{P}^n$ is the set of all lines through the origin in $\mathbf{R}^{n+1}$. It is defined by quotienting $\mathbf{R}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}$ by the equivalence relation that identifies two non-zero vectors if they are scalar multiples of one another.

The resulting space is a compact $n$-dimensional real analytic manifold. As a special case of the Grassmannian construction, $\mathbf{P}^n = G_{1,n+1}$ is the Grassmannian of $1$-dimensional subspaces (lines) in $\mathbf{R}^{n+1}$.
