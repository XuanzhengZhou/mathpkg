The Weierstrass Preparation Theorem asserts that a holomorphic function $F(w, z)$ whose restriction to $z=0$ vanishes to order $k$ at $w=0$ can be factored as $q^{-1} \cdot (w^k + \sum \lambda_i(z) w^i)$ with $q(0) \neq 0$. Thus the zero set of $F$ is locally a $k$-sheeted branched cover over the $z$-hyperplane. This is the complex-analytic foundation for the smooth Malgrange Preparation Theorem.
