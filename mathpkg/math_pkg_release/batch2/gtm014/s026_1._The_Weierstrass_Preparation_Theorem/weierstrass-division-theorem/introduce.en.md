The Weierstrass Division Theorem provides division with remainder by a Weierstrass polynomial: any holomorphic $G$ can be written as $G = qF + r$ where the remainder $r$ is a polynomial in $w$ of degree $< k$ whose coefficients are holomorphic in $z$. Uniqueness follows from counting zeroes via complex analysis. This theorem implies the Weierstrass Preparation Theorem.
