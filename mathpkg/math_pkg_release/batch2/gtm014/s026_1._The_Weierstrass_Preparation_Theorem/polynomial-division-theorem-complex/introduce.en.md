The Polynomial Division Theorem extends division to the universal family $P_k(w, \lambda) = w^k + \sum \lambda_i w^i$ parameterized by its coefficients $\lambda \in \mathbf{C}^k$. Any holomorphic $G$ can be divided by $P_k$ with remainder a polynomial in $w$ of degree $< k$. The proof uses the Cauchy Integral Formula. This is the crucial step for proving both the complex Weierstrass and smooth Malgrange Preparation Theorems.
