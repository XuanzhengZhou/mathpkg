Over a field of characteristic $p > 0$, a $k$-torus $T$ (a set of pairwise commuting semisimple linear transformations closed under addition, scalar multiplication, and $p$-powers) is diagonalizable precisely when it equals the $k$-linear span of its subset $T_\pi = \{t \in T \mid t^p = t\}$ of elements satisfying the separable polynomial $X^p - X = 0$. The elements of $T_\pi$ have all eigenvalues in the prime field $\pi$, and the weight space decomposition $V = \bigoplus_{\alpha \in T_\pi^*} V_\alpha(T_\pi)$ provides the diagonalization.
