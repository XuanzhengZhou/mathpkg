Let $k$ be an infinite field, $K$ and $L$ extension fields of $k$, $L'$ an extension of $L$, and $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_n$ distinct $k$-homomorphisms from $K$ to $L$. Then for $f(X_1, \ldots, X_n) \in L'[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$, the function $f(\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_n)$ is identically zero on $K$ if and only if the polynomial $f(X_1, \ldots, X_n)$ is zero.
