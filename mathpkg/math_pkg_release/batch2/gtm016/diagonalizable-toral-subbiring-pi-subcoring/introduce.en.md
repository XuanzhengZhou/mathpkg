Theorem 6.2.11 relates a diagonalizable toral \(k\)-subbiring (or \(k\)-subcoring) \(T\) of the Hopf algebra \(H(K/k)\) to its image under a prime \(p > 1\). The \(\pi\)-subcoring (or \(\pi\)-subbiring) \(T_\pi\) lives in \(H(K)\) and the Hopf algebra of the fixed field \(H(K/K^T)\) can be described in two equivalent ways: as the subalgebra \(K\langle T \rangle = K\langle T_\pi \rangle\) generated over \(K\) by \(T\) (or \(T_\pi\)), and as the product \(KT = KT_\pi\). This connects the purely inseparable descent theory to the Hopf-algebraic structures arising from Galois splittings.
