Observe that $x(abc) = x(a(bc)) = \sum_i x(a) \, {}_r(x_i)(b) \, (x_i)_r(c)$ and $x(a) = x(1a) = \sum_i x(1)x_i(a) = \sum_i \varepsilon({}_i x)x_i(a)$ for $a, b, c \in K$. The other two identities are similarly established using $x(abc) = x((ab)c)$ and $x(a) = x(a1)$.
