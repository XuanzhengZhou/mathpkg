Let $L/k$ be a splitting field of $t$ and let the eigenvalues of $t_L$ on $V_L$ be $a_1, \ldots, a_n$. Choose $e$ such that $(t_L)^{p^e}$ is diagonalizable on $V_L$ (see E.6.1) and such that $a_1^{p^e}, \ldots, a_n^{p^e}$ are separable over $k$ (see 2.2.4). Letting $L' = k(a_1^{p^e}, \ldots, a_n^{p^e})$, the splitting field of $t^{p^e}$ is the Galois extension $L'/k$ and $(t^{p^e})_{L'}$ is diagonalizable on $V_{L'}$. Thus, $t^{p^e}$ is semisimple. $\square$
