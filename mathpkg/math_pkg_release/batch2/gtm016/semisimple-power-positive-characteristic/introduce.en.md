In positive characteristic $p > 1$, not every linear transformation is semisimple, but this proposition guarantees that some sufficiently high $p^e$-th power of any linear transformation $t$ becomes semisimple. The proof constructs $e$ by examining the eigenvalues of $t_L$ on the splitting field $L/k$ and ensuring both diagonalizability of $(t_L)^{p^e}$ and separability of the $p^e$-th powers of the eigenvalues. This result is fundamental for the study of tori and radical extensions in later sections.
