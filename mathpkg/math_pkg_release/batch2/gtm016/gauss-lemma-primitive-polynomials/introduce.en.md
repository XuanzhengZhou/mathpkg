Gauss's Lemma states that the product of two primitive polynomials (polynomials whose coefficients have no common non-unit divisor) is again primitive. This is a key result used to prove that if $k[x]$ is a UFD, then so is $k[x][X]$, and that irreducible polynomials over a UFD remain irreducible over its field of fractions.
