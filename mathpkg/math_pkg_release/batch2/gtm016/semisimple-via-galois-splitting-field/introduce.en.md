This proposition characterizes when a linear transformation $t$ of a vector space $V$ over $k$ is semisimple. The condition is given in terms of the splitting field $L/k$ of $t$: $t$ is semisimple precisely when $L/k$ is a Galois extension and the base-changed transformation $t_L$ acts diagonalizably on $V_L = V \otimes_k L$. This connects the internal property of semisimplicity to the Galois-theoretic nature of the splitting field and the diagonalizability of the extended transformation.
