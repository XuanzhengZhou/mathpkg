A nonconstant polynomial $f(X) \in K[X]$ can be factored into $f(X) = \prod_{i=1}^m g_i(X)$ where the $g_i(X)$ are monic irreducible elements of $K[X]$. Moreover, for any other such factorization $f(X) = \prod_{i=1}^n h_i(X)$ with $h_i(X) \in K[X]$ irreducible, the factors can be rearranged so that $g_1(X) = h_{1'}(X), \ldots, g_m(X) = h_{n'}(X)$ (in particular, $m = n$).
