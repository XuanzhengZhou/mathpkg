Existence: By a Zorn's Lemma argument, there exists a maximal algebraically independent subset $S$ of $K$ over $k$. Then $K/k(S)$ is algebraic, so $S$ is a transcendency basis. Invariance: For infinite bases, each $s \in S$ is algebraic over $k(\bigcup_{s \in S} T_s)$ for finite $T_s \subset T$, and by cardinal arithmetic the bases have equal cardinality. For finite bases, a Steinitz exchange argument applies.
