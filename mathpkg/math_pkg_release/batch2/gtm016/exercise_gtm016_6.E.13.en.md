Let $K/k$ be a finite dimensional field extension and suppose that $k$ is the subfield of constants of the set of higher derivations of $K$ over $k$, that is, for each $a \in K - k$, there exists a higher derivation $D = (D_0, D_1, \ldots, D_m)$ such that $D_i(a) \neq 0$ for some $i$ with $1 \leq i \leq m$. Show that $K/k$ is a radical extension. (Hint: Show for a fixed higher derivation $D = (D_0, D_1, \ldots, D_m)$ that $K$ is a radical extension of the field $K^D = \{c \in K \mid D_i(c) = 0 \text{ for } 1 \leq i \leq m\}$ by showing that the $K$-span $C$ of $\{D_0, D_1, \ldots, D_m\}$ is a colocal subcoalgebra of $H(K/k)$.)
