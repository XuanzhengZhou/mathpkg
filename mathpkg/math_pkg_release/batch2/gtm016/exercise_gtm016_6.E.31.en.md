Let $K/k$ be a radical extension. Let $C$ be a finite dimensional $K$-subspace of $\operatorname{End}_k K$ such that $[x, y] \in C$ and $x^p \in C$ for all $x, y \in C$. Letting $L$ be a separable extension of $k$, $\bar{K} = L \otimes_k K$, $\bar{k} = L \otimes_k k$, $\bar{C} = L \otimes_k C$, show that $\bar{C}$ is a finite dimensional $\bar{K}$-subspace of $\operatorname{End}_{\bar{k}} \bar{K}$ such that $[x, y] \in \bar{C}$ and $x^p \in \bar{C}$ for all $x, y \in \bar{C}$. (Hint: After showing that the $\bar{k}$-span $[\bar{C}, \bar{C}]$ is a subset of $\bar{C}$, show that if $x, y \in \bar{C}$ and $x^p, y^p \in \bar{C}$, then $(x + y)^p \in \bar{C}$ because $(x + y)^p \equiv x^p + y^p \pmod{[\bar{C}, \bar{C}]}$.)
