Each nonzero $f(X) \in k[X]$ can be uniquely written as $\sum_{i=0}^n a_i X^i$ with $a_i \in k$. Define $e(f(X)) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i x^i \in R$. One verifies directly that $e$ respects addition and multiplication using the distributive, associative, and commutative properties of $R$. Uniqueness: any homomorphism satisfying $e(a)=a$ for $a \in k$ and $e(X)=x$ must send $\sum a_i X^i$ to $\sum a_i x^i$ by the homomorphism properties.
