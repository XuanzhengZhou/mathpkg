Let $T$ be a $k$-torus on $K$ containing $I$ such that $\varepsilon(T) \subset k$ and for each $t \in T$, there exist ${}_i t, t_i \in T$ such that $\Delta t = \sum_i {}_i t \otimes_K t_i$. Then $\bar{T}$ is a toral $k$-subcoring of $H(\bar{K}/k)$. Since $\bar{K}/k$ has a separable subfield $L$ such that $\bar{K}/L$ is radical and $L/k$ is separable, the $G$-stability condition ensures that $\bar{T}$ is diagonalizable. The mapping $T \mapsto \bar{T}$ sends toral $k$-subcorings of $H(K/k)$ to $G$-stable diagonalizable toral $k$-subcorings of $H(\bar{K}/k)$. Conversely, if $\bar{T}$ is a $G$-stable toral $k$-subcoring of $H(\bar{K}/k)$, then the fixed subring $\bar{T}^G$ is a toral $k$-subcoring of $H(K/k)$. The bijectivity follows from the Galois correspondence: $(\bar{T}^G)^- = \bar{T}$ and $(\bar{T})^G = T$ when $T = \bar{T}^G$, which holds because $G$ acts faithfully on $\bar{K}$ and the fixed field of $G$ is exactly $K$. The coradical property follows from the condition involving the coproduct $\Delta$.
