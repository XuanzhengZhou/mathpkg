Let $T$ be a $k$-torus on $K$ containing $I$ such that $\varepsilon(T) \subset k$ and for each $t \in T$, there exist ${}_i t, t_i \in T$ such that
$$\Delta t = \sum_i {}_i t \otimes_K t_i.$$
Thus, $\bar{T}$ is a toral $\bar{k}$-subcoring of $H(\bar{K}/k)$ which is $G$-stable and diagonalizable. The proof proceeds by the standard ascent-descent argument: properties of $\bar{T}$ are studied in the diagonalizable setting over $\bar{K}$, and then the Galois descent $\bar{T} \mapsto \bar{T}^G = T$ recovers the original subcoring.
