$f(X)$ has a multiple root iff $f(X)$ and $f'(X)$ have a common root, i.e., iff they are not relatively prime. Since $f(X)$ is irreducible, $f'(X) = 0$ is equivalent to $f(X)$ and $f'(X)$ having a common factor. But $f'(X) = 0$ iff every exponent is divisible by $p$, iff all roots are multiple. Thus separability is equivalent to $f'(X) \neq 0$.
