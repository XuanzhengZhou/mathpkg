Since $H \subseteq H(K/k)$ is coclosed, the comultiplication $\Delta$ on $H(K/k)$ restricts to $\Delta_H: H \to H \otimes_K H$. Properties (1) (coassociativity), (2) (coidentity), and (3) (preservation of products by $\Delta$) of Proposition 5.3.8 hold for the restrictions because they are identities that hold in $H(K/k)$.

If $H$ is a $K$-subbiring, then $H$ is closed under multiplication, and property (4) of Proposition 5.3.8---that $\varepsilon(xy) = \varepsilon(x)\varepsilon(y)$ for $x, y \in H$---follows from multiplicativity of $\varepsilon$ on $H(K/k)$ restricted to $H$. Thus $(H, \Delta_H, \varepsilon_H)$ satisfies all bialgebra axioms (1)-(4) of Appendix B.1, with ground field $K_H$.
