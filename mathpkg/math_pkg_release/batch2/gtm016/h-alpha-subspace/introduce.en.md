Given two $k$-subspaces $H$ and $T$ of the Hopf algebra $H(K/k)$ associated to a field extension, Winter defines the generalized weight space $H_\alpha(T)$ for each character $\alpha \in T^*$. An element $x \in H$ belongs to $H_\alpha(T)$ precisely when the commutator $tx - xt$ acts as scalar multiplication by $\alpha(t)$ for all $t \in T$. This construction generalizes the usual weight space decomposition from Lie theory to the Hopf-algebraic setting and plays a role in the structure theory of toral subcorings.
