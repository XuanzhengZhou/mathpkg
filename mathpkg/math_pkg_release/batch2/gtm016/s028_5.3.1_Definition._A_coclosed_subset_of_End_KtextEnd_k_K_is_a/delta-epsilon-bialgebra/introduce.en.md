This proposition states that $\Delta$ and $\varepsilon$ endow $H(K/k)$ with the structure of a $K$-bialgebra. Properties: (1) coassociativity, (2) $\varepsilon$ as coidentity, (3) $\Delta$ as algebra homomorphism, (4) multiplicativity of $\varepsilon$. Together these make $(H(K/k), \Delta, \varepsilon)$ a $K$-bialgebra.
