$K$-linearity follows from the definitions: $\Delta(x)$ via $x(ab)=\sum_i x(a)x_i(b)$, and $\varepsilon(x)=x(1)$. (1) Coassociativity follows from the two expansions of $x(abc)$ in Proposition 5.3.7. (2) From $x(a)=x(1a)=\sum_i x(1)x_i(a)$ and $x(a)=x(a1)$. (3) $\Delta(I)=I\otimes I$ since $I(ab)=ab=I(a)I(b)$; multiplicativity from the product formula in Proposition 5.3.3. (4) $\varepsilon(I)=1_K$, and $\varepsilon(xy)=(xy)(1)=x(y(1))=x(\varepsilon(y)\cdot 1)=\varepsilon(x)\varepsilon(y)$.
