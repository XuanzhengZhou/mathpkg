Let $A = \operatorname{Hom}_K(\operatorname{End}_k K, K)$ be the $K$-dual space of $\operatorname{End}_k K$. For $a \in K$, let $\hat{a}$ be the element of $A$ defined by $\hat{a}(x) = x(a)$ for $x \in \operatorname{End}_k K$. Letting $e_1,\ldots,e_n$ be a $k$-basis for $K$ and choosing $x_1,\ldots,x_n \in \operatorname{End}_k K$ such that $x_i(e_j) = \delta_{ij}$ for $1 \leq i,j \leq n$, we have a $K$-basis $x_1,\ldots,x_n$ for $\operatorname{End}_k K$ and a dual $K$-basis $\hat{e}_1,\ldots,\hat{e}_n$ for $A$. The result follows from the fact that $\operatorname{End}_k K$ is precisely the $K$-span of all finite products of such endomorphisms, which coincides with $H(K/k)$ by definition.
