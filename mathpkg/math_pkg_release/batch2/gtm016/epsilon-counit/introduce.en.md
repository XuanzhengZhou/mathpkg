The counit $\varepsilon: H(K/k) \to K$ evaluates a higher derivation at $1$: $\varepsilon(x) = x(1)$. Together with the comultiplication $\Delta$, the counit gives $H(K/k)$ the structure of a $K$-coalgebra, and when restricted to a $K$-subbiring, a $K$-bialgebra.
