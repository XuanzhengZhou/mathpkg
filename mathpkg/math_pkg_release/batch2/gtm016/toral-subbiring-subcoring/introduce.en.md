A toral $k$-subbiring (respectively $k$-subcoring) of the Hopf algebra $H(K/k)$ is simultaneously a $k$-torus on $K$ (a set of pairwise commuting semisimple endomorphisms closed under linear combinations and $p$-powers) and a subbiring (respectively subcoring) of the biring structure of $H(K/k)$. For a diagonalizable $k$-torus $T$ on $K/k$ containing $I$ to be a toral $k$-subcoring, it is necessary and sufficient that $\varepsilon(T) \subset k$ and for each $t \in T$, there exist ${}_i t, t_i \in T$ such that $\Delta t = \sum_i {}_i t \otimes_K t_i$.
