Since $f(X), g(X) \in I$, let $d(X)$ be a greatest common divisor of $f(X)$ and $g(X)$. Since $f(X)$ is irreducible, $d(X)$ is either a unit $c$ or an associate of $f(X)$. If $d(X)$ is an associate of $f(X)$, then $f(X)$ divides $g(X)$. If $d(X) = c$ is a unit, then $c = f(X)a(X) + g(X)b(X)$ for some $a(X), b(X) \in K[X]$. Multiplying by $h(X)$ gives $ch(X) = f(X)a(X)h(X) + g(X)h(X)b(X)$. Since $f(X)$ divides $g(X)h(X)$, $f(X)$ divides $ch(X)$, hence divides $h(X)$.
