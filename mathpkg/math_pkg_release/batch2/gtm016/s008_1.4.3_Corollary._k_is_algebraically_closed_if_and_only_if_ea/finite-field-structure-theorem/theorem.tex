If $k$ is a field extension of dimension $m$ of a prime field $\pi$ of $p$ elements, then $k$ has $p^m$ elements, $k$ is the splitting field of $X^{p^m} - X$ over $\pi$, and $k = \operatorname{Roots}_{k_{\text{Alg}}}(X^{p^m} - X)$. Conversely, for any prime $p$ and positive integer $m$, the splitting field of $X^{p^m} - X$ over $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is a field extension of dimension $m$ of $\mathbb{Z}_p$.
