This proposition states that $\Delta$ and $\varepsilon$ endow $H(K/k)$ with the structure of a $K$-bialgebra. The four properties are: (1) coassociativity of $\Delta$, (2) $\varepsilon$ as coidentity, (3) $\Delta$ as an algebra homomorphism, and (4) multiplicativity of $\varepsilon$. Together these make $(H(K/k), \Delta, \varepsilon)$ a $K$-bialgebra in the sense of Appendix B.1.
