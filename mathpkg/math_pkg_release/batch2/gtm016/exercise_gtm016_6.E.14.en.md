Let $K/k$ be a radical extension. Elements $y_1, \ldots, y_s$ of $K$ are $p$-independent over $k$ if $[k(y_1, \ldots, y_s) : k] = p^s$ and $y_i^p \in k$ for $1 \leq i \leq s$.

(e) If $y_1, \ldots, y_s$ are $p$-independent elements of $\sqrt[p]{K}$ over $\sqrt[p]{k}$, then $y_1^p, \ldots, y_s^p$ are $p$-independent elements of $\sqrt[p]{K}$ over $\sqrt[p]{k}$.

(f) Choosing $m$ such that $K = \sqrt[p^m]{k} \supseteq \sqrt[p^{m-1}]{k} \supseteq \cdots \supseteq \sqrt[p]{k} \supseteq k$, there exist elements $y_1, \ldots, y_{s_m}, y_{s_m+1}, \ldots, y_{s_{m-1}}, \ldots, y_{s_2+1}, \ldots, y_{s_1}$ of $K$ such that $y_1, \ldots, y_{s_m}$ is a $p$-basis for $K$ over $\sqrt[p]{k}$; $y_1^p, \ldots, y_{s_m}^p, y_{s_m+1}, \ldots, y_{s_{m-1}}$ is a $p$-basis for $\sqrt[p]{k}$ over $\sqrt[p^2]{k}$; ... and $y_1^{p^{m-1}}, \ldots$ is a $p$-basis for $\sqrt[p^{m-1}]{k}$ over $k$.

(g) $K = k(y_1) \otimes_k \cdots \otimes_k k(y_{s_1})$ (internal tensor product over $k$) where the $y_1, \ldots, y_{s_1}$ are as described in part (f).
