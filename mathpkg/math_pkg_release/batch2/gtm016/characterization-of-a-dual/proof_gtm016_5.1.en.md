Let $R \in \mathcal{A}$. We claim that $T(xR) = T(x)R$ for all $T \in \mathcal{A}, x \in K$ if and only if $R(K_0) \subset K^{\mathcal{A}}$, thereby establishing the assertion. From the equations

$$\hat{y}(T(xR)) = T(xR)(y) = T(xR(y)),$$

$$\hat{y}(T(x)R) = (T(x)R)(y) = T(x)R(y)$$

we see that $T(xR) = T(x)R$ for all $T \in \mathcal{A}, x \in K$ if and only if $T(xR(y)) = T(x)R(y)$ for all $T \in \mathcal{A}, x \in K$ and for all $y \in K_0$. The latter condition is that $R(K_0) \subset K^{\mathcal{A}}$. $\square$
