The set \(T(G) = \{\bar{t} \mid t \in (kG)^*\}\) consists of elements of the Hopf algebra \(H(K/k)\) obtained by applying the "bar" operation to elements of the \(k\)-dual \((kG)^*\) of the group algebra \(kG\) of a Galois splitting \(G\) of \(K/k\). The bar operation sends \(t \in (kG)^*\) to \(\bar{t} \in H(K/k)\) defined by \(\bar{t}(x) = t(U)x\) for \(x \in U\), \(U \in G\). The resulting set \(T(G)\) inherits a product, coproduct, and counit from those on \((kG)^*\) and forms a diagonalizable toral \(k\)-subbiring of the Hopf algebra \(H(K/k)\), with character group given by \(\hat{g}(\bar{t}) = t(g)\).
