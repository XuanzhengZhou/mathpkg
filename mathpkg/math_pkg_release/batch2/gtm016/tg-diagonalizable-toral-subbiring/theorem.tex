Let $G$ be a splitting of $K/k$ and let $T$ be the $k$-dual space $(kG)^*$ of the group $k$-algebra $kG$ of $G$. For $s, t \in T$, let $st$ be the element of $T$ such that $(st)(g) = s(g)t(g)$ for $g \in G$. For $t \in T$, let $\Delta_G t$ be the element of $T \otimes_k T$ such that $\Delta_G t = \sum_i t \otimes_k t_i$ if and only if $t(gh) = \sum_i t(g)t_i(h)$ for $g, h \in G$, and let $\varepsilon_G(t) = t(e)$ where $e = K^G$ is the identity element of $G$. For $t \in T$, let $\bar{t}$ be the element of $H(K/k)$ such that $\bar{t}(x) = t(U)x$ for $x \in U$ and $U \in G$.

$$
T(G) = \{ \bar{t} \mid t \in (kG)^* \}.
$$

Note that $\overline{st} = \bar{s}\bar{t}$, $\Delta_G t = \sum_i u \otimes_k u_i$ implies $\Delta \bar{t} = \sum_i \bar{t} \otimes_k \bar{t}_i$, and $\varepsilon_G(t) = \varepsilon(\bar{t})$ for all $s, t \in (kG)^*$. It follows that $T(G)$ is a diagonalizable toral $k$-subbiring of $H(K/k)$. Letting $\hat{g}: T \to k$ be defined by $\hat{g}(\bar{t}) = t(g)$ for $\bar{g} \in G$ gives the character group.
