Theorem C analyzes the auxiliary transformation $Q = S^{-1}RS$, which satisfies $Q(x,y) = (xy, y^{-1})$ and is an involution ($Q^{-1} = Q$). This theorem provides explicit formulas for the $x^{-1}$-sections and $y^{-1}$-sections of $Q(A \times B)$ in terms of the original sets $A$ and $B$. These formulas are essential tools for proving measurability and positivity results about translates and inverses of measurable sets.
