The reflected shearing transformation $T = R^{-1}SR$ is obtained by conjugating the shearing transformation $S$ with the coordinate swap $R(x,y) = (y,x)$. It satisfies $T(x,y) = (yx,y)$ and, like $S$, is measurability preserving on the product of a measurable group with itself. Together with $S$ and $R$, it generates a family of measurability preserving transformations used throughout the analysis of measurable groups.
