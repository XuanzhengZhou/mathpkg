Theorem E is a covering (or common-refinement) lemma for measurable groups: given two measurable sets $A$ and $B$ of positive measure, one can find a single measurable set $C$ of positive measure whose translates by suitable group elements lie inside $A$ and $B$ respectively. This result is a crucial technical tool for establishing the averaging formula (Theorem F) and for the subsequent development of the convolution product and Haar measure theory.
