If $A$ and $B$ are measurable sets of positive measure in a measurable group $X$, then there exist elements $x, y \in X$ and a measurable set $C$ of positive measure such that
$$xC \subset A \quad \text{and} \quad yC \subset B.$$
In particular, taking $y = e$, there exists $x_1$ and a measurable set $C_1$ of positive measure with $x_1 C_1 \subset A$ and $C_1 \subset B$. The same result applied to $A^{-1}$ and $B^{-1}$ yields $x_0, y_0$ and $C_0$ of positive measure with $x_0 C_0 \subset A^{-1}$ and $y_0 C_0 \subset B^{-1}$.
