Theorem D establishes fundamental closure properties of measurable groups: right translates $Ay$ of measurable sets are measurable and preserve positive measure, and the inverse $A^{-1}$ of a measurable set is measurable. The proof uses Theorem C (the sections of $Q(A \times A)$) and a clever covering argument to bootstrap from positive measure sets to all measurable sets. This theorem also implies that the map $x \mapsto x^{-1}$ preserves measurability of functions.
