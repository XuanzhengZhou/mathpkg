A \text{measurable group} is a $\sigma$-finite measure space $(X,\mathbf{S},\mu)$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\mu$ is not identically zero,
\item[(b)] $X$ is a group,
\item[(c)] the $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ and the measure $\mu$ are invariant under left translations, and
\item[(d)] the transformation $S$ of $X \times X$ onto itself, defined by $S(x,y) = (x,xy)$, is measurability preserving.
\end{enumerate}
