A transformation $T$ is a function defined for every point of a set $X$ and taking values in a set $Y$. The set $X$ is called the domain of $T$; the set of those points of $Y$ which are of the form $T(x)$ for some $x$ in $X$ is the range of $T$. A transformation whose domain is $X$ and whose range is in $Y$ is often described as a transformation from $X$ into $Y$; if the range of $T$ is $Y$, $T$ is called a transformation from $X$ onto $Y$.

For every subset $E$ of $X$, the image of $E$ under $T$, in symbols $T(E)$, is the range of the transformation $T$ from $E$ into $Y$.

For every subset $F$ of $Y$, the inverse image of $F$ under $T$, in symbols $T^{-1}(F)$, is defined to be the set of all those points of $X$ whose image is in $F$; i.e.
$$T^{-1}(F) = \{x: T(x) \in F\}.$$
