A measurable transformation between measurable spaces $(X,\mathbf{S})$ and $(Y,\mathbf{T})$ is a transformation $T$ such that $T^{-1}(F) \in \mathbf{S}$ for every $F \in \mathbf{T}$. When $X \in \mathbf{S}$ and $Y = \mathbb{R}$, this coincides with the notion of a measurable function.
