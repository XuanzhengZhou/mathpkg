If $(X, \mathbf{S})$ and $(Y, \mathbf{T})$ are measurable spaces and if $T$ is a transformation from $X$ into $Y$, $T$ is a measurable transformation if the inverse image of every measurable set is measurable. That is, for every $F \in \mathbf{T}$, $T^{-1}(F) \in \mathbf{S}$.

In the important case in which $X$ itself belongs to $\mathbf{S}$ and $Y$ is the real line, the two concepts, measurable transformation and measurable function, coincide.
