De Morgan's laws state that complementation interchanges unions and intersections. Together with the principle of duality, they allow any identity involving unions, intersections, and complements to be transformed into a dual identity by swapping $\cup$ with $\cap$, $\subset$ with $\supset$, and $0$ with $X$.
