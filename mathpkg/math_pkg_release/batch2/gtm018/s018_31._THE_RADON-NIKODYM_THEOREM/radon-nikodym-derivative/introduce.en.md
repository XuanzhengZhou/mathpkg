The Radon-Nikodym derivative $\frac{d\nu}{d\mu}$ is the measurable density function $f$ whose existence is guaranteed by the Radon-Nikodym Theorem, satisfying $\nu(E) = \int_E f \, d\mu$ for all measurable sets $E$. The suggestive differential notation $f = \frac{d\nu}{d\mu}$ or $d\nu = f \, d\mu$ is deliberately chosen because all the formal properties of ordinary derivatives — such as the chain rule and the substitution rule — correspond to true theorems for Radon-Nikodym derivatives. The derivative is unique only almost everywhere with respect to $\mu$.
