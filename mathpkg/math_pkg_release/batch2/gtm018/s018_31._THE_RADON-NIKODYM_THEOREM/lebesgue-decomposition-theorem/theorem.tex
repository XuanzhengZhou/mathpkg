If $(X, \mathbf{S})$ is a measurable space and $\mu$ and $\nu$ are totally $\sigma$-finite signed measures on $\mathbf{S}$, then there exist two uniquely determined totally $\sigma$-finite signed measures $\nu_0$ and $\nu_1$ whose sum is $\nu$, such that
$$\nu_0 \perp \mu \quad \text{and} \quad \nu_1 \ll \mu.$$
