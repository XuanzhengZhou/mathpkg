Given two sequences $\{\mu_n\}$ and $\{\nu_n\}$ of totally finite measures, write
$$\bar{\mu}_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \mu_i, \quad \bar{\nu}_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \nu_i, \quad \mu = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \mu_i, \quad \nu = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \nu_i,$$
and assume that $\mu$ and $\nu$ are finite measures. If $\bar{\nu}_n \ll \bar{\mu}_n$ for $n = 1, 2, \dots$, then $\nu \ll \mu$ and
$$\lim_n \frac{d\bar{\nu}_n}{d\bar{\mu}_n} = \frac{d\nu}{d\mu} \quad [\mu].$$
