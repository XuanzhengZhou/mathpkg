Theorem B is not necessarily true if $\nu$ is not finite.
(Hint: let $X$ be the set of all positive integers, and, for every subset $E$ of $X$, write
$$\mu(E) = \sum_{n \in E} 2^{-n}, \quad \nu(E) = \sum_{n \in E} 2^{n}.$$
Show that $\nu \ll \mu$ but the Radon-Nikodym conclusion fails.)
