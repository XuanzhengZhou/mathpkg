The class $\mathbf{P}$ of bounded left-closed, right-open intervals on the real line is a semiring. The ring $\mathbf{R}$ it generates consists of all finite disjoint unions of such intervals. Halmos uses semiclosed intervals because they partition nicely: the difference of two such intervals is a finite disjoint union of intervals of the same type.
