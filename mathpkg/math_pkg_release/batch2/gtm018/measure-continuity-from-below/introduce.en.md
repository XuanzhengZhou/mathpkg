Theorem D (continuity from below): for an increasing sequence of measurable sets whose limit is in the ring, the measure of the limit equals the limit of the measures. The proof writes the union as a disjoint union of differences $E_i - E_{i-1}$ and uses countable additivity. This is one of the most frequently used properties of measures.
