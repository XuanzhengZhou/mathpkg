Theorem D establishes that the class $\mathbf{A}$ is a directed set under reverse inclusion: for any two sets $A, B \in \mathbf{A}$, there exists $C \in \mathbf{A}$ with $C \subset A \cap B$. This property is critical for proving that the class $\mathbf{N}$ satisfies the neighborhood base axioms of a topological group.
