**Exercise 62.6c: Hamel basis**

A \emph{Hamel basis} is a linearly independent set $B$ in $X$ with the property that for every $x$ in $X$ there exists a finite subset $\{x_1, \dots, x_n\}$ of $B$ and a corresponding finite set $\{r_1, \dots, r_n\}$ of rational numbers such that $x = \sum_{i=1}^{n} r_i x_i$. 

Prove that:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The expression of $x$ as a rational linear combination of elements of $B$ is unique.
    \item Every linearly independent set is contained in a Hamel basis.
\end{enumerate}

(Hint: use transfinite induction or Zorn's lemma.)
