Theorem E is the central result of the Weil topology construction: if a measurable group is separated (meaning distinct elements can be distinguished measure-theoretically), then taking the class $N$ as a neighborhood base at the identity makes $X$ into a topological group. This provides the converse to the theorem that every locally compact group is a measurable group, showing that the measure-theoretic and topological structures are intimately linked.
