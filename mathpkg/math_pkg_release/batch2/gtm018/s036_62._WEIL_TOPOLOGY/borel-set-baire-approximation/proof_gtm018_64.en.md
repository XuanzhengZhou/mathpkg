**Corollary.** Given any Borel set $E$ in $X$, there exists a $\sigma$-compact full subgroup $Z$ of $X$ such that $E \subset Z$ (since every Borel set is contained in a $\sigma$-compact subgroup). By Theorem H, the restriction of $\mu$ to $Z$ is completion regular, and therefore there exist two subsets $A$ and $B$ of $Z$ which are Baire subsets of $Z$ and for which

$$A \subset E \subset B \quad \text{and} \quad \mu(B - A) = 0.$$

Since $Z$ is both open and closed in $X$, $A$ and $B$ are also Baire subsets of $X$. Thus every Borel set can be approximated from inside and outside by Baire sets up to a set of measure zero. $\blacksquare$
