Let $E$ and $F$ be measurable sets of positive, finite measure such that $A = EE^{-1}$ and $B = FF^{-1}$. By 59.E, there exists a measurable set $G$ of positive, finite measure and there exist two elements $x$ and $y$ in $X$ such that

$$xG \subset E \quad \text{and} \quad yG \subset F.$$

Then $GG^{-1} \subset (x^{-1}E)(x^{-1}E)^{-1} = x^{-1}EE^{-1}x$ and also $GG^{-1} \subset y^{-1}FF^{-1}y$. Since $A$ and $B$ contain neighborhoods of $e$, we can adjust by translation to obtain $C = GG^{-1} \in A$ with $C \subset A \cap B$.
