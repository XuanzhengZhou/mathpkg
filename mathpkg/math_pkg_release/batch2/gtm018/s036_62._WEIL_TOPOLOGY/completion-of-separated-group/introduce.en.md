Theorem H is the culmination of the Weil topology construction. It states that every separated measurable group can be embedded as a dense subgroup of a locally compact group (its Weil completion), and the original measure extends naturally to a Baire measure on the completion. This result, together with Theorem E, establishes a complete equivalence between the measure-theoretic notion of a measurable group and the topological notion of a locally compact group, forming one of the foundational pillars of abstract harmonic analysis.
