**Theorem B.** The metric space consists of all sets $E \in \mathbf{T}$ with $\mu(E) < \infty$, endowed with the metric $\rho(E,F) = \mu(E \Delta F)$. Since $\mathbf{E}$ is countable and generates $\mathbf{T}$, the class of finite unions, intersections, and differences of translates of sets in $\mathbf{E}$ forms a countable dense subset in this metric space. The separability follows from the fact that the $\sigma$-ring generated by a countable class, when restricted to sets of finite measure, yields a separable metric space under the symmetric difference metric. $\blacksquare$
