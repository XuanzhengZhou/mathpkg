The Weil topology is the natural topology induced on a separated measurable group by taking the class $N$ of all sets $\{x : \rho(xE, E) < \epsilon\}$ as a neighborhood base at the identity. This construction, due to André Weil, shows that the measure-theoretic structure of a measurable group uniquely determines a topological group structure, providing a converse to the result that every locally compact group is a measurable group.
