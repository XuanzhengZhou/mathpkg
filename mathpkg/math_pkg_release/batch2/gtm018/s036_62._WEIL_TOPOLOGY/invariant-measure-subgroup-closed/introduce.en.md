Theorem G is an auxiliary result used in the reformulation of the Weil topology theory. It states that in a locally compact topological group, the set of all elements that preserve a given Baire measure under left translation forms a closed subgroup. This result is used in Theorem H to construct the completion of a separated measurable group to a locally compact group.
