A measurable group is separated if distinct elements can be distinguished by the action on measurable sets: for any $x 
eq e$, there exists a measurable set $E$ of positive, finite measure such that $ho(xE, E) > 0$. This separation condition is necessary for the Weil topology to be Hausdorff, playing a role analogous to the $T_0$ axiom in topological groups.
