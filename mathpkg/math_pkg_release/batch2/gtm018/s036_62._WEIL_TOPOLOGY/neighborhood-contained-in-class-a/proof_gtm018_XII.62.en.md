It is trivial that $N \in N$; to see that $N \subset A$, observe that if $x \in N$, then $\rho(xE, E) < \epsilon$. Since $\epsilon < 2\mu(E)$, we have $\mu(xE \cap E) > \mu(E) - \epsilon/2 > 0$, and therefore $xE \cap E \neq \emptyset$. Thus $x \in \{x : xE \cap E \neq \emptyset\} = EE^{-1} = A$. Hence $N \subset A$.
