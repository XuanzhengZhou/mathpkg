Theorem D provides a characterization of compact invariant subgroups: if $\mathbf{E}$ is a dense class in the metric space of finite-measure sets of an invariant $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{T}$, then the subgroup $Y$ of elements $y$ satisfying $ho(yE,E) = 0$ for all $E \in \mathbf{E}$ is a compact invariant subgroup. This is a fundamental structural result for locally compact groups.
