Theorem E is the main result of the Weil topology construction: for a separated measurable group $X$, taking the class $\mathbf{N}$ as a neighborhood base at the identity yields a group topology on $X$, called the Weil topology. This shows that every separated measurable group can be naturally topologized as a topological group.
