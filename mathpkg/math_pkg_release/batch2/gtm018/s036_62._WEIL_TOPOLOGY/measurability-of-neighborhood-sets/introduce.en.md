Theorem C establishes that every set $N$ in the neighborhood base class $N$ is measurable and has positive measure. Together with Theorem A, this shows that $N$ is a non-trivial collection of measurable sets. Additionally, when $E^{-1}$ has finite measure, the corresponding $N$ is also of finite measure, which is essential for constructing a locally bounded (hence locally compact) topology.
