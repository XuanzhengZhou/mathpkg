Theorem F establishes that a separated measurable group with its Weil topology is locally bounded. Moreover, it connects the measure-theoretic and topological notions of size: any measurable set with nonempty interior must have positive measure, and any bounded measurable set must have finite measure. This result is optimal in its direction and serves as a stepping stone to viewing the group as a dense subgroup of a locally compact group.
