A simple function is a measurable function taking only finitely many values, each on a measurable set. Represented as $\sum \alpha_i \chi_{E_i}$, simple functions are the building blocks of integration theory — analogous to step functions in Riemann integration. They are always measurable, and their sums, products, and finite linear combinations remain simple functions.
