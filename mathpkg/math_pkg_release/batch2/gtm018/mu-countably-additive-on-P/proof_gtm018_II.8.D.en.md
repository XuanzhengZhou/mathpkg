If $\{E_i\}$ is a disjoint sequence of sets in $\mathbf{P}$ whose union, $E$, is also in $\mathbf{P}$, then from Theorem A we have

$$\sum_{i=1}^{n} \mu(E_i) \leq \mu(E) \quad \text{for} \quad n = 1, 2, \dots.$$

It follows that

$$\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \mu(E_i) \leq \mu(E);$$

an application of Theorem C completes the proof.
