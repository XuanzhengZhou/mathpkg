A $\sigma$-ring is closed not only under countable unions and differences, but also under the formation of superior and inferior limits of sequences of its sets. This follows from the representation of liminf and limsup in terms of countable unions and intersections, together with the fact that a $\sigma$-ring is closed under countable intersections (via the identity $\bigcap_i E_i = E_1 - \bigcup_i (E_1 - E_i)$).
