The inverse image $f^{-1}(M)$ is the set of all points in the domain that $f$ maps into $M$. A crucial property is that the inverse image mapping preserves all set operations (unions, intersections, complements). This makes $f^{-1}$ a structure-preserving map from subsets of the range to subsets of the domain, forming the basis for defining measurable functions.
