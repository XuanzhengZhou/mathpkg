The tail product space $X^{(n)} = \prod_{i=n+1}^\infty X_i$ is the infinite product of all factor spaces starting from index $n+1$. It allows the full infinite product $X$ to be decomposed as a finite product followed by an infinite tail: $X = (X_1 \times \cdots \times X_n) \times X^{(n)}$. This decomposition is essential for the recursive construction in the proof of the existence of infinite product measure, where the measure of a $\{1,\dots,n\}$-cylinder is first defined via the finite product measure on the first $n$ coordinates and then extended to cover the tail.
