We shall write

$$X^{(n)} = \prod_{i=n+1}^{\infty} X_i, \quad n = 0, 1, 2, \cdots;$$

in view of our identification convention for product spaces, we may write

$$X = \prod_{i=1}^{\infty} X_i = (X_1 \times \cdots \times X_n) \times X^{(n)}.$$

Each space $X^{(n)}$ is itself an infinite dimensional product space of the same type as $X = X^{(0)}$.
