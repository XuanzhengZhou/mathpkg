The infinite product measure $\mu = \prod_{i=1}^\infty \mu_i$ is the unique measure on the product $\sigma$-algebra $\prod_{i=1}^\infty \mathbf{S}_i$ whose existence and uniqueness are guaranteed by Theorem B. It satisfies $\mu(A \times X^{(n)}) = (\mu_1 \times \cdots \times \mu_n)(A)$ for every finite-dimensional measurable cylinder. The triple $(\prod X_i, \prod \mathbf{S}_i, \prod \mu_i)$ is called the Cartesian product measure space. This construction is the measure-theoretic foundation for infinite sequences of independent random variables in probability theory, where each factor space $X_i$ is a probability space and the product measure corresponds to the joint distribution of independent trials.
