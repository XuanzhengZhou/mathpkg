The infinite Cartesian product generalizes the familiar finite product construction to a countably infinite family of sets. Given a sequence $\{X_i\}_{i=1}^\infty$, the product $\prod_{i=1}^\infty X_i$ consists of all sequences $(x_1, x_2, \dots)$ with $x_i \in X_i$ for each positive integer $i$. This construction serves as the underlying set-theoretic foundation for infinite-dimensional product measure spaces. When each $X_i$ carries a measure-theoretic structure, the product becomes the ambient space on which the infinite product measure is defined.
