The generated $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}(\mathbf{E})$ is the smallest $\sigma$-ring containing $\mathbf{E}$. Every set in $\mathbf{S}(\mathbf{E})$ is contained in a $\sigma$-ring generated by some countable subclass of $\mathbf{E}$ (Theorem D), an important technical fact used repeatedly in measure-theoretic proofs.
