Convergence in measure requires that for any $\epsilon > 0$, the measure of the set where $|f_n - f| \geq \epsilon$ tends to zero. Almost everywhere convergence implies convergence in measure on sets of finite measure; conversely, every sequence converging in measure has a subsequence converging almost everywhere.
