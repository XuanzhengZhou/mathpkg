Theorem B gives a criterion for when the integral of a non-negative continuous function with compact support vanishes. Under the assumption that every non-empty Baire open set has strictly positive measure, the integral $\int f \, d\mu = 0$ forces $f$ to be identically zero on the whole space $X$. The sufficiency direction is trivial; the necessity direction uses the fact that the set where $f$ is non-zero is contained in a bounded open Baire set, and if the integral vanished while $f$ were positive somewhere, an open Baire set of positive measure would be forced to have measure zero.
