The class $\mathcal{L}(X)$ consists of all real-valued continuous functions on a locally compact Hausdorff space $X$ that vanish outside some compact set. These are precisely the continuous functions whose support $N(f) = \{x : f(x) \neq 0\}$ is bounded. When $X$ is interpreted via its one-point compactification, $\mathcal{L}(X)$ may be described as the class of continuous functions that vanish in a neighborhood of the point at infinity. This function space plays a fundamental role in the measure-theoretic study of locally compact spaces.
