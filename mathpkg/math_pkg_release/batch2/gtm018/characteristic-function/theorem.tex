If $E$ is any subset of $X$, the function $\chi_E$, defined for all $x$ in $X$ by the relations
$$\chi_E(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } x \in E, \\ 0 & \text{if } x \not\in E \end{cases}$$
is called the characteristic function of the set $E$. The correspondence between sets and their characteristic functions is one to one, and all properties of sets and set operations may be expressed by means of characteristic functions.
