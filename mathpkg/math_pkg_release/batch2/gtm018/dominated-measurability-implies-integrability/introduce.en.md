If a measurable function is dominated in absolute value by an integrable function, it is integrable. For non-negative $f$, this is proved by approximating $f$ by an increasing sequence of simple functions $f_n \leq |g|$, each integrable (as simple functions dominated by $|g|$). The sequence $\{\int f_n\}$ is bounded and increasing, hence $f$ is integrable. This generalizes to signed $f$ by decomposing into positive and negative parts.
