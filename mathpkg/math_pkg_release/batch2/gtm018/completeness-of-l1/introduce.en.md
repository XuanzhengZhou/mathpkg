The space of integrable functions is complete with respect to the $L^1$ seminorm: every mean fundamental (Cauchy) sequence converges in mean to an integrable function. This is the Riesz-Fischer theorem for $L^1$. The proof approximates each $f_n$ by a simple function $g_n$, extracts a mean fundamental sequence of simple functions, and uses the definition of integrability.
