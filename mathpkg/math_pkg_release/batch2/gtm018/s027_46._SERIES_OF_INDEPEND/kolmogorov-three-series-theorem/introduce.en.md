Kolmogorov's Three Series Theorem provides a complete characterization of almost everywhere convergence for series of independent random variables. For any truncation level $c > 0$, convergence holds if and only if three numerical series converge: the sum of tail probabilities beyond the truncation, the sum of truncated means, and the sum of truncated variances. The proof reduces the problem to bounded functions via truncation and applies Theorem D.
