If $\{f_n\}$ is a sequence of independent functions on a probability space, then a necessary and sufficient condition for the almost everywhere convergence of $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} f_n(x)$ is that there exist a positive constant $c$ such that, writing $E_n = \{x : |f_n(x)| \leq c\}$, the following three series are convergent:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \mu(E_n')$, where $E_n' = X \setminus E_n$;
  \item[(b)] $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \int_{E_n} f_n \, d\mu$;
  \item[(c)] $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left( \int_{E_n} f_n^2 \, d\mu - \left( \int_{E_n} f_n \, d\mu \right)^2 \right)$.
\end{enumerate}
