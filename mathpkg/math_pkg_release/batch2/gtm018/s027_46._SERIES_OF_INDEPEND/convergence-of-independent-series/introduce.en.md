This theorem gives a sufficient condition for the almost everywhere convergence of a series of independent functions on a probability space. If each function has zero mean and the sum of their variances is finite, then the series converges almost everywhere. The proof uses Kolmogorov's inequality to control the tail behavior of the partial sums, showing that the set of points where the series fails to be Cauchy has measure zero.
