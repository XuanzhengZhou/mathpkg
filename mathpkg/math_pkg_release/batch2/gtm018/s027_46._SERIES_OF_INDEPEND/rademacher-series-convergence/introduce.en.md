This result is a direct application of Theorem D to the Rademacher functions, which are independent and uniformly bounded by $1$. It shows that a random series with signs $\pm 1$ chosen independently and with equal probability converges with probability $1$ precisely when the coefficients are square-summable. The Rademacher functions have zero mean and variance $1$, reducing Theorem D's conditions to the convergence of $\sum c_n^2$.
