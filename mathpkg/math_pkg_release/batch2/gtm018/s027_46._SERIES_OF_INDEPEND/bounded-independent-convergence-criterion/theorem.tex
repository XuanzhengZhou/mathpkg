If $\{f_n\}$ is a sequence of independent functions on a probability space and $c$ is a positive constant such that $|f_n(x)| \leq c$ almost everywhere for $n = 1, 2, \dots$, then $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} f_n(x)$ converges almost everywhere if and only if both the series

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \int f_n \, d\mu \quad \text{and} \quad \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sigma^2(f_n)$$

are convergent.
