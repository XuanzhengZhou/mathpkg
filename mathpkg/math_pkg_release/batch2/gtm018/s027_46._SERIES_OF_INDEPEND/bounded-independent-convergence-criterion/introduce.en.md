For uniformly bounded independent functions, the almost everywhere convergence of the series is equivalent to the convergence of both the series of means and the series of variances. The forward direction uses Theorem B applied to the centered functions. The converse uses a product-space argument with the functions $h_n(x,y) = f_n(x) - f_n(y)$ and Theorem C to deduce finiteness of the variance sum, from which the convergence of the mean series follows.
