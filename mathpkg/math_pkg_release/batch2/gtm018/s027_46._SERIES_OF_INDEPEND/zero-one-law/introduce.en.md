Kolmogorov's zero-one law states that any event measurable with respect to the tail $\sigma$-algebra of a sequence of independent random variables must have probability either 0 or 1. In the product-space formulation, a set that is a cylinder with respect to all co-finite index sets (a tail event) can only have measure 0 or 1. This explains why the convergence set of a series of independent functions must have measure 0 or 1.
