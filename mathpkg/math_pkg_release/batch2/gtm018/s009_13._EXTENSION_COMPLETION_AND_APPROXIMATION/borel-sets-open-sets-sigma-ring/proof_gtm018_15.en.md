Each singleton $\{a\}$ is Borel, so $(a,b) = [a,b) - \{a\}$ shows bounded open intervals are Borel; open sets are countable unions of such intervals, so $\mathbf{U} \subset \mathbf{S}$ and $\mathbf{S}(\mathbf{U}) \subset \mathbf{S}$. Conversely, $\{a\} = \bigcap (a-1/n, a+1/n) \in \mathbf{S}(\mathbf{U})$, and $[a,b) = (a,b) \cup \{a\}$ gives $\mathbf{P} \subset \mathbf{S}(\mathbf{U})$, hence $\mathbf{S} \subset \mathbf{S}(\mathbf{U})$.
