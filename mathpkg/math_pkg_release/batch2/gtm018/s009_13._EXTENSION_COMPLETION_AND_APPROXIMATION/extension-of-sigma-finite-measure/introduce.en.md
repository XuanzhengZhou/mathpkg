The extension theorem for $\sigma$-finite measures is the central result of Carathéodory's extension theory. Given a $\sigma$-finite measure $\mu$ defined on a ring $\mathbf{R}$, there exists a unique extension $\bar{\mu}$ to the generated $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}(\mathbf{R})$. The extended measure agrees with $\mu$ on $\mathbf{R}$ and remains $\sigma$-finite.
