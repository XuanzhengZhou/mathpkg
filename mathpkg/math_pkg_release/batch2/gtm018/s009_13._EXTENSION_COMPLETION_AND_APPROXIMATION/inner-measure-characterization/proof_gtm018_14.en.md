Since $\mathbf{S} \subset \bar{\mathbf{S}}$, $\mu_*(E) \leq \sup \{ \bar{\mu}(F) : E \supset F \in \bar{\mathbf{S}} \}$. Conversely, by Theorem 13.B, for each $F \in \bar{\mathbf{S}}$ there exists $G \in \mathbf{S}$ with $G \subset F$ and $\bar{\mu}(F) = \mu(G)$. Thus every value of $\bar{\mu}$ on subsets of $E$ in $\bar{\mathbf{S}}$ is also attained by $\mu$ on subsets of $E$ in $\mathbf{S}$, giving the reverse inequality.
