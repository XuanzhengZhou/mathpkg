Let $A$ be a measurable cover of $F$ and $B$ a measurable kernel of $E \cup F$. Since $B - A \subset E$, $\mu_*(E \cup F) = \mu(B) \leq \mu(B - A) + \mu(A) \leq \mu_*(E) + \mu^*(F)$. Dually, let $A$ be a measurable kernel of $E$ and $B$ a measurable cover of $E \cup F$. Since $B - A \supset F$, $\mu^*(E \cup F) = \mu(B) = \mu(A) + \mu(B - A) \geq \mu_*(E) + \mu^*(F)$.
