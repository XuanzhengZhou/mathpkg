Theorem C extends subadditivity to countable covers: if a semiclosed interval is contained in a countable union of semiclosed intervals, its length is bounded by the sum of their lengths. The proof expands each interval slightly to an open interval and shrinks the covered interval to a closed one, then invokes compactness to reduce to Theorem B.
