Theorems A and B of §11 establish that the class $\bar{\mathbf{S}}$ of $\mu^*$-measurable sets is a $\sigma$-ring, and that $\mu^*$ is countably additive on disjoint sequences of measurable sets. This is the central technical result of the Carathéodory extension method, showing that restricting $\mu^*$ to $\bar{\mathbf{S}}$ yields a genuine measure.
