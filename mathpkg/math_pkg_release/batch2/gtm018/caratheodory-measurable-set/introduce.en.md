A set $E$ is $\mu^*$-measurable (in the sense of Carathéodory) if it "splits" every test set $A$ additively: the outer measure of $A$ equals the sum of the outer measures of the part inside $E$ and the part outside $E$. This elegant definition is the cornerstone of the Carathéodory extension method.
