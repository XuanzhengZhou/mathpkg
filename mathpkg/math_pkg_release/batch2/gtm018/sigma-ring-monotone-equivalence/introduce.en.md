Theorem A establishes the relationship between $\sigma$-rings and monotone classes: every $\sigma$-ring is a monotone class (trivially, since $\sigma$-rings are closed under countable unions and intersections), and a monotone ring is a $\sigma$-ring (because countable unions can be expressed as limits of increasing sequences of finite unions).
