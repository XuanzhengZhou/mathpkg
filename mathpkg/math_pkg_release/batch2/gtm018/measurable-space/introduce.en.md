A measurable space is a set $X$ together with a $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ of subsets whose union is the entire space. This structure provides the foundation for measure theory, declaring which sets are measurable without reference to any specific measure. The condition $\bigcup\mathbf{S}=X$ ensures every point belongs to some measurable set, eliminating pathologically irrelevant points.
