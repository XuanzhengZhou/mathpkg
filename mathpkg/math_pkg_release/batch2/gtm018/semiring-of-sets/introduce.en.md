A semiring is a class of sets closed under finite intersections, with the property that the difference of two sets can be expressed as a finite disjoint union of sets in the class. The class of left-closed right-open intervals on the real line is the canonical example. The ring generated by a semiring consists of all finite disjoint unions of sets from the semiring.
