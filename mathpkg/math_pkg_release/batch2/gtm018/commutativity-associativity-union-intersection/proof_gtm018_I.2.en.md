These properties follow directly from the definitions of union and intersection and the commutativity and associativity of the logical connectives "or" and "and".

For commutativity: $x \in E \cup F$ means $x \in E$ or $x \in F$, which is equivalent to $x \in F$ or $x \in E$, i.e. $x \in F \cup E$. Similarly for intersection.

For associativity: $x \in E \cup (F \cup G)$ means $x \in E$ or $(x \in F \text{ or } x \in G)$, which is equivalent to $(x \in E \text{ or } x \in F)$ or $x \in G$, i.e. $x \in (E \cup F) \cup G$. Similarly for intersection.
