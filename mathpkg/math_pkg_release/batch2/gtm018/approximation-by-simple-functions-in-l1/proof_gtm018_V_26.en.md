**Proof.** For any fixed $m$, $\{|f_n - f_m|\}$ is a mean fundamental sequence of integrable simple functions converging in measure to $|f - f_m|$. Therefore $\int |f - f_m| d\mu = \lim_n \int |f_n - f_m| d\mu$. Since $\{f_n\}$ is mean fundamental, the right side tends to $0$ as $m \to \infty$.
