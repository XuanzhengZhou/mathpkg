Every integrable function can be approximated arbitrarily well in $L^1$ by integrable simple functions. This theorem justifies the definition of integrable functions: the class of integrable functions is exactly the $L^1$ closure of integrable simple functions. It is the measure-theoretic analogue of approximating Riemann integrable functions by step functions.
