If the generating class $\mathbf{E}$ is countable, then the ring it generates is also countable. This theorem (Theorem C in Halmos) is proved by constructing an increasing sequence $\mathbf{E}_0 = \mathbf{E}, \mathbf{E}_{n+1} = \mathbf{E}_n^*$ where $\mathbf{C}^*$ denotes finite unions of differences, and showing the union is a ring.
