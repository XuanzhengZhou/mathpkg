The Hahn decomposition theorem partitions the whole space $X$ into disjoint positive and negative sets for any signed measure $\mu$. Although the decomposition is not unique, the measures induced on any measurable set $E$ by different Hahn decompositions coincide. The proof constructs the negative set $B$ via an infimum argument and shows by contradiction that $A = X - B$ must be positive.
