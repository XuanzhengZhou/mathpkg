When $\nu$ is a finite signed measure, the abstract definition of absolute continuity $\nu \ll \mu$ implies the more concrete epsilon-delta formulation: $\nu$-measure can be made arbitrarily small by making $\mu$-measure sufficiently small. The proof uses a limsup construction and contradiction with the absolute continuity hypothesis.
