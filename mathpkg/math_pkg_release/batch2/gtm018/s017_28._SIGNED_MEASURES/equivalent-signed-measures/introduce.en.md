Two signed measures are equivalent, $\mu \equiv \nu$, if each is absolutely continuous with respect to the other. This means $\mu$ and $\nu$ have exactly the same null sets (sets of total variation zero). Absolute continuity $\ll$ is a preorder; equivalence $\equiv$ is the associated equivalence relation.
