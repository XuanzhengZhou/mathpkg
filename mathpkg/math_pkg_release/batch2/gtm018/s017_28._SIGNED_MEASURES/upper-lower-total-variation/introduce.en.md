Given a Hahn decomposition $X = A \cup B$, the upper variation $\mu^+$, lower variation $\mu^-$, and total variation $|\mu|$ are defined via the restrictions of $\mu$ to $A$ and $B$. These variations are ordinary (nonnegative) measures, and they provide the Jordan decomposition $\mu = \mu^+ - \mu^-$. At least one of $\mu^+$ and $\mu^-$ is always finite.
