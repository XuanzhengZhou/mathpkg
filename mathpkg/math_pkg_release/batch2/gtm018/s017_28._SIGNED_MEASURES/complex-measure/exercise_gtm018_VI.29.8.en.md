By the separate consideration of real and imaginary parts, integrals such as $\int f\,d\mu$ may be defined for complex valued functions $f$ and complex measures $\mu$ (cf. 28.2). Motivated by (7) above, we define the total variation of a finite complex measure $\mu$ by $|\mu|(E) = \sup \left| \int_E f\,d\mu \right|$, where the supremum is extended over all (possibly complex valued) measurable functions $f$ such that $|f| \leq 1$. What is the relation between $|\mu|$ and the total variations of the real and imaginary parts of $\mu$?
