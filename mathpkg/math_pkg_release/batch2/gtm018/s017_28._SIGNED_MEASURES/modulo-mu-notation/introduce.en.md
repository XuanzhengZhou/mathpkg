The notation $\pi(x)\;[\mu]$ (read ``$\pi$ modulo $\mu$'') means that a proposition $\pi(x)$ holds for almost every $x$ with respect to the total variation measure $|\mu|$. For example, $f = g\;[\mu]$ means $f$ and $g$ agree $|\mu|$-almost everywhere. This notation streamlines statements about properties holding almost everywhere for signed measures.
