Mutual singularity $\mu \perp \nu$ is the antithesis of absolute continuity: $\mu$ and $\nu$ are supported on disjoint sets $A$ and $B$ whose union is $X$. This means each measure lives on a set where the other measure's total variation vanishes. Together with absolute continuity, singularity forms the basis of the Lebesgue decomposition theorem.
