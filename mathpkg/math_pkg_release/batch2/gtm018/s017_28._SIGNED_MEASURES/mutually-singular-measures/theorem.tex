If $(X, \mathbf{S})$ is a measurable space and $\mu$ and $\nu$ are signed measures on $\mathbf{S}$, we say that $\mu$ and $\nu$ are \textit{mutually singular}, or simply \textit{singular}, in symbols $\mu \perp \nu$, if there exist two disjoint sets $A$ and $B$ whose union is $X$ such that, for every measurable set $E$, $A \cap E$ and $B \cap E$ are measurable and $|\mu|(A \cap E) = |\nu|(B \cap E) = 0$.
