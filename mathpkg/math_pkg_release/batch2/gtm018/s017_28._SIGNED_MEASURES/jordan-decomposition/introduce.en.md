The Jordan decomposition expresses every signed measure as the difference $\mu = \mu^+ - \mu^-$ of two ordinary (nonnegative) measures, where $\mu^+$ and $\mu^-$ are the upper and lower variations obtained from a Hahn decomposition. At least one of the two measures is always finite, avoiding indeterminate forms.
