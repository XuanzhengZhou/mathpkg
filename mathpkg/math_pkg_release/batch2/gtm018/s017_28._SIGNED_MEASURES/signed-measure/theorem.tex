A signed measure is an extended real valued, countably additive set function $\mu$ on the class of all measurable sets of a measurable space $(X, \mathbf{S})$, such that $\mu(\emptyset) = 0$, and such that $\mu$ assumes at most one of the values $+\infty$ and $-\infty$.

Implicit in the requirement of countable additivity is the requirement that if $\{E_n\}$ is a disjoint sequence of measurable sets, then the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \mu(E_n)$ is either convergent or definitely divergent (to $+\infty$ or $-\infty$)---in any case the symbol $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \mu(E_n)$ makes sense.

A signed measure is finitely additive and, therefore, subtractive.
