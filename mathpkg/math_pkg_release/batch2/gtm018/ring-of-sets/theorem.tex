A non empty class $\mathbf{R}$ of sets is called a ring if it is closed under the formation of unions and differences, i.e. if
$$E \in \mathbf{R}, \; F \in \mathbf{R}$$
implies
$$E \cup F \in \mathbf{R} \quad \text{and} \quad E - F \in \mathbf{R}.$$
Equivalently, a ring is a non empty class of sets closed under the formation of intersections and symmetric differences, since
$$E \cup F = (E \Delta F) \Delta (E \cap F), \quad E - F = E \Delta (E \cap F).$$
