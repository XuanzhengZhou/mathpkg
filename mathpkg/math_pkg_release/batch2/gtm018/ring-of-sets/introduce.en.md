A ring of sets is a nonempty class closed under finite unions and differences (equivalently, under intersections and symmetric differences). Rings are the fundamental domains on which measures are defined in Halmos' approach. The terminology is justified by the fact that under the operations $\Delta$ (addition) and $\cap$ (multiplication), every ring of sets is a Boolean ring in the algebraic sense.
