An integrable function is an a.e. finite measurable function that can be approximated in the mean by integrable simple functions, and its integral is defined as the limit of their integrals. This definition extends Lebesgue integration from simple to all integrable functions. The absolute value, constant multiples, and sum of integrable functions are integrable, and $f^+$, $f^-$ are integrable when $f$ is.
