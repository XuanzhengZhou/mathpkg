For any continuous real-valued function $f$ on a locally compact Hausdorff space $X$, the superlevel sets $\{x: f(x) \geq c\}$, sublevel sets $\{x: f(x) \leq c\}$, and level sets $\{x: f(x) = c\}$ are all closed $G_\delta$ sets. Conversely, every compact $G_\delta$ set can be realized as the zero set of some normalized continuous function in $\mathfrak{F}$. This theorem establishes a fundamental bridge between the topological notion of a compact $G_\delta$ and the analytic notion of a continuous separating function, and is essential for the theory of Baire sets.
