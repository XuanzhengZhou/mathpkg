The class $\mathfrak{F}$ consists of all continuous real-valued functions on a locally compact Hausdorff space $X$ whose values lie in the closed unit interval $[0,1]$. These normalized functions serve as the fundamental building blocks for separation arguments and the construction of cut-off functions throughout the chapter. Many classical results such as Urysohn's lemma are formulated in terms of functions belonging to $\mathfrak{F}$.
