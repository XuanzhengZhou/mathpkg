The $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ of Borel sets (respectively $\mathbf{S}_0$ of Baire sets) is precisely the $\sigma$-ring generated by the ring consisting of all finite disjoint unions of proper differences of compact sets (respectively compact $G_\delta$ sets). A proper difference is a set of the form $C - D$ where $C$ and $D$ are compact with $D \subset C$. This structural result provides a convenient generating system for Borel and Baire $\sigma$-rings, making them amenable to measure-theoretic constructions via content functions defined initially on compact sets.
