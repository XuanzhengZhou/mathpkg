A real function $f$ is measurable exactly when, for every real number $c$, the set of points where $f(x) < c$ (intersected with the support $N(f)$) is measurable. This provides a convenient necessary and sufficient condition that avoids checking all Borel sets. The same criterion holds with $<$, $\leq$, $>$, or $\geq$, and $c$ may be restricted to a dense subset of $\mathbb{R}$.
