If $\mu$ is a signed measure on a $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$, then there exist two sets $A$ and $B$ in $\mathbf{S}$ such that $A \cup B = X$, $A \cap B = 0$, and such that $A$ is positive (i.e. $\mu(E) \geq 0$ for every measurable $E \subset A$) and $B$ is negative (i.e. $\mu(E) \leq 0$ for every measurable $E \subset B$).
