The Hahn Decomposition Theorem states that the underlying space can be split into a positive set $A$ (where the signed measure is non-negative on all measurable subsets) and a negative set $B$ (where it is non-positive). This decomposition is not necessarily unique, but it leads to the Jordan decomposition of signed measures into the difference of two ordinary measures.
