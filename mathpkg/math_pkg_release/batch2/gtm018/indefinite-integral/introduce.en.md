The indefinite integral $\nu(E) = \int_E f d\mu$ assigns to each measurable set $E$ the integral of $f$ over $E$. It is a countably additive, absolutely continuous set function — essentially a new measure when $f \geq 0$, weighted by $f$. The indefinite integral generalizes the concept of a primitive function from classical calculus.
