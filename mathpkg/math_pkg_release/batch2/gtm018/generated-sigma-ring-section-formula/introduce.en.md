Theorem E provides a useful formula relating generated $\sigma$-rings and sections (intersections with a fixed set $A$). For any class $\mathbf{E}$ of sets and any subset $A$ of $X$, taking the $\sigma$-ring generated by $\mathbf{E}$ and then intersecting with $A$ yields the same result as first intersecting $\mathbf{E}$ with $A$ and then taking the generated $\sigma$-ring.
