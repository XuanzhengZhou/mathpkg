The Radon-Nikodym theorem is one of the most important results in measure theory. It states that under $\sigma$-finiteness, absolute continuity $\nu \ll \mu$ is equivalent to $\nu$ having a density $f = d\nu/d\mu$ with respect to $\mu$. This generalizes the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus and is essential for probability theory (conditional expectations), functional analysis (dual of $L^1$), and differentiation theory.
