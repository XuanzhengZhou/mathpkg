If $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ and $(Y, \mathbf{T}, \nu)$ are totally $\sigma$-finite measure spaces, there exists a unique measure $\pi$ on the $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S} \times \mathbf{T}$ generated by measurable rectangles such that, for every $E \in \mathbf{S}$ and $F \in \mathbf{T}$,
$$\pi(E \times F) = \mu(E) \nu(F).$$
This measure $\pi$ is called the product measure and is denoted by $\mu \times \nu$.
