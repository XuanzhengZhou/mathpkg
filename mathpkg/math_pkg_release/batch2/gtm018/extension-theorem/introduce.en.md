The Carathéodory Extension Theorem is the central result that justifies the outer measure construction: every $\sigma$-finite measure on a ring extends uniquely to a measure on the generated $\sigma$-ring. The extension is obtained by restricting the induced outer measure to the class of measurable sets. This theorem is the foundation for constructing Lebesgue measure from interval length.
