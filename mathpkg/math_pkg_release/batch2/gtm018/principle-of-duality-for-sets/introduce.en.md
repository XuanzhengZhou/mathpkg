The principle of duality is a meta-theorem: every set-theoretic identity has a dual identity obtained by systematically swapping $\cup \leftrightarrow \cap$, $\subset \leftrightarrow \supset$, and $0 \leftrightarrow X$. This halves the number of proofs needed in set-theoretic arguments.
