Every Borel set in a locally compact topological group is contained in a $\sigma$-compact full subgroup. This theorem is crucial for measure theory on topological groups because it allows one to reduce problems about arbitrary Borel sets to the $\sigma$-compact setting, where many regularity properties (such as inner regularity by compact sets) hold. The proof is constructive: starting from a compact neighborhood of the identity, one builds an increasing sequence $D_n$ of compact sets whose union forms the desired subgroup.
