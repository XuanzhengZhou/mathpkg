If $Z$ is a full subgroup of a topological group $X$, then not only is $Z$ itself open, but every union of left cosets of $Z$ is simultaneously open and closed. Consequently, the quotient space $X/Z$ carries the discrete topology. This result shows that in a topological sense, the full subgroup $Z$ absorbs all the non-trivial topology of $X$, and what remains is purely algebraic (the coset structure).
