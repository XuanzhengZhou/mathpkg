For an extended real valued function $f$, the **positive part** is $f^+ = f \cup 0$ and the **negative part** is $f^- = -(f \cap 0)$, where $f \cup g = \frac{1}{2}(f + g + |f - g|)$ and $f \cap g = \frac{1}{2}(f + g - |f - g|)$. Then $f = f^+ - f^-$ and $|f| = f^+ + f^-$. The positive and negative parts of a measurable function are measurable; conversely, a function with measurable positive and negative parts is measurable.
