Exercise (5e) of Section 34 shows that a specific linear transformation on the second coordinate preserves measurability in product spaces. For any measurable set $E$ in $X \times Y$ and $\alpha > 0$, the set obtained by applying the transformation $(x,y) \mapsto (x, \alpha y + \beta)$ to preimages of $E$ is measurable. The proof uses the fact that the class of sets with this property contains all measurable rectangles and forms a $\sigma$-ring.
