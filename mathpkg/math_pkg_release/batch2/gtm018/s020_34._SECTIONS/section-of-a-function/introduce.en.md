A section of a function $f$ on a product space $X \times Y$ is obtained by fixing one variable and treating the resulting expression as a function of the remaining variable. The $X$-section $f_x(y) = f(x,y)$ is defined on the section $E_x$ of the domain $E$ of $f$. This concept is central to Fubini's theorem, where one integrates a section of a function over one factor space at a time.
