Let $(X, S)$ and $(Y, T)$ be measurable spaces and let $(X \times Y, S \times T)$ be their Cartesian product. If $E$ is any subset of $X \times Y$ and $x$ is any point of $X$, the set
$$E_x = \{ y : (x, y) \in E \}$$
is called an $X$-section of $E$ (determined by $x$). Similarly, for $y \in Y$, the set
$$E^y = \{ x : (x, y) \in E \}$$
is called a $Y$-section of $E$ (determined by $y$). A section of a set in a product space is a subset of one of the component spaces, not a set in the product space.
