This theorem establishes a fundamental connection between stochastic independence and integrability on product spaces. For two independent functions that are almost everywhere non-vanishing, the integrability of the individual functions is equivalent to the integrability of their product, and the integral of the product equals the product of the integrals. The result follows from the product measure representation of independence and Fubini's theorem.
