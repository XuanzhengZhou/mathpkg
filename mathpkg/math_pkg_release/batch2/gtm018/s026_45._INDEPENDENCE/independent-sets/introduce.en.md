Stochastic independence of measurable sets is the fundamental notion that formalizes the intuitive idea of unrelated events in probability theory. A class of sets is independent if the probability of any finite intersection equals the product of the individual probabilities. Pairwise independence (the $n=2$ case) is a necessary condition for full independence of a class, but it is not sufficient — independence requires the product rule to hold for all finite subcollections.
