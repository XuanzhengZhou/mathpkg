The theorem is an easy application of the relations established above between product spaces and independence. Suppose that $Y_{ij}$ is the real line, $i = 1, \dots, k$, $j = 1, \dots, n_i$, and $Y = \prod_{ij} Y_{ij}$. If we write

$$T(x) = \bigl(f_{11}(x), \dots, f_{1n_1}(x), \dots, f_{k1}(x), \dots, f_{kn_k}(x)\bigr),$$

$$g_{ij}(y_{11}, \dots, y_{1n_1}, \dots, y_{k1}, \dots, y_{kn_k}) = y_{ij}$$

and

$$g_i = \phi_i(g_{i1}, \dots, g_{in_i}),$$

then $f_i = g_i \circ T$, $i = 1, \dots, k$. Since the independence of the $g_i$'s is obvious, the independence of the $f_i$'s follows.
