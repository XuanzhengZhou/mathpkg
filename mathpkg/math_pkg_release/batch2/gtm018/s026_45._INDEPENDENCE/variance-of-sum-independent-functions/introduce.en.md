This theorem establishes the additivity of variance for independent random variables. Under independence, the variance of the sum equals the sum of the variances because the cross term $\int fg \, d\mu$ factors as $\int f \, d\mu \cdot \int g \, d\mu$ by Theorem A, causing the covariance to vanish. This result is fundamental to the behavior of sums of independent random variables in probability theory.
