If $\{E_i : i = 1, \dots, n\}$ is a finite class of measurable sets of positive measure, then

$$\mu(E_1 \cap \cdots \cap E_n) = \mu(E_1) \, \mu_{E_1}(E_2) \, \cdots \, \mu_{E_1 \cap \cdots \cap E_{n-1}}(E_n).$$

This result is known as the \text{multiplication theorem for conditional probabilities}.
