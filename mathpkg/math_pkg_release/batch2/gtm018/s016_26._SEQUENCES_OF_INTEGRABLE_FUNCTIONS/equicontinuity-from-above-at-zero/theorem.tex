If $\{\nu_n\}$ is a sequence of finite valued set functions on a class $\mathbf{E}$ of sets, the terms of the sequence are \textbf{equicontinuous from above at $0$} if, for every decreasing sequence $\{E_n\}$ of sets in $\mathbf{E}$ for which $\lim_n E_n = 0$, and for every positive number $\epsilon$, there exists a positive integer $m_0$ such that if $m \geq m_0$, then $|\nu_n(E_m)| < \epsilon$, $n = 1, 2, \dots$.
