For any fixed positive integer $m$, the sequence $\{|f_n - f_m|\}$ is a mean fundamental sequence of integrable simple functions which converges in measure to $|f - f_m|$, and, therefore,

$$\int |f - f_m| \, d\mu = \lim_{n \to \infty} \int |f_n - f_m| \, d\mu.$$

The fact that the sequence $\{f_n\}$ is mean fundamental implies the desired result.
