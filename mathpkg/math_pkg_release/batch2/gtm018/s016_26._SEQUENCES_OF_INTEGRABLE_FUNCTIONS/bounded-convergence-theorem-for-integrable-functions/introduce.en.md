Lebesgue's bounded convergence theorem states that if a sequence of integrable functions is pointwise bounded by a fixed integrable function and converges either in measure or almost everywhere, then the limit function is integrable and the convergence also holds in mean (the $L^1$ norm). This theorem is a precursor to the more general Lebesgue dominated convergence theorem and provides a fundamental tool for interchanging limits and integrals.
