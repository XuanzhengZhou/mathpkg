A signed measure $\nu$ is absolutely continuous with respect to $\mu$ if $\nu$ vanishes on all sets where the total variation of $\mu$ vanishes. Intuitively, $\nu$ has "no mass" on $\mu$-null sets. This concept is the key hypothesis of the Radon-Nikodym theorem, which asserts that absolute continuity is equivalent to the existence of a density function.
