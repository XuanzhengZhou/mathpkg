Both Borel sets and Baire sets can be constructed from compact sets (respectively compact $G_{\delta}$'s) by taking proper differences, finite disjoint unions, and then generating the $\sigma$-ring. This structural result is analogous to the generation of Borel sets from semiclosed intervals on the real line.
