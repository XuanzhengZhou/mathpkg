A purely atomic measure is a finite Borel measure whose entire mass is concentrated on a countable set. In other words, the complement of some countable set has measure zero. This concept captures the idea of a measure that is entirely discrete, composed solely of point masses (atoms) at a countable collection of points. Purely atomic measures form one of the three fundamental components in the decomposition theorem for finite Borel measures.
