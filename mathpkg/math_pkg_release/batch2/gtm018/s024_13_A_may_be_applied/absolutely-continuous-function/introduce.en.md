Absolute continuity is a stronger condition than uniform continuity, defined for real-valued functions of a real variable. It requires that the total variation of $f$ over any finite collection of disjoint intervals can be made arbitrarily small, provided the sum of the interval lengths is sufficiently small. This concept is fundamental in measure theory because it characterizes precisely those functions that are indefinite integrals of Lebesgue integrable functions, and it connects to the notion of absolute continuity of measures via the distribution function $f_\nu$.
