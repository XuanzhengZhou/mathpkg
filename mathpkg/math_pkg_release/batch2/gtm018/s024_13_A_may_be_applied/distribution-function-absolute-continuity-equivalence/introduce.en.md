Theorem D establishes the fundamental equivalence between the analytic notion of absolute continuity of a function and the measure-theoretic notion of absolute continuity of a measure with respect to Lebesgue measure. For a finite Borel measure $\nu$, its distribution function $f_\nu$ is absolutely continuous if and only if $\nu$ is absolutely continuous with respect to Lebesgue measure $\mu$. This theorem provides the crucial link that allows the Radon–Nikodym theorem to be applied to distribution functions.
