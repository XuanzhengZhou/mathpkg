The distribution function $f_\nu$ of a finite Borel measure $\nu$ on the real line encodes measure-theoretic information in an analytic form. Theorem A establishes a precise correspondence between the continuity of $f_\nu$ at a point $x$ and the absence of an atomic mass at $x$. This characterization bridges the analytic property of continuity with the measure-theoretic concept of atoms, and is fundamental to understanding the relationship between measures and their distribution functions developed throughout this chapter.
