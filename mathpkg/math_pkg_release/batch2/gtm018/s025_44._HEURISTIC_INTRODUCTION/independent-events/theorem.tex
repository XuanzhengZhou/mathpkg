Two events $E$ and $F$ are called independent (statistically or stochastically independent) if
$$\mu_F(E) = \mu(E),$$
or equivalently, in the more symmetric form,
$$\mu(E \cap F) = \mu(E) \, \mu(F).$$
The intuitive meaning is that knowledge of the occurrence of $F$ contributes nothing to knowledge of the probability of $E$.
