The conclusion of the heuristic reduction is that probability theory should be studied as the theory of Boolean $\sigma$-algebras of sets. In this model, events are sets, and all logical relations between events (complement, union, intersection) correspond to set-theoretic operations. The $\sigma$-algebra requirement (closure under countable unions) is needed to handle events involving infinite sequences of trials, such as the event $E = \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} E_n$ that a game ends in finitely many steps.
