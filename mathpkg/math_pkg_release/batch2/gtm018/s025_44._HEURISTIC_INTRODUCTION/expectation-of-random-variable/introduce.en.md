The expectation $\mathbb{E}[f]$ (also called the average or mean value) of a random variable $f$ is its integral with respect to the probability measure: $\mathbb{E}[f] = \int_X f \, d\mu$. For a fair six-sided die with $f(x) = x$ and equal probabilities $p_i = 1/6$, the expectation is $\frac{1}{6}(1+2+3+4+5+6) = 3.5$. If the die is loaded with general probabilities $p_x$, the expectation becomes the weighted sum $\sum_{x=1}^{6} x p_x$. The general definition via the Lebesgue integral handles random variables with infinite ranges and continuous distributions.
