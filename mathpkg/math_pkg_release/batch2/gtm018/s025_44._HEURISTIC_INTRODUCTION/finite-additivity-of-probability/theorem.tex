If $E$ and $F$ are mutually exclusive events, i.e., $E \cap F = \emptyset$, then
$$\mu(E \cup F) = \mu(E) + \mu(F).$$
More generally, if $\{E_1, E_2, \ldots, E_n\}$ is a finite collection of pairwise disjoint events, then
$$\mu\left(\bigcup_{i=1}^{n} E_i\right) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \mu(E_i).$$
Together with $\mu(X) = 1$, this makes $\mu$ a finitely additive measure.
