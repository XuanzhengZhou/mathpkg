The finite additivity axiom states that for any two mutually exclusive events $E$ and $F$ (i.e., $E \cap F = \emptyset$), the probability of the union is the sum of the probabilities: $\mu(E \cup F) = \mu(E) + \mu(F)$. This is motivated by the observation that if $E$ occurs a fraction $p$ of the time and $F$ occurs a fraction $q$ of the time, and they never occur together, then their union must occur a fraction $p + q$ of the time. Together with $\mu(X) = 1$, this axiom makes $\mu$ a finitely additive measure.
