The impossible event $\emptyset$ (also denoted $0$) is the event that never occurs under any circumstances. The certain event $X$ is the event that occurs with every trial. In the set-theoretic model of probability, $X$ corresponds to the entire sample space and $\emptyset$ to the empty set. For the die-rolling experiment, $X = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}$ and $\emptyset$ is the event "the number $x$ is not equal to any of the first six positive integers."
