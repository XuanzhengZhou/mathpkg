Two events $E$ and $F$ are called mutually exclusive (or incompatible) if the occurrence of one precludes the occurrence of the other. In the set-theoretic model, mutually exclusive events correspond to disjoint sets: $E \cap F = \emptyset$. This property is fundamental to the additivity axiom of probability, which identifies when probabilities of combined events simply add.
