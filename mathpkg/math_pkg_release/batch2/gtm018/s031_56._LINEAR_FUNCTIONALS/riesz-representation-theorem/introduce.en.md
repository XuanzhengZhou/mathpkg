The Riesz Representation Theorem (Theorem D) is the central result of Section 56. It asserts that every positive linear functional $\Lambda$ on the class $\mathcal{L}$ of continuous functions vanishing at infinity on a locally compact Hausdorff space $X$ can be represented as integration against a uniquely determined regular Borel measure $\mu$. The proof constructs $\mu$ from the content $\lambda(C) = \inf\{\Lambda(f) : C \subset f \in \mathcal{L}_+\}$, applies Theorem B to obtain $\int f \, d\mu \leq \Lambda(f)$, and uses Theorem C together with a truncation argument to establish the reverse inequality.
