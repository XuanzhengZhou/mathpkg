Theorem B establishes the inequality $\int f \, d\mu \leq \Lambda(f)$ for every nonnegative $f \in \mathcal{L}_+$, where $\mu$ is the Borel measure induced by the content $\lambda$ defined from $\Lambda$. The proof decomposes $f$ into a sum of truncations via the "layer cake" representation $f = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n f_i$, applies Theorem A to bound each piece, and lets $n \to \infty$. This is the easier half of the Riesz Representation Theorem; the reverse inequality requires Theorem C and Theorem D.
