Theorem A is the first major step in Halmos's proof of the Riesz Representation Theorem. It establishes that the set function $\lambda(C) = \inf\{\Lambda(f) : C \subset f \in \mathcal{L}_+\}$, defined from a positive linear functional $\Lambda$, is a regular content on the class of compact sets. Regularity is crucial because it guarantees the induced Borel measure $\mu$ behaves well under the extension procedure. The final inequality $\mu(U) \leq \Lambda(f)$ for $U \subset f$ is the key technical estimate used throughout the subsequent proofs of Theorems B, C, and D.
