Given a positive linear functional $\Lambda$ on $\mathcal{L}$, the induced content $\lambda$ assigns to each compact set $C$ the infimum of $\Lambda(f)$ over all nonnegative functions $f \in \mathcal{L}_+$ that equal $1$ on $C$. This auxiliary set function serves as the bridge between the algebraic data of $\Lambda$ and the measure-theoretic representing measure $\mu$: Theorem A proves $\lambda$ is a regular content, and its induced Borel measure $\mu$ is the unique regular Borel measure satisfying $\Lambda(f) = \int f \, d\mu$.
