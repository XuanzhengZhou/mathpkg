If $\Lambda$ is a positive linear functional on $\mathcal{L}$ and $f \geq g$ pointwise for $f, g \in \mathcal{L}$, then $f - g \in \mathcal{L}_+$, so $\Lambda(f - g) \geq 0$ by positivity, and linearity gives $\Lambda(f) \geq \Lambda(g)$. This trivial observation is used repeatedly throughout the proof of the Riesz Representation Theorem to compare integrals and functional values.
