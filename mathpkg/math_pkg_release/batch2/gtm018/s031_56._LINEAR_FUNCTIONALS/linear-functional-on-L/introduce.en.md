A linear functional on the class $\mathcal{L}$ of continuous functions vanishing at infinity is a real-valued map $\Lambda : \mathcal{L} \to \mathbb{R}$ that is linear with respect to pointwise addition and scalar multiplication. This concept generalizes the notion of integrating against a measure — every Baire measure induces a positive linear functional via $\Lambda(f) = \int f \, d\mu$, and the Riesz Representation Theorem (Theorem D of this section) establishes the converse: every positive linear functional arises from integration against a uniquely determined regular Borel measure.
