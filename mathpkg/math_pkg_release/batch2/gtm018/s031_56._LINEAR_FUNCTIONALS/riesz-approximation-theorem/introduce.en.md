Theorem C is the technical heart that enables the reverse inequality $\Lambda(f) \leq \int f \, d\mu$ in the Riesz Representation Theorem. Given any compact set $C$ and $\epsilon > 0$, it provides a "test function" $f_0 \in \mathcal{L}_+$ that equals $1$ on $C$, is bounded by $1$, and satisfies $\Lambda(f_0) \leq \int f_0 \, d\mu + \epsilon$. Combined with Theorem B (which gives $\int f \, d\mu \leq \Lambda(f)$), this yields the equality $\Lambda(f) = \int f \, d\mu$ for all $f \in \mathcal{L}$.
