A measure is the central object of study in measure theory. It is a countably additive, non-negative, extended real valued set function defined on a ring of sets, with the empty set assigned measure zero. Every measure is automatically finitely additive, since any finite disjoint union can be extended to a countable union by appending copies of the empty set. Measures generalize the notions of length, area, and volume, and serve as the foundation for Lebesgue integration.
