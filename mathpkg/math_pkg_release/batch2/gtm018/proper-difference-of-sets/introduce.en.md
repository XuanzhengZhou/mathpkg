The difference $E - F$ of two sets is called proper when $E$ contains $F$, i.e., $E \supset F$. The concept of proper difference is particularly useful in the theory of rings of sets, where closure under proper differences (along with other operations) characterizes ring structure. For instance, a nonempty class of sets closed under unions and proper differences is a ring.
