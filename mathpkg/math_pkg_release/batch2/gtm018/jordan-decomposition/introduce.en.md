The Jordan Decomposition Theorem represents every signed measure uniquely as the difference $\mu = \mu^+ - \mu^-$ of two ordinary (non-negative) measures, called the positive and negative variations. The total variation $|\mu| = \mu^+ + \mu^-$ is then a measure that dominates $|\mu(E)|$ for all measurable $E$. This decomposition is fundamental for the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
