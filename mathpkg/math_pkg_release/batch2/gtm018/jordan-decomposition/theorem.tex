If $\mu$ is a signed measure on a $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$, then there exist two uniquely determined measures $\mu^+$ and $\mu^-$ on $\mathbf{S}$ such that $\mu = \mu^+ - \mu^-$ and, for every $E$ in $\mathbf{S}$, $\mu^+(E) = \mu(E \cap A)$ and $\mu^-(E) = -\mu(E \cap B)$, where $A$ and $B$ form a Hahn decomposition for $\mu$. The measures $\mu^+$ and $\mu^-$ are called the upper and lower variations of $\mu$; the measure $|\mu| = \mu^+ + \mu^-$ is called the total variation of $\mu$.
