Let $\nu$ be any finite signed measure on the class of all Baire sets in a locally compact topological group $X$. Then there exists a Baire set $N_\nu$ such that $\nu(E) = \nu(E \cap N_\nu)$ for every Baire set $E$. If $\lambda$ and $\nu$ are any two such finite signed measures, their convolution $\lambda * \nu$ is defined, for every Baire set $E$, by

$$(\lambda * \nu)(E) = \int_{N_\lambda \times N_\nu} \chi_E(xy) \, d(\lambda \times \nu)(x,y).$$

If $\lambda$ and $\nu$ are the indefinite integrals (with respect to Haar measure $\mu$) of the integrable functions $f$ and $g$ respectively, then $\lambda * \nu$ is the indefinite integral of $h$, where

$$h(y) = \int f(x) g(x^{-1}y) \, d\mu(x).$$
