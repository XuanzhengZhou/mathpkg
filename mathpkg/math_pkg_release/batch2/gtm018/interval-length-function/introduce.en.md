The length function $\mu([a,b)) = b - a$ on semiclosed intervals is the canonical example of a measure. Halmos uses it to motivate the general theory: he proves it is countably additive on the semiring $\mathbf{P}$ and then extends it uniquely to a finite measure on the generated ring $\mathbf{R}$.
