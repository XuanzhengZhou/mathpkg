An additive set function is the most basic form of a measure-like function on a class of sets. It requires that the measure of the disjoint union of two sets equals the sum of their individual measures, provided the union also lies in the class. This property serves as the foundational requirement from which finite and countable additivity are built. Additivity together with non-negativity and the condition $\mu(0) = 0$ leads to the definition of a measure on a ring.
