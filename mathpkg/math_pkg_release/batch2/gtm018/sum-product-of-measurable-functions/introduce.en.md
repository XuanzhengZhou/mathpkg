If $f$ and $g$ are finite valued measurable functions, then their sum $f+g$ and product $fg$ are measurable. The sum follows from $\{x: f(x)+g(x) < c\} = \{x: f(x) < c - g(x)\}$ and Theorem A of \S18. The product follows from $fg = \frac{1}{4}[(f+g)^2 - (f-g)^2]$ and Theorem B (since squaring is Borel measurable).
