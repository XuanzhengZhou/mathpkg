An atom in a measure ring is a non-zero element that contains no strictly smaller non-zero elements. This is the algebraic analogue of an atom (or "point mass") in a measure space. The presence or absence of atoms is crucial for the classification of measure algebras, as the isomorphism theorem applies to non-atomic measure algebras, which possess no indivisible elements.
