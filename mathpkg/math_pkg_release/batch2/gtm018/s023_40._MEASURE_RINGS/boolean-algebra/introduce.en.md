A Boolean algebra is a Boolean ring that possesses a maximal element $X$ (different from $0$), which contains every element of the ring. This additional structure makes Boolean algebras the natural algebraic abstraction of algebras of sets, where the maximal element corresponds to the underlying set $X$. Boolean algebras arise as the domains of normalized and totally finite measures in the theory of measure algebras.
