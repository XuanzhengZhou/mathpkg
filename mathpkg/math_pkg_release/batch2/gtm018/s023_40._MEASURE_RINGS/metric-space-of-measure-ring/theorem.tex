If $(\mathbf{S},\mu)$ is a measure ring, we shall denote by $\mathbf{s}$ [or $\mathbf{s}(\mu)$] the set of elements of finite measure in $\mathbf{S}$ and, for any two elements $E$ and $F$ in $\mathbf{s}$, we shall write

$$\rho(E,F) = \mu(E \Delta F).$$

It is easy to verify that the function $\rho$ is a metric for $\mathbf{s}$; we shall call $\mathbf{s}$ the metric space associated with $(\mathbf{S},\mu)$, or, simply, the metric space of $(\mathbf{S},\mu)$. We shall also use the symbol $\mathbf{s}(\mu)$ for the metric space associated with the measure ring $(\mathbf{S}(\mu),\mu)$ of a measure space $(X,\mathbf{S},\mu)$.
