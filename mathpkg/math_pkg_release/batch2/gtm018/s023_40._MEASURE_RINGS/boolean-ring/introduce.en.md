A Boolean ring is an algebraic ring in which every element is idempotent, meaning $E \cdot E = E$ for all elements. It can be characterized by two commutative, associative operations where addition satisfies $E+E=0$ for all $E$, and there exists a unique zero element. The canonical example is a ring of subsets of a set, where symmetric difference serves as addition and intersection serves as multiplication. Boolean rings form the algebraic foundation upon which measure rings and measure algebras are built.
