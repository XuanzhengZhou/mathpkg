Given a measure space $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$, the associated measure ring $\mathbf{S}(\mu)$ is obtained by redefining equality in the $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ modulo sets of measure zero: $E = F[\mu]$ if $\mu(E \Delta F) = 0$. Under this new equality, $\mathbf{S}$ remains a Boolean $\sigma$-ring and $\mu$ becomes a positive measure. This construction is the bridge that allows the algebraic abstraction of measure theory from concrete measure spaces.
