A Boolean $\sigma$-ring is a Boolean ring enriched with the property that every countable collection of elements possesses a union (and consequently an intersection). This closure under countable operations makes Boolean $\sigma$-rings the natural algebraic counterpart of $\sigma$-rings of sets. They serve as the domain on which measures are defined in the context of measure rings.
