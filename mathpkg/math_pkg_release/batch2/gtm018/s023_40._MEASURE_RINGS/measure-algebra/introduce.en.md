A measure algebra is a Boolean algebra equipped with a positive measure, making it simultaneously a measure ring and a Boolean algebra. Measure algebras are the natural setting for the isomorphism theorem: every separable, non-atomic, normalized measure algebra is isomorphic to the measure algebra of the unit interval. The notions of total finiteness and $\sigma$-finiteness apply to measure algebras just as they do to measure spaces.
