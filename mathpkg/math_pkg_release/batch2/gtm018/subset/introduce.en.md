The subset relation $E \subset F$ captures the idea that one set is contained in another: every element of $E$ is also an element of $F$. This fundamental relation is reflexive ($E \subset E$) and transitive, and provides the basis for defining set equality. Halmos establishes it at the very beginning of Measure Theory as the essential language for all subsequent constructions.
