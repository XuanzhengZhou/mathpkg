Let $Y$ be the unit interval, $T$ the class of all Borel subsets of $Y$, and $\nu$ Lebesgue measure on $T$. Let $\{X_n\}$ be a decreasing sequence of thick subsets of $Y$ such that $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} X_n = \emptyset$, and let $S_n = X_n \cap T$. If $E$ is a $\{1, \ldots, n\}$-cylinder and $F$ is a measurable subset of $Y_n$, then $T_n^{-1}(F)$ is a $\{1, \ldots, n\}$-cylinder and

$$\mu(E \cap T_n^{-1}(F)) = \mu(E) \mu T_n^{-1}(F) = \int_F \mu(E) d\mu_n,$$

and therefore $p(E, T_n(x))$ is a constant ($= \mu(E)$) almost everywhere $[\mu]$. It follows from Theorem B that $\chi_E(x) = \mu(E) \; [\mu]$, and hence that $\mu(E)$ is either $0$ or $1$.
