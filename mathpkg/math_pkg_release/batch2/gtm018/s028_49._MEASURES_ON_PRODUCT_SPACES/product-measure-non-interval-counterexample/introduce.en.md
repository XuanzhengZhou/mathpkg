This exercise constructs a counterexample showing that Theorem A (the Kolmogorov extension theorem) fails when the coordinate spaces are not required to be intervals. The construction uses a decreasing sequence of thick subsets of the unit interval with empty intersection, together with Lebesgue measure. The conditional probabilities $p(E, T_n(x))$ turn out to be constant almost everywhere, forcing each cylinder set to have measure either 0 or 1 by Theorem B, which contradicts the existence of a non-trivial extension.
