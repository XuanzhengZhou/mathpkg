This zero-one law states that for any measurable set in an infinite product space, the conditional probabilities given the first $n$ coordinates converge almost everywhere to the indicator function of the set. Equivalently, as more coordinate information is revealed, the conditional probability of a fixed event converges to either 0 or 1. The proof establishes almost uniform convergence by approximating the set with a finite-dimensional cylinder and bounding the measure of the symmetric difference, then applying the conditional probability smoothing property.
