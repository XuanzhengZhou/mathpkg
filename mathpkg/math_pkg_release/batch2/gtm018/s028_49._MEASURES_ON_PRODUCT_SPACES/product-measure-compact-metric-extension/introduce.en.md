This exercise observes that Theorem A extends from the unit interval to arbitrary compact metric spaces, since the proof only uses the compactness of the product (via Tychonoff's theorem) and the ability to approximate Borel sets by closed subsets. By a trivial compactification (e.g., embedding the real line into a compact interval), Theorem A also holds when each $X_n$ is the real line. The question of whether Theorem A holds for arbitrary compact (non-metric) spaces is left open.
