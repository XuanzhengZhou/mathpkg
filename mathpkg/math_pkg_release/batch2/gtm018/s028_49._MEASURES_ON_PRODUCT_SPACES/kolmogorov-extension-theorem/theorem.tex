Suppose that, for each positive integer $n$, $X_n$ is the closed unit interval and $S_n$ is the class of all Borel sets in $X_n$, and write
$$(X, S) = \bigtimes_{n=1}^{\infty}(X_n, S_n).$$
Let $F_n$ be the $\sigma$-ring of all measurable $\{1, \cdots, n\}$-cylinders in $X$ and let $F = \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} F_n$ be the ring of all measurable, finite dimensional subsets of $X$.

If $\mu$ is a set function on $F$ such that, for each positive integer $n$, $\mu$ is a probability measure on $F_n$, then $\mu$ has a unique extension to a probability measure on $S$.
