The indefinite integral $\nu(E) = \int_E f d\mu$ is absolutely continuous with respect to $\mu$. For simple functions this is clear: if $|f| \leq c$, then $|\int_E f d\mu| \leq c\mu(E)$. For general integrable functions this follows by approximation, and it is the essential property that characterizes indefinite integrals in the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
