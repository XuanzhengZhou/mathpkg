A measure is totally $\sigma$-finite when defined on an algebra and the whole space $X$ has $\sigma$-finite measure. Lebesgue measure on $\mathbb{R}^n$ is the canonical example of a totally $\sigma$-finite measure. This is the natural setting for Fubini's theorem and the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
