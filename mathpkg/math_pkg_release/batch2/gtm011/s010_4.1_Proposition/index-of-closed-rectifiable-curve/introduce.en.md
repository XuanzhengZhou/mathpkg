The index (or winding number) $n(\gamma; a)$ of a closed rectifiable curve $\gamma$ around a point $a \notin \{\gamma\}$ is defined as the normalized contour integral of $(z-a)^{-1}$. By Proposition 4.1, this quantity is always an integer. The index counts how many times the curve winds around the point $a$, with counterclockwise orientation contributing positively and clockwise orientation contributing negatively. It is a fundamental topological invariant in complex analysis, underpinning results such as the Argument Principle and the Residue Theorem.
