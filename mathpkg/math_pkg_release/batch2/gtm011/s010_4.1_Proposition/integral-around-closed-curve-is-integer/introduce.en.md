This proposition states that for any closed rectifiable curve $\gamma$ and any point $a$ not on the curve, the normalized contour integral of $(z-a)^{-1}$ yields an integer. The proof reduces to the smooth case via Lemma 1.19, then uses the function $g(t) = \int_0^t \gamma'(s)/(\gamma(s)-a)\,ds$ and shows that $e^{-g}(\gamma-a)$ is constant, forcing $g(1) = 2\pi i k$ for some integer $k$. This result is the foundation for the definition of the winding number (or index) of a curve around a point.
