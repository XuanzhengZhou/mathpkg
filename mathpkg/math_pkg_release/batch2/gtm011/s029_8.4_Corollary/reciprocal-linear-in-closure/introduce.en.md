Lemma 1.10 is the critical technical step in Grabiner's proof of Runge's Theorem. It establishes that the simple pole functions $(z-a)^{-1}$ for any $a$ outside the compact set $K$ belong to $B(E)$, the closed algebra of uniform limits of rational functions with poles constrained to the set $E$. The proof uses a connectedness argument: the set $V$ of points $a$ for which $(z-a)^{-1} \in B(E)$ is shown to be both open (via geometric series expansion) and closed in $\mathbb{C} - K$, and since $E \subset V$, the condition that $E$ meets each component of $\mathbb{C}_\infty - K$ forces $V$ to be the whole complement.
