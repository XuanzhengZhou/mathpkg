Proposition 8.5 expresses the product $\zeta(z)\Gamma(z)$ as a single improper integral $\int_0^\infty (e^t-1)^{-1} t^{z-1} dt$ valid for $\operatorname{Re} z > 1$. This identity bridges the Dirichlet series definition of the zeta function and the integral representation of the Gamma function, using Corollary 8.4 to guarantee the integral defines an analytic function. The proposition serves as the foundation for extending $\zeta(z)$ meromorphically to the entire complex plane through the manipulation of this integral.
