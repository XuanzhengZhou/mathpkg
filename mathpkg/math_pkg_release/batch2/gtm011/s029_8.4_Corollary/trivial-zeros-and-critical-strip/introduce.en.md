Definition 8.16 introduces two fundamental concepts in the study of the Riemann zeta function. The trivial zeros are the points $z = -2, -4, -6, \ldots$ where $\zeta$ vanishes; these follow directly from the functional equation and the poles of $\Gamma(1-z)$ at positive integers. The critical strip $\{z : 0 \leq \operatorname{Re} z \leq 1\}$ is the vertical band in the complex plane where all non-trivial zeros of $\zeta$ must lie, setting the stage for the Riemann Hypothesis which asserts that all non-trivial zeros lie on the line $\operatorname{Re} z = 1/2$.
