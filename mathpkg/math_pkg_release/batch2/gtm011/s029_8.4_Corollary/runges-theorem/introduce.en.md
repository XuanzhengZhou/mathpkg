Runge's Theorem is the central result of Chapter VIII of Conway's book, generalizing the fact that analytic functions on disks are uniform limits of polynomials. It states that any function analytic on a neighborhood of a compact set $K \subset \mathbb{C}$ can be uniformly approximated on $K$ by rational functions whose poles are constrained to lie in a prescribed set $E$. The only requirement on $E$ is that it meets every connected component of the extended complement $\mathbb{C}_\infty \setminus K$, which ensures enough poles are available to capture the singular behavior in each "hole" of $K$. The proof, due to S. Grabiner, reformulates the problem as showing that a certain algebra $B(E)$ of uniform limits of rational functions equals the full space of functions analytic on a neighborhood of $K$.
