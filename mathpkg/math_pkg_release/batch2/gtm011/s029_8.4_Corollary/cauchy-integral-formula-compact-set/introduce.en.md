Proposition 1.1 provides a constructive version of the Cauchy Integral Formula tailored to an arbitrary compact subset $K$ of a region $G$. Unlike the classical formulation which assumes a given curve, this proposition shows that one can always find a finite collection of straight line segments $\gamma_k$ in $G \setminus K$ whose sum of integrals reproduces the Cauchy formula. The proof uses a fine rectangular grid: rectangles intersecting $K$ are selected, their boundaries are taken with positive orientation, and common sides (traversed in opposite directions) cancel, leaving exactly the $\gamma_k$ that border the grid approximation of $K$.
