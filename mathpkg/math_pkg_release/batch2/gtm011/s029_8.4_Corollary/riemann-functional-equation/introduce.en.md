Theorem 8.14 establishes the meromorphic continuation of the Riemann zeta function to the entire complex plane, showing that $\zeta(z)$ has a single simple pole at $z=1$ with residue $1$. The theorem also proves Riemann's celebrated functional equation $\zeta(z) = 2^z \pi^{z-1} \sin(\pi z/2) \Gamma(1-z) \zeta(1-z)$, which relates the values of $\zeta$ at $z$ and $1-z$ and reveals deep symmetry. From this functional equation one deduces that $\zeta$ vanishes at the negative even integers $-2, -4, -6, \ldots$ (the trivial zeros) and that all other zeros must lie in the critical strip $0 \leq \operatorname{Re} z \leq 1$.
