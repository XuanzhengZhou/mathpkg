\textbf{Lemma 1.5.} Let $\gamma$ be a rectifiable curve and let $K$ be a compact set such that $K \cap \{\gamma\} = \varnothing$. If $f$ is a continuous function on $\{\gamma\}$ and $\varepsilon > 0$, then there exists a rational function $R(z)$ having all its poles on $\{\gamma\}$ and such that
$$\left| \int_{\gamma} \frac{f(w)}{w-z} \, dw - R(z) \right| < \varepsilon$$
for all $z$ in $K$.
