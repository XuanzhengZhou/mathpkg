Gauss's formula expresses the gamma function as a limit of rational functions involving factorials and powers of $n$. It follows from the Weierstrass product representation of $1/\Gamma(z)$ by manipulating the partial products and using properties of Euler's constant $\gamma$. This limit representation is particularly useful for deriving the functional equation $\Gamma(z+1) = z\Gamma(z)$ and for studying the analytic continuation of the gamma function.
