This corollary follows by iterating the functional equation $\Gamma(z+1) = z\Gamma(z)$ exactly $n$ times. The formula $\Gamma(z+n) = z(z+1)\cdots(z+n-1)\Gamma(z)$ expresses the gamma function shifted by an integer $n$ in terms of $\Gamma(z)$ multiplied by a rising factorial. It is particularly useful for computing residues of the gamma function at its poles and for establishing the analytic continuation.
