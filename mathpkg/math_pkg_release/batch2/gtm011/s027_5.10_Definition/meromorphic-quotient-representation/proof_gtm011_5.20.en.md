Let $\{a_j\}$ be the poles of $f$ and let $m_j$ be the order of the pole at $a_j$. According to the preceding theorem (the Weierstrass product construction for arbitrary open sets), there is an analytic function $h$ on $G$ with a zero of multiplicity $m_j$ at each $z = a_j$ and with no other zeros.

Consider the product $h f$. At each pole $a_j$, the function $h$ has a zero of order $m_j$ while $f$ has a pole of order $m_j$, so $h f$ has a removable singularity at each $a_j$. Define $g$ to be the analytic extension of $h f$ to all of $G$. Then $g$ is analytic in $G$ and $f = g/h$ on $G \setminus \{a_j\}$, hence everywhere on $G$ as meromorphic functions.
