The logarithmic derivative $\Gamma'(z)/\Gamma(z)$ (the digamma function $\psi(z)$) has a simple series representation obtained by differentiating the logarithm of the Weierstrass product for $1/\Gamma(z)$. The formula expresses $\psi(z)$ in terms of Euler's constant $\gamma$, a simple pole term $-1/z$, and a convergent series. This representation is uniformly convergent on compact sets avoiding the poles, enabling term-by-term differentiation to obtain higher derivatives.
