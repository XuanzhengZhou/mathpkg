The Weierstrass Product Theorem solves the problem of constructing an entire function whose zeros are exactly a prescribed sequence $\{a_n\}$ (tending to infinity). The construction uses elementary factors $E_{p_n}(z/a_n)$, where the integers $p_n$ must grow sufficiently fast relative to the growth of $|a_n|$ to guarantee uniform convergence on compact sets via Lemma 5.11. The choice $p_n = n-1$ always works, though smaller $p_n$ may suffice for rapidly growing $\{|a_n|\}$.
