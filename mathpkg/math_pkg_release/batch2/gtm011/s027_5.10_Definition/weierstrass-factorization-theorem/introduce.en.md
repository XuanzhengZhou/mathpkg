The Weierstrass Factorization Theorem states that every entire function can be factored as a product of its zeros (via elementary factors) multiplied by $z^m$ (accounting for a zero at the origin) and an exponential factor $e^{g(z)}$ where $g$ is entire. This is the complex-analytic analogue of factoring a polynomial into linear factors. The theorem follows immediately from the Weierstrass Product Theorem by noting that $f(z)/h(z)$ is an entire function with no zeros (hence $e^{g(z)}$), where $h(z)$ is the product construction for the zeros of $f$.
