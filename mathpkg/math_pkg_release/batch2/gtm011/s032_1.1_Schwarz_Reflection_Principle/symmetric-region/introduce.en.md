A region $G \subset \mathbb{C}$ is symmetric with respect to the real axis if it is closed under complex conjugation, i.e., $G = G^*$ where $G^* = \{\bar{z} : z \in G\}$. For such a region, the real axis slices $G$ into an upper half $G_+$, a lower half $G_-$, and the real trace $G_0 = G \cap \mathbb{R}$. This geometric setup is essential for the Schwarz Reflection Principle, which extends a function defined on $G_+ \cup G_0$ analytically across the real axis to all of $G$.
