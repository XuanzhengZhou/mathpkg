Let $\gamma$ be a simple closed rectifiable curve with the property that there is a point $a$ such that for all $z$ on $\gamma$ the line segment $[a, z]$ intersects $\{\gamma\}$ only at $z$; i.e. $[a, z] \cap \{\gamma\} = \{z\}$. Define a point $w$ to be \textit{inside} $\gamma$ if $[a, w] \cap \{\gamma\} = \varnothing$ and let $G$ be the collection of all points that are inside $\gamma$.

Show that $G$ is a region and $G^- = G \cup \{\gamma\}$.
