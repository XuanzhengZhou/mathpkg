An entire function is a complex-valued function that is analytic (holomorphic) at every point in the complex plane $\mathbb{C}$. In other words, it is differentiable in the complex sense everywhere on $\mathbb{C}$. Entire functions are also sometimes called "integral functions". They generalize the notion of polynomials to infinite degree, admitting power series expansions with infinite radius of convergence.
