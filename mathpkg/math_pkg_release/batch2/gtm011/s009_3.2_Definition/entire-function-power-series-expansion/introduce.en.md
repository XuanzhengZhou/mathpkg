Any entire function admits a power series expansion centered at the origin, $f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n$, whose radius of convergence is infinite. This follows from the fact that $\mathbb{C}$ contains disks $B(0; R)$ of arbitrarily large radius $R$ together with the standard result that an analytic function on a disk has a power series expansion there. Consequently, entire functions can be viewed as "polynomials of infinite degree."
