This result follows from Theorem 2.8 and the fact that $\mathbb{C}$ contains $B(0; R)$ for arbitrarily large $R$. Since $f$ is entire, it is analytic on every disk $B(0; R) \subset \mathbb{C}$. By Theorem 2.8, $f$ has a power series expansion $f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n$ converging on $B(0; R)$. Because $R$ can be taken arbitrarily large, the radius of convergence is infinite.
