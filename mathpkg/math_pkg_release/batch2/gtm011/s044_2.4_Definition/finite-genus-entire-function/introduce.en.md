The genus of an entire function refines the notion of rank by also accounting for the exponential factor $g(z)$ in the Weierstrass factorization $f(z) = z^m e^{g(z)} P(z)$. When the canonical product $P$ is taken in standard form (using the rank of $f$), the function $g$ is determined up to an additive constant, so its degree is well-defined. The genus is then $\mu = \max(p, q)$ where $p$ is the rank and $q = \deg g$. This single integer controls the growth rate of the entire function.
