An entire function $f$ has \textbf{finite genus} if $f$ has finite rank and if
$$f(z) = z^m e^{g(z)} P(z),$$
where $P$ is in standard form, and $g$ is a polynomial. If $p$ is the rank of $f$ and $q$ is the degree of the polynomial $g$, then
$$\mu = \max(p, q)$$
is called the \textbf{genus} of $f$. The genus is well-defined because once $P$ is in standard form, $g$ is uniquely determined up to adding a multiple of $2\pi i$, so the degree of $g$ is determined.
