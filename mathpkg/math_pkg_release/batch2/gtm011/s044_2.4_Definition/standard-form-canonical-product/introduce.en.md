The standard form of a canonical product is defined with respect to a given entire function $f$. When $f$ has finite rank $p$, its canonical product $P(z) = \prod E_p(z/a_n)$ using precisely the rank $p$ as the genus of the Weierstrass primary factors is called the standard form. This choice makes the factorization $f(z) = z^m e^{g(z)} P(z)$ unique up to adding an integer multiple of $2\pi i$ to $g$.
