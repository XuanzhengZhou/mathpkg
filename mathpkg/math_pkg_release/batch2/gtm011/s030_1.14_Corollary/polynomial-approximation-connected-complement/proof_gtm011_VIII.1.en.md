Let $E = \{\infty\}$. Since $\mathbb{C}_{\infty} - G$ is connected and $E = \{\infty\}$ meets $\mathbb{C}_{\infty} - G$ (as $\infty \in \mathbb{C}_{\infty} - G$), Corollary 1.14 applies. A rational function whose only pole is at $\infty$ is a polynomial. Therefore $R(G, \{\infty\})$ is exactly the set of polynomials, and Corollary 1.14 gives that polynomials are dense in $H(G)$.
