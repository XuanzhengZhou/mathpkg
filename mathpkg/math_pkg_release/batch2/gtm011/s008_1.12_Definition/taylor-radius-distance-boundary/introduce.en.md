Corollary 2.11 refines Theorem 2.8 by identifying the maximal radius of convergence of the Taylor series about $a$ as exactly the distance from $a$ to the boundary of the domain $G$. Since $B(a; R) \subset G$, Theorem 2.8 applies directly. This corollary explains why power series of analytic functions "stop converging" precisely when they encounter a singularity on the boundary of the domain of analyticity.
