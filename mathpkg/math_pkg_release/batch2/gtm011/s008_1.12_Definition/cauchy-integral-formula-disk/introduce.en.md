Proposition 2.6 establishes Cauchy's Integral Formula for a disk: if $f$ is analytic on an open set containing a closed disk, then the value of $f$ at any interior point is given by a contour integral of $f(w)/(w-z)$ around the boundary circle. This remarkable formula shows that the values of an analytic function inside a disk are completely determined by its values on the boundary. The proof uses Leibniz's rule for differentiation under the integral sign and the fact that $\int_0^{2\pi} e^{is}/(e^{is} - z) ds = 2\pi$ for $|z| < 1$.
