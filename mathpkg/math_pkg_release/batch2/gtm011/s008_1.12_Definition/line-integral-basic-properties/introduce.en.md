Proposition 1.17 collects three elementary but essential properties of complex line integrals: reversal of orientation introduces a sign change, the absolute value of an integral is bounded by the integral of the absolute value (which in turn is bounded by the variation times the supremum), and the integral is invariant under simultaneous translation of both the path and the argument of the integrand. These facts are used ubiquitously in estimates throughout complex analysis.
