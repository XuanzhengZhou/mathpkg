Cauchy's Estimate provides a powerful bound on the $n$-th derivative of an analytic function at a point, in terms of the maximum modulus of the function on a disk centered at that point. The estimate follows from Corollary 2.13 (Cauchy's Integral Formula for derivatives) combined with the basic integral estimate of Proposition 1.17. It is a fundamental tool used to prove Liouville's theorem, the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra, and many other results in complex analysis.
