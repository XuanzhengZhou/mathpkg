Corollary 2.12 captures one of the most striking differences between real and complex analysis: if a complex function is once differentiable (analytic), it is automatically infinitely differentiable. This follows immediately from Theorem 2.8, since a convergent power series can be differentiated term-by-term arbitrarily many times within its radius of convergence. In real analysis, a function can be once differentiable without even having a continuous derivative, making this result particularly remarkable.
