By Theorem 2.8, $f$ can be represented as a convergent power series $f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (z - a)^n$ in a neighborhood of each point $a \in G$. A convergent power series is infinitely differentiable term-by-term within its radius of convergence, hence $f$ is infinitely differentiable at every point of $G$.
