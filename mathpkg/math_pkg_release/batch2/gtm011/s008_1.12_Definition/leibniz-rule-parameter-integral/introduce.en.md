Proposition 2.15 (Leibniz's Rule) justifies differentiating under the integral sign for complex contour integrals with a parameter. If the integrand $\varphi(w, z)$ is continuous jointly in $(w, z)$ and has a continuous partial derivative with respect to $z$, then the parameter-dependent integral $g(z) = \int_\gamma \varphi(w, z) dw$ is analytic and its derivative is obtained by differentiating under the integral sign. This result is essential for proving Cauchy's Integral Formula (Proposition 2.6) and for establishing that certain functions defined by integrals are analytic.
