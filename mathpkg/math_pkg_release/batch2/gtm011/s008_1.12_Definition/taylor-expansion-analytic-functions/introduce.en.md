Theorem 2.8 is one of the central results of complex analysis: every analytic function admits a convergent power series (Taylor) expansion about any point in its domain, with coefficients given by the derivatives at that point. The proof combines Cauchy's Integral Formula with the geometric series expansion of $1/(w-z)$ and uniform convergence to interchange summation and integration. This theorem reveals that analyticity and power series representability are equivalent concepts in complex analysis, a fact that has no analogue in real analysis.
