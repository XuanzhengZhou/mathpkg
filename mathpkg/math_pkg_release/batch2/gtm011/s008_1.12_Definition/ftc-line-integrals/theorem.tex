Let $G$ be open in $\mathbb{C}$ and let $\gamma$ be a rectifiable path in $G$ with initial and end points $\alpha$ and $\beta$ respectively. If $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ is a continuous function with a primitive $F: G \to \mathbb{C}$, then

$$\int_{\gamma} f = F(\beta) - F(\alpha).$$

(Recall that $F$ is a primitive of $f$ when $F' = f$.)
