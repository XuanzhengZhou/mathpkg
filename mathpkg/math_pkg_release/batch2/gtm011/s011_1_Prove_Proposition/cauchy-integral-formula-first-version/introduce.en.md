Cauchy's Integral Formula (First Version) expresses the value of an analytic function at a point $a$ as a contour integral involving the function and a factor $1/(z-a)$. The formula applies when the winding number of the closed rectifiable curve $\gamma$ around every point outside $G$ is zero. This foundational result bridges the local behavior of analytic functions with global contour integration and is proved elegantly using Liouville's Theorem applied to an auxiliary entire function.
