This theorem provides a formula for the $k$-th derivative $f^{(k)}(a)$ of an analytic function in terms of a contour integral involving $f(z)/(z-a)^{k+1}$. It generalizes the second version of Cauchy's Integral Formula by differentiating under the integral sign. The formula involves the winding numbers of multiple closed rectifiable curves and shows that analytic functions are infinitely differentiable, with their derivatives expressed through the same type of integral representation.
