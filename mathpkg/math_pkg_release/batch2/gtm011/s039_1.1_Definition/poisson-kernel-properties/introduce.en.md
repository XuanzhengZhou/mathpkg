This proposition collects the four essential properties of the Poisson kernel that make it an approximate identity on the circle. Part (a) states that $P_r$ integrates to $1$, part (b) gives positivity and symmetry, part (c) shows $P_r$ is strictly decreasing away from $\theta = 0$, and part (d) asserts that the kernel concentrates at the origin as $r$ approaches $1$. These properties are used to prove that the Poisson integral solves the Dirichlet problem on the disk.
