The Mean Value Property (MVP) is an integral averaging condition that characterizes harmonic functions among continuous functions. A continuous function on a region satisfies the MVP if its value at any point equals the average of its values over every sufficiently small circle centered at that point. The MVP abstracts the essential averaging behavior of harmonic functions and will later be shown to be equivalent to harmonicity for continuous functions.
