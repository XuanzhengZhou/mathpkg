This theorem provides the promised converse to the Mean Value Theorem (1.4): any continuous function possessing the Mean Value Property must be harmonic. The proof constructs a harmonic function on a small disk that matches $u$ on the boundary using the Poisson integral, then applies the Maximum Principle corollary to show the two functions coincide in the interior. This establishes that the MVP is not merely a consequence of harmonicity but an equivalent characterization of harmonic functions among continuous functions.
