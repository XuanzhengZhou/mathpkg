This can be proved either by appealing to Theorem 1.7 (the Second Maximum Principle) or by considering the function $-u$ and appealing to Theorem 1.6 (the First Maximum Principle).

For the second approach: If $u(a) \leq u(z)$ for all $z \in G$, then $-u(a) \geq -u(z)$ for all $z \in G$. Since $u$ has the MVP, so does $-u$. Applying Theorem 1.6 to $-u$, we conclude that $-u$ is constant on $G$, hence $u$ is constant on $G$.
