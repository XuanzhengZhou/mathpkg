Let $D$ be a disk such that $\overline{B}(a; r) \subset D \subset G$ and let $f$ be an analytic function on $D$ such that $u = \operatorname{Re} f$. It is easy to deduce from Cauchy's Integral Formula that

$$f(a) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} f(a + re^{i\theta}) \, d\theta.$$

By taking the real part of each side of this equation we complete the proof.
