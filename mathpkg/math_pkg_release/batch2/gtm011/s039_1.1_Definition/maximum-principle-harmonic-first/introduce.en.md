The First Version of the Maximum Principle states that a continuous function with the Mean Value Property on a region cannot attain a global maximum unless it is constant. The proof uses the MVP to show that the set of points where $u$ equals its maximum is both open and closed, hence by connectedness of the region it is either empty or the whole region. This is the direct analog of the Maximum Modulus Theorem for analytic functions.
