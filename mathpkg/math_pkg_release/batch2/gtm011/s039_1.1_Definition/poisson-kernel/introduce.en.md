The Poisson kernel $P_r(\theta)$ is a family of positive functions on the unit circle parametrized by $0 \leq r < 1$, which serves as the fundamental building block for solving the Dirichlet problem on the unit disk. It can be expressed as a Fourier series or in closed form as a rational function of $\cos\theta$ and $r$, and it equals the real part of the analytic function $(1+z)/(1-z)$. The Poisson kernel acts as an approximate identity as $r \to 1^-$, concentrating mass near $\theta = 0$.
