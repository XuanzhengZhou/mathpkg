The proof is left as an exercise for the reader (see text following the statement of Theorem 3.21).

A sketch of the proof: Since a Möbius transformation preserves the cross ratio, for any point $z$ we have $(Tz, Tz_1, Tz_2, Tz_3) = (z, z_1, z_2, z_3)$. The right side of $\Gamma_1$ with respect to $(z_1, z_2, z_3)$ is $\{z: \operatorname{Im} (z, z_1, z_2, z_3) > 0\}$, and the image of this set under $T$ is $\{Tz: \operatorname{Im} (Tz, Tz_1, Tz_2, Tz_3) > 0\}$, which is precisely the right side of $\Gamma_2$ with respect to $(Tz_1, Tz_2, Tz_3)$. The same argument applies to the left side.
