Let $\gamma: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ be of bounded variation and suppose that $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is continuous. Then there is a complex number $I$ such that for every $\epsilon > 0$ there is a $\delta > 0$ such that when $P = \{t_0 < t_1 < \ldots < t_m\}$ is a partition of $[a, b]$ with $\|P\| = \max \{(t_k - t_{k-1}): 1 \leq k \leq m\} < \delta$, then

$$\left| I - \sum_{k=1}^{m} f(\tau_k) [\gamma(t_k) - \gamma(t_{k-1})] \right| < \epsilon$$

for whatever choice of points $\tau_k$, $t_{k-1} \leq \tau_k \leq t_k$. This number $I$ is called the \textit{Riemann-Stieltjes integral} of $f$ with respect to $\gamma$ over $[a, b]$ and is designated by

$$I = \int_{a}^{b} f \, d\gamma = \int_{a}^{b} f(t) \, d\gamma(t).$$
