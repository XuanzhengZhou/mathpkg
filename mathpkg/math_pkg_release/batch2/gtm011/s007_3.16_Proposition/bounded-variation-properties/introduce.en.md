This proposition collects elementary properties of functions of bounded variation. Part (a) states that refining a partition can only increase the variation sum. Part (b) shows that the set of functions of bounded variation forms a vector space over $\mathbb{C}$, and the total variation is subadditive with respect to scalar multiplication. Together with the fact that non-decreasing real functions are of bounded variation, these properties provide a rich supply of functions for the Riemann-Stieltjes integral.
