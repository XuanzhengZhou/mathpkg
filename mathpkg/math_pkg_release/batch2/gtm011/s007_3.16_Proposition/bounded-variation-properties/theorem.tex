Let $\gamma: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ be of bounded variation. Then:

(a) If $P$ and $Q$ are partitions of $[a, b]$ and $P \subset Q$ then $v(\gamma; P) \leq v(\gamma; Q)$;

(b) If $\sigma: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is also of bounded variation and $\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{C}$ then $\alpha\gamma + \beta\sigma$ is of bounded variation and

$$V(\alpha\gamma + \beta\sigma) \leq |\alpha|\, V(\gamma) + |\beta|\, V(\sigma).$$
