This proposition provides a computable formula for the total variation of a smooth curve: if $\gamma$ has a continuous derivative on $[a,b]$, then its total variation equals the integral of $|\gamma'(t)|$. This establishes that every smooth curve is of bounded variation and connects the geometric notion of arc length (total variation) with the analytic notion of integration. The result is fundamental for reducing Riemann-Stieltjes integrals to ordinary Riemann integrals.
