This definition introduces the concept of orientation for a circle in the extended complex plane $\mathbb{C}_\infty$. An orientation is specified by an ordered triple of distinct points on the circle, which gives a direction — intuitively, one "walks" from $z_1$ to $z_2$ to $z_3$. Using the cross ratio, the right side and left side of the oriented circle are defined via the sign of the imaginary part of the cross ratio. This formalism allows one to distinguish the "inside" from the "outside" of a circle, a distinction not apparent on the Riemann sphere.
