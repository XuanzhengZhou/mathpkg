This proposition asserts that Möbius transformations preserve the symmetry relation between points with respect to circles. If $z$ and $z^*$ are symmetric with respect to a circle $\Gamma_1$, and a Möbius transformation $T$ maps $\Gamma_1$ onto another circle $\Gamma_2$, then the images $Tz$ and $Tz^*$ are symmetric with respect to $\Gamma_2$. The proof follows directly from the invariance of the cross ratio under Möbius transformations and the definition of symmetry via complex conjugation of cross ratios.
