This fundamental definition introduces functions of bounded variation on a real interval, a class of functions essential for defining the Riemann-Stieltjes integral along paths in $\mathbb{C}$. A function $\gamma: [a,b] \to \mathbb{C}$ has bounded variation if the sums of absolute increments over all partitions are uniformly bounded. The total variation $V(\gamma)$ is the supremum of these sums. A non-decreasing real-valued function is of bounded variation, and $\gamma$ is of bounded variation iff its real and imaginary parts are.
