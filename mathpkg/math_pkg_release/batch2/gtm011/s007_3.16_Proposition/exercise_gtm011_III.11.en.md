Show that the definition of symmetry (3.17) does not depend on the choice of points $z_2, z_3, z_4$. That is, show that if $\omega_2, \omega_3, \omega_4$ are also in $\Gamma$ then equation (3.18) is satisfied if and only if $(z^*, \omega_2, \omega_3, \omega_4) = \overline{(z, \omega_2, \omega_3, \omega_4)}$.
