(a) Let $GL_2(\mathbb{C}) =$ all invertible $2 \times 2$ matrices with entries in $\mathbb{C}$ and let $\mathcal{M}$ be the group of Möbius transformations. Define $\varphi: GL_2(\mathbb{C}) \to \mathcal{M}$ by $\varphi\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} = \frac{az+b}{cz+d}$. Show that $\varphi$ is a group homomorphism of $GL_2(\mathbb{C})$ onto $\mathcal{M}$. Find the kernel of $\varphi$.

(b) Let $SL_2(\mathbb{C})$ be the subgroup of $GL_2(\mathbb{C})$ consisting of all matrices of determinant $1$. Show that the image of $SL_2(\mathbb{C})$ under $\varphi$ is all of $\mathcal{M}$. What part of the kernel of $\varphi$ is in $SL_2(\mathbb{C})$?
