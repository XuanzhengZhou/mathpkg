If $\text{Re } z_n > 0$ for all $n$ then the infinite product $\prod z_n$ is said to converge absolutely if the series $\sum \log z_n$ converges absolutely.

According to Proposition 5.2 and the fact that absolute convergence of a series implies convergence, we have that absolute convergence of a product implies the convergence of the product. Similarly, if a product converges absolutely then any rearrangement of the terms of the product results in a product which is still absolutely convergent.
