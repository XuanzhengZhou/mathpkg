The Phragmén-Lindelöf Theorem extends the Maximum Modulus Principle to unbounded regions by introducing an auxiliary bounded analytic function $\varphi$ that never vanishes. The extended boundary $\partial_\infty G$ is partitioned into two sets $A$ and $B$: on $A$, $|f|$ is bounded by $M$ in the usual limsup sense, while on $B$, the product $|f(z)||\varphi(z)|^\eta$ is controlled for arbitrarily small positive exponents $\eta$. By forming $F(z) = f(z) \varphi(z)^\eta \kappa^{-\eta}$ and applying the Third Version of the Maximum Modulus Theorem, the bound $M$ propagates to the entire region $G$ after taking $\eta \to 0$.
