This corollary refines the Phragmén-Lindelöf principle for angular sectors by weakening the growth hypothesis: instead of requiring $|f(z)| \leq P \exp(|z|^b)$ with $b < a$, it suffices that $|f(z)| \leq P \exp(\delta |z|^a)$ for arbitrarily small $\delta > 0$. The proof multiplies $f$ by $\exp(-\varepsilon z^a)$ to create an auxiliary function $F$ that vanishes along the positive real axis, then applies Corollary 4.2 separately to the upper and lower half-sectors. A crucial maximality argument shows the supremum $M_1$ of $F$ on the positive real axis cannot exceed $M$.
