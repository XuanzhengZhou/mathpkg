This proposition states that the continuous image of a compact set is compact. It generalizes the well-known result from metric spaces to arbitrary topological spaces. The proof uses the definition of compactness via open covers: given an open cover of $f(K)$ in $\Omega$, its preimage under the continuous function $f$ yields an open cover of $K$ in $X$, from which a finite subcover can be extracted by compactness of $K$. This finite subcover maps forward to a finite subcover of $f(K)$.
