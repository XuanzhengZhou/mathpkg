A Hausdorff space is a topological space in which any two distinct points can be separated by disjoint open sets. This separation axiom, also known as the $T_2$ axiom, ensures that the topology has enough open sets to distinguish points. Every metric space is a Hausdorff space, but there exist topological spaces that fail to be Hausdorff. The Hausdorff property is fundamental in analysis and topology, as it guarantees uniqueness of limits of convergent sequences and is assumed in most standard settings.
