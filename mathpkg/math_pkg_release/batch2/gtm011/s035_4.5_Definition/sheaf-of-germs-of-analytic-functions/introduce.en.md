The sheaf of germs of analytic functions on an open set $G \subset \mathbb{C}$ is the collection of all pairs $(z, [f]_z)$ where $z \in G$ and $[f]_z$ is the germ of an analytic function at $z$. A germ $[f]_z$ is the equivalence class of all function elements $(g, B)$ with $z \in B$ that agree with $f$ on the component of $B \cap D$ containing $z$. The projection map $\rho$ sends each germ to its base point. The stalk $\rho^{-1}(z)$ over $z$ consists of all germs of analytic functions at $z$. This sheaf construction provides the natural setting for studying analytic continuation and the complete analytic function, and can be equipped with a topology induced by a neighborhood system to make it a topological space (specifically, a Hausdorff space).
