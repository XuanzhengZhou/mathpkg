For an open set $G$ in $\mathbb{C}$ let

$$S(G) = \{(z, [f]_z) : z \in G, f \text{ is analytic at } z\}.$$

Define a map $\rho: S(G) \to \mathbb{C}$ by $\rho(z, [f]_z) = z$. Then the pair $(S(G), \rho)$ is called the \textbf{sheaf of germs of analytic functions} on $G$. The map $\rho$ is called the \textbf{projection map}; and for each $z$ in $G$, $\rho^{-1}(z) = \rho^{-1}(\{z\})$ is called the \textbf{stalk} or \textbf{fiber} over $z$. The set $G$ is called the \textbf{base space} of the sheaf.
