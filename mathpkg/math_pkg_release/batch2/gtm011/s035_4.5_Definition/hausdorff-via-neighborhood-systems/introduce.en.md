This corollary to Proposition 4.17 gives a convenient criterion for checking whether the topology induced by a neighborhood system is Hausdorff. Instead of working with arbitrary open sets, one only needs to check that distinct points can be separated by elements of their respective neighborhood collections $\mathcal{N}_x$ and $\mathcal{N}_y$. This is the neighborhood-system analog of the Hausdorff separation axiom and is particularly useful when a topology is defined through a neighborhood system rather than directly by specifying open sets.
