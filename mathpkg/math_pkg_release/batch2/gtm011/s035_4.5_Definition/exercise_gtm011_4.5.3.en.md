Let $X$ and $\Omega$ be sets and let $\{\mathcal{N}_x : x \in X\}$ and $\{\mathcal{M}_\omega : \omega \in \Omega\}$ be neighborhood systems and let $\mathcal{T}$ and $\mathcal{S}$ be the induced topologies on $X$ and $\Omega$ respectively.

(a) Show that a function $f: X \to \Omega$ is continuous if and only if when $x \in X$ and $\omega = f(x)$, for each $\Delta \in \mathcal{M}_\omega$ there is a $U \in \mathcal{N}_x$ such that $f(U) \subset \Delta$.
