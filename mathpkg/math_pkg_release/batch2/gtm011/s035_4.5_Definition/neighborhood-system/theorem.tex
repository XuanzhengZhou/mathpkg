Let $X$ be a set and suppose that for each point $x$ in $X$ there is a collection $\mathcal{N}_x$ of subsets of $X$ having the following properties:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] for each $U$ in $\mathcal{N}_x$, $x \in U$;
\item[(b)] if $U$ and $V \in \mathcal{N}_x$, there is a $W$ in $\mathcal{N}_x$ such that $W \subset U \cap V$;
\item[(c)] if $U \in \mathcal{N}_x$ and $V \in \mathcal{N}_y$ then for each $z$ in $U \cap V$ there is a $W$ in $\mathcal{N}_z$ such that $W \subset U \cap V$.
\end{enumerate}

Then the collection $\{\mathcal{N}_x : x \in X\}$ is called a \textbf{neighborhood system} on $X$.
