A topological space is connected if it cannot be partitioned into two non-empty disjoint open subsets. Equivalently, the only subset that is both open and closed (clopen) in the space is the space itself or the empty set. This definition generalizes the notion of connectedness from metric spaces to arbitrary topological spaces. Connectedness is a fundamental topological invariant preserved by continuous functions.
