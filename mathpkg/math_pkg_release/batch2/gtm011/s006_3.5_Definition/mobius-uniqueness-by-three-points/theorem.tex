Let $a, b, c$ be three distinct points in $\mathbb{C}_\infty$ and let $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ be three distinct points in $\mathbb{C}_\infty$. Then there exists a unique Möbius transformation $S$ such that
$$S(a) = \alpha, \quad S(b) = \beta, \quad S(c) = \gamma.$$
In particular, a Möbius map is uniquely determined by its action on any three given points in $\mathbb{C}_\infty$.
