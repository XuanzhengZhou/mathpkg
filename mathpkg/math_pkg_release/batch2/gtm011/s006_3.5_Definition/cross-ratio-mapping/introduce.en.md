Given any three distinct points $z_2, z_3, z_4$ in the extended complex plane, there exists a unique Mobius transformation sending them respectively to $1, 0, \infty$. This mapping is given explicitly by piecewise formulas depending on whether any of the points is at infinity. In the generic case where all three are finite, it takes the form $S(z) = \frac{(z-z_3)(z_2-z_4)}{(z-z_4)(z_2-z_3)}$, which is the foundation for the definition of the cross-ratio.
