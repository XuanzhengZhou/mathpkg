Every Mobius transformation $S(z) = \frac{az+b}{cz+d}$ extends naturally to a bijection of the extended complex plane $\mathbb{C}_\infty = \mathbb{C} \cup \{\infty\}$. The point at infinity is mapped to $a/c$, while the pole $-d/c$ is sent to infinity. This extension makes each Mobius transformation a homeomorphism (indeed, a biholomorphic automorphism) of the Riemann sphere, and is essential for understanding their global mapping properties.
