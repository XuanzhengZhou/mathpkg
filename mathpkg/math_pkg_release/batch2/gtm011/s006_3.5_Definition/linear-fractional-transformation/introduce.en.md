A linear fractional transformation is a rational function of the form $S(z) = \frac{az+b}{cz+d}$ with complex coefficients $a, b, c, d$. When the additional condition $ad - bc \neq 0$ is imposed, the transformation becomes a Mobius transformation, which is bijective on the extended complex plane. Linear fractional transformations form the foundational building blocks for understanding conformal automorphisms of the Riemann sphere.
