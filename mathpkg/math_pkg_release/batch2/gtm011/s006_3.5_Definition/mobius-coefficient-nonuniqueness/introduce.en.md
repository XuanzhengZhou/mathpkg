The coefficients $a, b, c, d$ of a Mobius transformation are not uniquely determined by the mapping itself. Multiplying all four coefficients by the same non-zero scalar $\lambda$ yields the identical function, since the factor cancels in the fraction. This reflects the fact that Mobius transformations correspond to elements of the projective linear group $\mathrm{PGL}(2, \mathbb{C})$, i.e., equivalence classes of $2 \times 2$ matrices modulo scalar multiplication.
