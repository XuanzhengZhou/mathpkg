Let $z_1, z_2, z_3, z_4$ be points in $\mathbb{C}_\infty$ with $z_2, z_3, z_4$ distinct. The \text{cross-ratio} $(z_1, z_2, z_3, z_4)$ is defined as the image of $z_1$ under the unique Möbius transformation that sends $z_2$ to $1$, $z_3$ to $0$, and $z_4$ to $\infty$. Equivalently, when all points are finite and distinct,
$$(z_1, z_2, z_3, z_4) = \frac{(z_1 - z_3)(z_2 - z_4)}{(z_1 - z_4)(z_2 - z_3)}.$$
If any of $z_1, z_2, z_3, z_4$ is $\infty$, the cross-ratio is defined by the appropriate limiting value of this formula.
