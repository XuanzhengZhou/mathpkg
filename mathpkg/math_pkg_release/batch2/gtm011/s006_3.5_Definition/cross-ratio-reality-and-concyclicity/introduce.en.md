A fundamental geometric property of the cross-ratio is that four distinct points lie on a generalized circle (a circle or a straight line) in the extended complex plane if and only if their cross-ratio is a real number. This follows because Mobius transformations map generalized circles to generalized circles, and the real axis (together with $\infty$) is itself a generalized circle. The proposition is the foundation for using complex analysis to study circle geometry.
