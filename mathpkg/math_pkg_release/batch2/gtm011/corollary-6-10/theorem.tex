If $\gamma$ is a closed rectifiable curve in $G$ such that $\gamma \sim 0$ then $n(\gamma; w) = 0$ for all $w$ in $\mathbb{C} - G$.

The converse of the above corollary is not valid. That is, there is a closed rectifiable curve $\gamma$ in a region $G$ such that $n(\gamma; w) = 0$ for all $w$ in $\mathbb{C} - G$ but $\gamma$ is not homotopic to a constant curve (Exercise 8).
