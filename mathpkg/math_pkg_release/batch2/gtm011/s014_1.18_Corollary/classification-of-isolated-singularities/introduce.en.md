This corollary provides a complete classification of isolated singularities of a holomorphic function in terms of its Laurent series coefficients. A singularity at $z = a$ is removable precisely when all negative-index coefficients vanish, a pole of order $m$ when $a_{-m}$ is the first non-zero negative-index coefficient, and an essential singularity when infinitely many negative-index coefficients are non-zero. The result follows directly from the Laurent Expansion by analyzing the behavior of $(z-a)^m f(z)$ near the singularity and applying the definition of each singularity type. Combined with the Casorati-Weierstrass Theorem, this corollary forms the foundation for the local study of meromorphic functions and residue calculus.
