Suppose that $f$ has an essential singularity at $z = a$. Prove the following strengthened version of the Casorati--Weierstrass Theorem. If $c \in \mathbb{C}$ and $\epsilon > 0$ are given then for each $\delta > 0$ there is a number $\alpha$, $|c - \alpha| < \epsilon$, such that $f(z) = \alpha$ has infinitely many solutions in $B(a; \delta)$.
