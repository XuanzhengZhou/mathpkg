Let $R > 0$ and $G = \{ z : |z| > R \}$; a function $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ has a removable singularity, a pole, or an essential singularity at infinity if $f(z^{-1})$ has, respectively, a removable singularity, a pole, or an essential singularity at $z = 0$. Also, if $f$ has a pole at infinity, the order of the pole is the order of the pole of $f(z^{-1})$ at $z = 0$.
