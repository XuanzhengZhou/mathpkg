This lemma provides the necessary integral estimates to justify interchanging the sum and integral in the derivation of the functional equation for the Riemann zeta function. Part (a) controls the behavior of the integral $\int (e^t - 1)^{-1} t^{z-1} dt$ near the origin for $\text{Re } z \geq a > 1$, while part (b) controls the tail behavior for $\text{Re } z \leq A$. Together they function as an analogue of Lemma 7.16 (which handled the Gamma function integrals), adapted to the factor $(e^t - 1)^{-1}$ that arises from summing the geometric series in the zeta-Gamma product relation.
