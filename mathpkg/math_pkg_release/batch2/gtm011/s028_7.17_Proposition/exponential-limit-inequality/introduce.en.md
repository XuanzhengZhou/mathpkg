This lemma provides two essential facts used in the Gauss product formula proof for the Gamma function. Part (a) establishes that the classical compound-interest limit $(1 + z/n)^n$ converges to the exponential function $e^z$ uniformly on compact subsets of the complex plane, i.e., in the Fréchet space $H(\mathbb{C})$. The proof relies on the power series expansion of the complex logarithm. Part (b) gives a real-valued inequality bounding $(1 - t/n)^n$ by $e^{-t}$, which is used to control the tail behavior of integrands in the Gamma integral approximations.
