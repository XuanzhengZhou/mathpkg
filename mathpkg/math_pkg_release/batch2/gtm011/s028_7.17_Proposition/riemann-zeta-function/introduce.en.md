The Riemann zeta function $\zeta(z)$ is initially defined for complex numbers with real part strictly greater than $1$ by the absolutely convergent Dirichlet series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n^{-z}$. The uniform convergence on each closed half-plane $\{z: \text{Re } z \geq 1 + \epsilon\}$ guarantees that $\zeta(z)$ is analytic on the right half-plane $\text{Re } z > 1$. The zeta function occupies a central position at the intersection of complex analysis and number theory; its analytic continuation, functional equation, and the distribution of its zeros (the Riemann Hypothesis) constitute some of the deepest problems in mathematics.
