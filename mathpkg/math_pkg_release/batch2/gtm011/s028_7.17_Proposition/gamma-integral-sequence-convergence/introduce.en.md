This proposition establishes that the sequence of proper integrals $f_n(z) = \int_{1/n}^{n} e^{-t} t^{z-1} dt$ defines a family of analytic functions on the right half-plane $G = \{z : \text{Re } z > 0\}$, and that this sequence converges in the topology of $H(G)$. The limit function $f(z) = \lim_{n \to \infty} f_n(z)$ is then taken as the definition of the improper integral $\int_0^\infty e^{-t} t^{z-1} dt$, which is the Gamma function. The proof uses the fact that each $f_n$ is a parameter-dependent integral along a compact line segment (making it analytic by a standard exercise) and that the difference $f_m - f_n$ can be controlled via the decay estimates of Lemma 7.16 to obtain the Cauchy property in the complete space $H(G)$.
