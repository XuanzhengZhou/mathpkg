This topological lemma is a key ingredient in the proof that regions without point-components in their complement admit barriers. Given a closed connected subset $S$ of the complement that touches the boundary at a single point $a$ and contains $\infty$, the component of $\mathbb{C}_\infty - S$ containing $G$ is simply connected. The proof uses that the complement of this component is connected and then invokes a characterization of simple connectivity on the sphere.
