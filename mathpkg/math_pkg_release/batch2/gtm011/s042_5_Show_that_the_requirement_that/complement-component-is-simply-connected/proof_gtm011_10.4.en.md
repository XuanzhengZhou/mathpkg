*Proof.* Let $G_0, G_1, \ldots$ be the components of $\mathbb{C}_\infty - S$ with $G \subset G_0$; note that each $G_n$ is a region in $\mathbb{C}$. If $z \in \partial_\infty G_n$ then $G_n \cup \{z\}$ is connected (Exercise II.2.1). Since $G_n$ is a component it follows that $\partial_\infty G_n \subset S$. By Lemma II.2.6, $G_n \cup S \;(= G_n^- \cup S)$ is connected and, consequently, so is $\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} (G_n \cup S) = \mathbb{C}_\infty - G_0$. In virtue of Theorem VIII.2.2(c), $G_0$ is simply connected. $\square$
