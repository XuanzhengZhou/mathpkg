A Dirichlet Region is a region in the complex plane for which the classical Dirichlet Problem is always solvable. Specifically, for any continuous boundary function on the extended boundary, there exists a continuous extension to the closure of the region that is harmonic in the interior. The punctured disk is a classic example of a region that fails to be a Dirichlet Region, while the disk itself is one.
