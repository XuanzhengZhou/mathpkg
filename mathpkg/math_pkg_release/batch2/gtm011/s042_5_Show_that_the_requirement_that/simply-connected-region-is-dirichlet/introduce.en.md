This corollary states that every simply connected region in the complex plane is a Dirichlet Region. If the region is not the whole plane, then its complement in the extended plane has exactly one component (which contains infinity and is non-degenerate), so Corollary 4.17 applies. The case of the whole plane is trivial since there is no boundary.
