This theorem establishes that Green's Functions are preserved under conformal equivalence: pulling back the Green's Function on the target region via a conformal map yields the Green's Function on the source region with the corresponding singularity. The proof relies on the fact that $\log|f(z)-\alpha|$ and $\log|z-a|$ differ by a harmonic function near $a$, since $f$ is locally a translation composed with a non-vanishing analytic factor.
