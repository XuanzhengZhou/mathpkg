A barrier at a boundary point $a$ of a region $G$ is a family of superharmonic functions defined on annular neighborhoods of $a$. These functions act as "bump functions" near the boundary: they approach $0$ as $z$ approaches $a$ and approach $1$ as $z$ approaches other boundary points in the punctured ball. Barriers are the key tool for controlling the behavior of Perron functions near the boundary and are central to proving that certain regions are Dirichlet Regions.
