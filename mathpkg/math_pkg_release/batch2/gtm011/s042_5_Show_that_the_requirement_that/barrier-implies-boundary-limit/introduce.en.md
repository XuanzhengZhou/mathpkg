This theorem establishes that the existence of a barrier at a boundary point $a$ is sufficient for the Perron solution to attain the prescribed boundary value $f(a)$ as $z$ approaches $a$. The proof uses the barrier functions to construct subharmonic functions in the Perron family $\mathcal{P}(f, G)$ that provide lower bounds for the Perron solution, while symmetry gives upper bounds, yielding the desired limit.
