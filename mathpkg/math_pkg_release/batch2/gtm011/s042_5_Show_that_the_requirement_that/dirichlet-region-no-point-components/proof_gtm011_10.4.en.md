*Proof.* By hypothesis, for each $a \in \partial_\infty G$, the component of $\mathbb{C}_\infty - G$ containing $a$ does not reduce to a point. By Theorem 4.9, $G$ has a barrier at each $a \in \partial_\infty G$. Now let $f: \partial_\infty G \to \mathbb{R}$ be continuous and let $u$ be the Perron solution for $f$. By Theorem 4.3, $\lim_{z \to a} u(z) = f(a)$ for each $a \in \partial_\infty G$. Hence $u$ extends continuously to $G^-$ with the prescribed boundary values, so $G$ is a Dirichlet Region. $\square$
