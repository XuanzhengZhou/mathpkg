The Green's Function $g_a$ on a region $G$ with singularity at $a$ is a fundamental solution that is harmonic everywhere except at $a$, where it has a logarithmic singularity that cancels with $\log|z-a|$ to give a harmonic function. It vanishes on the extended boundary, making it the harmonic function with the smallest possible values among positive superharmonic functions with the same singularity. Green's Functions are conformal invariants and play a central role in solving the Dirichlet Problem via integral representations.
