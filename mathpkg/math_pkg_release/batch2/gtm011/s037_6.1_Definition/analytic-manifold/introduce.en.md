An analytic manifold (also called an analytic surface) is a Hausdorff connected topological space $X$ equipped with an analytic structure $\Phi$ — a collection of coordinate patches covering $X$ whose transition maps $\varphi_a \circ \varphi_b^{-1}$ are analytic. This definition generalizes the notion of a region in the complex plane, allowing one to do complex analysis on spaces that are only locally homeomorphic to open subsets of $\mathbb{C}$. The analytic structure determines which functions on $X$ are considered analytic.
