Proposition 6.6 shows that the graph $\Gamma = \{(z, f(z)) : z \in G\}$ of an analytic function $f$ with nowhere-vanishing derivative can be given a natural analytic structure. Using the local invertibility guaranteed by $f'(z) \neq 0$, one defines coordinate patches $(U_\alpha, \varphi_\alpha)$ where $\varphi_\alpha$ projects onto the second coordinate $f(z)$. This structure makes $(\Gamma, \Phi)$ into an analytic manifold, embedding the complex-analytic properties of $f$ directly into the geometry of its graph.
