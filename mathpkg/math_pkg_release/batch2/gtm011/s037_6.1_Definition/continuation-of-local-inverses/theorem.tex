Let $G$ be a region in the plane and let $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ be an analytic function with non-vanishing derivative. Suppose $a, b \in G$ and $\alpha = f(a), \beta = f(b) \in f(G)$. Let $\Delta_0$ and $\Delta_1$ be disks about $\alpha$ and $\beta$ respectively such that there are analytic functions $g_0: \Delta_0 \to \mathbb{C}$ and $g_1: \Delta_1 \to \mathbb{C}$ with $g_0(\alpha) = a$, $g_1(\beta) = b$, $f(g_0(\zeta)) = \zeta$ for all $\zeta$ in $\Delta_0$, $f(g_1(\zeta)) = \zeta$ for all $\zeta$ in $\Delta_1$. Then there is a path $\sigma$ in $f(G)$ from $\alpha$ to $\beta$ such that $(g_1, \Delta_1)$ is the continuation of $(g_0, \Delta_0)$ along $\sigma$.
