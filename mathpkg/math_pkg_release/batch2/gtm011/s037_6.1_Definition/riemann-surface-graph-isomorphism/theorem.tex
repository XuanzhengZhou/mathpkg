Let $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ be an analytic function with non-vanishing derivative on a region $G$, let $\mathcal{F}$ be the complete analytic function obtained from a local inverse of $f$, and let $(\mathcal{R}, \rho)$ be the Riemann surface of $\mathcal{F}$. If $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{R}) = G$ and $\rho(\mathcal{R}) = f(G)$ then

$$\tau(z, [g]_z) = (g(z), z)$$

defines an isomorphism between the analytic manifold $\mathcal{R}$ and the graph of $f$.
