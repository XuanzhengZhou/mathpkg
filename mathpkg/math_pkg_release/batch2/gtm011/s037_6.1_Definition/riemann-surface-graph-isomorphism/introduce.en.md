Proposition 6.19 establishes a concrete isomorphism between the Riemann surface $(\mathcal{R}, \rho)$ of a complete analytic function $\mathcal{F}$ (obtained from a local inverse of $f$) and the graph of $f$ with its natural analytic structure from Proposition 6.6. The map $\tau(z, [g]_z) = (g(z), z)$ sends a point in $\mathcal{R}$ (a germ of a local inverse) to the corresponding point on the graph. This provides a geometric picture: the Riemann surface of local inverses is isomorphic to the graph of $f$, making abstract Riemann surfaces more tangible.
