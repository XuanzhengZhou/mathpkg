Let $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ be an analytic function with non-vanishing derivative on a region $G$, let $\mathcal{F}$ be the complete analytic function obtained from a local inverse of $f$, and let $(\mathcal{R}, \rho)$ be the Riemann surface of $\mathcal{F}$. If $[g]_a \in \mathcal{F}$ then there is a disk $D$ about $a$ on which $g$ is defined and such that $f(g(z)) = z$ for all $z$ in $D$.
