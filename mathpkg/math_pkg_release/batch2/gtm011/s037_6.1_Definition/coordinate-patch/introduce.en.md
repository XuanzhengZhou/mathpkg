A coordinate patch is the fundamental building block for defining manifolds. On a topological space $X$, a coordinate patch $(U, \varphi)$ consists of an open set $U \subseteq X$ together with a homeomorphism $\varphi$ mapping $U$ onto an open subset of the complex plane. This allows one to transfer local properties of the plane (such as analyticity) to the space $X$, providing local coordinates at each point. A point $a \in X$ is said to be contained in the coordinate patch when $a \in U$.
