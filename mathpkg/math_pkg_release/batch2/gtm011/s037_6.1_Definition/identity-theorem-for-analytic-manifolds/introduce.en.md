The Identity Theorem generalizes from regions in the plane to arbitrary analytic manifolds: if two analytic functions $f, g: X \to \Omega$ agree on a set with a limit point in $X$, then they agree everywhere. The proof reduces to the ordinary Identity Theorem for analytic functions on $\mathbb{C}$ by using local coordinate patches and the connectedness of $X$. This is a powerful uniqueness result that constrains the behavior of analytic functions on manifolds.
