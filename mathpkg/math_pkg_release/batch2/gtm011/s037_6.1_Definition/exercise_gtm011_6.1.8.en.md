Furnish the details of the discussion of the surface for $\sqrt{z}$ at the end of this section. Specifically, construct the Riemann surface of $\sqrt{z}$ using two copies of the plane slit along the negative real axis, define the map $h: X \to \mathcal{R}$ using the principal branch of the square root on Plane 1 and its negative on Plane 2, and prove that the resulting function $h$ is an isomorphism (the space $X$ has a natural analytic structure).
