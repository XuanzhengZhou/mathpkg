Let $(X, \Phi)$ and $(\Omega, \Psi)$ be analytic manifolds. A function $f: X \to \Omega$ is analytic at a point $a \in X$ if there exist coordinate patches $(U, \varphi) \in \Phi$ with $a \in U$ and $(\Lambda, \psi) \in \Psi$ such that $f(U) \subset \Lambda$ and $\psi \circ f \circ \varphi^{-1}$ is an analytic function from $\varphi(U) \subset \mathbb{C}$ into $\mathbb{C}$. The function is analytic on $X$ if it is analytic at each point of $X$.
