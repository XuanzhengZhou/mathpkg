The Maximum Modulus Theorem extends from regions in the plane to analytic manifolds: if $f: X \to \mathbb{C}$ is analytic on an analytic manifold $(X, \Phi)$ and $|f|$ attains a maximum at some point, then $f$ must be constant. This follows from the ordinary Maximum Modulus Principle applied via local coordinate charts, together with the connectedness of $X$. The theorem is fundamental in the study of analytic functions on manifolds, limiting the behavior of non-constant analytic complex-valued functions.
