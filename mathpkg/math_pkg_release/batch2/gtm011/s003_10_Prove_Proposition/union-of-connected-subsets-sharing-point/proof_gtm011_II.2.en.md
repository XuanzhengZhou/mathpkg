Suppose that $D = A \cup B$ where $A$ and $B$ are open and closed, disjoint subsets of $D$. Since each $D_j$ is connected, either $D_j \subset A$ or $D_j \subset B$. Since $x_0 \in D_j$ for every $j$, all $D_j$ must lie in the same set (whichever contains $x_0$). Hence either $A = \varnothing$ or $B = \varnothing$, showing $D$ is connected.
