Define $f: X \to \mathbb{R}$ by $f(x) = d(x, B)$. Since $A \cap B = \varnothing$ and $B$ is closed, $f(a) > 0$ for each $a$ in $A$. But since $A$ is compact there is a point $a$ in $A$ such that $0 < f(a) = \inf \{f(x): x \in A\} = d(A, B)$.
