A set $K \subset X$ is compact iff every collection $\mathcal{F}$ of closed subsets of $K$ with the finite intersection property (f.i.p.) has $\bigcap \{F: F \in \mathcal{F}\} \neq \varnothing$.

Here $\mathcal{F}$ has the \textbf{finite intersection property} if whenever $\{F_1, F_2, \ldots, F_n\} \subset \mathcal{F}$, $F_1 \cap F_2 \cap \ldots \cap F_n \neq \varnothing$.
