An analytic (or holomorphic) function on an open set $G \subset \mathbb{C}$ is one that is continuously complex-differentiable at every point of $G$. Conway's definition initially requires continuous differentiability, though a remarkable theorem proved later (Goursat's theorem) shows that mere differentiability already implies analyticity.
