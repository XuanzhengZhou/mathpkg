Lebesgue's Covering Lemma asserts that for any open cover of a sequentially compact metric space, there exists a uniform "Lebesgue number" $\epsilon > 0$ such that every ball of radius $\epsilon$ is contained entirely within some member of the cover. This is a key tool linking compactness to uniform continuity and total boundedness.
