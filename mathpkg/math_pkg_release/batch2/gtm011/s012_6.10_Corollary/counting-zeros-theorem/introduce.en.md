This theorem, sometimes called the Argument Principle, provides a formula for counting the zeros of an analytic function inside a closed curve (weighted by winding numbers). The key idea is to factor $f(z) = (z-a_1)\cdots(z-a_m)g(z)$ where $g$ is analytic and nonzero, then apply logarithmic differentiation to obtain $\frac{f'(z)}{f(z)} = \sum_{k=1}^m \frac{1}{z-a_k} + \frac{g'(z)}{g(z)}$. Since $g$ is nonzero, $g'/g$ is analytic and its integral over a curve homologous to zero vanishes. The result follows from the definition of the winding number. Applications include another proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra (Exercise 7).
