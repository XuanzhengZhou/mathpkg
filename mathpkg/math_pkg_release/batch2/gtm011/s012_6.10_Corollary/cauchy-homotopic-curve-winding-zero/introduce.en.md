This corollary follows from the second version of Cauchy's Theorem by taking $\gamma_1$ to be a constant path in equation (6.7). It states that if a closed rectifiable curve is homotopic to zero (i.e., can be continuously deformed to a point within $G$), then its winding number about any point outside $G$ must be zero. The converse of this statement is false, as demonstrated by a curve in a doubly punctured plane that has zero winding number around both punctures yet is not homotopic to zero (Exercise 8).
