Let $G = \mathbb{C} - \{a, b\}$, $a \neq b$, and let $\gamma$ be the curve in the figure below.

(a) Show that $n(\gamma; a) = n(\gamma; b) = 0$.

(b) Convince yourself that $\gamma$ is not homotopic to zero. (Notice that the word is "convince" and not "prove". Can you prove it?) Notice that this example shows that it is possible to have a closed curve $\gamma$ in a region such that $n(\gamma; z) = 0$ for all $z$ not in $G$ without $\gamma$ being homotopic to zero. That is, the converse to Corollary 6.10 is false.
