This definition introduces the notion of a curve being homologous to zero, denoted $\gamma \approx 0$. A closed rectifiable curve is homologous to zero if its winding number about every point outside the region $G$ vanishes. Using this terminology, Corollary 6.10 states that $\gamma \sim 0$ (homotopic to zero) implies $\gamma \approx 0$ (homologous to zero). The converse is false, as demonstrated by a curve in a doubly punctured plane (Exercise 8). The homology relation is central to the most general form of Cauchy's Theorem developed in later chapters.
