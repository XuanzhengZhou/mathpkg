This corollary connects the topological property of simple connectedness with the analytic property of having a primitive (antiderivative). Earlier in the chapter, Corollary 1.22 established that if an analytic function has a primitive, then its integral around every closed rectifiable curve vanishes. Now, combined with the Fourth Version of Cauchy's Theorem (6.15), the converse holds in simply connected regions: the vanishing of all contour integrals implies the existence of a primitive. Together with Corollaries 6.16 and 6.17, this result is equivalent to simple connectedness — a region is simply connected if and only if every analytic function on it has a primitive.
