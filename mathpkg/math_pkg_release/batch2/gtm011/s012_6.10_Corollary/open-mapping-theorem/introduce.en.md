The Open Mapping Theorem is a fundamental result in complex analysis stating that non-constant analytic functions map open sets to open sets. The proof relies on the local behavior of analytic functions near points where $f(z) = \alpha$: by choosing a small circle around $a$, the image of the curve under $f$ winds around $\alpha$, forcing a neighborhood of $\alpha$ to be covered by values of $f$. This theorem has important consequences, including the maximum modulus principle and the fact that a one-to-one analytic function has a nowhere-vanishing derivative (Exercise 4).
