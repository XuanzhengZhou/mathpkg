This is the fourth and most general version of Cauchy's Integral Theorem. It states that in a simply connected region, the contour integral of any analytic function over any closed rectifiable curve vanishes. Simple connectedness is a topological condition: every closed curve can be continuously deformed to a point within the region. Examples of simply connected regions include star-shaped regions, the complement of an infinite spiral, and the domain of the principal branch of the logarithm. The theorem is equivalent to simple connectedness — a region is simply connected if and only if this integral property holds for every analytic function on it.
