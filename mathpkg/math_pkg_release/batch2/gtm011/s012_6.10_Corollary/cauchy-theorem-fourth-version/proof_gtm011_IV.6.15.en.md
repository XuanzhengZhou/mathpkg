The proof builds on the earlier versions of Cauchy's Theorem. Since $G$ is simply connected, every closed rectifiable curve $\gamma$ in $G$ is homotopic to zero: $\gamma \sim 0$. By the second version of Cauchy's Theorem (which applies to homotopic curves), for any analytic function $f$ on $G$,

$$\int_{\gamma} f = \int_{\gamma_0} f$$

where $\gamma_0$ is a constant path. But the integral over a constant path is zero, hence $\int_{\gamma} f = 0$.

The converse — that a region satisfying this integral condition for all analytic functions must be simply connected — will be proved in Chapter VIII using the characterization that $G$ is simply connected if and only if $\mathbb{C}_{\infty} - G$ is connected in the extended plane.
