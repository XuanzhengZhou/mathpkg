A convex function on a real interval is a real-valued function whose graph lies below any chord joining two points on the graph. Equivalently, the function lies below the linear interpolation between any two points in its domain. This definition is the starting point for the theory of convexity in real analysis and plays a crucial role in complex analysis through results such as Hadamard's Three Lines Theorem.
