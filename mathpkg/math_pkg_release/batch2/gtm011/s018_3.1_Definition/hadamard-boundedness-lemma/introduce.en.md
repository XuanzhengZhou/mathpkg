This lemma, used in the proof of Hadamard's Three Lines Theorem, states that if a bounded analytic function on a vertical strip has modulus bounded by 1 on the boundary of the strip, then it is bounded by 1 throughout the entire strip. The proof constructs an auxiliary function $g_\epsilon(z) = [1 + \epsilon(z-a)]^{-1}$ that decays along the strip, allowing one to apply the ordinary maximum modulus principle.
