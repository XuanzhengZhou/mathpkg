This is a direct consequence of part (a) of the preceding proposition since the characterization of open sets does not depend on the choice of the sets $\{K_n\}$. Specifically, part (a) characterizes open subsets of $(C(G, \Omega), \rho)$ purely in terms of the existence of a compact set $K$ and a $\delta > 0$ satisfying certain containment conditions, without any reference to the particular exhaustion used to define $\rho$. Since the same characterization applies to $(C(G, \Omega), \mu)$, the two metrics induce identical topologies.
