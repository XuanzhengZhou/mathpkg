The metric $\rho$ on $C(G, \Omega)$ is defined via a compact exhaustion $\{K_n\}$ of $G$ satisfying $K_n \subset \operatorname{int} K_{n+1}$. This corollary establishes that the resulting topology does not depend on which particular exhaustion is chosen: any two such exhaustions produce the same collection of open sets, hence the same topology. The proof follows directly from the characterization of open sets given in part (a) of the preceding proposition, which makes no reference to a specific choice of $\{K_n\}$.
