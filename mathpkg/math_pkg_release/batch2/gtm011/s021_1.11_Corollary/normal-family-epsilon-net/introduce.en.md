This proposition gives a practical, finitary characterization of normal families: $\mathcal{F}$ is normal precisely when, for any compact subset $K$ of $G$ and any prescribed precision $\delta > 0$, the restrictions of functions in $\mathcal{F}$ to $K$ can be covered by finitely many $\delta$-neighborhoods (in the uniform norm on $K$) centered at members of $\mathcal{F}$. This is essentially a total boundedness condition for $\mathcal{F}$ with respect to the topology of uniform convergence on compact sets.
