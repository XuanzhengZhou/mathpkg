This proposition provides an equivalent characterization of normal families in terms of compactness: a family $\mathcal{F} \subset C(G, \Omega)$ is normal precisely when its closure is a compact subset of the metric space $(C(G, \Omega), \rho)$. This bridges the classical definition of normality (sequential precompactness of sequences) with the topological notion of compactness, and is essential for applying general compactness arguments to normal families.
