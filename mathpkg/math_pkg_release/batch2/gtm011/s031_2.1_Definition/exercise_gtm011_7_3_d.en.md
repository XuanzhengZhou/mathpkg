Suppose there is an \(r > 0\) such that \(|a_n - a_m| \geq r\) for all \(n \neq m\). Show that \(\sum |a_n|^{-3} < \infty\). In particular, if the sequence \(\{b_n\}\) is bounded then the series

\[
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{b_n}{z - a_n} + \frac{b_n}{a_n} \sum_{j=0}^{k_n-1} \left( \frac{z}{a_n} \right)^j \right]
\]

with \(k = 2\) converges absolutely.
