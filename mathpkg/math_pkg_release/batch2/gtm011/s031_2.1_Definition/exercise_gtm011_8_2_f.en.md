An ideal \(\mathcal{I}\) is called a **fixed ideal** if \(\mathcal{Z}(\mathcal{I}) \neq \varnothing\); otherwise it is called a **free ideal**. Prove that if \(\mathcal{I} = (\mathcal{S})\) then \(\mathcal{Z}(\mathcal{I}) = \mathcal{Z}(\mathcal{S})\) and that a proper principal ideal is fixed.
