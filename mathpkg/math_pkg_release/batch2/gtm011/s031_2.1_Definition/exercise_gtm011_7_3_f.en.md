Let \(\omega\) and \(\omega'\) be two complex numbers such that \(\operatorname{Im}(\omega'/\omega) \neq 0\). Using the previous parts of this exercise show that the series

\[
\zeta(z) = \frac{1}{z} + \sum\nolimits' \left( \frac{1}{z-w} + \frac{1}{w} + \frac{z}{w^2} \right),
\]

where the sum is over all \(w = 2n\omega + 2n'\omega'\) for \(n, n' = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots\) but not \(w = 0\), is convergent in \(M(\mathbb{C})\).
