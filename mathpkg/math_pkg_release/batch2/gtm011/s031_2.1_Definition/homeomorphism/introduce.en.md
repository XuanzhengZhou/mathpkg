A homeomorphism is a bijective continuous map between metric spaces whose inverse is also continuous. Equivalently, a continuous bijection is a homeomorphism precisely when it is an open map (or a closed map). Two metric spaces are called homeomorphic if there exists a homeomorphism between them. In the context of complex analysis, homeomorphisms are used to relate topological properties: for example, \(\mathbb{C}\) and the open unit disk \(D = \{z : |z| < 1\}\) are homeomorphic via \(f(z) = z(1+|z|)^{-1}\), and any one-one analytic function on an open set gives a homeomorphism onto its image.
