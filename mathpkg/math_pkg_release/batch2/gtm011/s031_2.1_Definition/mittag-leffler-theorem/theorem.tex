Let \(G\) be an open set, \(\{a_k\}\) a sequence of distinct points in \(G\) without a limit point in \(G\), and let \(\{S_k(z)\}\) be the sequence of rational functions given by

\[
S_k(z) = \sum_{j=1}^{m_k} \frac{A_{jk}}{(z - a_k)^j},
\]

where \(m_k\) is some positive integer and \(A_{1k}, \ldots, A_{m_k k}\) are arbitrary complex coefficients. Then there is a meromorphic function \(f\) on \(G\) whose poles are exactly the points \(\{a_k\}\) and such that the singular part of \(f\) at \(a_k\) is \(S_k(z)\).
