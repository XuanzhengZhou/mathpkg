Mittag-Leffler's Theorem is the additive counterpart to Weierstrass's Factorization Theorem. Given an open set \(G \subseteq \mathbb{C}\) and prescribed poles \(\{a_k\}\) with prescribed singular parts (principal parts) \(S_k(z)\), it guarantees the existence of a meromorphic function on \(G\) having exactly those poles and singular parts. The proof uses Runge's Theorem to construct rational functions that cancel the singularities, producing a convergent series in the space of meromorphic functions \(M(G)\).
