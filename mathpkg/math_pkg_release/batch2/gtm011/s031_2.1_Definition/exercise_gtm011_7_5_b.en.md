Let \(\{a_n\}\) be a sequence of distinct points in \(\mathbb{C}\) with no limit point in \(\mathbb{C}\), let \(h\) be the meromorphic function obtained from part (a). Let \(z\) be an arbitrary but fixed point in \(\mathbb{C} \setminus \{a_1, a_2, \ldots\}\). Show that if \(\gamma_1\) and \(\gamma_2\) are any rectifiable curves in \(\mathbb{C} \setminus \{a_1, a_2, \ldots\}\) from \(0\) to \(z\), then there is an integer \(m\) such that

\[
\int_{\gamma_1} h - \int_{\gamma_2} h = 2\pi i m.
\]
