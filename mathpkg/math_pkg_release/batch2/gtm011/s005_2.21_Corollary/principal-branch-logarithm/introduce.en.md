The principal branch of the logarithm is the canonical choice among all possible branches, obtained by restricting the argument to the interval $(-\pi, \pi)$. Its domain is the complex plane with the non-positive real axis removed, creating a branch cut along $(-\infty, 0]$. By Corollary 2.21, it is analytic on this domain with derivative $1/z$. This is the default meaning of $\log z$ throughout complex analysis.
