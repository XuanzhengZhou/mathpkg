Not every harmonic function on an arbitrary domain admits a harmonic conjugate---a counterexample is $\log |z|$ on $\mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\}$. However, on simply connected domains like disks or the whole plane, harmonic conjugates always exist. The proof constructs $v$ explicitly via an integral of $u_x$, then adjusts with a function $\varphi(x)$ chosen to satisfy the second Cauchy-Riemann equation. This theorem requires Leibniz's rule for differentiation under the integral sign.
