Prove the following generalization of Proposition 2.20. Let $G$ and $\Omega$ be open in $\mathbb{C}$ and suppose $f$ and $h$ are functions defined on $G$, $g : \Omega \to \mathbb{C}$ and suppose that $f(G) \subset \Omega$. Suppose that $g$ and $h$ are analytic, $g'(\omega) \neq 0$ for any $\omega \in \Omega$, that $f$ is continuous, $h$ is one-to-one, and that they satisfy $h(z) = g(f(z))$ for $z \in G$. Show that $f$ is analytic. Give a formula for $f'(z)$.
