Let $f : G \to \mathbb{C}$ be an analytic function on an open set $G \subset \mathbb{C}$, and write $f(z) = u(x, y) + i v(x, y)$ where $z = x + iy$ and $u, v : G \to \mathbb{R}$. Then the partial derivatives of $u$ and $v$ satisfy the \textbf{Cauchy-Riemann equations}:

$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}.$$

Moreover, the derivative of $f$ can be expressed in terms of these partial derivatives as

$$f'(z) = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}(x, y) + i \frac{\partial v}{\partial x}(x, y) = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}(x, y) - i \frac{\partial u}{\partial y}(x, y).$$
