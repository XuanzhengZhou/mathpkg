This corollary shows that every analytic function yields two harmonic functions: its real and imaginary parts. Differentiating the Cauchy-Riemann equations and applying equality of mixed partials immediately gives $u_{xx} + u_{yy} = 0$ and $v_{xx} + v_{yy} = 0$. This connection between complex analysis and potential theory is one of the most fruitful in mathematics, linking analytic functions to solutions of Laplace's equation.
