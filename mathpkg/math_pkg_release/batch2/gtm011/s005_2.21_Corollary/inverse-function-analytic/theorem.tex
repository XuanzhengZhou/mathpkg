Let $G$ and $\Omega$ be open subsets of $\mathbb{C}$ and suppose $f : G \to \Omega$, $g : \Omega \to \mathbb{C}$, and $h : G \to \mathbb{C}$ are functions such that $f(G) \subset \Omega$. Suppose that $g$ and $h$ are analytic, $g'(\omega) \neq 0$ for all $\omega \in \Omega$, $f$ is continuous, $h$ is one-to-one, and they satisfy $h(z) = g(f(z))$ for all $z \in G$. Then $f$ is analytic and

$$f'(z) = \frac{h'(z)}{g'(f(z))}.$$

In particular, if $h(z) = z$ (so that $g$ is a local inverse of $f$), then $f$ is analytic and

$$f'(z) = \frac{1}{g'(f(z))}.$$
