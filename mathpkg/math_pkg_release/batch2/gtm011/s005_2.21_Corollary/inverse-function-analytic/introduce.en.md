If an analytic function $g$ has a continuous inverse $f$, then $f$ is also analytic, provided $g'$ never vanishes. This is the complex-analytic analogue of the Inverse Function Theorem from real analysis. The proof uses a clever algebraic manipulation of the difference quotient and the fact that $g'(f(a)) \neq 0$ to show the limit defining $f'(a)$ exists. This result is fundamental for establishing the analyticity of inverse functions such as branches of the logarithm and roots.
