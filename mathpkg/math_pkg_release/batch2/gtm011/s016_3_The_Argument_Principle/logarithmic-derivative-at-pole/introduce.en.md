Proposition 3.2 gives the form of the logarithmic derivative $f'/f$ near a pole of $f$. If $f$ has a pole of order $m$ at $z = a$, then $f'/f$ has a simple pole at $a$ with residue $-m$, plus an analytic remainder. Together with Proposition 3.1, this yields the residue calculus needed to prove the Argument Principle: the integral of $f'/f$ equals $2\pi i$ times (number of zeros minus number of poles).
