Let $G$ be an open set, let $f$ be meromorphic on $G$, and let $g$ be analytic on $G$. Let $\gamma$ be a closed rectifiable curve in $G$ such that $\gamma \sim 0$ in $G$ and no zero or pole of $f$ lies on $\{\gamma\}$. Then
$$
\frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\gamma} g(z) \frac{f'(z)}{f(z)}\,dz = \sum_{j} g(a_j)\, n(\gamma; a_j)\, m(a_j) - \sum_{k} g(b_k)\, n(\gamma; b_k)\, m(b_k)
$$
where $\{a_j\}$ are the zeros of $f$, $\{b_k\}$ are the poles of $f$, $m(a_j)$ is the multiplicity of $a_j$, $m(b_k)$ is the order of $b_k$, and $n(\gamma; z)$ is the winding number.
