The Generalized Argument Principle extends the standard Argument Principle by multiplying the integrand $f'/f$ by an analytic function $g$. The contour integral of $g \cdot f'/f$ then yields a sum of the values of $g$ at the zeros of $f$ minus the values of $g$ at the poles of $f$, each weighted by multiplicity and winding number. A particularly important application is taking $g(z) = z$, which yields a formula for the location of the unique zero of $f(z) - \xi$ inside a contour, leading directly to the inverse function formula (Proposition 3.7).
