Let $G$ be an open set and let $f$ be meromorphic on $G$. Let $\gamma$ be a closed rectifiable curve in $G$ such that $\gamma \sim 0$ in $G$ and no zero or pole of $f$ lies on $\{\gamma\}$. Then
$$
\frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\gamma} \frac{f'(z)}{f(z)}\,dz = \sum_{j} n(\gamma; a_j) \, m(a_j) - \sum_{k} n(\gamma; b_k) \, m(b_k)
$$
where $\{a_j\}$ are the zeros of $f$, $\{b_k\}$ are the poles of $f$, $m(a_j)$ is the multiplicity of the zero $a_j$, $m(b_k)$ is the order of the pole $b_k$, and $n(\gamma; z)$ is the winding number of $\gamma$ around $z$.
