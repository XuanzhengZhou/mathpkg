Proposition 3.1 gives the form of the logarithmic derivative $f'/f$ near a zero of $f$. If $f$ has a zero of order $m$ at $z = a$, then $f'/f$ has a simple pole at $a$ with residue $m$, plus an analytic remainder. This simple formula is the key computational step in proving the Argument Principle, as the integral of $f'/f$ around a contour counts zeros and poles.
