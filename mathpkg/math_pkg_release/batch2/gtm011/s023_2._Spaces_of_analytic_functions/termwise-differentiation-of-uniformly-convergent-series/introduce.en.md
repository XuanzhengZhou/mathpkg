If a series of analytic functions converges uniformly on compact subsets, the sum is analytic and the series may be differentiated term-by-term. For each $k \geq 1$, the $k$-th derivative of the sum equals the sum of the $k$-th derivatives, with the differentiated series again converging uniformly on compact sets. This follows by applying Theorem 2.1 to the sequence of partial sums.
