Since $C(G, \mathbb{C})$ is a complete metric space under the topology of uniform convergence on compact sets, and Theorem 2.1 shows that $H(G)$ is a closed subset of $C(G, \mathbb{C})$ (every limit in $C(G, \mathbb{C})$ of a sequence from $H(G)$ belongs to $H(G)$), it follows immediately that $H(G)$ is itself a complete metric space. A closed subset of a complete metric space is complete.
