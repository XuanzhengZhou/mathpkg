Let $G$ be a region and suppose the sequence $\{f_n\}$ in $H(G)$ converges to $f$ in $H(G)$ (i.e., uniformly on compact subsets). If $f \not\equiv 0$, $\overline{B}(a; R) \subset G$, and $f(z) \neq 0$ for $|z-a| = R$, then there exists an integer $N$ such that for all $n \geq N$, $f$ and $f_n$ have the same number of zeros in $B(a; R)$, counting multiplicities.
