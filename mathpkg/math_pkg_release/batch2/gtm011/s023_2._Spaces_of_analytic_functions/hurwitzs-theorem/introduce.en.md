Hurwitz's Theorem states that if a sequence of analytic functions converges uniformly on compact sets to a non-zero limit, then on any disk where the limit has no zeros on the boundary, the functions eventually have the same number of zeros as the limit. A key consequence: if each $f_n$ never vanishes and $f_n \to f$, then either $f \equiv 0$ or $f$ never vanishes.
