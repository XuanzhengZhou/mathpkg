The space $M(G)$ of meromorphic functions, equipped with the chordal metric topology, is not complete — constant sequences $f_n \equiv n$ form a Cauchy sequence converging to the constant function $\infty$, which is not meromorphic. This theorem shows that this is the only pathology: any chordal limit of meromorphic functions is either meromorphic or identically $\infty$. If the limit is finite at one point, it must be meromorphic everywhere.
