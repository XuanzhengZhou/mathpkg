In the space $H(G)$ of analytic functions with the compact-open topology, a subset is compact exactly when it is closed and locally bounded. This follows directly from Montel's Theorem: local boundedness gives normality (sequential compactness), and closedness ensures the limit stays in the set, yielding compactness in this metric topology.
