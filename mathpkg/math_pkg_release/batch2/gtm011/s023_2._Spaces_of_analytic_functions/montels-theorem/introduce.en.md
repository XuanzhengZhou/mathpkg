Montel's Theorem is a cornerstone of complex analysis: a family of analytic functions on a domain is normal (every sequence has a convergent subsequence) precisely when it is locally bounded. This characterizes compactness in $H(G)$ and is the complex-analytic analogue of the Arzela-Ascoli theorem, but with a much simpler hypothesis — analyticity replaces equicontinuity.
