Jensen's Formula relates the modulus of an analytic function at the origin to the distribution of its zeros inside a disk and the average of its logarithm on the boundary circle. It generalizes the Mean Value Property for harmonic functions to account for zeros of $f$ inside the disk $B(0; r)$. The formula is a fundamental tool in the study of the growth of entire and meromorphic functions, connecting the rate of growth of $M(r) = \sup\{|f(re^{i\theta})| : 0 \leq \theta \leq 2\pi\}$ to the density of zeros.
