**(a)** Let $D = B(0; 1)$ and suppose that $f: D \to \mathbb{C}$ is an analytic function which is bounded. If $\{a_n\}$ are the non-zero zeros of $f$ in $D$ counted according to multiplicity, prove that $\sum (1 - |a_n|) < \infty$. (Hint: Use Proposition VII. 5.4).

**(b)** If $f$ has a zero at $z = 0$ of multiplicity $m \geq 0$, prove that $f(z) = z^m B(z) \exp(-g(z))$ where $B(z)$ is the Blaschke product formed from the zeros of $f$,

$$
B(z) = \prod_{n} \frac{\bar{a}_n (a_n - z)}{|a_n| (1 - \bar{a}_n z)},
$$

and $g(z)$ is analytic on $D$ with $\operatorname{Re} g(z) \geq -\log M$ where $M$ is a bound for $|f(z)|$ on $D$.
