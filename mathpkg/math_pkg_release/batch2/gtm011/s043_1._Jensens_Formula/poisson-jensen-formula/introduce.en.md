The Poisson-Jensen Formula generalizes Jensen's Formula by expressing $\log |f(z)|$ at any point $z$ inside the disk, rather than only at the origin. It combines the Blaschke product construction (which removes the zeros) with the Poisson integral formula for harmonic functions. Setting $z = 0$ recovers Jensen's Formula as a special case, since the Poisson kernel reduces to $1$ and each Blaschke factor at the origin becomes $r/|a_k|$.
