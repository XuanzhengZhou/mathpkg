This lemma is a precursor to the Koebe 1/4 theorem and Landau's theorem in geometric function theory. Given a bounded analytic function $f$ on the unit disc normalized by $f(0)=0$ and $f'(0)=1$, it guarantees that the image $f(D)$ covers a disc of radius $1/(6M)$ centered at the origin, where $M$ is the uniform bound on $|f|$. The constant $1/6$ is not sharp (Koebe gives $1/4$) but the proof is elementary, combining Cauchy estimates with Rouche's theorem. The inequality $M \geq 1$ follows immediately from Cauchy's estimate applied to $a_1 = f'(0) = 1$.
