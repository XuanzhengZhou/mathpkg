5.3 Proposition from Conway's "Functions of One Complex Variable I."
Let $f: (X, d) \rightarrow (\Omega, \rho)$ be a function. The following are equivalent statements:

(a) $f$ is continuous;
(b) If $\Delta$ is open in $\Omega$ then $f^{-1}(\Delta)$ is open in $X$;
(c) If $\Gamma$ is closed in $\Omega$ then $f^{-1}(\Gamma)$ is closed in $X$....
