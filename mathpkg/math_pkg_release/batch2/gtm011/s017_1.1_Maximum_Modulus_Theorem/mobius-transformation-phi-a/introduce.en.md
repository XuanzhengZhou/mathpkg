The Möbius transformation $\varphi_a(z) = \frac{z-a}{1-\bar{a}z}$ (for $|a| < 1$) is a conformal automorphism of the open unit disk $D$. It is an involution: $\varphi_a(\varphi_a(z)) = z$, and it maps the unit circle onto itself. This family of transformations plays a central role in the characterization of all one-to-one analytic maps of $D$ onto itself — every such map is a rotation composed with some $\varphi_a$, a result proved using Schwarz's Lemma.
