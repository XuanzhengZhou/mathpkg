This theorem completely characterizes the group of conformal automorphisms of the open unit disk $D$. It asserts that every one-to-one analytic map of $D$ onto itself that sends $a$ to $0$ must be of the form $c \varphi_a$, where $|c| = 1$ and $\varphi_a$ is the Möbius transformation $\varphi_a(z) = (z-a)/(1-\bar{a}z)$. The proof uses Schwarz's Lemma applied to both $f$ and its inverse, yielding the derivative estimate $|f'(a)| \leq (1 - |a|^2)^{-1}$ and its counterpart, which together force equality in Schwarz's Lemma and thus the stated form.
