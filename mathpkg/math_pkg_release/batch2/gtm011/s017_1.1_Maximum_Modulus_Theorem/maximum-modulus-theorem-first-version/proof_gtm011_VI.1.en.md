Let $\Omega = f(G)$ and put $\alpha = f(a)$. From the hypothesis we have that $|\alpha| \geq |\xi|$ for each $\xi$ in $\Omega$; as in the discussion preceding the theorem, $\alpha$ is in $\partial \Omega \cap \Omega$. In particular, the set $\Omega$ cannot be open (because then $\Omega \cap \partial \Omega = \varnothing$). Hence the Open Mapping Theorem (IV. 7.5) says that $f$ must be constant.
