The limit inferior (liminf) of a real-valued function $f$ as $z$ approaches $a$ is the dual notion to limsup: it is obtained by taking the infimum of $f$ over shrinking balls around $a$ and then the limit as the radius goes to zero. Together, limsup and liminf provide a precise criterion for the existence of a limit: $\lim f(z) = \alpha$ if and only if both limsup and liminf equal $\alpha$.
