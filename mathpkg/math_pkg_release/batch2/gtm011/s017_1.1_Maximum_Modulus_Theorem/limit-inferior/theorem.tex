If $f: G \to \mathbb{R}$ and $a \in G^{-}$ or $a = \infty$, then the limit inferior of $f(z)$ as $z$ approaches $a$, denoted by $\liminf_{z \to a} f(z)$, is defined by

$$\liminf_{z \to a} f(z) = \lim_{r \to 0+} \inf \{ f(z): z \in G \cap B(a; r) \}.$$

(If $a = \infty$, $B(a; r)$ is the ball in the metric of $\mathbb{C}_{\infty}$.)

It is easy to see that $\lim_{z \to a} f(z)$ exists and equals $\alpha$ iff $\alpha = \limsup_{z \to a} f(z) = \liminf_{z \to a} f(z)$.
