If $f: G \to \mathbb{R}$ and $a \in G^{-}$ or $a = \infty$, then the limit superior of $f(z)$ as $z$ approaches $a$, denoted by $\limsup_{z \to a} f(z)$, is defined by

$$\limsup_{z \to a} f(z) = \lim_{r \to 0+} \sup \{ f(z): z \in G \cap B(a; r) \}$$

(If $a = \infty$, $B(a; r)$ is the ball in the metric of $\mathbb{C}_{\infty}$.)
