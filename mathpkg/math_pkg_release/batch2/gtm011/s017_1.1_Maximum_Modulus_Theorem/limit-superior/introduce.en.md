The limit superior (limsup) of a real-valued function $f$ as $z$ approaches a point $a$ (or infinity) is defined by taking the supremum of $f$ over shrinking balls around $a$, then letting the radius tend to zero. It captures the largest cluster value of $f$ near $a$. This notion is essential for stating the third version of the Maximum Modulus Theorem, where continuity on the closure is replaced by a limsup bound at each boundary point, including the point at infinity.
