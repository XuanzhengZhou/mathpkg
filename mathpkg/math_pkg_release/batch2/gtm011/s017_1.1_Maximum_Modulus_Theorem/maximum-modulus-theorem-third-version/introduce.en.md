The Maximum Modulus Theorem (Third Version) is the most general formulation of the maximum principle. It replaces the continuity-on-closure hypothesis with a limsup bound at each point of the extended boundary $\partial_{\infty} G$, which includes the point at infinity for unbounded regions. If an analytic function $f$ on a region $G$ satisfies $\limsup_{z \to a} |f(z)| \leq M$ for every boundary point $a$ (including $\infty$), then $|f(z)| \leq M$ throughout $G$, with equality at any interior point forcing $f$ to be constant. This version is powerful enough to handle growth conditions at infinity.
