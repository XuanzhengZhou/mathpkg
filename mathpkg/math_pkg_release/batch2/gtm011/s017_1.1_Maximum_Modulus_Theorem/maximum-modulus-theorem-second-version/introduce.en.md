The Maximum Modulus Theorem (Second Version) extends the first version to the setting of a bounded open set. It states that for a function analytic on a bounded open set $G$ and continuous on its closure $G^{-}$, the maximum modulus is attained on the boundary $\partial G$, not in the interior (unless the function is constant). This formulation is particularly useful in applications because it directly identifies where the supremum of $|f|$ must occur, without requiring an a priori point where the maximum is achieved.
