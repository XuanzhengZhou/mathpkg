Since $G^{-}$ is compact and $|f|$ is continuous on $G^{-}$, $|f|$ attains its maximum at some point $a \in G^{-}$. If $a \in G$, then by the Maximum Modulus Theorem (First Version, 1.1), $f$ is constant on the component of $G$ containing $a$, and the equality holds trivially. If $a \in \partial G$, then $\max\{|f(z)| : z \in G^{-}\} = |f(a)| \leq \max\{|f(z)| : z \in \partial G\}$. The reverse inequality is immediate since $\partial G \subset G^{-}$. Hence the two maxima are equal.
