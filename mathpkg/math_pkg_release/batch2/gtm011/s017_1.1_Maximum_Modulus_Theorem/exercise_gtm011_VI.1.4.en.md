Let $0 < r < R$ and put $A = \{z : r \leq |z| \leq R\}$. Show that there is a positive number $\varepsilon > 0$ such that for each polynomial $p$,

$$\sup \{|p(z) - z^{-1}| : z \in A\} \geq \varepsilon.$$

This says that $z^{-1}$ is not the uniform limit of polynomials on $A$.
