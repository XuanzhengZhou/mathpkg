A function $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is differentiable at a point $x$ in $(a, b)$ if the limit

$$\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$$

exists and is finite. The value of this limit is denoted by $f'(x)$. For the points $x = a$ or $b$, this definition is modified by taking right or left sided limits. If $f$ is differentiable at each point of $[a, b]$, then $f$ is a differentiable function and $f': [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ is called the derivative of $f$.
