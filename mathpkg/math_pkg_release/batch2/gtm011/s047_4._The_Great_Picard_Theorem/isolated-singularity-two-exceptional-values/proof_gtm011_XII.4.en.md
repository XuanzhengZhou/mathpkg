If $z = a$ were an essential singularity, then by the Great Picard Theorem, $f$ would assume every complex number infinitely often in any deleted neighborhood of $a$, with at most one exception. The hypothesis that there are two complex numbers not assumed infinitely often contradicts this conclusion. Therefore $z = a$ cannot be an essential singularity. Since it is an isolated singularity, it must be either a pole or a removable singularity.
