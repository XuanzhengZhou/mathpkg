This proposition establishes two forms of the Chain Rule for complex-valued functions defined on real intervals. Part (a) deals with the composition of a complex-valued function with a real-valued change-of-variable $g: [c,d] \to [a,b]$, which follows from the classical Chain Rule applied to the real and imaginary parts. Part (b) addresses the composition of a complex-valued function $f$ on an interval with an analytic function $h$ defined on an open subset of $\mathbb{C}$ containing the image of $f$; the proof mirrors the argument used for the composition of two analytic functions (Proposition III.2.4).
