To discuss normality in $M(G)$ one must introduce the quantity

$$\frac{2|f'(z)|}{1 + |f(z)|^2},$$

for each meromorphic function $f$. However if $z$ is a pole of $f$ then the above expression is meaningless since $f'(z)$ has no meaning. To rectify this take the limit of the above expression as $z$ approaches the pole. To show that the limit exists let $a$ be a pole of $f$ of order $m \geq 1$; then

$$f(z) = g(z) + \frac{A_m}{(z-a)^m} + \ldots + \frac{A_1}{(z-a)}$$

So if $m \geq 2$

$$\lim_{z \to a} \frac{2|f'(z)|}{1 + |f(z)|^2} = 0$$

$$\lim_{z \to a} \frac{2|f'(z)|}{1 + |f(z)|^2} = \frac{2}{|A_1|}.$$
