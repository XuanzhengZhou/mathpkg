The corollary states that the set of meromorphic functions on an open set $G$, together with the constant function $\infty$, forms a complete metric space. The metric is the one induced by viewing meromorphic functions as continuous maps into the extended complex plane $\mathbb{C}_\infty$ and using the topology of uniform convergence on compact subsets. This result parallels the completeness of $H(G)$ (analytic functions) established earlier, and it is fundamental for applying Baire category arguments and fixed-point theorems in the study of normal families of meromorphic functions.
