This corollary asserts that the set of analytic functions on an open set $G$, together with the constant function $\infty$, forms a closed subset of the space of all continuous functions from $G$ into the extended complex plane $\mathbb{C}_\infty$, under the topology of uniform convergence on compact sets. This is a strengthening of Weierstrass's theorem (that uniform limits of analytic functions are analytic) to the chordal metric setting. It plays an important role in the theory of normal families, where closure properties are essential for compactness arguments.
