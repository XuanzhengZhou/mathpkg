The spherical derivative $\mu(f)$ measures the infinitesimal distortion of the chordal (spherical) metric under a meromorphic function $f: G \to \mathbb{C}_\infty$. For $z$ close to $z'$, the chordal distance $d(f(z), f(z'))$ is approximated by $\mu(f)(z) |z - z'|$. If a bound can be obtained for $\mu(f)$, then $f$ is a Lipschitz function with respect to the chordal metric. When $\mu(f)$ is uniformly bounded for all $f$ in a family, the family is uniformly Lipschitz. This notion is central to Marty's characterization of normal families of meromorphic functions.
