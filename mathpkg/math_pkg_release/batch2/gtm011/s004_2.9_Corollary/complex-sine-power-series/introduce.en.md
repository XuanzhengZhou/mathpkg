The complex sine function is defined by analogy with the real sine power series, extending the domain to the entire complex plane. The series $\sum (-1)^n z^{2n+1}/(2n+1)!$ converges absolutely for all $z \in \mathbb{C}$ (infinite radius of convergence), so $\sin z$ is analytic in $\mathbb{C}$ by Corollary 2.9. Together with the complex cosine, this definition provides the fundamental trigonometric functions in complex analysis, which satisfy the same power series identities as their real counterparts.
