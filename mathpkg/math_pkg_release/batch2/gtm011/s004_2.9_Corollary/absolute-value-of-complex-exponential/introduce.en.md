This identity expresses the modulus of the complex exponential function purely in terms of the real part of its argument. It follows from the properties $e^z e^{\bar{z}} = e^{z+\bar{z}} = \exp(2\operatorname{Re} z)$ together with $|e^z|^2 = e^z \overline{e^z} = e^z e^{\bar{z}}$, where the conjugate identity $\overline{\exp z} = \exp \bar{z}$ holds because the power series coefficients of $\exp z$ are real. A direct consequence is that $|e^{i\theta}| = 1$ for real $\theta$, and that $e^z \neq 0$ for all $z \in \mathbb{C}$.
