The complex cosine function is defined by analogy with the real cosine power series, extending the domain to the entire complex plane. The series $\sum (-1)^n z^{2n}/(2n)!$ converges absolutely for all $z \in \mathbb{C}$ (infinite radius of convergence), so $\cos z$ is analytic in $\mathbb{C}$ by Corollary 2.9. This definition preserves the familiar real cosine when $z$ is real and satisfies the same power series identity.
