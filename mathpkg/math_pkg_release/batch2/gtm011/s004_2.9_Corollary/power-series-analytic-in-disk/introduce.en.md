A power series $\sum a_n(z-a)^n$ with positive radius of convergence $R$ defines a function that is analytic throughout the open disk $B(a;R)$. This follows because the power series can be differentiated term-by-term infinitely many times within its radius of convergence, which is the very definition of an analytic function. As an immediate application, the exponential function $\exp z = \sum_{n=0}^\infty z^n/n!$, whose radius of convergence is infinite, is analytic in the entire complex plane $\mathbb{C}$.
