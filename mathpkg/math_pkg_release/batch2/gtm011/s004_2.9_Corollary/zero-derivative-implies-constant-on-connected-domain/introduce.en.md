This proposition states that on a connected open subset of the complex plane, a differentiable function whose derivative vanishes everywhere must be constant. The proof uses a topological argument: the set of points where the function takes a fixed value is shown to be both open and closed in the connected domain, hence the entire domain. This result is fundamental for complex analysis and is used, for example, to prove the functional equation $e^{a+b} = e^a e^b$ for the complex exponential function.
