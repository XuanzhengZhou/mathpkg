Lemma 2.1 is a crucial construction used in the proof of Schottky's Theorem. Given an analytic function $f$ on a simply connected region that omits the values $0$ and $1$, the lemma constructs a chain of auxiliary analytic functions $\ell$, $F$, $H$, $g$ linking $f$ to a hyperbolic cosine exponential representation $f = -\exp[\pi i \cosh(2g)]$. This representation allows one to bound $|f|$ in terms of bounds on $|g|$, which is the key idea behind Schottky's estimate.
