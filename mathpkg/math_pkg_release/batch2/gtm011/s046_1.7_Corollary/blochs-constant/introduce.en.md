Bloch's constant $B$ is a fundamental invariant in complex analysis defined as the infimum of maximal schlicht disk radii over all normalized analytic functions on the unit disk. It satisfies $\frac{1}{72} \leq B \leq 1$, with the best known bounds being $0.43 \leq B \leq 0.47$. The exact value remains an open problem; the conjectured value involves gamma functions evaluated at rational arguments. The constant is named after Andre Bloch, whose theorem guarantees a uniform lower bound for schlicht disks of analytic functions.
