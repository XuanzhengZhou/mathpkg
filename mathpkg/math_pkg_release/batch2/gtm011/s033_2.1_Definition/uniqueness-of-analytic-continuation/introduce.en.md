This proposition states that if two analytic continuations of the same initial function element are carried along the same path $\gamma$ from $a$ to $b$, then the resulting germs at the endpoint $b$ are identical. In other words, analytic continuation of a germ along a fixed curve is unique. This result is crucial because it guarantees that the process of continuing an analytic function along a curve is well-defined; the final germ depends only on the initial germ and the curve, not on the particular choice of intermediate function elements.
