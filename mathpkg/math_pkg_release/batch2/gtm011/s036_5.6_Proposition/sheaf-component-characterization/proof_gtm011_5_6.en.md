**Proof.** Suppose $C$ is a component of $\mathcal{S}(G)$; by Corollary 5.9, $C$ is an open arcwise connected subset of $\mathcal{S}(G)$. So by the preceding theorem (Theorem 5.10), for each point $(b, [g]_b)$ in $C$, $[g]_b$ is the continuation of $[f]_a$ along some curve in $G$. Conversely, if $[g]_b$ is the continuation of $[f]_a$ along some curve in $G$, then $(b, [g]_b)$ belongs to the component of $\mathcal{S}(G)$ which contains $(a, [f]_a)$; that is, $(b, [g]_b) \in C$.

Now suppose that $C$ consists of all points $(b, [g]_b)$ such that $[g]_b$ is a continuation of $[f]_a$. Then $C$ is arcwise connected (by Theorem 5.10) and hence is connected. If $C_1$ is the component of $\mathcal{S}(G)$ containing $C$, then by the first half of this proof it follows that $C = C_1$.

Notice that the point $(a, [f]_a)$ in the statement has a transitory role; any point in the component will do.
