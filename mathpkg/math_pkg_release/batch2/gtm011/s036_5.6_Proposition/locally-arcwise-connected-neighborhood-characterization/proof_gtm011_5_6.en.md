The proof is left as an exercise (marked "Verify!" in the text). One direction: if $X$ is locally arcwise connected, then each $\mathcal{N}_x$ is a neighborhood basis at $x$, and the collection $\{\mathcal{N}_x : x \in X\}$ satisfies the axioms of a neighborhood system that induces the original topology. Conversely, if $\{\mathcal{N}_x : x \in X\}$ is a neighborhood system inducing the topology, then each $\mathcal{N}_x$ consists of open sets containing $x$, and for any open $U$ containing $x$, there exists $V \in \mathcal{N}_x$ with $V \subset U$, which means $X$ is locally arcwise connected.
