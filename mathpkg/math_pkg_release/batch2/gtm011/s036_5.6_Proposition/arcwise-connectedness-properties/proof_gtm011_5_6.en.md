The proof of Proposition 5.6 is left to the reader.

The converse to part (a) is not true. A counterexample is given by the set

$$X = \left\{ t + i \sin \frac{1}{t} : t > 0 \right\} \cup \{si : -1 \leq s \leq 1\}.$$

Since $X$ is the closure of a connected set it is itself connected. However, there is no arc from the point $1/\pi$ to $i$ which lies in $X$. This $X$ is also an example of a topological space which is connected but not locally arcwise connected.

On the other hand, if $X$ is connected and locally arcwise connected, then $X$ is arcwise connected. This is an abstract version of a theorem proved for open connected subsets of $\mathbb{C}$; since disks in the plane are connected, open subsets of $\mathbb{C}$ are locally arcwise connected.
