If $z \in G$ then there is a germ $[g]_z$ in $F$ and a disk $B$ about $z$ on which $g$ is defined. For any $w \in B$, the germ $[g]_w$ is the analytic continuation of $[g]_z$ along a radial segment, and thus belongs to $F$. Hence $B \subset G$, proving that $G$ is open.
