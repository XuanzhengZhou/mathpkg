Let $z = a$ be an isolated singularity of $f$ and let $f(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n(z-a)^n$ be its Laurent Expansion in ann $(a; 0, R)$. Then:

(a) $z = a$ is a removable singularity iff $a_n = 0$ for $n \leq -1$;
(b) $z = a$ is a pole of order $m$ iff $a_{-m} \neq 0$ and $a_n = 0$ for $n \leq -(m+1)$;
(c) $z = a$ is an essential singularity iff $a_n \neq 0$ for infinitely many negative integers $n$.
