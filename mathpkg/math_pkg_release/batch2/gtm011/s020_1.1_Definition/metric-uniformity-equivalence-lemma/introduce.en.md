Lemma 1.7 establishes that the metric $\rho$ on $C(G, \Omega)$ and the uniform distance on compact subsets of $G$ generate equivalent uniformities. In one direction, small $\rho$-distance implies small supremum distance on any prescribed compact set $K$. Conversely, small supremum distance on a sufficiently large compact set $K$ (specifically, $K_p$ for large enough $p$) implies small $\rho$-distance. This equivalence means that convergence in the metric $\rho$ is precisely uniform convergence on compact subsets of $G$, making $\rho$ the natural metric for studying sequences of analytic functions.
