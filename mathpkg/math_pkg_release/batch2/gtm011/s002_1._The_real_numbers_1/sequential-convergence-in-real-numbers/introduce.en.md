Sequential convergence in $\mathbb{R}$ is the notion that a sequence of real numbers approaches a limit. A sequence $(x_n)$ converges to $L$ if its terms eventually stay arbitrarily close to $L$. Conway assumes the reader is familiar with sequential convergence, as it is a fundamental concept used throughout complex analysis, particularly in the study of limits, continuity, and series of complex numbers.
