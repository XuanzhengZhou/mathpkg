The supremum (sup) and infimum (inf) are fundamental order-theoretic concepts in the real numbers. The supremum of a set is its least upper bound, while the infimum is its greatest lower bound. Conway assumes that the reader is familiar with the definitions and basic properties of sup and inf, as they are essential tools in analysis and will be used throughout the study of complex variables.
