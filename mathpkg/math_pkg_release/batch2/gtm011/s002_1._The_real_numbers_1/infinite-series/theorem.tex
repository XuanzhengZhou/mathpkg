Given a sequence $(a_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ of real numbers, the \textbf{infinite series} $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n$ is defined as the limit of the sequence of partial sums $S_N = \sum_{n=1}^{N} a_n$, provided this limit exists. That is,
$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n = \lim_{N \to \infty} \sum_{n=1}^{N} a_n.$$
The series \textbf{converges} if the limit exists; otherwise it \textbf{diverges}.
