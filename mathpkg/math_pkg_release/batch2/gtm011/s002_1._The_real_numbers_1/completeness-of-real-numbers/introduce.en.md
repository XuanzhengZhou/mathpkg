The completeness of the real numbers is the property that every non-empty subset of $\mathbb{R}$ which is bounded above possesses a supremum (least upper bound) in $\mathbb{R}$. This axiom distinguishes $\mathbb{R}$ from $\mathbb{Q}$ and underpins all of real analysis. Conway explicitly states this property as a foundational assumption for the study of complex variables.
