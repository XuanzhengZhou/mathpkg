The Riemann Mapping Lemma is the technical core of the Riemann Mapping Theorem. It asserts that any region which is not the whole plane and admits analytic square roots of non-vanishing analytic functions can be mapped conformally onto the unit disk via a one-one analytic function. The proof uses a normal families argument: one chooses a function with maximal derivative at a reference point, then derives a contradiction if its image does not fill the disk.
