Equicontinuity is a uniform version of continuity for families of functions. While continuity at a point guarantees that for a single function \(f\) and any \(\epsilon > 0\), all points sufficiently close to \(z_0\) have images within \(\epsilon\) of \(f(z_0)\), equicontinuity demands that the same \(\delta\) works for every function in the family \(\mathcal{F}\). The notion comes in two strengths: equicontinuity at a single point (analogous to pointwise continuity) and equicontinuity over a set (analogous to uniform continuity). This concept is central to the Arzelà-Ascoli Theorem, which characterizes compact subsets of function spaces.
