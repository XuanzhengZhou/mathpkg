This proposition shows that pointwise equicontinuity on an open set implies equicontinuity uniformly over any compact subset. The proof uses a compactness argument: cover the compact set \(K\) by \(\delta(z)\)-balls from the pointwise equicontinuity at each \(z \in K\), extract a finite subcover, and take the minimum of the finitely many \(\delta\) values. This connection between pointwise and uniform equicontinuity is analogous to how continuity on a compact set implies uniform continuity for a single function, but extended to families of functions.
