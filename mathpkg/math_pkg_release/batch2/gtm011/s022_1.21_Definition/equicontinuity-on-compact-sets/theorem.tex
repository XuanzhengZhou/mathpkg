Let \(G\) be an open subset of \(\mathbb{C}\) and \((\Omega, d)\) a complete metric space. Let \(C(G, \Omega)\) denote the set of continuous functions from \(G\) to \(\Omega\).

If \(\mathcal{F} \subset C(G, \Omega)\) is equicontinuous at each point of \(G\), then \(\mathcal{F}\) is equicontinuous on each compact subset of \(G\).
