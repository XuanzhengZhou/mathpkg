Harnack's Theorem is a fundamental result about the space of harmonic functions on a region. Part (a) asserts that $\text{Har}(G)$ is a complete metric space under the topology of uniform convergence on compact sets, which follows from the fact that the uniform limit of harmonic functions is harmonic. Part (b) describes the behavior of monotone increasing sequences of harmonic functions: either they diverge to infinity uniformly on compact sets, or they converge to a harmonic function. This result relies on Harnack's Inequality and the connectedness of the region $G$.
