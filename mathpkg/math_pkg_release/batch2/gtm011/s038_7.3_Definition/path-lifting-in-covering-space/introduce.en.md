This corollary is the classical path lifting property of covering spaces: any path in the base space can be uniquely lifted to a path in the covering space once the starting point of the lift is prescribed. It follows immediately from Theorem 7.4 by defining $F(s, t) = \gamma(s)$ (so $F$ is independent of the second coordinate) and taking the unique lift $\tilde{F}$, then restricting to $\tilde{\gamma}(s) = \tilde{F}(s, 0)$. Uniqueness is proved by contradiction: two distinct lifts would yield two distinct lifts of $F$, contradicting Theorem 7.4.
