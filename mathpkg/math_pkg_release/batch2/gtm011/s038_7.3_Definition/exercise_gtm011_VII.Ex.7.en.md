Let $G$ and $\Omega$ be regions in the plane and suppose that $f: G \rightarrow \Omega$ is an analytic function such that $(G, f)$ is a covering space of $\Omega$. Show that for every region $\Omega_1$ contained in $\Omega$ which is simply connected there is an analytic function $g: \Omega_1 \rightarrow G$ such that $f(g(\omega)) = \omega$ for all $\omega$ in $\Omega_1$.
