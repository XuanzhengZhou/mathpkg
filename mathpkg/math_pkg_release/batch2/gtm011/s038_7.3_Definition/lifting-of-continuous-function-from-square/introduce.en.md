This theorem generalizes the path lifting property to continuous maps defined on the unit square $[0,1] \times [0,1]$. Given a continuous map $F$ from the square into the base space $\Omega$ and a prescribed lift of the origin $(0,0)$, there exists a unique continuous lift $\tilde{F}$ of the entire map. This result simultaneously implies both the path lifting property (by restricting to one coordinate axis) and the homotopy lifting property, making it a cornerstone for the monodromy theory of covering spaces.
