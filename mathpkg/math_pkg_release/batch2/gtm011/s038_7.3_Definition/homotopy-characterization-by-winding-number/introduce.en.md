This theorem provides a complete homotopy classification of closed curves in the punctured plane $\mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\}$. Two closed curves based at $1$ are homotopic in the punctured plane if and only if they have the same winding number about the origin. The forward direction uses that winding number is a homotopy invariant (if two curves are homotopic, their lifts via the covering $\exp(2\pi i \cdot)$ have the same endpoint). The converse follows from constructing an explicit homotopy between curves with equal winding number, which establishes that the fundamental group of the punctured plane is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}$.
