This corollary translates absolute convergence of an infinite product into the more familiar language of absolute convergence of the series $\sum (z_n - 1)$. It provides the fundamental practical criterion: an infinite product converges absolutely precisely when the deviations of its factors from $1$ form an absolutely convergent series. This follows from combining Proposition 5.2, Proposition 5.4 (on the relationship between $\log z$ and $z-1$), and the definition of absolute convergence of a product.
