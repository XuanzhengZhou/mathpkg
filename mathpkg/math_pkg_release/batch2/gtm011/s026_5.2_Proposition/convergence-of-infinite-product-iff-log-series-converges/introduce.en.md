This proposition establishes the fundamental relationship between infinite products and infinite series of logarithms. When each term has positive real part, the infinite product converges to a non-zero limit precisely when the series of principal logarithms converges. The proof exploits the periodicity of the complex exponential, showing that the integer ambiguity $2\pi i k_n$ stabilizes as $n \to \infty$, thus forcing the partial sums of logarithms to converge.
