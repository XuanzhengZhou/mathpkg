Suppose $\{g_n\}$ is a sequence of continuous functions from $X$ into $\mathbb{C}$ such that $\sum g_n(x)$ converges absolutely and uniformly for $x$ in $X$. Then the product
$$f(x) = \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} (1 + g_n(x))$$
converges absolutely and uniformly for $x$ in $X$. Also there is an integer $n_0$ such that $f(x) = 0$ iff $g_n(x) = -1$ for some $n$, $1 \leq n \leq n_0$.
