Let $X$ be a set and let $\mathcal{T}$ be a collection of subsets of $X$ satisfying the following:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\varnothing \in \mathcal{T}$ and $X \in \mathcal{T}$;
\item[(b)] if $U_1, \ldots, U_n$ are in $\mathcal{T}$ then $\bigcap_{j=1}^{n} U_j \in \mathcal{T}$;
\item[(c)] if $\{U_i : i \in I\}$ is any collection of sets in $\mathcal{T}$ then $\bigcup_{i \in I} U_i \in \mathcal{T}$.
\end{enumerate}

The collection of sets $\mathcal{T}$ is called a \textbf{topology} on $X$, and each member of $\mathcal{T}$ is called an \textbf{open set}.
