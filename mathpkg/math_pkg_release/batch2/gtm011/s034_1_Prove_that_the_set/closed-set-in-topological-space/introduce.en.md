In a topological space $X$, a subset $F$ is called closed if its complement $X \setminus F$ is open. This definition generalizes the familiar notion of closed sets from metric spaces, where a set is closed precisely when its complement is open with respect to the metric topology. Closed sets are the natural duals of open sets and satisfy properties dual to those of a topology: the empty set and the whole space are closed, arbitrary intersections of closed sets are closed, and finite unions of closed sets are closed.
