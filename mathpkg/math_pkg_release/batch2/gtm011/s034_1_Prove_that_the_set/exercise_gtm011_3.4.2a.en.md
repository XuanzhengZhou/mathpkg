Let $(f, D)$ be a function element and let $a \in D$. If $\gamma: [0, 1] \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ is a path with $\gamma(0) = a$ and $\gamma(1) = b$ and $\{(f_t, D_t): 0 \leq t \leq 1\}$ is an analytic continuation of $(f, D)$ along $\gamma$, let $R(t)$ be the radius of convergence of the power series expansion of $f_t$ at $z = \gamma(t)$.

Show that $R(t)$ is independent of the choice of continuation. That is, if a second continuation $\{(g_t, B_t)\}$ along $\gamma$ is given with $[g_0]_a = [f]_a$ and $r(t)$ is the radius of convergence of the power series expansion of $g_t$ about $z = \gamma(t)$ then $r(t) = R(t)$ for all $t$.
