Let $\Gamma$ be a circle through points $z_2, z_3, z_4$. The points $z, z^*$ in $\mathbb{C}_\infty$ are said to be symmetric with respect to $\Gamma$ if

$$\left(z^*, z_2, z_3, z_4\right) = \overline{\left(z, z_2, z_3, z_4\right)}.$$

As it stands, this definition not only depends on the circle but also on the points $z_2, z_3, z_4$. It is left as an exercise for the reader to show that symmetry is independent of the points chosen (Exercise 11).

Also, by Proposition 3.10 $z$ is symmetric to itself with respect to $\Gamma$ if and only if $z \in \Gamma$.
