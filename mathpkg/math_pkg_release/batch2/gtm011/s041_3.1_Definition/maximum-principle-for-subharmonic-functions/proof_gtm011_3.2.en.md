The proof is identical to that of Theorem 1.7 (the Maximum Principle for analytic/harmonic functions). One argues by contradiction: if $\varphi$ attains a maximum $M$ at some point $a \in G$ and is not constant, then there exists a point $b \in G$ with $\varphi(b) < M$. Using the sub-mean-value inequality and continuity, one can show that the set where $\varphi = M$ is both open and closed in $G$, hence equals $G$ by connectedness, contradicting $\varphi(b) < M$.
