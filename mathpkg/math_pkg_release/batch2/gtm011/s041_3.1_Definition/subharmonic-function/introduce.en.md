A subharmonic function is a continuous real-valued function on a region whose value at any point is bounded above by its average over any sufficiently small circle centered at that point. This generalizes the mean value property of harmonic functions by replacing equality with an inequality. Subharmonic functions play a central role in potential theory and the solution of the Dirichlet Problem, as they provide a class of functions from which harmonic functions can be constructed via a supremum operation (the Perron method).
