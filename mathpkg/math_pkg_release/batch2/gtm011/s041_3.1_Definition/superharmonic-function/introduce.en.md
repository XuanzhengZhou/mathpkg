A superharmonic function is a continuous real-valued function on a region whose value at any point is bounded below by its average over any sufficiently small circle centered at that point. It is precisely the negative of a subharmonic function. Superharmonic functions appear naturally in the construction of barriers for the Dirichlet Problem and serve as upper envelope functions.
