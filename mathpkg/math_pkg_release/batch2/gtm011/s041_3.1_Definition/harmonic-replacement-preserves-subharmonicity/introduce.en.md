This corollary states that the Poisson modification of a subharmonic function -- replacing its values on a disk by the unique harmonic function with the same boundary values -- yields another subharmonic function. This operation, known as harmonic replacement or Poisson modification, is a crucial technical tool in the Perron method for solving the Dirichlet Problem. It ensures that the iterative process of taking maxima and performing harmonic replacements stays within the class of subharmonic functions.
