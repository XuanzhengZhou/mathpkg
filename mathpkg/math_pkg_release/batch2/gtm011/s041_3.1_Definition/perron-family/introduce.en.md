The Perron family $\mathcal{P}(f, G)$ associated with a continuous boundary function $f$ is the collection of all subharmonic functions on $G$ whose limsup on the boundary does not exceed $f$. This class of "admissible" subharmonic functions forms the foundation of Perron's method: the harmonic solution to the Dirichlet Problem (when it exists) is the pointwise supremum of this family.
