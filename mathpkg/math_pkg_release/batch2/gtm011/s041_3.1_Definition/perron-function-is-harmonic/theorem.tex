Let $G$ be a region and $f: \partial_\infty G \to \mathbb{R}$ a continuous function. Define the family of admissible subharmonic functions

$$\mathcal{P}(f, G) = \{\, \varphi: G \to \mathbb{R} \text{ subharmonic} : \limsup_{z \to a} \varphi(z) \leq f(a) \text{ for all } a \in \partial_\infty G \,\}.$$

Then the Perron function

$$u(z) = \sup \{\, \varphi(z) : \varphi \in \mathcal{P}(f, G) \,\}$$

is harmonic on $G$.
