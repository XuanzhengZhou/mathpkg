Verify the following equations:

(a) $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{dx}{(x^2 + a^2)^2} = \frac{\pi}{4a^3}, \quad a > 0$

(b) $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{(\log x)^3}{1 + x^2} \, dx = 0$

(c) $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{\cos ax}{(1 + x^2)^2} \, dx = \frac{\pi(a + 1) e^{-a}}{4}$ if $a > 0$

(d) $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \frac{d\theta}{a + \sin^2 \theta} = \frac{\pi}{2[a(a + 1)]^{\frac{1}{2}}},$ if $a > 0$

(e) $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{\log x}{(1 + x^2)^2} \, dx = -\frac{\pi}{4}$

(f) $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\infty} \frac{dx}{1 + x^2} = \frac{\pi}{2}$

(g) $\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{e^{ax}}{1 + e^x} \, dx = \frac{\pi}{\sin a\pi}$ if $0 < a < 1$

(h) $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{2\pi} \log \sin^2 2\theta \, d\theta = 4 \int_{0}^{\pi} \log \sin \theta \, d\theta = -4\pi \log 2$
