To compute residues in practice, one uses Proposition 2.4, which gives a derivative formula for the residue at a pole of order $m$. For a simple pole the formula simplifies to a limit, and when the function is a quotient $g/h$ with a simple zero of the denominator, the residue equals $g(a)/h'(a)$. This turns the Residue Theorem into a practical tool for evaluating contour integrals.
