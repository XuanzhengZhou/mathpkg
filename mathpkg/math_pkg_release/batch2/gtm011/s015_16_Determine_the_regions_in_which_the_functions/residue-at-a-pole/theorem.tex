Suppose $f$ has a pole of order $m$ at $z = a$. Then
$$\operatorname{Res}(f; a) = \frac{1}{(m-1)!} \lim_{z \to a} \frac{d^{m-1}}{dz^{m-1}} \bigl[(z-a)^m f(z)\bigr].$$

In particular, if $m = 1$ (a simple pole), then
$$\operatorname{Res}(f; a) = \lim_{z \to a} (z-a) f(z).$$

If $f(z) = g(z)/h(z)$ where $g$ and $h$ are analytic at $a$, $g(a) \neq 0$, $h(a) = 0$, and $h'(a) \neq 0$ (i.e., $a$ is a simple zero of $h$), then $f$ has a simple pole at $z = a$ and
$$\operatorname{Res}(f; a) = \frac{g(a)}{h'(a)}.$$
