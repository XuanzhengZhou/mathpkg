An essential singularity is the most severe type of isolated singularity. It is defined by exclusion: an isolated singularity that is neither removable nor a pole must be an essential singularity. Unlike poles, where $|f(z)| \to \infty$, functions near an essential singularity exhibit extremely wild behavior — by the Casorati–Weierstrass theorem, the image of any punctured neighborhood is dense in $\mathbb{C}$, and by Picard's great theorem, $f$ attains every complex value infinitely often with at most one exception.
