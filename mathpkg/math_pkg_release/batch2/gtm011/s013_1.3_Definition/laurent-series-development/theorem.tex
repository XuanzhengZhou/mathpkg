Let $f$ be analytic in the annulus $\operatorname{ann}(a; R_1, R_2)$. Then
$$f(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n(z-a)^n$$
where the convergence is absolute and uniform over $\operatorname{ann}(a; r_1, r_2)^-$ if $R_1 < r_1 < r_2 < R_2$. Also the coefficients $a_n$ are given by the formula
$$a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\gamma} \frac{f(z)}{(z-a)^{n+1}} \, dz$$
where $\gamma$ is the circle $|z-a| = r$ for any $r$, $R_1 < r < R_2$. Moreover, this series is unique.
