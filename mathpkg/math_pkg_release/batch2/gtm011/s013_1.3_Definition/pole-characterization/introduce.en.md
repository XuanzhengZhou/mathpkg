A function $f$ with a pole at $z = a$ can be expressed as the quotient of an analytic function $g$ and a power $(z-a)^m$, where $m$ is a positive integer. This shows that near a pole, $f$ behaves like a rational function with a single pole of finite order, and the analytic function $g$ captures the regular part of $f$ on the entire region $G$.
