A pole is a type of isolated singularity of a complex function $f$ at a point $z=a$ where the modulus $|f(z)|$ tends to infinity as $z$ approaches $a$. This is the second of the three basic types of isolated singularities, alongside removable singularities and essential singularities. The defining property is that $f$ becomes arbitrarily large in any punctured neighborhood of $a$, which is formalized by the $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ condition that $|f(z)| \geq M$ for any prescribed bound $M$ when $z$ is sufficiently close to $a$.
