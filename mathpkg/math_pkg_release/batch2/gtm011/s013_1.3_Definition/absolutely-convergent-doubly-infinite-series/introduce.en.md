A doubly infinite series $\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} z_n$ is said to be absolutely convergent when both the nonnegative-index part $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} z_n$ and the negative-index part $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} z_{-n}$ converge absolutely. This notion is essential for defining Laurent series, where a function is expanded in both positive and negative powers of $(z-a)$, and absolute convergence guarantees that the order of summation is irrelevant.
