Since $C$ is weakly normal, we have $E' = C' - C'$ by (3.3), Corollary 3. Thus the assumptions imply that $\mathcal{H} - \mathcal{H}$ contains the subspace $E' \otimes F$ of $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$. Indeed, every element of $E' \otimes F$ can be written as a finite sum $\sum x_i' \otimes y_i$ with $x_i' \in E'$ and $y_i \in F$. Writing each $x_i'$ as a difference of positive linear forms (from $C' - C' = E'$) and each $y_i$ as a difference of elements from $D - D = F$, we can express each $x_i' \otimes y_i$ as a difference of elements in $\mathcal{H}$.

On the other hand, the dual of $\mathcal{L}_s(E, F)$ (under the topology of simple convergence) can be identified with $E \otimes F'$ by (IV, 4.3), Corollary 4. Under the duality between $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$ and $E \otimes F'$, the subspace $E' \otimes F$ separates points in $E \otimes F'$ (Chapter IV, Section 1, Example 3).

It follows from (IV, 1.3) that $E' \otimes F$ is weakly dense in $\mathcal{L}_s(E, F)$. Since $\mathcal{H} - \mathcal{H}$ contains $E' \otimes F$ and is convex, it is also dense in $\mathcal{L}_s(E, F)$. Indeed, a convex subset of a locally convex space has the same closure in the original topology as in the weak topology.
