This proposition establishes a density result for positive operators between ordered locally convex spaces. When the positive cone $C$ of the domain $E$ is weakly normal (meaning the dual $E'$ equals $C' - C'$) and the codomain $F$ is the difference of its positive cone, the difference of the cone $\mathcal{H}$ of positive operators with itself is dense in $\mathcal{L}_s(E, F)$, the space of all continuous linear maps under the topology of pointwise convergence. This result is used in the study of extensions of positive linear forms and the automatic continuity of positive linear maps.
