Let $(E, \|\cdot\|)$ be a complex Banach space and let $\mathcal{L}(E)$ be the Banach algebra of continuous endomorphisms. For $u \in \mathcal{L}(E)$, the spectrum $\sigma(u)$ is the complement in $C$ of the largest open set $\rho(u)$ in which $\lambda \mapsto (\lambda e - u)^{-1}$ exists and is locally holomorphic. For $\lambda \in \rho(u)$, $R(\lambda) = (\lambda e - u)^{-1}$ is the resolvent. The radius $r(u)$ of the smallest circle centered at 0 containing $\sigma(u)$ is the spectral radius; $\{\lambda: |\lambda| = r(u)\}$ is the spectral circle.
