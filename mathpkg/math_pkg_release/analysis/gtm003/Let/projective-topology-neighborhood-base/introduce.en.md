This proposition gives the explicit form of neighborhood bases for the projective topology. A neighborhood of a point $x$ in the projective topology is determined by taking inverse images of finitely many neighborhoods from the target spaces. The finiteness of $\mathbf{H}$ is essential: it ensures the resulting topology is the coarsest one making all $f_\alpha$ continuous while still admitting a local base.
