Let $E, F$ be vector spaces over $K$ and let $\chi: E \times F \to E \otimes F$ be the canonical bilinear map defined by $\chi(x, y) = x \otimes y$. For every vector space $G$ over $K$ and every bilinear map $b: E \times F \to G$, there exists a unique linear map $\tilde{b}: E \otimes F \to G$ such that $b = \tilde{b} \circ \chi$. Thus the pair $(E \otimes F, \chi)$ is universal among all pairs $(V, \phi)$ where $V$ is a vector space and $\phi: E \times F \to V$ is a bilinear map. Consequently, the mapping $\tilde{b} \mapsto \tilde{b} \circ \chi$ is a vector space isomorphism of $L(E \otimes F, G)$ onto the space $B(E, F; G)$ of all bilinear maps of $E \times F$ into $G$.
