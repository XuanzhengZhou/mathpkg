The proof relies on the fact that for an equicontinuous set $H$ and a precompact set $S \subset E$, given a 0-neighborhood $V$ in $F$ and a 0-neighborhood $U$ in $E$ such that $\bigcup_{u \in H} u(U) \subset V$, one can cover $S$ by finitely many translates of $U$. Choosing a finite set of points in $S$ that $U$-approximate every point of $S$, the simple convergence on this finite set controls uniform convergence on $S$ up to $V$.
