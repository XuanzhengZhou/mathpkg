Let $H \subset \mathcal{L}_{\mathfrak{S}}(E, F)$ be closed and equicontinuous. If $\mathfrak{F}$ is a Cauchy filter on $H$, it is a fortiori a Cauchy filter on $H$ for the uniformity of simple convergence; hence for each $x \in E$ the sets $\{\Phi(x): \Phi \in \mathfrak{F}\}$ are bounded and form a Cauchy filter base in $F$. Since $F$ is quasi-complete, this filter base converges to $u_1(x) \in F$, and by (4.3), $x \mapsto u_1(x)$ is in $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$. A standard argument using the equicontinuity shows that the convergence is uniform on each $S \in \mathfrak{S}$, so $u_1 \in H$ and $\mathfrak{F}$ converges to $u_1$ in $\mathcal{L}_{\mathfrak{S}}(E, F)$.
