The canonical imbedding $\iota: M \hookrightarrow E$ is a linear map. The projective topology on $M$ with respect to $\{(E, \mathfrak{T}, \iota)\}$ is, by definition, the coarsest topology on $M$ making $\iota$ continuous. A topology on $M$ makes $\iota$ continuous if and only if $\iota^{-1}(U) = U \cap M$ is open in $M$ for every open $U \subset E$. The coarsest topology with this property is precisely the subspace topology induced by $\mathfrak{T}$, consisting of all sets $U \cap M$ with $U \in \mathfrak{T}$.
