We suppose $\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{T}$ to be directed under inclusion. Since $S \times T$ is dense in the uniform space $S_1 \times T_1$, the restriction $f_{S,T}$ of the bilinear map $f$ to $S \times T$ has by (5.3) a unique uniformly continuous extension $\tilde{f}_{S_1, T_1}$ to $S_1 \times T_1$ with values in $G$, since $f(S \times T)$ is bounded. These extensions are compatible and define a bilinear extension on $\bigcup (S_1 \times T_1) = E_1 \times F_1$ that is $(\mathfrak{S}_1, \mathfrak{T}_1)$-hypocontinuous.
