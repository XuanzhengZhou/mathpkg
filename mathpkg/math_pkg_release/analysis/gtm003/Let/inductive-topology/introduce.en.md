The inductive topology on a vector space $E$ with respect to a family of linear maps from locally convex spaces into $E$ is the finest locally convex topology making all those maps continuous. It is well-defined because the trivial topology is always a candidate, and the upper bound of all such topologies is locally convex. The inductive topology need not be Hausdorff even if all $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ are.
