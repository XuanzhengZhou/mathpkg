The weak topology $\sigma(E, F)$ is one of the most important examples of a projective topology. Given a vector space $E$ and a set $F$ of linear functionals on $E$, the weak topology is the coarsest topology making all $f \in F$ continuous. When $F = E'$ (the continuous dual), this yields the familiar weak topology of a locally convex space. The weak topology is always locally convex and plays a central role in duality theory.
