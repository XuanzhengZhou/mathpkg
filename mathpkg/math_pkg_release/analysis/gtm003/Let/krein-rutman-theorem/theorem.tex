Let $E$ be an ordered complex Banach space with proper positive cone $C$ such that $C$ is normal and $E = C - C$. Let $u$ be a positive endomorphism of $E$. If $r(u)$ is a pole of the resolvent of $u$, then $r(u) > 0$ and there exist eigenvectors $x \in C$, $x' \in C'$ of $u$ and $u'$, respectively, pertaining to $r(u)$.
