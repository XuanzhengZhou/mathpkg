Let $u: E \to F$ satisfy $u(C) \subset D$. For each $y' \in D'$, $x \mapsto \langle x, u^*y' \rangle = \langle ux, y' \rangle$ is a positive linear form on $E$, hence continuous. Since $F' = D' - D'$ by (3.3) Corollary 3 (weak normality), $u^*(F') \subset E'$. Thus $u$ is weakly continuous by (IV, 2.4). Since $E$ is a Mackey space, weak continuity implies continuity.
