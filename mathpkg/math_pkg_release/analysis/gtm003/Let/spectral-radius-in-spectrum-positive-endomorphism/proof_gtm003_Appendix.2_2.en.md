By (V, 5.5), any positive endomorphism is continuous. The cone $H$ of positive endomorphisms is normal in $\mathcal{L}(E)$ under the norm topology. If $r(u) > 0$, the resolvent expansion $R(\lambda) = \sum \lambda^{-(n+1)} u^n$ converges for $|\lambda| > r(u)$ and has positive coefficients. Apply (2.1) to the series in $z = r(u)/\lambda$ with radius 1; the singularity at $z = 1$ translates to $\lambda = r(u) \in \sigma(u)$.
