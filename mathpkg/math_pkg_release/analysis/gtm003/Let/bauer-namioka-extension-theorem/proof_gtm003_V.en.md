It suffices to consider $K = R$. If $f$ extends $f_0$ continuously and positively, and if $U$ is a convex 0-neighborhood with $f(U) \subset (-1, 1)$, then $f_0$ is bounded above on $M \cap (U - C)$ by 1. Conversely, the boundedness condition allows applying the Hahn-Banach theorem with a sublinear functional defined using the cone and the 0-neighborhood, yielding an extension $f$ that is positive and bounded on $U$, hence continuous.
