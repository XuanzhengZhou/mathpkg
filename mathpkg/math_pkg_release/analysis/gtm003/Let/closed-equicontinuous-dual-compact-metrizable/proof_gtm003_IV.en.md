The first assertion is a special case of (III, 4.7) for $F = K_0$. If $E$ is metrizable, let $\{U_n\}$ be a 0-neighborhood base. Each polar $U_n^{\circ}$ is equicontinuous and closed in $E'_{\sigma}$, hence a compact metrizable space. Let $A_n$ be a countable dense subset of $U_n^{\circ}$. Since $\{U_n^{\circ}\}$ covers $E'$, the countable set $A = \bigcup A_n$ is dense in $E'_{\sigma}$.
