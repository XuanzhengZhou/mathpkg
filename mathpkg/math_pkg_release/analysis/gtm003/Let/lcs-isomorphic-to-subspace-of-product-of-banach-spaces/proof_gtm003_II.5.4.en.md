By Theorem 5.4, every complete l.c.s. $E$ is isomorphic to a projective limit $\lim_{\leftarrow} g_{\alpha\beta} E_{\beta}$ of Banach spaces $E_{\beta}$. By (5.3), every projective limit is isomorphic to a closed subspace of the product $\prod_{\alpha} E_{\alpha}$ of the constituent spaces. Hence $E$ is isomorphic to a subspace of a product of Banach spaces. For an arbitrary (not necessarily complete) l.c.s. $E$, apply the result to its completion $\tilde{E}$: $\tilde{E}$ is isomorphic to a subspace of a product of Banach spaces, and $E$ embeds as a dense subspace of $\tilde{E}$, hence also as a subspace of that product.
