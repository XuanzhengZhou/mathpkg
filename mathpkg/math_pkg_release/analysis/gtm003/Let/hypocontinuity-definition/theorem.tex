Let $E, F, G$ be t.v.s., let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a family of bounded subsets of $E$, and let $f$ be a bilinear map on $E \times F$ into $G$. $f$ is called $\mathfrak{S}$-hypocontinuous if $f$ is separately continuous and if, for each $S \in \mathfrak{S}$ and each 0-neighborhood $W$ in $G$, there exists a 0-neighborhood $V$ in $F$ such that $f(S \times V) \subset W$. The $\mathfrak{T}$-hypocontinuity is defined analogously. A bilinear map is $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{T})$-hypocontinuous if it is both $\mathfrak{S}$-hypocontinuous and $\mathfrak{T}$-hypocontinuous.
