A locally convex space is barreled if every barrel is a neighborhood of 0. This means the barrels form a 0-neighborhood base. Barreled spaces are important because they include all Fr\'echet spaces and their defining property is invariant under the formation of inductive topologies. Equivalent characterizations involve lower semi-continuous semi-norms.
