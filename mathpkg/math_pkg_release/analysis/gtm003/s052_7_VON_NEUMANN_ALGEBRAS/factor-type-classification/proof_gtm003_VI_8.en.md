By Theorem 8.7, $W = W_I \oplus W_{II_1} \oplus W_{II_\infty} \oplus W_{III}$ is the unique type decomposition. Since $W$ is a factor, its center $Z(W)$ is one-dimensional: $Z(W) = \mathbb{C}e$. Each summand $W_T$ in the decomposition is of the form $W p_T$ where $p_T$ is a central projection. Because the only central projections in a factor are $0$ and $e$, exactly one of the $p_T$ equals $e$ and the others equal $0$. Hence $W$ coincides with exactly one of the summands, meaning it is purely of one type.
