Let $H$ denote any Hilbert space. An operator $T \in \mathcal{L}(H)$ is called a \text{Fredholm operator} if both the dimension of its kernel and the co-dimension of its range are finite. Prove the following:

(a) $T$ is Fredholm if and only if both $\dim(\ker(T))$ and $\dim(\ker(T^*))$ are finite and $T(H)$ is closed in $H$.

(b) (Atkinson's theorem): $T \in \mathcal{L}(H)$ is Fredholm if and only if its canonical image in the Calkin algebra $\mathcal{L}(H)/\mathcal{K}(H)$ is invertible. (Use the representation of $\mathcal{L}(H)/\mathcal{K}(H)$ established in Exercise 8.)
