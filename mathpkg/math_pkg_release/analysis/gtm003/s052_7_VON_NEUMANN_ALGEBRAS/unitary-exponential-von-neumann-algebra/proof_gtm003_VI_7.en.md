Let $A$ denote the $C^*$-subalgebra of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ generated by $u$. Since $u$ is unitary, $A$ contains the identity $e$ and is commutative (as a singly-generated $C^*$-algebra containing a normal element). Let $\bar{A}$ be the closure of $A$ in $\mathcal{L}_s(H)$ (strong operator topology). Then $\bar{A}$ is commutative and, being strongly closed and containing $e$, is a $W^*$-algebra by the bicommutant theorem.

By (6.9) Corollary 2, every commutative $W^*$-algebra is isometrically isomorphic to $L^\infty(X, \Sigma, \mu)$ for some measure space $(X, \Sigma, \mu)$. Under this isomorphism, the unitary $u$ corresponds to a function $f \in L^\infty(X, \Sigma, \mu)$ satisfying $|f| = 1_X$ almost everywhere. One can then write $f = \exp(ig)$ for some real-valued $g \in L^\infty(X, \Sigma, \mu)$ (this is left as an exercise: every measurable unimodular function admits a measurable logarithm). Letting $v \in \bar{A} \subset M$ be the self-adjoint element corresponding to $g$, we obtain $u = \exp(iv)$.
