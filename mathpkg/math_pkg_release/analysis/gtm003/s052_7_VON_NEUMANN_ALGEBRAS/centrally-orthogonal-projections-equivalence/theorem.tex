Let $p, q \in P(W)$ be projections in a $W^*$-algebra $W$, and let $z(p), z(q)$ denote their central supports. The projections $p$ and $q$ are called \text{centrally orthogonal} if $z(p) z(q) = 0$. The following assertions are equivalent:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $p$ and $q$ are not centrally orthogonal.
\item[(b)] $pWq \neq \{0\}$.
\item[(c)] There exist equivalent non-zero projections $p_1, q_1$ such that $0 < p_1 \leq p$ and $0 < q_1 \leq q$.
\end{itemize}
