This lemma gives three equivalent characterizations of when two projections in a $W^*$-algebra are not centrally orthogonal: algebraically via $pWq \neq \{0\}$, and geometrically via the existence of equivalent non-zero sub-projections. The result is a crucial ingredient in the proof of the comparability theorem for projections, which underlies the type classification of von Neumann algebras.
