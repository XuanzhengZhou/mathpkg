This theorem gives an equivalent characterization of topological vector space (t.v.s.) topologies purely in terms of a neighborhood base at the origin. Instead of checking the joint continuity of addition and scalar multiplication directly, one verifies that the topology is translation-invariant and that there exists a 0-neighborhood base consisting of radial and circled sets satisfying conditions (a) and (c). This is the standard practical criterion for constructing and verifying t.v.s. topologies. For the real or complex case, condition (c) is automatically satisfied and can be omitted.
