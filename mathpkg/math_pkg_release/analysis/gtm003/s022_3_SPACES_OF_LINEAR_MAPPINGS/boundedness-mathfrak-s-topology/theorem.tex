Let \(E\) and \(F\) be locally convex spaces and let \(\mathfrak{S}\) be a family of bounded subsets of \(E\). For a subset \(H\) of \(\mathcal{L}(E, F)\), the following assertions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \(H\) is bounded for the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology.
\item[(b)] For each \(0\)-neighborhood \(V\) in \(F\), \(\bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)\) absorbs every \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\).
\item[(c)] For each \(0\)-neighborhood \(V\) in \(F\) and each \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\), \(\bigcup_{u \in H} u(S)\) is bounded in \(F\).
\end{enumerate}
