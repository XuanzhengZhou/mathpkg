The linear hull \(E_M\) of \(M\) is dense in \(E\) and is a Baire space (since \(M\) is not meager in \(E\)). Let \(H_0\) be the set obtained by restricting all \(u \in H\) to \(E_M\). Then \(H_0\) is simply bounded in \(\mathcal{L}(E_M, F)\) by hypothesis. By Theorem 4.2, \(H_0\) is equicontinuous and thus norm bounded in \(\mathcal{L}(E_M, F)\).

Now since the unit ball of \(E_M\) is dense in the unit ball of \(E\), the mapping \(u \mapsto u_0\) (where \(u_0\) is the restriction of \(u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)\) to \(E_M\)) is a norm isomorphism of \(\mathcal{L}(E, F)\) into \(\mathcal{L}(E_M, F)\). Hence \(H\) is norm bounded as asserted.
