The Bourbaki biequicontinuity theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of bilinear maps on locally convex spaces. It generalizes the Banach-Steinhaus theorem to bilinear maps: a pointwise bounded family of separately continuous bilinear maps on a barreled space is automatically equicontinuous on products with bounded sets. This result underpins the duality theory of topological tensor products and nuclear spaces.
