For a subspace \(G\) of the function space \(F^T\), the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology makes \(G\) into a topological vector space provided each \(f \in G\) maps members of \(\mathfrak{S}\) to bounded subsets of \(F\) and the union of \(\mathfrak{S}\) is total in \(G\). This is a foundational structural result ensuring the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology interacts properly with the vector space operations of addition and scalar multiplication.
