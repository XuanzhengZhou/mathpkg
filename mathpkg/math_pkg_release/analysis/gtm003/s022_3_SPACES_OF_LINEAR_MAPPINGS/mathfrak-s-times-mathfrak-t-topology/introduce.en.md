The $(\mathfrak{S} \times \mathfrak{T})$-topology is a natural topology on spaces of bilinear maps, generalizing the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology for linear maps. Given families $\mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{T}$ of bounded sets in $E$ and $F$, a bilinear map is $(\mathfrak{S} \times \mathfrak{T})$-continuous if it is uniformly continuous on products $S \times T$. This topology interpolates between the topology of separate continuity and the topology of joint continuity, and is fundamental for the theory of hypocontinuous bilinear maps and topological tensor products.
