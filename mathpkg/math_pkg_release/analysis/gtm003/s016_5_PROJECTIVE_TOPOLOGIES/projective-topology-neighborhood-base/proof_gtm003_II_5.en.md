Since $\mathfrak{T}$ is the coarsest topology making each $f_\alpha$ continuous, the family of sets $f_\alpha^{-1}(U_\alpha)$ where $U_\alpha$ runs through a neighborhood base of $x_\alpha$ in $(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ and $\alpha$ runs through $\mathbf{A}$ forms a subbase for $\mathfrak{T}$ at $x$. Taking finite intersections of these subbasic sets yields a neighborhood base, giving the stated description. The finiteness of $\mathbf{H}$ in the intersection is essential because only finite intersections of open sets are guaranteed to be open in any topology.
