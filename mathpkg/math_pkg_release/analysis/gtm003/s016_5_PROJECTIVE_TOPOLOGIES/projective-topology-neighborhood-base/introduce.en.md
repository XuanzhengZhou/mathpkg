A neighborhood base for the projective topology is formed by finite intersections of inverse images of neighborhoods from the target spaces. Concretely, a basic neighborhood of a point $x$ is obtained by choosing finitely many indices $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n$ and neighborhoods $U_{\alpha_i}$ of $f_{\alpha_i}(x)$ in $E_{\alpha_i}$, then taking $\bigcap_i f_{\alpha_i}^{-1}(U_{\alpha_i})$. This description makes the projective topology computationally tractable and highlights its role as the initial topology.
