An integral linear map is a continuous linear map into the weak dual that arises from an integral bilinear form via the canonical identification between bilinear forms and linear maps. Such maps admit an explicit integral representation involving a weakly closed equicontinuous set and a Radon measure. Integral maps are central to the theory of nuclear spaces, as they provide a concrete analytic description of the linear maps between dual spaces.
