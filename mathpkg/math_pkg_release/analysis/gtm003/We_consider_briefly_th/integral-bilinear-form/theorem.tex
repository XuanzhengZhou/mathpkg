The elements of $\mathcal{J}(E, F)$ are called \textbf{integral bilinear forms} on $E \times F$. Here $\mathcal{J}(E, F)$ denotes the dual of $E \tilde{\otimes} F$, where $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ is the completion of $E \otimes F$ equipped with the topology of bi-equicontinuous convergence. This topology has as a base of $0$-neighborhoods the polars, with respect to the duality between $E \otimes F$ and $E' \otimes F' \subset B(E, F)$, of the sets $S \otimes T$, where $S, T$ are arbitrary equicontinuous subsets of $E'$, $F'$ respectively.
