Projections in a $C^*$-algebra admit a purely geometric characterization: they are exactly the extreme points of the order interval $[0, p]$. This result connects the algebraic notion of a projection (an idempotent self-adjoint element) with the convex-geometric notion of an extreme point. The proof uses that $pAp$ is a hereditary subalgebra, so the order interval $[0, p]$ is contained in $pAp$, and extreme points of this interval correspond to projections dominated by $p$.
