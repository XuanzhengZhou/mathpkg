A $W^*$-algebra $W$ carries three natural locally convex topologies compatible with the duality $\langle W, V \rangle$: the weak-* topology $\sigma(W, V)$, the strong topology $s(W, V)$ (generated by the seminorms $\|x\|_\varphi^2 = \varphi(x^*x)$ for $\varphi \in V_+$), and the Mackey topology $\tau(W, V)$. These satisfy the chain of inclusions $\sigma(W, V) \subset s(W, V) \subset \tau(W, V)$. Furthermore, multiplication is jointly $s(W, V)$-continuous on bounded sets, a crucial property for analysis in $W^*$-algebras.
