First, by (III, 2.1) Corollary 3, $\pi$ is norm continuous on $F$ whence $\pi(U) \subset \|\pi\| U$. By (6.1) it suffices to show that $\pi$ is $\sigma(F, F_0)$-continuous on $U$; let $\mathfrak{F}$ denote a filter on $U$ that $\sigma(F, F_0)$-converges to $x \in U$. Since $\|\pi\| U$ is compact and $F_1$ is closed for $\sigma(F, F_0)$, $\pi(\mathfrak{F})$ has an adherent point $y \in F_1 \cap \|\pi\| U$; clearly, then, $z = x - y$ is adherent to $(1 - \pi)(\mathfrak{F})$ and $z \in F_2$ as $F_2$ is $\sigma(F, F_0)$-closed. Now $x = y + z$ shows that $y$ is the unique adherent point of $\pi(\mathfrak{F})$ in the compact space $\|\pi\| U$. By a well-known result on compact spaces, this implies that $\lim \pi(\mathfrak{F}) = y = \pi(x)$.
