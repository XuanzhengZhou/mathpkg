A cyclic representation of a $C^*$-algebra is one that possesses a cyclic vector -- a vector whose orbit under the algebra action is dense in the Hilbert space. This concept is central to the GNS (Gelfand-Naimark-Segal) construction, which produces a cyclic representation from any positive linear form (state) on the algebra. The cyclic vector serves as a generating element for the entire Hilbert space under the algebra action.
