A linear functional $\phi$ on a $C^*$-algebra $A$ is positive if and only if its norm equals the limit of $\phi$ evaluated along any approximate unit. This gives an algebraic-topological characterization of positive linear forms that is essential for the Jordan decomposition and the GNS construction. The result shows that positivity of a linear form can be detected by its behavior on an approximate unit.
