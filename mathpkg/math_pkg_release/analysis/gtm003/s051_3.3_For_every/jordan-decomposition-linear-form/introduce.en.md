The Jordan Decomposition theorem for $C^*$-algebras states that every self-adjoint continuous linear form can be uniquely expressed as the difference of two positive linear forms. This mirrors the classical Jordan decomposition for signed measures and is fundamental for the duality theory of $C^*$-algebras. The proof relies on the existence of sufficiently many positive linear forms, which in turn depends on the normality of the positive cone $A_+$.
