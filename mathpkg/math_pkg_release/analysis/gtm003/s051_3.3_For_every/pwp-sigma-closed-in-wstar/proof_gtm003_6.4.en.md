By (5.1), $pWp$ is a hereditary $C^*$-subalgebra of $W$; hence the order interval $[-p, p]$, which equals the closed unit ball of $(pWp)_{sa}$, is $\sigma(W, V)$-closed and thus compact. Hence by (IV, 6.4) $pWp$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-closed in $W$ and so its own bipolar with respect to the duality $\langle W, V \rangle$; it follows that $pWp$ is the Banach dual of $V/(pWp)^\circ$, where $(pWp)^\circ$ denotes the polar of $pWp$ in $V$. Thus $pWp$ is a $W^*$-algebra.

For the continuity of $x \mapsto pxp$, note that the kernel of this map contains the $\sigma(W, V)$-closed subspace $Wq + qW$ where $q = e - p$. The result follows from the corollary of (6.1).
