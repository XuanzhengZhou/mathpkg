In a $W^*$-algebra, the projections form a norm-total set: every element, and in particular every positive element, can be approximated in norm by linear combinations of projections with positive real coefficients. The proof uses Zorn's lemma to embed any positive element in a maximal commutative $C^*$-subalgebra, whose spectrum space is Stonian (extremally disconnected), guaranteeing an abundance of projections.
