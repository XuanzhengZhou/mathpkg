Every normal element $x$ in a $C^*$-algebra $A$ attains its norm at some state. The proof passes to the commutative $C^*$-subalgebra generated by $x$ and the unit, uses the Gelfand representation to identify it with $C(K)$, and then applies the Hahn-Banach theorem to extend a point evaluation (Dirac measure) to a state on the whole algebra. This lemma is crucial for proving that the state space separates points of $A$.
