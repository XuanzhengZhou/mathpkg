A $C^*$-algebra is unital precisely when the supremum of the positive elements in its open unit ball exists, and that supremum is exactly the algebra's unit element. This provides an intrinsic order-theoretic characterization of unital $C^*$-algebras without reference to an explicitly given unit. The proof uses the lemma that such a supremum is a projection and that the hereditary subalgebra it generates is the whole algebra.
