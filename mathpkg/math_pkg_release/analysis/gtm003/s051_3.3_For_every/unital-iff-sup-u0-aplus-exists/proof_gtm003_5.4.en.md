Suppose $p := \sup(U_0 \cap A_+)$ exists in $A$; by the lemma, $p^2 = p$, and it is clear that $p$ is the unit of $pAp$. But since $pAp$ is a hereditary subalgebra of $A$ by (5.1), it follows that the interval $[0, p] \subset pAp$ contains $U_0 \cap A_+$. Because $A$ is the closed linear span (over $\mathbb{C}$) of $U_0 \cap A_+$, we have $A = pAp$, and $p$ is the unit of $A$.

Conversely, if $A$ is unital with unit $e$, then $e = \sup(U_0 \cap A_+)$ since $U_0 \cap A_+$ is directed and constitutes an approximate unit (3.3).
