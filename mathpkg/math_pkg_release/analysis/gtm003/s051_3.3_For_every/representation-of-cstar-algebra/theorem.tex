If $A$ is any $C^*$-algebra and $H$ is some Hilbert space, a homomorphism $\pi : A \to \mathcal{L}(H)$ is called a \textit{representation} of $A$; the representation is \textit{faithful} if $\pi$ is injective. $\pi$ is called a \textit{cyclic representation} if there exists $\xi \in H$ such that the orbit $\{\pi(x)\xi : x \in A\}$ is dense in $H$; $\xi$ is called a \textit{cyclic vector} of $\pi$ and usually assumed to satisfy $\|\xi\| = 1$.
