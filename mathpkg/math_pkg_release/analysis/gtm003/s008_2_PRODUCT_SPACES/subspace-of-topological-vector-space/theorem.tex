A subset $M \neq \emptyset$ of a vector space $L$ over $K$ is called a \textbf{subspace} if it is invariant under addition and scalar multiplication; that is, for all $x, y \in M$ and $\lambda \in K$, we have $x + y \in M$ and $\lambda x \in M$. When $L$ is equipped with a vector topology $\mathfrak{T}$, the pair $(M, \mathfrak{T}|_M)$ is a topological vector space in its own right.
