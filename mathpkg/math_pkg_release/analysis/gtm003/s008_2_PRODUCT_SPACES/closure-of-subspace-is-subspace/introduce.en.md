In a topological vector space, the topological closure of an algebraic subspace is again a subspace. This result connects the algebraic structure of the space with its topology: taking the closure preserves the subspace property. The proof relies on the continuity axioms $(LT)_1$ and $(LT)_2$ that govern the compatibility of vector addition and scalar multiplication with the topology.
