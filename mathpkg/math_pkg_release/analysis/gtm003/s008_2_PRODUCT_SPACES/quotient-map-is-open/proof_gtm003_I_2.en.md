**Proof.** By definition of the quotient topology $\hat{\mathfrak{T}}$, the open sets in $L/M$ are precisely the sets $\phi(H)$ where $H \subset L$ satisfies that $H + M$ is open in $L$. If $G \subset L$ is open, then $G + M = \bigcup_{y \in M} (G + y)$ is a union of open sets (since translation is a homeomorphism by $(LT)_1$), hence open in $L$. Thus $\phi(G)$ satisfies the defining condition for open sets in $L/M$, so $\phi(G)$ is open in $\hat{\mathfrak{T}}$. Therefore $\phi$ is an open map. $\square$
