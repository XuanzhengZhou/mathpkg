Given a topological vector space $L$ and a subspace $M$, the quotient space $L/M$ is endowed with the quotient topology -- the finest topology that makes the canonical projection $\phi: L \to L/M$ continuous. This construction yields another topological vector space, and the quotient map has the important property of being open: it sends open sets in $L$ to open sets in $L/M$. The quotient topology is the natural way to transfer the vector space topology to the quotient.
