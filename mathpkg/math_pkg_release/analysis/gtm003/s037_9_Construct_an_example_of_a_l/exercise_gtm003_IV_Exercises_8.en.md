Let $\{ \langle F_\alpha, G_\alpha \rangle : \alpha \in \mathbf{A} \}$ be a family of dualities over $K$ and let $F = \prod_\alpha F_\alpha$, $G = \bigoplus_\alpha G_\alpha$. $F$ and $G$ are placed in duality in the canonical way.

(a) $\beta(F, G)$ is the product of the topologies $\beta(F_\alpha, G_\alpha)$, and $\beta(G, F)$ is the l.c. direct sum topology of the topologies $\beta(G_\alpha, F_\alpha)$. (Use (4.2).)

(b) The weak topology $\sigma(G, F)$ is the l.c. direct sum topology of the topologies $\sigma(G_\alpha, F_\alpha)$ if and only if the number of spaces $F_\alpha \neq \{0\}$ is finite.

(c) A fundamental family of convex, circled, weakly compact subsets of $F$ (respectively, $G$) is obtained by forming arbitrary products of like subsets of the $F_\alpha$ (respectively, by forming arbitrary finite sums of like subsets of the $G_\alpha$).
