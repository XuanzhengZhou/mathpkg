Given a family of dualities $\langle F_\alpha, G_\alpha \rangle$, the product $F = \prod_\alpha F_\alpha$ and the direct sum $G = \bigoplus_\alpha G_\alpha$ are placed in duality by the bilinear form $f(x, y) = \sum_\alpha \langle x_\alpha, y_\alpha \rangle$. Since only finitely many components of $y$ are non-zero, the sum is always finite. This construction is the foundation for studying duality between products and direct sums of locally convex spaces.
