**Proof.** Let $C$ be a convex, circled, $\sigma(E', \tilde{E})$-compact subset of $E'$. Since the embedding $E \hookrightarrow \tilde{E}$ is continuous with dense image, the topology $\sigma(E', E)$ is coarser than $\sigma(E', \tilde{E})$. Hence $C$ is a fortiori $\sigma(E', E)$-compact. As $E$ is a Mackey space, every $\sigma(E', E)$-compact convex circled set is $\mathfrak{T}$-equicontinuous, where $\mathfrak{T}$ is the given topology on $E$. Thus $C$ is $\mathfrak{T}$-equicontinuous. By continuity of the extension of equicontinuous sets to the completion, $C$ is also equicontinuous for the topology of $\tilde{E}$. Therefore $\tilde{E}$ is a Mackey space. $\square$
