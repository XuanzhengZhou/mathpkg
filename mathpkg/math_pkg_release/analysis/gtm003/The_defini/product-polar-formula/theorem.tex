Let $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$ be a saturated family of $\sigma(G_\alpha, F_\alpha)$-bounded sets containing all finite subsets, for each $\alpha \in A$. Let $\mathfrak{S} = \{S = \prod_\alpha S_\alpha : S_\alpha \in \mathfrak{S}_\alpha\}$. Then

$$S^\circ = \Gamma_\alpha S_\alpha^\circ,$$

where $^\circ$ denotes the polar with respect to $\langle F, G \rangle$ and $\Gamma$ denotes the absolute convex hull. Thus the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology on $G$ is identical with the locally convex direct sum of the $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$-topologies.
