**Proof.** Let $C$ be a convex, circled, compact subset of $E'_\sigma$. Its polar $C^\circ$ in $E$ is a 0-neighborhood for the Mackey topology $\tau(E, E')$, by definition of the Mackey topology. Hence $C^\circ$ absorbs every $\tau(E, E')$-bounded set, and therefore every $\sigma(E, E')$-bounded set in $E$ (since boundedness is unchanged between consistent topologies). Consequently, $C$ is absorbed by the polar of every bounded set in $E$, which means precisely that $C$ is bounded in $E'_\beta$. $\square$
