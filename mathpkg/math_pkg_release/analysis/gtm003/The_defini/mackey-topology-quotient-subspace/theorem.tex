Let $M$ be a subspace and let $F$ be a quotient space of the l.c.s. $E$. The Mackey topology $\tau(F, F')$ is the quotient topology of $\tau(E, E')$; if the restriction of $\tau(E, E')$ to $M$ is metrizable, it is identical with $\tau(M, M')$.

Consequently, every (separated) quotient of a Mackey space is a Mackey space, and every metrizable subspace of a Mackey space is a Mackey space.
