Let $E, F$ be l.c.s. and denote by $\mathcal{L}_s(E, F)$ the space of continuous linear maps of $E$ into $F$ under the topology of simple convergence. The correspondence $\sum x_i \otimes y_i' \to f$, defined by

$$f(u) = \sum \langle u x_i, y_i' \rangle \quad (u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)),$$

is an (algebraic) isomorphism of $E \otimes F'$ onto the dual of $\mathcal{L}_s(E, F)$.
