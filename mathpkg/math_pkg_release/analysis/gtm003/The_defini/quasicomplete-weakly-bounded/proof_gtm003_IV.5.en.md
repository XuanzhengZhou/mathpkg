**Proof.** The polar $B^{\circ}$ of every convex, circled, bounded subset $B \subset E'$ is a barrel in $E$ by (1.6); hence $B^{\circ}$ absorbs every bounded subset of $E$ which is convex, circled, and complete, by (II, 8.5). But the bounded subsets of $E$ with these properties form a fundamental family of bounded sets, since $E$ is quasi-complete.

Thus $B^{\circ}$ absorbs all bounded subsets of $E$, meaning that $B$ is strongly bounded. This shows that every weakly bounded subset of $E'$ is strongly bounded, i.e., $\mathfrak{B}_\sigma = \mathfrak{B}$. The corollary follows since for a quasi-complete infrabarreled space, $\mathfrak{E} = \mathfrak{B} = \mathfrak{B}_\sigma$, which is the defining property of barreled spaces. $\square$
