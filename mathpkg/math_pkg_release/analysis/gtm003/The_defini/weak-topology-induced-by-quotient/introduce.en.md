This corollary of Theorem 4.1 specializes to weak topologies: the weak topology $\sigma(M, G/M^\circ)$ on a subspace always equals the topology induced by $\sigma(F, G)$. However, the weak topology on the quotient $G/M^\circ$ is the quotient of $\sigma(G, F)$ precisely when $M$ is closed in $F$, highlighting the crucial role of closed subspaces in duality theory.
