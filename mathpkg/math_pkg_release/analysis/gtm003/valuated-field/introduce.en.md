The scalar field $K$ for a topological vector space is always a non-discrete valuated field, typically $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$ with the usual absolute value. The theory also covers non-Archimedean fields. The non-discreteness ensures that scalar multiplication can produce arbitrarily small multiples, essential for the 0-neighborhood structure.
