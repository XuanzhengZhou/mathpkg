Let $A$ be a compact subset of a locally convex space, and suppose its closed convex hull $C = \overline{\operatorname{conv}}(A)$ is also compact. Then every extreme point of $C$ must belong to the original compact set $A$. In other words, taking the closed convex hull does not introduce new extreme points beyond those already present in $A$.
