A non-empty, closed subset $A$ of a convex set $C$ in a real vector space is called a \textit{supporting manifold} of $C$ if whenever $x, y \in C$ and $(1-t)x + ty \in A$ for some $t \in (0,1)$, then $x, y \in A$. A supporting manifold $H$ is called a \textit{supporting hyperplane} if it is a closed real hyperplane in the ambient locally convex space. A supporting manifold $S$ of $A$ is said to \textit{support} $A$ if $S \subset M$ and $S$ is a supporting manifold of $M$, supposing in addition that $M \cap A \neq \emptyset$. An \textit{extreme point} of $A$ is a point $x_0 \in A$ such that $\{x_0\}$ supports $A$.
