In the study of convex sets in topological vector spaces, a supporting manifold is a closed subset $A$ of a convex set $C$ with the property that any line segment lying in $C$ whose interior meets $A$ must have its endpoints also in $A$. A supporting hyperplane is a supporting manifold that is also a closed real hyperplane. An extreme point of a convex set is a point that forms a singleton supporting manifold, meaning it cannot be expressed as a nontrivial convex combination of other points in the set.
