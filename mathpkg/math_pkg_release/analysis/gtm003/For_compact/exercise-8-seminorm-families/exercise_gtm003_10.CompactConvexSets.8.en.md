(a) Let $P$ be a family of semi-norms on $E$, and let $U_{p,n} = \{x: p(x) < n^{-1}\}$ for $p \in P, n \in \mathbb{N}$. For $\{U_{p,n}: p \in P, n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ to be a $0$-neighborhood base of a locally convex topology on $E$, it is necessary and sufficient that the family $\{cp: c > 0, p \in P\}$ of semi-norms be directed.

(b) If $P_0$ is a family of semi-norms generating the topology of the l.c.s. $E$, the family $P$ of the suprema of non-empty finite subsets of $P_0$ is directed and generates the topology of $E$.

(c) Let $M$ be a subspace of $E$. For a given semi-norm $p$ on $E$, define $\hat{p}(\hat{x}) = \inf \{p(x): x \in \hat{x}\}$ $(\hat{x} \in E/M)$; $\hat{p}$ is a semi-norm on $E/M$. If $P$ is a directed family of semi-norms on $E$, the family $\{\hat{p}: p \in P\}$ generates on $E/M$ the quotient of the topology generated by the family $P$.
