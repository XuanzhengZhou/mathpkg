A radial (or absorbing) subset of a vector space over a non-discrete valuated field is a set that absorbs every finite subset. Equivalently, for any point in the space, a sufficiently large scalar multiple of the radial set contains that point. Radial sets generalize the idea of neighborhoods of zero in topological vector spaces — indeed, every neighborhood of zero in a topological vector space is radial. The family of radial subsets is closed under finite intersections.
