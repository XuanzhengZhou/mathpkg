A circled (or balanced) subset of a vector space over a non-discrete valuated field is a set that is invariant under multiplication by all scalars with absolute value at most 1. This property generalizes the geometric notion of a set that is symmetric about the origin and star-shaped in a particular way. The family of circled subsets is closed under arbitrary intersections, a crucial property for constructing the circled hull. In topological vector spaces, every neighborhood of zero contains a circled neighborhood.
