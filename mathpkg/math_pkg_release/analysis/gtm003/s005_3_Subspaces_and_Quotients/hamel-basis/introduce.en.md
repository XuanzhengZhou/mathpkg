A Hamel basis (or simply a basis) of a vector space is a maximal linearly independent subset. Every vector in the space can be expressed uniquely as a finite linear combination of basis elements. Zorn's lemma guarantees that every vector space has a basis, and any linearly independent set can be extended to one. In infinite-dimensional spaces, Hamel bases are typically uncountable and distinct from the Schauder bases used in functional analysis.
