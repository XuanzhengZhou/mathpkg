A valuated field is a field K equipped with an absolute value, which induces a metric and a uniform structure. This topological structure makes K a topological field, serving as the scalar field for the theory of topological vector spaces. The valuation allows one to speak of convergence, boundedness, and continuity in the field itself, which is a prerequisite for the entire framework of topological vector spaces over K.
