This proposition describes how the properties of being circled (balanced) and radial (absorbing) are preserved under linear maps between vector spaces over a non-discrete valuated field. Circled sets are preserved under both direct images and preimages of linear maps. Radial sets are always preserved under preimages, but direct images of radial sets require surjectivity of the linear map to guarantee the image is again radial.
