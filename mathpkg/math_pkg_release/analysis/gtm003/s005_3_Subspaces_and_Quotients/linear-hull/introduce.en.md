The linear hull (or span) of a subset A of a vector space L is the smallest subspace containing A. It can be constructed either as the intersection of all subspaces containing A or as the set of all finite linear combinations of elements of A. The linear hull of the empty set is the zero subspace {0}. This construction is fundamental to the theory of vector spaces and underlies the concept of a basis.
