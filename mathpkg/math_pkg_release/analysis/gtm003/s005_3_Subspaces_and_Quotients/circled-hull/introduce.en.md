The circled hull (or balanced hull) of a subset A of a vector space is the smallest circled set containing A, obtained as the intersection of all circled supersets. Since the family of circled sets is closed under arbitrary intersections, this construction is always well-defined. The circled hull can also be described explicitly as the set of all scalar multiples λx with x ∈ A and |λ| ≤ 1. This construction is used extensively in the study of topological vector spaces to generate circled neighborhoods of zero.
