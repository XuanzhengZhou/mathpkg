**Proof.** For any 0-neighborhood $U$ there exists another, $V$, such that $V + V \subset U$. Since $y \in \overline{V}$ only if $(y - V) \cap V$ is non-empty, it follows that $\overline{V} \subset V + V \subset U$. Hence $x + U$ contains the closed neighborhood $x + \overline{V}$ of $x$.
