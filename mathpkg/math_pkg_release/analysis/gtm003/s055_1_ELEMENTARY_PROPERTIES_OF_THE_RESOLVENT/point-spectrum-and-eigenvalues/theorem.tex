The subset of $\sigma(u)$ in which $(\lambda e - u)$ fails to be one-to-one is called the point spectrum $\pi(u)$ of $u$. An element $\lambda_0 \in \pi(u)$ is called an eigenvalue of $u$, and the null space of $(\lambda_0 e - u)$ is the corresponding eigenspace $N(\lambda_0)$. The dimension of $N(\lambda_0)$ is called the (geometric) multiplicity of $\lambda_0$, and the non-zero elements of $N(\lambda_0)$ are termed eigenvectors of $u$ for $\lambda_0$. (The terms characteristic value, characteristic space, and characteristic vector are also in current use.)
