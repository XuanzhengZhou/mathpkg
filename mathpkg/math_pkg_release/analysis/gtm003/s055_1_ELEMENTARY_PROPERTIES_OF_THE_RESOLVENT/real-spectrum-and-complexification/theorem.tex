If $E$ is a Banach space over $\mathbb{R}$ and $u \in \mathcal{L}(E)$, the real spectrum $\sigma_{\mathbb{R}}(u)$ can be defined as the subset of $\mathbb{R}$ in which $(\lambda e - u)$ fails to be an automorphism of $E$. Analogously, the real resolvent of $u$ is the function $\lambda \mapsto (\lambda e - u)^{-1}$ with domain $\mathbb{R} \setminus \sigma_{\mathbb{R}}(u)$.

Let $(E, \|\cdot\|)$ be a Banach space over $\mathbb{R}$; the complexification $E_1$ of the t.v.s. $E$ is a complete normable space over $\mathbb{C}$. The norm on $E_1$ can be defined so that the imbedding of $E$ into $E_1$ becomes a real norm isomorphism:
$$\|x + iy\|_1 = \sup_{0 \leq \theta < 2\pi} \|(\cos \theta)x + (\sin \theta)y\|.$$
This is a generalization of the definition of the usual absolute value on $\mathbb{C}$, considering $\mathbb{C}$ as the complexification of $\mathbb{R}$. Every $u \in \mathcal{L}(E)$ can be extended to its complexification in $\mathcal{L}(E_1)$, thereby subsuming the real case under the complex spectral theory.
