For a continuous linear operator $u$ on a non-trivial complex Banach space $E$, its spectrum $\sigma(u)$ is a non-empty compact subset of $\mathbb{C}$ defined as the complement of the resolvent set $\rho(u)$, where the resolvent $R(\lambda) = (\lambda e - u)^{-1}$ exists as a locally holomorphic function. The spectral radius $r(u)$ is the radius of the smallest closed disc centered at the origin that contains $\sigma(u)$, and the spectral circle is its boundary. An equivalent algebraic characterization is that $\lambda \in \sigma(u)$ precisely when $\lambda e - u$ is not an automorphism of $E$.
