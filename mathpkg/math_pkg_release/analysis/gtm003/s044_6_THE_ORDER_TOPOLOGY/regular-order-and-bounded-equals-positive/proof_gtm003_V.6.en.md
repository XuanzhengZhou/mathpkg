This is immediate in view of (6.1) and (3.3). By Theorem 6.1, the dual of $(L, \mathfrak{T}_0)$ is $L^b$. By Theorem 6.2, $C$ is normal for $\mathfrak{T}_0$, so by Proposition 3.3, the topological dual is $C' - C'$. Since $C$ is normal, $C'$ consists precisely of positive continuous linear forms, and the order of $L$ is Archimedean. The equality $L^b = L^+$ follows from the identification of the dual spaces, and regularity follows from the fact that $L^+$ separates points in $L$.
