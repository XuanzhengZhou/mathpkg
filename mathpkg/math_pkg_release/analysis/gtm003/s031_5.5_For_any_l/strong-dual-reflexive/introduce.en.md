This corollary of Theorem 5.6 establishes that reflexivity is inherited by the strong dual. If $E$ is reflexive, then the strong dual $E'_\beta$ is barreled (by 5.5(c) since $E$ is semi-reflexive) and every bounded subset of $E'_\beta$ is relatively $\sigma(E', E)$-compact (by 5.2, since $E$ is barreled). The converse is false in general, although quasi-complete Mackey spaces whose strong dual is semi-reflexive are reflexive (Exercise 18).
