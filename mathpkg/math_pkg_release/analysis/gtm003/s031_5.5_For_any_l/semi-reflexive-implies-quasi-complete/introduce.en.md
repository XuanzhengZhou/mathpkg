Quasi-completeness is a frequently used completeness property in the theory of locally convex spaces: a space is quasi-complete if every closed bounded subset is complete. This corollary follows from the characterization theorem (5.5) and the fact that equicontinuous sets in the dual correspond, via the bipolar theorem (1.6), to bounded subsets of the space. It implies in particular that semi-reflexive normed spaces are Banach spaces.
