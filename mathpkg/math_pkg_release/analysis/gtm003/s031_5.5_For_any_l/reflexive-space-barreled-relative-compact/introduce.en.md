A locally convex space $E$ is reflexive if it is semi-reflexive (i.e., the canonical map $E \to E''$ is surjective) and barreled. This theorem, stated as the most useful characterization of reflexive spaces among many possible, characterizes reflexivity purely in terms of the original topology: the space must be barreled, and every bounded set must be relatively compact in the weak topology $\sigma(E, E')$. The proof synthesizes the equivalences of (5.5) with the properties of barreled spaces established in (1.6).
