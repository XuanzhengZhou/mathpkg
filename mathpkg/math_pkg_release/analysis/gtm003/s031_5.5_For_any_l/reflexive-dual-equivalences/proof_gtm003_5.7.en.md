(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b): If $E_\tau$ is reflexive, then $\beta(E', E)$ is consistent with $\langle E, E' \rangle$, whence $\beta(E', E) = \tau(E', E)$. Thus $E'_\tau$ is the strong dual of $E$; hence $E'_\tau$ is reflexive by Corollary 1 of (5.6).

(b) $\Rightarrow$ (c): If $E'_\tau$ is reflexive, then clearly $E'_\sigma$ is semi-reflexive; moreover, by (5.6) $E'_\tau$ is barreled, hence every bounded subset of $E$ is relatively $\sigma(E, E')$-compact by (5.2), which shows $E_\sigma$ to be semi-reflexive by (5.5).

(c) $\Rightarrow$ (d): (c) implies that both $\beta(E', E)$ and $\beta(E, E')$ are consistent with $\langle E, E' \rangle$, hence that $\beta(E', E) = \tau(E', E)$ and $\beta(E, E') = \tau(E, E')$. Since all barrels in $E_\tau$ and $E'_\tau$, respectively, are $0$-neighborhoods for $\beta(E, E')$ and $\beta(E', E)$ by (1.6), it follows that $E_\tau$ and $E'_\tau$ are barreled.

(d) $\Rightarrow$ (a): $E_\tau$ is barreled and by (5.5) semi-reflexive, which implies that $E_\tau$ is reflexive.
