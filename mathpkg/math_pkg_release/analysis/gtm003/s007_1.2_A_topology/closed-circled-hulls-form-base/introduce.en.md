This corollary combines Theorem 1.2, Theorem 1.3, and the property that the closure of a circled set is circled (1.1(v)) to show that every t.v.s. admits a $0$-neighborhood base consisting of closed, circled sets. This is a convenient normalization: one may always assume without loss of generality that basic $0$-neighborhoods are both closed and circled.
