In a topological vector space, a Cauchy filter is defined by the condition that its members become arbitrarily "small" in the sense that their difference sets are eventually contained in any given $0$-neighborhood. A subset $A$ is complete if every Cauchy filter in $A$ converges to an element of $A$, and semi-complete (or sequentially complete) if every Cauchy sequence converges in $A$.
