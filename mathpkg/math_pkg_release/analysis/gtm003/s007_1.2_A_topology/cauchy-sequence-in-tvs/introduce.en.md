A Cauchy sequence in a topological vector space is the sequential analogue of a Cauchy filter: the differences of terms eventually lie in any prescribed $0$-neighborhood. This definition reduces to the usual metric notion when the t.v.s. topology is metrizable. A t.v.s. is semi-complete (sequentially complete) if every Cauchy sequence converges, which is a weaker condition than full completeness in general.
