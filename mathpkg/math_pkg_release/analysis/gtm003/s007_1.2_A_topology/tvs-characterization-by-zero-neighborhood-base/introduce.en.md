Theorem 1.2 provides a fundamental characterization of topological vector spaces purely in terms of the neighborhood base at zero. It establishes that the joint continuity axioms $(LT)_1$ and $(LT)_2$ are equivalent to translation invariance plus three algebraic-topological conditions on a $0$-neighborhood base: the existence of "half-size" neighborhoods (a), that every basic neighborhood is radial and circled (b), and a scaling property (c). For the real or complex numbers, condition (c) is automatically satisfied due to the Archimedean property.
