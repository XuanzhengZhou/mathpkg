Theorem 1.3 establishes that every topological vector space has a base of closed neighborhoods at each point. This follows from the continuity of addition: given any $0$-neighborhood $U$, one can find $V$ with $V + V \subset U$, and then the closure $\bar{V}$ is contained in $U$. Consequently, every Hausdorff t.v.s. is a regular topological space, and the closed $0$-neighborhoods form a neighborhood base.
