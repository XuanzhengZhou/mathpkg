A translation-invariant uniformity on a vector space is one whose entourages are invariant under simultaneous translation of both coordinates. This is the natural uniformity for topological vector spaces, as it makes the uniform structure compatible with the vector space operations. Every t.v.s. is uniformisable with a translation-invariant uniformity (Proposition 1.4), which implies that every Hausdorff t.v.s. is completely regular.
