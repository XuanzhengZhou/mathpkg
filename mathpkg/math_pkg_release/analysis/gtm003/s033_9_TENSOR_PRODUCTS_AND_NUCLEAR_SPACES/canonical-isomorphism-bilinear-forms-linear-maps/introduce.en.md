Let $E$ and $F$ be locally convex spaces. A separately continuous bilinear form $v$ on $E \times F$ induces, via the pairing, a continuous linear map $u \colon E \to F'_\sigma$ into the weak dual of $F$, and similarly an adjoint map $u' \colon F \to E'_\sigma$. These correspondences are algebraic isomorphisms, called the canonical isomorphisms, and they allow one to identify spaces of bilinear forms with spaces of linear maps valued in weak duals. The continuous bilinear forms correspond precisely to those linear maps that send a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$ to an equicontinuous subset of $F'$.
