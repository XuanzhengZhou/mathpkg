On the algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$ of two locally convex spaces, one can define a locally convex topology called the topology of bi-equicontinuous convergence. A base of neighborhoods of zero for this topology consists of the polars of elementary tensors $S \otimes T$, where $S$ and $T$ range over all equicontinuous subsets of the dual spaces $E'$ and $F'$, respectively. This topology is intermediate between the projective and inductive tensor product topologies, and its completion is denoted by $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ (equipped with the bi-equicontinuous topology). The dual of this completed space is the space $\mathcal{J}(E, F)$ of integral bilinear forms.
