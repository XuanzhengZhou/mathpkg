Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces with duals $E', F'$, and consider the algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$. A base of $0$-neighborhoods for the topology of bi-equicontinuous convergence on $E \otimes F$ is formed by the polars, with respect to the duality between $E \otimes F$ and $E' \otimes F' \subset B(E, F)$, of the sets $S \otimes T$, where $S \subset E'$ and $T \subset F'$ are arbitrary equicontinuous sets. Consequently, the bipolars $(\Gamma S \otimes T)^\circ$ in $B(E, F)$ (equivalently, the closures $(\Gamma S \otimes T)^-$ for $\sigma(B(E, F), E \otimes F)$) form a fundamental family of equicontinuous sets in the algebraic dual $B(E, F)$ of $E \otimes F$.
