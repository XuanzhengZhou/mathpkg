This theorem establishes a fundamental dichotomy for topological vector spaces over complete fields: a Hausdorff t.v.s. is locally compact if and only if it is finite-dimensional (and the field is locally compact). The forward direction, proved here, shows that any non-zero locally compact Hausdorff t.v.s. forces both local compactness of the scalar field and finite dimension of the space. Combined with Theorem 3.2, this completely characterizes locally compact Hausdorff t.v.s. as exactly the finite-dimensional spaces over locally compact complete fields. The proof uses a compact circled neighborhood and a null sequence to construct a neighborhood base, ultimately showing that the space is precompact, hence finite-dimensional.
