A hyperplane is a maximal proper affine subspace of a vector space; its corresponding subspace has codimension 1. Two hyperplanes are parallel exactly when they are translates of the same homogeneous hyperplane (one passing through the origin). Hyperplanes are fundamental objects in functional analysis, as they appear in the geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem and serve as separating sets for convex sets.
