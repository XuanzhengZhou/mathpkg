This theorem provides a complete classification of one-dimensional Hausdorff topological vector spaces over a field $K$: every such space is isomorphic to the canonical one-dimensional t.v.s. $K_0$. The isomorphism is simply scalar multiplication by a non-zero vector $x_0$, and all isomorphisms arise in this way. This result is the base case for the inductive classification of all finite-dimensional Hausdorff t.v.s. when the field $K$ is complete.
