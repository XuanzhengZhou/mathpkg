This fundamental theorem classifies all finite-dimensional Hausdorff topological vector spaces over a complete field $K$: every such space of dimension $n$ is isomorphic to $K_0^n$ with the product topology. The isomorphism is given by the coordinate map with respect to any Hamel basis. An important consequence is that the product topology is the unique Hausdorff vector space topology on $K^n$; there is no room for exotic Hausdorff topologies on finite-dimensional spaces over complete fields. This theorem is due to Tychonoff.
