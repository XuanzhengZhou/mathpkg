The text states this as "clearly" without proof. If the space is complete, then Cauchy filters converge, hence certainly have cluster points (limits are cluster points). Conversely, suppose every Cauchy filter has a cluster point. In a metrizable space, take a countable neighborhood base $\{V_n\}$ at a cluster point $x$ of a Cauchy filter $\mathfrak{F}$. Using the Cauchy property, for each $n$ find $F_n \in \mathfrak{F}$ with $F_n \times F_n$ contained in the $n$-th basic vicinity. Then $F_n \cap V_n \neq \varnothing$ (cluster point property), and the filter generated by these intersections converges to $x$, implying the original filter $\mathfrak{F}$ also converges to $x$.
