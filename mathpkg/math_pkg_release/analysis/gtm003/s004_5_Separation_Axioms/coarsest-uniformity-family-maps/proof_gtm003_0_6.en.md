The text states the existence without constructing the proof in detail. The standard construction: For each $\alpha \in \mathbf{A}$, the family of sets $(f_\alpha \times f_\alpha)^{-1}(V_\alpha)$, where $V_\alpha$ ranges over the vicinity filter $\mathfrak{W}_\alpha$ of $X_\alpha$, forms a filter subbase on $X \times X$. The filter $\mathfrak{W}$ generated by these sets is a uniformity on $X$: the diagonal condition holds since each $V_\alpha$ contains the diagonal of $X_\alpha \times X_\alpha$; symmetry follows from symmetry of each $\mathfrak{W}_\alpha$; and the composition property follows from the corresponding property in each $X_\alpha$ and the compatibility of the preimage operation. By construction, each $f_\alpha$ is uniformly continuous for $\mathfrak{W}$. Any uniformity making all $f_\alpha$ uniformly continuous must contain all sets $(f_\alpha \times f_\alpha)^{-1}(V_\alpha)$, hence must be finer than $\mathfrak{W}$, proving $\mathfrak{W}$ is coarsest.
