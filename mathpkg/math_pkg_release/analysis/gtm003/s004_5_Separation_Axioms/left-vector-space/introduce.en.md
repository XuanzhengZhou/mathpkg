A left vector space over a field $K$ is a set equipped with an abelian group structure (vector addition) and a left scalar multiplication by elements of $K$, satisfying the distributivity and associativity axioms. The adjective "left" specifies that scalars multiply on the left, which matters when $K$ is non-commutative. Over commutative fields, left and right vector spaces coincide.
