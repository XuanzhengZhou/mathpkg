The product topology is the coarsest topology on the Cartesian product of a family of topological spaces that makes all projection maps continuous. It is defined as the projective (initial) topology with respect to the projection maps. Equivalently, a basis for the product topology consists of products of open sets where only finitely many factors differ from the whole space.
