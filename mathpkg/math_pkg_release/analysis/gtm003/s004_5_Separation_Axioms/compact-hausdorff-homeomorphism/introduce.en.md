A continuous bijection from a compact space onto a Hausdorff space is automatically a homeomorphism (its inverse is continuous). This result follows because the image of a closed set under such a map is compact, hence closed in the Hausdorff codomain. An equivalent formulation: a compact topology cannot be strictly finer than a comparable Hausdorff topology.
