In a vector space over a field, every element $x$ has a unique additive inverse, denoted $-x$, such that $x + (-x) = 0$. The additive inverse satisfies $(-1)x = -x$ and $-(-x) = x$. This follows directly from the vector space axioms: the zero element is unique, and for each $x$, the element $z$ postulated by axiom (4) is uniquely determined. The existence and uniqueness of additive inverses enables subtraction and makes every vector space an abelian group under addition.
