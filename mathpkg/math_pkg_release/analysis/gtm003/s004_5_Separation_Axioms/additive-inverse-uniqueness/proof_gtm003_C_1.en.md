Let $x \in L$ and suppose $z, z' \in L$ satisfy $x + z = 0$ and $x + z' = 0$. Then:
\[
z = z + 0 = z + (x + z') = (z + x) + z' = (x + z) + z' = 0 + z' = z'.
\]
The steps use: axiom (3) (zero property), the assumption $x + z' = 0$, associativity (axiom (1)), commutativity (axiom (2)), the assumption $x + z = 0$, and axiom (3) again. Thus the additive inverse is unique.
