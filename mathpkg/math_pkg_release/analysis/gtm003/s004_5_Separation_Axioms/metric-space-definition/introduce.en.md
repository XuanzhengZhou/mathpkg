A metric space is viewed as a uniform space whose uniformity is generated by the metric: the basic vicinities are the $\varepsilon$-neighborhoods $W_n = \{(x,y) : d(x,y) < n^{-1}\}$ of the diagonal. This uniformity is always separated. Consequently, all uniform concepts (uniform continuity, completeness, precompactness) apply to metric spaces. Historically, uniform spaces were developed as a generalization of metric spaces.
