The text states this as an "if and only if" equivalence without providing a detailed proof. The forward direction (separated $\Rightarrow$ unique limits) is standard: if $\mathfrak{F} \to x$ and $\mathfrak{F} \to y$ with $x \neq y$, then by the Hausdorff property there exist disjoint neighborhoods $U_x, U_y$. Since $\mathfrak{F}$ converges to both points, it must eventually be contained in both $U_x$ and $U_y$, implying $U_x \cap U_y \neq \varnothing$, contradiction. Conversely, if $X$ is not separated, there exist $x \neq y$ such that every neighborhood of $x$ meets every neighborhood of $y$; the filter generated by all neighborhoods of both points converges to both $x$ and $y$, violating uniqueness.
