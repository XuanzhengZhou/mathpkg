A compact space is a Hausdorff topological space in which every open cover admits a finite subcover. This is the fundamental compactness notion in topology, generalising the Heine-Borel property of closed bounded sets in Euclidean space. Compactness can be equivalently characterised via the finite intersection property for closed sets, cluster points of filters, or convergence of ultrafilters.
