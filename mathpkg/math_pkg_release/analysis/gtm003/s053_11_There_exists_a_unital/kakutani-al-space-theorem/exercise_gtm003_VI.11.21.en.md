(Theorem of Kakutani). Every abstract $L$-space ($AL$-space, cf.\ Chapter~V, Section~8) $E$ is isometrically and lattice isomorphic with $L^1(X, \mu)$ for some locally compact space $X$ and Borel measure $\mu$ on $X$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If $E$ possesses a weak order unit $u$, then $E$ is isometrically and order isomorphic with $L^1(\mu)$ for some Borel measure $\mu$ on a compact space $K$. (Observe that the principal lattice ideal $E_u$ is isomorphic to some $C(K)$ by (V.8.5), and dense in $E$. The norm of $E$ then defines a (positive) Radon measure on $K$ such that $E = L^1(K, \mu)$; cf.\ Chapter~V, Exercise~22.)

\item[(b)] In the general case, denote by $(u_\lambda)_{\lambda \in \Lambda}$ a maximal orthogonal family of normalized positive elements of $E$. The closures $\overline{E_{u_\lambda}}$ are, by (a), isomorphic to spaces $L^1(K_\lambda, \mu_\lambda)$, and $E$ is the $\ell^1$-direct sum of these spaces.
\end{enumerate}
