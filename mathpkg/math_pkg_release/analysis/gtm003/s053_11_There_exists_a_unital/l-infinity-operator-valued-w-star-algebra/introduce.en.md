The space $L^\infty(X, \mu; \mathcal{L}(H))$ of essentially bounded, weakly measurable operator-valued functions on a $\sigma$-finite measure space forms a $W^*$-algebra (von Neumann algebra). This result generalizes the classical fact that $L^\infty(X, \mu)$ is a commutative $W^*$-algebra, and extends to any $W^*$-algebra with separable predual in place of $\mathcal{L}(H)$.
