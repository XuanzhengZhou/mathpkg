The $W^*$-group algebra $W(G)$ of a group $G$ is the von Neumann algebra generated by the left regular representation of $G$ on $\ell^2(G)$. It is defined as the bicommutant (double commutant) of the family of operators $\{u_g : g \in G\}$ where each $u_g$ acts by left translation. $W(G)$ is a fundamental object in the theory of von Neumann algebras, providing a rich source of examples including the hyperfinite $\mathrm{II}_1$ factor when $G$ is an amenable ICC group.
