The $W^*$-group algebra $W(G)$ is a factor (its center is trivial) precisely when every non-identity element of $G$ has infinitely many conjugates, i.e., $G$ is an ICC (Infinite Conjugacy Classes) group. Combined with the existence of a faithful normal trace (Exercise 26a), this implies $W(G)$ is a type $\mathrm{II}_1$ factor. This result provides a fundamental construction of $\mathrm{II}_1$ factors from discrete groups.
