Let $V$ denote a Banach space with dual $W$. If $W$ is the direct algebraic sum (Chapter~I, Section~2) of $n$ ($n \in \mathbb{N}$) $\sigma(W, V)$-closed subspaces $M_j$ ($j = 1, \ldots, n$), then the sum is topological (i.e., each of the projections $\pi_i : W \to M_i$ with kernel $N_i = \sum_{j \neq i} M_j$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-continuous). (Observe that each $\pi_i$ is norm continuous (cf.\ proof of III.2.1, Corollary~3), which implies that the unit ball of $N_i$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-compact; finally apply (IV.6.4) and the corollary of (6.1).)
