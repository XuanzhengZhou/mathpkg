The Bauer-Namioka extension theorem provides a necessary and sufficient condition for extending a positive linear form from a subspace to the entire ordered topological vector space while preserving positivity and continuity. The criterion involves boundedness of the real part of the form on the intersection of the subspace with a set of the form $U - C$, where $U$ is a convex zero-neighborhood. This theorem is a fundamental tool in the theory of positive linear forms and ordered vector spaces.
