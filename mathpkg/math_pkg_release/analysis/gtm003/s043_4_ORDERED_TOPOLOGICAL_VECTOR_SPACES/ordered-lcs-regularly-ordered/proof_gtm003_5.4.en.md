If $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ is an ordered l.c.s., then $C$ is closed by definition (LTO), and the bipolar theorem (IV, 1.5) shows $-C$ to be the polar of $C'$ with respect to $\langle E, E' \rangle$. Since $C \cap -C = \{0\}$ (the cone is proper), it follows that $C' - C'$ separates points in $E$, and consequently $C^* - C^* = L^+$ separates points in $E$. Hence $L_0$ and $L_0^+$ are placed in duality, which means precisely that $E$ is regularly ordered.
