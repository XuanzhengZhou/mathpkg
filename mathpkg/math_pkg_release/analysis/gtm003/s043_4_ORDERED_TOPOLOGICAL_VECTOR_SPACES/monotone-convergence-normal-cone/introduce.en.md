This theorem establishes a monotone convergence property for ordered locally convex spaces with normal positive cones. It states that if a directed set converges weakly to zero, then it also converges in the original topology. The result is a cornerstone of the theory of ordered topological vector spaces, providing a robust link between weak order-convergence and topological convergence under the normality hypothesis.
