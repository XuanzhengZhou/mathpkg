Let $M$ be a finite-dimensional subspace of the Archimedean ordered vector space $L$. The induced order on $M$ has positive cone $C_M = C \cap M$. Since $L$ is Archimedean ordered, the canonical order of $M$ is also Archimedean. In a finite-dimensional real vector space, there is a unique Hausdorff vector topology, and it is well known that the positive cone of an Archimedean ordered finite-dimensional vector space is closed in this topology (the positive cone is the intersection of closed half-spaces determined by the positive linear functionals that separate it from elements outside the cone). Hence $C_M$ is closed in $M$.
