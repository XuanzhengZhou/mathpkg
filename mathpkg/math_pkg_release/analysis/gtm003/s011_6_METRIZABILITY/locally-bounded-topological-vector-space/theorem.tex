A topological vector space $L$ is said to be \textbf{locally bounded} if $L$ possesses a bounded neighborhood of $0$. Such a space has a neighborhood base of $0$ consisting of bounded sets.

The spaces $L^p$ for $0 < p \leq 1$, equipped with the pseudo-norm

$$f \mapsto \int_I |f|^p \, d\mu,$$

are examples of locally bounded Hausdorff t.v.s.
