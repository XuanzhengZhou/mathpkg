**Proof of Theorem 6.2.** Let $V$ be a bounded $0$-neighborhood in $L$ and let $\{\lambda_n\}$ be a sequence of non-zero elements of $K$ such that $\lim \lambda_n = 0$. If $U$ is any circled neighborhood of $0$, there exists $\lambda \in K$ such that $V \subset \lambda U$, since $V$ is bounded. Choose $n$ such that $|\lambda_n \lambda| \leq 1$; then $\lambda_n V \subset U$, since $U$ is circled. It follows that $\{\lambda_n V : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ is a $0$-neighborhood base, whence $L$ is metrizable by Theorem 6.1. $\square$
