**Proof.** Let $u$ be irreducible and let $F \neq \{0\}$ be a closed solid subspace invariant under $u$. If $0 \neq x \in F \cap C$, then $y = uR(\lambda, u)x$ is, for suitable $\lambda > r(u)$, a quasi-interior point of $C$ contained in $F$; hence $F = E$.

Conversely, if $u$ leaves no closed proper solid subspace $\neq \{0\}$ of $E$ invariant, then $u(x) > 0$ for each $x > 0$. For suppose $u(x_0) = 0$ for some $x_0 > 0$. Then $u$ would leave the closed solid subspace $G$ invariant which is generated by $x_0$ (since $G$ is the band generated by $x_0$ and $u$ is positive, $u(G) \subset G$ follows from $u(x_0) = 0$). This would imply $G = E$, and consequently $u = 0$, contradicting $u \neq 0$. Therefore, if $x > 0$, then $y = uR(\lambda, u)x > 0$ for arbitrary $\lambda > r(u)$. The closed solid subspace generated by $y$ is invariant under $u$ (since $u$ commutes with its resolvent), and hence must equal $E$. Thus $y$ is a quasi-interior point of $C$, proving that $u$ is irreducible.
