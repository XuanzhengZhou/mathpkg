A positive endomorphism $u$ on an ordered Banach space is irreducible if, for sufficiently large $\lambda$, the transformed element $uR(\lambda, u)x$ is a quasi-interior point of the positive cone whenever $x \neq 0$ is positive. Quasi-interior points are those whose order interval generates a total subset. For Banach lattices, irreducibility is equivalent to the absence of non-trivial closed solid invariant subspaces, which justifies the terminology by analogy with irreducible matrices.
