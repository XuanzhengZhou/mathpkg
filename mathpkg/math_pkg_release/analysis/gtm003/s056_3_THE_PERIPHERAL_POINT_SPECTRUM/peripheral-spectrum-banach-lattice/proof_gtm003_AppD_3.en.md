**Proof.** We can suppose that $r > 0$, and hence for convenience we assume $r = 1$. Let $\alpha x = u(x)$, where $0 \neq x = x_1 + i x_2 \in E_1$ ($x_1, x_2 \in E$) and $|\alpha| = 1$. It follows that
$$|x| = |\alpha x| = |u(x)| \leq u(|x|).$$

Now $\langle |x|, x'_0 \rangle \leq \langle u(|x|), x'_0 \rangle \leq \langle |x|, x'_0 \rangle$ by virtue of the hypothesis $u'(x'_0) \leq x'_0$ (recall $r = 1$). Hence $\langle u(|x|) - |x|, x'_0 \rangle = 0$, and since $x'_0$ is strictly positive, we obtain $|x| = u(|x|)$.

Define $x_0 = |x|$, and consider the solid sublattice $F = \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} n[-x_0, x_0]$ of $E$. Since $E$ is complete and the closed unit ball of $F$ (in the norm induced by the order unit $x_0$) is closed in $E$, $F$ is an AM-space with order unit $x_0$. By Kakutani's representation theorem, $F$ is isometrically order-isomorphic to $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(K)$ for some compact space $K$, with $x_0$ corresponding to the constant function $\mathbf{1}$.

The restriction $v = u|_F$ is a positive endomorphism of $F$ satisfying $v(x_0) = x_0$. Moreover, the restriction $y'_0 = x'_0|_F$ is a strictly positive linear form on $F$ (corresponding to a strictly positive Radon measure on $K$). By construction, $v'(y'_0) \leq y'_0$, so $v$ satisfies the hypotheses of (3.3). The complexification $F_1$ inherits the extended absolute value from $E_1$, and $x \in F_1$ is an eigenvector of $v$ for $\alpha$. Applying Theorem (3.3)(ii) to $v$, we conclude that $\alpha^n$ is an eigenvalue of $v$ for every $n \in \mathbb{Z}$, and hence an eigenvalue of $u$. Thus $r\alpha^n$ ($r = 1$) belongs to the peripheral point spectrum of $u$, and consequently $rH$ is contained in the peripheral point spectrum, where $H$ is the cyclic group generated by $\alpha$.
