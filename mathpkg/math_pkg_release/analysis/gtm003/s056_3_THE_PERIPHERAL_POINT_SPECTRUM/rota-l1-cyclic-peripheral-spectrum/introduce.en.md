This corollary, due to G.-C. Rota, applies Theorem 3.4 to the concrete setting of $L^1(\mu)$ spaces. For any positive endomorphism $u$ of $L^1(\mu)$ whose norm equals its spectral radius, the peripheral point spectrum is always cyclic. The proof exploits the fact that integration against the measure $\mu$ provides a strictly positive linear form $h$ satisfying $u'(h) \leq r(u) h$, precisely the hypothesis required by Theorem 3.4.
