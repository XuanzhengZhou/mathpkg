The peripheral spectrum of a continuous linear operator $u$ on a Banach space is the part of its spectrum that lies on the spectral circle $\{\lambda : |\lambda| = r(u)\}$, where $r(u)$ is the spectral radius. The peripheral point spectrum is the intersection of the peripheral spectrum with the point spectrum $\pi(u)$. These concepts are fundamental in the spectral theory of positive operators on ordered Banach spaces, where the peripheral spectrum often exhibits additional structure such as cyclicity.
