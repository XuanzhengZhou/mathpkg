Let $\langle F, G \rangle$ be a dual system, let $M$ be a subspace of $F$, and let $\psi: M \to F$ be the canonical imbedding, $\phi: G \to G/M^\circ$ the canonical surjection. Let $\mathfrak{S}_1$, $\mathfrak{S}_1'$ be saturated families of subsets of $G$, and let $\mathfrak{S}_2$, $\mathfrak{S}_2'$ be saturated families of subsets of $G/M^\circ$ related by
$$\mathfrak{S}_2' = \phi(\mathfrak{S}_1'), \qquad \mathfrak{S}_1 = \psi^{-1}(\mathfrak{S}_2).$$
Denote by $\mathfrak{T}_1$, $\mathfrak{T}_1'$ the corresponding $\mathfrak{S}$-topologies on $F$ and $G$, and by $\mathfrak{T}_2$, $\mathfrak{T}_2'$ those on $M$ and $G/M^\circ$. Let the statements be:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $\mathfrak{S}_2' = \phi(\mathfrak{S}_1')$
\item[(b)] $\mathfrak{T}_2$ is the topology induced on $M$ by $\mathfrak{T}_1$
\item[(c)] $\mathfrak{S}_1 = \psi^{-1}(\mathfrak{S}_2)$
\item[(d)] $\mathfrak{T}_2'$ is the quotient topology of $\mathfrak{T}_1'$ on $G/M^\circ$
\end{itemize}
Then (a) $\Rightarrow$ (b) and (d) $\Rightarrow$ (c). If $\mathfrak{T}_1$ is consistent with $\langle F, G \rangle$, then (b) $\Rightarrow$ (a). If $\mathfrak{T}_1'$ is consistent with $\langle F, G \rangle$ and $M$ is closed in $F$, then (c) $\Rightarrow$ (d).
