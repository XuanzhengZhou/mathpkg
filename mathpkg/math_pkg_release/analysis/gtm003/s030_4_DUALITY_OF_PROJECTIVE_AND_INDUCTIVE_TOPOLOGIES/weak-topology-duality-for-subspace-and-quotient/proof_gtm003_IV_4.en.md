The first assertion follows from (a) $\Rightarrow$ (b) of Theorem 4.1 by taking $\mathfrak{T}_1'$ and $\mathfrak{T}_2'$ to be the saturated families generated by all finite subsets of $G$ and $G/M^\circ$, respectively. These families generate precisely the weak topologies $\sigma(F, G)$ and $\sigma(M, G/M^\circ)$.

The sufficiency part of the second assertion follows similarly from (c) $\Rightarrow$ (d) of Theorem 4.1, noting that closedness of $M$ is required for this implication. Conversely, if $\sigma(G/M^\circ, M)$ is the quotient of $\sigma(G, F)$, then since $M^\circ = \bar{M}^\circ$, we have $\sigma(G/M^\circ, M) = \sigma(G/M^\circ, \bar{M})$ by the first assertion, which implies $M = \bar{M}$ by the Corollary of (1.2). Hence $M$ must be closed.
