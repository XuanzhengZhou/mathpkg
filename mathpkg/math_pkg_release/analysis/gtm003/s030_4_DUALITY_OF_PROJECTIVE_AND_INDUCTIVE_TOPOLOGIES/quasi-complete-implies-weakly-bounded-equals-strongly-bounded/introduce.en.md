Theorem 5.3 provides a crucial sufficient condition for the strong topology on the dual to be consistent with the duality: quasi-completeness of $E$ implies that weakly bounded subsets of $E'$ are already strongly bounded. Combined with the infrabarreled condition, this characterizes barreled spaces within the class of quasi-complete spaces. The proof relies on the fact that in quasi-complete spaces, convex circled complete bounded sets form a fundamental family of bounded sets, and their polars are barrels.
