Theorem 4.3 identifies the topological dual of a product of locally convex spaces as the algebraic direct sum of the individual duals. Moreover, the weak and Mackey topologies on the product coincide with the product of the respective topologies, while the Mackey topology on the dual is the locally convex direct sum. The weak topology on the dual distributes over the direct sum only when the index set is finite, illustrating a fundamental difference between weak and Mackey duals for infinite products.
