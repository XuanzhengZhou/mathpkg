A locally convex space $E$ is called \textbf{infrabarreled} if every barrel in $E$ that absorbs all bounded subsets of $E$ (a \emph{bound-absorbing barrel}) is a neighborhood of $0$.

Equivalently, $E$ is infrabarreled if and only if the families of equicontinuous and strongly bounded subsets of $E'$ coincide, i.e., $\mathfrak{E} = \mathfrak{B}$.

Every bornological space and every barreled space is infrabarreled. The following inclusions hold for the families of subsets of $E'$:
$$\mathfrak{E} \subset \mathfrak{C} \subset \mathfrak{B}_\sigma, \qquad \mathfrak{E} \subset \mathfrak{B} \subset \mathfrak{B}_\sigma$$
where $\mathfrak{E}$ = equicontinuous, $\mathfrak{C}$ = convex circled $\sigma(E', E)$-compact, $\mathfrak{B}$ = strongly bounded, $\mathfrak{B}_\sigma$ = $\sigma(E', E)$-bounded.
