A subset $B$ is dense in a topological space $X$ if its closure equals $X$, meaning every point of $X$ is a contact point of $B$. Equivalent formulations include that every non-empty open set intersects $B$. Separable spaces are those that admit a countable dense subset, a property that is fundamental in functional analysis and measure theory.
