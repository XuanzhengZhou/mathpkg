This proposition establishes that local holomorphy, defined via the existence of the complex derivative at each point, implies continuity for vector-valued functions taking values in a locally convex space. This extends the familiar result from elementary complex analysis: differentiability implies continuity, now in the setting of $E$-valued functions where $E$ satisfies the compact convex hull condition. The proof likely relies on the vector-valued Cauchy integral representation of holomorphic functions.
