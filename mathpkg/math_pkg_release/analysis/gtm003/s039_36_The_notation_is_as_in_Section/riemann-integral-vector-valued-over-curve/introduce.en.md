The Riemann integral $\int_\delta f(\zeta) d\zeta$ of an $E$-valued function over a rectifiable curve $\delta$ is defined as the unique element of the locally convex space $E$ that represents the continuous linear functional $x' \mapsto \int_\delta \langle f(\zeta), x'\rangle d\zeta$ on the dual $E'$. This is the vector-valued analogue of the Pettis integral, adapted to the Riemann-Cauchy integration context along rectifiable curves.
