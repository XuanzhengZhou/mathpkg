A Banach algebra is the complete version of a normed algebra: the underlying normed vector space is required to be a Banach space. Completeness is the crucial property that enables powerful tools from functional analysis — such as the holomorphic functional calculus, the Gelfand representation theory, and spectral theory — to be applied to algebraic structures. Banach algebras form one of the central pillars of modern analysis.
