A locally convex algebra combines the algebraic structure of an associative algebra with a compatible locally convex topology. The compatibility condition requires only separate continuity of multiplication, meaning that for each fixed $a \in A$, the maps $b \mapsto ab$ and $b \mapsto ba$ are continuous linear operators. This is the weakest natural compatibility condition between the algebraic and topological structures, generalizing the notion of a topological algebra.
