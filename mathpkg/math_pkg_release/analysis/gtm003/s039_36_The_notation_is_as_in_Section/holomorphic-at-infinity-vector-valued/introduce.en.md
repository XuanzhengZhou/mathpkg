Holomorphy at the point at infinity for vector-valued functions is defined by reducing to the finite case via the inversion map $\zeta \mapsto \zeta^{-1}$. A function $f$ defined on a neighborhood of $\infty$ in the extended complex plane is holomorphic at $\infty$ precisely when the composition with inversion is holomorphic at the origin. This definition extends the usual scalar notion to functions taking values in a locally convex space $E$.
