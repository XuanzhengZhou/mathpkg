Let $E$ be the Montel space $K_0^d$, where $d$ is the cardinality of the continuum. Denote by $B$ the subset $[0, 1]^d$ of $E$, and by $B_0$ the subset of $B$ whose elements have not more than countably many non-zero coordinates. Show that (under the topology induced by $E$):

(a) $B$ and $B_0$ are completely regular topological spaces.

(b) $B_0$ is sequentially compact but not compact.

(c) $B$ is compact but not sequentially compact.

(d) $B_0$ is an example of a uniform space which is semi-complete and precompact but not complete.
