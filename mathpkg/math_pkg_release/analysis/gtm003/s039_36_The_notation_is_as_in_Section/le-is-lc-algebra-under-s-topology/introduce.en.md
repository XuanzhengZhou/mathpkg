The space $\mathcal{L}(E)$ of continuous linear operators on a locally convex space $E$ becomes a locally convex algebra when equipped with the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology (the topology of uniform convergence on members of a total saturated family $\mathfrak{S}$ of bounded sets). The multiplication is composition of operators. The continuity properties of multiplication improve as $E$ acquires stronger properties: for a barreled space $E$, multiplication becomes hypocontinuous even under the topology of simple convergence, and if $\mathcal{L}_{\mathfrak{S}}(E)$ is an (F)-space, multiplication is jointly continuous. This result connects the algebraic structure of operator algebras with the topological structure of the underlying space.
