Eberlein's theorem, with contributions by Dieudonné and Grothendieck, gives four equivalent characterizations of relative weak compactness for subsets of a locally convex space whose closed convex hull is complete. The conditions involve: (a) relative weak compactness itself; (b) a sequential criterion — every sequence has a weak cluster point; (c) a countable intersection property for closed convex sets meeting the subset; and (d) boundedness together with every $\sigma(E'',E')$-cluster point in the bidual actually belonging to $E$. The theorem shows that in the weak topology of an l.c.s., relative compactness, relative countable compactness, and relative sequential compactness are all equivalent, generalizing the classical Eberlein–Šmulian theorem for Banach spaces.
