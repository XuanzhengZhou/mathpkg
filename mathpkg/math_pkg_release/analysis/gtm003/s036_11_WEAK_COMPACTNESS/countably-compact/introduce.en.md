Countable compactness is a weakening of the usual notion of compactness for Hausdorff topological spaces. A subset $A$ is countably compact whenever every sequence in $A$ admits a cluster point belonging to $A$, or equivalently, every countable open cover of $A$ contains a finite subcover. While compactness and sequential compactness each imply countable compactness, the converse implications do not hold in general. This concept plays an important role in the study of weak compactness in locally convex spaces, where the distinction between these compactness notions becomes essential.
