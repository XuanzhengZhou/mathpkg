Sequential compactness is a notion of compactness defined in terms of convergent subsequences: a subset $A$ of a Hausdorff topological space is sequentially compact if every sequence in $A$ contains a subsequence that converges to a limit belonging to $A$. This property is particularly natural in metrizable spaces, where it coincides with ordinary compactness. In general non-metrizable settings (such as the weak topology of a locally convex space), sequential compactness is a strictly weaker condition than compactness, and the relationship among compactness, sequential compactness, and countable compactness becomes subtle.
