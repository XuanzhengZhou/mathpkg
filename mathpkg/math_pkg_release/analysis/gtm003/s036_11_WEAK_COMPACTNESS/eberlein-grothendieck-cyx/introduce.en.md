This theorem, due to Eberlein and Grothendieck, provides a criterion for relative compactness in the space of continuous functions from a compact space $X$ into a locally compact metric space $Y$, equipped with the pointwise convergence topology. The essential hypothesis is that every sequence in $H$ admits a cluster point in the full product space $Y^X$. The conclusion has two parts: first, $H$ is relatively compact in $Y^X$ by Tychonoff's theorem; second, every point in the closure of $H$ is actually a continuous function, i.e., limits of pointwise-cluster points of $H$ cannot acquire discontinuities. This result serves as the key technical lemma for Eberlein's theorem on weak compactness in locally convex spaces.
