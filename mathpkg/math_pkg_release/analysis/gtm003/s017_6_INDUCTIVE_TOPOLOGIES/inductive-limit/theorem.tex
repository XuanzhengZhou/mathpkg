Let $\{E_\alpha: \alpha \in A\}$ be a family of subspaces of a vector space $E$ such that $E_\alpha \neq E_\beta$ for $\alpha \neq \beta$, directed under inclusion and satisfying $E = \bigcup_\alpha E_\alpha$. Direct $A$ by "$\alpha \leq \beta$ if $E_\alpha \subset E_\beta$". On each $E_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in A$) a Hausdorff locally convex topology $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ is given such that, whenever $\alpha \leq \beta$, the topology induced by $\mathfrak{T}_\beta$ on $E_\alpha$ is coarser than $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$.

Let $g_\alpha (\alpha \in A)$ be the canonical imbedding of $E_\alpha$ into $E$ and $h_{\beta\alpha}$ the canonical imbedding of $E_\alpha$ into $E_\beta$ ($\alpha \leq \beta$). If the inductive topology $\mathfrak{T}$ on $E$ with respect to the family $\{(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha, g_\alpha): \alpha \in A\}$ is Hausdorff, then $E(\mathfrak{T})$ is called the \textit{inductive limit} of the family $\{E_\alpha(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha): \alpha \in A\}$ of subspaces (or \textit{strict inductive limit} if the topologies are strict, i.e., $\mathfrak{T}_\beta$ induces $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ on $E_\alpha$ for $\alpha \leq \beta$).

More generally, let $\{E_\alpha: \alpha \in A\}$ be a family of locally convex spaces, and for every pair $\alpha, \beta \in A$ with $\alpha \leq \beta$ let $h_{\beta\alpha}$ be a continuous linear map of $E_\alpha$ into $E_\beta$ such that the family $\{h_{\beta\alpha}\}$ is directed. Form the locally convex direct sum $F = \oplus_\alpha E_\alpha$, and in $F$ let $H$ be the closed subspace generated by all elements of the form $x_\alpha - h_{\beta\alpha} x_\alpha$ ($x_\alpha \in E_\alpha$). If the quotient $F/H$ is Hausdorff, the locally convex space $F/H$ is called the \textit{inductive limit} of the family $\{E_\alpha: \alpha \in A\}$ with respect to the mappings $h_{\beta\alpha}$, denoted $\lim_{h_{\beta\alpha}} E_\alpha$.
