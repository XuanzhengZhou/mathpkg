The inductive topology is the dual construction to the projective topology. Given a family of locally convex spaces $E_\alpha$ and linear maps into a common vector space $E$, the inductive topology is the finest locally convex topology on $E$ making all the structure maps continuous. It is a core tool for constructing new locally convex spaces from given ones, particularly in the theory of direct sums and inductive limits.
