If $\{E_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$ is a family of vector spaces over $K$, the \textit{algebraic direct sum} $\oplus_\alpha E_\alpha$ is the subspace of $\prod_\alpha E_\alpha$ for whose elements $x$ all but a finite number of the projections $x_\alpha = p_\alpha(x)$ are $0$. Denote by $g_\alpha (\alpha \in A)$ the injection map (canonical imbedding) $E_\alpha \rightarrow \oplus_\beta E_\beta$.

The \textit{locally convex direct sum} of the family $\{E_\alpha(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha) : \alpha \in A\}$ of locally convex spaces is $\oplus_\alpha E_\alpha$ under the inductive topology with respect to the family $\{(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha, g_\alpha) : \alpha \in A\}$, denoted by $E(\mathfrak{T}) = \oplus_\alpha E_\alpha(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$. Since $\mathfrak{T}$ is finer than the topology induced on $E$ by $\prod_\alpha E_\alpha(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$, $\mathfrak{T}$ is a Hausdorff topology and $E(\mathfrak{T})$ is a locally convex space.

A $0$-neighborhood base of $\oplus_\alpha E_\alpha(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ is provided by all sets of the form
$$
U = \left\{ \sum \lambda_\alpha g_\alpha(x_\alpha) : \sum |\lambda_\alpha| \leq 1,\; x_\alpha \in U_\alpha \right\}
$$
where $U_\alpha$ is a convex circled $0$-neighborhood in $E_\alpha(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$.

The locally convex sum of a finite family of locally convex spaces is identical with their product.
