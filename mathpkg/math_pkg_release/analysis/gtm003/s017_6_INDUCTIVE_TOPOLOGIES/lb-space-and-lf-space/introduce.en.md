(LB)-spaces and (LF)-spaces are the most important classes of strict inductive limits, obtained by taking increasing sequences of Banach spaces or Frechet spaces respectively. These spaces combine the completeness properties of their building blocks with the flexibility of the inductive limit construction. They appear naturally in analysis, for instance as spaces of test functions in distribution theory.
