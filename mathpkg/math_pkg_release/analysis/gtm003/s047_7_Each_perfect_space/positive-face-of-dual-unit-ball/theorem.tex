Let $E \neq \{0\}$ be an (AM)-space with unit $u$. The intersection of the hyperplane
$$
H = \{x' \in E' : \langle u, x' \rangle = 1\}
$$
with the dual cone $C'$ is a convex, $\sigma(E', E)$-closed subset $H_0$ of the dual unit ball $[-u, u]^\circ$. The set $H_0$ is called the \emph{positive face} of $[-u, u]^\circ$. It is $\sigma(E', E)$-compact, and $C'$ is a cone with weakly compact base. An element $t \in H_0$ is an extreme point of $H_0$ if and only if $\{\lambda t : \lambda \geq 0\}$ is an extreme ray of $C'$.
