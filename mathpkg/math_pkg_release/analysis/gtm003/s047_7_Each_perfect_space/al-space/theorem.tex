A Banach lattice $E$ is called an \emph{(AL)-space} (abstract $L$-space) if the norm of $E$ is additive on the positive cone $C$, i.e., for all $x, y \in C$,
$$
\|x + y\| = \|x\| + \|y\|.
$$
Every $L^1(\mu)$ (over $\mathbb{R}$) possesses this property, and conversely, every (AL)-space is isomorphic as a Banach lattice with a suitable space $L^1(\mu)$ (Kakutani).
