An (AL)-space (abstract $L$-space) is a Banach lattice whose norm is additive on the positive cone. The terminology derives from the fact that every concrete $L^1(\mu)$ space has this property, and Kakutani's representation theorem shows that, conversely, every (AL)-space is isomorphic to some $L^1(\mu)$ as a Banach lattice. The strong dual of an (AL)-space can be identified with $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ for a compact, extremally disconnected space $X$.
