Proposition 7.6 establishes that under suitable topological assumptions -- metrizability, separability, and continuity of lattice operations -- the algebraic notion of a weak order unit coincides with the topological notion of a quasi-interior point. The proof shows that the linear hull of $[0,x]$ is dense in $E$, which forces $y=0$ for any $y$ orthogonal to $[0,x]$ by continuity of the lattice operations. The remark notes that metrizability and separability are not dispensable.
