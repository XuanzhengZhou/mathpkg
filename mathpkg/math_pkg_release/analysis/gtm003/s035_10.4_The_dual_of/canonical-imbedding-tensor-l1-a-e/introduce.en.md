This theorem relates three natural topologies on the algebraic tensor product $l^1(\mathbf{A}) \otimes E$: the projective tensor product topology, the topology of bi-equicontinuous convergence, and the topologies induced by the canonical imbeddings into $l^1(\mathbf{A}, E)$ (absolutely summable families with the strong topology) and $l^1[\mathbf{A}, E]$ (summable families with the weak topology). The result shows that the projective topology corresponds to the imbedding into $l^1(\mathbf{A}, E)$, while the bi-equicontinuous convergence topology corresponds to the imbedding into $l^1[\mathbf{A}, E]$. The density of the algebraic tensor product in both completed spaces is a crucial approximation property used throughout the theory of nuclear spaces.
