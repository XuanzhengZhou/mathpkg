The canonical example of a commutative C*-algebra is the algebra $C(K)$ of continuous complex-valued functions on a compact Hausdorff space $K$, with supremum norm and pointwise complex conjugation as involution. Gelfand's theorem shows this example is universal: every unital commutative C*-algebra is isometrically *-isomorphic to some $C(K)$.
