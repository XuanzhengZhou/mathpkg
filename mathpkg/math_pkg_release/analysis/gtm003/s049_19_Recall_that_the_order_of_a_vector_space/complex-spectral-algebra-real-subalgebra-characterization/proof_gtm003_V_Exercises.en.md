Cf. (8.3) from the text. If $A$ is a complex spectral algebra, the self-adjoint elements $A_{sa}$ form a real spectral algebra $A_1$. Conversely, if $A_1$ is a real spectral algebra, its complexification $A_1 + iA_1$ is a complex spectral algebra. The spectral measure for a complex spectral algebra is obtained from the real spectral measure of $A_1$ by linear extension. The uniqueness of the decomposition $a = b + ic$ follows from the involution properties.
