Use part (b): the condition that $L^+$ distinguishes points implies $L^b$ also distinguishes points (since $L^+ \subset L^b$). By (b), $(L, \mathfrak{T}_O)$ is an inductive limit. Moreover, $\mathfrak{T}_O$ is Hausdorff because $L^b$ separates points, and the positive cone $L^+$ is closed in this topology. For an almost Archimedean space with closed cone and Hausdorff order topology, the order is necessarily Archimedean. Regularity follows from the Archimedean property in a vector lattice (cf. Chapter V, Section 1).
