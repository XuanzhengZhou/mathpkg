(Statement truncated in source material. See Gordon [1], Peressini [1] for the full treatment.) The typical approach is to examine the conditions under which the mappings $(x,y) \mapsto x \vee y$ and $(x,y) \mapsto x \wedge y$ are continuous. When the positive cone is generating and normal, and the space has the decomposition property, equicontinuity of order intervals yields the desired continuity. The dual statement concerns when the dual cone $E'_+$ is normal in the given dual topology.
