A \emph{left ideal} (resp. \emph{right ideal}) is a linear subspace $M$ of an algebra $A$ satisfying $AM \subset M$ (resp. $MA \subset M$). A subspace $M$ having both properties is called a \emph{two-sided ideal}, or simply an \emph{ideal} of $A$. Closures of left, right, and two-sided ideals retain the defining property.
