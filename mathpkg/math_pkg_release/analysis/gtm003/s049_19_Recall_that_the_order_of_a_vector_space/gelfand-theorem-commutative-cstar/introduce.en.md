Gelfand's celebrated theorem characterizes commutative unital C*-algebras precisely as the algebras $C(K)$ of continuous complex-valued functions on compact Hausdorff spaces. The space $K$ is the Gelfand spectrum (the set of characters, or maximal ideal space), and the isomorphism is given by the Gelfand transform.
