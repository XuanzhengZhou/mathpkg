Since $u$ is bijective, $u(L) = M$, so $u(L)$ is certainly not meager in $M$. Since $M$ is Hausdorff, $\{0\}$ is closed, so $u^{-1}(0) = \{0\}$ is closed in $L$. The map $u$ is a continuous bijection. By Theorem 2.1 (Banach's homomorphism theorem), $u$ is a topological homomorphism. Since $u$ is bijective, being a topological homomorphism means precisely that $u$ is open, i.e., $u^{-1}$ is continuous. Hence $u$ is an isomorphism of topological vector spaces.

Alternatively, one may note that since $u$ is surjective, its range is not meager, so Theorem 2.1 gives that $u(L) = M$ and $u$ is a topological homomorphism, hence open, hence an isomorphism.
