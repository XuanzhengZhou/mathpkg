A continuous linear functional (linear form) on a topological vector space is always a topological homomorphism. This is because its range is the one-dimensional field $K$, which is Hausdorff and finite-dimensional, so Proposition 1.3 applies. This simple fact is used implicitly in many constructions, including the proof of the Hahn-Banach separation theorem.
