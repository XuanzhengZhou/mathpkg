A topological homomorphism is a continuous linear map between topological vector spaces that sends open sets to sets that are open in the image subspace. This is the natural notion of a morphism that respects the topological quotient/embedding structure: the quotient map onto a quotient space and the inclusion of a subspace are the prototypical examples. Every linear map factors through its null space and its image; the map is a topological homomorphism precisely when the induced bijective map between the quotient and the image is an isomorphism of t.v.s.
