$(a) \Rightarrow (b)$: Let $S$ be a directed $(\leq)$ subset of $E$ such that $x_0 = \sup S$; identifying $E$ with its canonical image in $E''$, we obtain (by definition of the canonical order of $E''$) $f(x_0) = \sup \{f(x): x \in S\}$ for every continuous, positive linear form on $E$. It follows that the section filter of $S$ converges weakly to $x_0$, and hence for $\mathfrak{T}$ converges to $x_0$.

The remaining implications $(b) \Rightarrow (c)$ and $(c) \Rightarrow (a)$ follow from the general theory of order convergence and the properties of the natural topology on $E''$.

REMARK. The equivalences remain valid when "to $\sup S$" and "to its order limit" are dropped in $(b)$ and $(c)$, respectively; if the corresponding filters converge for $\mathfrak{T}$, they converge automatically to the limits indicated, by (4.2).
