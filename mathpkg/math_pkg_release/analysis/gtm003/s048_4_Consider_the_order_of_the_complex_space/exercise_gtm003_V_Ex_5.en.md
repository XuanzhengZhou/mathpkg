Let $\{E_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$ be a family of l.c.s., let $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$ be a saturated family of bounded subsets of $E_\alpha$, and let $C_\alpha$ be an $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$-cone in $E_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in A$). Show that $\prod_\alpha C_\alpha$ is an $\mathfrak{S}_1$-cone in $\prod_\alpha E_\alpha$ and that $\bigoplus_\alpha C_\alpha$ is an $\mathfrak{S}_2$-cone in $\bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha$, where $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ denote the families $\prod_\alpha \mathfrak{S}_\alpha$ and $\bigoplus_\alpha \mathfrak{S}_\alpha$ respectively (Chapter IV, Section 4). Derive an analogous result for families of strict $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$-cones, and discuss the permanence properties of $\mathfrak{S}$-cones under the transition to subspaces and quotient spaces. (Use (IV, 4.1), (3.3), and Exercise 3 above.)
