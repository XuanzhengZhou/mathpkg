Let $X \neq \emptyset$ be a set, let $\Sigma$ be a $\sigma$-algebra of subsets of $X$, and let $E$ denote the vector space (over $\mathbb{R}$) of all real-valued bounded $\Sigma$-measurable functions (that is, functions $f$ such that $f^{-1}(A) \in \Sigma$ for each Borel set $A \subset \mathbb{R}$). Under the sup-norm and the canonical order, $E$ is an (AM)-space. Every positive linear form on $E$ defines a real-valued, finitely additive non-negative set function on $\Sigma$, and conversely; hence $E^+$ can be identified with the vector lattice of all real-valued, finitely additive set functions on $\Sigma$ that are differences of non-negative functions of the same type.

(a) $E^+$ is order complete, and the set functions $\mu \in E^+$ that are countably additive on $\Sigma$ form a band $M$ in $E^+$. Deduce from this that every finitely additive, non-negative set function $\nu$ on $\Sigma$ has a unique representation $\nu = \nu_1 + \nu_2$, where $\nu_1, \nu_2 \geq 0$, $\nu_1$ is countably additive and $\nu_2$ is not countably additive unless $\nu_2 = 0$.

(b) Suppose that $\Sigma$ contains all finite (hence all countable) subsets of $X$, and call the elements $\mu \in M$ briefly measures on $X$. The measures $\mu$ such that $\mu(\{t\}) = 0$ for every $t \in X$ are called diffuse; the set of all diffuse measures is a band $M_d$ in $M$. The elements of the complementary band $M_a$ in $M$ are called atomic. Show that each atomic measure on $X$ is the sum (for $\sigma(E^+, E)$) of a summable family $\{\alpha_n \mu_n : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ (Chapter III, Exercise 23), where $(\alpha_n)$ is a summable family in $\mathbb{R}$.
