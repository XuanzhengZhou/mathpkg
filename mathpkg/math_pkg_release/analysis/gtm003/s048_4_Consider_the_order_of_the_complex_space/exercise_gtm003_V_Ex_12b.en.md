If $u$ is a linear map of $L_1$ onto $L_2$, then $u$ is a lattice homomorphism if and only if $u(C_1) = C_2$ and $N = u^{-1}(0)$ is solid in $L_1$, where $C_1, C_2$ denote the respective positive cones. In these circumstances, the biunivocal map $u_0$ associated with $u$ is a lattice isomorphism of $L_1/N$ onto $L_2$.
