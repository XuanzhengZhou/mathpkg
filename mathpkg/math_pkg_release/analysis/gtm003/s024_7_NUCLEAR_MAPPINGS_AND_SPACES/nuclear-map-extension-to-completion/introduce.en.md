Corollary 3 of Proposition 7.1 states that every nuclear map on a locally convex space extends uniquely to a nuclear map on its completion. The existence of the extension follows from uniform continuity and compactness of the nuclear map: the restriction to a suitable zero-neighborhood, whose image is relatively compact, extends continuously to the closure of that neighborhood in the completion.
