Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces. A linear map $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is called \emph{nuclear} if there exist a convex, circled $0$-neighborhood $U$ in $E$ and a convex, circled, bounded subset $B$ of $F$, with $F_B$ complete, such that $u = \psi_B \circ u_0 \circ \phi_U$ where $u_0 \in \mathcal{L}(\tilde{E}_U, F_B)$ is a nuclear map between the Banach spaces $\tilde{E}_U$ and $F_B$.

Equivalently, $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is nuclear if and only if it is of the form
$$
x \mapsto u(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda_n f_n(x) y_n,
$$
where $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} |\lambda_n| < +\infty$, $\{f_n\}$ is an equicontinuous sequence in $E'$, and $\{y_n\}$ is a sequence contained in a convex, circled, and bounded subset $B$ of $F$ for which $F_B$ is complete.
