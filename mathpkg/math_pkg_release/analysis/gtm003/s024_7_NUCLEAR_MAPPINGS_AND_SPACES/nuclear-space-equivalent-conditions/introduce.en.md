Theorem 7.2 provides three equivalent formulations of nuclearity for a locally convex space: the defining condition that every canonical map into an associated Banach space is nuclear (a), the property that every continuous linear map into an arbitrary Banach space is nuclear (b), and a local refinement condition stating that zero-neighborhoods can be chosen so that the transition maps between their associated Banach spaces are nuclear (c). The equivalence of these conditions is fundamental to the theory.
