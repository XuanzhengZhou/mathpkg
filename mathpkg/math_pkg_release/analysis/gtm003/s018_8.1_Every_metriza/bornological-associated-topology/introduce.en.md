Every locally convex space $E(\mathfrak{T})$ admits a canonical bornological topology $\mathfrak{T}_0$, which is the finest locally convex topology on $E$ having the same bounded sets as $\mathfrak{T}$. The space $E(\mathfrak{T}_0)$ is always bornological, and the original space is bornological precisely when its topology coincides with the associated bornological topology.
