Let $E(\mathfrak{T})$ be a locally convex space, let $\mathfrak{B}$ be the family of all bounded subsets of $E(\mathfrak{T})$, and let $\mathfrak{T}_0$ be the finest locally convex topology on $E$ whose family of bounded sets is identical with $\mathfrak{B}$. Then $E(\mathfrak{T}_0)$ is bornological; it is called the \textit{bornological space associated with} $E(\mathfrak{T})$. Thus $E(\mathfrak{T})$ is bornological if and only if $\mathfrak{T} = \mathfrak{T}_0$.
