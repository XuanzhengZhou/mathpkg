The Second Separation Theorem strengthens the First Separation Theorem for locally convex spaces. It guarantees that a closed convex set and a compact convex set that are disjoint can be strictly separated by a closed real hyperplane. The proof uses the local convexity to find a convex open $0$-neighborhood $V$ such that $A+V$ and $B+V$ remain disjoint, reducing to the open case of the First Separation Theorem. This result is widely applied in convex analysis and optimization.
