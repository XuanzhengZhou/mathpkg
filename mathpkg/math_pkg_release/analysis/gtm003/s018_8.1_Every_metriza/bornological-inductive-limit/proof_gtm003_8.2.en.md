Let $A$ be such a subset of $E$ and let $\mathfrak{T}$ be the inductive topology with respect to the family $\{(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha, g_\alpha): \alpha \in A\}$. If $B_\alpha$ is bounded in $(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$, then $g_\alpha(B_\alpha)$ is bounded in $E(\mathfrak{T})$ by (I, 5.4); hence $A$ absorbs $g_\alpha(B_\alpha)$, whence $g_\alpha^{-1}(A)$ absorbs $B_\alpha$, and so $g_\alpha^{-1}(A)$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$. Since this holds for all $\alpha \in A$, $A$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $E(\mathfrak{T})$.
