A hyperplane is a maximal proper affine subspace of a vector space, whose corresponding subspace has codimension 1. Two hyperplanes are parallel exactly when they are translates of the same subspace. A homogeneous hyperplane is a hyperplane that contains the origin, i.e., a hyperplane that is itself a subspace. Hyperplanes play a central role in the geometric theory of topological vector spaces, particularly in the Hahn-Banach theorem.
