Let $\phi$ denote the natural map of $L$ onto $L/M$. By (2.3), $L/M$ is Hausdorff. Since $N$ is finite-dimensional, $\phi(N)$ is a finite-dimensional subspace of the Hausdorff t.v.s. $L/M$, hence $\phi(N)$ is closed in $L/M$ by (3.2). Therefore $\phi^{-1}(\phi(N)) = M + N$ is closed in $L$, as $\phi$ is continuous.
