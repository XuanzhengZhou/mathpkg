This theorem is the converse of the observation that finite-dimensional Hausdorff t.v.s. over a locally compact complete field are locally compact. Assuming $K$ is complete, any non-zero locally compact Hausdorff t.v.s. over $K$ must be finite-dimensional, and moreover $K$ itself must be locally compact. This result, together with Theorem 3.2, establishes a complete characterization of finite-dimensional t.v.s. in terms of local compactness.
