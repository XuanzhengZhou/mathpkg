The dimension of a topological vector space is defined as its algebraic dimension, i.e., the cardinality of any maximal linearly independent subset (a Hamel basis). The one-dimensional t.v.s. $K_0$ is obtained by viewing the scalar field $K$ as a vector space over itself, and serves as the fundamental building block for finite-dimensional t.v.s.
