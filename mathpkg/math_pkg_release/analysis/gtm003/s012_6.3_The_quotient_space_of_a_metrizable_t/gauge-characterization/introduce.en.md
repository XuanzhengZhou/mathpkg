This theorem characterizes when a semi-norm $p$ is exactly the gauge $p_M$ of a radial, convex, circled set $M$. The necessary and sufficient condition is that $M$ lies between the open unit ball $\{x : p(x) < 1\}$ and the closed unit ball $\{x : p(x) \leq 1\}$ of $p$. This shows that the gauge does not uniquely determine $M$, but the ambiguity is confined to the boundary $\{x : p(x) = 1\}$.
