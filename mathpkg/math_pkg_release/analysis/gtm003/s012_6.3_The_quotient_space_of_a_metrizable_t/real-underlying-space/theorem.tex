If $L$ is a vector space (or t.v.s.) over a field $K = H(i)$ containing $i$, then the restriction of scalar multiplication to $H \times L$ turns $L$ into a vector space (or t.v.s.) $L_0$ over $H$. $L_0$ is called the **real underlying space** of (or associated with) $L$. A **real linear form** on $L$ is a linear form on $L_0$, and a **real hyperplane** in $L$ is a hyperplane in $L_0$.
