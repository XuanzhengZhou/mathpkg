In a topological vector space, one combines the properties of being convex, circled, and closed. The closed convex hull is the closure of the convex hull (which also equals the convex hull of the closure by Proposition II.1.2). The closed convex circled hull is similarly the closure of $\Gamma A$. These hulls are intersection-invariant since closure and convexity are preserved under intersections.
