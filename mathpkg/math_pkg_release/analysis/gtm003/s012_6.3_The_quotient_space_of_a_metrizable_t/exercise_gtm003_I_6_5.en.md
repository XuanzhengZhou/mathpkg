Let $B$ be a subset of a t.v.s. such that every sequence in $B$ has a cluster point; then $B$ is totally bounded. (For a given circled $0$-neighborhood $V$, let $B_0$ be a subset of $B$ such that $x \in B_0$, $y \in B_0$, and $x \neq y$ imply $x - y \notin V$, and which is maximal with respect to this property (Zorn's lemma); then $B \subset B_0 + V$. Show that the assumption "$B_0$ is infinite" is absurd.)
