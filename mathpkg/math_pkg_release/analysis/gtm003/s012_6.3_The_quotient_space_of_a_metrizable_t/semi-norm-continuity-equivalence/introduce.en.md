This theorem gives three equivalent conditions for the continuity of a semi-norm on a topological vector space. Continuity at the origin suffices for uniform continuity everywhere, due to the subadditivity inequality $|p(x) - p(y)| \leq p(x-y)$. The openness of the open unit ball $\{x : p(x) < 1\}$ is equivalent to continuity at $0$, which is a practical criterion frequently used in the theory of locally convex spaces.
