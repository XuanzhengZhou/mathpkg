This proposition collects the basic stability properties of convexity under common operations in a topological vector space. The interior $\mathring{A}$ and closure $\overline{A}$ of a convex set are convex, as is the sum $A+B$ of two convex sets and any scalar multiple $\alpha A$. The convexity of the interior follows from Theorem II.1.1, and the other properties follow from the linear structure of the space.
