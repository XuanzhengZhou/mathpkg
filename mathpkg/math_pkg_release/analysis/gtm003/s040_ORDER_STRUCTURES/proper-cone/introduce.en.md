A proper cone is the fundamental object that encodes the order structure of an ordered vector space. It consists of all non-negative elements and satisfies three simple algebraic conditions: closure under addition, closure under positive scalar multiplication, and containing no non-zero element together with its negative. There is a one-to-one correspondence between proper cones and vector space orderings, making proper cones the central tool for studying order structures algebraically.
