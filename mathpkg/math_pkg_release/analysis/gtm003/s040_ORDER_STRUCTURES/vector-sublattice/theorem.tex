A \textbf{vector sublattice} $M$ of a vector lattice $E$ is a vector subspace of $E$ such that $x \in M$, $y \in M$ implies that $\sup(x, y) \in M$, where the supremum is formed in $E$. It follows that $M$ is a vector lattice under its canonical ordering.

However, it can happen that a subspace $M$ of $E$ is a vector lattice under its canonical order but is not a sublattice of $E$.
