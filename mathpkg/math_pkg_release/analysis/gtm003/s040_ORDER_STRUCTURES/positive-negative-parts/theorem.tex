For each element $x$ of a vector lattice $E$, the \textbf{positive part} $x^+$ and \textbf{negative part} $x^-$ are defined by
$$x^+ = \sup(x, 0), \qquad x^- = \sup(-x, 0).$$

Then $x = x^+ - x^-$ and $|x| = x^+ + x^-$. Moreover, $x = x^+ - x^-$ is the unique representation of $x$ as a difference of disjoint elements $\geq 0$ (i.e., $x^+ \perp x^-$ and $x^+, x^- \geq 0$).
