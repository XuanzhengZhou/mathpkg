In the dual $E'$ of a locally convex space $E$, there are four fundamental families of subsets: the equicontinuous sets $\mathfrak{E}$, the weakly compact convex circled sets $\mathfrak{C}$, the strongly bounded sets $\mathfrak{B}$, and the weakly bounded sets $\mathfrak{B}_\sigma$. While these families coincide for certain classes of spaces (e.g., barreled spaces), they can all be distinct in general. A counterexample is given by taking a non-barreled space $F$ and considering $E = F'_\sigma$.
