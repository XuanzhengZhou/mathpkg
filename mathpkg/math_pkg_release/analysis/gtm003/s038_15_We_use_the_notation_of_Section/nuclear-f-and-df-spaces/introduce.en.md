This exercise establishes a criterion for nuclearity of (F)-spaces: if the strong dual of an (F)-space $E$ is nuclear, then $E$ itself is nuclear. The proof proceeds by showing that for any Banach space $F$, the canonical map $\psi: E \otimes F \to \mathfrak{B}(E'_\sigma, F'_\sigma)$ is surjective. This result connects nuclearity of a space with nuclearity of its strong dual and has important consequences for the theory of nuclear (F)- and (DF)-spaces.
