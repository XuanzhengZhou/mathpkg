A positive definite Hermitian form, also called an inner product, is a sesquilinear form on a complex vector space that is conjugate-symmetric and strictly positive on non-zero vectors. Conditions (i) and (ii) together imply that the form is conjugate-linear in the second argument, making it a Hermitian sesquilinear form. Conditions (iii) and (iv) ensure that the induced norm $\|x\| = \sqrt{[x,x]}$ is positive definite. This is the fundamental structure that distinguishes Hilbert spaces from general Banach spaces.
