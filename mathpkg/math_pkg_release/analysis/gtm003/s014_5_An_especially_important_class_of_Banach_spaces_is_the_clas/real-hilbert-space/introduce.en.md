A real Hilbert space is the analogue of a (complex) Hilbert space over the field of real numbers. The inner product is real-valued and conjugate-symmetry reduces to ordinary symmetry $[x,y] = [y,x]$. The theory of real Hilbert spaces parallels the complex case, with the Riesz representation theorem giving a genuine (linear) isomorphism between the space and its dual, rather than the conjugate-linear isomorphism obtained in the complex case.
