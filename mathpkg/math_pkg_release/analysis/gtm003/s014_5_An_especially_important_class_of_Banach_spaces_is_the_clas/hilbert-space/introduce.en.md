A Hilbert space is a complete inner product space -- a pre-Hilbert space whose induced norm makes it a Banach space. The presence of an inner product distinguishes Hilbert spaces quite drastically from general Banach spaces. Completeness ensures that Cauchy sequences converge, making Hilbert spaces the natural infinite-dimensional generalization of Euclidean space and the foundation for the theory of orthogonal projections, Fourier series, and spectral theory of operators.
