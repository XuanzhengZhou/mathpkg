The topological dual $L'$ of a topological vector space $L$ is the vector space consisting of all continuous linear functionals on $L$. Unlike the algebraic dual $L^*$, which consists of all linear functionals regardless of continuity, the topological dual captures only those functionals that respect the topology. In locally convex spaces, the topological dual is rich enough to separate points, making it the foundation of duality theory in functional analysis.
