This corollary to Mazur's theorem gives a necessary and sufficient condition for the existence of non-trivial continuous linear functionals on a topological vector space. The forward direction is immediate: if $f \neq 0$ is a continuous linear form, then $\{x : |f(x)| < 1\}$ is a non-empty convex open proper subset. The converse uses the geometric Hahn-Banach theorem to construct a closed hyperplane separating a point outside $A$ from $A$, which yields a non-zero continuous linear form.
