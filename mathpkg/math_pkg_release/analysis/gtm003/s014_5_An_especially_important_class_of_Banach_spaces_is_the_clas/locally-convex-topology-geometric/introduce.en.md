A locally convex topology on a vector space can be defined geometrically by specifying a filter base of radial, convex, and circled sets that serves as a neighborhood base at $0$. The condition $V \in \mathfrak{V} \implies \frac{1}{2}V \in \mathfrak{V}$ ensures that the filter base is compatible with the vector space structure. This geometric approach highlights the role of convexity in defining the topology and is dual to the analytic definition via families of seminorms.
