If $H_1, H_2$ are Hilbert spaces and $u$ is a continuous linear map of $H_1$ into $H_2$, then the identity
$$[u(x), y]_2 = [x, u^*(y)]_1 \quad \text{on } H_1 \times H_2$$
defines a continuous linear map $u^*$ of $H_2$ into $H_1$, called the conjugate of $u$. Moreover,
$$\|u\| = \|u^*\|$$
and $u \mapsto u^*$ is a conjugate-linear map of $\mathcal{L}(H_1, H_2)$ onto $\mathcal{L}(H_2, H_1)$.
