The Hilbert adjoint $u^*$ of a continuous linear map $u: H_1 \to H_2$ between Hilbert spaces is defined via the inner product identity $[u(x), y]_2 = [x, u^*(y)]_1$. Its existence is an immediate consequence of the Riesz representation theorem, which identifies each Hilbert space with its strong dual via a conjugate-linear isomorphism. The adjoint operation $u \mapsto u^*$ is conjugate-linear and an isometry with respect to the operator norm.
