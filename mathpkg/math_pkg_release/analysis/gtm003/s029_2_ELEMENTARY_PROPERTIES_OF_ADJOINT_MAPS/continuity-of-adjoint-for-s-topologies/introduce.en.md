This result characterizes when the adjoint $u' \colon G_1 \to G$ of a weakly continuous linear map $u \colon F \to F_1$ between dual pairs is continuous with respect to the polar topologies (also called $\mathfrak{S}$-topologies) induced by saturated families of bounded sets. The continuity condition $u'$ is $\mathfrak{T}_1$-to-$\mathfrak{T}$ continuous is equivalent to the forward image $u(\mathfrak{S})$ being contained in $\mathfrak{S}_1$. This is a fundamental tool for working with adjoints in the context of various polar topologies on dual spaces.
