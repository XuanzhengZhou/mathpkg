The Mackey-Arens theorem characterizes all locally convex topologies on a vector space $F$ that are consistent with a given duality $\langle F, G \rangle$, i.e., those topologies whose continuous dual is exactly $G$. The theorem establishes a biunivocal correspondence between consistent topologies and saturated families of $\sigma(G, F)$-relatively compact subsets of $G$ that cover $G$. This result, due independently to G. W. Mackey and R. Arens, is foundational in the theory of locally convex spaces, as it identifies precisely which polar topologies on $F$ are admissible.
