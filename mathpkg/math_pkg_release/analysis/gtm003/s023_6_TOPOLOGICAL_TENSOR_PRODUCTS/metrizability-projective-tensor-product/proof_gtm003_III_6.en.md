**Proof (A. Pietsch).** Let $\{p_n\}$, $\{q_n\}$ be increasing sequences of semi-norms generating the topologies of $E$, $F$ respectively, and denote by $r_n$ the semi-norm $p_n \otimes q_n$ for each $n \in \mathbb{N}$. The sequence $\{r_n\}$ is increasing and generates the projective topology of $E \otimes F$. Denote by $\bar{r}_n$ the continuous extension of $r_n$ to the completion $E \tilde{\otimes} F$.

Given $u \in E \tilde{\otimes} F$, there exists a sequence $\{u_n\} \subset E \otimes F$ such that
$$\bar{r}_n(u - u_n) < n^{-2} 2^{-(n+1)} \quad \text{for all } n \in \mathbb{N}.$$

Let $\sum_{i=1}^{i_1} \lambda_i (x_i \otimes y_i)$ be any representation of $u_1$, and set $v_n = u_{n+1} - u_n$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$. We estimate:
$$r_n(v_n) \leq \bar{r}_n(u - u_n) + \bar{r}_n(u - u_{n+1}) \leq \bar{r}_n(u - u_n) + \bar{r}_{n+1}(u - u_{n+1}) < n^{-2} 2^{-n}.$$

By Proposition 6.3, there exists a representation
$$v_n = \sum_{i=i_n+1}^{i_{n+1}} \lambda_i (x_i \otimes y_i)$$
such that $p_n(x_i) \leq n^{-1}$, $q_n(y_i) \leq n^{-1}$ whenever $i_n < i \leq i_{n+1}$, and
$$\sum_{i=i_n+1}^{i_{n+1}} |\lambda_i| \leq 2^{-n}.$$

Therefore,
$$u = u_1 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty v_n = \sum_{i=1}^\infty \lambda_i (x_i \otimes y_i),$$
where the sequences $\{x_i\}$, $\{y_i\}$, and $\{\lambda_i\}$ satisfy the stated properties. Since $\sum_{n=1}^\infty 2^{-n} = 1$, the series converges absolutely. The metrizability of $E \otimes_\pi F$ follows immediately from the fact that the topology is generated by the countable family $\{r_n\}$. $\square$
