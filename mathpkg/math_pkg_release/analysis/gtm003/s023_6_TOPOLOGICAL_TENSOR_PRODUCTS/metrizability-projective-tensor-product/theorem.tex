Let $E, F$ be metrizable locally convex spaces. Then $E \otimes_\pi F$ is metrizable, and its topology is generated by an increasing sequence of semi-norms $\{r_n\}$ where $r_n = p_n \otimes q_n$ and $\{p_n\}$, $\{q_n\}$ are increasing sequences of semi-norms generating the topologies of $E$ and $F$ respectively.

Moreover, every element $u \in E \tilde{\otimes} F$ (the completion) admits a representation as an absolutely convergent series
$$u = \sum_{i=1}^\infty \lambda_i (x_i \otimes y_i)$$
where $\{x_i\} \subset E$ and $\{y_i\} \subset F$ are null sequences and $\sum_{i=1}^\infty |\lambda_i| < \infty$. More precisely, there exist representations such that for each $n \in \mathbb{N}$, the semi-norms satisfy $p_n(x_i) \leq n^{-1}$, $q_n(y_i) \leq n^{-1}$ for all sufficiently large $i$, and $\sum |\lambda_i|$ can be made arbitrarily small for the "tail" terms.
