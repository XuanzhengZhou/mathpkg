The tensor product $E \otimes F$ of two vector spaces $E$ and $F$ over a field $K$ is constructed as the linear hull of the image of the canonical bilinear map $\chi: E \times F \to B(E,F)^*$, where $B(E,F)$ is the space of all bilinear forms on $E \times F$. Each element $x \otimes y$ is realized as the evaluation functional $f \mapsto f(x,y)$ on $B(E,F)$. Every element of $E \otimes F$ is expressible as a finite linear combination $\sum \lambda_i (x_i \otimes y_i)$. This algebraic construction, which satisfies the universal bilinear factorization property, serves as the foundation for introducing topological structures on tensor products of locally convex spaces.
