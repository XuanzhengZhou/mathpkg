Proposition 6.3 constructs the fundamental semi-norms that generate the projective tensor product topology. Given continuous semi-norms $p$ on $E$ and $q$ on $F$, the tensor product semi-norm $r = p \otimes q$ is defined as the infimum of $\sum p(x_i)q(y_i)$ over all finite representations $u = \sum x_i \otimes y_i$. This semi-norm is the Minkowski gauge of the convex circled hull $\Gamma(U \otimes V)$, where $U$ and $V$ are the open unit balls of $p$ and $q$. The proposition also provides a refined representation theorem: every element admits an "almost optimal" representation with controlled coefficients.
