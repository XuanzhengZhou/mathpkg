The projective tensor product topology $\mathfrak{T}_p$ is the finest locally convex topology on the algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$ for which the canonical bilinear map $\chi: E \times F \to E \otimes F$ is continuous. It is defined as the upper bound of the family of all such topologies. A fundamental system of zero-neighborhoods is given by the convex, circled hulls $\Gamma(U \otimes V)$ where $U$ and $V$ range over zero-neighborhood bases of $E$ and $F$. The resulting space $E \otimes_\pi F$ is always Hausdorff, and its completion $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ plays a central role in the theory of topological tensor products.
