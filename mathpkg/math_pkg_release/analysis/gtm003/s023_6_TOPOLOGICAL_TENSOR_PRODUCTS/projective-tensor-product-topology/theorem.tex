Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces over $K$. Consider the family $\mathcal{T}$ of all locally convex topologies on $E \otimes F$ for which the canonical bilinear map $\chi: E \times F \to E \otimes F$ is continuous.

The **projective tensor product topology** $\mathfrak{T}_p$ on $E \otimes F$ is the upper bound of $\mathcal{T}$ (i.e., the finest locally convex topology on $E \otimes F$ for which $\chi$ is continuous).

If $\mathfrak{U}$, $\mathfrak{V}$ are $0$-neighborhood bases in $E$, $F$ respectively, then the family of convex, circled hulls
$$\{\Gamma(U \otimes V) : U \in \mathfrak{U},\; V \in \mathfrak{V}\}$$
is a neighborhood base of $0$ for $\mathfrak{T}_p$.

The space $E \otimes F$ endowed with $\mathfrak{T}_p$ is denoted by $E \otimes_\pi F$, and its completion by $E \tilde{\otimes} F$. The projective topology $\mathfrak{T}_p$ is always a Hausdorff topology.
