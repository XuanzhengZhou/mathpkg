Let $F, G$ be a pair of vector spaces over $K$, and let $f$ be a bilinear form on $F \times G$ satisfying the separation axioms:
$$
\begin{aligned}
(S_1) &\quad f(x_0, y) = 0 \text{ for all } y \in G \text{ implies } x_0 = 0. \\
(S_2) &\quad f(x, y_0) = 0 \text{ for all } x \in F \text{ implies } y_0 = 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The triple $(F, G, f)$ is called a \textbf{dual system} or \textbf{duality} (over $K$). It is also customary to say that $f$ places $F$ and $G$ in duality, or \textbf{separated duality} if the validity of $(S_1)$ and $(S_2)$ is to be stressed.
