This proposition gives a precise necessary and sufficient condition for the canonical map $E \otimes F \to L(E^*, F)$ to be surjective: at least one of $\dim E^*$ or $\dim F$ must be finite. When both dimensions are infinite, the algebraic tensor product consists only of finite-rank operators, whereas $L(E^*, F)$ contains operators of infinite rank.
