Suppose $E$ to be a locally convex space. A family $\{x_\alpha : \alpha \in A\} \subset E$ is \textbf{absolutely summable} if it is summable in $E$ and if for each continuous semi-norm $p$ on $E$, the family $\{p(x_\alpha) : \alpha \in A\}$ is summable in $\mathbb{R}$.

If $A = \mathbb{N}$ and $\{x_n\}$ is absolutely summable, the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} x_n$ is called \textbf{absolutely convergent}.
