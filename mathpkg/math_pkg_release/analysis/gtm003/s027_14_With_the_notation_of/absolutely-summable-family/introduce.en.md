Absolute summability strengthens unconditional summability by requiring that the family remains summable after passing through every continuous seminorm. In a locally convex space, absolutely summable families form a fundamental class that is closely related to nuclearity: a locally convex space is nuclear precisely when every summable family is absolutely summable. This definition is the natural generalization of absolute convergence in normed spaces to the locally convex setting.
