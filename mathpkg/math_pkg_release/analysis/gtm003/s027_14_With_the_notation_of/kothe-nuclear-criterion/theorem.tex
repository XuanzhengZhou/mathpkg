Let $\{s_m^{(n)} : m \in \mathbb{N}\}$ ($n \in \mathbb{N}$) be a double sequence of non-negative numbers such that for each $n$, $\{x_m s_m^{(n)} : m \in \mathbb{N}\}$ is summable (in the sense of Exercise 23). Let $E$ be the space of all sequences $\{x_m\}$ such that the above holds for all $n$, provided with the topology generated by the semi-norms
$$
x \mapsto p_n(x) = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} |x_m s_m^{(n)}| \quad (n \in \mathbb{N}).
$$
Then $E$ is an (F)-space. This space is \textbf{nuclear} if and only if for each $n$, there exists $p \in \mathbb{N}$ such that
$$
\sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{s_m^{(n)}}{s_m^{(n+p)}} < +\infty,
$$
where any quotient $0/0$ is replaced by $0$.
