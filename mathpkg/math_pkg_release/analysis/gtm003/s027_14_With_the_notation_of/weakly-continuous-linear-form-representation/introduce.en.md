A fundamental fact about the weak topology: the continuous dual of $(E, \sigma(E, E'))$ coincides with the original dual $E'$. Weakly continuous linear forms are precisely those that are already continuous in the original topology. This result is central to duality theory and follows from the Hahn-Banach theorem together with the definition of the weak topology.
