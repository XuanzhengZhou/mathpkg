Let $E$ be a separable (B)-space (more generally, a separable barreled space), and let $\{x_n\}$ be a maximal, topologically free sequence in $E$. Then there exists a unique sequence $\{f_n\} \subset E'$ biorthogonal to $\{x_n\}$ (i.e., $f_i(x_j) = \delta_{ij}$). Moreover, $\{x_n\}$ is a Schauder basis of $E$ if for each $x \in E$ and each $g \in E'$, the numerical sequence $\left\{\sum_{k=1}^{n} f_k(x) g(x_k) : n \in \mathbb{N}\right\}$ is bounded.
