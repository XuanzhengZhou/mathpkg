Let $\langle F, G \rangle$ be a duality. Every $\sigma(F, G)$-continuous linear form $f$ on $F$ can be written as a finite linear combination of elements of $G$ (viewed as linear forms on $F$). That is, there exist $y_i \in G$ and scalars $\lambda_i \in K$ such that $f(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \lambda_i \langle x, y_i \rangle$ for all $x \in F$.
