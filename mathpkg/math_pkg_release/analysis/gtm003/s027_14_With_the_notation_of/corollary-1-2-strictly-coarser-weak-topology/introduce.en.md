This corollary states that in a dual system $\langle F, G \rangle$, any proper subspace $G_1 \subsetneq G$ induces a weak topology $\sigma(F, G_1)$ that is strictly weaker than $\sigma(F, G)$. Equivalently, the weak topology is maximally fine precisely when the dual is the full space $G$. This follows from Proposition 1.2: if the topologies coincide, they have the same continuous linear functionals, forcing $G_1 = G$.
