In any dual system $\langle F, G \rangle$, each $y \in G$ defines a linear form $f_y: x \mapsto \langle x, y \rangle$ on $F$. The map $y \mapsto f_y$ is linear and injective (by the separation axiom $(S_2)$), giving an isomorphism of $G$ onto a subspace of the algebraic dual $F^*$. This identification is the standard convention in duality theory and allows elements of $G$ to be treated as linear functionals on $F$.
