A Fréchet space (or (F)-space) is a locally convex space that is both complete and metrizable. This class of spaces generalizes Banach spaces: every Banach space is a Fréchet space, but the converse fails -- for instance, the space $K_0^{\mathbb{N}}$ of all numerical sequences with the product topology is a Fréchet space that is not normable. The category of Fréchet spaces is closed under taking closed subspaces, separated quotients, and countable products.
