Theorem 4.3 establishes that the property of being precompact is preserved under taking convex hulls and convex circled hulls in locally convex spaces. Since the circled hull of a precompact set is easily seen to be precompact, the main work of the proof lies in showing the convex hull of a precompact set remains precompact.
