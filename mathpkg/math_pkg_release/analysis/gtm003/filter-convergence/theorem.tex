Let $X$ be a topological space and $\mathfrak{F}$ a filter on $X$. $\mathfrak{F}$ is said to \text{converge} to $x \in X$ if the neighborhood filter $\mathfrak{U}(x)$ of $x$ is contained in $\mathfrak{F}$. If $X$ is a Hausdorff space, each convergent filter converges to exactly one $x \in X$; $x$ is called the \text{limit} of $\mathfrak{F}$.
