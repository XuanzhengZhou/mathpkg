Let $L$ and $M$ be metric topological vector spaces whose respective metrics $d$, $\delta$ are given by pseudonorms: $d(x_1, x_2) = |x_1 - x_2|$ and $\delta(y_1, y_2) = |y_1 - y_2|$. Denote by $S_r = \{x \in L : |x| \leq r\}$ and $S_\rho = \{y \in M : |y| \leq \rho\}$ the closed balls centered at 0.

Let $L$ be complete and let $u: L \to M$ be a continuous linear map. Suppose there exists $r > 0$ and a sequence $\{\rho_n\}$ with $\rho_n \to 0$ such that $S_\rho \subset \overline{u(S_r)}$ for some $\rho > 0$. Then $\overline{u(S_r)}$ contains $S_\rho$ for suitable $r, \rho > 0$.
