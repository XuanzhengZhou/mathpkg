A locally convex space $E$ is called \textit{nuclear} if for every continuous linear map $u$ of $E$ into any Banach space $F$, there exists a convex, circled $0$-neighborhood $U$ in $E$ such that the canonical map $\phi_U: E \to \tilde{E}_U$ factors through a nuclear map; equivalently, the canonical map $E \to \tilde{E}_U$ is nuclear for each convex, circled $0$-neighborhood $U$.
