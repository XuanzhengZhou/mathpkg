Let $L$ and $M$ be locally convex spaces over $\mathbb{K}$ whose topologies are generated by the families of seminorms $\mathcal{P}$ and $\mathcal{Q}$, respectively. A linear map $u: L \to M$ is continuous if and only if for each $q \in \mathcal{Q}$ there exist $p_1, \ldots, p_n \in \mathcal{P}$ and a constant $c > 0$ such that

$$q(u(x)) \leq c \sup_i p_i(x)$$

for all $x \in L$.
