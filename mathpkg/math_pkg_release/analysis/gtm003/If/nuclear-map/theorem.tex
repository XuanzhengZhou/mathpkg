Let $E$ and $F$ be Banach spaces and let $E'$ be the strong dual of $E$. The imbedding $v \mapsto u$ of $E' \otimes F$ into $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is continuous for the projective tensor product topology and the operator norm topology. By completeness of $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$, this imbedding extends uniquely to a continuous linear map $\tau: E' \mathbin{\widehat{\otimes}} F \to \mathcal{L}(E, F)$.

A linear map $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is called \textit{nuclear} if it belongs to the range of $\tau$; that is, $u = \tau(v)$ for some $v \in E' \mathbin{\widehat{\otimes}} F$.
