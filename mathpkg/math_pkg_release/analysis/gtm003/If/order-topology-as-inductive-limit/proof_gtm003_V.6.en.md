For each $a \geq 0$, $L_a$ is the subspace generated by the order interval $[-a, a]$, on which the gauge $p_a$ of $[-a, a]$ defines a norm. The inclusion maps $L_a \hookrightarrow L$ are continuous when $L$ carries $\mathfrak{T}_O$ because $[-a, a]$ is $\mathfrak{T}_O$-bounded. The inductive limit topology $\mathfrak{T}_{\text{ind}}$ is the finest locally convex topology making all inclusions $L_a \to L$ continuous.

To show $\mathfrak{T}_O = \mathfrak{T}_{\text{ind}}$: let $W$ be a convex $0$-neighborhood for $\mathfrak{T}_O$. Then $W$ absorbs every order interval $[-a, a]$, so $W \cap L_a$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $L_a$ for each $a$. Hence $W$ is a $0$-neighborhood for $\mathfrak{T}_{\text{ind}}$, showing $\mathfrak{T}_O \subset \mathfrak{T}_{\text{ind}}$. The reverse inclusion holds because each $L_a$ carries a topology coarser than $\mathfrak{T}_O$, so their inductive limit is coarser than $\mathfrak{T}_O$.
