Let $L$ and $M$ be topological vector spaces over $\mathbb{K}$, with $M$ separated and complete. Let $u: L \to M$ be a continuous linear map. Then $u$ possesses a unique continuous linear extension $\bar{u}: \bar{L} \to M$ to any topological vector space $\bar{L}$ of which $L$ is a dense subspace. In particular, if $L$ is separated, $u$ extends uniquely to the completion $\tilde{L}$ of $L$.
