The existence and uniqueness of the continuous extension $\bar{u}$ to the completion $\tilde{E}$ is a general property of uniformly continuous maps with values in a complete space. If $U$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $E$ such that $u(U) \subset C$ with $C$ compact, then the restriction of $u$ to $U$ extends uniquely to the closure $\overline{U}$ in $\tilde{E}$. Linearity ensures the extension is defined on all of $\tilde{E}$. Nuclearity of $\bar{u}$ follows from the series representation, as the same sequences $\{f_n\}$ (extended continuously to $\tilde{E}$) and $\{y_n\}$ serve.
