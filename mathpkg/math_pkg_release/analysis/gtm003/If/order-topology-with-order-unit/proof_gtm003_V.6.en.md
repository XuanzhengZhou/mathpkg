The order interval $[-e, e]$ is convex, circled, and radial in $L$ (by definition of order unit). Since $L$ is Archimedean ordered, the gauge $p_e$ of $[-e, e]$ is a norm on $L$.

The topology generated by $p_e$ is finer than $\mathfrak{T}_O$ because $[-e, e]$ is $\mathfrak{T}_O$-bounded. It is coarser than $\mathfrak{T}_O$ because it is locally convex and $[-e, e]$ absorbs order intervals (any order interval $[x, y]$ is contained in some multiple of $[-e, e]$ since $e$ is an order unit). Hence $p_e$ generates $\mathfrak{T}_O$.

To see that $C$ is normal: the gauge $p_e$ satisfies $0 \leq x \leq y \implies p_e(x) \leq p_e(y)$, which is the definition of normality for the cone.

For the equivalence of (a) and (b): completeness of $(L, \mathfrak{T}_O)$ under the norm $p_e$ is equivalent to the condition that every absolutely summable series converges, which translates precisely to the order summability of positive $\ell^1$-type sequences.
