Suppose $L$ is non-meager in itself. If $L$ were not a Baire space, there would exist a non-empty meager open subset, and hence (by translation) a meager $0$-neighborhood $U$. Since $L = \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} nU$ (each $U$ is radial in a t.v.s.), and each $nU$ is meager (being a homothetic image of a meager set), $L$ would be a countable union of meager sets, hence meager -- contradicting the hypothesis that $L$ is non-meager in itself. Therefore $L$ is a Baire space.
