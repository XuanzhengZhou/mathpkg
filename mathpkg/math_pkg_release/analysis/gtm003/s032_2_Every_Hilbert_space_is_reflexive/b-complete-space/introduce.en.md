A B-complete space (also called a Ptak space) is a locally convex space satisfying a dual closure condition: a subspace $Q$ of the dual $E'$ is weakly closed whenever its intersection with each equicontinuous set $A$ is weakly closed in $A$. In the definition, the family of all equicontinuous subsets of $E'$ may be replaced by any fundamental subfamily, in particular by the polars $U^\circ$ of members $U$ of a neighborhood base in $E$. Every (F)-space is B-complete by the Krein-Smulian theorem.
