Since every hyperplane $H$ in $E'$ is convex, the sufficiency of the condition is clear from (6.2), Corollary 2.

To prove its necessity, let $E$ be complete and let $M$ be a convex subset of $E'$ such that $M \cap U^\circ$ is $\sigma(E', E)$-closed for every $0$-neighborhood $U$ in $E$. Since $\mathfrak{T}_f$ is consistent with $\langle E, E' \rangle$ by Corollary 2 above, it suffices to show that $M$ is $\mathfrak{T}_f$-closed. Denote by $M'$ the complement of $M$ in $E'$; the assumption implies that $M' \cap U^\circ$ is open in $U^\circ$ for $\sigma(E', E)$ and hence for $\mathfrak{T}_f$. Since every equicontinuous set $A \subset E'$ is contained in a suitable $U^\circ$, it follows that $M' \cap A$ is open in $A$ for $\mathfrak{T}_f$, and hence $M'$ is open for $\mathfrak{T}_f$.
