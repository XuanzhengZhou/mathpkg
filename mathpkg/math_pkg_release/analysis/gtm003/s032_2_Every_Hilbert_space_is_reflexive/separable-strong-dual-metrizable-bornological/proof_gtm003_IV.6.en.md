It is sufficient to show that $E'_\beta$ is infrabarreled. Hence let $D$ be a barrel in $E'_\beta$ that absorbs strongly bounded sets, and let $\{x'_n\}$ be a countable dense subset of $E' \sim D$. By (II, 9.2) there exists a closed (real) semi-space $H_n$ such that $x'_n \notin H_n$ and $D$ is contained in the interior of $H_n$. Hence $D \subset \bigcap_n H_n$ and $U = \bigcap_n H_n$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $E'_\beta$ in view of (6.5). Now the complement of the interior of $U$ contains $\{x'_n\}$, which is dense, so the interior must be contained in $D$, making $D$ a neighborhood.
