This corollary provides a clean algebraic criterion for weak normality: a cone $C$ is weakly normal precisely when the entire dual space $E'$ can be expressed as the difference $C' - C'$ of its dual cone. As an immediate consequence, every normal cone is automatically weakly normal, linking the strong topological notion of normality to weak normality.
