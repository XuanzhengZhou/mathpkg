This corollary shows that the weak closure of an $\mathfrak{S}$-cone is always a strict $\mathfrak{S}$-cone, provided the saturated family $\mathfrak{S}$ covers $E'$ with $\sigma(E',E)$-relatively compact sets. The result follows from Theorem 3.3 together with the bipolar theorem, illustrating a key permanence property of $\mathfrak{S}$-cones under weak closure.
