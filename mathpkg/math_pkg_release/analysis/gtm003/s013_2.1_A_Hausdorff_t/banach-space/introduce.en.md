A Banach space (also called a B-space) is a normed vector space that is complete with respect to the metric induced by its norm. In other words, every Cauchy sequence converges to an element of the space. Banach spaces form the fundamental setting for functional analysis, with key examples including Hilbert spaces, $L^p$ spaces, and spaces of continuous linear operators.
