A norm isomorphism is a bijective linear map between normed spaces that preserves the norm. It is the natural notion of isomorphism in the category of normed vector spaces: two normed spaces related by a norm isomorphism are indistinguishable from the perspective of their normed structure. Norm isomorphisms are necessarily continuous and have continuous inverses, so they are also topological isomorphisms.
