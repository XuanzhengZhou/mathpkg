For $1 \leq p < \infty$, the $L^p(\mu)$ space associated with a measure space $(X, \Sigma, \mu)$ is the Banach space of equivalence classes of measurable functions whose $p$-th power is integrable. It is constructed by taking the vector space $\mathscr{L}^p$ of $p$-integrable functions, equipping it with the $L^p$ semi-norm, and quotienting by the subspace of $\mu$-null functions to obtain a genuine norm. The completeness of $L^p(\mu)$ follows from the Riesz-Fischer theorem.
