This proposition shows that arbitrary (possibly uncountable) products of Hilbert spaces possess the approximation property, and that this property is inherited by all subspaces of such products. The proof uses the fact that for a product of Hilbert spaces, the local Banach spaces $\tilde{E}_V$ associated to a suitable $0$-neighborhood base are themselves finite products of Hilbert spaces (hence Hilbert spaces, which are known to have the approximation property). The result for subspaces follows by identifying the corresponding local Banach space $\tilde{M}_W$ with the closure of the canonical image in $\tilde{E}_V$.
