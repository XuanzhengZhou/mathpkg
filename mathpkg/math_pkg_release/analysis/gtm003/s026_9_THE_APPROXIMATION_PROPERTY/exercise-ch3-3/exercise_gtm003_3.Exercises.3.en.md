Let $E$ be a vector space, let $M$ be a subspace of $E$, and let $U$ be a radial, convex, circled subset of $E$. Denote by $\phi_U$ the canonical map of $E$ into $\tilde{E}_U$. (For notation see the beginning of Section 7.)

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If $V = U \cap M$, there exists a natural norm isomorphism of $M_V$ onto the subspace $\phi_U(M)$ of $E_U$.

\item[(b)] Let $\phi$ be the quotient map $E \to E/M$ and set $W = \phi(U)$. There exists a natural norm isomorphism of $(E/M)_W$ onto $E_U/N$, where $N$ is the closure of $\phi_U(M)$ in the normed space $E_U$.

\item[(c)] In addition to the hypotheses above, suppose that $E$ is a locally convex space and that $U$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $E$. Let $B$ be the set of all $x' \in E'$ such that $|x'(x)| \leq 1$ whenever $x \in U$. There exists a natural norm isomorphism of the strong dual of $E_U$ (or of the strong dual of $\tilde{E}_U$) onto $[E']_B$.
\end{enumerate}
