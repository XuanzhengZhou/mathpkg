Theorem 9.6 is a fundamental structural result about Schauder bases in Banach spaces. It states that for a normalized Schauder basis $\{x_n\}$, the coordinate (coefficient) functionals $f_n$ that extract the $n$-th coefficient in the basis expansion are automatically equicontinuous. Moreover, the basis expansion $x = \sum_n f_n(x)x_n$ converges not just pointwise but uniformly on every compact subset of $E$. The proof is a beautiful application of the Banach-Steinhaus theorem and the homomorphism theorem (Open Mapping Theorem), using an auxiliary norm $\|x\|_1 = \sup_n \|\sum_{k=1}^n \alpha_k x_k\|$ to establish boundedness of the coefficient functionals.
