Corollary 3 reduces the approximation problem from general locally convex spaces to Banach spaces: if a counterexample to the approximation property exists anywhere in the category of locally convex spaces, then one must exist among Banach spaces. This justifies the subsequent focus on Banach spaces in the analysis of the approximation property. The result follows from the fact that every locally convex space can be represented as a subspace of a product of Banach spaces (namely, via the local Banach spaces associated to a 0-neighborhood base), combined with the hereditary nature of the approximation property for subspaces of Hilbert space products established in the preceding results.
