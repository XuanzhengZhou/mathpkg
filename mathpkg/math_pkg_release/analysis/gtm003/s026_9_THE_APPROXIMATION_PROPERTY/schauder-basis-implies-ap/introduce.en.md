This corollary provides a large and important class of Banach spaces that are guaranteed to have the approximation property: any separable Banach space admitting a Schauder basis. The proof is immediate from Theorem 9.6, which shows that the identity operator can be expressed as $e = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} f_n \otimes x_n$, where the series converges in the topology $\mathcal{L}_c(E)$ of uniform convergence on compact sets. Since the partial sums $\sum_{k=1}^{n} f_k \otimes x_k$ are finite-rank operators in $E' \otimes E$, this directly verifies condition (a) of the approximation property for compact subsets.
