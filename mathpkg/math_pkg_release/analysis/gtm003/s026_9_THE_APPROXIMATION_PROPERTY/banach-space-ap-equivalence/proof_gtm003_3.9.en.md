$(a) \Rightarrow (b)$: If $w \in \mathcal{L}(F, E)$ is compact and $V$ is the unit ball of $F$, then $A = w(V)$ is precompact in $E$. Thus there exists $e_0 \in E' \otimes E$ such that, given $\varepsilon > 0$, $\|e_0(x) - x\| < \varepsilon$ for all $x \in A$. Thus $\|w_0 - w\| \leq \varepsilon$, where $w_0 = e_0 \circ w$. Since $w_0$ is an element of $F' \otimes E$ (namely, $\sum w'(x_i') \otimes x_i$ if $e_0 = \sum x_i' \otimes x_i$), the implication is proved.

$(b) \Rightarrow (a)$: Given $\varepsilon > 0$ and a compact, convex, circled subset $A$ of $E$, let $E_A$ be the normed space generated by $A$ and let $\psi_A: E_A \to E$ be the canonical injection. By the lemma on compact sets in Banach spaces (which precedes this theorem), $\psi_A$ is compact. Applying (b) with $F = E_A$, there exists $v \in F' \otimes E$ such that $\|\psi_A - v\| < \varepsilon$. If $v = \sum y_i' \otimes x_i$ with $y_i' \in E_A'$, then for each $x \in A = \psi_A(U)$ (where $U$ is the unit ball of $E_A$), we have $\|x - \sum y_i'(x) x_i\| < \varepsilon$. Extending each $y_i'$ to $x_i' \in E'$ by Hahn-Banach, we obtain the required approximation of the identity on $A$.

$(c) \Rightarrow (d)$: Let $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ be compact. Then $u' \in \mathcal{L}(F', E')$ is compact by (9.4). By (c), given $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $e_1 \in E'' \otimes E'$ such that $\|e_1(y') - y'\| < \varepsilon$ for all $y'$ in the image of the unit ball of $F'$ under $u'$, which is precompact in $E'$. If $e_1 = \sum f_i \otimes x_i'$ with $f_i \in E''$, then by considering the bidual, we have $\sum (f_i \circ u') \otimes x_i'$ and $f_i \circ u' = u''(f_i) = y_i \in E$ by the canonical identification. This implies $\|u' - \sum y_i \otimes x_i'\| < \varepsilon$ and, therefore, $\|u - \sum x_i' \otimes y_i\| < \varepsilon$. In view of (9.3) the implication is proved.

$(d) \Rightarrow (c)$: Let $A$ be any compact, convex, circled subset of $E'$; $A$ is norm bounded, hence equicontinuous, and it follows that $U = \{x \in E : |x'(x)| \leq 1 \text{ for all } x' \in A\}$ is a convex, circled $0$-neighborhood in $E$. Using the fact that the dual of $(E', \sigma(E', E))$ can be identified with $E$, (IV, 1.2), it is quickly verified by an application of the Hahn-Banach theorem that $[E']_A$ can be identified with the strong dual of $E_U$ and that under this identification $\psi_A$ is the adjoint map of $\phi_U$ (Exercise 3). Since $\psi_A$ is compact, so is $\phi_U$ by (9.4). Hence (d) implies that, given $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists an element $w \in E' \otimes \tilde{E}_U$ satisfying $\|w - \phi_U\| < \varepsilon/2$. Since $\phi_U(E)$ is dense in $\tilde{E}_U$, there exists $w_0 \in E' \otimes \phi_U(E)$ such that $\|w_0 - \phi_U\| < \varepsilon$. Let $w_0 = \sum x_i' \otimes \phi_U(x_i)$. It follows that $\|w'_0 - \psi_A\| < \varepsilon$ or, equivalently, that

$$\|x' - \sum x'(x_i)x_i'\| < \varepsilon$$

whenever $x' \in A$; this shows that $E'$ has the approximation property.
