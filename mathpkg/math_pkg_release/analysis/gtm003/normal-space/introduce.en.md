A normal space is a Hausdorff space where disjoint closed sets can be separated by disjoint open sets. Every compact Hausdorff space and every metrizable space is normal. Normality is relevant for the Urysohn lemma and Tietze extension theorem, which are prerequisites for the theory of continuous functions on topological vector spaces.
