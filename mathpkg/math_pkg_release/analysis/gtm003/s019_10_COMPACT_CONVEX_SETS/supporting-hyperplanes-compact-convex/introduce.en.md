For a non-empty compact convex subset $C$ of a locally convex space, every closed real hyperplane $H$ determines at least one and at most two supporting hyperplanes of $C$ parallel to $H$. These are obtained by evaluating the continuous linear functional defining $H$ on $C$ and taking its infimum and supremum. Furthermore, $C$ can be recovered as the intersection of all closed half-spaces that contain $C$ and are bounded by its supporting hyperplanes.
