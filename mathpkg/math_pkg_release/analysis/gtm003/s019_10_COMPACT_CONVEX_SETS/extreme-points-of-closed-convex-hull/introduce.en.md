Given a compact subset $A$ of a locally convex space whose closed convex hull $C = \overline{\operatorname{co}}(A)$ is also compact, every extreme point of $C$ must belong to the original set $A$. This is a converse to the Krein-Milman theorem (often called Milman's theorem). The proof uses the fact that any extreme point of $C$ can be approximated arbitrarily well by points of $A$ via a finite convex combination argument and the compactness of the convex hulls of finite unions.
