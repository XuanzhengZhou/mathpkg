An extreme point of a convex set $A$ is a point that cannot be expressed as a convex combination of two other distinct points of $A$. Geometrically, this means that the singleton $\{x_0\}$ is itself a supporting manifold of $A$: the set $A$ lies on one side of some affine manifold that touches $A$ only at $x_0$. Extreme points are the "corners" of a convex set and play a central role in the Krein-Milman theorem.
