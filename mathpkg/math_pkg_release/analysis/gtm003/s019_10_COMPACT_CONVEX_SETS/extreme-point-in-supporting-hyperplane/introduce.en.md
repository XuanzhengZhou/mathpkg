Let $C$ be a compact convex subset of a locally convex space and $H$ a closed real hyperplane that supports $C$. Then $H$ must contain at least one extreme point of $C$. This theorem, proved via Zorn's lemma applied to the family of all closed real linear manifolds contained in $H$ that support $C$, is the crucial step toward the Krein-Milman theorem. The minimal supporting manifold in $H$ must be a singleton, which is then an extreme point.
