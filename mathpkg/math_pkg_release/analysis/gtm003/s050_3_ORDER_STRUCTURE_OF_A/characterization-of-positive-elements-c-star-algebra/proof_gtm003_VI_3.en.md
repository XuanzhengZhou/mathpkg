By Theorem 2.2, Corollary 1, the C*-algebra generated by a self-adjoint element $x$ and the unit $e$ is isometrically isomorphic to $C(\sigma(x))$. Under this isomorphism, positivity of $x$ corresponds to the function $t \mapsto t$ being non-negative on $\sigma(x)$, which is equivalent to $\sigma(x) \subset \mathbb{R}_+$. The norm conditions follow from the fact that in $C(K)$, a real-valued function $f$ satisfies $f \geq 0$ if and only if $\| \|f\| \cdot 1_K - f\| \leq \|f\|$.
