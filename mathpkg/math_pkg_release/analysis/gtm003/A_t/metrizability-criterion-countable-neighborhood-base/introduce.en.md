This theorem, due to G. Birkhoff and S. Kakutani, characterizes metrizable Hausdorff topological vector spaces as exactly those possessing a countable neighbourhood base at the origin. The function $x \mapsto |x|$ constructed in the proof is an $F$-norm: it is subadditive, satisfies $|\lambda x| \leq |x|$ for $|\lambda| \leq 1$, vanishes only at $0$, and generates a translation-invariant metric compatible with the vector space topology. The metric $(x,y) \mapsto |x-y|$ is thus compatible with both the topology and the canonical uniformity of the t.v.s.
