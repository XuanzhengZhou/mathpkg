This theorem shows that the property of being locally bounded is sufficient for metrizability in Hausdorff topological vector spaces. The proof constructs a countable neighbourhood base of $0$ from a single bounded neighbourhood by scaling with a null sequence of scalars, then invokes Theorem 6.1. The converse is false: a product of countably many one-dimensional t.v.s. is metrizable but not locally bounded. A quasi-complete locally bounded t.v.s. is automatically complete.
