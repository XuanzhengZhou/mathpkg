A topological vector space is metrizable if its vector space topology can be generated by a metric whose open balls form a neighbourhood base. In a metrizable t.v.s., the metric need not be translation-invariant; the metric uniformity may differ from the canonical uniformity associated with the vector space topology. Nevertheless, all uniformity notions (completeness, uniform continuity, etc.) are understood with respect to the canonical translation-invariant uniformity of the t.v.s.
