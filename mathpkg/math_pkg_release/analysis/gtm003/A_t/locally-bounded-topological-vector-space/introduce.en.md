A topological vector space is locally bounded if the origin admits a bounded neighbourhood. This implies the existence of a fundamental system of bounded neighbourhoods at the origin. Examples include the $L^p$ spaces for $0 < p \leq \infty$ (with the usual quasi-norm topology). Locally bounded spaces form an important class: by Theorem 6.2, every locally bounded Hausdorff t.v.s. is automatically metrizable. A quasi-complete locally bounded t.v.s. is complete.
