For any compact subset $C$ of $\mathbb{R}^k$, the space $\mathcal{D}_C$ of smooth functions supported in $C$ inherits nuclearity from the interval-supported space $\mathcal{D}_I$. The argument uses two elementary facts: every compact set $C$ is contained in some $k$-dimensional interval $I$, and $\mathcal{D}_C$ forms a closed subspace of the nuclear space $\mathcal{D}_I$. The hereditary property (7.4) of nuclear spaces then yields the result.
