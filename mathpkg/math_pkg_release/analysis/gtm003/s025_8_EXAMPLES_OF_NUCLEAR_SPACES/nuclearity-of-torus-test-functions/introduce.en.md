The space $\mathcal{D}_T$ of infinitely differentiable functions on the $k$-dimensional torus $T$, equipped with the topology of uniform convergence in all derivatives, provides a fundamental example of a nuclear Fréchet space. Using Fourier series expansions on the torus, one can represent every continuous linear map from $\mathcal{D}_T$ into an arbitrary quasi-complete locally convex space as a nuclear operator. This is one of the classical motivating examples for the theory of nuclear spaces developed by Grothendieck.
