The space $\mathcal{E}$ of all smooth functions on $\mathbb{R}^k$, equipped with the Fréchet topology of uniform convergence of all derivatives on compact subsets, is another fundamental example of a nuclear Fréchet space. Its topology is generated by a countable family of seminorms $p_n(f) = \sup \{|D^m f(t)| : t \in C_n, m \leq n\}$ where $\{C_n\}$ exhausts $\mathbb{R}^k$. The nuclearity of $\mathcal{E}$ follows from the general result (IV, 9.7) and is closely related to the nuclearity of $\mathcal{D}_C$ via duality.
