Theorem 9.9, due to Grothendieck, provides an explicit description of the strong dual and strong bidual of the completed projective tensor product $E \tilde{\otimes} F$. When $E$ is a nuclear (F)-space and $F$ is an (F)-space (or both are (DF)-spaces with $E$ nuclear), the strong dual is $\mathcal{B}_b(E, F)$, and the strong bidual is $E \tilde{\otimes} F''$. This result is fundamental for understanding the duality of tensor products in the nuclear setting.
