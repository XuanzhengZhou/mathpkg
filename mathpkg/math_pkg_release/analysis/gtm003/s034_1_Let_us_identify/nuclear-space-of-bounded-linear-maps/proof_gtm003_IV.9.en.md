Since $E$ is semi-reflexive (Section 5), each bounded subset of $E$ is an equicontinuous subset in the dual of $E'_\beta$, and hence $\mathcal{L}_b(E, F)$ can be identified with a subspace of $\mathfrak{B}_e(E_\sigma, F'_\sigma)$. Since $E'_\beta$ is nuclear, (9.4) implies that the completion of $\mathfrak{B}_e(E_\sigma, F'_\sigma)$ can be identified with $E'_\beta \otimes F$. Now $E'_\beta \otimes F$ is nuclear by (III, 7.5) and hence $\mathcal{L}_b(E, F)$ is nuclear by (III, 7.4). Compare Exercise 34.
