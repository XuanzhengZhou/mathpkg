A summable family in a locally convex space is called absolutely summable if the family of its seminorms is summable in $\mathbb{R}$ for every continuous seminorm. In finite-dimensional spaces, summability and absolute summability coincide, but in infinite dimensions they differ in general. A deep theorem states that within the class of (F)-spaces, the equality of summable and absolutely summable sequences characterizes nuclear spaces.
