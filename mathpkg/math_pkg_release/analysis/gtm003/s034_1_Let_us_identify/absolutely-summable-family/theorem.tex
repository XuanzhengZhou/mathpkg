\text{A summable family } \{x_\alpha : \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\} \text{ in a l.c.s. } E \text{ is absolutely summable if for every continuous semi-norm } p \text{ on } E, \text{ the family } \{p(x_\alpha) : \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\} \text{ is summable in } \mathbb{R}. \text{ It is well known that the two notions coincide when } E \text{ is finite-dimensional; within the class of (F)-spaces, the identity of the respective sets of summable and absolutely summable sequences characterizes nuclear spaces.}
