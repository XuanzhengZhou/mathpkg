A Hilbert-Schmidt transformation is a continuous linear map between Hilbert spaces characterized by the square-summability of the norms of the images of an orthonormal basis. This condition is independent of the choice of orthonormal basis and is symmetric: it equals the square-sum of the norms of the images under the adjoint. Every Hilbert-Schmidt transformation is compact and vanishes on all but countably many basis vectors. The concept is due to K. Maurin and plays a fundamental role in the theory of nuclear spaces.
