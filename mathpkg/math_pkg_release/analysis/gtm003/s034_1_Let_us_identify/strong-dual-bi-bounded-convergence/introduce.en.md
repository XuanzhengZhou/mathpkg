This theorem identifies two natural topologies on the space of continuous bilinear forms $\mathcal{B}(E, F)$. When $E$ and $F$ are both (DF)-spaces, or both (F)-spaces with $E$ nuclear, the strong dual topology $\beta(\mathcal{B}(E, F), E \tilde{\otimes} F)$ coincides with the topology of bi-bounded convergence. This is a key structural result in the duality theory of tensor products.
