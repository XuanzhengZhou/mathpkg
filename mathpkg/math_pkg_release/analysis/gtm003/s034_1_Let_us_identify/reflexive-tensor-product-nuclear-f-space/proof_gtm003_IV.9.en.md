This follows directly from Theorem 9.9. Since $F$ is reflexive, $F'' = F$, and the bidual identification $E \tilde{\otimes} F'' \cong E \tilde{\otimes} F$ shows that the canonical embedding of $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ into its bidual is surjective, hence $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ is reflexive. By symmetry, the same argument applied to the strong duals yields that $E'_\beta \tilde{\otimes} F'_\beta$ is also reflexive.
