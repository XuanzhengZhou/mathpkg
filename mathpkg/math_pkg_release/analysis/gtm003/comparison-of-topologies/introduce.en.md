One topology is finer than another if it has more open sets (stronger topology). The coarser topology has fewer open sets (weaker topology). The set of all topologies on a given set forms a complete lattice under inclusion. The existence of a finest topology coarser than a given family is essential for constructing projective (initial) topologies.
