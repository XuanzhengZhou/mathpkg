A cone $C$ in a topological vector space $L$ is called normal if the neighborhood filter of $0$ coincides with its $C$-saturated version, i.e., $\mathfrak{U} = [\mathfrak{U}]$. This means that the $C$-saturated hulls of $0$-neighborhoods again form a $0$-neighborhood base. Normal cones are fundamental to the duality theory of ordered topological vector spaces, linking the order structure (via $C$) with the topology. The concept is characterized by several equivalent conditions in Theorem 3.1.
