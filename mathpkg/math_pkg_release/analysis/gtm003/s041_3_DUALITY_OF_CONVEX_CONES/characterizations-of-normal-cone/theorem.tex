Let $L$ be a topological vector space and $C$ a cone in $L$. The following statements are equivalent:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $C$ is normal, i.e., $\mathfrak{U} = [\mathfrak{U}]$.
\item[(b)] The family $\mathfrak{B} = \{[U] : U \in \mathfrak{U}\}$ is a neighborhood base of $0$ in $L$.
\item[(c)] There exists a $0$-neighborhood base $\mathfrak{B}$ in $L$ such that $[V \cap C] \subset V$ for all $V \in \mathfrak{B}$.
\end{itemize}

If $K = \mathbb{R}$ and the topology of $L$ is locally convex, then each of the above statements is equivalent to:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(d)] There exists a $0$-neighborhood base in $L$ consisting of convex, circled, and $C$-saturated sets.
\item[(e)] The topology of $L$ can be defined by a family $\mathcal{P}$ of seminorms with the property that $0 \leq x \leq y$ (with respect to the order defined by $C$) implies $p(x) \leq p(y)$ for all $p \in \mathcal{P}$.
\end{itemize}
