This proposition states that normality of cones is preserved under arbitrary products. Given a family of topological vector spaces $L_\alpha$ each equipped with a cone $C_\alpha$, the product cone $C = \prod_\alpha C_\alpha$ in the product space $L = \prod_\alpha L_\alpha$ is normal exactly when every factor cone $C_\alpha$ is normal. This result is fundamental for constructing normal cones in product spaces.
