The polar $M^\circ$ of a subset $M$ in a dual pair $\langle F, G \rangle$ inherits fundamental geometric and algebraic properties from the duality. It is always $\sigma(G, F)$-closed, convex, and contains the origin. Circledness and the subspace property are preserved under the polar operation, making the polar a structure-preserving map between the two spaces of a duality.
