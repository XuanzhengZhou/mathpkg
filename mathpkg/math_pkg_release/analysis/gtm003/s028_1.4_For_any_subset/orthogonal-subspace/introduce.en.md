In the context of a general dual pair $\langle F, G \rangle$, the polar $M^\circ$ of a subspace $M \subset F$ is called the orthogonal subspace of $M$ in $G$. This terminology is motivated by the analogy with the orthogonal complement in Hilbert space theory, but extends to arbitrary dualities. The bipolar theorem implies that each $\sigma(F, G)$-closed subspace of $F$ is the orthogonal subspace of its orthogonal subspace in $G$, establishing a Galois correspondence between closed subspaces.
