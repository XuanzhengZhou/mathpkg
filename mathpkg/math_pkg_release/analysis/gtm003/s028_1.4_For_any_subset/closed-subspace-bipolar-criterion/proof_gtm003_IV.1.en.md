If $M$ is a subspace of $F$, then $M \cup \{0\} = M$, and the closed convex hull of $M$ is simply the $\sigma(F, G)$-closure of $M$ (since $M$ is already convex). By the bipolar theorem (1.5), $M^{\circ\circ}$ is the $\sigma(F, G)$-closed convex hull of $M \cup \{0\} = M$, which is exactly the $\sigma(F, G)$-closure of $M$. Therefore $M = M^{\circ\circ}$ if and only if $M$ equals its $\sigma(F, G)$-closure, i.e., $M$ is $\sigma(F, G)$-closed.
