An important consequence of the bipolar theorem is that a subspace equals its bipolar precisely when it is closed in the weak topology $\sigma(F, G)$. This provides a duality-theoretic characterization of weak closedness for subspaces. Equivalently, the polar map $M \mapsto M^\circ$ sets up a bijection between the $\sigma(F, G)$-closed subspaces of $F$ and the $\sigma(G, F)$-closed subspaces of $G$.
