These are elementary consequences of the definition of a topological vector lattice (recorded without explicit proof in the source text). The positive cone $C$ is closed because the topology is Hausdorff and locally solid; normality follows from the local solidity and the fact that each solid 0-neighborhood absorbs order intervals; the cone being a strict $\mathcal{B}$-cone follows from the existence of a base of circled solid 0-neighborhoods.
