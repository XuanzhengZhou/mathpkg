A lattice semi-norm is a semi-norm on a vector lattice that respects the lattice ordering: if the absolute value of $y$ is dominated by the absolute value of $x$, then the semi-norm of $y$ does not exceed that of $x$. The topology of any locally convex vector lattice can be generated by a family of such lattice semi-norms.
