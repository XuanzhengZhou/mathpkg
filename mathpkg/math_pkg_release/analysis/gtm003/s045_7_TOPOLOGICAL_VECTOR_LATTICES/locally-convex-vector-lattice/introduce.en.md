A locally convex vector lattice (l.c.v.l.) is a topological vector lattice whose topology is locally convex. Such spaces possess a 0-neighborhood base of convex solid sets. The topology of an l.c.v.l. can be generated by a family of lattice semi-norms. Examples include Fréchet lattices, normed lattices, and Banach lattices.
