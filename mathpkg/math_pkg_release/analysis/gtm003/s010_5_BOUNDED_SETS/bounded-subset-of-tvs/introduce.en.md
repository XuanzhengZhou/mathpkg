A subset of a topological vector space is called bounded if it can be absorbed by every neighborhood of zero. Equivalently, since circled zero-neighborhoods form a base at zero, a set is bounded exactly when every circled zero-neighborhood absorbs it. This generalizes the familiar notion of boundedness in normed spaces, where a set is bounded if it is contained in some ball of finite radius centered at the origin.
