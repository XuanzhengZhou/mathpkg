Continuous linear maps between topological vector spaces preserve both boundedness and total boundedness. If $u: L \to M$ is continuous and linear, and $B \subset L$ is bounded, then the image $u(B)$ is bounded in $M$. This follows directly from the definition: for any $0$-neighborhood $V \subset M$, the preimage $u^{-1}(V)$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $L$, so $B \subset \lambda u^{-1}(V)$ implies $u(B) \subset \lambda V$. The analogous statement holds for totally bounded sets.
