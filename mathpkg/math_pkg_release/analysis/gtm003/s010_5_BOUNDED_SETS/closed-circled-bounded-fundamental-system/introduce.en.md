Any bounded subset of a topological vector space is contained in the closure of its circled hull, which is closed, circled, and bounded by Theorem 5.1. Therefore the collection of all closed, circled bounded subsets forms a fundamental system of bounded sets. This is the natural bornological analogue of the fact that closed circled zero-neighborhoods form a fundamental system of zero-neighborhoods.
