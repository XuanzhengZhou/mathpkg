In a Hausdorff topological vector space, compact sets are well-behaved under algebraic operations. Finite unions, Minkowski sums, and scalar multiples of compact sets remain compact. When the scalar field is locally compact (as is the case for $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$), even the circled hull of a compact set is compact. These properties are essential for establishing the continuity of operations and for applications in the theory of convex compactness.
