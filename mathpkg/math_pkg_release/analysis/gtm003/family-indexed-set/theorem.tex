If $A$ is a non-empty set and $X$ is a set, a mapping $\alpha \mapsto x(\alpha)$ of $A$ into $X$ is called a family in $X$. One writes $x_\alpha$ for $x(\alpha)$ and denotes the family by $\{x_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$. Every non-empty set $X$ can be viewed as the family (identity map) $x \mapsto x$ over itself.
