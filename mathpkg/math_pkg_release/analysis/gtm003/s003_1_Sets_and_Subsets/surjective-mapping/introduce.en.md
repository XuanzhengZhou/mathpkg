A mapping $f: X \rightarrow Y$ is surjective (onto) if every element of the codomain $Y$ is the image of some element of the domain: $f(X) = Y$. Surjectivity ensures that the mapping covers the entire target set. A surjective mapping admits a right inverse (assuming the axiom of choice), providing a section of the mapping.
