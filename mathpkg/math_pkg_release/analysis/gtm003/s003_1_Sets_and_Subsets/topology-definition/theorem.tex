Let $X$ be a set and $\mathfrak{G}$ a set of subsets of $X$ invariant under finite intersections and arbitrary unions. It follows that $X \in \mathfrak{G}$ (as the intersection of the empty subset of $\mathfrak{G}$) and $\varnothing \in \mathfrak{G}$ (as the union of the empty subset of $\mathfrak{G}$). We say that $\mathfrak{G}$ defines a \text{topology $\mathfrak{T}$} on $X$. The sets $G \in \mathfrak{G}$ are called \text{open}, and their complements $F = X \setminus G$ are called \text{closed} with respect to $\mathfrak{T}$.
