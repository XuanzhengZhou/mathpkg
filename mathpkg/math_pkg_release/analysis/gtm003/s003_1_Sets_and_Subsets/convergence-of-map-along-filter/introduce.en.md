A map $f: X \to Y$ between topological spaces converges to a point $y \in Y$ along a filter $\mathfrak{F}$ on $X$ if the image filter $f(\mathfrak{F})$ converges to $y$. This definition unifies several convergence notions: continuity at a point means convergence along the neighborhood filter, and limits of sequences are the special case of convergence along the section filter of the sequence. This filter-based approach provides a consistent framework for discussing limits in general topological spaces without requiring countability axioms.
