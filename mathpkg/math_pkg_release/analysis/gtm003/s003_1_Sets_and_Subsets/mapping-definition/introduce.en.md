A mapping (or function) $f: X \rightarrow Y$ assigns to each element $x$ of its domain $X$ a unique element $f(x)$ of its codomain $Y$. This is the fundamental concept connecting sets: for any subset $A \subset X$, the image is the set of all values $f(x)$ as $x$ ranges over $A$. Mappings are the primary objects through which mathematical structures are compared and related.
