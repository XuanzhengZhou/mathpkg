A complete lattice is an ordered set in which every non-empty subset has both a supremum and an infimum. This is a stronger condition than being a lattice, which only requires suprema and infima for pairs. Complete lattices are always bounded (possessing a greatest and least element). The author notes that the term "complete lattice" is generally avoided in the book due to possible confusion with uniform completeness in topological vector spaces.
