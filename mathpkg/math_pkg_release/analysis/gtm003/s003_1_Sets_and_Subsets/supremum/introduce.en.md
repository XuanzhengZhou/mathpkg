The supremum $\sup A$ of a majorized subset $A$ of an ordered set $(X, \leq)$ is the least upper bound: it is an upper bound that is smaller than or equal to every other upper bound. When it exists, the supremum is unique. The supremum generalizes the notion of maximum to sets that may not contain their least upper bound, and is central to completeness in ordered structures such as the real numbers.
