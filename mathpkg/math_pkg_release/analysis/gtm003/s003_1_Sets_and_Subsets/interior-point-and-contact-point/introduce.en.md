An interior point of a subset $A$ in a topological space is a point that belongs to the interior $\mathring{A}$, meaning there exists an open neighborhood of the point entirely contained in $A$. A contact point (also called adherent point) of $A$ is a point belonging to the closure $\bar{A}$, meaning every open neighborhood of the point intersects $A$. These dual notions characterize the relationship between points and sets: interior points are "deeply inside" $A$, while contact points can be approximated by elements of $A$.
