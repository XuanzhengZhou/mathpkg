The composition $g \circ f$ of two mappings $f: X \rightarrow Y$ and $g: Y \rightarrow Z$ is the mapping from $X$ to $Z$ defined by $(g \circ f)(x) = g(f(x))$. Composition is the primary operation for chaining mappings and is associative: $(h \circ g) \circ f = h \circ (g \circ f)$ whenever the compositions are defined.
