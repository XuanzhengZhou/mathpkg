Let $A$ be a non-empty set and $X$ a set. A mapping $\alpha \mapsto x(\alpha)$ of $A$ into $X$ is also called a \text{family} in $X$. One writes $x_\alpha$ for $x(\alpha)$ and denotes the family by $\{x_\alpha: \alpha \in A\}$. The term family is used for mappings whose domain $A$ enters only in terms of its set-theoretic properties (i.e., cardinality and possibly order).
