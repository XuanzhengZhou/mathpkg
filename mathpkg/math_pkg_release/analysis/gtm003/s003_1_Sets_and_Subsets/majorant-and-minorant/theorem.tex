Let $(X, \leq)$ be an ordered set. A subset $A \subset X$ is called \text{majorized} if there exists $a_0 \in X$ such that $a \leq a_0$ for all $a \in A$; $a_0$ is called a \text{majorant} (upper bound) of $A$. Dually, $A$ is \text{minorized} by $a_0$ if $a_0 \leq a$ for all $a \in A$; then $a_0$ is called a \text{minorant} (lower bound) of $A$.
