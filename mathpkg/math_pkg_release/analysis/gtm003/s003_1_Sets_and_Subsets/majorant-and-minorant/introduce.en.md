In an ordered set $(X, \leq)$, a subset $A$ is majorized if it has an upper bound (majorant) $a_0$ satisfying $a \leq a_0$ for all $a \in A$. Dually, $A$ is minorized if it has a lower bound (minorant) $a_0$ with $a_0 \leq a$ for all $a \in A$. These notions are fundamental for defining completeness properties, suprema and infima, and are essential in the theory of lattices and real analysis.
