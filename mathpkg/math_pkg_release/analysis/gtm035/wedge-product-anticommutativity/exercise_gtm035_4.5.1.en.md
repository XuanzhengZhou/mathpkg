# Exercise 4.5

Prove Lemma 4.5: If $\tau \in \wedge^k(V)$ and $\sigma \in \wedge^l(V)$, then

$$\tau \wedge \sigma = (-1)^{kl} \sigma \wedge \tau.$$

**Hint:** Consider the permutation $\rho \in S_{k+l}$ that interchanges the first $k$ and last $l$ positions. This permutation has sign $(-1)^{kl}$. Re-index the sum in the definition of the wedge product using this permutation.
