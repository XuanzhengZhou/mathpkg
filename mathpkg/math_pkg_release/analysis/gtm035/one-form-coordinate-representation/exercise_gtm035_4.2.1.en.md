# Exercise 4.2

Prove Lemma 4.2: Every 1-form $\omega$ admits a unique representation

$$\omega = \sum_{j=1}^{N} C_j \, dx_j,$$

where the $C_j$ are scalar functions on $\Omega$.

**Hint:** Use Lemma 4.1 and duality. The dual basis $\{dx_1|_x, \ldots, dx_N|_x\}$ to $\{\partial/\partial x_1|_x, \ldots, \partial/\partial x_N|_x\}$ is characterized by $dx_j(\partial/\partial x_k) = \delta_{jk}$. Define $C_j(x) = \omega(x)(\partial/\partial x_j|_x)$.
