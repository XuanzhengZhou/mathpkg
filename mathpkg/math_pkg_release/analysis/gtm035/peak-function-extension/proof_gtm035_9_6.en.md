# Proof of the Extension of Peak Functions

Let $A$ be a Banach algebra with maximal ideal space $\mathcal{M}$. Let $T$ be a closed subset of $\mathcal{M}$ and $U$ an open neighborhood of $T$. Suppose there exists $\varphi \in A$ with $\hat{\varphi} = 1$ on $T$ and $|\hat{\varphi}| < 1$ on $\overline{U} \setminus T$. We construct $e \in A$ with $\hat{e} = 1$ on $T$ and $|\hat{e}| < 1$ on $\mathcal{M} \setminus T$.

**Step 1: Separating $T$ from $\mathcal{M} \setminus U$ in the Gelfand spectrum.** Since $T$ and $\mathcal{M} \setminus U$ are disjoint closed subsets of $\mathcal{M}$, there exist $g_2, \ldots, g_n \in A$ such that if $\hat{g} : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{C}^{n-1}$ is the map $m \mapsto (\hat{g}_2(m), \ldots, \hat{g}_n(m))$, then

$$\hat{g}(T) \cap \hat{g}(\mathcal{M} \setminus U) = \emptyset.$$

(This follows from the fact that $\hat{A}$ separates points and a standard compactness argument, cf. Lemma 8.7.)

Set $g_1 = \varphi - 1$. Then $\hat{g}_1 = 0$ on $T$ and $\operatorname{Re} \hat{g}_1 < 0$ on $\overline{U} \setminus T$ (since $|\hat{\varphi}| < 1$ there).

**Step 2: Embedding into $\mathbb{C}^n$.** Define $G : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{C}^n$ by

$$G(m) = (\hat{g}_1(m), \hat{g}_2(m), \ldots, \hat{g}_n(m)).$$

Then $G(\mathcal{M}) = \sigma(g_1, \ldots, g_n)$, the joint spectrum. We have:

- $G(T)$ is a compact subset of $\{z_1 = 0\}$,
- $G(T) \cap G(\mathcal{M} \setminus U) = \emptyset$,
- $G(\overline{U} \setminus T) \subset \{\operatorname{Re} z_1 < 0\}$.

**Step 3: Constructing a suitable covering.** Choose a neighborhood $\Omega$ of $G(T)$ in $\mathbb{C}^n$ with $\overline{\Omega} \cap G(\mathcal{M} \setminus U) = \emptyset$. There exists an open set $D_0$ in $\mathbb{C}^n$ such that

$$D_0 \cup \Omega \supset G(\mathcal{M}), \quad D_0 \cap \Omega \subset \{\operatorname{Re} z_1 < 0\}.$$

By the construction used in the proof of Theorem 8.2, there exist $C_1, \ldots, C_m \in A$ such that if $B$ is the closed subalgebra generated by $g_1, \ldots, g_n, C_1, \ldots, C_m$, then $\sigma_B(g_1, \ldots, g_n) \subset D_0 \cup \Omega$.

**Step 4: Projection to a polynomially convex set.** Let

$$\sigma = \sigma(g_1, \ldots, g_n, C_1, \ldots, C_m) \subset \mathbb{C}^{n+m},$$

and let $\hat{\sigma}$ be the polynomially convex hull of $\sigma$. Let $\pi$ be the natural projection $\mathbb{C}^{n+m} \to \mathbb{C}^n$. Since $\sigma \subset \sigma_B(g_1, \ldots, g_n, C_1, \ldots, C_m)$ and the latter is polynomially convex (because $g_1, \ldots, C_m$ generate $B$), we have

$$\hat{\sigma} \subset \sigma_B(g_1, \ldots, C_m),$$

so $\pi(\hat{\sigma}) \subset \sigma_B(g_1, \ldots, g_n) \subset D_0 \cup \Omega$. Thus

$$\hat{\sigma} \subset \pi^{-1}(D_0) \cup \pi^{-1}(\Omega),$$

with the intersection satisfying

$$\pi^{-1}(D_0) \cap \pi^{-1}(\Omega) \subset \{\operatorname{Re} z_1 < 0\}.$$

**Step 5: Applying the holomorphic separation lemmas.** Now $\hat{\sigma}$ is polynomially convex, and $\frac{\log z_1}{z_1}$ is holomorphic in $\pi^{-1}(D_0) \cap \pi^{-1}(\Omega)$. By Lemma 9.4 ( $\bar{\partial}$ solution on two open sets), there exist a neighborhood $W$ of $\hat{\sigma}$ and functions

$$h_1 \in H(\pi^{-1}(D_0) \cap W), \quad h_2 \in H(\pi^{-1}(\Omega) \cap W)$$

with $h_1 - h_2 = \frac{\log z_1}{z_1}$ on $\pi^{-1}(D_0) \cap \pi^{-1}(\Omega) \cap W$.

Setting $K = \sigma$, $U_1 = \pi^{-1}(D_0) \cap W$, $U_2 = \pi^{-1}(\Omega) \cap W$, we verify the hypotheses of Lemma 9.5:

1. $U_1 \cup U_2 \supset \hat{\sigma} \supset K = \sigma$,
2. $U_1 \cap U_2 \subset \{\operatorname{Re} z_1 < 0\}$,
3. $h_1, h_2$ exist as above,
4. By construction of $\Omega$ and the property $G(\overline{U} \setminus T) \subset \{\operatorname{Re} z_1 < 0\}$, we have $\sigma \cap \pi^{-1}(\Omega) \subset \{\operatorname{Re} z_1 \leq 0\}$, so $K \cap U_2 \subset \{\operatorname{Re} z_1 \leq 0\}$.

Lemma 9.5 yields $F$ holomorphic in a neighborhood of $\sigma$ with

$$F = 1 \text{ on } \{z_1 = 0\} \cap \pi^{-1}(\Omega) \cap \sigma, \quad |F| < 1 \text{ elsewhere on } \sigma.$$

**Step 6: Pulling back to the algebra.** By Theorem 8.2 (operational calculus), there exists $e \in A$ such that for all $M \in \mathcal{M}$,

$$\hat{e}(M) = F(\hat{g}_1(M), \ldots, \hat{g}_n(M), \hat{C}_1(M), \ldots, \hat{C}_m(M)).$$

For $M \in T$, the corresponding point in $\sigma$ lies in $\{z_1 = 0\} \cap \pi^{-1}(\Omega)$ (since $\hat{g}_1 = 0$ on $T$ and $G(T) \subset \Omega$), so $\hat{e}(M) = 1$.

For $M \in \mathcal{M} \setminus T$, the corresponding point is not in $\{z_1 = 0\} \cap \pi^{-1}(\Omega) \cap \sigma$, so $|\hat{e}(M)| < 1$.

Thus $e$ is the desired peak function extension. $\square$
