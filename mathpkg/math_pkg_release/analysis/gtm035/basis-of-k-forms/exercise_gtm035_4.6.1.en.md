# Exercise 4.6

Prove Lemma 4.6: Fix $k$. The set of elements

$$e_{i_1} \wedge e_{i_2} \wedge \cdots \wedge e_{i_k}, \quad 1 \leq i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_k \leq N,$$

forms a basis for $\wedge^k(V)$.

1. Show that these elements are linearly independent by evaluating a linear combination on $(v_{j_1}, \ldots, v_{j_k})$ where $\{v_j\}$ is the dual basis of $V$.
2. Show that they span $\wedge^k(V)$: every $\omega \in \wedge^k(V)$ can be recovered from its values $\omega(v_{i_1}, \ldots, v_{i_k})$ on the basis of $V$.

Conclude that $\dim \wedge^k(V) = \binom{N}{k}$.
