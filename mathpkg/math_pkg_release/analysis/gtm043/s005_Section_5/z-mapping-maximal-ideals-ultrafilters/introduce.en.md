The zero-set mapping $Z \colon C(X) \to Z(X)$ and its inverse image $Z^{\leftarrow}$ establish a fundamental correspondence between algebraic structures in the ring of continuous functions and topological structures on the space $X$. Theorem 2.5 shows that $Z$ is a bijection from the set of all maximal ideals in $C(X)$ onto the set of all $z$-ultrafilters on $X$, with $Z^{\leftarrow}[\mathcal{A}]$ being a maximal ideal whenever $\mathcal{A}$ is a $z$-ultrafilter. This correspondence follows directly from Theorem 2.3 and the fact that both $Z$ and $Z^{\leftarrow}$ preserve inclusion.
