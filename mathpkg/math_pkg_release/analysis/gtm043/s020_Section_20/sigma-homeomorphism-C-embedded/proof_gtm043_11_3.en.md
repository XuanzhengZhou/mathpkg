By definition of $\sigma$, $\pi_f \circ \sigma = f$, for each $f \in C(X)$. Therefore $\sigma$ is continuous, by 3.10(c). Next, the induced homomorphism $\sigma'$ (10.2) maps $C(P)$ onto $C(X)$; for,

$$\sigma' \pi_f = \pi_f \circ \sigma = f.$$

By Theorem 10.3(b), $\sigma$ is a homeomorphism, and $\sigma[X]$ is $C$-embedded.
