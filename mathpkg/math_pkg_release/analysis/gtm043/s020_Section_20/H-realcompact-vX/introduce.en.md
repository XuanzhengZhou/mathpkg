Theorem 11.8 completes the construction of the realcompactification $vX$ within the product $P$. Given any real maximal ideal $M$ in $C(H)$, the isomorphism $C(X) \cong C(H)$ from 11.4(a) pulls it back to a real maximal ideal $K$ in $C(X)$, which is the kernel of some homomorphism $p \in H$. This forces $M = M_p$ to be fixed, proving $H$ is realcompact. Notably, this proof does not rely on the axiom of choice, in contrast to the proof that $\beta X$ exists.
