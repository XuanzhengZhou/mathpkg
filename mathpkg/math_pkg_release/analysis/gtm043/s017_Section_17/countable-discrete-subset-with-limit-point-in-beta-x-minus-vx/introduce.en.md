Lemma 9.10 concerns a countable discrete subset $D$ of $\beta X$ that has a limit point $p$ lying in $\beta X \setminus vX$ (the complement of the Hewitt realcompactification). The conclusion is that $D$ contains a topological copy $N$ of the natural numbers such that the entire remainder $\beta N \setminus N$ is contained in $\beta X \setminus vX$. The proof constructs a continuous function $g$ mapping $D$ to $\{1/n\}$ and uses a nonnegative function $f$ vanishing at $p$ but nowhere on $vX$ to build a zero-set that isolates the desired copy via Lemma 9.4.
