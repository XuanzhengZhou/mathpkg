Example 9.15 constructs a counterexample to the conjecture that the product of two pseudocompact spaces is pseudocompact. The space $G$ is a countably compact subspace of $\beta N$, built inductively using a fixed-point-free involution $\tau$ on $\beta N$. The square $G \times G$ fails to be pseudocompact because the diagonal set $D = \{(p, \tau p) : p \in \beta N\} \cap (G \times G)$ is an infinite closed discrete subset of $G \times G$. This example stands in contrast to Theorem 9.14, which shows that the product of a compact space with a pseudocompact space is always pseudocompact.
