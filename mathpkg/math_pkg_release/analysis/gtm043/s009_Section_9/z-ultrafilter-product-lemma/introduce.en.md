This lemma is the crucial step in a \(z\)-filter proof of the Tychonoff product theorem. It states that if \(A\) is a \(z\)-ultrafilter on a product space \(X = \prod_\alpha X_\alpha\) and each projected \(z\)-filter \(\pi_\alpha^\# A\) is fixed (i.e., has nonempty intersection), then \(A\) itself is fixed. The proof exploits the primality of \(z\)-ultrafilters and the fact that basic zero-sets in the product are preimages of zero-sets under projections.
