Given a continuous map \(\tau : X \to Y\) and a \(z\)-filter \(\mathcal{F}\) on \(X\), the map \(\tau^\#\) produces a family of zero-sets on \(Y\): those zero-sets whose \(\tau\)-preimage belongs to \(\mathcal{F}\). Unlike the direct image, which may fail to preserve zero-sets, the preimage of a zero-set is always a zero-set, making \(\tau^\#\) a well-behaved construction for studying \(z\)-filters under continuous mappings.
