The Tychonoff product theorem states that any product of compact spaces is compact. This proof via \(z\)-filters is particularly elegant: since a product of completely regular spaces is completely regular, compactness is equivalent to every \(z\)-ultrafilter being fixed. By the product lemma, if each projection of a \(z\)-ultrafilter on the product is fixed (which holds because each factor is compact), then the ultrafilter itself is fixed, establishing compactness of the product.
