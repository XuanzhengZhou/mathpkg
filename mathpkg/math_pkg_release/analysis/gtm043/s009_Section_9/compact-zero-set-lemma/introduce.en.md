This lemma provides a \(z\)-filter characterization of compactness for zero-sets: a zero-set \(Z\) is compact exactly when it cannot belong to any free \(z\)-filter. The forward direction is immediate (compact sets have nonempty intersection for any family of closed subsets with the finite intersection property). The converse uses the fact that any family of closed subsets of \(Z\) with the finite intersection property generates a \(z\)-filter.
