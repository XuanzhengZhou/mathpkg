The residue class notation \(I(a)\) identifies an ideal \(I\) with the canonical quotient homomorphism \(A \to A/I\). This functional viewpoint treats the ideal as a map, so that \(I(a)\) is the coset \(a + I\) in the quotient ring \(A/I\). It is a convenient convention used throughout the study of rings of continuous functions, particularly when analyzing maximal ideals and their quotient fields.
