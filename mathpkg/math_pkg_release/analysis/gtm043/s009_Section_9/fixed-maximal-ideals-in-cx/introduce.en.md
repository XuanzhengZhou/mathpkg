Every point \(p\) of a completely regular space \(X\) determines a fixed maximal ideal \(M_p = \{f \in C(X) : f(p) = 0\}\) in the ring \(C(X)\) of continuous real-valued functions. The quotient \(C(X)/M_p\) is canonically isomorphic to \(\mathbf{R}\) via evaluation at \(p\). Distinct points yield distinct ideals, and every fixed maximal ideal arises uniquely in this way.
