For the ring \(C^*(X)\) of bounded continuous real-valued functions on a completely regular space \(X\), the fixed maximal ideals are exactly the sets \(M^*_p = \{f \in C^*(X) : f(p) = 0\}\). As in the unbounded case, the quotient \(C^*(X)/M^*_p\) is canonically isomorphic to \(\mathbf{R}\) via evaluation at \(p\), and distinct points yield distinct ideals.
