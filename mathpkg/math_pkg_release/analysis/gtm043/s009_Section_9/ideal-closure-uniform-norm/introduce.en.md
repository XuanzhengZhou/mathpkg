The uniform norm topology on \(C(X)\) (or \(C^*(X)\)) gives a notion of closed ideals. For any ideal \(I\), its closure \(\overline{I}\) in this topology is precisely the intersection of all maximal ideals containing \(I\), and also equals the set of functions whose zero-sets contain \(\bigcap Z[I]\). Consequently, an ideal is uniformly closed if and only if it is an intersection of maximal ideals, linking the analytic notion of uniform limits with the algebraic notion of maximal ideals.
