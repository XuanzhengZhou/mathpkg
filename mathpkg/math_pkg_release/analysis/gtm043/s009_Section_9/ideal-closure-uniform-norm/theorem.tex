For an ideal \(I\) in \(C(X)\), define

\[
\overline{I} = \bigcap \{ M_p : M_p \supset I \}.
\]

Then \(\overline{I}\) is a closed ideal in the uniform norm topology on \(C(X)\), and it consists of all \(g \in C(X)\) for which

\[
Z(g) \supset \bigcap Z[I].
\]

Furthermore, \(\bigcap Z[\overline{I}] = \bigcap Z[I]\).

Moreover, \(\overline{I}\) is the closure of \(I\) in the uniform norm topology. Hence an ideal is closed in the uniform norm topology if and only if it is an intersection of maximal ideals.
