The set $\mathbf{R}^X$ denotes the collection of all functions from a space $X$ into the real numbers $\mathbf{R}$. It serves as the foundational ambient algebraic structure from which the ring of continuous functions $C(X)$ is later derived as a subring. The pointwise operations of addition and multiplication are first defined on $\mathbf{R}^X$ since their definitions do not involve continuity, making it the natural starting point for studying function rings on topological spaces.
