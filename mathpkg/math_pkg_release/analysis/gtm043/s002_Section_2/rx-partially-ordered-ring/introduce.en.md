The pointwise partial ordering on $\mathbf{R}^X$ is compatible with the ring structure in two essential ways: translation invariance and preservation of nonnegativity under multiplication. These properties together make $\mathbf{R}^X$ a partially ordered ring in the sense of definition (0.19). This compatibility between algebraic and order structures is central to the study of rings of continuous functions.
