The partial ordering on $\mathbf{R}^X$ is the pointwise extension of the total order on $\mathbf{R}$. A function $f$ is said to be greater than or equal to $g$ if at every point $x$ in the domain, the value $f(x)$ is at least $g(x)$. This is a partial order (not total when $X$ has at least two points) and is invariant under translation, making $\mathbf{R}^X$ a partially ordered ring.
