The ring $C(X)$ consists of all continuous real-valued functions on a topological space $X$. It is the central object of study in Gillman and Jerison's book. By restricting to continuous functions, the algebraic structure of $\mathbf{R}^X$ is enriched with topological significance, and the interplay between the topology of $X$ and the algebraic properties of $C(X)$ becomes the main theme of investigation.
