The ring $C^*(X)$ is the subring of $C(X)$ consisting of all bounded continuous functions on $X$. While any continuous function on a compact space is automatically bounded, this is not true for general topological spaces. The relationship between $C(X)$ and $C^*(X)$, and in particular the condition under which they coincide, is central to the study of pseudocompactness and related topological properties.
