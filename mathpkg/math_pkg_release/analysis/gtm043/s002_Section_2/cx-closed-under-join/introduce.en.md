The ring $C(X)$ is not only a subring of $\mathbf{R}^X$, but also a sublattice. The key observation is that the absolute value of a continuous function is continuous, and then the join (and dually the meet) can be expressed algebraically in terms of addition, subtraction, and absolute value. This makes $C(X)$ a lattice-ordered ring in its own right, inheriting the order structure from $\mathbf{R}^X$.
