If $f, g \in C(X)$ are continuous, then $f+g$ and $fg$ are continuous as the pointwise sum and product of continuous real-valued functions into $\mathbf{R}$ (these are elementary results in topology). The function $-f$ is continuous since it is the composition of $f$ with the continuous map $x \mapsto -x$ on $\mathbf{R}$. The constant function $1$ is trivially continuous. Since $C(X) \subseteq \mathbf{R}^X$ and the operations agree, $C(X)$ is a subring. The constant function $1$ serves as the unity element in $C(X)$.
