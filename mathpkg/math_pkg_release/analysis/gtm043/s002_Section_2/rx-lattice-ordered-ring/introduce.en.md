The lattice structure of $\mathbf{R}^X$ follows from the fact that both the join and meet of any two functions exist as pointwise operations. This makes $\mathbf{R}^X$ a lattice-ordered ring (also called an $l$-ring), a structure that is richer than a mere partially ordered ring. The absolute value function $|f| = f \vee (-f)$ is then available as a natural lattice-theoretic construction, yielding the pointwise absolute value $|f(x)|$ at each point.
