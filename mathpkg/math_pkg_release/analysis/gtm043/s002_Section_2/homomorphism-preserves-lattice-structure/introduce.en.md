This fundamental theorem shows that the lattice structure of rings of continuous functions is algebraically determined: any ring homomorphism between such function rings automatically preserves order and the lattice operations (join and meet). The key insight is that nonnegativity is algebraically characterized by being a square ($f = k^2$), so ring homomorphisms necessarily send nonnegative functions to nonnegative functions. This result underlies much of the algebraic theory of $C(X)$.
