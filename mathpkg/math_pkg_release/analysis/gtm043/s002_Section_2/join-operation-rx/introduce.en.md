The join operation $f \vee g$ provides the least upper bound (supremum) of two functions in $\mathbf{R}^X$ with respect to the pointwise partial order. Since $\mathbf{R}$ is totally ordered, $f(x) \vee g(x)$ at each point is simply the maximum of the two real values. The function thus defined pointwise is indeed the supremum in the partially ordered set $\mathbf{R}^X$, satisfying the universal property of least upper bounds.
