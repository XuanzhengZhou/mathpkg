This lemma gives a sufficient condition for a $z$-ideal containing $O^p$ to be prime: that for every $f$, the ideal generated by the images of $f$ and $|f|$ in the quotient ring $C/I$ is principal. The proof uses the previous separation lemma to show that $C/I$ is totally ordered, from which primality follows for $z$-ideals. This result is a key stepping stone toward the characterization of $F$-spaces.
