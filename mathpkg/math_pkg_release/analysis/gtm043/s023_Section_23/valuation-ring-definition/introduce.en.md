A valuation ring is a commutative ring with unity whose principal ideals are totally ordered by inclusion. Equivalently, for any two elements $a$ and $b$, either $a$ divides $b$ or $b$ divides $a$. In the context of rings of continuous functions, the quotient $C/I$ of Theorem 14.24 satisfies an even stronger condition: the smaller of two positive elements is always a multiple of the larger.
