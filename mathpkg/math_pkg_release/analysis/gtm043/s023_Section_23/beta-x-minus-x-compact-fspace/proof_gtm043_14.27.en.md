Since $X$ is locally compact, it is open in $\beta X$ (6.9(d)); hence $\beta X - X$ is compact, and so it is compact.

To prove that $\beta X - X$ is an $F$-space, we verify condition (5) of Theorem 14.25. Let $f \in C(\beta X - X)$. We must show that $(f, |f|)$ is principal. Extend $f$ to a function $g \in C^*(\beta X)$ and consider $M^p(g)$ for $p \in \beta X$. The function $g$ is a unit of $C(X)$, so that $M^q(|g|) > 0$ for all $q \in \beta X$. When $M^p(f) = 0$, the set

$$V = \{q \in \beta X : M^q(|g| - |f|) > 0\}$$

contains $p$. But $V$ is an open set. And for all $q \in V - X$, we have, using Theorem 7.6,

$$0 = |g^\beta(q)| \geq |f^\beta(q)|,$$

i.e., $f^\beta(q) = 0$. Hence $p$ is an interior point of $Z(f^\beta)$. From this one deduces that the ideal generated by $f$ and $|f|$ is principal.

As an example, $\beta \mathbb{R}^+ - \mathbb{R}^+$, where $\mathbb{R}^+$ denotes the space of non-negative reals, is a compact, connected $F$-space (6.10), of cardinal $2^{\mathfrak{c}}$ (9.3).
