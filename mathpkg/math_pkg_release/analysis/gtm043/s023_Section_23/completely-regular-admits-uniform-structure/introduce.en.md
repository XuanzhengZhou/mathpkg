Theorem 15.6 establishes that complete regularity is exactly the condition for a Hausdorff space to admit a uniform structure. Furthermore, the canonical structures $\mathcal{C}(X)$ and $\mathcal{C}^*(X)$ are always admissible for completely regular spaces. This theorem bridges the gap between topological separation axioms and uniform space theory.
