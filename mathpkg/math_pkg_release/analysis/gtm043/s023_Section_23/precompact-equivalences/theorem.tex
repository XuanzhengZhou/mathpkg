The following are equivalent for any Hausdorff uniform space $[X; \mathcal{D}]$:

(1) $\gamma X$ is compact (in which case $X$ and $\mathcal{D}$ are said to be \textit{precompact}).

(2) For each $d \in \mathcal{D}$, every $d$-discrete set in $X$ is finite.

(3) For every $d \in \mathcal{D}$ and $\epsilon > 0$, $X$ is a finite union of zero-sets of $d$-diameter $\leq \epsilon$ (in which case $[X; \mathcal{D}]$ is said to be \textit{totally bounded}).

(4) Every $z$-ultrafilter on $X$ is a Cauchy $z$-filter, i.e., $\beta X = \gamma X$.
