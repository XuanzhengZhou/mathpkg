This result clarifies the relationship between $F$-spaces and $P$-spaces. While $F$-spaces are characterized by every ideal $O^p$ being prime, $P$-spaces represent the extreme case where every such ideal is maximal (equivalently, every prime ideal is maximal). An interesting connection is that the Stone-Cech compactification of an infinite $P$-space is an $F$-space but not a $P$-space.
