Theorem 14.25 provides ten equivalent characterizations of $F$-spaces, linking algebraic properties of the ring $C(X)$ (such as every finitely generated ideal being principal) with topological properties of $X$ (such as complete separation of positive and negative parts of functions). The equivalence shows that $F$-spaces are precisely those for which the ideals $O^p$ are prime, and that the quotient rings satisfy a strong valuation-like property where the smaller positive element is a multiple of the larger.
