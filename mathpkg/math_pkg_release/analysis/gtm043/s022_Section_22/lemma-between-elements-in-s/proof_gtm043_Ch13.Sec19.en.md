Let $\tau$ denote the smallest index for which $x_\tau < y_\tau$; then $x_\tau = 0$ and $y_\tau = 1$. Define $y'$ as follows: $y'_\xi = y_\xi$ for $\xi \leq \tau$, and $y'_\xi = 0$ for $\xi > \tau$. Then $y' \in Q$ and satisfies $x < y' < y$ in case (a). For case (b), a similar construction with an appropriate truncation yields the required $u \in Q_\sigma$.
