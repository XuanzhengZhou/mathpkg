Given $0 \leq I(a) \leq I(b)$, where $I(b) \in J/I$ (i.e., $b \in J$), there exist $u \geq 0$ and $v \geq 0$ in $A$, such that $a \equiv u \pmod{I}$ and $b - u \equiv b - a \equiv v \pmod{I}$. Then $b - (u + v) \in I \subset J$, and so $u + v \in J$. But also $0 \leq u \leq u + v$. Since $J$ is convex, $u \in J$, whence $I(a) = I(u) \in J/I$. Therefore $J/I$ is convex.
