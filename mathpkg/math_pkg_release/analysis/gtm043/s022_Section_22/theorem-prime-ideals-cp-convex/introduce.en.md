Theorem 14.3(a) states that every prime ideal in the totally ordered ring $C/P$ is convex. This follows from the more general Proposition 14.3(b) about convex ideals in quotients. Since every ideal in a ring is symmetric and convex ideals in totally ordered rings are symmetric intervals, this implies that of any two symmetric intervals, one contains the other -- yielding part (c) that the prime ideals form a chain.
