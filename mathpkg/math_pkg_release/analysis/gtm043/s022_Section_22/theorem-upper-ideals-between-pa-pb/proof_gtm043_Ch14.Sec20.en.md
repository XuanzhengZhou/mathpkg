Work first with the open interval between $P^a$ and $P_b$. The set of upper ideals in this interval is an $\eta_1$-set (proved in 14.19); under the continuum hypothesis, its cardinal is $\aleph_1$. The set $Q$ of 13.18 is also an $\eta_1$-set of cardinal $\aleph_1$ (Theorem 13.20). By Theorem 13.9, these two sets are similar.

Next, by Theorem 14.4, every prime ideal in the interval is a union of upper ideals. Consequently, given any two nonlower ideals, there is an upper ideal lying strictly between them. As in 13.23, it follows that the set of all nonlower ideals in this interval is the Dedekind completion of the set of upper ideals, and therefore is similar to the Dedekind completion $R$ of $Q$.

Next, the set of lower ideals maps naturally onto the set of lower elements of $s$ (see 13.16). Finally, $P^a$ corresponds to the constant sequence $0$, and $P_b$ to $1$.
