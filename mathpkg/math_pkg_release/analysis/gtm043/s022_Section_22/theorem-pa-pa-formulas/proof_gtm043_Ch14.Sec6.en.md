If $b \in P^a$, then $b^n = sa$ for some $s \in C/P$ and $n \in \mathbf{N}$; then $b^{2n} = (s^2a)a < a$ by 14.5(a). Conversely, $|b| < a^{1/n}$ implies $b \in P^a$, since $P^a$ is prime and convex.

Next, if $b \in P_a$, then $|b| < a^n$ for all $n$, since $P_a$ is convex and contains no power of $a$. Finally, if $|b| < a^n$ for every $n$, then $a \notin P^{|b|}$, whence $b \in P^{|b|} \subset P_a$.
