Theorem 14.6 provides explicit order-theoretic descriptions of the successive prime ideals $P^a$ and $P_a$. The upper ideal $P^a$ is the union over all $n$ of elements whose absolute value is bounded by $a^{1/n}$ -- capturing the idea of being "infinitesimal relative to $a^{-1}$." The lower ideal $P_a$ is the intersection over all $n$ of elements whose absolute value is bounded by $a^n$ -- capturing the idea of being "bounded by all powers of $a$." These formulas make the prime ideal structure computationally accessible.
