For each positive element $a > 0$ in $C/P$, define
$$P_a = \text{the largest prime ideal not containing } a,$$
and let $P^a$ be the immediate successor of $P_a$ in the chain of all prime ideals in $C/P$.

Thus $a \in P^a - P_a$, $P_a$ is a lower ideal, and $P^a$ is an upper ideal.

Conversely, if $I$ and $J$ are successive prime ideals with $I \subset J$, then for each positive $a \in J - I$, we have $I = P_a$ and $J = P^a$.
