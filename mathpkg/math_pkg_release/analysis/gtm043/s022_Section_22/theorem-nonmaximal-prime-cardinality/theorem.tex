Whenever $O^p \neq M^p$, there exist infinitely many upper ideals containing any given nonmaximal prime ideal $P$. Thus $M^p$ contains prime ideals that are not $z$-ideals. In the extreme case of $M_\sigma$, there are $c$ upper ideals and $2^{\aleph_1}$ prime ideals, none of which (except $M_\sigma$ and $O_\sigma$) is a $z$-ideal.
