Trivially, $\mathbf{R}^+$ is $C^*$-embedded in $\mathbf{R}$, so that $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ = \beta \mathbf{R}^+$. Since $\mathbf{R}^+$ is locally compact, it is open in $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+$, whence $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ is compact. Since $\mathbf{R}^+$ is homeomorphic with $\mathbf{R}^-$, $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+$ is homeomorphic with $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^-$, and $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ with $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^- - \mathbf{R}^-$. Every neighborhood of a point of $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ meets $\mathbf{R}^+$ in an unbounded set.
