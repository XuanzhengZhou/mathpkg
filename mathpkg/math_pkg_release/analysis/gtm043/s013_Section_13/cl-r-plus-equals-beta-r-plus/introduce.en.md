Since $\mathbf{R}^+$ (the positive reals) is $C^*$-embedded in $\mathbf{R}$, its closure in $\beta \mathbf{R}$ is its Stone-Čech compactification $\beta \mathbf{R}^+$. Because $\mathbf{R}^+$ is locally compact, it is open in its Stone-Čech compactification, so the remainder $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ is compact. The same holds for $\mathbf{R}^-$ (the negative reals), and the two remainders are homeomorphic.
