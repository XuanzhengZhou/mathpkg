We show that $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ is connected. If not, it admits a continuous function that assumes precisely the values $0$ and $1$. This has an extension to a function $f \in C(\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+)$ (since $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$ is compact), and $f$ must assume values near $0$, and values near $1$, at arbitrarily large $x \in \mathbf{R}^+$. Since $\mathbf{R}^+$ is connected, $f$ must assume the value $1/2$ on an unbounded set in $\mathbf{R}^+$, and hence also at some point of $\operatorname{cl} \mathbf{R}^+ - \mathbf{R}^+$.
