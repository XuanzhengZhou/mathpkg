Let $f$ be a function in $C^*(\mathbf{N})$ that assumes all rational values in $[0, 1]$. Then every real number in $[0, 1]$ belongs to the closure of $f[\mathbf{N}]$. Consequently, the compact set $f^\beta[\beta \mathbf{N}]$ is all of $[0, 1]$. In particular, the cardinal of $\beta \mathbf{N}$ must be at least the cardinal $\mathfrak{c}$ of the continuum. (As a matter of fact, its cardinal is $2^{\mathfrak{c}}$ (9.3 or 9O).)
