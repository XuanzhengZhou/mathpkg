For any continuous function $f \in C(X)$ (possibly unbounded), one can regard $f$ as a continuous mapping from $X$ into the one-point compactification $\mathbf{R}^* = \mathbf{R} \cup \{\infty\}$. Its Stone extension is then a continuous map $f^*: \beta X \to \mathbf{R}^*$. Unlike the Stone extension $f^\beta$ for bounded functions (which maps into $\mathbf{R}$), $f^*$ may take the value $\infty$ at points of $\beta X - X$. This device allows the treatment of unbounded functions within the compactification framework.
