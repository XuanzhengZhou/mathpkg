If $S$ is simultaneously open and closed in $X$, then the closures of $S$ and its complement $X - S$ in $\beta X$ are disjoint open sets whose union is all of $\beta X$. Consequently, the closure of an open-and-closed set in $\beta X$ is itself open-and-closed. This follows directly from Čech's condition (III) since $S$ and $X - S$ are disjoint zero-sets. An important application: the closure in $\beta N$ of every subset is open.
