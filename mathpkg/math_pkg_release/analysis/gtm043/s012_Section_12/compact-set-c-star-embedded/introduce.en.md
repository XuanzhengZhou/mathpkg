Every compact subset of a completely regular space $X$ is $C^*$-embedded in $X$. This follows from the Compactification Theorem: if $S$ is compact, then $\operatorname{cl}_{\beta X} S = S = \beta S$, whence $S$ is $C^*$-embedded in $\beta X$ and therefore in $X$. This generalizes the familiar fact that a compact Hausdorff space is $C^*$-embedded in any containing space.
