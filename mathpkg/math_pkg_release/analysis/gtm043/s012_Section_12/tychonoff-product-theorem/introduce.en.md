The Tychonoff Product Theorem states that any product of compact spaces is compact. Gillman and Jerison present an elegant proof using Stone's theorem: let $X = \prod_\alpha X_\alpha$ where each $X_\alpha$ is compact. Each projection $\pi_\alpha : X \to X_\alpha$ has a Stone extension $\bar{\pi}_\alpha : \beta X \to X_\alpha$, and the mapping $p \mapsto (\bar{\pi}_\alpha(p))$ from $\beta X$ onto $X$ is continuous, forcing $\beta X = X$, so $X$ is compact. This proof illustrates the power of the Stone-Čech compactification as a unifying tool.
