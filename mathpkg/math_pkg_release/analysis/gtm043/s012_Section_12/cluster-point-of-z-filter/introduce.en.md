A cluster point of a $z$-filter $\mathcal{F}$ on a dense subspace $X$ of $T$ is a point $p \in T$ whose every neighborhood meets every member of $\mathcal{F}$. This generalizes the usual notion of a cluster point of a filter to the setting of $z$-filters on completely regular spaces. The definition is fundamental for the construction of the Stone-Čech compactification, where points of $\beta X$ are characterized as limits (hence cluster points) of $z$-ultrafilters on $X$.
