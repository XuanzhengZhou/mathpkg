In the Stone-Čech compactification, isolated points of the original space $X$ remain isolated in $\beta X$. Moreover, $X$ is an open subset of $\beta X$ precisely when $X$ is locally compact. This characterizes the relationship between the topological properties of $X$ and its position within $\beta X$: for locally compact spaces (like $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{N}$), $X$ sits as an open dense subset of $\beta X$; for non-locally-compact spaces (like $\mathbb{Q}$), $X$ is not open in $\beta X$.
