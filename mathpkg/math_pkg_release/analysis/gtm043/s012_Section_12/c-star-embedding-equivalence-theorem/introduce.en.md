Theorem 6.4 provides five equivalent conditions characterizing when a dense subspace $X$ of $T$ is $C^*$-embedded. These equivalences link analytic properties (continuous extensions of bounded functions), topological properties (disjointness of closures of zero-sets), and algebraic properties (behavior of $z$-ultrafilters). The theorem is a critical stepping stone to the Compactification Theorem (6.5), showing that conditions (I)--(V) characterizing $\beta X$ are equivalent to one another and that any compactification satisfying one of them is essentially $\beta X$.
