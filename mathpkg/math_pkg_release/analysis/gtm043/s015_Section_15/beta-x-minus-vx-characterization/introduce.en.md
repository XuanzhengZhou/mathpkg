This theorem explains the relationship between the two function rings $C(X)$ and $C^*(X)$ and the spaces $\beta X$ and $vX$. Points in $\beta X \setminus vX$ are precisely those where some bounded continuous function without zeros (a unit of $C$) has Stone extension vanishing --- so it fails to be a unit of $C^*$. This explains why $C$ is more sensitive than $C^*$: for $p \in \beta X \setminus X$, the ring $C^*$ never distinguishes $X$ from $X \cup \{p\}$, while $C$ does precisely when $p \notin vX$.
