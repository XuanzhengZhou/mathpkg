The equivalence of conditions (I)-(V) follows from Theorem 8.6, taking $T = vX$. Specifically, by definition $X \subset vX \subset \beta X$, and by the definition of $vX$ (8.4), each $p \in vX$ corresponds to a real $z$-ultrafilter, so conditions (5) and (6) of Theorem 8.6 are satisfied; hence all seven conditions of that theorem hold for $T = vX$. Conditions (I)-(V) above correspond to conditions (1)-(5) of Theorem 8.6.

Only uniqueness remains to be proved. As in the case of $\beta X$, it follows from (I) and 0.12(a): if $T$ is any realcompactification of $X$ satisfying (I), then the identity map $X \to X$ extends to maps $vX \to T$ and $T \to vX$ which are mutually inverse homeomorphisms fixing $X$ pointwise.
