This theorem provides a closure property for the class of realcompact spaces: the intersection of any family of realcompact subspaces of a given ambient space is again realcompact. The proof uses the universal extension property of $vX$ (Theorem 8.7): for each realcompact subspace $Y_\alpha$, the identity map from the intersection $X$ into $Y_\alpha$ extends continuously from $vX$ to $Y_\alpha$, and by uniqueness these extensions coincide, showing $vX \subset X$, hence $X$ is realcompact. This is the realcompact analogue of the fact that closed subsets of compact spaces are compact.
