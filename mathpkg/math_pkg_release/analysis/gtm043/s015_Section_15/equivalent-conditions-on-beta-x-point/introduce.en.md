This theorem characterizes exactly which points of the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta X$ belong to the Hewitt realcompactification $vX$. A point $p \in \beta X$ lies in $vX$ precisely when its associated maximal ideal $M^p$ is real (equivalently, the $z$-ultrafilter $A^p$ has the countable intersection property). In terms of the Stone extension $f^*$, this means that $f^*$ never takes the value $\infty$ at $p$. The set of all such points is denoted $vX$.
