Theorem 3.10(a) states that the class of completely regular spaces is closed under arbitrary products. This is a fundamental closure property that, together with the fact that every completely regular space is a subspace of a compact space (a product of closed intervals), characterizes completely regular spaces as precisely the subspaces of compact Hausdorff spaces. The proof uses the description of the product topology as a weak topology and the fact that preimages of zero-sets under projections are zero-sets.
