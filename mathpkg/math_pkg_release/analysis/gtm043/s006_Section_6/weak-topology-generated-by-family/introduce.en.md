The weak topology generated by a family $C'$ of real-valued functions on a set $X$ is the coarsest topology on $X$ making every function in $C'$ continuous. It is defined by taking as a subbase the preimages of all open sets in $\mathbf{R}$ under functions in $C'$. This construction is the prototype for initial topologies and is central to the study of completely regular spaces, whose topology coincides with the weak topology induced by $C(X)$.
