Let $A$ and $A'$ be disjoint compact sets in a completely regular space $X$. For each $x \in A$, choose disjoint zero-sets $Z_x$ and $Z'_x$, with $Z_x$ a neighborhood of $x$, and $Z'_x \supset A'$. The cover $\{Z_x\}_{x \in A}$ of the compact set $A$ has a finite subcover, say
$$\{Z_{x_1}, \dots, Z_{x_n}\}.$$
Then $A$ and $A'$ are contained in the disjoint zero-sets
$$Z_{x_1} \cup \cdots \cup Z_{x_n} \quad \text{and} \quad Z'_{x_1} \cap \cdots \cap Z'_{x_n},$$
respectively.
