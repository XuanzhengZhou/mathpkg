The product topology is the prototypical example of a weak topology determined by a family of mappings: it is the coarsest topology on the Cartesian product making all coordinate projections continuous. Recognizing the product topology as a weak topology allows results about weak topologies (such as criteria for continuity of maps into the product, and the inheritance of complete regularity) to be applied directly to product spaces.
