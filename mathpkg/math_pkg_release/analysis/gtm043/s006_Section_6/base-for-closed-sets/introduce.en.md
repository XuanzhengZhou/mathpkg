A base for the closed sets is the dual notion of a base for open sets: a family of closed sets such that every closed set can be expressed as an intersection of members of the family. Equivalently, the family separates closed sets from points not belonging to them. In the theory of rings of continuous functions, working with bases of closed sets (particularly zero-sets) is often more natural than working with open sets.
