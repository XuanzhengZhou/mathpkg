Since the closed rays $[r, \infty)$ and $(-\infty, r]$ form a subbase for the closed sets in $\mathbf{R}$, their preimages under functions in the generating family $C'$ form a subbase for the closed sets in the weak topology. When the family $C'$ is closed under negation (as with $C(X)$ or $C^*(X)$), every such set is a zero-set, providing a direct link between the weak topology and the zero-set structure.
