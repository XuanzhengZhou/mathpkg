Theorem 3.10(c) is the familiar fact that continuity into a product space is equivalent to continuity in each coordinate separately. This is the special case of Theorem 3.10(b) where the determining family $\Phi$ consists of the coordinate projections. It is one of the most frequently used properties of the product topology.
