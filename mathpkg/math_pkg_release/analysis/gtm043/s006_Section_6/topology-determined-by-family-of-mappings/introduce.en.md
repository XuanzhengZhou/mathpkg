The notion of a weak topology is generalized to families of mappings into possibly different spaces. Given a family $\Phi = \{\varphi_{\alpha}: X \to Y_{\alpha}\}$, the weak topology is the coarsest topology on $X$ making every $\varphi_{\alpha}$ continuous. When this coincides with the existing topology on $X$, the topology is said to be determined by $\Phi$. This concept encompasses product topologies (where $\Phi$ is the family of projections) and is fundamental for constructing new completely regular spaces.
