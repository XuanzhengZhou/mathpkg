Theorem 3.2 provides the fundamental characterization of completely regular spaces in terms of their zero-sets: a Hausdorff space is completely regular precisely when the collection $Z(X)$ of all zero-sets forms a base for the closed sets. This result bridges the definition of complete regularity (which uses continuous functions) with a purely set-theoretic condition on zero-sets, and it underpins the entire approach of the book, where topological arguments are systematically cast in terms of zero-sets rather than open sets.
