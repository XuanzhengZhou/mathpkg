The Artin-Schreier theorem is a cornerstone of real algebra: every ordered field $E$ possesses a real-closed algebraic extension $\mathcal{R}E$ (its real-closure), unique up to $E$-isomorphism. For infinite $E$, $|\mathcal{R}E| = |E|$. The real-closure of $\mathbb{Q}$ is the field of real algebraic numbers.
