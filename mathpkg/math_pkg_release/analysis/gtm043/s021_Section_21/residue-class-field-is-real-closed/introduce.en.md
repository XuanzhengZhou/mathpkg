This fundamental theorem establishes that every residue class field of $C(X)$ is real-closed, regardless of $X$ or $M$. The proof verifies two conditions: every positive element has a square root (via exponentiation by $1/2$), and every odd-degree polynomial has a root (via continuity of root functions and primality of $M$). This is crucial for the order-theoretic analysis of hyper-real fields.
