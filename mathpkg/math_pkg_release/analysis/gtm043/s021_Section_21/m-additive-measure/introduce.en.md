An $\mathfrak{m}$-additive measure generalizes countable additivity to families of cardinality $\mathfrak{m}$. The equivalence between the two formulations (using null sets vs. full-measure sets) follows from a standard disjointification argument via well-ordering. This property is central to proving closure properties of nonmeasurable cardinals.
