An ordered field $F$ is \emph{real-closed} if it satisfies the equivalent conditions:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] Every positive element is a square, and every polynomial over $F$ of odd degree has a zero in $F$;
\item[(2)] $K = F(\sqrt{-1})$ is algebraically closed;
\item[(3)] $F$ has no proper algebraic extension to an ordered field.
\end{enumerate}
Every polynomial over a real-closed field of degree $>2$ is reducible. A real-closed field has a unique ordering: an element is nonnegative iff it is a square. Hence every isomorphism of a real-closed field to an ordered field is order-preserving.
