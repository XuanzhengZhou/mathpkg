An element $x \in F$ is algebraic over a subfield $E$ if there exists a nonzero polynomial $p(t) \in E[t]$ with $p(x) = 0$. Otherwise $x$ is transcendental. This fundamental distinction underlies the study of transcendence degree and real-closed fields.
