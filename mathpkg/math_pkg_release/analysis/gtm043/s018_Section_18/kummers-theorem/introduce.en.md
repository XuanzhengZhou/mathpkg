Theorem 10.3 characterizes when the homomorphism $\tau' : C(Y) \to C(X)$ induced by a continuous map $\tau: X \to Y$ is injective or surjective. Part (a) states that $\tau'$ is an isomorphism into its image precisely when the image $\tau[X]$ is dense in $Y$ — a condition equivalent to the statement that a function vanishing on $\tau[X]$ must be identically zero. Part (b) says $\tau'$ is surjective exactly when $\tau$ is a homeomorphism and its image is $C$-embedded in $Y$, meaning every continuous function on the image extends to all of $Y$.
