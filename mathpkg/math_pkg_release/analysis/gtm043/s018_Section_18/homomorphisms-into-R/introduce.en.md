When studying homomorphisms from a function ring into the reals, the special property that the only nonzero homomorphism from $\mathbf{R}$ into itself is the identity plays a crucial role. For any nonzero homomorphism $u: C(Y) \to \mathbf{R}$, we must have $u(1) = 1$, which forces $u(r) = r$ for all real constants $r$. The kernel of such a homomorphism onto $\mathbf{R}$ is a real maximal ideal, and distinct homomorphisms onto $\mathbf{R}$ have distinct kernels. For a point $y \in Y$, the fixed maximal ideal $M_y$ is precisely the kernel of the evaluation homomorphism $g \mapsto g(y)$.
