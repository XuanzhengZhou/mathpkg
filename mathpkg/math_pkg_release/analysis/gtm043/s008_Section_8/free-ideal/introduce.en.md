A free ideal in $C(X)$ or $C^*(X)$ is an ideal whose member functions have no common zero — the intersection of all their zero-sets is empty. Equivalently, for each point $x \in X$ one can find a function in the ideal that does not vanish at $x$. Free ideals are the more subtle and interesting case in the theory of rings of continuous functions: while fixed maximal ideals correspond simply to points of $X$, the characterization of free maximal ideals requires the full machinery of the theory and constitutes what the authors call its *sine qua non*.
