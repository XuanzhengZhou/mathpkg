Since every element of the principal ideal $(f)$ is of the form $fg$ for some $g \in C(X)$ (or $C^*(X)$), we have $Z(fg) \supseteq Z(f)$ for each $g$. Consequently,
$$Z(f) \subseteq \bigcap_{g} Z(fg) = \bigcap Z[(f)].$$
On the other hand, $f$ itself belongs to $(f)$, so $\bigcap Z[(f)] \subseteq Z(f)$. Hence $\bigcap Z[(f)] = Z(f)$. If $Z(f) \neq \emptyset$, then $\bigcap Z[(f)]$ is nonempty, and $(f)$ is fixed by definition.
