The sets $\operatorname{pos} f = \{x : f(x) > 0\}$ and $\operatorname{neg} f = \{x : f(x) < 0\}$ are open subsets of $X$ that can be expressed as cozero-sets. This follows from the identities using lattice operations in $C(X)$: $\{x : f(x) \geq 0\} = Z(f \wedge 0)$ and $\{x : f(x) \leq 0\} = Z(f \vee 0)$. Since cozero-sets are complements of zero-sets, the sets $\operatorname{pos} f$ and $\operatorname{neg} f$ themselves are cozero-sets.
