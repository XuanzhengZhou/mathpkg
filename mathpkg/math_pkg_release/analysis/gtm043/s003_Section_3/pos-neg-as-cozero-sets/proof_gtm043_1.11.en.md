The set $\{x: f(x) \geq 0\}$ is a zero-set because
$$\{x: f(x) \geq 0\} = Z(f \wedge 0) = Z(f - |f|).$$
Similarly,
$$\{x: f(x) \leq 0\} = Z(f \vee 0) = Z(f + |f|).$$
Thus, $\operatorname{pos} f = X - \{x: f(x) \leq 0\}$ and $\operatorname{neg} f = X - \{x: f(x) \geq 0\}$ are complements of zero-sets, hence cozero-sets.
