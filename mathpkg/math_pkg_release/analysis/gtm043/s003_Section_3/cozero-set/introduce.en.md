A cozero-set is the complement of a zero-set in a topological space $X$. These open sets arise naturally as the sets where a continuous function does not vanish. The sets $\operatorname{pos} f = \{x : f(x) > 0\}$ and $\operatorname{neg} f = \{x : f(x) < 0\}$ are always cozero-sets, as they can be expressed as complements of zero-sets using the lattice operations (join and meet) of $C(X)$.
