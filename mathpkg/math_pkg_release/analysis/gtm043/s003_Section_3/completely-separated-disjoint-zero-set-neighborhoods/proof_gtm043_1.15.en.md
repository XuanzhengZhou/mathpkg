If $A$ and $A'$ are completely separated, there exists $f \in C(X)$ equal to $0$ on $A$ and to $1$ on $A'$. The sets
$$F = \{x: f(x) \leq \tfrac{1}{3}\}, \quad F' = \{x: f(x) \geq \tfrac{2}{3}\}$$
are zero-sets (as sublevel/superlevel sets of a continuous function), they are neighborhoods of $A$ and $A'$ respectively (since $f \leq 0$ on $A \subset f^{-1}((-\infty, 1/3])$), and they are disjoint because $1/3 < 2/3$.
