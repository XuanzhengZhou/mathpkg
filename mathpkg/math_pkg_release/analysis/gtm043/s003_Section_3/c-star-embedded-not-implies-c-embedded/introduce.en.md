While every $C$-embedded subspace is automatically $C^*$-embedded, the converse is false. The simplest counterexample is $\mathbf{N}$ as a dense $C^*$-embedded subset of a certain space $\Sigma$, where unbounded functions on $\mathbf{N}$ fail to extend. A more striking example is $\mathbf{N}$ as a closed $C^*$-embedded subset of a pseudocompact space $\Lambda$, where extension of any unbounded function is impossible because all continuous functions on $\Lambda$ are bounded.
