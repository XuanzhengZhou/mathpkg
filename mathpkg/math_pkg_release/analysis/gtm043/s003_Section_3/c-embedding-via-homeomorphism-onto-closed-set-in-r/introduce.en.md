A sufficient condition for a subspace $S$ to be $C$-embedded in $X$ is the existence of a continuous function $h$ on $X$ whose restriction to $S$ is a homeomorphism onto a closed subset of $\mathbf{R}$. The proof uses the fact that closed sets in $\mathbf{R}$ are $C$-embedded (by Tietze's theorem) and composes extensions through the homeomorphism: a function $f$ on $S$ becomes $f \circ (h|S)^{-1}$ on the closed image, extends to $\mathbf{R}$, and then pulls back via $h$.
