Two subsets $A$ and $B$ of a topological space $X$ are completely separated iff they are contained in disjoint zero-sets. This provides a purely topological characterization in terms of the zero-set structure of $X$. The proof constructs an explicit Urysohn-type function $h = |f|/(|f|+|g|)$ from two functions with disjoint zero-sets, and conversely extracts zero-set-neighborhoods $\{x : f(x) \leq 1/3\}$ and $\{x : f(x) \geq 2/3\}$ from a separating function $f$.
