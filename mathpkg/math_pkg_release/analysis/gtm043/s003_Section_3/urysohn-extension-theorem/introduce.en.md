Urysohn's Extension Theorem provides the fundamental characterization of $C^*$-embedded subspaces: $S$ is $C^*$-embedded in $X$ exactly when any two sets that are completely separated in the subspace $S$ remain completely separated in the ambient space $X$. The sufficiency proof constructs a bounded continuous extension via a uniformly convergent series of intermediate functions, using the complete separation hypothesis to iteratively approximate the given bounded function.
