The family $Z(X)$ of all zero-sets in $X$ is closed under countable intersections, meaning that the intersection of countably many zero-sets is again a zero-set. The proof uses a uniformly convergent series construction: given $f_n \in C(X)$, define $g_n = |f_n| \wedge 2^{-n}$ and let $g = \sum_n g_n$, which converges uniformly and thus defines a continuous function whose zero-set is exactly the intersection.
