Let $\mathcal{D}$ be an admissible structure on a completely regular space $X$. Prove: $X$ is complete in $\mathcal{C} \vee \mathcal{D}$ if and only if $vX \cap c[X; \mathcal{D}] = X$, i.e., every real Cauchy $z$-ultrafilter in $\mathcal{D}$ is fixed.
