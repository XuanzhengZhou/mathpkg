Let $[X; \mathcal{D}]$ be a uniform space and let $d \in \mathcal{D}$. Prove: If a set $D$ is $d$-discrete, then there exist a family of sets $\{U_x\}_{x \in D}$ such that each $U_x$ is a neighborhood of $x$ and the family is discrete in $X$.
