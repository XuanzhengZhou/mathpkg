Prove: If $e$ is a pseudometric on a space $[X; \mathcal{D}]$, then $e \in \mathcal{D}$ if and only if $e$ is a uniformly continuous function from $X \times X$ (15M) into $\mathbf{R}$. [Necessity: 15G.1. Sufficiency: Given $\epsilon > 0$, there exist $d \in \mathcal{D}$ and $\delta > 0$ such that $d(x, y) \leq \delta$ implies $|e(x, y) - e(y, y)| \leq \epsilon$.]
