An order-preserving mapping (also called monotone or isotone) between partially ordered sets respects the order structure: if an element precedes another in the domain, its image precedes the image in the codomain. This is the natural notion of morphism in the category of partially ordered sets. The concept generalizes the familiar idea of a monotone increasing function on the real line and is fundamental to order theory and its applications in algebra and topology.
