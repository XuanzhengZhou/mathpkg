This theorem characterizes archimedean ordered fields as exactly those that embed as ordered subfields of $\mathbb{R}$. The forward direction is trivial (every subfield of $\mathbb{R}$ is archimedean). The converse is the substantial part: given an archimedean field $F$, one shows that $\mathbb{Q}$ is dense in $F$, so every element is determined by a Dedekind cut of rationals, yielding a unique order-embedding into $\mathbb{R}$. The algebraic operations are then shown to be preserved.
