The kernel of a ring homomorphism is the preimage of zero. It is always an ideal, and this fact establishes the fundamental link between homomorphisms and ideals. Conversely, every ideal arises as the kernel of some homomorphism — the canonical projection onto the quotient ring. This correspondence is the basis of the First Isomorphism Theorem: if $I$ is the kernel of a homomorphism from $A$ onto $B$, then $A/I$ is isomorphic to $B$.
