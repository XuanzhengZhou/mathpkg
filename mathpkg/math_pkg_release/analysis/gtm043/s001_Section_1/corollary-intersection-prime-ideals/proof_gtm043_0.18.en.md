If there exists a prime ideal $P$ containing $I$ but not $a$, then no power of $a$ can belong to $I$, since no power of $a$ belongs to $P$ (otherwise $a \in P$ by primality).

Conversely, if no power of $a$ belongs to $I$, then, by the preceding corollary (0.17), some prime ideal contains $I$ but not $a$.

Thus an element $a$ fails to belong to every prime ideal containing $I$ if and only if no power of $a$ lies in $I$. Equivalently, $a$ belongs to the intersection of all prime ideals containing $I$ if and only if some power of $a$ lies in $I$.
