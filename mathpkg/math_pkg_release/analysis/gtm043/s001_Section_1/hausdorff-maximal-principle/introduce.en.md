The Hausdorff Maximal Principle states that every partially ordered set contains a maximal chain — a chain that cannot be properly extended to a larger chain. It is one of the standard equivalents of the Axiom of Choice, alongside Zorn's Lemma and the Well-Ordering Theorem. In ring theory, the principle is used to prove the existence of maximal ideals and, in conjunction with multiplicatively closed sets, the existence of prime ideals with specified properties.
