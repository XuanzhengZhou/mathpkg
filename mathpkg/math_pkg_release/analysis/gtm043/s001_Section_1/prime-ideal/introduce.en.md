A prime ideal is an ideal satisfying the condition that if a product belongs to the ideal, then at least one factor belongs to it. Equivalently, the quotient ring $A/P$ is an integral domain. Prime ideals generalize prime numbers: in $\mathbb{Z}$, the nonzero prime ideals are exactly the ideals generated by prime numbers. In the theory of rings of continuous functions, prime ideals play a central role in the structure space (the prime spectrum).
