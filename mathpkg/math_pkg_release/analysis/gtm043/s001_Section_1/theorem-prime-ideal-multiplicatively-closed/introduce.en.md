This theorem guarantees the existence of a prime ideal that contains a given ideal $I$ while remaining disjoint from a multiplicatively closed set $S$ (with $0 \notin S$). The proof uses the Hausdorff Maximal Principle: one considers a maximal chain of ideals containing $I$ and disjoint from $S$, then takes their union, which is a maximal element in this class. The multiplicativity of $S$ is then used to prove this maximal ideal is prime. This theorem is the key to numerous results about prime ideals.
