Let $\mathcal{S}$ be a nonempty family of sets. $\mathcal{S}$ is said to have the **finite intersection property** provided that the intersection of any finite number of members of $\mathcal{S}$ is nonempty.

In order that $\mathcal{S}$ have the finite intersection property, it is not enough that any two members of $\mathcal{S}$ have nonempty intersection.
