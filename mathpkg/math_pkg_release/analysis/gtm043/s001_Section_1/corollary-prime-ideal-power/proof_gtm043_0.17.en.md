Apply Theorem 0.16 with $S = \{1, a, a^2, \ldots\}$, the multiplicatively closed set of all powers of $a$. By hypothesis, no power of $a$ belongs to $I$, so $I \cap S = \varnothing$. Theorem 0.16 then guarantees the existence of a prime ideal $P$ containing $I$ and disjoint from $S$. Since $1 \in S$, we have $1 \notin P$, so $P$ is proper. And since $a \in S$, we have $a \notin P$.
