Since $\beta X$ is compact, $\bar{\tau}$ is a closed mapping, and the inverse image of every compact set is compact. The mapping $\tau_0$ has the same properties, because it is the restriction of $\bar{\tau}$ to a total preimage $X_0 = \bar{\tau}^{\leftarrow}[Y]$. That $X_0$ maps onto $Y$ follows from the density of $\tau[X]$ in $Y$ and the closedness of $\bar{\tau}$.

The maximality and uniqueness claims regarding $X_0$ follow from the universal property of the Stone-Cech compactification and the preceding lemma (generalizing Lemma 6.11).
