This lemma generalizes Lemma 6.11 and states that a continuous map $\varphi: T \to Y$, when restricted to a proper dense subset $S$, cannot be a closed map provided that the intersection of each fiber with $S$ is compact. The proof constructs a specific closed set in $S$ whose image fails to be closed in $Y$, using a point $p \in T - S$ and its image $q = \varphi(p)$ together with a neighborhood separation argument.
