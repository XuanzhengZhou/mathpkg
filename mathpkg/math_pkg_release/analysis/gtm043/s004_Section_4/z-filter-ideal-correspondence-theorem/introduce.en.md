This theorem establishes the fundamental two-way correspondence between algebraic ideals in $C(X)$ and topological $z$-filters on $X$. The mapping $Z$ sends an ideal to its family of zero-sets, while $Z^{\leftarrow}$ sends a $z$-filter to the ideal of functions whose zero-sets belong to the filter. This correspondence is the central tool for studying $C(X)$ through topological means.
