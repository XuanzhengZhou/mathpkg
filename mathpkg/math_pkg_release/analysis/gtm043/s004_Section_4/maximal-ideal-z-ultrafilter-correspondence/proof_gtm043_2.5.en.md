Since $Z$ and $Z^{\leftarrow}$ preserve inclusion, the result follows at once from Theorem 2.3. If $M$ is a maximal ideal, then $Z[M]$ is a $z$-filter by Theorem 2.3(a). If $Z[M]$ were properly contained in a $z$-filter $\mathcal{F}$, then $Z^{\leftarrow}[\mathcal{F}]$ would be an ideal (Theorem 2.3(b)) properly containing $M$ (since $Z^{\leftarrow}[Z[M]] \supset M$), contradicting maximality of $M$. The converse argument is symmetric. The mapping $Z$ is one-one on maximal ideals because if $Z[M_1] = Z[M_2]$, then applying $Z^{\leftarrow}$ gives $M_1 = Z^{\leftarrow}[Z[M_1]] = Z^{\leftarrow}[Z[M_2]] = M_2$ (maximal ideals are $z$-ideals).
