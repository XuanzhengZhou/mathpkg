Theorem 2.8 states that every $z$-ideal in $C(X)$ can be expressed as an intersection of prime ideals. This gives a purely algebraic characterization: $f^n \in I$ implies $f \in I$ for every $z$-ideal $I$, which is the defining property of intersections of prime ideals (by 0.18).
