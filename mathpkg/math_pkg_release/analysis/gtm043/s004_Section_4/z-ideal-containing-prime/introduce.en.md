Theorem 2.9 gives a striking property of $z$-ideals: if a $z$-ideal contains a prime ideal, then it must itself be prime. This has important consequences: the intersection of two distinct maximal ideals is a $z$-ideal but not prime, hence it contains no prime ideal. This result is key to understanding the structure of prime ideals in $C(X)$.
