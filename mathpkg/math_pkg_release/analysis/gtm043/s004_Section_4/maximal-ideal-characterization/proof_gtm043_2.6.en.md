By Theorem 2.5, the two statements are equivalent. In (b), $\mathcal{A} \cup \{Z\}$ generates a $z$-filter. As this contains the maximal $z$-filter $\mathcal{A}$, it must be $\mathcal{A}$ itself; hence $Z \in \mathcal{A}$. Conversely, if a $z$-filter $\mathcal{A}$ contains every zero-set that meets all members of $\mathcal{A}$, then clearly $\mathcal{A}$ is a $z$-ultrafilter.
