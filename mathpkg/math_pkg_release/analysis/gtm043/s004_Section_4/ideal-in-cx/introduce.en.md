An ideal in the ring $C(X)$ of continuous real-valued functions is a proper subring closed under multiplication by arbitrary elements of $C(X)$. An ideal is proper precisely when it contains no unit (nowhere-vanishing function). Every ideal embeds in a maximal ideal, and maximal ideals are always prime. The notion of ideal in $C(X)$ plays a central role in connecting algebraic properties of the function ring with topological properties of the space $X$, particularly through the correspondence with $z$-filters.
