The structure space of a commutative ring with unity is a topological space whose points are the maximal ideals of the ring, with the Stone (or hull-kernel) topology. The basic closed sets are $\mathfrak{S}(a) = \{M : a \in M\}$ for ring elements $a$. This general construction specializes to the structure space of $C^*(X)$ when $A = C^*(X)$, and provides a fundamental bridge between algebra and topology that generalizes the Zariski topology of algebraic geometry.
