**Proof of (a) and (b), necessity.** If $P(f) \geq n$ in the ordered residue field $C/P$, then $f - n - |f - n| \in P \subset M^p$ (by Theorem 5.5), whence $M^p(f) \geq n$. A similar argument establishes the necessity in (b): if $|P(f) - r|$ is infinitely small or zero, the same holds for $|M^p(f) - r|$.

**Converses.** The converses follow from the fact that the three possibilities considered are mutually exclusive and exhaustive for each residue field. The conditions in terms of $f^*$ were derived in Theorem 7.6.

**Corollary.** It follows that $C/P$ contains infinitely large elements if and only if $C/M^p$ does---that is, if and only if $M^p$ is not a real maximal ideal.
