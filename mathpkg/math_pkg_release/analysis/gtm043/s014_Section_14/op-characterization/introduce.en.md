This proposition provides an alternative characterization of the $z$-ideal $O^p$: a function belongs to $O^p$ precisely when its zero-set contains the trace on $X$ of some neighborhood of $p$ in the Stone-Čech compactification. This formulation is often more convenient than the original definition in terms of closures of zero-sets being neighborhoods.
