When $|M^p(f)|$ is not infinitely large, the sets $\{s : s < M^p(f)\}$ and $\{s : s \geq M^p(f)\}$ form a Dedekind cut determining $f^*(p)$ as the unique real number satisfying $\sup A = \inf B$. The three possible relations between the residue class $M^p(f)$ and the extended real number $f^*(p)$ all actually occur, as demonstrated by adding or subtracting an infinitesimal function $g$ to a constant function $r$.
