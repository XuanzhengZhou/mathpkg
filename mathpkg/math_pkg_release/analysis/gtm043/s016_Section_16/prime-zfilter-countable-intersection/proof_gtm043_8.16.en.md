**Sufficiency.** If every prime $$z$$-filter with the countable intersection property is fixed, then in particular every $$z$$-ultrafilter with the countable intersection property is fixed. Hence $$X$$ is realcompact.

**Necessity.** Suppose $$X$$ is realcompact. Let $$\mathcal{F}$$ be a free, prime $$z$$-filter, and let $$\mathcal{Z}[M]$$ (where $$M$$ is a maximal ideal) be a $$z$$-ultrafilter containing $$\mathcal{F}$$. Since $$\mathcal{F}$$ is free and prime, $$\mathcal{Z}[M]$$ is also free. Because $$X$$ is realcompact, $$\mathcal{Z}[M]$$ does not have the countable intersection property. Therefore $$\mathcal{F}$$, being contained in $$\mathcal{Z}[M]$$, also fails to have the countable intersection property. Thus every prime $$z$$-filter with the countable intersection property must be fixed.
