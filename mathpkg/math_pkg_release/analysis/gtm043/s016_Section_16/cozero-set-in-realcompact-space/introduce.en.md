A cozero-set in a realcompact space is always realcompact. This follows immediately from Theorem 8.13 by writing a cozero-set $$X \setminus Z(f)$$ as the preimage $$f^{\leftarrow}[\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}]$$ of the realcompact space $$\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}$$ under the continuous function $$f$$. However, arbitrary open sets need not be realcompact, as witnessed by the space $$W$$ in $$W^*$$.
