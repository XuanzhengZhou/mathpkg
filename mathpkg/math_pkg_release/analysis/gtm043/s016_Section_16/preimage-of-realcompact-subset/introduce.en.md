If $$\tau: X \to Y$$ is continuous and $$X$$ is realcompact, then the inverse image under $$\tau$$ of any realcompact subset of $$Y$$ is itself realcompact. The proof uses the continuous extension of the identity on the preimage to its Hewitt realcompactification, combined with the extension of the restricted map, and a contradiction argument via Lemma 6.11 which describes where points of $$vS \setminus S$$ map under the extension.
