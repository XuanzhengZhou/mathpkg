Let $$X$$ be the enlargement of the Tychonoff plank obtained by deleting the point $$t = (\omega_1, \omega)$$ from the space where each point $$(\omega_1, n)$$ of $$N$$ serves as the point at infinity for a separate copy of $$N$$. Then $$t \in \beta X$$.
