When a realcompact space $$Y$$ has the property that every singleton is a $$G_{\delta}$$-set (i.e., each point is a countable intersection of open sets), then every subspace of $$Y$$ is realcompact. This generalizes from the fact that removing a single point yields a realcompact space by a previous theorem, and hereditary realcompactness then propagates to all subspaces through the equivalence established in Theorem 8.17.
