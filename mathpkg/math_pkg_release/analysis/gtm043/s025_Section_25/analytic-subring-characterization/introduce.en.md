Theorem 16.30 characterizes the analytic subrings of $C(X)$ for compact $X$: they are precisely the families of functions that are constant on some family of connected subsets. Equivalently, an analytic subring is the uniform closure of an algebraic subring. This result is a direct consequence of the Stone-Weierstrass theorem and the fact that connected stationary sets capture the uniformity of functions.
