Katětov's theorem is the culmination of Chapter 16, establishing that the topological dimension of a completely regular space $X$ equals the analytic dimension of its function ring $C^*(X)$. The proof uses the Baire category theorem on the complete metric space $C_n(X)$, the open-and-dense property of $G(\mathcal{U})$ (Theorem 16.28), and the Stone-Weierstrass theorem in its analytic formulation (Theorem 16.32). This result gives a purely algebraic characterization of the dimension of $X$ in terms of the ring $C^*(X)$.
