An absolutely convex ideal in a lattice-ordered ring is a stronger notion than a convex ideal: it requires that whenever the absolute value of $x$ is dominated by the absolute value of some element $y$ in $I$, then $x$ itself must belong to $I$. This property is precisely what is needed for the canonical quotient mapping to be a lattice homomorphism. Every absolutely convex ideal is convex, but convex ideals need not be absolutely convex.
