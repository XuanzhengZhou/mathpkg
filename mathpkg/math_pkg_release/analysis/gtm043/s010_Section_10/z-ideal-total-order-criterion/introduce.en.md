This proposition characterizes when the residue class ring $C/I$ modulo a $z$-ideal is totally ordered. By the order criterion for $z$-ideals, every residue class is comparable with $0$ precisely when, for each $f$, there is a zero-set of $I$ on which $f$ has constant sign. By Theorem 2.9, this sign-constancy condition is equivalent to $I$ being a prime ideal. Hence for $z$-ideals, total order of the quotient is equivalent to primality.
