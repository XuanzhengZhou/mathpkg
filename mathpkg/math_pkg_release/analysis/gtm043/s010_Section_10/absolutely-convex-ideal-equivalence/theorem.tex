The following conditions on a convex ideal $I$ in a lattice-ordered ring $A$ are equivalent.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(1)] $I$ is absolutely convex.
  \item[(2)] $x \in I$ implies $|x| \in I$.
  \item[(3)] $x, y \in I$ implies $x \vee y \in I$ (i.e., $I$ is a sublattice of $A$).
  \item[(4)] $I(a \vee b) = I(a) \vee I(b)$, whence $A/I$ is a lattice. Moreover,
  $$I(a \vee b) = I(a) \vee I(b), \quad I(a \wedge b) = I(a) \wedge I(b), \quad I(|a|) = |I(a)|.$$
  \item[(5)] $I(a) \geq 0$ if and only if $a \equiv |a| \pmod{I}$.
\end{enumerate}

The essential content is that the canonical mapping is a lattice homomorphism if and only if $I$ is absolutely convex. The condition that $A/I$ be a lattice is, by itself, not enough to guarantee that $I$ be absolutely convex.
