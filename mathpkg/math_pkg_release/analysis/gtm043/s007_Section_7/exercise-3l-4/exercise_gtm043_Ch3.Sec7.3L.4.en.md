Let $X$ be a completely regular space. Let $D$ be a countable discrete set in $X$. The following conditions are equivalent and imply that $D$ is closed. (Cf. 3B.) (1) $D$ and any disjoint closed set are completely separated. (2) $D$ and any disjoint closed set have disjoint neighborhoods. (3) $D$ is $C$-embedded in $X$. [Enclose the points of $D$ in disjoint neighborhoods, and apply 1.]
