This proposition states that the property of being locally compact is inherited by open subspaces. If $T$ is a locally compact Hausdorff space and $X$ is an open subset of $T$, then $X$ itself is locally compact. The proof is immediate: for each $x \in X$, the open neighborhood $X$ of $x$ in $T$ contains a compact neighborhood of $x$ (by local compactness of $T$), and this same compact set serves as a compact neighborhood of $x$ in $X$.
