This proposition gives a simple set-theoretic characterization of cluster points for $z$-filters: $p$ is a cluster point of $\mathcal{F}$ precisely when every member of $\mathcal{F}$ belongs to $A_p$, i.e., when every zero-set in $\mathcal{F}$ contains $p$. This follows directly from the definition of cluster point (that $p$ belongs to the closure, hence to the set itself, of each member of $\mathcal{F}$).
