A $z$-filter $\mathcal{F}$ is said to converge to a point $p$ if every neighborhood of $p$ contains some member of $\mathcal{F}$. Convergence implies that $p$ is a cluster point of $\mathcal{F}$, but the converse is not true in general. In a completely regular space, every neighborhood of $p$ contains a zero-set-neighborhood, so convergence can also be characterized in terms of zero-set-neighborhoods, making the notion well-adapted to the study of $C(X)$.
