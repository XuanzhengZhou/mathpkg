A locally compact space is a Hausdorff space in which every point possesses at least one compact neighborhood. An important consequence of the definition is that every neighborhood of any point contains a compact neighborhood of that point. Local compactness is a key property in topology, as every locally compact Hausdorff space is completely regular through the one-point compactification construction, and such spaces admit a particularly well-behaved theory of continuous functions vanishing at infinity.
