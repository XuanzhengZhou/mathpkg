Let $J$ denote the principal ideal in $C(\Sigma)$ (see 4M) generated by the continuous extension of $j$ to $\Sigma$. Then $O_{\sigma} \subset J \subset M_{\sigma}$ (properly), so that $J$ is not a $z$-ideal. Furthermore, $C/J$ is a totally ordered ring, although $J$ is not prime.
