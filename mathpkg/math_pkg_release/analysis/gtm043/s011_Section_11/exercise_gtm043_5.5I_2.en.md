Let $D = \{\omega_E\}_{E \in \mathcal{E}}$ be a new set of distinct points, and define $\Psi = \mathbb{N} \cup D$, with the following topology: points of $\mathbb{N}$ are isolated; a basic neighborhood of $\omega_E$ is $\{\omega_E\} \cup (E \setminus F)$ where $F$ is any finite subset of $E$. (The space $\Psi$ is pseudocompact but not countably compact.)
