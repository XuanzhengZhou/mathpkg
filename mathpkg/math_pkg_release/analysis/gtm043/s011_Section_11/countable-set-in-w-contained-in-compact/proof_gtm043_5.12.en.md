Let $A \subset W$ be countable. Define $\alpha = \sup A$. By 5.12(a), no countable set is cofinal, so $A$ cannot be cofinal; thus $\alpha < \omega_1$, i.e., $\alpha \in W$. Then $A \subset W(\alpha + 1)$, and $W(\alpha + 1)$ is compact (as a well-ordered space of a successor ordinal). Hence $A$ is contained in the compact subspace $W(\alpha + 1)$.
