Every countable subset $A$ of $W = W(\omega_1)$ lies inside a compact subspace. Indeed, let $\alpha = \sup A$; since $A$ is countable, $\alpha$ is a countable ordinal, hence $\alpha \in W$. Then $A$ is contained in the compact subspace $W(\alpha + 1)$. This corollary of 5.12(a) is used in the proof that every continuous function on $W$ is constant on a tail.
