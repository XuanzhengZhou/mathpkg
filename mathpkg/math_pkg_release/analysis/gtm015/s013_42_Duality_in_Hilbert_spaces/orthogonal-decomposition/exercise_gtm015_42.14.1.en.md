# Exercise 42.14

The analogue of the orthogonal projection theorem (42.7) holds for a complete linear subspace of an inner product space. That is, if $N$ is a complete linear subspace of an inner product space $E$, then for each $x \in E$ there exists a unique representation $x = y + z$ with $y \in N$ and $z \in N^\perp$.
