Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{C}$. If $g: E \to \mathbb{R}$ is an $\mathbb{R}$-form on $E$, then the formula
$$f(x) = g(x) - i g(ix) \qquad (x \in E)$$
defines a $\mathbb{C}$-form $f$ on $E$ such that $\operatorname{Re} f = g$. The correspondences $f \mapsto \operatorname{Re} f$ (Theorem 21.11) and $g \mapsto f$ (above) are mutually inverse; they define a bijection between the set of all $\mathbb{C}$-forms on $E$ and the set of all $\mathbb{R}$-forms on $E$.
