A linear form (or linear functional) on a vector space $E$ over $\mathbb{K}$ is a scalar-valued function $f: E \to \mathbb{K}$ such that
$$f(x + y) = f(x) + f(y) \quad \text{and} \quad f(\lambda x) = \lambda f(x)$$
for all $x, y \in E$ and all scalars $\lambda \in \mathbb{K}$. In other words, a linear form on $E$ is a linear mapping $f: E \to \mathbb{K}$, where $\mathbb{K}$ is regarded as a one-dimensional vector space over itself.
