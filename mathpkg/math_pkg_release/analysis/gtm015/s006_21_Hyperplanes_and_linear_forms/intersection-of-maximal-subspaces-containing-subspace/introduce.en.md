This theorem states that any linear subspace is the intersection of all maximal linear subspaces that contain it. In lattice-theoretic terms, the maximal subspaces are meet-dense in the subspace lattice. Taking $N = \{0\}$ yields the important special case that the intersection of all maximal subspaces is the zero subspace, meaning the family of maximal subspaces separates points. This result is used to prove the analogous statement for hyperplanes and linear varieties.
