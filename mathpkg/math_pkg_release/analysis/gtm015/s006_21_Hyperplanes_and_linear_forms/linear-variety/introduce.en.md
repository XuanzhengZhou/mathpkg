A linear variety is the affine analogue of a linear subspace -- a translate of a subspace by a fixed vector. The translating subspace is uniquely recoverable from the variety as the set of all differences of its elements. Linear varieties form the framework for studying hyperplanes: a hyperplane is defined as a maximal proper linear variety. When the variety contains the zero vector, it is precisely a linear subspace, called a homogeneous variety.
