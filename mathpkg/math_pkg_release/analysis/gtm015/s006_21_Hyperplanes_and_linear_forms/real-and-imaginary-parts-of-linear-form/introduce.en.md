This definition formalizes the notation $\operatorname{Re} f$ and $\operatorname{Im} f$ for the real-linear forms obtained from a complex-linear form $f$ by taking pointwise real and imaginary parts. These two real-linear forms satisfy the cross-relation $\operatorname{Re} f(ix) = -\operatorname{Im} f(x)$, and $f$ is completely determined by its real part alone. The notation is used throughout the study of complex topological vector spaces.
