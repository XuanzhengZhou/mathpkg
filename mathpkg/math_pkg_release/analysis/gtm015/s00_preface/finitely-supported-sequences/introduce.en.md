$c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$ is the subspace of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ consisting of sequences with only finitely many nonzero terms. It is the linear span of the standard basis vectors $e_n$. Under the Fourier transform, $c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$ corresponds to the space $\mathcal{T}$ of trigonometric polynomials. This subspace is dense in $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ (Remark 5), and the convolution product on $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ is first defined on $c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$ where it can be computed as a finite sum, then extended to all of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ by continuity.
