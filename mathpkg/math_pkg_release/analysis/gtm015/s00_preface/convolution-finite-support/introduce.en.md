On the dense subspace $c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$ of finitely supported sequences, convolution is defined by the discrete formula $(xy)(m) = \sum_n x(m-n) y(n)$, which is a genuine finite sum for each $m$. The key identity $e_m * e_n = e_{m+n}$ follows from the fact that the Fourier transform converts convolution to pointwise multiplication: $\widehat{e_m e_n} = \hat{e}_m \hat{e}_n = u_m u_n = u_{m+n} = \hat{e}_{m+n}$. This convolution formula on $c_{00}(\mathbb{Z})$ motivates the extension to all of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$.
