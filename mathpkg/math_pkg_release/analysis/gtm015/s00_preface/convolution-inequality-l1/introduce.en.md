This theorem justifies the extension of convolution from finitely supported sequences to all of $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$. For arbitrary $x, y \in \ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$, the double series $\sum_m \sum_n |x(m-n) y(n)|$ converges to $\|x\|_1 \|y\|_1$, which implies both the absolute convergence of the convolution series for each $m$ and the norm inequality $\|xy\|_1 \leq \|x\|_1 \|y\|_1$. The proof uses rearrangement of absolutely convergent double series, a discrete Fubini-type argument. This inequality is the key algebraic property that makes $\ell^1(\mathbb{Z})$ a Banach algebra.
