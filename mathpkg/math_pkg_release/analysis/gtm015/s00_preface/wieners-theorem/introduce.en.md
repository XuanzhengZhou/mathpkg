Wiener's Theorem states that the reciprocal of a nonvanishing absolutely convergent trigonometric series is itself an absolutely convergent trigonometric series. In functional-analytic terms, if an element $f$ of the Wiener algebra $\mathcal{A}$ never vanishes, then its multiplicative inverse $1/f$ also belongs to $\mathcal{A}$. Gel'fand's proof of this result uses Banach algebra techniques—particularly the Gelfand representation theory—and serves as a classic entry point into functional analysis.
