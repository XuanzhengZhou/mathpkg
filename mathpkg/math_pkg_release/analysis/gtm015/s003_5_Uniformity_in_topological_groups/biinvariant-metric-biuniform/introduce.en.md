If a topological group admits a metric that is both left- and right-invariant, then the uniform structure derived from that metric must simultaneously equal both the left and right uniformities (by the dual of Lemma 7.6), forcing them to coincide. This lemma provides the easy direction of Klee's characterization: bi-uniformity is equivalent to the existence of a bi-invariant metric for metrizable groups.
