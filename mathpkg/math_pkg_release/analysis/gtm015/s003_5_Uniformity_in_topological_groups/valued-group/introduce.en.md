A valued group is a group equipped with a nonnegative real-valued "absolute value" function satisfying natural axioms analogous to those of an absolute value on a field or a norm on a vector space. For topological groups, values serve as the algebraic counterpart of left-invariant metrics: every left-invariant metric arises from a value via $d(x, y) = |x^{-1}y|$, and every value gives rise to a left-invariant metric by the same formula.
