If $$G$$ is a topological group and $$Jx = x^{-1}$$ is the inversion mapping, then $$J$$ induces an isomorphism between the uniform structures $$(G, \mathcal{U}_s)$$ and $$(G, \mathcal{U}_r)$$. Explicitly, $$(x, y) \mapsto (Jx, Jy)$$ is a bijective mapping on $$G \times G$$ that transforms $$\mathcal{U}_s$$ into $$\mathcal{U}_r$$. Equivalently, $$J$$ is a uniformly continuous mapping of $$(G, \mathcal{U}_s)$$ onto $$(G, \mathcal{U}_r)$$, with uniformly continuous inverse.
