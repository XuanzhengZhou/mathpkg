Let $E$ be a locally convex, separated topological vector space over $\mathbb{K}$, and let $A \subset E$ be a nonempty compact convex set. If $H$ is a closed $\mathbb{R}$-hyperplane tangent to $A$, then $H$ contains at least one extremal point of $A$.

\noindent\textit{Proof sketch.} Let $\mathcal{F}$ be the family of all closed faces $F \subset H$ of $A$, ordered by reverse inclusion. For any chain $(F_\iota)$, the intersection $\bigcap_\iota F_\iota$ is nonempty (compactness), closed, and a face (36.5). By Zorn's lemma, $\mathcal{F}$ has a minimal element $F_0$. If $F_0$ were not a singleton, the Hahn-Banach theorem would yield a proper closed face of $F_0$, contradicting minimality. Thus $F_0 = \{a\}$, and $a$ is an extremal point of $A$ in $H$.
