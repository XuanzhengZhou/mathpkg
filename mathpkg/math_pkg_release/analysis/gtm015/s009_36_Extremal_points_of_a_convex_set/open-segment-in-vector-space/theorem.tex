Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{K}$ and $u, v \in E$. The **open segment** $\langle u, v \rangle$ and **closed segment** $[u, v]$ are defined by
$$\langle u, v \rangle = \{\alpha u + (1 - \alpha)v : 0 < \alpha < 1\},$$
$$[u, v] = \{\alpha u + (1 - \alpha)v : 0 \leq \alpha \leq 1\}.$$
A segment is **nondegenerate** if $u \neq v$. Both $\langle u, v \rangle$ and $[u, v]$ are convex, and $[u, v] = \operatorname{conv}\{u, v\}$. Moreover, $u \in \langle u, v \rangle$ iff $u = v$.
