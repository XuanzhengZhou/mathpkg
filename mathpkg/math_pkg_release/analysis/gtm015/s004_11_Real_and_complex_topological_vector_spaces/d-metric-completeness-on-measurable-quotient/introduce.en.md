The metric space $([\mathcal{M}], d)$ is complete. The proof uses the equivalence between $d$-Cauchy sequences and sequences fundamental in measure (Lemma 15.4), together with the Riesz-Weyl theorem: every sequence fundamental in measure admits a convergent subsequence (almost everywhere, hence in measure). The limit in measure provides the limit in $([\mathcal{M}], d)$. Together with the invariance of $d$, this makes the additive group of $[\mathcal{M}]$ a metrizable complete topological group.
