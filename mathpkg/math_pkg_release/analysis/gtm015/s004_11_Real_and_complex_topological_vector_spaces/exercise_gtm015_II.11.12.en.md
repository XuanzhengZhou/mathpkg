# Exercise 11.12

Regard $\mathbb{C}$ as a one-dimensional vector space over $\mathbb{C}$, and a two-dimensional vector space over $\mathbb{R}$, in the usual way. Define

$$p(\lambda) = |\operatorname{Im} \lambda| \quad (\lambda \in \mathbb{C}).$$

For all $\mu \in \mathbb{C}$ and $\varepsilon > 0$, write

$$U_{\varepsilon}(\mu) = \{\lambda \in \mathbb{C} : p(\lambda - \mu) < \varepsilon\}.$$

Then (i) there exists a unique topology $\tau$ on $\mathbb{C}$ for which the sets $U_{\varepsilon}(\mu)$, $\varepsilon > 0$, are a fundamental system of neighborhoods of $\mu$, (ii) $\tau$ is compatible with the real vector space structure of $\mathbb{C}$, and (iii) $\tau$ is not compatible with the complex vector space structure of $\mathbb{C}$.
