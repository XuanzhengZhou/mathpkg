# Proof of Linear subspace spanned by a convex cone

If $A$ is a nonempty convex cone in $E$, then the linear subspace of $E$ generated by $A$ is the set $A - A = \{x - y : x \in A, y \in A\}$.

Let $N$ be the linear subspace generated by $A$. Obviously $N \supset A - A$. On the other hand, $A \subset A - A$ (if $x \in A$ then $x = 2x - x \in A - A$). To establish $N \subset A - A$, it suffices to show that $A - A$ is a linear subspace of $E$.

Since $A$ is nonempty, $\theta \in A - A$. Obviously $-(A - A) = A - A$, i.e., $A - A$ contains negatives. If $\alpha > 0$ then $\alpha(A - A) = \alpha A - \alpha A \subset A - A$; combined with the preceding remarks, this shows that $\lambda(A - A) \subset A - A$ for all real $\lambda$. Finally, citing (27.5),

$$(A - A) + (A - A) = (A + A) - (A + A) \subset A - A,$$

thus $A - A$ is closed under addition.
