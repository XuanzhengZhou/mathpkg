# Proof of Precompactness equivalent to compactness of completion

With notation as in (24.15), we have $(A, \mathcal{V})$ precompact iff $(\hat{A}, \hat{\mathcal{V}})$ is compact (i.e., $\hat{A}$ is compact for the topology derived from $\hat{\mathcal{V}}$) iff the homeomorphic image $\bar{A}$ of $\hat{A}$ is compact.

This follows directly from the definition: a separated uniform structure is precompact if its completion is compact. Since $\bar{A}$ is the image of the completion $\hat{A}$ under the unimorphism $\varphi$, compactness of $\hat{A}$ is equivalent to compactness of $\bar{A}$.
