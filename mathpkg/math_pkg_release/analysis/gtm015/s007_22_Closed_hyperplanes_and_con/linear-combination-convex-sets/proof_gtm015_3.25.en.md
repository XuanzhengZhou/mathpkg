# Proof of Linear combination of convex sets is convex

Since $A \times B$ is a convex subset of the product vector space $E \times E$ by (25.8) and the mapping $u: E \times E \rightarrow E$ defined by $u(x, y) = \lambda x + \mu y$ is linear, it follows from (25.6)(i) that $u(A \times B) = \lambda A + \mu B$ is convex.
