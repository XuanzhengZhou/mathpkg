# Proof of Complex variant: strict separation of closed convex and compact convex

Viewing $E$ as a TVS over $\mathbb{R}$, there exist, by (34.1), a continuous $\mathbb{R}$-form $g$ on $E$ and a real number $\alpha$, such that $g < \alpha$ on $A$ and $g > \alpha$ on $B$. Let $f$ be the $\mathbb{C}$-form with $\operatorname{Re} f = g$ (22.6). Then $\operatorname{Re} f < \alpha$ on $A$ and $\operatorname{Re} f > \alpha$ on $B$.
