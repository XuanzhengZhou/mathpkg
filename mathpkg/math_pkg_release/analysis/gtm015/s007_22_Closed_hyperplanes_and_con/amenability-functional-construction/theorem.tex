If $T$ is an abelian semigroup, there exists a real-valued function $p$ on $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{B}_{\mathbb{R}}(T)$ such that (1) $p(x + y) \leq p(x) + p(y)$, (2) $p(\alpha x) = \alpha p(x)$ for $\alpha > 0$, (3) $\inf x \leq p(x) \leq \sup x$, (4) $p(x - x_s) = p(x_s - x) = 0$ for all $s \in T$, (5) $p(\alpha 1) = \alpha$, and (6) $x \geq 0$ implies $p(x) \geq 0$.
