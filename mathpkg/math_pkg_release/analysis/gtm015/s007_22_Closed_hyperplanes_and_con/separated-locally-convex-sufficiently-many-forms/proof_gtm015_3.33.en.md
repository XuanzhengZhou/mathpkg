# Proof of Separated locally convex spaces have sufficiently many continuous linear forms

If $E$ is a separated, locally convex TVS, then, given any nonzero vector $a$ in $E$, there exists a continuous linear form $f$ on $E$ such that $f(a) \neq 0$.

Since $\{\theta\}$ is a closed linear subspace (3.4, because $E$ is separated), the theorem (33.12) applies. By (33.12), $a \neq \theta$ implies the existence of a closed hyperplane containing $\{\theta\}$ but not $a$, i.e., a continuous linear form $f$ with $f(\theta) = 0$ and $f(a) \neq 0$.
