Let $E$ and $F$ be vector spaces over $\mathbb{K}$ and let $u: E \rightarrow F$ be a linear mapping. (i) If $A$ is a convex subset of $E$, then $u(A)$ is a convex subset of $F$. (ii) If $B$ is a convex subset of $F$, then $u^{-1}(B)$ is a convex subset of $E$. (iii) If $S$ is any subset of $E$, then $u(\operatorname{conv} S) = \operatorname{conv} u(S)$. (iv) If $T$ is any subset of $F$, then $u^{-1}(\operatorname{conv} T) \supset \operatorname{conv} u^{-1}(T)$.
