Let $E$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{R}$ and suppose $p$ is a real-valued function on $E$ such that (i) $p(x + y) \leq p(x) + p(y)$ for all $x, y \in E$, and (ii) $p(\alpha x) = \alpha p(x)$ for all $x \in E$ and $\alpha > 0$. If $g$ is a linear form defined on a linear subspace $M$ of $E$, such that $g(y) \leq p(y)$ for all $y \in M$, then $g$ may be extended to a linear form $f$ on $E$ such that $f(x) \leq p(x)$ for all $x \in E$.
