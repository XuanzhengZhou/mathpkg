# Proof of Properties of finite-dimensional separated TVS

Let $(E, \tau)$ be a finite-dimensional separated TVS over $\mathbb{K}$.

(i) Let $x \mapsto \|x\|$ be any norm on $E$. (For example, choose a basis $x_1, \ldots, x_r$ of $E$ and define, for any $x = \sum_1^r \lambda_k x_k$ in $E$, $\|x\| = \sum_1^r |\lambda_k|$.) Then the topology derived from the norm is separated and compatible with the vector space structure (16.5), therefore it coincides with $\tau$ by (23.3). Thus $\tau$ is normable.

(ii) Since $E$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{K}^r$ (23.1) and $\mathbb{K}^r$ is complete (7.8), $E$ is also complete (5.12). Since every norm on $E$ yields $\tau$ by (i), every norm on $E$ is complete. Thus $E$ is normable to be a Banach space.

(iii) Since $E$ is normable, it is metrizable (16.5); by (ii) it is complete.
