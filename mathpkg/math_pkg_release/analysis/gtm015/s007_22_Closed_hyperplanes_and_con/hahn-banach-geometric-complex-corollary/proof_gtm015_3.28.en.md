# Proof of Complex variant: continuous linear form with positive real part on open convex set

Viewing $E$ as a real TVS, there exists, by (28.4), a continuous $\mathbb{R}$-form $g$ on $E$ such that $g = 0$ on $M$ and $g > 0$ on $A$. Let $f$ be the $\mathbb{C}$-form such that $\operatorname{Re} f = g$ (22.6), that is,

$$f(x) = g(x) - i g(ix) \quad (x \in E).$$

Since $iM = M$ (as $M$ is a $\mathbb{C}$-linear subspace), clearly $f = 0$ on $M$. And $\operatorname{Re} f = g > 0$ on $A$.
