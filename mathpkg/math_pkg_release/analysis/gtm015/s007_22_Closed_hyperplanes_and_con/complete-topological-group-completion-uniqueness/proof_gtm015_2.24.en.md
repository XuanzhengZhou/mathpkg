# Proof of Uniqueness of completion for complete topological groups

By (24.3), there exists a unimorphism $\varphi: (H, U_s(H)) \rightarrow (H', U_s(H'))$ such that $\varphi(a) = a$ for all $a \in G$. It remains to show that $\varphi(xy) = \varphi(x)\varphi(y)$ for all $x, y \in H$. Indeed, the set of $(x, y)$ for which this equality holds is closed in $H \times H$ and contains the dense subset $G \times G$, therefore it is all of $H \times H$. Thus $\varphi$ is a group isomorphism as well, hence a bicontinuous group isomorphism.
