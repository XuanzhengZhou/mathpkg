# Proof of Locally convex TVS as subspace of a product of Banach spaces

This result is stated as (37.26) without a detailed proof in the text. The idea is as follows: given a separated locally convex TVS $E$, the topology is generated by a family $\mathcal{F}$ of continuous seminorms by (37.19). For each $p \in \mathcal{F}$, one forms the quotient $E/N_p$ where $N_p = \{x : p(x) = 0\}$; then $\|x + N_p\| = p(x)$ defines a norm on $E/N_p$ by (37.7)/(39.7), and the completion yields a Banach space $F_p$. The diagonal mapping $T: E \to \prod_{p \in \mathcal{F}} F_p$ is then injective (by separatedness) and is a linear homeomorphism onto its image, since the topology $\tau(\mathcal{F})$ coincides with the initial topology induced by the coordinate projections composed with the quotient maps.

Conversely, any subspace of a product of Banach spaces is locally convex because products and subspaces of locally convex spaces are locally convex, and Banach spaces are locally convex.
