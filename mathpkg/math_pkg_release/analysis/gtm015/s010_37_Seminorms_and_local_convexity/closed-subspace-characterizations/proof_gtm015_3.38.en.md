# Proof of Characterizations of closed subspaces in a locally convex TVS

Let $(E, \tau)$ be a locally convex, separated TVS over $\mathbb{K}$, and consider the canonical duality between $E$ and $E'$ (38.10). If $M$ is a linear subspace of $E$, the following conditions are equivalent:

**(a)** $M$ is closed (for $\tau$);

**(b)** $M$ is weakly closed (i.e., closed for $\sigma(E, E')$);

**(c)** $M = {}^\perp(M^\perp)$.

*Proof.* The equivalence of (a) and (b) follows from (38.12): a convex set is $\tau$-closed if and only if it is weakly closed. Since $M$ is a linear subspace, it is convex, so (a) $\iff$ (b).

The equivalence of (b) and (c) follows from (v) of (38.14): for any subset $S$ of $E$, ${}^\perp(S^\perp)$ is the $\sigma(E, F)$-closed linear subspace generated by $S$. When $S = M$ and $M$ is already weakly closed, this gives $M = {}^\perp(M^\perp)$.
