# Proof of Coincidence of $\tau$-closure and weak closure for linear subspaces

Let $(E, \tau)$ be a locally convex, separated TVS over $\mathbb{K}$, and consider the canonical duality between $E$ and $E'$ (38.10). If $M$ is any linear subspace of $E$, then the $\tau$-closure and the weak closure of $M$ coincide, and both are equal to ${}^\perp(M^\perp)$.

*Proof.* By (38.12), a convex set is $\tau$-closed if and only if it is weakly closed. Since $M$ is convex (being a linear subspace), the $\tau$-closure $\overline{M}^\tau$ and the weak closure $\overline{M}^{\sigma(E, E')}$ are both convex sets. Moreover, both closures are the intersection of all closed convex sets containing $M$ under the respective topologies. Since the $\tau$-closed convex sets are exactly the $\sigma(E, E')$-closed convex sets by (38.12), the two closures coincide.

That both closures equal ${}^\perp(M^\perp)$ follows from (38.14)(v): ${}^\perp(M^\perp)$ is precisely the $\sigma(E, E')$-closed linear subspace generated by $M$, which is the weak closure of $M$.
