# Proof of Fundamental properties of annihilators in a duality

Let $E, F$ be vector spaces over $\mathbb{K}$ with a duality $B$, and write $S, T$ for generic subsets of $E, F$, respectively. The annihilator operations satisfy:

**(i)** $S^\perp$ is a $\sigma(F, E)$-closed linear subspace of $F$; dually, ${}^\perp T$ is a $\sigma(E, F)$-closed linear subspace of $E$.

**(ii)** $S \subset {}^\perp(S^\perp)$ and $T \subset ({}^\perp T)^\perp$.

**(iii)** $S_1 \subset S_2$ implies $S_2^\perp \subset S_1^\perp$, and similarly for $T$.

**(iv)** $(S^\perp)^\perp = S^\perp$ and ${}^\perp({}^\perp T) = {}^\perp T$.

**(v)** ${}^\perp(S^\perp)$ is the $\sigma(E, F)$-closed linear subspace generated by $S$; dually, $({}^\perp T)^\perp$ is the $\sigma(F, E)$-closed linear subspace generated by $T$.

*Proof.* (i) $S^\perp = \bigcap_{s \in S} \ker B_s$, and each $\ker B_s$ is a $\sigma(F, E)$-closed linear subspace (the kernel of the continuous linear form $B_s$). Similarly for ${}^\perp T$.

(ii) If $s \in S$, then $B(s, y) = 0$ for every $y \in S^\perp$, so $s \in {}^\perp(S^\perp)$.

(iii) Clear from the definition.

(iv) From (ii), $S^\perp \subset ({}^\perp(S^\perp))^\perp$. Conversely, if $y \in ({}^\perp(S^\perp))^\perp$, we want $y \in S^\perp$. Let $s \in S \subset {}^\perp(S^\perp)$ by (ii), so $B(s, y) = 0$, giving $y \in S^\perp$.

(v) Let $M$ be the $\sigma(E, F)$-closed linear subspace generated by $S$. By (i) and (ii), ${}^\perp(S^\perp)$ is a $\sigma(E, F)$-closed linear subspace containing $S$, therefore $M \subset {}^\perp(S^\perp)$. On the other hand, $S \subset M$ and $M$ is $\sigma(E, F)$-closed implies $S^\perp \supset M^\perp$, so ${}^\perp(S^\perp) \subset {}^\perp(M^\perp) = M$ by (iii) and (v) applied to the closed subspace $M$. Hence equality holds.
