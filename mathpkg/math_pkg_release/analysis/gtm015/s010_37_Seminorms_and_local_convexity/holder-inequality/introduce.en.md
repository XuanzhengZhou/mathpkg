Hölder's inequality is one of the most important inequalities in analysis. It bounds the $L^1$ norm of a product by the product of $L^p$ and $L^q$ norms when $1/p + 1/q = 1$. The proof applies Young's inequality pointwise to $|f(x)|/\|f\|_p$ and $|g(x)|/\|g\|_q$ and integrates. Hölder's inequality is essential for establishing that $\mathcal{L}^p$ is a vector space, for proving Minkowski's inequality, and for the duality theory of $L^p$ spaces.
