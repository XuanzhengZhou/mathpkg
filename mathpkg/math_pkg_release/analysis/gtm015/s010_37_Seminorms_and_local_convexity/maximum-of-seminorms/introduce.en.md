This lemma shows that the family of all seminorms on a vector space is closed under finite pointwise maxima. The key observation is that $\{x : p(x) \leq \varepsilon\} = \bigcap_{k=1}^n \{x : p_k(x) \leq \varepsilon\}$, which is a neighborhood of zero when each $p_k$ is continuous. This closure property is essential for defining the saturation of a family of seminorms, which plays a central role in the duality between seminorm families and locally convex topologies.
