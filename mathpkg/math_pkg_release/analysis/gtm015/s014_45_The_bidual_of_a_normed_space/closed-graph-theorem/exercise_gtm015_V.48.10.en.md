# Exercise 48.10

Let $T: E \to F$ be a linear mapping, where $E$ and $F$ are metrizable TVS over $\mathbb{K}$, and $E$ is complete. Let $d$ and $\delta$ be additively invariant metrics generating the topologies of $E$ and $F$, and define

$$d'(x, y) = d(x, y) + \delta(Tx, Ty) \quad (x, y \in E).$$

Then $T$ is continuous for the given topologies if and only if $(E, d')$ is a complete metric space. (If $\mathbb{K} = \mathbb{C}$, the same result holds with appropriate modification.)
