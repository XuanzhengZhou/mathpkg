# Exercise 1

With notation as in the proof of (63.3), $\sigma(e_1) = \mathbb{T}$. If $U \in \mathcal{L}(l^1(\mathbb{Z}))$ is the multiplication operator $Ux = e_1x$, then $\sigma(U) = \mathbb{T}$. In view of the formulas $Ue_n = e_{n+1}, U^{-1}e_n = e_{n-1}$, $U$ is called a bilateral shift operator.
