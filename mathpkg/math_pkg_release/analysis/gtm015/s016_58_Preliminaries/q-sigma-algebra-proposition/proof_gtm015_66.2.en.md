# Proof of Q(sigma) is an algebra of functions

Proof. (i) Clear.
(ii) For compact $\sigma$, $\mathbb{C}(\sigma)$ is the commutative $C^*$-algebra described in (59.1); the inclusion $Q(\alpha) \subset \mathbb{C}(\sigma)$ is obvious since the functions in $Q(\sigma)$ are continuous and $\mathbb{C}$-valued.
