The algebra $Q(\sigma)$ of rational functions with poles off $\sigma$ is the domain of the rational functional calculus. Via the Riesz-Dunford formula $f(T) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int f(z)(z - T)^{-1} dz$, these rational functions are mapped to operators, forming the foundation of spectral set theory.
