# Exercise 4

Let $T \in \mathcal{L}(H)$, $H$ a Hilbert space, and suppose there exists an algebra homomorphism $\varphi : \mathcal{C}(\sigma(T)) \to \mathcal{L}(H)$ such that $\varphi(1) = I$ and $\varphi(u) = T$, where $u$ is the identity function $u(\lambda) \equiv \lambda$ on $\sigma(T)$. Then $\varphi$ is continuous and $T$ is similar to a normal operator (i.e., there exists an invertible operator $S$ such that $S^{-1}TS$ is normal).
