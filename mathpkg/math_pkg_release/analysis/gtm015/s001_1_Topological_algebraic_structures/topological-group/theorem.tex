Let $G$ be a group (notated multiplicatively). A topology on $G$ is said to be compatible with the group structure if the mappings $(x, y) \mapsto xy$ and $x \mapsto x^{-1}$ are continuous. A topological group is a pair $(G, \tau)$, where $G$ is a group and $\tau$ is a topology on $G$ compatible with the group structure.
