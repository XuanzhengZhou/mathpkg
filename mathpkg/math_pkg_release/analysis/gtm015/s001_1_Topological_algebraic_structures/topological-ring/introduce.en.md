A topological ring is a ring whose topology makes addition, negation, and multiplication continuous. The additive group of a topological ring is automatically an abelian topological group. However, even when the ring has a unity element, the multiplicative group of invertible elements need not be a topological group under the subspace topology, because the inversion map $x \mapsto x^{-1}$ may fail to be continuous on the unit group.
