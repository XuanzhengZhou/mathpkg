# Exercise 3

(68.24) (i) Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a linear subspace of $\mathcal{L}(H)$. The following conditions on a linear form $f: \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{C}$ are equivalent: (a) $f$ is ultraweakly continuous; (b) $f$ is ultrastrongly continuous; (c) there exists a pair of sequences $(x_i), (y_i)$ of vectors in $H$, with $\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \|x_i\|^2 < \infty$ and $\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \|y_i\|^2 < \infty$, such that $f(T) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} (Tx_i|y_i)$ for all $T \in \mathcal{M}$.

(ii) A convex subset of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is ultraweakly closed iff it is ultrastrongly closed.
