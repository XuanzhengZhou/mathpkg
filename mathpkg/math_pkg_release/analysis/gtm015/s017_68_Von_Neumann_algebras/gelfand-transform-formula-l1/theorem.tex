\begin{proposition}
If $u = [f] \in L^1(G)$ and $\hat{u}$ denotes the Gel'fand transform of $u$, then for all maximal ideals $M \in \mathcal{M}$,
$$\hat{u}(M) = \int f(t) \alpha_M(t)\, dt$$
where $\alpha_M$ is the continuous character of $G$ corresponding to $M$. The functions $\alpha \mapsto \int f(t) \alpha(t)\, dt$ and $\alpha \mapsto \int f(t) \alpha(t)^*\, dt$ on the dual group $\hat{G}$ are the Fourier cotransform and Fourier transform of $f$, respectively.
\end{proposition}
