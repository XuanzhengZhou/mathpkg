The von Neumann bicommutant theorem is the foundational result of von Neumann algebra theory. It states that for a nondegenerate $*$-subalgebra of bounded operators, the bicommutant equals the ultrastrong (equivalently, strong, or weak) closure. This justifies the definition of a von Neumann algebra as a $*$-subalgebra equal to its own bicommutant and links algebraic and topological characterizations.
