# Exercise 5

(68.26) Let $\mathcal{B} = \{T \in \mathcal{L}(H) : \|T\| \leq 1\}$.

(i) The strong topology induces a uniform structure on $\mathcal{L}(H)$ (11.10); $\mathcal{B}$ is complete for the relative uniform structure.

(ii) The ultrastrong and strong topologies coincide on $\mathcal{B}$ (hence on any norm-bounded subset of $\mathcal{L}(H)$); if $H$ is separable, then $\mathcal{B}$ is metrizable for this topology and has a countable neighborhood base.
