The convex hull of finitely many points in a topological vector space is the continuous image of the standard simplex $\{(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_n) \in \mathbb{R}^n : \alpha_i \ge 0,\ \sum \alpha_i = 1\}$ under the affine map $(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_n) \mapsto \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i x_i$. Since the standard simplex is compact and the affine map is continuous, the convex hull is compact. This elementary fact is a key lemma in the study of compactness properties of convex sets, used for instance in proving that the convex hull of a finite union of compact convex sets is compact.
