In a general topological vector space, the convex hull of a compact set need not be compact. However, when the set is itself a finite union of compact convex sets, this pathological behavior is avoided. The theorem establishes that the convex hull of such a finite union remains compact in any separated TVS. The proof proceeds by showing that the convex hull is totally bounded using a covering argument with convex symmetric neighborhoods, together with the fact that the convex hull of a finite set is compact, and that total boundedness implies compactness for closed subsets in this setting.
