In any topological group, the closure of the singleton containing the identity element forms a closed normal subgroup. This follows from the continuity of the group operations: the closure of a subgroup is always a subgroup, and since the identity is central to conjugation, its closure is invariant under all inner automorphisms, hence normal. Being a closure, it is automatically closed.
