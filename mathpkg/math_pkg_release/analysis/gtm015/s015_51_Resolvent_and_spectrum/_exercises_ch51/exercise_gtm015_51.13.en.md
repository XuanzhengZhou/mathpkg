# Exercise 51.13

(51.13) Let $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{L}(H)$, where $H$ is a separable, infinite-dimensional Hilbert space, let $(x_n)_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ be an orthonormal basis of $H$ indexed by the set of all integers, and let $U$ be the element of $\mathcal{A}$ such that $Ux_n = x_{n+1}$ for all $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. (Such an operator $U$ is called a bilateral shift.) Let $\mathcal{B}$ be the closed subalgebra of $\mathcal{A}$ generated by $I$ and $U$, that is, the closed linear span of the set $\{I, U, U^2, U^3, \ldots\}$. Then $U$ is invertible in $\mathcal{A}$ but $U^{-1} \notin \mathcal{B}$.
