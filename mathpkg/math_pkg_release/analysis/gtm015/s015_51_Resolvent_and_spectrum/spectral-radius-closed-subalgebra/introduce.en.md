Although the spectrum may shrink when passing from a subalgebra $B$ to a larger algebra $A$ (filling in holes), the spectral radius is invariant under such extensions. This follows directly from Gel'fand's formula, which expresses $r(x)$ purely in terms of the norm sequence $\|x^n\|$, which is independent of the containing algebra.
