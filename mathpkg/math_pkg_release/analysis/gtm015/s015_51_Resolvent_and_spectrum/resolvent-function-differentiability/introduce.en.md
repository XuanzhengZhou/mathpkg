The resolvent function $R(\lambda) = (\lambda 1 - x)^{-1}$ is analytic on the resolvent set: it vanishes at infinity and is complex-differentiable at every point of $\rho(x)$, with derivative $-(\lambda 1 - x)^{-2}$. This analyticity is the gateway to applying complex analysis methods to Banach algebras.
