Theorem 1.8 constructs the canonical flabby resolution $W^\bullet(\mathcal{S})$ of a sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ of $R$-modules and applies the global sections functor $\Gamma(X, -)$ to obtain a complex whose cohomology defines the sheaf cohomology groups $H^q(X, \mathcal{S})$. Together with Theorems 1.6 and 1.7, this theorem establishes the foundational framework for sheaf cohomology in the context of several complex variables. The statement in the source is truncated; the full statement appears in the complete text of Grauert and Fritzsche.
