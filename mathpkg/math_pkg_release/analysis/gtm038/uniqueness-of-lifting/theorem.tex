(Uniqueness of lifting). Let $\mathfrak{G} = (G, \pi, x_0)$ be a domain over $\mathbb{C}^n$ with base point, $Y$ a connected topological space and $y_0 \in Y$ a point. If $\psi_1, \psi_2: Y \rightarrow G$ are continuous mappings with $\psi_1(y_0) = \psi_2(y_0) = x_0$ and $\pi \circ \psi_1 = \pi \circ \psi_2$, then $\psi_1 = \psi_2$.
