**Proof.** The de Rham theorem states that the sheaf cohomology with values in the constant sheaf $\mathbb{C}$ is isomorphic to the de Rham cohomology: ^q(X, \mathbb{C}) \cong H^q_{dR}(X)$. The de Rham resolution /usr/bin/zsh \to \mathbb{C} \to \mathcal{A}^0 \xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{A}^1 \xrightarrow{d} \cdots$ (where $\mathcal{A}^k$ is the sheaf of smooth 569JNRXghklforms) is a fine resolution of the constant sheaf $\mathbb{C}$. The Poincaré lemma (exactness of this resolution locally) combined with the fact that the sheaves of smooth forms are fine (hence acyclic for the global section functor) yields the isomorphism. $\square$
