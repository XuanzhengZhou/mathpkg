Let $j_i: \mathcal{O} \hookrightarrow q\mathcal{O}$ be the canonical injections, and let $1 \in \Gamma(B, \mathcal{O})$ be the unit $1$-section. Define the unit sections $e_i := j_i \circ 1 = (0, \ldots, 1, \ldots, 0) \in \Gamma(B, q\mathcal{O})$.

If $\varphi: q\mathcal{O} \rightarrow \mathcal{S}$ is an analytic sheaf morphism and $s_i := \varphi \circ e_i \in \Gamma(B, \mathcal{S})$, then for $(a_1, \ldots, a_q) \in q\mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{z}}$,
$$
\varphi(a_1, \ldots, a_q) = \varphi\left( \sum_{i=1}^{q} a_i e_i(\mathfrak{z}) \right) = \sum_{i=1}^{q} a_i s_i(\mathfrak{z}).
$$

Thus the sections $s_1, \ldots, s_q$ determine $\varphi$ completely. Conversely, given sections $s_1, \ldots, s_q \in \Gamma(B, \mathcal{S})$, one can define an analytic sheaf morphism $\varphi = \varphi_{(s_1, \ldots, s_q)}: q\mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{S}$ by the above formula.
