This **corollary** to Theorem 4.9, combined with Oka's Coherence Theorem (which states that the structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}$ of holomorphic functions is coherent), implies that the quotient sheaf $\mathcal{O}/\mathcal{I}$ is coherent for any coherent ideal sheaf $\mathcal{I} \subset \mathcal{O}$.

This is a fundamental result in complex analytic geometry: it ensures that the structure sheaf of any analytic subset (defined by a coherent ideal) is well-behaved. In particular, for an analytic set $A \subset B$, the sheaf $\mathcal{O}/\mathcal{I}(A)$ is a coherent sheaf of $\mathbb{C}$-algebras.
