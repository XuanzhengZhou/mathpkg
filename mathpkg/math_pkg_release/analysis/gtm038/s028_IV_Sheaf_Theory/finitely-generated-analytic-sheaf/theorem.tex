An analytic sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ over $B$ is called **finitely generated** if for every point $\mathfrak{z} \in B$ there exist an open neighborhood $W(\mathfrak{z}) \subset B$, a natural number $q$, and a sheaf epimorphism
$$
\varphi: q\mathcal{O}|W \twoheadrightarrow \mathcal{S}|W.
$$

If $e_i$ is the $i$-th unit section of $q\mathcal{O}$ and $s_i := \varphi \circ (e_i|W)$, then for $\sigma \in \mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}}$,
$$
\sigma = \varphi(a_1, \ldots, a_q) = \sum_{i=1}^{q} a_i s_i(\mathfrak{z})
$$
for some $(a_1, \ldots, a_q) \in q\mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{z}}$. The sections $s_1, \ldots, s_q$ therefore generate the $\mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{z}}$-module $\mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}}$ simultaneously over all of $W$.
