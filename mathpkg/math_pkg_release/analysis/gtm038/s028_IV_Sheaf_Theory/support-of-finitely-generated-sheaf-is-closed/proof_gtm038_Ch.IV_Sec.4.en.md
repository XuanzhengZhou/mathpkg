**Proof.** We show that $B \setminus \operatorname{Supp}(\mathcal{S})$ is open in $\mathbb{C}^n$.

Let $\mathfrak{z}_0 \in B \setminus \operatorname{Supp}(\mathcal{S})$ be arbitrary, so $\mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}_0} = \mathbf{O}_{\mathfrak{z}_0}$. Since $\mathcal{S}$ is finitely generated, there exists an open neighborhood $W(\mathfrak{z}_0) \subset B$ and a sheaf epimorphism $\varphi: q\mathcal{O}|W \twoheadrightarrow \mathcal{S}|W$.

Let $s_1, \ldots, s_q \in \Gamma(W, \mathcal{S})$ be the images of the unit sections under $\varphi$. Since $\varphi_{\mathfrak{z}_0}$ is surjective and $\mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}_0} = \mathbf{O}_{\mathfrak{z}_0}$, we must have $s_i(\mathfrak{z}_0) = 0$ for all $i = 1, \ldots, q$.

By continuity of the sections $s_i$, there exists a neighborhood $V(\mathfrak{z}_0) \subset W$ such that $s_i|V = 0$ for all $i$. For any $\mathfrak{z} \in V$, the stalk $\mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}}$ is generated by $s_1(\mathfrak{z}), \ldots, s_q(\mathfrak{z})$ (since $\varphi$ is an epimorphism), hence $\mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}} = \mathbf{O}_{\mathfrak{z}}$. Thus $V \subset B \setminus \operatorname{Supp}(\mathcal{S})$, proving that the complement is open. $\square$
