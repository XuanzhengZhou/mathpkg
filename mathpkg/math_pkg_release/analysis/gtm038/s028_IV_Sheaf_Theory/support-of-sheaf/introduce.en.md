The **support of an analytic sheaf** $\mathcal{S}$ (Def. 4.2) is the set of points $\mathfrak{z} \in B$ where the stalk $\mathcal{S}_{\mathfrak{z}}$ is non-trivial.

The support is a fundamental invariant of a sheaf. For finitely generated sheaves, the support is always a closed subset of $B$ (Theorem 4.1). Important examples include: the support of the quotient sheaf $\mathcal{O}/\mathcal{I}(A)$ for an analytic set $A$, which coincides with $A$ itself.
