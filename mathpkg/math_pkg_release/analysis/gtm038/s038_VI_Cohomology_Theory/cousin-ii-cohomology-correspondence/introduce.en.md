Theorem 4.7 establishes the fundamental correspondence between Cousin II
distributions and \v{C}ech cohomology. Given a Cousin II distribution
$(U_t, f_t)_{t \in I}$ on a complex manifold $X$, the associated transition
functions $h_{t_0 t_1} = f_{t_0} / f_{t_1}$ form a $1$-cocycle with values in
the sheaf $\mathcal{O}^*$ of non-vanishing holomorphic functions, and the
distribution is solvable precisely when this cocycle is a coboundary.
