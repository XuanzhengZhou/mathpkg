Let $N_U$ be the additive abelian group of holomorphic functions on $U$,
and $M_U$ the multiplicative abelian group of nowhere-vanishing holomorphic
functions on $U$ (turned into an additive $\mathbb{Z}$-module).

There exists a $\mathbb{Z}$-module homomorphism
$$\exp_U: N_U \rightarrow M_U$$
defined by
$$f \mapsto e^{2\pi i f}.$$

For $V \subset U$ the commutative law
$$\exp_V \circ r_V^U = r_V^U \circ \exp_U$$
holds, where $r_V^U$ denotes the restriction maps.

This defines a sheaf homomorphism
$$\exp: \mathcal{O} \rightarrow \mathcal{O}^*$$
with $\exp(rf) = r(e^{2\pi i f})$, where $rf$ denotes the germ of $f$.
