An \textit{isomorphism} between complex ringed spaces $(X_1, \mathcal{H}_1)$ and $(X_2, \mathcal{H}_2)$ is a pair $\varphi = (\tilde{\varphi}, \varphi_*)$ with the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\tilde{\varphi}: X_1 \rightarrow X_2$ is a topological mapping (homeomorphism).
\item $\varphi_*: \mathcal{H}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{H}_2$ is a topological mapping.
\item $\varphi_*$ is stalk-preserving with respect to $\tilde{\varphi}$; that is, the following diagram is commutative:
\[
\begin{CD}
\mathcal{H}_1 @>{\varphi_*}>> \mathcal{H}_2 \\
@V{\pi_1}VV @VV{\pi_2}V \\
X_1 @>{\tilde{\varphi}}>> X_2
\end{CD}
\]
\item For every $x \in X_1$, $\varphi_*|_{(\mathcal{H}_1)_x}: (\mathcal{H}_1)_x \rightarrow (\mathcal{H}_2)_{\tilde{\varphi}(x)}$ is a homomorphism of $\mathbb{C}$-algebras.
\end{enumerate}
The existence of an isomorphism $\varphi$ between $(X_1, \mathcal{H}_1)$ and $(X_2, \mathcal{H}_2)$ is expressed briefly by
$$(X_1, \mathcal{H}_1) \simeq (X_2, \mathcal{H}_2).$$
