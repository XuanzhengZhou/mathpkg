A pair $(X, \mathcal{H})$ is called a \textit{complex ringed space} if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is a topological space;
\item $\mathcal{H}$ is a sheaf of local $\mathbb{C}$-algebras over $X$.
\end{enumerate}
If $W \subset X$ is an open set, then the set of all complex valued functions on $W$ will be denoted by $\mathcal{F}(W, \mathbb{C})$.
If $f \in \Gamma(W, \mathcal{H})$, then there is an element $[f] \in \mathcal{F}(W, \mathbb{C})$ defined by $[f](x) := \pi_x(f_x)$, where $\pi_x: \mathcal{H}_x \to \mathcal{H}_x/\mathfrak{m}_x \simeq \mathbb{C}$ is the canonical projection onto the residue field and $f_x$ is the germ of $f$ at $x$.
