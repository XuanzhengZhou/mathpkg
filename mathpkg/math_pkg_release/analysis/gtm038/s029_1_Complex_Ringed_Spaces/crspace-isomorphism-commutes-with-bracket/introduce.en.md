This theorem states that the isomorphism $\varphi$ of complex ringed spaces is compatible with the bracket operation that evaluates sections as complex-valued functions. The pullback $\varphi^*$ of functions commutes with the bracket map: applying the sheaf isomorphism $\varphi_*$ to a section and then taking its bracket yields the same function as first taking the bracket and then pulling back by $\tilde{\varphi}$. This ensures that the geometric structure (the topological map) and the algebraic structure (the sheaf isomorphism) are coherent.
