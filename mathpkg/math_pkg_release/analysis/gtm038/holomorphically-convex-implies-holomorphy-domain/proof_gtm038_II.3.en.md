**Proof.** Let $ be holomorphically convex. Choose a sequence of compact sets \nu \subset G$ with \nu \subset K_{\nu+1}^\circ$ and $\bigcup K_\nu = G$. Let $\hat{K}_\nu$ be their holomorphically convex hulls. Since $ is holomorphically convex, each $\hat{K}_\nu$ is compact. For each $\nu$, choose a point $\mathfrak{z}_\nu \in \partial G$ near $\hat{K}_\nu$. By a standard construction using the holomorphic convexity, one produces a function $ holomorphic on $ that cannot be continued beyond any boundary point, proving $ is a domain of holomorphy. $\square$
