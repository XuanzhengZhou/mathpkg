Let $G \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ be a domain with $0 \in G$, $\pi: \mathbb{C}^n - \{0\} \to \mathbb{P}^{n-1}$ the natural projection. Then $X := \{(3, x) \in (G - \{0\}) \times \mathbb{P}^{n-1}: x = \pi(3)\} \cup \{(0) \times \mathbb{P}^{n-1}\}$ is an analytic set of codimension $n-1$ in $G \times \mathbb{P}^{n-1}$ which is free of singularities, therefore an $n$-dimensional complex manifold.
