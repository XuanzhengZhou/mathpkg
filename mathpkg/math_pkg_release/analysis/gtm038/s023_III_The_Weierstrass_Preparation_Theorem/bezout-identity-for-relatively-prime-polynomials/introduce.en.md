For two relatively prime elements $\omega_1, \omega_2$ in a polynomial ring $A[u]$ over a Henselian ring $A$, there exists a Bezout-type identity. Specifically, one can find polynomial coefficients $q_1, q_2 \in A[u]$ such that the linear combination $q_1 \omega_1 + q_2 \omega_2$ is a nonzero element of the coefficient ring $A$ (a "constant" with respect to $u$). This result is a crucial algebraic tool used in the study of pseudopolynomials and analytic sets in several complex variables.
