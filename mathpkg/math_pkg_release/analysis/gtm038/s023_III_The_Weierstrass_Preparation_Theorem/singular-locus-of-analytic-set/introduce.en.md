Theorem 6.16 states that the singular locus $S(M)$ of an analytic set $M$ — the set of points where $M$ is not a complex manifold — is itself an analytic subset of $G$ and is nowhere dense in $M$. This means that the regular points form a dense open subset of $M$. The singular locus is defined by the vanishing of certain minors of the Jacobian matrix of the defining functions, making it an analytic set. This result is fundamental for the dimension theory of analytic sets.
