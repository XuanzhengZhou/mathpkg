**Proof.** The text references this as a known result in the theory of analytic sets. The proof uses the Weierstrass preparation theorem to locally represent the hypersurface $\{f = 0\}$ as the zero set of a pseudopolynomial. On the regular locus of $M$, one can apply the implicit function theorem and dimension theory for complex manifolds. The irreducibility of $M$ ensures that $f$ does not vanish on any component of the regular locus, so the intersection has codimension exactly one. The statement about irreducible components follows from the fact that each component of the intersection must contain regular points of $M$ where the dimension drop is exactly one.
