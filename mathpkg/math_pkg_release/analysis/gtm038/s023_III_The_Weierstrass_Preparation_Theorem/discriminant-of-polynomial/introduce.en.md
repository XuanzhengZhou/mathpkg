The discriminant $\Delta(f)$ of a monic polynomial $f(X) = X^s + a_1 X^{s-1} + \cdots + a_s$ is a polynomial expression in the coefficients $a_1, \ldots, a_s$ that detects multiple roots. It is defined via the fundamental theorem of symmetric polynomials applied to the squared product of differences $D(X_1, \ldots, X_s) = \prod_{\nu < \mu} (X_\nu - X_\mu)^2$. For a quadratic $f(X) = X^2 - aX + b$, the discriminant is $\Delta(f) = a^2 - 4b$, which vanishes precisely when the two roots coincide.
