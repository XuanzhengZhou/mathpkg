The Fundamental Theorem of Symmetric Polynomials states that every symmetric polynomial with integer coefficients can be expressed uniquely as a polynomial in the elementary symmetric polynomials. If $p(X_1, \ldots, X_s)$ is symmetric, there exists a unique $Q(Y_1, \ldots, Y_s)$ such that $p = Q(\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_s)$. This theorem is foundational in algebra and is used to define the discriminant of a polynomial $f(X) = X^s + a_1 X^{s-1} + \cdots + a_s$ as $\Delta(f) := Q(a_1, \ldots, a_s)$, where $Q$ expresses the squared differences of the roots of $f$.
