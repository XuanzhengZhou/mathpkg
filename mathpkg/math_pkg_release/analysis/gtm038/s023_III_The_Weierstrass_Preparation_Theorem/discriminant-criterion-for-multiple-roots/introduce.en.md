Theorem 6.10 provides the fundamental criterion for detecting multiple roots of a polynomial via its discriminant. A polynomial $f(X) = \prod_{\rho=1}^{s} (X - X_\rho)$ has a repeated root if and only if its discriminant $\Delta(f)$ vanishes. This is because $\Delta(f)$ equals the squared product of all differences between roots, so it is zero exactly when two roots coincide. This criterion is essential in the study of branched coverings of analytic sets, where the discriminant of a pseudopolynomial determines the branch locus.
