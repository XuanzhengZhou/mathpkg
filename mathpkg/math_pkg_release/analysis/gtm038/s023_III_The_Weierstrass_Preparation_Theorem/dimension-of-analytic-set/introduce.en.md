The dimension of an analytic set $M$ is first defined at regular points via the local biholomorphic equivalence to a plane segment (Theorem 6.15). The real dimension $\dim_{\mathbb{R}}(M)$ is the dimension of this plane segment; the complex dimension is half of that, $\dim_{\mathbb{C}}(M) = \frac{1}{2} \dim_{\mathbb{R}}(M)$. For an arbitrary analytic set decomposed into irreducible components $M = \bigcup_i M_i$, the dimension is the maximum over the dimensions of its components. An analytic set where all irreducible components have the same dimension $k$ is said to be of pure dimension $k$.
