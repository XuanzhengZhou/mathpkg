**Theorem 7.6.** Every biholomorphic mapping is open. In particular, a biholomorphic mapping $f: G \to \tilde{G}$ is a homeomorphism.

**Proof.** Let $f: G \to \tilde{G}$ be biholomorphic. Then $f$ is holomorphic with holomorphic inverse $f^{-1}: \tilde{G} \to G$. Both $f$ and $f^{-1}$ are continuous (holomorphic functions are continuous by Theorem 1.5). Therefore $f$ is a homeomorphism. Any homeomorphism maps open sets to open sets, so $f$ is open.
