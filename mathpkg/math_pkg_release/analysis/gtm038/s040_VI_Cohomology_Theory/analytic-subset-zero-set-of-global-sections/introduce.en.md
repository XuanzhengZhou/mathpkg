On a Stein manifold $X$, every analytic subset $A$ can be described locally as the common zero set of finitely many global holomorphic functions. This result shows that on Stein manifolds, analytic subsets have a purely function-theoretic description: near any compact subset of $X$, the analytic set is cut out by equations $f_1 = \cdots = f_\ell = 0$ with $f_i \in \Gamma(X, \mathcal{O})$. The proof uses the coherence of the ideal sheaf $\mathcal{I}(A)$ and the fact that global sections generate stalks over relatively compact open subsets.
