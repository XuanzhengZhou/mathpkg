A (real) tangent vector at $x_0 \in X$ is a mapping $D: \mathcal{D}_{x_0}^1 \to \mathbb{R}$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item $D$ is $\mathbb{R}$-linear;
  \item $D(1) = 0$; and
  \item $D(f \cdot g) = f(x_0) D(g) + g(x_0) D(f)$ for all $f, g \in \mathcal{D}_{x_0}^1$.
\end{enumerate}

The set of all tangent vectors at $x_0$ is denoted by $T_{x_0}$. The partial derivatives

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial x_1}, \ldots, \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n}, \frac{\partial}{\partial y_1}, \ldots, \frac{\partial}{\partial y_n}$$

form a basis for $T_{x_0}$, hence $\dim_{\mathbb{R}} T_{x_0} = 2n$.
