A test function on a paracompact complex manifold $X$ is a smooth ($C^\infty$) real-valued function with compact support. The collection of all test functions forms a ring (without multiplicative identity, since constant functions with value $1$ do not have compact support on non-compact manifolds). The associated sheaf $\mathcal{T}$ of germs of test functions is a fine sheaf, which plays a crucial role in establishing the fineness of the sheaf $\mathcal{A}^\ell$ of germs of differential $\ell$-forms.
