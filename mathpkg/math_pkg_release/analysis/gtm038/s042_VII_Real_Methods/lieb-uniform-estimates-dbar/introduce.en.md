Lieb's theorem provides uniform estimates for the $\bar{\partial}$-equation on strongly pseudo-convex domains with smooth boundary. If $\omega$ is a $d''$-closed $(0,q)$-form bounded by $M$ in the supremum norm, then there exists a solution $\psi$ to $d''\psi = \omega$ whose supremum norm is bounded by $k \cdot M$, where the constant $k$ depends only on the domain $G$ and not on the particular form $\omega$. This is a fundamental result in the $L^\infty$ theory of the $\bar{\partial}$-Neumann problem.
