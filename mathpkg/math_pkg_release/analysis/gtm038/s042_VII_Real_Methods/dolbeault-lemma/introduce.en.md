Dolbeault's Lemma is the $\bar{\partial}$-analogue of the Poincaré Lemma. It states that locally, every $d''$-closed differential form of type $(0, q)$ with $q \geq 1$ is $d''$-exact. The result is proved by induction on the dimension $n$, treating one complex variable at a time and using parametric integrals. This lemma is a cornerstone for establishing that the Dolbeault complex provides a fine resolution of the structure sheaf $\mathcal{O}$ on complex manifolds.
