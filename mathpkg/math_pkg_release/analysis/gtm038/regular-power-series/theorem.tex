Let $g \in \mathbb{C}\{z\}$ be convergent and regular of order $s$ in $z_1$. Then for every $\varepsilon > 0$ and every $t_0 \in \mathbb{R}_+^n$ there exists a $t \leqslant t_0$ such that $g$ lies in $B_t$, $g_s$ (the coefficient of $z_1^s$) is a unit in $B_t'$, and $\|z_1^s - g g_s^{-1}\|_t \leqslant \varepsilon \cdot t_1^s$.
