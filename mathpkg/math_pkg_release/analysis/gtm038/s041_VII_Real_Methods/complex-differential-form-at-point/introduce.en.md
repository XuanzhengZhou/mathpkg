A complex differential form at a point $x_0$ of a complex manifold generalizes the notion of a real differential form by allowing complex values. It is an alternating $\mathbb{R}$-multilinear map from $r$ copies of the real tangent space $T_{x_0}$ into $\mathbb{C}$. The space of all such $r$-forms is denoted $F_{x_0}^{(r)}$ and forms the foundation for studying differential forms on complex manifolds.
