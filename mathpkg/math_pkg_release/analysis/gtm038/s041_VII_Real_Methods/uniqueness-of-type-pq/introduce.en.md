This theorem states that the decomposition of a non-zero complex differential form into a type $(p,q)$ is unique. A form of type $(p,q)$ transforms by a factor $c^p \bar{c}^q$ under scaling of tangent vectors. The proof uses the fact that if a form were simultaneously of types $(p,q)$ and $(p',q')$, then $p-q = p'-q'$ and $p+q = p'+q' = r$, forcing $p=p'$ and $q=q'$.
