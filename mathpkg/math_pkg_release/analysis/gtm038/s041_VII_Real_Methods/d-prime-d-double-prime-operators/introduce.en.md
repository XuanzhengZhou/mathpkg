The operators $d'$ and $d''$ (commonly denoted $\partial$ and $\bar{\partial}$ in the English literature) are the Dolbeault operators that decompose the exterior derivative $d$ into holomorphic and antiholomorphic parts. For a differential form expressed as a sum of pure type components $\varphi = \sum \varphi^{(p,q)}$, $d'$ acts by summing $d'\varphi^{(p,q)}$ over all $(p,q)$ pairs, and similarly for $d''$. Together they satisfy $d = d' + d''$.
