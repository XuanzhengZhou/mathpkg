**Proof.** A function : X \to \mathbb{C}$ on a complex manifold is holomorphic if  \circ \varphi_\alpha^{-1}$ is holomorphic for every chart $\varphi_\alpha$. This definition is independent of the choice of atlas because transition maps are biholomorphic. The maximum principle, identity theorem, and Cauchy integral formula carry over from local coordinates. In particular, if $ is compact and connected, every holomorphic function is constant (by the maximum principle applied locally). $\square$
