Definition 6.3 introduces the notion of a pseudopolynomial without multiple factors. In the ring $A^0[u]$ of monic pseudopolynomials with holomorphic coefficients, Theorem 6.5 guarantees a unique prime decomposition of each element. A pseudopolynomial is said to be without multiple factors (i.e., square-free in the usual algebraic sense) if all factors in this prime decomposition are pairwise distinct. This property is analogous to the concept of a polynomial without repeated roots.
