Theorem 6.6 guarantees the existence of a greatest common divisor in the polynomial ring $Q[u]$ over the field of meromorphic functions $Q = Q(A)$, where $A = A(G)$ is the ring of holomorphic functions on a domain $G \subset \mathbb{C}^n$. Moreover, this GCD can be expressed as a linear combination with coefficients in $A[u]$ rather than merely in the full polynomial ring $Q[u]$. The result relies on the fact that $Q[u]$ is a Euclidean ring, allowing the Euclidean algorithm to produce the GCD, and the denominators appearing in the combination can be cleared using a holomorphic common denominator.
