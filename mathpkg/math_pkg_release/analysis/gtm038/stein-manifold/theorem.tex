$X$ is called a Stein manifold if: (a) $X$ is holomorphically convex, (b) $X$ is holomorphically separable (for any two distinct points there is a holomorphic function taking different values), and (c) for every $x \in X$ there are holomorphic functions $f_1, \ldots, f_n$ on $X$ forming a coordinate system at $x$.
