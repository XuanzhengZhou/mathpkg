For any complex manifold $X$, the diagonal $D = \{(x,x) : x \in X\}$ in the product $X \times X$ is itself a complex manifold that is biholomorphically equivalent to $X$. The natural identification is given by the diagonal mapping $d(x) = (x,x)$. While the inverse $d^{-1} = p_1|_D$ (restriction of the first projection) is clearly holomorphic, showing that $d$ itself is a holomorphic mapping requires verifying that holomorphic functions on $D$ pull back to holomorphic functions on $X$, which is established by extending functions from a neighborhood of $D$ to a neighborhood in $X \times X$.
