The Continuity Theorem (Kontinuitätssatz) is a fundamental extension principle in several complex variables. Given a domain $B$ and a polydisc $\tilde{P}$ containing a Hartogs figure $\tilde{H} \subset B \cap \tilde{P}$ with connected intersection, any holomorphic function on $B$ extends uniquely to a holomorphic function on $B \cup \tilde{P}$. The proof relies on the unique extension property of the Hartogs figure and the identity theorem to ensure the piecewise-defined extension is well-defined and holomorphic.
