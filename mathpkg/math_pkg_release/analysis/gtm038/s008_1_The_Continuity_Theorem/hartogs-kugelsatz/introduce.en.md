The Kugelsatz (Hartogs' ball theorem) is a cornerstone of several complex variables. It states that for $n \geq 2$, if $K$ is a compact subset of a domain $G \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ and $G \setminus K$ remains connected, then every holomorphic function on $G \setminus K$ extends uniquely to a holomorphic function on the whole domain $G$. This starkly contrasts with one complex variable, where isolated singularities are genuine. The proof is substantially more difficult than that of Theorem 1.3 and uses the Bochner-Martinelli integral formula.
