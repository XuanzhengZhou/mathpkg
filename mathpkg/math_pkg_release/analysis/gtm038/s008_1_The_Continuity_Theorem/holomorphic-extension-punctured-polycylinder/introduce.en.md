Theorem 1.3 states that for $n \geq 2$, a holomorphic function defined on a polycylinder $P$ minus a smaller closed polycylinder $\bar{P}_{r^0}$ extends uniquely to the entire polycylinder $P$. This is a special case of the Kugelsatz and a concrete instance of the Hartogs phenomenon: in several complex variables, holomorphic functions automatically extend across "holes" that are not too large. The proof uses a connectedness argument via torus paths and the Continuity Theorem.
