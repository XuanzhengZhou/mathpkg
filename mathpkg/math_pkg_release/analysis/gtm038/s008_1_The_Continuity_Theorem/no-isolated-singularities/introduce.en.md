Theorem 1.4 states that in $\mathbb{C}^n$ for $n \geq 2$, a holomorphic function defined on a region minus a single point extends uniquely to the whole region. This means there are no isolated singularities in several complex variables -- a stark contrast with the one-variable case where poles and essential singularities at isolated points are abundant. The proof reduces to Theorem 1.3 by choosing a suitable polycylinder around the point.
