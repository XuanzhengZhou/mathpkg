**Proof.** Let : U \to \mathbb{C}^n$ be holomorphic with (\mathfrak{z}_0) \neq 0$ (where $ is the complex Jacobian determinant). Then the real Jacobian has determinant $|J_f|^2 \neq 0$, so by the real inverse function theorem, $ is a local diffeomorphism. Since $ is holomorphic, ^{-1}$ satisfies the Cauchy-Riemann equations (the inverse of a complex linear isomorphism is complex linear), hence ^{-1}$ is holomorphic. $\square$
