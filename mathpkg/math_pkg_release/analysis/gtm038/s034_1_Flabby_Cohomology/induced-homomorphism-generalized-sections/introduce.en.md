A sheaf homomorphism $\varphi: \mathcal{S}_1 \to \mathcal{S}_2$ induces a natural map on generalized sections by post-composition: each generalized section $s$ is sent to $\varphi \circ s$. This operation yields an $R$-module homomorphism $\varphi_*$ between the modules of generalized sections. Together with the assignment $\mathcal{S} \mapsto \hat{\Gamma}(U,\mathcal{S})$, this defines the functorial structure examined in Theorem 1.1.
