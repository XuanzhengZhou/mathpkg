For a sheaf homomorphism $\varphi: \mathcal{S}_1 \to \mathcal{S}_2$, consider the induced maps between the flabby resolutions:
$$
W_{\ell-1}(\mathcal{S}_1) \xrightarrow{d_{\ell-1}} W_{\ell}(\mathcal{S}_1) \xrightarrow{q_1} W_{\ell}(\mathcal{S}_1) / \text{Im}(d_{\ell-1}) \xrightarrow{j_1} W_{\ell+1}(\mathcal{S}_1)
$$
$$
\downarrow W_{\ell-1}\varphi \qquad \qquad \downarrow W_{\ell}\varphi
$$
$$
W_{\ell-1}(\mathcal{S}_2) \xrightarrow{d_{\ell-1}} W_{\ell}(\mathcal{S}_2) \xrightarrow{q_2} W_{\ell}(\mathcal{S}_2) / \text{Im}(d_{\ell-1}) \xrightarrow{j_2} W_{\ell+1}(\mathcal{S}_2)
$$
This can be completed by a homomorphism
$$\psi: W_{\ell}(\mathcal{S}_1)/\text{Im}(d_{\ell-1}) \to W_{\ell}(\mathcal{S}_2)/\text{Im}(d_{\ell-1})$$
with $\psi \circ q_1 = q_2 \circ W_{\ell}\varphi$. Define $W_{\ell+1}\varphi := W\psi$. The system of homomorphisms $W_i\varphi$ is denoted by $\mathfrak{W}(\varphi)$, regarded as a homomorphism between flabby resolutions.
