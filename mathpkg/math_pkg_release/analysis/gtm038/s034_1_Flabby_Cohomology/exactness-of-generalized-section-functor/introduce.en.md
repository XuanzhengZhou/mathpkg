Theorem 1.1 of Chapter VI establishes that the assignment sending each $R$-module sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ to its $R$-module of generalized sections $\hat{\Gamma}(U,\mathcal{S})$ over a fixed open set $U$, together with the induced map $\varphi_*$ on sections, defines an exact covariant functor. This means the functor preserves exact sequences, kernels, and cokernels. The exactness property is crucial because it allows one to lift exactness from the level of sheaves to the level of generalized section modules, forming the algebraic foundation for the construction of flabby resolutions.
