Theorem 1.2(2) constructs a canonical injective sheaf homomorphism $\varepsilon: \mathcal{S} \to W(\mathcal{S})$ that embeds the original sheaf $\mathcal{S}$ into its flabby resolution $W(\mathcal{S})$. This embedding is induced by the natural inclusion of continuous sections into generalized sections and provides the first step of a flabby resolution. The injectivity is proven using the fact that if a section vanishes at the level of $W(\mathcal{S})$, its germ must already be zero in $\mathcal{S}$.
