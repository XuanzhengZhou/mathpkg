The functor $W$ not only preserves the categorical structure (it is a covariant functor) but also preserves exactness. Given a short exact sequence of $R$-module sheaves, applying $W$ yields another short exact sequence. This exactness property is the crucial technical result that makes the iterative construction of flabby resolutions possible: it ensures that at each step of the resolution, exactness is preserved, allowing cohomological information to be faithfully transferred.
