The induced sequence of global sections
$$
0 \rightarrow \Gamma(X, \Omega^p) \xrightarrow{\varepsilon} \Gamma(X, \mathcal{A}^{p,0}) \xrightarrow{d''} \Gamma(X, \mathcal{A}^{p,1}) \xrightarrow{d''} \cdots
$$
is called the **Dolbeault sequence**. This is an augmented cochain complex of $\mathbb{C}$-vector spaces. The associated cohomology groups
$$
H^{p,q}(X) := \frac{\operatorname{Ker}\bigl(\Gamma(X, \mathcal{A}^{p,q}) \xrightarrow{d''} \Gamma(X, \mathcal{A}^{p,q+1})\bigr)}{\operatorname{Im}\bigl(\Gamma(X, \mathcal{A}^{p,q-1}) \xrightarrow{d''} \Gamma(X, \mathcal{A}^{p,q})\bigr)}
$$
are called the **Dolbeault groups** (or Dolbeault cohomology groups) of $X$.
