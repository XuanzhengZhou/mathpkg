When an analytic set $A \subset X$ is free of singularities, it inherits a natural complex manifold structure from the ambient complex manifold $X$. The definition of holomorphicity on $A$ is given by local extension: a function on $A$ is holomorphic if near every point it can be extended to a holomorphic function on an open subset of $X$. This definition ensures that the structure sheaf of $A$ is the restriction of the structure sheaf of $X$, making the inclusion $j_A: A \hookrightarrow X$ a holomorphic mapping.
