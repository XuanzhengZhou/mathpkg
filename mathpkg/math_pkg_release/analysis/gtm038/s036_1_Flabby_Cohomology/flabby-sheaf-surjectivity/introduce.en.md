A flabby sheaf is one whose restriction maps are surjective for all open sets. This theorem shows that flabbiness is the key injectivity-like property on the level of global sections: if $\mathcal{S}'$ is flabby, then any global section of $\mathcal{S}''$ lifts to a global section of $\mathcal{S}$. This property is fundamental for sheaf cohomology theory and demonstrates why flabby sheaves are acyclic with respect to the global sections functor.
