The canonical double complex $C_{ij} = C^j(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S}_i)$ combines the Čech complex of the covering $\mathfrak{U}$ with a flabby resolution $\mathcal{S} \rightarrow \mathcal{S}_{\bullet}$ in a single bigraded structure. The maps $\varphi_{ij}$ and $\psi_{ij}$ provide homomorphisms between the cohomology groups $H_{ij}$ that shift the bidegree by $(-1,+1)$ and $(+1,-1)$ respectively. Their construction relies crucially on the flabbiness of the sheaves $\mathcal{S}_i$ and forms the algebraic heart of the Čech-derived functor cohomology comparison theorem.
