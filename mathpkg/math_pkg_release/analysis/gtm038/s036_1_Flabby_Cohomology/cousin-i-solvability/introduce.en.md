This theorem translates the solvability of a Cousin I distribution into a cohomological condition. The differences of the local meromorphic data define a $1$-cocycle $\gamma$ in the Čech complex with values in the structure sheaf $\mathfrak{H}$. The distribution admits a global meromorphic solution precisely when $\gamma$ is a coboundary, i.e., when its cohomology class vanishes in $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathfrak{H})$. This connects the classical Mittag-Leffler and Cousin problems to the vanishing of the first sheaf cohomology group $H^1(X, \mathfrak{H})$.
