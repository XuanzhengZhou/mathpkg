This theorem is central to sheaf cohomology: flabby sheaves are acyclic with respect to the Čech cohomology functor. For any open covering $\mathfrak{U}$, all higher Čech cohomology groups $H^{\ell}(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{S})$ with $\ell \geqslant 1$ vanish. The proof proceeds by induction on $\ell$, using an argument similar in spirit to the Poincaré lemma: cochains can be locally trivialized, and the flabbiness condition allows these local trivializations to be patched together via induction.
