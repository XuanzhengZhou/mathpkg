**Proof.** The second Cousin problem (the Mittag-Leffler problem) asks: given a Cousin-II distribution (a 1-cocycle of meromorphic functions), does there exist a global meromorphic function with the prescribed principal parts? On a Stein manifold, ^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*) = 0$, which implies the solvability of the second Cousin problem. The proof uses the exponential sheaf sequence /usr/bin/zsh \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathcal{O} \xrightarrow{\exp(2\pi i \cdot)} \mathcal{O}^* \to 0$, which gives the long exact cohomology sequence $\cdots \to H^1(X, \mathcal{O}) \to H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*) \to H^2(X, \mathbb{Z}) \to \cdots$. On a Stein manifold, ^1(X, \mathcal{O}) = 0$, so the solvability reduces to checking a topological obstruction in ^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$. $\square$
