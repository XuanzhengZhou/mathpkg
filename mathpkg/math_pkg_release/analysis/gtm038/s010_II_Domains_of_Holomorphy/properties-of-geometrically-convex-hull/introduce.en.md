Theorem 3.1 establishes that the geometrically convex hull is a closure operator on subsets of $\mathbb{R}^2$. It satisfies extensivity, monotonicity, idempotence, and preserves boundedness. These properties motivate the analogous properties of the holomorphically convex hull (Theorem 3.3).
