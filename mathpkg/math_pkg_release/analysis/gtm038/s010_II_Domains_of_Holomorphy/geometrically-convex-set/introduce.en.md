Definition 3.1 introduces geometric convexity in $\mathbb{R}^2$ as a stepping stone toward holomorphic convexity. A set $M$ is geometrically convex if every point outside $M$ can be separated from $M$ by a line, with $M$ lying entirely in one closed half-plane.
