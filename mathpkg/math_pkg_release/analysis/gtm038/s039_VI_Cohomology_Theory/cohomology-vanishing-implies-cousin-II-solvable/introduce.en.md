Theorem 4.9 strengthens the solvability criterion for Cousin II distributions by requiring the vanishing of all higher cohomology groups $H^\ell(X, \mathcal{O})$ for $\ell \geqslant 1$, rather than merely $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*)$. The statement in the source text is truncated, but the intended conclusion is that under this stronger vanishing hypothesis, every Cousin II distribution on $X$ is solvable. This relates to the theory of Stein manifolds, where Theorem B ensures the vanishing of higher cohomology of coherent analytic sheaves.
