Theorem 4.8 establishes a fundamental connection between the vanishing of sheaf cohomology groups and the solvability of the classical Cousin problems on complex manifolds. The first cohomology group $H^1(X, \mathcal{O})$ with coefficients in the structure sheaf controls Cousin I (additive) distributions, while $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*)$ with coefficients in the multiplicative sheaf of non-vanishing holomorphic functions controls Cousin II (multiplicative) distributions. This theorem provides the cohomological obstruction framework: a Cousin distribution is solvable precisely when its associated cohomology class vanishes.
