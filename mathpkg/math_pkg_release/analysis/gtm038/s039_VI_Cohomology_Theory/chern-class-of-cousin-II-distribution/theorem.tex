Let $h \in Z^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$ be the cocycle of a Cousin II distribution $(U_i, f_i)_{i \in I}$, let $h$ denote the corresponding cohomology class in $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*)$, and let

$$\partial: H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*) \to H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$$

be the boundary homomorphism from the long exact cohomology sequence of

$$0 \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathcal{O} \to \mathcal{O}^* \to 0.$$

Then

$$c(h) := \partial(h) \in H^2(X, \mathbb{Z})$$

is called the \text{Chern class} of $h$ (respectively of the Cousin II distribution $(U_i, f_i)_{i \in I}$).
