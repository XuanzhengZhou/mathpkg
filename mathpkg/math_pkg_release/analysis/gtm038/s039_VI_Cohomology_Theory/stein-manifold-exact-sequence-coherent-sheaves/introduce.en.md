Theorem 5.3 is a main result of the cohomology theory on Stein manifolds. It states that the global sections functor $\Gamma(X, -)$ is exact on the category of coherent analytic sheaves over a Stein manifold $X$. Given a short exact sequence of coherent sheaves, the induced sequence of global sections remains exact, and the global sections of the quotient sheaf are canonically isomorphic to the quotient of the global sections. This theorem, together with Theorem B (vanishing of $H^1$ for coherent sheaves on Stein manifolds), forms the foundation of Cartan's Theorems A and B.
