**1. Holomorphic separability.** If $x_0 \in U$, then there exist holomorphic functions $f_1, \ldots, f_\ell$ on $U_1$ such that $x_0$ is an isolated point in

$$\{x \in U_1 : f_1(x) = \cdots = f_\ell(x) = 0\}.$$

Then the restricted functions $f_1|_U, \ldots, f_\ell|_U$ are holomorphic on $U$, and $x_0$ is also isolated in

$$\{x \in U : f_1(x) = \cdots = f_\ell(x) = 0\}.$$

Therefore $U$ is holomorphically separable.

**2. Holomorphic convexity.** Let $K \subset U$ be compact. Then $K$ is also compact in $U_1$, so its holomorphically convex hull $\hat{K}_{U_1}$ in $U_1$ is compact. Since $\hat{K}_U \subset \hat{K}_{U_1} \cap U$ and $\hat{K}_{U_1}$ is compact in $X$, it follows that $\hat{K}_U$ is relatively compact in $U$, establishing holomorphic convexity of $U$.
