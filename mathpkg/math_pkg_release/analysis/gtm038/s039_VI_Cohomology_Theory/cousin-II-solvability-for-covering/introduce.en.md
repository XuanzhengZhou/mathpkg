This corollary provides a sufficient condition for the solvability of Cousin II distributions relative to a specific covering $\mathfrak{U}$ of a complex manifold. If the first sheaf cohomology group $H^1(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{O}^*)$ of the covering with coefficients in the sheaf of non-vanishing holomorphic functions vanishes, then every Cousin II distribution subordinate to $\mathfrak{U}$ admits a global solution. This is a localized version of the more general criterion given in Theorem 4.8, which concerns vanishing of $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}^*)$ on the entire manifold $X$.
