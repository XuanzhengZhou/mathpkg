Given paired linear spaces $E$ and $F$, a family $\mathcal{A}$ of $w(F,E)$-bounded subsets of $F$ induces a locally convex topology $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ on $E$, called the topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{A}$. This topology transports the standard topology of uniform convergence from the algebraic dual $F'$ back to $E$ via the canonical embedding $T \colon E \to F'$. Its local base consists of non-zero scalar multiples of finite intersections of polars of members of $\mathcal{A}$, and convergence of nets is characterized by uniform convergence of the images under $T$ on each member of $\mathcal{A}$.
