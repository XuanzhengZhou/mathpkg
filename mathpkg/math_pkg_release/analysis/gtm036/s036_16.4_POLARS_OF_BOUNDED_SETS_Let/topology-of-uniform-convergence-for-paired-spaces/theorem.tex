Let $E$ and $F$ be paired linear spaces. Suppose that $\mathcal{A}$ is a family of $w(F,E)$-bounded subsets of $F$ and that $T$ is the canonical map of $E$ into the algebraic dual $F'$ of $F$. The topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{A}$ for $E$, denoted by $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$, is defined as follows: a subset of $E$ is open relative to $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ if and only if the set is the inverse image under $T$ of a subset of $F'$ which is open relative to the topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{A}$. The space $(E,\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}})$ is a locally convex space, and non-zero scalar multiples of finite intersections of polars of members of $\mathcal{A}$ form a local base. A net in $E$ converges to a limit relative to $\mathcal{T}_{\mathcal{A}}$ if and only if its image under $T$ converges to the image of the limit uniformly on members of $\mathcal{A}$.
