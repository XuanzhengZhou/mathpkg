For paired linear spaces $E$ and $F$ with the weak topology $w(E,F)$, boundedness of a subset can be characterized via its polar. Specifically, a subset $A$ of $E$ is $w(E,F)$-bounded precisely when its polar $A^{o}$ is radial (absorbing) at the origin in $F$. Conversely, the polar $A^{o}$ is $w(F,E)$-bounded exactly when the $w(E,F)$-closed convex circled extension of $A$ is radial at zero. This theorem provides a fundamental link between the boundedness of a set and the absorbing properties of its polar in the dual pairing.
