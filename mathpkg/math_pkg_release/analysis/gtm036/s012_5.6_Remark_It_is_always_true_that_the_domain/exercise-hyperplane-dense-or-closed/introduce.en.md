This exercise asks to prove that every hyperplane in a linear topological space is either dense or closed. A hyperplane is a maximal proper linear subspace (equivalently, the null space of a nonzero linear functional). The result follows from the fact that the closure of a hyperplane is either the hyperplane itself (if closed) or the whole space (if dense).
