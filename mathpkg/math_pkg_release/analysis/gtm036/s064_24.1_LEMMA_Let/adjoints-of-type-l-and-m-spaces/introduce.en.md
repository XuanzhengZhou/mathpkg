This theorem establishes the fundamental duality relationship between $L$ spaces and $M$ spaces in the category of normed lattices. The adjoint (continuous dual) of any normed lattice is always a Banach lattice under the dual ordering. Moreover, the duality pairs $L$ and $M$ spaces: the dual of an $M$ space is an $L$ space, and conversely the dual of an $L$ space is an $M$ space possessing a unit. This result is central to the structure theory of Banach lattices and underlies the representation theorems for abstract $L$ and $M$ spaces.
