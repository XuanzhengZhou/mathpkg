This theorem gives three equivalent characterizations of lattice homomorphisms among linear functionals on a vector lattice. A positive functional $f$ is a lattice homomorphism precisely when it sends disjoint positive elements to real numbers with zero meet, or equivalently when every positive functional $g$ dominated by $f$ (i.e., $0 \leq g \leq f$) is necessarily a scalar multiple of $f$. This result plays a fundamental role in the duality theory of normed lattices and the classification of $L$ and $M$ spaces.
