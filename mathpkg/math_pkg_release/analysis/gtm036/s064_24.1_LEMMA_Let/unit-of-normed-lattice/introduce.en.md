In a normed lattice (a vector lattice equipped with a lattice-compatible norm), the unit is defined as the largest element of the unit sphere, whenever such an element exists. The presence or absence of a unit is a key structural distinction between $M$ spaces (which possess a unit) and general Banach lattices. An $M$ space with unit is precisely the dual of an $L$ space by Theorem 24.3.
