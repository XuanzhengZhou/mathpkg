A hyperplane in a real linear space $E$ is a translate of a maximal proper subspace. Equivalently, it is the set $f^{-1}[t] = \{x : f(x) = t\}$ for some nonzero linear functional $f$ and real number $t$. Hyperplanes are the maximal proper linear manifolds: they are contained in no linear manifold other than $E$ itself. Each hyperplane is exactly the intersection of the two complementary half-spaces $\{x : f(x) \geq t\}$ and $\{x : f(x) \leq t\}$.
