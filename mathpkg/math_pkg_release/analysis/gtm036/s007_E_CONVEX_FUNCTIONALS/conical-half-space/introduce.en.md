A conical half-space is a half-space that is also a cone, meaning it contains the origin and is closed under multiplication by non-negative scalars. Such sets are precisely those of the form $\{x : f(x) \geq 0\}$ for some nonzero linear functional $f$. Conical half-spaces are fundamental in the theory of separation of convex sets by hyperplanes and in the characterization of proper cones that are half-spaces.
