A proper cone in a real linear space is a cone that is neither empty nor the whole space. Proper cones are the building blocks of vector orderings (where the positive cone must be proper) and play a central role in separation theorems. The definition recalls a key property: the radial kernel of any convex set is itself convex, and half-open segments from a convex set point to a radial kernel point lie entirely in the radial kernel.
