A half-space in a real linear space is a set of the form $\{x : f(x) \geq t\}$ or $\{x : f(x) \leq t\}$ where $f$ is a nonzero linear functional and $t$ is a real number. These two complementary half-spaces intersect in the hyperplane $f^{-1}[t]$, which is a translate of the null space (kernel) of $f$. Half-spaces are the fundamental building blocks for the geometric theory of convex sets and separation theorems.
