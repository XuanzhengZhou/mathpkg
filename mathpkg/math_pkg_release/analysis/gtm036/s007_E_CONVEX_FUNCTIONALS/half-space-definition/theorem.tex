If $f$ is a non-identically-zero linear functional on a real linear space $E$ and $t$ is any real number, each of the sets
$$
\{x \in E : f(x) \geq t\}, \qquad \{x \in E : f(x) \leq t\}
$$
is a half-space, and the pair are complementary half-spaces. Their intersection,
$$
f^{-1}[t] = \{x \in E : f(x) = t\},
$$
is a linear manifold; it is the translate of the null space of $f$ by any vector $x$ such that $f(x) = t$.
