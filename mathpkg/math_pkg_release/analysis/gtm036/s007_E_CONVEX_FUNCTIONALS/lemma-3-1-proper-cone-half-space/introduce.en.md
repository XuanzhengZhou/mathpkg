Lemma 3.1 provides a geometric characterization of when a proper cone in a real linear space is actually a half-space (and therefore a conical half-space). The criterion requires two conditions: the cone must have a nonempty radial kernel, and the cone together with its negative must cover the entire space. This lemma is a crucial stepping stone toward the separation theorems for convex sets.
