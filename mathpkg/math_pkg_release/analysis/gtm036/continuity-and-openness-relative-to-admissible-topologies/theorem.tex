Let $E$ and $F$, and $G$ and $H$, be paired linear spaces, let $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ be admissible families of subsets of $F$ and $H$, respectively, and let $T$ be a linear transformation of $E$ into $G$ with dual $T^{\prime}$. Then $T$ is continuous relative to the $\mathcal{A}$-topology on $E$ and the $\mathcal{B}$-topology on $G$ if and only if $T^{\prime}[B] \in \mathcal{A}$ for each $B \in \mathcal{B}$.
