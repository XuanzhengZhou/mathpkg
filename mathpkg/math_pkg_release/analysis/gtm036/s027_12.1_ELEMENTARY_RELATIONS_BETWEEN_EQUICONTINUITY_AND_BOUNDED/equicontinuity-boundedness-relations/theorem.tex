Let $F$ be a family of linear functions, each from a linear topological space $E$ to a linear topological space $H$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The family $F$ is equicontinuous.
\item[(ii)] The family $F$ is uniformly bounded on each bounded set. \\
$\Leftrightarrow$ $F$ is bounded relative to the topology of uniform convergence on bounded sets.
\item[(iii)] The family $F$ is uniformly bounded on a set $A$. \\
$\Leftrightarrow$ The set $F[A]$ is bounded. \\
$\Leftrightarrow$ $F$ is bounded relative to the topology of uniform convergence on $A$. \\
$\Leftrightarrow$ For each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $H$, the set $\bigcap\{f^{-1}[V] : f \in F\}$ absorbs $A$.
\item[(iv)] The family $F$ is pointwise bounded on a set $A$. \\
$\Leftrightarrow$ For each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $H$, the set $\bigcap\{f^{-1}[V] : f \in F\}$ absorbs each point of $A$.
\end{enumerate}

The condition (i) implies each of the conditions listed in (ii), and each of the conditions in (iii) implies each of the conditions in (iv).
