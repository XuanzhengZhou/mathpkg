The Uniform Boundedness Theorem provides conditions under which pointwise boundedness of a family of continuous linear maps on a "nice" set (convex, circled, bounded, sequentially closed, and sequentially complete) implies uniform boundedness on that set. This extends the Banach-Steinhaus principle to settings where equicontinuity may not hold.
