Proposition 12.1 provides a geometrically clean proof of the Banach-Steinhaus theorem using the concept of a barrel. The key observation is that for any neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in the target space, the intersection of all inverse images $\bigcap\{f^{-1}[V] : f \in F\}$ is a barrel in $E$: it is closed (intersection of closed sets), circled, convex, and absorbs points (by pointwise boundedness, Theorem 12.1(iv)). Since $E$ is of second category, every barrel is a neighborhood of $0$, and this immediately yields equicontinuity. This barrel-based proof streamlines the original argument and motivates the definition of barrelled spaces.
