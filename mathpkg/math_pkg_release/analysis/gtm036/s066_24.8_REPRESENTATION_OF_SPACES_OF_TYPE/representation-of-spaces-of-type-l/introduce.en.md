Every $L$-space (abstract $L$-space, a Banach lattice whose norm is additive on the positive cone) can be represented concretely as a sublattice of the space of regular Borel measures on its spectrum. The spectrum here is that of its adjoint $E^*$, which is an $M$-space isomorphic to $C(X)$. The representation is an isometric lattice isomorphism, embedding the abstract $L$-space into the space of signed regular Borel measures on the compact Hausdorff space $X$.
