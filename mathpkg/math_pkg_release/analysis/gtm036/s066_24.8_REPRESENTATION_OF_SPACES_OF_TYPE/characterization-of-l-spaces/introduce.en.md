This theorem gives a precise characterization of $L$-spaces in terms of measures on the spectrum of their adjoint. An $L$-space is not merely isometric to a sublattice of regular Borel measures, but to exactly the subspace of signed regular Borel measures that vanish on every Borel set of the first category. The proof proceeds through a sequence of lemmas establishing the order completeness of $L$-spaces, the topological properties of the spectrum, and a domination argument using the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
