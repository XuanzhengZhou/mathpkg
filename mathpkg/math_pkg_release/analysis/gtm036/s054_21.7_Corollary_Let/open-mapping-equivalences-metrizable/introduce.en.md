For a continuous linear transformation $T \colon E \to F$ where $E$ is complete metrizable and $F$ is metrizable, five properties are equivalent: relative openness, weak relative openness, closedness of the range, weak$^*$ relative openness of the adjoint, and weak$^*$ closedness of the range of the adjoint. The proof uses the open mapping theorem, the fact that metrizable spaces are Mackey spaces, and results about the relationship between a transformation and its adjoint (Theorems 21.6 and 21.7). The theorem is described as, in substance, the best known result of its type on openness of mappings.
