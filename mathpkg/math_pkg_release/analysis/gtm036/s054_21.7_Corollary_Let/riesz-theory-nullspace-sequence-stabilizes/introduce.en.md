For a completely continuous operator $S = \lambda I - T$ on a linear topological space, the ascending sequence of nullspaces $S^{-r}[0]$ eventually stabilizes: there exists an integer $n \geq 0$ after which all inclusions become equalities. The proof proceeds by contradiction using the Riesz closed subspace lemma (part (a)): if stabilization fails, one constructs an infinite sequence of appropriately separated points that contradicts the total boundedness of the image of a compact neighborhood under $T$.
