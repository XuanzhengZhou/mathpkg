In a vector lattice, the join $x \vee y$ is the unique least upper bound of two elements $x$ and $y$. It satisfies $x \leq x \vee y$, $y \leq x \vee y$, and if $z$ is any element with $x \leq z$ and $y \leq z$, then $x \vee y \leq z$. The join is translation-invariant: $(x + z) \vee (y + z) = x \vee y + z$. Together with the meet operation $x \wedge y$, it forms the foundation of vector lattice arithmetic.
