This lemma gives a geometric criterion for normality of a cone in a pseudo-normed space. A cone $C$ is normal relative to the pseudo-norm if the intersection of the two translates $(S + C)$ and $(S - C)$ of the unit sphere $S$ is bounded. Intuitively, this means the angle between positive vectors, measured in terms of the distance, is bounded away from $\pi$. The lemma is a key tool connecting order-theoretic and topological properties of cones.
