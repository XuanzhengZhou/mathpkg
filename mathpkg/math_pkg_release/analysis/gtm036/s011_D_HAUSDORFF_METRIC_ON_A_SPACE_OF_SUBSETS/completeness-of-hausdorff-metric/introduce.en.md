When the underlying metric space $E$ is complete, the hyperspace $\mathcal{E}$ of all closed non-void subsets equipped with the Hausdorff metric is also complete. The proof constructs the limit set $A$ as the collection of all limits of Cauchy sequences $\{x_n\}$ in $E$ where each $x_n$ belongs to the corresponding set $A_n$ of a Cauchy sequence $\{A_n\}$ in $\mathcal{E}$. One then shows that $d(A, A_n) \to 0$, establishing that $A$ is the limit of $\{A_n\}$ in the Hausdorff metric.
