Let $\mathcal{U}$ and $\mathcal{V}$ be families satisfying the conditions (i) to (iv) of Theorem 5.1.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If each member of $\mathcal{U}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{V}$, then the topology determined by $\mathcal{V}$ is stronger (finer) than the topology determined by $\mathcal{U}$. The converse is also true.
\item[(b)] If each member of $\mathcal{U}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{V}$ and each member of $\mathcal{V}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{U}$, then $\mathcal{U}$ and $\mathcal{V}$ determine the same topology. In this case the families $\mathcal{U}$ and $\mathcal{V}$ are said to be equivalent.
\end{enumerate}
