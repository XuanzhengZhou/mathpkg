Two local bases generate the same vector topology exactly when each member of one base contains a member of the other, and vice versa. More generally, the inclusion relation between local bases directly reflects the comparison of the topologies they generate: if every member of $\mathcal{U}$ swallows a member of $\mathcal{V}$, then the topology from $\mathcal{V}$ is finer. This provides a convenient way to compare and identify vector topologies without directly examining open sets.
