For linear maps between linear topological spaces, continuity is a global property that can be checked at a single point. Specifically, if a linear map is continuous at any point (in particular at the origin), then it is automatically continuous everywhere. This follows because continuity at a point $x$ means that for each neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in the codomain there is a neighborhood $U$ of $0$ in the domain such that $T[U] \subset V$, which is independent of $x$ due to linearity and translation invariance.
