A linear topological space (also called a topological vector space) is a linear space equipped with a topology that makes the algebraic operations continuous. Specifically, the addition map $(x, y) \mapsto x + y$ from $E \times E$ to $E$ and the scalar multiplication map $(a, x) \mapsto ax$ from $K \times E$ to $E$ must both be continuous with respect to the product topologies. This compatibility between the algebraic and topological structures is the foundational concept for the entire theory developed in Kelley and Namioka's book.
