Theorem 5.4 gives six equivalent characterizations of continuity for a non-zero linear functional on a linear topological space. Remarkably, continuity can be detected purely algebraically (the null space being closed or not dense), topologically (boundedness on a neighborhood of zero), or through the image of open sets. This result is fundamental because linear functionals are the simplest linear maps, and their continuity properties govern much of the duality theory of topological vector spaces.
