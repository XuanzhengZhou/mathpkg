Suppose $E$ is a linear topological space. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] Translation by any member $x$ of $E$, given by $y \mapsto x + y$, is a homeomorphism of $E$ onto itself.
\item[(b)] Multiplication by a non-zero scalar $a$ is a homeomorphism of $E$ onto itself.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently, if a set $A$ is open (resp.\ closed), then $x + A$ and $aA$ are also open (resp.\ closed) for each $x \in E$ and each non-zero scalar $a$.
