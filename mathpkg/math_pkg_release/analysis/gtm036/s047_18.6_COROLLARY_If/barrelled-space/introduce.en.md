A barrelled space (also called a disk space or tonnelé space) is a locally convex linear topological space in which every barrel is a neighborhood of zero. Barrelled spaces are important because the Banach-Steinhaus theorem (theorem 12.3) applies to them. Every locally convex space of the second category, and in particular every locally convex complete pseudo-metrizable space, is a barrelled space.
