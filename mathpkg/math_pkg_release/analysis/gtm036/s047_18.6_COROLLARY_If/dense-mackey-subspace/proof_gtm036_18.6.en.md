Suppose $E$ is a locally convex space, $F$ is a dense subspace which with the relativized topology is a Mackey space, and $C$ is a $w(E^*,E)$-compact convex circled subset of $E^*$. It must be shown that $C_0$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$, and since $F$ is a dense subspace, it is sufficient to show $C_0 \cap F$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $F$. But if $R$ is the map of $E^*$ into $F^*$ which carries a linear functional $f$ into its restriction to $F$, then $R$ is continuous with respect to the weak* topologies, as may be seen directly. Hence $R[C]$ is $w(F^*,F)$-compact convex circled, and $R[C]_0 = C_0 \cap F$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $F$.
