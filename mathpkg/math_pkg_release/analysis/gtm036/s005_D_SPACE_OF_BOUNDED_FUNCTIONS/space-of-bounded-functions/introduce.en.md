The space $B(S)$ consists of all bounded scalar-valued functions on an infinite set $S$, equipped with the usual pointwise vector space operations. This space serves as a fundamental example in functional analysis and linear topological spaces, particularly for studying dimension in infinite-dimensional settings. When $S$ is endowed with additional structure (e.g., a topology), subspaces of $B(S)$ such as continuous bounded functions become important. The dimension of $B(S)$ depends on the cardinality of $S$ and the scalar field, yielding the formula $\dim B(S) = c^s$ where $s = |S|$ and $c$ is the cardinality of the scalars.
