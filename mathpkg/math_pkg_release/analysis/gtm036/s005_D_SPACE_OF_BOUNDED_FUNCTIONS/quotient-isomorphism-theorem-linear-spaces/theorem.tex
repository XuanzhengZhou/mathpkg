Let $\mathcal{J} : G \to F$ be a linear mapping of one linear space $G$ into another linear space $F$. Let $N \subseteq G$ and $M \subseteq F$ be subspaces such that $\mathcal{J}(N) \subseteq M$. Let $Q_N : G \to G/N$ and $Q_M : F \to F/M$ be the canonical quotient maps. Define $I$ by the composition
$$
I = Q_M \circ \mathcal{J} \circ Q_N^{-1}.
$$
Then $I : G/N \to F/M$ is a well-defined linear isomorphism, provided that $\mathcal{J}$ induces a bijection between the relevant coset spaces.
