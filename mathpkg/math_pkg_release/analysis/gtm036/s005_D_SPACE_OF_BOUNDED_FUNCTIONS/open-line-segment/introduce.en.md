The open line segment $(x:y)$ consists of all strict convex combinations of $x$ and $y$, excluding the endpoints. It is the line segment $[x:y]$ with the endpoints removed. Open segments are essential in the study of convexity: for instance, a point $z$ is an extreme point of a convex set $A$ if it cannot be expressed as a point of any open segment $(x:y)$ with $x, y \in A$ and $x \neq y$.
