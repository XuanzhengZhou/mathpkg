The Krein-Milman theorem states that every convex compact subset of a locally convex Hausdorff space is the closed convex hull of its extreme points. Moreover, every closed support (face) of the compact convex set contains at least one extreme point. This theorem is foundational in functional analysis and convex geometry, establishing that extreme points are the essential building blocks of convex compact sets.
