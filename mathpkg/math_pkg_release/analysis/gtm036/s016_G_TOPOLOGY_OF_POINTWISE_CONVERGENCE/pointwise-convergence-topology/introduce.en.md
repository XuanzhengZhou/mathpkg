The topology of pointwise convergence on the space $E = C([0,1])$ of continuous real-valued functions is defined by the local base consisting of sets $U_{T,\varepsilon}$ indexed by finite subsets $T$ of $[0,1]$ and positive $\varepsilon$. Each $U_{T,\varepsilon}$ consists of functions whose absolute value is bounded by $\varepsilon$ at all points of $T$. This topology is locally convex and Hausdorff, and is strictly weaker than the uniform norm topology on $E$.
