Let $S$ be the interval $[0,1]$ and $E$ the set of continuous real valued functions on $S$. For each finite subset $T$ of $S$, and each $\varepsilon > 0$ let
$$
U_{T,\varepsilon} = \{ x : |x(s)| < \varepsilon \text{ for all } s \in T \}.
$$
The sets $\{ U_{T,\varepsilon} : \varepsilon > 0,\; T \subset S,\; T \text{ finite} \}$ form a local base for a locally convex Hausdorff topology on $E$, called the topology of pointwise convergence.
