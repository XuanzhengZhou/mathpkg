There exists a linear topological space in which every Cauchy sequence converges to some point, but the space is not complete. Specifically, let $E = \prod_{t \in (0,1)} \mathbb{R}$ with the product topology (topology of pointwise convergence), and let $F \subset E$ be the subspace of all functions which vanish except at a countable number of points. Then every Cauchy sequence in $F$ converges in $F$, but $F$ is dense in $E$ and not complete.
