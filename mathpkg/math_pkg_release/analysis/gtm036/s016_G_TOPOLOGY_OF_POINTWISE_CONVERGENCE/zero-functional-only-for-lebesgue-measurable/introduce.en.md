On the space $S([0,1], \mu)$ of Lebesgue-measurable functions with the topology of convergence in measure, the only continuous linear functional is the identically zero functional. This striking result highlights the pathological nature of this topology: despite being a complete pseudo-metrizable topological vector space, it admits no non-trivial continuous dual. The proof uses a clever inductive construction of functions to show any non-zero linear functional would be unbounded.
