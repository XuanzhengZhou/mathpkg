On a finite measure space $(X, \mathcal{S}, \mu)$, the space $S(X, \mu)$ of all real-valued measurable functions can be topologized using the sets $U_n$ as a local base at $0$. Each $U_n$ consists of functions that are "small in measure": the set where $|f|$ exceeds $1/n$ has measure less than $1/n$. This topology captures the notion of convergence in measure.
