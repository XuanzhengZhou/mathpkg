An atom in a measure space is a measurable set of positive finite measure that cannot be partitioned into two measurable subsets of strictly smaller positive measure. In other words, every measurable subset of an atom either has full measure or zero measure. Atoms are the measure-theoretic analogue of points in a discrete space and play a crucial role in the structure of $L^p$ spaces.
