When a measure space contains an atom $A$, integration over $A$ defines a non-zero continuous linear functional on $L^p$ for $0 < p < 1$. The continuity follows from the fact that on an atom, pointwise behavior controls the $L^p$ norm, and the integral is bounded by $\mu(A)^{1-1/p} \|f\|_p$ (for atoms, the $L^p$ and $L^1$ topologies coincide up to scaling).
