The sets $U_n$ form a countable local base for a vector topology on $S(X, \mu)$, making it a pseudo-metrizable linear topological space. This topology is called the topology of convergence in measure because a sequence converges in this topology precisely when it converges in measure. The space is pseudo-metrizable (not necessarily metrizable) since functions equal almost everywhere are not separated.
