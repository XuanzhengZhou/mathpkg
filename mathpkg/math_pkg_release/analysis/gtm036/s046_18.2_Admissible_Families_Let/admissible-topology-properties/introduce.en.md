This theorem establishes the fundamental relationship between admissible families of subsets in a paired space and the admissible topologies they generate. Part (i) shows that every admissible family yields an admissible topology whose local base consists of polars of family members. Part (ii) states that replacing a weakly bounded cover with the admissible family it generates does not change the polar topology. Part (iii) characterizes pseudo-metrizability and pseudo-normability of admissible topologies in terms of countable co-bases and scalar-multiple co-bases, respectively.
