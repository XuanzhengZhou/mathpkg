The strong topology $s(E, F)$ on a paired linear space is the topology of uniform convergence on all weakly bounded subsets of the paired space. It can be characterized either by this uniform-convergence description or by the requirement that the family of weak barrels forms a local base. When applied to the adjoint $E^*$ of a locally convex space $E$, the strong topology coincides with $s(E^*, E)$ and is the strongest admissible topology for $E^*$. This definition is fundamental for duality theory in locally convex spaces.
