This theorem establishes that every finite dimensional subspace of a linear topological Hausdorff space is topologically isomorphic to a Euclidean space of the same dimension. Consequently, such subspaces inherit the completeness and closedness properties of Euclidean spaces. This result is fundamental because it shows that the topological structure of finite dimensional subspaces is uniquely determined, regardless of the ambient space.
