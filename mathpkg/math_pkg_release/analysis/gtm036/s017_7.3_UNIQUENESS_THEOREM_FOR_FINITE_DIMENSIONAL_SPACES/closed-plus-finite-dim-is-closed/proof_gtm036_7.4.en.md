Let $Q$ be the quotient map from $E$ onto $E/F$. Since $G$ is finite dimensional, $Q[G]$ is a finite dimensional subspace of $E/F$. By the uniqueness theorem for finite dimensional spaces (Theorem 7.3), $Q[G]$ is closed in $E/F$. Since $Q$ is continuous and $F + G = Q^{-1}[Q[G]]$, it follows that $F + G$ is closed in $E$.
