Let $E$ and $F$ be paired linear spaces. A vector topology $\mathcal{T}$ on $E$ is called admissible (relative to the pairing) if the canonical image of each $f \in F$ is $\mathcal{T}$-continuous. This result characterizes admissibility in terms of the weak topology $w(E,F)$: $\mathcal{T}$ is admissible precisely when it is stronger than the weak topology and admits a local base consisting of $w(E,F)$-barrels (i.e., closed, absolutely convex, absorbing sets with respect to $w(E,F)$).
