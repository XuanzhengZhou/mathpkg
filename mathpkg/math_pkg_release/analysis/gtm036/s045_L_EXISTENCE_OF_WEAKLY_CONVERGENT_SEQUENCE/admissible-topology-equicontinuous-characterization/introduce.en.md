Let $(E, \mathcal{T})$ be a locally convex space and let $E^*$ be its continuous dual. Consider the natural pairing $\langle x, f \rangle = f(x)$ between $E$ and $E^*$. Then $\mathcal{T}$ is an admissible topology relative to this pairing. Moreover, $\mathcal{T}$ coincides with the topology of uniform convergence on the family of all $\mathcal{T}$-equicontinuous subsets of $E^*$. Equivalently, $\mathcal{T}$ is the polar topology determined by the polars of all $\mathcal{T}$-neighborhoods of $0$ in $E$.
