Let $E$ be a metrizable locally convex space and $A \subseteq E$ a subset with the property that every sequence in $A$ has a weak cluster point in $E$. Under these conditions, the weak closure and the weak sequential closure of $A$ coincide: any point $x$ in the weak closure of $A$ can be realized as the weak limit of some sequence of points drawn from $A$. The proof uses a diagonal enumeration of approximating sequences together with the separability of the closed subspace spanned by them.
