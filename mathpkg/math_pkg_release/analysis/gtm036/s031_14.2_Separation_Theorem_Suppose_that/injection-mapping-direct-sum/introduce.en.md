The canonical injection $I_t$ maps a space $E_t$ into the direct sum $\sum \{E_s : s \in A\}$ by sending an element $z \in E_t$ to the tuple that equals $z$ in the $t$-th coordinate and zero in all other coordinates. Each $I_t$ is an algebraic isomorphism onto its image, allowing each factor space to be identified with the corresponding subspace of the direct sum.
