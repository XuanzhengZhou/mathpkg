The direct sum topology is the natural locally convex topology on the algebraic direct sum of a family of locally convex spaces. It is defined via the family of convex circled subsets whose preimages under each canonical injection are neighborhoods of zero in the respective factor spaces. Unlike the topology induced by the injections (which is generally not a vector topology), the direct sum topology is genuinely locally convex and serves as the correct categorical coproduct in the category of locally convex spaces.
