The canonical evaluation map from a vector lattice $E$ into its order bidual $E^{\star\star}$ preserves the lattice structure — it is a lattice homomorphism. This means that $J(x \vee y) = Jx \vee Jy$ and $J(x \wedge y) = Jx \wedge Jy$. This result is fundamental for the representation theory of vector lattices: it shows that $E$ embeds into its order bidual not just as a linear subspace, but as a sublattice.
