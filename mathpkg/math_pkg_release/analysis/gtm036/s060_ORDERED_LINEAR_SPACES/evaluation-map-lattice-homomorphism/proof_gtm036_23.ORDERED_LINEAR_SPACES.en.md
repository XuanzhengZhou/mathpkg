This result is stated in the introductory summary without proof. The full proof appears later in the chapter (see Section 24.4). The proof uses the fact that the order dual $E^\star$ consists of differences of positive functionals, and the evaluation map $Jx(f) = f(x)$ naturally respects the lattice operations because for positive functionals $f$, one has $f(x \vee y) = \sup\{f(z) : z \leq x, z \leq y\}$ when restricted to suitable domains.
