The positive cone of a vector ordering is the set of all elements $x$ satisfying $x \geq 0$. The ordering is completely determined by this cone: $x \geq y$ if and only if $x - y$ belongs to the positive cone. This establishes the natural one-to-one correspondence between cones and vector orderings, making the positive cone the central object for studying ordered linear spaces.
