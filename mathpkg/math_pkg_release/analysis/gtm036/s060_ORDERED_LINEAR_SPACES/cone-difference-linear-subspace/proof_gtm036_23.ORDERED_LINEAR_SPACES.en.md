The statement is noted as "clearly" true in the source text. Formally: for any $u_1 - v_1, u_2 - v_2 \in C - C$ and scalars $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$, we compute
$$
a(u_1 - v_1) + b(u_2 - v_2) = (au_1 + bu_2) - (av_1 + bv_2).
$$
Since $C$ is a cone, it is closed under non-negative scalar multiplication and addition. For arbitrary real scalars, one splits into positive and negative parts and uses the convexity of $C$. Thus $C - C$ is closed under linear combinations, hence a subspace. (Full proof omitted by the authors as "clearly".)
