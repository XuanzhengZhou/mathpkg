This theorem establishes the precise relationship between weak continuity of a linear map and the existence of its dual (adjoint) transformation. A linear map T admits a dual if and only if it is continuous with respect to the weak topologies induced by the respective pairings. Furthermore, the dual is itself weakly continuous, and its uniqueness depends on whether the domain space separates points of its paired space — a condition closely related to non-degeneracy of the pairing.
