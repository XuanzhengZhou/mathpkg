This proposition states James' theorem: the dual of James' space, equipped with an appropriately defined norm involving a supremum over finite sums of differences of the coordinate functionals, is actually isomorphic to the original space itself. This makes James' space a remarkable example of a Banach space that is isomorphic to its dual (and hence its bidual) yet is not reflexive under the canonical embedding.
