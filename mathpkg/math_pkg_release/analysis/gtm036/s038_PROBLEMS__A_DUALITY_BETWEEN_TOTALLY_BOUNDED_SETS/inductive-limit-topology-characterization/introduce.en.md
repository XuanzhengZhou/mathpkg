This result collects several fundamental properties of the inductive limit topology. Part (c) gives two equivalent descriptions of a local base. Part (d) states the universal mapping property: a linear map from an inductive limit is continuous precisely when its composition with each canonical map $f_t$ is continuous. Parts (e) and (f) show that both quotient topologies and direct sum topologies are special cases of inductive limits, and that every inductive limit of locally convex spaces is itself a quotient of a direct sum.
