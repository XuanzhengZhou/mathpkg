Suppose that $E$ and $F$ are paired linear spaces, that $A$ is a $w(E,F)$-bounded subset of $E$ and that $B$ is a $w(F,E)$-bounded subset of $F$. Then $A$ is totally bounded in the topology on $E$ of uniform convergence on $B$ if and only if $B$ is totally bounded in the topology on $F$ of uniform convergence on $A$.

More generally, if $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ are families of $w(E,F)$- and $w(F,E)$-bounded subsets of $E$ and $F$, then each $A \in \mathcal{A}$ is totally bounded in the topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{B}$ if and only if each $B \in \mathcal{B}$ is totally bounded in the topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{A}$.
