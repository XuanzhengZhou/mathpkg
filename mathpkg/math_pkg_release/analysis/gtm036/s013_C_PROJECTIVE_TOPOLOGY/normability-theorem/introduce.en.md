The Normability Theorem provides a clean topological characterization of pseudo-normable linear topological spaces: such a space possesses a pseudo-norm generating its topology precisely when the origin has a bounded convex neighborhood. The proof constructs the Minkowski functional of a bounded circled convex neighborhood of zero and shows it is the desired pseudo-norm. If the space is additionally Hausdorff, the pseudo-norm is actually a norm, yielding a genuine normed space.
