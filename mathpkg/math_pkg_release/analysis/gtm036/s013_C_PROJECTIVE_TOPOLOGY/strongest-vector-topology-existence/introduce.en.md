This theorem asserts that on any linear space, the family of all vector topologies has an upper bound in the lattice of topologies — a finest vector topology that is finer than every other vector topology. This strongest vector topology is obtained by taking the supremum (upper bound) of all vector topologies on the space. While the result establishes existence, the topology is typically quite fine and may not be metrizable or first-countable for infinite-dimensional spaces.
