A linear manifold (also called a flat or linear variety) is a translate of a linear subspace. While a linear subspace must contain the origin, a linear manifold can be offset by any vector. Linear manifolds are the affine analogs of subspaces and play an essential role in the separation theorems — a hyperplane is precisely a linear manifold of codimension one.
