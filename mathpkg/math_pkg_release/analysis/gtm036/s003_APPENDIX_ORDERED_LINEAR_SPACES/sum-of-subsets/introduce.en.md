The sum of two subsets $A$ and $B$ of a linear space is defined pointwise as $\{x + y : x \in A, y \in B\}$. Scalar multiplication of a set is likewise defined elementwise. These operations extend the vector space structure to the power set, forming the algebraic foundation for studying convex sets, translates of subspaces, and the Minkowski sum in topological vector spaces.
