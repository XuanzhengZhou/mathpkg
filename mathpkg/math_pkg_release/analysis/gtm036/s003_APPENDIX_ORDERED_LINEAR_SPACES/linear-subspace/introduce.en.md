A linear subspace is a nonempty subset of a linear space that is closed under addition and scalar multiplication — equivalently, closed under all finite linear combinations. Subspaces inherit the vector space structure from the ambient space. The sum of two subspaces is again a subspace, but translates of subspaces are generally only affine, not linear.
