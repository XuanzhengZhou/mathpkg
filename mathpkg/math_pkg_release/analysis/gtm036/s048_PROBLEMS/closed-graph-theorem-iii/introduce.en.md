This is a generalization of the Closed Graph Theorem to the setting of fully complete spaces. It shows that a linear mapping into a fully complete space is continuous if its graph is closed and the inverse image of each neighborhood has dense closure being a neighborhood. Furthermore, full completeness is characterized as being exactly the property needed for both this closed graph theorem and the corresponding open mapping theorem to hold.
