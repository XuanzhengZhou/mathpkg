This theorem describes the fundamental properties of the bound extension $\mathcal{T}^b$ of a locally convex topology. The bound extension is itself a bound topology sharing the same bounded sets, and is the strongest locally convex topology with that property. The result also characterizes $\mathcal{T}^b$-equicontinuity in terms of uniform boundedness on bounded sets, connecting the theory of bound spaces with equicontinuity.
