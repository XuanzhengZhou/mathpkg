Let $\mathcal{T}^b$ be the bound extension of a locally convex topology $\mathcal{T}$ for a linear space $E$. Then:

(i) $\mathcal{T}^b$ is a bound topology, and the class $\mathcal{B}$ of $\mathcal{T}$-bounded sets is identical with the class of $\mathcal{T}^b$-bounded sets; moreover, $\mathcal{T}^b$ is the strongest locally convex topology relative to which each member of $\mathcal{B}$ is bounded;

(ii) a family $F$ of linear functions on $E$ to a locally convex space $H$ is $\mathcal{T}^b$-equicontinuous if and only if $F$ is bounded relative to the topology of uniform convergence on members of $\mathcal{B}$.
