The construction of $E_p = E^p(\Omega)$ follows the general method of 22F. The space $E^p(\Omega)$ is defined as the set of all functions $x$ on $A = I \times I$ such that for each $n$, the norm $\|x\|_n = (\sum_{i,j} |x(i,j)|^p \omega_n(i,j))^{1/p}$ is finite. This space is a Fréchet space with the topology generated by the sequence of norms $\|\cdot\|_n$.

The choice of $\omega_n$ ensures that the weights grow rapidly enough along both coordinates to force a suitable Montel property. The closed subspace $F_p$ and the quotient isomorphism to $l_p$ are constructed by considering the appropriate restriction mappings on the double-indexed sequences, exploiting the specific asymptotic behavior of the weights $\omega_n(i,j)$ as $i, j \to \infty$.
