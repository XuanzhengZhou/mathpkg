This theorem establishes a kernel representation for every continuous linear operator $T$ on the space $A(U)$ of analytic functions on the open unit disk. The operator is represented as a contour integral against a kernel $B(\zeta, z)$ that is analytic in $z$, analytic in $\zeta$ outside a disk, and jointly continuous. The converse also holds: any kernel satisfying the three conditions defines a continuous linear operator. This is a fundamental result in the theory of analytic function spaces, connecting operator theory with complex analysis.
