Let $E$ be a linear topological space and suppose $E^*$, with the strong topology, is semi-reflexive. Consider any strongly bounded subset $B$ of $E^*$; then $B$ is bounded for the strong topology $\beta(E^*, E)$. Since $E^*$ is semi-reflexive, every bounded weakly closed subset of $E^*$ is weakly compact. In particular, the convex circled hull of $B$ (or more precisely, the weak closure of the convex circled hull of $B$) is a convex circled subset of $E^*$ and is weakly $(w(E^*, E^{**}))$ compact. Consequently, it is also weak$^*$ (that is, $w(E^*, E)$) compact. By the Banach-Alaoglu-type reasoning, such a subset is equicontinuous relative to the Mackey topology $m(E, E^*)$. Thus every strongly bounded subset of $E^*$ is equicontinuous for $m(E, E^*)$, which means precisely that $E$, with the Mackey topology, is evaluable.
