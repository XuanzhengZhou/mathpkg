If the strong dual $E^*$ of a linear topological space $E$ is semi-reflexive, then $E$ equipped with its Mackey topology $m(E, E^*)$ is evaluable. This means every strongly bounded subset of the adjoint is equicontinuous. The result follows from the fact that convex circled subsets of $E^*$ are weakly compact and hence weak$^*$ compact, making them equicontinuous for the Mackey topology. This corollary provides a convenient sufficient condition for evaluability in terms of the dual space.
