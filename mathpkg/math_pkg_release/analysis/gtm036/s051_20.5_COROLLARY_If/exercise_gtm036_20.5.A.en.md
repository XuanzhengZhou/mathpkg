\textbf{A. EXAMPLE OF A NON-EVALUABLE SPACE.}

Let $F$ be a non-reflexive Banach space and let $E = F^*$ with the topology $m(F^*, F)$. Then $E^* = F$ and the strong topology on $E^*$ is the norm topology (for the $w(F^*, F)$-bounded sets are norm bounded, by 18.5(iii)). Thus:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $E$ is a Mackey space which is not evaluable, and so neither barrelled nor bound;
\item[(b)] in $E^*$, there is a convex weak$^*$ closed strongly bounded set which is not weak$^*$ compact (cf.\ 18.5(ii));
\item[(c)] $E^*$ is evaluable;
\item[(d)] $E$ is semi-reflexive and $E^*$ is not semi-reflexive;
\item[(e)] $E$ is complete (see 19C).
\end{enumerate}
