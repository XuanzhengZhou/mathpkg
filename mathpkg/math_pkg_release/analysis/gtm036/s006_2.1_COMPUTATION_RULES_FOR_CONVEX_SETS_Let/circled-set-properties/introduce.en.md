Circled sets enjoy two immediate properties: they are invariant under multiplication by scalars of unit modulus (so $aA = A$ for all $|a| = 1$), and they are symmetric, meaning $A = -A$. The symmetry follows because $-1$ has unit modulus. These properties make circled sets particularly useful when studying absorbing and balanced neighborhoods of the origin in topological vector spaces.
