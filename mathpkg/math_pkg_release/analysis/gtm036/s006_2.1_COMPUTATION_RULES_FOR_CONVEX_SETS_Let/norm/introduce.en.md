A norm is a pseudo-norm with the additional definiteness property: the only vector with norm zero is the zero vector. Formally, a norm is an absolutely homogeneous (not merely non-negatively homogeneous), subadditive, non-negative functional that separates points. Normed spaces are the foundational objects of classical functional analysis. The notation $\|x\|$ is standard for the norm of a vector $x$.
