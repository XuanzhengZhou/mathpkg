Unlike arbitrary unions of convex sets (which need not be convex), the union of a family of convex sets that is directed by reverse inclusion $\supset$ is always convex. A family is directed by $\supset$ if for any two members there is a third member contained in both. This result complements the intersection stability property and is useful in inductive constructions involving convex sets.
