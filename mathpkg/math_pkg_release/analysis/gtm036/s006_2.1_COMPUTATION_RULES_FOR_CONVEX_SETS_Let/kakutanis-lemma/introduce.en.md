Kakutani's lemma is a combinatorial-geometric result about disjoint convex sets: given two disjoint convex sets $A$ and $B$ and a point $x$ outside their union, one can adjoin $x$ to one of them (by taking its convex hull with that set) while preserving disjointness from the other. This lemma is a key tool in separation theorems for convex sets, particularly in the proof of Stone's theorem on the separation of disjoint convex sets.
