Given any non-void subset $A$ of a real linear space, there exists a smallest cone containing $A$, called the conical extension (or the cone generated by $A$). This is the cone analogue of the convex hull: it is the intersection of all cones that contain $A$. The conical extension consists of all finite sums $\sum a_i x_i$ with $x_i \in A$ and $a_i \geq 0$.
