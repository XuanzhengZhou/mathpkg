A subset $A$ of a linear space is called circled (also commonly called balanced) if it is closed under multiplication by scalars of modulus at most one: $aA \subset A$ for all scalars $a$ with $|a| \leq 1$. Circled sets are important in the theory of topological vector spaces because every neighborhood of zero in a topological vector space contains a circled neighborhood. A circled set is always symmetric, meaning $A = -A$, and for any scalar $a$ with $|a| = 1$ one has $aA = A$.
