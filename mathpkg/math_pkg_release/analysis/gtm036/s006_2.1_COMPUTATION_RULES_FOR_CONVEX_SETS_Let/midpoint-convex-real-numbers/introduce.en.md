For subsets of the real line, midpoint convexity implies full convexity whenever the set is either open or closed. The proof uses density of dyadic rationals and the topological properties of open/closed sets to pass from dyadic convex combinations to arbitrary convex combinations. This result does not hold for arbitrary subsets of the reals (there exist midpoint convex sets that are not convex, constructed using a Hamel basis).
