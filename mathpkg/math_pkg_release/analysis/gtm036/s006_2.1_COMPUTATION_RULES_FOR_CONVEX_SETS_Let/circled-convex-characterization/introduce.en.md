A set that is both circled and convex admits a concise joint characterization: it contains every combination $ax + by$ with $x, y$ in the set and $|a| + |b| \leq 1$. This condition simultaneously encodes the convexity requirement (taking $a, b$ non-negative with sum one) and the circledness requirement (allowing arbitrary signs with the sum of absolute values bounded by one). Such sets are precisely the unit balls of seminorms and play a central role in the theory of locally convex spaces.
