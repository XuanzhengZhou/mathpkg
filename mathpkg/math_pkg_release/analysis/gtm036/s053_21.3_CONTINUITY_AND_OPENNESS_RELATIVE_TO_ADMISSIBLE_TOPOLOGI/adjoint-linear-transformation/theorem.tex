\begin{definition}[Adjoint of a Linear Transformation]
\label{def:adjoint}
Let $E$ and $F$ be linear topological spaces with the natural pairings $\langle E, E^* \rangle$ and $\langle F, F^* \rangle$. Let $T$ be a linear transformation of $E$ into $F$. Since $E$ always distinguishes elements of $E^*$, there is at most one transformation which is dual to $T$. This dual transformation, if it exists, is called the \emph{adjoint} of $T$ and is denoted by $T^*$. The condition for dual transformations becomes:
$$h(T(x)) = T^*(h)(x) \quad \text{for all } x \in E,\; h \in F^*.$$
Equivalently:
$$T^*(h) = h \circ T \quad \text{for all } h \in F^*.$$
\end{definition}
