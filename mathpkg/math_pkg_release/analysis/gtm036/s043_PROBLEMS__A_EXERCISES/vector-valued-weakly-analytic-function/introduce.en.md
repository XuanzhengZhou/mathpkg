A vector-valued function is weakly analytic if it is analytic when the target space is equipped with its weak topology. Equivalently, this means that composing with any continuous linear functional yields an ordinary complex-analytic function. This definition provides a bridge between the strong (norm/topology) and weak notions of analyticity: a classic theorem (often attributed to Dunford or Grothendieck) states that for sequentially complete locally convex spaces, weak analyticity implies analyticity, making the two notions equivalent in many important cases.
