A total subset of a linear topological space is one whose linear span is dense in the weak topology. This exercise gives three equivalent characterizations: (1) only the zero functional vanishes identically on $A$; (2) the intersection of the annihilators (in the dual) of points in $A$ is trivial; (3) the linear span of $A$ is weakly dense. Total subsets play a role analogous to separating sets in duality theory.
