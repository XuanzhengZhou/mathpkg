This result shows that the norm-weak convergence equivalence established in Problem C(a) for uniformly convex spaces is a strictly weaker property than uniform convexity itself. The space $\ell^1$ of absolutely summable sequences serves as the canonical counterexample: it possesses the Schur property, meaning weak sequential convergence already implies norm convergence, yet its norm is far from uniformly convex. This distinction is important in the geometric classification of Banach spaces and highlights the special role of the Radon-Riesz property.
