This proposition clarifies an important subtlety: uniform convexity is a metric property of the specific norm, not a topological invariant of the underlying linear topological space. A Banach space may admit many equivalent norms, some uniformly convex and others not. The Euclidean plane under the $\ell^1$ norm provides the simplest counterexample, as it is not strictly convex, let alone uniformly convex. The convergence property characterizing norm convergence via weak convergence plus norm convergence is similarly norm-dependent.
