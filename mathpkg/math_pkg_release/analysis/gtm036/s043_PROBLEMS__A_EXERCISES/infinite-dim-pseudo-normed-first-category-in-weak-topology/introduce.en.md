This exercise contrasts the Baire category properties of the original topology and the weak topology. While a pseudo-normed space is always of the second category in itself (by the Baire category theorem if complete), an infinite dimensional pseudo-normed space becomes of the first category when equipped with its weak topology. This reflects the fact that the weak topology is strictly coarser and has "fewer" open sets, making it harder for a set to be nowhere dense.
