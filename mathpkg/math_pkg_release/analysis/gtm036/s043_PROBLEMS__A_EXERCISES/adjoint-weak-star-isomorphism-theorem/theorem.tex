Let $E$ be a linear topological space.

(i) If $F$ is a quotient space of a linear topological space $E$, then the polar $F^\circ$ in $E^*$, endowed with the relativized weak* topology for $E^*$, is topologically isomorphic to $F^*$ with its weak* topology.

(ii) If $F$ is a subspace of a linear topological space $E$, then the subspace $F^\circ = \{f \in E^* : f(x) = 0 \text{ for all } x \in F\}$ of $E^*$, with the relativized weak* topology, is topologically isomorphic to $(E/F)^*$ with the weak* topology.

(iii) The adjoint of a direct sum, with the weak* topology, is topologically isomorphic to the product of the adjoints with the product of the weak* topologies. That is, $(\bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha)^*$ with the weak* topology is topologically isomorphic to $\prod_\alpha E_\alpha^*$ with the product of the weak* topologies.
