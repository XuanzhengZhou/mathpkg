Let $E$ be a linear topological space. A subset $A$ of $E$ is called \textit{total} if the following equivalent conditions hold:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Each continuous linear functional $f \in E^*$ vanishing on $A$ is identically zero.
\item[(ii)] $\bigcap \{N_x : x \in A\} = \{0\}$, where $N_x = \{f \in E^* : f(x) = 0\}$.
\item[(iii)] The subspace generated by $A$ is $w(E, E^*)$-dense in $E$, where $w(E, E^*)$ denotes the weak topology on $E$ induced by $E^*$.
\end{enumerate}
