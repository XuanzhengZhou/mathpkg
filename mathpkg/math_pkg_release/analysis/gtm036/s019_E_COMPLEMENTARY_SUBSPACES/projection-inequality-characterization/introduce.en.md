The projection $P(x)$ onto a closed convex set $F$ is characterized by a variational inequality: for every $y \in F$, the real part of the inner product between $x - P(x)$ and $y - P(x)$ is non-positive. This geometrically means the angle between $x - P(x)$ and $y - P(x)$ is obtuse. For linear subspaces, this reduces to orthogonality: $x - P(x)$ is perpendicular to every vector in $F$.
