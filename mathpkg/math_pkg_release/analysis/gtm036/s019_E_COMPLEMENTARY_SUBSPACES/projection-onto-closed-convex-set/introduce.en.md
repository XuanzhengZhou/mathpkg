The projection onto a closed convex set in a Hilbert space assigns to each point $x$ the unique point $P(x)$ in the set that is closest to $x$. Existence follows from the parallelogram law, and uniqueness from the strict convexity of the Hilbert space norm. This is the fundamental tool for orthogonal decompositions and best approximation theory.
