Suppose $F$ is a finite-dimensional subspace of $E$ and that $G$ is a closed complementary subspace. Then the projection $P: E \to F$ along $G$ is a continuous linear map (since $F$ is finite-dimensional and the graph of $P$ is closed). Any non-zero continuous linear functional on $F$ composed with $P$ yields a non-zero continuous linear functional on $E$, contradicting the hypothesis. Therefore no such closed complement $G$ can exist.
