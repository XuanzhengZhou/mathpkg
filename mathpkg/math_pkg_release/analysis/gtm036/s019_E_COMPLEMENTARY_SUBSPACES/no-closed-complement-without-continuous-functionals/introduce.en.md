If a Hausdorff linear topological space admits no non-trivial continuous linear functionals, then finite-dimensional subspaces cannot possess closed topological complements. This result highlights the essential role that continuous linear functionals play in the existence of complementary subspaces, and provides a striking contrast with the locally convex case where complements always exist.
