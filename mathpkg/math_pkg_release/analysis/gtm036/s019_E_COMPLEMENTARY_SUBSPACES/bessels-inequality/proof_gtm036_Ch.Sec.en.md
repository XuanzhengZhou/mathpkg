Let $y = \sum_{r=1}^n (x, x_r) x_r$ be the projection of $x$ onto the subspace generated by $\{x_1, \dots, x_n\}$. By the properties of the projection onto a linear subspace, $x - y$ is orthogonal to each $x_r$, hence to $y$. By the Pythagorean theorem:
$$\|x\|^2 = \|y\|^2 + \|x - y\|^2 \geq \|y\|^2 = \sum_{r=1}^n |(x, x_r)|^2,$$
where the last equality uses the orthonormality of $\{x_r\}$ and the definition of $y$.
