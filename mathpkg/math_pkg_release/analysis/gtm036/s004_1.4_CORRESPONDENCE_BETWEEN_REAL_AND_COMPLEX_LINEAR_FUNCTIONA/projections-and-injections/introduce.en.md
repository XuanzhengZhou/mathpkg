This proposition establishes the basic properties of the canonical projection and injection maps associated with direct products and direct sums of linear spaces. The projection $P_s$ extracts the $s$-th coordinate, linearly mapping the full product onto the coordinate space $E_s$. The injection $I_s$ embeds $E_s$ as a subspace of the direct sum by placing an element at the $s$-th coordinate and zeros elsewhere, yielding a linear isomorphism onto its image.
