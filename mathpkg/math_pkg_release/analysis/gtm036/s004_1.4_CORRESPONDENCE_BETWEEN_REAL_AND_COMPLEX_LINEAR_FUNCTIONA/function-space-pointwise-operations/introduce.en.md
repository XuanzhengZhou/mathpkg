This is the standard construction of a linear space of functions from an arbitrary set $X$ into a linear space $F$. Addition and scalar multiplication are defined pointwise, making the function space into a linear space in the natural way. Many important linear spaces arise as subspaces of such function spaces, including spaces of bounded functions, continuous functions, differentiable functions, and Borel functions on an interval.
