Given a linear subspace $F$ of a linear space $E$, the quotient space $E/F$ is the linear space whose elements are the cosets $x + F$. Addition and scalar multiplication are defined via representatives: the sum of cosets is the coset containing the sum of any representatives, and scalar multiplication multiplies the representative. This construction is fundamental throughout linear algebra and functional analysis, providing the algebraic foundation for quotient topologies in topological vector spaces.
