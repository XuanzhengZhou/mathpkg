A projective system (also called an inverse system) of linear spaces is a family of linear spaces indexed by a directed set, together with compatible linear maps that go from spaces with larger indices to spaces with smaller indices. The spaces are thought of as successive approximations to a limit, with the canonical maps $P_{st}$ connecting the various levels of approximation. The compatibility condition $P_{rt} = P_{rs} \circ P_{st}$ ensures coherence across the entire system.
