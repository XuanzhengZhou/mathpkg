For a discrete space $X$, every Borel measure is purely atomic and can be represented by a point-mass sum $\mu(A) = \sum_{x \in A} g(x)$. As a consequence of the Riesz representation theorem, the dual of $c_0(X)$ (the space of functions vanishing at infinity on a discrete set) is isometrically isomorphic to $l^1(X)$. Similarly, the dual of the convergence space $c$ (sequences tending to a limit) is $l^1$, with functionals of the form $\{x_n\} \mapsto \sum x_n y_n + (\lim x_n) \cdot y_0$.
