An extreme point of a convex set is a point that cannot be expressed as a non-trivial convex combination of two other distinct points in the set. Geometrically, it is a "corner point" — not lying in the interior of any line segment whose endpoints lie in the set. For example, in the Euclidean plane, the extreme points of the square $\{(x,y): \max\{|x|, |y|\} \leq 1\}$ are its four corners. Extreme points are central to the Krein-Milman theorem and Choquet theory.
