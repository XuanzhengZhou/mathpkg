The total variation $|\mu|$ of a complex measure $\mu$ is defined as the set function that assigns to each measurable set $A$ the supremum of $\sum |\mu(B_r)|$ over all finite partitions of $A$ into disjoint measurable subsets. This generalizes the total variation of a signed measure and yields a positive measure. The total variation provides the natural norm for the space of finite complex measures, making it a Banach space.
