For $1 < p < \infty$, the dual space $(L^p)^*$ is isometrically isomorphic to $L^q$ where $1/p + 1/q = 1$. The isomorphism is given by the natural pairing $\phi(f) = \int f g \, d\mu$ for a unique $g \in L^q$. The proof uses Holder's inequality to show $\|\phi\| \leq \|g\|_q$ and constructs $g$ via the Radon-Nikodym theorem or by approximating arguments in the totally finite case. This duality is central to functional analysis and was established by F. Riesz.
