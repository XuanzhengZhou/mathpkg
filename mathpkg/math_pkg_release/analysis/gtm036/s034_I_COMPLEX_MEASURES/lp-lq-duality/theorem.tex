For $1 < p < \infty$, let $q = p/(p-1)$ be the conjugate exponent. Then the adjoint of $L^p(X, \mathcal{S}, \mu)$ is isometrically isomorphic to $L^q(X, \mathcal{S}, \mu)$. Specifically, for each continuous linear functional $\phi$ on $L^p$, there exists a unique $g \in L^q$ such that
$$\phi(f) = \int_X f g \, d\mu$$
for all $f \in L^p$, and $\|\phi\| = \|g\|_q$.
