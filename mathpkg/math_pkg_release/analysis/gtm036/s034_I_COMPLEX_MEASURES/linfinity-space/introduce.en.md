$L^\infty(X, \mu)$ is defined as the space of locally measurable functions that are bounded locally almost everywhere, with the essential supremum norm $\|f\|_\infty$. This definition generalizes the usual $L^\infty$ space to measure spaces that need not be $\sigma$-finite. In such spaces, not all measurable functions are locally measurable, and the proper notion of "boundedness" requires the locally almost everywhere formulation.
