The adjoint of $l^p(X)$ is $l^q(X)$, where:
\begin{itemize}
\item For $1 < p < \infty$, $q = p/(p-1)$ is the conjugate exponent;
\item For $p = 1$, $q = \infty$.
\end{itemize}
Here the space $X$ is always localizable. However, if $X$ is uncountable, $\mathcal{S}$ can be taken to be the set of all finite or countable subsets of $X$, and then the functions of $L^\infty$ are not all measurable, but only locally measurable.
