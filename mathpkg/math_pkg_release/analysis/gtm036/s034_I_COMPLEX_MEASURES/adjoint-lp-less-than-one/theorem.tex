Let $0 < p < 1$.
\begin{itemize}
\item The adjoint of the sequence space $l^p(X)$ is $l^\infty(X)$.
\item On the other hand, if $X$ is an interval of the real line and $\mu$ is Lebesgue measure, the adjoint of $L^p(X, \mu)$ consists of the zero functional only. That is, $(L^p[0,1])^* = \{0\}$ for $0 < p < 1$.
\end{itemize}
