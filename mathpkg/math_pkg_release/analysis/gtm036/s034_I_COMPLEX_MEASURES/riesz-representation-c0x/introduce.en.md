The Riesz representation theorem identifies the dual space of $C_0(X)$ (continuous functions vanishing at infinity, equipped with the supremum norm) with the space of finite regular Borel measures on $X$. Each continuous linear functional $\phi$ on $C_0(X)$ corresponds uniquely to a regular Borel measure $\mu$ such that $\phi(f) = \int_X f d\mu$, and the operator norm $\|\phi\|$ equals the total variation $\|\mu\| = |\mu|(X)$. This is one of the central results in functional analysis, connecting topological measure theory with linear functional analysis.
