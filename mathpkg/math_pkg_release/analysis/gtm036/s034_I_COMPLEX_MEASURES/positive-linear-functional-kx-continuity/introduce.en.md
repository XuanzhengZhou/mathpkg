Every positive linear functional on the space $K(X)$ of continuous functions with compact support is automatically continuous with respect to the inductive limit topology. This result is fundamental to the Riesz representation theory: it ensures that the duality between $K(X)$ and measures is well-defined without additional continuity assumptions. The positivity condition is sufficient to guarantee continuity because of the special nature of the inductive limit topology on $K(X)$.
