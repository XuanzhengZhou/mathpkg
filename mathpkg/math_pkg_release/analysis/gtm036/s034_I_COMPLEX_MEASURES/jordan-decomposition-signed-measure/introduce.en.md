Every signed measure $\mu$ admits a unique decomposition $\mu = \mu^+ - \mu^-$ as the difference of two positive measures, called the upper and lower variations, at least one of which is finite. The total variation is then $|\mu| = \mu^+ + \mu^-$. This Jordan decomposition generalizes the decomposition of a function of bounded variation into the difference of monotone functions.
