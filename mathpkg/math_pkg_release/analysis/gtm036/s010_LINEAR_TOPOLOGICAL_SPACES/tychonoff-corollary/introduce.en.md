As an immediate consequence of the Tychonoff theorem, the space $X^A$ of all functions from a set $A$ into a compact space $X$, endowed with the topology of pointwise convergence, is compact. This is because $X^A$ is precisely the product $\prod_{a \in A} X$ of compact spaces, and pointwise convergence is exactly the product topology.
