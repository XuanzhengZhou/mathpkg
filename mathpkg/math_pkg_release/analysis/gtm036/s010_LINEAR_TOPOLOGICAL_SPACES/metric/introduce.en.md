A metric is a pseudo-metric satisfying the positivity (or separation) condition: $d(x,y) = 0$ implies $x = y$. Metric spaces are the classical setting for topology and analysis. Unlike pseudo-metric spaces, metric spaces are automatically Hausdorff: distinct points are separated by positive distance. Every metric space is first-countable, paracompact, and perfectly normal.
