The usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$ is the metric topology induced by the Euclidean distance $d(x,y) = |x-y|$. This topology makes both fields topological fields: addition, multiplication, and inversion (away from zero) are continuous. Unless otherwise specified, any reference to the topology of the real or complex numbers defaults to this usual topology.
