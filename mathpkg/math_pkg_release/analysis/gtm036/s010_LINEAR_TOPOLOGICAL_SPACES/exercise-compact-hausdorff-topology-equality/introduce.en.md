This exercise establishes that a compact topology cannot be strictly finer than a Hausdorff topology on the same set. If $\mathcal{T}$ is compact, $\mathcal{U}$ is Hausdorff, and $\mathcal{U} \subseteq \mathcal{T}$, then the two topologies must coincide. This is a fundamental rigidity result: compact Hausdorff topologies are maximal among compact topologies and minimal among Hausdorff topologies.
