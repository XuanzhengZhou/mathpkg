A function $f: (X,\mathcal{T}) \to (Y,\mathcal{U})$ between topological spaces is continuous if the inverse image of every open set in $Y$ is open in $X$. Alternatively, $f$ is continuous at each point $x \in X$: the inverse image of every neighborhood of $f(x)$ is a neighborhood of $x$. Continuity is the fundamental morphism condition in the category of topological spaces.
