A topological space is countably compact if every countable open cover has a finite subcover, equivalently if every sequence has a convergent subsequence. Countable compactness is a weaker condition than compactness and neither implies the other in general. In metric spaces, however, compactness, countable compactness, and sequential compactness are all equivalent.
