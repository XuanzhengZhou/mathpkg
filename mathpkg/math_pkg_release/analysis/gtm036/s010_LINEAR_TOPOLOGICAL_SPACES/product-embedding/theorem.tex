For a fixed point $\theta$ in the product, and for each $t$ in $A$, the map $I_t$, defined as follows, is a homeomorphism of $X_t$ into the product: for each $s$ in $A$, and each point $z$ in $X_t$, $I_t(z)(s)$, the $s$-th coordinate of $I_t(z)$, is either $z$ or $\theta(s)$, depending on whether $s$ is or is not equal to $t$. Thus a product (of non-void factors) contains topological copies of the factors.
