The Tychonoff theorem asserts that an arbitrary product of compact topological spaces is compact in the product topology. This is one of the most important results in general topology and is equivalent to the Axiom of Choice. It guarantees, for instance, that the space of all functions from a set to a compact space is compact in the topology of pointwise convergence, which is the basis for many compactness arguments in functional analysis.
