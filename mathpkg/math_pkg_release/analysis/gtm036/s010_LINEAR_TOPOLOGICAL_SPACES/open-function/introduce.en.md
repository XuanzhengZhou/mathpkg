A function $f: (X,\mathcal{T}) \to (Y,\mathcal{U})$ is open (or an interior map) if the image of every open set in $X$ is open in $Y$. Openness is dual to continuity: while continuity requires inverse images of open sets to be open, an open map sends open sets forward to open sets. A homeomorphism is precisely a bijective, continuous, open map.
