A topological space is compact if every open cover admits a finite subcover (the Borel-Lebesgue property). Equivalently, every net in the space has a cluster point. Compactness is one of the most important properties in topology and analysis, generalizing the Heine-Borel theorem for closed bounded subsets of $\mathbb{R}^n$. Compact Hausdorff spaces are particularly well-behaved: they are necessarily normal and every continuous bijection from a compact space onto a Hausdorff space is a homeomorphism.
