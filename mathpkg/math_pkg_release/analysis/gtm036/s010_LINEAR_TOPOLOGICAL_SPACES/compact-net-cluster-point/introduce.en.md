A topological space is compact exactly when every net in the space possesses at least one cluster point. This net characterization of compactness is dual to the open cover definition and is particularly useful in functional analysis. It generalizes the Bolzano-Weierstrass property: in a metric space, this is equivalent to every sequence having a convergent subsequence.
