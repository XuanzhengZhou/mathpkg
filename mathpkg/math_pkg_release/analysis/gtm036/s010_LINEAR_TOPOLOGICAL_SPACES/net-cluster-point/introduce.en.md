A cluster point (or accumulation point) of a net is a point every neighborhood of which is visited by the net cofinally often. Unlike a limit, a net may have many cluster points. A subnet of a sequence need not be a sequence, though a subsequence is always a subnet. Cluster points characterize compactness: a space is compact iff every net has a cluster point.
