A subset $A$ of a topological space $(X,\mathcal{T})$ is closed precisely when its complement $X \setminus A$ belongs to $\mathcal{T}$, i.e., is open. Closed sets are the dual notion to open sets; arbitrary intersections and finite unions of closed sets are again closed. The interplay between open and closed sets is fundamental to all of point-set topology.
