For a product space $\prod_{t \in A} X_t$, each coordinate projection $P_s: \prod X_t \to X_s$ is both continuous and open. Continuity follows directly from the definition of the product topology. Openness is a nontrivial fact: the image of a basic open set under a projection is either $X_s$ (if $s$ is not among the restricted coordinates) or an open set in $X_s$. The product topology is precisely the projective (weak) topology induced by the family of all projections.
