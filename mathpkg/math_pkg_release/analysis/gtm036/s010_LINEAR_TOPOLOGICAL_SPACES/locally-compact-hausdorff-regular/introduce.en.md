Every locally compact Hausdorff space is regular. This means that points can be separated from closed sets by disjoint open neighborhoods. The result is a basic link between the compactness-related property of local compactness and the separation axioms: local compactness together with the Hausdorff property implies regularity (and in fact complete regularity as well).
