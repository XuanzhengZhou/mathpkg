The proposition is stated without proof in this section, as it is a standard result in general topology. The proof proceeds as follows: let $X$ be locally compact Hausdorff, $C$ a closed set, and $x \notin C$. By local compactness, $x$ has a compact neighborhood $K$. In a Hausdorff space, compact sets are closed, and $K$ is compact. Since $C \cap K$ is a compact set not containing $x$, the Hausdorff property yields disjoint open sets separating $x$ from $C \cap K$. Using the compactness of $K$, one constructs disjoint open sets separating $x$ and $C$, establishing regularity.
