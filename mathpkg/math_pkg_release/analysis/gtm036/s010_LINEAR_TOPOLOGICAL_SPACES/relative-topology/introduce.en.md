Given a topological space $(X,\mathcal{T})$ and a subset $Z \subseteq X$, the relative (or subspace) topology on $Z$ consists of all intersections $Z \cap V$ with $V$ open in $X$. This is the coarsest topology on $Z$ for which the inclusion map $Z \hookrightarrow X$ is continuous.
