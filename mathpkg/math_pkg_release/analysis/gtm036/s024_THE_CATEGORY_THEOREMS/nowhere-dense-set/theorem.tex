A subset $A$ of a topological space $X$ is \text{nowhere dense in $X$} (rare) if and only if the interior of the closure of $A$ is void:
\[
\operatorname{int}(\overline{A}) = \varnothing.
\]
Equivalently, $A$ is nowhere dense in $X$ if and only if the interior of the complement of $A$ is dense in $X$:
\[
\overline{\operatorname{int}(X \setminus A)} = X.
\]
