A subset $A$ of a topological space $X$ is called **nowhere dense** (also known as *rare*) if its closure has empty interior. Intuitively, a nowhere dense set is "thin" in the sense that its closure contains no nonempty open set. An equivalent characterization is that the interior of the complement of $A$ is dense in $X$. The notion of nowhere denseness is a fundamental building block for the Baire category theory: sets of the first category are defined as countable unions of nowhere dense sets.
