The Banach-Alaoglu theorem states that the polar of any neighborhood of zero in a linear topological space is compact with respect to the weak* topology on the dual space. This is one of the most important compactness results in functional analysis, providing a rich supply of weak* compact sets. It is typically proved via Tychonoff's theorem by embedding the polar into a product of compact intervals. The theorem underpins many fundamental results including the existence of weak* limits of bounded sequences in dual spaces.
