This theorem provides a fundamental characterization of locally convex topologies: every locally convex topology on a space $E$ can be realized as the topology of uniform convergence on the equicontinuous subsets of its adjoint $E^*$. As a consequence, it yields a completeness criterion for locally convex spaces: $E$ is complete precisely when every linear functional on $E^*$ that is weak* continuous on each equicontinuous subset is automatically weak* continuous on the whole dual, which is equivalent to such functionals arising as evaluation at points of $E$. This result connects the Grothendieck completion theorem with the structure of the dual space.
