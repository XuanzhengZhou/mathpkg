A convex subset of a locally convex topological vector space is closed in the original topology if and only if it is closed in the weak topology. This theorem establishes that for convex sets, the notion of being closed is independent of whether one uses the given locally convex topology or the associated weak topology. The forward direction uses the Hahn-Banach separation theorem to strongly separate a point outside a closed convex set, and weak continuity of the separating functional then prevents weak convergence to that point. The converse is immediate since the original topology is stronger than the weak topology.
