The algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$ of two linear spaces is realized as a subspace of the space of bilinear forms on the product of their algebraic duals. This construction embeds the tensor product concretely into $B(E', F')$, avoiding the usual quotient-of-free-vector-space definition. The canonical bilinear map $k \colon E \times F \to E \otimes F$ sends each pair $(x, y)$ to the elementary tensor $x \otimes y$, and the tensor product itself is the linear span of the image of $k$. This viewpoint unifies the algebraic construction with duality theory from the outset.
