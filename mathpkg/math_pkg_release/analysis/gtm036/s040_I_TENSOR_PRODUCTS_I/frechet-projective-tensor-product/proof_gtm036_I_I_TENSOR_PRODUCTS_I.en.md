The proof relies on properties of the projective tensor product topology established in Section 12G(b).

**Metrizability.** If $E$ and $F$ are metrizable locally convex spaces, then each admits a countable fundamental system of neighborhoods of zero. By the definition of the projective tensor product topology $\mathcal{T}$ on $E \otimes F$, a fundamental system of neighborhoods of zero is generated by the convex circled hulls of sets of the form $U \otimes V = \{x \otimes y : x \in U, y \in V\}$, where $U$ and $V$ range over neighborhood bases of zero in $E$ and $F$ respectively. Since $E$ and $F$ are both metrizable, they admit countable neighborhood bases $\{U_n\}$ and $\{V_n\}$. The countable family $\{\Gamma(U_n \otimes V_n)\}_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ then forms a countable neighborhood base for $\mathcal{T}$, so $E \otimes F$ is metrizable under $\mathcal{T}$.

**Completeness.** The metrizable locally convex space $E \otimes F$ under $\mathcal{T}$ need not be complete. Its completion $E \hat{\otimes} F$ is then a complete metrizable locally convex space, i.e., a Fréchet space, because the completion of a metrizable locally convex space remains metrizable (a countable base at zero extends to the completion).

**Barrelledness.** If $E$ and $F$ are barrelled locally convex spaces, then by 12G(b), the projective tensor product topology on $E \otimes F$ is barrelled, and barrelledness is inherited by completions. Hence $E \hat{\otimes} F$ is barrelled whenever $E$ and $F$ are metrizable and barrelled.
