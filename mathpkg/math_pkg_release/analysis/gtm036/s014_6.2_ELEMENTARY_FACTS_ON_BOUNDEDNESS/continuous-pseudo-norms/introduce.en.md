This proposition describes how the family of all continuous pseudo-norms on a locally convex space completely determines its topology, continuity of linear maps into it, and boundedness. The $p$-unit spheres form a local base for the topology, a linear map into $E$ is continuous precisely when its composition with every continuous pseudo-norm is continuous, and a subset is bounded exactly when it has finite diameter with respect to every continuous pseudo-norm. These facts provide the standard functional-analytic description of locally convex topologies in terms of seminorms.
