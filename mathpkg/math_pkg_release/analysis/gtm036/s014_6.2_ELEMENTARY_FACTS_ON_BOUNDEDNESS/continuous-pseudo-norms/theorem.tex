Let $E$ be a linear space with a locally convex topology $\mathcal{T}$, and let $P$ be the family of all continuous pseudo-norms. Then
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] the family of $p$-unit spheres about $0$, for $p$ in $P$, is a local base for $\mathcal{T}$;
\item[(iii)] a linear mapping $T$ of a linear topological space $F$ into $E$ is continuous if and only if the composition $p \circ T$ is continuous for each $p$ in $P$; and
\item[(iv)] a subset of $E$ is bounded if and only if it is of finite $p$-diameter for each $p$ in $P$.
\end{enumerate}
