This theorem states that every linear topological space, without any additional hypotheses, is topologically isomorphic to a subspace of a product of pseudo-metrizable linear topological spaces. It follows from the Metrization Theorem (6.7), Corollary 6.8, and the Embedding Lemma (6.3). This is the most general embedding result in the chapter — while locally convex spaces embed into products of pseudo-normable spaces (Theorem 6.4), arbitrary linear topological spaces embed into products of the broader class of pseudo-metrizable spaces.
