This theorem gives a purely topological characterization of pseudo-metrizability for linear topological spaces: the existence of a countable local base at zero is necessary and sufficient. Moreover, when a space is pseudo-metrizable, one can always construct an invariant pseudo-metric (i.e., $d(x+z, y+z) = d(x,y)$) whose topology coincides with the given one and whose open balls about zero are circled sets. This result is fundamental for the study of metrizable topological vector spaces and, together with the Embedding Lemma 6.3, implies that every linear topological space embeds into a product of pseudo-metrizable spaces.
