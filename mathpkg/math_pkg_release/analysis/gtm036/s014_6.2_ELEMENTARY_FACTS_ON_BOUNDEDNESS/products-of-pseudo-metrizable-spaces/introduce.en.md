This theorem characterizes exactly which products of non-trivial linear topological spaces are pseudo-metrizable: every coordinate space must be pseudo-metrizable and the index set must be countable. If the index set is uncountable, the intersection of countably many neighborhoods of zero in the product can be made to contain only finitely many coordinate constraints, leaving infinitely many coordinates unrestricted — this prevents the existence of a countable local base, which by the Metrization Theorem (6.7) is equivalent to pseudo-metrizability.
