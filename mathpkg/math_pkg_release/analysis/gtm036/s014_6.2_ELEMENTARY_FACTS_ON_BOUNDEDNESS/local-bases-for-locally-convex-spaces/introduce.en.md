This proposition gives a convenient characterization of locally convex topologies in terms of local bases. In a locally convex space, one can always find a local base at zero consisting of closed, convex, circled sets that are radial at the origin, closed under nonzero scalar multiplication, and whose intersections contain another member. Conversely, any family of convex, circled, radial sets satisfying the intersection and scalar-closure conditions defines a unique locally convex topology, making this a practical tool for constructing locally convex spaces.
