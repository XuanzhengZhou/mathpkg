A linear map into a product is continuous if and only if the map followed by projection into each coordinate space is continuous, and, in view of the definition of $R$, $R$ followed by projection into $F_t$ is simply $R_t$. Consequently $R$ is continuous if each $R_t$ is continuous. To prove the assertion concerning relative openness, observe that if $U$ is a neighborhood of $0$ in $E$ and $V$ is an open neighborhood of $0$ in $F_t$ such that $R_t^{-1}[V] \subset U$, then $R[U]$ contains $\{y : y \in F \text{ and } y_t \in V\} \cap R[E]$; this set is open in $R[E]$, and the lemma follows.
