This theorem establishes that every locally convex linear topological space is topologically isomorphic to a subspace of a product of pseudo-normable spaces. The result is a consequence of the Embedding Lemma (6.3) applied to the family of continuous seminorms that define the locally convex topology. It shows that the class of locally convex spaces is precisely the class of subspaces of products of pseudo-normed spaces, placing locally convex spaces at the foundation of functional analysis.
