Let $\mathcal{A}$ be the family of all sets which, for some positive $y$, are of the form $\{x: -y \leq x \leq y\}$. Then $\mathcal{A}$ is a co-base for the order bounded sets of $E$, and each member of $\mathcal{A}$ is convex and circled. Let $\mathcal{A}^{-}$ be the family of all convex circled sets which absorb each member of $\mathcal{A}$. Then in view of Lemma 19.1, $\mathcal{A}^{-}$ is a local base for a bound locally convex topology $\mathcal{T}$ for $E$, and $\mathcal{T}$ is the strongest locally convex topology relative to which each member of $\mathcal{A}$ is bounded. Each $\mathcal{T}$-continuous linear functional is bounded on order bounded sets. Conversely, if a linear functional $f$ is bounded on order bounded sets, then $\{x: |f(x)| \leq 1\}$ is a member of $\mathcal{A}^{-}$ and $f$ is therefore $\mathcal{T}$-continuous. Hence the adjoint of $(E, \mathcal{T})$ is simply $E^{\star}$. Recall (19.4) that a bound topology is always the Mackey topology of the pairing of $E$ and $E^{*}$, and hence $\mathcal{T}$ must be the Mackey topology $m(E, E^{\star})$. Consequently the family of all convex circled sets which absorb each order bounded set is a local base for $m(E, E^{\star})$. Parts (i) and (ii) follow immediately from this characterization.
