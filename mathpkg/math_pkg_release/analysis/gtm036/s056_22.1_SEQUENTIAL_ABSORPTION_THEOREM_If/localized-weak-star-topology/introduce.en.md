This remarkable theorem states that for the dual of a pseudo-metrizable linear topological space, four apparently different topologies actually coincide. In particular, the topology of uniform convergence on null sequences equals that on compact or totally bounded sets, and all are the strongest topology agreeing with the weak* topology on equicontinuous subsets. This result is sometimes called the "Banach-Dieudonne theorem" in the context of Banach spaces.
