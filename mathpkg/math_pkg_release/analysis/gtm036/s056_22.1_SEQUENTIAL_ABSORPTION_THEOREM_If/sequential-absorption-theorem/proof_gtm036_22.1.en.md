Let $\{U_n\}$ be a local base for the topology of $E$, and assume that $A$ is not a neighborhood of $0$. Then, for each positive integer $n$, there is a point $x_n$ in $(\frac{1}{n}U_n) \setminus A$. Evidently the sequence $\{nx_n\}$ converges to $0$, but $nx_n \notin nA$. It follows that $A$ does not absorb the sequence $\{nx_n\}$, and hence each set which absorbs every sequence which converges to $0$ is necessarily a neighborhood of $0$.
