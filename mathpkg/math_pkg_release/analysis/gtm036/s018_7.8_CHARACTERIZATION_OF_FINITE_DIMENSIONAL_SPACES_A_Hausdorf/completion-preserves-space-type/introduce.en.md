The completion operation preserves several important structural properties of linear topological spaces. If a space is pseudo-metrizable, pseudo-normable, or locally convex, then its completion inherits the same property. This yields two important special cases: completing a locally convex metrizable space produces a Fréchet space, and completing a normed space produces a Banach space. This exercise establishes that the classical completions of functional analysis are instances of the general completion theorem.
