In a Hausdorff linear topological space, finite dimensionality can be characterized purely in topological terms. The existence of a totally bounded neighborhood of the origin is equivalent to the space being finite dimensional. This result shows that for Hausdorff linear topological spaces, finite dimensionality is not just an algebraic property but is intimately connected to the topological notion of total boundedness. Consequently, up to topological isomorphism, such a space is Euclidean $n$-space.
