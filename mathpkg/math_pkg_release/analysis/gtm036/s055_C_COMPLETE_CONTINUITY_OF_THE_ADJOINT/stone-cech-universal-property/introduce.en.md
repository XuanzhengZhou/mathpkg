The Stone-Čech compactification $\beta(X)$ satisfies a fundamental universal property: any continuous map $t: X \to Y$ from a completely regular space $X$ into a compact Hausdorff space $Y$ extends uniquely to a continuous map $F: \beta(X) \to Y$. The proof uses the induced linear mapping $T: B(Y) \to B(X)$ given by composition with $t$, and then obtains $F$ by restricting the adjoint $T^{*}$ to $\beta(X) \subset M(X)$. This characterizes $\beta(X)$ as the maximal (or "free") compactification of $X$.
