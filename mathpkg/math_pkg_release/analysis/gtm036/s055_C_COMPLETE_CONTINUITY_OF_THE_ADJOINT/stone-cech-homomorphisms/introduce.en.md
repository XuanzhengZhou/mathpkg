Within the dual space $M(X) = B(X)^{*}$, consider the subset $H$ of all multiplicative linear functionals -- that is, those $\phi \in M(X)$ that satisfy $\phi(fg) = \phi(f)\phi(g)$ for all $f, g \in B(X)$. These homomorphisms correspond to the maximal ideal space of the commutative Banach algebra $B(X)$. In the context of the Stone-Čech compactification, $H$ is closely related to $\beta(X)$; in fact, $\beta(X)$ is precisely the set of all (nonzero) homomorphisms, giving the Gel'fand representation of $B(X)$.
