If $T: E \to F$ is a completely continuous linear map between locally convex Hausdorff spaces, then its adjoint $T^{*}: F^{*} \to E^{*}$ is also completely continuous, provided the dual spaces carry the topology of uniform convergence on convex circled compact subsets. This result establishes that complete continuity is preserved under passage to the adjoint in a natural dual pairing, and it relies on the properties of the compact-open topology (specifically result 18E from the main text).
