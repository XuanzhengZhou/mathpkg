Every bounded continuous real-valued function $f$ on a completely regular space $X$ extends uniquely to a continuous function on the Stone-Čech compactification $\beta(X)$. After identifying $X$ with its homeomorphic image $e[X] \subset \beta(X)$, this means that $B(X)$ and $C(\beta(X))$ are isometrically isomorphic as Banach algebras. This property characterizes $\beta(X)$ as the maximal compactification.
