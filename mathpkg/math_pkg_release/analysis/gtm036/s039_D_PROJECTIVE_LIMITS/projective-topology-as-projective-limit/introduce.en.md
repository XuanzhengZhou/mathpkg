When the common null space $N = \bigcap_t g_t^{-1}(0)$ is trivial, the projective topology on $F$ is Hausdorff and the space can be realized as a projective limit (inverse limit) of the images $g_t[F] \subseteq F_t$, each carrying the subspace topology. The family of maps $\{g_t\}$ then serves as the family of projections defining the limit. This completes the circle: every projective topology with $N = \{0\}$ is a projective limit, and every projective limit's natural topology is a projective topology (by property (b)).
