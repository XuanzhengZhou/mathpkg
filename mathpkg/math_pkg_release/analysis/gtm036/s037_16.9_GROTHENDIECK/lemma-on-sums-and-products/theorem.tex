Let $E_t$ and $F_t$ be paired linear spaces for each $t$ in an index set $A$ and let $\sum \{E_t: t \in A\}$ and $\prod \{F_t: t \in A\}$ have the natural pairing. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $B_t$ is a subset of $E_t$ for each $t$ in $A$, then the polar of the union of the sets $B_t$ is the product $\prod \{B_t^\circ: t \in A\}$ of the polars $B_t^\circ$ in $F_t$ of $B_t$.

\item[(ii)] If $C_t$ is a circled subset of $F_t$ for each $t$ in $A$, then the polar of the product $\prod \{C_t: t \in A\}$ contains the convex extension $B$ of the union of the polars $(C_t)_0$ of $C_t$ in $E_t$ and is contained in $aB$ for each real number $a$, $a > 1$.
\end{enumerate}
