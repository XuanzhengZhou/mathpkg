If $f$ is a complex linear functional on $E$ and $r$ is its real part, then $r$ is clearly a real linear functional on the real restriction of $E$. Moreover, $f(x) = r(x) - i r(ix)$ for every $x$ in $E$. Conversely, if $r$ is a real linear functional on the real restriction of $E$ and $f(x) = r(x) - i r(ix)$ for every $x$ in $E$, then it is easy to see that $f$ is complex linear on $E$. These remarks establish the result.
