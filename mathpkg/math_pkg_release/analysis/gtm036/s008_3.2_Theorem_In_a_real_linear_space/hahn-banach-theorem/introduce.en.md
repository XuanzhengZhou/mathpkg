The Hahn-Banach theorem is the central extension principle of linear functional analysis. Given a real linear space $E$, a subspace $F$, and a subadditive positively homogeneous functional $p$ that dominates a linear functional $f$ on $F$, the theorem guarantees the existence of a linear extension $\overline{f}$ to all of $E$ that remains dominated by $p$. This is historically the first of the extension-separation theorems and is arguably the most immediately applicable form of the principle. It underlies the geometric form of Hahn-Banach and the separation theorems for convex sets that follow in the subsequent sections.
