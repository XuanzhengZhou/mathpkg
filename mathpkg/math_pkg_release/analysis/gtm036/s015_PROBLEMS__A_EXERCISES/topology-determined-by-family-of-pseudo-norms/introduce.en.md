This exercise shows that every linear topological space can be topologized by a family of pseudo-norms satisfying the four axioms from part (a). The topology has as local base the finite intersections of $\varepsilon$-balls of the individual pseudo-norms. This is the standard construction of locally convex topologies from families of seminorms, but here presented in the more general context of arbitrary (not necessarily locally convex) vector topologies using pseudo-norms instead of seminorms.
