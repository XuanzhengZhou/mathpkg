Let $X$ be a compact space and let $A$ be a subset of $C(X)$ which is closed under the lattice operations (that is, $f, g \in A$ implies $f \vee g \in A$ and $f \wedge g \in A$). Then a function $h$ in $C(X)$ belongs to the uniform closure of $A$ if and only if for each $x \in X$ and $\epsilon > 0$ there exists $g \in A$ such that $g(x) = h(x)$ and $g(y) \leq h(y) + \epsilon$ for all $y \in X$.
