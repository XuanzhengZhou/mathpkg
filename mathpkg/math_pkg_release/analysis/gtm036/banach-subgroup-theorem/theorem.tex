If $G$ is a subset of a linear topological space $E$ such that $G - G \subset G$ (that is, $G$ is a subgroup of $E$), and if $G$ contains a set $A$ that is of the second category in $E$ and satisfies the condition of Baire, then $G = E$. Hence if $G$ is any proper subgroup of $E$, then either $G$ is of the first category in $E$ or else $G$ fails to satisfy the condition of Baire.
