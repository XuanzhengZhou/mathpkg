This theorem establishes that the property of having a bound topology is preserved under two fundamental operations: taking quotients by subspaces and forming direct sums. If $E$ is a bound space and $F$ a subspace, then the quotient $E/F$ inherits a bound topology. Likewise, the direct sum of any family of Hausdorff bound spaces is again a bound space. These stability results are important for constructing new bound spaces from known ones.
