This follows from the characterization of bound spaces in 19.3 and the relation between barrelled and bound spaces. In a bound space, each strongly bounded subset of the adjoint is equicontinuous. If weak* bounded sets are strongly bounded, then weak* bounded sets are equicontinuous, which is the defining property of barrelled spaces. In a barrelled space, a linear functional that is weakly continuous on bounded subsets is necessarily continuous, hence bounded. The result then follows from the hypothesis that the space has a bound topology and the identification of weak* and strong boundedness.
