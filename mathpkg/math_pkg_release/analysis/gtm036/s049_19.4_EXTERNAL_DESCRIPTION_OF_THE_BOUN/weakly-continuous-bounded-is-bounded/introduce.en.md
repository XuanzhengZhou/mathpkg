In the study of bound spaces, a useful observation relates weak continuity on bounded sets to boundedness of linear functionals. If the space has a bound topology and the weak* bounded subsets of the dual coincide with the strongly bounded subsets, then any linear functional which is weakly continuous when restricted to bounded subsets is automatically bounded. This connects the weak and strong aspects of the theory.
