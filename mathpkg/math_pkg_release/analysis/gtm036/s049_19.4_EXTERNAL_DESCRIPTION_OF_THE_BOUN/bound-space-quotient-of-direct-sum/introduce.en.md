This structural theorem shows that every bound space can be represented as a quotient of a direct sum of pseudo-normed spaces. Given a dual base $\mathcal{A}$ of bounded sets, one equips the linear span $E_A$ of each $A$ with the Minkowski functional pseudo-norm. The natural sum-to-$E$ map then induces the quotient topology, which coincides with the original bound topology of $E$. This representation is useful for understanding the fine structure of bound spaces.
