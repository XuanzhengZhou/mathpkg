This corollary of Theorem 19.4 provides a simple criterion for recognizing bound topologies. A locally convex topology on a space $E$ is a bound topology exactly when two conditions hold: (1) every bounded linear functional on $E$ is continuous with respect to the topology, and (2) the topology makes $E$ into a Mackey space, meaning it is the Mackey topology $m(E, E')$ for its own continuous dual.
