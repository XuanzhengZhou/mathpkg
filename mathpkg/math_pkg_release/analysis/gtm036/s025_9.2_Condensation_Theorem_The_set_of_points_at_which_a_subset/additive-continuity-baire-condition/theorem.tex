\textbf{10.8 Corollary.} Let $E$ and $F$ be linear topological spaces and $T$ an additive map of $E$ into $F$. If $E$ is of the second category, then $T$ is continuous if and only if $T^{-1}[V]$ satisfies the condition of Baire for every open neighborhood $V$ of $0$ in $F$.

Dually, if $F$ is of the second category, then $T$ is an open mapping onto $F$ if and only if $T[U]$ is radial at $0$ and satisfies the condition of Baire for every open neighborhood $U$ of $0$ in $E$.
