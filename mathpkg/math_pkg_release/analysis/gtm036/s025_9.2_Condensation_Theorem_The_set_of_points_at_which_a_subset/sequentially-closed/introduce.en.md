A set is sequentially closed if it contains the limits of all convergent sequences within it. In the context of pseudo-normed linear spaces, sequentially closed sets play a role in completeness arguments: a sequentially closed subset of a complete space behaves well with respect to sequential convergence, and the property is used to show that Cauchy sequences converge in pseudo-norm topologies.
