In a Hausdorff or regular space, any compact set with nonempty interior must be of the second category. This provides a basic sufficient condition for second category and yields important corollaries: a locally compact Hausdorff space is of the second category in itself, and every non-void open subset of a compact regular (or Hausdorff) space is of the second category.
