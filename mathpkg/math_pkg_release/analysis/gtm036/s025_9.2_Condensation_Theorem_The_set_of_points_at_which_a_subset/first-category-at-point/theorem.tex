A subset $A$ of a topological space $X$ is \text{of the first category at a point} $x$ if and only if there is a neighborhood $W$ of $x$ such that $W \cap A$ is of the first category in $X$. If $A$ is not of the first category at $x$, then $A$ is \text{of the second category at} $x$. In the latter case, for every neighborhood $U$ of $x$, $U \cap A$ is of the second category in $X$.

The set of all points at which $A$ is of the first category is open.
