The absorption theorem states that if a closed convex set $A$ absorbs every point of a bounded convex set $B$ and its reflection $-B$, and $B$ is of the second category in itself, then $A$ absorbs the entire set $B$. The proof uses a category argument: the intersection of scaled copies of $A$ with $B$ must be somewhere dense, allowing a geometric scaling argument to show full absorption. This theorem is fundamental in the theory of topological vector spaces.
