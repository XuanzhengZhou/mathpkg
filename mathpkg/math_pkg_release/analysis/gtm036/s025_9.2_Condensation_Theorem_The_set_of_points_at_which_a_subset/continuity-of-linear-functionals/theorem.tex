\textbf{10.10 Continuity of Linear Functionals.} Let $f$ be a linear functional on a linear topological space $E$, let $r$ be the real part of $f$, and let $A$ be a set which is of the second category in $E$ and satisfies the condition of Baire. Then $f$ is continuous if either:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $r$ is bounded from above on $A$, or
\item $r$ is upper semi-continuous on $A$ (i.e., $\{x \in A : r(x) < n\}$ satisfies the condition of Baire for each $n$).
\end{enumerate}
