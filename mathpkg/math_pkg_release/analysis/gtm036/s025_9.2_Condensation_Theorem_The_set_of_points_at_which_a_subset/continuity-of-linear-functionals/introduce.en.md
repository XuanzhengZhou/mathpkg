For linear functionals, continuity can be deduced from category-theoretic conditions: if the real part is bounded above on a second category Baire set, or is upper semi-continuous on such a set, then the functional is continuous. The proof uses the difference theorem to show that the set where the real part is bounded yields a neighborhood of $0$ after taking differences, forcing continuity.
