The Hausdorff uniformity endows the collection of non-void subsets of a linear topological space $E$ with a uniform structure. This uniformity is generated by entourages $V_U$ parameterized by neighborhoods $U$ of $0$ in $E$, where two sets $A$ and $B$ are $V_U$-close if each is contained in the $U$-expansion of the other. In the special case where $E$ is metrizable, this uniformity coincides with the classical Hausdorff metric uniformity, hence the name.
