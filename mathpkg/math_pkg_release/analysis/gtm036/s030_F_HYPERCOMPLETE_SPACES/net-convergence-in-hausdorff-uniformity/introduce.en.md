Convergence of nets in the space $\mathcal{E}$ of non-void closed subsets with the Hausdorff uniformity is characterized by mutual approximation: a net of closed sets $\{A_\gamma\}$ converges to a closed set $A$ precisely when, for every neighborhood $U$ of $0$, each $A_\gamma$ is eventually contained in the $U$-expansion of $A$, and conversely $A$ is eventually contained in the $U$-expansion of each $A_\gamma$. This captures the intuition that two sets are close if each lies within a small uniform thickening of the other.
