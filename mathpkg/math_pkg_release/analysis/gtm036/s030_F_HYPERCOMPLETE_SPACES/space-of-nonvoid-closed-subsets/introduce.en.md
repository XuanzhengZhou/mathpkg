The space $\mathcal{E}$ is formed by taking all non-void closed subsets of a linear topological space $E$ and equipping this collection with the Hausdorff uniformity. Restricting to closed subsets is essential because the Hausdorff uniformity on arbitrary subsets fails to separate a set from its closure -- two sets that differ only in limit points are indistinguishable under this uniformity. By working exclusively with closed subsets, the space $\mathcal{E}$ becomes a genuine Hausdorff uniform space, providing a proper setting for studying convergence of sets.
