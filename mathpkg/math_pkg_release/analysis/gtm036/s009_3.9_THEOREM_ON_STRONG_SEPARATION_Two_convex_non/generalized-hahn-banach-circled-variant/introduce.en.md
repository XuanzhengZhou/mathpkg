This variant of the Generalized Hahn-Banach Theorem addresses the case where the domain is a circled convex set and the dominating functional is absolutely convex. Part (a) constructs an auxiliary subadditive functional $g$ from the original functional $p$ and a family $\mathcal{L}_1$ of linear operators. Part (b) characterizes when an extension $\bar{f}$ of $f$ exists satisfying $\bar{f} \leq p$ and vanishing on $\mathcal{L}_1$. Part (c) introduces the notion of admissible families of operators and provides three equivalent conditions for the existence of an $\mathcal{L}$-admissible extension. This formulation is particularly useful in contexts involving group actions or operator-invariant extensions.
