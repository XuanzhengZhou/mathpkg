A family $\mathcal{U}$ of subsets of a linear space $E$ is a local base for a vector topology if and only if: (i) each $U \in \mathcal{U}$ is radial at $0$ and circled; (ii) for each $U \in \mathcal{U}$ there is $V \in \mathcal{U}$ such that $V + V \subset U$; (iii) the intersection of two members of $\mathcal{U}$ contains a member of $\mathcal{U}$; and (iv) if $U \in \mathcal{U}$, then for each $x$ in $W$ there is $V \in \mathcal{U}$ with $x + V \subset W$. If further each $U$ satisfies $U = -U$, then the topology is a Hausdorff topology.
