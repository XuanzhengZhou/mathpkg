This proposition lists the fundamental computational rules for convex sets in a linear space. The convex hull (convex extension) $\langle A \rangle$ of a set $ consists of all convex combinations of its points. Convex hulls distribute over addition and scalar multiplication, and intersections/unions of directed families of convex sets remain convex.
