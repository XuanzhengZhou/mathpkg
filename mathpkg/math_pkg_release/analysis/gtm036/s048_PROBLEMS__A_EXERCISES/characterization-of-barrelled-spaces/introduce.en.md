A barrelled space is a locally convex space where every barrel (a closed, convex, circled, radial set) is a neighborhood of zero. This characterization provides an alternative definition: a locally convex space is barrelled precisely when every locally convex topology with a local base of closed sets is coarser than the original topology. This formulation highlights the maximality property of barrelled topologies among locally convex topologies with closed local bases.
