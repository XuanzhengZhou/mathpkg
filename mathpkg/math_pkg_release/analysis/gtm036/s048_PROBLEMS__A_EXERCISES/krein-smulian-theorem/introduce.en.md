The Krein-Šmulian theorem characterizes weak* closed convex sets in the dual of a metrizable locally convex space. Rather than checking closure in the full weak* topology, it suffices to verify that the intersection with every equicontinuous set is weak* closed. This is significant because equicontinuous sets are weak* relatively compact (by Alaoglu's theorem), making the condition easier to verify. The theorem generalizes an earlier result (17.7) which concerned hyperplanes rather than arbitrary convex sets. See 22.6 for the proof in the metrizable case.
