In a normed space $(E, \|\cdot\|)$, the norm function $x \mapsto \|x\|$ is lower semicontinuous with respect to the weak topology. This means that if a net $\{x_\alpha\}$ converges weakly to $x$, then $\|x\| \leq \liminf_\alpha \|x_\alpha\|$. This property is fundamental in optimization and in proving that weakly closed bounded sets are weakly compact in reflexive spaces.
