This theorem extends the classical Banach-Alaoglu theorem, which asserts weak* compactness of the polar of a neighborhood in the dual of a normed space. In an arbitrary linear topological space, the polar $U^\circ$ is compact not merely in the weak* topology, but in the stronger topology of uniform convergence on totally bounded subsets of $E$. Moreover, this topology is maximal among all admissible topologies with this compactness property. The proof relies on Theorem 8.17 and the duality between totally bounded sets (Problem 16A).
