This extension of the Banach-Steinhaus theorem applies to linear topological spaces possessing property $(P)$: every vector topology with a local base of $\mathcal{T}$-closed sets is coarser than $\mathcal{T}$. This property is closely related to barrelledness; indeed, for locally convex spaces, property $(P)$ is equivalent to being barrelled. Under this condition, pointwise boundedness of a family of continuous linear maps implies equicontinuity, generalizing the classical Banach-Steinhaus theorem from Banach spaces to a broader class.
