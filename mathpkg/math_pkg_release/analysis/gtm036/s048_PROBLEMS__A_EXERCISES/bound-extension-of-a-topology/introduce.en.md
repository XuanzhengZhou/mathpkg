The bound extension $\mathcal{T}^b$ of a locally convex topology $\mathcal{T}$ is defined by taking as a local base all convex circled sets that absorb every $\mathcal{T}$-bounded set (called bornivore or bound-absorbing sets). This proposition establishes that $\mathcal{T}^b$ is itself a bound topology, preserving the same class of bounded sets, and is maximal among locally convex topologies with that property. The second part characterizes $\mathcal{T}^b$-equicontinuity in terms of uniform boundedness on $\mathcal{B}$.
