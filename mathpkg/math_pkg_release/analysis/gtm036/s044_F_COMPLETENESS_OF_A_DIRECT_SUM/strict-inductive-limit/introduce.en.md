A strict inductive limit is formed from an increasing sequence of linear topological spaces where each $E_n$ is a closed subspace of $E_{n+1}$ and the topology of $E_n$ is the relative topology from $E_{n+1}$. The limit space $E$ is the union equipped with the finest locally convex topology making all injections continuous. This construction satisfies several natural properties regarding relativization of topologies, Hausdorff separation, and bounded subsets.
