Let $(E, \mathcal{T})$ be the strict inductive limit of a sequence $\{(E_n, \mathcal{T}_n)\}$ where each $E_n$ is a closed subspace of $E_{n+1}$ and $\mathcal{T}_n$ is the relativization of $\mathcal{T}_{n+1}$ to $E_n$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(i)] Each $\mathcal{T}_n$ is the relativization of $\mathcal{T}$ to $E_n$, and $E_n$ is a $\mathcal{T}$-closed subspace of $E$.
    \item[(ii)] If each $E_n$ is a Hausdorff space, then $E$ is also a Hausdorff space.
    \item[(iii)] Every $\mathcal{T}$-bounded subset of $E$ is contained in some $E_n$.
\end{enumerate}
