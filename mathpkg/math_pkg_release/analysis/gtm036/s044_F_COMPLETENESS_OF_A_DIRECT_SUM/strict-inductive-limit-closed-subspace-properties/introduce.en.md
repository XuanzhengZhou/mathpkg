The strict inductive limit construction satisfies three fundamental properties: the topology of each step $E_n$ is the relativization of the limit topology, and each $E_n$ is closed in $E$; the limit space inherits the Hausdorff property from its steps; and every bounded subset of the limit is already contained in some finite step $E_n$. The proofs use 13E(b) and 13E(c) along with careful constructions of convex circled neighborhoods.
