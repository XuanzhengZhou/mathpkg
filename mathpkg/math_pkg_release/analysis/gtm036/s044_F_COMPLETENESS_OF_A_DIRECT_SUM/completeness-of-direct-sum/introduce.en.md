The completeness of a locally convex direct sum of complete spaces can be established via Grothendieck's completeness theorem (17.7), providing an alternative to the direct argument given in 14.7(iv). The proof proceeds by considering the dual $E^*$ as the product of the duals $E_t^*$ and analyzing a weak*-continuous linear functional $\phi$ on $E^*$, showing it has finite support and is represented by a point in the direct sum.
