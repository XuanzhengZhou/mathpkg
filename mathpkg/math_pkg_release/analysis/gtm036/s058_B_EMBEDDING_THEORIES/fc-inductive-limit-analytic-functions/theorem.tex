Let $C$ be a closed proper subset of the Riemann sphere, and consider the family $\mathcal{U}$ of all open proper subsets containing $C$, directed by $U \geq V$ iff $U \subset V$. For each $U \in \mathcal{U}$, let $A(U)$ be the space of analytic functions on $U$ with the topology of compact convergence. For $U \geq V$, let $I_{UV}$ be the natural injection of $A(V)$ into $A(U)$; then $\{A(U): U \in \mathcal{U}, \geq, I_{UV}\}$ is an inductive system. Let

$$F(C) = \lim_{\substack{\longrightarrow \\ U \in \mathcal{U}}} \{A(U): U \in \mathcal{U}\}$$

with the inductive limit topology. The space $F(C)$ can be identified with the set of equivalence classes of functions analytic on (some open set containing) $C$, calling $f$ and $g$ equivalent iff there is some $U \in \mathcal{U}$ on which $f$ and $g$ agree.
