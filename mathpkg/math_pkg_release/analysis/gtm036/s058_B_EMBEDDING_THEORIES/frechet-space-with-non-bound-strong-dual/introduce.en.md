This provides an important counterexample in the theory of locally convex spaces. A Fréchet space $E$ is constructed whose strong dual $E^*$ is not a bound space (i.e., not evaluable, not barrelled). By a general theorem (22.15), for the strong dual of a metrizable space, the properties of being bound, evaluable, and barrelled are equivalent. Thus this example shows that the strong dual of a Fréchet space need not be barrelled, which contrasts with the behavior of Banach spaces.
