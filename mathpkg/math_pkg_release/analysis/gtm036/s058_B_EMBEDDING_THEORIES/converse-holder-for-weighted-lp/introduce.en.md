This proposition provides a converse to Hölder's inequality in the context of weighted $l^p$ spaces. If a function $y$ pairs with every $x \in l^p(\omega)$ to give an integrable product $xy \in l^1(\omega)$, then $y$ must belong to the conjugate space $l^q(\omega)$. This characterizes the dual space and is a key step in proving the duality $l^p(\omega)^* \cong l^q(\omega)$ for $1 \leq p < \infty$.
