This proposition states that for a topological space $X$ with a countable co-base for compact sets, the space $K(X)$ of continuous functions with compact support, equipped with the inductive limit topology, is an $LF$ space. Moreover, if $X$ is not compact, $K(X)$ is not metrizable. This shows that the inductive limit topology is essential for such function spaces and that metrizability fails for non-compact $X$ due to the structure of the inductive limit.
