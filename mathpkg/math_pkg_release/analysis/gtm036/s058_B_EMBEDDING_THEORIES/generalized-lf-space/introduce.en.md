A generalized $LF$ space is the inductive limit of an increasing sequence of Fréchet spaces. This generalizes the classical $LF$ space construction where each step is a Fréchet space and the inclusions are continuous linear maps. Generalized $LF$ spaces appear naturally in the theory of distributions, spaces of analytic functions, and spaces of continuous functions with compact support. They carry a natural locally convex inductive limit topology.
