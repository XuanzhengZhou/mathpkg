A non-negative real valued function $\omega$ defined on a set $A$ defines a measure $\mu_\omega$ on the $\sigma$-ring of all subsets of $A$ by the formula

$$\mu_\omega(B) = \sum \{\omega(t): t \in B\}.$$

$\omega$ is called the weight function for the measure $\mu_\omega$. The space $L^p(A, \mu_\omega)$ is denoted by $l^p(\omega)$. Thus $l^p(\omega)$ is the space of (equivalence classes of) real or complex valued functions $x$ with

$$\|x\|_p = \left( \int |x(t)|^p \, d\mu_\omega(t) \right)^{1/p} = \left( \sum \{|x(t)|^p \omega(t): t \in A \}\right)^{1/p} < \infty,$$

two functions $x$ and $y$ being regarded as equivalent iff $x(t) = y(t)$ whenever $\omega(t) \neq 0$. The adjoint of $l^p(\omega)$ for $1 \leq p < \infty$ is $l^q(\omega)$, where $q$ is the index conjugate to $p$ ($q = \infty$ if $p = 1$ and $q = p/(p-1)$ if $p > 1$).
