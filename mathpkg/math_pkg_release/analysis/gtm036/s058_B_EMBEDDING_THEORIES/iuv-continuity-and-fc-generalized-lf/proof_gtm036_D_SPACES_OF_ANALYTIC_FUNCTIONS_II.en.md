The continuity of each $I_{UV}$ follows from the fact that the compact-open topology on $A(V)$ is finer than the restriction to $A(U)$ of the compact-open topology on $A(V)$ when $U \subset V$, so the restriction map is continuous. The existence of a countable cofinal subset $\{U_n\}$ follows from the fact that $C$ is a closed subset of the Riemann sphere, which is a separable metric space, so one can take a countable neighborhood basis. Since each $A(U_n)$ is a Fréchet space, $F(C)$ is by definition a generalized $LF$ space.
