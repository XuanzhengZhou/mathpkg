This theorem shows that for an equicontinuous family $M$ of functions taking values in a linear topological space, the topology of pointwise convergence on any subset $A$ of the domain $S$ is identical to the topology of pointwise convergence on the closure $A^{-}$ of $A$. In other words, pointwise convergence of functions in $M$ on a set determines their pointwise convergence on the closure of that set, provided the family is equicontinuous.
