The closure $F^{-}$ of $F$ in the space of all functions on $S$ into $Z$ consists of continuous functions, by virtue of theorem 8.18, and it is necessary to show that the topology of pointwise convergence on $S$ coincides on $F^{-}$ with the topology of pointwise convergence on a suitably chosen countable dense subset $S_0$ of $S$.

*(The remainder of this proof was truncated in the source text. The proof proceeds by noting that the topology of pointwise convergence on $S_0$ is pseudo-metrizable — it is the weakest topology which makes evaluation at each of the countable number of members of $S_0$ continuous — and there is consequently a sequence in $F$ which converges to $f$. Finally, if a net in $F^{-}$ converges to $g$ relative to the topology of pointwise convergence on $S_0$, and $h$ is a member of $F^{-}$ which is a cluster point of the net relative to the topology of pointwise convergence on $S$, then $g = h$ on $S_0$ and therefore $g = h$ on $S$, because both $g$ and $h$ are continuous. It follows without difficulty that pointwise convergence on $S_0$ implies pointwise convergence on $S$.)*
