For an equicontinuous family $M$ of linear functions between linear topological spaces, the topology of pointwise convergence coincides with the topology of uniform convergence on totally bounded sets. This is a key result in functional analysis: while pointwise convergence is generally weaker than uniform convergence on totally bounded sets, equicontinuity (together with linearity) forces the reverse inclusion, establishing their equivalence on $M$.
