It can be seen easily that the functions $J$ and $M$ are continuous if and only if the function which takes $x$ to $x^+$ is continuous; indeed, one has the identities

$$x \vee y = (x - y)^+ + y, \qquad x \wedge y = x - (x - y)^+.$$

If $p$ is a lattice pseudo-norm, then from the remark preceding the theorem one obtains

$$p(x^+ - y^+) \leq p(x - y),$$

since $p(z) = p(|z|)$ for a lattice pseudo-norm and $|x^+ - y^+| \leq |x - y|$. Consequently the map $x \mapsto x^+$ is uniformly continuous relative to each lattice pseudo-norm $p$ in the base, and hence is continuous relative to the locally convex topology $\mathcal{T}$. Therefore the lattice operations $M$ and $J$ are continuous on $E \times E$ into $E$.

The Mackey topology $m(E, E^\star)$ is generated by the set of all absolutely convex $\sigma(E^\star, E)$-compact subsets of $E^\star$. The corresponding pseudo-norms are lattice pseudo-norms (see the discussion of the order dual), so the base of continuous pseudo-norms for $m(E, E^\star)$ admits a subbase of lattice pseudo-norms. Thus the lattice operations are continuous relative to $m(E, E^\star)$.
