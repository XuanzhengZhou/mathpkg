This lemma establishes a Lipschitz-type inequality for the map $x \mapsto x^+$ in a vector lattice: the absolute difference of the positive parts of two elements is bounded by the absolute difference of the elements themselves. It follows from the elementary subadditivity property $(u+v)^+ \leq u^+ + v^+$ via a series of rearrangements and symmetrization. This inequality is the key ingredient in proving that lattice operations are continuous with respect to any locally convex topology generated by lattice pseudo-norms.
