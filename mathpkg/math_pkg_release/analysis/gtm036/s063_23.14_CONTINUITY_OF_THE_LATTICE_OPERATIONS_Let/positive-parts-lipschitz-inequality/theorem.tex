For arbitrary $u$ and $v$ in a vector lattice $E$, $$(u+v)^+ \leq u^+ + v^+$$ holds. Hence for any $x$ and $y$, $$x^+ \leq (x-y)^+ + y^+ \quad \text{or} \quad x^+ - y^+ \leq (x-y)^+.$$ It follows that $$(x^+ - y^+)^+ \leq (x-y)^+.$$ By interchanging $x$ and $y$, we see that $$(x^+ - y^+)^- \leq (x-y)^-.$$ By adding the last two inequalities, we obtain $$|x^+ - y^+| \leq |x-y|.$$
