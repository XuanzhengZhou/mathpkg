Let \(\{a_n\}\) be a sequence of distinct points in \(\mathbb{C}\) with \(\lim |a_n| = \infty\), and let \(\{b_n\}\) be a sequence of complex numbers. Consider the series

\[
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ \frac{b_n}{z - a_n} + \frac{b_n}{a_n} \sum_{j=0}^{k_n-1} \left( \frac{z}{a_n} \right)^j \right]. \tag{3.4}
\]

Show that if \(\limsup |b_n| < \infty\) then (3.4) converges absolutely if \(k_n = n\) for all \(n\).
