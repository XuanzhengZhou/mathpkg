This central theorem of Chapter VIII establishes that simple connectedness — a purely topological condition — is equivalent to nine other conditions spanning complex analysis, potential theory, and topology. The equivalences include: the vanishing of winding numbers for points outside \(G\) (b), connectedness of the extended complement \(\mathbb{C}_\infty \setminus G\) (c), polynomial approximability of analytic functions (d, e), the existence of analytic logarithms and square roots for nonvanishing functions (g, h), the existence of harmonic conjugates (i), and being homeomorphic to the unit disk (j). The theorem is described in the text as "an aesthetic peak in Mathematics" precisely because it reveals the deep unity between topological and analytical properties of planar domains.
