A metric $\rho$ is defined on $C(G, \Omega)$ using the compact exhaustion $\{K_n\}$ of $G$. For each $n$, the pseudometric $\rho_n(f,g) = \sup_{z \in K_n} d(f(z), g(z))$ measures the uniform distance between $f$ and $g$ on the compact set $K_n$. These pseudometrics are combined via weighted sum with factor $(1/2)^n$ and the bounded transformation $t \mapsto t/(1+t)$, ensuring convergence of the series. This metric encodes the topology of uniform convergence on compact subsets of $G$.
