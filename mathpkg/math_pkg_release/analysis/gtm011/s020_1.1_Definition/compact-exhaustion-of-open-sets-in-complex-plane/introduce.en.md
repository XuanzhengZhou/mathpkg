Every open set $G \subseteq \mathbb{C}$ admits a canonical compact exhaustion: a nested sequence $\{K_n\}$ of compact subsets whose interiors cover $G$ and such that each $K_n$ is contained in the interior of $K_{n+1}$. The construction uses closed balls intersected with the complement of a shrinking collar around $\mathbb{C} \setminus G$, yielding $K_n = \{z : |z| \leq n\} \cap \{z : d(z, \mathbb{C} - G) \geq 1/n\}$. Additionally, each component of the complement $\mathbb{C}_{\infty} - K_n$ in the Riemann sphere contains a component of $\mathbb{C}_{\infty} - G$, a property used in Chapter VIII. This exhaustion provides the fundamental sequence of compact sets used to metrize $C(G, \Omega)$.
