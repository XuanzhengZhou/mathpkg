The Schwarz Reflection Principle provides a method to analytically continue a function defined on the upper half of a symmetric region across the real axis. Given a function $f$ that is continuous on $G_+ \cup G_0$ (where $G_0$ is the real trace of the region), analytic on $G_+$, and real-valued on $G_0$, the principle guarantees a unique analytic extension $g$ to the entire symmetric region $G$. The extension is defined by setting $g(z) = \overline{f(\bar{z})}$ for $z$ in the lower half-plane $G_-$. This technique generalizes to reflection across circles and is a fundamental tool in conformal mapping and analytic continuation theory.
