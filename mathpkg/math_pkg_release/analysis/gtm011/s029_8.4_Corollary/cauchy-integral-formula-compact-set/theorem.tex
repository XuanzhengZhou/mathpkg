\textbf{Proposition 1.1.} Let $K$ be a compact subset of the region $G$; then there exist straight line segments $\gamma_1, \ldots, \gamma_n$ in $G - K$ such that for every continuous function $\varphi$ on $\bigcup_{j=1}^{m} \partial R_j$,
$$\sum_{k=1}^{n} \int_{\gamma_k} \varphi = \sum_{j=1}^{m} \int_{\partial R_j} \varphi,$$
where the $R_j$ are rectangles from a sufficiently fine grid covering $K$ and $\partial R_j$ denotes the positively oriented boundary of $R_j$. In particular, for $f$ analytic on $G$,
$$f(z) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \int_{\gamma_k} \frac{f(w)}{w-z} \, dw$$
for all $z$ in $K$ not lying on any $\gamma_k$.
