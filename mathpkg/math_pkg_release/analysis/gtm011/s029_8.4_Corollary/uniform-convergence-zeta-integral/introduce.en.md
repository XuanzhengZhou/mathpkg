Corollary 8.4 establishes uniform convergence bounds for integrals of the form $\int (e^t-1)^{-1} t^{z-1} dt$ that are fundamental to the analytic continuation of the Riemann zeta function. Part (a) guarantees uniform convergence on compact vertical strips $\{a \leq \operatorname{Re} z \leq A\}$ strictly to the right of $\operatorname{Re} z = 1$, while Part (b) extends this to arbitrary left half-planes for the integral over $[1, \infty)$. These estimates are then used in Proposition 8.5 to express $\zeta(z)\Gamma(z)$ as an integral representation valid for $\operatorname{Re} z > 1$.
