A non-identity Mobius transformation can have at most two fixed points in the extended complex plane. This follows because the fixed-point equation $S(z) = z$, or $\frac{az+b}{cz+d} = z$, reduces to a quadratic equation $cz^2 + (d-a)z - b = 0$. This crucial property underlies the fact that a Mobius transformation is uniquely determined by its values on any three distinct points.
