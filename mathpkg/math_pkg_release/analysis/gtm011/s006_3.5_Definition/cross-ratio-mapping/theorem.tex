Let $z_2, z_3, z_4$ be distinct points in $\mathbb{C}_\infty$. Define $S: \mathbb{C}_\infty \to \mathbb{C}_\infty$ by

$$S(z) = \frac{z - z_3}{z - z_4} \Big/ \frac{z_2 - z_3}{z_2 - z_4} = \frac{(z - z_3)(z_2 - z_4)}{(z - z_4)(z_2 - z_3)} \quad \text{if} \quad z_2, z_3, z_4 \in \mathbb{C};$$

$$S(z) = \frac{z - z_3}{z - z_4} \quad \text{if} \quad z_2 = \infty;$$

$$S(z) = \frac{z_2 - z_4}{z - z_4} \quad \text{if} \quad z_3 = \infty;$$

$$S(z) = \frac{z - z_3}{z_2 - z_3} \quad \text{if} \quad z_4 = \infty.$$

In all cases $S(z_2) = 1$, $S(z_3) = 0$, $S(z_4) = \infty$, and $S$ is the unique Möbius transformation with this property.
