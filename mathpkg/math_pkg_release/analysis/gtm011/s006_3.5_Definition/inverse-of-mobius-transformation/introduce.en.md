Every Mobius transformation $S(z) = \frac{az+b}{cz+d}$ is invertible, with the inverse given explicitly by $S^{-1}(z) = \frac{dz-b}{-cz+a}$. The formula is verified by direct substitution: composing $S$ with this expression yields the identity. This invertibility is precisely what distinguishes Mobius transformations (where $ad-bc \neq 0$) from degenerate linear fractional transformations.
