A fundamental property of Mobius transformations is that each is uniquely determined by its action on any three distinct points of the extended complex plane. Given two ordered triples of distinct points, there exists exactly one Mobius transformation sending the first triple to the second. This follows from the fact that a non-identity Mobius transformation has at most two fixed points, so if two transformations agree on three points, their composition with an inverse has three fixed points and must be the identity.
