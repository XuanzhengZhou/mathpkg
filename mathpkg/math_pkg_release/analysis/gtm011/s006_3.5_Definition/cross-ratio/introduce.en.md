The cross-ratio $(z_1, z_2, z_3, z_4)$ of four points in the extended complex plane is defined as the image of $z_1$ under the unique Mobius transformation sending $z_2, z_3, z_4$ to $1, 0, \infty$, respectively. This quantity is the fundamental invariant of Mobius transformations: a mapping is a Mobius transformation if and only if it preserves cross-ratios. The cross-ratio plays a role in complex analysis analogous to that of the ratio of distances in Euclidean geometry.
