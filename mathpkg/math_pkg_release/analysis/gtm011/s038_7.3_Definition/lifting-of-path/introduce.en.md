A lifting of a path is a fundamental concept in the theory of covering spaces. Given a covering map $\rho: X \to \Omega$ and a path $\gamma$ in the base space $\Omega$, a lifting $\tilde{\gamma}$ is a path in the covering space $X$ whose image under $\rho$ recovers the original path. The existence and uniqueness of liftings is a central property of covering spaces and underpins the monodromy theorem, which relates homotopy of paths in the base to the endpoints of their liftings.
