An infinite product of complex numbers is defined as the limit of the sequence of finite partial products, provided this limit exists. This notion is fundamental for the Weierstrass Factorization Theorem, which constructs entire functions with prescribed zeros via convergent infinite products. Care is needed because a single zero factor forces the limit to zero regardless of other factors.
