This theorem, sometimes called Riemann's removable singularity theorem, gives a simple and powerful criterion for checking whether an isolated singularity is removable. Rather than verifying the existence of an analytic extension directly, one only needs to check that $(z-a)f(z) \to 0$ as $z \to a$. The proof constructs an analytic function $g(z) = (z-a)f(z)$ for $z \neq a$ with $g(a) = 0$, then applies Morera's Theorem to show $g$ is analytic in the full disk. Since $g(a) = 0$, $g(z) = (z-a)h(z)$ for an analytic $h$, and $h$ provides the required analytic extension of $f$.
