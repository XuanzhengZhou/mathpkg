FEP homotopy (fixed endpoint homotopy) is a refinement of the standard homotopy relation for curves that share the same starting and ending points. Unlike general homotopy of closed curves, FEP homotopy requires the endpoints to remain fixed throughout the deformation: $\Gamma(0,t) = a$ and $\Gamma(1,t) = b$ for all $t$. This relation is an equivalence relation on curves from one given point to another (Exercise 3). The notion is useful because if $\gamma_0$ and $\gamma_1$ are FEP homotopic rectifiable curves from $a$ to $b$, then $\gamma_0 - \gamma_1$ is a closed rectifiable curve homotopic to zero.
