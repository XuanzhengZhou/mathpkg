This theorem guarantees that any nowhere-vanishing analytic function on a simply connected region possesses an analytic logarithm. The proof uses the fact that $f'/f$ is analytic (since $f$ never vanishes) and thus, by Corollary 6.16, has a primitive $g_1$. Setting $h(z) = \exp g_1(z)$ and analyzing the quotient $f/h$ shows that $f$ differs from $\exp g_1$ only by a constant factor, yielding $f(z) = \exp g(z)$ for an appropriately chosen analytic branch $g$. This result is another consequence of simple connectedness equivalent to it.
