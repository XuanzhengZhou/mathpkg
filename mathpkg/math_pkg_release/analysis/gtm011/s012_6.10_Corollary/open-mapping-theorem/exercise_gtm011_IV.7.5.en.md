Let $X$ and $\Omega$ be metric spaces and suppose that $f: X \rightarrow \Omega$ is one-one and onto. Show that $f$ is an open map if and only if $f$ is a closed map. (A function $f$ is a closed map if it takes closed sets onto closed sets.)
