Goursat's Theorem establishes the equivalence of two fundamental definitions of analyticity: a function that is complex differentiable on an open set (without assuming continuity of the derivative) is necessarily analytic. The proof uses Morera's Theorem (5.10) by showing that $\int_T f = 0$ for every triangular path $T$ in $G$, which then implies $f$ is analytic. The key technique is a repeated subdivision argument: starting with a triangle $T$, one constructs a nested sequence of triangles $\Delta^{(n)}$ whose diameters shrink to a single point $z_0$, and uses the existence of $f'(z_0)$ to bound the integral. This result shows that complex differentiability alone implies infinite differentiability.
