The Residue Theorem is the central result in complex analysis for evaluating contour integrals. It states that the integral of an analytic function $f$ around a closed curve $\gamma$ equals $2\pi i$ times the sum of residues at the isolated singularities enclosed by $\gamma$, each weighted by its winding number. This theorem generalizes Cauchy's Integral Formula and reduces many definite integral computations to the algebraic problem of finding residues.
