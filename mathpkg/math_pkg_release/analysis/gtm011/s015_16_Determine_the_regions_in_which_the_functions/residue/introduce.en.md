The residue of a complex function $f$ at an isolated singularity $z = a$ is the coefficient $a_{-1}$ in its Laurent series expansion about $z = a$. It captures the "order $-1$" contribution and plays a central role in evaluating contour integrals. The Residue Theorem shows that the integral of $f$ around a closed curve equals $2\pi i$ times the sum of residues inside, weighted by winding numbers.
