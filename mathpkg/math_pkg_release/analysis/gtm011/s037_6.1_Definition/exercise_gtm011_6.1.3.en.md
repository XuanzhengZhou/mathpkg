The following is a generalization of Proposition 6.3(b). Let $(X, \Phi)$ be an analytic manifold, let $\Omega$ be a topological space, and suppose there is a continuous function $h$ of $X$ onto $\Omega$ that is locally one-one (that is, if $x \in X$ there is an open set $U$ such that $x \in U$ and $h$ is one-one on $U$). If $(U, \varphi) \in \Phi$ and $h$ is one-one on $U$ let $\Delta = h(U)$ and let $\psi : \Delta \to \mathbb{C}$ be defined by $\psi(\omega) = \varphi \circ (h|_U)^{-1}(\omega)$. Let $\Psi$ be the collection of all such pairs $(\Delta, \psi)$. Prove that $(\Omega, \Psi)$ is an analytic manifold and $h$ is an analytic function from $X$ to $\Omega$.
