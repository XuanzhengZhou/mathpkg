Lemma 6.20 provides a partial converse to Proposition 6.18: under the same hypotheses, every function element $[g]_a$ in the complete analytic function $\mathcal{F}$ is a local inverse of $f$ on some disk about $a$. That is, $f(g(z)) = z$ for all $z$ in a neighborhood of $a$. This lemma is a crucial technical step in the proof of Proposition 6.19, establishing that the complete analytic function obtained from a local inverse indeed consists entirely of local inverses.
