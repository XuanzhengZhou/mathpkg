Proposition 6.18 establishes that local inverses of an analytic function with non-vanishing derivative can be continued along paths in the image $f(G)$. Given two local inverses $g_0$ and $g_1$ at points $\alpha = f(a)$ and $\beta = f(b)$, one can find a path $\sigma$ in $f(G)$ connecting $\alpha$ to $\beta$ such that $(g_1, \Delta_1)$ is the analytic continuation of $(g_0, \Delta_0)$ along $\sigma$. This result is essential for understanding the relationship between the Riemann surface of the complete analytic function and the graph of $f$.
