An analytic function between two analytic surfaces $(X, \Phi)$ and $(\Omega, \Psi)$ is defined locally using coordinate patches. At a point $a \in X$, one selects a patch $(U, \varphi)$ around $a$ and a patch $(\Lambda, \psi)$ around $f(a)$ such that $f(U) \subset \Lambda$, then requires the coordinate representation $\psi \circ f \circ \varphi^{-1}$ to be an ordinary analytic function on an open subset of $\mathbb{C}$. This definition is independent of the choice of coordinate patches because the transition maps $\varphi_a \circ \varphi_b^{-1}$ in the analytic structure are themselves analytic.
