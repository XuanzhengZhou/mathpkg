The order of a pole at $z = a$ is the smallest positive integer $m$ for which multiplying $f$ by $(z-a)^m$ removes the singularity at $a$, yielding a removable singularity. Equivalently, by Proposition 1.4, $f$ can be written as $g(z)/(z-a)^m$ with $g$ analytic and $g(a) \neq 0$, and $m$ is precisely the order of the pole. A pole of order $1$ is called a simple pole.
