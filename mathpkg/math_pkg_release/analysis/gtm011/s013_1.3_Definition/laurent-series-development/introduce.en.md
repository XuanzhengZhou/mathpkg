The Laurent series development generalizes the power series expansion to functions analytic in an annulus rather than a full disk. Any function $f$ analytic in an annulus $\operatorname{ann}(a; R_1, R_2)$ can be uniquely expressed as a doubly infinite power series $\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n (z-a)^n$ converging absolutely and uniformly on compact subsets. The coefficients are given by Cauchy-type integrals $a_n = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_\gamma f(z)/(z-a)^{n+1} \, dz$, independent of the choice of the circle $\gamma$ inside the annulus. The series is unique, meaning the coefficients are uniquely determined by $f$.
