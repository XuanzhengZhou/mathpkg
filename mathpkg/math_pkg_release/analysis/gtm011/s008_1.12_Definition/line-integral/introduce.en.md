The line integral of a complex-valued function $f$ along a rectifiable path $\gamma$ generalizes the Riemann-Stieltjes integral to the complex plane. For a rectifiable path $\gamma: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$ and a function $f$ continuous on the trace of $\gamma$, the line integral $\int_\gamma f$ is defined via a limiting process of Riemann-Stieltjes sums. This fundamental construction is the starting point for all of complex integration theory and serves as the basis for Cauchy's theorem, Cauchy's integral formula, and the theory of analytic functions.
