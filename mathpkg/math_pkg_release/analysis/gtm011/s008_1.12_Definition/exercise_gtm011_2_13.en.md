Find the series expansion of $\frac{e^z - 1}{z}$ about zero and determine its radius of convergence. Consider $f(z) = \frac{z}{e^z - 1}$ and let

$$f(z) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{a_k}{k!} z^k$$

be its power series expansion about zero. What is the radius of convergence? Show that

$$0 = a_0 + \binom{n+1}{1} a_1 + \cdots + \binom{n+1}{n} a_n.$$

Using the fact that $f(z) + \frac{1}{2} z$ is an even function show that $a_k = 0$ for $k$ odd and $k > 1$. The numbers $B_{2n} = (-1)^{n-1} a_{2n}$ are called the Bernoulli numbers for $n \geq 1$. Calculate $B_2, B_4, \ldots, B_{10}$.
