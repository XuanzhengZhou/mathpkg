Theorem 1.18 generalizes the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus from real analysis to complex line integrals. If a continuous function $f$ on an open set $G$ possesses a primitive $F$ (i.e., $F' = f$), then the line integral of $f$ along any rectifiable path in $G$ depends only on the values of $F$ at the endpoints. This theorem highlights a fundamental difference between real and complex analysis: unlike real-valued continuous functions, not every complex continuous function admits a primitive.
