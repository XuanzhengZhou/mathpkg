This theorem extends the theory of infinite products to sequences of functions. If a series of continuous functions $\sum g_n(x)$ converges absolutely and uniformly on a set $X$, then the corresponding infinite product $\prod (1 + g_n(x))$ also converges absolutely and uniformly. Moreover, the zeros of the product function arise only from finitely many factors where $g_n(x) = -1$, since the tail product after a certain index $n_0$ never vanishes. The proof uses Lemma 5.7 (on exponentiating uniform limits) together with the fact that $|\log(1+w)| \leq \frac{3}{2}|w|$ when $|w| < \frac{1}{2}$.
