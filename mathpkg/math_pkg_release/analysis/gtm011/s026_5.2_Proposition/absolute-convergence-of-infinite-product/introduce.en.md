This definition provides the correct notion of absolute convergence for infinite products. Unlike the naive approach of requiring $\prod |z_n|$ to converge (which fails to imply convergence of $\prod z_n$, as shown by the counterexample $z_n = -1$), absolute convergence is instead defined via the absolute convergence of the associated log-series. This definition ensures that absolute convergence implies ordinary convergence and is invariant under rearrangement of factors, just as for infinite series.
