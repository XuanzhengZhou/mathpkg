Suppose $f$ and $g$ are meromorphic in a neighborhood of $\overline{B}(a; R)$ with no zeros or poles on the circle $\gamma = \{z : |z - a| = R\}$. Let $Z_f, Z_g$ ($P_f, P_g$) be the number of zeros (poles) of $f$ and $g$ inside $\gamma$ counted according to their multiplicities. If
$$
|f(z) + g(z)| < |f(z)| + |g(z)|
$$
on $\gamma$, then
$$
Z_f - P_f = Z_g - P_g.
$$
