Let $f$ be analytic in $B(0; R)$ with $f(0) = 0$, $f'(0) \neq 0$ and $f(z) \neq 0$ for $0 < |z| \leq R$. Put $\rho = \min\{|f(z)|: |z| = R\} > 0$. Define $g: B(0; \rho) \to \mathbb{C}$ by
$$
g(\omega) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\gamma} \frac{z f'(z)}{f(z) - \omega}\, dz
$$
where $\gamma$ is the circle $|z| = R$. Show that $g$ is the inverse function of $f$ restricted to some neighborhood of $0$.
