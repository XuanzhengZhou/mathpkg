If $G$ is an open subset of $\mathbb{C}$ then $\text{Har}(G)$ is the space of harmonic functions on $G$. Since $\text{Har}(G) \subset C(G, \mathbb{R})$ it is given the metric that it inherits from $C(G, \mathbb{R})$.

2.16 Harnack’s Theorem. Let $G$ be a region. (a) The metric space $\text{Har}(G)$ is complete. (b) If $\{u_n\}$ is a sequence in $\text{Har}(G)$ such that $u_1 \leq u_2 \leq \ldots$ then either $u_n(z) \to \infty$ uniformly on compact subsets of $G$ or $\{u_n\}$ converges in $\text{Har}(G)$ to a harmonic function.
