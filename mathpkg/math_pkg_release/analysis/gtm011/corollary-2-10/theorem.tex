1. Let $f, f_1, f_2, \ldots$ be elements of $H(G)$ and show that $f_n \rightarrow f$ iff for each closed rectifiable curve $\gamma$ in $G, f_n(z) \rightarrow f(z)$ uniformly for $z$ in $\{\gamma\}$.

2. Let $G$ be a region, let $a \in \mathbb{R}$, and suppose that $f: [a, \infty] \times G \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ is a continuous function. Define the integral $F(z) = \int_a^\infty f(t, z)dt$ to be uniformly convergent on compact subsets of $G$ if $\lim_{b \to \infty} \int_a^b f(t, z)dt$ exists uniformly for $z$ in any compact subset of $G$. Suppose that this integral does converge uniformly on compact subsets of $G$ and that for each $t$ in $(a, \infty), f(t, \cdot)$ is analytic on $G$. Prove that $F$ is analytic and

$$F^{(k)}(z) = \int_a^\infty \frac{\partial^k f(t, z)}{\partial z^k} dt$$

3. The proof of Montel’s Theorem can be broken up into the following sequence of definitions and propositions: (a) Definition. A set $F \subset C(G, \mathbb{C})$ is locally Lipschitz if for each $a$ in $G$ there are constants $M$ and $r > 0$ such that $|f(z) - f(a)| \leq M|z - a|$ for all $f$ in $F$ and $|z - a| < r$. (b) If $F \subset C(G, \mathbb{C})$ is locally Lipschitz then $F$ is equicontinuous at each point of $G$. (c) If $F \subset H(G)$ is locally bounded then $F$ is locally Lipschitz.
