The Second Version of the Maximum Principle is a comparison theorem for two bounded continuous functions possessing the MVP. It states that if the limsup of $u$ is bounded above by the liminf of $v$ at every point of the extended boundary, then either $u$ is strictly less than $v$ everywhere in the region, or the two functions are identically equal. By reducing to the case $v \equiv 0$, this generalizes the First Version to handle boundary behavior and provides a powerful tool for uniqueness proofs.
