A harmonic function is a twice continuously differentiable real-valued function defined on an open subset of the complex plane that satisfies Laplace's equation. This means the sum of its second partial derivatives with respect to the real and imaginary coordinates vanishes everywhere on its domain. Harmonic functions arise naturally as the real and imaginary parts of analytic functions, and they share many regularity properties with analytic functions, including infinite differentiability and mean value properties.
