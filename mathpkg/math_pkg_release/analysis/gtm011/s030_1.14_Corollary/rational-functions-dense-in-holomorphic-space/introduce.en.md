Corollary 1.14 is a consequence of Runge's Theorem that characterizes the density of rational functions in the space of holomorphic functions $H(G)$. It states that if $E$ is a subset of the complement of $G$ in the extended plane that meets every component of $\mathbb{C}_{\infty} - G$, then the set $R(G, E)$ of rational functions whose poles lie in $E$ is dense in $H(G)$ under the topology of uniform convergence on compact sets. This result shows that any holomorphic function on $G$ can be uniformly approximated on compact subsets by rational functions with prescribed pole locations.
