The polynomially convex hull $\hat{K}$ of a compact set $K$ in the complex plane is the set of all points $w$ for which $|p(w)| \leq \|p\|_K$ holds for every polynomial $p$. By the Maximum Modulus Theorem, $\hat{K}$ is obtained from $K$ by filling in any bounded complementary components (holes). For example, if $K$ is an annulus, $\hat{K}$ is the filled-in disk. This notion is central to Runge's Theorem, where polynomial approximation is possible precisely on sets that are polynomially convex.
