Let $G$ be a simply connected region in $\mathbb{C}$ and let $\varphi: G \to \mathbb{C}$ be a bounded analytic function that never vanishes. Suppose $\partial_\infty G = A \cup B$ where $A$ and $B$ are disjoint. Let $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ be analytic and suppose there exists a constant $M$ such that:
\[
\text{(a)}\quad \limsup_{z \to a} |f(z)| \leq M \quad \text{for every } a \in A,
\]
\[
\text{(b)}\quad \limsup_{z \to b} |f(z)| \, |\varphi(z)|^\eta \leq M \quad \text{for every } b \in B \text{ and every } \eta > 0.
\]
Then $|f(z)| \leq M$ for all $z \in G$.
