This corollary applies the Phragmén-Lindelöf Theorem to angular sectors $G = \{z : |\arg z| < \pi/(2a)\}$. If an analytic function $f$ on $G$ is bounded by $M$ on the boundary $\partial G$ and grows at most like $\exp(|z|^b)$ with exponent $b < a$, then $|f(z)| \leq M$ throughout $G$. The auxiliary function is $\varphi(z) = \exp(-z^a)$, which is bounded and non-vanishing on the sector. The condition $b < a$ ensures that $|f(z)||\varphi(z)|^\eta$ is controlled on the boundary component at infinity.
