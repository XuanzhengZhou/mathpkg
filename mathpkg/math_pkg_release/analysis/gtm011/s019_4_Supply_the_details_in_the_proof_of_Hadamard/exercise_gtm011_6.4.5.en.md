Let $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ be analytic and suppose that $G$ is bounded. Fix $z_0 \in \partial G$ and suppose that $\limsup_{z \to w} |f(z)| \leq M$ for all $w \in \partial G$, $w \neq z_0$. Show that if $\lim_{z \to z_0} |z - z_0|^\varepsilon |f(z)| = 0$ for every $\varepsilon > 0$, then $|f(z)| \leq M$ for all $z \in G$.

(Hint: If $a \notin G$, consider $\varphi(z) = (z - z_0)/(z - a)$.)
