Let $G = \{z : |\operatorname{Im} z| < \tfrac{1}{2}\pi\}$ and suppose $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ is analytic and $\limsup_{z \to w} |f(z)| \leq M$ for all $w \in \partial G$. Also, suppose $A < \infty$ and $a < 1$ can be found such that
\[
|f(z)| < \exp\bigl[A \exp(a |\operatorname{Re} z|)\bigr]
\]
for all $z \in G$. Show that $|f(z)| \leq M$ for all $z \in G$.

Examine $\exp(\exp z)$ to see that this is the best possible growth condition. Can we take $a = 1$ above?
