This definition introduces the notion of symmetric points with respect to a circle in the extended complex plane $\mathbb{C}_\infty$, generalizing the familiar concept of geometric reflection across a line. Using the cross ratio, two points $z$ and $z^*$ are symmetric with respect to a circle $\Gamma$ if their cross ratios with any three points on $\Gamma$ are complex conjugates. For a line, this coincides with ordinary reflection; for a circle $|z-a|=R$, it yields the inversion formula $z^* = a + R^2/(\overline{z} - \overline{a})$.
