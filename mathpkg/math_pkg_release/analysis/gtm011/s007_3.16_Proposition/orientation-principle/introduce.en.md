The Orientation Principle describes how Möbius transformations respect the right and left sides of oriented circles. Given two circles $\Gamma_1$ and $\Gamma_2$ and a Möbius transformation $T$ mapping one onto the other, $T$ sends the right (resp. left) side of $\Gamma_1$ to the right (resp. left) side of $\Gamma_2$, provided we use the induced orientation $(Tz_1, Tz_2, Tz_3)$ on $\Gamma_2$. This principle is essential for constructing conformal maps that send specified regions onto the unit disk.
