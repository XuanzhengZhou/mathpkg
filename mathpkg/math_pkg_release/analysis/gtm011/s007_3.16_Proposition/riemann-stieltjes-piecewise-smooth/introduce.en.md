This theorem shows that when the integrator $\gamma$ is piecewise smooth, the Riemann-Stieltjes integral reduces to an ordinary Riemann integral: $\int_a^b f \, d\gamma = \int_a^b f(t) \gamma'(t) \, dt$. This is the practical formula used to compute contour integrals in complex analysis: one simply replaces $d\gamma(t)$ by $\gamma'(t) \, dt$ and integrates as in ordinary calculus. Together with Theorem 1.4, this provides both the theoretical foundation and the computational tool for complex line integrals.
