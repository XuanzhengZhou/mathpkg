The Cauchy-Riemann equations are the fundamental necessary condition for complex differentiability. They are obtained by computing the derivative $f'(z)$ along the real and imaginary directions and equating the two expressions. If $f = u + iv$ is analytic, then $u_x = v_y$ and $u_y = -v_x$. These equations connect complex analysis to real partial differential equations and are the starting point for the study of harmonic functions.
