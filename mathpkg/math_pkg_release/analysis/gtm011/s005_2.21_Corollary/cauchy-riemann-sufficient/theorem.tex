Let $G \subset \mathbb{C}$ be open and let $f(z) = u(x, y) + i v(x, y)$ where $z = x + iy$. Suppose that $u$ and $v$ have continuous first partial derivatives on $G$ and satisfy the Cauchy-Riemann equations

$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}, \quad \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}$$

at every point of $G$. Then $f$ is analytic on $G$ and

$$f'(z) = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x}(x, y) + i \frac{\partial v}{\partial x}(x, y).$$
