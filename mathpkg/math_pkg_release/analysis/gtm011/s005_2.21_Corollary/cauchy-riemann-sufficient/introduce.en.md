While the Cauchy-Riemann equations are necessary for analyticity, they become sufficient when combined with $C^1$ regularity of $u$ and $v$. The proof uses the mean value theorem to express the increment of $f$ in terms of partial derivatives, then exploits the continuity of $u_x$, $u_y$, $v_x$, $v_y$ and the Cauchy-Riemann relations to show the complex difference quotient converges. This characterizes analytic functions as $C^1$ solutions of the Cauchy-Riemann system.
