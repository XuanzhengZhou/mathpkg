A limit point (also called an accumulation point) of a subset $A$ in a topological space $X$ is a point $a \in X$ such that every open neighborhood of $a$ contains some point of $A$ other than $a$ itself. In metric spaces, this corresponds to the existence of a sequence in $A \setminus \{a\}$ converging to $a$, but in general topological spaces sequential convergence and limit points are not equivalent notions. A point can be a limit point of a set without there being any sequence in the set that converges to it.
