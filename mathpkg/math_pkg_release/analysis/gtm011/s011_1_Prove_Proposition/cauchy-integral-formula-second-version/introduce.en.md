Cauchy's Integral Formula (Second Version) generalizes the first version to multiple closed rectifiable curves $\gamma_1, \ldots, \gamma_m$. The hypothesis is that the sum of winding numbers around any point outside $G$ is zero, which allows the formula to handle multiply connected regions. The value $f(a)$ is expressed as a weighted sum of contour integrals, where the weights are the winding numbers of each curve around $a$. This version is particularly useful for regions like annuli, where two curves with opposite orientations enclose the region.
