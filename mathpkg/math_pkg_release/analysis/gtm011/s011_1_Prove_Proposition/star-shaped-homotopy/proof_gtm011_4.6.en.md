Let $\gamma_1$ be a closed rectifiable curve in $G$. Define $\Gamma(s, t) = a + t(\gamma_1(s) - a)$ for $0 \leq s, t \leq 1$. Then $\Gamma(s, 0) = a = \gamma_0(s)$, $\Gamma(s, 1) = \gamma_1(s)$, and $\Gamma(0, t) = a = \Gamma(1, t)$ since $\gamma_1(0) = \gamma_1(1)$. Since $G$ is $a$-star shaped, $\Gamma(s, t) \in G$ for all $s, t$. Thus $\gamma_1 \sim \gamma_0$.
