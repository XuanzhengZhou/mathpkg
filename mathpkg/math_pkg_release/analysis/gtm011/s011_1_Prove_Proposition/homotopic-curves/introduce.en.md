Two closed rectifiable curves $\gamma_0$ and $\gamma_1$ in a region $G$ are homotopic if one can be continuously deformed into the other within $G$. The deformation is described by a continuous function $\Gamma(s,t)$ on the unit square, where $\Gamma(s,0) = \gamma_0(s)$ and $\Gamma(s,1) = \gamma_1(s)$, with the additional condition that $\Gamma(0,t) = \Gamma(1,t)$ for all $t$, ensuring each intermediate curve remains closed. Homotopy is central to the topological version of Cauchy's Theorem, which states that integrals of analytic functions are invariant under homotopy.
