Cauchy's Theorem (Third Version) states that the contour integral of an analytic function is invariant under homotopy of the integration curve. If two closed rectifiable curves $\gamma_0$ and $\gamma_1$ can be continuously deformed into one another within the region $G$ where $f$ is analytic, then the integrals along both curves are equal. The proof uses a grid approximation of the homotopy by small polygonal paths, exploiting the local validity of Cauchy's Theorem in convex disks. This version is the most general form, subsuming the earlier versions for star-shaped regions and winding number conditions.
