The elementary factors $E_p(z)$ are fundamental building blocks introduced by Weierstrass for constructing analytic functions with prescribed zeros. They are designed so that $E_p(z/a)$ has exactly one simple zero at $z = a$ while being nonzero elsewhere. The parameter $p$ controls the growth rate and complexity of the factor; larger $p$ yields factors that approximate $1$ more rapidly near the origin, which is essential for ensuring convergence of infinite products.
