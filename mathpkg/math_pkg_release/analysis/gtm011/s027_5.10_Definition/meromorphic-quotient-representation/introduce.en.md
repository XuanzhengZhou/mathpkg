This corollary shows that every meromorphic function on an open set $G$ can be expressed as the quotient of two analytic functions. The proof constructs an analytic function $h$ whose zeros exactly match the poles of $f$ (with matching multiplicities), using the Weierstrass product construction. Then $g = hf$ has removable singularities at the poles and extends analytically, giving $f = g/h$. In algebraic terms, this says that $M(G)$ is the quotient field of the integral domain $H(G)$.
