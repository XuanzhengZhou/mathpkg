The derivative of the digamma function (the trigamma function) is obtained by differentiating the series for $\Gamma'/\Gamma$ term by term. The resulting series $\sum (n+z)^{-2}$ is manifestly positive for real $z > 0$, which implies that $\log \Gamma(x)$ is strictly convex on $(0, \infty)$. This logarithmic convexity is the key property used in the Bohr-Mollerup theorem to characterize the gamma function uniquely.
