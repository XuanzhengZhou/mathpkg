This proposition computes the residue of the gamma function at each of its simple poles $z = -n$ ($n \geq 0$). The result $\operatorname{Res}(\Gamma; -n) = (-1)^n / n!$ follows from the shift formula $\Gamma(z+n) = z(z+1)\cdots(z+n-1)\Gamma(z)$ by analyzing the Laurent expansion of $\Gamma(z)$ near $z = -n$. These residues are essential for contour integration applications involving the gamma function and its relatives.
