This lemma provides a sharp estimate for the deviation of the elementary factor $E_p(z)$ from $1$ when $|z| \leq 1$. The inequality $|1 - E_p(z)| \leq |z|^{p+1}$ shows that as $p$ increases, the factor approaches $1$ more rapidly near the origin. This estimate is the crucial ingredient for applying Theorem 5.9 to prove convergence of infinite products of the form $\prod E_{p_n}(z/a_n)$ in the Weierstrass product construction.
