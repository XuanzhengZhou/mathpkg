This technical lemma establishes uniform bounds for the integral $\int e^{-t} t^{z-1} dt$ on vertical strips $S = \{a \leq \operatorname{Re} z \leq A\}$ with $0 < a < A$. Part (a) controls the behavior near $t = 0$ (uniformly for $z \in S$), and part (b) controls the tail as $t \to \infty$. Together, these estimates show that Euler's integral $\Gamma(z) = \int_0^\infty e^{-t} t^{z-1} dt$ converges uniformly on compact subsets of $S$, establishing the analyticity of this integral representation.
