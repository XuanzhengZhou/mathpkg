The functional equation $\Gamma(z+1) = z\Gamma(z)$ is the most fundamental property of the gamma function, extending the factorial recurrence $n! = n \cdot (n-1)!$ to the complex plane. Together with $\Gamma(1) = 1$, this implies $\Gamma(n+1) = n!$ for all non-negative integers $n$. The equation follows directly from Gauss's limit formula by algebraic manipulation of the limit expression.
