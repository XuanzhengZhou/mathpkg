Let $f_1$ and $f_2$ be entire functions of finite orders $\lambda_1$ and $\lambda_2$ respectively. Show that $f = f_1 + f_2$ has finite order $\lambda$ and $\lambda \leq \max(\lambda_1, \lambda_2)$. Show that $\lambda = \max(\lambda_1, \lambda_2)$ if $\lambda_1 \neq \lambda_2$ and give an example which shows that $\lambda < \max(\lambda_1, \lambda_2)$ with $f \neq 0$.
