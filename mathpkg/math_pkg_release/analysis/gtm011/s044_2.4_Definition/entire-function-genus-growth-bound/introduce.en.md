This theorem shows that the genus $\mu$ of an entire function controls its maximum modulus growth. Specifically, for any $\alpha > 0$, the inequality $|f(z)| < \exp(\alpha |z|^{\mu+1})$ holds for all sufficiently large $|z|$. The proof combines estimates for the Weierstrass primary factors with the convergence of $\sum |a_n|^{-(\mu+1)}$, which follows from the definition of rank. This result motivates the later equivalence between finite genus and finite order.
