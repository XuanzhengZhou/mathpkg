This corollary provides a practical reformulation of the Great Picard Theorem in terms of singularity classification. If an analytic function has an isolated singularity at $z = a$ and there exist two complex numbers each of which is assumed only finitely many times (rather than infinitely often) by $f$ in a deleted neighborhood of $a$, then the singularity at $a$ cannot be essential. Consequently, $z = a$ must be either a pole or a removable singularity. This gives a useful criterion: to verify that an isolated singularity is essential, it suffices to exhibit two values that are not assumed infinitely often.
