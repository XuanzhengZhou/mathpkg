This proposition establishes that differentiability of a complex-valued function on a real interval is equivalent to the simultaneous differentiability of its real and imaginary parts. The derivative decomposes linearly: $f'(x) = (\operatorname{Re} f)'(x) + i(\operatorname{Im} f)'(x)$. This allows one to reduce calculus questions about complex-valued functions to the well-understood theory of real-valued derivatives. The proof is straightforward by considering the real and imaginary parts of the defining difference quotient.
