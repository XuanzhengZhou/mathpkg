This definition extends the classical notion of differentiability from real-valued to complex-valued functions defined on a compact interval $[a,b]$. The limit definition is identical to the real case, with the only difference being that the values lie in $\mathbb{C}$. One-sided derivatives are used at the endpoints $a$ and $b$. This provides the foundation for studying calculus properties of complex-valued functions on intervals, including the Chain Rule and the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
