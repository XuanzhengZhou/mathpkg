Corollary 1.7 is a direct consequence of Bloch's Theorem, translating the statement about $g$ back to the original function $f$. It asserts that for an analytic function $f$ on a region containing the closed disk $\bar{B}(0; R)$, the image $f(B(0; R))$ contains a disk of radius at least $\frac{1}{72} R|f'(0)|$. This provides an explicit lower bound on the size of a schlicht disk in the image, expressed purely in terms of the domain radius and the magnitude of the derivative at the origin.
