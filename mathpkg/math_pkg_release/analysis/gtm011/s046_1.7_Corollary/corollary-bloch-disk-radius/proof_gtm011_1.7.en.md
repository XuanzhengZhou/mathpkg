Apply Bloch's Theorem to the function

$$g(z) = \frac{f(Rz) - f(0)}{R f'(0)}.$$

The result is trivial if $f'(0) = 0$, so it may be assumed that $f'(0) \neq 0$. By Bloch's Theorem, $g$ maps some subdisk of the unit disk one-to-one onto a disk of radius at least $\frac{1}{72}$. Translating this back to $f$ yields the stated result: $f(B(0; R))$ contains a disk of radius $\frac{1}{72} R|f'(0)|$.
