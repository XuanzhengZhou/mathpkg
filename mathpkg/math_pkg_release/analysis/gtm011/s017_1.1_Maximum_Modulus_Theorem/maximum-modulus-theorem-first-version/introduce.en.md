The Maximum Modulus Theorem (First Version) is a fundamental result in complex analysis. It states that if an analytic function on a region attains its maximum absolute value at an interior point, then the function must be constant. The proof is elegantly short, relying on the Open Mapping Theorem: if $f$ were non-constant, its image would be open, contradicting the fact that the maximum modulus point lies on the boundary of the image. This version highlights the deep interplay between the algebraic properties of analytic functions and their geometric behavior.
