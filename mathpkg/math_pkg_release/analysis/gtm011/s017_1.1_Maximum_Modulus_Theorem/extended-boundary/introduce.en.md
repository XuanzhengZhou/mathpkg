The extended boundary $\partial_{\infty} G$ of a subset $G \subset \mathbb{C}$ is its boundary in the extended complex plane $\mathbb{C}_{\infty}$ (the Riemann sphere). If $G$ is bounded, the extended boundary coincides with the usual boundary $\partial G$; if $G$ is unbounded, the point at infinity $\infty$ is adjoined, so $\partial_{\infty} G = \partial G \cup \{\infty\}$. This concept allows the Maximum Modulus Theorem to be stated uniformly for both bounded and unbounded regions.
