The relative topology, also known as the subspace topology, is the natural way to make a subset of a topological space into a topological space itself. Given a topological space $(X, \mathcal{T})$ and a subset $Y \subset X$, the open sets of the relative topology are the intersections of open sets of $X$ with $Y$. This construction generalizes the way a subset of a metric space inherits a metric. Whenever a subset of a topological space is regarded as a topological space, it is assumed to carry the relative topology unless stated otherwise.
