The set of all real numbers is denoted by $\mathbb{R}$. It is the fundamental number system upon which complex analysis is built. The real numbers are equipped with an ordering, and every reader of Conway's text is expected to be thoroughly acquainted with their properties including supremum, infimum, completeness, sequential convergence, and infinite series.
