Suppose $|f(z)| \leq M$ for all $z$ in $\mathbb{C}$. We will show that $f'(z) = 0$ for all $z$ in $\mathbb{C}$. To do this, use Cauchy's Estimate (Corollary 2.14). Since $f$ is analytic in any disk $B(z; R)$, we have that

$$|f'(z)| \leq \frac{M}{R}.$$

Since $R$ was arbitrary, it follows that $f'(z) = 0$ for each $z$ in $\mathbb{C}$. Hence $f$ is constant.
