Let $G$ be an open subset of the plane and $f: G \to \mathbb{C}$ an analytic function. If $\gamma_1, \ldots, \gamma_m$ are closed rectifiable curves in $G$ such that $n(\gamma_1; w) + \cdots + n(\gamma_m; w) = 0$ for all $w$ in $\mathbb{C} - G$ then for $a$ in $G - \{\gamma\}$ and $k \geq 1$

$$f^{(k)}(a) \sum_{j=1}^{m} n(\gamma_j; a) = k! \sum_{j=1}^{m} \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\gamma_j} \frac{f(z)}{(z-a)^{k+1}} dz.$$
