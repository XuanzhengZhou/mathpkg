Proposition 5.6 establishes two fundamental relationships between arcwise connectedness and topological connectedness. Part (a) states that arcwise connectedness is a stronger property than connectedness: every arcwise connected set is connected. Part (b) shows that in a locally arcwise connected space, the connected components are open sets, which is a crucial property for the topology of sheaf spaces and Riemann surfaces.
