Theorem 5.11 (with Corollary) characterizes the connected components of the sheaf of germs $\mathcal{S}(G)$ purely in terms of analytic continuation. The component containing a point $(a, [f]_a)$ consists exactly of all germs $[g]_b$ that can be reached from $[f]_a$ by analytic continuation along some curve in $G$. This gives a concrete description of the topological structure of the sheaf space and connects it intimately to the classical notion of analytic continuation.
