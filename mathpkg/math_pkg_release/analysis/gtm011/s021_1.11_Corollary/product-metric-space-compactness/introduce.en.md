This proposition states three key facts about the countable Cartesian product $X = \prod_{n=1}^\infty X_n$ of metric spaces $(X_n, d_n)$, equipped with the weighted metric $d(\xi, \eta) = \sum 2^{-n} d_n(x_n, y_n)/(1 + d_n(x_n, y_n))$. First, $d$ is indeed a metric on $X$. Second, convergence in $(X, d)$ coincides with coordinate-wise convergence. Third, and most importantly, a countable product of compact metric spaces is compact — this is the metric-space version of the Tychonoff theorem for countable products.
