Let $X$ be the set of all bounded sequences of complex numbers. That is, $\{x_n\} \in X$ iff $\sup \{|x_n| : n \geq 1\} < \infty$. If $x = \{x_n\}$ and $y = \{y_n\}$, define $d(x, y) = \sup \{|x_n - y_n| : n \geq 1\}$. Show that for each $x$ in $X$ and $\epsilon > 0$, $\overline{B}(x; \epsilon)$ is not totally bounded although it is complete. (Hint: you might have an easier time of it if you first show that you can assume $x = (0, 0, \ldots)$.)
