Put a metric $d$ on $\mathbb{R}$ such that $|x_n - x| \to 0$ if and only if $d(x_n, x) \to 0$, but that $\{x_n\}$ is a Cauchy sequence in $(\mathbb{R}, d)$ when $|x_n| \to \infty$. (Hint: Take inspiration from $\mathbb{C}_{\infty}$.)
