A component of a metric space is a maximal connected subset -- it is connected and not properly contained in any larger connected subset. Components partition the space into its "connected pieces." For example, the components of $X = \{0, 1, \frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{3}, \ldots\}$ are the singleton points.
