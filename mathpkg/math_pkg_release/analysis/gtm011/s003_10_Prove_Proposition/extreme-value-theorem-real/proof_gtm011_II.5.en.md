If $\alpha = \sup \{f(x): x \in K\}$ then $\alpha$ is in $\overline{f(K)} = f(K)$ because $f(K)$ is compact (hence closed and bounded) in $\mathbb{R}$. Similarly $\beta = \inf \{f(x): x \in K\}$ is in $f(K)$.
