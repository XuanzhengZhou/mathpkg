Just as in real analysis, complex differentiability at a point implies continuity at that point. The proof follows from the product rule for limits: $|f(z) - f(a)| = \frac{|f(z)-f(a)|}{|z-a|} \cdot |z-a| \to |f'(a)| \cdot 0 = 0$.
