The space $\text{Har}(G)$ consists of all real-valued harmonic functions defined on an open subset $G$ of the complex plane. It is naturally a subspace of $C(G, \mathbb{R})$, the space of continuous real-valued functions on $G$ equipped with the topology of uniform convergence on compact sets. The metric on $\text{Har}(G)$ is the one inherited from $C(G, \mathbb{R})$, making it a metric space whose completeness properties are studied via Harnack's Theorem.
