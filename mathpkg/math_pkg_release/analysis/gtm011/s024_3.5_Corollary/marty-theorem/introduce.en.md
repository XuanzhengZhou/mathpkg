Marty's Theorem characterizes normal families of meromorphic functions in terms of the spherical derivative $\mu(f)$. A family $\mathcal{F} \subset M(G)$ is normal (i.e., precompact in the topology of uniform convergence on compact sets with respect to the chordal metric) precisely when the set of spherical derivatives $\{\mu(f) : f \in \mathcal{F}\}$ is locally bounded on $G$. This extends Montel's theorem from analytic functions to meromorphic functions and is a fundamental tool in complex dynamics and value distribution theory. The forward direction uses the fact that bounded spherical derivative implies uniform Lipschitz continuity, hence equicontinuity.
