If a sequence of analytic functions on an open set $G$ converges uniformly on compact subsets to a continuous function $f$, then the limit function $f$ must also be analytic. Moreover, the sequence of $k$-th derivatives converges uniformly on compact subsets to $f^{(k)}$ for every $k \geq 1$. This theorem has no analogue in real analysis — the uniform limit of differentiable real functions need not even be differentiable.
