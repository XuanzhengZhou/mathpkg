This proposition establishes the equivalence between the Euclidean metric topology on $\mathbb{C}$ and the chordal metric topology on $\mathbb{C}_\infty$. Near any finite point, chordal balls and Euclidean balls are mutually contained within one another. Near $\infty$, chordal balls correspond to complements of large compact sets in $\mathbb{C}$, and vice versa. The proof is left to the reader.
