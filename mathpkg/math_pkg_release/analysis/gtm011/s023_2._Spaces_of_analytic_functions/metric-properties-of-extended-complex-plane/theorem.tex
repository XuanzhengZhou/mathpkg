Let $d$ denote the chordal metric on the extended complex plane $\mathbb{C}_\infty$, and let $B_\infty(a; \rho)$ denote the chordal ball of radius $\rho$ centered at $a$, while $B(a; r)$ denotes the Euclidean ball.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] If $a \in \mathbb{C}$ and $r > 0$, then there exists $\rho > 0$ such that $B_\infty(a; \rho) \subset B(a; r)$.
\item[(b)] Conversely, if $\rho > 0$ and $a \in \mathbb{C}$, then there exists $r > 0$ such that $B(a; r) \subset B_\infty(a; \rho)$.
\item[(c)] If $\rho > 0$, then there exists a compact set $K \subset \mathbb{C}$ such that $\mathbb{C}_\infty \setminus K \subset B_\infty(\infty; \rho)$.
\item[(d)] Conversely, if $K \subset \mathbb{C}$ is compact, then there exists $\rho > 0$ such that $B_\infty(\infty; \rho) \subset \mathbb{C}_\infty \setminus K$.
\end{enumerate}
