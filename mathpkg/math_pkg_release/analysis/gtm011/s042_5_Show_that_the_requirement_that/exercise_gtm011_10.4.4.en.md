This exercise asks for an easier proof of a special case of Theorem 4.9. Let $G$ be a bounded region and let $a \in \partial G$ such that there is a point $b$ with $[a, b] \cap G^- = \{a\}$. Show that $G$ has a barrier at $a$. (Hint: Consider the transformation $(z-a)(z-b)^{-1}$.)
