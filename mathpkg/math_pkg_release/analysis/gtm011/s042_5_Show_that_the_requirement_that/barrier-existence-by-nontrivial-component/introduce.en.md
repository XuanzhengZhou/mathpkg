This theorem provides a geometric criterion for the existence of a barrier at a boundary point: if the component of the complement containing that boundary point is not a singleton, then a barrier exists. The proof uses Lemma 4.8 to replace $\mathbb{C}_\infty - G$ with a closed connected set $S$, then constructs a conformal map from the resulting simply connected region to the right half-plane, finally synthesizing barrier functions from harmonic measures of complementary intervals on the imaginary axis via Harnack's Theorem.
