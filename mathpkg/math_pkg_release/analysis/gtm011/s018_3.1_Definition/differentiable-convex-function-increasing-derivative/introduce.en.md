For differentiable functions on a real interval, convexity admits a simple differential characterization: the function is convex if and only if its derivative is an increasing function. The forward direction uses the convexity inequality and limit arguments to compare slopes, while the reverse direction applies the Mean Value Theorem to reconstruct the convexity inequality from the monotonicity of the derivative.
