Hadamard's Three Lines Theorem states that for a bounded analytic function on a vertical strip in the complex plane, the logarithm of its supremum norm on each vertical line is a convex function of the horizontal coordinate. This result connects complex analysis with convexity theory and has an important corollary known as Hadamard's Three Circles Theorem for annuli. The theorem is a powerful tool for estimating the growth of analytic functions.
