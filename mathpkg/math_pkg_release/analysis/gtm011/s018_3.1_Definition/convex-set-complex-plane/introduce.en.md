A subset of the complex plane is convex if, for any two points in the set, the entire line segment joining them is also contained in the set. This extends the elementary geometric notion of convexity from real Euclidean space to the complex numbers. Convex sets play a fundamental role in complex analysis, appearing in results such as the characterization of convex functions via the convexity of their epigraphs and in geometric formulations of the maximum principle.
