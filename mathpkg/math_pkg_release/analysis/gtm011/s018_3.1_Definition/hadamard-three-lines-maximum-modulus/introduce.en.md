This corollary to Hadamard's Three Lines Theorem gives a strong maximum principle for bounded analytic functions on a vertical strip: the modulus of a non-constant analytic function in the interior of the strip is strictly less than the supremum of its modulus on the boundary. This follows from the convexity of $\log M(x)$ and the fact that a convex function on a closed interval attains its maximum at the endpoints.
