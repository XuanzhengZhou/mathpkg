A periodic distribution is a continuous linear functional on the space $\mathcal{P}$ of smooth $2\pi$-periodic functions. It generalizes the notion of a periodic function: every continuous periodic function induces a periodic distribution via integration, but not every periodic distribution arises this way. The theory of periodic distributions allows one to differentiate any periodic distribution infinitely many times, providing a powerful framework for solving differential equations with periodic boundary conditions.
