Proposition 5.2 states that the distributional derivative $DF$ is the limit of difference quotients $t^{-1}(T_{-t}F - F)$ in the sense of $\mathcal{P}'$ as $t \to 0$. This mirrors the classical definition of the derivative for smooth functions and confirms that the distributional derivative is the natural extension. The proof uses the fact that $t^{-1}[u - T_t u] \to Du$ in $\mathcal{P}$, which was established earlier for smooth periodic functions.
