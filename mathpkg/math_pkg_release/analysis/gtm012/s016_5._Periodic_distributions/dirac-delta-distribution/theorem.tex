Let $\delta: \mathcal{C} \to \mathbb{C}$ be defined by

$$\delta(u) = u(0), \quad u \in \mathcal{C}.$$

Then the restriction of $\delta$ to $\mathcal{P}$ is a periodic distribution, called the \textit{$\delta$-distribution} or \textit{Dirac $\delta$-distribution}. It is not a function: for

$$u_n(x) = \left(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} \cos x\right)^n,$$

we have $\delta(u_n) = 1$ for all $n$, but $u_n(x) \to 0$ uniformly for $x \in [\varepsilon, 2\pi - \varepsilon]$ for any $\varepsilon > 0$, and $0 \leq u_n(x) \leq 1$ for all $x,n$. It follows that for any $v \in \mathcal{C}$, $F_v(u_n) \to 0$. Thus $\delta \neq F_v$.
