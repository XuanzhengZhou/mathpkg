For $u \in \mathcal{P}$, let $\tilde{u}(x) = u(-x)$. For $v \in \mathcal{C}$,

$$F_v(u) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} v(-x)u(x) \, dx = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} v(x)u(-x) \, dx = F_v(\tilde{u}).$$

We define $F^\sim$, for $F \in \mathcal{P}'$, by

$$F^\sim(u) = F(\tilde{u}), \quad u \in \mathcal{P}.$$
