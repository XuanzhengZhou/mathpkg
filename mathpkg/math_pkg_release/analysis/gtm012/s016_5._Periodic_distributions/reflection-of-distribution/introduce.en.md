The reflection (or reversal) of a periodic distribution $F$ is defined by $F^\sim(u) = F(\tilde{u})$, where $\tilde{u}(x) = u(-x)$. This extends the classical reflection of functions: the reflected distribution acts on a test function exactly as the original distribution acts on the reflected test function. The operation is consistent with the function embedding: $(F_v)^\sim = F_{\tilde{v}}$.
