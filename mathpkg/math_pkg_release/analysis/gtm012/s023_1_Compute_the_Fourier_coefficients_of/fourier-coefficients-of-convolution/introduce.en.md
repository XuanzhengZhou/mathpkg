Theorem 2.1 shows that convolution in the space of periodic distributions corresponds to pointwise multiplication of Fourier coefficients. If $F$ and $G$ have Fourier coefficients $(a_n)$ and $(b_n)$, and a smooth function $u$ has coefficients $(c_n)$, then the convolution $F * u$ has Fourier coefficients $(a_n c_n)$ and the convolution $F * G$ has coefficients $(a_n b_n)$. This elegant relationship — the Fourier transform exchanges convolution with pointwise multiplication — mirrors the classical convolution theorem and is fundamental for solving PDEs on the circle via Fourier methods.
