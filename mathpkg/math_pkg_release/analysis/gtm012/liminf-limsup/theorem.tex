Let $(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ be a bounded sequence in $\mathbb{R}$. For each $n$, let
$$A_n = \{x_m \mid m \geq n\},$$
and let $x_n' = \operatorname{glb} A_n$, $x_n'' = \operatorname{lub} A_n$. Both $(x_n')$ and $(x_n'')$ are bounded monotone sequences. Define
$$\liminf_{n \to \infty} x_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n', \qquad \limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n''.$$
These numbers are called the \textbf{lower limit} and \textbf{upper limit} respectively.
