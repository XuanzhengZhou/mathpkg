A complex-valued function of a real variable is differentiable at a point if and only if both its real and imaginary parts are differentiable there. The derivative is then the sum of the real derivative and $i$ times the imaginary derivative. This follows from the fact that the difference quotient splits into real and imaginary parts, and a complex limit exists if and only if both real limits exist.
