The standard differentiation rules carry over from real-valued to complex-valued functions of a real variable without change: the derivative is linear, the product rule holds, and the quotient rule holds wherever the denominator is nonzero. The proofs are identical to the real case because the algebraic manipulations of difference quotients do not depend on the field being $\mathbb{R}$ rather than $\mathbb{C}$.
