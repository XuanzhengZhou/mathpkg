\textbf{Proposition 3.3.} Suppose $f: (a, b) \to \mathbb{C}$ and $g: (a, b) \to \mathbb{C}$ are differentiable at $x \in (a, b)$, and suppose $c \in \mathbb{C}$. Then the functions $f + g$, $cf$, and $fg$ are differentiable at $x$, and
\begin{align*}
(f + g)'(x) &= f'(x) + g'(x),\\
(cf)'(x) &= c f'(x),\\
(fg)'(x) &= f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x).
\end{align*}
If $g(x) \neq 0$ then $f/g$ is differentiable at $x$ and
$$(f/g)'(x) = \bigl[f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)\bigr]\,g(x)^{-2}.$$
