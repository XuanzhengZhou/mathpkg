# Exercise 3

Suppose \(f, g: [a, b] \to \mathbb{C}\) are bounded, \(f\) is integrable, and \(g(x) = f(x)\) except on a finite set of points in \([a, b]\). Show that \(g\) is integrable and \(\int_a^b g = \int_a^b f\).

*Hint:* Use induction on the number of exceptional points and Proposition 2.5 to reduce to the case of a single exceptional point. Show that changing the value at one point does not affect the limit of Riemann sums.
