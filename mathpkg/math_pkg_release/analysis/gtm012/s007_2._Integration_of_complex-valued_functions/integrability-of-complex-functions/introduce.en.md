This proposition reduces the integrability of a complex-valued function to the integrability of its real and imaginary parts. It uses the fundamental inequality $\frac{1}{2}|x| + \frac{1}{2}|y| \leq |x+iy| \leq |x| + |y|$ to show that the Riemann sums converge in the complex plane if and only if their real and imaginary parts converge separately. The integral of the complex function is then the sum of the integrals of its real and imaginary parts with the imaginary unit.
