A Riemann sum approximates the integral of a function by sampling the function at the right endpoint of each subinterval determined by a partition and summing the signed areas of rectangles. For complex-valued functions, the Riemann sum is a complex number whose real and imaginary parts are the Riemann sums of the real and imaginary parts of the function.
