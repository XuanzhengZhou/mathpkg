Theorem 7.3 gives a purely intrinsic characterization of the Green's function without reference to contour integrals. The five properties express: $G$ is a homogeneous solution for $t > 0$ (7); it is initially flat up to order $n-2$ (8); it has the exponential-polynomial form dictated by the roots (9); the $(n-1)$st derivative jumps by $1/a_n$ at the origin (10); and it is causal, vanishing for negative time (11). These conditions determine $G$ uniquely, as shown by factoring $p(D)$ into first-order operators.
