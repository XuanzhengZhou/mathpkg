# Exercise 1

To what extent are the functions $v$ and $f$ in Theorem 6.1 (Structure Theorem) uniquely determined?

Recall the structure theorem: if $F \in \mathcal{P}'$ is of order $k + 2$, $k \geq 0$, then there exist $v \in \mathcal{C}$ and a constant function $f$ such that

$$F = D^k F_v + F_f.$$

Investigate the non-uniqueness: if also $F = D^k F_w + F_g$ with $w \in \mathcal{C}$ and $g$ constant, what can be said about the relationship between $v$ and $w$, and between $f$ and $g$? Consider the kernel of the operator $D^k$ on the space of distributions induced by continuous functions.
