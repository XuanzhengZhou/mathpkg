# Exercise 4

Prove the identities in Exercise 3 by approximating the distributions $F, G, H$ by smooth periodic functions.

That is, take sequences $(f_n)$, $(g_n)$, $(h_n)$ in $\mathcal{P}$ such that $F_{f_n} \to F$, $F_{g_n} \to G$, $F_{h_n} \to H$ in $\mathcal{P}'$. Then prove each identity for the smooth case (where the convolution reduces to the ordinary convolution of functions), and pass to the limit using Corollary 7.6 (sequential continuity of convolution under distribution convergence).

This method illustrates the general principle that identities for distributions can often be proved by first establishing them for smooth functions and then taking limits.
