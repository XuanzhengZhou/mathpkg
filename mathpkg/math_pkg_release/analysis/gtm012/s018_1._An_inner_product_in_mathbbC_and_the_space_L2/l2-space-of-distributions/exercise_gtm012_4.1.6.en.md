# Exercise 6

Show that $\delta$, the $\delta$-distribution (defined by $\delta(v) = v(0)$ for $v \in \mathcal{P}$), is not in $\mathbf{L}^2$.

*Hint.* Suppose $\delta \in \mathbf{L}^2$. Then there exists a $\|\cdot\|$-Cauchy sequence $(u_n) \subset \mathcal{C}$ such that $F_{u_n} \to \delta$ in $\mathcal{P}'$. Derive a contradiction by considering test functions with small $\|\cdot\|$-norm but large value at $0$ (e.g., use the sequence from Exercise 1).
