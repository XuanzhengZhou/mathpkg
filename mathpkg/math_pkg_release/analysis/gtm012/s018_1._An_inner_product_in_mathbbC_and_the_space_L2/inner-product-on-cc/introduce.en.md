The inner product on the space $\mathbb{C}$ of continuous complex-valued periodic functions is defined as the normalized integral of the product of one function with the complex conjugate of the other over the interval $[0, 2\pi]$. This construction endows $\mathbb{C}$ with a Hermitian inner product structure, satisfying conjugate symmetry, linearity in the first argument, and positive definiteness. The definition serves as the foundation for the $L^2$ theory developed in this chapter.
