# Exercise 1

Carry out the proof that $\mathcal{C}$ is not complete with respect to the norm $\|u\|$.

*Hint.* Consider the sequence of periodic functions $u_n: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ whose graph contains the line segments joining the pairs of points

$$(0, 0), \quad \left(\frac{1}{2}\pi, 0\right);$$
$$\left(\frac{1}{2}\pi, 0\right), \quad \left(\frac{1}{2}\pi + \frac{1}{n}, 1\right);$$
$$\left(\frac{1}{2}\pi + \frac{1}{n}, 1\right), \quad \left(\frac{3}{2}\pi - \frac{1}{n}, 1\right);$$
$$\left(\frac{3}{2}\pi - \frac{1}{n}, 1\right), \quad \left(\frac{3}{2}\pi, 0\right);$$
$$\left(\frac{3}{2}\pi, 0\right), \quad (2\pi, 0).$$

Extend periodically. Show that $(u_n)$ is Cauchy with respect to $\|\cdot\|$ but does not converge to any continuous function.
