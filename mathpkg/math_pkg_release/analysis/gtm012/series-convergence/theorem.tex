Given a sequence $(z_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ in $\mathbb{C}$, the associated series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} z_n$ has partial sums $s_n = \sum_{m=1}^{n} z_m$. The series \textit{converges} if $(s_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ converges to a limit $s$, written $\sum z_n = s$. Otherwise it \textit{diverges}. A necessary condition for convergence is $z_n \to 0$.
