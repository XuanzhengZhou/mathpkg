A continuous linear functional on $\mathcal{L}$ is a linear functional that respects convergence in the Schwartz space topology. Continuity means that whenever a sequence of test functions converges in the sense of $\mathcal{L}$, the values of the functional converge as complex numbers. The set of all such functionals forms the space of distributions $\mathcal{L}'$.
