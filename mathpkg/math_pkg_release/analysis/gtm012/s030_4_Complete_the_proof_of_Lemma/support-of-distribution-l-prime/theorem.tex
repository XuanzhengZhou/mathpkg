A point $t \in \mathbb{R}$ is not in the support of $F \in \mathcal{L}'$ if and only if there exists $\varepsilon > 0$ such that
$$F(u) = 0$$
whenever $u \in \mathcal{L}$ and $\operatorname{supp}(u) \subset (t - \varepsilon, t + \varepsilon)$.

The support of $F$ is denoted $\operatorname{supp}(F)$; it is the smallest closed subset of $\mathbb{R}$ outside of which $F$ vanishes on all test functions supported there.
