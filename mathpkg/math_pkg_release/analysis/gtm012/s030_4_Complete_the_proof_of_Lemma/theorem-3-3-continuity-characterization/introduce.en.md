Theorem 3.3 gives a necessary and sufficient condition for a linear functional on $\mathcal{L}$ to be continuous: it must be bounded by some constant times a single seminorm $|u|_{k,a,M}$. This is the fundamental estimate that characterizes all distributions of type $\mathcal{L}'$. The theorem shows that although the topology of $\mathcal{L}$ is defined by a countable family of seminorms, continuity with respect to that family is equivalent to domination by a single seminorm.
