The support of a function $u: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ is the smallest closed subset $A$ of $\mathbb{R}$ such that $u(t) = 0$ for every $t \notin A$. Equivalently, $t$ is not in the support of $u$ if and only if there exists $\varepsilon > 0$ such that $u$ is zero on the interval $(t - \varepsilon, t + \varepsilon)$. The support is denoted $\operatorname{supp}(u)$.
