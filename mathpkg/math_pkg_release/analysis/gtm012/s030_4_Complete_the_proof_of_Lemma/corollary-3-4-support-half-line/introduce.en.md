Corollary 3.4 follows directly from Theorem 3.3: the seminorm estimate $|F(u)| \leq K|u|_{k,a,M}$ forces $F$ to vanish on test functions supported sufficiently far to the left. This means every distribution in $\mathcal{L}'$ is "causal" in the sense that its support lies in a right half-line. This property is essential for the Laplace transform theory, where distributions are assumed to vanish for large negative times.
