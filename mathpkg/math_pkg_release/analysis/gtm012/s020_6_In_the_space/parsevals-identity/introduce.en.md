Parseval's identity expresses the inner product of two Hilbert space elements as the sum of products of their Fourier coefficients. It follows directly from the continuity of the inner product and the orthogonal expansion theorem. Combined with Bessel's equality (which is the special case $u = v$), Parseval's identity shows that the mapping $u \mapsto ((u, e_n))_n$ is a unitary isomorphism from $H$ onto $\ell^2$.
