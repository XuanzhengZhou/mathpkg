# Exercise 7

Prove the second part of Proposition 3.4. That is, suppose $(x_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ is a bounded sequence in $\mathbb{R}$. Show that $\limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n$ is the unique number $x''$ with the properties

(i)$''$  for any $\varepsilon > 0$, there is an $N$ such that $x_n < x'' + \varepsilon$ whenever $n \geq N$,

(ii)$''$ for any $\varepsilon > 0$ and any $N$, there is an $n \geq N$ such that $x_n > x'' - \varepsilon$.

*Hint.* Recall that $\limsup x_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} x''_n$ where $x''_n = \sup\{x_m : m \geq n\}$. Mimic the proof for $\liminf$ (the first part of Proposition 3.4).
