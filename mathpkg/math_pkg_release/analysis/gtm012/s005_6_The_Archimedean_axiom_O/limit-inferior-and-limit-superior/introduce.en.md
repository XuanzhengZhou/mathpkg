For a bounded real sequence, the limit inferior (liminf) and limit superior (limsup) capture the smallest and largest accumulation points, respectively. They are defined via the limits of the monotone sequences of infima and suprema of the tail sets. Unlike the ordinary limit, liminf and limsup always exist for bounded sequences. A sequence converges if and only if its liminf and limsup coincide, in which case the common value is the limit.
