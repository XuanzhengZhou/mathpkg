The Fourier series of an $L^2$ distribution expands it in the orthonormal basis of complex exponentials $e^{inx}$. The expansion converges in the $L^2$ norm (not necessarily pointwise), and Parseval's identity equates the $L^2$ norm with the $\ell^2$ norm of the Fourier coefficients. For smooth functions, uniform convergence holds. This is one of the most important results in analysis, linking harmonic analysis to Hilbert space theory.
