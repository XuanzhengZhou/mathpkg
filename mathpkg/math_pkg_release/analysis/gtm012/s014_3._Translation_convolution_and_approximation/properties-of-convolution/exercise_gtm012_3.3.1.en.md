# Exercise 1

Let $e_k(x) = \exp(i k x)$, $k = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots$. These functions are in $\mathcal{P}$ (see $\S 6$ of Chapter 2). Suppose $u \in \mathcal{C}$. Show that

$$e_k * u = a_k e_k,$$

where

$$a_k = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} e^{-i k y} u(y) \, dy.$$
