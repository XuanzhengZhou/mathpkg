Corollary 3.3 strengthens Lemma 3.2 by showing that the difference quotient $t^{-1}(T_{-t}u - u)$ converges to the derivative $Du$ not only uniformly, but in the full $\mathcal{P}$ topology, meaning convergence holds for all derivatives. The proof uses the fact that $D^k$ commutes with $T_{-t}$ (up to a sign), reducing the problem to applying Lemma 3.2 to each derivative $D^k u$. This result is essential for establishing that convolution with a smooth function is differentiable term-by-term.
