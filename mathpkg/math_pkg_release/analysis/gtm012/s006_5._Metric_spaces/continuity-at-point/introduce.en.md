A function between metric spaces is continuous at a point if small changes in the input produce arbitrarily small changes in the output. This is the classic $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ definition due to Cauchy and Weierstrass. An equivalent sequential formulation states that continuity is equivalent to preservation of convergent sequences. These two definitions coincide for metric spaces, and the equivalence is left as an exercise in the text.
