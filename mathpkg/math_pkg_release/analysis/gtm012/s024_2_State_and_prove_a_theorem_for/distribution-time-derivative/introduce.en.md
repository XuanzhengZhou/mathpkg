This definition extends the notion of differentiability to functions taking values in the space $\mathcal{P}'$ of periodic distributions. The derivative is defined via convergence of difference quotients in the distribution sense: $(t-s)^{-1}(F_t - F_s)$ must converge to $G$ in $\mathcal{P}'$ as $t \to s$. This allows the heat equation to be reformulated for distributions as $\frac{d}{dt}F_t|_{t=s} = D^2 F_s$, where $D^2$ denotes the second spatial derivative in the distribution sense.
