Theorem 4.2 bridges the distribution theory of Theorem 3.1 with the classical PDE framework. Uniqueness follows from the maximum principle (Theorem 4.1): applying it to both $u$ and $-u$ yields the bound $|u(x,t)| \leq \sup|g|$, and linearity then forces the difference of two solutions to vanish. Existence is proved by reduction to the distribution case: extend $g$ to an odd periodic function, apply Theorem 3.1 to obtain a smooth solution $u(x,t)$ on $\mathbb{R} \times (0,\infty)$, and verify that its restriction to $[0,\pi]$ satisfies the boundary conditions and converges to $g$ uniformly as $t \to 0$.
