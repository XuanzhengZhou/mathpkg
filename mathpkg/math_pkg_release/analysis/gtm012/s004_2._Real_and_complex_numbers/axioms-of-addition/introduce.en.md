The axioms of addition make $(\mathbb{R}, +)$ into an abelian group. A1 (associativity) allows unambiguous omission of parentheses in sums of three or more terms. A2 (commutativity) means the order of summands does not matter. A3 guarantees the existence of a neutral element $0$, and A4 guarantees that every real number has an additive inverse, enabling subtraction. These four axioms, together with the axioms of multiplication and the distributive law, begin the axiomatic characterization of the real number field.
