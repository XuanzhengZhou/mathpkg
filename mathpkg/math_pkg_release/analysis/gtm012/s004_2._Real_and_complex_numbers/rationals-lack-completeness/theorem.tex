The set $\mathbb{Q}$ of rational numbers does not satisfy the completeness axiom.
\par
More explicitly, there is no rational number $x > 0$ such that $x^2 = 2$. Let
\begin{equation*}
A = \{\, y \in \mathbb{Q} \mid y^2 < 2 \,\}.
\end{equation*}
Then $A$ is bounded above in $\mathbb{Q}$ but has no least upper bound in $\mathbb{Q}$.
