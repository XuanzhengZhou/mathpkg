# Exercise 3

Suppose both $f$ and $Df$ are functions satisfying conditions (1) and (2) of Theorem 5.1. Show that

$$L(Df)(z) = z \, Lf(z) - e^{-zM} f(M),$$

where $\operatorname{supp}(f) \subset [M, \infty)$. Conclude that differentiation of the original function corresponds, up to a boundary term, to multiplication of the Laplace transform by $z$.
