Theorem 5.3 is a Paley-Wiener style converse: it characterizes exactly which holomorphic functions arise as Laplace transforms of continuous functions with support bounded below and exponential growth. The key condition is quadratic decay in $|z|$ combined with an exponential factor $\exp(-M \operatorname{Re} z)$ that encodes the support threshold $M$. The proof constructs $f$ via a contour integral and verifies the Laplace transform property by interchanging integration order and applying Cauchy's integral formula. Uniqueness follows from the inversion theorem (Theorem 5.2), since $Lf = 0$ implies $f = 0$.
