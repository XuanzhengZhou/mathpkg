The Laplace transform converts a continuous function of exponential type with support bounded below into a holomorphic function defined on a right half-plane in $\mathbb{C}$. It is the fundamental integral transform for studying initial-value problems and linear differential equations, as it turns differentiation into multiplication by the complex variable $z$. The exponential decay factor $e^{-zt}$ ensures convergence when $\operatorname{Re} z$ exceeds the growth rate $a$ of the original function.
