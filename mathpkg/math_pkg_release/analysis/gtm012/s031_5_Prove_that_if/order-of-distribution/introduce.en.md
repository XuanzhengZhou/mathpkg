The order of a distribution $F \in \mathcal{L}'$ is the smallest integer $k$ such that $|F(u)|$ is bounded by a constant multiple of the seminorm $|u|_{k,a,M}$ for all test functions $u \in \mathcal{L}$. This notion stratifies distributions by how many derivatives they effectively involve: a distribution of order $k$ is continuous with respect to the $C^k$-type topology on $\mathcal{L}$. Every distribution in $\mathcal{L}'$ has finite order, which is a key structural property.
