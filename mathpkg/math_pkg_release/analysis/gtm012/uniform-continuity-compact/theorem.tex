Suppose $(S, d)$ and $(S', d')$ are metric spaces and $f: S \to S'$ is continuous. If $S$ is compact, then $f$ is \textbf{uniformly continuous}: for each $\varepsilon > 0$ there is $\delta > 0$ such that
$$d'(f(x), f(y)) < \varepsilon \quad \text{whenever } d(x, y) < \delta,$$
for all $x, y \in S$.
