Theorem 1.1 establishes that $\mathbb{C}$, the space of continuous $2\pi$-periodic functions equipped with the supremum norm, is a Banach space. Every Cauchy sequence in $\mathbb{C}$ converges uniformly to a continuous periodic limit function. This completeness property is fundamental for applying Banach-space techniques such as the contraction mapping principle and the Hahn-Banach theorem to problems involving periodic functions.
