Convergence in the sense of $\mathcal{P}$ requires uniform convergence of the sequence of functions together with all their derivatives. This is much stronger than uniform convergence in $\mathbb{C}$ and reflects the natural Frechet space topology on $\mathcal{P}$, which is generated by the countable separating family of seminorms $p_k(u) = \|D^k u\|$. A sequence converges in $\mathcal{P}$ precisely when it converges with respect to each seminorm.
