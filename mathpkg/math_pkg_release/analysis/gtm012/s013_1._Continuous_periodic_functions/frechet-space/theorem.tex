Let $X$ be a vector space over the real or complex numbers. Suppose there is a sequence of seminorms $|\cdot|_1, |\cdot|_2, \ldots$ on $X$ with the separating property:
$$|u|_k = 0 \quad \text{for all } k \quad \text{implies} \quad u = 0.$$
Define a metric on $X$ by
$$d'(u, v) = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} 2^{-k} \, |u - v|_k \, \bigl[1 + |u - v|_k\bigr]^{-1}.$$
If $X$ is complete with respect to the metric $d'$, it is called a \emph{Frechet space}.
