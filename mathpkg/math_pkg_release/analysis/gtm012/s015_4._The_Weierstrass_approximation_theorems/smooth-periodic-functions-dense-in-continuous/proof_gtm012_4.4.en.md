# Proof of Corollary 4.4: Density of $\mathcal{P}$ in $\mathbb{C}$

This corollary follows immediately from Theorem 4.3. Taking $u \in \mathbb{C}$ and any $v \in \mathcal{P}$ (for instance, $v = 0$), Theorem 4.3 provides a sequence $(u_n)_{1}^{\infty}$ of trigonometric polynomials such that

$$|u_n - u| \to 0$$

uniformly. Since every trigonometric polynomial belongs to $\mathcal{P}$, this shows that $\mathcal{P}$ is uniformly dense in $\mathbb{C}$.
