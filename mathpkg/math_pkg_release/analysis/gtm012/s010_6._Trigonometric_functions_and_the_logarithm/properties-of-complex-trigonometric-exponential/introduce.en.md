Theorem 6.4 establishes the fundamental identities linking the complex exponential and trigonometric functions. Part (a) is Euler's formula, expressing the exponential of an imaginary argument as a combination of sine and cosine. Part (b) gives the periodicity of these functions with period 2π (or 2πi for exp). Part (c) proves that exp is surjective onto the punctured complex plane C\{0} and that its kernel is precisely the discrete subgroup 2πiZ, providing the theoretical basis for defining the complex logarithm.
