The complex logarithm is defined as the inverse relation of the complex exponential function. Because exp is periodic with period 2πi, each nonzero complex number has infinitely many logarithms differing by multiples of 2πi. A single-valued branch of the logarithm can be obtained by restricting the imaginary part to any half-open interval of length 2π; the choice of interval corresponds to choosing a branch cut in the complex plane.
