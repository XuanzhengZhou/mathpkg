The constant π is defined analytically as twice the smallest positive number p for which C(p) = 0, where C is the unique solution of f'' + f = 0 with C(0) = 1, C'(0) = 0. This definition avoids any geometric or trigonometric circularity, grounding π purely in the theory of differential equations and the exponential function.
