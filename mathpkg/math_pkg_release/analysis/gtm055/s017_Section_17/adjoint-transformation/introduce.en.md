The adjoint $T^*: \mathcal{F}^* \to \mathcal{E}^*$ of a continuous linear transformation $T: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{F}$ is defined by precomposition: $(T^*f)(x) = f(Tx)$. This construction reverses the direction of arrows and extends duality from spaces to mappings between them. The adjoint inherits boundedness and invertibility properties from the original transformation, and for matrices the adjoint corresponds to the transpose.
