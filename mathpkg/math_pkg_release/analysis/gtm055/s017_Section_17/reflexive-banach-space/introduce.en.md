A Banach space $$\mathcal{E}$$ is reflexive when the natural embedding $$j: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{E}^{**}$$ is surjective, meaning every bounded linear functional on the dual space $$\mathcal{E}^*$$ arises from evaluation at some element of $$\mathcal{E}$$. In a reflexive Banach space, $$\mathcal{E}$$ and $$\mathcal{E}^{**}$$ can be identified via $$j$$, making $$\mathcal{E}$$ and $$\mathcal{E}^*$$ mutual duals. Reflexivity implies completeness and characterizes a particularly well-behaved class of Banach spaces.
