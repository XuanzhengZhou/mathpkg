Corollary 16.3 is the bipolar identity for annihilators: applying the annihilator–preannihilator operations three times yields the same result as applying them once. This is an immediate consequence of Proposition 16.2 and mirrors the bipolar theorem for polars in dual pairs, establishing the formal analogy between the annihilator calculus and the polar calculus.
