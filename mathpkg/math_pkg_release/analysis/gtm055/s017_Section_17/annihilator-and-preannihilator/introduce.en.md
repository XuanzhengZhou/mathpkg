The annihilator $$M^a$$ of a subset $$M \subseteq \mathcal{E}$$ is the set of all continuous linear functionals that vanish on $$M$$, while the preannihilator $${}^a M$$ of a subset $$M \subseteq \mathcal{E}^*$$ is the set of all vectors annihilated by every functional in $$M$$. These operations establish a Galois connection between subsets of $$\mathcal{E}$$ and subsets of $$\mathcal{E}^*$$, forming the foundation of duality theory. Closed linear manifolds and weak$$^*$$ closed linear manifolds correspond precisely under the annihilator–preannihilator operations.
