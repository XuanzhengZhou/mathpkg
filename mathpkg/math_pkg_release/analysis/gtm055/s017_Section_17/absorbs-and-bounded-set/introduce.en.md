A set $M$ absorbs $N$ if every sufficiently large scalar multiple of $M$ contains $N$. A subset of a locally convex space is bounded precisely when it is absorbed by every neighborhood of the origin. This concept generalizes the intuitive notion of boundedness from normed spaces and is essential for defining polar topologies: the polar $M^0$ is absorbing if and only if $M$ is weakly bounded.
