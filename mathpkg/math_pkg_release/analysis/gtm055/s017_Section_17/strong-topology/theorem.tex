If $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ is a dual pair, then the \textbf{strong topology} on $\mathcal{F}$ is the topology of uniform convergence on all the weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$. The collection of all weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$ is admissible, so the strong topology is the finest admissible polar topology on $\mathcal{F}$ with respect to the dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$.

Every admissible polar topology on $\mathcal{F}$ lies between the topology induced by $\mathcal{E}$ (the coarsest admissible polar topology) and the strong topology.

In the dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}^*, \mathcal{E} \rangle$ where $\mathcal{E}$ is a Banach space, the strong topology on $\mathcal{E}$ is simply the norm topology.
