If $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$ is a dual pair, then any locally convex linear topology on $\mathcal{E}$ with respect to which the dual space $\mathcal{E}^*$ coincides with $\mathcal{F}$ (as a linear manifold in the full algebraic dual of $\mathcal{E}$) is called a \textbf{topology of the dual pair} $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$.

Such topologies always exist; the topology induced by $\mathcal{F}$ on $\mathcal{E}$ is the coarsest, and by the Mackey–Arens theorem, every admissible polar topology on $\mathcal{F}$ is a topology of the dual pair.
