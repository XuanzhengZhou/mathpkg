D. If $\mathcal{C}$ is a collection of subsets of $X$, and if $M$ is a fixed subset of $X$, then the collection $\mathcal{C}_M = \{C \cap M : C \in \mathcal{C}\}$ is called the trace of $\mathcal{C}$ on $M$. Show that the $\sigma$-ring generated by the trace $\mathcal{C}_M$ coincides with the trace on $M$ of the $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}(\mathcal{C})$ generated by $\mathcal{C}$. (Hint: It is readily verified that the trace of $\mathbf{S}(\mathcal{C})$ on $M$ is a $\sigma$-ring of subsets of $M$ that contains $\mathcal{C}_M$ and therefore contains $\mathbf{S}(\mathcal{C}_M)$; to go the other way, let $\mathbf{T}$ denote the collection of subsets $A$ of $X$ such that $A \cap M \in \mathbf{S}(\mathcal{C}_M)$, and show that $\mathbf{T}$ is a $\sigma$-ring.)
