If $K$ is a compact subset of $\mathbb{C}$ we shall denote by $\mathcal{R}(K)$ the closure in $\mathcal{C}(K)$ of the algebra of all rational functions with poles off $K$.

It is clear that $\mathcal{R}(K)$ is a closed subalgebra of $\mathcal{C}(K)$ and that

$$\mathcal{P}(K) \subset \mathcal{R}(K) \subset \mathcal{A}(K)$$

for any compact set $K$ of complex numbers. Example I shows that $\mathcal{R}(K)$ may coincide with $\mathcal{A}(K)$ when $K$ has a hole. But also in Example I every function in $\mathcal{A}(K)$ is approximable by means of special rational functions with poles only at 0 (and $\infty$). As a matter of fact, a similar restriction on the poles of approximating rational functions can be effected whenever, as in Example I, the functions in $\mathcal{R}(K)$ can be uniformly approximated on $K$ by functions that are locally analytic on some neighborhood of $K$.
