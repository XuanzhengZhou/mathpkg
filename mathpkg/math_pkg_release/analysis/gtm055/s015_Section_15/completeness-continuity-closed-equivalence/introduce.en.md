Theorem 13.6 establishes a three-way equivalence among completeness of the domain, continuity of the transformation, and closedness of the transformation for linear maps between quasinormed spaces. Any two of these properties force the third. In particular, a linear transformation that is both closed and continuous forces the domain to be complete, providing a useful converse to the closed graph theorem.
