Proposition 14.7 provides the fundamental characterization of locally convex topological linear spaces. It establishes the equivalence between having a topology induced by a separating family of pseudonorms and possessing a neighborhood base at zero consisting of (absolutely) convex sets. This result bridges the practical definition (via pseudonorms) with the geometric definition (via convex neighborhoods), and is central to the theory of locally convex spaces.
