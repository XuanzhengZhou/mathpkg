For a topological linear space $\mathcal{E}$, the following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The topology on $\mathcal{E}$ is induced by a separating family of pseudonorms,
\item[(ii)] There exists a neighborhood base at $0$ in $\mathcal{E}$ consisting of convex sets,
\item[(iii)] There exists a neighborhood base at $0$ in $\mathcal{E}$ consisting of absolutely convex sets,
\item[(iv)] The topology on $\mathcal{E}$ is locally convex.
\end{enumerate}
