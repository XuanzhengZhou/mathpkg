Corollary 14.11 is one of the most important consequences of the Hahn-Banach theorem. It guarantees that the dual space $\mathcal{E}^*$ of a normed space is always rich enough to separate points: for any nonzero vector $x_0$, there exists a bounded linear functional of norm one that attains the norm of $x_0$. This shows that the canonical embedding of $\mathcal{E}$ into its bidual $\mathcal{E}^{**}$ is isometric, and that the dual space contains plenty of non-trivial functionals, resolving the fundamental existence question for bounded linear functionals.
