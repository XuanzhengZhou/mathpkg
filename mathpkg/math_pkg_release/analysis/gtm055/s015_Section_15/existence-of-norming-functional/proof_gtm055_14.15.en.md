Apply Lemma 14.10 with $\mathcal{M} = (0)$. Then $d(x_0, \mathcal{M}) = \|x_0\|$, so there exists $f \in \mathcal{E}^*$ with $f(0) = 0$, $f(x_0) = 1$, and $\|f\| = 1/\|x_0\|$. Setting $g = \|x_0\| f$ gives $\|g\| = 1$ and $g(x_0) = \|x_0\|$. For the second statement, if $x \neq y$, apply the first part to $x_0 = x - y$ to obtain $f$ with $f(x - y) = \|x - y\| \neq 0$, so $f(x) \neq f(y)$.
