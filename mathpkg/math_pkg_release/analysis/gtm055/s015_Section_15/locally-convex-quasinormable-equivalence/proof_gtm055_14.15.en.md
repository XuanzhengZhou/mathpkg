If the topology is induced by a countably determined separating family, a countable cofinal subfamily also induces the topology, so (i) implies (ii). Arranging a countable family $\{\sigma_n\}$ as a sequence, the formula $|x| = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2^n} \frac{\sigma_n(x)}{1 + \sigma_n(x)}$ defines a quasinorm inducing the same topology, so (ii) implies (iii). Obviously (iii) implies (iv). For (iv) implies (i): suppose $\mathcal{E}$ is locally convex and metrizable, with countable neighborhood base $\{U_n\}$ at $0$. By Proposition 14.7, each $U_n$ contains an absolutely convex neighborhood $V_n$, whose Minkowski functionals provide the required countable family of pseudonorms.
