Show that the assumption that the space $\mathcal{F}$ is complete cannot be omitted from the open mapping theorem, even when $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{F}$ are both normed spaces. (Hint: Construct a bounded linear operator $T$ on a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$ so that $T$ is one-to-one but is not bounded below. If $\mathcal{R} = \mathcal{R}(T)$, then $T : \mathcal{E} \rightarrow \mathcal{R}$ is a bounded linear transformation of $\mathcal{E}$ onto $\mathcal{R}$, but this mapping is not open.)
