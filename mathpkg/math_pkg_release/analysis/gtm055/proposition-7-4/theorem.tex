Every measure $\mu$ is countably subadditive and also semi-continuous in the sense that

$$\mu \left( \bigcup_{n} E_{n} \right) = \lim_{n} \mu(E_{n})$$

for every monotone increasing sequence $\{E_{n}\}$ of measurable sets. Conversely, if $\nu$ is a finitely additive measure defined on the $\sigma$-ring of measurable sets in a measurable space $(X, S)$, and if $\nu$ is either countably subadditive or semicontinuous in the above-named sense, then $\nu$ is countably additive and is therefore a measure.
