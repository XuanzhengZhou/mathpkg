The collection $\mathcal{A}$ of all measurable complex-valued functions on a measurable space $X$ is a unital algebra that contains all constant functions, is self-conjugate (Ex. 2H), and is closed with respect to the formation of limits of pointwise convergent sequences. The collection of real-valued functions in $\mathcal{A}$ forms a unital real algebra that is also a function lattice (Prob. 1D).

This theorem has a converse which is sometimes useful, and we record it here for completeness. The proof is sketched in Problems T and U.
