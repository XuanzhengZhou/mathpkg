Let $X = \{x_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ be a linearly independent sequence of vectors in a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$, and let $\mathcal{L}$ denote the collection of all complex sequences $a = \{\alpha_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ such that the series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \alpha_n x_n$ converges in $\mathcal{E}$. Then the function
$$\|a\| = \sup_{N} \left\| \sum_{n=0}^{N} \alpha_n x_n \right\|$$
defines a norm on $\mathcal{L}$ with respect to which $\mathcal{L}$ is complete.
