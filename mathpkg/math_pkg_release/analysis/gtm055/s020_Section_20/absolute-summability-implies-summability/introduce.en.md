Corollary 19.6 states that absolute summability implies summability in Banach spaces: if the family of norms $\{\|x_{\gamma}\|\}$ is summable in $\mathbb{R}$, then the original family $\{x_{\gamma}\}$ is summable in $\mathcal{E}$. This follows from the Cauchy criterion and the triangle inequality. The converse is false in infinite-dimensional spaces (Dvoretsky-Rogers theorem).
