Let $X$ be a compact Hausdorff space.

(i) If $\{E_{\gamma}\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ is an arbitrary disjoint collection of nonempty subsets of $X$, then the collection $A$ of all continuous complex-valued functions on $X$ that are constant on each $E_{\gamma}$ is a closed self-conjugate subalgebra of $\mathcal{C}(X)$. The subset of $A$ consisting of functions vanishing on a designated set $E_{\gamma_0}$ is also a closed self-conjugate subalgebra.

(ii) Conversely, every closed self-conjugate subalgebra $A$ of $\mathcal{C}(X)$ coincides with the collection of all continuous functions constant on each equivalence class of the relation $x_1 \sim x_2 \iff f(x_1) = f(x_2)$ for all $f \in A$, possibly with a distinguished zero class.
