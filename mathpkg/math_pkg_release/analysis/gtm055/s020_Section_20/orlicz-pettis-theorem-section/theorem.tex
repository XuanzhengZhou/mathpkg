Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space and let $\{x_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ be a family of vectors in $\mathcal{E}$. If the family is weakly summable (i.e., $\sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} |f(x_\gamma)| < +\infty$ for every $f \in \mathcal{E}^*$), then it is already summable in the norm topology (and hence unconditionally convergent). Equivalently: a series in a Banach space that is weakly subseries convergent is norm subseries convergent.
