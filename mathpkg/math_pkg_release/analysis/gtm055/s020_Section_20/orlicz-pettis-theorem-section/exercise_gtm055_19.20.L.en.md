(Orlicz-Pettis Theorem) Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space, let $X = \{x_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ be a sequence in $\mathcal{E}$ such that the series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x_n$ is weakly convergent to a sum $x$, that is, such that $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} f(x_n) = f(x)$ for every $f$ in $\mathcal{E}^*$, and let $\mathcal{M}$ be the subspace of $\mathcal{E}$ spanned by $X$.

(i) Let $\{f_p\}_{p=1}^{\infty}$ be a sequence in the unit ball $(\mathcal{E}^*)_1$ and define $T: \mathcal{E}^* \to (\ell_1)$ by $Tf = \{f(x_n)\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$. Show that $T$ is a bounded linear transformation.

(ii)-(iv) [Outline of the proof that $T((\mathcal{E}^*)_1)^-$ is compact in $(\ell_1)$ using weak* compactness and sequential compactness arguments.]

(v) (Pettis [52]) Prove that a weakly subseries convergent infinite series in a Banach space is actually subseries convergent and therefore summable (in norm) as an indexed family. (Hint: Use projections $P_k$ on $(\ell_1)$ and the fact that $\|P_k\| \to 0$ uniformly on compact sets.)
