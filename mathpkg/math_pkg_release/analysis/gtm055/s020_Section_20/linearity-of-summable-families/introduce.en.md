Proposition 19.3 extends the linearity of infinite series to summable families indexed by arbitrary sets $\Gamma$. Summable families generalize the notion of unconditional convergence: the sum is independent of any ordering. The proposition confirms that the sum operation is linear and commutes with bounded linear transformations for these generalized sums.
