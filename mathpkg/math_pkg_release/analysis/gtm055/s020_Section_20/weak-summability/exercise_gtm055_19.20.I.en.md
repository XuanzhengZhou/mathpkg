An indexed family $\{x_{\gamma}\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ of vectors in a Banach space $\mathcal{E}$ is said to be weakly summable if the family of scalars $\{f(x_{\gamma})\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ is summable in $\mathbb{C}$ for every $f$ in $\mathcal{E}^*$. Likewise, $\{x_{\gamma}\}$ is weakly summable to sum $x$ if (for some $x$ in $\mathcal{E}$)
$$\sum_{\gamma} f(x_{\gamma}) = f(x)$$
for every $f$ in $\mathcal{E}^*$.

(i) Show that $\{x_{\gamma}\}$ is weakly summable if and only if the net $\{s_D\}_{D \in \mathcal{D}}$ of finite sums $s_D = \sum_{\gamma \in D} x_{\gamma}$ is weakly Cauchy in $\mathcal{E}$.

(ii) Show that if $\mathcal{E}$ is a reflexive Banach space, then every weakly summable indexed family in $\mathcal{E}$ is weakly summable to a sum in $\mathcal{E}$.
