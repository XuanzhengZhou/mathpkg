The algebra $\mathcal{R}(K)$ is the uniform closure on a compact set $K \subset \mathbb{C}$ of all rational functions whose poles lie outside $K$. It forms a closed subalgebra of the continuous functions $\mathcal{C}(K)$ and sits between the polynomial algebra $\mathcal{P}(K)$ and the algebra $\mathcal{A}(K)$ of functions locally analytic on $K$. The study of when $\mathcal{R}(K)$ coincides with $\mathcal{A}(K)$ is a central theme in rational approximation theory, with deep results due to Vitushkin.
