If $K$ is a compact subset of $\mathbb{C}$, then $\mathcal{R}(K)$ denotes the closure in $\mathcal{C}(K)$ of the algebra of all rational functions with poles off $K$.

It follows that $\mathcal{R}(K)$ is a closed subalgebra of $\mathcal{C}(K)$ and that
$$\mathcal{P}(K) \subset \mathcal{R}(K) \subset \mathcal{A}(K)$$
for any compact set $K$ of complex numbers.
