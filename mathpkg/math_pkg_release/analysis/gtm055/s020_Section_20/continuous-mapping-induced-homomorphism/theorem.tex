Let $X$ and $Y$ be compact Hausdorff spaces, and let $F: X \to Y$ be a continuous mapping. Define $\Phi_F(f) = f \circ F$ for every $f$ in $\mathcal{C}(Y)$. Then $\Phi_F: \mathcal{C}(Y) \to \mathcal{C}(X)$ is a contraction. If $F$ is onto, then $\Phi_F$ is an isometry. If $F$ is one-to-one, then $\Phi_F$ is onto. Consequently, if $F$ is a homeomorphism between $X$ and $Y$, then $\Phi_F$ is a unital Banach algebra isomorphism between $\mathcal{C}(Y)$ and $\mathcal{C}(X)$.
