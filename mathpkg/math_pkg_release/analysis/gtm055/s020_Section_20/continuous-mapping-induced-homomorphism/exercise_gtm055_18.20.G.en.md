Let $X$ and $Y$ be compact Hausdorff spaces, and let $F: X \to Y$ be a continuous mapping. If we write $\Phi_F(f) = f \circ F$ for every $f$ in $\mathcal{C}(Y)$, then $\Phi_F$ maps $\mathcal{C}(Y)$ into $\mathcal{C}(X)$. Show that $\Phi_F$ is a contraction. Show also that if $F$ is onto, then $\Phi_F$ is an isometry, and that if $F$ is one-to-one, then $\Phi_F$ is onto. Consequently, if $F$ is a homeomorphism between $X$ and $Y$, then $\Phi_F$ is a unital Banach algebra isomorphism between $\mathcal{C}(Y)$ and $\mathcal{C}(X)$. (Hint: Use the Tietze extension theorem; see Example 11G.)
