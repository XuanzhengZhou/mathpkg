An absolutely summable family in a Banach space is one whose norms form a summable family of scalars. Such families are naturally identified with elements of $\ell_1(\Gamma; \mathcal{E})$. Absolute summability implies unconditional summability in any Banach space, providing the foundation for the study of bases, biorthogonal systems, and the Orlicz--Pettis theorem.
