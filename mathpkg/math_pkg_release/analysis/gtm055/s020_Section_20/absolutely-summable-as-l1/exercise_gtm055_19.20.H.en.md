Let us equip an index family $\Gamma$ with the counting measure $\kappa$. (See Example 7J; it does not matter which $\sigma$-ring of subsets of $\Gamma$ we declare to be measurable so long as it contains all the singletons $\{\gamma\}$.) Show that for any separable Banach space $\mathcal{E}$ the collection of all absolutely summable vector families $\{x_{\gamma}\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ in $\mathcal{E}$ coincides with the Banach space $\mathcal{L}_1(\Gamma; \mathcal{E})$ of Theorem 17.11, and that for any such family we have
$$\sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} x_{\gamma} = \int_{\Gamma} x_{\gamma} \, d\kappa(\gamma)$$
(cf. Theorem 17.14 and Problem 7Q).
