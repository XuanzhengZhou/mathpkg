In a finite-dimensional Banach space, a family of vectors is summable if and only if it is absolutely summable. This generalizes the classical Riemann rearrangement theorem: in $\mathbb{R}^n$ (or $\mathbb{C}^n$), unconditional convergence of a series is equivalent to absolute convergence. In infinite-dimensional spaces, summability does not imply absolute summability (by the Dvoretzky--Rogers theorem).
