Proposition 19.1 states that convergent infinite series of vectors in a normed space are linear: the sum of two convergent series converges to the sum of their limits, and scalar multiplication distributes over series. Furthermore, bounded linear transformations commute with infinite summation. These elementary facts hold for series indexed by $\mathbb{N}_0$, $\mathbb{N}$, and $\mathbb{Z}$ (in both primary and Cauchy senses of convergence).
