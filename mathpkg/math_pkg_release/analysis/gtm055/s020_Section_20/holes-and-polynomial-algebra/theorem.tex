Let $K$ be a compact subset of the complex plane, and let $V$ denote the set of all those complex numbers $\alpha$ such that the function $\lambda \mapsto 1/(\lambda - \alpha)$ belongs to the algebra $\mathcal{P}(K)$.

(i) Each hole in $K$ is either disjoint from $V$ or wholly contained in $V$. The unbounded component $U_{\infty}$ of $\mathbb{C} \setminus K$ is always contained in $V$, and consequently $\mathcal{P}(\hat{K}) = \mathcal{R}(\hat{K})$ where $\hat{K} = \mathbb{C} \setminus U_{\infty}$.

(ii) The fact that $U_{\infty} \subset V$ provides an alternative proof of Runge's theorem on a simply connected domain.
