Problem 18.V studies the set $V$ of complex numbers $\alpha$ for which the Cauchy kernel $1/(\lambda - \alpha)$ belongs to the polynomial algebra $\mathcal{P}(K)$. The key result is that each hole (bounded component of the complement) of $K$ is either entirely contained in $V$ or disjoint from it, and the unbounded component is always in $V$. This gives the identity $\mathcal{P}(\hat{K}) = \mathcal{R}(\hat{K})$ and an alternative proof of Runge's theorem.
