Let $K$ be a nonempty compact subset of $\mathbb{C}$, let $P$ be a subset of the Riemann sphere $\hat{\mathbb{C}}$ (Prob. 3W) with the property that $P$ contains at least one point of each connected component of $\hat{\mathbb{C}}\backslash K$, and let $f$ be a locally analytic function on some open neighborhood $U$ of $K$. Then there exists a sequence $\{r_n\}$ of rational functions converging uniformly to $f$ on $K$ such that each $r_n$ has poles only in $P$.
