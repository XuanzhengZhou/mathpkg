Considered as a function of the interval of integration, the Riemann–Stieltjes integral of a Banach space valued mapping is additive. That is, if $a < b < c$ and $\Phi$ is integrable over $[a, b]$ and $[b, c]$ with respect to $\alpha$, then $\Phi$ is integrable over $[a, c]$ and

$$\int_a^c \Phi(t) d\alpha(t) = \int_a^b \Phi(t) d\alpha(t) + \int_b^c \Phi(t) d\alpha(t).$$
