The Mackey–Arens theorem characterizes all locally convex topologies on a space $\mathcal{E}$ that are compatible with a given dual pair $\langle \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{F} \rangle$. It states that a locally convex topology $\mathcal{T}$ on $\mathcal{E}$ is a topology of the dual pair if and only if it is the topology of uniform convergence on an admissible collection of absolutely convex subsets of $\mathcal{F}$ that are compact in the weak topology induced by $\mathcal{E}$. This result provides a fundamental bridge between the theory of dual pairs and the theory of polar topologies.
