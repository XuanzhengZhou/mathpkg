The Riesz--Dunford functional calculus extends the holomorphic functional calculus to elements of a Banach algebra. For an element $a$ and a function $f$ analytic on a neighborhood of the spectrum $\sigma(a)$, one defines $f(a)$ via a contour integral of $f(\zeta)(\zeta 1 - a)^{-1}$ around an oriented envelope of the spectrum. This construction is fundamental to spectral theory and provides the rigorous foundation for applying analytic functions to operators.
