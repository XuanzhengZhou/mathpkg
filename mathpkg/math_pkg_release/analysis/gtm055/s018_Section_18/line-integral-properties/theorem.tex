Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space and let $\gamma$ be a rectifiable curve. For continuous $\mathcal{E}$-valued functions $\Phi, \Psi$ on the trace of $\gamma$ and scalars $\alpha, \beta$, the following properties hold:
(1) Linearity: $\int_{\gamma} (\alpha \Phi + \beta \Psi) = \alpha \int_{\gamma} \Phi + \beta \int_{\gamma} \Psi$.
(2) Norm estimate: $\left\| \int_{\gamma} \Phi(\zeta) d\zeta \right\| \leq \int_{\gamma} \|\Phi(\zeta)\| |d\zeta| \leq \sup_{\zeta \in \gamma} \|\Phi(\zeta)\| \cdot V(\gamma)$,
where $V(\gamma)$ is the length (total variation) of $\gamma$.
(3) The integral is independent of the parametrization of $\gamma$ up to orientation.
