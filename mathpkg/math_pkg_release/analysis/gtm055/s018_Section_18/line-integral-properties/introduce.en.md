Banach-valued line integrals satisfy the same basic properties as scalar line integrals: linearity in the integrand, a norm estimate bounding the integral by the supremum norm times the curve length, and invariance under reparametrization (up to orientation). These properties make the line integral a powerful tool for constructing the Riesz--Dunford functional calculus.
