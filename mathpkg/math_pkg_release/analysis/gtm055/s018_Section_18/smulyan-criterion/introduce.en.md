The Šmul'yan criterion provides a fundamental characterization of reflexive Banach spaces in terms of weak compactness. It states that a normed space $\mathcal{E}$ is reflexive if and only if its closed unit ball $\mathcal{E}_1$ is compact in the weak topology. The proof uses the natural embedding $j: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{E}^{**}$, the Alaoglu theorem, and Lemma 16.21 to establish both directions of the equivalence.
