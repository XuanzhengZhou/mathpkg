Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space and let $\Phi$ be an $\mathcal{E}$-valued function that is locally analytic on an open subset $U$ of the complex plane. Then for any triangle $\Delta$ whose closed convex hull is contained in $U$, the contour integral of $\Phi$ around the boundary of $\Delta$ vanishes:
$$\int_{\partial \Delta} \Phi(\zeta) d\zeta = 0.$$
Consequently, $\Phi$ possesses a local primitive (antiderivative) and is infinitely differentiable in the norm topology of $\mathcal{E}$.
