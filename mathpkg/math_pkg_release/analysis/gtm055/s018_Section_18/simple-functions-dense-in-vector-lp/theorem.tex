For any measure space $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ and any normed space $\mathcal{E}$, the collection $\mathcal{S}$ of integrable simple $\mathcal{E}$-valued mappings is dense in $\mathcal{L}_p(X; \mathcal{E})$ for $1 \leq p < +\infty$. Moreover, if $\mathcal{C}_0$ is a countable collection of measurable sets of finite measure whose equivalence classes form a dense subset of the measure ring of $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$, and $V$ is a countable dense subset of $\mathcal{E}$, then the simple mappings of the form $\sum_{i=1}^N \chi_{E_i} v_i$, where $E_i \in \mathcal{C}_0$ and $v_i \in V$, constitute a countable dense set in $\mathcal{L}_p(X; \mathcal{E})$.
