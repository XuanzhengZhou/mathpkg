Let $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ be a measure space, let $\{f_n\}$ be a sequence of complex-valued functions measurable $[\mu]$ on $X$, and suppose that $\{f_n\}$ converges a.e. to a function $f$. Suppose also that there exists a function $g$ on $X$ such that $g$ is integrable $[\mu]$ and such that $|f_n| \leq g$ a.e. $[\mu]$ for every $n$. Then $f$ is integrable $[\mu]$,
$$\lim_n \int_X |f_n - f| d\mu = 0,$$
and therefore
$$\int_X f d\mu = \lim_n \int_X f_n d\mu.$$
