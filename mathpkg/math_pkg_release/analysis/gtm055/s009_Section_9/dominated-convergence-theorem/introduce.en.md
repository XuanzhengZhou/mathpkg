The Dominated Convergence Theorem (Theorem 7.12) is the central convergence result in Lebesgue integration theory. If a sequence of measurable functions converges almost everywhere and is bounded in absolute value by an integrable dominating function, then the limit function is integrable, the sequence converges in $L^1$ mean, and the limit of the integrals equals the integral of the limit. This removes the nonnegativity restriction of earlier convergence theorems.
