Let $\{(X_\gamma, \mathbf{S}_\gamma)\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ be an indexed family of measurable spaces with the property that the sets $X_\gamma$ are pairwise disjoint. The \textbf{direct sum} $\sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} \oplus (X_\gamma, \mathbf{S}_\gamma)$ is the measurable space $(X, \mathbf{S})$, where $X = \bigcup_\gamma X_\gamma$ and $\mathbf{S}$ denotes the $\sigma$-ring consisting of all those subsets $E$ of $X$ with $E \cap X_\gamma \in \mathbf{S}_\gamma$ for every $\gamma$. If a measure $\mu_\gamma$ is given on each $(X_\gamma, \mathbf{S}_\gamma)$, then
$$\mu(E) = \sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} \mu_\gamma(E \cap X_\gamma), \quad E \in \mathbf{S},$$
defines a measure, and $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ is the \textbf{direct sum} of the family $\{(X_\gamma, \mathbf{S}_\gamma, \mu_\gamma)\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$.
