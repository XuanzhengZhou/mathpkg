Fatou's Lemma (Theorem 7.11) provides the essential inequality for passing limits under the integral sign in Lebesgue integration theory. For a sequence of nonnegative measurable functions with uniformly bounded integrals, the integral of the limit inferior is bounded above by the limit inferior of the integrals. It is a key ingredient in the proof of the Dominated Convergence Theorem.
