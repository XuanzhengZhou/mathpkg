Proposition 8.2 establishes the change-of-variables formula for integration with respect to an induced measure. When a measure $\nu$ is induced on $Y$ by a measurable mapping $\Phi: X \to Y$, the integral of $f$ on $Y$ with respect to $\nu$ equals the integral of the composition $f \circ \Phi$ on $X$ with respect to the original measure $\mu$. This generalizes the classical change-of-variables formula to the abstract measure-theoretic setting.
