If $\mu$ is a signed measure on a measurable space $(X, \mathbf{S})$, and if $X = A \cup B$ is a Hahn decomposition of $X$ with respect to $\mu$ (where $A$ is a positive set and $B$ a negative set), then the set functions defined on $\mathbf{S}$ by
$$\mu_+(E) = \mu(E \cap A) \quad \text{and} \quad \mu_-(E) = -\mu(E \cap B), \quad E \in \mathbf{S},$$
are called the \textbf{positive} and \textbf{negative variations} of $\mu$. These definitions are unambiguous: if $\{A, B\}$ and $\{A', B'\}$ are two Hahn decompositions, then $\mu(E \cap A) = \mu(E \cap A')$ and $\mu(E \cap B) = \mu(E \cap B')$ for every measurable $E$.
