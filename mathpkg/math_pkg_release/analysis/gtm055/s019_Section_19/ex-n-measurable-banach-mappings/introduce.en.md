A Banach-valued mapping is measurable if it can be approximated pointwise by simple functions. Weak measurability (measurability of all scalar functionals applied to the mapping) is generally weaker, but for separable Banach spaces the two notions coincide. These definitions are fundamental for the theory of Bochner integration and vector-valued $L_p$ spaces.
