Lemma 18.9 establishes a crucial interpolation property for strongly separating subalgebras: given any two distinct points and any two prescribed real values, there exists a function in the subalgebra interpolating those values. This is used in the final step of the Stone-Weierstrass proof where one constructs, for each point $x$, a function that equals $g(x)$ at $x$ and strictly exceeds $g$ elsewhere.
