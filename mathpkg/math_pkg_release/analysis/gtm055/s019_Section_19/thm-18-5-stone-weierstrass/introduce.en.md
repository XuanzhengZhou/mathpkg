The Stone-Weierstrass Theorem (Theorem 18.5) is a far-reaching generalization of the classical Weierstrass approximation theorem. It states that any closed, self-conjugate, strongly separating subalgebra of $C(X)$ (where $X$ is compact Hausdorff) must equal $C(X)$. Equivalently, any subalgebra satisfying these conditions is dense in $C(X)$. The proof proceeds through a sequence of lemmas establishing that closed subalgebras are sublattices (Kakutani's theorem) and possess interpolation properties.
