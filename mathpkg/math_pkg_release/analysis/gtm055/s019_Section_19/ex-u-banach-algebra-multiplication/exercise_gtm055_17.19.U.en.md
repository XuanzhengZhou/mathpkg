Let $A$ be a Banach algebra, and for each element $a$ of $A$ let us write $L_{(a)}x = ax$ and $R_{(a)}x = xa, x \in A$. (The operators $L_{(a)}$ and $R_{(a)}$ are called left and right multiplication by $a$, respectively.)

(i) Show that $L_{(a)}$ and $R_{(a)}$ both belong to $\mathcal{L}(A)$ for every $a$ in $A$. Demonstrate also that if $A$ is unital, then the collection of all right multiplications by elements of $A$ constitutes the commutant in $\mathcal{L}(A)$ of the collection of all left multiplications by elements of $A$. Show, that is, that a bounded operator $T$ on $A$ is of the form $R_{(b)}$ for some (uniquely determined) element $b$ of $A$ if and only if $T$ commutes with every operator of the form $L_{(a)}$. Show that the analogous assertions with "left" and "right" interchanged are also valid. (Hint: Consider right multiplication by the element $T1_A$.)

(ii) Show that if $A$ is a unital Banach algebra, then setting $\rho_l(a) = L_{(a)}$, $a \in A$, defines an isometric unital Banach algebra isomorphism of $A$ into $\mathcal{L}(A)$.
