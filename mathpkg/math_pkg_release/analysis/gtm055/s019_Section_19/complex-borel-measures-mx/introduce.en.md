The space $\mathcal{M}(X)$ of all complex Borel measures on a locally compact Hausdorff space $X$ is the fundamental object needed for the Riesz Representation Theorem for $C(X)$. Measures are added and scaled setwise, making $\mathcal{M}(X)$ a linear space. The total variation norm $\|\xi\| = |\xi|(X)$ turns $\mathcal{M}(X)$ into a Banach space, and the regular measures $\mathcal{M}_0(X)$ form a closed linear subspace that is precisely the dual of $C_0(X)$.
