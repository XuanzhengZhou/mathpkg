Since $\mathcal{A}$ is self-conjugate, $\bar{f} \in \mathcal{A}$ whenever $f \in \mathcal{A}$. As $\mathcal{A}$ is a linear space (being an algebra), it follows that $\operatorname{Re} f = \frac{1}{2}(f + \bar{f}) \in \mathcal{A}$ and $\operatorname{Im} f = \frac{1}{2i}(f - \bar{f}) \in \mathcal{A}$ for every $f \in \mathcal{A}$.
