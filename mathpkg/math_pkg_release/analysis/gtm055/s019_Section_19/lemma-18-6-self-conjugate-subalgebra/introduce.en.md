Lemma 18.6 is a preparatory step for the Stone-Weierstrass Theorem. It states that a self-conjugate subalgebra $\mathcal{A}$ of $C(X)$ (meaning $\bar{f} \in \mathcal{A}$ whenever $f \in \mathcal{A}$) is also closed under taking real and imaginary parts. This is because $\operatorname{Re} f = (f + \bar{f})/2$ and $\operatorname{Im} f = (f - \bar{f})/(2i)$, and $\mathcal{A}$ is a linear space closed under complex conjugation. This lemma shows why self-conjugacy naturally reduces the complex case to the real case.
