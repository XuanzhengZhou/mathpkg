Exercise B generalizes the classical Hölder inequality from two functions to three (and then $k$) functions. The proof uses an inductive argument: by setting $s = 1 - 1/r$, the numbers $ps$ and $qs$ become Hölder conjugates, and the two-function Hölder inequality can be applied twice. This technique extends naturally to any finite number of functions.
