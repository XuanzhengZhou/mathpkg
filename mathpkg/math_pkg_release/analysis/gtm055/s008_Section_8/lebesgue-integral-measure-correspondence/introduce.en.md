Theorem 7.6 establishes the fundamental equivalence between the axiomatic notion of a Lebesgue integral and the more familiar notion of a measure. Given a Lebesgue integral $L$, defining $\mu(E) = L(\chi_E)$ when $\chi_E$ is integrable (and $+\infty$ otherwise) yields a measure $\mu$. Conversely, every measure $\mu$ determines a unique Lebesgue integral whose associated measure is $\mu$. This one-to-one correspondence justifies the use of the integral notation $\int f d\mu$ and unifies the axiomatic and constructive approaches to integration.
