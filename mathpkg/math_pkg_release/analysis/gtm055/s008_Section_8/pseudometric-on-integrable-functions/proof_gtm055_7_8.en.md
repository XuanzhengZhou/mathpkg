If $f \ge 0$ and $f \in \mathcal{L}$, then $f = |f|$. Set $g = 0$ in $(L_1)$ to get $L(f) \ge 0$. For real-valued $f \in \mathcal{L}$, decompose $f = f^+ - f^-$; by $(L_1)$, $f^\pm \in \mathcal{L}$ and $L(f) = L(f^+) - L(f^-)$ is real. For the pseudometric: $\sigma(f,g) = L(|f-g|) \ge 0$, $\sigma(f,f) = 0$, symmetry is clear, and the triangle inequality follows from $|f-h| \le |f-g| + |g-h|$ and positivity of $L$.
