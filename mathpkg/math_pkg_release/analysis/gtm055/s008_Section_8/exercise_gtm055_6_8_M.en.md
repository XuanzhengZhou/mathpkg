Let $F$ be a nonempty closed set in a metric space $X$, and for each positive integer $n$ let $G_n$ denote the set $G_n = \{x \in X : d(x, F) < 1/n\}$. Show that each $G_n$ is open and that $F = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} G_n$. Thus $F$ is a $G_\delta$. Dually, every open set in $X$ is the union of an increasing sequence of closed subsets. In particular, every open set is an $F_\sigma$. Show that the characteristic functions of open sets and closed sets are pointwise limits of sequences of continuous functions. (Hint: See Problem 4D.)
