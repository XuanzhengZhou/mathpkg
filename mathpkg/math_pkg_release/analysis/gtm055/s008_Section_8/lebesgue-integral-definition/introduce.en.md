The Lebesgue integral is defined axiomatically as a linear functional $L$ on a linear space $\mathcal{L}$ of measurable functions satisfying two conditions. $(L_1)$ ensures solidness: if $|g| \le |f|$ and $f$ is integrable, so is $g$, with $|L(g)| \le L(|f|)$. $(L_2)$ requires completeness in the mean: Cauchy sequences with respect to $L(|\cdot|)$ that converge pointwise have integrable limits, and $L$ is continuous. Functions in $\mathcal{L}$ are said to be integrable with respect to $L$.
