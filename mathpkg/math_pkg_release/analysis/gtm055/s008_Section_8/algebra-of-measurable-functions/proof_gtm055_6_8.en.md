Addition, scalar multiplication, and multiplication are continuous operations on $\mathbb{C}$, hence Borel measurable. The composition of a measurable function with a Borel measurable function is measurable, so sums, products, and scalar multiples of measurable functions are measurable. Constant functions are trivially measurable. Complex conjugation is continuous, so $\overline{f}$ is measurable. Closure under pointwise limits follows from Proposition 6.2. For the real subcollection: $\max(f,g) = \frac{1}{2}(f+g+|f-g|)$ and $\min(f,g) = \frac{1}{2}(f+g-|f-g|)$ are measurable since $|\cdot|$ is continuous.
