Let $(X, \mathbf{S}), (Y, \mathbf{T}),$ and $(Z, \mathbf{U})$ be measurable spaces, and let $\Phi : X \to Y$ and $\Psi : Y \to Z$ be measurable mappings. Then the composition $\Psi \circ \Phi$ is measurable. In particular, if $Y$ and $Z$ are topological spaces, and if $\Psi$ is a Borel measurable mapping, then $\Psi \circ \Phi$ is Borel measurable.
