Let $X$ be a set and let $\mathcal{A}$ be a real algebra of real-valued functions on $X$ that contains the constant functions and is closed with respect to the formation of limits of pointwise convergent sequences. Then there is a unique $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ of subsets of $X$ such that $X$ is a measurable space and the collection of all real-valued measurable functions on $X$ with respect to $\mathbf{S}$ coincides with $\mathcal{A}$.
