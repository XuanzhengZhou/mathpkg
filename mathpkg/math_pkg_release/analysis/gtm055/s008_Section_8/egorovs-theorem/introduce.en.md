Egorov's Theorem (Theorem 7.5) states that on a finite measure space, pointwise almost-everywhere convergence of a sequence of measurable functions to a measurable limit implies almost-uniform convergence. That is, for any $\varepsilon > 0$, one can remove a set of measure less than $\varepsilon$ on which the convergence becomes uniform. This result bridges the gap between pointwise and uniform convergence in the context of measure theory and is fundamental in integration theory.
