Let $f$ be a real-valued function on a closed interval $[a, b]$. For each partition $\mathcal{P} = \{s_i\}_{i=0}^n$ of $[a, b]$, define
$$
v_+(\mathcal{P}) = \sum_{i=1}^n [f(s_i) - f(s_{i-1})]^+, \qquad
v_-(\mathcal{P}) = \sum_{i=1}^n [f(s_i) - f(s_{i-1})]^-.
$$
The numbers $v_+(\mathcal{P})$ and $v_-(\mathcal{P})$ are called the \textit{positive} and \textit{negative variations} of $f$ over $\mathcal{P}$, respectively. The nets $\{v_+(\mathcal{P})\}$ and $\{v_-(\mathcal{P})\}$ are both monotone increasing with respect to refinement of partitions. The function $f$ is of bounded variation on $[a, b]$ if and only if both nets are bounded. In that case, defining
$$
V_+(f; a, b) = \sup_{\mathcal{P}} v_+(\mathcal{P}), \qquad
V_-(f; a, b) = \sup_{\mathcal{P}} v_-(\mathcal{P}),
$$
we have
$$
V(f; a, b) = V_+(f; a, b) + V_-(f; a, b)
$$
and
$$
f(b) - f(a) = V_+(f; a, b) - V_-(f; a, b).
$$
