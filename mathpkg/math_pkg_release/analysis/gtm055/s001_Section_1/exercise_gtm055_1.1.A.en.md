Let $X$ and $Y$ be sets, and suppose given a mapping $f$ of some subset $B$ of $X$ into $Y$. If $A$ is a subset of $B$, then the restriction of $f$ to $A$ is the mapping $f|A$ of $A$ into $Y$ defined by $(f|A)(x) = f(x)$ for every $x$ in $A$. If $f$ and $g$ are mappings of subsets $A$ and $B$ of $X$, respectively, into $Y$, and if $f$ is the restriction of $g$ to $A$, then $g$ is said to be an extension of $f$. Show that the relation of extension is a partial ordering on the collection of all functions defined on subsets of $X$ and taking values in $Y$.
