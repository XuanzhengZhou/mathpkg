This result characterizes the addition of infinite cardinal numbers. The key identity $\aleph_0 c = c$ (for infinite $c$) is proved using Zorn's lemma by constructing a maximal disjoint collection of countably infinite subsets. Once established, it implies that the sum of two cardinal numbers, one at least infinite, is simply their maximum. This extends to sums of finitely many cardinals, dramatically simplifying cardinal arithmetic in the presence of infinite cardinals.
