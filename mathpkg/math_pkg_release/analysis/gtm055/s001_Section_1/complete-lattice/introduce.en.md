A complete lattice is a partially ordered set in which every subset (not just finite or pairwise subsets) admits both a supremum and an infimum. Complete lattices are essential in fixed-point theory, as exemplified by the Banach--Knaster--Tarski lemma: every monotone increasing self-map of a complete lattice possesses a fixed point.
