A linear transformation (linear map) is a structure-preserving map between vector spaces that respects addition and scalar multiplication. The kernel and range are linear submanifolds measuring injectivity and surjectivity defects. Linear transformations form a vector space themselves and are the morphisms in the category of vector spaces. They are the fundamental objects of study in operator theory.
