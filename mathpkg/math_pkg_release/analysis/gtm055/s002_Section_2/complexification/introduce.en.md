The complexification of a real vector space is a canonical construction that produces a complex vector space from a real one. It is the algebraic analog of extending scalars from $\mathbb{R}$ to $\mathbb{C}$. Every real linear transformation extends naturally to a complex linear transformation on the complexification. This construction is essential when real and complex Banach spaces interact.
