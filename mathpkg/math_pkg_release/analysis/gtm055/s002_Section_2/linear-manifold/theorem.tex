Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a linear space. A nonempty subset $M$ of $\mathcal{E}$ is a \textbf{linear manifold} in $\mathcal{E}$ (or a \textbf{linear submanifold} of $\mathcal{E}$) if, for every pair of vectors $x, y \in M$ and every pair of scalars $\alpha, \beta$, the linear combination $\alpha x + \beta y$ belongs to $M$.
