Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a linear space. An element $x$ of $\mathcal{E}$ is a \textbf{convex combination} of vectors $y_1, \ldots, y_n \in \mathcal{E}$ if there exist nonnegative real numbers $s_1, \ldots, s_n$ such that $s_1 + \cdots + s_n = 1$ and $x = s_1 y_1 + \cdots + s_n y_n$. A subset $C$ of $\mathcal{E}$ is \textbf{convex} if, for every pair of vectors $x, y \in C$ and every real number $t$ with $0 \leq t \leq 1$, the point $(1-t)x + ty$ belongs to $C$.
