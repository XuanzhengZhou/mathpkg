A convex set in a vector space is a subset closed under taking convex combinations: for any two points in the set, the entire line segment joining them lies within the set. Convexity is fundamental in functional analysis, where it underpins the Hahn-Banach theorem, the Krein-Milman theorem, and the theory of locally convex spaces.
