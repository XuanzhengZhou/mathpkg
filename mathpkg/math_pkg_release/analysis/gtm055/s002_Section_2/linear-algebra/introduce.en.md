An (associative) algebra is a vector space equipped with a bilinear associative multiplication, making it simultaneously a ring and a vector space with compatible scalar multiplication. Important examples include matrix algebras, function algebras (with pointwise multiplication), and operator algebras. Unital algebras possess a multiplicative identity. The concept generalizes to Banach algebras when the space carries a complete norm.
