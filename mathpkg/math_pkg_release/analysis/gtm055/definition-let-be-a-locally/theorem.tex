Let $X$ be a locally compact Hausdorff space, and let $B$ denote the $\sigma$-ring of Borel subsets of $X$. If $\xi$ and $\zeta$ are complex Borel measures on $X$ the sum $\xi + \zeta$ is defined setwise:

$$(\xi + \zeta)(E) = \xi(E) + \zeta(E), \quad E \in B.$$

Similarly, if $\alpha$ is a complex number, then $\alpha\xi$ is defined setwise:

$$(\alpha\xi)(E) = \alpha\xi(E), \quad E \in B.$$

The collection of all complex Borel measures on $X$, which is clearly a linear space with respect to these setwise linear operations, will be denoted by $\mathcal{M}(X)$.

There is a natural and useful norm on the space $\mathcal{M}(X)$. (In this connection we recall that the total variation of a complex measure $\xi$ is denoted by $|\xi|$. For a discussion of the total variation of a complex measure see Chapter 8.)
