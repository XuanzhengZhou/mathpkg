Proof. We simply define $\Phi$ to be the result of factoring $\rho$ through $\mathcal{G}$ as in (ii), and denote by $\Psi$ the result of factoring $\sigma$ through $\mathcal{E} \dotplus \mathcal{F}$. It is clear that $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ are mutually inverse mappings on the ranges of $\sigma$ and $\rho$, respectively, and since the range of $\sigma$ generates $\mathcal{G}$ and the range of $\rho$ generates $\mathcal{E} \dotplus \mathcal{F}$, it follows that $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ are, in fact, mutually inverse linear isomorphisms between $\mathcal{G}$ and $\mathcal{E} \dotplus \mathcal{F}$.
