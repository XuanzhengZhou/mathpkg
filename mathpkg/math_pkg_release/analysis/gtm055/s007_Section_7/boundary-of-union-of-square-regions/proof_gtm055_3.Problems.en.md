Let $\gamma_i$ denote a standard parametrization of $\partial Q_i$, $i = 1, \ldots, n$, and set $\gamma = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \gamma_i$. One verifies that the winding number of $\gamma$ is one in the interior of each $Q_i$ and zero outside $L$. Write $\gamma$ as a formal sum of all edges of the squares $\partial Q_i$, hence as a formal sum of directed line segments. Remove all matched pairs of oppositely directed line segments (which cancel in the formal sum). All removed segments meet $L^\circ$ (each interior edge is traversed once in each direction). What remains is a parametrization of $\partial L$ given as a formal sum of directed line segments. The result then follows from the properties established in Problem I.
