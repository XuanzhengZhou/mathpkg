A nonempty collection $\mathcal{R}$ of subsets of a set $X$ is a \textit{ring} (of sets) if $\mathcal{R}$ is closed with respect to the formation of unions and differences; i.e., if for every pair of sets $A$ and $B$ in $\mathcal{R}$, $A \cup B$ and $A \setminus B$ belong to $\mathcal{R}$.

Every ring of sets contains the empty set and is closed under the formation of (finite) intersections.
