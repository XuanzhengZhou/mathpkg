A ring of sets is a nonempty collection of subsets closed under finite unions and set differences. This algebraic structure is fundamental to measure theory: rings provide the natural domain on which finitely additive measures are defined. Every ring automatically contains the empty set (as $A \setminus A$) and is closed under finite intersections (via $A \cap B = A \setminus (A \setminus B)$). The term "ring" reflects the analogy with algebraic rings, where symmetric difference plays the role of addition and intersection the role of multiplication.
