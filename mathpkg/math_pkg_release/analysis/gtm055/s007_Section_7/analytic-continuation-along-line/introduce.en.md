When a circle chain has all its centers lying on a common line, any analytic continuation along this chain extends to a single analytic function defined on the union of all the discs. The key observation is that along a line, the geometric arrangement of discs can be simplified: any disc contained in another is redundant, and intersecting discs that are not adjacent in the chain force adjacency. This result is fundamental for the theory of analytic continuation along polygonal arcs and ultimately for the monodromy theorem.
