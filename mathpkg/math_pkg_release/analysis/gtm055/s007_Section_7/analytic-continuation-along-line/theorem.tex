Let $\ell$ be a line in $\mathbb{C}$, and let $\mathcal{K} = \{D_0, \ldots, D_n\}$ be a circle chain such that the center $\alpha_i$ of each disc $D_i$ lies on $\ell$. If $\{(D_0, f_0), \ldots, (D_n, f_n)\}$ is an analytic continuation along $\mathcal{K}$, then there exists a (unique) analytic function $f$ on the union $\Delta = D_0 \cup \cdots \cup D_n$ that extends each $f_i$, $i = 0, \ldots, n$.

(If the centers $\alpha_i$ all lie on the line segment $\sigma(\alpha_0, \alpha_n)$, such an analytic continuation is known as an \textit{analytic continuation along the directed line segment} $\sigma(\alpha_0, \alpha_n)$.)
