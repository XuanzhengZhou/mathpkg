Apply Dixon's theorem (Problem N) to $f$ on $U$ with the given $\gamma$. The resulting function $h$ is locally analytic on $U \cup V$, where $V = \{\lambda : w_\gamma(\lambda) = 0\}$. By hypothesis $\mathbb{C} \setminus U \subset V$, so $U \cup V = \mathbb{C}$ and $h$ is entire. As $|\lambda| \to \infty$, $h(\lambda) \to 0$, so by Liouville $h \equiv 0$. The formula for $w_\gamma(\lambda) f(\lambda)$ follows from the definition of $h$ on $U$. The vanishing of $\int_\gamma f(\zeta) d\zeta$ when $\gamma \sim 0$ follows by considering $\lambda f(\lambda)$ and taking limits.
