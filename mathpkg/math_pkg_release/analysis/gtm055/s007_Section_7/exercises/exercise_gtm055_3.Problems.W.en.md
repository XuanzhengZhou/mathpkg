Let $\gamma$ be a smooth simple closed arc and let $\Delta$ denote either $\text{Int}(\gamma)$ or $\text{Ext}(\gamma)$.

(i) Show that for each point $\lambda_0$ on $\gamma$ there exists a polygonal Jordan loop $P$ such that $\lambda_0$ is the only point of $\gamma$ on $P$ (see Proposition 3.8). Hence the range of $\gamma$ lies (except for the one point $\lambda_0$) either in $\text{Int}(P)$ or $\text{Ext}(P)$.

(ii) Let $\alpha_i$ be one of the smooth arcs of which $\gamma$ is the chained sum, and for each $t$, $t_{i-1} < t < t_i$, let $\lambda_0 = \alpha_i(t)$, let $\lambda_1$ denote the point of the inward tending normal to $\alpha_i$ at $\lambda_0$ such that $|\lambda_1 - \lambda_0| = 1$, and let $\lambda_2$ denote the point on the tangent line to $\alpha_i$ at $\lambda_0$ such that $\lambda_2 - \lambda_0$ is a positive multiple of $\alpha_i'(t)$ and $|\lambda_2 - \lambda_0| = 1$. Show that if $\lambda_j - \lambda_0 = x_j + iy_j$, $j = 1, 2$, then the determinant
$$\delta(t) = \begin{vmatrix} x_2 & y_2 \\ x_1 & y_1 \end{vmatrix}$$
is either $+1$ for every $t$ or $-1$ for every $t$. When $\delta(t) \equiv 1$, we say that $\alpha_i$ is oriented positively with respect to $\Delta$.

(iii) Show that if the smooth arc $\alpha_i$ is positively oriented with respect to $\Delta$, then $\alpha_{i+1}$ is too.

(iv) Verify that if $\gamma$ is positively oriented with respect to $\Delta$, then $w_{\gamma} \equiv 1$ on $\Delta$.
