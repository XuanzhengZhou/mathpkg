It clearly suffices to show that $f = 0$ identically on $\Delta$ when $f = 0$ on $M$. Let $U$ denote the subset of $\Delta$ consisting of all those points $\lambda$ in $\Delta$ such that $f$ vanishes identically on some open disc of positive radius about $\lambda$. Then $U$ is an open subset of $\Delta$ which, according to the power series expansion (Example A), contains all of the accumulation points of $M$ that lie in $\Delta$, as well as all of the accumulation points of $U$ itself that belong to $\Delta$. Hence $U$ is a nonempty open subset of $\Delta$ such that $\partial U \cap \Delta = \varnothing$, whence it follows at once that $U = \Delta$ by Proposition 3.6 (characterizing connectedness via sets with empty boundary relative to the space).
