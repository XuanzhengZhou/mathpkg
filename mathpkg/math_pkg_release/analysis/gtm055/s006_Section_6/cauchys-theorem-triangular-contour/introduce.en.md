This is the cornerstone of complex analysis. Cauchy's theorem for a triangular contour states that the line integral of a continuous function, analytic except possibly at one point, around any triangle in the domain is zero. It holds even when the function has an isolated singularity at one point, provided it is continuous there. This theorem is the basis for the Cauchy integral formula.
