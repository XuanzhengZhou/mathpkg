Let $\varphi$ be an arbitrary strictly increasing continuous mapping of a real interval $[a, b]$ onto another real interval $[c, d]$. Show that if $\alpha$ is a rectifiable arc in $\mathbb{C}$ defined on the parameter interval $[c, d]$, then $\alpha \circ \varphi$ is also a rectifiable arc having the same length as $\alpha$.
