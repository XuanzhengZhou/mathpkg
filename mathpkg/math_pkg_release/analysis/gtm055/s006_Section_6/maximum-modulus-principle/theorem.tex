Let $f$ be an analytic function on a domain $\Delta$. If $|f|$ attains a local maximum at some point $\alpha \in \Delta$, then $f$ is constant on $\Delta$. In particular, if $\Delta$ is bounded and $f$ extends continuously to $\Delta^-$, then $\max_{\lambda \in \Delta^-} |f(\lambda)|$ is attained on the boundary $\partial \Delta$.
