The Maximum Modulus Principle asserts that a non-constant analytic function cannot attain its maximum absolute value at an interior point. The maximum of $|f|$ on a compact set is always achieved on the boundary. The proof uses the mean value property derived from the Cauchy integral formula: if $|f(\alpha)| = M_r$, the integrand in the mean value formula must be constant, forcing $f$ to be constant.
