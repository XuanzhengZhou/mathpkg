Taylor's Theorem states that every analytic function on a domain can be represented locally as a convergent power series about any point in its domain. The radius of convergence extends to the boundary of the domain. The coefficients are given by the familiar formula involving derivatives, and the series converges absolutely and uniformly on compact subsets of the disc of convergence.
