Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a Banach space, let $X = \{x_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ and $A = \{\alpha_n\}_{n=0}^{\infty}$ be sequences in $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{E}^*$, respectively, such that $(X, A)$ is a biorthogonal system for $\mathcal{E}$, and let $M$ denote the subspace of $\mathcal{E}$ spanned by $X$. Then $X$ is a basis for $M$ if and only if the sequence $\{E_N\}_{N=0}^{\infty}$ of expansion operators is uniformly bounded on $M$.
