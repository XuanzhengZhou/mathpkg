Proposition 12.7 states that completeness (the Banach space property) is preserved under equivalence of normed spaces. If two normed spaces are equivalent via an invertible bounded linear transformation, and one of them is complete, then the other must be complete as well. This follows because the equivalence and its inverse map Cauchy sequences to Cauchy sequences and preserve convergence.
