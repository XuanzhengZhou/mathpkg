Consider the collection $\mathcal{C}^{(k)} = \mathcal{C}^{(k)}([a, b])$ of all those complex-valued functions $f$ on the closed interval $[a, b]$, $a < b$, that possess $k$ continuous derivatives $f', f'', \ldots, f^{(k)}$. (Recall that we use right derivatives at $a$ and left derivatives at $b$. By convention $\mathcal{C}^{(0)}$ coincides with $\mathcal{C}([a, b])$.) Show that for every positive integer $k$ the space $\mathcal{C}^{(k)}$ is a dense linear submanifold of $\mathcal{C}^{(0)}$. Show also that in the norm

$$\|f\|_{(k)} = \|f\|_{\infty} + \|f'\|_{\infty} + \cdots + \|f^{(k)}\|_{\infty}$$

$\mathcal{C}^{(k)}$ becomes a Banach space in its own right.
