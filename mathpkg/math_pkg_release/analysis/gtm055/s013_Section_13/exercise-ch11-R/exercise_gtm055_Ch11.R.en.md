(i) Show that if $\mathcal{E}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{E}_n$ are normed spaces, then the norm topology on the direct sum $\mathcal{E}_1 \oplus_1 \cdots \oplus_1 \mathcal{E}_n$ coincides with the product topology. Show, more generally, that if $\mathcal{E}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{E}_n$ are arbitrary topological linear spaces, then the product topology is a linear topology on the direct sum $\mathcal{E}_1 + \cdots + \mathcal{E}_n$.

(ii) Show likewise that if $\mathcal{F}$ is a topological vector space and $T$ is a linear transformation of a linear space $\mathcal{E}$ into $\mathcal{F}$, then the coarsest topology on $\mathcal{E}$ that makes $T$ continuous (the topology inversely induced on $\mathcal{E}$ by $T$) is a linear topology. Develop these ideas to give a proof of Proposition 11.24.
