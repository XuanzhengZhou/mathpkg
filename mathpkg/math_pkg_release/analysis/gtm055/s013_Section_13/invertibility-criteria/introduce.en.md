Proposition 12.5 provides a practical criterion for the invertibility of a bounded linear transformation between normed spaces: an operator is invertible precisely when it is surjective and bounded below. Left invertibility implies boundedness below, while right invertibility implies surjectivity. The full invertibility requires both properties simultaneously, and the inverse is then automatically bounded with $\|T^{-1}\| \leq 1/M$ where $M$ is the lower bound.
