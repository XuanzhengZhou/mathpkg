(i) Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a topological linear space, and let $\mathcal{E}^n$ denote the direct sum of $n$ copies of $\mathcal{E}$ (equipped with the product topology). Show that

$$((\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n), (x_1, \ldots, x_n)) \rightarrow \lambda_1 x_1 + \cdots + \lambda_n x_n$$

is a continuous mapping of $\mathbb{C}^n \times \mathcal{E}^n$ into $\mathcal{E}$. In particular, the mapping $(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \rightarrow x_1 + \cdots + x_n$ is a continuous mapping of $\mathcal{E}^n$ into $\mathcal{E}$. Conclude that if $V$ is an arbitrary neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathcal{E}$, then there exists a neighborhood $W$ of $0$ in $\mathcal{E}$ such that $\overbrace{W + \cdots + W}^{n} \subset V$.

(ii) Show that if $M$ is a dense set in a topological linear space $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{V}$ is a base of open neighborhoods of $0$ in $\mathcal{E}$, then the collection of sets $\{x + V : x \in M, V \in \mathcal{V}\}$ is a base for the topology on $\mathcal{E}$, and conclude that if $\mathcal{E}$ is separable, then $\mathcal{E}$ satisfies the second axiom of countability if and only if it satisfies the first.
