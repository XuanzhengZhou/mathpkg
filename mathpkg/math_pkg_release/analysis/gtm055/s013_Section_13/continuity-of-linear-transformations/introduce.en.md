Proposition 12.1 establishes that for linear transformations between normed spaces, continuity is equivalent to boundedness. This crucial equivalence means that checking continuity reduces to verifying the existence of a uniform bound $M$ such that $\|Tx\| \leq M\|x\|$ for all vectors $x$. The proposition also shows that a continuous linear transformation is automatically Lipschitzian and therefore uniformly continuous.
