Two normed spaces $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{F}$ are called equivalent if there exists an invertible bounded linear transformation (an equivalence) between them. This is a genuine equivalence relation: the inverse of an equivalence and the product of two equivalences are equivalences. Two finite-dimensional Banach spaces are equivalent if and only if they have the same dimension, and two norms on the same linear space are equivalent norms precisely when the identity map is an equivalence.
