(ii) Let $\delta$ be a deminorm on a linear space $\mathcal{E}$ and let $\mathcal{Z}$ denote the zero space of $\mathcal{E}$ in the topology induced by $\delta$. Verify that $\mathcal{Z} = \{x \in \mathcal{E} : \delta(x) = 0\}$, and that if for each vector $x$ in $\mathcal{E}$ we denote by $\dot{x}$ the coset $x + \mathcal{Z}$, then

$$|\dot{x}| = \delta(x), \quad x \in \mathcal{E},$$

defines unambiguously a quasinorm $|\dot{x}|$ on $\mathcal{E} / \mathcal{Z}$. Show also that this quasinorm induces the topology of the associated space $\mathcal{E}$.

(iii) If $\{\delta_\gamma\}_{\gamma \in \Gamma}$ is a family of deminorms on a linear space $\mathcal{E}$, then the supremum of the collection of linear topologies induced by them is called the topology induced by the given family. Similarly, $\{\delta_\gamma\}$ is said to be separating on $\mathcal{E}$ if for each vector $x \neq 0$ in $\mathcal{E}$ there is a deminorm $\delta_\gamma$ in the family such that $\delta_\gamma(x) \neq 0$. Verify that Propositions 11.26-11.32 are all valid for families of deminorms in place of families of pseudonorms.
