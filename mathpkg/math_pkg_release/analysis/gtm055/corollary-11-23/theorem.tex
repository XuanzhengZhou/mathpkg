If $\mathcal{E}$ is an arbitrary topological linear space, then $\mathcal{L} = (0)^-$ is a closed linear submanifold of $\mathcal{E}$ with the property that the quotient space $\mathcal{E}/\mathcal{L}$ is separated. (The linear manifold $\mathcal{L}$ is called the zero space of $\mathcal{E}$; the space $\mathcal{E}/\mathcal{L}$ is called the separated space associated with $\mathcal{E}$, and is customarily denoted by $\dot{\mathcal{E}}$. Cf. Proposition 11.17.)
