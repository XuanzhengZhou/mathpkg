The positive and negative parts of a real number $t$ decompose it into two nonnegative components: $t^+ = \max(t, 0)$ captures the positive contribution, and $t^- = \max(-t, 0)$ captures the negative contribution. These satisfy $t = t^+ - t^-$ and $|t| = t^+ + t^-$. This decomposition is fundamental in measure theory, integration, and functional analysis, where it extends to functions and measures via the Jordan decomposition.
