If $\sigma$ and $\tau$ are pseudonorms on a linear space $\mathcal{E}$, and if $\sigma \leq \tau$, then the topology induced on $\mathcal{E}$ by $\tau$ refines the one induced by $\sigma$.

If $\sigma$ and $\tau$ are two pseudonorms on a linear space $\mathcal{E}$, then a trivial calculation discloses that $\sigma + \tau$ and $\sigma \vee \tau$ are also pseudonorms. This observation clarifies the first assertion of the next result.
