Let $(X, \mathbf{S})$ be a measurable space, and suppose that $\mu$ and $\nu$ are measures on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ with the property that $\nu \ll \mu$ and $\mu \ll \nu$. Then we say that $\mu$ and $\nu$ are equivalent measures, and we write

$$\mu \equiv \nu.$$

It is clear that two measures on the same measurable space $(X, \mathbf{S})$ are equivalent if and only if they possess exactly the same null sets, and that the relation of being equivalent is an equivalence relation on the set of all measures on $(X, \mathbf{S})$.
