Absolute continuity formalizes the idea that one measure is "dominated" by another: $
u \ll \mu$ means that every $\mu$-null set is also $
u$-null. This concept is the essential hypothesis of the Radon--Nikodym theorem, which asserts that under $\sigma$-finiteness conditions, absolute continuity is equivalent to the existence of a density function. Indefinite integrals provide the canonical examples of absolutely continuous measures, as the integral over a null set is always zero.
