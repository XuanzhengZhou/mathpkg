By hypothesis there exists a dense sequence $\{[E_n]\}$ in the associated metric space $(\dot{\mathbf{S}}_{\mathcal{F}}, ho)$. Set $X_0 = igcup_n E_n$. Any measurable subset $E$ of $X \setminus X_0$ must have measure either $0$ or $+\infty$; since $\mu$ is atom-free, this forces $\mu(X \setminus X_0) = 0$, so $\mu$ is $\sigma$-finite. It thus suffices to treat the case $0 < \mu(X) < +\infty$.

For each positive integer $n$, let $\mathcal{P}_n$ be the partition of $X$ determined by $E_1, \ldots, E_n$, consisting of all nonempty intersections $A_1 \cap \cdots \cap A_n$ where each $A_i$ is either $E_i$ or $X \setminus E_i$. The atoms of these partitions form a directed system. One constructs a sequence of measure-preserving maps from the Boolean algebras generated by these partitions into the Boolean algebra of subintervals of $[0, \mu(X)]$, using the density of $\{[E_n]\}$ to ensure that the measures match.

By continuity, this sequence of partial isomorphisms extends to an isometry $\Phi$ of $\dot{\mathbf{S}}$ onto $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$ (the measure ring of $[0, \mu(X)]$ with Lebesgue--Borel measure). Since $\Phi$ is isometric, $\dot{
u}(\Phi([E])) = \dot{\mu}([E])$ for all $E \in \mathbf{S}$. The operations $ee$ and $\setminus$ are preserved because they are continuous on both metric spaces and are preserved on the dense subalgebra. Order preservation then yields preservation of countable suprema. Thus $\Phi$ is a measure ring isomorphism, establishing that $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ is isomorphic to Lebesgue--Borel measure on $[0, \mu(X))$.
