The Tonelli Theorem is a cornerstone of integration theory on product spaces. It asserts that for nonnegative measurable functions on the product of two $\sigma$-finite measure spaces, all iterated integrals and the double integral coincide. This result provides a practical criterion for integrability: a complex-valued measurable function on the product space is integrable with respect to the product measure whenever one of the iterated integrals of its absolute value is finite. Together with Fubini's Theorem, the Tonelli Theorem forms the essential machinery for interchanging the order of integration.
