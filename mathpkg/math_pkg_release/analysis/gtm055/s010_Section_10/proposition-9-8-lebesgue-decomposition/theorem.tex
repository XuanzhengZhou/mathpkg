If $(X, \mathbf{S})$ is a measurable space, and if $\mu$ and $\nu$ are $\sigma$-finite measures on $(X, \mathbf{S})$, then there exists a unique decomposition of $\nu$ into the sum of two measures $\nu_1$ and $\nu_2$ on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ such that

$$\nu_1 \ll \mu \quad \text{and} \quad \nu_2 \perp \mu.$$

The decomposition $\nu = \nu_1 + \nu_2$ is called the Lebesgue decomposition of $\nu$ with respect to $\mu$.
