Let $(X, \mathbf{S})$ be a measurable space, and suppose $\mu$ and $\nu$ are measures on $(X, \mathbf{S})$ with the property that there exists a partition of $X$ into disjoint measurable sets $A$ and $B$ such that $\mu(A) = 0$ and $\nu(B) = 0$. Then $\mu$ and $\nu$ are (mutually) singular and we write

$$\mu \perp \nu$$

or, equivalently, $\nu \perp \mu$. We also say that $\nu$ is singular with respect to $\mu$, and that $\mu$ is singular with respect to $\nu$.
