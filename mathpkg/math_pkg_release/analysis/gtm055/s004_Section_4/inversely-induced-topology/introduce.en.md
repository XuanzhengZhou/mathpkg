Given a family of maps from a set $X$ into topological spaces $Y_\gamma$, one can equip $X$ with the coarsest topology that makes all these maps continuous. This is known as the topology inversely induced by the family, or the initial topology. A subbase consists of preimages of open sets, and a base consists of finite intersections of such preimages. This construction generalizes product topologies and weak topologies.
