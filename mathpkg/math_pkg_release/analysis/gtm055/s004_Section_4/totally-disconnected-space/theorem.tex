A topological space is \textbf{totally disconnected} if its connected components are all singletons, i.e., if its one-point subsets are the only nonempty connected subsets that it possesses.

The \textbf{discrete topology} on a set $X$ is the topology consisting of all the subsets of $X$; a set $X$ together with the discrete topology on $X$ is a \textbf{discrete} (topological) space. Such spaces are obviously totally disconnected.
