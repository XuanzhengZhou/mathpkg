The proof is omitted in the text. It relies on covering $K$ with sufficiently small open discs contained in $U$, extracting a finite subcover by compactness, and then approximating the union of these discs by a system of Jordan domains with disjoint closures. The construction uses the fact that the plane can be subdivided by a sufficiently fine grid of horizontal and vertical lines, creating Jordan polygons that approximate the disc cover.
