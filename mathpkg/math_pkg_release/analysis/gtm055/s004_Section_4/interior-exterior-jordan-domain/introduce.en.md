By the Jordan Curve Theorem, a Jordan curve separates $\mathbb{C}$ into two components. The bounded component is the interior domain $\text{Int}(J)$, and the unbounded component is the exterior domain $\text{Ext}(J)$. These notions are fundamental in complex analysis for defining winding numbers, Cauchy integrals, and for applying theorems about simply connected domains.
