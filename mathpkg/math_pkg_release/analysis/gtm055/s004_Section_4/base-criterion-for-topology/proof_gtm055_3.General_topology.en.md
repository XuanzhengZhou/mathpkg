The verification is routine and is omitted. The forward direction follows from the definition of a base: every point belongs to some basic open set, and the intersection of two basic open sets is open, hence expressible as a union of basic open sets. Conversely, if $\mathcal{B}$ covers $X$ and the intersection of any two members of $\mathcal{B}$ is a union of members of $\mathcal{B}$, then the collection of all unions of members of $\mathcal{B}$ satisfies the axioms for a topology, with $\mathcal{B}$ as a base. For a subbase $\mathcal{S}$, the finite intersections of its members generate a base satisfying the above conditions.
