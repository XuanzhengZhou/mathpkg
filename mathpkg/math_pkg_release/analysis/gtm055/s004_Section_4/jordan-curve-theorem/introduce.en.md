The Jordan Curve Theorem is the central fact concerning the topology of the plane. It states that a simple closed curve (Jordan curve) separates the complex plane into exactly two connected components, each a Jordan domain whose entire boundary is the curve itself. One component is bounded (the interior) and the other is unbounded (the exterior). Despite its intuitive obviousness, the theorem requires a nontrivial proof.
