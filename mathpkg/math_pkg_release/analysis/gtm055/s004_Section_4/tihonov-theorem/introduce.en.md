Tihonov's Theorem (also written Tychonoff's Theorem) states that an arbitrary product of compact Hausdorff spaces is compact and Hausdorff. It is one of the most important results in general topology, equivalent to the Axiom of Choice. The theorem guarantees that infinite products of compact spaces remain compact, a fact essential for functional analysis (e.g., the Banach-Alaoglu theorem) and for the construction of Stone-Cech compactifications.
