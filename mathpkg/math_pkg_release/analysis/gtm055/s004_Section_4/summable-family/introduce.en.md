An indexed family of complex numbers is summable if the net of its finite partial sums converges. This definition uses the directed set of finite subsets (ordered by inclusion) to capture unconditional summability -- the sum does not depend on any ordering of the index set. For families of nonnegative real numbers, summability is equivalent to the net of finite sums being bounded above.
