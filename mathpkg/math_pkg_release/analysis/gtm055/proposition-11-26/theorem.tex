Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a topological vector space with linear topology $\mathcal{T}$ and let $\sigma$ be a pseudonorm on $\mathcal{E}$. The following conditions are equivalent:

(i) The topology $\mathcal{T}$ refines the topology induced by $\sigma$,

(ii) For an arbitrary positive number $\varepsilon$ there exists in $\mathcal{E}$ a neighborhood $V$ of 0 with respect to $\mathcal{T}$ such that $V \subset \{x \in \mathcal{E} : \sigma(x) < \varepsilon\}$,

(iii) The pseudonorm $\sigma$ is continuous on $\mathcal{E}$ with respect to $\mathcal{T}$.
