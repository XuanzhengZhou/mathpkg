The linear isometry $j$ of Proposition 15.6 is known as the natural embedding of the normed space $\mathcal{E}$ in the second dual $\mathcal{E}^{}$ of $\mathcal{E}$. (This mapping will play a central role in Chapter 16.)

Using the natural embedding of a normed space $\mathcal{E}$ in $\mathcal{E}^{}$ we readily characterize the weakly bounded subsets of $\mathcal{E}$.
