Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a topological linear space and let $\mathcal{M}$ be a linear submanifold of $\mathcal{E}$. Then the quotient topology on $\mathcal{E} / \mathcal{M}$ (Prob. 3H) is a linear topology with the property that the natural projection $\pi$ of $\mathcal{E}$ onto $\mathcal{E} / \mathcal{M}$ is both open and continuous. The quotient space $\mathcal{E} / \mathcal{M}$ (consisting of the quotient linear space $\mathcal{E} / \mathcal{M}$ equipped with the quotient topology) is separated if and only if $\mathcal{M}$ is closed in $\mathcal{E}$.
