A closed linear subspace $M$ of a locally convex topological linear space $\mathcal{E}$ can be characterized by the continuous linear functionals that vanish on it: $M$ equals the intersection of the kernels of all $f \in \mathcal{E}^*$ with $f|_M = 0$. This follows from the Hahn-Banach separation theorem. When $\mathcal{E}$ is separated (Hausdorff), $\mathcal{E}^*$ separates points of $\mathcal{E}$.
