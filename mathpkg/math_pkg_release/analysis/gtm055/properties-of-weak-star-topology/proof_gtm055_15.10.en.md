This is a direct application of Proposition 15.8 to the case where $\mathcal{E}$ is replaced by $\mathcal{E}^*$ and the linear manifold of functionals is $j(\mathcal{E}) \subset \mathcal{E}^{**}$. The separation property follows because for any nonzero $f \in \mathcal{E}^*$, there exists $x \in \mathcal{E}$ with $f(x) \neq 0$, so the evaluation functional separates points. The characterization of net convergence follows directly from the definition of the induced topology.
