A complete lattice is a partially ordered set in which every subset (not just finite subsets or pairs) has both a supremum (least upper bound) and an infimum (greatest lower bound). This is a strictly stronger condition than being a lattice. Every complete lattice is automatically a lattice, but not conversely. Complete lattices are fundamental in fixed-point theory and domain theory.
