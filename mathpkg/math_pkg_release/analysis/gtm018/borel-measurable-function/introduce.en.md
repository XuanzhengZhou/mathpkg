When the underlying space is the real line, a Borel measurable function is one measurable with respect to the $\sigma$-ring of Borel sets, while a Lebesgue measurable function is measurable with respect to the $\sigma$-ring of Lebesgue measurable sets. This distinction highlights that measurability depends on the chosen $\sigma$-ring, not on a specific measure.
