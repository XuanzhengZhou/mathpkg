Each of the two operations, the formation of unions and the formation of intersections, is distributive with respect to the other, i.e.
$$E \cap (F \cup G) = (E \cap F) \cup (E \cap G)$$
and
$$E \cup (F \cap G) = (E \cup F) \cap (E \cup G).$$
More generally the following extended distributive laws are valid:
$$F \cap \bigcup \{E: E \in \mathbf{E}\} = \bigcup \{E \cap F: E \in \mathbf{E}\}$$
and
$$F \cup \bigcap \{E: E \in \mathbf{E}\} = \bigcap \{E \cup F: E \in \mathbf{E}\}.$$
