If $x$ is a point of $X$ and $E$ is a subset of $X$, the notation $x \in E$ means that $x$ belongs to $E$; the negation $x \not\in E$ means that $x$ does not belong to $E$.

If $E$ and $F$ are subsets of $X$, the notation
$$E \subset F \quad \text{or} \quad F \supset E$$
means that $E$ is a subset of $F$, i.e. that every point of $E$ belongs to $F$. In particular $E \subset E$ for every set $E$.
