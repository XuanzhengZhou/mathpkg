The Lebesgue integral defines $\int f d\mu$ first for non-negative measurable functions as the supremum of integrals of dominated simple functions, then for general measurable functions by splitting into positive and negative parts. A function is integrable if the integral of its absolute value is finite. This construction generalizes the Riemann integral and is the foundation of modern analysis.
