Let $X = Y$ be any uncountable set, and let $S = T$ be the class of all countable subsets. If $D = \{(x,y) : x = y\}$ is the ``diagonal'' in $X \times Y$, show that every section of $D$ is measurable but $D$ is not. Conclude that the converse of Theorem A is not true.
