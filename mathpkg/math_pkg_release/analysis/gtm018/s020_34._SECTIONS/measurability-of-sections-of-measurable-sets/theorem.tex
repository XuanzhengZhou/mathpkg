Let $(X, S)$ and $(Y, T)$ be measurable spaces and let $(X \times Y, S \times T)$ be their Cartesian product. If $E \in S \times T$, then for every $x \in X$, the section $E_x = \{ y : (x,y) \in E \}$ belongs to $T$, and for every $y \in Y$, the section $E^y = \{ x : (x,y) \in E \}$ belongs to $S$.
