Theorem A of Section 34 states that every section of a measurable set in a product space is measurable in the corresponding factor space. If $E$ belongs to the product $\sigma$-ring $S \times T$, then each $X$-section $E_x$ is in $T$ and each $Y$-section $E^y$ is in $S$. The proof shows that the class of sets whose sections are measurable forms a $\sigma$-ring containing all measurable rectangles.
