Theorem C completes the Carathéodory construction: restricting the outer measure $\mu^*$ to the $\sigma$-ring $\bar{\mathbf{S}}$ of $\mu^*$-measurable sets yields a complete measure $\bar{\mu}$. Moreover, all sets of outer measure zero are automatically measurable, ensuring completeness. This is how Lebesgue measure is obtained from the outer measure induced by interval length.
