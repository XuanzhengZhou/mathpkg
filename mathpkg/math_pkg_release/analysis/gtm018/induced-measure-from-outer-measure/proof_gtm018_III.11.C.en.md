If $E \in \mathbf{H}$ and $\mu^*(E) = 0$, then, for every $A$ in $\mathbf{H}$, we have

$$\mu^*(A) = \mu^*(E) + \mu^*(A) \geq \mu^*(A \cap E) + \mu^*(A \cap E'),$$

so that indeed $E \in \bar{\mathbf{S}}$. The fact that $\bar{\mu}$ is countably additive on $\bar{\mathbf{S}}$ follows from Theorem B upon replacing $A$ by $E$ in the countable additivity formula. If

$$E \in \bar{\mathbf{S}}, \quad F \subset E, \quad \bar{\mu}(E) = \mu^*(E) = 0,$$

then $\mu^*(F) = 0$, so that $F \in \bar{\mathbf{S}}$, which proves that $\bar{\mu}$ is complete.
