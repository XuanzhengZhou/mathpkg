Let $f = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha_i \chi_{E_i}$ be a representation of $f$ as a simple function with $\mu(E_i) < \infty$ for each $i$ with $\alpha_i \neq 0$. If $f \geq 0$ a.e., then for every $i$, either $\alpha_i \geq 0$ or $\mu(E_i) = 0$. (If $\alpha_i < 0$ on a set of positive measure, then $f$ would be negative on that set.) Consequently each term $\alpha_i \mu(E_i) \geq 0$, and therefore

$$\int f d\mu = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha_i \mu(E_i) \geq 0.$$
