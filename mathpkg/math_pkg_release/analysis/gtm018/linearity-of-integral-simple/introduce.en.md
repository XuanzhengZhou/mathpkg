The integral of simple functions is linear: the integral of a linear combination equals the linear combination of the integrals. This follows directly from the definition $\int \sum \alpha_i \chi_{E_i} = \sum \alpha_i \mu(E_i)$ and the additivity of measure. Linearity is the foundational algebraic property that extends to all integrable functions via limiting processes.
