Theorem E shows that the representing regular Borel measure $\mu$ in the Riesz Representation Theorem is uniquely determined by the positive linear functional $\Lambda$. The proof establishes $\mu(C) = \lambda(C)$ for every compact set $C$, where $\lambda(C) = \inf\{\Lambda(f) : C \subset f \in \mathcal{L}_+\}$. Since $\mu$ is regular, its values on compact sets determine it completely, yielding uniqueness. The proof uses regularity to sandwich $\mu(C)$ between $\lambda(C)$ and $\lambda(C) - \epsilon$ via compact-open approximation.
