If $\mu$ is a regular Borel measure, if, for every $f$ in $\mathcal{L}$, $\Lambda(f) = \int f \, d\mu$, and if, for every $C$ in $\mathbf{C}$,

$$\lambda(C) = \inf \left\{ \Lambda(f) : C \subset f \in \mathcal{L}_+ \right\},$$

then $\mu(C) = \lambda(C)$ for every $C$ in $\mathbf{C}$. Hence, in particular, the representation of a positive linear functional as an integral with respect to a regular Borel measure is unique.
