A linear functional $\Lambda$ on $\mathcal{L}$ is called *bounded* if there exists a constant $k$ such that $|\Lambda(f)| \leq k \sup \{ |f(x)| : x \in X \}$ for every $f$ in $\mathcal{L}$. Show that every bounded (but not necessarily positive) linear functional is the difference of two bounded positive linear functionals. (Hint: imitate the derivation of the Jordan decomposition of a signed measure.)
