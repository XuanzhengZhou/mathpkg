For any sequence of measurable functions, the pointwise supremum, infimum, limit superior, and limit inferior are all measurable. This is fundamental: the class of measurable functions is closed under countable lattice operations and under pointwise limits. Consequently, the set of convergence points of a sequence of measurable functions is measurable, and the pointwise limit function (where it exists) is measurable.
