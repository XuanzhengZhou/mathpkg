An integrable simple function is a finite linear combination of characteristic functions of measurable sets of finite measure. Its integral is defined as the sum of coefficients times measures. Simple functions are the elementary building blocks of the Lebesgue integral: the integral of a non-negative measurable function is defined as the supremum of integrals of simple functions dominated by it.
