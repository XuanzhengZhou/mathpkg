A sequence is mean fundamental (Cauchy in $L^1$) if the $L^1$ distance between terms tends to zero. This is the natural Cauchy condition for the $L^1$ seminorm. The definition is central to the construction of the Lebesgue integral: integrable functions are defined as limits of mean fundamental sequences of integrable simple functions.
