A $\sigma$-ring is a non empty class $\mathbf{S}$ of sets such that
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] if $E \in \mathbf{S}$ and $F \in \mathbf{S}$, then $E - F \in \mathbf{S}$, and
\item[(b)] if $E_i \in \mathbf{S}$, $i = 1, 2, \cdots$, then $\bigcup_{i=1}^{\infty} E_i \in \mathbf{S}$.
\end{itemize}
Equivalently, a $\sigma$-ring is a ring closed under the formation of countable unions. A $\sigma$-ring is also closed under countable intersections, and if $E_i \in \mathbf{S}$, then both $\liminf_i E_i$ and $\limsup_i E_i$ belong to $\mathbf{S}$.
