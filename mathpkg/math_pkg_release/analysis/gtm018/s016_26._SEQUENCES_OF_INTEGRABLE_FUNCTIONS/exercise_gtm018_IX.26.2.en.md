section: "§26. Sequences of Integrable Functions"
exercise_number: 2

If $\{f_n\}$ is a uniformly fundamental sequence of functions, integrable over a measurable set $E$ of finite measure, then the function $f$, defined by $f(x) = \lim_n f_n(x)$, is integrable over $E$ and $\int_E |f_n - f| \, d\mu \to 0$ as $n \to \infty$.
