Mean convergence is stronger than convergence in measure: if the $L^1$ distance between terms tends to zero, then the sequence is fundamental in measure. This follows from Chebyshev's inequality: $\mu(\{|f_n-f_m| \geq \epsilon\}) \leq \frac{1}{\epsilon} \int |f_n-f_m| d\mu$. Mean fundamental sequences are thus also fundamental in measure.
