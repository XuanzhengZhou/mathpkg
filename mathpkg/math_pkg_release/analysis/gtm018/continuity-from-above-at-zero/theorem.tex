A finite valued set function $\nu$ on a class $\mathbf{E}$ of sets is **continuous from above at $0$** if, for every decreasing sequence $\{E_n\}$ in $\mathbf{E}$ with $\lim_n E_n = 0$, we have $\lim_n \nu(E_n) = 0$. A sequence $\{\nu_n\}$ of such set functions is **equicontinuous from above at $0$** if, for every decreasing $\{E_n\}$ with $\lim_n E_n = 0$ and every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $m_0$ such that $m \geq m_0$ implies $|\nu_n(E_m)| < \epsilon$ for all $n$.
