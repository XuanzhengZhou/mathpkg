The integral can be extended to non-negative non-integrable functions by assigning them the value $+\infty$, and to functions where at least one of $f^+$ or $f^-$ is integrable by taking the difference. This extended integral never produces the indeterminate form $\infty-\infty$. The term "integrable" is reserved for functions with finite integral (in the original sense).
