In a probability measure space $(\mu(X) = 1)$, measurable functions are called independent if the measure of any joint event factors as the product of marginal measures. This measure-theoretic definition of independence is the foundation of probability theory, leading to laws of large numbers, central limit theorems, and the study of stochastic processes.
