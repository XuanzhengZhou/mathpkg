The indefinite integral is countably additive: for a disjoint sequence $\{E_n\}$ with union $E$, $\int_E f d\mu = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \int_{E_n} f d\mu$. For simple functions this follows from the countable additivity of $\mu$ and the linear representation of $f$. This property extends to all integrable functions and makes the indefinite integral a signed measure when $f$ is integrable.
