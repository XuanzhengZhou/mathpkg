Every extended real valued function decomposes as $f = f^+ - f^-$ where $f^+(x) = \max(f(x),0)$ and $f^-(x) = -\min(f(x),0)$ are non-negative. This decomposition is fundamental for integration theory: the integral of $f$ is defined as $\int f^+ - \int f^-$ whenever at least one of these integrals is finite. Both parts are measurable when $f$ is measurable.
