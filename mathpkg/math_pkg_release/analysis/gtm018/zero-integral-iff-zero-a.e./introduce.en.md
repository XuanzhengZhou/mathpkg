For non-negative integrable functions, the integral is zero exactly when the function vanishes almost everywhere. The sufficiency ($f=0$ a.e. $\implies$ $\int f = 0$) is clear from the definition; the necessity uses the fact that $\{f > 0\}$ can be written as a countable union of sets where $f \geq 1/n$, and if the integral is zero, each must have measure zero.
