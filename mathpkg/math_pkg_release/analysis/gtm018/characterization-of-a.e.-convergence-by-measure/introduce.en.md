On a set of finite measure, a sequence converges a.e. to $f$ exactly when, for every $\epsilon>0$, the measure of points where the deviation exceeds $\epsilon$ infinitely often tends to zero. This characterization expresses a.e. convergence quantitatively in terms of measure, providing the essential link to convergence in measure.
