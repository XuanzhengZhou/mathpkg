**Theorem.** Suppose $f$ and $f_n$ ($n=1,2,\dots$) are real valued measurable functions on a set $E$ of finite measure, and write $E_n(\epsilon) = \{x: |f_n(x)-f(x)| \geq \epsilon\}$. The sequence $\{f_n\}$ converges to $f$ a.e. on $E$ if and only if $\lim_n \mu(E \cap \bigcup_{m=n}^{\infty} E_m(\epsilon)) = 0$ for every $\epsilon > 0$.
