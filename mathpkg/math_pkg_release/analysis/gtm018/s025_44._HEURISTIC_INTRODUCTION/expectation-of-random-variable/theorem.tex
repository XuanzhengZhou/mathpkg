The expectation (or average, or mean value) of a random variable $f$ on a probability space $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ is defined as its integral with respect to $\mu$:
$$\mathbb{E}[f] = \int_X f \, d\mu,$$
provided $f$ is integrable. For a discrete random variable taking finitely many values $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$ with probabilities $p_i = \mu(\{x \in X : f(x) = x_i\})$, the expectation is the weighted average
$$\mathbb{E}[f] = \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i p_i.$$
