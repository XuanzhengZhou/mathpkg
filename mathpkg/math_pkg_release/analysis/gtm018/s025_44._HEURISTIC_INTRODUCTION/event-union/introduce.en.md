The union $E \cup F$ of two events $E$ and $F$ is the event that occurs if and only if at least one of $E$ or $F$ occurs. This corresponds to the logical "or" in the algebra of events. In the set-theoretic model, the union of events is simply set union. For instance, if $E = \{2,4,6\}$ and $F = \{1,2,3\}$, then $E \cup F = \{1,2,3,4,6\}$.
