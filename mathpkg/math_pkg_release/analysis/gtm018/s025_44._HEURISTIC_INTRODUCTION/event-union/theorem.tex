For any two events $E$ and $F$, their union $E \cup F$ is the event that occurs if and only if at least one of $E$ or $F$ occurs. In set-theoretic terms, $E \cup F$ is the union of the two sets. For example, if $E = \{2,4,6\}$ (even) and $F = \{1,2,3\}$ (less than 4), then $E \cup F = \{1,2,3,4,6\}$.
