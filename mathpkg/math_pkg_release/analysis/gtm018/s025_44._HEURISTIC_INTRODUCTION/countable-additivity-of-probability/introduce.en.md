Countable additivity extends finite additivity to infinite disjoint sequences of events: $\mu(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} E_n) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \mu(E_n)$. While it is a stronger assumption than finite additivity, it does not contradict intuition and the theory built on it is sufficiently successful to justify the assumption. It is needed to handle events like $E = \bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty} E_n$ where $E_n$ is the event that a game first terminates on the $n$th trial; without countable additivity, one could not assign a probability to $E$ from the probabilities of the $E_n$.
