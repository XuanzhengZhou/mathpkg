The intersection $E \cap F$ of two events $E$ and $F$ is the event that occurs if and only if both $E$ and $F$ occur simultaneously. This corresponds to the logical "and" in the algebra of events. In the set-theoretic model, the intersection is simply set intersection. For instance, $E = \{2,4,6\}$ and $F = \{1,2,3\}$ yield $E \cap F = \{2\}$, the event that the die roll is both even and less than 4.
