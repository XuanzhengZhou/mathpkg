The mathematical theory of probability is formulated in terms of Boolean $\sigma$-algebras of sets. In this framework:
\begin{itemize}
\item an event is a set;
\item the opposite (complementary) event is the complement of that set;
\item mutually exclusive events are disjoint sets;
\item the simultaneous occurrence of two events is their intersection;
\item the occurrence of at least one of two events is their union.
\end{itemize}
The $\sigma$-algebra structure ensures closure under countable unions and complements, which is needed to handle events involving infinite sequences of trials.
