For every measurable set $E$ in $X$,
$$\lim_n p(E, T_n(x)) = \chi_E(x) \quad [\mu];$$
in other words, the conditional probabilities of $E$, for given values of the first $n$ coordinates of a point $x$, converge (except perhaps on a set of $x$'s of measure zero) to $0$ or $1$ according as $x \in E$ or $x \in E'$.
