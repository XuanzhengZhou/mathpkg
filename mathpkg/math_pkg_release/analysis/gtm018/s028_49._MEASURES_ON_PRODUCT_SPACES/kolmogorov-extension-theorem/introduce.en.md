The Kolmogorov extension theorem guarantees the existence of a probability measure on an infinite product of Borel unit intervals, given a consistent family of finite-dimensional probability measures on cylinder sets. This theorem underpins the construction of stochastic processes: any prescribed family of finite-dimensional distributions that satisfies natural consistency conditions determines a unique probability measure on the infinite product space. The proof relies on a compactness argument via closed subsets and a decreasing-sequence construction, leveraging the topological nature of the unit interval to ensure countable additivity of the extension.
