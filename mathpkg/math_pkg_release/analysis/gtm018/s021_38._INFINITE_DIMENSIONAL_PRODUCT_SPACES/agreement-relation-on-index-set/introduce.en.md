For an infinite Cartesian product $X = \prod_{i=1}^\infty X_i$, the relation $x \equiv y \ (J)$ means that two points $x$ and $y$ in $X$ coincide on all coordinates indexed by a specified subset $J$ of the positive integers. This is an equivalence relation that partitions $X$ into classes of points differing only on coordinates outside $J$. A $J$-cylinder is then defined as a set that is a union of full equivalence classes under this relation — membership in a $J$-cylinder depends only on coordinates indexed by $J$. Cylinder sets, built upon this agreement relation, are the fundamental building blocks for the product $\sigma$-algebra and the infinite product measure.
