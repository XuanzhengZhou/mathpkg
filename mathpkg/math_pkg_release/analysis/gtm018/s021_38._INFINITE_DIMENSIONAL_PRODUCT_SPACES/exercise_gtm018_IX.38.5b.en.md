The set $\bar{E}$ of all points $x = (x_1, x_2, \cdots)$ in $X$ for which $x_i = 1$ for all but a finite number of values of $i$ is measurable and has measure zero.

*Hint:* $\bar{E}$ is countable. We shall write $\bar{X} = X - \bar{E}$, and, in what follows, we shall consider the measure space $(\bar{X}, \bar{\mathbf{S}}, \bar{\mu})$, where $\bar{\mathbf{S}} = \mathbf{S} \cap \bar{X}$ and $\bar{\mu}(E \cap \bar{X}) = \mu(E)$, $E \in \mathbf{S}$.
