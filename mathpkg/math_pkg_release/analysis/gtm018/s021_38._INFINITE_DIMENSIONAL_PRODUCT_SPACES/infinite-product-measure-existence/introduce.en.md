Theorem B is the central result on infinite product measures: given a sequence of probability spaces (totally finite measure spaces normalized to total measure 1), there exists a unique countably additive measure on the infinite product $\sigma$-algebra that agrees with the finite product measures on all finite-dimensional cylinders. The proof uses the continuity-from-above characterization of countable additivity (9.F and 13.A): it suffices to show that the set function $\mu$ defined on the algebra of finite-dimensional cylinders is continuous from above at the empty set. This is established by a recursive section argument that constructs, for any decreasing sequence of cylinders with measures bounded below, a point belonging to their intersection.
