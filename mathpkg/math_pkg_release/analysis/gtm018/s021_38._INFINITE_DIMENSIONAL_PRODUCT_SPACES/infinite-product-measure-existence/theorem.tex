If $\{(X_i, \mathbf{S}_i, \mu_i)\}$ is a sequence of totally finite measure spaces with $\mu_i(X_i) = 1$, then there exists a unique measure $\mu$ on the $\sigma$-algebra $\mathbf{S} = \prod_{i=1}^{\infty} \mathbf{S}_i$ with the property that, for every measurable set $E$ of the form $A \times X^{(n)}$,

$$\mu(E) = (\mu_1 \times \cdots \times \mu_n)(A).$$
