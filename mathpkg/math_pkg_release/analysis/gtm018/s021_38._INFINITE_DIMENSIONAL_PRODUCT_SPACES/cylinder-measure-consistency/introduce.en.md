This proposition establishes that the assignment of a measure to a finite-dimensional measurable cylinder is independent of the representing dimension. If a set $E$ can be viewed both as a $\{1,\dots,m\}$-cylinder and as a $\{1,\dots,n\}$-cylinder (with $m < n$), then the measure computed from the $m$-dimensional projection equals that computed from the $n$-dimensional projection. This consistency is the crucial step that guarantees the set function $\mu$, defined on the algebra of all finite-dimensional measurable cylinders, is well-defined. It then serves as the foundation on which Theorem B extends $\mu$ to a countably additive measure on the full product $\sigma$-algebra.
