A measure is called finite if every set in its domain ring has finite measure, and $\sigma$-finite if every set can be covered by countably many sets of finite measure. When the ring is an algebra (contains the whole space $X$) and $\mu(X)$ is finite or $\sigma$-finite, the measure is called totally finite or totally $\sigma$-finite respectively. Sigma-finiteness is a crucial technical condition that ensures the validity of many fundamental theorems, including the uniqueness of extensions and the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
