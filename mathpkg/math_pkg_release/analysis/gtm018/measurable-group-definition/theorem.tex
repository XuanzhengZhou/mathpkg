A measurable group is a $\sigma$-finite measure space $(X,S,\mu)$ such that (a) $\mu$ is not identically zero, (b) $X$ is a group, (c) the $\sigma$-ring $S$ and the measure $\mu$ are invariant under left translations, and (d) the shearing transformation $S$ of $X \times X$ onto itself, defined by $S(x,y) = (x,xy)$, is measurability preserving.
