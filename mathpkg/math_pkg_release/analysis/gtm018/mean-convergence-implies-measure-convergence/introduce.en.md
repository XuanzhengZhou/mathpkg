Mean convergence is stronger than convergence in measure. As with simple functions, this follows from Chebyshev-type estimate: $\mu(\{|f_n-f| \geq \epsilon\}) \leq \frac{1}{\epsilon} \int |f_n-f| d\mu$. Thus $L^1$ convergence implies convergence in measure, and hence a.e. convergence for a subsequence.
