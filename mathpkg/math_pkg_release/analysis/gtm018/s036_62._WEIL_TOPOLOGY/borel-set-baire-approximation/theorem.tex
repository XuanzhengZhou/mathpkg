Given any Borel set $E$ in $X$, there exists a $\sigma$-compact full subgroup $Z$ of $X$ such that $E \subset Z$. Moreover, there exist two Baire subsets $A$ and $B$ of $Z$ such that $$A \subset E \subset B \quad \text{and} \quad \mu(B - A) = 0.$$ Since $Z$ is both open and closed in $X$, $A$ and $B$ are also Baire subsets of $X$.
