Theorem D establishes that the class $A$ of all sets of the form $EE^{-1}$ (with $E$ measurable of positive finite measure) has the finite intersection property in the sense that any two members of $A$ contain a common subset which also belongs to $A$. This filter-like property of $A$ is essential for verifying that the class $N$ satisfies the neighborhood base axiom (b) requiring the intersection of two neighborhoods to contain a third.
