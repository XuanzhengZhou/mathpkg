**Theorem G.** By Theorem E, there exists a compact, invariant Baire subgroup $Y$ such that $E$ is a union of cosets of $Y$. If $\{U_n\}$ is a sequence of open sets such that $Y = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} U_n$, then, for each positive integer $n$, there exists an open set $\hat{U}_n$ in the quotient group $\hat{X} = X/Y$ such that

$$Y \subset \pi^{-1}(\hat{U}_n) \subset U_n,$$

where $\pi$ is the projection from $X$ onto $\hat{X}$ (cf. 63.A). It follows that $Y = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \pi^{-1}(\hat{U}_n)$ and hence that $\{\hat{e}\} = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \hat{U}_n$; the separability of $\hat{X}$ is now implied by Theorem F. $\blacksquare$
