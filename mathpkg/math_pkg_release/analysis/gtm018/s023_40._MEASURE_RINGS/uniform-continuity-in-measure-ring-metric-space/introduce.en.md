Theorem A establishes that the fundamental Boolean operations of union and intersection, as well as the measure $\mu$ itself, are uniformly continuous functions on the metric space $\mathbf{s}(\mu)$ of a measure ring. This result is essential because it guarantees that operations defined by continuity (such as the extension of an isomorphism from a dense subset) are well-defined, a key step in the proof of the isomorphism theorem for measure algebras.
