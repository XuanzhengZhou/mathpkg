A measure ring is the abstract algebraic counterpart of a measure space, consisting of a Boolean $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$ equipped with a positive measure $\mu$. Every measure space $(X, \mathbf{S}, \mu)$ gives rise to a measure ring $(\mathbf{S}(\mu), \mu)$ by identifying sets that differ by a set of measure zero. Measure rings provide the setting for the isomorphism theorems that classify all sufficiently nice measure algebras.
