An isomorphism between two measure rings is a bijection that preserves the Boolean operations (difference and union) and the measure. This definition captures the notion of structural equivalence between abstract measure algebras, abstracting away from the underlying point set. The central result of the theory (the isomorphism theorem) states that all separable, non-atomic, normalized measure algebras are isomorphic to the unit interval measure algebra.
