A Boolean $\sigma$-algebra combines the structures of a Boolean $\sigma$-ring and a Boolean algebra: it is a Boolean ring with a maximal element in which every countable set of elements has a union. Boolean $\sigma$-algebras are the natural domains for finite measures in the algebraic abstraction of measure theory, corresponding to $\sigma$-algebras of sets with a total underlying space.
