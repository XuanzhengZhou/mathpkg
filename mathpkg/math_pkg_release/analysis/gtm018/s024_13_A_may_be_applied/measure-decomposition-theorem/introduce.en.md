Theorem E provides the complete structure theorem for finite Borel measures on the real line. Every such measure decomposes uniquely into three mutually singular components: an absolutely continuous part (given by a density via the Radon–Nikodym theorem), a purely atomic part (concentrated on a countable set of point masses), and a singular continuous part (singular with respect to Lebesgue measure yet having no atoms). This refines the classical Lebesgue decomposition into absolutely continuous and singular parts by further splitting the singular part.
