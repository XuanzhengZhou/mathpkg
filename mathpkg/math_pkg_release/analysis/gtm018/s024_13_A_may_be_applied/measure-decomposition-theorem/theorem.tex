If $\nu$ is a finite measure on the class $\mathbf{S}$ of Borel sets on the real line, then there exist three uniquely determined measures $\nu_1, \nu_2,$ and $\nu_3$ on $\mathbf{S}$ whose sum is $\nu$ and which are such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\nu_1$ is absolutely continuous with respect to Lebesgue measure $\mu$;
\item $\nu_2$ is purely atomic;
\item $\nu_3$ is singular with respect to $\mu$ but $\nu_3(\{x\}) = 0$ for every point $x$.
\end{enumerate}
