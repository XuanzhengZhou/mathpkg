This exercise demonstrates the power of measure-theoretic methods in real analysis. By using the correspondence between measures and their distribution functions established in this section, several well-known properties of monotone and absolutely continuous functions can be proved elegantly. The exercise asks the reader to prove two specific examples: countable discontinuities of monotone functions (2a) and a property involving absolutely continuous functions.
