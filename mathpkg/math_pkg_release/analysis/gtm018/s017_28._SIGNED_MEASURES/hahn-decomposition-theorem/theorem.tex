If $\mu$ is a signed measure, then there exist two disjoint sets $A$ and $B$ whose union is $X$, such that $A$ is positive and $B$ is negative with respect to $\mu$. The sets $A$ and $B$ are said to form a \textit{Hahn decomposition} of $X$ with respect to $\mu$.

If $X = A_1 \cup B_1$ and $X = A_2 \cup B_2$ are two Hahn decompositions of $X$, then for every measurable set $E$,
$$\mu(E \cap A_1) = \mu(E \cap A_2) \quad \text{and} \quad \mu(E \cap B_1) = \mu(E \cap B_2).$$
