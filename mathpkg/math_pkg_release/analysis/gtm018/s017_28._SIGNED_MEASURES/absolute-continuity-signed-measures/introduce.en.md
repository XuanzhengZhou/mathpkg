Absolute continuity for signed measures generalizes the concept from ordinary measures: $\nu \ll \mu$ means that $|\nu|(E) = 0$ whenever $|\mu|(E) = 0$, i.e., $\nu$ does not charge sets that $\mu$ deems negligible. This notion is fundamental to the Radon-Nikodym theorem and the Lebesgue decomposition.
