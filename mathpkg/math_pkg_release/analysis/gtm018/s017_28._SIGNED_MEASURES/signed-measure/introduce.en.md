A signed measure generalizes the notion of an ordinary (positive) measure by allowing the set function to take negative values as well as positive ones. It is defined on the measurable sets of a measurable space, is countably additive, assigns zero to the empty set, and can take at most one of the infinite values $+\infty$ or $-\infty$. Signed measures arise naturally as differences of ordinary measures, analogous to how the Lebesgue integral of a signed function produces a signed set function.
