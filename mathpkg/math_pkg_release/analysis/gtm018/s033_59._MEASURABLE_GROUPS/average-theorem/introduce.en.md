Theorem F, known as the average theorem, provides a fundamental integral formula in measurable groups: the integral of $f(x) = \mu(x^{-1}A \cap B)$ equals the product $\mu(A)\mu(B^{-1})$. It also establishes the measurability of the set of points where the symmetric difference $xA \Delta B$ has measure below a given threshold. The proof exploits the measure-preserving property of the transformation $Q = S^{-1}RS$. This result underpins the convolution algebra on measurable groups and is essential for the construction of Haar measure.
