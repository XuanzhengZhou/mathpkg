The shearing transformation $S(x,y) = (x,xy)$ is a fundamental transformation on the product space $X \times X$ associated with a measurable group $X$. It serves as the measure-theoretic counterpart to the shearing homeomorphism that characterizes topological groups. Its measurability-preserving property is one of the defining axioms of a measurable group.
