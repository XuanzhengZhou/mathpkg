A measurable group is the measure-theoretic analog of a topological group. It is a $\sigma$-finite measure space $(X,\mathbf{S},\mu)$ that is simultaneously a group, where the $\sigma$-ring and measure are left-translation invariant, and the shearing transformation $S(x,y)=(x,xy)$ preserves measurability. This structure is fundamental to the development of Haar measure on locally compact groups.
