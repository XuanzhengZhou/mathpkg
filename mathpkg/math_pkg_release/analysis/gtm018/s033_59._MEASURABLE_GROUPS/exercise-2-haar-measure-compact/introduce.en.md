This exercise asks to show that if $X$ is a compact group whose cardinality exceeds that of the continuum, and $\mu$ is a Haar measure on $X$, then $(X,\mathbf{S},\mu)$ fails to be a measurable group. The result highlights that the measurable group axioms impose genuine restrictions: not every compact group with a Haar measure satisfies them. The key issue is the cardinality constraint on the $\sigma$-ring $\mathbf{S}$.
