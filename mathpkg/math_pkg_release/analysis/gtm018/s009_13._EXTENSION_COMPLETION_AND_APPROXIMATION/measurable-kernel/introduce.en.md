A measurable kernel of $E \in \mathbf{H}(\mathbf{S})$ is a maximal measurable subset: $F \in \mathbf{S}$ with $F \subset E$ such that every measurable subset of $E - F$ has measure zero. Measurable kernels are the inner-measure analog of measurable covers.
