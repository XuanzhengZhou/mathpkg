Let $\hat{E}$ be a measurable cover of $E$, let $N$ be a measurable cover of $\hat{E} - E$, and write $F = \hat{E} - N$. Then $F = \hat{E} - N \subset \hat{E} - (\hat{E} - E) = E$. If $G \subset E - F$, then $G \subset \hat{E} - F = N$, and since $N$ is a measurable cover of $\hat{E} - E$, $\mu(G) = 0$. Thus $F$ is a measurable kernel of $E$.
