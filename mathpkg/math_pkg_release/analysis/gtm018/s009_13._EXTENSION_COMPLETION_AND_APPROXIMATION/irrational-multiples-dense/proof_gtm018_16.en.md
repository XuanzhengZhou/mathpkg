For integer $i$, let $n_i$ satisfy $0 \leq n_i + i\xi < 1$ and set $x_i = n_i + i\xi$. For any open $U$, pick $k$ with $\mu(U) > 1/k$. Among $x_1,\dots,x_{k+1}$ in $[0,1)$, two satisfy $|x_i - x_j| < 1/k$. An integral multiple of $x_i - x_j$ (an element of $A$) belongs to $U$. For $B$ use $[0,2)$, and $C = B + 1$.
