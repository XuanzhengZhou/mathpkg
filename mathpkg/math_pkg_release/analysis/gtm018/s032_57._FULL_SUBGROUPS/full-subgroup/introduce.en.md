A full subgroup of a topological group is a subgroup that contains an open set (equivalently, has non-empty interior). As shown in the subsequent theorem, full subgroups and their cosets are open—hence also closed—so the quotient topology is discrete. This property makes full subgroups essential in topological group theory: everything beyond the subgroup is captured by the coset structure, and the subgroup itself carries the entire topological character of the group.
