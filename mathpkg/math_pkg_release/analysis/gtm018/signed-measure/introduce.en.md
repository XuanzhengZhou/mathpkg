A signed measure generalizes the concept of measure by allowing negative values. It is required to be countably additive, and to avoid $\infty - \infty$ ambiguities, it can take at most one of the infinite values. Signed measures arise naturally as differences of measures and are essential for the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
