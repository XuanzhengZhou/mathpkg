Conditional probability formalizes the notion of the probability of an event given that another event has occurred. For a fixed measurable set $F$ of positive measure, the mapping $\mu_F(E) = \mu(F \cap E)/\mu(F)$ defines a new probability measure concentrated on $F$. This construction yields a clean characterization of independence: $E$ and $F$ are independent precisely when conditioning on $F$ does not change the probability of $E$.
