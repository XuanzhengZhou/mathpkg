If $\{E_i : i = 1, \dots, n\}$ is a finite, disjoint class of measurable sets of positive measure whose union is $X$ (i.e. if $\{E_i\}$ is a partition of $X$), then, for every measurable set $F$,

$$\mu(F) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \mu(F \cap E_i) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \mu(E_i) \, \mu_{E_i}(F).$$

This result is known as the \text{law of total probability}.
