The law of total probability expresses the probability of an event as a weighted sum of conditional probabilities over a partition of the sample space. Given a finite partition of $X$ into sets of positive measure, the probability of any measurable set $F$ decomposes as $\mu(F) = \sum_i \mu(E_i) \mu_{E_i}(F)$. This fundamental identity is the basis for conditioning arguments throughout probability theory.
