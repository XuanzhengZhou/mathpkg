We have

$$\sigma^2(f + g) = \int (f + g)^2 \, d\mu - \left( \int (f + g) \, d\mu \right)^2$$

$$= \int f^2 \, d\mu + 2 \int fg \, d\mu + \int g^2 \, d\mu - \left( \int f \, d\mu \right)^2 - 2 \left( \int f \, d\mu \right) \left( \int g \, d\mu \right) - \left( \int g \, d\mu \right)^2;$$

the desired result follows from Theorem A.
