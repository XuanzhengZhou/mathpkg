If $f_1$ and $f_2$ are independent functions, neither of which vanishes a.e., then a necessary and sufficient condition that both $f_1$ and $f_2$ be integrable is that their product $f_1 f_2$ be integrable; if this condition is satisfied, then

$$\int f_1 f_2 \, d\mu = \int f_1 \, d\mu \cdot \int f_2 \, d\mu.$$
