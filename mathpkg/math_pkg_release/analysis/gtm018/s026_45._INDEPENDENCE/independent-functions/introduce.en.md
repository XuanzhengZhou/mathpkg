Stochastic independence of real-valued measurable functions extends the notion of independent sets to the functional setting. A family of measurable functions is independent if the probability of any finite joint event concerning the values of distinct functions factors as the product of marginal probabilities. Equivalently, the inverse images of arbitrary Borel sets under the functions form an independent class of sets.
