The variance of a random variable (measurable function) on a probability space measures its dispersion around its mean. For a function with finite second moment, the variance is defined as the expected squared deviation from the mean. Schwarz's inequality guarantees that finite second moment implies integrability, and the identity $\sigma^2(f) = \int f^2 d\mu - (\int f d\mu)^2$ provides a convenient computational formula.
