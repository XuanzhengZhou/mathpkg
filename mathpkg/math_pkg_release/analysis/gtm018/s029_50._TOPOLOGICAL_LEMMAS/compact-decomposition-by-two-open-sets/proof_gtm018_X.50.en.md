Since $C - U$ and $C - V$ are disjoint compact sets, there exist two disjoint open sets $\tilde{U}$ and $\tilde{V}$ such that $C - U \subset \tilde{U}$ and $C - V \subset \tilde{V}$. Define $D = C - \tilde{U}$ and $E = C - \tilde{V}$. It is easy to verify that $D \subset U$, $E \subset V$, and that $D$ and $E$ are compact. Since $\tilde{U} \cap \tilde{V} = \emptyset$, we have

$$D \cup E = (C - \tilde{U}) \cup (C - \tilde{V}) = C - (\tilde{U} \cap \tilde{V}) = C.$$
