The characteristic function $\chi_E$ maps each point to $1$ if it belongs to $E$ and $0$ otherwise. This bijection between subsets and $\{0,1\}$-valued functions allows set-theoretic operations to be translated into algebraic operations on functions, a technique used throughout measure theory when integrating characteristic functions of measurable sets.
