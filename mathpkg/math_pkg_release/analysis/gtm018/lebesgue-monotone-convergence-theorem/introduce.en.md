For an increasing sequence of non-negative integrable functions with bounded integrals, the pointwise limit is integrable and its integral equals the limit of the integrals. This is the fundamental theorem that justifies term-by-term integration of monotone sequences. Combined with the bounded convergence theorem, it forms the cornerstone of Lebesgue integration theory.
