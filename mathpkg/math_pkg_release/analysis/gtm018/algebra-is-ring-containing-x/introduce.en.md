An algebra (or Boolean algebra) of sets is a nonempty class closed under finite unions and complements. Every algebra is a ring because $E - F = E \cap F' = (E' \cup F)'$ expresses differences in terms of unions and complements. The relationship between rings and algebras is simple: an algebra is exactly a ring that contains the whole space $X$, since $E' = X - E$.
