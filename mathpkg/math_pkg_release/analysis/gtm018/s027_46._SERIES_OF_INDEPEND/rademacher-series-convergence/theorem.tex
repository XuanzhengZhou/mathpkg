Let $\{f_n\}$ be the sequence of Rademacher functions on the unit interval (cf. 45.5). If $\{c_n\}$ is a sequence of real numbers, then the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} c_n f_n(x)$ converges almost everywhere if and only if $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} c_n^2 < \infty$. Equivalently, in the language of probability: a necessary and sufficient condition for the convergence with probability $1$ of the series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \pm c_n$ (where $+$ and $-$ are equally likely and the signs are determined independently of each other) is the convergence of $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} c_n^2$.
