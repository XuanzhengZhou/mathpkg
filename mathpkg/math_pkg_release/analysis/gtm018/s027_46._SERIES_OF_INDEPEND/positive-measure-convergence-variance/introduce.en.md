This theorem provides a converse to Theorem B: if a series of independent, zero-mean, square-integrable functions converges on a set of positive measure, then the sum of the variances must be finite. The proof uses Egoroff's theorem to extract a set of positive measure where partial sums are uniformly bounded, then exploits independence to derive the variance bound. This result shows that for independent series, convergence on any positive-measure set forces the natural variance condition.
