Kolmogorov's inequality provides a maximal bound for the partial sums of a finite collection of independent, zero-mean functions with finite variance on a probability space. It states that the probability that the maximum absolute partial sum exceeds a threshold $\epsilon$ is bounded by $\epsilon^{-2}$ times the sum of the variances. This result generalizes Chebyshev's inequality from a single sum to the maximum of partial sums and is a fundamental tool in proving almost everywhere convergence of series of independent functions.
