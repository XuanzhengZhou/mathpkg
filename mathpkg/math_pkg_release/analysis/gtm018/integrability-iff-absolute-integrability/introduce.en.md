In Lebesgue integration (unlike improper Riemann integration), integrability is equivalent to absolute integrability: $f$ is integrable exactly when $|f|$ is. The nontrivial direction ($|f|$ integrable $\implies$ $f$ integrable) follows from Theorem A with $|f|$ dominating $f$. This property is fundamental — it means Lebesgue integration corresponds to absolutely convergent series in the discrete case.
