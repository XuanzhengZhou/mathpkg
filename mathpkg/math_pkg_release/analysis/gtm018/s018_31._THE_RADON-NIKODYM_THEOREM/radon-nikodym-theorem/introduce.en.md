The Radon-Nikodym Theorem is one of the fundamental results of measure theory. It states that if a signed measure $\nu$ is absolutely continuous with respect to a $\sigma$-finite measure $\mu$, then $\nu$ can be represented as an integral with respect to $\mu$ via a measurable density function $f$, called the Radon-Nikodym derivative $\frac{d\nu}{d\mu}$. The theorem has far-reaching consequences in probability theory (existence of conditional expectations), functional analysis, and the theory of differentiation of measures.
