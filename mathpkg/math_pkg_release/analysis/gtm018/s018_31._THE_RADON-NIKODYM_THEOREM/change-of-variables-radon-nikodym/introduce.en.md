Theorem B gives the substitution rule for integrals with respect to Radon-Nikodym derivatives: if $\mu \ll \lambda$, then an integral with respect to $\mu$ can be transformed into an integral with respect to $\lambda$ by multiplying the integrand by $\frac{d\mu}{d\lambda}$. Symbolically, $\int f \, d\mu = \int f \frac{d\mu}{d\lambda} \, d\lambda$, which is the measure-theoretic analogue of the ordinary calculus substitution rule $d\mu = \frac{d\mu}{d\lambda} d\lambda$.
