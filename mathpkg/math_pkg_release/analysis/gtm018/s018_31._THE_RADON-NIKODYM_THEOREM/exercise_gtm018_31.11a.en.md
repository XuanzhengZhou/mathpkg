If $\mu$ and $\nu$ are measures on a $\sigma$-algebra $\mathbf{S}$ such that $\mu$ is finite and $\nu \ll \mu$, then there exists a measurable set $E$ such that $X - E$ is of $\sigma$-finite measure with respect to $\nu$, and such that, for every measurable subset $F$ of $E$, $\nu(F)$ is either $0$ or $\infty$.
(Hint: use the method of exhaustion (cf. 17.3), to find a measurable set $E$ with the property that, for every measurable subset $F$ of $E$, $\nu(F)$ is either $0$ or $\infty$, and such that $\mu(E)$ is maximal; another application of the method of exhaustion shows that $X - E$ is of $\sigma$-finite measure with respect to $\nu$.)
