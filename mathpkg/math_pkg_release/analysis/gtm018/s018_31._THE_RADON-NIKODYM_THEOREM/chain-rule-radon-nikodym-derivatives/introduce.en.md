This theorem establishes the chain rule for Radon-Nikodym derivatives: if $\nu$ is absolutely continuous with respect to $\mu$ and $\mu$ is absolutely continuous with respect to $\lambda$, then the Radon-Nikodym derivative of $\nu$ with respect to $\lambda$ is the product of the derivatives, i.e., $\frac{d\nu}{d\lambda} = \frac{d\nu}{d\mu}\frac{d\mu}{d\lambda}$ almost everywhere $[\lambda]$. This mirrors the ordinary chain rule $(d\nu/d\lambda) = (d\nu/d\mu)(d\mu/d\lambda)$ from calculus, justifying the differential notation.
