If the indefinite integral of $f$ vanishes identically, then $f=0$ almost everywhere. Apply Theorem D to the sets $\{f>0\}$ and $\{f<0\}$; both must have measure zero. This theorem establishes that the indefinite integral uniquely determines the integrand up to a.e. equality — the foundation of the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
