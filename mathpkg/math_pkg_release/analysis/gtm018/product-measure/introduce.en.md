The product measure $\mu \times \nu$ generalizes the concept of area from the product of lengths. Under $\sigma$-finiteness, there exists a unique product measure on the product $\sigma$-ring satisfying $\pi(E \times F) = \mu(E)\nu(F)$ on measurable rectangles. This construction is the foundation for Fubini's theorem on iterated integrals.
