If $C$ is a compact set, $F$ is a closed set, and $C \cap F = 0$, then there exists a function $f$ in $\mathfrak{F}$ such that $f(x) = 0$ for $x$ in $C$ and $f(x) = 1$ for $x$ in $F$, where $\mathfrak{F}$ is the class of all real valued continuous functions $f$ on $X$ such that $0 \leq f(x) \leq 1$ for all $x$ in $X$.
