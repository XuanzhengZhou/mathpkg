Theorem A provides an integral representation formula that links two measures $\mu$ and $\nu$ on a measurable group $(X, S)$. Given any nonnegative measurable function $f$, the $\mu$-integral of $f$ can be expressed in terms of a $\nu$-integral involving the function $y \mapsto f(y^{-1})/\nu(Ey)$, scaled by the factor $\mu(E)$. This result is the key technical tool used to prove the qualitative uniqueness of the measure in a measurable group.
