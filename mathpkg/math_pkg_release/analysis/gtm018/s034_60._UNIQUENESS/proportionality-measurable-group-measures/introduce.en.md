Theorem B is the central uniqueness result for measures on a measurable group. It states that any two measures $\mu$ and $\nu$ on a measurable group $(X, S)$ are proportional: there exists a nonnegative finite constant $c = \mu(E)/\nu(E)$ (for any $E$ with $0 < \nu(E) < \infty$) such that $\mu(F) = c\,\nu(F)$ for all measurable sets $F$. The proof follows directly from Theorem A by setting $f$ to be the characteristic function of $F$ and applying the integral representation formula twice.
