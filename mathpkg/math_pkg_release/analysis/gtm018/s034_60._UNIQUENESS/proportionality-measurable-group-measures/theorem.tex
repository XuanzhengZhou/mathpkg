If $\mu$ and $\nu$ are two measures such that $(X, S, \mu)$ and $(X, S, \nu)$ are measurable groups, and if $E \in S$ is such that $0 < \nu(E) < \infty$, then, for every $F \in S$,
$$
\mu(E)\,\nu(F) = \nu(E)\,\mu(F).
$$
Equivalently, $\mu(F) = c\,\nu(F)$ for every $F \in S$, where $c = \mu(E)/\nu(E)$ is a nonnegative finite constant.
