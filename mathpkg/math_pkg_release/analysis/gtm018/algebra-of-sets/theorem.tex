An algebra (or Boolean algebra) of sets is a non empty class $\mathbf{R}$ of sets such that
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] if $E \in \mathbf{R}$ and $F \in \mathbf{R}$, then $E \cup F \in \mathbf{R}$, and
\item[(b)] if $E \in \mathbf{R}$, then $E' \in \mathbf{R}$.
\end{itemize}
An algebra may be characterized as a ring containing $X$.
