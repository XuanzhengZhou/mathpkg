If a sequence of integrable functions is dominated by an integrable function $g$ and converges in measure (or a.e.) to $f$, then $f$ is integrable and the convergence holds in $L^1$. This is one of the most important theorems in integration theory. The domination $|f_n| \leq |g|$ provides the uniformities needed in Theorem C, and the a.e. case reduces to the in-measure case using the domination.
