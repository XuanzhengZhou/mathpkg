Let $X$ and $Y$ be as in (7.14) and let $\varphi$ be a continuous mapping of $X$ into $Y$ with the following property

satisfy (7.14) for the preassigned $\varepsilon$. If $x$ and $x'$ are in $A'_{\varepsilon} \cap X$, then (1) may be applied. If at least one of $x$ and $x'$ is in $A_{\varepsilon}$, we may suppose that $x \in A_{\varepsilon}$. Then $x$ is in some $B_{\eta y_k}(y_k)$. If $\varrho(x, x') < \delta$, then we have

$$\varrho(y_k, x') \leq \varrho(y_k, x) + \varrho(x, x')$$
$$< \eta_{y_k} + \delta$$
$$\leq 2 \eta_{y_k}.$$

That is, $x'$ is in $B_{2\eta y_k}(y_k)$ and so (2) shows that $\sigma(\varphi(x'), \varphi(y_k)) < \frac{\varepsilon}{2}$. Since $x$ is also in $B_{2\eta y_k}(y_k)$, (2) implies that $\sigma(\varphi(x), \varphi(y_k)) < \frac{\varepsilon}{2}$, and hence

$$\sigma(\varphi(x), \varphi(x')) \leq \sigma(\varphi(x), \varphi(y_k)) + \sigma(\varphi(y_k), \varphi(x'))$$
$$< \frac{\varepsilon}{2} + \frac{\varepsilon}{2}$$
$$= \varepsilon.$$

Thus $\varphi$ is uniformly continuous. $\square$
