Let $F$ be an ordered field. A sequence $(a_n)$ $[= (a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n, \ldots)]$ of elements of $F$ is called bounded if there is an element $b \in F$ such that $|a_n| \leq b$ for each positive integer $n$. A sequence $(a_n)$ is called Cauchy if for every $e \in F$ such that $e > 0$, there is a positive integer $N(e)$ such that $|a_p - a_q| < e$ for all $p, q \geq N(e)$.
