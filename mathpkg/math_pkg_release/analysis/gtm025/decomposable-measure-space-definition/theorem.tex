A measure space $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ is \textbf{decomposable} if there exists a family $\mathcal{F} \subset \mathcal{A}$ of finite-measure sets such that every set in $\mathcal{A}$ of $\sigma$-finite measure can be covered by countably many members of $\mathcal{F}$, and for $E \in \mathcal{A}$ with $\mu(E) > 0$, there exists $F \in \mathcal{F}$ with $\mu(E \cap F) > 0$.
