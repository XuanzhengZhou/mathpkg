A relation $f$ is said to be single-valued if $(x, y) \in f$ and $(x, z) \in f$ imply $y = z$. If $f$ and $f^{-1}$ are both single-valued, then $f$ is called a one-to-one relation. The definitions of many-to-one, one-to-many, and many-to-many relations are analogous.
