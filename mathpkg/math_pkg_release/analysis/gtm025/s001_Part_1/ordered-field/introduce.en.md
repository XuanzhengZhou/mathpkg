An ordered field is a field equipped with a positive cone $P$ satisfying three axioms. The set $P$ partitions the nonzero elements into positive and negative classes, and is closed under addition and multiplication. This structure allows the comparison of field elements via inequalities. Ordered fields provide the algebraic foundation for constructing the real numbers and studying their properties.
