Let $\infty$ and $-\infty$ be two distinct objects, neither of which is a real number. The set $R^{\#} = R \cup \{-\infty\} \cup \{\infty\}$ is known as the set of extended real numbers. We make $R^{\#}$ a linearly ordered set by taking the usual ordering in $R$ and defining $-\infty < \infty$ and $-\infty < x < \infty$ for each $x \in R$. For $a, b \in R^{\#}$ with $a < b$, intervals $]a,b[$, $[a,b]$, $[a,b[$, and $]a,b]$ are defined analogously. Arithmetic is extended by: $\infty + x = x + \infty = \infty$ ($x \neq -\infty$); for $x > 0$: $\infty \cdot x = x \cdot \infty = \infty$, $(-\infty) \cdot x = x \cdot (-\infty) = -\infty$; $\infty \cdot 0 = 0 \cdot \infty = (-\infty) \cdot 0 = 0 \cdot (-\infty) = 0$; $\infty \cdot \infty = \infty$; for $x < 0$: $\infty \cdot x = x \cdot \infty = -\infty$, $(-\infty) \cdot x = x \cdot (-\infty) = \infty$. The expressions $\infty \cdot (-\infty)$, $(-\infty) \cdot \infty$, and $(-\infty) \cdot (-\infty)$ are not defined.
