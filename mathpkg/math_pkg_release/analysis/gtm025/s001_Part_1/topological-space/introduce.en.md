A topological space abstracts the notion of nearness and continuity from metric spaces using only the concept of open sets. The three axioms -- the whole space and empty set are open, arbitrary unions and finite intersections of open sets are open -- are motivated by the properties of open sets in $R$. This axiomatic framework unifies the study of continuity, compactness, connectedness, and convergence across diverse mathematical structures.
