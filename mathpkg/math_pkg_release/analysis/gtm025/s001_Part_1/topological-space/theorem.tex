A topological space is a pair $(X, \mathcal{O})$ where $X$ is a set and $\mathcal{O} \subset \mathcal{P}(X)$ satisfies:
(i) $\varnothing \in \mathcal{O}$ and $X \in \mathcal{O}$;
(ii) if $\{U_i\}_{i \in I} \subset \mathcal{O}$, then $\bigcup_{i \in I} U_i \in \mathcal{O}$;
(iii) if $U_1, U_2, \ldots, U_n \in \mathcal{O}$, then $U_1 \cap U_2 \cap \cdots \cap U_n \in \mathcal{O}$.
Members of $\mathcal{O}$ are called open sets.
