Unlike the real numbers, the complex numbers cannot be made into an ordered field. Any attempt to define a positive cone $P$ leads to a contradiction: either $i \in P$ or $-i \in P$, and in either case, squaring yields $-1 \in P$, which contradicts the theorem that squares are nonnegative in ordered fields. This fundamental difference distinguishes real from complex analysis.
