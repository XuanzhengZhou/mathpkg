Let $\mathcal{O}$ denote the family of all open subsets of $R$. Then:
(i) $\varnothing \in \mathcal{O}$ and $R \in \mathcal{O}$;
(ii) if $\mathcal{U}$ is a subfamily of $\mathcal{O}$, then $\bigcup \mathcal{U} \in \mathcal{O}$;
(iii) if $\{U_1, U_2, \ldots, U_n\} \subset \mathcal{O}$, then $U_1 \cap U_2 \cap \cdots \cap U_n \in \mathcal{O}$.
