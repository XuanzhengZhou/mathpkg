Let $U$ be a nonvoid open subset of $R$. Then there exists one and only one pairwise disjoint family $\mathcal{I}$ of open intervals of $R$ such that $U = \bigcup \mathcal{I}$. The family $\mathcal{I}$ is countable and the members of $\mathcal{I}$ are called component intervals of $U$. For each $I \in \mathcal{I}$, the endpoints of $I$ are not in $U$.
