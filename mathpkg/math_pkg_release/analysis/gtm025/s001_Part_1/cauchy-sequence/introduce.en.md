Three fundamental classes of sequences in an ordered field are defined: bounded sequences whose terms stay within a fixed absolute value bound, Cauchy sequences whose terms become arbitrarily close to each other, and null sequences that converge to zero. These concepts, introduced by Augustin-Louis Cauchy, are essential for constructing the completion of an ordered field and for the rigorous foundation of limits in analysis.
