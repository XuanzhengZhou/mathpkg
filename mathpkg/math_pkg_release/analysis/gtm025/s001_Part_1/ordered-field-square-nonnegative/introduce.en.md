In any ordered field, every nonzero square belongs to the positive cone $P$. Consequently, the multiplicative identity $1$ is always positive. This fundamental property follows from the closure of $P$ under multiplication and the trichotomy condition that partitions $F$ into $P$, $\{0\}$, and $-P$.
