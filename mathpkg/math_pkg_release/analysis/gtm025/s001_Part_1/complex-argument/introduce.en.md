The argument of a nonzero complex number $z$ is the set of angles $\theta$ such that $z = |z|(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$. The principal argument $\Arg(z)$ selects the unique angle in $(-\pi, \pi]$. The argument is central to the polar representation of complex numbers, multiplication (which adds arguments), and the geometric interpretation of complex arithmetic in the plane.
