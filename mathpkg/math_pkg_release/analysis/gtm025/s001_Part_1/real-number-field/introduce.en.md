The real number field $R$ is defined as any complete Archimedean ordered field, with the canonical example being the Cauchy completion $\bar{Q}$ of the rational numbers. By the uniqueness theorem, all such fields are isomorphic, so the definition is well-posed. The real numbers form the foundation for classical analysis, providing the domain for limits, continuity, differentiation, and integration.
