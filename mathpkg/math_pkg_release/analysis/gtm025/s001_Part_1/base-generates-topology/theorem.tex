Let $X$ be a set and let $\mathcal{B} \subset \mathcal{P}(X)$ satisfy:
(i) $\bigcup \mathcal{B} = X$;
(ii) if $B_1, B_2 \in \mathcal{B}$ and $x \in B_1 \cap B_2$, there exists $B_3 \in \mathcal{B}$ such that $x \in B_3 \subset B_1 \cap B_2$.
Then the family $\mathcal{O} = \{\bigcup \mathcal{A} : \mathcal{A} \subset \mathcal{B}\}$ is a topology for $X$, and $\mathcal{B}$ is a base for $\mathcal{O}$.
