For subsets $A, B$ of a topological space $X$:
(i) $\varnothing^{-} = \varnothing$;
(ii) $(A \cup B)' = A' \cap B'$;
(iii) $A \subset A^{-}$, $A^{-\,-} = A^{-}$;
(iv) $A \subset B$ implies $A^{-} \subset B^{-}$;
(v) $A$ is closed iff $A$ contains all its limit points;
(vi) $A'^{-} = A^{\circ\prime}$, $A^{\circ} = A'^{-'}$;
(vii) $\partial A = A^{-} \cap A^{\circ\prime} = A^{-} \cap A'$;
(viii) $(A \cup B)^{-} = A^{-} \cup B^{-}$;
(ix) $(A \cap B)^{\circ} = A^{\circ} \cap B^{\circ}$;
(x) $\bigcup A_i^{-} \subset (\bigcup A_i)^{-}$;
(xi) $\bigcap A_i^{\circ} \supset (\bigcap A_i)^{\circ}$;
(xii) $\varnothing$ and $X$ are closed.
