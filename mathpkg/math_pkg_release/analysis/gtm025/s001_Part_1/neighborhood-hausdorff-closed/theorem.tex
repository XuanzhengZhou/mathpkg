Let $X$ be a topological space.
A \textbf{neighborhood} of a point $x \in X$ is any open subset $U$ of $X$ such that $x \in U$.
$X$ is \textbf{Hausdorff} if each pair of distinct points of $X$ have disjoint neighborhoods.
A set $A \subset X$ is \textbf{closed} if $A'$ (its complement) is open.
A point $x \in X$ is a \textbf{limit point} of $A \subset X$ if every neighborhood of $x$ meets $A$ in a point different from $x$.
The \textbf{closure} $A^{-}$ of $A$ is $A \cup \{\text{all limit points of } A\}$.
The \textbf{interior} $A^{\circ}$ of $A$ is $\{x \in X : A \text{ is a neighborhood of } x\}$.
The \textbf{boundary} $\partial A$ of $A$ is $A^{-} \cap (A')^{-}$.
