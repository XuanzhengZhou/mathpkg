An ordered field is Archimedean if for every positive element $b$, the multiples $b, 2b, 3b, \ldots$ are unbounded above. Equivalently, the natural numbers embedded in the field form an unbounded set. The real numbers $R$ are Archimedean, but there exist non-Archimedean ordered fields containing infinitesimals. This property is crucial for the uniqueness of the real number system.
