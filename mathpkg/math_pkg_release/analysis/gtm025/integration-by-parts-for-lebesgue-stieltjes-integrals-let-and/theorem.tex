[Integration by parts for Lebesgue-Stieltjes integrals]. Let $\alpha$ and $\beta$ be any two real-valued nondecreasing functions on $R$ and let $\lambda_{\alpha}$ and $\lambda_{\beta}$ be their corresponding Lebesgue-Stieltjes measures (9.19). Then $a < b$ in $R$ implies

(i) $\lambda_{\alpha}([a, b]) = \alpha(b-) - \alpha(a-)$;

(ii) $\lambda_{\alpha}(\{b\}) = \alpha(b+) - \alpha(b-)$;

(iii) $\lambda_{\alpha}([a, b]) = \alpha(b+) - \alpha(a-)$;

(iv) $\int_{[a,b]} \beta(x+) d\lambda_{\alpha}(x) + \int_{[a,b]} \alpha(x-) d\lambda_{\beta}(x)$
$= \alpha(b+) \beta(b+) - \alpha(a-) \beta(a-)$;

and

(v) $\int_{[a,b]} \frac{\beta(x+) + \beta(x-)}{2} d\lambda_{\alpha}(x) + \int_{[a,b]} \frac{\alpha(x+) + \alpha(x-)}{2} d\lambda_{\beta}(x)$
$= \alpha(b+) \beta(b+) - \alpha(a-) \beta(a-)$.
