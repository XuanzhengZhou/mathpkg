These five fundamental principles of set theory -- the Axiom of Choice, Tukey's Lemma, the Hausdorff Maximality Principle, Zorn's Lemma, and the Well-Ordering Theorem -- are all logically equivalent. Any one implies all the others, making them interchangeable tools for proving existence theorems throughout mathematics.
