Consider a set $A$ with two binary operations $+$ and $-$ [called addition and multiplication, respectively], which is an Abelian group under $+$, with identity 0, and in which the equalities

$$a \cdot (b + c) = (a \cdot b) + (a \cdot c) \quad [\text{left distributive law}] ,$$

$$(a + b) \cdot c = (a \cdot c) + (b \cdot c) \quad [\text{right distributive law}] ,$$

and

$$(a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c) \quad [\text{associative law for multiplication}]$$

hold for all $a, b, c \in A$. Then $A$ is called a ring. If $a \cdot b = b \cdot a$ for all $a, b$ in a ring $A$, $A$ is called commut
