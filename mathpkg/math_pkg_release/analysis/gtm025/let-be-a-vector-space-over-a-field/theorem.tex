Let $X$ be a vector space over a field $F$ and let $B$ be a Hamel basis for $X$ over $F$. Then for each $x \in X$ there exists a unique function $\alpha$ from $B$ into $F$ such that $\alpha(b) = 0$ except for finitely many $b \in B$ and $x = \sum_{b \in B} \alpha(b) b$, i.e., $x$ can be expressed in just one way as a finite linear combination of members of $B$.
