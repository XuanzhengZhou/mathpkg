The Cartesian product generalizes the familiar product of finitely many sets to arbitrary indexed families. An element of the product is a choice function selecting one element from each set. For finite index sets, this reduces to ordered tuples. The sets \(R^n\) (Euclidean \(n\)-space) and \(K^n\) (unitary \(n\)-space) are important special cases.
