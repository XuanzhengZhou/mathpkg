The Axiom of Choice asserts that the Cartesian product of any nonempty family of nonempty sets is nonempty. Equivalently, for any family of nonempty sets there exists a choice function selecting one element from each set. This innocent-looking statement has far-reaching consequences throughout mathematics and is equivalent to Zorn's Lemma, the Well-Ordering Theorem, and other fundamental principles.
