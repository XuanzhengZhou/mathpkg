A complex [real] normed linear space that is complete in the metric $\|x - y\|$ is called a complex [real] Banach space. A complex [real] Banach space that is also a normed algebra is called a Banach algebra.

Banach spaces are very important in contemporary analysis; many basic theorems can be couched in abstract terms as assertions about Banach spaces of one kind or another. We will give many examples throughout the text of this technique [see in particular § 14].
