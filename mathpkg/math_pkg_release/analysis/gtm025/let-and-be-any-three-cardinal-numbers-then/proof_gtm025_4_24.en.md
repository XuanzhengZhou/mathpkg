All twelve of these conclusions are proved by defining appropriate one-to-one mappings. As a sample we prove (viii) while the remaining eleven are left as exercises.

Let $U, V,$ and $W$ be sets such that $\bar{U} = u, \bar{V} = v,$ and $\bar{W} = w$. We must show that $(U^V)^W \sim U^{V \times W}$. To do this we define a mapping $\varphi$ on $(U^V)^W$ by the rule

$$\varphi(f) = g \in U^{V \times W}$$

where

$$g(y, z) = (f(z))(y) \in U \text{ for } (y, z) \in V \times W.$$
