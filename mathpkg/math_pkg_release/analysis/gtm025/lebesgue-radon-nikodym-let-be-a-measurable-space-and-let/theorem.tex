[LEBESGUE-RADON-NIKODYM]. Let $(X, \mathcal{A})$ be a measurable space and let $\mu$ and $v$ be finite measures on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ such that $v \ll \mu$. Then there exists a function $f_0 \in \mathfrak{L}_1^+ (X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ such that

(i) $\int_X f dv = \int_X ff_0 d\mu$

for all nonnegative, extended real-valued, $\mathcal{A}$-measurable functions $f$ on $X$. For $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1 (X, \mathcal{A}, v)$, the function $ff_0$ is in $\mathfrak{L}_1 (X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ and (i) holds. In particular, we have

(ii) $v(A) = \int_A f_0 d\mu$

for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$.
