Let $X$ be a topological space. A set $A \subset X$ is said to be nowhere dense if $A^{-\circ} = \varnothing$. A set $F \subset X$ is said to be of first category if $F$ is a countable union of nowhere dense sets. All other subsets of $X$ are said to be of second category.

(6.54) Baire Category Theorem. Let $X$ be a complete metric space. Suppose that $A \subset X$ and that $A$ is of first category in $X$. Then $X \cap A'$ is dense in $X$. Thus $X$ is of second category [as a subset of itself].
