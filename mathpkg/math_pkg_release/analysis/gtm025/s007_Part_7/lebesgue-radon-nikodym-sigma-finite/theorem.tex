Let $\mu$ and $\nu$ be $\sigma$-finite measures on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ with $\nu \ll \mu$. Then there exists a nonnegative $\mathcal{A}$-measurable $f_0$ such that $\int_X f d\nu = \int_X f f_0 d\mu$ for all nonnegative measurable $f$, and $\nu(A) = \int_A f_0 d\mu$ for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$. Moreover, $f_0$ is unique $\mu$-a.e.
