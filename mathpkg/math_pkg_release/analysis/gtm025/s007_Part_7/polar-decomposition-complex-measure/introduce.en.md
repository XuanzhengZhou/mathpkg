Every complex measure can be expressed as integration of a unimodular function against its total variation. This is the measure-theoretic analog of the polar decomposition $z = e^{i\theta}|z|$ for complex numbers, yielding the useful estimate $|\int f d\nu| \leq \int |f| d|\nu|$.
