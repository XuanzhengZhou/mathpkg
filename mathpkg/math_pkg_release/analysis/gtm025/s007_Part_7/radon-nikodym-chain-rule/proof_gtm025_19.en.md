$\mu_2 \ll \mu_0$ is trivial by transitivity. Let $f_0 = d\mu_1/d\mu_0$, $f_1 = d\mu_2/d\mu_1$. Then $\int f d\mu_2 = \int f f_1 d\mu_1 = \int f f_1 f_0 d\mu_0$ for all nonnegative measurable $f$. By uniqueness of the Radon-Nikodym derivative, $d\mu_2/d\mu_0 = f_1 f_0$ $\mu_0$-a.e.
