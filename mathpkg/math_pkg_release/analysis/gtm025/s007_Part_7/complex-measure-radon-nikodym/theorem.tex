Let $\mu$ be $\sigma$-finite and $\nu$ a finite complex measure on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ with $\nu \ll \mu$. Then there exists a unique $f_0 \in \mathfrak{L}_1(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ such that: (i) $\int f d\nu = \int f f_0 d\mu$ for $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(|\nu|)$; (ii) $\nu(A) = \int_A f_0 d\mu$; (iii) $\int_A |f_0| d\mu \leq |\nu|(A)$; (iv) $\int_X |f_0| d\mu = |\nu|(X)$.
