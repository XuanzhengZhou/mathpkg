The culminating decomposition theorem: every regular Borel measure on $\mathbb{R}$ splits into three mutually singular parts -- absolutely continuous (the "smooth" part given by integrating the derivative), singular continuous (continuous but concentrated on a Lebesgue-null set), and purely discontinuous (a sum of point masses). The corresponding function $\alpha$ decomposes analogously.
