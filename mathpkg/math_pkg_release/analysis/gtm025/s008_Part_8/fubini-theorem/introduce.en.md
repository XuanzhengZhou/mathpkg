Fubini's theorem is the definitive result on interchanging the order of integration for integrable functions on product spaces. Under the hypothesis that the absolute integral is finite (in any of the three senses), the theorem guarantees that all sliced functions are integrable almost everywhere, the integrated functions are integrable, and that the three integrals coincide. Together with Tonelli's theorem, it forms the foundation of multivariable integration theory.
