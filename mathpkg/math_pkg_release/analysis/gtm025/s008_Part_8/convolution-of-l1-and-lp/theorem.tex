Suppose that $1 < p < \infty$, that $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$, and that $g \in \mathfrak{L}_p(R)$. Then for almost every $x \in R$, the function $y \mapsto f(x - y)g(y)$ is in $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. Define

$$(f * g)(x) = \int_R f(x - y)g(y) \, dy = \int_R f(y)g(x - y) \, dy.$$

Then $f * g \in \mathfrak{L}_p(R)$, $f * g = g * f$ almost everywhere, and

$$\|f * g\|_p \leq \|f\|_1 \cdot \|g\|_p.$$
