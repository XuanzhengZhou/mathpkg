For each of the functions $f$ on $R \times R$ defined by the following formulas, compute the iterated integrals in both orders and the absolute value integrals:

(a) $f(x,y) = \frac{x^2 - y^2}{(x^2 + y^2)^2}$;

(b) $f(x,y) = \begin{cases} \frac{1}{(x - \frac{1}{2})^3} & \text{for } 0 < y < |x - \frac{1}{2}|, \\ 0 & \text{otherwise}; \end{cases}$

(c) $f(x,y) = \frac{x - y}{(x^2 + y^2)^{3/2}}$;

(d) $f(x,y) = \frac{1}{(1 - xy)^p}$, where $p > 0$.

Compare your findings with Fubini's theorem (21.13).
