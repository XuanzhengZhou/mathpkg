Prove that there exists a subset $S$ of $I \times I$ $(I = [0, 1])$ such that $\overline{S_x} \leq 1$ and $\overline{S^y} \leq 1$ for all $x, y \in I$, but $S \notin \mathcal{M}_\lambda \times \mathcal{M}_\lambda$. [Outline. Let $\mathcal{F}$ be the family of all compact sets $F \subset I \times I$ such that $\lambda \times \lambda(F) > 0$. Let $\Delta$ be as in (21.26) and let $\alpha \to F_\alpha$ be a one-to-one mapping of $P_\Delta$ onto $\mathcal{F}$. Choose $(x_0, y_0) \in F_0$. If $\alpha < \Delta$, choose $(x_\alpha, y_\alpha) \in F_\alpha$ such that $x_\alpha \notin \{x_\beta : \beta < \alpha\}$ and $y_\alpha \notin \{y_\beta : \beta < \alpha\}$. This is possible since each $F_\alpha$ has positive measure. Let $S = \{(x_\alpha, y_\alpha) : \alpha < \Delta\}$. Then $S$ has the required properties.]
