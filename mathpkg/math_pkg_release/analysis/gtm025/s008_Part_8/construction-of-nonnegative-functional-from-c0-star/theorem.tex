Let $L \in \mathfrak{C}_0(X)^*$, where $X$ is a locally compact Hausdorff space. For $f \in \mathfrak{C}_0^+$, define
$$I(f) = \sup\{|L(g)| : g \in \mathfrak{C}_0, |g| \leq f\}.$$
Then $I$ extends uniquely to a nonnegative linear functional on $\mathfrak{C}_{00}(X)$ satisfying:
(i) $|L(f)| \leq I(|f|)$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{C}_0$;
(ii) $I(|f|) \leq \|L\| \cdot \|f\|_u$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{C}_0$;
(iii) $\|I\| = \|L\|$.
