Let $X$ and $Y$ be locally compact Hausdorff spaces, and let $(X, \mathcal{M}_\mu, \mu)$ and $(Y, \mathcal{M}_\nu, \nu)$ be $\sigma$-finite measure spaces as in §§ 9 and 10. Then the completion $\overline{\mu \times \nu}$ of $\mu \times \nu$ is regular on $\overline{\mathcal{M}_\mu \times \mathcal{M}_\nu}$ in the sense that if $E \in \overline{\mathcal{M}_\mu \times \mathcal{M}_\nu}$, then

(i) $\overline{\mu \times \nu}(E) = \inf\{\overline{\mu \times \nu}(U): E \subset U, U \text{ is open}\}$;

(ii) $\overline{\mu \times \nu}(E) = \sup\{\overline{\mu \times \nu}(F): E \supset F, F \text{ is compact}\}$.
