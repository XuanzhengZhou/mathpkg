Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space. Suppose that there is a subfamily $\mathcal{F}$ of $\mathcal{A}$ with the following properties:

(i) $0 \leq \mu(F) < \infty$ for all $F \in \mathcal{F}$;

(ii) the sets in $\mathcal{F}$ are pairwise disjoint and $\bigcup \mathcal{F} = X$;

(iii) if $E \in \mathcal{A}$ and $\mu(E) < \infty$, then $\mu(E) = \sum_{F \in \mathcal{F}} \mu(E \cap F)$;$^1$

(iv) if $S \subset X$ and $S \cap F \in \mathcal{A}$ for all $F \in \mathcal{F}$, then $S \in \mathcal{A}$.

Then $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ and $\mu$ itself are said to be decomposable and $\mathcal{F}$ is called a decomposition of $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$.
