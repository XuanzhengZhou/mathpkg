A sequence $(u_n)_{n=1}^{\infty} \subset \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$ is called an approximate unit [or a positive kernel] if:

(i) $u_n \geq 0$ for all $n$;
(ii) $\|u_n\|_1 = 1$ for all $n$;
(iii) for each neighborhood $V$ of 0 we have

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \int_{V'} u_n(t) dt = 0.$$

It is obvious that approximate units exist; e.g., take $u_n = \frac{n}{2} \xi \left[ -\frac{1}{n}, \frac{1}{n} \right]$.
