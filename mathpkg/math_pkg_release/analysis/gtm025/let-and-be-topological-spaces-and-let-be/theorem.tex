Let $X$ and $Y$ be topological spaces and let $f$ be a function from $X$ into $Y$. Then $f$ is said to be continuous at a point $x \in X$ if for each neighborhood $V$ of $f(x)$ there exists a neighborhood $U$ of $x$ such that $f(U) \subset V$. The function $f$ is said to be continuous on $X$ if $f$ is continuous at each point of $X$.
