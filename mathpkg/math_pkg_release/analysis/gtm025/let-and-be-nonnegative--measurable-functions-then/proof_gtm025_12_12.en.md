Let $(f_n)$ and $(g_n)$ be nondecreasing sequences of nonnegative simple functions with limits $f$ and $g$ respectively (11.35). The sequence $(f_n + g_n)$ increases to $f + g$, and so by (12.11) and the additivity of $L$ on nonnegative simple functions (12.7), we have

$$L(f) + L(g) = \lim_{n \to \infty} L(f_n) + \lim_{n \to \infty} L(g_n) = \lim_{n \to \infty} L(f_n + g_n) = L(f + g).$$
