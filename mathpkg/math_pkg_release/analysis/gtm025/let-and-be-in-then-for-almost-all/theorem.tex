Let $f$ and $g$ be in $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. Then for almost all $x \in R$ the function $y \rightarrow f(x - y)g(y)$ is in $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. For all such $x$ define

(i) $f * g(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x - y)g(y) dy$.

Then $f * g \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$ and $\|f * g\|_1 \leq \|f\|_1 \cdot \|g\|_1$. [The function $f * g$ is called the convolution of $f$ and $g$.]
