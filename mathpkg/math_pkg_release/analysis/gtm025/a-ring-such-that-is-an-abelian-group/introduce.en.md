(5.5) Definition. A ring $F$ such that $F \cap \{0\}'$ is an Abelian group under multiplication is called a field. is a definition from Chapter 5 (Set Theory and Algebra) of the book *Real and Abstract Analysis* by Edwin Hewitt and Karl Stromberg (GTM 25). It is part of the development of set-theory.
