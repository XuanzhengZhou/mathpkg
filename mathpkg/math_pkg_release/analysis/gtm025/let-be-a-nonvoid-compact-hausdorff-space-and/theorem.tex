Let $X$ be a nonvoid compact Hausdorff space, and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a subset of $\mathfrak{C}'(X)$ such that:

(i) $\mathfrak{S}$ is a separating family;
(ii) $\mathfrak{S}$ contains the function 1;
(iii) $f \in \mathfrak{S}$ and $\alpha \in R$ imply $\alpha f \in \mathfrak{S}$;
(iv) $f, g \in \mathfrak{S}$ implies $f + g \in \mathfrak{S}$;
(v) $f, g \in \mathfrak{S}$ implies $\max\{f, g\} \in \mathfrak{S}$.

Then $\mathfrak{S}$ is dense in $\mathfrak{C}'(X)$ in the topology induced by the uniform metric.¹
