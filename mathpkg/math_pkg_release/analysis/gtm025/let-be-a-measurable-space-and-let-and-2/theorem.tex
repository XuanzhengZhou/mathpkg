Let $(X, \mathcal{A})$ be a measurable space, and let $\mu$ and $\nu$ be measures on $(X, \mathcal{A})$ such that $\mu(X) < \infty$ and $\nu \ll \mu$. Then there exists a set $E \in \mathcal{A}$ such that:

(i) for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$ such that $A \subset E$, $\nu(A) = 0$ or $\nu(A) = \infty$;

(ii) for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$ such that $A \subset E$, $\mu(A) = 0$ if $\nu(A) = 0$;

(iii) $\nu$ is $\sigma$-finite on $E'$.
