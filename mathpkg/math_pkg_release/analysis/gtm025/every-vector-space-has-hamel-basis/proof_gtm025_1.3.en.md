Let \(X\) be a vector space with at least two elements. Let \(x \neq 0\) in \(X\). Then \(\{x\}\) is linearly independent. Thus the family \(\mathcal{F}\) of all linearly independent subsets of \(X\) is nonvoid. The definition of linear independence shows that \(\mathcal{F}\) is of finite character. Tukey's lemma proves that \(\mathcal{F}\) contains a maximal member, i.e., \(X\) contains a Hamel basis.
