Let $(X, \varrho)$ be a metric space. Let

$$\mathcal{B}_{\varrho} = \{B_{\varepsilon}(x) : \varepsilon > 0, x \in X\}.$$

Then $\mathcal{B}_{\varrho}$ is a base for a topology $\mathcal{O}_{\varrho}$ for $X$. We call $\mathcal{O}_{\varrho}$ the topology generated by $\varrho$. The members of $\mathcal{O}_{\varrho}$ are called $\varrho$-open sets.
