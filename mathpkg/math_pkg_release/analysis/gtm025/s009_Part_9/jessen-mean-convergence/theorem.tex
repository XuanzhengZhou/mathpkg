Let $\Gamma$ be an arbitrary infinite index set. For finite $\Omega \subset \Gamma$, $1 \leq p < \infty$, $f \in \mathfrak{L}_p(T,\mathcal{M},\mu)$, define
$$f_{\Omega'}(\mathbf{t}_\Omega, \mathbf{t}_{\Omega'}) = \int_{T_\Omega} f(\mathbf{u}_\Omega, \mathbf{t}_{\Omega'}) d\mu_\Omega(\mathbf{u}_\Omega).$$
Then $f_{\Omega'} \in \mathfrak{L}_p$ and $\lim_\Omega \|f_{\Omega'} - \int_T f d\mu\|_p = 0$, where the limit is taken along the directed set of all finite subsets $\Omega \subset \Gamma$.
