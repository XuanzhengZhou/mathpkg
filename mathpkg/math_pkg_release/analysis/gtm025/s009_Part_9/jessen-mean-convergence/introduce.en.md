Jessen's Mean Convergence Theorem provides a Fubini-type result for infinite product measures. For any $\mathfrak{L}_p$ function ($1 \leq p < \infty$), integrating out finitely many coordinates yields convergence in $L^p$ norm to the overall integral as more coordinates are integrated out. This is one of several distinct analogues of Fubini's theorem for infinite products.
