Let $f \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. If $\hat{f} \in \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$, then
$$(2\pi)^{-\frac{1}{2}} \int_R \hat{f}(y) \exp(ixy) \, dy = f(x)$$
for every Lebesgue point $x$ of $f$. Hence $f$ is equal a.e. to a function in $\mathfrak{C}_0(R) \cap \mathfrak{L}_1(R)$. If $f$ is continuous, the equality holds everywhere.
