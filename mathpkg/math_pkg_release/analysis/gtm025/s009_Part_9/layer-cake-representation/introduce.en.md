The layer cake representation expresses the integral of $\varphi \circ f$ via the distribution function $t \mapsto \mu(\{f > t\})$. By slicing the region under the graph horizontally, Fubini's theorem converts a vertical integral into an integral over level sets. This technique is fundamental in analysis and probability, leading to formulas for $L^p$ norms and maximal inequalities.
