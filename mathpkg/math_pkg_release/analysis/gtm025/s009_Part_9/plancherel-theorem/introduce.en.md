Plancherel's theorem extends the Fourier transform to a unitary operator on $\mathfrak{L}_2(R)$. It is a cornerstone of Fourier analysis, establishing an isometric isomorphism of $\mathfrak{L}_2(R)$ onto itself. It is the continuous analogue of the Riesz-Fischer theorem for Fourier series on $[-\pi,\pi]$. The proof uses density of $\mathfrak{L}_1 \cap \mathfrak{L}_2$ and the norm-preservation from (21.52).
