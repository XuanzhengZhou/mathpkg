Let $p, q, r$ be real numbers such that $p > 1$, $q > 1$, and $\frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} - 1 = \frac{1}{r} > 0$. Suppose $f \in \mathfrak{L}_p(R)$ and $g \in \mathfrak{L}_q(R)$. Prove that $f * g \in \mathfrak{L}_r(R)$ and $\|f * g\|_r \leq \|f\|_p \cdot \|g\|_q$.

[Hints. Let $a = r$, $\frac{1}{p} = \frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b}$, $\frac{1}{q} = \frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{c}$. Note $\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b} + \frac{1}{c} = 1$ and use the generalized Holder inequality (13.26) on
$$\left(|f(x-y)|^p |g(y)|^q\right)^{1/a} \left(|f(x-y)|^p\right)^{1/b} \left(|g(y)|^q\right)^{1/c}.$$]
