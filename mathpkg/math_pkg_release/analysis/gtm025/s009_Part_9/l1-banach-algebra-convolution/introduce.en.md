The space $\mathfrak{L}_1(R)$ of Lebesgue integrable functions, equipped with convolution as multiplication, forms a complex commutative Banach algebra. This algebraic structure is fundamental in harmonic analysis. The inequality $\|f * g\|_1 \leq \|f\|_1 \|g\|_1$ ensures convolution is a continuous bilinear operation.
