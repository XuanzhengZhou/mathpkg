This theorem extends the Hardy-Littlewood maximal inequality to endpoint cases. For the $L^1$ endpoint, the maximal function integral is controlled by $\lambda(E)$ plus an $L \log L$ term. For $0 < p < 1$, a different type of power estimate holds. These results complement the $L^p$ theorem for $p>1$ and describe the behavior of the maximal operator at the boundaries of its boundedness range.
