Let $E$ be a real linear space, $S$ a subspace, and $P$ a cone in $E$ such that $(-x + S) \cap P \neq \varnothing$ for all $x \in E$. Suppose $f$ is a linear functional on $S$ such that $x \in S$ and $x \geq 0$ imply $f(x) \geq 0$. Then $f$ can be extended to a linear functional $g$ on $E$ such that $x \in E$ and $x \geq 0$ imply $g(x) \geq 0$.
