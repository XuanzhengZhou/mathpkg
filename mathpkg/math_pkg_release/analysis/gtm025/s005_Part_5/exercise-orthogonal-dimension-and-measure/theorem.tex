Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a $\sigma$-finite measure space and $\mathfrak{d}$ the orthogonal dimension of $\mathfrak{L}_2(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$. Prove: if $\mathfrak{d}$ is finite then $m = 2^{\mathfrak{d}}$; if $\mathfrak{d}$ is infinite then $m = \mathfrak{d}$, where $m$ is the number of atoms.
