Let $H$ be a Hilbert space and let $\mathfrak{B}(H)$ denote the space of all bounded linear operators from $H$ into $H$. Then $\mathfrak{B}(H)$ is a Banach algebra with unit (composition as multiplication). Moreover, for each $T \in \mathfrak{B}(H)$ there exists a unique $T^* \in \mathfrak{B}(H)$ (the \textbf{adjoint}) such that
$$\langle T(x), y \rangle = \langle x, T^*(y) \rangle$$
for all $x, y \in H$. Also $T^{**} = T$ and $\|T^*\| = \|T\|$.
