Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space, $p > 1$, and $f$ an $\mathcal{A}$-measurable function such that $\{x : f(x) \neq 0\}$ is a countable union of sets of finite measure, and $fg \in \mathfrak{L}_1$ for all $g \in \mathfrak{L}_p$. Then $f \in \mathfrak{L}_{p'}$. Also prove Leach's generalization for measure spaces where every set of infinite measure contains a subset of finite positive measure.
