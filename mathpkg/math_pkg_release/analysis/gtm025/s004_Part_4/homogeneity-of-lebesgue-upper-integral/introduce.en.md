For any nonnegative function $f$ and any nonnegative real number $t$, the Lebesgue upper integral satisfies $L(tf) = tL(f)$. This homogeneity property, together with additivity, establishes that $L$ behaves like a linear functional on nonnegative simple functions. The proof is elementary.
