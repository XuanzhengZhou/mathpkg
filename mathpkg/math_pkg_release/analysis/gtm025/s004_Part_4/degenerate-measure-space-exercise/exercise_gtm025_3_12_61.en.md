Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space that is degenerate in the sense of (10.3): $\mu(A) = 0$ or $\mu(A) = \infty$ for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$. Show that every function $f \in \mathcal{L}_1(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ vanishes except on a set of $\mu$-measure zero and that $\int_X |f| d\mu = 0$. [This unpleasant property justifies the term 'degenerate' for the measure spaces under consideration.]
