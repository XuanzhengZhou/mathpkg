Let $f(x) = \lim f_n(x)$ whenever the limit exists. Then $f$ is defined a.e. and $\mathcal{A}$-measurable. Also $|f(x) - f_n(x)| \leq |f(x)| + |f_n(x)| \leq 2s(x)$ and $\lim |f(x) - f_n(x)| = 0$ a.e. By (12.24) and (12.28.ii), $\left| \int_X f d\mu - \int_X f_n d\mu \right| \leq \int_X |f - f_n| d\mu \to \int_X 0 d\mu = 0$.
