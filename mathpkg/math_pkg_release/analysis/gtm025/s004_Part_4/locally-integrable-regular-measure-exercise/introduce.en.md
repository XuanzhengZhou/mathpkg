Let $X$ be a locally compact, $\sigma$-compact Hausdorff space and let $\mu$ be a regular measure defined on a $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets of $X$ [$\mathcal{A} \supset \mathcal{B}(X)$]. Let $f$ be an $\mathcal{A}$-measurable function on $X$ such that $f(X) \subset [0, \infty]$ and such that $f\xi_F \in \mathcal{L}_1(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ for all compact subsets $F$ of $X$. [Such an $f$ is called locally $\mu$-integrable.] Define the set-function $\nu$ on $\mathcal{A}$ by

$$\nu(A) = \int_A f(x) d\mu(x).$$

Prove that $\nu$ is a regular measure on $\mathcal{A}$.
