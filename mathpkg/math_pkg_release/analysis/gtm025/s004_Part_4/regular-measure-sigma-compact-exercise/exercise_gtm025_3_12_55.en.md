Let $X$ be a locally compact Hausdorff space such that every open subset of $X$ is $\sigma$-compact. Suppose that $\mu$ is a measure defined on $\mathcal{B}(X)$ such that $\mu(F) < \infty$ for all compact sets $F \subset X$. Prove that $\mu$ is a regular measure. [First consider the case that $X$ is compact. Consider the family $\mathcal{R} = \{E \in \mathcal{B}(X) : \mu(E) = \inf\{\mu(U) : U \text{ is open}, U \supset E\} \text{ and } \mu(E) = \sup\{\mu(F) : F \text{ is compact}, F \subset E\}\}$.]
