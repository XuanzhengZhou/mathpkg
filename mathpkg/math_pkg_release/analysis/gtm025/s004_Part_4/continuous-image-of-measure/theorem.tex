Let $X$ and $Y$ be locally compact Hausdorff spaces, and let $\varphi$ be a continuous mapping of $X$ onto $Y$. Let $\mu$ be a measure on $X$ in the sense of §9, and suppose either (i) $\varphi^{-1}(F)$ is compact for every compact $F \subset Y$, or (ii) $\mu(X)$ is finite. For $f \in \mathfrak{C}_{00}(Y)$, define $\nu(f) = \int_X f \circ \varphi d\mu$. Then $\nu$ extends uniquely to a regular measure on $Y$ such that $\nu(B) = \mu(\varphi^{-1}(B))$ for every Borel set $B \subset Y$.
