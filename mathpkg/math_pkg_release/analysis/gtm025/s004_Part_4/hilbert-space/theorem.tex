Let $H$ be a linear space over $K$ having an inner product $(x, y) \mapsto \langle x, y \rangle$ satisfying: $\langle x_1 + x_2, y \rangle = \langle x_1, y \rangle + \langle x_2, y \rangle$; $\langle \alpha x, y \rangle = \alpha \langle x, y \rangle$; $\overline{\langle x, y \rangle} = \langle y, x \rangle$; and $\langle x, x \rangle > 0$ for $x \neq 0$. Then $\|x\| = \sqrt{\langle x, x \rangle}$ defines a norm. If $H$ is complete relative to this norm, $H$ is called a Hilbert space.
