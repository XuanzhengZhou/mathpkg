B. Levi's theorem (also known as the Monotone Convergence Theorem) states that for a nondecreasing sequence of measurable functions whose integrals are not all $+\infty$, the limit of the integrals equals the integral of the limit. This is one of the cornerstones of Lebesgue integration theory.
