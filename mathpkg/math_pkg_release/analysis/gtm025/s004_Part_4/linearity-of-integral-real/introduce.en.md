The Lebesgue integral is a linear functional on the space $\mathfrak{L}_1^r$ of real-valued integrable functions. This linearity is a consequence of the additivity and homogeneity properties established for the upper integral $L$, extended to signed functions via the decomposition $f = f^+ - f^-$.
