For nonnegative extended real-valued functions $f$ and $g$ on a measure space $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$, the Lebesgue upper integral $L$ is monotone: if $f(x) \le g(x)$ for every $x \in X$, then $L(f) \le L(g)$. This basic property follows directly from the definition of $L$ via measurable dissections and is essential for establishing the linearity and countable additivity of the integral.
