The special case of Young's inequality with $\varphi(u) = u^{p-1}$ yields the fundamental inequality $ab \le a^p/p + b^{p'}/p'$ for conjugate exponents $p, p'$ satisfying $1/p + 1/p' = 1$. This is the key ingredient in the proof of Holder's inequality.
