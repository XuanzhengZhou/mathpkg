Let $f$ be extended real-valued $\mathcal{A}$-measurable. Then $f^{-1}(\{\infty\}), f^{-1}(\{-\infty\}) \in \mathcal{A}$ and $f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{A}$ for all $B \in \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$. The family $\{S \subset \mathbb{R} : f^{-1}(S) \in \mathcal{A}\}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra containing all open sets, hence all Borel sets.
