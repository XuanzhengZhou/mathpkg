Let $X, \mathcal{A}, \mu$, and $\bar{\mu}$ be as in (10.36) and let $\mathcal{S}$ be the $\sigma$-algebra generated by $\mathcal{A}$. Prove that $\bar{\mu}$ is the unique countably additive extension of $\mu$ to $\mathcal{S}$ if $\mu$ is $\sigma$-finite. Show that if the $\sigma$-finiteness hypothesis fails, $\mu$ may have more than one extension [Hint: $X = [0, 1]$, $\mathcal{A}$ = finite unions of $[a, b]$, $\mu(\emptyset) = 0$, $\mu(A) = \infty$ for $A \neq \emptyset$].
