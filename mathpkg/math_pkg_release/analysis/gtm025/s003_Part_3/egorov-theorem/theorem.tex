Let $(X, \mathcal{A}, \mu)$ be a measure space with $\mu(X) < \infty$, and let $(f_n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ be $\mathcal{A}$-measurable functions converging $\mu$-a.e. to a finite-valued function $f$. For each $\delta > 0$, there exists $A \in \mathcal{A}$ such that $\mu(A') < \delta$ and $f_n \to f$ uniformly on $A$.
