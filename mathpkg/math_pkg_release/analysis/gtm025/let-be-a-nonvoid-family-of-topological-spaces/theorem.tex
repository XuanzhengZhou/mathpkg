If this were not the case, there would be a point $x \in X$ such that for every $\iota \in I$, $\pi_i(x) = x_i \in X_i \cap (U_i)'$; hence $x \notin \pi_i^{-1}(U)$ for all $\pi_i^{-1}(U) \in U$. That is, $U$ would not be a cover of $X$. Hence we can [and do] choose an $\eta \in I$ such that $U_\eta = X_\eta$. Since $X_\eta$ is compact, there is a finite family $\{U_1, \ldots, U_n\} \subset U_\eta$ such that $X_\eta = U_1 \cup U_2 \cup \cdots \cup U_n$. Plainly $\{\pi_\eta^{-1}(U_j): j = 1, \ldots, n\}$ is a finite subcover of $U$ for $X$.
