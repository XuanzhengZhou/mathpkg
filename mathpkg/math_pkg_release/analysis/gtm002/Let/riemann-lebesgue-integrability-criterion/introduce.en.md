The Riemann-Lebesgue Theorem (Theorem 7.5) is the definitive characterization of Riemann integrability: a bounded function is Riemann integrable if and only if it is continuous almost everywhere. This bridges Lebesgue measure theory with the classical Riemann integral. The forward direction uses Lemma 7.6 and the fact that the set of points where oscillation exceeds $1/n$ can be covered by intervals of arbitrarily small total length.
