Theorem 7.8 characterizes the discontinuity sets of monotone functions: they are exactly the countable sets. The forward direction follows from the fact that a monotone function can have at most finitely many jumps of size exceeding any given $\varepsilon$ on a bounded interval. The converse constructs a monotone function with prescribed countable discontinuity set using a convergent series of positive terms as jump sizes.
