Although $C[a,b]$ with the uniform metric is complete, the same set equipped with the integral metric $\sigma(f,g) = \int_a^b |f(x)-g(x)| \, dx$ is not complete. A counterexample is provided by a sequence of piecewise linear functions on $[0,1]$ that converge in the $L^1$ sense to a step function with a jump discontinuity, which is not continuous. This illustrates that completeness depends essentially on the choice of metric, not merely on the underlying set.
