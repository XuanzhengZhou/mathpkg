A pseudometric space relaxes the metric axioms by allowing distinct points to have zero distance. In analysis, pseudometrics arise naturally; for instance, the $L^1$ distance on Riemann-integrable functions is only a pseudometric because a function that vanishes except on a nullset has integral zero. Such spaces are canonically transformed into genuine metric spaces by taking the quotient under the equivalence relation $x \sim y \iff \varrho(x,y) = 0$.
