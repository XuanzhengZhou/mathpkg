Lemma 14.1 provides a useful equivalent characterization of Lebesgue nullsets: a set has measure zero if and only if it can be covered by a sequence of intervals not just once but infinitely many times, with finite total length. This formulation is particularly convenient for Fubini-type arguments, where pointwise properties (such as a given $x$ belonging to infinitely many rectangles) correspond to infinite covering, allowing the series convergence condition to enforce measure zero.
