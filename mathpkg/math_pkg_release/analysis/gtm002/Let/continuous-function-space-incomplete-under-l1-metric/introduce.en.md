The space $C[0,1]$ with the $L^1$ metric is not complete. A standard counterexample is the sequence of continuous functions $f_n$ that approximate the indicator function of $[0, 1/2]$: they form a Cauchy sequence in the $L^1$ metric, but converge (in $L^1$) to a function that is discontinuous at $1/2$, hence not in $C[0,1]$. This incompleteness motivates the introduction of the Lebesgue integral and $L^1$ spaces.
