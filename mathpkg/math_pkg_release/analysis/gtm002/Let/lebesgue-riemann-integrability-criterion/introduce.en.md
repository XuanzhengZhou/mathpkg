Lebesgue's criterion (Theorem 7.5) provides a complete characterization of Riemann integrability: a bounded function is Riemann integrable if and only if its set of discontinuity points has Lebesgue measure zero. This fundamental result bridges the Riemann and Lebesgue theories of integration, showing that the obstruction to Riemann integrability is precisely a set of discontinuities of positive measure. The proof uses the concept of oscillation to quantify the contribution of discontinuities to the upper and lower Darboux integrals.
