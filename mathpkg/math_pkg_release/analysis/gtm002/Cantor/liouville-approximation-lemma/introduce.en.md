Liouville's Approximation Lemma (Lemma 2.2) establishes a fundamental bound on how well an algebraic number of degree $n > 1$ can be approximated by rational numbers. For any such algebraic number $z$, there exists a constant $M$ such that $|z - p/q| > 1/(M q^n)$ for all rationals $p/q$. This shows that algebraic numbers of higher degree admit only poor rational approximations --- a fact Liouville exploited to construct the first explicit transcendental numbers.
