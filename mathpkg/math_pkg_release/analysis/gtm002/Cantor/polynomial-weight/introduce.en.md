The weight of a polynomial with integer coefficients is the sum of its degree plus the absolute values of all its coefficients. This notion provides a convenient way to enumerate all polynomials with integer coefficients: for each weight there are only finitely many such polynomials. Oxtoby uses this device to prove that the set of real algebraic numbers is denumerable.
