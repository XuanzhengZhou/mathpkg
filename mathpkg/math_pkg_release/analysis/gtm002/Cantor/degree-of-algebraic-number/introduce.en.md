The degree of an algebraic number $z$ is the minimal degree of a non-zero polynomial with integer coefficients having $z$ as a root. For example, any rational number is algebraic of degree $1$, $\sqrt{2}$ is algebraic of degree $2$, and $\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3}$ is algebraic of degree $4$. The degree is a fundamental invariant in Diophantine approximation, used in Liouville's bound on rational approximations.
