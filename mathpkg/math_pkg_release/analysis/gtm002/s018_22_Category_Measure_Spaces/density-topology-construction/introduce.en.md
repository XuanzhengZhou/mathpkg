Theorem 22.5 shows how to construct a topology on a complete finite measure space $(X, S, \mu)$ from a lower density $\phi$. The basic open sets are of the form $\phi(A) \setminus N_A$, where $N_A$ is a nullset. In this "density topology," the nowhere dense sets are exactly the nullsets, the Baire-property sets are exactly the measurable sets, and the original measure $\mu$ becomes a category measure. This provides a general method for turning measure spaces into category measure spaces.
