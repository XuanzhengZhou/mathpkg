The metric space $(\tilde{S}, \varrho)$ consists of equivalence classes of measurable sets of finite measure, where two sets are identified if they differ by a nullset. The distance is given by the measure of their symmetric difference: $\varrho(E, F) = m(E \triangle F)$. This is one of the key examples of a complete metric space constructed from measure-theoretic objects, and its completeness parallels that of the space of $R$-integrable functions under the $L^1$ metric.
