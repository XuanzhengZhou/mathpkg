Another interesting metric is obtained by introducing the distance between two measurable sets $E$ and $F$ of finite measure as

$$\varrho(E, F) = m(E \triangle F),$$

where $E \triangle F = (E \setminus F) \cup (F \setminus E)$ is the symmetric difference. The first two axioms for a metric are obviously satisfied, and the triangle inequality holds, since

$$\varrho(E, H) = m(E \triangle H) = m((E \triangle F) \triangle (F \triangle H)) \leq m(E \triangle F) + m(F \triangle H) = \varrho(E, F) + \varrho(F, H).$$

The fourth axiom holds when we identify sets that differ by a nullset. Thereby we obtain a metric space $(\tilde{S}, \varrho)$.
