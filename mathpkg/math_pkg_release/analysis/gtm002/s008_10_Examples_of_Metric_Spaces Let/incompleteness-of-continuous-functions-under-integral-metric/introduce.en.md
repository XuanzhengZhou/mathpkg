The space $C[a,b]$ of continuous functions is not complete when equipped with the integral metric $\sigma(f,g) = \int_a^b |f-g|$. A standard counterexample uses a sequence of piecewise linear functions on $[0,1]$ that approximate a step function: $f_n$ is 1 on $[0, 1/2 - 1/2n]$, 0 on $[1/2 + 1/2n, 1]$, and linear in between. This sequence is Cauchy in the $\sigma$ metric but does not converge to any continuous function, since its pointwise limit would need to be both 1 on $[0, 1/2]$ and 0 on $[1/2, 1]$, which is impossible for a continuous function.
