If $G$ is a dense open subset of $X$, then $G \times Y$ is a dense open subset of $X \times Y$. Hence $A \times B$ is nowhere dense in $X \times Y$ whenever $A$ is nowhere dense in $X$. Since $(\bigcup A_i) \times B = \bigcup (A_i \times B)$, it follows that $A \times B$ is of first category whenever $A$ is of first category. Similar reasoning applies to $B$.

Conversely, if $A \times B$ is of first category and $A$ is not, then by Theorem 15.1 there exists a point $x$ in $A$ such that $(A \times B)_x$ is of first category. Since $(A \times B)_x = B$ for all $x$ in $A$, it follows that $B$ is of first category. $\square$
