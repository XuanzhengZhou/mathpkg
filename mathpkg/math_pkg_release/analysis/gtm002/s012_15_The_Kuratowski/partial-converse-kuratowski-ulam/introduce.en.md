Theorem 15.4 is a partial converse of the Kuratowski-Ulam theorem (Theorem 15.1). While Theorem 15.1 states that a first-category plane set has first-category sections almost everywhere, Theorem 15.4 asserts that if a set $E$ has the property of Baire and almost all of its $x$-sections $E_x$ are of first category, then $E$ itself must be of first category. The additional hypothesis that $E$ has the property of Baire is necessary; the converse does not hold for arbitrary sets. Together, Theorems 15.1 and 15.4 establish that for sets with the Baire property, the category of the whole set and the category of its sections are closely linked.
