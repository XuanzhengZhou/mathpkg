Suppose the contrary. Then $E$ is not of first category. Since $E$ has the property of Baire, $E = G \triangle P$ with $G$ open and $P$ of first category. Since $E$ is not of first category, $G$ must be non-empty. Let $U \times V$ be a non-empty basic open set contained in $G$. For all $x \in U$ except a first-category set, the section $G_x$ contains $V$, hence $G_x$ is not of first category. Since $P_x$ is of first category for all $x$ except a first-category set (by Theorem 15.1), it follows that $E_x = G_x \triangle P_x$ fails to be of first category for all $x$ in a non-first-category subset of $U$. This contradicts the hypothesis that $E_x$ is of first category for all $x$ except a set of first category. $\square$
