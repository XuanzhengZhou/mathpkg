A metric space formalizes the notion of distance between points. It consists of a set equipped with a distance function (metric) satisfying non-negativity, symmetry, the triangle inequality, and the identity of indiscernibles. This abstraction, due to Fréchet, generalizes Euclidean distance and underpins modern analysis and topology.
