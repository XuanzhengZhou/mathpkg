A regular open set is an open set that equals the interior of its own closure — symbolically, $G = G^{-'-'}$. This condition ensures that the set has no "cracks" or missing boundary points that could be filled in. Regular open sets form a complete Boolean algebra under the operations $G \wedge H = G \cap H$, $G \vee H = (G \cup H)^{-'-'}$, and complement $G^\perp = (G')^{-'-'}$. In the context of the Baire property, every set can be represented with a regular open set as the "open part," and this representation is unique (Theorems 4.5 and 4.6).
