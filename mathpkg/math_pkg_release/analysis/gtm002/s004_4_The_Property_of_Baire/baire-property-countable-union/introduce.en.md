Theorem 4.3 proves that the class of sets with the property of Baire is closed under countable unions. The proof is constructive: given a sequence $A_i = G_i \triangle P_i$, the union $A = \bigcup A_i$ satisfies $G \triangle A \subset P$ where $G = \bigcup G_i$ and $P = \bigcup P_i$, showing that $A$ differs from the open set $G$ by a subset of the first-category set $P$. Together with complement-closure (Theorem 4.2), this establishes that the Baire-property sets form the smallest $\sigma$-algebra containing all open sets and all first-category sets.
