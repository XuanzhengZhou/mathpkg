Theorems 4.5 and 4.6 establish that every set with the property of Baire admits a canonical representation using a regular open set, and that this representation is unique. The regular open representative $G^{-'-'}$ (the interior of the closure of any open set $G$ in a Baire-property representation $A = G \triangle P$) serves as the maximal open set that can appear in such a representation. Uniqueness follows from the fact that if $G$ and $H$ are both regular open and $G \triangle H$ is of first category, then the open set $G \triangle H$ must be empty, forcing $G = H$.
