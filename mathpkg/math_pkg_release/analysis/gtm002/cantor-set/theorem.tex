The Cantor set $C$ consists of all numbers in $[0, 1]$ that admit a ternary development in which the digit 1 does not appear. It can be constructed by deleting the open middle third of $[0, 1]$, then deleting the open middle thirds of each remaining interval, and so on. If $F_n$ denotes the union of the $2^n$ closed intervals of length $1/3^n$ remaining at the $n$-th stage, then $C = \bigcap F_n$.
