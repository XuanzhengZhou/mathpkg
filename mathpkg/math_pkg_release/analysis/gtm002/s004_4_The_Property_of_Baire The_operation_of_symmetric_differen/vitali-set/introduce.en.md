A Vitali set is the classical example of a non-measurable subset of the real line, constructed by Giuseppe Vitali in 1905. It is obtained by selecting exactly one representative from each coset of the rational numbers in the additive group of reals, using the axiom of choice. The construction demonstrates that Lebesgue measure cannot be extended to all subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ while remaining translation-invariant and countably additive. The same construction shows that a Vitali set also lacks the property of Baire.
