For any linear set $A$ of second category having the property of Baire, there exists a $\delta > 0$ such that $(-\delta, \delta) \subset A - A$. Likewise, for any measurable set $A$ with $m(A) > 0$, there exists a $\delta > 0$ such that $(-\delta, \delta) \subset A - A$, where $A - A = \{x - y : x, y \in A\}$.
