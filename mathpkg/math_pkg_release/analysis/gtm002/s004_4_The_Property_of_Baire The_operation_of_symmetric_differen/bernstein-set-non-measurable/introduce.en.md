This theorem establishes that Bernstein sets are pathological from the perspectives of both measure and category. Not only are they non-measurable and lack the property of Baire, but they are extreme in both senses: any measurable subset is a nullset and any subset with the Baire property is of first category. This demonstrates that a set can be simultaneously "large" in measure (having full outer measure) yet "small" in category (its complement is residual).
