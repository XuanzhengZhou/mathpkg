This theorem complements Theorem 6.1 by characterizing when player A has a winning strategy. If $A$ contains a subinterval on which it is of second category (i.e., $B$ is of first category there), then A can start by selecting that subinterval and play the "B strategy" against the residual set. The proof reduces to Theorem 6.1 by swapping the roles of the players on the chosen subinterval.
