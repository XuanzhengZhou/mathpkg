This theorem, proved by Stefan Banach (unpublished) in response to Mazur's conjecture, establishes the fundamental connection between the Banach-Mazur game and Baire category. If $A$ is of first category, player B can win by ensuring that the $n$-th stage interval avoids the $n$-th nowhere dense component of $A$. The converse — that if B has a winning strategy then $A$ must be of first category — is the deep direction, proved by constructing a countable union of nowhere dense sets from the strategy functions.
