Theorem 19.3 is an immediate consequence of Theorem 19.4 (the set-theoretic lemma on similar $\sigma$-ideals). Both the $\sigma$-ideal $K$ of nullsets and the $\sigma$-ideal $L$ of sets of first category on the line satisfy the four conditions (a)-(d) of Theorem 19.5 under the continuum hypothesis:

(a) Both are $\sigma$-ideals.
(b) The union of each is $\mathbb{R}$.
(c) Each has a generating subclass of power $\leq \aleph_1$: the class of $G_\delta$ nullsets for $K$, and the class of $F_\sigma$ first category sets for $L$.
(d) The complement of each member contains a set of power $\aleph_1$ in the same class, by Theorem 19.1.

Applying Theorem 19.4 to $K$ and $L$ yields a bijection $f$ with $f = f^{-1}$ such that $f(E) \in L$ iff $E \in K$, which is precisely the statement of Theorem 19.3.
