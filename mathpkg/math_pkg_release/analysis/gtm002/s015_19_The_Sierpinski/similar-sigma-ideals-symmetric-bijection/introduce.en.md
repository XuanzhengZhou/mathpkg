Theorem 19.4 bridges the purely set-theoretic decomposition theorem (Theorem 19.5) to the measure-category duality theorems. It states that if two $\sigma$-ideals $K$ and $L$ on a set $X$ of power $\aleph_1$ each satisfy the four conditions of Theorem 19.5, then there exists a symmetric bijection $f: X \to X$ (with $f = f^{-1}$) that transports membership: $f(E) \in L$ exactly when $E \in K$. The proof uses the decompositions of $X$ provided by Theorem 19.5 for both $K$ and $L$, and pieces together a bijection from the isomorphisms between corresponding decomposition sets of power $\aleph_1$.
