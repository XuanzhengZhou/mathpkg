Sierpinski proved in 1934 that, assuming the continuum hypothesis, the $\sigma$-ideal of Lebesgue nullsets and the $\sigma$-ideal of sets of first category on the real line are similar: there exists a bijection $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ such that a set $E$ is null exactly when its image $f(E)$ is of first category. This theorem explains many of the observed parallels between measure and category by justifying a formal duality principle. It is not known whether the theorem can be proved without the continuum hypothesis.
