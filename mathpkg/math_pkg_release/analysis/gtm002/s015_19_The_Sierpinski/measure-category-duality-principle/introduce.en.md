The measure-category duality principle follows from Sierpinski's theorem (Theorem 19.2). It states that any purely set-theoretic proposition about nullsets has an equivalent dual proposition about sets of first category, and vice versa, provided the continuum hypothesis is assumed. Pure set-theoretic notions include cardinality, disjointness, and any property invariant under bijections. This principle formalizes the stereoscopic approach of simultaneously studying measure and category to illuminate both theories.
