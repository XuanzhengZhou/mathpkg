A metric space is topologically complete if it is homeomorphic to a complete metric space. This is a weaker condition than metric completeness: a space may be incomplete under one metric but topologically complete if a topologically equivalent complete metric exists. The importance of topological completeness lies in the fact that the Baire Category Theorem extends to all topologically complete metric spaces.
