A subset $A$ of a topological space $X$ is dense if its closure equals the whole space. Equivalently, every nonempty open set intersects $A$. Dense sets are fundamental in approximation theory and in the study of Baire category: a set is "large" in the topological sense if it contains a dense $G_\delta$.
