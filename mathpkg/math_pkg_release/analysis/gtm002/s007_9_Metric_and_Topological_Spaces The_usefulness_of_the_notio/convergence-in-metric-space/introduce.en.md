Convergence of a sequence in a metric space is defined by the condition that the distance between the sequence terms and the limit tends to zero. Crucially, convergence is a topological property: it can be equivalently expressed in terms of open sets alone, without reference to the metric. A sequence converges to $x$ precisely when every open neighborhood of $x$ contains all but finitely many terms.
