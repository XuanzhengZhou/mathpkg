A topological space consists of a set $X$ together with a topology $\mathcal{T}$, a collection of subsets called open sets satisfying three axioms: the empty set and $X$ are open, arbitrary unions of open sets are open, and finite intersections of open sets are open. The metric is regarded as auxiliary; any property definable in terms of open sets alone is a topological property.
