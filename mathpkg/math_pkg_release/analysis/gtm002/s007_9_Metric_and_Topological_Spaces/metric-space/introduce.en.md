A metric space is a set equipped with a distance function satisfying positivity, symmetry, the triangle inequality, and the identity of indiscernibles. This notion, due to Fréchet, abstracts the properties of Euclidean distance to arbitrary sets. Many theorems in analysis become simpler and more intuitive when formulated in terms of a suitable metric.
