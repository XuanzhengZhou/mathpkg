A Baire space is a topological space in which the Baire category theorem holds: every non-empty open set is of second category, equivalently the intersection of countably many dense open sets is dense. The definition in the source text is truncated by the page boundary, but the standard formulation is that the complement of any first-category set is dense. Complete metric spaces and locally compact Hausdorff spaces are Baire spaces.
