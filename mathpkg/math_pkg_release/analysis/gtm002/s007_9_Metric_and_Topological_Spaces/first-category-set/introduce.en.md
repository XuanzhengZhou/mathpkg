A set is of first category (also called meager) if it can be expressed as a countable union of nowhere dense sets. First category sets are the "small" or "thin" sets in the sense of Baire category, analogous to null sets in measure theory. The complement of a first-category set in a complete metric space is dense, by the Baire category theorem.
