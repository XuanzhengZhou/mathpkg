Theorem 21.1 restates a fundamental duality between measure and category: a linear set all of whose subsets are Lebesgue measurable must itself be a nullset; dually, a linear set all of whose subsets possess the property of Baire must itself be of first category. This result (originally Theorem 5.5) serves as the prototypical example of the analogy between measurability and the Baire property, motivating the question of whether a full "extended principle of duality" can be established between the two notions.
