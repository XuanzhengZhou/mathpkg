The Kolmogorov zero-one law states that any measurable tail set in a product probability space has either measure zero or measure one. In the concrete setting of the Cantor space $X = \{0,1\}^{\mathbb{N}}$ with product measure, the result follows from the fact that tail sets are independent of every cylinder set, so $E$ is independent of itself and therefore $\mu(E) = \mu(E \cap E) = \mu(E)^2$, forcing $\mu(E) \in \{0,1\}$. This theorem serves as the measure-theoretic counterpart to Theorem 21.4.
