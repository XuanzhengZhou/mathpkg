Theorem 21.4 is the category-theoretic analogue of the Kolmogorov zero-one law: any tail set in the Cantor space $X = \{0,1\}^{\mathbb{N}}$ that possesses the property of Baire must be either of first category or residual (i.e., its complement is first category). The proof uses the representation of a non-residual Baire set as $G \triangle P$ with $G$ open and nonempty, the product structure of basic open sets, and the Kuratowski-Ulam theorem (Theorem 15.3) to propagate the category information through the product factors. This result holds more generally for arbitrary products of Baire spaces with countable bases.
