Alexandroff's theorem characterizes precisely when a subspace of a complete metric space can be remetrized to itself be complete. The answer is that any non-empty $G_\delta$ subset (a countable intersection of open sets) of a complete metric space is topologically complete. This result is fundamental in descriptive set theory and Baire category theory, as it shows that $G_\delta$ sets inherit a complete metric structure, making the Baire category theorem applicable to them.
