Given a lower density $\phi$ on a complete finite measure space, one can construct a topology on $X$ consisting of all sets of the form $\phi(A)$ with a nullset removed. This is the density topology, which ties the measure-theoretic structure to a topological framework. Under this topology, the notions of "nullset" and "nowhere dense set" coincide.
