Proposition 20.2 asserts the existence of a one-to-one mapping from the real line onto a subset of itself that sends every uncountable set to a set of second category. The proof is remarkably simple: take $f$ to be any one-to-one mapping of the line onto a Lusin set. Since a Lusin set has the property that every uncountable subset is of second category, the image of any uncountable set under such a bijection must also be of second category.
