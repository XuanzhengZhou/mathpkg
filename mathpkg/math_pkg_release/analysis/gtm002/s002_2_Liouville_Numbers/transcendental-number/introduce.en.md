A transcendental number is a real number that is not algebraic---it is not the root of any non-zero polynomial with integer coefficients. The existence of transcendental numbers was first proved by Liouville in 1844 via an explicit construction (Liouville numbers). Cantor later gave a simpler non-constructive proof using cardinality: the algebraic numbers are countable while the reals are uncountable, so "most" real numbers are transcendental.
