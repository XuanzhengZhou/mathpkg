The weight of a polynomial with integer coefficients is the sum of its degree and the absolute values of all its coefficients. This combinatorial device ensures that only finitely many integer-coefficient polynomials share any given weight. It is used in the standard proof that the set of algebraic numbers is countable: enumerate all polynomials by increasing weight, then list their roots.
