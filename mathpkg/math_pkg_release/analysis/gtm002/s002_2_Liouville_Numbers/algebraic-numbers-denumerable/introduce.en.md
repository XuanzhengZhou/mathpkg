Theorem 2.1 establishes that the set of all real algebraic numbers is denumerable (countably infinite). Since Cantor had already shown the real numbers are uncountable, this immediately implies that transcendental numbers exist---indeed, "most" real numbers are transcendental in the sense of cardinality. The proof uses the weight of a polynomial as a combinatorial device to enumerate all integer-coefficient polynomials, then enumerates their roots.
