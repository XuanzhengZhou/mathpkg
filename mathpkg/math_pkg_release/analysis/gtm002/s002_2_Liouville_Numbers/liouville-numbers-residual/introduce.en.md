This proposition establishes that the set $E$ of Liouville numbers is residual---its complement $E'$ is a countable union of nowhere dense sets, i.e., of first category. The proof writes $E'$ as the union of the rationals $Q$ and the complements of dense open sets $G_n$. Since each $G_n$ is a union of rational-centered intervals covering all rationals, $G_n$ is dense and open; thus $G_n'$ is nowhere dense. Baire's theorem then guarantees that Liouville numbers exist in every interval, making them the "typical" case in the sense of category.
