The integral metric, also known as the $L^1$ metric, measures the area between the graphs of two continuous functions on a closed interval. Unlike the uniform metric, convergence in this metric does not imply pointwise convergence everywhere. This metric provides a fundamentally different topology on $C[a, b]$, leading to different completeness properties.
