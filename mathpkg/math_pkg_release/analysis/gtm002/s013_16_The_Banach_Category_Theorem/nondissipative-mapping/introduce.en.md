A nondissipative mapping is one for which every wandering set is negligible (belongs to the $\sigma$-ideal $\mathcal{I}$). Nondissipativity is the abstract condition that guarantees the recurrence property, as established in Theorem 17.2. In the measure-theoretic context with a finite measure, any measurable transformation is automatically nondissipative.
