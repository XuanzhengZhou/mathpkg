Theorem 17.3 is the ultimate synthesis: an abstract Poincare recurrence theorem that simultaneously yields both measure-zero and first-category exceptional sets. By applying Theorem 17.2 to both the measure-theoretic $\sigma$-ideal (nullsets) and the category $\sigma$-ideal (sets of first category), one obtains that the set of nonrecurrent points is both a nullset and meager. This theorem has profound significance for dynamical systems, implying that for almost all initial states in classical mechanical systems, the trajectory returns arbitrarily close to the starting point infinitely often.
