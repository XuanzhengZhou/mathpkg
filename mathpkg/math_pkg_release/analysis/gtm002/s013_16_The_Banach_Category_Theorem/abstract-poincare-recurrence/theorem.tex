Let $T$ be a homeomorphism of a metric space $X$ onto itself, with the property that there is no non-empty open wandering set, and let $X$ be equipped with a nonatomic finite Borel measure $\mu$ such that every set of infinite measure has a subset with positive finite measure. Then every point of $X$ is recurrent under $T$ except a set that is both of measure zero and of first category.

More precisely, there exists a set $E \subset X$ which is a nullset of first category such that for every $x \in X - E$ and every neighborhood $U$ of $x$, $T^i x \in U$ for infinitely many positive integers $i$.
