The Poincare Recurrence Theorem, discovered during Poincare's studies in celestial mechanics, states that for a volume-preserving homeomorphism on a bounded region, almost every point returns infinitely often to any neighborhood it visits. Poincare proved both a category version (recurrent points form a dense $G_\delta$) and a measure version (non-recurrent points have measure zero), anticipating the formal notions of both measure and category. This theorem initiated the modern study of ergodic theory.
