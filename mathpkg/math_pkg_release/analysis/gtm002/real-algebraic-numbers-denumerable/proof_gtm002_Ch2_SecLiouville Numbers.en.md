Define the weight of a polynomial (x) = \sum_0^n a_i x^i$ to be  + \sum_0^n |a_i|$. There are only finitely many polynomials of a given weight. Arrange these lexicographically. Taking polynomials of weight 2 in order, then weight 3, etc., we obtain a sequence , f_2, \ldots$ in which every non-constant polynomial appears once. Each polynomial has at most finitely many real zeros. Numbering the zeros of each polynomial in order (omitting repetitions) gives an enumeration of all real algebraic numbers.
