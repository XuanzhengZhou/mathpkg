Theorem 3.18 provides an equivalent characterization of measurability for sets of finite outer measure: a set $A$ is measurable if and only if its outer measure can be approximated from below by the measures of bounded closed subsets. In other words, $m^*(A)$ equals the supremum of $m(F)$ over all bounded closed sets $F \subset A$. This property, known as inner regularity, shows that Lebesgue measure is determined by its behavior on compact sets.
