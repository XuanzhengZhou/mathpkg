These abstract set-theoretic structures form the foundation of measure theory. A ring is closed under finite unions and differences; a $\sigma$-ring adds closure under countable unions. An algebra is a ring containing the whole space $X$ (equivalently, closed under complementation), and a $\sigma$-algebra is an algebra closed under countable unions. The class of measurable sets forms a $\sigma$-algebra, which justifies the use of countable operations in measure-theoretic arguments.
