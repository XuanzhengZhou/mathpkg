An interval in $r$-dimensional Euclidean space generalizes the notion of a rectangle to higher dimensions. It is defined as the Cartesian product of $r$ one-dimensional intervals, yielding a rectangular parallelepiped with edges aligned to the coordinate axes. The $r$-dimensional volume $|I|$ of such an interval is simply the product of the side lengths. Intervals serve as the elementary geometric objects from which Lebesgue measure in $r$-space is constructed by extension.
