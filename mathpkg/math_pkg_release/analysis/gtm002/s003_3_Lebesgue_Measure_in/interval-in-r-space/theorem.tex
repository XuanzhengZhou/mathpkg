An \textit{interval} $I$ in Euclidean $r$-space ($r = 1, 2, \ldots$) is a rectangular parallelepiped with edges parallel to the axes. It is the Cartesian product of $r$ one-dimensional intervals:

$$
I = I_1 \times I_2 \times \cdots \times I_r.
$$

The $r$-dimensional volume of $I$ is denoted by $|I|$ and equals the product of the lengths of the $r$ one-dimensional intervals.
