Theorem 3.19 establishes that Lebesgue measure is invariant under translations of $\mathbb{R}^r$: shifting a measurable set by a fixed vector preserves both its measurability and its measure. This follows directly from the definition of outer measure (since congruent intervals have equal volume) and the definition of measurability. Measurability and measure are also preserved by rotations and reflections, though this is not proved here.
