Kuratowski's remetrization lemma gives a sufficient condition for a metric space to admit an equivalent complete metric. The condition involves the existence of a countable family of continuous real-valued functions that collectively detect convergence: a Cauchy sequence converges if and only if its image under each function is bounded. The lemma provides an explicit construction of the new complete metric as a weighted sum of the original metric and the differences of the auxiliary functions. It serves as the key technical tool in proving Alexandroff's theorem on the topological completeness of $G_{\delta}$ sets.
