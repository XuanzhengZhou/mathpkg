Theorem 6.2 gives the condition under which the first player has a winning strategy in the Banach-Mazur game: player A can force a win exactly when there exists an interval in which the opponent's set $B$ is of first category. The proof elegantly reduces this situation to Theorem 6.1 by a change of perspective.
