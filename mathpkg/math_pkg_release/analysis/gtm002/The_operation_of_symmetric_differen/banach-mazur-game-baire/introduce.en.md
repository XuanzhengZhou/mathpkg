Theorem 6.3 establishes that the Banach-Mazur game is determined whenever the target set $A$ has the property of Baire: the winner is determined purely by whether $A$ is of first or second category. Combined with the existence of Bernstein sets (which lack the property of Baire), this shows the game is not always determined, making it particularly interesting for the general theory of games.
