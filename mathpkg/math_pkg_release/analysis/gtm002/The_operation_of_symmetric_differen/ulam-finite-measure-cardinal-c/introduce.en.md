Proposition 5.7 extends Ulam's theorem to sets of cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ (the power of the continuum), under the assumption that no cardinal less than or equal to $\mathfrak{c}$ is weakly inaccessible. In particular, if the continuum hypothesis ($\mathfrak{c} = \aleph_1$) holds, Theorem 5.6 directly implies this result. This proposition has deep implications for the foundations of measure theory.
