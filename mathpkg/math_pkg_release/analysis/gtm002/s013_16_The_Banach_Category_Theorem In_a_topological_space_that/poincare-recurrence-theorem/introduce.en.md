The Poincaré Recurrence Theorem states that for a volume-preserving homeomorphism of a bounded region, almost every point in any open set returns to that set infinitely often. "Almost every" here means both in the sense of measure (Lebesgue measure zero exceptions) and in the sense of category (first category exceptions). This theorem, originating from Poincaré's studies in celestial mechanics, initiated the modern study of measure-preserving transformations known as ergodic theory.
