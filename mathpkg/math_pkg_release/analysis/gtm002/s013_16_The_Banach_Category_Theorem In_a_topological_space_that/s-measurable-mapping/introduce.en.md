An $\mathcal{S}$-measurable mapping is a transformation $T: X \to X$ that pulls back sets in a $\sigma$-ring $\mathcal{S}$ to sets in $\mathcal{S}$. This notion generalizes the classical definition of measurable functions and is used in the abstract formulation of the Poincaré recurrence theorem (Theorem 17.2) to describe transformations that respect a given $\sigma$-ideal structure on the space.
