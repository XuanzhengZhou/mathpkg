A transcendental number is a real number that is not algebraic -- it cannot be expressed as the root of any non-zero polynomial with integer coefficients. The first proof of their existence was given by Liouville in 1844, who constructed explicit examples (Liouville numbers) via rapid rational approximation. Famous transcendental numbers include $\pi$ and $e$. In the sense of Baire category, almost all real numbers are transcendental.
