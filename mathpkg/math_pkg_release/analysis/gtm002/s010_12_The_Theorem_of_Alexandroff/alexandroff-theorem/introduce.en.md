The Alexandroff Theorem characterizes when a subspace of a complete metric space can be remetrized to become complete itself. Any closed subset of a complete metric space is automatically complete under the same metric, but the theorem shows that every $G_{\delta}$ subset (a countable intersection of open sets) is also topologically complete. The proof relies on a lemma of Kuratowski that provides a sufficient condition for remetrization using a sequence of real-valued continuous functions.
