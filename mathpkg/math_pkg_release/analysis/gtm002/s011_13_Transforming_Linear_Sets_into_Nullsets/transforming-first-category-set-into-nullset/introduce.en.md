Theorem 13.1 of Oxtoby's "Measure and Category" establishes a deep connection between the two fundamental $\sigma$-ideals on the real line: sets of first category (meager sets) and nullsets (sets of Lebesgue measure zero). Specifically, every set $A$ of first category in $[0,1]$ can be transformed into a nullset by some homeomorphism of $[0,1]$ onto itself. Moreover, the set of such homeomorphisms is residual (comeager) in the space $H$ of all automorphisms of $[0,1]$ fixing the endpoints. The proof is a delicate category argument: one shows that for each nowhere dense component $A_n$ of $A$, the set $E_{n,k} = \{h \in H : m(h(\bar{A}_n)) < 1/k\}$ is both open and dense in $H$, making the countable intersection a residual subset, and for any $h$ in this intersection, $h(A)$ has measure zero.
