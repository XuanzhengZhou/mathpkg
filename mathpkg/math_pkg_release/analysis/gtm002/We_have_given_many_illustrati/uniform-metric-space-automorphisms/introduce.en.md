The space $H$ of all automorphisms (homeomorphisms) of the closed unit square $X$ is metrized by the uniform metric $\varrho(S,T) = \sup_{x \in X} |Sx - Tx|$, which measures the maximum distance between the images of any point under two transformations. This space is topologically complete, a fact that follows by the same reasoning as in the one-dimensional case discussed in Chapter 13. The uniform metric topology on $H$ provides the natural setting for category arguments concerning the existence of automorphisms with special dynamical properties, such as transitivity.
