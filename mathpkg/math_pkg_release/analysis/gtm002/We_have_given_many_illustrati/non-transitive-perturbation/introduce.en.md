This lemma provides a key perturbation argument: near any transitive automorphism $T$ of the unit square, one can construct a non-transitive automorphism by composing $T$ with a suitable radial contraction $S$. The construction selects a disk $D$ centered at a point $x$ with dense positive semiorbit, such that some iterate $T^n$ maps $D$ back near $x$. By contracting $T^n(D)$ radially into the interior of $D$, the composition $S \circ T$ has $(S \circ T)^n(D) \subsetneq D$, which prevents transitivity. Since this perturbation can be made arbitrarily small in the uniform metric, transitive automorphisms do not form an open set in $H$.
