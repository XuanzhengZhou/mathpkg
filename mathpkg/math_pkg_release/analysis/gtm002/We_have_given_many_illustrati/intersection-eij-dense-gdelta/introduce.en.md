The sets $E_{ij}$ capture the property that some point of the basic open set $U_i$ eventually visits $U_j$ under forward iteration. Each $E_{ij}$ is open (since it only requires the existence of some finite $k$ such that $U_i \cap T^{-k} U_j \neq \emptyset$, and this condition is stable under small perturbations of $T$) and dense in $M$. By the Baire category theorem, their countable intersection $\bigcap_{i,j} E_{ij}$ is a dense $G_\delta$. This intersection is the central object in Oxtoby's category-method proof that transitive measure-preserving automorphisms of the unit square exist.
