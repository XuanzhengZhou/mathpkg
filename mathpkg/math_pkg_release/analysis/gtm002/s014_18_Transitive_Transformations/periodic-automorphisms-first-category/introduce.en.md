In the space of all measure-preserving automorphisms of the unit square, those for which every point is periodic form only a set of first category. The proof constructs, for each candidate periodic automorphism, an arbitrarily small perturbation that introduces a non-periodic point by rotating an annulus through an irrational angle. Thus periodic automorphisms are topologically negligible, and generically measure-preserving automorphisms possess non-periodic points.
