The culminating result of Chapter 18 establishes the existence of measure-preserving automorphisms of the unit square that are transitive. The proof uses the Baire category theorem twice: first to show the set of all such transformations is non-empty (as a countable intersection of dense open sets in $M$), and then to ensure that for any transformation in this intersection, the forward orbit of some point is dense. This result is historically significant as among the first existence proofs in dynamics obtained purely by category methods.
