A transitive automorphism is a homeomorphism of a topological space onto itself that possesses a dense orbit. This concept is fundamental in topological dynamics, capturing the idea that a single point can "visit" every region of the space under iterates of the transformation. The existence of transitive automorphisms on various spaces, including the closed unit square, was among the first problems solved by the Baire category method where no constructive example was previously known.
