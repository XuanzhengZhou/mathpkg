Lusin's theorem (Theorem 8.2) provides a topological characterization of Lebesgue-measurable functions: a function is measurable precisely when it can be made continuous by removing a set of arbitrarily small measure. This fundamental result bridges the gap between the measure-theoretic notion of measurability and the topological notion of continuity. The theorem's category-theoretic analogue is Theorem 8.1, which characterizes the Baire property via continuity off a first-category set.
