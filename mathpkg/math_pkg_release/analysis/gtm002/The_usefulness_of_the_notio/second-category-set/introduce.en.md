A set is of second category (nonmeager) if it is not of first category, i.e., it cannot be written as a countable union of nowhere dense sets. In a complete metric space, the whole space is of second category in itself (Baire category theorem). The distinction between first and second category provides a topological classification of "small" versus "large" sets.
