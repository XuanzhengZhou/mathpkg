A metric space is a set equipped with a distance function satisfying positivity, symmetry, the triangle inequality, and identity of indiscernibles. This notion, due to Fr\'{e}chet, abstracts the properties of distance in Euclidean spaces of any dimension. Many theorems in analysis become simpler and more intuitive when formulated in terms of a suitable metric.
