The Baire Category Theorem states that in a topologically complete metric space, the complement of any first category (meager) set is dense. Equivalently, the intersection of countably many dense open sets is dense. This theorem is one of the most powerful tools in analysis and topology, underlying the Open Mapping Theorem, the Closed Graph Theorem, and the Uniform Boundedness Principle.
