Proposition 20.1*, proved by Waclaw Sierpinski in 1924, is the dual of Lusin's Proposition 20.1 under the duality principle between measure and category. It states that any linear set of positive outer measure contains a subset of cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ such that every uncountable subset of it also has positive outer measure. This is the measure-theoretic analogue of the Lusin set construction. The proposition depends on the continuum hypothesis, and its proof follows by interchanging the roles of "measure zero" and "first category" in the argument for Proposition 20.1.
