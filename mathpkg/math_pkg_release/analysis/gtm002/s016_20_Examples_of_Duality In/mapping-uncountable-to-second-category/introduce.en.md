Proposition 20.2 asserts the existence of a bijection from the real line onto a subset of itself that sends every uncountable set to a set of second category. The proof is immediate: take $f$ to be any one-to-one mapping of the line onto a Lusin set. Since every uncountable subset of a Lusin set is of second category, the image of any uncountable $E$ under $f$ is of second category. This proposition illustrates how the Lusin set construction can be used to produce mappings with exotic measure-category behavior.
