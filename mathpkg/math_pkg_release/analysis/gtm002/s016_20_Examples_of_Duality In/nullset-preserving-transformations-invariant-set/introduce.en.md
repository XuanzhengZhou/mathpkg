Proposition 20.4, a generalization of a result by Sierpinski, shows that for any family $K$ of $\mathfrak{c}$ one-to-one nullset-preserving transformations of the real line, there exists a set $E$ of first category and cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ that is almost invariant under every $T \in K$, meaning $TE \triangle E$ is at most countable. The proof constructs $E$ by a transfinite induction of length $\Omega$, building the group generated by the transformations at each stage and ensuring their orbits over a fixed nullset $A$ avoid the elements selected for $E$.
