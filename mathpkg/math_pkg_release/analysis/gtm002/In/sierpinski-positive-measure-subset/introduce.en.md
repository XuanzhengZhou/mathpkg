Proposition 20.1*, proved by Sierpinski in 1924, is the measure-theoretic dual of Lusin's Proposition 20.1. It states that any linear set of positive outer measure contains a subset of cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ all of whose uncountable subsets also have positive outer measure. The set $N$ obtained must be of first category, illustrating the duality between measure and category: distinguishing measure properties of subsets reduces to the distinction between countable and uncountable.
