Proposition 20.1, due to Lusin (1914), states that any linear set of second category contains a subset of cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ whose every uncountable subset is also of second category — a so-called Lusin set. This proposition depends on the continuum hypothesis and is the category-side counterpart in Sierpinski's duality principle. Its measure-theoretic dual (Proposition 20.1*) replaces "second category" with "positive outer measure."
