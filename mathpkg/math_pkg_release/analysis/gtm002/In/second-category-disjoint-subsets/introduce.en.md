Proposition 20.3 states that any linear set of second category can be decomposed into continuum-many disjoint subsets, each of second category. The proof uses a one-to-one mapping of the plane onto a Lusin set contained in $E$, exploiting the fact that $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{R}^2$ have the same cardinality. This proposition depends on the continuum hypothesis (via the existence of Lusin sets) and is dual to Proposition 20.3* concerning positive outer measure.
