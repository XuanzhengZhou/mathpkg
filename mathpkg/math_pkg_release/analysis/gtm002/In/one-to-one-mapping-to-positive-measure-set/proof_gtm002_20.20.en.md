This proposition is the measure-theoretic dual of Proposition 20.2. By Proposition 20.1*, any linear set of positive outer measure contains a subset $N$ of cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ such that every uncountable subset of $N$ has positive outer measure. Let $f$ be any one-to-one mapping of $\mathbb{R}$ onto such a set $N$. If $E \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ is uncountable, then $f(E)$ is an uncountable subset of $N$, hence has positive outer measure. The proposition follows from the duality principle; no direct proof is given in the section text.
