Proposition 20.4, a generalization of a proposition of Sierpinski, states that for any class of cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ consisting of one-to-one nullset-preserving transformations of the real line, there exists a set of first category with cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ whose symmetric difference with its image under any transformation in the class is at most countable. The proof constructs such a set by transfinite induction, building a group generated by the transformations at each stage and taking the orbit of a carefully chosen nullset.
