A $\sigma$-ideal is a collection of sets with two closure properties: it contains every subset of any of its members, and the union of any countable subcollection is again in the collection. The class of first category sets, the class of nullsets (measure zero sets), and the class of countable sets are three important examples of $\sigma$-ideals on the real line. This shared algebraic structure underpins the duality between measure and category explored throughout the book.
