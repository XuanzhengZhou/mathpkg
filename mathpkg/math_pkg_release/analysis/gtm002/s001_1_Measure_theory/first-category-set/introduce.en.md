A set of first category, also called a meager set, is a countable union of nowhere dense sets. This concept, formulated by René Baire in 1899, is fundamental to the Baire category method for proving existence. The class of first category sets forms a $\sigma$-ideal (closed under subsets and countable unions). The rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ are of first category (being a countable union of singletons), while no interval in $\mathbb{R}$ is of first category.
