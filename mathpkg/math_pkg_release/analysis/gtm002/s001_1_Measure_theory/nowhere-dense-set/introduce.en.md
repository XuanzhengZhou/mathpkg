A nowhere dense set is one that is "full of holes" — it fails to be dense in any non-degenerate interval. This topological notion is the atomic ingredient in the definition of first category sets (meager sets). The concept admits two equivalent characterizations: the complement contains a dense open set, and the closure has empty interior. Singleton sets $\{x\}$ are nowhere dense in $\mathbb{R}$, and finite unions of nowhere dense sets remain nowhere dense.
