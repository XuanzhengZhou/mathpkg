A set of second category is one that is not of first category — that is, it cannot be expressed as a countable union of nowhere dense sets. In the sense of Baire category, second category sets are the "large" sets. Baire's category theorem asserts that every nonempty open interval in $\mathbb{R}$ is of second category, and more generally, every complete metric space is of second category in itself.
