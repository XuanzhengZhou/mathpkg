The oscillation of a function on an interval measures the maximum variation of the function's values over that interval. It is defined as the difference between the supremum and infimum of $f$ on $I$. This quantity is fundamental for studying continuity: a function is Riemann integrable precisely when its oscillation can be controlled on sufficiently fine partitions. The oscillation on intervals provides a bridge between pointwise behavior and global integrability properties.
