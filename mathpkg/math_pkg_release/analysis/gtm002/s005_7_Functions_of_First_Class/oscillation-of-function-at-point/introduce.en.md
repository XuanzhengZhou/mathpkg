The oscillation at a point, denoted $\omega(x)$, is the limit of the oscillation of $f$ on shrinking intervals centered at $x$. It serves as a quantitative measure of the discontinuity at $x$: $\omega(x_0) = 0$ if and only if $f$ is continuous at $x_0$, and when nonzero, $\omega(x_0)$ captures the magnitude of the jump or irregularity. The function $\omega(x)$ is upper semicontinuous, which makes the set $\{x : \omega(x) < \varepsilon\}$ open — a key observation leading to Theorem 7.1.
