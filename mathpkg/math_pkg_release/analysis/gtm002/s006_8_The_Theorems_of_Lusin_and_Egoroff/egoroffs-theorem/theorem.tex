Let $(f_n)$ be a sequence of measurable real-valued functions that converges to a function $f$ almost everywhere on a set $E$ of finite Lebesgue measure. Then for each $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists a measurable set $F \subset E$ with $m(E \setminus F) < \varepsilon$ such that $f_n \to f$ uniformly on $F$.
