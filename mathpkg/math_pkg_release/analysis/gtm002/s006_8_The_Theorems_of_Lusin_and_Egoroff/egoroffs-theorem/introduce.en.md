Egoroff's theorem is a fundamental result linking pointwise almost-everywhere convergence to uniform convergence in measure theory. It states that on a set of finite measure, almost-everywhere convergence of a sequence of measurable functions can be upgraded to uniform convergence after removing a subset of arbitrarily small measure. This theorem highlights the essential "near-uniformity" of pointwise limits and is a key tool in the theory of Lebesgue integration. Notably, the category analogue of Egoroff's theorem is false, as shown by a counterexample in the same section of Oxtoby's text.
