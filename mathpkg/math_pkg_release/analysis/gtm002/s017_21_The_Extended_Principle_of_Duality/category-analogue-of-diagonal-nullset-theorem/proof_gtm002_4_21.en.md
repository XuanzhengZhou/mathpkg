This proposition is false. Let $r_i$ be an enumeration of all rational points, and define $E_{i,j} = \{r_i\}$ for all $j$. Each $E_{i,j}$ is a singleton and hence has the property of Baire. The sets are nested downward (as a constant sequence), and $\bigcap_j E_{i,j} = \{r_i\}$ is a singleton, hence of first category. However, for any choice of mappings $n_k(i)$, the set $\bigcap_k \bigcup_i E_{i,n_k(i)}$ is simply $\bigcup_i \{r_i\}$ (since the $E_{i,j}$ are independent of $j$), which is the set of all rational points. A countable dense set is not of first category in $\mathbb{R}$ (it is of second category). Thus the conclusion fails.
