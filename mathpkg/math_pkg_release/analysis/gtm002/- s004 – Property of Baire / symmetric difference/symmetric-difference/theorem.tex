The operation of symmetric difference is defined by
$$A \triangle B = (A \cup B) - (A \cap B) = (A - B) \cup (B - A).$$
It is commutative, associative, and satisfies the distributive law
$$A \cap (B \triangle C) = (A \cap B) \triangle (A \cap C).$$
Evidently, $A \triangle B \subset A \cup B$ and $A \triangle A = \emptyset$. Any class of sets that is closed under $\triangle$ and $\cap$ is a commutative ring (in the algebraic sense) when these operations are taken to define addition and multiplication, respectively. Such a class is also closed under the operations of union and difference.
