The symmetric difference $A \triangle B$ of two sets is the set of elements belonging to exactly one of $A$ or $B$, i.e., their union minus their intersection. It is commutative, associative, and together with intersection forms a Boolean ring structure on any family of sets closed under both operations. This algebraic perspective on set operations is fundamental to the study of the property of Baire and the class of measurable sets.
