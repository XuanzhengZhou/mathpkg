Theorem 4.1 shows that the definition of the property of Baire — being the symmetric difference of an open set and a first-category set — is equivalent to being the symmetric difference of a closed set and a first-category set. The proof uses the fact that the boundary of an open set $G$, namely $N = \overline{G} - G$, is nowhere dense, so the roles of open and closed sets are symmetric under the Baire property.
