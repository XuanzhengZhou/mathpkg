If $A$ is closed and $x \notin A$, $x$ can be strongly separated from $A$ by a closed hyperplane $[\phi; \alpha]$ with $\phi \in X^*$. Thus $\phi(x) > \sup \phi(A)$, so no net in $A$ can converge weakly to $x$. Hence $x$ is not in the weak closure of $A$.
