Using Stone's Lemma, enlarge $A$ and $B$ to complementary convex sets $C$ and $D$. The set $M = \operatorname{lin}(C) \cap \operatorname{lin}(D)$ is shown to be an affine subspace. If $M \neq X$, find $p \in X \setminus M$ and construct a non-zero linear functional $\phi$ on the span of $M$ and $p$ such that $\phi \leqslant \alpha$ on $C$ and $\phi \geqslant \alpha$ on $D$. Extend $\phi$ to all of $X$.
