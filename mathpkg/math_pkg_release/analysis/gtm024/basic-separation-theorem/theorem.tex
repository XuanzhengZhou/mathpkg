Let $A$ and $B$ be disjoint convex subsets of a real linear space $X$. If $\operatorname{cor}(A) \neq \emptyset$, then $A$ and $B$ can be separated by a hyperplane $[\phi; \alpha]$: there exist $\phi \in X'$, $\phi \neq \theta$, and $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $\phi(a) \leqslant \alpha \leqslant \phi(b)$ for all $a \in A$, $b \in B$.
