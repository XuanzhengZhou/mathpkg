Since $L^1$ is separable, $L^\infty$ is congruent to a subspace of $m$ by 23D. Since $L^\infty$ is injective, it is complemented in $m$, so $m \sim X \times L^\infty$ for some $X$. Using $L^\infty \sim L^\infty \times L^\infty$ and $m \sim m \times m$, algebraic manipulations yield $m \sim L^\infty$.
