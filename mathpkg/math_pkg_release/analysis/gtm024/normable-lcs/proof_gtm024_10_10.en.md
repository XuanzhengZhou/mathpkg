If the topology is defined by a norm $\rho$, the $\rho$-unit ball is a proper bounded $\theta$-neighborhood. Conversely, if such a neighborhood exists, it contains a barrel $B$. Its gauge $\rho_B$ must be a norm since $\rho_B(x) = 0$ implies $x$ lies in all scalar multiples of $B$, hence $x = \theta$ by boundedness.
