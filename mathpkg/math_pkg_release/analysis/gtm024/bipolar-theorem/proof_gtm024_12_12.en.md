It is clear that $\{\theta\} \cup A$ lies in the bipolar. Since the bipolar is convex and weakly closed, it contains the closed convex hull $\overline{\operatorname{co}}(\{\theta\} \cup A)$. Conversely, any closed half-space containing $\{\theta\} \cup A$ must also contain the bipolar, giving the reverse inclusion.
