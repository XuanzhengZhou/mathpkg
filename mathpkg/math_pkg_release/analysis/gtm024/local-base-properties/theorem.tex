Let $\mathcal{U}$ be a local base in an LTS $X$. Then: a) Every $U \in \mathcal{U}$ is absorbing. b) If $U \in \mathcal{U}$, there exists a balanced $\theta$-neighborhood $V$ such that $V + V \subset U$. c) $\bar{A} = \bigcap\{A + U : U \in \mathcal{U}\}$. d) The topology is Hausdorff iff $\bigcap\{U : U \in \mathcal{U}\} = \{\theta\}$.
