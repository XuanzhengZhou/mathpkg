A sub-configuration is a configuration whose points and lines form subsets of those of a larger configuration, with the crucial condition that incidences are preserved in both directions. This means the containing configuration does not introduce new incidences between elements already present in the sub-configuration, nor does it remove any. The notation $\mathcal{A} < \mathcal{B}$ is used to express this relationship, which forms the basis for constructing free closures by iteratively extending configurations.
