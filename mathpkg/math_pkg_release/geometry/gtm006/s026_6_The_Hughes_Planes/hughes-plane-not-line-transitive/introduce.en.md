Corollary 2 strengthens the non-desarguesian property by showing that the Hughes plane lacks any $(V,\ell)$-transitivity. A projective plane is $(V,\ell)$-transitive if for every pair of points $P,Q$ not on $\ell$ and collinear with $V$ but not on $V\ell$, there exists an elation with center $V$ and axis $\ell$ mapping $P$ to $Q$. The result follows from Exercise 4.28 together with the non-desarguesian property established in Corollary 1, and implies in particular that the Hughes plane is neither a translation plane nor a dual translation plane.
