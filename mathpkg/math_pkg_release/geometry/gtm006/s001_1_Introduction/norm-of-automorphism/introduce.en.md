The norm of an automorphism generalizes the familiar norm from field theory. For a field extension with cyclic Galois group generated by $\alpha$, the norm maps an element to the product of all its Galois conjugates. The resulting values are always fixed by $\alpha$, hence lie in the fixed field. Norms are essential for understanding quadratic extensions, unitary polarities, and the structure of finite fields in later chapters.
