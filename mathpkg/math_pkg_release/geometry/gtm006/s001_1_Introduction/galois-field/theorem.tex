A \text{finite field}, also called a \text{Galois field}, is a field with a finite number of elements. It is denoted by $GF(q)$ where $q = p^n$ for some prime $p$ and positive integer $n$. Up to isomorphism, $GF(q)$ exists and is unique for each prime power $q$. The prime $p$ is called the characteristic of the field.
