A permutation isomorphism is a stronger notion than group isomorphism: it requires the isomorphism to be compatible with the action on the underlying sets. Two permutation groups that are abstractly isomorphic may not be permutation isomorphic if their actions are fundamentally different. This concept is important for classifying collineation groups of projective planes up to equivalence of their geometric actions.
