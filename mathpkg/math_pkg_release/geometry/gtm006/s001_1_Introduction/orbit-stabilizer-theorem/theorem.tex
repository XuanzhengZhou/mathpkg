Let $\Gamma$ be represented as a permutation group on a set $\mathcal{S}$, and let $x \in \mathcal{S}$. Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $|\Gamma| = |x\Gamma| \cdot |\Gamma_x|$.
\item[(b)] If $\mathcal{F}_1, \mathcal{F}_2$ are orbits of $\Gamma$ with $(|\mathcal{F}_1|, |\mathcal{F}_2|) = 1$, then for any $x \in \mathcal{F}_1$, $\Gamma_x$ is transitive on $\mathcal{F}_2$.
\end{itemize}
