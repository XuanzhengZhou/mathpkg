This result states that every element in a finite field GF(q) can be expressed as a^2 + b^2 for some a, b in the field. In characteristic 2, every element is already a square. In odd characteristic, exactly half the non-zero elements are squares, yet every element remains a sum of two squares. This property has important consequences for projective geometry over finite fields.
