The characteristic of a skewfield (or field) is a fundamental invariant: it is either 0 or a prime number $p$. The characteristic determines the prime subfield: $\mathbb{Q}$ for characteristic 0, or $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z} = GF(p)$ for characteristic $p$. This classification is crucial for understanding the structure of finite fields and their subfields.
