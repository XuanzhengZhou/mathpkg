Burnside's Lemma (also known as the Cauchy-Frobenius Lemma or orbit-counting theorem) gives a formula for the number of orbits as the average number of fixed points. It is proved by double-counting the flags $(x, \alpha)$ with $x^\alpha = x$. This result is used in enumerative geometry and in counting configurations in finite projective planes (e.g., the number of points, lines, flags, etc.).
