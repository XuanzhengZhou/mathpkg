The $F$-quaternions form a 4-dimensional associative division algebra over a field $F$. When $F = \mathbb{R}$, the real quaternions $\mathbb{H}$ are the classic example of a skewfield that is not a field. Quaternions serve as important counterexamples throughout the book: many theorems true for fields fail for skewfields, and the quaternions provide concrete counterexamples.
