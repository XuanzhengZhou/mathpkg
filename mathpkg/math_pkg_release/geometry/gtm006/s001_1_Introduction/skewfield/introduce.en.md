A skewfield (also called a division ring) is an algebraic structure satisfying all field axioms except possibly the commutativity of multiplication. The quaternions provide the most famous example of a non-commutative skewfield. Throughout the study of projective planes, vector spaces are often considered over skewfields rather than fields, requiring careful distinction between left and right vector spaces.
