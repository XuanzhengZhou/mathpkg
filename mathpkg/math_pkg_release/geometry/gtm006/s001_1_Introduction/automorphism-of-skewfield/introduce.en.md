An automorphism of a skewfield is a bijective homomorphism from the skewfield to itself, preserving both addition and multiplication. The set of all automorphisms forms a group under composition. Automorphisms are central to Galois theory and play an important role in defining norms, studying polarities, and understanding the symmetries of coordinate structures for projective planes.
