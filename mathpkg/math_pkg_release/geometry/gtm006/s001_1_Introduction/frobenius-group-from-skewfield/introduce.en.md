This construction produces a canonical Frobenius group from any skewfield: the affine transformations $x \mapsto xa + b$ (with $a \neq 0$) form a Frobenius group where the Frobenius kernel is the group of translations. When $K$ is a finite field, this yields the group $\operatorname{AGL}(1,q)$, the 1-dimensional affine group. This construction generalizes to nearfields and is fundamental for the coordinatization of non-Desarguesian projective planes in later chapters.
