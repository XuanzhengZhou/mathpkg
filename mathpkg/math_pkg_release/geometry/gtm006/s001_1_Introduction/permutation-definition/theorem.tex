If $\mathcal{S}$ is a set, a permutation of $\mathcal{S}$ is a one-to-one mapping of $\mathcal{S}$ onto itself. The set of all such mappings forms a group under composition: if $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are permutations then $\alpha\beta$ is defined by $x^{\alpha\beta} = (x^\alpha)^\beta$. This group is the symmetric group on $\mathcal{S}$, depending only on $|\mathcal{S}|$.
