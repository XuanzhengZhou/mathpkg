There are two distinct ways in which one projective geometry may be contained in another. If $W$ is a subspace of $V$, then $\mathcal{P}(W)$ is a subgeometry in the natural sense. However, a vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$ can also be viewed over a sub-skewfield $F$ of $K$, yielding a different vector space structure. A projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(U)$ is called a complete subgeometry of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ precisely when $U$ is a subspace of $V$ over the original skewfield $K$, distinguishing subspace containment from the more subtle containment induced by field restriction.
