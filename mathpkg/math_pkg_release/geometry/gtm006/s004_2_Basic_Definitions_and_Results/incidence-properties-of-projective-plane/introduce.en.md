Theorem 2.2 establishes the three fundamental incidence properties satisfied by the points and lines of a projective geometry of $g$-dimension two (a projective plane). These properties — unique line through two distinct points, unique intersection point of two distinct lines, and the existence of a non-degenerate quadrangle — later become the defining axioms of an abstract projective plane. This theorem bridges the algebraic construction of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ with the synthetic axiomatic approach studied in subsequent chapters.
