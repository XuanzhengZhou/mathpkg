A projective geometry arises naturally from the lattice of subspaces of a vector space. Given a vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$, the projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ consists of all subspaces of $V$ ordered by inclusion. Incidence between subspaces is defined symmetrically via containment. This algebraic construction provides the classical model for projective geometry and serves as the foundation for studying more general projective planes.
