A skewfield (or division ring) is an algebraic structure with two binary operations -- addition and multiplication -- satisfying all the field axioms except that multiplication need not be commutative. Every nonzero element has a multiplicative inverse, and the distributive laws hold. Skewfields provide the algebraic foundation for coordinatizing non-Desarguesian projective planes in this book.
