For any skewfield $K$, an automorphism of $K$ is a one-to-one mapping $\alpha$ of $K$ onto $K$ such that $(a+b)^\alpha = a^\alpha + b^\alpha$ and $(ab)^\alpha = a^\alpha b^\alpha$. We say that $\alpha$ has order $n$ if $n$ is the smallest positive integer $t$ such that $a^{\alpha^t} = a$ for all $a$ in $K$.

If $\alpha$ has order $n$, then the \textbf{norm} of $\alpha$ is the product
$$N(x) = x \cdot x^\alpha \cdot x^{\alpha^2} \cdots x^{\alpha^{n-1}}$$
for all $x$ in $K$. Clearly any norm of $\alpha$ is fixed by $\alpha$.
