If $\Gamma$ is a permutation group on a set $\mathcal{S}$ and $\Sigma$ is a permutation group on a set $\mathcal{T}$ then a \textbf{permutation isomorphism} between the permutation groups is an isomorphism $\alpha$ from $\Gamma$ onto $\Sigma$ together with a one-to-one mapping $\beta$ from $\mathcal{S}$ onto $\mathcal{T}$ such that, for any $s \in \mathcal{S}$ and any $\gamma \in \Gamma$, $(s^\gamma)^\beta = (s^\beta)^{\alpha(\gamma)}$.
