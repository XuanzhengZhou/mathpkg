A homomorphism of projective planes is a surjective incidence-preserving map that sends points to points and lines to lines. It generalizes the algebraic notion of homomorphism to the geometric setting of projective planes. This concept provides the foundation for studying when one projective plane can be mapped onto another while respecting the incidence structure, leading to results about isomorphisms and the structure of fibers in such mappings.
