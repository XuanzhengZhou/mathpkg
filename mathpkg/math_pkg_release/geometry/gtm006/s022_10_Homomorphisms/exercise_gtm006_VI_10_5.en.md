Prove the following three statements about the structure induced by a homomorphism $\phi$ of projective planes. Let $Z$ be the set of all $x$ in $R_1$ such that $(x, 0)_1^\phi = (0, 0)_2$. Let $J$ be the set of elements $x$ in $R_1$ such that $xz = 1$ for some element $z$ in $Z$, and let $\bar{R}_1$ be the set of elements of $R_1$ which are not in $J$.

(i) $(\bar{R}_1, T_1)$ is a ternary ring.

(ii) $[T_1(x, y, z)]^* = T_2(x^*, y^*, z^*)$, for all $x, y, z$ in $\bar{R}_1$.

(iii) $(x, y)_1^\phi = (x^*, y^*)_2$ if both $x$ and $y$ are in $\bar{R}_1$; and $(m)_1^\phi = (m^*)_2$ if $m$ is in $\bar{R}_1$.
