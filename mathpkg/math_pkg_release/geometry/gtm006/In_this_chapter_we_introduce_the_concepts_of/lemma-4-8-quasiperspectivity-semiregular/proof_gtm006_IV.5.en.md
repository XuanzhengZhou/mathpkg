We must show that if some power of $\alpha$, say $\alpha^i$, fixes an element not in $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$, then $\alpha^i = 1$. This follows immediately since $\mathcal{F}(\alpha) \subset \mathcal{F}(\alpha^i)$ and $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is maximal by Theorem 4.6. Any non-trivial power fixing an extra element would force $\mathcal{F}(\alpha^i) = \mathcal{P}$, i.e. $\alpha^i = 1$.
