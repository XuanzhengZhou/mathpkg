Since the identity is a $(V,\ell)$-perspectivity for any $V$ and $\ell$, the set is non-empty. For any two $(V,\ell)$-perspectivities $\alpha, \beta$, the product $\alpha\beta^{-1}$ fixes $\ell$ pointwise (since both do) and fixes $V$ linewise. By the definition, $\alpha\beta^{-1}$ is also a $(V,\ell)$-perspectivity. Thus the set forms a subgroup.
