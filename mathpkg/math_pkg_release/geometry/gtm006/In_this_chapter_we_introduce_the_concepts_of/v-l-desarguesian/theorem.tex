A projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is said to be \emph{$(V,\ell)$-desarguesian} if, for each pair of non-degenerate triangles $\Delta_i$ ($i=1,2$) with vertices $A_i, B_i, C_i$ and opposite sides $a_i, b_i, c_i$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\Delta_1$ and $\Delta_2$ are in perspective from $V$, and
\item[(b)] $a_1 \cap a_2$ and $b_1 \cap b_2$ are on $\ell$,
\end{enumerate}
it follows that $c_1 \cap c_2$ is also on $\ell$.
