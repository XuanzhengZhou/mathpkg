If $\alpha$ is a collineation of an affine plane $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{B}^\ell$, show that there is a unique collineation $\beta \in \operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$ such that $\ell^\beta = \ell$ and $\beta$ induces $\alpha$ on $\mathcal{A}$. (We say that $\alpha$ \emph{extends} to $\beta$.)
