Under the usual definition of products of mappings, the set of all collineations of a given plane $\mathcal{B}$ form a group. This group, denoted by $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$, is called the \emph{full collineation group} of $\mathcal{B}$. A \emph{collineation group} of $\mathcal{B}$ is a subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$.
