The collineation $\alpha\beta^{-1}$ fixes all of $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ and at least one extra element, namely $X$. By Lemma 4.2, $\mathcal{F}(\alpha\beta^{-1})$ is a closed configuration. But clearly $\mathcal{F}(\alpha\beta^{-1}) \supset \mathcal{F}(\alpha)$, which is a closed Baer subset. Thus, by Theorem 4.6, $\mathcal{F}(\alpha\beta^{-1}) = \mathcal{P}$, i.e. $\alpha\beta^{-1} = 1$ and $\alpha = \beta$.
