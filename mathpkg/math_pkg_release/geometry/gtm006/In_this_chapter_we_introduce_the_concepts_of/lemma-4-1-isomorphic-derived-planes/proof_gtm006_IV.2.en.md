This follows immediately from Lemma 3.11. If $\alpha \in \operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{P}$ maps $\ell$ to $k$, then the restriction of $\alpha$ to $\mathcal{P}^\ell$ yields an isomorphism $\mathcal{P}^\ell \cong \mathcal{P}^k$. Conversely, any isomorphism $\mathcal{P}^\ell \cong \mathcal{P}^k$ extends to a collineation of $\mathcal{P}$ mapping $\ell$ to $k$.
