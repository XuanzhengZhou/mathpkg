Let $x \in D$ and $n \in N_l$. Then, using the autotopism identity and the definition of $N_l$,
$$(nx)P = (nx \cdot 1)R = ((nx)R_a^{-1})P \cdot (1 \cdot b)Q = n^lpha_l \cdot xP.$$
Thus $P$ is a semi-linear transformation of the (left) vector space $D$ over $N_l$.

Similarly, if $x \in D$ and $n \in N_m$, then
$$(xn)P = (xn \cdot 1)R = (xn a)R \cdot (b \cdot 1)R = xP \cdot n^{lpha_m},$$
and hence $P$ is also a semi-linear transformation of the (right) vector space $D$ over $N_m$.

It is worth noting that the triple $(P, P, P)$ is an autotopism of $D$ if and only if $P$ is an automorphism of $D$, so that an automorphism of $D$ is a semi-linear transformation of $D$ as a vector space over any one of its semi-nuclei. $\square$
