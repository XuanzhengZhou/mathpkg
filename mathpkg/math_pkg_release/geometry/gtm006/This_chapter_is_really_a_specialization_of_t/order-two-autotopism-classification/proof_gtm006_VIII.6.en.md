The projective plane $\mathcal{P}(D)$ has an involution fixing the autotopism triangle. Since an elation cannot fix a triangle, this involution must, by Theorem 4.3 and Exercise 4.4, be either a homology (in which case the plane has odd order), or fix a Baer subplane pointwise.

In Theorem 8.2 we showed that any homology fixing $(\infty), (0), (0, 0)$ is induced by appropriate multiplications from the various semi-nuclei. An involution of order 2 corresponds to multiplication by elements of order 2 in the semi-nuclei, which in a division ring of characteristic not 2 is precisely $-1$. The three listed forms $(A, A, I), (A, 1, A), (I, A, A)$ with $A = R_{-1} = L_{-1}$ exhaust the possibilities for homologies of order 2. Any other involution in $\Delta_2$ must therefore be a Baer involution. $\square$
