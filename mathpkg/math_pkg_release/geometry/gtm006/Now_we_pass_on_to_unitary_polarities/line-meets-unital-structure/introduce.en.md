Theorem 2.48 is the main structural result describing how lines intersect a unital. Part (i) characterizes absolute lines as precisely those that meet the unital at exactly one point (namely, the pole of the line). Part (ii) describes the intersection of a non-absolute line with the unital: either the intersection is empty, or it is a set of points in bijection with the norm-one subgroup $N$. This gives a complete picture: through each point of the unital there is exactly one tangent (absolute) line, and every other line through that point meets the unital in $|N| = q+1$ points (in the finite case). The discriminant-like condition for a non-absolute line to intersect the unital is expressed in terms of the matrix $A$ of the unitary polarity.
