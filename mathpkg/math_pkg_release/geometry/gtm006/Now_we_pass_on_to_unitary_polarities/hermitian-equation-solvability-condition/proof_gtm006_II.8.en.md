Suppose equation (*) has a solution $x$. Applying the involutory automorphism $\phi$ to the equation and using that $a^\phi = a$, $c^\phi = c$ (since $a, c \in F$), we obtain conditions that force $b^{1+\phi} - ac$ to lie in $F$. For the case analysis, when $d = 0$, the equation reduces to a perfect square-type form admitting a unique solution. When $d \neq 0$, one can transform the equation via the substitution involving elements of $N$, showing that the solution set, if non-empty, is parametrized by $N$. $\square$
