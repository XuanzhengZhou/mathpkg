Lemma 2.44 gives a necessary condition for the solvability of a hermitian-type equation $x^{1+\phi}a + x^{\phi}b + x b^{\phi} + c = 0$ over a field $K$ with an involutory automorphism $\phi$. The discriminant-like quantity $d = b^{1+\phi} - ac$ must lie in the fixed field $F$ for solutions to exist. The case $d = 0$ yields a single solution, while $d \neq 0$ connects the solution set to the norm-one subgroup $N$. This lemma is the algebraic foundation for analyzing line-unitial intersections in the geometric context.
