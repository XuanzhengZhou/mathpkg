Let $\mathcal{S}$ be the subset of all points $(b)$ on $l_{\omega}$, $b \in GF(q)$, plus $(\infty)$. We must show that if $X$ and $Y$ are distinct points of $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{A}_2(q^2)$ and if $XY = l_{\omega} \in \mathcal{S}$, then $X$, $Y$, and $\mathcal{S}$ are in a Baer subplane of $\mathcal{A}$.

Since the translations of $\mathcal{A}$ fix $\mathcal{S}$ and are transitive on the points of $\mathcal{A}$, we may assume that $X = (0, 0)$. The line $XY$ passes through $\mathcal{S}$ and the group of $(X, l_{\omega})$-homologies fixes $\mathcal{S}$ and is transitive on the points of $XY$, other than $X$. Thus we may assume that $Y$ is any point on a line joining $(0, 0)$ to a point of $\mathcal{S}$ and, in particular, that $Y$ lies in the subplane of $\mathcal{A}$ coordinated by $GF(q)$. Since $\mathcal{A}_2(q)$ is a Baer subplane of $\mathcal{A}_2(q^2)$, the theorem is proved.
