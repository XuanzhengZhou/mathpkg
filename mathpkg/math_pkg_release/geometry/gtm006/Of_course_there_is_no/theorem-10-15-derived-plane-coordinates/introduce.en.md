Theorem 10.15 explicitly describes how to coordinatize the derived plane $\mathcal{A}_1 = \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}}(\mathcal{A})$ in terms of the planar ternary ring $(R,T)$ of the original plane $\mathcal{A}$. The new planar ternary ring $(R,T_1)$ satisfies five specific properties: the addition coincides with the original ($x \oplus y = x + y$), scalar multiplication by elements of $F$ is preserved, the line at infinity of the derived plane consists of points with slopes in $F$ together with $(\infty)$, and the ternary operation simplifies to the linear form $T_1(\lambda, x, y) = \lambda x \oplus y$ for scalars in $F$.
