Let $(R,T)$ be the planar ternary ring of a derivable affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ with derivation set $\mathcal{S}$, where $F$ is a subfield of the kernel of $R$ and $R$ has dimension $2$ over $F$. Let $t$ be a fixed element of $R \setminus F$. Then the planar ternary ring $(R,T_1)$ of the derived plane $\mathcal{A}_1 = \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}}(\mathcal{A})$ satisfies:

(1) if $\lambda$ is in $F$ and $x$ is in $R$, then $T_1(\lambda, x, 0) = \lambda x$;
(2) $x \oplus y = x + y$ for all $x, y \in R$;
(3) if $\lambda$ is in $F$ and $x$ is in $R$, then $\lambda * x = \lambda x$;
(4) $\mathcal{L}_{\infty}^* = \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}}(\mathcal{L}_{\infty})$ consists of the points $(\lambda)'$, $\lambda$ in $F$, and $(\infty)'$;
(5) if $\lambda$ is in $F$ and $x,y$ are in $R$, then $T_1(\lambda, x, y) = \lambda x \oplus y$.

Here $(x,y)'$, $(m)'$, etc., denote points of $\mathcal{A}_1$ and its special line in the new coordinate system.
