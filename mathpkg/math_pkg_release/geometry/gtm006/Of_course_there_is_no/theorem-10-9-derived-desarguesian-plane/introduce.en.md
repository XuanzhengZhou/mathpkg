Theorem 10.9 shows that the derived plane $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{F}}(\mathcal{A})$ of a desarguesian affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ is typically non-desarguesian. The sole exceptional case is when $\mathcal{A}$ has order $4$, corresponding to $q=2$. For $q>2$, the group of all shears of the derived plane is isomorphic to the additive group of $GF(q)$, which reveals the non-desarguesian structure. Together with Theorem 10.8, this provides a method for constructing non-desarguesian planes from the classical desarguesian ones.
