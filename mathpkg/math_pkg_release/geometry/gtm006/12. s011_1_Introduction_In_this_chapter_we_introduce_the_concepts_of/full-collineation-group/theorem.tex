A collineation (or automorphism) of a projective, or affine, plane $\mathcal{B}$ is an isomorphism of the plane onto itself. Under the usual definition of products of mappings, the set of all collineations of a given plane $\mathcal{B}$ forms a group. This group, denoted by $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{B}$, is called the \textbf{full collineation group} of $\mathcal{B}$. Any subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{B}$ is called a collineation group of $\mathcal{B}$.
