A collineation, also called an automorphism, of a projective or affine plane is an isomorphism of the plane onto itself. Under the usual definition of product of mappings, the set of all collineations of a given plane $\mathcal{B}$ forms a group, denoted by $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$ and called the full collineation group. A collineation group is any subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$. The identity collineation is denoted by $1$.
