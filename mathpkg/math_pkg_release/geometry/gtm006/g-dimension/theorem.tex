Let $V$ be an $(n+1)$-dimensional vector space over a skewfield $K$. Then the projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is said to have \textbf{$g$-dimension} $n$.

Subspaces of $V$ of (algebraic) dimension $i+1$ are said to have $g$-dimension $i$, written $gd(W) = i = \dim(W) - 1$.

Points are subspaces of $g$-dimension $0$ (algebraic dimension $1$). Lines have $g$-dimension $1$. Planes have $g$-dimension $2$. Hyperplanes have $g$-dimension $n-1$.

The zero subspace of $V$ (dimension $0$) has $g$-dimension $-1$ and is the empty space in $\mathcal{P}(V)$.
