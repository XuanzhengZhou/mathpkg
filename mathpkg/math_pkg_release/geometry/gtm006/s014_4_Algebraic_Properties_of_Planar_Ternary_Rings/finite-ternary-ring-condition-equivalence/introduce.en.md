Theorem 5.4 establishes that finiteness collapses the axiomatic requirements for a planar ternary ring. Specifically, in a finite ternary ring, conditions (C) and (D) together force condition (E) to hold. This is significant because it reduces the number of independent axioms that must be verified when checking whether a finite ternary ring is planar. The proof uses a counting argument: the mapping $A$ defined by $T(x, a, b) = T(x, c, xA)$ is shown to be injective, hence bijective on the finite set $R$.
