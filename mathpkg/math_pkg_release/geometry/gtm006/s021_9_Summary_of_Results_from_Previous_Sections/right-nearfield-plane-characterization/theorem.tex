A projective plane has a coordinating planar ternary ring that is a right nearfield if and only if, for a suitable choice of coordinates (with $V = (\infty)$), the plane is $(V,V)$-transitive together with an additional $(V,W)$-transitivity, where $V$ but not $W$ lies on a line $k$, with either $k = [\infty]$ and $W$ lying on $[\infty]$, or $k = [\infty]$ with $W$ on $[\infty]$.
