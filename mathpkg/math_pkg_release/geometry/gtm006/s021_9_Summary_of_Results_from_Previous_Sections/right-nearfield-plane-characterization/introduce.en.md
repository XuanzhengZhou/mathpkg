A right nearfield is a structure in which addition forms a group and nonzero elements form a group under multiplication, but with the right distributive law holding (instead of the left one, which defines a nearfield). This characterization identifies when a projective plane is coordinatized by a right nearfield: it requires $(V,V)$-transitivity for a point $V$ (chosen as $(\infty)$) plus an additional $(V,W)$-transitivity. The geometric configuration mirrors that of the nearfield case but with the roles of points and lines interchanged, reflecting the left-right duality of the distributive law.
