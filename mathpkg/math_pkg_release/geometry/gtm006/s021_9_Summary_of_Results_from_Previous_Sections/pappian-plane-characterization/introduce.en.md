A projective plane is pappian if it satisfies the Theorem of Pappus as a geometric configuration condition. This theorem summarizes the equivalence between three characterizations: the algebraic condition that every coordinating planar ternary ring is a field (necessarily commutative), the geometric condition of full $(V,k)$-transitivity for all point-line pairs, and the combined condition of full incidence transitivity plus the Pappus configuration. In a pappian plane, all coordinating PTRs are mutually isomorphic fields.
