When a projective plane is coordinatized with respect to a particular quadrangle, the PTR is a division ring precisely when the plane admits both $(k,k)$-transitivity and $(V,V)$-transitivity for the particular incident pair $(k,V)$ used in the coordinatization (where $V$ serves as the point $(\infty)$ and $k$ as the line $[\infty]$). This is the algebraic characterization of what are sometimes called "translation planes of dimension 2." The division ring need not be commutative; commutativity of multiplication corresponds to the stronger pappian condition.
