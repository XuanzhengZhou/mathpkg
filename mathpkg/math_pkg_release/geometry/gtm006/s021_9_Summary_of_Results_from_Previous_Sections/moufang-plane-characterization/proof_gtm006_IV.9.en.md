This equivalence is established in Sections 2-8 of Chapter IV. The coordinatization shows that the Moufang condition forces the PTR to satisfy the alternative law and the inverse property, making it an alternative division ring. Conversely, an alternative division ring PTR yields a Moufang plane. The transitivity equivalences follow from the theory of $(V,k)$-central collineations: the Moufang condition is equivalent to the existence of all $(V,k)$-central collineations for incident pairs. That this implies $(k,k)$-transitivity for all $k$ and $(V,V)$-transitivity for all $V$ follows from the algebraic properties of alternative division rings (in particular, the inverse property). The sufficiency of two distinct lines or points is a deeper result using the structure of the full collineation group.
