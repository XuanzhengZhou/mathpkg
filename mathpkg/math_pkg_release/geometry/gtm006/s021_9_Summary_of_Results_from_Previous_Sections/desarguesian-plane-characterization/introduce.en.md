A projective plane is desarguesian if it satisfies Desargues' Theorem. This characterization establishes the equivalence between three formulations: every coordinating planar ternary ring is a skewfield (a division ring, not necessarily commutative), the plane is $(V,k)$-transitive for all point-line pairs, or the plane is $(V,k)$-transitive for all incident pairs plus one additional non-incident pair. In a desarguesian plane, all coordinating PTRs are mutually isomorphic skewfields.
