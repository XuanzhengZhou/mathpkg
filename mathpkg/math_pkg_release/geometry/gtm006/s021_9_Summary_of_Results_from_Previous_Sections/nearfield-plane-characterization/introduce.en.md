A nearfield is an algebraic structure in which addition forms a group and multiplication (of nonzero elements) forms a group, but only one distributive law holds. This characterization states that a projective plane is coordinatized by a nearfield precisely when it admits a line $k$ (chosen as $[\infty]$) for which $(k,k)$-transitivity holds, together with a mixed transitivity $(V,m)$-transitivity where $V$ is on $k$ but not on $m$. The two possible configurations reflect the choice of which point serves as $(0)$ and which as $(\infty)$ in the coordinatization.
