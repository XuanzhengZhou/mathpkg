A right quasifield is the dual concept to a quasifield, with the right distributive law replacing the left one. This characterization identifies dual translation planes: a projective plane is a dual translation plane (with respect to a point $V$) exactly when it admits $(V,V)$-transitivity for that point. Choosing $V = (\infty)$ yields a right quasifield as the PTR. The geometric condition is dual to that of a translation plane, obtained by interchanging the roles of points and lines.
