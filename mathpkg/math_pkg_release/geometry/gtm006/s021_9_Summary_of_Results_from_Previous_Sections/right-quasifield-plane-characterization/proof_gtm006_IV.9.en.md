This equivalence is dual to the quasifield characterization, obtained by applying the duality principle of projective planes. If the plane is $(V,V)$-transitive for a point $V$, then choosing $V = (\infty)$ yields a PTR with associative addition and a right distributive multiplication, giving a right quasifield. The dual translation group with center $V$ provides the additive structure. The equivalence between $(V,V)$-transitivity for one point and for two distinct points follows from the group generated by the corresponding central collineations. Conversely, a right quasifield PTR implies the plane is a dual translation plane with respect to the point at infinity.
