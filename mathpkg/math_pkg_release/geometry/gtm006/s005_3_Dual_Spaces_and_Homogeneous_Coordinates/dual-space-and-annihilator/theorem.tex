If $V$ is a left vector space of dimension $n+1$ over the skewfield $K$, then the dual space $V'$ of $V$ is a right vector space of the same dimension over $K$; and if $V$ is a right vector space then $V'$ is a left one. The mapping $a_V$ from subspaces of $V$ to subspaces of $V'$ is given by: if $W \subset V$, then
$$W^{a_V} = \{v' \in V' \mid w v' = 0 \text{ for all } w \in W\}.$$
Since the subspaces of $V$ are the elements of $\mathcal{P}(V)$, $a_V$ is a mapping from $\mathcal{P}(V)$ to $\mathcal{P}(V')$.
