The projective linear groups form a hierarchy of transformation groups that act as automorphisms (collineations) of the projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$. The largest group $\Gamma L(V)$ consists of all invertible semi-linear transformations, which combine a linear automorphism of $V$ with a field automorphism of the underlying skewfield $K$. Its subgroups $GL(V)$ (linear transformations) and $SL(V)$ (determinant-one linear transformations, defined when $K$ is a field) induce corresponding projective groups $P\Gamma L(V)$, $PGL(V)$, and $PSL(V)$ by factoring out the scalar transformations that act trivially on the projective geometry. By the Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry, every automorphism of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ arises this way, making these groups the complete symmetry groups of the geometry.
