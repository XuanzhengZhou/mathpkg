Let $V$ be a left vector space over the skewfield $K$ of arbitrary finite dimension.

$\Gamma L(V)$ is the group of all non-singular semi-linear transformations of $V$ onto $V$.

$GL(V)$ is the subgroup of $\Gamma L(V)$ consisting of all non-singular linear transformations.

If $K$ is a field, $SL(V)$ is the subgroup of $GL(V)$ consisting of elements of determinant one.

Each of these groups induces a group of automorphisms of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto itself, denoted by prefixing "P":
$$P\Gamma L(V),\quad PGL(V),\quad PSL(V)$$
are the groups induced by $\Gamma L(V)$, $GL(V)$, and $SL(V)$ respectively on $\mathcal{P}(V)$.
