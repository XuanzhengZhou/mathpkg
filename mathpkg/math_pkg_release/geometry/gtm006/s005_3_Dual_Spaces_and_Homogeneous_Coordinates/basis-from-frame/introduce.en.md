Lemma 2.5 establishes the correspondence between frames and bases. Given a frame, one can select basis vectors for the first $n+1$ points, then adjust their scalars so that their sum represents the $(n+2)$-nd point. This determines the basis up to a single scalar factor, which precisely accounts for the scaling ambiguity in homogeneous coordinates.
