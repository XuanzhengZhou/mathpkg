If $E_1, E_2, \dots, E_{n+2}$ is a frame for $\mathcal{P}(V)$, then there exists a basis $f_1, f_2, \dots, f_{n+1}$ of $V$ such that $E_i = \langle f_i \rangle$ for $i = 1, \dots, n+1$ and $E_{n+2} = \langle f_1 + f_2 + \cdots + f_{n+1} \rangle$. This basis is unique up to a common non-zero scalar multiple.
