A frame is the projective analogue of a basis in a vector space. While $n+1$ points determine a basis only up to individual scalar multiples, the additional $(n+2)$-nd point fixes the basis up to a common scalar multiple. Frames are essential for coordinatizing projective geometries and for proving the Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry, which states that any isomorphism between projective geometries is induced by a semi-linear transformation.
