Theorem 2.9 extends the Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry to the case where the two projective geometries are built from vector spaces of opposite chirality: one left and one right. In this setting, an isomorphism between the projective geometries forces the underlying skewfields to be anti-isomorphic (i.e., $K \cong F^{\text{op}}$), and the isomorphism is induced by a semi-linear transformation. This result is essential for understanding correlations and dualities in projective geometry, where one naturally encounters mappings between $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\mathcal{P}(V')$ involving left and right vector spaces.
