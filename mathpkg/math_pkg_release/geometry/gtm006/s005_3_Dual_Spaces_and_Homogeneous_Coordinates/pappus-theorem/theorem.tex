Let $A_1, B_1, C_1$ be three distinct points on a line $L_1$ and $A_2, B_2, C_2$ be three distinct points on a line $L_2$, with no $A_i$ equal to $B_j$ for $i,j \in \{1,2\}$. Define $A_3 = (B_1 + C_2) \cap (B_2 + C_1)$, $B_3 = (A_1 + C_2) \cap (A_2 + C_1)$, $C_3 = (A_1 + B_2) \cap (A_2 + B_1)$. Then the points $A_3, B_3, C_3$ are collinear if and only if the underlying skewfield $K$ is commutative (i.e., is a field).
