Pappus' theorem is a classical configurational theorem in projective geometry. It states that for two lines each with three points, the three intersection points of certain cross-joins are collinear precisely when the underlying skewfield is commutative. Thus Pappus' theorem characterizes pappian (commutative) projective planes.
