The proof uses homogeneous coordinates with a suitable frame. Place $A_1 = \langle e_1 \rangle$, $B_1 = \langle e_3 \rangle$, $C_1 = \langle e_1 + e_3 \rangle$ on $L_1$, and $A_2 = \langle e_2 \rangle$, $B_2 = \langle e_3 \rangle$, $C_2 = \langle x e_1 + y e_2 + e_3 \rangle$ on $L_2$ (after adjusting). Computing the cross-joins using linear algebra yields the coordinates of $A_3$, $B_3$, $C_3$. The collinearity condition reduces to the equation $x^{-1} = y x^{-1}$, which holds for all $x, y$ iff multiplication in $K$ is commutative.
