If $V$ is a left vector space of dimension $n+1$ over the skewfield $K$, the dual mapping $a_V$ from subspaces of $V$ to subspaces of the dual space $V'$ is defined by: for $W \subseteq V$,
$$W^{a_V} = \{v' \in V' \mid w v' = 0 \text{ for all } w \in W\}.$$
This induces a mapping from $\mathcal{P}(V)$ to $\mathcal{P}(V')$ sending subspaces of $g$-dimension $d$ to subspaces of $g$-dimension $n-d$. In particular, points of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ map to hyperplanes of $\mathcal{P}(V')$, and vice versa.
