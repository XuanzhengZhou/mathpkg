A frame in a projective geometry plays the role that a basis plays in a vector space. Given a frame, one can uniquely determine homogeneous coordinates (up to a common scalar multiple) for all points. For the projective plane ($g$-dimension 2), a frame consists of 4 points with no three collinear — a quadrangle.
