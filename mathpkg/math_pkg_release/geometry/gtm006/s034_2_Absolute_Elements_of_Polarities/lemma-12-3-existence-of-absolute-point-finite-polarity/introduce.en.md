This lemma establishes that every polarity of a finite projective plane must possess at least one absolute point. The proof uses the incidence matrix $A$ of the plane, exploiting its symmetry under the polarity's labeling to express the number of absolute points in terms of eigenvalues. When the order $n$ is not a perfect square, the number of absolute points is forced to be exactly $n+1$.
