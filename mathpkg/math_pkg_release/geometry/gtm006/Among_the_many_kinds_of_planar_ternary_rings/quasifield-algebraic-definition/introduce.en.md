A quasifield is an algebraic structure with two binary operations that generalizes the notion of a field. It is defined purely algebraically by five axioms governing addition, multiplication, the left distributive law, and the unique solvability of certain linear equations. A quasifield serves as a coordinatizing structure for translation planes: every translation plane can be coordinatized by a quasifield, and conversely every quasifield determines a unique translation plane up to isomorphism.
