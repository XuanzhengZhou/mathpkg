The kernel of a weak quasifield $W$ consists of those elements that satisfy both right-distributivity and right-associativity with all other elements of $W$. While a weak quasifield only guarantees left-distributivity and the left action of multiplication, the kernel captures the elements that also behave well on the right. The kernel is a crucial structural invariant: it is always a skewfield, and $W$ can be regarded as a right vector space over its kernel.
