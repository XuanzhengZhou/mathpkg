A weak quasifield is a slight generalization of a quasifield in which addition is required to be abelian (rather than merely a group), but the unique solvability axiom for equations $ax = bx + c$ is dropped. Every quasifield is a weak quasifield (since Theorem 6.4 shows that addition in a quasifield is abelian, and the remaining axioms are satisfied). The class of weak quasifields is introduced primarily to facilitate the definition and study of the kernel of a quasifield.
