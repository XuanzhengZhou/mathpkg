Coordinatization is the process of assigning algebraic coordinates to the points and lines of an abstract projective plane, relative to a chosen non-degenerate quadrangle $O, X, Y, I$. Points not on the line at infinity $\ell_\infty$ receive ordered pairs $(x, y)$ from a coordinate set $R$, while points on $\ell_\infty$ receive single coordinates $(m)$ or $(\infty)$. Lines are coordinatized as $[m, k]$, $[k]$, or $[\infty]$, and this coordinatization forms the foundation for introducing the planar ternary ring as an algebraic structure describing the incidence geometry of the plane.
