Once a basis has been chosen, any point of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ may be represented by a row vector $\langle v \rangle$ (the subspace generated by $v$), and any hyperplane by a column vector $\langle w' \rangle$. These are called \textbf{homogeneous coordinates}. In the case of the projective plane, points are represented by $\langle x, y, z \rangle$ and lines by $\langle a, b, c \rangle$, with incidence given by $ax + by + cz = 0$.
