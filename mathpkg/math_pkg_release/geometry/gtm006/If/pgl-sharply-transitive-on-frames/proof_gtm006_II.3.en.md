By Theorem 2.12, the stabilizer in $P\Gamma L(V)$ of a frame is isomorphic to $\operatorname{Aut} K$, and the stabilizer in $PGL(V)$ is isomorphic to $\operatorname{In} K$. When $K$ is a field, $\operatorname{In} K = \{1\}$, so the $PGL(V)$-stabilizer of a frame is trivial. Given any two frames, Lemma 2.5 provides bases that realize them. The linear transformation sending one basis to the other maps the first frame to the second and lies in $GL(V)$, hence induces an element of $PGL(V)$. Thus $PGL(V)$ acts transitively on frames. Combined with the trivial stabilizer (for fields), $PGL(V)$ is sharply transitive on frames.

For the projective line $\mathcal{P}_1(K)$ over a field, Exercise 2.8 shows that a frame consists of three distinct points. Sharp transitivity on frames is therefore equivalent to sharp three-transitivity on points. $\square$
