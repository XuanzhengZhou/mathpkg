Every projective plane gives rise to an affine plane by removing any single line together with all points on that line. This construction, denoted $\mathcal{P}^l$, strips away the "points at infinity" and the "line at infinity" from the projective plane, yielding an incidence structure where any two points still determine a unique line, but where parallelism now emerges: lines that previously met on the deleted line become parallel in the affine plane.
