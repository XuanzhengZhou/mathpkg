When constructing an affine plane from a projective plane by deleting a line, the resulting affine plane depends on which line is chosen. This lemma gives a precise condition: two such affine planes $\mathcal{P}^l$ and $\mathcal{P}^{l'}$ are isomorphic precisely when the deleted lines are in the same orbit under the automorphism group of the projective plane $\mathcal{P}$. In particular, the proof shows that any isomorphism between the affine planes lifts uniquely to an automorphism of the projective plane.
