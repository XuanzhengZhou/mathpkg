An affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ is an incidence structure of points and lines satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Any two distinct points are incident with a unique line.
\item[(ii)] Given a line $m$ and a point $A$ not on $m$, there is a unique line through $A$ that does not intersect $m$.
\item[(iii)] There exist three non-collinear points.
\end{enumerate}
Two lines are called parallel (denoted $\parallel$) if they are equal or do not intersect.
