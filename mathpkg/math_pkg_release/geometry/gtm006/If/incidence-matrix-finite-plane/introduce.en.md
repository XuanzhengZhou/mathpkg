The incidence matrix of a finite incidence structure encodes point-line incidences as a $(0,1)$-matrix. For a finite projective plane of order $n$, the matrix is square of size $n^2+n+1$; for an affine plane it is $n^2 \times (n^2+n)$. This matrix is a powerful algebraic tool: its product with its transpose encodes intersection numbers between lines, and its determinant yields information about the existence of projective planes for certain orders (as in the Bruck-Ryser-Chowla theorem).
