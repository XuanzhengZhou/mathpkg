Having established that $PSL(V)$ is two-transitive (Lemma 2.15), three-transitivity requires that the stabilizer of two points acts transitively on the remaining points. Fix $\infty$ and $0$. The subgroup of $PSL(V)$ stabilizing both consists of maps $x \mapsto ax$ where $a$ is a non-zero square (since $c = 0$ and $b = 0$, and $ad - bc = ad$ must be a square).

The orbit of the point $1$ under this stabilizer is $\{a \cdot 1 = a \mid a \in (K^*)^2\}$, i.e., the set of all non-zero squares. For $PSL(V)$ to be three-transitive, this orbit must be all of $K^* \setminus \{1\}$, which happens precisely when every element of $K^*$ is a square. Conversely, if every element is a square, then the stabilizer of $\infty$ and $0$ can send $1$ to any other point, proving three-transitivity. $\square$
