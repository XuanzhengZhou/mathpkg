Lemma 2.16 gives a precise algebraic condition for $PSL(V)$ to be three-transitive on the projective line $\mathcal{P}_1(K)$. It states that $PSL(V)$ is three-transitive if and only if every element of $K^*$ is a square. Since this condition fails for most fields (e.g., $\mathbb{R}$, $\mathbb{Q}$), $PSL(V)$ is not generally three-transitive, unlike $PGL(V)$ which is always sharply three-transitive. The proof analyzes the stabilizer of two points and the orbit of a third point.
