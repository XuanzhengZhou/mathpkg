By Theorem 2.8, every isomorphism from $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto itself (i.e., every automorphism) is induced by a semi-linear transformation from $V$ onto $V$. A semi-linear transformation of $V$ onto $V$ is by definition an element of $\Gamma L(V)$. If the transformation is non-singular (which it must be to induce an automorphism of $\mathcal{P}(V)$), it lies in the subgroup of all non-singular semi-linear transformations. Hence $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V) = P\Gamma L(V)$, the group induced on $\mathcal{P}(V)$ by all non-singular semi-linear transformations of $V$. $\square$
