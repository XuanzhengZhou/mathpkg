The determinant of $AA'$ for the incidence matrix of a finite projective plane of order $n$ evaluates to $(n+1)^2 n^{n^2+n}$. The computation uses row operations on the matrix $nI + J$: subtracting the first row from all others, then adding all columns to the first column to obtain an upper triangular form. This determinant plays a role in the non-existence theory of projective planes: since $\det(AA')$ must be a perfect square (as $AA'$ is a Gram matrix), this imposes number-theoretic restrictions on possible orders $n$.
