Let $A_1, B_1, C_1$ be three distinct points on a line $L_1$, and let $A_2, B_2, C_2$ be three distinct points on another line $L_2$, with $L_1 \neq L_2$. Suppose further that none of these six points is the intersection $L_1 \cap L_2$. Then the three points
$$A_3 = (B_1 + C_2) \cap (B_2 + C_1), \quad B_3 = (A_1 + C_2) \cap (A_2 + C_1), \quad C_3 = (A_1 + B_2) \cap (A_2 + B_1)$$
are collinear if and only if the underlying skewfield $K$ is commutative (i.e., is a field).
