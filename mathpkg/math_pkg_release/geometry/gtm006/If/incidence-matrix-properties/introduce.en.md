For a finite projective plane of order $n$, the product $AA'$ of the incidence matrix with its transpose has a remarkably simple form: diagonal entries equal $n+1$ (each point lies on $n+1$ lines) and off-diagonal entries equal $1$ (any two distinct points determine exactly one line). This structure, $AA' = nI + J$, is fundamental for computing determinants and eigenvalues, and it underlies the Bruck-Ryser-Chowla non-existence theorem for projective planes.
