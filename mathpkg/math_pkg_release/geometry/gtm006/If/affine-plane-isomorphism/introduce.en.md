An isomorphism between affine planes is an incidence-preserving bijection on both points and lines. This mirrors the definition for projective planes: it is a structure-preserving map that is invertible. Isomorphisms preserve all incidence-geometric properties, including parallelism: a line $l$ is parallel to $m$ if and only if their images under the isomorphism are also parallel.
