If $K$ is a field and $\mathcal{P} = \mathcal{P}_1(K)$ is the projective line over $K$, then the group $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}$ can be represented on the points of $\mathcal{P}$ in parametric form by the set of mappings $\theta(a, b, c, d, \alpha)$: for each $\alpha \in \operatorname{Aut} K$ and for each choice of $a, b, c, d \in K$ with $ad - bc \neq 0$, the mapping is given by
$$x \mapsto (x^\alpha a + b)(x^\alpha c + d)^{-1},$$
with the conventions $\infty^\alpha = \infty$, $c \cdot \infty + d = c \cdot \infty$, and $(a \cdot \infty)(c \cdot \infty)^{-1} = ac^{-1}$.
