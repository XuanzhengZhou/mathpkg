Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a projective plane and let $l$ be any line of $\mathcal{P}$. Define $\mathcal{P}^l$ to be the set of points and lines of $\mathcal{P}$ obtained by deleting the line $l$ and all the points incident with it. The incidence relation on $\mathcal{P}^l$ is the restriction of the incidence relation of $\mathcal{P}$.

Any two points of $\mathcal{P}^l$ are incident with a unique line of $\mathcal{P}^l$. However, if $X$ is any point of $l$ and $m_1, m_2$ are any pair of distinct lines through $X$ such that neither is $l$, then $m_1, m_2$ are lines of $\mathcal{P}^l$ and $m_1$ and $m_2$ do not intersect in $\mathcal{P}^l$. Moreover, if $A$ is any point of $\mathcal{P}^l$ not on $m_1$, and if $m$ is the line joining $A$ to $X$ in $\mathcal{P}$, then $m$ is the only line of $\mathcal{P}^l$ through $A$ with the property that it does not intersect $m_1$.
