Theorem 2.48 completely classifies the possible intersections of a line with a unital in a projective plane. Absolute lines are characterized as tangent lines meeting the unital at exactly one point. Non-absolute lines either miss the unital entirely or intersect it in a set of points whose cardinality equals the size of the norm-one subgroup $N$. The intersection criterion is expressed in terms of a norm equation involving the polarity matrix, analogous to a discriminant condition.
