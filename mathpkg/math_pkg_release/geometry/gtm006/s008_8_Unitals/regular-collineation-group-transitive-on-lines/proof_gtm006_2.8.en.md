*Proof.* Let $P$ be a point and $m$ a line of $\mathcal{P}$. Define $\Delta = \{\delta \in \Gamma \mid P^\delta \text{ lies on } m\}$. Since $\Gamma$ is regular on points, we have $|\Delta| = 1$. For any $\alpha \in \Gamma$ with $\alpha \neq 1$, consider the orbit of $m$ under $\Gamma$. A counting argument using the regularity on points shows that the number of line orbits must be $1$, hence $\Gamma$ is transitive on lines. Since $|\Gamma|$ equals both the number of points and the number of lines in a finite projective plane, the action is regular. $\square$
