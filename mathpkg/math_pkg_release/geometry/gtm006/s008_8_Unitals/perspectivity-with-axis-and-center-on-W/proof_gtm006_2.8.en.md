*Proof.* If $\alpha = 1$, then $\alpha = \phi_{0,0}$. Suppose $\alpha \neq 1$. Let the effect of $\alpha$ on $E_3$ be to send it to some point of $\mathcal{A}$, which can be written as $\langle a e_1 + b e_2 + e_3 \rangle$. Since $\alpha$ is a perspectivity with axis $W$, it fixes every point on $W$, hence fixes $E_1 = \langle e_1 \rangle$, $E_2 = \langle e_2 \rangle$, and $\langle e_1 + e_2 \rangle$. A linear transformation inducing $\alpha$ must therefore have a diagonal-like action on the first two coordinates and fix the third coordinate up to scale. The conditions of being a perspectivity force the transformation to coincide with $\phi_{a,b}$. $\square$
