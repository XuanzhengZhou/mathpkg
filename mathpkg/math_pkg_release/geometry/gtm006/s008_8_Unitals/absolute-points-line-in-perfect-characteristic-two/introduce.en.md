Theorem 2.69 gives a complete description of the absolute points of an orthogonal polarity over a perfect field of characteristic two: they form exactly a line. This is fundamentally different from the odd characteristic case where absolute points form a conic. The proof uses the fact that every coefficient is a square, allowing the quadratic form to be written as a perfect square.
