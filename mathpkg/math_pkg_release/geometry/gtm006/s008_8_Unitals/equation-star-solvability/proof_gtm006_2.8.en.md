*Proof.* Assume $x$ is a solution of $(*)$. Multiplying $(*)$ by $x^{\phi}$ and applying $\phi$ yields a relation that forces $d = b^{1+\phi} - ac$ to be fixed by $\phi$, hence $d \in F$. The complete algebraic derivation uses the fact that $a, c \in F$ are fixed by $\phi$, and that the conjugate of $(*)$ must also hold. Computing $(x^{1+\phi}a + x^{\phi}b + xb^{\phi} + c)^{\phi}$ and comparing with the original equation gives the condition $b^{1+\phi} - ac \in F$. $\square$
