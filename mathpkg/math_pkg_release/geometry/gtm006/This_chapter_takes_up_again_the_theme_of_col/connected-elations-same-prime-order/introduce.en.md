Lemma 13.7 shows that in a finite projective plane, any two elations whose centres belong to the same non-trivial connected set $\mathcal{S}$ must have the same prime order. The proof chains together applications of Theorem 4.14 (and its dual), which says that two elations whose centre-axis flags share an element have the same prime order. Since the centres $A$ and $B$ are connected by a sequence of elation flags where consecutive flags share an element, repeated use of Theorem 4.14 propagates the prime order along the chain. This is the key result that justifies the definition of the "characteristic" of a connected set.
