A permutation matrix is a square $(0,1)$-matrix in which every row and every column contains exactly one entry equal to 1 and all other entries 0. Permutation matrices represent permutations of a finite set. In the context of projective planes, a collineation can be represented by two permutation matrices $P$ and $Q$ acting on an incidence matrix $A$ via $PA = AQ$, as established in Lemma 13.2.
