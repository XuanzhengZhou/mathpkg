Theorem 13.4, the Orbit Theorem, is one of the most important results in the study of collineation groups of finite projective planes. It states that any collineation group $\Pi$ has equally many point orbits and line orbits. The proof combines Theorem 13.3 (each individual collineation fixes equally many points and lines) with Burnside's Lemma (Result 1.14): the number of orbits equals the average number of fixed points over all group elements. Since corresponding terms in the two averages are equal by Theorem 13.3, the orbit counts must be equal. A direct corollary is that $\Pi$ is point-transitive if and only if it is line-transitive.
