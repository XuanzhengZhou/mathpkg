Lemma 13.8 provides sufficient conditions for a line $l$ in a finite projective plane to be a translation line. The conditions are: (i) the subgroup $\Gamma_{(l,l)}$ of collineations fixing $l$ pointwise and linewise is non-trivial, and (ii) for every point $A$ on $l$, there exists a non-trivial elation with centre $A$ and some axis $a \neq l$ through $A$ belonging to $\Gamma$. The proof combines Lemma 13.6, Lemma 13.7, and Corollary 2 of Theorem 4.26 to deduce that the group generated by these elations yields the full translation group with axis $l$.
