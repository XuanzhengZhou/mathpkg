Lemma 13.2 provides a matrix-theoretic representation of collineations in a finite projective plane. Given an incidence matrix $A$ of the plane, any collineation $\alpha$ corresponds to a pair of permutation matrices $(P, Q)$ satisfying $PA = AQ$, where $P$ describes the permutation of points and $Q$ the permutation of lines induced by $\alpha$. This representation is crucial for the proof of Theorem 13.3 (fixed points equal fixed lines), as it allows comparing traces of $P$ and $Q$ via the invertibility of $A$.
