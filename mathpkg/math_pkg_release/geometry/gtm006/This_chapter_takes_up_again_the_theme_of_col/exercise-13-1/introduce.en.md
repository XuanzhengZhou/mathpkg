Exercise 13.1 explores the relationship between non-faithful orbits of a collineation group and invariant subplanes. If a collineation group $\Pi$ acts non-faithfully on an orbit that contains a quadrangle (four points, no three collinear), the exercise asks to prove that $\Pi$ must leave some proper subplane invariant. The non-faithfulness implies that some non-trivial subgroup fixes all points in the orbit, and the quadrangle condition provides enough structure to generate a fixed subplane.
