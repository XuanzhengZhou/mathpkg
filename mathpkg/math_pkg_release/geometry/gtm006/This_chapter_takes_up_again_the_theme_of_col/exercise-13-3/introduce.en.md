Exercise 13.3 is a basic linear algebra exercise on permutation matrices. It asks to verify that every permutation matrix is invertible (non-singular) and that its inverse equals its transpose, i.e., permutation matrices are orthogonal. This property is used implicitly in the proof of Lemma 13.2 and Theorem 13.3, where permutation matrices represent collineations and their traces count fixed points and lines.
