Exercise 13.4 characterizes double transitivity of a collineation group on points via transitivity on flags and anti-flags. A flag is an incident point-line pair, and an anti-flag is a non-incident point-line pair. Double point-transitivity means the group can send any ordered pair of distinct points to any other. The equivalence reflects the geometric fact that specifying two distinct points determines a line (flag condition) and the choice of a third point not on that line (anti-flag condition) captures the full 2-transitive structure.
