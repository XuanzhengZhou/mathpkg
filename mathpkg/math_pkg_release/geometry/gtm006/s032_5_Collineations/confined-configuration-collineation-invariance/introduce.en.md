A subconfiguration $\mathcal{C}$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is called *confined* if every element of $\mathcal{C}$ is incident with at least three elements of $\mathcal{C}$. Lemma 11.16 states that the property of being confined is invariant under any collineation $\alpha$ of $\mathcal{P}$: $\mathcal{C}$ is confined exactly when its image $\mathcal{C}^\alpha$ under $\alpha$ is confined. The proof is immediate because collineations preserve incidence, and therefore preserve incidence counts.
