Every collineation of a configuration $\mathcal{A}$ can be extended to a collineation of its full free extension $\mathcal{B}$. The extension is constructed by mapping the unique intersection of two elements in $\mathcal{A}$ to the intersection of their images. This lemma requires both fullness and freeness.
