The kernel $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is the set of all elements of $\mathcal{P}$ that belong to no confined configuration. This corollary to Lemma 11.16 states that the kernel is invariant under every collineation $\alpha$ of $\mathcal{P}$: the image of $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ under $\alpha$ is exactly $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ itself. This follows directly from the fact that confined configurations are preserved by collineations, so an element escapes all confined configurations exactly when its image does.
