When $F$ is finite, $\Gamma = \operatorname{Gal}(F/K)$ is cyclic. In a cyclic group, the inversion map $g \mapsto g^{-1}$ is an automorphism, so any anti-homomorphism $\alpha(xy) = \alpha(y)\alpha(x)$ can be composed with inversion to give a homomorphism, and vice versa. Thus the anti-homomorphism condition from the Theorem is equivalent to $\phi$ being a homomorphism.

The fact that $N = K^*$ for finite fields follows from the surjectivity of the norm map $N_{F/K}: F^* \to K^*$ in the finite case (see Exercise 9.4). $\square$
