The Dickson commutative division ring is a variant of the construction in Section 4, specialized to produce a commutative but non-associative division ring. The key modification is using a symmetric multiplication formula that makes the algebra commutative. The construction requires a finite field of odd characteristic with a non-square element $a$ and a non-trivial automorphism $\theta$. These algebras were introduced by Leonard Eugene Dickson and represent an important class of finite non-associative division rings. They coordinatize certain non-Desarguesian projective planes.
