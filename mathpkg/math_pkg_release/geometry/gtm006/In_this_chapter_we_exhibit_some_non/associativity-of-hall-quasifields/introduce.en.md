This theorem classifies when the Hall quasifield construction yields an associative multiplication, and hence a nearfield or a field. The analysis of the right distributive law leads to the conditions $bx = bx^{-1}$ and $ax = a$ for all $x \in F, x \neq 0$. From these conditions, one can deduce that the only possible ground fields are $\mathrm{GF}(2)$ and $\mathrm{GF}(3)$ with the specific polynomial $s^2+1$. For $\mathrm{GF}(2)$, the Hall quasifield coincides with $\mathrm{GF}(4)$. For $\mathrm{GF}(3)$ with $f(s)=s^2+1$, the resulting quasifield is associative but not commutative, giving a nearfield of order 9.
