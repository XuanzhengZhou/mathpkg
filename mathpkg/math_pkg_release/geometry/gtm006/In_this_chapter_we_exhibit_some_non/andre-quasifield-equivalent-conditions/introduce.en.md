Lemma 9.4 establishes that the only way an André quasifield can be a division ring (satisfying both distributive laws) is if it is actually isomorphic to the original field $F$. This means the twisting by Galois automorphisms is trivial on the image of the norm map. In other words, non-trivial André quasifields are never division rings. This result is fundamental for understanding that André quasifields produce genuinely new algebraic structures that are not division rings, and hence the projective planes they coordinatize are strictly non-Desarguesian.
