Lemma 9.6 guarantees the existence of non-trivial André nearfields for every quadratic extension of a finite field of odd characteristic. This is a particularly important case because the André nearfields constructed this way have kernel $\mathrm{GF}(q)$ and provide examples of proper nearfields (nearfields that are not division rings). The corresponding André planes of order $q^2$ are among the most studied non-Desarguesian projective planes.
