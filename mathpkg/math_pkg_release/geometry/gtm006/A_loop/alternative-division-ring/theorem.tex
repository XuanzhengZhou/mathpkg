A division ring $D$ is called an \textbf{alternative division ring} (in the literature, sometimes called an \textit{alternative field}) if $D$ satisfies both alternative laws:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $x(xy) = x^2y$ \quad (left alternative law);
\item $(xy)x = xy^2$ \quad (right alternative law);
\end{enumerate}
for all $x, y \in D$.
