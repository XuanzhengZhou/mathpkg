In the theory of loops (non-associative algebraic structures with an identity element), the right inverse property (RIP) is a weakened form of the inverse property that holds in groups. A loop $G$ has RIP if for each element $x$, there exists a right inverse $x^{-1}$ such that right multiplication by $x$ followed by right multiplication by $x^{-1}$ returns the original element $y$. This property is central to the study of division rings that coordinatize projective planes, where RIP corresponds to certain transitivity properties of the associated plane.
