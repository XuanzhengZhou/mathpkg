Theorem 6.14 provides the geometric characterization of the left inverse property (LIP) in the presence of the right inverse property (RIP). For a division ring $R$ with RIP that coordinatizes a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$, the additional condition of LIP is equivalent to $\mathcal{P}$ being $((0,0), [0,0])$-transitive---that is, admitting elations with center $(0,0)$ and axis $[0,0]$. The proof shows that such an elation, when it exists, forces the algebraic identity $x^{-1}(xy) = y$ characterizing LIP; conversely, when $R$ has LIP, an explicit $((0,0), [0,0])$-elation can be constructed from the coordinate operations. This theorem together with Theorem 6.11 shows how the algebraic properties RIP and LIP correspond to distinct geometric transitivity conditions.
