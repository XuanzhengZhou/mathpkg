Theorem 6.11 establishes the geometric counterpart of the right inverse property (RIP) for a division ring $R$ that coordinatizes a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$. It states that $R$ has RIP precisely when $\mathcal{P}$ admits a $((0,0), [0])$-elation---the plane is $((0,0), [0])$-transitive. The proof proceeds in two directions: the existence of such elations forces the identity $(mk)u = m(ku)$ via an analysis of the action on coordinates, which characterizes RIP; conversely, RIP allows the construction of the required elation via a coordinate mapping $\phi_b$. This theorem is a crucial link between the algebraic structure of the coordinatizing ring and the geometric transitivity properties of the plane.
