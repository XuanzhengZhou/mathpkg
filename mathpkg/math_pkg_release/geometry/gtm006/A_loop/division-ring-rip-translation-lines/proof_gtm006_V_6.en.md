By Theorem 6.11, $R$ has RIP if and only if $\mathcal{P}$ is $((0,0), [0])$-transitive. A $((0,0), [0])$-elation of $\mathcal{P}$ fixes $(\infty)$ but moves $[\infty]$ to another line through $(\infty)$. Thus $\mathcal{P}$ has at least two translation lines through $(\infty)$: the original axis $[0]$ and the image of $[\infty]$ under the elation. By Theorem 4.20(a), the existence of two distinct translation lines through a point implies that every line through that point is a translation line. Hence every line through $(\infty)$ is a translation line. Conversely, if every line through $(\infty)$ is a translation line, then in particular $[0]$ is a translation line through $(\infty)$, so $\mathcal{P}$ is $((0,0), [0])$-transitive, and by Theorem 6.11, $R$ has RIP.
