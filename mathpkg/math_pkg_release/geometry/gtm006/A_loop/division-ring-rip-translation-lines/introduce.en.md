Theorem 6.12 is a geometric restatement of Theorem 6.11. A $((0,0), [0])$-elation of the projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ fixes the point $(\infty)$ but sends the line $[\infty]$ to a different line, giving $\mathcal{P}$ at least two translation lines through $(\infty)$. By Theorem 4.20(a), this implies that every line through $(\infty)$ is a translation line. Thus the algebraic right inverse property of the coordinatizing division ring $R$ is equivalent to the geometric condition that all lines through the distinguished point $(\infty)$ serve as translation axes, making $\mathcal{P}$ a translation plane with respect to $[\infty]$.
