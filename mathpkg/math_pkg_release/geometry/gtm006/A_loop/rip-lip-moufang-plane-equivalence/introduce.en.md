Theorem 6.15 is the culmination of Section 6's geometric-algebraic correspondence. It restates Theorem 6.14 by combining it with Theorem 6.12: since $R$ has RIP, every line through $(\infty)$ is a translation line (Theorem 6.12). If $R$ additionally has LIP, Theorem 6.14 implies $\mathcal{P}$ is $((0,0), [0,0])$-transitive, which together with the RIP-guaranteed translation lines through $(\infty)$ gives three non-concurrent translation lines. By Theorem 4.20, this forces every line of $\mathcal{P}$ to be a translation line---the defining property of a Moufang plane. Thus a division ring with both RIP and LIP coordinatizes exactly the Moufang planes, one of the most important classes of non-Desarguesian projective planes.
