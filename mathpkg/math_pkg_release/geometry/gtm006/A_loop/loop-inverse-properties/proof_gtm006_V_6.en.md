Suppose $G$ has RIP. Since $(yx)x^{-1}=y$ for all $y \in G$, put $y=x^{-1}$ to get $(x^{-1}x)x^{-1}=x^{-1}$. In a loop, the equation $bx^{-1}=x^{-1}$ has the unique solution $b=1$, so $x^{-1}x=1$. Now put $y=1$ to get $(1 \cdot x)x^{-1}=xx^{-1}=1$. Thus $x^{-1}x=1=xx^{-1}$. Consequently $(x^{-1})^{-1}=x$, since $x$ satisfies the defining equations for the inverse of $x^{-1}$. The proof for LIP is symmetric, using the left-handed identity $x^{-1}(xy)=y$.
