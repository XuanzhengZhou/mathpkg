A ternary operation is a generalization of the familiar binary operation, taking three arguments instead of two. On any set one can define such a ternary map $T(a,b,c)$, and the pair $(\mathcal{S}, T)$ is called a ternary ring. This algebraic structure is the foundational tool for introducing coordinates into a projective plane and is studied extensively in the theory of projective geometry.
