In an affine plane, two lines are called parallel if they are equal or have no point in common. This notion of parallelism arises naturally from the Playfair axiom: through any point not on a given line, there is exactly one line parallel to it. Parallelism forms an equivalence relation whose classes correspond to the "points at infinity" that are added to complete the affine plane to a projective plane.
