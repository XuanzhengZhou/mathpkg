Let $\mathcal{A}$ be any affine plane. Then there exists, up to isomorphism, a unique projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ such that $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{P}^l$ for some line $l$ of $\mathcal{P}$. Here $\mathcal{P}^l$ denotes the affine plane obtained from $\mathcal{P}$ by deleting the line $l$ and all points incident with it.
