For a finite projective plane of order $n$, the incidence matrix $A$ satisfies the fundamental relation $AA^T = nI + J$, where $I$ is the identity and $J$ is the all-ones matrix. This follows from the fact that any two distinct points determine a unique line (giving off-diagonal entries of $1$) and each point lies on exactly $n+1$ lines (giving diagonal entries $n+1$). This algebraic identity underlies many important results including the Bruck-Ryser-Chowla theorem.
