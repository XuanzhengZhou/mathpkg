A Baer subplane is a special kind of subplane of a finite projective plane. This theorem characterizes Baer subplanes precisely: a subplane of order $m$ inside a finite projective plane of order $n$ is a Baer subplane exactly when the order of the ambient plane is the square of the subplane's order, i.e., $n = m^2$. This connects the geometric property of being Baer to a purely arithmetic condition on the orders.
