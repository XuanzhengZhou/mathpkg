An affine plane is an incidence structure satisfying three axioms: any two points determine a unique line; through any point not on a given line there is exactly one parallel to that line (Playfair's axiom); and there exist three non-collinear points. Every affine plane can be obtained from a projective plane by removing one line and all its incident points, and conversely every such derived structure is an affine plane. Lines that do not intersect are called parallel, and parallelism is an equivalence relation on the lines of an affine plane.
