Lemma 4.2 establishes that the fixed structure of a collineation is well-behaved: the set of all fixed points and fixed lines is closed under taking joins and intersections. If the collineation additionally fixes a quadrangle (four points, no three collinear), then the fixed configuration is actually a subplane. This is the foundation for studying planar collineations and Baer involutions.
