A correlation $\theta$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is a bijection that maps points to lines and lines to points while preserving incidence. In other words, for any point $P$ and line $l$, $P$ is incident with $l$ if and only if $l^\theta$ is incident with $P^\theta$. The product (composition) of any two correlations is a collineation. Notably, a correlation does not exist for affine planes, since parallel lines would not have proper pre-images.
