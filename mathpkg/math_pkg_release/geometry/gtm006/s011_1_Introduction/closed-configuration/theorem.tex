A subset $\mathcal{C}$ of the points and lines of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is called a \textit{closed configuration} if it satisfies: for any two distinct points $A, B \in \mathcal{C}$, the line $AB \in \mathcal{C}$, and dually, for any two distinct lines $l, m \in \mathcal{C}$, the point $lm \in \mathcal{C}$. Equivalently, a closed configuration is closed under the operations of joining points and intersecting lines.
