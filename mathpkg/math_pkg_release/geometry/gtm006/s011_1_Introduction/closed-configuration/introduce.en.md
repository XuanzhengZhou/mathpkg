A closed configuration $\mathcal{C}$ in a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is a subset of points and lines that is closed under the geometric operations of joining two points (obtaining the line through them) and intersecting two lines (obtaining their common point). This concept generalizes the notion of a subplane, as every subplane is a closed configuration, but a closed configuration need not contain a quadrangle and therefore need not be a subplane.
