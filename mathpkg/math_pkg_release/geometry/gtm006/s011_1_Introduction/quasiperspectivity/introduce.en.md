A quasiperspectivity is a collineation $\alpha$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ whose fixed structure $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is a closed Baer subset (which by Theorem 4.4 is either a Baer subplane or a point-line flag). This generalizes the notion of a perspectivity: any perspectivity is a quasiperspectivity, and any non-planar quasiperspectivity is necessarily a perspectivity. Quasiperspectivities are a special family of collineations studied in detail throughout this chapter.
