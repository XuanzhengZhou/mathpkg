A Baer collineation is a special type of planar collineation whose fixed configuration $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is precisely a Baer subplane of the ambient projective plane $\mathcal{P}$. Baer collineations are important in the classification of collineations and play a role in the study of finite projective planes, where Baer subplanes appear naturally as the fixed structures of certain involutions.
