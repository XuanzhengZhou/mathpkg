An involution is a collineation $\alpha$ of a projective (or affine) plane satisfying $\alpha^2 = 1$, the identity collineation, with $\alpha \neq 1$. Involutions are fundamental in the classification of collineations: a classic result (Theorem 4.3) states that every involution is either planar (its fixed structure is a subplane) or a perspectivity (fixing a line pointwise and a point linewise).
