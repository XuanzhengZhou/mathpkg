A collineation is an isomorphism of a projective or affine plane onto itself, also called an automorphism of the plane. The set of all collineations of a given plane $\mathcal{B}$ forms a group under composition, denoted by $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$ and called the full collineation group. Any subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$ is referred to as a collineation group of the plane. The identity mapping is denoted by $1$.
