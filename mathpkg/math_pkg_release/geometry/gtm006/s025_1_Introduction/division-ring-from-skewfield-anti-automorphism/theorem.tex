Let $F$ be a skewfield with centre $Z$ (so $Z$ is a field), and let $\theta$ be an anti-automorphism of $F$ of finite order $m$. Let $a, b$ be elements of $F$ satisfying:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $F$ has finite dimension over $Z$ and $xx^{\theta} \in Z$ for all $x \in F$;
\item[(2)] $a = x^{1+\theta} + xb$ has no solution for $x \in F$.
\end{enumerate}

Define $D$ as the set of all ordered pairs $(x, y) \in F \times F$, written as $x + \lambda y$, with addition defined componentwise and multiplication defined by:

$$(x + \lambda y)(z + \lambda t) = (xz + aty^{\theta}) + \lambda(zy + x^{\theta}t + y^{\theta}bt).$$

Then $D$ is a division ring (not necessarily associative) of dimension $2k$ over $K$, where $k = [F : K]$.
