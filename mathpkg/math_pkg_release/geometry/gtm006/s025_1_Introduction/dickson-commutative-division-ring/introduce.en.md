The Dickson commutative division ring is a construction that produces a commutative but non-associative division ring from a finite field of odd characteristic. The construction is a two-dimensional vector space over $F = GF(p^n)$ with a twisted multiplication involving a non-square element $a$ and a field automorphism $	heta$. This provides an important example of a division ring that is commutative (so it is "close" to being a field) yet fails to be associative, demonstrating that commutativity alone does not force associativity in the context of division rings. The construction parallels that of Section 4 but with a symmetric multiplication formula that guarantees commutativity.
