An André quasifield is a quasifield constructed from a field $F$ by twisting the multiplication using field automorphisms. The construction depends on a mapping $\phi$ from the image $N$ of the norm-type homomorphism $
u$ into the automorphism group $\Gamma$. The resulting system $F_{\phi}$ always satisfies the left distributive law. André quasifields generalize Hall quasifields and produce the André planes, an important class of translation planes. Finite André planes include all finite Hall planes as a special case.
