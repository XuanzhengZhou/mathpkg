Let $H$ be a Hall quasifield over a field $F$ defined by the irreducible quadratic $f(s) = s^2 - as - b$. If $H$ is associative, then the following conditions hold for all $x \in F$, $x \neq 0$:

$$bx = bx^{-1},$$

$$ax = a.$$

Consequently:
\begin{itemize}
\item If $F = GF(2)$, associativity is possible.
\item If $|F| > 2$ and $H$ is associative, then $a = 0$ and $x^2 = 1$ for all $x \in F$, $x \neq 0$. The only field with these properties is $F = GF(3)$, and the only irreducible quadratic over $GF(3)$ with $a = 0$ is $f(s) = s^2 + 1$.
\end{itemize}
