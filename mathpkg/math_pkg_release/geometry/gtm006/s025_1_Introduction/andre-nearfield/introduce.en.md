An André nearfield is simply an André quasifield whose multiplication is associative. Since an associative quasifield is precisely a nearfield, André nearfields form a subclass of André quasifields and provide examples of proper nearfields (nearfields that are not division rings). The associativity condition translates algebraically into the requirement that the defining map $\phi$ be a group homomorphism, which in the finite case is always achievable when suitable subfields exist.
