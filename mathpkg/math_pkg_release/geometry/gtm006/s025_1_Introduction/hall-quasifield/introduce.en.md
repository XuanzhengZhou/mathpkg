A Hall quasifield is a construction that produces a quasifield (and hence a translation plane) from any field $F$ together with an irreducible quadratic polynomial $f(s) = s^2 - as - b$ over $F$. The quasifield $H$ is defined as a two-dimensional right vector space over $F$ with a non-standard multiplication rule that encodes the quadratic relation $f(\alpha) = 0$ for elements not in the base field $F$. This construction generalizes to produce the Hall planes, which are among the most important examples of non-desarguesian projective planes. Hall quasifields have the property that $F$ is contained in their kernel, making them two-dimensional over their kernel.
