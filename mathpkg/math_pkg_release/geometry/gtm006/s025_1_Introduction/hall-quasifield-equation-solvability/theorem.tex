Let $H$ be the Hall quasifield defined over a field $F$ with irreducible quadratic $f(s) = s^2 - as - b$.

For any $p, q, z, t \in F$ with $(z, t) \neq (0, 0)$, the equations:

$$(x + \lambda y)(z + \lambda t) = p + \lambda q$$

have a unique solution $(x, y) \in F^2$ except when either:
\begin{itemize}
\item $z = 0$ and $p = 0$, or
\item $t = 0$ and $q = 0$, or
\item $pz^{-1} = qt^{-1}$.
\end{itemize}

Consequently, $H$ is a weak quasifield.
