This theorem establishes the first fundamental link between the additive algebraic structure of a planar ternary ring $(R,T)$ and the geometric transitivity properties of the projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ it coordinatizes. Specifically, it shows that associativity of addition in a linear PTR corresponds exactly to the existence of sufficiently many elations with axis $[\infty]$ — that is, $((\infty), [\infty])$-transitivity. This is the geometric counterpart of the algebraic property that $(R,+)$ is a group. The proof is constructive in the forward direction, exhibiting explicit elations $\phi_a: (x,y) \mapsto (x, y+a)$, and uses Theorem 4.29 together with Theorem 6.1 for the converse.
