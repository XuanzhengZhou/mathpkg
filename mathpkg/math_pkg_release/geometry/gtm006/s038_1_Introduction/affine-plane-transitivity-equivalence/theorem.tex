Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite affine plane and $\Pi$ be a collineation group of $\mathcal{A}$. Then the following statements are equivalent:

(i) $\Pi$ is transitive on the affine lines of $\mathcal{A}$.

(ii) $\Pi$ is transitive on the affine points of $\mathcal{A}$ and $\Pi$ is transitive on the special points of $\mathcal{A}$.

(iii) $\Pi$ is transitive on the affine flags of $\mathcal{A}$.
