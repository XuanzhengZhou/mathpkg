Let $\Pi$ be a doubly transitive permutation group on a set $\mathcal{S}$ of $n+1$ elements, where $n$ is odd. If, for every element $a$ of $\mathcal{S}$, $\Pi_a$ contains a subgroup $\Gamma_a$ of even order which is a Frobenius group on $\mathcal{S} \setminus \{a\}$, then $\Pi_a$ is primitive on $\mathcal{S} \setminus \{a\}$.
