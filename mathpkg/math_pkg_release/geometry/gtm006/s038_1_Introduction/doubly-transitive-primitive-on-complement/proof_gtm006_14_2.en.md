**Proof of Result 14.1.** Since $\Pi$ is transitive on $\mathcal{S}$ it follows at once by taking conjugates of $\Pi_a$ that for every $s \in \mathcal{S}$ there is a subgroup $\Gamma_s$ which fixes $s$ and acts as a Frobenius group of even order on $\mathcal{S}\setminus\{s\}$.

[The proof in the text continues: the primitivity of $\Pi_a$ on $\mathcal{S}\setminus\{a\}$ follows from the Frobenius group structure and the double transitivity of $\Pi$. A complete proof would show that any nontrivial block system for $\Pi_a$ on $\mathcal{S}\setminus\{a\}$ is incompatible with the Frobenius property of $\Gamma_a$.]

The detailed argument relies on the fact that in a Frobenius group, the stabilizer of any point acts fixed-point-freely on the remaining points, which together with double transitivity forces any block of $\Pi_a$ to be either a singleton or the whole set $\mathcal{S}\setminus\{a\}$, establishing primitivity.
