**Proof of Theorem 14.7.** Let $\Pi$ split the points of $l_{\infty}$ into $s$ orbits.

**(1)** Suppose that $\Pi$ is transitive on the affine points of $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{P}^{l_{\infty}}$. Then the total number of point orbits of $\Pi$, considered as a collineation group of $\mathcal{P}$, is $s+1$ (the $s$ orbits on $l_{\infty}$ plus the one orbit of affine points). By Theorem 13.4, $\Pi$ has exactly $s+1$ line orbits in $\mathcal{P}$.

As one line orbit is $\{l_{\infty}\}$, $\Pi$ has exactly $s$ orbits of affine lines. If $X, Y$ are special points in different orbits of $\Pi$, then $\Pi$ cannot map an affine line through $X$ onto an affine line through $Y$. Thus, since the number of affine line orbits equals the number of special point orbits, for each special point $D$ the subgroup $\Pi_D$ must be transitive on the affine lines through $D$.

Conversely, if for every $D \in l_{\infty}$ the subgroup $\Pi_D$ is transitive on the affine lines through $D$, then $\Pi$ has at most $s$ affine line orbits. By Theorem 13.4, $\Pi$ has at most $s+1$ point orbits in $\mathcal{P}$, so $\Pi$ has exactly one orbit of affine points. $\square$
