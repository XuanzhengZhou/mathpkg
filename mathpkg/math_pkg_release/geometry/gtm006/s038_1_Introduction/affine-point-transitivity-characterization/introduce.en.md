Theorem 14.7 gives a useful characterization: a collineation group is transitive on affine points if and only if each stabilizer of a point at infinity acts transitively on the affine lines through that point. The proof uses Theorem 13.4, which bounds the number of line orbits in terms of point orbits, and the observation that $l_{\infty}$ forms a single line orbit.
