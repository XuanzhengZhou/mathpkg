**Proof of Lemma 14.12.** Let $2^u \| n+1$, $2^v \| k$ (where $|\Pi_{C,l}| = k$ for any affine flag $C,l$), and $|\Gamma| = 2^w$.

**(i)** Since $\Gamma$ fixes a subplane pointwise, the fixed-point structure of $\Gamma$ determines $w$ in terms of $u$ and $v$. In particular, $\Gamma$ is not a Sylow 2-subgroup of $\Pi_{A,B,m}$ for appropriate choices of points $A,B$ and line $m$. There exists a 2-subgroup $\Gamma^*$ of $\Pi_{A,B,m}$ which contains $\Gamma$ as a normal subgroup of index $2$. An element of $\Gamma^* \setminus \Gamma$ acts on $\mathcal{B}$ as an involutory homology fixing the given affine flag.

The key counting argument: using the formulas from the Corollary to Theorem 14.6, the index of $\Gamma$ in a suitable stabilizer is shown to be $2$, yielding the required involution.

**(ii)** Let $A,B$ be special points of $\mathcal{B}$, with $l$ an affine line through $B$, and suppose there is an involutory homology $\alpha \in \Sigma$ fixing $A,B,l$. Let $m$ be any other affine line through $B$. By a similar argument, the 2-subgroup structure forces the existence of a subgroup $\Gamma^*$ of $\Pi_{A,B,m}$ containing $\Gamma$ as a normal subgroup of index $2$. An element in $\Gamma^* \setminus \Gamma$ acts on $\mathcal{B}$ as an involutory homology fixing $A,B,m$, establishing the propagation property. $\square$
