Theorem 14.10 establishes a strong constraint on the relative orders of a projective plane and any of its 2-subplanes: the order of the larger plane must be a power of the order of the subplane, with the exponent being a power of 2. This result plays a key role in the final stage of Wagner's Theorem proof, where the reduction to a minimal 2-subplane allows one to bootstrap from a smaller to a larger plane.
