Theorem 14.9 completes the odd-order case of Wagner's Theorem. Under the assumption that $\Pi$ is transitive on affine lines but $n$ is odd and NOT a prime power, the group-theoretic machinery of Results 14.1–14.4 is deployed: the stabilizer of a point at infinity acts as a doubly transitive group, and the subgroup of elations with a common axis forms a Frobenius group whose kernel is normal. Result 14.4 then forces $n$ to be a prime power, completing the proof when combined with Lemma 14.8.
