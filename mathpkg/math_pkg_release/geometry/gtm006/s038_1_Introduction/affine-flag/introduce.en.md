An affine flag is an incident pair consisting of an affine point and an affine line in an affine plane. This notion is used in Theorem 14.6 to characterize collineation groups that are transitive on the affine lines of a finite affine plane: such a group is also transitive on affine points and on affine flags. The stabilizer of an affine flag plays a central role in the counting formulas used throughout the proof of Wagner's Theorem.
