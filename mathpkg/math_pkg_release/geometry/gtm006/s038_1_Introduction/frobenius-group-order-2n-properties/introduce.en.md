Result 14.2 describes structural properties of Frobenius permutation groups of order $2n$ on $n$ points, where $n$ is odd. Part (i) asserts the existence of an involution swapping any two distinct points. Part (ii) states that any subgroup containing $r \geq 1$ involutions has order exactly $2r$, establishing a one-to-one correspondence between involutions and points of a certain orbit. This result, proved in the appendix by Wagner, is crucial to the group-theoretic machinery underlying Wagner's Theorem.
