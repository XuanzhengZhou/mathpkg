**Proof of the Ostrom-Wagner Theorem (Theorem 14.13).** Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a finite projective plane with a collineation group $\Pi$ doubly transitive on the points of $\mathcal{P}$.

By Exercise 13.5, $\Pi$ is also doubly transitive on the lines of $\mathcal{P}$. Hence, for any line $l \in \mathcal{P}$, the stabilizer $\Pi_l$ is transitive on the affine lines of $\mathcal{P}^l$ (the affine plane obtained by removing $l$).

By Wagner's Theorem (Theorem 14.11), $\mathcal{P}^l$ is a translation plane with respect to $l$. Since this holds for every line $l$, $\mathcal{P}$ is a Moufang plane (each line serves as a translation axis).

By the Corollary to Theorem 6.20, a finite Moufang plane is desarguesian. Therefore $\mathcal{P}$ is desarguesian.

That $\Pi$ contains the little projective group follows from the fact that, by Wagner's Theorem, $\Pi$ contains all elations with axis $l$ for any line $l$ in $\mathcal{P}$, and the little projective group is generated by these elations. $\square$
