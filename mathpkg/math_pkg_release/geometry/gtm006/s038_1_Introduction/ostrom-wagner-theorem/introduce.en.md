The Ostrom-Wagner Theorem (Theorem 14.13) is a celebrated consequence of Wagner's Theorem: any finite projective plane admitting a collineation group that acts doubly transitively on points must be desarguesian. The proof uses Wagner's Theorem on the derived affine plane together with the fact that doubly transitive point action implies transitivity on affine lines. The plane is then a Moufang plane, and a finite Moufang plane is desarguesian by the Corollary to Theorem 6.20. Moreover, the group must contain the little projective group.
