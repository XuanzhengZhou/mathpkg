A minimal $2$-subplane is a $2$-subplane that contains no strictly smaller $2$-subplane with respect to the same collineation group. In a finite projective plane, minimal $2$-subplanes always exist (by finiteness, one can take a $2$-subplane of smallest order). In the proof of Wagner's Theorem, the minimal $2$-subplane $\mathcal{Q}$ provides the foundational case for an inductive argument: if $\mathcal{Q}$ is a proper subplane, properties of the collineation group on the larger plane are deduced from properties on $\mathcal{Q}$ via Lemmas 14.12 and 14.13.
