Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite affine plane of odd order $n$, let $\mathcal{Q}$ be a minimal $2$-subplane with respect to the collineation group $\Pi$, let $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{Q}^{l_\infty}$, let $\Gamma$ be a maximal $2$-subgroup of $\Pi$ whose fixed elements are the points and lines of $\mathcal{Q}$, and let $\Sigma$ be the subgroup of $\Pi$ leaving $\mathcal{Q}$ invariant. Regarding $\Sigma$ as a collineation group of $\mathcal{B}$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] for every affine flag in $\mathcal{B}$ there is an involutory homology in $\Sigma$ fixing that flag.
\item[(ii)] if $A, B$ are special points of $\mathcal{B}$ with $l$ an affine line of $\mathcal{B}$ through $B$ and if there is an involutory homology in $\Sigma$ fixing $A, B, l$, then if $m$ is any other affine line of $\mathcal{B}$ through $B$ there exists an involutory homology in $\Sigma$ fixing $A, B, m$.
\end{enumerate}
