Lemma 14.12 is a technical lemma in the proof of Wagner's Theorem that establishes the existence of involutory homologies with specific properties in the subgroup $\Sigma$ that stabilizes the minimal $2$-subplane $\mathcal{Q}$. Part (i) guarantees that every affine flag (incident point-line pair) in $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{Q}^{l_\infty}$ is fixed by some involutory homology. Part (ii) provides a "propagation" property: if an involutory homology fixes two special points $A, B$ and an affine line $l$ through $B$, then for any other affine line $m$ through $B$, there exists an involutory homology fixing $A, B, m$. The proof uses Sylow $2$-subgroup arguments and properties of Frobenius groups.
