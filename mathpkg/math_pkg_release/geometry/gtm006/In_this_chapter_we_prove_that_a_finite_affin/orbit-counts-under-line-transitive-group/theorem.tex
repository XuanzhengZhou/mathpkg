Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite affine plane of order $n$ with a collineation group $\Pi$ transitive on the affine lines. If $C, l$ is any affine flag of $\mathcal{A}$ and $D$ is any point of $l_\infty$ then, putting $|\Pi_{C, l}| = k$, we have:
$$|\Pi| = n^2(n+1)k,\quad |\Pi_D| = n^2k,\quad |\Pi_l| = nk,\quad |\Pi_C| = (n+1)k,\quad |\Pi_{C, D}| = k.$$
