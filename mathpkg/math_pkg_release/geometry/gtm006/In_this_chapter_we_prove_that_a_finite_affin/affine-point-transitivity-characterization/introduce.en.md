Theorem 14.7 gives a characterization of affine point-transitivity in terms of the action on lines through special points. Specifically, a collineation group $\Pi$ is transitive on affine points if and only if for each special point $D$ on the line at infinity $l_\infty$, the stabilizer $\Pi_D$ acts transitively on the set of affine lines passing through $D$ (i.e., on one parallel class). The proof uses orbit counting, splitting the points of $l_\infty$ into orbits under $\Pi$ and relating point orbits to line orbits via Theorem 13.4.
