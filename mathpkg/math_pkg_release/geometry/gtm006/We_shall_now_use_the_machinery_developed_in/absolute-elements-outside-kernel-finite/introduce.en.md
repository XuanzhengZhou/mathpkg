This exercise concerns the free closure $\mathcal{P} = \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A})$ of an o.f. configuration $\mathcal{A}$, equipped with a polarity $\theta$. The result states that only finitely many absolute elements of $\theta$ can lie outside the kernel configuration $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{A})$. In other words, when passing to the free closure, polarities have their absolute elements almost entirely confined to the kernel.
