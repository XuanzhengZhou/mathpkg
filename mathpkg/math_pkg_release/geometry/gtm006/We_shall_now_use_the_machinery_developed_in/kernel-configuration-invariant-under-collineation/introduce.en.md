As an immediate consequence of Lemma 11.16, the kernel configuration $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is invariant under every collineation $\alpha$ of $\mathcal{P}$. That is, applying any automorphism to $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ yields $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ itself. This follows because $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ is confined, and by Lemma 11.16 its image under any collineation remains confined.
