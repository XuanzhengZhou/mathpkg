Theorem 6.15 restates Theorem 6.14 in geometric language: a division ring $R$ with the right inverse property has the full inverse property (i.e., also has LIP) precisely when the associated projective plane is a Moufang plane. This follows because RIP already implies that every line through $(\infty)$ is a translation line; adding LIP gives a third non-concurrent translation line, which by Theorem 4.20 forces every line to be a translation line — the defining property of a Moufang plane.
