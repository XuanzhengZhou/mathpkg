Theorem 6.14 provides a geometric characterization of the left inverse property (LIP) for a division ring $R$ that already has the right inverse property (RIP). The projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ coordinatized by $R$ is $((0,0),[0,0])$-transitive exactly when $R$ also satisfies LIP. This connects an algebraic property of the planar ternary ring to a specific point-line transitivity condition in the associated projective plane, and is a key stepping stone to the Moufang plane characterization in Theorem 6.15.
