An alternative division ring (sometimes called an alternative field) is a division ring in which the two alternative laws hold: $x(xy) = x^2y$ and $(yx)x = yx^2$ for all $x, y$. These identities are weaker than associativity but stronger than the inverse property. Crucially, any division ring possessing the inverse property (IP) is alternative (Lemma 6.13), connecting the algebraic study of loops and planar ternary rings to the classification of projective planes via the Artin-Zorn theorem and Moufang planes.
