Gleason's theorem characterizes finite desarguesian projective planes in terms of the existence of elations. If a finite projective plane admits a collineation group such that every point is the centre of a non-trivial elation and every line is the axis of a non-trivial elation, then the plane must be desarguesian. Moreover, the collineation group contains the little projective group.
