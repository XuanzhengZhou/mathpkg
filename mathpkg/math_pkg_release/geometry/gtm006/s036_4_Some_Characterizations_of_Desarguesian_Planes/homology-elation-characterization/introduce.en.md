If every point of a finite projective plane is the centre of a non-trivial perspectivity and every line is the axis of a non-trivial perspectivity (in some collineation group $\Pi$), then either $\Pi$ already contains an elation, or a contradiction arises from constructing a polarity without absolute points. In either case the plane must be desarguesian.
