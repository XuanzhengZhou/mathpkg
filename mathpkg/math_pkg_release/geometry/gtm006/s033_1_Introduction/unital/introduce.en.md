A unital is a combinatorial incidence structure that arises naturally from a unitary polarity in a finite projective plane. It consists of the $n^{3/2}+1$ absolute points of the polarity together with the collection of non-absolute lines. Unitals are characterized by the property that every line of the plane meets the unital's point set in either 1 or $\sqrt{n}+1$ points, and through each absolute point there passes exactly one tangent (a line meeting the unital in a single point). Unitals play a role in finite geometry analogous to that of ovals, representing the geometric signature of the maximal Baer bound.
