When a polarity $\alpha$ attains the upper Baer bound $a(\alpha) = n^{3/2}+1$, the absolute points and non-absolute lines together form a unital — a combinatorial incidence structure that is the characteristic geometric footprint of unitary polarities. This generalizes the classical observation that unitary polarities in pappian planes produce unitals, establishing that the unital structure is forced by the maximality of $a(\alpha)$ alone, independent of any pappian or desarguesian hypothesis.
