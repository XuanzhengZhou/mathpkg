For any polarity $\alpha$ of a finite projective plane of order $n$, the number $a(\alpha)$ of absolute points is bounded below by $n+1$ and above by $n^{3/2}+1$. This result, due to Baer and Seib, generalizes the earlier observation from Chapter II that for orthogonal polarities $a(\alpha)=n+1$ and for unitary polarities $a(\alpha)=n^{3/2}+1$. The Baer–Seib bounds hold for all polarities, regardless of whether the plane is pappian or desarguesian.
