An oval is the natural non-desarguesian generalization of a conic in a finite projective plane. It is a set of $n+1$ points with the property that no three are collinear — the combinatorial essence of a conic, stripped of its algebraic definition via quadratic forms. In a pappian (desarguesian) projective plane, every conic is an oval, and by Segre's Theorem the converse holds for odd order. Ovals appear as the absolute point set of a polarity attaining the minimal Baer bound $a(\alpha)=n+1$ when the plane order is odd.
