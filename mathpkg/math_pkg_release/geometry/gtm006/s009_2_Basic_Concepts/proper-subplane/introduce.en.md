A proper subplane is a subplane strictly smaller than the containing projective plane. The study of proper subplanes is central to understanding the structure of projective planes. Bruck's Theorem (Theorem 3.7) sharply restricts the possible orders of proper subplanes: if the containing plane has order $n$ and the subplane has order $m$, then either $n = m^2$ or $n \geq m^2 + m$. This result has important consequences for the lattice of substructures and for classification problems in finite projective geometry.
