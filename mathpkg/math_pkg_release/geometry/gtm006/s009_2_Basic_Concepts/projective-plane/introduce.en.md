A projective plane is the central object of study in projective geometry. It consists of points and lines together with an incidence relation satisfying three axioms: any two points determine a unique line, any two lines meet at a unique point, and there exist four points in general position (no three collinear). This minimal set of axioms gives rise to a remarkably rich structure with deep combinatorial, algebraic, and geometric consequences.
