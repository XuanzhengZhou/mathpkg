The Principle of Duality is a meta-theorem that doubles the productivity of projective geometry: every theorem proved about points and lines immediately yields a dual theorem obtained by swapping the two roles. Since the axioms of a projective plane are self-dual (as established by the construction of the dual plane), the dual of any valid statement is also valid. This principle is foundational and is used throughout the entire theory of projective planes.
