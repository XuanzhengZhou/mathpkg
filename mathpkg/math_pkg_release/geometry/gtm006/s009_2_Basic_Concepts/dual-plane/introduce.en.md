The dual plane is obtained from a projective plane by interchanging the roles of points and lines while preserving the incidence relation. The fact that $\mathcal{P}^*$ is again a projective plane (as a consequence of Lemma 3.1) establishes the self-dual nature of the projective plane axioms. Together with the observation $(\mathcal{P}^*)^* = \mathcal{P}$, this duality is the foundation for the Principle of Duality, one of the most powerful tools in projective geometry.
