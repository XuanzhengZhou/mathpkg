The order of a finite projective plane is its most important numerical invariant. It determines the sizes of all lines and pencils, as well as the total numbers of points and lines ($n^2 + n + 1$). By Theorem 3.5, the order satisfies $n \geq 2$. A central problem in finite geometry is determining for which integers $n$ a projective plane of order $n$ exists; all known examples have prime power order, and the Bruck-Ryser Theorem provides a necessary condition excluding infinitely many composite orders.
