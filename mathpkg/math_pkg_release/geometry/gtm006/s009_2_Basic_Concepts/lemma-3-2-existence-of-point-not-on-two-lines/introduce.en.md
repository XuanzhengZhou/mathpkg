Lemma 3.2 guarantees that given any two lines in a projective plane, one can find a point lying on neither of them. This seemingly simple existence result is crucial in the proof of Theorem 3.4, where such a point is used to establish a one-to-one correspondence between the point sets of any two lines. The lemma relies on the existence of a quadrangle and the non-degeneracy axiom.
