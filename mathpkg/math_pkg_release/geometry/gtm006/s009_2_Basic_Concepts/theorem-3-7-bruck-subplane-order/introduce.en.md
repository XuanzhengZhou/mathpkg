Bruck's Theorem provides a severe restriction on the possible orders of proper subplanes of a finite projective plane. If a plane of order $n$ contains a proper subplane of order $m$, then either $n$ is exactly $m^2$ (the "square case", as exemplified by $\text{PG}(2, q^2)$ containing $\text{PG}(2, q)$), or $n$ is at least $m^2 + m$. This result is proved by a simple incidence counting argument and has far-reaching consequences for the classification and construction of finite projective planes.
