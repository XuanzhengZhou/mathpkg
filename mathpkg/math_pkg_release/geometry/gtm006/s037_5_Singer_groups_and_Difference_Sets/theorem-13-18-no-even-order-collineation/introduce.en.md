If a projective plane has order $n \equiv 2 \pmod{4}$ with $n > 2$, then it cannot admit any collineation of even order. The proof uses a clever matrix construction: an elation of order 2 forces a certain matrix equation $BB' = ntI$ to hold, which can only be satisfied when $n = 2$.
