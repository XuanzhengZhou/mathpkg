This non-existence theorem states that if a finite projective plane has order $n \equiv 2 \pmod{4}$ and admits any collineation of even order, then necessarily $n = 2$. The proof uses combinatorial matrix arguments, showing that the existence of an even-order collineation (hence an involution) forces the plane to be the Fano plane. Since $n$ cannot be a square when $n \equiv 2 \pmod{4}$, the involution cannot be a Baer involution, and must be an elation, leading to strong constraints via incidence matrix equations.
