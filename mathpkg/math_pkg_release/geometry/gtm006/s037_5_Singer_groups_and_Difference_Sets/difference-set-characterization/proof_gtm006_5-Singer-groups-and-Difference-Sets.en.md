Since $\Pi$ is regular on points, $X$ and $X^\beta$ are distinct, so there is a unique line $m^\alpha$ joining them. By transitivity on lines, $m^\alpha$ contains $X$, so $X^{\beta\alpha^{-1}}$ and $X^{\alpha^{-1}}$ are on $m$. Thus $\alpha^{-1} = \delta_2$ and $\beta\alpha^{-1} = \delta_1$ are both in $\mathcal{D}$, giving $\beta = \delta_1\delta_2^{-1}$.
