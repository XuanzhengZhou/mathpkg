A difference set encodes the entire incidence structure of a finite projective plane in a single subset of the Singer group. When the Singer group is abelian and written additively, the condition becomes: every non-zero group element has a unique representation as $d_1 - d_2$ with $d_i \in \mathcal{D}$. This is the origin of the name "difference set." The parameters are $(v, k, \lambda) = (n^2+n+1, n+1, 1)$, characterizing a projective plane of order $n$.
