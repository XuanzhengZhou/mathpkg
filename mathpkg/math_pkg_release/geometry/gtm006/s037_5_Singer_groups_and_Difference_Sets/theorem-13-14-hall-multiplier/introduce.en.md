Hall's multiplier theorem states that for any prime $p$ dividing the order $n$ of the plane, the map $\alpha \mapsto \alpha^p$ is a multiplier of the difference set: it sends $\mathcal{D}$ to a translate of itself. This provides a powerful method for constructing difference sets and proving non-existence results for certain orders.
