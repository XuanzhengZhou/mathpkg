The theorem follows immediately from Lemma 11.9, Lemma 11.10, and the Corollary to Theorem 11.8. By Lemma 11.10, any openly finite configuration $\mathcal{A}$ is free equivalent to a canonical configuration whose isomorphism type depends only on its open rank $r_0(\mathcal{A})$ and its core $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{A})$. Lemma 11.9 provides the explicit form of this canonical configuration for the non-degenerate finite open case. By Theorem 11.8 and its Corollary, two openly finite configurations have isomorphic free closures if and only if they are free equivalent. Hence $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A}) \cong \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{B})$ exactly when the invariants $r_0$ and $\mathcal{K}$ coincide.
