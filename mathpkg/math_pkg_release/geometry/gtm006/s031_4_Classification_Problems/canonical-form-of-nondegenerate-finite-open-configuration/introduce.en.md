Lemma 11.9 shows that any non-degenerate finite open configuration can be simplified via free equivalence to a canonical form consisting of a single line with a certain number of points on it, plus two additional points off the line. The number of points on the line is directly tied to the open rank of the configuration. This canonical form is a key ingredient in proving that the isomorphism type of a free closure is determined entirely by the open rank and core.
