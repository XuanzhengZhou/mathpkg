Free equivalence is a relation on configurations in projective planes, defined via chains of isomorphism and free extensions. Two configurations are free equivalent if one can be transformed into the other by a finite sequence of steps, each step either identifying isomorphic copies or adding/removing a free extension. This equivalence is central to the classification of free closures: when configurations are openly finite, their free closures are isomorphic precisely when the configurations are free equivalent.
