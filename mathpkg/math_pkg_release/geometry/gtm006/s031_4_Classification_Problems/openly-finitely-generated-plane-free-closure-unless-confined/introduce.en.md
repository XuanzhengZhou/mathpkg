This corollary rephrases Theorem 11.13 in more conceptual terms. An openly finitely generated projective plane is guaranteed to be a free closure --- the "generic" case --- with the only obstruction being the extreme situation where every single element belongs to some finite confined subconfiguration (equivalently, the plane equals its own core). This provides a practical criterion: to check whether a plane is a free closure, one need only verify that it is not "everywhere confined."
