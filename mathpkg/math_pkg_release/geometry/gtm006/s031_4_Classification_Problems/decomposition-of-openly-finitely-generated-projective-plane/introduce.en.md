Theorem 11.13 addresses the recognition problem: when is an openly finitely generated projective plane itself a free closure? The answer is a clean dichotomy: either the plane coincides with its core (meaning every element belongs to a finite confined subconfiguration), or it can be expressed as the free closure of a strictly smaller openly finite configuration. This result bridges the gap between the abstract notion of being openly finitely generated and the concrete construction of free closures.
