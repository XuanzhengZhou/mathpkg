A closed configuration is an incidence structure of points and lines that satisfies the first two axioms of a projective plane: any two distinct points determine a unique line, and any two distinct lines intersect in a unique point. The third axiom (existence of four points with no three collinear) is not required, so closed configurations include both projective planes and degenerate planes.
