Theorem 6.6 builds on the linearity assumption of the coordinatizing planar ternary ring $(R, T)$ and establishes a second transitivity equivalence. Given that $(R, T)$ is already linear, the additional algebraic properties of associative multiplication together with the left distributive law are exactly equivalent to the projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ being $((\infty), [0, 0])$-transitive. This complements Theorem 6.5 by characterizing a different transitivity condition—one centered at the point at infinity and the line $[0, 0]$—in terms of both multiplicative and additive-multiplicative algebraic structure.
