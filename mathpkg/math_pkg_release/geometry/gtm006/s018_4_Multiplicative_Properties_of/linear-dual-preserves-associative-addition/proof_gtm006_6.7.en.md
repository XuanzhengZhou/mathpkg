**Proof.** By Theorem 6.2, $\mathcal{P}$ is $((\infty), [\infty])$-transitive. However, if $\alpha$ is any $((\infty), [\infty])$-elation of $\mathcal{P}$ then $\alpha$ fixes every line through $(\infty)$ and every point on $[\infty]$ and so, considered as a collineation of $\mathcal{P}^*$, $\alpha$ fixes every point on $[\infty]'$ and every line on $(\infty)'$, i.e. $\alpha$ is a $((\infty), [\infty])$-elation on $\mathcal{P}^*$. Hence $\mathcal{P}^*$ is $((\infty), [\infty])$-transitive, and applying Theorem 6.2 again yields that $(R, T')$ is linear with associative addition. $\square$
