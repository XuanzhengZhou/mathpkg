Exercise 6.1 asks the reader to prove that a quasifield $(R, T)$ is a nearfield (i.e., has associative multiplication) if and only if the corresponding projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is $((0), [0])$-transitive. This exercise synthesizes the results of Theorems 6.5 and the definition of a nearfield, reinforcing the fundamental correspondence between algebraic properties of the coordinatizing ternary ring and geometric transitivity conditions in the projective plane.
