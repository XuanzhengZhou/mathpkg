A closed configuration is an incidence structure of points and lines satisfying the first two axioms of a projective plane: any two points determine a unique line, and any two lines meet at a unique point. Every projective plane is a closed configuration, but a closed configuration need not be a projective plane. The additional non-degeneracy condition (axiom (iii)) separates genuine projective planes from degenerate ones.
