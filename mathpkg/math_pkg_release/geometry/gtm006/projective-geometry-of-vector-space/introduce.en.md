The projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of a vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$ is defined as the lattice of all subspaces of $V$, ordered by containment. This construction provides the algebraic foundation for classical projective geometry, where points correspond to 1-dimensional subspaces and lines to 2-dimensional subspaces. The incidence relation is simply containment (or reverse containment) between subspaces.
