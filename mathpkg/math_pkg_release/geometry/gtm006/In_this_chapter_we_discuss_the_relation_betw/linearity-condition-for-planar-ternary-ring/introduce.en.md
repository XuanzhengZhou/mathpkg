Theorem 6.1 provides a geometric characterization of when a planar ternary ring $(R,T)$ is linear.
The condition is stated in terms of a Desargues-like configuration: two triangles whose corresponding
vertices lie on three lines $x=0$, $x=u$, $x=v$ through a common point. If two pairs of corresponding
sides intersect on the line at infinity $[\infty]$, then linearity of $(R,T)$ is equivalent to the
property that the third pair of corresponding sides both pass (or both fail to pass) through the
point $(0)$ on $[\infty]$. This theorem links the algebraic notion of linearity of the ternary
operation to a purely geometric incidence condition in the coordinatized plane.
