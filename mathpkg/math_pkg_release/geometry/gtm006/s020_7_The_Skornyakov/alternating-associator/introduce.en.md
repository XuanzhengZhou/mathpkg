In an alternative division ring, the associator $[x,y,z]$ is an alternating function of its three arguments: permuting the arguments changes the sign by the sign of the permutation. This implies the associator vanishes whenever two arguments coincide. This property is fundamental to the theory of alternative rings and is used crucially at the end of the Skornyakov-San Soucie proof and in the proof of Theorem 6.17.
