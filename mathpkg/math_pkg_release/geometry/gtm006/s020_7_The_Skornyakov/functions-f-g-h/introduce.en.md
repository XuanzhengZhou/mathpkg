The functions $f$, $g$, and $h$ are carefully constructed four-variable identities involving the associator that vanish identically in any ring (not necessarily associative). They were introduced by Skornyakov and San Soucie as the key algebraic tools to prove that the right inverse property forces alternativity. Each function encodes a specific combination of associators that telescopes to zero, allowing the manipulation of nested associator expressions throughout the proof.
