The identity $[[a,b],a,b] = 0$ states that the associator of the commutator $[a,b]$ with $a$ and $b$ always vanishes in a division ring with RIP. This is a crucial structural result obtained midway through the Skornyakov-San Soucie proof. It shows that commutators behave particularly well with respect to the associator and is used extensively in the subsequent Steps (A)-(E) to establish that the commutator $q = [a,b]$ is central.
