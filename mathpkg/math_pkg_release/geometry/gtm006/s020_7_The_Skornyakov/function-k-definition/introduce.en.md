The function $k(w,x,y,z)$ measures the failure of the associator $[wx, y, z]$ to behave like a derivation with respect to left multiplication by $w$ on the first argument. Defined as $[wx, y, z] - [x, y, z]w - x[w, y, z]$, this four-variable function is additive in all arguments and plays a role analogous to the functions $f, g, h$ from the proof of Theorem 6.16, but is adapted for proving that alternative division rings have the inverse property (Theorem 6.17).
