**Theorem.** Let $R = \{0, 1, \dots, n-1\}$ and suppose $\{A_1, A_2, \dots, A_{n-1}\}$ is a complete set of mutually orthogonal $n \times n$ Latin squares in normal form, with $A_1$ having $(0, 1, \dots, n-1)^\top$ as its left column. Label the squares as $\{x\}$ so that $\{x\}$ is the square with entry $x$ in the $(1,0)$-position, for $x = 1, 2, \dots, n-1$. Define a ternary operation $T$ on $R$ by:

$$T(x, i, j) = \text{the }(i,j)\text{-th entry in }\{x\}, \quad \text{for } x = 1, \dots, n-1,$$

and $T(0, i, j) = j$ for all $i, j$. Then $(R, T)$ is a planar ternary ring.
