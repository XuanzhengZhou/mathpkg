Two Latin squares of the same order are orthogonal if, when superimposed, every ordered pair of symbols occurs exactly once across all $n^2$ positions. Equivalently, the superposition of the two squares produces a complete set of all $n^2$ ordered pairs from the symbol set. Orthogonality of Latin squares is the central notion in the theory of mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS), which forms a bridge between combinatorial design theory and finite geometry—specifically, the coordinatization of finite projective planes via planar ternary rings.
