In the Hall coordinatization method, a planar ternary ring $(R, T)$ is called \textbf{linear} if the ternary operation satisfies
$$T(x, m, k) = xm + k$$
where addition $+$ and multiplication $\cdot$ are binary operations defined on $R$ (and where the expression $xm + k$ is evaluated as $(x \cdot m) + k$ under the usual precedence rules).

Once a quadrangle $O, X, Y, I$ is chosen, a given projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is coordinatizable by a linear Hall PTR if and only if the PTR obtained by the standard coordinatization method is linear.
