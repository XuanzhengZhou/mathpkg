A left vector space over a skewfield $K$ generalizes the usual notion of a vector space over a field to the setting where the scalars form a division ring (skewfield) rather than a commutative field. The key distinction is that scalar multiplication must always be written on the left, since multiplication in $K$ need not be commutative. All the familiar axioms of associativity and distributivity hold, but careful attention must be paid to the order of multiplication. This concept is fundamental to the study of projective planes coordinatized by non-commutative division rings.
