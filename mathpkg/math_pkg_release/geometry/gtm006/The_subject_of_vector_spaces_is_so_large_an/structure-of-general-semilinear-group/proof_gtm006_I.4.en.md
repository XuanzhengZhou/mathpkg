The proof is not given explicitly in this section. The result follows from the observation that for any semi-linear transformation $\phi$ with automorphism $\alpha$, the map $\psi \colon v \mapsto (v^{\phi})^{\alpha^{-1}}$ (acting coordinate-wise) is a linear transformation. Hence $\phi$ decomposes as a linear transformation composed with an automorphism-induced map, yielding the factorization $\Gamma L(V) = A \cdot GL(V)$. Normality of $GL(V)$ follows since conjugation by an element of $A$ sends a linear transformation to another linear transformation with conjugately scaled matrix entries.
