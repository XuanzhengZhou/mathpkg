A linear transformation over a skewfield is a semi-linear transformation whose associated automorphism is the identity. In other words, it is an additive map that truly commutes with scalar multiplication: $(kv)^\phi = k(v^\phi)$. The set of all non-singular linear transformations of a vector space $V$ onto itself forms the general linear group $GL(V)$.
