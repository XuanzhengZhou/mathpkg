All finite-dimensional vector spaces of the same dimension over a given field $K$ are isomorphic. This is a fundamental result in linear algebra: choosing a basis of $n$ elements for each space yields an isomorphism to the standard coordinate space $K^n$. The theorem underscores that dimension is a complete invariant for vector spaces over a field.
