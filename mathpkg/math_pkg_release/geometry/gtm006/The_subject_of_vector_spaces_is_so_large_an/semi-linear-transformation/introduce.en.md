A semi-linear transformation generalizes the notion of a linear transformation to the setting of vector spaces over skewfields. It is additive and scales vectors by first applying a fixed field automorphism to the scalar. This concept is fundamental in projective geometry because the group of all non-singular semi-linear transformations, $\Gamma L(V)$, describes all collineations of the associated projective space.
