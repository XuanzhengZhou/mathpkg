No explicit proof is given in this section. The result can be derived by noting that any $\gamma$ fixing every one-dimensional subspace must act as scalar multiplication on each subspace: for each $v \in V$, $v^\gamma = \lambda(v) v$ for some $\lambda(v) \in K^*$. By semi-linearity, $\lambda$ is independent of the choice of non-zero vector within each one-dimensional subspace, and the automorphism associated with $\gamma$ must be inner. This yields the isomorphism $N \cong K^*$, corresponding to scalar multiplication. The intersection with $GL(V)$ (where the automorphism is the identity) forces the scalar to lie in the center $Z(K)$.
