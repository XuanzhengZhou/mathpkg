When $V$ is a left vector space over a skewfield $K$, its dual space $V'$ of linear functionals naturally becomes a right vector space over $K$. The scalar multiplication is defined on the right -- $(f'b)(v) = f'(v)b$ -- precisely because $K$ need not be commutative. This left/right duality is a characteristic feature of linear algebra over skewfields and is essential for the contragredient representation theory used in projective geometry.
