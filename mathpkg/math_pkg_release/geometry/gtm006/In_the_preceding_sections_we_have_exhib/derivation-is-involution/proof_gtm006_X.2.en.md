This is an immediate corollary of Lemma 10.3. The complement $\mathcal{S}$ of $\mathcal{S}'$ on $\ell_{\infty}$ serves as the derivation set for $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$, because the retained lines of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ are precisely those meeting $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}'$, and applying the derivation construction with $\mathcal{S}$ replaces the Baer subplane "lines" (which belonged to $\mathcal{S}$) back with ordinary lines. By Lemma 10.3, each intersection of a retained line with the Baer subplanes forms an affine plane of order $n$, and the double derivation recovers the original incidence structure.
