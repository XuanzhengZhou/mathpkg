Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite affine plane of order $n^2$ with special line $\ell_{\infty}$, and let $\mathcal{S}$ be a derivation set for $\mathcal{A}$ with complement $\mathcal{S}'$ on $\ell_{\infty}$. The \textbf{derived affine plane} $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ is the incidence structure defined as follows:
\begin{itemize}
\item a point of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ is a point of $\mathcal{A}$;
\item a line of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ is either a line of $\mathcal{A}$ which meets $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}'$, or is a Baer subplane $\mathcal{A}_i = \mathcal{B}_i^{\ell_{\infty}}$ such that $\mathcal{B}_i$ contains $\mathcal{S}$;
\item incidence in $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ is the natural containment relation.
\end{itemize}
