The derived affine plane $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ is a new incidence structure constructed from a finite affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ of order $n^2$ and a derivation set $\mathcal{S}$. Its points are the same as $\mathcal{A}$, but its line set is modified: lines of $\mathcal{A}$ whose intersection with $\ell_{\infty}$ lies in the complement $\mathcal{S}'$ are retained, while those meeting $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}$ are replaced by the affine Baer subplanes belonging to $\mathcal{S}$. This construction, called "derivation", produces new finite affine (and hence projective) planes from existing ones.
