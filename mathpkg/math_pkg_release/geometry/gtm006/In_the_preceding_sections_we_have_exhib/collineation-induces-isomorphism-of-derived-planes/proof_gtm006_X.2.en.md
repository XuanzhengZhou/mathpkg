Let $\alpha \in \operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{A}$ be a collineation of $\mathcal{A}$. Then $\alpha$ sends $\ell_{\infty}$ to itself and maps the set $\mathcal{S}'$ to $\alpha(\mathcal{S}')$. Since $\alpha$ preserves incidence, it sends lines of $\mathcal{A}$ to lines of $\mathcal{A}$, and Baer subplanes of $\mathcal{A}$ to Baer subplanes of $\mathcal{A}$. Moreover, if a line meets $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}'$, its image meets $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\alpha(\mathcal{S}')$. A Baer subplane containing $\mathcal{S}$ is sent to a Baer subplane containing $\alpha(\mathcal{S})$. Consequently, the mapping $\alpha$ sends lines of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ to lines of $D_{\alpha(\mathcal{S}')}(\mathcal{A})$ while preserving incidence and bijectivity on points. Hence $\alpha$ induces the isomorphism $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A}) \cong D_{\alpha(\mathcal{S}')}(\mathcal{A})$.
