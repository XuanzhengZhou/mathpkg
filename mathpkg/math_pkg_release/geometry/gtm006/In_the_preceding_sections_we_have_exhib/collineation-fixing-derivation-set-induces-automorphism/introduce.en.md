Theorem 10.6 is a direct corollary of Theorem 10.5: if a collineation $\alpha$ of $\mathcal{A}$ fixes the derivation set $\mathcal{S}$ setwise, then $\alpha(\mathcal{S}') = \mathcal{S}'$, and the isomorphism from Theorem 10.5 becomes an automorphism of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ itself. This result is fundamental for relating the collineation groups of a plane and its derived plane, which is essential for studying the automorphism groups of planes obtained by derivation.
