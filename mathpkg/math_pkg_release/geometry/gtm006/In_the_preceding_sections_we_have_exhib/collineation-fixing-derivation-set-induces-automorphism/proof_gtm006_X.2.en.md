If $\alpha \in \operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{A}$ and $\alpha(\mathcal{S}) = \mathcal{S}$, then also $\alpha(\mathcal{S}') = \mathcal{S}'$. By Theorem 10.5, we have $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A}) \cong D_{\alpha(\mathcal{S}')}(\mathcal{A}) = D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$. The isomorphism constructed in the proof of Theorem 10.5 is induced by $\alpha$ itself, mapping points of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ to points of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ while preserving the line structure. Hence $\alpha$ induces an automorphism of $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$.
