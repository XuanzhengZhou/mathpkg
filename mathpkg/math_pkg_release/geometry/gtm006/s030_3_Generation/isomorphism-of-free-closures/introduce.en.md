Theorem 11.8 gives a complete criterion for two openly finite configurations to have isomorphic free closures. The condition is that they can be connected by a finite chain of configurations in which each step is a free extension in one direction. This is a powerful structural result: it reduces the problem of determining when $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A}) \cong \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{B})$ to finding a zigzag of free extensions linking $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$. The theorem combines Lemma 11.7 (common free extension) with the observation that a common free extension forces isomorphic free closures.
