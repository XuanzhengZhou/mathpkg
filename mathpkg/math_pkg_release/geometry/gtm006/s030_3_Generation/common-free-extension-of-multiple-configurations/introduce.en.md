This corollary to Lemma 11.7 extends the common free extension result to an arbitrary finite family of configurations $\mathcal{A}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{A}_n$ that all share the same free closure $\mathcal{P}$. It establishes the existence and uniqueness of a minimal common free extension $\mathcal{B}$: any configuration that is a free extension of all $\mathcal{A}_i$ must contain $\mathcal{B}$. This provides a meet-like operation in the poset of configurations ordered by free extension, and is used in structural analysis of configurations.
