Lemma 11.6 establishes a "two-out-of-three" property for free extensions among nested configurations $\mathcal{A} < \mathcal{B} < \mathcal{C}$. If any two of the three possible free-extension relationships hold, then the third must also hold. This transitivity-like lemma is essential for manipulating chains of free extensions and plays a key role in the proof of Theorem 11.8 on the isomorphism of free closures. Note that the word "free" is crucial; without it, the implication (a) and (b) $\Rightarrow$ (c) fails, as shown in Exercise 11.10.
