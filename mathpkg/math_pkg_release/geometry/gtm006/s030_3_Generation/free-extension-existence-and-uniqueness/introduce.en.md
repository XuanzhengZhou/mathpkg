Theorem 11.4 guarantees the existence and uniqueness (up to isomorphism) of the free closure $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{C})$ of any configuration $\mathcal{C}$. The free closure is constructed by iteratively taking free full one-step extensions: for each pair of like elements not yet incident, one adds a new unlike element incident with exactly those two. The union of this infinite process yields a closed configuration that is the universal free extension of $\mathcal{C}$, meaning every free extension of $\mathcal{C}$ embeds into $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{C})$.
