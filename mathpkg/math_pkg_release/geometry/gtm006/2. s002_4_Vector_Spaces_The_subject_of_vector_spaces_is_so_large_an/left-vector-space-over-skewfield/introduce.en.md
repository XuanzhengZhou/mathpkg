A left vector space over a skewfield $K$ generalizes the familiar notion of a vector space over a field by allowing the scalar field to be non-commutative (i.e., a skewfield or division ring). The key distinction is that scalars must always multiply vectors on the left to preserve the expected associativity and distributivity laws. Over a skewfield, isomorphism classes of left vector spaces are classified by dimension, just as in the field case, but left and right vector space structures are not interchangeable.
