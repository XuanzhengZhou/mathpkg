Result 1.21 describes the structure of the general semi-linear group $\Gamma L(V)$ as a semidirect product: every semi-linear transformation factors uniquely as a field automorphism (acting coordinate-wise) followed by a linear transformation. The subgroup $A \cong \operatorname{Aut}(K)$ corresponds to the automorphisms of the skewfield acting component-wise on the coordinates, and $GL(V)$ is the normal subgroup of linear transformations. This decomposition is essential for understanding the collineation group of a projective space, since the full collineation group is the projective image of $\Gamma L(V)$.
