Let $V$ be a finite dimensional left (or right) vector space over the field $K$. If a semi-linear transformation $\alpha \in \Gamma L(V)$ has the property that it fixes every one-dimensional subspace of $V$ (i.e., $\langle v^\alpha \rangle = \langle v \rangle$ for all $v \in V$), then $\alpha$ is necessarily linear and lies in the centre of $GL(V)$. In matrix terms, with respect to any basis of $V$, $\alpha$ is represented by a scalar multiple of the identity matrix.
