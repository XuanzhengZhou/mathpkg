The dual space $V'$ of a left vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$ is the set of all linear functionals on $V$, equipped with pointwise addition and right scalar multiplication. A key subtlety over skewfields is that duality reverses the side of scalar multiplication: if $V$ is a left vector space, then $V'$ is a right vector space, and vice versa. In coordinates, vectors of $V$ are represented by row vectors and linear functionals (vectors of $V'$) by column vectors, so that the evaluation $vf'$ corresponds to the ordinary matrix product of a row with a column. This formalism is fundamental for the coordinate treatment of projective spaces.
