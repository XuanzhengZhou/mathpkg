Let $K$ be a skewfield. A set $V$ is called a \textit{right vector space over $K$} if for every $v \in V$ and $k \in K$, the product $vk$ is defined as a vector of $V$, and the usual associativity and distributivity properties hold with the scalars always written on the right of the vectors. All right vector spaces of dimension $n$ over the same skewfield $K$ are isomorphic.
