A semi-linear transformation generalizes the notion of a linear transformation by allowing the scalar field to be twisted by an automorphism $\alpha$ of the skewfield $K$. Instead of the identity $k^\alpha = k$, the scalar $k$ is replaced by its image $k^\alpha$ before multiplication with the vector. Semi-linear transformations form the group $\Gamma L(V)$, the general semi-linear group, which plays a crucial role in projective geometry since collineations of projective spaces over skewfields correspond to semi-linear transformations of the underlying vector spaces.
