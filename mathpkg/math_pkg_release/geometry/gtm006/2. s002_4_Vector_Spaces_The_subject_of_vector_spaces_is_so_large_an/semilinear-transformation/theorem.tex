Let $V$ and $W$ be left vector spaces over the skewfield $K$, and let $\alpha$ be an automorphism of $K$. A mapping $\phi: V \to W$ is called a \textit{semi-linear transformation} from $V$ to $W$ with associated automorphism $\alpha$ if:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] For all $v_1, v_2 \in V$, $(v_1 + v_2)^\phi = v_1^\phi + v_2^\phi$.
\item[(ii)] For all $v \in V$ and $k \in K$, $(kv)^\phi = k^\alpha (v^\phi)$.
\end{enumerate}

The definition is completely analogous when $V$ and $W$ are right vector spaces over $K$.
