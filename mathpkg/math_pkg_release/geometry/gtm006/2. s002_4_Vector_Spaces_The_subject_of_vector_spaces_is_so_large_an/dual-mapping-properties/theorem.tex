Let $V$ be a finite dimensional left vector space over the skewfield $K$, with basis $e_1, \dots, e_n$. Let $\alpha$ be a non-singular semi-linear transformation of $V$ onto $V$ with associated automorphism $\sigma \in \operatorname{Aut}(K)$. In the given basis, suppose $\alpha$ is represented by the matrix $A$, so that for $v = (x_1, \dots, x_n)$,

$$v^\alpha = (x_1^\sigma, x_2^\sigma, \dots, x_n^\sigma) A.$$

The \textit{induced mapping} $\alpha'$ on the dual space $V'$ is defined by: for $w' \in V'$ represented as a column vector $(y_1, \dots, y_n)^\top$ (the transpose of a row vector),

$$(w')^{\alpha'} = A^{-1} (y_1^\sigma, y_2^\sigma, \dots, y_n^\sigma)^\top.$$

Here $A^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of the matrix $A$, and the automorphism $\sigma$ is applied component-wise to the entries of the column vector.
