A linear functional on a left vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$ is a linear map from $V$ to the one-dimensional vector space $K$ (viewed as a left vector space over itself). The functional is written on the right ($vf'$) rather than on the left ($f'(v)$), a convention chosen so that the set $V'$ of all linear functionals becomes a right vector space over $K$ under the natural operations $(f' + g')(v) = vf' + vg'$ and $(f'b)(v) = (vf')b$. This left-right duality is essential for the theory of dual spaces and homogeneous coordinates in projective geometry.
