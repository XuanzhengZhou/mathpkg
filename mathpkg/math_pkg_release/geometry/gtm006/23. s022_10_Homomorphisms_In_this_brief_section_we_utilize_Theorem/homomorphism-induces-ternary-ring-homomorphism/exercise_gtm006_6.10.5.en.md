Prove statements (i), (ii), and (iii) from Section 10 of Chapter 6. That is, given a homomorphism $\phi : \mathcal{P}_1 \to \mathcal{P}_2$ and the sets $Z$, $J$, and $\overline{R}_1 = R_1 \setminus J$ defined as in the proof of Theorem 6.21, show that:

(i) $(\overline{R}_1, T_1)$ is a ternary ring.

(ii) The induced mapping $\alpha : \overline{R}_1 \to R_2$ satisfies $[T_1(x, y, z)]^* = T_2(x^*, y^*, z^*)$ for all $x, y, z \in \overline{R}_1$.

(iii) $(x, y)^* = (x^*, y^*)$ if both $x$ and $y$ are in $\overline{R}_1$, and $[m]^* = [m^*]$ if $m \in \overline{R}_1$.
