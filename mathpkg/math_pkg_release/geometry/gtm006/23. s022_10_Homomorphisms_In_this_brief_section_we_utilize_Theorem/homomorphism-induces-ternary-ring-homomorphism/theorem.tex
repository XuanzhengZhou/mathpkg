Let $\phi : \mathcal{P}_1 \to \mathcal{P}_2$ be a homomorphism of projective planes, and let $\overline{R}_1$ and $Z$ be defined as in the analysis of Theorem 6.21. Then $\phi$ induces a mapping $\alpha : \overline{R}_1 \to R_2$, denoted by $x \mapsto x^*$, such that:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(ii)] $[T_1(x, y, z)]^* = T_2(x^*, y^*, z^*)$ for all $x, y, z \in \overline{R}_1$;
\item[(iii)] $(x, y)^* = (x^*, y^*)$ if both $x$ and $y$ are in $\overline{R}_1$, and $[m]^* = [m^*]$ if $m \in \overline{R}_1$.
\end{itemize}
