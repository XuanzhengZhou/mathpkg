Building on the coordinatization framework of Theorem 6.21, a deeper analysis reveals that the homomorphism $\phi$ induces a well-defined mapping $\alpha$ from the restricted coordinate set $\overline{R}_1$ onto the full coordinate ternary ring $R_2$ of the target plane. This mapping $\alpha$ is a morphism of planar ternary rings: it respects the ternary operation $T$ and is compatible with the coordinatization of points and lines. This result shows that homomorphisms of projective planes correspond algebraically to homomorphisms of their coordinate PTRs.
