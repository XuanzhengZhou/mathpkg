Let $\phi : \mathcal{P}_1 \to \mathcal{P}_2$ be a homomorphism of projective planes. Let $Z = \{ x \in R_1 \mid (x, 0)_1^\phi = (0, 0)_2 \}$ where $(R_1, T_1)$ and $(R_2, T_2)$ are planar ternary rings for $\mathcal{P}_1$ and $\mathcal{P}_2$ chosen as in the proof of Theorem 6.21. Let $J = \{ x \in R_1 \mid xz = 1 \text{ for some } z \in Z \}$ and let $\overline{R}_1 = R_1 \setminus J$. Then $(\overline{R}_1, T_1)$ is a ternary ring.
