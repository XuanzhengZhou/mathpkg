A \textbf{homomorphism} from the projective plane $\mathcal{P}_1$ onto the projective plane $\mathcal{P}_2$ is a mapping $\phi$ which sends the points and lines, respectively, of $\mathcal{P}_1$ onto the points and lines of $\mathcal{P}_2$, and which preserves incidence. That is, if a point $P$ lies on a line $\ell$ in $\mathcal{P}_1$, then $P^\phi$ lies on $\ell^\phi$ in $\mathcal{P}_2$.
