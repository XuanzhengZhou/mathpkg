This corollary follows immediately from Theorem 6.21: if either projective plane is finite, then no element can have infinitely many pre-images. Consequently, the homomorphism must be an isomorphism. This result highlights the special nature of finite projective planes, where homomorphic images are rigid — any surjective homomorphism between finite projective planes is necessarily bijective and thus a collineation.
