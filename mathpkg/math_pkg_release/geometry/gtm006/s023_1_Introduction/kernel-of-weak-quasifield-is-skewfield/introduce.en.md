Theorem 7.2 establishes that the kernel $K$ of a weak quasifield $W$ is a skewfield (division ring). The proof proceeds by showing $K$ is closed under subtraction (hence an additive subgroup), and then that $K^*$ is closed under multiplication and inverses, making it a multiplicative group. The right vector space structure comes from the natural scalar multiplication given by the quasifield multiplication itself. This theorem is central because it reveals that every weak quasifield (and hence every quasifield coordinatizing a translation plane) carries an underlying skewfield structure that controls much of its geometry.
