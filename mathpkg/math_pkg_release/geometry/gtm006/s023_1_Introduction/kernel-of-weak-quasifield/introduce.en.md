The kernel of a weak quasifield $W$ is the set of elements that satisfy right-distributivity and right-associativity. Since $W$ satisfies the left distributive law by definition, the kernel elements are exactly those that behave like scalars acting on the right. Theorem 7.2 shows that the kernel is in fact a skewfield and that $W$ can be regarded as a right vector space over it, making the kernel a fundamental invariant of the algebraic structure of $W$ and of the associated translation plane.
