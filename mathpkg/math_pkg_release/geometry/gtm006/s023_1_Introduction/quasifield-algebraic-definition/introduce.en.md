A quasifield is an algebraic structure that generalizes a field by relaxing the associativity of multiplication to a loop structure while retaining the left distributive law. It arises naturally as the coordinatizing algebraic system for a translation plane in projective geometry: the points of the affine plane correspond to pairs $(x, y)$ from $Q$, lines are given by equations $y = mx + b$ or $x = c$, and the ternary operation is the natural linear one $T(m, x, y) = mx + y$. This algebraic definition is equivalent to the geometric one obtained by coordinatizing a projective plane with a translation line (Exercise 7.1), and the axioms force $(Q, +)$ to be abelian (Exercise 7.2).
