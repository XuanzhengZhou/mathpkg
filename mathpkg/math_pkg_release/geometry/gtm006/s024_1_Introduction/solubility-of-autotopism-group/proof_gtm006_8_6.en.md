**Proof (sketch).** The proof proceeds by analyzing the subgroup $\Delta_1$ of autotopisms acting trivially on the semi-nuclei. By Lemma 8.14, $\Delta/\Delta_1$ is soluble, so it suffices to prove $\Delta_1$ is soluble under the stated hypotheses.

If $\mathcal{P}(D)$ has even order and no Baer subplanes, then any involution in $\Delta_1$ must be a homology, and by Lemma 8.16 such involutions are of the form $(A,A,I)$, $(A,I,A)$, or $(I,A,A)$, with $A = R_{-1}$. A detailed analysis of the subgroup generated by these shows $\Delta_1$ is soluble.

If $\mathcal{P}(D)$ has odd order and $\dim D$ over a semi-nucleus is odd, then Lemma 8.17 precludes Baer involutions in $\Delta_1$. In the absence of Baer involutions, similar reasoning yields solubility of $\Delta_1$. $\square$
