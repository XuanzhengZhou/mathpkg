**Proof.** An element of order $2$ in $\Delta_2$ corresponds to an involution of the projective plane $\mathcal{P}(D)$ fixing the autotopism triangle $(\infty), (0), (0,0)$. Since an elation cannot fix a triangle, this involution must, by Theorem 4.3 and Exercise 4.4, be either a homology (in which case the plane has odd order) or fix a Baer subplane pointwise. In the first case, by Theorem 8.2, any homology fixing $(\infty), (0), (0,0)$ is induced by appropriate multiplications from the various semi-nuclei, yielding the mappings $(A,A,I)$, $(A,I,A)$, or $(I,A,A)$ with $A = R_{-1} = L_{-1}$. The second case gives a Baer involution. $\square$
