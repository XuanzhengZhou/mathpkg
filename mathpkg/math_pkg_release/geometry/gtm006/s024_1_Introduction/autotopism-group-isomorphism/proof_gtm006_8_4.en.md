**Proof.** This is clear if we interpret the additive maps $P, Q, R$ as permutations of the points of the lines $[\infty]$, $[0,0]$, $[0]$ respectively. Indeed, an autotopism $\alpha(P,Q,R)$ of $D$ induces a collineation of $\mathcal{P}(D)$ fixing the autotopism triangle $(\infty), (0), (0,0)$, defined by:

$$(m)^\alpha = (mP),\quad (x)^\alpha = (xQ),\quad (0,y)^\alpha = (0, yR),$$

and conversely any such planar autotopism arises uniquely from a triple $(P,Q,R)$ of additive bijections satisfying $xP \cdot yQ = (xy)R$. The composition rule for triples matches the composition of collineations, giving the isomorphism. $\square$
