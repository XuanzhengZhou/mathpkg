This lemma addresses a natural question about projective lines embedded in projective planes: given a semi-linear transformation between two lines (2-dimensional subspaces), can it be realized as the restriction of a collineation of the whole plane? The answer is affirmative: any non-singular semi-linear transformation between two 2-dimensional subspaces of a 3-dimensional vector space can be extended to a non-singular semi-linear transformation of the entire space, and the extension is highly non-unique. In fact, for any choice of vectors $e \notin W_1$ and $f \notin W_2$, the transformation can be extended to send $e$ to $f$, demonstrating that the induced mapping on the projective line comes from an element of the full collineation group $P\Gamma L(V)$.
