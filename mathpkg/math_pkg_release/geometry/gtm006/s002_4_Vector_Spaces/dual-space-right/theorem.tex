If $V$ is a left vector space over $K$, the dual space $V'$ is a right vector space of the same dimension over $K$. If $V$ is a right vector space, $V'$ is a left vector space. The mapping $\alpha_V$ from subspaces of $V$ to subspaces of $V'$ given by $W^{\alpha_V} = \{v' \in V' \mid wv' = 0 \text{ for all } w \in W\}$ sends subspaces of dimension $i$ to subspaces of dimension $n+1-i$.
