A right vector space places scalar multiplication on the right side of vectors. The choice between left and right vector spaces affects the representation of linear transformations (matrices act on the left or right). When $V$ is a left vector space over $K$, its dual space $V'$ naturally becomes a right vector space over $K$, and vice versa. This interplay is essential in the study of dualities and polarities in projective geometry.
