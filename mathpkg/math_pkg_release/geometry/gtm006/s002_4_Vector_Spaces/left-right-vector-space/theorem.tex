$V$ is a left vector space over the skewfield $K$ if for all $v \in V$ and $k \in K$, the product $kv$ is defined as a vector of $V$, with the usual associativity and distributivity properties, but with scalars always on the left. A right vector space is defined analogously with scalars on the right. All left vector spaces of dimension $n$ over $K$ are isomorphic; similarly for right vector spaces.
