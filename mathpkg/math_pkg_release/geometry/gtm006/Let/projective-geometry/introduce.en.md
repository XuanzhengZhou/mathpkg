The projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is defined as the collection of all subspaces of a finite-dimensional vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$, equipped with the natural containment (incidence) relation. This construction allows us to study the lattice of subspaces of $V$ as a geometric object, where incidence corresponds to set-theoretic containment. The algebraic dimension of a subspace is translated into a geometric dimension ($g$-dimension), with points, lines, planes, and hyperplanes defined according to their $g$-dimension.
