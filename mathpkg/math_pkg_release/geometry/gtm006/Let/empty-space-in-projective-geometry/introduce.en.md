In projective geometry, the zero-subspace of $V$ (the subspace consisting only of the zero vector) plays the role of the empty space. While its algebraic dimension is $0$, its $g$-dimension is $-1$, reflecting the fact that it contains no points and serves as the natural "empty set" in the projective setting. This is the intersection of all points when points are considered as the fundamental objects.
