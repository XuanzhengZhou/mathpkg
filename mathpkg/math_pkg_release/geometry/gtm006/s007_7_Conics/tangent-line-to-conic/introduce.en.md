For a non-empty conic $\mathcal{C}$ associated with an orthogonal polarity $\beta$ over a field of characteristic not $2$, the tangent line at an absolute point $\langle v \rangle \in \mathcal{C}$ is the polar line $\langle A v \rangle$, where $A$ is the symmetric matrix defining the conic. This line meets the conic exactly once, at the point $\langle v \rangle$ itself. Tangent lines are precisely the absolute lines of the associated polarity and play a key role in the study of conics, including Pascal's theorem and the structure of the orthogonal group.
