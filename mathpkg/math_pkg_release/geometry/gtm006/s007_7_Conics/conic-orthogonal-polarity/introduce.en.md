A conic in a projective plane (over a field of characteristic $\neq 2$) is the set of absolute points of an orthogonal polarity. In coordinates, a conic is the zero-set of a non-degenerate quadratic form. Conics generalize circles and ellipses from Euclidean geometry to the projective setting.
