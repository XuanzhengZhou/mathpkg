This is an immediate consequence of Corollary 1 and Exercise 2.11. If $P$ is on a conic $\mathcal{C}$ which is associated with a polarity $\beta$ then $P$ is an absolute point whose image contains no other point of $\mathcal{C}$. Since the other lines through $P$ cannot be absolute they must contain another point of $\mathcal{C}$. By Corollary 1, the points of $\mathcal{C}$ are in one-to-one correspondence with the points on a line. In $\mathcal{P}_2(q)$, every line has exactly $q+1$ points. Hence a nonempty conic has $q+1$ points, and a conic may also be empty. $\square$
