Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a non-empty conic in the projective plane $\mathcal{P}(V)$, with a frame chosen to represent $\mathcal{C}$ in parametric form. Then the orthogonal group (for $\mathcal{C}$) is triply transitive on the points of $\mathcal{C}$, is faithfully represented on $\mathcal{C}$ and, in the parametric coordinates, induces the group of permutations:

$$y \rightarrow \frac{ay + b}{cy + d}, \quad \text{where } ad - bc \neq 0.$$

Thus the orthogonal group of a non-empty conic is isomorphic to the group $PGL(W)$, where $W$ is a two-dimensional vector space over the same field as $V$.
