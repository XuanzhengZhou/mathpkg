The orthogonal group of a nonempty conic $\mathcal{C}$ is the subgroup of the projective general linear group $PGL(V)$ that fixes $\mathcal{C}$ setwise. By Exercise 2.34, this is equivalent to requiring that the collineation commutes with the orthogonal polarity $\beta$ defining $\mathcal{C}$. Since Theorem 2.36 shows all nonempty conics are projectively equivalent, all orthogonal groups are conjugate in $PGL(V)$, making the definition independent of the particular conic chosen. The orthogonal group plays a role in projective geometry analogous to the classical orthogonal groups in linear algebra.
