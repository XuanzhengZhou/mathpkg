Theorem 2.36 establishes that all non-empty conics in a Desarguesian projective plane (char $\neq 2$) are projectively equivalent. By choosing a suitable frame (two points on the conic, their intersection with the tangent at one, and an additional point on the conic), the quadratic form can be brought to the canonical form $x^2 - 2yz = 0$. This is the projective analogue of the fact that all non-degenerate conics in the Euclidean plane are equivalent under affine transformations (ellipses, parabolas, hyperbolas are distinguished only by their relation to the line at infinity).
