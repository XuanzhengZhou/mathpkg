Lemma 2.34 establishes a fundamental intersection property of conics: any line in the projective plane can intersect a conic in at most two points. This follows from the fact that the condition for a point on the line joining $\langle v \rangle$ and $\langle w \rangle$ to lie on the conic reduces to a quadratic equation in the homogeneous parameters. The result is essential for understanding the structure of lines relative to a conic, including the classification of lines as secants, tangents, or external lines.
