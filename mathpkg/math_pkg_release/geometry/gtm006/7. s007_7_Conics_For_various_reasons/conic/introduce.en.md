A conic is the set of absolute points of an orthogonal polarity in a projective plane $\mathcal{P}(V)$ defined over a field whose characteristic is not two. This restriction on the characteristic is necessary because orthogonal polarities over fields of characteristic two exhibit pathological behavior. Conics generalize the familiar notion of a conic section from classical projective geometry and serve as the foundation for studying orthogonal groups and related geometric structures.
