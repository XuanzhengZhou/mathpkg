Let $R = \{0, 1, \dots, n-1\}$ and suppose $\{1\}, \{2\}, \dots, \{n-1\}$ is a complete set of mutually orthogonal $n \times n$ Latin squares in normal form, such that $\{1\}$ has $(0, 1, \dots, n-1)^\top$ as its left column and $\{x\}$ has $x$ in the $(1,0)$-position for $x = 1, 2, \dots, n-1$. Define $T(x, i, j)$ for $x \neq 0$ as the $(i, j)$-th entry of $\{x\}$, and set $T(0, i, j) = j$ for all $i, j$. Then $(R, T)$ is a planar ternary ring.
