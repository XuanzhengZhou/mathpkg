The Pickert ternary ring is a modification of Hall's coordinatization method, due to Gunter Pickert. While the point and line assignments follow the same geometric construction as Hall's method, the ternary operation $S$ is defined differently: $S(m,x,k) = y$ precisely when the point $(x,y)$ is incident with the line $[m,k]$. This approach yields a different perspective on the algebraic structure of projective planes, though the condition of linearity ($S(m,x,k) = mx + k$) aligns the two methods. The results in subsequent chapters of the book are independent of the specific coordinatization method chosen.
