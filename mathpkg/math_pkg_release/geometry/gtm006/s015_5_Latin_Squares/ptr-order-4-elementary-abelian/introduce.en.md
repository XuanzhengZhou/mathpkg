This corollary follows from Exercise 5.7, which shows that the Latin square corresponding to the addition table of $(\mathbb{Z}_4, +)$ admits no orthogonal mate. Since a PTR of order $n$ yields $n-1$ mutually orthogonal Latin squares (a complete set), the additive loop of a PTR of order $4$ cannot be the cyclic group of order $4$. Combined with Exercise 5.6 (the only loops of order $4$ are the two groups), this forces the additive loop to be the elementary abelian group $\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2$, which is precisely the additive group of the field $\mathrm{GF}(4)$.
