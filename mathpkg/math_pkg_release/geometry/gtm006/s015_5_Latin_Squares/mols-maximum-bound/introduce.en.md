Property 2 establishes the fundamental upper bound for mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS): there can be at most $n-1$ pairwise orthogonal Latin squares of order $n$. The proof uses normal form and a counting argument on the possible entries in the $(1,0)$-position of each square. A set achieving this bound is called a complete set of MOLS, and its existence is equivalent to the existence of a finite projective plane of order $n$.
