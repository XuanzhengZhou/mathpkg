Let $(R, T)$ be a planar ternary ring (PTR) of order $n$ with $R = \{0, 1, \dots, n-1\}$. For any $x \in R$, $x \neq 0$, define an $n \times n$ matrix $\{x\}$ by: the entry in row $i$ and column $j$ of $\{x\}$ is $T(x, i, j)$, where the top row is row $0$. Then

(1) $\{x\}$ is a latin square.

(2) If $x \neq y$, then $\{x\}$ is orthogonal to $\{y\}$.
