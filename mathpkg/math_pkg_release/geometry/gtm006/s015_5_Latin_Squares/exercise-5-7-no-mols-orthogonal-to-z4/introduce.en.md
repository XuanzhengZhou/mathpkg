Exercise 5.7 asks the reader to prove that the Latin square corresponding to the addition table of $(\mathbb{Z}_4, +)$ admits no orthogonal mate. The hint suggests attempting to construct one, starting with the top row $(0,1,2,3)$ and the beginning of the second row. This non-existence result is crucial because it implies that the additive loop of a PTR of order $4$ cannot be isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_4$, thereby forcing it to be the elementary abelian group.
