Let $O, X, Y, I$ be a quadrangle of a projective plane of order $n$ and let $R$ be a set of $n$ symbols with two distinguished elements labeled $0, 1$. The Hall coordinatization assigns coordinates as follows:
\begin{itemize}
\item The line $XY$ is labeled $[\infty]$ and $Y$ as $(\infty)$ where $\infty \notin R$.
\item Elements of $R$ are assigned to points of $OI$ not on $[\infty]$ such that $O \mapsto 0$ and $I \mapsto 1$. A point $A$ on $OI$ assigned $a \in R$ is coordinatized as $(a, a)$.
\item A point $B$ on $OY$ with $BX \cap OI = (b, b)$ is given coordinates $(0, b)$.
\item A point $C$ on $OX$ with $CY \cap OI = (c, c)$ is given coordinates $(c, 0)$.
\item For a point $E$ not on any side of triangle $OXY$, if $XE \cap OY = (0, y)$ and $YE \cap OX = (x, 0)$, then $E$ is coordinatized as $(x, y)$.
\item Points on $[\infty]$ except $Y$ are coordinatized by $(m)$ where $m \in R$, specifically $M$ on the line joining $O$ to $(1, m)$ is $(m)$.
\item Lines are coordinatized as $[m, k]$ (joining $(m)$ to $(0, k)$), $[a]$ (joining $(\infty)$ to $(a, 0)$), and $[\infty] = XY$.
\end{itemize}
