An inherited collineation of a derived affine plane $\mathcal{A}_1$ is a collineation induced by a collineation $\alpha$ of the original affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ that leaves the derivation set $\mathcal{S}$ invariant (i.e., $\mathcal{S}^\alpha = \mathcal{S}$). The study of inherited collineations provides a way to relate the symmetry groups of an affine plane and its derived plane, often yielding useful information about the structure of the derived plane.
