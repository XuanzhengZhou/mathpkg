Theorem 10.9 states that the operation of derivation almost always produces a non-desarguesian plane from a desarguesian one. The sole exception occurs when the original desarguesian plane has order $4$, in which case the derived plane remains desarguesian. This theorem is foundational because it provides a systematic method for constructing non-desarguesian projective planes from the well-understood desarguesian ones via derivation.
