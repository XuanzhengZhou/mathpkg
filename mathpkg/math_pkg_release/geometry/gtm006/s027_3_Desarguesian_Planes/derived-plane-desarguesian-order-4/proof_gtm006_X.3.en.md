The proof shows that if $\mathcal{A}$ is desarguesian and its derived plane $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{F}}(\mathcal{A})$ is also desarguesian, then $\mathcal{A}$ must have order $4$. The argument proceeds by analyzing the group of shears $\sigma_a$ in the derived plane: the group of all shears $\sigma_a$ is isomorphic to the additive group of $GF(q)$, and comparing this with the structure of desarguesian planes forces $q = 2$, hence $\mathcal{A}$ has order $4$. Conversely, for $q = 2$, the derived plane of $\mathcal{A}_2(4)$ is again desarguesian (indeed it is isomorphic to $\mathcal{A}_2(4)$ itself). $\square$
