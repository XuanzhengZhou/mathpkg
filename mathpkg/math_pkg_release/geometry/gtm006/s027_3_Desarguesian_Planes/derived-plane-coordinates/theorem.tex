Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a derivable affine plane coordinatized by a planar ternary ring $(R, T)$, let $\mathcal{S}$ be a derivation set for $\mathcal{A}$ containing $(\infty)$, and let $\mathcal{A}_1 = \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}}(\mathcal{A})$ be the derived plane. Then $\mathcal{A}_1$ can be coordinatized by a planar ternary ring $(R, T_1)$ with the same underlying set $R$, where the new ternary operation $T_1$ satisfies:

(1) $0$ and $1$ are the same additive and multiplicative identities as in $R$;

(2) if $\lambda \in F$ and $x, y \in R$, then $T_1(\lambda, x, y)$ is determined by the Baer subplane structure;

(3) if $\lambda \in F$ and $x \in R$, then $\lambda * x = \lambda x$;

(4) $\mathcal{L}^* = \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}}(\mathcal{S})$ consists of the points $(\lambda')$, $\lambda \in F$, and $(\infty)$;

(5) if $\lambda \in F$ and $x, y \in R$, then $T_1(\lambda, x, y) = \lambda x \oplus y$, where $\oplus$ and $*$ denote the new addition and multiplication in the derived PTR.
