Exercise 10.3 establishes two fundamental properties of multiple derivation with disjoint derivation sets. First, if $\mathcal{S}$ and $\mathcal{T}$ are disjoint derivation sets for $\mathcal{A}$, then $\mathcal{T}$ remains a derivation set for the derived plane $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}}(\mathcal{A})$. Second, the order of derivation does not matter: applying derivation with respect to $\mathcal{S}$ then $\mathcal{T}$ yields the same plane as applying $\mathcal{T}$ then $\mathcal{S}$. This commutativity property justifies the notation $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}_1} \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}_2} \cdots \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}_n}(\mathcal{A})$ for multiply derived planes.
