Theorem 10.12 shows that starting from a desarguesian affine plane $\mathcal{A}_2(q^2)$ and applying multiple derivation with respect to distinct derivation sets $\mathcal{S}_{t_1}, \dots, \mathcal{S}_{t_r}$, the resulting plane is an André plane. The André planes form a well-known class of translation planes, so this theorem places multiply derived desarguesian planes within the existing classification framework. The proof uses auxiliary lemmas about solutions to equations of the form $x^{q-1} = a$ in $GF(q^2)$ and analyzes the coordinatization of the derived structure.
