Theorem 10.8 establishes that every desarguesian affine plane $\mathcal{A}_2(q^2)$ over the finite field $GF(q^2)$ admits a derivation set. The derivation set $\mathcal{S}$ is concretely identified as the set of points at infinity whose coordinates lie in the subfield $GF(q)$, together with the point at infinity $(\infty)$. The proof relies on the transitivity of the translation group and the group of $(X, \ell_{\infty})$-homologies to reduce the verification to a subplane coordinatized by $GF(q)$, which is known to be a Baer subplane.
