Theorem 10.14 provides general sufficient conditions under which an affine plane is derivable. If the plane is coordinatized by a right quasifield of dimension $2$ over its kernel, and certain parametrized subsets $\mathcal{B}(a, b, c)$ form Baer subplanes, then the plane admits a derivation set. This theorem significantly generalizes the earlier result for desarguesian planes, showing that the class of derivable planes is quite large, encompassing many non-desarguesian translation planes and even some planes that are neither translation planes nor dual translation planes.
