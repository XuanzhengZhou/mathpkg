A configuration $\mathcal{A}$ is called a \textbf{sub-configuration} of a configuration $\mathcal{B}$ if the points and lines of $\mathcal{A}$ are all points and lines of $\mathcal{B}$, and for any point $P$ and line $m$ of $\mathcal{A}$, $P$ is on $m$ in $\mathcal{B}$ if and only if $P$ is on $m$ in $\mathcal{A}$. We write $\mathcal{A} < \mathcal{B}$. This condition prevents the larger configuration $\mathcal{B}$ from introducing new incidences between elements that already belong to $\mathcal{A}$.
