A configuration is the basic incidence structure studied in the theory of projective planes: a collection of points and lines equipped with an incidence relation in which any two distinct points lie on at most one common line. The definition is self-dual: by symmetry, any two distinct lines meet in at most one point. Configurations serve as the starting point for the free closure construction, where larger configurations are built by repeatedly adding meets and joins of elements that do not yet share a common incident element.
