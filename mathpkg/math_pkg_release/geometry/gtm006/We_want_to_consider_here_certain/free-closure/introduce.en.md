The free closure is a constructive method introduced by Hall in 1943 for generating projective planes from arbitrary configurations. Starting with a given configuration, one repeatedly adjoins new elements that serve as the unique intersection (meet) of two existing elements that previously lacked a common incident element. The union of this successive enlargement process typically yields a projective plane, providing a powerful tool for embedding configurations into projective planes and for constructing planes with prescribed properties such as trivial collineation groups.
