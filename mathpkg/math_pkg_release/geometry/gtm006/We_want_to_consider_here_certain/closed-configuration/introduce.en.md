A closed configuration is a configuration in which every pair of distinct elements of the same type share a common incident element: any two points are joined by some line of the configuration, and any two lines meet in some point of the configuration. Closed configurations that fail to contain a quadrangle are called degenerate; those that do are precisely the projective planes. This condition therefore separates projective planes from narrower incidence structures among the closed configurations.
