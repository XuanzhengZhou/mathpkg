Theorem 2.2 establishes that a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of g-dimension 2 (i.e., constructed from a 3-dimensional vector space) satisfies three fundamental incidence axioms: any two distinct points lie on exactly one line, any two distinct lines meet in exactly one point, and there exist four points no three of which are collinear. These three properties later serve as the axiomatic definition of a projective plane in Chapter III.
