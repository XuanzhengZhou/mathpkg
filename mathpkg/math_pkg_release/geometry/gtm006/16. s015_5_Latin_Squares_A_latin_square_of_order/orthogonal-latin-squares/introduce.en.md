Two latin squares of order $n$ are orthogonal if the $n^2$ ordered pairs $(i, j)$ formed by taking entries from the same position in the respective squares exhaust all $n^2$ possible ordered pairs from the symbol set. A set of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal latin squares of order $n$ is called a complete set. Orthogonal latin squares are closely connected to planar ternary rings and provide an equivalent formulation for the existence of projective planes of a given order.
