A latin square of order $n$ is an $n \times n$ matrix over a set of $n$ symbols where each symbol appears exactly once in every row and every column. The canonical symbol set is $\{0, 1, 2, \dots, n-1\}$. Latin squares arise naturally from the Cayley tables of quasigroups, and they play a fundamental role in the theory of finite projective planes through their connection to planar ternary rings.
