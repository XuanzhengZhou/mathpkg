By the classification theorem, a Moufang plane is coordinatized by an alternative division ring. In the finite case, every finite alternative division ring is a field (by the Artin-Zorn theorem or by the fact that finite alternative division rings are associative, hence skewfields, and finite skewfields are fields by Wedderburn's little theorem). Therefore the plane is coordinatized by a field, which characterizes pappian planes. A pappian plane is always desarguesian.
