A projective plane is a Moufang plane if and only if its coordinating planar ternary ring is an alternative (or IP) division ring. Equivalently, the plane admits $(V,k)$-transitivity for all incident pairs of point $V$ and line $k$; or $(k,k)$-transitivity for all lines $k$; or $(V,k)$-transitivity for all points $V$; or $(k,k)$-transitivity for two distinct lines $k$; or $(V,V)$-transitivity for two distinct points $V$.
