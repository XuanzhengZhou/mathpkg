A projective plane is desarguesian if and only if its coordinating planar ternary ring is a skewfield (division ring). Equivalently, the plane admits $(V,k)$-transitivity for all choices of point $V$ and line $k$; or equivalently, $(V,k)$-transitivity for all $V$ and $k$ such that $V$ is incident with $k$, together with $(V,k)$-transitivity for one non-incident pair $V$ and $k$.
