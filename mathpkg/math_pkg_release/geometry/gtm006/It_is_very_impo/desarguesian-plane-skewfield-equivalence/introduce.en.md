A projective plane is desarguesian exactly when its planar ternary ring is a skewfield (division ring). This is the classical coordinatization theorem: the plane can be represented as $\mathrm{PG}(2,D)$ for a division ring $D$. Geometrically, desarguesian planes are characterized by full $(V,k)$-transitivity for all point-line pairs, or more economically by transitivity at all incident pairs plus just one non-incident pair.
