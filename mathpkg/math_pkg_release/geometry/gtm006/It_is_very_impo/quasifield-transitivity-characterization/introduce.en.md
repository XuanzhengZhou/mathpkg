A projective plane is coordinatized by a quasifield precisely when it is a translation plane with respect to a distinguished line $k = [\infty]$, i.e., it admits $(k,k)$-transitivity for that line. Equivalently, it suffices that the plane admits $(V,k)$-transitivity for two distinct points $V$ on $k$. A quasifield is a set with two binary operations satisfying all field axioms except possibly both distributive laws and associativity of multiplication.
