A projective plane is coordinatized by a nearfield if and only if it admits $(k,k)$-transitivity and $(V,m)$-transitivity, where $V$ is incident with $k$ but not with $m$. In this case the distinguished line is $k = [\infty]$ and either $V = (0)$ with $m$ a line through $(\infty)$, or $V = (\infty)$ with $m$ a line through $(0)$.
