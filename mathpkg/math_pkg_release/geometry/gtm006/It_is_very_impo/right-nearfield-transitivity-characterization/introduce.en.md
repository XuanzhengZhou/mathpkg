A projective plane is coordinatized by a right nearfield when it admits $(V,V)$-transitivity at a distinguished point $V = (\infty)$ not on the line $k = [\infty]$, together with constraints involving an additional point $W$ on $k$. A right nearfield satisfies all field axioms except possibly the left distributive law and commutativity. This is the dual notion to the nearfield case (type (e)); the two types are interchanged by passing to the dual projective plane.
