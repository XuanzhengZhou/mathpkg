This equivalence is the dual of the quasifield characterization (type (g)). The $(V,V)$-transitivity at a point $V$ means the dual plane is a translation plane, so the original plane is coordinatized by the dual of a quasifield, i.e., a right quasifield. In a right quasifield, the multiplication is defined with the right distributive law as the primary property rather than the left distributive law. The alternate formulation using two distinct points follows because $(V,V)$-transitivity for two points implies full $(V,V)$-transitivity. The dual relationship between types (g) and (h) is a key symmetry in the overall classification.
