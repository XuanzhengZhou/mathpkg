A projective plane is coordinatized by a division ring (but is not necessarily desarguesian) if it satisfies both $(k,k)$-transitivity and $(V,V)$-transitivity for a single incident point-line pair. The distinguished line $k = [\infty]$ and point $V = (\infty)$ play the role of the line at infinity and the point at infinity in the standard coordinatization. This is a strictly weaker condition than being desarguesian, as proper division ring planes (non-skewfields) exist.
