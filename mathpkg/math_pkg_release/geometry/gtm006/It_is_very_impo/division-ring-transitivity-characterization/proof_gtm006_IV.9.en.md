This equivalence follows from the coordinatization theory developed in earlier sections. Choosing $k = [\infty]$ and $V = (\infty)$, the $(k,k)$-transitivity ensures that addition in the planar ternary ring is associative and satisfies the left distributive law, while $(V,V)$-transitivity ensures the right distributive law. Together these imply the ternary ring is linear and the algebraic structure is a division ring. Conversely, coordinatizing over a division ring canonically yields a plane with these two transitivity properties at the standard infinite elements.
