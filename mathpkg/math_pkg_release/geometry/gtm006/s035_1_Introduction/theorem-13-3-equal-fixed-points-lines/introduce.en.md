Every collineation of a finite projective plane fixes the same number of points as lines. The proof uses the incidence matrix $A$: a collineation $\alpha$ can be represented by permutation matrices $P, Q$ with $PA = AQ$, and since $A$ is nonsingular, $\mathrm{tr}(P) = \mathrm{tr}(Q)$. The trace of $P$ counts fixed points, the trace of $Q$ counts fixed lines.
