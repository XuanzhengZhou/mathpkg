Theorem 2.28 gives the algebraic conditions for a correlation to be a polarity (an involutory correlation). The conditions depend crucially on whether the associated automorphism $\phi$ is the identity (yielding orthogonal polarities with symmetric matrices) or an involution (yielding unitary/hermitian polarities). This classification is fundamental for the study of conics and unitals in classical projective planes.
