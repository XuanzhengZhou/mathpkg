Let $\beta$ be a polarity of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ represented in homogeneous coordinates by $\langle v \rangle^\beta = \langle A(v^\phi)' \rangle$ and $\langle w' \rangle^\beta = \langle (w')^\phi A^{-1} \rangle$. Then $\phi^2 = 1$ and $(A^\phi)^\beta = kA$ for some $k \in K$. If $\phi = 1$ (orthogonal polarity) then $k = 1$, so $A$ is symmetric. If $\phi \neq 1$ (unitary/hermitian polarity) then $kk^\phi = 1$.
