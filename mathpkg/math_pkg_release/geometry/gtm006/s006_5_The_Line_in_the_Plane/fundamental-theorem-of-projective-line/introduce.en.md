The Fundamental Theorem for the Projective Line (Theorem 2.23) characterizes the collineations of a projective line in terms of cross-ratio preservation. Unlike the higher-dimensional FTPG, which requires only incidence preservation, the one-dimensional case requires the stronger condition of cross-ratio preservation (since a projective line has no incidence structure beyond the set of points). This theorem completes the unification of FTPG for all dimensions.
