This theorem gives a purely geometric construction of the harmonic conjugate within a projective plane, without reference to coordinates. Given three collinear points, one constructs a complete quadrilateral whose diagonal line meets the original line at the harmonic conjugate. The proof uses coordinates to verify that the cross-ratio is indeed $-1$, but the construction itself is purely incidence-geometric.
