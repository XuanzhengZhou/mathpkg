Choose a basis $e_1, e_2$ with $P_1 = \langle e_1 \rangle$, $P_2 = \langle e_2 \rangle$, $P_3 = \langle e_1+e_2 \rangle$. Then $P_4 = \langle x e_1 + y e_2 \rangle = \langle (y^{-1}x) e_1 + e_2 \rangle$, so $(P_1, P_2; P_3, P_4) = y^{-1}x$. Expressing $x$ and $y$ in terms of the $t_i$ gives the formula.
