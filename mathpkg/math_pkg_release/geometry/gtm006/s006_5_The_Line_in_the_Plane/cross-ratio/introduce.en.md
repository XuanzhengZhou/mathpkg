The cross-ratio is the fundamental numerical invariant of projective geometry on a line. It is preserved by all projective transformations (semi-linear maps) and provides a complete invariant: a bijection of a projective line is a collineation if and only if it preserves cross-ratios (up to an automorphism of the field). The cross-ratio generalizes to arbitrary projective spaces and is essential for the concept of harmonic conjugates.
