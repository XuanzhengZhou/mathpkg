A subset $M$ of $\mathbb{R}^3$ is an \text{embedded surface} (or simply a \text{surface}) if, in the induced topology on $M$, there exists a family $(f_\alpha, U_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ of parameterized surfaces $f_\alpha: U_\alpha \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ satisfying:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] each $f_\alpha: U_\alpha \rightarrow M_\alpha$ is a homeomorphism of $U_\alpha$ onto an open subset $M_\alpha$ of $M$;
\item[(ii)] the sets $M_\alpha$ cover $M$, i.e., $\bigcup_\alpha M_\alpha = M$.
\end{enumerate}

The homeomorphisms $u_\alpha = f_\alpha^{-1}: M_\alpha \rightarrow U_\alpha$ are called \text{charts} or \text{coordinate systems}, denoted by $(u_\alpha, M_\alpha)$.
