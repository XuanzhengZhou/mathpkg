Let $M$ be a surface and let $p, q \in M$. Suppose $c_0 = c_0(t)$, $0 \leq t \leq 1$, and $c_1 = c_1(t)$, $0 \leq t \leq 1$, are two curves from $p$ to $q$. The curves $c_0$ and $c_1$ are said to be \textit{homotopic} if there exists a continuous function

$$h: [0, 1] \times [0, 1] \to M$$

such that each $c_s(t) = h(t, s)$, $0 \leq t \leq 1$, is a curve from $p$ to $q$, and $c_0(t) = h(t, 0)$ and $c_1(t) = h(t, 1)$ are the given initial curves. The family $(c_s)_{0 \leq s \leq 1}$ is called a \textit{homotopy} from $c_0$ to $c_1$.
