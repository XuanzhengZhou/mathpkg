Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface.
(i) If $B: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is an isometry, then $\tilde{f} = B \circ f$ satisfies $\tilde{I}_u(dB X, dB Y) = I_u(X, Y)$ for all $X, Y \in T_u f$.
(ii) If $\phi: V \to U$ is a change of variables and $\tilde{f} = f \circ \phi$, then $\tilde{I}_v(X, Y) = I_{\phi(v)}(d\phi X, d\phi Y)$ for all $X, Y \in T_v \mathbb{R}^2$.
