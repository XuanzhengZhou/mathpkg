A vector field along a surface is a differentiable map from the parameter domain into $\mathbb{R}^3$, interpreted as taking values in the tangent spaces of the ambient space at the surface points. This is the natural notion of a vector field attached to a parameterized surface, serving as the foundation for studying differential operators, covariant differentiation, and curvature on surfaces.
