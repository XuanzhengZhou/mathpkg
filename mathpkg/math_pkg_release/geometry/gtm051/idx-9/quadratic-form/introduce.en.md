A quadratic form, also called a symmetric bilinear form, is a real-valued map on pairs of vectors that is symmetric and bilinear. It generalizes the inner product on Euclidean space and serves as the algebraic foundation for defining the first fundamental form on surfaces, where it encodes metric information such as lengths and angles of tangent vectors.
