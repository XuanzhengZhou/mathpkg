The first fundamental form is the restriction of the Euclidean inner product to the tangent space of a surface, making it a Riemannian metric. It encodes all intrinsic metric data of the surface: lengths of curves, angles between tangent vectors, and surface area. Its matrix coefficients $g_{ik} = f_u^i \cdot f_u^k$ are the fundamental quantities of classical surface theory, traditionally denoted $E = g_{11}$, $F = g_{12} = g_{21}$, $G = g_{22}$.
