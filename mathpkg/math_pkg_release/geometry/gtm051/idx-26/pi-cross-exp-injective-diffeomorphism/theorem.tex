Let $M$ be a surface with Riemannian metric and let $p_0 \in M$. Then $p_0$ has a neighborhood $M_0 \subset M$ such that, for some $\epsilon > 0$, the map

$$\pi \times \exp: B_\epsilon M_0 \rightarrow M \times M$$

defined by $X \mapsto (\pi X, \exp X)$ is an injective diffeomorphism from $B_\epsilon M_0$ onto an open subset of $M \times M$.
