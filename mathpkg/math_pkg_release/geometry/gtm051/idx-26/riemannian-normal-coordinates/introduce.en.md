Riemannian normal coordinates are the coordinate system on a Riemannian surface obtained by composing the exponential map with a linear isomorphism $\mathbb{R}^2 \cong T_p M$ defined by an orthonormal basis. In these coordinates, geodesics passing through $p$ correspond to straight lines through the origin, and the metric takes a particularly simple form at $p$. Geodesic polar coordinates are the polar version of normal coordinates, where the radial coordinate $\rho$ measures geodesic distance from $p$ and the angular coordinate $\theta$ is measured from a chosen reference direction in $T_p M$. Both coordinate systems are essential tools for local computations in Riemannian geometry.
