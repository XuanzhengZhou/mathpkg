A moving $n$-frame along a curve is a smoothly varying orthonormal basis of $\mathbb{R}^n$ attached to each point of the curve. This construction generalizes the classical Frenet trihedron ($T, N, B$) for space curves to curves in $\mathbb{R}^n$. Moving frames provide a powerful tool for studying the local differential geometry of curves, as the derivatives of the frame vectors encode the curvature and torsion invariants through the Frenet equations.
