Let $c: I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$ be a curve such that for all $t \in I$, the vectors $\dot{c}(t), c^{(2)}(t), \ldots, c^{(n-1)}(t)$ are linearly independent. Then there exists a unique moving $n$-frame (Frenet frame) with the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item For $1 \leq k \leq n-1$, the subspaces spanned by $\dot{c}(t), \ldots, c^{(k)}(t)$ and by $e_1(t), \ldots, e_k(t)$ coincide and have the same orientation.
\item $e_1(t), \ldots, e_n(t)$ has the positive orientation in $\mathbb{R}^n$.
\end{enumerate}
This frame is called the \text{distinguished Frenet frame}.
