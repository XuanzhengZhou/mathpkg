A vector field along a curve assigns a vector in $\mathbb{R}^n$ to each point of the curve in a differentiable manner. Examples include the velocity vector field $\dot{c}(t)$, the acceleration $\ddot{c}(t)$, and the Frenet frame vectors $e_i(t)$. Vector fields along curves are essential building blocks for defining curvature, torsion, and the Frenet equations that govern the local geometry of curves.
