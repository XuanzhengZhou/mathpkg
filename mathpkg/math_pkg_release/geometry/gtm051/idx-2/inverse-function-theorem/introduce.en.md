The Inverse Function Theorem is a foundational result in multivariable calculus and differential geometry. It states that if a differentiable map $F$ from an open set in $\mathbb{R}^n$ to $\mathbb{R}^n$ has a bijective differential at a point, then $F$ is locally a diffeomorphism near that point. This theorem provides the theoretical basis for the local invertibility of smooth maps and is essential for defining differentiable manifolds, submanifolds, and for understanding the local behavior of differentiable functions.
