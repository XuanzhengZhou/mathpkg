A regular curve is a parametrized curve whose velocity vector never vanishes. This condition ensures the curve has a well-defined tangent line at every point and admits an arc-length parametrization. Regularity excludes singular points such as cusps, where the derivative vanishes and the geometric behavior can be pathological. The class of regular curves is the primary object of study in classical differential geometry of curves.
