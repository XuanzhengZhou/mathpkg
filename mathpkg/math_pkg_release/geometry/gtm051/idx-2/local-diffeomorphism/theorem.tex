A differentiable function $F: U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$ is said to be a \text{local diffeomorphism} at $0$ (or more precisely, a local diffeomorphism at $0$) if $F(0) = 0$, the differential $dF_0: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$ is bijective, and there exists an open neighborhood $U' \subset U$ of $0$ such that $F|_{U'}: U' \rightarrow F(U')$ is a diffeomorphism.
