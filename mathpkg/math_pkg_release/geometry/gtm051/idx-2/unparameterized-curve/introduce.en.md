An unparameterized curve captures the geometric essence of a curve, independent of the choice of parametrization. Two parametrized curves represent the same unparameterized curve if they differ only by a diffeomorphic reparametrization. This equivalence relation identifies curves that trace the same geometric set with the same orientation, allowing differential geometers to focus on parameter-invariant geometric quantities such as curvature and torsion.
