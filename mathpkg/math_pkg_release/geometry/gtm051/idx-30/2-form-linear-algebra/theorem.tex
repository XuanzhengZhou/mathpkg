A \textit{2-form} on a real vector space $T$ of dimension $n$ is a mapping $\Omega: T \times T \to \mathbb{R}$ satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] $\Omega(aX + bY, Z) = a\Omega(X, Z) + b\Omega(Y, Z)$ (linearity in the first variable),
  \item[(ii)] $\Omega(X, Y) = -\Omega(Y, X)$ (alternating).
\end{enumerate}
Linearity in the second variable follows from (ii) together with (i), where $X, Y, Z \in T$ and $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$.
