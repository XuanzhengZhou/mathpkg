The real projective plane $P^2$, obtained as the quotient of the 2-sphere $S^2$ by the antipodal map $x \mapsto -x$, is a fundamental example of a non-orientable surface. To see this, consider the antipodal map $i: S^2 \to S^2$, which reverses orientation. If $P^2$ possessed an oriented atlas, one could lift it to an oriented atlas on $S^2$ via the covering map $\varphi: S^2 \to P^2$, but the antipodal map would then have coordinate representation as the identity, contradicting the fact that $i$ is orientation-reversing.
