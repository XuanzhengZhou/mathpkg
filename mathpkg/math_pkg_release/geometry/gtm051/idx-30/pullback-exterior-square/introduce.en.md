Given a linear map $L: S \to T$ between vector spaces, the pullback $\Lambda^2 L^*$ transports 2-forms from the target space $T$ back to the source space $S$. For a 2-form $\Omega$ on $T$, the pulled-back form $(\Lambda^2 L^* \Omega)$ evaluates on vectors $X, Y$ in $S$ by first pushing them forward via $L$ and then applying $\Omega$. This construction is functorial and forms the algebraic basis for the pullback of differential forms on manifolds, which is essential for defining integrals of forms over mapped surfaces.
