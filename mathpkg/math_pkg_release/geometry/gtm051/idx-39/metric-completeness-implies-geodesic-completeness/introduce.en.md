This lemma establishes one direction of the Hopf-Rinow theorem for surfaces: metric completeness with respect to the intrinsic distance $d$ forces geodesic completeness. Given any unit tangent vector $X$ at a point $p$, the geodesic $\exp_p(tX)$ must be defined for all $t \geq 0$. The proof uses the fact that a Cauchy sequence along the geodesic must converge to a limit point $q$, and by the local existence of exponential maps near $q$, the geodesic can be extended beyond any finite supremum $t^*$, yielding a contradiction unless the geodesic is defined everywhere.
