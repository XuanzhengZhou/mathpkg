Let $M$ be a Riemannian surface and suppose $M$ is complete as a metric space with respect to the distance function $d(\cdot,\cdot)$. Then $M$ is geodesically complete: for every $p \in M$ and every unit tangent vector $X \in T_pM$, the geodesic $c_X(t) = \exp_p(tX)$ is defined for all $t \in \mathbb{R}^+ = \{t \in \mathbb{R} \mid t \geq 0\}$.
