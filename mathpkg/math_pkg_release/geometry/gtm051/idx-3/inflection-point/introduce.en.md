An inflection point of a plane curve is a point where the curvature vanishes and changes sign. Geometrically, this is where the curve transitions from bending one way to bending the opposite way — the osculating circle degenerates to a line at that point. The additional condition $\dot{\kappa}(t) \neq 0$ guarantees the zero of curvature is isolated, i.e., the curve does not have a flat segment.
