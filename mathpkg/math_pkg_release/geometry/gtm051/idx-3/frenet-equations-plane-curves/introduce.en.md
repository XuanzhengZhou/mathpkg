For plane curves, the Frenet equations simplify to a system of three equations involving the curve and its distinguished orthonormal frame $\{e_1, e_2\}$. The frame consists of the unit tangent $e_1$ and the unit normal $e_2$. The single curvature function $\kappa(t)$ governs how the frame rotates along the curve: the derivative of the tangent is proportional to the normal, and vice versa with a sign change, reflecting the skew-symmetry of the connection form.
