The fundamental theorem of curves in $\mathbb{R}^n$ states that the speed and curvature functions $\kappa_1, \dots, \kappa_{n-1}$ form a complete set of Euclidean invariants for a curve: two curves have the same speed and curvature functions if and only if they differ by a proper rigid motion (a rotation followed by a translation). This means the curvature functions uniquely determine the shape of the curve up to its position and orientation in space.
