Proposition 1.4.1 gives a fundamental geometric interpretation of curvature for plane curves. The curvature equals the derivative of the angle $\theta(t)$ that the unit tangent vector makes with a fixed reference direction, when the curve is arc-length parametrized. More generally, for any regular parametrization, $\dot{\theta}(t) = \kappa(t) |\dot{c}(t)|$. This shows that curvature measures the instantaneous rate of change of direction per unit arc length.
