Since $B$ is a congruence, its orthogonal component $R$ satisfies $R \in O(n)$. The distinguished Frenet frames are related by $\tilde{e}_i(t) = R e_i(t)$ (up to positive orientation). Then

$$\tilde{\omega}_{ij}(t) = \dot{\tilde{e}}_i(t) \cdot \tilde{e}_j(t) = R\dot{e}_i(t) \cdot R e_j(t) = \dot{e}_i(t) \cdot e_j(t) = \omega_{ij}(t),$$

where we use that $R$ is orthogonal, so $R\dot{e}_i(t) \cdot R e_j(t) = \dot{e}_i(t) \cdot e_j(t)$.
