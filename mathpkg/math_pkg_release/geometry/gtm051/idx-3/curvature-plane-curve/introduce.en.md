For a plane curve, there is only one curvature function $\kappa(t)$, which is a signed quantity. Unlike higher dimensions where curvatures $\kappa_1, \dots, \kappa_{n-2}$ are always positive, the plane curvature can be positive, negative, or zero. The sign carries geometric meaning: a positive curvature means the curve bends in the direction of the normal vector $e_2$, while a negative curvature means it bends oppositely.
