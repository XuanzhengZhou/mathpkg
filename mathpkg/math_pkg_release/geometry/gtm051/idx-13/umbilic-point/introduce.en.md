An umbilic (or umbilical point) of a surface is a point where the two principal curvatures coincide: $\kappa_1(u_0) = \kappa_2(u_0)$. At such points, every tangent direction is a principal direction and the surface locally resembles a sphere (or a plane, if the common curvature vanishes). Umbilic points are exceptional on generic surfaces; for instance, on a torus they occur at the latitudes where $K = 0$. The classification of umbilic points and the study of surfaces consisting entirely of umbilics (which are necessarily subsets of spheres or planes) form an important chapter in classical surface theory.
