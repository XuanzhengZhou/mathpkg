The Gauss curvature $K(u) = \kappa_1(u) \kappa_2(u)$ and the mean curvature $H(u) = \frac{1}{2}(\kappa_1(u) + \kappa_2(u))$ are the two fundamental scalar invariants derived from the principal curvatures of a surface. The Gauss curvature $K$ is the product of the principal curvatures and determines the intrinsic geometry of the surface (it is preserved under isometries), while the mean curvature $H$ is their average and governs extrinsic aspects such as minimal surfaces (where $H = 0$). Both are defined pointwise on the parameter domain $U$ of a surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ and are expressible directly in terms of the first and second fundamental forms.
