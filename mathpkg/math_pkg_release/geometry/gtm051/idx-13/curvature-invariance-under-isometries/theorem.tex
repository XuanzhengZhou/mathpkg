Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface and $X \in T_u f$ a principal direction with associated principal curvature $\kappa = \kappa(X)$. Let $K(u)$ and $H(u)$ be the Gauss and mean curvatures, respectively.

(i) If $B: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is an isometry, then $\tilde{f} := B \circ f$ is also a surface and $\tilde{X} := dB \, X \in T_u \tilde{f}$ is a principal direction of $\tilde{f}$, with
$$\tilde{\kappa}(\tilde{X}) = \pm \kappa(X), \quad \tilde{K}(u) = K(u), \quad \tilde{H}(u) = \pm H(u).$$
The signs are positive if $B$ is a congruence (orientation-preserving), negative if $B$ is a symmetry (orientation-reversing).

(ii) If $\phi: V \to U$ is a diffeomorphism (change of variables) and $\tilde{f} = f \circ \phi$, then the principal curvatures, Gauss curvature, and mean curvature are invariant: $\tilde{\kappa} = \kappa \circ \phi$, $\tilde{K} = K \circ \phi$, $\tilde{H} = H \circ \phi$.
