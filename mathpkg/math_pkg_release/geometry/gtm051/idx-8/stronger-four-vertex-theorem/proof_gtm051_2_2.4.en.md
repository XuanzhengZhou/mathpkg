The proof builds on the original four-vertex theorem and the fact that $\kappa$ is a periodic continuous function on a compact interval. The strict inequality between maxima and minima follows from the strict convexity and the fact that if a maximum were not strictly greater than a minimum, the curve would have additional self-intersections.
