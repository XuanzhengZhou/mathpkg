A simple closed convex plane curve must have either:
(i) $\kappa \equiv \text{constant} \neq 0$ (in which case the curve is a circle), or
(ii) a curvature function $\kappa$ with exactly two relative maxima and two relative minima. In the latter case, the values of $\kappa$ at the relative maxima are strictly greater than the values of $\kappa$ at the relative minima.
