Dubins' theorem solves the optimal control problem of finding the shortest path with bounded curvature (a simplified model of car-like motion). The solution curves consist of sequences of at most three pieces, each being either a circular arc (turning at maximum curvature) or a straight line segment. This is a foundational result in non-holonomic motion planning.
