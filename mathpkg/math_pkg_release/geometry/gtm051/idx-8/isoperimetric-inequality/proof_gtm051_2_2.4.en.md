The classical proof uses the fact that $\int \kappa ds = 2\pi$ (for a convex curve) and the Wirtinger inequality. For a curve parameterized by arc length, $L = \int ds$ and $A = \frac{1}{2}\int (x dy - y dx)$. The inequality $L^2 \geq 4\pi A$ follows from the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality applied to the Fourier series of the tangent angle function. Equality forces the curve to be a circle.
