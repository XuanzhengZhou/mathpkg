A simple proof is given by Horn (Amer. Math. Monthly, 78, 380-381, 1971). The key idea: if the curve is not contained in any open hemisphere, its tangent indicatrix on the unit sphere has total spherical length at least $2\pi$. Since the curve lies on the sphere of radius $r$, its length $L$ equals $r$ times the spherical length of the radial projection, giving $L \geq 2\pi r$.
