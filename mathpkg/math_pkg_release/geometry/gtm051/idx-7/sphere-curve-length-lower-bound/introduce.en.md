If a closed curve lies on a sphere of radius $r$ and is not contained in any open hemisphere, then its length must be at least $2\pi r$, the circumference of a great circle. This lemma (proved by Horn) is a key ingredient in the proof of Fenchel's theorem on the total curvature of closed space curves.
