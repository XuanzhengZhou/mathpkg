A closer look at our proof of the four vertex theorem will show that we may actually claim a stronger result: a simple closed convex curve must have either $\kappa \equiv \text{constant} \neq 0$ or a curvature function $\kappa$ with two relative maxima and two relative minima. In the latter case, we may also require that the values of $\kappa$ at the relative maxima be strictly greater than the values of $\kappa$ at the relative minima.
