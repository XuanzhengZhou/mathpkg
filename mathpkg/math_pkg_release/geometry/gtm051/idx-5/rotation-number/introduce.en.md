The rotation number (also called the winding number or index) of a closed plane curve measures the total angle through which the tangent vector turns as the curve is traversed. It is always an integer and is invariant under regular homotopy of closed curves. The rotation number is the fundamental topological invariant in the global theory of plane curves, leading to classical results such as the Umlaufsatz and the Four Vertex Theorem.
