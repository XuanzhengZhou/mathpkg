A closed curve is a curve that can be extended to a periodic function on the whole real line, with all derivatives matching at the endpoints. Equivalently, the curve and all its derivatives agree at the start and end points. Closed curves are the central objects of study in the global theory of plane curves, allowing one to define topological invariants such as the rotation number.
