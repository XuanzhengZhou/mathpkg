A minimal surface is a surface whose mean curvature is identically zero. The name derives from the fact that these are precisely the surfaces for which the first variation of area vanishes, making them critical points of the area functional. Minimal surfaces generalize the notion of geodesics (which minimize length) from curves to surfaces. Classical examples include the plane, the catenoid, and the helicoid. The study of minimal surfaces connects differential geometry with the calculus of variations and complex analysis.
