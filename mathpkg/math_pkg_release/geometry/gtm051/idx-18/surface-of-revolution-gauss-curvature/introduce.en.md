This result derives a simple formula for the Gauss curvature of a surface of revolution in terms of the profile curve. When the profile is parametrized by arc length, the Gauss curvature reduces to $K = -h''/h$, where $h(u)$ is the distance from the axis of revolution. The condition for constant Gauss curvature becomes the harmonic oscillator equation $h'' + K_0 h = 0$, whose solutions classify all surfaces of revolution with constant curvature: planes, cylinders, cones ($K_0 = 0$), spheres ($K_0 > 0$), and pseudospheres ($K_0 < 0$).
