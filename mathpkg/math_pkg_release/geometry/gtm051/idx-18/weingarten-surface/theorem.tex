A surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is called a **Weingarten surface** (or $W$-surface) if there exists a smooth function $\varphi: U \to \mathbb{R}$ with $d\varphi \neq 0$ such that the principal curvatures $\kappa_1(u) \geq \kappa_2(u)$ satisfy
$$\varphi(\kappa_1(u), \kappa_2(u)) = 0.$$

Equivalently, there exists a non-trivial functional relationship between the principal curvatures. For example, surfaces with constant mean curvature ($H = \text{constant}$) or constant Gauss curvature ($K = \text{constant}$) are $W$-surfaces.
