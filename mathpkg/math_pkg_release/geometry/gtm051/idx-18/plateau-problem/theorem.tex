**The Problem of Plateau.** Given a simple, closed, rectifiable curve $c$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$, find a minimal surface $f: D \to \mathbb{R}^3$ spanning $c$. That is, if $D$ is the open unit disk, $\bar{D}$ its closure, and $S^1 = \partial \bar{D}$ its boundary, does there exist a continuous function $\vec{f}: \bar{D} \to \mathbb{R}^3$ such that $f := \vec{f}|_D$ is a minimal surface and $\vec{f}|_{S^1}: S^1 \to c$ is a homeomorphism?

In 1930, T. Rado and J. Douglas independently proved the existence of such a minimal surface spanning $c$, which moreover achieves the minimum possible area among all surfaces spanning the curve. Their solution admitted surfaces with possible isolated singularities. In 1970, Osserman proved that singularities do not occur in the classical Douglas solution. Hildebrandt later showed that if $c$ is differentiable, then the classical solution $\vec{f}: \bar{D} \to \mathbb{R}^3$ is differentiable.
