The caustic surfaces (also called focal surfaces) of a given surface are the loci of centers of principal curvature. At each point of the original surface, the principal curvatures $\kappa_1, \kappa_2$ determine two focal points along the normal direction at distances $1/\kappa_1$ and $1/\kappa_2$. The families of these points form two surfaces, the caustic surfaces, which encode how the normal lines to the original surface focus. Caustic surfaces play an important role in the study of singularity theory and the geometric optics of wave fronts.
