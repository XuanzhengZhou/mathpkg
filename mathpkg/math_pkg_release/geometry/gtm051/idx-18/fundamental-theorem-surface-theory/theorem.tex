Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ be an open simply-connected set. Given smooth functions $g_{ij}, h_{ij}: U \to \mathbb{R}$, $i,j = 1,2$, with $g_{ij}$ positive definite and $g_{ij}, h_{ij}$ symmetric, satisfying the Gauss and Codazzi-Mainardi equations, there exists a surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ having these as its first and second fundamental forms. Moreover:

(i) **Existence.** The structural equations (3.8.1) form a system of linear PDEs for the frame $\{f_1, f_2, n\}$. The Gauss and Codazzi equations are precisely the integrability conditions $f_{i,jk} = f_{i,kj}$ and $n_{i,j} = n_{j,i}$. By the Frobenius theorem, there exists a unique solution to this system satisfying any initial conditions
$$f_i(u_0) = X_i, \quad n(u_0) = N,$$
where $X_i \cdot X_k = g_{ik}(u_0)$, $X_i \cdot N = 0$, $|N| = 1$, and $(X_1, X_2, N)$ is positively oriented. The surface is defined by
$$f(u) = \int_{u_0}^{u} \sum_i f_i(u) \, du^i + x_0.$$

(ii) **Uniqueness.** Any two surfaces $f$ and $\tilde{f}$ defined on $U$ which have the same first and second fundamental form differ by an isometry:
$$\tilde{f} = B \circ f,$$
where $B$ is an isometry of $\mathbb{R}^3$.
