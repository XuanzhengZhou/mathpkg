Theorem 3.7.9 is the central result characterizing developable surfaces in terms of intrinsic geometry. Under the hypothesis that the surface has no planar points (points where the second fundamental form vanishes identically), developability is equivalent to the vanishing of Gauss curvature $K = 0$. The forward direction follows from Proposition 3.7.5. The converse uses the existence of principal curvature directions (guaranteed by the absence of planar points) to construct $(s, t)$-coordinates in which the surface is ruled with $f_{ss} = 0$, thereby establishing developability. The theorem fails without the "no planar points" hypothesis; a counterexample is given in Section 3.9.4.
