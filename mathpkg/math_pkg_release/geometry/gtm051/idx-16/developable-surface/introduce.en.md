A developable surface is a ruled surface whose normal vector field is constant along each generator. Geometrically, this means the tangent plane does not change as one moves along a generator. Developable surfaces are precisely the ruled surfaces with vanishing Gauss curvature $K = 0$, provided there are no planar points. Important examples include tangential developables constructed from space curves and the osculating developable of a curve on a given surface.
