For a parametrized curve on a Riemannian surface $M$, the length $L(c)$ is the integral of the norm of the velocity vector with respect to the Riemannian metric, while the energy $E(c)$ is the integral of half the squared norm of the velocity. Both quantities are intrinsically defined: they do not depend on which representative $(U, g)$ in the isometry class is used for computation. The energy functional plays a central role in the study of geodesics via variational principles.
