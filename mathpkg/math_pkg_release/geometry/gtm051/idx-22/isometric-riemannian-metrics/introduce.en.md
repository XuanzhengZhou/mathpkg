Two Riemannian metrics $(U, g)$ and $(V, \tilde{g})$ on open subsets of $\mathbb{R}^2$ are equivalent (or isometric) if there exists a diffeomorphism $\phi: V \to U$ such that the metric $g$ pulled back by $\phi$ agrees with $\tilde{g}$. This defines an equivalence relation on the set of all local Riemannian metrics, and each equivalence class is precisely a local Riemannian surface.
