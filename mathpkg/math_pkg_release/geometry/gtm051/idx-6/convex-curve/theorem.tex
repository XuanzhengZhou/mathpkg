A regular plane curve $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ is \emph{convex} if, for all $t_0 \in I$, the curve lies entirely on one side of the tangent at $c(t_0)$. In other words, for every $t_0 \in I$ one and only one of the following inequalities hold:

$$(c(t) - c(t_0)) \cdot e_2(t_0) \geq 0, \quad \text{for all } t \in I$$

or

$$(c(t) - c(t_0)) \cdot e_2(t_0) \leq 0, \quad \text{for all } t \in I.$$
