A **2-form** on a differentiable manifold $M$ is a differentiable mapping
$$\Omega: TM \oplus TM \to \mathbb{R}$$
with the property that for every $p \in M$, $\Omega|_{T_pM \oplus T_pM} \in \Lambda^2 T_p^*M$, i.e., the restriction is an alternating bilinear form.

In local coordinates on a surface, every 2-form can be written as
$$\Omega = A(u) \, du^1 \wedge du^2,$$
where $du^1 \wedge du^2(X,Y) = \xi^1 \eta^2 - \eta^1 \xi^2$ for $X = \sum \xi^i e_i$, $Y = \sum \eta^j e_j$.
