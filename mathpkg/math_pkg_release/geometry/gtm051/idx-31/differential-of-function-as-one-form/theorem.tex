Let $f: M \to \mathbb{R}$ be a differentiable function on a manifold $M$. The **differential** of $f$ is the 1-form
$$df: TM \to \mathbb{R}, \quad X \mapsto d(f \circ u_\alpha^{-1}) X_\alpha,$$
where $X_\alpha$ is the representative of $X$ in $TU_\alpha$ with respect to the chart $(u_\alpha, M_\alpha)$. In particular, the coordinate 1-forms $du_\alpha^1, du_\alpha^2$ are the differentials of the coordinate functions $u_\alpha^k: M_\alpha \to \mathbb{R}$.
