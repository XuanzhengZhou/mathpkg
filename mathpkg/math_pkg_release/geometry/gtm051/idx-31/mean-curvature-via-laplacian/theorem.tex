Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a parameterized surface with coordinates $(x, y, z)$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$. Consider $x \circ f(u), y \circ f(u), z \circ f(u)$ as functions on the surface with the metric $(U, g) = (U, I)$ given by the first fundamental form. Then
$$\Delta f(u) := (\Delta(x \circ f(u)), \Delta(y \circ f(u)), \Delta(z \circ f(u))) = 2H(u) \, n(u),$$
where $H$ is the mean curvature and $n$ is the unit normal vector field.
