The Whitney sum $TM \oplus TM$ endows the fiberwise direct sum of the tangent bundle with itself with a differentiable structure, yielding a six-dimensional manifold. Its construction is parallel to that of the tangent bundle: local charts are obtained by taking the direct sum of the local trivializations. This bundle is the natural domain for 2-forms (as sections of $\Lambda^2 T^*M$) and for the Riemannian metric (as a section of $T^*M \otimes T^*M$ via the bundle isomorphism $TM \cong T^*M$).
