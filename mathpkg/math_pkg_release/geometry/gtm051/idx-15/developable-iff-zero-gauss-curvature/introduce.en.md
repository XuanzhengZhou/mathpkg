This theorem provides the definitive characterization of developable surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$: a surface without planar points is developable exactly when its Gaussian curvature vanishes identically. The "no planar points" condition is essential---a planar point is one where the second fundamental form vanishes, and at such points the curvature is zero without the surface being locally developable. The proof uses principal curvature coordinates and a delicate change of variables to show that $K = 0$ implies the surface can be parametrized as a ruled surface with constant normal along generators.
