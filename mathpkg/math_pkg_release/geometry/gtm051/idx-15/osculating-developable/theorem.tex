Let $c(t) = f \circ u(t)$ be a regular curve on a surface $f$. Suppose $Y(t)$ is a vector field along $c(t)$ tangential to $f$, satisfying $II(\dot{c}(t), Y(t)) = 0$, and linearly independent of $\dot{c}(t)$. Then
$$g(s, t) = sY(t) + c(t)$$
is a developable surface. Moreover, the surface $f$ and the constructed developable surface $g$ both contain the curve $c$, and at each point of $c$ they have identical tangent planes.
