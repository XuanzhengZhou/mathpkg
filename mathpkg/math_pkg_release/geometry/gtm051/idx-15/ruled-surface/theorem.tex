Let $f(s, t) = s X(t) + c(t)$ be a surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$, where $c(t)$ is a curve and $X(t)$ is a vector field along $c(t)$. The curves $t = \text{constant}$ are lines in $\mathbb{R}^3$ and are called \text{generators} of $f$. A curve $s = \text{constant}$ is called a \text{directrix}. The surface $f$ is called a \text{ruled surface}.
