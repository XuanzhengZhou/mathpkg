This proposition provides the local classification of developable surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$ based on the behavior of the generator direction $X(t)$. Given a developable surface written as $f(s, t) = sX(t) + c(t)$ with orthogonal generators and tangent vectors along the directrix, the derivative $\dot{X}(t)$ is proportional to $\dot{c}(t)$ via a function $r(t)$. The classification splits according to $r(t)$: cylinders have constant generator direction ($r = 0$), cones have the generator direction varying linearly with the directrix ($r$ nonzero constant), and tangential developables have both $r$ and $\dot{r}$ nonzero, representing the most general case.
