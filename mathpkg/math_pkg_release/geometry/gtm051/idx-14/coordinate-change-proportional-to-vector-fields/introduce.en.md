Given two linearly independent tangential vector fields on a surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$, one can locally change coordinates so that the coordinate vector fields of the reparametrized surface are proportional to the original vector fields. This theorem provides the fundamental tool for fitting local coordinates to prescribed directions on a surface, such as principal curvature directions. The result is specific to two-dimensional surfaces and fails for higher-dimensional manifolds.
