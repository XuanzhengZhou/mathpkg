A line of curvature on a surface is a regular curve whose unit tangent vector at every point coincides with a principal direction. Thus, along a line of curvature, the normal curvature is always extremal (equal to one of the principal curvatures). Lines of curvature form an orthogonal net on the surface away from umbilic points, and they are the natural coordinate lines in principal curvature coordinates.
