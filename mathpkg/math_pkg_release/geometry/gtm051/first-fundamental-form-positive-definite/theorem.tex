Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface.
(i) The first fundamental form $I$ is positive definite.
(ii) $I$ is differentiable, i.e., the coefficients $g_{ik}: U \to \mathbb{R}$ are differentiable. Equivalently, for any tangential vector fields $X, Y$ along $f$, the map $u \mapsto I_u(X(u), Y(u))$ is differentiable.
