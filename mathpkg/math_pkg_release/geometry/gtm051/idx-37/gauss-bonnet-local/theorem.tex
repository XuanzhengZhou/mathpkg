Let $M$ be an oriented surface with Riemannian metric. Suppose $P: F \to M$ is a diffeomorphism of a polygon $F$ onto a subset of $M$. Let $\alpha_j$, $0 \leq j \leq k$, denote the exterior angles at the vertices of $P(F)$ and let $\kappa_g$ be the geodesic curvature of the boundary curve $\partial P$ (traversed in the positive sense). Then
$$\iint_P K \, dM + \int_{\partial P} \kappa_g \, dt + \sum_j \alpha_j = 2\pi,$$
where $K$ denotes the Gauss curvature.
