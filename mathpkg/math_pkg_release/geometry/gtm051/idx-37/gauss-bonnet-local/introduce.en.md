The Gauss-Bonnet theorem (local version) is one of the most important results in the global theory of surfaces. It relates the integral of the Gauss curvature $K$ over a polygonal region $P(F)$ on an oriented Riemannian surface $M$ to the geodesic curvature $\kappa_g$ of the boundary curve and the sum of exterior angles $\alpha_j$ at the vertices. In contrast to earlier results in Chapter 6 which concern surfaces in Euclidean space, the Gauss-Bonnet theorem is a theorem of intrinsic differential geometry -- it depends only on the Riemannian metric of $M$ and not on any embedding.
