Rodriguez's theorem provides an algebraic characterization of principal directions: a unit tangent vector $X$ is a principal direction if and only if it is an eigenvector of the Weingarten map $L_u = -dn_u \circ df_u^{-1}$. The corresponding eigenvalue is precisely the principal curvature in that direction. This connects the variational definition of principal directions (as critical points of $\kappa(X)$) to the spectral theory of the self-adjoint linear map $L_u$, guaranteeing the existence of an orthonormal basis of principal directions.
