By Meusnier's theorem (3.4.3),

$$\frac{1}{\kappa(t_0)} = \frac{n(t_0) \cdot e_2(t_0)}{\kappa(X)},$$

provided $e_2(t_0) \cdot n(t_0) \neq 0$ and $\dot{c}(t_0) = \pm |\dot{c}(t_0)| X$ with $|X| = 1$. The center of the osculating circle of $c$ at $t_0$ is $c(t_0) + e_2(t_0)/\kappa(t_0)$. Substituting the expression for $1/\kappa(t_0)$ gives

$$c(t_0) + \frac{e_2(t_0)}{\kappa(t_0)} = f(u_0) + \frac{n(t_0) \cdot e_2(t_0)}{\kappa(X)} e_2(t_0).$$

Since $n(t_0) = \pm e_2(t_0)$ in the normal section case (the osculating plane contains the surface normal), this simplifies to $f(u_0) + n(u_0)/\kappa(X)$. The osculating circle therefore lies on the sphere of radius $1/|\kappa(X)|$ centered at this point, intersected with the osculating plane.
