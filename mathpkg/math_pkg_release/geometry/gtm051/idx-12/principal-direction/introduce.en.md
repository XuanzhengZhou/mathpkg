A principal direction on a surface is a unit tangent direction where the normal curvature attains a critical value. Since the unit circle in the tangent plane is compact, the continuous function $X \mapsto \kappa(X) = II(X,X)$ always achieves a maximum and minimum, yielding at least two linearly independent principal directions. The corresponding values $\kappa(X_0)$ are called the principal curvatures. They play a fundamental role in surface theory, determining the Gauss curvature (their product) and mean curvature (their average).
