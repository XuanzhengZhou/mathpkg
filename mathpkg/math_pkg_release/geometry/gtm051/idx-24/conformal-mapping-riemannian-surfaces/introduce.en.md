A conformal mapping between two surfaces equipped with Riemannian metrics is a diffeomorphism that scales the metric by a positive scalar function $\lambda(u)$. This means the Riemannian metric $\tilde{g}$ on the target surface, when pulled back by $\phi$, is proportional to the original metric $g$ at every point. Conformal mappings generalize the notion of angle-preserving transformations to the setting of Riemannian geometry and play a fundamental role in the study of surfaces of constant curvature, such as the hyperbolic plane model in the Poincare half-plane.
