Given two surfaces with Riemannian metrics $(U, g)$ and $(\tilde{U}, \tilde{g})$, a mapping $\phi: U \rightarrow \tilde{U}$ is called \text{conformal} if there exists a real strictly positive differentiable function $\lambda: U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ such that

$$\tilde{g}_{\phi(u)}(d\phi(X), d\phi(Y)) = \lambda(u) g_u(X, Y)$$

for all $X, Y \in T_u U$ and all $u \in U$.
