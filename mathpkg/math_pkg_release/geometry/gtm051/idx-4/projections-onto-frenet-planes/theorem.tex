Let $c: I \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a space curve parametrized by arc length and let $t_0 = 0 \in I$. Set $e_i(0) = e_i$ for $1 \leq i \leq 3$, and $\kappa(0) = \kappa$, $\tau(0) = \tau$, $\dot{\kappa}(0) = \dot{\kappa}$. Then the projections of $c(t)$ onto the

\begin{itemize}
  \item osculating plane at $c(t_0)$,
  \item normal plane at $c(t_0)$,
  \item rectifying plane at $c(t_0)$
\end{itemize}

have Taylor expansions at $0$ of the form

\begin{align*}
\text{osculating plane:}&\quad \left(t,\ \frac{t^2}{2} \kappa\right) + o(t^2)\\[4pt]
\text{normal plane:}&\quad \left(\frac{t^2}{2} \kappa + \frac{t^3}{6} \dot{\kappa},\ \frac{t^3}{6} \kappa \tau\right) + o(t^3)\\[4pt]
\text{rectifying plane:}&\quad \left(t - \frac{t^3}{6} \kappa^2,\ \frac{t^3}{6} \kappa \tau\right) + o(t^3)
\end{align*}
