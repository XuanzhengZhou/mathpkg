Projecting a space curve onto each of the three Frenet planes reveals its local shape from complementary perspectives. The osculating-plane projection shows the curve as a parabola up to second order, the normal-plane projection captures how the curve bends out of the osculating plane, and the rectifying-plane projection "straightens" the curve, showing it as a line up to second order. The third-order terms in these expansions involve only the curvature $\kappa$, torsion $\tau$, and the derivative $\dot{\kappa}$.
