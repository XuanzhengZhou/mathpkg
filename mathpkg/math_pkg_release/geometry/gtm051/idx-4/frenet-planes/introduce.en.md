At each point of a space curve, the Frenet frame $e_1, e_2, e_3$ spans three mutually orthogonal planes with geometric significance. The osculating plane (literally "the kissing plane") is spanned by the tangent and the principal normal — it is the plane that best fits the curve locally, and the curve's projection onto it is a parabola up to second order. The normal plane is perpendicular to the tangent vector. The rectifying plane is perpendicular to the curvature vector $\kappa e_2$, and projecting the curve onto it "straightens" the curve so that, up to second order, the projected curve appears as a straight line.
