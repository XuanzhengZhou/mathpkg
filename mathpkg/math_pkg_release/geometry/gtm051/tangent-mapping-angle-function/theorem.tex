Let $c: [a, b] \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be a regular curve. Then there exists a continuous, piecewise differentiable function $\theta: [a, b] \to \mathbb{R}$ such that
$$e_1(t) = \dot{c}(t)/|\dot{c}(t)| = (\cos \theta(t), \sin \theta(t)).$$
Moreover, the difference $\theta(b) - \theta(a)$ is independent of the choice of $\theta$.
