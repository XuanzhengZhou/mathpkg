Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface and $u_0 \in U$. There exists a change of variables $\phi: V_0 \to U_0 \subset U$ near $u_0$ with $\phi(0) = u_0$ such that if $\tilde{f} = f \circ \phi$,
$$\tilde{f}(v) - \tilde{f}(0) = v^1 X_1 + v^2 X_2 + r(v)n_0, \quad v = (v^1, v^2),$$
where $X_i = f_{u^i}(u_0)$, $n_0$ is the unit normal at $u_0$, and the Hessian of $r$ at $0$ equals the second fundamental form: $r_{v^i v^j}(0) = h_{ij}(u_0)$.
