The second fundamental form $II$ measures how a surface bends away from its tangent plane in $\mathbb{R}^3$, encoding the extrinsic curvature. It is defined using the differential of the unit normal map $n$ paired with the differential of the parametrization $f$. The closely related Weingarten map $L_u = -dn_u \circ df_u^{-1}$ (also called the shape operator) is a self-adjoint linear operator on each tangent space $T_u f$, and it satisfies $II_u(X, Y) = L_u X \cdot Y$. The matrix coefficients of $II$, traditionally denoted $L, M, N$ in Gauss' notation, give the classical second fundamental form as a quadratic form in the coordinate differentials.
