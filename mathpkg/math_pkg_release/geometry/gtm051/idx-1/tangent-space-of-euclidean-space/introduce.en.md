The tangent space $T_{x_0}\mathbb{R}^n$ formalizes the idea of vectors anchored at a point $x_0$ in Euclidean space. By tagging each vector $x \in \mathbb{R}^n$ with its base point $x_0$, we obtain a copy of $\mathbb{R}^n$ attached at $x_0$ that is vector-space isomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^n$ but geometrically distinct — different base points yield different tangent spaces. This construction is the local model for the tangent bundle of a smooth manifold, where each point carries its own linear space of tangent vectors.
