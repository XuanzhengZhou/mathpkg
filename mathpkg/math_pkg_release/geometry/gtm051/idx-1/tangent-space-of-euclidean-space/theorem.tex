For $x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n$, the \text{tangent space} of $\mathbb{R}^n$ at $x_0$, written $T_{x_0}\mathbb{R}^n$ or $\mathbb{R}_{x_0}^n$, is the $n$-dimensional vector space whose elements consist of pairs

$$(x_0, x) \in \{x_0\} \times \mathbb{R}^n.$$

The vector-space structure is defined via the bijection

$$T_{x_0}\mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n, \quad (x_0, x) \mapsto x,$$

so that

$$(x_0, x) + (x_0, y) = (x_0, x + y), \qquad \alpha(x_0, x) = (x_0, \alpha x)$$

for $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$.
