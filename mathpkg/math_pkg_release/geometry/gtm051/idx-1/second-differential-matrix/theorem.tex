Let $F: U \to \mathbb{R}^m$ be twice differentiable, $U \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ open. The differential $dF: U \to L(\mathbb{R}^n, \mathbb{R}^m)$ is a mapping into the $n \cdot m$-dimensional vector space of linear transformations. Its differential at $x_0$, denoted

$$d^2F_{x_0} = d(dF)_{x_0},$$

is determined by the $(m \times (n \cdot m))$-matrix of second-order partial derivatives

$$\left( \frac{\partial^2 F^i}{\partial x^j \partial x^k}\bigg|_{x_0} \right),$$

where $i$ is the row-index, $j$ indexes the components of $dF$, and $k$ is the column-index. The pairs $(i, j)$ are ordered lexicographically.
