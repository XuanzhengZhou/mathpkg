Let $U \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ be open and $F = (F^1, \ldots, F^m): U \to \mathbb{R}^m$ be differentiable at $x_0 \in U$. Then the matrix of the linear map $dF_{x_0}: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^m$ with respect to the standard bases is the Jacobian matrix:

$$J_F(x_0) = \left( \frac{\partial F^i}{\partial x^j}(x_0) \right)_{\substack{1 \leq i \leq m \\ 1 \leq j \leq n}}.$$

That is, for any $v \in \mathbb{R}^n$,

$$dF_{x_0}(v) = J_F(x_0) \cdot v.$$
