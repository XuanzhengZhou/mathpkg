The differential of a differentiable mapping at a point is unique: if two linear transformations both approximate $F$ up to first order with error $o(|x - x_0|)$, they must coincide. This justifies referring to "the" differential $dF_{x_0}$ rather than "a" differential, and the proof follows from estimating $|(L - L')(x - x_0)|$ and noting it must be $o(|x - x_0|)$, which forces $L = L'$.
