Buchner proved that the cut locus is stable under small perturbations of the Riemannian metric in a generic sense. For almost every point $p$ and for metrics belonging to a large generic class, there is a homeomorphism of the manifold mapping the cut locus of $p$ in the original metric to the cut locus of $p$ in the perturbed metric. However, Gluck and Singer later constructed examples showing that cut loci need not be triangulable, demonstrating that their topology can be quite wild despite this stability result.
