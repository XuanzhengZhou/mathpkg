The proof of this theorem is not included in the text; the result is cited as a theorem of Buchner. The statement involves genericity conditions: for almost all $p \in M$ and for metrics $\tilde{g}$ sufficiently close to $g$ in an appropriate $C^k$-topology, the cut loci $C(p)$ and $\tilde{C}(p)$ are homeomorphic via a homeomorphism of $M$. The result is contrasted with a construction by Gluck and Singer of cut loci that are not triangulable, indicating that cut loci can exhibit pathological topological behavior in non-generic situations.
