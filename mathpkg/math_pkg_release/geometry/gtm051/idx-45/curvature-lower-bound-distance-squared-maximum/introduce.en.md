This exercise from Section 6.8 of Klingenberg's differential geometry textbook asks to prove a curvature estimate for unit-speed curves in Euclidean space. If the squared distance from the origin attains a local maximum at a point $p_0 = c(t_0)$ along a unit-speed curve $c$, then the curvature at that point is at least $1/\rho$, where $\rho = |p_0|$. This is a quantitative relationship between the bending of a curve and how far it can get from the origin before turning back.
