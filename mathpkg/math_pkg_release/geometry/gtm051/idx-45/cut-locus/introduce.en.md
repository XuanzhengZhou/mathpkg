The cut locus $C(p)$ of a point $p$ on a compact Riemannian surface $M$ is the set of points where geodesics emanating from $p$ cease to be length-minimizing. For each unit tangent direction $X \in S^1_p M$, there is a maximal time $t(X)$ up to which the radial geodesic $\exp_p(tX)$ minimizes distance to $p$. The cut locus is the image under the exponential map of the curve $t(X)X$ in $T_p M$, and its complement can be radially contracted to $p$. The cut locus plays a fundamental role in understanding the global geometry of Riemannian manifolds.
