The proof is not provided in this section; the text notes that Cohn-Vossen initiated the detailed study of complete open surfaces and these results are summarized without proof. The bound $\int_M K\,dM \leq 2\pi$ follows from a Gauss-Bonnet-type argument adapted to open surfaces. The property that $M$ is diffeomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^2$ follows from the positivity of curvature and completeness, using the exponential map and the absence of conjugate points (since $K > 0$ but $M$ is open, the exponential map is a diffeomorphism). The statements about geodesics rely on a detailed analysis of the behavior of geodesics on positively curved open surfaces. The paraboloid of revolution is given as the canonical example where all these properties can be verified directly.
