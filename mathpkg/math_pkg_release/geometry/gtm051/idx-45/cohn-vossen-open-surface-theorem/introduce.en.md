Cohn-Vossen initiated the detailed study of complete open surfaces and proved a striking theorem: a complete, non-compact surface with everywhere positive Gauss curvature must be diffeomorphic to the plane, has total curvature at most $2\pi$, and exhibits rigid global geodesic behavior. There are no closed geodesics, every geodesic has at most one self-intersection, any two complete geodesics intersect, and through every point there passes a simple (self-intersection-free) complete geodesic. The paraboloid of revolution in $\mathbb{R}^3$ serves as the canonical example exhibiting all these properties.
