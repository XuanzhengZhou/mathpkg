This lemma asserts that at any given point, the algebraic data of a Riemannian metric — namely the metric coefficients $g_{ij}$ and the Christoffel symbols $\Gamma_{ij}^k$ — can be locally matched by those of a genuine surface $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ embedded in Euclidean space. It is a key technical step in proving the coordinate invariance of the covariant derivative in Riemannian geometry, since the transformation law for Christoffel symbols is already known to hold for embedded surfaces.
