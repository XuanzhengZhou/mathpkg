At the point $u_0$, the metric $g_{ij}(u_0)$ is a positive definite symmetric $2 \times 2$ matrix, and the Christoffel symbols $\Gamma_{ij}^k(u_0)$ are numbers satisfying the symmetry $\Gamma_{ij}^k = \Gamma_{ji}^k$. One can construct a local embedding $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ whose second-order Taylor expansion at $u_0$ is chosen so that the induced metric and its first derivatives match the prescribed data $g_{ij}(u_0)$ and $\Gamma_{ij}^k(u_0)$. The existence of such an $f$ follows from the fact that the Gauss--Codazzi equations impose compatibility conditions only on the second derivatives (curvature), while the $g_{ij}$ and $\Gamma_{ij}^k$ involve at most first derivatives of the embedding. Thus, at a single point, there is sufficient freedom in the Taylor coefficients to realize any prescribed metric and Christoffel symbols.
