Let $M$ be a surface with universal covering $\tilde{M}$ and fundamental group $\Gamma$. Suppose the Gauss curvature satisfies $K < 0$. Then for each nontrivial $\gamma \in \Gamma$, there exists at most one (up to parameter translation) $\gamma$-invariant geodesic in $\tilde{M}$. That is, if $\tilde{c}(t)$ and $\tilde{c}'(t)$ are geodesics satisfying

$$\gamma \tilde{c}(t) = \tilde{c}(t + \omega), \quad \gamma \tilde{c}'(t) = \tilde{c}'(t + \omega'),$$

then $\tilde{c}'(t) = \tilde{c}(t + t_0)$ for some $t_0 \in \mathbb{R}$.
