This technical lemma simplifies the formulas for the inverse metric coefficients and Christoffel symbols when working in orthogonal coordinates on a surface, where the off-diagonal metric component $g_{12}$ vanishes. In such coordinates, each contravariant metric coefficient $g^{ii}$ is simply the reciprocal of the corresponding covariant coefficient $g_{ii}$. The Christoffel symbols with two equal lower indices reduce to logarithmic derivatives of the square root of the metric coefficient, while those with distinct indices for the first two positions involve only derivatives of $g_{ii}$. These formulas greatly simplify calculations of covariant derivatives, geodesic equations, and curvature in orthogonal parametrizations.
