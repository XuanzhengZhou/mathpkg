Proposition 3.4.2 provides the fundamental link between the first fundamental form and the geometry of curves on a surface. Given a curve $c = f \circ u$ on a surface $f$, its velocity $\dot{c}(t)$ is a tangent vector to the surface at $u(t)$, expressed as a linear combination of the coordinate tangent vectors $f_{u^i}$. The squared length $|\dot{c}(t)|^2$ is then given by the first fundamental form $I = \sum g_{ij} du^i du^j$ evaluated on the velocity components $\dot{u}^i$. This justifies interpreting $ds^2 = \sum g_{ij} du^i du^j$ as the infinitesimal arc-length element on the surface.
