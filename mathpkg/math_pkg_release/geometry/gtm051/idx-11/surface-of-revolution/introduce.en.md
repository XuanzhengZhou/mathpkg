A surface of revolution is obtained by rotating a planar curve around a fixed axis in $\mathbb{R}^3$. The generating curve $(h(u), 0, k(u))$ lies in the $xz$-plane and is revolved about the $z$-axis, producing a surface with rotational symmetry. The condition $h'^2 + k'^2 \neq 0$ ensures regularity (the generating curve has a non-vanishing tangent), while $h \neq 0$ guarantees the curve does not intersect the axis of revolution. This construction yields a rich class of explicit surfaces used throughout differential geometry.
