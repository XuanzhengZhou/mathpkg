A \textbf{generated surface} is a surface produced by a curve $c(v)$, $v \in J$, and a one-parameter group of isometries $\gamma$ of $\mathbb{R}^3$, given by the parameterization
$$f(u, v) = \gamma(u, c(v)), \quad (u, v) \in I \times J.$$
Additional regularity conditions must be imposed to ensure that $f$ is a regular surface. The sphere, the torus, and more generally any surface of revolution are examples of generated surfaces.
