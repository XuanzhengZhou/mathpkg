A generated surface is obtained by applying a one-parameter group of isometries to a generating curve $c(v)$. The resulting surface $f(u,v) = \gamma_u(c(v))$ exhibits symmetry inherited from the group action. This construction unifies several important classes of surfaces: surfaces of revolution arise from the rotation group about an axis, while the helicoid arises from a helical (screw) motion. The condition that $f$ be regular is not automatic and must be verified by additional hypotheses on $c$ and $\gamma$.
