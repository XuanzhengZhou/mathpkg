This theorem establishes that geodesics are locally length-minimizing curves on a Riemannian surface. Given a unit-speed geodesic $c$ from $p$ to $q$, any other curve $b$ connecting the same endpoints has length $L(b) \geq L(c)$, with equality precisely when $b$ is a reparametrization of $c$ via an orientation-preserving diffeomorphism. The proof uses geodesic polar coordinates and the fact that the first fundamental form satisfies $g_{11}=1$, $g_{12}=0$ in these coordinates, so arc length is bounded below by the radial coordinate difference.
