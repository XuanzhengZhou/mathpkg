The Gauss Lemma asserts that geodesic polar coordinates, defined via the exponential map, are in fact geodesic coordinates -- meaning the metric coefficients satisfy $\tilde{g}_{11}=1$, $\tilde{g}_{12}=0$, and $\tilde{g}_{22}>0$. This result is fundamental to Riemannian geometry because it ensures that radial lines from the origin in polar coordinates are unit-speed geodesics and that they are orthogonal to the geodesic circles (level sets of constant radius). The lemma underlies many subsequent results including the computation of Gauss curvature in polar coordinates.
