In the Poincar\'e half-plane model $H_r^2$ of hyperbolic geometry, geodesics take a particularly simple Euclidean form: they are either vertical straight lines or semicircles centered on the real axis, both meeting the boundary $v = 0$ orthogonally. This description follows from the geodesic equations derived from the hyperbolic metric $ds^2 = (r^2/v^2)(du^2 + dv^2)$, and it provides a concrete geometric picture of hyperbolic straight lines as Euclidean circles orthogonal to the boundary.
