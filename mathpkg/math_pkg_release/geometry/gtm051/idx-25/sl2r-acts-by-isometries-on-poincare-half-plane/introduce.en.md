Proposition 5.1.6 establishes that the M\"obius action of $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ on the Poincar\'e upper half-plane $H_r^2$ is by isometries of the hyperbolic metric. Moreover, the action is highly transitive --- it can map any unit tangent vector to any other, making $H_r^2$ a homogeneous space for $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$. The isotropy subgroup at any point is $\mathrm{SO}(2)$, reflecting the local rotational symmetry of the hyperbolic plane.
