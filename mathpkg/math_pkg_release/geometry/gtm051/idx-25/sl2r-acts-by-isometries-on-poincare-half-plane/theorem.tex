The group $\mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ acts as a group of isometries on $H_r^2$. Moreover, the action is transitive on unit tangent vectors: given any two unit tangent vectors to $H_r^2$, there exists $g \in \mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{R})$ such that $\mathrm{d}g$ maps one into the other. The isotropy subgroup of any point of $H_r^2$ is isomorphic to $\mathrm{SO}(2)$, the group of rotations of the Euclidean plane.
