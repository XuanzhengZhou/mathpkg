The tangent space $\mathbb{T}_p M$ at a point $p$ of a surface $M$ is the fiber of the tangent bundle over $p$, consisting of all tangent vectors based at $p$. It is a two-dimensional real vector space whose inner product $g_p$ is inherited from the Riemannian metric $g$ on $M$. The tangent space encodes the first-order (linear) approximation to the surface at $p$ and serves as the domain for differential-geometric quantities such as the first and second fundamental forms.
