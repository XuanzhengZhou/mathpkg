Let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be a surface with Gauss normal field $n$. The second fundamental form $II$ is the quadratic form defined on $T_u \mathbb{R}^2$ by
$$II_u := -dn_u \cdot df_u: T_u\mathbb{R}^2 \times T_u\mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}.$$
The matrix representation with respect to the canonical basis is $(h_{ik}) := (-n_{u^i} \cdot f_{u^k}) = (n \cdot f_{u^i u^k})$. In Gauss' notation:
$$\begin{pmatrix} L & M \\ M & N \end{pmatrix} := \begin{pmatrix} h_{11} & h_{12} \\ h_{21} & h_{22} \end{pmatrix}.$$
