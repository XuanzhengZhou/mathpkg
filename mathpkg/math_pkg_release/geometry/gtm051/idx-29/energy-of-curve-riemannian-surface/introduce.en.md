The energy (or Dirichlet energy) of a curve on a Riemannian surface is half the integral of the squared norm of the velocity vector, measured with respect to the Riemannian metric $g$. Unlike length, energy is sensitive to the parametrization of the curve. Geodesics are precisely the critical points of the energy functional, making this concept fundamental to the calculus of variations on Riemannian manifolds.
