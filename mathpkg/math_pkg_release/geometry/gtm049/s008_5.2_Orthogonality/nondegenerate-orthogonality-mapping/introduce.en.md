Theorem 1 is the central structural result for orthogonal complements when the bilinear form is non-degenerate. It establishes $\perp$ and $\top$ as mutually inverse anti-automorphisms of the subspace lattice of $V$: they are bijective, reverse inclusions, interchange sums and intersections, and satisfy $M^{\perp\top} = M = M^{\top\perp}$ (a Galois connection that is perfect under non-degeneracy). The dimension formula $\dim M^{\perp} = \dim V - \dim M$ mirrors the familiar relation for orthogonal complements in inner product spaces.
