Proposition 4 gives the fundamental dimension relations for orthogonal complements with respect to an arbitrary bilinear form. The dimension of the orthogonal complement $M^{\perp}$ depends not only on $\dim M$ and $\dim V$ but also on the intersection $M \cap V^{\top}$, where $V^{\top}$ is the left radical of $\sigma$. When $\sigma$ is non-degenerate, $V^{\top}=0=V^{\perp}$ and the formulas simplify to $\dim M + \dim M^{\perp} = \dim V = \dim M + \dim M^{\top}$.
