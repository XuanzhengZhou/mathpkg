These four inequalities describe how the rank behaves under addition and multiplication of square matrices. Rank is subadditive (1), invariant under multiplication by invertible matrices (2), non-increasing under multiplication (3), and satisfies Sylvester's rank inequality (4). These results follow naturally by interpreting matrices as linear mappings: the rank of a matrix equals the dimension of the image of the corresponding linear map, and the inequalities reflect basic dimension-counting in vector spaces.
