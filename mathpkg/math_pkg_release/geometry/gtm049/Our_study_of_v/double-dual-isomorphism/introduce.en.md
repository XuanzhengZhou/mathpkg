The double dual $V^{**}$ is canonically isomorphic to $V$ via the evaluation map $\iota$, which sends a vector $v$ to the functional "evaluate at $v$". Unlike the isomorphism between $V$ and $V^*$ (which depends on a choice of basis), the isomorphism $V \cong V^{**}$ is natural -- it is defined without any arbitrary choices. This makes it a foundational example of a natural isomorphism in category theory. For infinite-dimensional spaces, $\iota$ remains injective but is generally not surjective.
