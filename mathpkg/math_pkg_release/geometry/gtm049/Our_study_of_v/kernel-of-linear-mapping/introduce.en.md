The kernel of a linear mapping is the set of all vectors that map to zero. Together with the image, it forms the two most important invariants associated with a linear mapping. The kernel is always a subspace of the domain, and its triviality characterizes injectivity: a linear mapping is one-to-one if and only if its kernel consists of only the zero vector. At the other extreme, the mapping is the zero mapping exactly when the kernel equals the entire domain.
