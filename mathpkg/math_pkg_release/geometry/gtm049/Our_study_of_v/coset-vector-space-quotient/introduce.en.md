Given a linear mapping $f: V \to V'$ with kernel $K$, the set of cosets $V/K$ inherits a natural vector space structure. Addition and scalar multiplication on cosets are well-defined because the kernel absorbs the ambiguity in choosing coset representatives. This construction is a special case of the quotient construction in universal algebra and is the vector-space analog of forming a quotient group by a normal subgroup. The resulting space $V/K$ is isomorphic to the image $Vf$.
