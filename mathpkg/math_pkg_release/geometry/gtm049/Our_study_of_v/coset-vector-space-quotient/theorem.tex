Let $f: V \rightarrow V'$ be a linear mapping with $K = \operatorname{Ker} f$. If $u \in a + K$ and $v \in b + K$, then $u + v \in (a + b) + K$ and $xu \in xa + K$ for any scalar $x$. Define an addition of cosets by
$$
(a + K) + (b + K) = (a + b) + K,
$$
and a scalar multiplication by
$$
x(a + K) = xa + K.
$$
If $V/K$ denotes the set of all cosets of $K$ in $V$, then $V/K$ is a vector space over $F$ with respect to these operations, called the \text{quotient space} of $V$ by $K$.
