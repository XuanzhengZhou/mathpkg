This corollary translates Theorem 2 into purely matrix-theoretic language: every matrix can be brought to a canonical block diagonal form by multiplying on the left and right by invertible matrices. The integer $r$ appearing in the normal form is precisely the row rank of $A$. Since the normal form is symmetric, this also implies that row rank equals column rank. The result provides the theoretical foundation for Gaussian elimination and the computation of matrix rank.
