A linear algebra over a field $F$ is a vector space equipped with an associative, bilinear multiplication that turns it into a ring compatible with the scalar multiplication. The motivating example is $\mathcal{L}(V, V)$, the space of all linear operators on a vector space, where multiplication is composition. Other important examples include polynomial rings $F[X]$ and field extensions. The structure captures the algebraic interplay between scalar multiplication and an internal product operation.
