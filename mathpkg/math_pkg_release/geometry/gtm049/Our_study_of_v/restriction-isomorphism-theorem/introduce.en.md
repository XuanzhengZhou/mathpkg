This theorem states that by decomposing the domain as a direct sum of the kernel and any complementary subspace $M$, the restriction of the linear mapping to $M$ becomes an isomorphism onto the image. This provides a canonical way to "factor out" the kernel and reveals the fundamental structure of any linear mapping: domain modulo kernel is isomorphic to the image. The result is a cornerstone of the First Isomorphism Theorem for vector spaces.
