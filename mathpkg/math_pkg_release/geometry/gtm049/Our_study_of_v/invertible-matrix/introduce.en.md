An invertible (or non-singular) square matrix is one that possesses a two-sided multiplicative inverse. The set of all invertible $n \times n$ matrices over a field $F$ forms the general linear group $\operatorname{GL}_n(F)$ under matrix multiplication. Invertibility corresponds exactly to the underlying linear mapping being an isomorphism, and thus to having non-zero determinant and full rank.
