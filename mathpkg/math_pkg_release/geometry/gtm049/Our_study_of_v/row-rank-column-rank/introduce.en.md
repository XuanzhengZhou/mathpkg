The row rank of a matrix is the dimension of the subspace spanned by its row vectors (viewed as elements of $F^n$), while the column rank is the dimension of the subspace spanned by its column vectors (viewed as elements of $F^m$). A fundamental result of linear algebra is that these two numbers are always equal; this common value is simply called the rank of the matrix. The row rank equals the rank of the associated linear mapping.
