Every ordered basis of a finite-dimensional vector space gives rise to a unique dual basis for the dual space. The dual basis $(a^i)$ is characterized by the condition $a_i a^j = \delta_{ij}$: the $j$-th dual basis vector evaluates to 1 on the $j$-th original basis vector and to 0 on all others. This theorem is a special case of the general fact that linear mappings are uniquely determined by their values on a basis. The dual basis plays a fundamental role in tensor algebra and the theory of bilinear forms.
