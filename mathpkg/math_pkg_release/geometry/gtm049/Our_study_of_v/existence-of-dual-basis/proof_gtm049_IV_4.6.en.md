Let $$(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$$ be an ordered basis of $$V$$. Define linear forms $$a^i$$ on $$V$$ by $$a_i a^j = \delta_{ij}$$ (extended linearly). Explicitly, for $$v = \sum x_j a_j$$, we have $$v a^i = x_i$$. Each $$a^i$$ is a linear mapping $$V \to F$$, so $$a^i \in V^*$$. The set $$(a^1, \ldots, a^n)$$ is linearly independent: if $$\sum y_i a^i = 0$$, then evaluating at $$a_j$$ gives $$y_j = 0$$ for all $$j$$. Since $$\dim V^* = n$$, $$(a^1, \ldots, a^n)$$ is a basis of $$V^*$$, called the dual basis of $$(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$$.
