This theorem states that by choosing appropriate bases in the domain and codomain, any linear mapping can be represented by the simplest possible matrix: a block diagonal form with an $r \times r$ identity matrix in the top-left corner (where $r$ is the rank) and zeros elsewhere. This canonical form reveals that the rank is the only invariant of a linear mapping under changes of bases. The theorem is the matrix counterpart of the First Isomorphism Theorem: every linear map factors as a projection onto a complement of the kernel, followed by an isomorphism, followed by an inclusion.
