Condition (1) means that $\operatorname{Ker} f = \{0\}$, so by Theorem 1, $f$ induces an isomorphism $V \cong Vf$, hence $\dim V = \dim Vf$. So (1) is equivalent to $\dim V = \dim Vf$. Condition (2) means $Vf = V'$, so (2) is equivalent to $\dim Vf = \dim V'$. The three equivalences now follow from the transitivity of equality of dimensions.
