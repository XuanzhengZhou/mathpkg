This corollary provides a practical criterion for determining when a linear mapping is an isomorphism. For linear maps between finite-dimensional vector spaces of equal dimension, injectivity and surjectivity are equivalent — checking one automatically yields the other. This is a special and useful feature of finite-dimensional vector spaces that fails in infinite dimensions, as illustrated by differentiation and integration operators on polynomial spaces.
