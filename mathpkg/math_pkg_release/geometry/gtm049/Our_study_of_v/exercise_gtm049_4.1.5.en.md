If $(a_1, \ldots, a_m)$ is an ordered basis of $U$ and if $b_1, \ldots, b_m$ are any vectors in $V$, show that there is one, and only one, linear mapping $f: U \rightarrow V$ such that $a_i f = b_i$, $i = 1, \ldots, m$.
