The principle of duality is a powerful meta-theorem in projective geometry: every true statement about subspaces and incidence relations has a corresponding dual statement that is also true, obtained by swapping points with hyperplanes, intersections with joins, and replacing each dimension $r$ by $n-1-r$. This effectively doubles the number of theorems available without additional work. The principle follows from the annihilator correspondence between subspaces of $V$ and $V^*$, which induces an inclusion-reversing isomorphism between the projective geometries $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\mathcal{P}(V^*)$.
