The set of all linear mappings between two vector spaces over the same field can itself be given the structure of a vector space. Addition is defined pointwise, and scalar multiplication is defined by scaling the output. This construction makes the hom-set in the category of vector spaces into an internal hom, enriched over vector spaces themselves. In the special case where domain and codomain coincide, the space acquires an additional algebra structure via composition.
