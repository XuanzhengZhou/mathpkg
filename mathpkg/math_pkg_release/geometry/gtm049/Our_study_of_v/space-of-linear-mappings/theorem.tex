Let $\mathcal{L}_F(V, V')$, or $\mathcal{L}(V, V')$ when the ground field $F$ is understood, denote the set of all linear mappings of $V$ into $V'$. If $f, g \in \mathcal{L}(V, V')$, define the mapping
$$
a \rightarrow af + ag
$$
by $f + g$; and for $x$ in $F$, the mapping
$$
a \rightarrow x(af)
$$
by $xf$. Relative to these definitions of addition and scalar multiplication, $\mathcal{L}(V, V')$ is a vector space over $F$.
