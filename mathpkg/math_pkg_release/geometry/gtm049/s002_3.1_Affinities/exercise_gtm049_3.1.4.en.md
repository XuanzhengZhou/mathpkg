Let $P$ be a projective plane and suppose that we are given a line $L$, distinct points $P, P'$ not on $L$ and a point $A$ on $PP'$ but distinct from $P$ and $P'$. Prove that there exists one, and only one, central collineation $\pi$ of $P$ with axis $L$, center $A$ and such that $P\pi = P'$. (For the definitions see immediately above.)
