An affinity is the natural notion of isomorphism between affine geometries (geometries of cosets). It is defined as a mapping induced by composing a translation, a vector space isomorphism, and another translation. The general form is $\mathcal{A}(t_{-a} g t_{a'})$, where $g$ is an isomorphism of the underlying direction subspaces $M$ and $M'$. The product of two affinities, when defined, is again an affinity. Affinities are the most natural isomorphisms to study for affine geometries over the same field.
