Let $P$ be a projective plane and $\pi$ a collineation having a line $L$ of fixed points (i.e., $X\pi = X$ for all points $X$ on $L$). If $\pi$ is not the identity mapping, prove that there exists a point $A$ (possibly on $L$) such that $A, P$ and $P\pi$ are collinear for every point $P$ in $P$.

(Such a collineation $\pi$ is called a central collineation. The line $L$ is the axis and the point $A$ is the center. Cf. also \S 3.7.)
