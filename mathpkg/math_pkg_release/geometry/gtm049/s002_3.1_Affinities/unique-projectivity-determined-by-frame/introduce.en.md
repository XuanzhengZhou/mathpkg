Theorem 2 is the projective analog of Theorem 1: a projectivity between projective geometries of the same dimension over the same field is uniquely determined by its action on a frame of reference. Given a simplex of reference $(A_0,\ldots,A_n)$ and a unit point $U$, one lets $a_0,\ldots,a_n$ be homogeneous vectors for $A_0,\ldots,A_n$ and constructs the unique isomorphism $f$ taking each $a_i$ to the standard basis vector $e_i$. The induced projectivity $\pi = \mathcal{P}(f)$ then satisfies the required mapping.
