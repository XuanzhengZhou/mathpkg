Theorem 1 establishes the fundamental uniqueness property of affinities: an affinity between affine geometries of the same dimension over the same field is uniquely determined by its action on a frame of reference. Given two frames of reference $(P_1,\ldots,P_n,Q)$ and $(P_1',\ldots,P_n',Q')$, there is exactly one affinity sending $Q$ to $Q'$ and each $P_i$ to $P_i'$. This is the affine analog of the fact that a linear map is uniquely determined by its action on a basis.
