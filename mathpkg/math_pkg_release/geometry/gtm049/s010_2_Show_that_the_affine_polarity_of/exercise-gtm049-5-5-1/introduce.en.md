This exercise derives the classical discriminant criterion for classifying affine conics. The sign of the discriminant $a_{11}a_{22} - a_{12}^2$ of the quadratic part determines whether the intersection of the corresponding projective conic with the line at infinity has 0, 1, or 2 points, yielding an ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola respectively. This connects the analytic condition to the projective-geometric definition.
