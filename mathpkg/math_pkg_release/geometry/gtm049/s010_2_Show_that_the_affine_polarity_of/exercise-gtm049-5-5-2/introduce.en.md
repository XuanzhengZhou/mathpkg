This exercise analyzes an affine polarity of the real plane whose associated conic is empty. The mapping $(x_1, x_2) \mapsto x_1X_1 + x_2X_2 + 1 = 0$ can be decomposed as a reflection in the origin followed by the standard polarity $X_1^2 + X_2^2 = 1$. The fact that the line $x_1X_1 + x_2X_2 + 1 = 0$ never passes through the origin accounts for the empty conic.
