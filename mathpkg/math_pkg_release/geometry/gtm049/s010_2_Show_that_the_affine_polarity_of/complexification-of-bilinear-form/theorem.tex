Let $V$ be a real vector space and $V_{(C)}$ its complexification. If $\sigma$ is a bilinear form on $V$, extend $\sigma$ to a mapping $\sigma_{(C)}$ on $V_{(C)}$ by
$$\sigma_{(C)}(a + ib, c + id) = \sigma(a, c) + \sigma(b, d) + i\bigl(\sigma(a, d) - \sigma(b, c)\bigr)$$
for all $a + ib, c + id \in V_{(C)}$. Then $\sigma_{(C)}$ is a sesquilinear form on $V_{(C)}$ with respect to complex conjugation, called the \text{complexification} of $\sigma$.

The complexified form $\sigma_{(C)}$ is positive definite if, and only if, the real form $\sigma$ is positive definite.
