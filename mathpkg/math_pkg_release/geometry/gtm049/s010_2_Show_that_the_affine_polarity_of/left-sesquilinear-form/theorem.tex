Let $F$ be a field and $\zeta$ an automorphism of $F$. Let $f \colon V \to V^*$ be a semi-linear mapping with respect to $\zeta$. Define $\sigma \colon V \times V \to F$ by
$$\sigma(a, b) = b(af)$$
for all $a, b \in V$. Then $\sigma$ is linear in the second variable and semi-linear with respect to $\zeta$ in the first variable. Explicitly, for all $a, b, c \in V$ and all $x, y \in F$:
\begin{align*}
\sigma(xa + yb, c) &= (x\zeta)\sigma(a, c) + (y\zeta)\sigma(b, c), \\
\sigma(a, xb + yc) &= x\sigma(a, b) + y\sigma(a, c).
\end{align*}
Such a mapping $\sigma$ is called a \text{left sesquilinear form} with respect to $\zeta$.
