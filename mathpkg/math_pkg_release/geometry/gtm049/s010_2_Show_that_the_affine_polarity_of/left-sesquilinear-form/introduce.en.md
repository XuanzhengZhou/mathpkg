A left sesquilinear form generalizes a bilinear form by allowing one variable to transform under a field automorphism rather than being strictly linear. When the automorphism is the identity, the form reduces to an ordinary bilinear form. Sesquilinear forms are essential for studying hermitian geometry and the structure of forms over fields with non-trivial automorphisms, particularly the complex numbers.
