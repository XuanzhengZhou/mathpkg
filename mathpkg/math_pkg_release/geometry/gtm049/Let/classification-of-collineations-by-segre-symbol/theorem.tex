Let $\mathcal{P}(f)$ and $\mathcal{P}(g)$ be two collineations of the projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ whose minimum polynomials factorize into linear factors over $F$. Then $\mathcal{P}(f)$ and $\mathcal{P}(g)$ have the same Segre symbol if, and only if, there exists a collineation of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ mapping the invariant configuration of $\mathcal{P}(f)$ onto the invariant configuration of $\mathcal{P}(g)$.
