This theorem shows that every collineation of a projective space admits a correlation (a projective duality transformation) that leaves its invariant configuration unchanged as a set. The proof uses the module isomorphism between $V_f$ and $(V^*)_{f^t}$ given by the transpose, and verifies that the induced correlation sends $f$-invariant subspaces to $f$-invariant subspaces and vice versa. This result is useful for analyzing collineations without relying on elementary divisor theory, particularly when the Segre symbol is not defined.
