If $f, g$ are linear mappings on the Hilbert space $(W, \sigma)$, prove

(i) $\|w f\| = \|w f^*\|$ for all $w$ in $W$;

(ii) if $w$ is an eigenvector of $f$ with eigenvalue $x$, then $w$ is an eigenvector of $f^*$ with eigenvalue $\bar{x}$;

(iii) if $[w]f \subset [w]$ then $[w]^\perp f \subset [w]^\perp$.

Deduce the following

THEOREM. If $f$ is a normal mapping on the Hilbert space $(W, \sigma)$ then there exists a cartesian basis all of whose vectors are eigenvectors of $f$.

Observe that $f$ is $\sigma$-symmetric if, and only if, $f^* = f$ and $f$ is an automorphism of $(W, \sigma)$ if, and only if, $f f^* = 1$. These mappings are often referred to in the literature as hermitian and unitary mappings, respectively, and are special cases of normal mappings. The reader should compare the above result with Proposition 4, p. 136 (from which we deduced Theorem 1) and Theorem 3H, p. 146.
