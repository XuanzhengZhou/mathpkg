A Hilbert space is a vector space equipped with a positive definite hermitian form (inner product) that induces a norm. The completeness property requires that every Cauchy sequence in the space converges to a limit within the space. For finite dimensional Hilbert spaces, completeness is automatically satisfied; the distinction only becomes meaningful in the infinite dimensional case, where completeness is a crucial additional axiom that underpins functional analysis.
