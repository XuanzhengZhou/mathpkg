This exercise demonstrates how the elementary divisors of a matrix change under field extension. The invariant factors are computed via the Smith normal form of $XI - A$ and remain the same as polynomials over any field containing the original field $F$. However, the elementary divisors (irreducible factors of invariant factors) may split further over larger fields, as illustrated by the matrix $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 2 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ whose single elementary divisor $X^2-2$ over $\mathbf{Q}$ splits into $X-\sqrt{2}$ and $X+\sqrt{2}$ over $\mathbf{R}$.
