Given two commuting linear mappings $f$ and $g$ on a vector space $V$, a reduction lemma allows us to pass from $V$ to a lower-dimensional subspace $M$ when a common eigenvector $c$ is present. By writing each $mf$ as $mf' + xc$ with $mf' \in M$, we obtain an induced mapping $f'$ on $M$. The key identity $(fg)' = f'g'$ ensures that the reduced mappings preserve the composition structure, enabling proofs by induction on $\dim V$.
