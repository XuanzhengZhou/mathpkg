This proposition adapts the simultaneous mapping reduction technique from §6.3 Exercise 3 to the setting of a vector space equipped with a bilinear form $\sigma$. When a common eigenvector $c$ of $f$ and $g$ satisfies $\sigma(c,c)=1$, the orthogonal complement $[c]^{\perp(\sigma)}$ provides a natural subspace $M$ to which the mappings can be restricted. The same induction argument then applies, with the orthogonal decomposition replacing an arbitrary direct sum decomposition.
