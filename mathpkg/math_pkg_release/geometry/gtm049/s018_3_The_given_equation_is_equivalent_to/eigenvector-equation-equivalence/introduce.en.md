This proposition provides an inductive method for solving functional equations of the form $f = \tau g^{-1}$ involving linear mappings and a bilinear form $\sigma$ on a vector space $V$. Starting from an eigenvector $a_1$ of $f$, one decomposes $V$ as the direct sum of the line spanned by $a_1$ and its orthogonal complement with respect to $\sigma$. The key observation is that the $\sigma$-orthogonal complement of $a_1$ is contained in the $\tau$-orthogonal complement, allowing the induction hypothesis to be applied on the lower-dimensional subspace.
