Let $\sigma$ be a bilinear form on $V$, let $\tau$ be a sesquilinear form on $V$, and let $f, g$ be linear mappings of $V$ such that the given equation is equivalent to $f = \tau g^{-1}$. Let $a_1$ be an eigenvector of $f$ and, without loss of generality, assume $\sigma(a_1, a_1) = 1$. Then
$$\sigma(a_1, b) = 0 \implies \sigma(a_1 f, b) = 0$$
and so $\tau(a_1, b) = 0$. Consequently,
$$W = [a_1] \oplus [a_1]^{\perp(\sigma)}$$
and
$$[a_1]^{\perp(\sigma)} \subset [a_1]^{\perp(\tau)}.$$
The result follows by induction on $\dim W$.
