This exercise interprets the structure of the multiplicative group $F^*$ of a finite field via invariant factor theory. Viewing $F^*$ as a $\mathbf{Z}$-module, its smallest invariant factor $m$ is the exponent of the group: every element satisfies $x^m = 1$, and there exists an element of exact order $m$. Since $F^*$ is in fact cyclic, the invariant factors give an alternative way to express this fundamental fact.
