This lemma captures the inductive step in the proof of uniqueness of factorization into irreducibles in a principal ideal domain. Given two factorizations of the same element, the irreducibility of the factors together with the prime property forces a matching up to units. Cancelling a matched pair reduces the number of factors, and induction on the smaller of the two factorization lengths completes the argument.
