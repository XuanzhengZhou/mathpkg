$(a + M) \cup (b + N)$ is a coset containing $a$ and $b$ and so is of the form $a + P = b + P$ for some subspace $P$ (Lemma 1). Then $P$ certainly contains $[-a + b]$ (Lemma 1), and also $M$ and $N$. But the join of $a + M, b + N$ is the least coset containing them both. Hence

$$P = [-a + b] + M + N.$$
