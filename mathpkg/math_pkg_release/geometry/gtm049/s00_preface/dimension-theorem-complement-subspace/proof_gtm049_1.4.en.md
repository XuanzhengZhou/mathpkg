By Corollary 1, any basis of $K$ can be extended to a basis of $V$. Let $\{a_1, \ldots, a_r\}$ be a basis of $K$ and extend it to a basis $\{a_1, \ldots, a_r, a_{r+1}, \ldots, a_n\}$ of $V$. Define $M = [a_{r+1}, \ldots, a_n]$. Then $K \cap M = 0$ (since the basis vectors are linearly independent) and $K + M = V$ (since together they span $V$). Hence $M \oplus K = V$.
