A group is a set equipped with an associative binary operation, an identity element, and inverses for every element. The axioms G.1 (associativity), G.2 (identity), and G.3 (inverses) capture the minimal structure needed for a set to admit an algebraic theory of symmetries. A subset that itself forms a group under the same operation is called a subgroup.
