A generalized projective geometry is a set $P$ together with a bijection $\psi$ from the concrete projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$. All geometric notions (inclusion, intersection, join, dimension) on $P$ are defined by transport through $\psi$. This abstraction is useful when the set $P$ already carries its own structure that may differ from the projective structure being imposed.
