Defines the intersection and union over an arbitrary indexed family of sets. The intersection $\cap (M_i: i \in I)$ consists of elements belonging to every $M_i$, while the union $\cup (M_i: i \in I)$ consists of elements belonging to at least one $M_i$. The index set $I$ must be non-empty for these definitions to be well-formed.
