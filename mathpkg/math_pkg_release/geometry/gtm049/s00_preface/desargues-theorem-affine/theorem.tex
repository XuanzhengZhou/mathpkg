**Theorem 3. (Desargues' Theorem for the affine plane.)** If $ABC, A'B'C'$ are two coplanar triangles in perspective from a point $P$, and if the pairs of corresponding sides intersect in points $L, M, N$, then $L, M, N$ are distinct and collinear and the line $LMN$ is distinct from the six sides of the triangles.
