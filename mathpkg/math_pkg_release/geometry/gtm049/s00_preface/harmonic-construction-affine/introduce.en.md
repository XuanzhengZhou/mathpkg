Theorem 5 (the Harmonic Construction) shows how to construct the harmonic conjugate of a point $G$ with respect to two points $A, B$ using only incidence relations. Given $A, B, G$ on a line, an arbitrary point $C$ off the line and a point $D$ on $GC$ determine auxiliary intersections $E = AD \cap BC$ and $F = BD \cap CA$; then $H = EF \cap AB$ is independent of the choices of $C$ and $D$. This is the classical construction of the fourth harmonic point.
