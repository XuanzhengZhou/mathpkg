**Theorem 7.** The mapping $\varphi: S \rightarrow [S]$ of $\mathcal{A}(c+H)$ into $\mathcal{P}(V)$ has the following properties:

(1) $\varphi$ is a one-one mapping;
(2) the image of $\mathcal{A}(c+H)$ under $\varphi$ is the subset $A$ of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ consisting of all subspaces of $V$ which are not contained in $H$;
(3) $S \subset T$ if, and only if, $[S] \subset [T]$ for all $S, T$ in $\mathcal{A}(c+H)$;
(4) $[\cap S_i] = \cap [S_i]$ if $\cap S_i$ is not empty, and
(5) $[\mathcal{J}(S_i)] = + [S_i]$;
(6) $\dim S = \operatorname{pdim} [S]$ for all cosets $S$ in $c+H$;
(7) $S$ and $T$ are parallel in $\mathcal{A}(c+H)$ if, and only if, one of the subspaces $[S] \cap H, [T] \cap H$ contains the other.
