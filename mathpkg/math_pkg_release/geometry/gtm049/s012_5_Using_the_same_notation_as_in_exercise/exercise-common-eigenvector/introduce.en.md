This exercise establishes that commuting linear mappings on a complex vector space share a common eigenvector and can be simultaneously triangularized. Using the polynomial eigenvector construction from Exercise 2 together with the fact that $f$ and $g$ commute, one shows that an eigenspace of $f$ is invariant under $g$, and induction on dimension yields simultaneous triangularization.
