Let $f$ be the automorphism of $(V, \sigma)$ whose matrix with respect to the cartesian basis $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ is $A$. By Theorem 3 there exists a cartesian basis $(b_1, \ldots, b_n)$ such that $B = (f; (b_i))$ has the required canonical form. Then $B = P^{-1} A P$ where $P = (1_V; (a_i), (b_j))$ is the change-of-basis matrix, which is orthogonal since it relates two cartesian bases.
