The rotation group (special orthogonal group) is the subgroup of the orthogonal group consisting of transformations with determinant $$+1$$. It has index 2 in the orthogonal group. The rotation group defines the notion of orientation: two Cartesian bases have the same orientation if the automorphism mapping one to the other is a rotation.
