Cross-ratio is invariant under all collineations (projective automorphisms). This means the cross-ratio is a projective invariant — it depends only on the projective structure, not on the choice of coordinate system. This invariance makes the cross-ratio a powerful tool for proving projective geometric theorems and for formulating metric relations in projective terms.
