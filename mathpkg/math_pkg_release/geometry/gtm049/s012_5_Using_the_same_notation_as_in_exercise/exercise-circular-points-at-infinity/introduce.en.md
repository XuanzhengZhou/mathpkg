This exercise explores the projective interpretation of orthogonal reflections in similarity euclidean geometry. The key result is that orthogonal reflections correspond to involutory collineations of the hyperplane at infinity that preserve the polarity, and their fixed points constitute the absolute quadric---the so-called circular points at infinity. This provides the bridge between metric (euclidean) and projective geometry.
