This lemma establishes the equivalence between preserving a definite polarity and preserving the corresponding distance class. The proof uses the formula $$\perp(\sigma^g)\mathcal{P}(g) = \mathcal{P}(g)\perp(\sigma)$$ relating the action of a collineation on polarities. This result connects the projective and metric structures of similarity Euclidean geometry.
