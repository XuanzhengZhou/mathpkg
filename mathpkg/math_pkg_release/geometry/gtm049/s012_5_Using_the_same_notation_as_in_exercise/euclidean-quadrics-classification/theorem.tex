Let $\mathcal{P}(V)$ be an $n$-dimensional real projective geometry, $H$ a hyperplane, and $\sigma$ a positive definite bilinear form on $H$. Let $\mathcal{A}(c+H, d)$ be the euclidean geometry determined by $d$ from $\sigma$. Any quadric $R$ in $\mathcal{A}(c+H)$ is the cross-section of a projective quadric $Q(\tau)$ in $\mathcal{P}(V)$, and there exists a euclidean frame of reference such that the equation of $R$ takes a canonical diagonal form.
