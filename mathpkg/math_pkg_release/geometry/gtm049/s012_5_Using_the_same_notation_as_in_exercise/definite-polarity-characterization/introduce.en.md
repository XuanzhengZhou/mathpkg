This proposition characterizes definite polarities in terms of the underlying bilinear form: a polarity is definite exactly when the form is positive or negative definite. The proof uses the intermediate value property: if the form took both positive and negative values, the quadratic equation $$\sigma(Xa-b, Xa-b)=0$$ would have a real root, giving a point on the quadric.
