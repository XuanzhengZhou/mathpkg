Let $$S$$ be a coset in a real vector space $$V$$ and $$D$$ a similarity class of distances on $$\mathcal{A}(S)$$. The affine geometry $$\mathcal{A}(S)$$ together with $$D$$ is called a **similarity Euclidean geometry** and is denoted by $$\mathcal{A}(S, D)$$.
If $$T$$ is a coset contained in $$S$$, the restriction of all distances in $$D$$ to $$\mathcal{A}(T)$$ yields a subgeometry $$\mathcal{A}(T, D)$$ of $$\mathcal{A}(S, D)$$.
