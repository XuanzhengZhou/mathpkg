A similarity Euclidean geometry consists of an affine geometry equipped with a similarity class of distances rather than a single distance. This generalization arises naturally when embedding Euclidean geometry into projective geometry: different choices of hyperplane sections produce distances that differ by a positive constant factor. The concept unifies the study of Euclidean geometry up to scaling.
