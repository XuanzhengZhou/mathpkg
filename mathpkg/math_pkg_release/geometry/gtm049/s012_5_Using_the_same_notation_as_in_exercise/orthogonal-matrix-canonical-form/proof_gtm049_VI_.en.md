Let $$f$$ be the automorphism of $$(V, \sigma)$$ whose matrix with respect to a Cartesian basis $$(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$$ is $$A$$. By Theorem 3, there exists a Cartesian basis $$(b_1, \ldots, b_n)$$ such that $$B = (f; (b_i))$$ has the required canonical form. Then $$B = P^{-1}AP$$ where $$P = (1_V; (a_i), (b_j))$$ is the change-of-basis matrix. Since both bases are Cartesian, $$P$$ is orthogonal.
