Proposition 5 identifies the similarity euclidean automorphism group of a similarity euclidean geometry $(A, \perp)$ in projective terms. The subgroup of the projective group $\operatorname{Pr}\mathcal{P}(V)$ whose elements restrict to collineations of $\mathcal{P}(H)$ preserving the definite polarity $\perp$ is isomorphic to the group of similarity euclidean automorphisms of $(A, \perp)$. This result unifies the projective and euclidean viewpoints: similarity euclidean transformations correspond precisely to projective transformations that preserve the polarity at infinity.
