This fundamental theorem states that two symmetric bilinear forms, one of which is positive definite, can be simultaneously diagonalized. The positive definite form provides the Euclidean structure (Cartesian basis), while the other form becomes diagonal in that same basis. The proof constructs the $$\sigma$$-symmetric mapping $$f = \tau\sigma^{-1}$$ and diagonalizes it. The result is essential for the classification of Euclidean quadrics.
