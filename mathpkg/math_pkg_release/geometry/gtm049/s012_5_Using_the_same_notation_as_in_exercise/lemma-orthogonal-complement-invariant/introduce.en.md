Lemma 6 states that if a subspace $M$ is invariant under an automorphism $f$ of a euclidean space (i.e., $Mf = M$), then its orthogonal complement $M^\perp$ is also invariant under $f$. The proof uses the fact that $f^{-1}$ maps $M$ to $M$, together with the property $\sigma(af, b) = \sigma(a, bf)$ for an orthogonal automorphism. This lemma is essential for the inductive decomposition of $V$ into mutually orthogonal $f$-invariant subspaces.
