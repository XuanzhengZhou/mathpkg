Theorem 1 is the principal result on simultaneous orthogonalization of symmetric bilinear forms. If $\sigma$ is a positive definite symmetric bilinear form on a real vector space $V$ and $\tau$ is any symmetric bilinear form on $V$, then there exists a basis of $V$ that is simultaneously orthonormal with respect to $\sigma$ and orthogonal with respect to $\tau$. The proof constructs the $\sigma$-symmetric linear mapping $f$ determined by $\sigma$ and $\tau$, diagonalizes $f$ via Proposition 4, and observes that the resulting eigenvectors are orthogonal with respect to $\tau$.
