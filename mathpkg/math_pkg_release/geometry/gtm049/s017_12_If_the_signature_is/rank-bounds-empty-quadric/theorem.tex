Let $Q$ be a quadric with equation $a_0 X_0^2 + \cdots + a_{r-1} X_{r-1}^2 = 0$, where $a_0 \cdots a_{r-1} \neq 0$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Over an ordered field (e.g. $\mathbb{R}$), as long as $a_0, \ldots, a_{r-1}$ are all positive or all negative, $Q(\sigma_M)$ is empty. Thus any rank $r \geq 1$ is possible.
\item[(ii)] Over an algebraically closed field, if $r > 1$, then $Q(\sigma_M)$ is not empty. Hence $r = 1$.
\item[(iii)] Over the real field $\mathbb{R}$: if $r > 2$, $Q(\sigma_M)$ is not empty; if $r = 2$ and $a_0 = a_1$, then $Q(\sigma_M)$ is empty. Hence $r$ is $1$ or $2$.
\end{enumerate}
