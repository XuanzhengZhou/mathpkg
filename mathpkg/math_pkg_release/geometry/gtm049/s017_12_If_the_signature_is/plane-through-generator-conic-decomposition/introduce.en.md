Given a quadric $Q$ and a generator $L$ of $Q$, any plane $M$ that contains $L$ intersects $Q$ in a degenerate conic of rank 2. This means the intersection $Q \cap M$ consists of two distinct lines through the point $M^\perp$. One of these lines is the generator $L$ itself; the other is the unique second line of the ruling through $M^\perp$. This result is fundamental for understanding the ruled structure of quadrics in projective 3-space.
