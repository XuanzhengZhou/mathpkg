Since $L$ is a generator, $L^\perp = L$. The point $M^\perp$ (the pole of the plane $M$ with respect to $Q$) lies on $L^\perp = L$, because $L \subseteq M$ implies $M^\perp \subseteq L^\perp$. Now consider the restriction of the bilinear form $\sigma$ to $M$. We have $M \cap M^\perp = M^\perp$, and since $M^\perp$ is a single point (a 0-dimensional subspace), the radical of $\sigma_M$ is 1-dimensional. Hence the rank of $\sigma_M$ is $\dim M - \dim(\text{radical}) = 3 - 1 = 2$. A symmetric bilinear form of rank 2 on a 3-dimensional space defines a degenerate conic, which consists of a pair of distinct lines intersecting at the radical (the point $M^\perp$). One of these lines must be $L$ since $L \subseteq M$ and $L$ lies on $Q$.
