An affine quadric $Q'$ contained in a hyperplane $K$ of affine space can be studied via its projective completion $Q$, which must lie in the union of $H$ (the hyperplane at infinity) and $K$. The classification has three generic possibilities: $Q'$ is either empty, consists of all affine points in a subspace, or consists of all points of $K$. A fourth exceptional case occurs only over the field $\mathbf{F}_3$, where $Q'$ comprises all affine points in a pair of hyperplanes within $K$.
