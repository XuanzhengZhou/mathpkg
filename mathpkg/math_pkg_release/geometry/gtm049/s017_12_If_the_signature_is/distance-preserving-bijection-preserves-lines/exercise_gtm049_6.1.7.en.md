If $d(a, c) = d(a, b) + d(b, c)$ show that $a, b, c$ are collinear in $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$.

If $\varphi$ is a one-one mapping of the points of $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ onto the points of $\mathcal{A}(S', d')$ which preserves distance, show that $\varphi$ induces a euclidean isomorphism of $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ onto $\mathcal{A}(S', d')$.
