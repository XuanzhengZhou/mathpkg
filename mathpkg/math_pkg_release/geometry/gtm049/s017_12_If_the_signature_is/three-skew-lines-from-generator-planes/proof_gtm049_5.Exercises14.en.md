Let $M_1, M_2, M_3$ be three distinct planes containing the generator $L$. By the previous result, each $Q \cap M_i$ is a pair of distinct lines through $M_i^\perp$, one of which is $L$. Let the other line in $M_i$ be $G_i$. Then $G_1, G_2, G_3$ are generators of $Q$ lying in distinct planes. Since $G_i$ and $G_j$ (for $i \neq j$) lie in different planes $M_i$ and $M_j$, and the only line common to $M_i$ and $M_j$ is $L$, the lines $G_i$ and $G_j$ do not intersect. Moreover, if $G_i$ and $G_j$ were coplanar, the plane containing them would intersect $Q$ in at least two lines, but a plane through two skew generators would contradict the structure of the quadric. Hence $G_1, G_2, G_3$ are pairwise skew generators of $Q$.
