Using the same notation as in exercise 4, assume now that $d$ satisfies all the rules D.1--D.6. Prove that, for fixed $a, b$ in $V$,

$$x \rightarrow \sigma(xa, b)$$

is a continuous real function. Deduce from this and exercise 4 that $\sigma$ is a bilinear form on the real vector space $V$.
