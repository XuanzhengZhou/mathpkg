The Jordan canonical form theorem provides a particularly simple matrix representation for a linear transformation $f$ when the minimum polynomial splits into linear factors over the base field $F$. In this case, the elementary divisor decomposition of the $F[X]$-module $V_f$ yields a basis in which the matrix of $f$ is block diagonal with Jordan blocks. Each Jordan block $C_{ij}$ has a single eigenvalue $x_i$ on the diagonal and $1$ on the superdiagonal. Over algebraically closed fields such as $\mathbf{C}$, every square matrix is similar to its Jordan canonical form.
