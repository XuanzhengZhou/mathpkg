Let $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ be an ordered basis of $V$ over $F$ and let vectors $a, b$ have coordinate rows $x = (x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, $y = (y_1, \ldots, y_n)$ with respect to this basis. Then

$$\sigma(a, b) = \sum_{i,j} s_{ij} x_i y_j,$$

where $s_{ij} = \sigma(a_i, a_j)$. If $S$ is the $n \times n$ matrix $(s_{ij})$, this may be written as

$$\sigma(a, b) = x S y^t.$$

The matrix $S$ is called the matrix of $\sigma$ with respect to $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$, denoted $(\sigma; (a_i)) = S$. The polynomial $\sum s_{ij} X_i Y_j$ is called the bilinear polynomial of $\sigma$ with respect to $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$.
