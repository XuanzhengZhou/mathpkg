Let $\sigma$ belong to $\mathcal{B}(V)$. Since $\sigma$ is linear in the second variable, the mapping

$$b \mapsto \sigma(a, b)$$

is a linear form on $V$. Denote this form by $a\varrho$. Since $\sigma$ is linear in the first variable, the assignment $a \mapsto a\varrho$ is a linear mapping $\varrho: V \to V^*$.

Similarly, define $\tilde{\sigma}: V \to V^*$ by $(b\tilde{\sigma})(a) = \sigma(a, b)$. If $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ is an ordered basis of $V$ and $s_{ij} = \sigma(a_i, a_j)$, then

$$(\varrho; (a_i), (a^j)) = (s_{ij}),$$
$$(\tilde{\sigma}; (a_i), (a^j)) = (s_{ij})^t.$$

The ranks of $\varrho$ and $\tilde{\sigma}$ are both equal to the rank of $\sigma$.
