A bilinear form $\sigma$ induces two natural linear maps from $V$ to its dual space $V^*$: the map $\varrho$ obtained by fixing the first argument, and the map $\tilde{\sigma}$ obtained by fixing the second argument. Their matrices with respect to dual bases are the matrix of $\sigma$ and its transpose, respectively. Both maps have the same rank as $\sigma$, connecting the theory of bilinear forms to the theory of linear transformations between a space and its dual.
