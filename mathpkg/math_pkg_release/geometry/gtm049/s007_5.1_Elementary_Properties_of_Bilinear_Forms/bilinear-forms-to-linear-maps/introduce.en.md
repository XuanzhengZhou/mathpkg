Proposition 3 establishes the fundamental connection between bilinear forms and linear transformations: the space $\mathcal{B}(V)$ of all bilinear forms on $V$ is naturally isomorphic to the space $\mathcal{L}(V, V^*)$ of all linear maps from $V$ to its dual. This isomorphism allows one to translate problems about bilinear forms into problems about linear maps between a vector space and its dual, and vice versa.
