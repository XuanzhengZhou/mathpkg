An isomorphism of bilinear spaces is a vector space isomorphism that additionally preserves the values of the bilinear form. When two bilinear spaces are isomorphic, the corresponding bilinear forms are called congruent. Congruence is the natural equivalence relation for bilinear forms, analogous to similarity for linear transformations. It means the two forms differ only by a change of basis.
