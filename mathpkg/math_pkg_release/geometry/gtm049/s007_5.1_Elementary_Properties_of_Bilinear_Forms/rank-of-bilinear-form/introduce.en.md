The rank of a bilinear form is defined as the rank of any matrix representing it. This definition is unambiguous because all representing matrices of a given bilinear form lie in the same congruence class, and congruent matrices have the same rank. A bilinear form is non-degenerate when its matrix has full rank, which means the form provides a perfect pairing between the vector space and itself.
