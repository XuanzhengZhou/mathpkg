Proposition 2 establishes that the matrices representing a bilinear form under different choices of ordered basis are all congruent to one another. Conversely, every matrix congruent to the matrix of $\sigma$ arises as the matrix of $\sigma$ with respect to some basis. This makes the congruence class of matrices an invariant of the bilinear form, independent of the choice of basis.
