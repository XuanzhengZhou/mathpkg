By Theorem 2, $V = L_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus L_s \oplus V^\perp$ where each $L_i$ is a non-degenerate 2-dimensional alternating subspace. Each $L_i$ has an ordered basis $(b_i, c_i)$ such that $\sigma(b_i, c_i) = x_i 
eq 0$. With respect to $(b_i, (1/x_i)c_i)$, the restriction of $\sigma$ to $L_i$ has matrix $egin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$. Taking such a basis for each $L_i$ and any basis for $V^\perp$, we obtain an ordered basis for $V$ with respect to which $\sigma$ has the matrix $A_{n,s}$.
