There exists an ordered basis with respect to which an alternating bilinear form $\sigma$ of rank $2s$ on an $n$-dimensional vector space has matrix

$$A_{n,s} = \begin{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} & & & & \\
& \ddots & & & \\
& & \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} & & \\
& & & 0 & \\
& & & & \ddots
\end{pmatrix}$$

where there are $s$ blocks of $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ on the diagonal and zeros elsewhere. Every $n \times n$ alternating matrix of rank $2s$ is congruent to $A_{n,s}$.
