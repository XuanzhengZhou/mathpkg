A lower triangular matrix is a square matrix where all entries above the main diagonal are zero. The importance of this definition in the context of bilinear forms comes from Theorem 4, which shows that over fields with more than two elements, any non-alternating bilinear form can be represented by a triangular matrix after a suitable change of basis.
