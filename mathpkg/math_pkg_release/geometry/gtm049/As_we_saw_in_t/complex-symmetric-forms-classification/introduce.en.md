Proposition 7 establishes the complete classification of symmetric bilinear forms over the complex numbers: any such form is congruent to a diagonal matrix with $r$ ones followed by zeros, where $r$ is the rank. The proof uses only the fact that every complex number has a square root, allowing us to scale the basis vectors to normalize the diagonal entries to 1.
