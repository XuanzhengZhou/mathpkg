An affine isomorphism is a composition of a linear isomorphism and a translation. For the linear part, if $T: M 	o M'$ is a linear isomorphism, then $q \circ T^{-1}$ is a quadratic form on $M'$ and $l \circ T^{-1}$ is a linear form on $M'$, so the image of the quadric $\{a: q(a) + l(a) + z = 0\}$ under $T$ is $\{b: q(T^{-1}b) + l(T^{-1}b) + z = 0\}$, which is again an affine quadric. For translations, if $m \in M$, then $q(a+m) = q(a) + arphi(a,m) + q(m)$ where $arphi$ is the associated bilinear form, so the translated equation is again of the form $q(\cdot) + l'(\cdot) + z' = 0$ for appropriate $l'$ and $z'$.
