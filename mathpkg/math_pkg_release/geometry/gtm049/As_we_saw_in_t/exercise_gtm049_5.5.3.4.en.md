Let $\mathcal{B}^+(V)$ denote the set of all symmetric bilinear forms on $V$ and $\mathcal{B}^-(V)$ the set of all skew-symmetric ones. Prove that $\mathcal{B}^+(V)$ and $\mathcal{B}^-(V)$ are subspaces of the vector space $\mathcal{B}(V)$ of all bilinear forms on $V$. If the ground field $F$ is not of characteristic 2, prove that

$$\mathcal{B}(V) = \mathcal{B}^+(V) \oplus \mathcal{B}^-(V).$$
