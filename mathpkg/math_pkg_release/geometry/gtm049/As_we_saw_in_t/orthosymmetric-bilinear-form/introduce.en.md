An orthosymmetric bilinear form is one for which the orthogonality relation is symmetric: $a$ is orthogonal to $b$ precisely when $b$ is orthogonal to $a$. This condition is equivalent to the equality of the left and right orthogonal complements, $\bot(\sigma) = \top(\sigma)$. The concept of orthosymmetry captures exactly when orthogonality is a truly symmetric relation, a property essential for the geometric theory of bilinear forms.
