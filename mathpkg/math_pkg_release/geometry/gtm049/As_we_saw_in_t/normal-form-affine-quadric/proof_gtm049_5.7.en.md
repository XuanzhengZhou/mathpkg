Let $A$ be the affine geometry determined by the hyperplane $H$ in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and let $Q(\sigma)$ be a quadric in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ that determines the given affine quadric $Q'$ in $A$.

	extbf{Case (i):} If $H^\perp 
ot\subset H$, choose any vector $a_0 \in H^\perp \setminus H$ and any ordered basis $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ of mutually orthogonal vectors for $H$ (using Theorem 3). The equation of $Q$ is then of the form $d_0 X_0^2 + d_1 X_1^2 + \cdots + d_n X_n^2 = 0$, which gives $d_1 X_1^2 + \cdots + d_n X_n^2 + d_0 = 0$ when substituting $X_0 = 1$.

	extbf{Case (ii):} If $H^\perp \subset H$, then by a more detailed analysis using Proposition 4, we can choose coordinates so that the equation takes the form $X_1 + d_2 X_2^2 + \cdots + d_n X_n^2 = 0$.
