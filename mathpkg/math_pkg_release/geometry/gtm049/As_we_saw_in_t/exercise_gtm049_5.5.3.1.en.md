Prove that a bilinear form $\sigma$ on a vector space $V$ is symmetric or skew-symmetric if, and only if, its matrix with respect to any one ordered basis is symmetric or skew-symmetric. (Recall that a matrix $A$ is symmetric if $A = A^t$; it is skew-symmetric if $A = -A^t$.)
