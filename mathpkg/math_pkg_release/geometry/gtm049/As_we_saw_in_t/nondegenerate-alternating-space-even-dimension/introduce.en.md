Corollary 1 states that if $(V, \sigma)$ is a non-degenerate alternating space, then the dimension of $V$ must be even. This follows from the structure theorem for alternating forms (Theorem 2), which decomposes $V$ into an orthogonal direct sum of hyperbolic planes and the radical. A fundamental consequence is that symplectic vector spaces always have even dimension.
