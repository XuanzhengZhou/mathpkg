A euclidean space is a real vector space equipped with a positive definite bilinear form $\sigma$, which serves as an inner product. This structure allows the definition of length (norm) and distance between vectors, providing the algebraic foundation for Euclidean geometry. The distance $d(a,b) = \|a-b\|$ satisfies the usual metric properties including symmetry, positivity, and the triangle inequality.
