Let $L, L'$ be two lines in $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$. Through any point $A$ on $L$ we take the line $L''$ parallel to $L'$ and choose points $B, C$ on $L, L''$, respectively, both distinct from $A$. As $B$ and $C$ vary, $\widehat{BAC}$ takes just two values in $[0, \pi]$ and the sum of the two values is $\pi$. The angle between $L$ and $L'$ is defined to be the value in $[0, \pi/2]$.

Thus the cosine of the angle between $L$ and $L'$ is given by
$$\frac{|\sigma(u, u')|}{\|u\| \|u'\|},$$
for any $u \neq 0$ in the subspace belonging to $L$ and any $u' \neq 0$ in the subspace belonging to $L'$, where $\sigma$ is the bilinear form giving the distance $d$.
