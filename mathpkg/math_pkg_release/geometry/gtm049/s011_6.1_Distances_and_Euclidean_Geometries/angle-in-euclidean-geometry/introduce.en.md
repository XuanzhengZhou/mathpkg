The angle $\widehat{BAC}$ in a Euclidean geometry is defined by translating the geometric configuration to the underlying Euclidean vector space. The vectors from $A$ to $B$ and from $A$ to $C$ are formed by taking coordinate differences, and their angle (as defined via the inner product) is taken as $\widehat{BAC}$. This reduces the geometric notion of angle to the algebraic one.
