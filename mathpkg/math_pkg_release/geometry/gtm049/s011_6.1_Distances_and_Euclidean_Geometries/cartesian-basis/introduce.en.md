A cartesian basis is an orthonormal basis of a Euclidean space, consisting of mutually orthogonal unit vectors. With respect to such a basis, the distance formula reduces to the standard Euclidean distance in $\mathbf{R}^n$ (the square root of the sum of squared coordinate differences), and the angle between vectors is expressed via the usual dot product formula.
