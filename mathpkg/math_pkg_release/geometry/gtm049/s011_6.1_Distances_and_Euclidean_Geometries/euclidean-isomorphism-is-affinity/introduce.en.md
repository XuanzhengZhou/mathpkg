This proposition establishes that every isomorphism between Euclidean geometries is automatically an affinity (an affine transformation). Moreover, its underlying linear part is an isomorphism of the corresponding Euclidean vector spaces, meaning it preserves the inner product. This connects the metric notion of isomorphism with the algebraic structure of the underlying space.
