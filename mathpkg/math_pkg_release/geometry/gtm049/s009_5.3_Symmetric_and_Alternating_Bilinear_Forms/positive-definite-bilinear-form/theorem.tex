A real-valued symmetric bilinear form $\sigma$ is \textbf{positive semi-definite} if $q(a) = \sigma(a, a) \geq 0$ for all $a$. If moreover $q(a) = 0$ implies $a = 0$, then $\sigma$ is \textbf{positive definite}. The definitions of \textbf{negative semi-definite} and \textbf{negative definite} are similar.
