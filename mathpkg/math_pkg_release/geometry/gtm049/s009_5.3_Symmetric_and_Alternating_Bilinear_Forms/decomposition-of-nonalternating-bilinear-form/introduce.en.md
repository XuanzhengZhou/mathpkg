Theorem 4 generalizes Theorem 3 to arbitrary non-alternating bilinear forms (not necessarily symmetric), provided the ground field has more than two elements. The decomposition yields one-dimensional non-degenerate subspaces $A_i$ with the "upper-triangular" orthogonality condition $\sigma(A_i, A_j) = 0$ for $i < j$. This result is false over $\mathbb{F}_2$.
