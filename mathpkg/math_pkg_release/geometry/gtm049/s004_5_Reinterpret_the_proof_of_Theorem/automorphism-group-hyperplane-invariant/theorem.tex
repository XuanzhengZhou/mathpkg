Let $H$ be a hyperplane in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and let $\mathcal{A}$ be the affine geometry in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ determined by $H$. Let $(\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V))_H$ and $(\operatorname{Pr} \mathcal{P}(V))_H$ be the subgroups of $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\operatorname{Pr} \mathcal{P}(V)$ whose elements leave $H$ invariant. Then the mapping $\rho: \pi \mapsto \alpha$ defined in Proposition 3 is an isomorphism of $(\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V))_H$ onto $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{A}$ and of $(\operatorname{Pr} \mathcal{P}(V))_H$ onto $\operatorname{Af} \mathcal{A}$.
