A field isomorphism is a bijective mapping between two fields that preserves both addition and multiplication. This concept generalizes the familiar automorphisms of fields such as complex conjugation. Field isomorphisms are the structure-preserving maps in the category of fields, and they play a critical role in the definition of semi-linear isomorphisms between vector spaces over different fields.
