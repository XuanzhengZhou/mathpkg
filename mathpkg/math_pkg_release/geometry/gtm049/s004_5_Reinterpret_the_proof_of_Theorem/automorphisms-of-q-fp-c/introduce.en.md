This exercise establishes that $\mathbf{Q}$ and $\mathbf{F}_p$ have no non-trivial automorphisms, while $\mathbf{C}$ has exactly two automorphisms that fix $\mathbf{R}$ pointwise: the identity and complex conjugation. This has important consequences for semi-linear isomorphisms: over $\mathbf{Q}$, $\mathbf{F}_p$, and $\mathbf{R}$, every semi-linear isomorphism is actually linear.
