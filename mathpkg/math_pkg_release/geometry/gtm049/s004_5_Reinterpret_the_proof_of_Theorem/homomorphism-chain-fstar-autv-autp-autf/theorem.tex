Let $V$ be a vector space of dimension $\geq 3$ over a field $F$ and let $\varphi, \psi, \theta$ be the homomorphisms

$$F^* \xrightarrow{\varphi} \operatorname{Aut} V \xrightarrow{\psi} \operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V) \xrightarrow{\theta} \operatorname{Aut} F$$

where $\varphi: z \mapsto z1$, $\psi: f \mapsto \mathcal{P}(f)$ and for any $\pi$ in $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V)$, $\pi\theta$ is the automorphism of $F$ determined by $\pi$ according to Theorem 6. Then

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\varphi$ is one--one;
\item[(ii)] $\psi$ has kernel $F^*\varphi$ and maps $\operatorname{Aut} V$ onto the projective group $\operatorname{Pr} \mathcal{P}(V)$;
\item[(iii)] $\theta$ has kernel $\operatorname{Pr} \mathcal{P}(V)$ and maps $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V)$ onto $\operatorname{Aut} F$.
\end{enumerate}
