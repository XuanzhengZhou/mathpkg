This exercise provides the canonical example of a semi-linear isomorphism: applying a field automorphism $\zeta$ componentwise to vectors in $F^n$. This mapping is semi-linear with respect to $\zeta$ and is linear only when $\zeta$ is the identity. It illustrates how non-trivial field automorphisms produce genuinely semi-linear (not linear) isomorphisms.
