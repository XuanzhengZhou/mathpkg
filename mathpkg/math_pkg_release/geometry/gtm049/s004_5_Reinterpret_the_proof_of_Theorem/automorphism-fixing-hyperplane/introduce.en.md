Lemma 3 is a technical linear algebra result used in the proof of Theorem 8. It asserts that given a subspace $M$ and two vectors $p, q$ outside $M$, one can find an automorphism of $V$ that sends $p$ to $q$ while fixing a hyperplane containing $M$. The induced projective transformation is then a central collineation, making this lemma the key construction step for decomposing arbitrary collineations into products of central collineations.
