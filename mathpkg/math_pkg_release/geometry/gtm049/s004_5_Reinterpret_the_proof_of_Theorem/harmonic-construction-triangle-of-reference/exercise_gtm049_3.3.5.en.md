Reinterpret the proof of Theorem 10 of Chapter II (the Harmonic Construction) by taking $ABC$ as triangle of reference. When $G$ is distinct from $A$ and $B$ (and the ground field is not a field of characteristic 2) then $EFG$ may be taken as triangle of reference and $D$ as unit point. Show that then $A, B, C$ have coordinate rows $(-1, 1, 1), (1, -1, 1), (1, 1, -1)$, respectively.
