A semi-linear isomorphism is a generalization of a linear isomorphism that allows for a field automorphism to act on the scalars. While a linear isomorphism satisfies $f(xa) = x f(a)$, a semi-linear isomorphism satisfies $f(xa) = \zeta(x) f(a)$ where $\zeta$ is a field isomorphism. This concept is fundamental in projective geometry because the fundamental theorem of projective geometry shows that every projective isomorphism arises from a semi-linear isomorphism.
