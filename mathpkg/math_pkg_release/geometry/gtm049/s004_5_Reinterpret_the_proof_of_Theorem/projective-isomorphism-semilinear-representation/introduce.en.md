Theorem 6 is the fundamental theorem of projective geometry. It states that every isomorphism between projective geometries of dimension at least 2 is induced by a semi-linear isomorphism of the underlying vector spaces. In dimension 1, the isomorphism only determines a set bijection between the fields. This theorem is a projective analogue of Theorem 5 and is foundational for the study of automorphism groups of projective spaces.
