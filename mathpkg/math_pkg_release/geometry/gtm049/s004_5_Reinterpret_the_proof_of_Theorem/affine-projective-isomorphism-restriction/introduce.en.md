Proposition 3 establishes the precise relationship between projective and affine isomorphisms. A projective isomorphism that maps a chosen hyperplane at infinity to the corresponding hyperplane restricts to an affine isomorphism. Conversely, every affine isomorphism extends uniquely to a projective isomorphism preserving the hyperplane at infinity. When the ground fields coincide, this correspondence respects the distinction between projectivities (collineations) and affinities.
