If $V$ is a vector space of dimension $n$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$, prove that $\operatorname{Aut} V$ is finite and that its order (i.e., the number of its elements) is

$$\left(p^n - 1\right)\left(p^n - p\right) \ldots \left(p^n - p^{n-1}\right).$$

(Use exercise 3 of §1.5 and Theorem 3 of Chapter I.)
Calculate the orders of $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{A}(V)$.
