Given fields $F$, $F'$ and a mapping $\varphi: F \to F'$ such that

$$\begin{align*}
(x+y)\varphi &= x\varphi + y\varphi, \\
(xy)\varphi &= (x\varphi)(y\varphi)
\end{align*}$$

for all $x, y$ in $F$; prove that $\varphi$ is either an isomorphism of $F$ onto $F\varphi$ or $x\varphi = 0_{F'}$ for all $x$ in $F$.
