Invariant factors are the uniquely determined ideals (or their monic polynomial generators) that appear in the cyclic decomposition of a finitely generated module over a principal ideal domain. They form an ascending chain $\mathfrak{a}(a_1) \subset \cdots \subset \mathfrak{a}(a_k)$, where each successive invariant factor divides the next. For a torsion module, all invariant factors are non-zero. The invariant factors provide a complete set of isomorphism invariants: two finitely generated modules over a PID are isomorphic if and only if they have the same invariant factors. In linear algebra, the invariant factors of the $F[X]$-module $V_f$ correspond to the invariant factors of the linear transformation $f$.
