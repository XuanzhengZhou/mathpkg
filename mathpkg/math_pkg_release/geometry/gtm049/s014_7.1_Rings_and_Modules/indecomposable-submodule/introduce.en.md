An indecomposable submodule is one that cannot be expressed as a direct sum of two non-zero proper submodules. In the context of finitely generated modules over a PID, the cyclic submodules appearing in the elementary divisor decomposition are indecomposable: each has an annihilator generated by a power of an irreducible element, which cannot be factored further as a direct sum. The invariant factor decomposition, by contrast, combines indecomposables into as few cyclic summands as possible. Thus the two decompositions represent extreme cases: the elementary divisor decomposition maximizes the number of summands, while the invariant factor decomposition minimizes it.
