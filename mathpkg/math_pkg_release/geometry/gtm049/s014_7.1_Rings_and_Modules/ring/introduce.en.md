A ring is an algebraic structure that generalizes the arithmetic of integers and polynomials, consisting of a set equipped with two binary operations: addition and multiplication. The additive structure forms a commutative group, while multiplication is associative and distributes over addition. Rings serve as the foundational structure for module theory, where the "scalars" are taken from a ring rather than a field. Common examples include the integers $\mathbb{Z}$, polynomial rings $F[X]$, and fields themselves, which are special cases of rings.
