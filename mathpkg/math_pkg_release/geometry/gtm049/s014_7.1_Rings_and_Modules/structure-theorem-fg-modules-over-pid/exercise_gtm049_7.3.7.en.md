In part (b) of the proof of Theorem 1 assume that
$$m_1b_1 + \cdots + m_kb_k = 0$$
is a relation in which $m_k$ is a non-zero coefficient of minimum length (as defined in exercise 5 above). Use exercises 5 and 6 to adapt the proof of Theorem 1 to the case of an arbitrary principal ideal domain.
