Let $M = [a]$ and suppose that $p = st$, where $s, t$ are relatively prime polynomials in $F[X]$. Since $F[X]$ is a principal ideal domain and $s, t$ are coprime, there exist polynomials $u, v \in F[X]$ such that $us + vt = 1$.

Set $b = s a$ and $c = t a$. Then $M = [b] \oplus [c]$. Indeed, $a = 1 \cdot a = (us + vt)a = u b + v c$, so $b$ and $c$ generate $M$. Moreover, $t b = t s a = p a = 0$, so $\mathfrak{a}(b)$ contains $t$. Similarly, $\mathfrak{a}(c)$ contains $s$.

If $x b = 0$, then $x s a = 0$, so $p = st$ divides $xs$. Since $s, t$ are coprime, $t$ divides $x$. Hence $\mathfrak{a}(b) = [t]$. Similarly, $\mathfrak{a}(c) = [s]$.

The intersection $[b] \cap [c] = 0$ because any element in the intersection is annihilated by both $s$ and $t$, hence by their gcd which is $1$, forcing the element to be $0$.

Applying this argument repeatedly to factor $p$ into powers of distinct irreducible polynomials yields the desired decomposition
$$M = [a_1] \oplus \cdots \oplus [a_n]$$
where $\mathfrak{a}(a_i)$ is generated by a power of the irreducible factor $p_i$.
