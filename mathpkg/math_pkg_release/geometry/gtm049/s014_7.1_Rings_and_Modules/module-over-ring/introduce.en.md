A module over a ring $R$ (or $R$-module) generalizes the notion of a vector space by replacing the field of scalars with a commutative ring with identity. The module axioms M.1--M.5 mirror the vector space axioms exactly, preserving the requirements of additive commutativity, distributivity of scalar multiplication over addition, associativity of scalar multiplication, and the identity action. Modules appear throughout algebra: vector spaces are modules over fields, abelian groups are modules over $\mathbb{Z}$, and ideals of a ring are submodules of the ring regarded as a module over itself.
