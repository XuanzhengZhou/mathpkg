The quotient module $M/K$ is the module-theoretic analogue of a quotient vector space. Its elements are the additive cosets of the submodule $K$ in $M$, with addition and scalar multiplication defined via representatives. When $M = R$ and $K = \mathfrak{a}$ is an ideal, the quotient $R/\mathfrak{a}$ is simultaneously a quotient ring and a cyclic $R$-module generated by $1 + \mathfrak{a}$. The first isomorphism theorem holds for modules: $M/\ker f \cong \operatorname{im} f$ for any $R$-homomorphism $f$.
