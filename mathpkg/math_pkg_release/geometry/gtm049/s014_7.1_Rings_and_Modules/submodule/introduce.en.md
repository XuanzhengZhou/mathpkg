A submodule is the module-theoretic analogue of a subspace in vector space theory. It is a non-empty subset of a module that is closed under addition and scalar multiplication, and itself satisfies the module axioms with the inherited operations. When a commutative group $A$ is viewed as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module, its submodules are precisely its subgroups. When a ring $R$ is viewed as an $R$-module over itself, its submodules are the ideals of $R$. The entire theory of subspaces from linear algebra (intersections, sums, quotients) extends verbatim to submodules.
