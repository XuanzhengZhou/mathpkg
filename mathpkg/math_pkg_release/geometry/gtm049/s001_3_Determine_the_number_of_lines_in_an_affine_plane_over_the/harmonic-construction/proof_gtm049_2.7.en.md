The proof is almost identical to the one given for Theorem 5 (the affine version). In the projective plane, the construction is always valid: the exceptional cases where $$E$$, $$F$$, or $$H$$ failed to exist in the affine plane are not exceptions here. The argument proceeds by choosing homogeneous coordinates and using the incidence relations to show that $$H$$ is independent of the choices of $$C$$ and $$D$$.
