In projective geometry, two triangles are said to have an axis of perspective when the three intersection points of their corresponding sides are distinct, collinear, and the line through them is distinct from the sides of the triangles. This concept is dual to the notion of a center of perspective and plays a central role in Desargues' theorem, which states that two triangles have a center of perspective if and only if they have an axis of perspective.
