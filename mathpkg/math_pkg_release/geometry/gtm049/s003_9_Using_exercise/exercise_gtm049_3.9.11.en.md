Let $z, x$ be elements of a division ring $D$ such that $zx \neq xz$ and let $M, N$ be the subspaces of $D^3$ spanned by $\{(1, 0, 0), (0, 1, 0)\}$, $\{(1, 0, 0), (0, 0, 1)\}$, respectively. If $g$ is the isomorphism of $M$ onto $N$ defined by $(1, 0, 0)g = (z, 0, 0)$ and $(0, 1, 0)g = (0, 0, z)$, show that $g$ does not extend to a collineation of the projective geometry $P$ associated with $D^3$.
