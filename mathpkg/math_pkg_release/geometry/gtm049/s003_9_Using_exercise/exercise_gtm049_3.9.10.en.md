Let $V$ and $V'$ be non-zero vector spaces over a division ring $D$ (cf. p. 39) and let $f$ be an isomorphism of $V$ onto $V'$. If $z$ is a non-zero element of $D$ show that the mapping

$$zf: v \mapsto z(vf)$$

is an isomorphism if, and only if, $zx = xz$ for every $x$ in $D$.

Show that the uniqueness part of Theorem 2 cannot be extended to geometries $P, P'$ over a division ring $D$ which is not commutative. (We remarked on page 39 that the truth of Pappus' Theorem in a projective plane over $D$ is sufficient to ensure that $D$ is commutative. This exercise gives another such criterion.)
