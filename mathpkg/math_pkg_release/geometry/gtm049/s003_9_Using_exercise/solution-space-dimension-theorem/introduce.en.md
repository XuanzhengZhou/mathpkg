The solution space of a homogeneous system of $m$ linear equations in $n$ unknowns over a field is always a subspace of $F^n$. Its dimension equals $n - r$, where $r$ is the rank of the coefficient matrix — that is, the maximum number of linearly independent rows. This is a fundamental result linking the algebraic structure of linear equations with the geometric concept of dimension in vector spaces.
