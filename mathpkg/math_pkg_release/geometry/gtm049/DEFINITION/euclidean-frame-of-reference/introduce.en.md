A Euclidean frame of reference is the geometric counterpart of a Euclidean coordinate system: it specifies an origin point $Q$ and $n$ points $P_i$ such that the vectors $p_i - q$ form a Cartesian basis of the underlying vector space. This gives a geometric description of what it means to set up coordinates in a Euclidean geometry: choose an origin and an orthonormal basis of directions.
