If $a \in M$ and $b \in M^{\perp}$, then by definition of $M^{\perp}$, $\sigma(a, b) = 0$. But this means that $a$ is orthogonal to every vector in $M^{\perp}$ when placed in the first argument, i.e., $a \in (M^{\perp})^{\top} = M^{\perp \top}$. Thus $M \subset M^{\perp \top}$. The other inclusion $M \subset M^{\top \perp}$ is proved similarly.
