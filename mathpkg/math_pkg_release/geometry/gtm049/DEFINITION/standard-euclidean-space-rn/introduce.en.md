The standard Euclidean space $\mathbf{R}^n$ is the prototypical example of a Euclidean space, with the usual dot product as its bilinear form. Any $n$-dimensional Euclidean space is isomorphic to $(\mathbf{R}^n, \sigma_0)$, which serves as a universal model. This isomorphism is realized by a Euclidean coordinate system.
