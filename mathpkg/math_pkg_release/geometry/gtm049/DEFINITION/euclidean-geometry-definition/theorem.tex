Consider a coset $S = a + M$ in a real vector space $V$ and suppose $d$ is a distance on $M$. A distance on $S$ is defined by

$$d(a + m_1, a + m_2) = d(m_1, m_2)$$

for all $m_1, m_2 \in M$. This definition is independent of the choice of representative $a$ because distances are invariant under translations (D.4).

The pair $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ is called a \textbf{euclidean geometry}.
