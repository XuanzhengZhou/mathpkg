Schwarz's inequality (also known as the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality) bounds the absolute value of the bilinear form evaluated at two vectors by the product of their lengths. It is the fundamental inequality of Euclidean geometry, from which the triangle inequality follows as a direct corollary. The condition for equality characterizes when the two vectors are collinear.
