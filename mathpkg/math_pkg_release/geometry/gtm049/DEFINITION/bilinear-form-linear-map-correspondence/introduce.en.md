Every bilinear form $\sigma$ on $V$ induces a linear map $\varrho: V \to V^*$ by fixing the first argument: $a \mapsto \sigma(a, \cdot)$. Since $\sigma$ is linear in the second variable, $\sigma(a, \cdot)$ is indeed a linear form. The correspondence $\sigma \leftrightarrow \varrho$ is a linear isomorphism, establishing a fundamental link between the theory of bilinear forms and the theory of linear mappings.
