An isomorphism of Euclidean geometries is an affine isomorphism that additionally preserves distances between points. This means that the underlying linear isomorphism of the direction spaces must preserve the bilinear form. Distance preservation and bilinear form preservation are equivalent conditions: a linear map preserves the distance if and only if it preserves the bilinear form.
