Let $S = a + M$ be a coset in a real vector space $V$, and let $d$ be a distance on $M$. Define a mapping (also denoted by $d$) on $S \times S$ by
$$d(a + m_1, a + m_2) = d(m_1, m_2).$$
This definition is independent of the choice of representative $a$ by translation invariance (D.4). The pair $\mathcal{A}(S, d)$ is called a \textbf{euclidean geometry}.
