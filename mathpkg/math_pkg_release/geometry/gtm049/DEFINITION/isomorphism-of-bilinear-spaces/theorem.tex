An \textbf{isomorphism} of the bilinear space $(V, \sigma)$ onto the bilinear space $(V', \sigma')$ is a linear isomorphism $f$ of $V$ onto $V'$ which satisfies

$$\sigma(a, b) = \sigma'(af, bf)$$

for all $a, b$ in $V$. If such an isomorphism $f$ exists we say that $(V, \sigma)$ and $(V', \sigma')$ are \textbf{isomorphic}.

If $(V, \sigma)$ and $(V', \sigma')$ are isomorphic then $\sigma$ and $\sigma'$ are said to be \textbf{congruent}.
