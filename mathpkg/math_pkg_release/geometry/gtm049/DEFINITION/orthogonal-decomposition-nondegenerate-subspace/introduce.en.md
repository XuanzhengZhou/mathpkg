If a subspace $M$ of a bilinear space is itself non-degenerate (meaning the restriction of the bilinear form to $M$ is non-degenerate), then the whole space decomposes as a direct sum of $M$ and its orthogonal complement. This is a fundamental decomposition theorem that generalizes the orthogonal decomposition of inner product spaces and is crucial for the structure theory of bilinear forms.
