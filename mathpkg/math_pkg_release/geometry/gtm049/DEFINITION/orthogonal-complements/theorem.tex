Let $\sigma$ be a bilinear form on $V$ and $M$ any subspace of $V$.

\begin{itemize}
\item $M^{\perp}(\sigma)$ is the set of all vectors $b \in V$ such that $\sigma(M, b) = 0$, i.e., $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a \in M$.
\item $M^{\top}(\sigma)$ is the set of all vectors $a \in V$ such that $\sigma(a, M) = 0$.
\end{itemize}

When the bilinear form $\sigma$ is understood, we write $\perp$ for $\perp(\sigma)$ and $\top$ for $\top(\sigma)$.

$M^{\perp}$ and $M^{\top}$ are always subspaces of $V$. Moreover, $M \subset N$ implies $M^{\perp} \supset N^{\perp}$ and $M^{\top} \supset N^{\top}$, and $0^{\perp} = 0^{\top} = V$.
