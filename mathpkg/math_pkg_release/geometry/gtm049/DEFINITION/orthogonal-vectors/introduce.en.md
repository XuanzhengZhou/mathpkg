Orthogonality with respect to a bilinear form generalizes the geometric notion of perpendicularity. Two vectors are orthogonal if the bilinear form vanishes on their ordered pair. Note that orthogonality need not be symmetric unless the bilinear form is symmetric. The concept extends naturally to subsets: two subsets are orthogonal if every vector in the first is orthogonal to every vector in the second.
