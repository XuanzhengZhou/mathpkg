Given an ordered basis of a finite-dimensional vector space, every bilinear form corresponds uniquely to an $n \times n$ matrix whose entries are the values of the form evaluated at pairs of basis vectors. This correspondence is a linear isomorphism between the space of all bilinear forms and the space of all $n \times n$ matrices. Similarly, bilinear forms correspond to bilinear polynomials in $2n$ variables. These isomorphisms allow bilinear algebra to be studied via matrix algebra.
