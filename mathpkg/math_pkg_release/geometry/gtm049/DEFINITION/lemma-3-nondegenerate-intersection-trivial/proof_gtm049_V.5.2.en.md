Suppose $a \in M \cap M^{\perp}$. Then $a \in M$ and $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $b \in M$. Since the restriction $\sigma|_M$ is non-degenerate, this forces $a = 0$. The same argument applies to $M \cap M^{\top}$ using the non-degeneracy of $\sigma|_M$ with respect to the first variable.
