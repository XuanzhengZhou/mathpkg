A \textbf{cartesian basis} of a euclidean space $(V, \sigma)$ is an ordered basis $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ of $V$ consisting of mutually orthogonal vectors of length 1. That is,

$$\sigma(a_i, a_j) = \delta_{ij} \quad (\text{Kronecker delta}).$$

With respect to a cartesian basis, if $d$ is the distance determined by $\sigma$ and $a = \sum x_i a_i$, $b = \sum y_i a_i$, then

$$d(a, b) = +\sqrt{(x_1 - y_1)^2 + \cdots + (x_n - y_n)^2}.$$

For non-zero vectors $a, b$, the angle $\theta$ between them is given by

$$\cos \theta = \frac{x_1 y_1 + \cdots + x_n y_n}{+\sqrt{(x_1^2 + \cdots + x_n^2)(y_1^2 + \cdots + y_n^2)}}.$$
