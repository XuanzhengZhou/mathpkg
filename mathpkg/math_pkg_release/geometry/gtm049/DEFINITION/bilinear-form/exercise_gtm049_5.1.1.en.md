Consider the following properties of a bilinear form $\sigma$ on a vector space $V$ (not necessarily finite dimensional):

(i) $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $b \in V$ implies $a = 0$;

(ii) $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a \in V$ implies $b = 0$.

Prove that (i) is equivalent to $\varrho_\sigma$ being one-one and (ii) is equivalent to $\tilde{\sigma}$ being one-one.

If $V$ is finite dimensional, show that (ii) is also equivalent to $\varrho_\sigma$ being onto $V^*$.
