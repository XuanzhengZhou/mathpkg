The vector $b$ lies in $(M\varrho)^{\circ}$ if and only if $b f = 0$ for all $f \in M\varrho$, i.e., for all $f = a\varrho$ with $a \in M$. By the definition of $\varrho$, the condition $b(a\varrho) = 0$ is equivalent to $\sigma(a, b) = 0$ for all $a \in M$, which means precisely $b \in M^{\perp}(\sigma)$. The proof for $\top$ is exactly analogous using $\tilde{\sigma}$.
