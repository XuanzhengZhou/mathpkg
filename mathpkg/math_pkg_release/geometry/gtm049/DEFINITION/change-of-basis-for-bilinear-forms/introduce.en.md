When the ordered basis of a vector space is changed, the matrix of a bilinear form transforms by congruence: the new matrix equals the transition matrix times the old matrix times the transpose of the transition matrix. This implies that all matrix representations of a given bilinear form belong to the same congruence class. The rank of the bilinear form is therefore well-defined, as congruent matrices have equal rank.
