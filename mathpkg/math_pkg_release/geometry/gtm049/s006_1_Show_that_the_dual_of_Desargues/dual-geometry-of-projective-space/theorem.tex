The set of all subspaces of $V$ together with the incidence mapping $\circ$ is a projective geometry. We denote this geometry by $\mathcal{P}^*(V)$ and call it the dual geometry of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. If $M$ is a member of $\mathcal{P}(V)$, then $M$ has projective dimension $n-1-r$ as a member of $\mathcal{P}^*(V)$ (where $\operatorname{pdim} V = n$ and $r$ is the projective dimension of $M$ in $\mathcal{P}(V)$).
