The Segre symbol is a combinatorial invariant extracted from the elementary divisors of a linear mapping whose minimal polynomial splits into linear factors over the base field. It records, for each eigenvalue, the tuple of sizes of the corresponding Jordan blocks (equivalently, the invariant factors of the module $V_f$), providing a complete invariant for the similarity class of the mapping. In projective geometry, the Segre symbol classifies collineations by encoding the structure of their invariant subspaces.
