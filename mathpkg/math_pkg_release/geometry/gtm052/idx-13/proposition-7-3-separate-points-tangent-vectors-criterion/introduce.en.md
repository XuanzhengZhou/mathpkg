Proposition 7.3 provides a geometric criterion for a morphism to $\mathbf{P}^n$ to be a closed immersion. Over an algebraically closed field, one needs the global sections generating $\mathcal{L}$ to separate points and separate tangent vectors. This is a fundamental tool for proving that a given linear system defines an embedding of a projective variety.
