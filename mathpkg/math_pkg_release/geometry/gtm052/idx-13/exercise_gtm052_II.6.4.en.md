Let $k$ be a field of characteristic $\neq 2$. Let $f \in k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ be a square-free nonconstant polynomial, i.e., in the unique factorization of $f$ into irreducible polynomials, there are no repeated factors. Let $A = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]/(z^2 - f)$. Show that $A$ is an integrally closed ring. [Hint: The quotient field $K$ of $A$ is $k(x_1, \ldots, x_n)[z]/(z^2 - f)$. It is a Galois extension of $k(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ with Galois group $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.]
