We only need to show every invertible sheaf is isomorphic to a subsheaf of $\mathcal{K}$, which is the constant sheaf $K$ (the function field). Let $\mathcal{L}$ be any invertible sheaf, and consider $\mathcal{L} \otimes_{\mathcal{O}_X} \mathcal{K}$. On any open set $U$ where $\mathcal{L} \cong \mathcal{O}_X$, we have $\mathcal{L} \otimes \mathcal{K} \cong \mathcal{K}$, so it is a constant sheaf on $U$. Since $X$ is irreducible, any sheaf whose restriction to each open set of a covering is constant is in fact constant. Thus $\mathcal{L} \otimes \mathcal{K} \cong \mathcal{K}$, and the natural map $\mathcal{L} \to \mathcal{L} \otimes \mathcal{K} \cong \mathcal{K}$ expresses $\mathcal{L}$ as a subsheaf of $\mathcal{K}$.
