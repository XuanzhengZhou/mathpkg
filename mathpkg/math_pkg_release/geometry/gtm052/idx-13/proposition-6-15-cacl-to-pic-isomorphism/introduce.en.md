Proposition 6.15 shows that for integral schemes, the Cartier class group and the Picard group are isomorphic. The proof uses the fact that on an integral scheme, every invertible sheaf embeds into the constant sheaf $\mathcal{K}$ (the function field), allowing the recovery of a Cartier divisor from an invertible sheaf.
