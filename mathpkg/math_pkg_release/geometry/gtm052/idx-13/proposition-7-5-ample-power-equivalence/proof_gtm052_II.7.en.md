(i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii) is immediate from the definition of ample. (ii) $\Rightarrow$ (iii) is trivial.

(iii) $\Rightarrow$ (i): Assume $\mathcal{L}^m$ is ample. Given a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$, there exists $n_0 > 0$ such that $\mathcal{F} \otimes (\mathcal{L}^m)^n$ is generated by global sections for all $n \geq n_0$. Considering the coherent sheaves $\mathcal{F} \otimes \mathcal{L}^k$ for $k = 0, 1, \ldots, m-1$, there exist $n_k$ such that $\mathcal{F} \otimes \mathcal{L}^k \otimes (\mathcal{L}^m)^n$ is generated by global sections for all $n \geq n_k$. Taking $N = \max\{k + mn_k\}$, for any $n \geq N$ we can write $n = k + mr$ with $r \geq n_k$, and hence $\mathcal{F} \otimes \mathcal{L}^n$ is generated by global sections. Thus $\mathcal{L}$ satisfies the definition of ampleness.
