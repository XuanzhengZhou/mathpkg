Let $X = \text{Spec } k[x,y,z]/(xy - z^2)$ be the affine quadric cone. The ruling $Y$ (defined by $y=z=0$) is a Weil divisor which is not locally principal. Thus $Y$ does not correspond to a Cartier divisor. However, $2Y$ is principal. In this case the group of Cartier divisors modulo principal divisors is $0$, whereas $\text{Cl} X \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.
