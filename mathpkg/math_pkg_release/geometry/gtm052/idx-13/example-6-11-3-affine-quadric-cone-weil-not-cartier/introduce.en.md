Example 6.11.3 provides a concrete instance where Weil and Cartier divisors differ. On the affine quadric cone, the ruling $Y$ is a Weil divisor but not Cartier (its ideal is not principal even locally at the vertex). The Cartier class group is trivial while $\text{Cl} X \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$, illustrating the gap between the two theories on singular schemes.
