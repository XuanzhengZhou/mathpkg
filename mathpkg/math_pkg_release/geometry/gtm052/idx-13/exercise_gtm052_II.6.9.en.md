**Singular Curves.** Here we give another method of calculating the Picard group of a singular curve. Let $X$ be a reduced projective curve over an algebraically closed field $k$. Let $\tilde{X}$ be its normalization, and let $f: \tilde{X} \to X$ be the natural morphism. Let the singular points of $X$ be $P_1, \ldots, P_r$, and let $f^{-1}(P_i) = \{Q_{i1}, \ldots, Q_{is_i}\}$. Define the sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ by the exact sequence
$$0 \to \mathcal{O}_X^* \to f_* \mathcal{O}_{\tilde{X}}^* \to \mathcal{F} \to 0.$$
Show that $\text{Pic} X \cong \text{Pic} \tilde{X} \times H^0(X, \mathcal{F}) / \text{Im } H^0(\tilde{X}, \mathcal{O}_{\tilde{X}}^*)$.
