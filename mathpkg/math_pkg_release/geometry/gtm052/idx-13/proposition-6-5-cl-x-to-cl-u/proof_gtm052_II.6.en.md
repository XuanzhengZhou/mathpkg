The surjectivity in (a) follows because any divisor on $U$ extends (by closure) to a divisor on $X$. For (b), when $\text{codim}(Z, X) \geq 2$, every prime divisor on $U$ extends uniquely to a prime divisor on $X$, giving the inverse map. For (c), when $Z$ is irreducible of codimension 1, it corresponds to a prime divisor; the kernel of the restriction map is generated by $Z$, giving the exact sequence.
