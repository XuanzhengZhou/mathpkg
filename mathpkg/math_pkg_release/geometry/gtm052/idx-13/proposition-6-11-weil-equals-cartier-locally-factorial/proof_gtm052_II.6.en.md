Given a Weil divisor $D = \sum n_i Y_i$, for each $Y_i$ the local ring $\mathcal{O}_{X,Y_i}$ is a UFD of dimension 1, hence a DVR. Let $t_i$ be a local parameter. On an open set $U$ meeting only finitely many $Y_i$, define $f = \prod t_i^{n_i} \in \Gamma(U, \mathcal{K}^*)$. This gives a Cartier divisor.

Conversely, given a Cartier divisor $\{(U_i, f_i)\}$, for each prime divisor $Y$ define $v_Y$ using the DVR $\mathcal{O}_{X,Y}$. The sum $\sum v_Y(f_i) \cdot Y$ gives a Weil divisor, independent of the chosen $U_i$ containing the generic point of $Y$. These constructions are mutually inverse, and principal divisors correspond under the isomorphism.
