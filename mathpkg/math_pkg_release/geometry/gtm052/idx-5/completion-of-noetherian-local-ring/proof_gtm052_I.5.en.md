See Atiyah–Macdonald [1, Ch. 10, 11] or Zariski–Samuel [1, vol. 2, Ch. VIII].

The proof relies on standard results in commutative algebra. For (a), the natural map $A \to \hat{A}$ is injective by Krull's intersection theorem. The maximal ideal is $\hat{\mathfrak{m}} = \mathfrak{m}\hat{A}$, and $\hat{A}/\hat{\mathfrak{m}} \cong A/\mathfrak{m}$. For (b), the isomorphism $\hat{M} \cong M \otimes_A \hat{A}$ holds for finitely generated modules because $\hat{A}$ is a flat $A$-module and completion is exact on finitely generated modules. For (c), $\dim A = \dim \hat{A}$ follows from the fact that the going-up and going-down theorems hold for the faithfully flat extension $A \to \hat{A}$. For (d), one uses the isomorphism $\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2 \cong \hat{\mathfrak{m}}/\hat{\mathfrak{m}}^2$ together with (c) and the definition of regularity.
