Theorem 5.3 shows that the singular locus $\operatorname{Sing} Y$ of an algebraic variety is always a proper closed subset. This means nonsingularity is a generic property: the nonsingular points form a dense open subset. The proof reduces to the case of an affine hypersurface, where the singular locus is defined by the vanishing of all partial derivatives, and uses the fact that a non-constant polynomial in characteristic $0$ (or separable in positive characteristic) has at least one non-vanishing partial derivative.
