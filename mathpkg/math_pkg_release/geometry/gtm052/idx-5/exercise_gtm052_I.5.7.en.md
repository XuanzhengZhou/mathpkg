Let $Y \subseteq \mathbf{P}^2$ be a nonsingular plane curve of degree $> 1$, defined by the equation $f(x,y,z) = 0$. Let $X \subseteq \mathbf{A}^3$ be the affine cone over $Y$ (defined by the same equation $f(x,y,z) = 0$ in $\mathbf{A}^3$). Show that $X$ has exactly one singular point, namely the origin $O = (0,0,0)$.
