Proposition 5.2A states that for any noetherian local ring $A$, the embedding dimension $\dim_k \mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2$ is always at least the Krull dimension $\dim A$. This inequality explains why regular local rings are characterized by equality: they are precisely the rings where the minimal possible value of the embedding dimension is achieved. The result follows from Krull's principal ideal theorem and Nakayama's lemma.
