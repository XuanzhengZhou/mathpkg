\textbf{Quadric Hypersurfaces.} Assume $\operatorname{char} k \neq 2$, and let $f$ be a homogeneous polynomial of degree $2$ in $x_0, \ldots, x_n$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] Show that after a suitable linear change of variables, $f$ can be brought into the form $f = x_0^2 + \cdots + x_r^2$ for some $0 \leqslant r \leqslant n$.
\item[(b)] Show that $f$ is irreducible if and only if $r \geqslant 2$.
\item[(c)] Assume $r \geqslant 2$, and let $Q$ be the quadric hypersurface in $\mathbf{P}^n$ defined by $f$. Show that the singular locus $Z = \operatorname{Sing} Q$ of $Q$ is a linear variety (Ex. 2.11) of dimension $n - r - 1$. In particular, $Q$ is nonsingular if and only if $r = n$.
\item[(d)] In case $r < n$, show that $Q$ is a cone with axis $Z$ over a nonsingular quadric hypersurface $Q' \subseteq \mathbf{P}^{n-Z-1}$.
\end{enumerate}
