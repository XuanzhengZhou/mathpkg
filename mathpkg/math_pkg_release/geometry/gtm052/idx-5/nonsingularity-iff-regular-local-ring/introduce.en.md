Theorem 5.1 establishes that the geometric definition of a nonsingular point (via the rank of the Jacobian matrix) coincides with the purely algebraic condition that the local ring is regular. This is the foundational result that allows one to study singularities using commutative algebra. The proof constructs an isomorphism between the Zariski cotangent space $\mathfrak{m}/\mathfrak{m}^2$ and the cokernel of the Jacobian transpose, linking the two definitions.
