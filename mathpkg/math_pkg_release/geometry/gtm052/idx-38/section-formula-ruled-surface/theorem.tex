If $D$ is any section of $X$, corresponding to a surjection $\mathcal{E} \rightarrow \mathcal{L} \rightarrow 0$, and if $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}(\mathfrak{d})$ for some divisor $\mathfrak{d}$ on $C$, then $\deg \mathfrak{d} = C_0.D$, and

$$D \sim C_0 + (\mathfrak{d} - \mathfrak{e})f.$$

In particular, we have $C_0^2 = -\deg \mathfrak{e} = e$.
