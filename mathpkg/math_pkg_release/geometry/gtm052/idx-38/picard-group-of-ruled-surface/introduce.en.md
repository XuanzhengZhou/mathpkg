The Picard group of a ruled surface $X$ splits as $\mathbf{Z} \oplus \pi^*\operatorname{Pic} C$, where the $\mathbf{Z}$ factor is generated by a chosen section $C_0$. The numerical equivalence group $\operatorname{Num} X$ is free of rank $2$, generated by $C_0$ and the fibre class $f$, with intersection numbers $C_0.f = 1$ and $f^2 = 0$. This simple structure underlies the entire theory of ruled surfaces.
