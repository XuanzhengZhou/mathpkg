When the base curve $C$ has genus $g = 1$ (an elliptic curve), the indecomposable ruled surfaces are severely restricted: the invariant $e$ can only be $0$ or $-1$. Moreover, for each value there is exactly one such surface up to isomorphism. The $e = 0$ case corresponds to the unique nontrivial extension $0 \to \mathcal{O}_C \to \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{O}_C \to 0$, while $e = -1$ corresponds to a similar construction.
