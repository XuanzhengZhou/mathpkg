First note that any two fibres of $\pi$ are isomorphic. The right-hand side is canonically isomorphic to $k$. Therefore the image of the global section $1$ of $\mathcal{O}_C$ via the structural map $\mathcal{O}_C \rightarrow \pi_*\mathcal{O}_X$ generates the stalk at every point, showing that $\pi_*\mathcal{O}_X \cong \mathcal{O}_C$.

For the general statement, consider the restriction of $\mathcal{L}(D)$ to a fibre $X_y \cong \mathbf{P}^1$. Since $D.f = n \geq 0$, the restricted sheaf $\mathcal{L}(D)|_{X_y}$ is an invertible sheaf of degree $n \geq 0$ on $\mathbf{P}^1$. By the theorem on cohomology and base change (III, 12.9 or III, Ex. 11.8), the sheaf $\pi_*\mathcal{L}(D)$ is locally free, and its rank at each point $y$ equals $h^0(X_y, \mathcal{L}(D)|_{X_y}) = n + 1$.
