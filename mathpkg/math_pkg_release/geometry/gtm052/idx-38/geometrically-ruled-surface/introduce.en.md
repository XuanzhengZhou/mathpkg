A geometrically ruled surface is an algebraic surface fibred over a nonsingular curve, with each fibre isomorphic to the projective line $\mathbf{P}^1$. The morphism $\pi$ is required to admit a global section $\sigma: C \to X$, which gives a preferred copy of the base curve inside the surface. Ruled surfaces form one of the fundamental classes in the Enriques--Kodaira classification of algebraic surfaces.
