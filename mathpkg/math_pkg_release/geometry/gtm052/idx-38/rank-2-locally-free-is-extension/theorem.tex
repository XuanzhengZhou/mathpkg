Any locally free sheaf $\mathcal{E}$ of rank $2$ on a curve $C$ is an extension of invertible sheaves. That is, there exists an exact sequence

$$0 \rightarrow \mathcal{N} \rightarrow \mathcal{E} \rightarrow \mathcal{L} \rightarrow 0$$

where $\mathcal{N}$ and $\mathcal{L}$ are invertible sheaves on $C$.

\textit{Remark.} The same result holds for locally free sheaves of arbitrary rank (Ex.~2.3).
