Any locally free sheaf $\mathcal{E}$ of rank $2$ on an algebraic curve $C$ can be expressed as an extension $0 \to \mathcal{N} \to \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{L} \to 0$ of two invertible sheaves. This structural result follows from the existence of a section of the associated ruled surface $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$, and it reduces the study of rank-$2$ bundles to extension classes in $H^1(C, \mathcal{N} \otimes \mathcal{L}^{-1})$.
