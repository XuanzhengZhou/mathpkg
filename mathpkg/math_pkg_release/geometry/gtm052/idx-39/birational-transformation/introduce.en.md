A birational transformation between projective varieties generalizes the notion of an isomorphism of function fields to a partially defined morphism. Two representations define the same birational transformation if they agree on the intersection of their domains, and the maximal domain of definition can be obtained by gluing. The complement of this maximal domain consists of the fundamental points, where the transformation is not defined. The graph of the birational transformation, together with the total transform of subsets, provides the technical machinery needed to study the factorization of birational morphisms of surfaces, leading to Castelnuovo's criterion and the theory of minimal models.
