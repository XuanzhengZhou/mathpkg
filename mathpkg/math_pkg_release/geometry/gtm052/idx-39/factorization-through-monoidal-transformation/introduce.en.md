This proposition is the key technical tool for factoring birational morphisms of surfaces. It states that any birational morphism between nonsingular projective surfaces can be resolved by factoring through a sequence of monoidal transformations (blow-ups) centered at the fundamental points. Combined with the fact that the number of fundamental points is finite and that each factorization reduces the number of fundamental points, this yields a complete factorization of any birational morphism into a composition of monoidal transformations, which is the essential ingredient for proving Castelnuovo's criterion for contracting exceptional curves of the first kind.
