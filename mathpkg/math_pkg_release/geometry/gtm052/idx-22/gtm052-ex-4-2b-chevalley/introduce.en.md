This exercise is an intermediate step in proving Chevalley's theorem via sheaf-theoretic methods. Given a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $Y$, one constructs a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{G}$ on $X$ and a morphism from its pushforward to a power of $\mathcal{F}$ that is an isomorphism at the generic point. The construction uses the Hom sheaf applied to a map $\alpha$ arising from the setup of Chevalley's theorem.
