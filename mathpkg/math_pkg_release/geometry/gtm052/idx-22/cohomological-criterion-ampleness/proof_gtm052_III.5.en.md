(i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii). If $L$ is ample on $X$, then for some $m > 0$, $L^m$ is very ample on $X$ over $\operatorname{Spec} A$, by (II, 7.6). Since $X$ is proper over $\operatorname{Spec} A$, it is necessarily projective (II, 5.16.1). Now applying (5.2) to each of the sheaves $F, F \otimes L, F \otimes L^2, \ldots, F \otimes L^{m-1}$ gives (ii). Cf. (II, 7.5) for a similar technique of proof.

(ii) $\Rightarrow$ (i). To show that $L$ is ample, we will show that for any coherent sheaf $F$ on $X$, there is an integer $n_0$ such that $F \otimes L^n$ is generated by global sections for all $n \geq n_0$. This is the definition of ampleness (II, §7).

Given $F$, let $P$ be a closed point of $X$, and let $I_P$ be the ideal sheaf of the closed subset $\{P\}$. Then there is an exact sequence

$$0 \rightarrow I_P F \rightarrow F \rightarrow F \otimes k(P) \rightarrow 0,$$

where $k(P)$ is the skyscraper sheaf $\mathcal{O}_X/I_P$. Tensoring with $L^n$, we get an exact sequence

$$0 \rightarrow I_P F \otimes L^n \rightarrow F \otimes L^n \rightarrow F \otimes k(P) \otimes L^n \rightarrow 0.$$

Now $F \otimes k(P) \otimes L^n \cong (F \otimes k(P))$ as $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules, since tensoring with an invertible sheaf does not change the skyscraper sheaf at $P$ (up to isomorphism). Applying condition (ii) to the coherent sheaf $I_P F$, we find $n_0$ such that $H^1(X, I_P F \otimes L^n) = 0$ for all $n \geq n_0$. Then the long exact cohomology sequence shows that the map

$$H^0(X, F \otimes L^n) \rightarrow H^0(X, F \otimes k(P) \otimes L^n)$$

is surjective for $n \geq n_0$. Using Nakayama's lemma, this implies that $F \otimes L^n$ is generated by global sections in a neighborhood $U_P$ of $P$.

Now cover $X$ by a finite number of the open sets $U_P$, for various closed points $P$, and let the new $n_0$ be the maximum of the $n_0$ corresponding to those points $P$. Then $F \otimes L^n$ is generated by global sections over all of $X$, for all $n \geq n_0$.
