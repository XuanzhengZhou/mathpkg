On a proper scheme over a noetherian ring, an invertible sheaf is ample precisely when twisting by sufficiently high powers kills all higher cohomology of any coherent sheaf. This cohomological criterion is a powerful tool: the forward direction follows from Serre's theorem on projective cohomology, while the converse uses the characterization of ampleness via generation by global sections. The proof constructs, for each closed point, a local section that generates the twisted sheaf near that point, then patches a finite cover.
