For a projective scheme over a noetherian ring, the cohomology groups of any coherent sheaf are finitely generated modules. Moreover, after twisting by a sufficiently high power of a very ample sheaf, all higher cohomology vanishes. This theorem is fundamental to the study of projective varieties, as it implies that the Euler characteristic and Hilbert polynomial are well-defined invariants. The proof reduces to the case of projective space via a closed immersion, where explicit Čech cohomology computations for the twisting sheaves $\mathcal{O}(q)$ establish the result.
