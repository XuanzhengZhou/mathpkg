Let $f: X \to Y$ be a flat morphism of schemes of finite type over a field $k$, and assume that $Y$ is irreducible. Then the following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] every irreducible component of $X$ has dimension equal to $\dim Y + n$;
\item[(ii)] for any point $y \in Y$ (closed or not), every irreducible component of the fibre $X_y$ has dimension $n$.
\end{enumerate}
