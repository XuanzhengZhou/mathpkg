Over a principal ideal domain, flatness is equivalent to torsion-freeness. This is verified by checking that for every ideal $\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A$ (which is principal, say generated by $t$), the map $\mathfrak{a} \otimes M \to M$ is injective, meaning that $t$ is not a zero divisor in $M$. This characterization is particularly useful for studying modules over discrete valuation rings.
