Let $f: X \to Y$ be a morphism of schemes and let $\mathcal{F}$ be an $\mathcal{O}_X$-module. We say $\mathcal{F}$ is \textbf{flat over $Y$ at a point $x \in X$} if the stalk $\mathcal{F}_x$ is a flat $\mathcal{O}_{Y, f(x)}$-module via the natural map $\mathcal{O}_{Y, f(x)} \to \mathcal{O}_{X, x}$. We say $\mathcal{F}$ is \textbf{flat over $Y$} if it is flat at every point of $X$.
