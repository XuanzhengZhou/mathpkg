Corollary 9.10 is the geometric consequence of Theorem 9.9 for flat projective families over connected noetherian base schemes. It states that three fundamental numerical invariants -- dimension, degree, and arithmetic genus -- are constant across all fibres. This result explains why these quantities are well-defined for families and justifies their use in moduli problems, particularly in the study of the Hilbert scheme.
