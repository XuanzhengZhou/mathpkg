Grothendieck's vanishing theorem is a fundamental result in sheaf cohomology. It states that on a noetherian topological space of dimension $n$, the cohomology groups $H^i(X, \mathcal{F})$ vanish for all $i > n$ and all abelian sheaves $\mathcal{F}$. This is analogous to the fact that singular cohomology of a real manifold of dimension $n$ vanishes in degrees above $n$. The theorem was first proved by Serre for coherent sheaves on algebraic varieties and later extended by Grothendieck to arbitrary abelian sheaves on noetherian spaces. It is essential for establishing finite-dimensionality results in algebraic geometry.
