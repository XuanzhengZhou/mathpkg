On a ringed space $(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$, there are two natural abelian categories in which to compute sheaf cohomology: the category $\mathfrak{Ab}(X)$ of sheaves of abelian groups, and the category $\mathfrak{Mod}(X)$ of sheaves of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules. Proposition 2.6 of Hartshorne asserts that the derived functors of the global sections functor agree in both settings. This means that the additional module structure does not affect the resulting cohomology groups, so one may freely work in whichever category is more convenient.
