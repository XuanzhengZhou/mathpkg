An affine scheme is the fundamental building block of scheme theory: it is a locally ringed space isomorphic to the spectrum of a ring. Every affine scheme is determined (up to isomorphism) by its ring of global sections, and the contravariant equivalence between rings and affine schemes established in Proposition 2.3 means that studying affine schemes is equivalent to studying commutative rings. The affine line $\mathbb{A}^1_k = \text{Spec } k[x]$, affine plane $\mathbb{A}^2_k = \text{Spec } k[x,y]$, and the singleton $\text{Spec } k$ for a field $k$ are basic examples.
