Let $f: X \to Y$ be a continuous map of topological spaces. Show that for any sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$ there is a natural map $f^{-1}f_*\mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{F}$, and for any sheaf $\mathcal{G}$ on $Y$ there is a natural map $\mathcal{G} \to f_*f^{-1}\mathcal{G}$. 

Use these maps to show that there is a natural bijection of sets, for any sheaves $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$ and $\mathcal{G}$ on $Y$,
$$\text{Hom}_X(f^{-1}\mathcal{G}, \mathcal{F}) = \text{Hom}_Y(\mathcal{G}, f_*\mathcal{F}).$$

Hence we say that $f^{-1}$ is a **left adjoint** of $f_*$, and that $f_*$ is a **right adjoint** of $f^{-1}$.
