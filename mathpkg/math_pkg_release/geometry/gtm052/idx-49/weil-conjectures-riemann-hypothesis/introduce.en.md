The third Weil conjecture, the analogue of the Riemann hypothesis, asserts that the reciprocal roots of the polynomials $P_i(t)$ are algebraic integers with absolute value $q^{i/2}$ in any complex embedding. This was the hardest part, finally proved by Deligne in 1973. It implies sharp bounds on the number of rational points on varieties over finite fields.
