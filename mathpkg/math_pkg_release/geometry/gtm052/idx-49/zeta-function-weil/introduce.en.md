The zeta function of a scheme over a finite field encodes the number of rational points over all finite extensions of the base field. Introduced by Weil, it generalizes the classical Riemann zeta function and Artin's zeta functions for curves. It is a formal power series with rational coefficients that packages the counting numbers $N_r$ (points rational over $\mathbf{F}_{q^r}$) into a single generating function.
