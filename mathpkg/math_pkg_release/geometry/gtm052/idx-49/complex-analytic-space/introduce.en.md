A complex analytic space, in the sense of Grauert, generalizes the notion of a complex manifold by allowing singularities and nilpotent elements in the structure sheaf. It is defined as a locally ringed space that is locally isomorphic to the zero set of finitely many holomorphic functions on a polydisc in $\mathbf{C}^n$, equipped with the quotient sheaf of germs of holomorphic functions. This provides the analytic counterpart to the algebraic notion of a scheme over $\mathbf{C}$.
