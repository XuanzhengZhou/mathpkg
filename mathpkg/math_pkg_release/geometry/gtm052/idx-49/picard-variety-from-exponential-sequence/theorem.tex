From the long exact cohomology sequence of the exponential sequence on $X_h$, where $X$ is a projective variety over $\mathbf{C}$, we obtain

$$0 \rightarrow H^1(X_h, \mathbf{Z}) \rightarrow H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X) \rightarrow \operatorname{Pic} X \rightarrow H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z}) \rightarrow H^2(X, \mathcal{O}_X) \rightarrow \dots.$$

Algebraically equivalent Cartier divisors give the same element in $H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$, so the Neron-Severi group $\operatorname{NS}(X)$ is a subgroup of $H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$ and hence is finitely generated. The group $\operatorname{Pic}^\circ X$ of divisors algebraically equivalent to zero modulo linear equivalence is isomorphic to $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)/H^1(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$, which is a complex torus. In fact, it is an abelian variety, the Picard variety of $X$.
