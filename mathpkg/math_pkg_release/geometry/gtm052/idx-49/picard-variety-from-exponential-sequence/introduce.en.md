The long exact cohomology sequence of the exponential sequence on a projective variety over $\mathbf{C}$ reveals the structure of the Picard group. The Neron-Severi group embeds into $H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$, proving it is finitely generated. The Picard variety $\operatorname{Pic}^\circ X$, parametrizing divisors algebraically equivalent to zero, is realized as the quotient $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)/H^1(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$, which carries the structure of an abelian variety.
