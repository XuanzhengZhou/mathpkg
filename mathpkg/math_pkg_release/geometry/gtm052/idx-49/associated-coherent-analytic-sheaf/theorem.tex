If $\mathcal{F}$ is a coherent sheaf on $X$ (a scheme of finite type over $\mathbf{C}$), one can define the associated coherent analytic sheaf $\mathcal{F}_h$ as follows. The sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ is locally (for the Zariski topology) a cokernel

$$\mathcal{O}_U^m \xrightarrow{\varphi} \mathcal{O}_U^n \rightarrow \mathcal{F} \rightarrow 0$$

of a morphism $\varphi$ of free sheaves. Since the usual topology is finer than the Zariski topology, $U_h$ is open in $X_h$. Furthermore, since $\varphi$ is defined by a matrix of local sections of $\mathcal{O}_U$, these give holomorphic functions on $U_h$, so we can define $\varphi_h: \mathcal{O}_{U_h}^m \rightarrow \mathcal{O}_{U_h}^n$. We define $\mathcal{F}_h$ to be the cokernel of $\varphi_h$.
