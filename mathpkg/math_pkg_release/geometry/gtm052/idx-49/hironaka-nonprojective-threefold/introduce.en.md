Hironaka's first example constructs a nonsingular complete algebraic threefold over $\mathbf{C}$ that is not projective. The construction glues two different sequences of blow-ups of intersecting curves, exploiting the fact that blowing up curves in different orders produces different local geometry while preserving the structure away from the intersection points. The proof of non-projectivity uses algebraic equivalence of cycles and the positivity of degree.
