$l$-adic cohomology is a Weil cohomology theory for algebraic varieties in characteristic $p$, defined using the etale topology. For a prime $l 
eq p$, one takes the inverse limit of etale cohomology with $\mathbf{Z}/l^r\mathbf{Z}$ coefficients, then tensors with $\mathbf{Q}_l$ to obtain finite-dimensional vector spaces over $\mathbf{Q}_l$. It serves as the algebraic substitute for singular cohomology and was developed by Grothendieck, M. Artin, and others to prove the Weil conjectures.
