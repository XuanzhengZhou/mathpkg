Hironaka's second example constructs a Moishezon manifold of dimension three that is not algebraic (i.e., not isomorphic to $X_h$ for any scheme $X$). Unlike the first example which is algebraic but not projective, this example shows Moishezon manifolds need not come from algebraic geometry. However, Moishezon showed any Moishezon manifold becomes projective algebraic after a finite number of monoidal transformations.
