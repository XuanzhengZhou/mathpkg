Let $C$ and $D$ be curves on a surface $X$, meeting at a point $P$. Let $\pi: \tilde{X} \to X$ be the monoidal transformation with center $P$. Show that $\tilde{C}.\tilde{D} = C.D - \mu_P(C) \cdot \mu_P(D)$. Conclude that $C.D = \sum \mu_P(C) \cdot \mu_P(D)$, where the sum is taken over all points $P \in C \cap D$, including infinitely near points.
