This theorem establishes that any reduced curve on a nonsingular surface can be resolved to a divisor with normal crossings by a finite sequence of monoidal transformations (blowups at singular points). The proof proceeds by first applying a known result to each irreducible component to make them nonsingular, then blowing up remaining singularities. The key observation is that any singularity worse than a node causes the arithmetic genus to strictly drop, ensuring termination after finitely many steps.
