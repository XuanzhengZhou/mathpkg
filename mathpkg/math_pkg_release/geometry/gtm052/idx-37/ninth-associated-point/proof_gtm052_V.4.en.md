By Corollary 4.4, the linear system $\mathfrak{d}$ of all cubics through $P_1, \ldots, P_8$ has dimension at least 1. Choose two distinct irreducible cubics $C, C' \in \mathfrak{d}$. By Bezout's theorem (I, 7.8), $C$ and $C'$ meet in $3 \cdot 3 = 9$ points counting multiplicities. Eight of these intersection points are $P_1, \ldots, P_8$ by construction. The ninth intersection point $P_9$ (possibly an infinitely near point, counted with appropriate multiplicity) is therefore determined. Now since $\dim \mathfrak{d} \geqslant 1$, any other curve $C'' \in \mathfrak{d}$ is a linear combination of $C$ and $C'$ in the pencil, hence must also pass through $P_9$. Thus $P_9$ is a common (unassigned) base point of $\mathfrak{d}$. The statement for infinitely near points follows by the same argument adapted to the language of assigned base points.
