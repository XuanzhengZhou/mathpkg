Corollary 4.4 computes the dimension of the linear system of plane cubic curves passing through $r$ points in general position. For $r \leqslant 7$, since there are no unassigned base points by Proposition 4.3, each assignment drops the dimension by one, giving $\dim \mathfrak{d} = 9 - r$. For $r = 8$, the dimension is at least 1, and the system has finitely many unassigned base points, leading to the classical theorem on the ninth associated point.
