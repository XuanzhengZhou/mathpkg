(a) For $r \leqslant 7$, by Proposition 4.3 there are no unassigned base points, so at each step the dimension drops by exactly one. The complete linear system of all plane cubics $|3H|$ has dimension $\binom{3+2}{2} - 1 = 9$. Thus $\dim \mathfrak{d} = 9 - r$.

(b) For $r = 8$, since there are only finitely many ways to pass three lines or one line and one irreducible conic through 8 points with no 4 collinear and no 7 on a conic, $\mathfrak{d}$ has finitely many unassigned base points and dimension at least 1.
