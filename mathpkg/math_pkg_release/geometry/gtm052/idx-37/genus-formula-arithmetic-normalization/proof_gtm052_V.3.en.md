By (3.8) we pass from $C$ to $\tilde{C}$ by blowing up the singular points in succession until none are left. At each blowup of a point of multiplicity $r$, by formula (3.7) the arithmetic genus drops by $\tfrac{1}{2}r(r-1)$. Summing these drops over all singular points (including infinitely near ones) gives the total difference between $p_a(C)$ and the genus of the normalization $g(\tilde{C})$. Since the normalization is nonsingular, its arithmetic genus equals its geometric genus $g(\tilde{C})$.
