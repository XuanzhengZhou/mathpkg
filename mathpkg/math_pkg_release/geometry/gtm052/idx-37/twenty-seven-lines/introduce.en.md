Theorem 4.9 is the celebrated result that every nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$ contains exactly 27 lines. When the cubic surface is realized as the blowup of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at six points, the 27 lines are explicitly described as the six exceptional curves $E_i$, the 15 strict transforms $F_{ij}$ of lines through pairs of blown-up points, and the six strict transforms $G_j$ of conics through five of the six points. Each line has self-intersection $-1$, making them the analogues of exceptional curves on the blown-up plane.
