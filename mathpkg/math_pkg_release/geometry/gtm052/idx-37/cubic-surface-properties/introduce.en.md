Theorem 4.8 summarizes the intersection-theoretic and geometric properties of divisors on a nonsingular cubic surface $X \subset \mathbf{P}^3$, realized as $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at six points. The canonical class is $K_X = -h$, making $X$ a Del Pezzo surface. Any divisor class $al - \sum b_i e_i$ can be interpreted as the strict transform of a plane curve of degree $a$ with $b_i$-fold points at $P_i$. The formulas (a)-(g) give the intersection numbers, degree, canonical pairing, and arithmetic genus of any divisor $D$.
