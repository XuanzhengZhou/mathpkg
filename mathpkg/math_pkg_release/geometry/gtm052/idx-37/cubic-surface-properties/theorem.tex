Let $X$ be a nonsingular cubic surface realized as the blowup of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at six points $P_1, \ldots, P_6$, with basis $l, e_1, \ldots, e_6$ for $\operatorname{Pic} X$. Let $h = 3l - \sum e_i$ be the hyperplane class, so that the embedding in $\mathbf{P}^3$ is given by $|h|$. For any divisor $D \sim al - \sum b_i e_i$, the following hold:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $D.l = a$, $D.e_i = b_i$;
\item[(b)] $D^2 = a^2 - \sum b_i^2$;
\item[(c)] $\deg D = D.h = 3a - \sum b_i$;
\item[(d)] $K_X = -h = -3l + \sum e_i$;
\item[(e)] $D.K_X = -D.h = -d$, where $d = \deg D$;
\item[(f)] $p_a(D) = \frac{1}{2}D.(D + K_X) + 1 = \frac{1}{2}(D^2 - d) + 1$;
\item[(g)] $p_a(D) = \frac{1}{2}(a - 1)(a - 2) - \sum \frac{1}{2}b_i(b_i - 1)$.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover, if $C$ is an irreducible curve on $X$ other than $E_1, \ldots, E_6$, then $\pi(C)$ is an irreducible plane curve $C_0$ of degree $a$ with a $b_i$-fold point at each $P_i$, and $C \sim al - \sum b_i e_i$ is the strict transform.
