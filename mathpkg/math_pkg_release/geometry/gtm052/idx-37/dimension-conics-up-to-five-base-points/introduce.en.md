This corollary computes the dimension of the linear system of plane conics passing through up to five points in general position. Since the complete linear system of all conics in $\mathbf{P}^2$ has dimension 5, and each assigned base point that is not already forced causes the dimension to drop by one, the formula $\dim \mathfrak{d} = 5 - r$ follows for $r \leqslant 5$. For exactly five points, there is a unique irreducible conic passing through them.
