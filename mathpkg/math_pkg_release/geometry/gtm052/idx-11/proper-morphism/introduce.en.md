A morphism of schemes $f: X \to Y$ is called proper if it is separated, of finite type, and universally closed (i.e., remains closed under any base change). Properness is the scheme-theoretic analogue of the notion of a compact morphism in topology, generalizing the concept of a complete variety. It is one of the most fundamental properties in algebraic geometry, playing a central role in cohomology and the theory of moduli spaces.
