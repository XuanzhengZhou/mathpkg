(a) Let $C$ be a local noetherian domain of dimension 1, with quotient field $K$, and let $L$ be a finite extension field of $K$. Show that there exists a discrete valuation ring $R$ of $L$ dominating $C$. Prove this by taking a polynomial ring over $C$, reducing to the case where $L$ is a finite extension of $K$, choosing suitable generators $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ of the maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$, forming $C' = C[x_2/x_1, \ldots, x_n/x_1]$, localizing at a minimal prime of $(x_1)$, and using Krull-Akizuki.

(b) Let $f: X \to Y$ be a morphism of finite type of noetherian schemes. Show that $f$ is separated (respectively, proper) if and only if the criterion of (4.3) (respectively, (4.7)) holds for all discrete valuation rings.
