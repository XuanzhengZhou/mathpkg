A morphism $f: X \to Y$ is quasi-projective if it can be expressed as an open immersion into some scheme $X'$ that is projective over $Y$. This captures the notion of a locally closed subscheme of projective space over $Y$, generalizing quasi-projective varieties from Chapter I. Most schemes encountered in practice (varieties, moduli spaces) are at least quasi-projective over their base.
