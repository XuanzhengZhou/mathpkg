A sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules on a scheme $X$ is quasi-coherent if it is locally of the form $\widetilde{M}$ for some module $M$ over the coordinate ring of an affine chart. Quasi-coherent sheaves are the natural sheaf-theoretic generalization of modules over a ring: on an affine scheme $\operatorname{Spec} A$, the category of quasi-coherent sheaves is equivalent to the category of $A$-modules via the tilde construction. They form the foundation for coherent sheaves and the development of scheme cohomology in Chapter III.
