Let $f: X \to Y$ be a separated morphism, let $Y' \to Y$ be any morphism, and let $X' = X \times_Y Y'$ be obtained by base extension. We must show that $f': X' \to Y'$ is separated. Suppose we are given morphisms of $T$ to $Y'$ and $U$ to $X'$ as in the valuative criterion, and two morphisms of $T$ to $X'$ making the diagram commutative. Composing with the map $X' \to X$, we obtain two morphisms of $T$ to $X$. Since $f$ is separated, these are the same. But $X'$ is the fiber product of $X$ and $Y'$ over $Y$, so by the universal property of the fiber product, the two maps of $T$ to $X'$ are the same. Hence $f'$ is separated.
