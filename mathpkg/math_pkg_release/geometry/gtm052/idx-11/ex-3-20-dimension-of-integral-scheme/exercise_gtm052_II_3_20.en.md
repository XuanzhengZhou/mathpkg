Let $X$ be an integral scheme of finite type over a field $k$, having function field $K(X)$.

(a) For any closed point $P \in X$, $\dim X = \dim \mathcal{O}_P$, where for rings, we always mean the Krull dimension.
(b) Then $\dim X = \operatorname{tr.deg.} K(X)/k$.
(c) If $Y$ is a closed subset of $X$, then $\operatorname{codim}(Y, X) = \inf\{ \dim \mathcal{O}_{P, X} \mid P \in Y \}$.
(d) If $Y$ is a closed subset of $X$, then $\dim Y + \operatorname{codim}(Y, X) = \dim X$.
(e) If $U$ is a nonempty open subset of $X$, then $\dim U = \dim X$.
(f) If $k \subseteq k'$ is a field extension, then every irreducible component of $X' = X \times_k k'$ has dimension $= \dim X$.
