Let $f: X \to Y$ be a morphism of separated schemes of finite type over a noetherian scheme $S$. Let $Z$ be a closed subscheme of $X$ which is proper over $S$. Show that $f(Z)$ is closed in $Y$, and that $f(Z)$ with its image subscheme structure (Ex. 3.11d) is proper over $S$. We refer to this result by saying that "the image of a proper scheme is proper." [Hint: Factor $f$ into the graph morphism $\Gamma_f: X \to X \times_S Y$ followed by the second projection $p_2$, and show that $\Gamma_f$ is a closed immersion.]
