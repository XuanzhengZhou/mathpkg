Let $f: X \rightarrow Y$ be a morphism of schemes. The diagonal morphism is the unique morphism $\Delta: X \rightarrow X \times_Y X$ whose composition with both projection maps $p_1, p_2: X \times_Y X \rightarrow X$ is the identity map of $X \rightarrow X$. We say that the morphism $f$ is separated if the diagonal morphism $\Delta$ is a closed immersion. In that case we also say $X$ is separated over $Y$. A scheme $X$ is separated if it is separated over $\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}$.
