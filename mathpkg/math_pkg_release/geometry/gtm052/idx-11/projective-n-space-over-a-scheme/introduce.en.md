Projective $n$-space over a scheme $Y$, denoted $\mathbf{P}_Y^n$, is defined as the fiber product $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbb{Z}}^n \times_{\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}} Y$. This definition is motivated by the fact that for any ring $A$, $\mathbf{P}_A^n \cong \mathbf{P}_{\mathbb{Z}}^n \times_{\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}} \operatorname{Spec} A$, so the general definition is the natural extension to arbitrary base schemes. It provides the ambient space for defining projective and quasi-projective morphisms.
