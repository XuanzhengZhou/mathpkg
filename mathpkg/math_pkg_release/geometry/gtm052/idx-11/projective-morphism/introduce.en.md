A morphism $f: X \to Y$ is projective if it can be expressed as a closed immersion into some projective space $\mathbf{P}_Y^n$ over $Y$, followed by the natural projection to $Y$. This generalizes the classical notion of a projective variety to the relative setting. Projective morphisms are automatically proper (Theorem 4.9), making them a crucial class of proper morphisms in algebraic geometry.
