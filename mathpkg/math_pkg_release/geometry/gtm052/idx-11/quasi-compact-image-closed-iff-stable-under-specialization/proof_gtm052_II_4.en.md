($\Rightarrow$) If $f(X)$ is closed, then it is stable under specialization since any closed subset of a scheme is stable under specialization.

($\Leftarrow$) Suppose $f(X)$ is stable under specialization. To show $f(X)$ is closed, we need to show it is constructible and stable under specialization (Ex. 3.18c). The stability under specialization is given. For constructibility, since $f$ is quasi-compact, $f(X)$ can be covered by finitely many affine open subsets, and the image of each is constructible by Chevalley's theorem. A finite union of constructible sets is constructible, so $f(X)$ is constructible. Hence $f(X)$ is closed.
