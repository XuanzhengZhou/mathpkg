Corollary 3.7 is a direct consequence of Theorem 3.5. Two affine varieties are isomorphic precisely when their affine coordinate rings are isomorphic as $k$-algebras. This reduces the geometric question of isomorphism to a purely algebraic one, and exemplifies the power of the algebra-geometry dictionary in algebraic geometry.
