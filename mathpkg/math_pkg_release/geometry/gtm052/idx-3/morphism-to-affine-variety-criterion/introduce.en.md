Lemma 3.6 provides a practical criterion for checking when a map from any variety to an affine variety is a morphism. It suffices to check that pulling back each coordinate function yields a regular function. This is used crucially in the proof of Theorem 3.5 to establish the correspondence between morphisms and algebra homomorphisms.
