Exercise 2.16(b) shows that even when the intersection of two varieties is a variety, the ideal of the intersection may not equal the sum of the ideals. A conic and a line in $\mathbf{P}^2$ intersect at a single point, but the sum of their homogeneous ideals is not the maximal ideal of that point.
