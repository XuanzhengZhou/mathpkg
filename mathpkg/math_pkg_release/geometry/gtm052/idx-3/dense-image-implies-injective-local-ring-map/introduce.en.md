Exercise 3.3(c) relates the geometric notion of dominant morphisms to the algebraic notion of injectivity on local rings. When the image of a morphism is Zariski dense, the induced pullback maps on local rings are injective. This reflects the fact that a regular function on $Y$ vanishing on a dense subset must be identically zero.
