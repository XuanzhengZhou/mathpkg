This is Theorem 1.3A from Chapter I, Section §1 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. Hilbert's Nullstellensatz (German for "theorem of zeros") is the foundational result of algebraic geometry, establishing that the operation of taking the ideal of the zero-set of an ideal returns precisely the radical of the original ideal. It is equivalent to the statement $I(Z(\mathfrak{a})) = \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$.
