Hartshorne refers the reader to standard references: Lang [2, p. 256], Atiyah--Macdonald [1, p. 85], or Zariski--Samuel [1, vol. 2, p. 164]. The proof uses the fact that $k$ is algebraically closed and $A$ is a Jacobson ring. A modern proof proceeds by the Rabinowitsch trick: embed the localization $A_f$ into a polynomial ring in one more variable, apply the weak Nullstellensatz to deduce $1 \in \mathfrak{a}A_f$, hence $f^r \in \mathfrak{a}$.
