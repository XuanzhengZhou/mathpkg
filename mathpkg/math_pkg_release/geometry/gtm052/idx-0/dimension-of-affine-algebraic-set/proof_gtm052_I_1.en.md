If $Y$ is an affine algebraic set in $\mathbf{A}^n$, then the closed irreducible subsets of $Y$ correspond to prime ideals of $A = k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$ containing $I(Y)$ via the bijection $Z \mapsto I(Z)$. These in turn correspond bijectively to prime ideals of $A(Y) = A/I(Y)$. Hence $\dim Y$, defined as the length of the longest chain of distinct irreducible closed subsets of $Y$, equals the length of the longest chain of distinct prime ideals in $A(Y)$, which is precisely the Krull dimension of $A(Y)$.
