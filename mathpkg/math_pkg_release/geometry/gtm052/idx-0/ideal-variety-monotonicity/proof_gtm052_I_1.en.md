(a) If $T_1 \subseteq T_2$, then any point simultaneously annihilating all polynomials in $T_2$ also annihilates all polynomials of $T_1$. Hence $Z(T_2) \subseteq Z(T_1)$.

(b) If $Y_1 \subseteq Y_2$, then a polynomial vanishing everywhere on $Y_2$ also vanishes everywhere on $Y_1$. Hence $I(Y_2) \subseteq I(Y_1)$.

(c) We have $f \in I(Y_1 \cup Y_2)$ if and only if $f(P) = 0$ for every $P \in Y_1 \cup Y_2$, i.e., $f(P) = 0$ for all $P \in Y_1$ and all $P \in Y_2$. This means $f \in I(Y_1) \cap I(Y_2)$.

(d) The inclusion $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \subseteq I(Z(\mathfrak{a}))$ follows from the definition. If $f \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$, then $f^r \in \mathfrak{a}$ for some $r > 0$. Then $f^r$ vanishes on $Z(\mathfrak{a})$, so $f$ vanishes on $Z(\mathfrak{a})$. The converse inclusion $I(Z(\mathfrak{a})) \subseteq \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}$ is exactly the statement of Hilbert's Nullstellensatz (Theorem 1.3A).
