Proposition 1.2A states that derived functors can be computed using any $F$-acyclic resolution, not just injective resolutions. This is a crucial practical result because many naturally occurring sheaves (such as flasque sheaves for the global sections functor) are acyclic but not injective. The proof uses dimension-shifting and the long exact cohomology sequence to relate the cohomology of $F(J^\bullet)$ to the derived functors.
