An injective resolution of an object $A$ is an exact sequence beginning with $A \hookrightarrow I^0$, where each $I^i$ is injective. This resolution provides a replacement of $A$ by a complex of injective objects, which is quasi-isomorphic to $A$ (viewed as a complex concentrated in degree zero). Applying a left exact functor $F$ to an injective resolution yields a complex whose cohomology defines the right derived functors $R^i F(A)$.
