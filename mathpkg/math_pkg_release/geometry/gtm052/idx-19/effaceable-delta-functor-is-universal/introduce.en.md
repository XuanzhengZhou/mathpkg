Theorem 1.3A provides the main practical criterion for verifying that a $\delta$-functor is universal: it suffices that each $T^i$ for $i > 0$ is effaceable. This is the standard way to prove that derived functors form a universal $\delta$-functor, since higher derived functors vanish on injective objects (which every object embeds into). The proof (due to Grothendieck) constructs the unique extension $f^i$ inductively using the effaceability condition.
