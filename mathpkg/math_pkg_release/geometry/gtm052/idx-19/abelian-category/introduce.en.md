An abelian category is the natural categorical setting for homological algebra, abstracting the essential properties of the category of abelian groups. It requires that hom-sets carry abelian group structures compatible with composition, that finite direct sums exist, and that every morphism has a kernel and cokernel satisfying the standard isomorphism theorems. The definition also demands that every morphism factor through its image, ensuring that diagram-chasing arguments generalize from module categories to any abelian category.
