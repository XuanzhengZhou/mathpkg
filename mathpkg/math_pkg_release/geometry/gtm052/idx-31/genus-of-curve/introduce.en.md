The genus $g$ is the most important single numerical invariant of an algebraic curve. It is defined as the common value of the arithmetic genus $p_a$ (defined via the Hilbert polynomial), the geometric genus $p_g$ (the dimension of the space of global canonical forms), and the cohomological invariant $\dim H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$. Proposition 1.1 establishes that these three quantities are equal. The genus is always a non-negative integer, and curves exist for every $g \geq 0$. The genus controls the Riemann--Roch formula, the structure of the canonical linear system, and the classification of curves.
