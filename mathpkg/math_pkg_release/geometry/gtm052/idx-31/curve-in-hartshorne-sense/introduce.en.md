In Hartshorne's treatment of algebraic geometry, particularly in Chapter IV on curves, a "curve" is defined as a complete, nonsingular curve over an algebraically closed field $k$. This is equivalent to an integral scheme of dimension 1, proper over $k$, all of whose local rings are regular. By a theorem from Chapter II, such a curve is necessarily projective. Throughout the chapter, "point" refers to a closed point unless the generic point is explicitly indicated. This definition unifies the various notions of curves studied earlier in the book and provides a clean setting for the Riemann--Roch theorem.
