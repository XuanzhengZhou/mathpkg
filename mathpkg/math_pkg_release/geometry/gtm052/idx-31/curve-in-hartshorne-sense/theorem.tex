A \textbf{curve} over an algebraically closed field $k$ is a complete, nonsingular curve. Equivalently, a curve is an integral scheme $X$ of dimension $1$, proper over $k$, all of whose local rings $\mathcal{O}_{X,P}$ are regular. Such a curve is necessarily projective over $k$.

Unless specified otherwise, a \textbf{point} on a curve means a closed point.
