The complete linear system $|D|$ of a divisor $D$ on a curve is the set of all effective divisors linearly equivalent to $D$. Via the correspondence between divisors and invertible sheaves, $|D|$ is naturally identified with the projectivization of the space of global sections $H^0(X, \mathcal{L}(D))$. This gives $|D|$ the structure of a projective space of dimension $l(D) - 1$, where $l(D) = \dim_k H^0(X, \mathcal{L}(D))$. The complete linear system is a fundamental tool for studying maps from curves to projective spaces and for understanding the geometry of divisors.
