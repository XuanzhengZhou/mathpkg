This corollary to Theorem 6.9 gives a complete classification of nonsingular curves up to isomorphism. Every abstract nonsingular curve can be realized as a quasi-projective variety, and conversely, every nonsingular quasi-projective curve is an open subvariety of a nonsingular projective curve. This bridges the gap between the abstract notion (function field of dimension 1) and the concrete geometric notion (quasi-projective variety).
