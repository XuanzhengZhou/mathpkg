This corollary establishes an equivalence of categories between three fundamental objects in the theory of algebraic curves: nonsingular projective curves with dominant morphisms, quasi-projective curves with dominant rational maps, and function fields of dimension 1 with k-homomorphisms. The functor $K \mapsto C_K$ from (iii) to (i) is inverse to the functor taking a curve to its function field. This categorical formulation encapsulates the birational classification of curves.
