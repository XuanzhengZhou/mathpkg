Theorem 5.5 from Chapter V, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry resolves arbitrary birational transformations between nonsingular projective surfaces. It asserts that any such transformation $T$ can be factored as $T = g \circ f^{-1}$, where $f$ and $g$ are birational morphisms from a common resolution $X''$. Combined with Theorem 5.4, this implies that every birational transformation between surfaces can be expressed as a sequence of monoidal transformations (blowing up points) and their inverses (blowing down). The proof proceeds by induction on an invariant $m = p_a(C) - p_a(C')$ measuring the failure of certain curves to be isomorphic.
