A sheaf is a presheaf that satisfies two additional axioms: the identity axiom (a section that vanishes locally vanishes globally) and the gluing axiom (compatible local sections can be uniquely glued to a global section). These axioms capture the essential property that sheaf data is determined by local behavior. Sheaves are the fundamental object for studying local-to-global phenomena in algebraic geometry, and they are indispensable for defining schemes.
