Let $H$ be defined by the homogeneous polynomial $f$ of degree $d$. We consider the exact sequence of graded $S$-modules

$$0 \rightarrow (S/I_Y)(-d) \xrightarrow{f} S/I_Y \rightarrow M \rightarrow 0,$$

where $M = S/(I_Y + I_H)$. The map is multiplication by $f$, which is injective because $H$ does not contain $Y$ (so $f$ is not a zero-divisor in $S/I_Y$). The cokernel is $M$ since $I_Y + I_H$ is generated by $I_Y$ and $f$.

Taking Hilbert polynomials, we obtain

$$P_M(z) = P_Y(z) - P_Y(z - d).$$

Now let $r = \dim Y$. The Hilbert polynomial of $Y$ can be written as

$$P_Y(z) = \frac{\deg Y}{r!} z^r + \text{lower terms}.$$

Expanding $P_Y(z) - P_Y(z-d)$, the leading term is

$$\frac{\deg Y}{r!} \cdot d \cdot z^{r-1} = \frac{(\deg Y)(\deg H)}{r!} \cdot \frac{1}{\deg Y \cdot d} \cdot d \cdot z^{r-1}$$

where we use that $\deg H = d$. More precisely, $P_Y(z-d) = \frac{\deg Y}{r!}(z-d)^r + \cdots = \frac{\deg Y}{r!}(z^r - r d z^{r-1} + \cdots)$, so

$$P_M(z) = \frac{\deg Y}{r!} \cdot r d \cdot z^{r-1} + \cdots = \frac{(\deg Y)d}{(r-1)!} z^{r-1} + \cdots.$$

Hence the leading coefficient of $P_M$ is $\frac{(\deg Y)d}{(r-1)!}$, and the degree of $M$ (as defined via the Hilbert polynomial) is $(r-1)!$ times this, giving $(\deg Y)(\deg H)$.

On the other hand, $M$ has support $Y \cap H$, and its Hilbert polynomial decomposes as a sum over the irreducible components $Z_j$ weighted by their multiplicities $\mu_{\mathfrak{p}_j}(M)$ times the Hilbert polynomial of $S/\mathfrak{p}_j$. Since $\deg Z_j$ is $(r-1)!$ times the leading coefficient of $P_{S/\mathfrak{p}_j}$, we obtain

$$\sum_{j=1}^{s} i(Y,H; Z_j) \cdot \deg Z_j = (\deg Y)(\deg H).$$
