Let $X$ be a topological space and $A$ an abelian group. Give $A$ the discrete topology. For any open set $U \subseteq X$, let $\underline{A}(U)$ be the group of all continuous maps of $U$ into $A$. With the usual restriction maps, $\underline{A}$ is a sheaf, called the \text{constant sheaf} on $X$ determined by $A$. For every connected open set $U$, $\underline{A}(U) \cong A$. If $U$ is an open set whose connected components are open, then $\underline{A}(U)$ is a direct product of copies of $A$, one for each connected component of $U$.
