The stalk $\mathcal{F}_P$ of a presheaf $\mathcal{F}$ at a point $P$ captures the local behavior of the presheaf near $P$. It is defined as the direct limit of the groups $\mathcal{F}(U)$ over all open neighborhoods $U$ of $P$, with respect to the restriction maps. Elements of the stalk are called germs of sections. The stalk is a fundamental tool for studying local properties — for example, a morphism of sheaves is an isomorphism if and only if it induces isomorphisms on all stalks.
