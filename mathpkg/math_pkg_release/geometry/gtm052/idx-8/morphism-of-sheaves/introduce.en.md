A morphism of (pre)sheaves is a natural transformation: a collection of group homomorphisms on each open set that commute with restriction maps. Categorically, it is a natural transformation between the corresponding functors. A morphism of sheaves induces homomorphisms on stalks at each point. An important consequence of the sheaf axioms is that a morphism of sheaves is an isomorphism if and only if it induces isomorphisms on all stalks, reflecting the local nature of sheaves.
