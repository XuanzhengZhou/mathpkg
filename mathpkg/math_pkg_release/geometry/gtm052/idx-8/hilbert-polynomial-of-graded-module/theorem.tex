Let $M$ be a finitely generated graded module over the polynomial ring $S = k[x_0, \ldots, x_n]$. There exists a polynomial $P_M(z) \in \mathbb{Q}[z]$ such that for all $l \gg 0$, the Hilbert function $\varphi_M(l) = \dim_k M_l$ equals $P_M(l)$. The polynomial $P_M$ is called the \text{Hilbert polynomial} of $M$. Its degree equals $\dim Z(\operatorname{Ann} M)$, and it is uniquely determined by $M$.
