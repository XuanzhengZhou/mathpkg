The sheaf of regular functions $\mathcal{O}_X$ is the canonical example of a sheaf in algebraic geometry. For an algebraic variety $X$, assigning to each open subset $U$ the ring of regular functions $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$ yields a sheaf of rings. The sheaf axioms hold because regularity is a local property: a function is regular if and only if it is regular in a neighborhood of each point. This sheaf is the structure sheaf that encodes the algebraic structure of the variety.
