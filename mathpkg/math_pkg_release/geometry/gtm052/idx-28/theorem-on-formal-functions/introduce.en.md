The Theorem on Formal Functions, due to Grothendieck, relates the formal completion of higher direct image sheaves to the cohomology of infinitesimal neighbourhoods of the fibre. For a projective morphism $f: X \to Y$ and a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$, it asserts that the $\mathfrak{m}_y$-adic completion of the stalk $(R^i f_* \mathcal{F})_y$ is naturally isomorphic to the inverse limit of the cohomology groups $H^i(X_n, \mathcal{F}_n)$ on the thickened fibres $X_n$. This theorem is a cornerstone of the cohomological study of proper morphisms and underlies Zariski's Connectedness Theorem, Zariski's Main Theorem, and the theorem on semicontinuity of cohomology.
