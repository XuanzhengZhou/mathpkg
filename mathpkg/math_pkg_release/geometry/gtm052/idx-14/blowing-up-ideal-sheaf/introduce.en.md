The generalized blowing-up construction extends the elementary notion of blowing up a point to blowing up an arbitrary closed subscheme of a noetherian scheme. By forming the Proj of the Rees algebra $\bigoplus \mathcal{I}^d$, one obtains a scheme $\tilde{X}$ where the ideal sheaf becomes invertible. This construction is the fundamental operation in birational geometry, used for resolving singularities, eliminating indeterminacy of rational maps, and proving that every variety can be completed.
