Exercise 7.6 introduces the Riemann-Roch problem: determining the dimension of the complete linear system $|nD|$ as a function of $n$. Part (a) treats the very ample case, where the dimension is governed by the Hilbert polynomial. Part (b) examines the opposite extreme, where $D$ is torsion in the Picard group. In general, the Riemann-Roch theorem implies that $\dim|nD|$ is a polynomial function of $n$ for large $n$ whenever $D$ is ample.
