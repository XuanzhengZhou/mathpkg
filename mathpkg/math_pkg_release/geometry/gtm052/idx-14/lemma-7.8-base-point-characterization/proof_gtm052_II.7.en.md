**Proof.** This follows immediately from the fact that for any $s \in \Gamma(X, \mathcal{L})$, the support of the divisor of zeros $(s)_0$ is the complement of the open set $X_s = \{Q \in X \mid s_Q \notin \mathfrak{m}_Q \mathcal{L}_Q\}$. A point $P$ belongs to $\operatorname{Supp}(s)_0$ exactly when $s_P \in \mathfrak{m}_P \mathcal{L}_P$. Since a point $P$ is a base point of $\mathfrak{d}$ if and only if $P \in \operatorname{Supp}(s)_0$ for every $s \in V$, the equivalence follows. $\square$
