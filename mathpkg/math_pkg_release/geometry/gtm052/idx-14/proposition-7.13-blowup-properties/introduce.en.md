Proposition 7.13 establishes the two most fundamental properties of blowing up. First, the ideal sheaf being blown up becomes an invertible sheaf (i.e., locally principal) on the blowup. Second, the blowup is an isomorphism away from the center $Y$, meaning the blowup only modifies the scheme along the closed subscheme being blown up. These properties ensure that blowing up is a controlled modification that does not affect the scheme outside the center.
