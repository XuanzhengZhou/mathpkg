**Proof.** (a) Since $\tilde{X}$ is defined as $\operatorname{Proj} \mathcal{S}$ where $\mathcal{S} = \bigoplus_{d \geqslant 0} \mathcal{I}^d$, it comes equipped with a natural invertible sheaf $\mathcal{O}(1)$. For any open affine $U \subseteq X$, this sheaf $\mathcal{O}(1)$ on $\operatorname{Proj} \mathcal{S}(U)$ is the sheaf associated to the graded $\mathcal{S}(U)$-module $\mathcal{S}(U)(1) = \bigoplus_{d \geqslant 0} \mathcal{I}^{d+1}(U)$. But this is clearly equal to the ideal $\mathcal{I} \cdot \mathcal{S}(U)$ generated by $\mathcal{I}$ in $\mathcal{S}(U)$. Therefore the inverse image ideal sheaf $\tilde{\mathcal{I}} = \pi^{-1}\mathcal{I} \cdot \mathcal{O}_{\tilde{X}}$ is in fact equal to $\mathcal{O}_{\tilde{X}}(1)$. Hence it is an invertible sheaf.

(b) If $U = X - Y$, then $\mathcal{I}|_U \cong \mathcal{O}_U$. Thus
$$\pi^{-1}(U) = \operatorname{Proj} \bigoplus_{d \geqslant 0} \mathcal{O}_U = \operatorname{Proj} \mathcal{O}_U[T] = U,$$
and the projection map is the identity. $\square$
