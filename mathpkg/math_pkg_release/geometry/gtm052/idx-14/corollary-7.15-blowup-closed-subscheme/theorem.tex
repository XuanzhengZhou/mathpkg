Let $f: Y \to X$ be a morphism of noetherian schemes, let $\mathcal{I}$ be a coherent sheaf of ideals on $X$, and let $Z$ be the closed subscheme of $Y$ defined by $f^{-1}\mathcal{I} \cdot \mathcal{O}_Y$. Let $\tilde{X}$ and $\tilde{Y}$ be the blowings-up of $X$ and $Y$ with respect to $\mathcal{I}$ and $f^{-1}\mathcal{I} \cdot \mathcal{O}_Y$, respectively. Then there is a unique morphism $\tilde{f}: \tilde{Y} \to \tilde{X}$ making the diagram commute, and $\tilde{Y}$ is isomorphic to the strict transform of $Y$ in $\tilde{X}$.
