The divisor of zeros assigns to each nonzero global section of an invertible sheaf an effective Cartier divisor that encodes where the section vanishes. This construction is well-defined because local trivializations of the invertible sheaf differ by multiplication by a unit, which does not change the associated Cartier divisor. The divisor of zeros provides the fundamental link between invertible sheaves and linear systems on a variety.
