A base point of a linear system is a point on the variety that lies in the support of every divisor belonging to the linear system. A linear system is called base-point-free when no such point exists. Base-point-free linear systems are precisely those that define morphisms to projective space, making this notion central to the study of maps from varieties to projective spaces.
