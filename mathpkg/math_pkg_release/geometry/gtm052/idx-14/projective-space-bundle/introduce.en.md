The projective space bundle generalizes the notion of a projective space over a field to a relative setting over an arbitrary noetherian scheme. Given a locally free sheaf $\mathcal{E}$, one forms its symmetric algebra and then takes Proj to obtain a scheme $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{E})$ that fibers over the base with fibers isomorphic to projective spaces. This construction is fundamental in algebraic geometry for studying families of projective varieties and for defining operations such as blowing up.
