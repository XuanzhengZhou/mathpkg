Let $S$ be a fixed scheme. A scheme over $S$ is a scheme $X$, together with a morphism $X \rightarrow S$. If $X$ and $Y$ are schemes over $S$, a morphism of $X$ to $Y$ as schemes over $S$ (also called an $S$-morphism) is a morphism $f: X \rightarrow Y$ which is compatible with the given morphisms to $S$. We denote by $\mathfrak{Sch}(S)$ the category of schemes over $S$. If $A$ is a ring, then by abuse of notation we write $\mathfrak{Sch}(A)$ for the category of schemes over $\operatorname{Spec} A$.
