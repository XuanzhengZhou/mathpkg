The absolute product $X \times Y$ of two schemes is defined as the fibred product over $\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}$, the terminal object in the category of schemes. Since every scheme admits a unique morphism to $\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{Z}$, this gives a well-defined product in the category of all schemes.
