Let $V$ be a variety over the algebraically closed field $k$.
(a) Show that a point $P \in t(V)$ is a closed point if and only if its residue field is $k$.
(b) If $f: X \to Y$ is a morphism of schemes over $k$, and if $P \in X$ is a point with residue field $k$, then $f(P) \in Y$ also has residue field $k$.
(c) Now show that if $V, W$ are any two varieties over $k$, then the natural map
$$\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{Var}(k)}(V, W) \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathfrak{Sch}(k)}(t(V), t(W))$$
is bijective. (Injectivity is easy. The hard part is to show it is surjective.)
