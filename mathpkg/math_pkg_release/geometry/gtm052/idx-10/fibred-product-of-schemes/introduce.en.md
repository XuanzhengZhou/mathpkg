The fibred product $X \times_S Y$ (also called the pullback or fiber product) is the scheme-theoretic analogue of the fiber product in set theory or topology. It satisfies a universal property: morphisms from any $S$-scheme $Z$ into $X$ and $Y$ over $S$ factor uniquely through $X \times_S Y$. This construction is fundamental for defining fibres of morphisms and base change, and underlies much of modern algebraic geometry.
