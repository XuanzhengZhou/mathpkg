A morphism of schemes $f: X \to Y$ is locally of finite type if locally on $Y$ and $X$, the corresponding ring homomorphisms are of finite type (i.e., make the target a finitely generated algebra over the source). This is the scheme-theoretic analogue of a morphism of affine varieties given by polynomial equations.
