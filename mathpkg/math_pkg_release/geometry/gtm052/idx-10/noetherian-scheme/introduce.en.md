A scheme $X$ is noetherian if it is both locally noetherian and quasi-compact. Equivalently, $X$ can be covered by finitely many open affine subsets $\operatorname{Spec} A_i$ with each $A_i$ noetherian. If $X$ is noetherian, its underlying topological space is noetherian (but the converse fails in general).
