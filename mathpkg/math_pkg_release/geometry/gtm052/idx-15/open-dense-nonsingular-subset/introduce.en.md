Every variety over an algebraically closed field contains an open dense subset that is nonsingular. The proof uses the fact that the function field $K$ is separably generated over $k$, so by Theorem 8.6A, $\dim_K \Omega_{K/k} = \dim X$. Since $\Omega_{K/k}$ is the stalk of $\Omega_{X/k}$ at the generic point, the sheaf is locally free in a neighborhood, and the nonsingularity criterion (Theorem 8.15) applies.
