We apply Proposition 8.7 (the local ring differential isomorphism) and Lemma 8.8 (the free module lemma). For a closed point $y \in Y$, let $B = \mathcal{O}_{y,X}$ with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$, and let $\mathfrak{p} \subset B$ be the prime ideal defining $Y$. Then $B/\mathfrak{p} = \mathcal{O}_{y,Y}$. The stalk of $\mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2$ at $y$ is $\mathfrak{p}/\mathfrak{p}^2$.

If $Y$ is nonsingular, then $\mathcal{O}_{y,Y}$ is a regular local ring. By Proposition 8.7, $\Omega_{Y/k,y} \otimes k(y)$ has dimension $\dim Y$. Using the exact sequence of Proposition 8.12, one deduces that $\mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2$ is free of rank $r = \operatorname{codim}(Y,X)$, the map $\delta$ is injective, and $\Omega_{Y/k}$ is locally free of rank $\dim Y$.

Conversely, assume (1) and (2). At a closed point $y$, the fiber of $\mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2$ injects into the fiber of $\Omega_{X/k} \otimes \mathcal{O}_Y$, which has dimension $n = \dim X$. If the image has rank $r$, then Lemma 8.8 implies $\mathcal{I}$ is locally generated by $r$ elements, and considering the exact sequence with a suitable subscheme $Y'$ defined by $r$ generators shows $Y = Y'$ is nonsingular.

The proof uses that $X$ is nonsingular implies $\Omega_{X/k}$ is locally free of rank $n = \dim X$ (Theorem 8.15).
