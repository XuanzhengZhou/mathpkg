This follows from the universal property of the module of differentials. For the morphism of $C$-modules $\Omega_{B/A} \otimes_B C \to \Omega_{C/A}$, one uses the fact that a derivation $B \to M$ induces a derivation $C \to M \otimes_B C$ by composition with $B \to C$. The map $\Omega_{C/A} \to \Omega_{C/B}$ is the natural map induced by the universal property of $\Omega_{C/A}$ applied to the derivation $d: C \to \Omega_{C/B}$ (viewing $\Omega_{C/B}$ as a $C$-module via $A \to C$). Exactness at the right follows because any $B$-derivation of $C$ lifts to an $A$-derivation. See Matsumura [2, Th. 57, p. 187].
