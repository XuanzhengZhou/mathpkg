Theorem 1.1 of Appendix A establishes the existence and uniqueness of an intersection theory on the class of nonsingular quasi-projective varieties over an algebraically closed field. An intersection theory is defined as a pairing on Chow groups $A^r(X) \times A^s(X) \to A^{r+s}(X)$ satisfying axioms A1--A7. The two main ingredients in the proof are Serre's algebraic definition of local intersection multiplicities via the Tor functor, and Chow's moving lemma.
