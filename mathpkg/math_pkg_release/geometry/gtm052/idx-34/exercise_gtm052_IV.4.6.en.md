(a) Let $X$ be a curve of genus $g$ embedded birationally in $\mathbf{P}^2$ as a curve of degree $d$ with $r$ nodes. Generalize the method of (Ex. 2.3) to show that $X$ has $6(g - 1) + 3d$ inflection points. A node does not count as an inflection point. Assume $\operatorname{char} k = 0$.
(b) Now let $X$ be a curve of genus $g$ and $D$ a divisor of degree $d \geq 2g+1$, embedded in $\mathbf{P}^n$ by $|D|$. Generalize the method of (Ex. 2.3) to compute the number of inflection points of $X$ in this embedding.
