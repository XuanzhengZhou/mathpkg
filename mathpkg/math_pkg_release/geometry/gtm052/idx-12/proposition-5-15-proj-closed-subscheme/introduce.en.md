Proposition 5.15 gives a structural description of closed subschemes of projective space and projective schemes. Part (a) shows that every closed subscheme of $\mathbf{P}^r_A$ arises as $\operatorname{Proj} S/I$ for a homogeneous ideal $I$ of the polynomial ring $S = A[x_0, \ldots, x_r]$; the ideal can be recovered as $\Gamma_*(\mathcal{I}_Y)$. Part (b) provides an intrinsic characterization: a scheme over $\operatorname{Spec} A$ is projective precisely when it is $\operatorname{Proj}$ of a graded ring finitely generated by its degree 1 elements over $A$. This bridges the classical definition of projective varieties with the scheme-theoretic approach.
