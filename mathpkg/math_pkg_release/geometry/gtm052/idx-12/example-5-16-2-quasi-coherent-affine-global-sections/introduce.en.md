Example 5.16.2 observes that on an affine scheme $X = \operatorname{Spec} A$, every quasi-coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F} \cong 	ilde{M}$ is automatically generated by global sections: one simply takes a set of generators of the $A$-module $M$ as the generating global sections. This is a direct consequence of the correspondence between $A$-modules and quasi-coherent sheaves on $\operatorname{Spec} A$, and it contrasts sharply with the projective case where one needs Serre's theorem to guarantee generation after sufficient twisting.
