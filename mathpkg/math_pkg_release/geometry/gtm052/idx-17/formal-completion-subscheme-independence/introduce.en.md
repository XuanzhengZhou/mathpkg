Remark 9.3.1 in Hartshorne establishes that the formal completion $\hat{X}$ along a closed subscheme $Y$ is well-defined independent of the specific sheaf of ideals chosen to represent the subscheme structure on $Y$. On a noetherian scheme, any two sheaves of ideals defining $Y$ are comparable up to powers: there exist integers $m, n$ such that $\mathcal{J}^m \subseteq \mathcal{I}$ and $\mathcal{I}^n \subseteq \mathcal{J}$. This cofinality of the inverse systems guarantees that the inverse limits coincide, so the formal completion depends only on the underlying closed subset.
