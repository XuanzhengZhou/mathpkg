An affine formal scheme is the formal scheme analog of an affine scheme. It is obtained by completing $\operatorname{Spec} A$ along a closed subscheme $V(I)$, yielding a locally ringed space whose underlying topological space is $V(I)$ and whose structure sheaf corresponds to the $I$-adic completion $\hat{A}$. Just as affine schemes serve as the local building blocks for ordinary schemes, affine formal schemes serve as the local models for all noetherian formal schemes. The study of coherent sheaves on formal schemes is reduced to the affine case, where it corresponds to finitely generated modules over the completed ring $\hat{A}$.
