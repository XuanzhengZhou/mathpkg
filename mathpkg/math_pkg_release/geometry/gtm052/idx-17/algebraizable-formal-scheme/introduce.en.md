An algebraizable formal scheme is one that arises as the formal completion of an honest (noetherian) scheme along a closed subscheme. In other words, it "comes from" ordinary algebraic geometry. While every formal completion $(\hat{X}, \mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}})$ of a noetherian scheme $X$ is trivially algebraizable, the class of all noetherian formal schemes is strictly larger. The existence of nonalgebraizable formal schemes, first demonstrated by Hironaka–Matsumura and later by Hartshorne, shows that the theory of formal schemes is a genuine generalization of scheme theory, not merely a convenient language.
