The inverse limit $\varprojlim A_n$ is the categorical limit of an inverse system. Intuitively, it consists of coherent sequences $\{a_n\}$ whose components are compatible under the transition maps. The universal property characterizes the inverse limit as the terminal object receiving compatible maps from any other object. This construction is fundamental for defining completions of rings and formal schemes in algebraic geometry.
