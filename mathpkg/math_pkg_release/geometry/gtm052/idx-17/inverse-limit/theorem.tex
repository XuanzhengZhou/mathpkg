Let $(A_n, \varphi_{n'n})$ be an inverse system of abelian groups. The \text{inverse limit} (or projective limit) is the set
$$\varprojlim A_n = \left\{ \{a_n\} \in \prod_{n} A_n \;\middle|\; \varphi_{n'n}(a_{n'}) = a_n \text{ for all } n' \geq n \right\},$$
with componentwise group structure. The inverse limit satisfies the universal property: given a group $B$ and homomorphisms $\psi_n: B \to A_n$ such that $\psi_n = \varphi_{n'n} \circ \psi_{n'}$ for all $n' \geq n$, there exists a unique homomorphism $\psi: B \to \varprojlim A_n$ such that $\psi_n = p_n \circ \psi$, where $p_n: \varprojlim A_n \to A_n$ is the restriction of the $n$th projection. The construction also makes sense for vector spaces, modules, rings, and topological spaces.
