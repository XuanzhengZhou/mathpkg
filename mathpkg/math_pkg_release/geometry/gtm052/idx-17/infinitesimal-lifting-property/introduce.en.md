The infinitesimal lifting property characterizes smooth (nonsingular) affine varieties over a field: given a surjection $B' \twoheadrightarrow B$ with square-zero kernel $I$ and a map $f: A \to B$, there always exists a lifting $g: A \to B'$. Moreover, any two liftings differ by a $k$-derivation of $A$ into $I$, which can be identified with an element of $\operatorname{Hom}_A(\Omega_{A/k}, I)$. This property is fundamental in deformation theory and is essentially the definition of formal smoothness in Grothendieck's EGA. The proof proceeds by lifting through a polynomial presentation and using the conormal exact sequence together with the nonsingularity hypothesis.
