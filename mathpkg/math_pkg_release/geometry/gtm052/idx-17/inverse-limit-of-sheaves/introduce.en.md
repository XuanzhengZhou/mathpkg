Inverse limits of sheaves of abelian groups always exist on any topological space. They are constructed by taking the inverse limit of sections over each open set, which yields a presheaf that the sheaf property verifies is already a sheaf. This result is crucial for defining the structure sheaf of a formal scheme as the inverse limit of sheaves $\mathcal{O}_X / \mathcal{I}^n$. However, one must be cautious: unlike for abelian groups, the inverse limit functor in the category of abelian sheaves is not exact, even when all transition maps are surjective.
