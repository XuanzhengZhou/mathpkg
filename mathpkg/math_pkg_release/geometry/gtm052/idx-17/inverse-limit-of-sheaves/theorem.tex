Let $X$ be a topological space and let $(\mathcal{F}_n)$ be an inverse system of sheaves of abelian groups on $X$. Then the inverse limit $\mathcal{F} = \varprojlim \mathcal{F}_n$ exists in the category $\mathfrak{C}$ of abelian sheaves on $X$. It is given by the sheaf associated to the presheaf
$$U \mapsto \varprojlim \Gamma(U, \mathcal{F}_n),$$
where the inverse limit on the right is taken in the category of abelian groups. The sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ satisfies the universal property of an inverse limit in $\mathfrak{C}$: given any sheaf $\mathcal{G}$ and a compatible system of morphisms $\psi_n: \mathcal{G} \to \mathcal{F}_n$, there exists a unique morphism $\mathcal{G} \to \mathcal{F}$ factoring the $\psi_n$.
