The Hodge numbers $h^{q,0}(X)$ are birational invariants of a projective nonsingular variety $X$, defined as the dimensions of the spaces of global regular $q$-forms $\Gamma(X, \Omega_X^{q/k})$. They form part of the Hodge diamond and provide a discrete invariant for the birational classification of varieties. In particular, $h^{\dim X, 0}$ recovers the geometric genus, and together with the plurigenera, the $h^{q,0}$ give powerful tools for distinguishing birational equivalence classes.
