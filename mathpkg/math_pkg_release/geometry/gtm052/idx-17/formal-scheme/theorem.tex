A \text{formal scheme} is a locally ringed space $(\mathfrak{X}, \mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{X}})$ which admits an open covering $\{\mathfrak{U}_i\}$ such that for each $i$, the pair $(\mathfrak{U}_i, \mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{X}}|_{\mathfrak{U}_i})$ is isomorphic (as a locally ringed space) to the formal completion of some noetherian scheme $X_i$ along a closed subscheme $Y_i$. Equivalently, a formal scheme is a locally ringed space that is locally of the form $(\hat{X}, \mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}})$ for a noetherian scheme $X$ and a closed subscheme $Y \subseteq X$.
