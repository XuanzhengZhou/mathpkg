Let $A$ be a noetherian ring, and let $C^\bullet$ be a complex of $A$-modules, bounded above, such that for each $i$, $h^i(C^\bullet)$ is a finitely generated $A$-module. Then there is a complex $L^\bullet$ of finitely generated free $A$-modules, also bounded above, and a morphism of complexes $g: L^\bullet \to C^\bullet$, such that the induced map $h^i(L^\bullet) \to h^i(C^\bullet)$ is an isomorphism for all $i$. Furthermore, if each $C^i$ is a flat $A$-module, then the map

$$h^i(L^\bullet \otimes M) \to h^i(C^\bullet \otimes M)$$

is an isomorphism for any $A$-module $M$.
