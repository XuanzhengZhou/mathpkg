Corollary 12.6 combines the results of Propositions 12.4 and 12.5 to characterize when $T^i$ is exact. Since $T^i$ is always exact in the middle (Proposition 12.1), exactness is equivalent to $T^i$ being both left and right exact. The criterion states that $T^i$ is exact if and only if it is right exact and $T^i(A)$ is a projective $A$-module (hence locally free). In this case, $T^i(M) \cong T^i(A) \otimes M$ for all $M$, so the functor $T^i$ is simply tensoring with the locally free sheaf $R^i f_*(\mathcal{F})$.
