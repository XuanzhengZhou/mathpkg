Theorem 12.11, the Cohomology and Base Change Theorem, is the fundamental result governing how cohomology behaves under base change for projective morphisms. It states that if the fibre cohomology dimension $h^i(y, \mathcal{F})$ is constant on $Y$, then the higher direct image sheaf $R^i f_*(\mathcal{F})$ is locally free, and the natural base change map $R^i f_*(\mathcal{F}) \otimes k(y) \to H^i(X_y, \mathcal{F}_y)$ is an isomorphism for all $y \in Y$. This means that cohomology commutes with arbitrary base change in this case. The proof uses the semicontinuity theorem together with the algebraic characterizations of exactness for the $T^i$ functors.
