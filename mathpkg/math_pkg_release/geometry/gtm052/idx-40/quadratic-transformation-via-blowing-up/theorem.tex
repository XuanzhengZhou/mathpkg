Let $\varphi: \mathbf{P}^2 \dashrightarrow \mathbf{P}^2$ be the quadratic transformation $\varphi(x_0, x_1, x_2) = (x_1 x_2, x_0 x_2, x_0 x_1)$, with fundamental points $P_1 = (1,0,0)$, $P_2 = (0,1,0)$, $P_3 = (0,0,1)$. Let $X'$ be $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at $P_1, P_2, P_3$, with exceptional curves $E_1, E_2, E_3$. Let $\tilde{L}_{ij}$ be the strict transform of the line $L_{ij}$ joining $P_i, P_j$.

Then $\varphi$ induces an isomorphism $\psi: X' \to X'$ identifying $X'$ with the second $\mathbf{P}^2$ blown up at $Q_1 = (1,0,0)$, $Q_2 = (0,1,0)$, $Q_3 = (0,0,1)$, such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\psi^{-1}(Q_i)$ is the strict transform $\tilde{L}_{jk}$ for $\{i,j,k\} = \{1,2,3\}$,
\item $\psi(E_i)$ is the strict transform of the line $M_{jk}$ joining $Q_j$ and $Q_k$.
\end{itemize}
Thus $\varphi$ is "blowing up $P_1, P_2, P_3$ and blowing down $\tilde{L}_{12}, \tilde{L}_{13}, \tilde{L}_{23}$."
