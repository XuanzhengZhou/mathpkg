Schläfli's double-six is a highly symmetric configuration of 12 lines in $\mathbf{P}^3$ arising from the 27 lines on a cubic surface. It consists of two sets of six mutually skew lines such that each line in one set meets all but one line in the other set. On a cubic surface, the six exceptional curves $E_1, \ldots, E_6$ and the six conic transforms $G_1, \ldots, G_6$ form a double-six. The configuration is "self-dual" and each double-six determines a unique cubic surface.
