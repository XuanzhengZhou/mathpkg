By (3.8) we pass from $C$ to $\tilde{C}$ by blowing up the singular points in succession, until there are none left. Each time a point of multiplicity $r$ is blown up, by (3.7) the arithmetic genus drops by $\frac{1}{2} r(r - 1)$. Summing over all singular points $P$ of $C$, including infinitely near singular points, yields the formula

$$g(\tilde{C}) = p_a(C) - \sum_P \frac{1}{2} r_P(r_P - 1).$$
