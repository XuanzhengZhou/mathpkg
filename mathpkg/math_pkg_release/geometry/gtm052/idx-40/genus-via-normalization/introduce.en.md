This theorem relates the geometric genus $g(\tilde{C})$ of the normalization of an irreducible curve $C$ on a surface to its arithmetic genus $p_a(C)$ and the multiplicities of its singular points. By repeatedly blowing up singular points until none remain, each blow-up reduces the arithmetic genus by $\frac{1}{2}r(r-1)$, where $r$ is the multiplicity of the blown-up point. The formula provides a practical way to compute the genus of the desingularization.
