This proposition is fundamental for the study of cubic surfaces: the linear system of plane cubics through up to 7 points in sufficiently general position has no unassigned base points. The proof considers the worst case $r = 7$ and constructs, for any point $Q$ not among the $P_i$, a cubic curve (a conic plus a line) containing all seven points but not $Q$, using combinatorial case analysis based on collinearity.
