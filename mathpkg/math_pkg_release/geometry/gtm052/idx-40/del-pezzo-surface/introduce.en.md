A Del Pezzo surface is an algebraic surface whose anticanonical embedding is projectively normal, meaning the canonical sheaf is $\mathcal{O}(-1)$ in the embedding. For $d = 3, 4, \ldots, 9$, these surfaces are realized as blow-ups of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at $9 - d$ points in general position. The cubic surface ($d = 3$) is the most famous example, obtained by blowing up 6 points. Del Pezzo surfaces form an important class of Fano varieties and rational surfaces.
