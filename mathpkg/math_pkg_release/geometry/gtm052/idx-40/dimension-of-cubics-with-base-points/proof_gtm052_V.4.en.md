For $r \leqslant 7$ there are no unassigned base points by (4.3), so at each step, the dimension drops by one. The cubics with no base points form a linear system of dimension 9. This proves (a). To prove (b), we observe that with no 4 points collinear and no 7 on a conic, there are only finitely many ways of passing three lines, or one line and one irreducible conic, through the 8 points.
