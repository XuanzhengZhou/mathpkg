Let $X$ be a nonsingular cubic surface in $\mathbf{P}^3$, realized as the blow-up of $\mathbf{P}^2$ at six points $P_1, \ldots, P_6$ in general position. Then $X$ contains exactly 27 lines. Each line has self-intersection $-1$, and they are the only irreducible curves with negative self-intersection on $X$.

The 27 lines consist of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The six exceptional curves $E_i$, $i = 1, \ldots, 6$.
\item[(b)] The strict transforms $F_{ij}$ of the lines in $\mathbf{P}^2$ containing $P_i$ and $P_j$, for $1 \leqslant i < j \leqslant 6$ (fifteen of these).
\item[(c)] The strict transforms $G_j$ of the conics in $\mathbf{P}^2$ containing the five points $P_i$ for $i \neq j$, for $j = 1, \ldots, 6$ (six of these).
\end{enumerate}
In terms of divisor classes:
$$E_i \sim e_i, \quad F_{ij} \sim l - e_i - e_j, \quad G_j \sim 2l - \sum_{i \neq j} e_i.$$
