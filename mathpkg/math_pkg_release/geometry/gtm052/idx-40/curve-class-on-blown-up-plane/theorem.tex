Let $\pi: X \to \mathbf{P}^2$ be the blowing-up of six points $P_1, \ldots, P_6$ in general position, giving the cubic surface $X$. If $C$ is any irreducible curve in $X$, other than $E_1, \ldots, E_6$, then $\pi(C)$ is an irreducible plane curve $C_0$, and $C$ is the strict transform of $C_0$.

Let $C_0$ have degree $a$, and suppose $C_0$ has multiplicity $b_i$ at each $P_i$. Then

$$\pi^* C_0 = C + \sum b_i E_i$$

by (3.6). Since $C_0 \sim a \cdot \text{line}$, it follows that

$$C \sim al - \sum_{i=1}^6 b_i e_i.$$

Thus any irreducible curve $C$ on $X$ in the class $al - \sum b_i e_i$ (with $a, b_1, \ldots, b_6 \geqslant 0$) can be interpreted as the strict transform of a plane curve of degree $a$ with a $b_i$-fold point at each $P_i$.
