This theorem summarizes the basic numerical properties of divisors and curves on a nonsingular cubic surface, expressed in terms of the standard basis $l, e_1, \ldots, e_6$ of the Picard group. The intersection form shows that exceptional curves have self-intersection $-1$, and the canonical divisor is the negative of the hyperplane class. These properties follow from earlier general results and the explicit realization as a blow-up of six points.
