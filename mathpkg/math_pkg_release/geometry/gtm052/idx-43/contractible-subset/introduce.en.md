This definition from Appendix A, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry introduces the notion of a contractible closed subset of a variety. A closed subset $Y$ is contractible if it can be blown down to a point via a birational morphism that is an isomorphism away from $Y$. The general contraction problem asks for necessary and sufficient conditions on $Y$ for it to be contractible, a problem that remains open in full generality.
