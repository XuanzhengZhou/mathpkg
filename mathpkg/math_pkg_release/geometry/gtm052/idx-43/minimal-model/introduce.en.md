This definition from Appendix A, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry introduces the concept of a minimal model for surfaces. A surface is called a minimal model if it is the unique relatively minimal model in its birational equivalence class. A classical theorem of Zariski, valid in all characteristics, states that except for rational and ruled surfaces, every surface is birational to a unique minimal model.
