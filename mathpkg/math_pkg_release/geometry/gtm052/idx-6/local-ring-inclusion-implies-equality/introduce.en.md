Lemma 6.4 states that the inclusion of local rings of points on a quasi-projective variety forces the points to coincide. This is a key technical result for the theory of abstract nonsingular curves: it guarantees that distinct points on a nonsingular curve give rise to distinct discrete valuation rings in the function field, so the set of DVRs of $K/k$ faithfully represents the points of any curve with function field $K$.
