Theorem 6.1A gives a purely ring-theoretic characterization of valuation rings: a local ring $R$ in a field $K$ is a valuation ring of $K$ precisely when it cannot be properly dominated by any other local ring in $K$. Moreover, every local ring in $K$ can be embedded into a valuation ring that dominates it, showing that valuation rings are abundant.
