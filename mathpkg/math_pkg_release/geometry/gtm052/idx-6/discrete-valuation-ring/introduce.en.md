A discrete valuation ring (DVR) is the valuation ring of a discrete valuation, whose value group is the integers. DVRs are precisely the regular local rings of dimension one, and they play a central role in algebraic geometry as the local rings of nonsingular points on algebraic curves. Every DVR is a principal ideal domain and is noetherian, unlike general valuation rings.
