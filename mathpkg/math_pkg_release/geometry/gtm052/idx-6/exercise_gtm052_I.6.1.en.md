Recall that a curve is rational if it is birationally equivalent to $\mathbf{P}^1$ (Ex. 4.4). Let $Y$ be a nonsingular rational curve which is not isomorphic to $\mathbf{P}^1$.

(a) Show that $Y$ is isomorphic to an open subset of $\mathbf{A}^1$.

(b) Show that $Y$ is affine.

(c) Show that $A(Y)$ is a unique factorization domain.
