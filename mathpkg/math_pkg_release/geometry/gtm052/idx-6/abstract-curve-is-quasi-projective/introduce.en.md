Corollary 6.10 follows immediately from Theorem 6.9: since $C_K$ is projective, it is in particular quasi-projective. Conversely, if $Y$ is a nonsingular quasi-projective curve with function field $K$, then by Theorem 6.7 it embeds as an open subset of $C_K$, which is projective. Thus every nonsingular curve can be compactified to a nonsingular projective curve by adding finitely many points.
