Let $P_1, \ldots, P_r, Q_1, \ldots, Q_s$ be distinct points of $\mathbf{A}^1$. If $\mathbf{A}^1 - \{P_1, \ldots, P_r\}$ is isomorphic to $\mathbf{A}^1 - \{Q_1, \ldots, Q_s\}$, show that $r = s$. Is the converse true? Cf. (Ex. 3.1).
