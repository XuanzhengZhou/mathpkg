The ideal $I(Y)$ of a subset $Y \subseteq \mathbf{A}^n$ consists of all polynomials that vanish at every point of $Y$. Together with the zero-set operation $Z$, this forms a Galois connection between subsets of $\mathbf{A}^n$ and ideals of the polynomial ring $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$. This correspondence is the bridge between algebraic geometry and commutative algebra, leading to Hilbert's Nullstellensatz which fully characterizes the relationship.
