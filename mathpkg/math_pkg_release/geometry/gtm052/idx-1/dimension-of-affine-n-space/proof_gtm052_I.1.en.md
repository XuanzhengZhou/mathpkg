According to Proposition 1.7, the dimension of $\mathbf{A}^n$ equals the Krull dimension of its affine coordinate ring $A(\mathbf{A}^n) = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$. By Theorem 1.8A(a), the Krull dimension of the polynomial ring $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ equals the transcendence degree of its quotient field $k(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ over $k$, which is $n$. Hence $\dim \mathbf{A}^n = n$.
