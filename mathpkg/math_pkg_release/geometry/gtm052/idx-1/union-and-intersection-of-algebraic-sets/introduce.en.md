Proposition 1.1 shows that algebraic sets in $\mathbf{A}^n$ satisfy the axioms for the closed sets of a topology: they are closed under finite unions and arbitrary intersections, and both the empty set and the whole space are algebraic. This defines the Zariski topology on affine space, which is the fundamental topology used throughout algebraic geometry.
