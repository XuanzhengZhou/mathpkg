If $Y$ is an affine algebraic set in $\mathbf{A}^n$, then the closed irreducible subsets of $Y$ correspond to prime ideals of $A = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ which contain $I(Y)$. By Corollary 1.4, these in turn correspond to the prime ideals of the affine coordinate ring $A(Y) = A / I(Y)$. Therefore the length of a chain of irreducible closed subsets in $Y$ equals the length of the corresponding chain of prime ideals in $A(Y)$. Hence $\dim Y$, which is the supremum of lengths of chains of irreducible closed subsets, is exactly the Krull dimension of $A(Y)$.
