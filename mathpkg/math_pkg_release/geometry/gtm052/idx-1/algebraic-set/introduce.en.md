An algebraic set is a subset of affine space defined as the common zero locus of a collection of polynomials. These sets form the closed sets of the Zariski topology on $\mathbf{A}^n$. The study of algebraic sets and their irreducible components leads directly to the definition of affine varieties, the central objects of classical algebraic geometry.
