The Hirzebruch-Riemann-Roch (HRR) theorem is a vast generalization of the classical Riemann-Roch theorem for curves and surfaces to nonsingular projective varieties of arbitrary dimension. It expresses the Euler characteristic $\chi(\mathcal{E}) = \sum_{i=0}^n (-1)^i \dim H^i(X, \mathcal{E})$ of a locally free sheaf $\mathcal{E}$ as the degree of the degree-$n$ component of the product of the Chern character $\operatorname{ch}(\mathcal{E})$ and the Todd class $\operatorname{td}(\mathcal{T}_X)$ of the tangent sheaf. The theorem reduces the computation of sheaf cohomology to intersection-theoretic calculations involving Chern classes and rational numbers. As a special case, it recovers the classical Riemann-Roch formula for surfaces: $\chi(\mathcal{L}(D)) = \frac{1}{2} D(D-K) + \frac{1}{12}(K^2 + c_2)$, and for $D = 0$ yields the relation $1 + p_a = \frac{1}{12}(K^2 + c_2)$ connecting the arithmetic genus to the Chern numbers of the surface.
