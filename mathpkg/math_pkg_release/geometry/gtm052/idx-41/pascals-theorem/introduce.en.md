Exercise 4.5 from Chapter V, Section 4 of Hartshorne asks the reader to prove Pascal's theorem, a classical result in projective geometry. Given six points on a conic, the three intersection points of opposite sides of the inscribed hexagon are collinear. This result is fundamental to the study of configurations of points on conics and plays a key role in establishing properties of cubic curves.
