Exercise 4.3 from Chapter V, Section 4 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry asks the reader to prove that any irreducible plane curve can be transformed, via a finite sequence of quadratic transformations, into a curve whose only singularities are ordinary multiple points (i.e., points where all tangent directions are distinct). The proof relies on the properties of quadratic transformations developed in (3.8), specifically that each transformation reduces certain singularity invariants.
