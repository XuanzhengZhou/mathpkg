This exercise proves that for a noetherian scheme $X$ with enough locally frees, the natural map $\varepsilon: K_1(X) \to K(X)$ from the Grothendieck group of locally free sheaves to that of coherent sheaves is an isomorphism. The proof uses finite locally free resolutions of coherent sheaves and shows that the Euler characteristic of such a resolution gives the inverse map $\delta: K(X) \to K_1(X)$.
