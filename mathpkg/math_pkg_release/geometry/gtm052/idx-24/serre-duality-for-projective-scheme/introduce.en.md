The Serre duality theorem for projective schemes generalizes the duality for projective space. For a projective scheme $X$ over an algebraically closed field with dualizing sheaf $\omega_X^\circ$, there are natural maps $\theta^i: \operatorname{Ext}^i(\mathcal{F}, \omega_X^\circ) \to H^{n-i}(X, \mathcal{F})^\vee$. These $\theta^i$ are isomorphisms for all $i$ and all coherent $\mathcal{F}$ precisely when $X$ is Cohen--Macaulay and equidimensional. The theorem thus links the geometry of $X$ with duality isomorphisms.
