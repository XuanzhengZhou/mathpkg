Let $X$ be a projective scheme of dimension $n$ over an algebraically closed field $k$. Let $\omega_X^\circ$ be a dualizing sheaf on $X$, and let $\mathcal{O}(1)$ be a very ample sheaf on $X$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] For all $i \geq 0$ and $\mathcal{F}$ coherent on $X$, there are natural functorial maps
$$\theta^i: \operatorname{Ext}^i(\mathcal{F}, \omega_X^\circ) \to H^{n-i}(X, \mathcal{F})^\vee$$
such that $\theta^0$ is the map given in the definition of dualizing sheaf.

\item[(b)] The following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $X$ is Cohen--Macaulay and equidimensional (i.e., all irreducible components have the same dimension);
\item[(ii)] for any $\mathcal{F}$ locally free on $X$, we have $H^i(X, \mathcal{F}(-q)) = 0$ for $i < n$ and $q \gg 0$;
\item[(iii)] the maps $\theta^i$ of (a) are isomorphisms for all $i \geq 0$ and all $\mathcal{F}$ coherent on $X$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
