Let $X$ be a proper scheme of dimension $n$ over a field $k$. A \textbf{dualizing sheaf} for $X$ is a coherent sheaf $\omega_X^\circ$ on $X$, together with a \textbf{trace morphism} $t: H^n(X, \omega_X^\circ) \to k$, such that for all coherent sheaves $\mathcal{F}$ on $X$, the natural pairing

$$\operatorname{Hom}(\mathcal{F}, \omega_X^\circ) \times H^n(X, \mathcal{F}) \to H^n(X, \omega_X^\circ)$$

followed by $t$ gives an isomorphism

$$\operatorname{Hom}(\mathcal{F}, \omega_X^\circ) \xrightarrow{\sim} H^n(X, \mathcal{F})^\vee.$$
