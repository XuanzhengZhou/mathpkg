A noetherian ring $A$ is called \textbf{normal} if its localization $A_\mathfrak{p}$ at every prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ is an integrally closed domain. This extends the geometric notion of normality from schemes to rings: a scheme is normal precisely when all its local rings are normal. A normal ring is necessarily a finite direct product of integrally closed domains, so it has no nilpotent elements. Normality is a key condition in Serre's criterion (Theorem 8.22A), which characterizes it via regularity in codimension one and depth conditions.
