\begin{proof}
(a) Since $X$ is nonsingular, it is Cohen--Macaulay by (8.21Aa). Since $\mathcal{I}_Y$ is locally generated by $r = \operatorname{codim}(Y, X)$ elements, those elements locally form a regular sequence in $\mathcal{O}_X$, by (8.21Ac), and so $Y$ is Cohen--Macaulay by (8.21Ad).

(b) We already know that normal implies regular in codimension $1$ (I, 6.2A). For the converse, we use (8.22A) applied to the local rings of $Y$. Condition (1) is our hypothesis, and condition (2) holds automatically because $Y$ is Cohen--Macaulay.
\end{proof}
