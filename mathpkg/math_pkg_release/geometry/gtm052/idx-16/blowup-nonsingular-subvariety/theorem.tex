Let $X$ be a nonsingular variety over $k$, and let $Y \subseteq X$ be a nonsingular closed subvariety, with ideal sheaf $\mathcal{I}$. Let $\pi: \tilde{X} \to X$ be the blowing-up of $\mathcal{I}$, and let $Y' \subseteq \tilde{X}$ be the subscheme defined by the inverse image ideal sheaf $\mathcal{I}' = \pi^{-1} \mathcal{I} \cdot \mathcal{O}_{\tilde{X}}$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\tilde{X}$ is also nonsingular.
\item[(b)] $Y'$, together with the induced projection map $\pi: Y' \to Y$, is isomorphic to $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2)$, the projective space bundle of the conormal sheaf of $Y$ in $X$.
\end{enumerate}
