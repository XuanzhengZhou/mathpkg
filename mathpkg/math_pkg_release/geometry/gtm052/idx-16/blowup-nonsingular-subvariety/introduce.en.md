\textbf{Theorem 8.24} is a fundamental result about blowing up a nonsingular variety $X$ along a nonsingular closed subvariety $Y$. Part (a) guarantees that the blow-up $\tilde{X}$ remains nonsingular -- the blowing-up operation preserves smoothness when both the ambient space and the center are smooth. Part (b) identifies the exceptional divisor $Y'$: it is isomorphic to the projective space bundle $\mathbf{P}(\mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2)$ associated to the conormal sheaf of $Y$ in $X$. This theorem is essential for comparing invariants of $X$ and $\tilde{X}$, as used in applications such as Exercise 8.5.
