Let $X$ be a curve of genus $g \geqslant 2$ over an algebraically closed field $k$, and let $\varphi_K: X \to \mathbf{P}^{g-1}$ be its canonical morphism. Then $\varphi_K$ is a closed embedding if and only if $X$ is not hyperelliptic. In this case, the image is a curve of degree $2g-2$ in $\mathbf{P}^{g-1}$, called the canonical curve.
