Curves of Genus 5. Assume $X$ is not hyperelliptic.
(a) The curves of genus 5 whose canonical model in $\mathbf{P}^4$ is a complete intersection $F_2.F_2.F_2$ of three quadric hypersurfaces form a family of dimension 12.
(b) $X$ has a $g_3^1$ if and only if it can be represented as a plane sextic curve with four nodes. The corresponding family has dimension 12.
(c) Show that the general curve of genus 5 is a complete intersection of three quadrics in $\mathbf{P}^4$, hence is not representable as a plane sextic with four nodes.
