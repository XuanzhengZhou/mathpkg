Let $X$ be a hyperelliptic curve of genus $g \geqslant 2$, with unique $g_2^1$. Then the canonical morphism $\varphi_K: X \to \mathbf{P}^{g-1}$ factors as $X \xrightarrow{f} \mathbf{P}^1 \xrightarrow{\nu_{g-1}} \mathbf{P}^{g-1}$, where $f$ is the 2-to-1 morphism determined by the $g_2^1$, and $\nu_{g-1}$ is the $(g-1)$-uple embedding of $\mathbf{P}^1$. In particular, the image $X' = f(X)$ is a rational normal curve of degree $g-1$, and $f$ is a morphism of degree 2 onto $X'$. Every effective canonical divisor on $X$ is a sum of $g-1$ divisors in the unique $g_2^1$, i.e., $|K| = \sum_{1}^{g-1} g_2^1$.
