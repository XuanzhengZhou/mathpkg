The canonical embedding is a fundamental construction in the theory of algebraic curves, introduced in Chapter IV, Section 5 of Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry. For a nonhyperelliptic curve of genus at least 2, the canonical linear system defines a closed immersion into projective space of dimension g-1. This embedding allows one to study the intrinsic geometry of the curve via its extrinsic projective geometry.
