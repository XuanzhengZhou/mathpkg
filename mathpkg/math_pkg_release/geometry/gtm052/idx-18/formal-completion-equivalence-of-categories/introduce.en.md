Theorem 9.7 establishes a fundamental equivalence of categories in the theory of formal schemes, analogous to the classical GAGA principle. Given a noetherian ring $A$ that is complete with respect to an ideal $I$, the functor $M \mapsto M^\triangle$ sends finitely generated $A$-modules to coherent sheaves on the formal completion $\hat{X}$ of $X = \text{Spec } A$ along $Y = V(I)$. The inverse functor takes global sections $\mathfrak{F} \mapsto \Gamma(\hat{X}, \mathfrak{F})$, and both functors are exact. This equivalence implies that the category of coherent sheaves on the formal completion is completely determined by the category of finitely generated modules over the complete ring $A$.
