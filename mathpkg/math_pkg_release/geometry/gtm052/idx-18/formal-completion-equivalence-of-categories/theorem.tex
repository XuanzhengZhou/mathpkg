Let $A$ be a noetherian ring, $I$ an ideal, and assume that $A$ is $I$-adically complete. Let $X = \text{Spec } A$, $Y = V(I)$, and $\mathfrak{X} = \hat{X}$. Then the functors

$$M \mapsto M^\triangle \qquad\text{and}\qquad \mathfrak{F} \mapsto \Gamma(\mathfrak{X}, \mathfrak{F})$$

are exact, and inverse to each other, on the categories of finitely generated $A$-modules and coherent $\mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{X}}$-modules respectively. Thus they establish an equivalence of categories. In particular, every coherent $\mathcal{O}_{\mathfrak{X}}$-module $\mathfrak{F}$ is of the form $M^\triangle$ for some $M$.
