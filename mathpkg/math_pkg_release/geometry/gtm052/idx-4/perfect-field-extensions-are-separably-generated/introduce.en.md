This theorem guarantees that over a perfect field $k$, every finitely generated field extension $K/k$ is separably generated. Since algebraically closed fields are perfect, this applies to the base field in classical algebraic geometry. It follows that the function field $K(X)$ of any variety $X$ over an algebraically closed field is separably generated, which is the crucial hypothesis needed to apply the Primitive Element Theorem and deduce that every variety is birationally equivalent to a hypersurface.
