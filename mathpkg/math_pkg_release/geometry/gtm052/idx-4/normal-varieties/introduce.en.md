A variety $Y$ is normal at a point $P$ if its local ring $\mathcal{O}_P$ is integrally closed; $Y$ is normal if this holds at every point. Normality is a key regularity condition in algebraic geometry, weaker than smoothness but strong enough to guarantee many desirable properties. For affine varieties, normality is equivalent to the affine coordinate ring being integrally closed. Every affine variety admits a normalization $\tilde{Y}$ — a normal affine variety with a finite surjective morphism $\pi: \tilde{Y} \to Y$ satisfying a universal property with respect to dominant morphisms from normal varieties.
