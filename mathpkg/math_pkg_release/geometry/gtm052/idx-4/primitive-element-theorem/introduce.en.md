The Theorem of the Primitive Element states that every finite separable field extension $L/K$ is simple, meaning it can be generated by a single element $\alpha \in L$. Moreover, when $K$ is infinite, one can take $\alpha$ as a generic $K$-linear combination of any given generating set. This result is fundamental to algebraic geometry: it implies every finitely generated separable field extension of transcendence degree $n$ is an algebraic extension of a purely transcendental extension $k(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$, and combined with separability arguments it shows every variety is birationally equivalent to a hypersurface.
