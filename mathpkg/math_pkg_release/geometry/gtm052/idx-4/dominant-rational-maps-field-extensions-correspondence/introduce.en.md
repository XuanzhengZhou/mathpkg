This fundamental theorem establishes that dominant rational maps between varieties are categorically equivalent to $k$-algebra homomorphisms between their function fields, with arrows reversed. A dominant rational map $\varphi: X \dashrightarrow Y$ induces a pullback $K(Y) \to K(X)$; conversely, given a $k$-algebra homomorphism $\theta: K(Y) \to K(X)$, one constructs a dominant rational map by using Proposition 4.3 to cover $Y$ by affines and mapping generators. This category equivalence is the algebro-geometric analog of the correspondence between compact Riemann surfaces and their meromorphic function fields.
