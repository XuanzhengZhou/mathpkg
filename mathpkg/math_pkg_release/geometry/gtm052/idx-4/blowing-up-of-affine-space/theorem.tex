Consider the product $\mathbf{A}^n \times \mathbf{P}^{n-1}$, which is a quasi-projective variety. Let $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ be the affine coordinates of $\mathbf{A}^n$ and $y_1, \ldots, y_n$ the homogeneous coordinates of $\mathbf{P}^{n-1}$. The blowing-up of $\mathbf{A}^n$ at the point $O = (0, \ldots, 0)$ is the closed subset $X \subseteq \mathbf{A}^n \times \mathbf{P}^{n-1}$ defined by the equations
$$\{x_i y_j = x_j y_i \mid i, j = 1, \ldots, n\}.$$

Let $\varphi: X \to \mathbf{A}^n$ be the restriction of the projection onto the first factor. Then:
(1) $\varphi$ gives an isomorphism of $X - \varphi^{-1}(O)$ onto $\mathbf{A}^n - O$.
(2) $\varphi^{-1}(O) \cong \mathbf{P}^{n-1}$.
(3) $X$ is irreducible.
