If $Y$ is a closed subvariety of $\mathbf{A}^n$ passing through $O$, the blowing-up of $Y$ at the point $O$ is $\tilde{Y} = \overline{\varphi^{-1}(Y - O)}$, where $\varphi: X \to \mathbf{A}^n$ is the blowing-up of $\mathbf{A}^n$ at $O$. We denote also by $\varphi: \tilde{Y} \to Y$ the morphism obtained by restricting $\varphi: X \to \mathbf{A}^n$ to $\tilde{Y}$.

The morphism $\varphi$ induces an isomorphism of $\tilde{Y} - \varphi^{-1}(O)$ onto $Y - O$, so $\varphi$ is a birational morphism. To blow up at any other point $P$ of $\mathbf{A}^n$, make a linear change of coordinates sending $P$ to $O$.
