This statement establishes a fundamental geometric interpretation of the local ring $\mathcal{O}_P$ of a variety $X$ at a point $P$. The prime ideals of $\mathcal{O}_P$ correspond bijectively to the closed subvarieties of $X$ that contain $P$, ordered by reverse inclusion. This generalizes the affine case where prime ideals of the coordinate ring correspond to closed subvarieties, and provides a crucial link between the algebraic structure of the local ring and the local geometry of the variety near $P$.
