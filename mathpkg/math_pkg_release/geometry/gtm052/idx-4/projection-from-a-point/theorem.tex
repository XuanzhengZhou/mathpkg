Let $\mathbf{P}^n$ be a hyperplane in $\mathbf{P}^{n+1}$ and let $P \in \mathbf{P}^{n+1} - \mathbf{P}^n$. Define a mapping $\varphi: \mathbf{P}^{n+1} - \{P\} \to \mathbf{P}^n$ by
$$\varphi(Q) = \text{the intersection of the unique line containing } P \text{ and } Q \text{ with } \mathbf{P}^n.$$

(a) $\varphi$ is a morphism.
(b) If $Y \subseteq \mathbf{P}^{n+1}$ is a projective variety not containing $P$, then $\varphi|_Y$ gives a morphism from $Y$ into $\mathbf{P}^n$.
