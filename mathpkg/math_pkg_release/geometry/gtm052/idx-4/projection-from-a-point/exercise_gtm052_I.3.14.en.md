Let $\mathbf{P}^n$ be a hyperplane in $\mathbf{P}^{n+1}$ and let $P \in \mathbf{P}^{n+1} - \mathbf{P}^n$. Define a mapping $\varphi: \mathbf{P}^{n+1} - \{P\} \to \mathbf{P}^n$ by $\varphi(Q) =$ the intersection of the unique line containing $P$ and $Q$ with $\mathbf{P}^n$.

(a) Show that $\varphi$ is a morphism.
(b) Let $Y \subseteq \mathbf{P}^{n+1}$ be a projective variety which does not contain $P$. Show that $\varphi|_Y: Y \to \mathbf{P}^n$ is a finite morphism.
