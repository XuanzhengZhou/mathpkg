A field extension $K/k$ is separably generated if there exists a transcendence base $\{x_i\}$ such that $K$ is a separable algebraic extension of the purely transcendental subextension $k(\{x_i\})$. Such a transcendence base is called separating. This notion is crucial in algebraic geometry because the function field $K(X)$ of a variety $X$ over an algebraically closed field $k$ is separably generated, which allows one to prove that every variety is birationally equivalent to a hypersurface via the Primitive Element Theorem.
