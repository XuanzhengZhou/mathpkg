A rational map $\varphi: X \dashrightarrow Y$ between varieties is defined as an equivalence class of morphisms from nonempty open subsets of $X$ to $Y$. By Lemma 4.1, if two such morphisms agree on the intersection of their domains, they agree on the whole intersection, so the equivalence relation is well-defined. Rational maps that are not everywhere defined are indicated by a dashed arrow. A dominant rational map has dense image, which induces a $k$-algebra homomorphism $K(Y) \to K(X)$ between function fields. Rational maps are fundamental for birational classification of varieties.
