**Curves of Genus 5.** Assume $X$ is not hyperelliptic.
(a) The curves of genus $5$ whose canonical model in $\mathbf{P}^4$ is a complete intersection $F_2 \cdot F_2 \cdot F_2$ of three quadric hypersurfaces form an irreducible family. Show that in this case $X$ has no $g_3^1$.
(b) Show that the general curve of genus $5$ has a $g_3^1$, and its canonical model is not a complete intersection.
(c) Show that there is an irreducible family of curves of genus $5$ whose canonical model lies on a quadric cone in $\mathbf{P}^4$, and that these have either a $g_3^1$ or a $g_4^1$.
