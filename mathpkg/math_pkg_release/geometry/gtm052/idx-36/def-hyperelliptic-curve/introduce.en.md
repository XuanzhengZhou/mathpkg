A hyperelliptic curve is a curve of genus at least $2$ that admits a degree-$2$ finite morphism to the projective line $\mathbf{P}^1$. This is a classical and important class of algebraic curves. In the language of linear systems, hyperellipticity is equivalent to the existence of a $g_2^1$ — a linear system of dimension $1$ and degree $2$. The presence or absence of a $g_2^1$ determines many key geometric properties, including the behavior of the canonical morphism.
