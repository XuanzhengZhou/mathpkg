A curve $X$ of genus $g \geqslant 2$ over an algebraically closed field $k$ is called \text{hyperelliptic} if there exists a finite morphism $f: X \to \mathbf{P}^1$ of degree $2$. Equivalently, $X$ is hyperelliptic if and only if it possesses a linear system of dimension $1$ and degree $2$, i.e., a $g_2^1$.
