Clifford's theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of algebraic curves, giving a sharp upper bound on the dimension of a special linear system in terms of its degree. Specifically, for an effective special divisor $D$, the dimension satisfies $\dim|D| \leqslant \frac{1}{2} \deg D$. The equality cases characterize hyperelliptic curves: equality holds (for $D \neq 0, K$) exactly when $X$ is hyperelliptic. The proof combines Lemma 5.5 with the Riemann–Roch theorem.
