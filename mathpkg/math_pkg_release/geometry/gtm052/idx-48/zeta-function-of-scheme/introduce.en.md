The zeta function $Z(X; t)$ of a scheme $X$ over a finite field $\mathbf{F}_q$ encodes the number of rational points of $X$ over all finite extensions $\mathbf{F}_{q^r}$ into a single generating function. It is the fundamental arithmetic invariant studied by the Weil conjectures. For example, for $X = \mathbf{P}^1$, one computes $Z(\mathbf{P}^1, t) = 1/((1-t)(1-qt))$, which is a rational function — a property that the Weil conjectures assert holds for all smooth projective varieties.
