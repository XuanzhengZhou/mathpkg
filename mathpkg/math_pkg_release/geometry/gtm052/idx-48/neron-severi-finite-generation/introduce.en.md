This corollary of the exponential sequence provides a transcendental proof that the Néron–Severi group $\operatorname{NS}(X)$ (divisors modulo algebraic equivalence) is a finitely generated abelian group. The key insight is that algebraically equivalent divisors map to the same class in $H^2(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$, so $\operatorname{NS}(X)$ embeds as a subgroup of the finitely generated integral cohomology group. This result was historically one of the major applications of transcendental methods to algebraic geometry.
