Deligne's theorem (1973) proves the analogue of the Riemann hypothesis for varieties over finite fields: the reciprocal roots $\alpha_{ij}$ of the polynomials $P_i(t)$ appearing in the factorization of the zeta function are algebraic integers whose complex absolute values are precisely $q^{i/2}$. This is the deepest part of the Weil conjectures. Deligne's proof relies on advanced properties of $l$-adic cohomology, Lefschetz pencils, and the study of monodromy actions on cohomology near singular fibers. Together with the earlier work of Grothendieck on rationality and the functional equation, this completed the solution of all four Weil conjectures.
