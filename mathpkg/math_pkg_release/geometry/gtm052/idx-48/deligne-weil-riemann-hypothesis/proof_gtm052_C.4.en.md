The proof of Deligne's theorem [Deligne 3] relies on the deeper properties of $l$-adic cohomology developed in [SGA 4], [SGA 5] and [SGA 7]. In particular it makes use of Lefschetz's technique of fibering a variety by a "Lefschetz pencil," and studying the monodromy action on the cohomology near a singular fibre. The key ingredients include: the weak Lefschetz theorem for $l$-adic cohomology; the construction of a Lefschetz pencil on $X$; the study of the Picard-Lefschetz formula for the monodromy action around singular fibers; and Deligne's weight theory for the eigenvalues of Frobenius. The independence of $l$ follows from Deligne's earlier work on the Weil conjectures for K3 surfaces [Deligne 2] combined with the general theory.
