Moishezon's theorem states that a Moishezon manifold (a compact complex manifold with the maximal possible number of algebraically independent meromorphic functions) which is also Kähler must be projective algebraic. Together with the Kodaira embedding theorem, this provides the complete chain of implications: Hodge $\iff$ projective algebraic $\implies$ abstract algebraic, and Kähler $\implies$ Moishezon. No further implications hold, as demonstrated by counterexamples.
