The functor $h$ sends a scheme $X$ of finite type over $\mathbf{C}$ to its associated complex analytic space $X_h$, and a coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ to its associated coherent analytic sheaf $\mathcal{F}_h$. This functor is the essential bridge between algebraic geometry and complex analytic geometry. It allows one to apply transcendental methods (complex analysis, differential geometry) to algebraic varieties, yielding results that may have no known purely algebraic proof.
