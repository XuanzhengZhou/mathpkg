The proof is due to Chow and Kodaira [1]. For a compact complex surface $\mathfrak{X}$ with two algebraically independent meromorphic functions $f, g$, one considers the meromorphic map $(f,g): \mathfrak{X} \dashrightarrow \mathbf{P}^1 \times \mathbf{P}^1$. The two algebraically independent functions give a dominant rational map to a surface, which after resolving indeterminacies and applying Kodaira's embedding theorem (or the characterization of algebraic surfaces), shows $\mathfrak{X}$ is projective algebraic. The dimension 2 hypothesis is essential; in higher dimensions, Hironaka and Moishezon gave counterexamples.
