The Chow-Kodaira theorem gives a sufficient condition for a compact complex surface to be projective algebraic: it suffices to have two algebraically independent meromorphic functions (the maximal possible number for a surface). This result is specific to dimension 2; in higher dimensions, Hironaka and Moishezon constructed examples of Moishezon manifolds (having the maximal number of algebraically independent meromorphic functions) that are nonetheless not algebraic.
