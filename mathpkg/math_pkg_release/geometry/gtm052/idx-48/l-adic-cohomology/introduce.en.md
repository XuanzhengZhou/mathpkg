$l$-adic cohomology, defined by Grothendieck using the étale topology, provides a Weil cohomology theory for schemes over fields of arbitrary characteristic. It takes values in vector spaces over $\mathbf{Q}_l$ (for a prime $l$ different from the characteristic) and satisfies the formal properties needed to prove the Weil conjectures: finite-dimensionality (for proper schemes), functoriality, cup-product, Poincaré duality, a cycle class map from the Chow ring, a Lefschetz trace formula, and a comparison theorem with ordinary cohomology over $\mathbf{C}$. Deligne's proof of the Riemann hypothesis analogue uses deeper properties of $l$-adic cohomology.
