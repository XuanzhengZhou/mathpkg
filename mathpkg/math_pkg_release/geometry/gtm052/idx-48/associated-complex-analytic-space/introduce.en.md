To every scheme $X$ of finite type over $\mathbf{C}$ one can functorially associate a complex analytic space $X_h$, called the analytification of $X$. The construction glues affine pieces $\operatorname{Spec} \mathbf{C}[x_1,\ldots,x_n]/(f_1,\ldots,f_q)$ by interpreting the polynomials $f_i$ as holomorphic functions and taking their common zero set. This functor $h$ also extends to coherent sheaves, sending $\mathcal{F}$ to $\mathcal{F}_h$, and is the foundation of the GAGA correspondence between algebraic and analytic geometry.
