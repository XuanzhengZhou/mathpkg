The cycle class map sends algebraic cycles on a smooth proper variety to classes in $l$-adic cohomology. It is a ring homomorphism from the Chow ring $A(X)$ to the cohomology ring $H^*(X, \mathbf{Q}_l)$, respecting rational equivalence, intersection products, and non-triviality. This map provides the crucial link between algebraic cycles (geometric objects) and cohomology (a topological-like invariant), and is essential for the cohomological interpretation of the Weil conjectures via the Lefschetz trace formula.
