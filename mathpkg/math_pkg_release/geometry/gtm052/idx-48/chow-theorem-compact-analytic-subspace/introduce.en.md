Chow's theorem states that every compact analytic subspace of complex projective space is actually algebraic — it arises as the analytification of a projective subscheme. This answers question Q1 (existence of an algebraic model for a given analytic space) in the projective case. The theorem is a corollary of Serre's GAGA results and shows that the class of projective algebraic varieties is precisely characterized among compact analytic subspaces of $\mathbf{P}^n$ by the algebraicity of their embedding.
