From the exponential sequence, the subgroup $\operatorname{Pic}^\circ(X)$ of the Picard group (divisors algebraically equivalent to zero modulo linear equivalence) can be identified as the quotient $H^1(X, \mathcal{O}_X)/H^1(X_h, \mathbf{Z})$. This quotient has the structure of a complex torus, and by deeper results it is actually an abelian variety — the Picard variety of $X$. This gives a transcendental construction of the Picard variety, showing how the continuous part of the Picard group comes from the interplay between algebraic coherent cohomology and topological integral cohomology.
