An irreducible nonsingular algebraic variety over the complex numbers is not only a complex manifold (of complex dimension $d$) but also a smooth real manifold (of real dimension $2d$). This theorem asserts that such a real manifold is always orientable. The proof generalizes the curve case by using local analytic parametrizations and showing that the Jacobian determinant of any transition map between coordinate charts is strictly positive.
