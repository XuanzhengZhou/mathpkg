In complex algebraic geometry, irreducibility (in the Zariski topology) implies connectedness (in the complex analytic topology). This theorem shows that any irreducible variety in projective or affine complex space is connected as a topological space. The proof proceeds by first establishing the result for curves via projection arguments, then extending to higher dimensions by joining any two points through connected curves lying on the variety.
