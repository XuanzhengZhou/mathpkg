Let $V = V^r$ be a pure $r$-dimensional variety in $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$ or $\mathbb{C}^n$, let $P \in V$, and let $L_0$ be an $(n-r)$-dimensional projective or affine subspace properly intersecting $V$ at $P$. The fixed number of distinct points of $V \cap L'$ near $P$ for almost all transforms $L'$ sufficiently near $L_0$ (given by Theorem 6.6) is called the \textit{local degree} (or \textit{local order}) of $V$ at $P$ in the direction $L_0$, denoted by $i(V, L_0; P)$.
