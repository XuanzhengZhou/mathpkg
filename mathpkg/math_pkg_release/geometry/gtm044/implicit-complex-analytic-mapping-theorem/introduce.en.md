The implicit complex-analytic mapping theorem describes the local structure of the zero set of a system of complex-analytic functions near a common zero. If the Jacobian matrix has constant rank $r$ near the origin, then the zero set is locally the graph of a unique complex-analytic map $\phi$ from an $(n-r)$-dimensional subspace to an $r$-dimensional one. This theorem is fundamental in algebraic geometry for establishing that varieties are locally complex manifolds at nonsingular points.
