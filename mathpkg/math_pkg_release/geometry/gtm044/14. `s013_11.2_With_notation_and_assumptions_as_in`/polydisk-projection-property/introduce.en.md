Theorem 2.13 bridges the algebraic notion of transcendence degree with the geometric structure of a variety. If the coordinate ring has transcendence degree $d$ and the fiber over the origin in the complementary coordinates is finite, then locally the variety projects surjectively onto a polydisk in the first $d$ coordinates. This is a key technical result used in the dimension theory of Chapter IV.
