For any two nonconstant polynomials in $\mathbb{C}[X]$

$$p_1(X) = (X - b_1) \cdot \ldots \cdot (X - b_r)$$

and

$$p_2(X) = (X - c_1) \cdot \ldots \cdot (X - c_s),$$

the resultant of $p_1$ and $p_2$ is the difference product

$$\mathcal{R}_X(p_1, p_2) = \prod_{\substack{i=1, \ldots, r \\ j=1, \ldots, s}} (b_i - c_j).$$

In particular, $\mathcal{R}_X(p_1, p_2) = (-1)^{rs}\,\mathcal{R}_X(p_2, p_1)$.
