Projective space $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$ is defined as the set of all one-dimensional subspaces of the vector space $k^{n+1}$. Each such subspace is called a point of the projective space, and the collection of $1$-subspaces within an $(r+1)$-subspace forms an $r$-dimensional projective subspace. When $k$ is a topological field, this definition naturally induces a topology on $\mathbb{P}^n(k)$ via the product topology on $k^{n+1}$. This formulation unifies the topological and algebraic perspectives on projective geometry.
