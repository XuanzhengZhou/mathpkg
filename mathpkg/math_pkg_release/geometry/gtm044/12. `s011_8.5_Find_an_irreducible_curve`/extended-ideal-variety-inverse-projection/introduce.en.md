Theorem 10.1 establishes a fundamental link between the algebraic operation of ideal extension and the geometric operation of taking the inverse image under the natural projection. For an embedding of coordinate rings $R \subset R^*$, the set of all maximal ideals of $R^*$ that contract to maximal ideals of $R$ containing a given ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ is exactly the zero-set $\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a}^e)$ of the extended ideal. In geometric language, the points of $V_{R^*}$ that lie above the subvariety defined by $\mathfrak{a}$ form precisely the subvariety defined by $\mathfrak{a}^e$, which equals the inverse projection $\pi^{-1}(\mathcal{V}(\mathfrak{a}))$. This theorem makes precise the intuition that extension of ideals corresponds to taking inverse images of varieties.
