The one-point (or Alexandroff) compactification adds a single point $P$ at infinity to a topological space $T$, making it compact. The open sets of $T^*$ consist of the original open sets of $T$, together with complements of closed compact subsets of $T$ (after adding $P$). Key examples: the one-point compactification of $\mathbb{R}$ is a circle, of $\mathbb{R}^2$ is a sphere, and of a sphere with finitely many points removed is the sphere with those points identified.
