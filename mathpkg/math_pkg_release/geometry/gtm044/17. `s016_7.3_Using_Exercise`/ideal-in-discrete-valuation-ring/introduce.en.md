Lemma 2.20 gives a complete description of all ideals in a discrete rank one valuation ring: every ideal is completely determined by the minimum order of its elements. Specifically, an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ consists precisely of those ring elements whose order is at least $\mathrm{ord}(\mathfrak{a})$. This simple structure is a consequence of the fact that the valuation ring is totally ordered with respect to divisibility, and it immediately implies that the ring is a principal ideal domain.
