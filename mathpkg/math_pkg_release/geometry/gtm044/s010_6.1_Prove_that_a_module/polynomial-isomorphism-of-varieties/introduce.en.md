A polynomial isomorphism between irreducible affine varieties is a bijective map that, together with its inverse, extends to polynomial maps on the ambient affine spaces. This definition generalizes the idea of isomorphism from other geometric categories (differentiable, analytic, linear) to the algebraic setting. The key requirement is that the isomorphism and its inverse are globally given by polynomials, not merely local ones, reflecting the rigid nature of polynomial functions.
