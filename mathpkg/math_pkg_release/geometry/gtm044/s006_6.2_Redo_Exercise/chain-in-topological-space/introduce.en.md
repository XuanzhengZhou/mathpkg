This definition formalizes the idea of a "chain" of overlapping connected open sets in a topological space. A chain is a finite sequence $(\mathcal{O}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{O}_m)$ where each $\mathcal{O}_i$ is connected and open, and consecutive members intersect in a connected, nonempty set. By Lemma 8.2, the union $\mathcal{O}_1 \cup \cdots \cup \mathcal{O}_m$ of a chain is connected. Chains are the fundamental tool used to define "chain connectivity" and to construct analytic continuations in the proof that complex algebraic curves are connected.
