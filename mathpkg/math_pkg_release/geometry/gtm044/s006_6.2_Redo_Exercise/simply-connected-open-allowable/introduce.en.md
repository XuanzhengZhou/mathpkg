This lemma states that in the branched covering defined by projection onto the $Y$-axis from the affine curve $V(p)$ (minus ramification points above the discriminant locus $\mathcal{D}$), every simply connected open subset of the base $\mathbb{C} \setminus \mathcal{D}$ is "allowable" — meaning it can be lifted to $n$ disjoint analytic function elements above it. This follows from the Monodromy Theorem (Theorem 8.14), which guarantees unique analytic continuation along any polygonal path within a simply connected domain. The lemma is crucial for the connectedness proof of complex algebraic curves, as it ensures that local analytic continuations can be consistently patched together over simply connected regions.
