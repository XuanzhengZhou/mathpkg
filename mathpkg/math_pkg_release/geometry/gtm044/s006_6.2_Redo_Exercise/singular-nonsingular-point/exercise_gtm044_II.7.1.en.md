# Exercise 7.1

It is evident from Definition 7.3 that smoothness at $P$ of a given curve $C \subset \mathbb{C}_{XY}$ is a geometric notion---that is, it is independent of the choice of coordinates $X, Y$ in $\mathbb{C}^2$. From Theorem 7.4 we then conclude that singularity and nonsingularity at $P$ are also geometric notions. Prove directly from Definition 7.5 that singularity and nonsingularity at points of $C$ are properties independent of the linear coordinates chosen in $\mathbb{C}^2$.
