This lemma is a crucial duality statement used in the proof of the Riemann-Roch theorem. It relates the behavior of the spaces $L(D)$ and $L(Z-D)$, where $Z$ is a canonical divisor. If there exists a function in $L(Z-D)$ that has a pole at a point $P$ (making $\dim L(Z-D) \neq \dim L(Z-(D+P))$), then adding $P$ to $D$ does not increase the dimension of $L(D)$. The proof translates the hypothesis into a condition on divisors induced by curves and applies Max Noether's AF+BG theorem (Theorem 7.11).
