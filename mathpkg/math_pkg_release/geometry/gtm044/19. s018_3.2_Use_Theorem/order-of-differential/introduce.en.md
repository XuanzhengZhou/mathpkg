This definition extends the notion of order from functions to differentials. Given a differential $\omega$ and a uniformizing variable $z$ at a point $P$, write $\omega = f\,dz$; then $\operatorname{ord}_P(\omega)$ is defined as $\operatorname{ord}_P(f)$. Geometrically, this is the leading exponent in the Laurent expansion of $f$ at $P$. A differential with nonnegative order at $P$ is called holomorphic at $P$. Lemma 6.16 guarantees that this definition does not depend on the choice of uniformizing variable $z$, making it well-defined.
