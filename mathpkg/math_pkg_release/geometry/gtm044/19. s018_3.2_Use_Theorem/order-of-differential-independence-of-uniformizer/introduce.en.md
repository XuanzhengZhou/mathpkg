This lemma ensures that the order of a differential form at a point is well-defined, i.e., independent of the choice of uniformizing variable. If $z$ and $w$ are two uniformizing variables, then $z = uw$ for some unit $u$, and one computes $dz/dw = u + w(du/dw)$. The proof shows that $\operatorname{ord}_P(du/dw) \geq 0$, i.e., $du/dw$ lies in the local ring $\mathfrak{o}(P;C)$, by analyzing the minimal order of $dx/dw$ and $dy/dw$ and extending to arbitrary elements of $\mathfrak{o}(P;C)$ via algebraic operations on differentials.
