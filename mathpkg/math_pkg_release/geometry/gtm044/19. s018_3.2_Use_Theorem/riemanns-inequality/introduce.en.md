Riemann's inequality (also called Riemann's theorem) provides a fundamental lower bound for the dimension of the Riemann-Roch space $L(D)$ of a divisor $D$ on a nonsingular curve $C$ of genus $g$. It states that $\dim L(D) \geq \deg D - (g-1)$, and moreover that $g-1$ is the optimal constant for such a bound. The proof computes $\dim L(lD)$ for divisors $lD$ induced by a line intersecting $C$ transversally, using an exact sequence relating $L(lD)$ to the quotient of two vector spaces of homogeneous forms, and then extends the inequality to arbitrary divisors via linear equivalence.
