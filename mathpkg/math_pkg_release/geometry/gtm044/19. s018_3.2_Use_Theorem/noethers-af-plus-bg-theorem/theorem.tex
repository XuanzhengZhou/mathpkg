Let $G$ and $H$ be forms in $\mathbb{C}[X, Y, Z]$ defining divisors $\operatorname{div}(G) \leqslant \operatorname{div}(H)$ on an irreducible nonsingular curve $C = \mathbf{V}(F) \subset \mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$. Then there are forms $G^*$ and $F^* \in \mathbb{C}[X, Y, Z]$ such that

$$H = GG^* + FF^*.$$
