Let $C \subset \mathbb{C}_{X_1 X_2}$ be a nonsingular irreducible curve, $C = \mathbf{V}(q)$ for an irreducible $q \in \mathbb{C}[X_1, X_2]$, with coordinate ring $\mathbb{C}[X_1, X_2]/(q) = \mathbb{C}[x_1, x_2]$. For any $p(X_1, X_2) \in \mathbb{C}[X_1, X_2]$ ($0 \neq p$, $q \neq p$), let the principal ideal $\mathfrak{a} = (p(x_1, x_2))$ have factorization

$$\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{p}_1^{m_1} \cdots \mathfrak{p}_r^{m_r}$$

into prime ideals. Let $P$ be any point of $\mathbf{V}(p) \cap C$, corresponding to one of the factors $\mathfrak{p}_i$. Then the exponent $m_i$ of $\mathfrak{p}_i^{m_i}$ equals the intersection multiplicity $i(\mathbf{C}(p), C; P)$. That is,

$$\text{The divisor } \mathfrak{p}_1^{m_1} \cdots \mathfrak{p}_r^{m_r} \text{ corresponds precisely to } \mathbf{C}(p) \cdot C.$$
