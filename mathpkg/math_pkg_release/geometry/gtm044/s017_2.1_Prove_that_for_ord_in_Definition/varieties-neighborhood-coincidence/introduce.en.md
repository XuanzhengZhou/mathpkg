Two varieties that agree locally near a point must be identical globally, provided every irreducible component of each variety passes through that point. The result holds in both affine space $\mathbb{C}^n$ and projective space $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$. For irreducible varieties, the proof follows directly from Theorem 2.11 of Chapter IV; the general case reduces to this by considering irreducible components.
