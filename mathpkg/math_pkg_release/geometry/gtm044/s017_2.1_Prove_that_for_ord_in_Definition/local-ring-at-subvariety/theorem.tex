Let $W \subset V$ be irreducible subvarieties of $\mathbb{P}^n(\mathbb{C})$, and let $K_V$ be the function field of $V$. The set
$$\left\{ \frac{p(x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1})}{q(x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1})} \;\middle|\;
\begin{array}{l}
p, q \text{ are forms in } x_1, \ldots, x_{n+1} \text{ of the same degree},\\
q \text{ is not identically zero on } W
\end{array}
\right\}$$
forms a subring of $K_V$. It is called the \text{local ring of $V$ at $W$} and is denoted by $\mathfrak{o}(W; V)$.
