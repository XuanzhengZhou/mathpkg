Let $V$ and $V'$ be two affine or two projective varieties, and let $W \subset V$ and $W' \subset V'$ be irreducible subvarieties. Define $V_W$ as the ordered pair $(V_{(W)}, W)$, where $V_{(W)}$ is the union of those irreducible components of $V$ that contain $W$ (with $V_{(W)} = \varnothing$ if there are no such components).

The operations $=$, $\subset$, $\cap$, and $\cup$ are defined as follows:
\begin{align*}
V_W = V'_{W'} &\iff W = W' \text{ and } V_{(W)} = V'_{(W')}, \\
V_W \subset V'_{W'} &\iff W = W' \text{ and } V_{(W)} \subset V'_{(W')}, \\
V_W \cap V'_{W'} &= (V \cap V')_W, \\
V_W \cup V'_{W'} &= (V \cup V')_W.
\end{align*}

$V_W$ is called \text{irreducible} if $V_W = V'_W \cup V''_W$ implies $V_W = V'_W$ or $V_W = V''_W$.
