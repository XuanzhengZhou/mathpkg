Let $R$ be the coordinate ring of an affine variety $V$ and let $\mathfrak{p} \subset R$ be a prime ideal defining an irreducible subvariety $W = V(\mathfrak{p})$. For each ideal $\mathfrak{a} \in \mathcal{J}(R)$, the contracted extension $\mathfrak{a}^{ec}$ is the intersection of those prime ideals which contain $\mathfrak{a}$ and are contained in $\mathfrak{p}$:
$$\mathfrak{a}^{ec} = \bigcap_{\substack{\mathfrak{q} \supset \mathfrak{a} \\ \mathfrak{q} \subset \mathfrak{p} \\ \mathfrak{q} \text{ prime}}} \mathfrak{q}.$$
(The intersection of an empty set of prime ideals is defined to be $R$.)

Geometrically, if $\mathfrak{a}$ defines the subvariety $X$ of $V$, then $\mathfrak{a}^{ec}$ defines the germ $X_{(W)}$—that is, the mapping $\mathfrak{a} \mapsto \mathfrak{a}^{ec}$ corresponds to taking the germ at $W$ of $X$ within $V$.
