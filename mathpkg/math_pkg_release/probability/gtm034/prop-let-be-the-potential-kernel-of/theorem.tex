It is of course of considerable interest to know more about the asymptotic behavior of $a(x)$ for the simple random walk with $P(0,x) = \frac{1}{4}$ when $|x| = 1$. But it turns out to be hardly more work to discuss a somewhat larger class of random walks, which satisfies the following conditions

(a) $P(x,y)$ is two dimensional, and aperiodic,

(b) $$\mu = \sum xP(0,x) = 0,$$

(c) $$Q(\theta) = \sum (x \cdot \theta)^2 P(0,x) = E[(\mathbf{X} \cdot \theta)^2] = \sigma^2 |\theta|^2 < \infty,$$

(d) $$E[|\mathbf{X}|^{2+\delta}] = \sum |x|^{2+\delta} P(0,x) < \infty \text{ for some } \delta > 0.$$

Of course simple random walk satisfies these conditions, and we also know (T8.1) that (b) and (c) imply that the random walk is recurrent. In addition (c) introduces the isotropy requirement that $Q(\theta)$ be proportional to $|\theta|^2$ rather than to any positive definite quadratic form.
