Let the random walk be aperiodic. If $T < \infty$ and $E[Z] < \infty$, then

(a) $$\lim_{n \to \infty} v(n) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{c} E[Z]} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{c}} \frac{e^{-\alpha}}{\sigma},$$

and if $T'^* < \infty$ and $E[-\bar{Z}] < \infty$, then

(b) $$\lim_{n \to \infty} u(n) = \frac{\sqrt{c}}{E[-\bar{Z}]} = \sqrt{2c} \frac{e^{\alpha}}{\sigma}.$$

In particular, if $\mu = 0$ and $\sigma^2 < \infty$, then both (a) and (b) hold and

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} u(n) \cdot \lim_{n \to \infty} v(n) = \frac{2}{\sigma^2}.$$
