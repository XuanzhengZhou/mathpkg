(a) If $f(x) = G\psi(x)$ is a potential, whose charge $\psi$ has support $A$, i.e. $\psi = 0$ on $R-A$, then

$$f(x) \leq \sup_{t \in A} f(t).$$

(b) If $f_1 = G\psi_1$, and $f_2 = G\psi_2$ are two potentials such that $\psi_1(x) = \psi_2(x) = 0$ for $x \in R-A$, and if $f_1(x) \leq f_2(x)$ for all $x$ in $A$, then

$$f_1(x) \leq f_2(x)$ for all $x$ in $R.$

(c) Under the same hypotheses as in (b) one can conclude that

$$\sum_{x \in A} \psi_1(x) \leq \sum_{x \in A} \psi_2(x).$$

Here the right sum, or even both, may be infinite.
