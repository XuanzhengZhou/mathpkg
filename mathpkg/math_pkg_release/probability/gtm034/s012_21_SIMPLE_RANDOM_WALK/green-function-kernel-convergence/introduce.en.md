This proposition states the natural analogue of Proposition 22.P1 for the Green function $g_N$: the scaled Green function converges to the integral kernel $R(s,t) = \min(s,t) - st$, which is the Green function of the second derivative operator. This result is mathematically deeper than Proposition 22.P1—its proof requires the probabilistic machinery developed in Section 23 (specifically Theorem 23.T1). The proposition serves as a bridge between the discrete potential theory of random walk and the classical potential theory of the Laplacian.
