Gabor Szegö's theory of orthogonal polynomials on the unit circle provides an alternative to eigenfunction expansions for symmetric difference operators. The polynomials \(p_n(z)\) are orthogonal with respect to the spectral measure \(1 - \phi(	heta)\) and yield a compact representation of the Green function on the interval. The limit of the polynomial coefficients recovers the ladder height variables from fluctuation theory, demonstrating a deep connection between orthogonal polynomials and random walk.
