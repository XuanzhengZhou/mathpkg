This proposition gives the limit distribution of the exit time $T_{2N}$ for simple symmetric random walk starting at the midpoint of the interval, scaled by $N^2$. The result is obtained by expressing the distribution of $T_{2N}$ in terms of powers of $Q_{2N}$ and then using the spectral decomposition to approximate the sum by an integral as $N \to \infty$. The limit distribution $F(x)$ admits an explicit series representation in terms of exponentials. This result serves as a prototype: in Section 23, it is shown that the same limit distribution arises for any one-dimensional random walk with mean zero and finite variance.
