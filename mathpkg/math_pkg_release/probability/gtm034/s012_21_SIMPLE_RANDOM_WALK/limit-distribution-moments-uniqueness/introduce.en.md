This proposition provides the technical foundation for the method of moments used to prove Theorem 23.T2. The explicit formula for the moments $m_p$ of the limit distribution $F$ is obtained by straightforward calculation from the series representation. The crucial fact is that the moment problem is determinate (has a unique solution) because the moment generating function has a positive radius of convergence ($\pi^2/8$). This uniqueness justifies the strategy of proving convergence in distribution by proving convergence of moments, which is technically simpler than a direct distributional argument.
