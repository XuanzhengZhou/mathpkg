This proposition shows that the number of visits to a fixed point $y$ before exiting a large symmetric interval, when scaled by $N$, converges to an exponential distribution. The parameter of the exponential is the variance $\sigma^2$ of the random walk. The proof exploits the fact that $\mathbf{N}_N(y)$ has a geometric distribution (because each visit to $y$ is followed, independently in the Markov sense, by a probability $r_N(y)$ of never returning before exiting), and the parameters are expressed in terms of the Green function $g_{2N}$, whose asymptotics are governed by Theorem 23.T1.
