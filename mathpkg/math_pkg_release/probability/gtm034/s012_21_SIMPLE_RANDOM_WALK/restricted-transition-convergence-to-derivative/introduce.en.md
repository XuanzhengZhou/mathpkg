This proposition establishes that the discrete operator $I_N - Q_N$, after appropriate scaling by $2N^2/\sigma^2$, converges to the negative second derivative operator $-d^2/dx^2$ as $N \to \infty$. This is a functional-analytic manifestation of the Central Limit Theorem: the local structure of the transition function (mean zero, finite variance) determines the limiting differential operator. The proof uses a Taylor expansion of $f$ and exploits the moment conditions $\mu = 0$ and $\sigma^2 < \infty$ to show that the drift and diffusion terms converge appropriately. This result provides the rigorous foundation for treating the absorption problem as a discrete analogue of the Dirichlet boundary value problem for the second derivative.
