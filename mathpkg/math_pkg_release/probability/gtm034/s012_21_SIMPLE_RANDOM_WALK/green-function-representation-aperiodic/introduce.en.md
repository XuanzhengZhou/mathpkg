This proposition provides a crucial factorization of the half-line Green function $g(x,y)$ as a sum of products of one-variable sequences $u$ and $v$. This factorization is a consequence of the fact that $P(x,y) = P(0, y-x)$ is a difference kernel (translation-invariant), which makes the Green function of the half-line expressible as a discrete convolution. The existence of positive limits $u$ and $v$ with product $2/\sigma^2$ is the key analytic input needed for the asymptotic analysis of the interval absorption problem. The aperiodicity assumption ensures the renewal theorem applies, and the finite variance condition controls the rate of convergence to the limits.
