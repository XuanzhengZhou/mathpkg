The exit time $T_N$ is the first time at which a random walk leaves the interval $[0,N]$. It is a fundamental stopping time in the study of the absorption problem, as all quantities of interest—Green functions, hitting probabilities, occupation times—are defined in terms of the behavior of the random walk up to time $T_N$. The asymptotic distribution of $T_N$ as $N \to \infty$ is one of the central objects of study in the theory of random walk on an interval.
