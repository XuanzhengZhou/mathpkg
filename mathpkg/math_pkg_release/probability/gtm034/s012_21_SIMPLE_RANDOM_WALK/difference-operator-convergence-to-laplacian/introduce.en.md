This proposition shows that the discrete operator \(I_N - Q_N\), properly scaled, converges to the negative second derivative operator \(-d^2/dx^2\) as the interval size grows. This justifies the heuristic that random walk restricted to an interval approximates a diffusion process with zero boundary conditions, establishing the connection between the absorption problem and the classical Sturm-Liouville problem.
