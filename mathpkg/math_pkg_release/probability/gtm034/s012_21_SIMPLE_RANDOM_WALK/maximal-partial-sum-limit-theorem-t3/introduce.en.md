This reformulation of T23.2 relates the exit time distribution to the distribution of the maximum absolute value of the partial sums up to time \(n\). Properly normalized by \(\sigma\sqrt{n}\), the maximum converges to a universal limit distribution. This result was first proved rigorously by Erdős and Kac in 1946 using an invariance principle that reduces the problem to Brownian motion.
