This theorem is the crowning achievement of the absorption problem theory: the limit distribution of the (scaled) exit time is universal — the same for all one-dimensional random walks with mean zero and finite variance. The limit distribution $F(x)$ is characterized by its Laplace transform $(\cosh \sqrt{2s})^{-1}$ and admits an explicit series representation. The proof uses the method of moments: moments of $\mathbf{T}_N$ are expressed in terms of powers of the Green function $g_{2N}$, whose asymptotic behavior is controlled by Theorem 23.T1. The moment problem for $F$ has a unique solution, so convergence of moments implies convergence in distribution. This generalizes P21.5 from simple random walk to the entire class of mean-zero finite-variance random walks.
