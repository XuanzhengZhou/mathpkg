For recurrent aperiodic random walk in the plane, removing the origin turns the walk into a transient kernel $Q$ on $S = R \setminus \{0\}$. The potential kernel $a(x)$ becomes a $Q$-regular function. Using $g(x,y) = a(x) + a(-y) - a(x-y)$ and $\lim_{|y| \to \infty} [a(x+y) - a(y)] = 0$, the Martin kernel limit evaluates to a constant multiple of $a(x)$. By P3, $a(x)$ is the unique non-negative regular function. This gives a clean potential-theoretic characterization: the potential kernel is the minimal positive harmonic function for the walk with the origin removed.
