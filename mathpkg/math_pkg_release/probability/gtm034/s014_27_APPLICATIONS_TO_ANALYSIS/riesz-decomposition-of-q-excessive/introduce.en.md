The Riesz decomposition theorem, discovered by F. Riesz for classical superharmonic functions and adapted here to discrete transient kernels, states that every $Q$-excessive function splits uniquely into the sum of a $Q$-regular part and a $Q$-potential. The regular part $h$ is obtained as the pointwise limit $h = \lim_n Q_n f$, while the potential part $u$ is given explicitly by the Green potential of the charge $\psi = f - Qf$. Uniqueness follows because the difference of two such decompositions would be simultaneously regular and a potential, forcing it to be zero by P1(3). This theorem is the cornerstone of the potential-theoretic analysis in Section 27 and is used in the proof of P3.
