Identify $S = R$, $Q(x,y) = P(x,y)$, $g(x,y) = G(x,y)$, and $\xi = 0$. By P26.1, under hypotheses (a)-(c) (mean zero, finite second moments),
$$\lim_{|y| \to \infty} \frac{G(x,y)}{G(0,y)} = 1, \quad x \in R.$$
Hence $f(x) \equiv 1$ is the limit function in the hypothesis of P3. The constant function is $P$-regular for every random walk. All hypotheses of P3 are satisfied, so the positive multiples of $f(x) \equiv 1$ are the only $Q$-regular functions.
