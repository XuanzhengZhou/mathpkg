This theorem resolves the Wiener-Hopf equation for recurrent aperiodic random walk on the half-line. By restricting the transition function $P(x,y)$ to $S = [0,\infty)$, one obtains a transient kernel $Q$, and the Wiener-Hopf equation becomes the statement that $f$ is $Q$-regular. Using the explicit factorization of the Green function from Chapter IV and the asymptotic property $\lim_{y \to \infty} v(y+1)/v(y) = 1$ (proved via a probabilistic argument), the hypothesis of P3 is verified, yielding the unique non-negative solution as the cumulative sum of the $u$-function from the Wiener-Hopf factorization. This gives a clean potential-theoretic characterization of $u(x)$.
