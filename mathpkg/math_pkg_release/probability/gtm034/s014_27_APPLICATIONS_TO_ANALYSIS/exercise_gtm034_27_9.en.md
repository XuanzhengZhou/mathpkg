Problem 8 strongly suggests that every regular function for a transient random walk should be a convex combination of minimal regular functions. As in problem 8 one can show that the minimal regular functions are exponentials of the form
$$f(x) = e^{a \cdot x}$$
where $a$ is a vector whose dimension is that of $R$ (see [25]). In view of problem 6, random walk with zero mean is not of interest. Therefore we shall consider strongly aperiodic random walk with mean vector $\mu \neq 0$, assuming also that $P_n(0,x) > 0$ for some $n$ which may depend on $x$, and that
$$\sum_{x \in R} P(0,x)e^{a \cdot x} < \infty$$
for all $a \in A$, where $A$ is the set of vectors $a$ such that the above sum equals one. Show that the set $A$ is non-empty, and that the vectors $a \in A$ are in one-to-one correspondence with the minimal regular functions.
