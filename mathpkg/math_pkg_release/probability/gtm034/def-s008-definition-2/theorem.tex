$T^* = \min [n \mid 1 \leq n \leq \infty; S_n < 0],$

$T'^* = \min [n \mid 1 \leq n \leq \infty; S_n \leq 0],$

$Z = S_T,$ and

$\bar{Z} = S_{T'},$ $Z$ and $\bar{Z}$ being defined only when $T$ and $T'^*$ are finite with probability one.

What motivates the notation in D1 is of course that $T^*$ plays the role of $T$ for the reversed random walk, and $T'^*$ the role of $T'$. $Z$ and $\bar{Z}$ were introduced mainly to avoid cumbersome notation.
