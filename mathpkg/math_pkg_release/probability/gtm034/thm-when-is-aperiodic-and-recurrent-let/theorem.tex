When $P(x,y)$ is aperiodic and recurrent, let $A \subset R$, $1 \leq |A| < \infty$, $\psi = 0$ on $R - A$, $f \geq 0$ on $R$, and suppose that

(1) $Pf(x) - f(x) = \psi(x) \geq 0, \quad x \in R.$

Then

$$f(x) = \sum_{y \in A} A(x,y)\psi(y) + c_1 \text{ when } d = 2 \text{ or } d = 1 \text{ with } \sigma^2 = \infty,$$

and

$$f(x) = \sum_{y \in A} A(x,y)\psi(y) + c_2x + c_3 \text{ when } d = 1 \text{ with } \sigma^2 < \infty.$$

Here $c_1, c_2, c_3$ are constants satisfying

$$c_1 + \min_{x \in A} \sum_{y \in A} A(x,y)\psi(y) \geq 0,$$

$$\sigma^2 |c_2| \leq \sum_{x \in A} \psi(x), \quad c_3 + \min_{x \in A} \left[ \sum_{y \in A} A(x,y)\psi(y) + c_2x \right] \geq 0.$$

Conversely every $f(x)$ of this form is non-negative and satisfies (1).
