Proposition P8 controls the difference between $q_n$ and $\tilde{q}_{n+1}$, showing they differ by at most $\epsilon_1 q_n$. The proof decomposes both quantities according to the last time the walk revisits its position at time $k$, and uses the asymptotic behavior of $U_n = P_n(0,0)$. Together with P7, this yields $F_{n+1} \geq (1-\epsilon_1)^2 F_n$, the crucial inequality completing the proof of T2.
