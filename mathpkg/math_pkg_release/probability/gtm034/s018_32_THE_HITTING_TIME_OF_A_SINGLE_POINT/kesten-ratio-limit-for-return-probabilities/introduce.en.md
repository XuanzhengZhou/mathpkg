Kesten's theorem (T2) states that for any strongly aperiodic recurrent random walk in one dimension, the ratio of consecutive return probabilities $F_{n+1}/F_n$ converges to $1$ as $n \to \infty$. This deceptively simple statement, proved in 1963, is a deep result requiring delicate combinatorial estimates spanning four propositions (P5 through P8). It implies $R_n/R_{n+1} \to 1$, a crucial ingredient for extending the ratio limit theorem T1 to the case of infinite variance.
