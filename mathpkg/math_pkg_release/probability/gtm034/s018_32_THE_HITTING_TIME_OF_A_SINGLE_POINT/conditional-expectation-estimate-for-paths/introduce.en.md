Proposition P6 is a technical probabilistic estimate controlling the expected number of indices $k$ for which, conditional on a first return at time $n$, the path either stays within distance $A$ of the origin or fails to revisit its position within $m$ to $M$ steps. The proof uses truncation arguments, the exponential bound for Bernoulli sums (P5.3), and crucially relies on the exponential lower bound from P5 to dominate the error terms as $n \to \infty$.
