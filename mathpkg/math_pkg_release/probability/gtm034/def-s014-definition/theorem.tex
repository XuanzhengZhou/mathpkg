$$Qf(x) = \sum_{y \in S} Q(x,y)f(y) = f(x), \quad x \in S.$$

They will be called $Q$-regular. Two more classes of functions are defined, the $Q$-potentials and also a convenient class of functions which contains all $Q$-potentials. The latter are called $Q$-excessive and form an analogue of the superharmonic functions in classical analysis.
