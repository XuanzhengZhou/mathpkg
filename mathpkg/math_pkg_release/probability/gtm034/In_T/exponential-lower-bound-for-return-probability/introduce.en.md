Proposition P5 establishes an exponential lower bound for the return probabilities $F_n$. The proof uses a combinatorial argument: choosing non-zero steps $a_1$ and $-a_2$ with positive probability, bounding the probability of a specific path from $(1,a_1)$ to $(n-1,a_2)$, and applying cyclic permutations to show at least one polygonal path lies above its chord, which forces the random walk to first return at time $n$.
