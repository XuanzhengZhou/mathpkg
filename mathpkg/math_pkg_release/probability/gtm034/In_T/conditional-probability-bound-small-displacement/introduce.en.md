Proposition P6 provides a crucial estimate for the proof of T2: the fraction of indices $k$ for which, conditional on $T = n$, the walk either stays near the origin ($|S_k| \leq A$) or fails to revisit its position within $m$ to $M$ steps, is asymptotically bounded by $\epsilon$. The proof uses exponential tail bounds for sums of Bernoulli variables together with the lower bound $F_n \geq (1-\epsilon)^n/(n-2)$ from P5.
