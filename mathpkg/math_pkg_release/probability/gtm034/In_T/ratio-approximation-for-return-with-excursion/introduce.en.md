Proposition P7 shows that $F_n$ can be well-approximated by $q_n$, the probability of return at time $n$ with an excursion condition: the walk is far from the origin at some time $k$, and returns to that position within $m$ to $M$ steps. The proof follows from P6, which guarantees that the complementary event (small displacement or no quick return) has negligible probability.
