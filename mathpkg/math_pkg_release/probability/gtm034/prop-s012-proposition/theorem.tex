22. THE ABSORPTION PROBLEM WITH MEAN ZERO, FINITE VARIANCE

There are good reasons for suspecting strong similarity between simple random walk and any other random walk with mean zero and finite variance. Thus the Central Limit Theorem asserts that the asymptotic behavior of $P_n(x,y)$ is in a sense the same for every random walk with $\mu = 0, \sigma^2 < \infty$. But it is not clear how relevant the Central Limit Theorem is to the absorption problem where we are concerned with $Q_N(x,y)$ and its iterates rather than with the unrestricted transition function $P(x,y)$. For this reason we shall begin by investigating to what extent the operator $Q_N - I_N$ has a behavior resembling that of the second-order differential operator.
