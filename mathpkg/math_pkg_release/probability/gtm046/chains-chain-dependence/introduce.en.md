A chain is a sequence of $\sigma$-fields where the past and future are conditionally independent given the present. This generalizes the Markov property: the conditional distribution of future events given the entire past depends only on the most recent past. When specialized to $\sigma$-fields induced by random variables, chain-dependence recovers the classical notion of Markov chains in their most general form.
