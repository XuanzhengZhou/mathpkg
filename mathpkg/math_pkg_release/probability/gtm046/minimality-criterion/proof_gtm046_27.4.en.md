According to Chapter II: Complements and Details 23, there exists a $Z_0$ such that $\mu_0 A = 0 \iff$ every $\mu_t A = 0$, or equivalently, up to $P$-null subsets, $Z_0 = 0$ on $A \iff$ every $Z_t = 0$ on $A$.

Since, on account of (i'), every $E_Z^{\beta} X$ common to all the equivalence classes $E_{Z_t}^{\beta} X$ belongs also to the equivalence class of $E_{Z_0}^{\beta} X$, it follows that every sufficient $\sigma$-field $\beta$ with $Z$ is also sufficient with $Z_0$. Therefore, the least fine sufficient $\sigma$-field with $Z_0$ is the minimal one.

On account of (i'), the corresponding factorization—every $Z_t = g_t Z_0$ a.s.—is such that $Z_0 = 0$ a.s. $\Rightarrow$ every $Z_t = 0$ a.s.
