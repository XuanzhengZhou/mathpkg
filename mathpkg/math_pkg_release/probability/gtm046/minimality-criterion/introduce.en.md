The minimality criterion identifies the coarsest sufficient $\sigma$-field for a family of probability measures. Given a dominating measure $Z_0$ that vanishes exactly where every member of the family vanishes, every sufficient $\sigma$-field that works for some $Z$ also works for $Z_0$, establishing $Z_0$'s associated $\sigma$-field as the minimal sufficient one. The factorization $Z_t = g_t Z_0$ a.s. characterizes the relationship between the family members and the minimal sufficient statistic.
