The following three properties are equivalent: (i) $\lim \sup \overline{P^n} A \to 0$ as $A \downarrow \emptyset$. (ii) $\lim \overline{P^n} = \overline{P}$ exists and is a probability on $\mathcal{A}$. (iii) There exists on $\mathcal{A}$ a probability $\overline{P}$ invariant under $T$ and coinciding with $P$ on the $\sigma$-field $\mathcal{C}$ of invariant events (and then $\lim \overline{P^n} = \overline{P}$).
