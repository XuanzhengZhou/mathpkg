For every $x$ such that $\bar{P}_s(x, R) = 0$ and for every $S$, $$\frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^{n} P^k(x, S) \to \bar{P}(x, S)$$ where $\bar{P}(x, S)$ is $\bar{P}$-continuous and $\bar{P}(x, S) = \int \bar{P}(x, dy) P(y, S)$. If $\bar{P}_s(x, R) = 0$ except for $x \in N_s$ with $\bar{P}N_s = 0$, then for every $x \notin N_s$ and every $S$, $\bar{P}(x, S)$ is idempotent: $$\bar{P}(x, S) = \int \bar{P}(x, dy) \bar{P}(y, S).$$
