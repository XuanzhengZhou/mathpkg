For $m < n$ and $S_0$ the $\bar{P}$-null set of points $y$ for which $P(X_m = y; X_n \in S_n) > 0$, then

$$P_s^n(x, R) = P^n(x, S_n) = \int P^m(x, dy) \, P(X_m = y; X_n \in S_n) \leq P^m(x, S_0) \leq P_s^m(x, R).$$

Thus $P_s^n(x, R)$ is nonincreasing in $n$, and being bounded below by 0, it converges. The limit $\bar{P}_s(x, R) = \lim P_s^n(x, R)$ is called the singular transition probability.
