Let $a_k$ be the first $m$-positive term and let $a_k + \cdots + a_l$ be the shortest positive sum starting with $a_k$. If one of the terms $a_h$ of this sum is not $m$-positive, then $a_h + \cdots + a_l \leq 0$ so that $a_k + \cdots + a_{h-1} > 0$ and the sum is not the shortest positive one. Thus, all its terms are $m$-positive. Hence, the successive $m$-positive terms form disjoint stretches of positive sums.
