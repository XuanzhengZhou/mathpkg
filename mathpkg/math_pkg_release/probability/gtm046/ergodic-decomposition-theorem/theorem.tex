**Ergodic Decomposition Theorem.** Every invariant probability $\bar{P}$ on the Borel $\sigma$-field $\mathcal{B}$ admits a decomposition

$$\bar{P}S = \int_{T} \bar{P}_t S \, \mu(dt)$$

where $\mu$ is a probability measure on a $\sigma$-field in $T$, each $\bar{P}_t$ is an invariant and $\bar{P}$-continuous probability measure concentrated on an invariant atom $S_t$, and $\bar{P}(x, S_t) = 1$ for $\bar{P}$-almost every $x \in S_t$.

Moreover, every probability $\bar{P}'$ of the form $\bar{P}'S = \int \bar{P}_t S \, \mu(dt)$ is invariant. Conversely, if an invariant probability is $\bar{P}$-continuous, then it admits such a representation.
