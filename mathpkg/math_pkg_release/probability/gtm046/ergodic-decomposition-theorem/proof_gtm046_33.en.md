Since the Borel field $\mathfrak{B}$ is generated by a denumerable field $\{S(n)\}$ of Borel sets (e.g., the field of finite sums of intervals with rational extremities) and the conditional probability $\bar{P}(x, S)$ given the $\sigma$-field of invariant Borel sets is regularized, the decomposition theorem 26.2A applies. This yields a decomposition into invariant atoms $S_t$ with corresponding conditional probabilities $\bar{P}(x, S)$.

By the vanishing singular tr.pr. theorem (B), for every fixed $S$,
$$\bar{P}(x, S) = \int \bar{P}(x, dy) P(y, S), \quad x \notin N_S, \quad \bar{P} N_S = 0.$$
It follows that
$$\bar{P}_t S = \int \bar{P}_t(dx) P(x, S)$$
except for indices $t \in T_S \subset T$ corresponding to sets $S_t$ of total $\bar{P}$-probability zero. Adding such sets for $S = S(1), S(2), \cdots$ to the $P$-null set $N$ of the partition, the equality holds for all remaining $t$ and every $S(n)$. Since the $S(n)$ form a field, the equality extends to all Borel sets $S$, proving invariance of the $\bar{P}_t$.

Conversely, if an invariant probability $\bar{P}'$ is $\bar{P}$-continuous, then $\bar{P}'S = \int \bar{P}_t S \, \mu(dt)$ for some probability $\mu$ on $T$. The component chains are stationary by invariance and indecomposable since the $S_t$ are invariant atoms.
