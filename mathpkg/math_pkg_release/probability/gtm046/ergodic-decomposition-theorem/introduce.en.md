The Ergodic Decomposition Theorem provides a disintegration of an invariant probability measure into ergodic components. Given any invariant probability $\bar{P}$ on a Borel $\sigma$-field, it can be expressed as a mixture (integral) of invariant, idempotent component probabilities $\bar{P}_t$, each concentrated on an invariant atom $S_t$. This decomposition is the measure-theoretic analog of the ergodic decomposition of a dynamical system into its indecomposable (ergodic) parts. The theorem relies on the regularity of conditional probabilities and applies the decomposition theorem for $\sigma$-fields (Theorem 26.2A) to the conditional probability $\bar{P}(x, S)$ given the invariant $\sigma$-field.
