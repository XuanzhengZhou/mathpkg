On atoms of the conditioning $\sigma$-field, the conditional expectation reduces to an ordinary average. This explains the "smoothing" terminology: $E^{\mathcal{B}}X$ replaces $X$ by its local averages over the atoms of $\mathcal{B}$, producing a function with "fewer values" than the original.
