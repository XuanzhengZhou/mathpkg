Riesz's Lemma for m-positive terms states that given a finite or infinite sequence of real numbers, if some terms are classified as "m-positive" (meaning a partial sum beginning at that term is strictly positive), then the sum of all m-positive terms taken together yields a positive total. This is proven by extracting the shortest positive sum starting from the first m-positive term, and showing that all contributing terms in this minimal sum must themselves be m-positive. The lemma is used as a combinatorial tool in the proof of the basic ergodic inequality.
