Assume $Y_n > 0$, $Y^n \uparrow \infty$, and the following conditions hold: (i) for every invariant $C$, $\int_C |X_{n+k}|/Y^n \to 0$ and $\int_C Y_{n+k}/Y^n \to 0$ for every fixed $k$ as $n \to \infty$, and (ii) for some $m$, the sum in the basic inequality exists from some $n$ on. Then the sequence $X^n/Y^n$ converges almost surely.
