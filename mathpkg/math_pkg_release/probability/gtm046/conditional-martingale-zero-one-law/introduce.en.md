The martingale convergence theorem applied to the sequence of conditional expectations $Z_n = E(Y \mid Y_1, \cdots, Y_n)$ yields zero-one laws. When $Y$ is an indicator of a tail event, this shows that tail events have trivial conditional probability given the full sequence, recovering Kolmogorov's zero-one law in the independent case.
