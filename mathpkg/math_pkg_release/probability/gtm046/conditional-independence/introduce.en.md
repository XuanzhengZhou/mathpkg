Conditional independence generalizes ordinary independence by replacing probabilities with conditional probabilities given a $\sigma$-field $\mathcal{B}$. Ordinary independence is recovered when $\mathcal{B} = \{\emptyset, \Omega\}$, while conditioning on the full $\sigma$-field $\mathcal{A}$ makes any two sub-$\sigma$-fields trivially conditionally independent.
