This theorem justifies the common notation $E(X \mid Y = y)$ by proving that the conditional expectation given a random variable $Y$ is almost surely a measurable function of $Y$. The proof uses the Radon-Nikodym theorem on the induced probability space of $Y$, showing that the indefinite integral of $X$ on the $\sigma$-field induced by $Y$ corresponds to a function on the range space.
