A second-order random function $X(t)$ continuous in q.m. on a closed interval has a representation via the orthonormalized proper functions $\psi_n$ of its covariance $\Gamma(t, t')$. The integrals $\lambda_n \xi_n = \int X(t) \bar{\psi}_n(t) dt$ exist in q.m., and $E |X(t) - X_n(t)|^2 \to 0$ uniformly on $I$ where $X_n(t) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \xi_k \psi_k(t)$. The harmonic (spectral) orthogonal decomposition yields amplitudes and energies corresponding to parts of the spectrum.
