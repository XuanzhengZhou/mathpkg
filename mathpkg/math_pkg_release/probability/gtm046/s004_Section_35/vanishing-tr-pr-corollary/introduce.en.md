When the singular transition probability vanishes for every state, the limit $\bar{P}(x,S)$ satisfies a symmetry relation: integrating the $m$-step transition probability $P^m(x,dy)$ against $\bar{P}(y,S)$ recovers $\bar{P}(x,S)$. The proof shifts the summation index and applies dominated convergence.
