Let $k=1,\ldots,n+m$, $k\leq l\leq\min(k+m-1,n+m)$, and $B^{mk}=[X_k-bY_k\text{ is }m\text{-positive}]=[\sup_l (X_k+\cdots+X_l)/(Y_k+\cdots+Y_l)>b]$. For $k\leq n$, $B^{mk}=B_k^m$, the translate by $k-1$ of $B^m$. By Riesz's lemma, the $m$-positive terms among $X_k-bY_k$ form disjoint stretches of positive sums. Summing over all stretches yields the inequality, and integrating preserves it.
