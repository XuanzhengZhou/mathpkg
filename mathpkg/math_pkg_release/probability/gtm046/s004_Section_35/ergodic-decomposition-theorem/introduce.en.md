The Ergodic Decomposition Theorem represents invariant probability measures as mixtures of ergodic components. Using decomposition theorem 26.2A and regularized conditional probabilities given the invariant $\sigma$-field, the state space partitions into invariant atoms $S_t$, each carrying an ergodic probability $\bar{P}_t$. Every $\bar{P}$-continuous invariant probability is a unique mixture of these $\bar{P}_t$, and vice versa.
