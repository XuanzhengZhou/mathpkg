Riesz's Lemma for m-positive terms states that given a sequence of real numbers, if some terms are "m-positive" (a partial sum beginning at that term is strictly positive), then the sum of all m-positive terms is positive. The proof extracts the shortest positive sum starting from the first m-positive term. The lemma is a combinatorial tool used in the proof of the basic ergodic inequality.
