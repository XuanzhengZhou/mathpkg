The conditional expectation $E^Y X$ of an integrable r.v. $X$ given $Y$ is a measurable function of $Y$: there exists a measurable $g$ on the range of $Y$ such that $E^Y X=g(Y)$ a.s. If $\varphi'(B')=\varphi(B)$ with $B=Y^{-1}(B')$, where $\varphi$ is the indefinite integral of $X$, then $\varphi'$ is $\sigma$-additive and $P'_Y$-continuous. The Radon-Nikodym theorem yields $g$ with $\int_{B'}g dP'_Y=\varphi'(B')=\int_B X dP=\int_B(E^Y X)dP$, so $g(Y)$ and $E^Y X$ have identical indefinite integrals.
