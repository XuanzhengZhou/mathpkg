If $g$ is a potential, then $P^n g \to 0$ by conclusion (3) of Theorem 9-15 and $f$ is its charge by conclusion (2). Hence $\alpha f$ is finite by definition. Conversely, if $(I-P)g = f$, we have by induction
$$g = P^n g + (I + \cdots + P^{n-1})f.$$
If $P^n g \to 0$, then $g = \lim_n [(I + P + \cdots + P^{n-1})f]$; if additionally $\alpha f$ is finite, then $g$ is a potential by Definition 9-1.
