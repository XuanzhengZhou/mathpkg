In the framework of Problem 4, introduce a second charge $\bar{f}$, its potential $\bar{g}$, and $\bar{s}$. Prove that if $\mu = \text{dual } f$, then
$$(g, \bar{g}) = \frac{1}{2} M_{\pi}[s \bar{s}] + \frac{1}{2} \mu \bar{f}.$$
Find the corresponding expression for $I(g)$.
