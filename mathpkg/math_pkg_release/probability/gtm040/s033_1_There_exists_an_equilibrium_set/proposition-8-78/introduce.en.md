Proposition 8-78 gives the Riesz decomposition for nonnegative supermartingales in the general setting of stochastic processes. Every nonnegative supermartingale splits uniquely into a martingale part (the "harmonic" component) and a potential part (the "transient" component). The martingale part $r_n$ is obtained as the limit of conditional expectations of far-future values, while the potential part $(g_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$ can be expressed using the charge process $(f_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$.
