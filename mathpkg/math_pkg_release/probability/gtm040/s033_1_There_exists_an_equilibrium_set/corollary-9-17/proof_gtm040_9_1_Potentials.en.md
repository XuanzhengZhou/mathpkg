We apply Corollary 9-16. By writing $g = g^+ - g^-$, we may assume $g \geq 0$. Then $P^n g$ is nonnegative and, for a null chain, $L = \lim P^n = 0$, so $P^n g \to 0$. Setting $f = (I-P)g$, we need to verify $\alpha f$ is finite. Since $g \geq 0$, $0 \leq Pg \leq g$ would make $g$ superregular; but even without this, the finiteness of $\alpha g$ and the fact that $P^n g \to 0$ ensure the result through the induction formula $g = P^n g + (I + \cdots + P^{n-1})f$ and the definition of a charge.
