This proposition (2-8) states that the probability measure on a sequence space is fully determined by two families of parameters: the starting probabilities $\Pr[x_0 = c_0]$ and the conditional transition probabilities $\Pr[x_n = c_n \mid x_0 = c_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge x_{n-1} = c_{n-1}]$. The proof follows by induction on $n$, repeatedly applying the definition of conditional probability to factor the joint distribution into a product of successive conditional probabilities.
