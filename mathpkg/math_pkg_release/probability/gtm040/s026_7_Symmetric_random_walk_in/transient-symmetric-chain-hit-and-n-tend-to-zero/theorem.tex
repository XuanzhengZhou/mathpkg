Let $P$ be a Markov chain with an infinite state space such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $P$ is transient.
\item[(2)] $P = P^T$.
\item[(3)] $P$ has only finitely many non-zero entries in each row.
\end{enumerate}
Then
$$\lim_{j \to \infty} H_{0j} = 0.$$
If, in addition, $P$ represents sums of independent random variables, then
$$\lim_{j \to \infty} N_{0j} = 0.$$
In particular, the conclusions apply to the symmetric random walk in any dimension $n \geq 3$.
