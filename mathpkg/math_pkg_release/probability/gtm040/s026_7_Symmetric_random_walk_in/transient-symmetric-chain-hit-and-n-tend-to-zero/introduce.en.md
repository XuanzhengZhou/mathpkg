This proposition establishes that for a transient Markov chain with an infinite state space that is symmetric ($P = P^T$) and has only finitely many non-zero entries in each row, the hitting probabilities $H_{0j}$ tend to zero as $j$ goes to infinity. When the chain additionally represents sums of independent random variables, the $N$-matrix entries $N_{0j}$ also tend to zero. This result is the discrete analogue of the decay of the potential kernel $1/|x|^{n-2}$ in Brownian motion theory and applies in particular to the symmetric random walk in dimensions $n \geq 3$.
