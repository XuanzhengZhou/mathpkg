**Proof:** Since $h$ is $P$-superregular,

$$h \geq Ph \geq P^2h

For uniqueness, suppose that $h = r' + Nf'$ with $r'$ regular. Then $Nf$ and $Nf'$ are finite-valued since $h$ is. Multiplying the equation

$$r + Nf = r' + Nf'$$

through by $I - P$ and applying Lemma 5-9, we obtain

$$f = f'.$$

Hence also $r = r'$.

We return now to the special case of transient chains, where $N = \sum Q^n$. A solution $g$ to an equation is the minimum non-negative solution if whenever $h$ is a non-negative solution, we have $h \geq g \geq 0$.
