This corollary follows from the determination of a potential by its values on the support of its charge. Since an equilibrium potential for $E$ must equal 1 on $E$ and be generated by a charge supported on $E$, two such potentials would agree on $E$ (the support of both charges), and hence must be identical everywhere. The uniqueness of the equilibrium potential is essential for defining the capacity of a set unambiguously.
