In potential theory, a charge distribution is modeled as a signed measure $\mu$ on $\mathbb{R}^3$. The total charge of the distribution is simply $\mu(\mathbb{R}^3)$, the net amount of charge present. This notion is fundamental to defining capacity---the total charge needed to produce a unit potential on a conductor---and appears in the principle of domination, where the total charges of two distributions are compared when one potential dominates another.
