This lemma ensures that the potential function $g(x) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{\mathbb{R}^3} |x-y|^{-1}d\mu(y)$ arising from a charge distribution $\mu$ is well-defined as an almost-everywhere finite function on $\mathbb{R}^3$. The proof reduces to the case where $\mu$ is a measure and uses Fubini's theorem to interchange the order of integration over a sequence of expanding balls $K_n$. Since the inner integral $\int_{K_n}|x-y|^{-1}dx$ is bounded uniformly in $y$, the double integral is finite, implying $g$ is finite a.e. on each $K_n$ and hence on all of $\mathbb{R}^3$.
