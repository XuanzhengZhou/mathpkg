The equilibrium potential is a central concept in potential theory that models the electrostatic equilibrium of a charged conductor. For a closed set $E$ representing a conductor, the equilibrium potential is a potential function that equals 1 everywhere on $E$ and is generated by a charge distribution supported entirely on $E$. Physically, this corresponds to the configuration where charge has distributed itself on the conductor so that the potential is constant---the condition for electrostatic equilibrium.
