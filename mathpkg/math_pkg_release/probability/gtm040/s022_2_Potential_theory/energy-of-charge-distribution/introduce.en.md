The energy of a charge distribution quantifies the total work required to assemble the distribution by bringing infinitesimal amounts of charge from infinity into position one at a time. Mathematically it is defined as the integral of the potential function against the charge distribution itself, yielding a double integral expression $\iint |x-y|^{-1}d\mu(x)d\mu(y)$. This quantity is fundamental to variational principles in potential theory and electrostatics, where equilibrium configurations minimize energy subject to constraints on total charge and support.
