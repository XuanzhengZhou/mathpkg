The $h$-process transformation is a similarity transform $Y \mapsto UYU^{-1}$ on matrices induced by a diagonal matrix $U$ with positive entries. Unlike duality (which swaps row and column vectors), the $h$-process preserves the character of row and column vectors while transforming superregularity, potentials, and the fundamental matrix in a natural way. It is the algebraic counterpart of conditioning a Markov chain to follow paths where a given function $h$ is large, and is central to the study of Martin boundaries.
