Proposition 8-7 is the Markov chain analog of the classical Riesz decomposition theorem for superharmonic functions. It states that any finite-valued solution $h$ of $(I-P)h = f$, with $Nf$ finite-valued, splits uniquely into a regular part $\lim_n P^n h$ and a potential part $Nf$. The proof iterates the relation $h = Ph + f$ to obtain $h = P^n h + (I + P + \cdots + P^{n-1})f$, then passes to the limit via dominated convergence.
