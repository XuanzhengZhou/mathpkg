Proposition 8-10 provides two sufficient conditions for well-behaved potentials. When $\alpha_i \geq k N_{ii}$ (the distinguished measure dominates the diagonal of $N$), every potential is bounded: $|g_i| \leq (1/k) \sum_j \alpha_j |f_j|$. If additionally $\lim_i N_{ij} = 0$, then all potentials vanish at infinity. Both conditions hold for the basic example of Chapter 7 with $\alpha = \beta$, but examples exist where the second condition fails.
