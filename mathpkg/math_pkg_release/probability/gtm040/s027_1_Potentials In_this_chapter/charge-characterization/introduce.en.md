Theorem 8-3 is the cornerstone of transient potential theory for Markov chains. It states that a function $f$ is a (right) charge precisely when it is integrable with respect to the distinguished superregular measure $\alpha$, i.e., $\alpha f < \infty$. In that case, the potential $g$ is simply $Nf$, where $N = \sum_{k=0}^\infty P^k$ is the fundamental matrix. This establishes $N$ as the potential operator and shows the equivalence of the charge notion for $P$ and its $\alpha$-dual $\hat{P}$.
