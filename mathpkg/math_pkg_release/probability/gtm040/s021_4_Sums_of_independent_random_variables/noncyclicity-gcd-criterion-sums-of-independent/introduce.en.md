Lemma 6-35 formulates the noncyclicity (aperiodicity) condition for a sums of independent random variables chain in number-theoretic terms. Let $c$ be the greatest common divisor of the set of step sizes $\{k : p_k > 0\}$. The lemma asserts that the $n$-step transition probability $P_{ii}^{(n)}$ can be positive only when $n$ is a multiple of $c$, and therefore the period of the chain divides $c$. In particular, the chain is noncyclic (aperiodic) if and only if $\gcd\{k : p_k > 0\} = 1$. This lemma is used in both proofs of the convergence theorem (Theorem 6-38) to ensure the noncyclicity hypothesis.
