This theorem completely classifies the recurrence behavior of the reflecting $p$-$q$ random walk on the nonnegative integers. When $p > 1/2$, the walk drifts to the right and is transient. At the critical value $p = 1/2$, the walk is symmetric and null recurrent: the expected return time is infinite. When $p < 1/2$, the walk is biased toward the origin and is positive recurrent, meaning all moments of the return time to $0$ are finite. The classification follows from analyzing the convergence of the moment series: the factor $4pq$ equals $1$ when $p = 1/2$ and is strictly less than $1$ when $p < 1/2$.
