This asymptotic estimate describes the behavior of the $r$-th moment summand for the reflecting random walk as $n \to \infty$. Using Stirling's approximation for the binomial coefficient $\binom{2n-2}{n-1} \sim 4^{n-1}/\sqrt{\pi(n-1)}$ combined with the Catalan factor $1/n$, one obtains that the $n$-th term in the moment series grows like $(4pq)^n \cdot n^{r-3/2}$. The factor $4pq$ controls convergence: when $4pq = 1$ (i.e., $p = q = 1/2$), the term decays as $n^{r-3/2}$, while for $4pq < 1$ it decays exponentially.
