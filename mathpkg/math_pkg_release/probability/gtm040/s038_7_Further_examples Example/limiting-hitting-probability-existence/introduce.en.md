This theorem establishes that for the reflecting random walk, the hitting probabilities from states far away stabilize to well-defined limits. The limit $iH_{-\infty,j}$ captures the probability of hitting $j$ when starting from "negative infinity" (i.e., states arbitrarily far to the left), and similarly for $iH_{+\infty,j}$. The proof reduces the problem to analyzing a ladder process $P^+$ associated with the walk, where the Renewal Theorem from Section 1-67 guarantees convergence of $H_{-n,0}^+$ to $1/\mu^+$, the reciprocal of the mean ladder height. The existence of these limits is the key step in showing that the $\lambda$-matrix exists, confirming the process is normal.
