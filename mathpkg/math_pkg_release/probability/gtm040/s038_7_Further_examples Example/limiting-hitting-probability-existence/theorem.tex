For the reflecting random walk, the limiting hitting probabilities

$$iH_{-\infty,j} = \lim_{k \to -\infty} iH_{kj} \quad \text{and} \quad iH_{+\infty,j} = \lim_{k \to +\infty} iH_{kj}$$

exist for all $i, j$. Consequently, the $i\lambda_j$ limits exist and the process is normal.

Moreover, let $P^+$ be the ladder process with $f_j = p_j^+$ and $u_n = H_{-n,0}^+$. Then $u_0 = 1$ and

$$u_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} f_{n-k} u_k,$$

so by the Renewal Theorem,

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} H_{-n,0}^+ = \frac{1}{\mu^+},$$

where $\mu^+ = \sum j p_j^+$ is the mean ladder height.
