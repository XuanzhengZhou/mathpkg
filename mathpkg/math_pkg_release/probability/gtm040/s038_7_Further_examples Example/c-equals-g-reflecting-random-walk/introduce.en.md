The $C$ matrix is the potential matrix of the reverse chain and relates to $G$ by $C_{ij} = \frac{\alpha_j}{\alpha_i} G_{ji}$, where $\alpha$ is the invariant measure. For the symmetric reflecting random walk, the transition matrix is self-dual ($P = \hat{P}$) and the invariant measure is uniform ($\alpha = 1^T$), so $C_{ij} = G_{ji} = G_{ij}$ in the symmetric case. More broadly, the proposition states that for this class of recurrent processes, $G$ and $C$ exist or fail together and are equal when they exist.
