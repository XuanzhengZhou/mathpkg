In an independent trials process, the transition matrix $P$ is idempotent: $P^n = P$ for all $n \geq 1$. This follows because each row of $P$ is the same probability vector $(p_j)$, so multiplying by $P$ simply reproduces the same row distribution. As a consequence, the $n$-step transition probabilities coincide with the one-step probabilities, meaning the process has no genuine multi-step dynamics beyond the first step.
