The explicit potential formulas for the basic example reveal the fundamental difference between the forward and reverse chains. In the forward chain $P$, each potential $g_i$ decays to zero as $i \to \infty$, expressed as a difference of a global average and a cumulative sum up to $i-1$. In the reverse chain $\hat{P}$, the potential $g_i$ is the global average minus the tail sum beyond $i$, which monotonically increases when the charge is nonnegative.
