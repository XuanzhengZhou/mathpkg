In the null recurrent case, the basic example's long-term behavior is dominated by the fact that the chain spends most of its time arbitrarily far to the right. Consequently, when entering a finite set, the chain must do so from the left (i.e., from smaller states), so the entry distribution $\lambda^E$ is concentrated at the leftmost element of $E$. Dually, the reverse chain enters from the right, concentrating at the rightmost element.
