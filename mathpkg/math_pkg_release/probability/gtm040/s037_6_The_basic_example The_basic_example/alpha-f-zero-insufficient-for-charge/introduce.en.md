For ergodic chains, integrable potentials are characterized by having integral zero ($\alpha g = 0$) with respect to the stationary measure. One might conjecture that a function $f$ with $\alpha f = 0$ and $f = (I-P)h$ for some $h$ is automatically a charge. This proposition refutes that conjecture by constructing an explicit counterexample in the basic example where $\alpha f = 0$ holds but the associated potential series diverges.
