A $P$-regular function, also called a regular function, is a column vector $f$ that is invariant under the action of the transition matrix $P$ of a Markov chain, satisfying $f = Pf$. This means that at each state, the value of the function equals the weighted average of its values at all states reachable in one step, with weights given by the transition probabilities. Regular functions are the discrete analogue of harmonic functions in classical potential theory and play a central role in connecting Markov chains to martingale theory.
