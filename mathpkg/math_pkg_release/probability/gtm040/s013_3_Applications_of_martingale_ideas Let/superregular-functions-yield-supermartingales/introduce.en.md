This corollary extends the fundamental theorem linking $P$-regular functions to martingales. Superregular functions (satisfying $h \geq Ph$) naturally give rise to supermartingales when evaluated along sample paths of the Markov chain, while subregular functions (satisfying $h \leq Ph$) correspond to submartingales. The proof follows identically to the regular case, with the only change being the insertion of the appropriate inequality sign ($\geq$ for superregular/supermartingale, $\leq$ for subregular/submartingale) in the penultimate step of the conditional expectation computation.
