This proposition completes the proof that $k$ is a Choquet capacity by showing that the alternating inequalities follow from sample-path considerations. The key observation is that the hitting time of an intersection is at most the time to enter all sets, so the expected time from the intersection to the reference point is smaller, which translates directly into the capacity inequalities.
