This proposition shows that no matter what scalar $k$ is chosen, the matrix $K + k(1\alpha)$ always has some negative entries in an infinite ergodic chain. In particular, $K$ itself is not a nonnegative-definite matrix. The proof examines the diagonal entries and shows that $(K + k(1\alpha))_{jj} / \alpha_j \to -\infty$ as $j$ varies, using the asymptotic properties of $C$ and $G$ from Proposition 9-63.
