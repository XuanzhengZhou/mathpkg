This proposition establishes that the capacity $k$ is a Choquet capacity: it is monotone and satisfies the alternating inequalities of Choquet. Monotonicity follows from Lemma 9-96: if $A \subset B$, then $k(B) - k(A) \geq 0$ because $^A\nu \geq ^B\nu$. The alternating inequalities follow from Lemma 9-97 via a similar line of reasoning. This Choquet property is fundamental for the potential-theoretic development in subsequent sections.
