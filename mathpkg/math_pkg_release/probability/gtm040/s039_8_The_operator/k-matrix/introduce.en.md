The $K$-matrix is a new potential operator introduced for recurrent Markov chains. It combines properties of both the $C$-matrix and the $G$-matrix, providing a single operator that serves as the potential operator in the recurrent setting. The $K$-matrix depends on the choice of a distinguished reference state $0$, and unlike $C$ and $G$, it satisfies the duality property $\hat{K} = \operatorname{dual} K$. Its main drawback is that some entries may be negative.
