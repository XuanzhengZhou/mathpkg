Since $k(E) = M_{\alpha}[t_0 - t_E]$, we have
$$\sum_{i \in E} \alpha_i M_{i0} \leq k(E) \leq M_{\alpha 0}.$$
Hence as $E_n \to S$, $k(E_n) \to M_{\alpha 0}$, which is finite only in a strong ergodic chain. In the strong ergodic case, the formula $k(E) = M_{\alpha 0} - M_{\alpha E}$ follows directly, and the duality $\hat{M}_{\alpha E} = M_{\alpha E}$ is a consequence.
