This lemma establishes a clean equivalence: the $K$-matrix exists precisely when the chain is normal. Normality ensures that both $C$ and $G$ exist, from which $K$ exists via Lemma 9-81. Conversely, if $K$ exists, then the special entries $K_{i0} = C_{i0}$ and $K_{0j} = G_{0j}$ guarantee that $C_{i0}$ and $G_{0j}$ exist for all $i,j$, which characterizes normality via Theorem 9-26.
