The proof follows from the definition of $K$ and the properties of potentials in normal chains. For a weak charge $\mu$ of finite support, the potential $\nu = \lim_n \mu N^{(n)}$ can be expressed through the defining limit of $K$. The duality property $\hat{K} = \operatorname{dual} K$ (Lemma 9-83) is crucial in establishing that both row and column potentials are represented by $K$. The uniqueness up to the term $k(1\alpha)$ follows because any two matrices that represent all potentials of finite support must differ by a matrix that annihilates all weak charges of finite support.
