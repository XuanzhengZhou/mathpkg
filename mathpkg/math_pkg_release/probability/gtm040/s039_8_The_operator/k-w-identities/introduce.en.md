This proposition establishes two fundamental matrix identities linking the $K$-operator with the taboo probability matrices for finite sets. The first identity $(-K)W^E = B^E - 1\lambda^E$ follows from Lemma 9-37, which shows that each column of $B^E - 1\lambda^E$ is a potential whose charge is the corresponding column of $W^E$. The second identity follows by duality.
