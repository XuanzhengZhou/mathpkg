This theorem provides the Yosida approximation, an explicit formula that recovers the semigroup $\{Q^t\}$ from its infinitesimal generator $A$. The formula expresses $Q^t f$ as a limit of exponential-type sums built from the resolvent $(\lambda I - A)^{-1}$. Crucially, only $A$, $t$, and the identity operator $I$ appear in the formula. In the context of the equivalence between Brownian motion and potential theory, this shows that if $A$ can be defined within potential theory, then each $Q^t$ is uniquely determined, completing the proof that the two theories are identical in a technical sense.
