The infinitesimal generator $A$ of a semigroup $\{Q^t\}$ is defined as the limit of the difference quotient $\frac{1}{t}(Q^t f - f)$ as $t \to 0$, taken on the domain of functions $f$ for which this limit exists uniformly. The generator $A$ captures the instantaneous rate of change of the semigroup at time zero. In the context of the equivalence between Brownian motion and potential theory, $A$ together with the algebraic properties of $Q^t$ completely determines the entire semigroup, making it the key to recovering the transition operators $Q^t$ from potential-theoretic data.
