Kakutani's 1944 observation established the first deep probabilistic interpretation of classical potential-theoretic quantities. It states that for an equilibrium set $E$, the equilibrium potential evaluated at a point $x$ equals the probability that a Brownian motion starting at $x$ will ever reach $E$. This insight revealed that Brownian motion and potential theory are fundamentally two descriptions of the same underlying mathematics, and it opened the door for Doob and Hunt to develop the full probabilistic potential theory.
