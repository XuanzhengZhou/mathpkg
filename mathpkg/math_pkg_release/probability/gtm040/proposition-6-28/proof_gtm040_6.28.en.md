**Proof:** Let $i$ and $j$ be any two states in the chain. Since the chain is recurrent, $i$ and $j$ communicate. Let $d$ be the period associated with state $i$ and let $\bar{d}$ be the period associated with state $j$. Suppose the minimum possible time for the process to go from state $i$ to state $j$ is $s$, and suppose the minimum time for the process to go from $j$ to $i$ is $t$. By Lemma 6-27 let $N$ be large enough so that the process can return to $j$ in $n\bar{d}$ steps for all $n \geq N$.

finite. If, for example, $P$ is a recurrent chain in which $P_{ii} > 0$ for some $i$, then $P$ has period one.
