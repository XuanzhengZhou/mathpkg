A Markov field $\{x_t\}_{t \in \mathbb{Z}}$ on the integers is called homogeneous if its local characteristics are translation-invariant: the conditional distribution at site $m$ given the complement $\bar{m}$ is the same as at site $m+n$ after shifting the configuration backward by $n$. This property encodes the idea that the probabilistic laws governing the field are the same at every lattice site. Homogeneous Markov fields are the natural analogs of stationary Markov processes in the Gibbsian framework on $\mathbb{Z}$.
