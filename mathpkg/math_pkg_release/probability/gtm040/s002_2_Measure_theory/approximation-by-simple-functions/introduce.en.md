This fundamental approximation result shows that every non-negative measurable function can be written as the pointwise monotone limit of simple measurable functions. The construction is explicit: for each $n$, partition the range $[0, n)$ into intervals of length $1/2^n$ and define $s_n(x)$ as the left endpoint of the interval containing $f(x)$. This result justifies defining the Lebesgue integral via approximation by simple functions.
