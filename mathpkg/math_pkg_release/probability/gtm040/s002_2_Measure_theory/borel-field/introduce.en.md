A Borel field (also known as a sigma-algebra or $\sigma$-field) is a field of sets that is additionally closed under countable unions. This closure property is essential for defining measures and performing limiting operations in probability and analysis. The term "Borel field" is used throughout this book synonymously with what is now commonly called a $\sigma$-algebra.
