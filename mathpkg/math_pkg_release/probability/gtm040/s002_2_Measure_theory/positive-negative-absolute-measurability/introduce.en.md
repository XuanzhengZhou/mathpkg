This proposition shows that the basic operations of taking the positive part, negative part, and absolute value all preserve measurability. These operations are essential for decomposing an arbitrary measurable function into the difference of two non-negative measurable functions ($f = f^+ - f^-$), which is the standard approach to defining the Lebesgue integral for signed functions.
