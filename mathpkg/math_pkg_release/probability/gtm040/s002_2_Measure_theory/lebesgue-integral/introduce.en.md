The Lebesgue integral generalizes the Riemann integral by partitioning the range rather than the domain. It is defined in three stages: first for non-negative simple functions via a natural weighted sum, then for non-negative measurable functions as a supremum over simple function approximations, and finally for signed measurable functions by decomposing $f = f^+ - f^-$. The integral may be infinite. The text notes an important application: on a denumerable space with a non-negative row vector $\pi$ as measure, the Lebesgue integral of a column vector $f$ is precisely the matrix product $\pi f$.
