An integrable function is one whose Lebesgue integral is well-defined and finite. This requires that both the positive part $f^+$ and negative part $f^-$ have finite integrals. The integral of $f$ is then the difference of these two integrals. This decomposition ensures that the ambiguity $\infty - \infty$ never arises. The space of integrable functions, denoted $L^1(X, \mathcal{B}, \mu)$, is the fundamental function space of measure theory.
