This proposition shows that the definition of a measurable function is robust: any of the four natural inequality conditions ($<$, $\leq$, $>$, $\geq$) can be used to define measurability. The proof uses countable intersections and complements to show the equivalence, establishing that intervals of the form $[c, d]$, $(c, d]$, $[c, d)$, and $(c, d)$ all have measurable preimages for measurable functions.
