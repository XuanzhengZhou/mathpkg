The Carathéodory Extension Theorem is a cornerstone of measure theory. It states that a sigma-finite measure defined on a field of sets can be uniquely extended to a measure on the generated Borel field (sigma-algebra). The proof constructs an outer measure, identifies the measurable sets via the Carathéodory criterion using symmetric difference, and establishes uniqueness. This theorem justifies the standard construction of Lebesgue measure from its definition on intervals.
