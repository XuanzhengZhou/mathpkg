This proposition establishes that the class of measurable functions is closed under countable suprema, infima, limit superior, and limit inferior. These closure properties are essential for proving the convergence theorems of Lebesgue integration (monotone convergence, dominated convergence). In particular, the pointwise limit of a sequence of measurable functions, when it exists, is measurable.
