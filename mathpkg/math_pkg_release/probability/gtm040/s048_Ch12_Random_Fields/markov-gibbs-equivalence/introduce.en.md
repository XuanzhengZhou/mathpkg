Theorem 12-16 is the central result of the chapter, establishing the equivalence between finite Markov fields and neighbor Gibbs fields. One direction shows that a neighbor Gibbs field is always a Markov field; the converse uses the canonical potential constructed in Theorem 12-12 and proves that the Markov property forces this potential to be a neighbor potential. This result is a version of the Hammersley-Clifford theorem, a cornerstone of the theory of Markov random fields with deep connections to statistical mechanics.
