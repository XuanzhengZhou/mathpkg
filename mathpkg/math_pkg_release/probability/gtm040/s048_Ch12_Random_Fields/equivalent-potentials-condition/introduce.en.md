Corollary 12-15 provides a necessary and sufficient condition for two potentials $U'$ and $U''$ to determine the same finite Gibbs field. The condition is that a certain alternating sum of their differences $\Delta_D = U_D' - U_D''$ vanishes for all nonempty $A$. This follows directly from Proposition 12-14 by comparing canonical potentials. The result underscores that the potential representation of a Gibbs field is not unique — many different potentials can give rise to the same probability distribution — and characterizes exactly when this happens.
