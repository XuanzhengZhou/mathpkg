Let $V'$ and $V''$ be the canonical potentials corresponding to $U'$ and $U''$ respectively. By Theorem 12-12, $U'$ and $U''$ determine the same Gibbs field if and only if their canonical potentials coincide: $V_A' = V_A''$ for all $A$.

Using the formula from Proposition 12-14,

$$V_A'(\iota) - V_A''(\iota) = \sum_{D \subset T} \sum_{B \subset A} (-1)^{|A - B|} \Delta_D(\iota^{B \cap D}),$$

where $\Delta_D = U_D' - U_D''$. The condition $V_A' = V_A''$ for all $A \neq \varnothing$ is equivalent to the vanishing of the stated alternating sum for every nonempty $A$.
