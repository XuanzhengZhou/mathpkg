Lemma 12-11 provides a second M\"{o}bius-type identity used in the construction of the canonical potential. It relates the logarithm of probability ratios for modified configurations to sums of potentials. Given a configuration $\iota$ and its modification $\iota^A$ (which agrees with $\iota$ on $A$ and takes value $0$ elsewhere), the identity expresses $\ln[\mu(\{\iota^B\})/\mu(\{\iota^{B+a}\})]$ as a sum of potential terms. This lemma bridges the gap between the probabilistic data (measures of cylinder sets) and the algebraic data (potential functions).
