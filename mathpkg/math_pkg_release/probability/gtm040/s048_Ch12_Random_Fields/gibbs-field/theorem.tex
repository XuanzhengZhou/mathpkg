A finite random field $\{x_t\}$ is a \textbf{Gibbs field} with potential $U$ if
$$\mu(\{\iota\}) = z^{-1} e^{H_U(\iota)} \quad \iota \in \Omega,$$
where $z = \sum_{\iota \in \Omega} \exp\{H_U(\iota)\}$ is the partition function. If $U$ is a neighbor potential, then $\{x_t\}$ is called a \textbf{neighbor Gibbs field}, and $H_U = \sum_{C \in \mathcal{C}} U_C$ where $\mathcal{C}$ is the set of cliques.
