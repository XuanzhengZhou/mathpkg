A Gibbs field is a finite random field whose probability distribution is given in exponential form $\mu(\{\iota\}) = z^{-1} e^{H_U(\iota)}$, where the energy $H_U$ is a sum of potential functions $U_C$ over cliques $C$. The partition function $z$ normalizes the distribution. This formulation originates from statistical mechanics, where the potentials represent interactions between particles. When the potential $U$ is a neighbor potential (vanishing on non-cliques), the field is called a neighbor Gibbs field. The distinction between "potential" and "energy" in this chapter differs from their usage in earlier chapters on Markov chain potential theory.
