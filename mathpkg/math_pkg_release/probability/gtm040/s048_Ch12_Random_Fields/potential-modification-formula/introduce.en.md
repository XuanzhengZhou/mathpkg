Proposition 12-14 gives an explicit formula expressing the canonical potential $V_A$ in terms of an arbitrary potential $U$. The formula involves a double sum that simplifies dramatically: only terms where $A \subset D$ survive. This proposition is used in the proof of Corollary 12-15, which characterizes when two different potentials produce the same Gibbs field. It also plays a role in the proof of the Markov-Gibbs equivalence (Theorem 12-16).
