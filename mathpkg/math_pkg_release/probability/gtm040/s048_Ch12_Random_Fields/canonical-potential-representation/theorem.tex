Let $\{x_t\}$ be a finite random field with local characteristics $\{\mu_a^\Lambda\}$. Then $\{x_t\}$ is a Gibbs field with canonical potential $V$ defined by $V_\emptyset = 0$, and for $A \neq \emptyset$,
$$V_A(\iota) = \sum_{B \subset A} (-1)^{|A-B|} \ln \mu(\{\iota^B\}) = \sum_{B \subset A} (-1)^{|A-B|} \ln \mu_a^T(\{\iota^B\})$$
for any fixed $a \in A$. Moreover, $V$ is the unique normalized potential for $\{x_t\}$ (normalized means $V_A(\iota) = 0$ whenever $i_a = 0$ for some $a \in A$).
