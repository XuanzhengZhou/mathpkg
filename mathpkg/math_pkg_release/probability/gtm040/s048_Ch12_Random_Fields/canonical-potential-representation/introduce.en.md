Theorem 12-12 is the foundational representation theorem for finite random fields. It states that every finite random field admits a representation as a Gibbs field, and that among all possible potentials there is a unique normalized canonical potential $V$. The canonical potential is constructed explicitly via a M\"{o}bius inversion formula applied to the logarithms of cylinder probabilities. Normalization means $V_A(\iota) = 0$ whenever any coordinate of $\iota$ on $A$ equals $0$. This theorem is the starting point for the equivalence between Markov fields and Gibbs fields.
