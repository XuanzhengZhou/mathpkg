A random field generalizes the notion of a stochastic process from a linearly ordered time set $\mathbb{N}$ to an arbitrary countable parameter set $T$. Instead of a sequence of random variables indexed by time, one considers a probability measure on the space $\Omega = S^T$ of all assignments of states to sites. The positivity condition on cylinder probabilities ensures that all conditional probabilities encountered in the theory are well-defined. This definition provides the foundational framework for the study of Markov fields and Gibbs fields.
