**Proof:** Potentials transform into potentials; hence $g^* = 1$ is a potential. Since 1 is then of the form $N^*f^*$, $\bar{P}^*$ is absorbing. The absorbing state $a$ can be reached only from a state $i$ such that $(P^*1)_i < 1$; for such a state $i$,

$$0 < \left[(I - P^*)\right]_i = \left[(I - P^*)\right]_i = f_i^*.$$

Hence $f_i > 0$ and $i$ must be in $E$. Thus the $P^*$-process with probability one reaches $E$ from all states, and $(B^E)^*1 = 1$.

What underlies Proposition 8-14 is this: The $h$-process tends to follow paths along which $h$ is large. But since potentials tend to zero on the average $(P^n h \rightarrow 0$ for a potential), if $h$ is a potential, then the paths in the $h$-process disappear. See Chapter 10 for details.
