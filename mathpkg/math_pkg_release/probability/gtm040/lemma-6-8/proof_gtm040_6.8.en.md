**Proof:** We have $L^{(n)}1 = 1$ or $\sum_j L_{ij}^{(n)} = 1$ for every $n$. Since the limit of a finite sum is the sum of the limits,

$$(L1)_i = \sum_j L_{ij} = \sum_j \lim_{n} L_{ij}^{(n)} = \lim_{n} \sum_j L_{ij}^{(n)} = 1.$$
