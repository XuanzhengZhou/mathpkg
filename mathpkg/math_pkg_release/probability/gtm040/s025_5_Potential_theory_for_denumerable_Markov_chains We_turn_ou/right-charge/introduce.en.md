A right charge in the potential theory of denumerable Markov chains is a column vector $f$ whose $\alpha$-weighted sum $\alpha f$ is well-defined and finite, and for which the partial sums $(I + P + \cdots + P^n)f$ converge to a finite-valued column vector $g$. The limit $g$ is called the potential function of the charge $f$. This definition complements that of a left charge (Definition 7-6) by reversing the roles — here the transition operator $P$ acts on column vectors (functions) rather than on row vectors (measures), and the superregular measure $\alpha$ appears as a weighting in the finiteness condition.
