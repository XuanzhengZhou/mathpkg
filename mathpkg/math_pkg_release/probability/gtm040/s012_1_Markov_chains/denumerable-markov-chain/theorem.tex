If a denumerable Markov process has the property that for any $m$ and $n$ and for any $c_n, c_{n+1}$ such that $\Pr[x_m = c_n] > 0$ and $\Pr[x_n = c_n] > 0$,
$$\Pr[x_{n+1} = c_{n+1} \mid x_n = c_n] = \Pr[x_{m+1} = c_{n+1} \mid x_m = c_n]$$
holds, then the process is called a \textbf{denumerable Markov chain}. This condition is called the \textbf{Markov chain property} (time-homogeneity).
