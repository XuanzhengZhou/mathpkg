This definition introduces entrywise convergence for sequences of matrices with common index sets. A sequence of matrices converges to a limit matrix if each entry converges individually in the extended real numbers. This pointwise notion of convergence (as opposed to norm convergence) is particularly natural in the context of denumerable Markov chains, where entrywise limits of the $n$-step transition matrices $P^n$ are of central importance.
