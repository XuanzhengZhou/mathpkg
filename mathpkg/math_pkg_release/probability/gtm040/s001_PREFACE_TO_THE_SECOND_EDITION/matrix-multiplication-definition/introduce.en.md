Matrix multiplication for denumerable (finite or countably infinite) index sets generalizes the familiar row-by-column product. The product $C = AB$ of two matrices is defined entrywise as the sum over the common index set of the products of corresponding entries. The definition requires the sums $\sum_{m \in M} A_{km} B_{mn}$ to be well defined (as extended real numbers) for every pair of indices $(k,n)$. This definition underpins the entire matrix-theoretic approach to denumerable Markov chains used throughout the book.
