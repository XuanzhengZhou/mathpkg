Let $K$, $M$, and $N$ be index sets, and let $K' \subseteq K$, $M' \subseteq M$, $N' \subseteq N$ be non-empty subsets with complements $\tilde{K}'$, $\tilde{M}'$, $\tilde{N}'$. Let $A$ be defined on $K \times M$ and $B$ on $M \times N$ such that $AB = C$ is well defined. Partition $A$ and $B$ into blocks according to these subsets:
$$A = \begin{pmatrix} A_1 & A_2 \\ A_3 & A_4 \end{pmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} B_1 & B_2 \\ B_3 & B_4 \end{pmatrix}, \quad C = \begin{pmatrix} C_1 & C_2 \\ C_3 & C_4 \end{pmatrix}.$$
Then
\begin{align*}
A_1 B_1 + A_2 B_3 &= C_1, \\
A_1 B_2 + A_2 B_4 &= C_2, \\
A_3 B_1 + A_4 B_3 &= C_3, \\
A_3 B_2 + A_4 B_4 &= C_4.
\end{align*}
