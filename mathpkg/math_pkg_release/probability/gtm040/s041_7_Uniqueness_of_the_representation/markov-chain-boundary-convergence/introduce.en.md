For any nonnegative normalized regular function $h$, the limit $\lim_{n \to \infty} h(x_n)$ exists almost surely along sample paths, and equals the Radon--Nikodym derivative $f(x_v)$ evaluated at the exit point. This is the Markov chain analog of Fatou's theorem for harmonic functions on the unit disk. The proof reduces the general case to the bounded case (Lemma 10-42) by considering an auxiliary regular function $k = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2}h$ whose $k$-process has full Martin boundary, then relating the limits of $h(x_n)/k(x_n)$ to a Radon--Nikodym derivative for the $k$-process.
