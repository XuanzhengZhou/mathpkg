This theorem completes the Martin boundary theory by establishing that every nonnegative superregular function $h$ admits a unique integral representation $h_i = \int_{\bar{S}} K(i, x) d\nu(x)$ over the exit space $\bar{S} = S \cup B_e$. The proof handles two cases: if $\pi h = 0$ then $h = 0$ trivially (since $\pi N$ is everywhere positive); if $\pi h > 0$, normalize to $\pi h = 1$, then uniqueness follows from Theorem 10-40 and existence follows from Theorem 10-26 together with the fact that $\mu^h(\bar{S}) = 1$, which is proved via Lemma 10-32.
