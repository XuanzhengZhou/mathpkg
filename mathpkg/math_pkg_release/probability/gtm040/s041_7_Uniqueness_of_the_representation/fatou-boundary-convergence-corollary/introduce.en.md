This corollary reformulates Theorem 10-43 in terms of the $K(\cdot, x)$-process: for $\mu$-almost every boundary point $x$ where $K(\cdot, x)$ is normalized, the $K(\cdot, x)$-process is absorbed at $x$ (by Proposition 10-35), and along almost every sample path, $h(x_n)$ converges to $f(x)$, the Radon--Nikodym derivative of the absolutely continuous part of $\mu^h$. This is the precise Markov chain analog of Fatou's theorem for nontangential convergence of harmonic functions on the unit disk.
