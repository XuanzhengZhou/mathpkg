For a Borel set $C$ in the normalized boundary $S_N$ and a $\mathcal{G}$-measurable event $A$, the joint probability that $\omega \in A$ and the exit point $x_v$ lands in $C$ equals the integral over $C$ of the $K(\cdot, x)$-process probability of $A$ with respect to harmonic measure $\mu$. The proof defines a set function $\sigma(A) = \int_C \Pr^{K(\cdot, x)}[A] d\mu(x)$, verifies complete additivity via the Monotone Convergence Theorem, and shows agreement with $\Pr[\omega \in A \land x_v \in C]$ on cylinder sets, extending to all of $\mathcal{G}$ by Theorem 1-19.
