The exit space $\bar{S}$ (the set of boundary points where the $K(\cdot, x)$-process is concentrated at $x$) is a Borel measurable subset of $S^*$ and has full harmonic measure: $\mu(\bar{S}) = 1$. The proof constructs an intermediate set $T$ as a countable intersection of Borel sets of measure one, shows $T$ is Borel with $\mu(T) = 1$, and then proves the equality $T = \bar{S}$ using Lemma 10-37 and the characterization of $\bar{S}$ via Proposition 10-35.
