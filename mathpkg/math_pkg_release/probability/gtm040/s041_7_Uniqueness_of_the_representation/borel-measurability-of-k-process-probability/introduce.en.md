For a fixed $\mathcal{G}$-measurable set $A$ in the probability space $\Omega$, the probability $\Pr^{K(\cdot, x)}[A]$ computed under the $K(\cdot, x)$-process is a Borel measurable function of the boundary point $x \in S_N$. The proof uses the continuity of $K(k, x)$ on $S^*$ together with a monotone class argument: the measurability holds first for basic cylinder sets, then extends to the generated $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{G}$ via the standard machinery of increasing and decreasing sequences of sets.
