For a normalized bounded regular function $h$, the harmonic measure $\mu^h$ is absolutely continuous with respect to $\mu$, with Radon--Nikodym derivative $f$ supported on the extreme boundary $B_e$. The function $f$ serves as the almost-sure limit of the martingale $\{h(x_n)\}$: with probability one, $h(x_n)$ converges to $f(x_v)$ as $n \to \infty$. The proof uses the integral representation (Theorem 10-41) to express conditional expectations and invokes the martingale convergence theorem on the filtration generated by the state sequence.
