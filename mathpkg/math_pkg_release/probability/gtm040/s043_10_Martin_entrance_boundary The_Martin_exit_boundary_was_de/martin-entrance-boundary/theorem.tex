The Martin entrance boundary $*S$ is the completion of the state space $S$ in the metric $d'$ defined by

$$d'(j,j') = \sum_i w_i (Nf)_{i} |J(j,i) - J(j',i)|,$$

where $J(i,j) = N_{ij}/(Nf)_i$ and the $w_i$ are positive weights such that $\sum w_i N_{ii}$ is finite.

By Fatou's Theorem, $J(x,\cdot)$ is a $P$-superregular measure for each $x \in *S$ and satisfies $J(x,\cdot)f \leq 1$.

A point $x$ of $*S$ is \textbf{extreme} if $J(x,\cdot)$ is minimal and normalized. The set of extreme points is denoted $B^e$. The intersection $B^e \cap S$ is empty.
