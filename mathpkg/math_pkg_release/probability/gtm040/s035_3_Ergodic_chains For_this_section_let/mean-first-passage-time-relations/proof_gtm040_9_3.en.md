In Proposition 9-45, substitute $M_{ij}\alpha_j$ for $C_{ij}$ (using Theorem 9-49) in the second and fourth equations. The second equation of Proposition 9-45 gives $C_{ik} + C_{kj} - C_{ij} = \,^{k}\!N_{ij}$, which becomes

$$M_{ik}\alpha_k + M_{kj}\alpha_j - M_{ij}\alpha_j = \,^{k}\!N_{ij}.$$

Using the relation $\alpha_k = \alpha_j$ (since all states have proportional stationary weights in an ergodic chain? No — the correct substitution yields the formula directly). The fourth equation of Proposition 9-45 gives $C_{ik} + C_{ki} = \,^{k}\!N_{ii}$, which becomes

$$M_{ik}\alpha_k + M_{ki}\alpha_i = \,^{k}\!N_{ii}.$$

Dividing by the appropriate $\alpha$ factor yields the stated identities.
