For existence, $B^E h$ is finite-valued by Corollary 9-56, and we let

$$g = B^E h - (\lambda^E h)1.$$

Then $g$ differs from $h$ by the constant $\lambda^E h$ on $E$. Since $\alpha g = 0$, $g$ is a potential by Theorem 9-53. Moreover,

$$f = (I - P)g = (I - P)B^E h$$

has support in $E$ (since $B^E$ sweeps the charge onto $E$), so $g$ has support in $E$. Uniqueness follows from the maximum principle for potentials.
