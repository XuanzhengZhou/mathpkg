This corollary establishes a fundamental distinction between infinite and finite noncyclic ergodic chains: in the infinite case, the potential matrix $C$ and the Green matrix $G$ are always different, whereas in the finite case they can coincide. The proof follows immediately from Proposition 9-63, which shows that $(C_{ij} - G_{ij})/\alpha_j \to \infty$. The example of the two-state symmetric chain demonstrates that $C = G$ is possible in finite chains.
