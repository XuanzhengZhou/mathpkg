This proposition describes the asymptotic behavior of mean first passage times in infinite noncyclic ergodic chains. As the target state $j$ tends to infinity, the return time $M_{ji}$ becomes negligible compared to the forward time $M_{ij}$, the forward time itself diverges to infinity, and the normalized difference $(C_{ij} - G_{ij})/\alpha_j$ also diverges. These results rely on the symmetrized time identities of Lemma 9-62 and the vanishing of small set measures from Proposition 9-60.
