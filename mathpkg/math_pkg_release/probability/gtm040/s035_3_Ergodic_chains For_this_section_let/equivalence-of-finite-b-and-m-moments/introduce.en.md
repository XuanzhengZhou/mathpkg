This proposition establishes the equivalence between the finiteness of the $r$-th moment vector of return times $b^{(r)}$ and the finiteness of the $r$-th moment matrix $M^{(r)}$ of first passage times. The proof uses a stopping time argument involving the minimum of hitting times to relate these quantities, together with the strong Markov property (Theorem 4-11). This equivalence is essential for the definition and study of ergodic degree.
