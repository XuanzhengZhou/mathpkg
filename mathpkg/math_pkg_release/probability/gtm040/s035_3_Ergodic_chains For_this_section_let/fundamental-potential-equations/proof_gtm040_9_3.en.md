A row of $I - P$ is a charge whose potential is the corresponding row of $I - A$. Since

$$0 \leq (PC)_{ij} = \sum_k P_{ik}C_{kj} = \alpha_j \sum_k P_{ik}M_{kj} = \alpha_j(\bar{M}_{ij} - 1) \quad \text{by Proposition 6-41} < \infty,$$

$(I - P)(-C) = PC - C$ is finite-valued. Hence $(I - P)(-C) = I - A$ by Theorem 9-50. The second result is dual.
