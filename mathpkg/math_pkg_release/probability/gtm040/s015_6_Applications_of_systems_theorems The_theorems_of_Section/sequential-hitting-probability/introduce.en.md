Proposition 4-16 shows that the probability of visiting state $j$ and then later visiting state $k$ factorizes as the product $H_{ij} H_{jk}$. This is a direct consequence of the strong Markov property applied at the hitting time $t_j$: once the chain reaches $j$, it starts afresh, and the probability of subsequently reaching $k$ is simply $H_{jk}$, independent of the past.
