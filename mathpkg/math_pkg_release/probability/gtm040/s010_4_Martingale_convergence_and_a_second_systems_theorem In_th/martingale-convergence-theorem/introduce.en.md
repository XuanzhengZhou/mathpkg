The Martingale Convergence Theorem (also called Doob's Submartingale Convergence Theorem) states that any $L^1$-bounded submartingale converges almost everywhere to a finite limit. The $L^1$-bounded condition means there is a uniform bound $K$ on the expected absolute values $M[|f_n|]$. The proof uses the Upcrossing Lemma to rule out both divergence to infinity and infinite oscillation on sets of positive measure. Since every martingale is also a submartingale, the theorem applies to $L^1$-bounded martingales as well.
