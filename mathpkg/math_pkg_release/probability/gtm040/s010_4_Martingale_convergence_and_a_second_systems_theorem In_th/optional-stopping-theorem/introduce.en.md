The Optional Stopping Theorem (also called the Second Systems Theorem) gives conditions under which the expected value of a martingale at a stopping time equals its initial expected value. This formalizes the idea that a fair game remains fair under any stopping strategy, provided the stopping time satisfies natural integrability conditions. The theorem requires that $f_t$ be integrable and that the tail contribution $\int_{\{t > n\}} f_n \, d\mu$ vanishes as $n 	o \infty$, ensuring that the process does not accumulate mass on the event that it never stops.
