This proposition states that stopping a martingale at a random time $t$ (by freezing the process at its value when $t$ occurs) produces another martingale adapted to the same filtration. The stopped process $\hat{f}_n$ equals $f_n$ until time $t$ and then remains constant thereafter. This result is fundamental for applications: it shows that the martingale property is preserved under stopping, which is used to prove that certain random times are indeed stopping times, and to analyze processes that are stopped upon hitting certain sets.
