The Upcrossing Lemma provides an upper bound on the expected number of upcrossings of any interval $[r, s]$ by a submartingale up to time $n$. The bound depends only on the expected absolute value of the submartingale at time $n$ and the constants $r$ and $s$, and does not diverge with $n$ when the submartingale is $L^1$-bounded. This is the crucial estimate that prevents a submartingale from oscillating infinitely often on a set of positive measure, leading directly to the Martingale Convergence Theorem.
