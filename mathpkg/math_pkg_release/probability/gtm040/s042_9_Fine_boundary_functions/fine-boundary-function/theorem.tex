Let $h \geq 0$ be a $\pi$-integrable regular function, and let $f$ be the function in the results of Section~8 (Theorems~10-42, 10-43, and Corollary~10-44). Since $f$ is equal to zero on $S$, we may think of $f$ as a function on $B_e$. When $f$ is so restricted to $B_e$, it is called the \textbf{fine boundary function} of $h$.
