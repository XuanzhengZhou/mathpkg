Lemma 10-46 provides a clean characterization of minimal non-negative normalized regular functions: $h$ is minimal if and only if the $h$-process admits no non-constant bounded regular functions on its support $S^h$. This criterion is a key tool for checking minimality and for establishing the zero-one law that underpins the fine topology. A bounded regular function for $P^h$ can be transformed into a bounded regular function for $P$ by multiplying by $h$, connecting the spectral properties of $P^h$ to the minimality of $h$.
