Set $i$ and $j$ both equal to $k$ in Corollary 9-27. Then
$${}^0N_{kk} - {}^0\nu_k = -[G_{kk} - G_{k0}(\alpha_k/\alpha_0)] = G_{k0}(\alpha_k/\alpha_0),$$
since $G_{kk} = 0$ for all $k$. Since ${}^0\nu_k = G_{0k}$, we have ${}^0N_{kk} = G_{k0}(\alpha_k/\alpha_0) + G_{0k}$. Thus
$$\sum_k {}^0N_{kk} f_k = \sum_k \left( \frac{\alpha_k G_{k0}}{\alpha_0} + G_{0k} \right) f_k = (\hat{C}f)_0 + (Gf)_0.$$

Since $f$ is a weak charge, the left-hand side is finite, hence the right-hand side is finite. Therefore $\sum_k {}^0N_{kk} f_k$ is finite. By Proposition 9-21, $f$ is a charge with bounded potential; by Corollary 9-28, $g = -Gf$.

The dual statement follows by applying the same argument to the dual chain $\hat{P}$, noting that the dual of a weak charge for $P$ is a weak charge for $\hat{P}$.
