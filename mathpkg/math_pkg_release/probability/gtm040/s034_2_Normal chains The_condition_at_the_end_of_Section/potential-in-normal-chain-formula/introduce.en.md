In a normal chain $P$, any function $f$ of zero total charge ($\alpha f = 0$) whose $f$-weighted sum of the diagonal entries of ${}^0N$ is finite has a unique potential $g$. The potential is bounded and admits the explicit representation $g = [{}^0N - 1\,{}^0\nu]f$, where ${}^0N$ is the fundamental matrix, $1$ is the column vector of all ones, and ${}^0\nu$ is the row vector of long-run mean occupation numbers. This result is the foundation for much of the potential theory of normal chains.
