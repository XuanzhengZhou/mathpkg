Since $P$ is normal, $P^n {}^0N \to 1\,{}^0\nu$. Furthermore,
$$\sum_k (P^n)_{ik} {}^0N_{kj} \leq \sum_k (P^n)_{ik} {}^0N_{jj} = {}^0N_{jj},$$
and ${}^0N f^+$ and ${}^0N f^-$ are both finite-valued. Thus we have dominated convergence in Proposition 9-13, which gives
$$g = {}^0N f - \lim_n P^n {}^0N f.$$
Substituting $\lim_n P^n {}^0N = 1\,{}^0\nu$ yields
$$g = {}^0N f - 1\,{}^0\nu f = [{}^0N - 1\,{}^0\nu]f.$$
By Theorem 9-15, $|g_i - g_0|$ is bounded, hence $g$ is bounded since $g_0$ is finite.
