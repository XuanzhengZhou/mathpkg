Lemma 9-23 establishes a practical criterion for determining whether a chain is normal. It states that one only needs to check two-point functions and signed measures: if every function supported on $\{0,j\}$ with total charge zero is a charge, and dually every signed measure supported on $\{0,j\}$ with total mass zero is a charge, then the chain is normal. The lemma also shows that if a single such function is a charge, then ${}^0\lambda_j$ exists and its potential at $0$ equals ${}^0\nu_j$.
