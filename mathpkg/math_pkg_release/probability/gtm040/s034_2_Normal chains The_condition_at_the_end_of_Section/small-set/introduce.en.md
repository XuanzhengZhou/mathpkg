A set of states $E$ in a Markov chain is called a small set if the limiting matrix $\lambda^E = \lim_n P^n B^E$ exists and its rows are probability vectors (row sum equals $1$). The concept of a small set is the recurrent analog of an equilibrium set in transient chains. In a normal chain, all finite sets are small (Proposition 9-38), providing a rich supply of examples.
