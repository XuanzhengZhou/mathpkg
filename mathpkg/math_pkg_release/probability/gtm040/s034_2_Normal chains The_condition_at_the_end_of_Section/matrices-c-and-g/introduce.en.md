Definition 9-24 introduces the matrices $C$ and $G$, which play a central role in the potential theory of recurrent Markov chains. The entry $C_{ij}$ is defined as the limiting difference between the expected number of visits to $j$ starting from $j$ and starting from $i$, while $G_{ij}$ is a weighted analog. According to Lemma 9-2, these quantities are bounded and non-negative wherever they exist, with $C_{ii} = G_{ii} = 0$. The matrices are related by duality: $\hat{G}_{ij} = \alpha_j C_{ji} / \alpha_i$.
