If the indicated limits exist, then
$${}^i\nu_j = \lim_n \sum_k (P^n)_{mk} {}^iN_{kj}$$
and
$${}^i\lambda_j = \frac{{}^i\nu_j}{{}^iN_{jj}} = \lim_n \sum_k (P^n)_{mk} {}^iH_{kj}.$$
Notation independent of $m$ is justified by Proposition 9-18. Furthermore, $0 \leq {}^i\lambda_j \leq 1$, and ${}^i\lambda_j$ is the probability that $j$ is entered in the long run before $i$, while ${}^i\nu_j$ is the mean number of times in $j$, in the long run, before reaching $i$.
