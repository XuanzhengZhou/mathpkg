Proposition 9-43 states the Principle of Balayage for total-charge-zero potentials in recurrent chains. For a small ergodic set $E$, any bounded function $x$ on $E$ can be uniquely extended to a potential $g$ with support in $E$ that differs from $x$ on $E$ by a constant. The explicit formula $g = B^E x - (\lambda^E x)1$ shows that the "balayage" of $x$ onto $E$ is obtained by applying the hitting matrix $B^E$ and subtracting the $\lambda^E$-average. This is the recurrent analog of sweeping a measure onto a set.
