$(f_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$ is a stochastic process charge with potential $(g_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$ if $\sum_n M[|f_n|] < \infty$

Thus $g$ is the potential of $f$ if and only if the two conditions of Definition 8-76 are fulfilled by $(f_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$ and $(g_n, \mathcal{R}_n)$.

We give two applications of the correspondence. The first is a decomposition of non-negative supermartingales.
