A neighbor potential $U$ on $\mathbb{Z}$ is called homogeneous if its singleton and nearest-neighbor pair interactions depend only on the state values at the involved sites, not on their absolute positions. Specifically, $U_{\{m\}}(\iota)$ reduces to a function $u_j$ of the state $j$ at site $m$, and $U_{\{m,m+1\}}(\iota)$ reduces to a function $u_{jk}$ of the states $j,k$ at sites $m,m+1$. This translation-invariance of the potential is the natural energy-side counterpart of the probabilistic homogeneity of the associated Gibbs field.
