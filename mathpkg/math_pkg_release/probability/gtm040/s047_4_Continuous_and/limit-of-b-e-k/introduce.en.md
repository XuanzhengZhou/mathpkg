Proposition 11-9 establishes that for a continuous column vector $h$, the sequence of taboo probability operators $B^{E_k}(x,\cdot)h$ converges pointwise to the integral of $h$ against the boundary measure $\beta^x$. The measure $\beta^x$ is obtained from Proposition 11-2 applied to the row vector $\nu = {}_0N(x,\cdot)$. This result is a crucial bridge between the algebraic taboo probability formalism and the analytic boundary theory of denumerable Markov chains.
