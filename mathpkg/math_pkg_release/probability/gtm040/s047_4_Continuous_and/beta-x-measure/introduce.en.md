The boundary measure $\beta^x$ on $*S$ is obtained by applying Proposition 11-2 to the row vector ${}_0N(x,\cdot)$, which is the potential kernel evaluated at a point $x$ in the Martin compactification. This measure is central to Section 4 as it defines the $T$-transform: $Th(x) = \int_{*S} h(y)\,d\beta^x(y)$. For $x \in S \cup B^e$, the measure $\beta^x$ concentrates its mass at $x$, so $Th(x) = h(x)$ for such $x$.
