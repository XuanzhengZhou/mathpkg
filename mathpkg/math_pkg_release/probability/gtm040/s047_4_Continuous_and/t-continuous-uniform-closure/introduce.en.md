Proposition 11-12 provides the definitive characterization of $T$-continuous functions: they form exactly the uniform closure of the set of elementary continuous functions (finite linear combinations of columns of $B^E$ for finite $E$). The forward inclusion uses Lemma 11-11 together with the fact that $B^{E_k}(x,j)$ vanishes outside $E_k$ to upgrade strong closure to uniform closure. The reverse inclusion follows from Lemma 11-10 (elementary continuous functions are $T$-continuous) and the fact that $T$ has norm at most one, so the uniform limit of $T$-continuous functions remains $T$-continuous.
