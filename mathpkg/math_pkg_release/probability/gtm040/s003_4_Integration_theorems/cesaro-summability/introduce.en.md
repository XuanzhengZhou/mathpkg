Cesaro summability is a method of assigning limits to divergent sequences by averaging. This proposition shows Cesaro summability is regular: it agrees with ordinary convergence. The converse is false (e.g., the sequence 0,1,0,1,... has Cesaro limit 1/2 but does not converge).
