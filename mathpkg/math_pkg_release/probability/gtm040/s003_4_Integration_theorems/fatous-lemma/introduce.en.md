Fatou's Lemma provides a fundamental inequality relating the integral of a pointwise limit inferior to the limit inferior of the integrals. It holds for any sequence of non-negative measurable functions without requiring convergence or domination conditions. The proof defines $g_n(x) = \inf_{k \geq n} f_k(x)$, which increases monotonically to $\liminf f_n$, and applies the Monotone Convergence Theorem.
