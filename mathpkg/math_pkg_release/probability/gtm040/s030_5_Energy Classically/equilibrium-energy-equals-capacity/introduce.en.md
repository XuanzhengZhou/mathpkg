This simple but fundamental lemma shows that the energy of the equilibrium potential $h^E$ on a set $E$ is exactly the capacity $C(E)$. Since the equilibrium potential equals $1$ on $E$, its energy $\mathbf{I}(h^E) = \eta^E h^E = \eta^E 1$ is simply the total equilibrium charge, which by definition is the capacity.
