Since $h^E$ is the equilibrium potential on $E$, its dual $\eta^E$ is the equilibrium charge. We have
$$\mathbf{I}(h^E) = \eta^E h^E = \eta^E 1 = C(E),$$
because $h^E$ equals $1$ on $E$ (by definition of equilibrium potential) and the equilibrium charge evaluated on the constant function $1$ gives the capacity $C(E) = \sum_i \eta^E_i$.
