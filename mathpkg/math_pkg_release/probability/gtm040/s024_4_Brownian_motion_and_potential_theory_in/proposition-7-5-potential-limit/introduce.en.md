Proposition 7-5 characterizes the limiting behavior of the time-integrated Brownian motion operator in dimensions 1 and 2. When the signed measure $\mu$ has nonzero total mass, the limit diverges to $+\infty$ or $-\infty$ almost everywhere according to the sign of $\mu(\mathbb{R}^n)$. This stands in contrast to the case $\mu(\mathbb{R}^n) = 0$, where the limit converges to the Newtonian potential $g(x)$. The proof for $n = 1$ relies on integration by parts of the heat kernel integral, the Dominated Convergence Theorem, and an estimate showing that $\sqrt{T}(e^{-|z|^2/2T} - 1)$ is bounded by $k|z|$.
