In $\mathbb{R}^n$ for $n = 1$ or $2$, let $\mu = \mu^+ - \mu^-$ be the difference of two finite measures, and suppose that
$$\int_{\mathbb{R}^1} |x - y| \, d\mu^+(y) < \infty \quad \text{and} \quad \int_{\mathbb{R}^1} |x - y| \, d\mu^-(y) < \infty \quad \text{a.e.\ if } n = 1$$
or
$$\int_{\mathbb{R}^2} |\log |x - y|| \, d\mu^+(y) < \infty \quad \text{and} \quad \int_{\mathbb{R}^2} |\log |x - y|| \, d\mu^-(y) < \infty \quad \text{a.e.\ if } n = 2.$$
If $\mu(\mathbb{R}^n) \neq 0$, then
$$\lim_{T \to \infty} \int_0^T \frac{1}{(2\pi t)^{n/2}} \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} e^{-|x - y|^2/2t} \, d\mu(y) \, dt = +\infty \quad \text{or} \quad -\infty \quad \text{a.e.}$$
according as $\mu(\mathbb{R}^n) > 0$ or $\mu(\mathbb{R}^n) < 0$.
