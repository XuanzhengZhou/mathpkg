The Newtonian potential $g(x)$ of a signed measure $\mu$ on $\mathbb{R}^n$ is the convolution of $\mu$ with the fundamental solution of the Laplacian, given piecewise by dimension: the Riesz kernel $c_n |x-y|^{-(n-2)}$ for $n \geq 3$, the logarithmic kernel for $n = 2$, and the negative absolute value kernel for $n = 1$. The constant $c_n$ involves the Gamma function and is chosen so that $-\Delta g = \mu$ in the sense of distributions. The behavior of the kernel at infinity differs sharply between dimensions: for $n \geq 3$ it decays to zero, while for $n = 1, 2$ it grows without bound, leading to fundamental differences in potential theory between low and high dimensions.
