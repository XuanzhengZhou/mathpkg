This example demonstrates that a boundary point can have an associated Martin kernel that is regular, normalized (integrates to 1 against the starting measure $\pi$), yet fails to be an extreme point of the boundary. The example constructs a chain on three types of states $\{a_i, a'_i, b_i\}$ where the $b$-row can be reached from either $a$-row. The boundary point $b_\infty$ corresponds to a convex combination of $a_\infty$ and $a'_\infty$, illustrating that not every boundary point need be extreme.
