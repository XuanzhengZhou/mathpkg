The $p$-$q$ random walk is the classical asymmetric nearest-neighbor random walk on $\mathbb{Z}$. With drift to the right ($p > q$), the process is transient and its exit boundary consists of two extreme points corresponding to $+\infty$ and $-\infty$. The functions $1$ and $(q/p)^i$ are both regular and minimal. This example provides a concrete illustration of how the choice of starting measure $\pi$ can affect which boundary points are extreme.
