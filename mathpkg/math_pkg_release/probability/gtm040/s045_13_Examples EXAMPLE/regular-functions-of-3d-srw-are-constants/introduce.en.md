For transient Markov chains, regular functions play a central role in the Martin boundary representation of non-negative superregular functions. This theorem shows that the symmetric random walk in three dimensions has the strongest possible rigidity: every non-negative regular function must be constant. The proof uses the methods of transient boundary theory and requires a delicate asymptotic estimate of the potential kernel $N_{0j}$, which is obtained via Fourier analysis.
