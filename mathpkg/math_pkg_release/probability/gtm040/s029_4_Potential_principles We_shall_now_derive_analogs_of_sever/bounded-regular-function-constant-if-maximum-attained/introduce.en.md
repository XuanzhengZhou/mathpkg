This corollary strengthens the maximum principle: if a bounded regular function off $E$ attains its maximum in the transient region $\tilde{E}$, the function must be globally constant. This follows from the uniqueness of the Dirichlet solution and the communication structure of the absorbing chain. It is the precise discrete analog of the strong maximum principle for harmonic functions.
