The Principle of Balayage (sweeping out) constructs, for any pure potential $g$ and any set $E$, a unique pure potential $\bar{g}$ supported on $E$ that agrees with $g$ on $E$ and is dominated by $g$ everywhere. The balayage potential is given by $\bar{g} = B^E g$. This operation sweeps the charge of $g$ onto the set $E$ while reducing the total charge. It is the Markov chain analog of sweeping out a measure onto a set in classical potential theory.
