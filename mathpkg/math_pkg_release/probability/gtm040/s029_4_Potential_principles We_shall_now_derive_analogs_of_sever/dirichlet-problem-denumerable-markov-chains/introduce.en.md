This theorem solves the Dirichlet problem for denumerable Markov chains. Given an absorbing chain $E^P$ (where states in $E$ are made absorbing) and bounded boundary data $h_E$ on $E$, there exists a unique bounded extension $\bar{h}$ that is regular (harmonic) on the complement $\tilde{E}$. The solution is given explicitly by the operator $B^E$, which represents the expected occupation measure before hitting $E$. This is the discrete analog of the classical Dirichlet problem for the Laplace equation.
