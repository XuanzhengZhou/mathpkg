Apply Proposition 8-28. The quantity $[(P^E)^n 1]_i$ is the probability starting in $i \in E$ of returning to $E$ at least $n$ times. Thus $(P^E)^n 1 \to 0$ is necessary and sufficient for being in $E$ only finitely often a.e. for any starting distribution. Moreover, $(I - P^E)1 = e^E_E$ and $\alpha_E e^E_E$ equals the capacity of $E$ in $P^E$.
