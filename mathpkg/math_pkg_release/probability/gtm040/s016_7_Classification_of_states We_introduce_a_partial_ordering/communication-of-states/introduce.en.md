Two states $i$ and $j$ are said to communicate, written $i \sim j$, if $R(i,j)$ and $R(j,i)$ both hold, meaning each can be reached from the other with positive probability. The communication relation $\sim$ is an equivalence relation (reflexive, symmetric, and transitive). It partitions the state space $S$ into equivalence classes within the partial ordering $R$, with movement between classes possible only upward through the ordering.
