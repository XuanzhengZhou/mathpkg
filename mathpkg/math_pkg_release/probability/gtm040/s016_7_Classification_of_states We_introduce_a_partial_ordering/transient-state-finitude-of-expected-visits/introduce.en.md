Corollary 4-21 extends the finiteness of expected visits from a transient state to itself ($N_{jj}$) to all pairs of states ($N_{ij}$). For any starting state $i$, the expected number of visits to a transient state $j$ is bounded by $N_{jj}$ and is given by the product $H_{ij} N_{jj}$ of the hitting probability and the self-visit count. This follows directly from the first-entrance decomposition in Proposition 4-15.
