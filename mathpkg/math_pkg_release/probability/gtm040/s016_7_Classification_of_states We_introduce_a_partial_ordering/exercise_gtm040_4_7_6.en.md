If the symmetric random walk in 3 dimensions is started at the origin, the probability of being at the origin after $n$ steps is 0 if $n$ is odd and is of the order of magnitude of $n^{-3/2}$ for $n$ even. Prove that the probability of ever returning to the origin is less than 1.
