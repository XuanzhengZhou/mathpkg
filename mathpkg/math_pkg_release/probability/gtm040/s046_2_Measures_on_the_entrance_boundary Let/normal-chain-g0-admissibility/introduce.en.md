Lemma 11-6 verifies that for a normal chain $P$, the expected number of visits starting from $0$ (the row $G_{0\cdot}$) provides a canonical admissible row vector. The non-negativity and $G_{00} = 0$ are immediate from the definition of the potential matrix. The subinvariance condition follows from multiplying $G_{0\cdot}$ by $P$ on the right and applying Fatou's Theorem, reflecting the fact that transitions from the last visit to $0$ cannot increase the expected remaining sojourn. The second formula connects $\nu_E(I-P^E)$ to the normalized entrance measures $\lambda^E$, linking Lemma 11-6 to Corollary 9-86 via the $K$-matrix construction.
