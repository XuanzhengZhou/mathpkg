Theorem 11-7 is the central convergence result for the entrance boundary of a normal Markov chain. It asserts that the finite-state normalized entrance measures $\lambda^{E_k}$ converge weak-star to the harmonic measure $\beta$ as the subsets $E_k$ expand to exhaust the state space $S$. The theorem further establishes that $\beta$ is canonically attached to the chain, being independent of which state is chosen as the distinguished reference point $0$. Finally, it extends the integral representation from the distinguished row $G_{0\cdot}$ to the full potential matrix: every entry $G_{ij}$ can be represented as the integral of the Martin kernel ${}_iN(x,j)$ against the same harmonic measure $\beta$.
