The escape probability $\theta^E$ records the distribution of the last state visited by the $Q^\nu$-process within a designated finite set $E$ containing state $0$. For each $j \in E$, $\theta^E_j$ is the probability that the process, starting from $0$, makes its final visit to $E$ at state $j$ before leaving $E$ permanently. When $E$ is finite, the process must eventually leave $E$ forever, so $\theta^E$ is a genuine probability measure on $E$. As $E$ expands to the full state space $S$, the measures $\theta^E$ converge weak-star to the entrance boundary measure $\beta^\nu$, providing a constructive approximation scheme.
