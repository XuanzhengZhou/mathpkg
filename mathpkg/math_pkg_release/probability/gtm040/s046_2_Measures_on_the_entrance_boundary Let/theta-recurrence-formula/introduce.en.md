Proposition 11-3 provides an algebraic identity linking the escape probability measure $\theta^E$ to the restricted potential theory of the Markov chain on a finite set $E$. The formula $\nu_E (I - P^E) = \theta^E_E - \delta_0$ expresses the row vector $\nu_E$ applied to the fundamental matrix $(I - P^E)^{-1}$ (inverted on the left) as the difference between the escape distribution $\theta^E$ and the unit row vector $\delta_0$ at the distinguished state. This relation is instrumental in proving the weak-star convergence of $\theta^{E_k}$ to $\beta^\nu$ as $E_k$ exhausts the state space.
