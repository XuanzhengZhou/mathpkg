Since $K(i, \cdot)$ is bounded by $N_{ii}/N_{\pi i}$, the image of $S^*$ under the map $x \mapsto \{w_i N_{\pi i} K(i, x)\}$ is a closed subset of a product of compact intervals, hence compact. The metric $d$ is induced by this embedding, so $S^*$ is compact. The extension of $K(i, \cdot)$ follows from Proposition 10-13, and injectivity follows from the definition of the metric.
