Form the extended chain associated with $P$ and $\pi$. By Theorem 10-18, almost every path satisfies $x_v \in S^*$. Hence the same is true of those paths that pass through $i$. On any path $\omega$ passing through $i$, $x_v(\omega) \in S^*$ if and only if $x_v(\omega_{u_i}) \in S^*$. Therefore
$$\Pr_{\mu^{(i)}}[x_v \in S^*] = \mu_i^{(i)} \Pr_i[x_v \in S^*].$$
But $\Pr_{\mu^{(i)}}[x_v \in S^*] = \mu_i^{(i)}$, and since $\mu_i^{(i)} \neq 0$, we get $\Pr_i[x_v \in S^*] = 1$.
