Let $P$ be a Markov chain with all states transient and let $\pi$ be a starting distribution with $\pi N > 0$. Then with probability one, for every path $\omega$, either $x_n(\omega)$ converges in $S^*$ or the path leaves $S$ in finite time (i.e., $v(\omega) < \infty$). In the latter case, $x_v$ is defined as the last state visited and is in $S^*$.
