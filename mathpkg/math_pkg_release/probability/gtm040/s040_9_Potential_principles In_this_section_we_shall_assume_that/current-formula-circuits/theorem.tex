In a chain representing a circuit, if terminal $0$ is kept at unit voltage and all terminals outside a finite set $F$ are grounded, then the current $\mu_0$ at terminal $0$ satisfies
$$\mu_0 = \alpha_0 \cdot {}_0 \bar{H}_{0\bar{F}},$$
where ${}_0 \bar{H}_{0\bar{F}}$ is the probability of hitting $\bar{F}$ before returning to $0$.
