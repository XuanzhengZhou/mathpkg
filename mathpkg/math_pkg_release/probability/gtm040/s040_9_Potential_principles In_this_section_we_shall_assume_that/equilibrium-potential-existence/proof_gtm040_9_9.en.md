Lemma 9-95, together with the fact that $\alpha l^E = 1$, shows that $-K l^E$ is an equilibrium potential and has the values specified in the theorem.

Conversely, if $f$ is an equilibrium charge, then by Proposition 9-109,
$$f_E = (I - P^E)g_E + (\alpha f)l^E = (I - P^E)g_E + l^E.$$
Since $g_E$ is constant on $E$ and $P^E 1 = 1$, we have $(I - P^E)g_E = 0$. Thus $f_E = l^E$, and the equilibrium potential is unique.
