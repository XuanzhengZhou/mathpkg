If $g$ is a pure potential and if $E \in \mathcal{L}$ then there is a unique pure potential $g'$ with support in $E$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $g'_E = g_E$,
\item $g' \leq g$,
\item $\alpha f' \geq \alpha f$,
\item $\alpha f' = -k(E)^{-1} \lambda^E g$,
\end{enumerate}
and this unique potential is $B^E g + (\lambda^E g/k(E)){}^E d$.
