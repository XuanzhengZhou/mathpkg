Since $g'$ is determined by its values on $E$, there is exactly one possibility for $f'_E$, namely $f'_E = -K_E^{-1} g_E$. Since
$$f'_E - f_E = -K_E^{-1}(g_E - g_E) = 0 \quad \text{by Proposition 9-108}$$
and the supports are in $E$, we must have $f' = f + ch$ for some vector $h$ with support in $\tilde{E}$ having $\alpha h = 0$. But $\alpha f' \geq \alpha f$, by Lemma 9-112, and we have (3). Result (4) follows by multiplying the second equation above by $\alpha$. Furthermore
$$g' = B^E g' - (\alpha f'){}^E d \quad \text{by Proposition 9-109}$$
$$\leq B^E g - (\alpha f'){}^E d \quad \text{by (1) and (3)}$$
$$\leq g \quad \text{by Proposition 9-110}.$$
Hence we have (2). The formula for $g'$ follows from (4) and the second part of Proposition 9-109.
