For a cylinder set $\{x_v = j\}$ with $j \in S$, we have $\Pr_i[x_v = j] = N_{ij} e_j$, where $e_j$ is the escape probability. Note that $\mu(\{j\}) = \Pr_\pi[x_v = j] = (\pi N)_j e_j$. Hence
$$K(i, j) \mu(\{j\}) = \frac{N_{ij}}{N_{\pi j}} (\pi N)_j e_j = N_{ij} e_j = \Pr_i[x_v = j].$$
The extension to general Borel sets follows by approximation and the continuity of $K(i, \cdot)$ on $S^*$.
