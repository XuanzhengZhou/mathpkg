By Theorem 10-26, $h = \int_{S^*} K(\cdot, x) d\mu^h(x)$. If the support of $\mu^h$ is not a single point, then $h$ can be decomposed as a sum of two non-zero regular functions supported on disjoint Borel sets, contradicting minimality. Thus $\mu^h$ is concentrated at a point $x_0$. Since $h = K(\cdot, x_0)$ and $h$ is minimal, $x_0$ must be extreme. By Proposition 10-14, such a point $x_0$ is unique.
