This proposition provides an explicit counterexample to the converse of Proposition 8-10. While that proposition shows that the condition $\alpha_i \geq k N_{ii}$ for all $i$ guarantees all potentials are bounded, the present section constructs a denumerable Markov chain $P$ (a modification of a biased random walk on $\mathbb{Z}$) for which this hypothesis fails and the associated potential $g$ is indeed unbounded. The potential grows quadratically as $i \to +\infty$, yet the chain almost surely drifts toward $-\infty$, so the unboundedness does not contradict the required decay of $P^n g$. The example also demonstrates that the potential can have a bounded charge whose support is simultaneously an equilibrium set and a dual equilibrium set.
