In a denumerable stochastic process we are given an increasing sequence of Borel fields $\mathcal{B}_n$ such that $\mathcal{B}_n \subset \mathcal{B}$ for every $n$. The field $\mathcal{B}_n$ represents the state of knowledge of the process up to time $n$. The fact that the Borel fields are increasing means that our knowledge never decreases, and the fact that all $\mathcal{B}_n$ are contained in $\mathcal{B}$ means that our total knowledge includes knowledge of what happens in a finite number of steps.
