For the basic example, equilibrium sets and their associated potentials and charges are fully characterized. In the forward chain $P$, only finite sets are equilibrium sets, and every non-empty finite set has the same capacity $\beta_\infty$. In the reverse chain $\hat{P}$, by contrast, every set is an equilibrium set. The explicit formulas for equilibrium potentials $h^E$, $\hat{h}^E$ and equilibrium charges $e^E$, $\hat{e}^E$ illustrate the general theory of Chapter 8 and explain why $\hat{N}$ does not have columns tending to zero, in contrast to $N$.
