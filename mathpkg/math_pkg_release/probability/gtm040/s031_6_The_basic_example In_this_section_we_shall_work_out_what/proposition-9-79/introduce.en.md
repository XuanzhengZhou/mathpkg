For a strongly ergodic denumerable Markov chain, Proposition 9-79 establishes three fundamental identities linking the mean passage time matrix $M$ to the fundamental matrix $Z$. Equation (1) expresses the stationary-mean passage time $M_{\alpha j}$ in terms of the diagonal entries of $Z$; equation (2) recovers $Z_{jj}$ from the reverse chain's mean passage time; and equation (3) provides a symmetry relation between forward and reverse mean passage time differences. These identities are essential for computing $M$ from the analytically more tractable fundamental matrix $Z$.
