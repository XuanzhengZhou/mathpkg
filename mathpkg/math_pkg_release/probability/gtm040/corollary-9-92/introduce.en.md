If $E$ is a set in $\mathcal{L}_0$ with $k(E) > 0$, then

$$P^E = I + K_E^{-1} - \frac{(K_E^{-1}1)(\alpha_E K_E^{-1})}{\alpha_E K_E^{-1}1}.$$

PROOF: By Proposition 9-91,

$$P^E = I + K_E^{-1} - \frac{1}{k(E)} l_E^E \lambda_E^E.$$

Multiplying through on the right by 1 gives

$$K_E^{-1}1 = \frac{1}{k(E)} l_E^E.$$

If we multiply both sides by $\alpha_E$, we get

$$k(E) = \frac{1}{\alpha_E K_E^{-1}1}.$$

Hence

$$l_E^E = \frac{K_E^{-1}1}{\alpha_E K_E^{-1}1}.$$

By duality,

$$\lambda_E^E = \frac{\alpha_E K_E^{-1}}{\alpha_E K_E^{-1}1},$$

and substitution for $k(E)^{-1}l_E^E \lambda_E^E$ gives the desired result.
