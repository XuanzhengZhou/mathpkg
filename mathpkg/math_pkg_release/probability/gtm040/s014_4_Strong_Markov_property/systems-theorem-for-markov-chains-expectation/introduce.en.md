Theorem 4-11 is the second systems theorem, generalizing Theorem 4-10 from probabilities of statements (characteristic functions) to expectations of arbitrary random variables. It states that the expected value of a random variable $f$ that depends only on outcomes beyond a random time $t$ can be decomposed as a weighted sum over the possible states $k$ at time $t$. The weights are the probabilities $\Pr_{\pi}[x_t = k]$ and the terms are the expected values $M_k[f']$ when the chain starts from state $k$. The proof proceeds by approximating $f$ by simple functions, applying Theorem 4-10 to each level set, and taking limits.
