The Strong Markov Property (Theorem 4-9) is the central result of Section 4 in Denumerable Markov Chains. It generalizes the ordinary Markov property by allowing the "present" time to be a random time $t$ rather than a fixed index $n$. The theorem states that given knowledge of the state $x_t = i$ at the random time $t$, the future (encoded by statements relative to the tail-field $\mathcal{T}_t$) is conditionally independent of the past up to time $t$ (encoded by statements relative to $\mathcal{F}_t$). In its alternative formulation, it asserts that past and future are conditionally independent given the present state.
