Lemma 4-7 is a symmetric strengthening of Lemma 4-6. It allows the past statement $q$ to depend on outcomes up to and including time $n$ (relative to $\mathcal{F}_n$ rather than $\mathcal{F}_{n-1}$), and the future statement $r$ to depend on outcomes from time $n$ onward (relative to $\mathcal{T}_n$ rather than $\mathcal{T}_{n+1}$). The key insight is that any information about $x_n$ present in $q$ can be absorbed into the condition $x_n = i$, reducing the problem to Lemma 4-6 via a decomposition of $q$ into a disjunction of cylinder statements.
