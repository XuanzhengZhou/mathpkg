Definition 4-8 generalizes the concepts of the field $\mathcal{F}_n$ and tail-field $\mathcal{T}_n$ from a fixed deterministic time $n$ to a random time $t$. The field $\mathcal{F}_t$ encodes all information about the chain up to the random time $t$ (on each set where $t = n$, it behaves like $\mathcal{F}_n$). The tail-field $\mathcal{T}_t$ encodes information about the future beyond the random time $t$. Together they provide the mathematical framework needed to state and prove the strong Markov property (Theorem 4-9), where the "present" is defined by the random time $t$ rather than a fixed index $n$.
