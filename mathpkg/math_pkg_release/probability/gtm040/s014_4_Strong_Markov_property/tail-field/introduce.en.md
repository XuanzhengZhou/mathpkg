The tail-field $\mathcal{T}_n$ is a fundamental concept in the study of Markov chains, formally capturing the idea of "the future beyond time $n$." It is the smallest augmented Borel field containing all truth sets of statements that depend only on outcomes from time $n$ onward. In contrast to the field $\mathcal{F}_n$ (which concerns the past up to time $n$), a set in $\mathcal{T}_n$ is invariant under changes to the outcomes before time $n$. The tail-field is the natural domain for applying the Markov property in conditional probability arguments.
