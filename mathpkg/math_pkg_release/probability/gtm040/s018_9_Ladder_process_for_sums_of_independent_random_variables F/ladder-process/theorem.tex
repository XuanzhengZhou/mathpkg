For a sums of independent random variables Markov chain defined on the integers, we define a sequence $s_i(\omega)$ of positive step times inductively as follows: $s_0(\omega)$ is the least $n$ such that $x_0(\omega) > 0$, and $s_i(\omega)$ is the least $n$ such that $x_n(\omega) > x_{s_{i-1}}(\omega)$. The stochastic process obtained by watching the original Markov chain only at the positive step times---that is, by calling the $n$th outcome in the new process the $s_n$th outcome in the old process---is called the \text{ladder process} associated with the original chain.
