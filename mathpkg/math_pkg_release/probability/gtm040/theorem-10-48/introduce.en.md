Theorem 10-48 is the Poisson-Martin Representation Theorem for $P$-superregular measures, the analog for the entrance boundary of the classical Martin representation for superregular functions. It establishes that every non-negative $P$-superregular measure $\sigma$ with $\sigma f$ finite can be uniquely represented as an integral of the Martin entrance kernel $J(x,i)$ against a finite Borel measure $\nu$ on $S \cup B^e$. The decomposition $\sigma = \gamma N + \rho$ into regular and singular parts corresponds to splitting the representing measure over $S$ and $B^e$ respectively.
