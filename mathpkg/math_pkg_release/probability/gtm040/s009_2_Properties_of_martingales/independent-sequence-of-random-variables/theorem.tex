A sequence of random variables $\{f_n\}$ is said to be \textbf{independent} if, for every $n$ and for every $A$, $$\Pr[f_{n+1} \in A \mid f_0 \wedge \cdots \wedge f_n] = \Pr[f_{n+1} \in A]$$ almost everywhere. In the special case where, for each $A$, $\Pr[f_n \in A]$ does not depend on $n$, the random variables are said to be \textbf{identically distributed}.
