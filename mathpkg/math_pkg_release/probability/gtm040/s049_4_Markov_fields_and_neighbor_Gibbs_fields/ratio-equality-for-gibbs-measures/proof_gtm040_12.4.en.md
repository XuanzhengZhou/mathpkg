**Proof.** The left-hand expression may be rewritten as

$$
\frac{\mu([\kappa^m, \ldots, \kappa^n])}{\nu([\kappa^m, \ldots, \kappa^n])}
\cdot
\frac{\mu_{\Lambda(m-1)}^{\Lambda(n)}(\iota)}{\nu_{\Lambda(m-1)}^{\Lambda(n)}(\iota)}.
$$

Since both $\mu$ and $\nu$ belong to $\mathcal{G}_V$, their local characteristics coincide:
$\mu_{\Lambda(m-1)}^{\Lambda(n)}(\iota) = \nu_{\Lambda(m-1)}^{\Lambda(n)}(\iota)$. Hence the second factor equals $1$, and the equality follows.
