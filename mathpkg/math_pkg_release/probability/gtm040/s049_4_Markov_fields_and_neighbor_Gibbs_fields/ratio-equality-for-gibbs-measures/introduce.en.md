This lemma establishes that for any two measures in $\mathcal{G}_V$, the density ratio evaluated on cylinder sets $[\kappa^0, \ldots, \kappa^n]$ depends only on the tail of the configuration $(\kappa^m, \ldots, \kappa^n)$, not on the initial segment. This shift-invariance property is the crucial link that allows measures in $\mathcal{G}_V$ to be represented via regular functions of a certain Markov chain, ultimately connecting Gibbs fields with Martin boundary theory.
