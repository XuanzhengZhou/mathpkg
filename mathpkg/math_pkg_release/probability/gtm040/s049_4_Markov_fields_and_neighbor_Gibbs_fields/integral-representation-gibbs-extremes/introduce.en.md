This theorem provides an integral representation of any Gibbs measure $\mu \in \mathcal{G}_V$ as an integral over the extreme Gibbs measures $\mathcal{E}_V$, analogous to the Choquet representation for convex sets. The representation uses the one-to-one correspondence between the extreme Martin boundary $B_e$ and $\mathcal{E}_V$, together with harmonic measure for the $h$-process associated with $\mu$. The measures $\lambda^{\rho(\mu)}$ on $B_e$ are precisely the representing measures for the integral decomposition of $\mu$ into extreme components.
