This theorem establishes a fundamental bridge between Gibbs fields and Markov chain theory. By fixing a reference measure $\nu \in \mathcal{G}_V$ and constructing a Markov chain on configurations, every Gibbs measure $\mu$ corresponds to a non-negative normalized $P$-regular function $h$, and conversely. This bijection preserves the convex structure, so extreme Gibbs measures correspond to minimal regular functions. The correspondence is the basis for applying Martin boundary theory to study phase transitions.
