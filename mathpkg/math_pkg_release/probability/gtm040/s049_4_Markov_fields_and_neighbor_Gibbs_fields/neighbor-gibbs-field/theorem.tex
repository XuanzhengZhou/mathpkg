A \textbf{neighbor Gibbs field} is a probability distribution on configurations of a graph that can be expressed in the form
$$\Pr[\omega] = \frac{1}{Z} \exp\left(-\sum_{C \in \mathcal{C}} V_C(\omega)\right),$$
where $\mathcal{C}$ is the set of cliques of the graph, $V_C$ are clique potentials, and $Z$ is the normalizing constant (partition function).
