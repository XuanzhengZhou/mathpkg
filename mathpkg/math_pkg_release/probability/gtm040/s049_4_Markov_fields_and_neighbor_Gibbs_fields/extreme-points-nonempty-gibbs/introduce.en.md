This theorem guarantees that extreme Gibbs measures always exist whenever any Gibbs measure exists. Moreover, almost every sample configuration $\omega$ under the reference measure $\nu$ has a tail-limit conditional measure $\nu^{\kappa^n(\omega)}$ that is extreme. The proof applies the Martin boundary theory of Chapter 10 to the constant regular function $h = 1$, using the fact that harmonic measure is concentrated on the extreme Martin boundary $B_e$, and the bijection between minimal regular functions and extreme Gibbs measures.
