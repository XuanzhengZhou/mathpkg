A denumerable collection of subsets $\mathcal{R} = \{R_1, R_2, \ldots\}$ is a \text{partition} of $\Omega$ if the sets $R_j$ are disjoint, exhaustive, and measurable. Each such subset $R_j$ is called a \text{cell} of the partition; a cell may have measure zero. The sets $\{\varnothing, R_1, R_2, \ldots\}$ together with all possible denumerable unions form a Borel field $\mathcal{R}^*$, and since the sets in $\mathcal{R}$ are measurable, $\mathcal{R}^* \subset \mathcal{B}$.
