The conditional mean $M[f \mid p]$ is the average value of a random variable $f$ restricted to the truth set $P$ of a statement $p$, normalized by the probability of $p$. It is the natural generalization of conditional probability: when $f$ is the indicator function of an event, $M[f \mid p]$ reduces to the conditional probability of that event given $p$. The definition only makes sense when $\Pr[p] \neq 0$, and it fits seamlessly into the measure-theoretic framework as a normalized Lebesgue integral over the set $P$.
