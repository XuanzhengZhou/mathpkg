A random time is a random variable that takes values in the nonnegative integers (together with $+\infty$) and whose value at time $n$ can be determined solely from the information available up to time $n$. This is the standard definition of a stopping time in the context of discrete-time stochastic processes with filtration $\{\mathcal{B}_n\}$. The condition $\{\omega \mid t(\omega)=n\} \in \mathcal{B}_n$ captures the idea that the decision to "stop" at time $n$ must not rely on future outcomes.
