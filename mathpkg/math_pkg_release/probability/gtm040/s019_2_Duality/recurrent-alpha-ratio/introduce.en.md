For a recurrent Markov chain, the expected number of visits to state $j$ before returning to state $i$ is simply the ratio $\alpha_j / \alpha_i$ of the regular measure $\alpha$. This elegant formula follows from Corollary 6-20 and the fact that in a recurrent chain, the hitting probability $\hat{H}_{ji} = 1$ for all pairs of states (the dual chain is also recurrent). The result shows that for recurrent chains, the measure $\alpha$ is uniquely determined up to a multiplicative constant and has a direct probabilistic interpretation.
