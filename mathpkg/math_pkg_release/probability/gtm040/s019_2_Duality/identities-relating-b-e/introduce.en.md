This proposition establishes two fundamental identities connecting the expected visit matrix $B^E$ to the potential kernel of the Markov chain. The first identity shows that multiplying $(I-P)$ by $B^E$ yields a block-diagonal matrix with $I - P^E$ in the upper-left block, reflecting how the taboo probability matrix $P^E$ relates to the original transition matrix. The second identity relates powers of $B^E$ to the partial sum $N^{(n)}$ of powers of $P$, which is dualized in Proposition 6-18 to obtain identities used in later chapters.
