The strong ratio limit property (SRLP) refines the classical ratio limit theorem for recurrent Markov chains. It asserts that for a noncyclic recurrent chain, the asymptotic ratio of $n$-step transition probabilities $P_{ij}^{(n+m)} / P_{kl}^{(n)}$ converges to the ratio $\pi_j / \pi_l$ of positive constants, independent of the starting states $i, k$ and the shift $m$. These constants $\pi_j$ form a positive regular measure for the chain. The property was established by Chung and Erdős [1951] for sums of independent random variables.
