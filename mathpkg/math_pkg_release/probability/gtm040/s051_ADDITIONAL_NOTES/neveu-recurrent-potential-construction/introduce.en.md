Neveu [1972b] developed recurrent potential theory by constructing a bounded potential kernel $W$ from the operator $U_h$. Starting from a recurrent chain with positive regular measure $\alpha$, he showed the existence of a function $h$ satisfying $U_h h = 1$ and the domination $U_h \geq 1\alpha$. Subtracting the rank-one term $1\alpha$ gives $V$, and iterating $V D_h$ yields the kernel $W$, which satisfies the Green-kernel identities $W h = c 1$ and $\alpha D_h W = c \alpha$. This construction provides a potential-theoretic framework for recurrent chains analogous to the transient case.
