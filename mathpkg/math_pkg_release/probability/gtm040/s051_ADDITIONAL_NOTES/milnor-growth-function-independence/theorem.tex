For a finitely generated group, the growth function --- the number of elements expressible as a product of $n$ generators --- is approximately independent of the choice of generating set.

Furthermore:
\begin{itemize}
\item The existence of a symmetric random walk $P$ with spectral radius less than $1$ implies exponential growth of the group.
\item There exist solvable groups admitting a symmetric random walk with spectral radius $1$ yet having exponential growth.
\end{itemize}
