Show that if $\{x_i\}$ has any neighbor potential $U$, then $\{x_i\}$ is a Markov field. Give an example of a Markov field with potential $U$, such that $U$ is not a neighbor potential. [Hint: For the first part, look carefully at the proof of Theorem 12-16.]
