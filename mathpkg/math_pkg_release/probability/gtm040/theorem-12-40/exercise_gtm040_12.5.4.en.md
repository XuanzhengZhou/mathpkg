Suppose that there is a metric $d$ defined on $T$, and say that $\{x_i\}$ is $L$-Markov ($L \geq 0$) if $\mu_a^A = \mu_a^{B(a,L)}$ whenever $B(a,L) = \{t \in T: d(a,t) \leq L\} \subset A \subset T$. What property for the canonical potential $V$ is equivalent to the $L$-Markov property for $\{x_i\}$? State and prove a theorem to justify your assertion. Describe the $\sqrt{2}$-Markov fields on $\mathbb{Z}^2$.
