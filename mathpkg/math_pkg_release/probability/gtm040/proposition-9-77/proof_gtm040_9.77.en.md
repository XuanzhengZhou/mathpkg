**Proof:** The second assertion follows from the first, since $C = MD^{-1}$. For the first we have

$$Z_{ij} - \alpha_j = \lim_n \sum_{m=0}^{n} (P^m - A)_{ij}$$
$$= \lim [N_{ij}^{(n)} - (n + 1)\alpha_j]$$

and similarly

$$Z_{jj} - \alpha_j = \lim [N_{jj}^{(n)} - (n + 1)\alpha_j]$$

Hence

$$Z_{jj} - Z_{ij} = \lim [N_{jj}^{(n)} - N_{ij}^{(n)}] = C_{ij}.$$

The next proposition shows that $Z$ may be used as a single potential operator in place of both $-C$ and $-G$. Since (dual $Z$) = $\hat{Z}$, duality takes as simple a form as for transient potentials. Beginning in Section 8 we shall develop an operator $-K$ which exists for all normal chains and which has properties similar to those of $Z$.
