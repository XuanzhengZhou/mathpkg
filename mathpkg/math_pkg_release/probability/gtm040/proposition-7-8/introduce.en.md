Brownian motion in one dimension is a limit of the symmetric random walk in this sense: If $t$ is a diadic rational and if $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are real numbers, then

$$\lim_{k \to \infty} \Pr_0[x_{4k}^t \in (\alpha, \beta)] = \Pr_0[x_t \in (\alpha, \beta)].$$
