This is the fundamental classification theorem for one-dimensional random walks with finitely many step sizes and span one. It asserts that recurrence is equivalent to the walk having zero mean drift. When $m \neq 0$, the strong law of large numbers forces the walk to drift to $\pm\infty$, making each state transient; when $m = 0$, a bounded martingale argument with an absorbing stopping time shows that the walk returns to every state with probability one. The result generalizes to infinitely many $k$-values provided the mean exists and the $k$-values have greatest common divisor one.
