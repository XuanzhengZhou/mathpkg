Theorem 10-9 is the foundational existence theorem for extended Markov chains on denumerable state spaces. It establishes that every positive $P$-superregular measure $\nu$ can be realized as the vector of mean occupation times for some extended stochastic process $\{x_n\}$ taking values in the augmented state space $S \cup \{a\} \cup \{b\}$. The process is constructed with enlarged transition matrix $\bar{P} = P^*_{S \cup \{b\}}$ and satisfies the four defining properties of an extended chain: accessibility of finite sets, asymptotic emptiness in the remote past, the Markov property when watched in finite sets, and the identification of $\nu$ as the mean time vector.

The proof proceeds by constructing probability assignments on cylinder sets from the balayage charges $\mu^E$ of $\nu$ on finite subsets $E$, fixing a common time scale so that the first entry into each designated state occurs at time $0$. Consistency of the cylinder set measures is verified using limiting arguments involving the convergence of balayage charges. The theorem provides the essential bridge between potential-theoretic data (superregular measures) and probabilistic objects (extended chains), playing a central role in the Martin entrance boundary theory developed in the same chapter.
