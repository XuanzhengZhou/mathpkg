This proposition characterizes when two homogeneous neighbor potentials define the same Gibbs field on $\mathbb{Z}$. By adapting the proof of Proposition 12-14 and Corollary 12-15 to the infinite parameter set, one obtains equivalence conditions involving the Q-matrices derived from the potentials. The result establishes that two such potentials are Gibbs-equivalent precisely when their associated Q-matrices differ by a multiplicative factor of the form $c h_j / h_i$, where $c > 0$ and $h_i > 0$. This provides an explicit algebraic criterion for identifying distinct representatives of the same Gibbsian equivalence class.
