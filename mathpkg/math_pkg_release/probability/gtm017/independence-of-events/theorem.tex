Two events $A_1, A_2$ are said to be \textbf{independent} if
$$P(A_1 \mid A_2) = P(A_1),$$
or equivalently,
$$P(A_1 \cap A_2) = P(A_1) P(A_2).$$

A denumerable collection of events $A_1, A_2, \ldots$ is called a collection of \textbf{independent events} if every finite subcollection $A_{k_1}, \ldots, A_{k_m}$ satisfies the product relation
$$P(A_{k_1} A_{k_2} \cdots A_{k_m}) = \prod_{i=1}^{m} P(A_{k_i}).$$
