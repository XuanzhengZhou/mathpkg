A \textbf{probability space} is a triple $(\Omega, \mathfrak{F}, P)$ where $\Omega$ is the space of sample points, $\mathfrak{F}$ is a field (or sigma-field) of events generated by the sample points, and $P$ is the probability function defined on the events of $\mathfrak{F}$.

A numerical-valued function $X(w)$ on the space $\Omega$ of sample points is called a \textbf{random variable}.

A random variable $X(w)$ generates a field (sigma-field) $\mathfrak{F}_X$ of events generated by events of the form $\{w \mid X(w) = a\}$. The probability function $P$ on $\mathfrak{F}_X$ is called the \textbf{probability distribution} of $X(w)$.
