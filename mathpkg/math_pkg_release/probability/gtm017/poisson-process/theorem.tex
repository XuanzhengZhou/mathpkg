The \text{Poisson process} $\{X_t, t \geq 0\}$ with intensity $\lambda > 0$ is a continuous-parameter process with independent increments satisfying $X_0 = 0$ and for $0 < t_1 < \cdots < t_k$:
$$P(X_{t_1} = j_1, \ldots, X_{t_k} = j_k) = \frac{(\lambda t_1)^{j_1} e^{-\lambda t_1}}{j_1!} \prod_{u=1}^{k-1} \frac{[\lambda(t_{u+1} - t_u)]^{j_{u+1} - j_u}}{(j_{u+1} - j_u)!} e^{-\lambda(t_{u+1} - t_u)}$$
when $0 \leq j_1 \leq \cdots \leq j_k$ (and zero otherwise). The increments $X_{t} - X_{\tau}$ ($t > \tau$) have a Poisson distribution with mean $\lambda(t - \tau)$.
