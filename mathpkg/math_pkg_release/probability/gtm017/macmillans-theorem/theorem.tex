Let $\{X_n\}$ be a strictly stationary process taking values in a finite alphabet, with entropy $H$. MacMillan's theorem states that for almost every sample path,
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} -\frac{1}{n} \log P(X_1, \ldots, X_n) = H$$
almost surely, where $H$ is the entropy rate of the process. This is the asymptotic equipartition property for stationary processes.
