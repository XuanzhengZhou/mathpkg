The extinction probability criterion for branching processes depends on the mean offspring $\mu$. When $\mu \leq 1$, extinction is certain (subcritical/critical case). When $\mu > 1$ (supercritical), there is a positive probability of survival. The proof uses the monotonicity of $\varphi(s)$ and the mean value theorem to locate the fixed points of $\varphi$.
