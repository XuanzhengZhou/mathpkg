A random process $\{X_t, t \in T\}$ where $T$ has an additive group structure is called \text{strictly stationary} if for any positive integer $m$, any $t_1, \ldots, t_m \in T$, and any $h \in T$, the joint distribution of
$$(X_{t_1}, X_{t_2}, \ldots, X_{t_m})$$
is the same as that of
$$(X_{t_1+h}, X_{t_2+h}, \ldots, X_{t_m+h}).$$

Equivalently, the probability structure is invariant under time translation, and finite-dimensional distributions depend only on the time differences $t_2 - t_1, \ldots, t_m - t_1$.
