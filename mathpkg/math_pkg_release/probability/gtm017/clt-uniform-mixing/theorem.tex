Under a uniform mixing condition on a strictly stationary process, normalized sums converge in distribution to a normal law. Specifically, if the process satisfies a sufficiently strong asymptotic independence (mixing) condition with summable mixing coefficients, then
$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \sum_{j=1}^{n} (X_j - EX_j) \xrightarrow{d} N(0, \sigma^2)$$
as $n \to \infty$, where $\sigma^2$ is the limiting variance of the normalized sum.
