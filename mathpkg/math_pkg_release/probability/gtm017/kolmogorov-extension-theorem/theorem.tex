Given a consistent family of finite-dimensional distribution functions, there exists a probability space $(\Omega, \mathfrak{F}, P)$ and a random process $\{X_t, t \in T\}$ on it having those distributions as its finite-dimensional distributions.

The space $\Omega$ can be taken as the product space of all real-valued functions on $T$, and the sigma-field $\mathfrak{F}$ is generated by the cylinder sets
$$\{w \mid w(t_1) \leq x_1, \ldots, w(t_k) \leq x_k\}, \quad t_1, \ldots, t_k \in T.$$
