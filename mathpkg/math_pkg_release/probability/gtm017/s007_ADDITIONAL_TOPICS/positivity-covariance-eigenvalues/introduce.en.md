Since the covariance function $r(t,\tau)$ is positive definite, the quadratic form $\iint \varphi(t) r(t,\tau) \varphi(\tau) dt d\tau$ is strictly positive for any nontrivial square-integrable $\varphi$. From the eigenvalue equation, $\int |\varphi(t)|^2 dt = \lambda \iint \varphi(t) r(t,\tau) \varphi(\tau) dt d\tau$, which forces $\lambda > 0$. Moreover, if $\varphi$ were complex, its real and imaginary parts would separately be eigenfunctions with the same eigenvalue, so one may take all eigenfunctions to be real-valued.
