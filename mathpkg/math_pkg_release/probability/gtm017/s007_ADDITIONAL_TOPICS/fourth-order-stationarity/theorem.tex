A random process $X_t$, $-\infty < t < \infty$, with $E X_t^4 < \infty$ and continuous in the mean of fourth order ($E|X_t - X_\tau|^4 \to 0$ as $t \to \tau$), is said to be stationary in moments of second and fourth order if, assuming $E X_t = 0$,
$$E[X_{t_1} X_{t_2}] = r(t_2 - t_1),$$
$$E[X_{t_1} X_{t_2} X_{t_3} X_{t_4}] = P(t_2 - t_1, t_3 - t_1, t_4 - t_1),$$
where $r$ is the covariance function and $P$ is the fourth-order moment function, both depending only on time differences.
