Let $r(t,\tau)$ be square integrable on $0 \leq t, \tau \leq T$, and let $R$ be the integral operator with kernel $r$. Then $R$ is completely continuous: given any sequence $g_n$ bounded in mean square ($\|g_n\| \leq K < \infty$), the sequence $h_n = R g_n$ contains a subsequence $h_{n_k}$ that converges in mean square. By the Riesz-Fisher theorem, the subsequence converges to a square-integrable function. The sum of two completely continuous integral operators is also completely continuous.
