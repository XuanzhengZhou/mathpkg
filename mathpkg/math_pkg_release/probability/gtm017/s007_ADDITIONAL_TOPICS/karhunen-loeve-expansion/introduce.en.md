The Karhunen-Loeve expansion is the analogue for random processes of diagonalizing a symmetric matrix. It represents a mean-square continuous process as an infinite series of deterministic eigenfunctions weighted by uncorrelated random coefficients. The basis functions are eigenfunctions of the covariance kernel, and the coefficients $Z_i$ are orthonormal random variables. The expansion converges in mean square. For Gaussian processes, the coefficients are independent standard normal.
