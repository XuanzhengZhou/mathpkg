An integral operator $R$ on $L^2[0,T]$ maps a square-integrable function $g$ to $h = Rg$ via integration against a kernel $r(t,\tau)$. If $r$ is square integrable on $[0,T] \times [0,T]$, then $R$ maps $L^2$ into itself. The operator norm $\|R\|$ is the supremum of $\|Rf\|$ over unit vectors. For symmetric kernels, the operator is self-adjoint. This framework is needed to prove complete continuity and the existence of eigenfunctions.
