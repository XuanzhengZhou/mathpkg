Mercer's theorem provides a diagonal expansion of the continuous, positive-definite covariance kernel $r(t,\tau)$ in terms of its eigenfunctions and eigenvalues. The expansion converges uniformly, stronger than pointwise or $L^2$ convergence. The proof relies on the complete continuity of the integral operator, Dini's theorem, and the symmetry of $r$. This expansion is the foundation for the Karhunen-Loeve representation.
