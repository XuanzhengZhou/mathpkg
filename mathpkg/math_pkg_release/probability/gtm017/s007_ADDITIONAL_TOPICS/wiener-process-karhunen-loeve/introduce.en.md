The Wiener process on $[0,1]$ provides an explicit illustration of the Karhunen-Loeve expansion. Its covariance kernel $r(t,\tau) = \min(t,\tau)$ leads to the differential equation $\varphi'' + \lambda\varphi = 0$ with boundary conditions $\varphi(0)=0$, $\varphi'(1)=0$. The eigenfunctions are shifted sine functions $\sqrt{2}\sin((k+1/2)\pi t)$, and eigenvalues grow as $(k+1/2)^2\pi^2$. The expansion expresses Brownian motion as a random Fourier sine series with independent Gaussian coefficients.
