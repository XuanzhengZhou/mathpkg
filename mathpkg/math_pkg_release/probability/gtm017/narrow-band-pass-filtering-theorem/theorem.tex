Let $X_t$, $EX_t = 0$, be a uniformly mixing process stationary up to fourth order moments with spectral density $f(\lambda)$, and let $w_T(t)$ be weight functions satisfying appropriate regularity conditions. Then
$$W(T)^{-1/2} \int_0^T w_T(t) X_t dt$$
is asymptotically normally distributed with mean zero and variance
$$2\pi \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(\lambda) dM(\lambda),$$
where $W(T) = \int_0^T w_T^2(t) dt$ and $M(\lambda)$ is the spectral measure of the limiting covariance of the normalized weight functions.
