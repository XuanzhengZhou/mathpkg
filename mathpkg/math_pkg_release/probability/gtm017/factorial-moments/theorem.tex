The $r$-th factorial moment of a non-negative integer-valued random variable $X$ is
$$E[X(X-1)\cdots(X-r+1)] = \lim_{s \to 1-} \frac{d^r}{ds^r} g(s),$$
where $g(s) = E(s^X) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} p_k s^k$ is the probability generating function. The $r$-th factorial moment is finite iff the $r$-th ordinary moment $EX^r$ is finite.
