Let $X(t)$ be the Wiener process. The Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process is $U(t) = \sqrt{\alpha} \exp(-\beta t) X(e^{2\beta t})$, $-\infty < t < \infty$. $U(t)$ is Gaussian and strictly stationary with $EU(t) = 0$ and $E U(t) U(\tau) = \alpha \exp(-\beta |t - \tau|)$. It satisfies $dU(t) + \beta U(t) dt = \sqrt{2\alpha\beta} dX(t)$. The position $B(t) = \int_0^t U(a) da$ with $(B(t), U(t))$ forms a two-dimensional Markov diffusion.
