By Bochner's theorem, $r(t)$ has the Fourier-Stieltjes representation $r(t) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{it\lambda} dF(\lambda)$ where $F$ is nondecreasing, right-continuous, bounded with $F(+\infty)-F(-\infty) = r(0)$. $F(\lambda)$ is the \textbf{spectral distribution function}. If absolutely continuous, $f(\lambda) = F'(\lambda)$ is the \textbf{spectral density}.
