A stationary process of independent, identically distributed random variables is mixing. Consequently, any derived process $Y_n(w) = f(T^n w)$ is mixing. In particular, linear processes $Y_n = \sum_{j} a_j X_{n-j}$ with $\sum_j a_j^2 < \infty$ and $X_n$ i.i.d. with mean zero and variance one are mixing.
