Several notions of convergence exist for sequences of random variables. Almost-everywhere convergence (pointwise except on a null set) is the strongest, implying convergence in probability. Uniform integrability is a compactness condition that upgrades convergence in probability to $L^1$ convergence. These concepts are essential for limit theorems in probability.
