A measure of concentration of the probability mass of a random variable $X$ about its mean $m = EX$ is given by the \textbf{variance}
$$\sigma^2 = E(X - m)^2 = EX^2 - m^2.$$

For independent random variables $X_1, \dots, X_s$, the variance of the sum satisfies:
$$\sigma^2\left(\sum_{i=1}^{s} X_i\right) = \sum_{i=1}^{s} \sigma^2(X_i).$$

For independent random variables $X, Y$:
$$E(XY) = (EX)(EY).$$
More generally, $E(f(X)g(Y)) = E(f(X)) E(g(Y))$ for independent $X, Y$.
