\textbf{Bochner's Theorem:} A function $\varphi(t)$ is the characteristic function of a probability distribution (i.e., the Fourier-Stieltjes transform of a distribution function) if and only if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\varphi$ is continuous
\item $\varphi(0) = 1$
\item $\varphi$ is positive definite:
  $$\sum_{i,j=1}^{k} c_i \bar{c}_j \varphi(t_i - t_j) \geq 0$$
  for all $k$, all real $t_1, \ldots, t_k$, and all complex $c_1, \ldots, c_k$.
\end{enumerate}
