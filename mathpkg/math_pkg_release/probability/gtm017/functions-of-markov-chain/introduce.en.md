A function of a Markov chain is generally not Markovian. This result gives necessary and sufficient conditions on the transition probabilities for the collapsed process to retain the Markov property. For reversible chains, the condition simplifies to requiring that the lumped transition probability from a set $S_\alpha$ to $S_\beta$ be independent of the specific state $i \in S_\alpha$.
