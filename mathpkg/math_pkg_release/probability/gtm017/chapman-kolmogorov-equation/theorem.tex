Higher order transition probabilities satisfy the \textbf{Chapman-Kolmogorov equation}:
$$P^{(r,s)} P^{(s,t)} = P^{(r,t)}, \quad r < s < t,$$
where $P^{(r,s)}$ is the matrix of transition probabilities from time $r$ to time $s$.

In the case of a Markov chain with stationary transition mechanism:
$$P^{(r,s)} = P^{s-r}, \quad s > r,$$
where $P = P^{(m,m+1)}$ is the one-step transition probability matrix.
