\textbf{Mercer's Theorem:} Let $r(t, \tau)$ be a continuous, symmetric, positive definite kernel on $0 \leq t, \tau \leq T$. Then $r(t, \tau)$ has the uniformly convergent expansion
$$r(t, \tau) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \frac{\varphi_i(t) \varphi_i(\tau)}{\lambda_i},$$
where $\{\lambda_i\}$ are the eigenvalues and $\{\varphi_i\}$ the corresponding orthonormal eigenfunctions of the integral equation
$$\varphi(t) = \lambda \int_0^T r(t, \tau) \varphi(\tau) d\tau.$$

The eigenvalues are countable, each of finite multiplicity, and positive.
