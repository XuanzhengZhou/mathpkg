Consider a particle on the integer points of the real line. Given the particle is at $i$, its probability of moving one step to the right is $p$ and one step to the left is $q$, where $p, q \geq 0$, $p+q=1$:
$$p_{i,i+1} = p, \quad p_{i,i-1} = q.$$

Starting at $i=0$ at $t=0$, the probability of being at $j$ at time $n$ is
$$\binom{n}{(n+j)/2} p^{(n+j)/2} q^{(n-j)/2},$$
for $n+j$ even (otherwise zero).

For an absorbing barrier at $-a < 0$:
$$p_{-a,-a} = 1, \quad p_{i,i+1} = p, \; p_{i,i-1} = q \;\; (i > -a).$$
