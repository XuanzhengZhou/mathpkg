A random variable is a measurable function from the sample space to the real numbers, representing a numerical observable in a random experiment. Each random variable generates a sub-sigma-field of events and has an associated probability distribution. Multiple random variables are independent when their generated sigma-fields are independent. This formalizes the intuitive notion of variables whose outcomes do not influence each other.
