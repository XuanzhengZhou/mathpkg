An $n \times n$ matrix $A$ with non-negative elements is said to be \textbf{irreducible} if one cannot bring $A$ into the form
$$\begin{pmatrix} A_{11} & A_{12} \\ 0 & A_{22} \end{pmatrix}$$
by a consistent relabeling of rows and columns, where $A_{11}$ is $r \times r$, $1 \leq r < n$, and $A_{22}$ is $(n-r) \times (n-r)$. Otherwise $A$ is reducible.
