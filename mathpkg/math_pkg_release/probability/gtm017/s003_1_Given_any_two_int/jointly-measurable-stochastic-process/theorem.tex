A continuous-time stochastic process $\{X_t(\omega), 0 \leq t \leq T\}$ is said to be jointly measurable in $(t, \omega)$ if $X_t(\omega)$ is measurable with respect to the product sigma-field generated by the Lebesgue measurable sets on $[0, T]$ and the sigma-field $\mathfrak{F}$ on $\Omega$. Under joint measurability, by Fubini's theorem, $\int_0^T X_t(\omega) dt$ is a well-defined random variable whenever $\int_0^T E|X_t| dt$ is finite.
