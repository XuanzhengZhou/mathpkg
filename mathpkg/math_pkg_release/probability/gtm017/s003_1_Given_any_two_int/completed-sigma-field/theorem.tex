Given a sigma-field $\mathfrak{F}$ and a probability measure defined on it, the completed sigma-field $\mathfrak{F}_c$ is generated by $\mathfrak{F}$ and all subsets of $\mathfrak{F}$-sets of probability zero. Every set of $\mathfrak{F}_c$ differs from an appropriately chosen set of $\mathfrak{F}$ by at most a subset of an $\mathfrak{F}$-set of measure zero. The measure on $\mathfrak{F}$ is extended to $\mathfrak{F}_c$ by assigning the same measure to the $\mathfrak{F}_c$-set as the $\mathfrak{F}$-set that approximates it to within a subset of measure zero.
