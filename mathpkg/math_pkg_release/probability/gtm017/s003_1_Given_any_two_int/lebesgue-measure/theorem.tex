Take $\Omega$ as the set of real numbers $\omega$, $0 < \omega \leq 1$. Let $\mathbb{C}$ consist of intervals of the form $\{\omega \mid \alpha < \omega \leq \beta\}$, $0 \leq \alpha \leq \beta \leq 1$, and take $m(\{\omega \mid \alpha < \omega \leq \beta\}) = \beta - \alpha$. The extension of $m$ from $\mathbb{C}$ to $\mathscr{F}(\mathbb{C})$ and then to the completed sigma-field $\mathscr{F}_c(\mathbb{C})$ is called Lebesgue measure. Equivalently, $\Omega$ may be taken as the set of all real numbers; then $\mathscr{F}(\mathbb{C})$ consists of the Borel subsets of the whole real line. Lebesgue measure on the real line is a sigma-finite measure, not a finite measure.
