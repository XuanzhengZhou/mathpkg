Let $X_1, \ldots, X_n$ be i.i.d. with Cauchy density $f_\mu(x) = \frac{1}{\pi} \frac{1}{1 + (x - \mu)^2}$. Find the distribution of $S_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum X_i$. The Cauchy distribution is not subject to the central limit theorem (no finite mean/variance), so $S_n$ has the same Cauchy distribution as each $X_i$ -- the sample mean is not a consistent estimator of $\mu$. The sample median is a better estimator.
