A random variable $X$ is said to be normally distributed with mean $m$ and variance $\sigma^2 > 0$ if its distribution function is
$$F(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi} \sigma} \int_{-\infty}^{x} e^{-\frac{(u-m)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \, du.$$
The mean is $EX = m$ and variance is $E(X-m)^2 = \sigma^2$. If $X$ is standard normal (mean 0, variance 1), then $Y = m + \sigma X$ is normal with mean $m$ and variance $\sigma^2$.
