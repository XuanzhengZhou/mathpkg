A Gaussian process is a stochastic process whose finite-dimensional distributions are all multivariate normal. It is completely characterized by its mean function $m(t) = E[X_t]$ and covariance function $r(s,t) = E[(X_s - m(s))(X_t - m(t))]$. Examples include Brownian motion, the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process, and Gaussian random fields used in spatial statistics and machine learning.
