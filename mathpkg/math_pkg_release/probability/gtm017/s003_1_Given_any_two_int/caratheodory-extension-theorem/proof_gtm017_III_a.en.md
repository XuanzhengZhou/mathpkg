Construct an outer measure $m^*$ from $m$: $m^*(E) = \inf\{\sum m(A_i) : E \subset \bigcup A_i, A_i \in \mathbb{C}\}$. The collection of sets satisfying Caratheodory's measurability condition forms a sigma-field containing $\mathbb{C}$, and $m^*$ restricted to it is a measure extending $m$. For uniqueness, if $\mu_1, \mu_2$ are two extensions and $m$ is sigma-finite, the collection of sets on which they agree is a monotone class containing $\mathbb{C}$, hence equals the generated sigma-field.
