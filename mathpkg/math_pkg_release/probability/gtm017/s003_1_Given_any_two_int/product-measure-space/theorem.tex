Let $\Omega_1, \Omega_2$ be spaces with sigma-fields $\mathfrak{F}_1, \mathfrak{F}_2$ and measures $m_1, m_2$ respectively. The product space $\Omega_1 \times \Omega_2$ consists of points $\omega = (\omega_1, \omega_2)$ with $\omega_1 \in \Omega_1, \omega_2 \in \Omega_2$. The product sigma-field $\mathfrak{F}_1 \times \mathfrak{F}_2$ is generated by sets of the form $A_1 \times A_2 = \{(\omega_1, \omega_2) \mid \omega_1 \in A_1, \omega_2 \in A_2\}$ with $A_1 \in \mathfrak{F}_1, A_2 \in \mathfrak{F}_2$. The product measure $m = m_1 \times m_2$ on $\mathfrak{F}_1 \times \mathfrak{F}_2$ is the extension of the set function $m(A_1 \times A_2) = m_1(A_1) \cdot m_2(A_2)$.
