The Kolmogorov extension theorem guarantees the existence of a stochastic process with prescribed finite-dimensional distributions, provided they satisfy consistency conditions. The construction takes $\Omega = \mathbb{R}^T$ with coordinate mappings, defines the sigma-field from cylinder sets, and uses Caratheodory's theorem to build the probability measure. A limitation is that the resulting sigma-field may not contain events involving uncountably many time points.
