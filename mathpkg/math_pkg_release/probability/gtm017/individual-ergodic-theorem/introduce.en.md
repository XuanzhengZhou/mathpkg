The individual (or Birkhoff) ergodic theorem is one of the fundamental results of probability theory. It states that for any integrable function of a stationary process, the long-run time average converges almost surely to the conditional expectation given the invariant sigma-field. For ergodic (metrically transitive) processes, this equals the ensemble mean, justifying the ergodic hypothesis.
