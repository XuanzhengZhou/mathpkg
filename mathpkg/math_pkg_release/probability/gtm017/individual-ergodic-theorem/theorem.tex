Let $T$ be a measure-preserving transformation on a probability space $(\Omega, \mathfrak{F}, P)$ and let $f$ be an integrable function, $E|f| < \infty$. Then the limit
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} f(T^j w)$$
exists with probability one and equals the conditional expectation $E[f|\mathfrak{G}]$, where $\mathfrak{G}$ is the sigma-field of invariant sets.

If the process is metrically transitive, $\mathfrak{G}$ is trivial and the limit equals $E[f]$ almost everywhere. Thus, time averages equal space averages:
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=0}^{n-1} f(T^j w) = \int f \, dP \quad \text{a.e.}$$
