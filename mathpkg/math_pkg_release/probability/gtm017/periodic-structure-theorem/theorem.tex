\textbf{Theorem 3:} Let $A$ be an irreducible non-negative matrix. Suppose there are $k$ roots of $\Phi(z) = 0$ of maximal absolute value $\lambda$. Then they must all be simple and of the form $\lambda e^{2\pi i j/k}$ ($j = 1, \ldots, k$). The set of all $n$ roots of $A$ is invariant under a rotation in the complex plane about zero through an angle of $2\pi/k$ but not through any smaller angle.

By appropriate relabeling, $A$ can be put in the cyclic block form:
$$\begin{bmatrix}
0 & A_{12} & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & A_{23} & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & A_{k-1,k} \\
A_{k1} & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0
\end{bmatrix}.$$
