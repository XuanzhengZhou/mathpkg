A martingale models a fair game: the expected future gain, given all past information, equals the current gain. Supermartingales (unfavorable games) and submartingales (favorable games) generalize the notion. The sequence of partial sums of independent zero-mean random variables is a martingale, as is a conditional expectation $E[f|\mathfrak{B}_n]$ for increasing sigma-fields.
