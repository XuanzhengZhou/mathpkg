A Markov process is a \textbf{jump process} if the transition probability satisfies, for small $t - \tau > 0$,
$$P(\tau, x; t, A) = \{1 - p(t, x)(t - \tau)\} \delta(x, A) + p(t, x)(t - \tau) \Pi(t, x, A) + o(t - \tau),$$
where $\delta(x, A) = 1$ if $x \in A$, $0$ otherwise. Here $p(t, x)$ is the intensity of leaving state $x$ and $\Pi(t, x, A)$ is the distribution of the new state after a jump.
