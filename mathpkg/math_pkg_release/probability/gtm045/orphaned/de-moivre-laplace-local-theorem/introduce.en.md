The De Moivre-Laplace local limit theorem (1732) is the first central limit theorem: it shows that the binomial probability mass function, properly centered and scaled, approaches the standard normal density $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-x^2/2}$. The proof uses Stirling's formula and logarithmic expansions. This result revealed the need for continuous (nonelementary) random variables to describe limiting distributions.
