This theorem provides a complete characterization of distribution functions: they are precisely the left-continuous, nondecreasing functions on $\mathbb{R}$ tending to 0 at $-\infty$ and 1 at $+\infty$. The converse shows that given any such $F$, one can construct a probability space and a random variable having $F$ as its distribution function (e.g., via the quantile transform on the uniform distribution).
