The Lindeberg condition ensures that no single term dominates the normalized sum: the contribution of large values to the total variance is asymptotically negligible. It is a sufficient (and, together with the uan condition, necessary) condition for the central limit theorem to hold for triangular arrays, generalizing the classical i.i.d. CLT.
