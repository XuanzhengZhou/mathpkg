The extrema criterion characterizes domains of attraction through the asymptotic behavior of tail probabilities and extremal values. For attraction to a stable law with exponent $\alpha < 2$, the tails must be regularly varying with exponent $-\alpha$. This connects the central limit problem to the theory of regularly varying functions and extreme value theory.
