The Tchebichev (Chebyshev) inequality provides a universal tail bound: the probability that a random variable deviates from zero by at least $\epsilon$ is bounded by its second moment divided by $\epsilon^2$. This inequality is a cornerstone for proving laws of large numbers and concentration results, requiring only the existence of second moments.
