A distribution belongs to the domain of attraction of a stable law if normalized sums of i.i.d. copies converge to that law. The characterization involves regular variation of tails: for the normal domain of attraction, the truncated second moment dominates the tail; for stable laws with index $\alpha < 2$, the tails are regularly varying with exponent $-\alpha$.
