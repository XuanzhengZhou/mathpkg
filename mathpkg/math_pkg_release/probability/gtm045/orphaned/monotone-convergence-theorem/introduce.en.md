The monotone convergence theorem (Beppo Levi) states that the integral of a pointwise nondecreasing limit of nonnegative measurable functions equals the limit of their integrals. This is one of the three pillars of Lebesgue integration theory, enabling the interchange of limit and integral under monotonicity.
