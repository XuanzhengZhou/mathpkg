Kolmogorov's inequality is a maximal inequality for partial sums of independent zero-mean random variables. It bounds the probability that the maximum of the partial sums exceeds a threshold by the total variance divided by the threshold squared. This is a vast strengthening of Chebyshev's inequality (which only bounds a single term) and is the key tool for proving strong laws and almost sure convergence of series.
