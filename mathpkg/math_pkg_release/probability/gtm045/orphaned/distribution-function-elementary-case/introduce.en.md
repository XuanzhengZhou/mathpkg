The distribution function $F$ of a random variable $X$ encodes its probability distribution through the left-continuous nondecreasing function $F(x) = P[X < x]$. From the distribution function, one recovers both interval probabilities and point masses. The empirical counterpart $F_n(x)$ constructed from repeated trials converges almost surely to the theoretical $F(x)$ by Borel's strong law.
