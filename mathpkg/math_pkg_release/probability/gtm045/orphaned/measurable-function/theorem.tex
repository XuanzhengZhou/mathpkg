Let $(\Omega, \alpha)$ and $(\Omega', \alpha')$ be measurable spaces. A function $X: \Omega \to \Omega'$ is \textit{measurable} if the inverse images of measurable sets are measurable:

$$X^{-1}(\alpha') \subset \alpha.$$

If $X'$ is measurable from $(\Omega', \alpha')$ to $(\Omega'', \alpha'')$, then $X' \circ X$ is measurable.
