The Carathéodory extension theorem is the cornerstone of measure theory. It guarantees that a countably additive set function defined on a field (or semiring) of sets can be extended to a measure on the generated $\sigma$-field. Uniqueness holds under $\sigma$-finiteness. This theorem justifies the construction of Lebesgue measure, product measures, and probability measures on Borel $\sigma$-fields.
