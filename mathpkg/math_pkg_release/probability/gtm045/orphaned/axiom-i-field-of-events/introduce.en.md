Axiom I of the finite case in probability theory requires that the collection of random events $\alpha$ forms a field of sets. This means $\alpha$ is closed under complementation, finite intersections, and finite unions. This algebraic structure ensures that any finite logical combination of events (using "not", "and", "or") is itself an event whose probability can be discussed.
