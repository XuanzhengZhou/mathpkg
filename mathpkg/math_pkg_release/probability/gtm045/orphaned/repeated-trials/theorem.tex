The \textit{Bernoulli case} consists of a sequence of events $A_k$, $k = 1, 2, \cdots$, which are independent whatever be their total number $n$ under consideration, and such that their probabilities $PA_k$ have the same value $p$. The number of occurrences in $n$ repeated trials is $S_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} I_{A_k}$, with

$$ES_n = np, \quad \sigma^2 S_n = npq,$$

where $q = 1-p$.
