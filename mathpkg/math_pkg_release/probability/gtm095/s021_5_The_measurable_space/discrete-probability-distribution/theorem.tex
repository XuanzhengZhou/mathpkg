A probability measure $P$ on $(R, \mathcal{B}(R))$ concentrated on a countable set $D = \{x_1, x_2, \ldots\} \subseteq R$ is called \textit{discrete}. The numbers

$$p_k = P(\{x_k\})$$

satisfy $p_k \geq 0$ and $\sum_{k} p_k = 1$. The set of numbers $(p_1, p_2, \ldots)$ is called a \textit{discrete probability distribution}, and the corresponding distribution function $F = F(x)$ is called \textit{discrete}.
