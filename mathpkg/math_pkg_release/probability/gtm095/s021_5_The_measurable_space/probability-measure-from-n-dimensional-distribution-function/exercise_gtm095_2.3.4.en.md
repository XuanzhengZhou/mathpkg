# Exercise 4

Let $F^1, \ldots, F^n$ be one-dimensional distribution functions on $R$ and define

$$F_n(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = F^1(x_1) \cdots F^n(x_n).$$

Show that $F_n$ is an $n$-dimensional distribution function and that the corresponding probability measure $P$ on $(R^n, \mathcal{B}(R^n))$ is the product measure $P = P^1 \times \cdots \times P^n$, where each $P^i$ is the probability measure on $(R, \mathcal{B}(R))$ corresponding to $F^i$.
