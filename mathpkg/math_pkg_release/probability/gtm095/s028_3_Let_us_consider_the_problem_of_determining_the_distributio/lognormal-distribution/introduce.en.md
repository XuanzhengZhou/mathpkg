The lognormal distribution arises when a normally distributed random variable is exponentiated: $\eta = e^\xi$ with $\xi \sim \mathcal{N}(m, \sigma^2)$. Its density is supported on the positive real line and is skewed to the right. The lognormal distribution appears naturally in many applications, including financial modeling (stock prices), reliability engineering (failure times), and biology (growth processes), where quantities are products of many small independent factors.
