The density transformation formula is the continuous analog of how probabilities transform under a change of variable. When $\varphi$ is strictly monotone, the formula $f_\eta(y) = f_\xi(h(y))|h'(y)|$ is a direct consequence of the chain rule applied to the distribution function. For non-monotone transformations like $\eta = \xi^2$, the piecewise version sums contributions from each monotone branch. This formula is essential for deriving distributions of derived quantities like $\chi^2$, lognormal, and $F$ distributions.
