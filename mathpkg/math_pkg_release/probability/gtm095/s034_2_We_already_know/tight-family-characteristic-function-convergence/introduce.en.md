This lemma characterizes weak convergence for tight families of probability measures on the real line in terms of pointwise convergence of characteristic functions. The sufficiency direction uses Prohorov's theorem to extract a weakly convergent subsequence, then applies Lemma 1 together with the fact that characteristic functions uniquely determine distributions. The necessity direction follows directly from the definition of weak convergence, since $x \mapsto e^{itx}$ is a bounded continuous function. This lemma bridges tightness and characteristic function convergence, paving the way for the Continuity Theorem.
