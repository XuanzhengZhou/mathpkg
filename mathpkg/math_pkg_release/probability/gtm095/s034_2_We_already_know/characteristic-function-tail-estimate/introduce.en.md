This lemma provides an estimate that bounds the tail mass of a probability distribution in terms of the behavior of its characteristic function near the origin. For any distribution function $F$ with characteristic function $\varphi$, the probability that $|X| \geq 1/a$ is bounded above by $7$ times the average of $1 - \operatorname{Re} \varphi(t)$ over the interval $[0, a]$. The proof applies Fubini's theorem to exchange the order of integration and uses the inequality $1 - \frac{\sin y}{y} \geq 1 - \sin 1 \geq \frac{1}{7}$ for $|y| \geq 1$. This estimate is a crucial ingredient in the proof of conclusion (2) of the Continuity Theorem, where it is used to establish tightness of $\{P_n\}$ from the continuity of the limiting characteristic function at zero.
