The Kolmogorov–Lévy–Khinchin representation gives a complete characterization of the characteristic functions of infinitely divisible distributions. A random variable is infinitely divisible if and only if the logarithm of its characteristic function can be expressed as the sum of a linear term, a Gaussian quadratic term, and an integral with respect to a Lévy measure $\lambda$ that encodes the jump behavior. The integrand uses the compensating factor $\frac{itx}{1+x^2}$ to ensure convergence of the integral at both zero and infinity.
