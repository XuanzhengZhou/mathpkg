# Exercise 11

Let $F$ and $\varphi$ be a distribution function and its characteristic function that are representable as

$$F = F^{(n)} \ast \cdots \ast F^{(n)} \quad \text{($n$ times)}, \qquad \varphi = [\varphi^{(n)}]^n$$

with some distribution functions $F^{(n)}$ and their characteristic functions $\varphi^{(n)}$, $n \geq 1$. Show that there exists a ("rich" enough) probability space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)$ and random variables $T$ and $(\eta_k^{(n)})_{1 \leq k \leq n}$ defined on it, where $T \sim F$ and $\eta_1^{(n)}, \ldots, \eta_n^{(n)}$ are independent and identically distributed with distribution $F^{(n)}$, such that

$$T \stackrel{d}{=} \eta_1^{(n)} + \cdots + \eta_n^{(n)}, \qquad n \geq 1.$$

This exercise shows that the algebraic characterization of infinite divisibility via characteristic functions is equivalent to the probabilistic one via sums of i.i.d. random variables.
