Definition. Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \mathsf{P})$ be a (finite) probability space and let $\xi = (\xi_0, \ldots, \xi_n)$ be a sequence of random variables with values in a (finite) set $X$. If (7) is satisfied, the sequence $\xi = (\xi_0, \ldots, \xi_n)$ is called a (finite) Markov chain. This definition introduces a fundamental concept in probability theory.
