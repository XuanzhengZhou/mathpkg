A collection $\mathcal{A}$ of subsets of a sample space $\Omega$ is called an \text{algebra (of events)} if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Omega \in \mathcal{A}$,
\item If $A \in \mathcal{A}$ and $B \in \mathcal{A}$, then $A \cup B \in \mathcal{A}$, $A \cap B \in \mathcal{A}$, and $A \setminus B \in \mathcal{A}$.
\end{enumerate}
