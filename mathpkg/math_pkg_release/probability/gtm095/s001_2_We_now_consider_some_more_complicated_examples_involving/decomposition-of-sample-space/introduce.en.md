A decomposition (partition) of the sample space breaks $\Omega$ into mutually exclusive, exhaustive pieces called atoms. Every finite algebra of events can be constructed from a decomposition by taking all possible unions of the atoms. This establishes a fundamental bijection between finite algebras of subsets and finite decompositions of the sample space, making decompositions an essential combinatorial tool for building probability models.
