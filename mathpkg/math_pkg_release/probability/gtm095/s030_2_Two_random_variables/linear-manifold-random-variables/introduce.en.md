The linear manifold spanned by a sequence of random variables is the collection of all finite linear combinations. Taking the closure in $L^2$ norm yields the closed linear manifold, which also includes all mean-square limits. This distinction is fundamental in $L^2$-theory: the best linear estimator of a random variable is precisely its orthogonal projection onto the appropriate closed linear manifold, and the projection theorem guarantees existence and uniqueness.
