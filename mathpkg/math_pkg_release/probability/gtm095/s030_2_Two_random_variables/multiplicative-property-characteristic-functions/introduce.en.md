The multiplicative property is the key reason why characteristic functions are the preferred tool for studying sums of independent random variables. While the distribution of a sum is given by the convolution of individual distributions (a complicated operation), the characteristic function simply factorizes. This elegant property underpins the proofs of all classical limit theorems, including the law of large numbers and the central limit theorem.
