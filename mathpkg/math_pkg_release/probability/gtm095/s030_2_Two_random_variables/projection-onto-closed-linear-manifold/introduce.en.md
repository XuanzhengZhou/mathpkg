This is the fundamental projection theorem in the $L^2$-theory of probability. Given a closed linear manifold spanned by an orthonormal system, every square-integrable random variable has a unique orthogonal projection onto that manifold, minimizing mean-square distance. The projection is given by the infinite Fourier series and is precisely the wide-sense conditional expectation. The proof uses the parallelogram law and completeness of $L^2$.
