The indicator function is the simplest bridge between set operations and arithmetic: union corresponds to $1 - \prod(1 - I_{A_i})$, intersection to the product $\prod I_{A_i}$, and complement to $1 - I_A$. This algebraic encoding makes indicator functions the tool of choice for elegant proofs of combinatorial identities like the inclusion-exclusion principle. They also provide the natural connection between events and Bernoulli random variables.
