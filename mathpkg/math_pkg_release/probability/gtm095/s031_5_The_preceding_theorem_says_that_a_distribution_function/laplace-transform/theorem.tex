The Laplace transform of a nonnegative random variable $X$ with distribution function $F$ is the function $\hat{F} = \hat{F}(\lambda)$ defined by

$$\hat{F}(\lambda) = E e^{-\lambda X} = \int_{[0, \infty)} e^{-\lambda x} \, dF(x), \quad \lambda \geq 0.$$

Bernstein's criterion: A function $f = f(\lambda)$ on $(0, \infty)$ is the Laplace transform of a distribution function $F = F(x)$ on $[0, \infty)$ if and only if $f$ is completely monotone, i.e., the derivatives $f^{(n)}(\lambda)$ of all orders $n \geq 0$ exist and $(-1)^n f^{(n)}(\lambda) \geq 0$.
