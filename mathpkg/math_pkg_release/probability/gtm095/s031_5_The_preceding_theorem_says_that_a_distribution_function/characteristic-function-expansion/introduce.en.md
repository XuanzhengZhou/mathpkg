When a random vector has finite absolute moments up to order $n$, its characteristic function admits a Taylor expansion whose coefficients are (up to powers of $i$) the mixed moments. This generalizes Theorem 1 from the one-dimensional case. The existence of the mixed moments follows from Hölder's and Lyapunov's inequalities, and the expansion provides the foundation for the method of moments and the theory of semi-invariants.
