De Morgan's laws describe the duality between union and intersection under complementation. In probabilistic terms, they state that the complement of "A or B occurs" is "neither A nor B occurs," and the complement of "both A and B occur" is "at least one does not occur." These laws are fundamental tools for manipulating event expressions and simplifying probability calculations.
