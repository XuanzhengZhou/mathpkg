Consider an urn containing $M$ balls, of which $M_1$ balls have color $b_1$, $\ldots$, $M_r$ balls have color $b_r$, with $M_1 + \cdots + M_r = M$. Draw a sample of size $n < M$ without replacement. The \text{multidimensional hypergeometric distribution} gives the probability that $n_1$ balls have color $b_1$, $\ldots$, $n_r$ balls have color $b_r$ (with $n_1 + \cdots + n_r = n$):
$$\text{P}(B_{n_1, \ldots, n_r}) = \frac{C_{M_1}^{n_1} C_{M_2}^{n_2} \cdots C_{M_r}^{n_r}}{C_M^n}.$$
