Consider an urn containing $M$ balls, of which $M_1$ are white and $M_2$ are black ($M_1 + M_2 = M$). Draw a sample of size $n < M$ without replacement under the classical assignment of probabilities. The probability that exactly $n_1$ white balls and $n_2 = n - n_1$ black balls are drawn is given by the \text{hypergeometric distribution}:
$$\text{P}(B_{n_1, n_2}) = \frac{C_{M_1}^{n_1} C_{M_2}^{n_2}}{C_M^n}, \quad n_1 + n_2 = n.$$
