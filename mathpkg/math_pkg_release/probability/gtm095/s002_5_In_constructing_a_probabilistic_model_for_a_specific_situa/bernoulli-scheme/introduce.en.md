The Bernoulli scheme is the prototypical model for a sequence of independent trials with two outcomes. By assigning each sequence of successes and failures a probability proportional to $p^{\text{#successes}} q^{\text{#failures}}$, the scheme encodes the idea that trials are independent and identically distributed. It is the probabilistic foundation for the binomial distribution, random walks, and the law of large numbers.
