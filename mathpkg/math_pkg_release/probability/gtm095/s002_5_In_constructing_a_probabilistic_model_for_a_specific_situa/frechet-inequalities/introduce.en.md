Frechet inequalities show that the Gumbel bounds improve monotonically as $m$ increases—the bound using $\tilde{S}_{m+1}$ is never worse than the bound using $\tilde{S}_m$. This provides a systematic way to obtain progressively tighter upper bounds on the probability of a union by considering larger subsets of events, at the cost of computing more complex summands.
