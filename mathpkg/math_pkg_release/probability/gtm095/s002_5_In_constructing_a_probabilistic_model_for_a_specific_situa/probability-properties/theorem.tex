Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \text{P})$ be a discrete probability space. The following properties hold:
\begin{align*}
\text{P}(\varnothing) &= 0, \\
\text{P}(\Omega) &= 1, \\
\text{P}(A \cup B) &= \text{P}(A) + \text{P}(B) - \text{P}(A \cap B), \\
\text{P}(A + B) &= \text{P}(A) + \text{P}(B) \quad \text{if } A \cap B = \varnothing, \\
\text{P}(\overline{A}) &= 1 - \text{P}(A).
\end{align*}
