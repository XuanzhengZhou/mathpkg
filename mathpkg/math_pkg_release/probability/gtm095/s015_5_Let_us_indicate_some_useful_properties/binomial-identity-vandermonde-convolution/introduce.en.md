Vandermonde's identity is proved elegantly using the convolution property of generating functions: since $(1+x)^{n+m} = (1+x)^n(1+x)^m$, equating coefficients of $x^k$ on both sides yields the identity immediately. This proof illustrates the general principle that algebraic identities for generating functions translate into combinatorial identities for their coefficients. The special case $\sum (C_n^k)^2 = C_{2n}^n$ has a natural probabilistic interpretation: it counts the number of ways to choose $n$ items from $2n$.
