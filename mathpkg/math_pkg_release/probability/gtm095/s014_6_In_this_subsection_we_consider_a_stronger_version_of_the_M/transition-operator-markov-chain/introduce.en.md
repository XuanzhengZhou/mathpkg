The transition operator $T$ provides a functional-analytic perspective on Markov chains. It acts on functions defined on the state space by computing their one-step-ahead conditional expectation. The $n$-fold iteration $T^n$ gives the $n$-step conditional expectation. This operator formulation connects Markov chain theory to linear algebra (via matrix representations), functional analysis (via spectral theory), and potential theory (via the relationship $I - T$ to the Green's operator).
