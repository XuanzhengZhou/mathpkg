7 Conditional Probabilities and Conditional Expectations with Respect to a $\sigma$-Algebra

1. Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})$ be a probability space and $A \in \mathcal{F}$ an event such that $\mathbb{P}(A) > 0$. As for finite probability spaces, the conditional probability of $B$ with respect to $A$ (denoted by $\mathbb{P}(B|A))$ means $\mathbb{P}(BA)/\mathbb{P}(A)$, and the conditional probability of $B$ with respect to the finite or countable decomposition $\mathcal{D} = \{D_1, D_2, \ldots\}$ such that $\mathbb{P}(D_i) > 0$, $i \geq 1$ (denoted by $\mathbb{P}(B|\mathcal{D})$) is the random variable equal to $\mathbb{P}(B|D_i)$ for $\omega \in D_i$, $i \geq 1$:

$$\mathbb{P}(B|\mathcal{D}) = \sum_{i \geq 1} \mathbb{P}(B|D_i) I_{D_i}(\omega).$$

In a similar way, if $\xi$ is a random variable for which $\mathbb{E}\xi$ is defined, the conditional expectation of $\xi$ with respect to the event $A$ with $\mathbb{P}(A) > 0$ (denoted by $\mathbb{E}(\xi|A))$ is $\mathbb{E}(\xi I_A)/\mathbb{P}(A)$ (cf. (10) in Sect. 8 of Chap. 1).

The random variable $\mathbb{P}(B|\mathcal{D})$ is evidently measurable with respect to the $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{G} = \sigma(\mathcal{D})$, and is consequently also denoted by $\mathbb{P}(B|\mathcal{G})$ (see Sect. 8 of Chap. 1).

Remark 3. Suppose that $\xi$ is a random variable for which $E\xi$ exists. Then $E(\xi \mid G)$ could be defined as a $G$-measurable function for which (1) holds. This is usually just what happens. Our definition $E(\xi \mid G) \equiv E(\xi^+ \mid G) - E(\xi^- \mid G)$ has the advantage that for the trivial $\sigma$-algebra $G = \{ \emptyset, \Omega \}$ it reduces to the definition of $E\xi$ but does not presuppose the existence of $E\xi$. (For example, if $\xi$ is a random variable with $E\xi^+ = \infty$, $E\xi^- = \infty$, and $G = F$, then $E\xi$ is not defined, but in terms of Definition 1, $E(\xi \mid G)$ exists and is simply $\xi = \xi^+ - \xi^-$).

Remark 4. Let the random variable $\xi$ have a conditional expectation $E(\xi \mid G)$ with respect to the $\sigma$-algebra $G$. The conditional variance $\text{Var}(\xi \mid G)$ of $\xi$ with respect to $G$ is the random variable

$$\text{Var}(\xi \mid G) = E[(\xi - E(\xi \mid G))^2 \mid G].$$
