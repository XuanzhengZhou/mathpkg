This lemma establishes two fundamental properties of the Kakutani--Hellinger framework. First, the Hellinger integral $H(\alpha; P, \tilde{P}) = E_Q[z^\alpha \tilde{z}^{1-\alpha}]$ for $\alpha \in (0,1)$ does not depend on the choice of the dominating measure $Q$, which justifies Definition 3. The proof uses the chain rule for Radon--Nikodym derivatives: if $Q \ll Q'$ with $v = dQ/dQ'$, then $z' = zv$ and $\tilde{z}' = \tilde{z}v$, and the expectation transforms as $E_Q[z^\alpha \tilde{z}^{1-\alpha}] = E_{Q'}[v z^\alpha \tilde{z}^{1-\alpha}] = E_{Q'}[(z')^\alpha (\tilde{z}')^{1-\alpha}]$. Second, $\rho(P,\tilde{P})$ satisfies the metric axioms: positive definiteness, symmetry, and the triangle inequality, making it a genuine distance on the space of probability measures.
