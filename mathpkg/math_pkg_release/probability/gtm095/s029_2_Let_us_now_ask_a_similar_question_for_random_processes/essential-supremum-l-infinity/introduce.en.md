$L^\infty$ consists of essentially bounded random variables -- those bounded except on a null set. The essential supremum $\|\xi\|_\infty$ is the smallest $c$ such that $|\xi| \leq c$ almost surely. Together with $L^p$ for $1 \leq p < \infty$, it completes the family of $L^p$ spaces on a probability space. All $L^p$ spaces for $p \geq 1$ (including $p = \infty$) are Banach spaces.
