This theorem identifies a special case where almost sure convergence implies $L^1$ convergence: when the random variables are nonnegative and their expectations converge to the finite expectation of the limit. The proof uses $E|\xi - \xi_n| = 2E(\xi - \xi_n)I_{\{\xi \geq \xi_n\}} + E(\xi_n - \xi)$ and the dominated convergence theorem. This result is closely related to Scheffé's lemma and is particularly useful in martingale convergence theory.
