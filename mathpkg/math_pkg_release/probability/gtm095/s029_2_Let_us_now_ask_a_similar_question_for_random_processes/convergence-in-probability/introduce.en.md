Convergence in probability is one of the four fundamental modes of convergence for sequences of random variables. It requires that the probability of the deviation $|\xi_n - \xi|$ exceeding any fixed positive threshold $\varepsilon$ tends to zero as $n \to \infty$. This is the probabilistic analogue of convergence in measure from real analysis. Convergence in probability is weaker than almost sure convergence and $L^p$ convergence, but stronger than convergence in distribution. It serves as the natural mode of convergence for the weak law of large numbers.
