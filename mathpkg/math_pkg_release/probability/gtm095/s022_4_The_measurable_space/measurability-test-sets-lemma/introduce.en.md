This lemma dramatically simplifies the verification of measurability: instead of checking the preimage condition for every Borel set, it suffices to check it for any generating system of $\mathcal{B}(R)$. In particular, the half-lines $\{(-\infty, x) : x \in R\}$ or $\{(-\infty, x] : x \in R\}$ suffice, explaining why distribution functions $F(x) = P(\xi \leq x)$ completely characterize probability distributions.
