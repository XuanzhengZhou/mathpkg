If $\xi_1, \xi_2, \ldots$ is a sequence of extended random variables, then $\sup \xi_n$, $\inf \xi_n$, $\limsup \xi_n$ and $\liminf \xi_n$ are also random variables (possibly extended). This follows from

$$\{\omega : \sup \xi_n > x\} = \bigcup_n \{\omega : \xi_n > x\} \in \mathcal{F},$$

and similar relations for $\inf$, $\limsup$, and $\liminf$.

If $\xi$ and $\eta$ are random variables, $\xi + \eta$, $\xi - \eta$, $\xi \eta$, and $\xi/\eta$ are also random variables (assuming no indeterminate forms $\infty - \infty$, $\infty/\infty$, $a/0$ occur). This is shown by approximating $\xi$ and $\eta$ by simple random variables $\{\xi_n\}$ and $\{\eta_n\}$ and using

$$\xi_n \pm \eta_n \to \xi \pm \eta, \quad \xi_n \eta_n \to \xi \eta, \quad \frac{\xi_n}{\eta_n + \frac{1}{n} I_{\{\eta_n=0\}}(\omega)} \to \frac{\xi}{\eta}.$$
