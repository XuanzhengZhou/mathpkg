The expectation of a general random variable is defined by decomposing it into its positive and negative parts: $\xi = \xi^+ - \xi^-$. Each part is nonnegative, so their expectations are already defined. The overall expectation exists when at least one of $E\xi^+$ and $E\xi^-$ is finite, avoiding the indeterminate form $\infty - \infty$. This definition makes the Lebesgue integral a conditionally convergent integral in the sense that $E\xi = E\xi^+ - E\xi^-$.
