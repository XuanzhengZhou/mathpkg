Definition 1. Any numerical function $\xi = \xi(\omega)$ defined on a (finite) sample space $\Omega$ is called a (simple) random variable. (The reason for the term “simple” random variable will become clear after the introduction of the general concept of random variable in Sect. 4, Chap. 2). This definition introduces a fundamental concept in probability theory.
