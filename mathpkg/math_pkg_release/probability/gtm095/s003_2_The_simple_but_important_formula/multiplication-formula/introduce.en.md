The multiplication formula (also called the chain rule of probability) expresses the probability of an intersection of events as a telescoping product of conditional probabilities. Starting from $\text{P}(A_1)$ and successively conditioning on more events, it provides a systematic way to decompose joint probabilities. This formula is essential for sequential models, Bayesian networks, and any setting where events occur in a temporal or logical order.
