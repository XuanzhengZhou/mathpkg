# Exercise 2

An urn contains $M$ balls, of which $M_1$ are white. Consider a sample of size $n$. Let $B_j$ be the event that the ball selected at the $j$th step is white, and $A_k$ the event that a sample of size $n$ contains exactly $k$ white balls. Show that

$$\mathbf{P}(B_j \mid A_k) = \frac{k}{n}$$

both for sampling with replacement and for sampling without replacement.
