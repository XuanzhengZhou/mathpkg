The derangement numbers satisfy a simple linear recurrence relation that can be proved combinatorially by considering the position of the element $n$ in a derangement. This recurrence provides an alternative method for computing $D_n$ and can be used, together with exponential generating functions, to re-derive the closed-form formula $D_n = n! \sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k/k!$.
