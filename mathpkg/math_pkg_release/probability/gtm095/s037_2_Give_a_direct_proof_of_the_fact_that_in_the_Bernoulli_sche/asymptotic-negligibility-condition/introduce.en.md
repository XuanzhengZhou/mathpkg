The condition of asymptotic negligibility requires that no single term in a triangular array of independent random variables carries non-negligible probability mass away from zero. This is a classical requirement for limit theorems: together with Lindeberg's condition it ensures the Central Limit Theorem. When this condition is violated, one enters the realm of "nonclassical" limit theorems, where condition $(\Lambda)$ provides an alternative path to asymptotic normality.
