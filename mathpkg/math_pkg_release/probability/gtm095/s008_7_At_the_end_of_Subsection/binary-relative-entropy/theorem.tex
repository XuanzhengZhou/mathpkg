For $a, p \in (0,1)$, the binary relative entropy function $H(a)$ (also called the Kullback-Leibler divergence for Bernoulli distributions) is defined by
$$
H(a) = a \log\frac{a}{p} + (1 - a) \log\frac{1 - a}{1 - p}.
$$
It is the Fenchel-Legendre transform of the cumulant generating function $\log \varphi(s) = \log(1 - p + p e^s)$:
$$
H(a) = \sup_{s \in \mathbb{R}} [as - \log(1 - p + p e^s)].
$$
For $p \leq a \leq 1$, the supremum is positive and is attained at $s_0$ solving $e^{s_0} = \frac{a(1-p)}{p(1-a)}$.
