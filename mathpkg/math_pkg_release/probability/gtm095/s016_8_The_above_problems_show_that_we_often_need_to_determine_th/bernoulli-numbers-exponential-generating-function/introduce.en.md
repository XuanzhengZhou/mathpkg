Bernoulli numbers are a sequence of rational numbers that appear in many areas of mathematics, including number theory, combinatorics, and probability. They are defined compactly through their exponential generating function $x/(e^x - 1)$. The odd-indexed Bernoulli numbers $b_{2k+1}$ vanish for $k \geq 1$. Bernoulli numbers arise in the series expansions of trigonometric and hyperbolic functions, in the Euler–Maclaurin summation formula, and in connection with the Riemann zeta function.
