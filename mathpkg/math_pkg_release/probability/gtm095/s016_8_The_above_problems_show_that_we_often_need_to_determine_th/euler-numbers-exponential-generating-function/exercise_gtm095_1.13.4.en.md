# Exercise 4

Let $a_1, \ldots, a_k$ be different positive numbers. Show that the number $N(k; n)$ of partitions of the number $n$ into a sum of the numbers from $\{a_1, \ldots, a_k\}$ is the coefficient of $x^n$ in the series expansion of the product

$$\left(1 + x^{a_1} + x^{2a_1} + \cdots\right)\left(1 + x^{a_2} + x^{2a_2} + \cdots\right) \cdots \left(1 + x^{a_k} + x^{2a_k} + \cdots\right).$$

(The partitions allow for repeated $a_i$'s, e.g., $8 = 2 + 2 + 2 + 2 = 3 + 3 + 2$, but the order of summation is immaterial, i.e., the representations $3 + 2$ and $2 + 3$ of the number 5 are counted as the same.)
