Consider the Hamiltonian $H(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}, t)$ along a trajectory of Hamilton's equations. Then

$$\frac{dH}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial t}.$$

In particular, for a system whose Hamiltonian function does not depend explicitly on time ($\partial H/\partial t = 0$), the law of conservation of the Hamiltonian holds:

$$H(\mathbf{p}(t), \mathbf{q}(t)) = \text{const}.$$
