This lemma states that for any quadratic form $f$, the values of $f$ and its Legendre conjugate $g$ coincide at corresponding points (where $\mathbf{p} = \partial f/\partial \mathbf{x}$). This property follows from Euler's theorem on homogeneous functions and is crucial for identifying the Hamiltonian with the total mechanical energy $H = T + U$ in classical mechanics, where the kinetic energy $T$ is a quadratic form in the velocities.
