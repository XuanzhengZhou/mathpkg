Phase space is the $2n$-dimensional space whose coordinates are the $n$ generalized positions $q_i$ and $n$ conjugate momenta $p_i$ of a Hamiltonian system. Every point in phase space uniquely determines the state of the mechanical system. Hamilton's equations define a vector field on phase space whose integral curves are the trajectories of the system. Phase space is the natural geometric setting for Hamiltonian mechanics and later acquires the structure of a symplectic manifold.
