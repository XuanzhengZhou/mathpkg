The $2n$-dimensional space with coordinates $p_1, \ldots, p_n; q_1, \ldots, q_n$ is called phase space. The right-hand sides of Hamilton's equations define a vector field on phase space: at each point $(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q})$ there is a $2n$-dimensional vector $(-\partial H/\partial \mathbf{q}, \partial H/\partial \mathbf{p})$.
