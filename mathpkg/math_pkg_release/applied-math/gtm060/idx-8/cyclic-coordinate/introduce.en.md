A cyclic coordinate is a generalized coordinate that does not appear explicitly in the Hamiltonian (or Lagrangian) function of a mechanical system. The name derives from the prototypical example of the angular coordinate in a central field problem. Cyclic coordinates correspond to symmetries of the system and give rise to conserved quantities (first integrals), enabling dimensional reduction of the equations of motion. Almost all exactly solvable problems in classical mechanics are solved by exploiting cyclic coordinates.
