If a coordinate $q_1$ is cyclic, i.e., $\partial H/\partial q_1 = 0$, then the corresponding conjugate momentum is conserved:

$$p_1 = \text{const}.$$

The equation for $q_1$ then reduces to

$$\frac{d}{dt} q_1 = f(t), \quad \text{where} \quad f(t) = \frac{\partial}{\partial p_1} H(p_1, \mathbf{p}'(t), \mathbf{q}'(t), t),$$

and is easily integrated once the reduced system of $2n-2$ equations for the remaining variables is solved.
