Choose an inertial coordinate system in which the center of mass is at rest. By the theorem on uniform linear motion, the center of mass has constant velocity. Passing to a coordinate system moving with that constant velocity (a Galilean transformation), the center of mass becomes stationary. In this frame, the center of mass acts as a fixed point $O$ about which the body rotates. The problem of free rigid body motion thus reduces to the study of a rigid body rotating about a fixed point, which has three degrees of freedom (the configuration manifold is $\mathrm{SO}(3)$).
