The inertia operator $A$ maps the angular velocity vector $\Omega$ in the body frame $K$ to the angular momentum vector $\mathbf{M} = m[\mathbf{Q}, [\Omega, \mathbf{Q}]]$ for a point mass $m$ at position $\mathbf{Q}$. The symmetry $(A\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}) = (\mathbf{X}, A\mathbf{Y})$ follows from the vector triple product identity $([\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}], \mathbf{c}) = ([\mathbf{c}, \mathbf{a}], \mathbf{b})$, which reveals that $(A\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}) = m([\mathbf{Y}, \mathbf{Q}], [\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Q}])$ is symmetric in $\mathbf{X}$ and $\mathbf{Y}$. This symmetry is fundamental: it guarantees the inertia operator can be diagonalized by an orthogonal basis -- the principal axes.
