The inertia ellipsoid is a geometric representation of the mass distribution of a rigid body at a point $O$. It is defined as the set of angular velocity vectors $\Omega$ whose associated kinetic energy is $\frac{1}{2}$: $\{\Omega : (A\Omega, \Omega) = 1\}$. In the principal axis coordinates, the ellipsoid equation is $I_1\Omega_1^2 + I_2\Omega_2^2 + I_3\Omega_3^2 = 1$, with semi-axes of lengths $1/\sqrt{I_1}, 1/\sqrt{I_2}, 1/\sqrt{I_3}$. The shape of the inertia ellipsoid reflects the body's geometry: elongation along an axis corresponds to small moment of inertia about that axis, so the ellipsoid is stretched in that direction and may resemble the body's shape.
