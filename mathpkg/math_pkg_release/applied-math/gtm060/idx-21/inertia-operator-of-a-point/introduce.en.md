The inertia operator $A$ encodes the distribution of mass of a rigid body relative to a fixed point $O$. For a single point mass at $\mathbf{Q}$, $A$ is the symmetric linear transformation such that $A\Omega$ is the angular momentum contributed by that point when the body rotates with angular velocity $\Omega$. The total inertia operator of an extended body is the sum of the inertia operators of its constituent point masses. In coordinates, the matrix of $A$ is the familiar moment of inertia tensor, whose diagonal entries are the moments of inertia and off-diagonal entries are the negative products of inertia.
