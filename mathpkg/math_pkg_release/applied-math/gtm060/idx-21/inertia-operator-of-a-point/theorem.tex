The symmetric operator $A: K \to K$ defined by

$$A\Omega = \mathbf{M} = m[\mathbf{Q}, [\Omega, \mathbf{Q}]]$$

for a point mass $m$ at position $\mathbf{Q}$ in the body frame $K$ is called the inertia operator (or inertia tensor) of the point $\mathbf{Q}$. For an extended body, the total inertia operator is $A = \sum_i A_i$, where $A_i$ is the inertia operator of the $i$-th point $\mathbf{Q}_i$ with mass $m_i$.
