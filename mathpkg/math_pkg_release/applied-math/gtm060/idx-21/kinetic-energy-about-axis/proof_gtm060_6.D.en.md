Let the angular velocity be $\Omega = \Omega \mathbf{e}$ with $|\mathbf{e}| = 1$. For a point mass $m_i$ at position $\mathbf{q}_i$ in space, the velocity is:

$$\mathbf{v}_i = [\omega, \mathbf{q}_i] = \Omega [\mathbf{e}, \mathbf{q}_i].$$

The magnitude is $v_i = \Omega \, |[\mathbf{e}, \mathbf{q}_i]| = \Omega \, r_i$, where $r_i$ is the perpendicular distance from the $i$-th point to the axis. Therefore:

$$T = \sum_i \frac{1}{2} m_i v_i^2 = \sum_i \frac{1}{2} m_i \Omega^2 r_i^2 = \frac{1}{2} \left( \sum_i m_i r_i^2 \right) \Omega^2 = \frac{1}{2} I_e \Omega^2,$$

where $I_e = \sum_i m_i r_i^2$ is the scalar moment of inertia about the $\mathbf{e}$ axis.
