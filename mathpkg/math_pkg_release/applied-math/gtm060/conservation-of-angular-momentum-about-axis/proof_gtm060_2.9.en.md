$M_z = (e_z, [r, \dot{r}])$. Since $\ddot{r} = F$ and $r$, $\ddot{r}$ lie in a plane passing through the $z$ axis (by axial symmetry), $[r, \ddot{r}]$ is perpendicular to $e_z$. Therefore,
$$\dot{M}_z = (e_z, [\dot{r}, \dot{r}]) + (e_z, [r, \ddot{r}]) = 0.$$
