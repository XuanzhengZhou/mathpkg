This theorem describes the asymptotic motion of a rapidly spinning Lagrange top released from rest at an initial inclination $\theta_0$. As the angular velocity $\omega_3$ about the body axis tends to infinity, the nutation frequency grows proportionally to $\omega_3$, while the nutation amplitude decays like $1/\omega_3^2$, and the precession frequency decays like $1/\omega_3$. The asymptotic formulas express these three quantities in terms of the moments of inertia $I_1, I_3$, the mass $m$, the distance $l$ from the fixed point to the center of mass, and the gravitational acceleration $g$. The proof reduces the problem to the case $g \to 0$ via a similarity argument and uses a perturbation lemma to locate the minimum of the effective potential energy.
