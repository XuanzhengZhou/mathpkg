The dependence of $r$ on $t$ is given by the quadrature
$$\dot{r} = \sqrt{2(E - V(r))}, \quad \int dt = \int \frac{dr}{\sqrt{2(E - V(r))}}.$$

Since $\dot{\varphi} = M/r^2$, the equation of the orbit in polar coordinates is found by quadrature:
$$\varphi = \int \frac{M/r^2 \, dr}{\sqrt{2(E - V(r))}}.$$
