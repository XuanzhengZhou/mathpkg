The inequality $V(r) \leq E$ gives one or several annular regions in the plane:

$$0 \leq r_{\min} \leq r \leq r_{\max} \leq \infty.$$

If $0 \leq r_{\min} < r_{\max} < \infty$, then the motion is bounded and takes place inside the ring between the circles of radius $r_{\min}$ and $r_{\max}$.

The points where $r = r_{\min}$ are called pericentral, and where $r = r_{\max}$, apocentral. Each of the rays leading from the center to the apocenter or to the pericenter is an axis of symmetry of the orbit.
