Assume planets move in a central field and their orbits are ellipses with the center of force at a focus (Kepler's first law). As shown in Section 2D, if the force law is $U(r) = -k/r$ (Newton's law), then bounded orbits are precisely ellipses with the force center at one focus. Conversely, if we observe that bounded orbits are ellipses with the force center at a focus, then the central field must be of the form $U(r) = -k/r$. This follows because the focal equation $r = p/(1 + e \cos \varphi)$ is derived from the conservation laws under the potential $U = -k/r$, and the functional form of the orbit uniquely determines the potential up to an additive constant for a central field.
