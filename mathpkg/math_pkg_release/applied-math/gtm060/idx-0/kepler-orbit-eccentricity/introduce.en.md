The eccentricity $e = \sqrt{1 + 2EM^2/k^2}$ relates the orbital shape to the conserved quantities $E$ and $M$. For bounded motion ($E < 0$), the eccentricity is strictly less than 1, yielding an elliptic orbit. For $E \geq 0$, the motion is unbounded with $e \geq 1$, corresponding to parabolic and hyperbolic escape trajectories.
