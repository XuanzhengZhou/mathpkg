Consider the equation $\ddot{x} = -\omega^2(1 + \varepsilon a(t))x$ with periodic $a(t)$ and the corresponding linear Hamiltonian system on the phase plane $\{(x_1, x_2)\}$ with $H = \frac{1}{2}\omega^2 x_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}x_2^2$.

The mapping $A$ at period $T$ is linear and preserves area ($\det A = 1$). The trivial solution of the original equation is stable if and only if the mapping $A$ is stable.
