This corollary combines the trace stability criterion with the definition of strong stability: when $|\operatorname{tr} A| < 2$, the map $A$ is a rotation (stable), and by continuity of the trace, any sufficiently close system $A'$ will also satisfy $|\operatorname{tr} A'| < 2$ and thus also be stable. This simple observation is the key to establishing structural stability of Hamiltonian periodic systems away from the resonance points.
