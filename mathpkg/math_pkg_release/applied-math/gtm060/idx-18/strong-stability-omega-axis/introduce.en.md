This theorem characterizes the stability boundary for the parametrically excited oscillator $\ddot{x} = -\omega^2(1 + \varepsilon a(t))x$. When $\varepsilon = 0$ (constant coefficients), the system is strongly stable for all $\omega$ except the resonant values $\omega = k/2$. At those exceptional points the trace satisfies $|\operatorname{tr} A| = 2$, putting them on the boundary of the stability region, where parametric resonance can cause instability for arbitrarily small $\varepsilon > 0$. The fact that instability tongues touch the $\omega$-axis only at half-integer frequencies explains the experimental observation that a swing is most efficiently excited by modulating its length at twice the natural frequency.
