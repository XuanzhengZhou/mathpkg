This theorem establishes the fundamental relationship between continuous-time periodic differential equations and discrete dynamical systems via the mapping at a period. The four properties connect periodic orbits to fixed points, stability to discrete stability, system linearity to linearity of the map, and Hamiltonian structure to volume preservation. This reduction is the cornerstone of Floquet theory and parametric resonance analysis.
