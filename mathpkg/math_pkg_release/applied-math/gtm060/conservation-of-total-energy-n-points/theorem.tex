\textbf{Theorem.} The total energy of a conservative system $E = T + U$ is preserved under the motion: $E(t_1) = E(t_0)$.

For pairwise central interactions $f_{ij}(|r_i - r_j|) e_{ij}$ that are conservative, the potential energy of interaction of all points is
$$U(r) = \sum_{i>j} U_{ij}(|r_i - r_j|), \quad \text{where } U_{ij}(r) = \int_{r_0}^{r} f_{ij}(\rho) d\rho.$$

If external forces are also conservative, $F'_i = -\partial U'_i/\partial r_i$, then the total potential energy is
$$U(r) = \sum_{i>j} U_{ij} + \sum_i U'_i,$$
and the total mechanical energy $E = T + U = \sum_i \frac{\dot{r}_i^2}{2} + \sum_{i>j} U_{ij} + \sum_i U'_i$ is conserved.
