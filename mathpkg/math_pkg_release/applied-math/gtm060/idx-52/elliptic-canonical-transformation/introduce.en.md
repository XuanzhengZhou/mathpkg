An elliptic canonical transformation is an area-preserving map of the plane fixing the origin, whose linearization is a rotation by some angle $\alpha$. In a suitable symplectic basis, the eigenvalues of the linear part are $e^{\pm i\alpha}$, purely on the unit circle. Elliptic transformations are the fundamental objects for studying stability of fixed points in Hamiltonian dynamics with two degrees of freedom via the surface-of-section method.
