In the reduction to Birkhoff normal form, to eliminate a term of order $N$ corresponding to the monomial $z_1^{\alpha_1}\cdots z_n^{\alpha_n} \bar{z}_1^{\beta_1}\cdots \bar{z}_n^{\beta_n}$ with $|\alpha| + |\beta| = N$, one must divide by the quantity
$$\omega_1(\alpha_1 - \beta_1) + \cdots + \omega_n(\alpha_n - \beta_n).$$

For time-periodic systems, the corresponding expression is
$$k_0 + \omega_1(\beta_1 - \alpha_1) + \cdots + \omega_n(\beta_n - \alpha_n), \quad |\alpha| + |\beta| = N,$$
where $k_0 \in \mathbb{Z}$ is the Fourier harmonic index.

A **small denominator** is such an expression that, while not identically zero, becomes very small for parameter values close to resonance. Division by a small denominator causes the normalizing transformation to have poles at resonant parameter values, and the region of validity of the normal form shrinks to zero at resonance.
