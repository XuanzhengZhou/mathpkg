A Hamiltonian system is resonant of order $K$ if there exists an integer linear relation among the characteristic frequencies with total weight $K$ (plus possibly a constant term $k_0$ for time-periodic systems). Resonances are obstructions to the Birkhoff normal form procedure: at a resonance of order $K$, the small denominator $\sum k_l\omega_l + k_0$ vanishes, preventing the elimination of the corresponding term in the Hamiltonian. Low-order resonances are especially dangerous because they affect the leading terms of the Taylor expansion.
