Theorem 3 generalizes Theorem 2 to $n$ degrees of freedom. The multi-dimensional elliptic transformation is a direct product of $n$ planar elliptic rotations with eigenvalues $\lambda_l = e^{\pm i\alpha_l}$. The non-resonance condition is that no product $\lambda_1^{k_1}\cdots\lambda_n^{k_n}$ equals $1$ for small total exponent sum $|k_1|+\cdots+|k_n| \leq s$. Under this condition, the map can be brought to the normal form $(\tau,\varphi) \mapsto (\tau, \varphi + \partial S/\partial\tau)$ with a polynomial $S$ in the action variables.
