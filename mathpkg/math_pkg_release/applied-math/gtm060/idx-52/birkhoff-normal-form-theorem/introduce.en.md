The Birkhoff normal form theorem states that near a non-resonant equilibrium, a Hamiltonian system can be transformed by a canonical change of variables into a polynomial normal form depending only on action variables, up to a remainder of arbitrarily high order. The condition is that the characteristic frequencies admit no resonance relations up to the desired order $s$. This is a fundamental result in Hamiltonian perturbation theory, reducing the local dynamics to an integrable approximation.
