In the reduction to normal form, the **resonant terms** are those terms in the Taylor expansion of the Hamiltonian for which the corresponding small denominator vanishes at resonance. Equivalently, resonant terms are those monomials that are constant along trajectories of the periodic motion obtained by disregarding the frequency deviation and nonlinearity.

For a resonance $\omega = p/q$, a term with exponents $(\alpha, \beta)$ and Fourier harmonic $k$ is resonant if
$$\omega(\alpha - \beta) + k = 0$$
at the resonant frequency. These terms cannot be eliminated by canonical transformations and must be retained in the normal form. Near resonance, the corresponding small denominator is not zero but very small, and preserving these resonant terms (even away from exact resonance) yields a normal form that varies continuously with parameters.
