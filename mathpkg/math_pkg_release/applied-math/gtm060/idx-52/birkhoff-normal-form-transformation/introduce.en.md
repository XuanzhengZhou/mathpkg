The Birkhoff normal form of a canonical transformation describes the map as a rotation whose angle depends polynomially on the action variable $\tau$. In one degree of freedom, it takes the form $(\tau, \varphi) \mapsto (\tau, \varphi + \alpha_0 + \alpha_1\tau + \cdots + \alpha_m\tau^m)$ in canonical polar coordinates. The coefficient $\alpha_0$ is the rotation angle of the linearization, while higher coefficients describe the nonlinear twist. In the multi-dimensional case, the angle shift is the gradient of a polynomial $S(\tau_1,\ldots,\tau_n)$.
