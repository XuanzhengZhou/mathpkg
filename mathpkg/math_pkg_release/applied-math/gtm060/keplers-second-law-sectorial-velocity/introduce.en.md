Kepler's second law states that a planet sweeps out equal areas in equal times. Mathematically, this means the sectorial velocity $dS/dt = \frac{1}{2}r^2\dot{\varphi}$ is constant. This is a direct consequence of angular momentum conservation: since $M = r^2\dot{\varphi}$ is constant, the rate at which area is swept is constant. Kepler discovered this empirically; Newton showed it follows from the central nature of the gravitational force.
