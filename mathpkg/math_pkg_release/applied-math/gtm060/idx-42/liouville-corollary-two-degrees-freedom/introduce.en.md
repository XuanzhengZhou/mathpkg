This corollary is the most immediately applicable consequence of Liouville's theorem. For a Hamiltonian system with two degrees of freedom (four-dimensional phase space), finding a single additional first integral $F$ independent of the hamiltonian $H$ guarantees integrability. Since $H$ and $F$ are automatically in involution (as $F$ is a first integral), the two functions provide the $n=2$ commuting integrals required by Liouville's theorem. Many classical mechanical systems — such as the Kepler problem, the harmonic oscillator, and the Euler top — are integrable precisely via this mechanism.
