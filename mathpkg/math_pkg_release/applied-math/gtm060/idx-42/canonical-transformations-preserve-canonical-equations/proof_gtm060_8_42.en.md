**Proof.** The variation of the hamiltonian function was computed using only an infinitesimal canonical transformation and the symplectic structure of $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ — the form $\omega^2$. Specifically, for an infinitesimal canonical transformation with hamiltonian function $H$, the geometric meaning of $H$ is captured by the limit

$$\lim_{\varepsilon \to 0} \frac{1}{\varepsilon} \iint_{\sigma(\varepsilon)} \omega^2 = H(x) - H(y),$$

which depends only on the symplectic form $\omega^2$ and not on the choice of canonical coordinates. Since $\omega^2$ is invariant under canonical transformations, the hamiltonian function $H$ transforms as a scalar, and the form of Hamilton's equations is preserved.
