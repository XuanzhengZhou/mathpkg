This corollary reinforces the geometric meaning of Hamilton's function $H$ as an invariant associated to the infinitesimal canonical transformation. The proof uses only the symplectic structure of $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ (the form $\omega^2$) and the infinitesimal canonical transformation, showing that the variation of the hamiltonian function is determined geometrically. As a result, Hamilton's equations are covariant under canonical transformations: the form of the equations is preserved and the hamiltonian function itself is unchanged.
