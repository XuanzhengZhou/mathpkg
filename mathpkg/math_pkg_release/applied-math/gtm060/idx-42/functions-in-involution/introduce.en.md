The notion of functions being in involution is central to the theory of integrable Hamiltonian systems. Two functions on a symplectic manifold are in involution if their Poisson bracket vanishes identically. This condition generalizes the concept of first integrals: a function $F$ is a first integral of a Hamiltonian system with hamiltonian $H$ precisely when $(H, F) \equiv 0$, meaning $H$ and $F$ are in involution. Liouville's theorem on integrable systems requires $n$ independent first integrals in involution on a $2n$-dimensional phase space to guarantee integrability by quadratures.
