Generalized coordinates are any set of independent parameters that completely specify the configuration of a mechanical system. They need not be Cartesian; they can be angles, distances along curves, or any other convenient variables. The power of the Lagrangian formalism lies in the fact that the equations of motion take the same form $\frac{d}{dt}\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} = \frac{\partial L}{\partial q}$ in any coordinate system.
