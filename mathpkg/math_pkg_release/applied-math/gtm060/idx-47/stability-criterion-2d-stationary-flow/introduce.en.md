This theorem provides concrete sufficient conditions for the Lyapunov stability of stationary planar flows of an ideal fluid. It expresses the second variation of the kinetic energy in terms of the stream function $\psi$, and shows that if the ratio $\nabla \psi / \nabla \Delta \psi$ is bounded between positive constants, then the stationary flow is stable. The theorem yields explicit perturbation bounds: the $L^2$ norms of the perturbation gradient and Laplacian remain controlled by their initial values. This is a direct application of Arnold's general stability method (Theorems 7-9) to the infinite-dimensional setting of the diffeomorphism group, with the crucial note that the stability is strict Lyapunov stability (not just linear stability), which is nontrivial for Hamiltonian systems where asymptotic stability is impossible.
