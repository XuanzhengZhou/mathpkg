In the two-dimensional simply connected case, two velocity fields $v_1$ and $v_2$ with scalar vorticities $r_1$ and $r_2$ are isovorticial if and only if the functions $r_1$ and $r_2$ are carried to one another under a suitable volume-preserving diffeomorphism. Equivalently, they have the same distribution function:
$$\operatorname{mes}\{x \in D: r_1(x) \leq c\} = \operatorname{mes}\{x \in D: r_2(x) \leq c\},$$
for every real number $c$.

Consequently, if two fields belong to the same coadjoint orbit, then all moments of the vorticity are equal:
$$\int_D r_1^k \, dS = \int_D r_2^k \, dS,$$
for every integer $k \geq 1$.
