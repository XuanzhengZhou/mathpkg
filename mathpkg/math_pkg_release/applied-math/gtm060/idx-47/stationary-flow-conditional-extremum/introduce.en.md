This theorem is a special case of Arnold's general variational principle for Lie groups applied to the group of volume-preserving diffeomorphisms. It states that stationary flows of an ideal fluid are precisely the critical points of the kinetic energy functional restricted to a coadjoint orbit (the set of fields isovorticial to the stationary flow). This variational characterization is the foundation for a powerful stability theory: if the critical point is a non-degenerate local extremum, then the stationary flow is Lyapunov stable. The second variation of the kinetic energy determines stability, analogous to how the second derivative test works in finite-dimensional calculus of variations.
