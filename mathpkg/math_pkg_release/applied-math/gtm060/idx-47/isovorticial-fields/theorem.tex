Let $D$ be a region in a three-dimensional Riemannian manifold. Let $v_1$ and $v_2$ be two divergence-free vector fields (velocities of an incompressible fluid) in $D$. The fields $v_1$ and $v_2$ are called **isovorticial** if there exists a volume-preserving diffeomorphism $g: D \to D$ which carries every closed contour $\gamma$ in $D$ to a contour $g \circ \gamma$ such that the circulation of $v_1$ around $\gamma$ equals the circulation of $v_2$ around $g \circ \gamma$:
$$\oint_{\gamma} v_1 = \oint_{g \circ \gamma} v_2.$$
