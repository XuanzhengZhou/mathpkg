If two fields of velocities of a three-dimensional ideal fluid on a region $D$ are isovorticial, then the corresponding volume-preserving diffeomorphism $g: D \to D$ carries the curl of the first field into the curl of the second:
$$g_* \operatorname{curl} v_1 = \operatorname{curl} v_2,$$
where $g_*$ denotes the pushforward by $g$.

Furthermore, if the region of the flow is simply connected, the isovorticity of two fields is equivalent to the equivalence of their fields of vorticity under a volume-preserving diffeomorphism.
