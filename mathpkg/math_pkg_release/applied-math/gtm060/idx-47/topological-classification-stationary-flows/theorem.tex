Assume that the region $D$ is a compact three-dimensional Riemannian manifold with boundary on which a stationary flow of an ideal fluid is given. Assume that the velocity field $v$ and its vorticity $\operatorname{curl} v$ are not collinear and are analytic. Then the flow lines lie on surfaces (Bernoulli surfaces).

On a closed Bernoulli surface, the fields of velocity and vorticity commute, so the group $\mathbb{R}^2$ acts on the closed Bernoulli surface. Consequently, the closed Bernoulli surface must be a torus (cf. the proof of Liouville's theorem). Non-closed Bernoulli surfaces consist of annuli with closed flow lines, determined by the boundary conditions on the boundary of $D$.
