Let $v_1$ and $v_2$ be two divergence-free vector fields on a two-dimensional oriented region $D$, with stream functions $\psi_1$ and $\psi_2$ respectively. Then the stream function of the commutator $[v_1, v_2]$ is given by the Jacobian (Poisson bracket) of the stream functions:
$$\psi_{[v_1, v_2]} = J(\psi_1, \psi_2),$$
where $J(\psi_1, \psi_2) = \frac{\partial \psi_1}{\partial x} \frac{\partial \psi_2}{\partial y} - \frac{\partial \psi_1}{\partial y} \frac{\partial \psi_2}{\partial x}$ is the Jacobian determinant (or Poisson bracket in the Hamiltonian formalism).
