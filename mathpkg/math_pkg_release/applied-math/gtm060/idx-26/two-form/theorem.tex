An exterior form of degree $2$ (or a $2$-form) is a function on pairs of vectors $\omega^2: \mathbb{R}^n \times \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$, which is bilinear and skew symmetric:
$$\omega^2(\lambda_1 \xi_1 + \lambda_2 \xi_2, \xi_3) = \lambda_1 \omega^2(\xi_1, \xi_3) + \lambda_2 \omega^2(\xi_2, \xi_3)$$
$$\omega^2(\xi_1, \xi_2) = -\omega^2(\xi_2, \xi_1),$$
$$\forall \lambda_1, \lambda_2 \in \mathbb{R}, \ \xi_1, \xi_2, \xi_3 \in \mathbb{R}^n.$$
