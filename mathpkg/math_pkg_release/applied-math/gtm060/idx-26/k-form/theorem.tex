An exterior form of degree $k$, or a $k$-form, is a function of $k$ vectors which is $k$-linear and antisymmetric:
$$\omega(\lambda_1 \xi'_1 + \lambda_2 \xi''_1, \xi_2, \ldots, \xi_k) = \lambda_1 \omega(\xi'_1, \xi_2, \ldots, \xi_k) + \lambda_2 \omega(\xi''_1, \xi_2, \ldots, \xi_k)$$
$$\omega(\xi_{i_1}, \ldots, \xi_{i_k}) = (-1)^\nu \omega(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_k),$$
where $\nu = 0$ if the permutation $(i_1, \ldots, i_k)$ is even and $\nu = 1$ if the permutation is odd.
