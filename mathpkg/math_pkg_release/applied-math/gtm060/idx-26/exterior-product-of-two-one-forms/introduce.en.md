The exterior product (or wedge product) of two $1$-forms $\omega_1 \wedge \omega_2$ produces a $2$-form by taking the determinant of their values on the two input vectors. Geometrically, $(\omega_1 \wedge \omega_2)(\xi_1, \xi_2)$ is the oriented area of the image of the parallelogram spanned by $\xi_1, \xi_2$ under the map $\xi \mapsto (\omega_1(\xi), \omega_2(\xi))$. The exterior product is bilinear and skew-symmetric: $\omega_1 \wedge \omega_2 = -\omega_2 \wedge \omega_1$.
