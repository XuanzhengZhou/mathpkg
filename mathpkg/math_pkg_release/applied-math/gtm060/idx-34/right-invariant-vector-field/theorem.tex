Let $G$ be a Lie group. Right translation $R_g$ is the map $R_g: G \to G$, $R_g h = hg$. The differential of $R_g$ at the point $e$ maps $TG_e$ into $TG_g$. Every vector $A \in TG_e$ corresponds to a vector field on the group consisting of the right translations $(R_g)_* A$, called a right-invariant vector field. A right-invariant vector field on a group is uniquely determined by its value at the identity.
