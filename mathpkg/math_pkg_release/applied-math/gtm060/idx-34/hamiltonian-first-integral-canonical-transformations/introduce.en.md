This theorem generalizes Emmy Noether's classical result to the context of symplectic manifolds. It states that if a Hamiltonian $H$ is invariant under the one-parameter group of canonical transformations generated by another Hamiltonian $F$, then $F$ is a conserved quantity (first integral) of the dynamics governed by $H$. The proof follows immediately from the skew-symmetry of the Poisson bracket.
