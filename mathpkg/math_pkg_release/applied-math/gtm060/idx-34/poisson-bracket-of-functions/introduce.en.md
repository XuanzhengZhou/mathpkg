The Poisson bracket of two smooth functions on a symplectic manifold is defined as the Lie derivative of one function along the hamiltonian vector field generated by the other. This bilinear operation endows the space of smooth functions with the structure of a Lie algebra. The Poisson bracket is a fundamental object in Hamiltonian mechanics, where it governs the time evolution of observables and encodes the symplectic structure of phase space.
