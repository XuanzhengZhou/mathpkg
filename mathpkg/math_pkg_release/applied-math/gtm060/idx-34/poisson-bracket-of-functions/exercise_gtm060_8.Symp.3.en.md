Show that the map $A: \mathbb{R}^{2n} \to \mathbb{R}^{2n}$ sending $(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}) \to (\mathbf{P}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}), \mathbf{Q}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}))$ is canonical if and only if the Poisson brackets of any two functions in the variables $(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q})$ and $(\mathbf{P}, \mathbf{Q})$ coincide:

$$(F, H)_{\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q}} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{p}} \frac{\partial F}{\partial \mathbf{q}} - \frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{q}} \frac{\partial F}{\partial \mathbf{p}} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{P}} \frac{\partial F}{\partial \mathbf{Q}} - \frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{Q}} \frac{\partial F}{\partial \mathbf{P}} = (F, H)_{\mathbf{P}, \mathbf{Q}}.$$
