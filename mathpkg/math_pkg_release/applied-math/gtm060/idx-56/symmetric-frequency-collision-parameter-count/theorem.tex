Let a system possess rotational symmetry of order $n \geq 3$. The characteristic oscillations are divided into symmetric oscillations (carried to themselves by the rotation) and asymmetric oscillations (carried by rotation by $2\pi/n$ into independent oscillations).

In a typical family of such systems, for the collision of $i$ simple characteristic frequencies (corresponding to $i$ symmetric oscillations) and $j$ double frequencies (corresponding to $j$ pairs of asymmetric oscillations) to occur, the number of parameters of the family must be at least
$$\frac{(i-1)(i+2)}{2} + j^{2}.$$

In particular:
\begin{itemize}
\item For two symmetric frequencies to collide ($i=2$, $j=0$), at least $2$ parameters are needed.
\item For two double frequencies to collide ($i=0$, $j=2$), at least $4$ parameters are needed.
\item For a simple and a double frequency to collide ($i=1$, $j=1$), at least $1$ parameter is needed.
\end{itemize}
