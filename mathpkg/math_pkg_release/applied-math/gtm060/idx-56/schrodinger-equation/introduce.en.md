The Schrödinger equation is the fundamental partial differential equation of non-relativistic quantum mechanics. It governs the time evolution of the wave function $\psi(q,t)$ of a particle moving in a potential $U(q)$. The parameter $h$ (reduced Planck constant) controls the scale of quantum effects; in the limit $h \to 0$, the quasi-classical (WKB) approximation connects the quantum evolution to the classical Hamiltonian dynamics of the corresponding mechanical system.
