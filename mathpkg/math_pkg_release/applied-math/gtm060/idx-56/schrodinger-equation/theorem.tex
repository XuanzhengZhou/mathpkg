The Schrödinger equation for a particle in a field with potential energy $U$ in Euclidean space is an equation for a complex-valued function $\psi(q, t)$:
$$ih \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial t} = -\frac{h^{2}}{2} \Delta \psi + U(q)\psi, \quad q \in \mathbb{R}^{n}, \ t \in \mathbb{R}.$$
Here $h$ is a real constant (the reduced Planck constant), which serves as a small parameter in the quasi-classical approximation, and $\Delta$ is the Laplace operator.
