The short-wave initial condition is the standard ansatz for constructing quasi-classical (WKB) asymptotics of the Schrödinger equation. It expresses the initial wave function as a rapidly oscillating exponential $e^{(i/h)s(q)}$ modulated by a smooth amplitude $\varphi(q)$. The phase function $s(q)$ determines the initial "momentum" distribution via $p = \partial s / \partial q$, linking the quantum evolution to the classical Hamiltonian flow on the corresponding Lagrangian manifold.
