Invariance with respect to rotations in three-dimensional space means that space is isotropic; there are no preferred directions.

If $\varphi_i: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$) is a motion of a system of points satisfying Newton's equation, and $G: \mathbb{R}^3 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ is an orthogonal transformation, then the motion $G\varphi_i: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ also satisfies the equation. In other words,

$$\mathbf{F}(G\mathbf{x}, G\dot{\mathbf{x}}) = G\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{x}, \dot{\mathbf{x}}),$$

where $G\mathbf{x}$ denotes $(G\mathbf{x}_1, \ldots, G\mathbf{x}_n)$.
