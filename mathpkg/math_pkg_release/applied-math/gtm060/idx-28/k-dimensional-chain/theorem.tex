A chain of dimension $k$ on a manifold $M$ consists of a finite collection of $k$-dimensional oriented cells $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_r$ in $M$ and integers $m_1, \ldots, m_r$, called multiplicities (the multiplicities can be positive, negative, or zero). A chain is denoted by
$$c_k = m_1 \sigma_1 + \cdots + m_r \sigma_r.$$
The following natural identifications are introduced:
$$m_1 \sigma + m_2 \sigma = (m_1 + m_2) \sigma,$$
$$m_1 \sigma_1 + m_2 \sigma_2 = m_2 \sigma_2 + m_1 \sigma_1, \qquad 0 \sigma = 0, \qquad c_k + 0 = c_k.$$
The set of all $k$-chains on $M$ forms a commutative group under addition.
