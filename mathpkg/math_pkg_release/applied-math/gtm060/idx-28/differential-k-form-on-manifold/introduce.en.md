A differential $k$-form on a manifold $M$ is a smooth assignment of an exterior $k$-form to each tangent space $TM_x$. This global object is the central character of exterior differential calculus, combining the pointwise multilinear algebra of exterior forms with the smooth structure of the underlying manifold. It allows for operations such as pullback, wedge product, exterior differentiation, and integration over chains.
