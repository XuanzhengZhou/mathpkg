Let $\sigma = (D, f, \text{Or})$ be a $k$-dimensional cell in the manifold $M$. Its boundary $\partial \sigma$ is the $(k-1)$-chain
$$\partial \sigma = \sum_i \sigma_i$$
consisting of the cells $\sigma_i = (D_i, f_i, \text{Or}_i)$, where the $D_i$ are the $(k-1)$-dimensional faces of $D$, $\text{Or}_i$ are orientations chosen by the outward normal rule, and $f_i$ are the restrictions of the mapping $f: D \to M$ to the face $D_i$.
