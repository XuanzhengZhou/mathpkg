The integral of a 1-form along a smooth path generalizes the familiar line integral of vector calculus. By evaluating the form on tangent vectors constructed from a partition of the parameter interval, one defines the integral as a Riemann sum limit. This construction makes no reference to coordinates or a metric — it depends only on the smooth structure of the manifold and the differential form itself, illustrating the intrinsic geometric nature of differential forms.
