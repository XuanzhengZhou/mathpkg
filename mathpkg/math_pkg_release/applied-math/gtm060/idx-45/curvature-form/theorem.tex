We choose an orientation of a surface $M$ in a neighborhood of a point and consider parallel translation of vectors along the boundary of a small region $D$ on the surface. The result of such a translation is rotation by a small angle $\varphi(D)$ (the sign of the angle is fixed by the choice of orientation).

If we divide the region $D$ into two parts $D_1$ and $D_2$, the result of parallel translation along the boundary of $D$ can be obtained by first going around one part, and then the other. Thus,

$$\varphi(D) = \varphi(D_1) + \varphi(D_2),$$

i.e., the angle $\varphi$ is an additive function of regions. The \text{curvature form} $\Omega$ is the differential 2-form defined by

$$\varphi(D) = \int_D \Omega.$$

The value of $\Omega$ on a pair of tangent vectors $\xi, \eta$ in $TM_x$ is given by

$$\Omega(\xi, \eta) = \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0} \frac{\varphi(\Pi_\varepsilon)}{\varepsilon^2},$$

where $\Pi_\varepsilon$ is the parallelogram spanned by $\varepsilon\xi, \varepsilon\eta$ on $M$ (constructed via local identification of a neighborhood of $x$ with a neighborhood of $0$ in $TM_x$).
