An adiabatic invariant is a quantity that remains nearly constant when the parameters of a Hamiltonian system are varied slowly compared to the characteristic periods of the motion. The definition captures this idea rigorously: over a time interval of order $1/\varepsilon$, the change in the adiabatic invariant can be made arbitrarily small by making the parameter variation sufficiently slow ($\varepsilon$ sufficiently small). For one-degree-of-freedom systems, the action variable $I$ is an adiabatic invariant, a fact proved using the averaging theorem.
