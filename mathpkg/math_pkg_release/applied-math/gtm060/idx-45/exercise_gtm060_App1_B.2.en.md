Show that the function defined by the formula $\Omega(\xi, \eta) = \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0} \frac{\varphi(\Pi_\varepsilon)}{\varepsilon^2}$ is really a differential 2-form, independent of the arbitrary choice involved in the construction, and that the rotation of a vector under translation along the boundary of a finite oriented region $D$ is expressed, in terms of this form, by $\varphi(D) = \int_D \Omega$.
