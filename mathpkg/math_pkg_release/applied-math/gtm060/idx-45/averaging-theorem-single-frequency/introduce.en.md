The averaging principle replaces a perturbed system of differential equations by a simpler averaged system where the rapid oscillatory part is integrated out. For single-frequency systems ($k = 1$), this theorem provides a rigorous justification: the true solution $I(t)$ stays within $O(\varepsilon)$ of the averaged solution $J(t)$ over a long time interval of order $1/\varepsilon$. The proof uses a clever change of variables $P = I + \varepsilon k(I, \varphi)$ that absorbs the oscillatory part of the perturbation, reducing the problem to a Gronwall-type estimate.
