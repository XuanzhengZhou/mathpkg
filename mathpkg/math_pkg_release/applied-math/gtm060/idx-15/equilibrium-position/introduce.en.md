An equilibrium position of a dynamical system \(\frac{dx}{dt} = f(x)\) is a point \(x_0\) where the vector field vanishes, so that the constant function \(x(t) \equiv x_0\) is a solution. Equivalently, it is a zero of the vector field: \(f(x_0) = 0\). In mechanics, equilibrium positions correspond to configurations where all forces balance, and they serve as the starting point for the study of small oscillations via linearization.
