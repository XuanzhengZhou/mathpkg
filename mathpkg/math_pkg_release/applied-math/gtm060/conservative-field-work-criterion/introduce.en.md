A vector field is conservative exactly when the work done between two points is path-independent. If this holds, a potential energy can be defined as $U(M) = -\int_{M_0}^M (\mathbf{F}, d\mathbf{S})$, and conversely any conservative field has path-independent work. This equivalence is fundamental to the theory of conservative forces.
