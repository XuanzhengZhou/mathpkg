Poincaré's recurrence theorem is a foundational result in ergodic theory and dynamical systems. Consider a volume-preserving continuous bijection $g$ of a bounded region $D$ onto itself. The theorem asserts that arbitrarily close to any point in $D$, there exists another point whose orbit under $g$ eventually returns to the same neighborhood. In other words, recurrence is ubiquitous: no matter how small a neighborhood one selects, some point in that neighborhood will revisit it under iteration of $g$. The proof is elegantly simple, relying only on the fact that mutually disjoint images of a set of positive volume under a volume-preserving map would require infinite total volume in a bounded region.
