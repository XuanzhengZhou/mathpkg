Consider the system of ordinary differential equations on the two-dimensional torus with angular coordinates $\varphi_1, \varphi_2$:

$$\dot{\varphi}_1 = \alpha_1, \quad \dot{\varphi}_2 = \alpha_2.$$

The divergence is $\text{div } \mathbf{f} = 0$, and the corresponding flow

$$g^t: (\varphi_1, \varphi_2) \mapsto (\varphi_1 + \alpha_1 t, \varphi_2 + \alpha_2 t)$$

preserves the volume $d\varphi_1 \, d\varphi_2$.

If $\alpha_1/\alpha_2$ is irrational, then the "winding line" on the torus, $g^t(\varphi_1, \varphi_2)$, is dense in the torus for any initial point $(\varphi_1, \varphi_2)$.
