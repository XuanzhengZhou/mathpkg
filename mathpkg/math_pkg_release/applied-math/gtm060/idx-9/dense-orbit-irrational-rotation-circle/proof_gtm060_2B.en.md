By Poincaré's recurrence theorem, for any $\delta > 0$ there exists $n$ such that $|g^n x - x| < \delta$. Since $g$ is a rotation, this means $|n\alpha \bmod 2\pi| < \delta$. The set of such rotation angles $\{n\alpha \bmod 2\pi\}$ forms a subgroup of the circle. If $\alpha/2\pi$ is irrational, this subgroup is infinite. A standard argument shows that an infinite subgroup of the circle is dense (otherwise its closure would be a finite subgroup, contradicting the irrationality of $\alpha/2\pi$). Therefore, the orbit $\{g^k x\}$ is dense in the circle.
