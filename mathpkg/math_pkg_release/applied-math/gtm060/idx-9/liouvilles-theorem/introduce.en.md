Liouville's theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of ordinary differential equations and classical mechanics. Consider a system $\dot{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x})$ whose solutions exist for all time, generating a flow $g^t$. The theorem states that if the divergence of the vector field $\mathbf{f}$ vanishes identically, then the flow $g^t$ is volume-preserving: for any initial region $D(0)$, the volume of the evolved region $D(t) = g^t D(0)$ remains constant. This generalizes the familiar fact that Hamiltonian systems preserve phase volume, since the divergence of a Hamiltonian vector field is identically zero.
