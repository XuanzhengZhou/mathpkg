Poincaré's lemma states that on a vector space (or more generally on a contractible manifold), every closed differential form is exact. This is a fundamental result in de Rham cohomology: it implies that the higher cohomology groups of $\mathbb{R}^n$ are all trivial, $H^k(\mathbb{R}^n) = 0$ for $k > 0$. The proof uses the cone construction, which provides an explicit homotopy operator $p$ satisfying $d \circ p + p \circ d = \text{id}$.
