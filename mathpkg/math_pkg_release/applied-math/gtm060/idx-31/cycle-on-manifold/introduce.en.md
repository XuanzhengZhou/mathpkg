A cycle on a smooth manifold $M$ is a $k$-dimensional chain whose boundary vanishes identically. Cycles are fundamental objects in homology theory: they are the chains that are "closed" in the sense of having no boundary. The set of all $k$-cycles forms a subgroup of the chain group $C_k(M)$, and cycles that differ by a boundary are called homologous. Cycles provide the natural domain of integration for closed differential forms via Stokes' theorem.
