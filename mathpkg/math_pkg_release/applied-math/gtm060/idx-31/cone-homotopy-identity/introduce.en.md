The cone homotopy identity $d \circ p + p \circ d = \text{id}$ is the algebraic heart of the proof of Poincaré's lemma. It shows that the co-cone operator $p$ is a cochain homotopy between the identity map and the zero map on the de Rham complex of a vector space. When applied to a closed form $d\omega = 0$, the identity reduces to $d(p\omega) = \omega$, proving $\omega$ is exact. The same identity on chains, $\partial p + p \partial = \text{id}$, shows that cycles on a vector space are boundaries.
