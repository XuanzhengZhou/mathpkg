Let $M^{2n}$ be an even-dimensional differentiable manifold. A \textit{symplectic structure} on $M^{2n}$ is a closed, nondegenerate differential 2-form $\omega^2$ on $M^{2n}$:
$$d\omega^2 = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \forall \xi \neq 0 \; \exists \eta: \omega^2(\xi, \eta) \neq 0 \quad (\xi, \eta \in TM_x).$$
The nondegeneracy condition means that at each point $x \in M$, the bilinear form $\omega^2_x$ on the tangent space $TM_x$ is nondegenerate; this forces $M$ to be even-dimensional.
