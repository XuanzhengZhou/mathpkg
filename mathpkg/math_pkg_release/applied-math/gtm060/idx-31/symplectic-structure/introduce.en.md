A symplectic structure on an even-dimensional manifold $M^{2n}$ is a closed, nondegenerate differential 2-form $\omega^2$. Closedness ($d\omega^2 = 0$) is a differential condition ensuring that the symplectic form is locally expressible as $dp \wedge dq$, while nondegeneracy means $\omega^2$ induces an isomorphism between tangent vectors and cotangent vectors at each point. Symplectic structures provide the natural geometric framework for Hamiltonian mechanics: the phase space of any mechanical system carries a canonical symplectic structure.
