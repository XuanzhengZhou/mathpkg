By Stokes' formula for a $(k+1)$-dimensional chain $c_{k+1}$ and a $k$-form $\omega^k$,
$$\int_{c_{k+1}} d\omega^k = \int_{\partial c_{k+1}} \omega^k.$$
If $c_{k+1}$ is a cycle, then by definition $\partial c_{k+1} = 0$, and the integral over the zero boundary vanishes. Hence $\int_{c_{k+1}} d\omega^k = 0$.
