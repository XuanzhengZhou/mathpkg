The set of all $k$-forms on a manifold $M$ forms a vector space. The closed $k$-forms constitute a subspace, and the differentials of $(k-1)$-forms form a subspace of the closed forms. The $k$-th \textit{cohomology group} of $M$ (with real coefficients) is the quotient space:
$$H^k(M, \mathbb{R}) = \frac{\{\text{closed } k\text{-forms}\}}{\{\text{differentials of } (k-1)\text{-forms}\}}.$$
An element of this group is a class of closed forms differing from one another only by a differential.
