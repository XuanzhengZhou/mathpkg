The $k$-th de Rham cohomology group $H^k(M, \mathbb{R})$ measures the failure of closed differential $k$-forms on a manifold $M$ to be exact. It is defined as the quotient of the vector space of closed $k$-forms by the subspace of exact $k$-forms (differentials of $(k-1)$-forms). The dimension of $H^k(M, \mathbb{R})$ is the $k$-th Betti number, a topological invariant. By Poincaré's lemma, $H^k(\mathbb{R}^n, \mathbb{R}) = 0$ for all $k > 0$.
