Two $k$-dimensional cycles $a$ and $b$ on a manifold $M$ are called \textit{homologous} if their difference is the boundary of a $(k+1)$-chain:
$$a - b = \partial c_{k+1}.$$
Homology is an equivalence relation on the set of cycles. If $a$ and $b$ are homologous and $\omega^k$ is a closed $k$-form ($d\omega^k = 0$), then
$$\int_a \omega^k = \int_b \omega^k.$$
