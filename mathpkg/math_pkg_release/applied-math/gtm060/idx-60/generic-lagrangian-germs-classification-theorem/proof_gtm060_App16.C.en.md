Every lagrangian mapping is locally equivalent to a gradient (or normal, or Gauss) mapping. The singularities of generic gradient (or normal, or Gauss) mappings are the same as those for arbitrary generic lagrangian mappings. The simplest of these are classified by the reflection groups $A_k, D_k, E_6, E_7, E_8$ (see Appendix 12).

The proof proceeds by studying the generating families of Lagrangian submanifolds. A Lagrangian submanifold in the cotangent bundle can be locally represented as the graph of the differential of a generating function. The singularities of the Lagrangian mapping correspond to the degenerations of critical points in the family of generating functions. The classification then reduces to the classification of simple singularities of functions, which is governed by the A, D, E reflection groups via the theory of versal deformations.

For dimensions $\leq 5$, the list of simple stable germs is finite and consists precisely of the A, D, E series. The correspondence is: $A_k$ corresponds to the caustic $x^{k+1}$, $D_k$ to the caustic $x^2 y \pm y^{k-1}$, and $E_6, E_7, E_8$ to the exceptional singularities.
