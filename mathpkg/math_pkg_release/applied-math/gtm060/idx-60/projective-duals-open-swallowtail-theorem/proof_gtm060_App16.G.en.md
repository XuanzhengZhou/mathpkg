The proof uses the correspondence between curves and their projective dual surfaces. For a space curve, the projective dual surface is formed by the osculating planes at points of the curve. When the curve has a point of double flatness of type $(1, 2, 5)$, the family of osculating planes develops a singularity that is diffeomorphic to the open swallowtail. The type $(1, 2, 5)$ refers to the orders of contact of the curve with its osculating plane, rectifying plane, and normal plane respectively at the exceptional point. The normal form analysis of the curve near such a point, combined with the projective duality transformation, yields the open swallowtail in the dual space.
