Platonova and Shcherbak (1981) classified all local projections of a generic surface in $\mathbb{R}P^3$ from points outside the surface. There are exactly 14 equivalence classes, given by 14 normal forms of the height function. The only generic singularities are folds and Whitney tucks; the remaining 12 types appear only from special viewpoints. This finiteness was unexpected given the continuum of inequivalent singularities in generic 3-parameter families of surface-to-plane maps.
