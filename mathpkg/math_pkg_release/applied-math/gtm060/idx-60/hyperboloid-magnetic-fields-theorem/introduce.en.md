This theorem provides a magnetic analogue of the Newton-Ivory theorems for hyperboloids. Currents flowing along meridians of a hyperboloid of one sheet produce a magnetic field that vanishes inside the hyperboloidal tube and circulates along parallels outside. Conversely, currents along parallels produce a field that vanishes outside and circulates along meridians inside. The current densities are induced from homeoidal densities on focal curves.
