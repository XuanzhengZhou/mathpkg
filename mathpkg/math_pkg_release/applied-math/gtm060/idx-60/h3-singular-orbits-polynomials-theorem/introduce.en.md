Lyashko's theorem (1981) gives an algebraic description of the $H_3$ singular orbit variety. It identifies the variety $\Sigma$ of non-regular orbits of the icosahedral reflection group $H_3$ with the set of polynomials $x^5 + ax^4 + bx^2 + c$ having a multiple root. This variety is the union of tangents to the space curve $(t, t^3, t^5)$, providing a concrete realization of the icosahedral discriminant.
