We project the quadrics in the confocal family along a pencil of parallel lines onto the hyperplane perpendicular to the pencil. Each quadric defines an apparent contour (the set of critical values of the projection of the quadric). For a projection whose direction is in general position, the apparent contour is a quadric (i.e., a surface of degree two) in the image hyperplane.

By the Lemma on apparent contours, the apparent contours of the quadrics in a confocal family form themselves a confocal family of quadrics. Applying Jacobi's Theorem 1 to this confocal family in the image hyperplane, $n-1$ of these apparent contours pass through each point, where they intersect at right angles.

The tangency of the original line to $n-1$ confocal quadrics corresponds exactly to the passage of the projected point through the $n-1$ apparent contours. This completes the proof of Theorem 2.
