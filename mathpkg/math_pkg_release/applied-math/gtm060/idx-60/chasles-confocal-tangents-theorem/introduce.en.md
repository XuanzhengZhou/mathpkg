Chasles' theorem extends Jacobi's result from points to lines: a line in general position is tangent to exactly $n-1$ distinct quadrics in a confocal family. The proof projects the confocal quadrics along parallel lines onto a perpendicular hyperplane. The apparent contours form another confocal family, to which Jacobi's theorem is applied.
