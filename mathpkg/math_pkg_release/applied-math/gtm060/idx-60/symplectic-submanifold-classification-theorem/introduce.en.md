Givental's theorem (1981) states that a germ of a submanifold in a symplectic manifold is determined up to symplectic diffeomorphism by the restriction of the symplectic form to the submanifold. This is stronger than Weinstein's earlier theorem (1971) which required values of the symplectic form on all vectors based on the submanifold. The result reduces classification of generic submanifold germs to classification of degenerate symplectic structures.
