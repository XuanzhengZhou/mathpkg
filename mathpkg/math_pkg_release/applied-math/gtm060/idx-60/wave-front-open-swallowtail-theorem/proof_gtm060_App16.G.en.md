The open swallowtail is the surface in the four-dimensional space of monic polynomials $x^5 + Ax^3 + Bx^2 + Cx + D$ formed by the polynomials with triple roots. Differentiation of the polynomials turns the open swallowtail into an ordinary one; when the swallowtail is opened, the cuspidal edge is retained, but the self-intersection disappears.

The proof uses the space-time formulation: the instantaneous wave fronts at different times form a "large front" in space-time. Near a swallowtail caustic, the large front has the structure of an open swallowtail. The time function, when reduced to normal form by the equivariant Morse lemma (preserving the large front), reveals that the cuspidal edges sweep out exactly the open swallowtail surface over the caustic.
