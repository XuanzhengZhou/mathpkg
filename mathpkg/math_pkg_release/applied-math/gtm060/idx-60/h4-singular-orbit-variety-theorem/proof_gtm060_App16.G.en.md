Consider those values of $\lambda$ for which the curve $x^5 + y^3 + \lambda_1 x^3 y + \lambda_2 x^3 + \lambda_3 y + \lambda_4 = 0$ is singular. The condition that the curve be singular means that there exists a point $(x, y)$ where both the polynomial and its gradient vanish. This defines a three-dimensional hypersurface in the four-dimensional $\lambda$-space. One of the irreducible components of this hypersurface corresponds to the $H_4$ discriminant.

The relationship is established via an embedding of the local algebra $D_4$ into $E_8$, which induces on the former a grading given by the convolution of invariants of $H_4$. O. P. Shcherbak showed that this identifies the $H_4$ singular orbit variety with a component of the discriminant of the curve singularity $x^5 + y^3$.
