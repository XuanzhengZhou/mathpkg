This corollary follows from the preceding theorem on symplectic structures near the product of a swallowtail and a linear space, together with the description of the manifold of tangent rays near biasymptotic directions.

In the obstacle problem, each minimizing path consists of segments of straight lines and segments of geodesics on the surface of the obstacle. The system of all rays tangent to these geodesics forms a lagrangian variety in the symplectic space of lines. Near an asymptotic ray, the variety develops a semicubic cuspidal edge (an $A_2$ singularity). Near a biasymptotic ray, where two asymptotic directions coalesce, the singularity becomes an open swallowtail (the $A_3$ singularity with the self-intersection removed, obtained by opening the swallowtail through the addition of a fifth-order term to the polynomial).
