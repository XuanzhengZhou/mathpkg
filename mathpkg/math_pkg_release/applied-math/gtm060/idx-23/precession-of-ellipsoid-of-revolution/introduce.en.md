When the inertia ellipsoid of a force-free rigid body is an ellipsoid of revolution ($I_2 = I_3 \neq I_1$), the rotational motion simplifies considerably. The vectors $B_t e_1$ (the body symmetry axis), $\omega$ (instantaneous angular velocity), and $m$ (angular momentum) remain coplanar at all times, and their relative geometry is preserved. Both the rotation axis and the symmetry axis sweep out cones around the angular momentum vector with the same angular velocity. This motion is known as precession, and it provides a complete geometric description of the body's behavior in this special case of Euler's equations.
