Decompose the angular velocity vector $\omega$ into components in the directions of the angular momentum vector $m$ and the symmetry axis of the body $B_t e_1$. The first component gives the angular velocity of precession:
$$\omega_{\text{pr}} = \frac{M}{I_2}.$$

The motion of the body can be represented as the product of a rotation around the axis of angular momentum and a subsequent rotation around the axis of the body. The sum of the angular velocity vectors of these two rotations equals the angular velocity vector of the product. Since the body is symmetric ($I_2 = I_3$), the component of $\omega$ along $m$ is $M/I_2$.
