The angular velocity of precession for a force-free rigid body whose inertia ellipsoid is an ellipsoid of revolution is obtained by decomposing the angular velocity vector $\omega$ into components along the angular momentum vector $m$ and the body symmetry axis $B_t e_1$. The component along $m$ gives the precession angular velocity, which equals $M/I_2$, where $M$ is the magnitude of the angular momentum and $I_2$ is the equatorial moment of inertia.
