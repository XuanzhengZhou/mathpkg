A euclidean space $E^n$ is an affine space $A^n$ whose associated vector space $\mathbb{R}^n$ is equipped with a euclidean structure (a positive definite symmetric bilinear form). The scalar product defines the distance between points, giving $E^n$ the full geometry of lengths, angles, and rigid motions.
