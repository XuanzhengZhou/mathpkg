On the linear phase space $\mathbb{R}^{2n} = \{(p, q)\}$, in addition to the symplectic structure $dp \wedge dq$ and the euclidean structure with scalar square $p^2 + q^2$, one introduces a complex structure given by multiplication by $i$:

$$I: \mathbb{R}^{2n} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{2n}, \quad I(p, q) = (-q, p), \quad z = p + iq, \quad \mathbb{C}^n = \{z\}.$$

The three structures are connected by the relation

$$[x, y] = (Ix, y),$$

where $[x, y]$ denotes the skew-scalar product and $(\cdot, \cdot)$ the euclidean inner product.
