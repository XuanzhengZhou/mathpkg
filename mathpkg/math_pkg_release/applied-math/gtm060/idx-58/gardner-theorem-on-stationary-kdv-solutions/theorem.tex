**Gardner's Theorem** (generalizing Lax's theorem). In the space of functions of $x$, consider a differential operator of the form

$$A = \sum_{i} p_i \partial^{m-i},$$

where $p_0 = 1$ and the remaining coefficients $p_i$ are polynomials in $u$ and its derivatives.

The stationary points of the functional $I_s = \int P_s(u, u', \ldots, u^{(s)}) \, dx$ on the functional space, which are invariant under the phase flows with Hamiltonians $I_s$ (including the KdV flow), are determined by the equations

$$\frac{d}{dx} \frac{\delta I}{\delta u} = 0, \quad \text{or equivalently} \quad \frac{\delta I}{\delta u} = d.$$

The second equation is the Euler-Lagrange equation for the functional $I - d I_{-1}$, involving derivatives of order $n$. It has order $2n$ and can be written as a Hamiltonian system of equations in $2n$-dimensional Euclidean space.

This Hamiltonian system with $n$ degrees of freedom has $n$ integrals in involution and is completely integrable. The resulting finite-dimensional family of particular solutions of the KdV equation depends on $3n+1$ parameters ($2n$ phase coordinates and $n+1$ further parameters $c_1, \ldots, c_n; d$).
