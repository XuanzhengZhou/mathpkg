A Lagrangian mapping is one of the central objects in the study of Lagrangian singularities. It is simply the restriction of the cotangent bundle projection to a Lagrangian submanifold. While generically a local diffeomorphism (by the implicit function theorem applied to the generating function), at caustic points the rank drops, producing the visible singularities studied in catastrophe theory. Lagrangian mappings form a much more restricted class than general smooth mappings, which is why their classification (the Arnold $A_k$, $D_k$, $E_k$ series) is both richer and more constrained than Whitney's classification of general map singularities.
