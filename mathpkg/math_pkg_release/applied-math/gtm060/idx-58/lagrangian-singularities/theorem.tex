Lagrangian singularities are singularities of projections of Lagrangian manifolds onto configuration space. Such singularities are encountered in:
- investigating global solutions to the Hamilton-Jacobi equation,
- studying caustics, focal or conjugate points,
- analyzing the propagation of discontinuities and shock waves in the mechanics of a solid medium,
- problems of short wave asymptotics.

By projecting an $n$-dimensional Lagrangian submanifold of the $2n$-dimensional phase space (cotangent bundle of the configuration manifold) onto the configuration space, one obtains a mapping of one smooth $n$-dimensional manifold to another. At most points this mapping is a local diffeomorphism, but at some points of the Lagrangian manifold the rank of the differential drops — these points are **singular**. The image of the set of singular points in the configuration space is called a **caustic** in the Lagrangian case.
