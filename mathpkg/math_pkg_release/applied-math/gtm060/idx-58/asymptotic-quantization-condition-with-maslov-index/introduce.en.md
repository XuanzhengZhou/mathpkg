This theorem gives the asymptotic quantization condition for the Schrödinger-type equation $\frac{1}{2}\Delta\psi = \lambda^2(U(q)-E)\psi$ in the semiclassical limit. The eigenvalues $\lambda_N$ grow asymptotically like $\mu_N$, which must satisfy a Bohr-Sommerfeld-type condition with a Maslov index correction modulo 4. In one dimension, the Lagrangian manifold is a circle with index 2, and the condition reduces to the familiar $\mu_N \oint p\,dq = 2\pi(N + 1/2)$ — the corrected Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rule with the 1/2 Maslov correction. This connects classical mechanics on Lagrangian manifolds with quantum spectral asymptotics.
