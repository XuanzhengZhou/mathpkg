To obtain a **soliton** moving with velocity $c$, substitute the traveling wave ansatz $u = \varphi(x - ct)$ into the KdV equation $u_t = 6uu_x - u_{xxx}$. This yields the ordinary differential equation for $\varphi$:

$$\varphi'' = 3\varphi^2 + c\varphi + d,$$

where $d$ is an integration parameter. This is Newton's equation with a cubic potential.

The phase portrait in the $(\varphi, \varphi')$ plane contains a saddle point. The separatrix going from the saddle to the saddle for which $\varphi = 0$ (as the spatial variable tends to $\pm\infty$) determines the soliton solution — a localized pulse that decays to zero at infinity.

When multiple solitons interact, there is complicated nonlinear behavior during collision, but numerically and analytically it is observed that the sizes and velocities of the solitons remain unchanged after collision.
