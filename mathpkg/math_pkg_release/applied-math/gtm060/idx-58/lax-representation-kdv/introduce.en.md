Lax's theorem provides the key insight that makes the KdV equation integrable: the nonlinear PDE can be written as an isospectral deformation $\dot{L} = [A, L]$ of the Sturm-Liouville operator $L = -\partial_x^2 + u$. This Lax pair formulation reveals that the eigenvalues of $L$ are conserved quantities (first integrals), and it opened the door to solving the KdV equation via the inverse scattering transform. The Lax representation is now a central paradigm in the theory of integrable systems, extending far beyond KdV to the nonlinear Schrödinger equation, sine-Gordon, and many other soliton equations.
