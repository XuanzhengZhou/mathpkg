**Classification theorem for singularities of Lagrangian mappings in dimensions $n \leq 5$:**

For a Lagrangian mapping $\pi|_L: L \to M$ in general position (i.e., for an open dense set in the space of Lagrangian mappings), every singular point is Lagrangian equivalent (in a neighborhood of that point) to one of the normal forms $A_k, D_k, E_k$ listed in the table of normal forms.

In particular:
- For $n = 1$: only $A_1$ (nonsingular) and $A_2$ (fold) are typical.
- For $n = 2$: only $A_1, A_2, A_3$ (fold and cusp) are typical.
- For $n = 3$: $A_1, A_2, A_3, A_4, D_4$ are typical.
- For $n \leq 5$: the classification is given by the ADE simple singularities.

All more complicated singularities are removable by a small perturbation that preserves the Lagrangian condition (i.e., a small deformation of the Lagrangian manifold in phase space within the class of Lagrangian manifolds). After such a perturbation, only the simple unremovable singularities listed above remain.
