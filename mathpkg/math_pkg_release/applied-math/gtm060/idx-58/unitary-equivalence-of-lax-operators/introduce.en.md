This corollary is the direct consequence of the Lax representation $\dot{L} = [A, L]$. The commutator form ensures that $L(t)$ evolves by unitary transformations $L(t) = U(t)L(0)U(t)^{-1}$, so its spectrum is invariant. This means every eigenvalue of the associated Sturm-Liouville problem is a conserved quantity under the KdV flow. These eigenvalues provide an infinite family of first integrals, a hallmark of complete integrability. Physically, this corresponds to the fact that solitons preserve their identity (amplitude and speed) even after nonlinear collisions — the scattering data (eigenvalues) are constants of motion.
