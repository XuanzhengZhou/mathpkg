The **Hamiltonian vector field** $\theta_a$ with Hamiltonian function $a$ on a Poisson manifold is the unique vector field satisfying

$$\{a, f\} = \theta_a(f)$$

for all smooth functions $f$. Existence follows from the derivation property (Leibniz rule) of the Poisson bracket: for each fixed $a$, the map $f \mapsto \{a, f\}$ is a derivation of $C^\infty(M)$, and every derivation of the algebra of smooth functions corresponds to a vector field.

The mapping $a \mapsto \theta_a$ is a Lie algebra homomorphism from the Lie algebra of functions $(C^\infty(M), \{\cdot, \cdot\})$ to the Lie algebra of vector fields $(\mathfrak{X}(M), [\cdot, \cdot])$:

$$\theta_{\{a, b\}} = [\theta_a, \theta_b].$$

The kernel of this homomorphism consists of **Casimir functions** — functions whose Poisson bracket with every other function vanishes.
