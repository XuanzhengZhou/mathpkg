Lagrangian equivalence is the correct notion of "sameness" for Lagrangian mappings. Two such mappings are equivalent if there is a symplectic diffeomorphism of the ambient phase spaces that intertwines the projections (maps fibers to fibers) and sends one Lagrangian submanifold to the other. This is stronger than mere diffeomorphism of caustics (the visible singular sets) because not every diffeomorphism of caustics lifts to a symplectic diffeomorphism of the phase space. The classification of Lagrangian singularities up to Lagrangian equivalence yields the celebrated $A_k$, $D_k$, $E_k$ normal forms that refine both Whitney's classification and the general right-left equivalence of smooth maps.
