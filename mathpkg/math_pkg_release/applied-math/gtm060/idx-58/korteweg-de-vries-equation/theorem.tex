The **Korteweg–de Vries (KdV) equation** is the nonlinear partial differential equation

$$u_t = 6uu_x - u_{xxx}.$$

This equation first arose in the theory of waves in shallow water and later appeared in numerous problems of mathematical physics. It describes the evolution of a real-valued function $u(x, t)$ of one spatial variable $x$ and time $t$.

The KdV equation possesses remarkable properties:
- As $t \to \pm\infty$, solutions with zero boundary conditions decompose into **solitons** — localized traveling waves of permanent form.
- It is a **completely integrable** infinite-dimensional Hamiltonian system.
- It has infinitely many first integrals $I_s = \int P_s(u, u', \ldots, u^{(s)}) \, dx$, where $P_s$ are polynomials.
- The Hamiltonian form is $\dot{u} = \frac{d}{dx} \frac{\delta I_1}{\delta u}$, where $I_1 = \int u^2 \, dx$.
