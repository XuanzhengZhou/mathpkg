The Korteweg–de Vries equation $u_t = 6uu_x - u_{xxx}$ is one of the most celebrated nonlinear partial differential equations. Originally derived to model shallow water waves, it later became the prototype of an integrable system — a nonlinear PDE that can be "solved" exactly via the inverse scattering transform. Arnold presents it as a prime example of "hidden symmetry" in mechanics: the equation possesses infinitely many conserved quantities not explained by any obvious geometric symmetry. These conservation laws, discovered numerically and then proved by Kruskal, Zabusky, Lax, Gardner, Greene, and Miura, make the KdV equation an infinite-dimensional completely integrable Hamiltonian system, with action-angle variables found by Gardner, Zakharov, and Faddeev.
