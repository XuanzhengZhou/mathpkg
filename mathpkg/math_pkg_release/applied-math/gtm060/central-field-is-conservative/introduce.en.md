Every central field $\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}) = \Phi(r)\mathbf{e}_r$ is conservative because its work integral reduces to a one-dimensional integral $\int \Phi(r)dr$, which depends only on the endpoints' radial distances. The potential energy $U(r) = -\int \Phi(r)dr$ is purely a function of $r$, reflecting the rotational symmetry.
