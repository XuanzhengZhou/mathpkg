Let $\xi$ be a vector tangent to a riemannian manifold $M$ at a point $x$, and $v$ a vector field given on $M$ in a neighborhood of $x$. Let $x(t)$ be any smooth curve with $x(0) = x$ and $\dot{x}(0) = \xi$. Parallel translate the vector $v(x(t))$ from the point $x(t)$ back to $x(0) = x$ along the curve. Consider the derivative of the resulting vector with respect to $t$, evaluated at $t = 0$. This derivative is a vector in the tangent space $TM_x$, called the \textbf{covariant derivative} of the field $v$ along $\xi$, denoted by $\nabla_\xi v$. The vector $\nabla_\xi v$ does not depend on the choice of curve, but only on $\xi$ and $v$.
