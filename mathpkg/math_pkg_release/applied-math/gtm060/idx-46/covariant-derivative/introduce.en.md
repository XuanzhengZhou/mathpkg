The covariant derivative $\nabla_\xi v$ generalizes the directional derivative to vector fields on a Riemannian manifold. It measures the rate of change of the vector field $v$ in the direction $\xi$, using parallel transport to compare vectors at different points. The resulting operation satisfies bilinearity, a Leibniz rule for scalar multiplication, compatibility with the metric, and torsion-freeness. Together these properties characterize the unique Levi-Civita connection on a Riemannian manifold.
