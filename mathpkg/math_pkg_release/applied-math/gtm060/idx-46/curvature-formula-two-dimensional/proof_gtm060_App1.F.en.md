The proof is obtained by comparing the definitions of the curvature tensor and of curvature in a two-dimensional direction. The curvature tensor $\Omega(\xi,\eta)$ is defined via parallel translation around an infinitesimal parallelogram spanned by $\xi$ and $\eta$, yielding a skew-symmetric operator representing an infinitesimal rotation. The curvature in the two-dimensional direction $L$ spanned by orthonormal $\xi, \eta$ is defined as the Gaussian curvature of the geodesic surface through $x$ with tangent plane $L$. Computing the Gaussian curvature of this surface in terms of parallel transport around small loops within the surface yields the formula $K = \langle \Omega(\xi,\eta)\xi, \eta \rangle$. Arnold notes that one may also take this formula as the definition of $K$.
