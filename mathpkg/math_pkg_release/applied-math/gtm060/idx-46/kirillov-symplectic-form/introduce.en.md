The Kirillov form endows each coadjoint orbit with a natural symplectic structure. This was first used by A. A. Kirillov to study representations of nilpotent Lie groups (the orbit method). Coadjoint orbits provide a rich source of examples of symplectic manifolds. For mechanical systems on Lie groups, the phase space reduction often leads to coadjoint orbits.
