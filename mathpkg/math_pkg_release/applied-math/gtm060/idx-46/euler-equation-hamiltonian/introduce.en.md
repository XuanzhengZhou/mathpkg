This theorem reveals the deep geometric structure behind Euler's equations: they are Hamiltonian systems on symplectic leaves (coadjoint orbits). The dimension of the phase space is reduced from $2n$ (on $T^*G$) to the dimension of the coadjoint orbit. For the classical rigid body ($G = SO(3)$), the coadjoint orbits are spheres, and the Euler equations describe the motion of the angular momentum vector on a sphere.
