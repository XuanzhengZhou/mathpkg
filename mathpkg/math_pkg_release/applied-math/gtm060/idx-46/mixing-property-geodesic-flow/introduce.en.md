Mixing is a stronger property than ergodicity: it means that the flow asymptotically decorrelates any two regions. The probability of finding a trajectory in $B$ at a large time $t$, given it started in $A$, approaches the volume of $B$ independently of $A$. Physically, this is the "stochastic" behavior: the system loses memory of initial conditions.
