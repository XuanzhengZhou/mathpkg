The group $SDiff\,D$ is the infinite-dimensional Lie group that serves as the configuration space for an ideal incompressible fluid. A fluid flow is a curve $g_t$ in this group, with velocity field obtained by right translation. The kinetic energy defines a right-invariant metric, and geodesics correspond to solutions of Euler's equations of hydrodynamics. This geometrizes fluid dynamics: the Euler equations for an ideal fluid are the Euler equations for a "generalized rigid body with group $SDiff\,D$."
