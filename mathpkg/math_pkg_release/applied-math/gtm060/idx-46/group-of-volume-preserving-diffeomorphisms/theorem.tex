Let $D$ be a bounded region in a riemannian manifold. The group of diffeomorphisms of $D$ which preserve the volume element is denoted $SDiff\,D$. Its Lie algebra consists of all divergence-free vector fields on $D$, tangent to the boundary. The scalar product on the Lie algebra is defined by

$$\langle v_1, v_2 \rangle = \int_D (v_1 \cdot v_2)\, dx,$$

where $(\cdot)$ is the riemannian metric and $dx$ the volume element. The kinetic energy of a fluid flow $t \mapsto g_t \in SDiff\,D$ defines a right-invariant riemannian metric on $SDiff\,D$.
