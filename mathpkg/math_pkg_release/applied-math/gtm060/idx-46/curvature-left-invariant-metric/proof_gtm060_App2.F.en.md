The proof is a tedious but straightforward calculation based on the formula for the covariant derivative at the identity: $(\nabla_\xi \eta)_e = B(\xi, \eta)$. Using this and the definition of the curvature tensor $\Omega(\xi,\eta)\zeta = \nabla_\xi \nabla_\eta \zeta - \nabla_\eta \nabla_\xi \zeta - \nabla_{[\xi,\eta]} \zeta$, one computes $K_{\xi,\eta} = \langle \Omega(\xi,\eta)\xi, \eta \rangle$ in terms of the bracket and $B$, yielding the stated formula. Arnold remarks that in Euler's equation the right-hand side changes sign under passage to the right-invariant case.
