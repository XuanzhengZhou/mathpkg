For a rigid body ($G = SO(3)$), these two vectors are the familiar "angular velocity in the body" and "angular velocity in space." The distinction arises because there are two natural ways to identify the tangent space at $g$ with the Lie algebra: by left or by right translation. This generalizes to any Lie group with a left-invariant metric.
