Consider the kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2}(A\Omega, \Omega)$. Let

$$\xi = \frac{\omega}{\sqrt{2T}},$$

where $\omega$ is the angular velocity in space. Then $(A\xi, \xi) = \frac{1}{2T}(A\omega, \omega) = 1$, so $\pm \xi$ lie on the inertia ellipsoid $B_t E$.

The normal to the ellipsoid $E$ at $\Omega$ is $\text{grad}(A\Omega, \Omega) = 2A\Omega = 2M$. Therefore, at the points $\pm \xi = \omega / \sqrt{2T}$, the normal to $B_t E$ is collinear with $\mathbf{m} = B_t M$.

Consider the plane $\pi$ tangent to $B_t E$ at $\xi$. Its normal is parallel to $\mathbf{m}$, and the scalar product of $\xi$ with the stationary vector $\mathbf{m}$ is

$$(\mathbf{m}, \xi) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2T}}(\mathbf{m}, \omega) = \frac{2T}{\sqrt{2T}} = \sqrt{2T},$$

which is constant. Hence the distance of $\pi$ from $O$ is constant, so $\pi$ is a stationary plane (the invariable plane).

Since the point of tangency $\xi$ lies on the instantaneous axis of rotation, its velocity is zero. This means the ellipsoid $B_t E$ rolls without slipping along the plane $\pi$.
