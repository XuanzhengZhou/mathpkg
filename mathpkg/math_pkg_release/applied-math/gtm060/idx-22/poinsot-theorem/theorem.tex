Let a rigid body be fixed at a point $O$. Let the inertia ellipsoid in the body frame $K$ be

$$E = \{ \Omega : (A \Omega, \Omega) = 1 \} \subset K,$$

where $A$ is the symmetric inertia operator. At every moment of time, the ellipsoid $E$ occupies a position $B_t E$ in the stationary space $k$.

Then the inertia ellipsoid $B_t E$ rolls without slipping along a stationary plane $\pi$ (the invariable plane), which is orthogonal to the conserved angular momentum vector $\mathbf{m}$ in space. The distance of $\pi$ from $O$ is constant, and the point of tangency lies on the instantaneous axis of rotation.
