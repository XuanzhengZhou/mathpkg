Poinsot's theorem provides an elegant geometric description of the motion of a rigid body fixed at a point. The inertia ellipsoid of the body rolls without slipping on a fixed plane (the invariable plane) in space. The point of tangency lies on the instantaneous axis of rotation, and the normal to the ellipsoid at the tangency point is collinear with the conserved angular momentum vector in space. This geometric picture completely determines the rotational motion.
