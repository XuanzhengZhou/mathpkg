The quantity $E$ is preserved by the law of conservation of energy: the kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2}(A\Omega, \Omega)$ of a rigid body fixed at a point is conserved, and substituting $M = A\Omega$ with $M_i = I_i \Omega_i$ yields

$$2E = 2T = \frac{M_1^2}{I_1} + \frac{M_2^2}{I_2} + \frac{M_3^2}{I_3}.$$

The quantity $M^2$ is preserved by the law of conservation of angular momentum: the angular momentum vector $m$ in space is constant, so its squared magnitude $m^2$ is constant. Since $B_t$ is an orthogonal transformation, we have $m^2 = (B_t M, B_t M) = (M, M) = M^2$.
