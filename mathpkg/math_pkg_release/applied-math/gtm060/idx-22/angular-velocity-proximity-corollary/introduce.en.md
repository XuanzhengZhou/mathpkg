Under initial conditions close to a stationary rotation about the largest or smallest principal axis of inertia, the angular velocity vector remains close to its initial position not only in the body frame (as guaranteed by Liapunov stability of the Euler equations) but also in the stationary space frame. This follows from Poinsot's geometric description: the point of tangency of the inertia ellipsoid with the invariable plane stays near the corresponding semiaxis endpoint, so the instantaneous axis of rotation in space also remains nearby.
