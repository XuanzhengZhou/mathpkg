Let a rigid body be fixed at a point $O$. Let $M$ be the angular momentum vector in the moving coordinate system $K$ attached to the body, and let $\Omega$ be the angular velocity vector in $K$. Then the angular momentum in space, $m = B_t M(t)$, is conserved, and the vector $M$ in the body satisfies the differential equation

$$\frac{dM}{dt} = [M, \Omega].$$

If $\Omega = \Omega_1 e_1 + \Omega_2 e_2 + \Omega_3 e_3$ and $M = M_1 e_1 + M_2 e_2 + M_3 e_3$ are the decompositions with respect to the principal axes at $O$, and $M_i = I_i \Omega_i$ where $I_i$ are the principal moments of inertia, then the Euler equations become the system

$$\frac{dM_1}{dt} = \frac{I_2 - I_3}{I_2 I_3} M_2 M_3, \quad \frac{dM_2}{dt} = \frac{I_3 - I_1}{I_3 I_1} M_3 M_1, \quad \frac{dM_3}{dt} = \frac{I_1 - I_2}{I_1 I_2} M_1 M_2.$$
