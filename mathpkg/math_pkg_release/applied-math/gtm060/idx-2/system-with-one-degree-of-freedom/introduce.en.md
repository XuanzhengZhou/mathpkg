A system with one degree of freedom is the simplest mechanical system, described by a single second-order differential equation $\ddot{x} = f(x)$ where $x$ is a real coordinate.
The kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2}\dot{x}^2$ is a quadratic form in the velocity, and the potential energy $U(x) = -\int f(\xi)d\xi$ encodes the force law.
The sign convention ensures potential energy increases with height, matching physical intuition.
To specify the system completely it suffices to give the potential energy $U(x)$.
