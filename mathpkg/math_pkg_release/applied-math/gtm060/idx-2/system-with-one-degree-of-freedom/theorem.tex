A \textit{system with one degree of freedom} is a system described by one differential equation
$$\ddot{x} = f(x), \quad x \in \mathbb{R}.$$
The kinetic energy is the quadratic form
$$T = \frac{1}{2} \dot{x}^2.$$
The potential energy is the function
$$U(x) = -\int_{x_0}^{x} f(\xi)\,d\xi.$$
Adding a constant to the potential energy does not change the equation of motion.
