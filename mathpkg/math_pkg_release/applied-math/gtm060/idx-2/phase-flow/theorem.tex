Let $M$ be a point in the phase plane. Denote by $M(t) = g^t M$ the value at time $t$ of the solution with initial condition $M$ at $t = 0$.

The mapping $g^t: \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2$ is a diffeomorphism for each $t$.
The family $\{g^t\}_{t \in \mathbb{R}}$ forms a \textit{one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms} of the phase plane:
$$g^{t+s} = g^t \circ g^s, \quad g^0 = \mathrm{id}, \quad g^{-t} = (g^t)^{-1}.$$
The mapping $g: \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}^2$ defined by $g(t, M) = g^t M$ is differentiable.
This group is called the \textit{phase flow} given by the system.
