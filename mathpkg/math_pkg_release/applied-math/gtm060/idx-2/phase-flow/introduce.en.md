The phase flow is the collection of time-evolution mappings $g^t$ that send each initial phase point $M$ to its position $g^t M$ after time $t$.
These transformations form a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms of the phase plane, encoding all possible motions of the system.
The phase flow exists whenever solutions can be extended to the whole time axis, which holds when the potential energy is positive.
If the potential energy allows solutions to escape to infinity in finite time, the phase flow is not defined.
