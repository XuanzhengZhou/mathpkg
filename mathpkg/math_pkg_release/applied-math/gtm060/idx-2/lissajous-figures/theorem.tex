Consider a system with two independent harmonic oscillators:
$$\ddot{x}_1 = -x_1, \quad \ddot{x}_2 = -\omega^2 x_2.$$
The conserved quantities are
$$E_1 = \frac{1}{2}\dot{x}_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}x_1^2, \quad E_2 = \frac{1}{2}\dot{x}_2^2 + \frac{1}{2}\omega^2 x_2^2.$$
The general solution is $x_1 = A_1 \sin(t + \varphi_1), x_2 = A_2 \sin(\omega t + \varphi_2)$.
The orbit in the $x_1, x_2$ plane given by these parametric equations is called a \textit{Lissajous figure}.
