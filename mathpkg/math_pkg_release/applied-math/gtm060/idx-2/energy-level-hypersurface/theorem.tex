The equation of conservation of energy
$$E = \frac{y_1^2 + y_2^2}{2} + U(x_1, x_2)$$
defines a three-dimensional hypersurface in four space: $E(x_1, x_2, y_1, y_2) = E_0$.
This surface $\pi_{E_0}$ remains invariant under the phase flow: $g^t \pi_{E_0} = \pi_{E_0}$.
The phase velocity vector field is tangent at every point to $\pi_{E_0}$. Therefore, $\pi_{E_0}$ is entirely composed of phase curves.
