A phase curve is the trajectory of a phase point moving under the phase velocity vector field.
By the law of conservation of energy, each phase curve is confined to a single energy level set $E(x, y) = h$.
This fact makes it possible to sketch the phase portrait directly from the graph of the potential energy $U(x)$, without solving the differential equation.
A phase curve may consist of a single point, which is then an equilibrium position.
