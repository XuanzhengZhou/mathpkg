The equation of motion $\ddot{x} = f(x)$ with $x \in E^2$ can be written as the system:
$$\dot{x}_1 = y_1, \quad \dot{x}_2 = y_2, \quad \dot{y}_1 = f_1(x), \quad \dot{y}_2 = f_2(x).$$
The \textit{phase space} of a system with two degrees of freedom is the four-dimensional space with coordinates $x_1, x_2, y_1, y_2$.
The system defines the phase velocity vector field in four space and the phase flow (a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms of four-dimensional phase space).
