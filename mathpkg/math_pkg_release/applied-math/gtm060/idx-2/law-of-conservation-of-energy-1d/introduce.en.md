The law of conservation of energy for a one-degree-of-freedom system states that the total energy $E = T + U$ remains constant along every solution of $\ddot{x} = f(x)$.
This is the simplest and most important first integral of the equations of motion.
It immediately implies that all trajectories lie on level sets $E(x, \dot{x}) = \text{const}$, enabling a qualitative analysis of the phase portrait directly from the graph of $U(x)$.
