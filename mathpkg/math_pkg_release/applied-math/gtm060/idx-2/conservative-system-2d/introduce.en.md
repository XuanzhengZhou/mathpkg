A conservative system with two degrees of freedom is one where the force vector field $f$ is the negative gradient of a scalar potential energy $U(x_1, x_2)$.
In contrast to the one-degree-of-freedom case where a potential energy can always be introduced via integration, not every two-degree-of-freedom system of the form $\ddot{x} = f(x)$ is conservative.
The existence of a potential energy is equivalent to the force field being a gradient field.
