The period-area derivative relation states that the period $T$ of a closed orbit in the phase plane equals the derivative of the enclosed area $S(E)$ with respect to energy.
This elegant formula connects the geometric area of the phase curve with its dynamical period.
It is a precursor to action-angle variables and the deeper relationship between geometry and dynamics in Hamiltonian mechanics.
