A lagrangian non-autonomous system is a Lagrangian system whose Lagrangian function depends explicitly on time. The Lagrangian is a smooth function on the product of the tangent bundle with the time axis:

$$L: TM \times \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}, \quad L = L(\mathbf{q}, \dot{\mathbf{q}}, t).$$

In a non-autonomous natural system, both the kinetic and potential energies may depend on time:

$$T: TM \times \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}, \quad U: M \times \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}.$$
