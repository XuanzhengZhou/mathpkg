Clairaut's theorem describes a conserved quantity for the motion of a point mass on a surface of revolution. The quantity $r \sin \alpha$ remains constant along any geodesic, where $r$ is the radial distance from the axis of revolution and $\alpha$ is the angle between the trajectory and the meridian direction. This follows from the conservation of angular momentum about the axis of symmetry. The theorem implies that the trajectory is confined to the region $r \geq r_0 \sin \alpha_0$, and the inclination relative to the meridians increases as the radius decreases, with reflection occurring at the minimum possible radius.
