A direct consequence of the theorem on averages is that every trajectory of a conditionally periodic motion with independent frequencies is dense on the torus. If a trajectory were not dense, one could construct a continuous function supported in a neighborhood that the trajectory avoids, giving a space average of 1 but a time average of 0, contradicting the theorem. This density property generalizes the fact that an irrational winding on the 2-torus fills it densely, and holds in any dimension when no resonance relations exist among the frequencies.
