The time average $f^*(\varphi_0)$ of a function $f$ on the torus $T^n$ is the long-term average of $f$ evaluated along the trajectory $\varphi(t) = \varphi_0 + \omega t$ starting at $\varphi_0$. Unlike the space average, the time average depends on the initial point $\varphi_0$ and on the frequency vector $\omega$. The existence of the time average is a subtle question; when the frequencies are independent, the time average exists everywhere and equals the space average for Riemann integrable functions, forming the content of the theorem on averages.
