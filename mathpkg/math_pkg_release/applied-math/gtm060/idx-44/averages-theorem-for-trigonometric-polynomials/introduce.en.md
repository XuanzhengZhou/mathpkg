The second lemma extends the averages theorem from single exponentials to trigonometric polynomials via linearity. Since both the time average and space average are linear functionals of $f$, and Lemma 1 establishes their equality on each exponential term $e^{i(k,\varphi)}$, the equality holds for any finite linear combination. This is a standard bootstrapping step in harmonic analysis: prove a result for characters, then extend to their linear span by the principle of superposition.
