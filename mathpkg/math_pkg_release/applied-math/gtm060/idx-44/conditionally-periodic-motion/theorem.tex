Definition. Let $T^n$ be the $n$-dimensional torus and $\varphi = (\varphi_1, \ldots, \varphi_n)$ mod $2\pi$ angular coordinates. A conditionally periodic motion is a one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms $T^n \to T^n$ given by the differential equations
$$\dot{\varphi} = \omega, \quad \omega = (\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n),$$
with solution $\varphi(t) = \varphi(0) + \omega t$. Each trajectory on the torus is called a winding of the torus.
