Conditionally periodic motion generalizes periodic motion to higher dimensions. It describes the simplest possible dynamics on an $n$-dimensional torus: each angular coordinate advances at a constant rate $\omega_i$. In the universal covering space $\mathbb{R}^n$, the trajectories are straight lines, which project to windings on the torus. When $n=2$ and the frequency ratio $\omega_1/\omega_2$ is rational, the trajectories are closed curves; when irrational, they are dense on the torus. Conditionally periodic motion appears naturally in integrable Hamiltonian systems through the action-angle formalism, where the $n$ angle variables undergo precisely this type of evolution.
