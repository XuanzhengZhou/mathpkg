The theorem on averages is a foundational result in the theory of conditionally periodic motion and serves as a prototype for ergodic theory. It states that for a Riemann integrable function on the $n$-torus, the long-term time average along any trajectory equals the spatial average over the whole torus, provided the frequencies are rationally independent. The proof proceeds in three lemmas: first for exponential functions $e^{i(k,\varphi)}$, then for trigonometric polynomials by linearity, and finally for general Riemann integrable functions by approximation with trigonometric polynomials from above and below. This theorem implies both the density and uniform distribution of trajectories when frequencies are independent.
