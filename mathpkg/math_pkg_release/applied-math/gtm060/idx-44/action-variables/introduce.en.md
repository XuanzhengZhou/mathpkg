For a Hamiltonian system with $n$ degrees of freedom possessing $n$ first integrals $F_1=H, F_2, \ldots, F_n$ in involution, Liouville's theorem ensures that compact connected level manifolds $M_f$ are $n$-dimensional tori. The action variables $I_i(f)$ are defined by integrating the $1$-form $p \, dq$ over the $n$ independent homology cycles $\gamma_i$ of the torus $M_f$. These integrals are independent of the choice of representative within each homology class because the symplectic form $\omega^2 = dp \wedge dq$ vanishes on $M_f$. When $\det(\partial I/\partial f) \neq 0$, the action variables together with angle variables form a coordinate system in a neighborhood of $M_f$.
