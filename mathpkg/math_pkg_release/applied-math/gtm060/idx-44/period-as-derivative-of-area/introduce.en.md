For a Hamiltonian system with one degree of freedom, the closed level curves $H = h$ in the phase plane represent periodic motions. The period $T$ of this motion can be elegantly expressed in terms of the action variable. In action-angle variables, the equations of motion give $\dot{\varphi} = \partial H/\partial I$, and since $I = \Pi(h)/(2\pi)$ for one degree of freedom, one obtains $T = 2\pi/\dot{\varphi} = d\Pi/dh$. This relation connects the geometric area enclosed by the phase curve to the dynamical period of oscillation.
