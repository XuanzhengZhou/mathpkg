A Galilean transformation is an affine transformation of four-dimensional Galilean space-time that preserves the time interval between events and the Euclidean distance between simultaneous events. These transformations form the Galilean group, which has dimension 10 and can be decomposed uniquely into a rotation, a space-time translation, and a uniform motion (boost). The Galilean group encodes the relativity principle of classical mechanics.
