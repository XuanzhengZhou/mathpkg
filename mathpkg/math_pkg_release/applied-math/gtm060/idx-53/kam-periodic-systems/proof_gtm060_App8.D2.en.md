The theorem follows by applying Kolmogorov's iterative scheme to the extended $(2n+1)$-dimensional phase space $\{(I, \varphi, t)\} = \mathbb{R}^n \times T^{n+1}$, treating time $t$ as an additional angle variable. The unperturbed system $H_0(I)$ is autonomous, so the $n+1$ frequencies are $(\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n, 1)$, where $\omega(I) = \partial H_0 / \partial I$. The Diophantine condition is imposed on these $n+1$ frequencies, and the nondegeneracy condition $\det |\partial^2 H_0 / \partial I^2| \neq 0$ guarantees that the first $n$ frequencies can be fixed independently by adjusting $I$.

For $n = 1$, the extended phase space is 3-dimensional, and the preserved 2-dimensional invariant tori divide the 3-dimensional extended phase space, preventing the action variable from drifting far from its initial value for all time -- this is the stability statement.
