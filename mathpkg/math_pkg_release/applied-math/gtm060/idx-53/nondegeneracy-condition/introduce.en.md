The nondegeneracy condition is a crucial hypothesis in the KAM theorem. It requires that the unperturbed Hamiltonian $H_0(I)$ have a non-singular Hessian matrix $\partial^2 H_0 / \partial I^2$. Geometrically, this means the frequency map from action space to frequency space is locally invertible, allowing one to fix a set of non-resonant frequencies and uniquely determine the action variables of the corresponding invariant torus. Systems in "general position" satisfy this condition, which also appears in various guises across all variants of the KAM theorem (autonomous, periodic, mappings, equilibrium positions, and fixed points).
