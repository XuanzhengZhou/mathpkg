The Kolmogorov-Arnold-Moser (KAM) theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of nearly-integrable Hamiltonian systems. It states that, under a nondegeneracy condition on the unperturbed Hamiltonian and for sufficiently small conservative perturbations, the majority of non-resonant invariant tori persist -- they are only slightly deformed. These preserved tori are densely filled with conditionally-periodic trajectories having the maximal number of independent frequencies. The complement of the union of these tori has small measure proportional to the perturbation size. This theorem resolved a longstanding problem about the stability of planetary motions and is one of the cornerstones of modern dynamical systems theory.
