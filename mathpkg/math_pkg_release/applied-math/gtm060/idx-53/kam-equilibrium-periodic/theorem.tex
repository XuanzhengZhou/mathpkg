Consider an equilibrium position of a Hamiltonian system with time-periodic coefficients. Assume stability in the linear approximation, so that $n$ characteristic frequencies $\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_n$ are defined. Assume no resonance relations
$$k_1 \omega_1 + \cdots + k_n \omega_n = 0 \quad \text{with } 0 < \sum |k_i| \leq 4$$
and also no resonances with the external frequency (the frequency of the periodic coefficients).

Then, under an appropriate nondegeneracy condition, there exist $(n+1)$-dimensional invariant tori in the $(2n+1)$-dimensional extended phase space near the circle $\tau = 0$ representing the equilibrium position.

In the case $n = 1$, the nondegeneracy condition reduces to the non-vanishing of the derivative of the period of small oscillations with respect to the square of the amplitude. Under this condition, the equilibrium position is Liapunov stable.
