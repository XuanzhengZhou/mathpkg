For an unperturbed integrable hamiltonian system $H_0(I)$, the isoenergetic nondegeneracy condition is
$$\det \begin{vmatrix}
\frac{\partial^2 H_0}{\partial I^2} & \frac{\partial H_0}{\partial I} \\[4pt]
\frac{\partial H_0}{\partial I} & 0
\end{vmatrix} \neq 0.$$

This is a weaker condition than full nondegeneracy $\det |\frac{\partial^2 H_0}{\partial I^2}| \neq 0$, and guarantees the existence on every energy level manifold of a set of invariant tori whose complement has small measure. The frequencies on these tori generally depend on the perturbation size $\varepsilon$, but the ratios of frequencies are preserved.

If $n = 2$, the condition for isoenergetic nondegeneracy also guarantees stability of the action variables for all time under sufficiently small perturbations.
