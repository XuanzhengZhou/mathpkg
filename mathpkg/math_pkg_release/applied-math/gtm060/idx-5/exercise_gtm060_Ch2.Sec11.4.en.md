**PROBLEM.** How does the height of an animal's jump depend on its size?

**ANSWER.** $\sim L^0$.

**Solution.** For a jump of height $h$ one needs energy proportional to $L^3 h$, and the work accomplished by muscular strength $F$ is proportional to $FL$. The force $F$ is proportional to $L^2$ (since the strength of bones is proportional to their section). Therefore, $L^3 h \sim L^2 L$, i.e., the height of a jump does not depend on the size of the animal. In fact, a jerboa and a kangaroo can jump to approximately the same height.
