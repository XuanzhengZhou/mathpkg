In a central force field, the force vector is always directed along the line connecting the particle to the center of force. This symmetry implies the conservation of angular momentum. The result is that $\mathbf{M} = [\mathbf{r}, \dot{\mathbf{r}}]$ is a constant vector, which geometrically means the motion is confined to a plane perpendicular to $\mathbf{M}$.
