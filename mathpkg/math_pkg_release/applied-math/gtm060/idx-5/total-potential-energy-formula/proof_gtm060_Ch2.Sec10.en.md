**Proof.** For a system consisting of two points $i$ and $j$ interacting via a central force, the potential energy of their interaction was shown to be $U_{ij}(|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j|) = \int f_{ij}(\rho) \, d\rho$. For a system of $n$ points with pairwise interactions, the total interaction potential energy is the sum over all unordered pairs: $\sum_{i > j} U_{ij}(|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j|)$. If each point is also subject to a conservative external force $\mathbf{F}'_i = -\partial U'_i / \partial \mathbf{r}_i$, then the total potential energy is $U(\mathbf{r}) = \sum_{i > j} U_{ij} + \sum_i U'_i$. Since each term is conservative, the total system is conservative and the total mechanical energy $E = T + U$ is conserved.
