**PROBLEM.** Show that if a field is axially symmetric and conservative, then its potential energy has the form $U = U(r, z)$, where $r$, $\varphi$, and $z$ are cylindrical coordinates. In particular, it follows from this that the vectors of the field lie in planes through the $z$ axis.
