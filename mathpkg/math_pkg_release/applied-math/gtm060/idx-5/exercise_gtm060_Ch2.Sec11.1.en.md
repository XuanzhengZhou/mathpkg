**PROBLEM.** If the radius of a planet is $\alpha$ times the radius of the earth and its mass $\beta$ times that of the earth, find the ratio of the acceleration of the force of gravity and the first and second cosmic velocities to the corresponding quantities for the earth.

**ANSWER.** $\gamma = \beta \alpha^{-2}$, $\delta = \sqrt{\beta/\alpha}$.

For the moon, for example, $\alpha = 1/3.7$ and $\beta = 1/81$. Therefore, the acceleration of gravity is about $1/6$ that of the earth ($\gamma \approx 1/6$), and the cosmic velocities are about $1/5$ those for the earth ($\delta \approx 1/4.7$).
