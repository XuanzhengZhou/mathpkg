The time variation of $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_1 - \mathbf{r}_2$ in the two-body problem is the same as that for the motion of a point of mass
$$m = \frac{m_1 m_2}{m_1 + m_2}$$
in a field with potential $U(|\mathbf{r}|)$, where $m$ is the \textit{reduced mass}. The center of mass $\mathbf{r}_0 = (m_1 \mathbf{r}_1 + m_2 \mathbf{r}_2)/(m_1 + m_2)$ moves uniformly and linearly.
