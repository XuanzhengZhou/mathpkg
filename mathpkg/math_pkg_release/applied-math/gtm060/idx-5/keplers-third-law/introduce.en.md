Kepler's Third Law establishes a precise quantitative relation between the orbital period and the size of an elliptical orbit. For any planet orbiting the sun, the square of its period is proportional to the cube of its major semi-axis. This law follows directly from the Newtonian inverse-square law of gravitation combined with the geometry of elliptical orbits.
