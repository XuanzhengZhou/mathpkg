This theorem characterizes canonical transformations via the exactness of a certain differential 1-form. Given a canonical transformation from $(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q})$ to $(\mathbf{P}, \mathbf{Q})$, the difference $\mathbf{p} \, d\mathbf{q} - \mathbf{P} \, d\mathbf{Q}$ is the total differential of a generating function $S(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{q})$. This generating function can be used, via the Hamilton-Jacobi method, to construct canonical transformations that simplify the Hamiltonian and thereby integrate the equations of motion. The converse also holds: exactness of the form implies the transformation is canonical.
