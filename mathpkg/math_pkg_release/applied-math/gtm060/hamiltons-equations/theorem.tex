\textbf{Theorem.} The system of Lagrange's equations is equivalent to the system of $2n$ first-order equations (Hamilton's equations):
$$\dot{p} = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}, \quad \dot{q} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p},$$
where $H(p, q, t) = p\dot{q} - L(q, \dot{q}, t)$ is the Legendre transform of the lagrangian function viewed as a function of $\dot{q}$.
