Hamilton's equations $\dot{q} = \partial H/\partial p$, $\dot{p} = -\partial H/\partial q$ are a system of $2n$ first-order differential equations equivalent to the $n$ second-order Lagrange equations. They exhibit a remarkable symmetry between coordinates and momenta, revealing the symplectic structure of mechanics. This canonical form is the starting point for Liouville's theorem on phase volume conservation, perturbation theory, and the transition to quantum mechanics.
