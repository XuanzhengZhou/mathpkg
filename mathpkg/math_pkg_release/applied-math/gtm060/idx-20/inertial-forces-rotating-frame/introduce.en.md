When describing motion from a rotating reference frame, Newton's equations must be supplemented by three additional inertial force terms. The first, arising only during nonuniform rotation, is proportional to the rate of change of the angular velocity. The Coriolis force depends linearly on the velocity of the moving point within the rotating frame and is responsible for the deflection of freely falling bodies and the precession of the Foucault pendulum. The centrifugal force is directed radially outward from the rotation axis and is independent of the relative velocity, acting even on bodies at rest in the rotating frame. Together, these three forces explain the observable effects of Earth's rotation on laboratory-scale experiments.
