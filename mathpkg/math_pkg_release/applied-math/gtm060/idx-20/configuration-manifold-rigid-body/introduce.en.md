The configuration manifold of a rigid body describes all possible positions the body can occupy in space. Despite the body consisting of potentially infinitely many point masses, the holonomic constraints fixing all pairwise distances reduce the degrees of freedom to exactly six: three parameters specifying the position of the center of mass (or any reference point) in $\mathbb{R}^3$, and three parameters describing the orientation via the rotation group $\text{SO}(3)$. This six-dimensional manifold structure is the foundation for the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations of rigid body dynamics.
