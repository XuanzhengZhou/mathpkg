When a coordinate system undergoes translational acceleration relative to an inertial frame, Newton's equations acquire an additional term. This term, called the inertial force, is proportional to the mass of the particle and the acceleration of the moving frame, directed opposite to that acceleration. The result is that from the perspective of an observer in the accelerating frame, it appears as if a uniform force field $-mW$ pervades all space. This explains phenomena such as the sensation of increased weight (overload) experienced during rocket takeoff or the apparent weightlessness during free fall.
