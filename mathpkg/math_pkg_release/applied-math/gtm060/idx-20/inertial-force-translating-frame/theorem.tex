In a coordinate system $K$ which moves by translation relative to an inertial system $k$, the motion of a mechanical system takes place as if the coordinate system were inertial, but on every point of mass $m$ an additional inertial force acts:
$$
F_{\text{inertial}} = -m\ddot{r},
$$
where $\ddot{r}$ is the acceleration of the system $K$ relative to $k$.

If $Q = q - r(t)$ denotes the position in the moving frame, then
$$
m\ddot{Q} = m\ddot{q} - m\ddot{r}.
$$
The effect of the translation of the coordinate system is reduced to the appearance of an additional homogeneous force field $-mW$, where $W = \ddot{r}$ is the acceleration of the origin.
