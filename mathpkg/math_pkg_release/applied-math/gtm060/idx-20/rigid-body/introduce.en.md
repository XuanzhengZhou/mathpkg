A rigid body is an idealized mechanical system composed of point masses whose mutual distances remain fixed throughout the motion. This holonomic constraint reduces the infinite degrees of freedom of an arbitrary system of particles to a finite-dimensional configuration manifold. The rigid body is a fundamental model in classical mechanics, serving as the starting point for the study of rotational dynamics, the motion of solid objects, and the theory of the inertia tensor.
