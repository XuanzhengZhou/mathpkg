The exterior derivative $d\omega^k$ of a $k$-form $\omega^k$ on a smooth manifold $M$ is defined as the principal multilinear part of the integral of $\omega^k$ over the boundaries of infinitesimal $(k+1)$-dimensional curvilinear parallelepipeds. Geometrically, this construction captures how the form varies from point to point, with the boundary integral measuring the "flux" of $\omega^k$ through an infinitesimal cell. The resulting object $d\omega^k$ is a $(k+1)$-form that is independent of the choice of local coordinates used in its definition, making it an intrinsic geometric operation on differential forms.
