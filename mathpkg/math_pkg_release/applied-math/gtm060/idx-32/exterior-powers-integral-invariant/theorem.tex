Let $(M^{2n}, \omega^2)$ be a symplectic manifold and $g^t$ a Hamiltonian phase flow. Consider the exterior powers of $\omega^2$:
$$
(\omega^2)^2 = \omega^2 \wedge \omega^2, \quad (\omega^2)^3 = \omega^2 \wedge \omega^2 \wedge \omega^2, \quad \ldots
$$
Each of the forms $(\omega^2)^2, (\omega^2)^3, (\omega^2)^4, \ldots$ is an integral invariant of the Hamiltonian phase flow $g^t$.

Moreover, $(\omega^2)^k = 0$ for $k > n$, and $(\omega^2)^n$ is a nondegenerate $2n$-form on $M^{2n}$. Defining the volume element on $M^{2n}$ via $(\omega^2)^n$, the Hamiltonian phase flow preserves volume (Liouville's theorem).
