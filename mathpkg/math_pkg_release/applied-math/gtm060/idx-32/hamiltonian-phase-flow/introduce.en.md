The Hamiltonian phase flow $g^t$ is the one-parameter group of diffeomorphisms generated by a Hamiltonian vector field $I\,dH$ on a symplectic manifold. It describes the time evolution of a Hamiltonian mechanical system: each point in phase space moves along the integral curves of the Hamiltonian vector field. The flow is characterized by the property that its velocity at each point equals the value of the Hamiltonian vector field at that point.
