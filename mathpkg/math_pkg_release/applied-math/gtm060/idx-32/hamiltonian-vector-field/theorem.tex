Let $H$ be a function on a symplectic manifold $(M^{2n}, \omega^2)$. Then $dH$ is a differential $1$-form on $M$, and at every point the symplectic isomorphism $I$ associates a tangent vector to it. The vector field $I\,dH$ on $M$ is called a \textbf{Hamiltonian vector field}; $H$ is called the \textbf{Hamiltonian function}.

On $\mathbb{R}^{2n} = \{(p, q)\}$ with the standard symplectic form, the Hamiltonian vector field gives the phase velocity vector field of Hamilton's canonical equations:
$$
\dot{x} = I\,dH(x) \;\Leftrightarrow\; \dot{p} = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}, \quad \dot{q} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p}.
$$
