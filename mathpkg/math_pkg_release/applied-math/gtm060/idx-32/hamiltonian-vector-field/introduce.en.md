A Hamiltonian vector field is the vector field obtained by applying the symplectic isomorphism $I$ to the differential $dH$ of a smooth function $H$ on a symplectic manifold. On $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ with coordinates $(p, q)$, this construction recovers the familiar phase velocity field of Hamilton's canonical equations $\dot{p} = -\partial H/\partial q$, $\dot{q} = \partial H/\partial p$. The function $H$ is called the Hamiltonian, and its Hamiltonian vector field generates the dynamics of the mechanical system.
