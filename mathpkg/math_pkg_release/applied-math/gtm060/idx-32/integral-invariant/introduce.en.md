An integral invariant of a differentiable map $g: M \to M$ is a differential $k$-form $\omega$ whose integral over any $k$-chain $c$ equals the integral over the image chain $gc$. This condition is equivalent to the pullback condition $g^*\omega = \omega$. Integral invariants are central to Hamiltonian mechanics: the symplectic form $\omega^2$ and its exterior powers are integral invariants of the Hamiltonian phase flow, leading to Liouville's theorem on volume preservation.
