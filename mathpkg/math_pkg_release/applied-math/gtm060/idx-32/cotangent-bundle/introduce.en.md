The cotangent bundle $T^*V$ of a differentiable manifold $V$ is the union of all cotangent spaces $T^*V_x$, each being the dual space of the tangent space $TV_x$. A point in $T^*V$ is a 1-form on $TV_x$ at some point $x \in V$, and in local coordinates the $2n$ numbers $(p, q)$ provide a coordinate system for $T^*V$. The cotangent bundle has a natural structure of a $2n$-dimensional differentiable manifold and serves as the phase space in Hamiltonian mechanics, equipped with the canonical symplectic form $\omega^2 = \sum dp_i \wedge dq_i$.
