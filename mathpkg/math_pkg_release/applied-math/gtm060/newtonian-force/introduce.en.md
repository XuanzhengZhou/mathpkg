Force in Newtonian mechanics is defined by $\mathbf{F} = m\ddot{\mathbf{x}}$ — the product of mass and acceleration. While mass is an intrinsic property of a body, force characterizes the interaction (e.g., a spring's extension). The unit of force, the newton, is defined such that a 1 kg mass experiences gravitational acceleration of 9.8 m/s^2 at Earth's surface.
