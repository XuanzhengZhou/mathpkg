Young's inequality $px \leq f(x) + g(p)$ is a direct consequence of the definition of the Legendre transform: $g(p)$ is the maximum of $px - f(x)$ over $x$, so $px - f(x) \leq g(p)$ for all $x$. This simple inequality has profound consequences, including the Holder inequality and the duality of $L^p$ spaces. In mechanics, it underlies the relationship between kinetic energy expressed in terms of velocity and in terms of momentum.
