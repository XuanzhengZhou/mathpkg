The Laplace vector (also known as the eccentricity vector or Runge-Lenz vector) points from the focus of a Keplerian ellipse to its center. It encodes both the orbital eccentricity (its magnitude) and the direction to perihelion (its direction). In the many-planet problem, the secular evolution of each planet's Laplace vector decomposes into a sum of uniformly rotating components, each with a characteristic frequency. This description arises from the linearization of the averaged Hamiltonian near the equilibrium of zero eccentricities.
