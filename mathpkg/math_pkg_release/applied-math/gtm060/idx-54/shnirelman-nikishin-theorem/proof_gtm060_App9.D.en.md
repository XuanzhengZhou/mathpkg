The proof proceeds along similar lines to the generating function method for the annulus. For an area-preserving diffeomorphism of the sphere, one constructs a generating function whose gradient vector field has index equal to the Euler characteristic of the sphere, namely $2$. By the Poincaré-Hopf theorem, a smooth vector field on $S^2$ with index $2$ must have at least two zeros (counting multiplicities). These zeros correspond to fixed points of the original diffeomorphism.

More concretely, the index of the gradient field reflects the fact that $S^2$ cannot admit a nowhere-vanishing vector field. The area-preserving condition ensures that the generating function can be made single-valued globally (up to a constant), and the fixed points arise as its critical points. The lower bound of two geometrically distinct fixed points is sharp, as demonstrated by an irrational rotation of the sphere about an axis, which has exactly two fixed points (the poles).
