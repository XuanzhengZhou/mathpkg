Suppose that we are given an area-preserving homeomorphic mapping of the planar circular annulus to itself. Assume that the boundary circles of the annulus are turned in different directions under the mapping. Then this mapping has at least two fixed points.

The condition that the boundary circles are turned in different directions means that, if we choose coordinates $(x, y) \bmod 2\pi$ on the annulus so that the boundary circles are $x = a$ and $x = b$, then the mapping is defined by the formula

$$(x, y) \mapsto (f(x, y), y + g(x, y)),$$

where the functions $f$ and $g$ are continuous and $2\pi$-periodic in $y$, with $f(a, y) \equiv a$, $f(b, y) \equiv b$, and $g(a, y) < 0$, $g(b, y) > 0$ for all $y$.
