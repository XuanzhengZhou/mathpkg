There exist infinitely many periodic solutions close to a given linearly stable periodic solution of general form in a Hamiltonian system. Equivalently, there exist infinitely many periodic points in a neighborhood of a fixed point of a linearly stable nondegenerate symplectic mapping of a space to itself.

In the proof, the mapping is first approximated by its normal form, and then the connection between fixed points of a mapping and critical points of the generating function is used.
