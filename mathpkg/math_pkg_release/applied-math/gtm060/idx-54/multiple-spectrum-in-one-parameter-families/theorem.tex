In typical one-parameter families of quadratic forms on Euclidean space, the spectrum is simple for all values of the parameter. A multiple spectrum (degeneracy of eigenvalues) is observed only for families depending on two or more parameters.

In other words, the eigenvalues of a quadratic form on Euclidean space (or the lengths of the axes of an ellipsoid) change continuously under continuous changes of the parameters. Although in a one-parameter family the eigenvalues can approach closely, when they are sufficiently close they begin to repel one another and do not coalesce.

Consequently, to make the ellipsoid of inertia of a rigid body into an ellipsoid of revolution by moving adjustable masses, one generally needs at least two adjustable masses (i.e., two parameters), not just one.
