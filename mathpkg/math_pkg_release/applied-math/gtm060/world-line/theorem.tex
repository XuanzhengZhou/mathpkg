A curve in galilean space which appears in some (and therefore every) galilean coordinate system as the graph of a motion, is called a world line.

A motion of a system of $n$ points gives, in galilean space, $n$ world lines. In a galilean coordinate system they are described by $n$ mappings $\mathbf{x}_i: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$, $i = 1, \ldots, n$.
