\textbf{Theorem.} The system of Lagrange's equations $\dot{p} = \partial L/\partial q$, $p = \partial L/\partial \dot{q}$, is equivalent to the system of Hamilton's equations $\dot{p} = -\partial H/\partial q$, $\dot{q} = \partial H/\partial p$, where $H(p, q, t) = p\dot{q} - L(q, \dot{q}, t)$.

This applies to all variational problems, not just to the lagrangian equations of mechanics.
