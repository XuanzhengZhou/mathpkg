($\Leftarrow$): Suppose the work of $F$ does not depend on the path. Then $U(M) = -\int_{M_0}^M (F, dS)$ is well defined as a function of $M$. It is easy to verify that $F = -\partial U / \partial x$, so the field is conservative.

($\Rightarrow$): Suppose $F$ is conservative and $U$ is its potential energy. Then
$$\int_{M_0}^M (F, dS) = -U(M) + U(M_0),$$
so the work does not depend on the shape of the path.
