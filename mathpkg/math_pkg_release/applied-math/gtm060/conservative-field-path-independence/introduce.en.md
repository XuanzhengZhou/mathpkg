A force field is conservative exactly when the work done is independent of the path taken, depending only on the endpoints. This is equivalent to the work around any closed loop being zero. In this case, a potential energy function $U$ exists such that $F = -\partial U/\partial x$, and the work from $M_0$ to $M$ equals $U(M_0) - U(M)$. This theorem bridges the geometric condition (path independence) with the existence of a scalar potential.
