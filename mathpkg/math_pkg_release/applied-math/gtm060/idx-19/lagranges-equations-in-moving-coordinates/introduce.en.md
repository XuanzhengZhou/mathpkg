This theorem establishes that Lagrange's equations are covariant under smooth, time-dependent coordinate transformations. If a trajectory satisfies the Euler-Lagrange equations in the original coordinates $(q, \dot{q}, t)$, then expressing the Lagrangian in new coordinates $Q = Q(q, t)$ and writing the equations in $Q$ produces the same form. The proof follows directly from the variational principle: the trajectory is an extremal of the action functional, and the action is invariant under change of variables, so the trajectory remains extremal in the new coordinates.
