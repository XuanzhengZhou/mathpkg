A motion of $K$ relative to $k$ is a smooth, time-dependent mapping $D_t: K \to k$ that preserves both the Euclidean metric and the orientation. This captures the kinematic notion of how one reference frame moves with respect to another without any deformation. The motion describes the position of any point of $K$ as seen from $k$ at each instant $t$. This concept serves as the foundation for studying rigid body motion, since a rigid body can be modeled as a moving coordinate system attached to it.
