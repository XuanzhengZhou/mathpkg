This lemma shows that the operator $A = \dot{B}B^{-1}$, which arises when computing the velocity of a point in a rotating frame, is necessarily skew-symmetric. The proof relies on the orthogonality condition $BB^t = E$ for a rotation operator between Euclidean spaces. Differentiating this identity yields $\dot{B}B^t + B\dot{B}^t = 0$, which after substituting $B^t = B^{-1}$ gives $A + A^t = 0$. The skew-symmetry of $A$ is the key step in establishing that the velocity field of a rigid body rotation can be expressed as a cross product with the angular velocity vector.
