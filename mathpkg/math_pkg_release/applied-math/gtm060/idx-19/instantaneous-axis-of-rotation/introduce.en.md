This corollary follows directly from the existence of the instantaneous angular velocity vector $\omega$. Since $\dot{q} = [\omega, q]$, points lying on the line through the origin parallel to $\omega$ have $q \parallel \omega$, so their cross product vanishes and they have zero velocity. This line is the instantaneous axis of rotation. Points farther from this axis have proportionally larger velocities, perpendicular to both $\omega$ and their position vector. The angular velocity vector in the body frame, $\Omega = B^{-1}\omega$, describes the same rotation as seen from the moving frame $K$.
