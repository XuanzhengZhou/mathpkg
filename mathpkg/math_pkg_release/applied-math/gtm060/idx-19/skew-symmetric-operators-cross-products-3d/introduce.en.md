This lemma states that every skew-symmetric linear operator on $\mathbb{R}^3$ corresponds uniquely to taking the cross product with some fixed vector $\omega$. The proof uses a dimension argument: the space of skew-symmetric $3 \times 3$ matrices has dimension 3, and the map $\omega \mapsto [\omega, \cdot]$ is an injective linear map from $\mathbb{R}^3$ (also dimension 3) into this space, hence surjective. This fact is fundamental to rigid body kinematics because it directly connects the skew-symmetric operator $A = \dot{B}B^{-1}$ to the angular velocity vector $\omega$.
