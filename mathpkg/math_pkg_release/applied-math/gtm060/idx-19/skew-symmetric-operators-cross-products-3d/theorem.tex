Every skew-symmetric operator $A$ on a three-dimensional oriented Euclidean space is the operator of vector multiplication by a fixed vector:

$$Aq = [\omega, q] \quad \text{for all } q \in \mathbb{R}^3.$$

The vector $\omega$ is uniquely determined by $A$: if $A$ has the matrix
$$\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -\omega_3 & \omega_2 \\ \omega_3 & 0 & -\omega_1 \\ -\omega_2 & \omega_1 & 0 \end{bmatrix},$$
then $\omega = \omega_1 e_1 + \omega_2 e_2 + \omega_3 e_3$.
