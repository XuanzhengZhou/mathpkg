\textbf{Theorem.} The curve $\gamma: x = x(t)$ is an extremal of the functional $\Phi(\gamma) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} L(x, \dot{x}, t) dt$ on the space of curves with fixed endpoints $x(t_0) = x_0$, $x(t_1) = x_1$, if and only if the Euler-Lagrange equation holds:
$$\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x}}\right) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial x} = 0.$$
