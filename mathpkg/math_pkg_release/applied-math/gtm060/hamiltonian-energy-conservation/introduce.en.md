In Hamiltonian form, energy conservation is elegantly expressed: the total time derivative of $H$ along a trajectory equals the partial derivative with respect to explicit time. If $H$ is autonomous ($\partial H/\partial t = 0$), then $H$ is a constant of motion. This follows immediately from the antisymmetry of Hamilton's equations: $dH/dt = (\partial H/\partial p)\dot{p} + (\partial H/\partial q)\dot{q} + \partial H/\partial t = (\partial H/\partial p)(-\partial H/\partial q) + (\partial H/\partial q)(\partial H/\partial p) + \partial H/\partial t = \partial H/\partial t$.
