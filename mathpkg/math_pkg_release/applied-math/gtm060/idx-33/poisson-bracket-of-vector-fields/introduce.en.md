The Poisson bracket $[A, B]$ of two vector fields $A$ and $B$ on a smooth manifold is the vector field whose associated differential operator is the commutator $L_B L_A - L_A L_B$. This bracket measures the non-commutativity of the flows generated by $A$ and $B$. In local coordinates, its components are given by a formula analogous to the Lie derivative. The Poisson bracket operation turns the infinite-dimensional vector space of smooth vector fields into a Lie algebra, making it a fundamental algebraic structure in differential geometry and mechanics.
