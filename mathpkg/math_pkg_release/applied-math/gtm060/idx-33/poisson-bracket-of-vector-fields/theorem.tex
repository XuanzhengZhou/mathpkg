The \textit{Poisson bracket} (or commutator) of two vector fields $A$ and $B$ on a manifold $M$ is the vector field $C$ for which
$$L_C = L_B L_A - L_A L_B,$$
where $L_A$ and $L_B$ are the first-order differential operators corresponding to $A$ and $B$. The Poisson bracket is denoted by
$$C = [A, B].$$

In local coordinates $(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ where $A = (A_1, \ldots, A_n)$ and $B = (B_1, \ldots, B_n)$, the components of the Poisson bracket are given by
$$[A, B]_j = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( B_i \frac{\partial A_j}{\partial x_i} - A_i \frac{\partial B_j}{\partial x_i} \right).$$
