A \textit{Lie algebra} is a vector space $L$, together with a bilinear skew-symmetric operation $[\cdot,\cdot]: L \times L \rightarrow L$ which satisfies the Jacobi identity:
$$[ [A, B], C] + [ [B, C], A] + [ [C, A], B] = 0.$$
The operation is usually denoted by square brackets and called the commutator.
