A Lie algebra is an algebraic structure consisting of a vector space $L$ equipped with a bilinear, skew-symmetric bracket operation $[\cdot,\cdot]$ that satisfies the Jacobi identity. The archetypal example is the three-dimensional Euclidean space with the vector cross product. Lie algebras capture the infinitesimal structure of Lie groups and provide the natural setting for studying continuous symmetries. In mechanics, the Poisson bracket of vector fields and the Poisson bracket of functions on phase space both give rise to Lie algebra structures.
