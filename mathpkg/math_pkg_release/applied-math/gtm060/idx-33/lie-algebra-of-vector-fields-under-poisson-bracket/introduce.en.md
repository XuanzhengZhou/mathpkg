The vector space of smooth vector fields on a manifold, equipped with the Poisson bracket $[A, B]$, forms a Lie algebra. Bilinearity and skew-symmetry are immediate from the definition $L_{[A,B]} = L_B L_A - L_A L_B$, while the Jacobi identity follows from a cancellation argument: the sum $L_{[[A,B],C]} + L_{[[B,C],A]} + L_{[[C,A],B]}$ expands to twelve terms, with each term appearing twice with opposite signs. This theorem connects differential geometry to the algebraic theory of Lie algebras.
