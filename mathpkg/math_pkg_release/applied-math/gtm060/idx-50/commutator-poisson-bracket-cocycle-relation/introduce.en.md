For a Poisson action with single-valued Hamiltonians, the linear map $a \mapsto H_a$ from the Lie algebra to Hamiltonian functions is not necessarily a Lie algebra homomorphism. Instead, the Hamiltonian of the commutator $H_{[a,b]}$ equals the Poisson bracket $(H_a, H_b)$ plus a constant $C(a,b)$. This constant measures the obstruction to the map being a homomorphism and encodes a nontrivial $2$-dimensional cohomology class of the Lie algebra of globally Hamiltonian vector fields.
