For two elements $a, b \in \mathfrak{g}$, the Hamiltonian functions $H_a$ and $H_b$ generate Hamiltonian vector fields whose flows are the actions of the corresponding one-parameter subgroups. The commutator $[a,b]$ in the Lie algebra corresponds to the Lie bracket of the associated vector fields. Since the Poisson bracket of Hamiltonian functions corresponds (up to sign convention) to the Lie bracket of their Hamiltonian vector fields, we have:

$$\text{Ham}_{(H_a, H_b)} = [\text{Ham}_{H_a}, \text{Ham}_{H_b}]$$

up to the chosen sign convention. The flow of $[a,b]$ is Hamiltonian with a Hamiltonian function $H_{[a,b]}$. The difference between $H_{[a,b]}$ and $(H_a, H_b)$ can only be a function whose Hamiltonian vector field vanishes, i.e., a locally constant function (a Casimir). On a connected component, this is a genuine constant $C(a,b)$. The bilinearity and skew-symmetry of $C$ follow from the corresponding properties of the Lie bracket and the Poisson bracket.
