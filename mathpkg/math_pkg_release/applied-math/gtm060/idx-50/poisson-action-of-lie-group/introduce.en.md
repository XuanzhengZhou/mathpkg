A Poisson action of a Lie group $G$ on a symplectic manifold $(M^{2n}, \omega^2)$ is a symplectic action where every one-parameter subgroup of $G$ acts as a locally Hamiltonian phase flow. In many important cases these flows admit single-valued Hamiltonian functions, leading to a deep connection between the Lie algebra structure of $G$ and the Poisson algebra of functions on $M$. This construction, studied by Jacobi and Poincare, is the foundation for the theory of reduction of mechanical systems with symmetries.
