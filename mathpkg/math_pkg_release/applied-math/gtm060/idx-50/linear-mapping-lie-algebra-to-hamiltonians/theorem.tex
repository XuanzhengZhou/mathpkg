Given a Poisson action of a Lie group $G$ on a symplectic manifold $M$ for which single-valued Hamiltonian functions exist, one can construct a linear mapping of the Lie algebra of $G$ into the Lie algebra of Hamiltonian functions on $M$. The Hamiltonian functions are determined up to addition of constants, which can be chosen so that the dependence of $H_a$ upon $a \in \mathfrak{g}$ is linear: it suffices to choose arbitrarily the constants in the Hamiltonians for a set of basis vectors of the Lie algebra of $G$, and then define the Hamiltonian function for each element of the algebra as a linear combination of the basis functions.
