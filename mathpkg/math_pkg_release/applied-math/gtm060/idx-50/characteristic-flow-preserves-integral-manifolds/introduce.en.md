The characteristic flow $g^t$ on a hypersurface in a contact manifold has the crucial property of preserving integral manifolds of the contact field. If $\eta(0)$ is tangent to such an integral manifold $I$, then for all small $t$ the transported vector $\eta(t) = g^t_* \eta$ remains in the contact plane, so $g^t I$ remains an integral manifold. By flowing an $(n-1)$-dimensional integral manifold along the characteristic direction, one obtains an $n$-dimensional Legendre manifold, providing a geometric method for integrating first-order PDEs.
