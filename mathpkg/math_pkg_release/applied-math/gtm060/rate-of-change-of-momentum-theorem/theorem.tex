\textbf{Theorem.} The rate of change of momentum of a system is equal to the sum of all external forces acting on points of the system:
$$\frac{dP}{dt} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} F'_i,$$
where $F'_i$ are external forces.

\textbf{Corollary 1.} The momentum of a closed system is conserved: $P = \text{const}$.

\textbf{Corollary 2.} If the sum of external forces is perpendicular to some direction, then the projection of momentum on that direction is conserved.
