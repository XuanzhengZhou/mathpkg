The work done by a continuous force field $\mathbf{F}$ along a rectifiable curve $l$ is defined as the line integral $\int_l (\mathbf{F}, d\mathbf{S})$, obtained as the limit of Riemann sums of scalar products. This generalizes the work of a constant force to arbitrary paths and non-uniform force fields.
