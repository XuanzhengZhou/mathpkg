Newton's law of universal gravitation states that the acceleration due to gravity at distance $r$ from the center of the earth is inversely proportional to the square of the distance:
$$\ddot{x} = -g \frac{r_0^2}{r^2},$$
where $r = r_0 + x$, $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, and $r_0$ is the radius of the earth.

Equivalently, the potential energy is
$$U = -\frac{k}{r}, \quad k = gr_0^2,$$
which is inversely proportional to the distance to the center of the earth.

For a system of $n$ gravitating bodies, the potential energy is
$$U = -\sum_{i<j} \frac{m_i m_j}{\|\mathbf{x}_i - \mathbf{x}_j\|}.$$
