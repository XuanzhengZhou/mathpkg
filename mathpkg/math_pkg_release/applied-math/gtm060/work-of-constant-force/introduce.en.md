The work done by a constant force $\mathbf{F}$ along a straight displacement $\mathbf{S}$ is the scalar product $A = (\mathbf{F}, \mathbf{S})$. Only the component of force along the direction of motion contributes to work. This is the foundation for defining work along curved paths via line integrals.
