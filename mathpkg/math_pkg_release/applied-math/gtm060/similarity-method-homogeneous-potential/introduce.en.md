The similarity method uses scaling (dimensional analysis) to extract qualitative information from the equations of motion without solving them. For homogeneous potentials $U(\alpha r) = \alpha^\nu U(r)$, scaling the spatial coordinates changes the orbit in a predictable way. This method gives Kepler's third law ($\nu = -1$) and the isochronism of the harmonic oscillator ($\nu = 2$) as immediate consequences of homogeneity, without integrating any equations.
