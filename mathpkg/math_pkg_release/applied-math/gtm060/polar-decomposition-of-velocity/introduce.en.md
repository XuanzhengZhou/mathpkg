In polar coordinates, the velocity vector splits into radial and tangential components: $\dot{r} = \dot{r} e_r + r\dot{\varphi} e_\varphi$. The radial component $\dot{r}$ measures change in distance from the origin, while the tangential component $r\dot{\varphi}$ measures rotational speed. This decomposition is fundamental for analyzing central force motion, as it directly connects the angular coordinate to the angular momentum $M = r^2 \dot{\varphi}$.
