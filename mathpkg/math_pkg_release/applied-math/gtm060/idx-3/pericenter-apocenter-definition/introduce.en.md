In a central force problem, the radial coordinate $r$ oscillates between a minimum $r_{\min}$ (pericenter) and a maximum $r_{\max}$ (apocenter), determined by the energy condition $V(r) \leq E$. These turning points are where $\dot{r} = 0$, though the angular velocity $\dot{\varphi} \neq 0$ so the point is not at rest. Each ray from the center to a pericenter or apocenter is an axis of symmetry of the orbit. In general the orbit is not closed: the angle $\Phi$ between successive pericenters is given by a quadrature, and the orbit closes only when $\Phi$ is commensurable with $2\pi$.
