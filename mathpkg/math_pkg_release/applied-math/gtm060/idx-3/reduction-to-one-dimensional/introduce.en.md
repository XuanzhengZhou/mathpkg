This theorem reduces the two-dimensional motion of a point in a central field to an equivalent one-dimensional problem. Angular momentum conservation provides $\dot{\varphi} = M/r^2$, which is substituted into the radial equation of motion. The resulting equation $\ddot{r} = -\partial V/\partial r$ with $V(r) = U(r) + M^2/(2r^2)$ governs the radial coordinate alone. The additional term $M^2/(2r^2)$ acts as a centrifugal barrier, preventing the point from reaching $r = 0$ when $M \neq 0$. The total energy $E_1 = \dot{r}^2/2 + V(r)$ in the reduced problem equals the original total energy $E$.
