This theorem establishes that every central vector field is automatically conservative, with a potential energy that is purely a function of the radial distance from the center. The proof uses the fact that in a central field we can write $F(r) = \Phi(r) e_r$, where $\Phi$ is a scalar function and $e_r$ is the radial unit vector. The work integral then reduces to a one-dimensional integral $\int_{r(M_1)}^{r(M_2)} \Phi(r) dr$, which depends only on the endpoints. This result underlies the analysis of Newtonian gravitation and other central force problems.
