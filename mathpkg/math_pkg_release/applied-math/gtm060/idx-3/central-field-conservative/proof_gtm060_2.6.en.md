According to the previous problem, we may set $F(r) = \Phi(r)e_r$, where $r$ is the radius vector with respect to $0$, $r$ is its length and the unit vector $e_r = r/|r|$ its direction. Then

$$\int_{M_1}^{M_2} (F, dS) = \int_{r(M_1)}^{r(M_2)} \Phi(r)dr,$$

and this integral is obviously independent of the path.
