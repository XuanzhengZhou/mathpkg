This definition formalizes the equation of motion for a unit-mass material point moving under a central force. The force is directed along the radial direction $e_r$ with magnitude $\Phi(r)$ depending only on the distance $r$ from the center. To define the angular momentum as a vector cross product, the plane of motion is embedded in three-dimensional oriented Euclidean space. The radial unit vector $e_r$ points from the center $0$ to the position $r$, and the equation $\ddot{r} = \Phi(r)e_r$ encapsulates Newton's second law for a central force.
