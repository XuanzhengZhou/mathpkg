Angular momentum is a fundamental conserved quantity in central force problems. For a unit-mass point, it is defined as the cross product $M = [r, \dot{r}]$ of the position vector and velocity vector. Geometrically, $M$ is perpendicular to the plane of motion, and its magnitude equals twice the sectorial velocity (the rate at which the radius vector sweeps out area). The Russian term for angular momentum translates literally as "kinetic moment." The concept generalizes the moment of a vector: the moment of a vector $a$ applied at point $r$ relative to point $0$ is $[r, a]$.
