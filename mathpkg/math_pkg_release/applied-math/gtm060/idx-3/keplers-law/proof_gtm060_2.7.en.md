Consequently, the angular momentum is

$$M = [r, \dot{r}] = [r, \dot{r} e_r] + [r, r \dot{\varphi} e_\varphi] = r \dot{\varphi}[r, e_\varphi] = r^2 \dot{\varphi}[e_r, e_\varphi].$$

Thus, the quantity $M = r^2 \dot{\varphi}$ is preserved.

Kepler called the rate of change of the area $S(t)$ swept out by the radius vector the sectorial velocity $C$:

$$C = \frac{dS}{dt}.$$

Since

$$\Delta S = S(t + \Delta t) - S(t) = \frac{1}{2} r^2 \dot{\varphi} \Delta t + o(\Delta t),$$

this means that the sectorial velocity

$$C = \frac{dS}{dt} = \frac{1}{2} r^2 \dot{\phi} = \frac{1}{2} M$$

is half the angular momentum of our point of mass 1, and therefore constant.
