The Legendre transformation converts a convex function $f(x)$ into its dual $g(p) = \max_x(px - f(x))$. Geometrically, $g(p)$ is the negative of the $y$-intercept of the tangent line to the graph of $f$ with slope $p$. In mechanics, the Legendre transform of the Lagrangian $L(q, \dot{q})$ with respect to $\dot{q}$ yields the Hamiltonian $H(p, q) = p\dot{q} - L$, converting the second-order Lagrange equations into the first-order Hamilton equations. The transformation is involutive: applying it twice returns the original function.
