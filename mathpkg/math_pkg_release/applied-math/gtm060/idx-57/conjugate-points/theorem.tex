As our Lagrangian manifold $M$, we may take the fiber of the cotangent bundle passing through the point $(p_0, q_0)$ (given by the condition $q = q_0$). In this case a focal point to $M$ on the phase curve going out from $(p_0, q_0)$ is called conjugate to the original point. More precisely, the projection of this focal point onto the configuration space is said to be conjugate to the point $q_0$ along the extremal in the configuration space starting at $q_0$ with momentum $p_0$.

In the even more special case of motion along a geodesic on a Riemannian manifold, a focal point to a fiber of the cotangent bundle is called conjugate to the initial point of the geodesic along this geodesic. For example, the south pole of a sphere is conjugate to the north pole along any meridian.
