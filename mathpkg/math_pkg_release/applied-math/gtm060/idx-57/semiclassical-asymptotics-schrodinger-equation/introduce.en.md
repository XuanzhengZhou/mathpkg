This theorem provides the semiclassical asymptotic form of the solution to Schrödinger's equation when the initial condition is a rapidly oscillating function (a WKB ansatz) supported on a Lagrangian manifold. As Planck's constant $h \to 0$, the wave function $\psi(Q, t)$ is expressed as a sum over classical trajectories that arrive at the configuration point $Q$ at time $t$. Each term involves the classical action $S_j$, the Morse index $\mu_j$ (counting focal points along the trajectory), and a Jacobian factor $|DQ/Dq_j|^{-1/2}$ accounting for focusing and caustics. The formula is the foundation of Maslov's canonical operator method.
