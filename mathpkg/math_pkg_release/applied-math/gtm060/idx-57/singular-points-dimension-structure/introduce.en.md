This proposition describes the stratified structure of the singular locus of a Lagrangian manifold under projection onto the configuration space. For an $n$-dimensional Lagrangian manifold in general position, the singular points are organized by the corank of the projection map. The simplest singularities form an $(n-1)$-dimensional manifold (corank 1), and the next level (corank 2) forms an $(n-3)$-dimensional manifold. Notably, there is no piece of dimension $n-2$ — this is a gap phenomenon in the singularity stratification. The caustic (the projection of the singular set) contains pieces of every dimension from 0 to $n-1$.
