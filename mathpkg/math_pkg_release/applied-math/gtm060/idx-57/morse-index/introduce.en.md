The Morse index $\mu_j$ is a nonnegative integer associated to a phase curve on a Lagrangian manifold. It counts the number of focal points encountered along the trajectory between the initial time $0$ and the final time $t$. Focal points are those moments where the Lagrangian manifold, when projected onto the configuration space, fails to satisfy a nondegeneracy condition (i.e., the projection develops a singularity). The Morse index enters the semiclassical asymptotics of the Schrödinger equation via the Maslov phase correction $e^{-(i\pi/2)\mu_j}$.
