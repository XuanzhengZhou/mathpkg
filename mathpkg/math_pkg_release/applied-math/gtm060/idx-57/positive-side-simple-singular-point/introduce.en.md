The positive side of a simple (corank 1) singular point on a Lagrangian manifold is defined using the sign of the partial derivative $\partial q_1 / \partial p_1$ in local Darboux-type coordinates. Near a simple singular point, the Lagrangian manifold is locally the graph $(q_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n)(p_1, q_2, \ldots, q_n)$, and the singular locus is given by $\partial q_1 / \partial p_1 = 0$. For generic Lagrangian manifolds, this derivative changes sign across the singular hypersurface, and the side where $\partial q_1 / \partial p_1 > 0$ is designated as the positive side. This definition is coordinate-independent and consistent across different singular points, providing a global co-orientation of the singular locus.
