Consider a simple singular point on a Lagrangian manifold. In coordinates $(q_1, \ldots, q_n)$ on the configuration space and dual coordinates $(p_1, \ldots, p_n)$ in the cotangent fiber, in a neighborhood of the singular point the Lagrangian manifold can be represented as the graph of a vector function $(q_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n)$ of the variables $(p_1, q_2, \ldots, q_n)$ (or an analogous form with a different distinguished coordinate).

Singular points near the given one are defined by the condition $\partial q_1 / \partial p_1 = 0$. For Lagrangian manifolds in general position, this derivative changes sign upon passing from one side of the manifold of singular points to the other. The side where this derivative is positive is called the positive side.
