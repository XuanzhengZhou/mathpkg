Focal points are those points along a phase curve where the Lagrangian manifold, when projected onto the configuration space, fails to be a local diffeomorphism -- that is, where the nondegeneracy condition of the projection breaks down. These points are intrinsic to the geometry of the phase flow on the cotangent bundle of the configuration space and are independent of Schrödinger's equation. Their count along a phase curve determines the Morse index, which contributes a phase shift in semiclassical asymptotics.
