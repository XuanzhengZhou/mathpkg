The number of degrees of freedom of a mechanical system is defined as the dimension of its configuration space, considered as a differentiable manifold. For a system of $n$ points without constraints the configuration space is $3n$-dimensional; each ideal holonomic constraint reduces the dimension by one. This geometric viewpoint unifies the treatment of constrained and unconstrained Lagrangian systems.
