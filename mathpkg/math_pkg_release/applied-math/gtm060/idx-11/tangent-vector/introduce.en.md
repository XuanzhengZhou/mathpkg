A tangent vector at a point $x$ on a manifold $M$ is defined intrinsically as an equivalence class of curves passing through $x$, where two curves are equivalent if their difference vanishes to first order in any (and therefore every) chart. This definition is independent of any embedding of $M$ into Euclidean space. The collection of all tangent vectors at $x$ forms the tangent space $TM_x$, and for embedded submanifolds this definition coincides with the geometric one: tangent vectors are velocity vectors of curves lying in $M$, or equivalently, vectors orthogonal to the gradients of the defining functions.
