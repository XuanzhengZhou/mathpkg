A Riemannian manifold is a differentiable manifold equipped with a smoothly varying positive-definite quadratic form (the Riemannian metric) on each tangent space. This additional structure allows one to measure the length of curves via the integral $l(\gamma) = \int \sqrt{\langle \dot{x}, \dot{x} \rangle}\, dt$, generalizing the notion of arc length from Euclidean space. The configuration space of a mechanical system, when endowed with the kinetic energy quadratic form, naturally acquires the structure of a Riemannian manifold.
