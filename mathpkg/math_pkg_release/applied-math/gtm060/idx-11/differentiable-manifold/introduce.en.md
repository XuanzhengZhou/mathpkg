A differentiable manifold is a topological space equipped with an atlas of compatible charts mapping open subsets of Euclidean space into the manifold. Charts are open sets in $\mathbb{R}^n$ together with one-to-one mappings onto subsets of $M$, and they are compatible if the transition functions between them are differentiable. An equivalence class of atlases (where two atlases are equivalent if their union is also an atlas) defines the differentiable structure. For connected manifolds, the dimension $n$ is constant across all charts.
