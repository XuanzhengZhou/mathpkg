Without loss of generality we may assume that $A = E$, i.e., that we are considering the Euclidean structure given by the kinetic energy $T = \frac{1}{2}(\dot{q}, \dot{q})$. In this Euclidean structure, the characteristic frequencies are the reciprocals of the semi-axes of the ellipsoid $U = \frac{1}{2}(Bq, q) = 1$. Under an increase of rigidity, $U$ increases, so the ellipsoid $U' = 1$ lies inside $U = 1$. By Theorem 2 (the geometric theorem on semi-axes of contained ellipsoids), the semi-axes of the inner ellipsoid are no larger than those of the outer one, hence their reciprocals—the characteristic frequencies—satisfy the required inequalities. $\square$
