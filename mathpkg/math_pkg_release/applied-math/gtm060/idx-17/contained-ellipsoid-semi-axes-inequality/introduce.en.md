This purely geometric theorem states that if one ellipsoid contains another and they share a common center, then the semi-axes of the inner ellipsoid are componentwise bounded above by those of the outer one. It is the geometric counterpart of the Rayleigh-Courant-Fisher theorem on the behavior of eigenvalues under perturbation. The proof uses the extremal characterization of eigenvalues (Theorem 5) applied to $k$-dimensional cross-sections.
