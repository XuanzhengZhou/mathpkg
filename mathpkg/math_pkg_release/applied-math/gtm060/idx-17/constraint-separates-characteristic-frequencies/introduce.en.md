When a linear constraint is imposed on an $n$-degree-of-freedom oscillatory system, reducing it to $n-1$ degrees of freedom, the new characteristic frequencies interlace with the original ones. This separation theorem shows that each constrained frequency lies between a pair of original frequencies. The result follows from the geometric fact that the semi-axes of a central cross-section of an ellipsoid separate the semi-axes of the original ellipsoid.
