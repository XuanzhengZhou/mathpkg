The phase space of a 2-DOF system is $\mathbb{R}^4$ with coordinates $(x_1, x_2, y_1, y_2) = (\mathbf{x}, \dot{\mathbf{x}})$. This doubling of dimension is a general feature: an $n$-DOF system has a $2n$-dimensional phase space. Phase curves in this 4D space encode the complete state evolution, and their projections onto configuration space give physical orbits.
