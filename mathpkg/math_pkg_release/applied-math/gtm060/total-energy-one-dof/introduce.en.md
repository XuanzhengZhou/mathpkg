The total energy $E = T + U$ is the sum of kinetic and potential energies. For a conservative one-degree-of-freedom system, $E$ is a constant of motion (first integral). This conservation law allows one to reduce the second-order equation of motion to a first-order equation $\frac{1}{2}\dot{x}^2 + U(x) = E$, from which the qualitative behavior and explicit solution by quadrature can be obtained.
