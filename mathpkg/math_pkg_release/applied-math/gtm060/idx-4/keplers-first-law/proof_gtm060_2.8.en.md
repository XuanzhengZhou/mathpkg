If we assume that the planets move in a central field of gravity with potential $U = -k/r$, then from the focal equation of a conic section

$$r = \frac{p}{1 + e \cos \varphi},$$

with $p = M^2/k$ and $e = \sqrt{1 + 2EM^2/k^2}$, we see that for $E < 0$ we have $e < 1$. In this case the curve described by the focal equation is an ellipse, with the origin (the center of the gravitational field) at one focus. Thus, Kepler's First Law is a consequence of Newton's law of gravity. Conversely, if Kepler's First Law holds for all planets, then the central force must be of the form $U = -k/r$, as shown in Section 2D.
