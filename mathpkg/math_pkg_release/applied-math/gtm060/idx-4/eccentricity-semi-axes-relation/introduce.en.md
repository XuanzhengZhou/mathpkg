For an ellipse with semi-major axis $a$ and semi-minor axis $b$, the eccentricity $e$ is defined geometrically as the ratio of the focal distance $c$ to the semi-major axis: $e = c/a$. Using the Pythagorean relation $a^2 = b^2 + c^2$ for an ellipse, this can be rewritten as $e = \sqrt{a^2 - b^2}/a$. The focal distance is then $c = ae$, which represents the distance from the center of the ellipse to either focus.
