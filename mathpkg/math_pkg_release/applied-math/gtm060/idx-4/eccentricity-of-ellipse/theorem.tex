The eccentricity $e$ of a conic section (ellipse) in central field motion is defined as

$$e = \sqrt{1 + \frac{2EM^2}{k^2}},$$

where $E$ is the total energy, $M$ is the angular momentum, and $k$ is the constant from the gravitational potential $U = -k/r$. For $E < 0$, we have $e < 1$ and the orbit is an ellipse. For $E = 0$, $e = 1$ (parabola). For $E > 0$, $e > 1$ (hyperbola).
