The major axis of the ellipse is the sum of the distances at pericenter and apocenter. From the focal equation $r = p/(1 + e \cos \varphi)$, the pericenter distance (at $\varphi = 0$) is $r_{\min} = p/(1 + e)$, and the apocenter distance (at $\varphi = \pi$) is $r_{\max} = p/(1 - e)$. Since the major axis $2a$ is the sum of these:

$$2a = \frac{p}{1 - e} + \frac{p}{1 + e} = \frac{p(1+e) + p(1-e)}{(1-e)(1+e)} = \frac{2p}{1 - e^2}.$$

Dividing by 2 yields

$$a = \frac{p}{1 - e^2}.$$
