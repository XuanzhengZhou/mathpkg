\textbf{Theorem.} The increase in the kinetic energy of a system is equal to the sum of the work of all forces acting on the points of the system:
$$T(t_1) - T(t_0) = \int_{r(t_0)}^{r(t_1)} (F, dr) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} (\dot{r}, F) dt.$$

In other words: the increase in kinetic energy is equal to the work of the force $F$ on the path $r(t)$ in configuration space.
