A cyclic (or ignorable) coordinate is one that does not appear explicitly in the Hamiltonian (or Lagrangian). The conjugate momentum is then conserved, reducing the effective dimensionality of the problem by two (one coordinate and one momentum). This is the Hamiltonian version of Noether's theorem: symmetry (independence from a coordinate) implies conservation (of the conjugate momentum). Almost all exactly solvable problems in mechanics are solved by exploiting cyclic coordinates.
