For a newtonian potential system with kinetic energy \(T = \sum m_i \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i^2/2\) and potential energy \(U(\mathbf{r})\), setting the lagrangian \(L = T - U\) makes Newton's equations of motion \(\frac{d}{dt}(m_i \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i) + \frac{\partial U}{\partial \mathbf{r}_i} = 0\) coincide with the Euler–Lagrange equations. This establishes the link between Newtonian mechanics and variational principles.
