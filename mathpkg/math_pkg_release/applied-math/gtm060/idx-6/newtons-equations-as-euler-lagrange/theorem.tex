Let a mechanical system consist of $n$ mass points with masses $m_i$, positions $\mathbf{r}_i$, kinetic energy $T = \sum m_i \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i^2/2$, and potential energy $U(\mathbf{r})$. Define the lagrangian $L = T - U$. Then Newton's equations of motion

$$\frac{d}{dt} (m_i \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i) + \frac{\partial U}{\partial \mathbf{r}_i} = 0$$

are equivalent to the Euler–Lagrange equations

$$\frac{d}{dt} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\mathbf{r}}_i} - \frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{r}_i} = 0.$$
