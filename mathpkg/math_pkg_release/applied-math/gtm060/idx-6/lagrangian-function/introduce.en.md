The Lagrange function, or lagrangian, \(L(\mathbf{q}, \dot{\mathbf{q}}, t) = T - U\) is defined as the difference between kinetic energy \(T\) and potential energy \(U\). This function is the fundamental object of Lagrangian mechanics; its integral (the action) is extremized by physical trajectories.
