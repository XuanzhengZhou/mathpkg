[Hamilton'\''s Principle of Least Action] The motion $\mathbf{q}(t)$ of a lagrangian mechanical system with lagrangian $L = T - U$ between times $t_0$ and $t_1$ is an extremal of the action functional

$$\Phi(\mathbf{q}) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} L(\mathbf{q}, \dot{\mathbf{q}}, t)\, dt.$$

In many cases, the action is not merely an extremal but a minimum.
