A curve \(\gamma: x = x(t)\) is an extremal of the functional \(\Phi(\gamma) = \int_{t_0}^{t_1} L(x, \dot{x}, t)\, dt\) on the space of curves with fixed endpoints \(x(t_0) = x_0\) and \(x(t_1) = x_1\) if and only if it satisfies the Euler–Lagrange equation \(\frac{d}{dt}(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x}}) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial x} = 0\). This is the fundamental necessary condition in the calculus of variations.
