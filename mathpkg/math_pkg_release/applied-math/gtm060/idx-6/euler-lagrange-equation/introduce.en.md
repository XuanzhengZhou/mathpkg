The Euler–Lagrange equation \(\frac{d}{dt}(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{x}}) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial x} = 0\) is the necessary condition for a curve to be an extremal of the integral functional \(\Phi = \int L(x, \dot{x}, t)\, dt\). It is the central differential equation of the calculus of variations and generalizes to vector-valued $.
