If \((q_1, \ldots, q_{3n})\) are any coordinates on the configuration space of a system of \(n\) mass points, then the evolution of \(\mathbf{q}\) with time satisfies the Euler–Lagrange equations \(\frac{d}{dt}(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\mathbf{q}}}) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{q}} = 0\) with \(L = T - U\). This shows that Lagrange's equations are coordinate-independent.
