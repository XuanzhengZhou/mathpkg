A central field is a vector field that is invariant under all rotations about a fixed center. In such a field, all vectors point radially (toward or away from the center) and their magnitudes depend only on the distance from the center. Central fields arise naturally from any spherically symmetric potential. Examples include the Newtonian gravitational field and the Coulomb electrostatic field.
