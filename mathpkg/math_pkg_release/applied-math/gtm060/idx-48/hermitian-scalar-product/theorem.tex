A hermitian scalar product (or hermitian structure) on a complex vector space is a complex-valued function on pairs of vectors $\langle \xi, \eta \rangle$ which satisfies:

1. (Linearity in the first argument) $\langle \lambda \xi, \eta \rangle = \lambda \langle \xi, \eta \rangle$,
2. (Conjugate symmetry) $\langle \eta, \xi \rangle = \overline{\langle \xi, \eta \rangle}$,
3. (Positive definiteness) $\langle \xi, \xi \rangle > 0$ for $\xi \neq 0$.

The standard example: $\langle \xi, \eta \rangle = \sum \xi_k \bar{\eta}_k$ where $\xi_k$ and $\eta_k$ are the coordinates of $\xi$ and $\eta$ in some basis.
