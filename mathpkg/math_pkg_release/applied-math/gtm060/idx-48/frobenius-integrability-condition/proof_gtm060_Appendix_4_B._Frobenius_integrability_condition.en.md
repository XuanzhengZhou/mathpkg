The value of the $3$-form $\omega \wedge d\omega$ on a triple of vectors (one vertical and two horizontal) is the product of the value of $\omega$ on the vertical vector with the value of $d\omega$ on the pair of horizontal vectors. If the field given by the form is integrable, then $d\omega|_{\omega=0} = 0$, so the $3$-form $\omega \wedge d\omega$ is zero on arbitrary triples of vectors.

Conversely, if $\omega \wedge d\omega = 0$ for any vectors, then in particular it vanishes for any triple of coordinate vectors consisting of one vertical and two horizontal vectors. The value on such a triple equals $\omega(\partial_z) \cdot d\omega(\partial_x, \partial_y)$. Since $\omega(\partial_z) \neq 0$, it follows that $d\omega|_{\omega=0} = 0$, and thus the field is integrable by Frobenius' theorem.
