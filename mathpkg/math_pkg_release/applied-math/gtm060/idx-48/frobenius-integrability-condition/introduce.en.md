The Frobenius integrability theorem for fields of hyperplanes states that a field is integrable (admits integral hypersurfaces through every point) if and only if the $2$-form $d\omega|_{\omega=0}$ vanishes identically on each hyperplane. This gives a computable criterion for integrability of a distribution defined by a single $1$-form.
