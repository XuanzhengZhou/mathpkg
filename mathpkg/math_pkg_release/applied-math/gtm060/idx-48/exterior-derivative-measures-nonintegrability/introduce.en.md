This theorem establishes that the exterior derivative $d\omega$ of a $1$-form $\omega$ defining a field of hyperplanes serves as a measure of nonintegrability. The value of $d\omega$ on a pair of horizontal vectors equals the difference in heights of two integral curves constructed along different paths to the same endpoint, up to third order.
