This follows immediately from the theorem that the exterior derivative $d\alpha$ of the canonical $1$-form $\alpha$ on the symplectified space is a nondegenerate $2$-form. Since $\alpha$ is defined canonically (without any arbitrary choices) in terms of the contact structure alone, the symplectic form $d\alpha$ is uniquely determined by the contact structure of the underlying odd-dimensional manifold.
