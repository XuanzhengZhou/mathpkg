The distance between two points of complex projective space is defined as the distance between the two corresponding circles on the unit sphere in the ambient complex vector space. These circles are parallel in the sense that the distance from any point of one to the other is constant, a consequence of the fact that multiplication by $e^{i\varphi}$ preserves the metric on the sphere.
