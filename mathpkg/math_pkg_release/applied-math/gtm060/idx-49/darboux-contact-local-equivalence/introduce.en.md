Darboux's theorem for contact structures asserts that, just as in the symplectic case, all contact manifolds of a given dimension are locally indistinguishable. There is no local invariant of a contact structure; every contact manifold looks locally like the standard contact structure on 3355360\mathbb{R}^{2n+1}3355360.
