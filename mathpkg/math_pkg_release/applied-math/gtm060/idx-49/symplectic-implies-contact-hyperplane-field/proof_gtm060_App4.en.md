The first term of the formula for $$d\alpha$$ is equal to $$d\lambda(\xi)\omega(\pi_*\eta)$$ (since $$\omega(\pi_*\xi) = 0$$). The second term is equal to $$\lambda d\omega(\pi_*\xi, \pi_*\eta) = \lambda\mu\omega(\pi_*\eta)$$. We choose the vertical component of the vector $$\xi$$ so that $$d\lambda(\xi) = -\lambda\mu$$. Then $$\xi$$ will be skew-orthogonal to all vectors $$\eta$$. Thus the form $$d\alpha$$ restricted to the hyperplane distribution is non-degenerate, establishing the contact property.
