\textbf{Theorem.} The Legendre transformation is involutive, i.e., its square is the identity: if under the Legendre transformation $f$ is taken to $g$, then the Legendre transform of $g$ is again $f$.

\textbf{Corollary.} Consider a family of straight lines $y = px - g(p)$. Its envelope has the equation $y = f(x)$, where $f$ is the Legendre transform of $g$.
