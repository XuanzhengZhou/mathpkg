By the property of central fields, $F(r) = \Phi(r) e_r$ where $r$ is the radius vector, $r = |r|$, and $e_r = r/|r|$. Then
$$\int_{M_1}^{M_2} (F, dS) = \int_{r(M_1)}^{r(M_2)} \Phi(r) dr,$$
and this integral is obviously independent of the path. Hence the field is conservative and $U(r) = -\int_{r_0}^r \Phi(\rho) d\rho$ depends only on $r$.
