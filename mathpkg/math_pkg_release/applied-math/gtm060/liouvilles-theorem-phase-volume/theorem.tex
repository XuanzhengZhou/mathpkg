\textbf{Liouville's Theorem.} The phase flow of Hamilton's equations preserves phase volume: for any region $D$ in phase space,
$$\text{vol}(g^t D) = \text{vol}(D).$$

Equivalently, the divergence of the hamiltonian vector field is zero:
$$\text{div}(\dot{p}, \dot{q}) = \frac{\partial}{\partial p}(-\frac{\partial H}{\partial q}) + \frac{\partial}{\partial q}(\frac{\partial H}{\partial p}) = 0.$$
