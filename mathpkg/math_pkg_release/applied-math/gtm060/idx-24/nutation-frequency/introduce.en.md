The nutational frequency is the angular frequency of small periodic oscillations in the inclination angle of a Lagrange top about its stably stationary configuration. It is derived by treating the one-dimensional effective potential for $\theta$ and applying the general formula $\omega^2 = U''(x_0)/a$ for small oscillations. The resulting expression shows that the nutational frequency is proportional to the ratio $I_3/I_1$ of the axial to transverse moments of inertia, multiplied by the angular velocity $\omega_3$ of rotation about the symmetry axis. This formula agrees with the frequency of precession in Poinsot's geometric description when the nutation amplitude tends to zero.
