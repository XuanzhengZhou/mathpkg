This lemma describes how the location of a minimum shifts when a function is perturbed by a small additive term. If $f$ has a nondegenerate quadratic minimum at the origin (second derivative $A > 0$) and a perturbation $\varepsilon h$ is added, the new minimum moves linearly in $\varepsilon$, with displacement proportional to $-C/A$ where $C$ is the linear coefficient of $h$. The second derivative at the new minimum remains close to $A$. The proof follows from the first-order Taylor expansion of the derivative and an application of the implicit function theorem.
