The action function $S(q, t)$ is the integral

$$S_{q_0, t_0}(q, t) = \int_{\gamma} L \, dt$$

along the extremal $\gamma$ connecting the points $(q_0, t_0)$ and $(q, t)$.

For this definition to be correct, the extremals going from the point $(q_0, t_0)$ must not intersect elsewhere, but instead form a central field of extremals: the mapping $(\dot{q}_0, t) \to (q, t)$ is nondegenerate at the point corresponding to the extremal under consideration. For $|t - t_0|$ small enough the extremal $\gamma$ is contained in a central field, and in a sufficiently small neighborhood of the endpoint every point is connected to $(q_0, t_0)$ by a unique extremal of the central field, depending differentiably on the endpoint $(q, t)$.
