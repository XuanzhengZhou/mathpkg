Among all curves $q = \gamma(\tau)$ connecting the two points $q_0$ and $q_1$ on the plane $q$ and parametrized so that the hamiltonian function has a fixed value $H(\partial L/\partial \dot{q}, q) = h$, the trajectory of the equations of dynamics is an extremal of the integral of reduced action

$$\int_{\gamma} p \, dq = \int_{\gamma} p \dot{q} \, d\tau = \int_{\gamma} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}}(\tau) \dot{q}(\tau) \, d\tau.$$

The interval $a \leq \tau \leq b$ parametrizing the curve $\gamma$ is not fixed and can be different for different curves being compared, but the energy (the hamiltonian function) must be the same. The principle determines the shape of a trajectory but not the time; in order to determine the time one must use the energy constant.
