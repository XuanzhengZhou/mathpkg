Maupertuis' principle of least action, also called the Euler–Lagrange–Jacobi principle, is a variational formulation of classical mechanics that arises as a corollary of Hamilton's principle. It states that among all curves connecting two fixed points and parametrized so that the Hamiltonian remains constant, the physical trajectory extremizes the reduced action integral \(\int p \, dq\). Unlike Hamilton's principle, this formulation determines only the shape (geometry) of the trajectory, not the time parametrization, since the integration interval is allowed to vary between compared curves.
