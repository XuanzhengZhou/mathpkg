This theorem applies the Jacobi metric construction to the planar double pendulum, whose configuration space is the torus \(T^2\). By a theorem from the calculus of variations (Morse theory), among all closed curves on a Riemannian torus with prescribed homology class \((m, n)\) there exists a shortest curve, which is a closed geodesic. Translating back to mechanics via the Jacobi metric, this geodesic corresponds to a periodic motion of the double pendulum where the two segments make exactly \(m\) and \(n\) rotations respectively. The condition on \(h\) ensures the Jacobi metric is everywhere nondegenerate.
