This theorem is a corollary of Maupertuis' principle of least action. When a mechanical system represents pure inertial motion on a smooth Riemannian manifold, the potential energy vanishes and the Jacobi metric \(d\rho = \sqrt{h - U(q)} ds\) reduces to the Riemannian metric \(ds\) up to a constant factor. The extremals of the reduced action integral then coincide with the geodesics of the Riemannian metric, establishing that free particles follow geodesic paths.
