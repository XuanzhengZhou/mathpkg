A phase curve is the trajectory of a phase point in the phase plane, parametrized by time as $(x(t), \dot{x}(t))$. By the energy conservation law, each phase curve lies on a constant-energy level set. The uniqueness theorem for ODEs guarantees exactly one phase curve through each non-equilibrium phase point.
