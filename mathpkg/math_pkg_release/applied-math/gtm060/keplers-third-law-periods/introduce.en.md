Kepler's third law relates the orbital period $T$ to the semi-major axis $a$: $T^2 \propto a^3$. The proof combines the area law ($dS/dt = M/2$) with the geometric area of an ellipse ($S = \pi ab$) and the relation between energy and semi-major axis ($a = k/(2|E|)$). The law holds for all Keplerian orbits and is fundamental to celestial mechanics, allowing masses to be determined from orbital parameters.
