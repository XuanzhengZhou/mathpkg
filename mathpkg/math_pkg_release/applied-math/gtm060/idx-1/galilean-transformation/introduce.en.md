A Galilean transformation is a symmetry of Galilean space-time that preserves the time interval between events and the Euclidean distance between simultaneous events. Every Galilean transformation can be uniquely decomposed into a rotation (3 parameters), a space-time translation (4 parameters), and a uniform motion or Galilean boost (3 parameters), giving the Galilean group a total dimension of 10. These transformations encode the classical principle of relativity: the laws of mechanics must be invariant under the Galilean group.
