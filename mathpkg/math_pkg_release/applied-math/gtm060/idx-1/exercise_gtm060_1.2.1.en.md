Show that every galilean transformation of the space $\mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^3$ can be written in a unique way as the composition of a rotation, a translation, and a uniform motion ($g = g_1 \circ g_2 \circ g_3$). Thus the dimension of the galilean group is equal to $3 + 4 + 3 = 10$.
