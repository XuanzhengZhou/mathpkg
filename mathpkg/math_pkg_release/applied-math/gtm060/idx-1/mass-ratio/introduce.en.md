The mass ratio is experimentally defined through the inverse proportionality of accelerations of two bodies subjected to the same force (e.g., the same spring extension). This operational definition captures the concept of inertial mass: a body with larger mass experiences proportionally smaller acceleration under the same force. The constancy of the mass ratio for a given pair of bodies, independent of the specific force applied, is an empirical fact that underpins the universality of Newton's second law.
