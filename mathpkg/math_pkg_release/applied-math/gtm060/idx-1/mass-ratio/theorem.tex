It is experimentally established that for any two bodies the ratio of the accelerations $\ddot{x}_1/\ddot{x}_2$ under the same extension of a spring is fixed (does not depend on the extent of extension of the spring or on its characteristics, but only on the bodies themselves). The value inverse to this ratio is by definition the ratio of masses:

$$\frac{\ddot{x}_1}{\ddot{x}_2} = \frac{m_2}{m_1}.$$

For a unit of mass we take the mass of some fixed body, e.g., one liter of water. We know by experience that the masses of all bodies are positive.
