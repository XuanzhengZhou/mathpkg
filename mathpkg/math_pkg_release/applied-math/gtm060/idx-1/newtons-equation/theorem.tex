According to Newton's principle of determinacy, all motions of a system are uniquely determined by their initial positions $(x(t_0) \in \mathbb{R}^N)$ and initial velocities $(\dot{x}(t_0) \in \mathbb{R}^N)$. In particular, the initial positions and velocities determine the acceleration. In other words, there is a function $F: \mathbb{R}^N \times \mathbb{R}^N \times \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^N$ such that

$$\ddot{x} = F(x, \dot{x}, t).$$

This is called \text{Newton's equation}. By the theorem of existence and uniqueness of solutions to ordinary differential equations, the function $F$ and the initial conditions $x(t_0)$ and $\dot{x}(t_0)$ uniquely determine a motion.
