A conservative system is a mechanical system in which all forces derive from a scalar potential energy function $U$ via the negative gradient: $F_i = -\partial U/\partial x_i$. The equations of motion take the form $m_i \ddot{x}_i = -\partial U/\partial x_i$. This is the most important class of mechanical systems, encompassing gravitational systems, spring-mass systems, and the three-body problem of celestial mechanics. Conservative systems conserve total mechanical energy and form the foundation for the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations of mechanics.
