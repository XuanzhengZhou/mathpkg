The acceleration vector is the second time derivative of a motion, representing the instantaneous rate of change of velocity. It is the central kinematic quantity in Newton's second law of motion, which equates acceleration (multiplied by mass) to the force acting on a particle. In classical mechanics, the equation of motion is a second-order differential equation precisely because it expresses acceleration as a function of position, velocity, and possibly time.
