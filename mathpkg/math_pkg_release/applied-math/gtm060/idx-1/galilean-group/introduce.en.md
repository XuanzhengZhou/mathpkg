The Galilean group is the symmetry group of classical space-time. It consists of all affine transformations of the Galilean space $A^4$ that preserve the Galilean structure: time intervals and the Euclidean distance between simultaneous events. The Galilean group is 10-dimensional, comprising rotations (3 parameters), space-time translations (4 parameters), and uniform motions (3 parameters, also called Galilean boosts). This group encodes Galileo's principle of relativity: the laws of mechanics must be invariant under Galilean transformations.
