The angle $\Phi$ for an orbit close to the circle of radius $r$ is

$$\Phi \approx \Phi_{\text{cir}} = \pi \frac{M}{r^2 \sqrt{V''(r)}} = \pi \sqrt{\frac{U'}{3U' + rU''}}.$$

For potentials of the form $U(r) = ar^\alpha$ ($\alpha \geq -2, \alpha \neq 0$) and $U(r) = b \log r$, the magnitude of $\Phi_{\text{cir}}$ is independent of the radius $r$, and

$$\Phi_{\text{cir}} = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{\alpha + 2}}.$$

The logarithmic case corresponds to $\alpha = 0$.
