For nearly circular orbits, the apsidal angle $\Phi_{\text{cir}} = \pi\sqrt{U'/(3U'+rU'')}$ depends only on the local behavior of $U(r)$. For power-law potentials $U = ar^\alpha$, this simplifies to $\Phi_{\text{cir}} = \pi/\sqrt{\alpha+2}$, giving $\Phi_{\text{cir}} = \pi/2$ for $\alpha=2$ (harmonic oscillator) and $\Phi_{\text{cir}} = \pi$ for $\alpha=-1$ (Kepler), consistent with Bertrand's theorem.
