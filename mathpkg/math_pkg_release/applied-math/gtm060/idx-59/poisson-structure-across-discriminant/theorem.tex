Varchenko and Givental observed that if one constructs, using a generic 1-form and a nondegenerate period mapping, a Poisson structure on the complement of the discriminant locus in the base of a versal deformation of a critical point of a function of two variables, then this structure may be holomorphically extended across the discriminant locus.

**Example.** Consider the three-dimensional space of polynomials $\mathbb{C}^3 = \{x^4 + \lambda_1 x^2 + \lambda_2 x + \lambda_3\}$ with coordinates $\lambda_k$. The polynomials with multiple roots form the discriminant surface (a swallowtail). The fibration is formed by the complex curves $\{(x, y): y^2 = x^4 + \lambda_1 x^2 + \lambda_2 x + \lambda_3\}$, and the period mapping is given by the form $y \, dx$. The resulting Poisson structure, reduced by diffeomorphisms preserving the swallowtail, has symplectic leaves given by the planes $\lambda_2 = \text{const}$, with symplectic structures of the form $d\lambda_1 \wedge d\lambda_3$.
