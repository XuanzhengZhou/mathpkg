This theorem describes a remarkable connection between the topology of fibrations and Poisson geometry. When a fibration has oriented even-dimensional fibres, the intersection pairing on the middle-dimensional homology defines a bilinear form. If this form is skew-symmetric, a nondegenerate period mapping transfers it to a Poisson structure on the base manifold. The constancy of Poisson brackets in the induced coordinates reflects the topological nature of the intersection pairing. This construction, studied by Varchenko and Givental, provides a geometric origin for many interesting Poisson structures arising from singularity theory.
