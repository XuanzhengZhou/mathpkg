Every leaf of a Poisson manifold carries a natural symplectic structure (closed nondegenerate 2-form). Consider the values of two Hamiltonian vector fields at a point of the leaf. The value of the 2-form on this pair of vectors is defined to be the value of the Poisson bracket of the Hamiltonian functions at the given point (this value depends only on the two vectors and not on the choice of Hamiltonian functions). The fact that the form is closed on the leaf follows from the Jacobi identity; nondegeneracy follows from the fact that, if the derivative of every function by a given tangent vector is zero, then the vector itself must be zero. The phase flow of every Hamiltonian vector field preserves the symplectic structures on the leaves. Consequently, the leaves of a Poisson manifold are even-dimensional.
