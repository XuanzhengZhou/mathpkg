Let $f(x, y)$ be a nondegenerate quasi-homogeneous polynomial of weight 1 with argument weights $w_1, w_2$. Then the form

$$\frac{h(x, y) \, dx \wedge dy}{f(x, y)}, \quad h(0, 0) \neq 0,$$

where $h$ is a smooth (or holomorphic) function in a neighborhood of 0, can be reduced by a suitable local diffeomorphism to a normal form

$$\frac{dx \wedge dy}{f(x, y)} \left(1 + \sum_{m,l} \lambda_{m,l} x^m f^l\right),$$

where the monomials $x^m = x_1^{m_1} x_2^{m_2}$ correspond to interior integral points of the Newton diagram of $f$ with $m_i = m_i' + 1$ for monomials $x^{m'}$ in the expansion.
