S. Lie's theorem on the local structure of Poisson manifolds states that near a generic point, a Poisson manifold decomposes as the Cartesian product of a symplectic leaf and a transverse space on which the Poisson structure is trivial. In adapted coordinates $(p_i, q_i, c_k)$, the $p_i, q_i$ satisfy canonical symplectic Poisson bracket relations while the $c_k$ (Casimir functions) Poisson-commute with all functions. This decomposition is fundamental for understanding the local geometry of Poisson manifolds and connects to the physics terminology of Clebsch variables (for the symplectic coordinates) and Casimir functions (for the central elements of the Poisson algebra).
