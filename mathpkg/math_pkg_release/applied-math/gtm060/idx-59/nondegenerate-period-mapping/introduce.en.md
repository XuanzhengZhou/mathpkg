A nondegenerate period mapping provides an identification between the base of a fibration and the cohomology of its fibres. When the base and the cohomology fibres have the same complex dimension, nondegeneracy means the period mapping is a local diffeomorphism. This induces an isomorphism between the tangent bundle of the base and the cohomology bundle, and dually between the homology bundle and the cotangent bundle. Through this identification, any additional structures on the homology groups (such as intersection forms) are transferred to geometric structures on the base, providing a powerful mechanism for constructing symplectic and Poisson structures.
