A Poisson mapping is a smooth map between Poisson manifolds that respects their Poisson bracket structures. The defining condition is that pulling back functions and then taking their Poisson bracket on the source manifold yields the same result as taking the Poisson bracket on the target manifold and then pulling back. An important example is the embedding of each symplectic leaf into the ambient Poisson manifold. The Cartesian product of two Poisson manifolds carries a natural Poisson structure for which both projections are Poisson mappings.
