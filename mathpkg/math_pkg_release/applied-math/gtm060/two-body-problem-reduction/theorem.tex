\textbf{Theorem.} The time variation of $r = r_1 - r_2$ in the two-body problem with interaction potential $U(|r_1 - r_2|)$ is the same as that for the motion of a point of reduced mass
$$m = \frac{m_1 m_2}{m_1 + m_2}$$
in a field with the same potential $U(|r|)$. The center of mass $r_0 = (m_1 r_1 + m_2 r_2)/(m_1 + m_2)$ moves uniformly and linearly.

In particular, for Newtonian attraction, the points describe conic sections with foci at their common center of mass.
