In a generic three-parameter family, the codimension-2 submanifold of ellipsoids of revolution is intersected along curves (since a 3-dimensional space and a codimension-2 submanifold generically intersect along 1-dimensional loci). At isolated points where three axes coincide, the singularity has the topology of two copies of $\mathbb{R}P^2$ meeting at a cone point in the nearby 4-sphere. This rich singularity structure explains the complexity of eigenvalue behavior in multiparameter systems.
