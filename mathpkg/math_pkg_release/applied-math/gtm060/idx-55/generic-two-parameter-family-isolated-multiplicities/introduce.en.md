Since ellipsoids of revolution form a subset of codimension 2, a generic two-parameter surface intersects this subset in isolated points (transversal intersection of a 2-dimensional surface with a codimension-2 submanifold). As an application, on a convex surface in $\mathbb{R}^3$, the second fundamental form defines a two-parameter family of ellipses; thus the principal curvatures are distinct at all but isolated umbilical points.
