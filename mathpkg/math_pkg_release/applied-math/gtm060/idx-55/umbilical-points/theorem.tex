On a surface of general form in $\mathbb{R}^3$, the second fundamental form determines an ellipse in each tangent space. At every point of the surface except at certain isolated points, this ellipse has axes of different lengths. The two orthogonal fields of directions given by the major and minor axes are the directions of principal curvature. The isolated singular points where these directions are not defined (the ellipse becomes a circle, i.e., both principal curvatures are equal) are called umbilical points.
