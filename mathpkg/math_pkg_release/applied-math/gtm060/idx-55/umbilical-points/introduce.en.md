Umbilical points are isolated points on a generic surface where the two principal curvatures coincide. They are the geometric manifestation of the codimension-2 property of ellipsoids of revolution applied to the ellipse of the second fundamental form. A classic example is the surface of a triaxial ellipsoid, which possesses exactly four umbilical points lying on the ellipse containing the major and minor axes.
