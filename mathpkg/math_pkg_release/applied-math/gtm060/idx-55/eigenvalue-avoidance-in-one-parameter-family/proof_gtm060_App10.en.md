By Theorem 1, ellipsoids of revolution have codimension 2. A generic one-parameter curve in parameter space avoids this subset entirely. When a curve passes near a multiplicity point, the eigenvalue graph over a neighborhood of that point is a two-sheeted cone. The curve, being one-dimensional, misses the cone's vertex but lies on its surface, producing a hyperbola-like pair of eigenvalue curves that approach each other to a minimum distance (given by the distance from the curve to the vertex) and then separate. In the completely degenerate case where the curve passes exactly through the singular point, the eigenvalues do cross, but this requires two conditions to be met simultaneously and is non-generic.
