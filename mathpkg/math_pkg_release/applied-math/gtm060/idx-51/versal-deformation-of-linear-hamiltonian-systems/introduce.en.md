This corollary of Galin's theorem characterizes which degenerate Jordan structures can appear in generic $l$-parameter families of linear Hamiltonian systems. A Jordan type with codimension $c > l$ is too special to occur in general position and can be removed by a small perturbation; types with $c \leq l$ are unavoidable and appear robustly. This result is the Hamiltonian analog of the classification of generic singularities in bifurcation theory and underpins the study of stability transitions in parameter-dependent mechanical systems.
