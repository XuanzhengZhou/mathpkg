There is a natural symplectic structure on the reduced phase space $F_p$. Consider any two vectors $\xi$ and $\eta$ tangent to $F_p$ at a point $f \in F_p$. The point $f$ is an orbit of $G_p$ on $M_p$. Let $x \in M_p$ be a point in this orbit. The vectors $\xi$ and $\eta$ are obtained from some vectors $\xi'$ and $\eta'$ tangent to $M_p$ at $x$, which project to $\xi$ and $\eta$ under the quotient map.

The symplectic form $\omega_p$ on $F_p$ is defined by:

$$[\xi, \eta]_p = [\xi', \eta'].$$

This skew-scalar product is independent of the choices of the point $x$ and representatives $\xi'$ and $\eta'$, and gives a symplectic structure on $F_p$.
