The theorem asserts that the hamiltonian function $H_a$ of the one-parameter group $h^t$ generated by $a \in \mathfrak{g}$ is carried over by the diffeomorphism $g \in G$ to the hamiltonian function $H_{\operatorname{Ad}_g a}$ of the one-parameter group $g h^t g^{-1}$ generated by $\operatorname{Ad}_g a$.

Let $g^s$ be a one-parameter group with hamiltonian function $H_b$, where $b \in \mathfrak{g}$. It is sufficient to show that the derivatives with respect to $s$ (for $s = 0$) of the functions $H_a(g^s x)$ and $H_{\operatorname{Ad}_{g^s} a}(x)$ are the same.

The first of these derivatives is the value at $x$ of the Poisson bracket $\{H_a, H_b\}$. The second is $H_{[a, b]}(x)$, since $\frac{d}{ds}\big|_{s=0} \operatorname{Ad}_{g^s} a = [a, b]$.

Since the action is Poisson, we have $\{H_a, H_b\} = H_{[a, b]}$, and the theorem is proved.
