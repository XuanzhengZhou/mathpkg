Since the reduced phase space $F_p$ inherits a symplectic structure from the original symplectic manifold $M$, it is automatically even-dimensional. This follows from the basic fact that every symplectic manifold has even dimension, as a nondegenerate skew-symmetric bilinear form can only exist on an even-dimensional vector space.
