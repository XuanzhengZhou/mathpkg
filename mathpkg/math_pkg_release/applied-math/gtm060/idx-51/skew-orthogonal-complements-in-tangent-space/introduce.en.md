This lemma is the technical heart of symplectic reduction. It states that the tangent space to a momentum level set $M_p$ and the tangent space to the group orbit through a point are skew-orthogonal complements in the full tangent space. This fact guarantees that the reduced symplectic form is well-defined: adding a vector tangent to the $G_p$-orbit does not change the symplectic pairing with vectors tangent to $M_p$. The proof connects the symplectic form with derivatives of hamiltonian functions, showing that lying in the skew-orthogonal complement is equivalent to having zero momentum derivative.
