A vector $\zeta$ lies in the skew-orthogonal complement to the tangent plane of an orbit of the group $G$ if and only if the skew-scalar product of the vector $\zeta$ with velocity vectors of the hamiltonian flow of the group $G$ is equal to zero (by definition of the skew-orthogonal complement).

These skew-scalar products are equal to the derivatives of the corresponding hamiltonian functions in the direction $\zeta$. Specifically, for any $a \in \mathfrak{g}$, the velocity vector of the one-parameter group generated by $a$ is the hamiltonian vector field $X_{H_a}$, and the skew-scalar product $\omega(\zeta, X_{H_a})$ equals the derivative $dH_a(\zeta)$.

Therefore, the vector $\zeta$ lies in the skew-orthogonal complement to the orbit of $G$ if and only if $dH_a(\zeta) = 0$ for all $a \in \mathfrak{g}$, which is equivalent to the derivative of the momentum $P$ in the direction $\zeta$ being zero. But this exactly means that $\zeta$ lies in the tangent space to the level set $M_p$, i.e., $\zeta \in T(M_p)$.

Thus $T(M_p) = T(Gx)^\perp$, and by taking skew-orthogonal complements, also $T(Gx) = T(M_p)^\perp$.
