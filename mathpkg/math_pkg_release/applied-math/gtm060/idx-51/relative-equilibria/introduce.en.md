A relative equilibrium is a motion of a symmetric Hamiltonian system that becomes an equilibrium point after reduction to the quotient phase space. Physically, this corresponds to motions where the system rotates rigidly without changing shape — for example, stationary rotations of a rigid body. Relative equilibria are the critical points of the reduced Hamiltonian and play a central role in understanding bifurcations and stability in mechanical systems with symmetry.
