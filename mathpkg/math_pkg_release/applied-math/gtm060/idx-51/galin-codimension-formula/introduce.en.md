Galin's theorem computes the codimension, in the space of all quadratic Hamiltonians, of the set of Hamiltonians having a specified Jordan block structure. This codimension determines how "degenerate" a given normal form is: higher codimension means the Jordan structure is more special and less likely to occur in generic families. The formula accounts separately for nonzero eigenvalues, even-dimensional zero blocks, odd-dimensional zero blocks, and interactions between them. This classification is essential for understanding which degeneracies can appear in $l$-parameter families of linear Hamiltonian systems.
