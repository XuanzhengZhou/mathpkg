When the original symplectic manifold is a cotangent bundle $M = T^*V$ and the group action comes from an action on the configuration space $V$, the reduced phase space $F_p$ is itself a cotangent bundle — namely $T^*(V/G)$, the cotangent bundle of the quotient configuration space. This result shows that symplectic reduction of cotangent bundles by lifted group actions corresponds to forming the quotient of the configuration manifold. The classical example is the reduction of a rigid body: factoring out rotations yields the reduced phase space $T^*S^2$.
