The momentum mapping is the central object that encodes all conserved quantities associated with a continuous symmetry of a Hamiltonian system. Given a Poisson action of a Lie group $G$ on a symplectic manifold $M$, the momentum mapping $P: M \to \mathfrak{g}^*$ collects the hamiltonian functions of all one-parameter subgroups into a single map. Linear momentum and angular momentum emerge as special cases when the group is translations or rotations. This construction is the geometric foundation of Noether's theorem.
