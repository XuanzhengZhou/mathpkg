Hamilton's principle of least action (more precisely, stationary action) states that physical trajectories extremize the action integral $S = \int L dt$. This variational formulation unifies all of mechanics: the Euler-Lagrange equations derived from this principle are the equations of motion. The principle generalizes to field theory, general relativity, and quantum mechanics. It also provides the foundation for Noether's theorem, connecting continuous symmetries of the Lagrangian to conserved quantities.
