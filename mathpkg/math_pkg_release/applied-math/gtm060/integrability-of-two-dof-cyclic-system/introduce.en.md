A closed two-degree-of-freedom system with one cyclic coordinate is always integrable by quadratures. The conservation of the cyclic momentum reduces the problem to a one-dimensional system in the remaining variables, which is then integrable using the energy integral. This is the simplest manifestation of Liouville integrability, which generalizes to $n$ degrees of freedom with $n$ commuting first integrals.
