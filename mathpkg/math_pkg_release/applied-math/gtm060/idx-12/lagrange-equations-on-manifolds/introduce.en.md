This theorem extends the Lagrange equations from Euclidean configuration spaces to arbitrary smooth manifolds. Given a Lagrangian function $L$ defined on the tangent bundle $TM$, the equations of motion in local coordinates take the same Euler-Lagrange form as in flat space. The result establishes that Lagrangian mechanics is intrinsically geometric, depending only on the smooth structure of the configuration manifold and not on any embedding in Euclidean space.
