The inequality $V(r) \leq E$ gives one or several annular regions in the plane:
$$0 \leq r_{\min} \leq r \leq r_{\max} \leq \infty.$$

If $0 \leq r_{\min} < r_{\max} < \infty$, then the motion is bounded and takes place inside the ring between the circles of radius $r_{\min}$ and $r_{\max}$.

The angle $\varphi$ varies monotonically while $r$ oscillates periodically between $r_{\min}$ and $r_{\max}$. The points where $r = r_{\min}$ are called pericentral, and where $r = r_{\max}$, apocentral.

The orbit is closed if and only if the angle between successive pericenters
$$\Phi = \int_{r_{\min}}^{r_{\max}} \frac{M/r^2 \, dr}{\sqrt{2(E - V(r))}}$$
is commensurable with $2\pi$, i.e., $\Phi = 2\pi(m/n)$ for integers $m, n$.
