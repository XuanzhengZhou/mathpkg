For a given energy $E$ and angular momentum $M$, the radial motion is confined to the region where the effective potential does not exceed $E$. This defines an annular region $r_{\min} \leq r \leq r_{\max}$ in which the orbit lies. The orbit is generally not closed: each radial oscillation advances the angular coordinate by $\Phi$, and the orbit closes only if $\Phi$ is a rational multiple of $2\pi$. Otherwise the orbit is everywhere dense in the annulus.
