Let $U(q_0) = h$. For sufficiently small $\varepsilon > 0$, the connected component of the set $\{q : U(q) \leq h + \varepsilon\}$ containing $q_0$ is an arbitrarily small neighborhood of $q_0$. Furthermore, the connected component of the corresponding region in phase space $\{p, q : E(p, q) \leq h + \varepsilon\}$ (where $p = \partial T / \partial \dot{q}$ is the momentum and $E = T + U$ is the total energy) is an arbitrarily small neighborhood of the point $(p = 0, q = q_0)$. But the region $\{p, q : E \leq h + \varepsilon\}$ is invariant with respect to the phase flow by the law of conservation of energy. Therefore, for initial conditions $p(0), q(0)$ close enough to $(0, q_0)$, the entire trajectory remains in an arbitrarily small neighborhood of the equilibrium point, which is precisely Liapunov stability. $\square$
