Every small oscillation of a Lagrangian system with quadratic kinetic and potential energies is a sum of characteristic oscillations. In the case where all eigenvalues $\lambda_k = \omega_k^2$ are nonzero, the general solution has the form

$$q(t) = \operatorname{Re} \sum_{k=1}^{n} C_k e^{i\omega_k t} \xi_k,$$

where $\xi_k$ are the pairwise orthogonal eigenvectors satisfying $B \xi_k = \lambda_k A \xi_k$. This result holds even when some $\lambda_k$ are multiple eigenvalues; resonance terms of the form $t \sin \omega t$ do not arise in Lagrangian systems.
