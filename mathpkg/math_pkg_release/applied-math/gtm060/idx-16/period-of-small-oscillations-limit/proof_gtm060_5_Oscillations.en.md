For the linearized system, set $q_0 = 0$ for convenience. Then $T_2 = \frac{1}{2}a\dot{q}^2$ and $U_2 = \frac{1}{2}bq^2$. Lagrange's equation gives $\ddot{q} = -\omega_0^2 q$ with $\omega_0^2 = b/a$. The general solution is

$$q = c_1 \cos \omega_0 t + c_2 \sin \omega_0 t$$

for any initial amplitude. This solution has period $\tau_0 = 2\pi/\omega_0$ independently of the amplitude. By the previous two theorems (stability and linearization), as the amplitude of the nonlinear oscillations tends to zero, the motion approaches the linearized motion, and therefore the period approaches $\tau_0$. $\square$
