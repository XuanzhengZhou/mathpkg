An equilibrium position in a Lagrangian mechanical system is a configuration where the system remains at rest indefinitely. This theorem gives a simple criterion: the generalized velocities must vanish, and the potential energy must have a critical point. The proof follows directly from Lagrange's equations, since when velocities are zero, the kinetic energy terms vanish from the equations of motion.
