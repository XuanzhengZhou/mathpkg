Small oscillations are the motions described by a Lagrangian system linearized about an equilibrium position. The kinetic and potential energies are replaced by constant-coefficient quadratic forms, yielding a system of linear differential equations that can be solved completely. In the one-dimensional case, the motion reduces to a simple harmonic oscillator with a well-defined period and frequency determined by the second derivatives of the potential energy.
