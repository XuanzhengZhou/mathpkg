The coordinate system $Q = Cq$ is orthogonal with respect to the scalar product $(Aq, q)$ because in these coordinates the kinetic energy takes the form $T = \frac{1}{2} \sum \dot{Q}_i^2$, i.e., $A$ becomes the identity matrix. The characteristic directions $\xi_i$ are precisely the columns of $C^{-1}$ (or equivalently, the inverse images of the standard basis vectors under $Q \mapsto q$). Since the standard basis vectors of the $Q$-coordinates are pairwise orthogonal under the identity inner product, and the transformation preserves this orthogonality when measured by $(A \cdot, \cdot)$, the $\xi_i$ are pairwise orthogonal with respect to $(A \cdot, \cdot)$. $\square$
