A positive affine function is a real-valued function defined on a connected open subset of $\mathbb{R}$ that takes the form $f(u) = cu + d$ with $c > 0$. The term "positive" refers to the positivity of the slope coefficient $c$, ensuring the function is strictly increasing. This terminology is introduced in the preliminaries of Sachs and Wu as part of the notation for working with real intervals and coordinate transformations in general relativity.
