Given any tangent vector $W$ at a point $\gamma(e)$ on an integral curve of $X$, there exists exactly one Lie parallel vector field $W$ over $\gamma$ that takes the value $W$ at parameter $e$. This result follows from Bishop--Goldberg 5.8.1, using the flow of $X$ to transport the initial vector along the curve. It provides a well-defined notion of Lie-transporting vectors along integral curves.
