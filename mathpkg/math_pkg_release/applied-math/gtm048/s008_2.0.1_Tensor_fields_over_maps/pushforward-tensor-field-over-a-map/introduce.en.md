Given a contravariant tensor field $B$ on the domain $N$, pushing forward each value $B y$ by the derivative $\phi_*$ produces a well-defined tensor field over the map $\phi$. This is the natural contravariant construction. A key application: for any vector field $X$ on $N$, $\phi_* X$ is always a well-defined vector field over $\phi$, even when no ordinary vector field on $M$ is $\phi$-related to $X$.
