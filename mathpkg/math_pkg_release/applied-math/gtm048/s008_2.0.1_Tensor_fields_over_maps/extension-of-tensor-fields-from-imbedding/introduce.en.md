When $\phi$ is an imbedding with closed image, the paracompactness of $M$ guarantees that any tensor field defined only along the image of $\phi$ can be extended to a tensor field on the entire ambient manifold $M$. The proof relies on a partition of unity argument, as detailed in Kobayashi--Nomizu. This result ensures that the concept of tensor fields over a map is not more general than ordinary tensor fields in the imbedded case.
