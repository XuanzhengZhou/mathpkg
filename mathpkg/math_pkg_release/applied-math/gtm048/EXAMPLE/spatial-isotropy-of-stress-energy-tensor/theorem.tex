Let $F$ be a photon gas on a spacetime $(M, g)$ and let $\hat{T}$ be its stress-energy tensor (as in Proposition 5.7.2). If $F$ is spatially isotropic for an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$, then $\hat{T}$ is also spatially isotropic for $(z, Z)$. That is, for all $\sigma \in \mathcal{O}^3$ (the orthogonal group of $Z^\perp$),
$$\sigma_0^2 \hat{T}_z = \hat{T}_z,$$
where $\sigma_s^r: T_s^r(M_z) \to T_s^r(M_z)$ is the extension of $\sigma$ to $(r,s)$-tensors.
