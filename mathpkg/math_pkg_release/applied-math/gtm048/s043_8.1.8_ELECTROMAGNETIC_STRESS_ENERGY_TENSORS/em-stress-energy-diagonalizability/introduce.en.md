The electromagnetic stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ is diagonalizable precisely when it satisfies the dominant energy condition, which is equivalent to the electromagnetic field being non-null in the sense that either the electric and magnetic fields are not orthogonal, or their squared norms differ. This connects algebraic properties of the stress-energy tensor to physical energy conditions in general relativity.
