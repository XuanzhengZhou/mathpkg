The proof is left as Exercise 8.1.8(a) to the reader. One proceeds by expressing $F$ in components relative to the instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$, relating $E$ and $B$ to the components of $F$, and computing both $g(E, B)$ and $F \wedge F$ in terms of these components. The equality $g(E, B) = 0$ is equivalent to the vanishing of the Pfaffian of $F$, which is equivalent to $F \wedge F = 0$.
