In the rest space $R$ of an instantaneous observer, the squared norms $\sigma^{ij}\sigma_{ij}$ and $\omega^{ij}\omega_{ij}$ are positive definite quadratic forms. Thus, if either squared norm vanishes, the corresponding tensor must be identically zero. This simple but important fact is used in the analysis of the Raychaudhuri equation and in characterizing rigid motion of a congruence of timelike curves.
