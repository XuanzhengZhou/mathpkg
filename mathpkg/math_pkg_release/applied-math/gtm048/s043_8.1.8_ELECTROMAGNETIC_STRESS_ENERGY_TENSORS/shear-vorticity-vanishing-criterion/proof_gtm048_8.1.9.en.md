The proof is left as Exercise 8.1.9(i) to the reader. Since the metric $g|_R = h|_R$ on the rest space $R$ is positive definite (Riemannian), the quadratic forms $\sigma^{ij}\sigma_{ij} = \sum_{i,j} g_{ik} g_{jl} \sigma^{ij} \sigma^{kl}$ and $\omega^{ij}\omega_{ij}$ are sums of squares in any orthonormal basis for $R$. A sum of squares of real numbers vanishes if and only if each term vanishes, hence each component $\sigma_{ij}$ (respectively, $\omega_{ij}$) is zero.
