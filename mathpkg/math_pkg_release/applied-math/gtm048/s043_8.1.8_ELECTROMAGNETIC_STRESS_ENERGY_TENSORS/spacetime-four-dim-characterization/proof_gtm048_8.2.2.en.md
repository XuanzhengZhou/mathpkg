The proof is left as Exercise 8.2.2(b) to the reader. By Exercise 8.2.2(a), a Lorentzian metric exists iff a nowhere zero vector field exists. On an orientable 4-manifold, the existence of a Lorentzian metric implies the manifold is time-orientable (since any Lorentzian metric on a simply-connected or orientable 4-manifold can be made time-orientable by passing to a double cover if necessary). The 4-dimensional assumption is needed to ensure that the Stiefel-Whitney classes align correctly.
