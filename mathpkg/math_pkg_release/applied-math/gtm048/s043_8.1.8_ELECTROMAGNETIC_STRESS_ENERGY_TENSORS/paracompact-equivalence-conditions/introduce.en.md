For connected, Hausdorff $C^\infty$ manifolds, several fundamental topological properties are equivalent: second countability, the Lindel\"of property, paracompactness, metrizability, and the existence of a Riemannian metric. This result connects the purely topological notion of paracompactness with the geometric existence of Riemannian structures, and is essential for ensuring that manifolds admit partitions of unity.
