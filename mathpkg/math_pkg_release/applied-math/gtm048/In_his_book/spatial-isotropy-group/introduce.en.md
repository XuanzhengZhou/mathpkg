The spatial isotropy group $\mathcal{O}^3$ formalizes the astronomical observation that the distribution of galaxies appears roughly uniform in all directions. For a given instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ on a spacetime, $\mathcal{O}^3$ consists of all linear automorphisms of the tangent space $M_z$ that preserve the observer's time direction $Z$ and the Lorentz metric $g$. The group is isomorphic to the ordinary rotation group $\mathrm{O}(3)$, reflecting the spatial rotational symmetry perceived by the observer.
