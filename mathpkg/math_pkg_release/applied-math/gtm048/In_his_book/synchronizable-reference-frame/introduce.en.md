A reference frame $Q$ on a spacetime induces a notion of simultaneity via its physically equivalent 1-form $\omega$. The four definitions of synchronizability describe progressively stronger conditions for when the observers in $Q$ can coherently synchronize their clocks. Local synchronizability ($\omega \wedge d\omega = 0$) is the Frobenius integrability condition, ensuring local time hypersurfaces. Proper time synchronizability ($\omega = -dt$) is the strongest condition, meaning all observers in $Q$ share a common proper time parameter. The intermediate notions correspond to the existence of a time function up to a positive scaling factor $h$.
