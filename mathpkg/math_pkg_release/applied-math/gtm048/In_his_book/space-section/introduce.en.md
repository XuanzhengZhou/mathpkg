A space-section $D$ is the oriented "spatial slice" of a causal box, serving as the 3-dimensional domain of integration for spatial flux integrals in general relativity. It consists of the "top" face $B_2$ (with reversed orientation) together with its boundary edge $C_2$. Space-sections provide the geometric setting for integral conservation laws: just as Stokes' theorem relates the integral of $df$ over a curve to boundary values, a 2-form $\psi$ is closed precisely when its integral over every space-section boundary vanishes. This characterization is fundamental for formulating conservation of charge, energy, and momentum in curved spacetime.
