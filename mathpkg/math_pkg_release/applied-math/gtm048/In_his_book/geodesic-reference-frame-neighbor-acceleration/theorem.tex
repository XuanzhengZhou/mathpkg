Let $Q$ be a geodesic reference frame on a spacetime $(M, g, D)$ (so $D_Q Q = 0$), and let $W$ be a neighbor of an observer $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to M$ in $Q$. Define the operator $\psi_Z$ at each $x = \gamma u$ by
$$\psi_Z X = R_{X Z} Z, \quad X \in M_x,$$
where $Z = \gamma_* u$ and $R$ is the curvature tensor. Then $\psi_Z$ restricts to a self-adjoint operator on the local rest space $R_u$ with respect to $g_z|_R$. The 3-acceleration of $W$ relative to $\gamma$ satisfies:
$$F_{\gamma_*}^2 W = \psi W,$$
where $(\psi W)u = \psi_{\gamma_* u}(W u)$ for all $u \in \mathcal{E}$.
