Let $Q$ be a reference frame on a spacetime $(M, g, D)$. Define the linear operator $A_Q$ on the local rest space $R_u$ at each $x = \gamma u$ by
$$g(A_Q X, Y) = g(D_X Q, Y)$$
for all $X, Y \in R_u$ (or equivalently, for $X, Y$ orthogonal to $Q$). Then $Q$ is called:

(a) **irrotational** at $x$ iff $A_Q$ is self-adjoint with respect to $g_x|_{R_u}$, i.e., $g(A_Q X, Y) = g(X, A_Q Y)$ for all $X, Y \in R_u$;

(b) **rigid** at $x$ iff $A_Q$ is skew-adjoint with respect to $g_x|_{R_u}$, i.e., $g(A_Q X, Y) = -g(X, A_Q Y)$ for all $X, Y \in R_u$.

$Q$ is called **irrotational** (resp. **rigid**) if it is irrotational (resp. rigid) at every $x \in M$.
