The spatial deformation tensor $A_Q$ gives a complete infinitesimal description of how neighboring observers in a reference frame $Q$ move relative to one another. The terminology "irrotational" and "rigid" parallels Newtonian hydrodynamics and aerodynamics, where a velocity gradient matrix $\{\partial v^i / \partial x^j\}$ being symmetric or antisymmetric corresponds to irrotational or rigid flow, respectively. In general relativity, $Q$ is irrotational when $A_Q$ is self-adjoint (symmetric part dominates) and rigid when $A_Q$ is skew-adjoint (antisymmetric part dominates). These definitions are independent of any observer choice within $Q$.
