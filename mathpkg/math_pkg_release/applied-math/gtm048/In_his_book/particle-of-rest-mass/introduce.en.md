Definition 3.1.1 formalizes the relativistic notion of a point particle. The rest-mass $m$ is the analogue of Newtonian inertial mass, with the crucial difference that $m = 0$ is allowed relativistically (corresponding to massless particles such as photons). The condition $g(\gamma_*, \gamma_*) = -m^2$ is the relativistic energy-momentum relation: for $m \neq 0$, the tangent vector's squared norm equals $-m^2$, while $m = 0$ describes lightlike particles. A particle is freely falling precisely when its worldline is a geodesic, generalizing Newton's first law to curved spacetime. Importantly, the authors reject the obsolete concept of "velocity-dependent mass," using only the invariant rest-mass $m$.
