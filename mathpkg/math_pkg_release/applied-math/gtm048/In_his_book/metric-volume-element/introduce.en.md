The metric volume element $\Omega$ is the canonical 4-form induced by the Lorentz metric $g$ and the orientation on a spacetime. It serves as the integration measure for all integral conservation laws in general relativity. The formula $\Omega = \sqrt{g}\, \omega^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \omega^4$ expresses $\Omega$ in an arbitrary basis, where $\sqrt{g}$ compensates for the non-orthonormality of the basis. For an orthonormal basis, $\Omega$ simplifies to the wedge product of the basis 1-forms. The interior product $i(X)\Omega$ plays a key role in converting between differential and integral forms of conservation laws.
