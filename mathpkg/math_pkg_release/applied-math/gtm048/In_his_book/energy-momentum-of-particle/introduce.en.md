In special and general relativity, the energy and momentum of a particle are unified into a single geometric object: the tangent vector $\gamma_*$ along the particle's worldline. This energy-momentum 4-vector is a basis-free formulation that supersedes the older, frame-dependent notion of "velocity-dependent mass." For a particle of rest-mass $m$, the invariant $g(\gamma_*, \gamma_*) = -m^2$ is the relativistic energy-momentum relation: in a local orthonormal frame where $\gamma_* = (E, p^1, p^2, p^3)$, this becomes $-E^2 + |\mathbf{p}|^2 = -m^2$, or equivalently $E^2 = |\mathbf{p}|^2 + m^2$.
