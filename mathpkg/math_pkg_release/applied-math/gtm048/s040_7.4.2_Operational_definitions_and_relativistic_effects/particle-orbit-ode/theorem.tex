Let $(\gamma, m)$ be a freely falling particle of rest-mass $m$ with inextendible geodesic $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to M$ in the Schwarzschild exterior spacetime. Let $E \in (0, \infty)$ be the total energy and $J \in [0, \infty)$ the total angular momentum. Let $\kappa: \mathbb{R} \to \mathcal{E}$ be a reparametrization such that $P \circ \gamma \circ \kappa$ is a unit-speed curve in $(S^2, h)$, and define $\tilde{\gamma} = r \circ \gamma \circ \kappa$. Set
$$
f = \Delta \circ \gamma \circ \kappa = \frac{\mu}{\tilde{\gamma}}, \qquad
a = \left( \frac{\mu m}{J} \right)^2 \in (0, \infty), \qquad
b = \left( \frac{\mu E}{J} \right)^2 - a \in (-a, \infty).
$$
Then $f$ obeys the ordinary differential equation
$$
(f')^2 = b + 2a f - f^2 + 2f^3.
$$
