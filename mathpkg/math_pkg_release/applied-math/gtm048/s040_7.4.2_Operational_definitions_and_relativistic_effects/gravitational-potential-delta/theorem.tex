Let $M$ be the Schwarzschild exterior spacetime with mass parameter $\mu > 0$ and area-radius function $r: M \to (a, \infty)$ for some $a > 2\mu$. Define the dimensionless gravitational potential parameter
$$
\Delta: M \to (0, \mu/a), \qquad \Delta = \frac{\mu}{r}.
$$
Thus $\Delta(x) < 10^{-6}$ for all $x \in M$ in the solar system context.
