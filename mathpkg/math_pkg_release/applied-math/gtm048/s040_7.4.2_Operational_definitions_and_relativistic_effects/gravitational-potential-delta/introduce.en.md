The parameter $\Delta = \mu/r$ is a dimensionless measure of the gravitational potential in the Schwarzschild exterior spacetime. In the solar system, $\Delta$ is extremely small (less than $10^{-6}$), which justifies treating Newtonian physics as a first-order approximation. Differences between Newtonian and relativistic predictions typically appear at order $\Delta$, so precise interpretation of $r$ and $t$ becomes essential when relativistic corrections are of interest.
