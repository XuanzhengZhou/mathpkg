The auxiliary function $\delta(x,z)$ measures the spatial distance between two points $x$ and $z$ in Einstein-de Sitter spacetime $M$, using only their first three coordinates $u^1, u^2, u^3$. It is symmetric and nonnegative, and invariant under the isometries of $M$. This function serves as a key tool for characterizing the causal relation $x \leq z$: together with the time coordinate $u^4$, it yields a simple inequality that determines when one event can influence another.
