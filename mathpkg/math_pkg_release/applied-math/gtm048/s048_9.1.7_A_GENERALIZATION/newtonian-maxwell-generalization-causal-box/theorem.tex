Consider a causal box $\phi: \mathcal{N}_0 \to \mathbb{R}^4$ corresponding to part of the history of a solid ball in $\mathbb{R}^3$ which expands at half the speed of light. There exists a Newtonian analogue of Proposition 3.7.5c (the integral form of Maxwell's equations) in this case. For an electromagnetic field $F$ with charge-current density $J$:
$$\int_{\partial(\phi\mathcal{N}_0)} {}^*F = 4\pi \int_{\phi\mathcal{N}_0} {}^*J,$$
where the integration region accounts for the expansion of the spatial domain. The Newtonian limits of the electromagnetic energy density, Poynting vector, and Maxwell stress tensor are recovered from the stress-energy tensor $\hat{E}$ of $F$.
