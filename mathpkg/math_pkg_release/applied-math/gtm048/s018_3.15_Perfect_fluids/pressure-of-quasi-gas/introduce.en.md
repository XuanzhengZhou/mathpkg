The pressure $p$ of a quasi-gas is the scalar field that appears as the coefficient of the spatial projection term $\hat{g} + Z \otimes Z$ in the canonical decomposition of the stress-energy tensor. Physically, $p$ encodes the isotropic stress exerted by the fluid in directions orthogonal to the reference frame $Z$. Exercise 3.15.6 provides a kinetic interpretation relating $p$ to the averaged random velocities of the constituent particle flows, yielding the approximate relation $3p \approx \rho \cdot |\text{average random 3-velocity}|^2$.
