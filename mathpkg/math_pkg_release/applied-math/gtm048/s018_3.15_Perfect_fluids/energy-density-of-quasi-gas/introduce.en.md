The energy density $\rho$ of a quasi-gas is the scalar field obtained by evaluating the physically equivalent $(0,2)$-tensor field $T$ on the distinguished reference frame $Z$. Physically, $\rho(z)$ represents the energy per unit volume measured by the instantaneous observer comoving with the fluid at $z$. This definition extends the discussion following Definition 3.3.4 and provides the first of two fundamental scalar fields (along with pressure $p$) that characterize a perfect fluid.
