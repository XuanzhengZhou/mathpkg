A stress-energy tensor on a spacetime is called spatially isotropic at a point if its physically equivalent $(0,2)$-tensor field appears isotropic to at least one instantaneous observer at that point. This definition, which references the notion of spatial isotropy from Section 2.1.7, serves as the foundational hypothesis for the perfect fluid matter model developed in this section. The existence of such an observer is the key condition that leads to the canonical decomposition of the stress-energy tensor into energy density and pressure components.
