**(a)** Let $(M, \mathbf{M}, z)$ be a simple cosmological model and let $\hat{T}$ be the stress-energy tensor of $\mathbf{M}$. Show that $T(Z, Z) = 3t^{-2}$, where $t = (R/\dot{R})(u^4 z)$ is the predicted Hubble time (Exercise 6.3.17c).

**(b)** In Definition 6.4.4, replace the assumption that $M$ be a simple cosmological spacetime by the assumption that $M$ be a Robertson--Walker spacetime of negative spatial curvature. Show that then $T(Z, Z) = 3a t^{-2}$, where $t = (R/\dot{R})(u^4 z)$ is the predicted Hubble time (Exercise 6.3.17c) and $a$ can be adjusted to have any value in the range $(0, 1)$ even if $\mathbf{M}$ is assumed to be a dust.
