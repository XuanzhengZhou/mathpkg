Lemma 6.4.3 establishes sharp upper and lower bounds for the energy density $\rho$ of a spatially homogeneous perfect fluid in a simple cosmological spacetime with $\dot{R} > 0$. The density is bounded between the third and fourth powers of the scale factor ratio $R(u^4 y) / R \circ u^4$, corresponding to the limiting cases of dust ($p = 0$) and pure radiation ($p = \rho/3$) respectively. These inequalities follow directly from the conservation law $\operatorname{div} \hat{T} = 0$ and provide fundamental constraints on how matter density evolves with cosmic expansion.
