A semi-Riemannian manifold is complete if the exponential map is defined on the entire tangent space at every point. Equivalently, every geodesic can be extended indefinitely to be defined on the whole real line. This theorem establishes the fundamental equivalence between these two natural notions of geodesic completeness. In the Riemannian case, the Hopf-Rinow theorem further equates completeness with metric completeness and the Heine-Borel property, but in the Lorentzian setting used in general relativity, completeness is a more subtle notion.
