A maximal (or inextendible) geodesic is a geodesic curve that cannot be extended to any larger domain. This is a central notion in the study of geodesic completeness: a semi-Riemannian manifold is complete precisely when every maximal geodesic is defined on all of $\mathbb{R}$. Maximal geodesics are particularly important in general relativity, where they correspond to the worldlines of freely falling particles and light rays that persist for as long as geometrically possible.
