The \textbf{exponential map} $\exp_x$ at $x \in M$ maps a subset $U_x \subset M_x$ into $M$ as follows. The zero vector $0 \in M_x$ is in $U_x$ and $\exp_x 0 = x$. A nonzero vector $X \in M_x$ is in $U_x$ iff there is a geodesic $\gamma: [0, 1] \rightarrow M$ such that $\gamma 0 = x$ and $\gamma_* 0 = X$. For $X \in U_x$, $X \neq 0$, the geodesic $\gamma$ is unique and $\exp_x X = \gamma 1$. The set $U_x$ is open and $\exp_x$ is $C^\infty$. For each $x \in M$, there exists an open neighborhood $V_x \subset U_x$ of $0 \in M_x$ such that $\exp_x|_{V_x}$ is a diffeomorphism onto its image.
