The exponential map $\exp_x$ at a point $x$ of a semi-Riemannian manifold maps tangent vectors to points on the manifold by following geodesics. For a tangent vector $X$, the point $\exp_x X$ is obtained by traveling along the unique geodesic starting at $x$ with initial velocity $X$ for one unit of affine parameter. The exponential map is a local diffeomorphism near the origin of each tangent space, providing a fundamental link between the linear structure of the tangent space and the nonlinear geometry of the manifold.
