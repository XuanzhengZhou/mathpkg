An inextendible curve is a curve that cannot be extended to a larger domain. That is, if any curve $\zeta$ defined on a superset $F \supset E$ coincides with $\gamma$ on $E$, then $\zeta$ must be $\gamma$ itself. Inextendible curves play a central role in the study of geodesic completeness in semi-Riemannian geometry.
