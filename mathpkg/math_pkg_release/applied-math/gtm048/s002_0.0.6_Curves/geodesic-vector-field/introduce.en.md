A geodesic vector field is a nowhere zero vector field $X$ satisfying $D_X X \equiv 0$, which means each integral curve of $X$ is a geodesic. This concept links the global notion of a vector field on a manifold to the local behavior of geodesics. Geodesic vector fields are essential in the study of the exponential map and geodesic sprays, providing a framework for understanding how geodesics foliate the tangent bundle.
