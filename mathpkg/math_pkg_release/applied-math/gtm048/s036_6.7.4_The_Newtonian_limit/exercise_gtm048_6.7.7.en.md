The method used at the end of Section 6.7.1 to identify the absolute luminosity physically depends in a crucial way on the assumption (Section 6.7.1e) that photons are conserved after emission. Give an alternate identification method, which is easier to generalize to the case that photon collisions are allowed. Specifically, show that
$$L(u) = 4\pi \lim \bigl[d^{2} \, T(X, X)\bigr]$$
where:
(a) the limit is taken for any sequence of instantaneous observers $(x, X)$ in $U$, with $x$ approaching the point $y$ on $\gamma$ for which $u^{4} y = u$ and $X$ approaching the tangent to $\gamma$ at $y$;
(b) $d$ is the "spatial distance" from $x$ to $y$---in the limit it will not matter which concept of spatial distance in Section 6.1.6 is used.
