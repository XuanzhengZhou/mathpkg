In the Einstein--de Sitter model, as one approaches the source---that is, for an instantaneous comoving observer very near the source---the apparent luminosity simplifies to the Newtonian inverse-square law form:
$$\ell = \frac{L}{4\pi d^2},$$
where $L$ is the absolute luminosity and $d$ is the spatial distance analyzed in Proposition 6.3.4. The energy density $\ell = T(\partial_4 x, \partial_4 x)$ measured by any comoving instantaneous observer $(x, \partial_4 x)$ is $\ell = (4\pi)^{-1}(L \circ T^3) \times [(u^4)^{-1/3} T]^4 d^{-2}$.
