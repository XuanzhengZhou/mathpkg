# Mass Independence of Gravitational Acceleration

A direct consequence of the equality $\tilde{m} = m$ between passive gravitational mass and inertial mass is that the gravitational acceleration of any particle is given simply by $\ddot{\vec{r}} = -\vec{\nabla}\phi$, independent of the particle's mass. This is the Galilean observation that all bodies fall at the same rate in a gravitational field, regardless of their composition or mass. In general relativity, this universality of free fall is elevated to a fundamental principle: gravity is not a force in the Newtonian sense but a manifestation of spacetime curvature, so the concept of passive gravitational mass $\tilde{m}$ never needs to be introduced at all.
