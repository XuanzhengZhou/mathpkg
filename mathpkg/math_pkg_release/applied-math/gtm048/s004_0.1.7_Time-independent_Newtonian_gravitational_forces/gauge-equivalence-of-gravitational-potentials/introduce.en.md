# Gauge Equivalence of Gravitational Potentials

In Newtonian gravity, only the gradient of the gravitational potential $\phi$ is physically measurable -- it determines the gravitational field $-\vec{\nabla}\phi$. Consequently, two potential functions $\phi$ and $\hat{\phi}$ are physically equivalent precisely when they have the same gradient everywhere. This gauge freedom means the absolute value of the potential is unobservable; only differences in potential (or equivalently, the field $-\vec{\nabla}\phi$) carry physical meaning.
