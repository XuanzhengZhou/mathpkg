# Equivalence of Inertial and Passive Gravitational Mass

High-precision experiments, notably those of Dicke, have established that a particle's passive gravitational mass $\tilde{m}$ (which determines its response to gravity) and its inertial mass $m$ (which determines its resistance to acceleration) are proportional to extraordinary precision: $\tilde{m}/m = 1 \pm 10^{-11}$. This empirical fact -- the weak equivalence principle -- is a cornerstone of gravitational theory. Following standard practice, Sachs and Wu adopt the exact identification $\tilde{m} = m$, from which it follows that all bodies fall with the same acceleration in a given gravitational field.
