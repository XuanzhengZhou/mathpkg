# Newtonian Gravitational Potential

In Newtonian physics, the gravitational influence of a time-independent mass distribution is encoded in a single scalar function $\phi: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}$ called the gravitational potential. The negative gradient of this potential, $-\vec{\nabla}\phi$, gives the gravitational field vector at each point in space. This formulation is the Newtonian precursor to the role played by the Lorentzian metric $g$ in general relativity, where $g$ simultaneously replaces both the Euclidean spatial metric and the Newtonian gravitational potential.
