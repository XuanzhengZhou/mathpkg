This proposition guarantees that for a given observer, every spacetime event sufficiently close to (but not on) the observer's worldline can be connected to the observer by exactly one past-directed and one future-directed light signal. The proof uses a simply convex neighborhood and the causal structure of the restricted spacetime to construct the unique intersection points of null geodesics with the observer's worldline. This result underpins the radar method for measuring distances in general relativity.
