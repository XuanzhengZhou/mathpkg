Proposition 5.4.1 establishes that for a photon described by a rectangle $\sigma$, the frequency ratio $r = f_a / f_b$ between emission and reception is precisely the inverse ratio of the proper time parametrization rates of the observer worldlines at the endpoints. The proof applies Gauss' lemma (Section 5.0.3) to show that the numerator and denominator of the frequency ratio expression cancel against the proper time factors, yielding $r = |\dot{\gamma}_b 0| / |\dot{\gamma}_a 0|$. This gives a geometric interpretation of the measured frequency ratio in terms of the rectangle structure.
