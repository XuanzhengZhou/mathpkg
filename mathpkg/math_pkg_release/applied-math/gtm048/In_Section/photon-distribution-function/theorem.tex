Let $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer on a spacetime $M$ and let $P_z = \mathcal{S}_Z \times (0,\infty)$ be $(z, Z)$'s direction-energy space. A \textbf{photon distribution function} for $(z, Z)$ is a function
$$
F_Z: P_z \to [0, \infty).
$$
Physically, $e^2 F_Z(U, e)$ models the number of photons per unit spatial volume per unit solid angle per unit energy interval at direction $U \in \mathcal{S}_Z$ and energy $e \in (0,\infty)$.
