The photon distribution function $F_Z: P_z \to [0,\infty)$ is the continuum idealization used by an instantaneous observer $(z,Z)$ to describe the aggregate behavior of many photons. It replaces the discrete counting of individual photon worldlines intersecting a solid ball with a smooth density. The quantity $e^3 F_Z(U,e)$ corresponds to the perceived brightness of light arriving from direction $U$ at energy $e$, and integrals of $F_Z$ over $P_z$ yield macroscopic observables such as energy density and flux.
