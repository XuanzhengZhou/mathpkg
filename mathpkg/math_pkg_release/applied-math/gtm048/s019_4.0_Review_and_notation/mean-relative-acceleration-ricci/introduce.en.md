Proposition 4.2.2 establishes a fundamental geometric interpretation of the Ricci curvature tensor: the mean relative-acceleration $\alpha$ of a geodesic reference frame $Q$ equals $-\frac{1}{3}$ times the Ricci curvature evaluated on the instantaneous observer direction $Z = Q(z)$. The mean relative-acceleration averages the 3-accelerations of neighboring observers relative to a central observer, averaged over the unit sphere of the local rest space. In vacuum ($\text{Ric}=0$), this implies zero mean relative-acceleration, meaning tidal forces average to zero — a prediction that has been experimentally verified to high accuracy in the solar gravitational field.
