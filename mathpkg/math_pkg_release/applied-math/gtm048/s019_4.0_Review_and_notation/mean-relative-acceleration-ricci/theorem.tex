Let $Q$ be a geodesic reference frame defined on an open subset $U \subset M$. For $z \in U$, set $Z = Q(z)$ and let $M_z = \mathbb{R} \oplus \mathcal{T}$ be the associated orthogonal decomposition. Let $\psi_z : \mathcal{T} \to \mathcal{T}$ be the self-adjoint linear transformation from Proposition 2.3.3. Define the mean relative-acceleration $\alpha : U \to \mathbb{R}$ by

$$\alpha(z) = \frac{1}{3} \operatorname{trace} \psi_z.$$

Then the mean relative-acceleration of $Q$ satisfies

$$\alpha(z) = -\frac{1}{3} \operatorname{Ric}(Z, Z).$$

Equivalently, using the integral formulation over the unit sphere $S^2$ of $\mathcal{T}$ with standard volume element $\zeta$ and total volume $\sigma = 4\pi$,

$$\alpha(z) = \frac{1}{\sigma} \int_{X \in S^2} g(\psi_z X, X) \, \zeta = -\frac{1}{3} \operatorname{Ric}(Z, Z).$$
