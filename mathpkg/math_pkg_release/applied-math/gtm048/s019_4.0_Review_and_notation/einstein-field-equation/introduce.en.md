The Einstein field equation $G = T + E$ is the fundamental postulate of general relativity, linking the geometry of spacetime (encoded in the Einstein tensor $G$) to the physical content of matter and electromagnetism (encoded in the stress-energy tensors $T$ and $E$). The Einstein tensor $G = \text{Ric} - \frac{1}{2} g S$ is constructed from the Ricci curvature and scalar curvature of the spacetime metric. This single tensorial equation encompasses a system of second-order quasilinear partial differential equations for the components of the metric tensor $g$, and in the vacuum case ($T = 0 = F$) it reduces to the Ricci-flat condition $\text{Ric} = 0$.
