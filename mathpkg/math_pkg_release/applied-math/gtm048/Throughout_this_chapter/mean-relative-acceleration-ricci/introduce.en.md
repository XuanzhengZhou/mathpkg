This proposition provides a geometric interpretation of the Ricci curvature tensor in terms of physically measurable quantities. For a geodesic reference frame $Q$, the mean relative-acceleration of neighboring observers equals $-\frac{1}{3}$ times the Ricci curvature evaluated on the frame vector. This connects the abstract curvature of spacetime to the observable phenomenon of tidal forces: the tendency of freely falling test particles to accelerate toward or away from one another. In a Ricci flat spacetime, the mean relative-acceleration vanishes, indicating zero net tidal effect.
