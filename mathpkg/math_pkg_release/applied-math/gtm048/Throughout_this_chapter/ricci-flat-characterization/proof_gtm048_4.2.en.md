Suppose $T = 0 = F$. Then $E = 0$, so $G = 0$; by Exercise 1.4.7, $\operatorname{Ric} = 0$.

Conversely, suppose $0 = G = T + E$. Suppose $x \in M$, and let $\omega \in M_x^*$ be causal. Then $\hat{T}(\omega, \omega) + \hat{E}(\omega, \omega) = 0$. By Definition 3.3.1, $\hat{T}(\omega, \omega) \geq 0$, and by Proposition 3.7.4b, $\hat{E}(\omega, \omega) \geq 0$. Thus $\hat{T}(\omega, \omega) = 0 = \hat{E}(\omega, \omega)$ for all causal $\omega \in M_x^*$. By Exercise 3.3.4, $T = 0 = E$ at each $x \in M$.
