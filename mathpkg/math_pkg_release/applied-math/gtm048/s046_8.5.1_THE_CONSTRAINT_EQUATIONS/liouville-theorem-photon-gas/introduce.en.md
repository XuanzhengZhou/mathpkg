Liouville's theorem for photon gases states that the natural 7-form $\omega$ on the future light cone is invariant under the flow of the Liouville operator $L$. This is the geometric analogue of the classical Liouville theorem in statistical mechanics, ensuring conservation of phase space volume along the Hamiltonian flow. The result guarantees that photon number densities are consistently defined and that no "6-volume gets lost" under the Liouville flow.
