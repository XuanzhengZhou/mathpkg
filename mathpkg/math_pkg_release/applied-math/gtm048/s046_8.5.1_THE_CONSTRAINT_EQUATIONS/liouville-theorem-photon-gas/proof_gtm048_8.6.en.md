The Liouville operator $L$ on $TM$ is the vector field whose flow is the fiberwise scaling $v \mapsto e^t v$. The 3-form $A$ on each fiber $\mathcal{L}^+_x$ is homogeneous of degree 3 under this scaling, while $\Xi^* \Omega$ is invariant (pulled back from the base). Hence $A$ scales as $e^{3t}$ under the flow of $L$ while $\Xi^* \Omega$ is unchanged. The key geometric computation shows that alongside the scaling of $A$, there is an additional contribution from the horizontal component of $L$ that exactly cancels the scaling effect when combined with the connection structure. The result $\mathcal{L}_L \omega = 0$ then follows. The second assertion $\mathcal{L}_L [i(L) \omega] = 0$ follows from the first and the identity $\mathcal{L}_L i(L) = i(L) \mathcal{L}_L$.
