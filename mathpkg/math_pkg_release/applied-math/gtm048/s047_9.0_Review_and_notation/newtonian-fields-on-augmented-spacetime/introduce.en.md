In Newtonian physics, one deals with time-dependent fields defined on $\mathbb{R}^3 \times T$, where $T = \mathbb{R}$ is the Newtonian time axis. This convention identifies such fields with corresponding fields on the augmented (naive) spacetime $\mathbb{R}^4$, thereby allowing a unified spacetime treatment. A time-dependent scalar field $\phi(\vec{x}, t)$ is regarded as a function on $\mathbb{R}^4$ by ignoring the distinction between spatial coordinates and time as a coordinate function. Similarly, a time-dependent vector field with only spatial components corresponds to a vector field on $\mathbb{R}^4$ whose fourth component vanishes, and conversely a general vector field on $\mathbb{R}^4$ decomposes into a spatial vector field and a scalar time component. This identification extends to tensor fields such as the stress-energy tensor, bridging Newtonian and relativistic formulations.
