Show that Newton's second law $\vec{F} = m\ddot{r}$ (Section 0.1.6) is invariant under the Galilean velocity transformations
$$
\vec{x} \to \vec{x} + \vec{v}t,\quad
\vec{F} \to \vec{F},\quad
t \to t,\quad
\vec{r} \to \vec{r} + \vec{v}t,\quad
m \to m,
$$
where $\vec{v}$ is a constant velocity vector.
