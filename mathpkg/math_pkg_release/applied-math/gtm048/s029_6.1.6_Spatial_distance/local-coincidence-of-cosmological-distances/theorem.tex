There exists a neighborhood $U$ of $z$ so small that, for an observer $(z, Z)$, spacetime curvature within $U$ is negligible compared to empirical inaccuracies. Within $U$, provided all relative speeds relevant to the measurements are negligible compared to $1$, one may identify the luminosity distance $d_L$, the angular diameter distance $d_A$, and the Newtonian distance $d_N$:
$$d_L = d_A = d_N,$$
where $d_N$ is the Euclidean distance within a flat $3$-dimensional submanifold $\mathbb{R}^3 \subset M_2$ parallel to $Z^\perp$. More specifically, if $d_L < 10^8$ \text{ years}, the identification holds to well within the limits of empirical accuracy.
