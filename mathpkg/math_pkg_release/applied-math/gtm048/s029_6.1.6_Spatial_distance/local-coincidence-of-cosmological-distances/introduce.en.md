In observational cosmology, several distinct distance measures are employed -- the luminosity distance $d_L$, the angular diameter distance $d_A$, and the Newtonian distance $d_N$. In general relativistic spacetimes these measures generally differ due to spacetime curvature and relative motion. However, when the observer restricts attention to a sufficiently small neighborhood of the observation event $z$, where spacetime curvature is negligible relative to empirical precision, and all relevant relative speeds are negligible compared to the speed of light, all three distance measures coincide. Concretely, when $d_L < 10^8$ years, the identification $d_L = d_A = d_N$ holds within empirical accuracy.
