Let $(K,g)$ be a Kruskal spacetime and let $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow K$ be a particle or observer. Suppose for some $s_0 \in \mathcal{E}$, $\gamma(s_0) \in M_{II}$, where $M_{II}$ is the black hole region determined by $u > 0$, $v < 0$ in Kruskal null coordinates. Define $\hat{\mathcal{E}} = \{s \in \mathcal{E} \mid s \geq s_0\}$. Then
$$\gamma(\hat{\mathcal{E}}) \subset M_{II}.$$
That is, any particle or observer entering the black hole region can never escape.
