Proposition 7.5.5 formalizes the defining property of a black hole: once a particle or observer enters the black hole region $M_{II}$ (defined by $u>0$, $v<0$ in Kruskal null coordinates), it can never escape back to the exterior region or to infinity. The proof uses the future-pointing lightlike vector field $V$ physically equivalent to $-dv$ to show that the radial coordinate $r$ is strictly decreasing along future-directed causal curves in $M_{II}$, so no causal curve can cross back into region $M_I$.
