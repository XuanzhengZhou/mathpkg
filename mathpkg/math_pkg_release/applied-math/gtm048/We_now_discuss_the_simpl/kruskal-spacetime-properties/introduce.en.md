Corollary 7.5.4 establishes key global properties of the Kruskal spacetime. The curvature invariant $f$ is explicitly computed to be $144\mu^2/r^6$ throughout $K$, matching the Schwarzschild value. The spacetime is shown to be a vacuum solution of the Einstein field equations, confirming it models empty space outside a gravitational source. Moreover, $K$ is shown to be non-flat and non-stationary: while the exterior region $M_I$ admits a timelike Killing vector field, this Killing field becomes spacelike in the black hole region $M_{II}$, precluding the existence of a globally timelike Killing field.
