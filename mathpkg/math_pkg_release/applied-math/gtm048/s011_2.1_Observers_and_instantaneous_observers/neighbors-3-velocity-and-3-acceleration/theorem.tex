Let $F$ be the Fermi-Walker connection over $\gamma$ and let $W$ be a neighbor of $\gamma$ in $Q$. Then:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $F_{\gamma_*}W$ is called the \textbf{neighbor's 3-velocity} relative to $\gamma$,
  \item $F_{\gamma_*}^2 W \equiv F_{\gamma_*} F_{\gamma_*} W$ is called the \textbf{neighbor's 3-acceleration} relative to $\gamma$.
\end{itemize}
Both $(F_{\gamma_*}W)u$ and $(F_{\gamma_*}^2 W)u$ lie in the local rest space of $(\gamma u, \gamma_* u)$ for all $u \in \mathcal{E}$.
