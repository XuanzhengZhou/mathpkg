The spatial isotropy group $\mathcal{O}^3$ is defined for an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ in a Lorentzian manifold. It consists of all linear automorphisms of the observer's tangent space $M_z$ that fix the timelike direction $Z$ and preserve the metric $g$. Using the orthogonal decomposition $M_z = R \oplus T$, the group is isomorphic to the rotation group of $\mathbb{R}^3$, capturing the idea that the universe appears isotropic (uniform in all spatial directions) to that observer.
