Let $(M, g, D)$ be an oriented spacetime. The \textbf{metric volume element} $\Omega$ is the unique 4-form such that for any orthonormal basis $\{X_1, \ldots, X_4\}$ consistent with the orientation,
$$\Omega(X_1, \ldots, X_4) = (4!)^{-1}.$$
For a general basis $\{X_1, \ldots, X_4\}$ with dual basis $\{\omega^1, \ldots, \omega^4\}$ and $g_{ij} = g(X_i, X_j)$, letting $g = |\det\{g_{ij}\}|$,
$$\Omega = \sqrt{g}\,\omega^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \omega^4.$$
In particular, if $\{\omega^1, \ldots, \omega^4\}$ is an orthonormal basis of 1-forms consistent with the orientation, then $\Omega = \omega^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \omega^4$.
