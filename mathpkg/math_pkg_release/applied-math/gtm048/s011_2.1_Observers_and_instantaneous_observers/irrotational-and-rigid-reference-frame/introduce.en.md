A reference frame $Q$ is called irrotational (resp. rigid) if its spatial expansion tensor $A_Q$ is self-adjoint (resp. skew-adjoint) on each local rest space. The terminology parallels Newtonian hydrodynamics, where the velocity gradient matrix $\{\partial v^i/\partial x^j\}$ being symmetric means irrotational flow, and antisymmetric means rigid rotation. $A_Q$ gives a complete infinitesimal description of how neighboring observers move relative to each other.
