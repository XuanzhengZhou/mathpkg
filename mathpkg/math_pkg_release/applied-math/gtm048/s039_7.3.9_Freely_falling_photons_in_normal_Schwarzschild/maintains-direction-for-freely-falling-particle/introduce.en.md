A freely falling particle $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow U$ in Schwarzschild spacetime is said to maintain direction if its total angular momentum vanishes, i.e., $J = 0$. This means the particle moves purely radially (in the $P$-direction), with no rotational component. The condition $J = 0$ is equivalent to $\delta = P \circ \gamma$ being a constant curve in the spatial sphere $\mathcal{S}^2$, so the angular coordinate of the particle does not change along the worldline.
