The total energy $E$ of a freely falling particle (or photon) $\alpha$ in Schwarzschild spacetime is defined as $E = -g(\alpha_*, \partial/\partial t)$, where $g$ is the Lorentzian metric and $\partial/\partial t$ is the timelike Killing vector field. When $\alpha$ is freely falling (i.e., a causal geodesic), $E$ is a constant of the motion by Proposition 7.3.7. The quantity $E$ is strictly positive because $\partial/\partial t$ is future-pointing on the normal Schwarzschild region $N$. This definition provides the relativistic analogue of the Newtonian conserved energy for a particle in a spherically symmetric, time-independent gravitational field.
