Let $\alpha: \hat{\mathcal{E}} \rightarrow N$ be a future-pointing causal curve in normal Schwarzschild spacetime. Define the total energy function $E: \hat{\mathcal{E}} \rightarrow (0, \infty)$ by

$$E(u) = -g(\alpha_*(u), \partial/\partial t|_{\alpha(u)}), \qquad u \in \hat{\mathcal{E}}.$$

The quantity $E(u)$ is called the total energy of $\alpha$ at $u$. Note that $E(u) > 0$ since $\partial/\partial t|_N$ is future pointing. If $\alpha$ is freely falling (a geodesic), then $E$ is constant along $\alpha$ by Proposition 7.3.7.
