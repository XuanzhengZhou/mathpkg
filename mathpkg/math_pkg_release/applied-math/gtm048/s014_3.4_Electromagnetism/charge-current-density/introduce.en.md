A charge-current density $J$ on a spacetime $M$ unifies two pre-relativistic concepts — electric charge per unit volume and electric current density — into a single vector field. Its physical interpretation is operational: any instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ measuring electric charge per unit 3-volume of their rest space $Z^\perp$ obtains the value $-g(Z, J)$. This prescription serves as both an operational definition and a physical law, with a uniqueness result ensuring consistency across observers.
