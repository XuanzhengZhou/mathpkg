The electric vector $E = \tilde{F}Z$ is the electric component of an electromagnetic field $F$ as measured by an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$. It lies in the observer's rest space $Z^\perp$ because $F$ is antisymmetric. Operationally, the observer can measure $E$ by Newtonian methods using a test charge, making this a physically grounded definition that bridges geometric relativity with laboratory measurement.
