Let $F$ be an electromagnetic field on a spacetime $M$, and let $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer. The \textbf{electric vector} that $(z, Z)$ measures for $F$ is
$$E = \tilde{F}Z \in Z^\perp,$$
where $\tilde{F}$ is the $(1,1)$-tensor field obtained from $F$ by raising an index with the metric: $\tilde{F}^i_{\;j} = g^{ik} F_{kj}$. The antisymmetry of $F$ guarantees $\tilde{F}Z \in Z^\perp$.
