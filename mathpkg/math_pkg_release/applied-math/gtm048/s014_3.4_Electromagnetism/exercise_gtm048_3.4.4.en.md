Let $F$ be an electromagnetic field on $M$, and $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer.

(a) Show that the measured electric vector $\tilde{F}Z$ is zero iff $F(Z, X) = 0$ for all $X \in Z^\perp$.

(b) Show that $F_z = 0$ iff $F(Z, X) = 0 = F(X, Y)$ for all $X, Y \in Z^\perp$.

(c) Show that if both the electric vector and the magnetic vector that $(z, Z)$ measures are zero then $F_z = 0$, and vice versa.

(d) Show that one could also regard measuring magnetic vectors as the primary operation—for example, that if every instantaneous observer $(z, X)$ at $z \in M$ measures zero magnetic vector, then $F_z = 0$.
