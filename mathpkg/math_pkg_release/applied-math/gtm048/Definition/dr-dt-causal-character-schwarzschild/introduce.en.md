This exercise explores the causal character of the coordinate differentials $dr$ and $dt$ in the two connected components $N$ (normal region, $r > 2\mu$) and $B$ (black hole region, $r < 2\mu$) of the Schwarzschild spacetime. In the normal region, $dr$ is spacelike (radial direction) and $dt$ is timelike (time direction), as expected. In the black hole region, however, the roles are reversed: $dr$ becomes timelike and $dt$ becomes spacelike, reflecting the fact that inside the Schwarzschild radius, the radial direction toward the singularity is inevitable for all observers.
