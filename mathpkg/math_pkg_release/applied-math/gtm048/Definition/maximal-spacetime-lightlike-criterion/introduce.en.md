This proposition characterizes maximal spacetimes in terms of lightlike geodesics. A spacetime is maximal if and only if one cannot "see into it or out of it" along lightlike geodesics. More precisely, if a larger spacetime contains the given spacetime and every lightlike geodesic that intersects the smaller spacetime lies entirely within it, then the two spacetimes coincide. This highlights the fundamental role of lightlike geodesics in determining the causal boundary structure of spacetimes.
