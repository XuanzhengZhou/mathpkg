The Einstein–de Sitter spacetime is a fundamental cosmological model in general relativity. It is a Robertson–Walker metric with $k = 0$ (spatially flat), describing a homogeneous and isotropic expanding universe. The metric is defined on $\mathbb{R}^3 \times (0, \infty)$ with a time-dependent scale factor $R(u) = u^{2/3}$ (the matter-dominated Einstein–de Sitter solution). This spacetime is not Ricci flat and is geodesically incomplete — geodesics cannot be extended backward beyond $u = 0$, corresponding to the Big Bang singularity. It serves as a running example throughout the book for illustrating concepts in Chapters 2–4.
