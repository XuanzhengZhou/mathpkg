Define $M = \mathbb{R}^3 \times \mathcal{F}$, where $\mathcal{F}$ is an open interval in $\mathbb{R}$. Let $R: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow (0, \infty)$ be a function and define $g$ on $M$ by considering $M$ as a subset of $\mathbb{R}^4$ and letting

$$g = (R \circ u^4)^2 \sum_{\mu=1}^{3} du^\mu \otimes du^\mu - du^4 \otimes du^4.$$

The Einstein–de Sitter spacetime is obtained by taking $\mathcal{F} = (0, \infty)$ and $R(u) = u^{2/3}$.
