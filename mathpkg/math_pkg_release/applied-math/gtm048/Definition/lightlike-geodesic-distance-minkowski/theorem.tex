If $x$ and $\hat{x}$ are points in Minkowski space $(M, g)$, then there is a geodesic $\gamma: [a, b] \rightarrow M$ such that $\gamma a = x$, $\gamma b = \hat{x}$. Define $k(x, \hat{x}) = \int_a^b |\gamma_* s| ds$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] Show that $k(x, \hat{x})$ is the same for all such geodesics $\gamma$.
\item[(b)] Find two geodesics $\psi: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow M$ and $\hat{\psi}: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow M$ such that $\psi \mathbb{R} \cap \hat{\psi} \mathbb{R} = \emptyset$, but $k(x, \hat{x}) = A \in \mathbb{R}$ for all $x \in \psi \mathbb{R}$, for all $\hat{x} \in \hat{\psi} \mathbb{R}$, where $A$ is independent of $x$ and $\hat{x}$.
\end{enumerate}
As a hint, $\psi$ and $\hat{\psi}$ must be lightlike.
