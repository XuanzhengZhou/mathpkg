This exercise establishes two fundamental relations between curvature quantities in general relativity. Part (a) shows that the scalar curvature $S$ equals the negative contraction of the $(1,1)$ tensor physically equivalent to the Einstein tensor $G$. Part (b) establishes the equivalence between Ricci flatness ($\operatorname{Ric} = 0$) and the vanishing of the Einstein tensor ($G = 0$). These relations are direct consequences of the definition $G = \operatorname{Ric} - \frac{1}{2}Sg$ and the algebraic properties of metric contraction.
