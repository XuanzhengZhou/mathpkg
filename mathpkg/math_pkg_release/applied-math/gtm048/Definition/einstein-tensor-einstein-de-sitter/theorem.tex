In the Einstein–de Sitter spacetime, let $\omega^\mu = (R \circ u^4) du^\mu$ for $\mu = 1, 2, 3$ and $\omega^4 = du^4$. Then the curvature forms are $\Omega_4^4 = 0$, $\Omega_\mu^4 = -(4/9)(u^4)^{-2}\omega^4 \wedge \omega^\mu = \Omega_4^\mu$, and $\Omega_\nu^\mu = (8/9)\omega^\mu \wedge \omega^\nu = -\Omega_\mu^\nu$. The curvature tensor is

$$R = \frac{4}{9}(u^4)^{-2} \left\{ \sum_{\rho,\sigma=1}^3 2X_\rho \otimes \omega^\sigma \otimes (\omega^\rho \wedge \omega^\sigma) - \sum_{\rho=1}^3 (X_\rho \otimes \omega^4 + X_4 \otimes \omega^\rho) \otimes (\omega^4 \wedge \omega^\rho) \right\}.$$

The Ricci tensor is $\operatorname{Ric} = \frac{2}{3}(u^4)^{-2} \sum_{k=1}^4 \omega^k \otimes \omega^k$, and the $(1,1)$ tensor field physically equivalent to Ric is $\frac{2}{3}(u^4)^{-2}(-X_4 \otimes \omega^4 + \sum_{\rho=1}^3 X_\rho \otimes \omega^\rho)$.
