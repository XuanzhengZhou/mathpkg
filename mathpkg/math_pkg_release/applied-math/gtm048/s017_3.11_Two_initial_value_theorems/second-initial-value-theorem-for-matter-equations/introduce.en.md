The Second Initial Value Theorem for Matter Equations guarantees that, neglecting the back-reaction of matter on spacetime and electromagnetism, a particle flow can be uniquely reconstructed from its initial data on a spacelike hypersurface. Given a fixed electromagnetic field, an initial future-pointing momentum vector field, and an initial particle density function, there exists a unique extension to a neighborhood of the initial surface that forms a particle flow obeying the simple matter equations. The proof combines earlier results on particle dynamics (Proposition 3.2.3, Theorem 3.8.3) with the first initial value theorem (Theorem 3.11.1) and is left as an exercise.
