The ideas needed for the proof are in Proposition 3.2.3, Theorem 3.8.3, and Theorem 3.11.1; the proof is left as an exercise.

The essential strategy is as follows. By Proposition 3.2.3, the condition that $M = (m, e, P, \eta)$ is a particle flow is equivalent to a system of first-order partial differential equations for $P$ and $\eta$. The initial data $P_0, \eta_0$ are prescribed on the spacelike hypersurface $\phi W$. Theorem 3.8.3 (the initial value theorem for particle dynamics in a fixed electromagnetic field) provides existence and uniqueness of the trajectory for each particle crossing $\phi W$, which together determine the vector field $P$ and density $\eta$ on a tubular neighborhood. Theorem 3.11.1 provides the technical framework for converting the initial value problem into a symmetric hyperbolic system. The matter equations then follow from the construction exactly as in the proof of Theorem 3.11.1, with the electromagnetic field $F$ held fixed and the particle variables $P, \eta$ playing the role of the unknowns $f$.
