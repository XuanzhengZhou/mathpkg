Let $M$ be a simple cosmological spacetime with Einstein tensor $G$. Suppose $G = T$, where $\hat{T}$ is a stress-energy tensor which is normal and obeys the timelike convergence condition. Suppose $\operatorname{div} \partial_4$ is somewhere nonnegative. Show the following generalizations of results in Section 6.0.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\partial_4$ is again canonically determined---for example, as the eigenvector reference frame of $\hat{T}$. The results in Section 6.0.2 on the expansion, synchronizability, and so on, of $\partial_4$ remain valid verbatim. $R$ satisfies $\dot{R} > 0$.
\item[(b)] $F$ again has a finite lower bound, which can be taken as $0$ without loss of generality. Then $u^4 z$ can be chosen as $10^{10}$ years as before.
\end{enumerate}

Now replace assumption (b) of Proposition 6.2.7 by the requirement that $M$ be a Robertson--Walker spacetime of negative spatial curvature. Show from the Einstein field equation that without loss of generality one may take $\mathcal{F} = (0, \infty)$ and take $R$ to be a function defined by:
$$R(u) = A(\cosh t - 1),$$
where $t$ is implicitly defined by $u = A(\sinh t - t)$, and $A \in (0, \infty)$. ($A$ is the extra adjustable parameter mentioned.)

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(c)] Derive the analogous result for the positive curvature case.
\item[(d)] For a Robertson--Walker spacetime with $\dot{R} \neq 0$, define the \textbf{deceleration parameter}
$$q = -\frac{\ddot{R} R}{\dot{R}^2}.$$
Show that in the Einstein--de Sitter spacetime, $q = \frac{1}{2}$ and can be interpreted as a measure of deceleration in the sense that $q = |a| / |v|^2$, where $a$ and $v$ are the relative 3-acceleration and relative 3-velocity, respectively, of Proposition 2.3.7. Show also that in case (b) above $0 < q < \frac{1}{2}$, and in case (c), $\frac{1}{2} < q$.
\end{enumerate}
