Let $(M, g)$ be a simple cosmological spacetime, so that $M = \mathbb{R}^3 \times \mathcal{F}$ and
$$g = R^2(u^4) \sum_{\mu=1}^3 du^\mu \otimes du^\mu - du^4 \otimes du^4,$$
where $R > 0$. Assume the electromagnetic field vanishes: $F = 0$.
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a dust matter model on $M$ with stress-energy tensor $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z$ and comoving reference frame $Z$.
Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $p_0 = 0$, where $p_0$ is the pressure component in the Einstein tensor $G = \rho_0 \, du^4 \otimes du^4 + p_0 (g + du^4 \otimes du^4)$ of the simple cosmological spacetime.
\item[(b)] $Z = \partial_4$; that is, the comoving reference frame coincides with the cosmological time direction.
\item[(c)] $\rho = \rho_0 = \frac{4}{3} (u^4)^{-2}$; the dust energy density is determined entirely by the cosmological time coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
