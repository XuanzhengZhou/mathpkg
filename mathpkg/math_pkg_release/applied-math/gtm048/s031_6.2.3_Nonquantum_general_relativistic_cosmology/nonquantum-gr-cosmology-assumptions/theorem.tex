The basic assumptions of nonquantum general relativistic cosmology are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The universe is described by a relativistic model $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$ which obeys Einstein's and Maxwell's equations.
\item[(b)] The spacetime $M$ is maximal.
\item[(c)] The stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ of the matter model $\mathcal{M}$ is normal and obeys the timelike convergence condition.
\item[(d)] There exists a distinguished instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$, designated the \text{actual observer} with $z$ designated \text{here-now}, who enjoys the properties $Z = Z_z$ and $\operatorname{div} Z(z) > 0$.
\end{enumerate}
