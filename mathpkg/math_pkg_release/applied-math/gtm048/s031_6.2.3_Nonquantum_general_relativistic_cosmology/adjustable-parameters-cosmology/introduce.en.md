Adjustable parameters are the free constants in a cosmological model that are not fixed by the fundamental equations. In the Einstein--de Sitter model, the cosmological time $u^4 z$ at here-now is such a parameter. Models with additional adjustable parameters (e.g., Robertson--Walker spacetimes with nonzero spatial curvature) have more flexibility to fit observational data. The presence or absence of adjustable parameters is a key criterion for comparing and evaluating competing cosmological models.
