A \textbf{cosmological model} is a triple $(M, \mathcal{M}, z)$, where $M$ is a spacetime, $z \in M$, and $\mathcal{M}$ is a matter model on $M$, such that: if the electromagnetic field $F$ is taken to be zero and $z$ is designated here-now, then:
\begin{itemize}
\item The universe is described by a relativistic model $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$ which obeys Einstein's and Maxwell's equations.
\item The spacetime $M$ is maximal.
\item The stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ of the matter model $\mathcal{M}$ is normal and obeys the timelike convergence condition.
\end{itemize}
