Assumptions as in Proposition 6.2.2 (i.e., $(M, \mathcal{M}, F)$ is a finite superposition of relativistic models and each component is either a particle flow, a perfect fluid, or a photon gas, with at least one of the generality conditions holding). Show:

(a) $\hat{T}$ obeys the timelike convergence condition (cf. Section 4.3.7a).

(b) Let $\hat{E}$ be the stress-energy tensor of $F$. Then $\hat{T} + \hat{E}$ is a normal stress-energy tensor which obeys the timelike convergence condition.

(c) If $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z$ for some reference frame $Z$ (i.e., $\hat{T}$ has the form of a dust stress-energy tensor), then: no component of $\mathcal{M}$ is a photon gas unless $F \equiv 0$; if any component is a perfect fluid, it is a dust with reference frame $Z$; and if any component is a particle flow $(P, \eta, m, e)$ with $\eta$ somewhere nonzero, then $m \neq 0$ and $P = mZ$ wherever $\eta \neq 0$.
