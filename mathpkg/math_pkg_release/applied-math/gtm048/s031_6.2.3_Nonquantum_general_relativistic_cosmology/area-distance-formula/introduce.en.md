Proposition 6.3.7 derives the area-distance formula $d_A = d/\tau$ for the Einstein--de Sitter model, connecting the observable solid angle $\Omega$ subtended by a galaxy to its intrinsic area $A$ and cosmological redshift. The relation $\Omega/A = (\tau/d)^2$ shows that the apparent angular size of an object depends on both its distance and redshift. The area-distance $d_A$ generalizes the Euclidean notion of distance through $d_A = \sqrt{A/\Omega}$, and the formula $d_A = d/\tau$ reveals that area-distance is smaller than the geometric distance $d$ by a factor of $1/\tau$. This is a key observable prediction of the model.
