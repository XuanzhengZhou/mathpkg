Proposition 6.3.5a characterizes the set $S$ of galaxies at the same observed cosmological redshift as a given galaxy at $x$. It states that $S$ is precisely the orbit of $x$ under the group of spatial rotations about here-now $z$. This geometrically elegant result follows from the fact that isometries preserve light signals and the cosmological frequency ratio, combined with the transitivity properties of the Einstein--de Sitter spacetime. The proposition bridges the observational notion of equal redshift with the mathematical notion of symmetry under spatial rotations.
