# Physical Equivalence of Tensor Fields

Two tensor fields on an open subset of a semi-Riemannian manifold are physically equivalent if they agree at every point under the metric-induced isomorphism that identifies tensors of equal total order. This concept is foundational for general relativity: all tensor fields in a given equivalence class encode the same physical information. In classical differential geometry, physical equivalence is precisely the operation of raising and/or lowering indices with the metric tensor. The notation $\tilde{A}$ or $\hat{A}$ conventionally denotes a tensor field physically equivalent to $A$.
