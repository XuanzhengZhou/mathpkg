# Physical Equivalence of Tensors at a Point

On a semi-Riemannian manifold $(M, g)$, the metric $g_x$ at each point $x \in M$ induces a canonical isomorphism $\phi_x: M_x \to M_x^*$ between the tangent space and its dual. This metric isomorphism extends to an isomorphism between tensor spaces of different types but equal total order. Two tensors at $x$ are declared physically equivalent if they map to the same purely covariant tensor under these isomorphisms. In classical differential geometry language, this means one tensor is obtained from the other by raising and/or lowering indices with the metric, and consequently they carry identical physical content in general relativity.
