A spacetime $(M, g)$ is spherically symmetric if its isometry group contains a subgroup $H$ isomorphic to $SO(3)$ whose orbits are spacelike and at most $2$-dimensional. This definition captures the physical notion of rotational symmetry in general relativity, where the $SO(3)$ action corresponds to spatial rotations. Spherically symmetric spacetimes include important exact solutions such as the Schwarzschild spacetime, the Einstein–de Sitter spacetime, and models of stable stars.
