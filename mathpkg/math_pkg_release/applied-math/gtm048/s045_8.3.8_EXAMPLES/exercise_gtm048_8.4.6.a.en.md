Let $(M, g)$ be spherically symmetric with subgroup $H \subseteq \mathcal{G}(M)$ as in the definition. Show that an orbit of $H$ diffeomorphic to the $2$-sphere has the intrinsic geometry of a $2$-sphere in Euclidean space.
