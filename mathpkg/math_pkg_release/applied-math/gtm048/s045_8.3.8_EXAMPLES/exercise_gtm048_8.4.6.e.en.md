Suppose $(M, g)$ is a normal Schwarzschild spacetime or a Schwarzschild black hole, and let $H$ be as in the definition of spherical symmetry. Show that there is no $0$-dimensional orbit (no "center"; in spherically symmetric models of stable stars one replaces part of a normal Schwarzschild spacetime with a different spacetime, using some appropriate matter model; then there is a timelike geodesic — the history of the center of the star — each point on which is a zero-dimensional orbit).
