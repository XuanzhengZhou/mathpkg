This proposition gives a characterization of maximal spacetimes: a spacetime $(M,g)$ contained in a larger spacetime $(N,h)$ is actually equal to $N$ if every lightlike geodesic of $N$ that intersects $M$ is entirely contained in $M$. Roughly speaking, a spacetime is maximal if and only if one cannot "see into it or out of it" along lightlike geodesics. This highlights the fundamental role of lightlike geodesics in determining the causal boundary of a spacetime. The proof uses techniques beyond the scope of the immediate discussion (involving once-broken lightlike geodesics from boundary points) and is deferred to Exercise 5.2.7.
