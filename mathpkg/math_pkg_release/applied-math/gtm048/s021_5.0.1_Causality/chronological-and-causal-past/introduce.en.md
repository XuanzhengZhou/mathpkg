The chronological and causal past sets of a point $z$ in a space-time formalize the idea of an event's "backward light cone." The chronological past consists of points that can signal $z$ via timelike curves (slower than light), while the causal past includes those that can signal $z$ via any causal curve (at or below light speed). Dual definitions for the future sets are obtained by reversing the time orientation, and these dual results are generally taken for granted throughout the theory.
