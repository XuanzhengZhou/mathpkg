Chronological precedence is the fundamental notion of ordering in a space-time $(M, g)$. It formalizes the idea that one event can influence another by means of a material observer traveling slower than light. The definition uses future-pointing timelike curves, whose arclength models the proper time measured by a comoving clock. This concept underpins the entire causal structure of Lorentzian manifolds and is the basis for defining the chronology relation $\ll$.
