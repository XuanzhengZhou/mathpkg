The causality relation $\leq$ generalizes the chronology relation $\ll$ by allowing causal curves (which may be lightlike) instead of requiring strictly timelike curves. It includes the identity $x = z$ as a base case, making it reflexive. Intuitively, $x \leq z$ captures the idea that information from $x$ can reach $z$ via signals traveling at or below the speed of light. While $\ll$ is always a well-behaved relation, $\leq$ can be globally subtle and messy, as explored in Section 8.3.
