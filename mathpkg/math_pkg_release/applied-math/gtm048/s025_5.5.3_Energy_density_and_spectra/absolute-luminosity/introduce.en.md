Absolute luminosity $L$ is an intrinsic property of an astronomical object measuring the total electromagnetic energy it radiates per unit of its own proper time. It is a fundamental quantity in astrophysics, used to characterize stars, galaxies, and other luminous objects independently of the observer's distance. The absolute luminosity contrasts with apparent luminosity, which depends on the observer's position.
