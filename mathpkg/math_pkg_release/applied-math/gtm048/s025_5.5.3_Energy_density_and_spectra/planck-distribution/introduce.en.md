A Planck distribution function (also called thermal equilibrium spectrum or blackbody radiation) is a photon distribution function $F_Z$ whose energy dependence is given by the Planck function $P$ evaluated at $e/kT$. The distribution is characterized by a single parameter, the temperature $T$, and is independent of spatial direction $U$, reflecting spatial isotropy. Such distributions can be produced by a gas held at temperature $T$ while emitting light, and their main properties were discovered by Planck in 1900-1901.
