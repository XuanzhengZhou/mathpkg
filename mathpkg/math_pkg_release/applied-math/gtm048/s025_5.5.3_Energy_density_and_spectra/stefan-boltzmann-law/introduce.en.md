The Stefan-Boltzmann law states that the total energy density $u$ of a Planck (thermal equilibrium) photon distribution is proportional to the fourth power of the temperature: $u = a_0 T^4$. The constant of proportionality $a_0 = 8\pi^5 k^4/(15h^3)$ is called the blackbody constant. The computation proceeds by integrating the specific intensity $I = e^3 P(e/kT)$ over all energies and all spatial directions, reducing to a standard dimensionless integral via the substitution $u = e/kT$.
