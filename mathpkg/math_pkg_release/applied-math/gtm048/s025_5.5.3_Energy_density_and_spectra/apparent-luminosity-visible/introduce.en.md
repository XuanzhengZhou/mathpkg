Apparent luminosity in the visible $\ell_V$ is the energy per unit volume received from an object that falls within the visible frequency range (approximately $3 \times 10^{14}$ to $10^{15}$ Hz). The human eye responds rather directly to this quantity, which is obtained by restricting the energy density integral to the visible energy interval $[a,b]$. Other detection devices (radio telescopes, x-ray counters) respond to different energy ranges or different quantities derived from the photon distribution.
