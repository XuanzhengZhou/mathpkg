An exhaustive sequence of integration regions is a nested sequence of compact subsets $\mathcal{K}_i$ of a manifold $N$ whose interiors expand to cover the whole manifold. This construction is used to define improper integrals of continuous functions on noncompact manifolds by taking limits over such sequences. A standard method to produce exhaustive sequences is via proper Morse functions $h$, setting $\mathcal{K}_i = h^{-1}[0,i]$.
