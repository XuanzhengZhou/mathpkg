A sequence $\{\mathcal{K}_i\}$ of compact subsets of an $n$-dimensional $C^\infty$ manifold $N$ (with a volume element $A$ on $N$) is called an exhaustive sequence of integration regions iff $\mathcal{K}_i \subset \mathcal{K}_{i+1}^0$ (the interior of $\mathcal{K}_{i+1}$) for all $i \in \mathbb{Z}^+$ and $\bigcup_i \mathcal{K}_i = N$.

One way to obtain such a sequence is to take a proper $C^\infty$ function $h: N \to \mathbb{R}$ with $h \geq 0$, whose critical points (points where $dh = 0$) form a discrete set, and with $0 \in hN$, then define $\mathcal{K}_i = h^{-1}[0, i]$.
