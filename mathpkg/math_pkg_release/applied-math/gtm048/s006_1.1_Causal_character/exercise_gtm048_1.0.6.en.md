Let $M_x$ be a tangent space to a Lorentzian manifold $(M, g)$, and let $L: M_x \to M_x$ be a linear transformation. Recall that $L$ is **self-adjoint** (respectively, **skew-adjoint**) with respect to $g_x$ iff $g(LX, Y) = g(X, LY)$ (respectively, $g(LX, Y) = -g(X, LY)$) for all $X, Y \in M_x$. Show that $L$ can be regarded as $L \in T_1^1(M_x)$ and is then self-adjoint (respectively, skew-adjoint) iff it is physically equivalent to a symmetric (respectively, skew-symmetric) tensor $S \in T_2^0(M_x)$.
