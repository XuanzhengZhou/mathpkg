This proposition characterizes the causal character of a subspace $W$ in a Lorentzian vector space in terms of its orthogonal complement $W^\perp$. Specifically, $W$ is timelike precisely when $W^\perp$ consists entirely of spacelike vectors, and $W$ is lightlike exactly when it shares a nonzero vector with its orthogonal complement (i.e., $W$ is degenerate with respect to $g$). These equivalences follow from the fact that the restriction of $g$ to $W^\perp$ is positive definite when $W$ contains a timelike vector, and that a lightlike subspace necessarily contains a null vector lying in $W \cap W^\perp$.
