In an $N$-dimensional Lorentzian vector space $(V, g)$, the metric $g$ is not positive definite, which leads to a trichotomy of causal character. A subspace $W \subset V$ is spacelike if $g$ restricted to $W$ is positive definite, lightlike if $g$ on $W$ is positive semi-definite but degenerate, and timelike otherwise (i.e., $g$ takes negative values on $W$). The causal character of a single vector $v \in V$ is defined via the subspace it spans. This classification is fundamental to all of Lorentzian geometry and general relativity, underpinning the structure of lightcones, causality, and time orientability.
