When the Lorentzian vector space $(V, g)$ is viewed as a Lorentzian manifold, its lightcone $\mathcal{L}_0$ (the set of all nonzero lightlike vectors) becomes a submanifold. This proposition asserts that the lightcone inherits a lightlike causal character as a submanifold. The result is geometrically natural: the tangent space to $\mathcal{L}_0$ at any point contains the radial direction (which is lightlike) and is orthogonal to itself in a degenerate manner. For $N \geq 3$, the lightcone splits into two connected components, each diffeomorphic to $\mathbb{R} \times S^{N-2}$.
