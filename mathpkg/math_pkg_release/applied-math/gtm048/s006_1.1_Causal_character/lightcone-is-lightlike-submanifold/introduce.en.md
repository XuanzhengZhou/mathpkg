Proposition 1.1.7 and its surrounding discussion establish the global structure of the lightcone $\mathcal{L}_0$ and the set of timelike vectors $\mathcal{T}_0$ in a Lorentzian vector space. Viewing $(V,g)$ as a Lorentzian manifold, $\mathcal{L}_0$ is a lightlike submanifold, while $\mathcal{T}_0$ is an open submanifold that splits into two connected components (future and past). For $N \geq 3$, the lightcone also decomposes into two components, each homeomorphic to $\mathbb{R} \times S^{N-2}$. This topological decomposition is crucial for defining time orientation and causal future/past distinctions in general relativity.
