Let $(M, \mathbf{M}, F)$ be a dust spacetime ($p = 0$) with irrotational comoving reference frame $Z$ and zero electromagnetic field. Retain the assumptions and notation of Lemma 4.3.3. Let $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to M$ be an observer in $Z$ (so $\gamma$ is an integral curve of $Z$). Suppose there exists $u_0 \in \mathcal{E}$ such that $(\operatorname{div} Z)(\gamma(u_0)) < 0$. Then $\gamma$ cannot have all of $\mathbb{R}$ as its domain parameter; in fact, $\operatorname{div} Z \circ \gamma$ must tend to $-\infty$ within a finite proper time bounded by

$$
\Delta u \leq -\frac{2}{(\operatorname{div} Z)(\gamma(u_0))}.
$$

Equivalently, the observer $\gamma$ is geodesically incomplete.
