This is the elementary collapse theorem of Raychaudhuri, which captures the essential mechanism behind the much deeper singularity theorems of Penrose, Hawking, and Geroch. Under the simplest possible assumptions — pressureless dust with an irrotational comoving frame — any observer experiencing a negative expansion scalar must encounter a singularity (infinite contraction) in finite proper time. The proof is a clean differential inequality argument: the Raychaudhuri equation forces the expansion to satisfy $f' < -\frac{1}{2}f^2$, which makes $f$ blow up to $-\infty$ in finite time whenever it starts negative.
