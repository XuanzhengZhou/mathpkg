Proposition 7.3.2 gives an explicit algebraic formula for the curvature tensor of a Schwarzschild spacetime $U = N \cup B$. The curvature is expressed in terms of the radial coordinate $r$, the Schwarzschild mass parameter $\mu$, the wedge product $dr \wedge dt$, an auxiliary tensor $T$ encoding the spherical part of the geometry, and the pullback $P^* \zeta$ of the area form on the unit 2-sphere. The factor $4\mu/r^3$ shows that curvature falls off as $r^{-3}$ and is proportional to the mass $\mu$. This formula underpins the subsequent corollaries characterizing the curvature magnitude $f$ and the geometric interpretation of the coordinate functions.
