The proof proceeds by direct computation of the curvature tensor from the Schwarzschild metric. Using the metric $g$ on $U = N \cup B$, the auxiliary tensor $j = g - r^2 P^* h$ is defined to isolate the non-spherical part of the metric. The $(4,0)$-tensor $T$ is constructed from $j$ and $P^* h$ with the antisymmetrization $(1 \leftrightarrow 2)$ and $[3 \leftrightarrow 4]$ to ensure the algebraic symmetries of a curvature-like tensor (as described in Section 1.0.2a-c). Computing the curvature tensor $\hat{R}$ of the Schwarzschild metric and expressing it in terms of $dr \wedge dt$, $T$, and $P^* \zeta$ yields the stated formula with the factor $4\mu/r^3$.

The reader is expected to have verified each step of the computation, analogous to the curvature computation performed in Proposition 1.4.4.
