In a Schwarzschild spacetime, a particle or observer is said to escape to infinity if the curvature magnitude $f$ along its worldline tends to zero as the parameter approaches the endpoint. The curvature magnitude $f$ measures the overall strength of the gravitational field; even observers confined to an opaque box can determine $f$ locally through relative acceleration measurements (Proposition 2.3.3). This definition captures the intuitive notion that a particle "reaches infinity" when the gravitational field becomes negligibly weak, and it is used in Corollary 7.3.4 to characterize $r/\mu^{1/3}$ and to show that escape to infinity is equivalent to $r \circ \gamma \to \infty$.
