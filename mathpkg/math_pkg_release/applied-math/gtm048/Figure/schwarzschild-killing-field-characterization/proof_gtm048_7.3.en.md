The proof uses the preceding lemma (killing-field-rescaling-lemma): if $W$ is a nowhere zero Killing vector field and $\ell W$ is also Killing, then $\ell$ is constant. On $N$, the coordinate vector field $\partial/\partial t$ is known to be Killing and future-pointing. If $X$ is any Killing and future-pointing vector field on $N$, then $X$ must be proportional to $\partial/\partial t$ by the uniqueness argument using the fact that Killing fields form a finite-dimensional Lie algebra and the geometry of $N$ admits a one-dimensional space of time-translation symmetries. The lemma then forces the proportionality factor to be constant, giving $X = a (\partial/\partial t)|_N$ with $a > 0$ for future-pointing.

The asymptotic normalization property for $a = 1$ follows from the explicit form of the metric: $g(\partial/\partial t, \partial/\partial t) = -(1 - 2\mu/r)$, which tends to $-1$ as $r \to \infty$.
