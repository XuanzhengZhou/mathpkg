Proposition 7.3.7 establishes the existence of three conserved quantities (first integrals) along any causal geodesic in a Schwarzschild spacetime. These constants, analogous to the Newtonian conservation of energy and angular momentum for a particle in a spherically symmetric, time-independent gravitational field, arise from the symmetries of the Schwarzschild metric. The energy $E$ is conserved because $\partial/\partial t$ is a Killing vector field (time-translation symmetry). The angular momentum component $J_x$ is conserved due to the rotational Killing field corresponding to rotations about the $x$-axis. The total angular momentum magnitude $J$ then follows from the spherical symmetry. These first integrals are essential for analyzing the motion of freely falling particles and photons in the Schwarzschild geometry, including the determination of orbits and the classification of geodesics.
