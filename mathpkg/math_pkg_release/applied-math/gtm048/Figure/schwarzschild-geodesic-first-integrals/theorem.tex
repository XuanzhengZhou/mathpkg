Let $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \to U$ be a future-pointing causal geodesic in a Schwarzschild spacetime $U = N \cup B$, where $P: U \to \mathcal{S}^2$ is the projection and $r: U \to (0,\infty)$ is the radial coordinate. Set $\delta = P \circ \gamma$ (a curve in $\mathcal{S}^2$) and $\rho = r \circ \gamma: \mathcal{E} \to (0,\infty)$. Define $\partial = (\partial/\partial t) \circ \gamma: \mathcal{E} \to TU$.

Then there exist unique numbers $E \in \mathbb{R}$, $J \in [0, \infty)$, $J_x \in [-J, J]$ such that:

(a) $g(\gamma_*, \partial) = -E$;

(b) $\rho^4 h(\delta_*, \delta_*) = J^2$;

(c) $\rho^2 h(\delta_*, K_x) = J_x$,

where $h$ is the standard round metric on $\mathcal{S}^2$ and $K_x$ is the generator of rotations about the $x$-axis on $\mathcal{S}^2$ (Section 7.0).

Physically, $E$ is the conserved energy, $J$ is the magnitude of the conserved angular momentum, and $J_x$ is its $x$-component. These are the "constants of the motion" for freely falling particles in Schwarzschild spacetimes.
