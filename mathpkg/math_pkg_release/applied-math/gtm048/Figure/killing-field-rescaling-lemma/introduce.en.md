This lemma states that if a $C^\infty$ function rescales a nowhere-zero Killing vector field to produce another Killing field, then the function must be constant. The proof uses the Killing equation $g(D_X(\ell W), Y) + g(X, D_Y(\ell W)) = 0$ to deduce that all directional derivatives $X\ell$ vanish, forcing $\ell$ to be locally constant. Connectedness of the manifold then implies global constancy. The lemma is used in GTM048 to prove Corollary 7.3.5, where it shows that any future-pointing Killing field on the normal Schwarzschild region $N$ must be a constant multiple of $\partial/\partial t$. The condition that $W$ is nowhere zero is essential; the weaker statement that $W$ is not identically zero (as noted in Exercise 7.3.14) can also suffice under appropriate conditions.
