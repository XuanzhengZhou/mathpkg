(a) By Corollary 7.3.4 and Proposition 7.5.2, the Kretschmann scalar $f = R_{abcd}R^{abcd}$ and the function $144\mu^2/r^6$ coincide on the open submanifold $M_I$ of $K$. Since both $f$ and $144\mu^2/r^6$ are real-analytic functions on $K$, their equality on the open set $M_I$ implies they coincide on all of $K$. Hence $f = 144\mu^2/r^6$ on $K$.

(b) That $K$ is vacuum (i.e., Ricci-flat) and not flat follows by the same analyticity argument: the vacuum condition $R_{ab} = 0$ holds on $M_I$, hence on $K$; the non-vanishing of $f$ on $M_I$ extends to $K$, so $K$ is not flat.

Now suppose $X: K \to TK$ is a Killing vector field. Using Proposition 7.5.2, the proof of Proposition 7.3.5 implies that $dr(X) = 0$ on $M_I$. By analyticity, $dr(X) = 0$ on all of $K$. But by the proof of Proposition 7.5.2, $dr$ is timelike on the black hole region $M_{II}$. Consequently, any Killing vector field $X$ must be spacelike on $M_{II}$ (since $dr(X) = 0$ and $dr$ is timelike). Therefore $K$ admits no timelike Killing vector field, so $K$ is not stationary, and a fortiori not static.
