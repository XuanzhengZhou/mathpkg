Proposition 6.2.2 establishes that for a finite superposition of relativistic models whose components are particle flows, perfect fluids, or photon gases, the overall stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ is normal and satisfies the timelike convergence condition — provided at least one of three mild generality conditions holds. This result justifies the standard modeling practice of treating cosmic matter as a superposition of simple components, ensuring the resulting spacetime satisfies physically reasonable energy conditions.
