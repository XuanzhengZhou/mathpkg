Planck's constant $h \cong 6.3 	imes 10^{-43}$ seconds$^2$ is a universal constant that relates the energy and frequency of a photon via $e = hf$. The square root $\sqrt{h} = 6.43 	imes 10^{-43}$ seconds is regarded as the natural time scale for quantum gravity; in current cosmology, quantum gravitational effects are usually assumed negligible at times later than about $10^{-42}$ seconds after a big bang.
