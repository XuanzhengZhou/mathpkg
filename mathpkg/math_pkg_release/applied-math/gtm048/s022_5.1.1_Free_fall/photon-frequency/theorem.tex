Let $e = -g(Y, Z) > 0$ be the energy that the instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ measures for the photon with energy-momentum $Y$. The \textbf{frequency} that $(z, Z)$ measures is
$$
f = \frac{e}{h},
$$
where $h$ is Planck's constant. Following a suggestion of Planck, Einstein postulated $e = hf$ in 1905.
