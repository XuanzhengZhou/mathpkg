Photon absorption refers to the destruction of a photon within spacetime at a specific future endpoint. If a photon curve $\lambda: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow M$ has a future endpoint $z \in M$, one says the photon is absorbed and received at $z$ as well as being destroyed there. The terms "emitted" and "absorbed" are part of the colorful language developed for dealing with photons in general relativity.
