Dust is the simplest matter model in general relativity, consisting of a single pressureless particle flow. The matter is characterized by a reference frame $Z$ (the comoving observer field) and an energy density function $\rho$. The stress-energy tensor takes the form $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z$, and the only matter equation is the conservation law $\operatorname{div} \hat{T} = 0$. Dust is a fundamental building block for more complex matter models such as superpositions and quasi-gases.
