Let $(m, 0, P, \eta)$ be a particle flow on a spacetime $M$ with $m \neq 0$ and $\eta$ nowhere zero. Then $Z = m^{-1} P$ is a reference frame on $M$ and $\rho = m \eta$ is a function $M \rightarrow (0, \infty)$ representing energy density as measured by a comoving instantaneous observer.

A dust on $M$ is a matter model $(M, M, F)$ where $M$ consists of a single particle flow of type $(m, 0)$, with stress-energy tensor $\hat{T} = \rho Z \otimes Z$ and charge-current density $J = 0$. The matter equations are simply the conservation law $\operatorname{div} \hat{T} = 0$.
