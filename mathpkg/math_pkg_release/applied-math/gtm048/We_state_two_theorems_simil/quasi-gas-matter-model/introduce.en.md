A quasi-gas generalizes the dust model by taking a finite superposition of particle flows. Each component particle flow has its own 4-velocity, and the generic conditions require that the set of component flows is nonempty and that no two distinct flows share the same 4-velocity at any spacetime point. The stress-energy tensor and charge-current density of a quasi-gas are the sums of those of its constituent particle flows, making it a natural model for multi-component collision-free matter in general relativity.
