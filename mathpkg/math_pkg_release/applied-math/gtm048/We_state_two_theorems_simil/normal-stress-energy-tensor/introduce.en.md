A stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ is called normal at a point $x$ if it maps every causal vector to a timelike vector. This condition captures the physical expectation that matter should not allow superluminal energy flux. For example, the matter stress-energy tensor of a dust is normal at every point. Normality is a key hypothesis in Proposition 3.14.1, which guarantees the existence of a unique timelike eigenvector for the stress-energy tensor.
