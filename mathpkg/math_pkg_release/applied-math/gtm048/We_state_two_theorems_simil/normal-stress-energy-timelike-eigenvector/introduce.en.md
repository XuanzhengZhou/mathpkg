Proposition 3.14.1 establishes that a normal stress-energy tensor at a point $x$ always possesses a timelike eigenvector, unique up to scaling. The proof uses Brouwer's fixed point theorem on a compact set diffeomorphic to a closed unit ball in $\mathbb{R}^3$. Uniqueness follows from a contradiction argument: if two linearly independent timelike eigenvectors existed, their span would contain a null vector, contradicting normality. This proposition provides the mathematical foundation for the physical interpretation of a stress-energy tensor's principal axis.
