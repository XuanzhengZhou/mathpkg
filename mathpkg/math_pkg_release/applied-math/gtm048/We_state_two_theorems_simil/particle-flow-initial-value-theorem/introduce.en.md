Theorem 3.11.2 is an initial value theorem for particle flows on a spacetime, analogous to Theorem 3.11.1 for Maxwell's equations. Given initial data -- a spacelike submanifold, an initial future-pointing momentum vector field $P_0$ of constant norm $-m^2$, and an initial particle density function $\eta_0$ -- the theorem guarantees local existence and uniqueness of a particle flow satisfying the simple matter equations. The proof combines ideas from Proposition 3.2.3, Theorem 3.8.3, and Theorem 3.11.1.
