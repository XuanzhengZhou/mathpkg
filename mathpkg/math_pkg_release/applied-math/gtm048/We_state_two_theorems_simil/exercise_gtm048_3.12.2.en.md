In Example 3.12.1 (a model for the decay of beryllium-8 nuclei into helium-4 nuclei with proportionality constant $k$ determined by microphysics), let $J$ be the charge-current density of $M$, and $\hat{D} = \hat{T} + \hat{E}$ be as in Section 3.10.

(a) Show $\operatorname{div} J = 0$ ("particles are being created and destroyed but electric charge is not").

(b) Show the universal criterion (a) of this section holds---that is, if $(M, M, F)$ also obeys Maxwell's equations, $\operatorname{div} \hat{D} = 0$.

(c) Motivate the factor "2" in Example 3.12.1a directly from the collision conservation laws of Section 3.8.

(d) State and prove a theorem that shows that our other universal criterion is also valid, in particular that $\eta_1$ cannot "overshoot" and become negative.
