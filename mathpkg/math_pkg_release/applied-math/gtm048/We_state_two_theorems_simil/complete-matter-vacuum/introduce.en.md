Complete matter vacuum is the simplest relativistic model: it contains no matter whatsoever, with vanishing stress-energy tensor and charge-current density. It represents the absence of all matter including test matter. When the electromagnetic field $F$ also vanishes, one obtains complete vacuum. This model serves as a baseline for understanding how matter influences spacetime geometry through the Einstein field equations.
