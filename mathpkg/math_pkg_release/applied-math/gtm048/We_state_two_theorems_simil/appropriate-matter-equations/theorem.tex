Let $(M, M, F)$ be a relativistic model. In general, deciding on appropriate matter equations for the model involves an intricate mixture of empirical and theoretical arguments. However, two universal criteria are used:

(a) When combined with Maxwell's equations, appropriate matter equations always imply the differential energy-momentum 'conservation' law $\operatorname{div} \hat{D} = 0$.

(b) All physical quantities that are physically required to be positive (e.g., particle number density, energy density as measured by any instantaneous observer) must be positive; furthermore, matter equations must not force these quantities to become negative during evolution.
