Suppose the following data are given: (a) a spacetime $M$; (b) a charge-current density $J$ on $M$ such that $\operatorname{div} J = 0$; (c) a coordinate neighborhood $U \subset M$; (d) an imbedded three-dimensional spacelike submanifold $\phi: W \rightarrow U$; (e) a 2-form $F_0$ over $\phi$ satisfying $$\phi^*(dF_0) = 0 \quad\text{and}\quad d(\phi^*(i(\hat{F}_0)(\Omega \circ \phi))) = 4\pi\phi^*(i(J)\Omega).$$

Then for $U$ sufficiently small, there exists a unique 2-form $F$ on $U$ such that: (a) $F \circ \phi = F_0$; (b) $(U, F, J)$ obeys Maxwell's equations.
