For a relativistic model whose matter consists of a finite collection of particle flows, the energy density measured by an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ vanishes precisely when the world densities of all constituent particle flows vanish at $z$. This proposition establishes the equivalence between the vanishing of the measured energy density $T(Z, Z)$, the vanishing of the stress-energy tensor evaluated at $z$, and the vanishing of each individual world density $\eta_A z$. The result relies on the nonnegativity of world densities and the fact that $g(P_A, Z) \neq 0$ for any instantaneous observer.
