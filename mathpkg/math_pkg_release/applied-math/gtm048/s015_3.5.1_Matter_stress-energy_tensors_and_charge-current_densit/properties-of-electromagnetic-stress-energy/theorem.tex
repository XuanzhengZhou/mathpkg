\textbf{Proposition 3.7.4.} Let $F$ be an electromagnetic field on $M$, let $E$ be its stress-energy tensor, and let $\hat{E}$ be the $(2,0)$-tensor field physically equivalent to $E$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\hat{E}$ is symmetric and $\operatorname{trace} \hat{E} = 0$.
\item[(b)] $\hat{E}(\omega, \omega) \geq 0$ for every causal 1-form $\omega$.
\item[(c)] If $(M, F, J)$ obeys Maxwell's equations, then $\operatorname{div} \hat{E} = -\tilde{F}J$, where $\tilde{F}J$ denotes the 1-form obtained by contracting the $(1,1)$-tensor $\tilde{F}$ (physically equivalent to $F$) with the vector field $J$.
\end{enumerate}
