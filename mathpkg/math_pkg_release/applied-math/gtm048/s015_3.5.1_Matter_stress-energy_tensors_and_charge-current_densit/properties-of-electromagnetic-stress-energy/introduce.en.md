Proposition 3.7.4 establishes three fundamental properties of the electromagnetic stress-energy tensor. Part (a) shows that $\hat{E}$ is symmetric and traceless, a consequence of the antisymmetry of $F$. Part (b) proves that $\hat{E}$ satisfies the dominant energy condition: its evaluation on any causal 1-form is nonnegative, guaranteeing that energy density is nonnegative for all observers. Part (c) gives the divergence identity $\operatorname{div} \hat{E} = -\tilde{F}J$, which describes how energy and momentum are exchanged between the electromagnetic field and charged matter, generalizing the classical Lorentz force law.
