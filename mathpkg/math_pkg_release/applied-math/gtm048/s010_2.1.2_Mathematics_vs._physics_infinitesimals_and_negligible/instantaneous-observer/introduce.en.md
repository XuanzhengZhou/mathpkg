An instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ is the mathematical model of an observer making measurements at a single spacetime point $z$. The vector $Z$ defines the observer's local direction of time (a future-pointing unit timelike vector), and its orthogonal complement $Z^\perp$ defines the observer's local rest space — the spatial directions orthogonal to the observer's worldline at that instant. The orthogonal decomposition $M_z = Z^\perp \oplus \operatorname{span} Z$ provides the foundation for defining Newtonian velocity, speed, angle, and length in the Lorentzian setting.
