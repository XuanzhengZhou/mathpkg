The Newtonian speed is the magnitude of the Newtonian velocity $p/e$, measuring how fast an object moves through the observer's local rest space relative to the observer's proper time. It is defined as $|p|/e$ where $|p|$ is the Riemannian norm of the spatial component $p \in Z^\perp$. A key property is that the Newtonian speed never exceeds $1$ (the speed of light in geometric units), and a vector is timelike precisely when its Newtonian speed is strictly less than $1$. This provides the relativistic generalization of the classical notion of speed.
