Let $(M, g)$ be a spacetime and let $(z, Z)$ be an instantaneous observer at $z \in M$. Let $Y \in M_z$ be a future-pointing lightlike (null) vector, i.e., $g(Y, Y) = 0$ and $Y$ is future-directed.

Then there exists a unique scalar $e > 0$ and a unique unit spacelike vector $U \in Z^\perp$ (so $g(U, U) = 1$ and $g(Z, U) = 0$) such that
$$Y = e(Z - U).$$

\textbf{Interpretation.} $U$ is the direction in $Z^\perp$ from which the observer sees the light arriving. For two such light particles $Y, Y'$ with corresponding unit vectors $U, U' \in Z^\perp$, the Newtonian angle $\theta$ between them satisfies
$$\cos \theta = g(U, U').$$
The scalar $e$ corresponds to the energy (and color) of the light: larger $e$ means bluer (higher frequency) light.
