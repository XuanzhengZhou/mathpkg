The Lorentz world-force law is the relativistic generalization of Newton's $\vec{F} = m\vec{a}$ for charged particles moving in a curved spacetime under the influence of an electromagnetic field $F$. It equates the electromagnetic force $e\tilde{F}\gamma_*$ (generalizing the Lorentz force $e(\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B})$) to the covariant acceleration $D_{\gamma_*}\gamma_*$ of the particle's world-line. This single tensorial equation simultaneously encodes the effects of electric, magnetic, and gravitational forces on the particle.
