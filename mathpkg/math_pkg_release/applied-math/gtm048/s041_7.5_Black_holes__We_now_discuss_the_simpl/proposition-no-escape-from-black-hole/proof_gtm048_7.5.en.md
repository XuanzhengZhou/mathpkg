**Proof.** Let $V$ be the vector field physically equivalent to $-dv$. Then $g(V, V) = 0$ and $g(U, V) < 0$, so $V$ is future pointing, lightlike, and nowhere parallel to $U$. Computing $(d/ds)(r \circ \gamma)$ on $M_{II}$: we have $dr = 2\mu(1 - 2\mu/r)(dv/v + du/u)$, and on $M_{II}$ (where $v < 0$), this forces $dr/ds < 0$ along any future-pointing causal curve. Since $r$ strictly decreases and cannot cross $r = 0$ (which is not in $K$), the curve remains trapped in $M_{II}$.
