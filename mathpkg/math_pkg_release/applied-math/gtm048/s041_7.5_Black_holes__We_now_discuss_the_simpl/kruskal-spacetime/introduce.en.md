The Kruskal spacetime is the maximal analytic extension of the Schwarzschild solution, discovered independently by Kruskal and Szekeres. It contains four regions: two asymptotically flat normal Schwarzschild regions and two black hole/white hole regions separated by the event horizon at $r = 2\mu$. It is the simplest spacetime used to model complete gravitational collapse and black hole formation.
