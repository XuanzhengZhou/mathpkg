The photon distribution function $F_Z$ is the relativistic analog of a kinetic distribution function for photons, describing the number density of photons as a function of spatial direction and energy from the perspective of an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$. When the number of photons is enormous, a discrete counting procedure is replaced by this smoothed-out idealization, much as a Newtonian number density replaces individual particle tracking. The function $F_Z: P_z \to [0, \infty)$ encodes the complete directional and spectral information available to the observer; physically, $e^3 F_Z(U, e)$ gives the perceived brightness in direction $U$ at energy $e$, and $e^2 F_Z$ models photon number per unit spatial volume per unit solid angle per unit energy interval.
