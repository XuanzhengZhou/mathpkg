This proposition provides a geometric interpretation of the frequency ratio in terms of proper time along a rectangle decomposition. When a rectangle $\sigma$ has its $\sigma_0$-curve as a freely falling photon, the frequency ratio measured by the instantaneous observers at the endpoints equals the inverse of the ratio of their proper time parametrization speeds. The proof is elegant and relies on Gauss' lemma, which forces the first factor in the frequency ratio expression to equal one. This result bridges the operational frequency measurement with intrinsic geometric quantities of the spacetime.
