By the definition of the frequency ratio,

$$\tau = \frac{g(\dot{\sigma}_0(a), \dot{\gamma}_a(0))}{g(\dot{\sigma}_0(b), \dot{\gamma}_b(0))} \cdot \frac{|\dot{\gamma}_b(0)|}{|\dot{\gamma}_a(0)|}.$$

The first factor equals one by Gauss' lemma (Section 5.0.3), since $\dot{\sigma}_0$ is lightlike and orthogonal to the timelike observers in the appropriate sense. Therefore

$$\tau = \frac{|\dot{\gamma}_b(0)|}{|\dot{\gamma}_a(0)|} = \left(\frac{|\dot{\gamma}_b(0)|}{|\dot{\gamma}_a(0)|}\right)^{-1} \, \cdot$$

Thus $\tau$ is the inverse of the proper time ratio $|\dot{\gamma}_b(0)| / |\dot{\gamma}_a(0)|$ for $\sigma$ at $\sigma_0$.
