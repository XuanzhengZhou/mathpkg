The direction-energy space $P_z$ is the natural configuration space for describing photon observations from the perspective of a single instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$. Each incoming photon is characterized by two pieces of data: its spatial direction of arrival $U$ on the celestial sphere $\mathcal{S}_Z$, and its measured energy $e$. The space $P_z = \mathcal{S}_Z \times (0, \infty)$ parametrizes these pairs and serves as the domain for the photon distribution function $F_Z$, which models the statistical distribution of many photons. Integration on $P_z$ is used to compute macroscopic quantities such as energy density from microscopic photon models.
