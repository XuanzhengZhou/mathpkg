A stationary spacetime corresponds to a gravitational field generated by a time-independent source. The existence of a stationary reference frame means there is a timelike Killing vector field (up to rescaling), implying that the local geometry does not change along the flow of the reference frame. A static spacetime is the further specialization where the source also does not rotate, corresponding to an irrotational stationary reference frame.
