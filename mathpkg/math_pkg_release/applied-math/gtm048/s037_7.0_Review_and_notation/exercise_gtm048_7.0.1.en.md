Suppose $x \in \mathcal{S}^2$ and $\eta$ is the 1-form physically equivalent to the generator of rotations $K_x$, i.e., $\eta(X) = h(K_x, X)$ for all $X$ tangent to $\mathcal{S}^2$. Show that there exist $y, z \in \mathcal{S}^2$ such that
$$
h = \eta \otimes \eta + \zeta \otimes \zeta + \theta \otimes \theta,
$$
where $\zeta, \theta$ are, respectively, the 1-forms physically equivalent to $K_y$ and $K_z$.
