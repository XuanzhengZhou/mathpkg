Suppose $x \in \mathcal{S}^2$, where $(\mathcal{S}^2, h, \zeta)$ is the unit sphere in $\mathbb{R}^3$. Then there is precisely one Killing vector field $K_x$ on $\mathcal{S}^2$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $K_x(x) = 0$.
\item[(b)] Let $\eta$ be the 1-form physically equivalent to $K_x$ with respect to $h$, i.e., $\eta(X) = h(K_x, X)$ for all $X$ tangent to $\mathcal{S}^2$. Then $d\eta$ is consistent with the orientation of $\mathcal{S}^2$, i.e., $d\eta = f\zeta$ for some nonnegative function $f$ on $\mathcal{S}^2$.
\item[(c)] $\max h(K_x, K_x) = 1$, where the maximum is taken over all points of $\mathcal{S}^2$.
\end{enumerate}

We call $K_x$ the generator of rotations around the $x$-axis.
