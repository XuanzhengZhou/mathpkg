Gravitational redshift is the phenomenon where photons lose energy (are redshifted) when climbing out of a gravitational potential well, or gain energy (are blueshifted) when falling in. In the Newtonian limit, the gravitational redshift $\tau - 1$ corresponds to the gravitational potential difference between the emission and observation points.
