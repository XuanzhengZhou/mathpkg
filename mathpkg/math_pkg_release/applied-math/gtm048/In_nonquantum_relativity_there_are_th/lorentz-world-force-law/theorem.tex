Let $(M, g, D)$ be a spacetime, $F$ an electromagnetic field on $M$, and $(\gamma, m, e)$ a particle on $M$ with $\gamma: \mathcal{E} \rightarrow M$. Then $\tilde{F}\gamma_*$ is a vector field over $\gamma$ satisfying $\tilde{F}\gamma_*u \in (\gamma_*u)^\perp$ for all $u \in \mathcal{E}$, and $D_{\gamma_*}\gamma_*$ is the covariant acceleration of $\gamma$.

The particle $(\gamma, m, e)$ \textbf{obeys the Lorentz world-force law} with respect to $F$ iff

$$e\tilde{F}\gamma_* = D_{\gamma_*}\gamma_*.$$

In local coordinates $\{x^i\}$, writing $\gamma^i = x^i \circ \gamma$, this is equivalent to

$$\ddot{\gamma}^i + (\Gamma^i_{jk} \circ \gamma)\,\dot{\gamma}^j \dot{\gamma}^k = e\,(F^i_j \circ \gamma)\,\dot{\gamma}^j,$$

where $\Gamma^i_{jk}$ are the connection coefficients defined by $D_{\partial_j}\partial_k = \Gamma^i_{jk}\partial_i$, and $F^i_j = g^{ik}F_{kj}$.
