The Lorentz world-force law is the relativistic generalization of Newton's $\vec{F} = m\vec{a}$ for charged particles in curved spacetime. Given an electromagnetic field $F$, the law equates the covariant acceleration $D_{\gamma_*}\gamma_*$ of a particle $(\gamma, m, e)$ to the Lorentz force $e\tilde{F}\gamma_*$, where $\tilde{F}$ raises an index of $F$ via the metric $g$. The law simultaneously incorporates electric, magnetic, and gravitational effects: the term $e\tilde{F}\gamma_*$ corresponds to the Coulomb force $eE$ measured by an instantaneous observer, while $D_{\gamma_*}\gamma_*$ encodes both inertial and gravitational acceleration through the Levi-Civita connection.
