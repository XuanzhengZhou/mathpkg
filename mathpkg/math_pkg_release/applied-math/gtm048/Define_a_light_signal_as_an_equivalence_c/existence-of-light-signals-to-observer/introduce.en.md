Proposition 5.2.3 asserts that given an observer and a parameter value on its worldline, there exists a neighborhood of the worldline point and an interval of the observer's proper time such that every spacetime point in the neighborhood (not on the worldline) has exactly one future-directed light signal to the observer and one past-directed light signal from the observer. This is the foundational result justifying the use of radar as a basic distance-measuring device in general relativity, since it guarantees unique round-trip light signals between nearby points and an observer.
