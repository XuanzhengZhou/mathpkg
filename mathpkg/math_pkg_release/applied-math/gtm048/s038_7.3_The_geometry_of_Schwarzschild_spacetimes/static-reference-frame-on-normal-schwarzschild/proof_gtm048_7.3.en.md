By Corollary 7.3.5, $\partial/\partial t$ is a timelike Killing field on $N$. The normalization factor $(1 - 2\mu/r)^{-1/2}$ ensures that $g(Z, Z) = -1$, so $Z$ is a unit timelike vector field. Since $Z$ is proportional to a Killing field by a function constant on integral curves, $Z$ is static. The condition $Z\hat{t} \to 1$ as $r \to \infty$ fixes the normalization: at spatial infinity, $g(\partial/\partial t, \partial/\partial t) \to -1$, so $Z \to \partial/\partial t$, and the requirement $d\hat{t} = dt$ follows from the definition of a time function for $Z$ together with this asymptotic condition.

For the rest condition: an observer in $N$ is at rest in the frame defined by $Z$ iff its 4-velocity is proportional to $Z$, which is equivalent to the constancy of the spatial coordinates. In Schwarzschild coordinates, this means $r \circ \gamma$ is constant; since the observer must be in $N$ (the exterior region), this constant lies in $(2\mu, \infty)$.
