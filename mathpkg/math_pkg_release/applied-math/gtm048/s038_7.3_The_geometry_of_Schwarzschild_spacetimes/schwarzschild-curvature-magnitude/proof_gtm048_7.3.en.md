Let $\tilde{T}$ be the $(4,0)$-tensor field physically equivalent on $U$ to $T$ in Proposition 7.3.2; let $A$ be the $(2,0)$-tensor field physically equivalent to $P^*\zeta$. Using the Lorentzian metric $g$, we find that the $(4,0)$-tensor field $\tilde{R}$ physically equivalent to $\hat{R}$ on $U$ (in the sense of raising all indices) is given by

$$\tilde{R} = \frac{4\mu}{r^3}\left[2\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial r} \wedge \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\right) \otimes \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial r} \wedge \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\right) - r^{-2}\tilde{T} + 2r^{-4}A \otimes A\right]$$

Taking the appropriate four traces of $\tilde{R} \otimes \tilde{R}: M \to T_4^4 M$ (which corresponds to the scalar curvature invariant $f$ defined in Proposition 2.3.3) gives

$$f = \frac{144\mu^2}{r^6}.$$

The computation reduces to contracting the pairwise products of the three terms in the bracket, using the normalizations of the wedge products and the trace properties of $\tilde{T}$ and $A \otimes A$ relative to the metric $g$.
