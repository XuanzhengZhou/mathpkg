Corollary 5.7.4 provides the explicit algebraic form of the stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ for a photon gas that is Planck-distributed with respect to a comoving reference frame $X$. The formula expresses $\hat{T}$ as a linear combination of the inverse metric $\hat{g}$ and the tensor square $X \otimes X$ of the reference frame, with coefficients involving the inverse temperature parameter $j = (kT)^{-1}$. This result is used in cosmological applications: together with the Einstein field equation, it yields the relation $ho = a_0 T^4$ where $ho$ is the energy density and $a_0$ is the blackbody constant, and it implies $\operatorname{div} \hat{T} = 0$ when $j$ is constant.
