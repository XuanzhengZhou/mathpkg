Proposition 5.7.3 states that spatial isotropy of a photon gas $F$ at a point $z$ with respect to an instantaneous observer $(z,Z)$ implies the spatial isotropy of the associated stress-energy tensor $\hat{T}$ at the same point and with respect to the same observer. This result connects the symmetry properties of the photon distribution function directly to the symmetry properties of its energy-momentum content. The proof uses the invariance of the natural volume element $\Lambda_z$ on the future lightcone under spatial rotations (Proposition 5.6.1) and the transformation behavior of tensors under the extended action of the rotation group $\mathcal{O}^3$.
