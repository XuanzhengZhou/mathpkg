Suppose that for all $x \in M$, the restriction of $F$ to $\mathcal{L}_x^+$ is somewhere nonzero. Show that $F$ is spatially isotropic for an instantaneous observer $(z, Z)$ if and only if $F \circ \pi_Z$ is independent of the direction $U$ in $\mathcal{S}_Z$, and that in this case $F$ is not spatially isotropic for $(z, Z')$ whenever $Z' 
eq Z$.
