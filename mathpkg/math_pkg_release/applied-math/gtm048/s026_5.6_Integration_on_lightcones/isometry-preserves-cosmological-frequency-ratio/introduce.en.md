Lemma 6.0.9 asserts that the cosmological frequency ratio $\iota(x, z)$ is invariant under isometries of Einstein--de Sitter spacetime. If $\psi$ is any isometry of $M$ and a light signal exists from $x$ to $z$, then $\iota(\psi x, \psi z) = \iota(x, z)$. This lemma is a key technical tool in the proof of Proposition 6.0.8: it allows one to reduce the computation of $\iota(x, z)$ for arbitrary events to the special case of the standard photon, by applying a suitable isometry.
