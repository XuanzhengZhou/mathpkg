In fact, by definition of the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology (Chapter III, Section 3) the bilinear map $(u, x) \to u(x)$ is separately continuous on $\mathcal{L}_{\mathfrak{S}}(E, F) \times E$ into $F$; hence the partial map $f_x: u \to u(x)$ is continuous for each $x \in E$. Since $\mathcal{H} = \bigcap \{f_x^{-1}(D): x \in C\}$ and the positive cone $D$ of $F$ is closed, $\mathcal{H}$ is closed in $\mathcal{L}_{\mathfrak{S}}(E, F)$. Further, since $D$ is proper, $u \in \mathcal{H} \cap -\mathcal{H}$ implies that $u(x) = 0$ for $x \in C$; hence $u = 0$ if $C$ is total in $E$. Finally, if $E$ is a l.c.s. and $C$ is not total in $E$, there exists an $f \in E'$ such that $f \neq 0$ but $f(C) = \{0\}$, by virtue of the Hahn-Banach theorem; if $y$ is any element $\neq 0$ of $F$, the mapping $u = f \otimes y$ (defined by $x \to f(x)y$) satisfies $u \in \mathcal{H} \cap -\mathcal{H}$.
