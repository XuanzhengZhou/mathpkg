Let $E, F$ be ordered t.v.s. and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a family of bounded subsets of $E$ that covers $E$. Then the positive cone $\mathcal{H} \subset \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is closed for the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology. For $\mathcal{H}$ to be a proper cone, it is sufficient (and, if $E$ is a l.c.s. and $F \neq \{0\}$, necessary) that the positive cone $C$ of $E$ be total in $E$.
