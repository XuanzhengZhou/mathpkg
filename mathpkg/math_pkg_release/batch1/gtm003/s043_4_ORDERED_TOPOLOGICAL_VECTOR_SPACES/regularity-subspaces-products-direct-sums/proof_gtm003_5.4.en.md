This follows immediately from the characterization theorem. For a subspace $M$ of a regularly ordered vector space $L$, the induced order on $M$ is Archimedean and the positive cone $C \cap M$ is closed in $M$ (it is the intersection of $M$ with the closed set $C$), hence $C \cap M$ is normal for the induced topology, so $M$ is regularly ordered by the equivalence (c) $\Leftrightarrow$ (a). For products and direct sums, the positive cone is the product (respectively direct sum) of the positive cones of the factors; since each factor cone is closed and normal under a suitable Hausdorff l.c. topology, the product and direct sum inherit these properties componentwise, and the characterization theorem again yields regularity.
