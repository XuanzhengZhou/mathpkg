This proposition shows that when the domain cone is weakly normal and the codomain is the difference of its positive cone, the set of differences of positive linear maps is dense in the space of all continuous linear maps under the simple (weak) topology. The proof identifies the difference cone with the algebraic tensor product of the dual cone and the codomain, then applies density results for tensor products and the bipolar theorem.
