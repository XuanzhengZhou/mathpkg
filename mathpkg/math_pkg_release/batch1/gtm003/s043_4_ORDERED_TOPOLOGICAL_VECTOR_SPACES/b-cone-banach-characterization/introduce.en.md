This corollary characterizes B-cones in Banach spaces via closure properties. A cone in a Banach space is a B-cone if and only if its closure generates the whole space, which is equivalent to the closure being a strict B-cone. The proof uses the Baire category theorem and the fact that the closed convex circled hull of certain sets is a zero-neighborhood. This result connects the order-theoretic notion of B-cones with the topological structure of Banach spaces.
