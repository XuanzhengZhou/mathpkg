(a) $\Rightarrow$ (c) is clear from the preceding since $C'$ is normal in $E'$ whenever $C$ is a $\mathfrak{B}$-cone, by (3.3). (c) $\Rightarrow$ (b) is trivial. (b) $\Rightarrow$ (a): Let $M$ denote the closure of $U \cap C - U \cap C$, where $U$ is the unit ball of $E$. Then $M$ is convex, circled (over $\mathbb{R}$), and such that $E = \bigcup_{1}^{\infty} nM$; since $E$ is a Baire space, it follows that $M$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $E$ and hence $C$ is a $\mathfrak{B}$-cone.
