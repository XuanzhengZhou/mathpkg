Let $E$ be an ordered locally convex space whose positive cone $C$ is normal. Then there exist a locally compact space $X$ and a map $x \mapsto f_x$ of $E$ into $\mathcal{R}(X)$ which is an algebraic and order isomorphism onto its image and a homeomorphism. Moreover, $\mathcal{R}(X)$ is complete; hence the image of $E$ is closed in $\mathcal{R}(X)$ if and only if $E$ is complete. If $E$ is metrizable, then $X$ can be chosen to be countable at infinity.
