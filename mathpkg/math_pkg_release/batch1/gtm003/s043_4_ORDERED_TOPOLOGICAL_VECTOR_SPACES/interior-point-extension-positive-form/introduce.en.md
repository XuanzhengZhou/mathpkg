This corollary provides a clean sufficient condition for extending positive linear forms: if the subspace contains an interior point of the positive cone, then any positive linear form on the subspace extends to a continuous positive linear form on the whole space. The proof follows from the Bauer-Namioka theorem by choosing a suitable convex neighborhood of zero such that the interior point condition yields the required boundedness.
