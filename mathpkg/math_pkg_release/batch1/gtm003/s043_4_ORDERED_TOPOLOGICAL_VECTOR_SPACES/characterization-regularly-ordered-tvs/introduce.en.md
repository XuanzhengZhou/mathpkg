This theorem provides equivalent characterizations of regularly ordered topological vector spaces. Regular order, defined via the duality between the real underlying space and the space of order-bounded linear forms, is shown to be equivalent to the sequential closedness of the positive cone under some Hausdorff locally convex topology, as well as to the normality of the cone under some Hausdorff locally convex topology. These equivalences form the backbone for studying the interplay between topological and order structures.
