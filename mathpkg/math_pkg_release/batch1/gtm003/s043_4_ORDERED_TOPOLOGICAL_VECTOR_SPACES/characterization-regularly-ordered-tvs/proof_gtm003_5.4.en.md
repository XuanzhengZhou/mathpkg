(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b): It suffices to show that the intersection of $C$ with every finite dimensional subspace $M$ is closed (Chapter II, Exercise 7), and this is immediate from the preceding lemma, since the order of $L$ is Archimedean, and hence the canonical order of each subspace $M \subset L$ is Archimedean.

(b) $\Rightarrow$ (c): If $\mathfrak{T}$ is a Hausdorff l.c. topology under which $C$ is sequentially closed, then, clearly, $L$ is Archimedean ordered. Moreover, since $L^+$ separates points in $L$, the canonical bilinear form on $L \times L^*$ places $L$ and $L^+ = C^* - C^*$ in duality, and by (3.3) $C$ is normal for the Hausdorff l.c. topology $\sigma(L, L^+)$.

(c) $\Rightarrow$ (a): It suffices to show that $L^+$ separates points in $L$. If $\mathfrak{T}$ is a Hausdorff l.c. topology for which $C$ is normal, then $(L, \mathfrak{T})' = C' - C'$ by (3.3); hence $C' - C'$, and a fortiori $L^+ = C^* - C^*$, separates points in $L$. This completes the proof.
