An ordered topological vector space combines the structures of a topological vector space and an ordered vector space, linked by the requirement that the positive cone be closed. This axiom (LTO) is fundamental to the theory of ordered topological vector spaces developed in Chapter V of Schaefer's monograph. The closedness of the positive cone ensures that the order structure is compatible with the topology, enabling the study of convergence of monotone nets and the interplay between order and topological dualities.
