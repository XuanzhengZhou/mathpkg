Canonical orderings are the natural induced order structures on subspaces, quotients, products, and function spaces constructed from ordered vector spaces. For subspaces, the positive cone is simply the intersection with the ambient cone. For products, the order is defined componentwise. Quotients receive an ordering only when the canonical image of the positive cone remains proper.
