When a vector lattice $E$ is evaluated into a solid sublattice $G$ of its order dual $E^+$, the canonical evaluation map preserves lattice operations. Specifically, the positive part $\tilde{x}^+$ computed in $G^+$ coincides with the evaluation of $x^+ \in E$. However, the canonical image of $E$ in $G^+$ need not be order complete even if $E$ itself is order complete.
