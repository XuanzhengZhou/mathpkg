A subset $A$ of a vector lattice $E$ is called \textbf{solid} if $x \in A$ and $|y| \leq |x|$, with $y \in E$, imply that $y \in A$.

A solid subspace of $E$ is necessarily a sublattice of $E$. For example, the algebraic direct sum $\bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha$ of a family $\{E_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$ of vector lattices is a solid subspace of $\prod_\alpha E_\alpha$ (under the canonical ordering of the product).
