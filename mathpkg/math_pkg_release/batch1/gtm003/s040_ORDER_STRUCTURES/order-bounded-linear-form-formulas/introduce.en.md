From the construction of $f^+ = \sup(f, 0)$ in the proof of Theorem 1.4, one obtains explicit formulas for the lattice operations on order bounded linear forms. The supremum, infimum, and absolute value of such forms on a vector lattice $E$ can be expressed as supremum/infimum over certain decompositions of $|x|$. These formulas also yield a useful characterization: two positive linear forms are disjoint iff their values can be made arbitrarily small on a suitable decomposition of each positive element.
