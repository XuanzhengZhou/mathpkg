Let $\beta$ be an ordinal and consider the space $R_0^\beta$ of all functions $f: \beta \to R$ with finite support, ordered lexicographically by the finest non-zero coordinate. Formally, define the subset $H$ of $R_0^\beta$ by the property: ``if there exists a smallest ordinal $\alpha < \beta$ such that $f(\alpha) \neq 0$, then $f(\alpha) > 0$''. Then $H$ is a proper cone in $R_0^\beta$, and the order determined by $H$ is called the \textbf{lexicographical order} of $R_0^\beta$.

The lexicographical order of $R_0^\beta$ is a total ordering (since $R_0^\beta = H \cup -H$) and thus $R_0^\beta$ is a vector lattice under this order. However, if $\beta > 1$, this order is not Archimedean (hence not regular), and not order complete. Moreover, up to a positive scalar factor, $f \mapsto f(0)$ is the only non-trivial positive linear form.
