A \textbf{vector lattice} (or \textbf{Riesz space}) is an ordered vector space $E$ over $R$ such that for each pair $(x, y) \in E \times E$, $\sup(x, y)$ and $\inf(x, y)$ exist. This implies, in particular, that $E$ is directed under the order relation $\leq$ (equivalently, that the positive cone $C$ of $E$ is generating).

For each $x \in E$, we define the \textbf{absolute value} $|x|$ by
$$|x| = \sup(x, -x).$$

Two elements $x, y$ of a vector lattice $E$ are \textbf{disjoint} if $\inf(|x|, |y|) = 0$, denoted by $x \perp y$. Two subsets $A \subset E$ and $B \subset E$ are \textbf{lattice disjoint} if $x \in A$, $y \in B$ implies $\inf(|x|, |y|) = 0$. If $A$ is a subset of $E$, $A^\perp$ denotes the set of all $y \in E$ such that $y$ is disjoint from each element of $A$.
