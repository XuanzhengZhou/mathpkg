Let $L$ be a vector space over $R$ endowed with an order relation $\leq$ satisfying the axioms $(LO)_1$ and $(LO)_2$ of an ordered vector space. The subset
$$C = \{x \in L : x \geq 0\}$$
is a convex cone of vertex $0$ satisfying $C \cap -C = \{0\}$. A cone in $L$ with these properties is called a \textbf{proper cone} in $L$. The elements $x \in C$ are called \textbf{positive}, and $C$ is called the \textbf{positive cone} of the ordered vector space $L$.

Conversely, if $L$ is any vector space over $R$, a proper cone $H \subset L$ is characterized by the properties
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $H + H \subset H$,
\item[(ii)] $\lambda H \subset H$ for all $\lambda > 0$,
\item[(iii)] $H \cap -H = \{0\}$.
\end{itemize}
Each proper cone $H \subset L$ defines, via ``$x \leq y$ if $y - x \in H$'', an order under which $L$ is an ordered vector space with positive cone $H$. Thus there is a biunivocal correspondence between proper cones in $L$ and orderings of $L$ satisfying $(LO)_1$ and $(LO)_2$.
