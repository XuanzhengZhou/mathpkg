By Theorem 1.2, any $0$-neighborhood contains a circled $0$-neighborhood. By (1.1)(v), the closure of a circled set is circled. By Theorem 1.3, closed $0$-neighborhoods form a base. Therefore, starting from any neighborhood base $\mathfrak{U}$ of $0$, replacing each $U \in \mathfrak{U}$ with its closed, circled hull yields a family of closed, circled $0$-neighborhoods which still forms a neighborhood base at $0$.
