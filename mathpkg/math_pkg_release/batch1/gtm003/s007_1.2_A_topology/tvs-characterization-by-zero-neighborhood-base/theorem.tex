A topology $\mathfrak{T}$ on a vector space $L$ over $K$ satisfies the axioms $(LT)_1$ and $(LT)_2$ if and only if $\mathfrak{T}$ is translation-invariant and possesses a $0$-neighborhood base $\mathfrak{V}$ with the following properties:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] For each $V \in \mathfrak{V}$, there exists $U \in \mathfrak{V}$ such that $U + U \subset V$.
\item[(b)] Every $V \in \mathfrak{V}$ is radial and circled.
\item[(c)] There exists $\lambda \in K$, $0 < |\lambda| < 1$, such that $V \in \mathfrak{V}$ implies $\lambda V \in \mathfrak{V}$.
\end{enumerate}

If $K$ is an Archimedean valuated field, condition (c) is dispensable (which is, in particular, the case if $K = \mathbb{R}$ or $K = \mathbb{C}$).
