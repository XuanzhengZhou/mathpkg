In the context of topological vector spaces, a "subspace" always means a vector subspace equipped with the induced topology. This convention ensures that every subspace of a t.v.s. is itself a t.v.s., since the restrictions of continuous addition and scalar multiplication to the subspace remain continuous in the subspace topology. Moreover, if the ambient space is Hausdorff (separated), so is every subspace.
