A topological vector space is Hausdorff precisely when the intersection of all $0$-neighborhoods is the singleton $\{0\}$. Equivalently, every non-zero vector can be separated from $0$ by a neighborhood. This criterion follows from the fact that a t.v.s. is uniformisable with a translation-invariant uniformity (Proposition 1.4), and a uniform space is separated if and only if the intersection of all entourages is the diagonal. Theorem 1.3 provides the direct proof that the $0$-neighborhood intersection condition implies every singleton is closed.
