A vector subspace (or linear subspace) of a vector space $L$ over a field $K$ is a non-empty subset closed under vector addition and scalar multiplication. This is the purely algebraic notion of subspace. In the context of topological vector spaces, the term "subspace" is understood to mean a vector subspace endowed with the induced topology, which is itself a t.v.s.
