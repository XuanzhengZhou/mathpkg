A translation-invariant topology on a vector space is one in which all translation maps are homeomorphisms. This property is fundamental to topological vector spaces, since continuity of addition implies that every translation is continuous, and its inverse (translation by $-x_0$) is also continuous. In a translation-invariant topology, the entire topological structure is determined by the neighborhood system at the origin.
