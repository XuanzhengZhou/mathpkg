A continuous linear map $u: E \to F$ between locally convex spaces is called nearly open if the image of each 0-neighborhood in $E$ is dense in some 0-neighborhood in $F$. This concept plays a key role in Ptak's open mapping theorem. When $F$ is barreled, the condition that $u(U)$ is weakly dense in some 0-neighborhood $V \subset F$ for each convex, circled 0-neighborhood $U$ implies that $u$ is nearly open.
