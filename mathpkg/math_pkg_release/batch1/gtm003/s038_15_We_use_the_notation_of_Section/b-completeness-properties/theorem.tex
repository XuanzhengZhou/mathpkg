(a) Let $E$ be an infinite-dimensional vector space such that there exists a topology $\mathfrak{T}_1$ under which $E$ is an (F)-space, and denote by $\mathfrak{T}_0$ the finest l.c. topology on $E$. Then $(E, \mathfrak{T}_0)$ is complete but not $B_r$-complete.

(b) Products and l.c. direct sums of B-complete spaces are, in general, not B-complete.

(c) The following assertions are equivalent: (i) Every $B_r$-complete space is B-complete. (ii) Every separated quotient of a $B_r$-complete space is $B_r$-complete.

(d) Let $X$ be a completely regular topological space, $R(X)$ the space of real-valued continuous functions on $X$ under the topology of compact convergence. The following propositions are equivalent: (i) $R(X)$ is $B_r$-complete. (ii) If $Y$ is a dense subset of $X$ such that $Y \cap C$ is compact whenever $C$ is compact, then $Y = X$.
