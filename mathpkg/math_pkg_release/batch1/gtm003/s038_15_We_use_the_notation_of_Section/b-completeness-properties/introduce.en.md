B-completeness and $B_r$-completeness (Ptak completeness) are important completeness notions for locally convex spaces, intimately connected to the open mapping theorem. This exercise collects several key properties: counterexamples showing B-completeness is not preserved under products or direct sums, the equivalence between $B_r$-completeness implying B-completeness and $B_r$-completeness being preserved by separated quotients, and a characterization of $B_r$-completeness for the space $R(X)$ of continuous functions under compact convergence.
