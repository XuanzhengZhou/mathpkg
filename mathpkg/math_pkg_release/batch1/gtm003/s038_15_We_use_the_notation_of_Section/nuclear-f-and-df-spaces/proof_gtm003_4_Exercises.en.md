Establish the result through the following steps, $F$ denoting an arbitrary (B)-space:

1. The canonical map $\psi: E \otimes F \rightarrow \mathfrak{B}(E'_\sigma, F'_\sigma)$ is surjective. Observe that each $T \in \mathfrak{B}(E'_\sigma, F'_\sigma)$ corresponds to a separately continuous bilinear form on $E'_\sigma \times F$, and using the nuclearity of $E'_\beta$ one shows that this bilinear form is represented by an element of $E \otimes F$.
