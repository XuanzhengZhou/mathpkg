We use the notation of Section 5. There exist l.c.s. $E$ such that the families $\mathfrak{E}, \mathfrak{C}, \mathfrak{B}, \mathfrak{B}_\sigma$ of subsets of $E'$ are all distinct. (Consider a suitable space $F$ which is not barreled (Chapter II, Exercise 14), and take $E$ to be the space $F'_\sigma$.)
