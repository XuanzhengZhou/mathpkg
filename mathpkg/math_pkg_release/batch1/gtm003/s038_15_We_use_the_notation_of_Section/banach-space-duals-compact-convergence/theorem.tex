Let $E, F$ be Banach spaces with respective strong duals $E', F'$.

(a) The dual of $\mathcal{L}_c(E, F)$ (topology of compact convergence) can be identified with a quotient of $E' \otimes F'$.

(b) If $E$ is nuclear and complete, then $E$ is an (M)-space, namely the strong dual of the (F)- and (M)-space $E'_\tau$.

(c) Suppose that $E$ is nuclear. The strong dual of $E \otimes F$ can be identified with $E'_\beta \otimes F'_\beta$, and the strong bidual with $E''_\beta \otimes F''_\beta$.

(d) Suppose that $E, F$ are strong duals of (F)-spaces and that $E$ is nuclear. Then $\mathfrak{B}(E, F) = \mathfrak{B}(E, F)$.
