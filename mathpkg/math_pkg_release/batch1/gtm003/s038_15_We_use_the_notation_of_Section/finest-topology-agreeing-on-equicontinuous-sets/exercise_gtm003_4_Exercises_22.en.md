Let $E$ be a l.c.s., let $E'$ be its dual, and let $\mathfrak{T}_f$ be the finest topology on $E'$ that agrees with $\sigma(E', E)$ on every equicontinuous subset of $E'$.

(a) $\mathfrak{T}_f$ is a translation-invariant topology possessing a 0-neighborhood base of radial and circled sets.

(b) In general, $\mathfrak{T}_f$ fails to be locally convex (Collins [1]). (Consider an infinite-dimensional space $E$ on which there exists a topology $\mathfrak{T}_1$ such that $(E, \mathfrak{T}_1)$ is an (F)-space; denote by $\mathfrak{T}_0$ the finest l.c. topology on $E$ (Chapter II, Exercise 7). Then $(E, \mathfrak{T}_1)' = E' \neq E^*$ (Exercise 21). Consider $\mathfrak{T}_f$ on $E^* = (E, \mathfrak{T}_0)'$; then $E'$ is $\mathfrak{T}_f$-closed in $E^*$. If $\mathfrak{T}_f$ were locally convex, it would be consistent with $\langle E, E^* \rangle$.)
