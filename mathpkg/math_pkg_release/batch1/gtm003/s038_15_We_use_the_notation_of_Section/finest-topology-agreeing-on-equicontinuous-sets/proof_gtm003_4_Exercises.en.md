(b) Consider an infinite-dimensional space $E$ on which there exists a topology $\mathfrak{T}_1$ such that $(E, \mathfrak{T}_1)$ is an (F)-space; denote by $\mathfrak{T}_0$ the finest l.c. topology on $E$ (Chapter II, Exercise 7). Then $(E, \mathfrak{T}_1)' = E' \neq E^*$ (Exercise 21). Consider $\mathfrak{T}_f$ on $E^* = (E, \mathfrak{T}_0)'$; then $E'$ is $\mathfrak{T}_f$-closed in $E^*$. If $\mathfrak{T}_f$ were locally convex, it would be consistent with $\langle E, E^* \rangle$, contradicting the properties of the constructions.
