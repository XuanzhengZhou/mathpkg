For a locally convex space $E$ with dual $E'$, one can define $\mathfrak{T}_f$ as the finest topology on $E'$ coinciding with the weak topology $\sigma(E', E)$ on each equicontinuous subset. This topology is always translation-invariant with a radial and circled 0-neighborhood base, but it need not be locally convex. The failure of local convexity was demonstrated by Collins, using the finest locally convex topology on an infinite-dimensional (F)-space as a counterexample.
