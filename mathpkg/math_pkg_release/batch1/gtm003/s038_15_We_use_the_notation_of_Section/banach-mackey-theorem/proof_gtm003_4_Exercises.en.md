Observe that for each barrel $D \subset E$, $D \cap E_B$ is a barrel in $E_B$, hence a 0-neighborhood in $E_B$ since $E_B$ is complete and thus barreled. Therefore $B$ absorbs every barrel, which means $B$ is bounded for $\beta(E, E')$.

(Alternative argument: $B$ is strongly precompact (Chapter I, Exercise 5), hence relatively compact since $E'_\beta$ is complete. From (6.2), Corollary 3, conclude that $E = E''$, hence that $E$ and $E'_\beta$ are reflexive; finally use (5.9).)
