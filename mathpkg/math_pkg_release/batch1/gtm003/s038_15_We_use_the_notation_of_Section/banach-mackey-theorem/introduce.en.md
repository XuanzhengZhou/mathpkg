The Banach-Mackey Theorem relates boundedness with respect to the strong topology $\beta(E, E')$ to completeness of the normed space $E_B$ generated by a bounded, convex, circled subset $B$. If $E_B$ is complete, then $B$ is strongly bounded. This theorem bridges the gap between the bornological and topological properties of locally convex spaces.
