When the Mackey topology $\tau(E, E')$ is the finest locally convex topology on a space $E$, strong finiteness consequences follow. In particular, if a metrizable (or normable) locally convex space has a weakly complete dual, the space must be finite-dimensional. This result connects the topological structure of a space (via its dual's completeness) with its algebraic dimension.
