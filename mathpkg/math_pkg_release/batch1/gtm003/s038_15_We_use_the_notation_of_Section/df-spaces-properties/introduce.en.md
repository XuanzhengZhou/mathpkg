(DF)-spaces (dual-Fréchet spaces) form an important class of locally convex spaces. This statement collects their stability properties: they are preserved under countable locally convex direct sums and sequential inductive limits, but not under arbitrary products or passage to closed subspaces. For reflexive (DF)-spaces, the strong dual of an inductive limit identifies with the projective limit of the strong duals. Furthermore, there exist complete (DF)-spaces that are not strong duals of metrizable spaces, showing the class is genuinely larger.
