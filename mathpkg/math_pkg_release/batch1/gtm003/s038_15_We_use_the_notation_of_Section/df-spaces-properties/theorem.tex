The l.c. direct sum of a sequence of (DF)-spaces is a (DF)-space, and an inductive limit of a sequence of (DF)-spaces is a (DF)-space. An infinite product of (DF)-spaces (each not reduced to $\{0\}$) is not a (DF)-space; a closed subspace of a (DF)-space is not necessarily a (DF)-space. If $E = \varinjlim h_{nm} E_m$ is an inductive limit of a sequence of reflexive (DF)-spaces, then the strong dual of $E$ can be identified with the projective limit $\varprojlim g_{mn} E'_n$ of the strong duals $E'_n$ with respect to the adjoint maps $g_{mn} = h'_{nm}$. There exist complete (DF)-spaces not isomorphic with the strong dual of a metrizable l.c.s.
