Every Hilbert space $H$ admits a weakly complete convex subset $C$ such that the entire space can be expressed as the difference set $H = C - C$. A canonical construction identifies $H$ with $\ell^2(A)$ over $\mathbb{R}$ and takes $C = \{x \in H : \xi_\alpha \geq 0 \text{ for all } \alpha \in A\}$, the non-negative orthant, which is weakly complete and satisfies the required property.
