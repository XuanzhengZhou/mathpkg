(c) In each Hilbert space $H$, there exists a weakly complete convex set $C$ such that $H = C - C$. (Suppose $H$ to be a space $\ell^2(A)$ over $\mathbb{R}$ (Chapter II, Section 2, Example 5), and take $C = \{x \in H : \xi_\alpha \geq 0 \text{ for all } \alpha \in A\}$.)
