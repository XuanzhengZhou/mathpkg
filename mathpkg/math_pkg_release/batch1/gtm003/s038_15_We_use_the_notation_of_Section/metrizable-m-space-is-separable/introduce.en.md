Due to Dieudonné, every metrizable (M)-space is necessarily separable. An (M)-space (Montel space) is a barreled locally convex space in which every bounded set is relatively compact. The proof proceeds by contradiction: assuming non-separability, one constructs a bounded sequence whose image under a canonical map contains no Cauchy subsequence, contradicting the relative compactness of bounded sets.
