A linear manifold (or affine subspace) in a vector space $L$ is a translate of a linear subspace $M$. It generalizes the notion of an affine plane not necessarily passing through the origin. The subspace $M$ is uniquely determined by the affine subspace, while the translation vector $x_0$ is determined only modulo $M$. Two linear manifolds are parallel when the subspace of one contains the subspace of the other. The dimension of a linear manifold is simply the dimension of its corresponding subspace.
