In a topological vector space over a complete field, the sum of a closed subspace and a finite-dimensional subspace is always closed. This lemma is a crucial technical tool: it guarantees that adding a finite-dimensional piece to a closed subspace does not destroy closedness. The proof uses the quotient map onto $L/M$, the finiteness of $\dim(N)$ to deduce that its image is closed in the Hausdorff quotient, and the continuity of the natural map to pull back closedness.
