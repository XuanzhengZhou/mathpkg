A topological vector space $L$ may be the algebraic direct sum of two subspaces $M$ and $N$, meaning every element of $L$ can be uniquely expressed as a sum of an element from $M$ and an element from $N$. The topological direct sum further requires that the projections onto each summand are continuous. This theorem characterizes when the algebraic direct sum is also a topological direct sum: it happens precisely when the natural map from the quotient $L/M$ to the complementary subspace $N$ (sending each coset to its unique representative in $N$) is an isomorphism of topological vector spaces.
