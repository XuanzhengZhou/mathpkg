Denote by $u$ the projection of $L$ onto $N$ vanishing on $M$, and by $\phi$ the natural map of $L$ onto $L/M$. Then $u = v \circ \phi$.

Let $L = M \oplus N$. Since $\phi$ is open and $u$ is continuous, $v$ is continuous; since $\phi$ is continuous and $u$ is open, $v$ is open. Conversely, if $v$ is an isomorphism then $v$ is continuous; hence $u = v \circ \phi$ is continuous, which implies $L = M \oplus N$.
