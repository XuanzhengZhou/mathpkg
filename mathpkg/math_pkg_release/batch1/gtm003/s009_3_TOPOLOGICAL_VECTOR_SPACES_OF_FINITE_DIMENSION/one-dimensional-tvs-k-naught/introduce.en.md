The field $K$ of scalars (typically $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$ equipped with its usual absolute value topology) can be regarded as a one-dimensional vector space over itself. This canonical one-dimensional topological vector space is denoted by $K_0$ and serves as the fundamental building block for all finite-dimensional Hausdorff topological vector spaces over $K$: every such space of dimension $n$ is isomorphic to the product $K_0^n$.
