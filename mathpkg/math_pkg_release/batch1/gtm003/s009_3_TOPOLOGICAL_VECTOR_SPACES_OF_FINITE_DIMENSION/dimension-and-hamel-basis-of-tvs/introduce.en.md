In the theory of topological vector spaces, the dimension of a t.v.s. is defined purely algebraically as the cardinality of a maximal linearly independent subset, called a Hamel basis. Unlike in Banach space theory where Schauder bases involve convergence of infinite series, a Hamel basis uses only finite linear combinations. This algebraic notion of dimension is fundamental for classifying finite-dimensional topological vector spaces, where the topology is uniquely determined by the field.
