The algebraic dual $L^*$ of a vector space $L$ is the space of all linear forms (linear functionals) from $L$ to the scalar field $K$. Unlike the topological (continuous) dual $L'$, the algebraic dual contains all linear functionals regardless of continuity. When $L$ has additional structure (e.g., is a topological vector space), the topological dual is a subspace of the algebraic dual. The algebraic dual plays a key role in describing hyperplanes: every non-zero linear form defines a homogeneous hyperplane as its kernel.
