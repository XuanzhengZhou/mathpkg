Let $K$ be complete. If $L \neq \{0\}$ is a Hausdorff topological vector space of finite dimension $n$ over $K$, then $L$ is isomorphic with $K_0^n$; more precisely, every basis $\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ of $L$ determines an isomorphism
$$(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n) \mapsto \lambda_1 x_1 + \cdots + \lambda_n x_n$$
of $K_0^n$ onto $L$, and every isomorphism of $K_0^n$ onto $L$ is of this form. Equivalently, if $K$ is a complete valuated field, then the product topology on $K_0^n$ is the only Hausdorff topology satisfying $(LT)_1$ and $(LT)_2$.
