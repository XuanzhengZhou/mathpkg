The linear maps $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F'_\sigma)$ and $u' \in \mathcal{L}(F, E'_\sigma)$ originating from a bilinear form $v \in \mathcal{J}(E, F)$ (via the canonical isomorphism) are called \textbf{integral linear maps}. An integral map $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F'_\sigma)$ admits the representation
$$u(x) = \int_{S \times T} \langle x, x' \rangle y' \, d\mu(x', y')$$
for suitable weakly closed, equicontinuous sets $S \subset E'$, $T \subset F'$, where $\mu$ is a Radon measure on $S \times T$. The integral $\int f \, d\mu$ is called the \textbf{weak integral}.
