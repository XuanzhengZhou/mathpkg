For locally convex spaces $E$ and $F$, separately continuous bilinear forms on $E 	imes F$ correspond algebraically to continuous linear maps from one space into the weak dual of the other. This canonical identification allows one to freely translate between spaces of bilinear forms and spaces of linear maps, a crucial tool in the study of tensor products and nuclear spaces. The topology on $\mathcal{B}(E, F)$ also carries over to the corresponding subspace of $\mathcal{L}(E, F'_\sigma)$.
