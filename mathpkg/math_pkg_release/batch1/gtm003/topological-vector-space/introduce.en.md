A topological vector space (t.v.s.) is a vector space equipped with a topology that makes addition and scalar multiplication jointly continuous. This is the fundamental object of study in functional analysis and the central concept of this book. Every t.v.s. is a commutative topological group because $(x, y) \to x - y$ is continuous. The pair $(L, \mathfrak{T})$ may also be denoted $L(\mathfrak{T})$ or simply $L$ when the topology is clear from context.
