A directed family \(\mathfrak{S}\) of subsets of a set \(E\) is said to cover \(E\) if every point of \(E\) belongs to at least one member of \(\mathfrak{S}\). This condition ensures that the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology can distinguish points. The four standard \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topologies on \(\mathcal{L}(E,F)\) (simple convergence, compact convex circled convergence, precompact convergence, and bounded convergence) all arise from covering families.
