Since every Banach space is barreled, the general theorem on pointwise limits of continuous linear maps applies directly: a pointwise convergent sequence of operators between Banach spaces has a continuous limit. This corollary of the uniform boundedness principle is used extensively in operator theory, particularly when constructing operators via approximation schemes or in the spectral theorem.
