The saturated hull \(\overline{\mathfrak{S}}\) of a family \(\mathfrak{S}\) of bounded subsets is the smallest saturated family containing \(\mathfrak{S}\). It is obtained by intersecting all saturated families that contain \(\mathfrak{S}\). An important fact is that for any family \(\mathfrak{S}\) of bounded subsets of a locally convex space \(E\), the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology and the \(\overline{\mathfrak{S}}\)-topology are identical on \(\mathcal{L}(E,F)\), so one may always replace \(\mathfrak{S}\) by its saturated hull without changing the topology.
