The Banach-Steinhaus theorem (also known as the uniform boundedness principle) is one of the cornerstones of functional analysis. It states that pointwise boundedness of a family of continuous linear maps implies uniform equicontinuity, provided the domain space satisfies a suitable completeness or barreledness condition. This result is fundamental for establishing continuity of limit operators and for the theory of distribution spaces.
