A saturated family of bounded subsets of a locally convex space is closed under three natural operations: taking subsets, scalar multiples, and the closed convex circled hull of finite unions. The family of all bounded subsets is always saturated, and the intersection of saturated families remains saturated. This notion is used to show that the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology is unchanged when replacing a family \(\mathfrak{S}\) by its saturated hull.
