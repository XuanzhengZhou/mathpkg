Let \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\) and let \(T\) be bounded in \(F\). Let \(W\) be a \(0\)-neighborhood in \(G\). By \(\mathfrak{S}\)-hypocontinuity, there exists a \(0\)-neighborhood \(V\) in \(F\) such that \(f(S \times V) \subset W\). Since \(T\) is bounded, there exists \(\lambda > 0\) with \(T \subset \lambda V\). Then by bilinearity,
\[
f(S \times T) \subset f(S \times \lambda V) = \lambda f(S \times V) \subset \lambda W.
\]
Since \(W\) was arbitrary, \(f(S \times T)\) is bounded in \(G\).
