A separately continuous bilinear map on a product of locally convex spaces, with one factor barreled, is automatically hypocontinuous with respect to the bounded sets of the other factor. This is an important consequence of the Banach-Steinhaus theorem (uniform boundedness principle). The result connects separate continuity (continuity in each variable individually) with a stronger form of joint continuity on bounded sets, providing a key tool in the theory of bilinear forms.
