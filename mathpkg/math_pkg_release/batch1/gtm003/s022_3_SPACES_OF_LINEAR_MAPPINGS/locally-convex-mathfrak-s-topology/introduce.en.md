When the target space \(F\) is locally convex, the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology on a space of continuous functions inherits local convexity, because the basic neighborhoods \(M(S,V)\) are convex whenever \(V\) is convex. The Hausdorff separation property follows from the density of the union of \(\mathfrak{S}\) and the continuity of the functions: a non-zero continuous function must take a non-zero value at some point in the dense union.
