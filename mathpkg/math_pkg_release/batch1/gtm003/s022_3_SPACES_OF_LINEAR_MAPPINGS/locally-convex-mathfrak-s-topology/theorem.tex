Let \(F\) be a locally convex space, let \(T\) be a topological space, and let \(\mathfrak{S}\) be a family of subsets of \(T\) whose union is dense in \(T\). If \(G\) is a subspace of \(F^T\) whose elements are continuous on \(T\) and bounded on each \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\), then \(G\) is a locally convex space under the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology.
