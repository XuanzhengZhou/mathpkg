An $\mathfrak{S}$-hypocontinuous bilinear map is separately continuous and, additionally, uniformly continuous on sets of the form $S \times V$ where $S$ belongs to a prescribed family $\mathfrak{S}$ of bounded sets and $V$ is a neighborhood of zero. This notion generalizes equicontinuity to bilinear maps and provides the right framework for the duality between topological tensor products and spaces of bilinear maps. When $\mathfrak{S}$ is the family of all bounded sets, one speaks simply of a hypocontinuous bilinear map.
