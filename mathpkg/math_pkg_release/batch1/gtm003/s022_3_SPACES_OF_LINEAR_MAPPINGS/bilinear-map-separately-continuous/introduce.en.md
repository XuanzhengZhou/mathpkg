A bilinear map is separately continuous if it is continuous in each variable when the other is held fixed. Separate continuity is a weaker condition than joint continuity (continuity on the product space). In general, separate continuity does not imply joint continuity for bilinear maps on infinite-dimensional spaces, but under additional hypotheses (such as barreledness), hypocontinuity can be deduced, providing an intermediate notion between the two.
