Equicontinuous sets of linear maps between topological vector spaces carry a natural uniform structure. When the domain is separable and the codomain metrizable, the topology of precompact convergence on equicontinuous sets is metrizable and coincides with the topology of simple convergence. This result is fundamental for compactness criteria (such as Alaoglu's theorem) and for the study of the strong dual topology.
