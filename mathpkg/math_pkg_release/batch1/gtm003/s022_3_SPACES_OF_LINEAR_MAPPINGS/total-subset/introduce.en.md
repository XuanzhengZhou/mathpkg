A subset \(A\) of a topological vector space is called total if its linear span is dense in the space. This property generalises the notion of a spanning set to the topological setting: a total subset need not generate the whole space algebraically, but every element of the space can be approximated by finite linear combinations of elements from \(A\). Totality is a crucial condition in the study of \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topologies on spaces of linear mappings.
