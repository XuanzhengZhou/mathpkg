Let \(F\) be a topological vector space, let \(T\) be a set, and let \(\mathfrak{S}\) be a family of subsets of \(T\) directed under inclusion. Let \(G\) be a subspace of \(F^T\) such that for all \(f \in G\) and \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\), the image \(f(S)\) is bounded in \(F\). Then, if the linear hull of the union of all members of \(\mathfrak{S}\) is dense in \(G\) for the simple convergence topology, \(G\) is a topological vector space under the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology (and the simple convergence topology is coarser).
