If \(H\) is simply bounded in \(\mathcal{L}(E, F)\) and \(V\) is a closed, convex, circled \(0\)-neighborhood in \(F\), then \(D = \bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)\) is a closed, convex, circled subset of \(E\). Since \(H\) is simply bounded, for each \(x \in E\) the set \(\{u(x) : u \in H\}\) is bounded in \(F\), so there exists \(\lambda > 0\) with \(u(x) \in \lambda V\) for all \(u \in H\), i.e., \(x \in \lambda D\). Thus \(D\) is radial (absorbing) and hence a barrel. By (II, 8.5), every barrel in a locally convex space absorbs every convex, circled, bounded, complete subset. Therefore \(D\) absorbs every \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\), which by Proposition 3.3(b) implies that \(H\) is bounded for the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology.
