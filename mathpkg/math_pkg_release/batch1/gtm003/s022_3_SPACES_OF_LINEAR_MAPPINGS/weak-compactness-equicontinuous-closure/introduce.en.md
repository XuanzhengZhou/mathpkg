This is a generalisation of Alaoglu's theorem. In the special case where \(H \subset E'\) is equicontinuous, its pointwise closure in \(\mathbb{K}^E\) is equicontinuous (by Proposition 4.3), contained in \(E'\), and compact by Tychonoff's theorem. Hence the weak closure of an equicontinuous set in \(E'\) is weakly compact.
