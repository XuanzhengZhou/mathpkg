The pointwise limit of a net of continuous linear maps from a barreled space to a locally convex space is automatically continuous. This is a consequence of the Banach-Steinhaus theorem (uniform boundedness principle). Moreover, the convergence is uniform on precompact sets. This result ensures that spaces of continuous linear maps on barreled spaces are complete under appropriate topologies, which is essential for functional analysis.
