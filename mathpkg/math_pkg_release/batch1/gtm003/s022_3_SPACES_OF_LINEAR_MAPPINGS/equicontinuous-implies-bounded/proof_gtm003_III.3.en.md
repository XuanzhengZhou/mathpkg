Condition (4.1)(b) states that for each \(0\)-neighborhood \(V\) in \(F\), \(\bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)\) is a \(0\)-neighborhood in \(E\). A fortiori, this intersection absorbs every bounded subset of \(E\), and in particular every \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\). Thus condition (3.3)(b) is satisfied, which by Proposition 3.3 implies that \(H\) is bounded for the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology.
