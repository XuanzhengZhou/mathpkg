The \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology on a space \(F^T\) of functions is a translation-invariant topology defined by uniform convergence on each member of a directed family \(\mathfrak{S}\) of subsets of the domain \(T\). A typical basic \(0\)-neighborhood consists of all functions mapping a given set \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\) into a given \(0\)-neighborhood \(V\) in the target space \(F\). This construction generalises the classical topologies of pointwise convergence, compact convergence, and bounded convergence by varying the choice of \(\mathfrak{S}\).
