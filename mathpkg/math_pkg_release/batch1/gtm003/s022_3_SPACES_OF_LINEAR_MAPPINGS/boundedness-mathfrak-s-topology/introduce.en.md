Proposition 3.3 gives three equivalent characterizations of boundedness for the \(\mathfrak{S}\)-topology on the space \(\mathcal{L}(E,F)\) of continuous linear mappings. The conditions relate \(\mathfrak{S}\)-boundedness of a set \(H\) of linear maps to absorption properties of the intersection of inverse images of zero-neighborhoods (condition b) and to the boundedness of the union of images of each \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\) (condition c). This result is fundamental for the study of bounded and equicontinuous sets.
