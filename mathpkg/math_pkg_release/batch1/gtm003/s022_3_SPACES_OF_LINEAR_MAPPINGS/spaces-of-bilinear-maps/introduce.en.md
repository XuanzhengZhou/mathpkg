The space $\mathcal{B}(E, F; G)$ of continuous bilinear maps between locally convex spaces carries several natural topologies, depending on which families of bounded sets are used to define uniform convergence. Common choices include the topology of biequicontinuous convergence and the topology of uniform convergence on products of bounded sets. These topologies are fundamental for the theory of topological tensor products and nuclear spaces.
