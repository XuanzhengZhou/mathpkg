Let \(H\) be a subset of \(\mathcal{L}(E, F)\). The following assertions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \(H\) is equicontinuous.
\item[(b)] For each \(0\)-neighborhood \(V\) in \(F\), \(\bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)\) is a \(0\)-neighborhood in \(E\).
\item[(c)] For each \(0\)-neighborhood \(V\) in \(F\), there exists a \(0\)-neighborhood \(U\) in \(E\) such that \(\bigcup_{u \in H} u(U) \subset V\).
\end{enumerate}
