Consider the order of the complex space $L = \mathbb{C}^{\mathbb{N}}$ defined by the cone $C$, where $x = (x_n) \in C$ if and only if either $x = 0$, or $\operatorname{Re} x_n \geq 0$ ($n \in \mathbb{N}$) and $\operatorname{Im} x_1 > 0$. Show that the dual cone $C^* \subset L^*$ separates points in $L$, but there exists no Hausdorff l.c. topology on $L$ for which $C$ is normal.
