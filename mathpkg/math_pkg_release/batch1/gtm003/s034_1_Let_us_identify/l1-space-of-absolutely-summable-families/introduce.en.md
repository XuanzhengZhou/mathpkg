Given an index set $A$ and a locally convex space $E$, the space $l^1[\mathbf{A}, E]$ is the vector space of all absolutely summable $\mathbf{A}$-indexed families in $E$, equipped with the locally convex topology generated by the seminorms $p_U(\mathbf{x}) = \sum_\alpha r_U(x_\alpha)$ for each 0-neighborhood $U$. A companion space of summable (not necessarily absolutely summable) families carries the topology generated by the seminorms $q_U(\mathbf{x}) = \sup_{x' \in U^\circ} \sum_\alpha |\langle x_\alpha, x' \rangle|$. The comparison of these two topologies is central to the characterization of nuclearity via summability.
