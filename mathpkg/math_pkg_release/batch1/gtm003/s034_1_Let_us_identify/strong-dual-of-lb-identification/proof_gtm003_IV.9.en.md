This follows from Theorem 9.9 applied to the pair $(E'_\beta, F)$. Since $E'_\beta$ is nuclear and $E$ is reflexive, $E$ is the strong dual of $E'_\beta$. The strong dual identification then gives $\mathcal{L}_b(E'_\beta, F)' \cong E'_\beta \tilde{\otimes} F'_\beta$. When $F$ is reflexive, the second part follows from Corollary 1.
