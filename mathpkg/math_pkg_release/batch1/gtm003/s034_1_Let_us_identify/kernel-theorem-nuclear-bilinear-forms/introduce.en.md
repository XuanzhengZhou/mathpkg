The kernel theorem, due to Grothendieck, characterizes continuous bilinear forms on the product of a nuclear space $E$ and a locally convex space $F$. Every such bilinear form admits a representation as an absolutely convergent series $\sum \lambda_i \langle x, x'_i \rangle \langle y, y'_i \rangle$ with $(\lambda_i) \in \ell^1$ and equicontinuous sequences $\{x'_i\} \subset E'$, $\{y'_i\} \subset F'$. This is the fundamental structure theorem that makes nuclear spaces the natural setting for Schwartz kernel-type representations.
