\text{If } E \text{ is nuclear and if } F \text{ is locally convex, then every } v \in \mathcal{B}(E, F) \text{ originates from a space } E'_A \tilde{\otimes} F'_B, \text{ where } A, B \text{ are suitable equicontinuous subsets of } E'_\sigma, F'_\sigma, \text{ respectively. Equivalently, every continuous bilinear form } v \text{ on } E \times F \text{ is of the form}

$$(x, y) \mapsto v(x, y) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \lambda_i \langle x, x'_i \rangle \langle y, y'_i \rangle,$$

\text{where } (\lambda_i) \in l^1 \text{ and } \{x'_i\}, \{y'_i\} \text{ are equicontinuous sequences.}
