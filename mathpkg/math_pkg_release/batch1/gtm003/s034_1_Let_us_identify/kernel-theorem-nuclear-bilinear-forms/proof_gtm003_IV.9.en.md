From Part 2 of the proof of (9.4), we obtain the kernel theorem. The theorem states that if $E$ is nuclear and $F$ is locally convex, then every $v \in \mathcal{B}(E, F)$ originates from a space $E'_A \tilde{\otimes} F'_B$, where $A, B$ are suitable equicontinuous subsets of $E'_\sigma, F'_\sigma$, respectively. Equivalently, every continuous bilinear form $v$ on $E \times F$ is of the form

$$(x, y) \mapsto v(x, y) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \lambda_i \langle x, x'_i \rangle \langle y, y'_i \rangle,$$

where $(\lambda_i) \in \ell^1$ and $\{x'_i\}, \{y'_i\}$ are equicontinuous sequences. Note that by (III, 6.5), these sequences can even be supposed to be null sequences in $E'_\sigma$ and $F'_\sigma$, respectively.
