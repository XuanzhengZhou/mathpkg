From (9.6) it follows that the strong duals of the nuclear (F)-spaces enumerated in Chapter III, Section 8, are nuclear; in particular, the spaces $\mathcal{D}'_C$ and $\mathcal{S}'$ are nuclear, and also $\mathcal{H}'$ is nuclear.

The space $\mathcal{D}'$ of distributions, strong dual of $\mathcal{D}$, is nuclear. Since $\mathcal{D}$ (Chapter II, Section 6, Example 2) is the strict inductive limit of a sequence of spaces $\mathcal{D}_{K_m}$, each bounded subset of $\mathcal{D}$ is contained in a suitable space $\mathcal{D}_{K_m}$ by (II, 6.5), so (4.1) $(a) \Rightarrow (b)$ implies that the strong dual $\mathcal{D}'$ is isomorphic with a subspace of $\prod_{m=1}^{\infty} \mathcal{D}'_{K_m}$; since the latter is nuclear, $\mathcal{D}'$ is nuclear.
