The composition of two Hilbert-Schmidt transformations between Hilbert spaces yields a nuclear map. This lemma provides the crucial link between the Hilbert-Schmidt concept and nuclearity: nuclear maps can be characterized as those that factor as a composition of two Hilbert-Schmidt transformations. This factorization property is central to the characterization of nuclear spaces via Hilbert space embeddings.
