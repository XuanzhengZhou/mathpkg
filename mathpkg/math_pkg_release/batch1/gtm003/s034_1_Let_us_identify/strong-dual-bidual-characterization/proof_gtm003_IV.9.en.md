For the proof of the first proposition, we use (9.8) to see that the strong dual of $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ is $\mathcal{B}_b(E, F)$.

For the proof of the second proposition, put $H = E'_\beta$ and $G = F'_\beta$. We use (9.8) again to see that the strong dual of $H \otimes G$ is $\mathcal{B}_b(H, G)$; then, in view of Lemma 3, $\mathcal{B}_b(H, G)$ can be identified with a subspace of $\mathfrak{B}_e(H_\sigma, G''_\sigma)$. Now since $H'_\beta = E$ (note that $E$ is reflexive) is nuclear, it follows from (9.4) that $\mathcal{B}_b(H, G)$ can be identified with a subspace of $H'_\beta \otimes G'_\beta = E \otimes F''$. On the other hand, (III, 6.4) shows that every element of $E \otimes F''$ defines a bilinear form on $H \times G$, which is continuous (cf. (6.5), Corollary 1), whence the proposition follows.

In the following corollaries of (9.9), we do not repeat the assumptions that $E$ be a nuclear (F)-space and $F$ an arbitrary (F)-space; the assertions hold equally when $E, F$ are (DF)-spaces ($E$ being assumed nuclear) (Exercise 32).
