The First Separation Theorem is a geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem. It states that any two convex sets in a topological vector space, one having non-empty interior and the other not intersecting that interior, can be separated by a closed real hyperplane. This result is fundamental in functional analysis, providing the basis for separating convex sets and establishing the existence of supporting hyperplanes. When both sets are open, the separation can be made strict, meaning the sets lie in opposite open half-spaces.
