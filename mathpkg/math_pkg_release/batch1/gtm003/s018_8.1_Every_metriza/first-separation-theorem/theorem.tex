Let $A$ be a convex subset of a t.v.s. $E$ such that $\mathring{A} \neq \emptyset$, and let $B$ be a non-empty convex subset of $E$ not intersecting the interior $\mathring{A}$ of $A$. There exists a closed real hyperplane in $E$ separating $A$ and $B$. Moreover, if $A$ and $B$ are open, the separation can be made strict.
