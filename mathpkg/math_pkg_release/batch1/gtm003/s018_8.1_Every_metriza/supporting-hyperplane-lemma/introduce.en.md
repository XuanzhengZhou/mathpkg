A supporting hyperplane of a convex body touches the body at its boundary but does not cut through its interior. This lemma formalizes that geometric intuition: if a supporting hyperplane $H = \{x: f(x) = \alpha\}$ for a convex body $C \subset \{f \leq \alpha\}$ contained an interior point, then there would exist points of $C$ on the other side of $H$ (with $f(x) > \alpha$), contradicting the support property. The proof uses the fact that interior points allow movement in all directions.
