A bornological space can be represented as the inductive limit of normed spaces, constructed from its bounded sets. For each closed, convex, circled bounded set $B$, one forms the subspace $E_B = \bigcup_{n} nB$ with the gauge norm $p_B$, obtaining a normed space (a Banach space if $B$ is complete). The inductive topology with respect to the canonical embeddings $g_B: E_B \to E$ coincides with the original bornological topology. This structural result shows that bornological spaces are precisely the inductive limits of normed spaces.
