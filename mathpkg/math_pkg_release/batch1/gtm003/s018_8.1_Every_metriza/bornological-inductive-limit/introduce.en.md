This theorem establishes that the inductive limit of a family of bornological spaces carries a bornological topology. The proof uses the fact that bounded sets are preserved under the structure maps of the inductive limit: if a convex circled set absorbs all bounded sets in the inductive limit, its preimages absorb bounded sets in each factor space, hence are 0-neighborhoods by the bornological property.
