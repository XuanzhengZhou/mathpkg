A bornological space is a locally convex space in which the bornivorous sets (those absorbing every bounded set) and the 0-neighborhoods coincide. This property ensures that boundedness determines the topology, connecting the bornology (collection of bounded sets) with the locally convex topology. Bornological spaces form an important class intermediate between metrizable and barreled spaces in the theory of locally convex spaces.
