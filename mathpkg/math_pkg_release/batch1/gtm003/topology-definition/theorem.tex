Let $X$ be a set, $\mathfrak{G}$ a set of subsets of $X$ invariant under finite intersections and arbitrary unions. Then $\mathfrak{G}$ defines a topology $\mathfrak{T}$ on $X$, making $(X, \mathfrak{T})$ a topological space. The sets $G \in \mathfrak{G}$ are called open; their complements $F = X \setminus G$ are called closed.
