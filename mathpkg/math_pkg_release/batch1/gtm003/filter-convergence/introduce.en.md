A filter $\mathfrak{F}$ on a topological space $X$ converges to a point $x$ if every neighborhood of $x$ belongs to $\mathfrak{F}$. This generalizes the notion of sequence convergence. In a Hausdorff space, limits are unique. Filter convergence provides a unified framework for studying limits in general topological spaces, where sequences may be insufficient.
