This theorem generalizes F. Riesz's classical result that a locally compact normed space is finite-dimensional. The proof uses the fact that a compact 0-neighborhood generates a sequence of neighborhoods $\lambda_n V$ forming a 0-neighborhood base, which forces finite-dimensionality via a covering argument. This is one of the fundamental structure theorems for topological vector spaces.
