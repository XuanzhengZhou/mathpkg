The predual of a $W^*$-algebra is unique up to isometric isomorphism and can be identified intrinsically as the subspace $W'_n$ of the dual generated by all normal (order continuous) linear forms. This fundamental result follows directly from Sakai's theorem (6.9), which identifies the positive part of the predual with the normal positive linear forms. The unit ball correspondence follows from the bipolar theorem.
