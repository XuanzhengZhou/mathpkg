In any $W^*$-algebra, both the self-adjoint part $W_{sa}$ and the positive cone $W_+$ are closed in the canonical weak-* topology $\sigma(W, V)$. This property is fundamental for the order-theoretic study of $W^*$-algebras, as it ensures that the natural order structure interacts well with the weak-* topology. The proof uses the weak-* compactness of the unit ball and properties of the involution.
