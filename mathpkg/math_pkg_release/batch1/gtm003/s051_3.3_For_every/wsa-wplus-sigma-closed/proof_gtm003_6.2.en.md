The involution $x \mapsto x^*$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-continuous (since $\langle x^*, v \rangle = \overline{\langle x, v^* \rangle}$ for appropriate $v^* \in V$). Thus $W_{sa} = \{x \in W : x = x^*\}$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-closed as the kernel of $x \mapsto x - x^*$.

For $W_+$, note that the unit ball $U$ of $W$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-compact (Alaoglu-Bourbaki). Since $W_+ \cap U = \{x \in U : x = x^*, \sigma(x) \subset [0, \infty)\}$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-closed in $U$, it is compact. As $W_+$ is the union of $n(W_+ \cap U)$ for $n \in \mathbb{N}$, and each $n(W_+ \cap U)$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-closed, it follows that $W_+$ is $\sigma(W, V)$-closed using the Krein-Smulian theorem or a direct argument using the fact that $W_+$ is a cone generated by a compact set.
