Let $M$ be a $\sigma(W, V)$-closed $C^*$-subalgebra of $W$. Then $M$ is its own bipolar with respect to the duality $\langle W, V \rangle$, i.e., $M = (M^\circ)^\circ$ where $M^\circ = \{v \in V : \langle m, v \rangle = 0 \text{ for all } m \in M\}$. By the bipolar theorem, $M$ is the dual Banach space of $V/M^\circ$. Since $M$ is a $C^*$-algebra in its own right (being a $C^*$-subalgebra of $W$) and its normed space is a dual Banach space, $M$ is a $W^*$-algebra.
