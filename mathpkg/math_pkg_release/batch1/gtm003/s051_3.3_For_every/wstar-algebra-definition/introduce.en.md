A $W^*$-algebra (abstract von Neumann algebra) is a $C^*$-algebra whose underlying Banach space is a dual space, meaning there exists a predual $V$ such that $W \cong V'$ isometrically. The prototypical example is $\mathcal{L}(H)$, the algebra of bounded operators on a Hilbert space, which is the dual of the trace class operators $L^1(H)$. The predual endows the $W^*$-algebra with a canonical weak-* topology $\sigma(W, V)$.
