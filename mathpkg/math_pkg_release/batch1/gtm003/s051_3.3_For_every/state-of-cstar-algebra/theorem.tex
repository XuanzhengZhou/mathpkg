A positive linear form $\phi \in A'$ is called a \textit{state} of $A$ if $\|\phi\| = 1$. The set $S(A)$ of all states is then called the \textit{state space} of $A$; if $A$ is unital, $S(A)$ is the intersection of the $\sigma(A', A)$-closed hyperplane $\{\phi \in A' : \phi(e) = 1\}$ with the cone $A'_+$. Hence in this case, $S(A)$ is $\sigma(A', A)$-compact.
