A Boolean algebra of projections (also called a resolution of the identity) is a commuting family of projections in a unital $C^*$-algebra that contains $0$ and the unit $e$, closed under the Boolean lattice operations. These structures are the principal tool for the spectral theory of self-adjoint and normal elements, providing a projection-valued measure that generalizes the spectral theorem from Hilbert space operators to abstract $C^*$-algebras.
