Sakai's theorem is a cornerstone of $W^*$-algebra theory: it identifies the normal positive linear forms on a $W^*$-algebra with precisely the elements of its predual. In other words, a positive functional is order continuous if and only if it is weak-* continuous. This result implies the uniqueness of the predual and establishes that the predual can be recognized intrinsically as the space of all normal linear forms on the $W^*$-algebra.
