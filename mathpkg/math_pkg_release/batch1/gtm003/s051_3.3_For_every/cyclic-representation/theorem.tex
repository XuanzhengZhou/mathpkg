A representation $\pi : A \to \mathcal{L}(H)$ is called \textit{cyclic} if there exists a vector $\xi \in H$ such that the orbit $\{\pi(x)\xi : x \in A\}$ is dense in $H$. The vector $\xi$ is called a \textit{cyclic vector} of $\pi$, usually assumed to satisfy $\|\xi\| = 1$. One denotes by $(\pi, H)$ a representation and by $(\pi, H, \xi)$ a representation with cyclic vector $\xi$.
