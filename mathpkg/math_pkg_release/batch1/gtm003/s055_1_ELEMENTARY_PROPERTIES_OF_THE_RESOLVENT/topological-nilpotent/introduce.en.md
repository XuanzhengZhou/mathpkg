An operator $u$ on a complex Banach space is called a topological nilpotent if its spectral radius $r(u)$ vanishes. Equivalently, the spectrum reduces to $\{0\}$, and the resolvent $R(\lambda) = (\lambda e - u)^{-1}$ (extended by $R(\infty) = 0$) becomes an entire function in the variable $\lambda^{-1}$. This notion generalizes algebraic nilpotence in the context of Banach algebras.
