For a real Banach space $E$, the real spectrum $\sigma_{\mathbb{R}}(u)$ of an operator $u$ consists of those real $\lambda$ for which $\lambda e - u$ is not an automorphism. Rather than developing a separate real spectral theory, one complexifies $E$ to obtain a complex Banach space $E_1$ with the norm $\|x+iy\|_1 = \sup_{0 \leq \theta < 2\pi} \|(\cos \theta)x + (\sin \theta)y\|$, which generalizes the absolute value on $\mathbb{C}$ viewed as the complexification of $\mathbb{R}$. Every real operator extends naturally to its complexification, subsuming the real case under the complex theory.
