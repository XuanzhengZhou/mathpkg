For a continuous linear operator $u$ on a complex Banach space, one can detect whether a limit point of the resolvent set belongs to the spectrum by examining the norm of the resolvent. If a sequence of points in $\rho(u)$ converges to $\lambda$, then $\lambda$ lies in the spectrum $\sigma(u)$ precisely when the operator norm of the resolvent $R(\lambda_n)$ tends to infinity. This provides a useful analytic criterion for membership in the spectrum.
