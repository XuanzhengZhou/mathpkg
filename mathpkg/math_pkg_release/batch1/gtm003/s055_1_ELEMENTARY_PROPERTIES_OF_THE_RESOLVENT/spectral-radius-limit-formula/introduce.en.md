The Gelfand–Beurling spectral radius formula states that for any continuous linear operator $u$ on a complex Banach space, the spectral radius $r(u)$ equals $\limsup_{n \to \infty} \|u^n\|^{1/n}$, and this limit actually exists as an ordinary limit. This fundamental identity links the algebraic concept of spectral radius to the asymptotic behavior of iterates of the operator.
