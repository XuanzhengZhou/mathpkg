If $u \in \mathcal{L}(E)$, the spectrum $\sigma(u)$ is the complement in $\mathbb{C}$ of the largest open set $\rho(u)$ in which $\lambda \mapsto (\lambda e - u)^{-1}$ exists and is locally holomorphic. For $\lambda \in \rho(u)$, we set $(\lambda e - u)^{-1} = R(\lambda)$; the map $\lambda \mapsto R(\lambda)$ is called the resolvent, and $\rho(u)$ the resolvent set of $u$.

Supposing that $E$ is not reduced to $\{0\}$, the spectrum $\sigma(u)$ is a non-empty compact subset of $\mathbb{C}$. The radius $r(u)$ of the smallest circle of center $0$ in $\mathbb{C}$ that contains $\sigma(u)$ is called the spectral radius of $u$. The set $\{\lambda \in \mathbb{C} : |\lambda| = r(u)\}$ is termed the spectral circle of $u$.

Moreover, by Banach's homomorphism theorem and the fact that the set of invertible elements of a Banach algebra is open, for each $u \in \mathcal{L}(E)$ the spectrum $\sigma(u)$ can be characterized as the set of those $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}$ for which $\lambda e - u$ fails to be an algebraic automorphism of $E$.
