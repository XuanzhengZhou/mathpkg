Within the spectrum $\sigma(u)$ of a continuous linear operator $u$, the point spectrum $\pi(u)$ consists of those $\lambda$ for which $\lambda e - u$ fails to be injective. Each $\lambda_0$ in the point spectrum is called an eigenvalue, and the kernel of $\lambda_0 e - u$ is the associated eigenspace. The dimension of this eigenspace defines the geometric multiplicity, and its non-zero elements are eigenvectors. Alternative terminology includes characteristic value, characteristic space, and characteristic vector.
