The bipolar theorem is one of the most fundamental results in duality theory for topological vector spaces. It states that applying the polar operation twice to a subset $M$ of a dual pair $\langle F, G \rangle$ recovers the $\sigma(F, G)$-closed convex hull of $M \cup \{0\}$. The result is a consequence of the Hahn-Banach separation theorem and serves as an indispensable tool in working with dualities, providing a tight link between the polar operation and the weak topology.
