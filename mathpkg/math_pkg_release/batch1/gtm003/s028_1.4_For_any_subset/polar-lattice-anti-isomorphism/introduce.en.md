A deeper consequence of the bipolar theorem is that the polar map $M \mapsto M^\circ$ establishes a perfect duality between the lattices of weakly closed subspaces of $F$ and $G$. This map is an anti-isomorphism (order-reversing bijection) that interchanges the lattice operations of intersection and closed sum. This structural result is fundamental to the duality theory of locally convex spaces and underpins many subsequent developments in the subject.
