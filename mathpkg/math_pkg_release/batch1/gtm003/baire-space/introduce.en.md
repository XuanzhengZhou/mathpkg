A Baire space is a topological space in which the conclusion of the Baire category theorem holds: countable intersections of dense open sets remain dense. Complete metric spaces and locally compact Hausdorff spaces are the prime examples. The Baire property is fundamental in functional analysis for proving the uniform boundedness principle and the open mapping theorem.
