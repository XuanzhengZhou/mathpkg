A topological vector space is Hausdorff precisely when the intersection of all 0-neighborhoods is $\{0\}$, or equivalently, when the origin (and hence every point) can be separated from any non-zero element by a 0-neighborhood. This simple criterion is one of the most useful facts in TVS theory.
