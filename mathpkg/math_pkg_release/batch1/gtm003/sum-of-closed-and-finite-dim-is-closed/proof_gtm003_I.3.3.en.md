**Proof.** Let $\phi$ denote the natural map of $L$ onto $L/M$; $L/M$ is Hausdorff by (2.3). Since $\phi(N)$ is a finite-dimensional subspace of $L/M$, it is isomorphic to $K_0^m$ for some $m$ by Theorem (3.2). Since $K$ is complete, $\phi(N)$ is complete, hence closed in $L/M$. Therefore $\phi^{-1}(\phi(N)) = M + N$ is closed in $L$, because $\phi$ is continuous.
