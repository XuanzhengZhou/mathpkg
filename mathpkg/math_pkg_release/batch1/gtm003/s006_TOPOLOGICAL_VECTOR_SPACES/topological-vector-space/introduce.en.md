A topological vector space (t.v.s.) is a vector space over a non-discrete valuated field equipped with a topology that makes vector addition and scalar multiplication jointly continuous operations. The field $K$ carries the uniformity derived from its absolute value, rather than an arbitrary topology. Every t.v.s. is in particular a commutative topological group, since the continuity of addition and subtraction follows from the axioms. This definition provides the foundational framework for the entire theory developed in the book, and it is general enough to work over any non-discrete valuated field, not just the real or complex numbers.
