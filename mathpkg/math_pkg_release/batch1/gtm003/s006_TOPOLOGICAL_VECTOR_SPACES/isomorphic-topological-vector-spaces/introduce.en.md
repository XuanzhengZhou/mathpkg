Two topological vector spaces over the same field $K$ are isomorphic if there exists a bijective (biunivocal) linear map between them that is simultaneously a homeomorphism. Such a map is called a topological isomorphism, or simply an isomorphism of t.v.s. This is the natural notion of equivalence in the category of topological vector spaces, combining algebraic isomorphism with topological equivalence. Occasionally the terms "topological isomorphism" and "topologically isomorphic" are used to distinguish from purely algebraic isomorphisms.
