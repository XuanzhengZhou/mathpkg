Krein's theorem gives a necessary and sufficient condition for the closed convex hull of a weakly compact set to again be weakly compact. Given a weakly compact subset $B$ of a locally convex space $E$, its closed convex hull $C$ is weakly compact precisely when $C$ is complete under the Mackey topology $\tau(E,E')$, the finest locally convex topology on $E$ compatible with the dual pair $(E,E')$. The necessity is clear (weak compactness implies weak completeness, hence Mackey completeness), while the sufficiency is proved via condition (d) of Eberlein's theorem. This result is fundamental in the theory of locally convex spaces, since the convex hull of a compact set need not be compact even in finite-dimensional spaces without completeness.
