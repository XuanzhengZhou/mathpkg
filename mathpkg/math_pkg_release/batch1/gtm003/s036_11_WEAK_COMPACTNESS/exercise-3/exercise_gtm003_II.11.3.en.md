Let $F$ be a l.c.s. over $\mathbb{C}$, let $G$ be a properly real subspace of $F$ (Chapter I, Exercise 16) such that $F_0 = G + iG$, where $F_0$ is the underlying real space of $F$, and denote by $G'$ the set of all real linear forms on $G$ that are continuous for the topology induced by $F$. Show the following assertions to be equivalent:

$\alpha)$ $F_0 = G + iG$ is a topological direct sum for $\sigma(F_0, F_0')$.

$\beta)$ $F'$ is the linear hull (over $\mathbb{C}$) of the linear forms

$$x + iy \mapsto f(x) + if(y), \qquad (x, y \in G),$$

where $f$ runs through $G'$.
