Let $E$ be a locally convex space (over $\mathbb{K} = \mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$) and let $H$ be a subset of $E$ whose closed, convex hull is complete. The following properties of $H$ are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $H$ is relatively weakly compact.
\item[(b)] Each sequence in $H$ has a weak cluster point in $E$.
\item[(c)] For every decreasing sequence $(H_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ of closed convex subsets of $E$ such that $H_n \cap H \neq \varnothing$ for all $n$, the intersection $\bigcap_{n \in \mathbb{N}} H_n$ is non-empty.
\item[(d)] $H$ is bounded in $E$, and the $\sigma(E'', E')$-closure of $H$ in the bidual $E''$ is contained in $E$.
\end{enumerate}
The equivalence $(a) \Leftrightarrow (b)$ is due to Eberlein; $(a) \Leftrightarrow (c)$ is due to Dieudonn\'e; $(a) \Leftrightarrow (d)$ is due to Grothendieck.
