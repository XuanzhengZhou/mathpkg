Let $X$ be a compact space and let $(Y, d)$ be a locally compact metric space that is countable at infinity. Denote by $\mathcal{C}_Y(X)$ the space of all continuous functions from $X$ into $Y$, endowed with the topology of pointwise convergence (i.e., the relativized product topology from $Y^X$). If $H \subset \mathcal{C}_Y(X)$ is such that every sequence in $H$ has a cluster point in $Y^X$, then $H$ is relatively compact in $Y^X$ and its closure $\overline{H}$ is contained in $\mathcal{C}_Y(X)$.
