Let $H$ be a vector space over $\mathbb{C}$ and let $(x, y) \mapsto [x, y]$ be a complex-valued function on $H \times H$ satisfying ($\alpha^*$ denoting the complex conjugate of $\alpha \in \mathbb{C}$):

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] For each $y \in H$, $x \mapsto [x, y]$ is a linear form on $H$.
\item[(ii)] $[x, y] = [y, x]^*$ for all $x \in H$, $y \in H$.
\item[(iii)] $[x, x] \geq 0$ for all $x \in H$.
\item[(iv)] $[x, x] = 0$ implies $x = 0$.
\end{enumerate}

The mapping $(x, y) \mapsto [x, y]$ is called a positive definite Hermitian form (or inner product).
