The analytic definition of a locally convex topology uses a family of seminorms to generate the topology. Each seminorm $p_\alpha$ defines a pseudometric, and the collection of all such pseudometrics induces the locally convex topology. This construction is dual to the geometric definition via convex, circled, radial sets: the Minkowski functional of each such set is a seminorm, and conversely the unit balls of seminorms are convex, circled, and radial.
