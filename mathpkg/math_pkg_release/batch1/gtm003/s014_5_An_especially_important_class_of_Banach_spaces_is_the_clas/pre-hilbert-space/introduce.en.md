A pre-Hilbert space is a vector space equipped with a positive definite Hermitian form (inner product) that induces a norm via $\|x\| = \sqrt{[x,x]}$. The norm satisfies the parallelogram law and the polarization identity, which characterize inner product spaces among normed spaces. The fundamental Schwarz inequality $|[x,y]| \leq \|x\|\|y\|$ follows directly from the inner product axioms and is the key to proving that the induced norm satisfies the triangle inequality.
