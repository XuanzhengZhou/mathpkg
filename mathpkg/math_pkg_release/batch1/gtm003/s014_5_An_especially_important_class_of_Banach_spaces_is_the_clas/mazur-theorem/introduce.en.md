Mazur's theorem is the geometric form of the Hahn-Banach theorem, providing a separation result in topological vector spaces. It states that a convex open set can be separated from a disjoint linear manifold by a closed hyperplane. This theorem is fundamental in functional analysis, as it yields the existence of non-trivial continuous linear functionals and underlies the duality theory of locally convex spaces.
