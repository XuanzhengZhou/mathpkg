A fundamental property of locally convex spaces is that their topological dual is rich enough to separate distinct points. This is an immediate consequence of the Hahn-Banach theorem (in its geometric form, Mazur's theorem): given two distinct points, one can be separated from the other by a convex open set, and the separating hyperplane yields a continuous linear functional distinguishing them. This separation property is essential for the weak topology and duality theory.
