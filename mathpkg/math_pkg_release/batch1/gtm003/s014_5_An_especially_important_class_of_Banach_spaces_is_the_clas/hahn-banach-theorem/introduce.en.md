The Hahn-Banach theorem is the analytic counterpart to Mazur's geometric theorem. It guarantees that any linear functional defined on a subspace and dominated by a seminorm can be extended to the whole space while preserving the seminorm domination. This is one of the most important theorems in functional analysis, with consequences including the existence of continuous linear functionals separating points in locally convex spaces, the rich duality theory of Banach spaces, and the Krein-Milman theorem.
