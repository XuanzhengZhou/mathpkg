This theorem characterizes the continuous dual of the locally convex space $l^1(\mathbf{A}, E)$ of absolutely summable families in a locally convex space $E$ indexed by an arbitrary set $\mathbf{A}$. The dual is identified with those families $\{x'_\alpha\}$ in the algebraic dual $(E')^\mathbf{A}$ that form a prenuclear family, which is a technical condition involving equicontinuous subsets and Radon measures. The canonical pairing extends the natural sum $\sum_\alpha \langle x_\alpha, x'_\alpha \rangle$, making the duality completely explicit. This result generalizes the scalar case (where the dual of $l^1(\mathbf{A})$ is the space of bounded families) to vector-valued summable families.
