This is the central result of Pietsch, providing a purely functional-analytic characterization of nuclear locally convex spaces. A locally convex space $E$ is nuclear precisely when the canonical imbedding of summable sequences $l^1[\mathbb{N}, E]$ into absolutely summable sequences $l^1(\mathbb{N}, E)$ is a topological isomorphism. This theorem connects the abstract Grothendieck notion of nuclearity (defined via tensor products) to the concrete property that every summable sequence is automatically absolutely summable, with equivalence of the associated locally convex topologies. The proof uses results (10.2) through (10.6) to establish a chain of equivalences linking nuclearity to prenuclearity of equicontinuous sets, density arguments, and the reduction to the countable case.
