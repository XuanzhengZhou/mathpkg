The Banach space $l^1(\mathbf{A})$ consists of all summable scalar families $\xi = \{\xi_\alpha : \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ under the natural norm $\xi \mapsto \|\xi\| = \sum_\alpha |\xi_\alpha|$. Its dual space admits a concrete identification: every continuous linear functional on $l^1(\mathbf{A})$ corresponds to a bounded scalar family $\eta = \{\eta_\alpha : \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ acting via the canonical bilinear form $\langle \xi, \eta \rangle = \sum_\alpha \xi_\alpha \eta_\alpha$. This is the natural generalization of the classical fact that the dual of $\ell^1(\mathbb{N})$ is $\ell^\infty(\mathbb{N})$.
