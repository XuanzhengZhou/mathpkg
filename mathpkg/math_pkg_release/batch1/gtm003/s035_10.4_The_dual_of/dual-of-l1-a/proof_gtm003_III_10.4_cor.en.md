This follows immediately from the definition of $l^1(\mathbf{A})$ as the Banach space of summable scalar families $\xi = \{\xi_\alpha : \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ under the norm $\|\xi\| = \sum_\alpha |\xi_\alpha|$. The dual of $l^1(\mathbf{A})$ is well known to be isometrically isomorphic to $\ell^\infty(\mathbf{A})$, the space of bounded scalar families. Indeed, every bounded family $\eta = \{\eta_\alpha\}$ defines a continuous linear functional via $\langle \xi, \eta \rangle = \sum_\alpha \xi_\alpha \eta_\alpha$, with norm $\|\eta\|_\infty = \sup_\alpha |\eta_\alpha|$. Conversely, the Riesz representation theorem for $l^1$ spaces (or the general fact that the dual of $l^1$ is $\ell^\infty$) shows every continuous linear functional arises this way.
