This proposition states that the question of whether the canonical imbedding $l^1[\mathbf{A}, E] \hookrightarrow l^1(\mathbf{A}, E)$ is an isomorphism reduces entirely to the countable case $\mathbf{A} = \mathbb{N}$. If every summable sequence in a locally convex space $E$ is automatically absolutely summable (so that $l^1[\mathbb{N}, E] = l^1(\mathbb{N}, E)$ algebraically or topologically), then the same property lifts to families indexed by arbitrary sets. The proof proceeds by contradiction: a summable but not absolutely summable family over an infinite index set would yield a countably infinite subset with the same pathology.
