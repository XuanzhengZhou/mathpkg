Let $E$ be a locally convex space and let $\tilde{E}$ be its completion. Under the canonical isomorphism of $l^1(\mathbf{A}) \mathbin{\tilde{\otimes}} E$ with $l^1[\mathbf{A}, \tilde{E}]$ and of $l^1(\mathbf{A}) \mathbin{\tilde{\otimes}} E$ with $l^1(\mathbf{A}, \tilde{E})$, the canonical map of $l^1(\mathbf{A}) \mathbin{\tilde{\otimes}} E$ into $l^1(\mathbf{A}) \mathbin{\tilde{\otimes}} E$ corresponds to the canonical imbedding of $l^1[\mathbf{A}, \tilde{E}]$ in $l^1(\mathbf{A}, \tilde{E})$ and is, therefore, biunivocal (injective).
