The dual of $E'_\beta$, denoted by $E''$, is called the bidual of the l.c.s. $E$. Via the canonical imbedding (or evaluation map) $x \mapsto f_x$, where $f_x(x') = \langle x, x' \rangle$, we have $E \subset E'' \subset E'^*$, where generally both inclusions are proper.

If $E''$ is given the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology with $\mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{B}$ (the family of strongly bounded subsets of $E'$), $E''$ is called the strong bidual. The $\mathfrak{S}$-topology with $\mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{E}$ (the family of equicontinuous subsets of $E'$) is called the natural topology; it induces the given topology on $E$ for any l.c.s. $E$.
