The bidual $E''$ of a locally convex space $E$ is the strong dual of $E'_\beta$, containing $E$ via the canonical evaluation map $x \mapsto (x' \mapsto \langle x, x' \rangle)$. The strong bidual carries the topology of uniform convergence on strongly bounded subsets of $E'$, while the natural topology uses equicontinuous subsets. The natural topology always induces the original topology on $E$, and equality with the strong topology characterizes infrabarreled spaces.
