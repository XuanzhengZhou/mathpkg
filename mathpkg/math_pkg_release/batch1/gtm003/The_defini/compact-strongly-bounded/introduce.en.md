Proposition 5.1 states that every convex, circled, weakly compact subset of the dual space $E'_\sigma$ is strongly bounded in $E'_\beta$. The proof uses the fact that the polar of such a set is a neighborhood for the Mackey topology, hence absorbs all bounded sets in $E$. This result bridges the weak and strong boundedness properties in the dual.
