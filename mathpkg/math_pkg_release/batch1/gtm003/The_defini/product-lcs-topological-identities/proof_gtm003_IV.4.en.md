**Proof.** It is immediate that each $f = (f_\alpha) \in \bigoplus_\alpha E'_\alpha$ defines a linear form $x \mapsto f(x) = \sum_\alpha f_\alpha(x_\alpha)$ on $E$ which is continuous, since $f = \sum_{\alpha \in H} f_\alpha \circ p_\alpha$ for a finite subset $H \subset A$. Conversely, every continuous linear form on $E$ is a finite sum of such forms (by a standard property of products). This establishes the algebraic isomorphism $E' \cong \bigoplus_\alpha E'_\alpha$.

1. The identity $\sigma(E, E') = \prod_\alpha \sigma(E_\alpha, E'_\alpha)$ follows from Theorem 4.2 since the saturated family for $\sigma$ is generated by finite subsets.

2. For the Mackey topology $\tau$, let $\mathfrak{S}'_{\alpha}$ denote the family of all convex, circled, weakly compact subsets of $E'_{\alpha}$ ($\alpha \in A$). By (4.2) it suffices to show that $\mathfrak{S}' = \bigoplus_{\alpha} \mathfrak{S}'_{\alpha}$ is a fundamental system of convex, circled subsets of $E'$ that are compact for $\sigma(E', E)$. If $C$ is such a set, $C$ is bounded for $\sigma(E', E)$ and hence bounded for $\tau(E', E)$. Thus by part 3 below and (II, 6.3), $C$ is contained in $\bigoplus_{\alpha \in H} \tilde{p}_{\alpha}(C)$, where $H$ is a suitable finite subset of $A$, and where $\tilde{p}_{\alpha}$ denotes the projection of $E'$ onto $E'_{\alpha}$. Since $\tilde{p}_{\alpha}$ is continuous for $\sigma(E', E)$ into $(E'_{\alpha}, \sigma(E'_{\alpha}, E_{\alpha}))$, it follows that $\tilde{p}_{\alpha}(C) \in \mathfrak{S}'_{\alpha}$, which is the desired conclusion, since clearly every member of $\mathfrak{S}'$ is convex, circled, and compact for $\sigma(E', E)$.

3. The identity $\tau(E', E) = \bigoplus_{\alpha} \tau(E'_{\alpha}, E_{\alpha})$ follows from Theorem 4.2 applied to the families of convex, circled, weakly compact subsets of each $E_\alpha$. $\square$
