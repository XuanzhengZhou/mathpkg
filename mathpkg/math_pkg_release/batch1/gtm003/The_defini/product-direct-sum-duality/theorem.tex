Let $\{ \langle F_\alpha, G_\alpha \rangle : \alpha \in A \}$ denote a family of dualities over $K$ and let $F = \prod_\alpha F_\alpha$, $G = \bigoplus_\alpha G_\alpha$. The bilinear form $f$ on $F \times G$, defined by

$$f(x, y) = \sum_\alpha \langle x_\alpha, y_\alpha \rangle$$

(note that the sum is over at most finitely many non-zero terms), places $F$ and $G$ in duality; denote by $\langle F, G \rangle$ the dual system $(F, G, f)$.
