Theorem 5.3 shows that in a quasi-complete locally convex space, every weakly bounded subset of the dual is automatically strongly bounded. The proof relies on the fact that the polar of a convex, circled, bounded set is a barrel that, by quasi-completeness, absorbs every complete bounded set, and these form a fundamental family. A corollary states that quasi-complete infrabarreled spaces are barreled.
