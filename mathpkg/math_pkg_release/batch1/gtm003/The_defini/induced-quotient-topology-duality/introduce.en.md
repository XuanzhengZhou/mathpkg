Theorem 4.1 establishes the fundamental relationship between $\mathfrak{S}$-topologies on a dual system $\langle F, G \rangle$ and those on the associated quotient duality $\langle M, G/M^\circ \rangle$. Under appropriate consistency conditions, the induced topology on $M$ and the quotient topology on $G/M^\circ$ correspond precisely to the images of the respective families of subsets under the canonical maps. The implications require both consistency assumptions and the closedness of $M$ for full equivalence.
