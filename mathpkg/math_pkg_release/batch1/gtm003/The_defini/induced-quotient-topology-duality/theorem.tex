Let $\langle F, G \rangle$ be a dual system, let $M$ be a subspace of $F$, and let $M^\circ$ be the subspace of $G$ orthogonal to $M$. Let $\psi$ denote the canonical imbedding of $M$ into $F$, and let $\phi$ denote the canonical (quotient) map $G \to G/M^\circ$.

For any given family $\mathfrak{S}_1'$ of bounded (resp. weakly bounded, etc.) subsets of $G$ and $\mathfrak{S}_1$ of bounded subsets of $G$, define $\mathfrak{S}_2' = \phi(\mathfrak{S}_1')$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2 = \psi^{-1}(\mathfrak{S}_1)$. Then we have the implications

$$\text{(a)} \ \mathfrak{S}_2 = \psi^{-1}(\mathfrak{S}_1) \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{(b)} \ \mathfrak{T}_1 \text{ induces } \mathfrak{T}_2 \text{ on } M,$$

and

$$\text{(d)} \ \mathfrak{S}_2' = \phi(\mathfrak{S}_1') \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{(c)} \ \mathfrak{T}_2 \text{ is the quotient topology of } \mathfrak{T}_1 \text{ on } G/M^\circ.$$

If $\mathfrak{T}_1$ is consistent with $\langle F, G \rangle$, then (b) $\Rightarrow$ (a); if $\mathfrak{T}_1'$ is consistent with $\langle F, G \rangle$ and $M$ is closed, then (c) $\Rightarrow$ (d).
