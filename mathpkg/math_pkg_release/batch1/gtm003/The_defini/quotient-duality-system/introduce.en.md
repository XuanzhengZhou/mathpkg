Given a dual system $\langle F, G \rangle$ and a subspace $M \subseteq F$, one can form a new duality between $M$ and the quotient $G/M^\circ$, where $M^\circ$ is the orthogonal of $M$ in $G$. The bilinear form is defined by $\langle x, [y] \rangle = \langle x, y \rangle$ for any representative $y$ of the coset $[y]$. This construction is fundamental for studying the duality between induced and quotient topologies.
