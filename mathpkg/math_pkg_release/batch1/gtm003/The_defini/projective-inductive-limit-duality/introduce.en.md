Theorem 4.4 extends the product/direct sum duality to projective and inductive limits. For a projective limit $E = \varprojlim E_\beta$ of locally convex spaces, the dual $E'$ is algebraically the inductive limit of the duals equipped with the Mackey topology. Conversely, the dual of an inductive limit is the projective limit of the duals. This duality is a cornerstone for applications involving spaces of distributions and test functions.
