Let $E = \lim g_{\alpha\beta} E_\beta$ be a projective limit of l.c.s. with linking maps $g_{\alpha\beta}: E_\beta \to E_\alpha$ ($\alpha \leq \beta$), and let $h_{\beta\alpha} = {}^t g_{\alpha\beta}: E_\alpha' \to E_\beta'$ be the adjoint maps. Then the dual $E'$ of $E$ is algebraically isomorphic with the inductive limit $\lim h_{\beta\alpha} E_\alpha'$, equipped with the Mackey topology $\tau(\lim h_{\beta\alpha} E_\alpha', E)$.

Conversely, if $F = \lim h_{\beta\alpha} F_\alpha$ is an inductive limit of l.c.s., then $F'$ is algebraically isomorphic with the projective limit $\lim {}^t h_{\beta\alpha} F_\alpha'$, and $\tau(F', F) = \lim {}^t h_{\beta\alpha} \tau(F_\alpha', F_\alpha)$.
