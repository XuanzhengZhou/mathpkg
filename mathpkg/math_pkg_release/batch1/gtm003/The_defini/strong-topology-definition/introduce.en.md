The strong topology $\beta(E, E')$ on a locally convex space $E$ is the topology of uniform convergence on all $\sigma(E', E)$-bounded subsets of $E'$. Dually, $\beta(E', E)$ is the strong topology on the dual. The notation $E_\sigma$, $E_\tau$, $E_\beta$ and $E'_\sigma$, $E'_\tau$, $E'_\beta$ conveniently distinguishes the weak, Mackey, and strong topologies. A crucial subtlety is that the strong topology may not be consistent with the duality, so the strong dual's dual may differ from the original space.
