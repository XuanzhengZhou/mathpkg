Let $E$ be a l.c.s. The topologies $\beta(E, E')$ and $\beta(E', E)$ are called the strong topologies on $E$ and $E'$, respectively; $(E', \beta(E', E))$ is called the strong dual of $E$. The following notation is used: $E_{\sigma}, E_{\tau}, E_{\beta}$ denote the space $E$ under the topologies $\sigma(E, E')$, $\tau(E, E')$, $\beta(E, E')$ respectively; $E_{\sigma}', E_{\tau}', E_{\beta}'$ denote the dual $E'$ of $E$ under $\sigma(E', E)$, $\tau(E', E)$, $\beta(E', E)$, respectively.
