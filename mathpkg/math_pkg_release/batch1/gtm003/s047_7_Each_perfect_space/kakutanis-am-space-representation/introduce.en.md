Theorem 8.5 is Kakutani's celebrated representation theorem: every non-trivial (AM)-space with unit is isometrically lattice-isomorphic to the Banach lattice $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ of continuous real-valued functions on a compact space $X$. The space $X$ is the set of extreme points of the positive face of the dual unit ball, endowed with the weak$^*$ topology. This theorem characterizes $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ spaces among Banach lattices and provides the functional-analytic foundation for the Gelfand-Naimark theorem.
