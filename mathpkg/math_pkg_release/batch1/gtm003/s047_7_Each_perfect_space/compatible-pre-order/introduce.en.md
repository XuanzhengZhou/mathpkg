A compatible pre-order on a real vector space is a pre-order that respects the vector operations: it is invariant under translation and preserved under multiplication by non-negative scalars. This is the minimal structure required to define an ordered vector space; when the pre-order is additionally antisymmetric, one obtains a (partial) order on the vector space, and the space becomes an ordered vector space in the usual sense.
