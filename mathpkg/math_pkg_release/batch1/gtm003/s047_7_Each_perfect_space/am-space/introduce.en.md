An (AM)-space (abstract $M$-space) is a Banach lattice in which the norm of the supremum of two positive elements equals the supremum of their norms. This property is characteristic of spaces of the form $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$: Kakutani's representation theorem shows that every (AM)-space is isomorphic to a closed vector sublattice of some $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$. When the space possesses a unit (an element $u$ such that $[-u,u]$ is the unit ball), it becomes an (AM)-space with unit and is isometrically lattice-isomorphic to $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ for a suitable compact space $X$.
