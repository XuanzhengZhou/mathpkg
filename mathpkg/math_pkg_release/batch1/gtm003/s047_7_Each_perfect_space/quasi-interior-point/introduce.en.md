A quasi-interior point of the positive cone $C$ in an ordered topological vector space $L$ is a positive element $x \geq 0$ whose order interval $[0,x]$ is total in $L$ (i.e., its linear span is dense). This is the topological counterpart of the algebraic notion of a weak order unit. Under suitable conditions (e.g., metrizable separable ordered l.c.s. with continuous lattice operations), every weak order unit is a quasi-interior point.
