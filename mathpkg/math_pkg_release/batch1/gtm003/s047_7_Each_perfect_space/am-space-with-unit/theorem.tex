A Banach lattice $E$ is called an \emph{(AM)-space with unit $u$} if $E$ is an (AM)-space and there exists an element $u$ in the positive cone $C$ of $E$ such that the order interval
$$
[-u, u] = \{x \in E : -u \leq x \leq u\}
$$
is the unit ball of $E$. Such an element $u$ is unique and is an order unit of $E$. Every Banach lattice $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ for compact $X$ is an (AM)-space with unit, the unit being the constantly-one function $e$.
