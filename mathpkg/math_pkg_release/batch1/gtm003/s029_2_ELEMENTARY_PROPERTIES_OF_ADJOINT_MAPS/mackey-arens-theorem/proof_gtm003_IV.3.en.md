The necessity of the condition has been noted above: by Corollary 3 of the bipolar theorem (1.5), every topology $\mathfrak{T}$ on $F$ consistent with $\langle F, G \rangle$ is an $\mathfrak{S}$-topology, namely the topology of uniform convergence on the saturated class of $\mathfrak{T}$-equicontinuous subsets of $G$. By the theorem of Alaoglu-Bourbaki, these classes consist entirely of sets relatively compact for $\sigma(G, F)$.

Let us prove the sufficiency. Let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a saturated family of subsets of $G$ which covers $G$ and consists of $\sigma(G, F)$-relatively compact sets. It follows from (III, 3.2) that $F$ is a locally convex space under the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology, which — since $\mathfrak{S}$ covers $G$ — is finer than $\sigma(F, G)$ (the topology of simple convergence). Hence the dual of $F$ with respect to the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology contains $G$; we must show it is exactly $G$. Every continuous linear form on $F$ for the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology is uniformly continuous on some $S \in \mathfrak{S}$, and since the elements of $G$ separate points of $F$, it follows by the Hahn-Banach theorem that this linear form belongs to $G$. Hence the dual of $F$ under the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology is precisely $G$, establishing consistency.
