This proposition provides the most practical formulation of the Mackey-Arens theorem: a locally convex topology on $F$ is consistent with the duality $\langle F, G \rangle$ precisely when it lies between the weak topology $\sigma(F, G)$ and the Mackey topology $\tau(F, G)$. In this range, closedness of convex sets is determined by the Mackey topology, while boundedness is determined by the weak topology. The more general statements show that any topology whose dual is contained in $G$ is coarser than the Mackey topology, and any topology whose dual contains $G$ is finer than the weak topology.
