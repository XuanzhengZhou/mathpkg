Given a linear map $u \colon F \to F_1$ between vector spaces, the algebraic adjoint $u^* \colon F_1^* \to F^*$ is the canonical linear map between the algebraic dual spaces defined by precomposition: $u^*(y^*) = y^* \circ u$. This construction yields the fundamental adjoint identity $\langle ux, y^* \rangle = \langle x, u^*y^* \rangle$, which underpins all duality theory in topological vector spaces. The algebraic adjoint serves as the purely algebraic precursor to the topological adjoint $u'$ studied in the context of dual pairs and locally convex spaces.
