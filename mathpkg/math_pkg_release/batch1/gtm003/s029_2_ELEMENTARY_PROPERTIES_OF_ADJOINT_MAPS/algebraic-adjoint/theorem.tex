Let $F$ and $F_1$ be vector spaces over $K$ and let $u$ be a linear map of $F$ into $F_1$. For each element $y^*$ of $F_1^*$, the mapping $y^* \circ u \colon x \mapsto \langle ux, y^* \rangle$ is a linear form $x^* \in F^*$ (we write $ux$ in place of $u(x)$) commonly denoted by $u^*y^*$. Thus we have the identity

$$\langle ux, y^* \rangle = \langle x, u^*y^* \rangle$$

on $F \times F_1^*$; the map $y^* \mapsto u^*y^*$, evidently linear on $F_1^*$ into $F^*$, is called the \text{algebraic adjoint} $u^*$ of $u$.
