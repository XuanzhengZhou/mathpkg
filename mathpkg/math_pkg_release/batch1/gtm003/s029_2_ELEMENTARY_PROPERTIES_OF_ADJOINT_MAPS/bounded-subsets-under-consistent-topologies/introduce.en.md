This corollary to the Mackey-Arens theorem establishes that boundedness is an invariant of the duality $\langle F, G \rangle$, not of any particular consistent topology. Specifically, a subset of $F$ is bounded for the weak topology $\sigma(F, G)$ if and only if it is bounded for the Mackey topology $\tau(F, G)$, and hence bounded for every locally convex topology consistent with the duality. This fact is used ubiquitously in functional analysis, often without explicit mention.
