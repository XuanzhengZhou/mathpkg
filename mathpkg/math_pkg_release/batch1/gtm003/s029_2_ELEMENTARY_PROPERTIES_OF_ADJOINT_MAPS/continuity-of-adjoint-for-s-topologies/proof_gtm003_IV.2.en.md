Let $\tilde{\mathfrak{S}}$ and $\tilde{\mathfrak{S}}_1$, respectively, be the families of all weakly closed, convex, circled sets contained in $\mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{S}_1$. Since $\tilde{\mathfrak{S}}$ and $\tilde{\mathfrak{S}}_1$ are fundamental subfamilies of $\mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{S}_1$, respectively, the families $\mathfrak{U} = \{S^\circ : S \in \tilde{\mathfrak{S}}\}$ and $\mathfrak{U}_1 = \{S_1^\circ : S_1 \in \tilde{\mathfrak{S}}_1\}$ form neighborhood bases of $0$ for $\mathfrak{T}$ and $\mathfrak{T}_1$, respectively. By definition of the adjoint, $\langle x, u'y' \rangle = \langle ux, y' \rangle$ for all $x \in F$, $y' \in G_1$. The continuity condition $u'(\mathfrak{T}_1) \subset \mathfrak{T}$ is equivalent to: for every $S \in \tilde{\mathfrak{S}}$ there exists $S_1 \in \tilde{\mathfrak{S}}_1$ such that $u'(S_1^\circ) \subset S^\circ$, which by polarity is equivalent to $u(S) \subset S_1^{\circ\circ} = S_1$ (since $S_1$ is weakly closed, convex, and circled). Hence $u'$ is continuous if and only if for every $S \in \mathfrak{S}$ there exists $S_1 \in \mathfrak{S}_1$ with $u(S) \subset S_1$, which is precisely the condition $u(\mathfrak{S}) \subset \mathfrak{S}_1$.
