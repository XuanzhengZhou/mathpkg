Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a family of semi-norms generating the topology of $E$. For any $p \in \mathcal{P}$, $p$ has a unique continuous extension $\bar{p}$ to $\tilde{E}$ by (1.6). Let $U = \{x \in E : p(x) \leq 1\}$; then the set $\bar{U} = \{x \in \tilde{E} : \bar{p}(x) \leq 1\}$ is the closure of $U$ in $\tilde{E}$. It follows from (I, 1.5) that $\tilde{E}$ is a locally convex space (since $\bar{U}$ is convex) and that the family $\{\bar{p} : p \in \mathcal{P}\}$ generates the topology of $\tilde{E}$.
