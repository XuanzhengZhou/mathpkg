Let $E$ be a vector space and let $A \neq \emptyset$ be a subset of $E$. The convex hull (the convex, circled hull) of $A$ consists of all finite sums $\sum_i \lambda_i x_i$ where $x_i \in A$, $\lambda_i \geq 0$ (respectively $\lambda_i \in \mathbb{R}$) and $\sum_i \lambda_i = 1$ (respectively $\sum_i |\lambda_i| \leq 1$). Show that the convex hull of $A$ is the intersection of all convex sets containing $A$, and similarly for the convex circled hull.
