In a locally convex space, the convex hull of a finite union of compact convex sets is itself compact. This result (Theorem 10.2) is used in the proof of Theorem 10.5 to show that the closed convex hull of a compact set retains compactness properties. The image of the compact product $\prod C_i \times \Delta_{n-1}$ under the continuous map $(x_1,\ldots,x_n,\lambda) \mapsto \sum \lambda_i x_i$ yields the compactness.
