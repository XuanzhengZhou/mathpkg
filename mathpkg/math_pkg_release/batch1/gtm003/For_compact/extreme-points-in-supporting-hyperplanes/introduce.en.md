Every closed real hyperplane that supports a compact convex subset $C$ of a locally convex space must contain at least one extreme point of $C$. This is a key step toward the Krein--Milman theorem. The proof uses Zorn's lemma on the family of all closed real linear manifolds contained in the given hyperplane and supporting $C$, ordered by reverse inclusion, and then shows that a minimal element must be a singleton.
