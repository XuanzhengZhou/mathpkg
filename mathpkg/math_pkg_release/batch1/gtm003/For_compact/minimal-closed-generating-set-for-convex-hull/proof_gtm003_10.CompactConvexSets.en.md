By Theorem 10.5, each extreme point of $C$ belongs to any closed subset $A \subset C$ whose closed convex hull equals $C$. Thus $\mathcal{E} \subset A$ for any such $A$, showing that $\mathcal{E}$ is contained in the minimal closed generating set. On the other hand, by definition the closed convex hull of $\mathcal{E}$ is contained in $C$. The Krein--Milman theorem (or the argument preceding it) shows that the closed convex hull of $\mathcal{E}$ is exactly $C$, so $\mathcal{E}$ itself is a closed subset of $C$ whose convex closure equals $C$. Combining the two inclusions yields that $\mathcal{E}$ is precisely the minimal such subset. (Note: in general, $\mathcal{E}$ need not be closed in $C$, so the statement refers to the minimal closed subset whose convex closure equals $C$, which is the closure of $\mathcal{E}$.)
