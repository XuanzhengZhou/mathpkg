For any compact convex set $C$ in a locally convex space, the set $\mathcal{E}$ of its extreme points is the smallest closed subset of $C$ whose closed convex hull recovers $C$. This follows from Theorem 10.5, which shows that extreme points of the closed convex hull must lie in the original compact set. The result is a precursor to the Krein--Milman theorem. However, $\mathcal{E}$ need not be closed in general; it is merely dense in $C$ in infinite-dimensional settings.
