This corollary to Gelfand's representation theorem establishes the continuous functional calculus for normal elements in a C*-algebra. It states that for a normal element $a$, there is an isometric *-isomorphism from the continuous functions on the spectrum of $a$ to the C*-subalgebra generated by $a$. The spectral mapping theorem follows: applying a continuous function to $a$ transforms the spectrum accordingly. This is a powerful generalization of the polynomial spectral mapping theorem.
