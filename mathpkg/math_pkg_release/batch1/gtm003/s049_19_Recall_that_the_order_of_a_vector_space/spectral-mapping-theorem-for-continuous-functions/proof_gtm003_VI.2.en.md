Since $a$ is normal, the $C^*$-subalgebra $B$ generated by $\{e, a, a^*\}$ is commutative. By Gelfand's theorem (2.2), $B$ is isometrically $*$-isomorphic to $\mathcal{C}(K)$ for some compact space $K$. The space $K$ can be identified with the maximal ideal space of $B$, and one shows that $K$ is homeomorphic to $\sigma(a)$ via the map $f \mapsto f(a)$ for characters $f$. This yields the isometric $*$-isomorphism $\Psi : \mathcal{C}(\sigma(a)) \to B$.

Denoting by $\sigma_C(x)$ the spectrum of $x$ viewed as an element of the unital $C^*$-algebra $C$, one sees that $X = \sigma_B(a)$ and, more generally, $\sigma_B(\Psi(f)) = f(X)$. To show that $\sigma_B(\Psi(f)) = \sigma_A(\Psi(f))$, assume $\Psi(f)$ is not invertible in $B$. Then $f$ is not invertible in $\mathcal{C}(X)$, thus $f(\xi) = 0$ for some $\xi \in X$. Choosing a sequence $(h_n)$ in $\mathcal{C}(X)$ with $0 \leq h_n \leq 1_X$, $h_n(\xi) = 1$, and $h_n(\eta) = 0$ when $|\eta - \xi| \geq 1/n$, we have $\lim \|f h_n\| = 0$. Applying $\Psi$ gives a sequence $(x_n) = (\Psi(h_n))$ of norm 1 with $\lim \Psi(f)x_n = 0$, so $\Psi(f)$ is not invertible in $A$. The assertion follows.
