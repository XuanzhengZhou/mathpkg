Use Exercise 24. The forward direction: in a spectral algebra, the order of self-adjoint elements is defined by the spectral measure. Conditions (i)-(iii) follow from the properties of the spectral measure. For the converse, the existence of such an order ensures that the algebra can be represented as $\mathcal{C}(X)$ for a suitable compact $X$ via the Gelfand transform, and the order summability condition ensures the spectral properties hold. See also Chapter IV, Exercise 40 for the definition of spectral algebra.
