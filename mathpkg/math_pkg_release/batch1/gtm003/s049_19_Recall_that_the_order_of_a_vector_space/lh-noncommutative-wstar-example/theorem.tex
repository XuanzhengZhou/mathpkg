The standard example of a non-commutative $C^*$-algebra is the operator space $\mathcal{L}(H)$, $H$ being a Hilbert space of dimension at least two. $\mathcal{L}(H)$ actually is a $W^*$-algebra. An example of a closed ideal in $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is given by the subspace of all compact operators; this ideal is proper if and only if $\dim H$ is infinite.
