By Birkhoff [1], Chapter XV, Theorem 1. The proof proceeds by analyzing the cone of the order-bound dual. Since $L$ is finite-dimensional and not Archimedean, the positive cone is not closed. Decompose $L$ as a direct sum of irreducible ordered spaces. The non-Archimedean component must be at least 2-dimensional and carries the lexicographic order. The remaining component has a closed (hence Archimedean) cone and is isomorphic to the canonical order on $\mathbf{R}^m$.
