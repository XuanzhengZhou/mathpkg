Birkhoff's classification theorem: every finite-dimensional non-Archimedean ordered vector space decomposes as a direct product of a lexicographically ordered component $\mathbf{R}_0^k$ (capturing the non-Archimedean part) and a canonically ordered component $\mathbf{R}_0^m$. The parameter $k \geq 2$ measures the degree of non-Archimedeanity.
