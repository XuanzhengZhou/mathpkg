Given a dual system $\langle F, G \rangle$ and a subspace $M$ of $F$, one can form a new dual system $\langle M, G/M^\circ \rangle$ where $M^\circ$ is the orthogonal of $M$ in $G$. The bilinear form descends from the original pairing because it is constant on cosets modulo $M^\circ$. This construction is fundamental for analyzing how duality interacts with subspaces and quotients, and serves as the basis for the projective-inductive duality theorem.
