For (1), apply Corollary 1 of Theorem 4.1 with the saturated family of all convex circled $\sigma(E', E)$-compact subsets of $E'$ generating $\tau(E, E')$ on $E$. Under the canonical surjection $\phi: E \to F$, these map to the corresponding family in $F'$, generating $\tau(F, F')$, so the quotient topology of $\tau(E, E')$ equals $\tau(F, F')$ by Theorem 4.1.

For (2), if $\tau(E, E')|_M$ is metrizable, then by the uniqueness of the Mackey topology among consistent topologies that are metrizable on the subspace (since $\tau(M, M')$ is the finest topology consistent with $\langle M, M'\rangle$ and $\tau(E, E')|_M$ is also consistent with this duality when restricted), the two topologies must coincide. The rephrasing follows immediately.
