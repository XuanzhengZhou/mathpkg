Let $M$ be a subspace and let $F$ be a quotient space of the locally convex space $E$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The Mackey topology $\tau(F, F')$ is the quotient topology of $\tau(E, E')$.
\item If the restriction of $\tau(E, E')$ to $M$ is metrizable, then it is identical with $\tau(M, M')$.
\end{enumerate}
Equivalently, every separated quotient of a Mackey space is a Mackey space, and every metrizable subspace of a Mackey space is a Mackey space.
