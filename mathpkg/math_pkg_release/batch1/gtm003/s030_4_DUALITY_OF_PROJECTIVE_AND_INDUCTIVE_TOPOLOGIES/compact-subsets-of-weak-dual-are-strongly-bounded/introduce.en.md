Proposition 5.1 states that weakly compact convex circled subsets of the dual are automatically strongly bounded. The proof is simple: the polar of such a compact set is a neighborhood for the Mackey topology $\tau(E, E')$, hence absorbs all bounded subsets of $E$, which is equivalent to strong boundedness in the dual. This relationship plays a key role in comparing the various families of bounded sets in $E'$.
