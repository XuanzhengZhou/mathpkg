Let $C$ be a convex, circled, $\sigma(E', E)$-compact subset of $E'$. Its polar $C^\circ$ in $E$ is a neighborhood of $0$ for the Mackey topology $\tau(E, E')$, by definition of the Mackey topology (it is generated by polars of all such compact sets). Hence $C^\circ$ absorbs every bounded subset of $E$. By the bipolar theorem, $C = C^{\circ\bullet}$ is the polar of $C^\circ$, which implies that $C$ is absorbed by the polars of all bounded subsets of $E$ — precisely the condition for $C$ to be bounded in the strong topology $\beta(E', E)$.
