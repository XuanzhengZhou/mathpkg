This corollary of Theorem 4.1 specializes the general $\mathfrak{S}$-topology duality to weak topologies. The weak topology on the induced dual system $\langle M, G/M^\circ \rangle$ is always the subspace topology of $\sigma(F, G)$. However, for the weak topology on the quotient $G/M^\circ$ to equal the quotient of $\sigma(G, F)$, the subspace $M$ must be closed in $F$, reflecting the fact that weak topology separates points only on the Hausdorff quotient.
