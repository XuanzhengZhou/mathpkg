Let $\{\langle F_\alpha, G_\alpha \rangle : \alpha \in A\}$ be a family of dual systems over $\mathbf{K}$. Let
$$F = \prod_{\alpha \in A} F_\alpha, \qquad G = \bigoplus_{\alpha \in A} G_\alpha$$
and define a bilinear form $f$ on $F \times G$ by
$$f(x, y) = \sum_{\alpha} \langle x_\alpha, y_\alpha \rangle$$
(the sum has at most finitely many non-zero terms). For each $\alpha \in A$, let $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$ be a saturated family of $\sigma(G_\alpha, F_\alpha)$-bounded subsets of $G_\alpha$, and let
$$\mathfrak{S} = \left\{ S \subset G : S \subset \bigoplus_{\alpha \in H} S_\alpha,\; S_\alpha \in \mathfrak{S}_\alpha,\; H \subset A \text{ finite} \right\}.$$
Then $\mathfrak{S}$ is a saturated family of $\sigma(G, F)$-bounded subsets of $G$, and the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology on $F$ is the product of the $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$-topologies on $F_\alpha$ for $\alpha \in A$. Dually, if $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$ are families on $F_\alpha$, the corresponding $\mathfrak{S}$-topology on $G$ is the locally convex direct sum of the $\mathfrak{S}_\alpha$-topologies on $G_\alpha$.
