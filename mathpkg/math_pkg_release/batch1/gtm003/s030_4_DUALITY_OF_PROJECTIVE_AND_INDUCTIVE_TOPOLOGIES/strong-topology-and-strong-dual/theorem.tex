Let $E$ be a locally convex space with dual $E'$, and consider the dual system $\langle E, E' \rangle$. Let $\mathfrak{B}$ be the family of all $\sigma(E, E')$-bounded subsets of $E$ (respectively of $E'$).

\begin{itemize}
\item The topology $\beta(E, E')$ on $E$ is the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology where $\mathfrak{S}$ is the family of all $\sigma(E', E)$-bounded subsets of $E'$.
\item The topology $\beta(E', E)$ on $E'$ is the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology where $\mathfrak{S}$ is the family of all $\sigma(E, E')$-bounded subsets of $E$.
\end{itemize}

These are called the \textbf{strong topologies} on $E$ and $E'$. The space $(E', \beta(E', E))$ is called the \textbf{strong dual} of $E$.

Notation:
\begin{itemize}
\item $E_\sigma$, $E_\tau$, $E_\beta$ denote $E$ under the topologies $\sigma(E, E')$, $\tau(E, E')$, $\beta(E, E')$ respectively.
\item $E'_\sigma$, $E'_\tau$, $E'_\beta$ denote $E'$ under $\sigma(E', E)$, $\tau(E', E)$, $\beta(E', E)$ respectively.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Caution:} In general, $(E_\beta)' \neq E'$ and $(E'_\beta)' \neq E$.
