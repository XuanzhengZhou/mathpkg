The strong topology $\beta(E, E')$ on a locally convex space is the topology of uniform convergence on all weakly bounded subsets of the dual. Unlike the weak and Mackey topologies, the strong topology is not necessarily consistent with the duality $\langle E, E' \rangle$, meaning the dual of $(E, \beta(E, E'))$ may be strictly larger than $E'$. The notation $E_\sigma, E_\tau, E_\beta$ (and corresponding dual notation) provides a convenient shorthand for discussing these three fundamental polar topologies on a locally convex space.
