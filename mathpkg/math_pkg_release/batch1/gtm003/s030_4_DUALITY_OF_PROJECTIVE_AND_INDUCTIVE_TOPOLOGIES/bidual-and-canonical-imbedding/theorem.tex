Let $E$ be a locally convex space. The dual of the strong dual $E'_\beta = (E', \beta(E', E))$ is called the \textbf{bidual} of $E$ and is denoted by $E''$.

Identifying $E$ and $E''$ with subspaces of the algebraic dual $E'^*$ of $E'$ via the canonical evaluation, we have the inclusions
$$E \subset E'' \subset E'^*,$$
where generally both inclusions are proper.

The algebraic isomorphism of $E$ into $E''$ is called the \textbf{canonical imbedding} (or \textbf{evaluation map}) of $E$ into $E''$, and is explicitly given by $x \mapsto f_x$, where $f_x$ is the linear form on $E'$ defined by
$$f_x(x') = \langle x, x' \rangle \qquad (x' \in E').$$

\begin{itemize}
\item The \textbf{strong bidual} is $E''$ endowed with the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology where $\mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{B}$ is the family of strongly bounded subsets of $E'$.
\item The \textbf{natural topology} on $E''$ is the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology where $\mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{E}$ is the family of equicontinuous subsets of $E'$; this topology always induces the original topology on $E$.
\item The natural topology is coarser than the strong topology on $E''$; equality holds if and only if $E$ is infrabarreled.
\end{itemize}
