The bidual $E''$ of a locally convex space $E$ is the dual of its strong dual. Since the strong topology $\beta(E', E)$ is generally not consistent with the duality $\langle E, E' \rangle$, $E''$ is typically larger than $E$. The canonical imbedding $x \mapsto (x' \mapsto \langle x, x' \rangle)$ maps $E$ injectively into $E''$. The natural topology on $E''$ (generated by equicontinuous sets) always induces the original topology on $E$, making it useful for embedding theory. Equality of the natural and strong topologies characterizes infrabarreled spaces.
