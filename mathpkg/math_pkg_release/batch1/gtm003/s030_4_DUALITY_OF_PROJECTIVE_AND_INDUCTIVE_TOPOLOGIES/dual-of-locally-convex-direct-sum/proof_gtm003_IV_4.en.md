Apply Theorem 4.3 to the dual system formed by the strong duals. By duality, the locally convex direct sum $E = \bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha$ is the dual of $\prod_\alpha E'_\alpha$ (in the sense of Mackey duality). The algebraic isomorphism $E' \cong \prod_\alpha E'_\alpha$ follows from the universal property of the direct sum: a linear form on $\bigoplus_\alpha E_\alpha$ is continuous precisely when its restriction to each $E_\alpha$ is continuous, giving a family $(f_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ with $f_\alpha \in E'_\alpha$. The topological identities follow by applying Theorem 4.2 with the roles of product and direct sum interchanged.
