Let $B$ be a convex, circled, $\sigma(E', E)$-bounded subset of $E'$. Its polar $B^\circ$ in $E$ is a barrel in $E$ by Theorem 1.6. Hence $B^\circ$ absorbs every bounded subset of $E$ that is convex, circled, and complete, by (II, 8.5).

Since $E$ is quasi-complete, the bounded subsets of $E$ with these properties (convex, circled, complete) form a fundamental family of bounded sets in $E$. Therefore $B^\circ$ absorbs all bounded subsets of $E$.

By the bipolar theorem, $B = B^{\circ\bullet}$ is the polar of $B^\circ$. Since $B^\circ$ absorbs all bounded sets in $E$, its polar $B$ is absorbed by the polars of all bounded subsets of $E$, which is precisely the condition for $B$ to be strongly bounded in $E'_\beta$.
