An integral linear map is the linear map $u \colon E \to F'_\sigma$ (or its adjoint $u' \colon F \to E'_\sigma$) obtained from an integral bilinear form $v \in \mathcal{J}(E, F)$ through the canonical isomorphism between bilinear forms and linear maps with values in weak duals. Every such map admits a concrete integral representation: for each $x \in E$, the value $u(x)$ is a weak integral of $\langle x, x' \rangle y'$ against a Radon measure $\mu$ on a product $S \times T$ of weakly closed equicontinuous sets. Integral maps form a natural bridge between the abstract theory of locally convex tensor products and the concrete theory of vector-valued integration.
