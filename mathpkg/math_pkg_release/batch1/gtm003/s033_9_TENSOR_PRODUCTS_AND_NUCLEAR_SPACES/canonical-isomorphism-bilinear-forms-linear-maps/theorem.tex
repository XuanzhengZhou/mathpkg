Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces with respective duals $E', F'$. If $v$ is a separately continuous bilinear form on $E \times F$, then the formula
$$v(x, y) = \langle ux, y \rangle = \langle x, u'y \rangle$$
defines a continuous linear map $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F'_\sigma)$ with adjoint $u' \in \mathcal{L}(F, E'_\sigma)$. The mappings $v \mapsto u$ and $v \mapsto u'$ are algebraic isomorphisms of $\mathcal{B}(E, F)$ onto $\mathcal{L}(E, F'_\sigma)$ and of $\mathcal{B}(E, F)$ onto $\mathcal{L}(F, E'_\sigma)$, respectively. These isomorphisms are called canonical.

Under $v \mapsto u$, the space $\mathcal{B}(E, F)$ of continuous bilinear forms on $E \times F$ is carried onto the subspace of $\mathcal{L}(E, F'_\sigma)$ each of whose elements maps a suitable $0$-neighborhood in $E$ onto an equicontinuous set in $F'$.
