An integral bilinear form is a continuous bilinear form on $E \times F$ belonging to the dual of the completed tensor product $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ equipped with the topology of bi-equicontinuous convergence. The space $\mathcal{J}(E, F)$ of all such forms is a subspace of $\mathcal{B}(E, F)$. Every equicontinuous subset of $\mathcal{J}(E, F)$ admits a representation via an adjoint map from a space of Radon measures, reflecting the integral nature of these forms. This characterization connects the abstract duality theory of tensor products with concrete measure-theoretic descriptions.
