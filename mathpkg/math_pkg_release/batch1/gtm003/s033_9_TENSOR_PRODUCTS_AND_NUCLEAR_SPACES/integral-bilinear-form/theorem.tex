Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces and let $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ denote the completion of the algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$ under the topology of bi-equicontinuous convergence. The dual of $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ is denoted by $\mathcal{J}(E, F)$ and is a subspace of $\mathcal{B}(E, F)$, the space of continuous bilinear forms on $E \times F$. The elements of $\mathcal{J}(E, F)$ are called integral bilinear forms on $E \times F$.

Every equicontinuous set $A \subset \mathcal{J}(E, F)$ can be represented as the image under an adjoint map $\psi'$ of an equicontinuous subset of $\mathcal{M}(S \times T)$, where $S \subset E'$ and $T \subset F'$ are weakly compact equicontinuous sets and $\mathcal{M}(S \times T)$ denotes the space of Radon measures on $S \times T$.
