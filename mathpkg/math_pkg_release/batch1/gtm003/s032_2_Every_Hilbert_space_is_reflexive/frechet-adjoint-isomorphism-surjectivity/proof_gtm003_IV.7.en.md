Since $u'$ is an isomorphism of $F'_\beta$ into $E'_\beta$, the range $u'(F')$ is closed in $E'_\beta$, hence closed in $E'_\sigma$. By the corollary of (7.3), $u$ is a weak homomorphism; by (7.7), $u$ is a topological homomorphism and $u(E)$ is closed in $F$. The isomorphism property of $u'$ forces $u(E) = F$, for otherwise the polar of $F/u(E)$ would give a non-trivial subspace of $F'$ annihilated by $u'$, contradicting injectivity.
