$(a) \Rightarrow (b_2)$ and $(b_1) \Rightarrow (b_2)$: For each $y' \in F'$, $x \mapsto \langle ux, y' \rangle$ is a continuous, hence weakly continuous linear form on $E$, which is equivalent to the weak continuity of $u$.

$(b_2) \Rightarrow (b_3)$: Immediate from (2.1).

$(b_3) \Rightarrow (b_4)$: Since $u(E) \subset F$ and $u$ is the adjoint of $v$ with respect to the dualities $\langle E, E' \rangle$ and $\langle F, F' \rangle$, $v$ is continuous for $\sigma(F', F)$ and $\sigma(E', E)$ by (2.1).

$(b_4) \Rightarrow (b_1)$: $v$ maps convex, circled, compact subsets of $F'_\sigma$ into such subsets of $E'_\sigma$, hence is continuous for the Mackey topologies.

$(b_4) \Rightarrow (c)$: Strong continuity follows from weak continuity and the definition of strong topologies.

If $F$ is semi-reflexive, $(c) \Rightarrow (b_4)$ since the strong and Mackey duals coincide. If $E$ is a Mackey space, $(b_1) \Rightarrow (a)$ since the given topology on $E$ coincides with the Mackey topology.
