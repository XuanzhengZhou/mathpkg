Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces, let $u$ be a linear map of $E$ into $F$, and let $v$ be the adjoint of $u$. Consider: (a) $u$ is continuous. (b_1) $u$ is continuous for the Mackey topologies. (b_2) $u$ is weakly continuous. (b_3) $v$ is defined on all of $F$. (b_4) $v$ is continuous on $F_\sigma$ into $E_\sigma$. (c) $v$ is continuous on $F_\beta$ into $E_\beta$. Then $(b_1) \Leftrightarrow (b_2) \Leftrightarrow (b_3) \Leftrightarrow (b_4)$, $(a) \Rightarrow (b_1)$, $(b_4) \Rightarrow (c)$. If $F$ is semi-reflexive, $(c) \Rightarrow (b_4)$. If $E$ is a Mackey space, $(b_1) \Rightarrow (a)$.
