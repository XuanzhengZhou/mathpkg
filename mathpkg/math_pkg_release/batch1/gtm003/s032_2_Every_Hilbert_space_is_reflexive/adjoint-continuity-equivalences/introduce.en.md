Theorem 7.4 establishes fundamental implications between continuity of a linear map $ and its adjoint $ under various topologies. Weak continuity and Mackey continuity are equivalent, and they are equivalent to $ being everywhere defined and weakly continuous. For semi-reflexive $, strong continuity of $ implies weak continuity; for Mackey spaces $, weak continuity of $ implies continuity.
