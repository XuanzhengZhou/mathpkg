Let $p$ be the projection of $E$ onto $M$ vanishing on $N$. Since $M$ is $B_r$-complete by (8.2), it suffices to show that $p$ has closed graph. The graph of $p$ is $\{(x, px) : x \in E\} = \{(m+n, m) : m \in M, n \in N\}$. Since $M$ and $N$ are closed and $E$ is the algebraic direct sum, the graph is closed. By the closed graph theorem (8.5), $p$ is continuous, hence the sum is topological.
