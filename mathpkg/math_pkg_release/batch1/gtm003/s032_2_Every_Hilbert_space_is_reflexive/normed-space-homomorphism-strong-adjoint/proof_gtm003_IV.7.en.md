If $u$ is a homomorphism and $N = u^{-1}(0)$, then the biunivocal map $u_0$ associated with $u$ is an isomorphism of $E/N$ onto $M \subset F$, where $M = u(E)$. It follows that $u'_0$ is a strong isomorphism of $M'_\beta$ onto $N^\circ$, hence $u'$ is a strong homomorphism. The converse follows from the fact that for complete $E$, the strong homomorphism property of $u'$ implies $u$ is a weak homomorphism, and completeness together with the closed graph theorem yields that $u$ is a homomorphism.
