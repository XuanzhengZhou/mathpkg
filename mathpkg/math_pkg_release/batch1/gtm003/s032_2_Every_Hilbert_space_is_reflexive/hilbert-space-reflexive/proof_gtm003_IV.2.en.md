Every Hilbert space $H$ is isomorphic with a space $l_d^2$, where $d$ is the Hilbert dimension of $H$ (Chapter II, Section 2, Example 5). The spaces $l_d^2$ are reflexive as special cases of $L^p(\mu)$ with $p = 2$, for $1 < p < +\infty$. Hence $H$ is reflexive.
