As in the foregoing proof, the general case can be reduced to the case in which $u$ is one-to-one. For $N = u^{-1}(0)$ is closed in $E$ and the bi-univocal map of $E/N$ into $F$ associated with $u$ has a closed graph by (7.2), and $E/N$ is barreled; it can moreover be assumed that $u(E)$ is dense in $F$, since by (8.2), each closed subspace of a $B_r$-complete space is $B_r$-complete.

Suppose, hence, that $u$ is one-to-one onto a dense subspace of $F$. Then since $u$ is closed, $u^{-1}$ is a closed linear map, densely defined in $F$, onto $E$, which is nearly open, since $E$ is barreled; by (8.4) $u^{-1}$ is open, hence $u$ continuous.
