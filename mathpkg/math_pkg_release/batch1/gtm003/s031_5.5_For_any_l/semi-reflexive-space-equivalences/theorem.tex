For any locally convex space $E$, the following assertions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $E$ is semi-reflexive.
\item[(b)] Every $\beta(E', E)$-continuous linear form on $E'$ is continuous for $\sigma(E', E)$.
\item[(c)] $E'_\tau$ is barreled.
\item[(d)] Every bounded subset of $E$ is relatively $\sigma(E, E')$-compact.
\item[(e)] $E$ is quasi-complete under $\sigma(E, E')$.
\end{enumerate}
