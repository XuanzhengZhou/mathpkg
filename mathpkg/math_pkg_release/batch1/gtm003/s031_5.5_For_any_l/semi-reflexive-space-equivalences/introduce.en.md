A locally convex space $E$ is called semi-reflexive if the canonical evaluation map from $E$ into its bidual $E''$ is surjective. This theorem provides five equivalent characterizations that are fundamental to the duality theory of locally convex spaces. The equivalences connect semi-reflexivity to the coincidence of strong and weak continuous linear forms on the dual, the barreledness of the Mackey dual $E'_\tau$, the relative weak compactness of bounded sets in $E$, and weak quasi-completeness of $E$.
