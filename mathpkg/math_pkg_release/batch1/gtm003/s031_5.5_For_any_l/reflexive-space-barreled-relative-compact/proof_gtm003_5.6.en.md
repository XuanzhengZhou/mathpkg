If $E$ is reflexive, then $E$ is barreled and semi-reflexive by definition; by (5.5)(d) every bounded subset of $E$ is relatively $\sigma(E, E')$-compact.

Conversely, if $E$ is barreled and every bounded subset of $E$ is relatively $\sigma(E, E')$-compact, then by (5.5)(d) $E$ is semi-reflexive. Since $E$ is barreled, the original topology $\tau(E, E')$ coincides with the strong topology $\beta(E, E')$, and thus $E$ is reflexive.
