The inductive limit is the categorical colimit in the category of locally convex spaces. It generalises the union topology: given a directed family of subspaces whose union is the whole space, the inductive limit topology on the union is the finest locally convex topology making all inclusion maps continuous. The general construction proceeds via a quotient of the locally convex direct sum, analogous to the construction of direct limits of modules.
