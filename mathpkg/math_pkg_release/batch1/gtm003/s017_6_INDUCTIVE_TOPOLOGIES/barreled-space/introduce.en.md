A barreled space is a locally convex space in which every barrel is a neighborhood of zero. This class of spaces is central in functional analysis because it includes all Frechet spaces and Banach spaces, and because the defining property is preserved under the formation of inductive topologies, quotient topologies, and arbitrary products. The Banach-Steinhaus theorem extends naturally to barreled spaces.
