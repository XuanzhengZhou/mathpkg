The order of $E$ is Archimedean, since $C$ is closed in $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ (by definition of ordered t.v.s.). If $\mathfrak{T} = \mathfrak{T}_0$, then $C$ is normal by Theorem 6.2.

Conversely, if $C$ is normal, then $\mathfrak{T}$ is coarser than $\mathfrak{T}_0$, because $\mathfrak{T}_0$ is the finest locally convex topology for which $C$ is normal (by Theorem 6.2). On the other hand, $[-e, e]$ is a barrel in $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ (we can suppose that $K = \mathbb{R}$). Since $E$ is a Banach space, barrels are neighborhoods of $0$, so the gauge of $[-e, e]$—which generates $\mathfrak{T}_0$—generates a topology coarser than $\mathfrak{T}$. Thus $\mathfrak{T} = \mathfrak{T}_0$.
