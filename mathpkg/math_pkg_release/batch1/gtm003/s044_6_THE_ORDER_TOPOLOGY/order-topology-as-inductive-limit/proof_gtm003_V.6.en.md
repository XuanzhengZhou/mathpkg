The assumption implies by Theorem 6.2 that each of the spaces $L_a$ ($a \in H$) is normable and complete and hence barreled. Since $L = \bigcup_{a \in H} L_a$ and the family $\{L_a : a \in H\}$ is directed under inclusion (if $a_1, a_2 \in H$, there exists $a \in H$ with $a_1, a_2 \leq a$), $(L, \mathfrak{T}_0)$ is the locally convex inductive limit of the $L_a$. By Proposition II.7.2, the locally convex inductive limit of barreled spaces is barreled, hence $(L, \mathfrak{T}_0)$ is barreled.
