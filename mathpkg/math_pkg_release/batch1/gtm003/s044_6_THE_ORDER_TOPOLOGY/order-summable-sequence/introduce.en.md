An order summable sequence generalizes the notion of absolute summability from real numbers to an arbitrary ordered vector space. A sequence of positive elements is order summable if the partial sums possess a supremum. This concept is used in Theorem 6.2 to characterize completeness of the order topology for Archimedean ordered vector spaces with an order unit.
