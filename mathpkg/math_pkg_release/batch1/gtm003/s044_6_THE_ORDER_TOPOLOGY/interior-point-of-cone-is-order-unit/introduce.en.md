This proposition establishes the equivalence between topological interior points of the positive cone and algebraic order units. Interior points of $C$ in any ordered t.v.s. are order units, and conversely, every order unit becomes an interior point of $C$ under the order topology $\mathfrak{T}_0$. This connection explains why ordered Banach spaces with normal cones having non-empty interior (such as $\mathcal{C}(X)$ and $L^\infty(\mu)$) are particularly well-behaved with respect to the order topology.
