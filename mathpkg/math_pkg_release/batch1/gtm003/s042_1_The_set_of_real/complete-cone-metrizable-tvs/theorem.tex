Let $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ be a metrizable topological vector space over $\mathbb{R}$, let $C$ be a cone in $E$ which is complete, and let $\{U_n : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ be a neighborhood base of $0$ consisting of closed, circled sets such that
$$U_{n+1} + U_{n+1} \subset U_n \qquad (n \in \mathbb{N}).$$
Then the sets
$$V_n = (U_n \cap C) - (U_n \cap C) \qquad (n \in \mathbb{N})$$
form a $0$-neighborhood base for a topology $\mathfrak{T}_1$ on $E_1 = C - C$ such that $(E_1, \mathfrak{T}_1)$ is a complete metrizable topological vector space over $\mathbb{R}$.
