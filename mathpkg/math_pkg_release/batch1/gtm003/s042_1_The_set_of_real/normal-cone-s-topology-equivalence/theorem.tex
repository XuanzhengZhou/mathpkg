Let $E$ be a locally convex space, let $C$ be a cone in $E$ with dual cone $C' \subset E'$, and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a saturated family of weakly bounded subsets of $E'$. If $C'$ is an $\mathfrak{S}$-cone, then the following three conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $C$ is normal for the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology.
\item[(b)] For any equicontinuous set $A \subset E'$, there exists an equicontinuous set $B \subset C'$ such that $A \subset B - B$.
\item[(c)] The topology of $E$ is the topology of uniform convergence on the equicontinuous subsets of $C'$.
\end{enumerate}
