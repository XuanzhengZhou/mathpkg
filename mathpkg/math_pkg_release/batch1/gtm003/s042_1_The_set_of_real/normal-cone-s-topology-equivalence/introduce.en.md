This theorem characterizes when a cone $C$ in a locally convex space is normal for the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology, under the hypothesis that the dual cone $C'$ is an $\mathfrak{S}$-cone. The equivalence connects normality with two concrete topological conditions: every equicontinuous set in $E'$ can be absorbed by the difference of an equicontinuous set in $C'$, and the topology of $E$ coincides with the topology of uniform convergence on equicontinuous subsets of $C'$.
