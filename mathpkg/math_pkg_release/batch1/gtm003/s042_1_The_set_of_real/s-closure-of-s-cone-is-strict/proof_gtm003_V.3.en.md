Consider the cone $C = -H^0$ (the negative polar of $H$). Since $H$ is an $\mathfrak{S}$-cone and $\mathfrak{S}$ is a saturated family of $\sigma(E',E)$-relatively compact sets covering $E'$, the cone $C$ is normal for the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology by Theorem 3.3, and this $\mathfrak{S}$-topology is consistent with the dual pairing $\langle E, E' \rangle$ (see IV, 3.2). Moreover, by the bipolar theorem (IV, 1.5), we have $C' = \bar{H}$, where $\bar{H}$ denotes the $\sigma(E',E)$-closure of $H$. Since $C'$ is the dual cone of a normal cone, it is a strict $\mathfrak{S}$-cone. Therefore $\bar{H} = C'$ is a strict $\mathfrak{S}$-cone.
