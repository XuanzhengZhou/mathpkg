The decomposition property, introduced by Bonsall, describes a normed space where every element in the closed unit ball can be approximated arbitrarily well by differences of cone elements whose norms are uniformly bounded. This property plays an important role in the theory of ordered vector spaces and relates to the structure of cones and their duals in topological vector spaces.
