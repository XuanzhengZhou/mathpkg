If a Hausdorff topological vector space has a bounded neighborhood of the origin, then scaling that neighborhood by a null sequence of scalars yields a countable neighborhood base at zero, which by Theorem 6.1 implies metrizability. The proof is elegantly simple: given a bounded $0$-neighborhood $V$ and $\lambda_n \to 0$, the sets $\lambda_n V$ form the required countable base because any circled $0$-neighborhood absorbs $V$ (by boundedness) and a small enough scalar pushes $\lambda_n V$ inside it. The converse fails: the countable product of one-dimensional spaces is metrizable but not locally bounded.
