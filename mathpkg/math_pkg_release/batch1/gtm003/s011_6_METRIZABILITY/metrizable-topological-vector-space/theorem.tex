A topological vector space $(L, \mathfrak{T})$ is called \textbf{metrizable} if its topology $\mathfrak{T}$ is metrizable, that is, if there exists a metric on $L$ whose open balls form a base for $\mathfrak{T}$.

The uniformity generated by such a metric need not be translation-invariant and can hence be distinct from the uniformity $\mathfrak{N}$ associated with $\mathfrak{T}$ by the standard construction (1.4). However, any uniformity notions employed in connection with a topological vector space (metrizable or not) refer to the uniformity $\mathfrak{N}$.
