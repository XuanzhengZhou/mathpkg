This theorem states that a Hausdorff topological space admits a uniformity compatible with its topology if and only if it is completely regular (Tychonoff). This fundamental result characterizes exactly which topological spaces can be viewed as uniform spaces. All topological vector spaces that are Hausdorff are completely regular and hence uniformisable.
