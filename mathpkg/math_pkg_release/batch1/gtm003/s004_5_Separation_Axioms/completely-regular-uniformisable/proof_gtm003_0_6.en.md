The text states this theorem without proof. The standard proof: If $X$ is uniformisable with uniformity $\mathfrak{W}$, then for any closed set $A$ and point $x \notin A$, one can use the uniformity to construct a uniformly continuous pseudometric, and from it a continuous function $f: X \to [0,1]$ with $f(x) = 0$ and $f(A) = \{1\}$. Conversely, if $X$ is completely regular, the family of all continuous functions $f: X \to [0,1]$ generates the coarsest uniformity making them all uniformly continuous (via the product uniformity on $[0,1]^{C(X,[0,1])}$), which is compatible with the topology.
