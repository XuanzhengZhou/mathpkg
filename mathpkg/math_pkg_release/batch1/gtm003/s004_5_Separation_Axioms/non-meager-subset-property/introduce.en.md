This proposition states that if a subset of a topological space is non-meager (of second category) in the ambient space, then it is also non-meager with respect to its own subspace topology. However, the converse situation can occur: a subspace may be a Baire space in its own right while simultaneously being nowhere dense in the larger space. This highlights the subtle interplay between relative and intrinsic category notions.
