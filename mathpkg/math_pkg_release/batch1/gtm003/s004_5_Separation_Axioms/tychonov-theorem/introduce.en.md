Tychonov's Theorem states that the topological product of an arbitrary family of compact spaces is compact. This is one of the most important theorems in general topology and is equivalent to the Axiom of Choice. In the context of topological vector spaces, Tychonov's theorem underpins the Banach-Alaoglu theorem on weak-* compactness of the unit ball in the dual space.
