The continuous image of a compact space is compact (assuming the codomain is Hausdorff). This is one of the most fundamental preservation properties of compactness. The proof is elementary: given an open cover of $f(X)$, its preimage under $f$ is an open cover of $X$, which has a finite subcover by compactness; the images of this finite subcover yield a finite subcover of $f(X)$.
