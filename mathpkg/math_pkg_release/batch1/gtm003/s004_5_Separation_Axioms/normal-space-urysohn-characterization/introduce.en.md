This theorem (Urysohn's Lemma) characterizes normal spaces via the existence of continuous separating functions: a Hausdorff space is normal exactly when any two disjoint closed sets can be separated by a continuous real-valued function taking values 0 and 1 respectively. This result is central to general topology and is the foundation for the Tietze Extension Theorem.
