An algebraic direct sum $L = M + N$ is topological if and only if the natural mapping from the quotient $L/M$ to the complementary subspace $N$ is an isomorphism of topological vector spaces. This connects the direct sum concept with quotient spaces and provides a useful criterion for checking topological direct sum decompositions.
