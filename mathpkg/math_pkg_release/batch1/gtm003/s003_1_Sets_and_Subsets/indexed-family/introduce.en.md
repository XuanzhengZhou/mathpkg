An indexed family $\{x_\alpha: \alpha \in A\}$ in a set $X$ is a mapping $\alpha \mapsto x_\alpha$ from an index set $A$ to $X$. This concept generalizes the notion of a sequence (where $A = \mathbb{N}$) to arbitrary index sets. Families are fundamental for constructing products, sums, nets, and for describing collections of subsets such as open covers or neighborhood bases in topology.
