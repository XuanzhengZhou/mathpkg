A mapping $f: X \rightarrow Y$ is bijective if it is both injective (one-to-one) and surjective (onto). A bijection establishes a perfect one-to-one correspondence between the domain and codomain, guaranteeing the existence of a two-sided inverse $f^{-1}: Y \rightarrow X$. In set theory, two sets have the same cardinality precisely when a bijection exists between them.
