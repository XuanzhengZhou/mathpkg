A subset $B$ of a topological space is dense relative to $A$ if every point of $A$ can be approximated arbitrarily well by points of $B$, meaning $A$ is contained in the closure of $B$. The subset $B$ is dense in $A$ when additionally $B \subset A$. Dense subsets are fundamental in analysis and topology: a space is separable if it contains a countable dense subset. Many extension theorems rely on the ability to uniquely extend continuous functions defined on a dense subset to the whole space.
