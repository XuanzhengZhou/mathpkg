The closure $\bar{A}$ of a subset $A$ of a topological space is the smallest closed set containing $A$. It is the intersection of all closed supersets of $A$. The closure operator $A \mapsto \bar{A}$ is idempotent, monotone, and satisfies $\overline{A \cup B} = \bar{A} \cup \bar{B}$. A set is closed precisely when it equals its own closure. The closure consists of all points that are adherent to $A$.
