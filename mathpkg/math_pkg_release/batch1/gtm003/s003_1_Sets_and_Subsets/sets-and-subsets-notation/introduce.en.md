The standard notation of set theory includes membership $x \in X$, subset inclusion $X \subset Y$, set equality, and set-builder notation $\{x \in X: (p)x\}$. The complement $Y \sim X$ of $X$ relative to $Y$, the empty set $\varnothing$, and the singleton $\{x\}$ are fundamental constructs. Logical implication $(p_1) \Rightarrow (p_2)$ and equivalence $(p_1) \Leftrightarrow (p_2)$ are defined as relations on propositions. The power set $\mathfrak{P}(X)$ is the set of all subsets of $X$.
