Let $X$ be a topological space, $\mathfrak{F}$ a filter (or filter base) on $X$, and $f: X \rightarrow Y$ a map into a topological space $Y$. Then $f$ is said to \text{converge to $y \in Y$ along $\mathfrak{F}$} if the filter generated by $f(\mathfrak{F})$ converges to $y$. In particular, $f$ is continuous at $x \in X$ if and only if $f$ converges to $y = f(x)$ along the neighborhood filter of $x$.
