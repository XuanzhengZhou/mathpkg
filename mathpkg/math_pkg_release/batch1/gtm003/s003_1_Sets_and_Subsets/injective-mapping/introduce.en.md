A mapping $f: X \rightarrow Y$ is injective (one-to-one, or biunivocal) if distinct elements of the domain have distinct images: $f(x_1) = f(x_2) \implies x_1 = x_2$. Injectivity ensures that the mapping does not collapse distinct points. An injective mapping admits a left inverse on its image and allows the domain to be identified with a subset of the codomain.
