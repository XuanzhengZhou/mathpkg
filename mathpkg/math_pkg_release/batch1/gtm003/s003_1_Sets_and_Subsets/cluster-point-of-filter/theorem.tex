Given a filter $\mathfrak{F}$ on a topological space $X$ and $x \in X$, $x$ is a \text{cluster point} (\text{contact point}, \text{adherent point}) of $\mathfrak{F}$ if $x \in \bar{F}$ for each $F \in \mathfrak{F}$. A cluster point of a sequence (more generally, of a directed family) is a cluster point of the section filter of that family.
