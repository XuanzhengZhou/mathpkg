A cluster point (also called contact point or adherent point) of a filter $\mathfrak{F}$ on a topological space $X$ is a point $x \in X$ that belongs to the closure of every member of the filter. This generalizes the notion of a cluster point of a sequence: a point is a cluster point of a sequence exactly when it is a cluster point of the sequence's section filter. Unlike limit points, a filter may have many cluster points. Every limit point of a filter is also a cluster point, but the converse fails in non-Hausdorff spaces.
