Given an arbitrary family of topologies on a set $X$, there exists a unique finest topology that is coarser than every topology in the family. This topology, sometimes called the infimum or projective limit of the given topologies, has as its open sets precisely those subsets that are open in every topology of the family. This construction is dual to the notion of the coarsest topology finer than a family (the supremum topology). Both constructions are essential in the theory of projective and inductive limits of topological vector spaces.
