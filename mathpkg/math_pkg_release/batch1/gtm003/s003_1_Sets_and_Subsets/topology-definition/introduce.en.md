A topology on a set $X$ is defined by a collection $\mathfrak{G}$ of subsets (called open sets) that is closed under arbitrary unions and finite intersections. Equivalently, the empty set and $X$ are open, and the family of open sets is stable under arbitrary unions and finite intersections. The complements of open sets are called closed sets. This axiomatic definition abstracts the properties of open sets in metric spaces and forms the foundation of general topology.
