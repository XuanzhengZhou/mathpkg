A topological space is separable if it contains a countable subset whose closure is the entire space. Separability is a countability condition that plays an important role in functional analysis and topological vector spaces: many classical spaces such as $\mathbb{R}^n$, $L^p$ spaces for $1 \leq p < \infty$, and $C([0,1])$ are separable. In metric spaces, separability is equivalent to second countability (having a countable base), but in general topological spaces these properties are independent.
