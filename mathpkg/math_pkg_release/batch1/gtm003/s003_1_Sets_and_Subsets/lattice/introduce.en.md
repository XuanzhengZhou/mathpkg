A lattice is an ordered set $(X, \leq)$ in which every pair of elements has both a supremum (join) $\sup(x, y)$ and an infimum (meet) $\inf(x, y)$. Lattices are fundamental algebraic structures that appear throughout mathematics: the power set under inclusion, the integers under divisibility, and the open sets of a topology under inclusion are all lattices. Lattices support two idempotent, commutative, and associative binary operations.
