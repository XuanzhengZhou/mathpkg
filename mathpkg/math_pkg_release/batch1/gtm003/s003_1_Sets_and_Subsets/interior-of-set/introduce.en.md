The interior $\mathring{A}$ (or $\operatorname{int} A$) of a subset $A$ of a topological space is the largest open set contained in $A$. It is the union of all open subsets of $A$. The interior operator $A \mapsto \mathring{A}$ is idempotent, monotone, and satisfies $\mathring{(A \cap B)} = \mathring{A} \cap \mathring{B}$. A set is open precisely when it equals its own interior.
