The image of a subset $A \subset X$ under a mapping $f: X \rightarrow Y$ is the set $\{f(x): x \in A\}$ of all values taken by $f$ on elements of $A$. This extends the pointwise action of $f$ to a mapping from the power set $\mathfrak{P}(X)$ to $\mathfrak{P}(Y)$. The image of the whole domain $f(X)$ is called the range of $f$.
