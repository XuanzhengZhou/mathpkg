The restriction $f_A: A \rightarrow Y$ of a mapping $f: X \rightarrow Y$ to a subset $A \subset X$ is the mapping that agrees with $f$ on $A$. Conversely, $f$ is an extension of a mapping $g: A \rightarrow Y$ to the larger domain $X$. Restriction and extension are dual operations fundamental to the study of functions on subspaces, partitions of unity, and the extension of continuous or linear maps.
