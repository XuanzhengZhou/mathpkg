An elementary filter is the filter generated by the tail sets (or sections) of a sequence $\{x_n: n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ in a set $X$. It is the simplest and most concrete example of a filter, bridging the gap between classical sequential convergence and filter-based convergence in topology. The section filter of a sequence consists of all sets containing a tail $\{x_n: n \geq k\}$ for some $k$.
