The infimum $\inf A$ of a minorized subset $A$ of an ordered set $(X, \leq)$ is the greatest lower bound: it is a lower bound that is larger than or equal to every other lower bound. When it exists, the infimum is unique. Together with the supremum, the infimum forms the dual notion in order theory and is essential for defining lattices and complete lattices.
