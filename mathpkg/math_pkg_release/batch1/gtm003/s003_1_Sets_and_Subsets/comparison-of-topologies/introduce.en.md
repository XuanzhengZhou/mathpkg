Given two topologies on the same set, one topology is said to be finer than the other if it has more open sets (equivalently, more closed sets). The coarser topology has fewer open sets. The family of all topologies on a set forms a complete lattice under this partial order: the finest topology is the discrete topology (every subset is open), and the coarsest is the trivial topology (only $\varnothing$ and $X$ are open). This comparison is fundamental for studying weak and strong topologies on vector spaces.
