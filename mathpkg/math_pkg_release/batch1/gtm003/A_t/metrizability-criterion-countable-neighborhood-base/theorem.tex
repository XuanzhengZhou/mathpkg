A Hausdorff topological vector space $L$ is metrizable if and only if it possesses a countable neighborhood base of $0$. In this case, there exists a function $x \mapsto |x|$ from $L$ into $\mathbb{R}$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $|\lambda| \leq 1$ implies $|\lambda x| \leq |x|$ for all $x \in L$.
\item[(ii)] $|x + y| \leq |x| + |y|$ for all $x \in L$, $y \in L$.
\item[(iii)] $|x| = 0$ is equivalent with $x = 0$.
\item[(iv)] The metric $(x, y) \mapsto |x - y|$ generates the topology of $L$.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover, since the metric $(x,y) \mapsto |x-y|$ is translation-invariant, it generates also the uniformity of the t.v.s. $L$.
