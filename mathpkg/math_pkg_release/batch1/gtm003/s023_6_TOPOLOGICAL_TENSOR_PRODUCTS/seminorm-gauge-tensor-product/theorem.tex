Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces, let $p, q$ be continuous semi-norms on $E, F$ respectively, and set $U = \{x \in E: p(x) < 1\}$, $V = \{y \in F: q(y) < 1\}$.

Define the **tensor product semi-norm** $r = p \otimes q$ on $E \otimes F$ by
$$r(u) = \inf \left\{ \sum_i p(x_i) q(y_i) : u = \sum_i x_i \otimes y_i \right\}.$$

Then $r$ is a semi-norm on $E \otimes F$ and is precisely the gauge (Minkowski functional) of $\Gamma(U \otimes V)$:
$$r(u) = \inf\{\lambda > 0 : u \in \lambda \Gamma(U \otimes V)\}.$$

Moreover, for any $u \in E \otimes F$ and any $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists a representation $u = \sum_i \lambda_i (x_i \otimes y_i)$ such that $p(x_i) \leq 1$, $q(y_i) \leq 1$ for all $i$, and $\sum_i |\lambda_i| \leq r(u) + \varepsilon$.
