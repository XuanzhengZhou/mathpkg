Proposition 6.4, proved by A. Pietsch, establishes that the projective tensor product $E \otimes_\pi F$ of two metrizable locally convex spaces is again metrizable. The topology is generated by the increasing sequence of tensor product semi-norms $r_n = p_n \otimes q_n$ derived from defining sequences of semi-norms for $E$ and $F$. Moreover, the proof provides a powerful series representation: every element of the completion $E \tilde{\otimes} F$ can be expressed as an absolutely summable series $\sum \lambda_i(x_i \otimes y_i)$ where the sequences $\{x_i\}$ and $\{y_i\}$ converge to zero. This representation theorem is fundamental for the analysis of completed projective tensor products.
