Theorem 6.2 establishes the fundamental universal property of the projective tensor product: the linear map $u \mapsto u \circ \chi$ is an isomorphism from the space of continuous linear maps $\mathcal{L}(E \otimes_\pi F, G)$ onto the space of continuous bilinear maps $\mathcal{B}(E, F; G)$. In other words, every continuous bilinear map $f: E \times F \to G$ factors uniquely through a continuous linear map $\tilde{f}$ on the projective tensor product. This universal property characterizes the projective topology as the finest locally convex topology on $E \otimes F$ making the canonical bilinear map $\chi$ continuous.
