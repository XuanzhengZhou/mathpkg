Let $E, F, G$ be locally convex spaces and provide $E \otimes F$ with the projective topology $\mathfrak{T}_p$. Then the linear isomorphism $u \mapsto u \circ \chi$ maps the space of continuous linear mappings $\mathcal{L}(E \otimes_\pi F, G)$ onto the space of continuous bilinear mappings $\mathcal{B}(E, F; G)$. Equivalently, for every continuous bilinear map $f: E \times F \to G$, there exists a unique continuous linear map $\tilde{f}: E \otimes_\pi F \to G$ such that $f = \tilde{f} \circ \chi$.
