It is evident from the series representation (7.1) that $v \circ u$ is nuclear, since the composition merely modifies the equicontinuous family $\{f_n\}$.

For $w \circ v$: by Corollary 1, there exists a convex, circled $0$-neighborhood $V$ in $F$ such that $\overline{v(V)} = B$ is compact in $G$. Then $B_1 = w(B)$ is compact in $H$ by continuity of $w$, hence $H_{B_1}$ is complete. From the series representation of $v$, composing with $w$ gives a representation of $w \circ v$ with terms $\{w(y_n)\}$ in the complete bounded set $B_1$, so $w \circ v$ is nuclear.
