If $u$ is a topological homomorphism, then $u_0$ is open by definition of the quotient topology on $L/N$ and the induced topology on $u(L)$. Since $u_0$ is bijective, continuous, and open, it is an isomorphism.

Conversely, if $u_0$ is an isomorphism, then for each open $G \subset L$, $\phi(G)$ is open in $L/N$, so $u_0(\phi(G)) = u(G)$ is open in $u(L)$. Hence $u$ is a topological homomorphism.
