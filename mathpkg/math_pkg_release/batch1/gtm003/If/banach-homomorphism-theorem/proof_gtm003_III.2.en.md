Suppose that $u(L)$ is not meager in $M$. By (I, 6.1), we may assume the topologies of $L$ and $M$ are generated by pseudonorms, using the notation of the lemma. The family $\{S_r : r > 0\}$ is a $0$-neighborhood base in $L$.

For fixed $r$, let $U = S_r$, $V = S_{r/2}$. Then $V + V \subset U$ and $u(L) = \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty n \, u(V)$ since $V$ is radial. Let $[A]$ denote the closure of $A$ in $M$. Since $u(L)$ is not meager in $M$, $[n u(V)] = n[u(V)]$ must have interior points for some $n$ by the Baire category property. Hence $[u(V)]$ contains an interior point.

It follows that for suitable $r, \rho > 0$, $S_\rho \subset \overline{u(S_r)}$. By the preceding lemma, this implies $S_\rho \subset u(S_t)$ for some $t > 0$. Thus the image of every $0$-neighborhood contains a $0$-neighborhood, which means $u$ is open (a topological homomorphism) and $u(L) = M$ since $u(L)$ is dense.
