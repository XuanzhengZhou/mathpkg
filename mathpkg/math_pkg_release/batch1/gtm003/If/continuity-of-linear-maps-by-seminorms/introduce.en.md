A linear map between topological vector spaces is continuous if and only if it is continuous at the origin. For locally convex spaces whose topologies are defined by families of seminorms, this reduces to a practical criterion: each generating seminorm of the target space must be dominated by the supremum of finitely many generating seminorms of the source space. This is the fundamental tool for verifying continuity in the locally convex setting.
