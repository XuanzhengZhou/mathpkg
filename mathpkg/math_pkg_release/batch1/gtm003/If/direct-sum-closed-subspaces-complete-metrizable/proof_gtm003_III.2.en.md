Since $M$ and $N$ are closed subspaces of a complete metrizable t.v.s., they are themselves complete and metrizable. Hence $M \times N$ is complete and metrizable. The addition map $(x_1, x_2) \mapsto x_1 + x_2$ is a continuous bijective linear map from $M \times N$ onto $L$. By Corollary 1 (to Theorem 2.1), this map is an isomorphism, so $L = M \oplus N$ as topological direct sum.
