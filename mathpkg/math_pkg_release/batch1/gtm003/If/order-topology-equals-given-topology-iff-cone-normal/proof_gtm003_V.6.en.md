The order of $E$ is Archimedean since the positive cone $C$ is closed in the Banach space $(E, \mathfrak{T})$.

If $\mathfrak{T} = \mathfrak{T}_O$, then $C$ is normal by Proposition 6.2.

Conversely, if $C$ is normal for $\mathfrak{T}$, then $\mathfrak{T}$ is coarser than $\mathfrak{T}_O$ (since $\mathfrak{T}_O$ is the finest locally convex topology with normal cone). On the other hand, the order interval $[-e, e]$ is a barrel in $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ (it is closed, convex, circled, and absorbing because $e$ is an order unit). Since $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ is a Banach space, it is barreled, so $[-e, e]$ is a $0$-neighborhood for $\mathfrak{T}$. Thus $\mathfrak{T}_O$, which is generated by the gauge of $[-e, e]$, is coarser than $\mathfrak{T}$. Hence $\mathfrak{T} = \mathfrak{T}_O$.
