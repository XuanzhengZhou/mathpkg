For a topological vector space, being non-meager in itself implies the Baire property: every non-empty open set is non-meager. This follows because if there existed a meager non-empty open set, then a meager zero-neighborhood would exist, and the whole space -- a countable union of homothetic images of that neighborhood -- would be meager, a contradiction.
