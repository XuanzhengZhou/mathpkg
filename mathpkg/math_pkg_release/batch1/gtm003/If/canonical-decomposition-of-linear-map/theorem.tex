Let $L$ and $M$ be topological vector spaces and $u: L \to M$ a linear map. Let $N = u^{-1}(0)$ be the null space of $u$, let $\phi: L \to L/N$ be the canonical quotient map, and let $\psi: u(L) \to M$ be the canonical inclusion. Then $u$ factorizes as

$$u = \psi \circ u_0 \circ \phi$$

where $u_0: L/N \to u(L)$ is the algebraic isomorphism mapping each equivalence class $\hat{x} \in L/N$ to $u(x)$.

$$L \xrightarrow{\phi} L/N \xrightarrow{u_0} u(L) \xrightarrow{\psi} M$$
