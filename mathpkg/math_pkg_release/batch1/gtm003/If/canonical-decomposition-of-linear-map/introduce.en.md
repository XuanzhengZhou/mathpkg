Every linear map between topological vector spaces admits a canonical factorization through its null space and range. The map is decomposed as the composition of the quotient map onto $L/\ker(u)$, a bijective algebraic isomorphism $u_0$, and the inclusion into the codomain. This decomposition is the standard tool for analyzing when a linear map is a topological homomorphism.
