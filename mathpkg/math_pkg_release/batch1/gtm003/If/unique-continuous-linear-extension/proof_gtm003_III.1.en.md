A continuous linear map $u: L \to M$ is uniformly continuous for the additive uniformities of $L$ and $M$. Since $M$ is separated and complete, the uniform continuity of $u$ guarantees a unique uniformly continuous extension $\bar{u}: \bar{L} \to M$ to any uniform space $\bar{L}$ containing $L$ as a dense subspace, in particular to the completion $\tilde{L}$ when $L$ is separated. That $\bar{u}$ is linear follows from continuity: for fixed $x, y \in \bar{L}$ and scalars $\alpha, \beta$, choose nets in $L$ converging to $x$ and $y$ and apply linearity of $u$ together with continuity of addition, scalar multiplication, and $\bar{u}$.
