The identity map $\mathrm{id}: (L, \mathfrak{T}_1) \to (L, \mathfrak{T}_2)$ is continuous since $\mathfrak{T}_1$ is finer than $\mathfrak{T}_2$. Both topologies are complete and metrizable, and $\mathrm{id}$ is bijective. By Corollary 1, $\mathrm{id}$ is an isomorphism, so $\mathfrak{T}_1 = \mathfrak{T}_2$.
