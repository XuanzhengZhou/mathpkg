A topological vector lattice combines the algebraic structure of a vector lattice (Riesz space) with a Hausdorff topological vector space topology over the real numbers. The key linking condition is that the topology is locally solid, meaning there is a neighborhood base at zero consisting of solid sets. In such spaces, the positive cone is automatically closed, making every topological vector lattice an ordered topological vector space.
