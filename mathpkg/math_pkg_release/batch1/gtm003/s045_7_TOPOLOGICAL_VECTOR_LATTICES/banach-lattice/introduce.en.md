A Banach lattice is a normed lattice that is complete with respect to its norm. It is the natural order-theoretic counterpart of a Banach space. Important subclasses include (AL)-spaces (abstract Lebesgue spaces) and (AM)-spaces (abstract spaces of continuous functions). Every Banach lattice is a locally convex vector lattice.
