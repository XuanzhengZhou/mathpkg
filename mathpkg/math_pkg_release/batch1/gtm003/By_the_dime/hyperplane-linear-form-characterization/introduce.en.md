This theorem gives an algebraic characterization of hyperplanes: a subset is a hyperplane exactly when it is the level set of a non-zero linear form. The linear form and the level are determined up to a common non-zero scalar factor. This bridges the geometric notion of hyperplanes with the algebraic concept of linear functionals, and is essential for the analytic form of the Hahn-Banach theorem.
