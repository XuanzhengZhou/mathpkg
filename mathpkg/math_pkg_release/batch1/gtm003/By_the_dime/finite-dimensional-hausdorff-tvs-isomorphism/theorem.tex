Let $K$ be complete. If $L$ is a Hausdorff t.v.s. over $K$ of finite dimension $n$, then every basis $\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ in $L$ determines an isomorphism
$$(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n) \rightarrow \lambda_1 x_1 + \cdots + \lambda_n x_n$$
of $K_0^n$ onto $L$; in particular, every $n$-dimensional Hausdorff t.v.s. over $K$ is isomorphic with $K_0^n$. Equivalently, if $K$ is a complete valuated field, then the product topology on $K_0^n$ is the only Hausdorff topology satisfying $(LT)_1$ and $(LT)_2$.
