A linear manifold (also called an affine subspace) in a vector space $L$ is a translate of a vector subspace. This generalizes the notion of a subspace by allowing an arbitrary origin. Parallel linear manifolds are defined via inclusion of their corresponding subspaces, and the dimension of a linear manifold is the dimension of its associated subspace. These concepts are fundamental for the study of hyperplanes and affine geometry.
