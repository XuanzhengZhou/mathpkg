Let $X$ be a set and $\mathfrak{G}$ a set of subsets of $X$ invariant under finite intersections and arbitrary unions. Then $\mathfrak{G}$ defines a topology $\mathfrak{T}$ on $X$. The pair $(X, \mathfrak{T})$ is called a \text{topological space}. The sets $G \in \mathfrak{G}$ are called \text{open}, and their complements $F = X \setminus G$ are called \text{closed} with respect to $\mathfrak{T}$.
