A topological space is a set equipped with a collection of subsets called open sets, which are closed under finite intersections and arbitrary unions. This structure generalizes the notion of nearness and continuity from metric spaces. The complement of an open set is called a closed set. Topological spaces form the foundational framework for studying continuity, convergence, and compactness without requiring a metric.
