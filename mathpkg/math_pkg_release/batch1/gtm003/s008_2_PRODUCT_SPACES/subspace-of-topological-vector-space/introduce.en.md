A subspace of a topological vector space is a nonempty subset that is closed under vector addition and scalar multiplication, i.e., an algebraic vector subspace. When endowed with the induced topology, a subspace is itself a topological vector space. The interplay between algebraic and topological closure properties of subspaces is fundamental to the structure theory of topological vector spaces.
