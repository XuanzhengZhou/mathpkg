A complementary (or supplementary) subspace to a given subspace $M$ of a topological vector space $L$ is a subspace $N$ such that $L$ is the topological direct sum of $M$ and $N$. In contrast to the purely algebraic setting, complementary subspaces need not exist in topological vector spaces -- even when $M$ is finite-dimensional. This is a distinctive feature of topological vector spaces that sets them apart from Hilbert or Banach spaces, where closed finite-dimensional subspaces always admit topological complements.
