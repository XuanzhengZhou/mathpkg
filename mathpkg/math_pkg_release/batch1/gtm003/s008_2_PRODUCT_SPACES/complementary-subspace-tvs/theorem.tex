Let $(L, \mathfrak{T})$ be a topological vector space and let $M$ be a subspace of $L$. A subspace $N$ of $L$ is called a \textbf{subspace complementary} (or \textbf{supplementary}) to $M$ if $L = M \oplus N$, i.e., $L$ is the algebraic direct sum of $M$ and $N$ and this decomposition is a topological direct sum.

Such complementary subspaces do not necessarily exist, even if $M$ has finite dimension.
