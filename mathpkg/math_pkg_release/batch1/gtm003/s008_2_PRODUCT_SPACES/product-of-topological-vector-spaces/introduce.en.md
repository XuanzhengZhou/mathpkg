The product of a family of topological vector spaces is constructed by taking the Cartesian product of the underlying vector spaces with componentwise algebraic operations, and endowing it with the product topology. This construction yields again a topological vector space. A key feature is that the product inherits separation and completeness properties: the product is Hausdorff (respectively, complete) precisely when every factor space is Hausdorff (respectively, complete).
