A key property of the quotient construction in topological vector spaces is that the canonical projection $\phi: L \to L/M$ is an open mapping. This follows directly from the definition of the quotient topology together with the fact that translation by $M$ preserves openness. The openness of $\phi$ is a convenient tool for studying the structure of quotient spaces and is used repeatedly in the theory of topological vector spaces.
