Let $(L, \mathfrak{T})$ be a topological vector space over $K$ and let $M$ be a subspace of $L$. Denote by $\phi : L \to L/M$ the natural (canonical, quotient) map that sends each $x \in L$ to its equivalence class $\hat{x} = x + M$. The \textbf{quotient topology} $\hat{\mathfrak{T}}$ on $L/M$ is defined as the finest topology for which $\phi$ is continuous.

Equivalently, a subset of $L/M$ is open in $\hat{\mathfrak{T}}$ if and only if it is of the form $\phi(H)$ where $H + M$ is open in $(L, \mathfrak{T})$. The pair $(L/M, \hat{\mathfrak{T}})$ is called the \textbf{quotient topological vector space} of $L$ by $M$.
