**Proof.** Since $\phi$ is a continuous linear map, for every $0$-neighborhood $V$ in $L/M$, the preimage $\phi^{-1}(V)$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $L$. Hence there exists $B \in \mathfrak{B}$ with $B \subset \phi^{-1}(V)$, which implies $\phi(B) \subset V$. On the other hand, since $\phi$ is an open map, each $\phi(B)$ is an open set in $L/M$ containing $0 = \phi(0)$. Thus the family $\{\phi(B) : B \in \mathfrak{B}\}$ satisfies the defining properties of a $0$-neighborhood base in $L/M$. $\square$
