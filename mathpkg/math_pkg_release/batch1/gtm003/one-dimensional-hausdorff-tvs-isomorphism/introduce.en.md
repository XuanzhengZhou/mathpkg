Every one-dimensional Hausdorff TVS is canonically isomorphic to the scalar field itself (viewed as a TVS). This is the base case for the classification of finite-dimensional TVS. The proof uses only the TVS axioms: scalar multiplication $\lambda \mapsto \lambda x_0$ is continuous by $(LT)_2$, and its inverse is continuous because $L$ is Hausdorff.
