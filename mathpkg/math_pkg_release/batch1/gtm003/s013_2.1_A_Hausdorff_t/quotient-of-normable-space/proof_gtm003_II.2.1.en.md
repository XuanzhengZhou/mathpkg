Since $M$ is closed, $L/M$ is Hausdorff by (I, 2.3). Since the natural map $\phi$ of $L$ onto $L/M$ is linear, open, and continuous, $\phi(V)$ is a convex $0$-neighborhood in $L/M$ which is bounded by (I, 5.4) if $V$ is a bounded, convex $0$-neighborhood in $L$; thus $L/M$ is normable by Theorem 2.1. By (I, 6.3), $L/M$ is complete when $L$ is, and the pseudonorm
$$\hat{x} \mapsto \|\hat{x}\| = \inf\{\|x\| : x \in \hat{x}\},$$
which is easily seen to be a norm, generates the topology of $L/M$.
