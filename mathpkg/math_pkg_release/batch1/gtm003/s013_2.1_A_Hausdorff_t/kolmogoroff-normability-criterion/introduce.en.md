Kolmogoroff's normability criterion provides a simple geometric characterization of normable Hausdorff topological vector spaces: a Hausdorff t.v.s. is normable exactly when the origin admits a bounded convex neighborhood. The forward direction follows from the fact that the unit ball of any norm is bounded and convex. The converse constructs a norm as the Minkowski gauge of a bounded, circled, convex 0-neighborhood, and uses the boundedness to show the topology is generated by this norm.
