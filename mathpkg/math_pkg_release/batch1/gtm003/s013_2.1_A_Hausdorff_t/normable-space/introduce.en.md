A normable space is a topological vector space whose topology is induced by a norm via the associated metric. This concept is weaker than that of a normed space: a normed space comes equipped with a specific norm, whereas a normable space merely admits some (non-unique) norm generating its topology. Every normable space is necessarily Hausdorff, since metric topologies are Hausdorff.
