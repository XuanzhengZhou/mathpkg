The vector space $\mathcal{L}(L, N)$ of all continuous linear maps between normed spaces $L$ and $N$ becomes a normed space under the operator norm. When the target space $N$ is complete (a Banach space), $\mathcal{L}(L, N)$ is also complete, hence a Banach space. An important special case is the dual space $L' = \mathcal{L}(L, K_0)$, which is always a Banach space regardless of whether $L$ itself is complete.
