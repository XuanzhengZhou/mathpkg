The $L^\infty(\mu)$ space associated with a measure space $(X, \Sigma, \mu)$ consists of equivalence classes of essentially bounded measurable functions. It is obtained by quotienting the vector space of essentially bounded functions by the $\mu$-null functions, and the resulting space is a Banach space. A deep result is that $L^\infty(\mu)$ is norm-isomorphic to the space $\mathcal{C}(X)$ of continuous functions on some compact Hausdorff space $X$, making it a commutative $C^*$-algebra.
