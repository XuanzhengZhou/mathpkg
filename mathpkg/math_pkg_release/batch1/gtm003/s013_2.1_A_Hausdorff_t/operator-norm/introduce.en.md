The operator norm assigns a nonnegative real number to each continuous linear map between normed spaces, measuring its maximum stretching factor on the closed unit ball. Finiteness of the operator norm is equivalent to continuity of the linear map. The operator norm turns the vector space of all continuous linear maps into a normed space itself, and when the codomain is a Banach space, this space becomes a Banach space.
