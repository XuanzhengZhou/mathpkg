Let $(X, \Sigma, \mu)$ be a measure space in the sense of Halmos. Denote by $\mathscr{L}^p$ ($1 \leq p < \infty$) the set of all $\Sigma$-measurable, scalar-valued functions $f$ for which $|f|^p$ is $\mu$-integrable. The inequalities of H\"older and Minkowski show that $\mathscr{L}^p$ is a vector space and that
$$f \mapsto \left(\int |f|^p \,d\mu\right)^{1/p}$$
is a semi-norm on $\mathscr{L}^p$. If $\mathcal{N}_\mu$ denotes the subspace of $\mathscr{L}^p$ consisting of all $\mu$-null functions and $[f]$ the equivalence class of $f \in \mathscr{L}^p$ modulo $\mathcal{N}_\mu$, then
$$[f] \mapsto \left(\int |f|^p \,d\mu\right)^{1/p}$$
is a norm on the quotient space $\mathscr{L}^p / \mathcal{N}_\mu$, which thus becomes a Banach space usually denoted by $L^p(\mu)$.
