This corollary improves the classical Dvoretzky-Rogers theorem, which states that in every infinite-dimensional Banach space there exists a summable sequence that is not absolutely summable. Here the result is strengthened: if a normable space has the property that every summable sequence is automatically absolutely summable, then the space must be finite dimensional. The proof follows from part (b) and the fact that a normable space is metrizable, hence infrabarreled.
