The vector-valued integration operator $f \mapsto \int_\delta f(\zeta) d\zeta$ is not merely well-defined for continuous functions; it is actually a continuous linear map when the space $C_E(\delta)$ of $E$-valued continuous functions is equipped with the topology of uniform convergence. This continuity property is essential for applications in the theory of vector-valued holomorphic functions, where integration along curves is a fundamental tool.
