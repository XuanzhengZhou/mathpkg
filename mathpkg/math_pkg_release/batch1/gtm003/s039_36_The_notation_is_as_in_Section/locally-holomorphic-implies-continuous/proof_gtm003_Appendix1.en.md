The proof uses the vector-valued Cauchy integral formula or a power series expansion argument. Since $f$ is holomorphic at each $\zeta \in G$, for any $x' \in E'$ the scalar function $\zeta \mapsto \langle f(\zeta), x'\rangle$ is holomorphic in the usual sense, hence continuous. To lift this to continuity of $f$ itself, one uses the fact that boundedness of $\{f(\zeta) : \zeta \in K\}$ for compact $K \subset G$ follows from the corresponding scalar boundedness via the uniform boundedness principle, and that separate continuity together with local boundedness implies joint continuity.

(Note: the text of the proof is truncated in the source; the full argument would proceed via representing $f$ locally by a power series with coefficients in $E$, or via the Cauchy integral representation.)
