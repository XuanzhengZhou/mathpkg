For a vector-valued function $f: G \to E$ defined on an open subset $G$ of the complex plane, holomorphy is defined via the existence of the complex derivative limit. The derivative $f'(\zeta)$ is characterized as the unique element of the locally convex space $E$ whose evaluation against each continuous linear form $x' \in E'$ gives the derivative of the scalar-valued function $\zeta \mapsto \langle f(\zeta), x' \rangle$. The $\sigma(E',E)$-continuity condition ensures that the derivative truly lies in $E$ rather than merely in the algebraic dual.
