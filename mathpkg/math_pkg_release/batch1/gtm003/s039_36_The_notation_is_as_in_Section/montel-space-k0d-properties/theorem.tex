Let $E = K_0^d$ be the Montel space where $d$ is the cardinality of the continuum. Let $B = [0, 1]^d \subset E$ and $B_0 \subset B$ be the subset whose elements have at most countably many non-zero coordinates. Then, under the topology induced by $E$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ and $B_0$ are completely regular topological spaces.
\item $B_0$ is sequentially compact but not compact.
\item $B$ is compact but not sequentially compact.
\item $B_0$ is a uniform space which is semi-complete and precompact but not complete.
\end{enumerate}
