This exercise provides a classic counterexample that distinguishes the notions of compactness, sequential compactness, and countable compactness in general topological spaces. In the Montel space $K_0^d$ (the product of $d$ copies of the scalar field, where $d$ is the cardinality of the continuum), the subset $B = [0,1]^d$ is compact by Tychonoff's theorem but not sequentially compact, while the subset $B_0$ of elements with countably many non-zero coordinates is sequentially compact but not compact. Moreover, $B_0$ serves as an example of a uniform space that is semi-complete and precompact yet fails to be complete.
