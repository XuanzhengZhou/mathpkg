A vector-valued function is locally holomorphic on an open set $G \subset \mathbb{C}$ if it is holomorphic at every point of $G$. Unlike the scalar theory where holomorphy on a domain automatically implies connectedness-related properties, the definition here explicitly allows $G$ to be disconnected, emphasizing the local nature of the concept.
