In the theory of locally convex spaces, the space $l^1[N, E]$ of summable $E$-valued sequences is generally larger than the space $l^1(N, E)$ of absolutely summable sequences, with a finer topology. This proposition states that when $l^1(N, E)$ is infrabarreled and every summable sequence in $E$ is automatically absolutely summable, the two topologies actually coincide and the canonical inclusion is a topological isomorphism. As a consequence, $E$ must be a nuclear space, showing that the absolute summability condition together with infrabarreledness forces a strong nuclearity property.
