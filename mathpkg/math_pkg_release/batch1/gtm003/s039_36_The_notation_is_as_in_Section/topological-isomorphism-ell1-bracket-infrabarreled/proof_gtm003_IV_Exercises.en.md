The proof uses part (a). By (a), under the hypothesis that each summable sequence in $E$ is absolutely summable, every bounded subset of $l^1(N, E)$ is bounded in $l^1[N, E]$. Since $l^1(N, E)$ is infrabarreled, the identity map $l^1(N, E) \to l^1[N, E]$ has a closed graph: if a filter converges in both topologies, its boundedness in $l^1(N, E)$ implies boundedness in $l^1[N, E]$ by (a), and the infrabarreled property ensures continuity. Thus the canonical map $l^1[N, E] \to l^1(N, E)$ (the inverse direction) is also continuous, making it a topological isomorphism.

The nuclearity of $E$ follows because $l^1[N, E] \cong l^1(N, E)$ implies, via the characterization of nuclear spaces in terms of summable sequences (cf. Section 10), that $E$ is nuclear.
