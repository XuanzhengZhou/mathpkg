Let $E$ be a locally convex space and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a total family of bounded subsets of $E$ such that $\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant under each $u \in \mathcal{L}(E)$. Then, with respect to the composition of maps, $\mathcal{L}(E)$ is a locally convex algebra under the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology. Moreover:

\begin{enumerate}
\item If $\mathfrak{B}$ denotes the family of all bounded subsets of $E$, multiplication is right and left $\mathfrak{B}$-hypocontinuous for the topology of bounded convergence.
\item If $E$ is barreled, multiplication is left $\mathfrak{B}$-hypocontinuous for the topology of simple convergence.
\item If $\mathcal{L}_{\mathfrak{S}}(E)$ is an (F)-space, then multiplication is continuous.
\end{enumerate}
