Riemann integrability for functions taking values in a locally convex space $E$ is defined via the weak criterion: $f$ is integrable over a rectifiable curve $\delta$ if all scalar compositions $\zeta \mapsto \langle f(\zeta), x'\rangle$ are Riemann integrable, and the resulting linear functional on the dual $E'$ is continuous for the weak* topology $\sigma(E', E)$. The continuity condition guarantees that the integral belongs to $E$ itself, not merely to the algebraic bidual.
