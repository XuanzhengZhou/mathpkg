Let $\delta$ denote a rectifiable oriented arc in the complex plane. A function $f: \delta \rightarrow E$ is called \emph{(Riemann) integrable over $\delta$} if for each $x' \in E'$, the scalar function $\zeta \mapsto \langle f(\zeta), x' \rangle$ is integrable over $\delta$ in the Riemann-Cauchy sense, and if the linear form

$$x' \mapsto \int_{\delta} \langle f(\zeta), x' \rangle \, d\zeta$$

is $\sigma(E', E)$-continuous.
