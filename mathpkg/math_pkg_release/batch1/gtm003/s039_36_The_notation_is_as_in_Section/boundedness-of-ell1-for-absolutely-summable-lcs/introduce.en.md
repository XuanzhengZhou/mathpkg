This proposition relates the boundedness of sets in the space $l^1(N, E)$ of absolutely summable $E$-valued sequences to their boundedness in the larger space $l^1[N, E]$ of summable $E$-valued sequences. Under the hypothesis that every summable sequence in the locally convex space $E$ is automatically absolutely summable, the two notions of boundedness coincide for subsets of $l^1(N, E)$. The proof reduces to showing that for any sequence $\{x'_n\}$ in $E'$ satisfying a summability condition, the map $x \mapsto \sum_n |\langle x_n, x'_n\rangle|$ is bounded on each bounded subset of $l^1(N, E)$.
