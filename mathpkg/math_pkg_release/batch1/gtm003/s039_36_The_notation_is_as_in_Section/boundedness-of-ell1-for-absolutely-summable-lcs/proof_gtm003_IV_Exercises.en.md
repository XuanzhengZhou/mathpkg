Consider a sequence $\{x'_n\}$ in $E'$ such that $\sum_n |\langle x_n, x'_n \rangle| < +\infty$ for each $x = (x_n) \in l^1(N, E)$. By hypothesis, every summable sequence in $E$ is absolutely summable. We need to show that the function

$$x \mapsto \sum_n |\langle x_n, x'_n \rangle|$$

is bounded on every bounded subset of $l^1(N, E)$. Since $l^1[N, E]$ carries a finer topology than $l^1(N, E)$, the boundedness of subsets of $l^1(N, E)$ in the topology of $l^1[N, E]$ follows from the fact that the above functional is bounded on bounded subsets of $l^1(N, E)$. See Pietsch [5], p. 54 for a detailed treatment.
