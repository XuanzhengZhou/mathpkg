A normed algebra strengthens the notion of a locally convex algebra by requiring that the topology be defined by a single submultiplicative norm. The condition $\|ab\| \leq \|a\|\|b\|$ ensures that multiplication is jointly continuous (not merely separately continuous), and the normalization $\|e\| = 1$ for the unit element is a standard convention that avoids trivialities and ensures compatibility with the algebraic structure. Normed algebras form the bridge between functional analysis and abstract algebra.
