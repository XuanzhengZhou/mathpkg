The Schwartz space $\mathcal{D}$ of all compactly supported smooth functions on $\mathbb{R}^k$ is one of the most important examples of a nuclear LF-space. Its nuclearity follows from the fact that $\mathcal{D} = \varinjlim \mathcal{D}_C$ is the strict inductive limit of a sequence of nuclear Fréchet spaces $\mathcal{D}_C$, and nuclearity is preserved under the formation of inductive limits (by the corollary of (7.4)). This result is foundational for distribution theory.
