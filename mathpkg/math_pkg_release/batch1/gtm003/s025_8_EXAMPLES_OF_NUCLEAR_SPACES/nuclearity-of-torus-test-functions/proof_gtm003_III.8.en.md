**Proof (sketch).** Via Fourier series expansion on the torus $T$, every continuous linear map from $\mathcal{D}_T$ into an arbitrary quasi-complete locally convex space can be represented in a nuclear form

$$f \mapsto [f, g_0] g_0 - \sum_{k \neq 0} k^{-2} h_k(f) y_k,$$

where the coefficients $k^{-2}$ form a summable family. Since this representation is a nuclear map and holds for arbitrary target spaces, $\mathcal{D}_T$ is a nuclear (F)-space. More generally, the same method applies when $m \in \mathbb{N}$ is arbitrary, confirming the nuclearity of $\mathcal{D}_T$.

The result also implies, via (7.4), that the spaces $\mathcal{D}_I$ (functions supported in a $k$-dimensional interval $I$) and $\mathcal{D}_C$ (functions supported in a compact subset $C \subset \mathbb{R}^k$) are nuclear.
