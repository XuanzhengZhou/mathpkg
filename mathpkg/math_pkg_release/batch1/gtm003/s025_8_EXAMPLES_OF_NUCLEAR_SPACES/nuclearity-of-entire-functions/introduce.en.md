The space $\mathcal{H}(\mathbb{C})$ of entire functions of one complex variable, equipped with the Fréchet topology of uniform convergence on compact subsets, is a nuclear (F)-space. The nuclearity follows from basic complex analysis: by Cauchy's integral formula, the Taylor coefficients of an entire function can be expressed as integrals over expanding circles, providing the required nuclear representation. This is another classical example illustrating that spaces of analytic functions are typically nuclear.
