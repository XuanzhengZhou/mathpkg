**Proof.** [Source truncated; the proof in the original text is incomplete.]

From the elements of complex function theory, one uses Cauchy's integral representation to express every entire function as a limit of polynomials uniformly on compact sets. This, together with the nuclearity criterion for power series spaces, yields that $\mathcal{H}(\mathbb{C})$ is isomorphic to the space of all sequences whose Taylor coefficients satisfy suitable growth conditions -- a Köthe nuclear space. Consequently, $\mathcal{H}(\mathbb{C})$ is a nuclear (F)-space.
