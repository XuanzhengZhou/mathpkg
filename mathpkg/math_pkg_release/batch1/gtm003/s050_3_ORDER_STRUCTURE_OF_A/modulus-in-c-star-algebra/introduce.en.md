In a C*-algebra $A$, the modulus of an element $x$ is defined as $|x| = \sqrt{x^*x}$, which is well-defined because $x^*x$ is always a positive element and therefore has a unique positive square root. The modulus generalizes the absolute value of complex numbers and shares some order properties analogous to the usual absolute value.
