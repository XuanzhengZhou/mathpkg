The self-adjoint part $A_{sa}$ of a $C^*$-algebra $A$ consists of all elements that equal their own adjoint under the involution: $x = x^*$. It is a closed real vector subspace of $A$ because it is the kernel of the continuous $\mathbb{R}$-linear map $x \mapsto x - x^*$. The decomposition $A = A_{sa} + iA_{sa}$ holds, meaning every element of the $C^*$-algebra can be expressed as $a + ib$ with $a, b$ self-adjoint. This subspace serves as the foundation for defining the canonical order on a $C^*$-algebra.
