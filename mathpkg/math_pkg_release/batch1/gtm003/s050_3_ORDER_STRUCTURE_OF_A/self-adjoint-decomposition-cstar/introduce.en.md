Since every element $x$ of a C*-algebra decomposes uniquely as $x = a + ib$ where $a = (x+x^*)/2$ and $b = (x-x^*)/(2i)$ are self-adjoint, and each self-adjoint element decomposes further as a difference of two positive elements, every C*-algebra element is a linear combination of at most four positive elements.
