The proof of this lemma is not present in the extracted section text. Part (a) follows from the spectral theorem / continuous functional calculus for normal elements: the function $f(t) = \sqrt{t}$ is continuous on $\mathbf{R}_+$, so $y = f(x)$ satisfies $\sigma(y) = f(\sigma(x)) \subset \mathbf{R}_+$ and $y^2 = x$. Uniqueness follows because any two positive square roots of $x$ commute with $x$ and hence with each other (via the $C^*$-algebra generated by $x$). Part (b) uses the Gelfand representation: identifying the $C^*$-algebra generated by $x$ and $e$ with $C(\sigma(x))$, the condition $\|t e - x\| \leq t$ is equivalent to $\max_{\lambda \in \sigma(x)} |t - \lambda| \leq t$, which holds if and only if $\sigma(x) \subset \mathbf{R}_+$.
