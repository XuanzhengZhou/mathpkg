In a $C^*$-algebra $A$, an element $x$ in the self-adjoint part $A_{sa}$ is called positive if its spectrum $\sigma(x)$ is contained in $\mathbf{R}_+$. This lemma gives two useful characterizations of positivity. Part (a) guarantees the existence and uniqueness of a positive square root for any positive self-adjoint element — a fundamental tool in $C^*$-algebra theory. Part (b) provides an equivalent norm-based condition: in the unital case, $x$ is positive precisely when the distance from $t e$ to $x$ is bounded by $t$ for all sufficiently large real numbers $t$.
