Let $\{L_\alpha : \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ be a family of vector spaces over the same scalar field $K$. The Cartesian product $L = \prod_\alpha L_\alpha$ is a vector space over $K$ if for $x = (x_\alpha)$, $y = (y_\alpha) \in L$ and $\lambda \in K$, addition and scalar multiplication are defined by
$$x + y = (x_\alpha + y_\alpha), \quad \lambda x = (\lambda x_\alpha).$$
If $(L_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ ($\alpha \in \mathbf{A}$) are t.v.s. over $K$, then $L$ is a t.v.s. under the product topology $\mathfrak{T} = \prod_\alpha \mathfrak{T}_\alpha$. Moreover, $L(\mathfrak{T})$ is a Hausdorff space and a complete uniform space, respectively, if and only if each factor is. $(L, \mathfrak{T})$ is called the product of the family $\{L_\alpha(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha) : \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$.
