Let $X$ and $Y$ be topological spaces. A map $f: X \to Y$ is continuous at $x \in X$ if and only if $f$ converges to $y = f(x)$ along the neighborhood filter of $x$. More generally, for a filter $\mathfrak{F}$ on $X$, $f$ converges to $y \in Y$ along $\mathfrak{F}$ if the filter generated by $f(\mathfrak{F})$ converges to $y$.
