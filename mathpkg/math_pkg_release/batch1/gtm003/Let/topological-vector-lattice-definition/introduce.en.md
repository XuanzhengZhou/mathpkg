A topological vector lattice is a vector lattice with a Hausdorff locally solid topology, meaning it has a base of solid 0-neighborhoods. The lattice operations (absolute value, positive/negative part, sup/inf) are then uniformly continuous. An l.c.v.l. additionally has a locally convex topology generated by lattice semi-norms satisfying $|y| \leq |x| \implies p(y) \leq p(x)$. A Banach lattice is a complete normed lattice.
