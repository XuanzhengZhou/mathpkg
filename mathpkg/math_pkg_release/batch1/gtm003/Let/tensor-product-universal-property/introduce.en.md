The universal property of the algebraic tensor product $E \otimes F$ states that every bilinear map $b: E \times F \to G$ factors uniquely through the canonical bilinear map $\chi: E \times F \to E \otimes F$ via a linear map $\tilde{b}: E \otimes F \to G$. This property characterizes the tensor product up to isomorphism and establishes a natural bijection between bilinear maps out of $E \times F$ and linear maps out of $E \otimes F$. In the context of topological vector spaces, this algebraic property underlies the construction of the projective tensor product topology, which is the finest locally convex topology on $E \otimes F$ making $\chi$ continuous.
