If $\mathfrak{B}$ is a 0-neighborhood base in $F$ consisting of convex sets, then each $M(S, V)$ is convex; hence by (3.1) the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology is locally convex. To show it is Hausdorff: let $f \in G$ and $f \neq 0$. Since $f$ is continuous and $\bigcup\{S: S \in \mathfrak{S}\}$ is dense, there exists $t_0 \in S_0 \in \mathfrak{S}$ such that $f(t_0) \neq 0$. Since $F$ is Hausdorff, $f(t_0) \notin V_0$ for some $V_0 \in \mathfrak{B}$. Thus $f \notin M(S_0, V_0)$, and the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology is Hausdorff on $G$.
