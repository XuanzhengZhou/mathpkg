When $F$ is locally convex and the union of $\mathfrak{S}$ is dense in $T$, the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology on a subspace $G$ of continuous functions that are bounded on each $S \in \mathfrak{S}$ is a Hausdorff locally convex topology. In particular, if $T$ is a t.v.s. and each $S \in \mathfrak{S}$ is bounded, and $G$ consists of continuous linear maps, the boundedness condition is automatic.
