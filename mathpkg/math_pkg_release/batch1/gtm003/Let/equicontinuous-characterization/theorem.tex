Let $H$ be a subset of $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$. The following assertions are equivalent: (a) $H$ is equicontinuous. (b) For each 0-neighborhood $V$ in $F$, $\bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)$ is a 0-neighborhood in $E$. (c) For each 0-neighborhood $V$ in $F$, there exists a 0-neighborhood $U$ in $E$ such that $\bigcup_{u \in H} u(U) \subset V$.
