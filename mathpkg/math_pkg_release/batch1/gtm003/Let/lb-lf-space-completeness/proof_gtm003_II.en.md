Let $E = \varinjlim E_n$ be an (LB) or (LF) space and let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a Cauchy filter on $E$. Since each $E_n$ is closed in $E_{n+1}$, the restriction of $\mathfrak{F}$ to each $E_n$ is a Cauchy filter. The completeness of each $E_n$ (being Banach or Fr\'echet) implies convergence in some $E_n$, and thus in $E$. Equivalently, using (6.5) one shows that every Cauchy sequence is contained and convergent in some $E_n$.
