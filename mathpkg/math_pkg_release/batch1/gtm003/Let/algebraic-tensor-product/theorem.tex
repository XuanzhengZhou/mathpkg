Let $E, F$ be vector spaces over $K$ and let $B(E, F)$ be the vector space of all bilinear forms on $E \times F$. For each pair $(x, y) \in E \times F$, the mapping $f \rightarrow f(x, y)$ is a linear form on $B(E, F)$, hence an element $u_{x,y}$ of $B(E, F)^*$. The bilinear map $\chi: (x, y) \rightarrow u_{x,y}$ of $E \times F$ into $B(E, F)^*$ has as its linear hull the tensor product $E \otimes F$ of $E$ and $F$. The element $u_{x,y}$ is denoted by $x \otimes y$.
