(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b): The intersection of $C$ with every finite-dimensional subspace $M$ is closed, which follows since the order is Archimedean. (b) $\Rightarrow$ (c): If $\mathfrak{T}$ is a Hausdorff l.c. topology under which $C$ is sequentially closed, then $L$ is Archimedean ordered. Since $L^+$ separates points, $L$ and $L^+ = C^* - C^*$ are in duality, and by (3.3) $C$ is normal for $\sigma(L, L^+)$. (c) $\Rightarrow$ (a): It suffices to show $L^+$ separates points. If $\mathfrak{T}$ is a Hausdorff l.c. topology with $C$ normal, then $(L, \mathfrak{T})' = C' - C'$ by (3.3); hence $C' - C'$, and a fortiori $L^+ = C^* - C^*$, separates points.
