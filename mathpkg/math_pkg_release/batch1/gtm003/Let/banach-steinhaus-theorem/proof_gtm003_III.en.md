Let $V$ be a closed, convex, circled 0-neighborhood in $F$. Then $D = \bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)$ is closed, convex, and circled. Since $H$ is simply bounded, for each $x \in E$ the set $\{u(x): u \in H\}$ is bounded in $F$, so there exists $\lambda > 0$ with $u(x) \in \lambda V$ for all $u \in H$. Hence $x \in \lambda D$, so $D$ is radial and therefore a barrel. If $E$ is barreled, $D$ is a 0-neighborhood. If $E$ is a Baire space and $V$ is a 0-neighborhood in $F$, then $D$ is closed; since $E = \bigcup_n nD$, Baire's theorem gives some $nD$ with interior, hence 0 is interior to $D$. In either case, $H$ is equicontinuous by (4.1).
