The projective topology is the coarsest topology on a vector space $E$ that makes a given family of linear maps $f_\alpha: E \to E_\alpha$ into locally convex spaces $(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ continuous. It represents the initial topology in the category of locally convex spaces, generalizing the product topology, subspace topology, and weak topologies. Every complete locally convex space can be realized as a projective limit of Banach spaces, making the projective topology fundamental to the structure theory of locally convex spaces.
