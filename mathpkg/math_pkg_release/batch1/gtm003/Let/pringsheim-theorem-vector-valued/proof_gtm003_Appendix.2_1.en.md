Let $f(z) = \sum a_n z^n$ for $|z| < 1$ with radius 1. For any real continuous linear form $x'$ on $E$, the radius $r_{x'}$ of $\sum \langle a_n, x' \rangle t^n$ is $\geq 1$, and $\inf\{r_{x'} : x' \in D\} = 1$ where $D$ are positive forms (otherwise the series would converge beyond 1 since $E'_0 = D - D$ by weak normality). For fixed $\rho < 1$ and $x' \in D$, studying $\sum \langle a_n, x' \rangle \rho^n \exp(in\theta)$ and taking limits as $\rho \to 1$ gives the result. The pole order argument follows by analyzing $(1-t)^p$ multipliers.
