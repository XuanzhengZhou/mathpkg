Define $S_E$ as the space of $E$-valued simple functions and the map $u \mapsto \tilde{u}$ from $S \otimes E$ to $S_E$. For $u = \sum \psi_i \otimes x_i$, one checks that $p(\tilde{u}) \leq r(u)$. Conversely, choosing a representation with disjoint carriers $S_i$ for the characteristic functions $\psi_i$ gives $r(u) \leq \sum \|\psi_i\| \|x_i\| = p(\tilde{u})$. Thus $u \mapsto \tilde{u}$ is a norm isomorphism of $S \otimes E$ onto $\hat{S}_E$, and the assertion follows since $S$ is dense in $L^1(\mu)$.
