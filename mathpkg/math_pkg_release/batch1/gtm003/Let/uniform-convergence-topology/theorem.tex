Let $F$ be a vector space over $K$, let $T$ be a set, and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a family of subsets of $T$ directed under set-theoretic inclusion $\subset$. Consider the vector space $F^T$. Suppose $F$ is a t.v.s. and let $\mathfrak{B}$ be a neighborhood base of 0 in $F$. When $S$ runs through $\mathfrak{S}$ and $V$ through $\mathfrak{B}$, the family $$M(S, V) = \{ f : f(S) \subset V \}$$ is a 0-neighborhood base in $F^T$ for a unique translation-invariant topology, called the topology of uniform convergence on the sets $S \in \mathfrak{S}$, or the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology.
