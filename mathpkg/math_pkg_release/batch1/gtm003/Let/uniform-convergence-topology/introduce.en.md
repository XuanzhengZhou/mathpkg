The $\mathfrak{S}$-topology on a space of functions $F^T$ is the topology of uniform convergence on members of a directed family $\mathfrak{S}$ of subsets of $T$. A 0-neighborhood base is given by sets $M(S, V) = \{f : f(S) \subset V\}$ where $S \in \mathfrak{S}$ and $V$ is a 0-neighborhood in $F$. This general construction encompasses many important function space topologies.
