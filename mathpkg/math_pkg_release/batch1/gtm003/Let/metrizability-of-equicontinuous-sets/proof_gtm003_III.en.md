In view of (4.5), it suffices to prove the theorem for the topology of simple convergence on a total subset of $E$. Since $E$ is separable, there exists an at most countable subset $A = \{x_n\}$ of $E$ which is linearly independent and total. Taking a countable 0-neighborhood base $\{V_m\}$ in $F$, the sets $M(\{x_i\}, V_m)$ form a countable subbase for the topology of simple convergence on $H$, establishing metrizability. The separability claim follows similarly.
