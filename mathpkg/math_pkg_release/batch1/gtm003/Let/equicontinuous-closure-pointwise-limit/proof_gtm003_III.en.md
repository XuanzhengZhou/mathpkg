Since $F$ is assumed to be Hausdorff and $f \mapsto f(x)$ is continuous on $F^E$ for each $x \in E$, the set $\{f \in F^E: f(\lambda x + \mu y) = \lambda f(x) + \mu f(y)\}$ is closed for fixed $x, y, \lambda, \mu$. Thus $L(E, F)$ is closed as an intersection of such sets. Since $\mathcal{L}(E, F) \subset L(E, F)$, the pointwise closure of any $H \subset \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is contained in $L(E, F)$. If $H$ is equicontinuous, the equicontinuity condition extends to the closure by continuity of the evaluation maps.
