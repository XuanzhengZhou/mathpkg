The product topology $\mathfrak{T}$ on $E = \prod_\alpha E_\alpha$ is defined (Chapter I, Section 2) as the coarsest topology making all canonical projections $\pi_\alpha: E \to E_\alpha$ continuous. By definition of the projective topology with respect to the family $\{(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha, \pi_\alpha): \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$, this is exactly the coarsest topology for which each $\pi_\alpha$ is continuous. Hence $\mathfrak{T}$ is the projective topology. The local convexity of $\mathfrak{T}$ follows from the fact that each $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ is locally convex and the projective topology of locally convex topologies is locally convex (as established in the general case).
