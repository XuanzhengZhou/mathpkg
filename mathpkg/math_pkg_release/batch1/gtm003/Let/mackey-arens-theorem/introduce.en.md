The Mackey-Arens theorem characterizes all locally convex topologies on $F$ compatible with a duality $\langle F, G \rangle$ (i.e., having $G$ as dual). A topology is consistent if and only if it is the topology of uniform convergence on a saturated family of $\sigma(G, F)$-relatively compact subsets of $G$ that covers $G$. The Mackey topology $\tau(F, G)$ is the topology of uniform convergence on all $\sigma(G, F)$-compact convex circled sets, and is the finest consistent topology.
