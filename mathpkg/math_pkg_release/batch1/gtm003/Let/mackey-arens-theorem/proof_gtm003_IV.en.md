Necessity follows from the bipolar theorem and Alaoglu-Bourbaki. For sufficiency, let $\mathfrak{S}$ be saturated and consist of $\sigma(G, F)$-relatively compact sets covering $G$. By (III, 3.2), $F$ is l.c.s. under the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology, finer than $\sigma(F, G)$, so the dual contains $G$. The Mackey-Arens argument shows the dual equals $G$.
