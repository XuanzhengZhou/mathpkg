If $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ is an ordered l.c.s., then $C$ is closed by definition, and the bipolar theorem (IV, 1.5) shows $-C$ to be the polar of $C'$ with respect to $\langle E, E' \rangle$. Since $C \cap -C = \{0\}$, the regular order property follows.
