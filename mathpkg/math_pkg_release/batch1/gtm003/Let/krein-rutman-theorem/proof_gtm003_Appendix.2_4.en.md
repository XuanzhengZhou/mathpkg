Since $r(u)$ is a pole, the leading coefficient $p$ of the principal part of the resolvent at $r(u)$ is positive (as a limit of positive operators). The residue $q$ is a projection onto the generalized eigenspace. Since $C$ and $C'$ are total, there exists $x \in C$ and $x' \in C'$ with $q(x) \neq 0$ and $q'(x') \neq 0$. These are eigenvectors (or generalized eigenvectors that can be reduced to eigenvectors). The positivity of $r(u)$ follows since otherwise the resolvent would be entire.
