For (i): If $f$ is positive, then $f^{-1}(0)$ is a hyperplane lying on one side of $C$; since $C$ has interior, $f$ is continuous. For (ii): Use the closed graph theorem / Baire category argument. For (iii): If $f$ were not continuous, there exists a bounded sequence $\{x_n\} \subset C$ with $f(x_n) > n$. Since $E$ is locally convex, $\{n^{-2}x_n\}$ is summable with sum $z \in C$, and $f(z) \geq \sum n^{-2}f(x_n) > \sum n^{-1}$ for all $p$, contradiction.
