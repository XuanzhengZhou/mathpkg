Clearly, $\mathfrak{T}$ is the upper bound (in the lattice of topologies on $E$) of the topologies $f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ ($\alpha \in \mathbf{A}$). A fundamental system of neighborhoods for the upper bound of a family of topologies is obtained by taking all finite intersections of neighborhoods from the individual topologies. Since each $f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ has as neighborhoods exactly the inverse images $f_\alpha^{-1}(U_\alpha)$ where $U_\alpha \in \mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ is a neighborhood, the stated description follows immediately. The finite subset $\mathbf{H}$ corresponds precisely to taking intersections of finitely many such inverse images, which is the defining property of the upper bound topology.
