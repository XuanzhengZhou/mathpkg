Let $p$ be the leading coefficient of the principal part at $r$ and $q$ the residue; $p$ is positive and $p = q(u - re)^{k-1}$ where $k$ is the pole order. $q$ and $q'$ are projections onto generalized eigenspaces. (ii): Since $C, C'$ are total, there exist eigenvectors in $C, C'$. For any positive eigenvector $x_0$: if $x_0$ were not quasi-interior, the band it generates would be invariant under $u$, contradicting irreducibility. (i): $r > 0$ otherwise irreducibility fails. To show $k=1$: if $k > 1$, then $p \neq 0$ and $p(C) \subset C$; then $p$ restricted to $p(E)$ would be an irreducible positive endomorphism whose spectrum is $\{0\}$, which is impossible. (iii): In cases (a)-(c), the eigenspace is shown to be 1-dimensional.
