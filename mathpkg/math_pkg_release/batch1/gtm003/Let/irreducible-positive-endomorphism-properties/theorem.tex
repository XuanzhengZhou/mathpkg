Let $E$ be an ordered real Banach space with positive cone $C$, and let $u$ be an irreducible positive endomorphism whose spectral radius $r$ is a pole of the resolvent. Then: (i) $r > 0$ and $r$ is a pole of order 1. (ii) There exist positive eigenvectors (pertaining to $r$) of $u$ in $C$ and $u'$ in $C'$; each positive eigenvector for $r$ is quasi-interior, each positive eigenvector of $u'$ is strictly positive. (iii) Under additional conditions ($C$ has interior, or $d(r)$ is finite, or $E$ is a Banach lattice), the multiplicity $d(r) = 1$.
