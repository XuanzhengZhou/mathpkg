The product $\prod_{\alpha} E_{\alpha}$ of a family $\{E_{\alpha}: \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ of l.c.s. is an example of a projective limit. If $\{H\}$ denotes the family of all non-empty finite subsets of $\mathbf{A}$, ordered by inclusion, $E_{\mathbf{H}} = \prod_{\alpha \in \mathbf{H}} E_{\alpha}$ and $g_{\mathbf{H}, \Lambda}$ denotes the projection of $E_{\Lambda}$ onto $E_{\mathbf{H}}$ when $\mathbf{H} \subset \Lambda$, then $\prod_{\alpha} E_{\alpha} = \varprojlim g_{\mathbf{H}, \Lambda} E_{\Lambda}$.
