Let $E, E_1, F, F_1$ be t.v.s. such that $E$ is a dense subspace of $E_1$ and $F$ is a dense subspace of $F_1$. Suppose $\mathfrak{S}$ is a family of bounded subsets of $E$ with the property that $\mathfrak{S}_1$ covers $E_1$, where $\mathfrak{S}_1$ are closures in $E_1$; similarly for $\mathfrak{T}$ and $F$. Let $G$ be a quasi-complete Hausdorff t.v.s. Then every $(\mathfrak{S}, \mathfrak{T})$-hypocontinuous bilinear mapping of $E \times F$ into $G$ has a unique extension to $E_1 \times F_1$ which is bilinear and $(\mathfrak{S}_1, \mathfrak{T}_1)$-hypocontinuous.
