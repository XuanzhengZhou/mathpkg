(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b): $\mathfrak{B} = \{[U]: U \in \mathfrak{U}\}$ is a base of $C$-saturated 0-neighborhoods. (b) $\Rightarrow$ (c): For $V \in \mathfrak{B}$, $[V \cap C] \subset [V] = V$. (c) $\Rightarrow$ (a): Given $U \in \mathfrak{U}$, pick $V \in \mathfrak{B}$ with $V+V \subset U$ and circled $W$ with $W+W \subset V$. Then $[W] = \bigcup_{x,y \in W} (x + [0, y-x]) \subset W + [(W+W) \cap C] \subset V + [V \cap C] \subset V+V \subset U$. For the l.c. case: (a) $\Rightarrow$ (d): $\mathfrak{B} = \{[U]: U \in \mathfrak{U}_1\}$ where $\mathfrak{U}_1$ are convex circled 0-neighborhoods. (d) $\Rightarrow$ (e): Gauges of sets in $\mathfrak{B}$. (e) $\Rightarrow$ (c): The sets $\{x: p(x) < 1\}$ form a base.
