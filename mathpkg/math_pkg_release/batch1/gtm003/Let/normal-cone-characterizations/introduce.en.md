A cone is normal precisely when the 0-neighborhood filter has a base of $C$-saturated sets. For locally convex spaces over $R$, this is equivalent to the existence of a generating family of monotone semi-norms: $0 \leq x \leq y \implies p(x) \leq p(y)$. Additional equivalent conditions involve the $[V \cap C] \subset V$ property.
