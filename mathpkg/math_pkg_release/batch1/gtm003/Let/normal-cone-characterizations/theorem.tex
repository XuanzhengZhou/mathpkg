Let $C$ be a cone in a t.v.s. $L$. The following are equivalent: (a) $C$ is normal. (b) The neighborhood filter of 0 has a base consisting of $C$-saturated sets. (c) There exists a 0-neighborhood base $\mathfrak{B}$ such that $V \in \mathfrak{B}$ implies $[V \cap C] \subset V$. When $L$ is locally convex over $R$, these are equivalent to: (d) There exists a 0-neighborhood base of convex, circled, $C$-saturated sets. (e) The topology can be generated by a family of semi-norms $p$ such that $0 \leq x \leq y$ implies $p(x) \leq p(y)$.
