A locally convex vector lattice (l.c.v.l.) possesses a 0-neighborhood base of convex solid sets. The gauge function $p$ of a radial, convex solid set is characterized by being a semi-norm such that $|y| \leq |x|$ implies $p(y) \leq p(x)$, called a lattice semi-norm. The topology of a l.c.v.l. can be generated by a family of lattice semi-norms.
