Let $\langle F, G \rangle$ and $\langle F_1, G_1 \rangle$ be dualities and let $u$ be a weakly continuous linear map of $F$ into $F_1$ with adjoint $u'$. Let $\mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{S}_1$ be saturated families of weakly bounded subsets. Then $u'$ is continuous on $(G, \mathfrak{T})$ into $(G_1, \mathfrak{T}_1)$ if and only if $u(\mathfrak{S}) \subset \mathfrak{S}_1$, where $\mathfrak{T}$ is the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology and $\mathfrak{T}_1$ the $\mathfrak{S}_1$-topology.
