A locally convex topology on $F$ has dual $G$ exactly when it lies between the weak topology $\sigma(F, G)$ and the Mackey topology $\tau(F, G)$. This is a practical corollary of the Mackey-Arens theorem. Furthermore, closedness for $\tau(F, G)$ implies closedness for any consistent topology, and weak boundedness implies boundedness for all consistent topologies.
