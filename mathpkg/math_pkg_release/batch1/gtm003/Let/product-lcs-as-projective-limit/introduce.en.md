This proposition expresses an arbitrary product of locally convex spaces as a projective limit of finite products. The finite subsets $\mathbf{H}$ of the index set $\mathbf{A}$ form a directed set under inclusion, and the finite products $E_{\mathbf{H}}$ together with the natural restriction projections form a projective system whose limit is the full product. This decomposition is useful because it reduces properties of arbitrary products to properties of finite products (which are often easier to handle) and projective limits.
