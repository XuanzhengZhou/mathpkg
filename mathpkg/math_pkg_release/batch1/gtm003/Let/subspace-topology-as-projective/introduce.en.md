This proposition identifies the subspace topology of a locally convex space as a projective topology with respect to the inclusion map. The canonical imbedding $M \hookrightarrow E$ is a linear map, and requiring it to be continuous with respect to the coarsest possible topology on $M$ yields precisely the induced subspace topology. This unifies the treatment of subspaces within the projective topology framework.
