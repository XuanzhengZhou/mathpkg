Assume $r = 1$. Let $\alpha x = u(x)$ with $|\alpha| = 1$, $x = x_1 + i x_2 \neq 0$. Then $|x| = |u(x)| \leq u(|x|)$. Now $\langle |x|, x'_0 \rangle \leq \langle u(|x|), x'_0 \rangle = \langle |x|, u'(x'_0) \rangle \leq \langle |x|, x'_0 \rangle$, so equality holds and $|x| = u(|x|)$. Working in the solid sublattice generated by $|x|$, the restriction of $u$ is an irreducible operator on a space with strictly positive functional, and cyclicity follows from the group structure of the spectrum.
