A Frechet space is, by definition, a complete metrizable locally convex space. By Theorem 5.4, every complete l.c.s. $E$ is isomorphic to a projective limit of Banach spaces (specifically, the projective limit $\lim_{\leftarrow} \bar{g}_{\alpha\beta} \tilde{F}_{\beta}$ constructed in the proof of (5.4), where each $\tilde{F}_{\beta}$ is a Banach space). A Frechet space is in particular a complete l.c.s., and its topology is generated by a countable family of semi-norms. In this case, the projective limit can be taken over a sequence (rather than an arbitrary directed set), yielding an isomorphism with a projective limit of a sequence of Banach spaces.
