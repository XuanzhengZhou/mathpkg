For a $C$-compact map (continuous on $C$ into $C$ and mapping bounded subsets of $C$ to relatively compact sets), the $C$-spectral radius $r_C$ (defined via norms on $C$) is an eigenvalue with a positive eigenvector, provided $r_C > 0$. This generalizes the Krein-Rutman theorem via compactness rather than pole assumptions.
