By (4.1)(b), if $H$ is equicontinuous, then for each 0-neighborhood $V$ in $F$, $\bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)$ is a 0-neighborhood in $E$. This implies condition (3.3)(b): for each 0-neighborhood $V$ in $F$, the set $\bigcap_{u \in H} u^{-1}(V)$ absorbs every member of $\mathfrak{S}$. By (3.3), this is equivalent to $H$ being bounded for the $\mathfrak{S}$-topology. Since $\mathfrak{S}$ is arbitrary, the statement holds for every $\mathfrak{S}$-topology.
