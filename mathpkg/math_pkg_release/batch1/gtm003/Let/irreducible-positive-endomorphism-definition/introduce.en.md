An irreducible positive endomorphism maps every non-zero positive element to a quasi-interior point (via the resolvent iteration). In a Banach lattice, irreducibility is equivalent to having no non-trivial closed solid invariant subspace. This generalizes the notion of irreducible matrices in Perron-Frobenius theory.
