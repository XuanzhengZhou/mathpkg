If $u$ is irreducible and $F \neq \{0\}$ is a closed solid subspace invariant under $u$, then for $0 \neq x \in F \cap C$, $y = uR(\lambda)x$ is a quasi-interior point of $C$ in $F$, so $F = E$. Conversely, if $u$ leaves no proper closed solid subspace invariant, then $u(x) > 0$ for each $x > 0$ (otherwise the solid subspace generated by $x_0$ with $u(x_0) = 0$ would be invariant). For $x > 0$, $y = uR(\lambda)x > 0$. If $y$ were not quasi-interior, the band generated by $y$ would be a proper invariant solid subspace.
