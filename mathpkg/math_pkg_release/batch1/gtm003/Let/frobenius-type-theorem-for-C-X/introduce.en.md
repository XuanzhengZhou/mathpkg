For an irreducible positive endomorphism on $\mathcal{C}_R(X)$ (a compact space), a detailed Frobenius-type theorem holds. The spectral radius $r > 0$ and $\|u\| = r$ characterizes constant-preserving operators. The peripheral point spectrum is a cyclic group. Eigenvalues on the spectral circle have multiplicity 1 and nowhere-vanishing eigenfunctions. If the peripheral point spectrum has an isolated point, it is a finite cyclic group. This generalizes the classical Perron-Frobenius theorem to operators on $C(X)$.
