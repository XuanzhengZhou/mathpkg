Let $X$ be a compact space and $u$ an irreducible positive endomorphism of $\mathcal{C}_R(X)$. Then: (i) $r(u) > 0$, and $\|u\| = r$ iff $u(1) = r1$. (ii) The peripheral point spectrum is cyclic. (iii) Each eigenvalue $r\alpha$, $|\alpha| = 1$, has multiplicity 1 and the eigenfunction is non-zero throughout; $\sigma(u)$ is invariant under rotation by $\theta$ where $\alpha = e^{i\theta}$. (iv) If the peripheral point spectrum contains an isolated point, it is $rH$ where $H$ is the group of $n$-th roots of unity. (v) If it contains a pole of the resolvent, all its points are poles of order 1. (vi) $r$ is the only eigenvalue with eigenfunction $\geq 0$; if $X$ is connected, $r\alpha$ with $\alpha \neq 1$ a root of unity cannot be in the peripheral point spectrum.
