The strict inductive limit of an increasing sequence of locally convex spaces, where each space embeds as a topological subspace of the next, is a well-behaved construction. When the constituent spaces are Banach spaces, the result is called an (LB)-space; when they are Fr\'echet spaces, an (LF)-space. Important examples include spaces of continuous functions with compact support.
