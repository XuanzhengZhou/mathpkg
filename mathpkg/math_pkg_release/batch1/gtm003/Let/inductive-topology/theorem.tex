Let $E$ and $E_\alpha$ $(\alpha \in \mathbf{A})$ be vector spaces over $K$, let $g_\alpha$ be a linear mapping of $E_\alpha$ into $E$, and let $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ be a locally convex topology on $E_\alpha$ $(\alpha \in \mathbf{A})$. The inductive topology on $E$ with respect to the family $\{(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha, g_\alpha): \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ is the finest locally convex topology for which each of the mappings $g_\alpha$ $(\alpha \in \mathbf{A})$ is continuous on $(E_\alpha, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ into $E$.
