Let $H = \{x: f(x) = 1\}$ be a hyperplane such that $H \cap C$ is a compact base. Define $V$ as the convex hull of $\{0\} \cup (H \cap C)$ and $U = V - V$. Then $\{\varepsilon U\}$ is a 0-neighborhood base for a normable topology on $E_0 = C - C$. The norm $\|z\| = \inf\{f(x) + f(y): z = x - y, x, y \in C\}$ generates this topology, and $T$ is finer than the induced topology. The restriction of $u$ to $H \cap C$, composed with normalization, gives a continuous self-map of the compact convex set $H \cap C$, to which the Schauder-Tychonoff fixed point theorem applies.
