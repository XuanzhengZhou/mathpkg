A nuclear locally convex space is one for which every canonical map into the Banach space associated with a zero-neighborhood is a nuclear operator. This definition, due to Grothendieck, captures the idea that the space is "small" in the sense that its identity map can be approximated by finite-rank operators in a very strong way. Nuclear spaces include all finite-dimensional spaces, Schwartz spaces of test functions, and spaces of holomorphic functions, but exclude infinite-dimensional Banach spaces (unless finite-dimensional).
