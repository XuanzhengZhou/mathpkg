Let $E, F$ be Banach spaces and let $E'$ be the strong dual of $E$ (a Banach space). The canonical imbedding $v \mapsto u$ of $E' \otimes F$ into $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is continuous for the projective topology on $E' \otimes F$ and the norm topology on $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$, since for every $v = \sum_{i=1}^r f_i \otimes y_i \in E' \otimes F$,
$$
\|u\| = \sup_{\|x\| \leq 1} \|u(x)\| \leq \sup_{\|x\| \leq 1} \sum_{i=1}^r |f_i(x)| \|y_i\| \leq \sum_{i=1}^r \|f_i\| \|y_i\|
$$
and therefore $\|u\| \leq r(v)$, where $r$ is the projective tensor product norm. Since $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is complete, this imbedding admits a unique continuous extension $\tau: E' \hat{\otimes} F \to \mathcal{L}(E, F)$. The linear maps contained in the range of $\tau$ are called \emph{nuclear}.
