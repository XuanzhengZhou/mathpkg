Nuclear spaces enjoy excellent permanence properties: they are closed under subspaces, separated quotients, completions, countable products and sums, and projective limits of sequences. This makes the class of nuclear spaces remarkably robust. Unlike Banach spaces (where only finite-dimensional spaces are nuclear), nuclear spaces abound in analysis: spaces of smooth functions, distributions, and holomorphic functions are typically nuclear. The permanence properties make nuclearity a useful structural concept.
