**1. Products and direct sums.** Let $\{E_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$ be a family of nuclear spaces. For a countable direct sum $E = \bigoplus_{n=1}^\infty E_n$, let $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ where $F$ is a Banach space. The restriction $u_n = u|_{E_n}$ is nuclear by Theorem 7.2(b). By (7.1), each $u_n$ has the series representation $u_n(x) = \sum_{i=1}^\infty \mu_n^{(i)} f_{n,i}(x) y_{n,i}$ with $\sum_i |\mu_n^{(i)}| < +\infty$, $\{f_{n,i}\} \subset E_n'$ equicontinuous, and $\{y_{n,i}\}$ bounded in a complete disk in $F$. Extend each $f_{n,i}$ by zero to the complement $\bigoplus_{j \neq i} E_j$ of $E_n$. The family $\{f_{n,i} : (n,i) \in \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}\}$ is equicontinuous, for if $U$ is the $0$-neighborhood $\Gamma_i U_i$ in $E$, then $x \in U$ implies $|f_{n,i}(x)| \leq 1$ for all $n$ and $i$. Since $u = \sum_{n,i=1}^{\infty} \mu_n^{(i)} f_{n,i} \otimes y_{n,i}$, it follows from (7.1) that $u$ is nuclear. Hence $E$ is nuclear by Theorem 7.2(b).

For the product $E = \prod_\alpha E_\alpha$, let $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ with $F$ a Banach space. There exists a $0$-neighborhood $V$ in $E$ such that $u(V)$ is bounded in $F$. By definition of the product topology, $V$ contains a $0$-neighborhood of the form $V_{\alpha_1} \times \cdots \times V_{\alpha_n} \times \prod_{\beta \neq \alpha_i} E_\beta$. It follows that $u$ vanishes on the subspace $G = \prod_{\beta \neq \alpha_i} E_\beta$. Since $E = \prod_{i=1}^{n} E_{\alpha_i} \oplus G$, it remains to show that the restriction of $u$ to $\prod_i E_{\alpha_i}$ is nuclear. But this is clear since $\bigoplus_{i=1}^{n} E_{\alpha_i} = \prod_{i=1}^{n} E_{\alpha_i}$ for finite families (Chapter II, Section 6), and the finite direct sum of nuclear spaces is nuclear by the preceding argument.

**2. Subspaces and quotients.** The proof is based on property (c) of Theorem 7.2 and Corollary 1 of Theorem 7.3. Let $E$ be a nuclear space and $M$ a subspace. For each convex, circled $0$-neighborhood $U$ in $E$, set $U_1 = U \cap M$; then $\tilde{M}_{U_1}$ is isometrically isomorphic to a subspace of $\tilde{E}_U$, hence to a subspace of $\ell^p$ for any $p$, by Theorem 7.3. Since closed subspaces of $\ell^p$ are themselves $\ell^p$-type spaces, one shows that $M$ satisfies the nuclearity criterion of Theorem 7.2(c).

For the quotient $E/M$, let $V$ be a convex, circled $0$-neighborhood in $E/M$; its preimage under the quotient map is a $0$-neighborhood $U$ in $E$. By Theorem 7.2(c), there exists a $0$-neighborhood $U_1 \subset U$ in $E$ such that $\phi_{U,U_1}: \tilde{E}_{U_1} \to \tilde{E}_U$ is nuclear. Let $L = \overline{\phi_U(M)}$ in $\tilde{E}_U$ and $L_1 = \overline{\phi_{U_1}(M)}$ in $\tilde{E}_{U_1}$. Then $\tilde{E}_U/L \cong \widetilde{(E/M)}_V$ and $\tilde{E}_{U_1}/L_1 \cong \widetilde{(E/M)}_{V_1}$ for corresponding neighborhoods $V_1 \subset V$. The map $\phi_{V,V_1}$ is induced by $\phi_{U,U_1}$ via the decomposition into orthogonal complements in the associated Hilbert spaces. Since $\phi_{U,U_1}$ is nuclear, write it as $\sum \lambda_i f_i \otimes y_i$ by (7.1). Decompose $\tilde{E}_{U_1} = L_1 \oplus L_1^\perp$, $\tilde{E}_U = L \oplus L^\perp$, and correspondingly $f_i = f_i' + f_i''$, $y_i = y_i' + y_i''$. Since $\phi_{U,U_1}$ maps $L_1$ into $L$, one obtains
$$\phi_{U,U_1} = \sum_{i} \lambda_i f_i' \otimes y_i' + \sum_i \lambda_i f_i'' \otimes y_i''.$$
The map $\phi_{V,V_1}$ induced on the quotient is then $\sum_i \lambda_i g_i \otimes w_i$, where $g_i$ is the linear form determined by $f_i''$ and $w_i$ is the class of $y_i''$. Hence $\phi_{V,V_1}$ is nuclear by (7.1). The proof is complete.
