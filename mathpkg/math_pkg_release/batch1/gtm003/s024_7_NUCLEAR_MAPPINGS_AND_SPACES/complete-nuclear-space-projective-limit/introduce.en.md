A fundamental structural theorem characterizes complete nuclear spaces as projective limits of Hilbert spaces. This representation is particularly useful because Hilbert spaces are the most well-behaved topological vector spaces. The projective limit structure allows one to reduce many problems about nuclear spaces to the corresponding problems in Hilbert spaces, where the rich theory of operators and spectral analysis is available.
