Theorem 7.3 is a fundamental structural result: every nuclear space embeds as a subspace of a product of $\ell^p$ spaces (for any $p$ with $1 \leq p \leq \infty$). Moreover, the Banach spaces $\tilde{E}_U$ associated with zero-neighborhoods are themselves subspaces of $\ell^p$. This theorem, in the spirit of the Grothendieck-Pietsch representation, shows that nuclear spaces are precisely those that can be realized inside products of the most classical sequence spaces.
