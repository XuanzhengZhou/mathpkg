The projective tensor product of two nuclear spaces is again nuclear. This is one of the most important permanence properties of nuclear spaces, as it shows that the class of nuclear spaces is closed under the natural tensor product operation. Combined with stability under countable limits and colimits, this makes nuclear spaces the natural setting for a large portion of functional analysis, including the theory of distributions and infinite-dimensional holomorphy.
