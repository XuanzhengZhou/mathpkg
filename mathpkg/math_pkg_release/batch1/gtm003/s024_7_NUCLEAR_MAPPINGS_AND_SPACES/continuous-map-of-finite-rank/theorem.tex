Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces. A continuous linear map $u \in \mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is called a \emph{continuous map of finite rank} if it originates from an element $v \in E' \otimes F$ via the canonical imbedding $E' \otimes F \hookrightarrow \mathcal{L}(E, F)$. The \emph{rank} $r$ of $u$ is defined to be the rank of $v$ (as defined in Section 6).
