Every topological vector space has a neighborhood base of closed sets at each point. This follows from the continuity of addition: for any 0-neighborhood $U$, there exists $V$ with $V + V \subset U$, and then $\overline{V} \subset U$. This implies every Hausdorff TVS is regular.
