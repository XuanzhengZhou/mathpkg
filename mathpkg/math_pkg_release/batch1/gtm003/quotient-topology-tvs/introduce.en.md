The quotient topology on $L/M$ is the final topology with respect to the canonical quotient map, making it the finest topology for which the quotient map is continuous. In topological vector spaces, the quotient map is always open, and the quotient is Hausdorff precisely when the subspace $M$ is closed. This construction is essential for studying exact sequences and duality.
