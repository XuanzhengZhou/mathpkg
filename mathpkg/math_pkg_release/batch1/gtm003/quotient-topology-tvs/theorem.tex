Let $(L, \mathfrak{T})$ be a t.v.s. over $K$ and $M$ a subspace. Let $\phi$ be the natural (canonical, quotient) map of $L$ onto $L/M$. The quotient topology $\hat{\mathfrak{T}}$ is the finest topology on $L/M$ for which $\phi$ is continuous. Then $\phi$ is an open map, and $\phi(\mathfrak{B})$ is a 0-neighborhood base in $L/M$ for every 0-neighborhood base $\mathfrak{B}$ in $L$. Moreover, $L/M$ is Hausdorff if and only if $M$ is closed in $L$.
