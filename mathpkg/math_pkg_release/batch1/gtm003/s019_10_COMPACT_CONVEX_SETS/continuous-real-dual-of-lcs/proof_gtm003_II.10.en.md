[Note: The original text of this corollary is truncated in the source extraction. The proof follows from Theorem 10.1: for any $y \notin C$ where $C$ is a compact convex set, there exists a closed real hyperplane strictly separating $\{y\}$ and $C$. Applying this to singletons, for distinct $x, y \in E$, take $C = \{x\}$ (which is compact and convex); then there exists a closed real hyperplane $H = \{z : f(z) = \gamma\}$ with $f \in E'_0$ separating $x$ and $y$, so $f(x) \neq f(y)$.]
