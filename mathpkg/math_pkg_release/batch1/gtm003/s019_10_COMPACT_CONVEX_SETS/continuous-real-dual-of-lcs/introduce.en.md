Let $E$ be a locally convex space and denote by $E'_0$ the vector space of all continuous real linear forms on $E$. As a corollary of Theorem 10.1, the family $E'_0$ separates the points of $E$; that is, for any distinct $x, y \in E$ there exists $f \in E'_0$ such that $f(x) \neq f(y)$. This follows from the fact that compact convex sets (in particular, singletons and points) can be separated by closed real hyperplanes.
