This four-part exercise investigates the bornological properties of products of locally convex spaces. It shows that a linear map from a product into a Banach space that is bounded (maps bounded sets to bounded sets) can only depend on finitely many coordinates, provided the scalar field product is bornological. As a consequence, a product of bornological spaces is bornological when the index set is countable.
