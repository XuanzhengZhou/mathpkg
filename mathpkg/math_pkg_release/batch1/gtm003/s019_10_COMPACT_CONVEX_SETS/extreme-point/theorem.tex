Let $A$ be a convex subset of a vector space. A subset $S \subset M$ is said to support $A$ if $M \cap A \neq \emptyset$ and $A$ lies entirely in one of the closed half-spaces determined by the affine manifold $M$. An extreme point of $A$ is a point $x_0 \in A$ such that $\{x_0\}$ supports $A$; equivalently, $x_0$ is not expressible as a proper convex combination of two distinct points of $A$.
