This exercise constructs a concrete counterexample in the product space $\mathbb{R}_0^X$ (where $X = C([0,1])$) showing that in infinite-dimensional locally convex spaces, the convex hull of a compact set need not be closed, and the closed convex hull of a compact set need not be compact. The key idea uses evaluation functionals $\phi_t$ and a Riemann integral element that lies in the closure but not in the convex hull itself.
