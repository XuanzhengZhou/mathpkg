Let $C$ be a compact convex set in a locally convex space and $\mathcal{E}$ the set of its extreme points. As a corollary of Theorem 10.5, $\mathcal{E}$ is the unique minimal closed subset of $C$ whose closed convex hull recovers $C$. In other words, any closed subset $F \subset C$ with $\overline{\operatorname{co}}(F) = C$ must contain $\mathcal{E}$. However, in infinite dimensions, $\mathcal{E}$ need not be closed; it is generally only dense in $C$ (see Exercise 29).
