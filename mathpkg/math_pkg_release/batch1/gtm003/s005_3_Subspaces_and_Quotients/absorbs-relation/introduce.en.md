The absorbs relation between subsets of a vector space over a non-discrete valuated field is a foundational concept in the theory of topological vector spaces. A set A absorbs B if, upon scaling A by sufficiently large scalars, it eventually covers B. This notion is used to define radial (absorbing) sets, which generalize the idea of a set containing a neighborhood of zero in topological vector spaces. The absorbs relation is central to the definition of bornivorous sets and the gauge (Minkowski functional) of a set.
