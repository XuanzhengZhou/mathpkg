A non-discrete valuated field is a valuated field whose topology is not the discrete topology. This means the absolute value takes values other than just 0 and 1; in particular, there exist elements with arbitrarily small positive absolute value. Non-discrete valuated fields are the natural scalar fields for topological vector spaces, since the discrete case would trivialize the topological structure. Any non-discrete valuated field is necessarily infinite.
