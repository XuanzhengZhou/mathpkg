Tychonoff's theorem: over a complete field, there is exactly one Hausdorff TVS topology on any finite-dimensional vector space — the product topology (or equivalently, any norm topology). This fundamental result implies that all linear maps between finite-dimensional Hausdorff TVS are automatically continuous, and finite-dimensional subspaces are always complete and closed.
