Let $K$ be a complete valuated field. Every $n$-dimensional Hausdorff t.v.s. $L$ over $K$ is isomorphic to $K_0^n$. More precisely, if $\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ is a basis of $L$, the mapping $(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n) \mapsto \lambda_1 x_1 + \cdots + \lambda_n x_n$ is an isomorphism of $K_0^n$ onto $L$, and every isomorphism of $K_0^n$ onto $L$ is of this form. Consequently, the product topology on $K_0^n$ is the only Hausdorff topology on an $n$-dimensional vector space over $K$ satisfying $(LT)_1$ and $(LT)_2$ (Tychonoff).
