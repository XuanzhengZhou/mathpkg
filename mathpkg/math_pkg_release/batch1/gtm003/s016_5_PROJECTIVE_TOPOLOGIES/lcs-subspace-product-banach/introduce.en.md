Every locally convex space (not necessarily complete) can be embedded as a subspace of a product of Banach spaces. This follows from Theorem 5.4 by first taking the completion, which is a complete l.c.s. and hence a projective limit (and thus a subspace) of a product of Banach spaces. This embedding theorem shows that Banach spaces are universal in the category of locally convex spaces.
