Since the $f_\alpha$ are linear and the $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ are locally convex, each $U_\alpha$ in the neighborhood base can be chosen convex and circled. The inverse image $f_\alpha^{-1}(U_\alpha)$ of a convex (resp. circled) set under a linear map is convex (resp. circled), and finite intersections of convex, circled sets remain convex and circled. Thus the neighborhood base of $0$ for $\mathfrak{T}$ consists of convex, circled sets. Moreover, $\mathfrak{T}$ is translation-invariant because each $f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ is translation-invariant (being the inverse image of a translation-invariant topology under a linear map) and the upper bound of translation-invariant topologies is translation-invariant. The conditions (a) and (b) of (I, 1.2) — that $0$-neighborhoods are absorbent and contain scaled versions — follow from the corresponding properties in each $E_\alpha$ via the linearity of $f_\alpha$. Hence $\mathfrak{T}$ satisfies all axioms of a locally convex topology.
