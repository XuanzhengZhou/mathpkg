A fundamental fact about the projective topology is that it preserves local convexity: if each target space $E_\alpha$ is a locally convex space and each map $f_\alpha$ is linear, then the resulting projective topology on $E$ is again locally convex. This ensures that the category of locally convex spaces is closed under the projective construction, making it a powerful tool for building new locally convex spaces from old ones.
