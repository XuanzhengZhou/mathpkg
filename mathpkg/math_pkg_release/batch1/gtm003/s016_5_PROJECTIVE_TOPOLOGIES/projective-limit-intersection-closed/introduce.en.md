A projective limit of Hausdorff locally convex spaces can be realized as a closed subspace of the product of the constituent spaces. Specifically, it is the intersection of the closed subspaces $V_{\alpha\beta}$ defined by the compatibility conditions $x_\alpha = g_{\alpha\beta}(x_\beta)$. This embedding is fundamental for transferring properties (such as completeness) from products to projective limits.
