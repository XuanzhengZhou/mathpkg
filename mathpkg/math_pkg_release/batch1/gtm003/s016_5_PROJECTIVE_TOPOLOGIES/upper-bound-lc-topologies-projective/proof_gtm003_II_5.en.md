The projective topology on $E$ with respect to $\{(E, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha, e): \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ is, by definition, the coarsest topology on $E$ making the identity map $e: (E, \text{this topology}) \to (E, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ continuous for each $\alpha$. The identity map is continuous from $(E, \mathfrak{S})$ to $(E, \mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ if and only if $\mathfrak{S}$ is finer than $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$. Thus the coarsest topology finer than all $\mathfrak{T}_\alpha$ is precisely their least upper bound $\mathfrak{T}$ in the lattice of topologies on $E$. Since this topology is a projective topology of locally convex topologies under linear maps, it is locally convex.
