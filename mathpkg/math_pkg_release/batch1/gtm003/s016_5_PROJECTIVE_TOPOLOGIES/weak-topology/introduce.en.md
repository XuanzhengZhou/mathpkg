The weak topology $\sigma(E, F)$ is the coarsest topology on $E$ making every linear functional $f \in F$ continuous, where $F$ is a subset of the algebraic dual $E^*$. It is a special case of the projective topology where each target space is the one-dimensional field $K$ with its standard topology. Weak topologies play a central role in functional analysis, particularly in duality theory and the study of reflexive spaces.
