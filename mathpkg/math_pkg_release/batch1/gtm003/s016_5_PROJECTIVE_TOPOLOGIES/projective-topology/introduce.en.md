The projective topology is the coarsest topology on a vector space $E$ that makes a given family of linear maps $f_\alpha \colon E \to E_\alpha$ continuous, where each codomain $E_\alpha$ carries a locally convex topology. It generalizes the notions of subspace topology, product topology, and weak topology. The projective topology is fundamental in the theory of locally convex spaces, as it provides a unified framework for constructing new locally convex topologies from existing ones.
