The projective limit $\varprojlim g_{\mathbf{H}, \Lambda} E_\Lambda$ consists of all threads $(x_{\mathbf{H}})_{\mathbf{H} \in \mathcal{H}}$ with $x_{\mathbf{H}} \in E_{\mathbf{H}}$ and $g_{\mathbf{H}, \Lambda}(x_\Lambda) = x_{\mathbf{H}}$ whenever $\mathbf{H} \subset \Lambda$. For any $x = (x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in \mathbf{A}} \in \prod_\alpha E_\alpha$, the assignment $x_{\mathbf{H}} = (x_\alpha)_{\alpha \in \mathbf{H}}$ defines a thread. Conversely, given a thread $(x_{\mathbf{H}})_{\mathbf{H}}$, define $x_\alpha$ as the $\alpha$-coordinate of $x_{\{\alpha\}}$; the compatibility condition ensures consistency, yielding an element of $\prod_\alpha E_\alpha$. These maps are mutual inverses and are continuous with respect to the respective topologies, giving the isomorphism.
