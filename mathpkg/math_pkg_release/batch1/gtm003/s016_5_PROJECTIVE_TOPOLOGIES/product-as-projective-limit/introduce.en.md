An arbitrary product of locally convex spaces can be expressed as a projective limit of finite sub-products. This representation is useful because it reduces statements about arbitrary products to statements about finite products and the general theory of projective limits. For instance, it allows properties of finite products (which are often easier to verify) to be lifted to arbitrary products via the projective limit machinery.
