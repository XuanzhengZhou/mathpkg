Since every Fréchet space is complete and metrizable, its topology is generated by a countable family of semi-norms. Applying Theorem 5.4 yields that a Fréchet space can be represented as a projective limit of a sequence (rather than an arbitrary directed family) of Banach spaces. This sequential representation is particularly useful in applications, as sequential projective limits are more tractable than general ones.
