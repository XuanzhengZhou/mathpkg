A Fréchet space $E$ is a complete metrizable locally convex space, so its topology can be generated by a countable increasing family $\{p_n : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ of semi-norms. Apply the construction in the proof of Theorem 5.4 with $\mathcal{P} = \{p_n : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$. The directed set $\mathcal{P}$ is now countable and totally ordered by $n \leq m$. The resulting projective system $\{\tilde{E}_n, g_{nm}\}$ is indexed by $\mathbb{N}$, giving an isomorphism $E \cong \varprojlim_n \tilde{E}_n$ as a projective limit of a sequence of Banach spaces.
