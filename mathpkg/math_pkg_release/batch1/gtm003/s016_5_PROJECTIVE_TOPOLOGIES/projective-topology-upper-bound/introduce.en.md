The projective topology can be characterized as the upper bound (supremum) of the inverse image topologies $f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ in the lattice of all topologies on $E$. This means $\mathfrak{T}$ is the coarsest topology that is finer than each $f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$. This lattice-theoretic characterization clarifies why the projective topology is also called the initial topology for the family $\{f_\alpha\}$.
