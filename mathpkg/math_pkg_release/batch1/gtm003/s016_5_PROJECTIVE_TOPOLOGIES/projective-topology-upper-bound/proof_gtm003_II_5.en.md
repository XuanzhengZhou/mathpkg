Since $\mathfrak{T}$ is the coarsest topology on $E$ making each $f_\alpha$ continuous, $f_\alpha$ is continuous for each $\alpha \in \mathbf{A}$ with respect to $\mathfrak{T}$. This is equivalent to $\mathfrak{T}$ being finer than $f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ for each $\alpha$. Conversely, any topology finer than all $f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha)$ makes all $f_\alpha$ continuous, so the coarsest such topology is precisely the upper bound of $\{f_\alpha^{-1}(\mathfrak{T}_\alpha): \alpha \in \mathbf{A}\}$ in the lattice of topologies on $E$.
