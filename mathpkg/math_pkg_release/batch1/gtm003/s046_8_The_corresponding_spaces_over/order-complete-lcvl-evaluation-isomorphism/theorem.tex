Let $(E, \mathfrak{T})$ be an order complete l.c.v.l., and let $E''$ be endowed with its natural topology and canonical order (under which it is an order complete l.c.v.l.). The following assertions are equivalent:
(a) Under evaluation, $E$ is isomorphic with an order complete sublattice of $E''$.
(b) For every majorized, directed $(\leq)$ subset $S$ of $E$, the section filter of $S$ $\mathfrak{T}$-converges to $\sup S$.
(c) Every order convergent filter in $E$ $\mathfrak{T}$-converges to its order limit.
