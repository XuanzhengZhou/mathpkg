Let $E$ be an ordered real Banach space with positive cone $C \neq \{0\}$. A continuous, positive endomorphism $u$ of $E$ is called **irreducible** if for some scalar $\lambda > r(u)$ and each non-zero $x \in C$, the element
$$u R(\lambda, u) x = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda^{-n} u^n(x)$$
is a quasi-interior point of $C$. Here $R(\lambda, u) = (\lambda - u)^{-1}$ denotes the resolvent operator of $u$ at $\lambda$.
