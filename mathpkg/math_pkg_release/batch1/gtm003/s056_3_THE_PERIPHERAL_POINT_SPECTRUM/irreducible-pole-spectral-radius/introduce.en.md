This theorem describes the spectral properties of an irreducible positive endomorphism $u$ on an ordered Banach space when the spectral radius $r(u)$ is a pole of the resolvent. Under this hypothesis, $r(u)$ is strictly positive and a simple pole; positive eigenvectors exist for both $u$ and its adjoint, with strong positivity properties (quasi-interior for $u$, strictly positive for $u'$). In many natural settings -- non-empty interior of the cone, finite multiplicity, or Banach lattice structure -- the multiplicity of the spectral radius is exactly 1.
