This fundamental theorem describes the peripheral point spectrum of an irreducible positive endomorphism acting on the space $\mathcal{C}_{\mathbb{R}}(X)$ of continuous real functions on a compact space $X$. The peripheral point spectrum is always cyclic -- closed under multiplication by roots of unity -- and every peripheral eigenvalue has multiplicity 1 with a nowhere-vanishing eigenfunction. When the peripheral point spectrum contains an isolated point, it is a finite cyclic group; when it contains a pole of the resolvent, all peripheral points are simple poles. The spectral radius $r$ is the unique eigenvalue admitting a non-negative eigenfunction.
