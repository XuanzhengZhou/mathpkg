**Proof.** The map $h: f \mapsto \int f \, d\mu$ is a strictly positive linear form on $L^1(\mu)$. The condition $\|u\| = r(u)$ implies that for every $f \geq 0$,
$$\langle u(f), h \rangle = \int u(f) \, d\mu \leq \|u\| \int f \, d\mu = r(u) \langle f, h \rangle.$$
It follows that $u'(h) \leq r(u) h$. Thus $u$ satisfies the hypotheses of Theorem (3.4), and the conclusion follows immediately.
