This theorem generalizes the cyclicity of the peripheral point spectrum from $\mathcal{C}(X)$ spaces to a broader class of Banach lattices whose complexification admits an extended absolute value. The key hypothesis is the existence of a strictly positive linear form $x'_0$ satisfying $u'(x'_0) \leq r x'_0$. Under this condition, if the peripheral point spectrum contains a point $r\alpha$ on the spectral circle, it automatically contains the entire cyclic group generated by $\alpha$. This result applies in particular to the spaces $L^p(\mu)$ for $1 \leq p \leq \infty$.
