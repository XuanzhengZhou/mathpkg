This proposition gives a simple lattice-theoretic characterization of irreducibility for positive endomorphisms of Banach lattices. A non-zero positive endomorphism is irreducible precisely when it has no non-trivial closed solid invariant subspaces. This explains the terminology: such an operator acts "irreducibly" on the lattice in the sense that it does not decompose as a direct sum of operators on smaller solid sublattices.
