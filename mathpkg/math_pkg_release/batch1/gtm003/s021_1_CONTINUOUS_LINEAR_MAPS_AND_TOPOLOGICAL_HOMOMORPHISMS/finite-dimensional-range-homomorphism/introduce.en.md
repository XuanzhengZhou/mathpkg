Every continuous linear map whose range is a separated, finite-dimensional topological vector space is automatically a topological homomorphism. The key point is that a finite-dimensional Hausdorff t.v.s. has a unique vector space topology, making any linear bijection onto it an isomorphism. Since the null space of a topological homomorphism must be closed, the three conditions — being a topological homomorphism, having closed null space, and being open — are all equivalent for maps with finite-dimensional separated range.
