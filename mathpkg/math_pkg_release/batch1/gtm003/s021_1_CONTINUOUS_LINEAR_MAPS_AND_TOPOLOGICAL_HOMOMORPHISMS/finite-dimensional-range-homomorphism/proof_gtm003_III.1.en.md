(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b): Since $u(L)$ is Hausdorff, $\{0\}$ is closed in $u(L)$. By continuity of $u$, the preimage $u^{-1}(0)$ is closed in $L$.

(b) $\Rightarrow$ (c): If $u^{-1}(0)$ is closed, then $L/u^{-1}(0)$ is a Hausdorff t.v.s. of finite dimension (by the isomorphism $L/u^{-1}(0) \cong u(L)$ as vector spaces). Since a finite-dimensional Hausdorff t.v.s. over $K$ admits a unique vector space topology (Chapter I, 3.4), the bijective algebraic homomorphism $u_0: L/u^{-1}(0) \to u(L)$ is necessarily an isomorphism of t.v.s. It follows from Proposition 1.2 that $u$ is a topological homomorphism, hence open onto $u(L)$.

(c) $\Rightarrow$ (a) is clear, since being open onto the image is the defining property of a topological homomorphism.
