A continuous linear bijection from a complete metrizable topological vector space onto a Hausdorff t.v.s. is automatically an isomorphism — the inverse map is continuous. This follows from Banach's homomorphism theorem because the image is the whole space (hence not meager) and the map is injective (so its null space is trivial). This is the t.v.s. analogue of the bounded inverse theorem for Banach spaces.
