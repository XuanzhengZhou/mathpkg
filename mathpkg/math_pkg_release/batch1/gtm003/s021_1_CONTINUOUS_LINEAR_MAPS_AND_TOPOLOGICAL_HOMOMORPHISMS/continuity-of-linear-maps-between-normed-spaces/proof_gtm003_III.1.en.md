This follows immediately from Proposition 1.1, since the topology of a normed space is generated by the single seminorm $x \mapsto \|x\|$. The condition from 1.1 reduces to $\|u(x)\| \leq c \|x\|$ for some $c > 0$ and all $x \in L$.

Alternatively, a direct argument: if $\|u(x)\| \leq c\|x\|$, then for any $\varepsilon > 0$, $\|x\| \leq \varepsilon/c$ implies $\|u(x)\| \leq \varepsilon$, so $u$ is continuous at $0$. Conversely, if $u$ is continuous, $u^{-1}(\{y: \|y\| \leq 1\})$ is a $0$-neighborhood, hence contains a ball $\{x: \|x\| \leq 1/c\}$ for some $c > 0$. Then $\|u(x)\| \leq c\|x\|$ for all $x$.
