A continuous linear map is a topological homomorphism precisely when the bijective map induced between the quotient by the null space and the image is an isomorphism of topological vector spaces. In other words, a topological homomorphism is exactly a linear map that, after factoring out its kernel, becomes an isomorphism onto its image. This provides a clean structural characterization: the map is open onto its image if and only if the induced bijection is a homeomorphism as well as a linear isomorphism.
