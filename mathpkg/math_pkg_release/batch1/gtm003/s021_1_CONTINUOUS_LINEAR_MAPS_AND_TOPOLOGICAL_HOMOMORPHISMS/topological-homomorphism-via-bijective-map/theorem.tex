Let $L$ and $M$ be topological vector spaces over $K$ and let $u: L \to M$ be a continuous linear map. Let

$$L \xrightarrow{\phi} L/N \xrightarrow{u_0} u(L) \xrightarrow{\psi} M$$

be the canonical decomposition of $u$, where $N = u^{-1}(0)$ and $u_0$ is the bijective map associated with $u$.

Then $u$ is a topological homomorphism if and only if $u_0$ is an isomorphism of topological vector spaces.
