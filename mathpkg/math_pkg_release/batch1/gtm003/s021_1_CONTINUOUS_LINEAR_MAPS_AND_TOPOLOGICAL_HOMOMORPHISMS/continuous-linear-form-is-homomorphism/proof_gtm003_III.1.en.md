A continuous linear form $f: L \to K$ has range either $\{0\}$ or $K$. In either case, the range is a separated (Hausdorff) topological vector space of finite dimension (at most $1$). By Proposition 1.3, any continuous linear map with finite-dimensional separated range is a topological homomorphism. Hence $f$ is a topological homomorphism.
