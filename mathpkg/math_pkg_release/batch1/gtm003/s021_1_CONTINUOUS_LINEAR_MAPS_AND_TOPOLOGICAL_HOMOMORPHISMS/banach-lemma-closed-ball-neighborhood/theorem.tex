Let $L$ and $M$ be metric topological vector spaces whose respective metrics $d, \delta$ are given by pseudonorms:

$$d(x_1, x_2) = |x_1 - x_2|, \qquad \delta(y_1, y_2) = |y_1 - y_2|.$$

Denote the closed balls of center $0$ and radius $r, \rho$ by

$$S_r = \{x \in L: |x| \leq r\}, \qquad S_\rho = \{y \in M: |y| \leq \rho\}.$$

Let $L$ be complete and let $u: L \to M$ be a continuous linear map. Suppose that for some $r > 0$, the set $u(S_r)$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $\overline{u(L)}$ (the closure of $u(L)$ in $M$). Then $u$ is a topological homomorphism of $L$ onto $\overline{u(L)}$, and in particular, $u(S_{2r})$ is a $0$-neighborhood in $\overline{u(L)}$.
