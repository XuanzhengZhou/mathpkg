If the same vector space admits two complete metrizable topologies, one finer than the other, then they must coincide. This follows by applying Corollary 1 to the identity map from the finer to the coarser topology. In particular, a Fréchet space topology on a given vector space is unique — no strictly weaker complete metrizable topology exists, and no strictly stronger complete metrizable topology exists either.
