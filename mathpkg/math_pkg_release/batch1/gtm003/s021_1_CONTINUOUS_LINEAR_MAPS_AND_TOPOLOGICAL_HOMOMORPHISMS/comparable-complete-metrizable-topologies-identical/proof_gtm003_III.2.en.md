Consider the identity map $\mathrm{id}: (L, \mathfrak{T}_1) \to (L, \mathfrak{T}_2)$. Since $\mathfrak{T}_1$ is finer than $\mathfrak{T}_2$, the identity map is continuous. It is bijective. The domain $(L, \mathfrak{T}_1)$ is complete and metrizable by hypothesis, and the codomain $(L, \mathfrak{T}_2)$ is Hausdorff (as a metrizable t.v.s.). By Corollary 1, $\mathrm{id}$ is an isomorphism, so its inverse is continuous. This means $\mathfrak{T}_1 \subset \mathfrak{T}_2$, hence $\mathfrak{T}_1 = \mathfrak{T}_2$.
