In a locally convex space, the topology is generated by a family of seminorms. A linear map between such spaces is continuous precisely when the pullback of each seminorm from the target space is dominated by a finite supremum of seminorms from the source space. This is the natural generalization of the operator norm bound from Banach space theory to the setting of locally convex spaces.
