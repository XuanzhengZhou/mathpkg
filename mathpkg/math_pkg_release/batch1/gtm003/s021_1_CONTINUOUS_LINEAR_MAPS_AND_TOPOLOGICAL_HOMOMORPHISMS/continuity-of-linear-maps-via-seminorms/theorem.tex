Let the topologies of $L$ and $M$ be given by the families of seminorms $\mathcal{P}$ and $\mathcal{Q}$, respectively. A linear map $u: L \to M$ is continuous if and only if for every seminorm $q \in \mathcal{Q}$ there exist finitely many seminorms $p_1, \ldots, p_n \in \mathcal{P}$ and a constant $c > 0$ such that

$$q(u(x)) \leq c \sup_{i=1,\ldots,n} p_i(x) \qquad \text{for all } x \in L.$$
