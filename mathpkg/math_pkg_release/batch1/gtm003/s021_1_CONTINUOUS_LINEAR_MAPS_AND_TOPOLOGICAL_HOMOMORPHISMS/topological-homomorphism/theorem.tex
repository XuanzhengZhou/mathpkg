Let $L$ and $M$ be topological vector spaces over $K$. A continuous linear map $u: L \to M$ is called a **topological homomorphism** (or briefly, **homomorphism** when no confusion is likely to occur) if for each open subset $G \subset L$, the image $u(G)$ is an open subset of $u(L)$ (for the topology induced by $M$).

Examples of topological homomorphisms are:
\begin{itemize}
\item For any subspace $N$ of $L$, the canonical (quotient) map $\phi: L \to L/N$.
\item For any subspace $H$ of $L$, the canonical imbedding $\psi: H \hookrightarrow L$.
\end{itemize}
