For a linear map between topological vector spaces, continuity is equivalent to continuity at the origin. This is a fundamental simplification: one need only check the behavior of the map near zero. The translation invariance of the vector space topology guarantees that continuity at a single point implies uniform continuity everywhere. Furthermore, when the target space is separated and complete, a continuous linear map on a dense subspace extends uniquely to a continuous linear map on the whole space, and in particular to the completion.
