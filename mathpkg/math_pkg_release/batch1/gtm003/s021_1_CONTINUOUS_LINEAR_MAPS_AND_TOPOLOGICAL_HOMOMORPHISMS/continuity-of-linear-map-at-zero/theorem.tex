Let $L$ and $M$ be topological vector spaces over $K$ and let $u: L \to M$ be a linear map.
Then $u$ is continuous if and only if $u$ is continuous at $0 \in L$.

Moreover, if $u$ is continuous at $0$, then $u$ is uniformly continuous on $L$ into $M$ for the respective uniform structures.

If $u$ is continuous and $M$ is separated and complete, then $u$ has a unique continuous extension $\bar{u}: \bar{L} \to M$, with values in $M$, to any t.v.s. $\bar{L}$ of which $L$ is a dense subspace (in particular, to the completion $\tilde{L}$ of $L$ if $L$ is separated); the extension $\bar{u}$ is linear.
