Since $M$ and $N$ are closed subspaces of the complete metrizable space $L$, they are themselves complete and metrizable. Hence the product $M \times N$ is complete and metrizable. The addition map $\sigma: M \times N \to L$ defined by $\sigma(x_1, x_2) = x_1 + x_2$ is a continuous linear map. By hypothesis, $L = M + N$ algebraically with $M \cap N = \{0\}$, so $\sigma$ is bijective. By Corollary 1 (or directly by Theorem 2.1), $\sigma$ is an isomorphism. Thus $L = M \oplus N$ as a topological direct sum.
