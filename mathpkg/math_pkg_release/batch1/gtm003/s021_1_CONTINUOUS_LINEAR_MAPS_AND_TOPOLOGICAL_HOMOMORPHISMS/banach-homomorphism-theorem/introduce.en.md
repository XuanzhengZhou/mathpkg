Banach's homomorphism theorem (also known as the open mapping theorem for Fréchet spaces) asserts that a continuous linear map from a complete metrizable t.v.s. with dense range is either meager or surjective and a topological homomorphism. This is a fundamental result in functional analysis with far-reaching consequences for the theory of locally convex spaces.
