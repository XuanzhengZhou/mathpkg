A fundamental system of bounded sets in a topological vector space is a family of bounded sets that is cofinal among all bounded sets with respect to inclusion. That is, every bounded subset of the space is contained in some member of the family. This notion is analogous to a neighborhood base at zero and serves to simplify arguments involving boundedness by reducing them to a manageable collection of bounded sets.
