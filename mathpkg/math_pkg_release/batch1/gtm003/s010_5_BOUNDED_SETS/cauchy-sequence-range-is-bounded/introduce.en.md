Any Cauchy sequence in a topological vector space has bounded range. This follows from the definition of a Cauchy sequence: for any zero-neighborhood U, all but finitely many terms lie in a translate of U, and the remaining finitely many terms together with the Cauchy tail form a set that is absorbed by a suitable multiple of any zero-neighborhood.
