Recall that a separated uniform space $P$ is called precompact if the completion $\tilde{P}$ of $P$ is compact. The natural uniform structure of a Hausdorff t.v.s. $L$ is the additive uniform structure generated by the $0$-neighborhood filter. Using the well-known characterization of precompact uniform spaces from the theory of uniform spaces (see Prerequisites), a subset $B$ of a Hausdorff t.v.s. is precompact if and only if for every $0$-neighborhood $U$ there exists a finite set $F \subset L$ such that $B \subset F + U$. Taking $F$ to be a finite subset of $B$ itself (by choosing one point from each nonempty intersection $B \cap (f + U)$ for $f \in F$, or by the equivalent definition), this is precisely the definition of total boundedness. The second characterization follows from the definition of precompactness: the closure of $B$ in the completion $\tilde{L}$ of $L$ is compact if and only if $B$ is precompact in $L$.
