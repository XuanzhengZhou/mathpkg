Let $L$ be a Hausdorff t.v.s. By definition, a subset $K \subset L$ is precompact if and only if the closure of $K$ in the completion $\tilde{L}$ of $L$ is compact. In a complete uniform space, every precompact set has compact closure; thus if $K$ is precompact and complete, $K = \overline{K}$ (in $L$) is compact. Conversely, if $K$ is compact, then $K$ is complete (since every compact subset of a Hausdorff uniform space is complete) and its closure in $\tilde{L}$ coincides with $K$ itself under the canonical embedding, hence $K$ is precompact.
