In a Hausdorff topological vector space, compactness of a subset is equivalent to the conjunction of precompactness and completeness. This generalizes the Heine-Borel theorem in finite dimensions. Precompactness ensures that the subset is totally bounded in the associated uniformity, while completeness guarantees that all Cauchy filters converge within the set. Together, these conditions characterize compact subsets in complete topological vector spaces.
