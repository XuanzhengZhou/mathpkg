A subset of a topological vector space over a non-discrete field is bounded if and only if multiplying its elements by any null sequence of scalars yields a sequence converging to zero. This sequential characterization provides a useful criterion for testing boundedness without directly invoking neighborhoods. The forward direction uses the definition of boundedness via absorption by $0$-neighborhoods; the converse uses a proof by contradiction, choosing appropriate scalars and points outside scaled neighborhoods.
