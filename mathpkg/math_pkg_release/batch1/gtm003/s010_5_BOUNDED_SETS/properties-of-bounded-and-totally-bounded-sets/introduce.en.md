Theorem 5.1 establishes the fundamental stability properties of bounded and totally bounded sets under basic set-theoretic and algebraic operations in a topological vector space. The closure, finite unions, and the circled hull of bounded sets remain bounded. The same holds for totally bounded sets, with the additional hypothesis on the scalar field for the circled hull operation. These properties are essential building blocks for the theory of bornological and barrelled spaces.
