Let $B$ be any bounded subset of $L$. By Theorem 5.1(iii), the circled hull $\Gamma(B)$ of $B$ is bounded. By Theorem 5.1(i), the closure $\overline{\Gamma(B)}$ of the circled hull is bounded. Clearly $\overline{\Gamma(B)}$ is closed and circled, and $B \subset \Gamma(B) \subset \overline{\Gamma(B)}$. Thus every bounded set is contained in a closed, circled bounded set. Therefore, the family of all closed and circled bounded subsets of $L$ forms a fundamental system of bounded sets of $L$.
