A subset of a topological vector space is totally bounded if it can be covered by finitely many translates of any given zero-neighborhood. In the Hausdorff setting, total boundedness is equivalent to precompactness of the subset with respect to the natural uniform structure of the space. The completion of a totally bounded subset in a Hausdorff t.v.s. is compact, making total boundedness a fundamental bridge between boundedness and compactness concepts in topological vector spaces.
