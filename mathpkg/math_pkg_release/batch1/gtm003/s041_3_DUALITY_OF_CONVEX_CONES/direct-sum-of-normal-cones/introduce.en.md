Theorem 3.2 extends the product result to locally convex direct sums. Given a family of locally convex spaces $L_\alpha$ with cones $C_\alpha$, the direct sum cone $C = \bigoplus_\alpha C_\alpha$ in the locally convex direct sum $L = \bigoplus_\alpha L_\alpha$ is normal exactly when every factor cone $C_\alpha$ is normal. The proof uses the characterization of normal cones via convex, circled, $C$-saturated $0$-neighborhoods (condition (d) of Theorem 3.1), leveraging the fact that each summand can be identified with a subspace.
