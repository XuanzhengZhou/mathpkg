Theorem 3.1 provides several equivalent characterizations of a normal cone in a topological vector space. A cone is normal precisely when the $C$-saturated hulls of $0$-neighborhoods form a $0$-neighborhood base, or equivalently when there exists a $0$-neighborhood base $\mathfrak{B}$ satisfying $[V \cap C] \subset V$ for all $V \in \mathfrak{B}$. In the locally convex case over $\mathbb{R}$, normality is further equivalent to the existence of a base of convex, circled, $C$-saturated $0$-neighborhoods, and to the topology being generated by seminorms monotone with respect to the order induced by $C$.
