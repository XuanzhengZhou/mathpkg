Let $E = \prod_{\alpha \in A} H_{\alpha}$ be a product of Hilbert spaces. There exists a $0$-neighborhood base $\mathfrak{V}$ in $E$ such that for each $V \in \mathfrak{V}$, $\tilde{E}_V$ is isomorphic with the Hilbert space $\prod_{\alpha \in \Phi} H_{\alpha}$, where $\Phi$ is a suitable finite subset of $A$; thus $\tilde{E}_V$ has the approximation property. If $M$ is a subspace of $E$ and $W = M \cap V$ ($V \in \mathfrak{V}$), then $\tilde{M}_W$ can be identified with the closure of $\phi_V(M)$ in $\tilde{E}_V$ (Exercise 3); hence $\tilde{M}_W$ is a Hilbert space if $\tilde{E}_V$ is.
