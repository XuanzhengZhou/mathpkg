Theorem 9.5 characterizes the approximation property for Banach spaces in terms of compact operators: $E$ has the approximation property if and only if every compact map into $E$ can be approximated in operator norm by finite-rank operators from $F' \otimes E$. Dually, $E'$ has the approximation property exactly when every compact map from $E$ can be approximated by elements of $E' \otimes F$. The theorem also reveals a remarkable symmetry: while (a) and (c) are not proved equivalent here (this requires deeper duality results from Chapter IV), the equivalence (a)⇔(b) and (c)⇔(d) already provides powerful tools for analyzing the approximation property. The proof of (a)⇒(b) uses the definition of AP to approximate the identity on the precompact image of the unit ball under a compact map.
