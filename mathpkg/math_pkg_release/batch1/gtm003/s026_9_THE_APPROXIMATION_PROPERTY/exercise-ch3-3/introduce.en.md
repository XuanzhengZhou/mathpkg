An exercise showing that every Hilbert space has the approximation property. The proof uses orthonormal bases: the identity operator is the strong limit of the sequence of orthogonal projections onto finite-dimensional subspaces spanned by initial segments of an orthonormal basis. Since these projections are of finite rank and converge uniformly on compact sets, the approximation property follows.
