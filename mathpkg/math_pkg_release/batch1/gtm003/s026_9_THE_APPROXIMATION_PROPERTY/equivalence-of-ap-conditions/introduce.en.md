This proposition establishes that the four defining conditions of the approximation property for a locally convex space are mutually equivalent. The proof proceeds by showing (a) implies (b), (b) implies (c) and (d), with (c) and (d) each clearly implying (b) by specialization. The key argument for (a)⇒(b) uses that for any continuous linear map $u$, composing the approximate identity $e_0$ with $u$ yields a finite-rank approximation $u \circ e_0$ of $u$ uniformly on precompact sets. The implications (b)⇒(c) and (b)⇒(d) exploit the continuity of pre-composition and post-composition with fixed linear maps in the topology of compact convergence.
