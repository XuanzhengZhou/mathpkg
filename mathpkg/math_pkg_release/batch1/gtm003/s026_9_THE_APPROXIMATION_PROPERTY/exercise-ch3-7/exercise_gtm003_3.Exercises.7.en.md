Let $E, F$ be locally convex spaces such that $F \neq \{0\}$, and let $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ be saturated families of bounded subsets of $E$ with $\mathfrak{S}_1 \subset \mathfrak{S}_2$. If $\mathfrak{S}_1 \neq \mathfrak{S}_2$, the $\mathfrak{S}_2$-topology is strictly finer than the $\mathfrak{S}_1$-topology on $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$. Deduce from this that, under the conditions stated, the family of $\mathfrak{S}$-topologies on $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$ is in biunivocal correspondence with the saturated families of bounded subsets of $E$.
