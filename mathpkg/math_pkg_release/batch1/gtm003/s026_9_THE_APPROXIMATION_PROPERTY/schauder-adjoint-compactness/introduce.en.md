Schauder's theorem establishes the fundamental duality between compactness of a continuous linear map and compactness of its adjoint. For normed spaces $E, F$ with $F$ complete, a map $u: E \to F$ is compact precisely when its adjoint $u': F' \to E'$ is compact. The adjoint $u'$ is defined by $u'(y') = y' \circ u$, and the Hahn-Banach theorem ensures $\|u'\| = \|u\|$. The proof uses the Alaoğlu-Bourbaki theorem (compactness of the dual unit ball in the weak-* topology) and metric properties of the dual spaces to relate precompactness of images under $u$ to precompactness of images under $u'$.
