(Theorem of Osgood). Let $X$ be a non-empty topological space which is a Baire space.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] Let $\{f_n\}$ be a pointwise convergent sequence of continuous functions on $X$ with values in a metric space $Y$; the set of points where the sequence is equicontinuous is not meager in $X$.

\item[(b)] Let $\{f_n\}$ be a simply bounded sequence of continuous functions with values in $F$, where $F$ is a locally convex space; the set of points where $\{f_n\}$ is equicontinuous is not meager in $X$.
\end{enumerate}
