An exercise referencing the celebrated result of Per Enflo (1973) that not every Banach space has the approximation property. This solved a long-standing open problem of Banach and was a major breakthrough in functional analysis. The exercise guides the reader through the construction or references the counterexample, which involves subtle combinatorial and geometric arguments about the structure of certain Banach spaces.
