This proposition establishes that the collection of compact linear maps between a normed space $E$ and a quasi-complete locally convex space $F$ forms a closed vector subspace of $\mathcal{L}_b(E, F)$, the space of all continuous linear maps endowed with the topology of bounded convergence. When $E$ and $F$ are both normed spaces, $\mathcal{L}_b(E, F)$ coincides with the normed space $\mathcal{L}(E, F)$. This result is fundamental for the study of compact operators and their approximation by finite-rank operators, which is intimately connected to the approximation property.
