The approximation property is a fundamental concept in the theory of locally convex spaces that captures whether the identity operator can be uniformly approximated on precompact sets by continuous finite-rank operators. The definition is motivated by the Hilbert space case, where an orthonormal basis expansion $x = \sum_{\alpha} f_\alpha(x) x_\alpha$ provides such an approximation via finite partial sums. The property admits four equivalent formulations involving the density of tensor products $E' \otimes E$, $E' \otimes F$, and $F' \otimes E$ in appropriate spaces of continuous linear maps endowed with the topology of uniform convergence on precompact sets. The question of whether every Banach space possesses the approximation property was a major open problem, eventually resolved negatively by Enflo (1973).
