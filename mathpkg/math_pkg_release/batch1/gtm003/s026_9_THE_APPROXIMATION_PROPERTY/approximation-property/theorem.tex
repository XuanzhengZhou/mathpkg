Let $E$ be a locally convex space. $E$ is said to have the \textbf{approximation property} if it satisfies the following four equivalent conditions:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] The identity map $e$ of $E$ lies in the closure of $E' \otimes E$ in $\mathcal{L}_c(E)$; i.e., for every precompact subset $A \subset E$ and every 0-neighborhood $V$ in $E$, there exists $e_0 \in E' \otimes E$ such that $x - e_0(x) \in V$ for all $x \in A$.

\item[(b)] $E' \otimes E$ is dense in $\mathcal{L}_c(E)$.

\item[(c)] For every locally convex space $F$, $E' \otimes F$ is dense in $\mathcal{L}_c(E, F)$.

\item[(d)] For every locally convex space $F$, $F' \otimes E$ is dense in $\mathcal{L}_c(F, E)$.
\end{itemize}
