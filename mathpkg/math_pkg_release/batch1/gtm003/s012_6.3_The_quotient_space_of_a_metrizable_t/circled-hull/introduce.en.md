The circled hull (sometimes called the balanced hull) of a subset $A$ of a vector space is the smallest circled set containing $A$. It is given explicitly by $\{\lambda a : a \in A, |\lambda| \leq 1\}$. Since the property of being circled is intersection-invariant, the circled hull is well-defined as the intersection of all circled supersets of $A$.
