Let $L$ be a vector space over a subfield $K = H + iH$ of $\mathbb{C}$, where $H$ is a subfield of $\mathbb{R}$. Call a real subspace $N$ of $L$ **properly real** if $N \cap iN = \{0\}$. There exists a properly real subspace $M$ of $L$ such that $L = M + iM$. (Use Zorn's lemma.)
