This proposition provides a canonical method to embed any real vector space $L$ into a complex vector space over $H(i)$. The construction uses the product $L \times L$ with the automorphism $(x, y) \mapsto (-y, x)$, which plays the role of multiplication by $i$. The map $x \mapsto (x, 0)$ embeds $L$ as a real subspace, and every element of the complexified space can be uniquely written as $x + iy$. This construction is fundamental for spectral theory.
