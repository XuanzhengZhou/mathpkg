If $L$ is a vector space over a field $H \subset \mathbb{R}$, consider the product $L \times L$ over $H$. The mapping $u: (x, y) \rightarrow (-y, x)$ is an automorphism of $L \times L$ satisfying $u^2 = -e$; thus scalar multiplication can be extended to $K = H(i)$ on $L \times L$ by $(\lambda + i\mu)(x, y) = \lambda(x, y) + \mu u(x, y)$. Writing $(x, 0) = x$ for all $x \in L$, each $z \in L \times L$ has a unique representation $z = x + iy$ with $x, y \in L$.
