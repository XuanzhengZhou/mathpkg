(a) $\Rightarrow$ (c): Since by (1.4), $|p(x) - p(y)| \leq p(x - y)$ for all $x, y \in L$, continuity of $p$ at $0$ implies uniform continuity on $L$.

(b) $\Rightarrow$ (a): If $M_0$ is open, then for any $\varepsilon > 0$, the set $\varepsilon M_0 = \{x : p(x) < \varepsilon\}$ is a neighborhood of $0$, proving continuity at $0$.

(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b): If $p$ is continuous at $0$, then $M_0 = p^{-1}((-\infty, 1))$ is the inverse image of an open set under a continuous function, hence open.
