This theorem shows that over a complex field $K = H(i)$, any hyperplane in $L$ can be uniquely expressed as the intersection of two real hyperplanes. Specifically, if the hyperplane is given by $\{x : f(x) = \alpha + i\beta\}$ with $f \in L^*$, then it equals $\{x : g(x) = \alpha\} \cap \{x : g(ix) = -\beta\}$ where $g = \operatorname{Re} f$. The closedness of the hyperplane corresponds to the closedness of both real hyperplanes.
