By (4.1), a hyperplane $G$ in $L$ is of the form $G = \{x: f(x) = \gamma\}$, where $f \in L^*$ and $\gamma = \alpha + i\beta \in K = H + iH$. If $g$ is the real part of $f$, then, clearly, $G = G_1 \cap G_2$, where $G_1 = \{x: g(x) = \alpha\}$, $G_2 = \{x: g(ix) = -\beta\}$. Since $f$ is determined by $G$ to within a non-zero factor, $G_1$ and $G_2$ are unique. Moreover, by (4.2) and (7.2), $G_1$ and $G_2$ are closed if and only if $G$ is closed in $L$.
