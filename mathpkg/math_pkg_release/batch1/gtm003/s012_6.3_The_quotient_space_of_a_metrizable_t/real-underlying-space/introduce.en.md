When a topological vector space is defined over the complex field $K = H(i) = H + iH$, the restriction of scalar multiplication to the real subfield $H$ produces a topological vector space over $H$, called the real underlying space $L_0$. A real linear form is then simply a linear form on $L_0$, and a real hyperplane is a hyperplane in $L_0$. This construction provides the framework for the relationship between complex and real structures discussed in Section 7.
