The convex hull of a subset $A$ of a vector space is the smallest convex set containing $A$. It consists of all finite convex combinations of elements of $A$. Since convexity is preserved under arbitrary intersections, the convex hull is well-defined. This definition is fundamental for the study of convexity in topological vector spaces.
