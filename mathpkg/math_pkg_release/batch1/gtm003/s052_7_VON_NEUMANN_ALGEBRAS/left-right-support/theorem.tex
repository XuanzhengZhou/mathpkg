Let $x \in W$ be arbitrary, with polar decomposition $x = u|x|$. Then $uu^* =: s_l(x)$ is the smallest projection $p \in W$ (with respect to $\leq$) satisfying $px = x$; by symmetry, $u^*u =: s_r(x)$ is the smallest projection satisfying $xq = x$. In particular, $s_l(x) \sim s_r(x)$.

$s_l(x)$ and $s_r(x)$ are called the \text{left support} and \text{right support} of $x \in W$, respectively. In the representation of $W$ as a von Neumann algebra in $\mathcal{L}(H)$, $s_l(x)$ (respectively, $s_r(x)$) is the orthogonal projection onto the closure of the range of $x$ (respectively, of the range of $x^*$).
