For any element $x$ in a $W^*$-algebra, the polar decomposition $x = u|x|$ yields two canonical projections: the left support $s_l(x) = uu^*$ and the right support $s_r(x) = u^*u$. These are the minimal projections annihilating $x$ from the left and right respectively. A fundamental fact is that the left and right supports are always equivalent ($s_l(x) \sim s_r(x)$). When represented on a Hilbert space, these correspond to the orthogonal projections onto the closures of the ranges of $x$ and $x^*$.
