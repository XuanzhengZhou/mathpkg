Since a factor has trivial center, the type decomposition theorem (8.7) forces it to be purely of a single type: I, II$_1$, II$_\infty$, or III. This corollary reduces the classification of all von Neumann algebras (on separable Hilbert spaces) to the classification of factors of these four types. Type I factors are isomorphic to $\mathcal{L}(H)$ for some Hilbert space $H$; factors of the other types are more subtle to construct and classify.
