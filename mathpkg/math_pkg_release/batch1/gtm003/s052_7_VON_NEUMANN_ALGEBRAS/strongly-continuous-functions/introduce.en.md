Theorem 7.3 establishes that bounded continuous functions on $\mathbb{R}$ induce strongly continuous maps on the self-adjoint part of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ via the continuous functional calculus. The set of strongly continuous functions forms an algebra closed under multiplication by bounded strongly continuous functions. This result is critical for extending approximation arguments in the strong operator topology, particularly in the proof of Kaplansky's density theorem.
