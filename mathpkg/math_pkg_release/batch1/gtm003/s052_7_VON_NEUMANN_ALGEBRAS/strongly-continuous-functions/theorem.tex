Every bounded continuous function $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{C}$ is strongly continuous. Moreover, if $f$ and $g$ are strongly continuous and $f$ is bounded, then $fg$ is strongly continuous.

Here a function $f$ is called \text{strongly continuous} if the map $a \mapsto f(a)$ is continuous for the strong operator topology on $\mathcal{L}(H)_{sa}$, where $f(a) := f_0(a)$ and $f_0$ denotes the restriction of $f$ to $\sigma(a)$.
