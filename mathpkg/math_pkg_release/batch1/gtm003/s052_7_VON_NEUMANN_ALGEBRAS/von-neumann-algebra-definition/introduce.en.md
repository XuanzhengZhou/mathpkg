A von Neumann algebra (also called a $W^*$-algebra) is a $C^*$-subalgebra of bounded operators on a Hilbert space that is closed in the weak operator topology. By von Neumann's bicommutant theorem, this is equivalent to the algebraic condition $M = M^{cc}$ (equality with its double commutant). Von Neumann algebras form the non-commutative analog of measure spaces and serve as the foundation for the theory of operator algebras.
