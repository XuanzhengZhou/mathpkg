A $C^*$-subalgebra $M$ of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ containing the identity is called a \text{von Neumann algebra} (or $W^*$-algebra) if it satisfies the equivalent conditions of the bicommutant theorem (7.1): $M = M^{cc}$, or $M$ is closed in the weak, strong, or $\sigma$-weak operator topology. More generally, a $C^*$-algebra $W$ is called a $W^*$-algebra if it is isometrically isomorphic to a von Neumann algebra in some $\mathcal{L}(H)$.
