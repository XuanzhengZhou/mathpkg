Kaplansky's density theorem strengthens von Neumann's bicommutant theorem by showing that a non-degenerate $C^*$-subalgebra $A$ is not just dense in its double commutant $M = A^{cc}$ in the strong operator topology, but that this density persists when restricting to bounded subsets: the self-adjoint part, unit ball intersections, and unitary groups of $A$ are each strongly dense in the corresponding subsets of $M$. This result is fundamental for approximation arguments in von Neumann algebra theory.
