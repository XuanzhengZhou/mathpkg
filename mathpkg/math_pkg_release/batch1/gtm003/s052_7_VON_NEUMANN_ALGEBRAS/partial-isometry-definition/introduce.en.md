A partial isometry is a bounded operator that acts isometrically on the orthogonal complement of its kernel. The operators $u^*u$ and $uu^*$ are then orthogonal projections onto the initial and final subspaces respectively. Partial isometries provide the natural notion of equivalence between projections in a von Neumann algebra: two projections are equivalent if one can be mapped to the other via a partial isometry in the algebra.
