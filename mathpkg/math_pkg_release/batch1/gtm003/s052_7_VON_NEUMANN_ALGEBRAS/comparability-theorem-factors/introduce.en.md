In a factor von Neumann algebra, any two projections are comparable in the Murray--von Neumann sense: either they are equivalent, or one is equivalent to a sub-projection of the other. This total comparability property is a crucial consequence of the factor having a trivial center and is the foundation for the type classification of factors into types I, II$_1$, II$_\infty$, and III.
