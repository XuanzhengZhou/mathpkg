The commutant of a subset of an algebra collects all elements that commute with every element of the subset. When the ambient algebra is a $C^*$-algebra and the subset is self-adjoint, the commutant inherits the $C^*$-structure and contains the unit element. This construction is the foundation for von Neumann's bicommutant theorem, which characterizes von Neumann algebras as those $C^*$-subalgebras of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ that equal their own double commutant.
