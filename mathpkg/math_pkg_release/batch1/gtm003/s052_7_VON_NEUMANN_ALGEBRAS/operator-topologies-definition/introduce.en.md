The study of von Neumann algebras relies on several locally convex topologies on $\mathcal{L}(H)$ that are coarser than the norm topology. The weak operator topology is the topology of pointwise convergence of matrix elements, generated by seminorms $u \mapsto |\langle u\xi, \eta \rangle|$. The strong operator topology uses the vector norms $\|u\xi\|$. The ultra-weak (or $\sigma$-weak) topology is the weak-* topology when $\mathcal{L}(H)$ is identified as the dual of the trace class operators $H \hat{\otimes} H$.
