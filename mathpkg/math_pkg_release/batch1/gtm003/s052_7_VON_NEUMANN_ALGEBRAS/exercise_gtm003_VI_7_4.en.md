An element $y$ of a Banach algebra $B$ is called a \text{left topological divisor of zero} if $\lim_{\gamma} yz_{\gamma} = 0$ for some normalized directed set $(z_{\gamma})$; accordingly, a \text{right topological divisor of zero} if $\lim_{\gamma} z_{\gamma}y = 0$.

(a) If $x \in B$ (with $B$ unital, complex) and $\lambda \in \partial \sigma(x)$ (the topological boundary of the spectrum), then $(\lambda e - x)$ is a left topological divisor of zero.

(b) For $B$ a unital $C^*$-algebra, show that $x \in B$ is invertible if and only if both $x^*x$ and $xx^*$ are invertible. Moreover, $x \in B$ is non-invertible if and only if $x$ is either a left or a right topological divisor of zero. (Note that $\sigma(x^*x)$ has void interior.)

(c) Let $A$ denote a $C^*$-algebra with unit $e$, and let $B$ be a $C^*$-subalgebra of $A$ containing $e$. For any $x \in B$, prove that the spectrum $\sigma_B(x)$ (of $x$ with respect to $B$) equals $\sigma_A(x)$.
