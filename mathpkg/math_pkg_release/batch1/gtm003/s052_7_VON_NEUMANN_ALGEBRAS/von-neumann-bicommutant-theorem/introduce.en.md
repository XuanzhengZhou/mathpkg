Von Neumann's bicommutant theorem is the foundational result in the theory of von Neumann algebras. It establishes the equivalence between the algebraic condition $M = M^{cc}$ (equality with its double commutant) and topological closure in any of the three natural operator topologies: weak, strong, and $\sigma$-weak. This theorem justifies the definition of a von Neumann algebra as a $C^*$-subalgebra of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ that is closed in the weak (equivalently strong, or $\sigma$-weak) operator topology.
