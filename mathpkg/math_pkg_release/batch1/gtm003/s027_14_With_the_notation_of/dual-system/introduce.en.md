A dual system (or duality) is a triple $(F, G, f)$ consisting of two vector spaces $F, G$ over a scalar field $K$ and a bilinear form $f$ on $F \times G$ that is non-degenerate in both arguments. The separation axioms $(S_1)$ and $(S_2)$ ensure that $f$ induces an injective linear map from $G$ into the algebraic dual $F^*$ and from $F$ into $G^*$. Dual systems form the foundation for the theory of weak topologies and all subsequent duality theory for locally convex spaces.
