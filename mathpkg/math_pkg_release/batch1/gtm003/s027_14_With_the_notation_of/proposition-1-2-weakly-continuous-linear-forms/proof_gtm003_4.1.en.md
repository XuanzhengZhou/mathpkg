In view of the definition of $\sigma(F, G)$, we have to show only that a given continuous $f$ can be written as indicated. By (III, 1.1) there exist elements $y_i \in G$ ($i = 1, \ldots, n$) such that $|f(x)| \leq c \sup_i |\langle x, y_i \rangle|$ for all $x \in F$ and a suitable constant $c$. Viewing the $y_i$ as linear forms on $F$, the corollary of (1.1) shows $f$ to be a linear combination of the $y_i$, whence the proposition follows.
