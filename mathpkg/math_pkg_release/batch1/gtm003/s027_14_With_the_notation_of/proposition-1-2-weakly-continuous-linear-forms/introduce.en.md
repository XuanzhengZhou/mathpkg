Proposition 1.2 characterizes all $\sigma(F, G)$-continuous linear forms on $F$ as precisely those functionals that are finite linear combinations of the evaluation maps $x \mapsto \langle x, y \rangle$ with $y \in G$. This shows that the weak dual of $F$ (with respect to the duality $\langle F, G \rangle$) is exactly $G$, establishing the fundamental duality between the weak topology and the pairing.
