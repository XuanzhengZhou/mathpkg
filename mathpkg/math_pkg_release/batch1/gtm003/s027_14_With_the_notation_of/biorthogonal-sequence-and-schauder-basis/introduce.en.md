This proposition combines two important results: first, every maximal topologically free sequence in a separable Banach (or barreled) space admits a unique biorthogonal sequence of continuous linear functionals; second, a boundedness condition involving the biorthogonal functionals characterizes when such a sequence forms a Schauder basis. The result connects the topological notion of freeness with the functional-analytic property of being a basis.
