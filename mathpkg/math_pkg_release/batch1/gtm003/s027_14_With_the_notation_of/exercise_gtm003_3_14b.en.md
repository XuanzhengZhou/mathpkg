Generalize the foregoing result to the case where $X$ is a metrizable, locally compact space, $\mu$ a bounded positive measure on $X$ with $\mu(G) > 0$ for each open $G \neq \emptyset$, with $E = \mathcal{C}(X)$, $H = L^2(\mu)$ (Chapter II, Section 2, Examples) and $P$ a countable subset of $X$ such that $\mu(\{t\}) = 0$ for every $t \in P$.
