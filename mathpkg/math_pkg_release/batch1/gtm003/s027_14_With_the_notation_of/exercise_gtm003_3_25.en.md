Let $\{s_m^{(n)} : m \in \mathbb{N}\}$ ($n \in \mathbb{N}$) be a double sequence of non-negative numbers such that for each $n$, $\{x_m s_m^{(n)} : m \in \mathbb{N}\}$ is summable (Exercise 23). Let $E$ be the space of all sequences $\{x_m\}$ such that the preceding holds for all $n$, provided with the topology generated by the semi-norms
$$
x \mapsto p_n(x) = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} |x_m s_m^{(n)}| \quad (n \in \mathbb{N}).
$$
Then $E$ is an (F)-space. Show that this space is nuclear if and only if for each $n$, there exists $p \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $\sum_{m=1}^{\infty} s_m^{(n)} / s_m^{(n+p)} < +\infty$. (Replace any quotients $0/0$ by $0$.) (For the proof, use (7.2)(c). Concerning the necessity of the condition, note that the dual $E'$ of $E$ can be identified with the space of sequences, each of which is absolutely majorized by some $n\sigma_n$. If $B_n$ denotes the set of sequences $\{y_m\}$ such that $|y_m| \leq s_m^{(n)}$ for all $m$, the family $\{nB_n\}$ is a fundamental family of equicontinuous subsets of $E'$.)
