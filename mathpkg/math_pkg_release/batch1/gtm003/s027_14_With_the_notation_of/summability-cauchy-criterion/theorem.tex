If $E$ is a complete Hausdorff topological vector space, a family $\{x_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$ is summable if and only if for each $0$-neighborhood $U$ in $E$, there exists a finite subset $\Phi_U \subset A$ such that $\sum_{\alpha \in \Phi} x_\alpha \in U$ whenever $\Phi \subset A$ is finite and $\Phi \cap \Phi_U = \emptyset$.
