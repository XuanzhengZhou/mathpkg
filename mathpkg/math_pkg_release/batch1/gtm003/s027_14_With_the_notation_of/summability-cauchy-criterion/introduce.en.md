This proposition gives a Cauchy-type characterization of summability in complete topological vector spaces: a family is summable precisely when the partial sums over finite sets disjoint from sufficiently large finite sets become arbitrarily small. This is the natural generalization of the Cauchy criterion for series to arbitrary families in complete t.v.s., and it shows that summability is equivalent to the convergence of the net of finite partial sums.
