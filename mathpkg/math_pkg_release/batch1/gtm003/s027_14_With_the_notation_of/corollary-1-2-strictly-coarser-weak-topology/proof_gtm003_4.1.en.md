If $\sigma(F, G_1) = \sigma(F, G)$, then by Proposition 1.2 the continuous linear forms coincide. Since the $\sigma(F, G)$-continuous linear forms are exactly $G$ and the $\sigma(F, G_1)$-continuous linear forms are exactly $G_1$, we must have $G_1 = G$. Thus if $G_1 \subsetneq G$, the topology $\sigma(F, G_1)$ is necessarily strictly coarser.
