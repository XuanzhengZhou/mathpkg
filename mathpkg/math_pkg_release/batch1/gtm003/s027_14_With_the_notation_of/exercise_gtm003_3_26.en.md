A family $\{x_\alpha : \alpha \in A\}$ in a topological vector space $E$ is \textbf{topologically free} if for each $\alpha \in A$, $x_\alpha$ is not contained in the smallest closed subspace of $E$ containing the subfamily $\{x_\beta : \beta \neq \alpha\}$. Let $E$ be a separable (B)-space (more generally, a separable barreled space), and let $\{x_n\}$ be a maximal, topologically free sequence in $E$. Show that there exists a unique sequence $\{f_n\} \subset E'$ biorthogonal to $\{x_n\}$, and show that $\{x_n\}$ is a Schauder basis of $E$ if for each $x \in E$ and each $g \in E'$, the numerical sequence $\left\{\sum_{k=1}^{n} f_k(x)g(x_k) : n \in \mathbb{N}\right\}$ is bounded.
