When a dual pair $\langle E, F \rangle$ is such that $F$ is a proper subspace of the algebraic dual, the weak topology $\sigma(E, F)$ is strictly coarser than the Mackey topology $\tau(E, F)$. The Mackey topology is the finest locally convex topology compatible with the pairing. The gap between weak and Mackey topologies measures how far the dual is from being the full algebraic dual.
