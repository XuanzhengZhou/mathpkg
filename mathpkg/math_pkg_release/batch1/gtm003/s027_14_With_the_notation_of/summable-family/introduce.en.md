A summable family generalizes the notion of convergent series to arbitrary index sets. Unlike ordinary convergence (which depends on the order of summation), summability is an unconditional notion: the net of finite partial sums converges regardless of the order in which terms are added. For countable index sets, this yields the concept of unconditional convergence, which is strictly stronger than ordinary convergence in general topological vector spaces (though equivalent in Banach spaces for scalar series).
