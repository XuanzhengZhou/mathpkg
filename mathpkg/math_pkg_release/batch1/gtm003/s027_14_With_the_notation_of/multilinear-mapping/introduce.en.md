A multilinear mapping (or $n$-linear mapping) generalizes the concept of a bilinear map to $n$ variables. A function $f: E_1 \times \cdots \times E_n \to G$ is $n$-linear if it is linear in each argument separately when all other arguments are held fixed. This notion is fundamental for defining the $n$-fold tensor product $\bigotimes_{i=1}^{n} E_i$ and its projective tensor product topology when the $E_i$ are locally convex spaces.
