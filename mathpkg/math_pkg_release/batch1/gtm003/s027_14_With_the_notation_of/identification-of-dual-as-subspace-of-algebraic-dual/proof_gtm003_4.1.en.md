The mapping $y \mapsto f_y$ where $f_y(x) = \langle x, y \rangle$ is clearly linear in $y$. By the separation axiom $(S_2)$, if $f_y = 0$ (i.e., $\langle x, y \rangle = 0$ for all $x \in F$), then $y = 0$. Thus the mapping is injective, hence an isomorphism onto its image. Its image is therefore a subspace of $F^*$ isomorphic to $G$.
