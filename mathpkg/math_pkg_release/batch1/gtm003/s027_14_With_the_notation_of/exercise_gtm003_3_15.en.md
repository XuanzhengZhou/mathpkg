Let $\{a_{mn}\}$ be a numerical double sequence such that for each $m \in \mathbb{N}$ there exists a summable sequence $\{x_n^{(m)}: n \in \mathbb{N}\} \in \ell^1$ for which $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_{mn} x_n^{(m)}$ is not convergent. Show that there exists a sequence $\{x_n\} \in \ell^1$ such that $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_{mn} x_n$ is divergent for all $m \in \mathbb{N}$. (Use Exercise 12.)
