A family in a topological vector space is topologically free if no member lies in the closed linear span of the others. This is the natural topological analogue of linear independence: ordinary linear independence uses algebraic span, while topological freeness uses closed linear span. Topologically free families are fundamental for studying Schauder bases and biorthogonal systems in Banach and more general spaces.
