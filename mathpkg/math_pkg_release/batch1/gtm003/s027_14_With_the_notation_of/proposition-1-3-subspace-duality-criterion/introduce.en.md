Proposition 1.3 gives a precise criterion for when the restriction of a duality $\langle F, G \rangle$ to a subspace $G_1 \subset G$ remains a duality: this happens exactly when $G_1$ is weakly dense in $G$, i.e., dense with respect to the weak topology $\sigma(G, F)$. The proof uses the $\sigma(G, F)$-continuity of evaluation maps and the separation axioms.
