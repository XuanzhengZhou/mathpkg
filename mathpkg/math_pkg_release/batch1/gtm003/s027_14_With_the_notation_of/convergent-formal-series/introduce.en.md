This is the standard definition of convergence for a formal series in a Hausdorff topological vector space: the series converges to $x$ if the sequence of partial sums converges to $x$ in the topology of $E$. This basic definition serves as the starting point for the study of summable families and unconditional convergence, which are more subtle notions in topological vector spaces than in normed spaces.
