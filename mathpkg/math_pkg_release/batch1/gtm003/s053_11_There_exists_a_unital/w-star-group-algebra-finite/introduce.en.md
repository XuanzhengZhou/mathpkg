The $W^*$-group algebra $W(G)$ admits a canonical faithful normal trace $\tau(x) = (x\delta_e \mid \delta_e)$ defined using the vector state associated to the Dirac function $\delta_e$ at the neutral element. This trace makes $W(G)$ a finite von Neumann algebra. Faithfulness follows from a Cauchy--Schwarz argument showing that $\tau(xu_g) = 0$ for all $g \neq e$ when $\tau(x) = 0$ and $x \geq 0$.
