Kakutani's theorem states that every abstract $L$-space (a Banach lattice whose norm is additive on the positive cone) is isometrically and lattice isomorphic to a concrete $L^1$-space $L^1(X, \mu)$ over some locally compact space $X$. When a weak order unit exists, the representing space $X$ may be taken compact. This is the $L$-space analog of Kakutani's $M$-space representation theorem (every $AM$-space is $C(K)$), and is fundamental in the theory of Banach lattices.
