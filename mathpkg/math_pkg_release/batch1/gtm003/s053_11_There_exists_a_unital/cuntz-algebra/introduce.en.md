The Cuntz algebra $O(n)$ is the unique (up to isomorphism) unital $C^*$-algebra generated by $n$ isometries $v_1, \ldots, v_n$ whose range projections sum to the identity. Specifically, each generator satisfies $v_j^* v_j = e$, and the projections $v_j v_j^*$ form a partition of unity: $\sum_{j=1}^n v_j v_j^* = e$. The Cuntz algebras are fundamental examples of simple, purely infinite $C^*$-algebras and play a central role in the classification program for nuclear $C^*$-algebras.
