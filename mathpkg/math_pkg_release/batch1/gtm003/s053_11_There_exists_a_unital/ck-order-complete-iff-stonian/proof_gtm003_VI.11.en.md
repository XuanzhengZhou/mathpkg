\textit{Proof sketch (Exercise, cf.\ Chapter~V, Exercise~23).} If $K$ is Stonian, the closure of every open set is open, which implies that the characteristic function of any clopen set is continuous. The order completeness of $C(K)$ follows from the fact that the supremum of an order-bounded family corresponds to the continuous extension of the pointwise supremum, which is guaranteed by extremal disconnectedness. Conversely, order completeness of $C(K)$ forces $K$ to be extremally disconnected.
