The $C^*$-algebra $C(K)$ of continuous functions on a compact space $K$ is order complete (every order-bounded increasing net has a supremum) precisely when $K$ is a Stonian (extremally disconnected) space. This result connects the order-theoretic properties of the function algebra with the topological disconnectedness properties of the underlying compact space.
