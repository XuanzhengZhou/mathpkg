(Spectral Theorem for Normal Operators.) Cf.\ Chapter~V, Exercises~25--27.

Let $H$ be any complex Hilbert space, $T \in \mathcal{L}(H)$ a normal element with spectrum $\sigma(T) =: K$. Denoting by $A$ the $C^*$-subalgebra of $\mathcal{L}(H)$ generated by $\{T, \mathrm{id}_H\}$, let $\Phi : C(K) \to A$ be the isomorphism given by Gelfand's theorem (2.2), Corollary. For any $f \in C(K)$, define $f(T) := \Phi(f)$. The mapping $f \mapsto f(T)$ is an isometric $*$-isomorphism from $C(K)$ onto $A$. The integral $\int_K f(\lambda)\,dE(\lambda)$ exists in the operator norm of $\mathcal{L}(H)$, where the mapping $f \mapsto f(T)$ is called an \textit{operational calculus} for $T$.
