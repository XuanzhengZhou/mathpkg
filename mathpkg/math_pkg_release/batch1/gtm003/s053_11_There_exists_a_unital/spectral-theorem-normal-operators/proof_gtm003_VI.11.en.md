\textit{Proof sketch (Exercise, cf.\ Chapter~V, Exercises~25--27).} Since $T$ is normal, the $C^*$-subalgebra $A$ generated by $T$ and $\mathrm{id}_H$ is commutative. By Gelfand's theorem (2.2), Corollary, $A$ is isometrically $*$-isomorphic to $C(X)$ where $X$ is the maximal ideal space (character space) of $A$, which can be identified with $\sigma(T) = K$. The isomorphism $\Phi: C(K) \to A$ sends the identity function $z \mapsto z$ on $K$ to $T$. For each $f \in C(K)$, define $f(T) = \Phi(f)$. The integral representation $\int_K f(\lambda) \, dE(\lambda)$ exists in the operator norm, where $E$ is the spectral measure associated to $T$.
