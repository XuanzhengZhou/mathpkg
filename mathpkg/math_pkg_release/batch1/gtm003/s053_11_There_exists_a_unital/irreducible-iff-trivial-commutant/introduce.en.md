A representation of a $C^*$-algebra is irreducible precisely when its commutant consists only of scalar multiples of the identity. More concretely, the representation admits a nontrivial invariant subspace if and only if the commutant contains a nontrivial projection $p$ with $0 \neq p \neq \mathrm{id}_H$. This characterization, equivalent to Schur's lemma for $C^*$-algebras, is fundamental for identifying irreducible representations via their commutants.
