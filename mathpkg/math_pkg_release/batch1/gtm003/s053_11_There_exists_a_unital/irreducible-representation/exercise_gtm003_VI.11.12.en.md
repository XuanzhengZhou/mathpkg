A representation (Section~4) $(\pi, H)$ of the $C^*$-algebra $A$ is called \textit{irreducible} if every element $\xi \neq 0$ in $H$ is a cyclic vector.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $(\pi, H)$ is irreducible iff the commutant $\pi(A)^c := \{y \in \mathcal{L}(H) : y\pi(x) = \pi(x)y \text{ for all } x \in A\}$ of its range $\pi(A)$ is trivial (i.e., contains only scalar multiples of $\mathrm{id}_H$). (Show that $(\pi, H)$ is not irreducible exactly when $\pi(A)^c$ contains a projection $p$, $0 \neq p \neq \mathrm{id}_H$.)

\item[(b)] Suppose $A$ is unital, and let $\phi \in \mathcal{S}(A)$. The representation $(\pi_\phi, H_\phi)$ is irreducible iff $\phi$ is an extreme point of the state space $\mathcal{S}(A)$. (Using part (a) consider the states $\phi_p$ and $\phi_q$, where \ldots)
\end{enumerate}
