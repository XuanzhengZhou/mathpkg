A representation $(\pi, H)$ of a $C^*$-algebra $A$ is called irreducible if it has no nontrivial closed invariant subspaces, or equivalently, every nonzero vector $\xi \in H$ is cyclic (i.e., the orbit $\pi(A)\xi$ is dense in $H$). Irreducible representations are the building blocks of representation theory and correspond, via the GNS construction, to pure states of the algebra.
