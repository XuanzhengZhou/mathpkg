Subdivision does not change simple-homotopy type, and consequently torsion is invariant under subdivision. This result is of fundamental importance in piecewise-linear (PL) topology: it means that torsion is a PL invariant, not merely a CW invariant. The proof uses an induction on the number of cells, showing that each step of subdivision can be realized by a sequence of elementary expansions and collapses. This theorem also implies that piecewise-linear homeomorphisms have zero torsion.
