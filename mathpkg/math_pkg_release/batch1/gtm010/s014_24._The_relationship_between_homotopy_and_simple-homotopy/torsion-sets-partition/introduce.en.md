The torsion sets $S_K$ give a partition of the Whitehead group $Wh(L)$ into subsets indexed by the distinct simple-homotopy types within a given homotopy type. Two complexes $K$ and $K'$ homotopy equivalent to $L$ have the same simple-homotopy type precisely when their associated torsion sets coincide or intersect. This structure theorem reveals that the Whitehead group precisely measures the difference between homotopy type and simple-homotopy type: the number of distinct simple-homotopy types within a homotopy type equals the index of a certain subgroup in $Wh(L)$.
