This corollary is a direct consequence of subdivision invariance. Any piecewise-linear homeomorphism can be expressed as a simplicial isomorphism after suitable subdivisions of domain and codomain, and subdivision is a simple-homotopy equivalence. Therefore PL homeomorphisms are always simple-homotopy equivalences. This is a crucial fact in the applications of Whitehead torsion to the classification of manifolds, where one studies the difference between homeomorphisms and simple-homotopy equivalences.
