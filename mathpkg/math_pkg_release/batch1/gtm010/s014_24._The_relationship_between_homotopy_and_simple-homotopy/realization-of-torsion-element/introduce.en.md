This theorem decisively answers Whitehead's original question (Conjecture I of §4): not every homotopy equivalence is a simple-homotopy equivalence. Given any element of the Whitehead group, one can construct a CW pair $(K, L)$ whose torsion is the negative of that element, and the homotopy inverse of the inclusion will realize the desired torsion. Since Whitehead groups are often nontrivial (e.g., $Wh(\mathbb{Z}_5) \neq 0$), there exist homotopy equivalences with nonzero torsion — these are homotopy equivalences that are not simple.
