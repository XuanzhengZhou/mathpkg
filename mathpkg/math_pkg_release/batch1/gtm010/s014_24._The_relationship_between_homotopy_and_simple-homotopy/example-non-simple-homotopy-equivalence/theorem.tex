If $p$ is a positive integer, $p \neq 1, 2, 3, 4, 6$ and if $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is the cyclic group of order $p$, then $Wh(\mathbb{Z}_p)$ is infinite (see (11.4) and (11.5)). For lens spaces $L = L(p; q_1, \ldots, q_n)$, $\pi_1 L = \mathbb{Z}_p$. Thus there exist infinitely many distinct simple-homotopy types within the homotopy type of a lens space, and in particular there exist homotopy equivalences that are not simple-homotopy equivalences.
