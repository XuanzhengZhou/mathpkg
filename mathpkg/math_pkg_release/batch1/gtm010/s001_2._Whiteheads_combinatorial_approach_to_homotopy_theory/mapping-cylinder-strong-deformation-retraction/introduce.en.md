The natural projection from the mapping cylinder $M_f$ back to the target space $Y$ is always a strong deformation retraction. This is a fundamental property of mapping cylinders: the target space $Y$ is a strong deformation retract of the mapping cylinder $M_f$. The proof consists of sliding along the rays of $M_f$, pushing points along the cylinder coordinate $t$ until they land in $Y$.
