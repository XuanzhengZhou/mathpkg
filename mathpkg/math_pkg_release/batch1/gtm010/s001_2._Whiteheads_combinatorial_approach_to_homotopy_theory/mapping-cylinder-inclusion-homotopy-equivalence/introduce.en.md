For any map $f: X 	o Y$, the inclusion $i: X 	o M_f$ into the mapping cylinder and the projection $p: M_f 	o Y$ form a commutative triangle. Moreover, the inclusion $i$ is a homotopy equivalence precisely when the original map $f$ is a homotopy equivalence. This follows from the fact that $p$ is always a strong deformation retraction, so $f = p \circ i$ is a homotopy equivalence if and only if $i$ is.
