A CW complex $K$ is a Hausdorff space together with a family $\{e_\alpha\}$ of open topological cells such that, letting $K^j = \bigcup \{e_\alpha \mid \dim e_\alpha \leq j\}$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[CW 1:] $K = \bigcup_\alpha e_\alpha$ and $e_\alpha \cap e_\beta = \varnothing$ for $\alpha \neq \beta$.
\item[CW 2:] For each cell $e_\alpha$ there is a characteristic map $\varphi_\alpha: Q^n \to K$ ($n = \dim e_\alpha$) such that $\varphi_\alpha|\mathring{Q}^n$ is a homeomorphism onto $e_\alpha$ and $\varphi_\alpha(\partial Q^n) \subset K^{n-1}$.
\item[CW 3:] Each $\bar{e}_\alpha$ is contained in the union of finitely many cells.
\item[CW 4:] $A \subset K$ is closed iff $A \cap \bar{e}_\alpha$ is closed in $\bar{e}_\alpha$ for all $e_\alpha$.
\end{enumerate}
