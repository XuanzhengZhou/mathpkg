A continuous map $f: X \to Y$ is a homotopy equivalence if it admits a homotopy inverse $g: Y \to X$ such that both compositions $g \circ f$ and $f \circ g$ are homotopic to the respective identity maps. The relation of homotopy equivalence is an equivalence relation on the class of topological spaces, partitioning them into homotopy types. Spaces that are homotopy equivalent share all homotopy-invariant properties (such as homotopy groups, homology groups, and cohomology rings), making homotopy equivalence the fundamental notion of "sameness" in algebraic topology.
