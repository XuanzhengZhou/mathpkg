A map $f: X \to Y$ is a \textbf{homotopy equivalence} if there exists a map $g: Y \to X$ such that
$$gf \simeq 1_X \quad \text{and} \quad fg \simeq 1_Y,$$
where $1_X$ and $1_Y$ denote the identity functions on $X$ and $Y$, respectively. The map $g$ is called a \textbf{homotopy inverse} of $f$. We write
$$X \simeq Y$$
if there exists a homotopy equivalence between $X$ and $Y$, and say that $X$ and $Y$ are homotopy equivalent or have the same homotopy type.
