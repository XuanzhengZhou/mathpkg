A p.l. $n$-manifold (without boundary) is a pair $(M, \mathcal{A})$ where $M$ is a separable metric space and $\mathcal{A}$ is a family of pairs $(U_i, h_i)$ such that $\{U_i\}$ is an open cover of $M$, $h_i: U_i \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$ is a homeomorphism onto an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$, and $h_j h_i^{-1}: h_i(U_i \cap U_j) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n$ is p.l. for all $i, j$. $\mathcal{A}$ is called a p.l. atlas and the $(U_i, h_i)$ are called coordinate charts. A map $f: M_1 \rightarrow M_2$ is p.l. if it is p.l. in local coordinates.
