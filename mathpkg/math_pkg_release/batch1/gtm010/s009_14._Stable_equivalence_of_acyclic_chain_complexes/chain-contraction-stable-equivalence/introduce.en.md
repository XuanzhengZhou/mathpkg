Any acyclic chain complex, via a choice of chain contraction, is stably equivalent to a complex concentrated in only two adjacent dimensions. This is the algebraic analog of the geometric cell trading lemma (7.3). The two-dimensional complex $C_\delta$ carries all the torsion information of $C$, and its boundary map $d + \delta$ restricted to odd dimensions is a matrix whose torsion class in $K_G(R)$ defines the Whitehead torsion of the original complex. This reduction is the essential bridge between geometry and algebra.
