The torsion $\tau(C)$ of an acyclic chain complex is the fundamental algebraic invariant of the theory. It is defined by choosing a chain contraction $\delta$ and taking the torsion (in the Whitehead group sense) of the matrix representing $d + \delta$ restricted to odd dimensions. The key results (15.1)-(15.3) establish that this definition is independent of all choices: the preferred basis, the chain contraction, and the decomposition into odd and even parts. This well-definedness is what makes Whitehead torsion a usable topological invariant.
