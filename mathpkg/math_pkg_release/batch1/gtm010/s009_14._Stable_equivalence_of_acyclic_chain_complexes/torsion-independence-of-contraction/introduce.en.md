The torsion of an acyclic complex does not depend on which chain contraction is used to define it. The proof uses algebraic manipulations showing that the difference between the two candidates for $\tau(C)$ can be expressed as the torsion of a matrix of the form $1 + \text{nilpotent}$, which has zero torsion in $K_G(R)$. Together with (15.2), this establishes that $\tau(C)$ is a well-defined invariant of the simple isomorphism class of $C$.
