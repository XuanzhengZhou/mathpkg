An elementary trivial complex is the simplest kind of acyclic chain complex: it is concentrated in two adjacent dimensions and its single nonzero boundary map is a simple isomorphism (represented by the identity matrix with respect to preferred bases). Trivial complexes are direct sums of elementary trivial complexes. They are the algebraic analog of trivial CW expansions and play the role of zero elements in the group of stable equivalence classes of acyclic complexes.
