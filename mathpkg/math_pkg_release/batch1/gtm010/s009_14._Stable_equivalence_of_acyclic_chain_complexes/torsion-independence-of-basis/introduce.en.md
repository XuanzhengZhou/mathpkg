Changing the preferred basis of an acyclic complex alters the torsion by the alternating sum of the torsions of the change-of-basis matrices. This formula is essential for proving that the geometric torsion $\tau(K, L)$ is well-defined independent of choices of lifts of characteristic maps to the universal cover. The alternating sign arises from the rearrangement of odd and even dimensional components in the matrix representation.
