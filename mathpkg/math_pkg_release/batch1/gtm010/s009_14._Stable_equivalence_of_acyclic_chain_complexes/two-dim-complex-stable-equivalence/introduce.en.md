This theorem is the culmination of the algebraic simplification process. Every acyclic chain complex over a group ring, regardless of its length, is stably equivalent to one concentrated in just two adjacent dimensions. This means the entire torsion information of an acyclic complex is captured by a single matrix. The proof uses an inductive construction with chain contractions, systematically collapsing dimensions from both ends of the complex until only two remain.
