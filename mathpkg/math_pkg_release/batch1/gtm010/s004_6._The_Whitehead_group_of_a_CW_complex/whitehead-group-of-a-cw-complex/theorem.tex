For a given finite CW complex $L$, define an equivalence relation on the class of CW pairs $(K, L)$ such that $K \to L$ (i.e., the inclusion $L \subset K$ is a homotopy equivalence) by
$$(K, L) \sim (K', L) \quad \text{iff} \quad K \wedge K' \; \mathrm{rel} \; L.$$
Let $[K, L]$ denote the equivalence class of $(K, L)$. An addition of equivalence classes is defined by setting
$$[K, L] + [K', L] = [K \cup_L K', L],$$
where $K \cup_L K'$ is the disjoint union of $K$ and $K'$ with the two copies of $L$ identified. This addition is associative and commutative. The element $[L, L]$ is an identity, denoted by $0$. The resulting abelian group is called the \textbf{Whitehead group} of $L$ and is denoted by $\mathrm{Wh}(L)$.
