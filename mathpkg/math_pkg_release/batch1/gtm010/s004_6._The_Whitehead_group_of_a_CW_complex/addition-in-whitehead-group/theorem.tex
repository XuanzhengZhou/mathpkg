An addition of equivalence classes is defined by setting
$$[K, L] + [K', L] = [K \cup_L K', L]$$
where $K \cup_L K'$ is the disjoint union of $K$ and $K'$, identified along $L$. The element $[L, L]$ is an identity, denoted by $0$. If $[K, L] \in Wh(L)$, let $D: K \rightarrow L$ be a cellular strong deformation retraction. Let $2M_D$ consist of two copies of the mapping cylinder $M_D$, identified by the identity on $K$. Then $[2M_D, L] = -[K, L]$.
