A composition of elementary expansions and collapses can be translated into a corresponding composition of mapping cylinders. Each individual mapping cylinder collapses to its domain, and by iterating the composition result (5.6), the mapping cylinder of the composed map is formally deformable to the union of the individual mapping cylinders, which in turn collapses to the original domain. This lemma provides the crucial link used in the proof of (5.8) to show that simple-homotopy equivalences correspond to mapping cylinders that collapse to the domain.
