Let $K = K_0 \cup e_1 \cup \ldots \cup e_r$ where the $e_i$ are the cells of $K - K_0$ arranged in order of increasing dimension. Then $K_i = K_0 \cup e_1 \cup \ldots \cup e_i$ is a subcomplex of $K$. We set $M_i = M_{f|K_i}$ and claim that $M_i \searrow M_{i-1}$ for all $i$. The claim follows because each cell of $K_i - K_{i-1}$ gives rise to two cells in $M_i - M_{i-1}$ forming an elementary collapse.
