The mapping cylinder of a cellular map collapses to the mapping cylinder of its restriction to a subcomplex. If $K = K_0 \cup e_1 \cup \dots \cup e_r$ where cells $e_i$ are arranged in order of increasing dimension, then each successive mapping cylinder $M_{f|K_i}$ collapses to $M_{f|K_{i-1}}$ through a sequence of elementary collapses involving the cell $e_i$ and an additional cell from the mapping cylinder structure. This is a fundamental technical tool for manipulating mapping cylinders in simple-homotopy theory.
