If two cellular maps are homotopic, then their mapping cylinders are formally deformable relative to the union of domain and codomain. This is proved by constructing a cellular homotopy $F: K \times I \to L$ between $f$ and $g$, and using the relationships $M_{F_0} \cup (K \times I) \nearrow M_F \searrow M_{F_1} \cup (K \times I)$ together with the relativity principle. This result is fundamental because it shows that the mapping cylinder, up to formal deformation, depends only on the homotopy class of the map.
