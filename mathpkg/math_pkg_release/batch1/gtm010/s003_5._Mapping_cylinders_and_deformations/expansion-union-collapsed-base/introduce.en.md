This technical result states that if a complex $K$ expands to $P$, and $K$ collapses to $L$ via a map $g$, then the adjunction space $P \cup_g L$ is formally deformable back to $K$ relative to $L$. This lemma provides the mechanism by which cell trading operations (used extensively in simplifying CW pairs) can be realized as formal deformations. It is applied repeatedly in the proof of the cell trading lemma (7.3).
