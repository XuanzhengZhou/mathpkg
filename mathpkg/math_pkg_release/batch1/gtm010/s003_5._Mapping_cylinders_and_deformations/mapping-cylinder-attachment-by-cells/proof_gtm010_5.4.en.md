Let $\varphi_i$ be a characteristic map for the cell $e_i$ of dimension $n$. In the mapping cylinder construction, adding the cell $e_i \times (0,1)$ of dimension $n$ and the cell $e^{n-1} \times (0,1)$ of dimension $n-1$ (from the boundary) to $K \cup M_{f|K_i}$ yields $K \cup M_{f|K_{i+1}}$. The quotient map $q$ composed with $\varphi \times 1$ provides characteristic maps for these cells. The pair $(I^n \times I, I^{n-1} \times I)$ with these characteristic maps satisfies the conditions for an elementary collapse, hence $K \cup M_{f|K_{i+1}} \searrow K \cup M_{f|K_i}$. The result follows by induction over all cells of $K - K_0$.
