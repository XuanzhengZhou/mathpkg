When attaching cells to a mapping cylinder, each additional cell from the domain produces a pair of cells in the expanded mapping cylinder. The characteristic maps of these new cells allow an elementary collapse back to the previous stage. This proposition provides the inductive step in proving that mapping cylinders collapse to restrictions on subcomplexes, and is essential for the cell-by-cell analysis used throughout the geometric approach to simple-homotopy theory.
