An equivariant map between the universal covers of lens spaces intertwines the cyclic group actions. This is the key technical concept for understanding maps between lens spaces, since any map $f: L \to L'$ lifts to an equivariant map between the universal covers. The degree of the lift and the relationship between the group actions (encoded by the exponent $a$ where $f_\#(g) = g'^a$) are the two fundamental invariants that classify lens spaces up to homotopy equivalence.
