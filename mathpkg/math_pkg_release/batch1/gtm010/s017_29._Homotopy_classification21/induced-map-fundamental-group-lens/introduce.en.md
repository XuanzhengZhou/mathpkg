For lens spaces, the induced map on fundamental groups is encoded by a single integer $a$ (mod $p$, coprime to $p$). This is because the fundamental group is cyclic of order $p$, and any homomorphism $\mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{Z}_p$ is multiplication by some $a$ coprime to $p$. The theorem establishes that this $a$ is independent of all choices (basepoints, lifts) and is a homotopy invariant. This integer $a$, together with the degree of the map, provides the complete homotopy classification of maps between lens spaces.
