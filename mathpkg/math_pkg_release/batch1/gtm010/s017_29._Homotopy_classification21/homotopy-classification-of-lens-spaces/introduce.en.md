This theorem provides the complete homotopy classification of lens spaces with a given fundamental group $\mathbb{Z}_p$ and dimension $2n-1$. Part (B) gives the precise condition for two lens spaces to be homotopy equivalent: the product of their parameters $q_1 \dots q_n$ must differ by a factor of $\pm a^n$ modulo $p$. When $n=1$ (3-dimensional lens spaces), this reduces to the classical condition $q \equiv \pm a^2 q' \pmod{p}$, which gives $L(p,q) \simeq L(p,q')$ iff $q \equiv \pm q'$ or $qq' \equiv \pm 1 \pmod{p}$. The contrast with the simple-homotopy classification (which requires exact equality of parameters up to permutation and sign) reveals the power of Whitehead torsion.
