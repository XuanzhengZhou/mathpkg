(a) If $P, K, K_0$ are finite CW complexes where $K \searrow K_0$ and $P$ is connected then
$$\tau(K \times P, K_0 \times P) = \chi(P) \cdot i_* \tau(K, K_0)$$
where $i: K_0 \rightarrow K_0 \times P$ by $i(x) = (x, y)$ for some fixed $y$, and $\chi(P)$ denotes the Euler characteristic of $P$.

(b) If $f \times g: K \times K' \rightarrow L \times L'$ where $f, g$ are homotopy equivalences between connected complexes then
$$\tau(f \times g) = \chi(L') \cdot i_* \tau(f) + \chi(L) \cdot j_* \tau(g)$$
where $i: L \rightarrow L \times L'$ and $j: L' \rightarrow L \times L'$ are constant sections.
