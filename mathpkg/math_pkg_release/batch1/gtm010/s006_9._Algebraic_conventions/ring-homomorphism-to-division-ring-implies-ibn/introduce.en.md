For a ring to have a well-defined notion of rank for free modules, any two bases must have the same cardinality. This property, called invariant basis number (IBN), holds whenever there is a nonzero ring homomorphism from the ring to a division ring. The proof uses the induced map on matrices: in a division ring, an invertible matrix must be square, and this property lifts back to the original ring. This result is crucial for Whitehead torsion theory because the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}(G)$ admits such a homomorphism (e.g., the augmentation map to $\mathbb{Z}$, which embeds in $\mathbb{Q}$).
