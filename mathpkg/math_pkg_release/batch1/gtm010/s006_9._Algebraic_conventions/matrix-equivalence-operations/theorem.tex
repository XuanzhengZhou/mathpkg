For disjoint $p \times n$ and $q \times n$ submatrices $P_1$, $P_2$ of a non-singular $n \times n$ matrix $A$ and a $p \times q$ matrix $X$:

$$I_R: \quad A = \begin{pmatrix} P_1 \\ P_2 \\ \vdots \end{pmatrix} \sim B = \begin{pmatrix} P_1 + XP_2 \\ P_2 \\ \vdots \end{pmatrix}$$

$$II_R: \quad \text{If } p = q, \quad A = \begin{pmatrix} P_1 \\ P_2 \\ \vdots \end{pmatrix} \sim B = \begin{pmatrix} P_2 \\ -P_1 \\ \vdots \end{pmatrix}$$

Analogous equivalences $I_C$ and $II_C$ hold for columns.
