Let $\langle f \rangle$ denote the matrix of a module homomorphism $f$. If $x = \{x_1, \ldots, x_p\}$ and $y = \{y_1, \ldots, y_p\}$ are two ordered bases of the same module $M$, $\langle x/y \rangle$ denotes the non-singular matrix $(a_{ij})$ where $x_i = \sum_j a_{ij} y_j$. Key identity:
$$\langle f_2 \circ f_1 \rangle = \langle f_1 \rangle \langle f_2 \rangle$$
and change-of-basis formula:
$$\langle f \rangle_{x',y'} = \langle x'/x \rangle \langle f \rangle_{x,y} \langle y/y' \rangle.$$
