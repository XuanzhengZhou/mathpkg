The torsion $\tau(K, L)$ of a CW pair $(K, L)$ synthesizes the geometric theory of expansions and collapses from Chapter II with the algebraic Whitehead torsion of chain complexes from Chapter III. Given a finite, connected CW pair with $K$ collapsing to $L$, one passes to the universal cover and takes the algebraic torsion of the cellular chain complex $C(\widetilde{K}, \widetilde{L})$, which lands in the Whitehead group $\operatorname{Wh}(\pi_1 L)$. This definition is the central object of study for the rest of the book and provides the bridge between homotopy-theoretic and simple-homotopy-theoretic questions.
