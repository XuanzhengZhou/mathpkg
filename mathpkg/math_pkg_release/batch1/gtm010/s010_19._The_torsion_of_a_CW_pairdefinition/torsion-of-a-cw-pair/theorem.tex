If $(K, L)$ is a pair of finite, connected CW complexes such that $K \searrow L$, then the torsion of $(K, L)$ — written $\tau(K, L)$ — is defined by
$$
\tau(K, L) = \tau(C(\widetilde{K}, \widetilde{L})) \in \operatorname{Wh}(\pi_1 L)
$$
where $(\widetilde{K}, \widetilde{L})$ is the universal covering of $(K, L)$ and $C(\widetilde{K}, \widetilde{L})$ is the cellular chain complex of the pair $(\widetilde{K}, \widetilde{L})$, regarded as a $\mathbb{Z}(G)$-complex with $G = \operatorname{Cov}(\widetilde{K})$ the group of covering homeomorphisms. The chain complex $C(\widetilde{K}, \widetilde{L})$ is made into a $\operatorname{Wh}(G)$-complex by designating as preferred bases those obtained from lifts of characteristic maps of cells in $K - L$.
