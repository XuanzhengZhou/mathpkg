If the matrix of a simplified pair can be reduced to the identity matrix by elementary row and column operations over $\mathbb{Z}\pi_1$, then the pair collapses. The operations of types I-V generate the elementary group $E(\mathbb{Z}\pi_1)$, and the obstruction to reducing a matrix to the identity is precisely measured by the Whitehead group.
