When a simplified pair has the identity matrix with respect to its characteristic maps, the pair actually collapses. This is the geometric meaning of the identity matrix: each $(r+1)$-cell together with its corresponding $r$-cell forms an elementary collapse. The algebraic condition of being the identity matrix is equivalent to the geometric condition of collapsibility.
