When two $n$-cells are attached to the same subcomplex $L$ by boundary maps that are homotopic in $L$, the resulting CW complexes are formally deformable relative to $L$. This lemma is one of the most frequently used tools in simplifying CW pairs. It allows one to "slide" attaching maps within their homotopy class without changing the simple-homotopy type. The dunce hat provides a classic example: it is simple-homotopy equivalent to a point because its attaching map is homotopic to the identity.
