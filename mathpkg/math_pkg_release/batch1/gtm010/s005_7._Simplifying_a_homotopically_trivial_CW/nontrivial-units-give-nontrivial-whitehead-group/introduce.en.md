The existence of non-trivial units in $\mathbb{Z}(G)$ implies the Whitehead group $Wh(G)$ is non-trivial. This is shown using determinant theory: elementary operations can only change the determinant by trivial units, so a matrix whose determinant is a non-trivial unit cannot be reduced to the identity. The group $\mathbb{Z}_5$ provides the simplest example.
