Iterating the cell trading lemma (7.3) produces a CW pair in "simplified form" — one where the cells of $K-L$ are concentrated in two adjacent dimensions $r$ and $r+1$, with all $r$-cells trivially attached at a chosen basepoint. This geometric simplification is the prerequisite for the algebraic analysis of Whitehead torsion. The simplified form reveals that the sole obstruction to $K$ formally deforming to $L$ is encoded in the boundary matrix between the $(r+1)$-cells and the $r$-cells, an element of the Whitehead group.
