The fundamental group of the subspace acts on the relative homotopy groups of a pair, endowing them with the structure of modules over the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}\pi_1$. This action is defined geometrically by dragging the basepoint of relative homotopy classes along loops in the subspace. The module structure is essential because the boundary matrices that encode Whitehead torsion have entries in $\mathbb{Z}\pi_1$, not merely in $\mathbb{Z}$, reflecting the fact that cells in the universal cover are permuted by the action of the fundamental group.
