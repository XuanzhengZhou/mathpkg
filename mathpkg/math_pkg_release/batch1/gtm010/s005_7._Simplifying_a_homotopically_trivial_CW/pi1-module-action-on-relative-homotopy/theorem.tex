Given a pair of connected complexes $(P, P_0)$ and a point $x \in P_0$, $\pi_1 = \pi_1(P_0, x)$ acts on $\pi_n(P, P_0; x)$ by the condition that $[\alpha] \cdot [\varphi] = [\varphi']$, where $\alpha$ and $\varphi$ represent the elements $[\alpha]$ and $[\varphi]$ of $\pi_1$ and $\pi_n(P, P_0; x)$ respectively, and $\varphi': (I^n, I^{n-1}, J^{n-1}) \to (P, P_0, x)$ is homotopic to $\varphi$ by a homotopy dragging $\varphi(J^{n-1})$ along the loop $\alpha^{-1}$.

This action satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(0)] $[*] \cdot [\varphi] = [\varphi]$
\item[(1)] $[\alpha] \cdot ([\varphi_1] + [\varphi_2]) = [\alpha] \cdot [\varphi_1] + [\alpha] \cdot [\varphi_2]$
\item[(2)] $([\alpha][\beta]) \cdot [\varphi] = [\alpha] \cdot ([\beta] \cdot [\varphi])$
\item[(3)] It commutes with all homomorphisms in the homotopy exact sequence.
\end{enumerate}
Thus $\pi_n(P, P_0; x)$ becomes a $\mathbb{Z}\pi_1$-module via $\left(\sum n_j [\alpha_j]\right)[\varphi] = \sum n_j ([\alpha_j] \cdot [\varphi])$.
