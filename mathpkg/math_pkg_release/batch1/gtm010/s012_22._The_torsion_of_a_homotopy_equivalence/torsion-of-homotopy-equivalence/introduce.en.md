The torsion of a homotopy equivalence extends the notion of torsion of a CW pair to maps between complexes. Given a cellular homotopy equivalence $f: K \to L$ between finite CW complexes, the induced map $f_*$ on Whitehead groups is an isomorphism, and one defines $\tau(f)$ by pushing forward the torsion of the mapping cylinder pair $(M_f, K)$. This definition is fundamental for the classification of homotopy equivalences up to simple-homotopy type and for the study of lens spaces and other classical manifolds.
