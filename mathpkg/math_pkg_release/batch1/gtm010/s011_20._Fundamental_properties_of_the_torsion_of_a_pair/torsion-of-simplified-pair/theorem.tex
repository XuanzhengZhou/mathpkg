Suppose that $(K, L)$ is a connected CW pair in simplified form, $K = L \cup \bigcup_j e_j^n \cup \bigcup_i e_i^{n+1}$ ($n \geq 2$), and that $\{\psi_j\}$ and $\{\varphi_i\}$ are characteristic maps for the $e_j^n$ and $e_i^{n+1}$. Set $K_n = L \cup \bigcup_j e_j^n$. Let $\langle \partial \rangle$ be the matrix -- with entries in $\mathbb{Z}(\pi_1(L, e^0))$ -- of the boundary operator $\partial: \pi_{n+1}(K, K_n; e^0) \to \pi_n(K_n, L; e^0)$ with respect to the bases $\{[\psi_j]\}$ and $\{[\varphi_i]\}$. Then
$$\tau(K, L) = (-1)^n \tau\langle \partial \rangle.$$
