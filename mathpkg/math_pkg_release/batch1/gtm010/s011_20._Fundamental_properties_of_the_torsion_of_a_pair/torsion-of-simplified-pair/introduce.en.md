This is the crucial theorem that connects the geometric definition of torsion with the algebraic boundary matrix. For a pair in simplified form (cells in only two adjacent dimensions), the torsion equals (up to sign) the Whitehead torsion of the boundary matrix from the homotopy exact sequence. This result makes torsion computable: one simplifies a pair geometrically using cell trading, then reads off the torsion from the boundary matrix. The sign $(-1)^n$ accounts for dimensional parity in the definition of the algebraic torsion.
