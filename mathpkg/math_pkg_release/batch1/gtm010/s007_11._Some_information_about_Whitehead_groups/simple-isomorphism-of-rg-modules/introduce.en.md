A simple isomorphism is an isomorphism of $(R, G)$-modules whose matrix representation (with respect to preferred bases) lies in the subgroup $E_G(R)$, i.e., has zero torsion. Simple isomorphisms are the algebraic analog of elementary expansions and collapses. They form the building blocks of the stable equivalence relation on chain complexes, playing the role that identity matrices play in ordinary linear algebra.
