An $(R, G)$-complex is the algebraic abstraction of a CW complex with a $\pi_1$-action. The ring $R$ is typically the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}(G)$, and the preferred basis corresponds to a choice of lifts of characteristic maps. The equivalence relation on preferred bases (change by matrices of zero torsion) ensures that the resulting torsion invariant is well-defined. This structure is the fundamental algebraic object of Whitehead torsion theory.
