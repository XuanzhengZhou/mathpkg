An elementary collapse is the fundamental building block of simple-homotopy theory. It describes the removal of a pair of adjacent-dimensional cells $e^{n-1}$ and $e^n$ from a CW complex under the geometric condition that the attaching map of $e^n$ restricts to a characteristic map for $e^{n-1}$ on one face of the ball $Q^n$. Geometrically, an elementary collapse of $L \cup e^{n-1} \cup e^n$ to $L$ corresponds to collapsing the ball $Q^n$ onto the complementary portion $P^{n-1}$ of its boundary, which lies in the $(n-1)$-skeleton of $L$. Every elementary collapse induces a cellular strong deformation retraction.
