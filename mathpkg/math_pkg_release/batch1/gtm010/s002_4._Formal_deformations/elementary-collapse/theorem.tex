Let $(K, L)$ be a finite CW pair. Then $K \not\sim L$ тАФ i.e., $K$ collapses to $L$ by an elementary collapse тАФ if and only if

(1) $K = L \cup e^{n-1} \cup e^n$ where $e^n$ and $e^{n-1}$ are not in $L$,

(2) there exists a ball pair $(Q^n, Q^{n-1}) \approx (I^n, I^{n-1})$ and a map $\varphi: Q^n \rightarrow K$ such that

\quad (a) $\varphi$ is a characteristic map for $e^n$

\quad (b) $\varphi|Q^{n-1}$ is a characteristic map for $e^{n-1}$

\quad (c) $\varphi(P^{n-1}) \subset L^{n-1}$, where $P^{n-1} \equiv \operatorname{Cl}(\partial Q^n - Q^{n-1})$.

Geometrically, $(K, L) \cong (L \cup_{\varphi_0} Q^n, L)$ where $\varphi_0 = \varphi|P^{n-1}$.
