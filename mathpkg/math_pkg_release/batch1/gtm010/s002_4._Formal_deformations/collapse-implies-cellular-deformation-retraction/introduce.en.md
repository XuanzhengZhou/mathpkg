When a CW complex $K$ collapses to a subcomplex $L$ by an elementary collapse, there is a naturally associated cellular strong deformation retraction. Moreover, any two such strong deformation retractions are homotopic relative to $L$. The proof uses the fact that an elementary collapse makes $(K, L)$ homeomorphic to the mapping cylinder of an attaching map, and the projection of a mapping cylinder is a strong deformation retraction.
