$L \not\sim K$ means $L$ expands to $K$ by an elementary expansion — i.e., $K$ collapses to $L$ by an elementary collapse. Geometrically, the elementary expansions of $L$ correspond precisely to the attaching of a ball to $L$ along a face of the ball by a map which is almost, but not quite, totally unrestricted. If $\varphi_0: (P^{n-1}, \partial P^{n-1}) \to (L^{n-1}, L^{n-2})$ is the restriction of a characteristic map, then

$$(K, L) \cong (L \cup_{\varphi_0} Q^n, L).$$
