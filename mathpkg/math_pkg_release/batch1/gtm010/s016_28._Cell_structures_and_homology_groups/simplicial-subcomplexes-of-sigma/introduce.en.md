The universal cover of a lens space is the odd-dimensional sphere $\Sigma^{2n-1}$, which is built as the join of $n$ copies of the circle $\Sigma^1$. Each circle is triangulated as a regular $p$-gon with vertices at the $p$-th roots of unity. The resulting simplicial structure is $\mathbb{Z}_p$-equivariant, with the generator acting by rotating each factor. This explicit cell decomposition makes it possible to write down the cellular chain complex with its $\mathbb{Z}(\mathbb{Z}_p)$-module structure, which is essential for computing Whitehead torsion.
