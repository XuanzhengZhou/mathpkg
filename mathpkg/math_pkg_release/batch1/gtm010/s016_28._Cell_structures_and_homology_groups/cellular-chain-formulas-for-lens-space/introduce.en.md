The explicit cellular chain formulas for lens spaces provide the algebraic data needed to compute Whitehead torsion. The universal cover $\Sigma^{2n-1}$ (a $(2n-1)$-sphere) is given a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-equivariant cell structure where the cellular chains form a $\mathbb{Z}(\mathbb{Z}_p)$-complex. The boundary formulas reflect the rotation action of the generator $g$: in odd dimensions the boundary involves $g^{r_{i+1}} - 1$, while in even dimensions it involves the sum $1 + g + \dots + g^{p-1}$, which is the norm element. These formulas are the starting point for all torsion computations for lens spaces.
