A homeomorphism (automorphism) of a topological space is called transitive if it admits a point whose full two-sided orbit under iteration is dense in the space. This notion captures the idea that a single trajectory can visit every region of the space arbitrarily closely. Transitivity is a fundamental concept in topological dynamics and is closely related to the Baire category method, which was historically the first technique to establish the existence of transitive transformations on the unit square.
