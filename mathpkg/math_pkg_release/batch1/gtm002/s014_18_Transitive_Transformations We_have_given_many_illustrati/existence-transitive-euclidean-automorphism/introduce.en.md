The existence of transitive automorphisms is not limited to the unit square. The same category-theoretic reasoning extends to any region (open connected set) in Euclidean space of dimension two or higher. The proof follows the same pattern: the space of automorphisms is topologically complete, and the set of those mapping any pair of basic open sets into each other is dense and open, so their intersection is a dense $G_\delta$, yielding transitive automorphisms via the Baire category theorem.
