Let $M$ be the space of all measure-preserving automorphisms of the unit square with the uniform metric. For positive integers $i$ and $j$, let $P_{ij}$ be the set of $T \in M$ such that $T$ has periodic points in every non-empty open subset and there exists a disk of radius at most $1/i$ whose orbit under $T$ satisfies a certain periodicity condition of order $j$.

Then each $P_{ij}$ is nowhere dense in $M$, and the set

$$P = \bigcup_{i,j} P_{ij}$$

is of first category in $M$. Consequently, the set $M \setminus P$ is residual in $M$ and consists of all measure-preserving automorphisms of the square that have nonperiodic points in every non-empty open subset of the square.
