This is the central result of the section: transitive automorphisms of the unit square not only exist, but form a topologically large (residual) set in the space of all measure-preserving automorphisms. The proof uses the Baire category theorem twice: first to show that each set of automorphisms mapping some basic open set into another is dense and open, then to conclude their intersection is non-empty. This was historically the first proof that any transitive homeomorphism of the square exists, establishing a new application of the category method.
