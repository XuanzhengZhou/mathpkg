The Kuratowski Remetrization Lemma gives a sufficient condition for a metric space to be remetrizable to a complete metric space. The condition involves the existence of a countable family of continuous real-valued functions such that a Cauchy sequence converges whenever its image under each function is bounded. This lemma is the key technical ingredient in the proof of the Alexandroff Theorem, which states that every $G_{\delta}$ subset of a complete metric space is topologically complete.
