Let $f$ be a bounded function on $[0,1]$ with discontinuity set $D$. Composing $f$ with a homeomorphism $h$ yields $f \circ h$, whose discontinuity set is $h^{-1}(D)$. By Theorem 7.5, a bounded function is Riemann integrable iff its discontinuity set is a nullset. Theorem 13.3 of Oxtoby thus characterizes when $f \circ h$ is Riemann integrable in terms of the three classical $\sigma$-ideals: $D$ countable guarantees integrability for every $h$, $D$ of first category guarantees integrability for some $h$, and $D$ a nullset is precisely the condition for the identity $h$.
