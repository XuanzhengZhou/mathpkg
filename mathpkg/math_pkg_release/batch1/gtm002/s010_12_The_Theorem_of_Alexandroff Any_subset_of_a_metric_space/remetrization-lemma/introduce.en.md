This lemma, due to Kuratowski, provides a sufficient condition under which a metric space can be remetrized to become complete. The condition involves the existence of a countable family of continuous real-valued functions $\{f_i\}$ that collectively detect convergence: if a Cauchy sequence has the property that each $f_i$ is bounded along the sequence, then the sequence must converge. The proof constructs an explicit new metric $\sigma$ that adds a weighted sum of truncated differences of the $f_i$ to the original metric $\varrho$. This lemma is the key technical tool used to prove Alexandroff's Theorem on the topological completeness of $G_{\delta}$ sets.
