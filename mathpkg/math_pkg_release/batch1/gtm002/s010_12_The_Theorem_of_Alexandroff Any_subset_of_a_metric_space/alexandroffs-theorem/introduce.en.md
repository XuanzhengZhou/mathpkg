Alexandroff's Theorem characterizes exactly which subspaces of a complete metric space admit a metric under which they themselves are complete. It states that every non-empty $G_{\delta}$ subset (a countable intersection of open sets) of a complete metric space is topologically complete. The proof uses Kuratowski's remetrization lemma, constructing an explicit new metric from distance-to-complement functions. This result is fundamental in descriptive set theory, where $G_{\delta}$ sets play a central role — for instance, the set of irrational numbers is a $G_{\delta}$ in $\mathbb{R}$ and thus admits a complete metric despite not being closed.
