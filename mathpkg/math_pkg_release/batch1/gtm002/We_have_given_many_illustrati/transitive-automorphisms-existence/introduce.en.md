This theorem is the culmination of Chapter 18: the existence of transitive measure-preserving automorphisms of the closed unit square, first established by the category method. For any $T$ in the dense $G_\delta$ set $\bigcap E_{ij}$, the open sets $G_j = \bigcup_{k=1}^{\infty} T^{-k} U_j$ are dense in the square, and their intersection is nonempty. Any point in $\bigcap G_j$ has a dense positive semiorbit under $T$, so $T$ is transitive. Thus the category method not only proves existence but shows that transitivity is the generic behavior among measure-preserving automorphisms.
