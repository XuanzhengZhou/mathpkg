In a complete separable metric space without isolated points, the existence of a single dense orbit under an automorphism $T$ has powerful consequences. The set of points whose forward orbit (positive semiorbit) is dense forms a residual set — a countable intersection of dense open sets. This means that in the sense of Baire category, almost every point has a dense positive semiorbit. The proof constructs $G_j = \bigcup_{n=0}^{\infty} T^{-n} U_j$ for a countable base $\{U_i\}$, showing each $G_j$ is dense and open, and then takes their intersection.
