Let $M$ be the space of all measure-preserving automorphisms of the closed unit square. For positive integers $i$ and $j$, let $P_{ij}$ denote the set of $T \in M$ such that every point of the basic open set $U_i$ is periodic under $T$ and has period dividing $j$. Then each $P_{ij}$ is nowhere dense in $M$, and the union
$$P = \bigcup_{i,j=1}^{\infty} P_{ij}$$
is of first category in $M$. The complement $M \setminus P$ consists of all measure-preserving automorphisms that have nonperiodic points in every nonempty open subset of the square.
