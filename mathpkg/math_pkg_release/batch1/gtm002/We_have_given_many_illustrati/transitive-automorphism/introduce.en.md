A homeomorphism $T$ of a topological space $X$ is called transitive when it possesses a dense orbit. This means there exists a point $x \in X$ such that the two-sided orbit $\{T^n x : n = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots\}$ is dense in $X$. Transitivity is a fundamental notion in topological dynamics, capturing the idea that a single trajectory visits every region of the space. When $X$ is a complete separable metric space without isolated points, the existence of a transitive point implies that almost every point (in the sense of Baire category) has a dense positive semiorbit.
