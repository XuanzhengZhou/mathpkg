This corollary extends Banach's theorem from right difference quotients to left difference quotients. The proof is immediate by symmetry: the isometry of $C[0,1]$ induced by the substitution $x \mapsto 1 - x$ interchanges right and left difference quotients, so the first-category nature of the right-side result implies the same for the left side. Together with the main theorem, this shows that the set of continuous functions possessing any finite one-sided derivative at any point is of first category.
