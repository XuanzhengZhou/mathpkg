The sets $E_n$ are a nested family of subsets of $C[0,1]$ introduced by Banach to prove that almost all continuous functions are nowhere differentiable in the sense of Baire category. Each $E_n$ captures those continuous functions whose right difference quotients are bounded by $n$ at some point $x$ in $[0, 1-1/n]$. The union $E = \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty E_n$ is precisely the set of all continuous functions possessing a bounded right difference quotient at some point of $[0,1)$. Banach's strategy is to show each $E_n$ is closed and nowhere dense, so that $E$ is of first category.
