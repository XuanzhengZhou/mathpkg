The indicator function $\chi_E$ of a set $E$ bridges the gap between properties of sets and properties of functions. It is measurable exactly when $E$ is measurable, and it has the property of Baire exactly when $E$ has the property of Baire. This follows immediately from the definitions and from the fact that the preimage of any open set under $\chi_E$ can only be one of four sets: $\emptyset$, $E$, $E^c$, or $\mathbb{R}$.
