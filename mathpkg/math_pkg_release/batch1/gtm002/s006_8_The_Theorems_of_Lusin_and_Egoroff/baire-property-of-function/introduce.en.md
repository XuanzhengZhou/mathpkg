A real-valued function on the real line has the property of Baire if the preimage of every open set has the property of Baire (as a set). This is the natural category-theoretic analogue of measurability: just as a function is measurable if preimages of open sets are measurable, a function has the property of Baire if preimages of open sets have the property of Baire. Like its set-theoretic counterpart, this property captures the idea that the function is "almost continuous" in the sense of Baire category.
