Theorem 8.1 gives the fundamental structural characterization of functions with the property of Baire: a function has the property of Baire precisely when it is continuous after removing a meager (first category) set. This is the category-theoretic analogue of Lusin's theorem, which characterizes measurable functions as those that are continuous after removing a set of arbitrarily small measure. Unlike Lusin's theorem, which requires an $arepsilon$-approximation, the Baire category version grants continuity on the complement of a single first-category set $P$.
