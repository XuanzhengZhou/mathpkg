Theorem 13.3 of Oxtoby's "Measure and Category" provides a striking characterization of the three fundamental $\sigma$-ideals on $[0,1]$ -- countable sets, sets of first category, and nullsets -- in terms of Riemann integrability under homeomorphic change of variable. Given a bounded function $f$ with discontinuity set $D$, the theorem tells us exactly when the composite $f \circ h$ is Riemann integrable after applying a homeomorphism $h$ of $[0,1]$. The answer depends on which $\sigma$-ideal $D$ belongs to: $f \circ h$ is integrable for all homeomorphisms $h$ precisely when $D$ is countable; integrable for some $h$ precisely when $D$ is of first category; and integrable for the identity $h$ precisely when $D$ is a nullset. This elegantly combines the measure-theoretic and category-theoretic viewpoints in a single functional-analytic criterion.
