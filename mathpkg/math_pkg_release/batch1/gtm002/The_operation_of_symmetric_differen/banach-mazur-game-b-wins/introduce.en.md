Theorem 6.1, proved by Stefan Banach, confirms Mazur's conjecture: the second player has a winning strategy in the Banach-Mazur game if and only if the set $A$ (the target set for player A) is of first category. This gives a game-theoretic characterization of first category sets and provides new insight into why sets of first category are considered "small."
