A set $G$ is \emph{regular open} if it is equal to the interior of its closure, i.e.,
$$G = G^{-'-'},$$
where $G^{-}$ denotes the closure of $G$ and $G^{'}$ denotes the interior. Every open set $G$ is contained in a unique smallest regular open set $G^{-'-'}$, and $G^{-'-'} \triangle G$ is nowhere dense.
