Let $Q$ denote the set of rational numbers, considered as a subgroup of the additive group of real numbers. The cosets of $Q$ constitute a partition of the line into an uncountable family of disjoint sets, each congruent to $Q$ under translation. By the axiom of choice, there exists a set $V$ having one and only one element in common with each of these cosets. Any such set is called a \emph{Vitali set}.

The countable family of sets of the form $r + V$ ($r \in Q$) covers the line. It follows that $V$ cannot be a nullset.
