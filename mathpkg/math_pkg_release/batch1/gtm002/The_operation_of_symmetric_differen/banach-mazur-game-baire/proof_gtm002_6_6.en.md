Let $A = G \triangle P$, where $G$ is open and $P$ is of first category. If $G$ is empty, then $A = P$ is of first category, and $(B)$ has a winning strategy by Theorem 6.1. If $G$ is not empty, $(A)$ has only to choose $I_1 \subset G$ to insure that he will be able to win: inside $G$, the set $B$ satisfies $B \cap G = (G - A) = G \triangle A = P$, which is of first category in $G$, so by Theorem 6.2, $(A)$ has a winning strategy.
