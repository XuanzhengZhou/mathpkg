If $A$ has the property of Baire, then by Theorem 4.1, $A = F \triangle Q$ where $F$ is closed and $Q$ is of first category. The complement $A^c = X \setminus A$ satisfies
$$A^c = X \setminus (F \triangle Q) = (X \setminus F) \triangle Q = G \triangle Q,$$
where $G = X \setminus F$ is open. Hence $A^c$ has the property of Baire.
