Theorem 5.6, due to Stanislaw Ulam (1930), states that any finite measure defined on all subsets of a set of cardinality $\aleph_1$ that assigns measure zero to every singleton must be identically zero. This is a foundational result linking measure theory with set-theoretic cardinality, and it places restrictions on the possible existence of real-valued measurable cardinals.
