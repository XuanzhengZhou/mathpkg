The symmetric difference $A \triangle B$ of two sets $A$ and $B$ consists of those elements that belong to exactly one of the two sets. It is a fundamental set-theoretic operation that is commutative, associative, and distributes over intersection. Together with intersection as multiplication, symmetric difference as addition turns any class of sets closed under these operations into a commutative ring in the algebraic sense, providing the algebraic framework for the study of the property of Baire and measurable sets.
