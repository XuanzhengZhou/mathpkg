The von Neumann–Maharam theorem guarantees the existence of a lower density for any complete finite measure space. A lower density is a mapping that selects a canonical representative from each equivalence class of measurable sets modulo nullsets, preserving Boolean operations and inclusion. For Lebesgue measure, the Lebesgue density theorem provides such a mapping, but the general result holds for arbitrary complete finite measure spaces.
