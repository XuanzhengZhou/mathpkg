The \textit{Cantor set} $C$ consists of all numbers in the interval $[0, 1]$ that admit a ternary development in which the digit $1$ does not appear. It can be constructed by deleting the open middle third of $[0, 1]$, then deleting the open middle thirds of each of the remaining intervals $[0, 1/3]$ and $[2/3, 1]$, and continuing this process indefinitely.

If $F_n$ denotes the union of the $2^n$ closed intervals of length $1/3^n$ that remain at the $n$-th stage, then
$$C = \bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} F_n.$$
