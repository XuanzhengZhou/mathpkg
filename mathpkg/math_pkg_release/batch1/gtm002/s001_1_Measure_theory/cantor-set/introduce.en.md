The Cantor set is a classic construction that yields an uncountable subset of $[0, 1]$ that is simultaneously a nullset (of Lebesgue measure zero) and nowhere dense (hence of first category). It is constructed by repeatedly removing the open middle third of each remaining interval. The Cantor set is closed, perfect (every point is a limit point), and totally disconnected. Its cardinality is $\mathfrak{c}$ (the continuum), demonstrated by a bijection between binary sequences and points of $C$ via ternary expansions. The Cantor set serves as a fundamental counterexample and building block in both measure theory and topology.
