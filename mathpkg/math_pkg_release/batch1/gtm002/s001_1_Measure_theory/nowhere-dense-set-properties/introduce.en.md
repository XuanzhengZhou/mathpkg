This theorem records the fundamental closure properties of nowhere dense sets. The class of nowhere dense sets is closed under taking subsets, finite unions, and closures. However, it is not closed under countable unions — for instance, the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ form a countable union of singletons (each nowhere dense) yet $\mathbb{Q}$ is dense, not nowhere dense. This failure of countable closure motivates the definition of first category sets.
