Borel's covering theorem (also known as the Borel lemma) states that the total length of any countable collection of intervals covering a given interval cannot be less than the length of that interval. This is a fundamental result in measure theory, as it immediately implies that no non-degenerate interval is a nullset, thereby providing another proof of Cantor's theorem that intervals are uncountable. The theorem is a precursor to the countable subadditivity and monotonicity properties of Lebesgue outer measure.
