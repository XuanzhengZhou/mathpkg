A nullset (or set of Lebesgue measure zero) is a subset of the real line that can be covered by a countable collection of intervals whose total length is arbitrarily small. This is the fundamental notion of "smallness" in measure theory. Singleton sets are nullsets, and the class of nullsets forms a $\sigma$-ideal containing all countable sets. The Cantor set provides an example of an uncountable nullset. The nullsets are dual (in a sense explored throughout the book) to the first category sets of Baire category theory.
