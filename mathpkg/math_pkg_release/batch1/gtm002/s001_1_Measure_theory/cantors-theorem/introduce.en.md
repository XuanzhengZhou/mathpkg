Cantor's theorem states that no interval of real numbers can be exhausted by any sequence; equivalently, every interval is uncountable. This theorem provides the natural starting point for both measure theory and Baire category theory, as it shows that the real line has a richness beyond mere countability. The proof uses a nested interval construction: given a sequence $\{a_n\}$, one inductively chooses closed subintervals $I_n$ avoiding $a_n$, and the intersection $\bigcap I_n$ yields a point $p$ in the original interval not equal to any $a_n$.
