The Baire category theorem, first proved by René Baire in 1899, states that on the real line, the complement of any first category set is dense, and consequently no interval can be of first category. This theorem is a cornerstone of functional analysis and topology, providing a powerful method for proving existence: to show a property holds for "most" points, one need only show the exceptional set is of first category. The theorem generalizes to complete metric spaces and locally compact Hausdorff spaces.
