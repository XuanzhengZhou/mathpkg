The class of all homeomorphisms of the line onto itself has cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$. The dual of Proposition 20.4 (obtained by interchanging "nullset" and "first category") asserts: for any class $K$ of one-to-one category-preserving transformations of the line with $\operatorname{card} K = \mathfrak{c}$, there exists a linear set $E$ of measure zero and power $\mathfrak{c}$ such that $TE \triangle E$ is countable for each $T$ in $K$. Applying this to the class of all homeomorphisms of the line, which are known to preserve first category, we obtain a set $E$ of measure zero (hence a nullset) and power $\mathfrak{c}$ such that for any homeomorphism $T$, $TE$ differs from $E$ by only a countable set. Since $E$ has measure zero and differs from $TE$ by only countably many points, $TE$ is also a nullset. Thus the image of $E$ under any automorphism of the line is a nullset.
