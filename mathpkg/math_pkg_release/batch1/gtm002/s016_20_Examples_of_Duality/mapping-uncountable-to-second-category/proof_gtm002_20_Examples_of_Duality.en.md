Let $f$ be any one-to-one mapping of the line onto a Lusin set (whose existence is guaranteed by Proposition 20.1 with $E = \mathbb{R}$). If $E$ is any uncountable subset of the line, then $f(E)$ is an uncountable subset of the Lusin set. By the definition of a Lusin set, every uncountable subset of it is of second category. Hence $f(E)$ is of second category.
