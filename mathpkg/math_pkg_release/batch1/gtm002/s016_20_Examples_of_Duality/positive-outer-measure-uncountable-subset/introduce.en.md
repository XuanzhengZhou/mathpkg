This proposition is the dual of Lusin's Proposition 20.1 under the measure-category duality principle, proved by Sierpiński in 1924. It asserts that any linear set of positive outer measure contains a subset of cardinality continuum in which every uncountable subset still has positive outer measure. The duality is striking: replace "second category" with "positive outer measure" and the statement transforms from category language to measure language. Since every subset of the line is the union of a nullset and a set of first category (Corollary 1.7), the set $N$ of this proposition must be of first category.
