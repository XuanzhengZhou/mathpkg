Proposition 20.4 is a slight generalization of a result due to Sierpiński. It states that for any family of $\mathfrak{c}$ one-to-one nullset-preserving transformations of the real line, there exists a set $E$ of first category and cardinality continuum that is almost invariant under every transformation in the family — differing from its image by only a countable set (via symmetric difference). The proof constructs $E$ by transfinite induction using a nullset $A$ whose complement is of first category, and considers the countable groups generated by initial segments of the transformation family.
