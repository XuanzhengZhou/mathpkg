Proposition 20.3 states that any linear set of second category can be decomposed into continuum many pairwise disjoint subsets, each also of second category. The proof uses a one-to-one mapping of the line onto a Lusin set contained in $E$ and exploits the fact that the line and plane have the same cardinality. This yields a family of $\mathfrak{c}$ disjoint Lusin sets within $E$, all of second category.
