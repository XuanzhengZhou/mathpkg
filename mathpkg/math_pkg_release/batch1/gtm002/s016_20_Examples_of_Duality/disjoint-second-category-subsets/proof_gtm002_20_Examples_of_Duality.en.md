Let $f$ be a one-to-one mapping of $\mathbb{R}$ onto a Lusin set contained in $E$ (such a Lusin set exists by Proposition 20.1). For each $x \in \mathbb{R}$, let $E_x = \{f(x, y) : y \in \mathbb{R}\}$. Since $\mathbb{R}^2$ and $\mathbb{R}$ have the same cardinality, $f$ can be chosen to be a bijection between $\mathbb{R}^2$ and the Lusin set. Each $E_x$ is an uncountable subset of a Lusin set, hence of second category. Moreover, the sets $E_x$ are pairwise disjoint, and there are $\mathfrak{c}$ of them.
