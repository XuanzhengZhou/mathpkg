Theorem 22.5 constructs the density topology on a complete finite measure space: the basic open sets are of the form $\phi(A) - N$ where $\phi$ is a lower density, $A$ is measurable, and $N$ is a nullset. This topology converts the measure space into a category measure space, where the measurable sets are precisely the sets with the property of Baire. The density topology on $\mathbb{R}$ (using the Lebesgue density lower density) makes $\mathbb{R}$ connected and provides a maximal topology in which the nullsets are exactly the nowhere dense closed sets.
