Let $(X, S, \mu)$ be a complete finite measure space and let $\phi$ be a lower density. Let $\mathcal{T}$ be the family of all sets of the form
$$\phi(A) - N,$$
where $A \in S$ and $N \in \mathcal{N}$ (the class of $\mu$-nullsets). Then $\mathcal{T}$ is a topology on $X$, called the \text{density topology}. Relative to this topology, $\mu$ is a category measure, and a set has the property of Baire if and only if it is measurable.
