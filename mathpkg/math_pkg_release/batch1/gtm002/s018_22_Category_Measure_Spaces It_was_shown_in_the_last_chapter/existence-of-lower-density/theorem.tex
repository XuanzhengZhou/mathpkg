Given a complete finite measure space $(X, S, \mu)$, there exists a mapping $\phi$ of $S$ into $S$ having the following properties, where $A \sim B$ signifies that $A \triangle B$ belongs to the class $\mathcal{N}$ of $\mu$-nullsets:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[1)] $\phi(A) \sim A$,
\item[2)] $A \sim B$ implies $\phi(A) = \phi(B)$,
\item[3)] $\phi(\emptyset) = \emptyset$, $\phi(X) = X$,
\item[4)] $\phi(A \cap B) = \phi(A) \cap \phi(B)$,
\item[5)] $A \subset B$ implies $\phi(A) \subset \phi(B)$.
\end{enumerate}
Such a mapping $\phi$ is called a \text{lower density}.
