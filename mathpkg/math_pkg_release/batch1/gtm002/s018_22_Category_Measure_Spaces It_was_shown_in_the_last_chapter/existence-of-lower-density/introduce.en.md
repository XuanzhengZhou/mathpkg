Theorem 22.4, due to von Neumann and Maharam, asserts that every complete finite measure space admits a lower density -- a mapping on the $\sigma$-algebra that selects a canonical representative from each equivalence class modulo nullsets. For the Lebesgue measure space, the Lebesgue density theorem (Theorem 3.21) provides such a mapping. The existence of a lower density is the key ingredient for converting a measure space into a category measure space by means of the density topology.
