The class of all automorphisms (homeomorphisms of $\mathbb{R}$ onto itself) has cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$. By the measure-theoretic dual of Proposition 20.4 (Proposition 20.4*), applied to the class $\mathcal{K}$ of all automorphisms of the line, there exists a linear set $E$ meeting the required condition: the image of $E$ under any automorphism differs from $E$ by at most a countable set and is therefore a nullset. The corollary follows directly from Proposition 20.4*.

This shows that in Theorem 13.2, the hypothesis that $A$ be a closed set cannot be omitted, though it can be replaced by a weaker hypothesis such as that $A$ be a Borel set. Sierpinski [33, p. 274] has shown that an even stronger form can be proved without assuming the continuum hypothesis, though it has not been proved that such a set can have power $\mathfrak{c}$ without CH.
