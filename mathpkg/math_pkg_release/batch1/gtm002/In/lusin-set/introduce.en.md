A Lusin set is an uncountable subset of the real line with the property that every one of its uncountable subsets is of second category. This notion was introduced by Lusin in 1914 in connection with the continuum hypothesis. A Lusin set must have Lebesgue measure zero, and its complement is necessarily of first category. The concept is central to Sierpinski's duality principle relating measure and category.
