Proposition 20.2 asserts the existence of a one-to-one mapping from the real line onto a subset of itself that sends every uncountable set to a set of second category. This is a direct consequence of Proposition 20.1: one simply takes any one-to-one mapping of the line onto a Lusin set, which exists since a Lusin set has cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$. The proposition depends on the continuum hypothesis through its reliance on the existence of Lusin sets.
