This proposition is the measure-theoretic dual of Proposition 20.1. It follows from the duality principle (Theorem 19.5 / the Sierpinski-Erdős duality theorem), which states that any proposition about sets of first category and second category formulated solely in terms of countable unions and intersections has a valid dual proposition with "first category" and "second category" replaced by "measure zero" and "positive outer measure" respectively, and vice versa, assuming the continuum hypothesis. No direct proof is given in the section text.
