Theorem 15.3 gives a complete characterization of when a product set $A \times B$ is of first category in the product space $X \times Y$: this happens precisely when at least one of the factors $A$ or $B$ is of first category in its respective space. The forward direction (if $A$ or $B$ is first category, then $A \times B$ is first category) follows from the fact that the product of a nowhere dense set with the whole space is nowhere dense. The converse relies on the Kuratowski-Ulam theorem (Theorem 15.1): if $A \times B$ is first category and $A$ is not, then some $x$-section $(A \times B)_x = B$ must be first category.
