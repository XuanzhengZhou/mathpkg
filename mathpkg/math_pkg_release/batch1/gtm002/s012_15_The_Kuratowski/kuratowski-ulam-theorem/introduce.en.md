The Kuratowski-Ulam theorem is the category-theoretic analogue of Fubini's theorem. It states that for a plane set $E$ of first category (respectively, nowhere dense), almost every $x$-section $E_x$ is of first category (respectively, nowhere dense) in the vertical fiber, where "almost every" means all $x$ outside a set of first category. This result, proved in 1932 by Kazimierz Kuratowski and Stanislaw Ulam, establishes a fundamental connection between the Baire category structure of a product space and the category properties of its sections.
