The recurrence property captures the essence of Poincare's theorem in an abstract setting: almost every point (relative to the $\sigma$-ideal $\mathcal{I}$) of any measurable set $E$ returns to $E$ infinitely often under iteration. When $\mathcal{I}$ is the ideal of nullsets, this is measure-theoretic recurrence; when $\mathcal{I}$ is the ideal of first-category sets, this is topological recurrence.
