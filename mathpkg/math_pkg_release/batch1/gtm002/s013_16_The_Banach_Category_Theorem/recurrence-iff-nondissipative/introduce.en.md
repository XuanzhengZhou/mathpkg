Theorem 17.2 establishes the fundamental equivalence in abstract recurrence theory: a measurable mapping has the recurrence property exactly when it is nondissipative (every wandering set is negligible). This single theorem unifies both the measure-theoretic and category-theoretic versions of the Poincare recurrence theorem, by appropriate choice of the $\sigma$-ideal $\mathcal{I}$.
