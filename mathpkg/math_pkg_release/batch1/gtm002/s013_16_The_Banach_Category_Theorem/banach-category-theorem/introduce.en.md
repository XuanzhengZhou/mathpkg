The Banach Category Theorem states that in any topological space, the union of an arbitrary family of open sets of first category remains of first category. This is remarkable because it holds without any countability assumption on the base of the topology, in contrast with the countable-base case where the result follows trivially by taking the union of basic open sets. The theorem generalizes a key property of Baire category beyond separable or second-countable spaces.
