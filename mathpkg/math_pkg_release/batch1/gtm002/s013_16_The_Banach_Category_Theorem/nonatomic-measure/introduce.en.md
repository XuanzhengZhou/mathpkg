A nonatomic (or diffuse) measure is one that assigns zero measure to every singleton. This means the measure has no atoms -- sets of positive measure that cannot be split into subsets of smaller positive measure. Nonatomic measures are essential in the study of continuous probability distributions and in the Banach Category Theorem's measure-theoretic analogue.
