An $\mathcal{S}$-measurable mapping is the natural generalization of a measurable function in the abstract setting of a $\sigma$-ring $\mathcal{S}$ with a $\sigma$-ideal $\mathcal{I}$. This concept provides the framework for the abstract Poincare recurrence theorem (Theorem 17.2), which unifies both the measure-theoretic and category-theoretic versions of recurrence.
