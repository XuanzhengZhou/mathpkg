The category assertion: Poincare showed that recurrent points are dense in $G$ by constructing a nested sequence of regions, which amounts to a proof of Baire's theorem for the case at hand. Since the set of points recurrent with respect to $G$ is a $G_\delta$ set, the category assertion follows. The measure assertion: Poincare used the volume-preserving property to exclude the possibility of an open set whose images under $T$ are mutually disjoint having positive measure. By a covering argument, the set of non-recurrent points must have measure zero.
