Liouville's Approximation Lemma gives a quantitative bound on how well an algebraic number of degree $n > 1$ can be approximated by rational numbers: the error $|z - p/q|$ must exceed $1/(M q^n)$ for some constant $M$ depending only on $z$. In other words, algebraic numbers cannot be approximated "too well" by rationals with small denominators. This inequality is the foundation of Liouville's explicit construction of transcendental numbers: any number that can be approximated to order $q^{-k}$ for arbitrarily large $k$ must be transcendental.
