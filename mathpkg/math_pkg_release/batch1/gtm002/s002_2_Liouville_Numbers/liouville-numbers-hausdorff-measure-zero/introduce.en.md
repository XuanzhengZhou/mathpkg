Theorem 2.4 strengthens the observation that Liouville numbers have Lebesgue measure zero: in fact, for every $s > 0$, the $s$-dimensional Hausdorff measure of the set of Liouville numbers is zero. This implies the Hausdorff dimension of the Liouville numbers is $0$, making them an extremely sparse set from the measure-theoretic perspective, despite being residual (large in the sense of Baire category). This striking contrast between category and measure illustrates a central theme of Oxtoby's book.
