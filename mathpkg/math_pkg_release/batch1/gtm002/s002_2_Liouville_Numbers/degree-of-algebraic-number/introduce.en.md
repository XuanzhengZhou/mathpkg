The degree of an algebraic number $z$ is the minimal degree of a non-zero polynomial with integer coefficients that has $z$ as a root. For example, any rational number has degree $1$, $\sqrt{2}$ has degree $2$ (root of $x^2 - 2 = 0$), and $\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3}$ has degree $4$. The degree plays a central role in Diophantine approximation: by Liouville's lemma, algebraic numbers of higher degree are harder to approximate by rationals.
