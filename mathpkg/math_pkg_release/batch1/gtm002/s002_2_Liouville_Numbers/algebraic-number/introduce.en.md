An algebraic number is a complex number that is a root of some non-zero polynomial with integer coefficients. This includes all rational numbers (roots of linear polynomials $a_0 + a_1 z = 0$) and many irrational numbers such as $\sqrt{2}$ (root of $z^2 - 2 = 0$). The complex numbers that are not algebraic are called transcendental; proving their existence was a major achievement of 19th-century mathematics.
