Any class $\mathcal{T}$ of subsets of a set $X$ such that $\emptyset, X$, the union of any subclass of $\mathcal{T}$, and the intersection of any finite subclass of $\mathcal{T}$ belongs to $\mathcal{T}$ is called a \text{topology} in $X$, and the pair $(X, \mathcal{T})$ is called a \text{topological space}.
