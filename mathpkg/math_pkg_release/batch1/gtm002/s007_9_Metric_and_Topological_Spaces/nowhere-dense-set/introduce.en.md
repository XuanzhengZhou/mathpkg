A set $A$ is nowhere dense if the interior of its closure is empty. Equivalently, for every non-empty open set $G$, there exists a non-empty open subset $H$ contained in $G \setminus A$. Nowhere dense sets are the fundamental "small" sets in the sense of category; a set of first category is a countable union of nowhere dense sets.
