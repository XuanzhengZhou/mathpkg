Convergence in a metric space is defined by the distance between sequence terms and the limit tending to zero. A sequence $x_n$ converges to $x$, written $x_n \to x$, if $\varrho(x_n, x) \to 0$. Convergence is a topological property, as it can be characterized purely in terms of open sets: $x_n \to x$ if and only if every open set containing $x$ contains all but finitely many terms of the sequence.
