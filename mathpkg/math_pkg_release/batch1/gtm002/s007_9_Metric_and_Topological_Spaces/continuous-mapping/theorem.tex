A mapping $f$ of a topological space $X$ into a topological space $Y$ is \text{continuous at the point} $x_0$ in $X$ if for every open set $V$ that contains $f(x_0)$ there is a neighborhood $U$ of $x_0$ such that $f(x) \in V$ for every $x \in U$. A mapping $f: X \to Y$ is \text{continuous} if it is continuous at each point of $X$.
