Theorem 2.1 of Oxtoby's \textit{Measure and Category} states that the set of real algebraic numbers is denumerable (countably infinite). This is proved by assigning a weight $n + \sum |a_i|$ to each polynomial with integer coefficients, noting only finitely many polynomials have a given weight, and enumerating all such polynomials in a sequence. Since each polynomial has finitely many real roots, the algebraic numbers are countable. Combined with the uncountability of $\mathbb{R}$, this gives a non-constructive proof that transcendental numbers exist.
