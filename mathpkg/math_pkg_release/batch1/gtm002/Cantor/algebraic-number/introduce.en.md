An algebraic number is a complex number that is a root of some non-zero polynomial with integer coefficients. The set of algebraic numbers includes all rational numbers, as well as many irrational numbers such as $\sqrt{2}$ and $\sqrt{2}+\sqrt{3}$. Algebraic numbers form a countable subfield of the complex numbers; this countability was Cantor's key insight for proving the existence of transcendental numbers.
