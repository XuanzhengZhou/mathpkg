A Liouville number is an irrational real number that admits exceptionally good rational approximations: for every positive integer $n$, there exist integers $p, q$ with $q > 1$ such that $|x - p/q| < 1/q^n$. By Liouville's Approximation Lemma, no algebraic number of degree $> 1$ can satisfy this property; hence every Liouville number is transcendental. Liouville numbers were the first explicit examples of transcendental numbers ever constructed.
