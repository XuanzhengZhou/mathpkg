Theorem 2.4 states that the set $E$ of Liouville numbers has $s$-dimensional Hausdorff measure zero for every $s > 0$. This is a much stronger statement than having Lebesgue measure zero: it means that $E$ cannot support any positive Hausdorff $s$-measure, no matter how small $s$ is. Combined with the Baire-category observation that $E'$ is of first category (so $E$ is a residual set), this gives a striking example of the divergence between measure and category: Liouville numbers are "topologically large" but "measure-theoretically extremely small."
