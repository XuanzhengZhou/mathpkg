Lemma 7.6 is a technical estimate used in the Riemann integrability criterion. It states that if the pointwise oscillation is bounded by $\varepsilon$ everywhere on an interval $I$, then the integral oscillation measure $F(I)$ is bounded by $\varepsilon|I|$. The proof uses a bisection argument: assuming the contrary leads to a nested sequence of intervals on which $F$ remains proportionally large, whose intersection point would have oscillation at least $\varepsilon$, contradicting the hypothesis. Here $F(I)$ is the difference between upper and lower Riemann integrals, measuring total oscillation over $I$.
