A set is of second category if it cannot be expressed as a countable union of nowhere dense sets. In particular, by the Baire Category Theorem, every complete metric space is of second category in itself. The distinction between first and second category is the central theme of Oxtoby's book, which develops a topological analog of Lebesgue measure.
