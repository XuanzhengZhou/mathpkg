A function $f$ is continuous at a point $x_0$ if the inverse image of every open set containing $f(x_0)$ contains a neighborhood of $x_0$. This topological definition generalizes the familiar $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ definition from metric spaces: in a metric setting, the neighborhood $U$ is a ball $B(x_0, \delta)$, and $V$ is $B(f(x_0), \varepsilon)$.
