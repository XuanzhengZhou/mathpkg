A topology is an axiomatic framework that abstracts the notion of open sets from metric spaces. It requires that the empty set and the whole space are open, arbitrary unions of open sets are open, and finite intersections of open sets are open. A topological space is simply a set equipped with a topology. This abstraction allows the study of continuity, convergence, and compactness without reference to a metric.
