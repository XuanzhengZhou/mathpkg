A metric space formalizes the notion of distance between points in a set. The metric $\varrho$ must be nonnegative, symmetric, satisfy the triangle inequality, and separate distinct points. This concept, introduced by Maurice Fr\'echet, abstracts the properties of Euclidean distance to arbitrary sets, enabling the development of analysis and topology in a unified framework.
