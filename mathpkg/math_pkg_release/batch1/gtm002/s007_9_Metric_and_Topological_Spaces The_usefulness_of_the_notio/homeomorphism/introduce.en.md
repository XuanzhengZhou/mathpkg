A homeomorphism is a bijective continuous map whose inverse is also continuous. It is the notion of isomorphism in the category of topological spaces: two spaces are homeomorphic if they are topologically indistinguishable. All topological properties (openness, closedness, compactness, connectedness, category) are preserved under homeomorphisms.
