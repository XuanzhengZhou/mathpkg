Kuratowski's remetrization lemma provides a sufficient condition for a metric space to be remetrized as a complete metric space. The condition involves the existence of a countable family of continuous real-valued functions $\{f_i\}$ such that boundedness of $\{f_i(x_n)\}$ for every $i$ guarantees convergence of any Cauchy sequence $\{x_n\}$. This lemma is the crucial technical device used in the proof of Alexandroff's theorem that every $G_\delta$ subset of a complete metric space is topologically complete.
