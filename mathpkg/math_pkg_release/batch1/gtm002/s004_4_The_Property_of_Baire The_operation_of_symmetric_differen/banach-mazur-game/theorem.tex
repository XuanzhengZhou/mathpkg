Let $I_0$ be a closed interval and let $A \subset I_0$, $B = I_0 - A$. The **Banach-Mazur game** $\langle A, B \rangle$ is played as follows: Player $(A)$ chooses a closed interval $I_1 \subset I_0$; then $(B)$ chooses $I_2 \subset I_1$; then $(A)$ chooses $I_3 \subset I_2$; and so on, alternately. Player $(A)$ wins if $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} I_n \cap A \neq \emptyset$; otherwise $(B)$ wins.
