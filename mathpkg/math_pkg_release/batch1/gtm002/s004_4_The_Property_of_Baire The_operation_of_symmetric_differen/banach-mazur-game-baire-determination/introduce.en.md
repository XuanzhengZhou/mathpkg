This theorem establishes that the Banach-Mazur game is determined for all sets possessing the property of Baire — a large class including all Borel sets. The proof relies on the representation $A = G \triangle P$ with $G$ open and $P$ of first category: if $G$ is empty, $B$ wins; if $G$ is non-empty, $A$ can choose a subinterval of $G$ and win. For sets without the Baire property (such as a Bernstein set), the game may be indeterminate, meaning neither player has a winning strategy.
