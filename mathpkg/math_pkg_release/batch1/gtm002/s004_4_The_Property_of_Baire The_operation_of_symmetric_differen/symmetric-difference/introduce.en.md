The symmetric difference $A \triangle B$ is the set of elements that belong to exactly one of $A$ or $B$. Together with the operations of symmetric difference (addition) and intersection (multiplication), any class of sets closed under these two operations forms a commutative ring in the algebraic sense. This algebraic structure makes the symmetric difference a fundamental tool in measure theory and category theory, where it is used to define when two sets differ by a "small" set (nullset or first category set).
