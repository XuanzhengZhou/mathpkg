This proposition follows from Ulam's theorem under the assumption that the continuum has cardinality $\aleph_1$ (the continuum hypothesis), or more generally that no cardinal up to $\mathfrak{c}$ is weakly inaccessible. It implies that under these set-theoretic assumptions, no non-trivial finite measure can be defined on all subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ that vanishes on points. This result is part of the broader investigation into which cardinals can support real-valued measurable cardinals.
