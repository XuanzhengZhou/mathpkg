Ulam's theorem (1930) is a fundamental result in set-theoretic measure theory. It states that on a set of cardinality $\aleph_1$, any finite measure that vanishes on singletons must be identically zero. The proof uses a well-ordering of $X$ to construct a matrix of sets with countably many rows and $\aleph_1$ columns, then applies a combinatorial argument to show the whole space has measure zero. Ulam generalized this to all cardinals below the first weakly inaccessible cardinal, showing that large cardinals are needed to support non-trivial countably additive measures defined on all subsets.
