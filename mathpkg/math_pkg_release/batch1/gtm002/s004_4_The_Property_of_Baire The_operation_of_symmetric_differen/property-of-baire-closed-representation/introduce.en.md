Theorem 4.1 provides an equivalent characterization of the property of Baire in terms of closed sets instead of open sets. While the original definition uses an open set plus a first-category perturbation, this theorem shows one may equally well use a closed set. The equivalence follows from taking closures and interiors, noting that the boundary of an open set is nowhere dense (hence of first category).
