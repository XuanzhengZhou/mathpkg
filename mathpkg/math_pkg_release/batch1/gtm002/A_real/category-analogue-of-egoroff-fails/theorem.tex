While Lusin's theorem (Theorem 8.2) has a very satisfactory category analogue in Theorem 8.1, the corresponding analogue of Egoroff's theorem is false. There exists a sequence of continuous functions $f_n$ on $\mathbb{R}$ converging pointwise to $0$ such that on every nonempty open interval, the convergence fails to be uniform off any first-category set. That is, for every first-category set $P \subset \mathbb{R}$ and every nonempty open interval $(a,b)$, the sequence $f_n$ does not converge uniformly on $(a,b) - P$.
