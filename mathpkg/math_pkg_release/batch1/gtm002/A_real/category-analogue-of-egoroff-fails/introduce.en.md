The section demonstrates a striking asymmetry between measure and category: whereas Lusin's theorem admits a clean category analogue (continuity off a first-category set characterizes the Baire property), the natural category analogue of Egoroff's theorem is false. A counterexample is constructed using a sequence of continuous functions that converge pointwise to zero everywhere, yet fail to converge uniformly on any nonempty open interval after removing any first-category set. This illustrates that pointwise convergence does not imply "quasi-uniform" convergence in the category sense.
