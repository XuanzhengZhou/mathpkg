Egoroff's theorem relates pointwise convergence to uniform convergence for sequences of measurable functions on sets of finite measure. It states that, given $\varepsilon > 0$, one can remove a set of measure less than $\varepsilon$ and obtain uniform convergence on the remaining set. Together with Lusin's theorem, it forms one of the foundational results connecting measure-theoretic and topological notions of convergence. The section notes that while Lusin's theorem has a satisfactory category analogue (Theorem 8.1), the category analogue of Egoroff's theorem is false.
