Theorem 8.1 characterizes the property of Baire for real-valued functions: a function has the Baire property precisely when it is continuous on the complement of some first-category set. This is the category-theoretic analogue of Lusin's theorem (Theorem 8.2), which characterizes measurability in terms of continuity off sets of arbitrarily small measure. The result highlights the parallel between measure and category, a central theme of the book.
