A complex number $z$ is called \text{algebraic} if it satisfies some equation of the form $a_0 + a_1 z + a_2 z^2 + \cdots + a_n z^n = 0$ with integer coefficients, not all zero. The \text{degree} of an algebraic number is the smallest positive integer $n$ such that $z$ satisfies an equation of degree $n$.
