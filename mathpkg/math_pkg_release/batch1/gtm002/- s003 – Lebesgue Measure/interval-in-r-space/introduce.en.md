An interval in Euclidean $r$-space is a rectangular parallelepiped whose edges are parallel to the coordinate axes, defined as the Cartesian product of $r$ one-dimensional intervals. The $r$-dimensional volume $|I|$ generalizes the notion of length, area, and volume to arbitrary finite dimension. Intervals serve as the elementary building blocks for defining Lebesgue outer measure in $r$-space, analogous to the role of 1-dimensional intervals on the real line.
