This proposition is the dual of Proposition 20.1 under the duality principle. The proof follows by interchanging the roles of "measure zero" and "first category" in the proof of Proposition 20.1, using the Sierpinski-Erdos duality theorem (Theorem 19.5). Specifically, let $\{X_\alpha : \alpha < \Omega\}$ be a decomposition of $\mathbb{R}$ into $\aleph_1$ nullsets. Let $N$ be obtained by selecting one point from each non-empty set $E \cap X_\alpha$. Since $E$ has positive outer measure, $N$ is uncountable and of power $\mathfrak{c}$. No uncountable subset of $N$ can be covered by countably many nullsets, hence every uncountable subset of $N$ has positive outer measure.
