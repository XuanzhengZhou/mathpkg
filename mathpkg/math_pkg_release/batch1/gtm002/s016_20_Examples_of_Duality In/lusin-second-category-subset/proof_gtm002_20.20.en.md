Let $\{X_\alpha : \alpha < \Omega\}$ be the decomposition of $X$ corresponding to the class $K$ of first category sets in the proof of Theorem 19.5. Let $N$ be a set obtained by selecting just one point from each non-empty set of the form $E \cap X_\alpha$. Since $E$ is of second category, $N$ is uncountable and therefore of power $\mathfrak{c}$. No uncountable subset of $N$ can be covered by countably many of the sets $X_\alpha$. Hence no uncountable subset of $N$ is of first category.
