Proposition 20.1, due to Nikolai Lusin (1914), states that any linear set of second category contains a subset of cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ with the property that every uncountable subset of it is also of second category. Such a subset is now known as a Lusin set. The proof uses the decomposition of the real line into $\aleph_1$ first category sets provided by the continuum hypothesis, selecting one point from each nonempty intersection with $E$. This proposition is one of the earliest examples illustrating the duality between measure and category under the continuum hypothesis.
