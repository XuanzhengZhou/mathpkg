This proposition is the dual of Proposition 20.2 under the duality principle. Let $f$ be any one-to-one mapping of the line onto a set $N$ as constructed in Proposition 20.1* (the measure-theoretic analogue of a Lusin set). Every uncountable subset of $N$ has positive outer measure. Therefore, if $E$ is uncountable, $f(E)$ is an uncountable subset of $N$ and hence has positive outer measure.
