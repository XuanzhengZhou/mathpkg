Proposition 20.2* is the measure-theoretic dual of Proposition 20.2. It asserts the existence of a bijection from the real line onto a subset of itself that maps every uncountable set to a set of positive outer measure. The proof follows by duality: take $f$ to be a one-to-one mapping onto a set $N$ as constructed in Proposition 20.1* (the measure-theoretic analogue of a Lusin set). Every uncountable subset of such an $N$ has positive outer measure, so $f(E)$ has positive outer measure for every uncountable $E$.
