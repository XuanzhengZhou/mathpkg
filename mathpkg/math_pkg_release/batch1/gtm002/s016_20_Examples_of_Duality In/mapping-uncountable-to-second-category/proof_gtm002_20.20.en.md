Let $f$ be any one-to-one mapping of the line onto a Lusin set. If $E$ is uncountable, then $f(E)$ is an uncountable subset of a Lusin set. By definition of a Lusin set, every uncountable subset is of second category. Hence $f(E)$ is of second category.
