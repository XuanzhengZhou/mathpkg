Corollary 20.5 follows from the dual Proposition 20.4* and the observation that the class of automorphisms (homeomorphisms) of the line onto itself also has cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$. Under the continuum hypothesis, there exists an uncountable set $E$ of real numbers such that its image under any homeomorphism of the line is a Lebesgue nullset. This shows that in Theorem 13.2, the hypothesis that $A$ be a closed set cannot be omitted (though it can be weakened to $A$ being a Borel set). Sierpinski later proved an even stronger form without assuming CH, but the question of whether such a set can have cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ remains open without CH.
