This corollary follows from the dual of Proposition 20.4 (Proposition 20.4*). The class of automorphisms (homeomorphisms) of the line onto itself has cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$. Applying Proposition 20.4* with $K$ equal to the class of all automorphisms of the line yields a set $E$ of power $\mathfrak{c}$ such that $T(E)$ is a nullset for every automorphism $T$ of the line.

This shows that in Theorem 13.2, the hypothesis that $A$ be a closed set cannot be omitted, though it can be replaced by a weaker hypothesis such as that $A$ be a Borel set. Sierpinski [33, p. 274] later proved an even stronger form without assuming the continuum hypothesis, but the question of whether such a set can have cardinality $\mathfrak{c}$ without CH remains open.
