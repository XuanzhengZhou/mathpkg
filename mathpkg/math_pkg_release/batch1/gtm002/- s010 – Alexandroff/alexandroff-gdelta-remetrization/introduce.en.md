Alexandroff's theorem characterizes which subsets of a complete metric space can themselves be made into complete metric spaces via a compatible change of metric. The result states that every non-empty $G_{\delta}$ set (a countable intersection of open sets) in a complete metric space is topologically complete. This provides a fundamental link between the topological notion of $G_{\delta}$ sets and the metric notion of completeness. The proof proceeds via a lemma of Kuratowski that gives a sufficient condition for remetrizability, then constructs appropriate continuous functions on the $G_{\delta}$ set to satisfy that condition.
