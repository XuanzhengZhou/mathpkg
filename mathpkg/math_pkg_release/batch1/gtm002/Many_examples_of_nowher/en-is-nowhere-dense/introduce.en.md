This lemma is the second key step in Banach's category argument. To prove $E_n$ is nowhere dense, one starts with an arbitrary piecewise-linear continuous function $g$ (these are dense in $C$) and perturbs it by adding a rapidly oscillating "saw-tooth" function $\varepsilon \phi(mx)$, where $\phi(x) = \min(x - [x], [x] + 1 - x)$. By choosing $m$ sufficiently large ($m\varepsilon > n + M$, where $M$ bounds the slopes of $g$), the perturbed function $h = g + \varepsilon\phi(mx)$ has right derivative exceeding $n$ everywhere, hence lies outside $E_n$. Since $\varrho(g, h) = \varepsilon/2$ can be made arbitrarily small, $E_n$ contains no open ball and is therefore nowhere dense.
