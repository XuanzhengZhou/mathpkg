This corollary follows immediately from the theorem on right difference quotients via the isometry of $C$ induced by the change of variable $x \mapsto 1-x$. Under this transformation, right difference quotients become left difference quotients, and the Baire category of sets is preserved. The result shows that having a bounded left difference quotient at any point of $[0,1]$ is likewise a first-category property — in the sense of Baire category, almost no continuous function possesses one.
