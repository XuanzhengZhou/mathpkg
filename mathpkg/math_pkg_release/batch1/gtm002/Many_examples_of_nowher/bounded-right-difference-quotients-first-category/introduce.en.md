By the Baire category theorem, a countable union of closed nowhere dense sets is of first category. Since each $E_n$ is closed and nowhere dense in the complete metric space $C$, their union $E = \bigcup_n E_n$ is of first category. This set $E$ consists exactly of those continuous functions that possess a bounded right difference quotient at some point of $[0,1)$. The result shows that, in the sense of Baire category, it is exceptional for a continuous function to have a bounded right difference quotient anywhere — the overwhelming majority (a residual set) have unbounded right difference quotients at every point.
