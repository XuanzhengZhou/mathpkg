Theorem 19.5 is the core set-theoretic result underpinning the Sierpinski-Erdos theorem. It states that any $\sigma$-ideal $K$ on a set $X$ of power $\aleph_1$ satisfying four natural conditions (being a $\sigma$-ideal, covering $X$, having a generating subclass of size $\aleph_1$, and having large complements) admits a decomposition of $X$ into $\aleph_1$ disjoint subsets of power $\aleph_1$, such that membership in $K$ is characterized by being contained in a countable union of these decomposition sets. The proof uses transfinite induction and the set of ordinals of first and second class.
