Let $X$ be a set of power $\aleph_1$, and let $K$ be a class of subsets of $X$ with the following properties:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $K$ is a $\sigma$-ideal,
\item[(b)] the union of $K$ is $X$,
\item[(c)] $K$ has a subclass $G$ of power $\leq \aleph_1$ with the property that each member of $K$ is contained in some member of $G$,
\item[(d)] the complement of each member of $K$ contains a set of power $\aleph_1$ that belongs to $K$.
\end{enumerate}

Then $X$ can be decomposed into $\aleph_1$ disjoint sets $X_{\alpha}$, each of power $\aleph_1$, such that a subset $E$ of $X$ belongs to $K$ if and only if $E$ is contained in a countable union of the sets $X_{\alpha}$.
