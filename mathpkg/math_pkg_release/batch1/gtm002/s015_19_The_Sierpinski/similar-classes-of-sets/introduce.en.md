Two classes of subsets, $K$ on $X$ and $L$ on $Y$, are called similar if there exists a bijection $f: X \to Y$ that transports membership: a set $E \subseteq X$ belongs to $K$ exactly when its image $f(E)$ belongs to $L$. This notion captures the idea that the two classes are structurally indistinguishable under a relabeling of points. The definition is used by Sierpinski to formalize the duality between the $\sigma$-ideal of nullsets and the $\sigma$-ideal of sets of first category on the real line.
