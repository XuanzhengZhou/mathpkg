Erdos proved in 1943 that, assuming the continuum hypothesis, there exists a symmetric bijection $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ with $f = f^{-1}$ that simultaneously maps the $\sigma$-ideal of nullsets onto the $\sigma$-ideal of sets of first category, and vice versa. This strengthens Sierpinski's original theorem (which only guaranteed one direction of the mapping) by providing a symmetric duality. The proof relies on a purely set-theoretic decomposition theorem (Theorem 19.5) and a lemma (Theorem 19.4) about similar $\sigma$-ideals on a set of power $\aleph_1$.
