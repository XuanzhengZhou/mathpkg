This theorem is a special case of Theorem 19.3 (Erdős), since the proof of Theorem 19.3 also yields the one-directional mapping claimed in Theorem 19.2. Alternatively, one may apply the purely set-theoretic Lemma 19.4 to the two $\sigma$-ideals $K$ (nullsets) and $L$ (sets of first category) on the line. Both satisfy conditions (a)-(d) of Theorem 19.5 under the continuum hypothesis.
