Beyond topological transitivity, the category method has been applied to prove the existence of automorphisms with the stronger property of metrical transitivity. Metrical transitivity, closely related to ergodicity in measure-preserving dynamics, requires that every invariant measurable set be either null or co-null. This illustrates the breadth of category arguments in establishing existence results for dynamical systems with prescribed measure-theoretic properties.
