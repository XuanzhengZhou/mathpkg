As an immediate corollary of the first-category result for periodic automorphisms, the set of measure-preserving automorphisms possessing non-periodic points in every non-empty open set is residual in the space $M$. This means that "almost every" measure-preserving automorphism (in the Baire category sense) has non-periodic points densely distributed throughout the square — a property essential for the existence of transitive measure-preserving dynamics.
