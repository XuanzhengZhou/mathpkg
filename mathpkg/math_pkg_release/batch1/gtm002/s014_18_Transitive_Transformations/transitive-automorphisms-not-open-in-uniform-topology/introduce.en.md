In the complete metric space of all homeomorphisms of the unit square under the uniform metric, transitivity fails to be an open property. Given any transitive automorphism, an arbitrarily small perturbation can destroy transitivity by creating an invariant compact subset whose image under an iterate is strictly contained in its interior. This construction shows that the set of transitive automorphisms, while residual, is not topologically stable under small perturbations.
