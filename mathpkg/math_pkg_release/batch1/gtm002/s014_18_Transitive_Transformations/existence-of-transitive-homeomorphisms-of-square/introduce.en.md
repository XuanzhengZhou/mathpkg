The existence of a transitive homeomorphism of the closed unit square — a transformation with a dense positive semiorbit — was a problem whose solution was first obtained by the Baire category method. No constructive example was previously known, making this a landmark application of category arguments to dynamical systems. The proof considers the complete metric space of all homeomorphisms of the square under the uniform metric and shows that the subset of transitive ones is residual.
