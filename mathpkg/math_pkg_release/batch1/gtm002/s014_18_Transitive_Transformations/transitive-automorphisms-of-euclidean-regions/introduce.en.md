The existence of transitive automorphisms extends from the closed unit square to any region in Euclidean space of dimension two or greater. This generalization, obtained by similar category-theoretic reasoning, shows that transitivity is not a special property of the square but a generic phenomenon in higher-dimensional Euclidean topology. The result underscores the power of the Baire category method for proving existence of objects with prescribed dynamical behavior.
