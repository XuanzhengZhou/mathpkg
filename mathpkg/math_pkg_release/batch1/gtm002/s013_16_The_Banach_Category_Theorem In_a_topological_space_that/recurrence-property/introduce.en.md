The recurrence property abstracts Poincaré's observation that almost all points return infinitely often. For a transformation $T$, the set $D(E)$ consists of those points in $E$ whose orbit visits $E$ only finitely many times. $T$ has the recurrence property if $D(E)$ is always negligible (in $\mathcal{I}$). Theorem 17.2 establishes that the recurrence property is equivalent to $T$ being nondissipative — a beautiful and general unification of measure-theoretic and category-theoretic recurrence phenomena.
