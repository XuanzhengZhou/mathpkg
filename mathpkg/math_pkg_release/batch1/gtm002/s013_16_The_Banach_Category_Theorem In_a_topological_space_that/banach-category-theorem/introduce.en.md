The Banach Category Theorem states that in any topological space, the union of an arbitrary family of open sets of first category remains of first category. This is remarkable because in spaces with a countable base the result is trivial, yet it holds in all topological spaces without any countability assumption. The theorem is a cornerstone of the theory of Baire category, extending the Baire Category Theorem itself.
