Theorem 17.2 (the abstract recurrence theorem) establishes that an $\mathcal{S}$-measurable transformation $T$ has the recurrence property if and only if it is nondissipative. This elegant equivalence unifies Poincaré's original recurrence theorem under a single abstract framework. When $\mathcal{I}$ is the $\sigma$-ideal of nullsets and $T$ is measure-preserving with finite total measure, measurable wandering sets are automatically nullsets, so $T$ is nondissipative and thus has the recurrence property. Similarly, when $\mathcal{I}$ is the $\sigma$-ideal of first category sets and using the property of Baire, one obtains the category-theoretic version.
