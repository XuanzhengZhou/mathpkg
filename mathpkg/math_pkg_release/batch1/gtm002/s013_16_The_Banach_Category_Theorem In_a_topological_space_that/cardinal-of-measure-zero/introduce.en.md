A cardinal has measure zero if no nontrivial finite measure can be defined on all subsets of a set of that cardinality without concentrating on points. This notion, due to Marczewski and Sikorski, plays a crucial role in the measure-theoretic generalization of the Banach Category Theorem. Any cardinal smaller than one of measure zero also has measure zero, and every cardinal below the first weakly inaccessible cardinal is known to have measure zero.
