The category assertion: Poincaré showed that recurrent points are dense in $G$ by constructing a nested sequence of regions, which amounts to a proof of Baire's theorem for the case in hand. Since the set of points recurrent with respect to $G$ is a $G_\delta$ set, the set of non-recurrent points is an $F_\sigma$ set disjoint from a dense $G_\delta$, hence of first category.

The measure assertion: The volume-preserving property implies that the images of any open set cannot be mutually disjoint unless the set has measure zero. A set whose images are mutually disjoint is called a wandering set. The existence of a wandering set of positive measure would contradict the finite total volume. The set of non-recurrent points is the countable union of such wandering sets of measure zero, and hence has measure zero.

Both parts of Poincaré's theorem are contained in the abstract recurrence theorem (Theorem 17.2), formulated without the assumptions of volume preservation or one-to-one-ness.
