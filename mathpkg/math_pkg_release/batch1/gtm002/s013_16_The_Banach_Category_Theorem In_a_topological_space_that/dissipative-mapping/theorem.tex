Let $X$ be a set, let $\mathcal{S}$ be a $\sigma$-ring of subsets of $X$, let $\mathcal{I}$ be a $\sigma$-ideal in $\mathcal{S}$, and let $T$ be a mapping of $X$ into $X$. The mapping $T$ is called dissipative if there exists a wandering set that belongs to $\mathcal{S} - \mathcal{I}$. Otherwise $T$ is called nondissipative.
