The symmetric difference $A \triangle B$ of two sets $A$ and $B$ consists of all points that lie in exactly one of the two sets. It is a fundamental set-theoretic operation, defined as $(A - B) \cup (B - A)$. In measure theory, the symmetric difference is used to define an equivalence relation $A \sim B$ when $m(A \triangle B) = 0$, meaning the two sets agree almost everywhere.
