Theorem 3.16 is the culmination of the construction of Lebesgue measure in $r$-space. It states that the measurable sets form a $\sigma$-algebra generated by the topology (open sets) together with the nullsets, that $m$ is a genuine countably additive measure extending volume on intervals, and that the resulting measure space is complete -- every subset of a nullset is measurable (and is itself a nullset). This theorem effectively summarizes Lemmas 3.4 through 3.14.
