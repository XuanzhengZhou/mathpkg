By Theorem 3.15, every measurable set can be represented as an $F_\sigma$ set plus a nullset. Since $F_\sigma$ sets are Borel sets (hence generated by open sets), the measurable sets are precisely the $\sigma$-algebra generated by the open sets together with the nullsets. Lemma 3.13 and its generalization (Lemma 3.14) establish that $m$ is countably additive on $S$. Theorem 3.3 guarantees $m(I) = |I|$. Completeness follows because any subset of a nullset has outer measure zero and is therefore measurable by Lemma 3.12.
