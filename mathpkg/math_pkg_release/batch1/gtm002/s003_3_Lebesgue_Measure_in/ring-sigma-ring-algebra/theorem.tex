Let $X$ be a set and $S$ a non-empty class of subsets of $X$.

\begin{itemize}
\item $S$ is a \textit{ring} (of subsets of $X$) if it contains the union and the difference of any two of its members.
\item $S$ is a \textit{$\sigma$-ring} if it is a ring that also contains the union of any sequence of its members.
\item $S$ is an \textit{algebra} (of subsets of $X$) if it is a ring and $X \in S$. Equivalently, an algebra is closed under union (or intersection) and complementation.
\item $S$ is a \textit{$\sigma$-algebra} if it is an algebra that is also closed under countable union (or countable intersection).
\end{itemize}
