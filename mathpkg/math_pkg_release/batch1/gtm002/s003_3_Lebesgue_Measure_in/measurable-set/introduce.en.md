A set is Lebesgue measurable if it can be simultaneously approximated from the inside by closed sets and from the outside by open sets, with the measure of the approximating difference made arbitrarily small. This definition ensures that measurable sets form a $\sigma$-algebra on which $m^*$ is countably additive, yielding a complete measure space $(\mathbb{R}^r, S, m)$. The class of measurable sets is the completion of the Borel $\sigma$-algebra by nullsets.
