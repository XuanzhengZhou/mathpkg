For any class of subsets of a set $X$, there is a smallest $\sigma$-algebra of subsets of $X$ that contains it, namely, the intersection of all such $\sigma$-algebras. The members of the $\sigma$-algebra of subsets of $r$-space generated by the class of open sets (equivalently, by the class of closed sets or intervals) are called \textit{Borel sets}.

Every Borel subset of $r$-space is measurable.
