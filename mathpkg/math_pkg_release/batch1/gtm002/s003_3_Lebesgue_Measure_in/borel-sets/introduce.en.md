Borel sets are the members of the smallest $\sigma$-algebra containing all open subsets of $\mathbb{R}^r$. They can be generated equally well by the closed sets or by the intervals. Every Borel set is Lebesgue measurable; together with the nullsets, Borel sets generate the full $\sigma$-algebra of measurable sets. Borel sets form a rich hierarchy ($F_\sigma$, $G_\delta$, $F_{\sigma\delta}$, $G_{\delta\sigma}$, etc.) that plays a central role in descriptive set theory.
