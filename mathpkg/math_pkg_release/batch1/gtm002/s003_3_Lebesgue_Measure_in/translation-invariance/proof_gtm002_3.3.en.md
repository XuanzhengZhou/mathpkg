This is clear from the definitions and from the fact that congruent intervals have equal volume. Since outer measure is defined in terms of coverings by intervals, and translation bijectively maps covering sequences of intervals to covering sequences of congruent intervals with the same total volume, we have $m^*(A) = m^*(B)$ whenever $A$ and $B$ are congruent by translation. Consequently, the measurability condition (existence of closed $F$ and open $G$ with $F \subset A \subset G$ and $m^*(G-F) < \varepsilon$) is also preserved under translation.
