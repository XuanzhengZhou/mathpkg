Lemma 3.13 establishes countable additivity for Lebesgue measure on a disjoint sequence of measurable sets contained in a common interval. The proof shows both that the union remains measurable and that the outer measure (now called Lebesgue measure) is exactly the sum of the individual measures. Combined with later results, this extends to all measurable sets without the boundedness restriction.
