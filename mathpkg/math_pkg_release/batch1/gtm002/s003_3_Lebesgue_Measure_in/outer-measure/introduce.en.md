Outer measure $m^*(A)$ is defined for every subset $A$ of $r$-space as the infimum of the total volumes of all countable coverings of $A$ by intervals. It is always defined (as either a finite nonnegative number or $\infty$) and enjoys the property of countable subadditivity. Outer measure extends the elementary notion of volume to arbitrary sets and is the first step in constructing Lebesgue measure.
