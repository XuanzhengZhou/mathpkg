The density of a measurable set $E \subset \mathbb{R}$ at a point $x$ measures the proportion of $E$ in small intervals centered at $x$. Formally, it is the limit of $m(E \cap [x-h, x+h]) / (2h)$ as $h \to 0$, if this limit exists. A point where the density equals $1$ is called a point of density $1$. The Lebesgue Density Theorem states that almost every point of a measurable set is a point of density $1$ of that set, revealing a fundamental local regularity of Lebesgue measure.
