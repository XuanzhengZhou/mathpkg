Let $E \subset \mathbb{R}$ be a measurable set. For $x \in \mathbb{R}$, the \textit{density} of $E$ at $x$ is defined as

$$
D(E, x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{m(E \cap [x - h, x + h])}{2h},
$$

provided the limit exists. The \textit{lower density} and \textit{upper density} are defined analogously using $\liminf$ and $\limsup$.

A point $x$ is called a \textit{point of density $1$} of $E$ if $D(E, x) = 1$. The set of all points of density $1$ of $E$ is denoted by $\phi(E)$.
