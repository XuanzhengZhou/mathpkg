Given any collection $\mathcal{C}$ of subsets of a set $X$, the $\sigma$-algebra generated by $\mathcal{C}$ is the smallest $\sigma$-algebra containing $\mathcal{C}$. It is obtained by intersecting all $\sigma$-algebras that contain $\mathcal{C}$. This construction is fundamental in measure theory: for instance, the Borel $\sigma$-algebra on $\mathbb{R}^r$ is defined as the $\sigma$-algebra generated by the open sets (or equivalently, by the closed sets or the intervals).
