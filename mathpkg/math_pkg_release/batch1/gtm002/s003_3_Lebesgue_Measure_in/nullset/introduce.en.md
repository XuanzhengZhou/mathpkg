A nullset is a subset of $r$-space whose outer measure is zero. Nullsets are the "negligible" sets in Lebesgue measure theory: they are always measurable, and any subset of a nullset is also a nullset. Together with Borel sets, nullsets generate the full $\sigma$-algebra of Lebesgue measurable sets.
