Theorem 3.3 confirms that outer measure is a genuine extension of the elementary notion of volume: for an interval $I$, the outer measure $m^*(I)$ equals its $r$-dimensional volume $|I|$. The inequality $m^*(I) \leq |I|$ is trivial (since $I$ covers itself), while the reverse inequality relies on the Heine-Borel theorem and a careful subdivision argument.
