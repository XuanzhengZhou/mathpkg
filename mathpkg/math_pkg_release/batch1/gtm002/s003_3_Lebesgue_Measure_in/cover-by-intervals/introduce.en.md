A cover of a set $A$ by intervals is a collection of intervals (finite or countably infinite) whose union contains $A$. This is the fundamental notion used to approximate arbitrary subsets of $r$-space from the outside, leading to the definition of outer measure. The intervals in the covering sequence can be taken with any combination of open, closed, or half-open edges without affecting the resulting measure.
