Lemma 3.12 confirms that the two most basic classes of sets — intervals and nullsets — are measurable. The measurability of intervals follows from Lemma 3.11 and Theorem 3.3, while for nullsets one can take $G$ as a union of open intervals whose total volume is arbitrarily small and $F = \emptyset$.
