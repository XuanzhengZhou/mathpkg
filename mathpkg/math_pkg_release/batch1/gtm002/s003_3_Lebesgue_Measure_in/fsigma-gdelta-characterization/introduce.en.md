Theorem 3.15 provides a beautiful structural characterization of measurable sets: every measurable set differs from a Borel set (specifically, an $F_\sigma$ or $G_\delta$ set) by at most a nullset. This reveals that the class of measurable sets is essentially the completion of the Borel $\sigma$-algebra with respect to Lebesgue measure, obtained by adjoining all subsets of nullsets.
