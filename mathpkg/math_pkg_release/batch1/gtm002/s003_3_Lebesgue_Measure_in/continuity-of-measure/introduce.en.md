Theorem 3.17 expresses the continuity of Lebesgue measure from below. For any measurable set $A$, its measure equals the limit of the measures of its bounded truncations $A \cap (-i, i)^r$. More generally, if a sequence of measurable sets increases to a limit set, the measure of the limit is the limit of the measures. This property is a direct consequence of countable additivity and is essential for approximating the measure of unbounded sets by bounded ones.
