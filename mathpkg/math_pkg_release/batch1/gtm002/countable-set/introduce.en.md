A countable set is one that is either finite or denumerable (can be put in one-to-one correspondence with a subset of the natural numbers). The notion of countability is fundamental to both measure theory and Baire category. The class of countable sets forms a $\sigma$-ideal, and every countable set is both a nullset and of first category.
