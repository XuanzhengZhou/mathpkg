The oscillation of $f$ at a point $x$, denoted $\omega(x)$, is the limit of the oscillation on symmetric intervals shrinking to $x$. It captures the local behavior of $f$: $\omega(x_0) = 0$ characterizes continuity at $x_0$, while a positive value quantifies the magnitude of the discontinuity. This concept reduces the study of discontinuities to the size of the sets $\{x : \omega(x) \geq \varepsilon\}$.
