The oscillation of a real-valued function $f$ on an interval $I$, denoted $\omega(I)$, measures the maximum variation of $f$ over that interval. It is defined as the difference between the supremum and infimum of $f$ on $I$. This quantity is fundamental to the study of continuity, Riemann integrability, and the classification of discontinuities.
