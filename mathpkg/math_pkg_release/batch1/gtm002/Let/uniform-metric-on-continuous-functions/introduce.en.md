The space $C[a,b]$ of continuous real-valued functions on a closed interval, equipped with the uniform metric $\varrho(f,g) = \sup |f(x) - g(x)|$, is one of the most important examples of a metric space in analysis. Convergence in this metric corresponds precisely to uniform convergence of functions. The triangle inequality follows from the pointwise inequality $|f(x)-h(x)| \leq |f(x)-g(x)| + |g(x)-h(x)|$ and the fact that the supremum of a sum is bounded by the sum of suprema.
