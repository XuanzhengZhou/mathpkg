The oscillation of a function at a point is defined as the limit of the oscillation on symmetric intervals shrinking to that point. It is an extended real-valued function that vanishes exactly at points of continuity. Its value at a discontinuity quantifies the magnitude of the jump or break. This concept is instrumental in Baire's classification of functions and the study of Riemann integrability.
