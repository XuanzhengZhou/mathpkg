Lemma 14.1 provides a useful equivalent characterization of Lebesgue nullsets: a set has measure zero exactly when it can be covered infinitely many times by a sequence of intervals whose total length sum converges. The forward direction follows by taking covers of measure $1/2^n$ and concatenating them. The converse holds because the tail of a convergent series becomes arbitrarily small, providing covers of arbitrarily small total measure.
