Theorem 13.3 provides a beautiful synthesis of the three $\sigma$-ideals studied in the book: countable sets, first category sets, and nullsets. It characterizes when a bounded function can be made Riemann integrable by a strictly monotone change of variable (a homeomorphic substitution). The three cases correspond exactly to the three possible "sizes" of the discontinuity set: countable (always integrable after any homeomorphism), first category (integrable after some homeomorphism), or nullset (integrable without any change). This encapsulates the relationship between topological and measure-theoretic notions of smallness.
