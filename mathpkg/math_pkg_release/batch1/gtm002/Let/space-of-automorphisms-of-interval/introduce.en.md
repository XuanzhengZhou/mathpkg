The space $H$ of endpoint-fixing homeomorphisms of $[0,1]$, metrized by the uniform distance, provides the natural setting for a category-based proof. Although $H$ is not complete under the uniform metric, it is a $G_\delta$ subset of the complete space $C[0,1]$, hence topologically complete by the Baire category theorem. This topological completeness is essential for the proof that "most" automorphisms map a given first category set to a nullset.
