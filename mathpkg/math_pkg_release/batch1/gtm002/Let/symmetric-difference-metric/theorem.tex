Let $S$ be the class of measurable subsets of $[0,1]$, and define
$$\varrho(E, F) = m(E \triangle F),$$
where $E \triangle F = (E - F) \cup (F - E)$ is the symmetric difference and $m$ is Lebesgue measure. Then $\varrho$ satisfies the first three metric axioms. Identifying sets that differ by a nullset yields a metric space $(\tilde{S}, \varrho)$.
