The symmetric difference metric, defined by $\varrho(E,F) = m(E \triangle F)$ on measurable subsets of $[0,1]$, measures how much two sets differ in terms of Lebesgue measure. The triangle inequality follows from the set identity $E \triangle H = (E \triangle F) \triangle (F \triangle H)$ and the subadditivity of measure. Identifying sets that differ by a nullset yields a complete metric space that plays an important role in measure theory.
