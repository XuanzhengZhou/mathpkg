A pseudo-metric space generalizes a metric space by dropping the requirement that distinct points have strictly positive distance. Only non-negativity, symmetry, and the triangle inequality are required. The key example in Oxtoby's text is the space $R[a,b]$ of Riemann-integrable functions with the integral metric $\sigma(f,g) = \int_a^b |f(x)-g(x)| \, dx$, where two functions that differ only on a null set have distance zero. A pseudo-metric space can always be turned into a genuine metric space by identifying points at distance zero.
