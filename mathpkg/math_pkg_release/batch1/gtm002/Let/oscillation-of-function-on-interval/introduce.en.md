The oscillation of a function on an interval measures the maximum variation of the function's values over that interval. It is defined as the difference between the supremum and infimum of the function on the interval. This quantity is fundamental in studying continuity, Riemann integrability, and the classification of discontinuities. A small oscillation on small intervals indicates that the function varies little locally, which is closely related to continuity.
