Baire's Theorem (Theorem 7.3) is a cornerstone result showing that the pointwise limit of continuous functions cannot be "too discontinuous" — its discontinuity set must be of first category. This was the original context in which Baire introduced the notion of category. It immediately implies, for instance, that the sum of an everywhere convergent trigonometric series cannot be everywhere discontinuous.
