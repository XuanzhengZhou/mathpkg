The symmetric difference metric $\varrho(E,F) = m(E \triangle F)$ provides a natural distance between measurable sets, measuring how much they differ in terms of Lebesgue measure. The triangle inequality follows from $E \triangle H \subset (E \triangle F) \cup (F \triangle H)$. Identifying sets differing by measure zero yields a complete metric space, demonstrating that measure-theoretic notions can be studied through metric space topology.
