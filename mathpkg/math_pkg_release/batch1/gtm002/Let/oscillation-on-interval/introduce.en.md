The oscillation of a function on an interval measures the maximum variation of the function over that interval. It is defined as the difference between the supremum and infimum of the function values on the interval. The oscillation provides a quantitative measure of how much a function fluctuates, and is fundamental to the study of continuity and Riemann integrability.
