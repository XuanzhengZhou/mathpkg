Let $F$ be a nowhere dense closed subset of $I = [0, 1]$. There exists a homeomorphism $h$ of $I$ onto itself such that $h(F)$ is a nullset. Explicitly, letting $G = I - F$, the map

$$h(x) = \frac{m([0, x] \cap G)}{m(G)}$$

is a strictly increasing continuous map of $I$ onto itself such that the intervals composing $G$ are mapped onto a sequence of intervals of total length $1$, so that $h(F)$ is a nullset.
