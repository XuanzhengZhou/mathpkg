The metric space $(\tilde{S}, \varrho)$ of measurable sets modulo nullsets, with distance given by the measure of the symmetric difference, is a complete metric space. The proof constructs the limit of a Cauchy sequence by taking liminf and limsup of an appropriately chosen subsequence. This space is an important example in Oxtoby's book, serving as the measure-theoretic counterpart to the Baire category metric space and illustrating the duality between measure and category.
