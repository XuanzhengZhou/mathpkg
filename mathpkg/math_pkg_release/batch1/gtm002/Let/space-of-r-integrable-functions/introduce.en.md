The space $(\tilde{R}, \sigma)$ of R-integrable functions on $[a,b]$ is obtained by starting with the set $R[a,b]$ of all Riemann-integrable functions, equipping it with the integral metric $\sigma(f,g) = \int_a^b |f(x)-g(x)| \, dx$, and then identifying functions that differ by a null function. This quotient construction turns what would otherwise be a pseudo-metric space into a proper metric space. The space $(\tilde{R}, \sigma)$ is an early precursor to the $L^1$ space of Lebesgue integration theory.
