A standard construction converts a pseudometric space into a genuine metric space by forming the quotient under the equivalence relation $x \sim y \iff \varrho(x,y) = 0$. The distance function descends to the quotient, where it satisfies all four metric axioms. This construction is used, for example, to obtain the space of $L^1$ functions from the space of Riemann-integrable functions by identifying functions that are equal almost everywhere.
