Fubini's Theorem for nullsets (Theorem 14.2) is the special case of Fubini's theorem restricted to sets of measure zero: a plane nullset has the property that almost every vertical (or horizontal) section is a linear nullset. This formulation suffices for many applications where the general measurability question can be reduced to the nullset case. The proof is constructive, using sequences of rectangles and the infinite covering characterization of nullsets.
