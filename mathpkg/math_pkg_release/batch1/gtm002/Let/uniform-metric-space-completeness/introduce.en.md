The completeness of $C[a,b]$ under the uniform metric is a cornerstone result. Given a Cauchy sequence of continuous functions, pointwise limits exist by completeness of $\mathbb{R}$, and the Cauchy condition in the uniform metric forces the convergence to be uniform. The uniform limit of continuous functions is continuous, so the limit stays in $C[a,b]$. This makes $(C[a,b], \varrho)$ a Banach space.
