The space of Riemann-integrable functions $R[a,b]$ with the $L^1$ metric is a pseudometric space, not a metric space, because two functions differing only on a nullset have distance zero. By taking the quotient modulo the subspace of null functions (functions $f$ with $\int |f| = 0$), one obtains a genuine metric space $\tilde{R}[a,b]$. This construction is a precursor to the $L^1$ space in Lebesgue theory.
