A concrete counterexample demonstrates that $(C[0,1],\sigma)$ is not complete. The sequence of piecewise linear functions $f_n$ that equal $1$ on $[0, 1/2 - 1/2n]$, $0$ on $[1/2 + 1/2n, 1]$, and interpolate linearly between, forms a Cauchy sequence in the $L^1$ metric. Its pointwise limit is the indicator function of $[0,1/2]$, which is not continuous, and no continuous function can serve as the $L^1$ limit.
