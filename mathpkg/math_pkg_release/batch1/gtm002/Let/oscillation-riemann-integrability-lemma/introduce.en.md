Lemma 7.6 establishes a quantitative link between the oscillation of a function at points of an interval and its Riemann integrability. It states that if the oscillation at every point of an interval is less than $\varepsilon$, then the difference between the upper and lower integrals is bounded by $\varepsilon$ times the interval length. The proof uses a bisection argument and the nested interval property.
