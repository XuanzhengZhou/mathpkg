Fubini's theorem for nullsets (Theorem 14.2) asserts that if a subset $E$ of the plane has two-dimensional Lebesgue measure zero, then for almost every $x$, the $x$-section $E_x$ is a linear nullset. The proof uses the infinite covering characterization (Lemma 14.1) along with a clever construction of a sequence of functions $\phi_n$ whose integrals control the measures of sections where the covering condition fails. This is the foundational case of Fubini's theorem before passing to general measurable sets.
