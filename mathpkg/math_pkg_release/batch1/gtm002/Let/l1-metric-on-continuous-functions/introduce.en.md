The $L^1$ metric on $C[a,b]$ is defined as the integral of the absolute difference of two functions. Unlike the uniform metric, convergence in the $L^1$ metric corresponds to convergence in mean. While it is a legitimate metric, the space $(C[a,b], \sigma)$ is not complete, as demonstrated by a simple counterexample of continuous functions converging in $L^1$ to a discontinuous limit.
