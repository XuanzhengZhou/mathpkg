Theorem 13.3 beautifully unifies the three $\sigma$-ideals — countable sets, first category sets, and nullsets — through the lens of Riemann integrability. Given a bounded function $f$, Riemann integrability of $f \circ h$ under all homeomorphisms characterizes countable discontinuity sets; existence of some homeomorphism making $f \circ h$ integrable characterizes first category sets; and integrability of $f$ itself characterizes nullsets. Each $\sigma$-ideal thus answers a distinct question about the effect of monotone substitution on Riemann integrability.
