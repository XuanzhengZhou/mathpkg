The space $C[a,b]$ of continuous real-valued functions on a closed interval, equipped with the uniform metric $\varrho(f,g) = \sup|f-g|$, is one of the most important examples of a metric space. Convergence in this metric coincides with uniform convergence, a stronger notion than pointwise convergence. This space is also a fundamental example in functional analysis as a Banach space.
