Showing that $H$ is a $G_\delta$ subset of $C[0,1]$ is the key lemma enabling a Baire category argument for Theorem 13.1. The proof defines $H_n$ as the set of functions $f \in C$ such that $f(x) \neq f(y)$ whenever $|x-y| \geq 1/n$, and shows each $H_n$ is open in $C$. The intersection of all $H_n$ gives exactly the injective functions; requiring surjectivity (via endpoint fixing) yields $H$ as a $G_\delta$ set.
