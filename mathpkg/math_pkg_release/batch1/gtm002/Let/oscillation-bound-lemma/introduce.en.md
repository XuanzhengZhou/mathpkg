Lemma 7.6 is a technical result used in the proof of Lebesgue's criterion for Riemann integrability. It states that if the pointwise oscillation of a function is uniformly bounded by $\varepsilon$ on an interval $I$, then the difference between the upper and lower Darboux integrals (denoted $F(I)$) is bounded by $\varepsilon|I|$. The proof uses a bisection argument to derive a contradiction from the contrary assumption.
