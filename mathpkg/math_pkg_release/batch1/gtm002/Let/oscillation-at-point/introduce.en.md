The oscillation of a function at a point is defined as the limit of the oscillation of the function on symmetric intervals shrinking to that point. This concept provides a precise quantitative measure of the size of a discontinuity: the oscillation is zero exactly at points of continuity, and positive at points of discontinuity. It is a key tool for analyzing the structure of the set of points of discontinuity of a real-valued function.
