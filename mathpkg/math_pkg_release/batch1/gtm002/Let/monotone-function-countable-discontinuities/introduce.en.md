Theorem 7.8 establishes a precise characterization of the discontinuity sets of monotone functions: they are exactly the countable sets. The forward direction follows from the fact that a monotone function can only have jump discontinuities whose total magnitude is bounded on any finite interval. The converse constructs, for any countable set, a monotone function with exactly those points as discontinuities by using a convergent series of step functions with jumps at the prescribed points.
