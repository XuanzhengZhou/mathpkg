The degree of an algebraic number is the smallest positive integer $n$ for which the number satisfies a polynomial equation of degree $n$ with integer coefficients. For example, rational numbers have degree $1$, $\sqrt{2}$ has degree $2$, and $\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3}$ has degree $4$. The degree plays a fundamental role in Diophantine approximation, particularly in Liouville's inequality, where the quality of rational approximation depends inversely on the degree of the algebraic number.
