Theorem 2.1 states that the set of all real algebraic numbers is denumerable (countably infinite). The proof enumerates polynomials by weight, defined as the sum of the degree and absolute values of coefficients, then lists their real zeros. Since each non-constant polynomial has only finitely many real zeros, the entire collection is countable. Combined with Cantor's theorem that $[0,1]$ is uncountable, this yields the simplest proof that transcendental numbers exist.
