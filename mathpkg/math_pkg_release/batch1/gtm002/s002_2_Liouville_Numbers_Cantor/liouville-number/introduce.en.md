A Liouville number is a real number that can be approximated by rational numbers to an extraordinary degree of accuracy: for every positive integer $k$, there exist integers $p$ and $q$ with $q > 1$ such that $|z - p/q| < 1/q^k$. This means the error of rational approximation shrinks faster than any power of the denominator. By Liouville's inequality, no algebraic number of degree greater than $1$ can satisfy this property, so all Liouville numbers are transcendental. They were the first explicit examples of transcendental numbers ever constructed.
