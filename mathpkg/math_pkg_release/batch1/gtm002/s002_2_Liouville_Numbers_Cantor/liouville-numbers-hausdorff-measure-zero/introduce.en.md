Theorem 2.4 establishes that the set $E$ of Liouville numbers has $s$-dimensional Hausdorff measure zero for every $s > 0$. This is proved by covering $E \cap (-m, m)$ with intervals centered at rationals $p/q$ of radius $1/q^n$, then choosing $n$ large enough that the sum of the $s$-th powers of the interval lengths becomes arbitrarily small. This result illustrates the complementary relationship between measure and category: Liouville numbers are residual (large in the sense of Baire category) yet form a nullset with respect to every Hausdorff measure.
