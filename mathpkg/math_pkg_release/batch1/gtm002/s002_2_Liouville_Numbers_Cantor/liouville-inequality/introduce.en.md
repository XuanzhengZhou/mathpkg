Liouville's inequality provides a fundamental lower bound on the distance between an algebraic number of degree $n > 1$ and any rational approximation $p/q$. Specifically, there exists a constant $M$ such that $|z - p/q| > 1/(Mq^n)$ for all integers $p, q$ with $q > 0$. This shows that algebraic numbers cannot be approximated too well by rationals relative to the denominator size. The inequality is the cornerstone of Liouville's constructive proof that transcendental numbers exist: any number that violates this bound for all $n$ must be transcendental.
