Liouville's theorem states that every Liouville number is transcendental. The proof uses Lemma 2.2 (Liouville's inequality): if a Liouville number $z$ were algebraic of degree $n > 1$, then for any $k > n$ the inequality $|z - p/q| < 1/q^k$ would contradict the required lower bound $|z - p/q| > 1/(Mq^n)$. This theorem provided the first constructive proof of the existence of transcendental numbers, preceding Cantor's non-constructive cardinality argument.
