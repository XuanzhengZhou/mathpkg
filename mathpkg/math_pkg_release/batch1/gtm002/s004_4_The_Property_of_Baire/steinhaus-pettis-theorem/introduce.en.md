Theorem 4.8 draws a striking parallel between the measurable/measure and Baire/category frameworks. For a set of positive measure, sufficiently small translations always intersect the original set (Steinhaus theorem). The category analogue states that for a second-category set with the Baire property, sufficiently small translations produce intersections that actually contain an interval -- a stronger conclusion reflecting the different nature of the two notions of "largeness." This theorem plays a crucial role in the construction of non-measurable sets (Vitali's construction in Chapter 5) and in establishing that neither the class of measurable sets nor the class of Baire-property sets contains the other.
