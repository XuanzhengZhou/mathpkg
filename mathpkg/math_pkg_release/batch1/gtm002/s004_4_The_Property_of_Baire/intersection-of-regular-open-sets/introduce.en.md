Theorem 4.7 shows that the intersection of two regular open sets is again regular open. The proof uses the monotonicity of the interior-of-closure operation: since $G \cap H$ is open and contained in both $G$ and $H$, its regularization $(G \cap H)^{-'-'}$ is also contained in $G^{-'-'} = G$ and $H^{-'-'} = H$. This result is a key step in establishing that the regular open sets form a complete Boolean algebra under suitably defined operations.
