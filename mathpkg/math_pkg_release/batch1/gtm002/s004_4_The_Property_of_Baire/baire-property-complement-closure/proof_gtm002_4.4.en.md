Let $A$ have the property of Baire, so $A = G \triangle P$ with $G$ open and $P$ of first category. Then $A' = (G \triangle P)' = G' \triangle P$, since symmetric difference with $P$ is unchanged under complementation of the first argument together with the whole expression. Now $G'$ is closed, so by Theorem 4.1, $A' = F \triangle Q$ with $F = G'$ closed and $Q = P$ of first category. Hence $A'$ has the property of Baire.
