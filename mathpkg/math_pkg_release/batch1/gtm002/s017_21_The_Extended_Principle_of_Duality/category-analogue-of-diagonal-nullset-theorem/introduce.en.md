This proposition is the naive category-theoretic analogue of Theorem 21.2, obtained by replacing "measurable" with "has the property of Baire" and "nullset" with "of first category." Unlike its measure-theoretic counterpart, this statement is false. A counterexample is constructed using an enumeration of the rational points. The failure of this dual statement demonstrates that the extended principle of duality between measure and category has fundamental limitations.
