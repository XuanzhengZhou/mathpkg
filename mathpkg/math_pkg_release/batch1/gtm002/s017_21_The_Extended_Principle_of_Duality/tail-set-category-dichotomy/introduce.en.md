This theorem is the category-theoretic analogue of the measure-theoretic zero-one law for tail sets. It states that any tail set in the Cantor space $X = \{0,1\}^\mathbb{N}$ that has the Baire property must be either topologically small (first category) or topologically large (residual). Unlike the measure-theoretic version, which holds for all measurable sets, this category version requires the stronger hypothesis of the Baire property. The theorem generalizes to products of arbitrary families of Baire spaces each having a countable base.
