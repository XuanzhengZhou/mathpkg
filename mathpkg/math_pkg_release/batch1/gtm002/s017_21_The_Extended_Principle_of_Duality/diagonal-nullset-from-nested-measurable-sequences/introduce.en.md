Given a double sequence of measurable sets that are nested downward in the second index and whose intersection over the second index is null for each fixed first index, one can cleverly choose a diagonal subsequence via a sequence of index mappings. The theorem asserts that the resulting iterated intersection of unions is still a nullset. This is a measure-theoretic selection principle used to contrast the behavior of measurability with that of the Baire property, since the category analogue of this statement turns out to be false.
