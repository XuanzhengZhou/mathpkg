The interior of a set $A$ is the largest open set contained in $A$, equivalently the union of all open subsets of $A$. It equals the complement of the closure of the complement: $\operatorname{int}(A) = X \setminus \overline{X \setminus A}$. A set is a neighborhood of a point if the point belongs to its interior.
