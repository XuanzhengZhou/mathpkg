A Baire space is a topological space in which every non-empty open set is of second category (nonmeager). Equivalently, the intersection of any countable family of dense open sets is dense. By Theorem 9.1, every topologically complete metric space is a Baire space. Baire spaces provide the natural context for the Baire category theorem and are essential in functional analysis.
