A sequence in a metric space converges to a limit point if the distance between the terms and the limit tends to zero. Convergence in a metric space is a topological property: it can be characterized entirely in terms of open sets. Specifically, $x_n \to x$ if and only if every open set containing $x$ contains all but finitely many terms of the sequence.
