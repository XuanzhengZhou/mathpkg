The uniform metric on $C[a,b]$, the space of continuous real-valued functions on a closed interval, is defined as $\varrho(f,g) = \sup_{a \leq x \leq b} |f(x) - g(x)|$. This metric measures the maximum pointwise distance between two functions. Convergence in the uniform metric is equivalent to uniform convergence of a sequence of functions. The uniform metric is a fundamental construction that makes $C[a,b]$ into a complete metric space, a property essential for many results in analysis.
