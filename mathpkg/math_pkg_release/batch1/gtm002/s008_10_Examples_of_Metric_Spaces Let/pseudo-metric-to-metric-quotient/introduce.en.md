Every pseudo-metric space can be canonically transformed into a genuine metric space through a quotient construction. Define an equivalence relation by $x \sim y \iff \varrho(x,y) = 0$, which is well-defined because the triangle inequality and symmetry ensure transitivity. The distance function $\varrho$ then descends to a well-defined metric on the quotient space $\tilde{X}$ of equivalence classes. This construction is widely used in analysis, for instance to obtain the $L^1$ space of Riemann-integrable functions from the pseudo-metric space $R[a,b]$.
