The symmetric difference metric is defined on the collection of measurable sets of finite measure by $\varrho(E,F) = m(E \triangle F)$, where $m$ is Lebesgue measure. This metric measures how much two sets differ in terms of measure. The triangle inequality follows from the set-theoretic identity $(E \triangle H) = (E \triangle F) \triangle (F \triangle H)$ and the subadditivity of measure. After identifying sets that differ by a null set, one obtains the complete metric space $(\tilde{S}, \varrho)$, which plays an important role in the study of the measure algebra associated with a measure space.
