The integral metric on $C[a,b]$ is defined as $\sigma(f,g) = \int_a^b |f(x) - g(x)| \, dx$, measuring the $L^1$ distance between two continuous functions. Unlike the uniform metric, convergence in the integral metric means convergence in the mean rather than uniform convergence. A key distinction is that $(C[a,b], \sigma)$ is not complete, which motivates the passage to the larger space of Riemann-integrable functions.
