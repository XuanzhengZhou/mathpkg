The space of R-integrable functions, denoted $(\tilde{R}, \sigma)$, is obtained from the set $R[a,b]$ of Riemann-integrable functions by identifying functions that differ by a null function. Formally, this is the quotient of the pseudo-metric space $(R[a,b], \sigma)$, where $\sigma(f,g) = \int_a^b |f-g|$, by the equivalence relation $f \sim g \iff \int_a^b |f-g| = 0$. The resulting space is a genuine metric space and serves as a completion of $(C[a,b], \sigma)$, analogous to the construction of $L^1$ spaces in Lebesgue integration theory.
