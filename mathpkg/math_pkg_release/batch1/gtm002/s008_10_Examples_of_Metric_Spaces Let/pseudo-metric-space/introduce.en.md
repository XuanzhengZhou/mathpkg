A pseudo-metric space is a set equipped with a distance function that satisfies the first three metric axioms (non-negativity, symmetry, and the triangle inequality) but not necessarily the fourth axiom of positive definiteness. In a pseudo-metric space, distinct points may have distance zero. A classic example is the space $R[a,b]$ of Riemann-integrable functions with the integral metric $\sigma$, where two functions that differ on a set of measure zero have $\sigma(f,g) = 0$ even if they are not identical. Any pseudo-metric space can be canonically converted into a genuine metric space by identifying points at distance zero.
