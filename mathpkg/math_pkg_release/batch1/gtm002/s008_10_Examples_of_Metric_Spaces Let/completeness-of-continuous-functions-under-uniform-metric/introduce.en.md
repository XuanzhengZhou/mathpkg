The space $C[a,b]$ of continuous real-valued functions equipped with the uniform metric $\varrho(f,g) = \sup |f-g|$ is a complete metric space. The proof shows that any Cauchy sequence $\{f_n\}$ converges pointwise to a limit $f$, and the convergence is uniform. The uniform limit of continuous functions is continuous, so $f \in C[a,b]$. This makes $(C[a,b], \varrho)$ one of the fundamental examples of a complete metric space in analysis.
