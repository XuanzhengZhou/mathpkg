Given a complete finite measure space $(X, S, \mu)$, a mapping $\phi : S \to S$ is called a \textbf{lower density} if, denoting by $A \sim B$ the relation $A \triangle B \in \mathcal{N}$ (where $\mathcal{N}$ is the class of $\mu$-nullsets), the following hold:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\phi(A) \sim A$,
\item $A \sim B \implies \phi(A) = \phi(B)$,
\item $\phi(\emptyset) = \emptyset$, $\phi(X) = X$,
\item $\phi(A \cap B) = \phi(A) \cap \phi(B)$,
\item $A \subset B \implies \phi(A) \subset \phi(B)$.
\end{enumerate}
