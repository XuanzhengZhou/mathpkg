The von Neumann--Maharam theorem guarantees that every complete finite measure space admits a lower density --- a mapping that selects a canonical representative from each measure-equivalence class. This selection respects Boolean operations (intersection, inclusion) and provides the essential tool for constructing a topology that turns the measure space into a category measure space. For Lebesgue measure, the Lebesgue density theorem (Theorem 3.21) already provides such a mapping.
