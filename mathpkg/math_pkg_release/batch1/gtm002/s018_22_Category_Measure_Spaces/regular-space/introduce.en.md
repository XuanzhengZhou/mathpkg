A regular space is a Hausdorff topological space with the additional property that every neighborhood of a point contains a closed neighborhood. This separation property ensures that points and closed sets can be separated by disjoint open sets, making regular spaces a fundamental class in point-set topology. In the context of category measure spaces, regularity is assumed throughout to guarantee that the category measure behaves well with respect to open and closed approximations.
