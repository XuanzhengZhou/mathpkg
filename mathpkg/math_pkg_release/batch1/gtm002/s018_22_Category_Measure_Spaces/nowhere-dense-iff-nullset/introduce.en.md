Theorem 22.6 establishes the fundamental identification in the density topology: a set is nowhere dense precisely when it is a $\mu$-nullset. Moreover, every such nowhere dense set is already closed. This identification is what makes $\mu$ a category measure --- the topological notion of "smallness" (nowhere dense, hence first category) coincides with the measure-theoretic notion of "smallness" (measure zero).
