Let $(X, S, \mu)$ be a complete finite measure space and let $\phi$ be a lower density on $X$. Let $\mathcal{N} = \{N : \mu(N) = 0\}$ and for each $A \in S$ and $N \in \mathcal{N}$ let $N_A$ be a nullset. Then the family
$$\mathcal{T} = \{\phi(A) - N_A : A \in S,\; N_A \in \mathcal{N}\}$$
forms a base for a topology on $X$ with respect to which:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The class of nowhere dense sets coincides with $\mathcal{N}$,
\item The class of sets having the property of Baire coincides with $S$,
\item The regular open sets are exactly the sets of the form $\phi(A)$ for $A \in S$,
\item $\mu$ is a category measure in $X$.
\end{enumerate}
