If $X$ is a topological space with a finite measure $\mu$ defined on the $\sigma$-algebra $S$ of sets having the property of Baire, and if $\mu(E) = 0$ if and only if $E$ is of first category, then $(X, S, \mu)$ is called a \textbf{category measure space} and $\mu$ is called a \textbf{category measure} in $X$. In any such space the extended principle of duality is not only valid, it is a tautology.
