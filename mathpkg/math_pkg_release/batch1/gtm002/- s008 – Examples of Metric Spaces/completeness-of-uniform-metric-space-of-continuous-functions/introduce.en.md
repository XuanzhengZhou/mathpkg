The space $C[a, b]$ of continuous real-valued functions on a closed interval, equipped with the uniform metric (or supremum metric), is a complete metric space. This means that every uniformly Cauchy sequence of continuous functions converges uniformly to a continuous limit. This property is fundamental in analysis and justifies the use of $C[a, b]$ as a Banach space under the supremum norm.
