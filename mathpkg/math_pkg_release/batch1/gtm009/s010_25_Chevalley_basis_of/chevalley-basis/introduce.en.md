The Chevalley Basis Theorem asserts that every semisimple Lie algebra over an algebraically closed field of characteristic zero possesses a basis whose structure constants are integers. This basis consists of root vectors $x_\alpha$ (one for each root $\alpha \in \Phi$) and Cartan elements $h_i$ (one for each simple root). The integrality of the structure constants is the crucial property: it allows one to define a $\mathbb{Z}$-form $L(\mathbb{Z})$ of $L$, and then reduce modulo a prime $p$ to obtain Lie algebras $L(K)$ over fields of positive characteristic. These Chevalley algebras in turn give rise to Chevalley groups, which include many of the finite simple groups.
