This proposition establishes three fundamental properties of $\alpha$-strings through a root $\beta$ in a root system $\Phi$. Part (a) relates the Cartan integer $\langle \beta, \alpha \rangle$ to the string parameters $r$ and $q$. Part (b) constrains the possible root lengths within any $\alpha$-string. Part (c) provides a formula linking $r$, $q$, and the root lengths when $\alpha+\beta$ is also a root. These facts depend only on the root system $\Phi$ and not on the underlying Lie algebra $L$, and they are essential for establishing the integrality of structure constants in a Chevalley basis.
