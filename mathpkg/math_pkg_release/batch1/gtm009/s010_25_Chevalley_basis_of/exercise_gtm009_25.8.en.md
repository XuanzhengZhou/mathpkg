Let $L = \mathfrak{sl}(\ell+1, F)$, and let $K$ be any field of characteristic $p$. If $p \nmid (\ell+1)$, then $L(K)$ is simple. If $p = 2$, $\ell = 1$, then $L(K)$ is solvable. If $\ell > 1$ and $p \mid (\ell+1)$, then $\operatorname{Rad} L(K) = Z(L(K))$ consists of the scalar matrices.
