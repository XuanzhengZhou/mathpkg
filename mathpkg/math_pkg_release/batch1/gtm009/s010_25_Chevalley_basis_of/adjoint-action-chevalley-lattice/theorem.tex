Let $\alpha, \beta \in \Phi$. For each nonnegative integer $m$, and with $r$ the largest integer such that $\beta - r\alpha \in \Phi$,
$$\frac{(\operatorname{ad} x_{\alpha})^{m}}{m!}(x_{\beta}) = \pm \binom{r+m}{m} x_{\beta+m\alpha}$$
if $\beta + m\alpha \in \Phi$, and equals $0$ otherwise. In particular, $\frac{(\operatorname{ad} x_{\alpha})^{m}}{m!}$ leaves the $\mathbb{Z}$-span $L(\mathbb{Z})$ of the Chevalley basis invariant, so that $L(\mathbb{Z})$ is stable under the endomorphisms $\frac{(\operatorname{ad} x_{\alpha})^{m}}{m!}$ and hence under $\exp(\operatorname{ad} x_{\alpha}) = \sum_{m \geq 0} \frac{(\operatorname{ad} x_{\alpha})^{m}}{m!}$.
