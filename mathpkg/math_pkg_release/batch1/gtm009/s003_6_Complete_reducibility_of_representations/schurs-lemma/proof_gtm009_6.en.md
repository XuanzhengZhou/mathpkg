The text states this is "well known Schur's Lemma" and does not reproduce the proof. The standard proof: Let $T$ be an endomorphism of $V$ commuting with all $\phi(x)$. Since $\mathbb{F}$ is algebraically closed (or by considering the field extension), $T$ has an eigenvalue $\lambda$. Then $\ker(T - \lambda I)$ is a nonzero $L$-invariant subspace of $V$, hence equals $V$ by irreducibility. Thus $T = \lambda I$.
