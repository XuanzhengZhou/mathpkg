An irreducible $L$-module has no nontrivial proper submodules, analogous to an irreducible representation. A completely reducible $L$-module decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible submodules, which is the key property guaranteed by Weyl's Theorem for representations of semisimple Lie algebras in characteristic zero.
