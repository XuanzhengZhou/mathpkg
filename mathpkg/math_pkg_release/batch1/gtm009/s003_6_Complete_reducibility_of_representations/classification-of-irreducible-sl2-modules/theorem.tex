Let $V$ be an irreducible module for $L = \mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$, with $\dim V = m+1$.

(a) Relative to $h$, $V$ is the direct sum of weight spaces $V_\mu$ for $\mu = m, m-2, \ldots, -(m-2), -m$, each of dimension $1$.

(b) $V$ has (up to nonzero scalar multiples) a unique maximal vector, whose weight (called the highest weight of $V$) is $m$.

(c) The action of $L$ on $V$ is given explicitly by formulas (a)-(c) of Lemma 7.2, when the basis is chosen in the prescribed fashion. In particular, there exists at most one irreducible $L$-module (up to isomorphism) of each possible dimension $m+1$, $m \geq 0$.
