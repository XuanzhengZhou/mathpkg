This theorem completely classifies all finite-dimensional irreducible representations of $\mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$. For each nonnegative integer $m$, there is exactly one irreducible module of dimension $m+1$, with highest weight $m$. The module's structure is determined by the $\mathfrak{sl}(2)$-triple acting via raising, lowering, and weight operators on a chain of basis vectors.
