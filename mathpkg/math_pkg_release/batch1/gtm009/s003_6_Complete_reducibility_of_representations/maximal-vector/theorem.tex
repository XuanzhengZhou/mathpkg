Let $V$ be a finite dimensional $L$-module for $L = \mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$. Since $\dim V < \infty$ and the weight space decomposition $V = \bigoplus V_\lambda$ is direct, there exists $V_\lambda \neq 0$ such that $V_{\lambda+2} = 0$. Then $x.v = 0$ for any $v \in V_\lambda$.

A nonzero vector $v \in V_\lambda$ such that $x.v = 0$ is called a maximal vector of weight $\lambda$.
