The Casimir element is a distinguished endomorphism constructed from a representation of a semisimple Lie algebra using the trace form. It commutes with the Lie algebra action and has trace equal to the dimension of the algebra. The Casimir element is the crucial technical tool in the proof of Weyl's complete reducibility theorem, as it provides a way to split off invariant complements via its kernel.
