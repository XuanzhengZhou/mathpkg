If $V$ and $W$ are two $L$-modules, then $L$ acts naturally on the space $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W)$ of linear maps by the rule:

$$(x.f)(v) = x.f(v) - f(x.v), \quad x \in L, f \in \operatorname{Hom}(V, W), v \in V.$$

In particular, if $V$ (hence $V^*$) is an $L$-module, then $\operatorname{End} V$ becomes an $L$-module via the isomorphism $V^* \otimes V \cong \operatorname{End} V$, with the same action.
