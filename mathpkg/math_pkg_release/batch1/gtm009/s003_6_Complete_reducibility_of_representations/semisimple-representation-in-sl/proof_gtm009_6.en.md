Since $L$ is semisimple, $L = [L, L]$ (Lemma 5.2). The derived algebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is $\mathfrak{sl}(V)$, because $\operatorname{Tr}(AB - BA) = 0$ for all $A, B \in \mathfrak{gl}(V)$. Therefore $\phi(L) = \phi([L, L]) = [\phi(L), \phi(L)] \subset [\mathfrak{gl}(V), \mathfrak{gl}(V)] = \mathfrak{sl}(V)$.

For the second statement: if $V$ is one-dimensional, then $\mathfrak{gl}(V) \cong \mathbb{F}$ and $\mathfrak{sl}(V) = 0$, so $\phi(L) \subset \mathfrak{sl}(V) = 0$, meaning $L$ acts trivially.
