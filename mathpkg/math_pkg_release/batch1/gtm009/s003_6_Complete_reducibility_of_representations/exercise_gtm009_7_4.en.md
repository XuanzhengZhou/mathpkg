The irreducible representation of $L = \mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$ of highest weight $m$ can also be realized "naturally", as follows. Let $X, Y$ be a basis for the two dimensional vector space $\mathbb{F}^2$, on which $L$ acts as usual. Let $\mathcal{R} = \mathbb{F}[X, Y]$ be the polynomial algebra in two variables, and extend the action of $L$ to $\mathcal{R}$ by the derivation rule: $z.fg = (z.f)g + f(z.g)$, for $z \in L$, $f, g \in \mathcal{R}$. Show that this extension is well defined and that $\mathcal{R}$ becomes an $L$-module. Then show that the subspace of homogeneous polynomials of degree $m$, with basis $X^m, X^{m-1}Y, \ldots, Y^m$, is invariant under $L$ and is an irreducible $L$-module of highest weight $m$.
