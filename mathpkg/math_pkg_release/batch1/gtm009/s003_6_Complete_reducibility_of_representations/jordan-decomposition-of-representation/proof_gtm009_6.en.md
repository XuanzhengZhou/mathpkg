This follows directly from Theorem 6.4. The theorem establishes that $\phi(L)$ contains the semisimple and nilpotent parts of each of its elements, and more strongly that $\phi(x)_s = \phi(x_s)$ and $\phi(x)_n = \phi(x_n)$. Therefore the abstract Jordan decomposition $x = x_s + x_n$ in $L$ maps to the usual Jordan decomposition $\phi(x) = \phi(x_s) + \phi(x_n) = \phi(x)_s + \phi(x)_n$ in $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$.
