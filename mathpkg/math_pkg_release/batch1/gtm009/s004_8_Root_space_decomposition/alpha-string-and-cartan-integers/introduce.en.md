This proposition establishes the fundamental rationality and integrality properties of the root system. Every root space is one-dimensional, and for each root $\alpha$ there is a natural $\mathfrak{sl}(2)$-subalgebra $S_\alpha$ spanned by $x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$, $y_\alpha \in L_{-\alpha}$, and $h_\alpha \in H$. Using the representation theory of $\mathfrak{sl}(2)$, one proves that the $\alpha$-string through $\beta$ is an unbroken chain $\beta - r\alpha, \ldots, \beta + q\alpha$ with $r - q = \beta(h_\alpha)$. All Cartan integers $\langle\beta, \alpha\rangle = 2(\beta, \alpha)/(\alpha, \alpha)$ are integers, which is the key property used in the axiomatic definition of a root system.
