We know from Theorem 5.1 (Cartan's criterion) that $\kappa$ is nondegenerate on $L$. By Proposition 8.1, $L_0 = C_L(H)$ is orthogonal to all $L_\alpha$ for $\alpha \in \Phi$ (since $\alpha + 0 = \alpha \neq 0$ for $\alpha \neq 0$). Suppose $z \in L_0$ is orthogonal to $L_0$ as well. Then $\kappa(z, L_0) = 0$ and $\kappa(z, L_\alpha) = 0$ for all $\alpha \in \Phi$. Since $L = L_0 \oplus \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Phi} L_\alpha$, this yields $\kappa(z, L) = 0$. By nondegeneracy of $\kappa$, we must have $z = 0$. Hence the restriction of $\kappa$ to $L_0$ is nondegenerate.
