A weight of an $L$-module $V$ is a linear functional on the Cartan subalgebra $H$ for which the corresponding simultaneous eigenspace is non-trivial. This concept provides the organizing principle for the structure theory of finite-dimensional representations of semisimple Lie algebras. Every finite-dimensional irreducible module is determined up to isomorphism by its highest weight, making weights the central combinatorial data of the representation theory.
