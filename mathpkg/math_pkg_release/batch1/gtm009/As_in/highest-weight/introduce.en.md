The highest weight of a standard cyclic module is the weight of its generating maximal vector. The terminology is justified by Theorem 20.2(b): all other weights of the module are strictly lower in the partial order defined by positive roots. For finite-dimensional irreducible modules, the highest weight must be a dominant integral linear functional, and this correspondence yields the celebrated classification theorem linking irreducible representations to dominant integral weights.
