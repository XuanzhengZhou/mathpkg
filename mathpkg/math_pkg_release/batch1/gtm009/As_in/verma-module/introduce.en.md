The Verma module $Z(\lambda)$ is the universal standard cyclic module of highest weight $\lambda$, constructed by inducing the one-dimensional $B$-module $D_\lambda$ up to $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. As a $\mathfrak{U}(N^-)$-module (where $N^-$ is spanned by negative root spaces), it is isomorphic to $\mathfrak{U}(N^-)$ itself, so its structure is completely determined by the PBW theorem. Every standard cyclic module of weight $\lambda$ is a quotient of $Z(\lambda)$, making Verma modules the fundamental objects in the study of highest weight representations.
