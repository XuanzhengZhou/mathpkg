Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra, $H$ a Cartan subalgebra, $\Delta$ a base, $B = B(\Delta) = H \oplus \bigoplus_{\alpha \succ 0} L_\alpha$ the Borel subalgebra. For $\lambda \in H^*$, let $D_\lambda$ be the one-dimensional $B$-module with basis $v^+$ and action
$$(h + \sum_{\alpha \succ 0} x_\alpha) \cdot v^+ = \lambda(h) v^+ \quad (h \in H,\; x_\alpha \in L_\alpha).$$
The \text{Verma module} (or standard cyclic module induced from $B$) is
$$Z(\lambda) = \mathfrak{U}(L) \otimes_{\mathfrak{U}(B)} D_\lambda,$$
made into a (left) $\mathfrak{U}(L)$-module via the natural left action of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$.
