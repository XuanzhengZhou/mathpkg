For a semisimple Lie algebra $L$ with Cartan subalgebra $H$, the weight space decomposition of an $L$-module $V$ generalizes the root space decomposition of the adjoint representation. By Theorem 6.4, when $V$ is finite-dimensional, $H$ acts diagonally and $V$ is the direct sum of its weight spaces. In the adjoint representation, the nonzero weights are precisely the roots of $L$, each with a 1-dimensional weight space $L_\alpha$, while weight $0$ has the $\ell$-dimensional weight space $H$.
