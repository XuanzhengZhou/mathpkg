A maximal vector (also called a highest weight vector) is a nonzero weight vector annihilated by all positive root spaces. Its existence in finite-dimensional modules follows from Lie's Theorem applied to the Borel subalgebra. In the adjoint representation of a simple Lie algebra, any nonzero element in the root space of the maximal root is a maximal vector. Every finite-dimensional irreducible module is generated by a unique (up to scalar) maximal vector, making this concept the key to the classification of representations.
