**Proof.** Suppose $w^+$ is another maximal vector in $V$, of weight $\lambda'$. Since $V$ is irreducible, $\mathfrak{U}(L) \cdot w^+ = V$, so the Structure Theorem applies equally to $w^+$. By part (b) of the theorem, applied first to $v^+$ and then to $w^+$, we have $\lambda' \preceq \lambda$ and $\lambda \preceq \lambda'$. Hence $\lambda = \lambda'$. But then $w^+ \in V_\lambda$, and by part (c) we have $\dim V_\lambda = 1$, so $w^+$ is a nonzero scalar multiple of $v^+$. $\square$
