This corollary of the Structure Theorem for standard cyclic modules establishes that an irreducible $L$-module has essentially only one maximal vector: any two maximal vectors are scalar multiples of each other. This uniqueness is crucial for the classification of irreducible finite-dimensional representations by their highest weights: the correspondence between irreducible modules and dominant integral weights is well-defined only because the highest weight is uniquely determined by the module.
