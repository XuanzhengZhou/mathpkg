A standard cyclic module is an $L$-module generated by a single maximal vector via the action of the universal enveloping algebra. This class of modules, though larger than the class of irreducible modules, serves as the primary tool for studying irreducible representations: every finite-dimensional irreducible $L$-module is the unique irreducible quotient of a standard cyclic module. The weight of the generating maximal vector is called the highest weight of the module.
