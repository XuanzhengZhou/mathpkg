This theorem gives the complete structural description of a standard cyclic module generated by a maximal vector of weight $\lambda$. Part (a) provides an explicit spanning set using the PBW basis. Part (b) justifies the term "highest weight". The finite-dimensionality of weight spaces (c) and the submodule decomposition (d) lead to the critical conclusion (e): standard cyclic modules are indecomposable and possess a unique irreducible quotient. Every finite-dimensional irreducible representation of a semisimple Lie algebra arises as the unique irreducible quotient of a standard cyclic module.
