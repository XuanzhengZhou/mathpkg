Let $V = \mathfrak{U}(L)\cdot v^+$ be a standard cyclic $L$-module with maximal vector $v^+$ of weight $\lambda$, and let $\Phi^+$ be the set of positive roots. Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $V$ is spanned by vectors of the form $y_{\beta_1}^{i_1} \cdots y_{\beta_m}^{i_m} \cdot v^+$, where $\beta_1, \ldots, \beta_m \in \Phi^+$ and $i_j \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$, where $y_\beta \in L_{-\beta}$.
\item[(b)] Every weight $\mu$ of $V$ satisfies $\mu \preceq \lambda$ (i.e., $\lambda - \mu$ is a sum of positive roots).
\item[(c)] For each weight $\mu$ of $V$, $\dim V_\mu < \infty$, and $\dim V_\lambda = 1$.
\item[(d)] Each nonzero submodule of $V$ is the direct sum of its weight spaces (each of which is the intersection of the submodule with $V_\mu$).
\item[(e)] $V$ is indecomposable, possesses a unique maximal submodule, and hence a unique irreducible quotient.
\item[(f)] Every nonzero homomorphic image of $V$ is also standard cyclic of weight $\lambda$.
\end{itemize}
