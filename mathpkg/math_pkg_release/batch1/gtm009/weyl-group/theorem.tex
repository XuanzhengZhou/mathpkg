The \text{Weyl group} $W$ of a root system $\Phi$ is the subgroup of the orthogonal group generated by the reflections $\sigma_\alpha$ for $\alpha \in \Phi$, where
$$\sigma_\alpha(\beta) = \beta - \frac{2(\beta, \alpha)}{(\alpha, \alpha)}\alpha.$$
$W$ is a finite group. It acts simply transitively on the bases (simple systems) of $\Phi$, and on the set of Weyl chambers.
