This follows from the theorem (Theorem 21.2), in view of Theorem 21.1 and Theorems A, B of (20.3).

Theorem 21.1 shows that if $V$ is a finite dimensional irreducible $L$-module, its highest weight $\lambda$ must lie in $\Lambda^+$, and then $V \cong V(\lambda)$. Theorem 21.2 shows that for every $\lambda \in \Lambda^+$, the module $V(\lambda)$ is indeed finite dimensional. Theorems A and B of (20.3) guarantee that an irreducible $L$-module is determined up to isomorphism by its highest weight. Therefore $\lambda \mapsto V(\lambda)$ is both injective and surjective onto the set of isomorphism classes of finite dimensional irreducible $L$-modules.
