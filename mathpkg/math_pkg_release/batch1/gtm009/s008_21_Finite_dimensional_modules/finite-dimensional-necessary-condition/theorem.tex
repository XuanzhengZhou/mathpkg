Let $V$ be a finite dimensional irreducible $L$-module, with maximal vector of uniquely determined weight $\lambda$. For each simple root $\alpha_i$, let $S_i = S_{\alpha_i}$ be the corresponding copy of $\mathfrak{sl}(2, F)$ in $L$, with CSA $H_i \subset S_i$. Then the weight for $H_i$ is completely determined by the scalar $\lambda(h_i)$, where $h_i = h_{\alpha_i}$. By Theorem 7.2, this forces $\lambda(h_i)$ to be a nonnegative integer for each simple root $\alpha_i$. Equivalently, $\lambda$ must be dominant integral.
