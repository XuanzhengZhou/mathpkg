These three commutator identities express the bracket relations between the Chevalley generators $x_i, y_i, h_i$ and powers of the negative root vectors $y_i$ inside the universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. They are essential for the proof that $y_i^{m_i+1}$ annihilates the maximal vector of $V(\lambda)$ when $\lambda$ is dominant integral, which is a crucial step in establishing the finite dimensionality of $V(\lambda)$ in Theorem 21.2.
