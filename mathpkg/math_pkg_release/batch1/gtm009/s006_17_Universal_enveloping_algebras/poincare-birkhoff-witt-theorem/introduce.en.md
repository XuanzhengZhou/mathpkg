The Poincaré–Birkhoff–Witt (PBW) Theorem is the fundamental structural result for universal enveloping algebras. It states that the associated graded algebra $\mathfrak{G}$ of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ (with respect to the natural filtration by tensor degree) is canonically isomorphic to the symmetric algebra $\mathfrak{S}(L)$. This implies that $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ is a filtered deformation of the polynomial ring and that $\dim \mathfrak{U}(L) = \dim \mathfrak{S}(L)$ as filtered vector spaces. The PBW theorem also guarantees the injectivity of the canonical map $L \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$.
