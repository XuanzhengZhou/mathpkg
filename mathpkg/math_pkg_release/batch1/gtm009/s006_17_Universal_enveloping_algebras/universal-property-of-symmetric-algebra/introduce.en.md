The symmetric algebra satisfies a universal property analogous to that of the tensor algebra but for commutative targets: any linear map from $V$ into a commutative associative algebra extends uniquely to an algebra homomorphism from $\mathfrak{S}(V)$. This makes $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ the "free commutative algebra" on $V$.
