$\mathfrak{S}(V)$ is universal for linear maps of $V$ into commutative associative $F$-algebras with $1$: given any linear map $\phi: V \to A$, where $A$ is a commutative associative $F$-algebra with $1$, there exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\psi: \mathfrak{S}(V) \to A$ sending $1$ to $1$ and extending $\phi$.
