Lemma A constructs the family of linear maps $f_m: L \otimes S_m \to \mathfrak{S}$ that form the technical backbone of the PBW theorem proof. The three conditions $(A_m)$, $(B_m)$, and $(C_m)$ capture, respectively: the action on basis elements with index not less than the existing indices, a filtration-preserving property, and compatibility with the Lie bracket. The proof proceeds by induction on $m$, using the ordered basis to define $f_m$ on a spanning set and verifying the conditions.
