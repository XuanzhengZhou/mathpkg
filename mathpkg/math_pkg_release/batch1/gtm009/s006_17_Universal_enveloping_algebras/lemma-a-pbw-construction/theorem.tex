Fix an ordered basis $(x_\lambda; \lambda \in \Omega)$ of $L$, identifying $\mathfrak{S}$ with the polynomial algebra in indeterminates $z_\lambda$. For each $m \in \mathbb{Z}^+$, there exists a unique linear map $f_m: L \otimes S_m \to \mathfrak{S}$ satisfying:
\begin{align}
(A_m)\quad & f_m(x_\lambda \otimes z_\Sigma) = z_\lambda z_\Sigma \quad \text{for } \lambda \leq \Sigma,\; z_\Sigma \in S_m, \\
(B_m)\quad & f_m(x_\lambda \otimes z_\Sigma) - z_\lambda z_\Sigma \in S_k \quad \text{for } k \leq m,\; z_\Sigma \in S_k, \\
(C_m)\quad & f_m(x_\lambda \otimes f_m(x_\mu \otimes z_T)) = f_m(x_\mu \otimes f_m(x_\lambda \otimes z_T)) + f_m([x_\lambda x_\mu] \otimes z_T), \quad z_T \in S_{m-1}.
\end{align}
