The symmetric algebra $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ is the quotient of the tensor algebra by the ideal generated by commutators $x \otimes y - y \otimes x$. Factoring out this ideal forces elements of $V$ to commute, making $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ the free commutative associative algebra on $V$. In the context of the PBW theorem, the symmetric algebra serves as the domain of the canonical isomorphism with the associated graded algebra of the universal enveloping algebra.
