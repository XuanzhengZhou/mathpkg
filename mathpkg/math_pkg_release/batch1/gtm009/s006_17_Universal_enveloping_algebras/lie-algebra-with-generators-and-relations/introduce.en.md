Just as groups can be presented by generators and relations, Lie algebras admit presentations. Starting from the free Lie algebra on a generating set $X$, one factors out the ideal generated by prescribed relations $f_j = 0$. The resulting quotient is the most general Lie algebra satisfying those relations. This construction is the foundation of Serre's theorem (Chapter VI), which gives a presentation of any semisimple Lie algebra purely in terms of its root system.
