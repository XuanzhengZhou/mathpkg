If $(\mathfrak{U}, i)$ and $(\mathfrak{U}', i')$ are universal enveloping algebras of $L$, then there exists a unique isomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{U}'$ such that $\phi \circ i = i'$ (and consequently $\phi^{-1} \circ i' = i$). In particular, a universal enveloping algebra is unique up to a unique isomorphism.
