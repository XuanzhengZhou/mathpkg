The universal property of the universal enveloping algebra implies its uniqueness up to unique isomorphism. This is a standard categorical argument: each universal enveloping algebra maps uniquely into the other, and the compositions must be the identity by the uniqueness clause of the universal property. Thus any two universal enveloping algebras of $L$ are canonically identified.
