A free Lie algebra on a set $X$ is the Lie-algebraic analogue of a free group: it is the "most general" Lie algebra generated by $X$, with no relations other than those forced by the Lie algebra axioms (bilinearity, alternativity, Jacobi identity). It is constructed as the Lie subalgebra generated by $X$ inside the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ (with commutator bracket). Free Lie algebras play a crucial role in Chapter VI, where Serre's theorem presents a semisimple Lie algebra by generators and relations.
