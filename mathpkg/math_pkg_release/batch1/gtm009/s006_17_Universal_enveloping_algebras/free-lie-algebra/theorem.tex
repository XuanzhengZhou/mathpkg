A Lie algebra $L$ is said to be \text{free} on a set $X$ if, given any mapping $\phi$ of $X$ into a Lie algebra $M$, there exists a unique Lie algebra homomorphism $\psi: L \to M$ extending $\phi$.

Existence: Begin with a vector space $V$ having $X$ as a basis, form the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ (viewed as a Lie algebra via the commutator bracket $[a,b] = a \otimes b - b \otimes a$), and let $L$ be the Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ generated by $X$. Then $L$ is free on $X$.
