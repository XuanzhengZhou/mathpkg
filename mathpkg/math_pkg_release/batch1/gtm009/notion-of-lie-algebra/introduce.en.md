A Lie algebra is an algebraic structure that arises naturally as vector spaces of linear transformations endowed with the commutator bracket $[x,y] = xy - yx$. It generalizes the notion of the cross product in $\mathbf{R}^3$ and the commutator of matrices. The three defining axioms capture bilinearity of the bracket, the vanishing of the bracket on equal elements, and the Jacobi identity which serves as a substitute for associativity.
