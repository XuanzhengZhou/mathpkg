The Poincaré-Birkhoff-Witt (PBW) theorem is the fundamental structure theorem for universal enveloping algebras. It provides an explicit basis of ordered monomials, showing that $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ has the same "size" as the symmetric algebra of the underlying vector space. A key consequence is that the map $L \rightarrow \mathfrak{U}(L)$ is injective, so every Lie algebra is a subalgebra of the Lie algebra associated to an associative algebra.
