\textbf{Theorem (Cartan's Criterion).} Let $L$ be a subalgebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$, $V$ finite dimensional. Suppose that $\text{Tr}(xy) = 0$ for all $x \in [LL]$ and $y \in L$. Then $L$ is solvable.

\textbf{Corollary.} Let $L$ be a Lie algebra such that $\text{Tr}(\text{ad } x \circ \text{ad } y) = 0$ for all $x \in [LL]$, $y \in L$. Then $L$ is solvable.

\textbf{Lemma.} Let $A \subset B$ be two subspaces of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$, $\dim V < \infty$. Set $M = \{x \in \mathfrak{gl}(V) \mid [x, B] \subset A\}$. Suppose $x \in M$ satisfies $\text{Tr}(xy) = 0$ for all $y \in M$. Then $x$ is nilpotent.
