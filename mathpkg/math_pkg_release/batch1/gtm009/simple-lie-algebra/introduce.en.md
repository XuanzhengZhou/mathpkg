A simple Lie algebra is a nonabelian Lie algebra with no nontrivial ideals. Simple Lie algebras are the irreducible building blocks of semisimple Lie algebras, analogous to simple groups in group theory. The complete classification of finite-dimensional simple Lie algebras over algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero is one of the great achievements of 19th-20th century mathematics, yielding the classical types $A_\ell, B_\ell, C_\ell, D_\ell$ and the exceptional types $E_6, E_7, E_8, F_4, G_2$.
