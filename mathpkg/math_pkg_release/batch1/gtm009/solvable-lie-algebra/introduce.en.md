A Lie algebra is solvable if its derived series terminates at zero after finitely many steps. Solvability generalizes the notion for groups and is a measure of how far the algebra is from being abelian. The radical of a Lie algebra (its maximal solvable ideal) plays a central role in the structure theory, leading to the Levi decomposition into a semidirect product of a semisimple subalgebra and the radical.
