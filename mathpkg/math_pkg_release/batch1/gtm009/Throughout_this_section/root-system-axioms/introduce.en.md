A **root system** $\Phi$ in a euclidean space $E$ is defined by four axioms: finiteness (R1), reducedness (R2), reflection invariance (R3), and the crystallographic condition (R4) that Cartan integers are integers. These axioms abstract the properties of the root system of a semisimple Lie algebra, and their classification (types A-G) is one of the central achievements of Lie theory.
