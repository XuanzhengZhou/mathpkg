Let $\Delta$ be a base of a root system $\Phi$ with Weyl group $W$.

(a) $W$ acts transitively on Weyl chambers: if $\gamma \in E$ is regular, there exists $\sigma \in W$ such that $(\sigma(\gamma), \alpha) > 0$ for all $\alpha \in \Delta$.

(b) $W$ acts transitively on bases: if $\Delta'$ is another base of $\Phi$, then $\sigma(\Delta') = \Delta$ for some $\sigma \in W$.

(c) Every root is $W$-conjugate to a simple root: if $\alpha$ is any root, there exists $\sigma \in W$ such that $\sigma(\alpha) \in \Delta$.

(d) $W$ is generated by the simple reflections $\sigma_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Delta$).

(e) $W$ acts simply transitively on bases: if $\sigma(\Delta) = \Delta$ for $\sigma \in W$, then $\sigma = 1$.
