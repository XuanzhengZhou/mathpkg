Theorem 10.3 is one of the central structural results about Weyl groups. It establishes that the Weyl group acts simply transitively on bases (equivalently, on Weyl chambers), that every root is conjugate to a simple root, and that the simple reflections generate the entire Weyl group. This theorem underlies the entire combinatorial theory of root systems and their representations.
