Let $W'$ be the subgroup of $W$ generated by all $\sigma_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Delta$). The proof shows (a)-(c) for $W'$, then deduces $W' = W$.

(a) Write $\delta = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{\alpha > 0} \alpha$, choose $\sigma \in W'$ maximizing $(\sigma(\gamma), \delta)$. If $\sigma(\gamma)$ were not dominant, some simple $\alpha$ would have $(\sigma(\gamma), \alpha) < 0$, contradicting maximality via $\sigma_\alpha \sigma$.

(b) Given base $\Delta'$, pick regular $\gamma$ with $\Phi^+(\gamma) = \Phi^+(\Delta')$, then $\Delta' = \Delta(\gamma)$. By (a), $\Delta' = \sigma^{-1}(\Delta)$ for appropriate $\sigma$.

(c) $\alpha$ is $W$-conjugate to a simple root since all bases are $W$-conjugate and $\alpha$ belongs to some base.

(d) $W'$ already satisfies (a)-(c) for the whole root system, so $W'$ contains all reflections, hence $W' = W$.

(e) If $\sigma(\Delta) = \Delta$, then $\sigma$ preserves the fundamental chamber, forcing $\sigma = 1$ by Lemma B.
