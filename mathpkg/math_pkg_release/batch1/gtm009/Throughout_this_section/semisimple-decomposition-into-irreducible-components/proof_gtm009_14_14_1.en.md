For each simple ideal $L_i$, let $H_i = H \cap L_i$. Since $H$ is maximal toral in $L$ and $L_i$ is an ideal, $H_i$ is a maximal toral subalgebra of $L_i$. For $\alpha \in \Phi$, the root space $L_\alpha$ lies in some $L_i$ (since $L = \bigoplus L_i$). Then $[H_i L_\alpha] \neq 0$ for some $i$ (otherwise $H$ would centralize $L_\alpha$), and $L_\alpha \subset L_i$, so $\alpha|_{H_i}$ is a root of $L_i$ relative to $H_i$. This yields the decomposition $\Phi = \Phi_1 \cup \ldots \cup \Phi_t$, which matches the decomposition into irreducible components (cf. Section 11.3).
