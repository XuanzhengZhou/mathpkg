Lemma 10.2A states that any positive non-simple root can be reduced by subtracting an appropriate simple root, with the result being another (positive) root. This is a crucial technical lemma used throughout the theory of root systems to build roots from simple ones and to study the action of the Weyl group. The proof uses Lemma 9.4 and the fact that $\alpha$ cannot be orthogonal to all simple roots.
