Let $\mu \in \Pi$. Then $\mu$ is $W$-conjugate to a unique dominant weight $\mu^+ \in \Pi$ (by saturation property). By definition of highest weight, $\mu^+ \prec \lambda$. By Lemma 13.3B:
$$(\mu + \delta, \mu + \delta) = (\mu^+ + \delta, \mu^+ + \delta).$$
And $(\mu^+ + \delta, \mu^+ + \delta) \leq (\lambda + \delta, \lambda + \delta)$, with equality only when $\mu^+ = \lambda$.
