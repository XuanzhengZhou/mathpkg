A **saturated set of weights** is a subset of the weight lattice closed under "subtracting roots" — if $\lambda$ is in the set and $\langle \lambda, \alpha \rangle > 0$, then $\lambda - \alpha, \lambda - 2\alpha, \ldots, \lambda - \langle \lambda, \alpha \rangle \alpha$ are all in the set. Saturated sets are $W$-invariant and correspond to the weights occurring in finite-dimensional irreducible representations. A saturated set with highest weight $\lambda$ consists of all dominant weights $\mu \prec \lambda$ together with their $W$-conjugates.
