This fundamental theorem guarantees the existence of a base for any root system. The constructive proof defines $\Delta(\gamma)$ as the set of indecomposable roots in $\Phi^+(\gamma)$ for a regular vector $\gamma$, and shows this is always a base. This establishes a natural bijection between Weyl chambers and bases of the root system.
