Let $\alpha \in \Phi$, and choose $x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$, $y_\alpha \in L_{-\alpha}$ such that $\kappa(x_\alpha, y_\alpha) \neq 0$. Then:
(a) $[L_\alpha, L_{-\alpha}]$ is one-dimensional, with basis $t_\alpha$.
(b) $\alpha(t_\alpha) = \kappa(t_\alpha, t_\alpha) \neq 0$.
(c) If $0 \neq x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$, there exists $y_\alpha \in L_{-\alpha}$ such that $x_\alpha, y_\alpha, h_\alpha = [x_\alpha, y_\alpha]$ span a three-dimensional subalgebra isomorphic to $\mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$.
(d) $h_\alpha = 2t_\alpha/\kappa(t_\alpha, t_\alpha)$.
(e) $\dim L_\alpha = 1$.
