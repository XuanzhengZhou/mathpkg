Let $\beta \in \Phi^+ - \{\alpha\}$, and write $\beta = \sum_{\gamma \in \Delta} k_\gamma \gamma$ with all $k_\gamma \geq 0$. Since $\beta \neq \alpha$, some $k_\gamma > 0$ for $\gamma \neq \alpha$. Then:
$$\sigma_\alpha(\beta) = \beta - \langle \beta, \alpha \rangle \alpha = \sum_{\gamma \in \Delta} k_\gamma \gamma - \langle \beta, \alpha \rangle \alpha.$$
The coefficient of $\gamma \neq \alpha$ in this expression is still $k_\gamma > 0$, so the uniqueness of expression in (B2) forces all coefficients of $\sigma_\alpha(\beta)$ to be nonnegative. Hence $\sigma_\alpha(\beta) > 0$.
