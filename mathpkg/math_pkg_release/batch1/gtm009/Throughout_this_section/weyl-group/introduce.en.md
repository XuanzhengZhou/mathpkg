The **Weyl group** $W$ of a root system $\Phi$ is the finite group generated by the reflections $\sigma_\alpha$ for all roots $\alpha \in \Phi$. Since $W$ permutes the finite set $\Phi$, it can be identified with a subgroup of the symmetric group on $\Phi$, hence is finite. The Weyl group plays a central role in the structure theory of semisimple Lie algebras, governing symmetries of the root system and of weight spaces.
