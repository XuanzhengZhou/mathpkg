The Chevalley involution is an order-2 automorphism of a semisimple Lie algebra that negates a Cartan subalgebra and interchanges positive and negative root vectors (up to sign). It is fundamental for the construction of Chevalley groups and integral forms of Lie algebras. For $\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{F})$, this is the familiar automorphism $X \mapsto -Y$, $Y \mapsto -X$, $H \mapsto -H$.
