The map $\alpha \mapsto -\alpha$ is an automorphism of $\Phi$. On $H$, define $\sigma(h) = -h$. This sends $h_\alpha$ to $-h_\alpha = h_{-\alpha}$ (by Proposition 8.3(g)). To apply Theorem 14.2, decree that $x_\alpha$ is sent to $-y_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Delta$). The unique $z \in L_\alpha$ such that $[-y_\alpha z] = h_{-\alpha}$ is $-x_\alpha$, so the theorem extends $\sigma$ to an automorphism of $L$ with $\sigma(x_\alpha) = -y_\alpha$, $\sigma(y_\alpha) = -x_\alpha$. Moreover, $\sigma$ has order 2 because $\sigma^2$ fixes a set of generators of $L$.
