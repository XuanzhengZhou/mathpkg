This useful criterion states that for **nonproportional roots**, the sign of the inner product determines which linear combination is again a root. If the angle is strictly acute ($(\alpha,\beta) > 0$), then $\alpha - \beta \in \Phi$; if strictly obtuse ($(\alpha,\beta) < 0$), then $\alpha + \beta \in \Phi$. This follows from the fact that one of the Cartan integers $\langle\alpha,\beta\rangle$, $\langle\beta,\alpha\rangle$ must equal 1 in these cases, as can be read off from the table of possible angles between roots.
