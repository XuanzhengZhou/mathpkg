Define $\phi: E \to E'$ by $\phi(\alpha_i) = \alpha'_i$ and extend linearly. Since $\langle \alpha_i, \alpha_j \rangle = \langle \alpha'_i, \alpha'_j \rangle$, the formula for a reflection shows that:
$$\phi \circ \sigma_{\alpha_i} = \sigma_{\phi(\alpha_i)} \circ \phi$$
for each simple root $\alpha_i \in \Delta$. The Weyl groups $W, W'$ are generated by simple reflections, so $\sigma \mapsto \phi \circ \sigma \circ \phi^{-1}$ is an isomorphism $W \to W'$. Every $\beta \in \Phi$ is $W$-conjugate to a simple root: $\beta = \sigma(\alpha_i)$. Then $\phi(\beta) = (\phi \circ \sigma \circ \phi^{-1})(\phi(\alpha_i)) \in \Phi'$. Moreover, $\phi$ preserves all Cartan integers since the reflection formula is preserved.
