Proceed by induction on $\ell(\sigma)$. The case $\ell(\sigma) = 0$ is clear ($\sigma = 1$, $n(\sigma) = 0$). Assume true for all $\tau$ with $\ell(\tau) < \ell(\sigma)$. Write $\sigma$ in reduced form: $\sigma = \sigma_{\alpha_1} \ldots \sigma_{\alpha_t}$, and set $\alpha = \alpha_t$. By Lemma 10.2C Corollary, $\sigma(\alpha) < 0$. Lemma 10.2B implies $n(\sigma \sigma_\alpha) = n(\sigma) - 1$. On the other hand, $\ell(\sigma \sigma_\alpha) = \ell(\sigma) - 1 < \ell(\sigma)$, so by induction $\ell(\sigma \sigma_\alpha) = n(\sigma \sigma_\alpha)$. Combining: $\ell(\sigma) = n(\sigma)$.
