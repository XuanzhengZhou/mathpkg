This theorem establishes the fundamental properties of the **Weyl group action**: $\mathcal{W}$ acts simply transitively on the set of bases (equivalently, Weyl chambers), is generated by simple reflections, and every root is conjugate under $\mathcal{W}$ to some simple root. This provides the group-theoretic foundation for the classification of root systems.
