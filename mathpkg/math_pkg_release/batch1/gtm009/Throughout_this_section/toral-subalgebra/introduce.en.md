A **toral subalgebra** of a semisimple Lie algebra $L$ is a subalgebra consisting entirely of semisimple (diagonalizable) elements with respect to the adjoint representation. Since a semisimple $L$ cannot consist entirely of ad-nilpotent elements (by Engel's Theorem), nonzero toral subalgebras always exist. Toral subalgebras are the starting point for the root space decomposition of $L$ and play a role analogous to Cartan subalgebras.
