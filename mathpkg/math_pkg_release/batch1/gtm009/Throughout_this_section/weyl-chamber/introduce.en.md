The hyperplanes $P_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Phi$) partition the euclidean space $E$ into finitely many regions called **Weyl chambers**. Each regular vector belongs to a unique chamber. There is a natural bijection between Weyl chambers and bases: $\mathfrak{C}(\Delta)$ is the open convex set where all inner products with simple roots are positive. The Weyl group acts simply transitively on the set of chambers.
