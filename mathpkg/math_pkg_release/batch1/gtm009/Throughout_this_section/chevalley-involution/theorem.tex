Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra as in the isomorphism theorem. Fix nonzero $x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Delta$) and let $y_\alpha \in L_{-\alpha}$ satisfy $[x_\alpha, y_\alpha] = h_\alpha$. There exists a unique automorphism $\sigma$ of $L$ of order 2 satisfying:
$$\sigma(x_\alpha) = -y_\alpha, \quad \sigma(y_\alpha) = -x_\alpha \quad (\alpha \in \Delta), \quad \sigma(h) = -h \quad (h \in H).$$
