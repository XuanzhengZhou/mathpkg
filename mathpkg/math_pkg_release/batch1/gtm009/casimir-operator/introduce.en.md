The Casimir operator is a distinguished element of the universal enveloping algebra associated to a faithful representation of a semisimple Lie algebra. It commutes with the action of the whole algebra and has nonzero trace, making it a powerful tool for decomposing representations. The Casimir operator is the key ingredient in the proof of Weyl's complete reducibility theorem.
