An automorphism of a Lie algebra is an invertible linear map preserving the bracket. The exponential of a nilpotent derivation yields an automorphism, and those arising from $\operatorname{ad} x$ are called inner automorphisms. The group of inner automorphisms $\operatorname{Int} L$ is a normal subgroup of the full automorphism group, playing a role analogous to inner automorphisms in group theory.
