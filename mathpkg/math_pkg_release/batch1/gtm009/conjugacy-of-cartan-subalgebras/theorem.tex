Let $L$ be a Lie algebra over an algebraically closed field of characteristic 0. Then any two Cartan subalgebras of $L$ are conjugate under the group $\mathcal{E}(L)$ generated by $\exp(\text{ad } x)$ for $x \in L$ strongly ad-nilpotent.

In particular, all Cartan subalgebras of a solvable Lie algebra are conjugate. For $L$ semisimple, this means that all maximal toral subalgebras are conjugate under $\text{Int } L$, and $L$ is uniquely determined (up to isomorphism) by its root system relative to any Cartan subalgebra.
