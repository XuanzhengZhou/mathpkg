An inner derivation of a Lie algebra $L$ is a derivation of the form $\operatorname{ad} x$, given by bracketing with a fixed element $x \in L$. The Jacobi identity guarantees that $\operatorname{ad} x$ satisfies the Leibniz rule. Inner derivations form an ideal in the Lie algebra of all derivations, and the quotient by them defines the first Lie algebra cohomology group.
