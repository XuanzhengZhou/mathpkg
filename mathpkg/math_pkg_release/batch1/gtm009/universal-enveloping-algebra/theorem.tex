Let $L$ be a Lie algebra over a field $F$. The \text{universal enveloping algebra} $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ is an associative algebra with unit, together with a linear map $i: L \rightarrow \mathfrak{U}(L)$ satisfying $i([xy]) = i(x)i(y) - i(y)i(x)$, such that for any associative algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ and any linear map $j: L \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$ with $j([xy]) = j(x)j(y) - j(y)j(x)$, there exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{U}(L) \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$ with $\phi \circ i = j$.

Concretely, $\mathfrak{U}(L) = \mathfrak{T}(L) / J$, where $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ is the tensor algebra of the vector space $L$ and $J$ is the two-sided ideal generated by all $x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy]$ for $x, y \in L$.
