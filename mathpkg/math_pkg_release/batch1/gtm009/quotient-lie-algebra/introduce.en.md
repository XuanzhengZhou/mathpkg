The quotient Lie algebra $L/I$ is constructed by factoring out an ideal $I$, analogous to quotient groups and quotient rings. The bracket is induced from that of $L$, and the well-definedness follows precisely because $I$ is an ideal. Quotient algebras are essential for the isomorphism theorems and for constructing new Lie algebras from old.
