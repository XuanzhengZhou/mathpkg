Weyl's Theorem is a cornerstone of Lie algebra representation theory. It states that every finite-dimensional representation of a semisimple Lie algebra over a field of characteristic zero decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible representations. The proof uses the Casimir operator, a canonical element of the universal enveloping algebra, to construct complementary submodules. The theorem fails in positive characteristic.
