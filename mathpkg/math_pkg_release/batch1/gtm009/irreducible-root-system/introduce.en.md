An irreducible root system is one that cannot be orthogonally decomposed. Irreducible root systems correspond one-to-one with simple Lie algebras. The classification of irreducible root systems yields the classical infinite families $A_\ell$ ($\ell \geq 1$), $B_\ell$ ($\ell \geq 2$), $C_\ell$ ($\ell \geq 3$), $D_\ell$ ($\ell \geq 4$) and the five exceptional types $G_2, F_4, E_6, E_7, E_8$.
