Let $L$ be a finite-dimensional Lie algebra over a field of characteristic 0. Let $R = \text{Rad } L$ be the radical (maximal solvable ideal) of $L$. Then there exists a semisimple subalgebra $S$ of $L$ (called a \text{Levi factor}) such that
$$L = S \oplus R$$
as vector spaces, with $[S, R] \subset R$ (a semidirect product of Lie algebras). Moreover, any two Levi factors are conjugate under $\text{Int } L$.
