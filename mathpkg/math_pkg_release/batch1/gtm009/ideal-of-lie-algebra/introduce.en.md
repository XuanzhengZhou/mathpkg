An ideal of a Lie algebra is a subspace closed under bracketing with arbitrary elements of the ambient algebra. Ideals are the natural kernels of Lie algebra homomorphisms and play a role analogous to normal subgroups in group theory. The construction of quotient algebras and the formulation of isomorphism theorems all depend on the notion of an ideal.
