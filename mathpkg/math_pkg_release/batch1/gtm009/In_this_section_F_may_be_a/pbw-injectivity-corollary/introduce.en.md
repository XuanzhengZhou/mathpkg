Corollary B of the PBW Theorem is one of its most important consequences: the natural map $i: L \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$ is injective. This justifies the identification of a Lie algebra $L$ with a Lie subalgebra of its universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ (equipped with the commutator bracket). The proof follows immediately from Corollary A by taking $W = T^1 L = L$, since $L$ maps isomorphically onto $S^1 L$ under the canonical projection.
