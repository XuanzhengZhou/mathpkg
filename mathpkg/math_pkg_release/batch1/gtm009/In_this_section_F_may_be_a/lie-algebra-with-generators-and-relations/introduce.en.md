A Lie algebra with generators and relations is obtained by quotienting a free Lie algebra by an ideal generated by the desired relations. This is the Lie algebra analogue of presenting a group by generators and relations. Given a free Lie algebra $L$ on a set $X$ and an ideal $R$ generated by elements $f_j$, the quotient $L/R$ consists of all formal Lie expressions in the generators $x_i$ (the images of elements of $X$) subject to the relations $f_j = 0$. This construction is used in Chapter VI (Serre's Theorem) to characterize semisimple Lie algebras entirely in terms of their root systems.
