Let $V$ be a finite dimensional vector space over a field $F$. Let $I$ be the two-sided ideal in $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ generated by all elements of the form $v \otimes w - w \otimes v$ ($v, w \in V$). Define the \textbf{symmetric algebra} on $V$ as
$$\mathfrak{S}(V) = \mathfrak{T}(V) / I.$$
The effect of factoring out $I$ is to make the elements of $V$ commute; thus $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ is universal for linear maps of $V$ into commutative associative $F$-algebras with $1$. If $(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is any fixed basis of $V$, then $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ is canonically isomorphic to the polynomial algebra over $F$ in $n$ variables, with basis consisting of $1$ and all $x_{i(1)} \cdots x_{i(t)}$, $t \geq 1$, $1 \leq i(1) \leq \cdots \leq i(t) \leq n$.

The construction extends to the case where $V$ is infinite dimensional.
