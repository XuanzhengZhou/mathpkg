The symmetric algebra $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ is the quotient of the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ by the two-sided ideal generated by commutators $v \otimes w - w \otimes v$. This forces the generators to commute, making $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ the free commutative associative algebra on $V$. When a basis is chosen, $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ is canonically isomorphic to the polynomial algebra in $\dim V$ variables. In the PBW Theorem, the symmetric algebra $\mathfrak{S}(L)$ is shown to be isomorphic to the associated graded algebra $\mathfrak{G}$ of the universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$.
