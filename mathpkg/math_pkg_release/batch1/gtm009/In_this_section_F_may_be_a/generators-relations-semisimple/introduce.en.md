A semisimple Lie algebra $L$ is generated by a standard set of generators $x_i, y_i, h_i$ ($1 \leq i \leq \ell$) associated to a base $\Delta$ of the root system. These generators satisfy the Serre relations: (S1)-(S3) encode the Cartan matrix and the $\mathfrak{sl}(2)$-triples; $(S_{ij}^+)$ and $(S_{ij}^-)$ are the Serre relations reflecting the finiteness of the root strings. The remarkable fact, proved by Serre's Theorem (18.3), is that these relations completely characterize the semisimple Lie algebra — any Lie algebra generated by elements satisfying these relations with a given Cartan matrix is semisimple with the prescribed root system.
