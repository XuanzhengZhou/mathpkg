For any set $X$, there exists a free Lie algebra on $X$, unique up to a unique isomorphism.

\textbf{Uniqueness.} If $L$ and $L'$ are both free on $X$, the universal property yields mutually inverse homomorphisms.

\textbf{Existence.} Let $V$ be a vector space with $X$ as basis, form the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ viewed as a Lie algebra via $[a, b] = a \otimes b - b \otimes a$, and let $L$ be the Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ generated by $X$. Given any map $\phi: X \to M$, extend $\phi$ to a linear map $V \to M \subset \mathfrak{U}(M)$, then canonically to an associative algebra homomorphism $\mathfrak{T}(V) \to \mathfrak{U}(M)$. This is also a Lie algebra homomorphism; its restriction to $L$ yields the required $\psi: L \to M$.
