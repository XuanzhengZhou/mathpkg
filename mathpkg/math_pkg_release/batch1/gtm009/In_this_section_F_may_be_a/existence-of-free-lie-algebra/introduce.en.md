The existence and uniqueness theorem for free Lie algebras guarantees that for any set $X$, there is a unique (up to unique isomorphism) Lie algebra freely generated by $X$. The uniqueness follows from the universal property by the standard argument. For existence, one constructs the free Lie algebra as the Lie subalgebra of the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ (equipped with the commutator bracket) generated by $X$. The universal property is verified by extending any map $\phi: X \to M$ to an associative algebra homomorphism $\mathfrak{T}(V) \to \mathfrak{U}(M)$ and restricting to the Lie subalgebra generated by $X$.
