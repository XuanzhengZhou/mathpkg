Let $L$ be a Lie algebra over a field $F$. A \textbf{universal enveloping algebra} of $L$ is a pair $(\mathfrak{U}, i)$, where $\mathfrak{U}$ is an associative algebra with $1$ over $F$ and $i: L \to \mathfrak{U}$ is a linear map satisfying
$$i([xy]) = i(x)i(y) - i(y)i(x) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in L,$$
such that the following universal property holds: given any associative $F$-algebra $\mathfrak{U}'$ with $1$ and any linear map $j: L \to \mathfrak{U}'$ satisfying $j([xy]) = j(x)j(y) - j(y)j(x)$, there exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{U}'$ (sending $1$ to $1$) with $\phi \circ i = j$.

Existence: Let $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ be the tensor algebra on $L$, and let $J$ be the two-sided ideal in $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ generated by all $x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy]$ ($x, y \in L$). Define $\mathfrak{U}(L) = \mathfrak{T}(L) / J$, and let $\pi: \mathfrak{T}(L) \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$ be the canonical homomorphism. Set $i = \pi|_L : L \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$. Then $(\mathfrak{U}(L), i)$ is a universal enveloping algebra of $L$.

Uniqueness: If $(\mathfrak{U}, i)$ and $(\mathfrak{U}', i')$ are two universal enveloping algebras of $L$, there exists a unique isomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{U}'$ with $\phi \circ i = i'$.
