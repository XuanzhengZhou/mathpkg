Serre's Theorem is one of the deepest results in the structure theory of semisimple Lie algebras. It states that the Lie algebra presented by the Serre relations (S1)-(S3) and $(S_{ij}^\pm)$, defined purely in terms of the Cartan matrix of a root system $\Phi$, is a finite-dimensional semisimple Lie algebra with that root system. The proof proceeds by constructing a suitable representation (using the tensor algebra), showing that the $h_i$ remain independent, and proving that $\operatorname{ad} x_i$ and $\operatorname{ad} y_i$ are locally nilpotent, which forces finite-dimensional weight spaces. Together with the uniqueness theorem (18.4), this gives a complete presentation-theoretic characterization of semisimple Lie algebras.
