Corollary A of the PBW Theorem states that any subspace $W$ of $T^m L$ that maps isomorphically onto the $m$-th symmetric power $S^m L$ provides a complement to $U_{m-1}$ inside $U_m$, the $m$-th term of the natural filtration of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. This follows from the commutativity of the diagram relating $U_m$, $G^m$, $S^m$, and $T^m$, combined with the PBW isomorphism $\mathfrak{S} \cong \mathfrak{G}$. In particular, this corollary is used to deduce that $L$ itself embeds injectively into $\mathfrak{U}(L)$.
