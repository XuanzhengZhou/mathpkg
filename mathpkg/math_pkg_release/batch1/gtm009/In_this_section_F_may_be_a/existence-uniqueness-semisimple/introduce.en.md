This theorem is the culmination of the classification of semisimple Lie algebras over an algebraically closed field of characteristic zero. Part (a) asserts that for every abstract root system $\Phi$, there exists a semisimple Lie algebra realizing it, which follows directly from Serre's Theorem (18.3). Part (b) is the isomorphism theorem: two semisimple Lie algebras with isomorphic root systems are themselves isomorphic, and the isomorphism can be chosen to send any prescribed nonzero root vectors for a base to corresponding root vectors for the image base. This proves that the root system is a complete invariant of a semisimple Lie algebra.
