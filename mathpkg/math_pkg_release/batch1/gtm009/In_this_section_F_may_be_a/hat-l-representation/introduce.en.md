In the proof of Serre's Theorem, one considers the free Lie algebra $\hat{L}$ on $3\ell$ generators $\hat{x}_i, \hat{y}_i, \hat{h}_i$ and constructs a representation on the tensor algebra $V$ on $\ell$ generators. The action is defined by explicit formulas: $\hat{h}_j$ acts diagonally via the Cartan integers, $\hat{y}_j$ acts by left multiplication, and $\hat{x}_j$ acts by a derivation-like formula. The key property is that the restriction to the Cartan part is faithful ($\sum F\hat{h}_j \cap \operatorname{Ker} \hat{\phi} = 0$), which ultimately implies that the Cartan subalgebra of the constructed Lie algebra has the correct dimension.
