Corollary C of the PBW Theorem provides an explicit basis for the universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. Given any ordered basis $(x_1, x_2, x_3, \ldots)$ of the Lie algebra $L$, the set of all ordered monomials $x_{i(1)} \cdots x_{i(m)}$ with non-decreasing indices, together with the unit $1$, forms a vector space basis of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. This is the most commonly cited form of the PBW Theorem and is fundamental for computations in $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. The result holds even when $L$ is infinite-dimensional, though in practice the countable basis case is adequate for the study of semisimple Lie algebras.
