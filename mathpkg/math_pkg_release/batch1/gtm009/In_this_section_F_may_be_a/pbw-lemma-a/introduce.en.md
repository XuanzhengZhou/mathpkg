Lemma A is the central technical component in the proof of the Poincaré-Birkhoff-Witt Theorem. It constructs, by induction on the filtration degree $m$, a family of linear maps $f_m: L \otimes S_m \to \mathfrak{S}(L)$ satisfying three axioms: $(A_m)$ forces agreement with multiplication when the index is compatible with the ordering; $(B_m)$ ensures the output lies in the correct filtration level; and $(C_m)$ encodes the Lie algebra action property. These maps collectively define an $L$-module structure on the symmetric algebra $\mathfrak{S}(L)$, which then yields a $\mathfrak{U}(L)$-module structure used to prove the PBW isomorphism.
