Let $L$ be a Lie algebra over a field $F$. Let $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ be its universal enveloping algebra, with natural filtration $U_0 \subset U_1 \subset U_2 \subset \cdots$ where $U_m$ is the image of $T^0 L \oplus \cdots \oplus T^m L$ under the canonical map $\pi: \mathfrak{T}(L) \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$. Let $\mathfrak{G} = \bigoplus_{m=0}^{\infty} G^m$ be the associated graded algebra, where $G^m = U_m / U_{m-1}$ (with $U_{-1} = 0$).

Let $\mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{S}(L)$ be the symmetric algebra on the vector space $L$, with its usual grading $\mathfrak{S} = \bigoplus_{m=0}^{\infty} S^m$. There is a canonical surjection $\omega: \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{G}$ defined as follows: the composite $T^m \to U_m \to G^m$ factors through $S^m$ (since the kernel of $T^m \to S^m$ is generated by $x \otimes y - y \otimes x$, which maps into $U_{m-1}$ in $\mathfrak{U}(L)$).

\textbf{Theorem (PBW).} The homomorphism $\omega: \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{G}$ is an isomorphism of algebras.
