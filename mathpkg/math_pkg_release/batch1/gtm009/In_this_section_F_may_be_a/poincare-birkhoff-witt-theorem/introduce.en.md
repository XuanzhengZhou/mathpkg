The Poincaré-Birkhoff-Witt (PBW) Theorem is the fundamental structure theorem for universal enveloping algebras. It states that the associated graded algebra $\mathfrak{G}$ of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ (with respect to the natural filtration) is canonically isomorphic to the symmetric algebra $\mathfrak{S}(L)$. This means that $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ is a "quantization" of $\mathfrak{S}(L)$, with the commutator in $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ recovering the Lie bracket modulo lower filtration degrees. The PBW Theorem is essential for establishing that $L$ embeds injectively into $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ and that ordered monomials form a basis.
