This proposition computes the scalar by which the Casimir element $c$ acts on a standard cyclic module of highest weight $\lambda$. Starting from the explicit construction $c = \sum h_i k_i + \sum_{\alpha \in \Phi} x_\alpha z_\alpha$ using dual bases adapted to the root space decomposition, one evaluates $c \cdot v^+$ on the maximal vector $v^+$. For negative roots $\alpha < 0$, one has $z_\alpha v^+ = 0$, while for positive roots $\alpha > 0$, rewriting $x_\alpha z_\alpha = z_\alpha x_\alpha + t_\alpha$ and using $x_\alpha v^+ = 0$ yields $t_\alpha v^+ = (\lambda, \alpha) v^+$. Summing the contributions gives the eigenvalue $(\lambda + \delta, \lambda + \delta) - (\delta, \delta)$, a fundamental invariant that distinguishes inequivalent highest weight modules.
