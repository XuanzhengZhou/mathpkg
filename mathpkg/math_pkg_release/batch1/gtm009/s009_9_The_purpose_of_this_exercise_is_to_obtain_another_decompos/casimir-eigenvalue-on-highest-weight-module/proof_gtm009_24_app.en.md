**Proof.** Recall from (22.1) the construction of the Casimir element $c = c_L$ in $\mathfrak{U}(L)$:
$$
c = \sum_{i=1}^{\ell} h_i k_i + \sum_{\alpha \in \Phi} x_\alpha z_\alpha,
$$
where $\{h_i\}$ and $\{k_i\}$ are dual bases of a Cartan subalgebra $H$ relative to the Killing form, and for each root $\alpha \in \Phi$, $\{x_\alpha\}$ and $\{z_\alpha\}$ are dual in the sense that $\kappa(x_\alpha, z_\alpha) = 1$ while $\kappa(x_\alpha, z_\beta) = 0$ for $\alpha \neq \beta$ (here $x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$, $z_\alpha \in L_{-\alpha}$). As noted in (22.1), any choice of dual bases leads to the same element $c$, and by (23.2), $c$ lies in the center of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$.

Let $Z(\lambda)$ be a standard cyclic module of highest weight $\lambda \in H^*$, generated by a maximal vector $v^+$ (so $x_\alpha \cdot v^+ = 0$ for all $\alpha > 0$ and $h \cdot v^+ = \lambda(h) v^+$ for all $h \in H$). Since $c$ is central, it acts as a scalar on the (indecomposable) module $Z(\lambda)$. We compute this scalar by evaluating $c \cdot v^+$.

For negative roots $\alpha < 0$, we have $z_\alpha \in L_\alpha$ with $\alpha < 0$ (or equivalently, $L_{-\alpha}$ with $-\alpha > 0$). Since $v^+$ is a maximal vector, $z_\alpha \cdot v^+ = 0$ whenever the corresponding root vector lies in a positive root space, which is the case for $\alpha < 0$ after the standard convention $x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$, $z_\alpha \in L_{-\alpha}$. Thus for $\alpha < 0$, $x_\alpha z_\alpha \cdot v^+ = x_\alpha \cdot (z_\alpha \cdot v^+) = 0$.

For positive roots $\alpha > 0$, rewrite $x_\alpha z_\alpha = z_\alpha x_\alpha + [x_\alpha, z_\alpha]$. The commutator $[x_\alpha, z_\alpha] = t_\alpha \in H$ satisfies $t_\alpha = \kappa(x_\alpha, z_\alpha) t_\alpha^\vee$ where $t_\alpha^\vee$ is the coroot. With the dual basis normalization $\kappa(x_\alpha, z_\alpha) = 1$, we have $[x_\alpha, z_\alpha] = t_\alpha$ where $\alpha(t_\alpha) = \kappa(t_\alpha, t_\alpha) = 2$. More directly, for any weight $\mu$, $t_\alpha$ acts as $\mu(t_\alpha) = (\mu, \alpha)$ under the identification of $H$ with $H^*$ via the Killing form. Since $x_\alpha \cdot v^+ = 0$, we obtain:
$$
x_\alpha z_\alpha \cdot v^+ = z_\alpha x_\alpha \cdot v^+ + t_\alpha \cdot v^+ = 0 + (\lambda, \alpha) v^+.
$$

For the Cartan part, $\{h_i\}$ and $\{k_i\}$ are dual bases of $H$, so $\kappa(h_i, k_j) = \delta_{ij}$. Then:
$$
\sum_{i=1}^{\ell} h_i k_i \cdot v^+ = \sum_{i=1}^{\ell} \lambda(h_i) \lambda(k_i) v^+.
$$

Under the isomorphism $H \cong H^*$ induced by the Killing form, the sum $\sum_i \lambda(h_i) \lambda(k_i)$ equals $(\lambda, \lambda)$, the squared length of $\lambda$ with respect to the induced bilinear form on $H^*$.

Summing all contributions:
$$
c \cdot v^+ = \left( (\lambda, \lambda) + \sum_{\alpha > 0} (\lambda, \alpha) \right) v^+.
$$

Since $\sum_{\alpha > 0} \alpha = 2\delta$, where $\delta$ is half the sum of positive roots, we have:
$$
c \cdot v^+ = \big( (\lambda, \lambda) + (\lambda, 2\delta) \big) v^+ = (\lambda, \lambda + 2\delta) v^+.
$$

Expanding $(\lambda + \delta, \lambda + \delta) = (\lambda, \lambda) + 2(\lambda, \delta) + (\delta, \delta) = (\lambda, \lambda) + (\lambda, 2\delta) + (\delta, \delta)$, we conclude:
$$
c \cdot v^+ = \big( (\lambda + \delta, \lambda + \delta) - (\delta, \delta) \big) v^+.
$$

By Schur's Lemma (or more precisely because $c$ is central and $Z(\lambda)$ is a standard cyclic module, hence indecomposable), $c$ acts as this same scalar on the entire module $Z(\lambda)$. $\square$
