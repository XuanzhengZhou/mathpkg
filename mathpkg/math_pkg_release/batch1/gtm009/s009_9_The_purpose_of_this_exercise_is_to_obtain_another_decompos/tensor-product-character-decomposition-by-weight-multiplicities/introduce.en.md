This theorem expresses the tensor product of two irreducible finite-dimensional modules over a semisimple Lie algebra as a sum of irreducible characters, weighted by the multiplicities of weights in one of the factors. Unlike Steinberg's formula (which involves the Weyl group twice), this decomposition requires explicit knowledge of the weight multiplicities of only one module. The coefficient $t(\lambda + \lambda'' + \delta)$ acts as a signed selector: it vanishes for weights with nontrivial stabilizer, and otherwise contributes $\pm 1$ depending on the Weyl group sign needed to bring the weight into the dominant chamber. The braces $\{\mu\}$ indicate conjugation to the unique dominant weight in the orbit.
