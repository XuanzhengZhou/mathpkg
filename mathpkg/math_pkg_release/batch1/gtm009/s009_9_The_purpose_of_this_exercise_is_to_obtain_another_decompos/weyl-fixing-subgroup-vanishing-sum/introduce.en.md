This lemma states that the alternating sum $\sum \operatorname{sn}(\sigma) \varepsilon_{\sigma(\lambda)}$ over the Weyl group vanishes whenever the weight $\lambda$ is fixed by some nontrivial element of $\mathcal{W}$. The proof exploits the fact that such a $\lambda$ lies in the closure (but not the interior) of some Weyl chamber, guaranteeing the existence of a reflection fixing $\lambda$. Consequently, the stabilizer of $\lambda$ in $\mathcal{W}$ has even order, forcing the alternating sum to be zero. This lemma is a key combinatorial input for deriving the tensor product character decomposition in terms of weight multiplicities.
