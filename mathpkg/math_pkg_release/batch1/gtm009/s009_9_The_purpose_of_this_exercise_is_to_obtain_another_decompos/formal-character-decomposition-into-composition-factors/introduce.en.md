This theorem expresses the formal character of an irreducible highest weight module $V(\lambda)$ as an integer linear combination of the formal characters of standard cyclic (Verma) modules $Z(\mu)$ for weights $\mu \preceq \lambda$ with the same Casimir eigenvalue. The proof proceeds by induction: if the standard cyclic module $Z = Z(\lambda)$ is irreducible, then $\operatorname{ch}_{\lambda} = \operatorname{ch}'_{\lambda}$ and the result is trivial. Otherwise, $Z$ contains a proper standard cyclic submodule $W$ of some weight $\mu \prec \lambda$, yielding a short exact sequence. Induction on $Z/W$ and $W$ produces composition series, whose characters can be written as $Z$-linear combinations. Inverting the resulting upper-triangular integer matrix with 1s on the diagonal gives the desired expression, with $c(\lambda) = 1$.
