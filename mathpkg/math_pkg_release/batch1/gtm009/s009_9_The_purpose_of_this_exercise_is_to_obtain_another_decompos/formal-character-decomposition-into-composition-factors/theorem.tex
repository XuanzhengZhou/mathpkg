Fix $\lambda \in \Lambda^+$ and consider the finite set of $\mu \in \Lambda$ satisfying
$$
(\mu + \delta, \mu + \delta) = (\lambda + \delta, \lambda + \delta).
$$
Order this set as $(\mu_1, \ldots, \mu_t)$ such that $\mu_i \prec \mu_j$ implies $i \leq j$. Let $\operatorname{ch}'_{\mu}$ denote the formal character of the standard cyclic module $Z(\mu)$ of highest weight $\mu$, and let $\operatorname{ch}_{\mu}$ denote the formal character of the irreducible module $V(\mu)$. Then there exist integers $c(\mu) \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that
$$
\operatorname{ch}_{\lambda} = \sum_{\mu \prec \lambda} c(\mu) \, \operatorname{ch}'_{\mu},
$$
where the sum is taken over all $\mu$ satisfying $(\mu + \delta, \mu + \delta) = (\lambda + \delta, \lambda + \delta)$, with $c(\lambda) = 1$. Moreover, the coefficients satisfy $c(\mu) \in \mathbb{Z}$.
