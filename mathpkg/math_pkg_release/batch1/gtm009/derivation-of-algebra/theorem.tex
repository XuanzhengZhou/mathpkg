Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an $F$-algebra (not necessarily associative), i.e., a vector space over $F$ endowed with a bilinear operation $\mathfrak{A} \times \mathfrak{A} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$. A \text{derivation} of $\mathfrak{A}$ is a linear map $\delta: \mathfrak{A} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$ satisfying the product rule
$$\delta(ab) = a\delta(b) + \delta(a)b$$
for all $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}$. The collection $\text{Der } \mathfrak{A}$ of all derivations of $\mathfrak{A}$ is a vector subspace of $\text{End } \mathfrak{A}$, and the commutator $[\delta, \delta']$ of two derivations is again a derivation. Thus $\text{Der } \mathfrak{A}$ is a subalgebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(\mathfrak{A})$.
