A derivation is a linear map that satisfies the Leibniz rule, generalizing the familiar notion of differentiation. The set of all derivations of an algebra forms a Lie algebra under the commutator bracket, providing a natural bridge between analysis and Lie theory. Derivations arise as infinitesimal generators of automorphisms and play a key role in the structure theory of Lie algebras.
