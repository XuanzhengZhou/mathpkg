Let $V$ be a finite dimensional vector space over $F$, and let $\operatorname{End} V$ be the set of linear transformations $V \to V$. Define the bracket operation
$$[x, y] = xy - yx$$
for $x, y \in \operatorname{End} V$. With this operation $\operatorname{End} V$ becomes a Lie algebra over $F$, denoted $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ and called the \text{general linear algebra}. When $V$ is infinite dimensional, the notation $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is also used.

If a basis is fixed for $V$, then $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is identified with the set of all $n \times n$ matrices over $F$, denoted $\mathfrak{gl}(n, F)$. Relative to the standard basis $\{e_{ij}\}$ (having $1$ in the $(i, j)$ position and $0$ elsewhere), the multiplication table is:
$$[e_{ij}, e_{kl}] = \delta_{jk}e_{il} - \delta_{li}e_{kj}.$$
