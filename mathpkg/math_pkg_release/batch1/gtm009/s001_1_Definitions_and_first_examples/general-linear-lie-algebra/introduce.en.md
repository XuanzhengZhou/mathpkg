The general linear Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is the most important example of a Lie algebra. It consists of all linear endomorphisms of a vector space $V$, with the commutator bracket $[x,y] = xy - yx$. This construction shows how every associative algebra gives rise to a Lie algebra by replacing the associative product with the commutator. When $V$ is $n$-dimensional, $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is isomorphic to the Lie algebra of $n \times n$ matrices $\mathfrak{gl}(n, F)$.
