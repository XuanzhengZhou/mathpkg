Let $V$ be a vector space of dimension $2\ell$ over $F$ endowed with a non-degenerate bilinear form $f$ satisfying $f(v, w) = -f(w, v)$. The \text{symplectic algebra} $\mathfrak{sp}(V)$, or $\mathfrak{sp}(2\ell, F)$, consists of all endomorphisms $x$ of $V$ satisfying
$$f(x(v), w) = -f(v, x(w)).$$
In matrix terms, writing $x = \begin{pmatrix} m & n \\ p & q \end{pmatrix}$ with $m, n, p, q \in \mathfrak{gl}(\ell, F)$, the condition for $x$ to be symplectic is that $sx = -x^t s$ ($x^t = \text{transpose of } x$), i.e., $n^t = n$, $p^t = p$, and $m^t = -q$, which forces $\operatorname{Tr}(x) = 0$.

The dimension of $\mathfrak{sp}(2\ell, F)$ is $2\ell^2 + \ell$.
