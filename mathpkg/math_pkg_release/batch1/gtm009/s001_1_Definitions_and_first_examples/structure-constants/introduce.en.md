Structure constants provide a coordinate description of a Lie algebra by expressing the bracket of basis vectors as linear combinations. For an $n$-dimensional Lie algebra with basis $\{x_1,\ldots,x_n\}$, the $n^3$ scalars $a_{ij}^k$ satisfying $[x_i,x_j] = \sum_k a_{ij}^k x_k$ completely determine the algebra structure. The axioms (L2) and (L3) translate into simple algebraic conditions on the $a_{ij}^k$, making it possible to classify low-dimensional Lie algebras by analyzing these constraints.
