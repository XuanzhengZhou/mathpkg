Let $V$ be a vector space over $F$ of dimension $2\ell$, equipped with a non-degenerate alternating bilinear form $f$. The symplectic algebra $\mathfrak{sp}(V)$, also denoted $\mathfrak{sp}(2\ell, F)$, consists of all endomorphisms $x$ of $V$ satisfying
$$f(x(v), w) = -f(v, x(w)) \quad \text{for all } v, w \in V.$$
In matrix terms, writing $x = \begin{pmatrix} m & n \\ p & q \end{pmatrix}$ with $m, n, p, q \in \mathfrak{gl}(\ell, F)$, the symplectic condition is $sx = -x^t s$, i.e., $n^t = n$, $p^t = p$, and $m^t = -q$. The dimension is $\dim \mathfrak{sp}(2\ell, F) = 2\ell^2 + \ell$.
