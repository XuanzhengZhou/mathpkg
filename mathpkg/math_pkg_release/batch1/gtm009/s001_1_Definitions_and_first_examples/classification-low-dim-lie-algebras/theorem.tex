Up to isomorphism, there are exactly the following Lie algebras of dimension $\leq 2$ over a field $F$:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Dimension 1:} A single basis vector $x$, with multiplication table $[xx] = 0$ (abelian). This is the unique 1-dimensional Lie algebra.
\item \textbf{Dimension 2:} Two possibilities:
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Abelian:} $[xy] = 0$ for a basis $x, y$.
  \item \textbf{Nonabelian:} There exists a basis $x, y$ such that $[xy] = x$. This is the unique (up to isomorphism) nonabelian 2-dimensional Lie algebra.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{itemize}
