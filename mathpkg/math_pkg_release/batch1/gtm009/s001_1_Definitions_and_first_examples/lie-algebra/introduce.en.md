A Lie algebra is a vector space equipped with a bilinear bracket operation satisfying anticommutativity (via $[xx]=0$) and the Jacobi identity. Lie algebras arise naturally as vector spaces of linear transformations under the commutator bracket $[x,y] = xy - yx$. They provide the infinitesimal counterpart to Lie groups and form the central algebraic structure in the theory of continuous symmetry.
