A vector space $L$ over a field $F$, with an operation $L \times L \to L$, denoted $(x, y) \mapsto [xy]$ and called the \text{bracket} or \text{commutator} of $x$ and $y$, is called a \text{Lie algebra} over $F$ if the following axioms are satisfied:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(L1)] The bracket operation is bilinear.
\item[(L2)] $[xx] = 0$ for all $x \in L$.
\item[(L3)] $[x[yz]] + [y[zx]] + [z[xy]] = 0$ for all $x, y, z \in L$.
\end{itemize}

Axiom (L3) is called the \text{Jacobi identity}.
