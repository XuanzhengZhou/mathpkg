An ad-nilpotent element is one whose adjoint operator is nilpotent. Engel's theorem shows that a Lie algebra is nilpotent precisely when all its elements are ad-nilpotent. This provides the key structural characterization linking nilpotency of the algebra to nilpotency of individual adjoint operators.
