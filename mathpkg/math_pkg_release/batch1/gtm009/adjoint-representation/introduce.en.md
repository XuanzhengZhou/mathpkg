The adjoint representation is the canonical way in which a Lie algebra acts on itself via the bracket. It sends each element $x$ to the linear operator $\operatorname{ad} x$, which brackets with $x$. This representation is essential for the structure theory of Lie algebras; its kernel is the center, and when the algebra is simple it provides an embedding into the general linear algebra.
