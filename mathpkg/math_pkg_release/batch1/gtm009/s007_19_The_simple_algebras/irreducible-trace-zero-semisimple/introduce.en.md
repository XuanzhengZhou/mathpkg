This proposition provides a practical criterion for testing semisimplicity without computing the Killing form. If a Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ consists of trace-zero endomorphisms and acts irreducibly on $V$, then it is semisimple. This criterion is the key tool used to prove that the classical Lie algebras $A_\ell$, $B_\ell$, $C_\ell$, and $D_\ell$ are semisimple. The proof relies on Proposition 19.1(a): one first shows $L$ is reductive, then uses the irreducibility to conclude $Z(L) = 0$, hence $L$ is semisimple.
