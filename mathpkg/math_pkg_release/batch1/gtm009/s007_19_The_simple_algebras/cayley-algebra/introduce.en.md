The Cayley algebra $\mathfrak{C}$ is an $8$-dimensional alternative algebra over $F$, also known as the algebra of octonions. It can be realized as the set of $2 \times 2$ matrices with entries in $F$ and $F^3$, equipped with a non-associative multiplication that combines scalar multiplication, the dot product, and the cross product on $F^3$. The commutator subspace $\mathfrak{C}_o$ has codimension $1$ with basis complementary to the identity element. The significance of the Cayley algebra for Lie theory is that its derivation algebra $\operatorname{Der}(\mathfrak{C})$ is precisely the $14$-dimensional exceptional simple Lie algebra $G_2$.
