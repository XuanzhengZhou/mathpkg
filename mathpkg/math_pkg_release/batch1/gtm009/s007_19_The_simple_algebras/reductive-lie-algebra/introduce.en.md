A Lie algebra is called reductive if its radical equals its center. This generalizes the notion of semisimple Lie algebras (where the radical is zero) and includes abelian Lie algebras as the other extreme case. The general linear Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ provides an intermediate example: it is reductive but neither abelian nor semisimple. Reductive Lie algebras play an important role in the structure theory because they decompose as a direct sum of their derived algebra (which is semisimple) and their center.
