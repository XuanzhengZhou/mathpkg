This proposition establishes the fundamental structure theorem for reductive Lie algebras. It states that every reductive Lie algebra decomposes as the direct sum of its derived algebra and its center, and moreover, the derived algebra is semisimple. The proof uses the fact that for a reductive (but non-abelian) Lie algebra $L$, the quotient $L' = L/Z(L)$ is semisimple, and $\operatorname{ad} L \cong \operatorname{ad} L'$ acts completely reducibly on $L$. By writing $L = M \oplus Z(L)$ with $M$ an ideal, one shows that $[LL]$ maps onto $L'$ under the canonical map, forcing $L = [LL] \oplus Z(L)$.
