A Dynkin diagram is a graph that encodes the Cartan matrix of a root system, providing a complete combinatorial description. The classification of connected Dynkin diagrams (achieved by Killing and Cartan, simplified by Dynkin) yields the complete list of simple Lie algebras. Dynkin diagrams are fundamental throughout mathematics, appearing in singularity theory, quiver representations, and mathematical physics.
