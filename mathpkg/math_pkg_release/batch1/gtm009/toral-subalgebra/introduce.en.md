A toral subalgebra consists entirely of semisimple elements (those whose adjoint operators are diagonalizable). In the theory of semisimple Lie algebras, maximal toral subalgebras play the role of "maximal tori" from Lie group theory. They are abelian and serve as the reference point for the root space decomposition, which is the foundation for the classification of semisimple Lie algebras.
