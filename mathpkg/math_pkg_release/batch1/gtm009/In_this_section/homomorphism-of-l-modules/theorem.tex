A homomorphism of $L$-modules is a linear map $\phi: V \to W$ such that

$$\phi(x.v) = x.\phi(v)$$

for all $x \in L$, $v \in V$, where $V$ and $W$ are $L$-modules. An $L$-module homomorphism that is bijective is called an isomorphism of $L$-modules. The kernel and image of an $L$-module homomorphism are submodules.
