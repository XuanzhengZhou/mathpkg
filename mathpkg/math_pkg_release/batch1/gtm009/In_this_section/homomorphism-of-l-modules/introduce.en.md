A homomorphism of $L$-modules is a linear map that intertwines the action of the Lie algebra $L$ on two modules $V$ and $W$. This is the natural notion of morphism in the category of $L$-modules. The kernel and image of an $L$-module homomorphism are submodules, and the usual isomorphism theorems carry over from vector spaces. The concepts of submodule, quotient module, irreducibility, and complete reducibility are defined in exact analogy with module theory over associative algebras. These notions also apply when $\dim V = \infty$.
