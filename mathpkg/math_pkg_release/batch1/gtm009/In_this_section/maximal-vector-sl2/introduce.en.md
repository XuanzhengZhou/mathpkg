A maximal vector (also called a highest weight vector) in an $\mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$-module is a nonzero weight vector annihilated by the raising operator $x$. Because the weights are bounded above in a finite dimensional module (thanks to Lemma 7.1), maximal vectors always exist. They serve as cyclic generators: applying powers of the lowering operator $y$ to a maximal vector generates a basis for the irreducible module. The weight of a maximal vector is the highest weight and completely determines the isomorphism class of the irreducible module.
