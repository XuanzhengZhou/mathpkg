Let $V$ be a finite dimensional $L$-module for $L = \mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$. Since $\dim V < \infty$ and the weight spaces $V_\lambda$ are distinct, there exists $\lambda \in \mathbb{F}$ such that $V_\lambda \neq 0$ but $V_{\lambda+2} = 0$. A nonzero vector $v_0 \in V_\lambda$ with $x.v_0 = 0$ is called a \text{maximal vector} of weight $\lambda$.
