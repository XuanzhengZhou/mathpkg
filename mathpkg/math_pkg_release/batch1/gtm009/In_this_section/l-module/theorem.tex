A vector space $V$ over a field $\mathbb{F}$, endowed with an operation $L \times V \rightarrow V$ (denoted $(x, v) \mapsto x.v$ or $xv$), is called an \text{$L$-module} if the following conditions are satisfied for all $x, y \in L$, $v, w \in V$, $a, b \in \mathbb{F}$:

\text{(M1)} \quad (ax + by).v = a(x.v) + b(y.v),

\text{(M2)} \quad x.(av + bw) = a(x.v) + b(x.w),

\text{(M3)} \quad [xy].v = x.y.v - y.x.v.
