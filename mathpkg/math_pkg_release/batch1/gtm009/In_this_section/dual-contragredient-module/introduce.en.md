The dual (or contragredient) module is a standard construction that turns the dual vector space $V^*$ of an $L$-module $V$ into another $L$-module. The action is defined with a minus sign to ensure the Jacobi identity (M3) is satisfied. This construction is essential for studying tensor products of modules and for defining the Casimir element via the isomorphism $V^* \otimes V \cong \operatorname{End} V$.
