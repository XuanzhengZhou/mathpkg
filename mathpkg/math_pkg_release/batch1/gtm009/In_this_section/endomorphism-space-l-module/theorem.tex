Let $V$ and $W$ be $L$-modules. Then $L$ acts naturally on the space $\operatorname{Hom}(V, W)$ of linear maps by the rule:

$$(x.f)(v) = x.f(v) - f(x.v), \quad x \in L, \; f \in \operatorname{Hom}(V, W), \; v \in V.$$

In particular, if $V = W$, this makes $\operatorname{End} V$ an $L$-module via:

$$(x.f)(v) = x.f(v) - f(x.v), \quad x \in L, \; f \in \operatorname{End} V, \; v \in V.$$
