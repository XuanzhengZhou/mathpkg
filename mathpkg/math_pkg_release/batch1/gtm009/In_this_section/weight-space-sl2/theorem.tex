Let $V$ be an $L$-module for $L = \mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$, with standard basis $(x, h, y)$. For $\lambda \in \mathbb{F}$, define the \text{weight space}

$$V_\lambda = \{v \in V \mid h.v = \lambda v\}.$$

When $V_\lambda \neq 0$, $\lambda$ is called a \text{weight} of $h$ in $V$ and any nonzero $v \in V_\lambda$ is a \text{weight vector} of weight $\lambda$.
