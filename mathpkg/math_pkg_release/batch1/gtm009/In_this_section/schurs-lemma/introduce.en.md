Schur's Lemma is a fundamental result in representation theory. For an irreducible representation of a Lie algebra, any linear endomorphism of the representation space that commutes with the action of the entire algebra must be a scalar multiple of the identity. This lemma underpins numerous structural results, including the uniqueness of maximal vectors in irreducible $\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{F})$-modules and the proportionality of invariant bilinear forms on simple Lie algebras.
