This simple but important lemma states that any representation of a semisimple Lie algebra $L$ maps into the special linear Lie algebra $\mathfrak{sl}(V)$, i.e., all representing endomorphisms are traceless. This follows because $L = [L, L]$ for semisimple algebras, and $\mathfrak{sl}(V)$ is the derived algebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$. As a consequence, a semisimple Lie algebra cannot act nontrivially on a one-dimensional module.
