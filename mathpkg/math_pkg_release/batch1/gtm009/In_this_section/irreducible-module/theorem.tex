An $L$-module $V \neq 0$ is called \text{irreducible} if its only submodules are $0$ and $V$. An $L$-module is called \text{completely reducible} if it is a direct sum of irreducible submodules.

These notions are standard and also make sense when $\dim V = \infty$. The terminology "irreducible" and "completely reducible" applies equally well to representations of $L$.
