An $L$-module $V$ is called irreducible (or simple) if it is nonzero and has no proper nonzero submodules. This is the Lie algebra analogue of an irreducible representation. An $L$-module is completely reducible if it decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible submodules. A semisimple Lie algebra $L$ itself, viewed as an $L$-module via the adjoint representation, is completely reducible (by Theorem 5.2). The standard results for modules over associative rings, such as the Jordan-Hölder theorem, carry over to $L$-modules.
