The trace form $\beta(x,y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\phi(x)\phi(y))$ generalizes the Killing form to an arbitrary faithful representation $\phi$ of a semisimple Lie algebra $L$. Like the Killing form, it is symmetric, associative, and nondegenerate — the latter following from Cartan's trace criterion for solvability (Theorem 4.3). The trace form provides the inner product structure needed to define the dual basis used in the construction of the Casimir element.
