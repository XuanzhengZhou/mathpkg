Lemma 7.1 describes how the standard generators $x$ and $y$ of $\mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$ shift weight spaces. The element $x$ raises the weight by $2$, while $y$ lowers it by $2$. This follows directly from the commutation relation $[h, x] = 2x$ and $[h, y] = -2y$. Since $V$ is finite dimensional, there must exist a nonzero weight vector annihilated by $x$ — this is the maximal vector, which serves as the starting point for constructing irreducible modules.
