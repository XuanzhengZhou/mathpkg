Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra. Then there exist ideals $L_1, \ldots, L_t$ of $L$ which are simple (as Lie algebras), such that
$$L = L_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus L_t.$$
Every simple ideal of $L$ coincides with one of the $L_i$. Moreover, the Killing form of $L_i$ is the restriction of $\kappa$ to $L_i \times L_i$, and the $L_i$ are pairwise orthogonal with respect to $\kappa$.
