Every semisimple Lie algebra decomposes uniquely as a direct sum of simple ideals. This decomposition is orthogonal with respect to the Killing form, and each simple summand's Killing form is nondegenerate. This theorem reduces the classification of semisimple Lie algebras to that of simple ones, which is achieved through the Cartan-Killing classification via root systems.
