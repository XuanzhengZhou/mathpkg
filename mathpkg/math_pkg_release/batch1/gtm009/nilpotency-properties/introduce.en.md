This proposition summarizes the fundamental properties of nilpotent Lie algebras. Nilpotency is inherited by subalgebras and quotients. A Lie algebra whose quotient by its center is nilpotent must itself be nilpotent. Moreover, every nonzero nilpotent Lie algebra has a nontrivial center, a fact that is crucial in inductive proofs about nilpotent algebras.
