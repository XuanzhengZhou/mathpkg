A semisimple element of a Lie algebra is called regular if its centralizer has minimal dimension among all elements. For $\mathfrak{sl}(n, F)$, a semisimple element is regular precisely when its $n$ eigenvalues are pairwise distinct, which is equivalent to the condition that its minimal polynomial equals its characteristic polynomial. Regular semisimple elements play a crucial role in the construction of Cartan subalgebras, as every Cartan subalgebra contains regular elements (and is in fact the centralizer of a regular semisimple element).
