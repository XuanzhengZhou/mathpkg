For $x \in L$, the Engel subalgebra is $L_0(\operatorname{ad} x)$, the generalized eigenspace of $\operatorname{ad} x$ corresponding to the eigenvalue $0$. Equivalently, $L_0(\operatorname{ad} x)$ consists of all $y \in L$ such that $(\operatorname{ad} x)^n y = 0$ for sufficiently large $n$. By Lemma 15.1, $L_0(\operatorname{ad} x)$ is a subalgebra of $L$. This terminology follows D. W. Barnes.
