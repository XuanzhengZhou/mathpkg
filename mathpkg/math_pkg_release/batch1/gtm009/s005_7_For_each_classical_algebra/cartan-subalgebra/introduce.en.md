A Cartan subalgebra (CSA) is a nilpotent, self-normalizing subalgebra of a Lie algebra. For semisimple Lie algebras over algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero, Cartan subalgebras coincide with maximal toral subalgebras and play a central role in the root space decomposition. More generally, Cartan subalgebras exist in any Lie algebra over an algebraically closed field whose cardinality is sufficiently large relative to the dimension, and they are precisely the minimal Engel subalgebras. Named after Elie Cartan, these subalgebras are fundamental to the classification of semisimple Lie algebras.
