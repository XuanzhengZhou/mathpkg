Let $L = \mathfrak{sl}(2, F)$ with standard basis $(x, h, y)$. For $c \in F^\times$, define:
$$x(c) = \exp(\operatorname{ad}(c x)), \quad y(c) = \exp(\operatorname{ad}(c y))$$
and inner automorphisms:
$$w(c) = x(c) y(-c^{-1}) x(c), \quad h(c) = w(c) w(1)^{-1}$$

Then $w(c)$ and $h(c)$ generate all diagonal automorphisms of $L$, and every diagonal automorphism of $\mathfrak{sl}(2, F)$ is inner. Consequently, $\operatorname{Aut} L = \operatorname{Int} L = \mathcal{E}(L)$.
