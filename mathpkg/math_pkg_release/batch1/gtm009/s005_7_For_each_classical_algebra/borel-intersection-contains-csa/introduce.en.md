This exercise establishes that the intersection of any two Borel subalgebras of a semisimple Lie algebra $L$ always contains a Cartan subalgebra. The proof is nontrivial and relies on an inductive argument using the structure of solvable subalgebras, the action of nilpotent ideals, and the conjugacy theorems for Borel and Cartan subalgebras. This result is important for understanding the geometry of the flag variety $L/B$ and the Bruhat decomposition.
