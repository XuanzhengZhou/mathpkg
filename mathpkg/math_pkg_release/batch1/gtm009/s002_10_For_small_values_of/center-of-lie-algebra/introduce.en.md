The center $Z(L)$ of a Lie algebra $L$ consists of all elements that commute (in the Lie bracket sense) with every element of $L$. The center is always an ideal of $L$, and it plays a role analogous to the center of a group. A Lie algebra is abelian precisely when it coincides with its own center, i.e., when every bracket vanishes.
