This exercise verifies that the set of derivations of an $F$-algebra forms a Lie algebra under the commutator bracket. Given two derivations $\delta_1, \delta_2$ of an $F$-algebra $\mathfrak{A}$, their commutator $[\delta_1, \delta_2] = \delta_1 \circ \delta_2 - \delta_2 \circ \delta_1$ is again a derivation, while the ordinary composition $\delta_1 \circ \delta_2$ generally fails to satisfy the Leibniz rule. This fact provides the fundamental link between derivations and Lie algebra structures.
