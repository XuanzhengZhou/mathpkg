This exercise verifies that the terms of the derived series $I^{(n)}$ and descending central series $I^n$ of an ideal $I \trianglelefteq L$ remain ideals of the ambient Lie algebra $L$. This follows from the fact that the adjoint action of $L$ on $I$ respects these series constructions, and the property is essential for proving that solvability and nilpotency are well-behaved with respect to ideals.
