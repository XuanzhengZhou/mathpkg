This exercise classifies 3-dimensional Lie algebras whose derived algebra is 1-dimensional and central. Up to isomorphism, there is exactly one such Lie algebra: it is the Heisenberg Lie algebra, with basis $(x, y, z)$ and the single nontrivial bracket $[x, y] = z$, where $z$ spans both the derived algebra and the center.
