This exercise proves that any 3-dimensional Lie algebra coinciding with its own derived algebra is necessarily simple. The argument uses the fact that the property $L = [LL]$ is preserved under homomorphic images, implying that any proper ideal would give a smaller-dimensional quotient also equal to its derived algebra, which is impossible. Applied to $\mathfrak{sl}(2, F)$ (char $F \neq 2$), this recovers its simplicity from the fact that $\mathfrak{sl}(2, F) = [\mathfrak{sl}(2, F), \mathfrak{sl}(2, F)]$.
