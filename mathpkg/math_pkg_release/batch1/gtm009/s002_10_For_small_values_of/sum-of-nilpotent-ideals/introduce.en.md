This exercise proves that the class of nilpotent ideals is closed under sums, which implies the existence of a unique maximal nilpotent ideal (the nilradical) in any Lie algebra. The proof uses the second isomorphism theorem $(I+J)/J \cong I/(I \cap J)$ to relate the sum to a quotient of each summand. The nilradical plays a role analogous to the Fitting subgroup in finite group theory.
