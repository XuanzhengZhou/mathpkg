The constructions are formally the same as for groups and rings. For (b), the map $\phi: L/I \to L/J$ given by $x+I \mapsto x+J$ is well-defined because $I \subset J$, and its kernel is $J/I$, giving the isomorphism. For (c), the map $\psi: I \to (I+J)/J$ given by $x \mapsto x+J$ has kernel $I \cap J$ and surjects onto $(I+J)/J$, yielding the isomorphism $I/(I \cap J) \cong (I+J)/J$.
