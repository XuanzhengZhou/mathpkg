Prove that $\mathfrak{sl}(3, F)$ is simple, unless $\operatorname{char} F = 3$ (cf. Exercise 3). [Use the standard basis $h_1, h_2, e_{ij} (i \neq j)$. If $I \neq 0$ is an ideal, then $I$ is the direct sum of eigenspaces for $\operatorname{ad} h_1$ or $\operatorname{ad} h_2$; compare the eigenvalues of $\operatorname{ad} h_1$, $\operatorname{ad} h_2$ acting on the $e_{ij}$.]
