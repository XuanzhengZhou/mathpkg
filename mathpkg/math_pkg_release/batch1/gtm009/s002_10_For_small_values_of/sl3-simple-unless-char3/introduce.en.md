This exercise proves the simplicity of $\mathfrak{sl}(3, F)$ in all characteristics except $3$ (where scalar matrices of trace zero appear in the center). The proof uses the standard basis $\{h_1, h_2, e_{ij}\}$ and analyzes how a nonzero ideal $I$ decomposes into eigenspaces under the adjoint action of the diagonal subalgebra spanned by $h_1, h_2$. By comparing eigenvalues on the root vectors $e_{ij}$, one shows that any nonzero ideal must contain all of $\mathfrak{sl}(3, F)$.
