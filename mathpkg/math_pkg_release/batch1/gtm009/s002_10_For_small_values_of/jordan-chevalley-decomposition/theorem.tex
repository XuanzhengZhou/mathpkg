Let $V$ be a finite dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field $F$. For any $x \in \operatorname{End} V$, there exist unique elements $x_s, x_n \in \operatorname{End} V$ satisfying:
(a) $x = x_s + x_n$, with $x_s$ semisimple, $x_n$ nilpotent, and $[x_s, x_n] = 0$;
(b) $x_s$ and $x_n$ are polynomials in $x$ (with zero constant term);
(c) If $A \subset B \subset V$ are subspaces and $x$ maps $B$ into $A$, then $x_s$ and $x_n$ also map $B$ into $A$.
\end{theorem}
