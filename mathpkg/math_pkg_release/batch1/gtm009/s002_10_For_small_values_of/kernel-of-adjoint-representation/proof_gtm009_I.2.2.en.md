By definition, $\operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{ad} = \{x \in L \mid \operatorname{ad} x = 0\}$. Now $\operatorname{ad} x = 0$ as a linear endomorphism of $L$ means that $\operatorname{ad} x(y) = 0$ for all $y \in L$. But $\operatorname{ad} x(y) = [xy]$, so this condition is $[xy] = 0$ for all $y \in L$, which is precisely the defining condition for $Z(L)$. Therefore $\operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{ad} = Z(L)$.
