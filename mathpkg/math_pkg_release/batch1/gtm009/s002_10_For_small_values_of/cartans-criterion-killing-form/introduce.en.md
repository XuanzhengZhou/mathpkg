This corollary reformulates Cartan's Criterion for an abstract Lie algebra using the adjoint representation. If the Killing form-like expression $\operatorname{Tr}(\operatorname{ad} x \operatorname{ad} y)$ vanishes for $x \in [LL]$ and $y \in L$, then $L$ is solvable. Since $\operatorname{Ker}(\operatorname{ad}) = Z(L)$ is abelian (hence solvable), the solvability lifts from $\operatorname{ad} L$ to $L$. This version directly connects Cartan's Criterion to the Killing form, which is investigated in detail in Chapter II, Section 5.
