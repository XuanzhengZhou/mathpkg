The adjoint representation $\operatorname{ad}: L \to \mathfrak{gl}(L)$ maps each element $x \in L$ to the linear transformation $\operatorname{ad} x$ defined by $\operatorname{ad} x(y) = [xy]$. The verification that $\operatorname{ad}$ preserves the bracket uses the Jacobi identity and shows that $\operatorname{ad}$ is a Lie algebra homomorphism. The adjoint representation is the most fundamental representation of any Lie algebra, as it represents $L$ on itself via the bracket.
