Cartan's Criterion gives a powerful purely trace-theoretic condition for the solvability of a linear Lie algebra: if $\operatorname{Tr}(xy) = 0$ for all $x \in [LL]$ and $y \in L$, then $L$ is solvable. Combined with Engel's Theorem, the proof reduces to showing that $[LL]$ consists of nilpotent endomorphisms, using the trace lemma. The criterion is fundamental because it leads directly to the nondegeneracy characterization of semisimple Lie algebras via the Killing form.
