A representation of a Lie algebra $L$ is a Lie algebra homomorphism $\phi: L \to \mathfrak{gl}(V)$ into the general linear Lie algebra of a vector space $V$. This equips $V$ with the structure of an $L$-module, where each $x \in L$ acts as a linear transformation on $V$. Representations are the primary tool for studying Lie algebras through linear algebra, analogous to group representations in group theory.
