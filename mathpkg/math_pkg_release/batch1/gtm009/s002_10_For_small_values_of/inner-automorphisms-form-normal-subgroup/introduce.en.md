The group $\operatorname{Int} L$ of inner automorphisms is normal in the full automorphism group $\operatorname{Aut} L$. The normality follows from the conjugation formula $\phi(\operatorname{ad} x)\phi^{-1} = \operatorname{ad} \phi(x)$, which implies $\phi \exp(\operatorname{ad} x)\phi^{-1} = \exp(\operatorname{ad} \phi(x))$. Thus conjugating an inner automorphism by any automorphism yields another inner automorphism.
