Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra. Since $\operatorname{Der} L = \operatorname{ad} L$ and $\operatorname{ad}: L \to \operatorname{ad} L$ is an isomorphism, each $x \in L$ determines unique elements $s, n \in L$ such that:
$$\operatorname{ad} x = \operatorname{ad} s + \operatorname{ad} n$$
is the usual Jordan decomposition of $\operatorname{ad} x$ in $\operatorname{End} L$. Write $x = s + n$ with $[s, n] = 0$, where $s = x_s$ is \textbf{ad-semisimple} (i.e., $\operatorname{ad} s$ is semisimple) and $n = x_n$ is \textbf{ad-nilpotent}. These are called the \textbf{semisimple} and \textbf{nilpotent parts} of $x$.
