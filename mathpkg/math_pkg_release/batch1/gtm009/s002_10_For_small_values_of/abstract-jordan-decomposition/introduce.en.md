In a semisimple Lie algebra $L$, the isomorphism $L \cong \operatorname{ad} L$ allows one to transfer the Jordan-Chevalley decomposition from $\operatorname{End} L$ to $L$ itself. Each element $x \in L$ decomposes uniquely as $x = x_s + x_n$ where $x_s$ is ad-semisimple, $x_n$ is ad-nilpotent, and $[x_s, x_n] = 0$. This abstract Jordan decomposition generalizes the usual Jordan decomposition of linear transformations and is consistent with it when $L$ is a linear Lie algebra.
