In characteristic 2, the simple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{sl}(2, F)$ becomes nilpotent (and hence solvable). This exercise demonstrates how the characteristic of the base field fundamentally alters the structure of Lie algebras. The standard basis $(x, y, h)$ with relations $[h, x] = 2x$, $[h, y] = -2y$, $[x, y] = h$ collapses when $2 = 0$ in $F$, making the algebra nilpotent rather than simple.
