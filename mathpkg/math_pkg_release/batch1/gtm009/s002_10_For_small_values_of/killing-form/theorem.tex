Let $L$ be any Lie algebra. If $x, y \in L$, define
$$\kappa(x, y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\operatorname{ad} x \operatorname{ad} y).$$
Then $\kappa$ is a symmetric bilinear form on $L$, called the \text{Killing form}. $\kappa$ is also \text{associative}, i.e., $\kappa([xy], z) = \kappa(x, [yz])$ for all $x, y, z \in L$.
