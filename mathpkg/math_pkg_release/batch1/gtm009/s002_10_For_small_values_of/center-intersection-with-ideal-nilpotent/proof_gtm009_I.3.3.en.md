$L$ acts on $K$ via the adjoint representation, and by hypothesis each $\operatorname{ad}_K x$ is nilpotent. Applying Engel's Theorem (Theorem 3.3) to the image of $L$ in $\mathfrak{gl}(K)$ yields a nonzero $x \in K$ killed by $L$, i.e., $[L, x] = 0$. This means $x \in K \cap Z(L)$, which is therefore nonzero.
