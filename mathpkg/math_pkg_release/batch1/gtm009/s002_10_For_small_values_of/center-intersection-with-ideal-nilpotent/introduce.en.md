This corollary of Engel's Theorem states that if a Lie algebra $L$ acts nilpotently on an ideal $K$ via the adjoint representation, then $K$ must intersect the center $Z(L)$ nontrivially. It is a key technical tool used throughout the structure theory of Lie algebras, particularly in proving Cartan's criterion for solvability.
