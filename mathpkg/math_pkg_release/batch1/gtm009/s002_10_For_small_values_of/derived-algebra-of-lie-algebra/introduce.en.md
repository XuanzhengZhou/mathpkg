The derived algebra $[LL]$ of a Lie algebra $L$ is the subspace spanned by all brackets $[x, y]$ with $x, y \in L$. It is always an ideal of $L$ and plays the role of the commutator subgroup in the Lie algebra setting. The quotient $L / [LL]$ is the largest abelian quotient of $L$, and $L$ is abelian if and only if $[LL] = 0$.
