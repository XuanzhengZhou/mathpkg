An inner automorphism of a Lie algebra $L$ is one of the form $\exp(\operatorname{ad} x)$ where $\operatorname{ad} x$ is nilpotent. More generally, for any nilpotent derivation $\delta$ of an $F$-algebra, the exponential series $\exp \delta = 1 + \delta + \delta^2/2! + \cdots$ terminates and defines an automorphism. The Leibniz rule for the exponential follows from the standard expansion and the nilpotency guarantees convergence of the series.
