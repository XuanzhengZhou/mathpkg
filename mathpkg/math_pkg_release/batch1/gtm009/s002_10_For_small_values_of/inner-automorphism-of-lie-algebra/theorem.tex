If $\delta$ is a nilpotent derivation of an $F$-algebra $\mathfrak{A}$, then $\exp \delta$ is an automorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$:
$$\exp \delta(x) \exp \delta(y) = \exp \delta(xy).$$
The fact that $\exp \delta$ is invertible follows by exhibiting the explicit inverse $1 - \eta + \eta^2 - \cdots \pm \eta^{k-1}$, where $\exp \delta = 1 + \eta$.

An automorphism of the form $\exp(\operatorname{ad} x)$, with $\operatorname{ad} x$ nilpotent, is called \text{inner}.
