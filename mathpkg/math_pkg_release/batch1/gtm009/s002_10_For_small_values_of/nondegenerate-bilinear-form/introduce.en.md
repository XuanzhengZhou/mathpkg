A bilinear form is nondegenerate if no nonzero vector pairs to zero with all vectors. Equivalently, the associated matrix (with respect to any basis) has nonzero determinant. For the Killing form on a Lie algebra, nondegeneracy is equivalent (by Cartan's criterion) to semisimplicity, making it one of the most important computational tests in Lie theory. The radical of an associative bilinear form on a Lie algebra is always an ideal.
