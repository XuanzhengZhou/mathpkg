A symmetric bilinear form $\beta(x, y)$ on a vector space $V$ is called \text{nondegenerate} if its radical $S$ is $0$, where
$$S = \{x \in V \mid \beta(x, y) = 0 \text{ for all } y \in V\}.$$
A practical way to test nondegeneracy: Fix a basis $x_1, \ldots, x_n$ of $V$. Then $\beta$ is nondegenerate if and only if the $n \times n$ matrix whose $i, j$ entry is $\beta(x_i, x_j)$ has nonzero determinant.
