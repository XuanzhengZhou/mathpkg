The normalizer $N_L(K)$ of a subspace $K$ of a Lie algebra $L$ is the set of elements $x \in L$ such that $[x, K] \subseteq K$. The Jacobi identity ensures that $N_L(K)$ is a subalgebra of $L$. When $K$ itself is a subalgebra, $N_L(K)$ is the largest subalgebra containing $K$ as an ideal, analogous to the normalizer of a subgroup in group theory.
