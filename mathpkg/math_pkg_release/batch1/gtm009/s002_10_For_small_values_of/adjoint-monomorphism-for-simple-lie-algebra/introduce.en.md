If $L$ is a simple Lie algebra, then it has no nontrivial ideals, which forces $Z(L) = 0$. Since $\operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{ad} = Z(L)$, the adjoint representation is injective, embedding $L$ as a subalgebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(L)$. This justifies the study of abstract Lie algebras within the concrete framework of linear Lie algebras — every simple Lie algebra is isomorphic to a linear one via its adjoint representation.
