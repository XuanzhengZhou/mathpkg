This exercise determines the centers of $\mathfrak{gl}(n, F)$ and $\mathfrak{sl}(n, F)$. The center of $\mathfrak{gl}(n, F)$ consists precisely of scalar multiples of the identity matrix. For $\mathfrak{sl}(n, F)$, the center is trivial except when the characteristic of $F$ divides $n$, in which case the scalar matrices (which have trace zero in this case) form the center.
