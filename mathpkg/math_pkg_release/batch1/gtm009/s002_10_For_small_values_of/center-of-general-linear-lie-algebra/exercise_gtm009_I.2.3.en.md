Prove that the center of $\mathfrak{gl}(n, F)$ equals $\mathfrak{s}(n, F)$ (the scalar matrices). Prove that $\mathfrak{sl}(n, F)$ has center $0$, unless $\operatorname{char} F$ divides $n$, in which case the center is $\mathfrak{s}(n, F)$.
