The nonabelian 2-dimensional Lie algebra (with basis $x, y$ and bracket $[x, y] = x$) provides the simplest example of a Lie algebra that is solvable but not nilpotent. Its derived algebra is 1-dimensional and abelian, so the second derived algebra vanishes, giving solvability. However, repeatedly bracketing with $x$ never reaches zero, showing it is not nilpotent. This illustrates that solvability is a strictly weaker condition than nilpotency.
