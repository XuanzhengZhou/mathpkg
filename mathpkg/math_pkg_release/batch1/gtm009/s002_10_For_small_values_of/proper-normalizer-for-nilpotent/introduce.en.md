This exercise is known as the "normalizer growth" property of nilpotent Lie algebras: every proper subalgebra is strictly contained in its normalizer. This is the Lie algebra analogue of the corresponding property for nilpotent groups, and it plays a crucial role in the proof of Engel's Theorem itself. The property fails for solvable algebras that are not nilpotent.
