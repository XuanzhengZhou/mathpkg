Engel's Theorem is one of the fundamental structure theorems for Lie algebras. It states that if $L \subseteq \mathfrak{gl}(V)$ consists entirely of nilpotent endomorphisms, then there is a common eigenvector annihilated by the whole algebra. This is the Lie algebra analogue of the theorem that a finite group of unipotent matrices can be simultaneously triangularized. The theorem forms the foundation for the theory of nilpotent Lie algebras and is the counterpart to Lie's Theorem for solvable algebras.
