A Baer subplane is a special type of subplane of a projective plane. It is named after Reinhold Baer, who studied projective planes extensively. In a finite projective plane, a subplane is a Baer subplane precisely when its order satisfies a specific numerical relationship with the order of the ambient plane, namely that the ambient order is the square of the subplane order. Baer subplanes play a fundamental role in the structure theory of finite projective planes.
