Given a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ and a distinguished line $l$, the derived affine plane $\mathcal{P}^l$ is obtained by removing the line $l$ (thought of as the "line at infinity") along with all points incident to it. The resulting structure satisfies the axioms of an affine plane: any two points determine a unique line, and given a point and a line not through it, there is a unique parallel line through the point. This construction shows that every projective plane naturally gives rise to many affine planes, one for each choice of the deleted line.
