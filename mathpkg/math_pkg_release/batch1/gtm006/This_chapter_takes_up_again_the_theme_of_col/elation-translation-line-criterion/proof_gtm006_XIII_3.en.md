The proof combines Lemmas 13.6 and 13.7 with Corollary 2 of Theorem 4.26.

By condition (i), $\Gamma_{(l,l)} \neq 1$, so there exists a non-trivial collineation fixing $l$ pointwise. By condition (ii), for each point $A$ on $l$, there exists a non-trivial elation $\alpha_A \in \Gamma$ with centre $A$ and some axis $a \neq l$ through $A$.

These elations, for all $A \in l$, have centres on $l$. By Lemma 13.6, all such elation flags belong to a non-trivial connected set. By Lemma 13.7, all these elations therefore have the same prime order $p$.

Now Lemma 13.6 and the structure of the connected set, together with Corollary 2 of Theorem 4.26 (which characterizes translation lines in terms of elations), imply that the group generated by all these elations is the full group of elations with axis $l$. Thus $l$ is a translation line.
