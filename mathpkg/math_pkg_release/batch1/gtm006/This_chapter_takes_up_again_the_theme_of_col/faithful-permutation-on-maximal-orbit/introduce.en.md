Theorem 13.1 establishes that for any finite collineation group of a projective plane, the induced permutation action on every orbit of maximal length is faithful. Equivalently, the subgroup fixing every point of a maximal orbit is trivial. The proof proceeds by contradiction, assuming a non-trivial elementwise stabilizer of a maximal orbit and then constructing a larger orbit, violating maximality. This theorem is fundamental to the analysis of collineation groups of finite projective planes, guaranteeing that the group structure is reflected in its action on at least one orbit.
