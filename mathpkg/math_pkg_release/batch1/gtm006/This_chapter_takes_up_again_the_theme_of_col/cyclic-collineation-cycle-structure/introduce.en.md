The corollary to Theorem 13.3 states that for a cyclic collineation group $\Pi = \langle \pi \rangle$ of a finite projective plane, the permutation representation on points $\Pi(P)$ and on lines $\Pi(l)$ have identical cycle structures -- that is, for each cycle length $x$, the number of point cycles of length $x$ equals the number of line cycles of length $x$. This follows from applying Theorem 13.3 to each power $\pi^k$ of the generator. The result is false for non-cyclic collineation groups, as demonstrated by the translation group of a translation plane.
