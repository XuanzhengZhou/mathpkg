For an abelian group $\Gamma$ acting on a set $\mathcal{S}$, since every subgroup of $\Gamma$ is normal, $\Gamma$ acts regularly on each of its orbits. Thus an orbit of $\Gamma$ is faithful if and only if its length is equal to the order $|\Gamma|$.

Now let $\alpha$ be a collineation of a finite projective plane of order $n$, and let $\Pi = \langle \alpha \rangle$ be the cyclic group generated by $\alpha$. Then $\Pi$ is abelian, so by the observation above, a faithful orbit of $\Pi$ must have length equal to $|\Pi| = t$, the order of $\alpha$.

By Corollary 1 of Theorem 13.1, $\Pi$ has at least one faithful orbit. The length of any orbit of $\Pi$ on the points of the plane cannot exceed the total number of points, which is $n^2 + n + 1$. Therefore $t = |\Pi| = \text{length of a faithful orbit} \leq n^2 + n + 1$.
