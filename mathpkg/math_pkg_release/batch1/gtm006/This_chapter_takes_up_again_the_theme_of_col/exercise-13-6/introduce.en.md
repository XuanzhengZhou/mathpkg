Exercise 13.6 asks to prove that for a finite affine plane obtained by removing the line at infinity from a projective plane, a collineation group is transitive on affine lines if and only if it is transitive on affine points. This is an affine analogue of the corollary to Theorem 13.4, and follows by considering the extension of the group to the full projective plane and applying the Orbit Theorem results.
