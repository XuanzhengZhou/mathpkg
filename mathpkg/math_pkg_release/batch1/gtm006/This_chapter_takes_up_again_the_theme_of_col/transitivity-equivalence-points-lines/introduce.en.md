The corollary to the Orbit Theorem (Theorem 13.4) gives a clean equivalence: a collineation group of a finite projective plane is transitive on points if and only if it is transitive on lines. Transitivity on points means $\Pi$ has exactly one point orbit, so by Theorem 13.4 it must also have exactly one line orbit, and vice versa. This is a special case of the general equality of orbit counts and provides a useful structural check in the study of collineation groups.
