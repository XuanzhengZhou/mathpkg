Theorem 13.3 states that any collineation of a finite projective plane fixes equally many points and lines. The proof uses Lemma 13.2 to represent the collineation by permutation matrices $P$ and $Q$ satisfying $PA = AQ$, where $A$ is the non-singular incidence matrix. The number of fixed points is the trace of $P$ and the number of fixed lines is the trace of $Q$. Since $P = AQA^{-1}$, the traces are equal. This result does not generalize to arbitrary collineation groups, but holds for cyclic groups.
