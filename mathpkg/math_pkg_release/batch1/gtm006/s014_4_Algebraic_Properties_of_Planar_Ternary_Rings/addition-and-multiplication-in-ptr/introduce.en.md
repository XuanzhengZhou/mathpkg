In a planar ternary ring $(R, T)$, the ternary operation $T$ can be specialized to define two binary operations: addition and multiplication. Setting the third argument of $T$ to $0$ yields multiplication, while fixing the first argument as $1$ and the second as the first summand yields addition. These derived operations endow $R$ and $R^*$ (the nonzero elements) with the structure of loops, as established by Theorem 5.3.
