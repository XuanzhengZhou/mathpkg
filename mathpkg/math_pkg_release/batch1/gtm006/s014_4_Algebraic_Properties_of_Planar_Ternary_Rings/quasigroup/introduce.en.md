A quasigroup is an algebraic structure with a binary operation in which the equation $a \cdot x = b$ always has a unique solution in $x$, and $y \cdot a = b$ always has a unique solution in $y$. Equivalently, the operation table of a finite quasigroup is a Latin square. A quasigroup that also possesses an identity element is called a loop.
