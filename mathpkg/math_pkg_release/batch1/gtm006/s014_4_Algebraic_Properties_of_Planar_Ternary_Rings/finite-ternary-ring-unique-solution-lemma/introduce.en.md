Lemma 5.6 is a technical counting lemma used in the proof of Theorem 5.5. In a finite ternary ring satisfying condition (D), the lemma upgrades the "at most one" property of certain equations to "exactly one." The proof constructs a mapping $A: R \to R$ via condition (D) and uses finiteness to deduce that injectivity implies surjectivity, hence the equation always has a solution.
