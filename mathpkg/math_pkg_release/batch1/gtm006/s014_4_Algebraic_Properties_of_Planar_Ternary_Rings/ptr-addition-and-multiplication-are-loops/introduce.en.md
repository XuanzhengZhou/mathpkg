Theorem 5.3 establishes that the binary addition and multiplication operations derived from a planar ternary ring $(R, T)$ each satisfy the loop axioms. The additive loop $(R, +)$ has $0$ as its identity, while the multiplicative loop $(R^*, \cdot)$ on the nonzero elements has $1$ as its identity. This theorem is fundamental because it connects the geometric notion of a projective plane (via its coordinatizing PTR) to algebraic loop theory.
