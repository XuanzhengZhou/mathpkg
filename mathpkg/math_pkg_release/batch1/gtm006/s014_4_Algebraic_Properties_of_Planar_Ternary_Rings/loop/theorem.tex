A non-empty set $G$ with a binary operation $\cdot$ is called a \textbf{loop} if:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $a \cdot x = b$ has a unique solution in $x$ for any $a, b \in G$.
\item[(ii)] $y \cdot a = b$ has a unique solution in $y$ for any $a, b \in G$.
\item[(iii)] $G$ has an element $e$ such that $e \cdot x = x \cdot e = x$ for all $x \in G$ (the element $e$ is called an \textit{identity} of $G$).
\end{enumerate}
