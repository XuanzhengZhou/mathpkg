Theorem 5.5 is a powerful structural result about subrings of planar ternary rings. It states that every finite non-empty ternary subring of a PTR is either trivial (containing only the zero element) or itself planar. This contrasts with the infinite case, where Exercise 5.4 provides a counterexample: the integers form a non-planar ternary subring of the rationals. The proof relies on Lemma 5.6 and the fact that the coefficient $1$ must belong to any nontrivial finite subring.
