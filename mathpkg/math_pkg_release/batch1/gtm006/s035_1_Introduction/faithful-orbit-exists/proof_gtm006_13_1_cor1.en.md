By Theorem 13.1, the collineation group $\Pi$ acts faithfully on every orbit of maximal length. Since $\Pi$ is finite, there exists at least one orbit of maximal length. Hence $\Pi$ acts faithfully on that orbit. Therefore $\Pi$ induces a faithful permutation group on at least one orbit.
