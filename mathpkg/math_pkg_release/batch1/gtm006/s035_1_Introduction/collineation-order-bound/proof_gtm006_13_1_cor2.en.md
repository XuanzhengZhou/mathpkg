If $\Gamma$ is an abelian group on a set $\mathcal{S}$ then, since every subgroup of $\Gamma$ is normal, $\Gamma$ acts regularly on each of its orbits. Thus an orbit of $\Gamma$ is faithful if and only if its length is equal to the order of $|\Gamma|$.

Let $\alpha$ be a collineation of order $t$ and let $\Pi = \langle \alpha \rangle$ be the cyclic group generated by $\alpha$. Then $\Pi$ is abelian. By Corollary 1 (to Theorem 13.1), $\Pi$ has at least one faithful orbit. Since $\Pi$ is abelian, it acts regularly on this orbit, so the orbit length equals $|\Pi| = t$. But the total number of points in the plane is $n^2 + n + 1$, so any orbit has length at most $n^2 + n + 1$. Hence $t \leq n^2 + n + 1$.
