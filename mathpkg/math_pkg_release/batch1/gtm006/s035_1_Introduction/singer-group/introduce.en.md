A Singer group is the projective analogue of a regular representation: the group acts transitively on points and lines with trivial stabilizers, so $|\Pi| = |\mathcal{P}| = |\mathcal{L}|$. For the Desarguesian plane $PG(2,q)$, the cyclic group of order $q^2+q+1$ generated by a Singer cycle is a Singer group. Singer groups are closely related to difference sets and provide a powerful tool for constructing and analyzing finite projective planes.
