Theorem 13.1 establishes a key property of finite collineation groups acting on projective planes: on any orbit of maximal length, the induced permutation group is faithful. This means that the subgroup of elements fixing every point in such an orbit is trivial. The theorem is fundamental for analyzing the orbit structure of collineation groups and underlies several important corollaries about faithful orbits and bounds on collineation orders.
