A configuration $\mathcal{C}$ is confined if it is contained in some finite subconfiguration of the projective plane. This lemma shows that confinement is preserved under any collineation $\alpha$ of the ambient plane $\mathcal{P}$: $\mathcal{C}$ is confined exactly when its image $\mathcal{C}^\alpha$ under $\alpha$ is confined.
