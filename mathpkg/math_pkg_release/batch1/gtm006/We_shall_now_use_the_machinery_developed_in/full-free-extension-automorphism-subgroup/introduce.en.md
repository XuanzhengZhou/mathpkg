If a configuration $\mathcal{B}$ is obtained from $\mathcal{A}$ by a full free extension, then every collineation of $\mathcal{A}$ can be extended to a collineation of $\mathcal{B}$. This yields a subgroup of the automorphism group of $\mathcal{B}$ that is isomorphic to the full automorphism group of $\mathcal{A}$. The proof requires both fullness (to guarantee that images of non-incident elements meet) and freeness (to guarantee that new elements have exactly two incidences).
