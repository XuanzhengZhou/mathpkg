Let $F = \mathrm{GF}(p^n)$ where $p$ is an odd prime and $n > 1$. Let $a \in F$ be any element which is not a square in $F$. Let $\theta$ be the automorphism of $F$ given by $x^\theta = x^{p^r}$ for some $1 \leq r < n$. Define a two-dimensional vector space $D$ over $F$ with basis $\{1, \lambda\}$, and define multiplication by:
$$
(x + \lambda y)(z + \lambda t) = (xz + a y^\theta t^\theta) + \lambda(yz + xt),
$$
with the natural addition. Then $D$ is called a \emph{Dickson commutative division ring}.
