In the finite case, the associativity condition for André quasifields simplifies considerably. Since the Galois group of a finite extension is cyclic, every anti-homomorphism is automatically a homomorphism. Moreover, for finite fields, the norm map is surjective onto $K^*$, so $N = K^*$. Thus a finite André quasifield is associative precisely when the defining map $\phi$ is a group homomorphism from the multiplicative group of the subfield into the Galois group.
