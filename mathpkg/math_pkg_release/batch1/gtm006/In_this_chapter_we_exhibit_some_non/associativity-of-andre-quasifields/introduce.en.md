This theorem characterizes exactly when an André quasifield is associative (i.e., is a nearfield). The associativity condition translates into the requirement that the composite mapping $\alpha = \nu \phi$ be an anti-homomorphism from $F^*$ into the Galois group $\Gamma$. Since $\nu$ is a homomorphism, this is equivalent to requiring that $\phi$ be an anti-homomorphism from $N$ into $\Gamma$. The proof follows from comparing the two ways of associating a triple product under the twisted multiplication.
