Let $F_\phi$ be an André quasifield constructed from a field $F$ with Galois group $\Gamma = \operatorname{Gal}(F/K)$ and a mapping $\phi: N \to \Gamma$, where $N = \nu(F^*)$. Then $F_\phi$ is associative if and only if the mapping $\alpha: F^* \to \Gamma$ defined by $\alpha_x = \nu(x)\phi(\nu(x))$ is an anti-homomorphism, i.e., $\alpha_{xy} = \alpha_y \alpha_x$ for all $x, y \in F^*$.
