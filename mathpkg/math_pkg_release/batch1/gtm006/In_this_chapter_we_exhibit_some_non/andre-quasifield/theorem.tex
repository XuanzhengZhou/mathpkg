Let $F$ be a field and let $K$ be a subfield of $F$ such that $F$ is a finite Galois extension of $K$. Let $\Gamma = \operatorname{Gal}(F/K)$ and let $\nu: F^* \to \Gamma$ be the natural homomorphism given by the norm map. Set $N = \nu(F^*) \subseteq \Gamma$. Let $\phi: N \to \Gamma$ be any mapping that sends the identity of $F^*$ to the identity of $\Gamma$. Define a new multiplication $\odot$ on the set $F$ (with the same addition) by:
$$
x \odot y = x \cdot y^{\nu(x)\phi(\nu(x))}, \qquad 0 \odot y = 0,
$$
for all $x, y \in F$, $x \neq 0$, where the superscript denotes the action of the Galois automorphism. The resulting algebraic system $F_\phi$ is called an \emph{AndrĂŠ quasifield}.
