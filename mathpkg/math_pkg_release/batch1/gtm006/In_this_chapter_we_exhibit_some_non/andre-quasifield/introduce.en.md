An André quasifield is a quasifield constructed by twisting the multiplication of a field $F$ using a Galois automorphism that depends on the element being multiplied. The construction starts from a finite Galois extension $F/K$ and a mapping $\phi$ from the image of the norm map into the Galois group. By redefining multiplication as $x \odot y = x y^{\sigma_x}$ where $\sigma_x$ is a Galois automorphism depending on $x$, one obtains a system that satisfies the left distributive law. André quasifields generalize the Hall quasifields and provide a rich class of examples of non-Desarguesian projective planes, known as André planes.
