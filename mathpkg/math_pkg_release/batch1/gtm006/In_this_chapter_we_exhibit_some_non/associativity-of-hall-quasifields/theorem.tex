Let $H$ be the Hall quasifield over a field $F$ defined by the irreducible quadratic $f(s) = s^2 - as - b$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item If $F = \mathrm{GF}(2)$, then $H$ is a field.
\item If $F = \mathrm{GF}(3)$ and $f(s) = s^2 + 1$, then $H$ is associative (and is a nearfield of order 9).
\item In all other cases where the Hall quasifield is defined, $H$ is not associative.
\end{enumerate}
