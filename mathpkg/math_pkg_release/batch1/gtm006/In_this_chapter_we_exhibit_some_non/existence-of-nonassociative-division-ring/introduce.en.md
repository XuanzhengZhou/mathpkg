Theorem 9.10 establishes the existence of non-associative division rings of finite order (which are not fields). Combined with Lemma 9.9, this shows that for any non-prime finite field, the construction yields a genuinely non-associative division ring. This is significant because it provides examples of finite non-associative division rings, which coordinatize non-Desarguesian projective planes. The proof constructs appropriate parameters $a$, $b$, and $\theta$ explicitly using the Galois structure of finite fields.
