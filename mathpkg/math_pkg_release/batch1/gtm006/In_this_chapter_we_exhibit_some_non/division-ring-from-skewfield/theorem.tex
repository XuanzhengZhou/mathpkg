Let $F$ be a skewfield with centre $Z$ and let $\theta$ be an anti-automorphism of $F$ of finite order $m$. Let $a, b \in F$ be elements such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ has finite dimension over $Z$ and $x x^\theta \in Z$ for all $x \in F$;
\item The equation $a = x^{1+\theta} + xb$ has no solution for $x \in F$.
\end{enumerate}
Define a two-dimensional vector space $D$ over $F$ with basis $\{1, \lambda\}$. Define multiplication on $D$ by:
$$
(x + \lambda y)(z + \lambda t) = (xz + a t y^\theta) + \lambda(z y + x^\theta t + y^\theta b t),
$$
with the natural addition $(x + \lambda y) + (z + \lambda t) = (x+z) + \lambda(y+t)$. Then $D$ is a (not necessarily associative) division ring.
