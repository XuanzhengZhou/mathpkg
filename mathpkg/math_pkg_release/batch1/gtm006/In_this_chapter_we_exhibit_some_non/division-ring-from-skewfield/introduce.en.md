This construction generalizes the classical Cayley-Dickson process and provides a general method for building division rings that need not be associative. Starting from a skewfield $F$ with an anti-automorphism $\theta$, one builds a two-dimensional algebra $D$ over $F$. Condition (1) ensures that the norm $x x^\theta$ lies in the centre, while condition (2) guarantees the absence of zero divisors. Important special cases include the quaternions (when $F$ is a field, $\theta$ is the identity, $a = -1$, $b = 0$) and the Cayley-Dickson algebra (when $F$ is the quaternions, $a = -1$, $b = 0$, $\theta$ is quaternionic conjugation).
