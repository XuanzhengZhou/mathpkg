Lemma 9.9 is a striking result: in the finite case, the construction never produces a non-trivial associative division ring. If $\theta \neq 1$, the resulting algebra $D$ is necessarily non-associative. This means that for finite fields, the construction genuinely yields non-associative division rings (such as the finite Cayley-Dickson algebras). The proof exploits the fact that in a finite field, any anti-automorphism is actually an automorphism, and analyzes the associator formula.
