Theorem 9.12 establishes the fundamental properties of the Dickson commutative division ring: it is always commutative and never associative. The commutativity is evident from the symmetric definition of multiplication. The proof that $D$ is a division ring uses the fact that $a$ is a non-square to show the absence of zero divisors. The non-associativity is a consequence of the non-trivial automorphism $\theta$, similar to Lemma 9.9. This theorem provides an infinite family of finite non-associative commutative division rings, demonstrating that commutativity does not imply associativity in the absence of other axioms.
