A Hall quasifield is a quasifield constructed from a field $F$ and an irreducible quadratic polynomial $f(s) = s^2 - as - b$ over $F$. It is defined on a two-dimensional vector space $H$ over $F$ with basis $\{1, \lambda\}$, where the multiplication is specially designed so that every element not in $F$ satisfies $f(\alpha)=0$. The Hall quasifield provides one of the fundamental examples of non-Desarguesian projective planes. The construction was introduced by Marshall Hall Jr. and demonstrates that translation planes exist which are not coordinatized by skewfields.
