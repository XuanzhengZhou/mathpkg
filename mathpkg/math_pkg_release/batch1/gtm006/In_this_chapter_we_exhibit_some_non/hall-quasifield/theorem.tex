Let $F$ be a field and $f(s) = s^2 - as - b$ an irreducible quadratic over $F$. Let $H$ be a two-dimensional right vector space over $F$, with basis elements $1$ and $\lambda$, so that $H$ consists of all elements of the form $x + \lambda y$ as $x$ and $y$ vary over $F$. Define a multiplication on $H$ as follows:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{If } y \neq 0, \quad (x + \lambda y)(z + \lambda t) &= xz - y^{-1}t f(x) + \lambda(yz - xt + at), \\
\text{If } y = 0, \quad x(z + \lambda t) &= xz + \lambda(xt).
\end{aligned}
$$
Then $H$ is a quasifield, called a \emph{Hall quasifield}, with the properties:
\begin{itemize}
\item Every element $\alpha \in H \setminus F$ satisfies the quadratic equation $f(\alpha) = 0$;
\item $F$ is contained in the kernel of $H$;
\item Every element of $F$ commutes with all elements of $H$.
\end{itemize}
