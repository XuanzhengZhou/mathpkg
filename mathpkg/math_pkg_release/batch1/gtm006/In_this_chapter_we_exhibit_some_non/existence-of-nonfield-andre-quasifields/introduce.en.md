This theorem gives a complete characterization of when non-trivial (i.e., non-field) André quasifields can be constructed from a finite field $\mathrm{GF}(p^n)$. The proof is constructive: for odd characteristic, one maps $-1$ to a non-identity element of the Galois group. For characteristic 2, one needs a proper intermediate subfield to define a non-trivial mapping. The theorem demonstrates that non-Desarguesian André planes exist for almost all prime power orders, with the only exceptions being fields of prime order (where there are no non-trivial Galois automorphisms) and fields of order $2^p$ with $p$ prime.
