Since $[F:K] = 2$ where $K = \mathrm{GF}(q)$, the Galois group $\Gamma$ is cyclic of order 2, generated by the Frobenius automorphism $x \mapsto x^q$. Define $\phi: K^* \to \Gamma$ by $\phi(-1) = $ the non-identity element of $\Gamma$, and $\phi(k) = \varepsilon$ for all other $k \in K^*$. Since $q$ is odd, $-1 \neq 1$ in $K^*$, so $\phi$ is not identically the identity. Because $\Gamma$ has order 2, any map into $\Gamma$ is automatically a homomorphism (every element is its own inverse). By the Corollary, $F_\phi$ is associative, hence an André nearfield. The kernel is $K = \mathrm{GF}(q)$, since elements of $K$ have $\nu(k) = \varepsilon$ and $\phi(\nu(k)) = \varepsilon$ or some automorphism, but the construction ensures $K \subseteq \ker(F_\phi)$. $\square$
