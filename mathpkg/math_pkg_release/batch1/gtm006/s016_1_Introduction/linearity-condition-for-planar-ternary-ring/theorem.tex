Let $(R, T)$ be a planar ternary ring coordinatizing a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$.
Then $(R, T)$ is linear if and only if whenever two triangles are given such that
the two pairs of corresponding sides which contain points of $[0]$ meet on $[\infty]$,
one of the remaining third sides passes through $(0)$ if and only if the final side does.
