The right inverse property (RIP) is an algebraic condition satisfied by certain loops. A loop $G$ has RIP if for every element $x \in G$ there exists a two-sided inverse $x^{-1}$ such that right-multiplication by $x^{-1}$ cancels right-multiplication by $x$. This property generalizes the familiar inverse behavior from group theory to the broader setting of loops and plays a central role in the study of division rings that coordinatize projective planes.
