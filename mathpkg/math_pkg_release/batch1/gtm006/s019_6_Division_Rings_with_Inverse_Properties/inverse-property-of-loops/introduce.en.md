A loop is said to have the inverse property (IP) if it satisfies both the right inverse property (RIP) and the left inverse property (LIP). In the context of planar ternary rings that coordinatize projective planes, a division ring with IP is alternative (Lemma 6.13), and the combination of RIP and LIP characterizes projective planes that are Moufang planes.
