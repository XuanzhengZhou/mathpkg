Lemma 6.10 establishes that in any loop possessing either the right inverse property (RIP) or the left inverse property (LIP), the distinguished element $x^{-1}$ acts as a genuine two-sided inverse. That is, $x^{-1}x = 1 = xx^{-1}$ for all $x$, and consequently $(x^{-1})^{-1} = x$. This lemma justifies that RIP and LIP each independently force the element $x^{-1}$ to behave in the familiar way known from group theory, even though loops need not be associative.
