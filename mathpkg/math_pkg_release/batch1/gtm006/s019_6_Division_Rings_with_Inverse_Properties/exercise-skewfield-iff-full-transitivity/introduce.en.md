Exercise 6.4 asks the reader to prove that a planar ternary ring $R$ is a skewfield (a division ring with associative multiplication) if and only if the coordinatized projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is $(V,l)$-transitive for every flag $(V,l)$. This is the ultimate strengthening of the transitivity conditions studied throughout Chapter VI, placing skewfields at the top of the algebraic hierarchy that corresponds to maximally transitive projective planes.
