Since $R$ has RIP, every line through $(\infty)$ is a translation line (by the geometric implications of RIP, established earlier in the chapter). If $R$ also has LIP, then by Theorem 6.14 the plane $\mathcal{P}$ is $((0,0),[0,0])$-transitive, which means $[0,0]$ is a translation line. Now $\mathcal{P}$ has three non-concurrent translation lines (two through $(\infty)$ and $[0,0]$ through $(0)$). By Theorem 4.20, this implies that every line of $\mathcal{P}$ is a translation line — which is precisely the definition of a Moufang plane.

Conversely, if $\mathcal{P}$ is a Moufang plane, then in particular it is $((0,0),[0,0])$-transitive, so by Theorem 6.14, $R$ has LIP.
