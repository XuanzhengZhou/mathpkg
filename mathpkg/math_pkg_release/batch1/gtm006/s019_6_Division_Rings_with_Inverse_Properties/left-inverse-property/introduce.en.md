The left inverse property (LIP) is an algebraic condition satisfied by certain loops. A loop $G$ has LIP if for every element $x \in G$ there exists a two-sided inverse $x^{-1}$ such that left-multiplication by $x^{-1}$ cancels left-multiplication by $x$. Together with the right inverse property (RIP), LIP defines the full inverse property (IP), which is central to the algebraic characterization of alternative division rings and Moufang planes.
