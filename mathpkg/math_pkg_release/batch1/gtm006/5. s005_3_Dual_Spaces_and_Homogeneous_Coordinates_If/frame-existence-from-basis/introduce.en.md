A frame is the projective-geometric analogue of a basis for a vector space. Given a basis $e_1,\dots,e_{n+1}$ of an $(n+1)$-dimensional left vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$, one obtains $n+2$ points in $\mathcal{P}(V)$: the $n+1$ one-dimensional subspaces generated by each basis vector, together with the point generated by the sum of all basis vectors. This ordered set of $n+2$ points constitutes a frame, meaning no $n+1$ of them lie in a hyperplane. Frames play a central role in coordinatizing projective geometries.
