Since $e_1, \dots, e_{n+1}$ is a basis for $V$, any $n+1$ of the vectors $e_1, \dots, e_{n+1}, e_1+\cdots+e_{n+1}$ are linearly independent. Indeed, if we omit $e_i$, the remaining $n$ basis vectors together with $e_1+\cdots+e_{n+1}$ clearly span an $n$-dimensional subspace whose coefficient for $e_i$ is non-zero (it is 1), so they are linearly independent. Therefore no $n+1$ of the points $E_1,\dots,E_{n+2}$ lie in a hyperplane, which is exactly the defining property of a frame for $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of $g$-dimension $n$.
