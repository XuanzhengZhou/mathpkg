Let $A_1, A_2, A_3$ be three distinct points on a line $W$. Choose points $P$ and $Q$ not on $W$ such that $A_1, A_2$ lie on $PQ$. Let $R = A_1 + Q \cap A_2 + P$ and let $S = A_3 + R \cap W$, etc. Working through the coordinate computation with an appropriately chosen basis yields that the constructed point $A_4$ has parametric coordinate $-1$ in the frame $A_1, A_2, A_3$, so $(A_1, A_2; A_3, A_4) = -1$.

If $K$ is a field, then the cross-ratio is $-1$, as claimed. From this theorem, the particular cross-ratio $-1$ has special geometric significance.
