We shall show that $\alpha$ can be extended to a non-singular semi-linear transformation of $V$ in a number of ways. In fact for any $e$ not in $W_1$ and any $f$ not in $W_2$, $\alpha$ can be extended to a non-singular semi-linear transformation $\alpha^*$ sending $e$ onto $f$. Since $V$ has dimension three and $W_1, W_2$ both have dimension two, any vector of $V$ has unique representations of the form $w_1 + x e$ and $w_2 + y f$ where $w_1 \in W_1, w_2 \in W_2$. It is now easy to see that the mapping $\alpha^*$ defined by $(w_1 + x e)^{\alpha^*} = w_1^\alpha + x^\phi f$, where $\phi$ is the automorphism of $K$ associated with $\alpha$, is a non-singular semi-linear transformation of $V$ extending $\alpha$.
