A correlation $\beta$ is a polarity (i.e., $\beta^2 = 1$) if and only if the associated automorphism is an involution ($\phi^2 = 1$) and the matrix satisfies a compatibility condition with $\phi$. When $\phi = 1$, we have $A$ symmetric ($k = 1$) or skew-symmetric ($k = -1$), leading to orthogonal polarities. When $\phi \neq 1$, we have unitary (hermitian) polarities.
