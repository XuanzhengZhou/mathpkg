This theorem links the algebraic notion of harmonic conjugates to the group-theoretic notion of involutions. In characteristic not two, the unique involution in $\operatorname{PGL}(V)$ fixing two distinct points $A$ and $B$ is the map $x \mapsto -x$ in the coordinate frame where $A = \infty$ and $B = 0$. This involution interchanges precisely the pairs of harmonic conjugates with respect to $A$ and $B$.
