Choose a frame $A, B, X$ for $\mathcal{P}(V)$. Then $Y$ is the harmonic conjugate of $X$ if and only if $Y$ has parametric coordinate $-1$ in this frame. But any element of $\operatorname{PGL}(V)$ fixing $A = \infty$ and $B = 0$ has the form $x \mapsto \frac{x\alpha + 0}{x\cdot 0 + d}$, so that the unique involution fixing $A$ and $B$ is $\alpha: x \mapsto -x$. Since this interchanges the points with parameters $+1$ and $-1$, $Y$ is the harmonic conjugate of $X$ if and only if $\alpha$ interchanges $X$ and $Y$.
