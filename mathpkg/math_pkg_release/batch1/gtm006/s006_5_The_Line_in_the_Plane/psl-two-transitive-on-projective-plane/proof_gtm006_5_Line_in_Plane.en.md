The linear transformation $\beta$ from Lemma 2.19 has determinant 1, hence is in $PSL(V)$. Since any two lines have a point on neither, $PSL(V)$ is transitive on lines. For a fixed line $W$, $PSL(W) \cong PSL(2,K)$ is three-transitive (by Theorem 2.15), so in particular two-transitive on $W$. Given $X,Y$ and $U,Z$ distinct pairs, first map the line $X+Y$ to $U+Z$, then use $PSL(U+Z)$ to map the images of $X,Y$ to $U,Z$.
