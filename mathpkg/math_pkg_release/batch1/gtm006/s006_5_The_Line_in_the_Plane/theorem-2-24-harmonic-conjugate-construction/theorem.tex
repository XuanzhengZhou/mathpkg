Let $A_1, A_2, A_3$ be three distinct points on a projective line. Construct a complete quadrilateral as follows: choose points $P, Q$ not on the line, draw lines $A_1P$, $A_2P$, $A_1Q$, $A_2Q$, and define $A_4$ as the intersection of the line joining remaining intersections with the original line. Then $(A_1, A_2; A_3, A_4) = -1$, i.e., $A_4$ is the harmonic conjugate of $A_3$ with respect to $A_1$ and $A_2$.
