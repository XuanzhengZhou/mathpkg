A perspectivity is a special type of collineation with a line of fixed points (the axis) and a pencil of fixed lines through a point (the centre). Perspectivities are the building blocks of the little projective group and play a fundamental role in the study of projective transformations. A key result (Theorem 4.9) states that for a non-identity perspectivity, no other points or lines are fixed.
