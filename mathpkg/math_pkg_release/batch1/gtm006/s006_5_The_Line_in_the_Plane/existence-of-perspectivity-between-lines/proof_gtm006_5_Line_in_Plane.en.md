Choose a frame: $E_1 = X = \langle e_1 \rangle$, $E_2 = W_1 \cap W_2 = \langle e_2 \rangle$, $E_3$ any other point on $W_1$, $E_4$ any point on $W_2$ not $E_2$ and not on the line $E_1+E_2$. Define $\beta$ as the linear transformation with matrix $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ in this basis. Then $\beta$ fixes $E_1$ (all lines through the centre are fixed), fixes all points on $W_1 \cap W_2 + E_3 = W_1$ (the axis), and maps $W_1$ to $W_2$.
