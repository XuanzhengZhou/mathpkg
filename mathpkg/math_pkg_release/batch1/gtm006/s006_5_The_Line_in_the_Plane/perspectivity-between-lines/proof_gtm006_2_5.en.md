Choose a frame for $\mathcal{P}(V)$ as follows: $E_1 = X = \langle e_1 \rangle$, $E_2 = W_1 \cap W_2 = \langle e_2 \rangle$, $E_3 = \langle e_3 \rangle$ any point on $W_1$ (not equal to $E_2$), and $E_4 = \langle e_1 + e_2 + e_3 \rangle$ any point on $W_2$ (not equal to $E_2$ or to $W_2 \cap \{E_1 + E_2\}$).

Let $\beta$ be the linear transformation of $V$ defined as follows:
$$\beta: x_1 e_1 + x_2 e_2 + x_3 e_3 \mapsto x_1 e_1 + x_2 e_2 + (x_1 + x_3) e_3.$$

This has axis $\langle e_1, e_2 \rangle = W_1 \cap W_2$ and centre $X = \langle e_1 \rangle$, and it maps $W_1$ onto $W_2$. Thus $\beta$ induces the required perspectivity.
