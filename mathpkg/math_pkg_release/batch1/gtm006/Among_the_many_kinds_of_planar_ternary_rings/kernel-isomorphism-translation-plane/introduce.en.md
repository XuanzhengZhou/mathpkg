Theorem 7.11 establishes that the kernel of a translation plane is a geometric invariant: it is isomorphic to the kernel of any quasifield used to coordinatize the plane. This resolves the ambiguity that a given translation plane may be coordinatized by non-isomorphic quasifields -- although the quasifields themselves may differ, their kernels are always isomorphic. The proof connects the algebraic kernel (elements satisfying right-distributivity and right-associativity) with the geometric kernel (the endomorphism ring of the translation group) via the group of $(0,0), [\infty])$-homologies.
