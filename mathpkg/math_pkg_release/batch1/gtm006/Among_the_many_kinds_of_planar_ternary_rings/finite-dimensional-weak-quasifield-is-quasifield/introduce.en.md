Theorem 7.3 provides a practical criterion for verifying that a given algebraic system is a quasifield: if a weak quasifield is finite-dimensional as a vector space over a sub-skewfield of its kernel, then it automatically satisfies the unique solvability axiom $ax = bx + c$ and is therefore a quasifield. This is exceptionally useful because finite-dimensionality is often easier to verify than the unique solvability condition directly. The proof uses linear algebra: left multiplication by an element defines a linear transformation, and the unique solvability condition reduces to showing that the difference of two such transformations is non-singular.
