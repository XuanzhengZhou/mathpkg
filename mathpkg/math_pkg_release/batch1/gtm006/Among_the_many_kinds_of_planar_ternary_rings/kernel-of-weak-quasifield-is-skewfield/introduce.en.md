Theorem 7.2 establishes the fundamental structural property of the kernel of a weak quasifield: it is always a skewfield (a division ring). This means the kernel is closed under addition and multiplication, contains 0 and 1, and every non-zero element has a multiplicative inverse. Furthermore, the weak quasifield $W$ can be regarded as a right vector space over its kernel $K$, which provides a powerful tool for analyzing the structure of quasifields and the translation planes they coordinatize.
