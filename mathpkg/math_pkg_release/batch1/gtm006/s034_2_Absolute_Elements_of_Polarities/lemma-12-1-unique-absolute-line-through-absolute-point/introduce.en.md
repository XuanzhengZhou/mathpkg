This lemma establishes a fundamental pairing between absolute points and absolute lines in a polarity. It states that each absolute point lies on exactly one absolute line and vice versa. The proof relies on the incidence-preserving property of the polarity: for any two points $A$ and $B$, $A$ is on $B^\alpha$ if and only if $B$ is on $A^\alpha$.
