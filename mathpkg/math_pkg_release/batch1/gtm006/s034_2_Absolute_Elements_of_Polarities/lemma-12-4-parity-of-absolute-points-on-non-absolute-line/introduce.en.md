This lemma gives a parity condition on the number of absolute points lying on a non-absolute line. The proof constructs an involutory permutation $\theta$ on the points of $m$ and observes that fixed points of $\theta$ correspond to absolute points on $m$, so the parity follows from the fact that an involution on a set of $n+1$ points must fix a number of points congruent to $n+1$ modulo $2$.
