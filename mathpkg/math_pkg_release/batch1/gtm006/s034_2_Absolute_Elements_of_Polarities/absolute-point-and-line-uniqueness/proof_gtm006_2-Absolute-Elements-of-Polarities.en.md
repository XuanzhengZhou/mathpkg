If $m$ is an absolute line, then $m^\alpha \in m$, so $m$ contains at least one absolute point $A = m^\alpha$. Suppose $B \neq A$ is another absolute point on $m$. Then $B \in A^\alpha$, so $A \in B^\alpha$. Since $B \in B^\alpha$, we have $B^\alpha = AB = m$. But this implies $m^\alpha = B \neq A$, contradicting $A = m^\alpha$. Thus exactly one absolute point lies on each absolute line. The dual statement follows by duality.
