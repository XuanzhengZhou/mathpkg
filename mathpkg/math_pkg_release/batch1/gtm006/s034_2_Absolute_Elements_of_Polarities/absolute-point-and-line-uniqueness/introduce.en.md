Lemma 12.1 establishes that the absolute elements of a polarity are paired: each absolute point determines a unique absolute line through it (its polar), and each absolute line contains a unique absolute point (its pole). This pairing implies that the number of absolute points equals the number of absolute lines. The proof uses the fundamental property of polarities that $A \in B^\alpha$ iff $B \in A^\alpha$.
