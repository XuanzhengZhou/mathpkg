In a projective plane equipped with a polarity, an element is called absolute if it is incident with its image under the polarity. Specifically, a point $A$ is absolute when it lies on its polar line $A^\alpha$, and dually a line $m$ is absolute when its pole $m^\alpha$ lies on $m$. The study of absolute elements is central to the classification of polarities, as their number and configuration determine whether the polarity is orthogonal, unitary, or irregular.
