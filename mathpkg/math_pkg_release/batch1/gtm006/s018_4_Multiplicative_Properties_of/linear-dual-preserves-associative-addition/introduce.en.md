Theorem 6.7 establishes that the algebraic property of being linear with associative addition is preserved when passing from a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ to its dual plane $\mathcal{P}^*$. If $(R, T)$ is the planar ternary ring coordinatizing $\mathcal{P}$ and is linear with associative addition, then the ternary ring $(R, T')$ coordinatizing the dual plane inherits the same properties. The proof leverages Theorem 6.2 (which characterizes this algebraic condition via $((\infty), [\infty])$-transitivity) and the fact that an elation of $\mathcal{P}$ with axis $[\infty]$ translates to an elation of $\mathcal{P}^*$ with the same transitivity parameters.
