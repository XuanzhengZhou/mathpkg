Theorem 6.5 establishes an equivalence between an algebraic property of the coordinatizing planar ternary ring $(R, T)$ and a geometric transitivity condition on the projective plane $\mathcal{P}$. Specifically, the ring being linear (i.e., $T(a, x, y) = ax + y$) with associative multiplication is exactly equivalent to $\mathcal{P}$ being $((0), [0])$-transitive. This means that for any point $P$ on the line $[0]$ distinct from $(0)$, there exists a $((0), [0])$-homology mapping a fixed reference point to $P$. The proof constructs an explicit homology $\phi_a$ mapping $(1, 0)$ to $(a, 0)$ and verifies incidence preservation using the algebraic assumptions.
