A nearfield is a quasifield whose multiplication operation is associative. In the context of projective planes, nearfields arise naturally as the coordinatizing algebraic structures of $((0), [0])$-transitive planes. This definition bridges the gap between quasifields (which need not have associative multiplication) and division rings (which are nearfields with two-sided distributivity). Every nearfield coordinatizes a projective plane that admits a transitive group of $((0), [0])$-homologies.
