This follows immediately from Corollary 1 and the result of Exercise 2.11, which establishes that a projective line in $\mathcal{P}_2(q)$ has exactly $q+1$ points. Since a nonempty conic is in one-to-one correspondence with the points on a projective line (Corollary 1), it must also contain exactly $q+1$ points. If the conic is empty, it trivially has $0$ points, so the only possibilities are $0$ or $q+1$. $\square$
