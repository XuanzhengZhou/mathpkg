If $P$ is on a conic $\mathcal{C}$ which is associated with a polarity $\beta$, then $P$ is an absolute point whose polar image contains no other point of $\mathcal{C}$. Since the other lines through $P$ cannot be absolute (otherwise they would contain exactly one point of $\mathcal{C}$ by Lemma 2.32), they must each contain exactly one additional point of $\mathcal{C}$ (since any line meets $\mathcal{C}$ in at most two points by Lemma 2.34, and if it contains $P$ and is not absolute, it cannot be tangent). Thus the lines through $P$ (other than the tangent at $P$) are in bijective correspondence with the remaining points of $\mathcal{C}$. The set of lines through $P$ forms a pencil which is in natural bijection with the points on any line not passing through $P$, i.e., a projective line. $\square$
