*Proof.* For any two non-zero vectors $x, y \in F^*$, let $f = x^{-1} y$. Then $x R_f = x(x^{-1} y) = y$, so the group of $R_f$'s is transitive on $F^*$, i.e., on non-zero vectors. Hence the induced group $\Sigma$ on $\mathcal{P}$ is transitive on points.

The kernel of the representation $f \mapsto R_f$ consists of those $f$ for which $R_f$ acts as scalar multiplication; these correspond to elements of $K^*$. So $\Sigma \cong F^* / K^*$ and $\Sigma$ acts regularly on the points of $\mathcal{P}$. $\square$
