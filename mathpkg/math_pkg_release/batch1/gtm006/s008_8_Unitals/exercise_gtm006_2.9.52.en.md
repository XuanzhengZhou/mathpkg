Let $K$ be a field and $F$ a quadratic extension field of $K$. Show that the affine plane $\mathcal{A}_2(K)$ possesses a collineation group $\Gamma$ which fixes the point $(0,0)$ and is regular on the remaining points. Furthermore, show that $\Gamma \cong F^* / K^*$, and that $\Gamma$ has two orbits on the lines of $\mathcal{A}_2(K)$: one consisting of the lines through $(0,0)$, and the other consisting of the remaining lines.
