Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a projective plane over a field $K$, let $W$ be a line, and let $\mathcal{C}$ be a non-empty conic not meeting $W$ (an ellipse). Then there exists a coordinate frame such that a point $\langle(x, y, 1)\rangle$ not on $W$ lies on $\mathcal{C}$ if and only if
$$a x^2 + y^2 = 1,$$
where $-a$ is not a square in $K$.
