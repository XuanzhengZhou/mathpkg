*Proof.* Choose a self-polar triangle $E_1, E_2, E_3$ of $\mathcal{C}$ with $W = E_1 + E_2$. Since $W$ contains no point of $\mathcal{C}$, no tangent line passes through $W^\varrho$, and we set $E_3 = W^\varrho$. Choose a line through $E_3$ meeting $\mathcal{C}$ twice, building a frame where the conic has equation $ax^2 + y^2 - z^2 = 0$ in projective coordinates. Setting $z = 1$ gives the affine equation $ax^2 + y^2 = 1$.

The condition that no point on $W$ (i.e. $z = 0$) lies on $\mathcal{C}$ means $ax^2 + y^2 = 0$ has only the trivial solution $x = y = 0$, which implies $-a = (y/x)^2$ has no solution; that is, $-a$ is not a square. $\square$
