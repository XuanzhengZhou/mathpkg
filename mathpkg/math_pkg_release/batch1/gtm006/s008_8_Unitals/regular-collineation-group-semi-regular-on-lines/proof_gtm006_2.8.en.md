*Proof.* If $\Gamma$ fixed a line $m$, then all points on $m$ would be mapped to points on $m$ by every element of $\Gamma$. Since $\Gamma$ is transitive on all points of $\mathcal{P}$, and $m$ contains only a proper subset of the points, this cannot happen. Hence no line is fixed by any non-identity element. $\square$
