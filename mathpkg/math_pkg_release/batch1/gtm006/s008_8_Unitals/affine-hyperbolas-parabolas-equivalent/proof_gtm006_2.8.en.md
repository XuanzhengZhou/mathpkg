*Proof.* The group $\text{Aut}\,\mathcal{A}$ is $3$-transitive on $W$. If two conics have the same number of intersections with $W$, we may apply an element of $\text{Aut}\,\mathcal{A}$ to make the two conics meet $W$ in the same set of points.

For a conic meeting $W$ twice (hyperbola), we can choose the two intersection points as $\langle(1,0,0)\rangle$ and $\langle(0,0,1)\rangle$. The projective theory shows that any such conic can be put in the normal form $y^2 = xz$. Hence any two hyperbolas through these two points are equivalent.

For a conic meeting $W$ once (parabola), we can take $W$ as the tangent line $\langle(0,0,1)\rangle$ and again obtain a normal form, showing all parabolas are equivalent. $\square$
