*Proof.* By Theorem 2.49, any non-empty unital can be brought to a canonical matrix form
$$A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & c \\ 0 & b & 0 \\ c^{\phi} & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$$
with $b + c + c^{\phi} = 0$ by an appropriate choice of projective frame. Since any two such frames are related by an element of $\text{PGL}(V)$, and the matrix form depends only on the parameters $b, c$ (which are determined up to the action of the frame), two non-empty unitals can be mapped to one another by composing the frame transformations. Equivalently, the unitary group is conjugate in $\text{PGL}(V)$ for any two non-empty unitals. $\square$
