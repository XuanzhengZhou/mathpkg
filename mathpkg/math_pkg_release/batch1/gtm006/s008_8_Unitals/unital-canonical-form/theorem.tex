Let $\mathcal{U}$ be a non-empty unital in the projective plane $\mathcal{P} = \mathcal{P}_2(K)$, let $\beta$ be the unitary polarity defining $\mathcal{U}$, and let $X, Z, J$ be any three non-collinear points of $\mathcal{U}$. Let $Y$ be the intersection of $X^{\beta}$ and $Z^{\beta}$. Then $X, Y, Z, J$ form a frame for $\mathcal{P}$, and with respect to this frame, $\beta$ can be represented by the matrix
$$A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & c \\ 0 & b & 0 \\ c^{\phi} & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix},$$
where $\phi$ is the associated automorphism of $\beta$, $b$ is an element of the fixed field $F$, and $b + c + c^{\phi} = 0$. A point $P = \langle (x, y, z) \rangle$ belongs to $\mathcal{U}$ if and only if its coordinates satisfy a certain quadratic equation derived from this matrix.
