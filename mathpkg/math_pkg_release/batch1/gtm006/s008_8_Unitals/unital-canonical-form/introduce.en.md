Theorem 2.49 provides a canonical matrix representation for a non-empty unital, analogous to the canonical form of a conic. By choosing a frame consisting of three points of the unital and the intersection of their polar lines, the polarity matrix takes a particularly simple anti-diagonal form with three parameters $b, c, c^{\phi}$ satisfying a linear relation. This canonical form is the foundation for studying the unitary group and for proving the equivalence of all non-empty unitals.
