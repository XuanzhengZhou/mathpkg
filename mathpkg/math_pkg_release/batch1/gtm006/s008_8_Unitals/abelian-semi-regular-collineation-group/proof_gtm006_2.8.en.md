*Proof.* Let $m$ be any line of $\mathcal{P}$. If $m^\alpha = m$ for some $\alpha \neq 1$, then the stabilizer of $m$ is non-trivial. Since $\Gamma$ is abelian, the stabilizer is normal in $\Gamma$ and therefore fixes all images of $m$ under $\Gamma$. Two distinct images of $m$ intersect in a point, and this point would be fixed by the stabilizer, contradicting semi-regularity of $\Gamma$ on points. Hence only one line can be fixed by $\Gamma$, and no non-identity element fixes any other line. $\square$
