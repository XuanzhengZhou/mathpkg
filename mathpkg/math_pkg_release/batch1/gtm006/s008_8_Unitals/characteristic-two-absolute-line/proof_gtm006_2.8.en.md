*Proof.* Let $X = \langle v \rangle$, $Y = \langle w \rangle$ be absolute points, so $v A v' = w A w' = 0$. In characteristic two, the $J$-ratio equation simplifies to
$$x^2(v A v') + y^2(w A w') = 0,$$
since the cross terms vanish modulo 2. As $v A v' = w A w' = 0$, this equation is identically satisfied for all $x, y \in K$. Hence every linear combination $\langle x v + y w \rangle$ is also absolute. $\square$
