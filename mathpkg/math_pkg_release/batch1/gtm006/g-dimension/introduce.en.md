The $g$-dimension (geometric dimension) of a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is one less than the algebraic dimension of the underlying vector space $V$. Points are the fundamental objects of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and correspond to 1-dimensional subspaces (g-dimension 0). Lines have g-dimension 1, planes have g-dimension 2, and hyperplanes have g-dimension $n-1$ where $n$ is the g-dimension of the entire geometry. The zero subspace is assigned g-dimension $-1$ and plays the role of the empty set.
