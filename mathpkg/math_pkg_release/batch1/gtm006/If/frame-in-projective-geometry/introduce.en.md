A frame in a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of $g$-dimension $n$ is an ordered set of $n+2$ points such that no $n+1$ lie in a hyperplane. Frames play the role in projective geometry that bases play in linear algebra: they provide a coordinate system. Once a frame is fixed, a basis for the underlying vector space is determined up to a common non-zero scalar multiple, allowing coordinatization of the projective space.
