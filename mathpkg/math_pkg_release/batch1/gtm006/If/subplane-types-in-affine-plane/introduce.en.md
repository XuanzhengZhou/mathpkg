When a projective subplane $\mathcal{Q}$ of $\mathcal{P}$ is viewed relative to an affine plane $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{P}^l$, three geometries arise depending on how the special line $l$ meets $\mathcal{Q}$. If $l$ is a line of $\mathcal{Q}$, removing it yields an affine subplane embedded compatibly in $\mathcal{A}$. If $l$ is disjoint from $\mathcal{Q}$, the subplane sits entirely at infinity as a projective subplane. The intermediate case (exactly one point in common) is not given a special name.
