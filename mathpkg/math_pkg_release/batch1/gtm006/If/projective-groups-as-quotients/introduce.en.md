Theorem 2.10 identifies the projective groups as quotients of the corresponding linear groups. Let $N$ be the subgroup of $\Gamma L(V)$ consisting of transformations that preserve every one-dimensional subspace (i.e., act as scalars on each ray). Then $P\Gamma L(V) \cong \Gamma L(V)/N$, $PGL(V) \cong GL(V)/(N \cap GL(V))$, and when $K$ is a field, $PSL(V) \cong SL(V)/(N \cap SL(V))$. This theorem makes precise the relationship between linear transformations and the projective automorphisms they induce.
