As a corollary to Theorem 2.12 (the Frame Stabilizer Theorem), $PGL(V)$ acts sharply transitively on the frames of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. In the special case of the projective line $\mathcal{P}_1(K)$ over a field $K$, this is equivalent to $PGL(V)$ being sharply three-transitive on the points. This sharp transitivity is the geometric foundation for the algebraic description of projective automorphisms as fractional linear transformations.
