Let $A$ be an incidence matrix for a set of points and lines satisfying the conditions of Exercise 3.9. Show that $AA' = nI + J$ and $AJ = JA = (n+1)J$. Hence show that there exist real numbers $x, y$ such that $A^{-1} = xA' + yJ$. Now use this to show $AA' = A'A$ (i.e., that $A$ is normal) and deduce that $A$ is an incidence matrix of a projective plane for $n \geq 2$. (Thus we have an alternative proof of Exercise 3.10.)
