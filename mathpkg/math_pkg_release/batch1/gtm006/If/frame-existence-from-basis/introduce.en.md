Lemma 2.4 establishes the existence of frames in projective geometry. Given any basis $e_1, \dots, e_{n+1}$ of an $(n+1)$-dimensional vector space $V$, the points $E_i = \langle e_i \rangle$ for $i = 1,\dots,n+1$ together with $E_{n+2} = \langle e_1 + \cdots + e_{n+1} \rangle$ form a frame. Conversely, Lemma 2.5 shows that every frame arises this way and determines the basis up to a common scalar. Together these lemmas establish the correspondence between bases and frames.
