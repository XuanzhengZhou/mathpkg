Homogeneous coordinates are a coordinate system for projective geometry derived from the dual space. Once a basis is chosen, points of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ are represented by row vectors (one-dimensional subspaces) and hyperplanes by column vectors. In the projective plane, points are $\langle x, y, z \rangle$ and lines are $\langle a, b, c \rangle$, with incidence given by the bilinear equation $ax + by + cz = 0$. This formalism unifies the treatment of points and lines and simplifies incidence calculations.
