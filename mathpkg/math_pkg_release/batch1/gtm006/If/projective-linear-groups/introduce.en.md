The projective linear groups $P\Gamma L(V)$, $PGL(V)$, and $PSL(V)$ are the automorphism groups of a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ induced by the corresponding groups of semi-linear transformations of the underlying vector space $V$. Here $\Gamma L(V)$ consists of all non-singular semi-linear transformations, $GL(V)$ of non-singular linear transformations, and $SL(V)$ (when $K$ is a field) of linear transformations of determinant one. Writing "$P$" before a group indicates the induced action on $\mathcal{P}(V)$, factoring out the scalar transformations that act trivially on projective space.
