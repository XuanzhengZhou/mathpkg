An affine plane is an incidence geometry axiomatized by three conditions: any two points determine a unique line, the Euclidean parallel postulate (Playfair's axiom), and existence of a non-degenerate triangle. Parallelism is an equivalence relation on lines, and the equivalence classes are called parallel classes. Every affine plane can be embedded as the complement of a line in a unique projective plane.
