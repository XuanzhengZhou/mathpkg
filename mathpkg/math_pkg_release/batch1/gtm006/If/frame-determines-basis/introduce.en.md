Lemma 2.5 establishes the converse to Lemma 2.4: any frame $F_1, \dots, F_{n+2}$ in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ arises from a basis $e_1, \dots, e_{n+1}$ with $F_i = \langle e_i \rangle$ and $F_{n+2} = \langle e_1 + \cdots + e_{n+1} \rangle$. This basis is unique up to multiplication by a common non-zero scalar. This lemma completes the correspondence between bases of $V$ and frames of $\mathcal{P}(V)$, making frames the projective analogue of bases.
