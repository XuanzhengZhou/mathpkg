If $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{P}^l$ for a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ and a line $l$ of $\mathcal{P}$, then:
\begin{itemize}
\item The points and lines of $\mathcal{A}$ are called the \text{affine elements} of $\mathcal{A}$.
\item $l$ is called the \text{special line} of $\mathcal{A}$.
\item The points of $l$ are called the \text{special points} of $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{itemize}
