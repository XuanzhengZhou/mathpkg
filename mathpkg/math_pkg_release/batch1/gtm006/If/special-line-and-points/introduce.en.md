When an affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ is realized as $\mathcal{P}^l$ for a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$, the deleted line $l$ and its points acquire special names. The line $l$ is the "line at infinity" or special line, its points are the "points at infinity" or special points, and the remaining points and lines are the affine elements. This terminology reflects the embedding of the affine plane into its unique projective completion.
