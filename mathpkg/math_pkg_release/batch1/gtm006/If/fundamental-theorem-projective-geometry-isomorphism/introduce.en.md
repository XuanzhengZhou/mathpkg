The Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry states that every isomorphism between projective planes $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\mathcal{P}(W)$ (where $V$ and $W$ are 3-dimensional left vector spaces over skewfields $K$ and $F$) is induced by a semi-linear transformation from $V$ onto $W$, and consequently $K \cong F$. This theorem shows that the algebraic structure (the skewfield and semi-linear maps) completely determines the geometric structure (the projective plane and its isomorphisms). It is proved for the projective plane in Theorem 2.8 and extends to higher dimensions.
