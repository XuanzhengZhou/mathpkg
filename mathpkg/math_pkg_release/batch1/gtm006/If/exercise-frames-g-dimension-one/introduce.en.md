Exercise 2.8 characterizes frames in a projective geometry of $g$-dimension one (the projective line). It asks to show that three points $E_1, E_2, E_3$ in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ form a frame if and only if they are distinct. This is the simplest case of the frame definition and establishes the link between frames on the projective line and the three-transitivity of $PGL(V)$.
