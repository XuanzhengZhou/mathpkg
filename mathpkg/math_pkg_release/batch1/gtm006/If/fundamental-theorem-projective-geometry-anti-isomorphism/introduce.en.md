Theorem 2.9 is the anti-isomorphism variant of the Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry. If $V$ is a left vector space over $K$, $W$ is a right vector space over $F$, and both have dimension three, then any isomorphism $\mathcal{P}(V) \to \mathcal{P}(W)$ forces $K$ and $F$ to be anti-isomorphic and is induced by a semi-linear transformation. This highlights the role of vector space chirality (left vs. right) in projective geometry: isomorphisms between projective spaces over left and right vector spaces correspond to anti-isomorphisms of the underlying skewfields.
