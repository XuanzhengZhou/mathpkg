This fundamental theorem establishes that affine planes and projective planes are two sides of the same coin. Every affine plane arises by deleting a line (the "line at infinity") and all its points from a uniquely determined projective plane. The projective completion is constructed by adding one "point at infinity" for each parallel class of lines, and one "line at infinity" incident with all these new points.
