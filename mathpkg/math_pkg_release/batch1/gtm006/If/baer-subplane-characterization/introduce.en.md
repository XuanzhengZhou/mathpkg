A Baer subplane of a projective plane is a maximal proper subplane with respect to the property that every line meets the subplane in at least one point. This theorem provides the precise numerical condition relating the order of the ambient projective plane to the order of the subplane: a subplane is Baer exactly when the order of the whole plane is the square of the order of the subplane.
