This follows from Theorem 10.14, as a right quasifield $Q$ of dimension two over its kernel $F$ satisfies the conditions of the theorem. The kernel $F$ is a subfield of the kernel of $Q$, $Q$ has dimension $2$ over $F$, and the sets $\mathcal{B}(a,b,c)$ defined in Theorem 10.14 form Baer subplanes of the affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ coordinatized by $Q$, with the property that lines joining two points of any such subplane meet the line at infinity in a fixed derivation set $\mathcal{S}$.

The derived plane obtained is not a translation plane in general; in fact it is not even a dual translation plane. This provides further examples (see Chapter IX) of planes which have no translation lines or points.
