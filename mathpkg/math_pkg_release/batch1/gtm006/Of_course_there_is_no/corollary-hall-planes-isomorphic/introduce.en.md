This corollary to Theorem 10.16 establishes that any two finite Hall planes of the same order are isomorphic. For infinite Hall planes, two planes are isomorphic precisely when the irreducible quadratics defining them are equivalent, meaning they have zeros in the same quadratic extension field. The converse (whether equivalent quadratics always yield non-isomorphic Hall planes) remains unresolved.
