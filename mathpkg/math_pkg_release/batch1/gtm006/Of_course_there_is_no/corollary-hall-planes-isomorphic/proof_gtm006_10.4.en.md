This follows directly from Theorem 10.16, which shows that the multiplication in the derived quasifield can be expressed in terms of an arbitrary irreducible quadratic $g(a) = a^2 + (v/u)a - 1/u$ over $GF(q)$. By the theorem, we can choose the parameters to give us any Hall quasifield, and from the multiplication formula $a * (c + \lambda d) = ac + \lambda ad$, all Hall quasifields of the same order yield isomorphic planes.

For infinite Hall planes, the statement extends: two Hall planes are isomorphic if and only if the irreducible quadratics which define them are equivalent, i.e., they have zeros in the same quadratic extension field. The converse direction remains an open problem.
