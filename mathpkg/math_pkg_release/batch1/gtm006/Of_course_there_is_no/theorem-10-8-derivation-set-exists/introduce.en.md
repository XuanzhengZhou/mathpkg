The desarguesian affine plane $\mathcal{A}_2(q^2)$ over the finite field $GF(q^2)$ always possesses a derivation set. This allows the construction of derived planes from the classical desarguesian planes. The derivation set is explicitly given as the set of all points $(b)$ on the line at infinity $l_{\infty}$ with $b \in GF(q)$, together with the point $(\infty)$. The proof uses translations and $(X, l_{\infty})$-homologies to reduce to the Baer subplane $\mathcal{A}_2(q)$.
