Theorem 10.14 gives general sufficient conditions for an affine plane to be derivable, based on the algebraic structure of its coordinate quasifield. If the plane is coordinatized by a right quasifield $R$ of dimension $2$ over a subfield $F$ of its kernel, and the sets $\mathcal{B}(a,b,c)$ form Baer subplanes with their point-at-infinity set independent of $a,b,c$, then the plane admits a derivation. This generalizes the construction of derived planes beyond the desarguesian case and provides a powerful criterion for derivability.
