Let $R$ be a right quasifield coordinatizing an affine plane $\mathcal{A}$, and suppose $F$ is a subfield of the kernel of $R$ such that $R$ has dimension $2$ over $F$. If for all $a,b,c$ in $R$ the set
$$\mathcal{B}(a,b,c) = \{(\alpha a + b, \beta a + c) \mid \alpha, \beta \in F\}$$
is a Baer subplane of $\mathcal{A}$, and if every line joining two points of $\mathcal{B}(a,b,c)$ meets the line at infinity in a fixed set $\mathcal{S}$ independent of $a,b,c$, then $\mathcal{A}$ is derivable with derivation set $\mathcal{S}$.
