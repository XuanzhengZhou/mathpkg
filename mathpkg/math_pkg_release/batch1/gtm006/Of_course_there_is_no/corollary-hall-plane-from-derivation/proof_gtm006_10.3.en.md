See the Corollary to Theorem 9.2.

The analysis preceding this corollary shows: Let $m$ be a line of $\mathcal{A}$ which meets $l_{\infty}$ in a point of $\mathcal{S}$. Then the subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal{A})$ which fixes $\mathcal{S}$ and consists of perspectivities with axis $m$ is the subgroup of $P\Gamma L_2(q)$ consisting of perspectivities with axis $m$ and centres on $l_{\infty}$. This group, denoted by $\Lambda$, has order $q(q-1)$. The group induced on $\mathcal{A}_H$ by $\Lambda$ fixes a Baer subplane pointwise, and since any Baer subplane is a maximal subplane, only the identity can fix a point not in the Baer subplane. Thus, as $\mathcal{S}'$ has exactly $q(q-1)$ points, the group induced by $\Lambda$ is transitive (and even regular) on $\mathcal{S}'$.

Let $X$ be an affine point of $\mathcal{A}$. If $\Sigma$ denotes the group of $(X, l_{\infty})$-homologies of $\mathcal{A}$, then $\Sigma$ is cyclic of order $q^2-1$, and since it is transitive on the affine points on any line through $X$ (other than $X$ itself), $\Sigma$ induces a transitive permutation group on the Baer subplanes which belong to $\mathcal{S}$ and contain $X$. Thus $\Sigma$ induces on $\mathcal{A}_H$ a collineation group which is transitive on lines through $X$ meeting $\mathcal{S}'$ and fixes every line through $X$ meeting $l_{\infty} \setminus \mathcal{S}'$.

Now the subgroup $\Lambda$ of Baer collineations fixing every point of $\mathcal{B}$ has order $q(q-1)$. No non-identity element of $\Lambda$ can fix any point on the special line outside of $\mathcal{S}'$, so $\Lambda$ must be transitive on the special points not in $\mathcal{S}'$. It is also clear that $\Lambda$ is induced by a group of automorphisms of the quasifield $Q$ (since it fixes $(1,1)$), and this group fixes every element of $K$ (since the Hall subplane $\mathcal{H}$ coincides with $\mathcal{B}$). It follows that $\Lambda$ is transitive and regular on the elements of $Q$ not in $K$.

The conclusion that $\mathcal{A}_H$ is a Hall plane follows from these structural properties, as referenced in the Corollary to Theorem 9.2.
