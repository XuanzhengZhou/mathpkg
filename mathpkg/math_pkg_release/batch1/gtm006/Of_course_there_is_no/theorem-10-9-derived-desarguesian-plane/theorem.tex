If $\mathcal{A}$ is desarguesian then, unless $\mathcal{A}$ has order $4$, the group of all shears $\sigma_a$ of $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{F}}(\mathcal{A})$ is isomorphic to the additive group of $GF(q)$ where $q^2=|\mathcal{A}|$. In particular, $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{F}}(\mathcal{A})$ is not desarguesian when $\mathcal{A}$ has order $q^2$ with $q>2$.
