Exercise 14.1 asks the reader to show that for any Hall plane of order $n > 9$, the full collineation group fails to act transitively on affine lines. This illustrates that the line-transitivity condition in Wagner's Theorem is genuinely restrictive — Hall planes are well-known translation planes, but their full collineation groups are not line-transitive for orders greater than 9.
