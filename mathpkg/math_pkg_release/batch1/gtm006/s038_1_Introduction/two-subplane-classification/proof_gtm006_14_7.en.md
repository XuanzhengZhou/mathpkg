**Proof of Lemma 14.13.** The proof proceeds by a detailed case analysis.

**Case 1.** $\Sigma$ contains no involutory homology with affine centre.

By condition (i) of Lemma 14.12, $\Sigma$ contains an involutory homology $\alpha$. Let $\alpha$ have centre $A \in l_{\infty}$ and axis $l$, and let $B = l \cap l_{\infty}$. For any other affine line $m$ through $B$, condition (ii) yields an involutory homology $\beta$ fixing $A,B,m$. If $B$ were the centre of $\beta$, then $\alpha\beta$ would be an involutory homology with affine axis, contradicting the case assumption. Thus $\beta$ has centre $A$ and axis $m$. Hence $A$ is the centre of a homology with axis $h$ for every affine line $h$ through $B$. By the dual of Corollary 1 to Theorem 4.25, $\mathcal{B}$ is $(A,l_{\infty})$-transitive.

**Case 1a.** $\Sigma$ does not fix $A$. Then conjugating yields $(X,l_{\infty})$-transitivity for all $X \in l_{\infty}$, so $\mathcal{B}$ is a translation plane.

**Case 1b.** $\Sigma$ fixes $A$. Further subcase analysis shows $\mathcal{B}$ is the dual of a translation plane.

**Case 2.** $\Sigma$ contains an involutory homology with affine centre. Further analysis leads to either a translation plane, dual translation plane, or type $\mathcal{H}(1)$.

**Case 3.** Mixed involutory homologies. The remaining cases are analyzed through subcases (IIIa, IIIb) involving the distribution of homology centres among affine points and the transitivity of certain subgroups on $l_{\infty}$.

In all cases, the plane $\mathcal{B}$ is forced to be one of: translation plane, dual translation plane, or type $\mathcal{H}(1)$. $\square$
