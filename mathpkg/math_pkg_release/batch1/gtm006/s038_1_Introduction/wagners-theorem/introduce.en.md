Wagner's Theorem is a landmark result in finite geometry: it characterizes translation planes among finite affine planes by a purely group-theoretic condition (line-transitivity), and characterizes Desarguesian planes among finite projective planes by double point-transitivity. The proof combines deep permutation group theory with incidence geometry, using Frobenius groups and the Feit-Thompson theorem via Results 14.1-14.4.
