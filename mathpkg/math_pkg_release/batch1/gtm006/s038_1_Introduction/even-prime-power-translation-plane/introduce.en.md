Lemma 14.8 disposes of the "easy" cases of Wagner's Theorem. When the order $n$ is even, a Sylow 2-subgroup argument shows that $\Pi$ must contain a nontrivial translation (otherwise a central involution would fix a Baer subplane pointwise, leading to a contradiction via the counting formulas). When $n$ is an odd prime power, a Sylow $p$-subgroup argument is used. In both cases, the existence of a nontrivial translation forces $\mathcal{A}$ to be a translation plane.
