Theorem 14.6 establishes the equivalence of three transitivity conditions for a collineation group acting on a finite affine plane: transitivity on affine lines, transitivity on affine points together with transitivity on special points (points at infinity), and transitivity on affine flags (incident point-line pairs). This theorem is the starting point of the proof of Wagner's Theorem and provides the fundamental framework for understanding line-transitive collineation groups.
