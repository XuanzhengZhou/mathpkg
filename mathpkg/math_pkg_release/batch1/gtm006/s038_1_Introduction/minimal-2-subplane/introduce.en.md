A minimal 2-subplane is a 2-subplane that is minimal with respect to inclusion among all 2-subplanes. In any finite projective plane, minimal 2-subplanes always exist for any collineation group. This notion is essential in the proof of Wagner's Theorem (Theorem 14.11), where the reduction to a minimal 2-subplane allows one to apply Lemma 14.12 and Lemma 14.13 to deduce that the original plane has prime-power order.
