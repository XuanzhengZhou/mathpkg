Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite affine plane of odd order $n$, let $\Pi$ be a collineation group transitive on the affine lines of $\mathcal{A}$, let $\mathcal{Q}$ be a minimal 2-subplane with respect to $\Pi$, and let $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{Q}^{l_{\infty}}$. Let $\Sigma$ be the subgroup of $\Pi$ leaving $\mathcal{Q}$ invariant, regarded as a collineation group of $\mathcal{B}$. Then:

(i) for every affine flag in $\mathcal{B}$ there is an involutory homology in $\Sigma$ fixing that flag.

(ii) if $A, B$ are special points of $\mathcal{B}$ with $l$ an affine line of $\mathcal{B}$ through $B$ and if there is an involutory homology in $\Sigma$ fixing $A, B, l$, then if $m$ is any other affine line of $\mathcal{B}$ through $B$ there exists an involutory homology in $\Sigma$ fixing $A, B, m$.
