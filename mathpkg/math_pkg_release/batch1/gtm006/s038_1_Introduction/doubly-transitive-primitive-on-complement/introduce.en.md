Result 14.1 states that under certain conditions, the stabilizer of a point in a doubly transitive permutation group acts primitively on the remaining points. Specifically, if $\Pi$ is doubly transitive on a set of $n+1$ points with $n$ odd, and each stabilizer $\Pi_a$ contains an even-order Frobenius subgroup $\Gamma_a$ on the complement, then $\Pi_a$ is primitive on $\mathcal{S}\setminus\{a\}$. This result was proved by Wagner and is essential to the proof of Wagner's Theorem on line-transitive affine planes.
