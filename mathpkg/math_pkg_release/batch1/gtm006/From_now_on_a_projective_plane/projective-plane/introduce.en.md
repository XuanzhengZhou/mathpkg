A projective plane is defined axiomatically as an incidence structure consisting of points and lines. The three axioms require that any two points determine a unique line, any two lines meet in a unique point, and there exist four points in general position (no three collinear). This definition captures the essential incidence properties shared by classical projective planes over fields and more exotic examples arising from non-Desarguesian geometries.
