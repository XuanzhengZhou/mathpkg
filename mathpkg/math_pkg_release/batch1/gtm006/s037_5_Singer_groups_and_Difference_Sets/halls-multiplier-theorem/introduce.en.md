Hall's Multiplier Theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of difference sets. It states that for any prime divisor $p$ of the order $n$ of a finite projective plane with an abelian Singer group, the map $\alpha \mapsto \alpha^p$ acts as a "multiplier" on the difference set: $\mathcal{D}^p = \mathcal{D}\lambda$ for some group element $\lambda$. This multiplier structure provides powerful tools for both constructing and proving non-existence of difference sets, and implies the existence of additional collineations normalizing the Singer group.
