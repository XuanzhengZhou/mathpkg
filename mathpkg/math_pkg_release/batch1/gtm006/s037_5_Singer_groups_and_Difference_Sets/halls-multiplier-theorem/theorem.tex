Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a finite projective plane of order $n$ and suppose that $\Gamma$ is an abelian Singer group for $\mathcal{P}$ with difference set $\mathcal{D}$. Let $p$ be any prime divisor of $n$ and let $\phi$ be the automorphism of $\Gamma$ given by $\alpha^\phi = \alpha^p$. Then there exists an element $\lambda \in \Gamma$ such that $\mathcal{D}^\phi = \mathcal{D}\lambda$. Furthermore, the mapping $\bar{\phi}$ defined by
$$\bar{\phi} : (\alpha) \mapsto (\alpha^p), \qquad [\beta] \mapsto [\lambda \beta^p]$$
is a collineation of $\mathcal{P}$ which normalizes $\Gamma$.
