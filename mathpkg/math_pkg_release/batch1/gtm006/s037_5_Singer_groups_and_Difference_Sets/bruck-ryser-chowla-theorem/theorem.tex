Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a finite projective plane of order $n$. If $n \equiv 1$ or $2 \pmod{4}$, and $n$ cannot be written as the sum of two squares, then $n$ must satisfy the condition that for any odd prime $p$ dividing the square-free part of $n$, the equation
$$x^2 = n y^2 + (-1)^{\frac{p-1}{2}} p z^2$$
has a non-trivial solution in integers $x, y, z$ not all zero.
