This theorem strengthens Hall's Multiplier Theorem by considering the group $\Phi$ generated by all multiplier collineations $\bar{\phi}$ (one for each prime divisor of $n$). It shows that despite the multitude of multipliers, there is always a common line fixed by the entire group $\Phi$. This result provides strong constraints on the structure of projective planes with abelian Singer groups and is used to prove non-existence for certain non-prime-power orders.
