Let $\Pi$ be a Singer group with difference set $\mathcal{D}$. Then for every $\beta \neq 1$ in $\Pi$, there exists a unique pair $\delta_1, \delta_2 \in \mathcal{D}$ such that $\beta = \delta_1 \delta_2^{-1}$, and also a unique pair $\delta_3, \delta_4 \in \mathcal{D}$ with $\beta = \delta_3^{-1} \delta_4$.
