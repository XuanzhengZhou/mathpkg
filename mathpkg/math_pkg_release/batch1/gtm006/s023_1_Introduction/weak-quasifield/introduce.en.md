A weak quasifield is an algebraic structure that relaxes the definition of a quasifield by dropping the unique solvability axiom $ax = bx + c$ ($a \neq b$) and instead requiring $(W,+)$ to be abelian from the start. Every quasifield is a weak quasifield (once $(Q,+)$ is shown to be abelian via Exercise 7.2), and the converse holds when $W$ is finite-dimensional over its kernel (Theorem 7.3). The weak quasifield framework is introduced primarily to develop the theory of the kernel, since the kernel is defined for weak quasifields and its properties do not require the full quasifield axiom.
