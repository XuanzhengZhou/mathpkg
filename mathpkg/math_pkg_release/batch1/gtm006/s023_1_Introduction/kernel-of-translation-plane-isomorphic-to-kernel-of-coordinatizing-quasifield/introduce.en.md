Theorem 7.11 establishes that the kernel of a translation plane (defined geometrically via the automorphism group) is isomorphic to the kernel of any quasifield that coordinatizes it. This is a fundamental invariance result: although a given translation plane may be coordinatized by non-isomorphic quasifields, their kernels are always isomorphic. The proof identifies the multiplicative group of the kernel with the group of $(0,0), [\infty]$-homologies, and shows that the right multiplications by kernel elements of $Q$ correspond exactly to the geometric kernel endomorphisms of the translation group.
