Lemma 7.4 describes structural properties of the elation groups in a translation plane $\mathcal{P}$. Part (i) states that the group of elations with axis $x_2$ and arbitrary center on $x_1$ is the union of the $(X, x_2)$-elation groups as $X$ varies. Part (ii) asserts that elation groups with distinct centers on the line at infinity intersect trivially. Part (iii) says the group of $(x_1, x_2)$-elations (intersecting $x_2$ at $[\infty]$) is generated by any two distinct $(A, x_2)$- and $(B, x_2)$-elation groups. These properties are fundamental for the analysis of translation planes via their collineation groups and for the connection with congruence partitions in Andre's approach.
