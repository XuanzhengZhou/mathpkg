Let $W$ be a $2d$-dimensional vector space over $\operatorname{GF}(q)$ and, clearly, any vector space of even dimension can be written in this form. Define
$$W_1 = \{(x, 0) \mid x \in W\}, \quad W_2 = \{(0, y) \mid y \in W\},$$
and, for $i = 3, 4, \dots, q+1$,
$$W_i = \{(x, xT_i) \mid x \in W\},$$
where $T_i$ is a non-singular linear transformation of $W$.
Show (a) that $W_1, W_2, \dots, W_{q+1}$ form a spread if and only if $T_i T_j^{-1}$ is fixed point free for $i \neq j$, and (b) that they form a spread if and only if $T_i - T_j$ is non-singular for $i \neq j$.
