Theorem 7.3 provides a crucial criterion for a weak quasifield to be a full quasifield: finite dimensionality over a sub-skewfield of its kernel suffices. The proof uses linear algebra — the left multiplication maps $L_a$ are linear transformations over $H$, and $L_a - L_b$ is non-singular for $a \neq b$, which is equivalent to the unique solvability of $ax = bx + c$. This result is exceptionally useful for verifying that a given algebraic system is a quasifield, particularly in the study of finite translation planes where finite-dimensionality is automatic.
