A permutation isomorphism is a stronger notion than group isomorphism: it requires not only that the abstract groups be isomorphic, but that there be a compatible bijection between the underlying sets being permuted. This concept captures when two permutation representations are "the same" in a geometric sense, which is essential for classifying collineation groups acting on different projective planes.
