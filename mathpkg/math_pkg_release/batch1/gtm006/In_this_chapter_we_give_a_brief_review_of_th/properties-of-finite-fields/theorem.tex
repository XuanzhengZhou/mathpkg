Let $q$ be a power of a prime $p$. Then there is a unique field $GF(q)$ and:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $q$ is a power of the prime $p$, then $GF(q)$ has characteristic $p$ and contains a subfield isomorphic to $GF(p)$.
\item[(ii)] The non-zero elements of $GF(q)$ form a cyclic multiplicative group of order $q-1$, and $GF(q)$ contains an element whose powers generate the entire multiplicative group.
\item[(iii)] If $p=2$ then every element of $GF(q)$ is a square, while if $p \neq 2$, then exactly half of the non-zero elements of $GF(q)$ are squares.
\item[(iv)] Every element of $GF(q)$ is a sum of two squares.
\end{enumerate}
