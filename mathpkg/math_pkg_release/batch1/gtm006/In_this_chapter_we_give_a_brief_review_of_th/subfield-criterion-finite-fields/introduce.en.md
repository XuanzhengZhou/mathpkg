This theorem characterizes exactly when one finite field contains another as a subfield: $GF(p^m)$ embeds into $GF(p^n)$ precisely when $m$ divides $n$. Moreover, the subfield is unique -- there is exactly one copy. This lattice structure of finite field extensions mirrors the divisibility lattice of positive integers and is essential for understanding the coordinatization of subplanes.
