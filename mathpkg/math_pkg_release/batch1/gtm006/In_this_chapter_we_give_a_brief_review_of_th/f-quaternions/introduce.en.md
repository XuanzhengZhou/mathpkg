The F-quaternions form a skewfield constructed from a field F by adjoining symbols i, j, k with the usual quaternion multiplication rules. When F is the real numbers, this yields Hamilton's quaternions. The quaternions are particularly useful as counterexamples to theorems that hold for fields but fail for non-commutative skewfields.
