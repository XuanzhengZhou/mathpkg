The orbit of a point under a permutation group is the set of all images of that point under group elements. Orbits partition the set. A group is transitive if there is only one orbit -- every point can be moved to every other point. Higher transitivity (doubly transitive, triply transitive, etc.) captures the ability to simultaneously map ordered tuples of distinct points, and is a key measure of the richness of a collineation group.
