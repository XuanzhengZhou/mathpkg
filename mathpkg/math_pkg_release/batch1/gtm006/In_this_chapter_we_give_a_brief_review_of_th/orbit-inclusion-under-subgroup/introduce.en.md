This simple but important result states that orbits of a subgroup are contained within the orbits of the full group. Consequently, each orbit of the larger group decomposes as a union of orbits of the subgroup. This is an immediate consequence of the definitions and is used repeatedly in analyzing how collineation subgroups act on projective planes.
