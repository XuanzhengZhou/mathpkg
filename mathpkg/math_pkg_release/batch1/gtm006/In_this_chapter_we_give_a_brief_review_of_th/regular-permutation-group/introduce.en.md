A permutation group is semi-regular if only the identity element fixes any point, and regular if it is both semi-regular and transitive. Regular groups act freely and transitively -- every point can be reached from any other by a unique group element. Regular permutation groups are the Frobenius kernels in Frobenius groups, which arise naturally in the study of translation planes and other structured projective planes.
