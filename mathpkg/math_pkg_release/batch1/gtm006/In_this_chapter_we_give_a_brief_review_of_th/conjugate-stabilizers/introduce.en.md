Points in the same orbit have conjugate stabilizers. This means that in a transitive permutation group, all point stabilizers are conjugate -- and therefore isomorphic. This fact allows us to speak of "the" stabilizer of a transitive group up to isomorphism, a crucial simplification in the theory of Frobenius groups and in analyzing collineation groups.
