To prove this result we note that the norms of $\alpha$ are merely the $(q+1)$-st powers of the elements of $K^*$ plus, of course, $0$. But since $K^*$ is a cyclic group of order $q^2-1$ and $F^*$ is a cyclic subgroup of order $q-1$ (see Result 1.4 part (ii)) the result now follows from elementary group theory. Specifically, in a cyclic group of order $q^2-1$, the map $x \mapsto x^{q+1}$ has as its image exactly the unique subgroup of order $(q^2-1)/(q+1) = q-1$, which is precisely $F^*$.
