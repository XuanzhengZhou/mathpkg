A projective plane has a coordinating planar ternary ring that is a nearfield if and only if, for a suitable choice of coordinates (with $k = [\infty]$), the plane is $(k,k)$-transitive and $(V,m)$-transitive, where $V$ is on $k$ but not on $m$, and either $V = (0)$ with $m$ containing $(\infty)$, or $V = (\infty)$ with $m$ containing $(0)$.
