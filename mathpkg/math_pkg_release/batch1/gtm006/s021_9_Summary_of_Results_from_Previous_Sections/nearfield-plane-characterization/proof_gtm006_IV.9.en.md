This equivalence is established in the coordinatization sections of Chapter IV. The $(k,k)$-transitivity ensures that addition in the PTR is associative and forms a group. The additional $(V,m)$-transitivity with $V$ on $k$ but not on $m$ provides the multiplicative group structure while not forcing the second distributive law (which would yield a division ring). Thus the PTR is a nearfield: a group under addition, nonzero elements form a group under multiplication, and one distributive law holds. The converse follows by constructing the required central collineations from the nearfield structure.
