This equivalence is established in Sections 2-8 of Chapter IV. The coordinatization process shows that if the plane is desarguesian, the PTR derived from any coordinatizing quadrangle satisfies the skewfield axioms (associative addition and multiplication, with multiplicative inverses for nonzero elements). Conversely, a skewfield PTR yields a desarguesian plane. The equivalence with full $(V,k)$-transitivity follows from the theory of central collineations: Desargues' theorem is equivalent to the existence of all $(V,k)$-central collineations. That one non-incident transitivity suffices (given all incident transitivities) is a consequence of the group structure of the collineation group.
