A quasifield is an algebraic structure where addition forms a group and nonzero elements form a loop under multiplication, with one distributive law holding. This characterization identifies translation planes: a projective plane is a translation plane (with respect to a line $k$) exactly when it admits $(k,k)$-transitivity for that line. In this case, the PTR obtained by choosing $k = [\infty]$ is a quasifield. An equivalent geometric condition is $(V,k)$-transitivity for two distinct points $V$ on $k$, which forces the full $(k,k)$-transitivity.
