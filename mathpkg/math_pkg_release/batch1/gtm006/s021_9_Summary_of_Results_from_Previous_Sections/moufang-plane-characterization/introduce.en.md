A projective plane is Moufang if it satisfies the Moufang condition, a weakening of Desargues' theorem. This characterization establishes the equivalence: the coordinate PTR is an alternative division ring (satisfying the inverse property or IP), which is equivalent to $(V,k)$-transitivity for all incident pairs. The Moufang condition admits several equivalent transitivity formulations: $(k,k)$-transitivity for all lines, $(V,V)$-transitivity for all points, or transitivity for just two distinct lines or points. In a Moufang plane, all coordinating PTRs are mutually isomorphic alternative division rings.
