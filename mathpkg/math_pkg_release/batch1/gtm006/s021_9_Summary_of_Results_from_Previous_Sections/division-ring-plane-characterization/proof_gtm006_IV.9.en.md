This equivalence follows from the coordinatization theory developed in Sections 2-8. When the plane is $(k,k)$-transitive, the addition in the PTR is associative and satisfies the group axioms. When the plane is also $(V,V)$-transitive for an incident $V$ on $k$, the multiplicative structure forms a group (with the zero element removed), yielding a division ring. The coordinatization choice $V = (\infty)$ and $k = [\infty]$ identifies these as the point and line at infinity in the affine construction. Conversely, if the PTR is a division ring, the central collineations with axis $k$ and center $V$ (and vice versa) exist, giving both transitivities.
