This equivalence is established in the coordinatization sections of Chapter IV. The $(V,V)$-transitivity provides the additive group structure in the PTR, while the additional $(V,W)$-transitivity gives the multiplicative group for nonzero elements. The fact that $W$ is not on $k$ (or the specific configuration described) ensures that only the right distributive law is enforced, not the left one. Thus the PTR is a right nearfield. The result is dual to the nearfield characterization, obtained by applying the duality principle of projective planes.
