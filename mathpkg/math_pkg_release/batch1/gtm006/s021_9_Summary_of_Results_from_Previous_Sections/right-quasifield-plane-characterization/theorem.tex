A projective plane has a coordinating planar ternary ring that is a right quasifield (and hence the plane is a dual translation plane) if and only if the plane is $(V,V)$-transitive for a unique point $V$ (which is then chosen as $(\infty)$). Equivalently, the plane is $(V,V)$-transitive for two distinct points $V$ on some line through a common point.
