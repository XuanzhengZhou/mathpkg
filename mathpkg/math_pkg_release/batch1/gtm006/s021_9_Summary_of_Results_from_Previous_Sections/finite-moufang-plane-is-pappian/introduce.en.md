A projective plane is called Moufang if it satisfies the Moufang condition (every coordinating planar ternary ring is an alternative division ring). In the finite case, every Moufang plane is necessarily pappian, meaning its coordinating PTR is a field. Consequently, every finite Moufang plane is also desarguesian. This result follows from the classification of finite alternative division rings, which are necessarily fields (by the Artin-Zorn theorem).
