This is a summary of equivalences proved in Sections 2-8 of Chapter IV. The equivalence between the field property of the coordinating PTR and the pappian condition is established by coordinatization: if the plane is pappian, the PTR constructed from any quadrangle satisfies the field axioms (associativity and commutativity of both addition and multiplication). Conversely, if a PTR is a field, the associated projective plane satisfies Pappus' theorem. The equivalence with $(V,k)$-transitivity for all $V$ and $k$ follows from the general theory of transitivity developed in Section 3 and the characterization of pappian planes via the complete collineation group.
