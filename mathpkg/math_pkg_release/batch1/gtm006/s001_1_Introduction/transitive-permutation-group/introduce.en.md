A permutation group is transitive if it can send any point to any other point. Higher transitivity ($t$-transitive) means any ordered $t$-tuple of distinct points can be mapped to any other. The collineation groups of projective planes over fields are typically 3-transitive on lines (projective line case) or 2-transitive on points (projective plane case).
