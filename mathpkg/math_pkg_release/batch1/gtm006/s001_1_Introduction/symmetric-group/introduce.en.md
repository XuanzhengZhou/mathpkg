The symmetric group $\operatorname{Sym}(\mathcal{S})$ is the group of all bijections from $\mathcal{S}$ to itself. For a finite set of size $n$, this is $S_n$, of order $n!$. Symmetric groups are universal objects in group theory (every group embeds in a symmetric group via Cayley's theorem), and are the natural setting for studying permutation actions in projective geometry.
