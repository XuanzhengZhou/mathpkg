Result 1.17 is a concise but powerful structural result: if an abelian group acts transitively on a set, the action must automatically be regular (semiregular). This follows because in an abelian group, the stabilizers of different points are conjugate (by transitivity), but in an abelian group conjugates are equal, so all stabilizers are equal and must be trivial since the intersection of all stabilizers fixes everything. This has important consequences for abelian collineation groups.
