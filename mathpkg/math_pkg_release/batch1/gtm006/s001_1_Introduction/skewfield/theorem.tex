A skewfield is a non-empty set $K$ with two binary operations $+$ and $\cdot$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Both operations are associative: $a+(b+c)=(a+b)+c$ and $a(bc)=(ab)c$.
\item Addition is commutative: $a+b=b+a$.
\item Each operation has an identity: $\exists 0 \in K: 0+b=b+0=b$, and $\exists 1 \in K: 1b=b1=b$, with $0 \neq 1$.
\item For each $b \in K$, $\exists -b \in K$ such that $b+(-b)=0$; for each $b \neq 0$, $\exists b^{-1} \in K$ such that $bb^{-1}=b^{-1}b=1$.
\item Distributivity: $a(b+c)=ab+ac$ and $(a+b)c=ac+bc$.
\end{enumerate}
