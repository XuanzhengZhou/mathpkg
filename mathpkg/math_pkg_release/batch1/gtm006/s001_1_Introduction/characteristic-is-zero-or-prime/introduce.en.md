Result 1.1 states a basic structural fact: the characteristic of any skewfield (or field) cannot be a composite integer. If $n = ab$ with $a, b > 1$ and $n \cdot 1 = 0$, then $(a \cdot 1)(b \cdot 1) = 0$, so one factor must be zero since a skewfield has no zero divisors. This forces the minimal positive $n$ with $n \cdot 1 = 0$ to be prime, or no such $n$ exists (characteristic 0).
