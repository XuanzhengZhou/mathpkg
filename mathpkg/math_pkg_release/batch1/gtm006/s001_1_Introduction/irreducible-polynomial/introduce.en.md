An irreducible polynomial over a field $K$ is a non-constant polynomial that cannot be factored as a product of two non-constant polynomials over $K$. Irreducible polynomials play the role of prime elements in the polynomial ring $K[x]$ and are essential for constructing field extensions and finite fields of non-prime order.
