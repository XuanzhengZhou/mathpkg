Frobenius' theorem states that every finite Frobenius group possesses a regular normal subgroup (the Frobenius kernel), making it a semi-direct product of the kernel by any point stabilizer. The proof of this theorem required character theory until relatively recently. The Frobenius kernel captures the translation-like aspect of Frobenius groups.
