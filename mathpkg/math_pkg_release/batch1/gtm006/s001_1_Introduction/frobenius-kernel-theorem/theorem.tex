Let $\Gamma$ be a finite Frobenius group on a set $\mathcal{S}$. Then $\Gamma$ has a normal subgroup $\Delta$ which is regular on $\mathcal{S}$, such that $\Gamma = \Delta \Gamma_x$ for any stabilizer $\Gamma_x$ (and hence $\Gamma$ is the semi-direct product of $\Delta$ by $\Gamma_x$). The normal subgroup $\Delta$ is called the Frobenius kernel of $\Gamma$.
