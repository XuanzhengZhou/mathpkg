For any prime power $q = p^n$, there exists a unique (up to isomorphism) finite field $\mathrm{GF}(q)$ with $q$ elements. The multiplicative group is always cyclic, and the fields have characteristic $p$. Part (iv) states that every element is a sum of two squares, a property used in the study of polarities and conics over finite fields.
