Let $K = \mathrm{GF}(q)$ be the finite field with $q = p^n$ elements where $p$ is prime. Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $K$ has characteristic $p$; every element $a$ of $K$ satisfies $a + a + \cdots + a$ ($p$ times) $= 0$.
\item[(ii)] The multiplicative group $K^*$ of $K$ is cyclic of order $q-1$.
\item[(iii)] If $p=2$ then every element of $\mathrm{GF}(q)$ is a square; if $p \neq 2$, exactly half the non-zero elements are squares.
\item[(iv)] Every element of $\mathrm{GF}(q)$ is a sum of two squares.
\end{itemize}
