Result 1.6 establishes that finite fields (Galois fields) $GF(p^n)$ exist for all prime powers and are unique up to isomorphism. The construction uses the splitting field of $x^{p^n} - x$ over $GF(p)$. This theorem guarantees that the symbol $GF(q)$ is well-defined and that finite projective planes coordinatized by finite fields of the same order are isomorphic as algebraic structures.
