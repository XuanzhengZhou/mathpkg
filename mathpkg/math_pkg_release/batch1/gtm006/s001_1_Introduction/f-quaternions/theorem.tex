Let $F$ be a field, and let $K$ be a vector space of dimension 4 over $F$ with basis elements $1, i, j, k$ (where 1 is the identity of $F$). The elements of $K$ are of the form $a + bi + cj + dk$ with $a, b, c, d$ in $F$, with addition given componentwise and multiplication given by the rule:
$$\left( a + bi + cj + dk \right) \left( a_1 + b_1 i + c_1 j + d_1 k \right) = \left( aa_1 - bb_1 - cc_1 - dd_1 \right) + \left( ab_1 + ba_1 + cd_1 - dc_1 \right) i + \left( ac_1 - bd_1 + ca_1 + db_1 \right) j + \left( ad_1 + bc_1 - cb_1 + da_1 \right) k.$$
Then $K$ is a skewfield which we call the $F$-quaternions or, if $F$ is the reals, merely the quaternions.
