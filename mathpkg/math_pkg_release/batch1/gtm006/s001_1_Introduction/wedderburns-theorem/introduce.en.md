Wedderburn's Theorem (Result 1.3) is a celebrated result in algebra: every finite division ring is necessarily commutative, and therefore a field. This means there are no finite non-commutative skewfields. The theorem is significant for the study of finite projective planes because it restricts the possible coordinate structures: all finite coordinate rings are fields. The proof uses a clever counting argument involving the class equation of the multiplicative group.
