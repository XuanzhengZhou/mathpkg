The orbit of a point $x$ under a permutation group $\Gamma$ is the set of all images of $x$ under elements of $\Gamma$. Orbits partition the underlying set $\mathcal{S}$ into equivalence classes. The orbit concept is fundamental to studying how collineation groups act on the points and lines of a projective plane.
