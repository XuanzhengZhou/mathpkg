Frobenius groups are transitive permutation groups where non-identity elements fix at most one point. They arise naturally from the affine group of a skewfield: the mappings $x \mapsto xa + b$ for $a \in G \leq K^*$, $b \in K$ form a Frobenius group. Finite Frobenius groups always possess a regular normal subgroup called the Frobenius kernel.
