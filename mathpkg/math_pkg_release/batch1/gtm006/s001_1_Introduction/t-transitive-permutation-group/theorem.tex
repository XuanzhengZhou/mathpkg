A permutation group $\Gamma$ on $\mathcal{S}$ is $t$-transitive if for every pair of ordered $t$-tuples of distinct elements in $\mathcal{S}$, there exists $\gamma \in \Gamma$ sending the first $t$-tuple onto the second. One-transitive is the same as transitive. A two-transitive group is called doubly transitive.
