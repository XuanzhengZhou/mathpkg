A regular permutation group combines transitivity with semiregularity: there is exactly one group element mapping any given point to any other. Regular groups are isomorphic to their underlying sets as $\Gamma$-sets via the orbit map. Singer groups of finite projective planes are regular cyclic collineation groups, and their existence is a deep structural property linked to difference sets.
