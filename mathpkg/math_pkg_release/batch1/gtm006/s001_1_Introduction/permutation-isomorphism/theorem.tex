If $\Gamma$ is a permutation group on $\mathcal{S}$ and $\Sigma$ is a permutation group on $\mathcal{T}$, a permutation isomorphism is an isomorphism $\alpha : \Gamma \to \Sigma$ together with a bijection $\beta : \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{T}$ such that for all $s \in \mathcal{S}$ and $\gamma \in \Gamma$: $(s^\gamma)^\beta = (s^\beta)^{\alpha(\gamma)}$.
