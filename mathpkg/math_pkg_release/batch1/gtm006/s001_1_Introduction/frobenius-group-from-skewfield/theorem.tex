Let $K$ be a skewfield and let $G$ be a multiplicative subgroup of $K^*$. For $a \in G$, $b \in K$, define the mapping $\phi(a,b) : K \to K$ by
$$x^{\phi(a,b)} = xa + b.$$
The set $\Gamma$ of all such mappings forms a group with $\phi(a,b)\phi(c,d) = \phi(ac, bc + d)$. This group is Frobenius with Frobenius kernel $\Delta = \{\phi(1,b) \mid b \in K\}$ (isomorphic to the additive group of $K$). In the finite case, this is consistent with Result 1.18.
