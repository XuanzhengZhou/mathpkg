The orbit of an element under a permutation group is the set of all images of that element under the group action. Orbits form a partition of the set on which the group acts. When a group has only one orbit, it acts transitively. The orbit concept is fundamental for analyzing collineation groups of projective planes: the transitivity of the collineation group on points or lines is a key geometric property.
