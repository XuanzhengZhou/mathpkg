Lemma 9.9 establishes a clean dichotomy for the division ring construction over finite fields: associativity holds precisely when the anti-automorphism $	heta$ is the identity. The proof proceeds by analyzing the associator $[r, s, t]$ of three elements and using the associative condition to deduce constraints on $	heta$, $a$, and $b$. The computation shows that associativity forces $	heta^2 = 1$ and $b = 0$, and then that $a$ must lie in the fixed field of $	heta$, which ultimately forces $	heta = 1$.
