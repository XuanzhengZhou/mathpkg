Let $q$ be an odd prime power and $F = GF(q^2)$. The automorphism group $\Gamma$ of $F$ over $GF(q)$ is cyclic of order $2$, generated by the Frobenius map $x \mapsto x^q$. The fixed field is $K = GF(q)$.

The norm map $\nu: F^* \to K^*$ is given by $x^{\nu} = x^{1+q}$. Since $q$ is odd, $q \equiv 1 \pmod{2}$, so $1+q$ is even. The image $N = (F^*)^{\nu}$ is a subgroup of $K^*$ of index dividing $2$.

To construct an André nearfield that is not a field, we need a homomorphism $\phi: N \to \Gamma$ (by the Corollary to Lemma 9.4) that is non-trivial on some element of $N$ other than the identity (by Lemma 9.4(iv)). Since $\Gamma \cong \mathbb{Z}_2$, we can define $\phi$ to send all elements of $N$ except those mapping under some rule to the non-trivial automorphism. The specific construction ensures the kernel is exactly $K = GF(q)$, providing a proper André nearfield that coordinatizes a non-desarguesian plane of order $q^2$.
