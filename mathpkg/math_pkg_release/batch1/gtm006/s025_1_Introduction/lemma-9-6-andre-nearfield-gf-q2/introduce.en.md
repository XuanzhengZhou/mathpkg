Lemma 9.6 guarantees the existence of André nearfields (associative André quasifields) over quadratic extensions of finite fields of odd characteristic. These nearfields have the base field $GF(q)$ as their kernel and are proper nearfields (not division rings). This construction is particularly important because it provides concrete finite nearfields that coordinatize non-desarguesian translation planes, complementing the Hall nearfield construction which exists only over $GF(3)$ in the associative case.
