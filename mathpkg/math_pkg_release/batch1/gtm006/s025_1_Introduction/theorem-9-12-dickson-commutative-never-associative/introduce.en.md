Theorem 9.12 is the fundamental result about Dickson commutative division rings: they are genuine division rings (no zero divisors), they are commutative, but they are never associative. The proof that $D$ is a division ring proceeds by showing that the equation $(x + \lambda y)(z + \lambda t) = 0$ implies either $x + \lambda y = 0$ or $z + \lambda t = 0$, using the fact that $a$ is a non-square to derive a contradiction from the assumption that both factors are non-zero.
