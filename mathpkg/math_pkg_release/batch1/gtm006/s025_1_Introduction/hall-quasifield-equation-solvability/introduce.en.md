This proposition establishes that the Hall construction does indeed produce a quasifield. The proof proceeds by reducing the defining equations to a linear system and analyzing the solvability conditions. The key observation is that the irreducibility of $f(s)$ over $F$ guarantees that the right-hand side of certain derived equations is always non-zero, which forces a unique solution in all cases except the three exceptional ones listed. Since finite dimensionality over the kernel is already established, being a weak quasifield suffices to guarantee that $H$ is a full quasifield.
