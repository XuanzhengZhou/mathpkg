Let $F = GF(p^n)$ where $p$ is an odd prime and $n > 1$, and let $a \in F$ be any element which is not a square in $F$. Let $\theta$ be an automorphism of $F$ given by $x^{\theta} = x^{p^r}$ where $1 \leq r < n$. Define $D$ as a two-dimensional vector space over $F$ with basis $\{1, \lambda\}$, with addition defined componentwise and multiplication defined by:

$$(x + \lambda y)(z + \lambda t) = (xz + a y^{\theta} t^{\theta}) + \lambda(yz + xt).$$

Then $D$ is called a **Dickson commutative division ring**.
