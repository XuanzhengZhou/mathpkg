This construction produces a broad class of division rings from a given skewfield $F$ equipped with an anti-automorphism $	heta$. The resulting system $D$ is a two-dimensional vector space over $F$ with a twisted multiplication that generalizes the classical Cayley-Dickson construction (which corresponds to the case $F = \mathbb{R}$, $	heta = 	ext{id}$, $a = -1$). The construction is particularly fruitful over finite fields, where $	heta$ must be an automorphism, leading to additional structural properties. When specialized to the quaternions with $a = -1, b = 0$, one obtains the Cayley-Dickson algebra, a non-associative division ring satisfying both inverse properties.
