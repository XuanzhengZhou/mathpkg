Theorem 9.5 completely determines which finite fields admit proper (non-field) André quasifields. By Lemma 9.4, constructing a non-field André quasifield reduces to finding a map $\phi$ from a proper subfield $K$ into the integers modulo $m$ (where $m$ is the dimension of $F$ over $K$) such that some non-identity element of $K$ maps non-trivially. For odd $p$ with $n \geq 2$, one can use $K = GF(p)$ and map $-1$ to $1$. For even $p = 2$, a proper intermediate subfield exists only when $n$ is composite, giving the necessary flexibility for non-trivial $\phi$.
