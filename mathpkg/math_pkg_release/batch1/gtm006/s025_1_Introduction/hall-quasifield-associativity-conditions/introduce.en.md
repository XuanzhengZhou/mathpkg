This proposition derives necessary algebraic conditions for a Hall quasifield to be associative. The proof uses the associativity condition $\lambda(x\lambda) = (\lambda x)\lambda$ for the basis element $\lambda$ to obtain the equations $bx = bx^{-1}$ and $ax = a$. These conditions severely restrict the possible fields $F$: only $GF(2)$ (where no conclusion can be drawn) and $GF(3)$ (with $a=0$, $b 
eq 0$) can support an associative Hall quasifield. This shows that Hall quasifields are almost never associative division rings.
