Lemma 9.4 gives a precise characterization of when an André quasifield is actually a division ring (or equivalently, a field). The four equivalent conditions show that André quasifields are "almost never" division rings unless the mapping $\phi$ is trivial on $N$. The proof uses the right distributive law to derive identities involving the automorphism images, ultimately showing that if $F_{\phi}$ is a division ring, then the mapping $\phi$ must send every element of $N$ to the identity automorphism, which forces $F_{\phi}$ to be isomorphic to the original field $F$.
