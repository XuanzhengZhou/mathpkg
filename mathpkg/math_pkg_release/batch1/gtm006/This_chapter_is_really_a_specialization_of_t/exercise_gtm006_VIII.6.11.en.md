Generalize the results on isotopy in this chapter to prove: if $\mathcal{P}_1$ and $\mathcal{P}_2$ are projective planes coordinatized by planar ternary rings $(R_1, T_1)$ and $(R_2, T_2)$ respectively, then there is an isomorphism from $\mathcal{P}_1$ to $\mathcal{P}_2$ which sends the points $(\infty)_1$, $(0)_1$ and $(0,0)$ onto $(\infty)_2$, $(0)_2$ and $(0,0)_2$ respectively if and only if there is a triple $(X, Y, Z)$ of one-to-one mappings of $R_1^*$ onto $R_2^*$ such that
$$(T_1(a, b, c))Z = T_2(aX, bY, cZ)$$
for all $a, b, c$ in $R_1^*$.
