If $D_1, D_2$ are isotopic division rings with respective multiplications $\cdot$ and $\odot$, then $D_2$ is called a \textbf{principal isotope} of $D_1$ if there exist $a, b \in D_1$ such that
$$x \odot y = xR_a^{-1} \cdot yL_b^{-1} \quad \text{for all } x, y \in D_2,$$
where $R_a$ is right multiplication by $a$ and $L_b$ is left multiplication by $b$ in $D_1$.
