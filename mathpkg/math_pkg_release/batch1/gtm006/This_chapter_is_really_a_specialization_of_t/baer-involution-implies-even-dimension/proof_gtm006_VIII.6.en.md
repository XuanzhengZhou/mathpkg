Let $eta = (P, Q, R)$ be a Baer involution in $\Delta_1$. The points $(x, 0)$ which are fixed by $eta$ are, on the one hand, equal in number to the square-root of the order of $D$ (since a Baer involution fixes a Baer subplane pointwise), and on the other hand are exactly those points for which $x$ is in the subspace $V$ of $D$ belonging to the eigenvalue $1$ of the linear transformation $Q$ (over $N_m$ or $N_r$). Thus $\dim D = 2 \dim V$, so the dimension is even. A similar argument for fixed points $(0, y)$ proves the claim for $N_l$. $\square$
