The right nucleus of a division ring $D$ consists of those elements that associate with all other elements when placed on the right. In the context of a quasifield, the right nucleus coincides with the kernel of $D$. It is one of the three semi-nuclei that play a central role in the study of division ring planes and their collineation groups.
