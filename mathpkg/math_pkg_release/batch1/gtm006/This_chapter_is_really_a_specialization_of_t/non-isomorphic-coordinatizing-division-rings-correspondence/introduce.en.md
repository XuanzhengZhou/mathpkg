When multiple division rings can coordinatize the same projective plane, they need not be isomorphic. This theorem establishes a precise correspondence: the isomorphism classes of coordinatizing division rings correspond bijectively to the orbits of the autotopism group acting on points off the autotopism triangle. The only known planes for which this number is one are the desarguesian planes.
