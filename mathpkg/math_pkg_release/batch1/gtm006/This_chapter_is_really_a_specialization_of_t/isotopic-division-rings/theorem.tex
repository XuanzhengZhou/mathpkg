Two division rings $D_1, D_2$ are called \textbf{isotopic} if there exists a triple $(P, Q, R)$ of non-singular additive mappings from $D_1$ onto $D_2$ such that
$$(xP) \odot (yQ) = (x \cdot y)R \quad \text{for all } x, y \in D_1,$$
where $\cdot$ is the multiplication of $D_1$ and $\odot$ is the multiplication of $D_2$. The triple $(P, Q, R)$ is called an \textbf{isotopism}.
