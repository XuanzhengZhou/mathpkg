Let $\pi$ be any collineation in $\Pi$ and suppose $(m)^\pi = (0)$ and $(a, b)^\pi = (0, 0)$. Since $\mathcal{P}(D)$ is a translation plane there is a translation $lpha$ with $(a, b)^lpha = (0, 0)$, while the $([\infty], [0])$-transitivity of $\mathcal{P}(D)$ implies the existence of a $([\infty], [0])$-elation $eta$ with $(m)^eta = (0)$. The collineation $lphaeta$ is in $\Sigma$ and $(lphaeta)^{-1}\pi$ is in $\Lambda$, so that $\Pi = \Sigma \cdot \Lambda$.

Suppose $\gamma \in \Sigma \cap \Lambda$. Then there exists a translation $\lambda$ and a shear $\mu$ such that $\gamma = \lambda\mu$. But $(0)^\gamma = (0)$ (since $(0)$ is on the axis of $\lambda$), so $(0) = (0)^\gamma = (0)^\mu$. Thus, since the identity is the only shear fixing $(0)$, $\mu = 1$ and $\gamma = \lambda$. But $\lambda$ is a translation fixing $(\infty)$ and $(0)$, and the only such translation is the identity. Hence $\gamma = 1$ and $\Sigma \cap \Lambda = 1$. $\square$
