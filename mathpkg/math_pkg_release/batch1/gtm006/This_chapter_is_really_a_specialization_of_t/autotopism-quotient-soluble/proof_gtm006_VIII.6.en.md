For any fixed $i$ ($i = r, l, m$), the mapping which sends each element of $\Delta$ onto its companion automorphism of $N_i$ is easily seen to be a homomorphism of $\Delta$ into the automorphism group of $N_i$. Since $D$ is finite, each semi-nucleus $N_i$ is a finite skewfield, hence a finite field by Wedderburn's little theorem. The automorphism group of any finite field is cyclic. Thus this homomorphism has a cyclic image.

The group $\Delta_1$ is the intersection of the kernels of the three homomorphisms obtained in this way. Hence $\Delta/\Delta_1$, which embeds into the product of three normal cyclic subgroups, is abelian and therefore soluble. $\square$
