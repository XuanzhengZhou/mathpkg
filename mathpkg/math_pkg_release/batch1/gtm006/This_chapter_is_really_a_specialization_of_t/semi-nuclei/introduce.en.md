The three semi-nuclei of a division ring $D$ -- the right nucleus $N_r$, left nucleus $N_l$, and middle nucleus $N_m$ -- are among the most helpful notions in the study of division ring planes, particularly in determining their collineation groups. Each consists of elements that satisfy a specific associative law with respect to all other elements.
