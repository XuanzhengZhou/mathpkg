The \textbf{semi-nuclei} of a division ring $D$ are the right nucleus $N_r$, the left nucleus $N_l$, and the middle nucleus $N_m$, defined respectively as:
\begin{align*}
N_r &= \{ d \in D \mid (xy)d = x(yd) \text{ for all } x, y \in D \}, \\
N_l &= \{ d \in D \mid d(xy) = (dx)y \text{ for all } x, y \in D \}, \\
N_m &= \{ d \in D \mid (xd)y = x(dy) \text{ for all } x, y \in D \}.
\end{align*}
