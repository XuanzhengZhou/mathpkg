Let $D$ be a division ring. Let $A$ be the set of all triples $(P, Q, R)$ of non-singular additive mappings of $D$ onto $D$ such that
$$(xP)(yQ) = (xy)R \quad \text{for all } x, y \in D.$$
Define a binary operation on $A$ by $(P_1, Q_1, R_1) \circ (P_2, Q_2, R_2) = (P_1 P_2, Q_1 Q_2, R_1 R_2)$. Then $A$ is a group under this operation.
