Segre's Theorem establishes that an oval in a finite projective plane is the canonical non-desarguesian generalization of a conic. In a pappian (desarguesian) plane, every conic is an oval, but the converse need not hold in arbitrary projective planes. Segre's result identifies ovals as the precise combinatorial structure that extends the notion of a conic beyond the desarguesian setting, preserving the key property that no three points are collinear.
