When a polarity $\alpha$ of a finite projective plane attains the lower Baer bound $a(\alpha) = n+1$, the geometric configuration of its absolute points is completely determined by the parity of the order $n$. In even order, the $n+1$ absolute points are collinear, forming a line. In odd order, the absolute points form an oval — a set of $n+1$ points with no three collinear, which is the natural non-desarguesian generalization of a conic.
