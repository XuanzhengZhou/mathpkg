When the order $n$ of a finite projective plane is not a perfect square, the number of absolute points of any polarity $\alpha$ is forced to the minimal possible value $a(\alpha) = n+1$. This result shows that the upper bound $n^{3/2}+1$ is attainable only when $n$ is a square, and further implies that for non-square orders the geometry of the absolute points is completely determined by the parity of $n$ (collinear for even $n$, an oval for odd $n$).
