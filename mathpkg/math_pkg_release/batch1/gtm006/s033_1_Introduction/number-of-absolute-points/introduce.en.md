For a polarity $\alpha$ of a finite projective plane of order $n$, the notation $a(\alpha)$ denotes the number of absolute points of $\alpha$. An absolute point is a point that is incident with its image under the polarity. The quantity $a(\alpha)$ is a fundamental invariant that governs the geometric structure of the polarity, and its possible values are constrained by the Baer-Seib bounds.
