Let $K$ be a skewfield (division ring). A set $V$ is a **right vector space over $K$** if:
\begin{itemize}
\item $V$ is an additive abelian group.
\item For every $v \in V$ and $k \in K$, the product $vk \in V$ is defined.
\item For all $u, v \in V$ and $k, \ell \in K$:
  $$(u + v)k = uk + vk, \quad v(k + \ell) = vk + v\ell, \quad v(k\ell) = (vk)\ell, \quad v \cdot 1_K = v.$$
\end{itemize}
The scalars always appear on the right of the vectors.
