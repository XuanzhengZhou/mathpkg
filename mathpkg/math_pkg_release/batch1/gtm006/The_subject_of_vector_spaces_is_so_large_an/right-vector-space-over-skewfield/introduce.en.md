A right vector space over a skewfield $K$ is the natural counterpart to a left vector space, where scalars are written on the right of vectors. All the usual vector space axioms hold, but with scalar multiplication $vk$ defined for $v \in V$ and $k \in K$. The distinction between left and right vector spaces becomes essential when the ground ring is a non-commutative skewfield, since the order of multiplication matters.
