The text states this as an analog of the field case without giving an explicit proof. The proof is identical to the field case: choosing a basis $\{e_1,\dots,e_n\}$ yields a coordinate representation, and the mapping $v = \sum x_i e_i \mapsto \sum x_i f_i$ is a well-defined isomorphism of left vector spaces since scalar multiplication takes place on the left and is thus compatible with the coordinate representation.
