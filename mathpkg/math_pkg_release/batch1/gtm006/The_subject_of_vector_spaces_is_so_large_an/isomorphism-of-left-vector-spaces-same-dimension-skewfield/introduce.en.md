Over a skewfield $K$, the isomorphism theorem weakens: only left vector spaces of the same dimension are guaranteed to be isomorphic. This is because the scalar multiplication must respect the left-module structure, and the non-commutativity of $K$ means that a left vector space and a right vector space of the same dimension need not be isomorphic.
