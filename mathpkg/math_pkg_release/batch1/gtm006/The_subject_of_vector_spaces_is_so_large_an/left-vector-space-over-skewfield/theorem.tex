Let $K$ be a skewfield (division ring). A set $V$ is a **left vector space over $K$** if:
\begin{itemize}
\item $V$ is an additive abelian group.
\item For every $v \in V$ and $k \in K$, the product $kv \in V$ is defined.
\item For all $u, v \in V$ and $k, \ell \in K$:
  $$k(u + v) = ku + kv, \quad (k + \ell)v = kv + \ell v, \quad (k\ell)v = k(\ell v), \quad 1_K \cdot v = v.$$
\end{itemize}
The scalars always appear on the left of the vectors.
