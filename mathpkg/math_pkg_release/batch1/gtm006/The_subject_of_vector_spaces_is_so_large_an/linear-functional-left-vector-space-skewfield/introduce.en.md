A linear functional on a left vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$ is a $K$-linear map from $V$ to $K$. The notation places the functional $f'$ on the right of vectors because, as the subsequent discussion shows, the set of linear functionals naturally forms a right vector space over $K$. This convention anticipates the duality theory where $V'$ is a right vector space when $V$ is a left vector space.
