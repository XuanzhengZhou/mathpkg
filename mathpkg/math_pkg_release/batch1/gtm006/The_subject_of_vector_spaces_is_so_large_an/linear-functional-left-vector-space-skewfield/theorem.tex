Let $V$ be a left vector space over a skewfield $K$. A **linear functional** on $V$ is a mapping $f' \colon V \to K$ (written with the function symbol on the right) such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] For all $v, w \in V$: $(v + w)f' = vf' + wf'$.
\item[(ii)] For all $v \in V$ and $k \in K$: $(kv)f' = k(vf')$.
\end{enumerate}
The notation $vf'$ denotes the image of $v$ under the linear functional $f'$.
