A correlation is a duality of a projective plane — a bijection between points and lines that preserves incidence. If such a mapping exists, the plane is self-dual. A polarity is a correlation that is its own inverse (order two). Correlations and polarities are fundamental to studying symmetries and configurations in projective geometry, particularly the theory of absolute elements.
