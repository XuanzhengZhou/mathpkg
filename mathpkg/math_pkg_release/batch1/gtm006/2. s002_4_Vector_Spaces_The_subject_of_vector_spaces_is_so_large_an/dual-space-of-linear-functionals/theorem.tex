Let $V$ be a left vector space over the skewfield $K$, and let $V'$ be the set of all linear functionals on $V$. For $f', g' \in V'$ and $b \in K$, define:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $f' + g'$ by: $v(f' + g') = vf' + vg'$ for all $v \in V$.
\item[(ii)] $f'b$ by: $v(f'b) = (vf')b$ for all $v \in V$.
\end{enumerate}

Under these operations, $V'$ is a \textit{right vector space over $K$}, called the \textit{dual space} of $V$. If $V$ is a right vector space, then $V'$ is a left vector space.

If $e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n$ is a basis of $V$, then a linear functional $f'$ is completely determined by the $n$-tuple $(e_1 f', e_2 f', \dots, e_n f')$, which is viewed as a column vector (the transpose of a row vector).
