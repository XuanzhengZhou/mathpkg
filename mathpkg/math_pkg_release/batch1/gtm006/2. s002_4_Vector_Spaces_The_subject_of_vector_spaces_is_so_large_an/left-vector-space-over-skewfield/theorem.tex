Let $K$ be a skewfield. A set $V$ is called a \textit{left vector space over $K$} if for every $v \in V$ and $k \in K$, the product $kv$ is defined as a vector of $V$, and the following properties hold:
\begin{itemize}
\item $(k_1 + k_2)v = k_1 v + k_2 v$ for all $k_1, k_2 \in K$, $v \in V$;
\item $k(v_1 + v_2) = k v_1 + k v_2$ for all $k \in K$, $v_1, v_2 \in V$;
\item $(k_1 k_2)v = k_1(k_2 v)$ for all $k_1, k_2 \in K$, $v \in V$;
\item $1_K \cdot v = v$ for all $v \in V$.
\end{itemize}
with the scalars always written on the left of the vectors. All left vector spaces of dimension $n$ over the same skewfield $K$ are isomorphic.
