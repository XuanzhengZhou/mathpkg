Result 1.24 specializes Result 1.23 to the case of a commutative field. Over a field, every automorphism-collineation that preserves all 1-dimensional subspaces is forced to be a scalar multiplication (i.e., a linear transformation in the centre of $GL(V)$). This means that the subgroup $N$ collapses to the scalar matrices: the semi-linear component (the field automorphism) must be trivial. In geometric terms, over a field, the only collineations of the projective space $\mathrm{PG}(V)$ that fix every point are the identity — there are no non-trivial field automorphisms that preserve all lines through the origin.
