Given a non-singular semi-linear transformation $\alpha$ of a left vector space $V$, represented by a matrix $A$ and field automorphism $\sigma$, one defines an induced transformation $\alpha'$ on the dual space $V'$. The definition uses the inverse matrix $A^{-1}$ and the same automorphism $\sigma$, acting on column vectors (the coordinates of linear functionals). This construction is designed so that the natural pairing $\langle v, w' \rangle = v w'$ (the matrix product of a row vector with a column vector) is compatible with the actions: $\langle v^\alpha, (w')^{\alpha'} \rangle = \langle v, w' \rangle^\sigma$. This compatibility is the essential Lemma for Result 1.26.
