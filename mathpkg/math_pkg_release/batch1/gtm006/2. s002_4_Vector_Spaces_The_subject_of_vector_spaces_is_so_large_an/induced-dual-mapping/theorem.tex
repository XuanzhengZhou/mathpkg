Let $V$ be a finite dimensional left vector space over the skewfield $K$, and let $V'$ be its dual space. If $\alpha$ is a non-singular semi-linear transformation of $V$ onto $V$ with associated automorphism $\sigma$, then the induced mapping $\alpha'$ on $V'$ (as defined above) satisfies:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\alpha'$ is a non-singular semi-linear transformation of $V'$ onto $V'$, with the same associated automorphism $\sigma$.
\item[(ii)] For all $v \in V$ and $w' \in V'$, $v^\alpha \cdot (w')^{\alpha'} = (v \cdot w')^\sigma$, where $\cdot$ denotes the natural pairing (matrix product).
\item[(iii)] The mapping $\alpha \mapsto \alpha'$ is an isomorphism of $\Gamma L(V)$ onto $\Gamma L(V')$, with the property that $(\alpha')' = \alpha$.
\item[(iv)] This isomorphism sends $GL(V)$ onto $GL(V')$.
\end{enumerate}

Note that if $V$ is a right vector space, then $V'$ is a left vector space; in this case vectors of $V$ are represented by column vectors and vectors of $V'$ by row vectors.
