Result 1.26 establishes a fundamental duality for semi-linear groups: the map $\alpha \mapsto \alpha'$ sending a semi-linear transformation of $V$ to the induced transformation on the dual space $V'$ is an involutive isomorphism between $\Gamma L(V)$ and $\Gamma L(V')$, and it restricts to an isomorphism between the general linear groups $GL(V)$ and $GL(V')$. This result justifies representing vectors of a left vector space as row vectors and linear functionals as column vectors (or vice versa for right vector spaces), and it ensures that the correspondence $\alpha \leftrightarrow \alpha'$ respects both the group structure and the pairing. This duality is essential for the theory of correlations and polarities in projective geometry (Chapter II).
