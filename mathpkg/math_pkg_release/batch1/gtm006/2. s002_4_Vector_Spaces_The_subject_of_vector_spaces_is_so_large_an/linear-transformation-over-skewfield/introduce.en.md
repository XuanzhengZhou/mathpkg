A linear transformation over a skewfield is the special case of a semi-linear transformation where the associated automorphism is the identity. In matrix terms, over a left vector space with a chosen basis, a linear transformation is represented by a square matrix $(a_{ij})$ sending a row vector $(x_1, \dots, x_n)$ to $(\sum_i x_i a_{i1}, \dots, \sum_i x_i a_{in})$. The set of all invertible linear transformations of $V$ onto itself forms the general linear group $GL(V)$, which is a normal subgroup of the general semi-linear group $\Gamma L(V)$.
