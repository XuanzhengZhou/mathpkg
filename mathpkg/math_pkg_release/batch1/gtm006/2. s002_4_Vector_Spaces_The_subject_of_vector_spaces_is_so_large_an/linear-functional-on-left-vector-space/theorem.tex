Let $V$ be a left vector space over the skewfield $K$. A \textit{linear functional} on $V$ is a mapping $f': V \to K$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] For all $v, w \in V$, $(v + w)f' = vf' + wf'$.
\item[(ii)] For all $v \in V$ and $k \in K$, $(kv)f' = k(vf')$.
\end{enumerate}

We write linear functionals as operators on the right (using the notation $vf'$ rather than $f'(v)$) in anticipation of the duality with right vector spaces.
