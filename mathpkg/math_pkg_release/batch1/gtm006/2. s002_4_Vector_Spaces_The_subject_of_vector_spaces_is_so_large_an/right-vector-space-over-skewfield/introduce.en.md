A right vector space over a skewfield $K$ is the obvious analogue of a left vector space, with scalar multiplication written on the right: $vk$ rather than $kv$. Over a commutative field, the distinction between left and right vector spaces disappears, but over a non-commutative skewfield they represent different algebraic structures. In the context of linear algebra for projective geometry, one must carefully track whether vector spaces are left or right.
