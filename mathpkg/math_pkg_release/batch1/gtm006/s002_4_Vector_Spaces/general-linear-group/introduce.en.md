The general linear group consists of all invertible linear transformations of a vector space. For an $n$-dimensional space over a field $K$, $GL(V) \cong GL(n, K)$, the group of invertible $n \times n$ matrices over $K$. $GL(V)$ is a normal subgroup of $\Gamma L(V)$ and is fundamental to the study of projective collineations.
