When working with vector spaces over skewfields (division rings) rather than fields, it is essential to distinguish left and right scalar multiplication because multiplication in the skewfield may not be commutative. A left vector space has scalars acting on the left. This distinction is crucial for the coordinatization of projective planes, where the coordinate structure may be a skewfield rather than a field.
