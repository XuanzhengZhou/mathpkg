Result 1.23 identifies the subgroup of $\Gamma L(V)$ that preserves every line through the origin. Over a skewfield, this subgroup is isomorphic to the multiplicative group $K^*$ (acting as scalar multiplication). When restricted to linear transformations, only central scalars (elements of $Z(K^*)$) work, because linearity requires the scalar to commute with all field elements. This distinction between skewfields and fields is crucial for understanding the kernel of the action of $\Gamma L(V)$ on the associated projective space.
