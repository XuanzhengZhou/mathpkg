The dual space $V'$ of a left vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$ is a right vector space of the same dimension. The annihilator mapping $\alpha_V$ establishes a dimension-reversing correspondence between subspaces, which in projective geometry translates to the duality between points and hyperplanes. This duality is the foundation for correlations and polarities.
