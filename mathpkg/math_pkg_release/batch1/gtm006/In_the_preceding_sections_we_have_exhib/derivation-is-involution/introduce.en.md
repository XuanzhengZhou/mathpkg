Theorem 10.4 establishes that derivation is an involutive process: the complement $\mathcal{S}'$ of a derivation set $\mathcal{S}$ is itself a derivation set for the derived plane $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$, and applying derivation again with respect to $\mathcal{S}$ recovers the original affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ up to natural isomorphism. This means the derivation construction is reversible, and planes obtained by derivation come in naturally isomorphic pairs.
