We coordinatize $\mathcal{H}$ by the method introduced in Chapter V. Let $(\infty)$ be $(0,0,1)$; $(0)$ be $(1,0,0)$; $(0,0)$ be $(0,1,0)$ and $(1)$ be $(1,0,-1)$. The line $l_{\infty}$ is, in the earlier representation, the line $y = 0$ and the new $x$-axis and $y$-axis are the earlier lines $z = 0$ and $x = 0$ respectively.

We label the points of the new $y$-axis by letting $(0,1,v)$ be $(0,v)$ in our new system. Every line through $(1)$, or $(1,0,-1)$, is a line of the form $x + yt + z = 0$. The point $(v,0)$ on the $x$-axis will be the point $(u,1,0)$ which is collinear with $(1,0,-1)$ and $(0,1,v)$. But $(1,0,-1)$ and $(0,1,v)$ are on the line $L(-v)$, if $v \notin F$, from which it follows that $u + 1(-v) + 0 = 0$ or $u = v$. If $v \in F$, then it is obvious that $u = v$ and thus $(v,0)$ is the point $(v,1,0)$.

Since the point $(m)$ is on $l_{\infty}$, it will be a point of the form $(1,0,v)$. Furthermore $(m)$ is the point on $l_{\infty}$ which is collinear with $(1,1,0)$ and $(0,1,m)$. Working through the incidence relations yields that $(m) = (1,0,-m)$.

Now consider a general point $(u,v)$. It is the intersection of the line through $(u,0)$ and $(\infty)$ with the line through $(0,v)$ and the point $(1)$ on $l_{\infty}$. The line through $(u,0) = (u,1,0)$ and $(\infty) = (0,0,1)$ has equation involving these points, and the line through $(0,v) = (0,1,v)$ and $(1) = (1,0,-1)$ similarly. Their intersection is computed to be $(u,1,v)$. Thus $(u,v) = (u,1,v)$.
