Theorem 10.2 establishes that the derived incidence structure $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ satisfies the axioms of an affine plane of the same order $n^2$. The proof verifies that there are $n^4$ points, each line contains $n^2$ points, and any two distinct points lie on exactly one line -- appealing to Exercise 3.14 to conclude the affine plane axioms. The key case distinction is whether the line through two affine points meets $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}'$ (ordinary line retained) or in $\mathcal{S}$ (replaced by the unique Baer subplane).
