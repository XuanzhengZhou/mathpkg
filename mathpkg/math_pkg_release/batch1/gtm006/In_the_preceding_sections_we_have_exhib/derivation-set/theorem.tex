Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite affine plane of order $n^2$, and let $\ell_{\infty}$ be its special line (so that $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{P}^{\ell_{\infty}}$, for the appropriate projective plane $\mathcal{P}$). A subset $\mathcal{S}$ of $n+1$ special points on $\ell_{\infty}$ is called a \textbf{derivation set} for $\mathcal{A}$ (or for $\mathcal{P}$) if, for every pair of distinct affine points $X, Y$ of $\mathcal{A}$ for which $XY$ meets $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}$, there is a Baer subplane of $\mathcal{P}$ containing $X, Y$ and $\mathcal{S}$.
