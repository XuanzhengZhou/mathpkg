A derivation set $\mathcal{S}$ is a subset of $n+1$ special points on the line at infinity $\ell_{\infty}$ of a finite affine plane of order $n^2$. The defining property is that any two affine points whose connecting line meets $\ell_{\infty}$ at a point in $\mathcal{S}$ are contained, together with $\mathcal{S}$, in some Baer subplane of the ambient projective plane $\mathcal{P}$. Derivation sets are the key ingredient in the method of "derivation", a construction technique for producing new finite projective planes from existing ones.
