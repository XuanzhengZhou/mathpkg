By Exercise 4.28, if a projective plane is $(V,\ell)$-transitive for some flag $(V,\ell)$, then the plane must be a translation plane or a dual translation plane. The Hughes plane $\mathcal{H}$ has a full collineation group that does not fix any point or line of $\mathcal{H}$. In particular, there is no point $V$ and line $\ell$ such that the full collineation group preserves the flag $(V,\ell)$ in a way that would yield $(V,\ell)$-transitivity. Hence $\mathcal{H}$ is not $(V,\ell)$-transitive for any choice of $V$ or $\ell$, and therefore is neither a translation plane nor a dual translation plane.
