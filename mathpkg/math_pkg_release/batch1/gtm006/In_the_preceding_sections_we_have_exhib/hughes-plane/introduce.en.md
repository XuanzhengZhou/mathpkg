The Hughes planes are a class of finite non-desarguesian projective planes of order $q^2$ for any odd prime power $q$. They were first discovered by Veblen and Wedderburn for the smallest case $q = 3$ (order 9). Unlike other known finite non-desarguesian projective planes, the full collineation group of a Hughes plane does not fix any point or line — a property that makes them unique among known examples. The construction uses a nearfield $N$ of order $q^2$ whose kernel is $\operatorname{GF}(q)$ to build a "left vector space" whose points and lines are defined using a matrix $A \in \operatorname{GL}_3(q)$ of order $q^2+q+1$.
