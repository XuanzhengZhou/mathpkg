Theorem 10.5 states that any collineation (automorphism) $\alpha$ of the original affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ induces an isomorphism between the derived planes $D_{\mathcal{S}'}(\mathcal{A})$ and $D_{\alpha(\mathcal{S}')}(\mathcal{A})$. The proof is straightforward: since $\alpha$ preserves incidence and sends derivation sets to derivation sets, it maps Baer subplanes belonging to $\mathcal{S}$ to Baer subplanes belonging to $\alpha(\mathcal{S})$, and lines meeting $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}'$ to lines meeting $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\alpha(\mathcal{S}')$, establishing the isomorphism.
