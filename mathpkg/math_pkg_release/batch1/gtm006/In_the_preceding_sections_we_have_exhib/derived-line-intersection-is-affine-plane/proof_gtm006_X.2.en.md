There are $n^2$ points of $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}'}(k)$, and its "lines" are the intersections of $k$ with the Baer subplanes of $\mathcal{A}$ belonging to $\mathcal{S}$. But two distinct points of $k$ are in a unique Baer subplane $\mathcal{B}_i$ belonging to $\mathcal{S}$, and since $k \cap \mathcal{B}_i^{\ell_{\infty}}$ contains more than one point, it is a line of $\mathcal{B}_i^{\ell_{\infty}}$. Thus $k \cap \mathcal{B}_i^{\ell_{\infty}}$ consists of exactly $n$ points. But now by Exercise 3.14, $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}'}(k)$ is an affine plane of order $n$.

A "line" of $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}'}(k)$ is the intersection of $k$ with a Baer subplane of $\mathcal{A}$ belonging to $\mathcal{S}$, and so does not meet $\ell_{\infty}$ in $\mathcal{S}'$.
