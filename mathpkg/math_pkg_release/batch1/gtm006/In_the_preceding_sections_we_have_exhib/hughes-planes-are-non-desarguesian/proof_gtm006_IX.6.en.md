By Lemma 9.17, the ternary ring of the Hughes plane $\mathcal{H}$ has addition $a \oplus b = a + b$ coinciding with nearfield addition. Since a projective plane is desarguesian if and only if it can be coordinatized by a skewfield (and hence its planar ternary ring is associative), and the nearfield $N$ of order $q^2$ with kernel $\operatorname{GF}(q)$ is not a skewfield for $q > 2$ (its multiplication is not associative), the coordinating planar ternary ring of $\mathcal{H}$ is not linear. Therefore $\mathcal{H}$ is non-desarguesian.
