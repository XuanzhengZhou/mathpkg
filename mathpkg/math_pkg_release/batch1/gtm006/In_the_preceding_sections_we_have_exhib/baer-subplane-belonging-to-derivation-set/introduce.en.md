A Baer subplane $\mathcal{B}$ of the projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is said to belong to a derivation set $\mathcal{S}$ if $\mathcal{B}$ contains $\mathcal{S}$. The collection of all Baer subplanes belonging to $\mathcal{S}$ plays a crucial role in the derivation construction: after removing the line at infinity, these Baer subplanes become the new "lines" in the derived affine plane, replacing those lines of the original affine plane whose intersection with $\ell_{\infty}$ lies in $\mathcal{S}$.
