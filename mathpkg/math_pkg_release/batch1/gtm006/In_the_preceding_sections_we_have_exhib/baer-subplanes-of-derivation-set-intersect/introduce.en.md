Lemma 10.1 establishes a crucial property of derivation sets: the affine restrictions $\mathcal{A}_i = \mathcal{B}_i^{\ell_{\infty}}$ of distinct Baer subplanes $\mathcal{B}_i$ belonging to the same derivation set $\mathcal{S}$ intersect in at most one affine point. This ensures that when these Baer subplanes are used as the "new lines" in the derived affine plane, two such lines cannot share more than one point, which is essential for the incidence axioms to hold in the derived structure.
