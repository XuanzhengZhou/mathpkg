A division ring $D$ of dimension $2$ over its left nucleus (or middle nucleus) is in particular a right quasifield of dimension $2$ over that nucleus. The nucleus being a subfield of $D$ plays the role of the kernel $F$. Since the left nucleus (respectively middle nucleus) ensures the required distributivity properties, the hypotheses of Theorem 10.14 are satisfied. Therefore $\mathcal{A}$ is derivable. Moreover, since $D$ is a division ring, the original plane is a translation plane, and the derivation process preserves the translation plane structure; thus the derived plane is also a translation plane. $\square$
