Corollary 2 to Theorem 10.14 specializes to division rings: if a division ring $D$ has dimension $2$ over its left nucleus or its middle nucleus, then the affine plane coordinatized by $D$ is derivable. Unlike the situation in Corollary 1, the derived plane obtained from a division ring plane is guaranteed to be a translation plane, reflecting the richer algebraic structure of division rings compared to general right quasifields.
