This corollary to Theorem 10.16 states that finite Hall planes of the same order are always isomorphic. For infinite Hall planes, the isomorphism condition depends on the equivalence of the defining irreducible quadratics. Since the multiplicative formula in Theorem 10.16 shows that the derived quasifield multiplication $a * (c + \lambda d) = ac + \lambda ad$ depends on a quadratic parameter $\lambda$, and all irreducible quadratics over a finite field $GF(q)$ are realized by elements of $GF(q^2)$, all Hall planes of a given finite order share the same algebraic structure up to isomorphism.
