Exercise 10.5 provides explicit algebraic criteria for equality and parallelism of the Baer subplanes $\mathcal{B}(a, b, c)$ introduced in Theorem 10.14. Two such subplanes are equal precisely when their parameters differ by multiplication with elements of $F^*$ in the first component and by translation in the second and third components. They are parallel exactly when the first components generate the same $F^*$-coset. These conditions are used in subsequent results, particularly in coordinatizing the derived plane in Theorem 10.15.
