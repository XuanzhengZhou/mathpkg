Let $K = GF(q^2)$, $F = GF(q) \subset K$, and let $t_1, t_2, \dots, t_r$ be distinct elements of $F^*$. If $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{A}_2(K)$ is the desarguesian affine plane over $K$, then the multiply derived plane $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}_{t_1}} \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}_{t_2}} \cdots \mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{S}_{t_r}} (\mathcal{A})$ is an André plane.
