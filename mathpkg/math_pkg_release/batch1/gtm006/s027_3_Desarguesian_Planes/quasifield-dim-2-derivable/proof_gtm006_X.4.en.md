This follows immediately from Theorem 10.14. For a right quasifield $Q$ of dimension $2$ over its kernel $F$, one can choose $a \in Q \setminus F$ and parameters $b, c$ to construct the Baer subplanes $\mathcal{B}(a, b, c)$ as described in the theorem. The dimension-$2$ condition ensures that the required algebraic properties (d) and (e) for the proof of Theorem 10.14 are satisfied. Hence by Theorem 10.14, the affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ coordinatized by $Q$ is derivable. The derived planes obtained from this construction are not translation planes in general, nor are they dual translation planes, giving further examples of planes with no translation lines or points. $\square$
