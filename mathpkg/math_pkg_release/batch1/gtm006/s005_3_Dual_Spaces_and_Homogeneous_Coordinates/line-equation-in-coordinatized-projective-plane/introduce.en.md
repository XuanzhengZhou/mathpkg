Lemma 2.7 provides the fundamental geometric interpretation of the algebraic operations in the coordinate skewfield. The equation $y = xm + b$ describes exactly the points on a line not through $E_1$, where $m$ represents the "slope" and $b$ the "intercept." This coordinatization is the starting point for the algebraic study of projective planes via planar ternary rings, which generalize this construction to arbitrary projective planes.
