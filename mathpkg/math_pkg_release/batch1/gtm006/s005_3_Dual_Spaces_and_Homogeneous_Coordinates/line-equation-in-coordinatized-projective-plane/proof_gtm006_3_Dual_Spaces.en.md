The point $\langle x e_1 + y e_2 + e_3 \rangle$ is on the line joining $\langle m e_2 + e_1 \rangle$ to $\langle b e_2 + e_3 \rangle$ iff $x e_1 + y e_2 + e_3$ is a linear combination of $m e_2 + e_1$ and $b e_2 + e_3$. Writing $x e_1 + y e_2 + e_3 = c(m e_2 + e_1) + d(b e_2 + e_3)$ and equating coefficients of $e_3$ gives $d = 1$, of $e_1$ gives $c = x$, and of $e_2$ gives $y = xm + b$. Conversely, if $y = xm + b$, then the vector is $x(m e_2 + e_1) + (b e_2 + e_3)$.
