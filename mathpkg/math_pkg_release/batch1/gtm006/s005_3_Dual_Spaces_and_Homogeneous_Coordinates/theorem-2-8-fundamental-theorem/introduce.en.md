The Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry states that every isomorphism between projective planes arises from a semilinear transformation of the underlying vector spaces. The proof uses frames to construct the associated field isomorphism and semilinear transformation, establishing that projective isomorphisms correspond to algebraic (semilinear) isomorphisms.
