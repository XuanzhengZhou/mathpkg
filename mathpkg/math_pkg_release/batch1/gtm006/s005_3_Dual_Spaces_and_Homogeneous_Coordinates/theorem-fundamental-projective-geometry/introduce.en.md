The Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry asserts that every isomorphism between projective planes arises from an underlying semi-linear transformation of the vector spaces. This is a profound structural result: the purely incidence-geometric notion of "isomorphism of projective planes" coincides exactly with the algebraic notion of "semi-linear isomorphism of the coordinatizing vector spaces." In particular, the theorem implies that the skewfields coordinatizing isomorphic projective planes must be isomorphic, and the automorphism group of a projective plane is precisely the projective semi-linear group $P\Gamma L(V)$. The proof proceeds by selecting corresponding frames, constructing from them a bijection between the underlying skewfields, and showing this bijection is a field isomorphism compatible with the geometric incidence structure.
