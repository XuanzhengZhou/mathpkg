The proof consists of verifying that the vectors $e_1, \dots, e_{n+1}$ and $e_1 + \cdots + e_{n+1}$ satisfy the condition that no $n+1$ of them lie in an $n$-dimensional subspace. Suppose some set of $n+1$ of these points were contained in a hyperplane $U$. If $E_{n+2}$ is not among them, then all $e_i$ lie in $U$, which is impossible since they form a basis. If $E_{n+2}$ is among them, then $n$ of the $e_i$ together with their sum generate $U$, but this would also contain the remaining $e_i$, again a contradiction.
