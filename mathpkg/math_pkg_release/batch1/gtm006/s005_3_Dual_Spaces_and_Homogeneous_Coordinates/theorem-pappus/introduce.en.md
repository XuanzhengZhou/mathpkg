Pappus' Theorem is one of the most celebrated configurational results in projective geometry. Given six points arranged three on each of two lines, the three intersection points of cross-joins are collinear precisely when the underlying skewfield is commutative. This makes Pappus' Theorem a geometric criterion for a projective plane to be coordinatized by a field rather than a proper skewfield; such planes are called Pappian. The theorem was also used historically by Hilbert in his Grundlagen der Geometrie to establish the commutativity of multiplication in the coordinate field from purely geometric incidence axioms.
