The Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry (FTPG) is the cornerstone of the algebraic approach to projective geometry for dimension $\geq 2$. It states that every collineation (automorphism) of a Desarguesian projective plane is induced by a semi-linear transformation of the underlying vector space. This establishes the deep connection between geometric symmetries and algebraic (semi-linear) transformations, and justifies the identification $\operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal{P}(V)) = P\Gamma L(V)$.
