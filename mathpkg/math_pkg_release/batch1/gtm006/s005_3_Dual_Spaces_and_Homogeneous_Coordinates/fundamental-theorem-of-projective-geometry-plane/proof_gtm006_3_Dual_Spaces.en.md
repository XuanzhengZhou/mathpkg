Let $\beta: \mathcal{P}(V) \to \mathcal{P}(W)$ be the isomorphism. Choose a frame $E_1, E_2, E_3, E_4$ for $\mathcal{P}(W)$ from a basis $e_1, e_2, e_3$, and let $G_i = \beta^{-1}(E_i)$ be the pre-image frame in $\mathcal{P}(V)$. Choose a basis $g_1, g_2, g_3$ for $V$ with $G_i = \langle g_i \rangle$ and $G_4 = \langle g_1+g_2+g_3 \rangle$. Define the mapping $\phi: K \to F$ by the condition that $\langle x g_1 + g_3 \rangle$ maps to $\langle x^\phi e_1 + e_3 \rangle$ under $\beta$. Using the incidence structure, one proves $\phi$ is an isomorphism of $K$ onto $F$: the multiplicative property follows from the construction of $xm$ via the intersection of lines, and the additive property is proved similarly.
