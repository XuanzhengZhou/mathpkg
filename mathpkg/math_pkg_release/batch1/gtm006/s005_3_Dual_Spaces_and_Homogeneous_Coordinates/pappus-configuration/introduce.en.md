Pappus' Configuration is the geometric arrangement of nine points and nine lines described by Pappus' Theorem. It is a symmetric $(9_3, 9_3)$ configuration: three points lie on each of two initial lines, and the three cross-joins intersect pairwise to produce three new points which are collinear. This configuration is fundamental in projective geometry because Pappus' Theorem holds in a projective plane if and only if the coordinatizing skewfield is commutative (i.e., is a field), making it a key axiom distinguishing Pappian planes from general projective planes.
