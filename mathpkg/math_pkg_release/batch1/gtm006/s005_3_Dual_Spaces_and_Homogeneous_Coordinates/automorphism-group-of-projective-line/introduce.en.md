Lemma 2.13 gives an explicit parametric description of the automorphism group of the projective line $P^1(K)$ over a field $K$. The group is isomorphic to $P\Gamma L(2, K)$ and consists of linear fractional transformations $x \mapsto (x^\alpha a + b)/(x^\alpha c + d)$ composed with field automorphisms. When $K$ is a finite field $GF(q)$, this group has order $(q+1)q(q-1) \cdot |\operatorname{Aut}(K)|$. This representation is fundamental for studying the action of $PGL(2,K)$ and $PSL(2,K)$ on the projective line, which are classical groups appearing throughout geometry and number theory.
