If $K$ is a field and $\mathcal{P} = \mathcal{P}_1(K)$ is the projective line over $K$, then $\operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal{P})$ consists of all mappings of the form:
$$x \mapsto \frac{x^\alpha a + b}{x^\alpha c + d}, \quad \text{with } ad - bc \neq 0, \; \alpha \in \operatorname{Aut}(K),$$
where the point with parametric coordinate $\infty$ maps to $a/c$ if $c \neq 0$, and to $\infty$ if $c = 0$ (with the conventions $\infty^\alpha = \infty$, $c \cdot \infty = \infty$, $(a \cdot \infty)(c \cdot \infty)^{-1} = ac^{-1}$, etc.).
