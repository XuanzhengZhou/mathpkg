Once a basis of $V$ is chosen, any point of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ may be represented by a row vector (a 1-dimensional subspace $\langle v \rangle$) and any hyperplane by a column vector (a 1-dimensional subspace of $V'$). In the projective plane, points are represented as $\langle x, y, z \rangle$ and lines as $\langle a, b, c \rangle'$, with incidence given by $ax + by + cz = 0$.
