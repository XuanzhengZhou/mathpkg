Homogeneous coordinates provide a concrete algebraic handle on projective geometry. A point is represented not by a single coordinate tuple but by an equivalence class of non-zero scalar multiples. The incidence condition $ax+by+cz=0$ elegantly encodes the relation between points and lines. This coordinatization is the foundation for the algebraic study of projective planes and the bridge between synthetic and analytic projective geometry.
