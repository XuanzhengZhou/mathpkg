Lemma 2.4 shows that frames always exist: given any basis of the underlying vector space $V$, the $n+1$ one-dimensional subspaces generated by the basis vectors, together with the subspace generated by their sum, form a frame of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. The key is verifying that no $n+1$ of these points lie in a hyperplane. This construction provides the standard frame used for introducing homogeneous coordinates.
