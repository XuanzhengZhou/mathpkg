Apply Theorem 2.8 with $W = V$ and $F = K$. An automorphism of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is an isomorphism from $\mathcal{P}(V)$ onto itself. By Theorem 2.8, this automorphism is induced by a semi-linear transformation from $V$ onto $V$. The semi-linear transformation is non-singular since it induces a bijection on the set of one-dimensional subspaces. Conversely, any non-singular semi-linear transformation of $V$ preserves the incidence relation among subspaces and thus induces an automorphism of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. Hence the group of all automorphisms of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is exactly $P\Gamma L(V)$. $\square$
