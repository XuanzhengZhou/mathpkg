The dual space $V'$ of a vector space $V$ over a skewfield $K$ provides a natural bridge between the projective geometries $\mathcal{P}(V)$ and $\mathcal{P}(V')$. The annihilator mapping $a_V$ sends each subspace $W$ of $V$ to the set of linear functionals in $V'$ that vanish on $W$, thereby establishing a dimension-reversing correspondence between the subspace lattices. When $K$ is a field, $V$ and $V'$ are isomorphic, but for general skewfields this may fail, making the dual construction essential for understanding the relationship between points and hyperplanes in projective geometry.
