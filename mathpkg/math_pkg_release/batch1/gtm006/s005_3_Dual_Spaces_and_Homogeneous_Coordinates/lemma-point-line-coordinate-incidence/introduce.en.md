Lemma 2.7 translates the incidence relation of projective geometry into a familiar affine linear equation $y = xm + b$ when working in homogeneous coordinates with respect to a fixed frame. In the affine patch where the third coordinate $e_3$ is non-zero, the line through the "point at infinity" $\langle m e_2 + e_1 \rangle$ (representing slope $m$) and the "$y$-intercept" $\langle b e_2 + e_3 \rangle$ consists precisely of points satisfying this equation. This gives a geometric interpretation of the skewfield operations: addition corresponds to shifting intercepts, while multiplication arises from varying the slope.
