This theorem establishes the converse direction of the equivalence between finite projective planes and complete sets of MOLS. Starting from a complete set of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal Latin squares of order $n$ in normal form, one can reconstruct a planar ternary ring $(R,T)$ by reading off the ternary operation from the square entries. The verification that $(R,T)$ satisfies the PTR axioms (A), (B), (D), (E) of Theorem 5.1 relies on the Latin square property and orthogonality. By Theorem 5.4, this PTR coordinatizes a projective plane of order $n$. Thus the existence of a complete set of MOLS of order $n$ is equivalent to the existence of a finite projective plane of order $n$.
