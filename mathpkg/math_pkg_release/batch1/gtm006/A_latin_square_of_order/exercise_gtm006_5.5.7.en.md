**Exercise 5.7.** Show that there is no Latin square orthogonal to

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\
1 & 2 & 3 & 0 \\
2 & 3 & 0 & 1 \\
3 & 0 & 1 & 2
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

*Hint:* Try to construct one starting with

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\
2 & & &
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Since the matrix in Exercise 5.7 is the addition table for $(\mathbb{Z}_4, +)$, this shows that if $(R,T)$ is a PTR of order 4 then $(R,+)$ must be elementary abelian.
