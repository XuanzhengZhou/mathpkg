Two Latin squares are equivalent if one can be transformed into the other by permuting rows and columns. Permuting the symbol names within a Latin square also preserves the Latin property, as does any combination of row, column, and symbol permutations. The notion of equivalence is useful for normalizing families of mutually orthogonal Latin squares: any set of MOLS can be transformed into an equivalent set in normal form, where each square has $(0,1,\dots,n-1)$ as its top row.
