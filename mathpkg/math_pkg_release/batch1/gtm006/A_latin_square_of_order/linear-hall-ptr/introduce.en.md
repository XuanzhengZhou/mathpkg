In the Hall coordinatization method for projective planes, a planar ternary ring is called linear if its ternary operation can be expressed as $T(x,m,k) = xm + k$, where addition and multiplication are binary operations on the coordinate set $R$. This notion parallels the standard concept of a linear PTR (where $T(a,b,c) = ab + c$). The Hall method differs from the standard coordinatization in the order of arguments and the geometric interpretation of coordinates, but linearity in the Hall sense is equivalent to linearity in the standard sense for the same choice of fundamental quadrangle. The Pickert variant further modifies the ternary operation to $S(m,x,k) = y$ read off the line $[m,k]$.
