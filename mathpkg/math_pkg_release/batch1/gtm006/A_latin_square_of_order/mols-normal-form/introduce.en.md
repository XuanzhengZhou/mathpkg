Property 1 states that any family of mutually orthogonal Latin squares can be transformed, via permutations of rows, columns, and symbol names, into an equivalent family in which every square has the standard top row $(0,1,\dots,n-1)$. Such a normalized family is said to be in normal form. Normalization is a fundamental normalization technique used in the study of MOLS: it simplifies counting arguments (e.g., the proof that at most $n-1$ MOLS of order $n$ can exist) and facilitates the construction of planar ternary rings from complete sets of MOLS.
