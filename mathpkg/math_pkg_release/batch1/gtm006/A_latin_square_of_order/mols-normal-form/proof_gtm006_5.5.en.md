**Proof of Property 1.** First, note that a permutation of the symbols (the "names" of the letters) within a Latin square leaves it a Latin square; such an operation also leaves it orthogonal to any Latin square it was already orthogonal to. Hence, by applying suitable symbol permutations, we can arrange that the top row of every square in the set is $(0, 1, \dots, n-1)$. Then, by permuting rows and columns (which preserves equivalence), we can standardize the remaining structure. Thus any set of mutually orthogonal Latin squares can be put in normal form.
