This theorem establishes one direction of the fundamental equivalence between finite projective planes and complete sets of mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS). Given a finite projective plane of order $n$, one can coordinatize it by choosing a quadrangle and constructing the associated planar ternary ring $(R,T)$ of order $n$. Lemma 5.7 then guarantees that the $n-1$ matrices $\{x\}$ (for $x \neq 0$) defined by $T(x,i,j)$ form a family of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal Latin squares—that is, a complete set of MOLS. Together with its converse, this theorem shows that the existence of a projective plane of order $n$ is equivalent to the existence of a complete set of MOLS of order $n$.
