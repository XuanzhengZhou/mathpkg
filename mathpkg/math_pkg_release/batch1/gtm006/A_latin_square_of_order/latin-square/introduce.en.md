A Latin square of order $n$ is an $n \times n$ array filled with $n$ distinct symbols such that each symbol appears exactly once in each row and exactly once in each column. The concept originates from Leonhard Euler's work on combinatorial designs and is intimately connected to the multiplication tables (Cayley tables) of quasigroups. Latin squares serve as a fundamental building block in the theory of mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS), which in turn provide an alternative characterization of finite projective planes via planar ternary rings.
