Lemma 5.7 establishes the fundamental connection between planar ternary rings (PTRs) and mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS). Given a PTR $(R,T)$ of order $n$, the ternary operation $T(x,i,j)$ defines a family of $n-1$ Latin squares (one for each nonzero $x \in R$), and any two distinct squares from this family are orthogonal. This construction is the key link in the equivalence between finite projective planes and complete sets of MOLS: a projective plane coordinatized by a PTR yields a complete set of MOLS, and conversely, a complete set of MOLS can be used to construct a PTR.
