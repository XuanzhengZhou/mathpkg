Property 2 gives the fundamental upper bound for families of mutually orthogonal Latin squares: at most $n-1$ MOLS of order $n$ can exist. The proof uses normalization (Property 1) and a simple pigeonhole argument on the $(1,0)$-position (second row, first column). In normal form, this entry cannot be $0$ (otherwise the left column would contain two zeros), and no two squares can share the same entry in this position (otherwise the ordered pair would repeat). This leaves exactly $n-1$ possibilities, establishing the bound. A set achieving this bound is called a complete set of MOLS.
