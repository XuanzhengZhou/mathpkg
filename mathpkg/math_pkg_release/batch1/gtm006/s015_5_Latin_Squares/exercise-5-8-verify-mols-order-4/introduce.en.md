Exercise 5.8 presents three explicit $4 \times 4$ matrices and asks the reader to verify that they form a complete set of mutually orthogonal Latin squares. The verification requires checking that each matrix is a Latin square (each row and column contains $\{0,1,a,b\}$ exactly once) and that each pair of matrices is orthogonal (all $16$ ordered pairs appear). This exercise provides a concrete example of the theoretical construction, and together with Exercises 5.6-5.7, demonstrates that the only projective planes of order $3$ or $4$ are the Desarguesian ones $\mathcal{P}_2(3)$ and $\mathcal{P}_2(4)$.
