Two Latin squares are equivalent if one can be obtained from the other by permuting rows and columns. Additionally, relabeling the symbols (permuting the names of the letters) preserves the Latin property and does not affect orthogonality relations. Equivalence allows sets of MOLS to be standardized into normal form, which is essential for establishing the correspondence between PTRs and complete sets of MOLS.
