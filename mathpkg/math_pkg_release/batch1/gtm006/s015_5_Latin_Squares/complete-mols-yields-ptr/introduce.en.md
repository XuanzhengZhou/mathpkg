This theorem provides the converse construction: from a complete set of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal Latin squares in normal form, one can define a ternary operation $T$ that satisfies the planar ternary ring axioms. The construction labels the squares by their $(1,0)$-entry, defines $\{0\}$ with $T(0,i,j)=j$, and verifies axioms (A), (B), (D), and (E) of Theorem 5.1. Together with the forward direction, this establishes that finite projective planes of order $n$ exist if and only if complete sets of MOLS of order $n$ exist.
