A Latin square of order $n$ is an $n \times n$ square array whose entries are drawn from an $n$-element set (typically $\{0,1,\dots,n-1\}$), with the property that each row and each column is a permutation of the symbol set. The Cayley table of any quasigroup furnishes an example. Latin squares are fundamental objects in combinatorial design theory and serve as a bridge to finite projective planes via their connection to planar ternary rings.
