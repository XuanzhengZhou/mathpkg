Lemma 5.7 establishes the fundamental link between planar ternary rings (PTRs) and mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS). Given a PTR $(R,T)$, each nonzero element $x \in R$ defines a Latin square $\{x\}$ via the ternary operation, and distinct elements of $R$ yield orthogonal squares. Together with the companion square $\{0\}$ (defined by $T(0,i,j)=j$), this produces a complete set of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal Latin squares of order $n$. This construction is the core mechanism connecting finite projective planes to combinatorial design theory.
