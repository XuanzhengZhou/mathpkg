**Proof of Property 1.**

First, note that a permutation of the symbols within a Latin square leaves it Latin; such an operation also preserves orthogonality with any Latin square it was already orthogonal to. Hence, by applying appropriate symbol relabelings, any set of mutually orthogonal Latin squares can be transformed so that each square has $(0, 1, \dots, n-1)$ as its top row. Further row and column permutations may be applied without destroying the Latin or orthogonality properties. The resulting set is said to be in normal form.
