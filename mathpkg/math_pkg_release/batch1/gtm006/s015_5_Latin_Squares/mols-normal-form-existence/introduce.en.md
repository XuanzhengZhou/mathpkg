Property 1 states that any collection of mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS) can be transformed, via permissible equivalence operations (row/column permutations and symbol relabeling), into a standardized form in which every square has the sequence $(0,1,\dots,n-1)$ as its first row. This normalized presentation is called normal form and is an essential prerequisite for constructing a planar ternary ring from a complete set of MOLS.
