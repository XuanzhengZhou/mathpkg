Two Latin squares of order $n$ are orthogonal if, when superimposed, every ordered pair of symbols occurs exactly once. Equivalently, the $n^2$ pairs formed by reading corresponding entries of the two squares contain each of the $n^2$ possible pairs exactly once. A set of Latin squares that are pairwise orthogonal is called a set of mutually orthogonal Latin squares (MOLS). The existence of complete sets of MOLS is intimately tied to the existence of finite projective planes.
