Property 3 strengthens the normal form for mutually orthogonal Latin squares. After putting a set of MOLS in normal form (each square has $(0,1,\dots,n-1)$ as its top row), one may additionally arrange that the first square has $(0,1,\dots,n-1)^\top$ as its first column. This further normalization is used in the proof that a complete set of MOLS yields a planar ternary ring, ensuring that the constructed PTR satisfies the required algebraic axioms.
