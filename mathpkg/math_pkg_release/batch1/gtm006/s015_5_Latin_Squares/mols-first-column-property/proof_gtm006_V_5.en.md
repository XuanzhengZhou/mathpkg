**Proof of Property 3.**

Given a set of mutually orthogonal $n \times n$ Latin squares in normal form, each square has $(0, 1, \dots, n-1)$ as its top row. We may apply an additional permutation of the symbols (relabeling) to arrange that the first square has $(0, 1, \dots, n-1)^\top$ as its left column. Since symbol relabeling preserves both the Latin square property and orthogonality, and since the top row can be restored to $(0, 1, \dots, n-1)$ by a further consistent relabeling if necessary, we may assume without loss of generality that the first square satisfies this additional normalization.
