This corollary encapsulates the central result of Section 5: the existence of a finite projective plane of order $n$ is equivalent to the existence of a complete set of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal Latin squares of order $n$. This equivalence provides a powerful combinatorial criterion for studying the existence problem for finite projective planes and links projective geometry with combinatorial design theory. For instance, it is used in the exercises to show that the only projective planes of orders 3 and 4 are the Desarguesian ones.
