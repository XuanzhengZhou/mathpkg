**Proof.**

The forward direction follows from the theorem that any finite projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ of order $n$ may be coordinatized by a PTR $(R, T)$, and Lemma 5.7 then yields a complete set of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal $n \times n$ Latin squares.

The converse direction follows from the theorem that given a complete set of $n-1$ mutually orthogonal $n \times n$ Latin squares in normal form, one can construct a planar ternary ring $(R, T)$ satisfying axioms (A), (B), (D), (E). By Theorem 5.4, this PTR coordinatizes a projective plane of order $n$.

Thus a finite projective plane of order $n$ exists if and only if a complete set of MOLS of order $n$ exists.
