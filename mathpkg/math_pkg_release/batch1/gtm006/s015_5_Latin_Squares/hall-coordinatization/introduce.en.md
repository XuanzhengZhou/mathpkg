The Hall coordinatization method, developed by Marshall Hall, is an alternative approach to assigning coordinates to points and lines of a projective plane, using a fixed quadrangle $O,X,Y,I$ as a frame of reference. The resulting algebraic structure is a Hall planar ternary ring, where linearity is defined by $T(x,m,k) = xm + k$. A given projective plane is coordinatizable by a linear Hall PTR if and only if it is coordinatizable by a linear PTR under the method introduced in Chapter V, so the two approaches are equivalent for the study of Desarguesian planes.
