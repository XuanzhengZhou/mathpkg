A planar ternary ring $(R,T)$ is called linear when its ternary operation can be expressed as $T(x,m,k) = xm + k$, where addition is defined by $a + b = T(1, a, b)$ and multiplication by $ab = T(a, b, 0)$. Linear PTRs correspond to coordinatizations where the plane is Desarguesian, and they form the simplest and most well-behaved class of planar ternary rings. The distinction between linear and non-linear PTRs is central to the classification of projective planes.
