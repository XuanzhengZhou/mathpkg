\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:ptr-linearity-condition}
Let $(R,T)$ be a planar ternary ring coordinatizing a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$.
Then $(R,T)$ is linear if and only if for any two triangles
$A_1B_1C_1$ and $A_2B_2C_2$ such that $A_1A_2$ is the line $x=0$, $B_1B_2$ is $x=u$,
$C_1C_2$ is $x=v$, and such that the two pairs of corresponding sides
$A_1B_1$, $A_2B_2$ and $C_1A_1$, $C_2A_2$ meet on $[\infty]$, the remaining pair
of corresponding sides $B_1C_1$ and $B_2C_2$ have the property that one passes
through $(0)$ if and only if the other does.
\end{theorem}
