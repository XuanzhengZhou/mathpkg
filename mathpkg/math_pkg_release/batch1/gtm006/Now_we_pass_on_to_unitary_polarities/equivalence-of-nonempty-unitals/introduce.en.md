This corollary establishes that all non-empty unitals in a given projective plane are collineation-equivalent. By choosing an appropriate coordinate frame, any non-empty unital can be put into a canonical form with matrix $A$ having entries determined by a single element $c$ of the field and an element $b$ of the fixed field satisfying $b + c + c^{\phi} = 0$. This parallels the analogous result for non-empty conics, where all non-empty conics are equivalent and can be represented by the projective form $y^2 = xz$. A consequence is that the unitary group, i.e. the stabilizer of a unital inside the collineation group, is uniquely determined up to conjugacy.
