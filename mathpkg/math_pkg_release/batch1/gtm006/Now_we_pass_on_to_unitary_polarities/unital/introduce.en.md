A unital is the incidence structure formed by the absolute points and non-absolute lines of a unitary polarity in a projective plane. Unlike conics, which arise from orthogonal polarities, unitals are associated with polarities defined by a sesquilinear form involving an involutory automorphism of the underlying field. The unital serves as the central geometric object for studying unitary polarities, analogous to how conics are the central objects for orthogonal polarities. Many properties of unitals, such as the number of points on a line intersecting the unital, are determined by the norm-1 subgroup of the associated field.
