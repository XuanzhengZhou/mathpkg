Lemma 2.46 is a fundamental structural result about unitals: no projective line can be entirely contained within a unital (provided the underlying field is not $GF(4)$). This contrasts with the behavior of conics, where a line can be fully contained in certain degenerate cases. The proof proceeds by contradiction using the incidence equation (JU) for the line-unitial intersection and applies Lemma 2.44: if all points on a line satisfied the unitial equation, the coefficients of the hermitian form would all vanish, forcing the polar image of one point to lie on the line, which ultimately contradicts the definition of the polarity.
