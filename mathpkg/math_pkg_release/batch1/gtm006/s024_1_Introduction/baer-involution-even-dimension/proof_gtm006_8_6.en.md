**Proof.** Let $\beta = \beta(P, Q, R)$ be a Baer involution in $\Delta_1$. The points $(x, 0)$ fixed by $\beta$ are, on the one hand, equal in number to the square-root of the order of $D$ (by definition of a Baer involution), and on the other hand are exactly those points for which $x$ lies in the subspace $V$ of $D$ belonging to the eigenvalue $1$ of the linear transformation $Q$ (over $N_m$ or $N_r$). Thus $\dim D = 2 \dim V$. A similar argument for the fixed points $(0, y)$ proves the claim for $N_l$. $\square$
