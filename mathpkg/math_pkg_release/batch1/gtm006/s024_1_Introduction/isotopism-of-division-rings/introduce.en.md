Isotopism is a generalization of isomorphism for division rings: it replaces the single bijection of an isomorphism with a triple of additive bijections $(P,Q,R)$ that relate the multiplications of $D_1$ and $D_2$. Two division rings coordinatize isomorphic projective planes if and only if they are isotopic, making isotopism the fundamental equivalence for coordinatizing division rings.
