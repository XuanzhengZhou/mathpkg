Two division rings $D_1, D_2$ are called {\em isotopic} if there exists a triple $(P, Q, R)$ of non-singular additive mappings from $D_1$ onto $D_2$ such that

$$(xP)(yQ) = (x \cdot y)R$$

for all $x, y$ in $D_1$, where $\cdot$ denotes the multiplication in $D_1$ and juxtaposition denotes the multiplication in $D_2$. The triple $(P, Q, R)$ is called an {\em isotopism}.
