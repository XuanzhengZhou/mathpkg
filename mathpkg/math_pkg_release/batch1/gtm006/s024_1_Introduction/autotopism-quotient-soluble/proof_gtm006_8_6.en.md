**Proof.** For any fixed $i$ ($i = r, l, m$), the mapping which sends each element of $\Delta$ onto its companion automorphism of $N_i$ is easily seen to be a homomorphism of $\Delta$ into the automorphism group of $N_i$. Since the automorphism group of any finite field is cyclic, this homomorphism has a cyclic image. The group $\Delta_1$ is the intersection of the kernels of the three homomorphisms obtained in this way, so that $\Delta / \Delta_1$ embeds into a product of three cyclic groups and is therefore abelian (hence soluble). $\square$
