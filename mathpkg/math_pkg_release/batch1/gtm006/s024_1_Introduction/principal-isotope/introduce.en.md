A principal isotope is the simplest type of isotope, obtained by pre- and post-multiplying by fixed elements of $D_1$. Every isotope is isomorphic to a principal isotope, so the study of isotopism reduces to the study of principal isotopes. This is analogous to the fact that isotopic loops are isomorphic to principal isotopes of each other.
