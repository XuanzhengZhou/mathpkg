If $D_1, D_2$ are isotopic division rings with respective multiplications $\cdot$ and $\odot$, then $D_2$ is called a {\em principal isotope} of $D_1$ if there exist $a, b$ in $D_1$ such that

$$x \odot y = (x R_a^{-1}) \cdot (y L_b^{-1})$$

where $R_a$ and $L_b$ denote right and left multiplication by $a$ and $b$ in $D_1$.
