A single projective plane may be coordinatized by multiple non-isomorphic division rings. This theorem establishes that such division rings correspond exactly to orbits of the autotopism group acting on points outside the autotopism triangle. The only known planes with exactly one such orbit are the desarguesian planes, and it has been conjectured that these are the only ones.
