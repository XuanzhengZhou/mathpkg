**Proof.** Suppose $\mathcal{P}$ is a division ring plane that can be coordinatized by two division rings $D_1$ and $D_2$, using the same choice of points for $(\infty), (0)$, and $(0,0)$ (this is always possible by suitable choice of coordinate system). Let $S$ be the point chosen as $(1,1)$ that yields $D_1$, and let $T$ be the point yielding $D_2$. By standard coordinatization theory (Exercise 5.2), $D_1$ is isomorphic to $D_2$ if and only if there exists a collineation $\theta$ of $\mathcal{P}$ such that

$$(\infty)^\theta = (\infty),\quad (0)^\theta = (0),\quad (0,0)^\theta = (0,0),\quad S^\theta = T.$$

But such a $\theta$ is precisely an autotopism of $\mathcal{P}$. Thus $D_1$ is isomorphic to $D_2$ if and only if $S$ and $T$ lie in the same orbit of the autotopism group. $\square$
