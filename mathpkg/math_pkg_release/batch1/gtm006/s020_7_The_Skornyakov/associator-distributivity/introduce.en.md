In any nonassociative ring, the associator $[a, bc]$ with a product in the second argument distributes as a commutator-like derivation: $[a, bc] = [a, b]c + b[a, c]$. This identity relates the associator to the commutator and is fundamental for manipulating expressions in the proof that RIP implies alternativity.
