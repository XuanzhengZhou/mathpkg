In any ring satisfying the right alternative law $(xy)y = xy^2$, the associator $[a,b,c]$ is antisymmetric in its last two arguments. This follows from expanding $[a, b+c, b+c] = 0$ using additivity and the fact that $[x, y, y] = 0$ for all $x, y$ under the right alternative law. This identity is a fundamental building block in the proof of the Skornyakov-San Soucie Theorem.
