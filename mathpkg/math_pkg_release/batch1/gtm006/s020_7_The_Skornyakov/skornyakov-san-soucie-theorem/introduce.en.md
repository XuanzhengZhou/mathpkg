The Skornyakov-San Soucie Theorem states that any division ring possessing the right inverse property (RIP) must be alternative. This is a fundamental structural result in the theory of nonassociative division rings, linking a seemingly weak one-sided inverse condition to the full two-sided alternative law. The theorem plays a central role in the algebraic foundations of projective geometry, specifically in establishing that certain configurations of translation planes force the coordinate ring to be associative.
