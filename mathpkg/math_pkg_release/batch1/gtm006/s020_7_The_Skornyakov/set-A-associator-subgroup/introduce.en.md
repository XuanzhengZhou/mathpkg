The set $A(a,b)$ consists of all elements $x$ whose associator $[x,a,b]$ equals $x$ times the commutator $[b,a]$. This additive subgroup plays a crucial role in the Skornyakov-San Soucie proof: it captures elements for which the associator and commutator interact in a controlled way. The equivalence $A(a,b) = A(b,a)$ and the characterization $(xa)b = x(ba)$ are used repeatedly to transfer between different associator expressions.
