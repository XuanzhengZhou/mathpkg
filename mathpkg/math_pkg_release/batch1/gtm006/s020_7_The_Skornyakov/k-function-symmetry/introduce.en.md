The function $k(w,x,y,z)$ defined from the associator is alternating in all four arguments: permuting the arguments multiplies the value by the sign of the permutation. This generalizes the alternating property of the associator (Lemma 18) to the four-variable setting. The fact that $k$ vanishes when two arguments coincide is the key observation used to prove that every alternative division ring has the inverse property (Theorem 6.17).
