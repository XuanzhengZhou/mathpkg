This identity shows how the associator interacts with multiplication by the commutator $[a,b]$. When the commutator $q = [a,b]$ multiplies $x$ from the right, the resulting associator $[xq, a, b]$ factors as $[x, a, b]q$. This "derivation-like" property is proved using the function $h(x, q, a, b) = 0$, the right alternative law (which gives $[x, q, q] = 0$), and Lemma (9) ($[q, a, b] = 0$).
