When the associator $[a,a,b]$ is nonzero, the auxiliary subgroups $A(a,b)$ and $A(a,ba)$ intersect only at zero. This lemma is critical for the Skornyakov-San Soucie proof: it forces any element lying in both subgroups to be zero, which is used in Step (A) to conclude $[q, a, ba] = 0$, and later to deduce that certain associator expressions vanish.
