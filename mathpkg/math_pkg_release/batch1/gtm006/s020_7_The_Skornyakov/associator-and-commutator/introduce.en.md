The associator $[a,b,c] = (ab)c - a(bc)$ measures the failure of associativity in a nonassociative ring, vanishing precisely when the three elements associate. The commutator $[a,b] = ab - ba$ measures the failure of commutativity. Both operations are additive in all arguments and serve as the fundamental tools for analyzing the structure of alternative and other nonassociative division rings.
