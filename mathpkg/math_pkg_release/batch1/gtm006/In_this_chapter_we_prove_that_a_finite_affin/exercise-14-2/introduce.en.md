Exercise 14.2 asks to show that the full collineation group of the Hall plane of order $9$ is transitive on its affine lines. This provides a concrete example of a non-desarguesian plane that satisfies the hypothesis of Wagner's Theorem (and hence must be a translation plane, which Hall planes are). Together with Exercise 14.1, it shows that order $9$ is a special threshold for Hall planes regarding line-transitivity.
