Lemma 14.13 classifies finite affine planes whose collineation groups satisfy the involutory homology properties established in Lemma 14.12. Under these conditions, the affine plane $\mathcal{B}$ must fall into one of three categories: a translation plane, the dual of a translation plane, or a plane of type $\mathcal{H}$ (a specific configuration related to certain exceptional planes). The proof is a detailed case analysis examining the possible configurations of involutory homology centers and axes. In the context of Wagner's Theorem, this classification feeds into the induction argument: type $\mathcal{H}$ planes and dual translation planes are ruled out by the global structure, leaving only translation planes as viable.
