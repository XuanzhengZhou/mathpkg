Theorem 14.6 provides three equivalent characterizations of line-transitivity for a collineation group $\Pi$ of a finite affine plane $\mathcal{A}$. The group acts transitively on affine lines if and only if it acts transitively on affine points and on special points (points at infinity), if and only if it acts transitively on affine flags (incident point-line pairs). This theorem is fundamental to Wagner's Theorem because it connects line-transitivity to flag-transitivity and point-transitivity, enabling the use of permutation group theoretic arguments on the line at infinity.
