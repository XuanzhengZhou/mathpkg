Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a finite affine plane and $\Pi$ be a collineation group of $\mathcal{A}$. Then the following statements are equivalent:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\Pi$ is transitive on the affine lines of $\mathcal{A}$.
\item[(ii)] $\Pi$ is transitive on the affine points of $\mathcal{A}$ and $\Pi$ is transitive on the special points of $\mathcal{A}$.
\item[(iii)] $\Pi$ is transitive on the affine flags of $\mathcal{A}$. (An affine flag is an incident point-line pair $(V, l)$ where $V$ is an affine point and $l$ is an affine line.)
\end{enumerate}
