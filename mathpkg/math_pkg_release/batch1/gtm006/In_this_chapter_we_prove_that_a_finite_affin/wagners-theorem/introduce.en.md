Wagner's Theorem (1965) is the central result of Chapter XIV. It states that any finite affine plane possessing a collineation group that acts transitively on its affine lines must be a translation plane. The proof, which follows Wagner's original argument, splits into two parts: Lemma 14.8 handles the cases where the order $n$ is even or a prime power, and the remainder of the chapter proves that when $n$ is odd, it must be a prime power (so Lemma 14.8 applies). This second part uses deep permutation group theory (Results 14.1-14.4) together with the theory of $2$-subplanes and involutory homologies. The theorem immediately yields the celebrated Ostrom-Wagner Theorem as a corollary.
