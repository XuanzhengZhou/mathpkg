Let $\mathcal{O}$ be an orbit of $\Sigma$ on $\mathcal{S}$. Since $\Sigma \trianglelefteq \Lambda$, the setwise stabilizer of $\mathcal{O}$ in $\Lambda$ contains $\Sigma$. For any $\lambda \in \Lambda$, the image $\mathcal{O}^\lambda$ is also an orbit of $\Sigma$ (since $\Sigma^\lambda = \Sigma$). Thus the orbits of $\Sigma$ form a system of imprimitivity for $\Lambda$. Since $\Lambda$ is primitive, this system must be trivial: either all orbits have size $1$ or there is only one orbit. If all orbits have size $1$, then $\Sigma$ fixes every point of $\mathcal{S}$, so $\Sigma = 1$, contradicting non-triviality. Hence there is exactly one orbit, meaning $\Sigma$ is transitive on $\mathcal{S}$.
