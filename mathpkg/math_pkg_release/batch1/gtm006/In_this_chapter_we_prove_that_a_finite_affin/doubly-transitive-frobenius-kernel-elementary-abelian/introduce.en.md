Result 14.4 combines Results 14.1, 14.2, and 14.3 into a powerful structural statement. Under the hypotheses that $\Pi$ is doubly transitive on $n+1$ points with $n$ odd, and each point stabilizer $\Pi_a$ contains a Frobenius subgroup $\Gamma_a$ of even order whose kernel $\Sigma_a$ is normal in $\Pi_a$, the conclusion is that $\Sigma_a$ is elementary abelian and $n$ is a prime power. This result is crucial in Wagner's Theorem: it is precisely the tool needed to show that when the affine plane has odd order, that order must be a prime power, completing the proof that the plane is a translation plane. The fact that $\Sigma_a$ is abelian follows because $\Gamma_a$ contains an element of order $2$ not in its kernel, making $\Sigma_a$ abelian and therefore soluble.
