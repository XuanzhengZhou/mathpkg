The Ostrom-Wagner Theorem is a celebrated result in finite geometry: any finite projective plane admitting a collineation group that acts doubly transitively on its points must be desarguesian (i.e., coordinatized by a finite field), and the group contains the little projective group. The proof follows directly from Wagner's Theorem: double transitivity on points implies (by Exercise 13.5) double transitivity on lines, hence the line stabilizer $\Pi_l$ is transitive on the affine lines of $\mathcal{P}^l$, making it a translation plane by Wagner's Theorem. Transitivity on lines then forces the plane to be Moufang, and a finite Moufang plane is necessarily desarguesian.
