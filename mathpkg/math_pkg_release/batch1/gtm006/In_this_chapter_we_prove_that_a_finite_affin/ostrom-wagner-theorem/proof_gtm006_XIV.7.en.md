By Exercise 13.5, since $\Pi$ is doubly transitive on the points of $\mathcal{P}$, it is also doubly transitive on the lines of $\mathcal{P}$. In particular, for any line $l$ of $\mathcal{P}$, the line stabilizer $\Pi_l$ is transitive on the affine lines of the affine plane $\mathcal{P}^l$ (the plane obtained by removing $l$).

By Wagner's Theorem, $\mathcal{P}^l$ is a translation plane with respect to $l$. Hence, by the transitivity on lines, $\mathcal{P}$ is a Moufang plane (every line is a translation line). By the Corollary to Theorem 6.20, a finite Moufang plane is desarguesian.

That $\Pi$ contains the little projective group follows from the fact that $\Pi$ contains all elations with axis $l$ for any line $l$ in $\mathcal{P}$ (a consequence of the translation plane property at every line). $\square$
