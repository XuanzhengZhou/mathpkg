Theorem 14.10 establishes a fundamental numerical relationship between a finite projective plane and any $2$-subplane: the order $n$ of the larger plane is a power of $2$ times the order $m$ of the $2$-subplane, specifically $n = m^{2^g}$ for some non-negative integer $g$. Equivalently, the index $[\mathcal{P}:\mathcal{Q}]$ in terms of order is a power of $2$. This theorem is essential in Wagner's proof because it constrains the possible orders of minimal $2$-subplanes relative to the original plane, enabling an inductive argument on the exponent $g$.
