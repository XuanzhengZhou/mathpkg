Result 14.2 describes structural properties of Frobenius permutation groups of order $2n$ acting on $n$ points, with $n$ odd. Part (i) asserts that any two distinct points can be interchanged by an involution in the group. Part (ii) gives a formula relating the number of involutions in a subgroup to its order: a subgroup containing $r \geq 1$ involutions has order exactly $2r$. This result, proved by Wagner specifically for the proof of Wagner's Theorem, is established in the appendix.
