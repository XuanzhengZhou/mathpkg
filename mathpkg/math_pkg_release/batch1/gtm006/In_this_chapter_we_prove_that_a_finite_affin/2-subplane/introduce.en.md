A $2$-subplane of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ with respect to a collineation group $\Pi$ is a subplane $\mathcal{Q}$ that arises as the fixed configuration of some $2$-subgroup of $\Pi$. In other words, there exists a subgroup of $\Pi$ whose order is a power of $2$ and whose set of fixed points and fixed lines are exactly the points and lines of $\mathcal{Q}$. Since the identity is a $2$-group, $\mathcal{P}$ itself is always a $2$-subplane. The concept of $2$-subplanes is crucial in the proof of Wagner's Theorem, where a minimal $2$-subplane is used to set up an inductive argument on the structure of the collineation group.
