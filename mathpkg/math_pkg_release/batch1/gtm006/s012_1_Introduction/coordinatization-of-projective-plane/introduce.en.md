Coordinatization is the process of assigning algebraic coordinates to the points and lines of an arbitrary projective plane using a chosen quadrangle (coordinate frame). Points are represented as ordered pairs $(x,y)$ or symbols $(m)$ and $(\infty)$, while lines are $[m,k]$, $[k]$, or $[\infty]$. The algebraic structure arising from the incidence relations in this coordinate system is a planar ternary ring (PTR), which generalizes the familiar coordinatization by fields in Desarguesian planes.
