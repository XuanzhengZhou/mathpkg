A planar ternary ring (PTR) is the algebraic structure that arises naturally from coordinating a projective plane. The ternary operation $F(x, m, b) = y$ encodes the incidence relation: the point $(x, y)$ lies on the line $[m, b]$. Different choices of the base quadrangle yield isomorphic planar ternary rings, and every projective plane gives rise to a PTR.
