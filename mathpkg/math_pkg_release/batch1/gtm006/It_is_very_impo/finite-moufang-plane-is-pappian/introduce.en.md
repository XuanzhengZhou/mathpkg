A finite Moufang plane is always pappian and therefore also desarguesian. This result follows from the main classification theorem connecting algebraic properties of planar ternary rings to geometric transitivity conditions: in the finite case, the algebraic structures of types (a), (b), and (c) collapse, so that every finite alternative division ring is a field.
