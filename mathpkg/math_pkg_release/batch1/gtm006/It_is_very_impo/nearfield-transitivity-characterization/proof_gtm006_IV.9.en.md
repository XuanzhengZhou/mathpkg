This equivalence follows from the coordinatization theory. Taking $k = [\infty]$, the $(k,k)$-transitivity ensures that the planar ternary ring is linear (addition is associative and satisfies the left distributive law). The additional $(V,m)$-transitivity with $V$ on $k$ but not on $m$ provides enough multiplicative structure to yield a nearfield. The two subcases correspond to the two possible positions of $V$ and $m$ relative to the standard reference points $(0), (\infty), (0,0)$. Conversely, coordinatizing over a nearfield canonically produces transitivity of the specified types.
