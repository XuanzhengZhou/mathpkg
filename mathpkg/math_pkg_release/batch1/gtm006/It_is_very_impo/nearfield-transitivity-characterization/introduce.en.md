A projective plane is coordinatized by a nearfield when it satisfies $(k,k)$-transitivity on the line at infinity plus $(V,m)$-transitivity for a point $V$ on $k$ and a line $m$ not through $V$. The coordinatizing nearfield satisfies all field axioms except possibly the right distributive law and commutativity of multiplication. The two subcases correspond to whether the translation line $m$ passes through $(\infty)$ or through $(0)$.
