This equivalence summarizes results established in earlier sections. The direction "skewfield implies desarguesian" follows from coordinatization over a division ring $D$, yielding $\mathrm{PG}(2,D)$, which satisfies Desargues' theorem. Conversely, if a plane is desarguesian, its planar ternary ring satisfies both distributive laws and is associative, hence is a skewfield. The equivalence with the transitivity conditions follows from the general theory: full $(V,k)$-transitivity for all pairs characterizes desarguesian planes, and it can be shown that transitivity at all incident pairs together with transitivity at one non-incident pair suffices.
