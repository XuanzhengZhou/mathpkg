This equivalence is the dual of the nearfield characterization (type (e)). Taking $V = (\infty)$ and $k = [\infty]$, the $(V,V)$-transitivity provides the right distributive law in the planar ternary ring, while the condition involving $W$ ensures sufficient multiplicative structure to yield a right nearfield. The coordinatizing right nearfield satisfies all field axioms except possibly the left distributive law and commutativity of multiplication. The duality between types (e) and (f) is a fundamental symmetry in the classification.
