This equivalence summarizes the main classification result for Moufang planes established in earlier sections. The direction "alternative division ring implies Moufang" follows from coordinatizing the plane over an alternative division ring, which yields a Moufang plane. Conversely, if a plane is Moufang, the alternative law holds in every planar ternary ring, and the ring is an alternative division ring. The multiple equivalent geometric formulations arise from the symmetry of the Moufang condition: $(V,k)$-transitivity for all incident pairs implies transitivity of both $(k,k)$ and $(V,V)$ types, and the converse implications also hold. Two distinct lines (or points) suffice because the configuration propagates transitivity through the plane.
