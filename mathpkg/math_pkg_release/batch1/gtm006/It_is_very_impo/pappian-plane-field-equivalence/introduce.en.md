A projective plane is pappian precisely when its planar ternary ring is a field. This is the strongest algebraic condition that can be imposed on the coordinatizing structure, and it implies all other types in the classification hierarchy. Geometrically, the pappian condition is characterized by $(V,k)$-transitivity for every incident or non-incident pair of point $V$ and line $k$, together with the validity of the Configuration of Pappus.
