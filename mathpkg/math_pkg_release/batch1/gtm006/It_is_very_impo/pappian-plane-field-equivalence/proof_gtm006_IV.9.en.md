This is an equivalence summarizing earlier results. The direction "field implies pappian" follows from coordinatizing the plane over a field, which yields a projective plane $\mathrm{PG}(2,F)$ that satisfies the Configuration of Pappus by the classical Pappus theorem. The converse, that a pappian plane is coordinatized by a field, is a fundamental theorem of projective geometry: in a pappian plane, the planar ternary ring satisfies both distributive laws and is associative and commutative, hence is a field. The transitivity characterization follows from Theorem 2.6 and the general theory of $(V,k)$-transitivities developed in earlier sections.
