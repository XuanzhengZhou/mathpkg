This equivalence characterizes translation planes. Taking $k = [\infty]$, the $(k,k)$-transitivity means the plane is $(k,k)$-transitive, i.e., a translation plane with translation line $k$. The coordinatizing planar ternary ring then becomes linear and the additive loop is a group; the multiplicative structure yields a quasifield. The alternative geometric formulation using two distinct points $V$ on $k$ follows because $(V,k)$-transitivity for two points on $k$ implies $(k,k)$-transitivity. Proper quasifield planes (not nearfields) have exactly one translation line.
