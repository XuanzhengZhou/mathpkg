A projective plane is coordinatized by a right quasifield exactly when it is the dual of a translation plane, i.e., it admits $(V,V)$-transitivity for a distinguished point $V$. Equivalently, it suffices to have $(V,V)$-transitivity for two distinct points. A right quasifield is the dual algebraic structure to a quasifield: it satisfies all field axioms except possibly both distributive laws and associativity of multiplication. The dual of a quasifield plane (type (g)) is a right quasifield plane (type (h)), and vice versa.
