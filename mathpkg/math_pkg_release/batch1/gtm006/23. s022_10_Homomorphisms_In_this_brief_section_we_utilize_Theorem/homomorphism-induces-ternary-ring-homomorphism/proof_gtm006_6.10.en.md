Given the homomorphism $\phi : \mathcal{P}_1 \to \mathcal{P}_2$ and the sets $Z$, $J$, and $\overline{R}_1 = R_1 \setminus J$ defined as in the proof of Theorem 6.21, one defines $\alpha : \overline{R}_1 \to R_2$ by $x^* = \text{the unique element of } R_2 \text{ corresponding to the image under } \phi \text{ of the point } (x, 0)_1$.

For (ii), one verifies that the ternary operation is preserved by tracing the geometric definition of $T$ through the homomorphism. Since $\phi$ preserves incidence and maps the coordinatizing quadrangle of $\mathcal{P}_1$ to that of $\mathcal{P}_2$, the algebraic relations encoded by $T_1$ are carried to $T_2$ under $\alpha$.

For (iii), the coordinate compatibility $(x, y)^* = (x^*, y^*)$ follows from the fact that $\phi$ maps points to points coordinatewise with respect to the chosen quadrangles. The line coordinate compatibility $[m]^* = [m^*]$ is verified similarly using the dual coordinatization.

The verification is laborious but elementary; see Exercise 6.5 for the detailed computation.
