Using the coordinatization setup from Theorem 6.21, one can define the set $Z$ of first coordinates mapping to zero and the set $J$ of elements that are "units" relative to $Z$. Removing $J$ from the coordinate ternary ring $R_1$ yields a subset $\overline{R}_1$ that remains closed under the ternary operation $T_1$ and forms a ternary ring in its own right. This is the first step in analyzing the algebraic structure that a homomorphism induces on the coordinate level.
