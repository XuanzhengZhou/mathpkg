A homomorphism between projective planes is a mapping that sends points to points and lines to lines while preserving incidence. Theorem 6.21 establishes a fundamental dichotomy: any surjective homomorphism between projective planes must either be a bijective isomorphism, or every element in the codomain must have infinitely many pre-images. The proof uses coordinatizing quadrangles and planar ternary rings (PTRs) to analyze the kernel of the homomorphism on the coordinate level, showing that the set of coordinates mapping to zero is either trivial or infinite.
