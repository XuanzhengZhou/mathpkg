A homomorphism between projective planes is a structure-preserving map that sends points to points and lines to lines while respecting the incidence relation. This is the natural notion of a morphism in the category of projective planes. Unlike collineations (which are bijective), homomorphisms may be non-injective, and understanding their kernel structure is a central theme of Chapter 6.
