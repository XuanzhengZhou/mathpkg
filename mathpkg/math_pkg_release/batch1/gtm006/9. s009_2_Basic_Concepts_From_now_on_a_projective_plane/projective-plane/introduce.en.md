A projective plane is the central object of study in projective geometry. It is an incidence structure consisting of points and lines satisfying three axioms: any two points determine a unique line, any two lines determine a unique point (no parallel lines), and there exist four points in general position (no three collinear). This third axiom rules out degenerate configurations and ensures the plane is genuinely two-dimensional.
