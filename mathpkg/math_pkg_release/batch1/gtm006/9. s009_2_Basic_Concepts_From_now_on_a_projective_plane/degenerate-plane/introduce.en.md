A degenerate plane is a closed configuration (a set of points and lines satisfying axioms (i) and (ii) of a projective plane: any two distinct points lie on a unique common line, and any two distinct lines meet at a unique common point) that fails to satisfy axiom (iii), i.e., it does not contain four points with no three collinear. Degenerate planes are precisely the closed configurations that are not proper projective planes, and classifying them helps clarify the minimal requirements for a genuine projective plane.
