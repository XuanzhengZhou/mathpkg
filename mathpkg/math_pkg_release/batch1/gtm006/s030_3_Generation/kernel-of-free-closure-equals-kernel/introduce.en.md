This corollary to Theorem 11.4 states that taking the free closure does not change the kernel of a configuration. In other words, the elements with at least three incidences (the "confined" core) of the free closure $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{B})$ are exactly those of the original configuration $\mathcal{B}$. This is because the free closure construction only adds new elements that are incident with exactly two existing elements, so no new element acquires three or more incidences.
