Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an affine plane, $k$ and $m$ distinct parallel lines of $\mathcal{A}$, and $Y$ a point of $\mathcal{A}$ not on $k$ or $m$. Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a configuration consisting of $\mathcal{A}$, the point $X$, and the line $n$, where $X$ is on $k$, $m$, and $n$ while $n$ passes through $X$ and $Y$. Let $\mathcal{B}$ be the union of $\mathcal{A}$ and $n$. Show:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] $\mathcal{A} < \mathcal{B} < \mathcal{C}$;
  \item[(ii)] $\mathcal{C}$ is an extension (free) of $\mathcal{A}$ and an extension of $\mathcal{B}$;
  \item[(iii)] $\mathcal{B}$ is not an extension of $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{enumerate}
This demonstrates that (a) and (b) do not imply (c) in Lemma 11.6 when the word "free" is omitted.
