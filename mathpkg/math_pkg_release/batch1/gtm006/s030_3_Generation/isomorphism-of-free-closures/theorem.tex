Let $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ be openly finite configurations. Then $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A})$ and $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{B})$ are isomorphic if and only if there is a sequence $\mathcal{C}_1, \mathcal{C}_2, \ldots, \mathcal{C}_n$ of configurations such that $\mathcal{C}_1 \cong \mathcal{A}$, $\mathcal{C}_n \cong \mathcal{B}$, and for each $i = 1, \ldots, n-1$, either $\mathcal{C}_{i+1}$ is a free extension of $\mathcal{C}_i$ or $\mathcal{C}_i$ is a free extension of $\mathcal{C}_{i+1}$.
