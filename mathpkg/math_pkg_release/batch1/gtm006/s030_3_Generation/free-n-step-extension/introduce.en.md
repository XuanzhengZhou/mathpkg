A free $n$-step extension is a chain of $n$ successive free one-step extensions. At each step, newly added elements have exactly two incidences with the existing configuration. Free extensions are minimal in the sense that no unnecessary incidences are introduced, making them crucial for constructing the free closure $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{C})$ of a configuration.
