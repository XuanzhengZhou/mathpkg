Lemma 11.7 provides a crucial existence result: if two configurations $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ in a closed configuration $\mathcal{P}$ are mutually extendable (each sits inside some extension of the other), then they admit a common extension $\mathcal{C}$ within $\mathcal{P}$. When $\mathcal{P}$ is the free closure of both, the common extension can be chosen to be a free extension of each. This lemma underpins the proof of Theorem 11.8 by establishing that configurations with isomorphic free closures must share a common free extension.
