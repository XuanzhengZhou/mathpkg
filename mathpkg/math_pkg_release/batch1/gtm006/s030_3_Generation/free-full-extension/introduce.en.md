A free full extension combines the properties of being free (new elements have minimal incidences) and full (all possible pairwise incidences are realized). This combined notion is central to the construction of the free closure $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{C})$: starting from $\mathcal{C}$, one iteratively adds, for each pair of like elements not yet incident, a new unlike element incident with exactly those two, producing a sequence of free full one-step extensions whose union is $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{C})$.
