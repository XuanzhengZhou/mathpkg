A full $n$-step extension is a tower of $n$ successive full one-step extensions. At each step, all pairs of distinct like elements from the previous configuration are made incident in the next. This guarantees that after $n$ steps, the configuration is "full" in the sense that all possible pairwise incidences among elements from earlier stages have been realized.
