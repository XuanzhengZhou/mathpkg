A full one-step extension of a configuration $\mathcal{C}$ is a one-step extension $\mathcal{C}_1$ in which every pair of distinct like elements (two points or two lines) of $\mathcal{C}$ that did not already meet in $\mathcal{C}$ are made to meet in $\mathcal{C}_1$. This ensures that all possible pairwise incidences among the original elements are realized. A configuration is closed if and only if it is a full extension of itself.
