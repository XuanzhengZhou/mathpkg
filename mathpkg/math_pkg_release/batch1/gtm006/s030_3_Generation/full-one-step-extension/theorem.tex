If $\mathcal{C}_1$ is a one-step extension of $\mathcal{C}$ such that every pair of distinct like elements in $\mathcal{C}$ always meet in $\mathcal{C}_1$, then $\mathcal{C}_1$ is a full one-step extension of $\mathcal{C}$. In other words, in a full one-step extension, every pair of distinct points of $\mathcal{C}$ are incident with a common line in $\mathcal{C}_1$, and every pair of distinct lines of $\mathcal{C}$ are incident with a common point in $\mathcal{C}_1$.
