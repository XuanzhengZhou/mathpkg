Consider $\alpha^{-1}\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta$. For any $X$ on $a$, $X^{\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta} = X^{\beta^{-1}}\alpha\beta$. Since $X^\beta$ is on $a$, $X^{\beta^{-1}\alpha} = X^{\beta^{-1}}$, so $X^{\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta} = X^{\beta^{-1}\beta} = X$. Thus $\alpha^{-1}\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta$ fixes $a$ pointwise. Similarly $\alpha^{-1}\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta$ fixes $B$ linewise. Finally since $A^\beta \neq A$, $\alpha\beta \neq \beta\alpha$ (by Corollary 1 to Lemma 4.11), so $\alpha^{-1}\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta$ is a non-trivial $(B, a)$-elation.
