The commutator $\alpha^{-1}\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta$ of an $(A,a)$-elation $\alpha$ and a $(B,b)$-homology $\beta$ is a non-trivial $(B,a)$-elation, provided $A \notin b$ and $B \in a$. The proof uses the fact that $\beta^{-1}\alpha\beta$ is an elation whose axis passes through $B$, and the commutator fixes $a$ pointwise and $B$ linewise. This lemma is a key tool in characterizing desarguesian planes via the existence of sufficient perspectivities.
