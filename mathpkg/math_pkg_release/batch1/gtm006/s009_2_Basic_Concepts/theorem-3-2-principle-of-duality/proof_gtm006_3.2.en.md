Let $\mathcal{P}$ be any projective plane and let $\mathcal{P}^*$ be its dual plane. By Lemma 3.1, $\mathcal{P}^*$ satisfies the projective plane axioms, so $\mathcal{P}^*$ is a projective plane. Now let $A$ be any theorem that holds for all projective planes. In particular, $A$ holds for the dual plane $\mathcal{P}^*$. But the statement $A$ interpreted in $\mathcal{P}^*$, after translating "point" to "line of $\mathcal{P}$" and "line" to "point of $\mathcal{P}$", is precisely the dual statement $A^*$ interpreted in $\mathcal{P}$. Since $\mathcal{P}$ was arbitrary and $(\mathcal{P}^*)^* = \mathcal{P}$, the dual $A^*$ holds for every projective plane. $\square$
