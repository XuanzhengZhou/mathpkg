The Bruck-Ryser Theorem is a landmark result in finite geometry: it provides the only general necessary condition for the existence of a finite projective plane. If the order $n$ is congruent to 1 or 2 modulo 4, then $n$ must be expressible as a sum of two squares. This rules out infinitely many orders (such as $n = 6, 14, 21, \ldots$) and was proved using elementary number-theoretic techniques. The smallest composite integer not covered by the theorem is $n = 10$, whose existence remains an open problem.
