A \textbf{projective plane} $\mathcal{P}$ is a set of points and lines, called the elements of $\mathcal{P}$, together with an incidence relation between the points and lines such that
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] Any two distinct points are incident with a unique line.
  \item[(ii)] Any two distinct lines are incident with a unique point.
  \item[(iii)] There exist four points such that no three are incident with one line.
\end{enumerate}
