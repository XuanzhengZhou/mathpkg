Theorem 3.5 is the fundamental counting theorem for finite projective planes. It shows that the incidence structure of a finite projective plane is highly regular: all lines have the same size ($n+1$), all points lie on the same number of lines ($n+1$), and the total numbers of points and lines are both $n^2 + n + 1$. The integer $n$ is called the order of the projective plane and must satisfy $n \geq 2$. This theorem underpins all combinatorial investigations of finite projective planes.
