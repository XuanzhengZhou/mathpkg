A triangle in a projective plane is the configuration formed by three non-collinear points together with the three lines joining them. It is the simplest non-trivial configuration in projective geometry and serves as a building block for more complex figures such as quadrangles and quadrilaterals. The dual of a triangle is again a triangle (three non-concurrent lines and their intersection points).
