A subplane is a projective plane contained within a larger projective plane, inheriting the same incidence relation. Every projective plane is a subplane of itself (the improper subplane). Proper subplanes are of particular interest: they correspond to subgeometries in the coordinate representation and their possible orders are restricted by Bruck's Theorem (Theorem 3.7). For example, the projective plane $\text{PG}(2, p^{ab})$ contains a subplane $\text{PG}(2, p^b)$ whenever $b \mid a$.
