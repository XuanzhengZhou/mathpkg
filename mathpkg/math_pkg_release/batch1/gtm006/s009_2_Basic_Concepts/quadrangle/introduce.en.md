A quadrangle is a set of four points in general position — that is, no three of them lie on a common line. This is the configuration guaranteed to exist by axiom (iii) in the definition of a projective plane. Quadrangles are essential for establishing the non-degeneracy and richness of projective planes, and they play a key role in proving fundamental results such as the existence of quadrilaterals and the Principle of Duality.
