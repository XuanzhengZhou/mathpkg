A quadrilateral is the dual concept to a quadrangle: it consists of four lines in general position, meaning no three lines pass through a common point. By the Principle of Duality, every property of quadrangles translates into a corresponding property of quadrilaterals, and vice versa. Lemma 3.1 establishes that the four lines joining the points of a quadrangle always form a quadrilateral.
