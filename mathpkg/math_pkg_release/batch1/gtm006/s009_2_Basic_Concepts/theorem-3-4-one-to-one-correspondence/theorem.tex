Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a projective plane.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] If $l$ and $m$ are any two lines of $\mathcal{P}$, there is a one-to-one correspondence between the points of $l$ and the points of $m$.
  \item[(b)] If $l$ is any line and $P$ is any point of $\mathcal{P}$, there is a one-to-one correspondence between the points of $l$ and the lines through $P$.
\end{enumerate}
Furthermore, the dual statements hold: any two pencils of lines are in one-to-one correspondence, and for any line $l$ and point $P$, the set of lines through $P$ is in one-to-one correspondence with the set of points on $l$.
