Theorem 3.4 shows that in a projective plane, all lines have the same cardinality (they are in bijection) and that this cardinality equals the number of lines through any given point. This uniform cardinality property is fundamental to the definition of the order of a finite projective plane and underlies the counting arguments used throughout finite projective geometry.
