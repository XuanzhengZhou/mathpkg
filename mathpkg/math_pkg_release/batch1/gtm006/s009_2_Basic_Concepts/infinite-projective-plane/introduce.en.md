An infinite projective plane is one that does not admit a finite order as defined by Theorem 3.5. In such planes, lines may have infinitely many points, and the counting arguments of finite projective geometry do not apply. Infinite projective planes arise naturally from coordinate geometry over infinite fields (such as the real or complex numbers), and their study involves topological and model-theoretic considerations beyond the combinatorial methods used for finite planes.
