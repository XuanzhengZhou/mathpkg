Theorem 10.18 describes the orbit structure of the full collineation group of a finite Hall plane of order $q^2 > 9$ acting on its special points. The special points split into exactly two orbits: a small orbit of size $q+1$ and a large orbit of size $q^2-q$. This classification is fundamental to understanding the symmetry of Hall planes and plays a key role in proving that the full collineation group coincides with the inherited group from the desarguesian plane.
