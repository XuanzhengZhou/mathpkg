An inherited collineation arises in the context of derivation of affine planes. Given an affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ with a derivation set $\mathcal{S}$, a collineation $\alpha$ of $\mathcal{A}$ that leaves $\mathcal{S}$ invariant induces a collineation of the derived plane $\mathcal{A}_1$. Such induced collineations are called inherited because they descend from collineations of the original plane. The set of all inherited collineations forms a subgroup of the full collineation group of the derived plane.
