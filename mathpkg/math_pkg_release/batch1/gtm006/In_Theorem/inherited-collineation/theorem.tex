Let $\mathcal{S}$ be a derivation set for an affine plane $\mathcal{A}$ and let $\alpha$ be a collineation of $\mathcal{A}$ with $\mathcal{S}^{\alpha} = \mathcal{S}$. Then $\alpha$ induces a collineation of the derived plane $\mathcal{D}_{\mathcal{g}}(\mathcal{A}) = \mathcal{A}_1$. Such an $\alpha$ is called an \textit{inherited collineation} of $\mathcal{A}_1$.
