The full collineation group of a projective or affine plane is the group of all automorphisms (isomorphisms from the plane to itself). It is denoted by $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{B}$ and serves as the fundamental symmetry group of the plane. Subgroups of the full collineation group are called collineation groups, and their study is central to understanding the geometric structure of projective planes.
