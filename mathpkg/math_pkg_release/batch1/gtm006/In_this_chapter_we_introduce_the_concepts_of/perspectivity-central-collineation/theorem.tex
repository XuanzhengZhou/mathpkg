If a collineation $\alpha$ fixes a line $\ell$ pointwise and a point $V$ linewise, then $\alpha$ is called a \emph{$(V,\ell)$-perspectivity} or a \emph{$(V,\ell)$-central collineation}. The point $V$ is called the \emph{centre} and $\ell$ is the \emph{axis}. If $V \in \ell$, $\alpha$ is an \emph{elation}; if $V \notin \ell$, $\alpha$ is a \emph{homology}. The identity is regarded as both a homology and an elation.
