The proof follows from the orbit-stabilizer theorem. In a finite plane of order $n$, there are $n$ points (other than $V$) on any line through $V$ not equal to $\ell \cap V$ (for elations), or $n-1$ such points (for homologies). The group $\Pi_{(V,\ell)}$ acts semi-regularly on these points. Transitivity means the action is transitive, which occurs exactly when the group order equals the number of points.
