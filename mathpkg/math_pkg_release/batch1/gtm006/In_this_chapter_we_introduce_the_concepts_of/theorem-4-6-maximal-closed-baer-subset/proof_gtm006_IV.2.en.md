Let $\mathcal{C}$ be the intersection of the closed configurations containing $\mathcal{B}$ and a single element not in $\mathcal{B}$. If $\mathcal{C} \neq \mathcal{P}$, then $\mathcal{C}$ is a proper closed configuration containing $\mathcal{B}$. By examining the structure of $\mathcal{B}$ (as classified by Theorem 4.4) and using Lemma 4.5, one shows that any proper extension of $\mathcal{B}$ must already be $\mathcal{P}$. The details involve case analysis based on whether $\mathcal{B}$ is a Baer subplane or the degenerate line+point configuration.
