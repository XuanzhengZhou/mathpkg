Let $\Pi = \operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{P}$.

\textbf{(a)} We show $\Pi_{(\ell,\ell)}$ is transitive on the lines through $Q = \ell m$ distinct from $\ell$, and then use Exercise 4.13. Let $h, k$ be distinct lines through $Q$, neither equal to $\ell$. Pick $A$ on $\ell$, $A \neq Q$, and choose $H$ on $h$, $K$ on $k$ such that $A, H, K$ are collinear. Since $\ell$ is a translation line, $\mathcal{P}$ is $(A,\ell)$-transitive, so there is an elation $\alpha \in \Pi_{(\ell,\ell)}$ with $H^\alpha = K$. Since $Q \in \ell$, $Q^\alpha = Q$, so $h^\alpha = (QH)^\alpha = Q^\alpha H^\alpha = QK = k$. Thus $\Pi_{(\ell,\ell)}$ is transitive on lines through $Q$ different from $\ell$. Exercise 4.13 now gives that every line through $Q$ is a translation line.

\textbf{(b)} The proof consists of showing $\Pi$ is transitive on the lines of $\mathcal{P}$ and again applying Exercise 4.13.
