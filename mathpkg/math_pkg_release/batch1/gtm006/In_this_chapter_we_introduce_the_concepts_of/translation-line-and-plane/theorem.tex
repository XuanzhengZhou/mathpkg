If $\ell$ is any line of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ such that $\mathcal{P}$ is $(\ell,\ell)$-transitive, then $\ell$ is called a \emph{translation line} of $\mathcal{P}$ and $\mathcal{P}$ is said to be a \emph{translation plane} with respect to the line $\ell$. Dually, a point $A$ is called a \emph{translation point} if $\mathcal{P}$ is $(A,A)$-transitive, and $\mathcal{P}$ is said to be a \emph{dual translation plane} with respect to $A$.
