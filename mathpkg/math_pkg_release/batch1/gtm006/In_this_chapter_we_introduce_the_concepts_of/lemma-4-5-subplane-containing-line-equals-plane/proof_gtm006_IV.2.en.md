Let $A$ be any point of $\mathcal{P}$ and let $B, C$ be any two distinct points of $\mathcal{P}_0$ not on $\ell$ such that $AB \neq AC$. Let $AB \cap \ell = X$ and $AC \cap \ell = Y$. Since $B \in \mathcal{P}_0$ and $X \in \mathcal{P}_0$, we have $BX \in \mathcal{P}_0$. Similarly $CY$ is also a line of $\mathcal{P}_0$. Thus, since $A$ is the intersection of two lines of $\mathcal{P}_0$, $A \in \mathcal{P}_0$, and so every point of $\mathcal{P}$ is a point of $\mathcal{P}_0$. Since every line of $\mathcal{P}$ is the join of two points of $\mathcal{P}_0$, every line of $\mathcal{P}$ is a line of $\mathcal{P}_0$ and the lemma is proved. $\square$
