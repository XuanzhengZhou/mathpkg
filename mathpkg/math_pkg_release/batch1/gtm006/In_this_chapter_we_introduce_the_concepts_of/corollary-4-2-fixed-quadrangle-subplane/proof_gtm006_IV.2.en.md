A quadrangle consists of four points, no three collinear. When a collineation fixes a quadrangle, the closed configuration $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ contains these four points and their joins, which generate a projective subplane. By Lemma 4.2, $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is closed; containing a quadrangle forces it to be a full subplane.
