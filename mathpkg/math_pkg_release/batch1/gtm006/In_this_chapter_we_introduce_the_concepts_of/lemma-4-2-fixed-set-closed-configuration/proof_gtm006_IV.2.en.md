Let $A, B$ be any two distinct points of $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$. Then, since $\alpha$ preserves incidence,
$$(AB)^\alpha = A^\alpha B^\alpha.$$
But since $A, B \in \mathcal{F}(\alpha)$, $A^\alpha = A$ and $B^\alpha = B$. Thus
$$(AB)^\alpha = AB,$$
i.e. $AB \in \mathcal{F}(\alpha)$. Dually, for any two distinct lines $\ell, m$ of $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$, $\ell m \in \mathcal{F}(\alpha)$. Hence $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is a closed configuration.
