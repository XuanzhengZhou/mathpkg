Since $\alpha$ fixes all lines through $V$, the line $VX$ is fixed by $\alpha$. Since $X^\alpha$ lies on $(VX)^\alpha = VX$, the points $V, X, X^\alpha$ are collinear. Dually, $\alpha$ fixes all points on $\ell$, so $\ell \cap m$ is fixed. Then $(\ell \cap m)^\alpha = \ell \cap m$ lies on $m^\alpha$, so $\ell, m, m^\alpha$ are concurrent.
