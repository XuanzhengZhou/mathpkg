By Lemma 4.8, $\langle\alpha\rangle$ induces a semi-regular permutation group on the non-fixed points of any line (other than the axis) through the centre $V$.

If $\alpha$ is an elation ($V \in \ell$), then the points on any line through $V$ (other than $\ell$) are $n$ in number, and $k$ must divide $n$.

If $\alpha$ is a homology ($V \notin \ell$), then the points on any line through $V$ (other than the line $V\ell$) that are not on $\ell$ are $n-1$ in number, and $k$ must divide $n-1$. $\square$
