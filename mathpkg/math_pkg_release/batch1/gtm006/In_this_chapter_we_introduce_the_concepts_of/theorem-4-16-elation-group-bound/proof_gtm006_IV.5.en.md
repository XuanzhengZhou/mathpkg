Since every element of $\Gamma_{(\ell,\ell)}$ is an elation, the non-identity elements of $\Gamma_{(\ell,\ell)}$ act as fixed-point-free permutations on the points of $\mathcal{P}^\ell$ (the affine plane obtained by removing $\ell$). Thus $\Gamma_{(\ell,\ell)}$ induces a semi-regular permutation group on the $n^2$ points of $\mathcal{P}^\ell$. By the orbit-stabilizer theorem, $|\Gamma_{(\ell,\ell)}|$ divides $n^2$. This combines with Theorem 3.12.
