If $\alpha$ is any collineation of $\mathcal{P}$ such that $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is a subplane of $\mathcal{P}$, we call $\alpha$ a \emph{planar collineation} (or simply say that $\alpha$ is \emph{planar}). In the special case where $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is a Baer subplane, $\alpha$ is called a \emph{Baer collineation}.
