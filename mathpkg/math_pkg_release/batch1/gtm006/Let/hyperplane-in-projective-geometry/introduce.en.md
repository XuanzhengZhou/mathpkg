In a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of $g$-dimension $n$, a hyperplane is a subspace of $g$-dimension $n-1$, corresponding to an $n$-dimensional subspace of the underlying $(n+1)$-dimensional vector space $V$. Hyperplanes are the maximal proper subspaces of the projective geometry, analogous to codimension-1 subspaces.
