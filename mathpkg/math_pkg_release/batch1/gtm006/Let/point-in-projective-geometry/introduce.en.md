In a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$, a point is a subspace of $g$-dimension zero, which corresponds to a 1-dimensional subspace of the underlying vector space $V$. Points are the fundamental objects of the projective geometry, and their intersection (as subspaces) is the zero-subspace, which serves as the "empty space" in the projective setting.
