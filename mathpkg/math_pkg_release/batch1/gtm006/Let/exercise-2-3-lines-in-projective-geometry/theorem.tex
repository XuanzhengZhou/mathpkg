Let $E$ and $F$ be distinct lines in $\mathcal{P}(V)$. Show that either $E$ and $F$ meet in a unique point, in which case $E$ and $F$ lie in a unique plane of $\mathcal{P}(V)$, or $E$ and $F$ have no point in common, in which case $E$ and $F$ are contained in a unique space of $g$-dimension three in $\mathcal{P}(V)$.
