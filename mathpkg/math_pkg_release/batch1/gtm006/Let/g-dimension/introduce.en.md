The $g$-dimension (geometric dimension) of a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is one less than the algebraic dimension of the underlying vector space $V$. For a subspace $W$ of $V$, its $g$-dimension satisfies $gd(W) = d(W) - 1$. This shift aligns geometric intuition with algebraic structure: points correspond to 1-dimensional subspaces, lines to 2-dimensional subspaces, and so on.
