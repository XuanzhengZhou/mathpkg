Let $W$ be a subspace of a vector space $V$. Then $\mathcal{P}(W)$ is contained in $\mathcal{P}(V)$, and $\mathcal{P}(W)$ is called a complete subgeometry of $\mathcal{P}(V)$. More generally, if $V$ is an $n$-dimensional left vector space over a skewfield $K$, and $K$ has a sub-skewfield $F$, then $F$ is also a left vector space over $F$ and contains a set of vectors $U$ which form an $m$-dimensional vector space over $F$. Then $\mathcal{P}(U)$ is contained in $\mathcal{P}(V)$, but $U$ need not be a sub-vector space of $V$. $\mathcal{P}(U)$ is a complete subgeometry of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ if and only if $U$ is a subspace of $V$.
