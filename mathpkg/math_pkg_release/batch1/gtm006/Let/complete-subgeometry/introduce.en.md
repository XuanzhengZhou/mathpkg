A complete subgeometry $\mathcal{P}(W)$ of a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ arises when $W$ is a vector subspace of $V$. This is distinguished from the more general situation where a subset $U \subseteq V$ forms a vector space over a sub-skewfield $F$ of $K$, yielding a subgeometry $\mathcal{P}(U)$ that is contained in $\mathcal{P}(V)$ but not necessarily complete. The distinction is important because in the latter case $U$ may have larger dimension than $V$ over $F$.
