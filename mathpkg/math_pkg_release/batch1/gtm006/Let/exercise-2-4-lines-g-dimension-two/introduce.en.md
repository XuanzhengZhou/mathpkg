Exercise 2.4 establishes that in a projective geometry of $g$-dimension two (i.e., a projective plane), every pair of distinct lines must intersect in a unique point. This is a corollary of Exercise 2.3, because in $g$-dimension two, any two distinct lines cannot be contained in a space of $g$-dimension three, forcing them to meet. Together with the dual statement about points determining lines, this gives the symmetric incidence structure characteristic of projective planes.
