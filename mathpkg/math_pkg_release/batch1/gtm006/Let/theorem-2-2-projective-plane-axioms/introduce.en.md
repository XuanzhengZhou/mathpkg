Theorem 2.2 establishes that a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of $g$-dimension two satisfies three fundamental incidence properties: any two distinct points determine a unique line, any two distinct lines meet in a unique point, and there exist four points no three of which are collinear. These three properties later serve as the axiomatic definition of a projective plane, and a central theme of the book is to investigate what additional assumptions beyond these three axioms are needed to recover the classical projective geometries.
