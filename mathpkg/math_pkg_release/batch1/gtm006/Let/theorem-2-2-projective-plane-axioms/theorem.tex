Let $\mathcal{P}(V)$ have $g$-dimension two. Then the points and lines of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ satisfy:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] every pair of distinct points are incident with a unique common line;
\item[(ii)] every pair of distinct lines are incident with a unique common point;
\item[(iii)] $\mathcal{P}(V)$ contains a set of four points with the property that no three of them lie on a common line.
\end{enumerate}
