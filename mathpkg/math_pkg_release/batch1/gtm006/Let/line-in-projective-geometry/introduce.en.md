In a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$, a line is a subspace of $g$-dimension one, corresponding to a 2-dimensional subspace of the underlying vector space $V$. Lines together with points form the basic incidence structure of projective geometries, particularly in the case of $g$-dimension two where the axioms of a projective plane are satisfied.
