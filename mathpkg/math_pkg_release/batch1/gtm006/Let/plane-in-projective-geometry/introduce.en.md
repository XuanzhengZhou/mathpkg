In a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$, a plane is a subspace of $g$-dimension two, corresponding to a 3-dimensional subspace of the underlying vector space $V$. Planes appear naturally when considering the geometry generated by two distinct intersecting lines, as described in Exercise 2.3.
