Theorem 3.10 establishes that every affine plane arises from a projective plane by removing one line (the "line at infinity") and all its points. Moreover, this projective plane is unique up to isomorphism. The construction adds one new "point at infinity" for each parallel class of lines, and one new "line at infinity" containing exactly these new points. This shows that the categories of affine planes and projective planes with a distinguished line are equivalent.
