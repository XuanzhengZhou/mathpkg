The Bruck-Ryser-Chowla theorem is one of the deepest non-existence results for finite projective planes. It states that if a projective plane of order $n$ exists and $n \equiv 1, 2 \pmod{4}$, then $n$ must be expressible as a sum of two integer squares. This rules out infinitely many orders — for example, $n = 6, 14, 21, 22$ are all impossible. The proof uses the incidence matrix relation $AA^T = nI + J$, Lagrange's four-square theorem, and an ingenious rational parametrization argument.
