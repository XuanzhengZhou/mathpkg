In an affine plane $\mathcal{A}$, two lines $l$ and $m$ are called \text{parallel}, written $l \parallel m$, if $l = m$ or $l$ and $m$ have no point in common. Then parallelism is an equivalence relation on the lines of $\mathcal{A}$. The equivalence classes are called \text{parallel classes}. Any two distinct parallel classes of lines intersect in exactly one point of the associated projective completion.
