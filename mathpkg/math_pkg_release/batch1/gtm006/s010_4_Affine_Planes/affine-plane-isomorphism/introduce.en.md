An isomorphism between two affine planes is a bijection on both points and lines that preserves the incidence relation. This is the natural notion of "sameness" for affine planes, analogous to isomorphism of projective planes. Isomorphisms of affine planes also preserve parallelism: two lines are parallel in the domain if and only if their images are parallel in the codomain.
