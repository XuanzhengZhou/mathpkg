Two affine planes $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{A}'$ are \text{isomorphic} if there exists a one-to-one mapping $\theta$ from the points of $\mathcal{A}$ onto the points of $\mathcal{A}'$ and from the lines of $\mathcal{A}$ onto the lines of $\mathcal{A}'$ such that a point $A$ is on a line $l$ in $\mathcal{A}$ if and only if $A^\theta$ is on $l^\theta$ in $\mathcal{A}'$. Such a mapping $\theta$ is called an \text{isomorphism}.
