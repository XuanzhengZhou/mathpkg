Let $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{P}^l$ for some projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ and line $l$, and let $\mathcal{Q}$ be a subplane of $\mathcal{P}$. There are three possibilities for the intersection of $l$ with $\mathcal{Q}$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $l$ is a line of $\mathcal{Q}$. Then $\mathcal{Q}^l$ is an affine plane. Its affine elements are elements of $\mathcal{A}$ and its special points are special points of $\mathcal{A}$. In this case $\mathcal{Q}^l$ is called an \text{affine subplane} (or simply \text{subplane}) of $\mathcal{A}$.
\item[(b)] $l$ contains exactly one point of $\mathcal{Q}$.
\item[(c)] $l$ contains no point of $\mathcal{Q}$. Then all elements of $\mathcal{Q}$ are special elements of $\mathcal{A}$, and $\mathcal{Q}$ is called a \text{projective subplane} of $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{enumerate}
