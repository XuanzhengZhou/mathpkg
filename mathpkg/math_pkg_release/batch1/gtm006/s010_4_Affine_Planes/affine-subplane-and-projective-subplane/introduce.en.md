When a projective plane contains a subplane, removing a line from the ambient plane yields an affine plane that may contain remnants of the subplane in three possible forms. If the removed line is also a line of the subplane, the result is an affine subplane. If the removed line avoids the subplane entirely, the subplane survives intact as a projective subplane embedded in the affine plane. The intermediate case (exactly one point of intersection) is not considered further in the book.
