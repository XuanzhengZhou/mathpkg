Let $\mathcal{B}$ be a finite plane of order $n$ (projective or affine). Let $P_1, P_2, \dots, P_v$ be a labelling of the points and $l_1, l_2, \dots, l_b$ be a labelling of the lines. An \text{incidence matrix} $A$ of $\mathcal{B}$ is a $v \times b$ matrix of zeros and ones such that $a_{ij} = 1$ if and only if $P_i$ is incident with $l_j$. For a projective plane of order $n$, $v = b = n^2 + n + 1$. For an affine plane of order $n$, $v = n^2$ and $b = n^2 + n$.
