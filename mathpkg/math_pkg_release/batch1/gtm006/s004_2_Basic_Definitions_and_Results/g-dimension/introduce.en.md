The geometric dimension (or $g$-dimension) of a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ is one less than the algebraic dimension of the underlying vector space $V$. Thus an $(n+1)$-dimensional vector space yields a projective geometry of $g$-dimension $n$. For any subspace $W$ of algebraic dimension $i+1$, its $g$-dimension equals $i$, reflecting the fact that projective geometry studies incidence properties one dimension lower.
