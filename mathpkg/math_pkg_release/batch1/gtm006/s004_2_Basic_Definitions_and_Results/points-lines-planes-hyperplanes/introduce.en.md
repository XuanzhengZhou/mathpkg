In a projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$ of $g$-dimension $n$, subspaces are classified by their $g$-dimension into familiar geometric objects. Points correspond to 1-dimensional (algebraic) subspaces, lines to 2-dimensional subspaces, planes to 3-dimensional subspaces, and hyperplanes to $n$-dimensional subspaces. This terminology aligns the algebraic lattice of subspaces with classical geometric intuition.
