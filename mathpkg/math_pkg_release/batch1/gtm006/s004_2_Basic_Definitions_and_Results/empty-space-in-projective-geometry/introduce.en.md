In the projective geometry $\mathcal{P}(V)$, the zero subspace of $V$ serves as the empty space analogous to the empty set in set theory. Since points correspond to 1-dimensional subspaces, the intersection of any two distinct points (as fundamental objects) is the zero subspace, which has $g$-dimension $-1$. This ensures that the lattice of subspaces behaves like a projective space where disjointness of points is the expected default.
