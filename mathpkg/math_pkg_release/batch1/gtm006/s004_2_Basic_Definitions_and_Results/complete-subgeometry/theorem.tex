Let $V$ be an $n$-dimensional left vector space over a skewfield $K$. If $K$ has a sub-skewfield $F$, then $V$ may also be viewed as a left vector space over $F$, containing vectors $U$ that form an $m$-dimensional vector space over $F$ (where $m$ may be larger than $n$). Since $U \subseteq V$, $\mathcal{P}(U) \subseteq \mathcal{P}(V)$. We say $\mathcal{P}(U)$ is a \textbf{complete subgeometry} of $\mathcal{P}(V)$ if $U$ is a subspace of $V$ (as a vector space over $K$), distinguishing this from the more general containment arising from a change of the underlying skewfield.
