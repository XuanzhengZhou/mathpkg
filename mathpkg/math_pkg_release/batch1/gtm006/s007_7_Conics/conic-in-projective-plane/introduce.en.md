A conic is the set of self-conjugate (absolute) points of an orthogonal polarity in a projective plane over a field of characteristic not two. In the classical projective plane $PG(2,K)$, conics correspond to non-degenerate quadratic forms and are the projective analogues of ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas in affine geometry. Conics are fundamental objects in classical projective geometry, with rich structure: they contain $q+1$ points in a finite plane of order $q$, each line meets a conic in at most 2 points, and through each point of a non-empty conic passes exactly one tangent line.
