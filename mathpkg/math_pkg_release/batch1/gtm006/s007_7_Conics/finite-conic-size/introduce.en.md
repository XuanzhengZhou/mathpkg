Corollary 2 of Lemma 2.34 specializes the one-to-one correspondence to finite projective planes: in $\mathcal{P}_2(q)$, a nonempty conic has exactly $q+1$ points, the same as a projective line. This is an immediate consequence of Corollary 1 combined with the fact that every line in $\mathcal{P}_2(q)$ contains $q+1$ points. A conic in a finite plane is therefore either empty or a $(q+1)$-set, making it a natural candidate for an oval.
