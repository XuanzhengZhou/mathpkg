This is an immediate consequence of Corollary 1 and Exercise 2.11. By Corollary 1, the points of a nonempty conic are in one-to-one correspondence with the points on a line. In the finite projective plane $\mathcal{P}_2(q)$, every line has exactly $q+1$ points (Exercise 2.11). Hence a nonempty conic has $q+1$ points. Otherwise, the conic is empty.
