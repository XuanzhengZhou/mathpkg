A conic in a projective plane is defined as the set of absolute points of an orthogonal polarity, provided the underlying field has characteristic different from $2$. This definition links the geometric notion of a conic to the algebraic structure of a symmetric bilinear form via the polarity. The characteristic restriction is essential because orthogonal polarities behave pathologically in characteristic $2$, requiring separate treatment. Conics are fundamental objects in projective geometry, serving as the projective analogues of the familiar ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas of Euclidean geometry.
