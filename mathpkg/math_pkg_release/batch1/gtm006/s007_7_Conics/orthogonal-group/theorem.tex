The subgroup of $PGL(V)$ fixing a non-empty conic $\mathcal{C}$ is called the \textit{orthogonal group} (for $\mathcal{C}$). Since all non-empty conics are equivalent, any two orthogonal groups are conjugate in $PGL(V)$. Equivalently, by Exercise 2.34, the orthogonal group may be defined as the set of elements in $PGL(V)$ commuting with the associated orthogonal polarity $\beta$.
