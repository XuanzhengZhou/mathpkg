Over finite fields, every orthogonal polarity has absolute points; equivalently, every conic is non-empty. This contrasts with the situation over fields like the real numbers, where conics can be empty (e.g., $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 0$). The proof uses the fact that every element of a finite field is a sum of two squares.
