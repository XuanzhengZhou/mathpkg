Lemma 2.34 establishes the fundamental incidence property of conics: every line in the projective plane intersects a conic in at most 2 points. This generalizes the familiar fact from Euclidean geometry that a line can intersect a circle, ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola in 0, 1, or 2 points. Lines meeting the conic in exactly one point are tangent lines; those meeting in two points are secant lines; those with no intersection are external lines.
