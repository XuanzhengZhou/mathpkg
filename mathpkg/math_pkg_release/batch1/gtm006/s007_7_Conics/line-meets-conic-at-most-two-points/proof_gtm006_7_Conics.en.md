An arbitrary point on the line joining $\langle v \rangle$ and $\langle w \rangle$ is $\langle xv + yw \rangle$. This point lies on the conic $\mathcal{C}$ with symmetric matrix $A$ iff $(xv + yw)A(xv + yw)' = 0$. Expanding using $A = A'$ gives $x^2(vAv') + 2xy(vAw') + y^2(wAw') = 0$. This is a homogeneous quadratic equation in $(x,y)$, which has at most two solutions up to scalar multiple in the projective sense (since a non-zero quadratic in $x/y$ or $y/x$ has at most two roots).
