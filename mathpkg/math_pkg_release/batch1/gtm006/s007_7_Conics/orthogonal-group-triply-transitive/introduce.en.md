Theorem 2.37 is a central structural result describing the orthogonal group of a nonempty conic. It states that the orthogonal group acts triply transitively on the points of the conic and that this action is faithful. In parametric coordinates, the group action is given by fractional linear transformations $y \mapsto (ay+b)/(cy+d)$ with $ad-bc \neq 0$, establishing an isomorphism between the orthogonal group and $PGL(2,K) \cong PGL(W)$ where $W$ is a $2$-dimensional vector space. This result provides the projective geometric foundation for the identification of conic automorphisms with Möbius transformations.
