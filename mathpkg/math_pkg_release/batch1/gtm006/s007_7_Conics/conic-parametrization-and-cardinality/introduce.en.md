A remarkable fact about projective conics (over fields of characteristic not 2) is that any non-empty conic can be parametrized by a projective line: there is a bijection between points of the conic and points of $P^1(K)$. In the finite case, this yields the count $|\mathcal{C}| = q+1$, matching the number of points on any line of $\mathcal{P}_2(q)$. The parametrization is achieved by taking a tangent line at one point and projecting the conic from that point onto any other line not through it.
