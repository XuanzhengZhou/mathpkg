Exercise 2.34 establishes the equivalence between an automorphism commuting with an orthogonal polarity and that automorphism preserving the conic (the set of absolute points). This is a fundamental exercise connecting the algebraic condition $\alpha\beta = \beta\alpha$ with the geometric condition $\mathcal{C}^{\alpha} = \mathcal{C}$, providing the justification for defining the orthogonal group either as the stabilizer of the conic or as the centralizer of the polarity in $PGL(V)$.
