If $P$ is on a conic $\mathcal{C}$ which is associated with a polarity $\beta$, then $P$ is an absolute point whose image contains no other point of $\mathcal{C}$. Since the other lines through $P$ cannot be absolute, they must contain another point of $\mathcal{C}$. Thus each line through $P$, other than the tangent line $P^\beta$, meets $\mathcal{C}$ in exactly one additional point. This establishes a bijection between the points of $\mathcal{C} \setminus \{P\}$ and the lines through $P$ other than the tangent line, which are in bijection with the points on any line not through $P$.
