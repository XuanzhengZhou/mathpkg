Theorem 2.36 provides a canonical matrix representation for any nonempty conic in a projective plane over a field of characteristic not equal to $2$. By choosing an appropriate basis -- specifically a self-polar triangle with two vertices on the conic -- the symmetric matrix $A$ associated with the orthogonal polarity can be reduced to the simple form $\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$. This yields the familiar rational parametrization $y \mapsto (y^2, y, 1)$ of the conic, establishing that all nonempty conics are projectively equivalent.
