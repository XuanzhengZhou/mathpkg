A line meeting the conic $\mathcal{C}$ with matrix $A$ exactly once (a tangent) is precisely an absolute line of the associated orthogonal polarity. If $\langle v \rangle \in \mathcal{C}$, its tangent is $\langle Av' \rangle$. Thus a non-empty conic has exactly one tangent line at each of its points, and each tangent line contains exactly one point of the conic.
