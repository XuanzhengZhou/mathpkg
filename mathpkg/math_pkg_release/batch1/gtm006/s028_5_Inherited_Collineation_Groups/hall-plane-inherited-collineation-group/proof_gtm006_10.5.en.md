This follows immediately from Theorem 10.18. The full collineation group of a Hall plane of order $q^2 > 9$ has exactly two orbits on the set of special points; this orbit structure forces every collineation to preserve the derivation set $\mathcal{S}$, and therefore the full collineation group is precisely the subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal{A}_H(q^2))$ consisting of those collineations that leave $\mathcal{S} = \{ m \mid m = \infty \text{ or } m \in \operatorname{GF}(q^2) \}$ invariant. In other words, the full collineation group equals the inherited collineation group under derivation from the desarguesian plane.
