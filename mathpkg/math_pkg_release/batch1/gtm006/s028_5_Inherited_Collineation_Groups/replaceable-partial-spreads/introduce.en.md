Replaceable partial spreads generalize the derivation construction for translation planes. Two partial spreads are replacements for each other if their unions cover the same set of points yet the individual components are all distinct. Given a spread for a translation plane, replacing a replaceable partial spread with one of its replacements yields a new spread and hence a new translation plane on the same point set, preserving the parallel classes of the non-replaced components. Derivation is the special case where $d = 2$.
