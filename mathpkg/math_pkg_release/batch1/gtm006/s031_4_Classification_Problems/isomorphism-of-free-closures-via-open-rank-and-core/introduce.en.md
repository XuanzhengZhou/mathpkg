Theorem 11.11 is the main classification theorem for free closures of openly finite configurations. It states that two such free closures are isomorphic precisely when their underlying configurations share the same open rank and have isomorphic cores. The proof combines the canonical form lemmas (11.9 and 11.10) with the earlier result that free equivalence characterizes isomorphism of free closures. This theorem reduces the isomorphism problem for free closures to the computation of two invariants: the open rank $r_0$ and the isomorphism type of the core $\mathcal{K}$.
