Let $\mathcal{P}$ be any projective plane. Choose a non-degenerate finite subconfiguration $\mathcal{A}$ that generates $\mathcal{P}$ (for instance, a complete quadrangle). Then $\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{A}$ is contained in $\mathcal{P}$, and the identity map $\beta : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ is a homomorphism of $\mathcal{A}$ onto $\mathcal{B}$. Since $\mathcal{P}$ is closed, Theorem 11.14 applies and $\beta$ extends to a homomorphism $\bar{\beta} : \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A}) \to \langle \mathcal{B} \rangle = \mathcal{P}$. As $\mathcal{P}$ is generated by $\mathcal{B}$, this map is onto, making $\mathcal{P}$ a homomorphic image of $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A})$.
