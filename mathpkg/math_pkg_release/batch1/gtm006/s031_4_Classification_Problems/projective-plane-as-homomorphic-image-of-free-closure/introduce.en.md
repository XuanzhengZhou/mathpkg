This corollary states a universality property of free closures: every projective plane, regardless of its structure, can be realized as a homomorphic image of some free closure. In categorical terms, free closures form a class of "projectively universal" objects. The result follows by applying Theorem 11.14 to the identity map on any non-degenerate finite subconfiguration that generates the plane. It underscores the fundamental role free closures play in the structural theory of projective planes.
