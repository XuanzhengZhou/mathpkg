Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an openly finite configuration with core $\mathcal{K} = \mathcal{K}(\mathcal{A})$ and open rank $r = r_0(\mathcal{A})$. Then $\mathcal{A}$ is free equivalent to a configuration $\mathcal{B}$ whose elements are exactly those of $\mathcal{K}$ together with $r$ additional points, all lying on a single line $k$ that meets $\mathcal{K}$ only in prescribed points, and such that no two of the additional $r$ points are collinear with any element of $\mathcal{K}$ except along $k$.
