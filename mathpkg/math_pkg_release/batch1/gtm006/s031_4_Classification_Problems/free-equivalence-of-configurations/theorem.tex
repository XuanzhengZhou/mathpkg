Two configurations $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ are called \textit{free equivalent} if there exists a sequence $\mathcal{C}_0, \mathcal{C}_1, \mathcal{C}_2, \ldots, \mathcal{C}_n$ of configurations such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\mathcal{A} \cong \mathcal{C}_0$, $\mathcal{B} \cong \mathcal{C}_n$,
\item[(ii)] for each $i = 0, 1, \ldots, n-1$, one of $\mathcal{C}_i, \mathcal{C}_{i+1}$ is a free extension of the other.
\end{enumerate}
It is easy to see that free equivalence is an equivalence relation.
