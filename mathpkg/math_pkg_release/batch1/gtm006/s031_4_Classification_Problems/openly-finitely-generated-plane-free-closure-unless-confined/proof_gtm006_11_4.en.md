If every element of $\mathcal{P}$ is contained in a finite confined subconfiguration, then $\mathcal{P}$ equals its own core $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ and the statement holds vacuously. Otherwise, by Theorem 11.13, $\mathcal{P} = \mathcal{F}(\mathcal{C})$ for some openly finite configuration $\mathcal{C} \neq \mathcal{P}$, which is precisely the assertion.
