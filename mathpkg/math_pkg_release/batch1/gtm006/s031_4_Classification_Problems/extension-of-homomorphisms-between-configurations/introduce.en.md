Theorem 11.14 is an extension (or lifting) theorem for homomorphisms between configurations. Given a homomorphism from a configuration $\mathcal{A}$ onto $\mathcal{B}$ (where $\mathcal{B}$ sits inside a closed configuration), the map can be uniquely extended to the entire free closure $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A})$, with the image being the closed span of $\mathcal{B}$. The proof is constructive: it extends the homomorphism one free extension step at a time, using the non-degeneracy condition to resolve ambiguities when the images of two elements coincide. An immediate consequence is that every projective plane arises as a homomorphic image of some free closure.
