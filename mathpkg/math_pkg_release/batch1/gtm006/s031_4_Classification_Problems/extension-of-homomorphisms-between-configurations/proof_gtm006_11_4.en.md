First we show how to extend a homomorphism from $\mathcal{A}$ onto $\mathcal{B}$ to a homomorphism from $\mathcal{A}_1$ into $\langle \mathcal{B} \rangle$, where $\mathcal{A}_1$ is a full free one-step extension of $\mathcal{A}$.

For any element $P$ in $\mathcal{A}$, define $P^{\bar{\beta}} = P^{\beta}$. If $X$ is in $\mathcal{A}_1$ but not in $\mathcal{A}$, then $X = yz$ for a unique pair of elements $y$ and $z$ of $\mathcal{A}$. If $y^{\beta} \neq z^{\beta}$, then $X^{\bar{\beta}} = y^{\beta} z^{\beta}$ in $\langle \mathcal{B} \rangle$ is uniquely determined. If $y^{\beta} = z^{\beta}$, then we choose $X^{\bar{\beta}}$ to be some point on $y^{\beta}$ which is also in $\langle \mathcal{B} \rangle$. The non-degeneracy of $\mathcal{B}$ guarantees that such a point exists.

Iterating this construction, we extend $\beta$ to a homomorphism of $\mathcal{F}(\mathcal{A})$ into $\langle \mathcal{B} \rangle$. Since the homomorphic image of a closed configuration is closed, the extended homomorphism must in fact be onto $\langle \mathcal{B} \rangle$.
