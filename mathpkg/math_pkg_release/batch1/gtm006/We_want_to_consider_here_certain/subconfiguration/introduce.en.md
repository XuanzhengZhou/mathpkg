A sub-configuration is an incidence substructure in the strict sense: the incidence relation of the smaller configuration $\mathcal{A}$ coincides exactly with the restriction of the incidence relation of the larger configuration $\mathcal{B}$ to the elements of $\mathcal{A}$. The defining "if and only if" condition ensures that $\mathcal{B}$ does not create new incidences among points and lines that were already present in $\mathcal{A}$; it may, however, introduce additional elements whose incidences with elements of $\mathcal{A}$ are not constrained by membership in $\mathcal{A}$.
