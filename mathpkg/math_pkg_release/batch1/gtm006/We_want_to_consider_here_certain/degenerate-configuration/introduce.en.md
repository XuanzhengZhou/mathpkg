Among closed configurations, only those containing a quadrangle qualify as projective planes. The remaining closed configurations -- those lacking a quadrangle -- are termed degenerate configurations. This classification exhausts the closed configurations and isolates the projective planes as the non-degenerate case, giving a clean dichotomy within the class of closed incidence structures.
