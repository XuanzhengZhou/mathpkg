A set $\mathcal{C}$ of points and lines, together with an incidence relation, is called a \textbf{configuration} if every pair of distinct points of $\mathcal{C}$ are incident with at most one line of $\mathcal{C}$. By duality, this condition implies that any two lines of $\mathcal{C}$ intersect in at most one point of $\mathcal{C}$.
