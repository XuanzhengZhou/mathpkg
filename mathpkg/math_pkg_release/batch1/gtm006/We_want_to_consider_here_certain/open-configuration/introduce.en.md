A configuration is open if it contains no confined sub-configuration -- that is, if no substructure of it has every element incident with at least three other elements. Openness is the complementary notion to confinedness and plays a crucial role in Lemma 11.1, where open configurations are shown to be closed under intersection and confined configurations closed under union, establishing a Galois-like relationship between the two classes.
