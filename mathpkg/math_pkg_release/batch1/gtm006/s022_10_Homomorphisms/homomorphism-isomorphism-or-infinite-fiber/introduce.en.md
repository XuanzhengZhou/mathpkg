Theorem 6.21 establishes a fundamental dichotomy for homomorphisms between projective planes. Using a coordinatizing quadrangle and planar ternary rings, the proof shows that if any single point has a finite pre-image, then all points have pre-images of the same size, and the homomorphism must be an isomorphism. Conversely, if the pre-image of any point is infinite, then all fibers are infinite.
