It suffices to prove the lemma when $\mathcal{B}$ is a full free one-step extension. Let $\beta \in \operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal{A})$ and let $P \in \mathcal{B} \setminus \mathcal{A}$. Since $\mathcal{B}$ is a free one-step extension, $P$ is incident with exactly two elements $x, y \in \mathcal{A}$. Fullness implies $x^\beta$ and $y^\beta$ meet in $\mathcal{B}$. Define $P^\beta = x^\beta \cap y^\beta$ and $X^\beta = X^\beta$ for $X \in \mathcal{A}$. This defines the extension.
