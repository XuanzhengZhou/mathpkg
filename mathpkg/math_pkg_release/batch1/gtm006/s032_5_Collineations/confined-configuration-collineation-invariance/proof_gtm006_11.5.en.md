The result is trivial, since a collineation is an incidence-preserving bijection of $\mathcal{P}$. If $\mathcal{C}$ is confined, every element of $\mathcal{C}$ is incident with at least three elements of $\mathcal{C}$. Applying $\alpha$, which preserves incidence, every element of $\mathcal{C}^\alpha$ is incident with at least three elements of $\mathcal{C}^\alpha$, so $\mathcal{C}^\alpha$ is confined. The converse follows by applying $\alpha^{-1}$.
