An element $X \in \mathcal{P}$ belongs to $\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ if and only if $X$ belongs to no confined subconfiguration of $\mathcal{P}$. By Lemma 11.16, a subconfiguration $\mathcal{C}$ is confined if and only if $\mathcal{C}^\alpha$ is confined. Hence $X \in \mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$ iff $X^\alpha$ belongs to no confined configuration, i.e., $X^\alpha \in \mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$. Thus $[\mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})]^\alpha \subseteq \mathcal{K}(\mathcal{P})$. Applying the same argument with $\alpha^{-1}$ yields the reverse inclusion, so equality holds.
