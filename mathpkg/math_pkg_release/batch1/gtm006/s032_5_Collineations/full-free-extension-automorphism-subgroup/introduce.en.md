Let $\mathcal{B}$ be a full free extension of a configuration $\mathcal{A}$. Then the automorphism group (group of collineations) of $\mathcal{B}$ contains a subgroup isomorphic to the automorphism group of $\mathcal{A}$. In other words, every collineation of $\mathcal{A}$ can be extended to a collineation of $\mathcal{B}$, and this extension defines an injective group homomorphism from $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{A}$ into $\operatorname{Aut} \mathcal{B}$. The lemma requires both fullness and freeness of the extension; counterexamples exist if either condition is dropped.
