A degenerate configuration is a closed configuration that fails to be a projective plane. The distinguishing criterion is the presence or absence of a quadrangle: a closed configuration containing a quadrangle is exactly a projective plane, while those lacking a quadrangle are degenerate. The known classification of which closed configurations are not projective planes is referenced in Exercise 3.1 of the text.
