A confined configuration is one in which every point lies on at least three lines and every line contains at least three points. This condition imposes a kind of finiteness or richness constraint: every element must have at least three incidences. A confined configuration necessarily has at least seven points and seven lines (Exercise 11.1). The notion of confinedness is central to the definition of open configurations and the theory of free closures.
