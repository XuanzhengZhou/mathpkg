A configuration is a set of points and lines endowed with an incidence relation satisfying the condition that any two distinct points lie on at most one common line. The dual condition -- any two distinct lines meet in at most one point -- follows automatically. Configurations are the basic building blocks from which projective planes are constructed via the process of free closure, where new elements are introduced as the meet or join of existing pairs that lack a common incident element.
