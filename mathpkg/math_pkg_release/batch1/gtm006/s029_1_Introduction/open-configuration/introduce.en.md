An open configuration is one in which no sub-configuration has the property that every element is incident with at least three others. This is the key condition that guarantees a configuration can serve as a generator for a free closure process. Open configurations are closed under arbitrary intersections (by Lemma 11.1(i)), making them well-behaved for the iterative construction that produces free closures.
