A configuration $\mathcal{A}$ is a \textbf{sub-configuration} of a configuration $\mathcal{B}$, denoted $\mathcal{A} < \mathcal{B}$, if the points and lines of $\mathcal{A}$ are all points and lines of $\mathcal{B}$ and, for any point $P$ and line $m$ of $\mathcal{A}$, $P$ is on $m$ in $\mathcal{B}$ if and only if $P$ is on $m$ in $\mathcal{A}$.
