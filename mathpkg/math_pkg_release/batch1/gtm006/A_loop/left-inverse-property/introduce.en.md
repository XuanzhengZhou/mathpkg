The left inverse property (LIP) is the left-handed analogue of the right inverse property in loop theory. A loop $G$ has LIP if each element $x$ admits a left inverse $x^{-1}$ satisfying $x^{-1}(xy) = y$ for all $y$. Together with RIP, LIP defines the full inverse property (IP). In the context of projective planes, LIP of the coordinatizing division ring corresponds to the plane being $((0,0),[0,0])$-transitive, which in the presence of RIP yields a Moufang plane.
