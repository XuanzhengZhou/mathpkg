An alternative division ring (also called an alternative field in the older literature) is a non-associative division ring that satisfies both the left and right alternative laws. These laws weaken full associativity while retaining enough algebraic structure to be useful in the theory of projective planes. Any division ring with the inverse property (IP) is alternative (Lemma 6.13 and Theorem 6.16). Conversely, a theorem of Bruck and Kleinfeld shows that finite alternative division rings are fields. The classification of alternative division rings plays a central role in understanding non-Desarguesian projective planes, particularly Moufang planes.
