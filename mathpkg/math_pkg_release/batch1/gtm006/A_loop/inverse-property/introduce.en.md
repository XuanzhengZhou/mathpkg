The inverse property (IP) for a loop is the conjunction of the right inverse property (RIP) and the left inverse property (LIP). A loop with IP is closely related to Moufang loops. In the study of projective planes, when the coordinatizing division ring $R$ has both RIP and LIP, the associated projective plane is a Moufang plane---a central result in the classification of non-Desarguesian projective planes. Division rings with IP are also known to be alternative division rings.
