Lemma 6.10 establishes that even a one-sided inverse property (either RIP or LIP alone) in a loop $G$ is sufficient to guarantee that the designated inverse $x^{-1}$ behaves as a two-sided inverse: $x^{-1}x = 1 = xx^{-1}$. Consequently, the inverse operation is an involution, i.e. $(x^{-1})^{-1} = x$. The proof exploits the unique solvability of equations in a loop: from $(yx)x^{-1}=y$, substituting $y=x^{-1}$ yields $(x^{-1}x)x^{-1}=x^{-1}$, whence $x^{-1}x=1$ by uniqueness of the solution to $bx^{-1}=x^{-1}$.
