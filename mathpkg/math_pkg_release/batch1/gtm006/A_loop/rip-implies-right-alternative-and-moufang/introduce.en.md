Lemma 6.13 is a critical algebraic result in the theory of non-associative division rings associated with projective planes. It shows that the right inverse property (RIP) alone forces a division ring to satisfy two important identities: the right alternative law $(xy)y = xy^2$ and the Moufang identity $x(yz)y = (xy)zy$. This lemma is used in the proof of the Skornyakov-San Soucie Theorem (Theorem 6.16), which states that every RIP division ring is alternative. Together with later results showing that alternative division rings have IP, this establishes the equivalence of RIP and IP for the class of division rings arising from projective planes.
