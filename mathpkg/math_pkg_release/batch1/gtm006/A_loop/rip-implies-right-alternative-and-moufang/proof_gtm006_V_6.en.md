The proof of Lemma 6.13 is given in Section 7 (the Skornyakov-San Soucie Theorem) via a series of algebraic manipulations using the associator $[a,b,c] = (ab)c - a(bc)$ and the commutator $[a,b] = ab - ba$. The strategy is to show that RIP implies $(RA)$: for all $x,y \in D$, $[x,y,y] = 0$, which is equivalent to $(xy)y = xy^2$. Then, using $(RA)$, one proves the Moufang identity $x(yz)y = (xy)zy$ through a sequence of identities involving the functions $f(d,a,b,c) = [da,b,c] - [d,ab,c] + [d,a,bc] - [d,a,c] - [d,a,b]c$ and similar expressions $g$ and $h$, showing each vanishes identically. The full detailed computation appears in Section 7 (Theorem 6.16).
