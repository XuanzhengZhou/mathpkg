Exercise 6.4 asks the reader to prove a definitive characterization of skewfields (associative division rings) in terms of the projective plane they coordinatize. The statement is that the coordinatizing ring $R$ is a skewfield precisely when the associated projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ enjoys the strongest possible transitivity property: it is $(V, l)$-transitive for every choice of point $V$ and line $l$. This exercise generalizes the results of Section 6 by showing that full associativity of multiplication corresponds to universal transitivity of the plane, making $\mathcal{P}$ a Desarguesian plane.
