The Hughes plane is a class of finite non-desarguesian projective planes of order $q^2$ for any odd prime power $q$, first discovered by Veblen and Wedderburn in the smallest case ($q = 3$, order 9). It is constructed from a nearfield $N$ of order $q^2$ whose kernel is $GF(q)$, by taking the homogeneous coordinates over $N$ and defining lines via the orbit of a particular line set under a Singer-type collineation $A$ of order $q^2+q+1$. Unlike the planes exhibited via quasifields in prior sections, the Hughes plane cannot be coordinatized by any linear planar ternary ring.
