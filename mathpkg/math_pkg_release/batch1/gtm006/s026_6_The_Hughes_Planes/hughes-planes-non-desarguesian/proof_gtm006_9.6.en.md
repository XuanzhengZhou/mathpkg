Having coordinatized the Hughes plane $\mathcal{H}$ by the method of Chapter V and having established in Lemma 9.17 that $a \oplus b = a + b$ for all $a,b \in N$, we now examine the multiplication $\otimes$ in the planar ternary ring. From the definition $a \otimes b = T(a,b,0)$ and the incidence equations (5) and (6), one can compute that for $a \notin F$, the multiplication $\otimes$ does not coincide with the multiplication in the nearfield $N$, and in particular the ternary ring is not linear.

Since a projective plane is desarguesian if and only if it can be coordinatized by a skewfield (equivalently, by a linear planar ternary ring that is a division ring), the failure of linearity implies that $\mathcal{H}$ is non-desarguesian.

Alternatively, one may argue directly: if $\mathcal{H}$ were desarguesian, its planar ternary ring would be a field, and the Hughes plane construction would yield a desarguesian plane of order $q^2$, contradicting the known structure of the collineation group and the properties of nearfields used in the construction. Therefore $\mathcal{H}$ is non-desarguesian.
