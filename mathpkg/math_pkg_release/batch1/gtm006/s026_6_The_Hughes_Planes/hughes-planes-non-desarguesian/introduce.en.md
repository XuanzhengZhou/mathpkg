Corollary 1 follows from the coordinatization of the Hughes plane and Lemma 9.17. Since the Hughes plane can be coordinatized but its associated planar ternary ring does not satisfy the linearity condition (the multiplication $\otimes$ differs from nearfield multiplication), the plane cannot be desarguesian. This makes the Hughes planes the first known examples of finite non-desarguesian projective planes whose full collineation group fixes neither a point nor a line.
