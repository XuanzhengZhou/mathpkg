Theorem 9.14 verifies that the incidence structure $\mathcal{H}$ constructed from a nearfield $N$ of order $q^2$ satisfies the axioms of a finite projective plane. The proof proceeds by counting points ($q^4 + q^2 + 1$), verifying each line contains $q^2 + 1$ points, checking the total number of lines equals $q^4 + q^2 + 1$, and then showing any two distinct lines intersect in a unique point. The counting argument uses the fact that the collineation $A$ has order $q^2+q+1$ and that $L(t)$ is a line for each admissible parameter $t$.
