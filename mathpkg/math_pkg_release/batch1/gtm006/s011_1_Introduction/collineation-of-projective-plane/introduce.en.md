A collineation is a symmetry of a projective plane -- a bijective incidence-preserving map from the plane to itself. Collineations form a group under composition, denoted $\operatorname{Aut}(\mathcal{P})$. The study of collineation groups is central to understanding the structure of projective planes: transitivity properties, fixed configurations, and the classification of planes by their automorphism groups.
