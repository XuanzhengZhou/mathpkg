A collineation $\alpha$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is called a \textit{planar collineation} (or simply a \textit{planar}) if $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$, the set of all points and lines fixed by $\alpha$, is a subplane of $\mathcal{P}$. In the special case where $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is a Baer subplane, $\alpha$ is called a \textit{Baer collineation}.
