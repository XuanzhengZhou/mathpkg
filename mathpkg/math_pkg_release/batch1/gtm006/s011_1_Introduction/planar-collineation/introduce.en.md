A planar collineation is a collineation $\alpha$ whose fixed point-line structure $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ forms a subplane of the ambient projective plane $\mathcal{P}$. When $\mathcal{F}(\alpha)$ is specifically a Baer subplane (a subplane of index 2), the collineation is called a Baer collineation. Planar collineations are distinguished from perspectivities, which fix a line pointwise and a point linewise.
