A \textit{collineation} (or \textit{automorphism}) of a projective, or affine, plane is an isomorphism of the plane onto itself. Under the usual definition of products of mappings, the set of all collineations of a given plane $\mathcal{B}$ forms a group. This group, denoted by $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$, is called the \textit{full collineation group} of $\mathcal{B}$. A \textit{collineation group} of $\mathcal{B}$ is a subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}\mathcal{B}$. The identity collineation is denoted by $1$.
