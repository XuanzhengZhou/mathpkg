A correlation is an incidence-preserving bijection that swaps points and lines. It is the geometric realization of duality: a correlation maps each point to a line (its polar) and each line to a point (its pole). When a correlation is an involution ($\theta^2 = 1$), it is called a polarity. The composition of two correlations is always a collineation, so correlations extend the collineation group to a larger structure.
