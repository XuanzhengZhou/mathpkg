A correlation $	heta$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ is a one-to-one mapping of the points of $\mathcal{P}$ onto the lines of $\mathcal{P}$ and the lines of $\mathcal{P}$ onto the points of $\mathcal{P}$ that preserves incidence. The product of any two correlations is a collineation. A correlation $	heta$ with $	heta^2 = 1$ is called a polarity.
