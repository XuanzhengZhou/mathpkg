A polarity is a special type of correlation $\theta$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ for which $\theta^2 = 1$, the identity collineation. In other words, applying the correlation twice returns every point and line to itself. Polarities are fundamental to the study of projective planes and are discussed extensively in Chapter XII. They give rise to geometric concepts such as conics and unitals.
