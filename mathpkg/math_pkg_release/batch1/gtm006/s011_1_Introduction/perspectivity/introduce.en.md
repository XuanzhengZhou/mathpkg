A perspectivity (also called a central collineation) is a collineation $\alpha$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ that fixes a line $l$ pointwise (the axis) and a point $V$ linewise (the centre). The dual formulation also holds: $\alpha$ fixes $l$ pointwise and $V$ linewise. Perspectivities come in two types: elations (when $V \in l$) and homologies (when $V \notin l$), with the identity treated as both. Every perspectivity is a quasiperspectivity, and every non-planar quasiperspectivity is a perspectivity.
