A \textit{$(V,l)$-perspectivity} (or \textit{$(V,l)$-central collineation}) is a collineation $\alpha$ of a projective plane $\mathcal{P}$ that fixes a line $l$ pointwise and a point $V$ linewise. The point $V$ is called the \textit{centre} of $\alpha$ and $l$ is its \textit{axis}. Two possibilities arise:
\begin{itemize}
\item If $V \in l$, $\alpha$ is called an \textit{elation}.
\item If $V \notin l$, $\alpha$ is called a \textit{homology}.
\end{itemize}
For convenience, the identity is regarded as both a homology and an elation. Any perspectivity is also a quasiperspectivity; any non-planar quasiperspectivity is a perspectivity.
