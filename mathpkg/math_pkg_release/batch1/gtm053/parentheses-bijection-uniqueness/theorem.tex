Given $\Pi^+$ and $\Pi^-$ (the sets of indices of left and right parentheses in an expression), if a parentheses bijection $c: \Pi^+ \to \Pi^-$ exists, then it is unique. Moreover, $c(i)$ is the least $l > i$ for which $\sum_{j=i}^{l} \varepsilon(j) = 0$, where $\varepsilon$ takes the value $1$ on $\Pi^+$, $-1$ on $\Pi^-$, and $0$ elsewhere.
