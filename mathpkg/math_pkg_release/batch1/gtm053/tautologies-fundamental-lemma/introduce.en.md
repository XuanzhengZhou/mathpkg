The Fundamental Lemma for tautologies is the core technical step in proving the completeness of the propositional calculus. It states that from the basis tautologies and the truth-value assignment to atomic components, one can deduce the formula itself (if it evaluates to true) or its negation (if it evaluates to false). The operation $P \mapsto P^v$ forces any formula to be v-true, enabling an inductive proof over the logical structure.
