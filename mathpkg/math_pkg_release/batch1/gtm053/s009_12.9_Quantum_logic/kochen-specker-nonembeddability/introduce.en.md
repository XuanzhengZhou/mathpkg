This theorem, a version of the Kochen–Specker theorem, states that for a Hilbert space of dimension at least 3, the partial Boolean algebra $B(\mathcal{H})$ of closed subspaces cannot be embedded into any Boolean algebra while preserving the algebraic operations. This is a rigorous "no hidden variables" result: it shows that it is impossible to assign definite truth values (0 or 1) to all quantum-mechanical propositions in a way consistent with the logical structure of quantum mechanics. The proof reduces the problem to a graph-coloring argument on the sphere $S^2$, specifically to the non-existence of a certain homomorphism onto the two-element Boolean algebra $\{0, 1\}$.
