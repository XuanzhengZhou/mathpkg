Let $\mathcal{H}$ be a Hilbert space. Define $B(\mathcal{H})$ to be the set of all closed subspaces of $\mathcal{H}$, equipped with the following structure:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] For $a, b \in B(\mathcal{H})$, set $a * b$ if and only if there exist three pairwise orthogonal closed subspaces $c, d, e \in B(\mathcal{H})$ such that $a = c \oplus d$ and $b = e \oplus d$. This condition is equivalent to commutativity of the orthogonal projections onto $a$ and $b$.
\item[(b)] $a \wedge b = a \cap b$ (the intersection of $a$ and $b$).
\item[(c)] $a \vee b = a + b$ (the sum, i.e., the closed linear span of $a$ and $b$).
\item[(d)] $a' = a^{\perp}$ (the orthogonal complement of $a$).
\item[(e)] $0 = \{0\}$ and $1 = \mathcal{H}$.
\end{enumerate}

With these definitions, $B(\mathcal{H})$ is a partial Boolean algebra.
