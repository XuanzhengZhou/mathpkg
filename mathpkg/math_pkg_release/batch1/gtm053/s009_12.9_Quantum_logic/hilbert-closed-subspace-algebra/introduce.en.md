$B(\mathcal{H})$ is the fundamental example of a partial Boolean algebra, arising directly from the formalism of quantum mechanics. The elements are the closed subspaces of a Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$, which correspond to yes/no questions about the state of a quantum system. Two subspaces $a$ and $b$ are compatible ($a * b$) precisely when the corresponding projection operators commute, which is equivalent to the existence of a decomposition into pairwise orthogonal pieces $c, d, e$. The operations $\wedge$, $\vee$, and $'$ correspond respectively to intersection, closed linear sum, and orthogonal complement. This structure replaces the Boolean algebra of classical logic in the quantum context.
