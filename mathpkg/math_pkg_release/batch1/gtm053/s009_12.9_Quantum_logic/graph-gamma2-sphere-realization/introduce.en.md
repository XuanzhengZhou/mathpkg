Lemma 12.14 constructs a specific finite graph $\Gamma_2$ that can be embedded on the unit sphere $S^2$ such that certain triples of vertices correspond to mutually orthogonal lines through the origin. This graph is obtained from a planar diagram (Figure 1 in the text) by identifying three special vertices $a = p_0$, $b = q_0$, and $c = r_0$. The realizability of this graph on $S^2$ is the crucial combinatorial-geometric input to the Kochen–Specker proof, because the graph encodes orthogonality constraints that cannot be simultaneously satisfied by a $\{0, 1\}$-valued coloring.
