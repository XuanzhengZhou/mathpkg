**Proof.** For $0 \leqslant k \leqslant 4$ set

$$p_k \mapsto \cos \frac{\pi k}{10} \cdot \bar{x} + \sin \frac{\pi k}{10} \cdot \bar{y},$$

$$q_k \mapsto \cos \frac{\pi k}{10} \cdot \bar{x} - \sin \frac{\pi k}{10} \cdot \bar{y},$$

$$r_k \mapsto \cos \frac{\pi k}{10} \cdot \bar{y} + \sin \frac{\pi k}{10} \cdot \bar{z}.$$

These assignments map the vertices of $\Gamma_2$ to points on the unit sphere $S^2$ (represented by the unit vectors $\bar{x}, \bar{y}, \bar{z}$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$) in such a way that the orthogonality relations encoded by the edges of $\Gamma_2$ are satisfied. The identification $a = p_0$, $b = q_0$, $c = r_0$ is respected by this mapping. Hence $\Gamma_2$ is realized on $S^2$. $\square$
