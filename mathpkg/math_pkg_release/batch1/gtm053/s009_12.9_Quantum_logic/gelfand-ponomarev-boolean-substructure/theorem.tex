Let $L$ be a modular structure and let $n \geqslant 1$. For an admissible sequence $\alpha = (i_1, \ldots, i_l)$ of elements of $I = \{1, \ldots, n\}$ (where admissibility means $i_j \neq i_{j+1}$ for all $j$), and a sequence $\beta = (k_1, \ldots, k_{l-1})$ of elements of $I$ subordinate to $\alpha$ (i.e., $k_j \notin \{i_j, i_{j+1}\}$ for all $j \leqslant l-1$), inductively define:

$$a_\alpha = a_{i_1} \cdots a_{i_l} = a_{i_1} \wedge \left(\bigvee_\beta a_\beta\right),$$

where $\beta$ runs through all sequences subordinate to $\alpha$. For $t \in \{1, \ldots, n\}$ define

$$A_t(l) = \bigvee_\alpha a_\alpha,$$

where $\alpha$ runs through all admissible sequences of length $l$ with last entry $t$. Finally, set

$$H_t(l) = \bigvee_{j \neq t} A_j(l).$$

The substructure in $L^n$ generated by the elements $H_1(l), \ldots, H_n(l)$ is a Boolean algebra consisting of $2^n$ elements, for all $l \geqslant 1$. Moreover, this substructure consists entirely of modular questions.
