This proposition provides an explicit counterexample showing that classical logical tautologies need not hold in the quantum-logical framework. Kochen and Specker constructed a logical polynomial $R$ in 117 variables that evaluates to "true" ($1$) under every Boolean valuation, yet admits an assignment in the partial Boolean algebra $B(E^3)$ (closed subspaces of Euclidean 3-space) that makes it evaluate to "false" ($0$). The construction uses the graph $\Gamma_2$ from Lemma 12.14, whose 117 vertices correspond to lines in $E^3$. The polynomial $R$ encodes the orthogonality constraints of $\Gamma_2$: it asserts that it is impossible for every orthogonal triple to contain exactly one "true" line while every orthogonal pair has at least one "false" line. This is simply another facet of the impossibility of embedding $B(E^3)$ into a Boolean algebra.
