A partial Boolean algebra generalizes the notion of a Boolean algebra by making the operations of conjunction ($\wedge$) and disjunction ($\vee$) partial rather than total. Two elements are declared compatible (denoted $a * b$) precisely when they are "simultaneously measurable" in the quantum-mechanical sense. The operations $\wedge$ and $\vee$ are defined only on compatible pairs. The key axiom is that every set of pairwise compatible elements can be embedded in a Boolean subalgebra. This structure provides the algebraic foundation for quantum logic, where the lattice of closed subspaces of a Hilbert space forms the primary example.
