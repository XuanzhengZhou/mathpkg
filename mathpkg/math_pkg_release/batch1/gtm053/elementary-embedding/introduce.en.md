An elementary embedding preserves not just the atomic structure but the truth of all first-order formulas. Elementary substructures and extensions are the fundamental building blocks of model theory. Two structures are elementarily equivalent if they satisfy exactly the same first-order sentences; this is a much coarser equivalence than isomorphism but captures all properties expressible in the formal language.
