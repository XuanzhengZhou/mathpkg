The notion of definability captures which subsets of the domain $M$ (or its Cartesian powers) can be characterized by a formula of the language under a given interpretation. The study of definable sets is central to model theory, as it connects syntactic properties of formulas with semantic properties of structures. Important problems include understanding the structure of definable sets and the limitations on what can be defined in a given language.
