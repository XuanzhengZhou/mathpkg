The theory of real closed fields axiomatizes the first-order properties of the real numbers as an ordered field. Real closed fields include the real numbers, real algebraic numbers, and nonstandard models. Tarski and Seidenberg proved that RCF is complete and admits quantifier elimination, which implies that the theory of the real field is decidable.
