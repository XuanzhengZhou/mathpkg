By the Hrushovski-Pillay theorem, any definable subset of $\Delta^n$ is a finite union of cosets of definable subgroups. Taking $A = \Delta^n$ as the ambient commutative algebraic group and considering any algebraic subvariety $V \subseteq A$, the intersection $\Gamma \cap V(K)$ with a subgroup $\Gamma \subseteq \Delta^n$ is a definable subset and therefore a finite union of cosets of subgroups of the form $\Gamma \cap B(K)$ where $B$ ranges over the definable (hence algebraic) subgroups of $\Delta^n$. This is precisely the Lang property for $\Delta^n$, and it descends to any subgroup $\Gamma \subseteq \Delta^n$.
