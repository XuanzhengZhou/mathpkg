The Lang property is a model-theoretic geometric concept introduced in the context of Diophantine geometry. For a subgroup $\Gamma$ of a commutative algebraic group $A(K)$ over a field $K$, the Lang property requires that the intersection of $\Gamma$ with any algebraic subvariety $V \subseteq A$ decomposes into finitely many cosets of subgroups of the form $\Gamma \cap B(K)$, where $B$ ranges over algebraic subgroups of $A$. This property connects the arithmetic of finitely generated subgroups (as in Faltings' theorem) with the model-theoretic notion of one-basedness.
