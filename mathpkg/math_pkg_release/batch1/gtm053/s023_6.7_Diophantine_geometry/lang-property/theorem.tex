Let $K$ be a field and let $\Gamma$ be a subgroup of a commutative algebraic group $A(K)$. We say that $\Gamma$ has the \textbf{Lang property} if for every algebraic variety $V \subseteq A$, the intersection $\Gamma \cap V(K)$ is a union of finitely many cosets of subgroups of the form $\Gamma \cap B(K)$, where $B$ is an algebraic subgroup of $A$.
