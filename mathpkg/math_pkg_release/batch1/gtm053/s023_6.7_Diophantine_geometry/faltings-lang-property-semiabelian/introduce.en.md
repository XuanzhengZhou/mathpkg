Faltings' theorem (the Mordell-Lang conjecture for function fields in characteristic zero) asserts that any finitely generated subgroup of a semiabelian variety over a field of characteristic zero satisfies the Lang property. This landmark result in arithmetic geometry is equivalent, via work of Pillay, to the superstable, one-based nature of the theory of algebraically closed fields expanded by a predicate for the subgroup, demonstrating a deep connection between Diophantine geometry and model-theoretic stability theory.
