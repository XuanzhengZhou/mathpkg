The theorem follows from the fact that $\Delta$ satisfies condition (ii) of Section 6.4 (local projectivity). Under this hypothesis, the strongly minimal group $\Delta$ is one-based, meaning that the model-theoretic independence relation is governed by a modular pregeometry. In a one-based strongly minimal group, every definable relation is a Boolean combination of cosets of definable subgroups. Specializing to the case of definable subsets of $\Delta^n$ and using elimination of imaginaries for $\omega$-stable groups, one obtains that each definable subset is in fact a finite union of cosets of definable subgroups. A more general version of this theorem was proved by Hrushovski and Pillay.
