The Hrushovski-Pillay coset decomposition theorem states that when a strongly minimal group $\Delta$ definable in an expansion of a field is locally projective (condition (ii) of the trichotomy classification), every definable subset of $\Delta^n$ decomposes as a finite union of cosets of definable subgroups. This structural result is a powerful consequence of local modularity in geometric stability theory and forms the bridge between the trichotomy of strongly minimal sets and Diophantine geometric properties such as the Lang property.
