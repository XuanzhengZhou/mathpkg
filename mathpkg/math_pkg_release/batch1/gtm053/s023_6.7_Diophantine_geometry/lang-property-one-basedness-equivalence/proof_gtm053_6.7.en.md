The forward direction (local projectivity implies the Lang property) follows from the Hrushovski-Pillay theorem: if $\Gamma \subseteq \Delta^n$ and $\Delta$ is one-based, then definable subsets of $\Delta^n$ are finite unions of cosets of definable subgroups, which yields the Lang property for $\Delta^n$ and hence for $\Gamma$.

For the converse, assume $\Gamma$ has the Lang property. Then the definable structure on $\Gamma$ is governed by coset decompositions, which forces the underlying pregeometry to be modular after localization. This implies that the strongly minimal set underlying $\Gamma$ satisfies a generalized version of condition (ii), i.e., one-basedness. The equivalence shows that the Lang property is a faithful Diophantine-geometric translation of the model-theoretic condition of one-basedness.
