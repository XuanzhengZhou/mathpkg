Pillay's proof establishes a two-way equivalence using the machinery of geometric stability theory. In one direction, the superstability and one-basedness of $\operatorname{Th}(K, \Gamma)$ imply, via the equivalence between one-basedness and the Lang property, that $\Gamma$ has the Lang property, recovering Faltings' theorem. In the other direction, Faltings' theorem asserting the Lang property for $\Gamma$ implies, by the characterization of the Lang property in terms of one-basedness, that the definable structure on $\Gamma$ is governed by a modular pregeometry. This modularity, together with the algebraically closed field structure on $K$, yields superstability of the expanded theory $\operatorname{Th}(K, \Gamma)$ with one-based geometry for $\Gamma$.
