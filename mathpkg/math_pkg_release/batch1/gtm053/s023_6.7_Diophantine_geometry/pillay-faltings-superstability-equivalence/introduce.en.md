A. Pillay established that Faltings' theorem in Diophantine geometry has a precise model-theoretic reformulation: it is equivalent to the statement that the theory of an algebraically closed field expanded by a predicate naming a finitely generated subgroup of a semiabelian variety is superstable with one-based geometry. This result bridges arithmetic geometry and geometric stability theory, showing that the Lang property is fundamentally a manifestation of superstability and one-basedness in the expanded language.
