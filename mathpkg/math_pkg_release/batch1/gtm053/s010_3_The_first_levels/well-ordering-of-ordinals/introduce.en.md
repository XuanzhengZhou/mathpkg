This fundamental theorem establishes that the class $\mathrm{On}$ of all ordinals is itself well-ordered by the membership relation $\in$. Consequently, every well-ordered set is isomorphic to a unique ordinal, making ordinals the canonical representatives of well-ordering isomorphism types. The theorem also shows that any well-ordered set is isomorphic to the initial segment of ordinals below $\alpha \cup \{\alpha\}$, where $\alpha$ is the corresponding ordinal.
