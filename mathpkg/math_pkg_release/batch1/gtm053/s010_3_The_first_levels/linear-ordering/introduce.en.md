A linear ordering (also called a total ordering) is a partial ordering that additionally satisfies trichotomy: for any two distinct elements, one is strictly less than the other. This property ensures that every pair of elements in $X$ is comparable under the relation $<$. Linear orderings are a special case of partial orderings and a prerequisite for well-orderings.
