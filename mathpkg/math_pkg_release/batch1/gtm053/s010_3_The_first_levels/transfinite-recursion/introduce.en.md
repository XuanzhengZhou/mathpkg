Transfinite recursion is the method for defining a function $F$ on the entire class of ordinals by specifying $F(\alpha)$ in terms of the values of $F$ on elements of $\alpha$ (i.e., on all smaller ordinals). Given any set-valued function $G$, there exists a unique $F$ satisfying $F(\alpha) = G(F \restriction \alpha)$. This principle is used to construct the von Neumann universe, define ordinal arithmetic, and carry out countless transfinite constructions in set theory.
