A limit ordinal is a nonzero ordinal that is not a successor. The first limit ordinal $\omega_0$ represents the order type of the natural numbers. Limit ordinals are characterized by the property $\alpha = \bigcup_{\beta < \alpha} \beta$: they are precisely the ordinals that equal the union of all smaller ordinals. This property is essential for extending constructions transfinitely.
