This fundamental lemma states that any two well-ordered sets are comparable via isomorphism: either they are isomorphic, or one is isomorphic to an initial segment of the other. Moreover, the isomorphism is unique in each case. This trichotomy is the basis for the well-ordering of the class of ordinals and justifies the definition of ordinals as canonical representatives of well-ordering isomorphism types.
