Transfinite induction extends ordinary mathematical induction to the class of all ordinals. To prove a property holds for every ordinal, it suffices to verify three cases: the base case ($\varnothing$), the successor step (if $\alpha$ has the property, so does $\alpha+1$), and the limit step (if all ordinals in a set have the property, then their supremum does). In practice, the $\varnothing$ and limit cases are often trivial and may be omitted.
