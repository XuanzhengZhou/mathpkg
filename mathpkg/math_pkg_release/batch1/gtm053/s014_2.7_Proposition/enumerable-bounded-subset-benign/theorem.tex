Let $G' = |\{a_1, \ldots, a_n\}|$, $n \geqslant 1$, be the group freely generated by the $a_i$. A subset $R' \subset G'$ is called bounded if there exists an $r > 1$ such that any element in $R'$ can be represented in the form $a_{i_1}^{x_1} \cdots a_{i_r}^{x_r}, x_i \in \mathbf{Z}$. If the subgroup $H' \subset G'$ is generated by a bounded enumerable subset $R' \subset G'$, then it is benign.
