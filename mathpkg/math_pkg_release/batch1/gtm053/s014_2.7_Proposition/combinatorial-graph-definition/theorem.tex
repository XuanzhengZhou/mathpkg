A combinatorial graph, or simply graph, $\tau$ is a quadruple $(F_{\tau}, V_{\tau}, \partial_{\tau}, j_{\tau})$, where $F_{\tau}, V_{\tau}$ are finite sets, and $(\partial_{\tau}, j_{\tau})$ are maps. Elements of $F_{\tau}$ are called flags, elements of $V_{\tau}$ are called vertices; vertices and flags are disjoint. The map $\partial_{\tau} : F_{\tau} \rightarrow V_{\tau}$ associates to each flag a vertex, its boundary. The map $j_{\tau} : F_{\tau} \rightarrow F_{\tau}$ is an involution: $j_{\tau}^2 = \text{id}$.

If $V_{\tau}$ is empty, $F_{\tau}$ must be empty as well (empty graph). $F_{\tau}$ may be empty whereas $V_{\tau}$ is not (graph with vertices but no flags).
