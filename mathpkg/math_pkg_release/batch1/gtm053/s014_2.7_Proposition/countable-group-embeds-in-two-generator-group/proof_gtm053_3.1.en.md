(a) The group $\mathbb{Z} * \mathbb{Z} = \langle b \rangle * \langle v \rangle$ has a free subgroup of countable rank:
$$S = \left\langle \{b^{-i}vb^i \mid i \geqslant 0\} \right\rangle.$$
It follows from Proposition 2.2 that there are no relations between the generators $b^{-i}vb^i$.

If $G$ is a free countable group, it embeds directly in $\mathbb{Z} * \mathbb{Z}$. If $G$ is not free, represent $G = F/N$ where $F$ is countable and free. Embed $F$ in $\mathbb{Z} * \mathbb{Z}$ and consider the induced homomorphism $F/N \rightarrow \mathbb{Z} * \mathbb{Z}/N'$, where $N'$ is the normal subgroup in $\mathbb{Z} * \mathbb{Z}$ generated by $N$. However, $N' \cap F$ may be strictly larger than $N$, so this homomorphism need not be an embedding.

A more sophisticated construction is used. One constructs a group $P$ having two rank-2 free subgroups $\langle b, v \rangle$ and $\langle u, b \rangle$ with no relations between $u$ and $b$. Then form the HNN-extension:
$$Q = \langle P \cup \{a\}: a^{-1}ba = u, a^{-1}va = b \rangle.$$
One then verifies that $Q$ is generated by $a$ and $b$ alone. The generators $g_i, t_i, u, v$ can all be eliminated: $g_i = u^{-1}t_i^{-1}ut_i$, $t_i = b^{-i}vb^i$, $u = a^{-1}ba$, $v = aba^{-1}$. Expressing $g_i$ in terms of $a, b$ yields
$$e_i = a^{-1}b^{-1}ab^{-i}ab^{-1}a^{-1}b^i a^{-1}bab^{-i}aba^{-1}b^i.$$
The subgroup $E = \langle \{e_i \mid i \geqslant 1\} \rangle$ in $\langle a \rangle * \langle b \rangle$ has the property that any normal subgroup $N \subset E$ generates a normal subgroup $N'$ in $\langle a \rangle * \langle b \rangle$ such that $E \cap N' = N$. This completes the proof of part (a). Part (b) follows by analyzing the constructivity of this embedding.
