(a) Define $H'' = |\{\tau(h)c^n de^n \mid \text{image of } h \in H, n \in \mathbf{Z}^+, h = \gamma(n)\}| \subset H'$. Then $H'' = H' \cap |\{ a, b, t, v, c^n de^n \mid n \in \gamma^{-1}(H) \}|$. The inclusion $\subset$ is obvious; the converse follows because the set of images of $c^n de^n$ ($n \geqslant 1$) in the quotient of $G'$ by the kernel generated by $a, b, t, v$ is free. Hence in any reduced word in $\tau(g)c^n de^n$, the sequence of $n$'s can be uniquely recovered from the word, and if all $n$'s lie in $\gamma^{-1}(H)$, the word lies in $H''$.

Thus $H''$ is the intersection of $H'$ with a subgroup generated by a bounded enumerable set of generators (since $\gamma^{-1}(H)$ is enumerable whenever $H$ is). Consequently $H''$ is benign if $H'$ is benign.

(b) Set $\overline{H} = |\{ \tau(h) \mid h \in H \}| \subset G'$. Then $|\overline{H} \cup \{c, d, e\}| = |H'' \cup \{c, d, e\}|$, hence $\overline{H} = |H'' \cup \{c, d, e\}| \cap |\{a, b, v, t\}|$. Since $H''$ is benign, the subgroup generated by $H'' \cup \{c, d, e\}$ is benign, and its intersection with the finitely generated subgroup $|\{a, b, v, t\}|$ remains benign. Furthermore $\overline{H}$ is conjugate to $H$, so $H$ is benign as well.
