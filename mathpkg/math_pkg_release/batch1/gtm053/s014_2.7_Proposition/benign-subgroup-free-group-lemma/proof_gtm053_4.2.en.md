Let $i$ be an embedding of $F *_R F$ in a finitely presented group $K$ (see 4.1(c)), and let $\phi_1, \phi_2 : F \rightarrow F *_R F$ be the structure maps. Consider two embeddings of $F$ in $K \times F / \overline{R}$:

$$\alpha : f \mapsto \langle i \circ \phi_1(f), f \overline{R} \rangle;$$
$$\beta : f \mapsto \langle i \circ \phi_2(f), 1 \rangle.$$

Construct the HNN-extension with stable letter $t$ conjugating $\alpha(F)$ to $\beta(F)$. The group is presented with relations:
(a) the relations in $K$ and $F/\overline{R}$;
(b) $t^{-1}\langle i \circ \phi_1(f), 1 \rangle t = \langle i \circ \phi_2(f), 1 \rangle$;
(c) $t^{-1}\langle i \circ \phi_1(f), 1 \rangle t = \langle i \circ \phi_1(f), f \rangle$, $t^{-1}\langle i \circ \phi_2(f), 1 \rangle t = \langle i \circ \phi_2(f), 1 \rangle$, where $f$ runs through generators of $F$;
(d) the relations in $R$ between generators of $F$.

Take $R_0 = (a) \cup (b) \cup (c)$ as a finite system. Verify that (d) follows from $R_0$. Let $R' \subset F$ be the normal subgroup generated by $R_0$, i.e., the kernel of $F \rightarrow |K \cup F \cup \{t\}: R_0|$. The inclusion $R' \subset \overline{R}$ is obvious. For the converse: if $f \in F$, set $f' = f \bmod R'$ and $f_{1,2} = i \circ \phi_{1,2}(f) \in K$. From relations (b) and (c) in $K \times F/R'$:

$$t^{-1}\langle f_1, 1 \rangle t = \langle f_1, 1 \rangle \langle 1, f' \rangle \quad \text{and} \quad t^{-1}\langle f_2, 1 \rangle t = \langle f_2, 1 \rangle.$$

If $f \in R$, then since $F *_R F$ is embedded in $K$, $f_1 = f_2$ by (a), hence $f' = 1$, so $R \subset R'$. Thus $R' = \overline{R}$ and the result is finitely presented.
