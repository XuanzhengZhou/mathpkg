A Boolean circuit $S$ of length $L$ in the basis $B$ can be processed into a reversible Boolean circuit $\tilde{S}$ of length $O((L+m+n)^2)$ calculating a permutation $H : \mathbf{F}_2^{m+n+L} \rightarrow \mathbf{F}_2^{m+n+L}$ with the property:
$$H(x,y,0) = (x,F(x)+y,0) = (\tilde{F}(x,y),0),$$
where $x,y,z$ have sizes $m,n,L$ respectively.
