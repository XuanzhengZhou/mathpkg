This proposition lists the formal axioms of first-order arithmetic $\mathrm{L}_1\mathrm{Ar}$, which are true in the standard interpretation (the natural numbers). The axioms include equality axioms, the usual commutative semiring axioms for addition and multiplication, distributivity, and the induction axiom schema.

The induction axiom schema asserts that for any formula $P$ with one free variable, if $P(0)$ holds and $P(x)$ implies $P(x+1)$ for all $x$, then $P(x)$ holds for all $x$. Importantly, this is a first-order schema — there is one axiom for each definable property, rather than a single second-order axiom quantifying over all subsets of the natural numbers. Since there are only countably many formulas, the formal induction axiom is weaker than the intuitive (second-order) induction principle.
