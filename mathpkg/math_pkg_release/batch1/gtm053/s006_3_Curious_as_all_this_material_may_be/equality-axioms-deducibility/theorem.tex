\textbf{(a)} The formulas
$$t = t; \quad t_1 = t_2 \Rightarrow t_2 = t_1; \quad t_1 = t_2 \wedge t_2 = t_3 \Rightarrow t_1 = t_3;$$
$$x = t \Rightarrow (P(x, x) \Rightarrow P(x, t))$$
are $\phi$-true for any interpretation $\phi$ in which $\phi(=)$ is equality.

\textbf{(b)} The formulas $t = t$ and $t_1 = t_2 \Leftrightarrow t_2 = t_1$ are deducible from the axioms of equality. Moreover, $t_1 = t_2 \wedge t_2 = t_3 \Rightarrow t_1 = t_3$ and the substitution schema $x = t \Rightarrow (P(x, x) \Rightarrow P(x, t))$ are deducible from the axioms of equality together with the logical axioms.
