A structure $\mathbf{A}$ is called saturated (without qualification) when it is $\kappa$-saturated with $\kappa = \text{card}(A)$. In other words, $\mathbf{A}$ realizes every $1$-type over any parameter set of cardinality strictly smaller than the cardinality of $\mathbf{A}$ itself. This is the maximal possible degree of saturation for a structure of its size.
