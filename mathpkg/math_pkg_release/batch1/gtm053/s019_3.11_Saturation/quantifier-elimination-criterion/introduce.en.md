This theorem gives a semantic criterion for quantifier elimination in a complete theory $T$: it suffices to check, in some saturated model $\mathbf{A}$ of $T$, that equality of quantifier-free types implies the existence of an automorphism sending one tuple to the other. In other words, the quantifier-free type is a complete invariant for the automorphism orbit, which forces every definable relation to be quantifier-free definable. This is a powerful tool for proving quantifier elimination in concrete theories.
