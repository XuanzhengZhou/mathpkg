A back-and-forth system between two $L$-structures $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ is a nonempty family $I$ of partial isomorphisms between finitely generated substructures. The key requirements are: (forth) any partial isomorphism can be extended to include any prescribed element of $\mathbf{A}$ in its domain, and (back) any partial isomorphism can be extended to include any prescribed element of $\mathbf{B}$ in its range. Back-and-forth systems are the fundamental combinatorial tool for proving elementary equivalence and isomorphism between saturated models.
