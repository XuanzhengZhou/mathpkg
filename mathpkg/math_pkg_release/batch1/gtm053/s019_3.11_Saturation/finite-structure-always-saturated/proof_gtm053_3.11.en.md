This is immediate from the definition. Let $\mathbf{A}$ be a finite structure and $\kappa$ any infinite cardinal. For any $\lambda < \kappa$ and any expansion $\mathbf{A}_C$ by constants $C = \{c_i : i \leq \lambda\}$, the number of distinct constant symbols in $C$ is $\lambda$. Since $\mathbf{A}$ is finite and $\lambda$ is at most the cardinality of $\mathbf{A}$ (otherwise the expansion cannot be formed with distinct constants interpreted in $\mathbf{A}$), the condition is either vacuous or trivially satisfied because there are only finitely many $1$-types over any expansion of a finite structure, and all are realized.
