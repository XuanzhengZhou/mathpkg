A finite structure is vacuously $\kappa$-saturated for every infinite cardinal $\kappa$. This is because the condition requires the structure to realize types over parameter sets of cardinality $\lambda < \kappa$, but a finite structure cannot have a set of $\lambda$ distinct constant symbols when $\lambda$ exceeds the size of the structure. The remark clarifies that saturation is primarily interesting for infinite structures.
