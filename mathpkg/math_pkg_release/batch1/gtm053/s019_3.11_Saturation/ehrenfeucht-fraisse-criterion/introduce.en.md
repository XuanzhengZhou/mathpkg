The Ehrenfeucht–Fraïssé criterion states that for $\aleph_0$-saturated structures, elementary equivalence is precisely characterized by the existence of a back-and-forth system. This theorem connects the semantic notion of elementary equivalence with the combinatorial notion of partial isomorphism extension. It is a cornerstone of model theory, enabling geometric and algebraic arguments to replace syntactic ones.
