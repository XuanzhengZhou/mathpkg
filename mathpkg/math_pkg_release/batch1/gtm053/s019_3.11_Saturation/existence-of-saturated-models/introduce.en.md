For any complete theory $T$, saturated models exist in all sufficiently large cardinalities (at least the cardinality of $T$) and are unique up to isomorphism when they have the same size. Part (i) guarantees the existence of $\kappa$-saturated models of size at most $\kappa^+$, while part (ii) asserts that saturated models serve as canonical models for $T$: any two saturated models of the same cardinality are isomorphic. The proof of (i) uses a standard tower construction, and (ii) uses a back-and-forth argument.
