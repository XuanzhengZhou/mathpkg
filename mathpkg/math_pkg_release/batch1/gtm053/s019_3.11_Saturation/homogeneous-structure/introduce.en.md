A structure $\mathbf{A}$ is homogeneous if every partial elementary map defined on a subset of size smaller than $|A|$ can be extended to a full automorphism. This property expresses that the structure is maximally symmetric: any two tuples that look the same (have the same type) can be mapped to each other by a global symmetry. Saturated models are homogeneous, making homogeneity an important consequence of saturation.
