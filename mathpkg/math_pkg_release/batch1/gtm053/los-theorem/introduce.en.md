Los's theorem is the fundamental theorem of ultraproducts. It states that truth in an ultraproduct is equivalent to truth in "almost all" factor structures (where "almost all" is measured by the ultrafilter). The theorem is proved by induction on formula complexity and relies on the ultrafilter properties, particularly the fact that the intersection of two sets in the ultrafilter is again in the ultrafilter.
