The Continuum Hypothesis asserts that there is no cardinality strictly between countable and continuum. It was the first problem on Hilbert's famous 1900 list. Cohen's 1963 proof that CH is independent of ZFC (along with Godel's 1938 proof of its relative consistency) earned Cohen the Fields Medal and is one of the crowning achievements of mathematical logic.
