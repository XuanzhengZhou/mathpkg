This proposition formalizes the intuitive idea that the truth of a formula depends only on the values assigned to its free variables. If two assignments $\xi$ and $\xi'$ agree on all free variables of $P$, then $P$ has the same truth value under both assignments. The proof proceeds by induction on the structure of $P$, first establishing the analogous fact for terms, then extending through atomic formulas, connectives, and quantifiers.
