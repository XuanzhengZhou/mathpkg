Closed formulas are formulas in which all variable occurrences are bound by quantifiers. They correspond to assertions that are completely determined in terms of truth or falsity, independent of how free variables are interpreted. In any interpretation, a closed formula has a well-defined truth value. Non-closed formulas, by contrast, may be true or false depending on the values assigned to their free variables.
