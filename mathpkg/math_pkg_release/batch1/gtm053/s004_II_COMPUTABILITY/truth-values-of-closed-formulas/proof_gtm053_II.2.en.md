A closed formula $P$ has no free variables. For any $\xi, \xi' \in \overline{M}$, the condition that $x^\xi = x^{\xi'}$ for all free variables $x$ of $P$ is vacuously satisfied (since there are none). By Proposition 2.10, $|P|_\phi(\xi) = |P|_\phi(\xi')$. Hence $|P|_\phi(\xi)$ is independent of $\xi$, and the truth value of $P$ is well-defined.
