The rules of deduction MP (modus ponens) and Gen (generalization) are the inference rules of first-order logic. MP captures the argument form "If $P$ is true and $P$ implies $Q$, then $Q$ is true." Gen captures the mathematical practice of stating universal truths: when we prove $P$ for an arbitrary $x$, we may conclude $\forall x P$. The set $T_\phi L$ of $\phi$-true formulas is closed under both rules: if the premises are true in an interpretation, so are the conclusions. These rules, together with logical axioms, define the notion of formal deducibility.
