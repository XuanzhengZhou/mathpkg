Every expression in a language $L$ of class $\mathfrak{L}_1$ is either a term, or a formula, or neither. These alternatives, as well as all alternatives for the form of a term (variable, constant, $f(t_1,\ldots,t_r)$) or formula (atomic, compound with a connective, quantified), are mutually exclusive. Every term (resp. formula) can be represented in exactly one of the admissible forms, and in a unique way. Moreover, if an expression is the concatenation of a finite sequence of terms, then it is uniquely representable as such a concatenation.
