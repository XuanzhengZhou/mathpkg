The interpretation of formulas, also called the truth function, assigns a truth value ($0$ or $1$) to every formula relative to a point $\xi$ in the interpretation class $\overline{M}$. Atomic formulas are true if the tuple of interpreted terms belongs to the interpreted relation. The truth values of compound formulas are determined by the standard truth tables for connectives and by minimization/maximization over variable variations for quantifiers. This definition is Tarski's inductive definition of truth.
