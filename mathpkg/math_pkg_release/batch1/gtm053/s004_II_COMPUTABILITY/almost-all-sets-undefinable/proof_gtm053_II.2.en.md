By Proposition 2.12, the number of $\phi$-definable sets is at most $\operatorname{card}(\text{alphabet}) + \aleph_0$. If $M$ is infinite, then $2^{\operatorname{card} M} > \operatorname{card} M \geqslant \aleph_0$. Under the hypothesis $\operatorname{card}(\text{alphabet}) < 2^{\operatorname{card} M}$, we have $\operatorname{card}(\text{alphabet}) + \aleph_0 < 2^{\operatorname{card} M}$, since the sum of two cardinals each less than $2^{\operatorname{card} M}$ is also less than $2^{\operatorname{card} M}$ (by König's theorem or simply because the power set cardinal is a strong limit cardinal). Therefore, the number of definable sets is strictly less than the total number of subsets of $M$, so "almost all" sets are undefinable.
