Terms are the syntactic objects of a first-order language that denote elements of the domain of discourse. Together with formulas, they constitute the two categories of distinguished expressions. Any term is built up from variables and constants by a finite number of applications of operation symbols, giving a recursive definition that guarantees unique parsing. The most primitive terms are atomic (variables and constants); compound terms are formed by applying an operation symbol of degree $r$ to $r$ already-constructed terms.
