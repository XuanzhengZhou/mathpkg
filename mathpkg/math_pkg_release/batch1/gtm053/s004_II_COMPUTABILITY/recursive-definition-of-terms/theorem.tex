Terms are the elements of the least subset of the expressions of the language that satisfies the following two conditions:

(a) Variables and constants are (atomic) terms.

(b) If $f$ is an operation of degree $r$ and $t_1, \ldots, t_r$ are terms, then $f(t_1, \ldots, t_r)$ is a term.

In (a) we identify an element with a sequence of length one. The alphabet does not include commas, which are part of abbreviated notation: $f(t_1, t_2, t_3)$ means the same as $f(t_1t_2t_3)$. If two sets of expressions satisfy conditions (a) and (b), then their intersection also satisfies these conditions, so the definition is correct.
