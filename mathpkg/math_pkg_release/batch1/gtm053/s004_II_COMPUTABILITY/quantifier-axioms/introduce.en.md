The quantifier axioms, together with the tautology axioms, constitute the logical axioms $\mathrm{Ax}\,L$ of a first-order language $L$. Axiom (a) allows moving a universal quantifier past the antecedent of an implication when the variable does not occur free in the antecedent. Axiom (b) is the axiom of specialization: if $P$ holds for all $x$, then it holds for any particular term $t$. Axiom (c) is the dual: if $P$ holds for a specific $t$, then there exists some $x$ for which $P$ holds. The condition that $t$ be free for $x$ prevents variable capture.
