A $\phi$-definable set is a subset of $M^r$ whose membership is characterized by the truth of a formula $P$ under the interpretation $\phi$. This concept formalizes the idea that a formula expresses a property of tuples of elements from the interpretation domain. Understanding the structure of $\phi$-definable sets is one of the central problems of model theory. For $L_1\mathrm{Ar}$ with the standard interpretation, the definable sets are precisely those obtained from algebraic sets (zero sets of polynomials) by Boolean operations and projections.
