The tautology axioms are the propositional foundation of first-order logic. Together with the quantifier axioms, they form the set of logical axioms $\mathrm{Ax}\,L$. Axiom B1 (double negation elimination and introduction) encodes the law of excluded middle. Axiom B2 encodes the principle of explosion (ex contradictione quodlibet): from a contradiction, any formula follows. The truth of these axioms can be verified using truth tables or algebraic computation with the truth functions defined in §2.5.
