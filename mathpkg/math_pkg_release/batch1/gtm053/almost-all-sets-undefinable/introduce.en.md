This corollary highlights a fundamental expressive limitation of formal languages with finitely many primitive symbols: they can define at most countably many subsets, whereas an infinite set has uncountably many subsets. Consequently, any attempt to capture "all" mathematical properties of an infinite structure within a formal language is doomed to incompleteness. This observation provides important context for understanding the phenomena of incompleteness and undefinability.
