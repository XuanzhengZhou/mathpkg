This simple lemma shows that the conjunction of two deducible formulas is itself deducible. While intuitively obvious, the formal proof illustrates how tautologies are used in formal deductions: the tautology $P \Rightarrow (Q \Rightarrow (P \wedge Q))$ provides the bridge from the individual deductions of $P$ and $Q$ to the deduction of their conjunction.
