A formal deduction is the precise mathematical analogue of a mathematical proof. It consists of a finite sequence of formulas, each of which is either an axiom, a hypothesis (from $\mathcal{E}$), or follows from earlier formulas by one of the deduction rules (Modus Ponens or Generalization). The notation $\mathcal{E} dash P$ expresses that $P$ is formally deducible from the hypotheses $\mathcal{E}$. This concept is central to proof theory and underlies Gödel's completeness and incompleteness theorems.
