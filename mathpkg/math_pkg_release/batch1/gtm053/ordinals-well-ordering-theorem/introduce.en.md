This theorem establishes that the von Neumann ordinals form a universal well-ordered class. Every well-ordering is isomorphic to exactly one ordinal, making ordinals the canonical measure of well-ordered "lengths." The theorem also shows that the ordinals themselves are well-ordered by membership, providing the foundation for transfinite induction and recursion.
