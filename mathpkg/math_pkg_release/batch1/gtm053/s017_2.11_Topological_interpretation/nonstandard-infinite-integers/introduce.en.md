In any nonstandard arithmetic, every nonstandard element $c$ satisfies $c \geq \bar{n}$ for all standard $n \in \mathbb{N}$. Such elements are called "infinite integers." This follows from the fact that the ordering $x_1 \leq x_2$ is definable in the language of arithmetic by $\exists y \; x_1 + y = x_2$, and the sentence $\forall x (x \geq 0 \lor x = 0 \lor \cdots \lor x = n-1 \lor x \geq \bar{n})$ holds in $\mathbf{N}$, hence in any elementarily equivalent model every element not equal to some $\bar{n}$ must be larger than all of them.
