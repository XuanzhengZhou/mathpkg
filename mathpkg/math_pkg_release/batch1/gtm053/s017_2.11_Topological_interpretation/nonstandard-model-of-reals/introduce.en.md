A nonstandard model of the reals, denoted ${^*\mathbf{R}}$, is any model of the complete theory $\operatorname{Th}(\mathbb{R})$ of the ordered field of real numbers that is not isomorphic to the standard model $\mathbb{R}$. Such models contain infinitesimal and infinite elements and provide the foundation for nonstandard analysis, where infinitesimals are used to rigorously formalize arguments that were historically based on intuitive notions of infinitely small quantities.
