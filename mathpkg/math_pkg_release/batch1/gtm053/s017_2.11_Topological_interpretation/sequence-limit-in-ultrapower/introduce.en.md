A remarkable property of ultrapowers is that every sequence $\{a_i : i \in I\}$ of elements of the base structure $\mathbf{A}$ has a canonical "limit" in the ultrapower $\mathbf{A}^I / U$, namely the equivalence class of the sequence itself modulo the ultrafilter $U$. This limit-like behavior along ultrafilters is a key feature that makes ultrapowers useful in nonstandard analysis and in the construction of saturated models.
