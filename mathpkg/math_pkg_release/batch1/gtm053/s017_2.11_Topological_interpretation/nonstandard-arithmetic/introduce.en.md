A nonstandard arithmetic is a model of complete arithmetic $\operatorname{Th}(\mathbf{N})$ that is not isomorphic to the standard model $\mathbf{N}$. Such models contain "infinite integers"---elements that are larger than every standard natural number. The existence of nonstandard arithmetics is an immediate consequence of the compactness theorem.
