Any nonstandard model ${^*\mathbf{R}}$ of the reals necessarily contains a positive infinitesimal---an element $\alpha$ satisfying $0 < \alpha < 1/n$ for every standard positive integer $n$. The proof uses the Dedekind cut $([\gamma]^-, [\gamma]^+)$ of a new element $\gamma \in {^*\mathbf{R}}$ not equal to any standard real. By case analysis on the cut, one constructs either a positive infinitesimal directly or derives one from the difference $\gamma - r$ where $r$ is the standard real defined by the cut.
