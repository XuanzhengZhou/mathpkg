An element $\gamma$ of a nonstandard model ${^*\mathbf{R}}$ is called bounded if both its lower Dedekind cut $[\gamma]^-$ and its upper Dedekind cut $[\gamma]^+$ are nonempty. Equivalently, there exist standard rational numbers $p, q \in \mathbb{Q}$ such that $p < \gamma < q$. The set $B$ of all bounded elements forms a ring containing the standard reals, and every bounded element can be uniquely expressed as $r + \alpha$ where $r \in \mathbb{R}$ is standard and $\alpha$ is infinitesimal.
