An element $\gamma$ of a nonstandard model ${^*\mathbf{R}}$ is called infinite if its lower Dedekind cut $[\gamma]^- = \{q \in \mathbb{Q} : q \leq \gamma\}$ or its upper Dedekind cut $[\gamma]^+ = \{q \in \mathbb{Q} : q > \gamma\}$ is empty. Equivalently, $\gamma$ is larger than every standard rational (positively infinite) or smaller than every standard rational (negatively infinite). An infinite element is not bounded.
