By Łoś's theorem, the diagonal embedding of a structure $\mathbf{A}$ into its ultrapower $\mathbf{A}^I / U$ is an elementary embedding: $\mathbf{A} \prec \mathbf{A}^I / U$. This means that $\mathbf{A}$ and its ultrapower satisfy exactly the same first-order sentences with parameters from $\mathbf{A}$. This property makes ultrapowers a powerful tool for constructing elementary extensions with additional saturation or limit properties.
