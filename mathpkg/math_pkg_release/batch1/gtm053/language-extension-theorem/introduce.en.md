The Language Extension Theorem justifies the standard mathematical practice of introducing new notation for definable functions and constants. Adding a new function symbol for a provably unique object does not increase the deductive power of the theory; it is a conservative extension. This allows the formal introduction of notations like ordered pairs, finite ordinals, and other definable set-theoretic operations.
