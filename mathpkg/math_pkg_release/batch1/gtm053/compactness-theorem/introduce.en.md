The Compactness Theorem is one of the most important results in model theory. It states that if every finite subset of a set of sentences has a model, then the entire set has a model. Equivalent to Godel's Completeness Theorem via the finiteness of proofs, it also admits a direct ultraproduct proof. The theorem underlies nonstandard analysis, the construction of saturated models, and many other fundamental constructions.
