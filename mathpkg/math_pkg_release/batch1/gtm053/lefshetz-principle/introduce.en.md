The Strong Lefshetz Principle is a consequence of Tarski's completeness theorem for algebraically closed fields. It states that a first-order sentence in the language of fields holds in the complex numbers if and only if it holds in all algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero, if and only if it holds in the algebraic closures of finite fields for all sufficiently large primes. This provides a rigorous foundation for the informal Lefshetz principle used in algebraic geometry.
