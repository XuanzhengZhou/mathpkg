For each $n \geq 2$, let $P_n$ be a unary predicate symbol. The language $L_{\text{valf}}^{\text{Mac}}$ extends the language of valued fields by these countably many predicates, with the defining axiom:

$$\forall x \; [P_n(x) \leftrightarrow \exists y \; (y^n = x)].$$

That is, each $P_n$ singles out the set of $n$-th powers in the field.
