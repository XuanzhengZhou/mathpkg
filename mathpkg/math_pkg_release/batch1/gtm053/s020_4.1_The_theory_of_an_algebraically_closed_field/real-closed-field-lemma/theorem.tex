Let $A, B$ be real closed fields and $A_0 \subseteq A$, $B_0 \subseteq B$ subfields such that $A_0 \cong_{\varphi} B_0$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The isomorphism $\varphi$ can be extended to an isomorphism $\psi : \tilde{A}_0 \to \tilde{B}_0$ between the relative algebraic closures of the respective subfields in $A$ and $B$.
\item[(ii)] Assuming that $A_0$ and $B_0$ are respectively algebraically closed in $A$ and $B$, and $a_0 \in A \setminus A_0$, $b_0 \in B \setminus B_0$ are such that for any $a \in A_0$, $a_0 \leq a$ if and only if $b_0 \leq \varphi(a)$, then $\varphi$ can be extended to an isomorphism of ordered fields $\psi : A_0(a_0) \to B_0(b_0)$.
\item[(iii)] Assuming that $A_0$ is algebraically closed in $A$, a finite system of inequalities $f(x) \leq 0$, for $f(x) \in A_0[x]$, has a solution in $A$ if and only if it has a solution in $A_0$.
\end{enumerate}
