This lemma provides three key facts about extending isomorphisms between real closed fields, which are essential for the back-and-forth proof of the Tarski–Seidenberg theorem. Part (i) states that an isomorphism between subfields extends uniquely to their relative algebraic closures. Part (ii) gives a criterion for extending the isomorphism by one additional element while preserving the ordering. Part (iii) establishes a transfer principle for the existence of solutions to systems of polynomial inequalities between a real closed field and an algebraically closed subfield.
