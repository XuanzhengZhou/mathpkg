We do not give a proof of the theorem here. The model-theoretic methods of known proofs are essentially the same as for $\text{ACF}_p$ and $\text{RCF}$ (back-and-forth arguments using saturation and isomorphism extension lemmas), but the algebra is much more involved. 

The first proofs of completeness, decidability, and elimination of quantifiers (in a different language) were given by Ax–Kochen in 1965. Independently, Yu. Ershov proved completeness and decidability.

Macintyre proved elimination of quantifiers in the present form in 1976. The choice of language is essential for applications: the introduction of the predicates $P_n$ made the quantifier elimination statement much more useful and powerful. The first consequence of this quantifier elimination is the manifestation of similarities between the theory of the reals and the theory of the $p$-adics. Recall that in the reals, the predicate $P_2(x)$ (which means $x \geq 0$) is used for quantifier elimination and is basic for describing the topology and geometry over the reals.
