The Ax–Kochen–Ershov–Macintyre theorem is a landmark result in the model theory of valued fields. It states that the theory $TQ_p$ of $p$-adically closed fields (fields elementarily equivalent to $\mathbf{Q}_p$) is complete, decidable, and admits quantifier elimination in Macintyre's extended language $L_{\text{valf}}^{\text{Mac}}$ with predicates for $n$-th powers. The first proofs of completeness and decidability were given by Ax and Kochen in 1965; independently, Ershov proved completeness and decidability. Macintyre later proved the quantifier elimination in 1976. The introduction of the predicates $P_n$ made the quantifier elimination far more powerful, leading to applications such as Denef's proof of the rationality of Igusa's local zeta functions and of the Poincaré series of algebraic varieties over $p$-adic fields.
