The Tarski–Seidenberg theorem states that the first-order theory $\text{RCF}$ of real closed fields is both complete and admits quantifier elimination. This is one of the most important results in real algebraic geometry and model theory. The proof uses the same back-and-forth method as for $\text{ACF}_p$, relying on the lemma about extending isomorphisms between real closed fields. A fundamental consequence is that the definable subsets of a real closed field (with parameters) are precisely the semialgebraic sets, and the projection of a semialgebraic set is semialgebraic — a non-trivial geometric fact.
