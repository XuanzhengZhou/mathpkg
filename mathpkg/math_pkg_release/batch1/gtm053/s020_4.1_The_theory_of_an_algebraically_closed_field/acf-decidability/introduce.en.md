The decidability of $\text{ACF}_p$ and $\text{RCF}$ follows directly from the general proposition about complete recursively axiomatizable theories, since the axiomatizations given in 4.1 and 4.7 are clearly recursive. More interesting is the case of the incomplete theory $\text{ACF}$ (algebraically closed fields without specifying the characteristic): it is still decidable because one can enumerate the complete extensions $\text{ACF}_p$ for all $p$ (prime or zero) and use the fact that if $P$ is not provable from $\text{ACF}$, then $\neg P$ must be consistent with some $\text{ACF}_p$.
