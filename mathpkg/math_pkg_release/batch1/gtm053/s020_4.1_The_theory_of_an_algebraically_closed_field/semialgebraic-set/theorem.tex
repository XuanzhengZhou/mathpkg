A subset of $R^n$ (where $R$ is a real closed field) is called \emph{semialgebraic} if it is a finite Boolean combination of solution sets of polynomial equations $p(\bar{x}) = 0$ and polynomial inequalities $p(\bar{x}) > 0$ with coefficients in $R$.

By the Tarski–Seidenberg theorem, the semialgebraic sets are precisely the subsets of $R^n$ that are definable (using parameters) in the language of ordered rings. In particular, the projection of a semialgebraic set is semialgebraic.
