Tarski's theorem states that the first-order theory $\text{ACF}_p$ of algebraically closed fields of a fixed characteristic $p$ is both complete and admits quantifier elimination. The proof relies on the Steinitz theorem: two algebraically closed fields $A$ and $B$ of the same characteristic are isomorphic over a common subfield $k$ if and only if they have the same transcendence degree over $k$. The quantifier elimination follows by applying the automorphism criterion (Theorem 3.18).
