$\text{ACF}_0$ is the first-order theory of algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero. It extends the field axioms and the algebraic closedness axiom schema with the infinite set of axioms asserting that the characteristic is not any prime $p$. An elegant model-theoretic fact is that the ultraproduct $\prod_{p \in \text{Primes}} K_p / U$ of models $K_p$ of $\text{ACF}_p$ along a nonprincipal ultrafilter $U$ is a model of $\text{ACF}_0$. Consequently, any $L_1\text{Ar}$-sentence that holds in all but finitely many $\text{ACF}_p$ models also holds in every algebraically closed field of characteristic zero. This principle is formalized as the Strong Lefschetz Principle.
