Chevalley's theorem provides a geometric description of the definable sets in an algebraically closed field: the subsets of $F^n$ that are definable in the language $L_1\text{Ar}$ with parameters are precisely the constructible sets, i.e., Boolean combinations of zero-sets of polynomials. This is a direct translation of the quantifier elimination statement for $\text{ACF}_p$ into geometric language. An important consequence is that the projection of a constructible set is constructible, so the class of constructible sets is closed under all Boolean operations and projections.
