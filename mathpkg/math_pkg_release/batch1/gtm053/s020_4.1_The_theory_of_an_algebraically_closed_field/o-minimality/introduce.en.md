$o$-minimality (order-minimality) is a property of ordered structures that provides a tame geometric behavior for definable sets. A structure is $o$-minimal if every definable subset of its domain (in one variable) is a finite union of open intervals and points. The real closed field $\mathbf{R}$ is $o$-minimal, as follows from the Tarski–Seidenberg theorem: any definable subset of $\mathbf{R}$ is semialgebraic, and a one-variable semialgebraic set is determined by the sign of finitely many polynomials, hence decomposes into finitely many intervals and points. The theory of $o$-minimal structures was developed extensively by Pillay, Steinhorn, van den Dries, and others.
