An ordered structure $(M, <, \ldots)$ is called \emph{$o$-minimal} (order-minimal) if every definable subset of $M$ (using parameters) is a finite union of open intervals and points.

For a real closed field $R$, the solution set of a one-variable polynomial inequality $f(x) > 0$ is a finite union of open intervals $(a, b)$, where $-\infty \leq a < b \leq +\infty$. It follows that any definable subset of $R$ is a finite union of open intervals and points; hence the theory $\text{RCF}$ is $o$-minimal.
