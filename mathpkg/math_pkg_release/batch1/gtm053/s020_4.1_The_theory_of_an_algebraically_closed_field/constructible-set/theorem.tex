A subset of $F^n$ (for an algebraically closed field $F$) is called \emph{constructible} if it is representable as a Boolean combination of zero-sets of polynomials.

The family of $L_1\text{Ar}$-definable (using parameters) subsets of $F^n$, for all $n$, coincides with the family of constructible subsets. Moreover, the family of $L_1\text{Ar}$-definable sets is the same as the family of sets obtained from zero-sets of polynomials by applying Boolean operations (union, intersection, complement) and projections $F^{n+1} \to F^n$.
