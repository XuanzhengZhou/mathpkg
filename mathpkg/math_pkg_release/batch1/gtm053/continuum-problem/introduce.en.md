The Continuum Problem, first posed by Cantor, asks whether there exists a set whose cardinality is strictly between that of the integers and that of the real numbers. It is one of the most famous problems in mathematics. The problem was resolved in the negative sense by the combined work of Godel (who showed CH cannot be disproved from ZFC) and Cohen (who showed CH cannot be proved from ZFC).
