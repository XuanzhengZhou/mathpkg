Let $A$ be a finite or countable alphabet with a numbering $N_0: A \to \mathbb{Z}^+$. Then there exists an admissible numbering $N: S(A) \to \mathbb{Z}^+$ of all expressions in $A$ compatible with $N_0$. An admissible numbering means the length function, $i$-th coordinate function, and juxtaposition function are all recursive. Moreover, any two admissible numberings are equivalent up to recursive isomorphism.
