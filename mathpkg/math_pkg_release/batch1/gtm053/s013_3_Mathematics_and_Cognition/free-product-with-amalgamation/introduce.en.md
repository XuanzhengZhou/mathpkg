The free product with amalgamation generalizes both the free product of groups and the topological Seifert-van Kampen theorem. Given groups G_i with a common subgroup A identified across all factors, the amalgamated free product is the 'most general' group containing all G_i with the copies of A identified. The canonical form theorem ensures that the natural inclusions are embeddings and that the intersection of distinct factors is exactly the amalgamated subgroup.
