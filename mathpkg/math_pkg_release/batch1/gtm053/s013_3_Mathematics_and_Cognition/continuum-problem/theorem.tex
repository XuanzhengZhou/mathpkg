What place does the continuum occupy on the scale of cardinalities?
By Theorem 1.2(b), we have $2^{\omega_0} > \omega_0$.
Hence, in any case, $2^{\omega_0} \geqslant \omega_1$.
On the other hand, if $2^{\omega_0} > \omega_1, 2^{\omega_0} > \omega_2, \ldots, 2^{\omega_0} > \omega_n, \ldots$ for any $n$, then we would have $2^{\omega_0} > \omega_{\omega_0}$, since the continuum cannot be a union of countably many subsets of lower cardinality (K\"onig).
