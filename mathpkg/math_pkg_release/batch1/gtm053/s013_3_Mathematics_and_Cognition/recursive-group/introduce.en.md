A recursive group is a group that can be presented as $G/H$ where both $G$ (a subgroup of the free group on countably many generators) and $H$ (a normal subgroup of $G$) are enumerable. This definition connects combinatorial group theory with recursion theory. Finitely presented groups, finite groups, and finitely generated abelian groups are all recursive. The concept is central to the study of algorithmic problems in group theory, such as the word problem and the isomorphism problem.
