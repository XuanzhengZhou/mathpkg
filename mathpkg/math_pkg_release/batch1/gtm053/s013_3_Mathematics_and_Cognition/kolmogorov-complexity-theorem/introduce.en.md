Kolmogorov's theorem on complexity establishes the existence of asymptotically optimal enumerable families of recursive functions, relative to which the complexity (the minimal index of a function in the family) is well-defined up to a multiplicative constant. The complexity $C_u(f)$ measures the minimal amount of information needed to specify a recursive function $f$ within a given enumeration. An optimal family is one that yields the smallest possible complexity bounds compared to any other enumeration. Part (b) shows that the choice of optimal family affects complexity by at most a bounded factor.
