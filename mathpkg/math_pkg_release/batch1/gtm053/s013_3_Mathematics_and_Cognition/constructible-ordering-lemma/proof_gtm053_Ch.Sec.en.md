The proof follows immediately from the definitions. To find the least triple in a nonempty class $C$, set $\gamma = \min\{\max(\alpha, \beta) \mid \langle i, \alpha, \beta \rangle \in C\}$. Then analyze the cases where $C_\gamma$ does or does not contain triples of the form $\langle i, \alpha, \gamma \rangle$, and within each case pick the minimum coordinate values.
