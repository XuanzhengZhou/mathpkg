There exists a finitely presented group with an algorithmically undecidable word problem. For example, let $G$ be the group with four generators $a, b, c, d$ and relations
$$b^{-m}ab^{m} = d^{-m}cd^{m}, \quad \text{for all } m \in E,$$
where $E \subset \mathbb{Z}^+$ is an undecidable enumerable set. Then the equation $b^{-x}ab^{x} = d^{-x}cd^{x}$ holds in $G$ only if $x \in E$, making the word problem undecidable.
