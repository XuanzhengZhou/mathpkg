Let $M$ and $N$ be two sets. Then:
(a) Any two sets are comparable. If both $\operatorname{card} M \leqslant \operatorname{card} N$ and $\operatorname{card} N \leqslant \operatorname{card} M$, then $\operatorname{card} M = \operatorname{card} N$. In other words, the cardinalities are linearly ordered.
(b) Let $\mathcal{P}(M)$ be the set of all subsets of $M$. Then $\operatorname{card} \mathcal{P}(M) > \operatorname{card} M$. In particular, there does not exist a largest cardinality.
(c) In any class of cardinalities there is a least cardinality. In other words, the cardinalities are well-ordered.
