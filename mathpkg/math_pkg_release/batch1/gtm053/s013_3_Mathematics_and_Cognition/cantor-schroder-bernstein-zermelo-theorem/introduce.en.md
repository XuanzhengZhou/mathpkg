The Cantor-Schröder-Bernstein-Zermelo theorem establishes the fundamental properties of cardinal comparability for infinite sets. It asserts that cardinalities are linearly ordered (any two sets are comparable), that the power set of any set has strictly larger cardinality (so there is no largest cardinality), and that cardinalities are well-ordered. The proof uses the well-ordering principle and the construction of admissible well-orderings via a choice function.
