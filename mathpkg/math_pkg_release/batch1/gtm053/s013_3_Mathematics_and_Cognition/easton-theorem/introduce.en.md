Easton's theorem (1965) shows that the continuum function at regular cardinals can be almost arbitrary, subject only to Konig's theorem (cofinality constraint) and monotonicity. If the domain of F is a set, the result is obtained using forcing over a model of GCH. For class-sized domains, class forcing is required.
