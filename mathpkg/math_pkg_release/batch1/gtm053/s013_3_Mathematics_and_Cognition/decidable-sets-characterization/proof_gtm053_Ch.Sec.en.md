(a) = (b): trivial. (b) $\subset$ (c): obvious. (c) $\subset$ (a): Let $E = D(f)$ and $E' = D(f')$ with $f \equiv 1$, $f' \equiv 2$ on their domains. The graph $\Gamma_g$ of the characteristic function $g$ is $\Gamma_f \cup \Gamma_{f'}$, which is r.e. Then Proposition 4.15 (graph characterization) implies $g$ is partial recursive, and since $g$ is everywhere defined, it is general recursive.
