The proof uses recursive embedding functions $\theta^{(n)}: (\mathbb{Z}^+)^n \to \mathbb{Z}^+$ satisfying $\theta^{(n)}(k_1, \ldots, k_n) \leqslant \text{const} \prod k_i (\log \prod k_i)^{n-1}$. Two optimal families $v$ (of $p$-functions) and $u$ (of $m$-functions) are combined into a family $w$ where the index of $F$ is bounded by a function of the individual indices.
