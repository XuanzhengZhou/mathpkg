The alphabet consists of variable symbols $x, y, z, \ldots$; symbols for degree-1 functions $f, g, h, \ldots$; constants 0 and 1; degree-2 operations $+$ and $\cdot$; degree-2 relations $=$ and $\leqslant$; and the usual connectives, quantifiers, and parentheses.

Terms are $x, y, z, \ldots$ and 0, 1; and also $f(t), t_1 \cdot t_2$, and $t_1 + t_2$ if $f$ is a function symbol and $t, t_1, t_2$ are terms.

Atomic formulas are $t_1 = t_2$ and $t_1 \leqslant t_2$. Formulas are defined inductively, with the addition that $\forall f(Q)$ and $\exists f(Q)$ are formulas if $Q$ is a formula and $f$ is a function variable symbol.
