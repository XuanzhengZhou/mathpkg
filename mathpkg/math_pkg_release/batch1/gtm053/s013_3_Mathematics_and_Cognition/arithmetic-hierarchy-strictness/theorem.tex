For all $n \geq 1$, the following strict inclusions hold:
$$\Sigma_n \setminus \Pi_n \neq \varnothing, \quad \Pi_n \setminus \Sigma_n \neq \varnothing.$$

In particular, the arithmetic hierarchy is strict: for each $n \geq 1$, there exist sets definable by $\Sigma_n$-formulas that are not definable by $\Pi_n$-formulas, and vice versa.
