The strictness theorem for the arithmetic hierarchy asserts that the hierarchy of $\Sigma_n$ and $\Pi_n$ sets does not collapse at any finite level. In other words, for each $n$, there are arithmetically definable sets that require exactly $n$ quantifier alternations and cannot be expressed with fewer. The proof proceeds by a diagonal argument using a versal family of enumerable sets, generalizing the basic fact that $\Sigma_1 \setminus \Pi_1 \neq \varnothing$, which is equivalent to the existence of undecidable enumerable sets.
