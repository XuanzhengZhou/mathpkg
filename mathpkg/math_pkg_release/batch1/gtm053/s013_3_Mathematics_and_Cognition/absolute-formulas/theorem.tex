(a) Atomic formulas are $M$-absolute for all $M$.
(b) If $P$ and $Q$ are $M$-absolute, then so are $\neg P$, $P \vee Q$, $P \wedge Q$, $P \Rightarrow Q$.
(c) Let $f(y_1, \ldots, y_r)$ be an everywhere defined operation of degree $r$ on $M \cup V$ such that $f(M \times \cdots \times M) \subset M$. Then formulas of the form $\forall x \in f(y_1, \ldots, y_r) P$ and $\exists x \in f(y_1, \ldots, y_r) P$ are $M$-absolute whenever $P$ is.
A formula all of whose quantifiers are restricted in this way is called a $\Sigma_0$-formula.
