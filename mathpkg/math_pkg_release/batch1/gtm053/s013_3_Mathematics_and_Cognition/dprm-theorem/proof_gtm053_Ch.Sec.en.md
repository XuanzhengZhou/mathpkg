The proof shows that the class of D-sets (the closure of polynomial level sets under finite products, unions, intersections, projections, and bounded universal quantification) coincides with the class of Diophantine sets. The key steps are: (1) graphs of primitive recursive functions are D-sets; (2) the exponential function $m = a^n$ is Diophantine (using Pell's equation); (3) the bounded universal quantifier preserves Diophantine definability (reduction step using Godel coding and remainder arithmetic).
