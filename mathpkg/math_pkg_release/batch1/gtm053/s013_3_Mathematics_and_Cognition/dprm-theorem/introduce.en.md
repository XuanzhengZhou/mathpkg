The DPRM theorem, proved by Davis, Putnam, Robinson, and Matiyasevich, establishes that recursively enumerable sets are exactly Diophantine sets. This solved Hilbert's tenth problem negatively: there is no algorithm to decide solvability of Diophantine equations. An r.e. but undecidable set translates to an undecidable family of Diophantine equations.
