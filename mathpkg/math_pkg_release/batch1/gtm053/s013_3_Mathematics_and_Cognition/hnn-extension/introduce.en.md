The HNN-extension is a fundamental construction in combinatorial group theory, introduced by Higman, Neumann, and Neumann (1949). It embeds a group G into a larger group where two isomorphic subgroups become conjugate via a new 'stable letter' t. The construction preserves many properties of G and is essential for Higman's embedding theorem, which states that every recursive group embeds in a finitely presented group.
