A mass problem $P = (P_n)$ is a countable sequence of mathematical problems each with a "yes" or "no" answer, written out effectively as a function of $n$. Associate to $P$ the function $f: \mathbb{Z}^+ \to \mathbb{Z}^+$ with $D(f) = \{i \mid P_i \text{ has answer "yes"}\}$ and $f(i) = 1$ for $i \in D(f)$. $P$ is algorithmically decidable if both $f$ and $\chi_{D(f)}$ are partial recursive. Otherwise $P$ is algorithmically undecidable. If only $\chi_{D(f)}$ is not partial recursive, this is a weaker form of undecidability.
