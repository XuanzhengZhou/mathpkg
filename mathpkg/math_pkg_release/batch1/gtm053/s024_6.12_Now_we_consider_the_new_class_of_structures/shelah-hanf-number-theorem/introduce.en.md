Shelah's Hanf Number Categoricity Transfer Theorem (7.8) is one of the most powerful results in the theory of abstract elementary classes. It states that for any AEC satisfying amalgamation and joint embedding, categoricity at a single successor cardinal above the Hanf number $\mu$ (which depends only on the Löwenheim–Skolem number) implies categoricity in all larger cardinals. This generalizes Morley's categoricity theorem from first-order logic to the vastly broader AEC framework, showing that the structural essence of categoricity transfer is algebraic rather than syntactic.
