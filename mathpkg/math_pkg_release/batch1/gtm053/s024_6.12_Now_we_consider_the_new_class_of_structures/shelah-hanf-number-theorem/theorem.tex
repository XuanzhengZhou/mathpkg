There exists a Hanf number $\mu$ (depending only on the Löwenheim--Skolem number $\operatorname{LS}(K)$) such that if an abstract elementary class $K$ has arbitrarily large models and satisfies the amalgamation property and the joint embedding property for its models, then provided that $K$ is categorical in a successor cardinal larger than $\mu$, it is categorical in all larger cardinals.
