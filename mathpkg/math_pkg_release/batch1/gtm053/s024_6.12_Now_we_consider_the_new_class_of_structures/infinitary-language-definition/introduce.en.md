The infinitary languages $L_{\lambda,\mu}$ generalize first-order logic by allowing quantification over sequences of variables of length less than $\mu$ and Boolean operations (conjunction and disjunction) over sets of formulas of cardinality less than $\lambda$. The most studied case is $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$, which allows countable conjunctions and disjunctions but only finite quantifier strings. A key feature of these languages is the failure of the compactness theorem, which makes their model theory significantly more challenging than first-order model theory.
