An abstract elementary class (AEC) is Shelah's framework for non-first-order model theory that abstracts away from specific logical syntax. Instead of specifying a language of formulas, an AEC is defined by closure properties: it is a class of structures equipped with a "strong submodel" relation satisfying axioms of coherence, unions of chains, downward Löwenheim–Skolem, and closure under isomorphism. This shift from syntax to algebra brought model theory closer to abstract algebra while retaining the power of classification theory. Examples include $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-axiomatizable classes, classes of models of a complete first-order theory with elementary substructure, and the class of fields with pseudoexponentiation (with strong embedding corresponding to the Schanuel predimension).
