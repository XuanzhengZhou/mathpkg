Conjecture 6.17 states that the unique (up to isomorphism) algebraically closed field with pseudoexponentiation of cardinality the continuum is isomorphic to the complex numbers equipped with the usual exponential function $\exp$. This conjecture implies Schanuel's conjecture and also leads to the further conjecture that $(\mathbf{C}, +, \cdot, \exp)$ is algebraically closed as a field with exponentiation — meaning that every consistent exponential-polynomial system has a regular zero.
