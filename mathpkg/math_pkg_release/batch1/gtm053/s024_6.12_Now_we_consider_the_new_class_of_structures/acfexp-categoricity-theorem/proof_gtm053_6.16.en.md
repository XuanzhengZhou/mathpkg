The proof of this theorem uses Hrushovski's amalgamation construction applied to the Schanuel predimension $\delta(X) = \text{trd}(X \cup \text{ex}X) - \text{ldim}(X)$. The Hrushovski inequality $\delta(X) \geq 0$ is equivalent to Schanuel's conjecture. By carefully collapsing the generic structure $M^\sharp$ built from the class $\mathcal{H}_{\delta}$, following one of continuum many procedures, one obtains a strongly minimal substructure $M^\sharp_\mu$ whose pregeometry agrees with that of $M^\sharp$. The categoricity in uncountable powers follows from the general theory of strongly minimal structures. The homogeneity of the pregeometry follows from the construction's preservation of the predimension and dimension notions exactly as defined in Section 6.12.
