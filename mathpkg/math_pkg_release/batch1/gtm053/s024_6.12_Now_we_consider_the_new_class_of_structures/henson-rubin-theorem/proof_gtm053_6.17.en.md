The theorem is a formal corollary of the complex exponentiation conjecture (6.17). Assuming the conjecture, $(\mathbf{C}, +, \cdot, \exp)$ is isomorphic to the unique ACFExp of continuum power, which is algebraically closed as a field with pseudoexponentiation. The algebraic closedness condition (axiom (b) of ACFExp) then guarantees that any exponential-polynomial equation $f(x) = 0$ not directly contradicting Schanuel's conjecture has a solution. The condition that $f(x)$ is not of the form $e^{g(x)}$ ensures the equation does not trivially contradict the axioms.
