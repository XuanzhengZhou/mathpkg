The Henson–Rubin Theorem (1983) states that any exponential-polynomial equation $f(x) = 0$ in one variable over the complex numbers has a complex solution, provided $f(x)$ is not merely the exponential of another term. This result is a formal corollary of the complex exponentiation conjecture (6.17) and provides evidence that $(\mathbf{C}, +, \cdot, \exp)$ is algebraically closed as a field with exponentiation.
