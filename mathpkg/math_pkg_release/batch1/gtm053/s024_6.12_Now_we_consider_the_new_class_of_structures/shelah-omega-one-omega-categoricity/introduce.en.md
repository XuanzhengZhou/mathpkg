Shelah's Theorem 7.9 shows that for an $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-sentence $\Sigma$, categoricity at sufficiently many uncountable cardinals (specifically, all $\beth_n$ cardinals) forces categoricity in every infinite cardinal. The proof assumes mild set-theoretic hypotheses ($2^{\aleph_n} < 2^{\aleph_{n+1}}$ for all $n$) and uses the technology of AECs to transfer categoricity from a countable set of cardinals down to $\aleph_0$ and up to all larger cardinals.
