The proof uses the machinery of abstract elementary classes. The class of models of an $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-sentence $\Sigma$ with the relation of $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-elementary substructure forms an AEC with Löwenheim–Skolem number $\aleph_0$. Under the set-theoretic assumption $2^{\aleph_n} < 2^{\aleph_{n+1}}$, the categoricity of $\Sigma$ at each $\beth_n = 2^{\aleph_n}$ implies, via Shelah's categoricity transfer machinery for AECs, that $\Sigma$ is categorical in all cardinals above the Hanf number. Since the Hanf number for an AEC with $\operatorname{LS}(K) = \aleph_0$ is below $\beth_{\omega_1}$, the categoricity at the $\beth_n$ cardinals propagates to all uncountable cardinals. A further argument using the set-theoretic hypothesis then forces categoricity also at $\aleph_0$.
