For a new unary function $f$ on a field, the \text{predimension} in $(\mathbf{M}, f)$ is the counting function
$$\delta(X) = \text{trd}(X \cup f(X)) - |X|.$$

More generally, for a fusion between two structures $(M, L_1)$ and $(M, L_2)$ carrying combinatorial pregeometries with dimension notions $d_1(X)$ and $d_2(X)$ respectively, the predimension $\delta(X)$ in the fused structure $(M, L_1 \cup L_2)$ is a linear combination of $d_1$ and $d_2$, uniquely determined by the free fusion principle.
