A strong embedding $A \leq_{\delta} B$ is Hrushovski's refinement of the substructure relation tailored to the predimension $\delta$. It requires that for every finite subset $X$ of $A$, the minimal $\delta$-value achievable by extending $X$ within $A$ equals the minimal $\delta$-value achievable by extending $X$ within the larger structure $B$. This notion is the key to defining algebraically closed objects in the class $\mathcal{H}_{\delta}$.
