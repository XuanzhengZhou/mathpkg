The distinction between free and bound occurrences of variables is central to the semantics of first-order logic. Free occurrences correspond to indeterminate objects whose values can vary with the interpretation point $\xi$, while bound occurrences are those "captured" by quantifiers and thus do not refer to external values. A closed formula (or sentence) is a formula with no free variables; its truth value is completely determined, independent of any particular assignment of values to variables.
