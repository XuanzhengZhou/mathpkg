Boolean algebras abstract the algebraic properties of the logical operations AND, OR, and NOT, and of the set-theoretic operations intersection, union, and complement. Examples include the power set of any set, the algebra of open-and-closed subsets of a topological space, and the measure algebra of a probability space (modulo null sets). Boolean algebras are central to the Boolean-valued approach to forcing developed by Scott and Solovay.
