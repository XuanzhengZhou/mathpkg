A combinatorial pregeometry is a set equipped with a finitary closure operator satisfying the exchange property. This structure abstracts the notion of linear span in vector spaces and algebraic closure in fields, providing a unified framework for dependence relations. In model theory, the algebraic closure operator on a strongly minimal set naturally forms a combinatorial pregeometry, which is the foundation for developing a well-behaved dimension theory (Morley rank) and analyzing the structure of definable sets.
