A pair $(M, \operatorname{cl})$ is a \emph{combinatorial pregeometry} if $\operatorname{cl}$ is a finitary operator on $M$ satisfying axioms (i)--(iii) of a closure operator together with the exchange property. Here $\operatorname{cl}(U, v) := \operatorname{cl}(U \cup \{v\})$.

The standard axioms for a pregeometry are:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $U \subseteq \operatorname{cl}(U)$ (reflexivity),
\item[(ii)] $U \subseteq V \implies \operatorname{cl}(U) \subseteq \operatorname{cl}(V)$ (monotonicity),
\item[(iii)] $\operatorname{cl}(\operatorname{cl}(U)) = \operatorname{cl}(U)$ (idempotence),
\item[(iv)] (Exchange) if $a \in \operatorname{cl}(U, b) \setminus \operatorname{cl}(U)$, then $b \in \operatorname{cl}(U, a)$.
\end{itemize}
