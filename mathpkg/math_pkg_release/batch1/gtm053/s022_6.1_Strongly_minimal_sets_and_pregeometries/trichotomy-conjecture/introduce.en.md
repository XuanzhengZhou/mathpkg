Zilber's trichotomy conjecture asserts that every strongly minimal structure falls into one of three fundamental classes based on its associated combinatorial geometry: trivial (disintegrated), locally modular (vector-space-like / projective geometry over a division ring), or field-like (interpreting an algebraically closed field). The conjecture in its original strong form was disproved by Hrushovski's counterexamples, but a refined version — the theory of Zariski geometries — has been successfully developed. The trichotomy remains a guiding principle connecting model theory to mainstream algebraic geometry.
