A \emph{combinatorial geometry} is a pregeometry $(M, \operatorname{cl})$ such that $\operatorname{cl}(\{u\}) = \{u\}$ for any $u \in M$. This notion is also known under the names \emph{matroid} and \emph{dependence relation}, and was used in combinatorics and algebra by van der Waerden to develop a unified theory of dependence relations such as linear dependence and algebraic dependence in fields.
