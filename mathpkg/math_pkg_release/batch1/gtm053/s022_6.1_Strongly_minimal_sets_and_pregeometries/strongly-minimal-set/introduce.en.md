A set (or structure) is strongly minimal if it remains minimal in every elementarily equivalent model — minimality is preserved under elementary equivalence. This invariance property makes strongly minimal sets amenable to model-theoretic analysis via the compactness theorem and saturation arguments. In the theory of algebraically closed fields $\mathrm{ACF}_p$, strongly minimal subsets correspond precisely to irreducible algebraic curves up to finitely many points, establishing the deep link between geometric stability theory and algebraic geometry.
