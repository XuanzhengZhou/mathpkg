Theorem 6.5 establishes a complete classification of infinite locally finite homogeneous combinatorial geometries. Under the additional assumption of local finiteness (the closure of any finite set is finite), algebraically closed fields are excluded, and the only possibilities are trivial geometries and projective or affine geometries over finite fields. This result provides strong evidence for Zilber's trichotomy conjecture by confirming its predictions in the locally finite case. The proof proceeds via delicate combinatorial-geometric analysis using model-theoretic ranks and an intersection theory on pseudoplanes analogous to Bezout's theorem.
