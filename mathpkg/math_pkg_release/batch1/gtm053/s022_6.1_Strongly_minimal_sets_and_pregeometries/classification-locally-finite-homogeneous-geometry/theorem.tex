\textbf{Theorem 6.5.} An infinite locally finite homogeneous geometry is isomorphic to one of the following:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] a trivial geometry;
\item[(ii)] a projective geometry over a finite field;
\item[(iii)] an affine geometry over a finite field.
\end{enumerate}

Note that (ii) and (iii) are special cases of the locally modular non-trivial type in the trichotomy (6.4(ii)).
