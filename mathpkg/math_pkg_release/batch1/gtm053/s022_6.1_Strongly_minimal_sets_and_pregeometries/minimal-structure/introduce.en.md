A structure is called minimal when its Morley rank is exactly 1 and it cannot be partitioned into two definable subsets both of rank 1. Equivalently, any definable subset of a minimal structure is either finite (rank 0) or cofinite (its complement has rank 0). Minimal structures form the building blocks of the nonorthogonality analysis in geometric stability theory, as more complex structures can be decomposed and studied via their minimal components.
