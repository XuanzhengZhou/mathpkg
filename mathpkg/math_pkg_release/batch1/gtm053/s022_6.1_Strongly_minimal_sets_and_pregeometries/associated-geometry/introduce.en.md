Every combinatorial pregeometry gives rise to an associated geometry by removing the closure of the empty set and identifying points with the same singleton closure. This construction strips away the "trivial" or "degenerate" elements and collapses algebraically indistinguishable points, yielding a genuine combinatorial geometry. In model theory, this associated geometry carries the essential structural information about the strongly minimal set up to interalgebraicity.
