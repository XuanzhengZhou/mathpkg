The text states that "it is not difficult to prove" this proposition. The standard proof proceeds by showing that in a strongly minimal theory, the algebraic closure operator $\operatorname{acl}$ defines a combinatorial pregeometry on any model. For uncountable models, any two bases of the pregeometry have the same cardinality (the dimension), and any two models of the same uncountable dimension are isomorphic. The exchange property of $\operatorname{acl}$ in strongly minimal theories follows from the minimality condition, and the categoricity in uncountable powers follows from the existence and uniqueness of prime models over independent sets (or equivalently, from the fact that any model is prime over a basis).
