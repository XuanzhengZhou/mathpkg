A closure operator is finitary (or of finite character) when the closure of any set is the union of closures of its finite subsets. Equivalently, an element belongs to the closure of an infinite set if and only if it belongs to the closure of some finite subset. This property is essential for the definition of a combinatorial pregeometry and ensures that dimension can be defined via finite bases, making the theory of independence tractable in model-theoretic contexts.
