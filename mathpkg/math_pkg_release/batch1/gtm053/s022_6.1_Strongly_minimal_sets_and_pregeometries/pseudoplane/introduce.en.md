A pseudoplane is an incidence structure that behaves locally like a projective plane but may be infinite-dimensional. In geometric stability theory, pseudoplanes serve as the characteristic obstruction to local modularity: a non-locally-modular strongly minimal set must contain a definable pseudoplane. This insight lies at the heart of Zilber's trichotomy, where pseudoplanes are shown (under additional assumptions) to interpret an algebraically closed field, thereby connecting the abstract classification to concrete algebraic geometry.
