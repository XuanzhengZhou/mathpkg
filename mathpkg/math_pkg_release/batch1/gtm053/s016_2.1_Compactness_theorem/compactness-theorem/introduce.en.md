The Compactness Theorem is a fundamental result in first-order model theory. It states that if every finite subset of a set $E$ of first-order sentences has a model, then the entire set $E$ itself has a model. Moreover, the model can be chosen to have cardinality at most $|L| + \aleph_0$, where $|L|$ is the cardinality of the language. The theorem was first proved by Kurt Godel in 1930 (for countable languages) and independently by Anatoly Mal'tsev in 1936 (for arbitrary languages). Each known proof relies on the axiom of choice, making the model construction ineffective in general.
