A filtered product (also called reduced product) is a construction in model theory that builds a new $L$-structure from a family of $L$-structures $\{\mathbf{A}_i\}_{i \in I}$ by factoring their Cartesian product by the equivalence relation induced by a filter $U$ on the index set $I$. Relations and functions are interpreted "almost everywhere" with respect to the filter. When the filter $U$ is an ultrafilter, the filtered product is called an ultraproduct; when all factor structures are identical, it is called an ultrapower. This construction is central to the third proof of the Compactness Theorem.
