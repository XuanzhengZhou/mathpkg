Suppose $E \vdash P$. Then $E^0 \vdash P$ for some finite $E^0 \subseteq E$, since only finitely many formulas are involved in the proof. In particular, if $E$ were inconsistent, already its finite subset $E^0$ would be inconsistent. But then $E^0$ could not be satisfiable, contradicting the assumption that $E$ is finitely satisfiable.
