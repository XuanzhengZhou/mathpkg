An ultrapower is the ultraproduct of identical copies of a single structure $\mathbf{A}$. It is written $\mathbf{A}^I / U$. By Łoś's theorem, the ultrapower is elementarily equivalent to $\mathbf{A}$. Ultrapowers are a cornerstone of nonstandard analysis (e.g., the hyperreal numbers are an ultrapower of $\mathbb{R}$) and provide a method for constructing elementary extensions with desirable saturation properties.
