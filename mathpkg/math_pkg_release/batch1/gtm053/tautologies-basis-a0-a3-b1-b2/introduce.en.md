Tautologies are formulas that are true in all interpretations, independent of the truth values of their atomic components. They form the logical core of classical first-order logic. The basis listed here (A0-A3, B1, B2 together with the quantifier axioms) is sufficient to generate all tautologies via the rule of Modus Ponens. B1 is the law of the excluded middle (double negation), and B2 is the principle that a contradiction implies any formula (ex falso quodlibet).
