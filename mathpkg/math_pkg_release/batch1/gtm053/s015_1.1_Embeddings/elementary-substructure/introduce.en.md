An elementary substructure $A \preceq B$ is an embedding where truth of every $L$-formula (with parameters from $A$) is preserved in both directions. This is stronger than an ordinary embedding, which only preserves atomic formulas (relation, operation, and constant symbols). Elementary substructures preserve all first-order properties expressible in the language $L$, making them a central notion in model theory.
