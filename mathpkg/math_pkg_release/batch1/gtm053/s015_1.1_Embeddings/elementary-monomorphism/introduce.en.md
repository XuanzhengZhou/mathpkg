An elementary monomorphism is a partial map between $L$-structures that preserves the truth of all $L$-formulas on its domain. It generalizes the notion of elementary embedding to partial functions, requiring only that the domain of $h$ be a subset of $A$ rather than the whole structure. This notion is useful when one cannot embed the entire structure elementarily but can do so on a fragment.
