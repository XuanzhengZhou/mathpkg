Logical consequence is the fundamental semantic relation of model theory: a sentence $S$ follows logically from a set of sentences $E$ if every model of $E$ is also a model of $S$. For infinite $E$, the definition incorporates a compactness condition — $S$ follows from $E$ exactly when it follows from some finite subset. This semantic approach, based on truth in structures rather than formal derivability, is more flexible and coincides with syntactic consequence for first-order logic by the Godel completeness theorem.
