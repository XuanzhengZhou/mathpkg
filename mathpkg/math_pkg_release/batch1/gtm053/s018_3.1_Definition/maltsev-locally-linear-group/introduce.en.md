Mal'tsev's local theorem for linear groups states that the property "being a linear group of rank $n$" is local: a group is linear of rank $n$ precisely when every finitely generated subgroup is. The proof applies the method of diagrams together with the compactness theorem, rewriting the group diagram into the language of fields and using the fact that the interpretation of $\text{GL}_n(F)$ in a field $F$ is independent of $F$.
