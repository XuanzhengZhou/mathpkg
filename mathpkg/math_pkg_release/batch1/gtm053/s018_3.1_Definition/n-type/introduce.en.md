Given a complete theory $T$, an $n$-type is a set of formulas in $n$ free variables that is finitely consistent with $T$ — every finite subset can be simultaneously realized in some model of $T$. Types capture the possible "descriptions" of $n$-tuples in models of $T$ and are the fundamental objects of stability theory and classification theory. By the compactness theorem, any $n$-type is realized in some model of $T$.
