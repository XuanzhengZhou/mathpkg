Tarski's elementary chain theorem states that the union of an elementary chain remains an elementary extension of every earlier structure. The proof proceeds by induction on formulas: atomic formulas are preserved because the limit structure is defined by union; Boolean connectives are trivial; and the existential quantifier step uses the fact that a witness found in the union must already appear in some $A_\beta$ with $\alpha < \beta < \delta$, where the induction hypothesis applies.
