The complete diagram $\text{CDiag}(A)$ of an $L$-structure $A$ is the set of all $L_A$-sentences (not just atomic ones) that are true in the natural expansion $A_A$. Where the ordinary diagram captures the atomic truths, the complete diagram captures the entire first-order theory of $A$ with all elements named. An expansion of $B$ satisfies $\text{CDiag}(A)$ precisely when the map $a \mapsto c_a$ is an elementary embedding of $A$ into $B$.
