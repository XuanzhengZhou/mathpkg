A set of axioms for a theory $T$ is a subset $E \subseteq T$ whose deductive closure equals $T$ — every sentence in $T$ is a logical consequence of $E$, and every logical consequence of $E$ belongs to $T$. This formalizes the notion that $E$ generates $T$; one often specifies a theory by listing its axioms and taking $T$ to be all sentences derivable from them.
