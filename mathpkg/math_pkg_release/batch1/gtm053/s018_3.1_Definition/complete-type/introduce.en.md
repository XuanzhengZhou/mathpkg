A complete $n$-type is a maximal consistent set of formulas in $n$ free variables: for every formula $P(\bar{x})$, either it or its negation belongs to the type. Complete types correspond precisely to the first-order descriptions of $n$-tuples in models of $T$ — each $n$-tuple $\bar{a}$ in any model of $T$ determines a unique complete type $\text{tp}(\bar{a}/T)$, the set of all formulas it satisfies.
