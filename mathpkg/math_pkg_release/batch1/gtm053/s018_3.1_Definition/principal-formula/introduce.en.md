A principal formula for a type $\tau$ is a single formula $P(\bar{x})$ that logically implies (modulo $T$) every formula in $\tau$ and is itself consistent with $T$. Such a formula isolates the type: any tuple satisfying $P(\bar{x})$ in a model of $T$ automatically has type $\tau$. This is the syntactic counterpart of an isolated point in the Stone space of types.
