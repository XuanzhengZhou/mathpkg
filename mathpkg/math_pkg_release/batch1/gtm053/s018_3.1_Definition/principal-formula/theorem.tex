If an $n$-type $\tau$ in a complete theory $T$ is principal, witnessed by a formula $P(\bar{x})$ such that $T \models \exists \bar{x}\, P(\bar{x})$ and $T \models \forall \bar{x}\,(P(\bar{x}) \to Q(\bar{x}))$ for all $Q \in \tau$, then $P(\bar{x})$ is called a principal formula for the type $\tau$.
