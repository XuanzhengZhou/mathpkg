Tarski's Undefinability Theorem for arithmetic states that arithmetical truth cannot be defined within arithmetic itself. The proof uses a diagonal argument: if a truth predicate existed, one could construct (via the Display Lemma) a formula that asserts its own falsehood, leading to a contradiction. This theorem is a precursor and companion to Godel's Incompleteness Theorems.
