The special axioms of $\mathrm{L}_1\mathrm{Ar}$ (Peano arithmetic) formalize the basic properties of the natural numbers with addition and multiplication. The axioms include the standard commutative semiring axioms and the induction schema. Note that the formal induction axiom is a schema with one formula for each definable property $P$, which is weaker than the full second-order induction principle (which quantifies over all subsets of the natural numbers). Since there are only countably many formulas, the formal induction captures only countably many instances of the intuitive induction principle.
