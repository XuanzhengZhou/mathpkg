Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a set of formulas in $L$, let $\psi$ be an interpretation of $L$ in $N$, and let $M_0 \subset N$ be a subset. Then there exists a set $M$, $M_0 \subset M \subset N$, having cardinality $\leqslant \operatorname{card} M_0 + \operatorname{card} \mathcal{E} + \aleph_0$, such that all the formulas in $\mathcal{E}$ are $(M,N)$-absolute.
