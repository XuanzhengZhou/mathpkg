This proposition provides the technical heart of the downward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem. Given any set of formulas $\mathcal{E}$ and any model $N$ containing a subset $M_0$, one can find an intermediate submodel $M$ (with $M_0 \subset M \subset N$) whose cardinality is bounded by the sum of the cardinalities of $M_0$, $\mathcal{E}$, and a countable factor, such that every formula in $\mathcal{E}$ is $(M,N)$-absolute. The construction iteratively closes $M_0$ under witnesses for existential formulas—if $\exists x P$ is true in $N$, a witness is added to $M$. The proposition immediately yields the classical Löwenheim–Skolem corollary: every countable-language theory with an infinite model has a countable submodel.
