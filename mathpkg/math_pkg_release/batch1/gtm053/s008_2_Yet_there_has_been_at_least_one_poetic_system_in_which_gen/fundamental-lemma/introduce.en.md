The Fundamental Lemma is the core constructive step in the proof of Gödel's Completeness Theorem. It states that any consistent, complete set of formulas containing the logical axioms, with a sufficient alphabet, has a model whose cardinality is bounded by the alphabet size plus countable infinity. The model is constructed as a term model: its domain consists of equivalence classes of constant terms, and the interpretation of function and relation symbols is defined directly from membership in the formula set. The proof then shows by induction that a closed formula is true in this model exactly when it belongs to the set.
