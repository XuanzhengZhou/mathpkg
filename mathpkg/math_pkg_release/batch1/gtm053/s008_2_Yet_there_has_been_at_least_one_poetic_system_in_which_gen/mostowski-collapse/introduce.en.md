The Mostowski Collapse Theorem provides a fundamental normalization for models of set theory. Given a class $N$ with a well-founded, extensional binary relation $\varepsilon$, the theorem constructs an isomorphism $f$ collapsing $(N,\varepsilon)$ onto a transitive class $(M,\in)$ where $\in$ is the genuine membership relation. This is crucial for the study of countable models of Zermelo–Fraenkel set theory: the downward Löwenheim–Skolem theorem yields a countable model, but elements of this model may not themselves belong to it (the membership relation may not be transitive). Mostowski's result replaces such a model with an isomorphic transitive one, enabling the "standard" interpretation of $\in$. The theorem is applied in Section 7.5 to obtain a transitive countable model of ZF, which is the starting point for forcing and independence proofs.
