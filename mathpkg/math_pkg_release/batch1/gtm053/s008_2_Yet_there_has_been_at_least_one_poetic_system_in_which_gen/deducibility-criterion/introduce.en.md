The Deducibility Criterion is a direct corollary of Gödel's Completeness Theorem that characterizes when a formula is deducible from a set of axioms. Part (a) states that deducibility is equivalent to truth in all models of bounded cardinality (bounded by the alphabet size plus countable infinity), unless the axiom set is inconsistent. Part (b) characterizes independence: a formula is independent of the axioms precisely when both the formula and its negation can be consistently added, which is equivalent to both having models.
