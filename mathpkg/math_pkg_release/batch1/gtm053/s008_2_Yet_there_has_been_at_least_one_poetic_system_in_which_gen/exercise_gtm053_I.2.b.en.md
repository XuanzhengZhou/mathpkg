Construct the kennings of maximum length that are deducible from all the simple kennings in the above text (the text of Section 2). Prove that it is impossible to deduce longer kennings.

The simple kennings available are:
- "storm of spears" for "battle"
- "tree of battle" for "warrior" or "man"
- "bush of the helmet" for "warrior" or "man"
- "thrower of swords" for "warrior" or "man"
- "giver of gold" for "warrior" or "man"
- "sea of the wagon" for "earth"
- "fire of war" for "gold"
- "sky of sand" for "sea"
- "field of seals" for "sea"

Recall the rule of deduction: any word in a kenning may be replaced by a (not necessarily simple) kenning for that word. A kenning is of maximum length if no further substitution (replacement of a word by a kenning for that word) can increase its length.
