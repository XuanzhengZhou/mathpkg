There does not exist a formula $P(x)$ in SAr with one free variable $x$ such that the set of expressions satisfying $P$ coincides with the set of true formulas of SAr. More generally, let $?Ar$ be any language of arithmetic with a finite alphabet containing the alphabet of SAr, where the rules for forming distinguished expressions and the standard interpretation of formulas extend those of SAr, and where for any formula $P(x)$ in $?Ar$ with a free variable $x$, the expression $x(P(x))\bar{k}$ is a formula in $?Ar$ interpreted by the same recipe as in SAr. Then the undefinability theorem holds for $?Ar$.
