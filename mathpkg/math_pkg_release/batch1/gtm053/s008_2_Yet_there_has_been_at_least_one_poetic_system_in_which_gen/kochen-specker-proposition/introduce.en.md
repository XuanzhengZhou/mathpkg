The Kochen–Specker proposition is a landmark no-go theorem in the foundations of quantum mechanics. It shows that for a spin-1 system (such as orthohelium), it is impossible to assign definite pre-existing values (hidden variables) to all spin projections simultaneously while respecting the quantum-mechanical predictions. The proof constructs a finite set $\Gamma$ of 117 points on the unit sphere $S^2$ such that no assignment of values 0 or 1 can satisfy the requirement that on every orthogonal frame (triple of mutually perpendicular directions), exactly two directions get value 1 and one gets value 0. This impossibility is purely combinatorial/geometric and does not depend on the details of quantum dynamics. The result implies that the classical picture of measurement as "revealing a pre-existing value" is untenable; it is a vivid example of a "metaphysical" theorem about the language of physics rather than about the subatomic world itself.
