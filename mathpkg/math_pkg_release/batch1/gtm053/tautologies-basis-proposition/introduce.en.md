This proposition (a form of the completeness theorem for propositional logic) states that all tautologies can be generated from a finite basis by substitution and modus ponens. The proof uses the Fundamental Lemma, which shows that for any truth-value assignment, a formula or its negation is deducible from the basis and the assignment. This result is used in the full completeness proof for first-order logic and in the analysis of Boolean-valued truth functions.
