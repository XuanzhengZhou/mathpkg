Let $I$ be a set, $\Omega = [0,1]^I$ with Lebesgue measure, and $B$ the algebra of measurable sets in $\Omega$ modulo sets of measure zero. The operations are: $a'$ (complement), $a \wedge b$ (intersection), $a \vee b$ (union), with $0$ the class of the empty set and $1$ the class of $\Omega$. $B$ is a complete Boolean algebra satisfying the countable chain condition: if $a_\alpha \wedge a_\beta = 0$ for all distinct $\alpha, \beta$, then $a_\alpha \neq 0$ for at most countably many $\alpha$.
