The axioms of equality formalize the standard properties of the equality relation: reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and substitutivity (equals can be substituted for equals in any formula). In any normal model (where $=$ is interpreted as actual equality), these axioms are true. They are typically included among the special axioms of any first-order theory that uses equality. The substitutivity axiom schema ensures that equal terms can be freely exchanged in any context.
