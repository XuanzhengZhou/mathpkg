The language $L_2$Real has an alphabet consisting of variable symbols $x, y, z, \ldots$; symbols for degree-1 functions $f, g, h, \ldots$; constants $0$ and $1$; degree-2 operations $+$ and $\cdot$; degree-2 relations $=$ and $\leqslant$; and the standard connectives, quantifiers, and parentheses. Unlike $\mathfrak{L}_1$ languages, $L_2$Real allows quantification over function variables: $\forall f(Q)$ and $\exists f(Q)$ are formulas.
