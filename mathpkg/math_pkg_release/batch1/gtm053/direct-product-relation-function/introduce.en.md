These definitions implement the standard mathematical concepts of Cartesian product, relation, and function entirely within ZF set theory. Functions are identified with their graphs (sets of ordered pairs), and the notation $W = f(U)$ abbreviates $\langle U, W \rangle \in f$. This reduction of functions to sets is essential for the formalization of mathematics in first-order set theory.
