In model theory, a theory is a satisfiable set of sentences closed under logical consequence. This is the semantic counterpart of the syntactic notion of a deductively closed set. A set of axioms for a theory is any subset from which all sentences of the theory follow. The study of complete theories and their models is the central focus of classification theory.
