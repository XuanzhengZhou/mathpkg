The alphabet of SAr consists of: $x$ (variable), $'$ (prime, to form $x, x', x'', \ldots$), $\cdot$ (multiplication), $\uparrow$ (exponentiation), $=$ (equality), $\downarrow$ (conjunction of negations), $(~)$ (parentheses), and $\bar{1}$ (the constant one). The language allows class terms $x_k(P)$ (naming the set $\{x \mid P(x)\}$) and formulas $x_k(P)\bar{k}$ (asserting $k$ satisfies $P$). The syntax is defined by an inductive hierarchy of term sets $Tm_{2i}$ and formula sets $Fl_{2i+1}$.
