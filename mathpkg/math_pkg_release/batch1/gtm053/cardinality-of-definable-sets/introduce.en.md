This proposition establishes a fundamental limitation of formal languages: the collection of all subsets of the domain that can be defined by formulas is bounded in cardinality by the size of the language's alphabet plus countably many variables. This limitation is the source of many important phenomena in model theory, such as the existence of undefinable sets and the Skolem paradox. In particular, if the domain is infinite and larger than the alphabet, "almost all" subsets are undefinable.
