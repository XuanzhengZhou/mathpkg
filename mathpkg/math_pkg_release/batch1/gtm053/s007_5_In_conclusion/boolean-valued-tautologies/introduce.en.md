This proposition generalizes the notion of tautological truth from the two-element Boolean algebra $\{0,1\}$ to an arbitrary Boolean algebra $B$. It states that if a logical polynomial $P$ is a tautology (i.e., evaluates to $1$ under every $\{0,1\}$-valued assignment to its variables), then $P$ also evaluates to $1$ under any assignment of values from any Boolean algebra $B$. The proof reduces to verifying that the basis tautologies are $\|\cdot\|$-true and that modus ponens preserves $\|\cdot\|$-truth, using the algebraic axioms of Boolean algebras. This result is foundational for Boolean-valued models and Cohen forcing (Chapter III).
