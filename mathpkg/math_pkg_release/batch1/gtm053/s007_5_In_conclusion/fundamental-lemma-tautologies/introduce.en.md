This lemma is the key technical device used to prove that every tautology is deducible from the basis tautologies (Proposition 5.1). The main obstacle to a direct induction on formula length is that subformulas of a tautology need not themselves be tautologies. The lemma overcomes this by introducing the $v$-transform $P^v$, which forces any formula $P$ to be ``$v$-true'' relative to a truth valuation $v$. It asserts that from the basis $\mathcal{F}$ together with the $v$-transformed hypotheses $\mathcal{E}^v$, one can deduce $P^v$ using only modus ponens, making induction on formula structure feasible.
