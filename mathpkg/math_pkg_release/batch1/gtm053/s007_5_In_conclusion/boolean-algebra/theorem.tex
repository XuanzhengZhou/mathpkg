A \textbf{Boolean algebra} $B$ is a set with an operation $'$ of rank one, with two operations $\lor$ and $\land$ of rank two, and with two distinguished elements $0$ and $1$, such that the following axioms hold:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $(a')' = a$ for all $a \in B$;
\item[(b)] $\land$ and $\lor$ are each associative and commutative;
\item[(c)] $\land$ and $\lor$ are distributive with respect to one another;
\item[(d)] $(a \lor b)' = a' \land b', \quad (a \land b)' = a' \lor b'$;
\item[(e)] $a \lor a = a \land a = a$;
\item[(f)] $1 \land a = a; \quad 0 \lor a = a$.
\end{enumerate}
