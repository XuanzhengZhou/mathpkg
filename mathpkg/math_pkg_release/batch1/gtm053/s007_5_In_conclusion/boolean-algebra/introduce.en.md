A Boolean algebra is an algebraic structure that captures the essential properties of logical operations and set-theoretic operations. It consists of a set $B$ equipped with a unary complement operation $'$, two binary operations $\lor$ (join) and $\land$ (meet), and distinguished constants $0$ and $1$, satisfying axioms of involution, associativity, commutativity, distributivity, De Morgan's laws, idempotence, and identity. Key examples include the power set of any set (with complement, union, intersection), the algebra of clopen subsets of a topological space, and the algebra of measurable sets modulo null sets in a probability space.
