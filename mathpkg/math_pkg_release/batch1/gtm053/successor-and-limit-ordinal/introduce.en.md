The distinction between successor ordinals (those with an immediate predecessor) and limit ordinals (nonzero ordinals without an immediate predecessor) is fundamental for definitions and proofs by transfinite induction. The three cases (zero, successor, limit) form the basis of every transfinite induction argument, mirroring the structure of ordinary induction on natural numbers.
