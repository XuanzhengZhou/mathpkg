This proposition formalizes the destructive power of a contradiction in classical logic: if a set of formulas $\mathcal{E}$ can deduce both $P$ and its negation $
eg P$ for some $P$, then it can deduce every formula. This is the principle of "ex contradictione quodlibet" (from contradiction, anything follows), also known as the principle of explosion. The proof relies on the tautology B2, which serves as the mechanism by which a contradiction propagates through the deductive system.
