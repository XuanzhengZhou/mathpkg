Hrushovski's theorem that ACFA is simple (but not stable) is a landmark result in model theory. It shows that the theory of algebraically closed difference fields in characteristic zero sits in the "simple" region of Shelah's classification hierarchy, below stable but still amenable to a well-behaved independence calculus (nonforking). The fixed field plays a role analogous to the field of constants in DCF$_0$, but with SU-rank 1 instead of Morley rank 1, reflecting the shift from stability to simplicity.
