Superstability is the middle tier in Shelah's stability hierarchy. While $\aleph_0$-stable theories are stable in all infinite cardinals, superstable theories are stable in all cardinals above a threshold ($\kappa \geq 2^{\operatorname{card} T}$). This distinguishes superstable theories from merely stable ones, which may fail to be stable at cardinals of low cofinality. Superstability thus represents a significant degree of tameness beyond ordinary stability.
