Let $\operatorname{DCF}_0$ be the theory of differentially closed fields of characteristic zero. The solution space $S_f = \{y \in K : f(D^n y, D^{n-1} y, \ldots, y) = 0\}$ of a differential equation of order $n$ is of Morley rank $n$. Furthermore:

\begin{itemize}
\item The nonorthogonality of the generic type of $S_f$ to the generic type of the field of constants $C_K$ translates into the statement that $S_f$ is parametrized by $C_K$. A typical example is a linear differential equation $f(D^n y, \ldots, y) = 0$, where $S_f$ is in a definable bijective correspondence with $C_K^n$.
\item For a generically chosen differential equation $f$, the definable set $S_f$ is orthogonal to the constants.
\end{itemize}
