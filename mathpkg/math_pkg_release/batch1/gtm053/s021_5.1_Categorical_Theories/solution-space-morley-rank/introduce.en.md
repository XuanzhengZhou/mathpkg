This theorem establishes a precise model-theoretic interpretation of the order of a differential equation: the solution space of an $n$-th order equation has Morley rank exactly $n$. Moreover, the nonorthogonality relation between the generic type of the solution space and the generic type of the constants captures whether the solution space is "parametrized by constants" -- linear equations are nonorthogonal to the constants, while generically chosen equations are orthogonal. This forms the foundation of the geometric analysis of differential equations in stability theory.
