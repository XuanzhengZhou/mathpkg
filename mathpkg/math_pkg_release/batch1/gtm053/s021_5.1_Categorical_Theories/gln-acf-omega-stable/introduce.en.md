This proposition shows that the general linear group over an algebraically closed field, viewed as a structure in the pure group language, inherits $\aleph_0$-stability from the field via definability. Moreover, Morley rank coincides with algebraic dimension for definable sets in this theory, so every definable set is constructible and its rank equals its Zariski dimension. This exemplifies the power of stability hereditariness for algebraic structures.
