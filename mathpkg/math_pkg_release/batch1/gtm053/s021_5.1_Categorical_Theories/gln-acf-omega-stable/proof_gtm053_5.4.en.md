The group $\operatorname{GL}_n(F)$ consists of $n \times n$ invertible matrices over $F$, which is a definable subset of $F^{n^2}$ defined by the non-vanishing of the determinant. The group operation (matrix multiplication) and inversion are polynomial maps, hence definable in the field language. Thus $\operatorname{GL}_n(F)$ as a pure group is definable in the field $F$. Since $\operatorname{ACF}_p$ is $\aleph_0$-stable, by the hereditariness of stability (see stability-hereditary-definable), the theory of $\operatorname{GL}_n(F)$ is also $\aleph_0$-stable. Therefore Morley rank is well-defined. By quantifier elimination in $\operatorname{ACF}_p$, every definable set in $\operatorname{GL}_n(F)$ (translated back to the field) is a Boolean combination of Zariski closed sets, i.e. constructible. The Morley rank of a constructible set in $\operatorname{ACF}_p$ coincides with its algebraic dimension.
