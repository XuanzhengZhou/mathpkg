Real closed fields provide a canonical example of an unstable theory. Since the real field carries a definable total order, and any theory with a definable order on an infinite set is unstable, RCF cannot be stable. This makes RCF fundamentally different from the theory of algebraically closed fields (ACF), which is $\aleph_0$-stable and $\omega$-stable, illustrating the sharp stability-theoretic contrast between the algebraic and real geometries.
