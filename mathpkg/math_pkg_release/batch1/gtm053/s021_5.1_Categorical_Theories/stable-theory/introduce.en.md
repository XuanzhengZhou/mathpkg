Stability is the fundamental dividing line in Shelah's classification theory. A theory is stable if there exists at least one infinite cardinal $\kappa$ in which the number of types is controlled. Stability guarantees that the forking calculus behaves well, preventing the uncontrolled "diversity" of types that characterizes unstable theories. Theories that are not stable (such as the theory of the reals with order) admit a definable linear order on an infinite set.
