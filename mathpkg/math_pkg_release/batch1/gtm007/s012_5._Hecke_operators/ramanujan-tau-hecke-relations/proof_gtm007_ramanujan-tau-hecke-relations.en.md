The space $M_6^0$ of cusp forms of weight $12$ has dimension $1$, spanned by the discriminant function $\Delta(z) = (2\pi)^{12} q \prod_{m=1}^{\infty} (1-q^m)^{24}$. By Corollary 3 of Proposition 12, $M_6^0$ is stable under all Hecke operators $T(n)$. Since the space is one-dimensional, $\Delta$ (or equivalently $F = (2\pi)^{-12}\Delta = \sum \tau(n)q^n$) is necessarily an eigenfunction of all $T(n)$. The multiplicativity and recurrence relations for the coefficients $\tau(n)$ then follow from Corollary 2 of Theorem 7 (formulas (79) and (80)) with $k=6$, giving $p^{2k-1} = p^{11}$.
