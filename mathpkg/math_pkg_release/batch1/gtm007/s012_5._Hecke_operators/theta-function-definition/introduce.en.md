The theta function $\theta_\Gamma(z)$ of a quadratic module $\Gamma$ encodes the representation numbers $r_\Gamma(m)$ --- the count of vectors $x \in \Gamma$ with $(x,x)/2 = m$ --- into a generating function: $\theta_\Gamma(z) = \sum_{m=0}^\infty r_\Gamma(m) q^m$. Defined on the upper half-plane with $q = e^{2\pi iz}$, it is holomorphic and serves as the fundamental link between lattices (and quadratic forms) and modular forms.
