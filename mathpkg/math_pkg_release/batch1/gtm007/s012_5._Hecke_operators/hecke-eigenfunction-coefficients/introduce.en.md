Theorem 7 is a central result in Hecke theory: for a nonzero modular form $f$ that is an eigenfunction of all $T(n)$, the first Fourier coefficient $c(1)$ is necessarily nonzero, and after normalizing $c(1)=1$, all coefficients equal the corresponding eigenvalues: $c(n) = \lambda(n)$. This means the $q$-expansion of a normalized Hecke eigenform is completely determined by the eigenvalues.
