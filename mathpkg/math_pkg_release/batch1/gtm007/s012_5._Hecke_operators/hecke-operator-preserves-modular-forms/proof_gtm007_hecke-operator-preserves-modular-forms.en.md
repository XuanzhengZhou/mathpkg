From Proposition 12, $T(n)f$ is a modular function. If $f$ is a modular form (holomorphic at infinity), then $c(m) = 0$ for $m < 0$. From the coefficient formula $\gamma(m) = \sum_{a \mid (n,m)} a^{2k-1} c(mn/a^2)$, if $m < 0$, then $mn/a^2 < 0$ for all $a$, so $c(mn/a^2) = 0$, hence $\gamma(m) = 0$. Thus $T(n)f$ is also holomorphic at infinity, i.e., a modular form. If $f$ is a cusp form ($c(0)=0$), Corollary 1 gives $\gamma(0) = \sigma_{2k-1}(n)c(0) = 0$, so $T(n)f$ is also a cusp form.
