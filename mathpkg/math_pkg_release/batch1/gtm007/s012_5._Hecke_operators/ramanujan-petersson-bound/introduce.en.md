The Ramanujan-Petersson conjecture, proved by Deligne in 1973 as a consequence of the Weil conjectures, gives the optimal bound for Fourier coefficients of cuspidal Hecke eigenforms: $|c(p)| \le 2p^{k-1/2}$ for all primes $p$, or equivalently $|c(n)| \le n^{k-1/2}\sigma_0(n)$. For the Ramanujan tau function ($k=6$), this yields $|\tau(p)| \le 2p^{11/2}$. The proof uses deep results from algebraic geometry (etale cohomology) and is one of the great achievements of 20th-century mathematics.
