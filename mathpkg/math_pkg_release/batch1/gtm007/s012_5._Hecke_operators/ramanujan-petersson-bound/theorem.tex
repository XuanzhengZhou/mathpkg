Let $f = \sum_{n \geq 1} c(n)q^n$, with $c(1) = 1$, be a cusp form of weight $2k$ which is a normalized eigenfunction of all Hecke operators $T(n)$. For each prime $p$, let the polynomial $\Phi_{f,p}(T) = 1 - c(p)T + p^{2k-1}T^2$ factor as
$$\Phi_{f,p}(T) = (1 - \alpha_p T)(1 - \alpha_p' T)$$
with $\alpha_p + \alpha_p' = c(p)$ and $\alpha_p \alpha_p' = p^{2k-1}$. Then (Ramanujan-Petersson conjecture, proved by Deligne in 1973):
$$|\alpha_p| = |\alpha_p'| = p^{k-1/2}.$$
Equivalently, $|c(p)| \leq 2p^{k-1/2}$ for all primes $p$, and $|c(n)| \leq n^{k-1/2} \sigma_0(n)$ for all $n \geq 1$.
