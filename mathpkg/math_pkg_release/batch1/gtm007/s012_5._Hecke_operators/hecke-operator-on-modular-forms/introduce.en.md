The Hecke operator $T(n)$ acts on modular functions of weight $2k$ through the lattice correspondence, with an extra factor $n^{2k-1}$. The explicit formula $T(n)f(z) = n^{2k-1}\sum_{ad=n, a \ge 1} d^{-2k} \sum_{b=0}^{d-1} f((az+b)/d)$ is derived by parametrizing sublattices of index $n$ by upper-triangular matrices. This action preserves modularity and is fundamental to the spectral theory of modular forms.
