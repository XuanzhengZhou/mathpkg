Let $m$ and $n$ be relatively prime integers $\geq 1$, and let $\Gamma''$ be a sublattice of a lattice $\Gamma$ of index $mn$. Then there exists a unique sublattice $\Gamma'$ of $\Gamma$, containing $\Gamma''$, such that
$$(\Gamma:\Gamma') = n \quad \text{and} \quad (\Gamma':\Gamma'') = m.$$
This follows from the fact that the group $\Gamma/\Gamma''$, of order $mn$, decomposes uniquely into a direct sum of a group of order $m$ and a group of order $n$ (Bezout's theorem).
