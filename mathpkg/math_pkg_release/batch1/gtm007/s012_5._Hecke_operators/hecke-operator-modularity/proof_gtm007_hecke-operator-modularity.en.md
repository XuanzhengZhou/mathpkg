Formula (71) (the explicit expression for $T(n)f$ as a finite sum of $f((az+b)/d)$ terms) shows that $T(n)f$ is meromorphic on $H$, hence weakly modular. If $f$ is holomorphic on $H$, each term in the sum is holomorphic, so $T(n)f$ is also holomorphic.

Formulas (72) and (73) follow from the corresponding identities (67) and (68) for the Hecke correspondences $T(n)$ on lattices, taking into account the numerical coefficient $n^{2k-1}$ incorporated into the definition of $T(n)f$. The identities for lattice correspondences are: $T(m)T(n) = T(mn)$ when $(m,n)=1$, and $T(p)T(p^n) = T(p^{n+1}) + p^{2k-1}T(p^{n-1})R_p$ (where $R_p$ is the operator multiplying a lattice by $p$). Applying these to a function $f$ on lattices and translating back to a function on $H$ yields the stated formulas.
