Proposition 11 establishes the fundamental properties of Hecke operators on modular forms: $T(n)f$ remains weakly modular of weight $2k$ and holomorphic if $f$ is. Moreover, Hecke operators form a commutative family satisfying $T(m)T(n) = T(mn)$ for coprime indices and the prime-power recurrence $T(p)T(p^n) = T(p^{n+1}) + p^{2k-1}T(p^{n-1})$. These relations are the algebraic heart of Hecke theory.
