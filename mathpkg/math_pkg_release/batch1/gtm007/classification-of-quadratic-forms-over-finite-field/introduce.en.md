The corollary of Proposition 4 and Theorem 1' shows that nondegenerate quadratic forms over finite fields are completely classified by rank and discriminant in $\mathbb{F}_q^*/\mathbb{F}_q^{*2}$ (which has only 2 elements). Thus for each rank $n \geq 1$, there are exactly two isometry classes, distinguished by whether the discriminant is a square or not.
