\textbf{Lemma B (Davenport-Cassels).} Let $f(X) = \sum_{i,j=1}^p a_{ij} X_i X_j$ be a positive definite quadratic form with integer coefficients satisfying:
\begin{center}
(H) For every $x \in \mathbb{Q}^p$ there exists $y \in \mathbb{Z}^p$ such that $f(x-y) < 1$.
\end{center}
If $n \in \mathbb{Z}$ is represented by $f$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, then $n$ is also represented by $f$ over $\mathbb{Z}$.
