Dirichlet's theorem: every arithmetic progression $a \bmod m$ with $\gcd(a,m) = 1$ contains infinitely many primes. The proof uses the orthogonality of characters to isolate primes in the given progression, the analytic properties of $L(s,\chi)$, and the crucial fact that $L(1,\chi) \neq 0$ for $\chi \neq 1$.
