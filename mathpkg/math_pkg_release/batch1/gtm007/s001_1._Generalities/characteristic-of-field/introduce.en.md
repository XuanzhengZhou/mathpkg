The characteristic of a field $K$ is defined by considering the canonical homomorphism $\mathbb{Z} \to K$ sending $n \mapsto n \cdot 1$. The image is an integral domain; its field of fractions then determines whether $\operatorname{char}(K) = 0$ (the field contains $\mathbb{Q}$) or $\operatorname{char}(K) = p$ prime (the field contains $\mathbf{F}_p$). In the nonzero case, $p$ is the smallest positive integer annihilating the multiplicative unit. This classification is fundamental for all subsequent study of finite fields.
