The Euler totient function $\phi(n)$ counts the number of integers between $1$ and $n$ that are coprime to $n$. A classic identity states that summing $\phi(d)$ over all positive divisors $d$ of $n$ recovers $n$ itself. This identity reflects the partition of the cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ according to the order of its elements: for each divisor $d$, there are exactly $\phi(d)$ elements of order $d$, and every element has some order dividing $n$.
