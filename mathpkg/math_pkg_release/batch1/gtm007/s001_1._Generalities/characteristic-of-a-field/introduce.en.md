The characteristic of a field is a fundamental invariant that classifies fields into two disjoint families: those of characteristic zero (containing an isomorphic copy of the rational numbers) and those of positive characteristic $p$ (containing the finite prime field $\mathbb{F}_p$). It is defined via the canonical unital ring homomorphism $\mathbb{Z} \to K$. In characteristic $p \neq 0$, the integer $p$ is the smallest positive integer annihilating the multiplicative identity $1_K$.
