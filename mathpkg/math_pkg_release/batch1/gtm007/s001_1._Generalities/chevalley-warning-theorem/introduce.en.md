The Chevalley–Warning theorem is a fundamental result about polynomial equations over finite fields. It states that if the sum of the degrees of a system of polynomials in $n$ variables is strictly less than $n$, then the number of common solutions is divisible by the characteristic $p$. A particularly useful corollary is that any such system of homogeneous polynomials (with no constant term) automatically has a nontrivial solution. The proof uses the power-sum lemma together with a clever combinatorial argument involving the polynomial $P = \prod_\alpha (1 - f_\alpha^{q-1})$.
