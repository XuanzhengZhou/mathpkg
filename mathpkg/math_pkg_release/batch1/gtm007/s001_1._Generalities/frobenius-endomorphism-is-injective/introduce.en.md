In a field of prime characteristic $p$, the Frobenius map $x \mapsto x^p$ is always a field homomorphism (the "Freshman's dream" holds because all intermediate binomial coefficients $\binom{p}{k}$ vanish modulo $p$). The map is injective since fields have no zero divisors. When the field is finite or perfect, the Frobenius map is in fact an automorphism; in general it is an isomorphism onto the subfield of $p$-th powers $K^p$.
