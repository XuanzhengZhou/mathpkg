This theorem provides the complete classification of finite fields. Part (i) shows that every finite field has prime power cardinality and its prime subfield is $\mathbb{F}_p$. Part (ii) establishes existence and uniqueness: inside a fixed algebraically closed field of characteristic $p$, there is exactly one field of each cardinality $p^f$, characterized as the splitting field of $X^{p^f} - X$. Part (iii) states that any two finite fields of the same cardinality are isomorphic, so one may speak of "the" finite field $\mathbf{F}_q$.
