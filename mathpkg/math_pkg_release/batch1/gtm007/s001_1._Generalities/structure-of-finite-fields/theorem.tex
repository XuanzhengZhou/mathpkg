\textbf{Theorem 1.}\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The characteristic of a finite field $K$ is a prime number $p \neq 0$; if $f = [K:\mathbb{F}_p]$, the number of elements of $K$ is $q = p^f$.
\item[(ii)] Let $p$ be a prime number and let $q = p^f$ ($f \geq 1$) be a power of $p$. Let $\Omega$ be an algebraically closed field of characteristic $p$. There exists a unique subfield $\mathbf{F}_q$ of $\Omega$ which has $q$ elements. It is the set of roots of the polynomial $X^q - X$.
\item[(iii)] All finite fields with $q = p^f$ elements are isomorphic to $\mathbf{F}_q$.
\end{enumerate}
