This theorem gives the complete classification of finite fields. Part (i) shows every finite field has prime-power cardinality $p^f$ and prime characteristic $p$. Part (ii) establishes existence and uniqueness: inside any algebraically closed field of characteristic $p$, there is exactly one field of $q = p^f$ elements, characterized as the splitting field of $X^q - X$. Part (iii) confirms that up to isomorphism, there is only one finite field of each prime-power order, justifying the notation $\mathbf{F}_q$.
