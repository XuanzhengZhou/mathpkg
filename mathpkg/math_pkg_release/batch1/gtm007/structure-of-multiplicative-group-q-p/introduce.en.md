This theorem gives the complete structure of $\mathbb{Q}_p^*$ as the product of the discrete valuation group $\mathbb{Z}$, a finite cyclic group $V$, and a pro-$p$ group $U_1 \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$. Consequently, $\mathbb{Q}_p^* / \mathbb{Q}_p^{*2}$ is a vector space over $\mathbb{F}_2$ of dimension $2$ for $p \neq 2$, and dimension $3$ for $p = 2$.
