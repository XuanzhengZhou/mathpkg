This theorem gives explicit formulas for computing the Hilbert symbol over $\mathbb{R}$ (sign-based) and over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ (in terms of Legendre symbols). The formulas for $\mathbb{Q}_p$ are analogous to the quadratic reciprocity law. For $p \neq 2$, the symbol splits into a "tame" part (Legendre symbols) and a "wild" part depending on the valuations $\alpha, \beta$ modulo $2$.
