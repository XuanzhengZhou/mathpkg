The modular $j$-invariant is the unique modular function of weight $0$ (i.e., invariant under $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$) that is holomorphic on $\mathcal{H}$, has a simple pole at $\infty$, and satisfies $j(e^{2\pi i/3}) = 0$, $j(i) = 1728$. It can be expressed as:
$$j(z) = \frac{1728 \, g_2(z)^3}{g_2(z)^3 - 27 g_3(z)^2} = \frac{1728 \, g_2(z)^3}{\Delta(z)}$$
where $g_2, g_3$ are Eisenstein series and $\Delta$ is the discriminant modular form.
