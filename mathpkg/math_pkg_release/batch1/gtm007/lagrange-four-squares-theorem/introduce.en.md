Lagrange's classical theorem: every positive integer can be expressed as $x_1^2 + x_2^2 + x_3^2 + x_4^2$ with $x_i \in \mathbb{Z}$. The proof writes $n = 4^a m$ with $4 \nmid m$, notes that numbers $\equiv 1,2,3,5,6 \pmod{8}$ are sums of three squares, and handles $m \equiv -1 \pmod{8}$ by writing $m-1$ as three squares.
