The three-squares theorem (Gauss-Legendre): a positive integer $n$ is representable as $x^2 + y^2 + z^2$ with $x,y,z \in \mathbb{Z}$ unless $n = 4^a(8b-1)$. The proof uses the Hasse-Minkowski theorem to establish representation over $\mathbb{Q}$, and the Davenport-Cassels lemma to lift to $\mathbb{Z}$.
