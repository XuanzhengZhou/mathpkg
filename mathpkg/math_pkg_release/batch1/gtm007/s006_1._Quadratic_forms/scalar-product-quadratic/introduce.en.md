When the base field has characteristic different from 2, every quadratic form $Q$ determines a unique symmetric bilinear form via the polarization identity $x \cdot y = \frac{1}{2}(Q(x+y) - Q(x) - Q(y))$. Conversely, $Q$ is recovered as $Q(x) = x \cdot x$. This bijection fails in characteristic 2, which is why Serre restricts to fields of characteristic $\neq 2$ throughout the chapter.
