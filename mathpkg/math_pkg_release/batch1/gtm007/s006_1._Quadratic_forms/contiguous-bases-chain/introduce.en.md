Theorem 2 states that in a nondegenerate quadratic module of dimension at least 3, any two orthogonal bases can be connected by a finite chain where consecutive bases are contiguous (share a common element). This is a crucial lemma for Witt's extension theorem: it allows one to build automorphisms step-by-step by moving from one basis to another through a sequence of bases that agree on at least one direction. The proof requires the field to have at least 4 elements, with the exceptional case $k = \mathbf{F}_3$ handled separately.
