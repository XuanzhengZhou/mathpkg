Let $V$ be a module over a commutative ring $A$. A function $Q: V \to A$ is called a \textbf{quadratic form} on $V$ if:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item $Q(ax) = a^2 Q(x)$ for all $a \in A$ and $x \in V$,
  \item The function $(x, y) \mapsto Q(x+y) - Q(x) - Q(y)$ is a bilinear form.
\end{enumerate}

Such a pair $(V, Q)$ is called a \textbf{quadratic module}.
