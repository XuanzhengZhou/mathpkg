A quadratic form on a module $V$ over a commutative ring $A$ is a function $Q: V \to A$ satisfying two axioms: homogeneity of degree 2 under scalar multiplication, and the condition that the associated polar form $(x,y) \mapsto Q(x+y)-Q(x)-Q(y)$ is bilinear. This is the most general definition of a quadratic form, valid over any commutative ring. When the ring is a field of characteristic not 2, the scalar product $x.y = \frac{1}{2}(Q(x+y)-Q(x)-Q(y))$ establishes a bijection between quadratic forms and symmetric bilinear forms.
