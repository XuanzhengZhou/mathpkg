\textbf{Theorem 1'.} Let $f$ be a quadratic form in $n$ variables. There exist $a_1, \ldots, a_n \in k$ such that

$$f \sim a_1 X_1^2 + \cdots + a_n X_n^2.$$

The \textbf{rank} of $f$ is the number of indices $i$ such that $a_i \neq 0$. It is equal to $n$ if and only if the discriminant $a_1 \cdots a_n$ of $f$ is $\neq 0$ (in other words, if $f$ is nondegenerate).
