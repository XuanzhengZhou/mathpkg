This follows from the corollary to Witt's theorem (Witt's Extension Theorem). Let $V$ and $V'$ be the quadratic modules corresponding to $f$ and $f'$. The equivalence $f \sim f'$ gives an isometry $\phi: V \to V'$. The equivalence $g \sim g'$ gives an isometry between the subspaces corresponding to $g$ and $g'$. By Witt's Extension Theorem, this partial isometry extends to an isometry of the whole spaces, and by restriction to the orthogonal complements, one obtains an isometry between the subspaces corresponding to $h$ and $h'$, hence $h \sim h'$.
