Corollary 1 provides three equivalent formulations of representation: (i) $g$ takes the value $a$, (ii) $g$ is equivalent to $h + aZ^2$, and (iii) the form $g - aZ^2$ represents 0. This equivalence is a powerful reduction tool: the problem of representing an element $a$ is reduced to the problem of representing 0 by a form in one more variable.
