An orthogonal direct sum $V = U_1 \hat{\oplus} \cdots \hat{\oplus} U_m$ is a decomposition of a quadratic module into pairwise orthogonal subspaces whose direct sum equals $V$. In such a decomposition, the quadratic form splits additively: $Q(x) = \sum Q(x_i)$. This is the fundamental tool for breaking quadratic forms into simpler pieces, analogous to block-diagonalization of matrices.
