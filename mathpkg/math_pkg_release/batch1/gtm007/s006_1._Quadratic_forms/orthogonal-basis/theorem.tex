A basis $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ of $V$ is called an \textbf{orthogonal basis} if $e_i \cdot e_j = 0$ for all $i \neq j$. This amounts to saying that the matrix of $Q$ with respect to this basis is a diagonal matrix:

$$\begin{pmatrix}
a_1 & 0 & \dots & 0 \\
0 & a_2 & \dots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \dots & a_n
\end{pmatrix}.$$

If $x = \sum x_i e_i$, one has $Q(x) = a_1 x_1^2 + \cdots + a_n x_n^2$.
