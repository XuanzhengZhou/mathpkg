A quadratic module having a basis formed of two isotropic elements $x, y$ such that $x \cdot y \neq 0$ is called a \textbf{hyperbolic plane}.

After multiplying $y$ by $1/(x \cdot y)$, we can suppose that $x \cdot y = 1$. Then the matrix of the quadratic form with respect to $x, y$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$; its discriminant is $-1$ (in particular, it is nondegenerate).
