A quadratic form is nondegenerate when its radical is trivial, i.e., no nonzero vector is orthogonal to the whole space. Equivalently, the discriminant (determinant of the Gram matrix) is nonzero in $k^*/k^{*2}$. Another equivalent condition is that the map $q_V: V \to V^*$ is an isomorphism, linking nondegeneracy to the duality between $V$ and its dual.
