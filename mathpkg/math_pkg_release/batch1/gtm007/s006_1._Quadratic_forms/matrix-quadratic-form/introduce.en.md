Once a basis is chosen, a quadratic form can be represented by a symmetric matrix $A = (a_{ij})$ where $a_{ij}$ equals the scalar product $e_i \cdot e_j$ of basis vectors. The quadratic form then evaluates as $Q(x) = \sum a_{ij} x_i x_j$. This matrix representation is the standard tool for computational manipulation of quadratic forms.
