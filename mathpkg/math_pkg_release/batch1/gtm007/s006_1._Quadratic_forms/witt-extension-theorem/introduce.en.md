Witt's Extension Theorem is one of the foundational results in the theory of quadratic forms. It states that any isometry between subspaces of nondegenerate quadratic modules can be extended to an isometry of the whole spaces. This implies that the isometry class of a quadratic form is determined by its anisotropic part (the Witt cancellation theorem), and it underpins the definition of the Witt ring. The proof uses Theorem 2 (chain of contiguous bases) to reduce to the case where the subspace has dimension 1, then constructs a suitable reflection.
