If $V_1$ and $V_2$ are two vector subspaces of $V$, they are said to be \textbf{orthogonal} if $V_1 \subset V_2^0$, i.e., if $x \in V_1, y \in V_2$ implies $x \cdot y = 0$.

Let $U$ be a vector subspace of $V$, and let $U^*$ be the dual of $U$. Let $q_U: V \to U^*$ be the function which associates to each $x \in V$ the linear form $(y \in U \mapsto x \cdot y)$. The kernel of $q_U$ is $U^0$.
