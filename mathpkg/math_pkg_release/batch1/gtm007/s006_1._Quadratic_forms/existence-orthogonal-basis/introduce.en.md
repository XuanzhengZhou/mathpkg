Theorem 1 asserts that every quadratic module over a field of characteristic not 2 admits an orthogonal basis. This is the fundamental diagonalization theorem for quadratic forms, showing that any quadratic form can be written as $a_1 x_1^2 + \cdots + a_n x_n^2$ after a suitable change of basis. The proof uses induction on dimension, leveraging the existence of a non-isotropic vector when the form is nonzero.
