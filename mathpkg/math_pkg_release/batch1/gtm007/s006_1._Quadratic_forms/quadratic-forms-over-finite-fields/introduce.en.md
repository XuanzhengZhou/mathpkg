Proposition 4 characterizes the representation properties of quadratic forms over finite fields of odd characteristic: any form of rank at least 2 represents every nonzero element, and any form of rank at least 3 represents 0 (hence is universal). This is a remarkable simplification compared to general fields and follows from the fact that every element of a finite field is a sum of two squares.
