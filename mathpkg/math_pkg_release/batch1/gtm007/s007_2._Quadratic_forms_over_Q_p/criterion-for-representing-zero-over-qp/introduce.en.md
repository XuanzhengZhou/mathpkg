This theorem gives a complete criterion for a nondegenerate quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ to represent zero nontrivially (i.e., to be isotropic). The answer depends only on the rank $n$, the discriminant $d$, and the $arepsilon$-invariant of the form. The proof proceeds by diagonalizing the form and analyzing the cases $n = 2, 3, 4$, and $n \geq 5$ separately, using the structure of $k^*/k^{*2}$ and properties of the Hilbert symbol. A notable consequence is that every quadratic form in at least five variables over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ is automatically isotropic — in stark contrast to the real case.
