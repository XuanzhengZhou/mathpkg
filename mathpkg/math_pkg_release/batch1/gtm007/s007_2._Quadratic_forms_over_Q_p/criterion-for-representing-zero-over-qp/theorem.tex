Let $f$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form of rank $n$ over $k = \mathbb{Q}_p$, with discriminant $d = d(f) \in k^*/k^{*2}$ and invariant $\varepsilon = \varepsilon(f) \in \{\pm 1\}$. Then $f$ represents $0$ (nontrivially) if and only if one of the following holds:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $n = 2$ and $d = -1$ (in $k^*/k^{*2}$),
\item[(ii)] $n = 3$ and $(-1, -d) = \varepsilon$,
\item[(iii)] $n = 4$ and either $d \neq 1$, or $d = 1$ and $\varepsilon = (-1, -1)$,
\item[(iv)] $n \geq 5$.
\end{itemize}

In particular, every quadratic form in at least $5$ variables over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ represents $0$ nontrivially.
