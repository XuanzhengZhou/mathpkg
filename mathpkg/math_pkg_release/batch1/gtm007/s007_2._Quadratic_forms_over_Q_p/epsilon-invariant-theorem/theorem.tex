**Theorem 5.** Let $(V,Q)$ be a quadratic module of rank $n$ over the $p$-adic field $\mathbf{Q}_p$. Let $e = (e_1,\ldots,e_n)$ be an orthogonal basis of $V$ and put $a_i = e_i \cdot e_i$. The number

$$\varepsilon(e) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j)$$

does not depend on the choice of the orthogonal basis $e$.

Consequently, we write $\varepsilon(Q)$ (instead of $\varepsilon(e)$) and call it the \emph{epsilon invariant} of the quadratic module $(V,Q)$.
