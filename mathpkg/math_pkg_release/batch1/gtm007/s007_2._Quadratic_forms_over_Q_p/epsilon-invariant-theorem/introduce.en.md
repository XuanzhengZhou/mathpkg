Theorem 5 establishes that $\varepsilon(e)$, defined as the product of Hilbert symbols $\prod_{i<j}(a_i,a_j)$ over an orthogonal basis, is independent of the particular orthogonal basis chosen. This justifies defining $\varepsilon(Q)$ as a genuine invariant of the quadratic module $(V,Q)$. The proof proceeds by showing that when changing one basis vector at a time, the value of $\varepsilon(e)$ remains unchanged, using properties of Hilbert symbols and induction on the orthogonal complement.
