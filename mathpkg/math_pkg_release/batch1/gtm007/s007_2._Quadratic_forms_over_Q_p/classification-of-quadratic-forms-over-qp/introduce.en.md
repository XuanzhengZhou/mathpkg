Theorem 7 gives a complete classification of nondegenerate quadratic forms over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ up to equivalence. The three invariants — rank, discriminant, and $arepsilon$ — completely determine the equivalence class. The proof proceeds by induction on the rank, using the fact (from the corollary to Theorem 6) that equivalent forms represent the same elements, and applying Witt's cancellation theorem. Combined with Proposition 6 (which characterizes which triples $(n, d, arepsilon)$ can occur), this yields a finite classification: there are only finitely many equivalence classes of quadratic forms of a given rank over $\mathbf{Q}_p$.
