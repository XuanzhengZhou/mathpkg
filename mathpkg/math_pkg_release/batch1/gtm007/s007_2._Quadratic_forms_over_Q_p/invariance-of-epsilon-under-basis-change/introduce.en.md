Theorem 5 states that the product $\varepsilon(e) = \prod_{i<j} (a_i, a_j)$ of Hilbert symbols computed from the diagonal entries of any orthogonal basis does not depend on the particular choice of basis. This invariance is what makes $\varepsilon(Q)$ a legitimate invariant of the quadratic module, alongside the discriminant $d(Q)$. The proof proceeds by induction on the rank $n$, using properties of the Hilbert symbol to relate the values obtained from two different orthogonal bases.
