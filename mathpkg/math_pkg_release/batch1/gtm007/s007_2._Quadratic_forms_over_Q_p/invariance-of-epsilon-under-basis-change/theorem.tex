Let $(V, Q)$ be a quadratic module of rank $n$ over $k = \mathbb{Q}_p$. Let $e = (e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ be an orthogonal basis of $V$ with $a_i = e_i \cdot e_i$, and define
$$\varepsilon(e) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j).$$
If $e' = (e'_1, \ldots, e'_n)$ is another orthogonal basis of $V$, then
$$\varepsilon(e') = \varepsilon(e).$$
Consequently, the value $\varepsilon(Q) = \varepsilon(e)$ is an invariant of the quadratic module, taking values in $\{\pm 1\}$.
