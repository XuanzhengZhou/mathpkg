This corollary to Theorem 7 gives the exact number of distinct equivalence classes of nondegenerate quadratic forms of a given rank over $\mathbf{Q}_p$. Since the rank, discriminant, and $arepsilon$-invariant completely classify such forms, and the possible values of these invariants are constrained by Proposition 6, one can count the classes combinatorially. The answer depends on whether $p = 2$ or $p 
eq 2$, reflecting the different structures of $\mathbf{Q}_p^*/ \mathbf{Q}_p^{*2}$ (dimension $2$ over $\mathbf{F}_2$ when $p 
eq 2$, dimension $3$ when $p = 2$).
