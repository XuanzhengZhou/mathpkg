This corollary to Theorem 6 characterizes exactly when a nondegenerate quadratic form $f$ over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ represents a given nonzero element $a$. The criterion reduces to checking simple conditions involving the discriminant $d(f)$, the $arepsilon$-invariant, and the Hilbert symbol. The proof applies Theorem 6 to the augmented form $f_a = f \oplus (-a)Z^2$, using the fact that $f$ represents $a$ if and only if $f_a$ represents $0$. Together with Theorem 6, this gives a complete local representation theory for quadratic forms over $\mathbf{Q}_p$.
