Proposition 6 characterizes exactly which triples $(n, d, \varepsilon)$ can be realized as invariants of a nondegenerate quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}_p$. The only obstruction occurs in rank $2$: the combination $d = -1$ and $\varepsilon = -1$ is impossible because for a binary form $f \sim aX^2 + bY^2$ with $d = -1$, we necessarily have $\varepsilon = (a, b) = (a, -a) = 1$. For all $n \geq 3$, any pair $(d, \varepsilon)$ is realizable. The proof constructs explicit forms case by case.
