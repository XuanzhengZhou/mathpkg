**Theorem 6.** Let $f$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form in $n$ variables over $k = \mathbf{Q}_p$, with discriminant $d = d(f) \in k^*/k^{*2}$ and invariant $\varepsilon = \varepsilon(f) \in \{\pm 1\}$.

Then $f$ represents $0$ nontrivially (i.e., there exists $x \neq 0$ with $f(x) = 0$) if and only if one of the following holds:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $n = 2$ and $d = -1$ in $k^*/k^{*2}$ (equivalently, $-d$ is a square);
\item[(ii)] $n = 3$ and $\varepsilon = (-1, -d)$;
\item[(iii)] $n = 4$ and either $d \neq 1$ or $d = 1$ and $\varepsilon = (-1, -1)$;
\item[(iv)] $n \geq 5$.
\end{itemize}

In particular, all forms in at least $5$ variables represent $0$ nontrivially.
