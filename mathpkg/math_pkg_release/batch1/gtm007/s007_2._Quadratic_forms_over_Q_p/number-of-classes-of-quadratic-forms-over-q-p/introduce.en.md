This corollary follows from Theorem 7 (classification by rank $n$, discriminant $d$, and $\varepsilon$) together with Proposition 6 (which invariants are realizable). For $p \neq 2$, the square class group $k^*/k^{*2}$ has $4$ elements, and $\varepsilon$ can take $2$ values, but Proposition 6 restricts the allowed pairs. When $n \geq 3$, all pairs $(d, \varepsilon)$ are realizable except one impossiblity when $n = 2$, yielding $4$ classes for $p \neq 2$ ($8$ for $p = 2$) for each fixed $n \geq 3$. For small $n$, the count is adjusted by the constraints in Proposition 6.
