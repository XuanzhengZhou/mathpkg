The epsilon invariant $\varepsilon(Q)$ is a key invariant of quadratic forms over the $p$-adic field $\mathbf{Q}_p$, taking values in $\{\pm 1\}$. It is defined as the product $\prod_{i<j} (a_i, a_j)$ of Hilbert symbols over all pairs of coefficients in any diagonalization of the form. Together with the rank and discriminant $d(Q)$, the epsilon invariant completely classifies quadratic forms over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ up to equivalence.
