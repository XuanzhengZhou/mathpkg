The discriminant $d(Q)$ of a quadratic module $(V,Q)$ over the $p$-adic field $\mathbb{Q}_p$ is the product of the diagonal entries $a_i = e_i \cdot e_i$ from any orthogonal basis, taken as an element of the square-class group $k^*/k^{*2}$. It is one of the two fundamental invariants used to classify quadratic forms over $p$-adic fields, alongside the Hilbert symbol product $\varepsilon(Q)$. The discriminant is well-defined because a change of orthogonal basis does not affect its class in $k^*/k^{*2}$.
