Theorem 7 gives a complete classification of nondegenerate quadratic forms over the $p$-adic field $\mathbf{Q}_p$ up to equivalence. Two forms are equivalent precisely when they share the same three invariants: rank, discriminant $d$ (in the square class group $k^*/k^{*2}$), and epsilon invariant $\varepsilon$. The proof uses the corollary to Theorem 6 to show that forms with equal invariants represent exactly the same elements, and then constructs an equivalence by induction on the rank via Witt's theorem.
