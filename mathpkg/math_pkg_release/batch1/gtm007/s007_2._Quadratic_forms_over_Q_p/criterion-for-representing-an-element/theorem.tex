**Corollary.** Let $f$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form in $n$ variables over $k = \mathbf{Q}_p$, with discriminant $d = d(f)$ and invariant $\varepsilon = \varepsilon(f)$. Let $a \in k^*/k^{*2}$. In order that $f$ represent $a$ (i.e., there exists $x$ with $f(x) \equiv a \bmod k^{*2}$) it is necessary and sufficient that:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $n = 1$ and $a = d$;
\item[(ii)] $n = 2$ and $(a, -d) = \varepsilon$;
\item[(iii)] $n = 3$ and either $a \neq -d$ or $a = -d$ and $(-1, -d) = \varepsilon$;
\item[(iv)] $n \geq 4$.
\end{itemize}

(Here $a$ and $d$ are viewed as elements of $k^*/k^{*2}$; the inequality $a \neq -d$ means that $a$ is not equal to the product of $-d$ by a square.)
