This corollary derives from Theorem 6 a complete criterion for when a quadratic form $f$ over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ represents a given nontrivial square class $a$. The proof applies Theorem 6 to the augmented form $f_a = f \oplus a Z^2$ and translates the zero-representation condition using the formulas $d(f_a) = -ad$ and $\varepsilon(f_a) = (-a, d)\varepsilon$. Notably, any form in at least $4$ variables represents every element of $k^*/k^{*2}$.
