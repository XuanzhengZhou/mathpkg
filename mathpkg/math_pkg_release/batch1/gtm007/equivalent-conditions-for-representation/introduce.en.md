Three equivalent formulations of "$g$ represents $a$": (i) direct representation by a nonzero vector, (ii) $g$ is equivalent to $h + aZ^2$, (iii) the augmented form $g - aZ^2$ represents zero. This equivalence is used constantly in the classification of quadratic forms.
