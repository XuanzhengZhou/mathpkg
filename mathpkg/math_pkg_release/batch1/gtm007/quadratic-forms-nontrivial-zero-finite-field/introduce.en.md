A direct application of the Chevalley-Warning theorem: a quadratic form in $n \geq 3$ variables has degree sum $3 \cdot 2 = 6 < 3 \cdot 3$? Actually, a single quadratic form has degree $2$, and the theorem applies when $\sum \deg f_\alpha < n$, i.e., $2 < n$, so $n \geq 3$ suffices. This implies that every projective conic over a finite field has a rational point, a fact fundamental to the geometry of finite fields.
