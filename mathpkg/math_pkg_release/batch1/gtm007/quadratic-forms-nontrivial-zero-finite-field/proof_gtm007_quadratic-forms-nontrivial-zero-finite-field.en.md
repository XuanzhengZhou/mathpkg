A quadratic form is a homogeneous polynomial of degree $2$. By the Chevalley-Warning theorem, a system of polynomials $f_\alpha$ with $\sum \deg f_\alpha < n$ has $\operatorname{Card}(V) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$. For a single quadratic form, $\deg f = 2 < n$ when $n \geq 3$, and since $0$ is a trivial zero, $\operatorname{Card}(V) \geq p \geq 2$, giving a nontrivial zero.
