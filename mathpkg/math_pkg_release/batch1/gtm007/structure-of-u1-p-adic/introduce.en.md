The group $U_1 = 1 + p\mathbb{Z}_p$ is isomorphic to the additive group $\mathbb{Z}_p$ for odd $p$, with $\alpha = 1+p$ serving as a topological generator. For $p=2$, $U_1 \cong \{\pm 1\} \times \mathbb{Z}_2$, and the squares in $U$ are exactly $U_3 = 1 + 8\mathbb{Z}_2$.
