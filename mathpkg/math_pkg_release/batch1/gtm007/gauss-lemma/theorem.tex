\textbf{Lemma (Gauss).} Let $p$ be a prime number $\neq 2$ and let $S$ be a subset of $\mathbb{F}_p^*$ such that $\mathbb{F}_p^*$ is the disjoint union of $S$ and $-S$. (One may take $S = \{1, 2, \ldots, \frac{p-1}{2}\}$.) If $s \in S$ and $a \in \mathbb{F}_p^*$, write $as = e_s(a) s_a$ with $e_s(a) = \pm 1$ and $s_a \in S$. Then:
$$\left(\frac{a}{p}\right) = \prod_{s \in S} e_s(a).$$
