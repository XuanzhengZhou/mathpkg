Artin's conjecture about zeros of homogeneous polynomials over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ is a natural generalization of the results for quadratic forms. It turns out to be false in general (a counterexample exists over $\mathbb{Q}_2$), but is "almost" true: for each degree $d$, it holds for all but finitely many primes $p$ (Ax-Kochen theorem, 1965).
