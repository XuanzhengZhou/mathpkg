Artin conjectured that every homogeneous polynomial of degree $d$ in $n > d^2$ variables over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ has a nontrivial zero. This is false in general; a counterexample for $p = 2$, $d = 4$ is given by a form in $18$ variables related to an $18$-variable quadratic form that does not represent zero nontrivially over $\mathbb{Q}_2$ (by a norm form of a quaternion algebra). The Ax-Kochen theorem says Artin's conjecture is true for all but finitely many $p$ for each fixed degree $d$.
