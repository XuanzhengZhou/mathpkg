This elementary trigonometric identity expresses $\sin(mx)/\sin x$ as a product over the squared sines of the roots of unity. The function $\sin(mx)/\sin x$ is a polynomial of degree $(m-1)/2$ in $\sin^2 x$, whose roots are precisely $\sin^2(2\pi j/m)$ for $1 \le j \le (m-1)/2$. The leading coefficient $(-4)^{(m-1)/2}$ is obtained by comparing the coefficients of $e^{i(m-1)x}$ on both sides. This identity is a key ingredient in Eisenstein's proof of quadratic reciprocity.
