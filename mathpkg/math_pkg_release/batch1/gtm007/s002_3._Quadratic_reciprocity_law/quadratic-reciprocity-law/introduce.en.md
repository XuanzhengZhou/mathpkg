The quadratic reciprocity law, first proved by Gauss, is the fundamental theorem about quadratic residues. It relates the Legendre symbols $(l/p)$ and $(p/l)$ for distinct odd primes $l$ and $p$ through the sign factor $(-1)^{\varepsilon(l)\varepsilon(p)} = (-1)^{(l-1)(p-1)/4}$. Together with the supplementary laws for $-1$ and $2$ (Theorem 5), it allows efficient computation of any Legendre symbol by successive reduction, as illustrated by the example $(29/43) = -1$.
