Let $l$ and $p$ be distinct odd primes. Let $y = \sum_{x \in F_l} \left(\frac{x}{l}\right) w^x$ be the quadratic Gauss sum, where $w$ is a primitive $l$-th root of unity in an extension $\Omega$ of $F_p$. Then
$$y^{p-1} = \left( \frac{p}{l} \right).$$
Equivalently, $-y^{p-1} = \left( \frac{p}{l} \right)$ if one works with the sign convention from Lemma 1.
