Gauss's lemma gives a combinatorial expression for the Legendre symbol $(a/p)$. Choose the canonical set of representatives $S = \{1, 2, \dots, (p-1)/2\}$. For each $s \in S$, write $as = e_s(a) s_a$ where $e_s(a) = \pm 1$ and $s_a \in S$. The map $s \mapsto s_a$ is a permutation of $S$, and the Legendre symbol equals the product of the signs $e_s(a)$. This lemma forms the basis of Eisenstein's elegant trigonometric proof of quadratic reciprocity.
