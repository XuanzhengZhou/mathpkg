For an odd integer $n$, define $\varepsilon(n) \in \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ as $(n-1)/2 \bmod 2$, which detects the residue class of $n$ modulo $4$: it is $0$ when $n \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ and $1$ when $n \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$. Similarly, $\omega(n) \in \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ is $(n^2-1)/8 \bmod 2$, detecting the residue class modulo $8$: it is $0$ when $n \equiv \pm 1 \pmod{8}$ and $1$ when $n \equiv \pm 3 \pmod{8}$. Both are group homomorphisms from the respective unit groups onto $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$, and they appear as exponents in the supplementary laws of quadratic reciprocity.
