Over a finite field $F_q$ of characteristic $p$, the structure of squares is completely determined. When $p=2$ the Frobenius map $x \mapsto x^2$ is an automorphism, so every element is a square. When $p \neq 2$, the multiplicative group $F_q^*$ is cyclic of even order $q-1$, so its squares form the unique subgroup of index $2$, which coincides with the kernel of the homomorphism $x \mapsto x^{(q-1)/2} \in \{\pm 1\}$.
