The Legendre symbol $\left(\frac{x}{p}\right)$ is a multiplicative character on $F_p^*$ that detects whether an element is a quadratic residue modulo $p$. By Theorem 4, the map $x \mapsto x^{(p-1)/2}$ has kernel exactly the subgroup of squares, so the Legendre symbol equals $+1$ on squares and $-1$ on non-squares. It extends to all integers coprime to $p$ by reduction modulo $p$.
