The Chevalley-Warning theorem bounds the number of simultaneous zeros of a system of polynomial equations over a finite field. If the sum of the degrees is less than the number of variables, the number of solutions is divisible by the characteristic $p$. A striking corollary: any system of homogeneous polynomials of total degree less than $n$ in $n$ variables over a finite field has a nontrivial zero. This generalizes the fact that quadratic forms in $\geq 3$ variables over a finite field always have a nontrivial zero.
