This is the $p$-adic analogue of Newton's method for refining approximate roots. If $x$ is an approximate root with $f(x) \equiv 0 \pmod{p^n}$ and the derivative has valuation $k < n/2$, one can find a better approximation $y \equiv x \pmod{p^{n-k}}$ satisfying the congruence one power higher. By iterating, one obtains a true $p$-adic root.
