\textbf{Lemma (p-adic Newton's method).} Let $f \in \mathbb{Z}_p[X]$ and let $f'$ be its derivative. Let $x \in \mathbb{Z}_p$, $n, k \in \mathbb{Z}$ such that $0 \leq 2k < n$, $f(x) \equiv 0 \pmod{p^n}$, $v_p(f'(x)) = k$. Then there exists $y \in \mathbb{Z}_p$ such that
$$f(y) \equiv 0 \pmod{p^{n+1}}, \quad v_p(f'(y)) = k, \quad y \equiv x \pmod{p^{n-k}}.$$
