The quadratic Gauss sum is a fundamental object in number theory. Its square evaluates to $\pm l$, with the sign determined by $l \bmod 4$. This identity is the crucial step in the proof of the quadratic reciprocity law. When computed in characteristic $p \neq l$, it relates $\left(\frac{p}{l}\right)$ to $\left(\frac{l}{p}\right)$.
