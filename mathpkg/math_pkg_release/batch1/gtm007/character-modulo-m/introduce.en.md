A Dirichlet character modulo $m$ is a character of $(\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z})^*$. Examples include the Legendre symbol (mod $p$), $\varepsilon(n)$ (mod $4$), and $\omega(n)$ (mod $8$). Dirichlet characters are the coefficients of $L$-functions, the central tool in the proof of the theorem on arithmetic progressions.
