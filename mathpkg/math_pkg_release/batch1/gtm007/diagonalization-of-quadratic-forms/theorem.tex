\textbf{Theorem 1'.} Let $f$ be a quadratic form in $n$ variables. There exist $a_1, \ldots, a_n \in k$ such that $f \sim a_1 X_1^2 + \cdots + a_n X_n^2$. The rank of $f$ is the number of indices $i$ with $a_i \neq 0$; it equals $n$ iff the discriminant $a_1 \cdots a_n$ is nonzero, i.e., $f$ is nondegenerate.
