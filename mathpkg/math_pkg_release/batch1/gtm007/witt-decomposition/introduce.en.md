Witt decomposition: every quadratic form uniquely decomposes as a sum of hyperbolic planes and an anisotropic form (one that does not represent zero). The number $m$ of hyperbolic planes is the Witt index. The anisotropic part $h$ is the "core" of the form, determined up to isometry.
