The dual group $\hat{G}$ is itself a finite abelian group, isomorphic (non-canonically) to $G$. The duality $G \mapsto \hat{G}$ is a contravariant functor, and the map $G \to \hat{\hat{G}}$ sending $x$ to $(\chi \mapsto \chi(x))$ is a natural isomorphism (Pontryagin duality for finite abelian groups).
