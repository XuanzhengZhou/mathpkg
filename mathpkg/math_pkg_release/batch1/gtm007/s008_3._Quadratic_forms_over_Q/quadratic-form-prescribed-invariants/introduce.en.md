This theorem characterizes exactly which collections of local invariants $(d, arepsilon_v, r, s)$ can arise from a genuine quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}$ of rank $n$. The conditions are purely local: the signature determines the behavior at the real place, the discriminants at all places must come from a single $d \in \mathbf{Q}^*/\mathbf{Q}^{*2}$, and the $arepsilon_v$ must satisfy the Hilbert product formula. The proof constructs the form case by case ($n = 1, 2, 3, \geq 4$) using Theorem 4 of Chapter III (existence of rational numbers with prescribed Hilbert symbols) and the approximation theorem.
