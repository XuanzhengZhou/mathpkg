Let $f$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}$. The real quadratic form $f_\infty$ obtained from $f$ via $\mathbf{Q} \hookrightarrow \mathbf{R}$ is, by Sylvester's law of inertia, equivalent over $\mathbf{R}$ to a diagonal form

$$X_1^2 + \cdots + X_r^2 - X_{r+1}^2 - \cdots - X_{r+s}^2$$

with $r+s = n = \operatorname{rank}(f)$. The pair $(r, s)$ is called the \textit{signature} of $f$ (or of $f_\infty$). It is another local invariant of $f$.
