The signature $(r,s)$ records the numbers of positive and negative squares when the quadratic form is diagonalized over $\mathbf{R}$. It is determined by the real embedding $\mathbf{Q} \hookrightarrow \mathbf{R}$ and forms part of the complete system of local invariants. Unlike $d_v(f)$ and $\varepsilon_v(f)$, which are defined at every place $v$, the signature is specific to the archimedean place $\infty$.
