The product of the Hasse–Witt invariants $\varepsilon_v(f)$ over all places $v$ of $\mathbf{Q}$ always equals $1$. This is a consequence of the Hilbert reciprocity law (Chapter III, Theorem 3 of Serre's book). It provides a nontrivial global constraint on the possible local invariants of a quadratic form and is fundamental to the local-global classification theory.
