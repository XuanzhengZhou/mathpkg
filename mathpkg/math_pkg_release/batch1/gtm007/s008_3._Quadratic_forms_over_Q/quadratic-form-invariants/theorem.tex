Let $f \sim a_1 X_1^2 + \cdots + a_n X_n^2$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form of rank $n$ with coefficients in $\mathbf{Q}$. Let $V$ be the union of the set of prime numbers and the symbol $\infty$, and put $\mathbf{Q}_{\infty} = \mathbf{R}$. The following invariants are associated to $f$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The \textbf{discriminant} $d(f) \in \mathbf{Q}^*/\mathbf{Q}^{*2}$, equal to the product $a_1 \cdots a_n$.

\item[(b)] For each $v \in V$, the injection $\mathbf{Q} \to \mathbf{Q}_v$ allows one to view $f$ as a quadratic form $f_v$ over $\mathbf{Q}_v$. The \textbf{local invariants} of $f_v$ are:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item $d_v(f)$, the image of $d(f)$ under $\mathbf{Q}^*/\mathbf{Q}^{*2} \to \mathbf{Q}_v^*/\mathbf{Q}_v^{*2}$;
  \item $\varepsilon_v(f) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j)_v$, the Hilbert symbol product.
  \end{itemize}
  By the product formula, $\prod_{v \in V} \varepsilon_v(f) = 1$.

\item[(c)] The \textbf{signature} $(r, s)$ of the real quadratic form $f_{\infty}$.
\end{enumerate}

The invariants $d_v(f)$, $\varepsilon_v(f)$, and $(r, s)$ are sometimes called the \textbf{local invariants} of $f$.
