For a nondegenerate quadratic form $f$ over $\mathbf{Q}$ of rank $n$, one attaches three types of invariants that capture its arithmetic behavior. The discriminant $d(f)$ lies in the square class group $\mathbf{Q}^*/\mathbf{Q}^{*2}$ and is the product of the diagonal coefficients. For each place $v$ of $\mathbf{Q}$ (finite primes and the real place), one considers the localization $f_v$ and its Hilbert symbol invariant $\varepsilon_v(f) = \prod_{i<j} (a_i,a_j)_v$, with the global constraint $\prod_v \varepsilon_v(f) = 1$ given by Hilbert's product formula. Finally, the signature $(r,s)$ of the real form $f_\infty$ records the numbers of positive and negative squares when diagonalized over $\mathbf{R}$. These local invariants completely determine the quadratic form up to equivalence over $\mathbf{Q}$.
