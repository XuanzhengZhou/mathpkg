The Davenport–Cassels lemma provides a crucial bridge between rational and integral representations of integers by positive definite quadratic forms. Under the hypothesis (H) — which roughly says that rational points can be approximated by integral points with error less than 1 in the quadratic norm — any integer representable over $\mathbf{Q}$ is automatically representable over $\mathbf{Z}$. This lemma is a key ingredient in the proof of the three-square theorem.
