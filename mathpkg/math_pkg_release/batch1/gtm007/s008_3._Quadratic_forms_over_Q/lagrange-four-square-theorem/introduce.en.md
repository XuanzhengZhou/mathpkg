Lagrange's four-square theorem states that every positive integer can be expressed as the sum of four integer squares. It follows directly from the three-square theorem: write $n = 4^a m$ with $4 
mid m$; if $m 
ot\equiv -1 \pmod{8}$, then $m$ (and hence $n$) is a sum of three squares; if $m \equiv -1 \pmod{8}$, then $m-1$ is not of the forbidden form and is a sum of three squares, so $m = (m-1) + 1^2$ is a sum of four squares. This is one of the earliest and most celebrated results in additive number theory.
