Let $f \sim a_1 X_1^2 + \cdots + a_n X_n^2$ be a quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}$. Let $V$ be the set of prime numbers together with the symbol $\infty$, and put $\mathbf{Q}_\infty = \mathbf{R}$. For $v \in V$, the injection $\mathbf{Q} \hookrightarrow \mathbf{Q}_v$ allows us to view $f$ as a quadratic form $f_v$ over $\mathbf{Q}_v$.

The local invariants of $f$ are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The local discriminant $d_v(f) \in \mathbf{Q}_v^*/\mathbf{Q}_v^{*2}$, which is the image of $d(f)$ under $\mathbf{Q}^*/\mathbf{Q}^{*2} \to \mathbf{Q}_v^*/\mathbf{Q}_v^{*2}$.
\item The Hasse–Witt invariant
$$\varepsilon_v(f) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j)_v,$$
where $(\;,\;)_v$ denotes the Hilbert symbol in $\mathbf{Q}_v$.
\end{enumerate}

These are sometimes called the \textit{local invariants} of $f$, along with the signature $(r,s)$ of the real quadratic form $f_\infty$.
