Theorem 9 is the local-global principle for equivalence of quadratic forms over $\mathbf{Q}$: two forms are globally equivalent if and only if they are locally equivalent at every place. The proof uses induction on the rank together with the Hasse-Minkowski theorem (Theorem 8) and Witt's cancellation theorem. Combined with the classification results over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ (Theorem 7) and over $\mathbf{R}$ (Sylvester's law of inertia), this gives a complete set of local invariants that classify quadratic forms over $\mathbf{Q}$ up to equivalence.
