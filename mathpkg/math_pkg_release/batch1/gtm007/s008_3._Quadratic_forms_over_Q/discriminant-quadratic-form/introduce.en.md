The discriminant $d(f)$ of a quadratic form $f$ over $\mathbf{Q}$ takes values in the square class group $\mathbf{Q}^*/\mathbf{Q}^{*2}$. It is the product of the coefficients when $f$ is expressed in diagonal form, modulo squares. Together with the local Hilbert-symbol invariants $\varepsilon_v(f)$ and the real signature, the discriminant forms part of the complete set of invariants that classify quadratic forms over $\mathbf{Q}$.
