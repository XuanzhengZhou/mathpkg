Nondegeneracy means that if $(a,b) = 1$ for all $b$, then $a$ is a square. This property is essential for the global theory: combined with the product formula, it implies the existence of rational numbers with prescribed Hilbert symbols at finitely many places, a key step in the Hasse-Minkowski theorem.
