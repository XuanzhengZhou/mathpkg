This lemma is a technical tool used in the proof of Theorem 1 (computation of the Hilbert symbol for $p \neq 2$, case $\alpha = 1$, $\beta = 0$). It states that if the equation $z^2 - p x^2 - v y^2 = 0$ has any nontrivial solution in $\mathbf{Q}_p$, then it has a "nice" solution where both $z$ and $y$ are $p$-adic units and $x$ is a $p$-adic integer. The proof uses Proposition 6 of Chapter II, n$^\circ$2.1 to obtain a primitive solution, then argues by contradiction: if either $y$ or $z$ were divisible by $p$, primitivity would be violated.
