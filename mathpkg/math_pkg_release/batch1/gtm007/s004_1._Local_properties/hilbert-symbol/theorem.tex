Let $k$ denote either the field $\mathbf{R}$ of real numbers or the field $\mathbf{Q}_p$ of $p$-adic numbers ($p$ being a prime number). For $a, b \in k^*$, the \textbf{Hilbert symbol} $(a, b)$ is defined by:
$$
(a, b) = 1 \text{ if } z^2 - a x^2 - b y^2 = 0 \text{ has a nontrivial solution } (z, x, y) \neq (0, 0, 0) \text{ in } k^3,
$$
$$
(a, b) = -1 \text{ otherwise.}
$$
The Hilbert symbol is invariant under multiplication of $a$ and $b$ by squares, hence defines a map
$$
k^*/k^{*2} \times k^*/k^{*2} \to \{\pm 1\}.
$$
