Proposition 1 establishes the fundamental connection between the Hilbert symbol and norm groups: for $a, b \in k^*$, we have $(a, b) = 1$ if and only if $a$ belongs to the norm subgroup $N k_b^*$, where $k_b = k(\sqrt{b})$. When $b$ is a square, $k_b = k$ and the result is trivial. When $b$ is not a square, $k_b$ is a quadratic extension and every element $\xi = z + \beta y$ (with $\beta^2 = b$) has norm $N(\xi) = z^2 - b y^2$, directly linking the norm condition to the representability of $a$ by the quadratic form.
