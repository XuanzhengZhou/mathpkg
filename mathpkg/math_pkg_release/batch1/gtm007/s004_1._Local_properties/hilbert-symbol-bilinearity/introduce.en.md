The Hilbert symbol is bilinear: $(a a', b) = (a, b)(a', b)$ for all $a, a', b \in k^*$. This formula is stated as a remark in §1 but its proof is deferred to the following section. Combined with symmetry (which follows from the definition), bilinearity makes the Hilbert symbol a symmetric bilinear form on the $\mathbf{F}_2$-vector space $k^*/k^{*2}$, taking values in $\{\pm 1\} \cong \mathbf{F}_2$. This bilinearity is a crucial structural property used throughout the local and global theory of quadratic forms.
