Let $E$ be of type I. Choose $u \in E$ such that $u \cdot u$ is odd. The image of $u \cdot u$ in $\mathbb{Z}/8\mathbb{Z}$ is independent of the choice of $u$ (since replacing $u$ by $u+2x$ changes $u \cdot u$ by a multiple of 8). This invariant is denoted $\sigma(E) \in \mathbb{Z}/8\mathbb{Z}$. If $E$ is of type II, one sets $\sigma(E) = 0$.
