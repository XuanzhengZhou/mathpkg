The Hilbert symbol is symmetric and bimultiplicative, making it a symmetric bilinear form on the $\mathbb{F}_2$-vector space $k^*/k^{*2}$. The identity $(a, -a) = 1$ follows from the solution $(z,x,y) = (0,1,1)$ to $z^2 - ax^2 + ay^2 = 0$. The bilinearity $(aa', b) = (a,b)(a',b)$ is nontrivial and is proved in the next section.
