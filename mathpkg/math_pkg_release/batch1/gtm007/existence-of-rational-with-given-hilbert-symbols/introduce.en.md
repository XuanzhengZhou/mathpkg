This is the Hasse principle for Hilbert symbols: local solutions can be glued into a global one if they satisfy the product formula compatibility. The proof uses the Chinese remainder theorem, the approximation theorem, and Dirichlet's theorem on arithmetic progressions. It is a key ingredient in the proof of the Hasse-Minkowski theorem.
