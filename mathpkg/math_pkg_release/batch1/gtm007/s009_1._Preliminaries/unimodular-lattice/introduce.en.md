A unimodular (or self-dual) integral lattice is a free $\mathbf{Z}$-module of finite rank with an integral symmetric bilinear form whose Gram matrix has determinant $\pm 1$. This condition is equivalent to the bilinear form inducing an isomorphism between the lattice and its dual. Such lattices are the central objects of study in Chapter V, where they are classified up to isomorphism in the indefinite case.
