Let $E \in S$ be a unimodular lattice of type I (i.e., containing an element $u$ with $u \cdot u$ odd). The $\sigma$-invariant of $E$ is defined as the image of $u \cdot u$ in $\mathbf{Z}/8\mathbf{Z}$:
$$
\sigma(E) = u \cdot u \pmod{8}.
$$
This value is independent of the choice of $u$: replacing $u$ by $u + 2x$ changes $u \cdot u$ by $4(u \cdot x + x \cdot x) \equiv 0 \pmod{8}$. If $E$ is of type II (all norms even), we set $\sigma(E) = 0$.
