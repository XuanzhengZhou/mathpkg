The Hasse invariant $\varepsilon_p(E) = \pm 1$ is the local invariant of the $\mathbf{Q}_p$-vector space $V_p = E \otimes \mathbf{Q}_p$. It is trivial ($=1$) for all odd primes $p \neq 2$, and for $p=2$ it is given by $\varepsilon_2(E) = (-1)^j$ with $j = \frac{1}{4}(d(E) + r(E) - \sigma(E) - 1)$. This invariant connects the global lattice theory to local quadratic form theory.
