Let $n = 4k$ with $k \geq 1$. Let $V = \mathbf{Q}^n$ equipped with the standard quadratic form $x.x = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i^2$. Let $E_1 = \mathbf{Z}^n \subset V$, and let $E_0$ be the submodule of $E_1$ formed of elements $x$ such that $x.x \equiv 0 \pmod{2}$, i.e., $\sum x_i \equiv 0 \pmod{2}$. One has $(E_0 : E_1) = 2$.

Let $E$ be the submodule of $V$ generated by $E_1$ and by $e = (\frac{1}{2}, \ldots, \frac{1}{2})$. One has $2e \in E_1$ (since $n \equiv 0 \pmod{4}$) and $e \notin E_1$, hence $(E : E_1) = 2$. For an element $x = (x_i)$ of $V$ to belong to $E$, it is necessary and sufficient that

$$2x_i \in \mathbf{Z}, \quad x_i - x_j \in \mathbf{Z}, \quad \sum_{i=1}^n x_i \in 2\mathbf{Z}.$$

The quadratic module $E$ is an element of $S_n = S_{4k}$; we denote it by $\Gamma_n$.

When $k$ is even (i.e., when $n \equiv 0 \pmod{8}$), $e.e = k$ is even and this implies that $x.x$ is even for all $x \in E$; $\Gamma_n$ is thus of type II when $n \equiv 0 \pmod{8}$. One has

$$r(\Gamma_{8m}) = 8m, \quad \tau(\Gamma_{8m}) = 8m, \quad \sigma(\Gamma_{8m}) = 0, \quad d(\Gamma_{8m}) = 1.$$
