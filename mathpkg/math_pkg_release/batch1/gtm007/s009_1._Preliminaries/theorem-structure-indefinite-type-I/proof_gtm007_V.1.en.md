The proof proceeds by induction on $n$. By Lemma 4, $E \simeq I_+ \oplus I_- \oplus F$ with $F \in S_{n-2}$. The invariants of $F$ are $r(F) = n-2$ and $\tau(F) = \tau(E)$ (since $I_+ \oplus I_-$ has $\tau = 0$). If $n = 2$, then $F = 0$ and $E \simeq I_+ \oplus I_-$, which has $s = t = 1$. For larger $n$, the induction hypothesis applied to $F$ (which remains indefinite of type I) gives $F \simeq s' I_+ \oplus t' I_-$, hence $E \simeq (s'+1)I_+ \oplus (t'+1)I_-$. The parameters follow.
