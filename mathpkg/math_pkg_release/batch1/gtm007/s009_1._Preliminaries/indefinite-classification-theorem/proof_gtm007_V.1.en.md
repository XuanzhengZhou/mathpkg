By Theorems 4 and 5, every indefinite $E \in S$ is isomorphic to a direct sum of copies of $I_+$, $I_-$, $U$ (hyperbolic plane), and $\Gamma_8$ (for type II). Since the number of copies of each is determined by the rank $r(E)$ and index $\tau(E)$, and the type determines whether $\Gamma_8$ appears, the isomorphism class is uniquely determined.
