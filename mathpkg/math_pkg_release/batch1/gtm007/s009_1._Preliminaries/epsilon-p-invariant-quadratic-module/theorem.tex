Let $E \in S$ be a unimodular integral quadratic module and let $p$ be a prime number. The invariant $\varepsilon_p(E) = \pm 1$ is defined as $\varepsilon(V_p)$, where $V_p = E \otimes \mathbf{Q}_p$ is the $\mathbf{Q}_p$-vector space obtained by extending scalars, and $\varepsilon(V_p)$ is the invariant defined in Chapter IV, §2.1. One has:
$$
\varepsilon_p(E) = 1 \text{ if } p \neq 2,
$$
$$
\varepsilon_2(E) = (-1)^j, \text{ where } j = \frac{1}{4}\bigl(d(E) + r(E) - \sigma(E) - 1\bigr).
$$
