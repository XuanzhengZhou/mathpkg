The Grothendieck group $K(S)$ is constructed from the semigroup $K_+(S)$ of stable isomorphism classes by the standard group-completion procedure (as one defines $\mathbf{Z}$ from $\mathbf{Z}_+$). Elements are formal differences $(E)-(F)$ of quadratic modules. The group $K(S)$ turns out to be isomorphic to $\{(a,b) \in \mathbf{Z}^2 : a \equiv b \pmod{2}\}$ via the rank and index invariants.
