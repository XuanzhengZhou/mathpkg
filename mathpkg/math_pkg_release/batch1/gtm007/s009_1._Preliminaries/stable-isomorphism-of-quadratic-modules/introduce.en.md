Two integral quadratic modules $E, E' \in S$ are stably isomorphic if there exists some $F \in S$ such that $E \oplus F \simeq E' \oplus F$. This is an equivalence relation that is weaker than isomorphism. The stable isomorphism classes form a commutative semigroup $K_+(S)$ under orthogonal direct sum, from which the Grothendieck group $K(S)$ is constructed.
