The discriminant $d(E)$ of an integral quadratic module is the determinant of the Gram matrix $(e_i.e_j)$ with respect to any basis. It is well-defined (independent of basis choice) because a change of basis over $\mathbf{Z}$ multiplies the determinant by $(\det X)^2 = 1$. For $E \in S$, one always has $d(E) = \pm 1$, and its sign is $(-1)^s = (-1)^{(r(E) - \tau(E))/2}$.
