If $E = 0$, the statement is clear. Otherwise, one of $E \oplus I_+$ or $E \oplus I_-$ is indefinite (since $I_+$ has signature $(1,0)$ and $I_-$ has signature $(0,1)$). Suppose $E \oplus I_+$ is indefinite. Since $E$ and $E'$ have the same signature, $E' \oplus I_+$ is also indefinite. By applying Theorem 4, both are isomorphic to $s I_+ \oplus t I_-$ with $s, t$ determined by the rank and index. Since $E$ and $E'$ share the same invariants, the decomposition parameters match, giving $E \oplus I_+ \simeq E' \oplus I_+$.
