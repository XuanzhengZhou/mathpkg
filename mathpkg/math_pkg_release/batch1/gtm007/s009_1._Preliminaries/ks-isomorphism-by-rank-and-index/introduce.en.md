Corollary 1 identifies the Grothendieck group $K(S)$ explicitly: every element can be uniquely written as $s \cdot (I_+) + t \cdot (I_-)$, and the map $(r, \tau)$ gives an isomorphism from $K(S)$ onto $\{(a,b) \in \mathbf{Z}^2 : a \equiv b \pmod{2}\}$. The integers $s$ and $t$ are recovered from the rank and index by $r = s+t$ and $\tau = s-t$.
