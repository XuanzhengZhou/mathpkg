Every element $f \in K(S)$ can be written uniquely as $f = s \cdot (I_+) + t \cdot (I_-)$ with $s, t \in \mathbf{Z}$. One has $r(f) = s + t$ and $\tau(f) = s - t$, which shows that $s$ and $t$ are determined by $r$ and $\tau$. Thus the pair $(r, \tau)$ defines an injective homomorphism from $K(S)$ to $\mathbf{Z} \times \mathbf{Z}$. Its image consists of those $(a, b)$ for which $a \equiv b \pmod{2}$, since $a + b = 2s$ is even.
