This follows immediately from Corollary 1: two elements of $S$ are stably isomorphic if and only if they have the same image in $K(S)$, which by the isomorphism $(r, \tau)$ means they have the same rank and the same index.

[Note that this does not imply $E \simeq E'$. For example, $U = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ defines in $K(S)$ the same element as $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} = I_+ \oplus I_-$, but $U$ and $I_+ \oplus I_-$ are of different types.]
