Corollary 2 provides a practical criterion: two integral quadratic modules are stably isomorphic if and only if they have the same rank and the same index. This is an immediate consequence of Corollary 1, since the map $(r, \tau)$ is an isomorphism. Note that stable isomorphism does not imply isomorphism, as shown by the counterexample of $U$ and $I_+ \oplus I_-$ (different types).
