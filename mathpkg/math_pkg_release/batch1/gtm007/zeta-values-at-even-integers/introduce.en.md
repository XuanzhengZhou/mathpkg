Euler's formula expresses $\zeta(2k)$ as a rational multiple of $\pi^{2k}$. The proof uses the partial fraction expansion of $\cot z$ and comparing coefficients. This gives explicit values: $\zeta(2) = \pi^2/6$, $\zeta(4) = \pi^4/90$, etc.
