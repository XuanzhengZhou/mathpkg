If $d$ divides $n$, let $C_d$ be the unique subgroup of $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ of order $d$. Every element of $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ belongs to exactly one of the $C_d$ (namely, the subgroup generated by that element). The number of generators of $C_d$ is exactly $\phi(d)$. Hence $n = \sum_{d \mid n} \phi(d)$.
