Let $\mathbb{Z}_p$ be the ring of $p$-adic integers and let $U = \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ be its group of units.

\textbf{(a)} An element of $\mathbb{Z}_p$ (resp.\ of $A_n = \mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z}$) is invertible if and only if it is not divisible by $p$.

\textbf{(b)} Every nonzero element $x \in \mathbb{Z}_p$ can be written uniquely in the form
$$x = p^n u,$$
with $u \in U$ a $p$-adic unit and $n \geq 0$ an integer.
