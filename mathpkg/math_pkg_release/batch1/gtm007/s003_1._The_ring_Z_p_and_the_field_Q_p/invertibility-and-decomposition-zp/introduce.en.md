This proposition characterizes the group of units $U = \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ of the $p$-adic integers. Part (a) states that non-divisibility by $p$ is equivalent to invertibility, mirroring the fact that integers coprime to $p$ are invertible modulo $p^n$. Part (b) establishes that every nonzero $p$-adic integer admits a unique factorization $x = p^n u$ with $u$ a unit, providing a canonical decomposition that is fundamental to the study of $p$-adic valuations and the multiplicative structure of $\mathbb{Z}_p$.
