This lemma provides a structure theorem for short exact sequences of finite abelian groups with coprime orders. When $0 \to A \to E \to B \to 0$ is exact and $|A| = a$, $|B| = b$ with $\gcd(a,b) = 1$, the sequence splits uniquely: $E \cong A \oplus B'$ where $B'$ is the $b$-torsion subgroup, and $B'$ is the unique subgroup of $E$ isomorphic to $B$. This result is a special case of the Schur-Zassenhaus theorem for abelian groups and is used by Serre to analyze the structure of the unit group of $\mathbb{Z}_p$.
