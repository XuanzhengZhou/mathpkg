Let $f^{(i)} \in \mathbb{Z}_p[X_1, \ldots, X_m]$ be homogeneous polynomials with $p$-adic integer coefficients. The following are equivalent:

\textbf{(a)} The $f^{(i)}$ have a nontrivial common zero in $(\mathbb{Q}_p)^m$.

\textbf{(b)} The $f^{(i)}$ have a common primitive zero in $(\mathbb{Z}_p)^m$.

\textbf{(c)} For all $n > 1$, the reductions $f_n^{(i)}$ modulo $p^n$ have a common primitive zero in $(A_n)^m$.

(An element $x = (x_1, \ldots, x_m) \in (\mathbb{Z}_p)^m$ is called \textit{primitive} if at least one $x_i$ is a $p$-adic unit, i.e., $\inf(v_p(x_1), \ldots, v_p(x_m)) = 0$.)
