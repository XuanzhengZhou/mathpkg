This proposition (Proposition 6 in Serre) provides three equivalent formulations for the existence of nontrivial common zeros of a system of homogeneous polynomials over $\mathbb{Q}_p$. It connects the problem of finding rational $p$-adic solutions (a), integral primitive solutions (b), and solutions modulo $p^n$ for all $n$ (c). The equivalence is a powerful tool for reducing questions about the existence of $p$-adic solutions to finite (modular) computations, and is closely related to the Hasse principle for projective varieties.
