This corollary determines the structure of the quotient group $\mathbb{Q}_p^*/\mathbb{Q}_p^{*2}$ for odd primes $p$. It is a Klein four-group (type $(2,2)$) with canonical representatives $\{1, p, u, up\}$, where $u$ is a quadratic non-residue modulo $p$. This result follows from the decomposition $\mathbb{Q}_p^* \cong \mathbb{Z} \times V \times U_1$ (Theorem 2 in Serre) and the fact that $U_1 \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ has only squares as $2$-power elements when $p \neq 2$. The structure of this quotient is essential for the local theory of quadratic forms and the Hasse-Minkowski theorem.
