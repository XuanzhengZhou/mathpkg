Proposition 8 determines the topological group structure of $U_1$, the group of $1$-units. For odd $p$, $U_1 \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ is a free $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module of rank 1, generated by $1+p$. For $p=2$, the situation is slightly different due to the presence of $\pm 1$ torsion: $U_1 = \{\pm 1\} \times U_2$ and $U_2 \cong \mathbb{Z}_2$. The proof constructs an explicit isomorphism via exponentiation, showing that the discrete logarithm $\theta: \mathbb{Z}_p \to U_1$ given by $\alpha^z$ (with $\alpha = 1+p$) is an isomorphism.
