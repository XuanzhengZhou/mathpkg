This lemma states a basic but crucial property of projective limits of finite sets: if each term in a projective system is finite and nonempty, then the projective limit itself is nonempty. This is a compactness-type result in the category of sets, analogous to the fact that an inverse limit of nonempty compact spaces is nonempty (Tychonoff's theorem for the cofiltered case). The lemma is a key ingredient in many $p$-adic existence proofs, particularly in establishing the existence of solutions to equations via lifting arguments.
