This proposition summarizes the key topological properties of the $p$-adic field $\mathbb{Q}_p$: local compactness, the fact that $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is an open subring, and the density of the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ in $\mathbb{Q}_p$. These properties make $\mathbb{Q}_p$ a locally compact field, placing it alongside $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$ in the classification of locally compact fields. The density of $\mathbb{Q}$ means that $\mathbb{Q}_p$ can be viewed as the completion of $\mathbb{Q}$ with respect to the $p$-adic metric.
