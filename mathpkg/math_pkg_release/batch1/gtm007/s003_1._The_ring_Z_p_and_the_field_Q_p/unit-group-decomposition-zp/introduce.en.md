Proposition 7 describes the decomposition of the unit group $U = \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ into a product $V \times U_1$, where $V \cong \mathbb{F}_p^*$ is the finite group of $(p-1)$-th roots of unity (Teichmuller representatives) and $U_1 = \{x \in U : x \equiv 1 \pmod{p}\}$ is the pro-$p$ part. This decomposition is fundamental to understanding the multiplicative structure of $\mathbb{Q}_p^*$ and is used to determine which elements are squares. The subgroups $U_n$ form a natural filtration with $U_n/U_{n+1} \cong \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$.
