This lemma is the $p$-adic analogue of Newton's method for finding roots of real functions. Given an approximate root $x$ of a polynomial $f$ to precision $p^n$, and assuming the derivative $f'(x)$ has controlled $p$-adic valuation ($v_p(f'(x)) = k$ with $2k < n$), one can refine the approximation to precision $p^{n+1}$. By iteration, this yields a genuine root $y \in \mathbb{Z}_p$ with $f(y) = 0$. This is a constructive version of Hensel's lemma and is absolutely central to $p$-adic analysis.
