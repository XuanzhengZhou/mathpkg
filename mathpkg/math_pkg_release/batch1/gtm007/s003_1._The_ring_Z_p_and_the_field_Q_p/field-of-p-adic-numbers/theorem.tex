The field of $p$-adic numbers $\mathbb{Q}_p$ is the field of fractions of $\mathbb{Z}_p$:
$$\mathbb{Q}_p = \mathbb{Z}_p[p^{-1}] = \bigcup_{n \geq 0} p^{-n}\mathbb{Z}_p.$$
For $x \in \mathbb{Q}_p^*$, there exists a unique integer $n \in \mathbb{Z}$ and a $p$-adic unit $u \in \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ such that $x = p^n u$. The $p$-adic valuation is
$$v_p(x) = n, \quad v_p(0) = +\infty.$$
The $p$-adic absolute value (or distance) is defined by
$$d(x, y) = |x-y|_p = p^{-v_p(x-y)}.$$
This is an ultrametric (non-archimedean) absolute value, satisfying $|x+z|_p \leq \max(|x|_p, |z|_p)$.
