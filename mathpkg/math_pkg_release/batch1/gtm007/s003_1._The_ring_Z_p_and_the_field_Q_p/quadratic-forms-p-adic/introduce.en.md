Corollary 2 applies the $p$-adic Newton method (or Hensel's lemma) to quadratic forms. For odd primes $p$, if a quadratic form $f$ has coefficients in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ and its discriminant is a $p$-adic unit, then $f$ represents every element of $\mathbb{Q}_p$ (and similarly every element of $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is represented). This result is a crucial step in the classification of quadratic forms over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ and is used in the proof of the Hasse-Minkowski theorem for quadratic forms over $\mathbb{Q}$.
