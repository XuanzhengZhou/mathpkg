Gauss's "Eureka" theorem: every positive integer $n$ can be written as $\frac{x(x+1)}{2} + \frac{y(y+1)}{2} + \frac{z(z+1)}{2}$ for integers $x,y,z$. The proof applies the three-squares theorem to $8n+3$: if $8n+3 = a^2+b^2+c^2$, then $a,b,c$ are odd, say $a=2x+1$, $b=2y+1$, $c=2z+1$, and $n = \frac{x(x+1)}{2} + \frac{y(y+1)}{2} + \frac{z(z+1)}{2}$.
