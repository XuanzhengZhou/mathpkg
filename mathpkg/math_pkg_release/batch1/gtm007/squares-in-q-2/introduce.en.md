In $\mathbb{Q}_2$, the structure is more delicate because $U_1 = \{\pm 1\} \times U_2$ and $U_2 \cong \mathbb{Z}_2$. The squares in $U$ are exactly $U_3 = 1 + 8\mathbb{Z}_2$. Thus a $2$-adic number is a square iff its valuation is even and its unit part is congruent to $1$ modulo $8$.
