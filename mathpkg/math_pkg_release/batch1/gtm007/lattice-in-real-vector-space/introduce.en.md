A lattice is a discrete cocompact subgroup. In $\mathbb{C} \cong \mathbb{R}^2$, every lattice is of the form $\mathbb{Z}\omega_1 \oplus \mathbb{Z}\omega_2$ with $\operatorname{Im}(\omega_1/\omega_2) > 0$. The ratio $\omega_1/\omega_2$ gives a point in $H$, and the action of $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ on $H$ corresponds to changing basis.
