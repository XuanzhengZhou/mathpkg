The Hasse-Minkowski theorem is the fundamental local-global principle for quadratic forms: a form has a nontrivial rational zero if and only if it has a nontrivial zero over every completion $\mathbb{Q}_v$ (including $\mathbb{R}$). The proof uses induction on the number of variables, reduction to Legendre's theorem for $n=3$, and the theorem on the existence of rational numbers with prescribed Hilbert symbols.
