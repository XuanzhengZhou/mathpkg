\textbf{Theorem 6.} Let $f$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form of rank $n$ over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ with discriminant $d$ and invariant $\varepsilon$. Then $f$ represents $0$ if and only if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $n = 2$ and $d = -1$ (in $\mathbb{Q}_p^*/\mathbb{Q}_p^{*2}$),
\item[(ii)] $n = 3$ and $(-1, -d) = \varepsilon$,
\item[(iii)] $n = 4$ and either $d \neq 1$ or $d = 1$ and $\varepsilon = (-1, -1)$,
\item[(iv)] $n \geq 5$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, all forms in at least 5 variables represent 0.
