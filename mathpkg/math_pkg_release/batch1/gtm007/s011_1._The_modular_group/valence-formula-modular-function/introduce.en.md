The valence formula is the central structural identity for modular functions. It relates the total weighted count of zeros and poles of a modular function $f$ of weight $2k$ to the weight: the sum of $v_p(f)/e_p$ over $H/G$ plus $v_\infty(f)$ equals $k/6$. Since each term is nonnegative for a modular form, this imposes strong constraints on the existence of modular forms of small weight and underlies the structure theorems for $M_k$.
