Let $f$ be a modular function of weight $2k$, not identically zero. For each $p \in H$, let $v_p(f)$ denote the order of $f$ at $p$ (zero or pole). Let $e_p$ be the order of the stabilizer of $p$ in $G$: $e_p = 2$ if $p$ is congruent to $i$ modulo $G$, $e_p = 3$ if $p$ is congruent to $\rho = e^{2\pi i/3}$, and $e_p = 1$ otherwise. Let $v_\infty(f)$ be the order of $\tilde{f}(q)$ at $q = 0$.

Then
$$v_\infty(f) + \sum_{p \in H/G} \frac{1}{e_p} v_p(f) = \frac{k}{6}.$$

Equivalently,
$$v_\infty(f) + \frac{1}{2} v_i(f) + \frac{1}{3} v_\rho(f) + \sum_{p \in H/G}^* v_p(f) = \frac{k}{6},$$
where $\sum^*$ denotes summation over points of $H/G$ distinct from the classes of $i$ and $\rho$.
