The valence formula is the central result around which the dimension theory of modular forms is built. For holomorphic modular forms with no poles, the left side is nonnegative, forcing $k/12$ to be nonnegative, so $k \geq 0$. More strongly, it gives upper bounds on dimensions of modular form spaces.
