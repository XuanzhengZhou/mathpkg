Let $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ be the subgroup of $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{R})$ consisting of matrices with coefficients in $\mathbf{Z}$. It is a discrete subgroup of $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{R})$.

The group $G = \mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z}) / \{\pm 1\}$ is called the \textbf{modular group}; it is the image of $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ in $\mathbf{PSL}_2(\mathbf{R}) = \mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{R}) / \{\pm 1\}$.

The modular group acts on the upper half-plane $H = \{z \in \mathbf{C} \mid \operatorname{Im}(z) > 0\}$ by fractional linear transformations:
$$gz = \frac{az + b}{cz + d}, \qquad g = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z}).$$
