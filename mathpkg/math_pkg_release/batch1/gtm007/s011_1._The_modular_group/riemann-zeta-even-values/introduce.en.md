The values of $\zeta(s)$ at even positive integers are rational multiples of powers of $\pi$. These values appear naturally as the constant terms in the Fourier expansions of Eisenstein series. The connection between $\zeta(k)$ and Bernoulli numbers is a classical result going back to Euler and has deep connections to the theory of modular forms.
