The formula can be derived from the Fourier expansion of $G_k(z)$: the constant term $2\zeta(k)$ is linked to the $q$-series coefficients. Alternatively, Euler's original proof used the product formula for $\sin z$:
$$
\frac{\sin \pi z}{\pi z} = \prod_{n=1}^\infty \left(1 - \frac{z^2}{n^2}\right).
$$
Comparing coefficients of the Taylor expansion with the Bernoulli number expansion of $z \cot z$ yields the formula.
