The modular group $G = \mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})/\{\pm 1\}$ is generated by the inversion $S(z) = -1/z$ and the translation $T(z) = z + 1$. This is fundamental: any modular condition (invariance under $G$) can be checked by verifying invariance under $S$ and $T$ alone, as seen in Proposition 1.
