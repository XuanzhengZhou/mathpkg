A cusp form is a modular form that vanishes at the cusp $i\infty$. This vanishing condition implies that cusp forms decay rapidly as $\operatorname{Im}(z) 	o \infty$ and belong to $L^2$ with respect to the Petersson scalar product. The first nonzero cusp form for $\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ occurs in weight 12 and is the discriminant function $\Delta$.
