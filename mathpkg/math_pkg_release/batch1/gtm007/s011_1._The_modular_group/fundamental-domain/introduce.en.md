The fundamental domain provides a geometric understanding of the modular group action on the upper half-plane. The domain $\mathcal{F}$ is a hyperbolic triangle with one vertex at $i\infty$ and two finite vertices at $i$ and $ho = e^{2\pi i/3}$. The identification on the boundary (reflection across the imaginary axis) corresponds to the transformation $z \mapsto -1/z$.
