For even $k \geq 4$, the Eisenstein series
$$
G_k(z) = \sum_{(m,n) \in \mathbf{Z}^2 \setminus \{(0,0)\}} \frac{1}{(mz+n)^k}
$$
admits the Fourier ($q$-) expansion
$$
G_k(z) = 2\zeta(k) + 2\frac{(2\pi i)^k}{(k-1)!} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \sigma_{k-1}(n) q^n,
$$
where $q = e^{2\pi i z}$, $\zeta(k) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty 1/n^k$, and $\sigma_{k-1}(n) = \sum_{d \mid n} d^{k-1}$.
