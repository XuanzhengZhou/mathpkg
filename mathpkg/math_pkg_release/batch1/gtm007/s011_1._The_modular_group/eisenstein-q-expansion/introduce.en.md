The $q$-expansion of Eisenstein series reveals their arithmetic content: the Fourier coefficients are divisor functions $\sigma_{k-1}(n)$, which are multiplicative and encode deep number-theoretic information. This formula is derived using the Poisson summation formula or the Lipschitz summation formula.
