Let $\mathcal{R}$ be the set of lattices of $\mathbf{C}$ considered as an $\mathbf{R}$-vector space. Let $M$ be the set of pairs $(\omega_1, \omega_2)$ of elements of $\mathbf{C}^*$ such that $\operatorname{Im}(\omega_1/\omega_2) > 0$. To such a pair one associates the lattice
$$\Gamma(\omega_1, \omega_2) = \mathbf{Z}\omega_1 \oplus \mathbf{Z}\omega_2.$$
The map $(\omega_1, \omega_2) \mapsto \omega_1/\omega_2$ gives, by passing to the quotient under the action of $\mathbf{C}^*$ (homothety), a bijection
$$\mathcal{R}/\mathbf{C}^* \xrightarrow{\sim} H/G.$$
Thus an element of $H/G$ can be identified with a lattice of $\mathbf{C}$ defined up to homothety. Equivalently, $H/G$ is the set of isomorphism classes of elliptic curves $E_\Gamma = \mathbf{C}/\Gamma$.
