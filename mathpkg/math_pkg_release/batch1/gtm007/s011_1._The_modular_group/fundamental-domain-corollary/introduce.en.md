The fundamental domain $\mathcal{F}$ provides a tessellation of the upper half-plane. The quotient space $\Gamma ackslash \mathcal{H}$ is non-compact but can be compactified by adding a single cusp (at $i\infty$), yielding a compact Riemann surface isomorphic to $\mathbf{P}^1(\mathbf{C})$. This compactification is essential for the valence formula.
