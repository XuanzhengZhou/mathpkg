For every integer $k \geq 2$, the Eisenstein series $G_k(z)$ has the $q$-expansion
$$G_k(z) = 2\zeta(2k) + 2 \frac{(2i\pi)^{2k}}{(2k-1)!} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sigma_{2k-1}(n) q^n,$$
where $q = e^{2\pi i z}$ and $\sigma_{2k-1}(n) = \sum_{d \mid n} d^{2k-1}$ is the sum of $(2k-1)$-th powers of the positive divisors of $n$.

Equivalently, writing $G_k(z) = 2\zeta(2k) E_k(z)$ with $E_k(z) = 1 + \gamma_k \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \sigma_{2k-1}(n) q^n$, one has
$$\gamma_k = (-1)^k \frac{4k}{B_k},$$
where $B_k$ is the $k$-th Bernoulli number.
