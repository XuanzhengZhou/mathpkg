Lemma 1 provides the basic convergence estimate needed for defining Eisenstein series. Since a lattice in $\mathbf{C}$ is a discrete rank-2 subgroup, the number of lattice points within each annular region grows linearly, making the series $\sum 1/|\gamma|^\sigma$ converge precisely when $\sigma > 2$. This lemma justifies the absolute convergence of Eisenstein series $G_k(\Gamma) = \sum_{\gamma \neq 0} 1/\gamma^{2k}$ for all integers $k \geq 2$.
