Proposition 3 establishes a fundamental dictionary: points of the quotient $H/G$ correspond bijectively to lattices in $\mathbf{C}$ modulo homothety (scaling by $\mathbf{C}^*$), and hence to isomorphism classes of elliptic curves $\mathbf{C}/\Gamma$. This bridge between complex analysis, algebraic geometry, and number theory is the reason modular forms are central to all three subjects.
