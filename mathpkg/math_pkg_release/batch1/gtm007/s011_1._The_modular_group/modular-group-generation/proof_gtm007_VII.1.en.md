The action of an element $\gamma = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ on $z \in \mathcal{H}$ is $\gamma z = (az+b)/(cz+d)$. The translation $T$ sends $z \mapsto z+1$, and the inversion $S$ sends $z \mapsto -1/z$. Using the Euclidean algorithm, any matrix in $\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ can be expressed as a word in $S$ and $T$. The relations $S^2 = (ST)^3 = -I_2$ (in $\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$) give the presentation.
