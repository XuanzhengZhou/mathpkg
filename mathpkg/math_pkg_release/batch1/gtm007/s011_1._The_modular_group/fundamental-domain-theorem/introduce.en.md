The fundamental domain $D = \{z \in H : |z| \geq 1, |\operatorname{Re}(z)| \leq 1/2\}$ for the modular group $G = \mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})/\{\pm 1\}$ captures the essential geometry of the action of $G$ on the upper half-plane. Every point in $H$ can be mapped into $D$ by an element of $G$, and the only identifications on the boundary of $D$ occur through the transformations $S: z \mapsto -1/z$ and $T: z \mapsto z+1$. The points $i$, $\rho = e^{2\pi i/3}$, and $-\bar{\rho} = e^{\pi i/3}$ are the only points with nontrivial stabilizers, of orders 2, 3, and 3 respectively.
