This corollary follows directly from assertions (1) and (2) of Theorem 1. It states that the fundamental domain $D$ maps onto the quotient space $H/G$ (surjectivity), and that the interior of $D$ maps injectively into $H/G$, so each $G$-orbit in $H$ is represented by exactly one point in the interior of $D$, with only boundary points identified under $G$.
