The Eisenstein series $G_k(z)$ of weight $2k$ (for integer $k > 1$) is defined by summing $(mz+n)^{-2k}$ over all nonzero integer pairs. It converges absolutely and defines a modular form of weight $2k$. At infinity, $G_k(\infty) = 2\zeta(2k)$, connecting modular forms to special values of the Riemann zeta function. The Eisenstein series are the building blocks of the ring of modular forms.
