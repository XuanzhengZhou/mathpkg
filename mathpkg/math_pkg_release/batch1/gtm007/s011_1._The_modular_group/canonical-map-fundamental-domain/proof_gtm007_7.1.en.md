Assertion (1) of Theorem 1 states that for every $z \in H$, there exists $g \in G$ such that $gz \in D$. This means every $G$-orbit in $H$ meets $D$, so the canonical map $D \to H/G$ is surjective.

Assertion (2) of Theorem 1 states that if two distinct points $z, z' \in D$ are congruent modulo $G$, then they lie on the boundary of $D$. Thus no two distinct interior points of $D$ are $G$-equivalent, so the restriction of the canonical map to the interior of $D$ is injective.
