A modular form is a weakly modular function that is everywhere holomorphic, including at the cusp at infinity. Its $q$-expansion $\tilde{f}(q) = \sum a_n q^n$ has no negative powers. A cusp form additionally satisfies $a_0 = 0$, i.e. vanishes at infinity. The spaces $M_k$ and $M_k^0$ of modular and cusp forms of weight $2k$ are the central algebraic objects in the theory of modular forms.
