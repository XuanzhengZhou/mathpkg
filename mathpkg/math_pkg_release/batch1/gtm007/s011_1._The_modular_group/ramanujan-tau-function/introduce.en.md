The Ramanujan $	au$-function is one of the most famous arithmetic functions in number theory. Defined from the discriminant cusp form $\Delta$, it satisfies remarkable properties: multiplicativity ($	au(mn) = 	au(m)	au(n)$ for coprime $m,n$) and the Ramanujan conjectures ($|	au(p)| \leq 2p^{11/2}$, $	au(p) \equiv \sigma_{11}(p) \pmod{691}$, etc.), all now proved.
