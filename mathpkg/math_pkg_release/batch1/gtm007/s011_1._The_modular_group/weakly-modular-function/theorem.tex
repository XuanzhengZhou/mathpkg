A meromorphic function $f: \mathcal{H} \to \mathbf{C}$ is called \textbf{weakly modular of weight $k$} for the modular group $\Gamma = \operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})/\{\pm 1\}$ if it satisfies the transformation law:
$$
f\!\left(\frac{az+b}{cz+d}\right) = (cz+d)^k f(z)
$$
for all $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$. When $k$ is odd, this forces $f = 0$ (since $-I \in \operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ acts trivially on $\mathcal{H}$ but contributes $(-1)^k$).
