The discriminant modular form $\Delta(z)$ can be expressed as
$$
\Delta(z) = (2\pi)^{12} q \prod_{n=1}^\infty (1 - q^n)^{24} = (2\pi)^{12} \eta(z)^{24},
$$
where $\eta(z) = q^{1/24} \prod_{n=1}^\infty (1 - q^n)$ is the Dedekind eta function. The Fourier expansion
$$
\Delta(z) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \tau(n) q^n
$$
defines the Ramanujan $\tau$-function, which satisfies $\tau(1) = 1$.
