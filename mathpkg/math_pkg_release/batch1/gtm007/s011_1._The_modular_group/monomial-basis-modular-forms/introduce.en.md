Corollary 2 of Theorem 4 states that every modular form for $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ is uniquely expressible as a polynomial in $G_2$ and $G_3$. The condition $2\alpha + 3\beta = k$ reflects the weights: $G_2$ has weight 4 ($k=2$) and $G_3$ has weight 6 ($k=3$). This makes the graded ring of modular forms isomorphic to $\mathbf{C}[X, Y]$ with $\deg X = 2$, $\deg Y = 3$.
