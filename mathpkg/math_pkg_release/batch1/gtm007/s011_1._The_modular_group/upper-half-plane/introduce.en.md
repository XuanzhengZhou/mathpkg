The upper half-plane $\mathcal{H}$ is the natural domain for the theory of modular forms. The action of $\operatorname{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ on $\mathcal{H}$ preserves the hyperbolic metric $ds^2 = (dx^2 + dy^2)/y^2$, making $\mathcal{H}$ a model for the hyperbolic plane. Modular forms are holomorphic functions on $\mathcal{H}$ satisfying specific transformation laws under this action.
