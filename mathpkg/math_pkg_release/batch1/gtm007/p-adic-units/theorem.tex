\textbf{Proposition 2.}
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] An element of $\mathbb{Z}_p$ (resp. of $A_n$) is invertible if and only if it is not divisible by $p$.
\item[(b)] If $U$ denotes the group of invertible elements of $\mathbb{Z}_p$, every nonzero element of $\mathbb{Z}_p$ can be written uniquely in the form $p^n u$ with $u \in U$ and $n \geq 0$. (An element of $U$ is called a $p$-adic unit.)
\end{enumerate}
