A place of $\mathbf{Q}$ is either a prime number $p$ or the symbol $\infty$. Each place $v$ gives a completion $\mathbf{Q}_v$ of $\mathbf{Q}$: for $v = p$, this is the field of $p$-adic numbers $\mathbf{Q}_p$, and for $v = \infty$, it is the real numbers $\mathbf{R}$. The set $V$ of all places provides the framework for the local-global principle (Hasse principle) studied in Chapter III. The embedding $\mathbf{Q} \hookrightarrow \mathbf{Q}_v$ for every $v \in V$ allows one to study rational numbers and quadratic forms by examining their behavior at each place.
