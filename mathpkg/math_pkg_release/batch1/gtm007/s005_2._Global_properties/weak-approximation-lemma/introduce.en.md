The weak approximation lemma asserts that a rational number can simultaneously approximate finitely many prescribed $p$-adic numbers and a real number to arbitrarily high precision. Given distinct primes $p_1, \ldots, p_n$, target values $x_i \in \mathbb{Q}_{p_i}$ and $x_\infty \in \mathbb{R}$, and tolerance parameters $\varepsilon > 0$ and $N \geq 1$, there exists a rational number $x$ whose $p_i$-adic distance to each $x_i$ is at most $p_i^{-N}$ and whose archimedean distance to $x_\infty$ is at most $\varepsilon$. The proof combines the Chinese Remainder Theorem with the density of rationals of the form $a/q^m$ in $\mathbb{R}$.
