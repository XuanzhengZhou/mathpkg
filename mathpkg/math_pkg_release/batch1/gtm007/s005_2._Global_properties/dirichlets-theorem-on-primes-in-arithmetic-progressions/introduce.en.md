Dirichlet's theorem asserts that every arithmetic progression $a, a+m, a+2m, \ldots$ with $\gcd(a,m) = 1$ contains infinitely many prime numbers. This is one of the most celebrated results in analytic number theory and is proved in Chapter VI using Dirichlet characters and the non-vanishing of $L(1, \chi)$ for nontrivial characters $\chi$. The theorem is stated as Lemma 3 in Chapter III, where it is used as an ingredient in proving the existence of rational numbers with prescribed Hilbert symbols.
