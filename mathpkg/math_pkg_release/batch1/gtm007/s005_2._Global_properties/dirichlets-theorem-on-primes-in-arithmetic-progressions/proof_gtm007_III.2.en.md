The proof is deferred to Chapter VI (n° 4.3). It uses Dirichlet characters modulo $m$, the associated $L$-functions $L(s, \chi)$, and the crucial fact that $L(1, \chi) 
eq 0$ for every nontrivial character $\chi$. A sketch of the argument: one shows that
$$
\sum_{p \equiv a \pmod{m}} rac{1}{p^s} 	o \infty \quad 	ext{as } s 	o 1^+,
$$
which forces the existence of infinitely many primes in the progression $a mod m$. The convergence and non-vanishing properties of $L(s, \chi)$ at $s = 1$ are the essential analytic inputs.
