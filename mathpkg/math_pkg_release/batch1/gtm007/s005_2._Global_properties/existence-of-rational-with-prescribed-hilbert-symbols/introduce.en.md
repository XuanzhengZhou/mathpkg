Theorem 4 (Chapter III, §2.2) provides a local-to-global criterion for the existence of a rational number $x$ whose Hilbert symbols with a finite family $(a_i)$ match prescribed values $arepsilon_{i,v}$ at every place $v$. The necessity of conditions (1) and (2) follows from the Hilbert product formula; condition (3) is the obvious local obstruction. The sufficiency proof combines the Chinese remainder theorem, Dirichlet's theorem on arithmetic progressions, and an approximation argument. This result is a crucial ingredient in the classification of quadratic forms over $\mathbf{Q}$ via the Hasse-Minkowski theorem.
