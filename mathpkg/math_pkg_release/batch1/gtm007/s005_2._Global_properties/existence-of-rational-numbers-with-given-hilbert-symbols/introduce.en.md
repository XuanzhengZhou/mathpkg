Theorem 4 provides necessary and sufficient conditions for a prescribed family of local Hilbert symbol values to be realized by a single global rational number $x$. The three conditions require that: (1) only finitely many local symbols differ from $1$; (2) the product formula is satisfied for each $a_i$; and (3) locally at each completion, there exists some element $x_v$ producing the desired symbols. The proof uses the Chinese Remainder Theorem, a weak approximation lemma, and Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions.
