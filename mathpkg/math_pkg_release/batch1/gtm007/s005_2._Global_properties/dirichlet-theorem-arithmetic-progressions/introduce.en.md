Dirichlet's theorem on arithmetic progressions states that for any two coprime positive integers $a$ and $m$, the arithmetic progression $a, a+m, a+2m, \ldots$ contains infinitely many prime numbers. This deep result in analytic number theory is used in Serre's proof of the existence theorem for Hilbert symbols to produce a prime $p$ satisfying a prescribed congruence condition. The full proof is given in Chapter VI of the book and relies on Dirichlet characters and $L$-functions.
