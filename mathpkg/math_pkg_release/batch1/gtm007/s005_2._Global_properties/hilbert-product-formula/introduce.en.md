The Hilbert product formula states that for any two non-zero rational numbers $a$ and $b$, the product of their Hilbert symbols over all completions of $\mathbb{Q}$ (including the real numbers and all $p$-adic fields) equals $1$. Only finitely many factors differ from $1$, making the infinite product well-defined. This theorem is essentially equivalent to the quadratic reciprocity law and serves as a prototypical example of local-global principles in number theory.
