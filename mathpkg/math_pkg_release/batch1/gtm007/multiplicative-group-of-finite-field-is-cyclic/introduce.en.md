The multiplicative group of any finite field is cyclic. This means there exists a generator (primitive element) whose powers exhaust all nonzero elements of the field. The proof uses the Euler totient function and the identity $n = \sum_{d|n} \phi(d)$. This cyclicity is essential for power sum formulas, the definition of the Legendre symbol, and the structure of $p$-adic units.
