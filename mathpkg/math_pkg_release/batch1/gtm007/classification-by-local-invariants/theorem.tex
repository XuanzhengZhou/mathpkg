\textbf{Corollary.} Let $(r,s)$ and $(r',s')$ be signatures and let $d_v, \varepsilon_v, d'_v, \varepsilon'_v$ be local invariants satisfying the product formula. For two quadratic forms over $\mathbb{Q}$ to be equivalent it is necessary and sufficient that they have the same local invariants at all $v \in V$.
