The Hilbert symbol $(a,b) = 1$ exactly when $a$ is a norm from the quadratic extension $k(\sqrt{b})$. If $b$ is a square, $k_b = k$ and the norm map is the identity, so $(a,b) = 1$ for all $a$. This connection to norm groups is central to class field theory.
