A Dirichlet character modulo $m$ is a character of the finite abelian group $G(m) = (\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z})^*$. It is a completely multiplicative function on the integers that is periodic with period $m$ and vanishes on integers not coprime to $m$. There are exactly $\phi(m)$ distinct characters modulo $m$. They are the essential building blocks for constructing Dirichlet $L$-functions $L(s, \chi) = \sum \chi(n)/n^s$, which Dirichlet used to prove his theorem on arithmetic progressions.
