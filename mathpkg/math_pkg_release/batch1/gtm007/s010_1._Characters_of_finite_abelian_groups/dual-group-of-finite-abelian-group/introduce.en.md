The dual group $\widehat{G} = \operatorname{Hom}(G, \mathbb{C}^*)$ of a finite abelian group $G$ is the set of all characters of $G$, endowed with pointwise multiplication: $(\chi_1 \chi_2)(x) = \chi_1(x) \chi_2(x)$. The trivial character $\chi_0(x) = 1$ for all $x \in G$ serves as the identity element. The dual group plays a central role in Fourier analysis on finite abelian groups and in the theory of Dirichlet characters.
