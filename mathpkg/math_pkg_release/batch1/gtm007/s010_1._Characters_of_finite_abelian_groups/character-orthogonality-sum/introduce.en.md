The orthogonality relation states that the sum of all characters evaluated at an element $x$ of a finite abelian group $G$ equals $|G|$ if $x$ is the identity and $0$ otherwise. This follows from applying Proposition 3 (bidual isomorphism) to the dual group $\widehat{G}$. The orthogonality relations are the finite abelian group analog of Fourier orthogonality and are used repeatedly in Dirichlet's proof to isolate primes in a given arithmetic progression.
