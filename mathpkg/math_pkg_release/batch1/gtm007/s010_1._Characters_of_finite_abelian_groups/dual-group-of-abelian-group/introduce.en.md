The dual group $\widehat{G} = \operatorname{Hom}(G, \mathbb{C}^*)$ of a finite abelian group $G$ is the set of all its characters equipped with pointwise multiplication. This construction is a special case of Pontryagin duality for locally compact abelian groups. For finite abelian groups, the dual $\widehat{G}$ is itself a finite abelian group of the same order as $G$, and taking the dual twice recovers $G$ up to canonical isomorphism.
