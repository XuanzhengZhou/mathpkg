Since $G$ and $\widehat{\widehat{G}}$ have the same order (by applying Proposition 2 twice), it suffices to prove that the evaluation homomorphism $\varepsilon \colon G \to \widehat{\widehat{G}}$ is injective.

Let $x \in G$ be a non-identity element. We must show that there exists a character $\chi \in \widehat{G}$ such that $\chi(x) \neq 1$, i.e., that $x$ is not in the kernel of $\varepsilon$.

Consider the cyclic subgroup $H = \langle x \rangle$ generated by $x$. Since $x \neq 1$, $H$ is nontrivial. There exists a nontrivial character $\psi$ of $H$ (for example, map the generator $x$ to a primitive $|H|$-th root of unity). By Proposition 1, $\psi$ extends to a character $\chi$ of $G$. Then $\chi(x) = \psi(x) \neq 1$, so $\varepsilon(x)(\chi) \neq 1$ and thus $\varepsilon(x) \neq 1_{\widehat{\widehat{G}}}$.

Therefore $\varepsilon$ is injective, and since $|G| = |\widehat{\widehat{G}}|$, it is an isomorphism.
