Proposition 3 establishes that every finite abelian group $G$ is canonically isomorphic to its double dual (bidual) $\widehat{\widehat{G}}$ via the evaluation map $x \mapsto (\chi \mapsto \chi(x))$. Since $|G| = |\widehat{G}| = |\widehat{\widehat{G}}|$ by Proposition 2, it suffices to prove injectivity. This reflexivity property is fundamental to the orthogonality relations and the Fourier inversion formula on finite abelian groups.
