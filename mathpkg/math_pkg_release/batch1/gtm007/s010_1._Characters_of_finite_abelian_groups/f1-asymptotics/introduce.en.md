Lemma 7 (Chapter VI, §4.2) captures the contribution of the pole of $\zeta(s)$ at $s=1$. Since the zeta function has a simple pole, the sum over reciprocals of primes diverges logarithmically. This is the "prime source" that ultimately drives Dirichlet's theorem.
