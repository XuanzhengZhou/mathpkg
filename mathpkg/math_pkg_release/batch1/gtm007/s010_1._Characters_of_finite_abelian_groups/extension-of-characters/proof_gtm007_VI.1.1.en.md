We use induction on the index $(G:H)$ of $H$ in $G$. If $(G:H) = 1$, then $H = G$ and there is nothing to prove. Otherwise let $x$ be an element of $G$ not contained in $H$, and let $n$ be the smallest integer $> 1$ such that $x^n \in H$. Let $\chi$ be a character of $H$. We need to define an extension $\tilde{\chi}$ to the subgroup $\langle H, x \rangle$ generated by $H$ and $x$.

Every element of $\langle H, x \rangle$ can be written uniquely as $h x^k$ with $h \in H$ and $0 \leq k < n$. Let $\chi(x^n) = w \in \mathbb{C}^*$. Choose an $n$-th root $\tilde{w}$ of $w$ (i.e., $\tilde{w}^n = w$). Define
$$\tilde{\chi}(h x^k) = \chi(h) \tilde{w}^k.$$
This is well-defined and is a homomorphism extending $\chi$. Repeating this process increases the subgroup until we reach $G$, completing the induction.
