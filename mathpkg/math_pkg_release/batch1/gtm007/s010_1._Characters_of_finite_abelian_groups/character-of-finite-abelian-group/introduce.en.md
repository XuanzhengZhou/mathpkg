A character of a finite abelian group $G$ is a homomorphism from $G$ into the multiplicative group $\mathbb{C}^*$ of nonzero complex numbers. Characters are fundamental tools in harmonic analysis on finite groups, generalizing the notion of group homomorphisms to complex-valued multiplicative functions. They form the basis for defining Dirichlet characters and $L$-functions in analytic number theory.
