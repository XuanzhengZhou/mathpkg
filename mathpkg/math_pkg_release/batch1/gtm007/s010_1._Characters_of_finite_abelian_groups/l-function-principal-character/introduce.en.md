Proposition 11 (Chapter VI, §3.3): The $L$-function for the trivial character is essentially the Riemann zeta function with the Euler factors for primes dividing $m$ removed. This is the only $L$-function with a pole at $s=1$, which is the analytic reason why the primes are equidistributed among residue classes.
