The Riemann zeta function is the most fundamental Dirichlet series in analytic number theory. It arises from the trivial multiplicative function $f(n) = 1$ and converges absolutely for $R(s) > 1$. Its analytic properties (meromorphic continuation, pole at $s=1$, functional equation) form the backbone of modern analytic number theory.
