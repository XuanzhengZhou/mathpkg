The partial sums $\sum_{n=1}^N \chi(n)$ are bounded: for any full period of length $m$, the sum of character values over a complete set of residues modulo $m$ is $0$. More precisely, the sums $A_{u,v}$ for $v-u < m$ are bounded by $\phi(m)$. Hence the hypothesis of Proposition 9 is satisfied, giving conditional convergence for $R(s) > 0$.

For $R(s) > 1$, absolute convergence follows from Proposition 8 since $|\chi(n)| \leq 1$. The holomorphy of $L(s,\chi)$ in the half-plane follows from the uniform convergence on compact subsets (Proposition 6).
