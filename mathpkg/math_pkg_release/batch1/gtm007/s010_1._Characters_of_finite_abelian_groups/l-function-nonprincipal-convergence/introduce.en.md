Proposition 12 (Chapter VI, §3.3): The convergence of $L(s,\chi)$ for nontrivial characters extends to $R(s) > 0$, which is better than the absolute convergence abscissa at $R(s) > 1$. This is proved using the boundedness of character sums over intervals.
