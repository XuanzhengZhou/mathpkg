For a cyclic group $G$ of order $n$, the dual group $\widehat{G}$ has a very explicit description: it is isomorphic to the group $\mu_n$ of $n$th roots of unity via the evaluation map $\chi \mapsto \chi(s)$. Thus the dual of a cyclic group is again cyclic of the same order. This example is fundamental because any finite abelian group decomposes into a product of cyclic groups, and duality respects this decomposition.
