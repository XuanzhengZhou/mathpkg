Let $G$ be a cyclic group of order $n$ with generator $s$.

If $\chi \colon G \to \mathbb{C}^*$ is a character of $G$, the element $w = \chi(s)$ satisfies $w^n = 1$, i.e., $w$ is an $n$th root of unity. Conversely, every $n$th root of unity $w$ defines a character of $G$ by $s^a \mapsto w^a$.

The map $\chi \mapsto \chi(s)$ is an isomorphism of $\widehat{G}$ onto the group $\mu_n$ of $n$th roots of unity. In particular, $\widehat{G}$ is cyclic of order $n$.
