Modular characters are the fundamental objects for building Dirichlet $L$-functions. For $m = 4$, the unique nontrivial character is $x \mapsto (-1)^{arepsilon(x)}$; for $m = 8, p$ prime, etc., these characters generalize the Legendre symbol. They satisfy $\chi(ab) = \chi(a)\chi(b)$ for all integers $a,b$.
