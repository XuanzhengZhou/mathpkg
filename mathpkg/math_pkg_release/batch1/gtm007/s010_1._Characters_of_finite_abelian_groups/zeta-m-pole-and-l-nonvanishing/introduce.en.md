Theorem 1 (Chapter VI, §3.4): This is the central result of the chapter. The nonvanishing of $L(1,\chi)$ for nontrivial characters is the crux of Dirichlet's proof. The proof uses an ingenious argument: if some $L(1,\chi)=0$, then the product $\zeta_m$ would be holomorphic, yet its Dirichlet series has positive coefficients and diverges at some $s<1$, contradicting Landau's theorem (Proposition 7).
