Lemma 5 (Chapter VI, §3.1): For strictly multiplicative functions (like Dirichlet characters), each local factor is a geometric series. This yields the classic Euler product for the Riemann zeta function ($f=1$): $\zeta(s) = \prod_p 1/(1-p^{-s})$, and for $L$-functions: $L(s,\chi) = \prod_p 1/(1-\chi(p)p^{-s})$.
