Proposition 5 (as referenced in the text) constructs a quadratic character $\chi_a$ modulo $m = 4|a|$ (or $8|a|$) for any square-free integer $a \neq 1$. This character satisfies $\chi_a(p) = \left( \frac{a}{p} \right)$ for odd primes $p \nmid m$, where $\left( \frac{a}{p} \right)$ is the Legendre/Jacobi symbol. The construction uses the factorization of $a$ into prime factors and leverages quadratic reciprocity. This character is used in the application (Proposition 14) showing that primes with a given quadratic residue have density $1/2$.
