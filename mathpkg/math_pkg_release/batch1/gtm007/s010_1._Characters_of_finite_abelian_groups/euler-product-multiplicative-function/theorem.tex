Let $f$ be a bounded multiplicative function (i.e., $f(mn) = f(m)f(n)$ when $\gcd(m,n)=1$). Then the Dirichlet series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty f(n)/n^s$ converges absolutely for $R(s) > 1$, and in this domain its sum equals the convergent infinite product:
$$
\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{f(n)}{n^s} = \prod_{p \in P} \left(1 + \frac{f(p)}{p^s} + \frac{f(p^2)}{p^{2s}} + \cdots\right),
$$
where $P$ denotes the set of prime numbers.
