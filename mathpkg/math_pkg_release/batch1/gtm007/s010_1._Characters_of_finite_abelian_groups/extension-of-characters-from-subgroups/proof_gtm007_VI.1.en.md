Proceed by induction on $(G:H)$. If $(G:H) = 1$, then $H = G$ and there is nothing to prove. Otherwise, let $x \in G \setminus H$ and let $n$ be the smallest integer $> 1$ such that $x^n \in H$. Let $\chi$ be a character of $H$. Choose a suitable $n$-th root of unity to extend $\chi$ to the subgroup $\langle H, x \rangle$ generated by $H$ and $x$. Since $(\langle H, x \rangle : H) < (G:H)$, the induction hypothesis extends $\chi$ further to all of $G$.

For the exact sequence: the kernel of the restriction map $\rho$ consists of characters trivial on $H$, which are precisely characters of $G/H$, i.e., elements of $(\widehat{G/H})$.
