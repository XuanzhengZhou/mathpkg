Corollary 1 to Proposition 10: The simple pole of $\zeta(s)$ at $s=1$ is the analytic reflection of the infinitude of primes (Euler's proof) and drives the divergence of $\sum 1/p$. Corollary 2 makes this precise: the sum over primes diverges logarithmically.
