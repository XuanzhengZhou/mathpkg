Proposition 10 (Chapter VI, §3.2): The meromorphic continuation of $\zeta(s)$ to $R(s) > 0$ with a single simple pole at $s=1$ is the key analytic fact needed for Dirichlet's theorem. The proof uses a comparison between the sum and an integral.
