\textbf{Lemma.} Let $0 \to A \to E \to B \to 0$ be an exact sequence of commutative groups with $A$ and $B$ finite of orders $a$ and $b$ that are relatively prime. Let $B'$ be the set of $x \in E$ such that $bx = 0$. Then $E$ is the direct sum of $A$ and $B'$. Moreover, $B'$ is the only subgroup of $E$ isomorphic to $B$.
