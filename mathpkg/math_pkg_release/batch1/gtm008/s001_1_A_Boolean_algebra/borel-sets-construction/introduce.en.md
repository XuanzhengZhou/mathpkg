This theorem constructs the $\sigma$-algebra of Borel sets in a topological space $\langle X, T \rangle$ by transfinite induction through the ordinals less than $\aleph_1$. Starting from $A_0 = T \cup \{X - a \mid a \in T\}$ (the open sets and their closed complements), successor stages $A_{\alpha+1}$ add all countable unions and intersections of sets from $A_\alpha$, while limit stages take the union of all previous stages. The process stabilizes by $\aleph_1$, yielding exactly the Borel $\sigma$-algebra. This is a fundamental result in descriptive set theory connecting topology to measure-theoretic structure.
