A $\sigma$-homomorphism is a Boolean algebra homomorphism between $\sigma$-algebras that additionally preserves countable suprema and infima. For any countable subset $A$ of the domain, the image of the join equals the join of the images, and similarly for meets. This condition ensures that the homomorphism respects the additional $\sigma$-structure, making it the natural notion of morphism in the category of Boolean $\sigma$-algebras.
