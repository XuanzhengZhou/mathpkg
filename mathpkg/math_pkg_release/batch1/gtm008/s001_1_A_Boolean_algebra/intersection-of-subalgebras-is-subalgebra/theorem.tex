If $I \neq 0$ and $B_a$ is a subalgebra of the Boolean algebra $B = \langle B, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1 \rangle$ for $a \in I$, then
$$
B' = \left\langle \bigcap_{a \in I} |B_a|, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1 \right\rangle
$$
is a subalgebra of $B$. If in addition each $B_a$ is a $\sigma$-algebra, then $B'$ is a $\sigma$-algebra.
