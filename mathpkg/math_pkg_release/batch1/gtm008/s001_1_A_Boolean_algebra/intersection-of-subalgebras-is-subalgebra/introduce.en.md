This theorem states that the intersection of any nonempty indexed family of subalgebras of a Boolean algebra $B$ is itself a subalgebra of $B$. Furthermore, if each subalgebra in the family is a $\sigma$-algebra, the intersection retains the $\sigma$-algebra property. The proof follows directly from the definition of subalgebra and is left as an exercise to the reader. This result is fundamental for constructing generated subalgebras as intersections.
