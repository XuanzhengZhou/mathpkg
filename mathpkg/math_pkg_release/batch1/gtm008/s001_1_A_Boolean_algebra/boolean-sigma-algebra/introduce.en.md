A Boolean $\sigma$-algebra is a Boolean algebra that is closed under countable joins and meets. For every countable subset $A$ of its universe (denoted by $\bar{A} = \omega$), the supremum $\sum_{a \in A} a$ and the infimum $\prod_{a \in A} a$ both belong to the algebra. This concept generalizes the familiar notion of a $\sigma$-algebra of sets to the algebraic setting of Boolean algebras, and serves as the foundational structure for Chapter 3 of Takeuti and Zaring's axiomatic set theory.
