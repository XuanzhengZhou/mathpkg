In the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$, the canonical embedding of a set $x$ is denoted by $\check{x}$. This theorem states that if an ordinal $\alpha$ is not a cardinal in the ground model $V$, then with Boolean value $1$, $\check{\alpha}$ fails to be a cardinal in $V^{(B)}$. The proof uses Corollary 13.18, which concerns the interpretation of bounded formulas, together with the fact that the failure of being a cardinal can be expressed by a bounded formula.
