This theorem establishes the topological significance of the sharp operator: when the complete Boolean algebras $B_0, B_1$ are viewed as partial order structures (by removing the zero element), the sharp operator becomes an open, continuous, and onto map between the corresponding topological spaces. Moreover, the complete monomorphism associated with this open continuous map (via the regular open set construction) recovers the original monomorphism $i$. This provides the crucial link between the algebraic notion of a complete monomorphism and the geometric notion of an open continuous onto map.
