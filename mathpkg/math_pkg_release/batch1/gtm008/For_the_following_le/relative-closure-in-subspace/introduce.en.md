This theorem establishes the fundamental relationship between topological closure and interior operations in a subspace versus the ambient space. If $A$ is a subset of the subspace $S$, then the closure of $A$ in $S$ equals the closure of $A$ in $X$ intersected with $S$. The interior of $A$ in $S$ is expressed as $S$ minus the closure of $S \setminus A$ in $X$. These formulas are essential tools for working with regular open sets and Boolean algebras of regular open sets, which play a central role in the theory of complete Boolean algebras.
