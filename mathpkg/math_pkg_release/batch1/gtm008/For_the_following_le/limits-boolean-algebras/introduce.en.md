This definition introduces a notion of limit for $\mathrm{On}$-indexed sequences in complete Boolean algebras and Boolean-valued universes. A sequence $b_{\alpha}$ converges to 1 if the supremum over $\alpha$ of the infimum of all later terms equals 1, and converges to $b$ if the sequence of Boolean equivalences $b_{\alpha} \Leftrightarrow b$ converges to 1. This notion is extended to Boolean-valued universes by defining $u_{\alpha} \to u$ when the Boolean truth values $\llbracket u_{\alpha} = u \rrbracket$ converge to 1. These limits are used to analyze the structure of elements in class-sized Boolean algebras as limits of set-sized approximations.
