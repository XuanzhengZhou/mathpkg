If $b \in \tilde{B}$ and $\{b_{\alpha} \mid \alpha \in \mathrm{On}\} \subseteq \tilde{B}$, then:

$$\lim_{\alpha \in \mathrm{On}} b_{\alpha} = 1 \quad \text{iff} \quad \sum_{\alpha \in \mathrm{On}} \prod_{\alpha \leq \beta} b_{\beta} = 1,$$

$$\lim_{\alpha \in \mathrm{On}} b_{\alpha} = b \quad \text{iff} \quad \lim_{\alpha \in \mathrm{On}} (b_{\alpha} \Leftrightarrow b) = 1,$$

where $b_{\alpha} \Leftrightarrow b = (b_{\alpha} \Rightarrow b) \cdot (b \Rightarrow b_{\alpha})$ is the Boolean equivalence.

Similarly, for $u, u_{\alpha} \in V^{(B)}$ or $V^{(\tilde{B})}$:

$$\lim_{\alpha \in \mathrm{On}} u_{\alpha} = u \quad \text{iff} \quad \lim_{\alpha \in \mathrm{On}} \llbracket u_{\alpha} = u \rrbracket = 1.$$

We occasionally write $u_{\alpha} \rightarrow u$ for $\lim_{\alpha \in \mathrm{On}} u_{\alpha} = u$.
