We need only show that $(A \cap S)^{-S 0S} \subseteq A^{-0} \cap S$, for by the proof of Theorem 22.2 we know that the reverse inclusion holds. Let
$$p \in (A \cap S)^{-S 0S}.$$

Then there exists an open neighborhood $N(p)$ in $X$ such that
$$N(p) \cap S \subseteq (A \cap S)^{- \cap S}.$$

By Theorem 22.1,
$$N(p) = N(p) \cap S^{-} \subseteq (N(p) \cap S)^{-} \subseteq (A \cap S)^{-} \subseteq A^{-}.$$

Thus $p \in A^{-0}$, and since $p \in S$, we obtain $p \in A^{-0} \cap S$. Therefore
$$(A \cap S)^{-S 0S} \subseteq A^{-0} \cap S,$$
completing the proof.
