Definition 23.10 introduces the Uniform Convergence Law (UCL), a crucial condition on a complete Boolean algebra $\tilde{B}$ (possibly a proper class). The UCL states that if nonincreasing sequences $b_{\alpha i}$ converge to 0 for each $i$ in a set $I$, then they converge uniformly to 0. This condition is needed to ensure $V^{(\tilde{B})}$ satisfies the Axiom Schema of Replacement and the Axiom of Powers.
