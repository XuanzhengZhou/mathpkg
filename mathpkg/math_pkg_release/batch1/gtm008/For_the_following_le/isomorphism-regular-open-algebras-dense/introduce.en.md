When $S$ is a dense subspace of $X$, the complete Boolean algebras of regular open sets in $X$ and in $S$ are isomorphic. The isomorphism $i$ maps each regular open set $b_0$ in $S$ to the interior of its closure $b_0^{-0}$ in $X$, and the inverse map is simply intersection with $S$. This theorem is crucial for the completion theory of Boolean algebras, as it allows one to replace a dense subspace with the full space when studying the algebra of regular open sets.
