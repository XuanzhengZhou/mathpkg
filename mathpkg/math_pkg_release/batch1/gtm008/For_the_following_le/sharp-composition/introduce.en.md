When complete monomorphisms between complete Boolean algebras are composed, the corresponding sharp maps (open continuous onto maps between Stone spaces) compose contravariantly. Specifically, if $i_1: B_0 \to B_1$ and $i_2: B_1 \to B_2$ have associated sharp maps $\#_1: B_1 \to B_0$ and $\#_2: B_2 \to B_1$, then the composition $i = i_2 \circ i_1$ has associated sharp map $\# = \#_1 \circ \#_2$. This functorial property is essential for constructing limits and direct systems of complete Boolean algebras.
