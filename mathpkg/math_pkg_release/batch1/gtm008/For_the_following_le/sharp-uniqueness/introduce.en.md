Any open continuous onto map $f$ between the Stone spaces (partial order structures) of two complete Boolean algebras that induces a given complete monomorphism $i$ must coincide with the sharp operator $\#$ associated to $i$. This uniqueness result justifies defining the sharp operator as the canonical topological counterpart of a complete monomorphism, and it ensures that the correspondence between complete monomorphisms and open continuous onto maps is well-defined.
