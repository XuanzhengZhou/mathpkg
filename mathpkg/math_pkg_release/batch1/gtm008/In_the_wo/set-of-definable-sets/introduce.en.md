$\operatorname{Def}(x)$ is the definable power set of $x$ -- those subsets of $x$ that can be specified by a first-order formula using parameters from $x$. This operation is strictly more restrictive than the full power set: $\operatorname{Def}(x) \subseteq \mathcal{P}(x)$ but is in general much smaller (for countable $x$, $\operatorname{Def}(x)$ is countable while $\mathcal{P}(x)$ may be uncountable). The iteration of $\operatorname{Def}$ along the ordinals yields Godel's constructible universe $L$.
