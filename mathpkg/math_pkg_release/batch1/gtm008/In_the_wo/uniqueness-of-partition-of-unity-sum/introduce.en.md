The element $a$ whose existence is guaranteed by completeness is unique up to equality with truth value $1$. The proof uses the transitivity axiom for $B$-valued equality: each $b_i$ bounds the product of the equality truth values, and since the $b_i$'s form a partition of unity, their supremum is $1$, forcing the equality to hold with full truth. This unique element is denoted $\sum_{i \in I} b_i a_i$ and is called the mixed sum or Boolean mixture of the $a_i$'s with weights $b_i$.
