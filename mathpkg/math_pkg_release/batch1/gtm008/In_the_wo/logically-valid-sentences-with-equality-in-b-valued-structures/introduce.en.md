The equality axioms of first-order logic are valid (have Boolean value $1$) in any $B$-valued structure. This verification is essential for establishing that $B$-valued semantics is a legitimate generalization of classical semantics. The proof uses the definition of $\|u = v\|$ and the properties of Boolean meets and joins. The validity of the equality axioms ensures that the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$ satisfies the logical axioms of ZF.
