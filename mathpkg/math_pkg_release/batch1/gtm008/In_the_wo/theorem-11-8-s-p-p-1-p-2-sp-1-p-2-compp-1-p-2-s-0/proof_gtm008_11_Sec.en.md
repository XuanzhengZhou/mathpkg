Since $M \subseteq M[G]$ and $On^M = On^{M[G]}$, every cardinal in $M[G]$ is a cardinal in $M$ because “$a$ is not a cardinal” $\leftrightarrow (\exists f) \varphi(f, a)$, for some $\varphi$ that is absolute with respect to transitive models.

Conversely if there is a cardinal in $M$ that is not a cardinal in $M[G]$ then there is a cardinal $\lambda$ in $M[G]$, and hence in $M$, but

$$\gamma = (\lambda^+)^M$$

is not a cardinal in $M[G]$. Let $\lambda$ be the smallest such cardinal. Then

$$\lambda = \bar{\gamma}^{M[G]} < \gamma = (\lambda^+)^M.$$

Hence

$$(\exists f \in M[G])[f: \lambda \xrightarrow{\text{onto}} \gamma].$$

Furthermore $f$ is denoted by a term $t$ of the ramified language and

$$(\exists p \in P)[p \in G \wedge p \models t: \lambda \xrightarrow{\text{onto}} \gamma].$$
