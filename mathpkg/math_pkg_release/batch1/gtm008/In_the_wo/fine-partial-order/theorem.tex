A partial order structure $\mathcal{P} = \langle P, \leq \rangle$ is called \text{fine} if the following condition is satisfied:
$$(\forall p, q \in P)[q \not\leq p \rightarrow (\exists r \in P)[r \leq q \land \neg \operatorname{Comp}(r, p)]],$$
where $\operatorname{Comp}(r, p)$ means $r$ and $p$ are compatible (there exists a nonzero lower bound).
