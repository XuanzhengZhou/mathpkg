In a complete $B$-valued structure, any family of elements $\{a_i\}$ can be mixed using a partition of unity $\{b_i\}$ to obtain a unique (up to equality with value $1$) element. This mixed sum operation is the Boolean-valued analogue of "gluing" or "patching" constructions. The uniqueness property is essential for the Maximum Principle: the witness for an existential formula is constructed by mixing candidate witnesses weighted by their respective truth values.
