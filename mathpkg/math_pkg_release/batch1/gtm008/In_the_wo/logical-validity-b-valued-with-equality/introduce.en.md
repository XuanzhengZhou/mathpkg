This theorem extends the logical validity result to languages with equality. Every first-order sentence that is logically valid (provable in the predicate calculus with equality) is satisfied in any $B$-valued structure that satisfies the equality axioms. The inequality relating truth values before and after substitution is the $B$-valued analog of Leibniz's law: the truth value of a formula after substituting equals is bounded below by the product of the equality truth values times the original truth value.
