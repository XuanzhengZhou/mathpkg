This theorem connects the notion of a proper filter in a Boolean algebra with the notion of a filter in its associated partial order structure (obtained by removing the zero element). It shows that the two definitions coincide: a nonempty subset $F$ of the Boolean algebra is a proper filter (meaning $0 \notin F$) exactly when it satisfies the strongly compatible and upward hereditary conditions for the partial order.
