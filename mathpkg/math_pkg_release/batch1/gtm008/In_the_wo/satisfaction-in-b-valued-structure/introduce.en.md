A $B$-valued structure satisfies a formula $\varphi$ (with parameters) if the Boolean truth value of $\varphi$ equals $1$. This generalizes the Tarskian satisfaction relation: a structure satisfies $\varphi$ exactly when the truth value attains the maximal element of the Boolean algebra. The satisfaction relation respects the logical connectives in the expected way, making $B$-valued semantics a conservative extension of classical two-valued model theory.
