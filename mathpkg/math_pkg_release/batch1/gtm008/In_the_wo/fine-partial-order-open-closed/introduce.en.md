In a fine partial order, the basic open sets $[p] = \{q \in P \mid q \leq p\}$ are equal to their own regularization $[p]^{-0}$ (the interior of the closure of $[p]$). This means each basic open set is itself a regular open set, which greatly simplifies the study of the regular open algebra of the partial order. Many of the partial orders used later in the book for forcing and independence proofs satisfy this fineness condition.
