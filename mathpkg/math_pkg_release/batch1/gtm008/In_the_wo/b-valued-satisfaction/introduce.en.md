A $B$-valued structure $A$ satisfies a closed formula $\varphi$ (written $A \models \varphi$) if and only if the truth value of $\varphi$ in $A$ equals the top element $1$ of the complete Boolean algebra $B$. When $B = 2$, this reduces to the usual Tarskian notion of satisfaction in two-valued logic. This definition allows us to speak of formulas being "true" in a $B$-valued structure even when the truth values themselves are more nuanced elements of $B$.
