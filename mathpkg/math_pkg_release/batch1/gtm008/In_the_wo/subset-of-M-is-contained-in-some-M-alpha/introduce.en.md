In a cumulative hierarchy $M = \bigcup_\alpha M_\alpha$, any subset of $M$ is bounded in rank — there is some stage $\alpha$ by which all elements of $x$ have appeared. This property is essential for the Replacement axiom to hold in $M$ and for establishing the Reflection Principle. It follows from the fact that the ordinals indexing the hierarchy are well-ordered and the hierarchy is cumulative.
