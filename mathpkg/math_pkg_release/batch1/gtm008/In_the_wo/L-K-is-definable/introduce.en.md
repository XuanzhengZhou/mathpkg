$L(K)$, the class of sets constructible relative to a class $K$, is definable by a formula in the language of set theory. This means that the constructible hierarchy can be formalized within ZF itself, allowing metamathematical arguments about constructibility to be carried out within the theory. The definability of $L(K)$ is a prerequisite for proving that $L(K)$ is a model of ZF and for establishing absoluteness results.
