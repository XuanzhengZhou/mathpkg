The truth value of a closed formula in a $B$-valued structure is defined recursively on the structure of the formula. Boolean connectives are interpreted by their corresponding Boolean algebra operations (negation by complement, conjunction by meet, disjunction by join). Quantifiers are interpreted by infinite meets and joins over the domain: the universal quantifier corresponds to the Boolean product (infimum) over all elements, while the existential quantifier corresponds to the Boolean sum (supremum). This requires the Boolean algebra to be complete.
