Semi-normal functions on the ordinals share a crucial property with normal functions: their fixed points form a closed unbounded (club) class. This is proved by iterating $F$: given any $\alpha$, the sequence $\alpha, F(\alpha), F(F(\alpha)), \ldots$ has supremum a fixed point of $F$. The closure property (limits of fixed points are fixed points) follows from the monotonicity of $F$. This club property guarantees that there are arbitrarily large ordinals where reflection holds.
