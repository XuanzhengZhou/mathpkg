A $B$-valued interpretation generalizes the classical two-valued interpretation of first-order logic to a complete Boolean algebra $B$. Instead of assigning truth or falsehood to atomic formulas, a $B$-valued interpretation assigns an element of $B$ (a "truth value" in the Boolean algebra). Individual constants are mapped to elements of the domain $A$, and $n$-ary predicate constants are mapped to functions from $A^n$ to $B$. The ordinary two-valued case corresponds to the special case $B = 2$, the two-element Boolean algebra.
