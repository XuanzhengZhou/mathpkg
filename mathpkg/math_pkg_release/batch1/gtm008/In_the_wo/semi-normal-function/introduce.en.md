A semi-normal function on the ordinals is inflationary ($\alpha \leq F(\alpha)$), monotone, and continuous at limit ordinals. Semi-normal functions are used extensively in the reflection arguments of relative constructibility: for each formula, one constructs a semi-normal function whose fixed points are ordinals where reflection holds. Unlike normal functions, semi-normal functions need not be strictly increasing.
