A topological space is called a $T_1$-space if it satisfies the $T_1$ separation axiom: for any two distinct points, each has a neighborhood not containing the other. This is a basic separation property in topology, weaker than the Hausdorff ($T_2$) property. The $T_1$ condition is equivalent to the requirement that all singleton sets are closed.
