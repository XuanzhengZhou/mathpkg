A formula $\varphi$ is absolute with respect to a transitive model $M$ of ZF if its truth value is the same whether evaluated in the full universe or relativized to $M$, for all parameters from $M$. Absoluteness is a crucial concept in set theory, as it allows one to transfer truth between models. Many fundamental notions (such as "$x$ is an ordinal", "$x$ is a finite set") are absolute, while others (such as "$x$ is countable") are not.
