The Reflection Principle states that for any formula $\varphi$, the truth of $\varphi$ in the full model $M$ is reflected down to some sufficiently large stage $M_\beta$. This means any finite collection of statements true in $M$ is already true in some initial segment $M_\beta$. The Reflection Principle is a crucial tool for proving that $M$ satisfies the Replacement axiom schema and is a consequence of the cumulative nature of the hierarchy combined with the fact that there are only finitely many formulas at any finite level.
