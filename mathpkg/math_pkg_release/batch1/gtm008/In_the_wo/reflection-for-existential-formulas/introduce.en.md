When the Reflection Principle is applied to existential formulas, one obtains the existence of closure ordinals: witnesses for existential statements can be found within the reflected stage $M_\beta$. This strong form of reflection is equivalent to the existence of semi-normal functions (functions with the fixed-point property). It provides the technical machinery needed to prove that the Replacement axiom holds in constructible hierarchies.
