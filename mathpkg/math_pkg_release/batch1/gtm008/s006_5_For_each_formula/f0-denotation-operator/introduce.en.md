Definition 9.36 completes the construction of the generic extension $M[F_0]$ by defining the denotation operator $D$ that maps constant terms of the ramified language to actual sets in the two-valued model. Given an $M$-complete homomorphism $h: |B| \to |2|$, the set $F_0$ is obtained by applying $h$ to the Boolean-valued characteristic function $f_0: K \to B$. The denotation operator interprets the atomic predicates $\in$, $V(\cdot)$, and $F(\cdot)$ in the standard two-valued sense, producing the generic extension $M[F_0] = M[h] = M[G]$.
