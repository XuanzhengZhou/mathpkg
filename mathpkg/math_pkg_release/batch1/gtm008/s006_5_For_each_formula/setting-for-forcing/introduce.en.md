Definition 10.5 formalizes the minimal requirements for a forcing construction: a countable transitive model $M$ of $\mathsf{ZF}$ together with a partial order $P \in M$. The countability condition ensures that for each $p \in P$, there exists a filter $G$ that is $P$-generic over $M$ with $p \in G$ (by the Rasiowa-Sikorski Theorem). The weakened condition $3'$ (that $\mathcal{P}(P) \cap M$ is countable) is often sufficient in practice.
