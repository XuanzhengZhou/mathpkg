Define $F(\alpha) = \mu_{\gamma}[\gamma \geq \alpha \land (\forall a_1, \ldots, a_n \in M_{\alpha})[\llbracket (\exists y)\varphi(a_1, \ldots, a_n, y) \rrbracket = \sum_{a \in M_{\gamma}} \llbracket \varphi(a_1, \ldots, a_n, a) \rrbracket]]$. Corollary 9.15 guarantees that such $\gamma$ exists for each $\alpha$, so $F(\alpha)$ is well-defined. The function $F$ is semi-normal: it is increasing ($F(\alpha) \geq \alpha$) and continuous at limits because the defining condition is preserved under suprema. The proof follows the same pattern as the earlier results on bounding existential quantifiers.
