Theorem 9.34 establishes that in the ramified $B$-valued hierarchy, every element $t$ of the successor level $T_{\alpha+1}$ is defined over the preceding level $T_{\alpha}$. This mirrors condition 4 of the earlier $M_{\alpha}$ construction (page 87) and is a fundamental structural property ensuring that the complexity of elements increases gradually through the hierarchy. The proof follows from the remark after Theorem 9.30, which shows that terms of the form $\hat{x}^{\beta}\varphi(x^{\beta})$ are defined over $T_{\beta}$.
