Theorem 9.32 establishes the fundamental substitution properties of equality in the ramified $B$-valued structure $\mathbf{T}_{\alpha}$, confirming that $T_{\alpha}$ is a genuine $B$-valued structure as defined in Chapter 6. The four clauses state that equality is compatible with membership (clauses 1-2) and is transitive (clauses 3-4). Together with Theorem 9.28 (reflexivity and symmetry), this shows that the Boolean-valued equality behaves as an appropriate $B$-valued equivalence relation satisfying Leibniz's law for the language $\mathcal{L}$.
