The proof follows from Theorems 9.12 and 9.13. Theorem 9.12 establishes that the Boolean-valued structure satisfies the maximum principle, and Theorem 9.13 provides the existence of a bounding ordinal for existential formulas. Specifically, the function $g(b) = \mu_{\beta}(b \leq F(\beta))$ maps $b \in B$ to ordinals, and since $B$ is a set, there exists $\beta$ such that $\beta = \sup g'' B$, after which the values stabilize. Applying this to the Boolean values of $\varphi(a)$ for all $a \in M$ yields the required $\beta$ such that the supremum over all $a \in M$ equals the supremum over $a \in M_{\beta}$.
