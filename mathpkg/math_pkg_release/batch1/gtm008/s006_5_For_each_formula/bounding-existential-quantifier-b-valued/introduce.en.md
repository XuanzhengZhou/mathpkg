Theorem 9.14 states that the Boolean truth value of an existential formula $\llbracket (\exists y)\varphi(y) \rrbracket$ can be computed as a sum (Boolean supremum) over a bounded initial segment $M_{\beta}$ of the cumulative hierarchy, rather than over the entire universe $M$. This is crucial for the well-foundedness of the recursive definition of $\llbracket \cdot \rrbracket$, as it ensures the existential quantifier does not require ranging over a proper class. The proof uses Theorems 9.12 and 9.13.
