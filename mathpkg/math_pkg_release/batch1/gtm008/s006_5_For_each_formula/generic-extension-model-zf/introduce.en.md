Theorem 10.1 is the foundational result of forcing: given a $P$-generic filter $G$ over a countable transitive model $M$, the generic extension $M[G]$ is again a standard transitive model of $\mathsf{ZF}$ with the same ordinals as $M$. It also preserves the Axiom of Choice. The theorem establishes that truth in $M[G]$ corresponds to membership of the Boolean truth value $\llbracket \varphi \rrbracket$ in the generic filter $G$, connecting the Boolean-valued semantics to actual two-valued satisfaction.
