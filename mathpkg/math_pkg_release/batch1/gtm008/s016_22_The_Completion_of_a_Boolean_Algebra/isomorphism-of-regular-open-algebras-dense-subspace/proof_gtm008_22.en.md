For $b_0 \in B_0$, define $i(b_0) = b_0^{-0}$. Then $i: B_0 \rightarrow B$ and $b_0 = i(b_0) \cap S$, by Theorem 22.2. Let $b \in B$. Since $b \cap S$ is regular open in $S$ (by Theorem 22.4), $i(b \cap S) = b$. Therefore $i$ is onto, is one-to-one (by Theorem 22.2), and by Theorem 22.3 it preserves $\leq$.
