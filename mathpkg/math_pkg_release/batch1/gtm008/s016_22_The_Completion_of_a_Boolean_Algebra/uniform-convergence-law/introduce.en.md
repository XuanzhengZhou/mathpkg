This definition introduces the Uniform Convergence Law (UCL), a distributive-like condition on complete Boolean algebras. The UCL states that if ordinal-indexed decreasing sequences converge to 0 pointwise, then they converge uniformly to 0. This condition is crucial for ensuring that the Boolean-valued universe satisfies the Axiom of Replacement and Powers.
