Let $B$ be a Boolean algebra. There exists a complete Boolean algebra $\tilde{B}$ and a monomorphism $j: B \rightarrow \tilde{B}$ such that $j``B$ is dense in $\tilde{B}$. Moreover, if $\langle \tilde{B}_1, f \rangle$ is any completion of $B$, then there exists an isomorphism $k: \tilde{B} \rightarrow \tilde{B}_1$ such that the diagram commutes, i.e., $k \circ j = f$.
