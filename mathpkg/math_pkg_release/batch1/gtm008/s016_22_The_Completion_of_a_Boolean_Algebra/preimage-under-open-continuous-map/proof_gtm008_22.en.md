Let $x \in (f^{-1})``(B^{-})$. Then $f(x) \in B^{-}$ and hence

$$(\forall N(f(x)))[N(f(x)) \cap B \neq 0]$$
$$(\forall N(x))[f``N(x) \cap B \neq 0]$$

since $f$ is an open map onto $Y$. Therefore every neighborhood of $x$ meets $(f^{-1})``B$, i.e., $x \in ((f^{-1})``B)^{-}$. The reverse inclusion and part 2 follow similarly, using that $f$ is continuous.
