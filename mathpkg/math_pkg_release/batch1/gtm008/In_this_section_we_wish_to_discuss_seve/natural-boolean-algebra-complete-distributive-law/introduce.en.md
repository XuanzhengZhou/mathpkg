Theorem 4.3 establishes that the natural Boolean algebra of all subsets of any nonempty set $A$ (i.e., the power set algebra $\mathcal{P}(A)$) satisfies the complete distributive law for all cardinalities. The proof is a direct set-theoretic verification: an element belongs to the intersection of unions if and only if there exists a choice function witnessing membership in the union of intersections. This result provides the canonical example of a completely distributive complete Boolean algebra.
