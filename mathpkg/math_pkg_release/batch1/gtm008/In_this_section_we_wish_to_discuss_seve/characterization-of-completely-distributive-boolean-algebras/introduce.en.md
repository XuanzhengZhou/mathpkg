Theorem 4.4 provides a striking characterization: up to isomorphism, the natural Boolean algebras $\mathcal{P}(A)$ of all subsets of a nonempty set are the **only** completely distributive complete Boolean algebras. The proof constructs the set $A$ from products of atoms generated by the elements of $B$ and their complements, then defines an isomorphism $F: \mathcal{P}(A) \to |B|$ via joins. This representation theorem shows that complete distributivity is a very strong condition in the class of complete Boolean algebras.
