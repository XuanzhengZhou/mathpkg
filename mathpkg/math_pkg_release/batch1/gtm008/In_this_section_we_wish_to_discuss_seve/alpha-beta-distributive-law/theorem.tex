If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are cardinal numbers then a complete Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the $(\alpha, \beta)$-distributive law $((\alpha, \beta)\text{-DL})$ iff for each
$$\{b_{ij} \in |B| \mid i \in I \land j \in J\} \text{ with } \bar{I} \leq \alpha \land \bar{J} \leq \beta$$
$$\prod_{i \in I} \sum_{j \in J} b_{ij} = \sum_{f \in J^I} \prod_{i \in I} b_{i,f(i)}$$
$B$ satisfies the complete distributive law iff it satisfies the $(\alpha, \beta)$-distributive law for all $\alpha$ and $\beta$.
