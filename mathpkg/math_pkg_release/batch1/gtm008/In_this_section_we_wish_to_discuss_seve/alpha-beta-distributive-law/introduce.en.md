The $(\alpha,\beta)$-distributive law generalizes the familiar finite distributive law to arbitrary cardinal bounds $\alpha$ and $\beta$ in a complete Boolean algebra. It requires that the meet of joins indexed by a set of size at most $\alpha$ over sets of size at most $\beta$ equals the join of the corresponding meets over all choice functions $f \in J^I$. A Boolean algebra satisfies the complete distributive law when this equality holds for all cardinalities $\alpha$ and $\beta$. These laws are central to the study of Boolean-valued models and forcing constructions.
