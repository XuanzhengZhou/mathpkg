Example 6 proves the absoluteness of $\omega$: the set of natural numbers in the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$ is, with Boolean truth value $1$, equal to the canonical name of the standard $\omega$. This means that $V^{(B)}$ has no "non-standard" natural numbers — they are all mixtures of standard natural numbers. Together with Theorem 13.22 (ordinals are mixtures of standard ordinals), this shows that $V^{(B)}$ shares the same natural numbers and ordinals as $V$.
