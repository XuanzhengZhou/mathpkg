The Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$ is the analogue of the cumulative hierarchy $V$ in the context of Boolean-valued models. Starting from a complete Boolean algebra $B$, one builds a hierarchy of $B$-valued sets by transfinite recursion: each $B$-valued set is a function from a set of previously constructed $B$-valued sets (its domain) into $B$. This construction, developed by Scott and Solovay, provides an alternative to Cohen's method of forcing that avoids ramified languages.
