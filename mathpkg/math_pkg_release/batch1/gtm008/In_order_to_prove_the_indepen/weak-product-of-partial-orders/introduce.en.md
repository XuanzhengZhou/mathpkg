The weak product (or finite support product) of a family of partial order structures is the subset of the full product consisting of elements that differ from the greatest element on at most finitely many coordinates. This construction is fundamental to forcing with infinitely many generic sets, as in the proof of the independence of the Axiom of Choice, where countably many Cohen reals are added with finite support.
