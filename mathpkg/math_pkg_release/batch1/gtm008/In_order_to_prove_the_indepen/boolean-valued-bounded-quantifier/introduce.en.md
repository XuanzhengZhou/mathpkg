Theorem 13.13 provides the fundamental reduction of bounded quantifier formulas in $V^{(B)}$ to Boolean algebraic operations. For a $B$-valued set $u$, the Boolean truth value of "$\exists x \in u\, \varphi(x)$" is the Boolean sum (supremum) of $u(x) \cdot [\![\varphi(x)]\!]$ over all $x$ in the domain of $u$, weighted by the degree $u(x)$ to which $x$ belongs to $u$. Similarly, the universal bounded quantifier reduces to a Boolean product (infimum) of implications. These formulas are essential for computing truth values in $V^{(B)}$.
