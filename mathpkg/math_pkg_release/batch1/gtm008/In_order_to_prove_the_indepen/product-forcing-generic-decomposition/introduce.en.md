Theorem 12.7 establishes the fundamental decomposition property for product forcing: a generic filter $G$ for the product $P_1 	imes P_2$ can be uniquely factored as $G = G_1 	imes G_2$, where $G_1$ is $P_1$-generic over the ground model $M$ and $G_2$ is $P_2$-generic over the intermediate extension $M[G_1]$. This is the basis for the two-step iteration theorem, allowing forcing arguments to be broken into stages.
