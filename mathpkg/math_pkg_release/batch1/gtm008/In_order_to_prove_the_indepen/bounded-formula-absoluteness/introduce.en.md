Corollary 13.19 is the Boolean-valued version of the absoluteness of $\Delta_0$-formulas. It states that a bounded formula $\phi$ (one whose quantifiers are all of the form $\exists x \in y$ or $\forall x \in y$) holds in the real universe $V$ if and only if its Boolean truth value in $V^{(B)}$ (with parameters replaced by their canonical names) is $1$. This follows from the combination of Theorems 13.17 and 13.18, taking $B' = 2$.
