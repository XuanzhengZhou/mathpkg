Example 4 proves by induction on $n \in \omega$ that the usual induction principle for natural numbers holds in $V^{(B)}$: if a $B$-valued set $a$ contains $0$ and is closed under successor, then each standard natural number $\check{n}$ belongs to $a$. This lemma is used in the proof that $\omega$ is absolute between $V$ and $V^{(B)}$.
