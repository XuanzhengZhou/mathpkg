Theorem 13.5 establishes the Boolean-valued analogues of the standard equality axioms for first-order logic: substitution of equals for equals in membership (parts 1 and 2) and transitivity of equality (part 3). The proof proceeds by induction on the rank $\alpha$. These properties are essential for showing that $V^{(B)}$ forms a proper $B$-valued structure satisfying the logical axioms of equality.
