Theorem 12.2 establishes that the forcing relation is invariant under permutations of the indices of the forcing conditions, provided formulas and terms are transformed accordingly. This symmetry principle is a key tool in constructing a model of ZF where the Axiom of Choice fails, as it allows one to show that certain sets (like the set of generic reals) cannot be well-ordered.
