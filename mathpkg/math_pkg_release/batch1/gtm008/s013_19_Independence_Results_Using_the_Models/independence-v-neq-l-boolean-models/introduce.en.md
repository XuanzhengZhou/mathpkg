If a complete Boolean algebra $B$ fails to satisfy the $(\omega, 2)$-distributive law, then the statement $V \neq L$ (i.e., the universe is not equal to the constructible universe) holds with Boolean value $1$ in the Boolean-valued model $V^{(B)}$. This theorem establishes the independence of $V = L$ from the axioms of ZF set theory using the method of Boolean-valued models rather than classical forcing. The proof proceeds by contradiction: one takes a countable transitive model $M$ of ZF elementarily equivalent to $V$ with respect to $B$, considers a generic extension $M[G]$, and shows that $M[G]$ cannot satisfy $V = L$ because otherwise $M[G] = M$, contradicting the proper extension guaranteed by the failure of the distributive law.
