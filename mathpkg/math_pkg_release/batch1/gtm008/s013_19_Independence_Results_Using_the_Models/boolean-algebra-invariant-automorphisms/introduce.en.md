For the complete Boolean algebra $B$ arising as the regular open algebra of the partial order $P$ used to prove the independence of $V = L$, the only elements fixed by every automorphism of $B$ are the trivial ones: $0$ and $1$. This shows that $B$ is in a strong sense rigid — no non-trivial element is preserved under all automorphisms. The result is used to establish that while GCH holds in $V^{(B)}$, the statement "there is a definable well-ordering of $\mathcal{P}(\omega)$" fails, demonstrating the independence of the existence of a definable well-ordering.
