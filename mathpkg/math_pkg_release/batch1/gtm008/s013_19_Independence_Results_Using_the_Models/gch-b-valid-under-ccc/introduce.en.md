If a complete Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the countable chain condition (c.c.c.) and has cardinality at most $2^{\aleph_0}$, then assuming the Generalized Continuum Hypothesis (GCH) holds in the ground model $V$, it follows that GCH is $B$-valid in the Boolean-valued model $V^{(B)}$. This provides a consistency result: when these conditions on $B$ are met, the Boolean-valued extension preserves the GCH. The proof uses the definiteness of the canonical embedding $(\omega_\alpha)^\vee$ and cardinality estimates on $B^{\mathcal{D}((\omega_\alpha)^\vee)}$ to show that the power set of $(\omega_\alpha)^\vee$ in $V^{(B)}$ has cardinality at most $\omega_{\alpha+1}$, establishing the GCH in the Boolean-valued universe.
