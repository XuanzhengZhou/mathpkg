The measure algebra $B = \mathcal{B}/N$ obtained from the $\sigma$-algebra of Borel sets of the generalized Cantor space $X = 2^{\omega \times I}$ (with $|I| > 2^{\aleph_0}$) modulo the $\sigma$-ideal $N$ of null sets for the product measure satisfies the countable chain condition (c.c.c.). Consequently, $B$ is a complete Boolean algebra. This result is essential for the measure-algebra approach to proving the independence of the Continuum Hypothesis, as c.c.c. Boolean algebras preserve cardinals and ensure that $\omega_1$ in $V^{(B)}$ equals $\check{\omega}_1$.
