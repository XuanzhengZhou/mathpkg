A measure algebra is a Boolean $\sigma$-algebra equipped with a strictly positive $\sigma$-additive probability measure. The measure assigns values in $[0,1]$, is strictly positive on nonzero elements, and is countably additive on disjoint sequences. Importantly, every measure algebra automatically satisfies the countable chain condition (c.c.c.) and is therefore a complete Boolean algebra. Measure algebras play a central role in the measure-theoretic approach to forcing and in the classification of complete Boolean algebras satisfying the $(\omega, \omega)$-weak distributive law.
