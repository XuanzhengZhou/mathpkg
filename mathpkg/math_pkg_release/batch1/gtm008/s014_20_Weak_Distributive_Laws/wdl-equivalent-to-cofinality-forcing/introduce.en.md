Theorem 20.3 provides a forcing-theoretic characterization of the $(\omega, \omega_\alpha)$-weak distributive law: a complete Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the law precisely when it forces that the cofinality of $(\omega_\alpha)^\vee$ is strictly greater than $\check{\omega}$. This equivalence is fundamental for understanding how weak distributive laws control the preservation of cardinal properties in Boolean-valued models of set theory.
