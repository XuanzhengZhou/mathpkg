Theorem 20.7 provides a concrete counterexample to the $(\omega, \omega)$-weak distributive law: the regular open algebra of the Baire space $\omega^\omega$ with the product topology. Using the clopen sets $\{p \in \omega^\omega : p(n) = m\}$, one can show that the product of sums equals $1$ while the sum over all choice functions of the corresponding products equals $0$. This demonstrates that the $(\omega, \omega)$-WDL is a nontrivial condition not satisfied by all complete Boolean algebras, contrasting with Theorem 20.6 which guarantees the law for measure algebras.
