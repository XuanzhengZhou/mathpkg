Theorem 20.2 establishes that the countable chain condition (c.c.c.) together with the assumption that $\omega_\alpha$ has uncountable cofinality is sufficient to guarantee the $(\omega, \omega_\alpha)$-weak distributive law. This is a key structural result linking chain conditions to distributivity properties in complete Boolean algebras, with important consequences for the preservation of cardinals in forcing extensions.
