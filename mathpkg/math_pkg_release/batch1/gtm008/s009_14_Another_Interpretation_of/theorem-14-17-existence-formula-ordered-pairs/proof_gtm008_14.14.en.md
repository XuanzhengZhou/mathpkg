By the same method as Theorem 14.16, replacing unordered pairs with ordered pairs:

$$[(\exists x)(\exists y)[\langle x, y \rangle = \check{k}_1 \land \varphi(x, y)]] = \sum_{\langle k_2, k_3 \rangle = k_1} [\varphi(\check{k}_2, \check{k}_3)].$$

The proof uses Theorem 13.13.1 together with the Boolean value of ordered-pair equality, which is 1 exactly when the ordered pairs are equal in the ground model and 0 otherwise.
