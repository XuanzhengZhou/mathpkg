When $B$ is a complete nonatomic Boolean algebra, the generic filter $F$ does not belong to the ground model $M$ in the Boolean sense. This is the Boolean-valued counterpart of the classical fact that a generic filter over a nonatomic forcing poset is not in the ground model. Together with Theorem 14.7, this establishes the atomic/nonatomic dichotomy.
