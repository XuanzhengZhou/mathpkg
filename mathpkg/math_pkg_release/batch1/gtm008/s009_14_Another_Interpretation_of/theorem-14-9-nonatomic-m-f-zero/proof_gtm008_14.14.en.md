$$[M(F)] = \sum_{k \in V} [F = \check{k}].$$

If $[F = \check{k}] \neq 0$ for some $k$, then $0 < [F = \check{k}] \cdot [F \subseteq \check{B}]$ by Theorem 14.8, so $0 < [\check{k} \subseteq \check{B}]$. This would imply $\check{k}$ determines a complete ultrafilter on $B$. But by Theorem 14.6, if $B$ is nonatomic, no such $k$ can exist (otherwise taking $S = k \cap B$ would produce a nonzero product contradicting Theorem 14.6). Hence $[F = \check{k}] = 0$ for all $k$, and $[M(F)] = 0$.
