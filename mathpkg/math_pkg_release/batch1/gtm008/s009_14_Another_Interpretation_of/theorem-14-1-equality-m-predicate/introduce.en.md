In the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$, the predicate $M(u)$ is defined as $\sum_{k \in R(\alpha)} [u = \check{k}]$, expressing that $u$ belongs to the canonical ground model. This theorem ensures that the M predicate is well-defined with respect to equality: if two elements are equal and one is in the ground model, then the other is also in the ground model in the Boolean sense.
