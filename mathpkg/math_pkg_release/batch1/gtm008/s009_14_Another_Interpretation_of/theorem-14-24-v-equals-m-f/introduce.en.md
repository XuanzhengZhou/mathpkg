This is the second main result of Section 14, establishing that in the Boolean-valued universe $\mathbf{V}^{(B)}$, the entire universe $V$ equals the generic extension $M[F]$ of the ground model $M$ by the generic filter $F$, with Boolean value 1. Together with Theorem 14.21 (M is a standard transitive model of ZF), this demonstrates the fundamental equivalence: any Boolean-valued model of ZF is, in the Boolean sense, a generic extension of an inner model. The proof uses Theorem 14.22 and Corollary 14.23; if $[[V = M[F]]] \neq 1$, then by the properties of the homomorphism $h$, one could construct a counterexample in some $N[G]$, leading to a contradiction.
