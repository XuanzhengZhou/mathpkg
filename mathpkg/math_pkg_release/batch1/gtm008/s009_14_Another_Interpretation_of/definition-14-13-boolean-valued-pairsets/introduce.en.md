In the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$, unordered pairs $\{u, v\}^{(B)}$, singletons $\{u\}^{(B)}$, and ordered pairs $\langle u, v \rangle^{(B)}$ are defined by multiplying the standard set-theoretic constructions with $\{1\}$ (i.e., assigning the Boolean value 1 to every element). This ensures that these Boolean-valued constructions are equivalent to their ground-model counterparts in the Boolean sense, as verified by Theorem 14.14.
