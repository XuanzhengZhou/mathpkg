This theorem establishes that the Boolean truth value of the equality $\{\check{k}_2, \check{k}_3\} = \check{k}_1$ in $V^{(B)}$ is 1 exactly when the unordered pair $\{k_2, k_3\}$ equals $k_1$ as sets in the ground model, and 0 otherwise. It demonstrates that the canonical check embedding preserves unordered pair equality, which is fundamental for working with Boolean-valued pair constructions.
