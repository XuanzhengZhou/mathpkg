For the relativization $\phi^M$ of any axiom $\phi$ of ZF, $[\phi^M] = 1$ in $\mathbf{V}^{(B)}$. In particular, if $\phi$ is $(\forall x)\psi(x)$, then
$$[\phi^M] = \prod_{k \in V} [\psi(\check{k})] = 1,$$
and if $\phi$ is $(\exists x)\psi(x)$, then
$$[\phi^M] = \sum_{k \in V} [\psi(\check{k})] = 1.$$
