$$\check{k}_1 \cap \check{k}_2 = \{ \langle \check{k}, 1 \rangle \mid k \in k_1 \} \cap \{ \langle \check{k}, 1 \rangle \mid k \in k_2 \}
= \{ \langle \check{k}, 1 \rangle \mid k \in k_1 \cap k_2 \} = (k_1 \cap k_2)^{\vee}.$$

The equality of the intersections follows directly from the definition of the check operation and the extensionality of sets.
