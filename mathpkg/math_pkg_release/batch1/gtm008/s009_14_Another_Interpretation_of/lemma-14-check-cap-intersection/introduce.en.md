This lemma (Lemma 4 of Section 14) shows that the Boolean-valued intersection of two check sets equals the check of their ground-model intersection with Boolean value 1. Together with Lemma 2 (set difference) and Lemma 3 (membership in intersection), this establishes that the canonical check embedding preserves all basic set-theoretic operations in the Boolean-valued sense.
