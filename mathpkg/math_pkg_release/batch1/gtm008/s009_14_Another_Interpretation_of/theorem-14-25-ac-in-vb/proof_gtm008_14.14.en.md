Choose $N$ and $\mathbf{B}$ as in Corollary 14.23 (using the system GB + Mathematical Induction). Then $N$ satisfies the Axiom of Choice (since $V$ satisfies AC and $N$ is a model of a sufficiently large fragment of ZF). Consequently, for every $G$ that is $\mathbf{P}$-generic over $N$, the generic extension $N[G]$ also satisfies the Axiom of Choice. By Theorem 14.24, it follows that $[[AC]] = 1$ in $\mathbf{V}^{(B)}$.

The key point is that Theorem 14.24 reduces the truth of AC in $V^{(B)}$ to its truth in all generic extensions $N[G]$, and generic extensions of models of AC are known to preserve AC.
