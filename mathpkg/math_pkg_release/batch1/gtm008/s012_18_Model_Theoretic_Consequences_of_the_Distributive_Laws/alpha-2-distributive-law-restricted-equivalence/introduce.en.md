For any infinite cardinal $\alpha$, a complete Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the $(\alpha, 2)$-distributive law if and only if it satisfies the restricted $(\alpha, 2)$-distributive law, which imposes the additional condition that the complementary entries are negations of each other. This equivalence simplifies verification of the distributive law by reducing the general case to a special configuration of the Boolean values.
