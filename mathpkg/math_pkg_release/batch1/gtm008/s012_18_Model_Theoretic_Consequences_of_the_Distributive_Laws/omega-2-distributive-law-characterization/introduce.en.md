A complete Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the $(\omega, 2)$-distributive law exactly when the Boolean value of the equality between the canonical image of the ground-model powerset $\mathcal{P}(\omega)^{\vee}$ and the Boolean-valued powerset $\mathcal{P}(\check{\omega})$ is $1$. This theorem provides a model-theoretic reformulation of a purely algebraic property of the Boolean algebra $B$, linking the distributivity condition to the behavior of powersets in the corresponding Boolean-valued model $V^{(B)}$.
