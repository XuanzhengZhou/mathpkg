Theorem 18.4 establishes that for any infinite cardinal $\alpha$, a complete Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the full $(\alpha, 2)$-distributive law if and only if it satisfies the restricted $(\alpha, 2)$-distributive law. This equivalence means that when checking the full distributive law, one may assume without loss of generality that the paired elements are complementary, i.e. $b_{i0} = -b_{i1}$ for each $i$.
