This lemma provides another equivalent formulation of the $(I, J)$-distributive law in a complete Boolean algebra. It states that if for a family $\{b_{ij}\}$ all products along selection functions $f \in J^I$ are zero, and all row sums $\sum_{j \in J} b_{ij}$ equal a constant element $b$, then necessarily $b = 0$. The proof shows that this criterion is equivalent to the distributive law via an algebraic manipulation using Theorem 4.2.
