The Maximum Principle is the central result of Chapter 16. It asserts that in a Boolean-valued universe $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$, the truth value of any existential statement $(\exists u)\varphi(u)$ is always attained by some particular element $v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$: there exists $v$ such that $\llbracket (\exists u)\varphi(u) \rrbracket = \llbracket \varphi(v) \rrbracket$. This eliminates the need to quantify over classes in many arguments and is a cornerstone for working with Boolean-valued models. The proof uses a partition-of-unity argument together with Theorem 16.1 to construct $v$ from an enumeration of $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$.
