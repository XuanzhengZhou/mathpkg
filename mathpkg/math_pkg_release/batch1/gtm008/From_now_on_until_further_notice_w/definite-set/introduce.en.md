A definite set $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is a Boolean-valued set that assigns the maximal truth value $1$ to every element in its domain. The canonical embedding $\check{k}$ of any set $k$ is definite. Definite sets play a special role because they behave most like ordinary sets: every element has full membership. Together with extensional functions, definite sets allow a precise correspondence between real functions on $\mathcal{D}(u)$ and functions internal to $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$. Corollary 16.21 shows that every $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ can be factored as $b \cdot v$ with $v$ definite and uniform.
