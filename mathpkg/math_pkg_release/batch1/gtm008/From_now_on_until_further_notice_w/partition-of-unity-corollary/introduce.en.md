This corollary is extracted from the construction used in the proof of the Maximum Principle. Given a partition of unity $\{b_\xi\}$ in the complete Boolean algebra $B$ and a family of Boolean-valued sets $\{u_\xi\}$ with domains contained in a common set $d$, one can construct a single $v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ whose Boolean-valued equality with each $u_\xi$ is at least $b_\xi$. This $v$ is built by mixing the $u_\xi$ with coefficients $b_\xi$. An important consequence is that every $v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ can be represented by a set with complete domain.
