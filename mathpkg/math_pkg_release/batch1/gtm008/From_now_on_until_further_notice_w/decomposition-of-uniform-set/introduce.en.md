Theorem 16.20 provides a canonical decomposition of any uniform set $u$ as $b \cdot v$ where $b = \sup(u)$ and $v$ is definite and uniform. The set $v$ is defined by restricting the domain of $u$ to those elements where $u$ attains its maximum value (the supremum), and assigning them value $1$. The proof uses Theorem 16.14 and the partition-of-unity $\{u(x), \neg u(x)\}$ to show that for any $x \in \mathcal{D}(u)$, we can mix it with an element attaining the supremum to get an element in $\mathcal{D}(v)$ that is sufficiently close to $x$ in the Boolean-valued sense. Corollary 16.21 generalizes this to all $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$.
