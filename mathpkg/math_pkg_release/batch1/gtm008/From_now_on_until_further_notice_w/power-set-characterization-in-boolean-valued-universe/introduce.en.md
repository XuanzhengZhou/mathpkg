This theorem resolves a fundamental issue in Boolean-valued set theory: while the class of all $v$ with $\llbracket v \subseteq u \rrbracket = 1$ may have domains of unbounded size, the truth value $\llbracket v \subseteq u \rrbracket$ can be expressed entirely in terms of witnesses $v'$ whose domains are restricted to $\mathcal{D}(u)$. The set $A$ of such restricted witnesses is a genuine set (not a proper class), making the Boolean-valued power set operation well-defined and manageable within the universe.
