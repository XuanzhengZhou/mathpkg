The $U^{(\mathbf{B})}$ hierarchy is an alternative cumulative construction of the Boolean-valued universe, analogous to the standard $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ hierarchy. Here $U_0^{(\mathbf{B})} = 0$, $U_{\alpha+1}^{(\mathbf{B})} = B^{U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}}$ (all functions from $U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ into $B$), and limits are taken as unions. Unlike $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$, each $u \in U^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is a function whose domain is the entire $U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ for some $\alpha$, rather than an arbitrary subset. Despite this difference, $U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ and $V_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ are essentially equivalent (Theorem 16.6), and the $U^{(\mathbf{B})}$ hierarchy has the advantage that $U_{\alpha+1}^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is always complete.
