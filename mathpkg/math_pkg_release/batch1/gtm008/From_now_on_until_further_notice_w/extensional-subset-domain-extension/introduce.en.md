This theorem shows that any family of Boolean-valued sets whose domains are contained in a common set $d$ can be replaced by an equivalent family (with Boolean value $1$ of equality) whose domains all equal $d$. The construction extends each $u_i$ by defining the new value at $x \in d$ as $\llbracket x \in u_i \rrbracket$. This technical result is essential for the proof of the Maximum Principle.
