This exercise extends the previous one by exploring the Boolean consequences of comparing $b$ with $\sup(u)$. It formalizes how the scaling operation $b \cdot u$ interacts with the ordering in the Boolean algebra: a scalar $b$ greater than or equal to the supremum leaves the set unchanged; a scalar strictly smaller reduces it; and the relationship between $b \cdot u = 0$ and $u = 0$ is given by a Boolean logical formula. These results are fundamental for understanding how Boolean truth values in $B$ control the behavior of sets in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$.
