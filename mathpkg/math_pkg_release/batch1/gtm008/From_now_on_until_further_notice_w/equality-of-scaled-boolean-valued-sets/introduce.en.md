This exercise establishes the relationship between scaling $u$ by $b$ and the supremum $\sup(u)$ for extensional sets. The main identity $\llbracket u = b \cdot u \rrbracket = \llbracket \sup(u) \Rightarrow b \rrbracket$ shows that $u$ equals $b \cdot u$ to exactly the extent that $\sup(u)$ implies $b$ in the Boolean algebra. As consequences: if $b$ dominates $\sup(u)$, then $b \cdot u = u$; if $b$ is strictly below $\sup(u)$, then $b \cdot u$ is strictly different from $u$; and the truth value of $u$ being empty equals the negation of its supremum. This is used critically in Theorem 16.22 part 3.
