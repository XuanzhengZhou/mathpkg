Let $u$ be uniform. If $\{x_i \mid i \in I\} \subseteq \mathcal{D}(u)$ and $\{b_i \mid i \in I\} \subseteq B$ is a partition of unity (i.e., $b_i \cdot b_j = 0$ for $i \neq j$ and $\sum_{i \in I} b_i = 1$), then
$$u\!\left(\sum_{i \in I} b_i x_i\right) = \sum_{i \in I} b_i \, u(x_i).$$
\end{theorem}

(Here $\sum_{i \in I} b_i x_i$ denotes the Boolean mixture of elements of $\mathcal{D}(u)$, which exists since $\mathcal{D}(u)$ is complete.)
