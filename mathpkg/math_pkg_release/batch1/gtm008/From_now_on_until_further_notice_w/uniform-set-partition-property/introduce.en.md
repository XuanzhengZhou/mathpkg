Theorem 16.14 establishes a crucial linearity property of uniform Boolean-valued sets with respect to partitions of unity. If $u$ is uniform and $\{x_i\} \subseteq \mathcal{D}(u)$, then the value $u$ assigns to a Boolean mixture $\sum b_i x_i$ equals the same Boolean mixture of the individual values $u(x_i)$. This property reflects that $u(x) = \llbracket x \in u \rrbracket$ for uniform $u$, and the Boolean truth of membership in $u$ behaves linearly under mixing. The proof uses the completeness of $\mathcal{D}(u)$ and the disjointness of the $b_i$ to obtain both inequalities. This theorem is essential for establishing that uniform sets attain their supremum (Theorem 16.19).
