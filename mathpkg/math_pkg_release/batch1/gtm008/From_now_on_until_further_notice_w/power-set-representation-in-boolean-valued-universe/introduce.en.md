Theorem 16.25 provides a crucial identity for working with power sets in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$. The Boolean truth value of $v$ being a subset of $u$ can be expressed as the join of $\llbracket v = v' \rrbracket$ over all $v'$ that have the same domain as $u$ and are fully subsets of $u$ (truth value $1$). This identity follows from Theorem 16.24 which allows any subset $v'$ of $u$ to be adjusted so that its domain equals $\mathcal{D}(u)$. It is a key step toward determining the power set $\mathcal{P}(u)$ in the Boolean-valued universe, as it shows $\llbracket v \subseteq u \rrbracket$ can be reconstructed from information within the set-sized collection $A$.
