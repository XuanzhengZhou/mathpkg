Let $u, v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ be definite and let $\varphi: \mathcal{D}(u) \to \mathcal{D}(v)$ be an extensional function. Then
$$(\exists f \in V^{(\mathbf{B})})\; \llbracket f: u \to v \rrbracket = 1 \land (\forall x \in \mathcal{D}(u))\; \llbracket f(x) = \varphi(x) \rrbracket = 1.$$
That is, there exists a Boolean-valued set $f$ that is a function from $u$ to $v$ with truth value $1$, and for each $x \in \mathcal{D}(u)$, $f(x)$ equals $\varphi(x)$ with truth value $1$ in the sense of $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$.
