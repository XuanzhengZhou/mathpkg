\textbf{Maximum Principle (Theorem 16.2).}
$$(\exists v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}) [\llbracket (\exists u) \varphi(u) \rrbracket = \llbracket \varphi(v) \rrbracket].$$

That is, for every formula $\varphi$, there exists a $v \in V^{(B)}$ such that the truth value of $(\exists u)\varphi(u)$ is exactly equal to the truth value of $\varphi(v)$.

\textbf{Corollary 16.3 (Partition of Unity Form).}
Suppose $\{u_\xi \mid \xi < \alpha\} \subseteq V^{(B)}$, $\{b_\xi \mid \xi < \alpha\} \subseteq B$ is a partition of unity, and $d \subseteq V^{(B)}$. Then there exists $v \in V^{(B)}$ such that $\mathcal{D}(v) = d$ and $b_\xi \leq \llbracket v = u_\xi \rrbracket$ for all $\xi < \alpha$.
