Corollary 16.21 is a powerful structure theorem for the Boolean-valued universe: every $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is equal, with Boolean truth value $1$, to $b \cdot v$ where $b \in B$ and $v$ is both definite and uniform. This decomposition reduces the study of arbitrary Boolean-valued sets to the study of definite uniform sets multiplied by a scalar. The proof combines Theorem 16.20 (decomposition of uniform sets) with the earlier results that every set can be made extensional (Theorem 16.11). This factorization is fundamental for the subsequent development of power sets and the rank function in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$.
