$$(\forall u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}) (\exists b \in B) (\exists v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})})\; [v \text{ is definite and uniform} \land \llbracket u = b \cdot v \rrbracket = 1].$$
That is, every element of $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ can be represented (with truth value $1$) as a scalar $b \in B$ times a definite uniform set $v$.
