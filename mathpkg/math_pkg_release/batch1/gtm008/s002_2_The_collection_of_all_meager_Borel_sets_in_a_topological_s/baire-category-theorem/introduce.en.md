The Baire Category Theorem (Theorem 3.23) states that in a locally compact Hausdorff space, no nonempty open set can be covered by countably many nowhere dense sets. Equivalently, the whole space is not meager -- it is of second category. The proof constructs a nested sequence of closed neighborhoods whose intersection is nonempty by compactness, yet lies outside the countable union of nowhere dense sets, yielding a contradiction.
