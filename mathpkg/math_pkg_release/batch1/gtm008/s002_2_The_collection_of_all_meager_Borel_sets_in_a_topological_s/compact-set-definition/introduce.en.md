Definition 3.12 formalizes the notion of a compact subset of a topological space: every open cover of $A$ admits a finite subcover. The quantifier structure uses the predicate $\mathrm{Fin}^*(S')$ to assert that $S'$ is a finite collection. This purely set-theoretic definition (via the topology $T$) is the starting point for all compactness properties developed in the chapter.
