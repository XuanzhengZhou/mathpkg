Theorem 3.18 establishes that the restriction of a topology to a subset yields a well-defined topological space. More importantly, it shows that compactness of a subset is equivalent to the subspace itself being a compact space, and that the Hausdorff property is hereditary (passes to arbitrary subspaces). These facts are essential for working with subsets as independent topological entities.
