This classical theorem (appearing before Theorem 3.18 in the section, likely numbered 3.16) asserts that in any Hausdorff topological space, every compact subset is necessarily closed. The proof uses the Hausdorff separation property to construct, for each point $b \notin A$, a finite family of neighborhoods covering $A$ whose closures miss $b$, establishing that the complement $X - A$ is open. This result is fundamental for the interplay between compactness and the separation axioms.
