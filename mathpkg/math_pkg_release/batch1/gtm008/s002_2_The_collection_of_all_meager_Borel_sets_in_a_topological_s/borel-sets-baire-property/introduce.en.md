Theorem 3.10 states that every Borel subset of a topological space admits a representation as the symmetric difference of an open set and a meager set; in other words, all Borel sets possess the property of Baire. The proof proceeds by transfinite induction on the Borel hierarchy, verifying the property for basic open and closed sets, then showing it is preserved under countable unions. This theorem links measure-theoretic and topological regularity.
