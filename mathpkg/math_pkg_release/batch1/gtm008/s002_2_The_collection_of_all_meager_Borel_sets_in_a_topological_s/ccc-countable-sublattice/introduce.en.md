A Boolean algebra satisfies the countable chain condition (c.c.c.) if every family of pairwise disjoint nonzero elements is at most countable. This property is fundamental in forcing and in the study of measure algebras. The proof given in the text uses a maximality argument and an auxiliary construction to show that from any infinite family of disjoint elements, a countable subfamily can be extracted.
