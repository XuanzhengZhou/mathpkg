Theorem 3.9 establishes that the family of all meager subsets of a topological space $X$ forms a proper $\sigma$-ideal within the power-set algebra $\mathcal{P}(X)$. When restricted to the Boolean $\sigma$-algebra of Borel sets, the meager Borel sets likewise constitute a proper $\sigma$-ideal. This is a foundational result for the Baire category approach, ensuring that countable unions of meager sets remain meager and that the whole space is never meager.
