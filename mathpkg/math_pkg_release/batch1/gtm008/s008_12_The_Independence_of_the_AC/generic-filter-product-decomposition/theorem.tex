Let $P = P_1 \times P_2$ be a product of two partial order structures.

\textbf{Theorem 12.6.} If $G_1$ is $P_1$-generic over $M$ and $G_2$ is $P_2$-generic over $M[G_1]$, then $G_1 \times G_2$ is $P$-generic over $M$.

\textbf{Theorem 12.7.} If $G$ is $P$-generic over $M$, then there exists $G_1$ which is $P_1$-generic over $M$ and $G_2$ which is $P_2$-generic over $M[G_1]$ such that $G = G_1 \times G_2$.
