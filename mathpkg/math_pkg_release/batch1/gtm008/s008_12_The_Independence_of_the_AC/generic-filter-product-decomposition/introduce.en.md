These two theorems establish the equivalence between a generic filter on a product forcing $P_1 \times P_2$ and a pair of generics obtained through iterated forcing. Theorem 12.6 shows that iterated generics produce a product generic; Theorem 12.7 provides the converse decomposition. This product-generic analysis is a fundamental tool in forcing theory, allowing complex forcing notions to be factored into simpler components.
