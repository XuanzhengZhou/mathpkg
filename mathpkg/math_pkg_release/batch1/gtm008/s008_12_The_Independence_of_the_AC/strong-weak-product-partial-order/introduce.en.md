The strong and weak products of a family of partial order structures generalize the componentwise partial order to the full Cartesian product and its finitely-supported subproduct, respectively. The strong product consists of all choice functions ordered pointwise, while the weak product restricts to functions that differ from the greatest element on only finitely many coordinates. These constructions are essential for the independence proof of the Axiom of Choice, where the weak product of countably many copies of a Cohen forcing poset is employed.
