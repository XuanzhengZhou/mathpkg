This theorem establishes that forcing with a countably closed partial order does not add new countable sequences of ordinals. If every countable compatible subset of the forcing poset has a lower bound in the ground model, then the generic extension contains no new $\omega$-sequences of ordinals. This is a fundamental preservation result in forcing theory, with applications to independence proofs involving cardinal characteristics and the Axiom of Choice.
