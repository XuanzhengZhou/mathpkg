Let $D_{M[G]}(t)$ be an $\omega$-sequence of ordinals in $M[G]$. By Theorem 10.9, it suffices to show that $\{p \in \mathbf{P} \mid p \Vdash V(t)\}$ is dense below any given condition. Using the countable closure hypothesis, one constructs a descending $\omega$-chain of conditions $p_0 \geq p_1 \geq \cdots$, where $p_n$ decides the $n$-th value of the sequence. Since the chain is countable and compatible, there exists a lower bound $q \in \mathbf{P}$. By construction, $q$ forces that $t$ equals some $\omega$-sequence of ordinals from the ground model $M$, establishing that the sequence belongs to $M$.
