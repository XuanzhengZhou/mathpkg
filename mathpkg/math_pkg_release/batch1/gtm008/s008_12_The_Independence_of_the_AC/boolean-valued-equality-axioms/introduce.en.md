This theorem, proved by induction on the rank $\alpha$, establishes the fundamental properties of Boolean-valued equality: substitutivity in both arguments of membership (parts 1 and 2) and transitivity of equality (part 3). These properties make equality a congruence with respect to membership, which is essential for $V^{(B)}$ to be a valid Boolean-valued structure for set theory.
