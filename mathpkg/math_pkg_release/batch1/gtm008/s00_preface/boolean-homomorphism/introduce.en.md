A Boolean homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between Boolean algebras that respects addition, multiplication, and complementation. An epimorphism is a surjective homomorphism. Boolean homomorphisms are central to the study of ideals, quotients, and the representation theory of Boolean algebras, including the isomorphism between a complete Boolean algebra and its associated regular open set algebra (Theorem 1.40).
