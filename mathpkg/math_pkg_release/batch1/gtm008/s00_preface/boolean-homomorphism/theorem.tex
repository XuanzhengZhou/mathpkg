$f$ is a \text{Boolean homomorphism} iff there are Boolean algebras $B_1$ and $B_2$ such that $f: |B_1| \rightarrow |B_2|$ and $\forall x, y \in |B_1|$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $f(x + y) = f(x) + f(y)$.
\item $f(xy) = f(x)f(y)$.
\item $f(-x) = -f(x)$.
\end{enumerate}
Under these conditions $f$ is a homomorphism from $B_1$ into $B_2$. If $f: |B_1| \xrightarrow[\text{onto}]{} |B_2|$, then $f$ is an \text{epimorphism} from $B_1$ onto $B_2$.
