The Rasiowa-Sikorski theorem is a fundamental result linking Boolean algebras, partial orders, and generic sets. Given a countable family of prescribed suprema in a Boolean algebra, there exists a homomorphism to the 2-element Boolean algebra that preserves these suprema and sends a given nonzero element to 1. The proof constructs a generic filter by traversing a countable family of dense sets, extending it to an ultrafilter, and defining the homomorphism via the ultrafilter. This theorem is a key ingredient in the independence proofs of set theory.
