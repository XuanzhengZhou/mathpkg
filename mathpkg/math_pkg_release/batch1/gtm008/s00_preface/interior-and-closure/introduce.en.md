The interior $A^0$ of a set $A$ consists of all points having a neighborhood entirely contained in $A$. The closure $A^-$ consists of all points every neighborhood of which meets $A$. These are the fundamental topological operators. A set is open iff $A = A^0$, closed iff $A = A^-$, and regular open iff $A = A^{-0}$.
