**Proof.** The proof uses Theorem 1.22 (interior/closure properties) and Theorem 1.27 (distribution of closure over union). Items 1 and 2 follow directly from the definitions. For item 3, if $x \in A^0 \cap B^-$, then there exists a neighborhood of $x$ contained in $A$, and every neighborhood of $x$ meets $B$, so every neighborhood of $x$ meets $A \cap B$, giving $x \in (A \cap B)^-$. Item 4 uses monotonicity of the $^{-0}$ operation.
