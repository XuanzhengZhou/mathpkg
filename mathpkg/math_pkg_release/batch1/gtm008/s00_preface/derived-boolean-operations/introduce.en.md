This definition introduces four derived operations in a Boolean algebra. Subtraction $a-b$ is defined as $a$ multiplied by the complement of $b$. Logical implication $a \Rightarrow b$ is defined as $-a + b$. Logical equivalence $a \Leftrightarrow b$ is the conjunction of both directions of implication. The natural order $\leq$ is defined by the absorption condition $ab = a$.
