A subset $G$ of a partial order $P$ is $P$-generic over a class $A$ if it is compatible (consistent), upward-closed, and meets every dense subset of $P$ that belongs to $A$. Generic sets are the fundamental objects in forcing: they represent "generic" collections of conditions that are sufficiently rich to determine a generic extension of the ground model. The third condition (meeting all dense sets in $A$) is the key genericity requirement.
