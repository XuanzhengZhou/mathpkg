A filter in a Boolean algebra is a nonempty subset (not containing $0$) that is closed under finite meets (multiplication) and upward closed under the natural order. Filters are the dual notion to ideals (by the correspondence $a \in F \leftrightarrow -a \in I$). Ultrafilters -- maximal proper filters -- are central to the representation theory of Boolean algebras and to the construction of generic extensions via Boolean-valued models.
