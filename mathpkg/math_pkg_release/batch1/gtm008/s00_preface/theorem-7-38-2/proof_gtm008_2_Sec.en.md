1. Similar to Theorem 7.10 for a language with constants $K$ ( ) and $F$ ( ).

2. If $\alpha = \text{rank}(a)$ then $a \subseteq R(\alpha)$. Therefore $\bar{F}_\alpha = a$ and hence

$$a \in Df(\mathbf{B}_\alpha) = B_{\alpha+1}.$$

3. For each $\gamma$ it is easily shown by transfinite induction on $\alpha$ that $B_\alpha(\gamma) \subseteq L[K]$, where $L[K \cap R(\gamma)] = \bigcup_{\alpha \in On} B_\alpha(\gamma)$. Conversely, if $a \in L[K] (\exists \alpha)[a \in B_\alpha]$. But for each $\alpha$ there is a $\gamma(\geq \alpha)$ such that $B_\alpha \subseteq L[K \cap R(\gamma)]$. Therefore

$$a \in \bigcup_{\alpha \in On} L[K \cap R(\alpha)].$$
