This definition introduces the notation for arbitrary (possibly infinite) sums (suprema) and products (infima) in a Boolean algebra. The sum $\sum_{a \in A} a$ is the least upper bound of the subset $A$, while the product $\prod_{a \in A} a$ is the greatest lower bound. When these exist for all subsets, the Boolean algebra is complete (Definition 1.10).
