The quotient $B/I$ of a Boolean algebra by a proper ideal is defined by the equivalence relation $a \sim b \leftrightarrow a(-b) + b(-a) \in I$ (the symmetric difference belongs to $I$). The equivalence class of $a$ is denoted $a/I$. This construction parallels the quotient of a ring by an ideal and yields a new Boolean algebra (Theorem 1.48).
