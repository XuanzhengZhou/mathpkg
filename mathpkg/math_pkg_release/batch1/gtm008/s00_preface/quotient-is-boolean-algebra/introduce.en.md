This theorem completes the quotient construction by stating that $B/I$ inherits the Boolean algebra structure from $B$. The operations are defined by $(a/I) + (b/I) = (a+b)/I$, $(a/I)(b/I) = (ab)/I$, $-(a/I) = (-a)/I$, with $0/I$ and $1/I$ as the distinguished elements. The proof is a routine verification of the Boolean algebra axioms using the well-definedness established in Theorem 1.47.
