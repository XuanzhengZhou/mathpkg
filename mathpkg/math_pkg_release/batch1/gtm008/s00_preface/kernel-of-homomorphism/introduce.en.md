The kernel of a Boolean homomorphism is the set of elements mapped to $0$. The kernel is always a proper ideal (Theorem 1.44), and the quotient construction (Definition 1.46) shows that every proper ideal arises as the kernel of some homomorphism. This establishes the fundamental homomorphism theorem for Boolean algebras.
