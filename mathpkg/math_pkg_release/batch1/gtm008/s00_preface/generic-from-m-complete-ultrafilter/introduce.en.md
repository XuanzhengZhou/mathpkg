This theorem provides the reverse direction of the correspondence: from a proper $M$-complete ultrafilter $F$ on the regular open set algebra, one recovers a $P$-generic set $G = \{p \in P \mid [p]^{-0} \in F\}$. The proof verifies that $G$ meets every dense set in $M$ by using the $M$-completeness of $F$ to show that the supremum of the regular open closures of elements of a dense set equals $1$, forcing $G$ to intersect the dense set.
