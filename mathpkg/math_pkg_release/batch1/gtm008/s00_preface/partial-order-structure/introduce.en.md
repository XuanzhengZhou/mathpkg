A partial order structure consists of a set $P$ together with a reflexive, antisymmetric, and transitive relation $\leq$. This generalizes the natural order of a Boolean algebra (Theorem 1.6). Partial order structures are fundamental to the theory of forcing and Boolean-valued models, as they provide the framework for the order topology and the duality with complete Boolean algebras.
