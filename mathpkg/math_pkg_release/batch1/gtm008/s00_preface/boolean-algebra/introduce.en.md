A Boolean algebra is an algebraic structure with two binary operations (addition $+$ and multiplication $\cdot$), a unary operation (complementation $^-$), and two distinguished constants $0$ and $1$, satisfying the commutative, associative, distributive, identity, and complementation laws. This axiomatization captures the algebraic properties of classical propositional logic and set-theoretic operations. Boolean algebras serve as the foundational algebraic structure for much of the subsequent theory developed in this text.
