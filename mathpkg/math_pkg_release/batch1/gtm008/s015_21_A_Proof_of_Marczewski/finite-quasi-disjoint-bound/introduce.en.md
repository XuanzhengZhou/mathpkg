Theorem 21.3 provides a cardinality bound for an index set $T$ when there exist two associated families $\{A_t\}$ and $\{B_t\}$ satisfying a separating property: each $A_t$ is disjoint from its corresponding $B_t$, but $A_t$ intersects $B_{t'}$ for any distinct $t, t' \in T$. Under the condition that each $A_t$ has cardinality at most $\alpha$ and each $B_t$ has cardinality at most $\gamma$ (with $\gamma$ infinite), the index set $T$ has cardinality at most $\gamma^\alpha$. When $\alpha$ is finite, this yields $|T| \leq \gamma$, which is the key step in proving Corollary 21.4 and Marczewski's theorem.
