A family of sets $a$ is quasi-disjoint if any two distinct members of the family intersect in exactly the same set, namely the intersection of the entire family $\bigcap a$. Equivalently, any element not in the global intersection $\bigcap a$ belongs to at most one member of the family. Quasi-disjointness is a key structural property used in the Erdős–Rado theorem and Marczewski's theorem to bound the cardinality of certain families of sets.
