Corollary 21.6 applies Marczewski's theorem to product topology: if each factor space $X_i$ has a base of cardinality at most $\gamma$ (an infinite cardinal), then the Boolean algebra of regular open subsets of the product $\prod_i X_i$ satisfies the $\gamma$-chain condition — every pairwise disjoint family of regular open sets has cardinality at most $\gamma$. The special case $\gamma = \aleph_0$ yields the countable chain condition (ccc) for products of second-countable spaces. This is a foundational result in set-theoretic topology, with implications for Suslin lines and the consistency of Suslin's hypothesis.
