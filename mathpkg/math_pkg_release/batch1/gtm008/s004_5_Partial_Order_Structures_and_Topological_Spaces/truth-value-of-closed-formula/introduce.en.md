The truth value $[\![\varphi]\!]$ of a closed formula $\varphi$ in a $B$-valued structure is defined recursively using the Boolean algebra operations: conjunction becomes meet ($\cdot$), disjunction becomes join ($+$), negation becomes complement ($-$), universal quantification becomes the product (greatest lower bound) over all elements, and existential quantification becomes the sum (least upper bound). For the special case $B = 2$, this reduces to the usual definition of satisfaction in ordinary 2-valued logic.
