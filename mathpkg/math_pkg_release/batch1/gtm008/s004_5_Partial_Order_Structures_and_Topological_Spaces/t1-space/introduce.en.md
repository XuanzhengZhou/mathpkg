A $T_1$-space is a topological space satisfying the $T_1$ separation axiom: for any two distinct points $x$ and $y$, each has a neighborhood not containing the other. This is one of the weakest separation axioms, stronger than $T_0$ but weaker than Hausdorff ($T_2$). In a $T_1$-space, all singleton sets are closed.
