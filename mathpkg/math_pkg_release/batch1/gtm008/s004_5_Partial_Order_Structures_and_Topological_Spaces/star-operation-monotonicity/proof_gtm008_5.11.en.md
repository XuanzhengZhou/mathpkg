(1) If $G_1 \subseteq G_2$, then for any $p \in P$ with $[p] \subseteq G_1$, we also have $[p] \subseteq G_2$. Therefore $N(p) \subseteq G_1^*$ implies $N(p) \subseteq G_2^*$, giving $G_1^* \subseteq G_2^*$.

(2) Similarly, if $G_1 \subseteq G_2$ are open subsets of $\mathcal{F}$, then $N(p) \subseteq G_1$ implies $N(p) \subseteq G_2$, so $[p] \subseteq G_1^\Delta$ implies $[p] \subseteq G_2^\Delta$, giving $G_1^\Delta \subseteq G_2^\Delta$.
