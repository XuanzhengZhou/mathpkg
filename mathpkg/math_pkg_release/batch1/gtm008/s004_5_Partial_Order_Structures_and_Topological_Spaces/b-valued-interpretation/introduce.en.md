A Boolean-valued interpretation replaces the usual two-valued truth semantics (true/false) with truth values in a complete Boolean algebra $B$. An interpretation $\langle A, \phi \rangle$ consists of a nonempty domain $A$ and an assignment $\phi$ that maps individual constants to elements of $A$ and $n$-ary predicate constants to functions $A^n \to B$. This is the foundation of Boolean-valued model theory, which generalizes ordinary first-order model theory.
