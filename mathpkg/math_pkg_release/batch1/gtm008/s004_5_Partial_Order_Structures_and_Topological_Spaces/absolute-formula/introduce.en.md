A formula $\varphi$ is absolute with respect to a transitive model $M$ of ZF if its truth value is the same whether evaluated in the universe or relativized to $M$, for all parameters from $M$. Absoluteness is crucial in the study of constructibility because it ensures that the definitions used to build $L$ work correctly when relativized to larger models, allowing the construction to be carried out in ZF without additional assumptions.
