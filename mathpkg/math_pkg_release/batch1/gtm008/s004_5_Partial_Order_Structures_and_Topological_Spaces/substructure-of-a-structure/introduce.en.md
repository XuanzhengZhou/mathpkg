A structure $\mathbf{A}$ is a substructure of $\mathbf{B}$ if the domain of $\mathbf{A}$ is a subset of the domain of $\mathbf{B}$, the interpretations of relation symbols agree on the smaller domain, and the interpretations of constant symbols coincide. This is the standard model-theoretic notion of substructure, used in the general theory of relative constructibility to build the constructible hierarchy as a chain of substructures.
