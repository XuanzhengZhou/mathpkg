Theorem 7.16 is a reflection principle for the constructible hierarchy: for any formula $\varphi$, given an ordinal $\alpha$, there exists a larger ordinal $\beta$ such that any existential statement true in the full structure $\mathbf{M}$ with parameters from $M_\alpha$ is already witnessed by some element in $M_\beta$. This is proved using Theorem 7.7 (which expresses satisfaction by a formula) and taking the supremum of witnessing ordinals; the Axiom of Replacement ensures this supremum is bounded.
