For languages with equality, a $B$-valued interpretation must satisfy additional axioms ensuring that the equality predicate behaves appropriately as a $B$-valued equivalence relation. These axioms require reflexivity (truth value 1 for $c = c$), symmetry, a form of transitivity using the Boolean meet, and substitutivity: equal objects should be indistinguishable by all predicates, modulo the truth values of the equalities.
