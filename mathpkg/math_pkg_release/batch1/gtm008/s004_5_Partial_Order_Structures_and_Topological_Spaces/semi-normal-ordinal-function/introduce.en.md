A semi-normal function $F: On \to On$ is an ordinal function that is inflationary ($\alpha \leq F(\alpha)$), monotone non-decreasing, and continuous at limit ordinals (i.e., $F(\alpha) = \bigcup_{\beta < \alpha} F(\beta)$ for limit $\alpha$). Semi-normal functions are essential in the proof of the reflection principle, as they allow one to find closure ordinals at which the constructible hierarchy reflects formulas.
