If $b_{ij} \subseteq A$ for $i \in I$ and $j \in J$ then

$$b \in \bigcap_{i \in I} \bigcup_{j \in J} b_{ij} \leftrightarrow (\forall i \in I)(\exists j \in J)[b \in b_{ij}]$$

$$\leftrightarrow (\exists f \in J^I)(\forall i \in I)[b \in b_{ij}]$$

$$\leftrightarrow b \in \bigcup_{f \in I^I} \bigcap_{i \in I} b_{i,f(i)}.$$

Remark. We next show that to within isomorphism the complete natural Boolean algebras are the only completely distributive complete Boolean algebras.
