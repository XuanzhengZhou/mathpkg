In Boolean-valued model theory, a formula $\varphi$ is said to be satisfied in (or true in) a $B$-valued structure $A$ if its truth value $[\![\varphi]\!]$ equals $1$, the greatest element of the Boolean algebra $B$. This generalizes the classical notion of satisfaction: in ordinary 2-valued logic, a formula is true iff its truth value is the maximal truth value.
