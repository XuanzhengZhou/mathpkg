Theorem 7.15 establishes that any finite collection of semi-normal functions has arbitrarily large common fixed points. The proof constructs an $\omega$-sequence above $\alpha$ by iteratively applying the functions and taking suprema; the limit of this sequence is a common fixed point. This result is crucial for the reflection principle: one can find ordinals closed under finitely many definability operations simultaneously.
