Theorem 6.6 extends the correspondence between logical validity and $B$-valued satisfaction to languages with equality. Every logically valid sentence of such a language is satisfied in all $B$-valued structures that respect the equality axioms. As a corollary, the substitutivity principle generalizes from predicate symbols to arbitrary formulas: the Boolean product of the equality truth values times the truth value of $\varphi(c_1, \ldots, c_n)$ is bounded above by the truth value of $\varphi$ with substituted arguments.
