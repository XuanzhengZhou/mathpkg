Definition 23.31 introduces the notion of $(\aleph_\alpha, \aleph_\beta)$-splitable Boolean algebras. This property captures the ability to "split" a family of $\aleph_\alpha$-many partitions of unity (each of size $\aleph_\beta$) using a set $\Lambda$ of size at most $\aleph_\alpha$. The splitable property is crucial for Easton's construction of models where the Generalized Continuum Hypothesis fails at regular cardinals, as it ensures that cardinals are preserved in the forcing extension.
