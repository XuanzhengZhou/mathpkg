Theorem 23.30 establishes sufficient conditions for a Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$ to be a model of Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory with the Axiom of Choice (ZF+AC): the completion $\tilde{B}$ must satisfy the Uniform Convergence Law (UCL), and $B$ must satisfy the Sieve Law (SL). The proof involves constructing a function $K: \mathcal{P}^M(B_0 \times I) \to \mathcal{P}^{M[F]}(D(u))$ and showing it is onto, which demonstrates that the power set axiom holds in $V^{(B)}$.
