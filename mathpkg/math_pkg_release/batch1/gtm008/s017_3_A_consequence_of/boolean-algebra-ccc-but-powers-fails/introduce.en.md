Theorem 23.26 provides a crucial counterexample in the theory of Boolean-valued models: there exists a Boolean algebra $\tilde{B}$ that satisfies the countable chain condition (c.c.c.), and hence the Uniform Convergence Law (UCL), but whose Boolean-valued universe $V^{(B)}$ fails to satisfy the Axiom of Powers. This demonstrates that even well-behaved Boolean algebras do not guarantee all ZF axioms hold in $V^{(B)}$. The construction uses the partial order that adds $\alpha$-many subsets of $\omega$.
