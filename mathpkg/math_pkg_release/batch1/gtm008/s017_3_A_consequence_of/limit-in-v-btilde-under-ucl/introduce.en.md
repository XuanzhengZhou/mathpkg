Theorem 23.16 establishes that when the completion $\tilde{B}$ of a Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the Uniform Convergence Law (UCL), every element of the Boolean-valued universe $V^{(\tilde{B})}$ can be expressed as the limit of a sequence of elements from $V^{(B)}$. This result shows that $V^{(B)}$ is dense in $V^{(\tilde{B})}$ in the sense of the limit operation. The theorem is fundamental for passing between $V^{(B)}$ and $V^{(\tilde{B})}$ while preserving structural properties.
