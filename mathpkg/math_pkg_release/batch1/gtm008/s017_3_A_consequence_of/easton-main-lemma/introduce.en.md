Theorem 23.44, known as Easton's Main Lemma, is the central technical result in Easton's construction of models where the Generalized Continuum Hypothesis fails at regular cardinals. Given an $\aleph_\alpha$-sized family of elements $b_i$, each expressed as a sum $\sum_{j \in J_i} b_{ij}$, the lemma produces a condition $\bar{r}$ and a small set $\Lambda$ such that below $\bar{r}$, each $b_i$ is "decided" by some element of $\Lambda$ in the sense that either $p \cdot \bar{r} \leq b_{ij}$ or $p \cdot \bar{r} \leq -b_i$. The proof proceeds by cases on whether $\aleph_\alpha$ is regular or singular.
