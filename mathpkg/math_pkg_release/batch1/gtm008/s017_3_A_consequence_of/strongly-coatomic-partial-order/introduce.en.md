A coatomic partial order structure $P$ is called strongly coatomic if every subset $S$ of $CA(p)$ (the set of coatomic elements above $p$) can be realized as $CA(q)$ for some $q \in P$. This is a much stronger condition than mere coatomicity: it requires that the mapping $p \mapsto CA(p)$ be surjective onto the power set of $CA(p)$ in a certain sense. Strongly coatomic partial orders are used in Easton's construction to control the interactions between different levels of the forcing.
