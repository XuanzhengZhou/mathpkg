Theorem 23.21 states that the strong countable chain condition (s.c.c.) implies the Uniform Convergence Law (UCL) in a Boolean algebra. The s.c.c. is a condition on sequences of elements of $B$: if for a family of sequences $\langle b_{\alpha i} \mid \alpha \in On \rangle$ ($i \in I$, $I$ a set) that are nonincreasing and each has product $0$, then the complement sequences must eventually become constant. This result connects a structural chain condition to a convergence property important for Boolean-valued models.
