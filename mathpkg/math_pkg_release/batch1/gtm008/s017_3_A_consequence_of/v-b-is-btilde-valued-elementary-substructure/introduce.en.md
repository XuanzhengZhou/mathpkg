Theorem 23.18 establishes that $V^{(B)}$ is a $\tilde{B}$-valued elementary substructure of $V^{(\tilde{B})}$, provided the completion $\tilde{B}$ satisfies the Uniform Convergence Law (UCL). This means that any formula $\varphi$ evaluated in $V^{(B)}$ has exactly the same Boolean truth value when evaluated in $V^{(\tilde{B})}$ for elements of $V^{(B)}$. This theorem is crucial for transferring logical properties between the original Boolean-valued universe and the universe over the completion.
