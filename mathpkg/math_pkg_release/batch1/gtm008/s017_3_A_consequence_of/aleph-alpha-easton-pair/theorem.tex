Let $P_0 = \langle \Gamma, \leq_0, 1_0 \rangle$ and $P_1 = \langle \Delta, \leq_1, 1_1 \rangle$ be strongly coatomic partial order structures. They form an \emph{$\aleph_\alpha$-Easton pair} if $P_0 \times P_1$ is an $\aleph_\alpha$-bounded partial order and the product structure $P = P_0 \times P_1$ (with the abbreviated notation $p \cdot q$ for $\langle p, q \rangle$) has the property that two elements $p_1 \cdot q_1$ and $p_2 \cdot q_2$ are compatible iff $p_1, p_2$ are compatible and $q_1, q_2$ are compatible. Then $P$ is fine and $[p \cdot q]^{-0} = [p \cdot q]$.
