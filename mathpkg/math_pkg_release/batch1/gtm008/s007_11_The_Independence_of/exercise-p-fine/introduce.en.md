This exercise asks the reader to verify that the forcing poset $\mathbf{P}$ defined in Definition 11.1 satisfies the fine condition (Definition 5.21). A partial order is fine if for any condition $p$ and any finite set of ordinals, there exists an extension of $p$ that decides membership of those ordinals in the generic set. This property is crucial for the forcing construction used to prove independence results.
