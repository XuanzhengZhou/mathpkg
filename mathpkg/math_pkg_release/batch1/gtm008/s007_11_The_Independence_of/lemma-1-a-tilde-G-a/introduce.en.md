This lemma establishes that $\tilde{a}$ is a left inverse of $\tilde{G}$: for any subset $a$ of $\omega$, applying $\tilde{G}$ to produce a subset of $P$ and then $\tilde{a}$ to recover a subset of $\omega$ returns the original $a$. Together with Lemma 2, it shows that $\tilde{a}$ and $\tilde{G}$ form a bijective correspondence between subsets of $\omega$ and $P$-generic filters.
