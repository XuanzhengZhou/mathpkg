**Proof of Lemma 3.** If $G_1 = G_2$, then trivially $\tilde{a}(G_1) = \tilde{a}(G_2)$.

Conversely, suppose $\tilde{a}(G_1) = \tilde{a}(G_2)$. By Lemma 2, $G_1 = \tilde{G}(\tilde{a}(G_1)) = \tilde{G}(\tilde{a}(G_2)) = G_2$. The equality $\tilde{G}(\tilde{a}(G_i)) = G_i$ follows from Lemma 2 (showing $\supseteq$) together with Lemma 1 applied to the generic sets.

Thus $\tilde{a}$ is a one-to-one correspondence between $P$-generic sets over $M$ and subsets of $\omega$ obtained as $\tilde{a}(G)$. $\square$
