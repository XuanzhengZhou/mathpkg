This is Cohen's celebrated independence result: the Continuum Hypothesis is not provable from $\mathsf{ZFC}$. By forcing with finite partial functions from $\alpha \times \omega$ to $\{0, 1\}$ (the Cohen forcing adding $\alpha$-many Cohen reals), one obtains a model where there are at least $\alpha$ subsets of $\omega$. Since $\mathbf{P}$ satisfies the countable chain condition, cardinals are preserved, so choosing $\alpha > \omega_1$ yields a model of $\mathsf{ZFC} + \neg\mathsf{CH}$. This, together with Godel's result that $\mathsf{CH}$ holds in $L$, establishes the full independence of $\mathsf{CH}$ from $\mathsf{ZFC}$.
