Let $M$ be a countable standard transitive model of $\mathsf{ZF} + \mathsf{AC}$ (or $\mathsf{ZF} + V = L$). Let $\alpha$ be a cardinal in $M$. Define the forcing poset $\mathbf{P}$ in $M$ as the set of finite partial functions from $\alpha \times \omega$ to $\{0, 1\}$, ordered by reverse inclusion.

If $G$ is $\mathbf{P}$-generic over $M$, then $M[G]$ is a standard transitive model of $\mathsf{ZF} + \mathsf{AC} + \neg \mathsf{CH}$. Furthermore, for any given cardinal $\alpha \in M$, one can find $G$ and $\mathbf{P}$ such that

$$\overline{\mathcal{P}(\omega)} \geq \alpha \quad \text{in} \quad M[G].$$

More concretely, for each $\delta < \alpha$, define

$$a_\delta(G) \triangleq \{ n \in \omega \mid (\exists p \in G)[p(\delta, n) = 1] \}.$$

Starting with some $\alpha > \omega_1^M$, one obtains $\alpha > \omega_1^{M[G]}$, so that in $M[G]$ there are at least $\alpha$-many distinct subsets of $\omega$, and the continuum hypothesis fails.
