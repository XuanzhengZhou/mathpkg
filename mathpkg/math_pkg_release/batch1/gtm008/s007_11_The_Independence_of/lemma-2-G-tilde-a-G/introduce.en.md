This lemma shows that for any $P$-generic filter $G$, the set $\tilde{G}(\tilde{a}(G))$ is contained in $G$. The proof uses the density argument typical of forcing: given a condition $p = \langle p_1, p_2 \rangle$, one constructs a dense set $S$ that intersects $G$ and yields a condition below $p$, establishing $p \in G$. Together with Lemma 1, this yields Lemma 3's bijection.
