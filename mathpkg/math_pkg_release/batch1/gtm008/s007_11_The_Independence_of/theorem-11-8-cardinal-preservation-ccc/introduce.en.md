This fundamental theorem of forcing establishes that posets satisfying the countable chain condition (ccc) preserve cardinals. If $M \models \mathsf{ZFC}$ and $P$ has the ccc in $M$, then no cardinals are collapsed in the generic extension $M[G]$. The proof uses the Boolean algebra of regular open sets and the fact that antichains in ccc posets are countable to show that any would-be cardinal collapse would produce an uncountable antichain, a contradiction.
