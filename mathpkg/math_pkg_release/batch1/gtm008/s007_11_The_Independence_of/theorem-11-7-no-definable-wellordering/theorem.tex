Let $P$ be the partial order structure of Definition 11.1 and let $G$ be $P$-generic over $M$. Then in $M[G]$ there is no well-ordering of $\mathcal{P}(\omega)$ definable in $\mathcal{L}_0(C(M))$.

For $k \subseteq \omega$ with $\overline{k} < \omega$, define the automorphism $\pi_k$ of $P$ by

$$\pi_k(\langle p_1, p_2 \rangle) = \langle q_1, q_2 \rangle \quad \text{where} \quad q_1 = (p_1 - k) \cup (p_2 \cap k), \quad q_2 = (p_2 - k) \cup (p_1 \cap k).$$

Then $\pi_k \in M$, and the theorem follows from the identity

$$\tilde{a}(\pi_k''(G)) = \tilde{a}(G) \Delta k.$$
