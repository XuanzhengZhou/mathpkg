This result shows that not all complete Boolean algebras satisfy the weak distributive law. The regular open algebra of the Baire space $\omega^{\omega}$ (the topology underlying Cohen forcing) provides a concrete counterexample. The proof constructs a specific family $\{b_{nm}\}$ of clopen sets for which the left side of the WDL equation equals $1$ but the right side equals $0$, using a diagonalization argument involving dominating functions.
