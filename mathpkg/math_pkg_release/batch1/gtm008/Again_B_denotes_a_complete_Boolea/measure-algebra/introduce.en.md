A measure algebra is a Boolean $\sigma$-algebra equipped with a strictly positive $\sigma$-additive probability measure taking values in $[0,1]$. The measure is strictly positive (nonzero elements have positive measure) and countably additive on pairwise disjoint sequences. Every measure algebra automatically satisfies the countable chain condition and is therefore complete, making it suitable for forcing constructions that add random reals.
