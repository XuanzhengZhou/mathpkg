This theorem connects the countable chain condition (c.c.c.) with weak distributive laws. It shows that if a complete Boolean algebra has no uncountable antichains, and the cofinality of $\omega_\alpha$ is uncountable, then the $(\omega, \omega_\alpha)$-weak distributive law holds automatically. This is a key result in the theory of Boolean-valued models, because it guarantees that c.c.c. forcing notions preserve the cofinality of uncountable regular cardinals. In particular, it implies that c.c.c. forcing does not collapse cardinals or change cofinalities.
