The weak distributive law is a generalization of the ordinary distributive law $a \cdot (b + c) = a \cdot b + a \cdot c$ to infinite meets and joins in a complete Boolean algebra. The $(\omega, \omega)$-WDL states that any countable intersection of countable unions can be expressed as a union over dominating functions. This property is crucial in forcing arguments where one needs to control the cardinality of certain sets in generic extensions, particularly when proving that cardinals are preserved.
