This theorem provides the fundamental semantic characterization of the weak distributive law in terms of Boolean-valued models. It states that a complete Boolean algebra $B$ satisfies the $(\omega, \omega_\alpha)$-WDL precisely when the Boolean value of the statement "the cofinality of $\omega_\alpha^\vee$ is greater than $\check\omega$" is $1$ in $V^{(B)}$. Equivalently, the weak distributive law holds exactly when the Boolean-valued model thinks the cardinal $\omega_\alpha$ retains uncountable cofinality. This is the key mechanism by which forcing with $B$ preserves that $\omega_\alpha$ is not collapsed to have cofinality $\omega$.
