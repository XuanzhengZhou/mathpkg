Unlimited formulas of $R(K, F)$ are defined as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item If each of $t_1$ and $t_2$ is a constant term or a variable, then $K(t_1), F(t_1)$, and $t_1 \in t_2$ are unlimited formulas.
\item If $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are unlimited formulas, then $\neg \varphi$ and $\varphi \wedge \psi$ are unlimited formulas.
\item If $\varphi(x)$ is an unlimited formula in which $x$ is a variable, ranked or unranked, that does not occur as a bound variable, then $(\forall x)\varphi(x)$ is an unlimited formula.
\end{enumerate}
The ordinal measure of a formula:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\text{Ord}^3(\varphi)$ is the length of $\varphi$, i.e., the number of logical symbols in $\varphi$ where atomic formulas are assigned length 1.
\item $\text{Ord}(\varphi) = \omega^2 \cdot \text{Ord}^1(\varphi) + \omega \cdot \text{Ord}^2(\varphi) + \text{Ord}^3(\varphi)$.
\end{enumerate}
