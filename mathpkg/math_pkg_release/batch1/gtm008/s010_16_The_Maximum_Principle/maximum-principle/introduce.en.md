The Maximum Principle is the fundamental theorem of Boolean-valued models, asserting that for any formula $\varphi$, the Boolean truth value of an existential statement $\llbracket (\exists u) \varphi(u) \rrbracket$ is actually attained by some specific witness $v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$. In other words, the supremum of all $\llbracket \varphi(x) \rrbracket$ over $x \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is realized as $\llbracket \varphi(v) \rrbracket$ for some $v$. This principle relies heavily on the Axiom of Choice in the ground model.
