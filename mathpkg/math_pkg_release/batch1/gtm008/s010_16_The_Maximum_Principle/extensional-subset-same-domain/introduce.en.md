Theorem 16.23 is crucial for the treatment of power sets in Boolean-valued models. If $v$ is an extensional subset of $u$ (with Boolean truth value $1$), the domain of $v$ may be much larger than the domain of $u$. This theorem shows one can always find a replacement $v'$ with the same domain as $u$ that represents the same Boolean-valued subset. This is essential because it bounds the domain of all subsets of $u$, enabling set-theoretic constructions like the power set in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$.
