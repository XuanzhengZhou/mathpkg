Theorem 16.25 provides a set-theoretically meaningful characterization of the Boolean subset relation. The Boolean truth value $\llbracket v \subseteq u \rrbracket$ can be expressed as the supremum of $\llbracket v = v' \rrbracket$ over all "canonical representatives" $v'$ that are subsets of $u$ and have the same domain as $u$. This formula is essential for computing power sets in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ because it shows the subset relation can be reduced to equality with elements of a set-sized collection $A$, enabling the set-theoretic definition of $\mathcal{P}(u)$.
