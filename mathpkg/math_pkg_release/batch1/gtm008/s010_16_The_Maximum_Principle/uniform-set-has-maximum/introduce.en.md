Theorem 16.19 is a remarkable consequence of completeness: for uniform Boolean-valued sets, the supremum $\sup(u) = \sum_{x \in \mathcal{D}(u)} u(x)$ is actually attained as $u(x)$ for some specific element $x$ of the domain. The proof constructs disjoint Boolean components $b_\xi$ from the membership values and uses the completeness of the domain to mix them into a witness $x$. This theorem is the key structural result that leads to the factorization of uniform sets into a Boolean scalar times a definite set (Theorem 16.20).
