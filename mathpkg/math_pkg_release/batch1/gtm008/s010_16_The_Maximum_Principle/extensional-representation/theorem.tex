For all $v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ and for all $d \subseteq V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ such that $\mathcal{D}(v) \subseteq d$, there exists $u \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ such that $u$ is extensional, $\mathcal{D}(u) = d$, and $\llbracket u = v \rrbracket = 1$.

In particular, each $v \in V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ can be represented by an extensional set.
