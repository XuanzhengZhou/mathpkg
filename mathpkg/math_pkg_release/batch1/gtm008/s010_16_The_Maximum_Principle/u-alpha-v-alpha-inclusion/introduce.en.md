Theorem 16.6 establishes that the two hierarchies $U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ and $V_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$ are equivalent up to Boolean-valued equality with value $1$. Parts 1 and 2 are proved by induction on $\alpha$, and part 3 follows from Corollary 16.3 with $d = U_\alpha^{(\mathbf{B})}$. The completeness property of $U_{\alpha+1}^{(\mathbf{B})}$ means it is closed under Boolean-linear combinations with respect to any partition of unity, which is critical for the construction of witnesses in the Maximum Principle.
