An extensional function $g: d \to V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ is a real (ground-model) function defined on a subset $d$ of the Boolean-valued universe that respects the Boolean-valued equality relation: if two elements are Boolean-equal (i.e., $\llbracket x = x' \rrbracket$ is large), then their images must also be Boolean-equal. Extensional functions are the ground-model counterpart to functions in $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$ and are used to bridge the gap between real functions and Boolean-valued functions, as shown in Theorem 16.8.
