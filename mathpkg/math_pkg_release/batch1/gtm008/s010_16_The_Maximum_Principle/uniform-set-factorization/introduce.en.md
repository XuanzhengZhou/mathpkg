Theorem 16.20 provides a canonical factorization of uniform Boolean-valued sets: every uniform $u$ can be written as $b \cdot v$ where $b = \sup(u) \in B$ and $v$ is definite and uniform. The definite set $v$ has as its domain those elements of $\mathcal{D}(u)$ that attain the maximal membership value $\sup(u)$, and assigns them value $1$. This decomposition is fundamental because it separates the "fuzziness" (measured by the Boolean scalar $b$) from the "definite structure" (captured by $v$). Combined with Corollary 16.21, this yields structural insight into arbitrary Boolean-valued sets.
