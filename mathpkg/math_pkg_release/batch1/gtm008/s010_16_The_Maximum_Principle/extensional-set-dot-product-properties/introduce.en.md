This exercise (Exercise 1 after Corollary 16.21) explores the relationship between an extensional set $u$, its dot product with a Boolean element $b$, and the supremum $\sup(u)$. The key identity $\llbracket u = b \cdot u \rrbracket = \llbracket \sup(u) \Rightarrow b \rrbracket$ shows that scaling an extensional set by $b$ leaves it unchanged exactly when $b$ dominates $\sup(u)$. Important corollaries include: $\llbracket u = 0 \rrbracket = \neg \sup(u)$ (the zero set has zero supremum, and vice versa), and $\llbracket b \cdot u \subseteq u \rrbracket = 1$ (scaling produces a subset).
