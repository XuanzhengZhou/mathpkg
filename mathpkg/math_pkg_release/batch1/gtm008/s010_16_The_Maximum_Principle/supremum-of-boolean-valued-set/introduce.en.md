Definition 16.15 introduces $\sup(u)$, the Boolean supremum (sum) of all membership values $u(x)$ for $x$ in the domain of $u$. In a complete Boolean algebra, this sum always exists. Intuitively, $\sup(u)$ measures the "total degree of existence" of $u$ as a subset of $V^{(\mathbf{B})}$. For uniform (extensional with complete domain) sets, this supremum is actually attained as $u(x)$ for some $x \in \mathcal{D}(u)$ (Theorem 16.19), giving a canonical "maximum membership" element.
