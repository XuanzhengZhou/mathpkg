Corollary 16.3 is a direct consequence of the Maximum Principle. Given a partition of unity $\{b_\xi\}$ in the Boolean algebra $B$ and corresponding Boolean-valued sets $u_\xi$, one can mix them into a single Boolean-valued set $v$ by defining $v(x) = \sum_{\xi} b_\xi \cdot u_\xi(x)$. The corollary ensures that $b_\xi \cdot v(x) = b_\xi \cdot u_\xi(x)$, so $v$ behaves like $u_\xi$ on the Boolean region where $b_\xi$ holds.
