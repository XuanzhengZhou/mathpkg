\textbf{Theorem 1 (Coherence for Symmetric Monoidal Categories).}
In each symmetric monoidal category $M$ there is a function which assigns to each pair $(v\sigma, w\tau)$ of permuted words of the same length $n$ a unique natural isomorphism
$$\operatorname{can}_M(v\sigma, w\tau) : (v\sigma)_M \longrightarrow (w\tau)_M : M^n \to M,$$
called the \emph{canonical map} from $v\sigma$ to $w\tau$, satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The identity natural transformation of any functor $(v\sigma)_M$ is canonical.
\item All instances of the associativity isomorphism $\alpha$ and the symmetry isomorphism $\gamma$ are canonical.
\item The composite of two canonical maps is canonical.
\item The $\square$-product of two canonical maps is canonical.
\end{enumerate}
