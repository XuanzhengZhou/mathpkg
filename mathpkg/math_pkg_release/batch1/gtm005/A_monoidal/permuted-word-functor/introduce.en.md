Given a monoidal category $M$ and a word $w$ built from the tensor product $\square$, each permutation $\tau$ of the $n$ arguments yields a new functor $(w\tau)_M$ by rearranging the inputs according to $\tau$. This construction is essential for formulating the coherence theorem for symmetric monoidal categories, which compares the functors associated to different word-permutation pairs.
