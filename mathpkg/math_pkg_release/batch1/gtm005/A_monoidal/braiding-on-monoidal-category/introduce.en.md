A braiding on a monoidal category is a natural family of isomorphisms $\gamma_{a,b} : a \square b \cong b \square a$ that swaps tensor factors. Unlike symmetry, a braiding does not require $\gamma^2 = 1$; the hexagon axioms ensure compatibility between the braiding and the associativity. Braided monoidal categories arise naturally in quantum group theory and knot theory, where the braiding corresponds to the crossing of strands.
