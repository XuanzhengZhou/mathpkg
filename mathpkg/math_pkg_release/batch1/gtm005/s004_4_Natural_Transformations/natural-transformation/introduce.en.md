A natural transformation between two functors $S, T: C \rightarrow B$ provides a way of 'translating' the image of $C$ under $S$ into its image under $T$, while respecting all the arrows of $C$. For each object $c$, a component $\tau_c: Sc \to Tc$ is given, and the naturality condition ensures these components commute with the images of every morphism. This is the central concept of category theory, formalizing the intuitive notion of a 'natural' construction.
