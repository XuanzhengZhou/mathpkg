In the category $\mathbf{Ab}$ of abelian groups, let $D(G) = \hom(G, \mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z})$ denote the character group of $G$. For each $G$, there is a homomorphism $\tau_G: G \rightarrow D(DG)$ defined by $(\tau_G g)(t) = t(g)$ for $g \in G$, $t \in DG$. This defines a natural transformation $\tau: I \xrightarrow{\cdot} D \circ D$ from the identity functor to the twice-iterated character group functor.
