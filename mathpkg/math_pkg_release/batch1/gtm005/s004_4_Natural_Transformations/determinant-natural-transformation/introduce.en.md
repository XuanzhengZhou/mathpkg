The determinant provides a classic example of a natural transformation. For each commutative ring $K$, the determinant map $\det_K: \operatorname{GL}_n(K) \to K^*$ is a group homomorphism. Moreover, for any ring homomorphism $f: K \to K'$, the induced diagram commutes, exhibiting naturality of the determinant in the ring $K$. This example illustrates how natural transformations capture the idea of a construction being "coordinate-free" or "canonical" across all objects of a category.
