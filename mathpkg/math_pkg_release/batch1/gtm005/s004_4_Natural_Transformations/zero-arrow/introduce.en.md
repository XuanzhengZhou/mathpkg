The zero arrow is the arrow that factors through the null object. It provides a canonical trivial morphism between any two objects and satisfies $0 \circ f = 0$ and $g \circ 0 = 0$ for any composable arrows $f, g$. Zero arrows are essential for defining kernels, cokernels, and exactness.
