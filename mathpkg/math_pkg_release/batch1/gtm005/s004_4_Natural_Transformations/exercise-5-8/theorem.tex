Consider the category with objects $\langle X, e, t\rangle$, where $X$ is a set, $e \in X$, and $t: X \rightarrow X$, and with arrows $f: \langle X, e, t\rangle \rightarrow \langle X', e', t'\rangle$ the functions $f$ on $X$ to $X'$ with $fe = e'$ and $ft = t'f$. Prove that this category has an initial object, which is essentially the natural numbers with the successor function.
