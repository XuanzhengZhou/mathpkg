Let $\mathbf{Finord}$ be the category of finite ordinal numbers $n = \{0,1,\ldots,n-1\}$ with all functions between them, and $\mathbf{Set}_f$ the category of all finite sets. The inclusion functor $S: \mathbf{Finord} \rightarrow \mathbf{Set}_f$ and the cardinality functor $\#: \mathbf{Set}_f \rightarrow \mathbf{Finord}$ (taking each finite set $X$ to $\#X$, the number of elements) yield natural isomorphisms $I_{\mathbf{Set}_f} \cong S \circ \#$ and $I_{\mathbf{Finord}} = \# \circ S$. Thus these categories are equivalent.
