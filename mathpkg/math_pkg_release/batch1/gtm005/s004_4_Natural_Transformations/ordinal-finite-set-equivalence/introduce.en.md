The categories of finite ordinal numbers and finite sets are equivalent. The cardinality functor selects a skeleton (the finite ordinals) of the category of finite sets, while the inclusion provides the inverse equivalence up to natural isomorphism. This is a prototypical example of an equivalence of categories.
