The determinant provides a classic example of a natural transformation. For each commutative ring $K$, the determinant maps the general linear group $\operatorname{GL}_n K$ to the group of units $K^*$, and this map commutes with ring homomorphisms. This naturality captures the fact that the determinant is defined by a universal polynomial formula independent of the particular ring.
