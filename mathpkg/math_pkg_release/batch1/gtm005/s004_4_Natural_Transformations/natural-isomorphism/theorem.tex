A natural transformation $\tau: S \xrightarrow{\cdot} T$ is a \textbf{natural isomorphism} (or \textbf{natural equivalence}), written $\tau: S \cong T$, if every component $\tau_c: Sc \rightarrow Tc$ is invertible in $B$. In this case, the inverses $(\tau_c)^{-1}$ in $B$ are the components of a natural isomorphism $\tau^{-1}: T \xrightarrow{\cdot} S$.
