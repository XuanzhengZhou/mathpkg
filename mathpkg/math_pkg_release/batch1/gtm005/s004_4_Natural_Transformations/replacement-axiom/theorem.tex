Let $a$ be a set and $\varphi(x, y)$ a property which is \textbf{functional} for $x$ in $a$, in the sense that $\varphi(x, y)$ and $\varphi(x, y')$ for $x \in a$ imply $y = y'$, and that for each $x \in a$ there exists a $y$ with $\varphi(x, y)$. Then there exists a set consisting of all those $y$ such that $\varphi(x, y)$ holds for $x \in a$.
