The Replacement Axiom is one of the Zermelo-Fraenkel axioms of set theory. It asserts that the image of a set under a definable "function" (given by a functional property) is again a set. This axiom is crucial for constructing large sets by transfinite recursion and is equivalent to the assertion that the set-theoretic universe is closed under definable mappings. In the context of category theory, condition (v) in the definition of a universe corresponds to the Replacement Axiom restricted to $U$.
