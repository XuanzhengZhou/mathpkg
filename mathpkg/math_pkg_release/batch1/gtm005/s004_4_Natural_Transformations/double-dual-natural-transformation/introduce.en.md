The double dual (or double character group) construction in the category of abelian groups yields a natural transformation. For each abelian group $G$, there is a canonical homomorphism $\tau_G: G \to D(DG)$ sending each element $g$ to the evaluation map on characters. This map is natural in $G$: for any homomorphism $f: G \to G'$, the corresponding diagram commutes. This example is analogous to the double dual in linear algebra, where $V \to V^{**}$ is a natural isomorphism for finite-dimensional vector spaces.
