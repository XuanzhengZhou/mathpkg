In the category $\mathbf{Ab}$ of abelian groups, let $D(G) = \hom(G, \mathbf{R}/\mathbf{Z})$ be the character group of $G$, where $\mathbf{R}/\mathbf{Z}$ is the additive group of real numbers modulo $1$. For each group $G$, define the homomorphism

$$\tau_G: G \to D(DG)$$

by $(\tau_G g)(t) = t(g)$ for each $g \in G$ and each character $t \in DG$. Then $\tau$ is a natural transformation from the identity functor $I$ on $\mathbf{Ab}$ to the twice-iterated character group functor $D \circ D$.
