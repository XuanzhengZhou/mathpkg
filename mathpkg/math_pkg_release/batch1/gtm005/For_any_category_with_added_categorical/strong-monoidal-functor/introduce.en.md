A strong monoidal functor is a monoidal functor in which the structure maps $F_0$ and $F_2(a,b)$ are all isomorphisms. This means the functor preserves the monoidal structure up to coherent isomorphism, rather than up to arbitrary morphism. Strong monoidal functors are the most commonly used notion of monoidal functor in practice, as they ensure that the monoidal structure is genuinely preserved in the target category.
