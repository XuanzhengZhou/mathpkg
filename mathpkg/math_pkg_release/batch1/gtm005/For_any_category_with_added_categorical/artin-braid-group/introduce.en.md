The Artin braid group $B_n$ is a classical object in topology that captures the idea of braiding $n$ strings in three-dimensional space without cutting or tying them. Formally, it is defined as the fundamental group of the configuration space $T_n$ of $n$ distinct unordered points in the plane. Each braid corresponds to a loop in this configuration space.
