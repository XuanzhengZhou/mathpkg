Given a monoidal functor $(F, F_2, F_0)$, the structure maps $F_2$ (for binary tensor products) and $F_0$ (for the unit) extend inductively to arbitrary tensor words built from $n$ objects. For each such word $v$, one obtains a canonical natural transformation $F_v$ comparing the word evaluated at images under $F$ with the image under $F$ of the word evaluated directly. This provides the necessary coherence for handling iterated monoidal products with a monoidal functor.
