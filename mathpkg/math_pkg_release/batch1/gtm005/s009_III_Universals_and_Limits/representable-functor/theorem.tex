Let $D$ have small hom-sets. A \emph{representation} of a functor $K: D \to \mathbf{Set}$ is a pair $\langle r, \psi \rangle$, with $r$ an object of $D$ and

\[
\psi: D(r, -) \cong K
\]

a natural isomorphism. The object $r$ is called the \emph{representing object}. The functor $K$ is said to be \emph{representable} when such a representation exists.
