For a functor $S: D \to C$ and an object $c \in C$, a universal arrow $\langle r, u: c \to Sr \rangle$ from $c$ to $S$ determines, for each $d \in D$, a bijection
$$\hom_D(r, d) \cong \hom_C(c, Sd),$$
natural in $d$, sending $f': r \to d$ to $Sf' \circ u$. Conversely, such a natural bijection determines a universal arrow.
