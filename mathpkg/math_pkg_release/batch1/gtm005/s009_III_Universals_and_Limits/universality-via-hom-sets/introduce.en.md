Mac Lane shows that a universal arrow $\langle r, u \rangle$ from $c$ to $S$ is equivalent to a natural isomorphism $\hom_D(r, -) \cong \hom_C(c, S-)$ between hom-functors. This characterization via hom-sets reveals the deep connection between universal properties and representable functors: the functor $\hom_C(c, S-)$ is represented by $r$.
