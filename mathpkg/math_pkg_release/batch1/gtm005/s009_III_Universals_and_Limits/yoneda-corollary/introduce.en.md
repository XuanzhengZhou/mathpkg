A direct corollary of the Yoneda Lemma: every natural transformation between covariant hom-functors $D(r, -) \to D(s, -)$ is induced by precomposition with a unique arrow $h: s \to r$ in the opposite direction. This means that the Yoneda embedding $y: D^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}^D$, sending $r$ to $D(r, -)$, is fully faithful. In other words, an object $r \in D$ is determined up to isomorphism by the functor $D(r, -)$ it represents. This is often summarized by the slogan: "an object is completely determined by its relationships to all other objects."
