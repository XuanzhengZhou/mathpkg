A universal element of a set-valued functor $H: D \to \mathbf{Set}$ is a pair $\langle r, e \rangle$ with $e \in Hr$ such that every element $x \in Hd$ is the image of $e$ under a unique arrow $Hf$. Examples include: the quotient set $S/E$ with projection $p$ as a universal element for the functor of $E$-respecting functions; the tensor product $V \otimes V'$ with the bilinear map $\otimes$ as a universal element for $\mathrm{Bilin}(V, V'; -)$; and the completion of a metric space. The notion of universal element is a special case of universal arrow, and conversely, when hom-sets are small, universal arrows can be rephrased as universal elements.
