Given a functor $S: D \rightarrow C$ and an object $c \in C$, a \textbf{universal arrow} from $S$ to $c$ is a pair $\langle r, v\rangle$ consisting of an object $r \in D$ and an arrow $v: Sr \rightarrow c$ of $C$ such that for every pair $\langle d, f\rangle$ with $d \in D$ and $f: Sd \rightarrow c$, there is a unique arrow $f': d \rightarrow r$ of $D$ with $v \circ Sf' = f$.
