A functor $H: C \to \mathbf{Set}$ is representable if it is naturally isomorphic to a hom-functor $\hom(r, -)$ for some object $r$. Representability is a central concept in category theory: many important constructions (products, coproducts, limits, adjoints) are instances of representable functors. The Yoneda lemma establishes that representations are essentially unique when they exist.
