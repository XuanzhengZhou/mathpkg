Proposition 1 of Section III.2 establishes the equivalence between universal arrows and certain natural isomorphisms of hom-sets. A pair $\langle r, u: c \to Sr \rangle$ is a universal arrow from $c$ to $S: D \to C$ if and only if there is a natural isomorphism $D(r, -) \cong C(c, S -)$ sending $f': r \to d$ to $Sf' \circ u$. This means that universal arrows correspond precisely to representations of the functor $C(c, S -): D \to \mathbf{Set}$, with representing object $r$. The proof uses the Yoneda idea: the natural transformation is completely determined by the image of the identity $1_r$.
