Apply the universal property of $\langle r, u\rangle$ to the pair $\langle r', u'\rangle$ to obtain a unique $f: r \rightarrow r'$ with $Sf \circ u = u'$. Symmetrically, obtain $g: r' \rightarrow r$ with $Sg \circ u' = u$. Then $g \circ f: r \rightarrow r$ satisfies $S(g \circ f) \circ u = Sg \circ Sf \circ u = Sg \circ u' = u = S(1_r) \circ u$. By uniqueness, $g \circ f = 1_r$. Similarly $f \circ g = 1_{r'}$.
