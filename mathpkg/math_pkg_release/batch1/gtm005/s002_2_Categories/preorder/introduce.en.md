A preorder is a category in which there is at most one arrow between any two objects. Such a category corresponds exactly to a set equipped with a reflexive and transitive relation: the objects are the elements, and an arrow $p \to p'$ exists precisely when $p \leq p'$. This shows how order theory embeds into category theory, with preorders being the "thin" categories and partially ordered sets being the skeletal thin categories.
