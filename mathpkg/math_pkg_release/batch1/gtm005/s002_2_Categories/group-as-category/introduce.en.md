A group can be characterized categorically as a one-object category in which every arrow is an isomorphism (has a two-sided inverse). This perspective reveals that groups are special cases of categories, and it motivates the general categorical notions of isomorphisms and groupoids. The group axioms of associativity, identity, and inverses correspond exactly to the category axioms plus the invertibility requirement.
