The matrix category $\operatorname{Matr}_K$ over a commutative ring $K$ is a fundamental example of a category where objects are positive integers and arrows are rectangular matrices with entries in $K$. An $m \times n$ matrix is viewed as an arrow from $n$ to $m$, and composition is given by the usual matrix product. This example connects category theory with linear algebra.
