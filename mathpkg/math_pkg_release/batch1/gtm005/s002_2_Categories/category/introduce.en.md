A category is the fundamental structure studied in category theory. It consists of a directed graph equipped with an identity arrow for each object and a composition operation on composable pairs of arrows. These must satisfy the associativity law (composition is associative) and the unit law (identity arrows act as units for composition). The category axioms abstract the properties of functions between sets, making categories a unifying framework across all of mathematics.
