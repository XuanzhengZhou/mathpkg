A left $C$-object, also called an internal diagram, is the internal analogue of a (covariant) functor from a small category $C$ to the ambient category $\mathbf{Set}$. In the internal setting, the "set of objects" of the functor is replaced by a single object $H$ with a projection $\pi : H \to C_0$ (replacing the disjoint union $\coprod_{c \in C_0} H(c)$), and the action of arrows is encoded by a morphism $\mu : C_1 \times_{C_0} H \to H$ satisfying unit and associativity laws analogous to those of a group action. This construction is essential for formulating the Yoneda lemma and presheaf concepts internally.
