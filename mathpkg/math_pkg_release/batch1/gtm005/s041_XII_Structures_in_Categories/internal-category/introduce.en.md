An internal category is a way to define a category using only the objects and morphisms of an ambient category $E$ that has finite limits. Instead of speaking of "sets" of objects and arrows, one uses objects $C_0$ and $C_1$ of $E$, together with morphisms for identity, domain, codomain, and composition, all expressed via commutative diagrams. The composition is defined on the pullback $C_1 \times_{C_0} C_1$, which internalizes the notion of composable pairs of arrows. An internal category in $\mathbf{Set}$ recovers the ordinary notion of a small category.
