An internal natural transformation is the internalization of the notion of a natural transformation between functors, formulated within a finitely complete ambient category $E$. Given two internal functors $f, g : C \to D$, an internal natural transformation is a morphism $\alpha : C_0 \to D_1$ that assigns to each "object" of the internal category $C$ an "arrow" of $D$, satisfying internal analogues of the naturality squares. Together with internal categories and internal functors, these form the 2-categorical structure of category objects internal to $E$.
