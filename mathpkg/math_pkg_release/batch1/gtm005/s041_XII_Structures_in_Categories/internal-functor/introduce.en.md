An internal functor is the internalization of the notion of a functor between categories, expressed within a finitely complete ambient category $E$. It consists of a pair of morphisms $f_0$ (on the object of objects) and $f_1$ (on the object of arrows) that respect the internal category structure: they commute with domain, codomain, identity, and composition. When $E = \mathbf{Set}$, an internal functor between internal categories recovers the ordinary notion of a functor between small categories.
