A morphism of $C$-objects is the internal analogue of a natural transformation between functors from $C$ to $\mathbf{Set}$. It is a morphism $\phi : H \to K$ in the ambient category $E$ that respects both the projection to the object of objects $C_0$ (ensuring that $\phi$ maps each fiber $H(c)$ to the corresponding fiber $K(c)$) and the action of arrows via $\mu$ (ensuring naturality, i.e., commutativity with composition). The first diagram encodes the object-wise assignment property, and the second diagram encodes the naturality condition.
