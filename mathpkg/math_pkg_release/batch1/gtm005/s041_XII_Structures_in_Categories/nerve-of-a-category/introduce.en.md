The nerve of a category $C$ is a simplicial set $N(C)$ that encodes the entire categorical structure in combinatorial form. The $0$-simplices are the objects of $C$, the $1$-simplices are the arrows, and an $n$-simplex is a composable string of $n$ arrows. The face operators $d_i$ correspond to dropping an object (for $d_0, d_n$) or composing two adjacent arrows (for intermediate $d_i$), while the degeneracy operators $s_j$ insert identity arrows. The nerve construction is fundamental in algebraic topology and higher category theory, as it connects categories to simplicial homotopy theory via the classifying space.
