Let $X$ and $Y$ be types of algebraic structures definable by finite limits (e.g., monoids, groups, categories, group objects). Then there is a canonical equivalence of categories:
$$
X\text{-}\mathbf{Obj}(Y\text{-}\mathbf{Obj}) \simeq Y\text{-}\mathbf{Obj}(X\text{-}\mathbf{Obj}),
$$
where $X\text{-}\mathbf{Obj}(\mathcal{C})$ denotes the category of $X$-objects in a category $\mathcal{C}$. In particular, a category object in $\mathbf{Grp}$ corresponds to a group object in $\mathbf{Cat}$.
