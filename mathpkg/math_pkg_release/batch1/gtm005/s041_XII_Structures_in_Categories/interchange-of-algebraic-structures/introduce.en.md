The interchange principle states that for algebraic structures definable by finite-limit theories (such as monoids, groups, categories, and group objects), one can swap the order in which two types of structure are imposed. For instance, the category of category objects in groups is equivalent to the category of group objects in categories. This is a powerful meta-principle in category theory, generalizing the observation that an internal category in $\mathbf{Grp}$ is the same as a group object in $\mathbf{Cat}$. It arises because both types of structure are described by the same kind of finite-limit diagrams, and limits commute with each other.
