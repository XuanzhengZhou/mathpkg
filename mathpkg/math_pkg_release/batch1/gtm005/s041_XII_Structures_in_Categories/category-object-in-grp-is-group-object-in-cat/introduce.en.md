This proposition illustrates the interchange principle for algebraic structures defined by finite limits. An internal category in $\mathbf{Grp}$ requires both the object-of-objects $C_0$ and object-of-arrows $C_1$ to be groups, with the structural maps being group homomorphisms. The group multiplication $m_0 : C_0 \times C_0 \to C_0$ and $m_1 : C_1 \times C_1 \to C_1$, together with the internal category structure, yield a functor $m : C \times C \to C$ that makes $C$ a group object in $\mathbf{Cat}$ — a category equipped with a "multiplication" functor that is associative and has an inverse.
