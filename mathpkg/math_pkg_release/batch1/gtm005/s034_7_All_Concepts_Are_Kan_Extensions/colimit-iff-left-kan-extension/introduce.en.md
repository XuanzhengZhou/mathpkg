This theorem establishes that colimits are a special case of left Kan extensions. By taking the codomain category as the terminal category $\mathbf{1}$, the universal property of a left Kan extension along the unique functor $K_1: M \to \mathbf{1}$ reduces exactly to the universal property of a colimit. The dual statement identifies limits with right Kan extensions along the same functor. This result justifies the slogan that "all concepts are Kan extensions," as both limits and colimits -- the fundamental universal constructions of category theory -- are expressible in terms of Kan extensions.
