A continuous functor is one that preserves all small limits. The terminology is motivated by the analogy with continuous functions in topology, since limit-preserving functors correspond under suitable dualities to continuous maps between spaces. Important examples include right adjoints (which are always continuous) and hom-functors $C(c, -)$ (which preserve all existing limits, small or large).
