Let $G : A \rightarrow X$ be a functor that preserves small limits. For any fixed object $x \in X$, the forgetful functor
$$Q : (x \downarrow G) \rightarrow A, \qquad Q(x \stackrel{f}{\rightarrow} G a) = a$$
creates all small limits. In particular, $(x \downarrow G)$ is small-complete whenever $A$ is.
