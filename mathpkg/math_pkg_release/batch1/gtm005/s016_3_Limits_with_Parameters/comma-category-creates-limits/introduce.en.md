This proposition is the technical heart of the Freyd Adjoint Functor Theorem. It states that when $G$ preserves limits, the forgetful projection $Q : (x \downarrow G) \rightarrow A$ creates them — meaning limits in the comma category are uniquely determined by limits of their underlying objects in $A$. The proof constructs products and equalizers in $(x \downarrow G)$ explicitly using the preservation properties of $G$, establishing that the comma category inherits small-completeness from $A$.
