Limits with parameters formalize the natural situation where a bifunctor $T : J \times P \rightarrow X$ depends on an indexing category $J$ and a parameter category $P$. When each $T(-, p)$ has a limit, the parameter theorem asserts that these individual limits assemble functorially into a single limit functor $P \rightarrow X$. The equivalent formulation via the adjunction between product and functor categories states that limits in a functor category $X^P$ are computed pointwise: applying the evaluation functor $E_p$ to a limit yields the limit of the evaluated diagram.
