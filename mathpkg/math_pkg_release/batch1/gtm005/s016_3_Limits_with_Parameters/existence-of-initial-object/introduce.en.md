This theorem characterizes when a small-complete category possesses an initial object. The solution set condition requires a small "weakly initial" family of objects — every object admits at least one arrow from some member of the family. The proof constructs the initial object as a suitable equalizer of endomorphisms of a large product. This result is the key ingredient in Freyd's Adjoint Functor Theorem.
