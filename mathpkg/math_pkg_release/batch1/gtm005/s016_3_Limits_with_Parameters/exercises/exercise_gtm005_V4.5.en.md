(Preservation of Limits.) Given $H : C \rightarrow C'$ and a functor $F : J \rightarrow C$ such that $F$ and $HF$ have limits, prove that $H$ preserves the limits of $F$ if and only if the canonical arrow $H \circ \lim F \rightarrow \lim HF$ of Exercise 2.4 is an isomorphism. (This is a natural way to describe the preservation of limits when both categories $C$ and $C'$ are given with specified limits.)
