This theorem establishes that covariant hom-functors are the prototypical continuous functors. Given a limiting cone $\nu : b \rightarrow F$ in $C$, applying $C(c, -)$ yields a cone $C(c, \nu) : C(c, b) \rightarrow C(c, F-)$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, which is again limiting. The isomorphism $\operatorname{Lim} C(c, F-) \cong C(c, \operatorname{Lim} F)$ shows that limits in $C$ are determined by limits in $\mathbf{Set}$ evaluated through the hom-functor — a perspective that can be used to define limits representably.
