This is one of the most useful properties of adjoints: right adjoints are automatically continuous. It follows from the fact that the limit functor $\operatorname{Lim}$ is itself a right adjoint (to the diagonal functor $\Delta$), and compositions of adjoints yield adjoints. Consequently, all forgetful functors in algebra (which are right adjoints to free constructions) preserve products, equalizers, and pullbacks — explaining why the underlying set of a product of groups is the Cartesian product of their underlying sets.
