A category $E$ is \emph{cartesian closed} if each functor ``product with $b$'', i.e. the functor $a \mapsto a \times b$, has for all objects $b$ in $E$ a right adjoint $c \mapsto c^b$. In other words, for all objects $a, b, c$ in $E$ there is a natural isomorphism
$$
\hom(a \times b, c) \cong \hom(a, c^b).
$$
The object $c^b$ is called the \emph{exponential} of $c$ by $b$. The category $\mathbf{Sets}$ is cartesian closed, with $c^b$ the set of all functions from $b$ to $c$.
