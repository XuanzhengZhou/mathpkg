A category is cartesian closed when the functor $a \mapsto a \times b$ (product with a fixed object $b$) has a right adjoint $c \mapsto c^b$ for every object $b$. The object $c^b$ is called an exponential and satisfies the natural isomorphism $\hom(a \times b, c) \cong \hom(a, c^b)$. Cartesian closure captures the idea that the category has internal hom-objects, making it a model of simply typed lambda calculus. Together with finite limits and a subobject classifier, cartesian closure is one of the three defining axioms of an elementary topos.
