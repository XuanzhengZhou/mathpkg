An \emph{elementary topos} (or simply \emph{topos}) is a category $E$ with the following properties:
\begin{itemize}
  \item[(i)] $E$ has all finite limits;
  \item[(ii)] $E$ has a subobject classifier;
  \item[(iii)] $E$ is cartesian closed.
\end{itemize}
The category $\mathbf{Sets}$ of all (small) sets is a topos, and so is the category $\mathbf{Sets}^{C^{\mathrm{op}}}$ of all set-valued contravariant functors (presheaves) on a small category $C$. Every topos also has all finite colimits. The subobject classifier $\Omega$ of a topos reflects various logical properties, and under many circumstances a topos provides an alternative foundation for mathematics.
