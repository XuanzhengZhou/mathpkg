A functor reflects isomorphisms if whenever the image of an arrow under the functor is an isomorphism, the original arrow was already an isomorphism. Such functors are also called conservative. This property appears in weak versions of Beck's monadicity theorem as a hypothesis that, together with preservation of coequalizers, ensures the comparison functor is an equivalence.
