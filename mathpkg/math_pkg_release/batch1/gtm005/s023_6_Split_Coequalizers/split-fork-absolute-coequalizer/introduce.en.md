This corollary states that in any split fork, the arrow $e$ is an absolute coequalizer. The proof is immediate: a split fork is defined by equations involving only compositions and identities, so it remains a split fork under the application of any functor $T$. Since a split fork always yields a coequalizer, $Te$ is a coequalizer for every $T$, making $e$ absolute.
