Since a split fork is defined by equations involving only composites and identities, it remains a split fork under the application of any functor $T: C \to X$. In any split fork, $e$ is a coequalizer of $\partial_0$ and $\partial_1$. Therefore, for any functor $T$, $Te$ is a coequalizer of $T\partial_0$ and $T\partial_1$, which means $e$ is an absolute coequalizer by definition.
