This theorem states that for any variety of algebras (defined by a set $\Omega$ of operators and a set $E$ of identities), the category of algebras is isomorphic to the Eilenberg-Moore category of algebras for the induced monad on $\mathbf{Set}$. The proof uses Beck's theorem by showing that the forgetful functor creates coequalizers for parallel pairs whose underlying functions have an absolute coequalizer. This unifies the earlier results for semigroups, monoids, and rings.
