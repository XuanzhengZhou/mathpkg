This lemma shows that when a functor $G$ creates coequalizers for split pairs (hypothesis (iii) of Beck's theorem), any two adjunctions inducing the same monad are related by a unique comparison functor. The lemma is used in Beck's proof to construct both the comparison functor $K: A \to X^T$ and its inverse $M: X^T \to A$, establishing that $K$ is an isomorphism. It also incidentally provides a new proof of the comparison theorem.
