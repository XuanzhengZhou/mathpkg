Let $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle: X \rightharpoonup A$ be an adjunction, $\langle T, \eta, \mu \rangle$ the monad which it defines in $X$, $X^T$ the category of $T$-algebras for this monad, and $\langle F^T, G^T, \eta^T, \varepsilon^T \rangle: X \rightharpoonup X^T$ the corresponding adjunction. Then the following conditions are equivalent:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] The (unique) comparison functor $K: A \to X^T$ is an isomorphism;
\item[(ii)] The functor $G: A \to X$ creates coequalizers for those parallel pairs $f, g$ in $A$ for which $Gf, Gg$ has an absolute coequalizer in $X$;
\item[(iii)] The functor $G: A \to X$ creates coequalizers for those parallel pairs $f, g$ in $A$ for which $Gf, Gg$ has a split coequalizer in $X$.
\end{itemize}
