A functor $G: A \to X$ reflects colimits if whenever a cone in $A$ becomes a colimiting cone after applying $G$, it was already colimiting in $A$. This is a "reflection" property: colimits in $X$ that lift to cones in $A$ already were colimits in $A$. Reflection of colimits (and specifically reflection of coequalizers and isomorphisms) appears in weak and equivalence versions of Beck's monadicity theorem.
