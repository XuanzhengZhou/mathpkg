An absolute coequalizer is a coequalizer that remains a coequalizer after applying any functor whatsoever. This is a strong preservation property: while ordinary coequalizers may be destroyed by functors that fail to preserve colimits, absolute coequalizers are indestructible under all functors. The concept is central to Beck's theorem, where absolute (or split) coequalizers are used to characterize the category of algebras for a monad.
