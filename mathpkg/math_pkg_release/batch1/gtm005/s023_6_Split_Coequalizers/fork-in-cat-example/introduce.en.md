This example exhibits a natural fork in the category of small categories $\mathbf{Cat}$. For any category $C$, the arrow category $C^2$ together with the domain and codomain functors forms a parallel pair, and the unique functor to the terminal category $\mathbf{1}$ coequalizes them. When $C$ has a terminal object $a_0$, this fork is split: $s$ picks out $a_0$ and $t$ sends each object to the unique arrow from that object to $a_0$.
