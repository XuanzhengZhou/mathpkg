Given the adjunction $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle: X \rightharpoonup A$, the associated monad $\langle T, \eta, \mu \rangle$, the Eilenberg-Moore adjunction $\langle F^T, G^T, \eta^T, \varepsilon^T \rangle: X \rightharpoonup X^T$, and the comparison functor $K: A \to X^T$, let $P$ be the set of all those parallel pairs $f, g: a \rightrightarrows b$ in $A$ such that $Gf, Gg$ has a split coequalizer in $X$. Using Exercise 4(b), prove:

If $A$ has coequalizers of all pairs in $P$, then $K$ has a left adjoint $M: X^T \to A$.
