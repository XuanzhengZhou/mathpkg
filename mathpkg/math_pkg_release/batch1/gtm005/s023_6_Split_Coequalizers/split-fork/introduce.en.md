A split fork is a fork equipped with two additional splitting arrows $s$ and $t$ satisfying four equations: the fork equation $e\partial_0 = e\partial_1$, right-inverse conditions $es = 1$ and $\partial_0 t = 1$, and a compatibility condition $\partial_1 t = se$. These purely equational conditions ensure that the fork is preserved by any functor, making the coequalizer $e$ absolute. Split forks are fundamental to Beck's monadicity theorem.
