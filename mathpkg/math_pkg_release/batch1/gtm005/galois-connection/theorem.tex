Let $P$ and $Q$ be preorders (categories where each hom-set has at most one element). A \textbf{Galois connection} from $P$ to $Q$ is a pair of order-preserving functions $L: P \to Q$ and $R: Q \to P$ such that
$$Lp \leq q \quad\text{iff}\quad p \leq Rq$$
for all $p \in P$, $q \in Q$. In categorical terms, this is precisely an adjunction $\langle L, R \rangle: P \to Q$ between preorders. The unit satisfies $p \leq RLp$ and the counit $LRq \leq q$. When $P$ and $Q$ are posets, these become $L = LRL$ and $R = RLR$. Fundamental example: for a group $G$ acting on a set $U$, $LX = \{\sigma \in G \mid \sigma \cdot x = x \text{ for all } x \in X\}$ and $RS = \{x \in U \mid \sigma \cdot x = x \text{ for all } \sigma \in S\}$ form a Galois connection between subsets of $U$ and subsets of $G$.
