The data for an \textbf{arrows-only metacategory} $C$ consist of:
\begin{itemize}
\item arrows $f, g, h, \ldots$;
\item certain ordered pairs $\langle g, f \rangle$, called the composable pairs of arrows;
\item an operation assigning to each composable pair $\langle g, f \rangle$ an arrow $g \circ f$, called their composite.
\end{itemize}
An \textbf{identity} of $C$ is an arrow $u$ such that $f \circ u = f$ whenever $f \circ u$ is defined, and $u \circ g = g$ whenever $u \circ g$ is defined. The axioms require that composition be associative whenever defined, and that for each arrow $f$ there exist identities $u, v$ with $u \circ f$ and $f \circ v$ defined.
