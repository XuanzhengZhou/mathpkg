A metagraph is the simplest categorical structure: a directed graph whose vertices are called "objects" and whose edges are called "arrows." Each arrow has an assigned domain (source) and codomain (target). Metagraphs serve as the underlying data for metacategories, which add identity arrows and a composition operation subject to axioms.
