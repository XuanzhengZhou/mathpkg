A metacategory extends a metagraph by equipping it with identity arrows for every object and a composition operation for consecutive arrows. The composition must satisfy associativity (so bracketing is irrelevant whenever composition makes sense) and the unit law (identity arrows act as neutral elements for composition). The prefix "meta" indicates that this definition is given without reference to set theory; a category proper is a metacategory whose collections of objects and arrows are sets or classes.
