This proposition establishes that the identity arrow of each object in a metacategory is uniquely determined by the unit law axiom. The standard proof uses the fact that if $u$ and $v$ are both two-sided units for the object $b$, then $u = u \circ v = v$. This uniqueness justifies the common practice in category theory of identifying objects with their identity arrows, which in turn enables the arrows-only formulation of a metacategory.
