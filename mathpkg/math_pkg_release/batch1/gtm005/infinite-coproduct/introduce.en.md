An infinite coproduct generalizes the binary coproduct to an arbitrary set-indexed family of objects. In Set, this is the disjoint union over the family. The universal property expresses that a map out of the coproduct is uniquely determined by giving, for each index $x$, a map out of the corresponding object $a_x$.
