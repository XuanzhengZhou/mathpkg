Proposition 4 provides an elegant characterization of additive functors between $\textit{Ab}$-categories: a functor is additive precisely when it preserves binary biproduct diagrams. Since the additive structure of an $\textit{Ab}$-category can be expressed entirely in terms of biproducts (via the diagonal and codiagonal morphisms), preserving biproducts is equivalent to preserving the addition of parallel arrows. This result is fundamental for working with functors between additive categories.
