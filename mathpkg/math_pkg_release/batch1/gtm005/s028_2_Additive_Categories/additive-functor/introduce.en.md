An additive functor between $\textit{Ab}$-categories is a functor that respects the additive structure on hom-sets: it sends sums of parallel arrows to sums of their images. Equivalently, an additive functor preserves the zero morphism and commutes with the formation of biproducts, when they exist. Additive functors are the natural notion of morphism between $\textit{Ab}$-categories, analogous to additive homomorphisms between abelian groups.
