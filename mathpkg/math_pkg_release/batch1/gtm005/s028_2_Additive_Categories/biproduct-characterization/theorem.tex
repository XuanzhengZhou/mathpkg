Let $A$ be an $\textit{Ab}$-category with a null object. For objects $a, b \in A$, suppose there exists a diagram
$$
a \xleftarrow{p_1} c \xrightarrow{p_2} b, \qquad a \xrightarrow{i_1} c \xleftarrow{i_2} b
$$
satisfying the equations
$$
p_1 i_1 = 1_a, \quad p_2 i_2 = 1_b, \quad i_1 p_1 + i_2 p_2 = 1_c. \tag{2}
$$
Then $p_1 i_2 = 0$ and $p_2 i_1 = 0$, and the diagram $a \xleftarrow{p_1} c \xrightarrow{p_2} b$ is a product of $a$ and $b$, while $a \xrightarrow{i_1} c \xleftarrow{i_2} b$ is a coproduct of $a$ and $b$. The assignment $h \mapsto \langle p_1 h, p_2 h \rangle$ yields an isomorphism of abelian groups
$$
A(d, c) \cong A(d, a) \oplus A(d, b).
$$
