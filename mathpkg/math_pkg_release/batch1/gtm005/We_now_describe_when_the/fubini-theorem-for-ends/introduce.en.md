The Fubini theorem for ends states that a "double end" $\int_{\langle p, c \rangle}$ over a product category can be computed as an iterated end $\int_p \int_c$, provided the inner end exists for all parameter pairs. This is the end-analogue of the classical Fubini theorem for iterated integrals in analysis, and it is essential for manipulating ends in category theory.
