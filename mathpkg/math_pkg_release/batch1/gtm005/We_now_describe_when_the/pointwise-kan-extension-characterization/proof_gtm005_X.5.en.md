This follows from the cone bijection lemma and the Yoneda lemma. By the pointwise formula, $Rc$ is the limit of $TQ: (c \downarrow K) \to A$. The set of cones from $a$ to $TQ$ is representable, and the lemma identifies this with $\mathrm{Nat}(C(c, K-), A(a, T-))$. The representability condition is exactly that $\mathrm{Cone}(a, TQ) \cong A(a, Rc)$. Composing with the lemma's bijection gives the stated characterization.
