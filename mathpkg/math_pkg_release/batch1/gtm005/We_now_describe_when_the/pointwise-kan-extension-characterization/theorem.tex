$\langle R, \varepsilon: RK \to T \rangle$ is a pointwise right Kan extension of $T$ along $K$ if and only if, for all $a \in A$ and $c \in C$, the map

$$A(a, Rc) \to \mathrm{Nat}\bigl(C(c, K-), A(a, T-)\bigr)$$

sending $g: a \to Rc$ to the natural transformation whose component at $m \in M$ is

$$C(c, Km) \xrightarrow{A(g, \varepsilon_m)} A(a, Tm)$$

is a bijection.
