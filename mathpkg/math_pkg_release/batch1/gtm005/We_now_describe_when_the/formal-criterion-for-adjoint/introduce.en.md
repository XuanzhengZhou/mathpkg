The formal criterion for the existence of an adjoint (Theorem 2) gives necessary and sufficient conditions for a functor $G: A \to X$ to have a left adjoint: $G$ must preserve all existing limits in $A$, and for each object $x \in X$, the limit of the projection functor $Q: (x \downarrow G) \to A$ from the comma category must exist. The left adjoint is then given pointwise as $Fx = \lim Q$.
