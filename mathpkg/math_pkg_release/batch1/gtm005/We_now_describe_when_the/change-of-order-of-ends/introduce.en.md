The change of order of ends theorem states that under suitable existence conditions, iterated ends can be commuted: $\int_p \int_c S(p,p,c,c) \cong \int_c \int_p S(p,p,c,c)$. This follows from the Fubini theorem for ends by expanding the double end in two different ways, analogous to the classical theorem on changing the order of iterated integrals.
