($\operatorname{Lim}$ and $\operatorname{Lim}$ as functors.) If $F, F' : J \to C$ have limiting cones $\mu, \mu'$ (or colimiting cones $\nu, \nu'$), show that each natural transformation $\beta : F \to F'$ determines a unique arrow $\varprojlim \beta = \lim \beta : \varprojlim F \to \varprojlim F'$ (or $\varinjlim \beta : \varinjlim F \to \varinjlim F'$) such that all relevant composites commute. Conclude that when $C$ is complete, $\varprojlim$ is a functor from the functor category $C^J$ to $C$.
