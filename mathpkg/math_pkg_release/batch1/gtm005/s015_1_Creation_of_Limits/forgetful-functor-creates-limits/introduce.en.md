This theorem succinctly encapsulates Theorem 2 in the formal language of "creation of limits." It states that the forgetful functor from groups to sets creates limits: given any diagram in $\mathbf{Grp}$, a limit in $\mathbf{Set}$ of the underlying sets lifts uniquely to a limit in $\mathbf{Grp}$. This is the prototypical example motivating the general definition of limit creation, and the same proof pattern applies to many other algebraic categories such as $\mathbf{Ab}$, $\mathbf{Ring}$, and $\mathbf{Mod}_R$.
