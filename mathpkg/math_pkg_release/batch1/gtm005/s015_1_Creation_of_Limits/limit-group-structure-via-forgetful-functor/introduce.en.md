This theorem demonstrates how limits in algebraic categories are built from limits in $\mathbf{Set}$ via the forgetful functor. Given a diagram $H : J \to \mathbf{Grp}$, one first computes the limit of the underlying sets $UH$ in $\mathbf{Set}$. The theorem asserts that this limit set carries a unique group structure making all the cone projections into group homomorphisms, and with this structure it becomes the limit in $\mathbf{Grp}$. This is a prototypical example of the general notion of "creation of limits" by a forgetful functor.
