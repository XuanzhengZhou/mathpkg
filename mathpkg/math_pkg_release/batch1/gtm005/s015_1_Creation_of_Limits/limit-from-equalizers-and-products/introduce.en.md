This theorem provides a constructive method for obtaining all small limits from two simpler kinds of limits: products and equalizers. Given a diagram $F : J \to C$, one forms the product $\Pi_i F_i$ over all objects and the product $\Pi_u F_{\operatorname{cod}u}$ over all arrows of $J$. Two canonical maps $f$ and $g$ between these products are defined using the diagram's structure. The equalizer of $f$ and $g$ then yields the limit of $F$, with the limiting cone obtained by composing the equalizer with the product projections. This result is fundamental because it reduces the problem of establishing completeness to checking the existence of products and equalizers.
