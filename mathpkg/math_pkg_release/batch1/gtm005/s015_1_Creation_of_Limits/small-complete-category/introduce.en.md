A category is called small-complete (or simply complete) when every functor from a small category into it possesses a limit. This notion is central to category theory since many important categories — such as $\mathbf{Set}$, $\mathbf{Grp}$, $\mathbf{Ab}$, and categories of algebras — are small-complete. The concept generalizes the idea of having all small products and equalizers, which together suffice to construct all small limits. Completeness is a key hypothesis in the adjoint functor theorems and in the study of universal constructions.
