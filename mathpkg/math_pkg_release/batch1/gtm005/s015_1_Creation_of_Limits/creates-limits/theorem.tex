A functor $V : A \to X$ \textbf{creates limits} for a functor $F : J \to A$ if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] To every limiting cone $\tau : x \to VF$ in $X$ there is exactly one pair $\langle a, \sigma \rangle$ consisting of an object $a \in A$ with $Va = x$ and a cone $\sigma : a \to F$ with $V\sigma = \tau$, and if, moreover,
\item[(ii)] This cone $\sigma : a \to F$ is a limiting cone in $A$.
\end{enumerate}
Similarly, one may define "$V$ creates products" (the above, with $J$ restricted to be discrete); "$V$ creates finite limits" (the above, with $J$ finite), or "$V$ creates colimits" (the above with the arrows in all cones reversed).
