Given two arrows $f: b \to a$ and $g: d \to a$ with a common codomain, a \textbf{pullback} is an object $b \times_a d$ and arrows $p: b \times_a d \to b$, $q: b \times_a d \to d$ such that (i) $f \circ p = g \circ q$, and (ii) for any object $c$ and arrows $h: c \to b$, $k: c \to d$ with $f \circ h = g \circ k$, there is a unique $r: c \to b \times_a d$ with $p \circ r = h$, $q \circ r = k$. The diagram is called a \textbf{pullback square} or \textbf{cartesian square}.
