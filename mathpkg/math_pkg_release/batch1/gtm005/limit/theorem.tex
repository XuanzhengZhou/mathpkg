Let $C$ and $J$ be categories and $\Delta: C \to C^J$ the diagonal functor. For a functor $F: J \to C$, a \textbf{limit} of $F$ is a universal arrow $\langle r, v \rangle$ from $\Delta$ to $F$. It consists of an object $r = \varprojlim F$ (or $\operatorname{Lim} F$) and a natural transformation $v: \Delta r \to F$ (a \textbf{cone}) which is universal among natural transformations $\Delta c \to F$. There is a natural bijection
$$C(c, \operatorname{Lim} F) \cong \operatorname{Nat}(\Delta c, F) = \operatorname{Cone}(c, F).$$
