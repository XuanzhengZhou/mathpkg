Let $B$ and $C$ be categories with product category $B \times C$ and projection functors $P: B \times C \to B$, $Q: B \times C \to C$. Given any category $D$ and two functors
$$B \xleftarrow{R} D \xrightarrow{T} C,$$
there exists a unique functor $F: D \to B \times C$ such that $PF = R$ and $QF = T$. Explicitly, for any arrow $h$ in $D$,
$$Fh = \langle Rh, Th \rangle.$$
