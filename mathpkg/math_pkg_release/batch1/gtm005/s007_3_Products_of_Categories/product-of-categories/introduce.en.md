The product of two categories $B$ and $C$, denoted $B \times C$, is a new category whose objects are ordered pairs of objects from $B$ and $C$ and whose morphisms are ordered pairs of morphisms. Composition and identities are defined componentwise, using the composition and identities from $B$ and $C$. This construction generalizes the familiar Cartesian product of sets to the categorical setting and provides the domain for functors of two variables (bifunctors).
