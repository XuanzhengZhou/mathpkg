A bifunctor is simply a functor whose domain is a product category $B \times C$. This definition automatically captures the notion of a "functor of two variables" — it is a mapping that assigns to each pair of objects $(b, c)$ an object $S(b, c)$ in $D$ and to each pair of arrows $(f, g)$ an arrow $S(f, g)$ in $D$, satisfying functoriality in each variable separately and an interchange condition between them. Common examples include the Cartesian product of sets and the hom-functor.
