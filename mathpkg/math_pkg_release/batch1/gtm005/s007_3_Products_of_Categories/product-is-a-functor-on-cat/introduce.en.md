The product construction $\times$ is not just an operation on individual categories — it is a bifunctor $\text{Cat} \times \text{Cat} \to \text{Cat}$. On objects, it sends a pair of small categories to their product category. On morphisms (functors), it sends a pair of functors to their product functor. Functoriality follows from the identity $(U' \times V') \circ (U \times V) = U'U \times V'V$ and the fact that the product of identity functors is the identity. Analogous bifunctors exist for other categories of structured sets, such as $\text{Grp}$ and $\text{Top}$.
