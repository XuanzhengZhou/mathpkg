The opposite of a product category is naturally isomorphic to the product of the opposite categories. This means that the operations of taking opposites and taking products commute up to isomorphism: $(B \times C)^{\text{op}} \cong B^{\text{op}} \times C^{\text{op}}$. The isomorphism is straightforward — it swaps the order of arrows (via the opposite) and the pairing simultaneously, and these two operations are independent of each other.
