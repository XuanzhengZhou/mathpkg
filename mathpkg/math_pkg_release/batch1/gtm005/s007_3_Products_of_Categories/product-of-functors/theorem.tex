Let $U: B \to B'$ and $V: C \to C'$ be functors. The \textbf{product functor} $U \times V: B \times C \to B' \times C'$ is defined on objects by
$$(U \times V)\langle b, c \rangle = \langle Ub, Vc \rangle$$
and on arrows by
$$(U \times V)\langle f, g \rangle = \langle Uf, Vg \rangle.$$

The product functor $U \times V$ is the unique functor making the following diagram commute:
$$\begin{CD}
B @<{P}<< B \times C @>{Q}>> C \\
@V{U}VV @VV{U \times V}V @VV{V}V \\
B' @<<{P'}< B' \times C' @>>{Q'}> C'
\end{CD}$$
