The hom bifunctor $\hom_C: C^{\text{op}} \times C \to \text{Set}$ is the prototypical example of a bifunctor that is contravariant in its first argument and covariant in its second. For a fixed object $c$, the partial application $\hom_C(-, c)$ is the contravariant hom-functor; for fixed $b$, $\hom_C(b, -)$ is the covariant hom-functor. The fact that these two families of ordinary functors satisfy the interchange condition is exactly what makes the hom-sets assemble into a single bifunctor.
