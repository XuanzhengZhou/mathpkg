The coend of a functor $S: C^{\text{op}} \times C \rightarrow X$ is the dual notion to an end. It is defined as a universal dinatural transformation (a universal wedge) from the bifunctor $S$ to a constant object, written $\int^c S(c,c)$. Coends unify many familiar constructions in mathematics: the tensor product of modules over a ring $A \otimes_R B$ and the geometric realization of a simplicial set $\int^n (S_n) \cdot \Delta^n$ are both naturally expressed as coends. The integral notation reflects the analogy with integration: the bound variable $c$ runs over all objects of the indexing category.
