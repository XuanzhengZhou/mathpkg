The left adjoint $D$ to the forgetful functor $G: \mathbf{Top} \to \mathbf{Set}$ equips a set $S$ with the discrete topology, where every subset is open. The adjunction $D \dashv G$ implies that $G$ preserves all limits. For instance, the underlying set of a product of topological spaces is the Cartesian product of their underlying sets.
