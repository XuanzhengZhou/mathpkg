The subspace topology on a subset $S \subset GX$ of a topological space $X$ can be described categorically via a slice category adjunction. The forgetful functor $G$ induces a functor $G \downarrow X$ between slice categories, and its right-adjoint-right-inverse $L$ equips each subset with the subspace topology. The universal property refers not only to other subspaces of $X$, but to any continuous map $f: Y \to X$ whose image is contained in the subset $S$.
