The familiar gluing condition for continuous maps on an open cover has a clean categorical expression: the set of continuous maps $\mathbf{Top}(X, Y)$ is the equalizer of a diagram formed by products of mapping sets restricted to the cover and to pairwise intersections. Dually, $X$ is the colimit of its open cover diagram. This perspective explains why many constructions in topology (gluing, quotients) are special cases of general categorical limits and colimits.
