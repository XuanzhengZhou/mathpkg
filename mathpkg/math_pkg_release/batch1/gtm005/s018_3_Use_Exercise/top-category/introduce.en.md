The category $\mathbf{Top}$ has all (small) topological spaces as objects and continuous maps as morphisms. It is one of the most important examples in category theory, serving as a rich source of adjunctions and (co)limit constructions. The forgetful functor from $\mathbf{Top}$ to $\mathbf{Set}$ admits both left and right adjoints, corresponding to discrete and indiscrete topologies.
