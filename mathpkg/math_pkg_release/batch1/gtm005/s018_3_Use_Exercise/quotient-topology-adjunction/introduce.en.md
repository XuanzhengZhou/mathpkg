The quotient topology on a set $S$ induced by a surjection $t: GX \to S$ can be described categorically as a left-adjoint-right-inverse $M$ to the induced functor $X \downarrow G$ on slice categories. The adjunction $(X \downarrow \mathbf{Top}) (Mt, f) \cong (GX \downarrow \mathbf{Set}) (t, Gf)$ captures the universal property of the quotient topology. This is dual to the subspace topology adjunction and is used to construct coequalizers in $\mathbf{Top}$.
