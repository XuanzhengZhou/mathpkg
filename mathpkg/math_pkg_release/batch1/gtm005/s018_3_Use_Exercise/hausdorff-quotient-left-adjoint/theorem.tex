The left adjoint $H$ to the inclusion $\mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{Top}$ assigns to each space $X$ a Hausdorff space $HX$ and a continuous map $\eta: X \to HX$, universal from $X$ to a Hausdorff space. The universal property implies $\eta$ is a surjection, so $HX$ is the "largest Hausdorff quotient" of $X$. If $X$ is already Hausdorff, $HX = X$ and $\eta = 1$, so $H$ is a left-adjoint-left-inverse to the inclusion.
