A reflective subcategory is one where the inclusion has a left adjoint (the reflector). The reflector provides a universal way to map each object of the larger category into the subcategory. Key examples include abelianization (reflecting groups into abelian groups), completion of metric spaces, and Stone-Cech compactification. Reflective subcategories are full subcategories closed under limits.
