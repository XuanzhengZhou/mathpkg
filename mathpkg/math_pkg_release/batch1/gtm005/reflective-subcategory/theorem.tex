A subcategory $A$ of $B$ is called \textbf{reflective} in $B$ when the inclusion functor $K: A \to B$ has a left adjoint $F: B \to A$. The functor $F$ is called a \textbf{reflector}, and the adjunction $\langle F, K, \varphi \rangle: B \to A$ with unit $\eta_b: b \to KFb$ gives a universal arrow from each $b \in B$ to $A$. Examples: $\mathbf{Ab}$ is reflective in $\mathbf{Grp}$ (reflector: $G \mapsto G/[G, G]$); complete metric spaces are reflective in metric spaces (reflector: completion); compact Hausdorff spaces are reflective in completely regular spaces (reflector: Stone-Cech compactification).
