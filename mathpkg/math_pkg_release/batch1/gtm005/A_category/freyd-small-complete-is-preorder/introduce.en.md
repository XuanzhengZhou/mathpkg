**Proposition 3 (Freyd).** A small category that is small-complete must be a preorder — that is, there is at most one arrow between any two objects. Moreover, completeness reduces to the existence of all small meets (greatest lower bounds). This striking result shows that genuine completeness (with non-trivial morphisms) requires the category to be large. The proof uses a cardinality argument: in a non-preorder, there would be too many arrows to a product.
