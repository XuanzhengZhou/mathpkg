**Theorem 1 (General Limit Existence).** Any limit in a category $C$ can be constructed from products and equalizers. Specifically, if $C$ has all products over the object and arrow sets of a small category $J$, and has equalizers of all parallel pairs, then every functor $F: J \to C$ has a limit. This theorem is foundational: it reduces the problem of establishing completeness to checking the existence of products and equalizers.
