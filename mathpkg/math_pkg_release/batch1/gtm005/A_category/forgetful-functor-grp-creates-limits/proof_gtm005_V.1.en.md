This is Theorem 2 restated in the terminology of Definition (creates limits). By Theorem 2, given a diagram $H: J \to \mathbf{Grp}$, if $UH$ has a limiting cone $\tau: L \to UH$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, there exists exactly one group structure on $L$ making each $\tau_j$ a homomorphism, and the resulting cone is limiting in $\mathbf{Grp}$. This is precisely condition (i) and (ii) of the definition of "creates limits," taking $V = U$, $A = \mathbf{Grp}$, $X = \mathbf{Set}$, $F = H$, $a = L$, and $\sigma = \tau$.
