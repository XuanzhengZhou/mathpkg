A functor $V: A \to X$ **creates limits** for a diagram $F: J \to A$ if, whenever the composite $VF$ has a limiting cone in $X$, there exists a unique lift to a limiting cone in $A$. This concept formalizes the idea that limits in $A$ are constructed exactly as limits in $X$ with additional structure. The forgetful functors from $\mathbf{Grp}$, $\mathbf{Ab}$, $\mathbf{Rng}$, and many other algebraic categories to $\mathbf{Set}$ all create limits.
