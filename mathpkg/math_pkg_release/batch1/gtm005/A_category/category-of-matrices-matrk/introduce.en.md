For a commutative ring $K$, the category $\operatorname{Matr}_K$ of all rectangular matrices over $K$ provides a concrete example of a category. The objects are positive integers, and an $m \times n$ matrix $A$ is an arrow $A : n \to m$. Composition is the usual matrix product, which is associative and has identity matrices as units. This example illustrates how familiar algebraic structures fit into the categorical framework.
