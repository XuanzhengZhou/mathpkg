To handle categories like the "category of all sets" without running into set-theoretic paradoxes, Mac Lane introduces the device of a universe $U$ -- a sufficiently large set. A set is called "small" if it belongs to $U$. Using this, one constructs large categories: $\mathbf{Set}$ (all small sets), $\mathbf{Set}_*$ (pointed small sets), $\mathbf{Ens}$ (sets in a variable $V$), and $\mathbf{Cat}$ (all small categories). This foundational device is detailed in Section 7 of Chapter I.
