Each ordinal $n$ is linearly ordered, and hence is a category (a preorder). For example, the categories $\mathbf{1}$, $\mathbf{2}$, and $\mathbf{3}$ are the preorders belonging to the (linearly ordered) ordinal numbers $1$, $2$, and $3$. The first infinite ordinal $\omega = \{0, 1, 2, \ldots\}$ as a category consists of the arrows
$$
0 \to 1 \to 2 \to 3 \to \cdots,
$$
all their composites, and the identity arrows for each object.
