Since ordinals are linearly ordered sets, each ordinal $n$ can be regarded as a category -- specifically, a preorder. The finite categories $\mathbf{1}$, $\mathbf{2}$, $\mathbf{3}$ correspond to the ordinals $1$, $2$, $3$. The first infinite ordinal $\omega$ as a category consists of a chain of arrows $0 \to 1 \to 2 \to 3 \to \cdots$ together with all composites and identity arrows. This construction links set-theoretic ordinals with categorical structures.
