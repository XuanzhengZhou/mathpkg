The simplicial category $\Delta$ has finite ordinals as objects and order-preserving functions as arrows. This category is fundamental to simplicial homotopy theory: a simplicial set is a contravariant functor from $\Delta$ to $\mathbf{Set}$. The structure of $\Delta$ encodes face and degeneracy maps, making it the combinatorial foundation for simplicial methods in algebraic topology, which are explored in Chapter VII of the book.
