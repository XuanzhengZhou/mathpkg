The $p$-adic solenoid is a classical example from topology, constructed as the inverse limit of circles $S^1$ where each bonding map wraps the circle $p$ times around itself. The resulting space $L$ is endowed with the initial topology making all projection maps $\mu_n: L \to S^1$ continuous. This space is a connected, compact, metrizable topological group that is not locally connected, and it provides a key example illustrating limits in the category $\mathbf{Top}$.
