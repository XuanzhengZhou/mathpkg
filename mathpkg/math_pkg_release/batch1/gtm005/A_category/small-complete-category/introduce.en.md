A category is called **small-complete** (or simply **complete**) if every functor from a small category into it possesses a limit. This concept provides the fundamental setting for discussing the existence of limits across entire categories. Standard examples of complete categories include $\mathbf{Set}$, $\mathbf{Grp}$, $\mathbf{Ab}$, and many other categories of algebraic structures. Completeness is central to the development of adjoint functor theorems and the general theory of limits.
