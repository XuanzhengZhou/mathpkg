A functor $K \colon D \to \mathbf{Set}$ is representable if it is naturally isomorphic to a covariant hom-functor $D(r, -)$ for some object $r$ of $D$. The object $r$ is called the representing object, and the natural isomorphism $\psi \colon D(r, -) \cong K$ is the representation. This central notion of category theory tightly links the three formulations of universality: a universal arrow from $c$ to $S$, a universal element of $C(c, S-)$, and a representation of $C(c, S-)$ are all equivalent statements.
