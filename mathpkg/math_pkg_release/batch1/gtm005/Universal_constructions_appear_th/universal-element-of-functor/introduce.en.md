A universal element of a set-valued functor $H \colon D \to \mathbf{Set}$ is an object $r$ in $D$ and a distinguished element $e \in Hr$ such that every other element $x \in Hd$ arises uniquely as $(Hf)e$ for some $f \colon r \to d$. This notion is a special case of a universal arrow (from the one-point set $*$ to $H$) and captures many quotient constructions: for example, the projection $S \to S/E$ onto a quotient set, the tensor product $\otimes \colon V \times V' \to V \otimes V'$, and kernels of homomorphisms — all are universal elements for appropriate functors.
