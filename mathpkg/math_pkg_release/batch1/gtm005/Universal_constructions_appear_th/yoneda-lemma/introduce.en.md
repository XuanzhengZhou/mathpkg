The Yoneda Lemma is arguably the most fundamental result in category theory. It states that for any set-valued functor $K \colon D \to \mathbf{Set}$ and any object $r$ of $D$, natural transformations from the hom-functor $D(r, -)$ to $K$ correspond bijectively to elements of the set $Kr$ — specifically, each natural transformation $\alpha$ is uniquely determined by where it sends the identity arrow $1_r$. The Yoneda Lemma thus embeds $D$ faithfully into the functor category $\mathbf{Set}^{D^{\mathrm{op}}}$, and it underpins the equivalence between universal arrows, universal elements, and representable functors.
