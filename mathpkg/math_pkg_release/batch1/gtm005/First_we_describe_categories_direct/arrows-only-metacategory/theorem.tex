An \emph{arrows-only metacategory} $C$ consists of arrows and certain ordered pairs $\langle g, f \rangle$, called the \emph{composable pairs} of arrows, together with an operation assigning to each composable pair $\langle g, f \rangle$ an arrow $g \circ f$, called their \emph{composite}. We say ``$g \circ f$ is defined'' for ``$\langle g, f \rangle$ is a composable pair''.

With these data one defines an \emph{identity} of $C$ to be an arrow $u$ such that $f \circ u = f$ whenever the composite is defined, and $u \circ g = g$ whenever the composite is defined.
