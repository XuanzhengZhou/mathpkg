The associativity axiom states that composition of arrows in a metacategory is associative whenever it makes sense, i.e., whenever the composites on either side of the equality are defined. This parallels the associativity of function composition and ensures that the order of composition does not affect the resulting arrow. Together with the unit law, it forms the complete set of axioms characterizing a metacategory.
