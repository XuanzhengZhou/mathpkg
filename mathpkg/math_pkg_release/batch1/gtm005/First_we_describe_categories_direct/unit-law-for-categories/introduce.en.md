The unit law, also called the identity law, asserts that the identity arrow $1_b$ of each object $b$ serves as a two-sided identity for the operation of composition. When composed on the right with any arrow landing at $b$, or on the left with any arrow starting at $b$, the identity arrow leaves the other arrow unchanged. Together with the associativity axiom, this completes the axiomatic definition of a metacategory.
