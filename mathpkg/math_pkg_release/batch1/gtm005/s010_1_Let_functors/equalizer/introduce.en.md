An equalizer is the categorical formulation of the solution set of an equation. Given two parallel arrows $f, g: a \to b$, their equalizer is the "largest subobject" of $a$ on which $f$ and $g$ agree. In $\mathbf{Set}$, the equalizer is the subset $\{x \in a \mid f(x) = g(x)\}$ with the inclusion map. Equalizers are always monic.
