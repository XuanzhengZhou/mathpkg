The power $a^J$ is the categorical notion of "iterated product of $a$ with itself $J$ times." When $C = \mathbf{Set}$, $a^J$ is exactly the set of functions $J \to a$, the usual exponential. More generally, in any category with products, $a^J$ is the $J$-fold product of copies of $a$.
