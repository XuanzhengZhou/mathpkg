Given two adjunctions
$$\langle F, G, \varphi, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A, \quad \langle F', G', \varphi', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$$
with the same categories $X$ and $A$, natural transformations
$$\sigma : F \to F', \qquad \tau : G' \to G$$
are said to be \textbf{conjugate} (for the given adjunctions) when the diagram
$$\begin{array}{c}
A(F'x, a) \cong X(x, G'a) \\
(\sigma_x)^* = A(\sigma_x, a) \downarrow \quad \quad \downarrow X(x, \tau_a) \\
A(Fx, a) \cong X(x, Ga)
\end{array}$$
commutes for every pair of objects $x \in X, a \in A$.
