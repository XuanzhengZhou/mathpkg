Given two adjunctions
$$\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A, \quad \langle \bar{F}, \bar{G}, \bar{\eta}, \bar{\varepsilon} \rangle : A \rightharpoonup D$$
the composite functors yield an adjunction
$$\langle \bar{F}F, G\bar{G}, G\bar{\eta}F \cdot \eta, \bar{\varepsilon} \cdot \bar{F}\varepsilon\bar{G} \rangle : X \rightharpoonup D.$$

Using this composition, we may form a category $\mathbf{Adj}$, whose objects are all (small) categories and whose arrows are the adjunctions $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$, composed as above. The identity arrow for each category $A$ is the identity adjunction $A \rightharpoonup A$.
