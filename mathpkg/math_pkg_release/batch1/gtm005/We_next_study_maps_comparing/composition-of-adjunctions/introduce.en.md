This theorem shows that two successive adjunctions compose to yield a single adjunction. Given adjunctions $X \rightharpoonup A$ and $A \rightharpoonup D$, the composite functors $\bar{F}F : X \to D$ and $G\bar{G} : D \to X$ form an adjunction whose unit is $G\bar{\eta}F \cdot \eta$ and whose counit is $\bar{\varepsilon} \cdot \bar{F}\varepsilon\bar{G}$. This composition law allows one to form the category $\mathbf{Adj}$ whose objects are small categories and whose morphisms are adjunctions, providing a global perspective on adjunctions as arrows between categories.
