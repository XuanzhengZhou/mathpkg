A map of adjunctions is a notion of morphism between two adjunctions. It consists of a pair of functors $K$ (between the target categories) and $L$ (between the source categories) that make the squares formed by the adjoint pairs commute. Furthermore, the hom-set bijections of the two adjunctions must be compatible with the actions of $K$ and $L$ on morphisms. This definition allows one to compare different adjunctions and forms the basis for studying the category of adjunctions.
