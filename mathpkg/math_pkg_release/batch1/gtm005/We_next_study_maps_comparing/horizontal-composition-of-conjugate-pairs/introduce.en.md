This theorem establishes the horizontal composition law for conjugate pairs of natural transformations. Given two conjugate pairs living in adjacent adjunctions, their horizontal composites (formed by composing the left components $\bar{\sigma}\sigma$ and right components $\tau\bar{\tau}$ in the appropriate direction) again form a conjugate pair for the composite adjunctions. This is the 2-categorical structure of $\mathbf{Adj}$: conjugate pairs serve as 2-cells, with both vertical composition (from Section 7) and horizontal composition (this theorem).
