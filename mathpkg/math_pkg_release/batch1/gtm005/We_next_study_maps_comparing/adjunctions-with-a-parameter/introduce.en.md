This theorem (also called "adjunctions with a parameter") describes how a family of adjunctions parametrized by a category $P$ naturally extends to a bifunctorial adjunction. Given a bifunctor $F: X \times P \to A$ such that each $F(-, p)$ has a right adjoint $G(p, -)$, there is a unique way to make $G$ into a bifunctor $P^{\text{op}} \times A \to X$ so that the adjunction is natural in the parameter $p$ as well. The key idea is that the arrow $G(h, a)$ must be chosen as the conjugate natural transformation to $F(-, h)$.
