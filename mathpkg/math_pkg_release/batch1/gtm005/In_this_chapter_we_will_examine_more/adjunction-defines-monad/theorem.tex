Every adjunction $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle: X \rightleftharpoons A$ gives rise to a monad in the category $X$. The composite $T = GF$ is an endofunctor $T: X \to X$, the unit $\eta: I_X \to T$ is the unit of the adjunction, and the multiplication $\mu = G\varepsilon F: GFGF \to GF = T$ is obtained by horizontal composition of the counit $\varepsilon: FG \to I_A$ with $G$ and $F$.
