A monad in a category is the categorical analog of a monoid, where the Cartesian product of sets is replaced by composition of endofunctors and the unit set is replaced by the identity functor. Formally, a monad consists of an endofunctor $T$ together with natural transformations $\eta: I_X \to T$ and $\mu: T^2 \to T$ satisfying associativity and unit laws expressed as commutative diagrams. The terminology "monad" was chosen over earlier terms such as "triple" or "dual standard construction" to avoid confusion with ordered triples and other unrelated concepts.
