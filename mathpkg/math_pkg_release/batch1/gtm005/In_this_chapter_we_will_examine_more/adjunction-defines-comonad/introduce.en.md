Every adjunction $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle: X \rightleftharpoons A$ yields a comonad on the codomain category $A$ by composing $F$ and $G$ in the opposite order: the composite endofunctor is $FG$, the counit is the counit $\varepsilon$ of the adjunction, and the comultiplication is $F\eta G$. This is the formal dual of the construction that produces a monad from the same adjunction on the domain category.
