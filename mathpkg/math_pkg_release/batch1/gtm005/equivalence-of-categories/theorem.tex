A functor $S: A \to C$ is an \textbf{equivalence of categories} (and $A$ and $C$ are \textbf{equivalent}) when there exists a functor $T: C \to A$ and natural isomorphisms
$$ST \cong I_C: C \to C, \qquad TS \cong I_A: A \to A.$$
In this case, $T$ is both a left adjoint and a right adjoint of $S$. Equivalently (Theorem IV.4.1), the following are equivalent for a functor $S: A \to C$:
(i) $S$ is an equivalence of categories.
(ii) $S$ is part of an adjoint equivalence $\langle T, S; \eta, \varepsilon \rangle: C \to A$ where both unit $\eta$ and counit $\varepsilon$ are natural isomorphisms.
(iii) $S$ is full, faithful, and each object $c \in C$ is isomorphic to $Sa$ for some $a \in A$.
