An equivalence of categories is the correct notion of sameness for categories. Unlike isomorphism of categories, equivalence allows objects to be identified only up to isomorphism. A functor is an equivalence iff it is full, faithful, and essentially surjective on objects. Equivalent categories share all categorical properties: limits, colimits, adjunctions, etc. Examples include the equivalence between finite sets and finite ordinals, and Pontrjagin duality between compact abelian groups and (discrete) abelian groups.
