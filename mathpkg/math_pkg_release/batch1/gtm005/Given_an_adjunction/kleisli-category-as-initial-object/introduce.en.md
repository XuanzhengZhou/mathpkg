This theorem synthesizes the two comparison theorems by characterizing the Kleisli construction via a universal property. For a fixed monad $T$ on $X$, one forms the category $\mathbf{Adj}(T)$ of all adjunctions that induce $T$, with morphisms being maps of adjunctions that fix $X$. The Kleisli adjunction $F_T \dashv G_T : X \to X_T$ is the initial object of $\mathbf{Adj}(T)$, while the Eilenberg-Moore adjunction is the terminal object. This provides a complete classification of all resolutions of a monad into an adjunction.
