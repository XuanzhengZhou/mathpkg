This comparison theorem establishes a universal property of the Kleisli category $X_T$: for any adjunction $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \to A$ that induces the monad $T$, there is a unique comparison functor $L : X_T \to A$ that commutes with the Kleisli functors. The condition $LF_T = F$ and $GL = G_T$ means that $L$ mediates between the Kleisli resolution of the monad and any other resolution via an adjunction.
