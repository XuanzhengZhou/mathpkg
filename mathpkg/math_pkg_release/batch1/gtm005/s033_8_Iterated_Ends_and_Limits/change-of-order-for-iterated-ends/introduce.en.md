This corollary of the Fubini theorem for ends shows that the order of iterated ends can be interchanged when the relevant inner ends exist. By expanding a double end in two different ways as iterated ends, one obtains an isomorphism $\int_p \int_c S(p,p,c,c) \cong \int_c \int_p S(p,p,c,c)$. This is the end-analogue of the classical theorem on changing the order of integration.
