This theorem provides an explicit limit formula for computing right Kan extensions. When the required limits exist, the right Kan extension of $T$ along $K$ at an object $c$ is the limit of $T$ composed with the projection from the comma category $(c \downarrow K)$. The universal natural transformation $\varepsilon$ is extracted from the limiting cones. This construction is fundamental because it shows right Kan extensions exist whenever the relevant limits do, linking the theory of Kan extensions to the theory of limits.
