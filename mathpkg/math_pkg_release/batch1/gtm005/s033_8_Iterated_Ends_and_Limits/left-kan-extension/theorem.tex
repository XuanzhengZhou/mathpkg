Given functors $K: M \to C$ and $T: M \to A$, a **left Kan extension** of $T$ along $K$ is a pair $\langle L, \eta: T \to LK \rangle$ consisting of a functor $L: C \to A$ and a natural transformation $\eta$ which is universal from $T$ to the functor $A^K: A^C \to A^M$ defined by precomposition with $K$. This gives a bijection
$$\mathrm{Nat}(\mathrm{Lan}_K T, S) \cong \mathrm{Nat}(T, SK),$$
natural in $S \in A^C$. The functor $L$ is denoted $\mathrm{Lan}_K T$.
