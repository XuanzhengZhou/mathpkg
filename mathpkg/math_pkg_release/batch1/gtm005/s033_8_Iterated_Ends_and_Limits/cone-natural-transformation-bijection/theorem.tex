Given functors $K: M \to C$ and $T: M \to A$, there is a bijection
$$\mathrm{Cone}\big(a, (c \downarrow K) \xrightarrow{Q} M \xrightarrow{T} A\big) \cong \mathrm{Nat}\big(C(c, K-), A(a, T-)\big),$$
natural in $a$ and $c$, where $\mathrm{Cone}(a, F)$ denotes the set of cones from the constant functor at $a$ to $F$.
