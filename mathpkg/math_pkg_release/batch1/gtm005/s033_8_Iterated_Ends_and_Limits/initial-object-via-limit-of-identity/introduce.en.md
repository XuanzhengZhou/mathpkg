This lemma states that if a cone over the identity functor becomes a limiting cone when restricted along some functor $F$, then the vertex of the cone is an initial object. It provides a crucial link between the notions of initial objects and limits, showing that initial objects can be characterized as limits of the identity functor. The result is used in the formal criterion for the existence of adjoints (Theorem 2 of Chapter X, Section 1).
