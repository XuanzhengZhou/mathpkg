The Fubini theorem for iterated ends expresses a double end over a product category $P \times C$ as an iterated end, first over $C$ then over $P$, provided the inner ends $\int_c S(p,q,c,c)$ exist for all pairs $\langle p,q \rangle$. This is the end-analogue of the classical Fubini theorem for double integrals. The result relies on the parameter theorem to regard the inner ends as a bifunctor of the remaining parameters, and the universal property of ends determines the isomorphism uniquely.
