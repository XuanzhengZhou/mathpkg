Let $M$ be a full subcategory of $C$ and let $K: M \to C$ be the inclusion. If for each $c \in C$, the composite functor $TQ: (c \downarrow K) \to A$ has a limit in $A$, then there is a functor $R: C \to A$ with $RK = T$ (i.e., $R$ extends $T$) such that the identity natural transformation $1: RK \to T$ makes $R$ the right Kan extension of $T$ along the inclusion $K: M \to C$.
