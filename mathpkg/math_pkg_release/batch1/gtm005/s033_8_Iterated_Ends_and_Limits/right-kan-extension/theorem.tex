Given functors $K: M \to C$ and $T: M \to A$, a **right Kan extension** of $T$ along $K$ is a pair $\langle R, \varepsilon: RK \to T \rangle$ consisting of a functor $R: C \to A$ and a natural transformation $\varepsilon$ such that for every functor $S: C \to A$ and every natural transformation $\alpha: SK \to T$, there is a unique natural transformation $\sigma: S \to R$ with $\alpha = \varepsilon \cdot \sigma K$. The assignment $\sigma \mapsto \varepsilon \cdot \sigma K$ is a bijection
$$\mathrm{Nat}(S, \mathrm{Ran}_K T) \cong \mathrm{Nat}(SK, T),$$
natural in $S$. The functor $R$ is denoted $\mathrm{Ran}_K T$.
