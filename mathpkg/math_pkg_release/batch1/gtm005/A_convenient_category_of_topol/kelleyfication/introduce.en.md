The Kelleyfication functor $K: \mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{CGHaus}$ assigns to each Hausdorff space $Y$ a compactly generated Hausdorff space $KY$ with the same underlying set, where a set is declared closed precisely when its intersection with every compact subset of $Y$ is closed. The identity map $\varepsilon_Y: KY \to Y$ is continuous and is the counit of an adjunction making $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ coreflective in $\mathbf{Haus}$. This construction ensures that any continuous map from a compactly generated space into $Y$ factors uniquely through $\varepsilon_Y$, and the right adjoint $K$ preserves limits, a fact used to prove $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ is complete.
