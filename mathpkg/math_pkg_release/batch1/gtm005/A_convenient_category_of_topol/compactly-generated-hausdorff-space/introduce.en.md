A compactly generated Hausdorff space is a Hausdorff space whose topology is determined by its compact subsets: a set is closed exactly when its intersection with every compact subset is closed. These spaces are also called Kelley spaces, after J. L. Kelley who introduced them. The category $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ of compactly generated Hausdorff spaces forms a cartesian closed category with the Kelleyfication of the compact-open topology as its internal hom, making it a convenient category for topological studies, homotopy theory, and topological algebra.
