The category $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ of compactly generated Hausdorff spaces is coreflective in the category $\mathbf{Haus}$ of all Hausdorff spaces. The right adjoint to the inclusion functor is the Kelleyfication $K$, which endows each Hausdorff space with the finest compactly generated topology coarser than the original. This coreflection is fundamental because it transfers completeness and cocompleteness from $\mathbf{Haus}$ to $\mathbf{CGHaus}$: $K$ as a right adjoint preserves limits, giving $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ completeness, while the inclusion as a left adjoint allows construction of colimits.
