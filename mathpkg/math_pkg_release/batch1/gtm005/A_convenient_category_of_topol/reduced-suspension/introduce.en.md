The reduced suspension $\Sigma X = X \wedge S^1$ is a fundamental functor in pointed homotopy theory, obtained by smashing a pointed space $X$ with the circle $S^1$. Geometrically, $\Sigma X$ is the double cone over $X$ with the base-point generator collapsed. It is left adjoint to the loop space functor $\Omega$, forming the adjunction $\Sigma \dashv \Omega$ which encodes the relationship between suspension (increasing dimension) and looping (decreasing dimension). This adjunction is central to stable homotopy theory and the study of spectra.
