The loop space $\Omega X$ of a pointed space $X$ is the space of all base-point-preserving maps from the circle $S^1$ into $X$, topologized as a subspace of the Kelleyfied compact-open function space $X^{S^1}$. Its points are loops in $X$ based at the base point $*_X$, i.e., continuous maps $f: [0,1] \to X$ with $f(0) = f(1) = *_X$. The loop space functor $\Omega$ is right adjoint to the reduced suspension $\Sigma$, giving the fundamental adjunction $\Sigma \dashv \Omega$ of pointed homotopy theory.
