The suspension-loop adjunction $\Sigma \dashv \Omega$ is the fundamental adjunction of pointed homotopy theory. It states that the reduced suspension functor $\Sigma X = X \wedge S^1$ is left adjoint to the loop space functor $\Omega X = X^{(*)S^1}$, giving a natural bijection $\mathbf{CGHaus}_*(\Sigma X, Y) \cong \mathbf{CGHaus}_*(X, \Omega Y)$. This adjunction follows from the more general smash-product/internal-hom adjunction $\mathbf{CGHaus}_*(Z \wedge Y, X) \cong \mathbf{CGHaus}_*(Z, X^{(*)Y})$ by specializing $Y = S^1$. The unit of the adjunction sends a point $x \in X$ to the constant loop at $x$, while the counit evaluates a loop at its parameter.
