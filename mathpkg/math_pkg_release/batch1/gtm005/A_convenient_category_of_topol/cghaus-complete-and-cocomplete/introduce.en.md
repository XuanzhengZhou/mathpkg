Proposition 2 of Mac Lane's "Categories for the Working Mathematician" states that $\mathbf{CGHaus}$, the category of compactly generated Hausdorff spaces, is both small-complete and small-cocomplete. Completeness follows because $\mathbf{Haus}$ is complete and the right adjoint $K$ (Kelleyfication) preserves limits. The $\square$-product in $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ is defined by $X \mathbin{\square} Y = K(X \times Y)$, i.e., the Kelleyfication of the ordinary product. Cocompleteness is obtained by verifying that colimits constructed in $\mathbf{Haus}$ already lie in $\mathbf{CGHaus}$.
