Theorem 3 is the central result of Section VII.8: the category $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ of compactly generated Hausdorff spaces is cartesian closed. The internal hom $X^Y$ is defined as the Kelleyfication of the compact-open topology on the set of continuous maps $Y \to X$, and the $\square$-product (Kelleyfication of the ordinary product) serves as the monoidal product. The evaluation map $e: X^Y \mathbin{\square} Y \to X$ is continuous, and the familiar set-theoretic adjunction $\operatorname{Top}(Z \times Y, X) \cong \operatorname{Top}(Z, \operatorname{Cop}(Y, X))$ transfers to $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ after applying $K$, establishing $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ as a convenient category for topology and homotopy theory.
