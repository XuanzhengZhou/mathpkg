The functor category $\mathbf{Sets}^{\mathbf{2}}$ (objects are functions, morphisms are commutative squares) admits a subobject classifier given by the inclusion $j : \{0,2\} \hookrightarrow \{0,1,2\}$ with $j(0)=0, j(2)=2$. A subobject in this category is a subset $S \subset X$ such that $f(S) \subset T$, and the corresponding three-valued characteristic function tracks whether an element belongs to $S$, whether its image under $f$ belongs to $T$, or neither. This generalizes the ordinary two-valued characteristic function and shows how subobject classifiers encode richer logical structure in functor categories.
