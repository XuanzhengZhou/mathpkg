An elementary topos is a category with three fundamental properties: all finite limits, a subobject classifier, and cartesian closure (existence of exponentials). The category $\mathbf{Sets}$ of all small sets is a topos, and so is every presheaf category $\mathbf{Sets}^{\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}}}$. Topos theory provides an alternative foundation for mathematics: suitable toposes can replace $\mathbf{Sets}$ as the ambient category, and the subobject classifier $\Omega$ encodes internal logical structure, enabling interpretations of higher-order logic and independence results such as the consistency of the negation of the continuum hypothesis via sheaf toposes.
