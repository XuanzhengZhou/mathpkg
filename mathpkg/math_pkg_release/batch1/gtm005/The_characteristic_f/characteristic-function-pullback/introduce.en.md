The characteristic function of a subset $S \subset X$ produces a pullback square in $\mathbf{Sets}$: the subset $S$ is recovered as the pullback of the inclusion $t : \{0\} \hookrightarrow \{0,1\}$ along $\psi_S$. This means $S = \psi_S^{-1}(\{0\})$, showing that every subset can be obtained by pulling back the simplest non-trivial monomorphism $\{0\} \to \{0,1\}$. This observation is the motivation for the general categorical definition of a subobject classifier.
