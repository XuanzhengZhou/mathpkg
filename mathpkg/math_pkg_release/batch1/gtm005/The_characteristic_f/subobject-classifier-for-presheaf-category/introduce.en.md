For any small category $\mathcal{C}$, the presheaf category $\mathbf{Sets}^{\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}}}$ possesses a subobject classifier $\Omega$, where $\Omega(c)$ is the set of all sieves on the object $c$. This generalizes the subobject classifier of $\mathbf{Sets}^{\mathbf{2}}$ and is fundamental to topos theory: every presheaf topos is a topos. The construction of $\Omega$ via sieves encodes the Yoneda lemma and subobject classification in a unified framework, forming the basis for Grothendieck topologies and sheaf theory.
