The Joyal-Street braided coherence theorem establishes that for any braided monoidal category $M$, the category of strong braided monoidal functors from the braid category $B$ into $M$ is equivalent to the underlying ordinary category $M_0$. The equivalence is realized simply by evaluating each functor at the object $1$ of $B$. This theorem generalizes the coherence result for symmetric monoidal categories to the braided setting, where diagrams involving the braiding $\gamma$ need not commute — instead, the braid groups $B_n$ act on $n$-fold products, reflecting the topological nature of braids.
