Theorem 3 unifies the two comparison theorems for monads. It states that the collection of all adjunctions defining a given monad $\langle T, \eta, \mu \rangle$ on $X$ forms a category $\mathbf{Adj}(T)$, where morphisms are maps of adjunctions that fix $X$. In this category, the Kleisli construction $X_T$ is the initial object (Theorem 2: there is a unique functor from $X_T$ to any other resolution), while the Eilenberg–Moore construction $X^T$ is the terminal object (the comparison theorem of § VI.2: there is a unique functor from any resolution into $X^T$). This elegantly positions the Kleisli and Eilenberg–Moore categories as the extremal resolutions of a monad.
