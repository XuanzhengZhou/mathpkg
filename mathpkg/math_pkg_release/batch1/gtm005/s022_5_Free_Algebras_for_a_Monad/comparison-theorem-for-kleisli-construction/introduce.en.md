The comparison theorem for the Kleisli construction states that the Kleisli category $X_T$ is the initial resolution of a monad $T$. For any adjunction $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightleftharpoons A$ that gives rise to the same monad $T$ on $X$, there is a unique comparison functor $L : X_T \to A$ making the diagram of adjunctions commute. This universal property characterizes the Kleisli category as the "smallest" adjunction defining $T$, confirming that one and the same monad can be presented by many different adjunctions, with the Kleisli construction providing the minimal such presentation.
