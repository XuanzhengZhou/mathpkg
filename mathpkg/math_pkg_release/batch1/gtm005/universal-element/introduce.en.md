A universal element of a set-valued functor $H$ is a generic element $e \in Hr$ from which all other elements of $H$ can be obtained by applying $H$ to a unique arrow. The quotient set $S/E$ with projection $p$ and the tensor product $V \otimes V'$ with $\otimes$ are examples. Universal elements are equivalent to universal arrows from a one-point set.
