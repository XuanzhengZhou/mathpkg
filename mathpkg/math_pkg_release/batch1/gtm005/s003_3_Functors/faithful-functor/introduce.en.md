A functor is faithful if it is injective on parallel morphisms — distinct morphisms between the same pair of objects are never identified by the functor. Equivalently, the induced map on each hom-set is injective. Composites of faithful functors are again faithful. The forgetful functor from groups to sets is the canonical example of a faithful functor.
