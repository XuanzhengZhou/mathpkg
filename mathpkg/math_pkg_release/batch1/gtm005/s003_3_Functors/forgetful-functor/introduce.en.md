A forgetful functor "forgets" some or all of the algebraic structure of an object, sending it to the underlying set. For instance, the forgetful functor from groups to sets sends each group to its set of elements and each group homomorphism to itself, viewed merely as a set-theoretic function. Forgetful functors are among the most common examples of functors and are typically faithful.
