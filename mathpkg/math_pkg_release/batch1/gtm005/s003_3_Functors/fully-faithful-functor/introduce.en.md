A functor is fully faithful if it is simultaneously full and faithful — the induced map on every hom-set is a bijection. While this means that all morphism data is perfectly preserved, a fully faithful functor need not be an isomorphism of categories, because there may be objects of the codomain that are not in the image of the functor. This notion is fundamental in the theory of equivalences of categories.
