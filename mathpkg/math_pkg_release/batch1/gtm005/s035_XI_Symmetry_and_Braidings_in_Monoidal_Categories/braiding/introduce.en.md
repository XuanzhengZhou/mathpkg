A braiding on a monoidal category provides a natural isomorphism that swaps the order of the tensor product. It generalizes the symmetry axiom of symmetric monoidal categories by dropping the requirement that swapping twice returns the identity. The braiding must satisfy two hexagonal coherence conditions that relate it to the associativity isomorphism, ensuring that re-associating and then braiding is consistent with braiding and then re-associating. Braided monoidal categories arise naturally in quantum groups, knot theory, and the representation theory of Hopf algebras.
