A \textbf{braiding} for a monoidal category $M$ consists of a family of isomorphisms
$$\gamma_{a,b} : a \square b \cong b \square a$$
natural in $a$ and $b$, which satisfy the following two hexagonal diagrams:
\[
\begin{array}{ccc}
a \square (b \square c) & \xrightarrow{\alpha} & (a \square b) \square c & \xrightarrow{\gamma} & c \square (a \square b) \\
& {}_{1 \square \gamma} \searrow & & \nearrow_{\alpha} \\
& & a \square (c \square b) & \xrightarrow{\alpha} & (a \square c) \square b
\end{array}
\]
\[
\begin{array}{ccc}
(a \square b) \square c & \xrightarrow{\alpha^{-1}} & a \square (b \square c) & \xrightarrow{1 \square \gamma} & a \square (c \square b) \\
& {}_{\gamma \square 1} \searrow & & \nearrow_{\alpha^{-1}} \\
& & (b \square a) \square c & \xrightarrow{\alpha^{-1}} & b \square (a \square c)
\end{array}
\]
These hexagons assert that $\gamma$ is compatible with the associativity isomorphism $\alpha$.
