This theorem justifies the name "final": a final functor $L: J' \to J$ induces an isomorphism between the colimit of $F$ over $J$ and the colimit of the restriction $FL$ over $J'$. In other words, to compute a colimit over $J$, it suffices to compute it over $J'$. The converse also holds under mild conditions (see Exercise 5). The dual notion, "initial functor," is useful for limits.
