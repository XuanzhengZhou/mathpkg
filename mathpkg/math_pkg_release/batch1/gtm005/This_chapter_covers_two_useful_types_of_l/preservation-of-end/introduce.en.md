A functor $H$ preserves an end if applying $H$ to the universal wedge yields another universal wedge. This is the end-analogue of a functor preserving limits. In integral notation, it states that $H$ commutes with the integral sign. This property is important in enriched category theory, where hom-functors and tensor products are often required to preserve certain ends.
