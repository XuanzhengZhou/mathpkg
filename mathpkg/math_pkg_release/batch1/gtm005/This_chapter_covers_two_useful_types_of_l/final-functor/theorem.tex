A functor $L: J' \rightarrow J$ is called **final** if for each $k \in J$ the comma category $(k \downarrow L)$ is non-empty and connected. This means that to each $k$ there is an object $j' \in J'$ and an arrow $k \rightarrow Lj'$, and that any two such arrows can be joined to give a finite commutative diagram of the form

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
k &= k &= k \\
\downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow \\
Lj' & \leftarrow & Lj'' \rightarrow Lj'
\end{array}$$
