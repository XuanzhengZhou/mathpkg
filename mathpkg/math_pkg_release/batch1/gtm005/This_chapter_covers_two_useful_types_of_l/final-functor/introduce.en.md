A final functor $L: J' \to J$ generalizes the classical notion of a cofinal subset in a preorder. The condition that each comma category $(k \downarrow L)$ be non-empty and connected ensures that colimits over $J$ can be computed by restricting to $J'$. For a linear order, finality of an inclusion reduces to the familiar condition that every element of $J$ is bounded above by some element of $J'$.
