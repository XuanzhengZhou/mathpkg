A filtered category generalizes the notion of a directed preorder to an arbitrary category. Rather than requiring a preorder structure, it requires that every finite diagram admit a cone, meaning that any two objects map into a common target and any two parallel arrows are coequalized. Some authors (e.g., Mac Lane--Moerdijk) call such a category "cofiltered." Filtered categories serve as the natural index categories for filtered colimits, which enjoy special commutation properties with finite limits.
