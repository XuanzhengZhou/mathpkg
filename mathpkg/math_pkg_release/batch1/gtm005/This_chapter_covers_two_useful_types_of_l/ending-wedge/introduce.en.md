The ending wedge is the universal dinatural transformation from a constant object into a bifunctor $S$ that defines its end. The wedge condition is a specialized form of dinaturality where the domain is a constant functor. By abuse of language, the vertex object $e$ itself is called the "end" of $S$, but the full structure includes the ending wedge $\omega$. Uniqueness up to unique isomorphism follows from the universal property.
