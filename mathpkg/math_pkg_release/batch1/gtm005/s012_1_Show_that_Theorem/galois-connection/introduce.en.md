A Galois connection between two posets $P$ and $Q$ is an adjoint pair of order-preserving maps $L \dashv R$. The fundamental example comes from a group $G$ acting on a set $U$: take $P = \mathcal{P}(U)$ and $Q = \mathcal{P}(G)$, ordered by inclusion, with $LX$ being the subgroup fixing all points of $X$ and $RS$ being the set of points fixed by all elements of $S$. Another key example: the existential and universal quantifiers $\exists$ and $\forall$ are left and right adjoints (respectively) to the inverse image operation, revealing that basic logical operations are adjoints. This illustrates Lawvere's slogan: "adjoints are everywhere."
