A category $J$ is connected if any two objects can be joined by a finite zigzag of arrows (going in either direction). Every category is a disjoint union of its connected components. The notion of connectedness is important for limits: for a connected category $J$, the limit and colimit of a constant functor $\Delta c: J \to C$ are both $c$ itself. Moreover, all limits can be obtained from products and limits over connected categories, which is a useful decomposition in the general theory of limits.
