A functor is an equivalence of categories if and only if it is full, faithful, and essentially surjective on objects (each object of the target is isomorphic to the image of some object). An adjoint equivalence is one where the natural isomorphisms witnessing the equivalence also serve as the unit and counit of an adjunction. This characterization is fundamental: it shows that equivalences can always be upgraded to adjoint equivalences, and it provides a practical way to recognize when two categories are equivalent. The theorem also shows that any equivalence $S$ has a quasi-inverse $T$ which is simultaneously a left and right adjoint of $S$.
