The braid category $\mathcal{B}$ furnishes a canonical example of a strict monoidal category. The tensor product is given by addition of natural numbers on objects and by placing braids side by side on morphisms. Associativity and the unit property hold strictly, making $\mathcal{B}$ a strict monoidal category. Importantly, $\mathcal{B}$ is not quite symmetric: while $m+n = n+m$ as objects, the natural isomorphism $\gamma_{m,n}$ involves actual braiding rather than a simple swap.
