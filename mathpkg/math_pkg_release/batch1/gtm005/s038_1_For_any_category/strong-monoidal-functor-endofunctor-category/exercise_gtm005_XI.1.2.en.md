If, in Exercise 1, $C = M$ is a monoidal category, show that there is a strong monoidal functor $(T, T_2, T_0) : M \to M^M$ in which, for $a, b, c \in M$,

$$T(a) = a \mathbin{\square} -,$$

$$T_2(a, b)_c = \alpha_{a,b,c} : a \mathbin{\square} (b \mathbin{\square} c) \to (a \mathbin{\square} b) \mathbin{\square} c,$$

$$(T_0)_a = \lambda(a)^{-1} : a \to e \mathbin{\square} a.$$

In particular, note that the conditions (3) and (4) above for this monoidal functor $T$ became the conditions (5), (7), and (9) of \S VII.1 in the definition of a monoidal category.
