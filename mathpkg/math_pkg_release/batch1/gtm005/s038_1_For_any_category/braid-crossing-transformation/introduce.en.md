The braid crossing transformation $\gamma_{m,n}$ in the braid category $\mathcal{B}$ is the morphism that crosses $m$ strings over $n$ strings, implementing the commutativity of the tensor product $+$ at the level of objects. Unlike a symmetric monoidal category where $\gamma_{n,m} \circ \gamma_{m,n} = \mathrm{id}$, in the braid category the composition $\gamma_{n,m} \circ \gamma_{m,n}$ yields a non-trivial braid. This makes $\mathcal{B}$ a braided monoidal category — the motivating example for the entire theory of braidings.
