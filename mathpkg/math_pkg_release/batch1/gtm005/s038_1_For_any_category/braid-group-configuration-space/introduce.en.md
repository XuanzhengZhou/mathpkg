The braid group $B_n$ admits an elegant topological definition as the fundamental group of the configuration space $T_n$ of $n$ distinct points in the Euclidean plane. This perspective connects braid theory to algebraic topology: a loop in $T_n$ corresponds to a continuous motion of $n$ non-colliding particles in the plane, whose worldlines trace out a geometric braid. The fundamental group $\pi_1(T_n)$ captures exactly the group structure of braids under concatenation.
