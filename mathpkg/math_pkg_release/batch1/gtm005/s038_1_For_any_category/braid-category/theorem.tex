The \emph{braid category} $\mathcal{B}$ is the category whose objects are all natural numbers $n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$ (including zero). For objects $n, m \in \mathbb{N}$, the hom-set $\mathcal{B}(n, m)$ consists of braids on $n$ strings from left to right, with
$$
\mathcal{B}(n, m) = \begin{cases}
B_n, & \text{if } n = m, \\
\varnothing, & \text{if } n \neq m,
\end{cases}
$$
where $B_n$ is the Artin braid group on $n$ strings. The only arrow with domain $0$ is the identity arrow $\varnothing \to \varnothing$. Composition of arrows is given by concatenation of braids.
