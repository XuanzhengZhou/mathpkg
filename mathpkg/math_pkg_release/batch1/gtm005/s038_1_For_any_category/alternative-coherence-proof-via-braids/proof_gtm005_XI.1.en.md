The coherence theorem for braided monoidal categories can be proved using geometric braid diagrams. Each canonical map built from $\alpha$, $\lambda$, $\rho$, and the braiding $\gamma$ corresponds to a braid on strands representing the tensor factors.

The hexagon axioms for $\gamma$ correspond to the braid relation $\sigma_i \sigma_{i+1} \sigma_i = \sigma_{i+1} \sigma_i \sigma_{i+1}$ (Artin relation). Naturality of $\gamma$ corresponds to far commutativity $\sigma_i \sigma_j = \sigma_j \sigma_i$ for $|i-j| > 1$.

Two canonical maps are equal iff the corresponding braids are isotopic, which is equivalent to equality in the braid group $B_n$ (generated by $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_{n-1}$ with the two relations above). The coherence axioms capture precisely the braid group presentation, establishing the theorem.
