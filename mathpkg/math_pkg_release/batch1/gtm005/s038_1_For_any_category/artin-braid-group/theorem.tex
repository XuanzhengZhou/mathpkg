The \emph{Artin braid group} $B_n$ on $n$ strings is the group generated by the $n-1$ elements $\sigma_1, \sigma_2, \ldots, \sigma_{n-1}$, subject to the relations
$$
\sigma_i \sigma_{i+1} \sigma_i = \sigma_{i+1} \sigma_i \sigma_{i+1}, \quad \text{for all } i = 1, \ldots, n-2,
$$
$$
\sigma_i \sigma_j = \sigma_j \sigma_i, \quad \text{for } |i-j| \neq 1.
$$
The generator $\sigma_i$ corresponds to twisting the $i$-th string once under the $(i+1)$-st string. The braid group $B_2$ is the infinite cyclic group on the single generator $\sigma_1$. There is a canonical surjective homomorphism $B_n \twoheadrightarrow S_n$ onto the symmetric group, sending each $\sigma_i$ to the transposition $\tau_i = (i\; i+1)$.
