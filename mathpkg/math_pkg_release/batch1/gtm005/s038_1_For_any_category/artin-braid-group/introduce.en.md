The Artin braid group $B_n$ is a fundamental object in geometric group theory and low-dimensional topology. It is defined by $n-1$ generators $\sigma_i$ that represent crossing the $i$-th string under the $(i+1)$-st string, subject to the braid relations and far-commutativity. The group $B_n$ captures the algebraic structure of braiding $n$ strings in three-dimensional space, and serves as a central example in the theory of braided monoidal categories.
