For any ring $R$, the tensor product $T(A) = R \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} A$ defines an endofunctor on the category $\mathbf{Ab}$ of abelian groups. Together with the unit $\eta_A(a) = 1 \otimes a$ and the multiplication $\mu_A(r_1 \otimes (r_2 \otimes a)) = r_1 r_2 \otimes a$, this forms a monad $T$ on $\mathbf{Ab}$. The algebras of this monad are precisely the left $R$-modules: an algebra structure map $h : R \otimes A \to A$ corresponds exactly to a left $R$-module structure on the abelian group $A$, where the scalar multiplication is $r \cdot a = h(r \otimes a)$. This is a fundamental example connecting the categorical notion of monad to classical algebra.
