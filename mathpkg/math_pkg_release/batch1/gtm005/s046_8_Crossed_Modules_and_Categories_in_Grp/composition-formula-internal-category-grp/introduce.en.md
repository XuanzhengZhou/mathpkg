In an internal category in the category of groups, the categorical composition is not independent data but is forced by the group multiplication together with the domain, codomain, and identity homomorphisms. Using the middle four exchange law and the fact that inversion is a functor, one derives the explicit formulas $g \circ f = f 1_b^{-1} g = g 1_b^{-1} f$. These formulas show that the commutator $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1]$ must be trivial, which is precisely the condition that distinguishes internal categories in $\mathbf{Grp}$ from arbitrary diagrams of group homomorphisms.
