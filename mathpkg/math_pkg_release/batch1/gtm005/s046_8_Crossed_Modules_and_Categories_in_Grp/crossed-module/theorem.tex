A \textbf{crossed module} consists of two groups $G$ and $H$, a group homomorphism $\partial: H \rightarrow G$, and an action of $G$ on $H$, denoted $(g, h) \mapsto {}^{g}h$, such that for all $g \in G$, $h, h' \in H$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\partial({}^{g}h) = g(\partial h)g^{-1}$ (equivariance);
\item ${}^{\partial h}h' = h h' h^{-1}$ (Peiffer identity).
\end{enumerate}
