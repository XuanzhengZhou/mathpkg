The main result of Section XII.8 of Mac Lane's "Categories for the Working Mathematician" establishes that internal categories in the category of groups (i.e., category objects in $\mathbf{Grp}$) are equivalent to crossed modules. This theorem, formulated by George Janelidze, reveals a deep connection between categorical algebra and homotopy theory. In one direction, taking the kernel of the domain map yields the group $H$, while the object-of-objects becomes $P$; in the converse direction, the composition of the internal category is recovered from the group multiplication via the formula $g \circ f = f 1_b^{-1} g$. The crucial commutator condition $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$ is precisely what makes this correspondence work.
