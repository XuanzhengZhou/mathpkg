Conversely, given just the homomorphisms $d_0, d_1, i$ between groups $C_1$ and $C_0$ satisfying the identity conditions $d_0 i = 1 = d_1 i$ and the vanishing commutator $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$, one can reconstruct the composition of an internal category by the formula $\gamma(g, f) = f 1_b^{-1} g$. This shows that for internal categories in $\mathbf{Grp}$, the composition is entirely determined by the group structure and these three homomorphisms. It provides the converse direction of the equivalence between crossed modules and category objects in groups.
