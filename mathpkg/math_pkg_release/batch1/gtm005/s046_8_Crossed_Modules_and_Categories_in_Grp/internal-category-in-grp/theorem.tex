An \text{internal category} (or \text{category object}) in the category $\mathbf{Grp}$ of groups consists of two groups $C_0$ and $C_1$ together with four group homomorphisms

$$d_0, d_1 : C_1 \rightarrow C_0, \quad i : C_0 \rightarrow C_1, \quad \gamma : C_1 \times_{C_0} C_1 \rightarrow C_1$$

satisfying the usual category axioms (where $\gamma$ is the composition on composable pairs). The identities satisfy

$$d_0 i = 1 = d_1 i,$$

and the commutator subgroup of the kernels of $d_0$ and $d_1$ vanishes:

$$[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1.$$

In the category $\mathbf{Grp}$, the composition $\gamma$ is determined by the group structure of $C_1$ together with $d_0, d_1, i$.
