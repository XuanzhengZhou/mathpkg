Prove Theorem 1, using split coequalizers rather than absolute coequalizers, noting that each $\omega_X$ must be defined in terms of a splitting $\langle s, t \rangle$ of the fork (1) as

$$\omega_X(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = e \omega_B(sx_1, \ldots, sx_n), \quad x_i \in X.$$

(For $n = 2$, observe that this is like the usual definition of the product of cosets of a normal subgroup.)
