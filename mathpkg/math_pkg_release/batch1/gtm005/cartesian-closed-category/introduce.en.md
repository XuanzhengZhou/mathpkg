A cartesian closed category (CCC) is a category with finite products and exponentials (function spaces). In a CCC, the functor $- \times b$ has a right adjoint $(-)^b$, giving a natural isomorphism $\hom(a \times b, c) \cong \hom(a, c^b)$. CCCs provide the categorical semantics for typed lambda calculus and are fundamental in theoretical computer science. Key examples include Set and Cat.
