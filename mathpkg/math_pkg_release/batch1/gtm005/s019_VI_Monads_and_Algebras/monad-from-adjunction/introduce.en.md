Every adjunction $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$ gives rise to a monad on the domain category $X$. The endofunctor $T = GF$ is the composite of the right adjoint followed by the left adjoint, the unit of the monad is simply the unit $\eta$ of the adjunction, and the multiplication $\mu = G\varepsilon F$ is obtained from the counit by whiskering with $F$ and $G$. This construction shows that monads are ubiquitous in category theory: wherever there is an adjunction, there is a monad.
