Let $T = \langle T, \eta, \mu \rangle$ be a monad in a category $X$.
A \textbf{$T$-algebra} $\langle x, h \rangle$ consists of
an object $x \in X$ (called the \textbf{underlying object}) and
an arrow $h: Tx \to x$ of $X$ (called the \textbf{structure map} of the algebra)
which make the following diagrams commute:

\[
\begin{CD}
T^2 x @>{Th}>> Tx \\
@V{\mu_x}VV @VV{h}V \\
Tx @>>{h}> x
\end{CD}
\qquad
\begin{CD}
x @>{\eta_x}>> Tx \\
@| @VV{h}V \\
x @= x
\end{CD}
\]

The first diagram expresses the \textbf{associative law} for the algebra,
the second the \textbf{unit law}.
