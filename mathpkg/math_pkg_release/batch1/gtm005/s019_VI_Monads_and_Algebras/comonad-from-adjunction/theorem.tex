Let $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$ be an adjunction with
$F: X \to A$, $G: A \to X$, unit $\eta: I_X \to GF$, and counit $\varepsilon: FG \to I_A$.
Then the composite endofunctor $L = FG: A \to A$, together with the counit $\varepsilon: FG \to I_A$
and the natural transformation
$$\delta = F\eta G: FG \to FGFG = FG FG = L^2$$
defines a \textbf{comonad} $\langle FG, \varepsilon, F\eta G \rangle$ in the category $A$.
This is called the \textbf{comonad defined by the adjunction}.
