A monad is a categorical abstraction of algebraic structure, generalizing the notion of a monoid to the setting of endofunctors. Formally, a monad on a category $X$ consists of an endofunctor $T: X \to X$ equipped with two natural transformations $\eta: I_X \to T$ (the unit) and $\mu: T^2 \to T$ (the multiplication) satisfying associativity and unit laws expressed by commutative diagrams. Monads arise naturally from adjunctions and provide a powerful framework for studying universal algebra, computational effects, and descent theory.
