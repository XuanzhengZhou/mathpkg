A \textbf{comonad} in a category $A$ consists of a functor $L: A \to A$ and two natural transformations
$$\varepsilon: L \to I_A, \qquad \delta: L \to L^2$$
which make the following diagrams commute:

\[
\begin{CD}
L @>{\delta}>> L^2 \\
@V{\delta}VV @VV{L\delta}V \\
L^2 @>>{\delta L}> L^3
\end{CD}
\qquad
\begin{CD}
L @= L @= L \\
@| @VV{\delta}V @| \\
IL @<<{\varepsilon L}<< L^2 @>>{L\varepsilon}>> LI
\end{CD}
\]

The natural transformation $\varepsilon$ is called the \textbf{counit} and $\delta$ the \textbf{comultiplication}.
Comonads are the formal dual of monads, obtained by reversing all arrows in the definition.
