A comonad is the categorical dual of a monad, obtained by reversing the direction of all arrows in the definition. It consists of an endofunctor $L: A \to A$ together with a counit $\varepsilon: L \to I_A$ and a comultiplication $\delta: L \to L^2$ satisfying coassociativity and counit laws. Comonads arise naturally from adjunctions (on the opposite side from monads) and play an important role in descent theory, homology, and the theory of model categories.
