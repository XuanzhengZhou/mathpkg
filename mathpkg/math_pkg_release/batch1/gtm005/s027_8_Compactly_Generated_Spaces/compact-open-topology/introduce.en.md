The compact-open topology is a natural topology on the set of continuous functions between two topological spaces. It is generated by subbasic open sets $N(C, U)$ consisting of all continuous maps sending a given compact subset $C$ of the domain into a given open subset $U$ of the codomain. This topology is fundamental to the study of function spaces and underlies the cartesian closed structure of convenient categories of topological spaces such as CGHaus.
