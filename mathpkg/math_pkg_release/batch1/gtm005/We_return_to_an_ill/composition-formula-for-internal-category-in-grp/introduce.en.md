In any internal category in the category of groups, the categorical composition is forced by the group structure rather than being independent data. Specifically, for composable arrows $f: a \to b$ and $g: b \to c$, the composition can be expressed as $g \circ f = f 1_b^{-1} g$ (or equivalently $g 1_b^{-1} f$), where $1_b$ is the identity morphism at $b$ and multiplication is the group operation in $C_1$. This formula reveals that the commutator condition $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$ is both necessary and sufficient for the group structure to define a well-behaved category composition.
