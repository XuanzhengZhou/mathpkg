In an internal category in Grp with groups $C_0$ (objects) and $C_1$ (morphisms), the categorical composition $\gamma$ is determined by the group structure of $C_1$. For composable morphisms $a \xrightarrow{f} b \xrightarrow{g} c$, the composition is given by:

$$g \circ f = f \, 1_b^{-1} \, g.$$

Equivalently, the same composition can be expressed as:

$$g \circ f = g \, 1_b^{-1} \, f.$$

Hence the group product satisfies the commutation relation:

$$f \, 1_b^{-1} \, g = g \, 1_b^{-1} \, f.$$

In particular, if the object $b$ of $C_0$ is the unit element of the group $C_0$, so that $1_b = i(b)$ is the unit element of $C_1$, then $f g = g f$ for all $f \in \ker d_1$ and $g \in \ker d_0$, which is precisely the condition $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$.
