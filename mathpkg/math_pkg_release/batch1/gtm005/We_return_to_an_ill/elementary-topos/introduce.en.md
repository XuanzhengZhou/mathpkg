An elementary topos is a category equipped with structure that allows it to serve as a foundation for mathematics, generalizing the category of sets. The axioms require a terminal object, pullbacks, a subobject classifier $\Omega$ (which plays the role of the set of truth values $\{0, 1\}$), and power objects $Pb$ (which internalize the notion of the power set of $b$). In the category of sets, $\Omega$ is any two-element set and $Pb$ is the set of all subsets of $b$, with the evaluation map $\varepsilon_b$ sending $(x, s)$ to $1$ if $x \in s$ and $0$ otherwise.
