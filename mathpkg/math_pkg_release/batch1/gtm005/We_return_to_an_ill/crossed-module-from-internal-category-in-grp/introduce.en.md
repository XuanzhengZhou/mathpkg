Every internal category in the category of groups naturally gives rise to a crossed module. The construction takes $H$ as the kernel of the domain map $d_0$ and $P$ as the group of objects $C_0$, with the boundary map $\partial$ being the restriction of the codomain map $d_1$ to $\ker d_0$. The action of $P$ on $H$ comes from the standard conjugation action in the group extension determined by the short exact sequence $1 \to \ker d_0 \to C_1 \to C_0 \to 1$. This theorem, due to George Janelidze, establishes that category objects in Grp and crossed modules are essentially the same structure.
