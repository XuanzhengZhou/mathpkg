A crossed module consists of two groups $H$ and $P$ along with a right action of $P$ on $H$ and a homomorphism $\partial: H \to P$ that is equivariant with respect to the conjugation action of $P$ on itself. This algebraic structure captures the homotopy-theoretic information of a connected 2-type, linking group theory with higher category theory. Crossed modules provide a purely algebraic model for connected homotopy 2-types and arise naturally as the structure of a category object internal to the category of groups.
