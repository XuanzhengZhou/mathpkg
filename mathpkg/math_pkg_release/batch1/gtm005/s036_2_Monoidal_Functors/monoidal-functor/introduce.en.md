A monoidal functor is the notion of a structure-preserving map between monoidal categories. It consists of an ordinary functor $F : M \to M'$ together with coherence maps $F_2(a,b) : F(a) \square F(b) \to F(a \square b)$ and $F_0 : e' \to F e$ that relate the monoidal products and units of the two categories. These maps must satisfy three commutative diagrams ensuring compatibility with the associativity $\alpha$, left unit $\lambda$, and right unit $\rho$ isomorphisms.
