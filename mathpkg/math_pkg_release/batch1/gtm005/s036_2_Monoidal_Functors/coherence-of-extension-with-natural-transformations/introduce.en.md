When a monoidal natural transformation $\theta : F \to G$ is given, the extensions $F_v$ and $G_v$ to arbitrary tensor words are compatible with $\theta$. Specifically, the naturality square involving $F_v$, $G_v$, and the components of $\theta$ commutes for every word $v$. This generalizes the two basic conditions in the definition of a monoidal natural transformation (which correspond to the cases $v = \square$ and $v = e$).
