A strong monoidal functor is a monoidal functor in which the structure maps $F_2(a,b) : F(a) \square F(b) \to F(a \square b)$ and $F_0 : e' \to F e$ are all isomorphisms. This is the natural notion of equivalence between monoidal categories: a strong monoidal functor with an essentially surjective underlying functor that is fully faithful gives a monoidal equivalence.
