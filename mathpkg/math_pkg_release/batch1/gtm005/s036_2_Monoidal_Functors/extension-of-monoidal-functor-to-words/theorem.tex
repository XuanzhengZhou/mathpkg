For a monoidal functor $F$, the maps $F_2$ for the product and $F_0$ for the unit can be extended to the functors defined by arbitrary tensor words $v$ in $n$ letters (as defined in \S VII.2). This gives for each such word $v$ a natural transformation

$$F_v : v(F a_1, \ldots, F a_n) \to F v(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$$

natural in $a_1, \ldots, a_n$, such that $F_\square$ is $F_2$ and $F_e$ is $F_0$. Words are defined inductively as tensor products $v \square v'$ of shorter words and we take $F_{v \square v'}$ as $F_2(F_v \square F_{v'})$.
