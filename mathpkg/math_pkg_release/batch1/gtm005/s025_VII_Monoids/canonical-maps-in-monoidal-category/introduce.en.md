As a consequence of the coherence theorem, for any monoidal category $B$ and any pair of parenthesizations $v, w$ of the same word length, there is a unique canonical natural isomorphism from the functor $v_B$ to $w_B$. These canonical maps are closed under composition and the monoidal product, providing a consistent way to rebracket iterated tensor products. This result justifies the common practice of writing iterated monoidal products without parentheses.
