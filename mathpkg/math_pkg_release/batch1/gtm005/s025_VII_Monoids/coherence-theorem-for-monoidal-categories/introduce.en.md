Mac Lane's coherence theorem for monoidal categories asserts that every formal diagram constructed from the associativity and unit isomorphisms commutes. More precisely, there exists a free monoidal category $W$ on one generator such that for any monoidal category $B$ and any object $b \in B$, there is a unique strict monoidal functor $W \to B$ sending the generator to $b$. This justifies the common practice of omitting parentheses in iterated tensor products.
