A monoidal category generalizes the notion of a strict monoidal category by allowing the associativity and unit laws to hold only up to coherent natural isomorphism, rather than as strict equalities. The coherence axioms—the pentagon and triangle diagrams—ensure that all formal diagrams built from the structure isomorphisms $\alpha$, $\lambda$, and $\varrho$ commute. This is the framework in which ordinary tensor products (of vector spaces, abelian groups, modules) are treated categorically, since in those cases associativity $U \otimes (V \otimes W) \cong (U \otimes V) \otimes W$ is only an isomorphism, not an identity.
