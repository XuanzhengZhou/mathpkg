A strict monoidal category is a category equipped with an associative bifunctor and a two-sided identity object. It provides the simplest setting for defining monoids internal to a category. The bifunctor, often denoted by $\square$ (or $\otimes$ in many texts), acts as a product operation on both objects and arrows, with the interchange law ensuring compatibility with composition. The strictness conditions require that associativity and unit laws hold as exact equalities of functors, rather than merely up to natural isomorphism.
