Categorical duality is the process of reversing all arrows in a category. Given any categorical statement or construction, its dual is obtained by systematically interchanging domain and codomain, and reversing the order of composition. This principle, when formalized via the elementary theory of an abstract category, yields the duality principle: any theorem provable from the category axioms remains true after reversing all arrows, producing the dual theorem for free.
