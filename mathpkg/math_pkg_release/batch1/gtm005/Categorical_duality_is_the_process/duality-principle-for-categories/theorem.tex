Let $\Sigma$ be a statement in the elementary theory of an abstract category (ETAC). If $\Sigma$ is a consequence of the axioms for a category, then the dual statement $\Sigma^{*}$ is also a consequence of the axioms.

Equivalently: In any proof of a theorem $\Sigma$ about an arbitrary category from the category axioms, replacing each statement by its dual yields a valid proof of the dual statement $\Sigma^{*}$.

Moreover, the duality principle extends to statements involving several categories and functors between them. For a functor $T : C \rightarrow B$, the dual statement is formed by simultaneously dualizing the atomic parts referring to $C$ and to $B$ (i.e., reversing arrows in both $C$ and $B$). Since the statement that $T$ is a functor (i.e., $T(g \circ f) = Tg \circ Tf$ and $T(1_a) = 1_{Ta}$) is self-dual, the duality principle remains valid in this extended setting.
