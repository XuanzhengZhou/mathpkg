The Elementary Theory of an Abstract Category (ETAC) is the formal first-order language used to state and prove theorems about categories on an axiomatic basis. Its atomic formulas capture the fundamental undefined notions of category theory: domain, codomain, identity arrows, and composition. From these atomic statements, well-formed formulas are built using the usual logical connectives and quantifiers. A sentence is a formula in which all variables are bound by quantifiers. ETAC provides the precise formal framework within which the duality principle is stated.
