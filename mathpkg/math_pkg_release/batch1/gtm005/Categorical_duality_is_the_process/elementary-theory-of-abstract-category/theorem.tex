The elementary theory of an abstract category (ETAC) consists of statements $\Sigma$ built from atomic statements involving the undefined terms of category theory:

\begin{itemize}
\item ``$a$ is the domain of $f$'' \quad (written $a = \operatorname{dom} f$)
\item ``$b$ is the codomain of $f$'' \quad (written $b = \operatorname{cod} f$)
\item ``$i$ is the identity arrow of $a$'' \quad (written $i = 1_a$)
\item ``$g$ can be composed with $f$ and $h$ is the composite'' \quad (written $h = g \circ f$)
\item $a = b$ and $f = g$
\end{itemize}

These atomic statements are combined using ordinary propositional connectives (and, or, not, implies, if and only if) and quantifiers (``for all $a$'', ``for all $f$'', ``there exists $a$ \ldots'', ``there exists $f$ \ldots''). The notation $f : a \rightarrow b$ abbreviates ``$a$ is the domain of $f$ and $b$ is the codomain of $f$''. A sentence is a statement with all variables quantified (no free variables).
