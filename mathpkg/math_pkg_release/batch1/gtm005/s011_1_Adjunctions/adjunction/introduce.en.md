An adjunction is the most important concept in category theory after the basic definitions. It formalizes the idea of a 'most efficient' or 'optimal' solution to a universal problem. Every free construction, every universal property, and every Galois connection is an instance of an adjunction. The slogan is: 'Adjoint functors arise everywhere.'
