Mac Lane gives a criterion for the existence of a left adjoint: a functor $G: A \to X$ has a left adjoint precisely when, for each $x$, the composite functor $\hom_X(x, G-)$ is representable. This is the theoretical basis for the General Adjoint Functor Theorem: under suitable completeness and solution-set conditions, the representability of these functors is guaranteed.
