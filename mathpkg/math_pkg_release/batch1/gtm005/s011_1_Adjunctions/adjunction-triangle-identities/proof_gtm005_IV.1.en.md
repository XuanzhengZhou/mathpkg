For any $f: Fc \rightarrow d$, the adjunction isomorphism is $\varphi(f) = Gf \circ \eta_c$. For any $g: c \rightarrow Gd$, $\varphi^{-1}(g) = \varepsilon_d \circ Fg$. The triangle identities follow from the bijection being natural and the definition of $\eta$, $\varepsilon$. Conversely, given $\eta$ and $\varepsilon$ satisfying the triangle identities, define $\varphi(f) = Gf \circ \eta_c$ and $\varphi^{-1}(g) = \varepsilon_d \circ Fg$; the triangle identities ensure these are mutual inverses.
