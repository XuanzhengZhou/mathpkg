The unit $\eta: I \xrightarrow{\cdot} GF$ and counit $\varepsilon: FG \xrightarrow{\cdot} I$ of an adjunction satisfy the \textbf{triangle identities}:
\[
G \xrightarrow{\eta G} GFG \xrightarrow{G\varepsilon} G = G \xrightarrow{1_G} G,
\]
\[
F \xrightarrow{F\eta} FGF \xrightarrow{\varepsilon F} F = F \xrightarrow{1_F} F.
\]
Conversely, given $F, G$, a natural $\eta: I \xrightarrow{\cdot} GF$, and a natural $\varepsilon: FG \xrightarrow{\cdot} I$ satisfying these identities, there is a unique adjunction with this unit and counit.
