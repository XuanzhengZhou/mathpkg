Categorical duality is the fundamental symmetry principle of category theory. Every categorical statement has a dual obtained by reversing all arrows, and this dual statement holds automatically if the original does. This simple idea doubles the power of categorical reasoning, allowing each theorem to yield a dual theorem without additional proof. The duality principle underpins the relationship between concepts such as products and coproducts, limits and colimits, and monomorphisms and epimorphisms.
