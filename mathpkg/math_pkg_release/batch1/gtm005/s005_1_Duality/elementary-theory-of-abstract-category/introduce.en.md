The Elementary Theory of an Abstract Category (ETAC) is the formal first-order language used to axiomatize category theory without reference to set theory. Its atomic formulas express the fundamental categorical relations: domain, codomain, identity arrows, and composition, together with equality on objects and arrows. From these atomic statements, well-formed formulas are built using propositional connectives and quantifiers, with the sentence $f: a \to b$ serving as the standard abbreviation for "$a$ is the domain of $f$ and $b$ is the codomain of $f$". This purely axiomatic formulation is essential for the precise statement of the duality principle, which operates at the level of formal sentences.
