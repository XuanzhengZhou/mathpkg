The involution property of duality states that applying the dualization operator twice returns the original statement: $\Sigma^{**} = \Sigma$. This follows immediately from the definition of the dual statement, since reversing every arrow twice restores each arrow to its original direction, each domain to its original interpretation, and the order of composition to its original order. This property confirms that duality is an involution on the language of ETAC, a fundamental syntactic fact that underpins the duality principle.
