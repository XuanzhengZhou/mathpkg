The Elementary Theory of an Abstract Category (ETAC) provides the formal first-order language in which categorical statements are expressed. It defines the atomic formulas --- domain, codomain, identity, composition, and equality --- from which all categorical assertions are built using logical connectives and quantifiers. This formalization is essential for stating the duality principle precisely: a statement is dualized by reversing all arrows in its atomic components. The distinction between statements (possibly with free variables) and sentences (fully quantified) underpins the axiomatic treatment of categories in Chapter II.
