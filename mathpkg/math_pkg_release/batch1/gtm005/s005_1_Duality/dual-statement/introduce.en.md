The dual statement $\Sigma^{*}$ is the formal syntactic operation that underlies categorical duality. Given any well-formed formula $\Sigma$ in the language of ETAC, its dual is obtained by systematically reversing every arrow: domain and codomain are interchanged, and the order of composition is reversed. In diagrammatic terms, every arrow in a commutative diagram is flipped. This operation is purely formal and does not appeal to any set-theoretic construction; it is the essential prerequisite for the duality principle.
