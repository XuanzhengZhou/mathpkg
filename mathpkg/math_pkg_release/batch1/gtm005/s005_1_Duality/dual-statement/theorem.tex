For any statement $\Sigma$ of the elementary theory of an abstract category (ETAC), the dual statement $\Sigma^{*}$ is the statement obtained from $\Sigma$ by reversing the direction of every arrow. Formally, this means interchanging domain and codomain in each atomic statement: each occurrence of ``$a$ is the domain of $f$'' is replaced by ``$a$ is the codomain of $f$'', and each occurrence of ``$b$ is the codomain of $f$'' is replaced by ``$b$ is the domain of $f$''. In the arrow notation, the dual of $f: a \to b$ is $f: b \to a$. The composition $h = g \circ f$ is replaced by $h = f \circ g$ (composition in the reverse order). If a statement involves a diagram, the dual statement involves that diagram with all arrows reversed.
