The duality principle is the fundamental metatheorem of category theory. It asserts that if a sentence $\Sigma$ is provable from the axioms of ETAC, then its dual $\Sigma^{*}$ is also provable. The proof is straightforward: the dual of each axiom for a category is also an axiom (the axioms are self-dual as a set). Therefore, taking the dual of every step in a proof of $\Sigma$ from the axioms yields a valid proof of $\Sigma^{*}$ from the same axioms. As a practical consequence, any theorem proved about arbitrary categories immediately yields a dual theorem without additional work. For example, the uniqueness of terminal objects up to isomorphism dualizes to the uniqueness of initial objects up to isomorphism.
