(Coherence result for an action.) Given a left action $\nu : c \square a \rightarrow a$ of a monoid $c$, describe the properties of canonical maps $\nu_w : w_c a \rightarrow a$, where $w$ is any word of length $\geq 1$ with "last argument" $(-)$ (define what this means), while $w_c a$ results from substituting $a$ for the last argument and $c$ for all the other arguments in $w$.
