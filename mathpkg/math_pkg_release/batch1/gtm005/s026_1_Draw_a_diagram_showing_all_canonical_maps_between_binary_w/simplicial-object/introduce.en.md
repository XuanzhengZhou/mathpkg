A simplicial object in a category $X$ is a contravariant functor from the simplicial category $\Delta$ to $X$. In more traditional and explicit terms, it consists of a sequence of objects $S_n$ (the "$n$-simplices") equipped with face operators $d_i: S_n \to S_{n-1}$ and degeneracy operators $s_i: S_n \to S_{n+1}$ satisfying the simplicial identities. These identities capture the geometric intuition of omitting or doubling vertices of ordered simplices. Simplicial objects are the foundation of simplicial homotopy theory: simplicial sets (when $X = \mathbf{Set}$) model topological spaces up to homotopy, while simplicial abelian groups correspond to chain complexes via the Dold-Kan correspondence.
