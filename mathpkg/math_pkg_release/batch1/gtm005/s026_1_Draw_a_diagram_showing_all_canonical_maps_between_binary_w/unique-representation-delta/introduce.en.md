This lemma provides a canonical factorization of every arrow $f: n \to n'$ in the simplicial category $\Delta$ as a composition of face maps $\delta_i$ (which are injective, "skip" one element) followed by degeneracy maps $\sigma_j$ (which are surjective, "double" one element). The uniqueness lies in the ordering constraints: the upper indices of the $\delta$ maps strictly decrease from left to right, while those of the $\sigma$ maps strictly increase, with all indices appropriately bounded. The parameters $h$ and $k$ count the number of degeneracy and face maps respectively, satisfying $n - h + k = n'$, which reflects how the domain cardinality $n$ is modified by the factorization. This normal form is fundamental to the combinatorial structure of simplicial sets.
