The general associative law extends the binary associativity of a monoid to arbitrary $n$-fold products. It states that multiplying $n$ blocks of factors (with $k_1, \ldots, k_n$ factors each) using the $n$-fold product $\mu^{(n)}$ yields the same result as multiplying all $k_1 + \cdots + k_n$ factors at once via the $n$-fold product of that total arity. This law follows from the coherence of the monoidal category and ensures that iterated products are well-defined regardless of grouping.
