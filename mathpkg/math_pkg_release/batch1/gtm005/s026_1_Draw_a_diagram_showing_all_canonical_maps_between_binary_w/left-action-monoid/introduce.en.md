In a monoidal category $B$, a left action of a monoid $\langle c, \mu, \eta \rangle$ on an object $a$ generalizes the concept of a module over a ring or a group action on a set. The action is given by an arrow $\nu: c \square a \to a$ satisfying two coherence conditions: associativity (acting twice is equivalent to multiplying in the monoid first and then acting once) and unit (acting via the unit is the identity). The monoid always acts on itself via its own multiplication $\mu$, which is called the left regular representation. The category of left $c$-actions, denoted $c\mathbf{Lact}$, has as morphisms those arrows $f: a \to a'$ in $B$ compatible with the action structure.
