Given a monoidal category $B$, the collection of all monoid objects in $B$ together with monoid morphisms between them forms a category denoted $\mathbf{Mon}_B$. There is a canonical forgetful functor $U: \mathbf{Mon}_B \to B$ that maps each monoid $\langle c, \mu, \eta \rangle$ to its underlying object $c$, forgetting the algebraic structure. This construction unifies many familiar categories: ordinary monoids arise when $B = \mathbf{Set}$ with the cartesian product, rings when $B = \mathbf{Ab}$ with the tensor product, and $K$-algebras when $B = K\text{-}\mathbf{Mod}$.
