Theorem 2 establishes sufficient conditions for the existence of free monoids in a monoidal category $B$. If $B$ has denumerable coproducts and the monoidal product $\square$ distributes over them (i.e., the functors $a \square -$ and $- \square a$ preserve coproducts), then the forgetful functor from the category of monoids to the base category admits a left adjoint. This left adjoint sends each object $a \in B$ to the free monoid on $a$, which is constructed as the coproduct $\coprod_n a^n$ equipped with multiplication derived from the distributive law and unit given by the coproduct injection of $a^0 = e$. In many important cases ($B = \mathbf{Set}, \mathbf{Ab}$, etc.), the functors $a \square -$ and $- \square a$ themselves have right adjoints and thus automatically preserve coproducts.
