A symmetric monoidal category is a monoidal category equipped with a natural symmetry isomorphism $\gamma_{a,b}: a \square b \cong b \square a$ that is involutive ($\gamma \circ \gamma = 1$) and compatible with the associator $\alpha$ and unitors $\lambda, \varrho$ via coherence conditions. These conditions, identified by Mac Lane, suffice to prove that all diagrams built from $\alpha, \lambda, \varrho, \gamma$ commute — a coherence theorem for symmetric monoidal categories. Cartesian monoidal categories (where $\square$ is the product) and cocartesian ones (where $\square$ is the coproduct) are automatically symmetric. Symmetric monoidal categories are the natural setting for defining commutative algebraic structures and serve as the foundation for closed categories.
