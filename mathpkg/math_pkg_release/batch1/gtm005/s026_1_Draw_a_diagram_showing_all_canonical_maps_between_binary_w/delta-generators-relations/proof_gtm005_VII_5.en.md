**Proof.** These relations suffice to put any composite of $\delta$'s and $\sigma$'s into the unique form (10) of the Lemma. The relations allow one to reorder compositions: the first relation lets one reorder face maps so that their indices appear in strictly decreasing order; the second relation reorders degeneracy maps so that indices appear in strictly increasing order; and the third relation allows one to push all degeneracy maps past the face maps. After finitely many applications of these relations, any composite reduces to the form with all face maps after all degeneracy maps and with the index ordering constraints satisfied, yielding the unique representation of the Lemma.
