The coherence theorem for monoidal categories (Theorem 1 of Chapter VII) states that for any monoidal category $B$ and any object $b \in B$, there exists a unique strict morphism from the free monoidal category $W$ on one generator to $B$, sending the generator $(-)$ to $b$. This theorem formalizes the intuition that all diagrams built from the associativity and unit isomorphisms $\alpha$, $\lambda$, $\varrho$ commute. The proof proceeds by constructing basic arrows via boxing instances of $\alpha$ with identities, and showing via case analysis that any two directed paths in a graph of such arrows between the same vertices are equal.
