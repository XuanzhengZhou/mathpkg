The Canonical Map Corollary provides a systematic way to assign, for any monoidal category $B$, a unique natural isomorphism $\mathrm{can}_B(v,w)$ between the functors $v_B$ and $w_B$ associated to any two words $v$ and $w$ of the same length. These canonical maps are closed under composition and the monoidal product, and include the fundamental structure isomorphisms $\alpha$, $\lambda$, $\varrho$ and their inverses. This result formalizes the idea that any two ways of building a functor of $n$ variables from iterated applications of $\square$ are naturally isomorphic via a uniquely determined canonical isomorphism.
