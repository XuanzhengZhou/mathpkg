A strict morphism of monoidal categories is a functor between two monoidal categories that preserves the entire monoidal structure on the nose — the bifunctor, the unit object, and all three coherence isomorphisms ($\alpha$, $\lambda$, $\varrho$). This means the functor commutes strictly with the monoidal product on both objects and arrows, maps the unit object to the unit object, and carries each coherence isomorphism to the corresponding one in the target category. With these morphisms, one can form the category $\mathbf{Moncat}$ of all small monoidal categories.
