Prove that the pentagon axiom (5) and the triangle axiom (7) imply the identity (9), namely $(\lambda \square 1) \alpha = \lambda$.

Hint: Take the pentagon (5) with $a = b = e$ and fill in the inside, adding $\varrho$ in two places, the basic identity (7) twice, and suitable naturalities to get $(\lambda \square 1) \alpha \lambda = \lambda \lambda : e \square (e \square (c \square d)) \rightarrow c \square d$, and hence (since $\lambda$ is an isomorphism) $(\lambda \square 1) \alpha = \lambda$.
