This technical lemma states that the projection functor from a comma category $(x \downarrow G)$ to the base category $A$ preserves kernel pairs and hence preserves monomorphisms. This fact is used in the proof of the Special Adjoint Functor Theorem to transfer the intersection-of-subobjects property from $A$ to the comma category $(x \downarrow G)$. The result follows because the projection preserves all limits that $G$ preserves, and the kernel pair of an arrow is a specific kind of limit (a pullback of the arrow with itself).
