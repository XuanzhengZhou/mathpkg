A spanning arrow is a morphism $f: x \to Ga$ that does not factor through the image under $G$ of any proper subobject of $a$. In other words, $f$ "spans" all of $a$ — its image under the adjunction cannot be confined to a smaller subobject. This concept is used in constructing solution sets for the General Adjoint Functor Theorem: the spanning arrows from a fixed set $x$ form a solution set because every arrow factors through a spanning arrow, and there is only a set of isomorphism classes of objects spanned by arrows from a given small set.
