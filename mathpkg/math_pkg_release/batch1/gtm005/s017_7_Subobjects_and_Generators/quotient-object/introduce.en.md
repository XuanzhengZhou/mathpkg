A quotient object is the dual notion to a subobject. While subobjects are defined via monomorphisms into a common codomain, quotient objects are defined via epimorphisms from a common domain. Two epis $r: a \to s$ and $p: a \to t$ are equivalent if each factors through the other. In concrete categories like Grp, quotient objects correspond to the usual notion of quotient algebras — for a group $G$, quotient objects are represented by projections $G \to G/N$ onto factor groups by normal subgroups. The partial order $\leq$ on quotient objects reverses the inclusion order of the corresponding normal subgroups.
