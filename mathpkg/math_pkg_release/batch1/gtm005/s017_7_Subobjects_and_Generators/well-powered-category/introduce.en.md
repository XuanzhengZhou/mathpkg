A category is well-powered if every object has only a set (rather than a proper class) of subobjects. This size condition is a standard hypothesis in both the General and Special Adjoint Functor Theorems, ensuring that intersections of arbitrary families of subobjects are well-defined. Most familiar large categories (Set, Grp, Ab, R-Mod, Top) are well-powered. The term "locally small" is sometimes used synonymously, though that phrase more commonly refers to categories with small hom-sets; Mac Lane recommends avoiding the term "locally small" for this reason.
