This lemma states that in any pullback square, if one of the two given arrows is a monomorphism, then its pullback is also a monomorphism. In particular, the pullback of a monomorphism along any arrow remains monic. This property is essential for the categorical treatment of subobjects because it ensures that the intersection of subobjects (defined via pullback) yields another subobject. The result follows directly from the universal property of the pullback and the definition of a monomorphism.
