Consider a parallel pair $h, k$, as shown, with $f'h = f'k$.
Then $gf'h = gf'k$, so $fg'h = fg'k$.
Since $f$ is monic, this gives $g'h = g'k$.
But we also have $f'h = f'k$; these two equations, since the square is a pullback, imply $h = k$.
Therefore $f'$ is monic.

The symmetric statement for $g$ and $g'$ follows by the same argument.
