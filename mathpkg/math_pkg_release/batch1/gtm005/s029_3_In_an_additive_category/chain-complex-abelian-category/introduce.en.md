A chain complex in an abelian category is a graded sequence of objects and composable arrows (called boundary maps or differentials) satisfying the condition that the composition of any two consecutive boundary maps is the zero morphism. This fundamental structure provides the framework for homological algebra in the general setting of abelian categories. The condition $\partial_n \partial_{n+1} = 0$ implies $\operatorname{Im} \partial_{n+1} \subseteq \operatorname{Ker} \partial_n$, and the failure of this inclusion to be an equality is measured by the homology objects.
