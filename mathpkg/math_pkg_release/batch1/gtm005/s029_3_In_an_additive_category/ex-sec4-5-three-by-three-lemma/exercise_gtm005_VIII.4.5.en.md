A $3 \times 3$ diagram is one of the form (bordered by zeros)

\[
\begin{array}{cccccc}
& 0 & 0 & 0 & \\
0 \rightarrow & A_{11} \rightarrow & A_{12} \rightarrow & A_{13} \rightarrow & 0 \\
0 \rightarrow & A_{21} \rightarrow & A_{22} \rightarrow & A_{23} \rightarrow & 0 \\
0 \rightarrow & A_{31} \rightarrow & A_{32} \rightarrow & A_{33} \rightarrow & 0 \\
& 0 & 0 & 0 &
\end{array}
\]

Give a direct proof of the $3 \times 3$ lemma: If a $3 \times 3$ diagram is commutative and all three columns and the last two rows are short exact sequences, then the first row is also a short exact sequence.
