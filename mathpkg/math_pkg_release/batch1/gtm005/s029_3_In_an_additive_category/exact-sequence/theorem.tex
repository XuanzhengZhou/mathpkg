In an abelian category, a sequence of arrows $\cdots \rightarrow a_{n+1} \xrightarrow{f_{n+1}} a_n \xrightarrow{f_n} a_{n-1} \rightarrow \cdots$ is \textbf{exact} at $a_n$ if $\operatorname{im} f_{n+1} = \ker f_n$ (as subobjects). A \textbf{short exact sequence} is one of the form $0 \rightarrow a \rightarrow b \rightarrow c \rightarrow 0$ which is exact at each object.
