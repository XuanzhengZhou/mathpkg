In an abelian category $A$, call an arrow $x$ with codomain $a \in A$ a \emph{member} of $a$, written $x \in_m a$. For two members $x, y$ of the same object $a$, define $x \equiv y$ to mean that there exist epimorphisms $u, v$ with $x u = y v$. This equivalence relation captures the idea that $x$ and $y$ have the "same image" in $a$. A member $x \in_m a$ is said to have a property if any member equivalent to $x$ also has that property under pullback along epis.
