In an abelian category, members serve as a substitute for elements, enabling diagram chases without requiring a concrete underlying set. A member $x \in_m a$ is simply an arrow with codomain $a$, and two members are considered equivalent when they factor through each other via epimorphisms. This formalism, together with the rules established in Theorem 3, allows all standard diagram lemmas (Five Lemma, Snake Lemma, etc.) to be proved in any abelian category using "member chasing" exactly analogous to element chasing in $\mathbf{Ab}$.
