The $n$-th homology object of a chain complex measures the failure of the complex to be exact at position $n$. It is defined as the quotient of the kernel of the outgoing boundary map by the image of the incoming boundary map. In the context of an abelian category, this quotient is constructed as the cokernel of the inclusion of the image of $\partial_{n+1}$ into the kernel of $\partial_n$, generalizing the classical notion of homology groups from $\mathbf{Ab}$ to any abelian category.
