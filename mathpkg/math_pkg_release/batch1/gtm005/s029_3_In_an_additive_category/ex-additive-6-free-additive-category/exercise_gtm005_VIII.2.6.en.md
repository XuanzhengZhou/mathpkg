(The free additive category.)

(a) Given an $\mathbf{Ab}$-category $A$, construct an additive category $\operatorname{Add}(A)$ and an additive functor $A \rightarrow \operatorname{Add}(A)$ which is universal from $A$ to an additive category. (Hint: Objects of $\operatorname{Add}(A)$ are $n$-tuples of objects of $A$, for $n = 0, 1, \ldots$, while arrows are matrices of arrows of $A$.)

(b) If $A$ is an $\mathbf{Ab}$-category with one object, describe $\operatorname{Add}(A)$ explicitly.
