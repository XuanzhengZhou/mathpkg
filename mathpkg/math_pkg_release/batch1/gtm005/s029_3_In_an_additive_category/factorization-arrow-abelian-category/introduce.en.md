In any abelian category, every morphism admits a canonical factorization into an epimorphism followed by a monomorphism. The epimorphism is given by the cokernel of the kernel, and the monomorphism by the kernel of the cokernel. This factorization is functorial: any morphism between arrows respects the factorization structure. The uniqueness up to isomorphism means that any two such factorizations of the same arrow are related by a unique isomorphism, making this a well-defined structural decomposition of morphisms in abelian categories.
