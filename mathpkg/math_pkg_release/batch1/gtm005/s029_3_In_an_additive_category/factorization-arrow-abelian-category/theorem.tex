In an abelian category $A$, every arrow $f$ has a factorization $f = me$, with $m$ monic and $e$ epi; moreover,

$$m = \ker (\operatorname{coker} f), \quad e = \operatorname{coker} (\ker f).$$

Given any other factorization $f = m'e'$ with $m'$ monic and $e'$ epi, there exists a unique isomorphism $k$ such that $m = m'k$ and $e' = ke$, making the factorization unique up to isomorphism. Moreover, any morphism $\langle g, h \rangle : f \rightarrow f'$ of arrows must carry a factorization of $f$ to a factorization of $f'$, so that the factorization is functorial.
