(Alternative definition of addition of arrows, to get an additive category.)

(a) Let $A$ be a category with a null object, finite products, and finite coproducts in which the canonical arrow $a_1 \amalg a_2 \rightarrow a_1 \times a_2$ from the coproduct to the product is always an isomorphism. For $f, f' : a \rightarrow b$ define $f + f' = \Delta^b (f \times f') \nabla_a$, where $\Delta^b : b \rightarrow b \times b$ is the diagonal and $\nabla_a : a \amalg a \rightarrow a$ is the codiagonal. Prove that this addition makes each set $A(a, b)$ a commutative monoid, and that composition is distributive over this addition.

(b) If, moreover, there is for each $a \in A$ an arrow $v_a : a \rightarrow a$ with $v_a + 1_a = 0 : a \rightarrow a$, prove that each $A(a, b)$ is a group under the addition defined above, and hence that this addition gives $A$ the structure of an additive category (Mac Lane [1950]).
