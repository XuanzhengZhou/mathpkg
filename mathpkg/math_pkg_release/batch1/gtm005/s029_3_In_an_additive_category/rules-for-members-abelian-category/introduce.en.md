This theorem establishes a complete calculus of members for any abelian category, providing element-free characterizations of monomorphisms, epimorphisms, zero arrows, exact sequences, and subtraction. Rules (i)-(v) effectively translate the familiar element-based definitions from $\mathbf{Ab}$ into the language of members, while rule (vi) captures the ability to form "differences" of members, which is essential for diagram chases. Together, these rules make it possible to perform all standard diagram lemmas in any abelian category by "chasing members" instead of elements.
