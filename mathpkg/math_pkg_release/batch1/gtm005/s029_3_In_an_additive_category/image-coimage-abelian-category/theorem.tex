From the monic-epi factorization $f = me : a \rightarrow b$ of any arrow $f$ in an abelian category, define the image and coimage of $f$ as

$$m = \operatorname{im} f, \quad e = \operatorname{coim} f,$$

uniquely up to isomorphism. Thus the image $m$ of $f$ is a subobject of its codomain $b$, its coimage a quotient object of its domain $a$. More generally, if $f = m_1 t e_1$ with $m_1$ monic, $t$ an isomorphism, and $e_1$ epi, then $m_1 \equiv \operatorname{im} f$, $e_1 \equiv \operatorname{coim} f$, and $t$ is the usual isomorphism of coimage to image.
