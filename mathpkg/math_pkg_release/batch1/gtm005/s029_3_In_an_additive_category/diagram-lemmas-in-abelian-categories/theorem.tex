In any abelian category, the standard diagram lemmas hold by embedding into a category of modules via the Freyd-Mitchell embedding theorem:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Five Lemma}: In a commutative diagram with exact rows, if four of the five vertical arrows are isomorphisms, so is the fifth.
\item \textbf{Snake Lemma}: A short exact sequence of complexes yields a long exact sequence in homology.
\item \textbf{Nine Lemma (3x3 Lemma)}: A $3 \times 3$ diagram with exact columns and exact middle row has exact top and bottom rows.
\end{itemize}
