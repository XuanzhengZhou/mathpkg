(Converse of the Theorem of the text.) Let $L : J' \to J$ be a functor, where $J'$ and $J$ have small hom-sets, such that for every $F : J \to X$ with $X$ cocomplete the canonical map $\varinjlim (F \circ L) \to \varinjlim F$ is an isomorphism. Prove that $L$ must be final. (Hint: Use $F = J(k, -)$, $X = \mathbf{Set}$, and Exercise 4.)
