First, $\mathbf{Grp}$ has finite coproducts: the coproduct $G * H$ of two groups is the free product, whose elements are finite words in letters from $G$ and $H$ with multiplication by juxtaposition and equality given by successive cancellations. The morphisms $G \to G * H$ and $H \to G * H$ are the evident inclusions. The universal property holds: any pair of homomorphisms $G \to L$, $H \to L$ factors through the subgroup of $L$ generated by the images, whose cardinality is bounded, verifying the solution set condition for the adjoint functor theorem.

By Proposition 2, $\mathbf{Grp}$ has all filtered colimits. Hence, by Theorem 1, $\mathbf{Grp}$ has all small coproducts. The infinite coproduct $\coprod_i G_i$ is obtained explicitly as the directed union of all finite free products $G_{i_1} * \cdots * G_{i_k}$ identified along inclusion maps.

For coequalizers: given homomorphisms $u, v : G \to H$, let $N$ be the least normal subgroup of $H$ containing all elements $(u g)(v g)^{-1}$ for $g \in G$. Then the projection $H \to H/N$ is the coequalizer of $u$ and $v$.

With all small coproducts and coequalizers, the dual of Theorem V.2.1 implies $\mathbf{Grp}$ has all small colimits.
