Proposition 2 establishes that the category of groups admits filtered colimits, and moreover that the forgetful functor to sets creates them. The proof shows that given a filtered diagram of groups, the colimit of the underlying sets carries a unique group structure making all the structural maps into group homomorphisms. The key idea is that any element of the colimit can be represented in a single group of the diagram (using filteredness), and multiplication is then defined by pushing to a common target.
