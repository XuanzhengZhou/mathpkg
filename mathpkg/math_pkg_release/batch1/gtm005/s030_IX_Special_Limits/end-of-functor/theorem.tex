Let $S : C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to X$ be a functor. An \text{end} of $S$ is a pair $\langle e, \omega \rangle$ consisting of an object $e \in X$ and a dinatural transformation $\omega : e \xrightarrow{\bullet} S$ (called the \text{ending wedge} or \text{universal wedge}) which is universal among all dinatural transformations from constant objects to $S$. Explicitly, for each dinatural $\tau : x \xrightarrow{\bullet} S$, there exists a unique arrow $h : x \to e$ such that $\omega_c \circ h = \tau_c$ for all $c \in C$. The object $e$ is written with integral notation as
$$
e = \int_c S(c, c) = \operatorname{End}(S).
$$
The variable $c$ is bound by the integral sign; the result does not depend on $c$.
