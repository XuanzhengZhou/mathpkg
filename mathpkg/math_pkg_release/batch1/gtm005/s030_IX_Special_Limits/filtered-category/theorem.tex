A category $J$ is \text{filtered} if $J$ is nonempty and satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}
\item For any two objects $j, j' \in J$, there exists an object $k \in J$ and arrows $j \to k$, $j' \to k$.
\item For any two parallel arrows $u, v : i \to j$ in $J$, there exists an object $k \in J$ and an arrow $w : j \to k$ such that $w \circ u = w \circ v$.
\end{enumerate}
It follows that any finite diagram in a filtered category $J$ is the base of at least one cone with a vertex in $J$.
