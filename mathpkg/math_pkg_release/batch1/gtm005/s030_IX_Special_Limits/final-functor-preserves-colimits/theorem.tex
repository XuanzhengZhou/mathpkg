\text{Theorem.} Let $L : J' \to J$ be a final functor and let $F : J \to X$ be a functor such that the colimit of $F$ exists. Then the colimit of $F \circ L$ exists and the canonical map
$$
h : \varinjlim (F \circ L) \to \varinjlim F
$$
is an isomorphism. Moreover, if $X$ is cocomplete and $L$ is final, then the canonical map is an isomorphism for every functor $F : J \to X$.
