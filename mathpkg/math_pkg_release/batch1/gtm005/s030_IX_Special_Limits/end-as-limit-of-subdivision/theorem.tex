\text{Proposition 1.} For any functor $S : C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to X$, let $C^{\S}$ be the subdivision category of $C$ (objects $c^{\S}$ and $f^{\S}$ for each $c \in C$ and each arrow $f$ in $C$, with arrows $b^{\S} \to f^{\S} \leftarrow c^{\S}$ for each $f : b \to c$), and let $S^{\S} : C^{\S} \to X$ be the induced functor. Then a limit of $S^{\S}$ is exactly an end of $S$. Equivalently, every end is a limit of a suitable diagram.
