A \emph{coend} of a functor $S: C^{\text{op}} \times C \rightarrow X$ is a pair $\langle d, \zeta: S \rightarrow d \rangle$ consisting of an object $d \in X$ and a dinatural transformation $\zeta$ (a wedge), universal among dinatural transformations from $S$ to a constant. The object $d$, when it exists, is unique up to isomorphism and is written with an integral sign with the bound variable $c$ as superscript:

$$S(c, c) \xrightarrow{\zeta_c} \int^{c} S(c, c) = d.$$

The formal properties of coends are dual to those of ends.
