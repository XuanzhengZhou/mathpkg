The coend of a functor $S: C^{\text{op}} \times C \rightarrow X$ is the dual notion to an end. It is defined as a universal dinatural transformation (a universal wedge) from the bifunctor $S$ to a constant object. Coends unify many familiar constructions in mathematics: the tensor product of modules over a ring, and the geometric realization of a simplicial set are both naturally expressed as coends. The notation $\int^c S(c,c)$ for the coend object evokes the integral-like character of this universal construction.
