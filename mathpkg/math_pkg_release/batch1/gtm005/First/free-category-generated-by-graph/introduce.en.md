Theorem 1 constructs the free category $C_G$ generated by a graph $G$. Analogous to the free monoid on a set, the free category replaces points by objects and transitions by arrows. The objects of $C_G$ are the vertices of $G$, and its arrows are finite paths (strings of composable arrows) in $G$. Composition is concatenation of paths, and identity arrows are the empty paths. The universal property states that any morphism from $G$ into the underlying graph of a category $B$ extends uniquely to a functor from $C_G$ to $B$, exhibiting $C_G \dashv U$ as a free-forgetful adjunction.
