A category can be presented by specifying a generating graph $G$ and a set of relations among the paths (arrows) of the free category $C_G$. The resulting category is the quotient $C_G / R$ of the free category by the least congruence generated by the relations. This construction generalizes the familiar notion of presenting a monoid (or group) by generators and relations to the categorical setting. For example, the category $\mathbf{3}$ (three objects, three non-identity arrows forming a commutative triangle) can be described by generators $0, 1, 2$ with arrows $f : 0 \to 1$, $g : 1 \to 2$, $h : 0 \to 2$, and the single relation $h = g \circ f$.
