A congruence on a category $C$ is an equivalence relation on each hom-set that is compatible with composition: if two morphisms are equivalent, then precomposing or postcomposing them with any morphism yields equivalent results. This is the categorical analogue of a congruence on an algebraic structure (such as a monoid or group). Congruences are precisely the equivalence relations that allow one to form a well-defined quotient category $C/R$, where morphisms are equivalence classes and composition descends to the quotient.
