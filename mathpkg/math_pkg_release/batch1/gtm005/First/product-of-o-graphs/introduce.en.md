The product over $O$ of two $O$-graphs $A$ and $B$ is the set of composable pairs $\langle g, f \rangle$ where the codomain of $f$ matches the domain of $g$. This operation makes the collection of $O$-graphs into a monoidal structure with $O$ itself as the unit. The product is associative up to canonical isomorphism and is used to express the composition operation of a category as a morphism $c : A \times_O A \to A$ of $O$-graphs in the algebraic definition of a category.
