Given any family of relations $R$ on the hom-sets of a category $C$, there is a quotient category $C/R$ whose morphisms are equivalence classes under the least congruence $R'$ containing $R$. The construction proceeds by first closing $R$ to a congruence (an equivalence relation compatible with composition), then taking equivalence classes. The quotient functor $Q : C \to C/R$ has the universal property that any functor $H$ out of $C$ that identifies $R$-related arrows factors uniquely through $Q$. A special case yields the familiar presentation of monoids or categories by generators and relations.
