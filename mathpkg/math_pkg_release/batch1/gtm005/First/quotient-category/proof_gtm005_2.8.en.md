**Sketch of proof.** Call $R$ a congruence on $C$ if (i) for each pair $a, b$ of objects, $R_{a,b}$ is a reflexive, symmetric, and transitive relation on $C(a, b)$; (ii) if $f, f' : a \to b$ satisfy $f \, R_{a,b} \, f'$, then for all $g : a' \to a$ and all $h : b \to b'$ one has $(h \circ f \circ g) \, R_{a',b'} \, (h \circ f' \circ g)$. Given any $R$, there is a least congruence $R'$ on $C$ with $R \subset R'$ (one takes the intersection of all congruences containing $R$, or constructs $R'$ as the equivalence relation generated by the condition that composition respects the relation — the proof is an exercise).

Now take the objects of $C/R$ to be the objects of $C$, and take each hom-set $(C/R)(a, b)$ to be the quotient $C(a, b) / R'_{a,b}$ of $C(a, b)$ by the equivalence relation $R'$ there. Because the relation is preserved by composition, the composite in $C$ carries over to $C/R$: define $[h] \circ [g] = [h \circ g]$ where $[f]$ denotes the $R'$-equivalence class of $f$. This is well-defined precisely because $R'$ is a congruence. The projection $Q : C \to C/R$ sending each arrow to its equivalence class is then a functor.

Now for any functor $H : C \to D$, the sets
$$S_{a,b} = \{\, (f, f') : a \to b \mid H f = H f' \,\}$$
evidently form a congruence on $C$. Thus, if $S \supset R$ one also has $S \supset R'$, and $H$ factors as $H = H' \circ Q$ with $H'([f]) = H(f)$, as required.
