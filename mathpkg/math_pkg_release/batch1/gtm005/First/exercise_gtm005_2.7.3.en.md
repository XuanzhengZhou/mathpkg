Show that each graph $G$ generates a free groupoid $F$. That is, there exists a groupoid $F$ and a morphism $P : G \to UF$ of graphs satisfying the following universal property (analogous to Theorem 1 with "category" replaced by "groupoid"): for any groupoid $E$ and any morphism $D : G \to UE$ of graphs, there is a unique morphism of groupoids $D' : F \to E$ with $D = UD' \circ P$.

Deduce as a corollary that every set $X$ generates a free group. (Hint: regard $X$ as a graph with one object and $X$ as the set of arrows.)
