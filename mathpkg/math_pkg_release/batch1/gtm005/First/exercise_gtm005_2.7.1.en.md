Define "opposite graph" and "product of two graphs" to agree with the corresponding definitions for categories (i.e., so that the forgetful functor $U : \text{Cat} \to \text{Grph}$ will preserve opposites and products). Specifically:

(a) For a graph $G$, define its opposite graph $G^{\text{op}}$ such that $U(C^{\text{op}}) \cong (U C)^{\text{op}}$ for any category $C$.

(b) For graphs $G$ and $H$, define their product $G \times H$ such that $U(C \times D) \cong UC \times UD$ for any categories $C, D$.
