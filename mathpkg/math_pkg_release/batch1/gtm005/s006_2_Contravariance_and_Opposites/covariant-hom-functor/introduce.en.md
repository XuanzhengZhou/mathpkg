The covariant hom-functor $C(a, -): C \to \mathbf{Set}$ is a fundamental example of a functor in category theory. For a fixed object $a$, it assigns to each object $b$ the set of all arrows from $a$ to $b$, and to each arrow $k: b \to b'$ the function "compose with $k$ on the left." Hom-functors are the prototypical examples of representable functors and play a central role in the Yoneda lemma.
