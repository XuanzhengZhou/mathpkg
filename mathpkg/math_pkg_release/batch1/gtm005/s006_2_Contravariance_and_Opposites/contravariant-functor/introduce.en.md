A contravariant functor $\bar{S}$ from $C$ to $B$ reverses the direction of arrows: it sends each arrow $f: a \to b$ in $C$ to an arrow $\bar{S}f: \bar{S}b \to \bar{S}a$ in $B$. For theoretical convenience, a contravariant functor is canonically represented as an ordinary covariant functor $S: C^{\text{op}} \to B$ on the opposite category, or as $S^{\text{op}}: C \to B^{\text{op}}$. This unified treatment eliminates the need for a separate theory of contravariant functors.
