The notation $k_* = \hom(a, k)$ is a convenient shorthand for the function induced by the arrow $k: b \to b'$ on hom-sets. Given any arrow $f: a \to b$, the map $k_*$ sends $f$ to the composite $k \circ f: a \to b'$. This "composition on the left" notation is standard in category theory and simplifies calculations involving hom-functors.
