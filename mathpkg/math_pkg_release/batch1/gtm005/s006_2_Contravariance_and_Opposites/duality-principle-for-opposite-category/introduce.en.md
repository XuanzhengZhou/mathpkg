The duality principle for the opposite category formalizes the relationship between a category $C$ and its opposite $C^{\text{op}}$. It states that any statement about arrows and composition in $C$ holds exactly when its dual (obtained by reversing all arrows and the order of composition) holds in $C^{\text{op}}$. This allows one to interpret the dual of a property as the original property applied to the opposite category.
