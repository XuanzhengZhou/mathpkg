In the opposite category $C^{\text{op}}$, the direction of every arrow is reversed. Consequently, the property of being monic in $C^{\text{op}}$ corresponds exactly to the property of being epi in the original category $C$. This is the simplest and most immediate example of categorical duality: the concepts of monomorphism and epimorphism are dual to each other.
