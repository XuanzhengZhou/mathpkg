The word monad $W = (W, \eta, \mu)$ on the category of sets is the monad arising from the adjunction between the forgetful functor $G: \mathbf{Semigrp} \to \mathbf{Set}$ and the free semigroup functor. The endofunctor $W$ sends a set $X$ to the set of all nonempty words in $X$; the unit $\eta_X$ embeds each element as a one-letter word; and the multiplication $\mu_X$ flattens a word of words by removing the inner brackets. This monad is one of the most concrete illustrations of how monads encode algebraic structure.
