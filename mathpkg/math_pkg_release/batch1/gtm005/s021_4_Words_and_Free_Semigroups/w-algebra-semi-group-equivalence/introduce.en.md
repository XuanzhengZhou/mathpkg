This corollary to Proposition 2 establishes that the category of $W$-algebras is isomorphic to the category of semigroups. A $W$-algebra structure is equivalent to a set with a single associative binary operation: the unary operation $v_1$ must be the identity, and every higher-arity operation $v_n$ for $n \geq 2$ is forced to be the $n$-fold iteration of the binary operation $v_2$. The comparison functor $K$ simply augments a semigroup by adding all its derived iterated products, yielding an isomorphism rather than just an equivalence.
