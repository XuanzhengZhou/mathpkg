Proposition 2 characterizes the algebras over the word monad $W$. A $W$-algebra is a set $S$ equipped with a family of $n$-ary operations $v_n : S^n \to S$, one for each positive integer $n$. The structure map $\theta : WS \to S$ evaluates a word of length $n$ by applying the $n$-ary operation $v_n$ to its letters. The monad algebra axioms force $v_1$ to be the identity and each $v_n$ to be the $n$-fold iteration of the binary operation $v_2$.
