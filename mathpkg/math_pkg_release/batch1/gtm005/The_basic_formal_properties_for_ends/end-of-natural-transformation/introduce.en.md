A natural transformation between two bifunctors induces a canonical arrow between their ends, analogous to how a natural transformation between two functors induces a map between their limits. This proposition establishes that ends are 2-functorial: the assignment $S \mapsto \int_c S(c,c)$ extends to a functor from the functor category $X^{C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C}$ (restricted to those functors that have ends) to $X$, with the arrow part given by integrating the diagonal components of the natural transformation. The key idea is that the composites $\gamma_{c,c} \circ \omega_c$ form a wedge over $S'$, so the universal property of the end $\int_c S'(c,c)$ yields the unique arrow $\int_c \gamma_{c,c}$.
