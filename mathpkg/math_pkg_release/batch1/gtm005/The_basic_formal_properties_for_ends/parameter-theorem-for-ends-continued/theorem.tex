Let $T: P \times C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to X$ be a functor satisfying the hypotheses of Theorem 2. Define the transposed functor $T^\#: C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to X^P$ as the image of $T$ under the standard adjunction

$$\mathrm{Cat}(P \times C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C, X) \cong \mathrm{Cat}(C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C, X^P),$$

so that on arrows (or objects) $f, f'$ of $C$,

$$T^\#(f, f') = T(-, f, f'): P \to X.$$

Then the functor $T^\#$ has an end

$$\omega^\#: \int_c T(-, c, c) \to T^\#$$

in the functor category $X^P$, whose component at each $c \in C$ is the natural transformation

$$\omega_c^\#: \int_c T(-, c, c) \to T(-, c, c)$$

given at each $p \in P$ by

$$(\omega_c^\#)_p = (\omega_p)_c,$$

where $\omega_p$ is the end wedge for $T(p, -, -)$ as in Theorem 2.
