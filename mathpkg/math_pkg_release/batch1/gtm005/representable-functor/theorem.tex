Let $D$ have small hom-sets. A \textbf{representation} of a functor $K: D \to \mathbf{Set}$ is a pair $\langle r, \psi \rangle$ where $r$ is an object of $D$ and
$$\psi: D(r, -) \cong K$$
is a natural isomorphism. The object $r$ is called the \textbf{representing object}. The functor $K$ is said to be \textbf{representable} when such a representation exists.
