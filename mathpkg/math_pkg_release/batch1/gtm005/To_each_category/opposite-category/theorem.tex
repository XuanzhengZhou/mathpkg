To each category $C$ we associate the \textbf{opposite category} $C^{\text{op}}$. The objects of $C^{\text{op}}$ are the objects of $C$, the arrows of $C^{\text{op}}$ are arrows $f^{\text{op}}$, in one-one correspondence $f \mapsto f^{\text{op}}$ with the arrows $f$ of $C$. For each arrow $f: a \to b$ of $C$, the domain and codomain of the corresponding $f^{\text{op}}$ are as in $f^{\text{op}}: b \to a$ (the direction is reversed). The composite $f^{\text{op}} g^{\text{op}} = (g f)^{\text{op}}$ is defined in $C^{\text{op}}$ exactly when the composite $g f$ is defined in $C$. Moreover, $f^{\text{op}}$ is monic if and only if $f$ is epi, and so on.
