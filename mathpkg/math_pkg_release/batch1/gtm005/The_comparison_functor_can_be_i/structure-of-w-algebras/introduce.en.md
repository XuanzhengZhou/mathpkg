Proposition 2 characterizes the Eilenberg-Moore algebras for the word monad $W$ on $\mathbf{Set}$. A $W$-algebra is a set $S$ equipped with a family of $n$-ary operations $v_n$ for all $n \geq 1$, satisfying generalized associativity conditions that express how operations compose. The unary operation $v_1$ acts as the identity, while $v_2$ gives an associative binary operation. This proposition reveals that $W$-algebras are precisely the algebraic systems obtained from a single associative binary operation by taking all its iterates.
