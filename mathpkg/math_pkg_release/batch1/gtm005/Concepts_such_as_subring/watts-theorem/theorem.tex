Any ring $R$ is a generator in the category $R\text{-}\mathbf{Mod}$, hence a cogenerator in $(R\text{-}\mathbf{Mod})^{\mathrm{op}}$. It follows that any contravariant additive functor $T$ on $R\text{-}\mathbf{Mod}$ to $\mathbf{Ab}$ which takes small colimits to limits (i.e., is a limit-preserving functor $(R\text{-}\mathbf{Mod})^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Ab}$) is representable. That is, there exists an $R$-module $C$ and a natural isomorphism
\[
T \cong \mathrm{Hom}_R(-, C).
\]
