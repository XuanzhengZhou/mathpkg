In any category, the class of monomorphisms is stable under pullback: if one leg of a pullback square is monic, the opposite parallel leg is also monic. This lemma is fundamental for the theory of subobjects, since it guarantees that pulling back a subobject along any morphism yields another subobject. Together with the definition of subobject via equivalence classes of monics, this lemma ensures that subobjects behave well with respect to change of base, forming the foundation for categorical treatments of images, inverse images, and the subobject functor.
