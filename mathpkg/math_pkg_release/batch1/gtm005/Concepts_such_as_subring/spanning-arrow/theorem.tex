Given a functor $G : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{X}$, an arrow $f : x \to G a$ is said to \textbf{span} $a$ when there is no proper subobject $s \to a$ through which $f$ factors. That is, if $f$ factors as $x \xrightarrow{f'} G s \xrightarrow{G u} G a$ for some monic $u : s \to a$, then $u$ must be an isomorphism.

In the context of the adjoint functor theorem applied to categories of algebras, a subobject is often redefined to be a morphism $u : s \to a$ for which $G u$ is injective in $\mathbf{Set}$.
