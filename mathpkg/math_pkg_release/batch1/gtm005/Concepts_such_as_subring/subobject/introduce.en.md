A subobject in a category generalizes the notion of subset, subgroup, or subspace to an arbitrary category. Instead of using elements, subobjects are defined via monomorphisms (monics) with a common codomain, preordered by factorization: $u \leq v$ when $u = v u'$. The equivalence classes under mutual factorization ($u \equiv v$ when $u \leq v$ and $v \leq u$) are precisely the subobjects of the target object. In familiar algebraic categories like $\mathbf{Grp}$, $\mathbf{Ab}$, and $R\text{-}\mathbf{Mod}$, these equivalence classes correspond to the usual element-based subobjects, but notably not in $\mathbf{Top}$ where monics are not simply injective continuous maps up to equivalence.
