The Yoneda Lemma is the most fundamental result in category theory. It establishes that natural transformations from a representable functor $D(r, -)$ to any set-valued functor $K$ correspond bijectively to elements of $Kr$. Every object is determined up to isomorphism by the functor it represents, justifying the philosophy that objects are understood through their morphisms to other objects.
