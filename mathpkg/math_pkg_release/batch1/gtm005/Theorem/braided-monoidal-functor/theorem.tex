Let $B$ and $B'$ be braided monoidal categories, with braidings $\gamma$ and $\gamma'$ respectively. A \textbf{braided monoidal functor} is a monoidal functor
$$(F, F_2, F_0) : B \rightarrow B'$$
which commutes with the braidings $\gamma$ and $\gamma'$ in the sense that the following diagram commutes:
\[
\begin{CD}
F a \otimes F b @>{\gamma'}>> F b \otimes F a \\
@VVV @VVV \\
F(a \otimes b) @>{F(\gamma)}>> F(b \otimes a)
\end{CD}
\]
where the vertical arrows are given by the monoidal structure maps $F_2$, and $\gamma$, $\gamma'$ are the braidings of $B$ and $B'$, respectively.
