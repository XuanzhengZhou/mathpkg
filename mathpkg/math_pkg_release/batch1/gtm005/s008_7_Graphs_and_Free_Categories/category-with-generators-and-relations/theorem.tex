If $C$ is the free category generated by a graph $G$ and $R$ is a congruence on $C$, the quotient $C/R$ is a \textbf{category with generators $G$ and relations $R$}. As a special case (one object), this includes the notion of a monoid given by generators and relations. For example, the category $\mathbf{3}$ is generated by three objects $0, 1, 2$, three arrows $f: 0 \to 1$, $g: 1 \to 2$, $h: 0 \to 2$, and one relation $h = g \circ f$.
