A directed graph (or simply graph in the categorical sense) consists of vertices and directed edges between them, formalized via domain and codomain functions. Graphs serve as the underlying combinatorial data for categories: every category has an underlying graph obtained by forgetting composition and identities. This forgetful functor from categories to graphs has a left adjoint, the free category functor.
