A diagram in a category $B$ can be defined concisely as a graph morphism from a shape graph $G$ into the underlying graph of $B$. By the universal property of the free category, such graph morphisms correspond bijectively to functors from the free category $C_G$ into $B$. This natural bijection unifies the two perspectives on diagrams -- as graph homomorphisms into the underlying graph, or as functors from the free category on the shape -- and expresses the free category functor $C$ as left adjoint to the forgetful functor $U$.
