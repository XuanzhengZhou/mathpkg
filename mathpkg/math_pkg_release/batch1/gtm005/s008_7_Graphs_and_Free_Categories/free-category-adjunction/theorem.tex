The bijection

$$\mathbf{Cat}(C_G, B) \cong \mathbf{Grph}(G, UB),$$

natural in $G$ and $B$, asserts that the free category functor $C: \mathbf{Grph} \to \mathbf{Cat}$ is left adjoint to the forgetful functor $U: \mathbf{Cat} \to \mathbf{Grph}$. Explicitly, for any graph $G$ and category $B$, graph morphisms $D: G \to UB$ correspond bijectively, via $D' \mapsto D = UD' \circ P$, to functors $D': C_G \to B$, where $P: G \to UC_G$ is the universal morphism of Theorem 1.
