The product over $O$ of two $O$-graphs $A$ and $B$ is the $O$-graph $A \times_O B$ whose arrows are composable pairs $\langle g, f \rangle$ with $\partial_0 g = \partial_1 f$. This product is associative (up to isomorphism) and has $O$ itself as a two-sided unit. This structure makes the collection of $O$-graphs into a monoidal category, and it allows the definition of a category as an $O$-graph equipped with a composition morphism $c: A \times_O A \to A$ and an identity morphism $i: O \to A$ satisfying commutative diagrams that express associativity and unit laws.
