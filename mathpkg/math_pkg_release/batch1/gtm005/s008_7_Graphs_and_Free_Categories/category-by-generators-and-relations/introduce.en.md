A category can be presented by specifying a graph $G$ of generators and a set $R$ of relations. The resulting category is the quotient $C_G/R$, where $C_G$ is the free category on $G$ and $R$ generates a congruence on $C_G$. For example, the category $\mathbf{3}$ with three objects $0, 1, 2$ and three non-identity arrows can be presented by generators $f: 0 \to 1$, $g: 1 \to 2$, $h: 0 \to 2$ with the single relation $h = g \circ f$. When the graph has a single object, this construction specializes to the presentation of a monoid by generators and relations.
