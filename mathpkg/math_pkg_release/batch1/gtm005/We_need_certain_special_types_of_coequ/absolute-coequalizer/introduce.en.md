An absolute coequalizer is a coequalizer that is preserved by every functor. For any functor $T : C \to X$ to an arbitrary category $X$, the image $T e$ must still coequalize $T \partial_0$ and $T \partial_1$. This is a strong condition: the coequalizer property must be equationally determined, not relying on any special properties of the ambient category. Split coequalizers are the canonical examples of absolute coequalizers.
