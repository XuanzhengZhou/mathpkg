If $G : A \to X$ satisfies hypothesis (iii) of Beck's theorem on the creation of coequalizers, then for any other adjunction $\langle F', G', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle : X \rightleftharpoons A'$ defining the same monad in $X$, there is a unique comparison functor $M : A' \to A$ with $M F' = F$ and $G M = G'$.
