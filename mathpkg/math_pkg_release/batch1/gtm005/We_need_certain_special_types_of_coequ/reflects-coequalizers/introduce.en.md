Reflecting coequalizers is the special case of reflecting colimits for the diagram shape $\bullet \rightrightarrows \bullet$. A functor reflects coequalizers if whenever the image of a fork under the functor is a coequalizer, the original fork must already have been a coequalizer. This property is crucial in Beck's Theorem and related monadicity results.
