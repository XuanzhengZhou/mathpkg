Let $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightleftharpoons A$ be an adjunction, $\langle T, \eta, \mu \rangle$ the monad which it defines in $X$, $X^T$ the Eilenberg--Moore category of $T$-algebras, and $\langle F^T, G^T, \eta^T, \varepsilon^T \rangle : X \rightleftharpoons X^T$ the corresponding adjunction. Then the following conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] The (unique) comparison functor $K : A \to X^T$ is an isomorphism;
\item[(ii)] The functor $G : A \to X$ creates coequalizers for those parallel pairs $f, g$ in $A$ for which $\langle G f, G g \rangle$ has an absolute coequalizer in $X$;
\item[(iii)] The functor $G : A \to X$ creates coequalizers for those parallel pairs $f, g$ in $A$ for which $\langle G f, G g \rangle$ has a split coequalizer in $X$.
\end{enumerate}
