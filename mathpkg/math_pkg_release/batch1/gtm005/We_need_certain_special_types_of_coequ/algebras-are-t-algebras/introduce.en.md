This theorem generalizes the fact that semigroups, monoids, and rings are monadic over $\mathbf{Set}$ to arbitrary varieties of algebras. For any set $\Omega$ of operators and any set $E$ of identities, the category of all $\langle \Omega, E \rangle$-algebras is isomorphic to the Eilenberg--Moore category $\mathbf{Set}^T$ of the corresponding monad $T$. The proof uses Beck's Theorem: the forgetful functor creates coequalizers for parallel pairs whose underlying functions have absolute coequalizers.
