A functor $G : A \to X$ reflects colimits if colimits in the image force colimits in the domain. More precisely, whenever a cone in $A$ becomes a colimiting cone after applying $G$, that cone must already have been colimiting in $A$. This property is dual to preservation and is essential in monadicity theorems, where the forgetful functor is often required to reflect certain colimits.
