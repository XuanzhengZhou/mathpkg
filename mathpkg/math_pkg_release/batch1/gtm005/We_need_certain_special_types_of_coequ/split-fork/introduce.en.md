A split fork is a fork equipped with two additional arrows $s : c \to b$ and $t : b \to a$ satisfying four equations: $e s = 1_c$, $\partial_0 t = 1_a$, $\partial_1 t = s e$, plus the fork condition $e \partial_0 = e \partial_1$. The splitting data guarantees that $e$ is a split epimorphism. Split forks are equationally defined, so they are preserved by all functors, making them the prime source of absolute coequalizers.
