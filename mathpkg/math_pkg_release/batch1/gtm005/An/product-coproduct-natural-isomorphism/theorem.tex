The identification of the product functor $a \times b$ with the coproduct functor $a \amalg b$, given by the biproduct $a \oplus b$, is a natural isomorphism. For any arrows $f_1 : a \rightarrow a'$ and $f_2 : b \rightarrow b'$, the arrow $f_1 \oplus f_2 : a \oplus b \rightarrow a' \oplus b'$ is defined by either of the equivalent conditions:

$$\begin{aligned}
p'_k (f_1 \oplus f_2) &= f_k p_k, \quad k = 1, 2, \\
(f_1 \oplus f_2) i_k &= i'_k f_k, \quad k = 1, 2,
\end{aligned}$$

where $p_k, i_k$ are the projections and injections of the first biproduct and $p'_k, i'_k$ are those of the second. These two definitions coincide.
