In an $\text{Ab}$-category $A$, a diagram

$$a \xrightarrow{i_1} c \xleftarrow{i_2} b, \qquad a \xleftarrow{p_1} c \xrightarrow{p_2} b$$

satisfying

$$p_1 i_1 = 1_a, \quad p_2 i_2 = 1_b, \quad i_1 p_1 + i_2 p_2 = 1_c$$

makes $c$ a biproduct of $a$ and $b$; that is, $c$ is simultaneously a product of $a$ and $b$ (with projections $p_1, p_2$) and a coproduct of $a$ and $b$ (with injections $i_1, i_2$). Conversely, given a product diagram $a \xleftarrow{p_1} a \times b \xrightarrow{p_2} b$, the universal property provides unique arrows $i_1 : a \rightarrow a \times b$ and $i_2 : b \rightarrow a \times b$ such that $p_1 i_1 = 1_a$, $p_2 i_2 = 1_b$, $p_1 i_2 = 0$, $p_2 i_1 = 0$, and $i_1 p_1 + i_2 p_2 = 1_{a \times b}$, making $a \times b$ a biproduct.
