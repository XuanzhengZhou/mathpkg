Adjunctions compose in a natural way. Given an adjunction from $X$ to $A$ and another from $A$ to $D$, their composite yields an adjunction from $X$ to $D$. The unit of the composite is built from the two units as $G\bar{\eta}F \cdot \eta$, and the counit is built from the two counits as $\bar{\varepsilon} \cdot \bar{F}\varepsilon\bar{G}$. This composition operation allows one to form the category Adj whose objects are categories and whose arrows are adjunctions.
