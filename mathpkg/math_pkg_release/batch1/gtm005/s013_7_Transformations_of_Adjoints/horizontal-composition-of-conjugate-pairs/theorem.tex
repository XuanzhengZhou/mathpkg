Given two conjugate pairs
$$\langle \sigma, \tau \rangle : \langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle \xrightarrow{\cdot} \langle F', G', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A,$$
$$\langle \bar{\sigma}, \bar{\tau} \rangle : \langle \bar{F}, \bar{G}, \bar{\eta}, \bar{\varepsilon} \rangle \xrightarrow{\cdot} \langle \bar{F}', \bar{G}', \bar{\eta}', \bar{\varepsilon}' \rangle : A \rightharpoonup D$$
the (horizontal) composite natural transformations $\bar{\sigma}\sigma$ and $\tau\bar{\tau}$ yield a conjugate pair
$$\bar{\sigma}\sigma : \bar{F}F \xrightarrow{\cdot} \bar{F}'F', \quad \tau\bar{\tau} : G'\bar{G}' \xrightarrow{\cdot} G\bar{G}$$
of natural transformations for the composite adjunctions.
