The category Adj organizes all adjunctions between small categories into a single structure. Its objects are small categories and its morphisms are adjunctions. Composition of morphisms is given by composing adjunctions as described in the previous theorem. Moreover, Adj is enriched over categories: each hom-set $\operatorname{Adj}(X, A)$ is itself a category whose objects are adjunctions and whose morphisms are conjugate pairs of natural transformations. This gives Adj the structure of a 2-category, formalizing the idea that adjunctions themselves admit a notion of morphism.
