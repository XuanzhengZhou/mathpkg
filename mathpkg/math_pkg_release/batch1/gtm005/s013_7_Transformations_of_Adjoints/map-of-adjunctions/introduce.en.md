A map of adjunctions is a morphism between two adjunctions. Given adjunctions $\langle F, G, \varphi, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$ and $\langle F', G', \varphi', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle : X' \rightharpoonup A'$, a map from the first to the second consists of a pair of functors $K : A \to A'$ and $L : X \to X'$ such that both squares of functors commute and the hom-set diagram involving the adjunction isomorphisms commutes for all objects. This notion allows comparison of different adjunctions and forms the basis for studying transformations of adjoints.
