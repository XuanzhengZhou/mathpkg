Given two adjunctions
$$\langle F, G, \varphi, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle, \langle F', G', \varphi', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$$
natural transformations $\sigma : F \xrightarrow{\cdot} F' : X \to A$ and $\tau : G' \xrightarrow{\cdot} G : A \to X$ are said to be \textbf{conjugate} (for the given adjunctions) when the diagram
$$\begin{array}{c}
A(F'x, a) \cong X(x, G'a) \\
(\sigma_x)^* = A(\sigma_x, a) \downarrow \quad \quad \downarrow X(x, \tau_a) \\
A(Fx, a) \cong X(x, Ga)
\end{array}$$
commutes for every pair of objects $x \in X, a \in A$.
