Theorem 3 of Mac Lane (Chapter IX, Section 7) identifies a parameterized end as an end internal to a functor category. Via the exponential adjunction $\mathrm{Cat}(P \times C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C, X) \cong \mathrm{Cat}(C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C, X^P)$, the trifunctor $T$ corresponds to a bifunctor $T^{\#}: C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \rightarrow X^P$. The theorem states that the end of $T^{\#}$ in $X^P$ exists and coincides with the functor $p \mapsto \int_c T(p, c, c)$ constructed in Theorem 2. The universal wedge $\omega^{\#}$ has components $(\omega^{\#}_c)_p = (\omega_p)_c$, connecting the pointwise ends to the internal end in the functor category.
