This theorem recasts pointwise limits as an instance of creation of limits. The discrete subcategory $|P|$ forgets all non-identity arrows of $P$, and the induced functor $i^*$ between functor categories creates limits. This provides a clean conceptual formulation of the fact that limits in $X^P$ are determined by pointwise limits in $X$.
