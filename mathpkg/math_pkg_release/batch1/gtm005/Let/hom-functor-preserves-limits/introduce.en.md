This theorem establishes that hom-functors are always continuous: for any object $c$ in a locally small category $C$, the covariant hom-functor $C(c, -)$ sends limits in $C$ to limits in $\mathbf{Set}$. Dually, the contravariant hom-functor $C(-, c)$ sends colimits in $C$ to limits in $\mathbf{Set}$. The proof uses the description of a cone as a point in a set of natural transformations, and implies the equation $\operatorname{Lim} C(c, F-) \cong C(c, \operatorname{Lim} F)$.
