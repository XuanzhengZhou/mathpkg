When a bifunctor $T : J \times P \to X$ has limits for each fixed parameter $p \in P$, those limits assemble naturally into a functor $P \to X$. This theorem shows that limits in a functor category $X^P$ are computed "pointwise": evaluating the limit of a diagram $S : J \to X^P$ at each object $p$ yields the limit of the evaluated diagram $E_p S : J \to X$. The result depends crucially on the adjunction between the product category and the functor category.
