Let $T : J \times P \rightarrow X$ be a bifunctor, and suppose for each value $p \in P$ of the "parameter" $p$ that $T(-, p) : J \rightarrow X$ has a limit. Then these limits for all $p$ form the object function $p \mapsto \operatorname{Lim}_j T(j, p)$ of a functor $P \rightarrow X$.

Equivalently, for the adjunct $S : J \rightarrow X^P$ of $T$ under the adjunction $\operatorname{Cat}(J \times P, X) \cong \operatorname{Cat}(J, X^P)$, the conclusion may be written

$$E_p(\operatorname{Lim} S) = \operatorname{Lim}(E_p S)$$

where $E_p : X^P \rightarrow X$ is the evaluation functor at $p \in P$. In a functor category, limits may be calculated pointwise (provided the pointwise limits exist).
