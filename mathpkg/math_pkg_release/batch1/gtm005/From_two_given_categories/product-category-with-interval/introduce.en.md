The interval category $\mathbf{2} = \{0 	o 1\}$ acts as a 'cylinder' for natural transformations: a functor $C 	imes \mathbf{2} 	o B$ is the same thing as a natural transformation between two functors $C 	o B$. This provides the geometric intuition of natural transformations as homotopies between functors.
