The product $C 	imes \mathbf{2}$ (where $\mathbf{2} = \{0 	o 1\}$) and the functor category $C^{\mathbf{2}}$ are distinct but related: natural transformations in a functor category correspond to functors from $C 	imes \mathbf{2}$ to the target category, providing the 'cylinder' interpretation of natural transformations.
