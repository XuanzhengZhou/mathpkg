Given functors $U: B \to B'$ and $V: C \to C'$, the \textbf{product functor}
$$U \times V: B \times C \to B' \times C'$$
is defined on objects and arrows by
$$(U \times V)\langle b, c \rangle = \langle Ub, Vc \rangle, \quad (U \times V)\langle f, g \rangle = \langle Uf, Vg \rangle.$$
Equivalently, $U \times V$ is the unique functor making the diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
B & \xleftarrow{P} & B \times C & \xrightarrow{Q} & C \\
{\scriptstyle U}\downarrow & & \downarrow{\scriptstyle U \times V} & & \downarrow{\scriptstyle V} \\
B' & \xleftarrow{P'} & B' \times C' & \xrightarrow{Q'} & C'
\end{array}$$
commute.
