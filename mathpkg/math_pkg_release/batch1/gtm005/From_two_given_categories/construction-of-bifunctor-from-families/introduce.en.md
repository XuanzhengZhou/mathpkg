This theorem provides a practical way to construct bifunctors from one-variable functors. Given a family of functors $L_c: B \to D$ parameterized by objects of $C$ and a family $M_b: C \to D$ parameterized by objects of $B$, the interchange condition on the resulting commutative diagram is both necessary and sufficient for these families to assemble into a bifunctor $S: B \times C \to D$.
