By using the opposite category, a bifunctor can be contravariant in one argument and covariant in another. The notation $B^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to D$ captures this mixed variance elegantly. The most important example is the hom-functor $\hom: C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to \mathbf{Set}$, which is contravariant in the first argument and covariant in the second.
