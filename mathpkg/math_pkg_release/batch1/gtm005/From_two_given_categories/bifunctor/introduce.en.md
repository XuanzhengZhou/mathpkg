A bifunctor is simply a functor whose domain is a product category $B \times C$. It takes two arguments -- one from $B$ and one from $C$ -- and produces values in $D$. Bifunctors occur frequently: the Cartesian product of sets is a bifunctor $\mathbf{Set} \times \mathbf{Set} \to \mathbf{Set}$, and the tensor product in a monoidal category is a bifunctor.
