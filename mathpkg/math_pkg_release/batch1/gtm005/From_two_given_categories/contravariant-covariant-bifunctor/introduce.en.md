A bifunctor that is contravariant in one argument and covariant in the other (a 'profunctor-like' bifunctor) is a functor from $C^{\mathrm{op}} 	imes D$ to $E$. The hom-functor $\hom_C: C^{\mathrm{op}} 	imes C 	o \mathbf{Set}$ is the canonical example. Such mixed-variance bifunctors are the basis for the calculus of ends and coends.
