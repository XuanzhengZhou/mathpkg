An invertible arrow, also called an isomorphism, is the categorical generalization of a bijective function or a group isomorphism. The definition uses only the composition and identity arrows of the category, making it purely arrow-theoretic. An isomorphism expresses that two objects are essentially the same within the category. The uniqueness of the inverse follows from the standard argument using associativity, and the composition of isomorphisms is again an isomorphism.
