An equivalence of categories is the correct notion of sameness for categories. Unlike isomorphism of categories, which requires the composite functors to be literally equal to the identity functors, an equivalence allows them to be only naturally isomorphic to the identity. This weaker condition captures all category-theoretic properties while permitting flexibility -- for instance, the category of finite sets is equivalent (but not isomorphic) to the category of finite ordinals. The pair $(S,T)$ together with the two natural isomorphisms constitutes an adjoint equivalence.
