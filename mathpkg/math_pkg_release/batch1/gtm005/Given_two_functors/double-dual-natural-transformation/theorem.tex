Let $D(G) = \hom(G, \mathbf{R}/\mathbf{Z})$ denote the character group of an abelian group $G$, where $\mathbf{R}/\mathbf{Z}$ is the additive group of real numbers modulo $1$. Then the twice-iterated character group $G \mapsto D(DG)$ and the identity $I(G) = G$ are both functors $\text{Ab} \rightarrow \text{Ab}$. For each group $G$ there is a homomorphism
$$
\tau_G: G \rightarrow D(DG)
$$
obtained by assigning to each $g \in G$ the function $\tau_G g: DG \rightarrow \mathbf{R}/\mathbf{Z}$ given for any character $t \in DG$ by $t \mapsto t(g)$; thus $(\tau_G g)t = t(g)$. Then $\tau$ is a natural transformation $\tau: I \mathbin{\raisebox{0.5pt}{\hspace{0.5pt}\tikz\draw[->](0,0)--(0.5,0);\hspace{0.5pt}}} D \circ D$.
