The evaluation map $\tau_G: G \to D(DG)$ sending each group element to evaluation-at-that-element on the character group is a canonical natural transformation from the identity functor to the double-dual functor on the category of abelian groups. This is the prototype of many "double dual" constructions in mathematics -- for instance, the embedding of a vector space into its double dual. The character group functor $D$ is contravariant, so the double dual $D \circ D$ is covariant, and $\tau$ provides a comparison between a group and its double character group.
