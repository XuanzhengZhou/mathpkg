An initial object is the categorical dual of a terminal object: it admits exactly one arrow to every other object. In $\text{Set}$, the empty set is initial; in $\text{Grp}$, the trivial group is initial. Like terminal objects, initial objects are unique up to unique isomorphism. Together, initial and terminal objects provide the simplest examples of limits and colimits.
