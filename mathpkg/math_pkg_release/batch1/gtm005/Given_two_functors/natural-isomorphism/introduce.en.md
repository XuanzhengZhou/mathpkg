A natural isomorphism is a natural transformation whose every component is an isomorphism in the target category. It provides the correct notion of "sameness" between functors, analogous to how an isomorphism of objects expresses sameness within a single category. The invertibility condition guarantees that the inverse arrows also assemble into a natural transformation, yielding the symmetric relation $\tau^{-1}: T \cong S$. Natural isomorphisms are essential for defining equivalences of categories.
