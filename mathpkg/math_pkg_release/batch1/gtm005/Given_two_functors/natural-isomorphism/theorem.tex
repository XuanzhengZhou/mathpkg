A natural transformation $\tau: S \mathbin{\raisebox{0.5pt}{\hspace{0.5pt}\tikz\draw[->](0,0)--(0.5,0);\hspace{0.5pt}}} T$ with every component $\tau c$ invertible in the target category $B$ is called a \text{natural equivalence} or a \text{natural isomorphism}. In symbols, $\tau: S \cong T$. In this case, the inverses $(\tau c)^{-1}$ in $B$ are the components of a natural isomorphism $\tau^{-1}: T \mathbin{\raisebox{0.5pt}{\hspace{0.5pt}\tikz\draw[->](0,0)--(0.5,0);\hspace{0.5pt}}} S$.
