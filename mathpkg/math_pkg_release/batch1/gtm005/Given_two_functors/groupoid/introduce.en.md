A groupoid is a category where every morphism has an inverse. Groupoids generalize both groups (a group is a groupoid with one object) and equivalence relations. The fundamental groupoid $\pi(X)$ of a topological space encodes the homotopy information of all paths between points, generalizing the fundamental group (which fixes a base point). Groupoids are important in homotopy theory, algebraic geometry, and the theory of stacks.
