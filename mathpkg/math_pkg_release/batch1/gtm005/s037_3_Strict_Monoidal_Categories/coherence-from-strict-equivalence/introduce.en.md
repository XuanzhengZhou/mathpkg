This theorem states that if a monoidal category $M$ is monoidally equivalent to a strict monoidal category $S$ via a strong monoidal functor, then the coherence theorem for associativity holds in $M$. This means that any two tensor words formed from the same ordered list of objects, but with different parenthesizations, are connected by a unique canonical isomorphism built from the associativity isomorphisms $\alpha$. The proof uses the strictness of $S$ -- where all associativity maps are identities -- and the strong monoidal functor $G$ to transfer uniqueness back to $M$.
