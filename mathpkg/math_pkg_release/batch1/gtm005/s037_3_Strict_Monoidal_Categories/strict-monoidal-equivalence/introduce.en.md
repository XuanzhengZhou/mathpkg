This theorem shows that every monoidal category is monoidally equivalent to a strict monoidal category, where the associativity and unit isomorphisms are all identities. The proof constructs a strict monoidal category $S$ as the free monoid on the objects of $M$, with arrows between strings defined via the corresponding objects in $M$. The strong monoidal functors $F: S \rightarrow M$ and $G: M \rightarrow S$ together provide the categorical equivalence, with $FG$ equal to the identity on $M$ and $GF$ naturally isomorphic to the identity on $S$.
