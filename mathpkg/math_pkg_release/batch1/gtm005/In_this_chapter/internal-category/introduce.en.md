An internal category generalizes the notion of a small category by replacing the ambient category of sets with an arbitrary finitely complete category $\mathcal{E}$. Instead of a set of objects and a set of arrows, one works with objects $C_0$ and $C_1$ of $\mathcal{E}$, and the structure maps (identity, domain, codomain, composition) become morphisms in $\mathcal{E}$. The usual category axioms — unit laws and associativity — are expressed via commutative diagrams involving pullbacks. This construction yields ordinary small categories when $\mathcal{E} = \mathbf{Sets}$, and reveals an elegant interchange principle: the category of $X$-objects in $Y$-objects is also the category of $Y$-objects in $X$-objects.
