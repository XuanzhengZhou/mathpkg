An internal functor is the natural notion of a morphism between internal categories in the same ambient category $\mathcal{E}$. It consists of a pair of morphisms $f_0, f_1$ in $\mathcal{E}$ that respect the category structure: they commute with domain, codomain, identity, and composition. In the case $\mathcal{E} = \mathbf{Sets}$, this reduces to the usual definition of a functor between small categories. Notably, internal functors go from one internal category to another within $\mathcal{E}$ — there is no internal category corresponding to the universe $\mathcal{E}$ itself, which necessitates a separate treatment of functors into $\mathcal{E}$ (internal diagrams).
