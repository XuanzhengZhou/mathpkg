A morphism of internal diagrams $\phi : H \to K$ is the analogue of a natural transformation in the internal category framework. It is simply a morphism in the ambient category $\mathcal{E}$ that respects both the projection structure (sending fibers to fibers) and the action structure (commuting with composition). In the case $\mathcal{E} = \mathbf{Set}$, this definition precisely recovers the notion of a natural transformation between set-valued functors. This demonstrates that the entire 2-categorical structure of functor categories can be internalized when the ambient category is sufficiently rich.
