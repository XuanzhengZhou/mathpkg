**Proof.** If $\gamma \in \sup(A)$ then $(\exists \alpha)[\gamma \in \alpha \land \alpha \in A]$. But $\alpha \in A \rightarrow (\exists \beta)[\beta \in B \land \alpha \le \beta]$, i.e., $(\exists \beta)[\gamma \in \beta \land \beta \in B]$. Therefore $\gamma \in \sup(B)$ and hence $\sup(A) \le \sup(B)$.
