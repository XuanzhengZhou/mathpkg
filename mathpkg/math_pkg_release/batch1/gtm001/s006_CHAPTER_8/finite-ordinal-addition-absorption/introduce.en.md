For any natural number $n \in \omega$ and any infinite ordinal $\alpha \ge \omega$, we have $n + \alpha = \alpha$. Finite ordinals are absorbed on the left when added to an infinite ordinal. This shows that ordinal addition is not commutative: $1 + \omega = \omega \neq \omega + 1$.
