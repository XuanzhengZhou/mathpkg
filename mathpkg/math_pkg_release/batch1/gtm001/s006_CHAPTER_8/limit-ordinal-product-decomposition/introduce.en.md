If $\beta$ is a limit ordinal and $\gamma$ is less than the product $\alpha \cdot \beta$, then $\gamma$ is already bounded by some partial product $\alpha \cdot \delta$ with $\delta < \beta$. This follows directly from the definition of multiplication for limit ordinals: $\alpha \cdot \beta = \bigcup_{\delta < \beta} \alpha \cdot \delta$.
