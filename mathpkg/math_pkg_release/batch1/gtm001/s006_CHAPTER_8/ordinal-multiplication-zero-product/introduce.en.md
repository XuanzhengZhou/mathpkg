The product of two ordinals is zero if and only if at least one of the factors is zero. In other words, the ordinal numbers have no zero divisors. This follows from the fact that if both $\alpha \ge 1$ and $\beta \ge 1$, then $\alpha \cdot \beta \ge 1 \cdot 1 = 1 > 0$.
