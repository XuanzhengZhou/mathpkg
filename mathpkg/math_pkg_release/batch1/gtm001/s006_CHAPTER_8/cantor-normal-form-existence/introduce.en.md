Every nonzero ordinal $\beta$ can be uniquely expressed in Cantor Normal Form: a finite sum $\beta = \omega^{\beta_0} \cdot \gamma_0 + \cdots + \omega^{\beta_n} \cdot \gamma_n$ where the exponents are strictly decreasing ($\beta_0 > \beta_1 > \cdots > \beta_n$) and the coefficients $\gamma_i$ are nonzero natural numbers. This representation is fundamental for analyzing the structure of countable ordinals.
