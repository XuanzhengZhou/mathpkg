If $\alpha$ is nonzero and $\beta$ is a limit ordinal, then the product $\alpha \cdot \beta$ is also a limit ordinal. This is a key structural property showing that multiplication by a nonzero ordinal preserves limit ordinals. The proof uses Proposition 8.23 and a contradiction argument.
