**Proof.** Since $\alpha \ge 0$, it follows from Propositions 8.7 and the fact that $\alpha + \beta \ge \beta$ (which follows from Proposition 8.4 with $\gamma = \alpha$ and the fact that $\alpha + 0 = \alpha$) that $\alpha + \beta \ge \beta$. Thus for $\alpha \le \beta$, there exists an ordinal $\gamma \le \beta$ such that $\alpha + \gamma = \beta$. Uniqueness follows from the left cancellation law (Corollary 8.5): if $\alpha + \gamma_1 = \beta = \alpha + \gamma_2$, then $\gamma_1 = \gamma_2$.
