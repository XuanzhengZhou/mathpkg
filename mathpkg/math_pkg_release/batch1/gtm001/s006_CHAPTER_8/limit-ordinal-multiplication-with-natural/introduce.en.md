For a limit ordinal $\gamma$ and natural numbers $m \neq 0, n$, we have $m \cdot (\gamma + n) = \gamma + m \cdot n$. In particular, for $\gamma = \omega$ we have $m \cdot \omega = \omega$ (since $m \cdot \omega = \bigcup_{k < \omega} m \cdot k = \omega$). The proof uses induction on $\gamma + n$ and the division algorithm for the base case.
