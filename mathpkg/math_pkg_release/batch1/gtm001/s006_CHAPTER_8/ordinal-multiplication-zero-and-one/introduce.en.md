In ordinal multiplication, $0$ is an absorbing element and $1$ is the identity element. Specifically, $0 \cdot \alpha = \alpha \cdot 0 = 0$ for all $\alpha$, and $1 \cdot \alpha = \alpha \cdot 1 = \alpha$ for all $\alpha$. Both parts are proved by transfinite induction.
