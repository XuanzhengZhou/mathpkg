For any ordinals $\alpha$ and $\beta > 0$, there exist unique ordinals $\gamma$ (the quotient) and $\delta$ (the remainder) such that $\alpha = \beta \cdot \gamma + \delta$ with $\delta < \beta$. This is the ordinal analog of the Euclidean division algorithm for natural numbers.
