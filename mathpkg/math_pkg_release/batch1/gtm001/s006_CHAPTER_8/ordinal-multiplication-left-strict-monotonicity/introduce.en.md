If $\alpha < \beta$ and $\gamma > 0$, then multiplying both sides by $\gamma$ on the left preserves the strict inequality: $\gamma \cdot \alpha < \gamma \cdot \beta$. The factor must be nonzero for the strict inequality to hold. The proof uses transfinite induction on $\beta$.
