**Proof.** $x \in F''(b \cap \eta)$ gives $\mathrm{Od}'x \in \eta$ and $\mathrm{Od}'x \in b$ (Lemma 2). $y \in x \cap F''b$ gives $\mathrm{Od}'y < \mathrm{Od}'x$ and $\mathrm{Od}'y \in b$. Hence $\mathrm{Od}'y \in b \cap \eta$, so $y \in F''(b \cap \eta)$.
