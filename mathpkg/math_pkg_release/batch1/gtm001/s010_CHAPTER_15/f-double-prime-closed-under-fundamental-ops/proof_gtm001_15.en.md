**Proof.** If $x, y \in F''b$ then $(\exists \alpha, \beta \in b)[x = F' \alpha \land y = F' \beta]$. Hence $\mathcal{F}_n(x, y) = \mathcal{F}_n(F' \alpha, F' \beta) = F' J' \langle \alpha, \beta, n \rangle$. Since $b$ is closed w.r.t. $J$, $J' \langle \alpha, \beta, n \rangle \in b$, so $\mathcal{F}_n(x, y) \in F''b$.
