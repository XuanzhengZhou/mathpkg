**Proof.** If $J' \langle \alpha, \beta, m \rangle = J' \langle \gamma, \delta, n \rangle$ then $9 \cdot J'_0 \langle \alpha, \beta \rangle + m = 9 \cdot J'_0 \langle \gamma, \delta \rangle + n$. By uniqueness in Proposition 8.27, $J'_0 \langle \alpha, \beta \rangle = J'_0 \langle \gamma, \delta \rangle$ and $m = n$. Since $J_0$ is one-to-one, $\alpha = \gamma$ and $\beta = \delta$, so $J$ is one-to-one. For surjectivity, by Proposition 8.27: $(\forall \gamma)(\exists \delta)(\exists m < 9)[\gamma = 9 \cdot \delta + m]$. Since $J_0$ is onto, $(\exists \alpha, \beta)[\delta = J'_0 \langle \alpha, \beta \rangle]$, so $\gamma = J' \langle \alpha, \beta, m \rangle$.
