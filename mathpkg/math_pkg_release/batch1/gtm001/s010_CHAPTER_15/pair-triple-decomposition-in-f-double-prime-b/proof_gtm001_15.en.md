**Proof.** (1) Since $\{x, y\} \neq 0$, by Lemma 3: $\{x, y\} \cap F''b \neq 0$, so $x \in F''b \lor y \in F''b$. If $x \in F''b$ and $x \neq y$, then $\{x\} \in F''b$ (Lemma 1) and $\{y\} = \{x, y\} - \{x\} \in F''b$. By Lemma 3, $y \in F''b$. (2) and (3) follow from (1) and definitions.
