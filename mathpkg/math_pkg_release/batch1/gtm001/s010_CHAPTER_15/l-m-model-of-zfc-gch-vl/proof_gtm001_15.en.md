**Proof.** (1) $L^M$ is nonempty ($0 \in L^M$) and transitive (if $y \in x \in L^M$, then $y = F'\beta$ for some $\beta < \alpha \in M$, so $\beta \in M$ and $y \in L^M$). Since $L \models \text{ZF+AC+GCH+}V=L$, for any axiom $\varphi$, $\varphi^L$ is a theorem of ZF. Since $M \models \text{ZF}$, $(\varphi^L)^M$ holds. By Theorem 15.48, $\varphi^{L^M}$ holds, so $L^M \models \varphi$. By Theorem 15.27, $\mathrm{On}^L = \mathrm{On}$, and relativizing gives $\mathrm{On}^{L^M} = \mathrm{On}^M$.
