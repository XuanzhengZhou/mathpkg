**Proof.** Let $X \subseteq \eta$ be a subalgebra of $\mathcal{P}_<^\eta$, $\pi: \xi \rightarrow X$ the collapsing map. Let $Z = \{\boldsymbol{\alpha} \mid J(\boldsymbol{\alpha}) \in X\}$. Then $Z \subseteq X^\omega$, and $Z$ is an initial segment of $X^\omega$. The collapsing map preserves the operations because the codes in the pairing machine preserve the relevant structure.
