Corollary 5.19 states that no set can be an element of itself: $a \notin a$. This is the special case $n = 1$ of Proposition 5.18 (no membership cycles), and it follows directly from the Axiom of Regularity. This result eliminates the possibility of a Russell-style paradox arising from self-membership.
