The union of a class $A$, denoted $\cup(A)$, is the class of all $x$ such that $x$ belongs to some element $y$ of $A$. This generalizes the binary union operation to arbitrary classes. The Axiom of Unions (Axiom 3) postulates that the union of a set is a set.
