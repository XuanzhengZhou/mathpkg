The unordered $n$-tuple $\{a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n\}$ generalizes the unordered pair to $n$ elements. It is the class of all $x$ such that $x$ equals one of the $a_i$. That unordered $n$-tuples are sets can be proved by induction using properties of set union.
