The proposition **If $R$ Wfr $A$, if $K = \{f | (\exists y \subseteq A)[\text{Cl}(R^{-1}, y) \wedge f \mathcal{F}_n y \wedge (\forall x \in y)[f'x = G'(f \upharpoonright (R^{-1})'{x})]\}$ and if $F = \cup(K)$, then** (Proposition 9.7.) is a fundamental notion in axiomatic set theory, introduced in Chapter 9 of Takeuti and Zaring's *Introduction to Axiomatic Set Theory* (2nd ed., 1982). It forms part of the formal development of Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory with classes (GB or ZF).
