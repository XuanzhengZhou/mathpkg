The ordinal $\text{Ord}(\varphi)$ assigns a well-ordering to limited formulas in the forcing language, enabling definitions and proofs by induction on formula complexity. It combines three components with decreasing weight: the grade $g(\varphi)$ (multiplied by $\omega^2$), the count of membership statements at the top grade (multiplied by $\omega$), and the count of logical symbols not inside terms. This ensures that when a formula is decomposed, its constituents have strictly smaller ordinals.
