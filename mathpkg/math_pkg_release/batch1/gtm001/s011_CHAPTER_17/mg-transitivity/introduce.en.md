This proposition establishes two fundamental properties of the generic extension $M[G]$. First, $M[G]$ is a transitive set, meaning that elements of elements of $M[G]$ are themselves in $M[G]$. Second, forcing does not add new ordinals: the ordinals of $M[G]$ are exactly the ordinals of the ground model $M$. This is crucial because it ensures that cardinalities and other ordinal-based properties are preserved when passing to the generic extension.
