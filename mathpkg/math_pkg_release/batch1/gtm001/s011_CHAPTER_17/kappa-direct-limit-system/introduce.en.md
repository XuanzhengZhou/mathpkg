A $\kappa$-direct limit system is a directed diagram of acceptable triples connected by acceptable maps, with each $\delta_i$ below $\kappa$ and the $\alpha_i$ cofinal in $\kappa$. The sup condition on $\pi_{ij}$ ensures a continuity property. When the system is well-founded, its direct limit $\delta_\infty$ satisfies $\delta_\infty \geq \kappa$ and the limit maps $\pi_{i\infty}$ satisfy an analogous sup condition.
