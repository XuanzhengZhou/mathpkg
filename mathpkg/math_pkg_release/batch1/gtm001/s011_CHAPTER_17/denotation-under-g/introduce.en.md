The denotation function $D^G$ interprets the constant terms (forcing names) of the language $\mathcal{L}(M, \mathbf{G})$ using a generic filter $G$. For an abstraction term $\hat{x}_i^\alpha \varphi(x_i)$, its denotation is the set of all $D^G(t)$ for terms $t$ of rank less than $\alpha$ that satisfy $\varphi$. Constants $\mathbf{k}$ denote the corresponding element $k$ of $M$, while $\mathbf{G}$ denotes the generic filter $G$ itself. The generic extension $M[G]$ is then defined as the collection of all such denotations.
