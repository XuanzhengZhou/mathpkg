Let $h: L_{\bar{\kappa}} \rightarrow L_\kappa$ be an elementary embedding such that $\mathcal{W}(h) = Z$. For any $\lambda \in S$ and $\lambda$-direct limit system $\Pi \subseteq Z$ that is not well-founded, there exist sequences $\langle i_n: n \in \omega \rangle$ and $\langle \sigma_n: n \in \omega \rangle$ witnessing the failure of well-foundedness. We construct $Z'$ by closing $Z$ under witnesses for all such failures. The key step is showing that if $\Pi$ is not well-founded then it is not $Z'$-well-founded for a suitably chosen $Z'$, which follows by tracing the descending chain through the connecting maps.
