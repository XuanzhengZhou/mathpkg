This lemma establishes that generic filters exist. Given any condition $p_0$ in the forcing poset $P$, one can construct a $\mathcal{P}$-generic set $G$ containing $p_0$. The proof crucially uses the countability of the ground model $M$: since $M$ is countable, the collection of all dense subsets of $P$ that lie in $M$ is countable, allowing a diagonal construction of $G$ by successively meeting each dense set.
