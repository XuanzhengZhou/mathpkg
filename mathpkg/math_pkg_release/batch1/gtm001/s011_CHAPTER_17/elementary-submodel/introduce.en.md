An elementary submodel $A \prec B$ is a substructure that is indistinguishable from the larger structure by first-order properties: any first-order sentence with parameters from $A$ holds in $A$ if and only if it holds in $B$. The inclusion map from $A$ to $B$ is then an elementary embedding. This notion is fundamental in model theory and is used extensively in the Silver machine constructions.
