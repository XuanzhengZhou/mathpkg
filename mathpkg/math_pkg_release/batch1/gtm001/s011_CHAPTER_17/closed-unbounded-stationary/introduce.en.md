This definition introduces three fundamental notions in infinite combinatorics on limit ordinals. A subset $C$ of $\kappa$ is closed if it contains the supremum of any of its subsets whose supremum is below $\kappa$, and unbounded if its supremum equals $\kappa$. A stationary set $S$ is one that intersects every closed unbounded (club) subset of $\kappa$, making it a "large" subset in the sense of the club filter on $\kappa$.
