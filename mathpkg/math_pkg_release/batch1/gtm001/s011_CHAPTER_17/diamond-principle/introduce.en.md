The diamond principle $\diamond(\kappa)$ asserts the existence of a $\diamond(\kappa)$-sequence. It is a combinatorial prediction principle stating that there exists a sequence of subsets that correctly guesses initial segments of every subset of $\kappa$ on a stationary set of ordinals. This principle is a consequence of $V = L$ and has numerous applications in set-theoretic topology and infinite combinatorics.
