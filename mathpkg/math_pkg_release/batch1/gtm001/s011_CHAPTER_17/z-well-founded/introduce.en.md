The notion of $Z$-well-foundedness relativizes the usual well-foundedness of a direct limit system to the set $Z$. While ordinary well-foundedness forbids any infinite descending chain of ordinals under the connecting maps, $Z$-well-foundedness only forbids descending chains where the witnessing ordinals $\sigma_n$ lie in $Z$. This relativization is crucial for the transfer arguments using elementary embeddings: if $h: L_{\bar{\kappa}} \rightarrow L_\kappa$ with $Z = \mathcal{W}(h)$ and $\Pi = h(\bar{\Pi})$, then $\bar{\Pi}$ is well-founded iff $\Pi$ is $Z$-well-founded.
