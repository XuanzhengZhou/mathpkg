The Truth Lemma establishes the fundamental connection between the syntactic forcing relation and semantic truth in the generic extension. It states that for any first-order formula $\varphi$ (without bounded quantifiers or abstraction terms) and any forcing names $t_1, \ldots, t_n$, the limited sentence $\varphi(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$ holds under $D^G$ exactly when the formula $\varphi$ is true in $M[G]$ of the corresponding denotations. This lemma is central to proving that $M[G]$ satisfies the axioms of ZF.
