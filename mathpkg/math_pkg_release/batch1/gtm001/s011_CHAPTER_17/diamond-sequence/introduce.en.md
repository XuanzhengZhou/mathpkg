A $\diamond(\kappa)$-sequence is a guessing sequence of length $\kappa$ where each $S_\alpha$ is a subset of $\alpha$. The defining property is that for any subset $X$ of $\kappa$, the set of ordinals $\alpha$ where the sequence correctly predicts the initial segment $X \cap \alpha$ is stationary in $\kappa$. Read as "diamond kappa," this principle plays a central role in infinite combinatorics and the theory of constructibility.
