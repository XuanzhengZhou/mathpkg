Two transitive sets are elementarily equivalent if they satisfy exactly the same first-order sentences in the language of set theory with membership as the only relation symbol. This means the structures $\langle a, \varepsilon \rangle$ and $\langle b, \varepsilon \rangle$ are indistinguishable by any first-order property expressible in the language.
