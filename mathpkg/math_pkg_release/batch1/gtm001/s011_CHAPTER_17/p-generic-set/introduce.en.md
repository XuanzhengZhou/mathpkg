A $\mathcal{P}$-generic set $G$ (also called a generic filter) is a subset of a partial order $P$ that lies outside the ground model $M$ and satisfies three conditions: it is directed (any two conditions have a common strengthening), it is upward closed, and it meets every dense subset of $P$ that belongs to $M$. Generic filters are the fundamental tool in Cohen's forcing method for proving independence results in set theory.
