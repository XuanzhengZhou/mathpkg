** If $\alpha$ is finite, then $(\exists n)[n \simeq \alpha]$. But by Proposition 10.22, this implies that $\alpha = n$ and so $\alpha \in \omega$.

Conversely if $\alpha \in \omega$, then since $\alpha \simeq \alpha$ it follows that $\alpha$ is finite.
