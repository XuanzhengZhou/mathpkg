** (1) If $b \neq 0$, then $(\exists y)[y \in b]$. Then the function $f$ defined by

$$f'x = \langle x, y \rangle, \quad x \in a$$

maps $a$ one-to-one into $a \times b$. Consequently $a \simeq f''a \subseteq a \times b$. Then by *Proposition 10.13,

$$\bar{a} \leq \bar{a} \times b.$$

(2) If $\bar{b} \leq \bar{c}$, then $(\exists f)[f: b \xrightarrow{1-1} c]$. Let $g$ be defined by

$$g' \langle x, y \rangle = \langle x, f'y \rangle, \quad \langle x, y \rangle

If $f: a \xrightarrow{\text{onto}} b$, then from *Proposition 10.34*

$$\bar{b} = \overline{f''a} \leq \bar{a}.$$

Furthermore, if $a \neq 0$, then $b \neq 0$ and hence $\bar{b} > 0$.

Conversely if $0 < \bar{b} \leq \bar{a}$, then $a \neq 0$ and $b \neq 0$. Moreover we have $b \simeq \bar{b} \leq \bar{a} \simeq a$, and so $(\exists g)[g: b \xrightarrow{1-1} a]$. Since $b \neq 0, (\exists y)[y \in b]$. Let $f$ be defined by

$$f'x = (g^{-1})'x, \quad x \in g''b$$

$$= y, \quad x \in a - g''b.$$

Then $f: a \xrightarrow{\text{onto}} b.$
