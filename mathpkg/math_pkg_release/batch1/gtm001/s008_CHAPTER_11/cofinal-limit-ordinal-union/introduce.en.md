If a limit ordinal $\alpha$ is cofinal with $\beta$, then $\alpha$ can be expressed as the union of $\beta$ many sets, each bounded in $\alpha$. A limit ordinal $\alpha$ is cofinal with $\beta$ exactly when $\alpha$ can be reached as the union of $\beta$ bounded subsets.
