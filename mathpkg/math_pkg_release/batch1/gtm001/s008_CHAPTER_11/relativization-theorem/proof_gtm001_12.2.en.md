By induction on the length of a proof. For logical axioms, one verifies that their relativizations are tautologies when restricted to $A$. For nonlogical axioms, the hypothesis that $A$ satisfies them gives $A \models \eta_k$, hence $\eta_k^A$ holds. For modus ponens and generalization, the relativization commutes with the inference rules, so the relativized proof is a valid proof of $\varphi^A$ (with the hypothesis that free variables belong to $A$).
