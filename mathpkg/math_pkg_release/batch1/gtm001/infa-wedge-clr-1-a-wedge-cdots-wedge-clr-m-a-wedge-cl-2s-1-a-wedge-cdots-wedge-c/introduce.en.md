The proposition **Inf($a$) $\wedge$ Cl($R_1$, $A$) $\wedge \cdots \wedge$ Cl($R_m$, $A$) $\wedge$ Cl$_2$($S_1$, $A$) $\wedge \cdots \wedge$ Cl$_2$($S_n$, $A$) $\wedge$ Un($R_1$) $\wedge \cdots \wedge$ Un($R_m$) $\wedge$ Un($S_1$) $\wedge \cdots \wedge$ Un($S_n$) $\wedge a \subset A$ then there exists a set $b$ such that $a \subseteq b \subseteq A$, $\bar{a} = \bar{b}$, and** (Proposition 11.32.) is a fundamental notion in axiomatic set theory, introduced in Chapter 11 of Takeuti and Zaring's *Introduction to Axiomatic Set Theory* (2nd ed., 1982). It forms part of the formal development of Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory with classes (GB or ZF).
