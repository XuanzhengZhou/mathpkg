This corollary of Fuhrken's theorem establishes that Q-languages satisfy a restricted compactness property. Under the $(\exp \aleph_0)^+$-interpretation, any countable set of Q-sentences where every finite subset has a model also has a model. The $\aleph_1$-interpretation is particularly significant: Keisler gave an elegant axiom system, and Vaught showed the validities are recursively enumerable, making it one of the most interesting extensions of first-order logic.
