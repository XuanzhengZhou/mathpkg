Formulas in a type language extend the grammar of first-order logic to a typed higher-order setting. Atomic formulas are either equalities between terms of the same type or relational applications where a relation symbol of compound type is applied to terms of matching types. The propositional connectives $\neg$, $\lor$, $\land$ and the universal quantifier $\forall$ operate as in first-order logic, but the quantifier ranges over variables of arbitrary types, enabling quantification over relations and higher-type objects. This generality is the syntactic foundation for higher-order and type-theoretic logics.
