In a type-theoretic extension of first-order logic, terms are syntactic expressions that denote objects of specific types. The type $o$ is the base type of individuals, and operation symbols with compound types $(\sigma_0, \ldots, \sigma_m)$ act as function symbols taking $m$ arguments of types $\sigma_0, \ldots, \sigma_{m-1}$ and producing a result of type $\sigma_m$. Relation symbols of type $\sigma \neq o$ double as terms of type $\sigma$, enabling a unified treatment of relations and functions within the type hierarchy.
