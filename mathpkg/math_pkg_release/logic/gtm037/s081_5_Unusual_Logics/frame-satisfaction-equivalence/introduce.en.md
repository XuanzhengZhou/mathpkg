Lemma 30.7 establishes that frame satisfaction $(\mathfrak{A}, G) \models \varphi[x]$ is equivalent to ordinary first-order satisfaction in the extended structure: $\mathfrak{A}_G^* \models \varphi^*[x]$. Here $\varphi^*$ is the translation of the higher-order formula $\varphi$ into the first-order language $\mathcal{L}''$. This reduction is the key step in proving completeness for higher-order logic with frame semantics: since $\mathfrak{A}_G^*$ is an ordinary first-order structure, the completeness theorem for first-order logic can be applied, yielding a completeness theorem for the type-theoretic logic under frame semantics.
