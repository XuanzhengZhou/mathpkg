Proposition 8.33 establishes the fundamental duality laws for sentential logic. Parts (i)-(iii) show how the duality operator interacts with conjunction, disjunction, and double duals. Part (iv) relates the valuations of dual sentences under related models. Parts (v)-(viii) assert that duality preserves and reverses various provability relations, including that $\varphi$ is a theorem iff the negation of its dual is a theorem, and that implication is contravariant under duality.
