Over a fixed theory $\Gamma$, two $m$-variable formulas are equivalent if $\Gamma$ proves their biconditional, and the resulting equivalence classes form the Lindenbaum--Tarski Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}_m^\Gamma$. An $m$-atomic formula is one whose equivalence class is an atom in this algebra: it is consistent with $\Gamma$ and decides every other $m$-variable formula up to $\Gamma$-equivalence. An $m$-atomless formula is one whose equivalence class is a nonzero element with no atom below it. These notions provide the Boolean-algebraic scaffolding for the omitting types theorem and the construction of atomic models.
