Theorem 7.18 establishes that ISOL, the collection of isolic recursive equivalence types, is closed under addition and multiplication and satisfies cancellation laws. This makes $(\text{ISOL}, +, \cdot)$ a cancellation semiring, in contrast to the full structure $(\text{RET}, +, \cdot)$ which lacks these properties. The natural numbers $(\omega, +, \cdot)$ embed isomorphically into ISOL via $n \mapsto \bar{n}$.
