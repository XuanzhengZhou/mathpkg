In the poset $(D, \leq)$ of Turing degrees, any two degrees $\alpha, \beta \in D$ possess a least upper bound (join). This means the semilattice structure of the Turing degrees is an upper semilattice. The join of two degrees corresponds to the degree of the recursive join of representative functions from each degree.
