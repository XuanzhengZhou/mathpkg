The unique readability theorem guarantees that every sentence has a unique parse tree: it is either atomic, a negation, or an implication, and this decomposition is unique. Furthermore, no proper prefix of a sentence is itself a sentence. This property is essential for defining functions by recursion on sentences and for proving properties by induction --- both depend on unambiguous parsing of formulas.
