The induction principle for sentences states that to prove a property holds for all sentences, it suffices to show: (1) the property holds for all atomic sentences (the base case), and (2) the property is preserved under the operations of negation and implication (the inductive steps). This follows directly from the definition of sentences as the smallest set closed under these operations.
