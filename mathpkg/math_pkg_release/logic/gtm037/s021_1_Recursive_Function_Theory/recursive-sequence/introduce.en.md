A recursive sequence of recursive real numbers is one whose values can be effectively approximated with arbitrary precision. The definition uses four binary recursive functions $f, g, h, k$ to encode the approximation process, where $f$ and $g$ provide signed numerator approximations, $h$ the denominator, and $k$ the modulus of convergence.
