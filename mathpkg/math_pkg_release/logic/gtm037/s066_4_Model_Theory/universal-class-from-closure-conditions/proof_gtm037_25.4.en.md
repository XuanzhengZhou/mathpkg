By 25.2 and 25.3 it suffices to show that $((\text{Mod } \Gamma) \upharpoonright \mathcal{L}') \upharpoonright \mathbf{U}$ is closed under ultraproducts. Suppose that $\mathfrak{A}_i \in ((\text{Mod } \Gamma) \upharpoonright \mathcal{L}') \upharpoonright \mathbf{U}$ for each $i \in I$, say $\mathfrak{A}_i = (\mathfrak{B}_i \upharpoonright \mathcal{L}') \upharpoonright \mathbf{U}$ with $\mathfrak{B}_i \models \Gamma$. By Proposition 25.7, $\mathfrak{P}_{i \in I} \mathfrak{A}_i / F \cong (\mathfrak{P}_{i \in I} \mathfrak{B}_i / F) \upharpoonright \mathcal{L}' \upharpoonright \mathbf{U}$. Since $\Gamma$ is closed under ultraproducts (by the characterization of universal classes or by \L o\'{s}'s theorem), $\mathfrak{P}_{i \in I} \mathfrak{B}_i / F \models \Gamma$, hence the ultraproduct belongs to the class.
