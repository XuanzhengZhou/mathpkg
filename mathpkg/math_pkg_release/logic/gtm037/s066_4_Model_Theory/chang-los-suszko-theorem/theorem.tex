Let $\Gamma$ be a set of sentences. Then the following conditions are equivalent:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\mathrm{Mod}\,\Gamma$ can be characterized by a set of universal-existential sentences;
\item[(ii)] $\mathrm{Mod}\,\Gamma$ is closed under unions of directed subsets;
\item[(iii)] $\mathrm{Mod}\,\Gamma$ is closed under unions of chains of type $\omega$.
\end{enumerate}
