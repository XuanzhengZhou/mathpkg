Corollary 25.10 is a preservation theorem characterizing when a sentence is preserved under taking substructures relative to a background theory $\Gamma$. It states that $\varphi$ is preserved under substructures relative to $\Gamma$ if and only if $\varphi$ is equivalent (modulo $\Gamma$) to a universal sentence. This result is analogous to Theorem 24.23 and follows from the characterization of universal classes via closure under substructures and ultraproducts (Theorem 25.2 and Corollary 25.9). The proof constructs a set $\Delta$ of universal sentences axiomatizing $S\,\mathrm{Mod}(\Gamma \cup \{\varphi\})$ and extracts a finite conjunction equivalent to $\varphi$.
