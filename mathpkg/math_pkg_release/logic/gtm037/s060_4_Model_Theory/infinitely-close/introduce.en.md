The relation $a \simeq b$ ("$a$ is infinitely close to $b$") captures the notion that two nonstandard reals differ by an infinitesimal. This is an equivalence relation on $^*\mathbb{R}$, and the equivalence classes partition the nonstandard reals into monads. Each monad contains at most one standard real number, which, when it exists, is precisely the standard part of every element in the monad.
