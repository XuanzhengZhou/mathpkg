Proposition 20.3 establishes a striking equivalence between the classical twin prime conjecture and a statement in nonstandard arithmetic. The twin prime conjecture — that there exist infinitely many primes $p$ such that $p+2$ is also prime — is shown to be equivalent to the existence of a single infinite nonstandard prime $p \in {}^*\omega$ whose successor $p+2$ is also prime. This illustrates how nonstandard analysis can transform infinitary statements about standard numbers into statements about individual nonstandard elements.
