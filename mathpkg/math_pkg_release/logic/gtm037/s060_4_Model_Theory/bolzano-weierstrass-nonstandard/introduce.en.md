Proposition 20.7 is a nonstandard proof of the classical Bolzano–Weierstrass theorem. Given a bounded sequence $x$, its nonstandard extension $t[x]$ is finite, so it has a standard part $y = \operatorname{st} t[x]$. The proof shows that $y$ is a limit point of $x$ by using the ultrafilter property: since $t[x] \simeq y$, the set of indices where $|x_n - y| < \varepsilon$ belongs to the ultrafilter and is therefore infinite. This is an early example of a standard theorem proved by nonstandard methods without $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ arguments.
