The nonstandard characterization of continuity states that $f$ is continuous at $a$ precisely when $f$ maps the monad of $a$ into the monad of $f(a)$: infinitesimal changes in the input produce only infinitesimal changes in the output. This formulation replaces the classical $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ definition with a simple statement about infinite closeness, and is one of the most elegant applications of nonstandard analysis to elementary real analysis.
