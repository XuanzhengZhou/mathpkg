Proposition 20.5 provides a bridge between the classical $\varepsilon$-$\delta$ definition of limit and the nonstandard framework. A real sequence $(x_n)$ converges to $s$ in the standard sense if and only if its nonstandard extension $^*x_n$ is infinitely close to $s$ for every infinite index $n \in {}^*\omega$. This characterization is a central tool in nonstandard analysis, converting statements about limits into simpler statements about infinite closeness.
