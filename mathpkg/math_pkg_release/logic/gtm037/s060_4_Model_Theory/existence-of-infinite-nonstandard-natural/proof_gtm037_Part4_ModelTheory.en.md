Define $x \in {}^\omega\omega$ by $x_i = i$ for all $i \in \omega$. For each $m \in \omega$, $\{i : m < x_i\} = \omega \setminus m \in F$. Hence $[\langle m : i \in \omega \rangle] < [x]$ in ${}^\omega\langle \mathbb{R}, < \rangle / F$, so $m^* < t[x]$ in $^*\langle \mathbb{R}, < \rangle$. Since $m$ is arbitrary, this shows that $t[x]$ is infinite. Also, $\{i : x_i \in \omega\} = \omega \in F$, so $t[x] \in {}^*\omega$.
