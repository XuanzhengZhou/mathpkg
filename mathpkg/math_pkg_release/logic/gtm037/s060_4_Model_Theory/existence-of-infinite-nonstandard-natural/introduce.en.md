Proposition 20.1 asserts the existence of an infinite element in the nonstandard natural numbers $^*\omega$. The proof constructs an explicit element $[x]$ in the ultrapower $^{\omega}\langle \mathbb{R}, < \rangle / F$ by taking the identity sequence $x_i = i$, and shows that it dominates every constant sequence representing a standard natural number. This demonstrates that the nonstandard universe is a proper extension containing infinitely large natural numbers.
