Lemma 26.5 shows that the relation $\mathfrak{A} \equiv_m \mathfrak{B}$ of elementary equivalence of degree $m$ precisely captures agreement on all sentences of quantifier rank at most $m$ (in prenex normal form). The proof proceeds by induction on $m$: the base case $m = 0$ follows from the definition of $\equiv_0$ via isomorphism of the constant-free reducts, while the inductive step uses the back-and-forth property built into the definition of $\equiv_{m+1}$. This lemma is fundamental for connecting the purely structural notion of $\equiv_m$ to logical equivalence up to a given quantifier complexity.
