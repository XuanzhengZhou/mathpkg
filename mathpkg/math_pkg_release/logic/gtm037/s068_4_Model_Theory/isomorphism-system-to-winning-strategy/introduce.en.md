Lemma 26.12 is a key technical result connecting the algebraic notion of systems of partial isomorphisms to the game-theoretic notion of winning strategies. Given an $(m+1)$-system $\langle I_0, \ldots, I_{m+1} \rangle$ of partial isomorphisms, Player II can use the "forth" property (condition (iii) of Definition 26.9) to respond to each of Player I's moves: when Player I plays an element from one structure, Player II extends the current partial isomorphism $f \in I_{k+1}$ to a $g \in I_k$ that includes the new element, and responds with the image of that element. The nesting condition $I_{k+1} \subseteq I_k$ ensures that the partial isomorphisms remain valid as the game progresses through the $m$ rounds.
