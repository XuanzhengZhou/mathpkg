Lemma 26.10 establishes that the two formulations of back-and-forth relations are equivalent: if there exists an $(n+1)$-sequence (in the sense of Definition 26.6, using relations between tuples), then there also exists an $(n+1)$-system of partial isomorphisms (in the sense of Definition 26.9, using partial functions that preserve atomic formulas). This lemma shows that the older sequence-based formulation implies the more modern partial-isomorphism-based formulation, which is preferred in contemporary model theory for its cleaner treatment of Fraisse's theorem and Ehrenfeucht-Fraisse games.
