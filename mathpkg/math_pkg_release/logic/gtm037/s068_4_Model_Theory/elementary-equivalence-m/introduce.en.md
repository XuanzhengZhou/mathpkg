The relation $\mathfrak{A} \equiv_m \mathfrak{B}$ of elementary equivalence of degree $m$ is defined by induction on the natural number $m$. The base case $m = 0$ reduces to isomorphism of the reducts obtained by forgetting individual constants. The inductive step $m+1$ captures the back-and-forth property: for every element of one structure there is a matching element in the other such that the expanded structures are equivalent at degree $m$. This relation is the central tool for comparing structures up to quantifier rank $m$, and it generalizes elementary equivalence by stratifying it into finite levels.
