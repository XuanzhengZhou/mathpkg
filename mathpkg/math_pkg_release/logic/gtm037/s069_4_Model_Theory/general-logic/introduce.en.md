A general logic is a weak general logic that additionally satisfies two natural closure conditions: every elementary class is closed under isomorphism, and the property of being an elementary class is preserved under renaming of the language symbols. These conditions ensure that the logic behaves well with respect to the structural relationships between languages and structures. Together with the weak general logic axioms, this definition sets up the framework for Lindström's theorems, which characterize first-order logic as the maximal logic satisfying compactness and the downward Löwenheim-Skolem theorem.
