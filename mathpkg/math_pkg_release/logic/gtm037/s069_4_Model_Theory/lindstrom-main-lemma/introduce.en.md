Lemma 26.22 is the central technical lemma in Lindström's first theorem. It constructs, under the assumption that a general logic $\mathscr{L}$ strictly extends first-order logic while satisfying the downward Löwenheim-Skolem theorem, a class $\mathbf{K}$ of structures in a language with a single unary predicate $P$ with very specific properties: every member is infinite, $P$ is always finite and nonempty, there are countable members with $P$ of any prescribed finite size, and $\mathbf{K}$ is $\mathscr{L}$-projective. This class is then used in the proof of Lindström's theorem (Theorem 26.24) to derive a contradiction with countable compactness, showing that no such logic can properly extend first-order logic.
