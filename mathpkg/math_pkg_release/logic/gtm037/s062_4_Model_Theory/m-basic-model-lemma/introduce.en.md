Lemma 21.17 states that any model of the theory $\Gamma_{\text{equiv}}$ whose equivalence classes each have size at most $m$ (with $m \in \omega \setminus \{1\}$) can be reduced to an $m$-basic submodel that is elementarily equivalent up to $m$ formulas. The proof uses a choice function to select subsets of the equivalence classes, constructing $\mathfrak{B}$ from each $R$-equivalence class by selecting at most $m$ elements per class, then applying Lemma 21.12 (an automorphism-based criterion for $\preceq_m$) to verify the elementary submodel relation.
