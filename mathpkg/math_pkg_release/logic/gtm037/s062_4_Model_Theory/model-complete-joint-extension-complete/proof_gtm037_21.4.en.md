The proof is analogous to that of Proposition 21.29. Since $\Gamma$ has the joint extension property, any two models $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ of $\Gamma$ can be jointly embedded into a common extension $\mathfrak{C}$ which is a model of $\Gamma$. By model completeness, $\mathfrak{A} \preceq \mathfrak{C}$ and $\mathfrak{B} \preceq \mathfrak{C}$, hence $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are elementarily equivalent. Since this holds for any two models, $\Gamma$ is complete.
