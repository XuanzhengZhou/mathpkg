Proposition 21.23 records fundamental preservation properties of universal and existential formulas with respect to the substructure relation. Part (i) states that universal formulas are preserved downward: if a universal formula holds in a larger structure $\mathfrak{B}$ under an assignment $x$, it also holds in the substructure $\mathfrak{A}$. Part (ii) is the dual statement for existential formulas, which are preserved upward from $\mathfrak{A}$ to $\mathfrak{B}$. These properties are described as "trivial but useful" in the text and underpin many arguments about model completeness.
