Let $\Gamma$ be a theory satisfying the following two conditions:

(i) If $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are any two models of $\Gamma$, then every finitely generated substructure of $\mathfrak{A}$ is embeddable in $\mathfrak{B}$.

(ii) If $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ are any two models of $\Gamma$, $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$, and $\mathfrak{B}'$ is a finitely generated substructure of $\mathfrak{B}$, then there is an embedding $f$ of $\mathfrak{B}'$ into $\mathfrak{A}$ such that $f \upharpoonright A \cap B' = \mathbf{I} \upharpoonright A \cap B'$.

Then $\Gamma$ is complete.
