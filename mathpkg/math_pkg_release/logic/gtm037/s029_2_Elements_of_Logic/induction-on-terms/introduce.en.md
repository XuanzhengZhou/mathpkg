Proposition 10.11 states the induction principle for terms of a first-order language $\mathcal{L}$. To prove that every term belongs to a class $\Gamma$, it suffices to show that $\Gamma$ contains all individual variables and is closed under the application of operation symbols. This follows directly from the definition of $\text{Trm } \mathcal{L}$ as the intersection of all classes satisfying these two closure conditions; hence $\text{Trm}$ itself is the smallest such class.
