$\Gamma_{\text{At}}^\infty$ is the first-order theory extending $\Gamma_{\text{At}}$ with axioms asserting the existence of $m$ distinct elements for each $m \in \omega \sim 1$. A structure $\mathfrak{A}$ is a model of $\Gamma_{\text{At}}^\infty$ if and only if $\mathfrak{A}$ is an infinite atomic Boolean algebra. This theory is used in model-theoretic arguments concerning Boolean algebras, including embedding and substructure completeness results.
