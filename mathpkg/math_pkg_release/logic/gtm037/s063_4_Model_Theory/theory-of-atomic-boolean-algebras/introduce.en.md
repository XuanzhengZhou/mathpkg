$\Gamma_{\text{At}}$ is the first-order theory extending $\Gamma_{\text{BA}}$ (the theory of Boolean algebras) with the additional axiom $\forall v_1\{\neg(v_1 = 0) \rightarrow \exists v_0[\varphi_{\text{At}}(v_0) \land v_0 \cdot v_1 = v_0]\}$. This axiom asserts that every nonzero element lies above an atom. Consequently, a structure $\mathfrak{A}$ is a model of $\Gamma_{\text{At}}$ if and only if $\mathfrak{A}$ is an atomic Boolean algebra.
