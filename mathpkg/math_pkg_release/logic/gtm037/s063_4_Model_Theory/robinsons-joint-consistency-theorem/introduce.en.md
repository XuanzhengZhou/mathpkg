Robinson's Joint Consistency Theorem states that if two consistent theories $\Gamma_1$ and $\Gamma_2$ in languages $\mathcal{L}_1$ and $\mathcal{L}_2$ share a common complete subtheory $\Gamma_0$ in the intersection language $\mathcal{L}_0 = \mathcal{L}_1 \cap \mathcal{L}_2$, then their union $\Gamma_1 \cup \Gamma_2$ is consistent. This theorem is a direct consequence of Craig's Interpolation Theorem and plays a fundamental role in model-theoretic arguments about combining theories and conservative extensions.
