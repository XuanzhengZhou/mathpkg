Beth's Definability Theorem is the converse of Padoa's method. It states that if a nonlogical constant $\pi$ is implicitly definable in a theory $\Gamma$ (meaning any two models of $\Gamma$ that agree on all other nonlogical constants must also agree on $\pi$), then $\pi$ is explicitly definable in terms of the other constants. This result is a kind of completeness theorem in the theory of definition and is derived from Craig's Interpolation Theorem.
