Padoa's Method provides a sufficient condition for proving that a nonlogical constant is not definable in a given theory. If one can exhibit two models of the theory that agree on all other nonlogical constants but differ on the constant in question, then that constant cannot be explicitly defined from the others. The method is illustrated by showing that the order relation $<$ is not definable in the theory of the additive group of integers $\langle \mathbb{Z}, + \rangle$.
