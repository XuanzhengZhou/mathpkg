This fundamental proposition establishes that the diagonal embedding of a structure into its ultraproduct is always an elementary embedding. Given an $L$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$, an index set $I$, and an ultrafilter $F$ over $I$, the map $f$ sending each element $a \in A$ to the equivalence class of the constant sequence $\langle a : i \in I \rangle$ preserves all first-order properties. The proof first verifies that $f$ is injective using the ultrafilter property, then applies the technique of Proposition 19.7 to construct the image as an $L$-structure with $f$ becoming an isomorphism onto its image, hence an elementary embedding.
