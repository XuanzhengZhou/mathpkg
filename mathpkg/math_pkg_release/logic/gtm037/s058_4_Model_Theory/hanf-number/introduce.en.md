The Hanf number of a Hanf system $R$ is the least cardinal $m$ such that once a parameter $\Gamma$ of the relation admits a witness with size at least $m$, it admits arbitrarily large witnesses. This definition is well-posed by Theorem 19.28, which guarantees the existence of such a cardinal for every Hanf system. The Hanf number captures the precise threshold at which the upward Löwenheim–Skolem-type phenomenon kicks in for the abstract relation $R$, generalizing the classical result for first-order logic where the Hanf number is $\omega$.
