Let $L$ be a general logic such that $L_{\mathrm{fo}} \subseteq L$. Assume that $L$ satisfies the downward L\"owenheim--Skolem theorem and the countable compactness theorem; that is, assume the following conditions:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] any $L$-elementary class with an infinite member has a denumerable member;
  \item[(ii)] if $\mathfrak{K}$ is a countable collection of $L$-elementary classes and $\bigcap \mathfrak{F} \neq 0$ for every finite $\mathfrak{F} \subseteq \mathfrak{K}$, then $\bigcap \mathfrak{K} \neq 0$.
\end{enumerate}

Then $L_{\mathrm{fo}} \equiv L$.
