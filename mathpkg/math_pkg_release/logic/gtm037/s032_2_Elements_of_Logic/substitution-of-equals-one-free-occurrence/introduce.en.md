Lemma 10.48 establishes that replacing a single free occurrence of a term in a formula preserves logical equivalence under the equality connective. More precisely, if one formula is obtained from another by substituting one free occurrence, then the equality of the substituted terms implies the biconditional of the two formulas. This lemma is a key step toward the main theorem on substitution of equals for equals in first-order logic.
