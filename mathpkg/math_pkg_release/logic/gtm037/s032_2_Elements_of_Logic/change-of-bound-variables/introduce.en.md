Theorem 10.59 (Change of Bound Variables) is a fundamental result in first-order logic establishing that bound variables can be renamed without affecting logical equivalence. If a variable $\beta$ does not occur at all in a formula $\varphi$, then replacing every bound occurrence of $\alpha$ in $\varphi$ by $\beta$ yields a formula logically equivalent to $\varphi$. This theorem justifies the common practice of alpha-conversion in logical reasoning.
