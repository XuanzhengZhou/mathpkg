This theorem establishes the fundamental structural properties of the deducibility relation $\vdash$ in propositional logic. Part (i) states monotonicity (adding hypotheses preserves deducibility). Part (ii) asserts finiteness of proof (any deduction uses only finitely many hypotheses). Part (iii) is a cut or transitivity property. Part (iv) is the syntactic analogue of modus ponens at the level of the deducibility relation, stating that if $\varphi$ and $\varphi \to \psi$ are both deducible from $\Gamma$, then so is $\psi$.
