A formal proof in a sentential language is a finite sequence of sentences where each step is either a logical axiom, a hypothesis from a given set $\Gamma$, or follows by modus ponens (detachment) from two earlier sentences in the sequence. This formalizes the intuitive notion of a mathematical proof within the object language, allowing rigorous study of deductive consequence via the relation $\Gamma \vdash \varphi$.
