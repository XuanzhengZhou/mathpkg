The standard sign function $sg$ on natural numbers is defined by composing the modified sign function $\overline{sg}$ with itself: $sg(x) = \overline{sg}(\overline{sg}(x))$. This yields $sg(0) = 0$ and $sg(x) = 1$ for all positive $x$. Since $\overline{sg}$ is primitive recursive and the class of primitive recursive functions is closed under composition, $sg$ is primitive recursive.
