Integer (floor) division $[x/y]$ on natural numbers is primitive recursive. It uses an auxiliary function $f$ that counts how many times the divisor goes into the dividend by incrementing a counter whenever the remainder resets to zero. The construction depends on the previously established primitive recursiveness of addition, the modified sign function $\overline{sg}$, absolute difference, successor, and the remainder function $rm$.
