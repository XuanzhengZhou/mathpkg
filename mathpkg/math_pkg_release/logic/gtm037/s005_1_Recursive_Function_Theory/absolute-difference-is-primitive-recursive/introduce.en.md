The absolute difference of two natural numbers $|x - y|$ is defined as the sum of the two truncated subtractions $(x \div y) + (y \div x)$. Since truncated subtraction is primitive recursive and addition is primitive recursive, their composition yields a primitive recursive absolute difference function. This provides the usual notion of distance between natural numbers within the primitive recursive framework.
