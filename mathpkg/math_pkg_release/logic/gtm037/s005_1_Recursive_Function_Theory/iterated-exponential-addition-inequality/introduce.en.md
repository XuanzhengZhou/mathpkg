Lemma 2.40 provides an upper bound for the sum of two iterated exponentials with the same base $m > 1$ but possibly different iteration counts $n$ and $p$: the sum is bounded by $a(m, \max(n, p) + 1)$, i.e., the iterated exponential with one additional iteration beyond the larger of the two counts. This inequality is useful for estimating combinations of iterated exponentials and plays a role in proving that the iterated exponential grows faster than any elementary function.
