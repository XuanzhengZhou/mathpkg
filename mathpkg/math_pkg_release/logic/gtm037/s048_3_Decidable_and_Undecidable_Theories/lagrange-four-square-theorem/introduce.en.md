Lagrange's Four-Square Theorem states that a non-negative integer is a sum of four integer squares. More precisely, for any integer $a$, the condition $a \in \omega$ (i.e., $a$ is a natural number) is equivalent to $a$ being expressible as $x_1^2 + x_2^2 + x_3^2 + x_4^2$ for some integers $x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4$. In the context of mathematical logic, this theorem is used to give a first-order definition of the natural numbers $\omega$ within the ring of integers $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$, a crucial step in proving that the complete theory of $(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)$ is inseparable.
