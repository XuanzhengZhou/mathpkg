The class of \textbf{general recursive functions} is the intersection of all classes $A$ of number-theoretic functions such that the successor function $s$ and all projection functions $U_i^n$ ($n > 0$, $i < n$) belong to $A$, and $A$ is closed under composition, primitive recursion, and minimalization (applied to special functions).

A relation $R$ is \textbf{general recursive} iff its characteristic function $\chi_R$ is general recursive. Frequently, both for functions and relations, we say merely \textit{recursive} instead of \textit{general recursive}.
