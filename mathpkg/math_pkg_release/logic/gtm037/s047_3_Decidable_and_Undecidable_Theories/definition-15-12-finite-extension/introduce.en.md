Definition 15.12 formalizes the notion of a finite extension of a theory. A theory $\Delta$ in a language $\mathcal{L}'$ is a finite extension of a theory $\Gamma$ in $\mathcal{L}$ if $\Delta$ extends $\Gamma$ (so $\mathcal{L}'$ expands $\mathcal{L}$) and $\Delta$ can be axiomatized by adding only finitely many sentences $\Theta$ of $\mathcal{L}'$ to $\Gamma$. This concept is useful for transferring undecidability properties between theories, as shown in Proposition 15.13.
