Let $\Theta = \{\varphi : \varphi \text{ is a sentence of } \mathcal{L}' \text{ and } \Gamma \cup \Delta \models \varphi\}$. Clearly $\Theta$ is a theory in $\mathcal{L}'$ which is an extension of $\Gamma$ and a finite extension of $\Delta$.

Since $\Gamma$ is essentially undecidable, by 15.10 the theory $\Theta$ is essentially undecidable. Since $\Theta$ is a finite extension of $\Delta$, by 15.13 $\Delta$ is undecidable.
