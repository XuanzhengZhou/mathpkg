If a syntactical $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ in $\mathcal{L}'$ is effective (i.e., the defining formula $\chi$, the operation-symbol map $f$, and the relation-symbol map $R$ are all recursive), then the induced translations $\sigma \mapsto \sigma^{\mathfrak{A}}$ on terms and $\varphi \mapsto \varphi^{\mathfrak{A}}$ on formulas are also effective. Concretely, the partial functions obtained by composing the Gödel numbering, the translation $f$, and the inverse Gödel numbering are partial recursive when restricted to the Gödel sets of terms and formulas of $\mathcal{L}$.
