If $\mathfrak{A}$ is an interpretation of a theory $\Gamma$ (in language $\mathcal{L}$) into another theory $\Gamma'$ (in language $\mathcal{L}'$), then logical consequence is preserved under the translation: whenever a formula $\varphi$ of $\mathcal{L}$ is a semantic consequence of $\Gamma$, its $\mathfrak{A}$-translation $\varphi^{2\mathfrak{A}}$ is a semantic consequence of $\Gamma'$. This follows directly from Proposition 11.45 by considering an arbitrary model $\mathfrak{B}$ of $\Gamma'$ and the associated interpreted structure.
