This proposition establishes the fundamental relationship between satisfiability in the interpreted structure and satisfiability of the translated formula. Given a syntactical $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ in $\mathcal{L}'$ and any model $\mathfrak{B}$ of the associated theory $\Gamma'$, a formula $\varphi$ of $\mathcal{L}$ holds in the $\Gamma'$-$\mathfrak{A}$-reduct of $\mathfrak{B}$ under assignment $x$ precisely when the $\mathfrak{A}$-translation $\varphi^{2\mathfrak{A}}$ holds in $\mathfrak{B}$ under the same assignment. The proof proceeds by induction on the structure of $\varphi$.
