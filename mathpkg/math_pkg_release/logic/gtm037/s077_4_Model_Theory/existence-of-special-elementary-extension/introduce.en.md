This theorem establishes the existence of special elementary extensions without assuming the generalized continuum hypothesis (GCH). Given a structure $\mathfrak{A}$ and a cardinal $m$ satisfying certain cardinal arithmetic conditions (in particular that $2^n \leq m$ for all $n < m$), the structure admits a special elementary extension of cardinality $m$. The proof splits into the successor and limit cardinal cases.
