This proposition summarizes the key metamathematical properties of the three standard arithmetic theories. $Q$ (Robinson arithmetic) is finitely axiomatizable by its seven axioms. $P$ (Peano arithmetic) is recursively axiomatizable via the induction schema but not finitely axiomatizable. All three theories -- $Q$, $P$, and $N$ (true arithmetic) -- are $\omega$-consistent, making them suitable foundations for the syntactic definability results.
