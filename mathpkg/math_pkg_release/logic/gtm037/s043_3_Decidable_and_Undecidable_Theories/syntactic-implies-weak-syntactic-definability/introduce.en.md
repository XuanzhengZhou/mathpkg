Syntactic definability is a stronger notion than weak syntactic definability in formal theories. This proposition establishes that for any relation $R$ on natural numbers, if $R$ can be syntactically defined within a consistent theory $\Gamma$ in the language of number theory, then $R$ is also weakly syntactically definable in $\Gamma$. The result is straightforward since the two defining conditions for syntactic definability directly imply the single condition required for weak syntactic definability.
