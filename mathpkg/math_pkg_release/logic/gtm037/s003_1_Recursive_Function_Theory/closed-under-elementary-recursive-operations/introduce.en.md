A class $\mathcal{A}$ of number-theoretic functions is closed under elementary recursive operations if it satisfies two conditions: first, it contains all the basic elementary functions listed in Definition 2.1 items (1) through (5); second, it is closed under the three fundamental operations of composition, summation, and multiplication. The class of all elementary functions is the prototypical example of such a class. All wider classes of effective functions discussed later will also satisfy this closure property.
