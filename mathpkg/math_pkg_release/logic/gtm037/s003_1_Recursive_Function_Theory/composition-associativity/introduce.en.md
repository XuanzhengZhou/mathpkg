The composition operator $K_n^m$ on number-theoretic functions is associative. Given functions $f$ of arity $m$, $g_0, \ldots, g_{m-1}$ of arity $n$, and $h_0, \ldots, h_{n-1}$ of arity $p$, composing $f$ with the $g_i$ and then applying the $h_j$ yields the same result as first composing each $g_i$ with the $h_j$ and then composing $f$ with the resulting functions. This associativity property is fundamental for working with iterated compositions in recursive function theory.
