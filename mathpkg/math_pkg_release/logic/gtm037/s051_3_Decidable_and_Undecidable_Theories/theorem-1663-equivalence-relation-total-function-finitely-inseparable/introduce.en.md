Theorem 16.63 concerns the theory of a structure equipped with an equivalence relation on its universe along with a total (unary) function. The theorem states that this combined theory is finitely inseparable and therefore undecidable. This result adds to the catalogue of simple relational/functional structures whose first-order theories turn out to be undecidable, emphasizing that undecidability can arise from very modest linguistic resources.
