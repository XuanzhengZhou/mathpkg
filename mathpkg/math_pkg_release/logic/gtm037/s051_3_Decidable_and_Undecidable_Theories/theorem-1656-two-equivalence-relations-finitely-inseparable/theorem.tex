Let $\mathcal{L}$ be a language with a single binary relation symbol $\mathbf{R}$, and let
$$
\Gamma = \{\varphi \in \text{Sent}_{\mathcal{L}} : \Theta \models \varphi\},
\quad\text{where}\quad
\Theta = \{\forall v_0 \forall v_1 (\mathbf{R}v_0v_1 \rightarrow \mathbf{R}v_1v_0)\}.
$$
Let $\mathcal{L}'$ be a language with two binary relation symbols $\mathbf{L}$ and $\mathbf{M}$, and let
$$
\Gamma' = \{\varphi \in \text{Sent}_{\mathcal{L}'} : \Omega \models \varphi\},
$$
where $\Omega$ consists of the axioms for two equivalence relations:
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\forall v_1 \mathbf{L}v_0v_0; \\
&\forall v_0 \forall v_1 (\mathbf{L}v_0v_1 \rightarrow \mathbf{L}v_1v_0); \\
&\forall v_0 \forall v_1 \forall v_2 (\mathbf{L}v_0v_1 \wedge \mathbf{L}v_1v_2 \rightarrow \mathbf{L}v_0v_2); \\
&\forall v_0 \mathbf{M}v_0v_0; \\
&\forall v_0 \forall v_1 (\mathbf{M}v_0v_1 \rightarrow \mathbf{M}v_1v_0); \\
&\forall v_0 \forall v_1 \forall v_2 (\mathbf{M}v_0v_1 \wedge \mathbf{M}v_1v_2 \rightarrow \mathbf{M}v_0v_2).
\end{aligned}
$$
Then $\Gamma$ and $\Gamma'$ are finitely inseparable.
