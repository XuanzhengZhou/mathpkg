Theorem 16.66 establishes that the first-order theory of lattices--structures with two binary operations (meet and join) satisfying the standard lattice axioms--is finitely inseparable. As lattices form a fundamental algebraic structure in many areas of mathematics, this undecidability result is particularly significant. It shows that even well-behaved algebraic theories can cross the threshold into undecidability, further delineating the boundary between decidable and undecidable first-order theories studied throughout Part 3 of Monk's book.
