Let $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ be distinct variables. Prove:

$$\vdash \exists \alpha (\neg \alpha = \beta \land \neg \alpha = \gamma) \leftrightarrow [\beta = \gamma \land \exists \alpha \exists \beta (\neg \alpha = \beta)] \lor [\neg \beta = \gamma \land \exists \alpha \exists \beta \exists \gamma (\neg \alpha = \beta \land \neg \beta = \gamma \land \neg \alpha = \gamma)].$$
