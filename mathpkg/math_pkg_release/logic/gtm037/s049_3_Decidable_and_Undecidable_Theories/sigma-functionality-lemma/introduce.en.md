Lemma 16.33 establishes the functionality of the ternary relation $\sigma$ in the language $\mathcal{L}_6$: if $\sigma(x, y, z)$ and $\sigma(x, y, w)$ both hold, then $z = w$. This means that the addition-like operation encoded by $\sigma$ is single-valued. The proof constructs witnessing functions $f$ and $g$ from the two $\sigma$ assumptions, considers their intersection, and uses well-foundedness properties ($\Omega$) to show the domains must agree on $\mathbf{U} y$.
