Lemma 16.27 states that in the language $\mathcal{L}_4$, every nonzero set is the successor of some set. This is another Peano-like axiom being established within the formal set theory. The proof splits into two cases: if $\Omega x$ holds then the conclusion follows from earlier lemmas about $\Omega$ and $\mathbf{U}$, and if $\Omega x$ fails then $x$ is its own successor.
