Lemma 16.37 states that in $\mathcal{L}_7$, the singleton $\{1\}$ does not satisfy the ordinal-like predicate $\Omega$. The proof observes that $0 \in 1 \in \{1\}$ but $0 \notin \{1\}$, demonstrating that $\{1\}$ fails transitivity ($\operatorname{Trans}$). Since $\Omega$ requires transitivity, the conclusion follows. This lemma is used to justify one of the technical clauses in the definition of addition (the case where $\sigma$ fails for an $\Omega$-satisfying argument).
