This theorem asserts that in a definitional extension $(\Gamma', \mathcal{L}')$ of a theory $(\Gamma, \mathcal{L})$, every formula $\psi$ in the expanded language $\mathcal{L}'$ is provably equivalent (relative to $\Gamma'$) to a formula $\psi'$ in the original language $\mathcal{L}$ having the same free variables.

The significance is that defined symbols (new nonlogical constants in $\mathcal{L}'$) can always be eliminated in favor of old symbols from $\mathcal{L}$. In the effective case, the translation from $\psi$ to $\psi'$ can be computed algorithmically, so definitions do not add any essential expressive power to the language.
