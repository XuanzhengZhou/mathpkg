A class $\mathbf{K}$ of structures is closed under $\mathbf{H}$, $\mathbf{S}$, and $\mathbf{P}$ if and only if it can be expressed as $\mathbf{HSPK}$ (the class of homomorphic images of substructures of products of members of $\mathbf{K}$). Equivalently, $\mathbf{K} = \mathbf{HSPL}$ for some class $\mathbf{L}$. This is a key step toward Birkhoff's HSP theorem, which states that varieties are exactly those classes closed under $\mathbf{H}$, $\mathbf{S}$, and $\mathbf{P}$.
