Obviously $(i) \Rightarrow (ii) \Rightarrow (iii)$. Now assume $(iii)$. Clearly $\mathbf{HK} = \mathbf{K}$ and $\mathbf{K} \subseteq \mathbf{SK}$, $\mathbf{K} \subseteq \mathbf{PK}$. Next,

$$\mathbf{SK} = \mathbf{SHSPL} \subseteq \mathbf{HSSPL} \quad \text{by 24.10(i)}$$
$$= \mathbf{HSPL} = \mathbf{K}.$$

Finally,

$$\mathbf{PK} = \mathbf{PHSPL} \subseteq \mathbf{HPSPL} \subseteq \mathbf{HSPPL} \quad \text{by 24.10(ii), (iii)}$$
$$= \mathbf{HSPL} = \mathbf{K}.$$
