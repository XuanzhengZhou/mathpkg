For any first-order formula $\varphi$, one can effectively determine a finite set of combinatorial conditions that completely characterize when $\varphi$ holds in a set algebra structure $(\mathbf{S}I, \cup, \cap, \sim, 0, I, F_I)$ where $F_I$ is the collection of finite subsets of $I$. The conditions are expressed in terms of the cardinalities (exact, at least, at-least-but-finite, or infinite) of Boolean combinations of the assigned sets. The proof proceeds by induction on the structure of $\varphi$, starting from atomic formulas of the forms $v_i = v_j$ and $v_i + v_j = v_k$.
