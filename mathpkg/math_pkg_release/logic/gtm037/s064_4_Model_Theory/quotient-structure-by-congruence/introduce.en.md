Given a congruence relation $R$ on an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$, the quotient structure $\mathfrak{A}/R$ is formed by taking the set of $R$-equivalence classes as the universe and defining operations componentwise on representatives. The congruence condition ensures that this definition is well-defined. The natural map $[\,\cdot\,]_R: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}/R$ sending each element to its equivalence class is a homomorphism.
