The ideal generated by a subset $X$ has an explicit syntactic description: an element $y$ belongs to the generated ideal iff it is bounded above by a finite cylindrification of a finite sum of elements from $X$. This characterization is crucial for working constructively with ideals and generalizes the Boolean case where the ideal consists of elements below finite sums of generators.
