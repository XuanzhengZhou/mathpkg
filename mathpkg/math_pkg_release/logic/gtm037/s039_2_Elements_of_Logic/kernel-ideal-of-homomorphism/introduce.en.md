Every surjective homomorphism between cylindric algebras gives rise to an ideal — its kernel — and the target algebra is isomorphic to the quotient by that ideal. This is the first isomorphism theorem for cylindric algebras, directly generalizing the corresponding result for Boolean algebras and establishing the equivalence between congruences and ideals.
