Let $\mathfrak{A} = \langle A, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1, c_{\kappa}, d_{\kappa\lambda} \rangle_{\kappa,\lambda < \alpha}$ be a $\mathrm{CA}_{\alpha}$. An \textit{ideal} of $\mathfrak{A}$ is an ideal of the Boolean algebra $\langle A, +, \cdot, -, 0, 1 \rangle$ such that for all $\kappa < \alpha$, if $x \in I$ then $c_{\kappa} x \in I$.
