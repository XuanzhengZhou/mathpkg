This proposition states that term evaluation commutes with taking substructures. If $\mathfrak{A}$ is a substructure of $\mathfrak{B}$, then evaluating a term $\sigma$ in $\mathfrak{A}$ yields the same result as evaluating it in $\mathfrak{B}$. This follows immediately from the fact that the operations of $\mathfrak{A}$ are the restrictions of those of $\mathfrak{B}$.
