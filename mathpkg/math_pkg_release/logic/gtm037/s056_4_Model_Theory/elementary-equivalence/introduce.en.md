Elementary equivalence is the fundamental notion of sameness in first-order model theory. Two $L$-structures are elementarily equivalent if they satisfy exactly the same first-order sentences. Thus no first-order property can distinguish them. Isomorphic structures are always elementarily equivalent, but the converse is far from true, as will be demonstrated throughout model theory.
