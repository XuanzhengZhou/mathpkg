Let $X$ be any set. An $X$-expansion of $\mathfrak{L}$ is an expansion $\mathfrak{L}'$ of $\mathfrak{L}$ obtained from $\mathfrak{L}$ by adding new distinct individual constants $\mathbf{c}_x$ for all $x \in X$.

We denote $\mathfrak{L}'$ by $(\mathfrak{L}, \mathbf{c}_x)_{x \in X}$, and $\mathfrak{L}'$-structures are denoted by $(\mathfrak{A}, l_x)_{x \in X}$, where $\mathfrak{A}$ is an $\mathfrak{L}$-structure and $l_x$ is a member of $A$ for each $x \in X$.
