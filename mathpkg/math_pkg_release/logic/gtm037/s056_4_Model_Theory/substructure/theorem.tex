Let $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ be two $\mathcal{L}$-structures. We say that $\mathfrak{A}$ is a substructure of $\mathfrak{B}$, and $\mathfrak{B}$ is an extension of $\mathfrak{A}$, $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$ or $\mathfrak{B} \supseteq \mathfrak{A}$, provided that the following conditions hold:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $A \subseteq B$;
\item[(ii)] for each operation symbol $\mathbf{O}$ (say $m$-ary), $\mathbf{O}^{\mathfrak{A}} = \mathbf{O}^{\mathfrak{B}} \upharpoonright {}^m A$;
\item[(iii)] for each relation symbol $\mathbf{R}$ (say $m$-ary), $\mathbf{R}^{\mathfrak{A}} = \mathbf{R}^{\mathfrak{B}} \cap {}^m A$.
\end{enumerate}

Consequently, if $\mathfrak{B}$ is an $\mathcal{L}$-structure, $\emptyset \neq A \subseteq B$, and $A$ is closed under all of the operations of $\mathfrak{B}$, then there is a unique $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ with universe $A$ such that $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$.
