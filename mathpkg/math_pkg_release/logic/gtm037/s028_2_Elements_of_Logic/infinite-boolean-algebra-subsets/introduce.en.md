Exercise 9.67 from Chapter 9 of Monk's Mathematical Logic. This exercise asks to show that every infinite Boolean algebra contains both an infinite set of pairwise disjoint elements and an infinite chain (totally ordered subset). These structural properties are useful in the analysis of infinite Boolean algebras and relate to the existence of independent families and the cellularity of the algebra.
