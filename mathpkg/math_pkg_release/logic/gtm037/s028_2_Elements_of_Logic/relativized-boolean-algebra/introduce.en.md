The relativization of a Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ to an element $a$ produces a new Boolean algebra $\mathfrak{A} \upharpoonright a$ whose universe is the principal ideal $\{x \in A : x \leq a\}$, with $a$ serving as the unit element. The operations of addition and multiplication are inherited unchanged from $\mathfrak{A}$, while complementation is modified to $a \cdot -x$. This construction is fundamental for decomposition theorems, as it allows a Boolean algebra to be factored into the product of its relativizations to complementary elements.
