This theorem guarantees the existence of a free Boolean algebra on any nonempty set of generators. The proof proceeds by constructing the Lindenbaum algebra $\mathfrak{M}_{\mathcal{P}}$ of the sentential language $\mathcal{P}$ whose propositional variables are the elements of $X$. Theorem 9.58 then shows that $\mathfrak{M}_{\mathcal{P}}$ is freely generated by the equivalence classes of the propositional variables, providing the desired free Boolean algebra. An alternative algebraic proof constructs the algebra inductively by closing $X$ under the Boolean operations.
