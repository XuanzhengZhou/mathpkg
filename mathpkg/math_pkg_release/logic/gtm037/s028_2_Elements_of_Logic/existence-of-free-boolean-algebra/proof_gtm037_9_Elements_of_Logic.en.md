Let $X$ be a nonempty set. Consider the sentential language $\mathcal{P}$ with $X$ as the set of propositional variables (with appropriate indexing). By Theorem 9.58, the Lindenbaum algebra $\mathfrak{M}_{\mathcal{P}}$ is freely generated by $\{[\langle s \rangle] : s \in P\}$, and the map $s \mapsto [\langle s \rangle]$ is one-one. Identifying $X$ with its image under this map via Lemma 9.52 (transport of structure), we obtain a Boolean algebra freely generated by $X$.

Alternatively, one may give a direct algebraic construction: let $Y_0 = X$ and recursively define
$$Y_{m+1} = Y_m \cup \{-x : x \in Y_m\} \cup \{x + y : x, y \in Y_m\} \cup \{0, 1\}.$$
Then $\bigcup_{m \in \omega} Y_m$ is the universe of a Boolean algebra. One shows by induction that $|Y_m| \leq |X| + \aleph_0$, so the resulting algebra has cardinality at most $|X| + \aleph_0$. The universal mapping property is verified by extending any function $f: X \to B$ recursively over the construction.
