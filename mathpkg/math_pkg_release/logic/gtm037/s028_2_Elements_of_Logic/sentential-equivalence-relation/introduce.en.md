For a sentential language $\mathcal{P}$ and a set of sentences $\Gamma$, two sentences $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are equivalent modulo $\Gamma$ if $\Gamma$ proves their biconditional $\varphi \leftrightarrow \psi$. This equivalence relation is the foundation of the Lindenbaum algebra construction: the quotient of the set of sentences by $\equiv_{\Gamma}$ yields a Boolean algebra whose operations are induced by the logical connectives.
