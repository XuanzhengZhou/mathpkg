Exercise 9.69 from Chapter 9 of Monk's Mathematical Logic, introducing the Stone space of a Boolean algebra. The Stone space $F\mathfrak{A}$ is the set of all ultrafilters on $\mathfrak{A}$, topologized by taking the sets $\{F : x \in F\}$ as a basis. This yields a compact Hausdorff totally disconnected space. The construction is the object part of Stone duality, which establishes a contravariant equivalence between the category of Boolean algebras and the category of Stone spaces (compact Hausdorff totally disconnected spaces).
