A cylindric field of sets provides the concrete, set-theoretic model for cylindric algebras, much as fields of sets provide concrete models for Boolean algebras. For a nonempty set $U$ and ordinal $\alpha$, one considers subsets of the $\alpha$-dimensional Cartesian power ${}^\alpha U$. The operation $C_\kappa$ extends a set $X$ parallel to the $\kappa$-th coordinate axis, producing a generalized cylinder, while $D_{\kappa\lambda}$ picks out those $\alpha$-sequences whose $\kappa$-th and $\lambda$-th coordinates are equal, forming a diagonal hyperplane. A cylindric set algebra of dimension $\alpha$ is the algebraic structure consisting of an $\alpha$-dimensional cylindric field of sets together with the natural Boolean and cylindrification operations.
