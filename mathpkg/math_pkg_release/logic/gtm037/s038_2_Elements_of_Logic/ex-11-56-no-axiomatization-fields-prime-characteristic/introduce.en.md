Exercise 11.56 asks to show that the class of fields of prime characteristic is not finitely axiomatizable in first-order logic. The proof uses the compactness theorem: any set of sentences true in all fields of prime characteristic would also be true in some field of characteristic zero, by a compactness argument adding sentences $p \neq 0$ for each prime $p$.
