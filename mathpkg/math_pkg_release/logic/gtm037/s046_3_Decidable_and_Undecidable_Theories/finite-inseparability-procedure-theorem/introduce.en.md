This theorem links the notion of finite inseparability (from Definition 6.21) with essential undecidability. If a theory $\Gamma$ is such that its set of theorems and the set of sentences whose negations hold in finite models are effectively inseparable, then there is a mechanical procedure $P$ that, given any consistent finitely axiomatizable extension $\Delta$ of the "finite-model theory" of $\Gamma$, produces a concrete independent sentence -- one that is not in $\Delta$ but true in all finite models of $\Delta$. This provides a uniform method for demonstrating the incompleteness of such extensions.
