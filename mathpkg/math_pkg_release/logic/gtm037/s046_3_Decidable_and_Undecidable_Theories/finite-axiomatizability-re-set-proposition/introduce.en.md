Proposition (*) states that for any finitely axiomatizable theory $\Gamma$, one can effectively enumerate all sentences whose negations are satisfied in some finite model of $\Gamma$. The enumeration proceeds by systematically listing all finite $\mathcal{L}$-structures (up to isomorphism), checking which are models of $\Gamma$ (which is decidable in finitely many steps since $\Gamma$ is finitely axiomatizable), and then evaluating sentences in those models. This result connects finite axiomatizability with recursive enumerability and plays a key role in relating finite inseparability to essential undecidability.
