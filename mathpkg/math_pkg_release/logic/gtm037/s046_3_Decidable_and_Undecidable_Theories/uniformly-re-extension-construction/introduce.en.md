The uniformly r.e. extension construction defines a family of complete theories $\Theta_m$ that uniformly extend $\Gamma$. Using a binary recursive function $f(m,n)$ and an enumeration $k$ of all sentences, each $\Delta_m$ is built by iterating through all sentences and adding those consistent with the growing set relative to $\Gamma$. The $\Theta_m$ are the deductive closures of the $\Delta_m$. This construction ensures that $\Gamma$ equals the intersection of the $\Theta_m$ and that their Gödel number sets are uniformly recursively enumerable, a key step in characterizing the decidability of $\Gamma$.
