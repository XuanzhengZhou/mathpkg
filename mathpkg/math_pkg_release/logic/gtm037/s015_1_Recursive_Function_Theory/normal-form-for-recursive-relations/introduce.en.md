The Normal Form Theorem for recursive relations establishes that existential and universal quantifications over recursive relations can be expressed in a canonical form using Kleene's $T$-predicate. For any $m$-ary recursive relation $R$, there exist indices $e$ and $e'$ such that $\exists y R(\vec{x}, y)$ is equivalent to $\exists y \, T_{m-1}(e, \vec{x}, y)$, and dually $\forall y R(\vec{x}, y)$ is equivalent to $\forall y \, T_{m-1}(e', \vec{x}, y)$. This theorem serves as the foundation for the arithmetical hierarchy, allowing recursive relations to be classified according to the quantifier complexity of their definitions.
