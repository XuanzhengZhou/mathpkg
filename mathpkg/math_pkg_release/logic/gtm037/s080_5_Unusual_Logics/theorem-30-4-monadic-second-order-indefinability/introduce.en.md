Theorem 30.4 is the monadic second-order analogue of Theorem 30.1. It states that the set of Godel numbers of sentences valid in the unique model $Q^m$ is not elementarily definable in standard arithmetic, and consequently the set of all monadic second-order validities is not recursively enumerable. The proof follows the same pattern as Theorem 30.1, using the categoricity of $Q^m$ together with the first-order undefinability results. This definitively shows that no complete, effective deductive system exists for monadic second-order logic.
