This lemma, analogous to Lemma 19.16 for first-order logic, is the crucial technical step needed to carry over the downward Lowenheim--Skolem theorem to weak second-order logic. The construction of $D_{m+1}$ from $D_m$ is extended to handle existential quantification over finite-set variables: whenever $\mathfrak{B} \models \exists F_i \varphi$, a finite subset $H$ witnessing the existential is included as a Skolem witness. With this modification, the cardinality bound argument from the first-order proof goes through unchanged, yielding the downward Lowenheim--Skolem theorem for weak second-order logic despite the failure of its upward counterpart.
