Let $\mathcal{L}$ be a relational language without equality. An $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ is \textbf{primitive} if there is no congruence on $\mathfrak{A}$ except the identity. Show that if $|A| > 1$ and $\mathfrak{A}$ is elementarily equivalent (without equality) to a one-element $\mathcal{L}$-structure, then $\mathfrak{A}$ is not primitive.
