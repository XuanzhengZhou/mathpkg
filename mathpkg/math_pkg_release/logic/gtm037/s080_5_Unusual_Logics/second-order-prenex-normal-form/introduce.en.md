Theorem 30.6 provides a very specific prenex normal form for second-order logic: all second-order quantifiers can be moved to the front, followed by all universal first-order quantifiers, then all existential first-order quantifiers. This is achieved by using various inter-change principles for quantifiers (e.g., swapping $\exists v_i$ with $\exists R_k^j$, or replacing $\forall v_i \exists R_k^j$ by $\exists R_k^{j+1} \forall v_i$ with an appropriate transformation).
