Theorem 30.6 establishes that every formula of full second-order logic is logically equivalent to one in a special prenex normal form where all second-order quantifiers come first (in any order of $\forall$ and $\exists$), followed by blocks of universal and existential individual quantifiers, with a quantifier-free matrix. The proof uses six quantifier-interchange equivalences (1)-(6) that allow shifting relation quantifiers to the left and individual existential quantifiers to the right. This normal form is technically useful because it separates the second-order and first-order quantification, simplifying subsequent metatheoretic arguments.
