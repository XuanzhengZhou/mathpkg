Weak second-order logic augments first-order logic with variables ranging over finite subsets of the universe. This logic is significantly stronger than first-order logic: the compactness theorem fails, and the upward Löwenheim-Skolem theorem does not hold. However, the downward Löwenheim-Skolem theorem survives, and there are interesting decidability results for restricted theories.
