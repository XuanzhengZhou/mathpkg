In \textbf{monadic second-order logic} we strengthen a first-order language by adjoining variables $P_0, P_1, \ldots$ which are to range over all subsets of a given universe. The negative facts mentioned above for weak second-order logic (failure of compactness, upward Lowenheim--Skolem, recursive enumerability of validities) still hold for the monadic case.

Let $Q^m$ consist of the theory $Q$ together with the monadic second-order induction axiom:
$$\forall P_0 [P_0 \mathbf{0} \wedge \forall v_0 (P_0 v_0 \rightarrow P_0 \mathbf{s} v_0) \rightarrow \forall v_0 P_0 v_0].$$

Then up to isomorphism $(\omega, +, \cdot, s, 0)$ is the only model of $Q^m$.
