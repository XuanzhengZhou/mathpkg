$Q^m$ consists of the theory $Q$ (Robinson arithmetic) together with the monadic second-order induction axiom:
$$\forall P_0 [P_0 \mathbf{0} \wedge \forall v_0 (P_0 v_0 \rightarrow P_0 \mathbf{s} v_0) \rightarrow \forall v_0 P_0 v_0].$$

Up to isomorphism $(\omega, +, \cdot, s, 0)$ is the only model of $Q^m$. Hence the upward Lowenheim--Skolem theorem fails for monadic second-order logic.
