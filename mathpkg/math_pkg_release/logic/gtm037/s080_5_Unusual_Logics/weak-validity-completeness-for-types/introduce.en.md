Although full second-order validity is not recursively enumerable, a weakened notion of validity (truth in all structures with all frames) admits a complete proof theory. This theorem, due to Henkin, shows that by generalizing the notion of structure to include non-standard ("general") models via frames, one recovers both compactness and recursive enumerability of validities, making type theory amenable to proof-theoretic treatment.
