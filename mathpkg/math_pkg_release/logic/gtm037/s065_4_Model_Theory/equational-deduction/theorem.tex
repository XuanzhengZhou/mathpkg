Let $\Gamma$ be a set of equations in an algebraic language. Then $\Gamma\text{-eqthm}$ is the intersection of all sets $\Delta$ of equations such that:
(i) $\Gamma \subseteq \Delta$;
(ii) $v_0 = v_0 \in \Delta$;
(iii) if $\varphi \in \Delta$, $i < \omega$, $\sigma$ is a term, and $\psi$ is obtained from $\varphi$ by replacing $v_i$ throughout $\varphi$ by $\sigma$, then $\psi \in \Delta$;
(iv) if $\sigma = \tau \in \Delta$ and $\tau = \rho \in \Delta$, then $\sigma = \rho \in \Delta$.
