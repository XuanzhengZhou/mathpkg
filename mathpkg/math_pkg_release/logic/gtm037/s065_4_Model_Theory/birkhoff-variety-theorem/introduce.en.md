Birkhoff's Variety Theorem (Theorem 24.22) is a cornerstone of universal algebra: a class $K$ of algebras is a variety (equationally definable) if and only if it is closed under homomorphic images, substructures, and direct products ($K = \mathbf{HSP}K$). This establishes the equivalence between syntactic definability by equations and semantic closure under fundamental algebraic constructions.
