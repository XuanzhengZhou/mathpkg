Corollary 25.5 is an important consequence of Lós's theorem: if $\mathbf{K}$ is an elementary class in an expanded language, then the class of substructures of reducts is a universal class. This yields many natural examples of universal classes, such as the class of semigroups embeddable in groups, or rings embeddable in fields.
