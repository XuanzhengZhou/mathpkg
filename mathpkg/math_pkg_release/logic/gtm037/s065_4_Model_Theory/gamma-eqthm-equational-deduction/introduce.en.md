$\Gamma$-eqthm provides a purely proof-theoretic characterization of equational consequence. Unlike the semantic definition $\Gamma \models \sigma = \tau$, this definition references only syntactic closure conditions: containing $\Gamma$, reflexivity of equality, substitution of terms for variables, and transitivity. The completeness theorem for equational logic establishes that $\Gamma \models \sigma = \tau$ iff $\sigma = \tau \in \Gamma$-eqthm.
