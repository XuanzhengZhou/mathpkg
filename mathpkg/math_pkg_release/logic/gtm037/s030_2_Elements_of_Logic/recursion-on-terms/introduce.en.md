This proposition establishes the principle of definition by recursion on the set of terms. Given a base mapping $f$ on the individual variables (the values of the variable function $v$) and a family of combining functions $g_O$ for each operation symbol $O$, there exists a unique function $h$ defined on all terms that behaves correctly on variables and respects the inductive construction of compound terms. This is the term-level analogue of the familiar recursion theorem for natural numbers, adapted to the syntactic algebra of first-order languages.
