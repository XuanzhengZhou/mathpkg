This proposition gives the equivalent sequential characterization of the deductive consequence relation $\Gamma \vdash \varphi$. A formula $\varphi$ is derivable from hypotheses $\Gamma$ exactly when there exists a finite sequence of formulas ending with $\varphi$ where each formula is either a logical axiom, a hypothesis from $\Gamma$, obtained by modus ponens from two earlier formulas, or obtained by universal generalization from an earlier formula. This definition of a formal proof (or $\Gamma$-proof) is the mathematically rigorous formulation of the intuitive notion of a mathematical proof.
