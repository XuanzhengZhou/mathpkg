This definition introduces the most basic building blocks of the first-order language: atomic formulas. There are two kinds: equality formulas $\sigma = \tau$ between terms, and relational formulas $\langle R \rangle \sigma_0 \cdots \sigma_{m-1}$ applying a predicate symbol $R$ to a tuple of terms. The full set $\operatorname{Fmla}$ of all formulas is then defined as the inductive closure of the atomic formulas under the logical connectives $\neg$, $\vee$, $\wedge$ and universal quantification $\forall \alpha$.
