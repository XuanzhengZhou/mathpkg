An expression of the form $\sigma = \tau$, where $\sigma$ and $\tau$ are terms, is called an \textbf{atomic equality formula}. If $R \in \operatorname{Dmn} \mathcal{R}$, say $\mathcal{R}R = m$, and $\sigma_0, \ldots, \sigma_{m-1}$ are terms, then $\langle R \rangle \sigma_0 \cdots \sigma_{m-1}$ is an \textbf{atomic nonequality formula}. These two kinds of expressions together constitute the \textbf{atomic formulas}.

The set of formulas, $\operatorname{Fmla}$, is the intersection of all sets $\Gamma$ of expressions such that each atomic formula is in $\Gamma$ and $\Gamma$ is closed under all the operations $\neg, \vee, \wedge, \forall \alpha$ (for each individual variable $\alpha$).
