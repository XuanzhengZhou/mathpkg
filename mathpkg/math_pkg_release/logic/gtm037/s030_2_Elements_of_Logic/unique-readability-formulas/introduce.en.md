This proposition establishes two fundamental facts about first-order formulas. First, the set of Godel numbers of formulas is recursive -- there is an effective procedure to decide whether a given expression is a formula. Second, it provides the unique readability theorem for formulas: every formula decomposes uniquely into one of six possible forms (atomic equality, atomic relation, negation, disjunction, conjunction, or universal quantification), and no proper initial segment of a formula is itself a formula. This is the formula-level analogue of the unique readability theorem for terms.
