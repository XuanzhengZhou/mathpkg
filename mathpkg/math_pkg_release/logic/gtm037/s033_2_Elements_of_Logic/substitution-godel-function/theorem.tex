If $x$ is the Godel number of a formula $\varphi$, $m \in \omega$, and $y_0, \dots, y_{m-1}$ are Godel numbers of terms $\sigma_0, \dots, \sigma_{m-1}$ respectively, let

$$s(x, y_0, \dots, y_{m-1}) = g^{+}(\varphi(\sigma_0, \dots, \sigma_{m-1})),$$

where $g^{+}$ denotes the Godel numbering function. If $x$ and $y_0, \dots, y_{m-1}$ do not satisfy these conditions, let $s(x, y_0, \dots, y_{m-1}) = 0$. Then $s$ is a recursive function.
