A quantifier-free formula is a formula in which the quantifier symbol $L_3$ (representing $\forall$ or $\exists$ in the formal language) does not appear. Such formulas are built solely from atomic formulas using propositional connectives like negation, conjunction, and disjunction. Quantifier-free formulas form the syntactic building blocks of prenex normal forms: every formula can be equivalently expressed as a string of quantifiers followed by a quantifier-free matrix. They also play an important role in quantifier elimination procedures and decision problems in mathematical logic.
