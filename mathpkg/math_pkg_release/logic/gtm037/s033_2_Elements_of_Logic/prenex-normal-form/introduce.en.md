A formula is in prenex normal form when all its quantifiers appear at the front (the prefix), followed by a quantifier-free formula (the matrix). Formally, $\psi = Q_0\alpha_0\cdots Q_{m-1}\alpha_{m-1}\varphi$ where each $Q_i$ is either $\forall$ or $\exists$, each $\alpha_i$ is a variable, and $\varphi$ contains no quantifiers. The prefix encodes the quantifier structure, while the matrix captures the propositional content. The Prenex Normal Form Theorem (10.81) guarantees that every formula is provably equivalent to one in prenex normal form that preserves the free-variable occurrences.
