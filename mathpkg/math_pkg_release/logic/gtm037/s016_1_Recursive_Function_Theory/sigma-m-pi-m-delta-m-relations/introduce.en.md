The arithmetic hierarchy classifies $n$-ary relations on $\omega$ according to the complexity of their defining formulas in the language of arithmetic. The classes $\Sigma_m$ and $\Pi_m$ are defined inductively based on the number of alternating quantifiers, starting from recursive relations at the base level. The class $\Delta_m$ is defined as the intersection $\Sigma_m \cap \Pi_m$, consisting of relations that are both $\Sigma_m$ and $\Pi_m$. These classes form the foundation for studying the fine-grained structure of undecidable and non-recursive relations in computability theory.
