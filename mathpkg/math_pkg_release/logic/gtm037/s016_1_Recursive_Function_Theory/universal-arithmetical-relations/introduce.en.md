Theorem 5.35 establishes the existence of universal relations $R_m^n$ for the arithmetic hierarchy. For each level $m$ and each arity $n$, there is an $(n+1)$-ary $\Sigma_m$ relation that parametrizes all $n$-ary $\Sigma_m$ relations in the sense that every such relation is obtained by fixing the first coordinate. These universal relations are the key tool for proving the Hierarchy Theorem (5.36) via diagonalization, analogous to the universal Turing machine in computability theory. Their construction proceeds by induction on $m$ using the properties of the classes established in earlier propositions.
