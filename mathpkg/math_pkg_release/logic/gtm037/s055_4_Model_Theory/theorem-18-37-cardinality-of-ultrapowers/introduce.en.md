This theorem establishes a lower bound on the cardinality of an ultrapower when the ultrafilter is regular. For any infinite set $A$ and infinite index set $I$ with a regular ultrafilter $F$ over $I$, the ultrapower $^I A / F$ has cardinality at least $2^{|I|}$. This shows that regular ultrafilters produce large ultrapowers, and is key to constructing arbitrarily large elementary extensions of infinite structures.
