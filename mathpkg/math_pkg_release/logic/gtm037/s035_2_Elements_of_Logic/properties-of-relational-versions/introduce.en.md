This theorem establishes the fundamental properties of the relational version construction. Part (i) states that formulas already free of operation symbols are unchanged by the translation — the translation is the identity on the purely relational fragment. Part (ii) asserts a semantic correspondence: an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{A}$ and its relational version $\mathfrak{A}'$ satisfy the same formulas in the sense that $\varphi$ holds in $\mathfrak{A}$ under assignment $x$ precisely when the translated formula $\varphi'$ holds in $\mathfrak{A}'$ under the same assignment. This justifies the standard model-theoretic practice of assuming, without loss of generality, that languages are purely relational: any first-order theory can be translated into an equivalent theory in a relational language.
