By the method of proof of Theorem 13.5 we see that it suffices to describe a method for determining the truth in $\mathfrak{A}$ of basic sentences. The basic sentences are easily seen to be effectively equivalent to sentences of the forms

$$\mathbf{m} = \mathbf{n},$$

$$\mathbf{m} < \mathbf{n},$$

$$\mathbf{m} \equiv_n \mathbf{p},$$

which are true in $\mathfrak{A}$ if and only if, respectively, $m = n$, $m < n$, or $m \equiv_n p$ holds in the standard integers. Since there is an effective decision method for determining each of these three questions (equality, comparison, and modular arithmetic of natural numbers), there is a decision procedure for $\Gamma_2$.
