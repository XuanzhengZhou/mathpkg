The pure theory of equality, denoted $\Gamma_3$, is the set of all logically valid sentences in a first-order language that contains no nonlogical constants (no function symbols, no relation symbols other than equality). Unlike the theories $\Gamma_1$ and $\Gamma_2$ studied earlier, $\Gamma_3$ is not complete — for example, the sentence asserting "there are at least two distinct elements" is true in some structures but false in others, so neither it nor its negation is in $\Gamma_3$. The study of $\Gamma_3$ illustrates quantifier elimination in the simplest possible setting, where the only atomic formulas are equalities between variables.
