This lemma establishes quantifier elimination for the pure theory of equality $\Gamma_3$. It shows that every formula in a language with no nonlogical constants is equivalent to a Boolean combination of the sentences $\varepsilon_k$, which assert "there exist at least $k$ distinct elements." The key step reduces an existentially quantified formula $\exists\alpha$ where $\alpha$ is required to be distinct from a set of variables $\beta_i$ to a conjunction of implications: for any subset $J$ of those variables that are pairwise distinct, the universe must contain at least $|J|+1$ elements. This gives a complete classification of definable sets in the pure theory of equality.
