Exercise 30.22 asks to construct a counterexample demonstrating that $\omega$-logic is not compact: a set $\Gamma$ of sentences where every finite subset has an $\omega$-model but the whole set $\Gamma$ has no $\omega$-model. This is an analog of the failure of compactness in second-order logic.
