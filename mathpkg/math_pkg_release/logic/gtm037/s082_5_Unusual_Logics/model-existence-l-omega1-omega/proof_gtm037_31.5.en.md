The proof proceeds by a Henkin-style construction. Using Lemma 31.2, we may assume $S$ satisfies (C0)-(C9), (C3'), and (C4'). Since $L$ has only countably many nonlogical constants and $L'$ adds only countably many new constants, the set of all sentences of $L'$ is countable.

We extend $\Gamma$ to a maximal set $\Gamma^*$ in $S$ by enumerating all sentences and applying conditions (C3') and (C4') at each step to handle countable conjunctions and disjunctions. The term model constructed from $\Gamma^*$ (using the equivalence relation $c \sim d$ iff $c = d \in \Gamma^*$) then satisfies all sentences in $\Gamma^*$, hence in $\Gamma$.
