A $\mathcal{Q}$-language extends a first-order language $\mathcal{L}$ with a new quantifier symbol $\mathbf{Q}$, used syntactically like $\forall$. The formula $\mathbf{Q}v_i\varphi$ is well-formed whenever $\varphi$ is. The symbol $\mathbf{Q}$ can be given various semantic interpretations, most notably cardinality interpretations where $\mathbf{Q}v_i$ means "there are at least $m$ elements $v_i$ such that."
