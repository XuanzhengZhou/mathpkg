The two preceding lemmas give us this result. Lemma 31.6 shows that a strongly compact cardinal is a strong limit cardinal. A separate lemma (Lemma 31.7, sketched in the text) shows that a strongly compact cardinal is regular (using a well-ordering argument with an $L_{mm}$-theory). A cardinal that is both regular and a strong limit cardinal is by definition strongly inaccessible. Thus every strongly compact cardinal is strongly inaccessible.

Recall that the existence of strongly inaccessible uncountable cardinals is not provable in ordinary set theory. Thus it is not provable that any of the languages $L_{mm}$ for $m > \aleph_0$ are $(m, \infty)$-compact.
