This theorem provides multiple equivalent characterizations of strongly compact cardinals, connecting logic (compactness of $L_{mm}$), algebra (ultrafilters in Boolean algebras), set theory (extending $m$-complete filters), and topology (products of $(m, \infty)$-compact spaces). These equivalences demonstrate the deep unity between apparently disparate areas of mathematics at the level of large cardinals.
