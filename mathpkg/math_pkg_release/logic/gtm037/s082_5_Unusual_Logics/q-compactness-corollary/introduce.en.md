Corollary 30.14 derives a compactness theorem for $Q$-languages from Fuhrken's ultraproduct theorem. In the $m$-interpretation, any $Q$-language is $(\aleph_0, \pi m)$-compact: countable sets of sentences that are finitely satisfiable have a model. For the $(\exp \aleph_0)^+$-interpretation, this yields $(\aleph_0, \aleph_1)$-compactness. Notably, the $\aleph_1$-interpretation is also $(\aleph_0, \aleph_1)$-compact (Fuhrken), and the validities are recursively enumerable (Vaught, Keisler).
