Hanf's theorem reveals a deep structural property of weakly compact cardinals: below any uncountable weakly compact cardinal, there exists an uncountable weakly inaccessible cardinal that is not a power of $2$. The proof uses an intricate $L_{mm}$-sentence construction encoding well-orderings, functions, and cardinal arithmetic.
