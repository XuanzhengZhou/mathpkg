# Proposition 29.9: Satisfaction Equivalence under Equality

This proposition states that for a relational first-order language $L$ with equality, satisfaction of a sentence $\varphi$ in an $L$-structure $A$ is equivalent to satisfaction in the expanded structure $(A, =)$ where equality is interpreted as the actual identity relation on $A$. It follows directly from the definition of satisfaction and establishes the fundamental compatibility between $L$-semantics and $L^{e}$-semantics (the language where equality is treated as an additional binary relation symbol).
