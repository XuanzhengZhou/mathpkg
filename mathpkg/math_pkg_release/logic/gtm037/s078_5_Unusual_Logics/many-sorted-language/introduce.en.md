Many-sorted logic generalizes ordinary first-order logic by allowing multiple universes (sorts) instead of a single one. Each variable is assigned a sort, and relation/operation symbols have ranks that specify the sorts of their arguments and (for operations) the sort of the result. This formalism is essential for formalizing mathematics with multiple types of objects, such as vector spaces (scalars and vectors) or geometry (points and lines).
