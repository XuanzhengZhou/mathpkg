A descriptive triple extends a first-order language with a description operator $\tau$ and a distinguished constant $\mathbf{O}$. The term $\tau v_i \varphi$ is interpreted as "the unique $v_i$ such that $\varphi$, or $\mathbf{O}$ if there is no such $v_i$ or several such $v_i$." This formalizes definite descriptions in the style of Russell and is particularly useful in axiomatic set theory where class terms $\{x : \varphi(x)\}$ are naturally interpreted via $\tau$.
