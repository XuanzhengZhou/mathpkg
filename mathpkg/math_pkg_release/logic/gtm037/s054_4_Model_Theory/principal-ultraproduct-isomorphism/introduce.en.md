On a finite index set, every ultrafilter is principal—generated by a singleton. This means that ultraproducts over finite index sets are trivial: they are isomorphic to one of the factor structures. Nontrivial ultraproduct constructions therefore require infinite index sets, where nonprincipal (free) ultrafilters exist (assuming the Axiom of Choice). This proposition explains why the study of ultraproducts in model theory focuses on infinite index sets.
