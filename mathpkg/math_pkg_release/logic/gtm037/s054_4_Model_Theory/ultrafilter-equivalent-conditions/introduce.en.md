This proposition gives two useful characterizations of ultrafilters over a set $I$. Condition (ii) states that an ultrafilter is "prime": if a union belongs to the ultrafilter, then one of the components must belong. Condition (iii) states that an ultrafilter is maximal among proper filters: any strictly larger filter must be improper (contain the empty set). These equivalences are fundamental for working with ultrafilters in model-theoretic constructions such as ultraproducts.
