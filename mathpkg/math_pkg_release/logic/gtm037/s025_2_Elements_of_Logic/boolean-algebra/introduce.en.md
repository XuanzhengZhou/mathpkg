A Boolean algebra is an algebraic abstraction of a field of sets. The operations $+$, $\cdot$, $-$ correspond respectively to union $\cup$, intersection $\cap$, and complement $\sim$ (relative to the universal set), while $0$ and $1$ correspond to the empty set and the universal set. The axioms capture the essential algebraic identities satisfied by set-theoretic operations: commutativity, associativity, absorption, distributivity, and complementation.
