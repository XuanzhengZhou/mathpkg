The pure theory of equality $\Gamma_3$ is not complete — for example, the sentence asserting "there are at least 2 elements" is independent of $\Gamma_3$. Theorem 13.14 classifies all complete and consistent extensions of $\Gamma_3$: for each finite cardinality $m \geq 2$, there is the theory $\Gamma_3^m$ of sets of size exactly $m$, and there is $\Gamma_3^\infty$, the theory of infinite sets. Each of these is complete, consistent, and decidable, and together they exhaust all possibilities. This result provides a complete understanding of the Lindenbaum algebra of $\Gamma_3$.
