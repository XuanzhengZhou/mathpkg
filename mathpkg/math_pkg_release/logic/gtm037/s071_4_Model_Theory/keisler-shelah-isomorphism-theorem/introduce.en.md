The Keisler–Shelah Isomorphism Theorem (Theorem 26.42) is one of the deepest results in model theory: two structures are elementarily equivalent if and only if they have isomorphic ultrapowers (over the same index set and ultrafilter). This provides a purely algebraic characterization of the fundamental logical notion of elementary equivalence. The proof is a tour de force of combinatorial set theory and ultrapower construction.
