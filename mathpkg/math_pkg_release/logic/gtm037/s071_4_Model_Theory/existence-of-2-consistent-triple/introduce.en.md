This lemma constructs the initial triple for the transfinite induction in the proof of the Keisler-Shelah theorem. It shows that one can start with a family $F$ of $2^n$ one-one functions from $n$ into $n^{\partial}$, with empty $G$ and the trivial filter $\{n\}$, so that the triple is $2$-consistent over $n$. The maximal size $|F| = 2^n$ ensures enough room for the subsequent reduction steps where $F$ is gradually shrunk while $D$ is enlarged.
