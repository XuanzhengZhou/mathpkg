The proof is elementary and follows directly from the definition of the generated filter $\mathcal{F}_g(K)$. If $X \in \mathcal{F}_g(K)$, then by definition $X$ belongs to the intersection of all filters containing $K$. One shows that the collection of all supersets of finite intersections from $K$ forms a filter containing $K$, and is therefore the smallest such filter. Conversely, if $X$ contains $Y_0 \cap \cdots \cap Y_{m-1}$ for some $Y_i \in K$, then $X$ must belong to every filter containing $K$, hence $X \in \mathcal{F}_g(K)$.
