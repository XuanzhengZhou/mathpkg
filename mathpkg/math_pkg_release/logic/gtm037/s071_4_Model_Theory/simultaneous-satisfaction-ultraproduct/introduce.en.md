Lemma 26.41 is the crucial lemma linking the combinatorial $m$-consistency framework to actual formulas in the ultraproduct construction. Condition (i) ensures the system of formulas $\varphi_\nu$ is essentially closed under conjunction, while condition (ii) ensures each formula is satisfied $D$-almost everywhere by some element. The lemma then guarantees the existence of a simultaneous choice of witnesses $b_\delta$ such that all formulas $\varphi_\nu$ are satisfied $D$-almost everywhere by the same elements. This enables the back-and-forth construction of the isomorphism in the Keisler-Shelah theorem.
