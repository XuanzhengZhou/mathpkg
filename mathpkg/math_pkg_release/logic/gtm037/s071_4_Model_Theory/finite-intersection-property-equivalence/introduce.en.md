Proposition 26.31 connects the finite intersection property (FIP) of a collection $K$ of subsets with the filter it generates. It states that $K$ has the FIP exactly when the empty set is not in the generated filter $\mathcal{F}_g(K)$. This equivalence is fundamental for understanding when a collection of sets can be extended to a proper filter.
