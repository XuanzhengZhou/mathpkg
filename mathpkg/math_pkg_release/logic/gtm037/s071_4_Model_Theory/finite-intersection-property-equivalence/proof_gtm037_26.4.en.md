(i) $\Rightarrow$ (ii): Suppose $K$ has the finite intersection property, and assume for contradiction that $0 \in \mathcal{F}_g(K)$. By Proposition 26.29, there exist finitely many $Y_0, \ldots, Y_{m-1} \in K$ such that $Y_0 \cap \cdots \cap Y_{m-1} \subseteq 0$, i.e., $Y_0 \cap \cdots \cap Y_{m-1} = 0$. This contradicts the finite intersection property. Hence $0 \notin \mathcal{F}_g(K)$.

(ii) $\Rightarrow$ (i): If $0 \notin \mathcal{F}_g(K)$, then any finite intersection of members of $K$ must be nonempty, for if some finite intersection were empty, then by Proposition 26.29 the empty set would belong to $\mathcal{F}_g(K)$. Thus $K$ has the finite intersection property.
