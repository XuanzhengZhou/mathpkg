Lemma 26.40 generalizes the ability to extend the filter $D$ toward an ultrafilter within the $m$-consistency framework. Given an $m$-consistent triple and up to $m$ subsets $\Gamma_\alpha$ of $n$, one can remove at most $m$ elements from $F$ and extend $D$ to $D'$ such that the new triple remains $m$-consistent and each $\Gamma_\alpha$ or its complement belongs to $D'$. This is the key step that ensures the final filter in the Keisler-Shelah construction is an ultrafilter.
