Let $G = \{g_0, \ldots, g_n\}$ where each $g_i$ is one-one. Suppose there is no $F'$ of the kind indicated. Then one defines by induction sequences $\rho$, $f$, $\chi$, $q$, $h$, $k$ of length $m^+$ that violate the $m$-consistency condition. The construction produces elements witnessing the failure for every candidate $F'$, eventually exhausting the available functions and yielding a contradiction through cardinality considerations.
