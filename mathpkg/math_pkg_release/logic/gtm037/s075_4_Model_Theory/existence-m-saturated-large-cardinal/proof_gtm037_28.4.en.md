The proof of Theorem 28.11 builds on Theorems 28.12 and 28.13 (stated later in the text) and uses the fact that when $m > |\mathrm{Fmla}_{\mathcal{L}}|$, the types over small parameter sets are more manageable. The proof strategy is similar to that of Theorem 28.12: one constructs an elementary chain of length $m^+$ where at each step new types are realized, using the compactness theorem. An alternative approach via ultraproducts is also possible (see Exercise 28.39).

Under GCH, Corollary 28.14 follows: every theory in $\mathcal{L}$ with infinite models has a saturated model of each regular cardinality $|\mathrm{Fmla}_{\mathcal{L}}|$.
