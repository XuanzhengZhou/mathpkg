Assuming the Generalized Continuum Hypothesis (GCH), this corollary guarantees that every theory with infinite models possesses a saturated model at every regular cardinality equal to the size of its language. This follows from Theorem 28.13. The result is significant because saturated models are unique up to isomorphism for a given cardinality, providing canonical models for complete theories. Alternative proofs not relying on GCH can be given via universal and homogeneous structures (see Definition 28.15 and subsequent discussion).
