This definition introduces the model-theoretic analogues of the algebraic notions of universal and homogeneous structures. An $m$-universal structure elementarily embeds all elementarily equivalent structures of size $< m$. An $m$-homogeneous structure has the property that any partial elementary map of size $< m$ can be extended by one additional element while preserving elementary equivalence. These notions, together with Lemma (*), provide an alternative, more conceptual route to constructing saturated models without relying on the full machinery of Theorems 28.12 and 28.13.
