Let $m \in \omega$. Each $m$-type over $\Gamma$ is realized in some countable model of $\Gamma$, by Theorem 27.2. Since $\Gamma$ has up to isomorphism only countably many countable models, there are only countably many $m$-types over $\Gamma$ (cf.\ the proof of $(i) \Rightarrow (ii)$ in Theorem 28.7). Thus for each $m \in \omega$, there are only countably many $m$-types over $\Gamma$. Hence by Theorem 28.7, $\Gamma$ has a countable $\aleph_0$-saturated model.
