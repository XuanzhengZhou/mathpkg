This corollary generalizes Corollary 28.8 by weakening the hypothesis from $\aleph_0$-categoricity to having only countably many countable models up to isomorphism. If a consistent theory in a countable language has at most countably many isomorphism types of countable models, it must possess a countable $\aleph_0$-saturated model. The proof uses Theorem 27.2 to bound the number of $m$-types and then applies Theorem 28.7. This result shows, for example, that the theory of algebraically closed fields of a fixed characteristic has an $\aleph_0$-saturated model.
