The theory of dense linear orders without endpoints is both complete and decidable. Completeness follows from the Łoś–Vaught test together with the fact that this theory is $\aleph_0$-categorical (any two countable dense linear orders without endpoints are isomorphic, by Cantor's back-and-forth argument). Decidability additionally requires that the theory is recursively axiomatizable, which is immediate since the axioms can be written down finitely.
