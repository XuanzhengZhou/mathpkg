For any field $F$, the theory of infinite vector spaces over $F$ is complete and decidable. This follows from the fact that any two vector spaces over $F$ of the same dimension are isomorphic, and for infinite vector spaces the dimension is determined by the cardinality of the space. Thus the theory is categorical in all powers strictly greater than $|F|$, and the Łoś–Vaught test applies. The axioms include the Abelian group axioms together with unary function symbols $s_a$ for scalar multiplication by each $a \in F$, satisfying the standard vector space axioms. *(Note: the source text for this theorem is partially truncated.)*
