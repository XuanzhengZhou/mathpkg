A first-order theory is categorical if it has essentially only one model — any two models are isomorphic. More generally, for a nonzero cardinal $m$, a theory is $m$-categorical if it has exactly one model (up to isomorphism) of cardinality $m$. This notion captures the idea that the axioms of the theory uniquely determine the structure of its models. Categoricity is closely tied to completeness: categorical theories are complete, and $m$-categoricity in sufficiently large powers provides a practical test for completeness.
