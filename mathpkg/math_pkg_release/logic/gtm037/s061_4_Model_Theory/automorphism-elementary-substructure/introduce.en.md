This corollary provides a sufficient condition for a substructure $\mathfrak{B}$ to be an elementary substructure of $\mathfrak{A}$, expressed in terms of automorphisms. If for every finite subset $X$ of $B$ and every element $a$ of $A$, there exists an automorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ that fixes $X$ pointwise and maps $a$ into $B$, then $\mathfrak{B}$ is an elementary substructure. This condition is often easier to verify than the Tarski–Vaught test in structures with rich automorphism groups.
