Definition 5.1 formalizes partial Turing computability: an $m$-ary partial function $f$ is partial Turing computable if there exists a Turing machine $M$ such that membership of a tuple in the domain of $f$ is equivalent to the existence of a computation of $M$ beginning with an appropriate initial configuration. This generalizes the earlier definition of Turing computability to partial functions, where the machine may fail to halt on inputs outside the domain.
