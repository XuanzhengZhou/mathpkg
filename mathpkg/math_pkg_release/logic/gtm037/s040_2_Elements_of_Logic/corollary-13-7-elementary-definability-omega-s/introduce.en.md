Corollary 13.7 provides a complete characterization of the subsets of $\omega$ that are elementarily definable in the structure $(\omega, s)$ (natural numbers with successor) or $(\omega, s, 0)$ (natural numbers with successor and zero). A set is first-order definable in these structures precisely when it is finite or cofinite. The forward direction uses the quantifier elimination result of Theorem 13.4 to reduce any defining formula to a Boolean combination of basic formulas, from which it follows that the defined set is built from singletons and complements using unions and intersections, hence finite or cofinite.
