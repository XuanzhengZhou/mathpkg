This definition introduces three progressively stronger notions of inseparability for pairs of disjoint sets of natural numbers. Recursive separability is the weakest: two sets are recursively separable if a recursive set can separate them. Recursive inseparability means no such recursive separator exists. Effective inseparability is the strongest notion, requiring a uniform effective procedure (via the recursive function $f$) that, given any pair of r.e. supersets of $A$ and $B$ respectively, produces a witness outside both supersets. Effectively inseparable pairs play a crucial role in establishing undecidability results.
