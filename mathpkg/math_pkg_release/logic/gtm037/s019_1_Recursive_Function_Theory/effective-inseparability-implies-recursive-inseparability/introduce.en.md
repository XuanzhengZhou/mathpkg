This simple theorem establishes the expected relationship between two notions of inseparability: effective inseparability implies recursive inseparability. If there exists a uniform effective procedure that produces witnesses outside any pair of disjoint r.e. supersets of $A$ and $B$, then certainly no recursive set can separate $A$ and $B$. The converse fails, as shown in Exercises 6.47 and 6.48.
