This theorem reveals an important structural property of productive sets: despite not being recursively enumerable themselves, every productive set necessarily contains an infinite recursive subset. In fact, the proof constructs an infinite r.e. subset of the productive set, which by earlier results (Theorem 6.8) suffices to guarantee an infinite recursive subset. This result is significant in the general theory of recursively enumerable sets.
