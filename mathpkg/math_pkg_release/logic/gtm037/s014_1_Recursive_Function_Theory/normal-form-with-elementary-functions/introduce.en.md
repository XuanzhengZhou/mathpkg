Corollary 5.10 makes the normal form more explicit: for any $m$-ary partial recursive function $h$, there exist elementary (i.e., primitive recursive) functions $f$ and $g$ and an index $e$ such that $h(\vec{x}) \simeq f(\mu u[g(e,\vec{x},u)=0])$. The proof sets $f = V$ (the output-extracting function) and $g = \overline{sg} \circ \chi_{T_m}$ (the characteristic function of the T-predicate), and the question of whether $f$ can be dropped is answered negatively by Exercise 5.43.
