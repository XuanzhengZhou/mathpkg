Every field of sets naturally gives rise to a Boolean algebra by interpreting the set-theoretic operations of union, intersection, and complement (relative to the universe) as the Boolean operations $+$, $\cdot$, and $-$ respectively. This construction demonstrates that the axioms of Boolean algebra faithfully capture the algebraic properties of sets, and it provides the canonical model for the theory of Boolean algebras.
