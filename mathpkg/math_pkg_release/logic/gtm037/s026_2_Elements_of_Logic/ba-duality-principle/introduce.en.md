The Boolean algebra axioms are symmetric with respect to interchanging $+$ with $\cdot$ and $0$ with $1$. This symmetry gives rise to the duality principle: swapping these symbols in any valid statement about all Boolean algebras yields another valid statement. The duality principle is a powerful tool that effectively halves the work needed to prove basic algebraic properties of Boolean algebras.
