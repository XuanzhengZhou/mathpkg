Theorem 3.7 establishes that the set of all recursive functions is countably infinite. The proof proceeds by constructing an increasing hierarchy of sets $A_n$, where $A_0$ consists of the basic functions (successor and projections), and $A_{n+1}$ is formed by applying one step of composition, primitive recursion, or minimalization to functions in $A_n$. Since each $A_n$ is countable and the countable union of countable sets remains countable, the set of all recursive functions has cardinality $\aleph_0$.
