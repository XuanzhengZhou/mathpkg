Although Theorem 3.8 follows from 3.7 purely on grounds of cardinality, we can also explicitly exhibit a nonrecursive function. Let $f_0, f_1, \ldots$ be an enumeration of all unary recursive functions (by 3.7). Define $g(m) = f_m(m) + 1$ for all $m \in \omega$. Then $g$ is obviously not in our enumeration, so $g$ is not recursive.
