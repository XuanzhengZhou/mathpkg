The Boolean Prime Ideal Theorem states that every proper ideal in a Boolean algebra can be extended to a maximal ideal. The proof uses Zorn's Lemma: consider the collection of all proper ideals extending $I$, ordered by inclusion. The union of any chain of proper ideals is again a proper ideal, providing an upper bound. Zorn's Lemma then guarantees a maximal element, which must be a maximal ideal. This theorem is a cornerstone of Boolean algebra theory, underpinning the Stone representation theorem and numerous applications in logic.
