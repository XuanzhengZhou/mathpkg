Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a finite Boolean algebra and let $x \in A$ with $x \neq 0$. If $x$ is an atom, we are done. Otherwise, there exists $y$ such that $0 < y < x$. If $y$ is an atom, we have an atom below $x$. Otherwise, there exists $z$ with $0 < z < y$. Since $A$ is finite, this descending chain must terminate, yielding an atom $a \leq x$. Thus $\mathfrak{A}$ is atomic.
