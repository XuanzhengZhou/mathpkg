The proof uses the equivalence from Theorem 31.15: condition (iv) states that in the field of all subsets of a set, any proper m-complete filter can be extended to an m-complete ultrafilter. Applying this to the Frechet filter (the filter of co-finite subsets, or more precisely its m-complete closure) on m itself yields an m-complete nonprincipal ultrafilter on m, which is exactly a witnessing measure for the measurability of m.
