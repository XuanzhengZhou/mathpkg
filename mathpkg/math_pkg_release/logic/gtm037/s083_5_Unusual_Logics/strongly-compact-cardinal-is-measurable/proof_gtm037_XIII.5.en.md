Recalling the proof of $(vi) \Rightarrow (ii)$ from the equivalences established earlier in this section:

Let $m$ be strongly compact. Consider the product space $Y = \prod_{i \in I} X_i$ with the $m$-topology. A base for the $m$-topology on $Y$ is the family of all sets of the form

$$\{f : f \upharpoonright J \in \bigcap_{i \in J} U_i\},$$

where $J$ is a subset of $I$ of power $< m$ and $U_i$ is open in $X_i$ for each $i \in J$.

To verify the required compactness property, take a family $B$ of basic open sets of $Y$ and assume that no subset of $B$ of power $< m$ covers $Y$. Let $K$ be the $m$-complete ideal in $SY$ generated by $B$. By construction, $K$ is a proper ideal. Hence by a previously established property (iv), there is an $m$-complete prime ideal $L$ in $SY$ which extends $K$.

For each $i \in I$, let

$$C_i = \{U : U \text{ is an open set in } X_i \text{ and } \{f \in Y : f_i \in U\} \in L\}.$$

Then each $C_i$ is an $m$-complete ultrafilter on the set of open sets of $X_i$. From this we obtain an $m$-complete ultrafilter on the Boolean algebra of subsets of $m$, establishing that $m$ is measurable.

The construction proceeds by defining a measure $\mu$ on $\mathcal{S}(m)$. For any $\Gamma \subseteq m$:

$$\mu\Gamma = 1 \quad \text{if there is a } \xi < 2^m \text{ such that } \Gamma = \{ \alpha < m : t_\alpha\xi = u\xi \},$$
$$\mu\Gamma = 0 \quad \text{otherwise.}$$

One verifies that $\mu$ satisfies the properties of an $m$-additive two-valued measure: $\mu(m) = 1$, $\mu(\emptyset) = 0$, $\mu(\{\alpha\}) = 0$ for each $\alpha < m$, and for any collection $Y$ of pairwise disjoint nonempty subsets of $m$ with $|Y| < m$, if $\mu(\bigcup_{y \in Y} y) = 1$ then $\mu(y) = 1$ for some $y \in Y$. The existence of such a measure on $m$ is equivalent to $m$ being measurable.
