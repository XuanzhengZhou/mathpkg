For each cardinal $\mathfrak{m}$ (finite or infinite) there is a Hanf number $h(\mathfrak{m})$; this is the smallest cardinal $\mathfrak{n}$ such that for any language $\mathcal{L}$ with $|\mathcal{L}| \leq \mathfrak{m}$ and any set $\Gamma$ of sentences of $\mathcal{L}$, if $\Gamma$ has a model of power $\geq \mathfrak{n}$, then $\Gamma$ has arbitrarily large models.
