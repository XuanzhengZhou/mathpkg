Proposition 19.33 establishes a fundamental connection between the elementary diagram of a structure and elementary embeddings. If a structure $(B, l_a)_{a \in A}$ satisfies the full elementary diagram of $A$ (in an $A$-expansion $L'$ of the language), then the mapping $l$ that sends each $a \in A$ to the interpretation of its corresponding constant $l_a$ is an elementary embedding. This is a key tool for constructing elementary extensions and ultrapowers in model theory.
