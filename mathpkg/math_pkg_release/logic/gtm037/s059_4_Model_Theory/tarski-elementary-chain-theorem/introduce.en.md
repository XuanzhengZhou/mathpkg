Theorem 19.36, known as Tarski's elementary chain theorem, is a fundamental result in model theory. It states that the union of a directed family of $L$-structures, where the direction is given by the elementary substructure relation $\preceq$, is an elementary extension of each member. The proof proceeds by induction on formulas: atomic formulas are handled by Proposition 19.35, propositional connectives are routine, and the quantifier step relies on the directedness of the family. This theorem is essential for constructing large elementary extensions via unions of chains.
