Trachtenbrot's Theorem states that every recursive function is spectrally representable, establishing the converse of Lemma 14.23 (which asserts that every spectrally representable function is recursive). Together, these results characterize the class of spectrally representable functions as exactly the recursive functions. The proof uses Julia Robinson's theorem (3.48) to show closure of the class of spectrally representable functions under addition, multiplication, generalized composition, and inversion.
