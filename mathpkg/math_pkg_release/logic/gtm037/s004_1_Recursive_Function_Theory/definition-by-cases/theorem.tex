Let $R_0, \ldots, R_{m-1}$ be pairwise disjoint $n$-ary $A$-relations with $\bigcup_{i < m} R_i = {}^n\omega$, and let $g_0, \ldots, g_{m-1}$ be $n$-ary $A$-functions. Define the $n$-ary function $f$ by
$$f(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}) = \begin{cases}
g_0(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}) & \text{if } \langle x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1} \rangle \in R_0, \\
g_1(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}) & \text{if } \langle x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1} \rangle \in R_1, \\
\;\;\vdots & \;\;\vdots \\
g_{m-1}(x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1}) & \text{if } \langle x_0, \ldots, x_{n-1} \rangle \in R_{m-1}.
\end{cases}$$
Then $f$ is an $A$-function.
