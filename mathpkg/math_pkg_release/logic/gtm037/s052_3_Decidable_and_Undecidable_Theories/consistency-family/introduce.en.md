A consistency family is the central concept of the consistency family method for constructing models, introduced by Henkin, Smullyan, Makkai, and Keisler. It formalizes the idea of a family of sets of sentences that is closed under certain logical operations: subsets (C0), non-contradiction (C1), negation pushing (C2), conjunction decomposition (C3), disjunction branching (C4), universal instantiation (C5), and existential witnessing (C6). This method abstracts the core argument from the completeness theorem proof and serves as the primary tool for model construction with countable languages throughout the subsequent development.
