The formula transformation $\varphi \rightarrow$ is a syntactic operation on first-order formulas that pushes a negation sign one step inward while also eliminating any initial double negation. It is defined inductively: atomic formulas become their negations, negated formulas drop the negation, disjunctions and conjunctions become their De Morgan duals, and universal quantifiers become existential quantifiers over the negated subformula. This transformation serves as a technical tool for performing a special kind of induction on formulas, particularly in the construction of consistency families for model-theoretic arguments.
