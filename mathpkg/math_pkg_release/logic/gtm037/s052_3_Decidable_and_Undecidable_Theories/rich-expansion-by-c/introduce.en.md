A rich expansion by $C$ extends a first-order language $\mathcal{L}$ by adding a set $C$ of new individual constants whose cardinality equals that of the set of formulas of $\mathcal{L}$. This definition makes more precise the procedure used in Henkin-style completeness proofs, where sufficiently many new constants are needed to witness existential quantifiers. Primitive terms are those built by applying an operation symbol directly to new constants from $C$, including the constants themselves as the rank-zero case.
