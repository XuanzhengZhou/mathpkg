A **simple ordering structure** is a pair $(A, \leq)$ consisting of a set $A$ and a simple (total) ordering $\leq$ whose field is exactly $A$. This is the basic relational structure through which indiscernibility is articulated.

Given an $\mathcal{L}$-structure $\mathfrak{B}$ containing $A$, the ordering $\mathfrak{A} = (A, \leq)$ is **homogeneous** for $\mathfrak{B}$, or equivalently, $A$ is a **set of indiscernibles** for $\mathfrak{B}$, whenever any two $n$-tuples from $A$ that are similarly ordered (i.e., have the same order type) satisfy exactly the same formulas in $\mathfrak{B}$. In other words, the elements of $A$ cannot be distinguished by first-order formulas beyond their relative order.
