**Proof of Theorem 27.17 (Ehrenfeucht--Mostowski).**  

Let $\mathfrak{C}$ be an infinite model of $\Gamma$, and let $\leq$ be a simple ordering of $C$. Expand the language $\mathcal{L}$ to $\mathcal{L}'$ by adding Skolem functions. Let $\mathfrak{C}'$ be a Skolem expansion of $\mathfrak{C}$. By Ramsey's theorem, there exists an infinite subset $D \subseteq C$ which is a set of indiscernibles for $\mathfrak{C}'$. Let $\mathfrak{D}$ be the substructure of $\mathfrak{C}'$ generated by $D$.

Now, given the simple ordering structure $\mathfrak{A} = \langle A, \leq \rangle$, we construct the desired model $\mathfrak{B}$ by a compactness argument. For each finite subset $A' \subseteq A$, consider the set of all order-preserving maps from $A'$ into finite subsets of $D$. Using the fact that $D$ is indiscernible in $\mathfrak{C}'$, any two such maps that preserve the order type yield the same truth values for all formulas.

Specifically, we work with finite approximations: for each $m \in \omega$, we construct a finite set $D_m \subseteq D$ and build a partial elementary map $f$ preserving the order from a finite subset of $A$ into $D_m$. Set $\mathfrak{C}'' = (\mathfrak{C}', l_x)_{x \in A}$, where $l : A \to C$ is arbitrary subject only to the restriction that the finite approximation $f \subseteq l$ (i.e., $l$ extends the partial map $f$). We claim that $\mathfrak{C}''$ is a model of $\Gamma' \cup \Theta$, where $\Theta$ encodes the indiscernibility conditions.

It is obvious that $\mathfrak{C}''$ is a model of $\Gamma' \cup \Theta'$ (where $\Theta'$ is a finite subset of $\Theta$). Now suppose that $i \leq m$; we must check that the $i$-th indiscernibility sentence holds in $\mathfrak{C}''$. Given the finite sequences involved, by construction we have the required order relations, and since $\mathrm{Rng}\, f \subseteq D_{m+1} \subseteq D_{i+1}$, the indiscernibility of $D$ in $\mathfrak{C}'$ guarantees that the relevant formulas are satisfied on both sides. Since $f \subseteq l$, it follows that the indiscernibility condition holds in $\mathfrak{C}''$. Thus $\mathfrak{C}''$ is a model of $\Gamma' \cup \Theta$.

Hence, by the compactness theorem, $\Gamma' \cup \Delta$ has a model, which we may assume has the form $\mathfrak{B}$ with $A \subseteq B$, where the elements of $A$ are interpreted as themselves and form a set of indiscernibles. This establishes condition (i).

For condition (ii), any automorphism of the ordering $\mathfrak{A}$ preserves the order type of tuples from $A$, and by the indiscernibility condition (*) of Definition 27.15, the truth of all formulas is preserved under such order-preserving permutations. Thus the automorphism extends to an automorphism of $\mathfrak{B}$.

Condition (iii) follows from the construction, as the model is built from the Skolem hull of the indiscernibles together with the original set $A$, and the number of formulas in the expanded language bounds the cardinality.
