In formalized set theory, the description operator $\tau$ (read "the unique $x$ such that") is used to form definite descriptions. This theorem establishes that the $\tau$-operator is eliminable: any formula or term involving $\tau$ can be equivalently expressed by a formula that does not involve $\tau$, relative to a given set $\Gamma$ of sentences. The proof proceeds by simultaneous induction on the structure of terms and formulas, constructing the $\tau$-free equivalents step by step.
