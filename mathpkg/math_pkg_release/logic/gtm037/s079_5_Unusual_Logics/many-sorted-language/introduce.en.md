Definition 29.26 introduces many-sorted first-order logic, a variant considerably more important than logics with description or choice operators. Instead of a single universe, structures have several universes (sorts), and variables, relation symbols, and operation symbols are typed by sorts. Many-sorted logic is widely used for formalizing mathematics with multiple types of objects (e.g., vector spaces with scalars and vectors).
