This theorem establishes that the Hilbert $\varepsilon$-operator is eliminable, analogous to the elimination of the description operator. Given any formula with $\varepsilon$, one can produce an $\varepsilon$-free formula in an expanded language with Skolem functions that is equivalent in all choice structures. This implies the possibility of a complete proof theory (the $\varepsilon$-calculus) and leads to results such as the first and second $\varepsilon$-theorems.
