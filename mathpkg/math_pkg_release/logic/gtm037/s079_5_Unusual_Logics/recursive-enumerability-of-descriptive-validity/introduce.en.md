This theorem states that the collection of all sentences valid in all descriptive triples is recursively enumerable. It follows from Theorem 29.20, which shows that every descriptive formula can be equivalently expressed without the $\tau$-operator. Since ordinary first-order validities form a recursively enumerable set, the descriptive validities do as well. This result underpins the formal development of a complete proof theory for the description operator.
