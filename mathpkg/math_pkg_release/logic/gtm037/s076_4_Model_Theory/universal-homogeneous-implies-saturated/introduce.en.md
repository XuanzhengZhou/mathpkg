An $m$-universal structure embeds all elementarily equivalent structures of cardinality less than $m$. Lemma 28.20 provides a converse to a previous implication: when the language has fewer than $m$ formulas, $m$-universality combined with $m$-homogeneity is sufficient to guarantee $m$-saturation. This result is a key step toward the full characterization of saturated structures.
