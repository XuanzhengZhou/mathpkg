Henkin's Embedding Theorem provides a powerful criterion for embedding an entire first-order structure into a class of structures, using only local information. If a class $\mathbf{K}$ is closed under isomorphism and every finitely generated substructure of $\mathfrak{A}$ embeds into some member of $\mathbf{K}$, then $\mathfrak{A}$ itself embeds into a member of $\mathbf{K}$. The proof combines the diagram method with the compactness theorem, encoding the desired embedding through the diagram of $\mathfrak{A}$ together with sentences asserting the existence of witnesses in $\mathbf{K}$. The theorem admits an alternative proof via ultraproducts (Exercise 19.42).
