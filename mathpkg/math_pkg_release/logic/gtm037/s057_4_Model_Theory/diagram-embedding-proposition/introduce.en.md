If an expanded structure is a model of the diagram of a given structure $\mathfrak{A}$, then the interpretation map $l$ that assigns to each constant $\mathbf{c}_a$ (for $a \in A$) its denotation in the expanded structure is an embedding of $\mathfrak{A}$ into the reduct of that structure. This proposition formalizes the tight connection between the syntactic diagram of a structure and the semantic notion of embedding: satisfying the diagram sentences precisely forces the constant-interpretation map to be injective and to respect all operations.
