A symmetric monoidal functor from $DCor$ (the category of disjoint sums of corollas with mergers and automorphisms) to $(\mathcal{B}, \times)$ assigns to each corolla an object $\mathcal{P}(n,m)$ as in the previous claim, and to the disjoint union operation (which is the monoidal structure on $DCor$) the horizontal product on $\mathcal{P}$. The associativity and compatibility conditions for horizontal products correspond exactly to the monoidal functor axioms. The action of symmetric groups arises from automorphisms of corollas, as before.
