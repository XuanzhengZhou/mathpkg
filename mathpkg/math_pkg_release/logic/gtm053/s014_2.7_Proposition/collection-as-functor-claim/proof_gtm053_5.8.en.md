The identification is straightforward: given a collection $\mathcal{P}$, define the functor on a corolla with $m$ inputs and $n$ outputs by $\mathcal{P}(n,m)$. The automorphisms of this corolla are given by permutations of inputs and outputs, yielding the actions of $\mathbf{S}_m \times \mathbf{S}_n^{\text{op}}$. Conversely, given a functor on the groupoid of oriented corollas, restricting to a fixed corolla with $m$ inputs and $n$ outputs gives an object with the required group actions. The two constructions are inverse to each other.
