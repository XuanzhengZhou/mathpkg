Let $G$ be a group, and suppose we are given a family of pairs of embeddings $\alpha_i, \beta_i : A_i \rightarrow G$ ($i \in I$) and a subgroup $H \subset G$ with the property that $\alpha_i^{-1}(\alpha_i(A_i) \cap H) = \beta_i^{-1}(\beta_i(A_i) \cap H) = B_i \subset A_i$ are subgroups. Define

$$K_G = \left\langle G \cup \{t_i \mid i \in I\} : t_i^{-1}\alpha_i(a)t_i = \beta_i(a) \text{ for all } i \in I, a \in A_i \right\rangle;$$

$$K_H = \left\langle H \cup \{t_i' \mid i \in I\} : t_i'^{-1}\alpha_i(b)t_i' = \beta_i(b) \text{ for all } i \in I, b \in B_i \right\rangle.$$

Then:
(a) the $\{t_i\}$ freely generate a free subgroup in $K_G$;
(b) the natural maps $G \rightarrow K_G$ and $K_H \rightarrow K_G$ (the latter given by $t_i' \mapsto t_i$) are embeddings. In addition, $K_H \cap G = H$ in $K_G$.
