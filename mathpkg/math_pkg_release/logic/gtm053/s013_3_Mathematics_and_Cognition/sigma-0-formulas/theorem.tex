A formula in the language of set theory is called a $\Sigma_0$-formula (or $\Delta_0$-formula) if all its quantifiers are restricted, i.e., of the form $\forall x \in f(y_1, \ldots, y_r)$ or $\exists x \in f(y_1, \ldots, y_r)$, where $f$ is an operation closed under the relevant transitive model. $\Sigma_0$-formulas are absolute for all transitive models: their truth value does not depend on the ambient universe.
