This definition formalizes the notion of an infinite family of yes/no problems being solvable by a single algorithm. The mass problem is decidable if membership in the 'yes' set is computable. Hilbert's tenth problem is the most famous example: the mass problem of Diophantine equation solvability is algorithmically undecidable (as a consequence of the DPRM theorem).
