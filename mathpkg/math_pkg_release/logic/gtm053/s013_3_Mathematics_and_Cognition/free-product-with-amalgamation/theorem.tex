Given a family of groups $(G_i)_{i \in I}$ and embeddings $\alpha_i: A \to G_i$, the free product $*_A G_i$ with amalgamated subgroup $A$ has the following properties:
(1) Every element can be uniquely written in canonical form as an alternating product of coset representatives from distinct $G_i$, followed by an element of $A$.
(2) The structure homomorphisms $\phi_i: G_i \to *_A G_i$ and $\phi: A \to *_A G_i$ are embeddings.
(3) $\phi_i(G_i) \cap \phi_j(G_j) = \phi(A)$ for $i \neq j$.
(4) If $\beta_i: H_i \to G_i$ are embeddings with $\beta_i(H_i) \cap \alpha_i(A) = \alpha_i(B)$ for a common subgroup $B \subset A$, then the natural map $*_B H_i \to *_A G_i$ is an embedding.
