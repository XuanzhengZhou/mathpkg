If the domain of $F$ is a set, Easton's theorem can be obtained using a model of the form $M[G]$, where $M$ is a model in which the generalized continuum hypothesis holds (G\"odel proved that such an $M$ exists), and $G$ is generic for an Easton product forcing. If the domain of $F$ is a proper class, the concept of forcing must be generalized to the case that $C$ is a class.
