The case $n=1$ is Theorem 5.8 of Chapter V (existence of undecidable enumerable sets). For general $n$, a diagonal process is applied to a versal family $\{E_k\}$ of enumerable $(n+1)$-sets. Define $F = \{\langle x_1, \ldots, x_n \rangle \mid \langle \langle x_1, \ldots, x_n \rangle, x_1, \ldots, x_n \rangle \notin E\}$. Then $F \in \Pi_n \setminus \Sigma_n$, and its complement is in $\Sigma_n \setminus \Pi_n$.
