Church's Thesis identifies the intuitive notion of algorithmic computability with the mathematically precise notion of partial recursiveness. It is not a theorem but an empirical claim supported by the equivalence of many different formalizations (Turing machines, Post systems, Markov algorithms, etc.). Every semicomputable function is partial recursive, and conversely.
