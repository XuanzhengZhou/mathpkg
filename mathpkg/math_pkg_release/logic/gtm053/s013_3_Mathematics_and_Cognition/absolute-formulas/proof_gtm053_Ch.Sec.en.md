The proof proceeds by induction. For atomic formulas, $M$-truth and $V$-truth coincide because $M$ is transitive. For Boolean combinations, the induction is straightforward. For restricted quantifiers, the condition $f(M \times \cdots \times M) \subset M$ ensures that the range of the quantified variable lies within $M$, so $M$-truth and $V$-truth agree.
