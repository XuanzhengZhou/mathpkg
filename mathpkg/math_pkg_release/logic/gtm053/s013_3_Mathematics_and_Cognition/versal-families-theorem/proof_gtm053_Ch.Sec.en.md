For versal families of polynomials, construct a recursive numbering: $f[4k] = k$, $f[4k+1] = x_k$, $f[4k+2] = f[t_1(k)] + f[t_2(k)]$, $f[4k+3] = f[t_1(k)]f[t_2(k)]$. The $k$-th Diophantine set is the projection of the zero-set of $f[k]$. The DPRM theorem ensures this gives a versal family. For functions, versal families are constructed from versal families of graphs using the $\mu$-operator.
