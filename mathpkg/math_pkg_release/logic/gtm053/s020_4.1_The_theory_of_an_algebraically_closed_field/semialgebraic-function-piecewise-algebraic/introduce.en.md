This fact provides a precise structural description of semialgebraic (definable) functions from a real closed field to itself. Any such function, after restricting to finitely many open intervals and points covering its domain, coincides with a specific algebraic branch — i.e., it is one of the roots of a polynomial equation in two variables. This means that definable functions in $\text{RCF}$ are piecewise algebraic, a strong tameness property that follows from quantifier elimination and $o$-minimality.
