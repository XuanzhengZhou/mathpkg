A semialgebraic set over a real closed field $R$ is a finite Boolean combination of sets defined by polynomial equations and strict polynomial inequalities. By the Tarski–Seidenberg theorem, these are exactly the quantifier-free definable sets in the language of ordered rings, and because $\text{RCF}$ admits quantifier elimination, they coincide with all definable sets (with parameters). A crucial consequence is that the projection of a semialgebraic set is again semialgebraic. For one-variable polynomial inequalities $f(x) > 0$, the solution set is a finite union of open intervals $(a, b)$ with $-\infty \leq a < b \leq +\infty$.
