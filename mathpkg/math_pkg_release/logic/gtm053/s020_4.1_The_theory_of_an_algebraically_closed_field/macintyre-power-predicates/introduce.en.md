Macintyre introduced the predicates $P_n$ ($n \geq 2$) as an extension of the language of valued fields to obtain quantifier elimination for the theory of $p$-adically closed fields. Each $P_n$ is a unary predicate that holds exactly for the $n$-th powers in the field. This language extension does not impose extra conditions on the valued field (since the definition is explicit: $P_n(x) \leftrightarrow \exists y (y^n = x)$), but it makes the quantifier elimination statement much more powerful and useful. The introduction of $P_n$ is analogous to the role of the predicate $P_2(x)$ (i.e., $x \geq 0$) in the quantifier elimination for the theory of real closed fields.
