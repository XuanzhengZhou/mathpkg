A constructible set over an algebraically closed field $F$ is a subset of $F^n$ that can be expressed as a Boolean combination (using union, intersection, and complement) of zero-sets of polynomials in $n$ variables. Chevalley's theorem states that the $L_1\text{Ar}$-definable subsets of $F^n$ are exactly the constructible sets. Consequently, the projection of a constructible set is again constructible, which is a non-trivial geometric fact that follows from quantifier elimination for $\text{ACF}_p$.
