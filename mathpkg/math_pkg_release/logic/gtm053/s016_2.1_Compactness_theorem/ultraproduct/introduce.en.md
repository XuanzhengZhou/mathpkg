An ultraproduct is a special case of the filtered product construction where the filter $U$ is an ultrafilter. This construction is fundamental in model theory: it preserves all first-order properties (by Łoś's theorem) and provides a powerful tool for building models with specific properties. Ultraproducts are used in the third proof of the Compactness Theorem, in nonstandard analysis (via ultrapowers of $\mathbb{R}$), and in the study of saturated models.
