Łoś's theorem is the fundamental theorem of ultraproducts, proved by Jerzy Łoś in 1955. It states that a first-order formula holds in the ultraproduct $\prod \mathbf{A}_i / U$ if and only if it holds in "almost all" factor structures (i.e., in a set belonging to the ultrafilter $U$). This theorem implies that ultraproducts preserve all first-order properties, making them a powerful tool in model theory. It is used in the third proof of the Compactness Theorem: one takes models of all finite subsets of $E$, forms their ultraproduct, and obtains a model of the entire set $E$.
