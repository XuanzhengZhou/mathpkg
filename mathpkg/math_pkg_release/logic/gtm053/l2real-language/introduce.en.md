L2Real is a second-order language designed to describe the real numbers. Its key feature is the ability to quantify over functions (and hence over sets, since sets can be encoded via characteristic functions). This extra expressive power allows the formulation of categoricity results (such as the fact that the real numbers are the unique complete ordered field) but at the cost of losing compactness and completeness. The language is used in the simplified presentation of Cohen forcing.
