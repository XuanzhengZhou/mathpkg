The axiomatizability of algebraically closed difference fields (ACFA) is due to Hrushovski, who showed that the class of existentially closed difference fields is elementary. The theory ACFA is the model companion of the theory of difference fields.

To prove simplicity, one develops a notion of independence (nonforking) for difference fields, based on the algebraic geometry of difference varieties. The key point is that the fixed field $F$ has SU-rank 1, analogous to a strongly minimal set in the stable context. In a simple theory, SU-rank 1 sets play the role that strongly minimal sets play in $\aleph_0$-stable theories. The definable closure of parameters in ACFA is controlled by the difference-algebraic closure, which allows one to verify the local character and symmetry axioms of simplicity.

That ACFA is not stable follows from the fact that it interprets the theory of the fixed field with the induced automorphism, or more directly because the fixed field is pseudofinite and admits a definable structure with the independence property (the automorphism $\sigma$ acts as a generic automorphism). In characteristic zero, ACFA sits strictly in the simple-unstable region of Shelah's classification, providing one of the most important natural examples of a simple theory.
