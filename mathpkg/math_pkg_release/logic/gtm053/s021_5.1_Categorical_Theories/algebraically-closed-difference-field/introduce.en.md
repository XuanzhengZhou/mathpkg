An algebraically closed difference field is the difference-field analogue of an algebraically closed field. The definition uses existential closedness: any finite system of quantifier-free equations and inequations with parameters from $K$ that is solvable in some extension must already have a solution in $K$. Hrushovski proved that the class of algebraically closed difference fields of characteristic zero is first-order axiomatizable, and its theory (ACFA) is simple in the sense of Shelah's classification theory.
