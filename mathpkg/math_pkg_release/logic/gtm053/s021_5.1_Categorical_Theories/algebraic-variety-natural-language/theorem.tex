Let $V = V(F)$ be the set of $F$-points of an algebraic variety defined over some $C \subseteq F$, where $F \models \operatorname{ACF}_p$. For each $n$ and each $C$-definable subvariety $W \subseteq V^n$, introduce the symbol $p_W$ of an $n$-ary predicate on $V$. The \textit{natural language} for the algebraic variety $V$ consists of all the $p_W$ for all such $W$.

The structure $V$ on the domain $V(F)$ with the obvious interpretation of the predicates of the natural language is definable in the field $F$. Its theory is $\aleph_0$-stable and, for $C$ big enough (e.g., if $C$ contains an algebraically closed subfield), Morley rank coincides with dimension. In general $\operatorname{rk} S \leq \dim S$ for constructible sets $S$ definable in $V$.
