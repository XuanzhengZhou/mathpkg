This definition constructs a canonical first-order language for any algebraic variety over an algebraically closed field, where predicates name all definable subvarieties of powers of the variety. The resulting structure is definable in the field, hence $\aleph_0$-stable. When the parameter set is large enough, Morley rank on definable sets coincides with algebraic dimension, providing a model-theoretic interpretation of dimension in algebraic geometry.
