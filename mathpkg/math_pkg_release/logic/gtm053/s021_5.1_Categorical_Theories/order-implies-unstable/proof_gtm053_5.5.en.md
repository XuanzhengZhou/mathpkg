Suppose $T$ has a definable order relation $<$ on some infinite definable subset of a model. By the method of diagrams, we can embed any ordered set $(C, <)$ into a model of $T$. Fix an infinite cardinal $\kappa$. There exists an ordered set $(C, <)$ of cardinality $\kappa$ with more than $\kappa$ Dedekind cuts. A Dedekind cut $(L, R)$ in $C$ determines a complete 1-type over $C$: the type expressing that $x$ lies between $L$ and $R$. Distinct cuts give distinct types. Thus over a parameter set $C$ of size $\kappa$, there are strictly more than $\kappa$ complete 1-types. This violates $\kappa$-stability for every infinite $\kappa$. Hence $T$ cannot be $\kappa$-stable for any infinite $\kappa$, and therefore $T$ is not stable.
