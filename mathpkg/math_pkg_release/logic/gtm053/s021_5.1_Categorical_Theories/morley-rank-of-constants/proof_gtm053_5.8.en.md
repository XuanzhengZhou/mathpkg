In $\operatorname{DCF}_0$, the constants $C_K$ form a pure algebraically closed field (the derivation vanishes identically on $C_K$, and $\operatorname{DCF}_0$ axiomatizes that $C_K$ is algebraically closed). An algebraically closed field is strongly minimal, meaning every definable subset is either finite or cofinite. Strongly minimal sets have Morley rank exactly 1. For the solution space $S_f$ of a differential equation $f(D^n y, \ldots, y) = 0$ of order $n$, the rank computation uses the fact that the Kolchin topology on $S_f$ has dimension $n$ in the sense of differential algebra, and Morley rank in $\operatorname{DCF}_0$ coincides with this differential-algebraic dimension.
