In $\operatorname{DCF}_0$, Morley rank coincides with the differential-algebraic dimension (also called Kolchin dimension). For a differential equation $f(D^n y, \ldots, y) = 0$ of order $n$, the solution space $S_f$ is a Kolchin closed set of differential dimension at most $n$, and generically exactly $n$ (when $f$ involves $D^n y$ nontrivially). Therefore $\operatorname{rk} S_f = n$.

For the nonorthogonality statement: $S_f$ is nonorthogonal to $C_K$ precisely when there exists a definable finite-to-finite correspondence between (a large subset of) $S_f$ and $C_K^n$. In the linear case, the solution space is a vector space over the constants of dimension $n$, giving a definable bijection with $C_K^n$, so the types are nonorthogonal. For a generic (nonlinear) equation, the Galois group of the associated Picard-Vessiot extension is more complex, and $S_f$ is orthogonal to the constants, meaning there is no definable correspondence with the constant field.
