Nonorthogonality is a central equivalence relation on types in stability theory. Two types are nonorthogonal if their maximal indiscernible sets have the same cardinality in every model, meaning the types are "interdependent" — they cannot be realized independently. This notion generalizes the geometric idea of non-orthogonal subspaces: for example, in differentially closed fields, the generic type of the solution space of a differential equation is nonorthogonal to the generic type of the field of constants precisely when the solution space is parametrized by the constants.
