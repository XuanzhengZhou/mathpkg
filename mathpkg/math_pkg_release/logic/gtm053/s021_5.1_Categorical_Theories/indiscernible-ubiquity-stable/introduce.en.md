This theorem captures a defining property of stable theories: in any sufficiently saturated model, indiscernible sequences over a small parameter set are plentiful. This ubiquity of indiscernibles is a powerful tool for analyzing types and constructing nonforking extensions, forming the backbone of the stability-theoretic analysis of models. The theorem contrasts sharply with unstable theories where indiscernibles may be scarce.
