This proposition gives an algebraic reinterpretation of $\mu$-categoricity: if the theory of a structure $\mathcal{A}$ is $\mu$-categorical, then the first-order sentences true in $\mathcal{A}$ together with its cardinality $\mu$ form a complete set of invariants. Any other structure of cardinality $\mu$ satisfying exactly the same sentences must be isomorphic to $\mathcal{A}$, which is a very strong classification result.
