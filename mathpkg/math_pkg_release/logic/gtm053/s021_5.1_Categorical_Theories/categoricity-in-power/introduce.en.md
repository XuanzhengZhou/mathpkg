$\mu$-categoricity is the central notion in Shelah's classification theory. Rather than requiring a unique model in all cardinalities, it only demands uniqueness up to isomorphism among models of a fixed cardinality $\mu$. This relaxes absolute categoricity and makes the concept applicable to infinite structures, which are the main objects of interest in model theory.
