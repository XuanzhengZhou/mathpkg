This proposition has two parts: (a) establishes that the equality axioms are true under any interpretation where the relation symbol $=$ is interpreted as genuine equality, and (b) demonstrates that key instances of these axioms, such as $t = t$ and the equivalence $t_1 = t_2 \Leftrightarrow t_2 = t_1$, are formally deducible from the axioms of equality together with the logical axioms.

Part (b) provides concrete deductions: for instance, $t = t$ is obtained by starting from $x = x$ (axiom of equality), applying generalization to get $\forall x(x = x)$, and then specializing to $t = t$ via the logical axiom of specialization and modus ponens. The symmetry deduction constructs $x = y \Rightarrow y = x$ from the substitution axiom with $=$ for $P$, then generalizes and specializes.
