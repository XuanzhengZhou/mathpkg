The axioms of equality are the formal rules governing the equality relation $=$ in first-order languages $\mathfrak{L}_1$. They express the fundamental properties of equality: reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and substitutivity (equals can be substituted for equals in any formula).

These axioms are assumed as special axioms in any language that includes the equality symbol. A model in which $=$ is interpreted as genuine equality (rather than an arbitrary equivalence relation) is called a normal model.

The substitution axiom schema, $x = t \Rightarrow (P(x, x) \Rightarrow P(x, t))$, captures Leibniz's principle of the identity of indiscernibles within the formal language, with the technical restriction that $t$ must be free for $x$ in $P$.
