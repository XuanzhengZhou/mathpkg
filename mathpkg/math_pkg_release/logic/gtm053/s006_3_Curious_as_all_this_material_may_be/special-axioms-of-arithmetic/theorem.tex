The following formulas are true in the standard interpretation of $\mathrm{L}_1\mathrm{Ar}$, and are called the special axioms of $\mathrm{L}_1\mathrm{Ar}$:

\textbf{(a)} The axioms of equality.

\textbf{(b)} The axioms of addition:
$$x + \bar{0} = x; \quad x + y = y + x; \quad (x + y) + z = x + (y + z);$$
$$x + z = y + z \Rightarrow x = y.$$

\textbf{(c)} The axioms of multiplication:
$$x \cdot \bar{0} = \bar{0}; \quad x \cdot 1 = x; \quad x \cdot y = y \cdot x; \quad (x \cdot y) \cdot z = x \cdot (y \cdot z).$$

\textbf{(d)} The distributive axiom:
$$x \cdot (y + z) = x \cdot y + x \cdot z.$$

\textbf{(e)} The axioms of induction:
$$P(\bar{0}) \wedge \forall x(P(x) \Rightarrow P(x + \bar{1})) \Rightarrow \forall x P(x),$$
where $P$ is any formula in $\mathrm{L}_1\mathrm{Ar}$ having one free variable.
