The theory of algebraically closed fields is one of the fundamental examples in model theory. Tarski proved that ACF_p is complete and admits quantifier elimination. This means every definable set is a Boolean combination of zero-sets of polynomials (Chevalley's theorem), making ACF a paradigm of a well-behaved first-order theory.
