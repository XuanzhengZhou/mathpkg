The trichotomy conjecture in its original strong form was proposed by Zilber. The text notes that counterexamples were later found (Hrushovski's constructions), leading to a refined version. The proof strategy for the positive direction proceeds as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
\item If a strongly minimal set is not locally modular, then it contains a definable pseudoplane. This follows from the analysis of the lattice of algebraically closed sets.
\item A classical theorem states that any abstract affine or projective plane satisfying the combinatorial Desargues theorem is definably equivalent to a division ring. Under $\omega$-stability assumptions, such a division ring must be an algebraically closed field.
\item Thus, the weak trichotomy theorem (Theorem 6.4 in the text) states that the pregeometries of the three examples in 6.3 are the only possible types. When local finiteness is assumed, type (3) (algebraically closed fields) is excluded.
\end{enumerate}

The original conjecture was refined into the theory of Zariski geometries, where topological axioms replace the purely combinatorial ones, eliminating Hrushovski-style counterexamples.
