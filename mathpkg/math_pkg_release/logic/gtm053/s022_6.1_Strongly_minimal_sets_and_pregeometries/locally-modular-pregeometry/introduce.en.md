A pregeometry is locally modular if naming (localizing at) a single parameter makes it modular. Affine geometries over fields are the canonical example: they are not modular but become modular after fixing an origin. In Zilber's trichotomy, locally modular strongly minimal sets split into two types: those whose associated geometry is trivial (disintegrated) and those whose associated geometry is a projective geometry over a division ring. Non-locally-modular strongly minimal sets are conjectured to interpret an algebraically closed field.
