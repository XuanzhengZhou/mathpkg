A combinatorial geometry is a pregeometry in which every singleton is closed — no point is in the closure of the empty set. Geometries arise naturally from pregeometries by factoring out the closure of the empty set (the "degenerate" part), yielding a structure whose closed sets correspond to projective geometries, affine spaces, and other classical geometric objects. The terms "matroid" and "dependence relation" are synonyms that appear across combinatorics, algebra, and model theory.
