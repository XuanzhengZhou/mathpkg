$M$ is said to be \emph{strongly minimal} if every $M' \equiv M$ is minimal. This is also applicable when $M$ is a definable subset in an ambient structure $A$; one treats the set $M$ as the domain of a structure with relations on $M$ induced from $A$, and in this case one calls $M$ a \emph{strongly minimal set}. In algebraic geometry, or rather the theory $\mathrm{ACF}_p$, the strongly minimal subsets of $F^n$ are (irreducible) algebraic curves with a finite number of points added or removed.
