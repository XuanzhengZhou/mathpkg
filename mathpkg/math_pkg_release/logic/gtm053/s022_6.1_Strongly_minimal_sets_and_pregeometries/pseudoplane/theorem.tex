A \emph{pseudoplane} is a pregeometry satisfying the following incidence conditions:
\begin{itemize}
\item The set of lines incident to a given point is of rank $1$;
\item Every two lines intersect in at most finitely many points;
\item Through any two points pass finitely many lines.
\end{itemize}

An example of a pseudoplane is an algebraic surface $P$ (not necessarily closed) with a $2$-dimensional family $L$ of curves on it (with $F$-points, for $F$ algebraically closed). Removing, if necessary, exceptional $1$-dimensional subsets from $P$ and $L$ one can always obtain a structure satisfying the above conditions. A special case of a pseudoplane is an abstract affine (or projective) plane.
