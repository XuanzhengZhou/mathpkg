A pregeometry is modular if the lattice of closed sets is modular, which translates to the condition that whenever $w$ is in the closure of $U \cup \{v\}$, there is already some $u$ in the closure of $U$ such that $w$ and $v$ are interdependent over $u$. Modular pregeometries correspond to projective geometries over division rings. Examples include trivial geometries and projective geometries over fields. Non-modular pregeometries include algebraically closed fields; such pregeometries may still be locally modular.
