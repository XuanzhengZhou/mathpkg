Localizing a pregeometry at a point $a$ means redefining the closure operator so that $a$ is always adjoined to the argument: $\operatorname{cl}_a(X) = \operatorname{cl}(X \cup \{a\})$. In model-theoretic terms, this corresponds to expanding the language by a constant symbol naming $a$, thereby enriching the definable sets. Localization is the key to defining local modularity: a pregeometry is locally modular if some localization of it becomes modular.
