The Tarski-Seidenberg theorem establishes that the theory of real closed fields is complete and admits quantifier elimination. This implies that every formula in the language of ordered rings is equivalent (over RCF) to a quantifier-free formula, and hence that the first-order theory of the real numbers as an ordered field is decidable. The geometric consequence is that the projection of a semialgebraic set is semialgebraic.
