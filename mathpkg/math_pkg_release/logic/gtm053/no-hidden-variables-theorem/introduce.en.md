The Kochen-Specker theorem (also known as von Neumann's no-hidden-variables theorem in a strengthened form) demonstrates that quantum mechanics cannot be interpreted as a classical probability theory over hidden variables. The theorem constructs a finite set of 117 directions in 3-dimensional space for which no consistent assignment of 0/1 values can satisfy the quantum-mechanical constraints. This is a landmark result in the foundations of quantum mechanics.
