The Method of Diagrams reduces the problem of finding embeddings or elementary embeddings to satisfiability problems. By expanding the language with constant symbols for all elements of a structure and adding the atomic (or all) truths as axioms, the existence of an embedding (or elementary embedding) into another structure is equivalent to the expanded theory being satisfiable. This is a fundamental technique in model-theoretic constructions.
