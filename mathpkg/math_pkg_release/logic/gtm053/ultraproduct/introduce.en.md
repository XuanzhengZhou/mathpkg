The ultraproduct construction is a powerful method for building new models from families of existing ones. The key property is captured by Los's theorem: a formula holds in the ultraproduct if and only if it holds in "almost all" factors (i.e., on a set in the ultrafilter). Ultraproducts provide an elegant proof of the compactness theorem and are essential tools in classification theory.
