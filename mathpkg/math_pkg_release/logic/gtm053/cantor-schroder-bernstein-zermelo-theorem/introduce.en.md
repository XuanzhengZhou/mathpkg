This theorem combines Cantor's theorem (the power set is strictly larger), the Schroder-Bernstein theorem (mutual cardinal inequality implies equality), and Zermelo's well-ordering theorem (every set can be well-ordered). Together they establish that cardinalities form a well-ordered proper class without a maximum. The proof uses the Axiom of Choice (for well-ordering) and a clever fixed-point argument for the Cantor-Bernstein direction.
