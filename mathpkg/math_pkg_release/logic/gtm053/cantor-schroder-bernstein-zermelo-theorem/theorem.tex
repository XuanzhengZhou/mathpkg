\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] Any two sets are comparable. If both $\operatorname{card} M \leqslant \operatorname{card} N$ and $\operatorname{card} N \leqslant \operatorname{card} M$, then $\operatorname{card} M = \operatorname{card} N$. The cardinalities are linearly ordered.
\item[(b)] $\operatorname{card} \mathcal{P}(M) > \operatorname{card} M$. There does not exist a largest cardinality.
\item[(c)] In any class of cardinalities there is a least cardinality. The cardinalities are well-ordered.
\end{enumerate}
