The topological reformulation of the compactness theorem states that the Stone space $\mathcal{S}$ of $L$-structures is compact. This means that any family of basic closed sets with the finite intersection property has nonempty intersection. The classical compactness theorem---that a finitely satisfiable set of sentences is satisfiable---follows immediately: if every finite subset of $\Sigma$ has a model, then the family $\{[P] : P \in \Sigma\}$ has the finite intersection property, so $\bigcap_{P \in \Sigma} [P]$ is nonempty, yielding a model of all of $\Sigma$.
