A positive infinitesimal in a nonstandard model ${^*\mathbf{R}}$ of the reals is an element $\alpha$ that is positive but smaller than every standard positive rational (or, equivalently, smaller than $1/n$ for every positive integer $n$). Positive infinitesimals are the building blocks of nonstandard analysis, representing quantities that are "infinitely small" yet strictly greater than zero.
