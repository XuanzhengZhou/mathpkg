Complete arithmetic is the theory $\operatorname{Th}(\mathbf{N})$ consisting of all first-order sentences in the language of arithmetic $L_1\mathrm{Ar} = \{+, \cdot, 0, 1\}$ that are true in the standard model $\mathbf{N} = (\mathbb{N}, +, \cdot, 0, 1)$. Unlike Peano arithmetic, which is given by a recursive axiomatization and is incomplete by Gödel's theorem, complete arithmetic is (by definition) complete but not recursively axiomatizable.
