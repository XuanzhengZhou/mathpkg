The Stone space of $L$-structures is a topological space constructed from the class of all $L$-structures of bounded cardinality, modulo elementary equivalence. The topology is generated by basic open sets $[P]$ corresponding to $L$-sentences $P$, where $[P]$ collects all equivalence classes of structures satisfying $P$. This construction provides a bridge between classical model theory and topology, allowing the compactness theorem to be expressed as a topological compactness statement.
