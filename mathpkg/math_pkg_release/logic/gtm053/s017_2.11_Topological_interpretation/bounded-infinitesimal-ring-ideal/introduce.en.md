In a nonstandard model ${^*\mathbf{R}}$ of the reals, the subset $B$ of bounded elements forms a ring under the inherited operations, and the subset $\mu \subseteq B$ of infinitesimals is a maximal ideal of this ring. This algebraic structure underpins the standard part map: the quotient $B / \mu$ is canonically isomorphic to the standard reals $\mathbb{R}$, providing a rigorous algebraic framework for nonstandard analysis.
