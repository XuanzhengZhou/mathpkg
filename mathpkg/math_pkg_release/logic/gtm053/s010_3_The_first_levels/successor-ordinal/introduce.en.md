The successor of an ordinal $\alpha$ is defined as $\alpha \cup \{\alpha\}$, denoted $\alpha + 1$. In the von Neumann construction, this yields the familiar finite ordinals: $0 = \varnothing$, $1 = \{\varnothing\}$, $2 = \{\varnothing, \{\varnothing\}\}$, and so on. The successor operation generates all non-limit ordinals and is fundamental for transfinite induction and recursion.
