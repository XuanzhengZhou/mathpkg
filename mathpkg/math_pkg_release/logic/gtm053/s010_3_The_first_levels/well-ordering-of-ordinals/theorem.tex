\textbf{Theorem 7.}

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] The class of ordinals $\mathrm{On}$ is well-ordered by the relation $\alpha \in \beta$ (which we shall also write $\alpha < \beta$).
\item[(b)] Any well-ordered set is isomorphic to a unique ordinal $\alpha$, and also to a unique initial segment of ordinals (those less than $\alpha \cup \{\alpha\}$).
\end{enumerate}
