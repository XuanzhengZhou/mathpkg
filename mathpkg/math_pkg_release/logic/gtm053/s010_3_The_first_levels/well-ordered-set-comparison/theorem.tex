Let $X$ and $Y$ be two well-ordered sets. Then exactly one of the following alternatives holds:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $X$ and $Y$ are isomorphic.
\item[(b)] $X$ is isomorphic to an initial segment in $Y$.
\item[(c)] $Y$ is isomorphic to an initial segment in $X$.
\end{enumerate}

In each case the isomorphism is uniquely determined.
