This corollary of the Method of Diagrams reformulates embedding problems as consistency problems: $A$ embeds into a model of $T$ exactly when the theory $T$ is consistent with the diagram (or complete diagram) of $A$. Together with the Compactness Theorem, this transforms structural embedding questions into finite satisfiability conditions, a central strategy in model-theoretic constructions.
