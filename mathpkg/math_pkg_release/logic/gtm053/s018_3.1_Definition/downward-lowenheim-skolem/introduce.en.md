The downward Lowenheim-Skolem theorem states that inside any infinite first-order structure $A$ one can find elementary substructures of every smaller infinite cardinality $\kappa$. The proof uses Skolem functions: starting from any subset $B_0 \subseteq A$ of size $\kappa$, one closes under all Skolem functions $g_P$ to obtain a set $B$ that is closed under all definable witnesses, and the Tarski-Vaught criterion then guarantees $B \preceq A$ with $|B| = \kappa$.
