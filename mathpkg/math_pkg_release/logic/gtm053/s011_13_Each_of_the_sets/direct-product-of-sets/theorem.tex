The direct product of two sets is defined as:
$$X \times Y = \{\, \langle U, W \rangle \mid U \in X,\; W \in Y \,\}.$$

Similarly, the direct product of $n$ sets is:
$$X_1 \times \cdots \times X_n = (\cdots ((X_1 \times X_2) \times X_3) \times \cdots).$$

In general, $(X \times Y) \times Z \neq X \times (Y \times Z)$; there is only a canonical one-to-one correspondence between these two sets. However, it is usually harmless to identify them and write $X \times Y \times Z$.
