Two fundamental operations on set-theoretic functions are defined: the inverse image $f^{-1}(X)$ collects all arguments whose values lie in $X$, and the restriction $f|_X$ selects those ordered pairs of $f$ whose first component belongs to $X$. Both are expressed purely in terms of set membership, consistent with the identification of functions with their graphs.
