An ordered $n$-tuple is defined by iterating the Kuratowski ordered pair construction: $\langle X_1, \ldots, X_n \rangle$ is the nested pair $\langle \cdots \langle \langle X_1, X_2 \rangle, X_3 \rangle \cdots \rangle$. This reduces $n$-tuples to repeated binary pairing, ensuring they remain within the set-theoretic universe and can be expressed purely in terms of the membership relation $\in$.
