The von Neumann hierarchy $V_\alpha$ is transitive at every stage: any element of an element of $V_\alpha$ is itself an element of $V_\alpha$. Equivalently, $V_\alpha \subseteq V_{\alpha+1}$, meaning the hierarchy is cumulative. This property is proved by transfinite induction on the ordinals, treating successor and limit cases separately.
