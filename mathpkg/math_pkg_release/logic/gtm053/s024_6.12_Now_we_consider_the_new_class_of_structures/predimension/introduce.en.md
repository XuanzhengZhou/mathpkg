A predimension is a counting function $\delta(X)$ introduced by Hrushovski to control the construction of new strongly minimal structures via amalgamation. For a unary function $f$ on a field, it measures the balance between the transcendence degree of $X \cup f(X)$ and the size of $X$. In the general fusion setting, the predimension is a linear combination of the dimensions coming from two pregeometries living on the same domain, uniquely determined by the free fusion principle.
