The proof is a deep result in the classification theory of abstract elementary classes, developed by Shelah. It proceeds by analyzing the structure of Galois types (orbits under automorphisms of strong extensions) in the AEC $K$. Under the assumptions of amalgamation and joint embedding, one can develop a notion of independence and a stability-theoretic machinery. The Hanf number $\mu$ is defined as $\beth_{(2^{\operatorname{LS}(K)})^+}$. The categoricity assumption at a successor cardinal above $\mu$ forces the existence of a superlimit model and saturated models in all larger cardinals, from which categoricity in all larger cardinals follows via the uniqueness of saturated models in the same cardinality.
