The Hrushovski class $\mathcal{H}_{\delta}$ is the class of all fused structures $(M, L_1 \cup L_2)$ whose predimension $\delta$ is nonnegative on all finite subsets. This inequality is the key restriction that makes the amalgamation construction work: by requiring $\delta(X) \geq 0$ everywhere, one ensures that the generic structure built by amalgamation is strongly minimal and inherits a well-behaved pregeometry.
