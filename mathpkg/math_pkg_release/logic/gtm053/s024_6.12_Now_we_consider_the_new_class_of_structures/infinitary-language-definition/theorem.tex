Given cardinals $\lambda$ and $\mu$ and an alphabet $L$, the infinitary language $L_{\lambda,\mu}(L)$ is the smallest collection of formulas that:
\begin{itemize}
\item contains all atomic $L$-formulas in the variables $v_\alpha$, $\alpha < \mu$;
\item is closed under taking $\neg$;
\item is closed under applying universal quantifiers to a string of variables $\forall v_{i_1} \cdots \forall v_{i_\alpha} \cdots$ (of length $< \mu$);
\item is closed under applying existential quantifiers to a string of variables $\exists v_{i_1} \cdots \exists v_{i_\alpha} \cdots$ (of length $< \mu$);
\item is closed under Boolean operations (conjunction and disjunction) over sets of formulas of cardinality $< \lambda$.
\end{itemize}
