The axioms of ACFExp (algebraically closed fields with pseudoexponentiation) are equivalent to:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] the validity of Schanuel's conjecture;
\item[(b)] any system of $n$ independent exponential-polynomial equations in $n$ variables that does not directly contradict Schanuel's conjecture has a regular zero, but not more than countably many.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(ii)] ACFExp is categorical in uncountable powers $\kappa$: for every such $\kappa$ there is a unique, up to isomorphism, algebraically closed field with pseudoexponentiation of cardinality $\kappa$;
\item[(iii)] An algebraically closed field with pseudoexponentiation carries a homogeneous pregeometry; in particular, any bijection between two bases of the pregeometry can be extended to an automorphism of the field with pseudoexponentiation.
\end{enumerate}
A consequence is that Schanuel's conjecture is consistent with field-theoretic algebra.
