The Löwenheim–Skolem situation for infinitary languages is considerably more complex than for first-order logic. The downward theorem holds in a restricted form: for any $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-sentence true in an infinite structure $A$, one can find a substructure of any infinite cardinality up to $|A|$ that also satisfies the sentence. However, the upward theorem fails completely — there are $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$-sentences that have a model but are bounded in cardinality, a phenomenon impossible in first-order logic.
