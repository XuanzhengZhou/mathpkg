Scott's Theorem shows that every countable $L$-structure $A$ (with countable language $L$) can be characterized up to isomorphism by a single sentence of the infinitary language $L_{\omega_1,\omega}$. This is a remarkable strengthening of the finite case for first-order logic, where every finite structure can be characterized by a single first-order sentence. The theorem highlights the special role of categoricity in cardinalities small relative to the language size.
