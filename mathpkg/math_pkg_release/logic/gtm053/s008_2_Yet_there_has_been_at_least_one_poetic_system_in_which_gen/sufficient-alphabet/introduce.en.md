The sufficient alphabet condition is a technical requirement for Henkin-style completeness proofs. An alphabet is sufficient for a set of formulas $\mathcal{E}$ if, whenever $\mathcal{E}$ contains a closed formula asserting "not all $x$ satisfy $P$", there exists a witnessing constant $c_P$ in the alphabet and the corresponding Henkin axiom $R_P$ belongs to $\mathcal{E}$. This ensures that existential statements have concrete witnesses in the language, which is essential for constructing a term model in the proof of Gödel's Completeness Theorem.
