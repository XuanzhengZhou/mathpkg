The semantic definition of logical consequence captures the intuitive notion that S follows from E if S is true in every possible interpretation where E is true. The compactness of the definition (reducing infinite E to a finite subset) mirrors the Compactness Theorem. By Godel's Completeness Theorem, semantic consequence coincides with syntactic deducibility for first-order logic.
