Modular structures (or modular lattices) were introduced into quantum logic by Birkhoff and von Neumann in their seminal 1936 paper. The closed subspaces of a Hilbert space form a modular lattice under intersection and closed sum, but unlike Boolean algebras, the distributive law fails. Despite lacking the compatibility relation of partial Boolean algebras, modular structures remain an active area of investigation in quantum logic.
