The Display Lemma is the technical heart of the self-referential construction in SAr. It shows that the property of being displayed in a formula P is itself definable. This lemma enables the construction of a formula that "says of itself" that it has a certain property, which is the key step in proving Tarski's undefinability theorem for arithmetic.
