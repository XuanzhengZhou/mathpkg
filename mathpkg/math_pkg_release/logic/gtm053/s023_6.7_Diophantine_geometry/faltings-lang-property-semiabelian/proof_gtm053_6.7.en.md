Faltings proved this theorem (originally the Mordell-Lang conjecture for function fields in characteristic zero) using methods from arithmetic algebraic geometry, specifically p-adic Hodge theory and the theory of abelian varieties. The theorem can be re-interpreted in model-theoretic terms: A. Pillay showed that Faltings' theorem is equivalent to the statement that the theory of an algebraically closed field $K$ expanded with a predicate for the finitely generated subgroup $\Gamma$ is superstable, with the geometry of $\Gamma$ one-based. This equivalence demonstrates that the Lang property for finitely generated subgroups of semiabelian varieties in characteristic zero is a consequence of the model-theoretic structure being one-based.
