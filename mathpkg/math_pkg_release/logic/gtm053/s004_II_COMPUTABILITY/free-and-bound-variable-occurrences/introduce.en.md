This definition distinguishes between free and bound occurrences of variables in terms and formulas. A variable occurrence is bound if it falls within the scope of a quantifier $\forall x$ or $\exists x$; otherwise it is free. This distinction is crucial for semantics: a closed formula (one with no free variables) has a definite truth value, while formulas with free variables express properties that depend on how the free variables are interpreted.
