The Unique Reading Lemma guarantees that terms and formulas in languages of class $\mathfrak{L}_1$ can be parsed unambiguously. This is essential because the syntax rules are recursive and could potentially allow an expression to be interpreted in multiple ways. The lemma is proved by an inductive algorithm on the length of expressions, using the parentheses bijection. It serves as the foundation for inductive arguments throughout formal logic.
