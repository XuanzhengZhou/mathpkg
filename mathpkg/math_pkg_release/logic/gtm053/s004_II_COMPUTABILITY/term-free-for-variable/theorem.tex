A term $t$ is said to be free for a variable $x$ in a formula $P$ if, after the substitution of $t$ in place of each free occurrence of $x$, no free occurrence of a variable in $t$ becomes bound. More precisely, $t$ is free for $x$ in $P$ if for every variable $y$ occurring in $t$, no free occurrence of $x$ in $P$ lies within the scope of a quantifier $\forall y$ or $\exists y$ in $P$.
