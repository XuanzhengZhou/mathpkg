The tautologies are formulas that are true regardless of the truth or falsity of their component parts. The following are axiom schemas for tautologies in $L$:

\textbf{B1.} $\neg\neg P \Rightarrow P$ and $P \Rightarrow \neg\neg P$ (law of the excluded middle: double negation is equivalent to the original assertion).

\textbf{B2.} $\neg P \Rightarrow (P \Rightarrow Q)$ (the mechanism by which a contradiction leads to the deducibility of any formula, i.e. ex falso quodlibet).
