In languages of class $\mathfrak{L}_1$, formulas are the expressions that represent statements or propositions. They are built inductively from atomic formulas (obtained by applying relation symbols to terms) using logical connectives ($\Leftrightarrow$, $\Rightarrow$, $\vee$, $\wedge$, $\neg$) and quantifiers ($\forall$, $\exists$). Together with terms, formulas constitute the distinguished expressions of any first-order language.
