The Unique Reading Lemma is the fundamental syntactic result for first-order languages. It guarantees that every well-formed expression can be parsed in exactly one way, eliminating any ambiguity in the syntactic structure of terms and formulas. This lemma justifies all subsequent inductive definitions and proofs on the structure of expressions, including the definition of free and bound variables, substitution, and the truth function.
