Tarski's theorem that the theory of algebraically closed fields of a fixed characteristic is complete and admits quantifier elimination is a landmark result in model theory. It follows from the Steinitz theorem on the uniqueness of algebraically closed fields of given characteristic and transcendence degree. The quantifier elimination implies that every definable set is constructible (a Boolean combination of algebraic varieties), leading to the Lefshetz principle in algebraic geometry.
