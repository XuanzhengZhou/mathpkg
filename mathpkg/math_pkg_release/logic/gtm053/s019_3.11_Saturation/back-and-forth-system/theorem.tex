A back-and-forth system between $L$-structures $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ is a nonempty set $I$ of isomorphisms of substructures of $\mathbf{A}$ and substructures of $\mathbf{B}$ such that:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item For every $a \in A$ and $b \in B$ there exists $f_0 \in I$ with $a \in \text{Dom}(f_0)$ and $b \in \text{Range}(f_0)$.
  \item (Forth) For every $f \in I$ and $a \in A$ there exists $g \in I$ such that $f \subseteq g$ and $a \in \text{Dom}(g)$.
  \item (Back) For every $f \in I$ and $b \in B$ there exists $g \in I$ such that $f \subseteq g$ and $b \in \text{Range}(g)$.
\end{enumerate}
