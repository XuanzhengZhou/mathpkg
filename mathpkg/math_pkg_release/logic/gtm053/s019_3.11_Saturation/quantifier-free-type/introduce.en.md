The quantifier-free type $\text{qftp}(\bar{a})$ of a tuple $\bar{a}$ in a structure $\mathbf{A}$ is the set of all quantifier-free formulas satisfied by $\bar{a}$ in $\mathbf{A}$. Unlike the full type $\text{tp}(\bar{a})$, the quantifier-free type only records the atomic and Boolean combinations of atomic relations that hold of the tuple, ignoring quantifier complexity. It is the natural notion when studying quantifier elimination and model-completeness.
