For any complete theory $T$ and cardinal $\kappa \geq \operatorname{card} T$, there exists a $\kappa$-saturated model of $T$ of cardinality at most $\kappa^+$. Moreover, any two saturated models of the same cardinality are isomorphic. This theorem is fundamental in model theory: it guarantees the existence of saturated models with sufficient capacity to realize all small types, and it provides a strong uniqueness result — saturated models are determined up to isomorphism by their cardinality, analogous to the uniqueness of algebraically closed fields of a given characteristic and transcendence degree.
