In a saturated structure, the type of a tuple is a complete invariant for its orbit under the automorphism group. Two $n$-tuples have the same type if and only if there is an automorphism sending one to the other. This is a powerful structural property that connects the logical notion of type equality with the algebraic notion of symmetry (automorphisms), and it is a direct consequence of saturation/homogeneity.
