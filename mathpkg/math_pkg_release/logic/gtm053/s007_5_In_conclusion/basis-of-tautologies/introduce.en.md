This proposition asserts that propositional logic admits a finite axiomatization. A specific finite set of tautologies (the "basis") in three variables $P, Q, R$ is given, with the property that every tautology in any language $L$ of $\mathfrak{L}_1$ can be derived from substitution instances of the basis tautologies using only modus ponens (MP). The basis includes tautologies A0, A1, A2, A3, B1, B2 from Section 3.4, together with additional tautologies C1, C2, and others that encode the logical connectives. This result is a completeness theorem for the Hilbert-style deductive system based on the basis.
