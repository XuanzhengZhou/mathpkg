An interpretation (or model) assigns meaning to the symbols of a first-order language by mapping them to a concrete domain $M$. Constants become elements of $M$, operation symbols become functions on $M$, and relation symbols become subsets of Cartesian products of $M$. The truth of formulas is then defined recursively following the syntactic construction, with quantifiers interpreted as ranging over all possible assignments of values to variables. This Tarski-style definition provides the formal semantics for first-order logic.
