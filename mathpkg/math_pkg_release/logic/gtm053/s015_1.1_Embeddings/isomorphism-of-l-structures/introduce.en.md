An isomorphism between two $L$-structures is a surjective embedding. Since every embedding is injective (Exercise 1.2), an isomorphism is a bijective embedding — it preserves all $L$-symbols in both directions. Isomorphic structures are structurally identical from the perspective of the language $L$; they satisfy exactly the same $L$-sentences.
