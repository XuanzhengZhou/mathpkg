An embedding between two $L$-structures $A$ and $B$ is a map from the domain of $A$ to the domain of $B$ that preserves all symbols of the language $L$: relation symbols, operation symbols, and constant symbols. For relation symbols, it preserves truth in both directions (the "iff" condition). For operation and constant symbols, it preserves equality of terms. Embeddings are the natural notion of structure-preserving map in model theory, analogous to homomorphisms in algebra.
