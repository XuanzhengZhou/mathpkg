Definability of a structure generalizes the notion of a definable set: a whole $L_1$-structure can be defined inside an $L_0$-structure $A$ using $L_0$-formulas. An interpretation requires a definable domain $Q(A)$, a definable equivalence relation $E(A)$ on it, and definable relations $P_i(A)$ for each predicate symbol of $L_1$, all compatible with the equivalence. The quotient $Q(A)/E(A)$ then carries a natural $L_1$-structure that is said to be definable in $A$.
