Two $L$-structures are elementarily equivalent if they satisfy exactly the same $L$-sentences. Unlike isomorphism, elementary equivalence does not require the structures to have the same cardinality or be related by any map. It is a semantic notion capturing the idea that the two structures are indistinguishable by first-order properties expressible in the language $L$.
