This theorem, due to Gelfand and Ponomarev, constructs a Boolean subalgebra of size $2^n$ inside the $n$-fold product $L^n$ of a modular structure $L$. The construction uses iterated meets and joins over admissible sequences to define elements $H_t(l)$, and the substructure they generate turns out to be a Boolean algebra. This result is significant because substituting elements of this Boolean algebra for variables in classical Boolean tautologies yields "quantum tautologies" -- formulas that evaluate to $1$ in the modular structure. The theorem provides a bridge between classical propositional logic and the non-Boolean algebraic structures of quantum mechanics.
