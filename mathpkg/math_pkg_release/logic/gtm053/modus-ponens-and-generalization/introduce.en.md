Modus Ponens and Generalization are the two fundamental rules of deduction in first-order logic. MP captures the logical principle that if $P$ is true and $P$ implies $Q$, then $Q$ must be true. Gen captures the mathematical practice of asserting universally quantified identities: if $P$ is true regardless of the value of $x$, then the universal statement $\forall x P$ is true. These rules, together with the logical axioms, define the notion of formal deducibility.
