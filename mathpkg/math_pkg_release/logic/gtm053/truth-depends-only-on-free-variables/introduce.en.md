This proposition formalizes the intuition that the truth value of a formula depends only on the values assigned to its free variables. Bound variables are "dummy" variables whose particular values do not affect the truth of the formula. This fact justifies the standard practice of considering formulas as defining relations on the free variables, and it underlies the definition of closed formulas as those whose truth values are completely determined.
