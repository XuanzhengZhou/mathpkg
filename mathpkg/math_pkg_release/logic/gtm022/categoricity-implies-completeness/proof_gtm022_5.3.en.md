Let $p \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T})$ and suppose neither $p$ nor $\sim p$ is a theorem. Then both $\mathcal{T} \cup \{p\}$ and $\mathcal{T} \cup \{\sim p\}$ are consistent, so each has an infinite model. By Löwenheim-Skolem, each has a model of cardinal $\aleph$. These models are isomorphic by categoricity, but $p$ is true in one and false in the other, contradiction.
