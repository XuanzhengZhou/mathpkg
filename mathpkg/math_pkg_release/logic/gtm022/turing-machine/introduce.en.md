A Turing machine is a formal model of computation consisting of a finite state control, a tape divided into squares, and a read/write head. At each step, the machine reads the symbol under the head, and based on its current state, writes a new symbol, moves the head left or right, and changes state. The machine's program is a finite list of such instructions. Turing machines formalize the intuitive notion of an effective procedure.
