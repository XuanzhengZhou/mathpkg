A logic is adequate (or complete) if semantic truth implies syntactic provability: whenever $A \models p$ (every valuation making $A$ true also makes $p$ true), there exists a formal proof $A \vdash p$. Adequacy is the converse of soundness; together, soundness and adequacy establish that the notions of semantic consequence ($\models$) and syntactic deduction ($\vdash$) coincide. For the propositional calculus, the Adequacy Theorem (completeness theorem) asserts that every tautology is a theorem.
