A valuation on the free proposition algebra $P(X)$ is defined as a homomorphism into the two-element Boolean ring $\mathbb{Z}_2$. This formalizes the intuitive notion that each proposition is assigned a truth value (true = 1, false = 0), and that the truth value of a compound proposition is determined by the truth values of its components in a way compatible with the algebraic structure of $P(X)$. Since $X$ freely generates $P(X)$, any assignment of values in $\mathbb{Z}_2$ to the generators $x \in X$ extends uniquely to a valuation on the whole algebra.
