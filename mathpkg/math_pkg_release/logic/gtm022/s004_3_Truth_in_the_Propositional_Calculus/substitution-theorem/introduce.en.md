The Substitution Theorem states that homomorphisms between free proposition algebras preserve the semantic consequence relation. If $\varphi: P(X) \to P(Y)$ is a homomorphism and $w(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ is a proposition built from the generators $x_i$, then substituting $\varphi(x_i)$ for each $x_i$ throughout $w$ yields $\varphi(w)$. The theorem asserts that if a set $A$ of propositions semantically entails $w$ (i.e., $A \models w$), then the image $\varphi(A)$ semantically entails $\varphi(w)$ in $P(Y)$. This is a fundamental structural property of free algebras that enables substitution of equivalent expressions while preserving logical consequence.
