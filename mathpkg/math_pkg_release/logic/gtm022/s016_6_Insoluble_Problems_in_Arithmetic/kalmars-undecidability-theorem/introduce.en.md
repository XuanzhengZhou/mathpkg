Kalmar's theorem strengthens the undecidability results for predicate calculus by showing that even the fragment with a single binary predicate symbol $r$ is undecidable. The result implies that if the set of relation symbols $\mathcal{R}$ contains at least one $n$-ary relation symbol with $n \geq 2$, then $\text{Pred}(V, \mathcal{R})$ is undecidable. The proof constructs a reduction from the predicate calculus with a 4-ary relation symbol (known to be undecidable) to the binary case, using an ingenious encoding of 4-ary relations in terms of binary relations on a related set.
