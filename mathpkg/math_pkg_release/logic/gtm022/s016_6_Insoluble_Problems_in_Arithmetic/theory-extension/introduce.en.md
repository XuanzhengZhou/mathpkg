A first-order theory $\mathcal{T}$ is a triple $(\mathcal{R}, A, C)$ consisting of a set of relation symbols $\mathcal{R}$, a set of axioms $A$, and a set of constant symbols $C$. The extension relation $\mathcal{T}' \supseteq \mathcal{T}$ captures the idea that $\mathcal{T}'$ is built by adding more relation symbols, axioms, or constants to $\mathcal{T}$. This notion is fundamental for comparing the expressive power of different formal theories and for constructing stronger theories from weaker ones.
