When a first-order theory $\mathcal{T}'$ extends $\mathcal{T}$, it is natural to ask whether a given model of the smaller theory can be expanded to a model of the larger theory. Definition 6.2 formalizes this by requiring the existence of extended interpretation maps $v'$ and $\psi'$ for the new constant and relation symbols, such that the resulting structure satisfies all axioms of $\mathcal{T}'$. This concept is central to the model-theoretic analysis of theory extensions, including the construction of models for stronger arithmetical theories from models of weaker ones.
