The word problem for finitely presented semigroups (and groups) is algorithmically unsolvable. The proof encodes Turing machine computations into semigroup relations: the equivalence of words corresponds to whether the machine halts. This result (Novikov-Boone, 1950s) connects algebra with computability theory.
