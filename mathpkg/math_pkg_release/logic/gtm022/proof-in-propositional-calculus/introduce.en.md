A proof in the propositional calculus is a finite sequence of propositions where each step is either an axiom, an assumption, or follows from two earlier steps by Modus Ponens. The axioms are the standard Hilbert-style axioms for implication and negation. Modus Ponens is the sole rule of inference: from $p$ and $p \Rightarrow q$, deduce $q$.
