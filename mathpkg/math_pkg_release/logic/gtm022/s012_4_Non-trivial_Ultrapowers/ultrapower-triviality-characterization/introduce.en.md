Theorem 4.7 gives a precise characterization of when an ultrapower collapses to the original structure: the ultrapower $M^I / \mathcal{F}$ is isomorphic to $M$ (via the natural embedding) exactly when the ultrafilter $\mathcal{F}$ is $|M|$-complete. The forward direction uses Lemma 4.5 to show that an $\alpha$-complete ultrafilter forces every element of the ultrapower to be represented by a constant function. The converse shows that $\alpha$-incompleteness produces a non-trivial ultrapower containing elements not in the image of the diagonal embedding. This theorem is fundamental for producing non-standard models: by taking a non-principal ultrafilter on $\mathbb{N}$ (which is $\omega$-incomplete), one obtains proper ultrapower extensions of $\mathbb{N}$, $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathbb{Q}$, $\mathbb{R}$, and $\mathbb{C}$ that contain infinite and infinitesimal elements.
