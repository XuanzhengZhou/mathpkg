A tautology (or valid proposition) is a proposition that evaluates to true under every possible valuation. The notation $\models p$ expresses that $p$ is a logical truth — its truth depends only on the logical structure, not on the particular truth values assigned to its variables. The more general notion $A \models p$ captures semantic consequence: $p$ is true whenever all propositions in $A$ are true.
