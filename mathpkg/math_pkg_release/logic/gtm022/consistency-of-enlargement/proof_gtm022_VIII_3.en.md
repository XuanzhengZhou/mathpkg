Proof. Suppose $\mathcal{T}^\sigma \vdash F$. Then $A_0 \vdash_{\mathcal{T}} F$ for some finite subset $A_0$ of $A_\sigma$. Let $A_0 = \{\rho_j(x_{ij}, c_{\rho_j}) \mid i = 1, \ldots, r_j; j = 1, \ldots, n\}$. Since $\rho_j$ is concurrent, there exists $b_j \in D_{\rho_j}$ satisfying $\rho_j(x_{ij}, b_j)$ simultaneously for all $i$. Replacing each $c_{\rho_j}$ by $b_j$ yields true statements in $S$, so the corresponding subset of $A$ is true in $S$, contradicting $A_0 \vdash_{\mathcal{T}} F$. $\square$
