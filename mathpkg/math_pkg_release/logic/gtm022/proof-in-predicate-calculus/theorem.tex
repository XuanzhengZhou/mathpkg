Let $A \subseteq P(V, \mathcal{R})$, $p \in P(V, \mathcal{R})$. A proof of length $n$ of $p$ from $A$ in $\operatorname{Pred}(V, \mathcal{R})$ is a sequence $p_1, \ldots, p_n$ such that $p_n = p$, and for each $i$:
(a) $p_i \in \mathcal{A} \cup A$ (axiom or assumption), or
(b) $p_j = p_k \Rightarrow p_i$ for some $j, k < i$ (Modus Ponens), or
(c) $p_i = (\forall x)w(x)$ and some subsequence is a proof of $w(x)$ from $A_0 \subseteq A$ with $x \notin \operatorname{var}(A_0)$ (Generalisation).

The axioms $\mathcal{A}$ include all axioms of the propositional calculus plus:
$\mathcal{A}_4 = \{(\forall x)(p \Rightarrow q) \Rightarrow (p \Rightarrow ((\forall x)q)) \mid x \notin \operatorname{var}(p)\}$,
$\mathcal{A}_5 = \{(\forall x)p(x) \Rightarrow p(y) \mid p(x) \in P, y \in V\}$,
$\mathcal{A}_6 = \{p \Rightarrow (\forall x)p \mid x \notin \operatorname{var}(p)\}$.
