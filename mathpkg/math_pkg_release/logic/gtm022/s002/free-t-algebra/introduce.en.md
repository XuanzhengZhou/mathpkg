A free $T$-algebra on a set $X$ is the "most general" $T$-algebra generated by $X$, satisfying the universal property that any function from $X$ into any $T$-algebra $A$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism from the free algebra to $A$. The elements of $X$ are called free generators, and the map $\sigma$ embedding them into the free algebra is necessarily injective, provided there exists a $T$-algebra with more than one element.
