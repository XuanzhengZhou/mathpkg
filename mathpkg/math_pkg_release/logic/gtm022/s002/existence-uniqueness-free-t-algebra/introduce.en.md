This theorem establishes that for any type $T$ and any set $X$, a free $T$-algebra on $X$ exists and is unique up to isomorphism. The proof is in two parts: uniqueness follows from the universal property by showing that any two free algebras on the same set are mutually inverse isomorphisms. Existence is proved by an inductive construction building the free algebra as a union of layers $F_n$, where $F_0$ contains the generators and nullary operations, and $F_n$ contains formal expressions of increasing complexity.
