The Peano axioms characterize the natural numbers. The first four are first-order; the induction axiom ($P_5$) is second-order in its full form. The first-order theory $\mathcal{P}$ formalizes Peano arithmetic with a unary predicate for zero and a binary predicate for the successor relation.
