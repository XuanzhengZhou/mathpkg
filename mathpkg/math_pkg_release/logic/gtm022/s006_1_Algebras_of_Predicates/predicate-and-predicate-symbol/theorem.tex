Let $U$ be a set of objects under discussion. A predicate is a phrase such as "has the property $p$" relevant to elements of $U$. The notation $p(u)$ for $u \in U$ expresses the statement "$u$ has the property $p$", and $p$ is called a predicate symbol.

More generally, if $r$ is an $n$-ary relation on $U$, the statement "$(u_1, \ldots, u_n)$ is in the relation $r$" is denoted by $r(u_1, \ldots, u_n)$, and $r$ is called an $n$-ary predicate. A $0$-ary predicate is a statement which does not depend on any elements of $U$, and so corresponds to an unanalysed statement.
