A predicate expresses a property that elements of a given set $U$ may or may not possess. A predicate symbol $p$ is the syntactic name used to denote a predicate; the expression $p(u)$ asserts that $u$ has property $p$. An $n$-ary predicate generalizes this to relations among $n$-tuples, while a $0$-ary predicate is simply a statement with no dependence on elements of $U$ — the analogue of an unanalysed proposition in the Propositional Calculus.
