The existential quantifier $(\exists u)$ is not taken as primitive in this algebraic treatment. Instead, it is defined as the negation of a universal quantifier applied to a negated formula: $(\exists u)p(u)$ abbreviates $\sim((\forall u)(\sim p(u)))$. This expresses the familiar intuition that "there exists $u$ with property $p$" is equivalent to "it is not the case that for all $u$, $p(u)$ is false."
