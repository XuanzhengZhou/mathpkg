A proof from assumptions $A$ in the predicate calculus is an inductively defined sequence of formulas where each step is either a logical axiom or an assumption from $A$, follows by Modus Ponens from two earlier formulas, or is obtained by the Generalisation rule applied to a subproof. The Generalisation rule requires that the quantified variable does not occur free in any assumption used in that subproof. This definition extends the propositional notion of proof by incorporating the rule for universal quantifier introduction.
