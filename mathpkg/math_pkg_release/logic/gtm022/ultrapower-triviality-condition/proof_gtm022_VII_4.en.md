Proof. If $\mathcal{F}$ is $\alpha$-complete, then for any $f: I \to M$, the partition $\{J_m = \{i \mid f(i) = m\} \mid m \in M\}$ of $I$ into $\alpha$ sets has some $J_m \in \mathcal{F}$ by Lemma 4.6, so $f\mathcal{F} = m$. Thus every element of the ultrapower is standard. Conversely, if $\mathcal{F}$ is $\alpha$-incomplete, then $\alpha$ is infinite and one can construct a counterexample showing the ultrapower properly extends $M$. $\square$
