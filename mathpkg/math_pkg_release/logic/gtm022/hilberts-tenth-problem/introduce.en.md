Hilbert's Tenth Problem, posed in 1900, asked for an algorithm to determine whether a Diophantine equation has integer solutions. The problem was proved unsolvable in 1970 by Matiyasevich, building on work of Davis, Putnam, and Robinson. The negative solution follows from the MRDP theorem: recursively enumerable non-recursive sets can be encoded as Diophantine sets.
