Proof. Let $\tilde{A} = I \cup A$. Since $F \notin \operatorname{Ded}_g(A)$, $F \notin \operatorname{Ded}(\tilde{A})$ in $\operatorname{Pred}(V, \mathcal{R} \cup \{\mathcal{I}\})$. By the Satisfiability Theorem for Pred, there is an interpretation satisfying $\tilde{A}$. The identity axioms force $\psi(\mathcal{I})$ to be an equivalence relation respecting all other relations. Factoring the universe by this equivalence relation yields a proper interpretation satisfying $A$. $\square$
