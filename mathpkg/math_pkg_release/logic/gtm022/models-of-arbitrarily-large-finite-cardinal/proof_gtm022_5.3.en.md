Let $\mathcal{T} = (\mathcal{R}, A, C)$ and put $\mathcal{T}' = (\mathcal{R}, A \cup \{\operatorname{al}(n) \mid n \in \mathbb{N}^+\}, C)$. Any finite subset of the new axioms is satisfied by a sufficiently large finite model of $\mathcal{T}$, so $\mathcal{T}'$ is consistent by Compactness. A model of $\mathcal{T}'$ is infinite.
