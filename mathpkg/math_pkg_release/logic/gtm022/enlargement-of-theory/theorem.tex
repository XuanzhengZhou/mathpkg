Let $\sigma$ be a set of concurrent relations of $S$. For each $\rho \in \sigma$, take a new constant $c_\rho \notin V$ and form $V^\sigma = V \cup \{c_\rho\}$, $P^\sigma = P(V^\sigma, R)$. Put $A_\sigma = \{\rho(x, c_\rho) \mid \rho \in \sigma, x \in D_\rho\}$. The enlargement of $\mathcal{T} = (\mathcal{R}, A, S)$ with respect to $\sigma$ is $\mathcal{T}^\sigma = (\mathcal{R}, A \cup A_\sigma, S \cup \{c_\rho\})$. When $\sigma$ is the set of all concurrent relations, $\mathcal{T}^\sigma$ is the full enlargement $*\mathcal{T}$.
