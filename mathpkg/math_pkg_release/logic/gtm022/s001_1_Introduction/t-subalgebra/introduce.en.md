A $T$-subalgebra is the natural notion of substructure in universal algebra: a subset $B$ of a $T$-algebra $A$ that is closed under all the operations of $A$. For each $n$-ary operation symbol $t$, applying $t_A$ to elements of $B$ must yield a result still in $B$. This generalizes familiar concepts such as subgroups, subrings, and submodules to arbitrary algebraic structures defined by a type $\mathcal{T}$.
