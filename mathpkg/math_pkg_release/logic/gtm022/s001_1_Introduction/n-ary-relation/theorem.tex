An $n$-ary relation $\rho$ on the sets $A_1, \ldots, A_n$ is a subset of the Cartesian product $A_1 \times \cdots \times A_n$. That is, $\rho \subseteq A_1 \times \cdots \times A_n$, and we say $(a_1, \ldots, a_n) \in \rho$ to express that the $n$-tuple is in the relation. For binary relations ($n=2$), the notations $a_1 \rho a_2$, $(a_1, a_2) \in \rho$, and $\rho(a_1, a_2)$ are all used.
