An isomorphism of $T$-algebras is a bijective homomorphism $\varphi: A \rightarrow B$. By the preceding result, the inverse $\varphi^{-1}$ is automatically a homomorphism, so an isomorphism is precisely an invertible morphism in the category of $T$-algebras. Two $T$-algebras are said to be isomorphic if there exists an isomorphism between them, and this is denoted $A \cong B$. This completes the basic categorical framework for universal algebra: $T$-algebras as objects, homomorphisms as morphisms, and isomorphisms as the invertible morphisms.
