This simple proposition states that the composition of two $T$-algebra homomorphisms is again a homomorphism. For any $n$-ary operation symbol $t$, the homomorphism condition $(\psi \circ \varphi)(t_A(a_1, \ldots, a_n)) = t_C((\psi \circ \varphi)(a_1), \ldots, (\psi \circ \varphi)(a_n))$ follows by applying the homomorphism property of $\varphi$ and then $\psi$. Together with identity maps, this establishes that $T$-algebras and their homomorphisms form a category.
