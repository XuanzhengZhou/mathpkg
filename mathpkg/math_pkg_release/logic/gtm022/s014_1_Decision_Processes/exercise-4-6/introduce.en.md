This exercise asks the reader to use a cardinality argument to prove the existence of non-recursive functions. Since there are only countably many Turing machines (each with a finite description) but uncountably many functions from $\mathbb{N}$ to $\mathbb{N}$, most functions must be non-computable. This simple counting argument provides a non-constructive proof that uncomputable functions exist.
