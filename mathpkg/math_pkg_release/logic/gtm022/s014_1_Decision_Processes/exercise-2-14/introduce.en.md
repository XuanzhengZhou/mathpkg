This conceptual exercise invites the reader to reflect on the relationship between real-world digital computers and the formal Turing machine model. By considering how finite tapes can be extended by an operator replacing them, the exercise illustrates that the computational capabilities of physical computers -- despite their practical sophistication -- are subsumed by the Turing machine formalism, supporting the Church-Turing thesis.
