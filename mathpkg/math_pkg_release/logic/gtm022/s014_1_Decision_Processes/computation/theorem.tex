A \textbf{computation} by the machine $M$ is a finite sequence $[d_0], [d_1], \ldots, [d_p]$ of states such that $[d_0]$ is initial, $[d_p]$ is terminal, and $[d_i] \xrightarrow{M} [d_{i+1}]$ for $i = 0, 1, \ldots, p - 1$.

Computations are by definition finite. Given $M$ and $[d]$, there is no guarantee that $M$, started in state $[d]$ and allowed to operate, will ever stop (i.e., will execute a computation).
