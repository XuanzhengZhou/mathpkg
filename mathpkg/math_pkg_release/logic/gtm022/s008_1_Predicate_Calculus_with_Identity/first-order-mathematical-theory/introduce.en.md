A first-order mathematical theory $\mathcal{T} = (\mathbb{R}, A, C)$ is a triple consisting of a set $\mathbb{R}$ of relation symbols (always containing the identity symbol $\mathcal{I}$), a set $A$ of axioms, and a set $C$ of individual constants whose variables appear in the axioms. The language $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T})$ consists of all propositions whose free variables are among the constants $C$, and theorems are those language elements provable from the axioms in $\text{Pred}_g$.
