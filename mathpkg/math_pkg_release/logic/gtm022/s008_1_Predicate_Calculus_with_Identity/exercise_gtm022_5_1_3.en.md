Let $w(x, z)$ be any element of $P(V, \mathcal{R})$, possibly involving other variables besides $x, z$. Show that

$$I \vdash \mathcal{I}(x, y) \Rightarrow (w(x, z) \Rightarrow w(y, z)).$$

(This establishes that the identity axioms imply full substitutivity for arbitrary formulas.)
