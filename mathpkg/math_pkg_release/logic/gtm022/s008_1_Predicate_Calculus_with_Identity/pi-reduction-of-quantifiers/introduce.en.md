A theory $\mathcal{T}$ admits $\Pi$-reduction of quantifiers if every formula $p$ is provably equivalent in $\mathcal{T}$ to a quantifier-free propositional combination of formulas from a distinguished set $\Pi$ of "primary propositions." The utility of such a reduction depends on the simplicity of the elements of $\Pi$ relative to arbitrary formulas. When $\Pi$ consists of particularly simple formulas (e.g., equalities and cardinality statements), this yields a decision procedure for the theory.
