Every consistent first-order theory $\mathcal{T}$ can be extended to a consistent and complete theory by taking the set $A'$ of all sentences in the language that are true in a given model $M$ of $\mathcal{T}$. The proof is immediate: since $\mathcal{T}$ is consistent it has a model by the completeness theorem, and the set of all truths in that model forms a complete, consistent extension.
