$\text{Pred}_g(V, \mathcal{R})$ is the first-order predicate calculus enriched with a built-in identity relation. Proof in $\text{Pred}_g$ is defined by augmenting the hypothesis set with the identity axioms $I$ and working within the standard predicate calculus $\text{Pred}$. The notation $A \vdash_g p$ indicates provability from $A$ in this system, and the corresponding semantic consequence relation $A \vDash_g p$ is defined using only proper interpretations where $\mathcal{I}$ is interpreted as true equality.
