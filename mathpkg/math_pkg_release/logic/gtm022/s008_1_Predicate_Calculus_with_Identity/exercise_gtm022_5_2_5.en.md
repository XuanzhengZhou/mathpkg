Show that

$$(\forall x)m(x, e, x),$$
$$(\forall e')((\forall x)m(e', x, x) \Rightarrow \mathcal{I}(e, e')),$$
$$(\forall x)(\forall y)(m(y, x, e) \Rightarrow m(x, y, e))$$

are theorems of the elementary group theory of Example 2.4. Here $m$ is a ternary relation symbol representing the group multiplication (with $m(x, y, z)$ interpreted as $xy = z$), and $e$ is a constant symbol for the identity element.
