Godel's First Incompleteness Theorem (1931): any consistent, effectively axiomatised theory containing enough arithmetic is incomplete---there exist true statements about natural numbers that cannot be proved within the theory. The proof constructs a self-referential sentence (the Godel sentence) that asserts its own unprovability. This is one of the most profound results in the foundations of mathematics.
