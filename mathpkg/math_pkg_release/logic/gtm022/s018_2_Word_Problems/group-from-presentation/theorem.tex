Every group presentation $(X, R)$ determines a group: take the free group $F$ on $X$ and the smallest normal subgroup $K$ of $F$ which contains $R$; then the group determined by $(X, R)$ is the factor group $F/K$. We write $G = \langle X \mid R \rangle$ to indicate that $G$ is the group determined by the presentation $(X, R)$.
