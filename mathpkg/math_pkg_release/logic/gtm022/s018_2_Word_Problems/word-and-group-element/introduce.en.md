In the context of a group presentation $(X, R)$ with $G = F/K$, a formal word is an element of the free group $F$ on the generators $X$. The image of that word under the canonical quotient map $F \to F/K = G$ is the group element represented by the word. This distinction is essential for the word problem: given two words in $F$, determine whether they represent the same element of $G$, i.e., whether their difference lies in the normal closure $K$ of the relations.
