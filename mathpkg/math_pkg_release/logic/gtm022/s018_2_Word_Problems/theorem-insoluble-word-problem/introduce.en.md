Theorem 2.9 establishes the existence of a finitely presented semigroup with an insoluble word problem. The proof takes a recursively enumerable but non-recursive subset $E$ of the natural numbers and a Turing machine $M$ that halts exactly on inputs $n \in E$. The semigroup presentation associated with $M$ (via the construction of relations $(b_0)$, $(c)$, $(c_0)$, $(d)$) encodes the halting behavior: by Lemma 2.8, equivalence of two special words reduces to the existence of a halting computation, which is undecidable since $E$ is non-recursive.
