A presentation $(X, R)$ is called *abelian* if, for every $x, y \in X$, we have $x^{-1}y^{-1}xy \in R$. Show that the word problem for a finite abelian presentation is soluble. Show also that the isomorphism problem is soluble for pairs of finite abelian presentations.
