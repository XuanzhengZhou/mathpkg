Let $\mathcal{T}$ be a consistent, effectively axiomatised, and complete first-order theory. Then $\mathcal{T}$ is decidable. Moreover, there exists a Turing machine which, when given the Gödel number of an element $p \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T})$, answers the question of whether or not $p$ is a theorem of $\mathcal{T}$, and also provides a proof of its answer.
