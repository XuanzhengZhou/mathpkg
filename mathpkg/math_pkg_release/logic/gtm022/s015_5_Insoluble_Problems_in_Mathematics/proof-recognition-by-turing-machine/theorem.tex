Let $\mathcal{T} = (\mathcal{R}, A, C)$ be an effectively axiomatised first-order theory. Then there exists a Turing machine which, when given the Gödel numbers of $p \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{T})$ and of the sequence $p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n$ of elements of $P(V, \mathcal{R})$, determines whether $p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_n$ is a proof of $p$ in $\mathcal{T}$.
