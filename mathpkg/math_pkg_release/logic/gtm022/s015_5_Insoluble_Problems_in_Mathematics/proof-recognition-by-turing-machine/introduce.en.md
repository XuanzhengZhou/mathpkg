For an effectively axiomatised first-order theory, the notion of a proof is mechanically checkable. Given the Gödel numbers of a formula and a candidate proof sequence, a Turing machine can verify whether the sequence constitutes a valid proof in the theory. This follows from the fact that the set of axioms is recursively enumerable and the rules of consequence are effective.
