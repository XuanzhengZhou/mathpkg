For any effectively axiomatised theory $\mathcal{T}$, the question of whether an arbitrary formula $p$ is a theorem is soluble in principle — though not necessarily by a uniform effective procedure. If $p$ is a theorem, one can in principle find a proof. If $p$ is not a theorem, then $\mathcal{T} \cup \{\sim p\}$ is consistent and therefore has a model; exhibiting such a model demonstrates that $p$ is not provable. Note that this does not provide a general decision procedure — that is a separate and typically harder problem.
