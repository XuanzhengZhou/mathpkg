Let $I$ be a set. A set $\mathcal{F}$ of subsets of $I$ is called a **filter** on $I$ if it satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\varnothing \notin \mathcal{F}$.
\item[(ii)] Every subset of $I$ which contains a set of $\mathcal{F}$ belongs to $\mathcal{F}$.
\item[(iii)] Every finite intersection of sets of $\mathcal{F}$ belongs to $\mathcal{F}$.
\end{enumerate}
A filter which additionally satisfies
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(iv)] For each $A \subseteq I$, exactly one of $A$ and $I \setminus A$ belongs to $\mathcal{F}$,
\end{enumerate}
is called an **ultrafilter**.
