A theory $\mathcal{T}$ is called algebraic if every axiom of $\mathcal{T}$ is either universal or has the form $(\forall x_1) \cdots (\forall x_r)(\exists y_1) \cdots (\exists y_s) p(x_1, \ldots, x_r, y_1, \ldots, y_s, c_1, \ldots, c_t)$, where $c_i \in C$ and $p$ is constructed from primitive elements using $\lor$ and $\land$ only (no negation, no implication).
