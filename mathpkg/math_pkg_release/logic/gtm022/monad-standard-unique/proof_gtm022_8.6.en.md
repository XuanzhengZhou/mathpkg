Uniqueness: if $r, s \in \mu(a)$ are standard, then $|r-s|$ is an infinitesimal standard real, so $|r-s|=0$ and $r=s$. Existence: consider $\{r \in \mathbb{R} \mid r < a\}$, a Dedekind cut, and by completeness of $\mathbb{R}$ it has a least upper bound $r_0$. Then $r_0 \in \mu(a)$. That $R_0$ is a ring and $R_1$ an ideal follows from the algebraic properties of infinitesimals. The map sending each finite $a$ to the unique standard real in $\mu(a)$ induces the isomorphism $R_0/R_1 \cong \mathbb{R}$.
