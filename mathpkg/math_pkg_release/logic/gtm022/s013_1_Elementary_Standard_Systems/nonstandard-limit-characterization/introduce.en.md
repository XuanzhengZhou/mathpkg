In non-standard analysis, the classical epsilon-delta definition of a limit is replaced by a simple monad condition: $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \ell$ if and only if the non-standard extension $*f$ maps every $x \neq a$ in the monad of $a$ into the monad of $\ell$. This elegantly captures the intuition that $f(x)$ is "infinitely close" to $\ell$ whenever $x$ is "infinitely close" to $a$.
