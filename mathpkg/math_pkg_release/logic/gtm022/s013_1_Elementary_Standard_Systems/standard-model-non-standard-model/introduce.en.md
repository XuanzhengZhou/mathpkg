A model $*\mathbb{S}$ of the complete theory $\mathcal{T}(\mathbb{S})$ is called standard if it is isomorphic to the original system $\mathbb{S}$, and non-standard otherwise. Since first-order axiomatizations of standard mathematical systems are never categorical, non-standard models always exist. The study of non-standard models provides a powerful method for discovering properties of the original standard system.
