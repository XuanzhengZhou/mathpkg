We have that

$$\mathcal{T}(\mathcal{O}^1(\mathbb{S})) \vdash (\forall x)(\forall y) ((x \in \mathcal{R}_n^{(1)}) \wedge (y \in \mathcal{R}_n^{(1)}) \Rightarrow (x \cap y \in \mathcal{R}_n^{(1)})),$$

and similarly for union and complement. Since these are theorems of the complete theory, they remain true in the enlargement $(\mathcal{O}^1(\mathbb{S}))^\sigma$. As $u, v \in (\mathcal{R}_n^{(1)})^\sigma$ are internal, their Boolean combinations are also elements of $(\mathcal{R}_n^{(1)})^\sigma$, hence internal.
