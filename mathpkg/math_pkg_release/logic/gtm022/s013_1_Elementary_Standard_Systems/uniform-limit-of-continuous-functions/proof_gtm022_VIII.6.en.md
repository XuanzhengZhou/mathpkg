Let $a \in U$. If $x \in *U$ and $x \cong a$, and if $n$ is infinite, then by Corollary 6.11 and Theorem 6.14,

$$*r(x) \cong *s_n(x) \cong *s_n(a) \cong *r(a),$$

showing that $r$ is continuous at $a$. 

The reasoning: The first congruence $*r(x) \cong *s_n(x)$ and the last congruence $*s_n(a) \cong *r(a)$ follow from Theorem 6.16 (uniform convergence: for all $x \in *U$ and infinite $n$, $*s_n(x) \cong *r(x)$). The middle congruence $*s_n(x) \cong *s_n(a)$ follows from Corollary 6.11, since each $s_n$ is continuous at $a$ and $x \cong a$.
