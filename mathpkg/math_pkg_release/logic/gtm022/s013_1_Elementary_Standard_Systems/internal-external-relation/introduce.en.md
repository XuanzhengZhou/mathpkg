In an enlargement $\mathbb{S}^\sigma$, relations that come from the enlargement of the set of all $n$-ary relations $\mathcal{R}_n^{(1)}$ are called internal. Relations that are not internal are called external. Every standard (definable) relation is internal, but the converse fails for infinite systems: since $\mathcal{R}_n^{(1)}$ is infinite, its star-extension properly contains it, producing internal relations that are not standard.
