Every monad of a finite non-standard real number contains exactly one standard real number, making the standard reals a set of canonical representatives. The finite reals $R_0$ form a ring, the infinitesimals $R_1$ form a maximal ideal in $R_0$, and the quotient $R_0/R_1$ is isomorphic to the standard real numbers $\mathbb{R}$. This provides the algebraic foundation for Robinson's non-standard analysis.
