This follows directly from Theorem 6.10 with $\ell = f(a)$. By that theorem, $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)$ if and only if $*f(x) \in \mu(f(a))$ for all $x \neq a$ in $\mu(a)$. The condition $x = a$ is handled separately: $*f(a) = f(a)$ by definition of the star extension, so $*f(a) \cong f(a)$ trivially. Thus $f$ is continuous at $a$ (i.e., $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)$) if and only if $*f(x) \cong *f(a)$ for all $x \in \mu(a)$.
