An element $p \in P(V, \mathcal{R})$ is said to be expressed in *prenex normal form* when it is expressed in the form $p = Q_1 Q_2 \cdots Q_k q$, where $Q_i$ is either $(\forall x_i)$ or $(\exists x_i)$ for some $x_i \in V$, and $q$ contains no quantifiers. Show that every element of $P(V, \mathcal{R})$ can be expressed in prenex normal form.
