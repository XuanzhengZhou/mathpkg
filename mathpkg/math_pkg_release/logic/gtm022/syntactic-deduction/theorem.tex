Let $q \in P(X)$ and let $A \subseteq P(X)$. We say that $q$ is \emph{deducible} from $A$, or $q$ is a \emph{syntactic consequence} of $A$, written $A \vdash q$ (or $q \in \operatorname{Ded}(A)$), if there exists a proof of $q$ from $A$ in the propositional calculus. If $\emptyset \vdash q$, we call $q$ a \emph{theorem} of the propositional calculus and write $\vdash q$.
