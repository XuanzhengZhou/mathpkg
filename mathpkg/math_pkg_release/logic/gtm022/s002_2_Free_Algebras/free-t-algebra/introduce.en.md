A free $T$-algebra on a set $X$ is the most general $T$-algebra generated by $X$, subject only to the operations of the type $T$ and no additional relations. The defining universal property states that any mapping from the generators $X$ into an arbitrary $T$-algebra $A$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism from $F$ to $A$. If $(F, \sigma)$ is free on $X$, then the map $\sigma: X \to F$ is necessarily injective, since there exists a $T$-algebra with more than one element so distinct generators cannot be identified.
