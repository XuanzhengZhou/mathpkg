Proof. If $r, s \in \mu(a)$ are standard, then $|r - s|$ is an infinitesimal standard real number, so $r = s$. For existence, if $a$ is finite, the Dedekind cut determined by $a$ in the standard reals has a least upper bound $r$, which is the unique standard real in $\mu(a)$. The ring and ideal properties follow from the algebraic closure of finite and infinitesimal elements. $\square$
