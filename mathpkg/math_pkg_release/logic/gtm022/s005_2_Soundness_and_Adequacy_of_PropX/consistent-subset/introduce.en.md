A subset $A$ of the propositional algebra $P(X)$ is called consistent if the false proposition $F$ is not in its deductive closure $\text{Ded}(A)$, meaning no contradiction can be derived from $A$. A consistent set $A$ is maximal consistent if it cannot be properly extended to a larger consistent set — every proper superset becomes inconsistent. These concepts are central to the Lindenbaum-style proof of the Adequacy Theorem, where maximal consistent sets are used to construct valuations witnessing non-provability.
