The Soundness Theorem for propositional logic states that provability implies semantic consequence: if a proposition $p$ can be proved from a set of assumptions $A$ within the formal system Prop$(X)$, then $p$ is a logical consequence of $A$ (every valuation that makes all formulas in $A$ true also makes $p$ true). The proof proceeds by induction on the length of the proof, using the fact that every axiom is a tautology and that modus ponens preserves truth under any valuation.
