The Consistency Theorem for propositional logic states that the propositional constant $F$ (falsum, representing falsehood) is not provable within the formal system $\text{Prop}(X)$. This is an immediate corollary of the Soundness Theorem: every theorem is a tautology, but $F$ evaluates to $0$ under any valuation and is thus not a tautology, so it cannot be a theorem. The result establishes that the propositional calculus is consistent, meaning it does not prove a contradiction.
