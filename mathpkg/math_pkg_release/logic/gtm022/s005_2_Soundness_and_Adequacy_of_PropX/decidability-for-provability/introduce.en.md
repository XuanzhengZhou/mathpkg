Corollary 3.6 states that $\text{Prop}(X)$ is decidable for provability. This follows immediately from the Soundness and Adequacy Theorems: a proposition $p$ is a theorem if and only if it is valid. Since validity is decidable by the truth-table algorithm (Theorem 3.5), provability is likewise decidable by the same algorithm. Thus the propositional calculus admits a mechanical decision procedure for theoremhood.
