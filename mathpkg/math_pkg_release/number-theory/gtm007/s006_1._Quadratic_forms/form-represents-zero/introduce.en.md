Proposition 3' is the translation (into the language of quadratic forms) of the module-theoretic fact that a nondegenerate quadratic module containing a nonzero isotropic vector splits off a hyperbolic plane. A key consequence is that such a form is universal: it represents every element of the field. This is the starting point of Witt decomposition.
