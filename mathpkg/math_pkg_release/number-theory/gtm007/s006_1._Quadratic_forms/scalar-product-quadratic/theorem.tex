For a quadratic module $(V,Q)$ over a field $k$ of characteristic $\neq 2$, define

$$x \cdot y = \frac{1}{2}\bigl(Q(x+y) - Q(x) - Q(y)\bigr).$$

The map $(x,y) \mapsto x \cdot y$ is a symmetric bilinear form on $V$, called the \textbf{scalar product} associated with $Q$. One has $Q(x) = x \cdot x$. This establishes a bijective correspondence between quadratic forms and symmetric bilinear forms on $V$.
