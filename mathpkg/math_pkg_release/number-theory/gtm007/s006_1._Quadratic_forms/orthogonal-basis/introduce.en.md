An orthogonal basis diagonalizes the quadratic form: the Gram matrix becomes diagonal with entries $a_i = e_i \cdot e_i$, and the quadratic form simplifies to $Q(x) = \sum a_i x_i^2$. The existence of orthogonal bases (Theorem 1) is fundamental — it shows that every quadratic form over a field of characteristic not 2 can be diagonalized, which is the algebraic analogue of completing the square.
