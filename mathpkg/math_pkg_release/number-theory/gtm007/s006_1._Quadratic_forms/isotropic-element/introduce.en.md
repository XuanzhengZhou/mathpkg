An isotropic vector is a nonzero (or zero) vector on which the quadratic form evaluates to zero, generalizing the notion of light-like vectors in relativity. A subspace is isotropic if every vector in it is isotropic, which is equivalent to the subspace being contained in its own orthogonal complement. Isotropic subspaces play a central role in the structure theory of quadratic forms, particularly in Witt decomposition.
