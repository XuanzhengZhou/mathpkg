\textbf{Theorem 2.} Let $(V,Q)$ be a nondegenerate quadratic module of dimension $\ge 3$, and let $e = (e_1, \dots, e_n)$, $e' = (e'_1, \dots, e'_n)$ be two orthogonal bases of $V$. There exists a finite sequence $e^{(0)}, e^{(1)}, \dots, e^{(m)}$ of orthogonal bases such that $e^{(0)} = e$, $e^{(m)} = e'$, and $e^{(k)}$ is contiguous to $e^{(k+1)}$ for each $k$.
