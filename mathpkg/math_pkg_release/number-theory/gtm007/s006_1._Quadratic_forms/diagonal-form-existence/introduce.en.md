Theorem 1' is the translation of Theorem 1 into the language of quadratic forms (polynomials in $X_i$): every quadratic form can be diagonalized to $\sum a_i X_i^2$. The rank is the number of nonzero coefficients, equal to the codimension of the radical. The form is nondegenerate precisely when all coefficients are nonzero, i.e., when the discriminant is nonzero in $k^*/k^{*2}$.
