source_section: "§1.7 Quadratic forms over F_q"
---

In view of Corollary 1 to Proposition 3', it suffices to prove that all quadratic forms in 3 variables over $\mathbf{F}_q$ represent 0. This has been proved in Chapter I, §2 (using the fact that in a finite field, the equation $ax^2 + by^2 = c$ always has a solution when $a, b \neq 0$). Once a form in 3 variables represents 0, by Proposition 3' it represents all elements of $\mathbf{F}_q$. For rank $\ge 2$, restricting to a 2-dimensional nondegenerate subspace and applying the rank-3 result to its orthogonal sum with a suitable 1-dimensional form yields the claim.
