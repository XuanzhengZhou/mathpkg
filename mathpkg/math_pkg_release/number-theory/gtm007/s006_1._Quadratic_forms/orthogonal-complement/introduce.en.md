Two subspaces are orthogonal if every vector in one is orthogonal to every vector in the other. The map $q_U: V \to U^*$ sending $x$ to the linear form $y \mapsto x \cdot y$ on $U$ is a fundamental tool for relating orthogonality to duality; its kernel is precisely the orthogonal complement $U^0$.
