Witt's Cancellation Theorem states that equivalent forms can be "cancelled": if $g + h \sim g' + h'$ and $g \sim g'$, then $h \sim h'$. This is a direct corollary of Witt's Extension Theorem and is fundamental to the definition of the Witt group, where the equivalence class of a form is its "Witt class". The theorem implies that the hyperbolic part of a Witt decomposition is uniquely determined.
