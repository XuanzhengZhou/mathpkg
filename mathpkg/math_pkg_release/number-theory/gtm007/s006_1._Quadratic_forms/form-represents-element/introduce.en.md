A quadratic form $f$ represents $a \in k$ if $a$ is a nonzero value taken by $f$ on $k^n \setminus \{0\}$. The representation of 0 is equivalent to the existence of a nonzero isotropic vector, which is a central notion in the classification of quadratic forms. Understanding which elements a form represents is one of the main problems in the arithmetic theory of quadratic forms.
