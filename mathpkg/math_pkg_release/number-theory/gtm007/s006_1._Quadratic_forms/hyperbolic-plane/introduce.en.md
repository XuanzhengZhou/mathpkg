A hyperbolic plane is the simplest example of an isotropic quadratic module that is still nondegenerate. It is spanned by two isotropic vectors whose scalar product is nonzero (normalized to 1). Its matrix $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ has discriminant $-1$. Hyperbolic planes are the building blocks of the Witt decomposition: every quadratic form splits as an orthogonal sum of hyperbolic planes and an anisotropic part.
