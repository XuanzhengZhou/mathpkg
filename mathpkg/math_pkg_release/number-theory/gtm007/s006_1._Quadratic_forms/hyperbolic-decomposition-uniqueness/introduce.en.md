The Witt decomposition theorem states that every nondegenerate quadratic form splits uniquely (up to equivalence) as an orthogonal sum of hyperbolic planes plus an anisotropic form that does not represent 0. The number $m$ of hyperbolic planes is the Witt index, equal to the dimension of any maximal isotropic subspace. This decomposition is the cornerstone of the Witt group classification of quadratic forms.
