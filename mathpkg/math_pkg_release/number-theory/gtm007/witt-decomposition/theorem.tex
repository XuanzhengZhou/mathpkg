\textbf{Corollary (Witt decomposition).} If $f$ is nondegenerate, then $f \sim g_1 + \cdots + g_m + h$ where $g_1, \ldots, g_m$ are hyperbolic and $h$ does not represent $0$. This decomposition is unique up to equivalence. (The number $m$ of hyperbolic factors equals the dimension of maximal isotropic subspaces of the associated quadratic module.)
