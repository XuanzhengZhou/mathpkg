A character of a finite abelian group is a multiplicative homomorphism into $\mathbb{C}^*$. Characters form the foundation of Fourier analysis on finite abelian groups and are the key tool for Dirichlet's proof of the theorem on arithmetic progressions. For a cyclic group, a character is determined by its value on a generator, which must be an $n$-th root of unity.
