The Frobenius endomorphism $\sigma(x) = x^p$ is a fundamental structural feature of fields of prime characteristic $p$. It is always a field homomorphism because $(x+y)^p = x^p + y^p$ in characteristic $p$ (all intermediate binomial coefficients vanish modulo $p$), and it is injective, hence an isomorphism onto the subfield $K^p$ of $p$-th powers. The Frobenius map plays a central role in the classification and Galois theory of finite fields.
