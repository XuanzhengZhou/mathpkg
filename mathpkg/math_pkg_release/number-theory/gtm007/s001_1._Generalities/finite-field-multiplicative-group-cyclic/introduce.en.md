This theorem states that for any finite field $\mathbf{F}_q$ with $q$ elements, the group of nonzero elements under multiplication is cyclic. In particular, $\mathbf{F}_q^*$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}/(q-1)\mathbb{Z}$. This implies the existence of primitive elements (generators of the multiplicative group) in every finite field, a fact of fundamental importance in the theory of finite fields, coding theory, and number theory.
