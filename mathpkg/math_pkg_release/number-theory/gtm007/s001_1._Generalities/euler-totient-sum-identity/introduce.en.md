This elementary identity states that the sum of Euler's totient function $\phi(d)$ over all positive divisors $d$ of $n$ equals $n$ itself. The proof is a simple counting argument using the cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$: every element generates a unique cyclic subgroup $C_d$, and the number of generators of $C_d$ is $\phi(d)$. This identity is a key ingredient in proving that the multiplicative group of a finite field is cyclic.
