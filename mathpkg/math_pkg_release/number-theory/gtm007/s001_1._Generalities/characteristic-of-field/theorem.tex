Let $K$ be a field. The image of $\mathbb{Z}$ in $K$ is an integral domain, hence isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}$ or to $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$, where $p$ is prime; its field of fractions is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Q}$ or to $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z} = \mathbf{F}_p$. In the first case, one says that $K$ is of \text{characteristic zero}; in the second case, that $K$ is of \text{characteristic } $p$.

The characteristic of $K$ is denoted by $\operatorname{char}(K)$. If $\operatorname{char}(K) = p \neq 0$, then $p$ is also the smallest integer $n > 0$ such that $n \cdot 1 = 0$.
