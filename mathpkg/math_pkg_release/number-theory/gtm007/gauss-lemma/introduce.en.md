Gauss's lemma provides a combinatorial characterization of the Legendre symbol: $\left(\frac{a}{p}\right)$ equals the parity of the number of elements in $\{a, 2a, \ldots, \frac{p-1}{2}a\}$ whose least absolute residue modulo $p$ is negative. The proof uses that the map $s \mapsto s_a$ is a bijection of $S$, and that multiplying the equalities $as = e_s(a)s_a$ yields $a^{(p-1)/2} = \prod e_s(a)$.
