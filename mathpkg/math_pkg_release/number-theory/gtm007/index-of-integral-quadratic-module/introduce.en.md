The index $\tau(E) = r - s$ measures the signature of the quadratic form extended to $\mathbb{R}$. A positive definite form has $\tau = r$, a negative definite form has $\tau = -r$, and indefinite forms have intermediate values. The parity condition $r \equiv \tau \pmod{2}$ always holds.
