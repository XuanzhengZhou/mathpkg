Let $K$ be a field. The image of $\mathbb{Z}$ in $K$ is an integral domain, hence isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}$ or to $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$ where $p$ is prime. In the first case, $K$ is of characteristic zero; in the second case, $K$ is of characteristic $p$.

The characteristic of $K$ is denoted by $\operatorname{char}(K)$. If $\operatorname{char}(K) = p \neq 0$, $p$ is the smallest integer $n > 0$ such that $n \cdot 1 = 0$.
