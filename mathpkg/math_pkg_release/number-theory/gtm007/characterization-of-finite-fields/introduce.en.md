This theorem gives a complete classification of finite fields. Every finite field has prime power order $p^f$, and for each $q = p^f$ there exists exactly one finite field up to isomorphism. The construction is explicit: $\mathbb{F}_q$ is the set of roots of $X^q - X$ in an algebraic closure of $\mathbb{F}_p$. The uniqueness is fundamental for all subsequent arithmetic applications in the book.
