The scalar product associated with $Q$ is defined by
$$x \cdot y = \frac{1}{2}\{Q(x+y) - Q(x) - Q(y)\}.$$
The map $(x,y) \mapsto x \cdot y$ is a symmetric bilinear form on $V$, and $Q(x) = x \cdot x$. This establishes a bijective correspondence between quadratic forms and symmetric bilinear forms (valid only in characteristic $\neq 2$).
