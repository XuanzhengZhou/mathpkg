Dirichlet's theorem is strengthened by the density result: primes are equidistributed among the $\phi(m)$ residue classes coprime to $m$, each receiving density $1/\phi(m)$. The proof uses the behavior of $f_\chi(s) = \sum_{p \nmid m} \chi(p)p^{-s}$ as $s \to 1^+$: the term for $\chi = 1$ gives $\log(1/(s-1))$, while all other terms remain bounded.
