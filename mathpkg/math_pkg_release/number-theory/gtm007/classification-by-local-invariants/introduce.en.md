The corollary makes the classification explicit: a quadratic form over $\mathbb{Q}$ is uniquely determined by its rank $n$, signature $(r,s)$, discriminant $d \in \mathbb{Q}^*/\mathbb{Q}^{*2}$, and the family $(\varepsilon_v)_{v \in V}$ of local Hasse-Witt invariants satisfying the product formula.
