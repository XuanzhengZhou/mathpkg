The functions $\varepsilon(n)$ and $\omega(n)$ encode the residue of an odd integer $n$ modulo $4$ and $8$, respectively. They are used to express the supplementary laws of quadratic reciprocity: $\left(\frac{-1}{p}\right) = (-1)^{\varepsilon(p)}$ and $\left(\frac{2}{p}\right) = (-1)^{\omega(p)}$. The function $\varepsilon$ is a homomorphism of $(\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z})^*$ onto $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$, and $\omega$ is a homomorphism of $(\mathbb{Z}/8\mathbb{Z})^*$ onto $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.
