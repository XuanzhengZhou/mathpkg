Theorem 4 gives a complete criterion for an element of $\mathbb{Q}_2^*$ to be a square: $x = 2^n u$ is a square precisely when the exponent $n$ is even and the unit $u$ satisfies $u \equiv 1 \pmod{8}$. The condition $u \equiv 1 \pmod{8}$ is the $2$-adic analogue of the familiar fact that an odd integer is a square modulo a power of $2$ only if it is congruent to $1$ modulo $8$. This theorem relies on the structure of $U_2 \cong \mathbb{Z}_2$, where the squares correspond exactly to the subgroup $U_3$.
