For every $n \geq 1$, let $A_n = \mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z}$ be the ring of integers modulo $p^n$. The natural reduction map defines a surjective homomorphism
$$\phi_n: A_n \rightarrow A_{n-1},$$
whose kernel is $p^{n-1}A_n$. The ring of $p$-adic integers $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is the projective limit
$$\mathbb{Z}_p = \varprojlim_{n \geq 1} (A_n, \phi_n).$$
An element of $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is a sequence $x = (\ldots, x_n, \ldots, x_1)$ with $x_n \in A_n$ and $\phi_n(x_n) = x_{n-1}$ for $n \geq 2$. Addition and multiplication are defined coordinate-wise. Equipped with the topology inherited from the product $\prod A_n$ (each $A_n$ discrete), $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is a compact topological ring.
