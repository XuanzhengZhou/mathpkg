*Proof.* **Case 1: $p \neq 2$.** Choose an element $\alpha \in U_1 \setminus U_2$, for example $\alpha = 1 + p$. We need a preliminary lemma: for such $\alpha$, one has $\alpha^{p^i} \in U_{i+1} \setminus U_{i+2}$. This follows by induction using the binomial expansion and the fact that $p \mid \binom{p}{j}$ for $1 \leq j \leq p-1$, together with the condition that $n \geq 1$ (and $n \geq 2$ when $p=2$ as treated separately).

Let $\alpha_n$ be the image of $\alpha$ in $U_1/U_n$. The lemma implies $(\alpha_n)^{p^{n-2}} \neq 1$ and $(\alpha_n)^{p^{n-1}} = 1$. But $U_1/U_n$ has order $p^{n-1}$ (as shown in Proposition 7); hence it is a cyclic group generated by $\alpha_n$. Now define the homomorphism
$$\theta_{n,\alpha}: \mathbb{Z}/p^{n-1}\mathbb{Z} \longrightarrow U_1/U_n, \quad z \mapsto \alpha_n^z.$$
The commutative diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
\mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z} & \xrightarrow{\theta_{n+1,\alpha}} & U_1/U_{n+1} \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow \\
\mathbb{Z}/p^{n-1}\mathbb{Z} & \xrightarrow{\theta_{n,\alpha}} & U_1/U_n
\end{array}$$
shows that the $\theta_{n,\alpha}$ define, by passage to the projective limit, an isomorphism
$$\theta: \mathbb{Z}_p = \varprojlim \mathbb{Z}/p^{n-1}\mathbb{Z} \xrightarrow{\sim} U_1 = \varprojlim U_1/U_n.$$

**Case 2: $p = 2$.** One has $U_1 = \{\pm 1\} \times U_2$, since $U_1 = \{x \in \mathbb{Z}_2^\times : x \equiv 1 \pmod{2}\} = \{1, -1, 5, -5, \ldots\}$ and $-1$ has order $2$ while $U_2$ contains no $2$-torsion. For $U_2$, the same construction works with $\alpha = 5 = 1 + 2^2$, and one obtains $U_2 \cong \mathbb{Z}_2$. The filtration becomes $U_n = \{x \in \mathbb{Z}_2^\times : x \equiv 1 \pmod{2^n}\}$ and the generator $\alpha = 5$ satisfies $\alpha^{2^{n-2}} \in U_n \setminus U_{n+1}$ for $n \geq 3$. $\square$
