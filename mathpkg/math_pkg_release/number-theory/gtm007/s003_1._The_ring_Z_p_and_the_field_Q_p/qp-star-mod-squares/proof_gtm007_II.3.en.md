*Proof.* From the structure theorem for $\mathbb{Q}_p^*$ (Theorem 2 in Serre), we have the decomposition
$$\mathbb{Q}_p^* \cong \mathbb{Z} \times V \times U_1,$$
where the $\mathbb{Z}$ factor corresponds to the valuation $v_p$ (generated by $p$), $V \cong \mathbb{F}_p^*$ is the group of Teichmüller representatives (order $p-1$), and $U_1 \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ is the group of $1$-units. An element $x = p^n v u_1$ (with $n \in \mathbb{Z}$, $v \in V$, $u_1 \in U_1$) is a square if and only if each component is a square.

- In the $\mathbb{Z}$ factor: $p^n$ is a square iff $n$ is even.
- In $V \cong \mathbb{F}_p^*$: since $p \neq 2$, the squaring map on the cyclic group of order $p-1$ has image of index $2$; $v$ is a square iff it is a quadratic residue modulo $p$, i.e., $\left(\frac{v}{p}\right) = +1$.
- In $U_1 \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$: since $p \neq 2$, $2$ is invertible in $\mathbb{Z}_p$, so every element of $U_1$ is a square (the map $z \mapsto z^2$ on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is surjective because $2 \in \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$).

Therefore, passing to the quotient $\mathbb{Q}_p^*/\mathbb{Q}_p^{*2}$, each factor contributes a group of order $2$: $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ from the valuation, and $V/V^2 \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ from the Teichmüller part, while $U_1/U_1^2 = \{1\}$. Hence
$$\mathbb{Q}_p^*/\mathbb{Q}_p^{*2} \cong (\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}) \times (\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}),$$
a group of type $(2,2)$ (the Klein four-group). Representatives are $\{1, p, u, up\}$ where $u \in V \subset U$ is any element with $\left(\frac{u}{p}\right) = -1$ (a quadratic non-residue modulo $p$). $\square$
