This corollary is a direct consequence of the $p$-adic Newton method (Hensel's lemma). It states that if a polynomial has a simple zero modulo $p$ (i.e., the reduction has a root at which some partial derivative is nonzero), then that root lifts uniquely to a genuine $p$-adic integer root. This is the most commonly applied form of Hensel's lemma and is fundamental for studying equations over $\mathbb{Z}_p$, as it allows one to check for solutions by examining the much simpler reduction modulo $p$.
