The nonvanishing of $L(1,\chi)$ for $\chi \neq 1$ is the crucial step in Dirichlet's proof. The proof shows that if $L(1,\chi) = 0$ for some $\chi$, then $\zeta_m(s) = \prod_\chi L(s,\chi)$ would be holomorphic at $s=1$, but $\zeta_m$ is a Dirichlet series with nonnegative coefficients that diverges at $s = 1/\phi(m)$, a contradiction.
