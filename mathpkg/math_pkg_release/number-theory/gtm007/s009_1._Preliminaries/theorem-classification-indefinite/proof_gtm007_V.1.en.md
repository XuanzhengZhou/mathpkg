Combine Theorems 4 and 5. For type I: $E \simeq s I_+ \oplus t I_-$ where $s = (r+\tau)/2$, $t = (r-\tau)/2$. For type II: $E \simeq pU \oplus q\Gamma_8$ where $p = (r-\tau)/2$, $q = \tau/8$. The triple $(r, \tau, \text{type})$ uniquely determines these parameters, hence the isomorphism class.
