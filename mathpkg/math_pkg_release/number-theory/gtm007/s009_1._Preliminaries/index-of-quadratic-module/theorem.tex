Let $E \in S$ and let $V = E \otimes \mathbf{R}$ be the $\mathbf{R}$-vector space obtained by extending the scalars from $\mathbf{Z}$ to $\mathbf{R}$. The quadratic form of $V$ has a well-defined signature $(r, s)$. The integer

$$\tau(E) = r - s$$

is called the \emph{index} of $E$. One has

$$-r(E) \leq \tau(E) \leq r(E) \quad \text{and} \quad r(E) \equiv \tau(E) \pmod{2}.$$

$E$ is called \emph{definite} if $\tau(E) = \pm r(E)$, i.e., if $x.x$ has constant sign; otherwise $E$ is called \emph{indefinite}.
