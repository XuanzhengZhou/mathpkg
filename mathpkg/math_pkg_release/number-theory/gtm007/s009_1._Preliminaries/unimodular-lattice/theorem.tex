The category $S_n$ consists of free abelian groups $E \cong \mathbf{Z}^n$ of rank $n$ equipped with a symmetric bilinear form $E \times E \to \mathbf{Z}$, denoted $(x, y) \mapsto x \cdot y$, such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] The homomorphism $E \to \operatorname{Hom}(E, \mathbf{Z})$ defined by $x \mapsto (y \mapsto x \cdot y)$ is an isomorphism.
\end{itemize}
Equivalently:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(ii)] If $(e_i)$ is a basis of $E$ and $a_{ij} = e_i \cdot e_j$, then $\det(a_{ij}) = \pm 1$.
\end{itemize}
The union $S = \bigcup_{n \geq 0} S_n$ is the category of all such modules. Objects are called \textbf{unimodular integral lattices}.
