Theorem 6 (Chapter V, §2.3) is the main classification result for indefinite unimodular lattices. Unlike the definite case (which has many isomorphism classes), the indefinite case is completely rigid: the rank, index (signature), and type suffice to identify the lattice uniquely. The proof uses the structure theorems for type I (Theorem 4) and type II (Theorem 5).
