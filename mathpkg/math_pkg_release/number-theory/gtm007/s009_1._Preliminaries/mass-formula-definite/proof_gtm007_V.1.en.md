The proof of the mass formula is beyond the scope of the main text; it is a deep result in the arithmetic theory of quadratic forms. The formula is stated as Theorem 7 (Chapter V, §2.4). For $k = 1$ ($n = 8$), $M_8 = B_4/8 \cdot B_2/4 = 2^{-14}3^{-5}5^{-2}7^{-1}$. For $k = 2$ ($n = 16$), one knows $\Gamma_{16}$ and $\Gamma_8 \oplus \Gamma_8$, with automorphism group orders $2^{15}(16!)$ and $2^{29}3^{10}5^4 7^2$ respectively, and $M_{16}$ equals the sum of their reciprocals.
