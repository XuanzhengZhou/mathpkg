The discriminant of $E$ with respect to a basis $(e_i)$ does not depend on the choice of this basis. Indeed, changing the basis $(e_i)$ multiplies the discriminant by $\det(X^t X) = \det(X)^2$ where $X$ is an invertible matrix over $\mathbf{Z}$; the determinant of $X$ is equal to $\pm 1$, and its square is equal to $1$.

The discriminant of $E$ is denoted by $d(E)$; one has $d(E) = \pm 1$. If $V = E \otimes \mathbf{R}$ is of signature $(r, s)$, the sign of $d(E)$ is $(-1)^s$; since $d(E) = \pm 1$, we get the formula:

$$d(E) = (-1)^{(r(E) - \tau(E))/2}.$$
