Theorem 2 establishes the fundamental congruence $\sigma(E) \equiv \tau(E) \pmod{8}$ for every integral quadratic module $E \in S$. The proof uses the fact that both $\sigma$ and $\tau$ (reduced modulo 8) are homomorphisms from $K(S)$ to $\mathbf{Z}/8\mathbf{Z}$ that coincide on the generators $I_+$ and $I_-$. This relation is the key connection between the index and the sigma invariant.
