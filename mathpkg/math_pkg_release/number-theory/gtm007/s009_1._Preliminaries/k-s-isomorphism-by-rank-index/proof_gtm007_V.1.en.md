The homomorphisms $r, \tau : K(S) \to \mathbf{Z}$ are surjective onto the subgroup $\{(a,b) : a \equiv b \pmod{2}\}$ (since $I_+$ has $(r,\tau) = (1,1)$ and $I_-$ has $(1,-1)$). The pair $(r,\tau)$ is injective because every $f \in K(S)$ can be written uniquely as $s(I_+) + t(I_-)$, and $r(f) = s+t$, $\tau(f) = s-t$, which determine $s, t$. Hence $(r,\tau)$ is an isomorphism onto the stated subgroup.
