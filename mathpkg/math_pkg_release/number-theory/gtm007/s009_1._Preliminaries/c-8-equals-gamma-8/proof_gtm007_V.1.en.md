The proof uses the Minkowski-Siegel mass formula (Theorem 7): for $n = 8$ (i.e., $k = 1$), the mass $M_8$ is given by
$$
M_8 = \frac{B_4}{8} \cdot \frac{B_2}{4} = 2^{-14} 3^{-5} 5^{-2} 7^{-1}.
$$
On the other hand, the order of the automorphism group of $\Gamma_8$ is known to be $2^{14} 3^5 5^2 7$ (this follows from the structure of the Weyl group of $E_8$). Since $M_8 = 1/g_{\Gamma_8}$, the genus of $\Gamma_8$ (which coincides with $C_8$ since all elements of $S_8$ have the same invariants) consists of only one class, namely $\Gamma_8$ itself.
