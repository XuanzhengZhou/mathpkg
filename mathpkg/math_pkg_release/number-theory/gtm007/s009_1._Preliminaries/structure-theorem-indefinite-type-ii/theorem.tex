\textbf{Theorem 5.} — If $E \in S$ is indefinite of type II, and if $\tau(E) \geq 0$, then $E$ is isomorphic to $pU \oplus q\Gamma_8$ where $p$ and $q$ are positive integers, $U = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$, and $\Gamma_8$ is the $E_8$ lattice.

Moreover, $q = \frac{1}{8}\tau(E)$ and $p = \frac{1}{2}(r(E) - \tau(E))$.

When $\tau(E) \leq 0$, one has a corresponding result obtained by applying the theorem to the module $-E$ deduced from $E$ by changing the sign of the quadratic form.
