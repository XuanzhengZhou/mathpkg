\textbf{Theorem 5.} Let $(V,Q)$ be a nondegenerate quadratic module over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ and let $e = (e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ be an orthogonal basis with $a_i = e_i \cdot e_i$. The number
$$\varepsilon(e) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j)$$
does not depend on the choice of the orthogonal basis $e$. It is denoted $\varepsilon(Q)$.
