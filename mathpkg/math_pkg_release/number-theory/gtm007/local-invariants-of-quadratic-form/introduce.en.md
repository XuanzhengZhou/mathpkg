A quadratic form over $\mathbb{Q}$ has both local invariants (at each place $v \in V$) and a global invariant (the signature). The local invariants satisfy the product formula $\prod_v \varepsilon_v(f) = 1$, which follows from the product formula for the Hilbert symbol. Together, these invariants completely classify quadratic forms over $\mathbb{Q}$.
