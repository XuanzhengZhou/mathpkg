A direct consequence of the Chevalley-Warning theorem: any quadratic form in $n \geq 3$ variables defines a homogeneous polynomial of degree $2 < n$, hence has a nontrivial zero. Geometrically, this means every projective conic over a finite field has a rational point. This fact is used to prove that quadratic forms over finite fields of rank $\geq 2$ represent all nonzero elements, and of rank $\geq 3$ represent zero.
