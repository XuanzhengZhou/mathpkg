A quadratic form in $n$ variables is a homogeneous polynomial of degree $2$. If $n \geq 3$, then $\sum \deg f_\alpha = 2 < n$. By the Chevalley-Warning theorem (Theorem 3), the number of common zeros is divisible by $p$. Since $(0,\ldots,0)$ is a zero, there must be at least $p$ zeros. Hence there exists at least one nontrivial zero.
