\textbf{Lemma 2 (Approximation theorem).} Let $p_1, \ldots, p_n$ be distinct prime numbers, let $x_i \in \mathbb{Q}_{p_i}$ and let $x_\infty \in \mathbb{R}$. For every $\varepsilon > 0$ there exists $x \in \mathbb{Q}$ such that $|x - x_i|_{p_i} \leq \varepsilon$ for all $i = 1, \ldots, n$ and $|x - x_\infty| \leq \varepsilon$.
