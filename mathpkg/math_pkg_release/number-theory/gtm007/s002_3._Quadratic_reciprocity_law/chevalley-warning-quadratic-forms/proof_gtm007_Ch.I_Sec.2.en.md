Follows from the Chevalley--Warning theorem: if $P_1, \dots, P_r \in K[x_1, \dots, x_n]$ are polynomials over a finite field $K$ with $\sum \deg P_i < n$, then the number $N$ of common zeros satisfies $N \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$ where $p = \operatorname{char}(K)$. For a single quadratic form in $n \ge 3$ variables, $\deg Q = 2 < n$, so $N \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$. Since $(0,\dots,0)$ is a zero, there must be at least $p-1$ other zeros, hence a non trivial zero exists.
