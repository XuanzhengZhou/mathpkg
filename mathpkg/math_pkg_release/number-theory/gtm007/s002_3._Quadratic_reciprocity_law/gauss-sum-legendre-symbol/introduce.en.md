Lemma 2 computes $y^{p-1}$ for the quadratic Gauss sum $y$ over $F_l$, when working in characteristic $p$. Raising the Gauss sum to the $p$-th power applies Frobenius to each term, which permutes the summation index and pulls out a factor of $(p/l)$. This gives $y^{p-1} = (p/l)$, which combined with Lemma 1 directly yields the quadratic reciprocity law.
