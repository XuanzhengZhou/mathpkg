This lemma computes the square of the quadratic Gauss sum $y = \sum_{x \in F_l} (x/l) w^x$, where $w$ is a primitive $l$-th root of unity. The result $y^2 = (-1)^{\varepsilon(l)} l = (-1)^{(l-1)/2} l$ is fundamental to the Gauss sum proof of quadratic reciprocity. It shows that the Gauss sum lives in a quadratic extension, with its square being an ordinary integer up to sign.
