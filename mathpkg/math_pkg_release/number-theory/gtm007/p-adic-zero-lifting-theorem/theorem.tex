\textbf{Theorem 1.} Let $f^{(1)}, \ldots, f^{(m)} \in \mathbb{Z}_p[X_1, \ldots, X_m]$ be polynomials. Let $x \in (\mathbb{Z}_p)^m$, $n, k \in \mathbb{Z}$ with $0 \leq 2k < n$. Suppose $f^{(i)}(x) \equiv 0 \pmod{p^n}$ for all $i$, and suppose the Jacobian determinant $\det(\partial f^{(i)}/\partial X_j)$ has $p$-adic valuation $k$. Then there exists $y \in (\mathbb{Z}_p)^m$ such that $f^{(i)}(y) = 0$ for all $i$ and $y \equiv x \pmod{p^{n-k}}$.
