This lemma describes the structure of the square-class group $k^*/k^{*2}$ for $k = \mathbb{Q}_p$ and the preimage sets $H_a^\varepsilon$ of the Hilbert symbol. Part (a) gives the size of $k^*/k^{*2}$ as $2^r$, where $r$ depends on whether $p$ is odd or even. Part (b) characterizes the number of solutions to $(x, a) = \varepsilon$ in the square-class group. Part (c) establishes that the nonempty sets $H_a^\varepsilon$ uniquely determine both $a$ and $\varepsilon$, which is crucial for the case analysis in the proof of Theorem 6.
