Let $(V,Q)$ be a quadratic module of rank $n$ over the $p$-adic field $\mathbf{Q}_p$. Let $e = (e_1,\ldots,e_n)$ be an orthogonal basis of $V$ and put $a_i = e_i \cdot e_i$. The \emph{epsilon invariant} $\varepsilon(e)$ is defined by

$$\varepsilon(e) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j),$$

where $(a_i, a_j)$ denotes the Hilbert symbol. One has $\varepsilon(e) = \pm 1$.

\textit{Translation.} If $f$ is a quadratic form in $n$ variables and

$$f \sim a_1 X_1^2 + \cdots + a_n X_n^2,$$

then the two elements

$$d(f) = a_1 \cdots a_n \quad (\text{in } k^*/k^{*2}),$$
$$\varepsilon(f) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j) \quad (\text{in } \{\pm 1\})$$

are invariants of the equivalence class of $f$.
