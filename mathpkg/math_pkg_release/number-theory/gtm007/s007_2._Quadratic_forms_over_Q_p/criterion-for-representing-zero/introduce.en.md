Theorem 6 provides a complete criterion for a nondegenerate quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ to represent zero nontrivially, based solely on its rank $n$, discriminant $d$, and invariant $\varepsilon$. The most striking consequence is that any form in at least $5$ variables automatically represents zero. The proof treats cases $n = 2, 3, 4$ separately using the structure lemma on $k^*/k^{*2}$ and the Hilbert symbol calculus, while the case $n \geq 5$ follows by reduction to $n = 4$.
