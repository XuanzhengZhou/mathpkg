The $\varepsilon(Q)$ invariant of a quadratic module over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ is the product of all Hilbert symbols $(a_i, a_j)$ for $i < j$, where the $a_i$ come from any orthogonal basis. Together with the discriminant $d(Q)$, it forms a complete set of invariants for the classification of quadratic forms over $p$-adic fields. The invariance of $\varepsilon(Q)$ under change of orthogonal basis is established by Theorem 5, making it a well-defined quantity.
