Let $(V, Q)$ be a quadratic module of rank $n$ over $k = \mathbb{Q}_p$. Let $e = (e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ be an orthogonal basis of $V$ and set $a_i = e_i \cdot e_i$. Let $(a, b) \in \{\pm 1\}$ denote the Hilbert symbol of $a, b \in k^*$. The invariant $\varepsilon(e)$ is defined by
$$\varepsilon(e) = \prod_{i < j} (a_i, a_j).$$
This value is an invariant of $(V, Q)$, denoted $\varepsilon(Q)$, and takes values in $\{\pm 1\}$.
