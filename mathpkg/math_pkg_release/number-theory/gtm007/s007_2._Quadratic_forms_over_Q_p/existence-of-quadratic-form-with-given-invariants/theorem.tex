**Proposition 6.** Let $n \geq 1$, $d \in k^*/k^{*2}$, and $\varepsilon = \pm 1$. In order that there exists a nondegenerate quadratic form $f$ of rank $n$ over $k = \mathbf{Q}_p$ such that $d(f) = d$ and $\varepsilon(f) = \varepsilon$, it is necessary and sufficient that one of the following holds:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $n = 1$ and $\varepsilon = 1$;
\item[(ii)] $n = 2$ and either $d \neq -1$ or $\varepsilon = 1$;
\item[(iii)] $n \geq 3$.
\end{itemize}

(Equivalently: the only impossible combination is $n = 2$, $d = -1$, $\varepsilon = -1$.)
