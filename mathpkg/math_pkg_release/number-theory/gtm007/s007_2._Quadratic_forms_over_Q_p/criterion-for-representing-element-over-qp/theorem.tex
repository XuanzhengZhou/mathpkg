Let $f$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form of rank $n$ over $\mathbf{Q}_p$ with discriminant $d = d(f) \in k^*/k^{*2}$ and invariant $\varepsilon = \varepsilon(f)$. Let $a \in k^*/k^{*2}$. Then $f$ represents $a$ if and only if:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $n = 1$ and $a = d$,
\item[(ii)] $n = 2$ and $(a, -d) = \varepsilon$,
\item[(iii)] $n = 3$ and either $a \neq -d$ or $a = -d$ and $(-1, -d) = \varepsilon$,
\item[(iv)] $n \geq 4$.
\end{itemize}
(Here $a$ and $d$ are viewed as elements of $k^*/k^{*2}$; the inequality $a \neq -d$ means that $a$ is not equal to the product of $-d$ by a square.)
