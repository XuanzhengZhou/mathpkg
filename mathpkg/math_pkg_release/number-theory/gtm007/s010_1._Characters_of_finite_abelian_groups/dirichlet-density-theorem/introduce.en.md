Theorem 2 (Chapter VI, §4.3) strengthens Dirichlet's theorem: not only are there infinitely many primes in each primitive residue class modulo $m$, but they are equidistributed among the $\phi(m)$ classes, each having analytic density $1/\phi(m)$. The proof combines the pole of $\zeta(s)$ at $s=1$ with the analytic properties of $L$-functions.
