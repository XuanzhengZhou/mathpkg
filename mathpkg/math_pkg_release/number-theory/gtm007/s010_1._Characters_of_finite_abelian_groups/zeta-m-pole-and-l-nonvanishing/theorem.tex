Let $\zeta_m(s) = \prod_\chi L(s,\chi)$, where the product is taken over all characters $\chi$ modulo $m$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\zeta_m(s)$ has a simple pole at $s=1$.
\item[(b)] $L(1,\chi) \neq 0$ for all $\chi \neq 1$.
\end{enumerate}
These two statements are equivalent: (b) implies (a), and if $L(1,\chi) = 0$ for some $\chi \neq 1$, then $\zeta_m$ would be holomorphic, leading to a contradiction via Landau\'s theorem.
