Dirichlet's theorem on arithmetic progressions states that for any two coprime positive integers $a$ and $m$, the arithmetic progression $a, a+m, a+2m, \ldots$ contains infinitely many prime numbers. This was conjectured by Legendre and later proved by Dirichlet in 1837, marking the birth of analytic number theory. The proof uses Dirichlet characters (homomorphisms from $(\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z})^*$ to $\mathbb{C}^*$) and the non-vanishing of $L(1, \chi)$ for non-principal characters $\chi$.
