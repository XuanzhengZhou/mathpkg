Let $G$ be a finite abelian group and let $H$ be a subgroup of $G$. Then every character of $H$ extends to a character of $G$.

Equivalently, the restriction homomorphism
$$\rho \colon \widehat{G} \longrightarrow \widehat{H}$$
is surjective. Moreover, the kernel of $\rho$ is the set of characters of $G$ which are trivial on $H$, which is isomorphic to $\widehat{G/H}$. Hence there is an exact sequence:
$$\{1\} \longrightarrow \widehat{G/H} \longrightarrow \widehat{G} \longrightarrow \widehat{H} \longrightarrow \{1\}.$$
