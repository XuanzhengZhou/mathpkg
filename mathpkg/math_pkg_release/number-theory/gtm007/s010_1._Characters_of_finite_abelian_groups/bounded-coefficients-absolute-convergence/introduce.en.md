Proposition 8 (Chapter VI, §2.4): For a Dirichlet series with bounded coefficients, the abscissa of absolute convergence is at most 1. This follows from comparison with the Riemann zeta function $\zeta(s) = \sum 1/n^s$, which converges absolutely for $R(s) > 1$.
