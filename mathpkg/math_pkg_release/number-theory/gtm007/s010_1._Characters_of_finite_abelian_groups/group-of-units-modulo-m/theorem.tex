$G(m)$ denotes the group $(\mathbf{Z}/m\mathbf{Z})^*$, an abelian group of order $\phi(m)$. Characters of $G(m)$ are called \textbf{characters modulo $m$}. Examples include:
\begin{itemize}
\item $m=4$: $G(4) = \{1,3\}$, the nontrivial character is $x \mapsto (-1)^{\varepsilon(x)}$.
\item $m=8$: $G(8) = \{1,3,5,7\}$, three nontrivial characters: $(-1)^{\varepsilon(x)}$, $(-1)^{\omega(x)}$, $(-1)^{\varepsilon(x)+\omega(x)}$.
\item $m=p$ (prime): $G(p) \cong \mathbf{F}_p^*$ is cyclic of order $p-1$.
\end{itemize}
