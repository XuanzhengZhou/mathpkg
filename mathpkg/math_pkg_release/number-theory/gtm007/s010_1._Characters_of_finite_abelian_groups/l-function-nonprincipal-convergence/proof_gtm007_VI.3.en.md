The absolute convergence for $\Re(s) > 1$ follows from Proposition 8 since $|\chi(n)| \leq 1$.

For the conditional convergence in $\Re(s) > 0$, note that for a nonprincipal character, the sums $\sum_{n=u}^{v} \chi(n)$ are bounded. Indeed, the sum over any complete residue class modulo $m$ is zero: $\sum_{n=a}^{a+m-1} \chi(n) = 0$ (by the orthogonality relation). Hence for any interval, $|\sum_{n=u}^{v} \chi(n)| \leq \phi(m)$. The result follows from Proposition 9.
