Let $f(z) = \sum a_n e^{-\lambda_n z}$ be a Dirichlet series whose coefficients $a_n$ are real and $\geq 0$. Suppose $f$ converges for $\Re(z) > \rho$ (with $\rho \in \mathbb{R}$) and that $f$ can be extended analytically to a function holomorphic in a neighborhood of $z = \rho$. Then there exists $\varepsilon > 0$ such that $f$ converges for $\Re(z) > \rho - \varepsilon$.
