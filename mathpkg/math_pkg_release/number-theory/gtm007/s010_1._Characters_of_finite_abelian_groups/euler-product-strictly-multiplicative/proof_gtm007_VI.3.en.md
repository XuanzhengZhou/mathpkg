This follows from Lemma 4 and the identity $f(p^m) = f(p)^m$, which holds for strictly multiplicative functions. The inner sum in the Euler product becomes a geometric series:
$$\sum_{m=0}^{\infty} f(p)^m p^{-ms} = \frac{1}{1 - f(p)/p^s}.$$
