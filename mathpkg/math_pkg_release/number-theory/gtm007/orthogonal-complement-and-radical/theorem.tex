Two elements $x, y \in V$ are orthogonal if $x \cdot y = 0$. The orthogonal complement of a subset $H \subseteq V$ is $H^0 = \{x \in V : x \cdot y = 0 \text{ for all } y \in H\}$. The radical (or kernel) of $V$ is $\operatorname{rad}(V) = V^0$. Its codimension is the rank of $Q$. If $V^0 = 0$, $Q$ is nondegenerate; equivalently, the discriminant of $Q$ is nonzero (viewed as an element of $k^*/k^{*2}$).
