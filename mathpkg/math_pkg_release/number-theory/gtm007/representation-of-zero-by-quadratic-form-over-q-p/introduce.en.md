This theorem gives necessary and sufficient conditions, in terms of $d$ and $\varepsilon$, for a quadratic form over $\mathbb{Q}_p$ to represent zero. The result depends on the rank: forms of rank $\geq 5$ always represent zero. This is the $p$-adic analogue of Meyer's theorem over $\mathbb{Q}$.
