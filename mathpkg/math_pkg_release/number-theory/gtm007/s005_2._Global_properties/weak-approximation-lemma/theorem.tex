Let $p_1, \ldots, p_n$ be distinct primes, let $x_i \in \mathbb{Q}_{p_i}$ for $1 \leq i \leq n$, and let $x_\infty \in \mathbb{R}$. For every $\varepsilon > 0$ and every integer $N \geq 1$, there exists $x \in \mathbb{Q}$ such that
$$|x - x_i|_{p_i} \leq p_i^{-N} \quad \text{for all } i, \quad \text{and} \quad |x - x_\infty| \leq \varepsilon.$$
