The Chinese Remainder Theorem states that a system of simultaneous congruences with pairwise coprime moduli always admits a solution. Given integers $a_1, \ldots, a_n$ and pairwise relatively prime moduli $m_1, \ldots, m_n$, there exists an integer $a$ satisfying $a \equiv a_i \pmod{m_i}$ for each $i$. This classical result is fundamental in elementary number theory and is used extensively in Serre's proof of the existence theorem for Hilbert symbols.
