Let $V$ be the set of prime numbers together with the symbol $\infty$. For each $v \in V$, define the completion $\mathbf{Q}_v$ as:
$$
\mathbf{Q}_v = \begin{cases}
\mathbf{Q}_p & \text{if } v = p \text{ is a prime number} \\
\mathbf{R} & \text{if } v = \infty.
\end{cases}
$$
The field $\mathbf{Q}$ embeds as a dense subfield into each $\mathbf{Q}_v$. The elements $v \in V$ are called the \textbf{places} of $\mathbf{Q}$.
