Gauss's "Eureka" theorem states that every positive integer is a sum of three triangular numbers. This is Corollary 2 to the three-square theorem: applying the three-square theorem to $8n + 3$ yields $x_1^2 + x_2^2 + x_3^2 = 8n + 3$. Reducing modulo $8$ forces each $x_i$ to be odd, so writing $x_i = 2y_i + 1$ and substituting yields $n = y_1(y_1+1)/2 + y_2(y_2+1)/2 + y_3(y_3+1)/2$. Gauss famously recorded this discovery in his diary with the entry "Eureka! num = \Delta + \Delta + \Delta".
