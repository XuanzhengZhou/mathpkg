This is Gauss's famous "Eureka" theorem: every positive integer is a sum of exactly three triangular numbers. It follows from the three-square theorem by considering the representation of $8n+3$ as a sum of three squares, each of which must be odd (since $8n+3 \equiv 3 \pmod{8}$). Substituting $x_i = 2m_i+1$ transforms the square sum into a sum of three triangular numbers. Gauss recorded this result on July 10, 1796 in his mathematical diary with the entry: "Eureka! num = \Delta + \Delta + \Delta."
