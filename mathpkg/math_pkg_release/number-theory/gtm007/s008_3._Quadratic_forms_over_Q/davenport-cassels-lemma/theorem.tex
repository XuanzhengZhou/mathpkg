Let $f(X) = \sum_{i,j=1}^{p} a_{ij} X_i X_j$ be a positive definite quadratic form with symmetric integer coefficient matrix $(a_{ij})$. Assume:

\textbf{(H)} For every $x = (x_1, \ldots, x_p) \in \mathbf{Q}^p$ there exists $y \in \mathbf{Z}^p$ such that $f(x-y) < 1$.

If an integer $n \in \mathbf{Z}$ is represented by $f$ in $\mathbf{Q}$ (i.e., there exists $x \in \mathbf{Q}^p$ with $f(x) = n$), then $n$ is also represented by $f$ in $\mathbf{Z}$ (i.e., there exists $y \in \mathbf{Z}^p$ with $f(y) = n$).
