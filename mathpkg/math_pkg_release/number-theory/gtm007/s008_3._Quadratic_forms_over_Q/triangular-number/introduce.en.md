A triangular number is an integer of the form $m(m+1)/2$ for some integer $m$. These numbers are called "triangular" because they count the number of dots in an equilateral triangular arrangement with $m$ dots on each side. Gauss's theorem (Corollary 2 to the three-square theorem) states that every positive integer is a sum of three triangular numbers — a beautiful complement to Lagrange's four-square theorem.
