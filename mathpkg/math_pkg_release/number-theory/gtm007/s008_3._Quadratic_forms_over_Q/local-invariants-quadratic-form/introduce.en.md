By embedding $\mathbf{Q}$ into its completions $\mathbf{Q}_v$, a rational quadratic form $f$ yields a family of local quadratic forms $f_v$. The local discriminant $d_v(f)$ is simply the image of the global discriminant in $\mathbf{Q}_v^*/\mathbf{Q}_v^{*2}$. The Hasse–Witt invariant $\varepsilon_v(f)$ is a product of Hilbert symbols of the coefficients, taking values $\pm 1$, and captures the local equivalence class of the form. Together they form a core part of the local-to-global classification of quadratic forms.
