Let $f$ be a nondegenerate quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}$, and for each place $v \in V$ let $f_v$ be the quadratic form over $\mathbf{Q}_v$ obtained from $f$ via the embedding $\mathbf{Q} \hookrightarrow \mathbf{Q}_v$.

\textbf{Theorem 8 (Hasse–Minkowski).} In order that $f$ represent $0$ (i.e., there exists a nonzero vector $x \in \mathbf{Q}^n$ with $f(x) = 0$), it is necessary and sufficient that, for all $v \in V$, the form $f_v$ represent $0$ in $\mathbf{Q}_v$.

In other words: $f$ has a ``global'' zero if and only if $f$ has everywhere a ``local'' zero.
