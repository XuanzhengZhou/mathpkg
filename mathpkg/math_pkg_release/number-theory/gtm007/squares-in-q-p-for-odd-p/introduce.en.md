A $p$-adic number is a square iff its valuation is even and its unit part is a quadratic residue modulo $p$. Since $U_1 \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ and $2$ is invertible in $\mathbb{Z}_p$, every element of $U_1$ is a square, so the square condition depends only on the Teichmüller representative modulo $p$.
