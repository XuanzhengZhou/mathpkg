By Theorem 8, $\theta_\Gamma$ is a modular form of weight $n/2$. The space $M_{n/4}$ of modular forms of weight $n/2$ decomposes as $M_{n/4} = \mathbf{C} E_{n/4} \oplus M_{n/4}^0$, where $E_{n/4}$ is the Eisenstein series of weight $n/2$ and $M_{n/4}^0$ is the space of cusp forms. (See Chapter VII, \S 3.2, Corollary 2 of Theorem 5.) Thus $\theta_\Gamma = c E_{n/4} + f_\Gamma$ for some constant $c$ and cusp form $f_\Gamma$. Comparing constant terms: $r_\Gamma(0) = 1$ and the constant term of $E_{n/4}$ is $1$, so $c = 1$. When $M_{n/4}^0 = 0$, as happens for $n = 8$ (weight $4$) and $n = 16$ (weight $8$), we have $\theta_\Gamma = E_{n/4}$ exactly, yielding explicit formulas for $r_\Gamma(m)$.
