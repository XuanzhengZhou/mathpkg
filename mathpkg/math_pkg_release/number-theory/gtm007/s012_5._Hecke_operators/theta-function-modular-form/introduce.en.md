Theorem 8 is one of the cornerstone results in the theory of modular forms. For an even unimodular lattice $\Gamma$ (self-dual with even norm), the dimension $n$ is necessarily divisible by $8$, and its theta function $\theta_\Gamma$ is a modular form of weight $n/2$. The modularity follows from the functional equation of $\Theta_\Gamma$ combined with the properties $v=1$ and $\Gamma=\Gamma'$. This theorem connects the classification of definite quadratic forms to the theory of modular forms.
