Let $V$ be a real vector space of dimension $n$ with a positive definite symmetric bilinear form, and let $\Gamma$ be a lattice in $V$ satisfying:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $\Gamma' = \Gamma$ (self-duality: $x \cdot y \in \mathbf{Z}$ for $x,y \in \Gamma$, and the form defines an isomorphism of $\Gamma$ onto its dual),
\item[(ii)] $x \cdot x$ is an even integer for all $x \in \Gamma$.
\end{itemize}
Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] The dimension $n$ of $V$ is divisible by $8$.
\item[(b)] The theta function $\theta_\Gamma$ is a modular form of weight $n/2$.
\end{itemize}
