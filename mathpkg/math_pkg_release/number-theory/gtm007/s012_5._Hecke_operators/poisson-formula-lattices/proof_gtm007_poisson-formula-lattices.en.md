After replacing the Lebesgue measure $\mu$ by $v^{-1}\mu$, we may assume $\mu(V/\Gamma) = 1$. Choose a basis $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ of $\Gamma$, identifying $V$ with $\mathbf{R}^n$, $\Gamma$ with $\mathbf{Z}^n$, and the normalized measure with the product Lebesgue measure $dx_1 \cdots dx_n$. Under this identification, the dual lattice $\Gamma'$ corresponds to $\mathbf{Z}^n$, and the Poisson sum $\frac{1}{v} \sum_{y \in \Gamma'} \hat{f}(y)$ becomes the sum of Fourier coefficients of $f$ over $\mathbf{Z}^n$. The stated formula thus reduces to the classical Poisson summation formula on $\mathbf{R}^n$ (see Schwartz, Th\'eorie des distributions, formule (VII, 7;5)):
$$\sum_{x \in \mathbf{Z}^n} f(x) = \sum_{y \in \mathbf{Z}^n} \hat{f}(y).$$
