The Poisson summation formula for lattices generalizes the classical Poisson formula from $\mathbf{R}^n$ to arbitrary lattices in a real vector space. It states that $\sum_{x\in\Gamma} f(x) = v^{-1} \sum_{y\in\Gamma'} \hat{f}(y)$, where $\Gamma'$ is the dual lattice and $v$ is the covolume. After choosing coordinates and normalizing the measure, this reduces to the classical formula. It is the key analytic tool for proving the modularity of theta functions.
