Proposition 12 gives the explicit action of $T(n)$ on the Fourier ($q$-) expansion of a modular function. If $f(z) = \sum c(m)q^m$, then $T(n)f(z) = \sum \gamma(m)q^m$ where $\gamma(m) = \sum_{a \mid (n,m)} a^{2k-1} c(mn/a^2)$. This formula makes Hecke operators computationally tractable and connects them directly to Dirichlet series and $L$-functions.
