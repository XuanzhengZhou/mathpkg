Corollary 2 of Proposition 12 specializes the coefficient formula to the case $n=p$ prime. The resulting formulas $\gamma(m) = c(pm)$ when $p \nmid m$ and $\gamma(m) = c(pm) + p^{2k-1}c(m/p)$ when $p \mid m$ reveal the inductive structure underlying Hecke operators and are the starting point for studying eigenforms and their associated Galois representations.
