The Ramanujan-Petersson conjecture was proved by Pierre Deligne in 1973 (Publ. Math. I.H.E.S. n\textdegree 43, p. 302) as a consequence of the Weil conjectures concerning algebraic varieties over finite fields. The proof proceeds by associating to a Hecke eigenform $f$ a compatible system of $\ell$-adic Galois representations $\rho_\ell: \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbf{Q}}/\mathbf{Q}) \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\overline{\mathbf{Q}}_\ell)$, constructed from the \'etale cohomology of Kuga-Sato varieties. The Weil conjectures (proved by Deligne) imply that the characteristic polynomial of the Frobenius element at $p$ has roots of absolute value $p^{k-1/2}$, yielding the bound $|c(p)| \leq 2p^{k-1/2}$ and its consequences for all coefficients. For $k=6$, this gives $|\tau(p)| \leq 2p^{11/2}$, settling Ramanujan's original conjecture.
