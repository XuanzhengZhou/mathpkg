Proposition 16 gives the functional equation $\Theta_\Gamma(t) = t^{-n/2} v^{-1} \Theta_{\Gamma'}(t^{-1})$ for the theta series $\Theta_\Gamma(t) = \sum_{x\in\Gamma} e^{-\pi t (x\cdot x)}$. This is derived from the Poisson summation formula applied to the Gaussian function $f(x) = e^{-\pi t (x\cdot x)}$, whose Fourier transform is $t^{-n/2} e^{-\pi (y\cdot y)/t}$. This identity is the essential step in proving that theta functions of even unimodular lattices are modular forms.
