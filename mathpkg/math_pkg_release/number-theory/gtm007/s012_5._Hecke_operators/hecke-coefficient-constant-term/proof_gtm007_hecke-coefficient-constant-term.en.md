From Proposition 12, $\gamma(m) = \sum_{a \mid (n,m)} a^{2k-1} c(mn/a^2)$. For $m = 0$, the condition $a \mid (n,0)$ means $a \mid n$, and $c(0 \cdot n/a^2) = c(0)$ for all such $a$. Thus $\gamma(0) = c(0) \sum_{a \mid n} a^{2k-1} = \sigma_{2k-1}(n)c(0)$. For $m = 1$, the condition $a \mid (n,1)$ means $a = 1$, so $\gamma(1) = 1^{2k-1} c(1 \cdot n/1) = c(n)$.
