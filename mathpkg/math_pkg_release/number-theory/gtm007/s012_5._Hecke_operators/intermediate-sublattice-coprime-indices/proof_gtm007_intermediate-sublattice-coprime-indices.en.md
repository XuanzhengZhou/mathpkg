The group $\Gamma/\Gamma''$ is a finite abelian group of order $mn$. Since $m$ and $n$ are relatively prime, by Bezout's theorem (or the structure theorem for finite abelian groups), $\Gamma/\Gamma''$ decomposes uniquely as a direct sum $A \oplus B$ where $|A| = n$ and $|B| = m$. Let $\Gamma'$ be the preimage of $B$ under the quotient map $\Gamma \to \Gamma/\Gamma''$. Then $\Gamma'' \subset \Gamma' \subset \Gamma$, $(\Gamma:\Gamma') = |A| = n$, and $(\Gamma':\Gamma'') = |B| = m$. The uniqueness follows from the uniqueness of the decomposition of a finite abelian group into its $p$-primary components for different primes.
