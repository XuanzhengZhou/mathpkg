Corollary 3 of Theorem 7 states that the Dirichlet series $\Phi_f(s) = \sum c(n) n^{-s}$ associated to a normalized Hecke eigenform admits an Euler product: $\Phi_f(s) = \prod_p (1 - c(p)p^{-s} + p^{2k-1-2s})^{-1}$. This factorization, proved via multiplicativity and the prime-power recurrence, is the key link between modular forms and $L$-functions, generalizing the Euler product of the Riemann zeta function.
