Let $f, g$ be two cusp forms of weight $2k$ with $k > 0$. The measure
$$\mu(f,g) = f(z)\overline{g(z)} y^{2k} \frac{dxdy}{y^2} \quad (x = \Re(z),\; y = \Im(z))$$
is invariant by the modular group $G$ and is bounded on the quotient $H/G$. The Petersson scalar product is defined by
$$\langle f, g \rangle = \int_{H/G} \mu(f, g) = \int_{D} f(z)\overline{g(z)} y^{2k-2} dxdy,$$
where $D$ is a fundamental domain of $G$. This defines a positive, non-degenerate Hermitian scalar product on the space $M_k^0$ of cusp forms.
