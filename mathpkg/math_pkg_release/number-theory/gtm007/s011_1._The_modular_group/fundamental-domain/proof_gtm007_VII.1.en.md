The proof follows from the elementary properties of the Möbius action: given $z \in \mathcal{H}$, the numbers $\operatorname{Im}(\gamma z)$ for $\gamma \in \Gamma$ attain a maximum. Choose $\gamma$ such that $\operatorname{Im}(\gamma z)$ is maximal. Then $-1/2 \leq \operatorname{Re}(\gamma z) \leq 1/2$ (by applying $T: z \mapsto z+1$ repeatedly), and $|\gamma z| \geq 1$ (since otherwise applying $S: z \mapsto -1/z$ would increase the imaginary part). Thus $\gamma z \in \mathcal{F}$. For the second property, the only nontrivial identifications are $z \sim -1/z$ (for $|z| = 1$) and $z \sim z+1$ (for $\operatorname{Re}(z) = \pm 1/2$).
