Proposition 1 reduces the full modular invariance condition $f(gz) = (cz+d)^{2k}f(z)$ for all $g \in G$ to just two functional equations involving the generators $S$ and $T$. Since $G$ is generated by $S$ and $T$ (Theorem 2), the periodicity $f(z+1) = f(z)$ and the symmetry $f(-1/z) = z^{2k}f(z)$ together imply invariance under the entire modular group. This characterization is the practical working definition of weak modularity and is fundamental for constructing and verifying modular forms.
