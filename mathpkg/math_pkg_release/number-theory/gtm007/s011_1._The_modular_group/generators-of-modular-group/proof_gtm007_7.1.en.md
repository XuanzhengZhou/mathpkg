Let $G'$ be the subgroup of $G$ generated by $S$ and $T$. Given any $z \in H$, by the proof of Theorem 1 part (1), there exists $g \in G'$ such that $gz \in D$. Now let $g_0$ be an arbitrary element of $G$. Applying this to $z_0 = g_0^{-1} z$ for any $z$ in the interior of $D$, we obtain $g \in G'$ with $gg_0^{-1} z \in D$. Since $z$ is an interior point of $D$, the injectivity part of the Corollary to Theorem 1 implies $gg_0^{-1} = 1$, hence $g_0 = g \in G'$. Thus $G = G'$.
