Theorem 4 gives the complete structure of the graded algebra $M = \bigoplus M_k$ of modular forms for $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$. It shows that all modular forms are polynomials in $G_2$ (weight 4) and $G_3$ (weight 6), with $\Delta = g_2^3 - 27g_3^2$ serving as the unique (up to scalar) cusp form of weight 12. The dimension of $M_k$ grows by one every six weights, and cusp forms are precisely multiples of $\Delta$.
