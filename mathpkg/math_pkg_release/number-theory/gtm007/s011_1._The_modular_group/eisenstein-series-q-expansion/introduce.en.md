Proposition 8 provides the Fourier expansion ($q$-expansion) of the Eisenstein series $G_k(z)$ in powers of $q = e^{2\pi i z}$. The constant term is $2\zeta(2k)$, and the $n$-th coefficient is proportional to $\sigma_{2k-1}(n)$, the sum of $(2k-1)$-st powers of divisors of $n$. This expansion is the starting point for the arithmetic study of modular forms.
