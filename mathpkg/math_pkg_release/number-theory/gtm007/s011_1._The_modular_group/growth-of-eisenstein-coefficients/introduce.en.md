The Fourier coefficients $a_n$ of the Eisenstein series $G_k$ grow like $n^{2k-1}$. This follows from the explicit formula $a_n \propto \sigma_{2k-1}(n)$ and the bounds $n^{2k-1} \leq \sigma_{2k-1}(n) \leq \zeta(2k-1) n^{2k-1}$. This contrasts sharply with cusp forms, whose coefficients grow at most like $n^k$ (Theorem 5).
