A \textbf{modular form} of weight $2k$ is a modular function of weight $2k$ that is holomorphic on the upper half-plane $H$ and holomorphic at infinity. This means that its $q$-expansion has the form
$$\tilde{f}(q) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n q^n, \quad q = e^{2\pi i z},$$
with $a_n \in \mathbf{C}$ and $a_n = 0$ for $n < 0$. One sets $f(\infty) = a_0$.

A \textbf{cusp form} of weight $2k$ is a modular form of weight $2k$ that vanishes at infinity, i.e., $a_0 = 0$ in its $q$-expansion.

For an integer $k$, one denotes by $M_k$ the $\mathbf{C}$-vector space of modular forms of weight $2k$, and by $M_k^0$ the subspace of cusp forms.
