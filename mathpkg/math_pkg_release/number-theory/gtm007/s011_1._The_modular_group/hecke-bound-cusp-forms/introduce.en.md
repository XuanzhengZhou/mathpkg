Hecke's theorem (Theorem 5) establishes that the Fourier coefficients $a_n$ of a cusp form of weight $2k$ satisfy $a_n = O(n^k)$. This is in contrast to Eisenstein series whose coefficients grow like $n^{2k-1}$. The bound was later improved by Deligne to $O(n^{k-1/2}\sigma_0(n))$, confirming the Ramanujan-Petersson conjecture.
