From Theorem 1, the canonical projection $D \to H/G$ is surjective because every $G$-orbit meets $D$, and its restriction to the interior of $D$ is injective because interior points are not $G$-equivalent to any other point of $D$. Thus $D$ faithfully parametrizes the quotient space, with only boundary identifications.
