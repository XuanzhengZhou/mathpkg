A modular function is a weakly modular function that is also meromorphic at infinity. Using the periodicity $f(z+1) = f(z)$, one writes $f$ in terms of $q = e^{2\pi i z}$, and the condition means the Laurent expansion $\tilde{f}(q)$ has only finitely many negative powers of $q$. When $f$ is holomorphic at infinity, one sets $f(\infty) = \tilde{f}(0)$.
