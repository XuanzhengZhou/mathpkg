The values $\zeta(2k)$ for $k \geq 1$ appear as the constant terms of the Eisenstein series $G_{2k}$:
$$
G_{2k}(z) = 2\zeta(2k) + 2\frac{(2\pi i)^{2k}}{(2k-1)!} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \sigma_{2k-1}(n) q^n.
$$
In particular, $\zeta(2k) = (-1)^{k+1} B_{2k} (2\pi)^{2k} / (2(2k)!)$, connecting zeta values, Eisenstein series, and Bernoulli numbers.
