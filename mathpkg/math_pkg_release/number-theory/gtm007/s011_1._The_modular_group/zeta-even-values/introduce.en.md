The Eisenstein series provide a deep connection between modular forms and special values of $L$-functions. The constant terms of $G_{2k}$ are $2\zeta(2k)$, so computing these constant terms via the Fourier expansion directly yields Euler's formula for $\zeta(2k)$ in terms of Bernoulli numbers and powers of $\pi$.
