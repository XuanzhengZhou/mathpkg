Since $S$ and $T$ generate $\Gamma$, any element $\gamma \in \Gamma$ can be expressed as a word in $S$ and $T$. The transformation law $f(\gamma z) = (cz+d)^k f(z)$ for $\gamma = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ follows from (i) and (ii) by induction on the word length. The compatibility is ensured by the relations $S^2=1$ and $(ST)^3=1$: one checks that applying (ii) twice gives $f(z) = z^k (-1/z)^k f(z)$, which holds since $(-1)^k = 1$ for even $k$.
