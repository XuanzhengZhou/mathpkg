The derivation uses the Lipschitz formula:
$$
\sum_{m=-\infty}^\infty \frac{1}{(m+z)^k} = \frac{(-2\pi i)^k}{(k-1)!} \sum_{n=1}^\infty n^{k-1} e^{2\pi i n z},
$$
valid for $\operatorname{Im}(z) > 0$ and $k \geq 2$. Applying this to the inner sum of $G_k(z)$ gives the $q$-expansion. The $2\zeta(k)$ term is the contribution of the $n=0$ terms.
