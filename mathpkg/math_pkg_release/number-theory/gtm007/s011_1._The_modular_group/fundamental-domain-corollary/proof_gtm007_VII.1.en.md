The map $\mathcal{F} \to \Gamma \backslash \mathcal{H}$ is surjective by property (i) and injective on the interior by property (ii) of the fundamental domain. The boundary identifications (reflection about the $y$-axis and about the unit circle arc) yield a topological sphere. Adding the cusp at $i\infty$ (which corresponds to the limit $y \to \infty$) compactifies the space.
