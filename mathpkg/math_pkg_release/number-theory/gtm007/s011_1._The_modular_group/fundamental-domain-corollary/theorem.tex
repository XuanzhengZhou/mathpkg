The canonical projection $\pi: \mathcal{H} \to \Gamma \backslash \mathcal{H}$ restricts to a surjection $\mathcal{F} \to \Gamma \backslash \mathcal{H}$ that is injective on the interior of $\mathcal{F}$. The quotient $\Gamma \backslash \mathcal{H}$ (endowed with the quotient topology) is homeomorphic to the Riemann sphere minus one point; its compactification is obtained by adding the cusp at $i\infty$, giving a compact Riemann surface of genus 0.
