The normalized Eisenstein series $E_k(z) = \frac{1}{2\zeta(k)} G_k(z)$ has Fourier expansion
$$
E_k(z) = 1 + \gamma_k \sum_{n=1}^\infty \sigma_{k-1}(n) q^n, \quad q = e^{2\pi i z},
$$
where $\sigma_{k-1}(n) = \sum_{d \mid n} d^{k-1}$ and $\gamma_k = (-1)^{k/2} \cdot 2k/B_k$ (with $B_k$ the $k$-th Bernoulli number). In particular, $\sigma_{k-1}(n) = O(n^{k-1})$ and grows at most polynomially in $n$.
