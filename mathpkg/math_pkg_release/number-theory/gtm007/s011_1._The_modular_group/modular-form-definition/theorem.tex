A \textbf{modular form} of weight $2k$ is a weakly modular function of weight $2k$ that is holomorphic on $H$ and holomorphic at infinity. The latter means that in the $q$-expansion
$$\tilde{f}(q) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n q^n, \qquad q = e^{2\pi i z},$$
the coefficients $a_n$ are zero for $n < 0$.

A modular form is called a \textbf{cusp form} if it vanishes at infinity, i.e. $a_0 = 0$ in the $q$-expansion.

The $\mathbf{C}$-vector space of modular forms of weight $2k$ is denoted $M_k$, and the subspace of cusp forms is denoted $M_k^0$.
