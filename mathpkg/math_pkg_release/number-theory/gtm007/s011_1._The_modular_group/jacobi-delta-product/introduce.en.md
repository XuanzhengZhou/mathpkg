Jacobi's theorem identifies the cusp form $\Delta$ of weight 12 with an explicit infinite product: $\Delta = (2\pi)^{12} q \prod (1-q^n)^{24}$. The coefficients $\tau(n)$ of the normalized form $F(z) = (2\pi)^{-12} \Delta(z)$ define the celebrated Ramanujan tau function. This product formula is the source of deep arithmetic properties of $\tau(n)$, including multiplicativity and recurrence relations.
