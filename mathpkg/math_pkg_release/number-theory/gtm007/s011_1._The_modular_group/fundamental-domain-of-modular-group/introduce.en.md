The subset $D = \{z \in H \mid |z| \geq 1,\; |\operatorname{Re}(z)| \leq 1/2\}$ of the upper half-plane is a fundamental domain for the action of the modular group $G$. Theorem 1 proves three essential properties: every $G$-orbit meets $D$, the only identifications on the boundary are the obvious ones (via translation by $\pm 1$ and inversion $z \mapsto -1/z$), and the stabilizers are trivial except at $i$ (order 2) and at $\rho, -\bar{\rho}$ (order 3).
