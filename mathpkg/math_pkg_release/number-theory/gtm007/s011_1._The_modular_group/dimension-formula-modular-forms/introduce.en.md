From Theorem 4, the dimension of $M_k$ increases by one every six weights. Explicitly, $\dim M_k$ is $\lfloor k/6 \rfloor$ when $k \equiv 1 \pmod{6}$ and $\lfloor k/6 \rfloor + 1$ otherwise. This simple formula is a consequence of the structure theorem: $M_k$ has basis $\{G_2^\alpha G_3^\beta : 2\alpha + 3\beta = k\}$, and the number of such pairs $(\alpha, \beta)$ matches the dimension formula.
