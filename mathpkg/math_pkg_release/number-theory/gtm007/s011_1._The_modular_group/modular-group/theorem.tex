Let $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ be the subgroup of $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{R})$ consisting of matrices with integer coefficients. The group
$$G = \mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z}) / \{\pm 1\}$$
is called the \text{modular group}; it is the image of $\mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{Z})$ in $\mathbf{PSL}_2(\mathbf{R}) = \mathbf{SL}_2(\mathbf{R}) / \{\pm 1\}$.
