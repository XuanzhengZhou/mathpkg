Normalized Hecke eigenforms have multiplicative Fourier coefficients. This is the starting point for the Langlands program: the $L$-function $\sum c(n)n^{-s}$ of a Hecke eigenform has an Euler product and an analytic continuation. Examples include Eisenstein series and the Ramanujan $\Delta$ function.
