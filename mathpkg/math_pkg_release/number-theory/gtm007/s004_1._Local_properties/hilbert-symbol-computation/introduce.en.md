Theorem 1 gives an explicit formula for computing the Hilbert symbol $(a, b)$ over $\mathbf{R}$ and $\mathbf{Q}_p$. Over the reals, the symbol is determined by signs: it is $+1$ unless both $a$ and $b$ are negative. Over the $p$-adic numbers, the computation depends on the parity of $p$. For odd $p$, the answer involves Legendre symbols of the unit parts and a factor depending on the product of the exponents. For $p = 2$, the formula uses the invariants $\varepsilon(u)$ and $\omega(u)$ defined in Chapter II, reflecting the more complicated structure of squares in $\mathbf{Q}_2$. This theorem is the computational backbone for working with Hilbert symbols in local class field theory.
