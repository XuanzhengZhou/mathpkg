Theorem 2 asserts that the Hilbert symbol is a nondegenerate bilinear form on the $\mathbf{F}_2$-vector space $k^*/k^{*2}$. This means the pairing $( \cdot , \cdot ) : k^*/k^{*2} \times k^*/k^{*2} \to \{\pm 1\} \cong \mathbf{F}_2$ is bilinear (proved in §2) and nondegenerate: for any fixed nonzero square class, there exists another square class whose pairing with it is $-1$. Equivalently, the radical of the bilinear form is trivial. This nondegeneracy, together with bilinearity, makes the Hilbert symbol the fundamental object encoding the arithmetic of quadratic forms over local fields.
