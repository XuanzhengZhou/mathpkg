The Hilbert symbol $(a, b) = \pm 1$ is a fundamental invariant attached to a pair of nonzero elements in a local field $k$ (the real numbers $\mathbf{R}$ or the $p$-adic numbers $\mathbf{Q}_p$). It encodes whether the ternary quadratic form $z^2 - a x^2 - b y^2$ represents zero nontrivially over $k$. Since the value depends only on the square classes of $a$ and $b$, the Hilbert symbol descends to a map on $k^*/k^{*2} \times k^*/k^{*2}$, giving it the structure of a bilinear pairing. It is the central object of study in Chapter III of Serre's *A Course in Arithmetic* and connects quadratic forms, norm groups, and local class field theory.
