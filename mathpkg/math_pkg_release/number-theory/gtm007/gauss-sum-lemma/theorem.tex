Let $l$ be a prime number $\neq 2$. Let $\Omega$ be an algebraically closed field of characteristic $p \neq l$, and let $w \in \Omega$ be a primitive $l$-th root of unity. Define the quadratic Gauss sum:
$$y = \sum_{x \in \mathbb{F}_l} \left(\frac{x}{l}\right) w^x.$$
Then $y^2 = (-1)^{\varepsilon(l)} l$, where $\varepsilon(l) = (l-1)/2 \pmod{2}$.
