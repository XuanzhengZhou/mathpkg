Over finite fields, quadratic forms of rank at least 2 represent all nonzero scalars, and forms of rank at least 3 represent zero (hence all scalars). This follows from the Chevalley-Warning theorem. Consequently, over $\mathbb{F}_q$ there are exactly two isometry classes of nondegenerate quadratic forms of each rank $\geq 1$, distinguished by their discriminant.
