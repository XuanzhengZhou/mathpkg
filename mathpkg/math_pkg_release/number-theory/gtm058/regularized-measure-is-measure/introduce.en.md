Theorem 6 establishes that the regularized measure $\mu_{1,\alpha}$ is bounded by 1, making it a genuine $p$-adic measure. This boundedness is essential for integrating continuous functions against $\mu_{1,\alpha}$, which is the foundation for constructing the $p$-adic zeta function. In fact, $\mu_{1,\alpha}$ is the first interesting example of a $p$-adic measure and plays a role as fundamental as $dx$ in real integration.
