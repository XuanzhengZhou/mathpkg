The $p$-adic ordinal extends the notion of divisibility by a prime $p$ to all rational numbers. It measures exactly how many factors of $p$ appear in the prime factorization of a number. The function $\operatorname{ord}_p$ behaves like a discrete logarithm: $\operatorname{ord}_p(xy) = \operatorname{ord}_p(x) + \operatorname{ord}_p(y)$. This additive valuation is the foundation for defining the $p$-adic absolute value and metric on $\mathbb{Q}$.
