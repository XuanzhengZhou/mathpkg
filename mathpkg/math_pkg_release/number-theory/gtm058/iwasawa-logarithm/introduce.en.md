Iwasawa extended the $p$-adic logarithm from the open unit disc around 1 to all nonzero elements of $\Omega$ by using the decomposition $x = p^r \omega(x_1) \langle x_1 \rangle$ (power of $p$ times root of unity times principal unit). Setting $\log_p(p^r) = 0$ (since $\log_p p = 0$) and $\log_p(\omega) = 0$ (since roots of unity are torsion), the logarithm is determined by its value on the principal unit part.
