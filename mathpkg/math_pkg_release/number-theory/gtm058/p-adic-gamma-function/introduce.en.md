The $p$-adic gamma function is Morita's $p$-adic interpolation of the factorial function. Unlike the classical gamma function, it is defined on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ rather than on $\mathbb{R}_{>0}$, and it takes values in $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$. It satisfies functional equations analogous to the classical case: $\Gamma_p(s+1) = -s\Gamma_p(s)$ if $s \in \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$, and a reflection formula $\Gamma_p(s)\Gamma_p(1-s) = (-1)^{s_0}$.
