A metric space is a set equipped with a distance function satisfying positivity, symmetry, and the triangle inequality. This abstract notion generalizes the familiar Euclidean distance on $\mathbb{R}^n$. The theory of metric spaces provides the framework for defining limits, continuity, and completeness, which are essential for the construction of the $p$-adic numbers in later sections.
