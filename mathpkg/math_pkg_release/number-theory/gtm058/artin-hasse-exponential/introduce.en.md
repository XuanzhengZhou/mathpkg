The Artin-Hasse exponential improves on $\exp_p$ by having a larger disc of convergence ($D(1^-)$ versus $D(p^{-1/(p-1)})$ for $\exp_p$). It is obtained from the formal infinite product for $\exp(X)$ by deleting all factors where $n$ is divisible by $p$. The coefficients of $E_p(X)$ are $p$-adic integers, which can be proved using Dwork's lemma. $E_p(X)$ plays a crucial role in Dwork's proof of the rationality of the zeta function.
