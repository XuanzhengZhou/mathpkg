Bernoulli numbers are rational numbers appearing in the Taylor expansion of /(e^t-1)$. They appear prominently in number theory: in formulas for $\zeta(2k)$, in the von Staudt-Clausen theorem, and in Kummer's work on Fermat's Last Theorem. All odd Bernoulli numbers $ for  > 1$ vanish.
