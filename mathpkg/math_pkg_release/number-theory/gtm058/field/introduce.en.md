A field is a commutative ring in which every nonzero element has a multiplicative inverse. The standard examples are the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$, the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$, and the complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$. Fields are the basic algebraic structures on which norms and absolute values are defined, leading eventually to the construction of $\mathbb{Q}_p$ and its extensions.
