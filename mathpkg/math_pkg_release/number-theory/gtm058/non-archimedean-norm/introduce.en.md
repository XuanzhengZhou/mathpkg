A non-Archimedean norm satisfies the ultrametric inequality, which is stronger than the ordinary triangle inequality. This property makes non-Archimedean analysis fundamentally different from classical analysis on $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$. Every triangle is isosceles with the non-Archimedean metric, and every point in an open disc serves as its center. The $p$-adic absolute value on $\mathbb{Q}$ is the prototypical example.
