The Weil Conjectures, formulated by Andre Weil in 1949, are a series of profound statements about the zeta functions of algebraic varieties over finite fields. Dwork proved rationality (i) in 1960; Grothendieck's school proved much of (i) and (ii) using etale cohomology; and Deligne completed the proof of the Riemann Hypothesis (iii) in 1973. The conjectures established deep connections between algebraic geometry, number theory, and topology.
