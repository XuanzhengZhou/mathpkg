Cyclotomic integers are the elements of the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\alpha]$ where $\alpha$ is a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity for an odd prime $\lambda$. They generalize ordinary integers and are the central objects of Kummer's theory of ideal factorization. The relation $1 + \alpha + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1} = 0$ implies that representations in the form $\sum a_i \alpha^i$ are not unique.
