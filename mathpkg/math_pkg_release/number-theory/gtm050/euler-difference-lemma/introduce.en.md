Euler's difference lemma is the constructive heart of his proof that every prime $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ is a sum of two squares. It guarantees the existence of integers $a, b$ such that $p \mid a^{2n} + b^{2n}$, which with Fermat's theorem yields $p \mid m^2 + 1$ for some $m$. This provides the starting point $kp = m^2 + 1^2$ for the infinite descent that ultimately yields $p = a^2 + b^2$.
