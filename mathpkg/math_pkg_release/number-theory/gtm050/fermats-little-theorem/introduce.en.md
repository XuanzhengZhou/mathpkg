Fermat's Little Theorem (so-called to distinguish it from his "Last" Theorem) is one of the most fundamental results in number theory. Fermat discovered it while studying perfect numbers and Mersenne primes. The theorem states that raising any integer to the $p$-th power leaves the same remainder modulo $p$ as the original integer. It is proved in the text using the observation that the remainders of $1, a, a^2, \ldots$ modulo $p$ must eventually repeat, and the first repeat determines the order $d$ of $a$ modulo $p$, which must divide $p-1$.
