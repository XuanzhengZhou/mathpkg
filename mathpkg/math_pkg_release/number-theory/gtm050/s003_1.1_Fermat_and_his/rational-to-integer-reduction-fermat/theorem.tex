If $x, y, z$ are rational numbers satisfying $x^n + y^n = z^n$ for some integer $n$, and if $d$ is the least common denominator of $x, y, z$, then $xd, yd, zd$ are integers and
$$(xd)^n + (yd)^n = (x^n + y^n)d^n = (zd)^n.$$
Thus the existence of rational solutions to $x^n + y^n = z^n$ implies the existence of integer solutions.
