A perfect number is one that equals the sum of its proper divisors (e.g., $6 = 1+2+3$, $28 = 1+2+4+7+14$). Euclid proved that if $2^p-1$ is prime, then $2^{p-1}(2^p-1)$ is perfect. Euler proved the converse: every even perfect number must be of this form. Whether any odd perfect numbers exist remains one of the oldest unsolved problems in mathematics. The connection to Mersenne primes makes the search for perfect numbers equivalent to the search for Mersenne primes.
