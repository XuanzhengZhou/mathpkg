The $n$-gonal numbers $0, 1, a_2, a_3, a_4, \ldots$ are obtained by successively adding the terms of the arithmetic progression $1, n-1, 2n-3, 3n-5, \ldots$.

Thus:
$$
a_0 = 0,\quad a_1 = 1,\quad a_2 = n,\quad a_3 = 3n - 3,\quad a_4 = 6n - 8,\quad a_5 = 10n - 15,\quad \ldots
$$

In general:
$$
a_j = \frac{1}{2}j(j-1)n - j(j-2).
$$

Special cases:
\begin{itemize}
\item Triangular numbers ($n=3$): $0, 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, \ldots$ obtained by adding $1, 2, 3, 4, 5, \ldots$
\item Square numbers ($n=4$): $0, 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, \ldots$ obtained by adding $1, 3, 5, 7, 9, \ldots$
\item Pentagonal numbers ($n=5$): $0, 1, 5, 12, 22, 35, \ldots$ obtained by adding $1, 4, 7, 10, 13, \ldots$
\end{itemize}
