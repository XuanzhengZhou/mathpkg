Polygonal numbers, also called $n$-gonal numbers, are a classical sequence of figurate numbers. They are constructed by starting at $0$ and $1$, then repeatedly adding successive terms of an arithmetic progression whose first term is $1$ and common difference is $n-2$. For $n=3$ one obtains the triangular numbers $0,1,3,6,10,\ldots$; for $n=4$ the square numbers $0,1,4,9,16,\ldots$; and for $n=5$ the pentagonal numbers $0,1,5,12,22,35,\ldots$. The general closed-form formula is $a_j = \frac{1}{2}j(j-1)n - j(j-2)$. These numbers were studied extensively in antiquity, including by Diophantus.
