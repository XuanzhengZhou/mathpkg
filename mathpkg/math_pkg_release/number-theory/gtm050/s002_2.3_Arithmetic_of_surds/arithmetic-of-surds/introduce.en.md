A surd is an algebraic expression involving a square root of a non-square integer, such as $p + q\sqrt{-3}$. The arithmetic of surds studies how to add, multiply, and factor such expressions. This arithmetic plays a central role in Euler's approach to proving Fermat's Last Theorem for the exponent $n = 3$, where numbers of the form $p + q\sqrt{-3}$ arise naturally from the factorization $x^3 + y^3 = (x + y)(x + y\omega)(x + y\omega^2)$ with $\omega$ a primitive cube root of unity. The key algebraic property exploited is the norm map $N(p + q\sqrt{D}) = p^2 - Dq^2$, which is multiplicative and connects arithmetic in the surd ring to ordinary integer arithmetic.
