A \textbf{surd} is an expression of the form $x + y\sqrt{D}$ where $x, y$ are integers (or rational numbers) and $D$ is a squarefree integer. The \textbf{arithmetic of surds} is the study of algebraic operations (addition, multiplication, factorization) on such expressions, treating $\sqrt{D}$ as a formal algebraic quantity satisfying $(\sqrt{D})^2 = D$. In the context of Fermat's Last Theorem for exponent $n = 3$, one works with surds of the form $p + q\sqrt{-3}$.
