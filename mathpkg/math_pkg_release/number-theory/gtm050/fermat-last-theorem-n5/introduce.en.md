The case $n=5$ of Fermat's Last Theorem was proved independently by Dirichlet and Legendre in 1825. The proof splits Case II (where 5 divides $xyz$) into two subcases and uses the method of infinite descent combined with the arithmetic of numbers of the form $a + b\sqrt{5}$. The technique generalizes Euler's proof for $n=3$ but requires the additional condition that $5 \nmid (p^2 - 5q^2)$ for the unique factorization argument to hold.
