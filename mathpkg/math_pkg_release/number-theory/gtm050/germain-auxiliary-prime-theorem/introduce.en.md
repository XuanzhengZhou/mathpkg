This theorem generalizes the argument used for $n=5$ (where $2 \cdot 5 + 1 = 11$ is prime) to any prime $n$ for which $2n+1$ is also prime. The proof uses the same technique of analyzing $n$-th powers modulo $2n+1$. This result motivated Sophie Germain to search for more general auxiliary primes, leading to her famous theorem. The condition that $2n+1$ is prime is quite restrictive, but it covers the important case $n=5$ and illustrates the general approach.
