For a prime $\lambda$ and a primitive root $\gamma$ modulo $\lambda$, the integer $\gamma_k$ is defined as the unique integer satisfying

$$0 < \gamma_k < \lambda, \qquad \gamma_k \equiv \gamma^k \pmod{\lambda}.$$

When $\alpha = e^{2\pi i/\lambda}$ is chosen as the specific primitive $\lambda$th root of unity, the conjugate $\sigma^k \alpha = e^{2\pi i \gamma^k/\lambda}$ corresponds to the angle $\theta = 2\pi \gamma_k / \lambda$, with $\theta$ reduced mod $2\pi$ to lie in $(0, 2\pi)$. The $\gamma_k$ appear in the explicit formula for $L(1, \chi_{2\nu+1})$ (the odd character case) and give the formula its arithmetic character.
