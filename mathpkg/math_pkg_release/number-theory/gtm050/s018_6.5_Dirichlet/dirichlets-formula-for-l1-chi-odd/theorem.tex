Let $\lambda$ be a prime, let $\alpha = e^{2\pi i/\lambda}$ be the specific primitive $\lambda$th root of unity, let $\gamma$ be a primitive root modulo $\lambda$, and let $\sigma : \alpha \mapsto \alpha^\gamma$ be the corresponding conjugation. For each $k$, let $\gamma_k$ be the integer determined by $0 < \gamma_k < \lambda$, $\gamma_k \equiv \gamma^k \bmod \lambda$. Let $\beta$ be a primitive $(\lambda-1)$st root of unity, and for each odd index $j = 2\nu+1$ let $\chi_{2\nu+1}$ be the Dirichlet character modulo $\lambda$ determined by $\chi_{2\nu+1}(\gamma) = \beta^{2\nu+1}$. Then

$$L(1, \chi_{2\nu+1}) = \frac{i\pi}{\lambda} m_{2\nu+1} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \gamma_k \beta^{-(2\nu+1)k},$$

where

$$m_{2\nu+1} = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \beta^{(2\nu+1)k} \sigma^k \alpha.$$
