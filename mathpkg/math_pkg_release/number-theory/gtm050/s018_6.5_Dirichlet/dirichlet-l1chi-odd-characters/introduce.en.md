For odd characters $\chi_{2\nu+1}$ (where $\chi(\gamma) = \beta^{2\nu+1}$), the evaluation of $L(1,\chi)$ takes a different form. The logarithmic terms combine to produce a purely imaginary expression proportional to a sum involving the integers $\gamma_k$ (the least positive residues of $\gamma^k$ modulo $\lambda$). Unlike the even case which involves logarithms of absolute values of cyclotomic expressions, the odd case yields a formula reminiscent of a generalized Gauss sum. These formulas are essential for Kummer's class number formula, which relates the class number of the cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_\lambda)$ to the product of these $L(1,\chi)$ values.
