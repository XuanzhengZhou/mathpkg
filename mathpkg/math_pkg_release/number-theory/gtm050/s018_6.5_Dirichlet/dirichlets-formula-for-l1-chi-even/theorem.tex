Let $\lambda$ be a prime, let $\alpha$ be a primitive $\lambda$th root of unity, let $\gamma$ be a primitive root modulo $\lambda$, and let $\sigma : \alpha \mapsto \alpha^\gamma$ be the corresponding conjugation. Let $\mu = (\lambda-1)/2$. Let $\beta$ be a primitive $(\lambda-1)$st root of unity, and for each even index $j = 2\nu$ let $\chi_{2\nu}$ be the Dirichlet character modulo $\lambda$ determined by $\chi_{2\nu}(\gamma) = \beta^{2\nu}$. Then

$$L(1, \chi_{2\nu}) = -2 m_{2\nu} \sum_{k=0}^{\mu-1} \beta^{-2\nu k} \log |1 - \sigma^k \alpha|,$$

where

$$m_{2\nu} = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \beta^{2\nu k} \sigma^k \alpha.$$
