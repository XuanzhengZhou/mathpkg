When the Dirichlet character $\chi_j$ corresponds to an even index $j = 2\nu$, the terms in Dirichlet's master formula combine in conjugate pairs to yield a purely real expression. Specifically, since $\beta^{-j\mu} = 1$ (where $\mu = (\lambda-1)/2$), the two terms $\beta^{-jk} \log \frac{1}{1-\sigma^k \alpha}$ and $\beta^{-j(k+\mu)} \log \frac{1}{1-\sigma^{k+\mu} \alpha}$ combine to give $-2\beta^{-jk} \log |1 - \sigma^k \alpha|$. The resulting formula,

$$L(1, \chi_{2\nu}) = -2 m_{2\nu} \sum_{k=0}^{\mu-1} \beta^{-2\nu k} \log |1 - \sigma^k \alpha|,$$

expresses $L(1, \chi_{2\nu})$ as a linear combination of logarithms of absolute values of cyclotomic units. This formula plays a central role in Kummer's derivation of the class number formula for real subfields.
