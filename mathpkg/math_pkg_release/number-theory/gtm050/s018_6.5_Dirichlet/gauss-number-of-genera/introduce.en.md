Gauss's theorem on the number of genera states that for a given determinant $D$, exactly half of the possible characters actually occur as characters of divisor classes. The number of genera is therefore $2^{m+\varepsilon-1}$, where $m$ is the number of odd prime factors of $D$ and $\varepsilon$ depends on the congruence of $D$ modulo 4 and 8. This theorem is equivalent to the statement that at most half of the possible characters occur, which Gauss proved by counting ambiguous classes and forms the basis of his second proof of quadratic reciprocity.
