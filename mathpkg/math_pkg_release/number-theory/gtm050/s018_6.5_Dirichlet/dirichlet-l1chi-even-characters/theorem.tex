Let $\lambda$ be a prime, let $\gamma$ be a primitive root modulo $\lambda$, let $\sigma: \alpha \mapsto \alpha^\gamma$ be the corresponding conjugation of cyclotomic integers, and let $\mu = \frac{1}{2}(\lambda - 1)$. For $j$ even, $\chi_j(\gamma) = \beta^j$ where $\beta$ is a primitive $(\lambda-1)$-st root of unity. Then

$$L(1, \chi_{2\nu}) = -2 m_{2\nu} \sum_{k=0}^{\mu-1} \beta^{-2\nu k} \log |1 - \sigma^k \alpha|$$

where $\alpha = e^{2\pi i/\lambda}$ and

$$m_{2\nu} = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{k=0}^{\lambda-2} \beta^{2\nu k} \sigma^k \alpha.$$
