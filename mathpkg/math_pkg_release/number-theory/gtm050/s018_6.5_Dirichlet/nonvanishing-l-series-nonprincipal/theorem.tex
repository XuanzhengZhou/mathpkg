Let $\chi$ be a nonprincipal Dirichlet character modulo a prime $\lambda$. Then
$$L(1, \chi) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\chi(n)}{n} \neq 0.$$
Moreover, $L(1, \chi) = 0$ can occur for at most one nonprincipal character $\chi$, and for the specific character $\chi_{\mu}$ defined by $\chi_{\mu}(\gamma) = -1$ (where $\gamma$ is a primitive root modulo $\lambda$), one has $L(1, \chi_{\mu}) \neq 0$.
