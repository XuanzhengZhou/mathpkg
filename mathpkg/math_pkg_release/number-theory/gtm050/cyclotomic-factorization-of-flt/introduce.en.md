The factorization of $x^\lambda + y^\lambda$ using a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity transforms the Fermat equation into a multiplicative problem in cyclotomic integers. The factors $x + \alpha^j y$ are pairwise relatively prime (under suitable conditions), so unique factorization (or ideal factorization) forces each factor to be a $\lambda$-th power up to a unit. This reduction is the gateway to Kummer's proof for regular primes.
