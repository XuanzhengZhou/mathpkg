This lemma is the crucial step in completing Euler's proof of Fermat's Last Theorem for $n=3$. It states that if $a^2+3b^2$ is a cube, then $a+b\sqrt{-3}$ itself is a cube in the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$. Euler assumed this by analogy with the integer case (if a product of two relatively prime numbers is a cube, each is a cube), but the proof requires more sophisticated arguments about factorization in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ that are developed in Section 2.5 using Euler's techniques for sums of two squares.
