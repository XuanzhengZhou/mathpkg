Kummer defined a prime $\lambda$ to be regular if $\lambda$ does not divide the class number of the $\lambda$-th cyclotomic field. He showed that this is equivalent to $\lambda$ not dividing the numerators of the Bernoulli numbers $B_2, B_4, \ldots, B_{\lambda-3}$. Kummer's great theorem states that FLT holds for all regular prime exponents. Among primes less than 100, only 37, 59, and 67 are irregular.
