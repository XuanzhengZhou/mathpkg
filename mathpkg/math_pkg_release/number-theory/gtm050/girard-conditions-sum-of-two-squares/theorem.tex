A positive integer $n$ can be written as a sum of two squares $n = x^2 + y^2$ (with $x, y$ integers, possibly zero) if and only if in the prime factorization of $n$, every prime of the form $4k+3$ appears with an even exponent.

Equivalently: if $n$ divided by the largest square it contains has a prime factor of the form $4n+3$, then $n$ is not a sum of two squares.
