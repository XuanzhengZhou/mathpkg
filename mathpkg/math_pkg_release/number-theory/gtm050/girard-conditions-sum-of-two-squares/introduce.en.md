Albert Girard (1595-1632) was the first to state necessary and sufficient conditions for a number to be representable as a sum of two squares. Fermat later claimed to have rigorous proofs of these conditions. The necessity (every prime factor of the form $4n+3$ must appear with even exponent) is relatively straightforward to prove using congruences modulo 4. The sufficiency is much deeper and was first proved by Euler, building on the key fact that every prime $p = 4n+1$ is itself a sum of two squares.
