This is the theorem for which Fermat's only complete proof survives. It states that the area of a right triangle with integer sides cannot be a perfect square. Interestingly, this theorem implies the case $n=4$ of Fermat's Last Theorem (since $z^4 - x^4 = (z^2-x^2)(z^2+x^2)$ cannot be a square). Fermat's proof uses the method of infinite descent and is notable for its ingenuity and obscurity at certain points.
