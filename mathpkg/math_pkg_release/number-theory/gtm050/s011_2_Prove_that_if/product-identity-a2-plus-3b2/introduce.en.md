This identity shows that the product of two numbers, each representable in the form $a^2+3b^2$, is again representable in the same form. It is the analog for the quadratic form $x^2+3y^2$ of the Brahmagupta-Fibonacci identity for $x^2+y^2$. The identity can be derived using the arithmetic of the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$ by computing $(a+b\sqrt{-3})(c+d\sqrt{-3}) = (ac-3bd) + (ad+bc)\sqrt{-3}$ and taking norms. This lemma is Statement (1') in Euler's proof of the characterization of primes representable as $a^2+3b^2$, which is needed for the $n=3$ case of Fermat's Last Theorem.
