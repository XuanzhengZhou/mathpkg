This proposition is Statement (2) in Euler's proof of Fermat's two-square theorem. It asserts that the set of integers representable as a sum of two squares is closed not only under multiplication (by the Brahmagupta-Fibonacci identity) but also under division by another sum of two squares, provided the division is exact. The proof uses the Brahmagupta-Fibonacci identity together with a case analysis based on which linear combination $pb \pm aq$ is divisible by $p^2+q^2$.
