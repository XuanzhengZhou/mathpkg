This identity generalizes the Brahmagupta-Fibonacci identity to arbitrary quadratic forms $x^2+cy^2$. It states that the product of two numbers, each representable as $x^2+cy^2$, can itself be represented in the same form via the formula $(x^2+cy^2)(u^2+cv^2) = (xu-cyv)^2 + c(xv+yu)^2$. This identity underlies Euler's idea of computing with "numbers" of the form $a+b\sqrt{-c}$, as it is exactly the statement that the norm map $N(a+b\sqrt{-c}) = a^2+cb^2$ is multiplicative. The formula has appeared repeatedly in Edwards' exposition for the specific cases $c=1,2,3$, and is fundamental to the arithmetic of quadratic integer rings used in the proof of Fermat's Last Theorem for $n=3$.
