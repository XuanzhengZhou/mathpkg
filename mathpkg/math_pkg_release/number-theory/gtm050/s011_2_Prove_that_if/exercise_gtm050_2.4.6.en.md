Prove that every prime of the form $8n + 3$ is of the form $a^2 + 2b^2$ as follows. Let $p = 8n + 3$ be prime. Show that $-2$ is a quadratic residue modulo $p$, so there exist integers $x, y$ with $x^2 + 2y^2 \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$ and $x, y$ coprime. Apply the analog of Statement (4) for the form $a^2 + 2b^2$ to conclude that $p$ itself is of the form $a^2 + 2b^2$.
