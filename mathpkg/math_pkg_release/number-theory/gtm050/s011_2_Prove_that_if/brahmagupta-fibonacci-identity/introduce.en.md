The Brahmagupta-Fibonacci identity states that the product of two integers, each expressed as a sum of two squares, can itself be expressed as a sum of two squares. Specifically, $(a^2+b^2)(p^2+q^2) = (ap-bq)^2 + (aq+bp)^2$. This identity was known to Brahmagupta in the 7th century and later rediscovered by Fibonacci. It is the fundamental algebraic tool underlying Euler's proof of Fermat's theorem that every prime $4n+1$ is a sum of two squares, as it ensures closure of the set of sums of two squares under multiplication.
