Prove that if $f^2 - 10g^2 = 1$ and if $x\sqrt{2} + y\sqrt{5} = (f + g\sqrt{10})(a\sqrt{2} + b\sqrt{5})^3$ then $2x^2 - 5y^2$ is a cube even though $x\sqrt{2} + y\sqrt{5}$ may not be a cube of the form $(u\sqrt{2} + v\sqrt{5})^3$. Euler believed that this phenomenon explained the failure of his method to find the solution $x = 4$ of $2x^2 - 5 = \text{cube}$. Show that $4\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{5}$ is of this form with $a = 2, b = -1$.
