In the proof of Fermat's Last Theorem for exponent $n = 5$, Case II (where $5 \mid xyz$) requires a further subdivision into two subcases. The first subcase occurs when the term divisible by $5$ is also the term divisible by $2$, so the equation takes the form $u^5 \pm v^5 = w^5$ with $w$ divisible by $10$. The second subcase occurs when the even number and the number divisible by $5$ are distinct. Dirichlet proved the impossibility of the first subcase in July 1825, and Legendre completed the proof for both subcases by September 1825.
