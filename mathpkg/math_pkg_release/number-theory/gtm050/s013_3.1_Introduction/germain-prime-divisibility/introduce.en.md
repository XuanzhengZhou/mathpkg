Let $n$ be an odd prime such that $2n + 1$ is also prime. Then any integer solution to $x^n + y^n = z^n$ must have $n$ dividing at least one of $x, y, z$. This generalizes the $n = 5$ case (where $2 \cdot 5 + 1 = 11$ is prime) and is proved using the same argument: factor $x^n + y^n$, use relative primality of the factors, and derive a contradiction modulo $2n+1$ using the fact that $n$th powers modulo $2n+1$ are limited to $0, \pm 1$.
