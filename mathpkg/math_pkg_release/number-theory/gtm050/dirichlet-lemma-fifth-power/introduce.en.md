This lemma is the crucial step in Dirichlet's proof of Fermat's Last Theorem for $n=5$. Unlike the case $n=3$ where $a^2+3b^2$ being a cube automatically implies $a+b\sqrt{-3}$ is a cube, the situation for $n=5$ is more subtle because Pell's equation $9^2 - 5 \cdot 4^2 = 1$ introduces a unit factor. Dirichlet discovered that the additional condition $5 \mid Q$ is exactly what is needed to eliminate the unit ambiguity and force $P+Q\sqrt{5}$ to be a genuine fifth power. This insight was crucial for the subsequent development of Kummer's theory.
