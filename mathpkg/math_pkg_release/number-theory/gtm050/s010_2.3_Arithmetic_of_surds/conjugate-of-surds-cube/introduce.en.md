In the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$, the conjugation map $a + b\sqrt{-3} \mapsto a - b\sqrt{-3}$ is a ring automorphism. When one factor $p + q\sqrt{-3}$ of the factorization $p^2 + 3q^2 = (p+q\sqrt{-3})(p-q\sqrt{-3})$ is expressed as a cube $(a+b\sqrt{-3})^3$, applying conjugation yields that the other factor $p - q\sqrt{-3}$ must be the cube of the conjugate, namely $(a-b\sqrt{-3})^3$. This simple observation is the key step in Euler's argument that $p^2+3q^2$ itself is a cube.
