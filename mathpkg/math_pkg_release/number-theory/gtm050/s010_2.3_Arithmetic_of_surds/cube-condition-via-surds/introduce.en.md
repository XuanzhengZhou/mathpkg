Euler's approach to proving that $p^2 + 3q^2$ is a cube relies on factoring it as $(p+q\sqrt{-3})(p-q\sqrt{-3})$ in the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$. If the factor $p+q\sqrt{-3}$ is itself a cube of some surd $a+b\sqrt{-3}$, then by taking conjugates one finds that $p-q\sqrt{-3}$ is the cube of the conjugate $a-b\sqrt{-3}$. Multiplying the two cube factors together, one concludes via commutativity that $p^2+3q^2 = (p+q\sqrt{-3})(p-q\sqrt{-3})$ is a cube. This sufficient condition provides a pathway for characterizing when numbers of the form $p^2+3q^2$ are cubes.
