Euler's key insight was to factor the integer $p^2 + 3q^2$ as a product of conjugate surds in the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$: $p^2 + 3q^2 = (p + q\sqrt{-3})(p - q\sqrt{-3})$. This factorization transforms a purely arithmetic question about integers into a question about the multiplicative structure of the ring of surds. If one of the factors can be shown to be a cube, then the product is a cube, providing a sufficient condition for $p^2 + 3q^2$ to be a cube.
