All primitive Pythagorean triples are parametrized by pairs of relatively prime integers $(p,q)$ of opposite parity with $p > q$. This characterization goes back to Euclid (Elements, Book X) and was known to Diophantus. It provides a complete, constructive description of all integer right triangles.
