This lemma is Step (5) of Euler's proof of Fermat's theorem on sums of two squares. It asserts that for a prime $p = 4n + 1$, among the consecutive pairs of integers raised to the power $2n$, at least one sum of the form $k^{2n} + (k+1)^{2n}$ is divisible by $p$. This fact provides the constructive starting point for finding a representation $p = a^2 + b^2$: it guarantees the existence of integers $x$ and $y$ such that $x^{2n} + y^{2n} \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$, which can then be refined through the division process to obtain the actual squares.
