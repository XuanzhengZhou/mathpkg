Fermat's Theorem on Sums of Two Squares states that every prime number congruent to 1 modulo 4 can be represented as the sum of two perfect squares. Fermat stated this theorem in a 1654 letter to Pascal and again in correspondence with Digby. Euler provided the first proof using the method of infinite descent. The theorem is a foundational result in number theory connecting prime congruences with quadratic forms, and serves as a precursor to the theory of quadratic reciprocity and the study of representations by binary quadratic forms.
