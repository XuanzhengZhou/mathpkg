The number of primes which ramify is $m + \varepsilon$, where $m$ is the number of distinct odd prime factors of $D$, and $\varepsilon = 1$ when $D \equiv 2$ or $3 \pmod{4}$ (in which case 2 ramifies), and $\varepsilon = 0$ when $D \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$. When $D < 0$, there are therefore $2^{m + \varepsilon}$ divisors of the form $(p_1, *)(p_2, *) \cdots (p_k, *)$, including the empty product $I$.
