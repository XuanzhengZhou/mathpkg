This theorem gives a clean characterization of ambiguous divisors in the divisor class group of quadratic integers. A divisor is ambiguous exactly when it lies in the same class as a divisor of $4D$ (or of $D$, depending on the congruence of $D$ modulo 4). This reduces the problem of counting ambiguous classes to counting inequivalent divisors of $4D$ or $D$, which are far easier to enumerate because their prime factors are the ramified primes.
