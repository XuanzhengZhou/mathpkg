Show that if $D$ is a (positive) prime that is 1 mod 4 then the cycle of $I$, say $I \sim A_1 \sim A_2 \sim \cdots$ contains consecutive divisors with $N(A_i) = -N(A_{i+1})$. Conclude that this gives a solution of Fermat's problem of finding a representation $D = u^2 + v^2$. Apply this method in the cases $D = 13$ and $D = 233$ (Disquisitiones Arithmeticae, Art. 265).
