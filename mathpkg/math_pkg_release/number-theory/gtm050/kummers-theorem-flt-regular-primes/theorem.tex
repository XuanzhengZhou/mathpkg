Let $\lambda$ be an odd prime. A sufficient condition for Fermat's Last Theorem to hold for the exponent $\lambda$ is that $\lambda$ be regular, i.e., $\lambda$ does not divide the class number of the cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(e^{2\pi i/\lambda})$. Equivalently, $\lambda$ does not divide the numerators of the Bernoulli numbers $B_2, B_4, \ldots, B_{\lambda-3}$.
