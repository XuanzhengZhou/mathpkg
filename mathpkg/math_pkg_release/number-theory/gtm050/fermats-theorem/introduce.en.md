Fermat's theorem (often called Fermat's Little Theorem) states that for any prime $p$ and integer $a$ not divisible by $p$, $a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$. Fermat discovered this fundamental result while studying Mersenne primes $2^p - 1$. The theorem is foundational to modular arithmetic and has far-reaching consequences in number theory and cryptography.
