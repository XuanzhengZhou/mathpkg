A \textbf{Mersenne prime} is a prime number of the form
$$M_p = 2^p - 1$$
where $p$ is itself a prime. If $2^p - 1$ is prime, then $2^{p-1}(2^p - 1)$ is a perfect number.

The condition that $p$ be prime is necessary but not sufficient: $2^{11} - 1 = 2047 = 23 \cdot 89$ is composite despite 11 being prime.
