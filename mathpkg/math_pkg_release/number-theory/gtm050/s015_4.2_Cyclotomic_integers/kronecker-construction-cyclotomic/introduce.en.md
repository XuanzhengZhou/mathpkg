Kronecker, a student and close associate of Kummer, proposed an abstract algebraic approach to cyclotomic integers: they are the quotient of the polynomial ring $\mathbb{Z}[X]$ by the ideal generated by the cyclotomic polynomial $\Phi_\lambda(X) = 1 + X + \cdots + X^{\lambda-1}$. This construction emphasizes the algebraic computational rules and avoids reliance on complex numbers. In modern algebra, this is the standard definition: the ring of cyclotomic integers is $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_\lambda] \cong \mathbb{Z}[X]/(\Phi_\lambda(X))$, where all algebraic properties follow from the single relation $\Phi_\lambda(\alpha) = 0$.
