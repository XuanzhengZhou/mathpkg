Although cyclotomic integers are complex numbers, their norms are always ordinary integers. This follows from the fact that the norm is a symmetric polynomial in the conjugates of $\alpha$ and thus belongs to the fixed field of the Galois group of $\mathbb{Q}(\alpha)/\mathbb{Q}$, which is $\mathbb{Q}$. Since the coefficients $a_i$ are integers, the norm is actually an integer. This property is what makes the norm useful for reducing factorization questions about cyclotomic integers to questions about ordinary integers.
