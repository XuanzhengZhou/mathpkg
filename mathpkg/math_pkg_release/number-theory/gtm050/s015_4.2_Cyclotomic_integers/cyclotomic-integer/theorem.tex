Let $\lambda$ be a prime and let $\alpha$ be a primitive $\lambda$th root of unity, i.e., $\alpha^\lambda = 1$ and $\alpha \neq 1$. A \textbf{cyclotomic integer} is a number of the form
$$a_0 + a_1\alpha + a_2\alpha^2 + \cdots + a_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$$
where $a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_{\lambda-1} \in \mathbb{Z}$ are ordinary integers.
