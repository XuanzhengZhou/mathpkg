Euclid proved that if $2^p - 1$ is prime, then $2^{p-1}(2^p-1)$ is perfect. Euler later proved the converse: every even perfect number has this form. The existence of odd perfect numbers remains an open problem. This theorem connects the ancient study of perfect numbers to the modern theory of Mersenne primes.
