Mersenne primes are primes of the form $2^p - 1$ with $p$ prime. They are named after Father Marin Mersenne, a contemporary of Fermat. The condition that $p$ be prime is necessary (if $n$ is composite, $2^n-1$ is composite), but not sufficient (e.g., $2^{11} - 1 = 2047 = 23 \times 89$). Mersenne primes are intimately connected to even perfect numbers via Euclid's formula.
