The necessity of the condition (a prime $x^2 + 3y^2$ must be of the form $6n+1$) is straightforward using congruences modulo 6 (see Exercise 2 of this section). The sufficiency -- that every prime $p \equiv 1 \pmod{3}$ can be written as $x^2 + 3y^2$ -- is proved in the next chapter (Chapter 2).
