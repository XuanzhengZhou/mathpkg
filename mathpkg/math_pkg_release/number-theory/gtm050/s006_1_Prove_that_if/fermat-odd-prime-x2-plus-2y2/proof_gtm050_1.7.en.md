Fermat stated and explicitly claimed a rigorous proof that an odd prime is of the form $x^2 + 2y^2$ if and only if it is of the form $8n+1$ or $8n+3$. The necessity (only if) direction is straightforward using congruences modulo 8 (see Exercise 3 of this section). The sufficiency is much deeper and is addressed in Exercises 6 and 7 of Section 2.4.
