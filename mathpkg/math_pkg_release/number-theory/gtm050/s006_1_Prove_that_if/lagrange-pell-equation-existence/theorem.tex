For every positive integer $A$ that is not a perfect square, Pell's equation $y^2 - Ax^2 = 1$ has infinitely many integer solutions $(x, y)$. The cyclic method (or the English method of Wallis and Brouncker) always succeeds in finding a solution.

The first proof was given by Lagrange approximately 110 years after Wallis produced his answer to Fermat's challenge. Neither Wallis, Brouncker, nor even Euler had succeeded in proving that the method always terminates.
