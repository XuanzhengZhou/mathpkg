Lagrange gave the first rigorous proof that Pell's equation $y^2 - Ax^2 = 1$ always has infinitely many solutions for any non-square $A$. This resolved the defect in the English solution to Fermat's challenge -- Wallis and Brouncker had given a procedure but had not proved it always succeeds. Even Euler could not provide such a proof. Lagrange's proof is outlined in the exercises following this section. It is unclear whether Fermat himself possessed a fully satisfactory proof.
