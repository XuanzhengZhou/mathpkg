Fermat stated this theorem many times throughout his career and claimed he could prove it rigorously, though as usual he did not put the proof in writing. The result is sometimes called Fermat's Christmas theorem. Fermat further claimed to have a general method for finding the representation without trial and error. Proofs are given later in Sections 2.4 and 2.6 of the book.
