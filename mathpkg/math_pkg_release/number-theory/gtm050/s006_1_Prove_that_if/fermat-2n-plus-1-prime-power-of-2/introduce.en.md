This theorem establishes that if $2^n + 1$ is prime, then $n$ must be a power of 2. This follows from the algebraic factorization of $x^k + 1$ when $k$ is odd. The converse is false: $F_5 = 2^{32} + 1$ is composite despite $5$ being a power of 2. This observation motivated Fermat's conjecture that all Fermat numbers $2^{2^n} + 1$ are prime.
