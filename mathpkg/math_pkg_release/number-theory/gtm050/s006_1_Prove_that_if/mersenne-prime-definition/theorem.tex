A \textit{Mersenne prime} is a prime number of the form $M_p = 2^p - 1$ where $p$ is prime. They are named in honor of Father Marin Mersenne (1588--1648), a frequent correspondent of Fermat.

If $n$ is composite, say $n = km$, then $2^n - 1 = 2^{km} - 1 = (2^k - 1)(2^{k(m-1)} + 2^{k(m-2)} + \cdots + 2^k + 1)$ is composite. Thus $p$ must be prime for $2^p - 1$ to be prime.
