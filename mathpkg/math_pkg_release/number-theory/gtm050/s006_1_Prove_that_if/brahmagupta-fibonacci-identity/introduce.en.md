The Brahmagupta-Fibonacci identity states that the product of two numbers that are sums of two squares is itself a sum of two squares in two distinct ways. This identity was known to Diophantus, to Leonardo of Pisa (Fibonacci) in the thirteenth century, and is implicit in the algebra of ancient India. It serves as the fundamental multiplicative property underlying the theory of representations by the quadratic form $x^2 + y^2$, and is the key to proving that Girard's sufficient conditions for representability as a sum of two squares hold.
