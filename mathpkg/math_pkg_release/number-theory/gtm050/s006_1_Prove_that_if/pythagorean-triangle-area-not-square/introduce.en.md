Fermat proved that no Pythagorean triangle can have an area equal to a square using the method of infinite descent, the only complete proof of Fermat's that survives. The proof shows that if such a triangle existed with $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$ and area $\frac12 xy$ a square, then one could construct a smaller such triangle indefinitely, yielding an impossible infinite descending sequence of positive integers. This result immediately implies the $n=4$ case of Fermat's Last Theorem, since $z^4 - x^4$ being a square would contradict it.
