Fermat discovered his fundamental theorem while studying the problem of determining which Mersenne numbers $2^p - 1$ are prime. The theorem states that for any prime $p$ not dividing $a$, the $(p-1)$-th power of $a$ is congruent to 1 modulo $p$. Fermat proved this by observing that among the remainders of successive powers of $a$, there must be a repetition, which then constrains the structure of all remainders. The generalization that the order $d$ must divide $p-1$ follows from partitioning all $p-1$ non-zero remainders into sets of size $d$.
