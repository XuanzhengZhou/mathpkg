In 1657, Fermat posed this problem as a challenge to the English mathematicians, particularly Wallis and Brouncker. The problem asks for a proof that for any non-square positive integer $A$, there are infinitely many $x$ such that $Ax^2 + 1$ is a square. This special quadratic Diophantine equation $y^2 - Ax^2 = 1$ is of fundamental importance for solving general quadratic equations in two variables over integers. The problem is now known as Pell's equation due to a historical mistake by Euler.
