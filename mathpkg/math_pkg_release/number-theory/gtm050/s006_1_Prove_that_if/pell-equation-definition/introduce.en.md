Pell's equation $y^2 - Ax^2 = 1$ is a fundamental Diophantine equation whose importance was first recognized by Fermat. Despite its name, Pell had nothing to do with it -- Euler mistakenly attributed the solution method to him. The problem has deep connections to the theory of quadratic forms, algebraic number theory, and the structure of units in real quadratic fields.
