This theorem generalizes Fermat's little theorem by showing that the order $d$ of $a$ modulo $p$ must divide $p-1$. The proof partitions the $p-1$ non-zero remainders into equivalence classes of size $d$, given by the orbit of multiplication by $a$. This result is the foundation for understanding the multiplicative structure of $\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$ and is essential to Fermat's approach to testing primality of Mersenne numbers.
