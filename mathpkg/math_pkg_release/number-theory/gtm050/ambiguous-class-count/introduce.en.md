The count of ambiguous classes is a crucial step in Gauss's second proof of quadratic reciprocity. An ambiguous class is one that is equal to its own conjugate (or equivalently, whose square is the principal class). The bound on the number of ambiguous classes, combined with the factorization $h = sa$ (where $s$ is the number of squares and $a$ the number of ambiguous classes), implies that at most half of the possible characters of divisor classes actually occur. This bound on characters is equivalent to quadratic reciprocity.
