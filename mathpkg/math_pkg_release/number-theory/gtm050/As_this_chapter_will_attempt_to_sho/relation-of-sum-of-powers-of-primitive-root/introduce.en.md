For a prime $\lambda$ and a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity $\alpha \neq 1$, the sum of all $\lambda$ powers of $\alpha$ from exponent $0$ to $\lambda-1$ vanishes. This follows from the observation that $1 + \alpha + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1} = \alpha^\lambda + \alpha + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1} = \alpha(1 + \alpha + \cdots + \alpha^{\lambda-1})$, which implies either the sum is zero or $\alpha = 1$. Since $\alpha \neq 1$ by assumption, the sum must be zero. This identity is the fundamental extra relation satisfied by cyclotomic integers beyond the ring operations.
