A unit in the ring of cyclotomic integers is an element whose norm equals $1$. Units are the invertible elements of the ring: they have multiplicative inverses that are also cyclotomic integers. Because a unit divides every cyclotomic integer (via $h(\alpha) = f(\alpha) \cdot f(\alpha)^{-1} h(\alpha)$), units are treated as trivial factors in factorization theory, analogous to $\pm 1$ in the ordinary integers. The classification of cyclotomic units is a central problem in Kummer's theory.
