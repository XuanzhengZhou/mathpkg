Let $f(\alpha) \neq 0$ and $h(\alpha)$ be cyclotomic integers. To determine whether there exists a cyclotomic integer $g(\alpha)$ such that $f(\alpha)g(\alpha) = h(\alpha)$, one may proceed as follows. Consider the product
$$h(\alpha) \cdot f(\alpha^2) f(\alpha^3) \cdots f(\alpha^{\lambda-1}) = \frac{h(\alpha) \cdot Nf(\alpha)}{f(\alpha)}.$$
If the quotient $g(\alpha)$ exists, then this product equals $g(\alpha) \cdot Nf(\alpha)$. Thus a necessary condition for divisibility is that
$$h(\alpha) \cdot f(\alpha^2) f(\alpha^3) \cdots f(\alpha^{\lambda-1})$$
be divisible by the ordinary integer $Nf(\alpha)$, which can be checked using the criterion for divisibility by an integer (all coefficients congruent modulo $Nf(\alpha)$). If this condition holds, then $g(\alpha)$ is obtained by dividing each coefficient by $Nf(\alpha)$.
