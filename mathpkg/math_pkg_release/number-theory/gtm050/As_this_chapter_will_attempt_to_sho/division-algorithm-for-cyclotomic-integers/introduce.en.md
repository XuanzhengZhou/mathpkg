Kummer's division algorithm reduces the problem of testing whether one cyclotomic integer divides another to the case of division by an ordinary integer, which was already solved. The method multiplies $h(\alpha)$ by the product of all conjugates of $f(\alpha)$ except $f(\alpha)$ itself, yielding $g(\alpha) \cdot Nf(\alpha)$ if division is possible. One then checks whether this product is divisible by the ordinary integer $Nf(\alpha)$ using the coefficient-congruence criterion. This algorithm is fundamental to Kummer's computational approach to factorization in cyclotomic integers.
