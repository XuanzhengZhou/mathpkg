Let $a_0$ be a nonzero integer and let $h(\alpha) = b_0 + b_1\alpha + \cdots + b_{\lambda-1}\alpha^{\lambda-1}$ be a cyclotomic integer. Then $h(\alpha)$ is divisible by $a_0$ in the ring of cyclotomic integers, i.e., $h(\alpha) = a_0 \, g(\alpha)$ for some cyclotomic integer $g(\alpha)$, if and only if all coefficients of $h(\alpha)$ are congruent to one another modulo $a_0$:
$$b_i \equiv b_j \pmod{a_0} \quad \text{for all } i, j.$$
