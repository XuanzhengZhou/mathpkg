An analogous theorem to Fermat's two-square theorem states that every prime of the form $3n+1$ can be represented as $a^2 + 3b^2$. This result belongs to the broader theory of representations of primes by binary quadratic forms, connecting to the arithmetic of the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$. The proof follows a similar structure to the $x^2 + y^2$ case, using properties of congruences and reduction steps.
