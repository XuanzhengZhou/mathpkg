This corollary provides a classification of prime cyclotomic integers by their norms. For each rational prime $p \equiv 0$ or $1 \pmod{\lambda}$, all prime cyclotomic integers of norm $p$ lie in a single orbit under the combined action of the unit group and the Galois group of the cyclotomic field. This means that once one prime divisor of $p$ is found, all others are obtained from it by applying automorphisms $\alpha \mapsto \alpha^i$ and multiplying by units. The result is a strong uniqueness statement that mirrors the fact that in $\mathbb{Z}$, the only prime divisors of a prime $p$ are $\pm p$ (units times the canonical prime).
