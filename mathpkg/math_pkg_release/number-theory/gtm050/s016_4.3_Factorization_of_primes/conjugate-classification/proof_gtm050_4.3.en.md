Since $h_2(\alpha)$ is prime and divides $p = Nh_1(\alpha) = \prod_{i=1}^{\lambda-1} h_1(\alpha^i)$, the primality of $h_2(\alpha)$ implies that $h_2(\alpha)$ must divide one of the conjugates $h_1(\alpha^i)$ for some $i$. On the other hand, $h_1(\alpha^i)$ also has norm $p$ (since the norm is invariant under Galois conjugation) and is prime (since conjugation preserves primality). Therefore $h_2(\alpha)$ and $h_1(\alpha^i)$ are two prime cyclotomic integers dividing each other, which in the ring of cyclotomic integers means they differ by a unit factor. Thus $h_2(\alpha) = u \cdot h_1(\alpha^i)$ for some unit $u$.
