The congruence defined on cyclotomic integers mirrors the familiar integer congruence but operates in the ring $\mathbb{Z}[\alpha]$ where $\alpha$ is a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity. For a fixed modulus $h(\alpha)$, the relation $f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \bmod h(\alpha)$ partitions the cyclotomic integers into equivalence classes. Like its integer counterpart, this congruence is an equivalence relation compatible with the ring operations, meaning one can add, subtract, and multiply congruences in the usual way. This makes it possible to reduce questions about divisibility by $h(\alpha)$ to algebraic manipulations of congruences.
