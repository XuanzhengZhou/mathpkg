Let $h(\alpha)$ be a prime cyclotomic integer dividing the binomial $x + \alpha^j y$, where $x$ and $y$ are relatively prime integers. Let $p = Nh(\alpha)$ and let $k$ be an integer such that $\alpha \equiv k \pmod{h(\alpha)}$. Then for any two cyclotomic integers $f(\alpha)$ and $g(\alpha)$,

$$f(\alpha) \equiv g(\alpha) \mod h(\alpha) \iff f(k) \equiv g(k) \mod p,$$

where $f(k)$ and $g(k)$ denote the integers obtained by substituting $\alpha = k$ in $f(\alpha)$ and $g(\alpha)$.
