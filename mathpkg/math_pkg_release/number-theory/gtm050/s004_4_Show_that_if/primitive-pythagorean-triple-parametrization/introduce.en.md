Euclid's formula provides a complete parametrization of all primitive Pythagorean triples. Starting from the equation $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$ with $\gcd(x,y,z)=1$, parity considerations force $z$ to be odd and $x,y$ to have opposite parity. Factoring $x^2 = z^2 - y^2 = (z+y)(z-y)$ and using coprimality arguments yields the existence of parameters $p,q$ uniquely determining the triple. This characterization reduces the problem of finding Pythagorean triples to choosing pairs of coprime integers with opposite parity.
