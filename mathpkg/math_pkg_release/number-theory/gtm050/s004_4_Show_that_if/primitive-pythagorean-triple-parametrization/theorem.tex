Let $x, y, z$ be a primitive Pythagorean triple, i.e., positive integers satisfying
$$x^2 + y^2 = z^2, \quad \gcd(x,y,z) = 1,$$
with $x$ even, $y$ odd, and $z$ odd. Then there exist relatively prime positive integers $p, q$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $p > q$,
\item $p$ and $q$ have opposite parity,
\item $x = 2pq$, $y = p^2 - q^2$, $z = p^2 + q^2$.
\end{enumerate}
Conversely, for any pair of integers $p, q$ satisfying (1)-(3), the numbers $2pq$, $p^2 - q^2$, $p^2 + q^2$ form a primitive Pythagorean triple.
