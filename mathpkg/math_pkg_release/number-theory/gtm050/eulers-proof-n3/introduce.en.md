Euler proved the case $n=3$ of Fermat's Last Theorem using the method of infinite descent and the arithmetic of numbers of the form $a + b\sqrt{-3}$. Although Euler's original proof contained a gap (assuming unique factorization in $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]$), the argument can be corrected using techniques that Euler himself developed for sums of two squares.
