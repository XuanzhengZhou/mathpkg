For a primitive $\lambda$-th root of unity $\alpha$, the following product identity holds for arbitrary $\lambda$:

$$
(\alpha^2 - 1)(\alpha^3 - 1) \cdots (\alpha^{\lambda-1} - 1) = (\lambda - 1)\alpha^{\lambda-1} + (\lambda - 2)\alpha^{\lambda-2} + \cdots + 2\alpha^2 + \alpha.
$$

Equivalently, expressed as a polynomial identity in $\alpha$,

$$
\prod_{j=2}^{\lambda-1} (\alpha^j - 1) = \sum_{k=1}^{\lambda-1} k \,\alpha^{\lambda-k}.
$$
