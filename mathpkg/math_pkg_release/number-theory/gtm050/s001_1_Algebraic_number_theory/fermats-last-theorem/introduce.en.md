Fermat's Last Theorem is the assertion that the Diophantine equation $x^n + y^n = z^n$ admits no nontrivial integer solutions for any integer exponent $n > 2$. Pierre de Fermat famously claimed to have discovered a "truly marvelous proof" of this fact around 1637 in the margin of his copy of Diophantus' Arithmetica, though no complete proof was found among his papers. The theorem remained unproven for over 350 years, serving as one of the most famous unsolved problems in mathematics until Andrew Wiles provided a complete proof in 1995 using deep results from the theory of elliptic curves and modular forms.
