The case $n = 4$ of Fermat's Last Theorem states that the equation $x^4 + y^4 = z^4$ has no nontrivial integer solutions. This is the simplest non-trivial case and was proved by Fermat himself using the method of infinite descent. The proof is elementary and can be presented in a few lines, making it accessible to beginning students of number theory.
