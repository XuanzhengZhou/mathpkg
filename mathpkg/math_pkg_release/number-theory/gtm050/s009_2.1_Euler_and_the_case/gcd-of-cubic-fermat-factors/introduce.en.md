This lemma analyzes the greatest common divisor of the two factors $2p$ and $p^2 + 3q^2$ appearing in the reduced equation. Since $p$ and $q$ have opposite parity, $p^2 + 3q^2$ is odd, so any common divisor must divide $p$ and $3q^2$. With $p, q$ coprime, the only possible common prime factor is 3. The proof therefore splits into two cases depending on whether 3 divides $p$ or not. Euler first treats the case $3 \nmid p$, where the two factors are coprime.
