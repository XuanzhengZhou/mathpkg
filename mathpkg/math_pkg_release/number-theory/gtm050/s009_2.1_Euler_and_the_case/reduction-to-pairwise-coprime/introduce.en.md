This lemma provides the initial reduction in Euler's proof of the case $n=3$ of Fermat's Last Theorem. It shows that if $x^3 + y^3 = z^3$ holds, any common prime factor dividing two of $\{x, y, z\}$ must also divide the third. This allows dividing out all common factors, reducing to the case where $x, y, z$ are pairwise relatively prime -- a crucial simplification that makes the subsequent descent argument possible.
