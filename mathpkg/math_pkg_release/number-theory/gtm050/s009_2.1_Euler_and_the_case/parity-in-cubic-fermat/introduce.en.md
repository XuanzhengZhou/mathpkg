Once the solution is reduced to pairwise coprime integers, a simple parity argument shows that exactly one of $x, y, z$ must be even. If all three were odd, then $x^3 + y^3$ would be even while $z^3$ would be odd. If two were even, they would share the factor 2, contradicting pairwise coprimality. This parity classification determines which variable is even and sets up the algebraic reduction in the next step.
