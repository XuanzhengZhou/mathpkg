Fermat's method of infinite descent is a powerful proof technique for showing that certain Diophantine equations have no positive integer solutions. The principle relies on the well-ordering of the natural numbers: if the existence of a positive integer solution implies the existence of a strictly smaller positive integer solution, then by infinite descent no solution can exist. Fermat himself used this method to prove the case $n=4$ of his Last Theorem, and Euler adapted it for the case $n=3$.
