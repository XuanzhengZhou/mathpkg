A prime cyclotomic integer is defined analogously to a prime integer: it is a nonzero non-unit with the property that if it divides a product, it must divide at least one factor. However, unique factorization into primes fails in general for cyclotomic integers, which motivated Kummer to introduce the theory of ideal prime factors.
