A \textbf{divisor} (or \textbf{ideal divisor}) in the ring of cyclotomic integers is a formal product of prime divisors $P_1^{n_1}P_2^{n_2}\cdots P_k^{n_k}$ where each $P_i$ is determined by a pair $(p, u)$ with $p$ a prime integer and $u$ a cyclotomic integer, and $n_i \geq 0$ are integers.

A cyclotomic integer $g(\alpha)$ is said to be \textbf{divisible} by a divisor $A$ if every prime divisor that divides $A$ also divides $g(\alpha)$ with at least the same multiplicity.

Two divisors $A$ and $B$ are \textbf{equivalent}, written $A \sim B$, if there exist cyclotomic integers $f(\alpha), g(\alpha)$ such that $Af(\alpha) = Bg(\alpha)$ as divisors.
