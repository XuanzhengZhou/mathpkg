Fermat numbers are integers of the form $F_n = 2^{2^n} + 1$. Fermat believed all such numbers to be prime, based on the primality of $F_0 = 3$, $F_1 = 5$, $F_2 = 17$, $F_3 = 257$, and $F_4 = 65537$. Euler later disproved this by showing $F_5 = 641 \times 6700417$. Remarkably, no Fermat primes beyond $F_4$ are known.
