The method of infinite descent was Fermat's most powerful and characteristic proof technique. It works by contradiction: assuming a solution exists, one constructs a strictly smaller solution, leading to an infinite descending chain of positive integers, which is impossible. Fermat used this method for most of his number-theoretic proofs, including the case $n=4$ of his Last Theorem.
