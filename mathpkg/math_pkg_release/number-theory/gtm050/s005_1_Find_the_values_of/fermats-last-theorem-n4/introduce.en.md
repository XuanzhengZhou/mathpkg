Fermat's Last Theorem for exponent $n = 4$ states that the equation $x^4 + y^4 = z^4$ has no nontrivial integer solutions. This is the only case for which Fermat himself gave a complete proof, using the method of infinite descent combined with the parametrization of Pythagorean triples. The proof actually establishes the stronger result that the sum of two fourth powers cannot even be a square.
