Kummer's Lemma (also known as Kummer's Lemma on Units) is a crucial technical result needed for the proof of Fermat's Last Theorem for regular primes. It states that if the class number is prime to $\lambda$ (Condition A), then units that are congruent to integers modulo $\lambda$ are automatically $\lambda$-th powers (Condition B). The proof involves deep properties of the cyclotomic units and the logarithm mapping. Historically, Kummer first proved Condition (B) directly for all primes (not just regular ones), and only later realized that (A) implies (B), which simplified the proof of his main theorem.
