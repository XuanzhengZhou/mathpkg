$$5^{2\alpha-1} = (1 + 2^\alpha u)^2 = 1 + 2^{\alpha+1} u + 2^{2\alpha} u^2 = 1 + 2^{\alpha+1} u (1 + 2^{\alpha-1} u).$$

Therefore we have the validity of the assertion for $\alpha + 1$.

We observe further that for composite moduli $n$, the group $\Re(n)$ is not cyclic in general. If $p$ is a divisor of $\varphi(n)$, then, by Theorem 42, the basis number $e(p)$ of $\Re(n)$, which belongs to $p$, is equal to the sum of the basis numbers $e_i(p)$, which belong to $p$ in $\Re(p_i^{\alpha_i})$, where $p = p_1^{\alpha_1} p_2^{\alpha_2} \cdots$ is the decomposition of $n$ into primes. However for odd $p_i$, 2 is a divisor of $\varphi(p_i^{\alpha_i})$ and consequently $e_i(2) = 1$. Thus if two odd primes divide $n$, so does $e(2)$ for $\Re(n) \geq 2$. Hence the group is not cyclic.
