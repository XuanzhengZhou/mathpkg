# Proof of If $r \geq 0$, then the number $k$ of independent units of the field is exactly $r$

By Lemma (b), there is a unit $\varepsilon_1$ such that

$$\log |\varepsilon_1^{(1)}| \neq 0.$$

Then if $r > 1$, there is likewise a unit $\varepsilon_2$ such that the $2 \times 2$ determinant

$$\begin{vmatrix} \log |\varepsilon_1^{(1)}| & \log |\varepsilon_2^{(1)}| \\ \log |\varepsilon_1^{(2)}| & \log |\varepsilon_2^{(2)}| \end{vmatrix} \neq 0$$

and so on. Thus we conclude from Lemma (b) the existence of $r$ units $\varepsilon_1, \ldots, \varepsilon_r$ for which the $r \times r$ determinant of logarithms of absolute values of conjugates is nonzero. This shows $k \geq r$.

Combined with Lemma (d), which gives the upper bound $k \leq r$, we obtain $k = r$. Together with Lemma (d), the validity of Dirichlet's unit theorem (Theorem 100) is established.
