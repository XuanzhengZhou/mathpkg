# Proof of Theorem 116: Relative Quadratic Extensions

**Theorem.** If $\mu \in k$ is not an $l$th power in $k$ and $k$ contains a primitive $l$th root of unity $\zeta$, then $K = k(\sqrt[l]{\mu})$ has relative degree $l$ over $k$ and is a Galois (relatively cyclic) extension with Galois group generated by the automorphism $s : \sqrt[l]{\mu} \mapsto \zeta \sqrt[l]{\mu}$.

*Proof.* Set $M = \sqrt[l]{\mu}$. The minimal polynomial of $M$ over $k$ divides $x^l - \mu$, whose roots are

$$\zeta^a M \qquad (a = 0, 1, \ldots, l-1).$$

Let $m \leq l$ be the number of distinct relative conjugates of $M$ over $k$, say $\zeta^{a_1} M, \ldots, \zeta^{a_m} M$. Their product $N_{K/k}(M) = \zeta^{a_1 + \cdots + a_m} M^m$ lies in $k$, so $M^m \in k$. We also have $M^l = \mu \in k$ by definition.

If $m < l$, then $\gcd(m, l) = 1$ (since $l$ is prime), so there exist integers $r, s$ with $rm + sl = 1$. Then

$$M = M^{rm + sl} = (M^m)^r \cdot (M^l)^s \in k,$$

contradicting the hypothesis that $\mu = M^l$ is not an $l$th power in $k$. Hence $m = l$, the relative degree is $l$, and all $l$ conjugates $\zeta^a M$ are distinct.

Since all roots of $x^l - \mu$ lie in $K$, the extension is normal and separable (characteristic zero), hence Galois. The automorphism $s$ defined by $s(M) = \zeta M$ generates a cyclic group of order $l$, which is the full Galois group. Thus $K/k$ is a relatively cyclic extension of degree $l$. $\square$
