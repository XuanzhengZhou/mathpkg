# Proof of Decomposition of Rational Primes in Galois Fields

In a Galois number field $K(\zeta)$ generated by the $m$th roots of unity, the decomposition of a rational prime $p$ is determined by the residue class of $p$ modulo $m$.

If $p \nmid D \cdot m$, then by Theorem 91, $p$ is squarefree (not divisible by the square of any prime ideal). By Theorem 92, if $f$ is the smallest positive integer such that $p^f \equiv 1 \pmod{m}$, then $p$ splits into exactly $h/f$ distinct prime ideals of degree $f$, where $h$ is the degree of the field.

Thus primes in the same residue class modulo $m$ exhibit identical decomposition behavior—with only finitely many exceptional primes (those dividing the discriminant $D$ or $m$).
