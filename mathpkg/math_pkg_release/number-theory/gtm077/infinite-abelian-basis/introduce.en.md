For infinite Abelian groups with a finite generating set (finitely generated), the concept of a basis extends naturally: elements of infinite order contribute free cyclic factors $\mathbb{Z}$, while elements of finite order contribute finite cyclic factors. Finitely generated Abelian groups are thus direct products of finitely many copies of $\mathbb{Z}$ and finite cyclic groups, generalizing the fundamental theorem to the infinite case.
