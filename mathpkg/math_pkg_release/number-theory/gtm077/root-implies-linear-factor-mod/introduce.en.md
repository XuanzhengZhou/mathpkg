This is Theorem 10 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke. If $a$ is a root of the integral polynomial $f(x)$ mod $n$, then $f(x)$ is divisible by $x - a$ mod $n$ and conversely.  Since $f(a) \equiv 0 \pmod{n}$ we have  $$f(x) \equiv f(x) - f(a) \pmod{n}.$$... This result appears in the section §3 of the book and is part of the foundational development of algebraic number theory.
