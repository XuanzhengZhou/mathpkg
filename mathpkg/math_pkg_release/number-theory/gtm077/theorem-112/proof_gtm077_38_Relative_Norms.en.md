# Proof of Theorem 112

**Statement.** The relative different $\mathfrak{D}_k$ of $K$ with respect to $k$ is the greatest common divisor (GCD) of all relative differents of integers of $K$ with respect to $k$.

*Proof.* The proof proceeds almost exactly as in the proof of Theorem 105 (the corresponding statement for the absolute different). Let $\theta$ be an integer generating the field $K$ over $k$, and let $\Phi(x)$ be the irreducible polynomial over $k$ with leading coefficient $1$ having $\theta$ as a root.

The relative ring $R_k(\theta)$ is the set of all numbers of the form

$$\alpha_0 + \alpha_1 \theta + \cdots + \alpha_{m-1} \theta^{m-1}$$

where $\alpha_0, \ldots, \alpha_{m-1}$ run through all integers in $k$.

By Lemma (a), if $A$ is an integer in $K$ such that $A \mathfrak{D}_k$ is integral, then $A = B/\Phi'(\theta)$ with $B \in R_k(\theta)$. Hence $\Phi'(\theta)$ is divisible by $\mathfrak{D}_k$.

By Lemma (c), for each prime ideal $\mathfrak{P}$ in $K$, there exists a relative ring $R_k(\theta)$ such that $\mathfrak{P} \nmid \mathfrak{F}$ where $\mathfrak{F} = \Phi'(\theta)/\mathfrak{D}_k$.

Now, the relative different of a single integer $A$ is defined as the ideal generated by $\Phi_A'(A)$, where $\Phi_A$ is the minimal polynomial of $A$. One needs to show that the GCD of all such relative differents equals $\mathfrak{D}_k$. The proof uses:

1. Lemmas (a)-(c) to relate the local behavior at each prime ideal to the relative ring $R_k(\theta)$.
2. By Theorem 113, the GCD of all ideals in $K$ containing only numbers in $R_k(\theta)$ is $\mathfrak{F}$, and $\mathfrak{F} \mathfrak{D}_k = \Phi'(\theta)$.
3. Running through all possible choices of $\theta$, the GCD of the corresponding $\Phi'(\theta)$ divided by the corresponding $\mathfrak{F}$ yields exactly $\mathfrak{D}_k$.

The local argument at each prime $\mathfrak{P}$ (using Lemma (c)) shows that the exponent of $\mathfrak{P}$ in the GCD of relative differents matches the exponent in $\mathfrak{D}_k$, completing the proof.
