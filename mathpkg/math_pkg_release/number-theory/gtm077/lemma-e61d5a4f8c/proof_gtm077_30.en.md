# Proof of The group $\mathfrak{G}$ of all units has a finite basis, and the number $k$ of basis elements of in

For the proof let $\varepsilon_1, \ldots, \varepsilon_k$ be $k$ units of infinite order and suppose there do not exist $k + 1$ independent elements of infinite order. Then, by Lemma (b) and (c) [of §34], for each unit $\eta$ in $K$ a system of equations

$$\log|\eta^{(i)}| = \sum_{m=1}^{k} \frac{g_m}{M} \log|\varepsilon_m^{(i)}| \quad (i=1, 2, \ldots, n)$$

holds, for a certain positive rational integer $M$, where the $g_m$ are rational integers. From this it follows by Lemma (a) [of §34] that

$$\eta^M = \varepsilon_1^{g_1} \varepsilon_2^{g_2} \cdots \varepsilon_k^{g_k} \zeta,$$

where $\zeta$ is a root of unity in $K$, that is, a $w$th root of unity. Hence

$$\eta = \varepsilon_1^{g_1/M} \varepsilon_2^{g_2/M} \cdots \varepsilon_k^{g_k/M} \zeta_0^x,$$

where $\zeta_0 = e^{2\pi i/Mw}$, with rational integral $x$.

Considering the totality of products of powers of the $k+1$ numbers $H_1 = \varepsilon_1^{1/M}, \ldots, H_k = \varepsilon_k^{1/M}, H_{k+1} = \zeta_0$ (with arbitrary but fixed values of the roots), these form a (mixed) Abelian group for which $H_1, \ldots, H_{k+1}$ is a basis. The group $\mathfrak{G}$ of all units of $K$ is contained as a subgroup of finite index, since the $M$th power of each unit lies in the group generated by $\varepsilon_1, \ldots, \varepsilon_k$ and $\zeta$.

It follows that the group $\mathfrak{G}$ has a finite basis, and the number $k$ of basis elements of infinite order is $\leq r$.
