This is Theorem 119 from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

Let $l$ be a prime factor of $1 - \zeta$, which divides $1 - \zeta$ exactly to the $a$th power: $1 - \zeta = l^a l_1$. Suppose that $l$ does not divide $\mu$. Then $l$ splits into $l$ factors which are distinct from one another in $K(\sqrt{l}\mu; k)$ if the congruence

$$\mu = \xi^l (\bmod l^{a+1})$

This result is part of the foundational material in algebraic number theory, appearing in the section on Decomposition Laws in the Relative Fields Ksqrtlmu.
