Every polynomial $S(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n)$ with coefficients in a field $k$ that is symmetric in the variables $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n$ can be expressed as a polynomial in the elementary symmetric functions
$$f_1 = \sum_i \alpha_i, \quad f_2 = \sum_{i<j} \alpha_i \alpha_j, \quad \ldots, \quad f_n = \alpha_1 \alpha_2 \cdots \alpha_n$$
with coefficients in $k$, computed from those of $S$ by addition, subtraction, and multiplication.
