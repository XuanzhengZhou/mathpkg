# Proof of Theorem 68

The proof is word for word the same as for Theorem 68 (the cancellation law for integral ideals, established in §27).

For integral ideals $\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{c}$ with $\mathfrak{a} \neq (0)$, if $\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b} = \mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{c}$, then $\mathfrak{b} = \mathfrak{c}$.

The extension to fractional ideals follows from the integral case by multiplying through by a suitable integer $v$ that makes all ideals integral: if $\mathfrak{g}\mathfrak{r} = \mathfrak{g}\mathfrak{\eta}$ and $\mathfrak{g} \neq (0)$, choose $v$ such that $v\mathfrak{g}, v\mathfrak{r}, v\mathfrak{\eta}$ are all integral. Then $(v\mathfrak{g})(v\mathfrak{r}) = (v\mathfrak{g})(v\mathfrak{\eta})$ implies $v\mathfrak{r} = v\mathfrak{\eta}$, hence $\mathfrak{r} = \mathfrak{\eta}$.
