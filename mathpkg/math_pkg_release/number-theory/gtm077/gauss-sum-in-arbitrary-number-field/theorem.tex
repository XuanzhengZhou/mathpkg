For an integer $\omega$ of $k$ and a denominator ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ (odd and coprime to $\omega$), the Gauss sum is defined as
$$C(\omega) = \sum_{x \bmod \mathfrak{a}} \left(\frac{x}{\mathfrak{a}}\right) e^{2\pi i S(x\omega)},$$
where $S$ denotes the trace from $k$ to $\mathbb{Q}$ and $x$ runs through a complete residue system modulo $\mathfrak{a}$. If $\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{q}^2$ is the square of an ideal, $C(\omega)$ is essentially a product of local Gauss sums.
