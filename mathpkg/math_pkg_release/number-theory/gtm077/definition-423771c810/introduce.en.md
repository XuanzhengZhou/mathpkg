This is Definition  from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke. An algebraic number $\alpha$ of degree $n$ is called an algebraic integer if in the irreducible equation for $\alpha$ in $k(1)$ with leading coefficient 1, all coefficients are rational integers.
