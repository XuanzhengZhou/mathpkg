When $\gcd(a,n) = d > 1$, the linear congruence $ax + b \equiv 0 \pmod{n}$ requires the compatibility condition $d \mid b$ for solvability, and then has exactly $d$ distinct solutions modulo $n$. The proof reduces to the coprime case by dividing through by $d$, and the $d$ solutions are spaced $n/d$ apart. This is the complete companion to Theorem 14.
