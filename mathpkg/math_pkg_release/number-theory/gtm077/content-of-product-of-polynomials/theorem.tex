Furthermore, by Equation (157) the discriminant is

$$D = \frac{(\alpha_1 \alpha'_2 - \alpha_2 \alpha'_1)^2}{N(\alpha)^2} = e.$$

If a form $F$ is derived from the ideal $\alpha$ in this way, then we say: $F$ belongs to $\alpha$ and we write $F \rightarrow \alpha$.

With $e < 0$ we obviously obtain only positive definite forms, for the first coefficient is

$$A = \frac{\alpha_1 \alpha'_1}{|N(\alpha)|} = \frac{|N(\alpha_1)|}{|N(\alpha)|} > 0.$$
