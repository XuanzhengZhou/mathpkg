Theorem 34 is a crucial structural result: the property of having a finite basis is inherited by subgroups. This means that subgroups of finitely generated Abelian groups are themselves finitely generated — the class of finitely generated Abelian groups is Noetherian. This theorem is fundamental for the study of unit groups and ideal class groups in algebraic number theory.
