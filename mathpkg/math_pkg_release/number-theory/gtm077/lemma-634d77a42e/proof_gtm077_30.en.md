# Proof of If $r = 0$, the group $\mathfrak{E}$ is identical to the group $\mathfrak{W}$ of the roots of unity 

For in the imaginary quadratic field it follows immediately from $N(\varepsilon) = \pm 1$ that $\varepsilon^{(1)} \cdot \varepsilon^{(2)} = +1$, and since $|\varepsilon^{(1)}| = |\varepsilon^{(2)}|$, this unit, along with its conjugate has absolute value 1; hence this unit is a root of unity.

More generally, if $r = 0$, the field has no real embeddings (or the unit rank is zero). Every unit $\varepsilon$ satisfies $|\varepsilon^{(i)}| = 1$ for all conjugates, and thus by the argument of Theorem 99, $\varepsilon$ is a root of unity. Hence $\mathfrak{E} = \mathfrak{W}$.
