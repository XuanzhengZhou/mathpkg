We deal with the second part of the theorem in the same manner. For even $d$ exactly the following residue classes mod 8 appear as norm residues mod 8:

$$N(\alpha) \equiv 1 \text{ or } 5 \pmod{8}, \quad \text{if} \quad \frac{d}{4} \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$$

$$N(\alpha) \equiv 1 \text{ or } -1 \pmod{8}, \quad \text{if} \quad \frac{d}{4} \equiv 2 \pmod{8}$$

$$N(\alpha) \equiv 1 \text{ or } 3 \pmod{8}, \quad \text{if} \quad \frac{d}{4} \equiv 6 \pmod{8}.$$

Note that for $d/4 \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$ the only norm residues mod 4 lie in the residue class of 1 mod 4, hence also that $\Re(4)$ is different from $\Re(4)$.
