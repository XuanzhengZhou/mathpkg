A coset of a subgroup $\mathfrak{U}$ is the set $A\mathfrak{U}$ obtained by multiplying a fixed group element $A$ by all elements of $\mathfrak{U}$. Distinct cosets are disjoint and have the same cardinality as $\mathfrak{U}$. Cosets partition the group into equal-sized pieces and generalize the notion of residue classes in $\mathbb{Z}$. This partition is the foundation of Lagrange's theorem and the construction of factor groups.
