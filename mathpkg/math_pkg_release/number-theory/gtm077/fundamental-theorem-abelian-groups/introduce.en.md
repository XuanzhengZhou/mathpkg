The Fundamental Theorem of Finite Abelian Groups states that every such group is isomorphic to a direct product of cyclic groups of prime-power order. The orders of these cyclic factors (the invariant factors) uniquely determine the group up to isomorphism. Hecke's proof first reduces to groups of prime-power order via the coprime-order decomposition, then constructs a basis by taking a maximal independent system and showing it generates the whole group via three key lemmas about $p$th powers.
