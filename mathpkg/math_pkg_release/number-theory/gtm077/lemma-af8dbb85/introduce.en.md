This is Lemma None from Lectures on the Theory of Algebraic Numbers by Erich Hecke.

If the complex variables $t_1, \ldots, t_n$ simultaneously tend to zero in such a way that the real parts of $1/t_p$ tend to plus infinity, then

$$\lim_{t \to 0} \sqrt{t_1 \cdots t_n} \theta(t, z; \mathfrak{a}) = \frac{1}{N(\mathfrak{a}) \sqrt{d}},$$

independently of $z$.
