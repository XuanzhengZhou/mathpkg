# Proof: Group of Residue Classes modulo $n$ under Addition

Let $\mathfrak{G} = \mathbb{Z}$ be the additive group of integers. For a fixed positive integer $n$, let $\mathfrak{U}_n = \{kn \mid k \in \mathbb{Z}\}$ be the subgroup of multiples of $n$.

**Step 1: $\mathfrak{U}_n$ is a subgroup.** Clearly $0 \in \mathfrak{U}_n$. If $a = kn$ and $b = \ell n$ are in $\mathfrak{U}_n$, then $a + b = (k+\ell)n \in \mathfrak{U}_n$ and $-a = (-k)n \in \mathfrak{U}_n$. Hence $\mathfrak{U}_n$ is a subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$.

**Step 2: Index of $\mathfrak{U}_n$.** The index of $\mathfrak{U}_n$ in $\mathfrak{G}$ is the number of distinct integers that are incongruent modulo $n$, i.e., the number of integers whose differences are not multiples of $n$. Two integers $a, b$ lie in the same coset of $\mathfrak{U}_n$ if and only if $a - b \in \mathfrak{U}_n$, which means $a - b = kn$ for some $k$, i.e., $a \equiv b \pmod{n}$. Hence the cosets of $\mathfrak{U}_n$ are precisely the residue classes modulo $n$. There are exactly $n$ such cosets, corresponding to the residues $0, 1, 2, \ldots, n-1$. Therefore $[\mathfrak{G} : \mathfrak{U}_n] = n$.

**Step 3: Factor group structure.** The cosets (residue classes) form a group under the operation induced by addition:
$$(a + \mathfrak{U}_n) + (b + \mathfrak{U}_n) = (a + b) + \mathfrak{U}_n.$$
This is well-defined: if $a' \in a + \mathfrak{U}_n$ and $b' \in b + \mathfrak{U}_n$, then $a' = a + k_1n$, $b' = b + k_2n$, so $a' + b' = a + b + (k_1 + k_2)n \in (a+b) + \mathfrak{U}_n$.

**Step 4: Group properties.** The factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{U}_n$ inherits the group axioms from $\mathfrak{G}$:
- Associativity: inherited from integer addition.
- Identity: the coset $\mathfrak{U}_n$ itself (the residue class $0 \bmod n$).
- Inverse: the coset of $-a$ is the inverse of the coset of $a$.
- Commutativity: inherited from the commutativity of $\mathfrak{G}$.

Thus the $n$ residue classes modulo $n$, with composition by addition, form an Abelian group isomorphic to the factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{U}_n$. This group is cyclic of order $n$, generated by the residue class of $1$ (the coset $1 + \mathfrak{U}_n$), since adding this class to itself $n$ times yields the identity. $\square$
