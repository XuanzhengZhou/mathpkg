# Proof of Theorem 88: Norm as Product of Conjugate Ideals

Let $\mathfrak{a} = (\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_r)$ be an ideal of the number field $K$ of degree $n$. Form the polynomial

$$P(x) = \alpha_1 x + \alpha_2 x^2 + \cdots + \alpha_r x^r$$

from a new variable $x$, where $\mathfrak{a}$ is the GCD of the coefficients $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_r$.

The product of the $n$ conjugate polynomials

$$f(x) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} (\alpha_1^{(i)} x + \cdots + \alpha_r^{(i)} x^r)$$

is a polynomial with rational integral coefficients. Let $a$ be the GCD of these coefficients as rational integers. Since $1$ can be expressed as a linear combination of the coefficients of $(1/a)f(x)$, the ideal $(a)$ is also the GCD of the coefficients when regarded as an ideal in $K$. Applying Theorem 87 yields

$$\mathfrak{a}^{(1)} \mathfrak{a}^{(2)} \cdots \mathfrak{a}^{(n)} = (a).$$

All conjugate ideals have equal norm, so

$$N(\mathfrak{a}^{(1)}) N(\mathfrak{a}^{(2)}) \cdots N(\mathfrak{a}^{(n)}) = N(\mathfrak{a}^{(i)})^n = N((a)) = |a|^n.$$

Hence $N(\mathfrak{a}^{(i)}) = \pm a$, and therefore

$$(N(\mathfrak{a}^{(i)})) = (a) = \mathfrak{a}^{(1)} \mathfrak{a}^{(2)} \cdots \mathfrak{a}^{(n)}.$$

This proves that the product of all $n$ conjugate ideals equals the principal ideal generated by the norm:

$$\mathfrak{a}^{(1)} \mathfrak{a}^{(2)} \cdots \mathfrak{a}^{(n)} = (N(\mathfrak{a})).$$

In particular, for a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of degree $f$ divisible by the rational prime $p$,

$$\mathfrak{p}^{(1)} \mathfrak{p}^{(2)} \cdots \mathfrak{p}^{(n)} = (p^f),$$

where $n = f \cdot r$ and $r$ is the number of distinct conjugate prime ideals dividing $p$.
