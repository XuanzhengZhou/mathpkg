Gauss's Lemma is a fundamental result about integral polynomials: the product of two primitive polynomials (those with coprime coefficients) remains primitive. Equivalently, if a prime does not divide all coefficients of $f$ or of $g$, then it cannot divide all coefficients of $fg$. The proof uses the fact that for prime moduli, the leading coefficient of a product is the product of leading coefficients, and $\mathbb{F}_p[x]$ is an integral domain. This lemma is the key step in proving that $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ is a UFD.
