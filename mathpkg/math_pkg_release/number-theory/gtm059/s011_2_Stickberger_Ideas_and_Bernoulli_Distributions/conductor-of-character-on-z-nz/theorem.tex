Let $\chi$ be a character on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$. The \textbf{conductor} of $\chi$ is the smallest positive divisor $d$ of $n$ such that $\chi$ factors through the natural surjection $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}/d\mathbb{Z}$. If $d = n$, then $\chi$ is primitive. In the prime power case $n = p^v$, the conductor has the form $p^g$ where $g$ is the smallest integer such that $\chi$ is trivial on $1 + p^g \mathbb{Z}/p^v\mathbb{Z}$.
