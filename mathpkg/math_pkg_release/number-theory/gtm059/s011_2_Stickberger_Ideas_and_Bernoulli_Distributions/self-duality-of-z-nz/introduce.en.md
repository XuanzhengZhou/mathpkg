The additive group $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ admits a natural self-duality via the pairing $(x, y) \mapsto \zeta^{xy}$, where $\zeta$ is a fixed primitive $n$-th root of unity. This pairing identifies each element $x \in \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ with the character $y \mapsto \zeta^{xy}$. The self-duality is fundamental for the theory of Gauss sums and character sums over finite cyclic groups, and serves as the basis for Fourier analysis on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$.
