For any function $f$ on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$, its \textbf{Fourier transform} $\widehat{f}$ is the function on the dual group $\widehat{\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}}$ defined by
$$\widehat{f}(\chi) = \sum_{x \in \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}} f(x) \, \overline{\chi(x)}$$
where $\chi$ ranges over the characters of $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$. Equivalently, using the self-duality pairing $(x, y) \mapsto \zeta^{xy}$ with a primitive $n$-th root of unity $\zeta$:
$$\widehat{f}(y) = \sum_{x \in \mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}} f(x) \, \zeta^{-xy}.$$
