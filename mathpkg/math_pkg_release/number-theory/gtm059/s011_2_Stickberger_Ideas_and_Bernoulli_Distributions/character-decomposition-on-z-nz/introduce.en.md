The Chinese remainder theorem gives a product decomposition $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z} \cong \prod_i \mathbb{Z}/p_i^{e_i}\mathbb{Z}$ for the prime power factorization of $n$. This induces a corresponding decomposition of characters: any character $\chi$ on $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ can be uniquely expressed as a product $\chi = \prod_i \chi_i$ where each $\chi_i$ is a character on the $\mathbb{Z}/p_i^{e_i}\mathbb{Z}$ component. This decomposition is essential for reducing questions about Gauss sums and conductors to the prime power case.
