A character $\chi$ on the finite cyclic group $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ is called primitive if it is not induced from a character modulo any proper divisor of $n$. This means $\chi$ does not factor through any quotient $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}/d\mathbb{Z}$ with $d \mid n$, $d < n$. In the prime power case $n = p^v$, primitivity is equivalent to $\chi$ being non-trivial on $1 + p^{v-1}\mathbb{Z}/p^v\mathbb{Z}$. Primitive characters are the fundamental building blocks for Gauss sums, as imprimitive characters lead to vanishing Gauss sums in the mixed case.
