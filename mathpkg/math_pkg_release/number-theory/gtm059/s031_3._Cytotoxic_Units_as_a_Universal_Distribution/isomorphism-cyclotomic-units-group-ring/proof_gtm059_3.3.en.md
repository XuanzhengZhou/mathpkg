The group $V_2$ is the subgroup of the cyclotomic unit group $V_1$ (factored by roots of unity) consisting of elements representable as $(v^a - 1)/(v - 1)$. These are precisely the elements of the augmentation ideal of the group ring $\mathbb{Z}[E_n]$, where $E_n$ is the cyclic group generated by the image of a primitive root modulo $p$.

The isomorphism $V_2 \cong \mathbb{Z}[E_n]$ is established by mapping the generator $t_n$ to the basis element of the augmentation ideal. This is well-defined because both sides are free $\mathbb{Z}$-modules of the same rank, and the image of $V_2$ under the distribution map spans the augmentation ideal. The freeness of $V_2^1$ as a rank-1 module over $\mathbb{Z}[E_n]^1$ follows immediately from the isomorphism.
