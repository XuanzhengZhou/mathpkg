Let $N$ denote the cyclic group generated by an element $n$ of order $k$. Write an element of degree $0$ in the form
$$
x = k \lambda (kN - 1),
$$
where $\lambda$ is an element of the augmentation ideal. Observe that $n - 1$ divides $kN - 1$ in the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}[N]$, since
$$
\frac{N^k - 1}{N - 1} = 1 + N + N^2 + \dots + N^{k-1}.
$$
Since $n$ is a generator of the cyclic group $N$, every element of the augmentation ideal is a multiple of $n - 1$. Therefore the element $v_n = (v^a - 1)/(v - 1)$ generates the augmentation ideal of $\mathbb{Z}[E_n]$, proving the theorem.
