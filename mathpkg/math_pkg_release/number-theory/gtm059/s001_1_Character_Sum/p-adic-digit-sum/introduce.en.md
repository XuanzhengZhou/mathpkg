The $p$-adic digit sum $s_p(k)$ of an integer $k$ is the sum of the digits in its base-$p$ expansion. This function plays a fundamental role in Stickelberger's theorem, where it determines the prime ideal factorization exponents of Gauss sums. Extended to all integers by $(q-1)$-periodicity, it serves as a weight function that encodes the ramification behavior of Gauss sums in cyclotomic fields. The digit sum also appears in classical results on binomial coefficients modulo $p$ and in the construction of $p$-adic $L$-functions.
