Under the hypotheses of Theorem 4.1, the group $G = \operatorname{Gal}(M_\infty / K)$ fits into a split exact sequence

$$1 \to G_1 \to G \to \mathbb{Z}_p \to 1$$

where $G_1 = \operatorname{Gal}(M_\infty / K_1)$ is the commutator subgroup $[G, G]$, and the splitting is given by the inertia group $I \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$.

Furthermore, for each $n \geq 1$, writing $G_n = \operatorname{Gal}(M_\infty / K_n)$, we have the descending series

$$G \supset G_1 \supset G_2 \supset \cdots \supset \bigcap G_n = \{1\}$$

with $G/G_n \cong \operatorname{Gal}(M_\infty/K)$ modulo the subgroup fixing $K_n$, and $G_n = G^{p^n} \cdot [G, G]$.
