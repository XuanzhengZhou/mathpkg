Let $K$ be a number field and fix a prime $p$. Define:

$$M(K) = \text{the maximal } p\text{-abelian unramified extension of } K,$$

$$G_M(K) = \operatorname{Gal}(M(K) / K).$$

By class field theory, $G_M(K)$ is isomorphic to the $p$-part of the idele class group quotient:

$$G_M(K) \cong \left( J_K / \overline{K^* \prod_{v \mid \infty} K_v^* \prod_{v \nmid \infty} U_v} \right)(p),$$

where $J_K$ is the idele group of $K$, $U_v$ are local units, and the bar denotes closure in the idele topology. Equivalently, $M(K)$ is the maximal abelian extension of $K$ of $p$-power degree that is unramified at all primes.
