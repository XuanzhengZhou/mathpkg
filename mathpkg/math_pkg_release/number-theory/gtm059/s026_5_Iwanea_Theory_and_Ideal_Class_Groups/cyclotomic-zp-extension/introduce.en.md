The cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension is the prototypical example of a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. It is obtained by adjoining all $p$-power roots of unity to $\mathbb{Q}$. The Galois group of $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty})/\mathbb{Q}$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$, whose torsion subgroup gives a finite cyclic extension; the fixed field of this torsion subgroup yields the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. Every number field possesses at least one $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension, namely its cyclotomic one.
