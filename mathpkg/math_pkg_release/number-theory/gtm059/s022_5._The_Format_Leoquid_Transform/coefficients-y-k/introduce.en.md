The coefficients $y_k(n)$ arise from the exponential change of variables $X = e^z - 1$ used in defining the Leopoldt transform. They are the Taylor coefficients of $(e^X - 1)^k$ expanded in powers of $X^n / n!$ with respect to the differential operator $D_X = (1+X)\frac{d}{dX}$. These coefficients encode the combinatorial data linking the multiplicative and additive structures, and their arithmetic properties (such as divisibility by $n!$) are essential for establishing $p$-adic integrality results.
