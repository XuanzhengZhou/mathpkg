This theorem extends the Leopoldt transform to a continuous linear map $\Gamma_a$ on the Banach space $\mathcal{L}$, twisted by a Dirichlet character $\omega^a$ where $a$ is taken modulo $p-1$. The map is uniquely characterized by its values on the polynomials $\frac{X}{n}$ and extends continuously to the whole Leopold space. This result is the key step in constructing $p$-adic $L$-functions via interpolation, establishing that the Leopoldt transform provides the analytic machinery for $p$-adic continuity.
