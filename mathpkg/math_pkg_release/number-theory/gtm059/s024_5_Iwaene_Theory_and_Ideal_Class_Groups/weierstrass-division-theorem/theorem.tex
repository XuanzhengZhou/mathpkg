Let $A$ be a complete local ring with maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$. Let

$$f(X) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_k X^k \in A[[X]]$$

be a power series such that not all coefficients $a_k$ lie in $\mathfrak{m}$. Let $n$ be the smallest integer such that $a_n$ is a unit in $A$ (i.e., $a_0, \dots, a_{n-1} \in \mathfrak{m}$ and $a_n \in A^\times$). Then for any power series $g(X) \in A[[X]]$, there exist unique elements

$$q(X) \in A[[X]], \quad r(X) \in A[X]$$

with $\deg r < n$ such that

$$g(X) = q(X) f(X) + r(X).$$

Moreover, if $g$ and $f$ lie in a subring $B[[X]]$ where $B$ is a subring of $A$ containing the coefficients of $f$ and $g$, and if $a_n$ is a unit in $B$, then $q$ and $r$ also lie in $B[[X]]$ and $B[X]$ respectively.
