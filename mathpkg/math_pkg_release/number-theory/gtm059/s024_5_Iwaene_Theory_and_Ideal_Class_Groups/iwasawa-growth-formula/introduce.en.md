Iwasawa's celebrated growth formula describes the asymptotic behavior of the $p$-part of the ideal class group in the layers of a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. The invariant $\mu$ (the "$\mu$-invariant") measures the growth of the $p$-primary part; in many important cases, including the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of an abelian number field, Ferrero and Washington proved that $\mu = 0$. The invariant $\lambda$ (the "$\lambda$-invariant") measures linear growth and is related to the degree of the characteristic polynomial. This formula is the foundation of Iwasawa theory.
