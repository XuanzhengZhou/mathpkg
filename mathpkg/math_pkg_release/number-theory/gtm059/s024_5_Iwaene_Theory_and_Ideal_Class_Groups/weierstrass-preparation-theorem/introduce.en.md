The Weierstrass Preparation Theorem states that a power series $f(X)$ over a complete local ring $A$ whose first unit coefficient occurs at degree $n$ can be uniquely factored as $f = P \cdot u$, where $P$ is a monic polynomial of degree $n$ with all lower coefficients in the maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ (a distinguished polynomial), and $u$ is a unit in $A[[X]]$. When $A = \mathbb{Z}_p$, this theorem is the fundamental algebraic tool for the structure theory of finitely generated modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$, allowing the decomposition of torsion modules into cyclic components determined by distinguished polynomials.
