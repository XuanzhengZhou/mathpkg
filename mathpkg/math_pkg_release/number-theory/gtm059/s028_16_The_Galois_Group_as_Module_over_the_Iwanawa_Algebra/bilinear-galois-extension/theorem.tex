Let $K_\infty$ be a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of a number field $K$ containing the $p$-th roots of unity. A Galois extension $L/K_\infty$ is called a \textit{bilinear extension} if its Galois group $\text{Gal}(L/K_\infty)$ admits a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-bilinear pairing
$$\text{Gal}(L/K_\infty) \times K_\infty^\times \to \mu_{p^\infty}$$
compatible with the Kummer pairing, making the Galois group a projective module of finite rank over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$.
