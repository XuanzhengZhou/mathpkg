Let $f$ be a function on $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$. The \textbf{generalized Bernoulli numbers} (of Leopoldt) are the constant terms of the generalized Bernoulli polynomials:
$$B_{k,f} = B_{k,f}(0).$$
Explicitly,
$$B_{k,f} = N^{k-1} \sum_{a=1}^{N} f(a) \, B_k\!\left(\frac{a}{N}\right).$$
When $f$ is a Dirichlet character $\chi$, these are the generalized Bernoulli numbers $B_{k,\chi}$ that appear in the formula $L(1-k, \chi) = -B_{k,\chi}/k$ for the special values of Dirichlet $L$-functions at negative integers. In terms of the Bernoulli distribution, one has the integral representation
$$B_{k,f} = \int_{\mathbb{Z}_N} f(x) \, x^{k-1} \, d\varphi$$
where $\varphi$ is the Bernoulli distribution.
