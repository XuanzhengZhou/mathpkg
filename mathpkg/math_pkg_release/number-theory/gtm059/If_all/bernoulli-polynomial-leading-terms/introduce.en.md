# Leading Terms of Bernoulli Polynomials

This proposition identifies the two highest-degree terms of the Bernoulli polynomials: $B_k(X) = X^k - \frac{k}{2}X^{k-1} + \cdots$. The monic leading term $X^k$ reflects the normalization, while the coefficient $-\frac{k}{2}$ of $X^{k-1}$ is related to $B_1 = -\frac{1}{2}$. The remaining lower terms have rational coefficients. This structural information is useful when analyzing the growth and arithmetic properties of Bernoulli polynomials in the context of distribution theory.
