# Bernoulli Distribution on the Cyclic Group

The distribution relation for Bernoulli polynomials implies that the assignment $x \mapsto M^{k-1} B_k(x)$ satisfies the compatibility condition required for a distribution on the projective system $\{\frac{1}{M}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z}\}$. This gives rise to the Bernoulli distribution of degree $k-1$, which is bounded and therefore defines an integration theory on the projective limit $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}$. Historically, this distribution arises from the partial zeta function $\zeta(s, x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (n+x)^{-s}$ and is the foundation for the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic $L$-function.
