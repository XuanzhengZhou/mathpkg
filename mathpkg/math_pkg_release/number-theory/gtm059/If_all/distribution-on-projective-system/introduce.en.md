# Distribution on a Projective System

A distribution is a compatible family of maps $\varphi_n$ on a projective system of finite sets. The compatibility condition requires that the value at a coarser level equals the sum of values over all elements mapping to it at a finer level. This structure generalizes measures and gives rise to an integration theory: each distribution defines a linear functional on locally constant (step) functions on the projective limit $X$. Important examples include the Bernoulli distributions constructed from Bernoulli polynomials, which play a central role in $p$-adic $L$-functions and Iwasawa theory.
