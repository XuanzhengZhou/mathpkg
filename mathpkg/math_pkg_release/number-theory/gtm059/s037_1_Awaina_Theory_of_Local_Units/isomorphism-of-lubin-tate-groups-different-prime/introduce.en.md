Theorem 3.1 shows that the Lubin-Tate construction is essentially independent of the choice of Frobenius power series and uniformizer up to a unit. If two uniformizers differ by a unit, the corresponding Lubin-Tate formal groups are canonically isomorphic, and this isomorphism respects the $\mathfrak{o}$-module structure. For the cyclotomic case over $\mathbb{Z}_p$, this means that choosing $f(X) = (1+X)^p - 1$ yields the formal multiplicative group, and any other choice yields an isomorphic formal group. Thus the local class field theory constructed via Lubin-Tate groups depends only on the field $K$, not on the auxiliary choices.
