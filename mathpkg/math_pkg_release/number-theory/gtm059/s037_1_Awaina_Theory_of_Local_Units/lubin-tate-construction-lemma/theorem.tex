Let $\mathfrak{o}$ be a commutative ring, and let $f, g \in \mathfrak{o}[[X]]$ be power series such that $f(X) \equiv g(X) \equiv \pi X \pmod{\deg 2}$ for some $\pi \in \mathfrak{o}$ with the property that multiplication by $\pi$ is injective on $\mathfrak{o}$. Let
$$
L(X_1, \dots, X_n) = a_1 X_1 + \cdots + a_n X_n
$$
be a linear form with coefficients $a_i \in \mathfrak{o}$. Then there exists a \textbf{unique} power series $F(X_1, \dots, X_n) \in \mathfrak{o}[[X_1, \dots, X_n]]$ such that
$$
F(X_1, \dots, X_n) \equiv L(X_1, \dots, X_n) \pmod{\deg 2}
$$
and
$$
f(F(X_1, \dots, X_n)) = F(g(X_1), \dots, g(X_n)).
$$
Moreover, completeness of $\mathfrak{o}$ is not required; the result holds in any extension ring of $\mathfrak{o}$.
