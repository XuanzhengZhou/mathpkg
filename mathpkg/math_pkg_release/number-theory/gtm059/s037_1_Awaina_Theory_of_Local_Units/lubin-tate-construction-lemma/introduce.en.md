This is the fundamental technical lemma of Lubin-Tate theory. It constructs, by induction on the degree, a unique power series in several variables that interpolates between a given linear form (to first order) and commutes with prescribed Frobenius-type power series. The lemma is applied in two crucial ways: first to construct the formal group law $F(X,Y)$ itself (taking $L(X,Y) = X+Y$ and $f = g$), and second to construct the endomorphism $[a]_F$ for each $a \in \mathfrak{o}$ (taking the linear form $aX$). The proof proceeds degree-by-degree, with the injectivity of $\pi$ ensuring uniqueness at each step.
