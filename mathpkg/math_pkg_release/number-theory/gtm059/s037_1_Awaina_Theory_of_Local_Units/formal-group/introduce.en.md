A formal group over a commutative ring is a formal power series in two variables that behaves like a group operation at the level of formal power series. The condition $F(X,Y) = X+Y \pmod{\deg 2}$ ensures that the identity is $0$ (to first order), while associativity and commutativity are the usual group axioms expressed in terms of the formal power series. Strictly speaking, these should be called commutative one-parameter formal groups. The theory of formal groups provides a powerful algebraic framework for constructing abelian extensions of local fields, as developed by Lubin and Tate.
