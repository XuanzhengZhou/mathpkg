For any formal group, there exists a unique formal inverse power series, which plays the role of the group-theoretic inverse. The condition $\iota(X) = -X \pmod{\deg 2}$ reflects that the inverse should agree with the additive inverse to first order. The identity $F(X, \iota(X)) = 0$ is the formal analog of the statement that an element composed with its inverse yields the identity. The existence and uniqueness of $\iota$ can be established by a recursive construction on the coefficients of the power series.
