Theorem 1.1 is the foundational result of Lubin-Tate theory. It establishes that every Frobenius power series determines a unique one-parameter formal group law for which the given series is an endomorphism. The proof is an immediate consequence of the Construction Lemma: apply it with $L(X,Y) = X+Y$ and $f = g$ to obtain $F(X,Y)$ satisfying $f(F(X,Y)) = F(f(X), f(Y))$ and $F(X,Y) \equiv X+Y \pmod{\deg 2}$. Commutativity and associativity are then verified using the uniqueness clause of the lemma.
