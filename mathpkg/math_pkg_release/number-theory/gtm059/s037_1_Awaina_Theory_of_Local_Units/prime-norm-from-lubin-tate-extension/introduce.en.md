Theorem 2.2 establishes that the uniformizer $\pi$ lies in the norm group from the Lubin-Tate extensions. This is a crucial fact for local class field theory: it shows that the Lubin-Tate construction yields the totally ramified part of the maximal abelian extension. Combined with the unramified extensions (obtained by extending the residue field), one obtains the complete local class field theory. In the cyclotomic case over $\mathbb{Q}_p$, this corresponds to the fact that $p$ is a norm from $\mathbb{Q}_p(\zeta_{p^n})$.
