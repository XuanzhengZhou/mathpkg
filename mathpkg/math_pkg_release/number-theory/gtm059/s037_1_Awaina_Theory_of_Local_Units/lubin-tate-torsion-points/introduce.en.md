The set $A_n$ consists of the $\pi^n$-torsion points of the Lubin-Tate formal group. These are the formal group analogs of the $p^n$-th roots of unity in the multiplicative group. Just as the $p^n$-th roots of unity are the kernel of the $p^n$-power map on the multiplicative group, the elements of $A_n$ are killed by $[\pi^n]_{F_f}$. Taking the union over all $n$ and forming the corresponding field extensions yields the Lubin-Tate tower, which provides an explicit construction of the totally ramified abelian extensions of $K$.
