Theorem 1.2 endows the Lubin-Tate formal group with an action of the full ring of integers $\mathfrak{o}$. Each element $a \in \mathfrak{o}$ gives rise to an endomorphism $[a]_f$, and this correspondence respects both addition (via the formal group law) and multiplication (via composition). In particular, $[\pi]_f = f$, so the Frobenius power series corresponds to the uniformizer. This rich endomorphism structure is what makes Lubin-Tate formal groups the correct formal analog of the multiplicative group: for $G_m$ over $\mathbb{Z}_p$, the endomorphism $[a]$ is given by $(1+X)^a - 1$.
