Let $A_n = \ker[\pi^n]_{F_f}$ be the $\pi^n$-torsion points of the Lubin-Tate formal group $F_f$. The \textbf{Tate module} of $F_f$ is defined as the projective limit
$$
T_\pi(A) = \varprojlim_n A_n,
$$
where the transition maps are given by multiplication by $\pi$: $[\pi]_{F_f}: A_{n+1} \to A_n$. The Tate module $T_\pi(A)$ is a free $\mathfrak{o}$-module of rank $1$, and the absolute Galois group $G_K = \operatorname{Gal}(\overline{K}/K)$ acts $\mathfrak{o}$-linearly on $T_\pi(A)$, yielding a continuous character
$$
\chi: G_K \to \operatorname{Aut}_\mathfrak{o}(T_\pi(A)) \cong \mathfrak{o}^*.
$$
