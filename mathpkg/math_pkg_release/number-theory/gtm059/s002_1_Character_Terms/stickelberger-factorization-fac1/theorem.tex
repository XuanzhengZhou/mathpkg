As a special case of Theorem 2.2, we obtain the factorization

$$S_{\chi(\chi)}(\mathfrak{n}) = \mathfrak{p}^{\mathfrak{n}-1}.$$

Therefore, if $b$ is an integer prime to $m$, and $\chi = \chi_{(b)}$, then

$$S_{\chi_{(b)}}(\mathfrak{n}) = \mathfrak{p}^{\mathfrak{n}-1} + \mathfrak{p}^{\mathfrak{n}-2}.$$
