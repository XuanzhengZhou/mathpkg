This is derived from Theorem 2.2 by applying the Stickelberger element of level $\mathfrak{n}$ to the prime ideal factorization. The factor $\mathfrak{p}^{\mathfrak{n}-2}$ appears from the action of the Frobenius element on the Teichmüller character, and the principal ideal property is verified using the group ring structure of $\mathbb{Z}[G]$.
