Take an element $b$ of the form $b = \frac{1}{2} \cdot p \cdot p$ where $p$ is a radical and $z = \omega(b)$ is a $1/18$th root of unity. This makes (i) clear from the definition of the Teichmüller character and its relation to the eigenspace. For (ii), when $\chi$ is non-trivial and distinct from the Teichmüller character, the condition $2\chi = 0$ forces $t_{\chi} = 1$, which is immediate from the definitions of the $\chi$-eigenspace and the action of the Galois group.
