A sum involving products of Dirichlet characters, generalizing Gauss sums. For two multiplicative characters $\chi, \psi$ modulo $m$ (with $\chi\psi$ nontrivial), the Jacobi sum $J(\chi,\psi) = \sum_a \chi(a)\psi(1-a)$ relates to Gauss sums via the factorization $J(\chi,\psi) = S(\chi)S(\psi)/S(\chi\psi)$. Jacobi sums appear in the study of Stickelberger relations, the factorization of Gauss sums, and have deep connections to the arithmetic of cyclotomic fields and Fermat's Last Theorem.
