A fundamental theorem in cyclotomic field theory, also known as Stickelberger's theorem. It states that every element of the Stickelberger ideal acts as the zero map on the ideal class group of the cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$. The proof uses the Gauss sum factorization from Theorem 2.2: for any element in the Stickelberger ideal, the corresponding product of Gauss sums yields a principal ideal, demonstrating that the ideal class is trivialized by the Stickelberger action.
