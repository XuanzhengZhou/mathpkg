An element in the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}[\operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)/\mathbb{Q})]$ constructed from fractional parts of rational numbers. For each integer $a$ coprime to $m$, one associates the fractional part $\langle a/m \rangle$ and the automorphism $\sigma_a$ sending $\zeta_m \mapsto \zeta_m^a$. The Stickelberger element is the sum $\sum \langle a/m \rangle \sigma_a^{-1}$. It plays a central role in Stickelberger's theorem, which states that the Stickelberger ideal (generated by group ring elements annihilated by the Stickelberger element) annihilates the ideal class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$.
