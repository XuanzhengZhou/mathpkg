A fundamental result in the theory of cyclotomic fields giving the prime ideal factorization of certain Gauss sums. For a prime $\mathfrak{P}$ lying above $p$ in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$ and a Teichmüller character $\mathfrak{a}$, the Gauss sum $S(\chi\chi^{*-1})$ factorizes as a power of $\mathfrak{P}$. The exponent involves the values $p(\chi^*)$ and $q(\chi^*)$ derived from the action of the Galois group on the Stickelberger element. This factorization is a key step toward Stickelberger's theorem on the annihilation of the ideal class group.
