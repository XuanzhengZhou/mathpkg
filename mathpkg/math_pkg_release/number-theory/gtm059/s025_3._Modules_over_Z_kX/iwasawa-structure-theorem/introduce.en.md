The Iwasawa structure theorem classifies all finitely generated modules over the Iwasawa algebra $\mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ up to pseudo-isomorphism. This is the foundational result of Iwasawa theory, linking the algebraic structure of $\Lambda$-modules to analytic invariants such as $p$-adic $L$-functions. Every such module is pseudo-isomorphic to a direct sum of a free part $\Lambda^r$ and elementary torsion modules $\Lambda/(f_i^{n_i})$ for distinguished irreducible polynomials $f_i$, together with $\Lambda/(p^{m_j})$ factors. The uniqueness of the decomposition allows one to define algebraic Iwasawa invariants $\mu$ and $\lambda$ that correspond to the number of $p$-power factors and the total degree of distinguished polynomials, respectively.
