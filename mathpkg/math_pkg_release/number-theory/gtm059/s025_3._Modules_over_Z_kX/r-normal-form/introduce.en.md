The $r$-normal form is a canonical diagonal representation for relation matrices over the Iwasawa algebra $A = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$. In this form, the first $r$ diagonal entries are distinguished polynomials $\lambda_i$ whose degrees equal the invariant $\deg^0(R)$. The remaining entries are all zero. Achieving this normal form through admissible operations is the key technical step in proving the Iwasawa structure theorem, as each diagonal distinguished polynomial corresponds to an elementary divisor $A/(\lambda_i)$ in the pseudo-isomorphism decomposition of the module.
