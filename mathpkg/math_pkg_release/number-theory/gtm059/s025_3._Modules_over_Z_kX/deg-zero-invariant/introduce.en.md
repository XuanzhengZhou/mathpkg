The invariant $\deg^0(R)$ is defined for relation matrices over the Iwasawa algebra $A = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ and plays a crucial role in the inductive proof of the structure theorem. It measures the minimum Weierstrass degree among entries of the matrix after all admissible transformations have been applied. If a matrix $W$ is obtained from $R$ by admissible operations leaving the first $k-1$ rows unchanged, then $\deg^0(R) \le \deg^0(W)$. The invariant provides a well-ordering that allows one to inductively construct the $r$-normal form of a relation matrix.
