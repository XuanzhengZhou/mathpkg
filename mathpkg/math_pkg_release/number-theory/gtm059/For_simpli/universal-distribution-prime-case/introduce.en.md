Theorem 9.1 establishes the structure of the universal distribution when the level $N$ is prime. In this case the universal distribution is a free abelian group of rank $N-2$, generated by the symbols $[a/N]$ for $1 \leq a \leq N-1$, subject only to the single relation that their sum vanishes. This clean structure is fundamental to the proof of Stickelberger's theorem and to understanding the relationship between distributions and cyclotomic units.
