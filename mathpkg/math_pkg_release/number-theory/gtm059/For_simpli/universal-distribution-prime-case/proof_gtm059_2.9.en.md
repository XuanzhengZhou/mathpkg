\textbf{Proof.} The first statement is obvious from the preceding remarks: the elements $[0]$ and $[a/N]$ for all $a$ generate $U(N)$ by definition, and the element $[0]$ is expressed in terms of the others via the distribution relation. Specifically, for $N$ prime, applying the distribution relation with $d = N$ gives
$$\sum_{a=0}^{N-1} [a/N] = [0].$$
Since $[0]$ is not primitive, the primitive elements $[a/N]$ for $a = 1, \dots, N-1$ generate $U(N)$.

The cardinality of the set of primitive elements $T_N$ is $N-1$, which is clearly the rank of the universal distribution. For part (ii), we consider the abelian group generated by $T_N$ with the single relation $\sum_{a=1}^{N-1} [a/N] = 0$ (which follows from the distribution relation and $[0] = 0$). To show there are no further relations, one exhibits a distribution of rank $N-2$ taking linearly independent values on the generators; the cyclotomic unit distribution (or equivalently, the Bernoulli distribution) provides such a lower bound. Since the universal distribution has at most rank $N-2$ (by the single relation), and the exhibited distribution has exactly rank $N-2$, the generators must be free subject only to the single relation.

For part (iii), the natural distribution arising from Stickelberger elements provides an explicit realization of $U(N)$ with the required rank, establishing the universality property. $\square$
