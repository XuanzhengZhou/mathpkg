Let $Z_n = \frac{1}{N} \mathbb{Z}(N)$ where $\mathbb{Z}(N)$ denotes the group of elements in $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ of order dividing $N$. The \textbf{universal distribution} is the abelian group $U(N)$ generated by symbols $[x]$ for $x \in \frac{1}{N}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z} \setminus \{0\}$, modulo the distribution relations:
$$\sum_{dy = x} [y] = [x]$$
for all positive integers $d$. The universal distribution is the free abelian group on the set of primitive elements in $Z_n$, i.e., elements having exact period $N$, subject to the single linear relation
$$\sum_{x \in \frac{1}{N}\mathbb{Z}/\mathbb{Z}} [x] = 0.$$
The natural map $[x] \mapsto g(x)$ gives a universal homomorphism from $U(N)$ to any distribution taking values in an abelian group.
