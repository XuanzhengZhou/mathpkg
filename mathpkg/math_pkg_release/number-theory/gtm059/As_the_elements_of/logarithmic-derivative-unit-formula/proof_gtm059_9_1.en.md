Write $u = g_i(u)$ as a unit. Then

$$
\operatorname{em} = g_i(u) g_i(u_1) u_2.
$$

Thus we set $g_i(X) = g_i(u)[X]$, so that $\operatorname{em} = g_i(u)$.

On one hand, we have

$$
u^* \lambda_i(u) = \frac{1}{u^2} \lambda_i(u)^\alpha = \frac{1}{u^2} \frac{g_i(u)[u_1] u_2}{\operatorname{em}}.
$$

On the other hand, since $\lambda_i(u)[u] = u(u)(X)$, we find

$$
\lambda_i(u)[u_1] u_2(\lambda_i(u)) = u(\lambda_i(u))(X).
$$

Furthermore

$$
u^* \lambda_i(u) = \frac{1}{u^2} \frac{g_i(u)}{\operatorname{em}}
$$

and

$$
g_i(X) = g_i(u)[X] X(u)(X).
$$

Putting these relations together yields the desired property.
