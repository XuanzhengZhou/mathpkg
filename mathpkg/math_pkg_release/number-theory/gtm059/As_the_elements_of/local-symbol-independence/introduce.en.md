Lemma 3.2 establishes that the value of the local symbol $[x, x_n]$ is independent of the index $m$ when $x_n$ is the $n$-th component of a norm-coherent (infinite) vector $x = (x_0, \dots, x_{n-1})$ and $m$ is sufficiently large. This allows one to define $[x]$ as the common value. Two cases are distinguished depending on the size of $m$ relative to $n$ and the structure of the different ideal. The proof uses the trace operator $T_k^*$ and factorization via the Weierstrass preparation theorem.
