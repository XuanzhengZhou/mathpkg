DI 1 establishes the fundamental properties of the logarithmic derivative map $D_n$ in the context of Lubin-Tate theory. For an element $x = g(\pi_n)$ expressed as a power series in the Lubin-Tate generator, the derivative $g'(\pi_n)$ is well-defined modulo the different $\mathfrak{D}_n$. This map is a homomorphism from the multiplicative group $K_n^\times$ to a fractional ideal, and its image on units is controlled by the inverse different. This lemma is essential for making the bracket symbol $[x, \alpha]_n$ well-defined.
