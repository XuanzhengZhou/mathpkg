Let $x, u_n \in H^2$ and $k \in K^2$. For $m \geq 2n + 1$, the symbol $[x, \alpha]_n$ takes values in $\mathfrak{p}^{m+1}$ and we have

$$
(x, \alpha)_n = [x, \alpha]_n |u_n|
$$

where $(x, \alpha)_n$ denotes the classical norm residue symbol and $[x, \alpha]_n$ is the bracket symbol.

In the special case $K = \mathbb{Q}_p$ and $\pi = p$, with $\xi$ a primitive $p^2$-th root of unity, we have Iwasawa's formula:

$$
(x, \xi)_n = \xi^{-\frac{1}{p^n} T_n(\log(1+x) + D_n \log x)}
$$

where $D_n$ is the logarithmic derivative operator.
