The proof relies on the formalism of the Lubin-Tate group and the logarithmic derivative developed in Section 2. Under Condition (0), the bracket symbol $[x, a]_n$ is well-defined in $\mathfrak{p}^{n+1}$. The equality

$$
\langle x, \alpha \rangle_n = [x, N_n \alpha]_{A_f(\pi)}
$$

is established by comparing the defining properties of both pairings. Both sides are bimultiplicative and satisfy the same normalization with respect to the Lubin-Tate group law. The key step uses the logarithm map to transfer the multiplicative norm residue symbol to the additive setting where the bracket symbol is defined. For $m \geq 2n+1$, the value is independent of the choice of $m$, giving the formula

$$
(x, \alpha)_n = [x, \alpha]_n.
$$
