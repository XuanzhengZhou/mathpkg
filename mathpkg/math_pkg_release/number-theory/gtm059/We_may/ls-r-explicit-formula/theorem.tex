Let $a \in \operatorname{Gal}(K, \mathbb{R})$ and let $a \mapsto a(\alpha)$ be the representation $a^*$ on $T_{\alpha}(R)$. Then

$$
\psi_a(\alpha) = \psi(a) \psi_a(0)^T.
$$

More explicitly,

$$
\varepsilon(\sigma^{-1}a, \alpha) = (\lambda, \alpha) = \left( \sum_i \lambda_i d_i(\phi_a)(\alpha) \right)_{a_i}
$$

and

$$
\varepsilon(\sigma^{-1}a, \alpha) = \left( \sum_i \lambda_i d_i(\phi_a)(\alpha) \right)_{a_i}.
$$

Using $ex_a = \varepsilon(a)(x)$ and the relations $[ab] = [a][b]$ and $T_{\alpha}(R) = T_{\alpha}(R)$, the formula follows.
