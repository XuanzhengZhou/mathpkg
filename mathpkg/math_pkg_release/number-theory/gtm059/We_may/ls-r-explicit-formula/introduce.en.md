LS R provides an explicit formula for computing the local symbol in terms of representation-theoretic data. When a Galois element $a$ is represented on the Tate module $T_{\alpha}(R)$, the value $\psi_a(\alpha)$ can be expressed as a product involving $\psi(a)$ and the transpose of $\psi_a(0)$. This formula is crucial for making the local symbol computationally accessible and is used in the proof of the main explicit reciprocity law.
