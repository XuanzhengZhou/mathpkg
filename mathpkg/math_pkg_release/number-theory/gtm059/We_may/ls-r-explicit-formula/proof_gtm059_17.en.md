The proof of LS R proceeds as follows. By definition of the representation $a^*$ on the Tate module $T_{\alpha}(R)$, we have the decomposition:

$$
\varepsilon(\sigma^{-1}a, \alpha) = (\lambda, \alpha) = \left( \sum_i \lambda_i d_i(\phi_a)(\alpha) \right)_{a_i}.
$$

Using the relation $ex_a = \varepsilon(a)(x)$ and the multiplicativity property $[ab] = [a][b]$, we obtain

$$
\psi_a(\alpha) = \psi(a) \psi_a(0)^T.
$$

The invariance $T_{\alpha}(R) = T_{\alpha}(R)$ under the action of the Galois group then concludes the proof. The formula expresses the compatibility of the local symbol with the representation-theoretic structure of the Lubin-Tate formal group.
