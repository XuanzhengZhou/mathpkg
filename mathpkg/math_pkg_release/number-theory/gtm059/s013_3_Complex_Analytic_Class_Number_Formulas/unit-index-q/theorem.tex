Let $K$ be an abelian extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ and let $K^+$ be its maximal real subfield. Let $E = E_K$ be the group of units in $K$, let $E^+ = E_{K^+}$ be the group of units in $K^+$, and let $W$ be the group of roots of unity in $K$. The **unit index** $Q = Q_K$ is defined as:

$$Q = Q_K = (E : W \cdot E^+)$$

the index of the subgroup generated by the roots of unity and the units of the real subfield inside the full unit group of $K$.

Following Hasse, this index is also called the *unit index*. It measures the extent to which $K$ possesses units that are not products of roots of unity and real units.
