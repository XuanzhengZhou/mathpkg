Let $K$ be an imaginary abelian extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ and let $K^+$ be its maximal real subfield. Let $E$ and $E^+$ denote the group of units in $K$ and $K^+$ respectively, and let $W$ be the group of roots of unity in $K$. Let $h, h^+$ be class numbers, $R, R^+$ regulators, and $N = [K : \mathbb{Q}]$, $N^+ = [K^+ : \mathbb{Q}] = N/2$.

Then the unit index $Q = (E : W \cdot E^+)$ satisfies:

$$Q = \frac{2^{r_1} h R / w}{h^+ R^+ / w^+} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{|d^+|}}{\sqrt{|d|}}$$

Equivalently, using the analytic class number formula:

$$(E : W \cdot E^+) = \frac{2^{n} h}{h^+} \cdot \frac{R}{R^+}$$

where $n = [K^+ : \mathbb{Q}]$.

More concretely, for a cyclotomic field $K = \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$ with $m$ odd or $m \equiv 2 \pmod 4$:

$$Q = \prod_{\chi \text{ odd}} \left( -\frac{1}{2} B_{1,\chi} \right)$$

where the product runs over all odd primitive Dirichlet characters of conductor dividing $m$.
