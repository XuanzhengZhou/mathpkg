The Davenport-Hasse theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of character sums over finite fields. It relates the Gauss sum of a character lifted to a finite extension field to the Gauss sum of the original character over the base field. Specifically, if $E/F$ is a finite extension of finite fields with degree $m = [E:F]$, and $\chi_E$, $\lambda_E$ are the characters obtained by composing $\chi$, $\lambda$ with the norm and trace maps respectively, then the Gauss sum over $E$ equals the $m$-th power of the Gauss sum over $F$ (up to sign). This theorem is essential for computing zeta functions of varieties defined over finite fields, particularly the Fermat curve.
