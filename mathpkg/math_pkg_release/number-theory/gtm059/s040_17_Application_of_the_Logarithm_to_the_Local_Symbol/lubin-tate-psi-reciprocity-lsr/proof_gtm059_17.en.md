We have

$$\psi_a(\alpha) = \psi(a) \psi_a(0)^T$$

and

$$\varepsilon(\sigma^{-1} a, \alpha) = (\lambda, \alpha) = \left( \sum_i \lambda_i \, d_i(\phi_a)(\alpha) \right)_{a_i}.$$

But $$\operatorname{ex}_a = \varepsilon(a)(x)$$ by the definition of $$\operatorname{ex}_a$$, with $$x \in \sigma^*$$. Using $$[ab] = [a][b]$$ and the compatibility $$T_{\alpha}(R) = T_{\alpha}(R)$$, the proof of LS R is concluded.
