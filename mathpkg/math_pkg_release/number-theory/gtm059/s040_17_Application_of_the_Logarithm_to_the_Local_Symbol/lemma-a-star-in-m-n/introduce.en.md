Let $$A$$ be the Lubin-Tate formal group associated to a prime element $$\pi$$ of a local field $$K$$. Let $$u$$ be the canonical Lubin-Tate logarithm map, and let $$A^*(u)$$ denote the kernel of the logarithm on the formal group. Then for integers $$k, n \geq 1$$,

$$A^*(u)_{k \times n} \subseteq m_n$$

where $$m_n$$ denotes the maximal ideal of the ring of integers of the $$n$$-th division field, and $$A^*(u)_{k \times n}$$ is the subgroup of $$k \times n$$-torsion points in the kernel of the logarithm.
