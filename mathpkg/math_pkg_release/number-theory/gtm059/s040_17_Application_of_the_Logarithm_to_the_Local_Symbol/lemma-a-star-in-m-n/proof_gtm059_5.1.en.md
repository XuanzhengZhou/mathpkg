A point $$x$$ is a torsion point if and only if $$[u^*]^k(d_n)$$ is a torsion point for some positive $$n_i$$ or for every large positive integer $$n$$. But $$[u^*]^k$$ approaches $$0$$ and for $$k$$ large, $$n_i$$ lies in the neighbourhood of $$0$$ which contains no elements of finite order. It follows that the kernel of the logarithm is precisely $$0$$ in the formal group.
