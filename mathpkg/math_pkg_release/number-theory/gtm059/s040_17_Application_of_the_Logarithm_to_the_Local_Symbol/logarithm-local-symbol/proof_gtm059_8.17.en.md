The proof uses the Coleman power series and the theory of the $p$-adic logarithm on Lubin-Tate formal groups. For the multiplicative group, the logarithm series $\log(1+X) = \sum (-1)^{n+1} X^n/n$ converges on the principal units. The Hilbert symbol is computed by evaluating the Kummer pairing, which by Lubin-Tate theory corresponds to the trace of the logarithm. The explicit formula follows from the compatibility of the logarithm with the Artin-Hasse exponential and the fact that the Kummer pairing factors through the differential $d\log$.
