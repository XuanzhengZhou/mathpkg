The symbol

$$[x, a_n] = \frac{1}{\pi^2} \, T_f(\ell, \ell) \, A_f(n, n)$$

is well-defined for $$\pi \neq 1$$ independently of the choices, provided one of the following holds:

either $$n$$ is sufficiently divisible in $$A_f(n)$$, or $$n$$ is sufficiently large.

The value lies in $$K_n / \mathfrak{p}^{n+1}$$.

**Condition (0):** $$n \geq 2$$ and there exists an integer $$k = \lfloor n/2 \rfloor - 2n + 1$$ such that $$a_n = N_n a_n \in K_n$$ and $$K_n \geq 2$$. Also $$m \geq n$$ and $$n \geq 2$$.
