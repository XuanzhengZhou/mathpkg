The proof proceeds by identifying the dual $$\mathfrak{a}^*$$ with $$\operatorname{Hom}(\mathfrak{a}, \mathcal{O})$$ via the trace pairing. If $$B_0 = \mathfrak{D}_{K_i/K}$$ is the different, then $$\mathfrak{a}^* = \mathfrak{a}^{-1} \mathfrak{B}_0^{-1}$$.

Returning to the pairing given by the local symbol

$$A(\mathfrak{b}_0)^{g+1} A(\mathfrak{b}_1) \times K_2 \to A_{\mathfrak{a}_0},$$

we note as a consequence of LS 4 that

$$A_{\mathfrak{b}_0} = N_{\mathfrak{a}_0} K_{\mathfrak{b}_1}^*.$$

There are two orthogonality relations: one referring to the local symbol, and one referring to the duality $$K_0 \times K_1 \to K_2$$ (where the trace is viewed as having values in the factor group). Considering the pairing

$$A(\mathfrak{b}_0)^{g+1} A(\mathfrak{b}_1) \times A_{\mathfrak{a}_0} \to A_{\mathfrak{a}_0}$$

and applying the logarithm map, we obtain the desired identification. Since the kernel is trivial (there are no elements of finite order in the neighbourhood of zero), the logarithm gives an isomorphism, and the formula follows.
