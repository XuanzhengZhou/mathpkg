**Proof.** The theorem has already been proved when $x$ is a power of $\pi_n$, or when $y$ is a power of $\pi_n$. We may therefore assume that $x$ is a unit with $x \neq 1$ and $x \neq 0$. We reduce the theorem to the result of the preceding section by applying the main lemma for highly divisible $x$ (Lemma 4.1) and the explicit formulas established in Theorem 6.1 and Theorem 6.2.

The reduction proceeds by verifying that the order conditions imposed on $k$ guarantee the convergence of all power series expansions involved, and that the symbols are well-defined in the range where the norm residue symbol takes its values in $K \bmod \mathfrak{p}^{n+k} x_n$. The key step uses the relation DL 6 (derived in Chapter 8) which gives an equality implying the stronger statement needed to pass from the special case to the general case.

The value $d_{n-1} - a_n$ is computed explicitly, and the alternating property LS 4 of the symbol $(x, \alpha) = 0$ together with the group law congruence from Lemma 6.3 allows us to conclude that

$$(x, w_n) = [x_n, y_n]_B(x)$$

holds for all choices of $x$ and $n$ satisfying the stated conditions.
