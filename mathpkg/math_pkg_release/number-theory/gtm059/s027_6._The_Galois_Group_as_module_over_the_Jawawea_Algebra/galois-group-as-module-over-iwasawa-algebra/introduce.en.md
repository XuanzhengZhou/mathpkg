In Iwasawa theory, one studies towers of number fields whose Galois groups are pro-$p$ groups. Given a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension $K_\infty/K_0$ and a $p$-abelian extension $Q$ of $K_\infty$ which is Galois over $K_0$, the Galois group $G = \operatorname{Gal}(Q/K_\infty)$ carries a natural action of $\Gamma = \operatorname{Gal}(K_\infty/K_0) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ by conjugation. The commutator subgroup equals $G^{\gamma-1}$, and passing to the projective limit over intermediate fields yields a compact module structure over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[\![X]\!]$. This construction is fundamental to the algebraic study of class groups and unit groups in $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions.
