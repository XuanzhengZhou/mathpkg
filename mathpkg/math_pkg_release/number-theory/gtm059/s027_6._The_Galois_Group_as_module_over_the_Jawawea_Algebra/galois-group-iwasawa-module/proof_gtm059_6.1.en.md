Via class field theory, the Artin reciprocity map identifies the projective limit of $p$-parts of class groups with the Galois group $X$. The action of $\Gamma = \operatorname{Gal}(K_{\infty}/K)$ on $X$ is defined by lifting an element $\gamma \in \Gamma$ to $\operatorname{Gal}(L_{\infty}/K)$ and conjugating. This makes $X$ a compact $\Lambda$-module. Finite generation follows from Nakayama's lemma applied to $X/\mathfrak{m}X$, whose finiteness is equivalent to the finiteness of the class number of $K$.
