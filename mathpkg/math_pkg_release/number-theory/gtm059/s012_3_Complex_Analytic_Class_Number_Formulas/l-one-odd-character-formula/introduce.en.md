When $\chi$ is an odd primitive character ($\chi(-1) = -1$), the general formula for $L(1, \chi)$ simplifies to a weighted sum of the residues $a$ modulo $m$. Pairing terms $a$ and $m-a$ in the logarithm sum, the oddness of $\chi$ causes the real parts to cancel while the imaginary parts combine to produce a factor of $\pi i a/m$. The result is an expression purely in terms of the Gauss sum $\tau(\chi)$ and the first moment $\sum \overline{\chi}(a) a$, which is essentially the first generalized Bernoulli number $B_{1, \overline{\chi}}$.
