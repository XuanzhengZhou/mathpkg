Every Dirichlet character $\chi$ modulo $m$ is induced from a unique primitive character $\psi$ modulo its conductor $f \mid m$. The $L$-series $L(s, \chi)$ factors as $L(s, \psi)$ times a finite Euler product over the primes dividing $m$ but not $f$. This decomposition is fundamental because it reduces the analytic class number formula to the study of $L(1, \psi)$ for primitive characters. For applications to cyclotomic fields, only primitive characters need to be considered, since the additional Euler factors contribute only algebraic units.
