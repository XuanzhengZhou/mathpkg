The proof proceeds by an analog of the Smith normal form algorithm, adapted to the Iwasawa algebra. Given a presentation matrix for $M$, one applies admissible operations (row/column operations, Weierstrass preparation, and operations corresponding to finite-index embeddings). The key observation is that any element can be reduced, modulo the maximal ideal $(p, X)$, to a distinguished polynomial times a unit. Iterating this process diagonalizes the relation matrix, yielding the direct sum decomposition. The operations correspond to quasi-isomorphisms, i.e., maps with finite kernel and cokernel, which preserve the asymptotic growth invariants.
