The structure theorem for finitely generated Iwasawa modules is the analog, over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$, of the structure theorem for finitely generated modules over a principal ideal domain. Since $\Lambda$ is not a PID but a regular local ring of dimension 2, the theorem is more subtle: torsion modules decompose into cyclic modules of the form $\Lambda/(p^m)$ and $\Lambda/(f^m)$ where $f$ is a distinguished polynomial. The characteristic polynomial encodes the growth of the $p$-parts of class groups in a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension, leading to Iwasawa's celebrated class number formula.
