Let $\mathfrak{m} = (p,X)$ be the maximal ideal of $A = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$. Since $M$ is compact, for each $n$ there exists a neighborhood $U_n$ of $0$ in $M$ and a positive integer $j$ such that $\mathfrak{m}^j M \subset U_n$. A finite number of neighborhoods $U_n$ cover $M$. If $M = \mathfrak{m}M$, then by induction $M = \mathfrak{m}^k M$ for all $k$, and since $\bigcap_k \mathfrak{m}^k M = 0$ by compactness and the properties of the $\mathfrak{m}$-adic topology on a compact module, we conclude $M = 0$.

The statement $M/\mathfrak{m}M = 0 \implies M = 0$ for compact $\Lambda$-modules is the form most commonly used in Iwasawa theory.
