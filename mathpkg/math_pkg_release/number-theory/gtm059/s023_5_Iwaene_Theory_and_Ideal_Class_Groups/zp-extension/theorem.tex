Let $K$ be a number field. A \textbf{$\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension} of $K$ is a Galois extension $K_{\infty}/K$ whose Galois group $\operatorname{Gal}(K_{\infty}/K)$ is isomorphic to the additive group of $p$-adic integers $\mathbb{Z}_p$.

Equivalently, a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension is given by a tower of fields
$$K = K_0 \subset K_1 \subset K_2 \subset \dots \subset K_{\infty} = \bigcup_n K_n$$
such that $\operatorname{Gal}(K_n/K) \cong \mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z}$ for each $n \ge 1$.

\textbf{Examples:}
\begin{itemize}
\item $K = \mathbb{Q}$, $K_{\infty} = \mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^{\infty}})^+$ (the real subfield of the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension) for $p$ odd.
\item More generally, for any number field $K$, the field $K(\mu_{p^{\infty}})$ obtained by adjoining all $p$-power roots of unity contains a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of $K$ (the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension).
\end{itemize}
