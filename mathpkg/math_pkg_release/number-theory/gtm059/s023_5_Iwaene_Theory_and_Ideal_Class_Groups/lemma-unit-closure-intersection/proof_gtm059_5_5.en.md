Let $U_{\mathfrak{p}}^{(n)}$ be the group of units in $U_{\mathfrak{p}}$ which are congruent to $1$ modulo $\mathfrak{p}^n$. The groups $U_{\mathfrak{p}}^{(n)}$ form a fundamental system of neighborhoods for $U_{\mathfrak{p}}$, and their intersection is the closure. For primes $\mathfrak{p}$ not dividing $p$, the unit group $U_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is finite modulo its $p$-primary part, so the intersection with the product over all $\mathfrak{p}$ coincides with the intersection over $\mathfrak{p} \mid p$. Taking the intersection over all $n$ and all $\mathfrak{p}$ proves the equality.
