Let $K$ be a number field, let $H$ be the $p$-Hilbert class field of $K$, and let $G(K) = \operatorname{Gal}(M(K)/K)$ be the Galois group of the maximal $p$-abelian unramified extension. Then there is a quasi-isomorphism (i.e., a homomorphism with finite kernel and cokernel) of $\mathbb{Z}_p$-modules:
$$G(K) \sim \prod_{\mathfrak{p} \mid p} U_{\mathfrak{p}}^{(1)} \Big/ \overline{E}$$
where $U_{\mathfrak{p}}^{(1)}$ denotes the group of principal units (units congruent to $1$ modulo the maximal ideal) at each prime $\mathfrak{p}$ above $p$, and $\overline{E}$ is the closure of the diagonal embedding of the global units $E$ of $K$.

Furthermore, $U_{\mathfrak{p}}^{(1)} \cong \mathbb{Z}_p^{[K_{\mathfrak{p}}:\mathbb{Q}_p]}$ contains an open subgroup of finite index, giving a quasi-isomorphism
$$G(K) \sim \mathbb{Z}_p^{r_p}$$
where $r_p = r_2 + 1 + \delta_p(K)$ under the Leopoldt conjecture (with $\delta_p(K)$ the Leopoldt defect).
