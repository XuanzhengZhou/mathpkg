Since $M$ is compact, there exist open neighborhoods $U_m$ of the identity and integers $j$ such that $\mathfrak{m}^j U_m \subset U$ for any neighborhood $U$ of $0$. A finite number of neighborhoods cover $M$. Hence there exists an integer $n$ such that $\mathfrak{m}^n M \subset U$ for all neighborhoods $U$. If $M/\mathfrak{m}M$ is generated by $x_1, \ldots, x_k$, then $M$ is generated by lifts of these elements modulo $\mathfrak{m}^n M$, which implies finite generation. If $M/\mathfrak{m}M = 0$, then $M = \mathfrak{m}M = \mathfrak{m}^2M = \cdots$, and since $\bigcap \mathfrak{m}^n M = 0$ by compactness, $M = 0$.
