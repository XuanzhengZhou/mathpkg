Let $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ be the Iwasawa algebra and let $V$ be a finitely generated torsion $\Lambda$-module. For each $n \geq 1$, let $V_n = V/\omega_n V$ where $\omega_n = (1 + X)^{p^n} - 1$, and suppose $V_n$ is finite for all $n$. Set $s_n = s_n(V) = v_p(|V_n|)$, the $p$-adic valuation of the order of $V_n$.

Then there exist integers $\mu \geq 0$, $\lambda \geq 0$, and $\nu$ such that for all sufficiently large $n$,

$$s_n(V) = \mu p^n + \lambda n + \nu.$$

More precisely, any finitely generated torsion $\Lambda$-module $V$ is pseudo-isomorphic to a direct sum of elementary modules:

$$V \sim \Lambda^r \oplus \bigoplus_{i=1}^{s} \Lambda/(p^{\mu_i}) \oplus \bigoplus_{j=1}^{t} \Lambda/(f_j^{m_j}),$$

where $r, s, t \geq 0$ are integers, $\mu_i \geq 1$, and each $f_j \in \mathbb{Z}_p[X]$ is an irreducible distinguished polynomial. The invariants $\mu = \sum \mu_i$ and $\lambda = \sum m_j \deg f_j$ appear in the asymptotic formula above.
