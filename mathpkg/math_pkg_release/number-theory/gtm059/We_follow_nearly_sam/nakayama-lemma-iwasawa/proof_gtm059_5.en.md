Since $M$ is compact, the closed submodule $N$ is also compact. The quotient $M/N$ is a compact $\Lambda$-module satisfying $\mathfrak{m} \cdot (M/N) = M/N$. Since $\mathfrak{m}$ is the maximal ideal of the local ring $\Lambda$, every element of $\Lambda \setminus \mathfrak{m}$ is a unit. If $M/N \neq 0$, then by compactness there exists a nonzero simple quotient, but $\mathfrak{m}$ acts nilpotently on any finitely generated $\Lambda$-module; the compactness and the condition $\mathfrak{m} \cdot (M/N) = M/N$ force $M/N = 0$, hence $M = N$.
