In Iwasawa theory, arithmetic objects such as ideal class groups, unit groups, or Galois groups are studied along an infinite tower of cyclotomic or $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions. The modules $V_n$ at each finite level $n$ are modules over the corresponding finite group ring $\mathbb{Z}_p[\Gamma_n]$. Taking the projective limit yields a module $V$ over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda$, which encodes the asymptotic behavior of the entire tower. This construction is fundamental for applying the structure theory of finitely generated $\Lambda$-modules to arithmetic problems.
