Let $(p, X)$ denote the maximal ideal of $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ generated by $p$ and $X$. We first prove by induction that

$$h_n = (1 + X)^{p^n} - 1 \in (p, X)^{n+1}$$

for all $n \geq 0$. The base case $n = 0$ gives $h_0 = X \in (p, X)$. Assume the statement holds for $n$. Then

$$(1 + X)^{p^{n+1}} - 1 = ((1 + X)^{p^n})^{p} - 1 = (1 + h_n)^{p} - 1 = \sum_{k=1}^{p} \binom{p}{k} h_n^{k}.$$

Since $h_n \in (p, X)^{n+1}$ by induction hypothesis and $p \mid \binom{p}{k}$ for $1 \leq k \leq p-1$, each term satisfies $\binom{p}{k} h_n^{k} \in (p, X)^{(n+1)+1} = (p, X)^{n+2}$. The term $h_n^{p} \in (p, X)^{p(n+1)} \subseteq (p, X)^{n+2}$ for $p \geq 2$. Thus $h_{n+1} \in (p, X)^{n+2}$, completing the induction.

It follows that the intersection $\bigcap_{n} (h_n) = \{0\}$ in $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$, since the ideals $(p, X)^{n+1}$ form a neighborhood basis of $0$ in the $(p, X)$-adic topology and $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ is complete.

The kernel of $e$ consists of those power series $f \in \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ whose image in each $\mathbb{Z}_p[X]/(h_n)$ is zero, i.e., $f \in (h_n)$ for all $n$. Hence $\ker(e) = \bigcap_n (h_n) = \{0\}$, so $e$ is injective.

For surjectivity, note that an element of the projective limit is represented by a sequence $(f_n)$ with $f_n \in \mathbb{Z}_p[X]$ and $f_{n+1} \equiv f_n \pmod{h_n}$. Since $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ is complete and the natural map $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]] \to \varprojlim \mathbb{Z}_p[X]/(h_n)$ has dense image (polynomials are dense in $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$) and both sides are compact, the map is surjective. Alternatively, one may use the Euclidean algorithm: since each $h_n$ is a monic polynomial, the natural map $\mathbb{Z}_p[X]/(h_{n+1}) \to \mathbb{Z}_p[X]/(h_n)$ is surjective and the projective limit of the system of quotient rings is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$.
