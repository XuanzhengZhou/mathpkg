The unit index $Q$ is a fundamental arithmetic invariant of a CM field $K$, introduced in the context of the analytic class number formula. It quantifies the relationship between the full unit group $E$ of $K$ and the subgroup $\mu_K \cdot E^+$ generated by the roots of unity and the units of the maximal real subfield $K^+$. The index $Q$ is always a power of $2$ (specifically, $Q = 2^{r_2}$ or $Q = 2^{r_2-1}$ depending on whether $K$ contains a primitive fourth root of unity), and it plays a crucial role in comparing the class numbers and regulators of $K$ and $K^+$.
