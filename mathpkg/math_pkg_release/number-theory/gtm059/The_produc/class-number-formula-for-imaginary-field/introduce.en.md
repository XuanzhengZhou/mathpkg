This theorem provides a decomposition of the class number $h$ of a totally imaginary CM field $K$ in terms of the class number $h^+$ of its maximal real subfield $K^+$ and the values of odd $L$-functions at $s = 0$. The formula is derived by comparing the analytic class number formulas for $K$ and $K^+$, using the product decomposition of the zeta function quotient $\zeta_K(s)/\zeta_{K^+}(s)$ established in Theorem 3.1. The unit index $Q$ and the ratio of regulators $R_{K^+}/R_K$ capture the arithmetic discrepancy between the two fields, while the product of $L(0,\chi)$ over odd characters accounts for the analytic part of the decomposition.
