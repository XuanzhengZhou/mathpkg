Let $K$ be a totally imaginary CM field with maximal real subfield $K^+$. Let $h$ and $h^+$ be the class numbers of $K$ and $K^+$ respectively. Let $Q = (E_K : \mu_K \cdot E_{K^+})$ be the unit index. Let $N = [K : \mathbb{Q}]$ and $N^+ = [K^+ : \mathbb{Q}] = N/2$. Then the class number of $K$ decomposes as
$$h = h^+ \cdot Q \cdot 2^{r_2 - 1} \cdot \frac{R_{K^+}}{R_K} \cdot \prod_{\chi \text{ odd}} L(0, \chi)$$
where the product is taken over all odd primitive Dirichlet characters $\chi$ associated to the extension $K/\mathbb{Q}$, $r_2 = N/2$ is the number of complex places of $K$, and $R_K$, $R_{K^+}$ are the regulators. Equivalently, applying the analytic class number formula to $K^+$,
$$h = \frac{h^+ \cdot Q \cdot w_{K^+} \cdot \sqrt{|d_K|}}{2^{r_2} \cdot w_K \cdot \sqrt{|d_{K^+}|}} \cdot \prod_{\chi \text{ odd}} L(0, \chi)$$
where $w_K$, $w_{K^+}$ are the numbers of roots of unity and $d_K$, $d_{K^+}$ are the discriminants.
