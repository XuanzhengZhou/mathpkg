Let $p$ be an odd prime and let $D_p$ be the Maillet determinant formed from residues $R_{ij}^\pm$ as in Theorem~7.1. Then by Hadamard's inequality,

$$| D_p | \leq \left( \sqrt{ \frac{p-1}{2} } \cdot p \right)^{(p-1)/2} = p^{(p-1)/2} \left( \frac{p-1}{2} \right)^{(p-1)/4}.$$

Consequently, the relative class number $h^- = h_A$ of the $p$-th cyclotomic field satisfies

$$h^- \leq p^{(p-1)/2} \, \left(\frac{p-1}{2}\right)^{(p-1)/4}.$$

Carlitz [Ca] observed this as an immediate consequence of Hadamard's inequality applied to the Maillet determinant. Montgomery and Mozgovoy [M-M] later proved refined two-sided inequalities: for all primes $p \geq 200$,

$$(2k)^p \;<\; 1.2^{p^2} \, n^{2+1/2} \;\leq\; h_A \;\leq\; (2k)^p + 1.2^{p^2} \, n^{2+1/2},$$

where $k$ and $n$ are constants related to the determinant, showing the Carlitz bound is asymptotically sharp.
