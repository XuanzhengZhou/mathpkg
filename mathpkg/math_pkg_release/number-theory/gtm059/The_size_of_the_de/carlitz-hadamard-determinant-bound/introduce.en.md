Applying Hadamard's determinant inequality to the Maillet determinant $D_p$ yields an upper bound for the relative class number $h^-$ of the $p$-th cyclotomic field. Since each entry of the scaled determinant has absolute value at most $p$, Hadamard's inequality (which bounds a determinant by the product of the Euclidean lengths of its row vectors) gives an exponential-type bound. Carlitz first noted this observation, and subsequent work by Montgomery-Mozgovoy provided sharp two-sided inequalities confirming the asymptotic sharpness of this elementary estimate for large primes $p \geq 200$.
