The Maillet determinant is a classical construction connecting elementary residue arithmetic with the deep invariant of the class number. Formed from least positive residues modulo an odd prime $p$, the determinant $D_p$ captures the relative class number $h^-$ of the cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_p)$. Theorem 7.1 in Lang's Cyclotomic Fields gives the precise formula relating $D_p$ to $h^-$ up to powers of $2$ and $p$. The size of this determinant is studied further using Hadamard's inequality, leading to bounds on the class number.
