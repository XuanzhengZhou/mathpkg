The Kummer pairing provides a fundamental duality in Iwasawa theory relating the Galois group of the maximal $p$-abelian extension to the $p$-primary ideal class group. This theorem establishes a compact-discrete duality: the projective limit $G$ of the Galois groups pairs non-degenerately with the direct limit $A$ of the $p$-primary class groups, taking values in the group $\mu_{p^{\infty}}$ of all $p$-power roots of unity. The pairing is compatible with the natural $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module structures on both sides and encodes deep arithmetic information about the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension.
