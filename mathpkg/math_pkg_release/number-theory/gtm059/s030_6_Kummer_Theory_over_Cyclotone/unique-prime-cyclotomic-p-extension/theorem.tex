Let $K_{\infty}/K$ be a cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension with finite layers $K_n$. Assume that there is only one prime in $K_n$ lying above $p$.

Then the maximal $p$-abelian extension $Q_n$ of $K_n$ satisfies
$$Q_n = Q_{p_n} = Q(E_n^{(p^{n+1})})$$
where $E_n$ denotes the group of units in $K_n$, and $Q_{p_n}$ denotes the maximal $p$-abelian extension of $K_n$ generated by the $p^{n+1}$-th powers of units.

Equivalently,
$$Q_n = Q(E_n^{(p^{n+1})})$$
that is, the maximal $p$-abelian extension is generated by adjoining $p^{n+1}$-th roots of all units.
