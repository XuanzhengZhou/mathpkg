For a CM field $K$, complex conjugation $\tau$ acts on the ideal class group $C_K$ as an involution. The plus and minus eigenspaces $C_K^+$ and $C_K^-$ decompose the class group into the $\tau$-invariant part and the anti-invariant part respectively. These eigenspaces play a fundamental role in Iwasawa theory, particularly in the analytic class number formula where the minus part $h^- = |C_K^-|$ is expressed as a product of values of $L$-functions at $s = 1$. The plus eigenspace is closely related to the class group of the maximal real subfield $K^+$.
