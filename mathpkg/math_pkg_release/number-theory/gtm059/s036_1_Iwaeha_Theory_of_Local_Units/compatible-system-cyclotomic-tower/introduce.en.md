In the Iwasawa theory of cyclotomic fields, a norm-compatible system $(v_n)_{n \ge 1}$ consists of local units $v_n$ at each level $n$ of the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension such that the norm map $N_{m,n}$ sends $v_m$ to $v_n$ for $m \ge n$. Typical constructions use quotients of cyclotomic numbers $(1 - \zeta_n^a)/(1 - \zeta_n)$ to produce such compatible families. The projective limit $v = \varprojlim v_n$ is an element of the Iwasawa module $V$, and its Coleman power series $f_v(T)$ encodes the entire compatible system. This correspondence between norm-compatible systems and power series is the basis of the Coleman isomorphism.
