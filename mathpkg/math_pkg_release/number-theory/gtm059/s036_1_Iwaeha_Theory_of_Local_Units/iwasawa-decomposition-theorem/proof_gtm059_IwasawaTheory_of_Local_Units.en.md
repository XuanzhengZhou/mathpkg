The result follows immediately from Theorem 3.2 of Chapter 6 (equivalently Theorem 3.2 of Chapter 15). For completeness, we outline the argument.

Let $\chi$ be an even character of $G_n = \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}_p(\zeta_{p^n})/\mathbb{Q}_p)$ with $\chi \neq 1$. Let $\xi_\chi$ be the idempotent associated to $\chi$ in the group ring $\mathbb{Z}_p[G_n]$, and set $u(\chi) = \xi_\chi \cdot v$, where $v = \varprojlim v_n$ is the projective limit of a norm-compatible system of cyclotomic units.

The element $v$ has an associated Coleman power series $f_v(X) \in \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]^\times$. Projecting onto the $\chi$-component via the idempotent $\xi_\chi$, the power series associated to the element $u(\chi)$ is
$$f_\chi(X) = \frac{X}{1 + F(X)} - 1$$
for some $F(X) \in \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$. The key point is that the $\chi$-component $V(\chi)$ is exactly the $\mathbb{Z}_p[\Gamma]$-submodule generated by $u(\chi)$. The cyclicity follows from the fact that the Iwasawa algebra $\mathbb{Z}_p[\Gamma] \cong \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ is a principal ideal domain, and the structure theory of finitely generated modules over $\mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ then implies that $V(\chi)$ is a cyclic $\mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$-module generated by $u(\chi)$.
