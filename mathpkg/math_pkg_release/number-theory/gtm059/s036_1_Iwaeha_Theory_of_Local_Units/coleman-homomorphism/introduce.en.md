The Coleman homomorphism is a fundamental tool in Iwasawa theory that provides a bridge between norm-compatible systems of local units in a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension and the Iwasawa algebra. It maps a norm-compatible sequence $u = (u_n)$ of local units to a formal power series $f_u(T) \in \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]^\times$, the Coleman power series. The homomorphism $\delta_k$ is then obtained by applying the Iwasawa logarithm to this power series, yielding an element in the Iwasawa algebra that encodes arithmetic information about the original units. This construction is central to the proof of Iwasawa's main conjecture and the study of cyclotomic units.
