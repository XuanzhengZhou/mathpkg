This is the same as Lemma 1 of Section 1, adapted to the odd case. The group $2r$ is generated by elements of the form $(v_r, -v_r) = v_r(-v_r)$. An element $\xi \in L$ lies in $R[x]$, and the argument proceeds exactly as in the even case, with the additional factor of 2 accounting for the sign change in the odd character.
