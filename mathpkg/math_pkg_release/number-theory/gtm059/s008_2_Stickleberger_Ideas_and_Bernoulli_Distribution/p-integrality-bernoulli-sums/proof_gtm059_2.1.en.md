The argument uses the same expression for the Bernoulli polynomial as in the previous lemma. We have

$$
p \sum_{i=1}^{k-1} \binom{n}{i} \binom{n}{i-1} p^{i-1} = p\text{-integral}.
$$

The leading term is $p^{10} N^e$. For the Bernoulli numbers $B_i$:
- If $i < p$, then $B_i$ is $p$-integral by Kummer's criterion (a consequence of the von Staudt-Clausen theorem).
- If $i \geq p$, the factor $N^{i-1}$ appearing in front of the Bernoulli polynomial expression ensures that $\frac{N^{i-1}}{N} p^{i-1}$ is $p$-integral.

Since the Stickelberger ideal $I$ is generated by elements of the form $c \equiv 1 \pmod{N}$ and $c \equiv 0 \pmod{1}$, a straightforward computation shows that $I \cap \mathbb{Z}$ consists precisely of the integers divisible by $p^e$. Hence $I \cap \mathbb{Z} = (p^e)$.
