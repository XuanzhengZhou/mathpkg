A distribution can be decomposed as the sum of an odd and an even part, provided that multiplication by 2 is invertible on the target module. The proof proceeds by showing that the distribution arising from the binomial polynomial $X^k$ is non-vanishing for all $k$, and that on the space of odd distributions, the Stickelberger distribution achieves the maximal attainable rank.

The key step uses the distribution relation to reduce the computation to the $p$-component at a prime $p$, where the non-vanishing follows from the $p$-adic properties of Bernoulli numbers established in the earlier lemmas. Under the condition $S_{k_0} h_0 = 0$, we obtain the relation

$$
S_{k_0} h_0 = (1 - z) f(k/k_0) S_{k_0} h_0,
$$

which forces non-vanishing when the factor $(1-z)$ is a non-zero-divisor.
