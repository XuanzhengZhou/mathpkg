Following Kubert-Lang, a **Stickelberger distribution** $g$ on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ with values in an abelian group $A$ is defined as follows.

For each positive integer $N$, let $G(N) = (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^\times$ and define

$$
b_a(N) = \left(\frac{1}{N}\right)^{k-1} \quad \text{for } a \in \mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}.
$$

For a function $h$ on $G(N)$, the Stickelberger sum is

$$
S_g(h) = \sum_{a \in G(N)} g_a(N) h(a).
$$

The distribution $g$ is then characterized by its values on the group algebra of $G(N)$ and satisfies the distribution relation: if $N \mid M$, then the value at level $N$ is obtained from the value at level $M$ by the trace (sum over fibers) map.
