Let $\chi$ be a Dirichlet character with conductor $f = f_\chi$. The **generalized Bernoulli numbers** $B_{k,\chi}$ (in the sense of Leopoldt) are defined by

$$
B_{k,\chi} = f^{k-1} \sum_{a=1}^{f} \chi(a) \, B_k\!\left(\frac{a}{f}\right),
$$

where $B_k(X)$ is the $k$-th Bernoulli polynomial. Equivalently,

$$
B_{k,\chi} = \sum_{a=1}^{f} \chi(a) \, B_{k}\!\left(\left\langle\frac{a}{f}\right\rangle\right)
$$

where $\langle x \rangle$ denotes the fractional part. In particular, when $\chi = \chi_0$ is the trivial character, one recovers the ordinary Bernoulli number $B_k$ up to a factor:

$$
B_{k,\chi_0} = B_k \quad \text{for } k \geq 2.
$$

For applications to the Stickelberger element, it is conventional to sum over $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$ where $N$ is divisible by the conductor $f$.
