Let $R$ be the Stickelberger ring (the subring of the group ring generated by Stickelberger elements) and let $R_k$ denote the $k$-th component. Then:

(i) $R_k \cap R = R^{(\infty)}$, where $R^{(\infty)}$ denotes the set of elements whose $p$-components lie in $R$ for all primes $p$.

(ii) Moreover, letting $Q = R_k$, we have $R_k \cap R = Q$ whenever $Q$ satisfies the condition that if $c \in R$ has $Q(c) \in R$, then the corresponding element lies in the Stickelberger ideal $J^{(\infty)}$.
