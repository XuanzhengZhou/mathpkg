The $p$-adic $L$-function $L_p(s, \chi)$ is defined as the Mellin transform of a measure $\mu$ on $\mathbb{Z}_p^{\times}$, multiplied by a regularization factor that removes the pole. For a non-trivial character $\chi$, the factor $(1 - \chi(c)/c^2)^{-1}$ can be chosen to be analytic at $s = 0$. Together with the lemma on power series expansions of measures, this representation proves the analyticity of the $p$-adic $L$-function as a function of the $p$-adic variable $s$. This integral representation is the central construction in the Iwasawa-theoretic approach to $p$-adic $L$-functions.
