The $p$-adic logarithm, originally defined on the principal units $U_1 = \{u : u \equiv 1 \pmod{\mathfrak{p}}\}$, can be extended uniquely to a continuous function on the full multiplicative group $C_p^\times$ of the completion such that $\log p = 0$. The extension is constructed in stages: first, using the product decomposition $U = U_0 \times U_1$ of the unit group into the finite group $U_0$ of roots of unity of order prime to $p$ and the principal units $U_1$, one defines $\log u = \log(a)$ where $a$ is the projection of $u$ onto $U_1$. This yields a continuous homomorphism on the entire unit group. The logarithm is then further extended to all of $C_p^\times$ by fixing $\log p = 0$ and using the decomposition of the multiplicative group as $C_p^\times = p^{\mathbb{Z}} \times U$. The resulting extension is a continuous homomorphism from the multiplicative group $C_p^\times$ into the additive group $C_p$.
