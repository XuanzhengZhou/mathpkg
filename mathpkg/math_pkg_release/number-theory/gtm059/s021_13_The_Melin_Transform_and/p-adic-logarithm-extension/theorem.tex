The $p$-adic logarithm is extended to a continuous function on the full multiplicative group $C_p^\times$ of the $p$-adic completion as follows. The unit group $U$ of $C_p$ admits a product decomposition
$$
U = U_0 \times U_1,
$$
where $U_0$ is the group of roots of unity in $C_p$ of order prime to $p$, and $U_1$ is the group of principal units $\{u \in U : u \equiv 1 \pmod{\mathfrak{p}}\}$. For a unit $u \in U$, let $a$ be its projection onto $U_1$; then define
$$
\log u = \log(a).
$$
This extension is continuous and is a homomorphism. The logarithm is further extended to the whole multiplicative group $C_p^\times$ by considering the decomposition $C_p^\times = p^{\mathbb{Z}} \times U$, and defining the $\log$ of an element $\alpha \in C_p^\times$ to be the $\log$ of its projection onto $U_1$. In particular,
$$
\log p = 0.
$$
The extension is a continuous homomorphism from the multiplicative group $C_p^\times$ into the additive group $C_p$.
