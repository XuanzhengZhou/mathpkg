The $p$-adic regulator is the $p$-adic analogue of the classical Dirichlet regulator. Instead of using the ordinary real logarithm, it employs the $p$-adic logarithm (extended to $C_p^\times$ with $\log_p p = 0$). For a number field $K$, given a basis $\varepsilon_1,\dots,\varepsilon_r$ of the unit group modulo roots of unity, the $p$-adic regulator is defined as the determinant of the matrix whose $(i,j)$-entry is $\log_p$ of the $j$-th conjugate of $\varepsilon_i$. This regulator plays a central role in the $p$-adic class number formula and its non-vanishing is equivalent to Leopoldt's conjecture.
