Theorem 3.6 of Lang's Cyclotomic Fields provides a fundamental formula relating the value at $s=1$ of the $p$-adic $L$-function $L_p(s,\chi)$ of a primitive Dirichlet character $\chi$ to a sum involving the $p$-adic logarithm and the Gauss sum $\tau(\chi)$. This result is a $p$-adic analogue of the classical formula expressing $L(1,\chi)$ in terms of Gauss sums and logarithms of cyclotomic numbers. The theorem is a key ingredient in the proof of the $p$-adic class number formula and connects the $p$-adic $L$-function to the $p$-adic regulator via the Mellin transform formalism developed in Chapter 13.
