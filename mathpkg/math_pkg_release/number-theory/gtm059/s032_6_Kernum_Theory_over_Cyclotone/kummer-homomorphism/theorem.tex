Let $K_0 = \mathbb{Q}_p(\zeta)$ with a primitive $p$th root of unity $\zeta$, where $p$ is an odd prime. Let $\pi = \zeta - 1$. Let $U_1$ be the group of units $\equiv 1 \bmod \pi$ in $K_0$.

Let $T = 1 + X = e^u$ and let $D = (1 + X)\frac{d}{dX}$ be the differential operator. For a unit $u \in U_1$ with associated power series $f(X)$ such that $u = f(X)$ and $f \equiv 1 \bmod (p, X)$, the Kummer homomorphism is defined by

$$\mu_0(u) = D^*\left(\frac{D^* f}{D f}\right)(0) \bmod p.$$

More precisely,
$$\mu_0(u) = \left[D^{-1}\left\{\frac{f'}{f}(0)\right\}\right] \bmod p,$$
which is well-defined modulo $p$ since the denominator is an Eisenstein polynomial.
