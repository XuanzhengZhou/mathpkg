*Proof.* Let $a$ be an ideal representing an element of $C_1$ becoming principal in $K_m$. With $a \in K_m$ for any element $a \in K_m$ we have $a = u$. Hence, up to a unit, the association

$$a \mapsto \text{cohomology class of } \rho(u)$$

is a homomorphism of $K_1 \to C_2$ into $H^i(\text{the units})$, which is immediately verified to be injective.

Assume now the Vandiver conjecture. Then the cohomology $H^i(\operatorname{Vandiver} \cdot K_m)$ vanishes, and consequently the map $C_1 \to C_2$ is injective.
