\textbf{Lemma.} Let $\mathcal{G}$ be a Lubin-Tate formal group over the local field $K$ with associated prime $P$. Let $\mathcal{G}_A(W) = h_1 + b_1 W + \dots$ be a power series with coefficients in $K$, and let $\mathcal{G}_A(Z) = h_2 + d_2 Z + \dots$ be the power series obtained under the change of variables $Z = \lambda_{\mathcal{G}}(W) = W \bmod W^{q-1}$.

Taking the logarithmic derivative, i.e., the operator $g \mapsto g'/g$, we have:

If
$$\mathcal{G}_A'(W) W = \sum_{k=0}^{q-1} \mathcal{G}_A(W)^{k-1}$$
and
$$\mathcal{G}_B'(Z) Z = \sum_{k=0}^{q-1} \mathcal{G}_B(Z)^{k-1},$$
then
$$c_{k} = d_{k} \quad \text{for } k = 1, \dots, q-2.$$
