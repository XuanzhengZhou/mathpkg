The Fourier transform on a finite field $F = \mathbb{F}_q$ provides a discrete analogue of the classical Fourier transform on $\mathbb{R}$. Fixing a non-trivial additive character $\lambda$ of $F$, the transform $Tf(y) = \sum_{x \in F} f(x)\lambda(-xy)$ maps complex-valued functions on $F$ to functions on $F$. This transform is a fundamental tool in the theory of character sums, enabling the analysis of Gauss sums and Jacobi sums through convolution and multiplicative characters.
