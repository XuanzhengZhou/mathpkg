Let $F = \mathbb{F}_q$ be a finite field with $q = p^n$ elements. Fix a non-trivial additive character $\lambda: F \to \mathbb{C}^\times$. For any function $f: F \to \mathbb{C}$, its Fourier transform $Tf: F \to \mathbb{C}$ is defined by

$$Tf(y) = \sum_{x \in F} f(x) \lambda(-xy).$$

The map $T$ is a linear isomorphism on the space of complex-valued functions on $F$.
