**Proof.** By definition, $S(1) = \sum_{x \in F^*} 1 \cdot \lambda(x) = \sum_{x \in F^*} \lambda(x)$. Since $\lambda$ is a non-trivial additive character of $F$, the sum of $\lambda$ over all elements of $F$ vanishes: $\sum_{x \in F} \lambda(x) = 0$. The term at $x = 0$ is $\lambda(0) = 1$, while the remaining terms form the sum over $F^*$. Therefore $\sum_{x \in F^*} \lambda(x) + 1 = 0$, giving $S(1) = -1$.
