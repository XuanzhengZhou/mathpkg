Let $F = \mathbb{F}_q$ be a finite field with $q = p^n$, where $p$ is prime. Let $\chi$ be a multiplicative character of $F^*$ and let $\chi^p$ denote the character obtained by composing $\chi$ with the Frobenius automorphism $x \mapsto x^p$. Then

$$S(\chi^p) = S(\chi).$$

More generally, for any integer $c$ prime to the order of $\chi$, one has a Galois automorphism $\sigma_c$ acting on the values of $\chi$ by $\sigma_c(\chi(x)) = \chi(x)^c$, and $S(\chi)^c$ is related to $S(\chi^c)$.
