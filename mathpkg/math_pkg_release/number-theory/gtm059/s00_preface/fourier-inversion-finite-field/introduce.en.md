Theorem 1.1 of Lang's Cyclotomic Fields states that applying the Fourier transform twice recovers the original function composed with sign negation: $TTf = f^{-}$ where $f^{-}(x) = f(-x)$. The proof uses the orthogonality of additive characters: the sum $\sum_y \lambda(y(z+x))$ vanishes unless $z + x = 0$. This establishes that $T$ is an isomorphism on the space of functions on $F$, with inverse given by $T$ itself up to sign reversal.
