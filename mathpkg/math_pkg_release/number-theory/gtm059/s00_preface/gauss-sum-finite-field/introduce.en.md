The Gauss sum $S(\chi) = \sum_{x \in F^*} \chi(x)\lambda(x)$ intertwines a multiplicative character $\chi$ and an additive character $\lambda$ of a finite field $F = \mathbb{F}_q$. Gauss sums are fundamental objects in algebraic number theory, appearing in factorization of primes in cyclotomic fields, Stickelberger's theorem, and the functional equation of $L$-functions. They satisfy a rich set of identities relating products, complex conjugation, and Galois conjugation.
