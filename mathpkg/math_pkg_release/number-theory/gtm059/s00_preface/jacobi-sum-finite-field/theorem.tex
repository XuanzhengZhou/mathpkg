Let $F = \mathbb{F}_q$ be a finite field. For multiplicative characters $\chi, \psi: F^* \to \mathbb{C}^\times$ extended to $F$ as usual, the Jacobi sum $J(\chi, \psi)$ is defined by

$$J(\chi, \psi) = \sum_{x \in F^*} \chi(x) \psi(1 - x).$$

Equivalently, $J(\chi, \psi) = -\sum_{x \in F} \chi(x) \psi(1-x)$ depending on conventions. Note that $J(\chi, 1) = -\chi(-1)$ and $J(1, 1) = q - 2$.
