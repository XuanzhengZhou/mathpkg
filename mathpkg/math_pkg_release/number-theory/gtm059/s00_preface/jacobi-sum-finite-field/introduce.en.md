The Jacobi sum $J(\chi, \psi) = \sum_{x \in F^*} \chi(x)\psi(1-x)$ combines two multiplicative characters on a finite field. Jacobi sums are intimately related to Gauss sums via the identity $J(\chi, \psi) = S(\chi)S(\psi)/S(\chi\psi)$ when $\chi\psi \neq 1$. They play a central role in the arithmetic of cyclotomic fields, particularly in Stickelberger's theorem and in the determination of the decomposition of prime ideals.
