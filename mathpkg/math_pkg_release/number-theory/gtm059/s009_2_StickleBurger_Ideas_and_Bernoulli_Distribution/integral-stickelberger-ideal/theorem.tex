Let $p$ be a prime and $N = p^n$. Let $G = (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^\times$ and
$R = \mathbb{Z}[G]$. The augmentation ideal $I_G$ is the kernel of the augmentation
map $\varepsilon: R \to \mathbb{Z}$ defined by $\varepsilon(\sum a_\sigma \sigma) = \sum a_\sigma$.

The \textbf{integral Stickelberger ideal} $J = J(N)$ consists of elements
$\sum_{a \in G} x_a \sigma_a \in R$ such that

$$\sum_{a \in G} x_a a \equiv 0 \pmod{N}.$$

The \textbf{Stickelberger ideal} $S = S(N)$ is $R \cap \theta R$, where $\theta$ is the
Stickelberger element. The ideal $P(N)$ consists of elements $\sigma_c - c$ with
$(c, N) = 1$.
