Let $N$ be a prime. Let $T_N$ be the set of elements of $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$ having
exact period $N$ (the primitive elements). Let $A$ be the abelian group generated by
symbols $g(a)$ for $a \in T_N$, modulo the single relation

$$\sum_{a \in T_N} g(a) = 0.$$

Then:
(i) The elements $g(a)$ for $a \in T_N$ are free generators of $A$ (subject only to
the given relation).
(ii) There exists a universal distribution $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ taking
values in $A$ such that any distribution factors uniquely through $\varphi$.
