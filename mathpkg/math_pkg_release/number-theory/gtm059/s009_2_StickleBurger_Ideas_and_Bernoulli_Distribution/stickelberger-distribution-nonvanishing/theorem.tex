The Stickelberger distribution associated to the Bernoulli polynomial $B_k(X)$
(for $k \geq 1$) is nonvanishing on the space of distributions. In other words,
the Bernoulli distribution attains the maximal possible rank for an
odd distribution on the projective system $\{\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}\}$.

More precisely, for each $k \geq 1$, the elements $E_k(x)$ for primitive $x$ span
a space of dimension equal to the rank of the universal odd distribution, and the
Stickelberger sums $S_k(\chi) = \sum_{a} \chi(a) B_k(\langle a/N \rangle)$ are
non-zero for all odd characters $\chi$ of sufficiently large conductor.
