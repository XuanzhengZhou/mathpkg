A distribution on a projective system is a compatible family of functions that respects the summation over fibers of the connecting maps. This concept generalizes the classical notion of a measure and provides the framework for constructing Bernoulli distributions, which are central to the theory of Stickelberger ideals. The projective limit formulation allows distributions to be viewed as functionals on locally constant functions over a compact totally disconnected space.
