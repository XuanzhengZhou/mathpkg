The Bernoulli distribution on $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$ is the fundamental example of a distribution arising from Bernoulli polynomials. For each integer $k \geq 1$, the formula $E_k^{(N)}(x) = N^{k-1} B_k(\langle x/N \rangle)$ defines a $\mathbb{Q}$-valued distribution. Via a "smoothing" or "imprimitive" process (multiplying by $1 - c^k \sigma_c^{-1}$ for suitable $c$), one obtains $p$-integral distributions that are central to the construction of the Stickelberger ideal.
