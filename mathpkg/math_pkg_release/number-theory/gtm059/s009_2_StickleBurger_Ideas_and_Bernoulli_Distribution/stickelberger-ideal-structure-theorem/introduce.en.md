This theorem characterizes the Stickelberger ideal $S = R \cap \theta R$ in terms of the integral Stickelberger ideal $J^{(p)}$. The equality $S = J^{(p)}$ shows that the apparently analytic definition of $S$ (via the Stickelberger element $\theta$) has a purely algebraic description in terms of congruences on coefficients. This structure theorem is fundamental for computing indices and relating the Stickelberger ideal to class groups.
