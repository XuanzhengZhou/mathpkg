The space generated by the elements $g(x)$ with $x$ primitive of exact period $N$ is an $F[G]$-module. For a character $\chi$ of $G$ with conductor $m$, the idempotent $e_\chi$ acts on this space. The image $e_\chi \cdot g(x)$ is non-zero if and only if the Stickelberger sum $S(\chi) = \sum_{a} \chi(a) \langle a/N \rangle$ is non-zero.

By analyzing the kernel of the reduction map $G(N) \to G(M)$ for divisors $M \mid N$, one shows that the dimension contributed by characters of exact conductor $N$ is $\varphi(N)$. Summing over all divisors $d \mid N$ gives the total dimension $\sum_{d \mid N} \varphi(d)$.

The key lemma: for a character $\chi$ with conductor $m \mid N$, the element $e_\chi \cdot g(x)$ is proportional to $S(\chi)$, and the proportionality constant depends only on the level. Thus $S(\chi) = 0$ is the necessary and sufficient condition for $\chi$ to contribute zero to the distribution space.
