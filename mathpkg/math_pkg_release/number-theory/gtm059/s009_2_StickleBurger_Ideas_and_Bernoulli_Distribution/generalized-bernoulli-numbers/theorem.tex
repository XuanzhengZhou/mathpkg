Let $\chi$ be a Dirichlet character of conductor $f_\chi$. The \textbf{generalized
Bernoulli numbers} $B_{k,\chi}$ (also called \textbf{Leopoldt Bernoulli numbers}) are
defined by:

$$B_{k,\chi} = f_\chi^{k-1} \sum_{a=1}^{f_\chi} \chi(a) B_k\!\left(\frac{a}{f_\chi}\right).$$

In terms of the distribution integral:

$$\frac{1}{k} B_{k,\chi} = \int_{\mathbb{Z}_p^\times} \chi(x) x^{k-1} \, dE_1(x),$$

where $dE_1$ is the Bernoulli distribution.
