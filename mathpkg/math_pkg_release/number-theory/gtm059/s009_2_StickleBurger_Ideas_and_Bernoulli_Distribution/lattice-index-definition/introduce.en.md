The lattice index generalizes the notion of subgroup index to lattices in a rational vector space. It provides the correct framework for measuring the "size" of ideals in $\mathbb{Z}[G]$ such as the Stickelberger ideal. The key property that $(R : \theta R) = |\det(\theta)|$ connects the algebraic index computation to linear algebra over the group ring, allowing the use of character theory to factor the index into a product of generalized Bernoulli numbers.
