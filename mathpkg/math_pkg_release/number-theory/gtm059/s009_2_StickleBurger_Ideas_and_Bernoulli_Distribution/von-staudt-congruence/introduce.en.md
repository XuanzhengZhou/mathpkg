The von Staudt-Clausen theorem describes the fractional part of Bernoulli numbers: the denominator of $B_k$ is exactly the product of primes $p$ such that $p-1$ divides $k$. In $p$-adic terms, this means that for $k \equiv 0 \pmod{p-1}$, $B_k$ has a pole at $p$ with residue $-1/p$. This result is essential for understanding the integrality properties of the Stickelberger ideal.
