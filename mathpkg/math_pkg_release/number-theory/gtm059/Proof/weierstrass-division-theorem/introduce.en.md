The Weierstrass Division Theorem provides a Euclidean-style division algorithm for power series over complete local rings. Given a power series $f$ that is regular of order $n$ (its first $n$ coefficients lie in the maximal ideal and the $n$-th coefficient is a unit), any power series $g$ can be uniquely divided by $f$ yielding a quotient $q$ in the power series ring and a remainder $r$ of degree less than $n$. This is the foundational result underlying the Weierstrass Preparation Theorem and is essential for the study of power series rings over $p$-adic and local rings.
