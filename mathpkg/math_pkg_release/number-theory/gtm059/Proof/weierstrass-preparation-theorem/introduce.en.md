The Weierstrass Preparation Theorem states that any power series $f$ over a complete local ring that is regular of order $n$ can be factored uniquely as a monic polynomial of degree $n$ with coefficients in the maximal ideal, multiplied by a unit power series. This is a fundamental result in commutative algebra and $p$-adic analysis. It provides a crucial link between power series and polynomials, enabling the use of algebraic methods in analytic contexts. The theorem is central to the study of the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$.
