Let $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ be the Iwasawa algebra. A compact (resp. discrete) $\Lambda$-module $V$ is said to be of \textit{Iwasawa type} if there exist elements $v_1, \dots, v_k \in V$ such that, letting $U$ be the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-submodule of $V$ generated by the elements $(T-1)^j v_i$ for all $j \geq 0$ and $i = 1, \dots, k$, the quotient $V/U$ is finite. In particular, $V$ is finitely generated over $\Lambda$ and its $\Lambda$-torsion submodule is finite.
