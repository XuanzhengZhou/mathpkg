The units are generated by $u_i$, where the units in the filtration $\mu[1]$ (when $u_i \neq 0$) and the higher units of the quotient are considered. A triangular set of generators for these units consists of the elements $i_1, \dots, i_n$ with $i = 1, 2, \dots, n$ where $n$ is related to the $13$-th roots of unity (in the case of the special Lubin-Tate group). These elements form additive generators for $d(x_i)$ over $u_i$.

It suffices to prove the lemma for the symbol $[a_1 - 1\, s_1]a_2$ with appropriate values of $a_1$ and $s_1$, reducing the problem to the same verification for those specific parameters.

Starting with the symbol $[a_1 - 1\, s_1]$, since
$$a_1[1 - s_1] = a_2\left[\frac{1 - s_1^2}{1 + s_1^2}\right] = \frac{-j}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i^2,$$
we find
$$[a_1 - 1\, s_1] = \frac{-j}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} T_i\left(\frac{1}{1 + s_1^2} a_i^2\right) = -j \sum_{i=1}^{n} (a_i^2)a_i.$$

The above formal steps are justified: first, the sums taken modulo $s^*$ are actually finite; second, we have replaced $a_i$ by $v_i$ and vice versa to obtain the equality in $a_{i+1}^*$ where the symbol takes its values.

By Theorem 5 we have $[a_{i+1}^*]a_i = (a_{i+1}^*)a_i$. Therefore this latter sum is taken on $a_i$. Since $(x_i - 1) = 0$ by the local reciprocity law when $b_{i+1}x_i$ is sufficiently large, it suffices to prove the final result in Theorem 6.2.
