This theorem establishes the compatibility between addition in the Lubin-Tate formal group $A$ and ordinary addition in the ground field $G_a$, when both arguments have sufficiently large valuation. When the conditions on the valuations $\text{ord}_p(x)$ and $\text{ord}_p(y)$ are satisfied, the formal group addition and the usual field addition become interchangeable modulo the product $xy$, both for the Hilbert symbol and for the generalized bracket symbol. This interchangeability is a crucial technical tool for completing the proof of the explicit reciprocity law, as it allows one to manipulate expressions in the formal group using ordinary arithmetic.
