The augmentation homomorphism $\deg$ is the fundamental map from the group ring $R = \mathbf{Z}[G]$ to $\mathbf{Z}$ that sends a formal linear combination of group elements to the sum of its coefficients. In the context of Stickelberger ideals, the group $G$ is either $\mathbf{Z}(N)^*$ or its quotient by $\pm 1$, depending on the parity of $k$. The augmentation ideal $R_0 = \ker(\deg)$ consists of elements whose coefficient sum is zero, and plays a crucial role in the index computations of Stickelberger ideals in §3-§7.
