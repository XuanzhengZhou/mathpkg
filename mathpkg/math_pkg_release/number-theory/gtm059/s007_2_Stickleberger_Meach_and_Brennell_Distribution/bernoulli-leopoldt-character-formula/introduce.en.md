Theorem 2.4 generalizes the integral representation of Bernoulli numbers to the generalized Bernoulli-Leopoldt numbers $B_{n,\psi}$ attached to Dirichlet characters $\psi$ of finite order. The formula expresses $(1/n)B_{n,\psi}$ as an integral of $\psi(a)a^{n-1}$ against the integralized distribution $E_{1,c}$, with a denominator $1-\psi(c)c^n$ that is a $p$-adic unit when $\psi(c)c^n \neq 1$. This representation is essential for studying $p$-adic $L$-functions and Stickelberger ideals.
