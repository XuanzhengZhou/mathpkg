Theorem 3.1 provides product expressions linking the Gauss sums and $L$-values of primitive characters to the discriminant and class number invariants of a number field. These identities form the bridge between the analytic theory of $L$-functions and the arithmetic of cyclotomic fields. The key idea is that the discriminant and class number invariants factor according to the characters of the Galois group, allowing one to study the arithmetic of $K$ through the analytic properties of its associated $L$-series and their functional equations.
