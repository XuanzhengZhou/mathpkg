Let $K$ be a number field and let $\chi$ be a Dirichlet character modulo $m$. The $L$-series $L(s,\chi)$ for $\Re(s) > 1$ admits the Euler product factorization

$$L(s,\chi) = \prod_{p \nmid m} \left(1 - \frac{\chi(p)}{p^s}\right)^{-1}$$

where the product is taken over all primes $p$ not dividing $m$. For the Dedekind zeta function $\zeta_K(s)$ of $K$, one has the decomposition

$$\zeta_K(s) = \prod_{\chi} L(s,\chi^*)$$

where the product runs over all primitive characters $\chi^*$ induced by the characters of the Galois group of $K$ over $\mathbb{Q}$. Equivalently, for each prime $p$, the local factor decomposes as

$$\prod_{\mathfrak{p} \mid p} \left(1 - \frac{1}{N\mathfrak{p}^s}\right)^{-1} = \prod_{\chi} \left(1 - \frac{\chi(p)}{p^s}\right)^{-1}$$

where $\mathfrak{p}$ runs over the prime ideals of $K$ lying above $p$.
