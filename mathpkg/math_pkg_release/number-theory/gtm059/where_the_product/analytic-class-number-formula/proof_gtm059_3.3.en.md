The proof relies on the analytic continuation and functional equation of the Dedekind zeta function. From the decomposition of $L$-series, we have

$$\zeta_K(s) = \prod_{\chi} L(s, \chi^*)$$

where $\chi^*$ runs over primitive characters of the Galois group. Each $L(s,\chi^*)$ admits an analytic continuation to the whole complex plane except for a simple pole at $s=1$ when $\chi^*$ is the trivial character. Comparing residues at $s=1$ yields the product expression. The factor $2^{r_1}(2\pi)^{r_2}$ arises from the gamma factors in the functional equation, while $w$ accounts for the roots of unity. The regulator $R$ emerges from the determinant of the logarithmic embedding of units, and $\sqrt{|d|}$ appears from the transformation theory of theta functions or from the functional equation of $\zeta_K(s)$. For the real case ($K$ totally real, $r_2 = 0$), the even characters contribute to the product, while odd characters contribute in the imaginary case.
