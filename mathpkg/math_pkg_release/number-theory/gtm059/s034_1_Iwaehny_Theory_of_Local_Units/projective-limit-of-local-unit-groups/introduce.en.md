The projective limit $U = \varprojlim U_n$ of the local principal unit groups forms the fundamental object of study in Iwasawa theory of local units. Each $U_n$ is the group of units congruent to 1 modulo the maximal ideal in the $n$-th layer $K_n = \mathbb{Q}_p(\zeta_{p^n})$ of the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. The limit is taken with respect to the norm maps, yielding a compact $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module that also admits a natural action of the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda \cong \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$. The structure of $U$ as a $\Lambda$-module encodes deep arithmetic information about the growth of ideal class groups in the tower of fields.
