The proof follows from the properties of Gauss sums and the definition of generalized Bernoulli numbers. Let $S(\chi) = \sum_{a=1}^{p-1} \chi(a) \zeta_p^a$ denote the Gauss sum. By Stickelberger's theorem on the prime ideal factorization of Gauss sums, one obtains the congruence relations for $B_{1,\chi}$. The case $\chi = \omega$ follows from the fact that the Gauss sum $S(\omega)$ satisfies $S(\omega)^{p-1} = p$, which by the character sum formulas yields the stated congruence $p B_{1,\omega} \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$.
