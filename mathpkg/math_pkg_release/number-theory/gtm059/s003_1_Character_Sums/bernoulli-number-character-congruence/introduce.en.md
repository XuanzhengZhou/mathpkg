The first generalized Bernoulli numbers $B_{1,\chi}$ play a fundamental role in Iwasawa theory and the study of $p$-adic $L$-functions. These corollaries establish the basic $p$-adic congruence properties that distinguish the Teichm\"uller character $\omega$ from other Dirichlet characters. For $\chi \neq \omega$, the Bernoulli number is $p$-integral; for $\chi = \omega$, it has a simple pole at $p$ with residue $1/p$. These congruences are essential for the construction of the Stickelberger ideal and the proof of the analytic class number formula.
