Let $m$ be a positive integer and let $\chi_1, \chi_2$ be Dirichlet characters modulo $m$ with $\chi_1\chi_2 \neq 1$. The Jacobi sum $J(\chi_1, \chi_2)$ is defined by

$$J(\chi_1, \chi_2) = \sum_{a \in (\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z})^\times} \chi_1(a) \chi_2(1-a).$$

For a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_m)$ not dividing $m$, define the Hecke character (Grossencharakter) $\psi_{\chi_1,\chi_2}$ on fractional ideals prime to $m$ by multiplicativity, with values in the cyclotomic field, determined by the Jacobi sum.
