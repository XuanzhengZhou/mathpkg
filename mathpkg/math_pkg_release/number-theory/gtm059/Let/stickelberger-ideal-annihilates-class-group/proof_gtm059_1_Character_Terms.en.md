Let
$$x = \sum i_t j_h(i) m \in R$$
be an element of the Stickelberger ideal, with $i_t j_h(i) = \mathbb{Z}$, and the sum taken with only a finite number of nonzero coefficients. Then
$$\sum i_t j_h(i) = 0 \pmod{m}.$$

By Theorem 2.2 we have the factorization
$$\prod i_t j_h(i)^{p+1} = \psi^p$$
and it is immediately verified that the left-hand side lies in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)$ by using the properties of Gauss sums from the preceding section. This proves the theorem.
