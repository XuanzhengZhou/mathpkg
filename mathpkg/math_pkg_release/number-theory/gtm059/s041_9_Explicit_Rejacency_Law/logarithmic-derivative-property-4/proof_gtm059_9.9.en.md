The proof is similar to DL 3. We know that

$$
\lambda_i(u^{m-1})(X) = u^{m-1} \lambda_i(u)
$$

so by the chain rule,

$$
\lambda_i(u)^{m-1}(X) = u.
$$

We have the representations

$$a = g(s) = g[s]^{(n+1)} = g_{n}(s)$$

where

$$g(s) = g[s]^{n+1}X.$$

Since $a$ is assumed to be a unit, the power series with a unit, and so do the power series, so both power series can be used to compute the logarithm. The rest of the proof then follows immediately from the chain rule and the definition.
