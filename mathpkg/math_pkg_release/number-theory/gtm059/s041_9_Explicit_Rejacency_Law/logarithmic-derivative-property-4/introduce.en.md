This lemma (designated DL 4 in the text) establishes a congruence property for the logarithmic derivative $\lambda_i$ on units. For $m \geq 2$ and a unit $u$ in $K\mathbb{Z}$, the logarithmic derivative satisfies $\lambda_i(u) \equiv \lambda_i(u) \pmod{\tau_{u^m}}$. The proof uses the chain rule and the power series representation of the unit, following a similar pattern to DL 3. This congruence is essential for controlling the growth of the logarithmic derivative and is used in the proof of the explicit reciprocity law.
