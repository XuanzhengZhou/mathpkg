The Explicit Reciprocity Law provides an explicit formula for the norm residue symbol in terms of the logarithmic derivative. In the Lubin-Tate framework, the law expresses the norm coherence relation $N_{k+1,k}(-x_{k+1}) = -x_k$ equivalently through a trace pairing: $\frac{1}{Y(x_{k+1})} = T_{k+1,k}(\frac{1}{Y(x_{k+1})})$. This theorem extends the classical explicit reciprocity law of Artin-Hasse-Iwasawa and serves as a fundamental tool in local class field theory.
