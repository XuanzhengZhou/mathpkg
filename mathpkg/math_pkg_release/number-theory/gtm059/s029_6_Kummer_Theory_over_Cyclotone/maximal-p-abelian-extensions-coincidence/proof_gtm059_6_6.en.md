By Kummer theory, $G$ is a composite of cyclic extensions. Let $K_n(p)$ be the set of some $n \in K_n$. Then $a \in K_n$ for some $n$. We take $n \geq m$ and also such that

$$K_n(b^{n(p)}) = p^{n(p)}\text{ over }K_n.$$

Then $a$ necessarily has an ideal factorization

$$\langle a \rangle = a^{n(p)}$$

where $h$ is $p$-primary and $a$ is prime to $p$. Let $h$ be the class number of $K_n$ and write $h = p^A$ with $A$ prime to $p$. Then

$$\langle b^n \rangle = (b^n)^{f(p)}$$

where $(a)_n = a^n$ and $(f)_n = b^n$. Furthermore,

$$K_{n,p}(b^{n(p)}) = K_{n,p}(b^{n(p)}) = K_{n,p}(b^{n(p)})$$

and also

$$K_{n,p}(b^{n(p)}) < K_{n,p}(b^{n(p)}) \cdot (f(p)^{n(p)}, E_{n,p}^{n(p)}).$$

This proves the lemma.
