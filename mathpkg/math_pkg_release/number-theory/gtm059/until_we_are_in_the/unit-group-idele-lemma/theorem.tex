Let $K$ be a number field and $p$ a fixed prime. Let $U_p = \prod_{\mathfrak{p} \nmid p} U_\mathfrak{p}$ be the group of unit ideles (ideles whose components at primes not dividing $p$ are units), and let $U_p^\perp$ be its orthogonal complement in the idele group. Let $E = K^\times \cap \prod_{\mathfrak{p}\mid p} K_\mathfrak{p}^\times$ be the group of global units (embedded diagonally).

Then

$$
U_p \cap \overline{K^\times} = \overline{E}
$$

where the closures are taken in the idele topology. More precisely, let $U^{(n)}$ be the group of ideles whose components at primes above $p$ are $\equiv 1 \bmod \mathfrak{p}^n$. Then the groups

$$
U^{(n)} \cap U_p^\perp K^\times
$$

form a fundamental system of neighborhoods for the closure $U_p^\perp K^\times$, and

$$
\bigcap_n \left(U^{(n)} \cap U_p^\perp K^\times\right) = \overline{E}.
$$

Taking the intersection over all $p$ completes the proof.
