Let $K_\infty / K$ be a $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. Then:

(i) Only finitely many prime ideals of $K$ ramify in $K_\infty / K$. If a prime ramifies, it is totally ramified (i.e., its ramification index is a power of $p$, and after a finite layer it becomes totally ramified).

(ii) There exists an integer $n_0 \geq 0$ such that for all $n \geq n_0$, exactly the same finite set of primes of $K$ ramify in $K_n/K$, and each such prime is totally ramified in $K_\infty / K_{n_0}$.

In particular, there exists a finite layer $K_{n_0}$ such that the extension $K_\infty / K_{n_0}$ is unramified at all primes not dividing $p$.
