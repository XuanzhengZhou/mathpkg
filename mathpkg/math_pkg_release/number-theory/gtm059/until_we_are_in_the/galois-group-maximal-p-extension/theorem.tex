Let $K$ be a number field and $p$ a fixed prime. Let $H$ be the $p$-Hilbert class field of $K$ (the maximal unramified abelian $p$-extension). Let $M = M(K)$ be the maximal $p$-abelian unramified extension. Then we have an isomorphism (up to a finite kernel):

$$
\operatorname{Gal}(M/H) \sim U_p^\perp / \overline{E}
$$

where $U_p^\perp$ is the orthogonal complement of $U_p = \prod_{\mathfrak{p} \nmid p} U_\mathfrak{p}$ in the idele group, $E = \mathcal{O}_K^\times$ is the group of global units, and the bar denotes closure in the idele topology. More precisely, there is a quasi-isomorphism (homomorphism with finite kernel and cokernel)

$$
\operatorname{Gal}(M/K) \sim U_p^\perp / \overline{E}.
$$

Furthermore, $U_p$ contains an open subgroup of finite index isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_p^{r_2+1}$, where $r_2$ is the number of complex places of $K$. Consequently, the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank of $\operatorname{Gal}(M/K)$ satisfies

$$
\operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb{Z}_p} \operatorname{Gal}(M/K) \geq r_2 + 1.
$$

The Leopoldt conjecture asserts that this rank is exactly $r_2 + 1$.
