$M(K)$ is the maximal abelian $p$-extension of a number field $K$ that is unramified at all primes. Its Galois group over $K$ is isomorphic, via the Artin reciprocity map of class field theory, to the $p$-primary part of the idele class group. The maximal unramified abelian $p$-extension (the $p$-Hilbert class field) is a subextension whose Galois group is the $p$-part of the ideal class group. These objects are fundamental in Iwasawa's study of class groups in $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions.
