Let $K$ be a number field and let $p$ be a fixed prime number. Define

$$
M(K) = \text{the maximal } p\text{-abelian unramified extension of } K.
$$

That is, $M(K)$ is the maximal abelian extension of $K$ whose Galois group is a pro-$p$-group and in which every prime of $K$ (including archimedean primes) is unramified. By class field theory, the Galois group $\operatorname{Gal}(M(K)/K)$ is canonically isomorphic to the $p$-primary part of the idele class group of $K$ modulo the connected component of the identity:

$$
\operatorname{Gal}(M(K)/K) \cong \left( \mathbf{J}_K / K^\times \overline{U_p} \right)[p^\infty]
$$

where $\mathbf{J}_K$ is the idele group, $U_p = \prod_{\mathfrak{p} \nmid p} U_\mathfrak{p}$ is the group of unit ideles at primes not dividing $p$, and the bar denotes closure in the idele topology.

The $p$-Hilbert class field $H$ is the maximal unramified abelian $p$-extension of $K$, and its Galois group is isomorphic to the $p$-primary part of the ideal class group $\operatorname{Cl}(K)[p^\infty]$.
