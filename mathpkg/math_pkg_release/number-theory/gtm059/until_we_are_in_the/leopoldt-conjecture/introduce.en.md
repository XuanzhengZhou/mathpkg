The Leopoldt conjecture states that the $p$-adic regulator of a number field $K$ is non-zero, or equivalently, that the global units of $K$ remain $\mathbb{Z}_p$-linearly independent when embedded $p$-adically. This conjecture is crucial for Iwasawa theory: it implies that the maximal $p$-abelian unramified extension of $K$ has $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank exactly $r_2 + 1$, and consequently that $K$ admits exactly $r_2 + 1$ independent $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions. The conjecture is proved for abelian extensions of $\mathbb{Q}$.
