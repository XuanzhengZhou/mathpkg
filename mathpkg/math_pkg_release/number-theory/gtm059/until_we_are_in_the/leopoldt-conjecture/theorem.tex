**Leopoldt Conjecture.** Let $K$ be a number field and let $p$ be a prime number. Let $r_1$ (resp. $r_2$) be the number of real (resp. complex) places of $K$, so that $[K:\mathbb{Q}] = r_1 + 2r_2$. Then the $p$-adic regulator of $K$ is non-zero. Equivalently:

The $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank of the closure $\overline{E}$ of the global units $E = \mathcal{O}_K^\times$ in the product $\prod_{\mathfrak{p}\mid p} \mathcal{O}_{K_\mathfrak{p}}^\times$ (under the diagonal embedding) equals the $\mathbb{Z}$-rank of $E$, which is $r_1 + r_2 - 1$ by Dirichlet's unit theorem.

As a consequence, the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-rank of $\operatorname{Gal}(M(K)/K)$ is exactly $r_2 + 1$, where $M(K)$ is the maximal $p$-abelian unramified extension of $K$.

The Leopoldt conjecture is known to hold for abelian extensions of $\mathbb{Q}$ (a theorem of Brumer), but remains open in general.
