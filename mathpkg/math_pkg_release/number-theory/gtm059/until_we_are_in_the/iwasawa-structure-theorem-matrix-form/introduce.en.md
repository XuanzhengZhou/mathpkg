The Iwasawa structure theorem in matrix form provides an analogue of the Smith normal form for matrices over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$. Given a finitely generated $\Lambda$-module presented by a matrix of relations, admissible row and column operations reduce this matrix to a diagonal form whose non-zero entries are distinguished polynomials. This theorem is the foundation for the classification of $\Lambda$-modules up to pseudo-isomorphism and is central to Iwasawa's theory of $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions.
