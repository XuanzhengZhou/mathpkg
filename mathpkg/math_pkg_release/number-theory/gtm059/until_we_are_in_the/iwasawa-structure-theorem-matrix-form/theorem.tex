Let $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[X]]$ be the Iwasawa algebra, and let $R$ be an $m \times n$ matrix with entries in $\Lambda$, viewed as a matrix of relations for a finitely generated $\Lambda$-module. Then $R$ can be transformed by a finite number of admissible row and column operations into a diagonal matrix $R'$ of the form

$$
R' = \begin{pmatrix}
f_1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & f_2 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & f_r & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0
\end{pmatrix}
$$

where each $f_i$ is either $0$ or a distinguished polynomial in $\mathbb{Z}_p[X] \subset \Lambda$, and $f_1 \mid f_2 \mid \cdots \mid f_r$ (divisibility in $\Lambda$). The number $r$ of non-zero rows is uniquely determined by the module.
