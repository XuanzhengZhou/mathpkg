In the theory of $p$-adic measures and the $p$-adic $L$-function, Lang introduces a sup norm on the space of $p$-adic measures on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ and the associated space of power series. The norm of a measure $f$ is defined as the supremum of the absolute values of its values on elements of $\mathbb{Z}_p$, while the norm of a point $x$ (representing the measure's values on cosets) is the supremum of its coordinates. This norm structure is fundamental for establishing continuity properties and convergence results in the construction of the $p$-adic $L$-function.
