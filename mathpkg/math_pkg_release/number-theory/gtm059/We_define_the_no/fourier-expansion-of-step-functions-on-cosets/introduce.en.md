In the context of operations on $p$-adic measures, Lang introduces the Fourier expansion of step functions that are constant on the cosets of $p^N \mathbb{Z}_p$. This expansion expresses the action of the measure-valued operator $\psi$ on such a step function as a linear combination of the functions $(1+X)^j$, with coefficients $\phi_j(u)$ obtained by a discrete Fourier transform. This construction provides the link between $p$-adic measures and Dirichlet characters, which is essential for the interpolation properties of the $p$-adic $L$-function.
