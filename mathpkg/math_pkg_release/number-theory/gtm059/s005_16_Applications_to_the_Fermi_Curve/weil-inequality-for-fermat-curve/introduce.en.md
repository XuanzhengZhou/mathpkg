Weil's results on the zeta functions of curves over finite fields apply concretely to the Fermat curve. The zeta function factorizes as a product of Hecke $L$-series, each of which is linear in $T$ with coefficient of absolute value $\sqrt{q}$. This factorization yields explicit bounds on the number of points on the Fermat curve over finite fields, and connects the arithmetic of the curve to the theory of cyclotomic characters.
