For a variety $V$ defined over the finite field $\mathbf{F}_q$ with $q$ elements, let $N_\nu$ denote the number of $\mathbf{F}_{q^\nu}$-rational points of $V$. The \textit{zeta function} of $V$ is the formal power series
$$Z(V, T) = \exp\left( \sum_{\nu=1}^{\infty} N_\nu \frac{T^\nu}{\nu} \right).$$
It satisfies $Z(V, 0) = 1$ and has a product expansion governed by the Weil conjectures.
