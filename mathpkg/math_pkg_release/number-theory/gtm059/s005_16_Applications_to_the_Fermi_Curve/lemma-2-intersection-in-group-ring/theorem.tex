Let $R = \mathbf{Z}[G]$ be the group ring with $G = \operatorname{Gal}(\mathbf{Q}(\zeta_m)/\mathbf{Q})$. Let $S$ be the Stickelberger ideal and let $R^- = (1-e)R$ be the minus part. Then
$$S \cap R = S \cap R^-$$
and the natural inclusion $S \subset R^-$ holds, i.e., the Stickelberger ideal is contained in the minus part of the group ring.
