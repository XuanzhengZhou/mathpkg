The zeta function of the Fermat curve admits an explicit factorization into a product of Hecke $L$-series, one for each pair of characters $(\chi^j, \chi^k)$. This is a concrete instance of the decomposition of the zeta function of a diagonal hypersurface. Each $L$-factor corresponds to a Jacobi sum $J(\chi^j, \chi^k)$, and the overall factorization provides the link between the arithmetic of the Fermat curve and the analytic theory of Hecke $L$-functions over cyclotomic fields.
