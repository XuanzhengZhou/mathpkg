Let $p$ be a prime, $q = p^f$, and let $\chi$ be a character of $F_q^*$. Let $\mathfrak{P}$ be a prime ideal above $p$ in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{q-1}, \zeta_p)$.

For an integer $k \geq 0$ with $p$-adic expansion $k = k_0 + k_1 p + \dots + k_t p^t$ where $0 \leq k_i \leq p-1$, define the $p$-adic digit sum
$$s(k) = k_0 + k_1 + \dots + k_t.$$

Then the $\mathfrak{P}$-adic valuation of the Gauss sum is given by:
$$\operatorname{ord}_{\mathfrak{P}} S(\chi) = \frac{1}{p-1} \sum_{a \in F_p^*} a \cdot s\left( \langle a^{-1} \chi \rangle \right),$$
where the sum extends over a set of representatives for $F_p^*$, and $\langle \cdot \rangle$ denotes the least positive residue modulo $q-1$.

In particular, for a suitably normalized Gauss sum, one has the congruence
$$\frac{S(\chi)}{(\zeta_p - 1)^{s(\chi)}} \equiv \frac{-\chi(-1)}{\prod k_i!} \pmod{\mathfrak{P}},$$
where $s(\chi)$ is the digit sum associated to the character $\chi$.
