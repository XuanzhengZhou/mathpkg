Let $p$ be a prime, and let $\mathbb{F}_p$ be the field with $p$ elements. The Teichmüller character is the unique multiplicative character
$$\omega: \mathbb{F}_p^* \to \mathbb{Z}_p^*$$
such that for every $a \in \mathbb{F}_p^*$, the value $\omega(a)$ is the unique $(p-1)$-th root of unity in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ satisfying the congruence
$$\omega(a) \equiv a \pmod{p}.$$

Equivalently, the Teichmüller character generates the character group of $\mathbb{F}_p^*$ and may be written in the form
$$\omega(a) = \langle a \rangle$$
where $\langle a \rangle$ denotes the unique $(p-1)$-th root of unity congruent to $a$ modulo $p$.
