This lemma records key properties of the formal derivative $\lambda_d'(X)$ of the Lubin-Tate logarithm. It shows that the derivative has integral coefficients, that the logarithm admits a specific $p$-adic expansion reminiscent of the Artin-Hasse exponential, and that for sufficiently large $q$ the derivative is congruent to $1$ modulo $X^q$. These properties are essential for establishing convergence of the exponential map and for understanding the structure of the formal group over its domain of convergence.
