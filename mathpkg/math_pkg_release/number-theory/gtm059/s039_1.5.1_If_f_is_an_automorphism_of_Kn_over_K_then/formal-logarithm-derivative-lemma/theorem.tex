Let $\lambda_d(X)$ be the formal logarithm associated to a Lubin-Tate formal group with respect to a prime element $\pi_d$.

(i) The formal derivative $\lambda_d'(X)$ has coefficients in the ring of integers $\mathfrak{o}_d$.

(ii) The series $\lambda_d(X)$ can be written in the form

$$\lambda_d(X) = \sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \frac{X^{p^j}}{\pi_d^j} \pmod{\mathfrak{o}_d}$$

and converges on the maximal ideal. In particular, $\lambda_d'(0) = 1$.

(iii) Suppose $q \geq 3$ and $X^q \nmid \lambda_d(X)$. Then

$$\lambda_d'(X) \equiv 1 \pmod{X^q}.$$
