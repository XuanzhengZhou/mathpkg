Let $K$ be a finite extension of $\mathbb{Q}_p$ with ring of integers $\mathcal{O}_K$ and uniformizer $\pi$. A Lubin-Tate formal group law over $\mathcal{O}_K$ relative to $\pi$ is a formal group law $F(X,Y) \in \mathcal{O}_K[[X,Y]]$ such that $F(X,Y) \equiv X+Y \pmod{\pi}$ and the endomorphism $[\pi]_F(X) \equiv \pi X \pmod{X^2}$ satisfies $[\pi]_F(X) \equiv X^q \pmod{\pi}$ where $q = |\mathcal{O}_K/(\pi)|$.
