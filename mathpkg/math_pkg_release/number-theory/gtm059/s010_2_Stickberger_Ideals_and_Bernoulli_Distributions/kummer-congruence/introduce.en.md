The Kummer congruence is a fundamental $p$-adic property of Bernoulli numbers. It states that the expressions $B_k/k$ are $p$-adically continuous as functions of $k$ in each residue class modulo $p-1$ (excluding multiples of $p-1$). This was Kummer's key observation that led to the construction of $p$-adic zeta functions and his work on Fermat's Last Theorem. The congruence can be proved elegantly using the integral representation of Bernoulli numbers in terms of the Bernoulli distribution.
