Generalized Bernoulli numbers, introduced by Leopoldt, extend the classical Bernoulli numbers by twisting them with a Dirichlet character. For a primitive character $\chi$ modulo $N$, the generalized Bernoulli number $B_{k,\chi}$ is defined as a weighted sum of values of the Bernoulli polynomial at rational arguments. These numbers appear in the formula for the value of the Dirichlet $L$-function at negative integers: $L(1-k, \chi) = -B_{k,\chi}/k$. They are essential in Iwasawa theory and the study of $p$-adic $L$-functions.
