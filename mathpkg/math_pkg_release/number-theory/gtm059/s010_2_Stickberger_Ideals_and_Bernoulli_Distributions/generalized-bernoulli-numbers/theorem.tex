Let $f$ be a function on $\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z}$, extended periodically. The \text{generalized Bernoulli polynomials} (relative to $f$) are defined by
$$
f B_k(X) = N^{k-1} \sum_{a=1}^{N} f(a) \, B_k\!\left(\frac{X + a}{N}\right).
$$
The \text{generalized Bernoulli number} is the constant term:
$$
B_{k,f} = f B_k(0).
$$
In particular, when $f = \chi$ is a primitive Dirichlet character modulo $N$, this yields the \text{Leopoldt generalized Bernoulli numbers}:
$$
B_{k,\chi} = N^{k-1} \sum_{a=1}^{N} \chi(a) \, B_k\!\left(\frac{a}{N}\right).
$$
