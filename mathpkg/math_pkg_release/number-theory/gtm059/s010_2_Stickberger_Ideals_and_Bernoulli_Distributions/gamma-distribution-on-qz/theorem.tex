Define
$$
G(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{t}} \, \Gamma(t)
$$
viewed as a map on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ with values in the factor group $\mathbb{C}^\times / \overline{\mathbb{Q}}^\times$ of the multiplicative group of complex numbers modulo algebraic numbers. The classical multiplication formula for the gamma function,
$$
\prod_{a=0}^{N-1} \Gamma\!\left(t + \frac{a}{N}\right) = (2\pi)^{(N-1)/2} N^{\frac{1}{2} - Nt} \, \Gamma(Nt),
$$
shows that $G$ defines an odd distribution. Rohrlich has conjectured that $G$ is the universal odd distribution with values in groups where multiplication by $2$ is invertible.
