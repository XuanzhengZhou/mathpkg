For a character $\chi$ of $\mathbb{Z}(m)^\times$ and a character $\lambda$ of $\mathbb{Z}(m)$, define the \text{Gauss sum}
$$
S(\chi, \lambda) = \sum_{x \in \mathbb{Z}(m)^\times} \chi(x) \lambda(x).
$$
When $m = \prod p_i^{n_i}$ is a product of prime powers, the ring decomposes as
$$
\mathbb{Z}(m) \cong \prod \mathbb{Z}(p_i^{n_i}), \quad \mathbb{Z}(m)^\times \cong \prod \mathbb{Z}(p_i^{n_i})^\times,
$$
and characters decompose accordingly: $\chi = \prod \chi_i$, $\lambda = \prod \lambda_i$. The Gauss sum factorizes as
$$
S(\chi, \lambda) = \prod_i S(\chi_i, \lambda_i).
$$
If $\chi$ is not primitive to the conductor, we set $S(\chi, \lambda) = 0$ when $\lambda$ is not prime to the conductor.
