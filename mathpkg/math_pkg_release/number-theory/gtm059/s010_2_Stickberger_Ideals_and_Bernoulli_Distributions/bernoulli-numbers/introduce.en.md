Bernoulli numbers are rational numbers that appear in many areas of mathematics, including number theory, analysis, and topology. They are defined by the exponential generating function $t/(e^t - 1)$. The Bernoulli numbers with odd index greater than 1 vanish identically. They play a crucial role in the study of cyclotomic fields, particularly through the Stickelberger relation and the theory of $p$-adic $L$-functions.
