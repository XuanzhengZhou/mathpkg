The Bernoulli distribution is the fundamental example of an ordinary distribution on the projective system $\{\mathbb{Z}/M\mathbb{Z}\}$. It is constructed from the Bernoulli polynomials via the formula $E_k^{(M)}(x) = M^{k-1} B_k(\langle x/M \rangle)$. The distribution relation satisfied by the Bernoulli polynomials guarantees that this family is compatible under the transition maps. The Bernoulli distribution is rational-valued and plays a central role in the theory of Stickelberger ideals, $p$-adic $L$-functions, and the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic zeta function.
