Let $\chi$ be a character of $\mathbb{Z}(m)^\times$ with conductor $f_\chi$, and let $\lambda$ be a character of $\mathbb{Z}(m)$. If $\chi$ is primitive (i.e., its conductor equals $m$) and $\lambda$ is not primitive (i.e., does not have exact period $m$), then the Gauss sum vanishes:
$$
S(\chi, \lambda) = 0.
$$
More generally, if $d \mid m$ and $\chi$ factors through $\mathbb{Z}(d)^\times$ but $\lambda$ does not factor through $\mathbb{Z}(d)$, then $S(\chi, \lambda) = 0$.
