This theorem establishes that every Liouville number is transcendental, providing a concrete method for producing transcendental numbers. The proof is a direct application of Liouville's approximation theorem: if a Liouville number $\theta$ were algebraic of degree $n$, it would satisfy both $|\theta - h_r/k_r| < 1/k_r^r$ (by definition) and $|\theta - h_r/k_r| > C(\theta)/k_r^n$ (by Liouville's theorem), leading to $0 < C(\theta) < 1/k_r^{r-n}$, a contradiction for sufficiently large $r$.
