This theorem, a one-dimensional case of Kronecker's approximation theorem, shows that for any irrational $\theta$, the sequence of fractional parts $\{n\theta\}$ is dense in the unit interval $[0,1]$. This means the linear form $\theta x + y$ can be made arbitrarily close to any prescribed $\alpha \in [0,1]$ by choosing integers $x$ and $y$ appropriately. The proof constructs an increasing equally-spaced chain of fractional parts that subdivides the unit interval into subintervals of length less than $\varepsilon$.
