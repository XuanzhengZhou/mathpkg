Let $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_k$ be arbitrary real numbers such that the numbers $1, \alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_k$ are linearly independent over the rationals. Then for any real numbers $x_1, \ldots, x_k$ and any $\varepsilon > 0$, there exist integers $q > 0$ and $p_1, \ldots, p_k$ such that

$$|q\alpha_j - p_j - x_j| < \varepsilon \quad \text{for } j = 1, 2, \ldots, k.$$
