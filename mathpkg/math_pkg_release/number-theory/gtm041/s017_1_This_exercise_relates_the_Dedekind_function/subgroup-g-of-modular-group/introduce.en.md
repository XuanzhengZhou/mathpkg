The subgroup $G$ of the full modular group $\Gamma = \text{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$ is generated by the inversion $S\tau = -1/\tau$ and the square of the translation $T^2\tau = \tau + 2$. This subgroup plays an important role in the transformation theory of the Jacobi theta function $\vartheta(\tau)$, as $\vartheta$ is periodic with period $2$ (so it is invariant under $T^2$) and satisfies a functional equation under $S$. The subgroup $G$ has index $3$ in $\Gamma$ and consists of matrices $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$ with $a \equiv d \pmod{2}$ and $b \equiv c \pmod{2}$.
