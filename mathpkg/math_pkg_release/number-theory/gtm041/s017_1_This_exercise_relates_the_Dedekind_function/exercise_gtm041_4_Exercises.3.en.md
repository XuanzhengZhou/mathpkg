Let $p$ be a prime and let $k$ be an integer, $1 \leq k \leq p - 1$. Show that there exists an integer $h$ such that

$$\tau^{12}\Delta\left(\frac{\tau + h}{p}\right)$$

can be expressed in terms of $\Delta(\tau)$ and other modular forms. (This exercise is part of Mordell's proof [28] of the multiplicativity of Ramanujan's function $\tau(n)$.)

Recall that

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \tau(n)e^{2\pi i n\tau} = (2\pi)^{-12}\Delta(\tau) = e^{2\pi i\tau} \prod_{m=1}^{\infty} (1 - e^{2\pi i m\tau})^{24}.$$
