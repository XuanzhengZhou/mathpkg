Lehner derived a family of congruences for the coefficients $c(n)$ of the modular function $j(\tau)$, which generalize Ramanujan-type congruences for the partition function. These congruences state that for any $\alpha \geq 1$, the coefficient $c(p^\alpha n)$ is divisible by a power of the prime $p$, for $p \in \{2, 3, 5, 7\}$. The exponents in the moduli grow linearly with $\alpha$, with different rates for each prime. Notably, no such congruence exists for $p = 13$, since $c(13)$ is known not to be divisible by $13$.
