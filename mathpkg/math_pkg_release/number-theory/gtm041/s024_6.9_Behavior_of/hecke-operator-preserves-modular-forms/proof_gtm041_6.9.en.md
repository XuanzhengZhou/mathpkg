If $f \in M_k$ the definition of $T_n$ shows that $T_n f$ is analytic everywhere in $H$. Theorem 6.6 shows that $T_n f$ has a Fourier expansion of the required form and that $T_n f$ is analytic at $i\infty$. And Theorem 6.10 shows that $T_n f$ has the proper behavior under transformations of $\Gamma$. Finally, if $f$ is a cusp form, the Fourier expansion in Theorem 6.6 shows that $T_n f$ is also a cusp form.
