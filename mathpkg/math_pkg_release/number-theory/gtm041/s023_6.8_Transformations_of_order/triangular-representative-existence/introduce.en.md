Theorem 6.7 establishes that every equivalence class in $\Gamma(n)$ under the left action of $\Gamma$ contains a representative that is an upper triangular matrix. This is a key structural result because triangular matrices are far easier to work with than general integer matrices. Given an arbitrary transformation $A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ in $\Gamma(n)$, one can find integers $r, s$ such that $ra + sc = 0$ with $\gcd(r,s)=1$, extend to a matrix $V \in \Gamma$, and obtain $VA$ in triangular form.
