A complete system of nonequivalent elements in $\Gamma(n)$ is given by the set of transformations of triangular form
$$\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ 0 & d \end{pmatrix},$$
where $d$ runs through the positive divisors of $n$ and, for each fixed $d$, $a = n/d$, and $b$ runs through a complete residue system modulo $d$.
