This identity is the special case $k = 1$ of Equation (16) of Lemma 2. It relates the sum of $\lambda(nz)$ to the sum of $\lambda(n/z)$ with a logarithmic correction term. The formula is obtained from Iseki's formula by setting $\beta = 0$ and taking the limit $\alpha \to 0^+$, which requires a delicate treatment of the $r = 0$ terms whose individual limits are infinite but whose difference converges to $-\log z$. Because $\lambda(x)$ is periodic with period $i$, the integer shifts $ih$ and $iH$ are irrelevant when $k = 1$, making this the cleanest instance of the transformation law.
