Use the series in (51) to show that

$$\tau^{-2} G_2\left(\frac{-1}{\tau}\right) = 2\zeta(2) + \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(m+n\tau)^2},$$

the iterated series in (52) being the same as that in (51) except with the order of summation reversed. Therefore, proving (50) is equivalent to showing that

$$\sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(m+n\tau)^2} = \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(m+n\tau)^2} - \frac{2\pi i}{\tau}.$$
