Two general Dirichlet series with the same exponent sequence $\Lambda$ are said to be equivalent relative to a basis $B$ if their coefficients differ by a unimodular factor determined by a real sequence $Y$ through the Bohr matrix $R$ expressing $\Lambda$ in terms of $B$. This equivalence relation, introduced by Harald Bohr, is fundamental to the theory of general Dirichlet series and plays a central role in Bohr's equivalence theorem, which classifies Dirichlet series taking the same set of values in certain half-planes.
