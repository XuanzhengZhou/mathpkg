The subspace $M_{k,0} \subset M_k$ consists of all cusp forms of weight $k$, i.e., entire modular forms that vanish at the cusp $i\infty$. By the canonical basis from Theorem 6.3, a cusp form is exactly a linear combination where the coefficient $a_0$ of the pure Eisenstein term $G_k$ is zero. This immediately yields the dimension relation $\dim M_{k,0} = \dim M_k - 1$, showing that cusp forms form a hyperplane in the space of entire modular forms.
