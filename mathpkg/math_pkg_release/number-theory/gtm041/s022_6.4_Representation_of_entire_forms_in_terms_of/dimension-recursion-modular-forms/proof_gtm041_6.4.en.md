From Theorem 6.3 we have $\dim M_{k,0} = \dim M_k - 1$, since the cusp forms are those sums with $a_0 = 0$. From the isomorphism $T: M_{k-12} \to M_{k,0}$ established by multiplication by $\Delta$, we have $\dim M_{k,0} = \dim M_{k-12}$ for $k \geq 12$. Combining these two equalities yields
$$
\dim M_k = 1 + \dim M_{k-12}
$$
for $k \geq 12$. The base values $\dim M_k = 1, 0, 1, 1, 1, 1$ for $k = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10$ are verified directly from the definition of entire modular forms and the valence formula.
