If $k \geq 12$ and $f \in M_{k,0}$, then $f$ vanishes at $i\infty$. Since $\Delta$ has a first-order zero at $i\infty$ and no other zeros in $H$, the quotient $h = f/\Delta$ is holomorphic on $H$ and at $i\infty$, hence $h \in M_{k-12}$. Conversely, if $h \in M_{k-12}$, then $f = \Delta h$ vanishes at $i\infty$, so $f \in M_{k,0}$. Thus $f \in M_{k,0}$ if and only if $f = \Delta h$ for some $h \in M_{k-12}$. The map $T: M_{k-12} \to M_{k,0}$ defined by $T(h) = \Delta h$ is clearly linear and injective (since $\Delta$ is not identically zero), and the above shows it is surjective. Hence $T$ is an isomorphism. Consequently, $\dim M_{k,0} = \dim M_{k-12}$ for $k \geq 12$.
