Two quadratic forms $Q(x, y) = ax^2 + bxy + cy^2$ and $Q_1(x', y') = a'x'^2 + b'x'y' + c'y'^2$ are called equivalent if there exists a unimodular matrix $\begin{pmatrix} \alpha & \beta \\ \gamma & \delta \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$ such that under the transformation
$$x = \alpha x' + \beta y', \quad y = \gamma x' + \delta y',$$
the form $Q(x, y)$ is transformed into $Q_1(x', y')$.
