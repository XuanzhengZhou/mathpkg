The congruence subgroup $\Gamma^{(n)}$ is not merely a subgroup of the modular group $\Gamma$, but a normal subgroup. This normality follows from the compatibility of matrix congruence modulo $n$ with conjugation, and it ensures that the quotient group $\Gamma/\Gamma^{(n)}$ is well-defined, leading to the theory of modular forms of level $n$.
