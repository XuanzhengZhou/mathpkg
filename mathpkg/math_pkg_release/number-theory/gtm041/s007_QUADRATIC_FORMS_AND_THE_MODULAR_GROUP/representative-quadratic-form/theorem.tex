Consider a quadratic form $Q(x, y) = ax^2 + bxy + cy^2$ with discriminant $d = 4ac - b^2 > 0$, $a > 0$, and $c > 0$. The associated quadratic polynomial $f(z) = az^2 + bz + c$ has two complex roots. The root $\tau$ with positive imaginary part ($\text{Im}(\tau) > 0$) is called the representative of the quadratic form $Q(x, y)$.
