Matrix congruence modulo $n$ extends the notion of integer congruence to $2 \times 2$ unimodular matrices by requiring entrywise congruence. This equivalence relation is compatible with matrix multiplication and inversion, making it a congruence relation in the algebraic sense. It is the foundation for defining congruence subgroups of the modular group.
