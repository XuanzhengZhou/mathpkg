The index of a subgroup $H$ in a group $G$ is the number of distinct left (or right) cosets of $H$ in $G$. In the context of the modular group, the index of $\Gamma^{(n)}$ in $\Gamma$ is finite and plays a crucial role in the theory of modular forms, as it measures the number of inequivalent cusps and determines the dimension of spaces of modular forms.
