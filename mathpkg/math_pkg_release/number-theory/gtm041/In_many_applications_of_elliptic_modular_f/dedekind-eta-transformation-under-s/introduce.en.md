Theorem 3.1 gives the transformation formula for the Dedekind eta function under the inversion $S\tau = -1/\tau$, one of the two generators of the modular group. The formula states $\eta(-1/\tau) = (-i\tau)^{1/2} \eta(\tau)$, where the square root is taken to be positive on the positive real axis. This chapter presents two proofs: a short proof by C. L. Siegel using residue calculus, and a second proof deriving it as a special case of the general functional equation for $\eta((a\tau+b)/(c\tau+d))$ with $c > 0$. A third proof via interchange of summation in a conditionally convergent iterated series is outlined in the exercises.
