The modular group, denoted $\Gamma$, is the group of all M\"obius transformations $\tau \mapsto (a\tau + b)/(c\tau + d)$ where $a, b, c, d$ are integers and $ad - bc = 1$. It is isomorphic to $\mathrm{PSL}(2, \mathbb{Z})$, the projective special linear group of $2 \times 2$ integer matrices with determinant $1$ modulo $\pm I$. The modular group acts on the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H} = \{\tau \in \mathbb{C} : \operatorname{Im}(\tau) > 0\}$ by these fractional linear transformations. This group is fundamental to the theory of modular forms, as modular forms are defined by their transformation behavior under elements of $\Gamma$.
