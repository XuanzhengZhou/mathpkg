The discriminant function $\Delta(\tau)$ is one of the most important examples of a modular form. It is a cusp form of weight $12$, meaning that it vanishes at the cusp $i\infty$ (equivalently, its Fourier expansion has no constant term). The function $\Delta$ can be expressed in terms of the Eisenstein series $G_4$ and $G_6$, or as an infinite product $\Delta(\tau) = (2\pi)^{12} q \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} (1 - q^n)^{24}$ where $q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$. Its Fourier coefficients $\tau(n)$ define Ramanujan's tau function, which has remarkable multiplicative properties.
