Ramanujan's tau function $\tau(n)$ arises as the normalized Fourier coefficients of the discriminant modular form $\Delta(\tau)$. It is a multiplicative arithmetic function (i.e., $\tau(mn) = \tau(m)\tau(n)$ for coprime $m, n$) and satisfies remarkable congruences. Ramanujan conjectured, and Deligne later proved as part of the proof of the Weil conjectures, that $|\tau(p)| \leq 2p^{11/2}$ for all primes $p$. The tau function is one of the most studied arithmetic functions in number theory.
