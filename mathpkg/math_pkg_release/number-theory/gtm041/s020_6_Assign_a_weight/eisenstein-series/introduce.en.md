Eisenstein series $G_{2k}(\tau)$ are the most basic examples of modular forms, defined for integers $k \geq 2$ as a lattice sum over all nonzero integer pairs. The series converges absolutely for $k \geq 2$ and defines a holomorphic function on the upper half-plane. For odd indices the series vanishes identically, so only even weights $2k$ with $k \geq 2$ are of interest. Their Fourier expansions involve the divisor sum function $\sigma_{2k-1}(n)$ and values of the Riemann zeta function at even integers, connecting modular forms to both number theory and analysis.
