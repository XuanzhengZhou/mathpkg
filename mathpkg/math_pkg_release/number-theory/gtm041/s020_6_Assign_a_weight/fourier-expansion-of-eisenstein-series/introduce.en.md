The Fourier expansion (or $q$-expansion) of the Eisenstein series $G_{2k}(\tau)$ reveals a deep connection between modular forms, the Riemann zeta function, and arithmetic divisor sums. The constant term $2\zeta(2k)$ involves the value of the zeta function at an even integer, while the higher Fourier coefficients are proportional to $\sigma_{2k-1}(n)$, the sum of the $(2k-1)$-th powers of the divisors of $n$. This expansion demonstrates how modular forms encode arithmetic information in their Fourier coefficients and is fundamental to applications in number theory.
