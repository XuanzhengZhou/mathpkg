The modular transformation property is the defining functional equation of a modular form. A function $f$ on the upper half-plane of weight $r$ must satisfy $f((a\tau + b)/(c\tau + d)) = (c\tau + d)^r f(\tau)$ for all $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ in the modular group $\Gamma$. This property is shared by both the discriminant $\Delta(\tau)$ (weight 12) and the Eisenstein series $G_{2k}(\tau)$ (weight $2k$). The factor $(c\tau + d)^r$, called the automorphy factor, encodes how the function transforms under the fractional linear action of $\Gamma$.
