This is the classic form of Dirichlet's inequality: for every real number $\theta$, there exist coprime integers $h, k$ with $k > 0$ such that $|\theta - h/k| < 1/k^2$. It follows immediately from Theorem 7.2 by noting that $1/(Nk) \leq 1/k^2$ since $k \leq N$. This form eliminates the auxiliary integer $N$ and is fundamental to the theory of Diophantine approximation and continued fractions.
