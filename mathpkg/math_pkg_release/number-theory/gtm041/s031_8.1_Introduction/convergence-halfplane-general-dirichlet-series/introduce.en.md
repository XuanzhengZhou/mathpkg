Theorem 8.1 establishes a fundamental convergence result for general Dirichlet series. If the series converges at some point $s_0$ with positive real part $\sigma_0 > 0$, then the quantity $L$ defined as the limsup of $\log|A(n)|/\lambda(n)$ (where $A(n) = \sum_{k=1}^n a(k)$) satisfies $L \leq \sigma_0$. Furthermore, the series converges for all $s$ with $\operatorname{Re}(s) > L$, and this convergence is uniform on compact subsets. The proof proceeds in two parts: first showing $L \leq \sigma_0$ using partial summation, then establishing convergence for $\sigma > L$ via the Cauchy criterion.
