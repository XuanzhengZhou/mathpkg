Theorem 8.2 identifies the quantity $L = \limsup \log|A(n)|/\lambda(n)$ as precisely the abscissa of convergence $\sigma_c$ for a general Dirichlet series, under the mild assumption that the series converges somewhere with positive real part and diverges for negative real parts. The proof combines Theorem 8.1 (which gives convergence for $\sigma > L$) with a contradiction argument: if $\sigma_c > L$, there would exist $\sigma$ with $L < \sigma < \sigma_c$ where the series converges, contradicting the definition of $\sigma_c$ as the infimum of convergent real parts. Hence $\sigma_c = L$.
