Theorem 8.6 describes how the Bohr matrices representing the exponent sequence $\Lambda$ in terms of different bases are related. If $R_B$ represents $\Lambda$ in basis $B$ and $R_\Gamma$ represents $\Lambda$ in basis $\Gamma$, and $A$ is the change-of-basis matrix from $B$ to $\Gamma$, then $R_B = R_\Gamma A$. This has the suggestive multiplicative notation $\Lambda/B = (\Lambda/\Gamma) \cdot (\Gamma/B)$, analogous to the chain rule or functoriality of change-of-basis.
