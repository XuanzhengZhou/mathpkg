Theorem 8.7 shows how the Bohr function transforms when the underlying basis is changed. If $B$ and $\Gamma$ are two bases for the exponent sequence $\Lambda$, related by $\Gamma = AB$, then the Bohr function in basis $B$ evaluated at $Z$ equals the Bohr function in basis $\Gamma$ evaluated at $AZ$. This compatibility under change of basis is essential for establishing that properties defined via the Bohr function (such as the set of values $U_f(\sigma; B)$) are independent of the choice of basis.
