Let $z$ be any complex number with $\operatorname{Re}(z) > 0$, and let $h, k$ and $H$ be any integers satisfying $(h, k) = 1$, $k > 0$, $hH \equiv -1 \pmod{k}$. Then

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda\!\left\{ \frac{n}{k} (z - ih) \right\} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda\!\left\{ \frac{n}{k} \left( \frac{1}{z} - iH \right) \right\} + \frac{1}{2} \log z - \frac{\pi}{12k} \left( z - \frac{1}{z} \right) + \pi i\,s(h, k).$$

Here $\lambda(z)$ is defined in terms of the exponential integral, and $s(h,k)$ is the Dedekind sum.
