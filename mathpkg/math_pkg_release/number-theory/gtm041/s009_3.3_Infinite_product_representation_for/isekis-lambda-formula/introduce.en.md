Iseki's formula for $\Lambda(\alpha,\beta,z)$ is the key analytic identity that drives the proof of Lemma 2 and ultimately Dedekind's functional equation. It expresses a double series involving the $\lambda$-function as its own image under the transformation $(\alpha,\beta,z) \mapsto (1-\beta,\alpha,z^{-1})$ plus a correction term involving Bernoulli polynomials. The proof uses the Mellin transform representation of $\lambda$, expresses $\Lambda$ as a contour integral involving the Hurwitz zeta function $\zeta(s,\alpha)$ and the periodic zeta function $F(x,s)$, shifts the line of integration, and computes residues at $s=1,0,-1$.
