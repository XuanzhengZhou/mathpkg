Define
$$\Lambda(\alpha, \beta, z) = \sum_{r=0}^{\infty} \lambda\bigl((r + \alpha)z - i\beta\bigr) + \sum_{r=0}^{\infty} \lambda\bigl((r + 1 - \alpha)z + i\beta\bigr)$$
where $\lambda(z)$ is the function appearing in the expansion of $\log\eta(\tau)$.

If either $0 \leq \alpha \leq 1$ and $0 < \beta < 1$, or $0 < \alpha < 1$ and $0 \leq \beta \leq 1$, then
$$\Lambda(\alpha, \beta, z) = \Lambda(1 - \beta, \alpha, z^{-1}) - \pi z \sum_{n=0}^{2} \binom{2}{n} (iz)^{-n} B_{2-n}(\alpha) B_n(\beta).$$

The finite sum on the right is equal to
$$-\pi z \left( \alpha^2 - \alpha + \frac{1}{6} \right) + \frac{\pi}{z} \left( \beta^2 - \beta + \frac{1}{6} \right) + 2\pi i \left( \alpha - \frac{1}{2} \right) \left( \beta - \frac{1}{2} \right),$$
where $B_n(x)$ are the Bernoulli polynomials.
