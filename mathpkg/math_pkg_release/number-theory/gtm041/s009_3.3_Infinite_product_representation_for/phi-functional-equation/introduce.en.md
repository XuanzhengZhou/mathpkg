The function $\Phi(\alpha,\beta,s)$ combines the Hurwitz zeta function $\zeta(s,\alpha)$ and the periodic zeta function $F(x,s)$ with a Gamma factor $\Gamma(s)/(2\pi)^s$. Its functional equation $\Phi(\alpha,\beta,-s) = \Phi(1-\beta,\alpha,s)$ is a consequence of Hurwitz's formula for $\zeta(s,\alpha)$ and is the key identity that allows the contour integral proof of Iseki's formula to succeed: after shifting the integration line and changing variable $s \mapsto -s$, the functional equation transforms the resulting integral back into $\Lambda(1-\beta,\alpha,z^{-1})$.
