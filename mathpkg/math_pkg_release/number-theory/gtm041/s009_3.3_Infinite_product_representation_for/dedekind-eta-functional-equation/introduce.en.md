Theorem 3.4 gives the transformation law of the Dedekind eta function $\eta(\tau)$ under an arbitrary element of the modular group $\Gamma = \operatorname{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$ with $c > 0$. The multiplier $\varepsilon(a,b,c,d)$ is a $24$th root of unity expressed in terms of the Dedekind sum $s(-d,c)$. Taking logarithms, the functional equation is equivalent to a transformation formula for $\log\eta(\tau)$ involving the Dedekind sum and the principal branch of $\log\{-i(c\tau+d)\}^{1/2}$.
