Exercise 5 of Chapter 7 demonstrates that $1/\sqrt{5}$ is the optimal constant in Hurwitz's Diophantine approximation theorem by showing the golden ratio $\alpha = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ cannot be approximated with a smaller constant. For any $c < 1/\sqrt{5}$, the inequality $|\alpha - h/k| < c/k^2$ has only finitely many solutions. The proof uses the fact that $\alpha$ and its conjugate $\beta$ are roots of $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$, yielding the key estimate linking $|h^2 - hk - k^2|/k^2$ to the approximation error.
