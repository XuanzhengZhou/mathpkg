For a fixed real $\sigma > 1$, the quantities $m(\sigma)$ and $M(\sigma)$ capture the extremal behavior of the Riemann zeta function along the vertical line $\Re(s) = \sigma$. The function $M(\sigma)$ is the least upper bound and $m(\sigma)$ is the greatest lower bound of $|\zeta(\sigma + it)|$ as $t$ varies over all real numbers. These bounds are determined explicitly using Kronecker's approximation theorem in Section 7.6.
