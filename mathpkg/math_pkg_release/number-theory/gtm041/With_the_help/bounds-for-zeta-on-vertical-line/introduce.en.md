For $\sigma > 1$, the Riemann zeta function has a known absolute bound: $|\zeta(\sigma + it)| \leq \zeta(\sigma)$, so the supremum $M(\sigma)$ equals $\zeta(\sigma)$ and is attained on the real axis. The infimum $m(\sigma)$ equals $\zeta(2\sigma)/\zeta(\sigma)$, established by bounding the reciprocal $1/\zeta(s)$ via the Euler product and applying Kronecker's approximation theorem to construct $t$ making $p^{-it}$ approximate $-1$ for many primes simultaneously.
