In Chapter 1 we showed that a function with arbitrarily small nonzero periods is constant on every open connected subset in which it is analytic. By Theorem 7.13, if $f$ has three periods $\omega_1, \omega_2, \omega_3$ which are linearly independent over the integers, then $f$ has arbitrarily small nonzero periods. Therefore $f$ is constant on every connected component of its domain of analyticity. In particular, if $f$ is meromorphic on all of $\mathbb{C}$ (or on any connected domain), then $f$ must be constant.
