A meromorphic function with two periods whose ratio is irrational already possesses arbitrarily small nonzero periods (Theorem 7.12). Theorem 7.13 extends this to three periods linearly independent over the integers: any such triple forces the function to have arbitrarily small nonzero periods. The proof splits into cases depending on whether the ratios are real or complex, using Kronecker's approximation theorem for the irrational cases to construct small integer linear combinations of the periods.
