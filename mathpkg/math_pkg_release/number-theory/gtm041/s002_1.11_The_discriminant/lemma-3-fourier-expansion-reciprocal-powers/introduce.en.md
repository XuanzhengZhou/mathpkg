Lemma 3 computes the Fourier expansions (or $q$-expansions with $q = e^{2\pi i n\tau}$) of the sums $\sum_{m} (m+n\tau)^{-4}$ and $\sum_{m} (m+n\tau)^{-6}$. These are the one-dimensional building blocks for the double Eisenstein series. The proof uses the partial fraction decomposition of the cotangent function and the geometric series expansion of $\cot \pi\tau$ in terms of $e^{2\pi i\tau}$, then differentiates to obtain the required power sums. These formulas are essential for deriving the full Fourier expansions of $g_2$ and $g_3$.
