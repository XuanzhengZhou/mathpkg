Lehner [23], [24] discovered the following congruences for the coefficients $c(n)$ of $12^3 J(\tau)$:
$$c(5n) \equiv 0 \pmod{25}, \quad c(7n) \equiv 0 \pmod{7}, \quad c(11n) \equiv 0 \pmod{11}.$$

The proof uses the action of Hecke operators $T_p$ for $p = 5, 7, 11$ on the space of modular forms of weight 0. The function $J(\tau)$ is an eigenfunction of all Hecke operators (it generates the field of modular functions). By studying the eigenvalues, one shows that certain combinations of coefficients vanish modulo powers of $p$. Specifically, the operator $T_p$ acting on $J$ yields relations among the $c(n)$, and reducing modulo $p$ (or $p^2$) gives the stated congruences. For higher powers of these primes, the congruences involve higher powers of $p$, reflecting deeper structure in the Hecke algebra.
