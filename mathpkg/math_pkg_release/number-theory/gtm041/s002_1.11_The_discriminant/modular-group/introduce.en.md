The modular group is the group of all Möbius transformations $\tau \mapsto (a\tau + b)/(c\tau + d)$ with integer coefficients $a, b, c, d$ satisfying $ad - bc = 1$. It is isomorphic to $\text{PSL}(2, \mathbb{Z}) = \text{SL}(2, \mathbb{Z})/\{\pm I\}$ and acts on the upper half-plane $H$ by holomorphic automorphisms. The modular group plays a fundamental role in the theory of modular forms, elliptic curves, and complex multiplication, and is the starting point for the theory of Fuchsian groups.
