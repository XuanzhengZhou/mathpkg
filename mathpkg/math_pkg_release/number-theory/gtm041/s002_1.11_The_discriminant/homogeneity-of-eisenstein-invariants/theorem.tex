The Eisenstein series show that $g_2$ and $g_3$ are homogeneous functions of degrees $-4$ and $-6$, respectively. That is,

$$g_2(\lambda\omega_1, \lambda\omega_2) = \lambda^{-4}g_2(\omega_1, \omega_2) \quad \text{and} \quad g_3(\lambda\omega_1, \lambda\omega_2) = \lambda^{-6}g_3(\omega_1, \omega_2)$$

for any $\lambda \neq 0$. Hence $\Delta$ is homogeneous of degree $-12$,

$$\Delta(\lambda\omega_1, \lambda\omega_2) = \lambda^{-12}\Delta(\omega_1, \omega_2).$$
