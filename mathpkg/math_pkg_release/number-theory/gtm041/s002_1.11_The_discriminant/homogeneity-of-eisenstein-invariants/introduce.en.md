The Eisenstein invariants $g_2$ and $g_3$, when viewed as functions of the period lattice generators $\omega_1, \omega_2$, exhibit simple scaling behavior. Under scaling of both periods by a nonzero factor $\lambda$, $g_2$ transforms with weight $-4$ and $g_3$ with weight $-6$. This homogeneity is a direct consequence of the definition of $g_2$ and $g_3$ as Eisenstein series. Consequently, the discriminant $\Delta = g_2^3 - 27g_3^2$ is homogeneous of degree $-12$, which makes it a modular form of weight $-12$.
