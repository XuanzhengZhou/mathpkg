Klein's modular function $J(\tau)$ is invariant under the action of the modular group. This follows from the fact that $g_2$ and $g_3$ depend only on the period lattice $\Omega$, so changing the generators via a unimodular matrix does not change $g_2$ or $g_3$, and hence leaves $\Delta$ and $J = g_2^3/\Delta$ unchanged. This invariance property is the defining characteristic of a modular function and makes $J$ a fundamental object in the theory of modular forms.
