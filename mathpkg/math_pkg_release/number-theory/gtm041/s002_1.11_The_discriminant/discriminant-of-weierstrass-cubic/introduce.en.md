The discriminant of the Weierstrass cubic is the quantity $\Delta = g_2^3 - 27g_3^2$, where $g_2$ and $g_3$ are the Eisenstein invariants arising from the Weierstrass $\wp$-function. It serves as the discriminant of the cubic polynomial $4x^3 - g_2 x - g_3$ whose roots are the values $e_1, e_2, e_3 = \wp(\omega_1/2), \wp(\omega_2/2), \wp((\omega_1+\omega_2)/2)$. Since these three roots are distinct, $\Delta$ is nonzero, which guarantees that the elliptic curve associated to the lattice is nonsingular. As a function of the periods, $\Delta$ is a modular form of weight $-12$.
