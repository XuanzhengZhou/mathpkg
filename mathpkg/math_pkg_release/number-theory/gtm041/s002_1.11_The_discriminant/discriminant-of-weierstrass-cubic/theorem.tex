The number

$$\Delta = g_2^3 - 27g_3^2$$

is called the discriminant. We regard the invariants $g_2$ and $g_3$ and the discriminant $\Delta$ as functions of the periods $\omega_1$ and $\omega_2$ and we write

$$g_2 = g_2(\omega_1, \omega_2), \quad g_3 = g_3(\omega_1, \omega_2), \quad \Delta = \Delta(\omega_1, \omega_2).$$

The discriminant is homogeneous of degree $-12$,

$$\Delta(\lambda\omega_1, \lambda\omega_2) = \lambda^{-12}\Delta(\omega_1, \omega_2).$$

If a polynomial has distinct roots, its discriminant does not vanish. The discriminant of the cubic polynomial $4x^3 - g_2x - g_3$ is $g_2^3 - 27g_3^2$. When $x = \wp(z)$ the roots of this polynomial are distinct so $\Delta \neq 0$.
