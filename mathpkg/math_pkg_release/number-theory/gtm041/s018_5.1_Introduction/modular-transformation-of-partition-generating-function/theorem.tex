Let $F(x) = \prod_{m=1}^{\infty} (1-x^m)^{-1} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} p(n)x^n$ and let $\eta(\tau)$ be the Dedekind eta function, related by

$$F(e^{2\pi i\tau}) = \frac{e^{\pi i\tau/12}}{\eta(\tau)}.$$

For $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \Gamma$ (the modular group) with $c > 0$, the functional equation for $\eta(\tau)$ implies the transformation formula

$$F(e^{2\pi i\tau}) = F(e^{2\pi i\tau'}) \exp\left(\frac{\pi i(\tau - \tau')}{12}\right) \{-i(c\tau + d)\}^{1/2} \exp\left\{\pi i\left(\frac{a + d}{12c} + s(-d, c)\right)\right\},$$

where $\tau' = (a\tau + b)/(c\tau + d)$. In particular, near the root of unity $e^{2\pi ih/k}$, for small $|z|$ the function $F$ behaves asymptotically like

$$z^{1/2} \exp\left(\frac{\pi}{12z}\right),$$

up to a constant factor.
