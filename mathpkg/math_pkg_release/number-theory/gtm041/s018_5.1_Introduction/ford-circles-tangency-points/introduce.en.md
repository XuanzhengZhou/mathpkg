Theorem 5.7 gives explicit formulas for the tangency points of a Ford circle with its two neighbors in the Farey sequence. These coordinates are crucial for Rademacher's contour: instead of following a circular arc, the integration path follows arcs of Ford circles through their tangency points. The theorem also notes the geometric fact that each tangency point lies on a semicircle spanning between the corresponding Farey fractions.
