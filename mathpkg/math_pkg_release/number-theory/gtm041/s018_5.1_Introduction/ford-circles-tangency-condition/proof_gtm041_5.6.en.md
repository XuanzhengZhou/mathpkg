The square of the distance $D$ between the centers of $C(a, b)$ and $C(c, d)$ is

$$D^2 = \left(\frac{a}{b} - \frac{c}{d}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{1}{2b^2} - \frac{1}{2d^2}\right)^2,$$

whereas the square of the sum of their radii is

$$(r + R)^2 = \left(\frac{1}{2b^2} + \frac{1}{2d^2}\right)^2.$$

The difference $D^2 - (r + R)^2$ equals

$$D^2 - (r + R)^2 = \left(\frac{ad - bc}{bd}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{1}{2b^2} - \frac{1}{2d^2}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{1}{2b^2} + \frac{1}{2d^2}\right)^2$$

$$= \frac{(ad - bc)^2 - 1}{b^2d^2} \geq 0.$$

The expression is nonnegative, so the distance between centers is at least the sum of the radii, meaning the circles do not intersect. Equality holds (the circles are tangent) if and only if $(ad - bc)^2 = 1$, i.e., $|ad - bc| = 1$ or equivalently $bc - ad = \pm 1$.
