Two Ford circles $C(a, b)$ and $C(c, d)$ (of radius $1/(2b^2)$ and $1/(2d^2)$ centered at $a/b + i/(2b^2)$ and $c/d + i/(2d^2)$, respectively) are either tangent to each other or they do not intersect. They are tangent if, and only if,
$$bc - ad = \pm 1.$$
In particular, Ford circles of consecutive Farey fractions are tangent to each other.
