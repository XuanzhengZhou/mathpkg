This theorem provides an asymptotic upper bound for the Fourier coefficients of non-cusp modular forms. While cusp forms satisfy $c(n) = O(n^k)$ by Theorem 6.17, non-cusp forms (which include Eisenstein series as their non-cuspidal component) have the larger bound $c(n) = O(n^{2k-1})$. The proof reduces to the case of the Eisenstein series $G_{2k}$, whose coefficients are proportional to the divisor function $\sigma_{2k-1}(n)$, which is bounded by $O(n^{2k-1})$.
