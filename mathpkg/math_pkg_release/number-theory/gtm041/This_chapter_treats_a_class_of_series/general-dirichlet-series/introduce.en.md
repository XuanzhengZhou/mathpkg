A general Dirichlet series is a series of the form $\sum a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$ where $\{\lambda(n)\}$ is a strictly increasing sequence of real numbers tending to $+\infty$. This construction generalizes both ordinary Dirichlet series (when $\lambda(n) = \log n$) and power series (when $\lambda(n) = n$). The parameter $s = \sigma + it$ is a complex variable, and the numbers $\lambda(n)$ and $a(n)$ are called the exponents and coefficients of the series, respectively. A general Dirichlet series can also be viewed as a Laplace-Stieltjes transform where the integrator $\alpha(t)$ is a step function with jumps at the exponents.
