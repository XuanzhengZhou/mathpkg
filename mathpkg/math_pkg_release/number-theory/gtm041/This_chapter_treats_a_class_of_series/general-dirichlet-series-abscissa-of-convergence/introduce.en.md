Theorem 8.2 sharpens Theorem 8.1 by proving that $L = \limsup_{n \to \infty} (\log|\sum_{k=1}^n a(k)|)/\lambda(n)$ is exactly the abscissa of convergence $\sigma_c$ of a general Dirichlet series, provided the series converges somewhere with positive real part and diverges for all negative real parts. The proof shows that $\sigma_c$ (defined as the greatest lower bound of real parts where the series converges) satisfies $\sigma_c = L$, establishing that the series converges precisely in the half-plane $\sigma > L$ and diverges for $\sigma < L$.
