Assume that the series $\sum a(n)e^{-s\lambda(n)}$ converges for some $s$ with positive real part, say for $s = s_0$ with $\sigma_0 > 0$. Let
$$L = \limsup_{n \to \infty} \frac{\log\left|\sum_{k=1}^n a(k)\right|}{\lambda(n)}.$$
Then $L \leq \sigma_0$. Moreover, the series converges in the half-plane $\sigma > L$, and the convergence is uniform on every compact subset of the half-plane $\sigma > L$.
