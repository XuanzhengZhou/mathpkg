Theorem 8.11 verifies that the relation $\sim$ introduced for general Dirichlet series satisfies the three axioms of an equivalence relation. Reflexivity follows by taking all $y_n = 0$ (so each $x_n = 0$). Symmetry follows from the fact that if $b(n) = a(n)e^{ix_n}$ with $X = R_B Y$, then $a(n) = b(n)e^{-ix_n}$ with $-X = R_B(-Y)$. Transitivity is proved by adding the exponent sequences from two equivalences: if $b(n) = a(n)e^{ix_n}$ and $a(n) = c(n)e^{iu_n}$, then $b(n) = c(n)e^{i(x_n + u_n)}$ where $X+U = R_B(Y+V)$.
