Theorem 8.10 establishes two important properties of equivalent Dirichlet series. First, equivalence preserves the abscissa of absolute convergence, which follows immediately from the fact that $|b(n)| = |a(n)|$. Second, and more subtly, the equivalence relation defined with respect to one basis $B$ for the exponent sequence $\Lambda$ is independent of the particular basis chosen -- if two bases $B$ and $\Gamma$ are related by a Bohr matrix $A$ (so that $\Gamma = AB$), then any equivalence realized via $B$ can also be realized via $\Gamma$.
