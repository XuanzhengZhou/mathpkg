Two ordinary Dirichlet series
$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{a(n)}{n^s} \quad \text{and} \quad \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{b(n)}{n^s}$$
are equivalent if, and only if, there exists a completely multiplicative function $f$ such that

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $b(n) = a(n)f(n)$ for all $n \geq 1$, and
\item[(b)] $|f(p)| = 1$ whenever $a(n) \neq 0$ and $p$ is a prime divisor of $n$.
\end{enumerate}
