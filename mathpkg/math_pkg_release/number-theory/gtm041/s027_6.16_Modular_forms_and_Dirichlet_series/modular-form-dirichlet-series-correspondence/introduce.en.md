The modular-form-to-Dirichlet-series correspondence, introduced by Hecke, associates to each modular form its Dirichlet series formed with the same Fourier coefficients (omitting the constant term). If the modular form lies in $M_{2k}$, the growth rate of the coefficients determines the half-plane of absolute convergence: cusp forms yield $c(n) = O(n^k)$ and convergence for $\operatorname{Re}(s) > k+1$, while non-cusp forms yield $c(n) = O(n^{2k-1})$ and convergence for $\operatorname{Re}(s) > 2k$. This correspondence is the foundation for Hecke's theory connecting modular forms with analytic properties of Dirichlet series.
