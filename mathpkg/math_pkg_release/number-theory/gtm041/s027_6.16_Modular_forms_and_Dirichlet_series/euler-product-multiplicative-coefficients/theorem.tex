If the coefficients $c(n)$ satisfy the multiplicative property
$$c(m)c(n) = \sum_{d \mid (m,n)} d^{2k-1} c\!\left(\frac{mn}{d^2}\right),$$
then the Dirichlet series $\varphi(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} c(n)/n^s$ admits an Euler product representation
$$\varphi(s) = \prod_p \frac{1}{1 - c(p)p^{-s} + p^{2k-1-2s}},$$
valid whenever the Dirichlet series converges absolutely.

The identity $c(m)c(n) = \sum_{d \mid (m,n)} d^{2k-1} c(mn/d^2)$ implies, for each prime $p$,
$$c(p)c(p^n) = c(p^{n+1}) + p^{2k-1} c(p^{n-1}),$$
which yields the power series identity
$$(1 - c(p)x + p^{2k-1}x^2)\left(1 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} c(p^n)x^n\right) = 1$$
for all $|x| < 1$. Setting $x = p^{-s}$ reduces the product over primes to the stated Euler product form.
