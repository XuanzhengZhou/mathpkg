Given $\omega_1', \omega_2'$ with $\omega_2'/\omega_1'$ not real, let
$$\Omega = \{m\omega_1' + n\omega_2' : m, n \text{ integers}\}.$$
Then there exists a fundamental pair $(\omega_1, \omega_2)$ equivalent to $(\omega_1', \omega_2')$ such that
$$\begin{pmatrix} \omega_2 \\ \omega_1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \omega_2' \\ \omega_1' \end{pmatrix} \quad \text{with} \quad ad - bc = 1,$$
and such that
$$|\omega_2| \geq |\omega_1|, \quad |\omega_1 + \omega_2| \geq |\omega_2|, \quad |\omega_1 - \omega_2| \geq |\omega_2|.$$
