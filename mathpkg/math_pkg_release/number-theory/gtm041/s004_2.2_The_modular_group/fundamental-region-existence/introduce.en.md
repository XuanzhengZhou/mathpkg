Theorem 2.2 states that every point $\tau'$ in the upper half-plane $H$ is equivalent under the modular group $\Gamma$ to some point $\tau$ satisfying the inequalities $|\tau| \geq 1$, $|\tau + 1| \geq |\tau|$, and $|\tau - 1| \geq |\tau|$. These three conditions define the standard fundamental region $R_{\Gamma}$ of the modular group. The proof applies Lemma 1 to the lattice generated by $1$ and $\tau'$, producing a fundamental pair whose ratio gives the desired point $\tau$.
