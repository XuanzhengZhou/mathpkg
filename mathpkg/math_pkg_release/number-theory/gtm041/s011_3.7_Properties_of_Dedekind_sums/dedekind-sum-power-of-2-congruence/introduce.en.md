Theorem 3.10 extends the congruence modulo 8 to a congruence modulo higher powers of 2. When $k$ contains a factor $2^{\lambda}$, the quantity $12hk\,s(h,k)$ satisfies a congruence modulo $2^{\lambda+3}$ expressed in terms of $h$, $k$, and a sum of floor functions. This result is essential for establishing the transformation formula for Dedekind sums under modular substitutions.
