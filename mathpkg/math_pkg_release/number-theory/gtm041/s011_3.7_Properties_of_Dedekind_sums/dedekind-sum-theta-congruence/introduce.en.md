Theorem 3.8 establishes congruence relations for Dedekind sums involving the factor $\theta = (3,k)$. Part (a) characterizes when $12ks(h,k)$ is divisible by $3$, and part (b) provides a congruence modulo $\theta k$ that, when combined with Theorem 3.6(c), yields a complete characterization: $s(h,k)$ vanishes precisely when $h^2 + 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{k}$.
