The Dedekind sum $s(h,k)$ is an arithmetic sum introduced by Richard Dedekind in connection with the functional equation of the Dedekind eta function $\eta(\tau)$. For coprime positive integers $h$ and $k$, the sum encodes information about the fractional parts of multiples of $h/k$ and plays a fundamental role in the transformation theory of modular forms. Dedekind sums satisfy a remarkable reciprocity law and appear in diverse areas including number theory, topology, and geometry.
