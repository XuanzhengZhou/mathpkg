Theorem 2.4 establishes that a non-constant modular function has exactly as many zeros as poles in the closure of the fundamental region, when zeros and poles at vertices are counted with appropriate fractional weights (one-third at $\rho$ and one-half at $i$). This is a fundamental counting principle that underlies the proof that every modular function is a rational function of the modular invariant $J$.
