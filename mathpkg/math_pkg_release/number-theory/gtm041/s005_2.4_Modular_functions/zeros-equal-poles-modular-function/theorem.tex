If $f$ is modular and not identically zero, then in the closure of the fundamental region $R_{\Gamma}$, the number of zeros of $f$ is equal to the number of poles of $f$. In other words, $f$ takes on the value $0$ in $R_{\Gamma}$ as often as it takes the value $\infty$.

More precisely, if $f$ has no zeros or poles on the finite part of the boundary of $R_{\Gamma}$, then
$$N = P + m$$
where $N$ is the number of zeros, $P$ the number of poles of $f$ inside $R_{\Gamma}$, and $m$ is the order of the pole (or negative of the order of the zero) of $f$ at $i\infty$.

In the general case where $f$ may have zeros or poles at the vertices $\rho = e^{2\pi i/3}$ and $i$, the formula becomes
$$N + \frac{k}{3} + \frac{l}{2} = P + m$$
where $k$ is the order of the zero (or negative of the order of the pole) at $\rho$, and $l$ is the order of the zero (or negative of the order of the pole) at $i$.
