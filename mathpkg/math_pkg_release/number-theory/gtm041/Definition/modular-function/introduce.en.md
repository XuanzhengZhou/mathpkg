A modular function is a meromorphic function on the upper half-plane $H$ that is invariant under the action of the modular group $\Gamma = \mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{Z})$ and has a Fourier expansion of the form $\sum_{n=-m}^{\infty} a(n) e^{2\pi i n \tau}$ with at worst a pole at $i\infty$. These functions are the fundamental objects of study in the theory of modular forms and play a central role in number theory, particularly through their connection to elliptic curves and Dirichlet series. The prototypical example is the $j$-invariant, which is analytic in $H$ with a first-order pole at $i\infty$.
