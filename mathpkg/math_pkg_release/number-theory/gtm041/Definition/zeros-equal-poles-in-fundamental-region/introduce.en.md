Theorem 2.4 of Apostol states that any non-zero modular function has the same number of zeros as poles in the closure of the fundamental region $R_{\Gamma}$ of the modular group. The proof uses the argument principle, splitting the contour integral along the boundary of a truncated fundamental region and exploiting the invariance properties of modular functions under the generators $S(\tau) = -1/\tau$ and $T(\tau) = \tau + 1$. This counting principle is fundamental for understanding the value distribution of modular functions and leads directly to the result that non-constant modular functions take every value equally often.
