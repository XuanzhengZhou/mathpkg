The Weierstrass $\wp$ function satisfies a first-order differential equation $[\wp'(z)]^2 = 4\wp^3(z) - g_2\wp(z) - g_3$, where $g_2$ and $g_3$ are the lattice invariants. This equation reveals that $(\wp(z), \wp'(z))$ parametrizes an elliptic curve. The proof exploits that the difference between the two sides is an elliptic function with no poles, hence constant, and the constant is evaluated by examining the Laurent expansions near $z = 0$.
