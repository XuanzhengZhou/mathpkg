Theorem 1.14 establishes that the cubic polynomial $4x^3 - g_2 x - g_3$ appearing in the Weierstrass differential equation factors completely as $4(x - e_1)(x - e_2)(x - e_3)$, where $e_1, e_2, e_3$ are the values of $\wp$ at the three non-zero half-periods. Moreover, the three roots are always distinct, which is equivalent to the non-vanishing of the discriminant $\Delta = g_2^3 - 27g_3^2$. This ensures that the elliptic curve defined by $\wp$ is nonsingular.
