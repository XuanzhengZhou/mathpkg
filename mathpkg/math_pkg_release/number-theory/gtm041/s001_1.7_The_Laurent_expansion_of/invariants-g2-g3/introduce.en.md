The invariants $g_2$ and $g_3$ are defined as scaled versions of the Eisenstein series $G_4$ and $G_6$: $g_2 = 60 G_4$ and $g_3 = 140 G_6$. These numbers encode the lattice structure and appear as the coefficients in the Weierstrass differential equation $[\wp'(z)]^2 = 4\wp^3(z) - g_2\wp(z) - g_3$. They are called "invariants" because they determine the elliptic curve associated to the lattice up to isomorphism.
