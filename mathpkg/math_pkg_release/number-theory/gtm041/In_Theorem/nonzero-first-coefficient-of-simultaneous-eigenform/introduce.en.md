Theorem 6.14 establishes that any simultaneous eigenform $f \in M_k$ (with $k \geq 4$ even) must have a nonzero first Fourier coefficient $c(1)$. This is a fundamental structural result: if $c(1) = 0$, then all coefficients $c(n)$ would vanish, contradicting the assumption that $f$ is a nonzero eigenform. The proof uses the relation $\gamma_n(1) = c(n)$ from the action of Hecke operators, together with the eigenform property $T_n f = \lambda(n) f$, to deduce $c(n) = \lambda(n) c(1)$.
