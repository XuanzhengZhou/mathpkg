An eigenfunction (or eigenform) of a Hecke operator $T_n$ is a nonzero modular form $f \in M_k$ such that the action of $T_n$ on $f$ simply multiplies $f$ by a complex scalar $\lambda(n)$. When the space $M_k$ is one-dimensional, every nonzero form is automatically an eigenform of each $T_n$. For example, the Eisenstein series $G_4, G_6, G_8, G_{10}, G_{14}$ are eigenforms for every Hecke operator because the spaces $M_4, M_6, M_8, M_{10}, M_{14}$ are all one-dimensional.
