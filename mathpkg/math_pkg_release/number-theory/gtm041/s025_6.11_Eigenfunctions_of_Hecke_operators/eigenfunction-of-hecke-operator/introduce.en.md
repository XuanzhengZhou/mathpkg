An eigenfunction (or eigenform) of a Hecke operator $T_n$ is a nonzero modular form $f$ for which the action of $T_n$ scales $f$ by a constant factor $\lambda(n)$. This concept generalizes the usual notion of eigenvectors to the space of modular forms. Examples include Eisenstein series $G_4, G_6, G_8, G_{10}, G_{14}$ and cusp forms $\Delta, \Delta G_4, \Delta G_6, \Delta G_8, \Delta G_{10}, \Delta G_{14}$, which are eigenforms whenever the corresponding space of modular forms is one-dimensional.
