Theorem 6.14 of Apostol establishes that any simultaneous eigenform $f$ in the space $M_k$ of modular forms (with $k \geq 4$ even) must have a non-vanishing first Fourier coefficient $c(1)$. This result is essential because it permits the normalization $c(1) = 1$, which then implies the multiplicative property of Fourier coefficients. The proof uses the relation between the $n$-th Fourier coefficient and the eigenvalue $\lambda(n)$.
