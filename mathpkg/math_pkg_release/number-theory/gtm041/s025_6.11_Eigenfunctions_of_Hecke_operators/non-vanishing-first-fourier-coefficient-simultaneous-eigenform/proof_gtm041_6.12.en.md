The coefficient of $x$ in the Fourier expansion of $T_n f$ is $\gamma_n(1) = c(n)$. Since $f$ is a simultaneous eigenform, this coefficient is also equal to $\lambda(n)c(1)$, so

$$c(n) = \lambda(n)c(1)$$

for all $n \geq 1$. If $c(1) = 0$ then $c(n) = 0$ for all $n \geq 1$, which would imply $f$ is identically zero (its constant term is also zero since we are in the context of cusp forms or by considering the expansion). But $f$ is a nonzero eigenfunction by definition. Therefore $c(1) \neq 0$.
