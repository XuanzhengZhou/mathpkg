The difference formula $f(w)-f(z) = \frac{(ad-bc)(w-z)}{(cw+d)(cz+d)}$ is a fundamental algebraic identity for Möbius transformations. It shows that the non-degeneracy condition $ad-bc \neq 0$ is equivalent to $f$ being non-constant. This identity is the key tool for establishing injectivity, deriving the derivative, and proving the circle-preserving property of Möbius transformations.
