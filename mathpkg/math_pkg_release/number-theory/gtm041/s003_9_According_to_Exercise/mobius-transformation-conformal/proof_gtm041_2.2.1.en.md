A holomorphic function is conformal at a point if and only if its derivative does not vanish there. From the derivative formula
$$f'(z) = \frac{ad-bc}{(cz+d)^2},$$
we have $f'(z) \neq 0$ at every $z \neq -d/c$ because $ad-bc \neq 0$. Therefore $f$ is conformal at every point of $C^*$ except possibly at the pole $z = -d/c$, where $f$ is not analytic. Hence $f$ is conformal everywhere on its domain of analyticity.
