The derivative of a Möbius transformation is given by $f'(z) = (ad-bc)/(cz+d)^2$. It follows immediately from the difference formula $f(w)-f(z) = (ad-bc)(w-z)/((cw+d)(cz+d))$ by dividing by $w-z$ and taking the limit as $w \to z$. Since $ad-bc \neq 0$, the derivative never vanishes on the domain of analyticity, which is a key property implying conformality.
