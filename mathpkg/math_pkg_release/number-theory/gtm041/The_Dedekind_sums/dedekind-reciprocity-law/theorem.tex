Let $h$ and $k$ be positive integers with $(h, k) = 1$. Then the Dedekind sums satisfy the reciprocity law:
$$s(h, k) + s(k, h) = \frac{h^2 + k^2 + 1}{12hk} - \frac{1}{4}.$$
Equivalently,
$$12hk\bigl(s(h, k) + s(k, h)\bigr) = h^2 + k^2 + 1 - 3hk.$$

Moreover, for any integers $a, c$ with $(a, c) = 1$ and $c > 0$,
$$12c\,s(a, c) \equiv a^2 + 1 \pmod{\theta c},$$
where $\theta = (3, c)$.
