Theorem 3.6 establishes the fundamental symmetry properties of Dedekind sums under congruence transformations of the first argument. Part (a) shows that $s(h,k)$ depends only on $h$ modulo $k$ up to sign. Part (b) relates $s(h,k)$ to $s(\bar{h},k)$ where $\bar{h}$ is the multiplicative inverse of $h$ modulo $k$. Part (c) gives a simple criterion for the vanishing of $s(h,k)$.
