Theorem 3.11 establishes a crucial parity property for the Dedekind eta multiplier system. When transforming $\eta(\tau)$ under a modular substitution, the resulting multiplier involves Dedekind sums. The quantity $\delta$ defined here measures the consistency of the multiplier system under congruence conditions on $c$. The theorem states that $12\delta$ is always even (i.e., $\delta$ is a half-integer multiple of $1/6$), which is necessary for the multiplier system to be well-defined and consistent.
