Prove the following extension of Dirichlet's approximation theorem.

Given $n$ real numbers $\theta_1, \ldots, \theta_n$ and given an integer $N \geq 1$, there exist integers $h_1, \ldots, h_n$ and $k$, with $1 \leq k \leq N^n$, such that
$$|k\theta_i - h_i| < \frac{1}{N}, \quad \text{for } i = 1, 2, \ldots, n.$$
