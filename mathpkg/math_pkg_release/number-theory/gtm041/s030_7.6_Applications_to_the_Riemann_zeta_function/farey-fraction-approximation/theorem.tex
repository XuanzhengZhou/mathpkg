Let $\theta$ be irrational, $0 < \theta < 1$. Let $a/b$ and $c/d$ be consecutive Farey fractions such that

$$\frac{a}{b} < \theta < \frac{c}{d}.$$

Then either

$$\left|\theta - \frac{a}{b}\right| < \frac{1}{2b^2} \quad \text{or} \quad \left|\theta - \frac{c}{d}\right| < \frac{1}{2d^2}.$$

Consequently, there exist infinitely many fractions $h/k$ with $(h, k) = 1$ and $k > 0$ such that

$$\left|\theta - \frac{h}{k}\right| < \frac{1}{2k^2}.$$
