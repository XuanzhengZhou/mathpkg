This result sharpens Dirichlet's approximation theorem by using Farey fractions to obtain an error bound of $1/(2k^2)$ instead of $1/k^2$. The proof exploits the property of consecutive Farey fractions $a/b < c/d$ that $bc - ad = 1$, which implies the interval length $(c/d - a/b) = 1/(bd)$. Since $\theta$ lies in this interval, the distance to one endpoint must be at most half the interval length, yielding the $1/(2k^2)$ bound.
