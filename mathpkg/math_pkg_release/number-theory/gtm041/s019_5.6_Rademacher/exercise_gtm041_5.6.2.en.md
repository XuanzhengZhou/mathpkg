If $a, b, c, d$ are positive integers such that $a/b < c/d$ and if $\lambda$ and $\mu$ are positive integers, prove that the fraction

$$\theta = \frac{\lambda a + \mu c}{\lambda b + \mu d}$$

lies between $a/b$ and $c/d$, and that

$$\frac{c - d\theta}{\theta b - a} = \frac{\lambda}{\mu}.$$

When $\lambda = \mu$, $\theta$ is the **mediant** of $a/b$ and $c/d$.
