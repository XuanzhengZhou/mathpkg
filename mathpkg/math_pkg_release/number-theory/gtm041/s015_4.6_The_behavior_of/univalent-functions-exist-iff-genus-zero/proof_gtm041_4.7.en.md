This result is proved using Riemann surface theory. The fundamental region $R_G$, after identifying congruent boundary edges under the action of $G$, becomes a compact Riemann surface. The automorphic functions on $G$ correspond to meromorphic functions on this Riemann surface. A univalent function provides a biholomorphic map from this Riemann surface to the Riemann sphere $\mathbb{C} \cup \{\infty\}$. Such a map exists if and only if the Riemann surface has genus zero (i.e., is topologically equivalent to a sphere).

For the full modular group $\Gamma$, $R_\Gamma$ is the familiar fundamental domain $\{|\tau| \geq 1, |\Re(\tau)| \leq \frac{1}{2}\}$. Identifying the left and right vertical boundaries (via $T$) and the two arcs of the unit circle (via $S$) produces a surface homeomorphic to a sphere, confirming genus zero.
