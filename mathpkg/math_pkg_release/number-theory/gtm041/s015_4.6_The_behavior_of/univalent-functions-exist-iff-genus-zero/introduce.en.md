This proposition gives the topological criterion for the existence of univalent functions on a subgroup $G$ of the
modular group. A univalent function (automorphic, valence 1) exists on $G$ precisely when the Riemann surface
obtained from the fundamental region $R_G$ by identifying congruent boundary edges has genus zero. For the full
modular group $\Gamma$, the genus is zero (the surface is a sphere), which is consistent with the existence of
the modular invariant $J$. This criterion motivates studying $\Gamma_0(p)$ for those primes $p$ where the genus is zero.
