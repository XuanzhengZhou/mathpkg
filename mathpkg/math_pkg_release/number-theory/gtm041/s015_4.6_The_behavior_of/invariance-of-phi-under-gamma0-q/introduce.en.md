This proposition establishes that $\varphi(\tau) = \Delta(q\tau)/\Delta(\tau)$ is automorphic under the congruence
subgroup $\Gamma_0(q)$. The proof combines the transformation law of the discriminant $\Delta$ (weight 12) with the
fact that for $V = \binom{a\;b}{c\;d} \in \Gamma_0(q)$, the lower-left entry $c = c_1 q$ is divisible by $q$, which
allows the factor $q$ in $\Delta(q\tau)$ to be absorbed into the transformation. Combined with the Fourier expansion
of $\varphi$, this shows $\varphi$ is a candidate univalent function on $\Gamma_0(q)$.
