$$p(n) = \sum_{k < \alpha\sqrt{n}} P_k(n) + O(n^{-1/4}),$$

where $\alpha$ is a constant and $P_1(n)$ is the dominant term, asymptotic to $\dfrac{e^{K\sqrt{n}}}{4n\sqrt{3}}$ with $K = \pi\sqrt{2/3}$. The terms $P_2(n), P_3(n), \ldots$ are of similar type but with smaller constants in place of $K$ in the exponential. Since $p(n)$ is an integer, the finite sum gives $p(n)$ exactly when $n$ is large enough to ensure the error term is less than $1/2$. The infinite sum $\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} P_k(n)$ diverges for each $n$.
