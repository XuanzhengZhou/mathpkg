The formula is obtained through the circle method, which divides a contour integral of $F(x)/x^{n+1}$ near the unit circle into arcs surrounding roots of unity. On each arc, $F(x)$ is approximated using the modular transformation of the Dedekind eta function. The contributions from arcs at $x = e^{2\pi i h/k}$ produce the terms $P_k(n)$. The error estimate $O(n^{-1/4})$ follows from bounding the approximation error on each arc. The divergence of the infinite sum was demonstrated by D. H. Lehmer [21] in 1937. A complete treatment occupies the remainder of Chapter 5 of GTM041, culminating in Theorem 5.10 (Rademacher's series).
