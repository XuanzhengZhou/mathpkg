Theorem 5.5 completely characterizes the structure of Farey sequences. It shows that $F_{n+1}$ is obtained from $F_n$ by inserting, between each pair of consecutive fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$, their mediant $(a+c)/(b+d)$ whenever the denominator $b+d$ equals $n+1$. More importantly, it establishes the unimodular property $bc-ad=1$ for all consecutive Farey fractions, which is the crucial algebraic fact that makes Ford circles of consecutive Farey fractions tangent -- the geometric heart of Rademacher's improved contour.
