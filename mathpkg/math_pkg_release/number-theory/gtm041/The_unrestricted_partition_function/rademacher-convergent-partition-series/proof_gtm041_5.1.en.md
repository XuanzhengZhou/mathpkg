The full proof occupies the remainder of Chapter 5 of GTM041 and culminates in Theorem 5.10. Rademacher's key insight was to replace the circular contour in the Hardy-Ramanujan circle method with a more carefully chosen contour in the $\tau$-plane, constructed using Ford circles. By choosing the contour to pass through the points of tangency of consecutive Ford circles, the approximation of $F(x)$ on each arc is improved, and the resulting integrals produce the convergent terms $R_k(n)$. The Dedekind eta function transformation formula provides the exact functional equation for $F$ near each singularity $e^{2\pi i h/k}$.
