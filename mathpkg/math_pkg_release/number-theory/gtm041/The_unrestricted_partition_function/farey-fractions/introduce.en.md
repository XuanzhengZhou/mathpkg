Farey fractions provide a systematic enumeration of rational numbers in $[0,1]$ ordered by denominator size. Named after the British geologist John Farey (1816), their mathematical properties were first established by Cauchy. In Apostol's GTM041, Farey fractions play a crucial role in Rademacher's proof of the partition formula: consecutive Farey fractions define Ford circles whose points of tangency determine the optimal integration contour in the $\tau$-plane.
