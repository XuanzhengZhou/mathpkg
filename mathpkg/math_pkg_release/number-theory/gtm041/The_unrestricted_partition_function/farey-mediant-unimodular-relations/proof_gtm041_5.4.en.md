Since $h/k$ lies between $a/b$ and $c/d$, we have $bh - ak \geq 1$ and $ck - dh \geq 1$. Equation (9) shows that $k = b + d$ if, and only if, $bh - ak = ck - dh = 1$.

More explicitly: Since $h = a + c$ and $k = b + d$, we compute

$$bh - ak = b(a + c) - a(b + d) = ab + bc - ab - ad = bc - ad = 1,$$
$$ck - dh = c(b + d) - d(a + c) = cb + cd - ad - cd = cb - ad = 1.$$

Thus both unimodular relations hold automatically when the mediant of two fractions satisfying $bc - ad = 1$ is formed, establishing $a/b < h/k < c/d$.
