This landmark result, discovered independently by Hardy-Ramanujan (1918) and Uspensky (1920), gives the first-order asymptotic growth of the partition function. The dominant term $e^{\pi\sqrt{2n/3}}/(4n\sqrt{3})$ grows faster than any polynomial but slower than a pure exponential. The constant $K = \pi\sqrt{2/3}$ appears naturally from the modular transformation properties of the Dedekind eta function via the circle method. Hardy and Ramanujan's proof used a contour integral approach that Rademacher later refined into a convergent series.
