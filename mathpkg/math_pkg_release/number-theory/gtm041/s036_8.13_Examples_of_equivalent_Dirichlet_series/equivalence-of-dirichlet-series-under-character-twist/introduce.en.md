Theorem 8.17 establishes the Bohr equivalence between an ordinary Dirichlet series $\sum a(n)n^{-s}$ and its twist by a Dirichlet character $\chi$, provided that the coefficients $a(n)$ vanish whenever $n$ shares a common factor with the modulus $k$. The equivalence relies on Theorem 8.12 and the fact that a Dirichlet character $\chi$ is completely multiplicative and satisfies $|\chi(p)| = 1$ for all primes $p$ coprime to $k$. This result is a key application of Bohr's equivalence theorem to $L$-series.
