The sets $\{[\pi]: \|\pi(x)\| \leqslant r\}$ for $x$ in a dense subset of $A$ and rational $r$ form a countable separating family. For if $[\pi] \neq [\sigma]$, then $\pi$ and $\sigma$ are inequivalent irreducible representations, so there exists $x$ such that $\|\pi(x)\| \neq \|\sigma(x)\|$ by the definition of the Mackey Borel structure. $\square$
