(i)$\Rightarrow$(ii): Apply 2.1.8 to the identity representation of $\mathcal{R}'$. Central projections $C_n$ give subrepresentations of multiplicity $n$ for $\mathcal{R}'$, so $C_n\mathcal{R} \cong M_n(\mathcal{R}_0)$ with $\mathcal{R}_0'$ abelian. Choose any nonzero $\xi$, let $E = [\mathcal{R}_0'\xi]$. Then $E$ is an abelian projection. (ii)$\Rightarrow$(iii): The central cover of an abelian projection yields a homogeneous summand. $\square$
