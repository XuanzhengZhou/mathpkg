Choose an irreducible operator $T \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{K})$, and let $F(x) \equiv T$. Since $\mathcal{A}'' = \mathcal{Z}'$, $L_F \in \mathcal{A}''$. By the fiberwise characterization of the double commutant, $T = F(x) \in \pi_x(\mathcal{A})''$ a.e. Hence $\pi_x(\mathcal{A})'' = \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{K})$ a.e., meaning each $\pi_x$ is irreducible almost everywhere. $\square$
