Define $[x,y] = f(y^*x)$. This is a positive semidefinite sesquilinear form since $f(x^*x) \geqslant 0$. The standard Cauchy–Schwarz inequality for such forms gives $|[x,y]|^2 \leqslant [x,x][y,y]$, which translates to $|f(y^*x)|^2 \leqslant f(x^*x)f(y^*y)$. $\square$
