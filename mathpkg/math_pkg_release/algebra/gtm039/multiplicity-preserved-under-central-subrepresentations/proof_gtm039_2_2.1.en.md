(i) If $\pi \cong n \cdot \mu$, the commutant consists of $n \times n$ matrices over $\mu(A)'$. Central subrepresentations correspond to central projections, which are scalar matrices, preserving the $n$-fold structure. (ii) Write each $\sigma_j = \sum_{k=1}^n \sigma_{jk}$ with $\sigma_{jk}$ equivalent multiplicity-free. Summing first over $j$ gives $\mu_k = \sum_j \sigma_{jk}$, each multiplicity-free with all $\mu_k$ equivalent, yielding multiplicity $n$ for the sum. $\square$
