By 2.1.6(i), any central subrepresentation of multiplicity $n$ is contained in the $n$-homogeneous part constructed above. Thus the decomposition into homogeneous parts is uniquely determined. Moreover, if $n\cdot\sigma_n \cong n\cdot\sigma_n'$, then $\sigma_n \cong \sigma_n'$ by the discussion preceding 2.1.7. $\square$
