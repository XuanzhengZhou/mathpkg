For a self-adjoint $x$, the $C^*$-identity gives $\|x\|^2 = \|x^*x\| = \|x^2\|$. Replacing $x$ by $x^2$ yields $\|x\|^4 = \|x^4\|$, and inductively $\|x\|^{2^n} = \|x^{2^n}\|$. Hence $\|x\| = \lim_n \|x^n\|^{1/n} = r(x)$. For general normal $x$, $\|x\|^2 = \|x^*x\| = r(x^*x) = \|\widehat{x^*x}\|_\infty = \|\widehat{x}\|_\infty^2 = r(x)^2$, where $\widehat{\cdot}$ denotes the Gelfand transform on the commutative $C^*$-algebra generated by $x$. $\square$
