A cyclic representation of an abelian $C^*$-algebra has abelian commutant (since $\pi(A)$ is dense in $\pi(A)'$), hence is multiplicity-free. Conversely, 2.2.3 shows every multiplicity-free representation has a cyclic vector. For von Neumann algebras: $\mathcal{R}$ is maximal abelian iff $\mathcal{R}' = \mathcal{R}$ is abelian, i.e. the identity representation is multiplicity-free, equivalently cyclic. $\square$
