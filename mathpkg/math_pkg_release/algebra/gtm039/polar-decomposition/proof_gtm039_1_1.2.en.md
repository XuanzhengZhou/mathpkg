Define $U$ on $|T|\mathcal{H}$ by $U|T|\xi = T\xi$. Since $\||T|\xi\| = \|T\xi\|$, $U$ extends to an isometry from $[|T|\mathcal{H}]$ onto $[T\mathcal{H}]$. Extend by 0 on the orthogonal complement. Then $U$ is a partial isometry satisfying $U|T| = T$. The initial space is $[|T|\mathcal{H}] = (\ker T)^\perp$. For uniqueness, any other factorization $T = VR$ with $R \geqslant 0$ and $V$ a partial isometry with initial space $[R\mathcal{H}]$ forces $R^2 = T^*T$, so $R = |T|$ and $V = U$ on $[|T|\mathcal{H}]$. $\square$
