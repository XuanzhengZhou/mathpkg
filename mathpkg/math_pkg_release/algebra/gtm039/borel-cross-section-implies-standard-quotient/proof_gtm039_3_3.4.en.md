If $g$ is a Borel cross section, then $g$ is 1-1 and its image $g(Y)$ is a Borel subset of $X$ by 3.3.2. Thus $Y$ is Borel-isomorphic to a Borel set, hence standard. Conversely, if the range is Borel, Dixmier's theorem gives a cross section. $\square$
