Let $\pi$ be type I. For each $n$, take a maximal family $\{\mu_i^{(n)}\}$ of mutually orthogonal, equivalent, multiplicity-free subrepresentations. Define $\pi_n = \sum_i \mu_i^{(n)}$. By maximality and 2.1.6, each $\pi_n$ is a central subrepresentation of multiplicity $n$. Separability ensures the sum over $n$ exhausts $\pi$. Each $\pi_n \cong n \cdot \sigma_n$ for a uniquely determined multiplicity-free $\sigma_n$. Uniqueness follows because any central subrepresentation of multiplicity $n$ must be contained in $\pi_n$. $\square$
