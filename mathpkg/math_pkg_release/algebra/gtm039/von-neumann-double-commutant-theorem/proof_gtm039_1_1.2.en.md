Clearly $\mathcal{A} \subseteq \mathcal{A}''$ and $\mathcal{A}''$ is strongly closed, so $\mathcal{A}_s \subseteq \mathcal{A}''$. For the reverse, use the $n$-tuple reduction: for $T \in \mathcal{A}''$ and $\xi_1,\ldots,\xi_n$, work on $\mathcal{H}^n$ with the diagonal algebra. The case $n=1$: $P$ = projection onto $[\mathcal{A}\xi_1]$. Then $P \in \mathcal{A}'$, so $T$ commutes with $P$ and leaves $[\mathcal{A}\xi_1]$ invariant. Hence $T\xi_1 \in [\mathcal{A}\xi_1]$, so $T\xi_1$ can be approximated by $S\xi_1$ with $S \in \mathcal{A}$. $\square$
