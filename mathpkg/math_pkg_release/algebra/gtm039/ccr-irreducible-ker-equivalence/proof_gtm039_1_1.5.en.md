Since $A$ is CCR, $\pi(A) = \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H})$ and $\sigma(A) = \mathcal{C}(\mathcal{K})$. The kernel inclusion gives a well-defined $*$-homomorphism $\lambda: \pi(x) \mapsto \sigma(x)$ from $\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{H})$ onto $\mathcal{C}(\mathcal{K})$. By Corollary 3 of 1.4.4, every $*$-isomorphism between algebras of compact operators is implemented by a unitary, so $\pi \sim \sigma$. $\square$
