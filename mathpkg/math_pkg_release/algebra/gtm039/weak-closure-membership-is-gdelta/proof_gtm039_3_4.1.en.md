By Kaplansky's density theorem, $T \in \pi(A)''$ iff $T \in \pi(A_r)^{-w}$ for $r > \|T\|$. For a dense sequence $\{\xi_i\}$, define $f_{n,x}(\pi) = \sum_{i,j=1}^n |((\pi(x)-T)\xi_i,\xi_j)|$. Then $T \in \pi(A_r)^{-w}$ iff $\inf_{x \in A_r} f_{n,x}(\pi) = 0$ for all $n$. This is a countable intersection of open conditions, hence $G_\delta$. $\square$
