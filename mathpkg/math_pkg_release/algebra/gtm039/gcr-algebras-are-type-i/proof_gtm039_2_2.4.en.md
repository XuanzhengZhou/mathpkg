Let $\pi$ be a nondegenerate representation and $C$ a nonzero central projection in $\pi(A)'$. Using the CCR composition series $\{J_\alpha\}$ (1.5.5), choose the first $\alpha$ such that $\pi(J_\alpha) \neq 0$ on $C\mathcal{H}$. Then $\alpha$ has a predecessor and $J_\alpha/J_{\alpha-1}$ is CCR. The restriction of $\pi$ to this CCR algebra contains an irreducible subrepresentation, which extends to $A$ and yields a multiplicity-free subrepresentation of $\pi$ under $C$. $\square$
