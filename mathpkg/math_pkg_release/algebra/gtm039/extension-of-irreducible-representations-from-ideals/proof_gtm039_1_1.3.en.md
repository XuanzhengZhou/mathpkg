If $\pi$ is irreducible and $\pi(J) \neq 0$, then for $\xi \neq 0$, $[\pi(J)\xi]$ is $\pi(A)$-invariant, so it is either $\{0\}$ or $\mathcal{H}$. If $\{0\}$, then $\xi \perp [\pi(J)\mathcal{H}]$, forcing $[\pi(J)\mathcal{H}] = 0$, contradiction. Hence $\pi|_J$ is irreducible. Conversely, let $\pi$ be irreducible on $J$. Define $\tilde{\pi}(x)$ on $\mathcal{H}$ by $\tilde{\pi}(x)\pi(y)\xi = \pi(xy)\xi$. Using an approximate unit, $\|\pi(xy)\xi\| \leqslant \|x\|\|\pi(y)\xi\|$, so $\tilde{\pi}(x)$ is bounded. Uniqueness and equivalence preservation are straightforward. $\square$
