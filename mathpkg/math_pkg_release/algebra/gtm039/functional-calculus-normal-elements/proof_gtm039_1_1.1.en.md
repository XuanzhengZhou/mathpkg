The map $\omega \mapsto \omega(x)$ from $\hat{B}$ to $\mathbb{C}$ is continuous and injective. Since $\hat{B}$ is compact, it is a homeomorphism onto its image $\text{sp}(x)$. Composing with the Gelfand transform yields an isometric $*$-isomorphism $C(\text{sp}(x)) \cong B$. For polynomial $f(\zeta,\bar{\zeta})$, the image $f(x)$ coincides with the polynomial evaluated at $x,x^*$. For general continuous $f$, it is obtained by uniform approximation. $\square$
