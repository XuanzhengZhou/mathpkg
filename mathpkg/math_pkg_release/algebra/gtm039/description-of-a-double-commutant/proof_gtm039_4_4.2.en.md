By Kaplansky's density theorem, for self-adjoint $L_F \in \mathcal{A}''$, there exist $T_n = T_n^* \in \mathcal{A}$ with $T_n \to L_F$ strongly. By the strong convergence lemma, a subsequence satisfies $\pi(T_n)(x) \to F(x)$ strongly a.e. Thus $F(x) \in \pi_x(\mathcal{A})''$ a.e. The converse inclusion is similar. $\square$
