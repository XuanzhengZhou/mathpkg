The space of skew-symmetric tensors of rank $r$ has a natural basis indexed by $r$-element subsets of $\{1, \ldots, n\}$, with elements $[e_{i_1} e_{i_2} \cdots e_{i_r}]$ defined as the antisymmetrized product of basis vectors with $i_1 < i_2 < \cdots < i_r$. The dimension of this space is therefore $\binom{n}{r}$. This basis is fundamental for the definition of the $r$th compound of a matrix.
