Let $(\alpha)$ be the matrix of $A$ with entries $\alpha_{ij}$ so that $e_i A = \sum_j \alpha_{ij} e_j$. Then
$$[e_{i_1} e_{i_2} \cdots e_{i_r}] A_0^r = \sum_{j_1 < j_2 < \cdots < j_r} \begin{vmatrix}
\alpha_{i_1, j_1} & \alpha_{i_1, j_2} & \cdots & \alpha_{i_1, j_r} \\
\alpha_{i_2, j_1} & \alpha_{i_2, j_2} & \cdots & \alpha_{i_2, j_r} \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
\alpha_{i_r, j_1} & \alpha_{i_r, j_2} & \cdots & \alpha_{i_r, j_r}
\end{vmatrix} [e_{j_1} e_{j_2} \cdots e_{j_r}]. \tag{30},(31)$$
Thus the $(I, J)$-entry of $C_r(\alpha)$ (where $I = (i_1 < \cdots < i_r)$ and $J = (j_1 < \cdots < j_r)$ are ordered $r$-tuples) is the $r \times r$ minor $\det(\alpha_{i_p, j_q})_{p,q=1}^r$.
