Every real normal matrix can be put into a block diagonal canonical form by an orthogonal similarity transformation. The diagonal blocks are either $1 \times 1$ (real eigenvalues) or $2 \times 2$ blocks of the form $\begin{bmatrix} \rho & \epsilon \\ -\epsilon & \rho \end{bmatrix}$ corresponding to complex conjugate eigenvalue pairs $\rho \pm i\epsilon$. This generalizes the symmetric (purely diagonal) and skew (purely $2 \times 2$ skew blocks) canonical forms.
