If $(\alpha)$ is a real normal matrix, there exists an orthogonal matrix $(\sigma)$ such that $(\sigma)(\alpha)(\sigma)^{-1}$ has the block diagonal form
$$\begin{bmatrix}
(\beta_1) & & \\
& (\beta_2) & \\
& & \ddots
\end{bmatrix}$$
where each $(\beta_i)$ is either a one-rowed matrix (real scalar) or a two-rowed matrix of the form
$$(\beta_i) = \begin{bmatrix} \rho_i & \epsilon_i \\ -\epsilon_i & \rho_i \end{bmatrix}, \quad \epsilon_i \neq 0.$$
