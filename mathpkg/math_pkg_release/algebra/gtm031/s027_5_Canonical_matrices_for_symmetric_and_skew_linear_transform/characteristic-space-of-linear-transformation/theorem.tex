Let $A$ be a symmetric or skew linear transformation on a Euclidean space, and let $f(\lambda)$ be its characteristic polynomial.

\begin{itemize}
\item If $A$ is \textbf{symmetric} and $\rho$ is a root of $f(\lambda)$, then the subspace
$$\mathfrak{R}_{\lambda-\rho} = \{ y \mid yA = \rho y \}$$
is called the \textbf{characteristic space} corresponding to the root $\rho$ (or to the factor $\lambda - \rho$ of $f(\lambda)$).

\item If $A$ is \textbf{skew} and $\lambda$ is a factor of $f(\lambda)$, the \textbf{characteristic space} $\mathfrak{R}_{\lambda}$ is defined analogously: $\mathfrak{R}_{\lambda} = \{ y \mid yA = 0 \}$.

\item For an irreducible quadratic factor $\pi(\lambda) = \lambda^2 + \delta^2$, $\delta \neq 0$, the characteristic space $\mathfrak{R}_{\pi(\lambda)}$ is defined correspondingly.
\end{itemize}
