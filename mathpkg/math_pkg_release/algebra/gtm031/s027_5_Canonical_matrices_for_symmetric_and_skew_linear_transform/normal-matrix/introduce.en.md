A normal matrix is one that commutes with its own transpose. This property is preserved under orthogonal changes of basis, and a linear transformation on a Euclidean space is normal precisely when its matrix representation with respect to any Cartesian (orthonormal) basis is a normal matrix. The class of normal matrices includes symmetric, skew symmetric, and orthogonal matrices.
