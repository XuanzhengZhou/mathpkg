If $(\alpha)$ is a real symmetric matrix, then there exists a real orthogonal matrix $(\sigma)$ such that
$$(\sigma)(\alpha)(\sigma)^{-1} = \operatorname{diag}\{\rho_1, \rho_2, \ldots, \rho_n\},$$
where the $\rho_i$ are the characteristic roots of $(\alpha)$. Equivalently, every real symmetric linear transformation is orthogonally diagonalizable.
