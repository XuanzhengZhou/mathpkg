A positive definite linear transformation on a Euclidean space is a symmetric transformation whose associated bilinear form $(xA, y)$ is a genuine inner product -- strictly positive on all nonzero vectors. This property implies the transformation is injective and all its characteristic roots are strictly positive. Positive definite transformations play a central role in the polar decomposition of arbitrary linear transformations.
