This proposition establishes a bijective correspondence between invariant subspaces of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ (relative to a set of linear transformations $\Omega$) that contain a fixed invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, and invariant subspaces of the quotient space $\bar{\mathfrak{R}} = \mathfrak{R}/\mathfrak{S}$ (relative to the induced transformations $\bar{\Omega}$). The forward direction is straightforward: if $\mathfrak{U}$ is invariant and contains $\mathfrak{S}$, then $\mathfrak{U}/\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant in the quotient. The converse, proved by lifting cosets back to vectors in $\mathfrak{R}$, shows that this correspondence is a lattice isomorphism between the two posets of invariant subspaces.
