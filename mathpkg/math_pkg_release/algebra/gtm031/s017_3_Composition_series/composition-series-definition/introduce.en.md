A composition series is a maximal chain of invariant subspaces for a set $\Omega$ of linear transformations acting on a finite-dimensional vector space. Each step $\mathfrak{S}_{i-1} \subset \mathfrak{S}_i$ cannot be refined further: the quotient $\mathfrak{S}_i/\mathfrak{S}_{i-1}$ admits no proper nonzero invariant subspace under the induced transformations. This is the linear-algebraic analogue of the Jordan-Hölder composition series for groups or modules, and it provides the foundation for decomposing a vector space into irreducible constituents relative to a family of operators.
