A subspace $\mathfrak{U}$ is irreducible over a smaller invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ when the quotient $\mathfrak{U}/\mathfrak{S}$ has no nontrivial invariant subspaces under the induced transformations. This is the "atomic" step condition in a composition series: you cannot insert an intermediate invariant subspace between $\mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{U}$. In matrix language, irreducibility means no simultaneous change of basis can put all the induced transformation matrices into a common reduced block diagonal or block triangular form with smaller blocks.
