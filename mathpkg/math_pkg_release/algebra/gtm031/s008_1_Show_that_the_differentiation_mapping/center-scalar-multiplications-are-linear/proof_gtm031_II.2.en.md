For $\gamma \in \Phi$ (the center of $\Delta$), define $\gamma_l(x) = \gamma x$. We verify linearity:

1. $\gamma_l(x + y) = \gamma(x + y) = \gamma x + \gamma y = \gamma_l(x) + \gamma_l(y)$.

2. $\gamma_l(\alpha x) = \gamma(\alpha x)$. Since $\gamma \in \Phi$, $\gamma$ commutes with $\alpha$, so $\gamma(\alpha x) = (\gamma\alpha)x = (\alpha\gamma)x = \alpha(\gamma x) = \alpha\,\gamma_l(x)$.

Thus $\gamma_l$ is an endomorphism of the additive group and satisfies the scalar compatibility condition, hence $\gamma_l \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$.
