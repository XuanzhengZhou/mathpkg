The identity mapping $1$ is the linear transformation of $\mathfrak{R}$ into itself defined by

$$x1 = x$$

for all $x \in \mathfrak{R}$. It is the multiplicative identity in the ring $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$, satisfying $A1 = A = 1A$ for all $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$.
