The identity linear transformation maps every vector to itself. Denoted by $1$, it serves as the multiplicative identity in the ring $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ of linear transformations on a single vector space. That is, composing any linear transformation $A$ with the identity on either side leaves $A$ unchanged: $A1 = A = 1A$.
