Let $\Delta$ be a division ring and $\Phi$ its center. The set of $n_1 \times n_2$ matrices over $\Delta$ is a vector space over $\Delta$ of dimension $n_1 n_2$, with addition defined entrywise and scalar multiplication $\rho(\alpha)$ having entries $\rho \alpha_{ij}$. When $\Delta = \Phi$ is a field, the correspondence $A \mapsto (\alpha)$ between linear transformations and their matrices (relative to fixed bases) is a vector space isomorphism. Consequently, $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2) \cong \Phi^{n_1 n_2}$ as vector spaces over $\Phi$.
