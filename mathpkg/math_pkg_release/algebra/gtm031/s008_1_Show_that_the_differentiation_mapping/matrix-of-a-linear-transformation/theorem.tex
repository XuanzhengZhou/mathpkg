Let $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$, let $(e_1, \dots, e_{n_1})$ be a basis of $\mathfrak{R}_1$, and let $(f_1, \dots, f_{n_2})$ be a basis of $\mathfrak{R}_2$. Write

$$e_i A = \sum_{j=1}^{n_2} \alpha_{ij} f_j, \quad i = 1, 2, \dots, n_1.$$

Then the $n_1 \times n_2$ matrix $(\alpha) = (\alpha_{ij})$ with entries in $\Delta$ is called the **matrix of $A$ relative to** $(e_1, \dots, e_{n_1})$ and $(f_1, \dots, f_{n_2})$.

For any $x = \sum_i \xi_i e_i \in \mathfrak{R}_1$, the image is
$$xA = \sum_{i,j} \xi_i \alpha_{ij} f_j.$$
