The composition (or product) of two linear transformations $A: \mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$ and $B: \mathfrak{R}_2 \to \mathfrak{R}_3$ is defined as the mapping that first applies $A$ and then $B$. This operation generalizes matrix multiplication and is associative. Composition equips the set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ of linear transformations on a single vector space with a ring structure.
