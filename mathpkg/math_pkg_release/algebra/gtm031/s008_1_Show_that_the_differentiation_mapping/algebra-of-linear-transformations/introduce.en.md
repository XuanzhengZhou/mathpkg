An algebra over a field is a system that is simultaneously a ring and a vector space over the field, with the compatibility condition $\gamma(AB) = (\gamma A)B = A(\gamma B)$. The set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ of all linear transformations on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ forms an algebra over the center field $\Phi = Z(\Delta)$. This unifies the additive, multiplicative, and scalar-multiplicative structures of linear operators.
