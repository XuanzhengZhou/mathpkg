Let $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ be the set of linear transformations of $\mathfrak{R}_1$ into $\mathfrak{R}_2$, and let $+$ be the addition defined by $x(A+B) = xA + xB$. Then $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ with the operation $+$ is a commutative group. The zero mapping $0$ defined by $x0 = 0$ serves as the identity element, and for any $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$, the mapping $-A$ defined by $x(-A) = -xA$ serves as the additive inverse.
