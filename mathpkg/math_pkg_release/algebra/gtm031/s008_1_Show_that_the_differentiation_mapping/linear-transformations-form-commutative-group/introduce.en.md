The set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_1, \mathfrak{R}_2)$ of all linear transformations from $\mathfrak{R}_1$ to $\mathfrak{R}_2$, equipped with pointwise addition, forms a commutative group. The zero mapping acts as the identity element, and each transformation $A$ has an additive inverse $-A$ defined by $x(-A) = -xA$. This group structure is the foundation for the richer algebraic structures of rings and algebras of linear transformations introduced later.
