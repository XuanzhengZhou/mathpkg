Let $\mathfrak{A}_1$ and $\mathfrak{A}_2$ be algebras over a field $\Phi$ with multiplication maps $P_1$ and $P_2$ respectively. The Kronecker product algebra $\mathfrak{A} = \mathfrak{A}_1 \times \mathfrak{A}_2$ is the Kronecker product of the underlying vector spaces equipped with the linear map $P$ defined by composing

$$(\mathfrak{A}_1 \times \mathfrak{A}_2) \times (\mathfrak{A}_1 \times \mathfrak{A}_2) \cong \mathfrak{A}_1 \times \mathfrak{A}_1 \times \mathfrak{A}_2 \times \mathfrak{A}_2 \xrightarrow{P_1 \times P_2} \mathfrak{A}_1 \times \mathfrak{A}_2.$$

Equivalently, for elements $\Sigma x_1 \times x_2$ and $\Sigma y_1 \times y_2$ in $\mathfrak{A}$,

$$(\Sigma x_1 \times x_2)(\Sigma y_1 \times y_2) = \Sigma x_1 y_1 \times x_2 y_2.$$
