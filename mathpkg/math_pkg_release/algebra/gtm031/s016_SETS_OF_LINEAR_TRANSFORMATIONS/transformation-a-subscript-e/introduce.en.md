When studying a linear transformation $A$ relative to an invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, the reduced matrix form reveals three blocks: the restriction to $\mathfrak{S}$ (upper-left), the induced transformation on the quotient (lower-right), and a remaining lower-left block. This third block corresponds to the transformation $A_E = AE - EA$, where $E$ is a projection onto $\mathfrak{S}$. The map $A_E$ sends elements of the quotient space $\bar{\Re}$ into $\mathfrak{S}$ and captures the "mixing" between the subspace and its complement. The relation $AE - EA = PA_E$ connects $A_E$ to the projection $E$ and natural mapping $P$.
