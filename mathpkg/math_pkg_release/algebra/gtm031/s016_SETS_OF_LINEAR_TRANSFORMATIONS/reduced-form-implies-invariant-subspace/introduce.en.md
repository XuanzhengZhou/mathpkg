If all matrices in a set $\Omega$ of linear transformations can be simultaneously brought to reduced (block upper-triangular) form with the same zero block structure via a single change of basis, then the span of the first few basis vectors forms a proper invariant subspace. This is the converse of the statement that reducibility implies the existence of a reduced form, and together they establish an equivalence between the geometric notion of reducibility and the algebraic condition of simultaneous block triangularization.
