Let $\Omega$ be a set of linear transformations in a finite dimensional vector space $\Re$ over a division ring $\Delta$, and let $\omega$ be the set of matrices of $\Omega$ relative to a basis $(e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_n)$.

If there exists a non-singular matrix $(\mu)$ such that all the matrices of $(\mu)\omega(\mu)^{-1}$ have the same reduced form (i.e., an $r \times (n-r)$ block of zeros in the upper right corner, with the same $r$ for all $A \in \Omega$), then the space $\mathfrak{S} = [f_1, f_2, \ldots, f_r]$ spanned by the first $r$ vectors of the new basis is a proper invariant subspace under $\Omega$, so $\Omega$ is reducible.
