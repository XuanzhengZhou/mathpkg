Given a vector space $\Re$ and a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, the natural mapping (canonical projection) $P$ sends each vector $x$ to its coset $x + \mathfrak{S}$ in the quotient space $\bar{\Re}$. This mapping is a surjective linear transformation and serves as the fundamental bridge between the original space and the quotient. When $\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant under a linear transformation $A$, the natural mapping relates $A$ to its induced transformation $\bar{A}$ on the quotient via the commutativity relation $P\bar{A} = AP$.
