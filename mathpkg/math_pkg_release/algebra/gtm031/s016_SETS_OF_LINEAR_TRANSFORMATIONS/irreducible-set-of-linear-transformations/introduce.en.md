A set $\Omega$ of linear transformations in a vector space $\Re$ is called irreducible if no proper nontrivial subspace of $\Re$ is invariant under every transformation in $\Omega$. In other words, the only $\Omega$-invariant subspaces are the zero space and $\Re$ itself. For a single linear transformation $A$, the cyclic subspaces provide nontrivial invariant subspaces, so a single transformation is never irreducible. The concept of irreducibility is central to the theory of representations of rings and algebras.
