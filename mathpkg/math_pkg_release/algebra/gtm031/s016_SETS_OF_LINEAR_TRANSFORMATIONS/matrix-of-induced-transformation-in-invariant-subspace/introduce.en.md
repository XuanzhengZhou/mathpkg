When the matrix of a linear transformation $A$ is written in reduced (block upper-triangular) form with respect to a basis adapted to an invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, the upper-left diagonal block is precisely the matrix of the restriction $A|_{\mathfrak{S}}$. This gives a concrete matrix interpretation of how $A$ acts within the invariant subspace. The lower-right block similarly represents the induced transformation on the quotient space.
