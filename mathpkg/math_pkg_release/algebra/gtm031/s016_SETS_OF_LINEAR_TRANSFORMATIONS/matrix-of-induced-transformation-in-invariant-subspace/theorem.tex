Let $\Re$ be a vector space with basis $(f_1, \ldots, f_n)$ such that $(f_1, \ldots, f_r)$ is a basis for an invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ under $A \in \Omega$. Let the matrix of $A$ relative to this basis have the reduced form

$$\begin{bmatrix}
A_{11} & 0 \\
A_{21} & A_{22}
\end{bmatrix}$$

where $A_{11}$ is $r \times r$, $A_{22}$ is $(n-r) \times (n-r)$, and the upper-right $r \times (n-r)$ block is zero.

Then the $r \times r$ block $A_{11}$ is a matrix of the linear transformation induced by $A$ in the invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ (i.e., the restriction $A|_{\mathfrak{S}}$) relative to the basis $(f_1, \ldots, f_r)$.
