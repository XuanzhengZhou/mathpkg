Let $\Omega$ be a set of linear transformations, and let $B$ be a linear transformation that commutes with every $A \in \Omega$. Show that if $\mathfrak{S}$ is an invariant subspace relative to $\Omega$, then $\mathfrak{S}B$ is also invariant. Also, show that the subset of vectors $y \in \mathfrak{S}$ such that $yB = 0$ is invariant relative to $\Omega$.
