Any ring $\mathfrak{R}$ containing a division subring $\Delta$ can be regarded as a vector space over $\Delta$ by using the ring multiplication as scalar multiplication. This construction shows that vector spaces arise naturally in ring theory. For example, the field of complex numbers is a vector space over the real numbers, and the polynomial ring $\Delta[\lambda]$ is a vector space over $\Delta$.
