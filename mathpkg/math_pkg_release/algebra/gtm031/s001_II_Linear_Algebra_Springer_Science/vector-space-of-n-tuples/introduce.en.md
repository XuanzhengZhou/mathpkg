The vector space $\Delta^{(n)}$ of $n$-tuples over a division ring $\Delta$ is the canonical example of a finite-dimensional vector space. Any $n$-dimensional vector space over $\Delta$ is isomorphic to $\Delta^{(n)}$. The componentwise addition and scalar multiplication generalize the familiar operations on coordinate vectors in three-dimensional real analytic geometry.
