Let $\Phi$ be a field and let $\mathfrak{L}$ be the ring of all linear transformations of a vector space over $\Phi$. Then the center of $\mathfrak{L}$ is the set $\Phi_1 = \{\alpha 1 : \alpha \in \Phi\}$ of scalar multiplications. In matrix terms, the only $n \times n$ matrices over $\Phi$ that commute with every $n \times n$ matrix are the scalar matrices $\alpha I$.
