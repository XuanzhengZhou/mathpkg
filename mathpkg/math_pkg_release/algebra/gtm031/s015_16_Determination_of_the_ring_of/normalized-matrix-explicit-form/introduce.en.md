Using the divisibility relations $\delta_i \mid \delta_j$ for $i \leq j$, the general matrix condition $\delta_i \beta_{ij} \equiv 0 \pmod{\delta_j}$ simplifies into four cases. Below the diagonal ($i \geq j$), entries are unconstrained. Above the diagonal but before the zero-invariant-factor region ($i < j \leq u$), entries are constrained modulo $\eta_{ij} = \delta_i^{-1}\delta_j$. Entries whose row index is at or before $u$ and column after $u$ must vanish. Beyond $u$, all entries are free since the corresponding invariant factors are zero.
