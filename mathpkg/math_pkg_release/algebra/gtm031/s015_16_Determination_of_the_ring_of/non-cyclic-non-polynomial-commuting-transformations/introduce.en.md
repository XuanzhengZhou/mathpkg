This corollary is the converse to the statement that if $A$ is cyclic, every transformation commuting with $A$ is a polynomial in $A$. The proof uses the dimension computation: when $t > 1$, the dimension of $\mathfrak{B}$ as a vector space over $\Phi$ strictly exceeds the dimension of $\Phi[A]$, so there must exist elements in $\mathfrak{B}$ that are not polynomials in $A$. The condition $t = 1$ is exactly the definition of a cyclic transformation.
