Theorem 20 characterizes the center of the endomorphism ring $\mathfrak{B}$ of a finitely generated module over a principal ideal domain: it consists exactly of the scalar multiplications $x \mapsto \alpha x$. The proof uses special endomorphisms $E_k$ (projection-like maps sending $f_j$ to $\delta_{jk} f_k$) and the fact that any central element must commute with these, forcing it to act as a scalar on each generator.
