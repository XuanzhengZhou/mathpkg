Theorem 7 is the main structural result for sets of commutative matrices over an algebraically closed field. It states that any such set can be simultaneously put into block diagonal form, where each diagonal block is a lower triangular matrix with a single eigenvalue repeated on and below the diagonal. The proof combines the decomposition of the space into indecomposable components with a composition series argument: on each composition factor, the induced transformation acts as a scalar, forcing the blocks to be one-dimensional and the matrices to be triangular.
