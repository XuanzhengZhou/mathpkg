Let $\Phi$ be an algebraically closed field and let $\omega$ be a set of commutative matrices belonging to $\Phi_n$. Then there exists a non-singular matrix $(\mu)$ in $\Phi_n$ such that for all $(\alpha) \in \omega$,

$$(\mu)(\alpha)(\mu)^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix}
(\alpha_1) & & \\
& (\alpha_2) & \\
& & \ddots \\
& & & (\alpha_n)
\end{bmatrix}$$

where each block $(\alpha_i)$ has the form

$$(\alpha_i) = \begin{bmatrix}
\rho_i & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\rho_i & \rho_i & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
\rho_i & \rho_i & \cdots & \rho_i
\end{bmatrix}$$

with $\rho_i \in \Phi$ depending on $(\alpha)$.
