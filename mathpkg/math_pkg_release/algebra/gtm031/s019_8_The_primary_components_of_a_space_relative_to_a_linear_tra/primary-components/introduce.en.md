The primary components of a vector space relative to a linear transformation $A$ are a canonical collection of $A$-invariant subspaces defined by the factorization of the minimum polynomial of $A$. For each distinct irreducible factor $\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$ of $\mu(\lambda)$, the corresponding primary component $\Re_i$ consists of all vectors annihilated by $\pi_i(A)^{k_i}$. Unlike the decomposition into indecomposable subspaces, the primary decomposition $\Re = \Re_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus \Re_s$ is uniquely determined and provides a coarser but structurally essential invariant of the linear transformation.
