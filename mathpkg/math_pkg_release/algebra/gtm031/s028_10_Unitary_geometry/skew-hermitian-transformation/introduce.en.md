A skew-hermitian linear transformation satisfies $A' = -A$, meaning its adjoint equals its negative. In a unitary basis, its matrix is skew-hermitian (the negative of its conjugate transpose). The theory of skew-hermitian transformations reduces to that of hermitian transformations, since if $A$ is skew-hermitian then $iA$ is hermitian.
