Positive definite hermitian transformations are the complex analogue of positive definite symmetric transformations in Euclidean space. The definition uses the real-valued quadratic form $(xA, x)$ associated with a hermitian transformation $A$. The theory parallels the Euclidean case, including the result that any positive definite hermitian transformation has a unique positive definite square root, and the polar decomposition of invertible linear transformations.
