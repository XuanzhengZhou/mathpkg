Let $A$ be unitary, so $A'A = 1$. Suppose $yA = \rho y$ with $y \neq 0$. Then
$$
|\rho|^2 (y, y) = (\rho y, \rho y) = (yA, yA) = (yA, yA)
$$
Since $A$ is unitary, $AA' = 1$, and thus $(yA, yA) = (yAA', y) = (y, y)$. Therefore
$$
|\rho|^2 (y, y) = (y, y)
$$
and since $(y, y) > 0$ (positive definiteness), we obtain $|\rho|^2 = 1$, so $|\rho| = 1$.
