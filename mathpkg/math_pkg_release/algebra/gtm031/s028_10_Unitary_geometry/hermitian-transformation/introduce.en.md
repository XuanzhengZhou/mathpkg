A hermitian linear transformation is the complex analogue of a symmetric linear transformation in Euclidean space. It satisfies $A' = A$, meaning it equals its own adjoint. In a unitary basis, the matrix of a hermitian transformation is a hermitian matrix, i.e., equal to its own conjugate transpose. Hermitian transformations have real characteristic roots and are diagonalizable by a unitary change of basis.
