This theorem generalizes the spectral theorem to a family of commuting matrices. Any set of pairwise commuting hermitian and skew-hermitian matrices can be simultaneously diagonalized by a single unitary change of basis. The result extends to normal matrices (including unitary matrices). For unitary matrices, all characteristic roots lie on the unit circle, which follows from the fact that $|\rho|^2(y, y) = (yA, yA) = (y, y)$ when $yA = \rho y$.
