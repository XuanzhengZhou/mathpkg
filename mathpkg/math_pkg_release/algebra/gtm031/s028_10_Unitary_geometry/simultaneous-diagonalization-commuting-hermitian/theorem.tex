Let $\omega$ be a set of pairwise commutative hermitian and skew-hermitian matrices with complex entries. Then there exists a single unitary matrix $(\sigma)$ such that for every $(\alpha) \in \omega$, the matrix $(\sigma)(\alpha)(\sigma)^{-1}$ is diagonal. Moreover, the same conclusion holds for normal matrices (and consequently for unitary matrices), with the word "real" omitted from the diagonal entries. For unitary matrices, the characteristic roots are of absolute value $1$.
