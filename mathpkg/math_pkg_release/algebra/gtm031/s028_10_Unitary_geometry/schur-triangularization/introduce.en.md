Theorem 14, known as Schur's triangularization theorem, states that every complex square matrix is unitarily similar to an upper triangular matrix. This is a fundamental result in linear algebra that holds over the complex numbers thanks to algebraic closure. The proof proceeds by induction on the dimension: one extracts an eigenvector, extends to a unitary basis, and applies the induction hypothesis to the lower-right block.
