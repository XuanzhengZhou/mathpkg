By Theorem 10 the division algebra of linear transformations commuting with the $A \in \mathfrak{A}$ is $\Phi_l$. Hence by the density theorem, $\mathfrak{A}$ is a dense set of linear transformations of $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Phi$. Since $\mathfrak{R}$ has a finite basis over $\Phi$, this implies that $\mathfrak{A} = \mathfrak{L}$.
