Burnside's Theorem is a classical result in the theory of algebras of linear transformations. It states that any nonzero irreducible algebra of linear transformations on a finite-dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field must be the full endomorphism algebra. The theorem follows directly from Theorem 10 (which characterizes the commutant of an irreducible set) and the General Density Theorem. It is fundamental in representation theory, as it implies that an irreducible representation of an algebra over an algebraically closed field is surjective onto the full matrix algebra.
