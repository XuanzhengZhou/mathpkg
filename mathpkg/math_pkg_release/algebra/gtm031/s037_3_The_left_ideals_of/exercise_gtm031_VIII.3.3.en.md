Prove that, if $\mathfrak{J}'$ is a left ideal in the matrix ring $\Delta_n$, where $\Delta$ is a division ring, then there exists a matrix $(\mu)$ in $\mathrm{GL}(\Delta, n)$ such that $(\mu)\mathfrak{J}'(\mu)^{-1}$ is the set of matrices of the form

$$\begin{bmatrix}
\alpha_{11} & \alpha_{12} & \cdots & \alpha_{1r} & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\alpha_{21} & \alpha_{22} & \cdots & \alpha_{2r} & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
\alpha_{n1} & \alpha_{n2} & \cdots & \alpha_{nr} & 0 & \cdots & 0
\end{bmatrix}$$

where the first $r$ columns are arbitrary and the remaining $n-r$ columns are zero, for some $0 \leq r \leq n$. (That is, every left ideal of $\Delta_n$ is equivalent under conjugation to the set of matrices whose nonzero entries are confined to the first $r$ columns.)
