A mapping $U$ of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}_1$ over a division ring $\Delta_1$ into a vector space $\mathfrak{R}_2$ over a division ring $\Delta_2$ is called a semilinear transformation if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is a homomorphism of the additive groups: $(x + y)U = xU + yU$ for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{R}_1$.
\item There exists an isomorphism $u: \Delta_1 \to \Delta_2$ such that
$$(\alpha x)U = \alpha^u (xU)$$
for all $\alpha \in \Delta_1$ and $x \in \mathfrak{R}_1$.
\end{enumerate}
