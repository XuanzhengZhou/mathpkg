This is a fundamental structure theorem for rings of linear transformations (endomorphism rings of vector spaces). It states that the isomorphism type of the ring of all linear transformations on a vector space completely determines both the underlying division ring (up to isomorphism) and the dimension of the vector space. In categorical terms, the endomorphism ring functor from vector spaces over division rings is "injective on objects" up to isomorphism of the base ring and equality of dimension. The proof constructs a semi-linear transformation between the two vector spaces using minimal right ideals.
