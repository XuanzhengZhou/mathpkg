The element $\beta$ of $\Delta$ is said to be \textbf{represented} by the scalar product $g$ if there exists a vector $u \neq 0$ such that $g(u, u) = \beta$. Since $g(u, u) = \overline{g(u, u)}$, we have $\beta = \bar{\beta}$; hence the elements represented by the scalar product are invariant under the anti-automorphism.
