Sylvester's law of inertia states that for a real symmetric matrix (or equivalently, a real quadratic form), the number of positive diagonal entries in any diagonal representation is an invariant of the cogredience class. This number $p$ is called the **signature** or **inertial index** of the matrix. Together with the rank $r$, the pair $(p, r-p)$ (where $r-p$ is the number of negative entries) provides a complete invariant for real symmetric matrices under cogredience.
