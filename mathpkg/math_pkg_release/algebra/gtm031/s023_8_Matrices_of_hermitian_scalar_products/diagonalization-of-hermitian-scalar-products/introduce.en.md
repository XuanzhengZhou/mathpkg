Theorem 3 is the fundamental diagonalization theorem for hermitian scalar products. It asserts that every finite-dimensional vector space equipped with a hermitian scalar product admits an orthogonal basis in which the scalar product matrix is diagonal, with the first $r$ diagonal entries non-zero and all remaining entries zero. The number $r$ is precisely the rank of the scalar product. The constructive proof uses the Lagrange method: inductively extract non-isotropic vectors using the preceding Lemma.
