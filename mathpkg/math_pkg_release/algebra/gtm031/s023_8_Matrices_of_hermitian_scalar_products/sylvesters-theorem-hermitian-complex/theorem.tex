Let $\Phi$ be the field of complex numbers with the usual conjugation mapping $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$. Let $g(x, y)$ be a hermitian scalar product. If $\{\beta_1, \cdots, \beta_n\}$ and $\{\beta_1', \cdots, \beta_n'\}$ are cogredient in the sense that there exists a matrix $(\mu) \in L(\Phi, n)$ such that

$$\{\beta_1', \cdots, \beta_n'\} = (\mu)\{\beta_1, \cdots, \beta_n\}(\bar{\mu})',$$

then the number of positive $\beta_i$ equals the number of positive $\beta_i'$. Consequently, Sylvester's law of inertia holds for complex hermitian matrices.
