Sylvester's law of inertia extends to hermitian scalar products over the complex numbers. For a hermitian form $g(x, y)$ with $g(u, u)$ always real, a diagonal basis exists by Theorem 3 with real diagonal entries $\beta_i$. Scaling by $\gamma_i u_i$ replaces $\beta_i$ by $|\gamma_i|^2 \beta_i$, preserving the sign. The proof that the number of positive entries is invariant proceeds exactly as in the real symmetric case (Theorem 5), because the essential property that $g(y, y) \geq 0$ on $\mathfrak{R}_+ + \mathfrak{R}^\perp$ continues to hold.
