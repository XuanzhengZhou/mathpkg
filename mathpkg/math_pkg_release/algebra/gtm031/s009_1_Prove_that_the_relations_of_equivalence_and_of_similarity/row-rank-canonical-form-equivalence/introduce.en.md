Theorem 5 establishes that every $n_1 \times n_2$ matrix over a division ring with row rank $\rho$ is equivalent (via elementary row and column operations) to a canonical diagonal form with $\rho$ ones and zeros elsewhere. This is the matrix analogue of the fact that any linear transformation can be represented, after suitable choice of bases, by a matrix whose only nonzero entries are $\rho$ identity elements on the diagonal.
