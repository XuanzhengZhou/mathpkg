If $(\alpha)$ is an $n_1 \times n_2$ matrix with elements in a division ring $\Delta$ and if $(\alpha)$ has row rank $\rho$, then $(\alpha)$ is equivalent to the matrix
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 1 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$
where there are $\rho$ ones on the diagonal and zeros elsewhere (the canonical form of equation (18)).
