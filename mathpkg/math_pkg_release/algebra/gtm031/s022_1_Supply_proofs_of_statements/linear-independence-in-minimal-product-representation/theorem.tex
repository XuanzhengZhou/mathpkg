Let $\mathfrak{R}_i$ and $\mathfrak{R}_i'$, $i = 1, 2$, be dual vector spaces relative to non-degenerate bilinear forms $g_i$. Let $A : \mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$ be a linear transformation, and suppose

$$A = \sum_{i=1}^r u_i' \times v_i$$

is an expression for $A$ as a sum of products for which $r$ is minimal. Then the sets $(u_1', u_2', \dots, u_r')$ in $\mathfrak{R}_1'$ and $(v_1, v_2, \dots, v_r)$ in $\mathfrak{R}_2$ are each linearly independent.
