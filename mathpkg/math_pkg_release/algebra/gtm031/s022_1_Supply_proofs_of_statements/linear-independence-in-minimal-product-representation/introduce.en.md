If a linear transformation $A$ is expressed as a sum $\sum u_i' \times v_i$ of elementary products with the smallest possible number of terms $r$, then the vectors $\{u_i'\}$ in the dual space and $\{v_i\}$ in the target space are necessarily linearly independent sets. This minimal $r$ equals the rank of $A$, and the result confirms that any redundancy in the representing vectors would allow a further reduction in the number of terms, contradicting minimality.
