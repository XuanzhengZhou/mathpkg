Let $\Delta$ be a division ring equipped with an **involutorial** anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ (i.e., $\bar{\bar{\alpha}} = \alpha$ for all $\alpha \in \Delta$). Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a (left) vector space over $\Delta$ with a scalar product $g : \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{R} \to \Delta$.

The scalar product $g$ is called **hermitian** if it satisfies the symmetry condition

$$g(y, x) = \overline{g(x, y)}$$

for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{R}$.

In the classical case where $\Delta = \Phi$ is a field and the anti-automorphism is the identity $\bar{\alpha} = \alpha$, a hermitian scalar product reduces to a symmetric bilinear form: $g(y, x) = g(x, y)$.
