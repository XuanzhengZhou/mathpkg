Let $\Delta$ be a division ring with an anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$. Two $n \times n$ matrices $(\beta)$ and $(\gamma)$ with entries in $\Delta$ are said to be **cogredient** (relative to the given anti-automorphism) if there exists a non-singular matrix $(\mu)$ such that

$$(\gamma) = (\mu)\, (\beta)\, (\bar{\mu})'.$$

Cogredience is an equivalence relation on the set of $n \times n$ matrices over $\Delta$. Two matrices are cogredient precisely when they represent the same scalar product $g$ on $\mathfrak{R}$ with respect to different bases.
