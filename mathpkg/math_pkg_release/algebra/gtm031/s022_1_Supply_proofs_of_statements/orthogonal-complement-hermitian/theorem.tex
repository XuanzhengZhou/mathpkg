Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space with a hermitian scalar product $g$, and let $\mathfrak{S} \subseteq \mathfrak{R}$ be a subspace. The **orthogonal complement** of $\mathfrak{S}$ is the set

$$\mathfrak{S}^{\perp} = \{\, v \in \mathfrak{R} \mid g(u, v) = 0 \text{ for all } u \in \mathfrak{S} \,\}.$$

The orthogonal complement $\mathfrak{S}^{\perp}$ is always a subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$. This definition coincides with the earlier notion of $j(\mathfrak{S})$ for bilinear forms. In general, $\mathfrak{S}^{\perp}$ is not a complement of $\mathfrak{S}$ in the lattice of subspaces, except in important special cases (such as finite-dimensional non-degenerate hermitian spaces).
