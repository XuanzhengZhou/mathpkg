Using an anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ on a division ring $\Delta$, a left vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Delta$ can be canonically turned into a right vector space by defining $x\bar{\alpha} = \alpha x$. The construction is natural: left and right dimensions coincide, and any left basis serves simultaneously as a right basis after appropriate conjugation of coordinates. This technique is essential for defining scalar products $g(x, y)$ that are linear in the first argument and conjugate-linear in the second.
