The radical $\mathfrak{R}^{\perp}$ of a scalar product $g$ on $\mathfrak{R}$ is the set of vectors orthogonal to the entire space. It measures the degeneracy of $g$: the scalar product is non-degenerate precisely when the radical is zero. For a non-degenerate hermitian scalar product, the orthogonal complement operation $\mathfrak{S} \mapsto \mathfrak{S}^{\perp}$ enjoys many nice properties, including being an involutorial anti-automorphism of the subspace lattice.
