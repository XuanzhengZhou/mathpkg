Let $\Delta$ be a division ring with an anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$, and let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a (left) vector space over $\Delta$ endowed with a scalar product $g : \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{R} \to \Delta$. If $(e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{R}$ over $\Delta$, the **matrix of the scalar product** relative to this basis is the matrix $(\beta)$ defined by

$$\beta_{ij} = g(e_i, e_j), \quad i, j = 1, 2, \dots, n.$$

The matrix and the basis completely determine the scalar product: for $x = \sum \xi_i e_i$ and $y = \sum \eta_i e_i$,

$$g(x, y) = \sum_{i,j} \xi_i\, \beta_{ij}\, \bar{\eta}_j.$$

Conversely, given a basis $\{e_i\}$ and any matrix $(\beta)$, the above formula defines a scalar product on $\mathfrak{R}$.
