An anti-automorphism of a division ring $\Delta$ is a bijection $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ that preserves addition but reverses the order of multiplication: $\overline{\alpha\beta} = \bar{\beta}\bar{\alpha}$. When the mapping is an involution (i.e., applying it twice returns the original element), it generalizes the familiar complex conjugation. This concept serves as the algebraic foundation for defining hermitian scalar products and sesquilinear forms over non-commutative division rings.
