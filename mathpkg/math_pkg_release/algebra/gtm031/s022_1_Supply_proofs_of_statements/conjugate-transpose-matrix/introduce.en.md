The conjugate transpose of a matrix generalizes the familiar Hermitian conjugate $A^* = \bar{A}^T$ from complex linear algebra to the setting of an arbitrary division ring with an anti-automorphism. It appears naturally in the change-of-basis formula for scalar products: if left bases transform by $(\mu)$, the corresponding right bases transform by the conjugate transpose $(\bar{\mu})'$.
