A hermitian scalar product $g$ on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ (over a division ring $\Delta$ with involutorial anti-automorphism) is called **non-degenerate** if its radical is zero:

$$\mathfrak{R}^{\perp} = \{0\}.$$

Equivalently, $g$ is non-degenerate if the condition $g(x, y) = 0$ for all $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ implies $y = 0$.
