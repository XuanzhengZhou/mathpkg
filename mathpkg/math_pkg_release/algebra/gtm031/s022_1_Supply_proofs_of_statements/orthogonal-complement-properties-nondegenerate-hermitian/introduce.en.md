For a non-degenerate hermitian scalar product on a finite-dimensional space $\mathfrak{R}$, the orthogonal complement operation has three fundamental properties: it satisfies the dimension formula $\dim \mathfrak{S}^{\perp} = n - \dim \mathfrak{S}$, it is involutorial ($\mathfrak{S}^{\perp\perp} = \mathfrak{S}$), and it defines an anti-automorphism of the subspace lattice. These properties are the foundation of orthogonal geometry, underpinning the decomposition of spaces into orthogonal direct sums and the spectral theorem for hermitian operators.
