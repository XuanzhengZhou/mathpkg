Two subspaces $\mathfrak{S}_1$ and $\mathfrak{S}_2$ of a space with scalar product $g$ are $g$-equivalent (i.e., isometric via a linear isomorphism preserving $g$) if and only if the matrices of the restricted scalar products are cogredient. This theorem provides a practical algebraic criterion for recognizing when two subspaces carry the same scalar product geometry, reducing the geometric question of $g$-equivalence to the matrix condition of cogredience.
