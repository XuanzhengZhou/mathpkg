An alternate scalar product is a skew symmetric bilinear form that vanishes on the diagonal, i.e., $g(x, x) = 0$ for every vector $x$. This condition implies skew symmetry, and conversely, if the scalar product is skew symmetric and the underlying ring is commutative (so $\bar{\alpha} = \alpha$), then $g$ is alternate. Alternate scalar products play a fundamental role in the study of symplectic geometry and canonical forms for skew symmetric matrices.
