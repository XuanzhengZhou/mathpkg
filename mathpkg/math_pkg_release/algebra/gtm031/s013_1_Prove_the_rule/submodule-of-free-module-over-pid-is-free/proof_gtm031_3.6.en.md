*Proof.* Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a free $\mathfrak{o}$-module with basis $(t_1, t_2, \dots, t_n)$ and let $\mathfrak{N}$ be any submodule. We prove by induction on $n$ that $\mathfrak{N}$ is free with a basis of $m \leq n$ elements.

If $n = 0$, then $\mathfrak{F} = 0$ and the only submodule is $0$, which is free with a vacuous basis. Now assume the statement holds for all free modules with bases of $n-1$ elements.

Consider the set $\mathfrak{I}$ of elements $\beta_1 \in \mathfrak{o}$ such that $\beta_1 t_1 + \beta_2 t_2 + \cdots + \beta_n t_n \in \mathfrak{N}$ for some $\beta_2, \dots, \beta_n \in \mathfrak{o}$. It is immediate that $\mathfrak{I}$ is an ideal in $\mathfrak{o}$. Since $\mathfrak{o}$ is a principal ideal domain, $\mathfrak{I} = (\delta_1)$ for some $\delta_1 \in \mathfrak{o}$.

If $\delta_1 = 0$, then every element of $\mathfrak{N}$ is of the form $\beta_2 t_2 + \cdots + \beta_n t_n$, and therefore belongs to the free submodule $\mathfrak{F}'$ generated by $(t_2, \dots, t_n)$. By the induction hypothesis applied to $\mathfrak{N}$ as a submodule of $\mathfrak{F}'$, $\mathfrak{N}$ is free with $m \leq n-1 \leq n$ basis elements.

If $\delta_1 \neq 0$, choose an element $v_1 = \delta_1 t_1 + \beta_2 t_2 + \cdots + \beta_n t_n \in \mathfrak{N}$. For any $v = \gamma_1 t_1 + \gamma_2 t_2 + \cdots + \gamma_n t_n \in \mathfrak{N}$, we have $\gamma_1 \in \mathfrak{I} = (\delta_1)$, so $\gamma_1 = \eta_1 \delta_1$ for some $\eta_1 \in \mathfrak{o}$. Then $v - \eta_1 v_1 \in \mathfrak{N}$ has coefficient $0$ for $t_1$, hence belongs to $\mathfrak{N} \cap \mathfrak{F}'$. Let $\mathfrak{N}' = \mathfrak{N} \cap \mathfrak{F}'$. By the induction hypothesis, $\mathfrak{N}'$ is free with basis $(v_2, v_3, \dots, v_m)$ where $m-1 \leq n-1$, i.e., $m \leq n$. Then $(v_1, v_2, \dots, v_m)$ is a basis for $\mathfrak{N}$. Indeed, they generate $\mathfrak{N}$ because any $v \in \mathfrak{N}$ can be expressed as $\eta_1 v_1$ plus an element of $\mathfrak{N}'$, and they are linearly independent: if $\sum \alpha_i v_i = 0$, then the coefficient of $t_1$ gives $\alpha_1 \delta_1 = 0$, hence $\alpha_1 = 0$ (since $\mathfrak{o}$ is an integral domain and $\delta_1 \neq 0$), and then the independence of $(v_2, \dots, v_m)$ in $\mathfrak{N}'$ forces $\alpha_2 = \cdots = \alpha_m = 0$. $\square$
