This exercise asks for the minimum polynomials of two concrete linear operators on the space of polynomials of bounded degree. The differentiation operator $D$ is nilpotent (since $D^n$ annihilates all polynomials of degree $\leq n-1$), so its minimum polynomial is $\lambda^n$. The translation operator $A$ shifts the argument by $1$ and can be expressed as $\exp(D)$; its minimum polynomial is $(\lambda - 1)^n$ since $A - I$ is nilpotent of index $n$.
