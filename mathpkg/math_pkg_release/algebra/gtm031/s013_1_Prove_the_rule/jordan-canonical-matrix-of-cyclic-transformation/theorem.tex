Let $A$ be a cyclic linear transformation on $\Re$ with minimum polynomial $\mu(\lambda) = \lambda^n - \mu_{n-1}\lambda^{n-1} - \cdots - \mu_0$. Relative to the basis $(e, eA, \dots, eA^{n-1})$, the matrix of $A$ is the companion matrix of $\mu(\lambda)$:

$$\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1 \\
\mu_0 & \mu_1 & \mu_2 & \cdots & \mu_{n-1}
\end{bmatrix}.$$

This matrix is called the \text{Jordan canonical matrix} of the cyclic linear transformation $A$. The minimum polynomial can be read off directly from this matrix.
