Elementary divisors are the prime power factors of the minimum polynomial of a cyclic linear transformation. They are the fundamental similarity invariants: each elementary divisor corresponds to an indecomposable block in the classical canonical matrix. For a general (non-cyclic) linear transformation, the elementary divisors generalize to include those from all invariant factors, forming a complete set of similarity invariants.
