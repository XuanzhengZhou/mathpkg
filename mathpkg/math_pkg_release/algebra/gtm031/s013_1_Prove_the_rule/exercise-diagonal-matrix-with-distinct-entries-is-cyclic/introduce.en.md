This two-part exercise first asks to show that a diagonal matrix with $n$ distinct diagonal entries has minimum polynomial $\prod (\lambda - \alpha_i)$ of degree $n$. Since the degree equals the dimension, the transformation is cyclic (a cyclic vector can be constructed, e.g., the sum of all standard basis vectors).
