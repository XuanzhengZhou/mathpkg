This exercise proves that an upper triangular nilpotent matrix with $1$'s on the superdiagonal (and arbitrary entries above that) is cyclic, and moreover is similar to the simple nilpotent Jordan block (with only $1$'s on the superdiagonal). The proof constructs a cyclic vector by showing that the standard basis vector $e_1$ generates the whole space.
