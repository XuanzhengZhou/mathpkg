Let $A$ be a linear transformation on a vector space $\Re$ over a field $\Phi$. Let $u$ be a particular vector in $\Re$ and let $\mathfrak{S}$ denote the smallest invariant subspace containing $u$. Since $u \in \mathfrak{S}$, so is $uA$; hence also $uA^2 = (uA)A$, $uA^3 = (uA^2)A$, etc. Thus $\mathfrak{S}$ contains the vectors $u, uA, uA^2, \dots$. The subspace

$$\{u\} = [u, uA, uA^2, \dots]$$

is called the \text{cyclic space} (relative to $A$) generated by the vector $u$. If $u \neq 0$ and $uA^r$ is the first vector in the sequence that is linearly dependent on the preceding ones, then

$$\{u\} = [u, uA, \dots, uA^{r-1}]$$

and $(u, uA, \dots, uA^{r-1})$ is a basis for this space, with $r = \dim \{u\}$.
