Theorem 1 states that for any linear transformation $A$ on a finite-dimensional vector space $\Re$, there exists at least one vector $u \in \Re$ whose order $\mu_u(\lambda)$ equals the minimum polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ of $A$. This means the minimum polynomial is not merely an abstract annihilating relation but is concretely realized as the order of some vector in the space. Together with cyclic subspace theory, this implies that $\Re$ contains a cyclic subspace of dimension $\deg \mu(\lambda)$.
