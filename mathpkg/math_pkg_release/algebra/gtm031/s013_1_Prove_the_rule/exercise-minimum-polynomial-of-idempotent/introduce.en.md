An idempotent linear transformation satisfies $E^2 = E$. This exercise asks for the minimum polynomial of such a transformation. Since $E^2 - E = 0$, the minimum polynomial must divide $\lambda^2 - \lambda = \lambda(\lambda - 1)$. Excluding the zero transformation eliminates $\lambda$ and $\lambda - 1$, leaving $\lambda^2 - \lambda$ as the minimal relation.
