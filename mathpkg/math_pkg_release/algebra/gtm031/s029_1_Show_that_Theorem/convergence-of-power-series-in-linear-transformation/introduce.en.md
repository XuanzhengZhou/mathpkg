Theorem 15 gives a convergence criterion for formal power series evaluated at a linear transformation $A$. If the scalar power series $\sum \gamma_k \lambda^k$ has radius of convergence $r$, then the matrix power series $\sum \gamma_k A^k$ converges whenever every characteristic root $\rho_i$ of $A$ satisfies $|\rho_i| < r$. The proof uses the classical canonical form of $A$, expressing each diagonal block as $\rho 1 + z$ where $z$ is nilpotent, and expanding $S_k(A)$ in terms of $S_k(\rho), S_k'(\rho), S_k''(\rho)/2!, \dots$ until the derivatives converge. This theorem is fundamental for defining analytic functions of matrices such as the exponential, logarithm, and more general power series.
