For normal linear transformations, power series are particularly simple to handle. A normal transformation can be diagonalized by a unitary basis, so $A = \operatorname{diag}\{\rho_1, \dots, \rho_n\}$. Consequently, any convergent power series $S(A)$ is simply $\operatorname{diag}\{S(\rho_1), \dots, S(\rho_n)\}$ in the same unitary basis. This shows that $S(A)$ is itself normal, preserving the important property of normality under analytic functional calculus.
