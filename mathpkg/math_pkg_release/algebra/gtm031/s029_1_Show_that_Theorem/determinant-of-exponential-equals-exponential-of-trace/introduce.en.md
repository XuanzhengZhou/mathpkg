A fundamental identity in linear algebra relates the determinant of the matrix exponential to the exponential of the trace: $\det(\exp A) = \exp(\operatorname{tr} A)$. The proof uses the block diagonal structure of $\exp A$ derived from the classical canonical form, noting that the determinant is the product of the $\exp \rho_i$ over all characteristic roots $\rho_i$, and that $\sum \rho_i = \operatorname{tr} A$. This formula generalizes the scalar property $|e^z| = e^{\operatorname{Re} z}$ to matrices and plays a key role in Lie theory.
