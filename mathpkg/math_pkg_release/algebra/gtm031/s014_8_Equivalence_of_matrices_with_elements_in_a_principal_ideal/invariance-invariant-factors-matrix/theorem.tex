If $\operatorname{diag}\{\delta_1, \delta_2, \ldots, \delta_r, 0, \ldots, 0\}$ and $\operatorname{diag}\{\delta_1', \delta_2', \ldots, \delta_{r'}', 0, \ldots, 0\}$ are equivalent $m \times n$ matrices over a principal ideal domain $\mathfrak{o}$, with $\delta_i \mid \delta_j$ for $i \leq j$ and $\delta_k' \mid \delta_l'$ for $k \leq l$, then $r = r'$ and each $\delta_i$ is an associate of $\delta_i'$ for $i = 1, 2, \ldots, r$.
