Let $A$ be a linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space over a field $\Phi$, and let the elementary divisors of $A$ be $\pi_1(\lambda)^{k_1}, \pi_2(\lambda)^{k_2}, \ldots, \pi_s(\lambda)^{k_s}$, where each $\pi_i(\lambda)$ is a monic irreducible polynomial. The **classical canonical matrix** of $A$ is the block diagonal matrix
$$C = \operatorname{diag}\{C(\pi_1^{k_1}), C(\pi_2^{k_2}), \ldots, C(\pi_s^{k_s})\}$$
where each $C(\pi_i^{k_i})$ is the companion matrix block associated to the elementary divisor $\pi_i(\lambda)^{k_i}$. Two matrices are similar if and only if they have the same classical canonical form (up to ordering of blocks).
