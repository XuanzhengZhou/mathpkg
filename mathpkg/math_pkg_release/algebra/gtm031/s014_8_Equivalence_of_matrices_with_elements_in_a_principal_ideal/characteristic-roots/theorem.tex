Let $A$ be a linear transformation of an $n$-dimensional vector space over a field $\Phi$, with characteristic polynomial $\chi(\lambda)$. The roots of $\chi(\lambda)$ in the algebraic closure $\overline{\Phi}$ are called the **characteristic roots** (or eigenvalues) of $A$. If $\overline{\Phi}$ contains all roots, then
$$\chi(\lambda) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} (\lambda - \rho_i)$$
where $\rho_1, \ldots, \rho_n$ are the characteristic roots (counted with multiplicity).
