A matrix equivalent to $(\sigma)$ having the diagonal form

$$\operatorname{diag}\{\delta_1, \delta_2, \cdots, \delta_r, 0, \cdots, 0\}$$

where the non-zero elements $\delta_i$ satisfy the divisibility conditions $\delta_1 \mid \delta_2 \mid \cdots \mid \delta_r$, is called a \text{normal form} for the matrix $(\sigma)$.
