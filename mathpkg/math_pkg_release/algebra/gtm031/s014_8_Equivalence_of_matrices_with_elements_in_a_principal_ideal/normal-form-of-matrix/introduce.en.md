A normal form of a matrix over a principal ideal domain is a diagonal matrix, equivalent to the original, whose non-zero diagonal entries satisfy successive divisibility conditions. The existence of such a normal form is established constructively for Euclidean domains using elementary row and column operations that reduce the degree (or length) of non-zero entries. For general principal ideal domains, a similar argument using the length of elements (number of prime factors) succeeds. The diagonal entries in normal form are the invariant factors of the matrix.
