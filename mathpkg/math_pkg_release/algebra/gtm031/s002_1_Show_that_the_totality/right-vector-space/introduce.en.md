A right vector space is the analogue of a (left) vector space where scalars multiply on the right. The axioms parallel those of left vector spaces. A right vector space over a non-commutative division ring $\Delta$ cannot be regarded as a left vector space over $\Delta$ directly; however, it can be converted into a left vector space over the opposite division ring $\Delta'$ by defining $\alpha' x' = x' \alpha$.
