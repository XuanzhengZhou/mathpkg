A free module is a module that possesses a basis---a linearly independent set of generators. For vector spaces (modules over division rings), every module is free; over general rings this property does not hold. Free modules are the module-theoretic analogue of vector spaces, with the crucial property that every element has a unique representation as a linear combination of basis elements.
