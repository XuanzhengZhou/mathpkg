A left $\mathfrak{o}$-module generalizes the notion of a vector space by replacing the division ring of scalars with an arbitrary ring $\mathfrak{o}$ that has an identity. The axioms S1--S4 are identical to those of a vector space. Important examples include: any commutative group as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module (with integer multiples), and any ring with identity regarded as a module over itself.
