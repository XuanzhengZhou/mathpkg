Let $S = (x_\alpha)$ be an arbitrary subset of an $\mathfrak{o}$-module $\mathfrak{R}$ and let $[S]$ denote the totality of sums of the form

$$
\xi_1 x_{\alpha_1} + \xi_2 x_{\alpha_2} + \cdots + \xi_m x_{\alpha_m}
$$

where the $\xi_i$ are arbitrary in $\mathfrak{o}$ and the $x_{\alpha_i}$ are arbitrary in $S$. Then $[S]$ is a submodule of $\mathfrak{R}$, called the \textbf{submodule generated by} the set $S$. It is the smallest submodule of $\mathfrak{R}$ that contains $S$.
