A tensor space based on a vector space $\Re$ is a Kronecker product of $r$ copies of $\Re$ and $s$ copies of its dual $\Re^*$, denoted $\Re_s^r$. Its elements are tensors with $r$ contravariant indices and $s$ covariant indices. Given a basis $(e_i)$ of $\Re$ and the complementary dual basis $(e^j)$, the tensor space has a natural basis consisting of Kronecker products of basis vectors. The coordinates $\xi_{j_1 \cdots j_s}^{i_1 \cdots i_r}$ of a tensor transform according to the usual tensor transformation laws under a change of basis in $\Re$.
