Let $\Re$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ be two vector spaces over a field $\Phi$. Regard $\Re$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ momentarily as two-sided vector spaces in the trivial way that $\alpha x = x\alpha$ for all $\alpha$ and $x$, and form the direct product $\Re \times \mathfrak{S}$. This is a two-sided vector space of the trivial sort since

$$\alpha\left(\sum x_i \times y_i\right) = \sum \alpha x_i \times y_i = \sum x_i \alpha \times y_i = \sum x_i \times \alpha y_i = \sum x_i \times y_i \alpha = \sum (x_i \times y_i)\alpha.$$

Hence we may regard $\Re \times \mathfrak{S}$ simply as a left vector space. The (left) vector space thus obtained is called the \text{Kronecker product} of $\Re$ and $\mathfrak{S}$.
