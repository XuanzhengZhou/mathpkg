The Kronecker product of two vector spaces $\Re$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ over a field $\Phi$ is constructed by first regarding each space as a trivial two-sided vector space (with $\alpha x = x\alpha$), forming their direct product $\Re \times \mathfrak{S}$, and then treating the result as a left vector space. The key property that makes this possible is that scalar multiplication commutes across the product: $\alpha(\sum x_i \times y_i) = \sum \alpha x_i \times y_i = \sum x_i \times \alpha y_i$. This construction generalizes the notion of a tensor product and serves as the foundation for defining tensor spaces and free associative algebras.
