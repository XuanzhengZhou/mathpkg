Viewing a field extension as a vector space over the base field is a fundamental connection between field theory and linear algebra. Given a field $P$ that contains $\Phi$ as a subfield, the larger field $P$ naturally acquires the structure of a vector space over $\Phi$ by using field addition as vector addition and field multiplication as scalar multiplication. The vector space axioms follow directly from the field axioms, in particular from associativity, distributivity, and the fact that $\Phi$ contains the multiplicative identity of $P$. This vector space may be finite-dimensional (e.g., $\mathbb{C}$ over $\mathbb{R}$ is two-dimensional) or infinite-dimensional (e.g., $\Phi(\lambda)$ over $\Phi$ for an indeterminate $\lambda$).
