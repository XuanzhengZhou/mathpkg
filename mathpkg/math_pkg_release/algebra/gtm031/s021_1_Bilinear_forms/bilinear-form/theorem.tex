Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a left vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ and let $\mathfrak{R}'$ be a right vector space over $\Delta$. A function $g(x, y')$ defined for all pairs $(x, y')$ with $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ and $y' \in \mathfrak{R}'$, taking values $g(x, y') \in \Delta$, is called a \textbf{bilinear form} if it satisfies:

$$g(x_1 + x_2, y') = g(x_1, y') + g(x_2, y'), \quad g(\alpha x, y') = \alpha g(x, y')$$

$$g(x, y_1' + y_2') = g(x, y_1') + g(x, y_2'), \quad g(x, y'\alpha) = g(x, y')\alpha$$

for all $x, x_1, x_2 \in \mathfrak{R}$, $y', y_1', y_2' \in \mathfrak{R}'$, and $\alpha \in \Delta$.
