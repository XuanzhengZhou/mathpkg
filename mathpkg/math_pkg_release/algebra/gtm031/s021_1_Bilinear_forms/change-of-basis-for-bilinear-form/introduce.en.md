Under a change of bases in both vector spaces, the matrix of a bilinear form transforms by left and right multiplication by the change-of-basis matrices: the new matrix is $(\mu)(\beta)(\nu)$, where $(\mu)$ is the transition matrix from the old to the new basis in $\mathfrak{R}$ and $(\nu)$ is the transition matrix from the old to the new basis in $\mathfrak{R}'$. This transformation law is fundamental for understanding equivalence of bilinear forms and leads directly to the canonical form theorem.
