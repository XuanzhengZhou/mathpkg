Let $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ and $(u_1, \dots, u_n)$ be bases for $\mathfrak{R}$, with $u_i = \sum_{j} \mu_{ij} e_j$ where $(\mu) \in L(\Delta, n)$. Let $(f_1', \dots, f_{n'}')$ and $(v_1', \dots, v_{n'}')$ be bases for $\mathfrak{R}'$, with $v_k' = \sum_{l} f_l' \nu_{lk}$ where $(\nu) \in L(\Delta, n')$. If $(\beta_{ij}) = (g(e_i, f_j'))$ is the matrix of the bilinear form $g$ relative to the first pair of bases, then the matrix of $g$ relative to $(u_i), (v_k')$ is

$$g(u_i, v_k') = g\!\left(\sum_j \mu_{ij} e_j, \sum_l f_l' \nu_{lk}\right) = \sum_{j,l} \mu_{ij} \, \beta_{jl} \, \nu_{lk}.$$

In matrix notation, the new matrix is $(\mu) (\beta) (\nu)$.
