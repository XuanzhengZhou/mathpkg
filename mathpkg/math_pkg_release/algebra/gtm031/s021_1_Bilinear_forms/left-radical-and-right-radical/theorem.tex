Let $g(x, y')$ be a bilinear form on $\mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{R}'$.

The \textbf{left radical} of $g$ is the subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$ defined by
$$\mathfrak{N}_L = \{ z \in \mathfrak{R} \mid g(z, y') = 0 \text{ for all } y' \in \mathfrak{R}' \}.$$

The \textbf{right radical} of $g$ is the subspace of $\mathfrak{R}'$ defined by
$$\mathfrak{N}_R = \{ z' \in \mathfrak{R}' \mid g(x, z') = 0 \text{ for all } x \in \mathfrak{R} \}.$$

The left radical is the null space (kernel) of the linear transformation $L: \mathfrak{R} \to (\mathfrak{R}')^*$, and the right radical is the null space of the linear transformation $R: \mathfrak{R}' \to \mathfrak{R}^*$.
