The double transpose theorem establishes that the transpose operation $A \mapsto A'$ is an involution: taking the transpose twice returns the original linear transformation. Moreover, this operation is a bijection between the space of linear transformations $\mathfrak{R}_1 \to \mathfrak{R}_2$ and the space $\mathfrak{R}_2' \to \mathfrak{R}_1'$. This generalizes the familiar fact from linear algebra that $(A^T)^T = A$ for matrices, now in the abstract setting of bilinear forms between arbitrary vector spaces over a division ring.
