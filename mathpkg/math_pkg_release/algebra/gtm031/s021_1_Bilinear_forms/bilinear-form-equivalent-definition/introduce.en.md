The concept of a bilinear form can be reformulated in the language of linear transformations: a bilinear form on $\mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{R}'$ is precisely a linear transformation from $\mathfrak{R}'$ into the dual space $\mathfrak{R}^*$ (or symmetrically, from $\mathfrak{R}$ into $(\mathfrak{R}')^*$). This equivalent definition emphasizes the structural connection between bilinear forms and duality theory, though the original symmetric formulation as a function of two variables is often preferred for its conceptual clarity.
