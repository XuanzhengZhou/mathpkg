Let $g(x, y')$ be a non-degenerate bilinear form on $\mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{R}'$. Bases $(e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n)$ of $\mathfrak{R}$ and $(e_1', e_2', \dots, e_n')$ of $\mathfrak{R}'$ are called \textbf{complementary bases} (or \textbf{dual bases}) relative to $g$ if

$$g(e_i, e_j') = \delta_{ij}, \quad i, j = 1, 2, \dots, n.$$

Any basis of $\mathfrak{R}$ (respectively $\mathfrak{R}'$) determines a unique complementary basis in $\mathfrak{R}'$ (respectively $\mathfrak{R}$). Specifically, if $(e_i)$ is given and $(f_j')$ is any basis of $\mathfrak{R}'$, then the complementary basis is $e_j' = \sum_k f_k' \nu_{kj}$ where $(\nu) = (\beta)^{-1}$ and $\beta_{ij} = g(e_i, f_j')$.
