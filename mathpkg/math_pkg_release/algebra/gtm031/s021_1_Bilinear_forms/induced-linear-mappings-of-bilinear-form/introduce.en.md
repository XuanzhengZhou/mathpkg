A bilinear form induces two natural linear transformations: one maps vectors of the right space $\mathfrak{R}'$ into the dual of the left space $\mathfrak{R}^*$, and the other maps vectors of the left space $\mathfrak{R}$ into the dual of the right space $(\mathfrak{R}')^*$. These mappings, denoted $R$ and $L$, are fundamental for understanding the relationship between a bilinear form and the duality of vector spaces. They reveal the symmetry inherent in the definition of a bilinear form and provide the basis for the equivalent definition of bilinear forms as linear transformations into conjugate spaces.
