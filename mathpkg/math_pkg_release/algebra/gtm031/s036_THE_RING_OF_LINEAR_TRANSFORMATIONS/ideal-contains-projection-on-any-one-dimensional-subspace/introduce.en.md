A key step in the operator-theoretic proof of the simplicity of $\mathfrak{L}$ is showing that any non-zero two-sided ideal $\mathfrak{B}$ contains a projection onto an arbitrary one-dimensional subspace $[x]$. Starting from a non-zero element $B \in \mathfrak{B}$, one constructs a projection $E_1 = B A_1$ onto a one-dimensional subspace $[x_1]$, then uses suitable linear transformations $C$ and $D$ to conjugate this projection to one onto the desired subspace $[x]$, keeping it inside the ideal.
