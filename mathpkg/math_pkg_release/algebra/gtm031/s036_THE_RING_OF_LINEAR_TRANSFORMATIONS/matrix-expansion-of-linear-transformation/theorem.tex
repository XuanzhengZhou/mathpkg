Let $A \in \mathfrak{L}$ be a linear transformation on a finite dimensional vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$, and let $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$ be a basis. If $e_i A = \sum_{j=1}^n \alpha_{ij} e_j$ with $\alpha_{ij} \in \Delta$, then

$$A = \sum_{i,j=1}^n \bar{\alpha}_{ij} E_{ij} = \sum_{i,j=1}^n E_{ij} \bar{\alpha}_{ij}.$$

Thus any $A \in \mathfrak{L}$ can be written as a linear combination with coefficients in $\bar{\Delta}$ of the matrix units $E_{ij}$.
