Let $(e_1, e_2, \cdots, e_n)$ be a basis for a finite dimensional vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$. For each $\alpha \in \Delta$, the \textbf{scalar transformation} $\bar{\alpha}: \mathfrak{R} \to \mathfrak{R}$ (relative to the chosen basis) is the linear mapping defined by

$$e_i \bar{\alpha} = \alpha e_i, \quad i = 1, 2, \cdots, n.$$

The set of all such transformations is denoted $\bar{\Delta} = \{\bar{\alpha} \mid \alpha \in \Delta\}$.
