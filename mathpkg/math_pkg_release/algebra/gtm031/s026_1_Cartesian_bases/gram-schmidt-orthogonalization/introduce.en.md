The Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process (also called E. Schmidt's orthogonalization process) provides an explicit algorithm to transform any basis of a Euclidean space into a Cartesian (orthonormal) basis. Starting from the first basis vector normalized to unit length, each subsequent basis vector is made orthogonal to all previously constructed orthonormal vectors by subtracting their projections, then normalized. The construction proceeds inductively and yields a Cartesian basis in finitely many steps.
