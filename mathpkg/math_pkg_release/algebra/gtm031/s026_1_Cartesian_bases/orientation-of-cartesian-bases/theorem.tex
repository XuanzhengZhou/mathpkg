Let $(u_1, u_2, \cdots, u_n)$ and $(v_1, v_2, \cdots, v_n)$ be two Cartesian bases for a Euclidean space $\Re$, related by $u_i = \sum \sigma_{ij} v_j$ with $(\sigma)$ orthogonal.

The bases are said to have the \textbf{same orientation} if $(\sigma)$ is proper ($\det(\sigma) = 1$), and \textbf{opposite orientation} if $(\sigma)$ is improper ($\det(\sigma) = -1$).

Thus the Cartesian bases fall into two mutually exclusive orientation classes. A change of sign of one basis vector or an odd permutation of the vectors changes the orientation.
