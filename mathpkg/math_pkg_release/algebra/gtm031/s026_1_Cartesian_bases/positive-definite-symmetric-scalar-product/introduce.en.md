A positive definite symmetric scalar product (or inner product) on a real vector space is a bilinear form that is symmetric and strictly positive on every nonzero vector. The four axioms capture bilinearity in both arguments, symmetry, and positive definiteness. This is the fundamental structure that turns a real vector space into a Euclidean space, allowing the measurement of lengths via $(x,x)^{1/2}$ and angles via the cosine law.
