An orthogonal matrix is a square matrix whose transpose equals its inverse. In the context of Euclidean spaces, orthogonal matrices characterize the change-of-basis transformations between Cartesian (orthonormal) bases. The defining condition $(\sigma)(\sigma)' = 1$ implies that the columns form an orthonormal basis and that the linear transformation preserves the scalar product.
