The Gram-Schmidt process produces a Cartesian (orthonormal) basis whose transition matrix from the original basis is lower triangular. This follows from the inductive construction: at each step $i$, the new orthonormal vector $u_i$ is a linear combination of $e_1, \ldots, e_i$ only, since $[u_1, \ldots, u_i] = [e_1, \ldots, e_i]$. The triangular structure reflects that Gram-Schmidt processes vectors in order without "looking ahead."
