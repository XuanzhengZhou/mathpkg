If $\Omega$ and $\Omega'$ have the same invariant subspaces, then for any invariant subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ under $\Omega$, Theorem 3 implies that $\mathfrak{S}^\perp$ is invariant under $\Omega'$. Since $\Omega'$ has the same invariant subspaces as $\Omega$, $\mathfrak{S}^\perp$ is also invariant under $\Omega$. Moreover, $\Re = \mathfrak{S} \oplus \mathfrak{S}^\perp$ (the orthogonal complement is a true complement because the scalar product is non-degenerate on any subspace $\mathfrak{S} \neq 0$). Thus the space decomposes orthogonally into invariant subspaces.

Conversely, if $\Omega$ is orthogonally completely reducible and $\mathfrak{S}$ is invariant under $\Omega$, then $\mathfrak{S}^\perp$ is also invariant under $\Omega$. Theorem 3 then gives that $\mathfrak{S}^\perp$ is invariant under $\Omega'$, and applying the same reasoning to $\mathfrak{S}^\perp$ shows invariance goes both ways between $\Omega$ and $\Omega'$. Hence they have the same invariant subspaces.
