Theorem 2 establishes that any two direct products of the same pair of vector spaces $\mathfrak{R}'$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ are canonically isomorphic. The isomorphism is given by the natural mapping that sends a formal sum of pure products in the first product to the same formal sum interpreted in the second product. This is the analogue, in Jacobson's non-commutative setting, of the universal property of the tensor product: the direct product is uniquely determined up to a canonical isomorphism.
