A direct product of a right vector space $\mathfrak{R}'$ and a left vector space $\mathfrak{S}$ over a division ring $\Delta$ is a product group $\mathfrak{P}$ satisfying a strong uniqueness condition: if a finite sum of pure products equals zero, then either one set of factors is linearly dependent or the other set consists entirely of zero vectors. This condition ensures that the representation of elements as sums of pure products is as unique as possible, given the bilinearity constraints. The direct product is essentially the tensor product $\mathfrak{R}' \otimes_\Delta \mathfrak{S}$, constructed here without assuming commutativity of the coefficient ring.
