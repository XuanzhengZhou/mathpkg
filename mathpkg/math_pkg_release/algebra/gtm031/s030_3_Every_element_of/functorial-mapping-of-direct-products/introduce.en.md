Given a direct product $\mathfrak{P}$ of $\mathfrak{R}'$ and $\mathfrak{S}$, and any product $\mathfrak{Q}_1$ of spaces $\mathfrak{U}'$ and $\mathfrak{B}$, a pair of linear maps $A': \mathfrak{R}' \to \mathfrak{U}'$ and $B: \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{B}$ induces a natural homomorphism from $\mathfrak{P}$ to $\mathfrak{Q}_1$ by acting componentwise on pure products. This functorial property is the key structural result for direct products: it implies both Theorem 1 (by taking $A' = 1$, $B = 1$, and $\mathfrak{Q}_1 = \mathfrak{P}$) and Theorem 2 (uniqueness of direct product). It shows that the direct product construction is functorial in both arguments.
