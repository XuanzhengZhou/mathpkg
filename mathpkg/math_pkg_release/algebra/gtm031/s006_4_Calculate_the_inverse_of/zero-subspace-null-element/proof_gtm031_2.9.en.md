For any subspace $\mathfrak{S}$, the zero subspace $0 = \{0\}$ satisfies:

1. $\mathfrak{S} + 0 = \{x + 0 \mid x \in \mathfrak{S}\} = \mathfrak{S}$. Similarly $0 + \mathfrak{S} = \{0 + x \mid x \in \mathfrak{S}\} = \mathfrak{S}$.

2. $\mathfrak{S} \cap 0 = \{0\} = 0$, and $0 \cap \mathfrak{S} = \{0\} = 0$.

These equalities follow immediately from the definitions of sum and intersection of subspaces and the fact that the zero vector is the additive identity in $\mathfrak{R}$.
