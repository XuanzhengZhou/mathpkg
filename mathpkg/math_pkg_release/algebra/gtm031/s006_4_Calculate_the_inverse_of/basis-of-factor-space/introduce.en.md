Given a subspace $\mathfrak{S}$ of a finite-dimensional vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ with basis $(f_1,\ldots,f_r)$, we can extend this to a full basis $(f_1,\ldots,f_r,f_{r+1},\ldots,f_n)$ of $\mathfrak{R}$. The cosets $\bar{f}_{r+1},\ldots,\bar{f}_n$ then form a basis of the factor space $\mathfrak{R}/\mathfrak{S}$, showing that $\dim(\mathfrak{R}/\mathfrak{S}) = \dim\mathfrak{R} - \dim\mathfrak{S}$. This is the fundamental dimension formula for quotient spaces.
