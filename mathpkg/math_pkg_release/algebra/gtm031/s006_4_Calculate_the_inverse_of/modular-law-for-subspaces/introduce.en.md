The full distributive law $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap (\mathfrak{S}_2 + \mathfrak{S}_3) = \mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2 + \mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_3$ does not hold generally in the lattice of subspaces of a vector space. However, a weakened version called the modular law does hold: when $\mathfrak{S}_1$ contains $\mathfrak{S}_2$, the distributive identity is valid. This property makes the subspace lattice a modular lattice, which is a fundamental algebraic structure in lattice theory.
