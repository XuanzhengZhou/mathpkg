Every subspace lattice $L$ of a finite-dimensional vector space satisfies two fundamental finiteness conditions. The descending chain condition (DCC) states that any infinite descending chain $\mathfrak{S}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{S}_2 \supseteq \cdots$ of subspaces stabilizes after finitely many steps. Dually, the ascending chain condition (ACC) asserts that any infinite ascending chain $\mathfrak{S}_1 \subseteq \mathfrak{S}_2 \subseteq \cdots$ also stabilizes. Both follow immediately from the fact that the dimension of a subspace is a non-negative integer and that proper inclusion strictly decreases (resp. increases) the dimension.
