Let $\mathfrak{S}_1, \mathfrak{S}_2, \ldots, \mathfrak{S}_r$ be independent subspaces of a vector space, and set $\mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{S}_1 + \mathfrak{S}_2 + \cdots + \mathfrak{S}_r$. Then every $y \in \mathfrak{S}$ admits a unique representation
$$y = y_1 + y_2 + \cdots + y_r, \qquad y_i \in \mathfrak{S}_i.$$
Conversely, if the representation of each element of $\mathfrak{S}_1 + \cdots + \mathfrak{S}_r$ as a sum of elements from the subspaces $\mathfrak{S}_i$ is unique, then the $\mathfrak{S}_i$ are independent.
