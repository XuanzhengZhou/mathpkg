The collection $L$ of all subspaces of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$, ordered by inclusion $\supseteq$, forms a partially ordered set satisfying reflexivity, antisymmetry, and transitivity. The logical intersection $\mathfrak{S}_1 \cap \mathfrak{S}_2$ serves as the greatest lower bound (meet), while the join $\mathfrak{S}_1 + \mathfrak{S}_2$ (the subspace spanned by their union) serves as the least upper bound, giving $L$ the structure of a lattice.
