Let $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}_1 \oplus \mathfrak{R}_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{R}_r$ be a direct sum decomposition of a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$. Every $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ can be written uniquely as

$$x = x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_r$$

where $x_i \in \mathfrak{R}_i$. The mapping $E_i : \mathfrak{R} \to \mathfrak{R}$ defined by $x E_i = x_i$ is a linear transformation satisfying

$$(x + y)E_i = xE_i + yE_i, \quad (\alpha x)E_i = \alpha(xE_i)$$

for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{R}$ and $\alpha \in \Delta$.
