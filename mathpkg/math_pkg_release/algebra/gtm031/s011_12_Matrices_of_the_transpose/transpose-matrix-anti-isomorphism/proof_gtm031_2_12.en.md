In the case $\mathfrak{R}_1 = \mathfrak{R}_2 = \mathfrak{R}$, the mappings $A \to (\alpha)$ and $A^* \to (\alpha)'$ are isomorphisms of the rings $\mathfrak{L} = \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ and $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}^*, \mathfrak{R}^*)$ respectively onto the matrix ring $\Phi_n$. Since $A \to A^*$ is an anti-isomorphism (by (28) and (29)), composing these isomorphisms yields that $(\alpha) \to (\alpha)'$ is an anti-isomorphism in $\Phi_n$. This can also be verified directly by matrix computation.
