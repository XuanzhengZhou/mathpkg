Every idempotent linear transformation $E_1$ (satisfying $E_1^2 = E_1$) can be represented by a diagonal matrix with entries $1$ and $0$ in a suitable basis. The number of $1$'s on the diagonal is exactly the rank of $E_1$. This follows because $E_1$ and $E_2 = 1 - E_1$ form a supplementary orthogonal pair of projections, corresponding to the direct sum decomposition $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{R}E_1 \oplus \mathfrak{R}E_2$.
