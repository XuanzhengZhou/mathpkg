From the decomposition $x = x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_r = xE_1 + xE_2 + \cdots + xE_r$, which holds for every $x \in \mathfrak{R}$, we obtain $x = x(E_1 + E_2 + \cdots + E_r)$ for all $x$. Hence $E_1 + E_2 + \cdots + E_r = 1$, the identity transformation.
