Matrix multiplication over a division ring $\Delta$ is defined by the usual row-by-column formula: the $(i,k)$-entry of the product $(\alpha)(\beta)$ is the sum $\sum_{j=1}^{n} \alpha_{ij} \beta_{jk}$. This operation is associative whenever the dimensions are compatible, as can be verified by comparing the $(i,l)$-entries of $[(\alpha)(\beta)](\gamma)$ and $(\alpha)[(\beta)(\gamma)]$, both of which equal $\sum_{j,k} \alpha_{ij} \beta_{jk} \gamma_{kl}$.
