Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be an $n$-dimensional left vector space over a field $\Phi$ and let $(e_1, e_2, \dots, e_n)$ be a left basis for $\mathfrak{R}$. Let $S_1, S_2, \dots, S_n$ be automorphisms in $\Phi$ and define the right multiplication by $\alpha \in \Phi$ to be the linear transformation in the left space $\mathfrak{R}$ defined by the matrix

$$\operatorname{diag}\{\alpha^{S_1}, \alpha^{S_2}, \dots, \alpha^{S_n}\}.$$

Then $e_i \alpha = \alpha^{S_i} e_i$ and $(\sum \xi_i e_i)\alpha = \sum \xi_i \alpha^{S_i} e_i$, making $\mathfrak{R}$ a two-sided vector space over $\Phi$.
