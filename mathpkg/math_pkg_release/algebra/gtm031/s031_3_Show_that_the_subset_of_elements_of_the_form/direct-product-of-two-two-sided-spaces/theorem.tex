If both $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ are two-sided vector spaces over a division ring $\Delta$, then the direct product $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$ can be converted into a left vector space via $(9)$ and a right vector space via $(10)$, and any left multiplication defined in this way commutes with any right multiplication. Hence $\mathfrak{P}$ is a two-sided vector space over $\Delta$.
