Analogous to the left direct product construction, when $\mathfrak{R}$ is a right vector space and $\mathfrak{S}$ is a two-sided vector space over the same division ring $\Delta$, the direct product $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$ can be given a right vector space structure. The right scalar multiplication acts only on the $\mathfrak{S}$-component. This construction also yields the product relation for right dimensionalities: $\dim_r(\mathfrak{P}) = \dim_r(\mathfrak{R}) \cdot \dim_r(\mathfrak{S})$.
