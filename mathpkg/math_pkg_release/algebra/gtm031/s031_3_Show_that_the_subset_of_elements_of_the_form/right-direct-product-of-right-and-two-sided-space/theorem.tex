Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a right vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a two-sided vector space over $\Delta$. Form the direct product $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$ by regarding $\mathfrak{S}$ as a right vector space. Then $\mathfrak{P}$ is a right vector space over $\Delta$ by defining

$$\left(\sum x_i \times y_i\right) \alpha = \sum x_i \times y_i \alpha \qquad (10)$$

for $\alpha \in \Delta$, where the multiplication $y_i \alpha$ is the right scalar multiplication in $\mathfrak{S}$.
