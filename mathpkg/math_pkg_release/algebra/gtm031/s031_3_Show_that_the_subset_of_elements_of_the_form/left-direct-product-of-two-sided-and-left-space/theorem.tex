Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a two-sided vector space over a division ring $\Delta$ and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a left vector space over $\Delta$. Form the direct product $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$ by regarding $\mathfrak{R}$ as a right vector space. Then $\mathfrak{P}$ is a left vector space over $\Delta$ by defining

$$\alpha\left(\sum x_i \times y_i\right) = \sum \alpha x_i \times y_i \qquad (9)$$

for $\alpha \in \Delta$, where the multiplication $\alpha x_i$ is the left scalar multiplication in $\mathfrak{R}$.
