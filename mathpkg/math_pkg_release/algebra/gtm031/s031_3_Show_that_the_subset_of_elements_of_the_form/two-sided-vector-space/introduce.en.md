A two-sided vector space is a vector space over a division ring that is simultaneously a left and a right vector space, with the crucial compatibility condition that left and right scalar multiplications commute. This concept is introduced to overcome the limitation that the direct product of two one-sided vector spaces (one right, one left) yields only a group rather than a vector space. By requiring both left and right structures to coexist, two-sided vector spaces allow direct products to again be vector spaces.
