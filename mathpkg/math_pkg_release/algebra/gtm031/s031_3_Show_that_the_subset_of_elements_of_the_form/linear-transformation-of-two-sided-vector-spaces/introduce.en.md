A linear transformation between two-sided vector spaces is required to be linear with respect to both the left and the right vector space structures simultaneously. This is stronger than just being a linear transformation of the underlying left (or right) spaces; the map must respect right scalar multiplication as well. These doubly-linear maps serve as the natural morphisms in the category of two-sided vector spaces, and they are used to define the notion of equivalence between two-sided spaces.
