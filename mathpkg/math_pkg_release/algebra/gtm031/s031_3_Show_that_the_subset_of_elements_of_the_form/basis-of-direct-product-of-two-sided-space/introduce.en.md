When $\mathfrak{R}$ is a two-sided vector space with left basis $(e_i)$ and $\mathfrak{S}$ is a left vector space with basis $(f_j)$, the direct product $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R} \times \mathfrak{S}$ (with the left structure defined by formula (9)) has as a basis the formal products $e_i \times f_j$. This means the left dimension of $\mathfrak{P}$ equals the product of the left dimensions: $\dim(\mathfrak{P}) = \dim(\mathfrak{R}) \cdot \dim(\mathfrak{S})$. The proof shows that every element of $\mathfrak{P}$ is a left linear combination of these vectors and that they are linearly independent.
