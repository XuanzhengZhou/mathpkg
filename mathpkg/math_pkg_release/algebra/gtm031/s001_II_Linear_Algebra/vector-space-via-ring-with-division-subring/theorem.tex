Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be an arbitrary ring with an identity element $1$ and suppose that $\mathfrak{R}$ contains a division subring $\Delta$ that contains $1$. Define scalar multiplication for $\rho \in \Delta$ and $x \in \mathfrak{R}$ as the ring product $\rho x$. Then:

\begin{itemize}
\item Axioms A1--A4 hold because $\mathfrak{R}$ is an abelian group under ring addition.
\item S1 follows from the left distributive law: $\rho(x + y) = \rho x + \rho y$.
\item S2 follows from the right distributive law: $(\rho + \sigma)x = \rho x + \sigma x$.
\item S3 follows from associativity of ring multiplication: $(\rho \sigma)x = \rho(\sigma x)$.
\item S4 holds because the identity element of $\Delta$ is the identity $1$ of $\mathfrak{R}$.
\end{itemize}

Hence the additive group $\mathfrak{R}$, the division ring $\Delta$, and the multiplication $\rho x$ constitute a vector space over $\Delta$.
