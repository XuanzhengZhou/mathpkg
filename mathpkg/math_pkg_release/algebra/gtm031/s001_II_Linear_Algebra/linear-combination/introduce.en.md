A linear combination is the fundamental algebraic operation that builds new vectors from a given set by scaling and adding. In the context of a vector space over a division ring $\Delta$, we take finitely many vectors $v_i$ and multiply each by a scalar coefficient $\xi_i \in \Delta$ (on the left, in Jacobson's convention), then sum the results. The concept of linear combination underpins the entire theory of linear dependence, spanning sets, and bases. Axiom F for finite dimensional spaces asserts precisely that every vector can be expressed uniquely as a linear combination of a fixed finite set of basis vectors.
