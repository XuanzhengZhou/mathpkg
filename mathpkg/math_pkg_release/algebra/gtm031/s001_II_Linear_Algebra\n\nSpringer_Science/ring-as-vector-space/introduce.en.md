Jacobson observes that any ring \(\mathfrak{R}\) with identity that contains a division subring \(\Delta\) (with the same identity) naturally becomes a vector space over \(\Delta\) by using the ring multiplication as scalar multiplication. The vector space axioms S1--S4 are then consequences of the ring axioms. This construction shows that vector spaces arise throughout algebra. Two examples are given: the complex numbers \(\mathbb{C}\) form a 2-dimensional vector space over \(\mathbb{R}\) with basis \(\{1, i\}\); the polynomial ring \(\Delta[\lambda]\) is infinite dimensional over \(\Delta\). The polynomial ring example will be revisited in later exercises.
