The concrete model for abstract vector spaces is \(\Delta^{(n)}\), the set of all ordered \(n\)-tuples with entries from a division ring \(\Delta\). Addition is defined coordinate-wise: \((x+y)_i = x_i + y_i\), and scalar multiplication is likewise coordinate-wise: \((\alpha x)_i = \alpha x_i\). Jacobson verifies that this system satisfies the vector space axioms A1--A4 and S1--S4, and by choosing the standard basis vectors \(e_i\) (with 1 in the \(i\)-th position and 0 elsewhere) one sees that property F also holds, making \(\Delta^{(n)}\) a finite dimensional vector space. The text adopts left scalar multiplication by convention, though right multiplication would yield a parallel theory.
