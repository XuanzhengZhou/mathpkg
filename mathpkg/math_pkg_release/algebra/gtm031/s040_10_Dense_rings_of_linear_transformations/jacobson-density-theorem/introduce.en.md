The Jacobson Density Theorem (also known as the Chevalley-Jacobson Density Theorem) is the crowning result of the theory of dense rings. It states that every irreducible ring of endomorphisms of a commutative group is isomorphic to a dense ring of linear transformations over a division ring. The proof proceeds in three steps: first, Schur's Lemma gives the division ring $\mathfrak{B}$; then one proves $2$-fold transitivity; finally, an induction argument shows that $2$-fold transitivity implies full density. The theorem establishes the complete equivalence of the algebraic notion of irreducibility and the topological notion of density.
