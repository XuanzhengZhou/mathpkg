Let $\mathfrak{R}_i$, $i = 1, 2$, be a vector space over a division ring $\Delta_i$ and let $\mathfrak{R}_i'$ be a total subspace of linear functions on $\mathfrak{R}_i$. Suppose that $\mathfrak{A}_i$ is a subring of $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}_i' \mid \mathfrak{R}_i)$ containing $\mathfrak{J}(\mathfrak{R}_i' \mid \mathfrak{R}_i)$ and let $\phi$ be an isomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}_1$ onto $\mathfrak{A}_2$. Then there exists a $1$-$1$ semi-linear transformation $U$ of $\mathfrak{R}_1$ onto $\mathfrak{R}_2$, whose transpose $U^*$ maps $\mathfrak{R}_2'$ onto $\mathfrak{R}_1'$, such that
$$A_1 \phi = U^{-1} A_1 U$$
holds for all $A_1 \in \mathfrak{A}_1$. In particular, the base division rings $\Delta_1$ and $\Delta_2$ are isomorphic, and $\mathfrak{R}_1$ and $\mathfrak{R}_2$ have the same dimensionality over their respective division rings.
