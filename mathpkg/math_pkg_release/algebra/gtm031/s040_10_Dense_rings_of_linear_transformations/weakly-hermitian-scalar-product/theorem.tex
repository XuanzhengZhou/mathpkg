A non-degenerate scalar product $h$ on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a division ring $\Delta$, with associated anti-automorphism $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$, is called \textbf{weakly hermitian} if there exists an anti-automorphism $a$ of $\Delta$ and a $1$-$1$ semi-linear mapping $Q$ of $\mathfrak{R}$ onto itself, with associated automorphism $a^2$, such that
$$\overline{h(y, x)} = h(x, yQ)$$
for all $x, y \in \mathfrak{R}$, where the involution $\overline{\cdot}$ on $\Delta$ is given by $\bar{\alpha} = \alpha^a$. Equivalently, $h(x, \alpha y) = h(x, y)\bar{\alpha}$ and the mapping $Q$ satisfies $(\alpha x)Q = \alpha^{a^2}(xQ)$.
