Let $r$ be arbitrary and assume that the vectors $x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_\rho$ form a basis for the set of $x$'s. Then by Theorem 9 there exists a non-vanishing $\rho$-rowed minor in the first $\rho$ rows of $(\alpha)$. Moreover, since any $\rho + 1$ $x$'s are linearly dependent, every $(\rho + 1)$-rowed minor in $(\alpha)$ vanishes. Hence the determinantal rank equals the row rank $\rho$.

If we apply the same arguments to right vector spaces, we can show that the column rank and the determinantal rank are equal. As a consequence, in the commutative case the two ranks (row and column) of a matrix are equal.
