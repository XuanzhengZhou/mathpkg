As a consequence of the equalities between row rank and determinantal rank, and between column rank and determinantal rank, it follows that in the commutative case the row rank and the column rank of a matrix are equal. This fundamental fact, taken for granted in elementary linear algebra over fields, is established here for matrices over any commutative division ring (i.e., a field).
