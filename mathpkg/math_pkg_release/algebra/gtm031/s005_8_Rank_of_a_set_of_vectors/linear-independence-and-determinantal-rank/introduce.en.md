Theorem 9 establishes the fundamental equivalence between linear independence of row vectors and the non-vanishing of an $r$-rowed minor. Specifically, the row vectors of an $r \times n$ matrix are linearly independent if and only if the matrix has determinantal rank $r$. This result serves as the bridge between the geometric notion of linear independence and the algebraic notion of determinantal rank, and is the key step in proving the equality of row rank and determinantal rank.
