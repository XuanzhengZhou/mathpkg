The row rank of a matrix is defined by forming row vectors relative to a chosen ordered basis of the underlying vector space and taking the rank (i.e., the maximal number of linearly independent vectors) of that set. Notably, the row rank is independent of the choice of basis. This definition extends the abstract notion of the rank of a set of vectors to the concrete setting of matrices with entries in a division ring.
