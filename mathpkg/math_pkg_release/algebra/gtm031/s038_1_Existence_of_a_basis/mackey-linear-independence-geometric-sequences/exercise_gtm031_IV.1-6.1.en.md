(Mackey) Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over a field $\Phi$. Prove that the collection of sequences $\xi_\gamma = (\gamma, \gamma^2, \gamma^3, \cdots)$, $\gamma \neq 0$ in $\Phi$, is linearly independent. (Note: This leads to an elementary proof of Theorem 2 for the commutative case.)
