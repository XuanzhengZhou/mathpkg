We can verify easily as in the finite case that the correspondence $A \to (\alpha)$ determined by $e_i A = \sum_j \alpha_{ij} f_j$ is 1-1 of the set $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{S})$ of linear transformations onto the set of row finite $I \times J$ matrices. The matrix corresponding to the sum of two transformations is obtained from the two matrices by adding components $\alpha_{ii'}, \beta_{ii'}$. It follows that the row finite matrices form a group relative to this operation.
