In the finite topology on $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{S})$ (and $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$):
\begin{enumerate}
\item The addition mapping $(A, B) \mapsto A + B$ is continuous.
\item The scalar multiplication $(\gamma, A) \mapsto \gamma A$ for $\gamma \in \Phi$ (the center of $\Delta$) is continuous.
\item The composition mapping $(A, B) \mapsto AB$ in $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ is continuous.
\end{enumerate}
Thus $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ is a topological ring and a topological algebra over $\Phi$.
