The finite topology makes the space of linear transformations into a topological vector space and, in the case of endomorphisms, a topological ring. Addition and scalar multiplication are continuous, and crucially, composition (matrix multiplication) is also continuous: if $X$ is near $A$ and $Y$ is near $B$ in the finite topology, then $XY$ is near $AB$. This continuity of multiplication is essential for treating $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ as a topological algebra and for studying closures of subrings, such as the closure of the polynomial ring $\Phi[A]$ for a fixed operator $A$.
