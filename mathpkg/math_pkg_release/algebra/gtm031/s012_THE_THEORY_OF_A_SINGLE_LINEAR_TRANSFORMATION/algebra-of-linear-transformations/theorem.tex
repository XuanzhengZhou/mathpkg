Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be a vector space over a field $\Phi$, and let $\mathfrak{L} \equiv \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ be the set of linear transformations of $\mathfrak{R}$ into itself. Then $\mathfrak{L}$ is an algebra over $\Phi$, meaning:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{L}$ is a ring;
\item $\mathfrak{L}$ is a vector space over $\Phi$ with addition coinciding with ring addition;
\item $\gamma(AB) = (\gamma A)B = A(\gamma B)$ for all $A, B \in \mathfrak{L}$ and $\gamma \in \Phi$, where $\gamma A = \gamma_l A = A \gamma_l$.
\end{enumerate}

The dimension of $\mathfrak{L}$ over $\Phi$ is $n^2$, where $n = \dim_\Phi \mathfrak{R}$.
