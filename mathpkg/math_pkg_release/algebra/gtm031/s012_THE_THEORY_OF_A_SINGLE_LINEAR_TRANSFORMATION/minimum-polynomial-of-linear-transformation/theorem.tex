Let $A \in \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ be a linear transformation on a vector space $\mathfrak{R}$ over a field $\Phi$. Consider the polynomial evaluation homomorphism
$$\Phi[\lambda] \to \mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R}), \quad f(\lambda) \mapsto f(A).$$
Let $\mathfrak{K} = \{\, \nu(\lambda) \in \Phi[\lambda] \mid \nu(A) = 0 \,\}$ be the kernel of this homomorphism. Since $\Phi[\lambda]$ is a principal ideal domain, $\mathfrak{K} = (\mu(\lambda))$ consists of all multiples of a particular polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$. The generator $\mu(\lambda)$ is uniquely determined by requiring that its leading coefficient be $1$. The polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ is called the \textit{minimum polynomial} of the linear transformation $A$.
