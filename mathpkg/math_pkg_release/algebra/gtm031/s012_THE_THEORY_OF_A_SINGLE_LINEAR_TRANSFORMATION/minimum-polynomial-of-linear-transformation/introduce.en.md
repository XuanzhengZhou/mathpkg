The minimum polynomial (or minimal polynomial) of a linear transformation $A$ on a finite-dimensional vector space is the unique monic polynomial $\mu(\lambda)$ of least degree such that $\mu(A) = 0$. Equivalently, it is the monic generator of the kernel of the evaluation homomorphism $f(\lambda) \mapsto f(A)$ from the polynomial ring $\Phi[\lambda]$ into the algebra of linear transformations. Every polynomial annihilating $A$ is a multiple of the minimum polynomial. Its existence for any linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space follows from the finite-dimensionality of $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$.
