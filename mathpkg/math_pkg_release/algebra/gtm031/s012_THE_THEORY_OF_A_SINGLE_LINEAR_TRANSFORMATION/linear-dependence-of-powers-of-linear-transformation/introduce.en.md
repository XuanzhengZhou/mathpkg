Since the algebra $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{R})$ of linear transformations on an $n$-dimensional vector space is itself $n^2$-dimensional, the infinite sequence of powers $1, A, A^2, A^3, \ldots$ of any single linear transformation $A$ must eventually become linearly dependent. This elementary observation is the starting point for the entire theory of a single linear transformation: it guarantees the existence of a polynomial relation satisfied by $A$, which leads directly to the concept of the minimum polynomial.
