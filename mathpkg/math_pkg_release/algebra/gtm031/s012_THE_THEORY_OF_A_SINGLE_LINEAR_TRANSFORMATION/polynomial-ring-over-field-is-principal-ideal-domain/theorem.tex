Let $\Phi$ be a field and let $\Phi[\lambda]$ be the ring of polynomials in the indeterminate $\lambda$ with coefficients in $\Phi$. Then $\Phi[\lambda]$ is a principal ideal domain: every ideal $\mathfrak{a} \subseteq \Phi[\lambda]$ is of the form $\mathfrak{a} = (p(\lambda))$ for some polynomial $p(\lambda) \in \Phi[\lambda]$. The generator $p(\lambda)$ is uniquely determined up to a nonzero constant factor, and is uniquely determined if required to be monic.
