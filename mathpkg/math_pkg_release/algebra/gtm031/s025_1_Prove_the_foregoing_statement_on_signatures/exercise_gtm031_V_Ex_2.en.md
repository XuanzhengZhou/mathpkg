If $g$ is an alternate non-degenerate scalar product, a $1$-$1$ linear transformation $S$ of $\mathfrak{R}$ such that $g(xS, yS) = g(x, y)$ is called a symplectic transformation. Prove the following analogue of Theorem 8: Any $g$-equivalence of a subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$ can be extended to a symplectic transformation.
