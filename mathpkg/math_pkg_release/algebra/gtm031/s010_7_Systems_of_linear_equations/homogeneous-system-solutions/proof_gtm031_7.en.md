Consider the homogeneous system $\sum_{i=1}^n \xi_i \alpha_{ij} = 0$, $j = 1, 2, \dots, m$. The mapping
$$
(\xi_1, \xi_2, \dots, \xi_n) \mapsto \sum_{i=1}^n \xi_i u_i
$$
is a linear transformation from the $n$-dimensional space of $n$-tuples to the subspace spanned by $u_1, u_2, \dots, u_n$. The nullity $\nu$ of this transformation is the dimension of the solution space. By the relation between rank and nullity, $\nu = n - \rho$ where $\rho$ is the rank of the transformation, which equals the row rank of $(\alpha)$. Choosing a basis of the null-space yields $n - \rho$ linearly independent solutions. Since the null-space consists precisely of the solutions, any solution is a left linear combination of the basis elements.
