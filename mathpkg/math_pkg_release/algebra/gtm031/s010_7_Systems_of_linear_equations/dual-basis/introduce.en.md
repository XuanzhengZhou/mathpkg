The dual basis $\{e_i^*\}$ is the basis of $\mathfrak{R}^*$ canonically associated to a given basis $\{e_i\}$ of $\mathfrak{R}$. Each $e_i^*$ picks out the $i$-th coordinate of a vector relative to the original basis. Any linear function $f$ can be uniquely expressed as $f = \sum e_i^* \alpha_i$ where $\alpha_i = f(e_i)$.
