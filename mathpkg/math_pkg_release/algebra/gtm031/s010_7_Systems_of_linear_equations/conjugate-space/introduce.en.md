The conjugate space $\mathfrak{R}^*$ is the dual space of a left vector space $\mathfrak{R}$. Because scalar multiplication is defined on the right ($f\mu$ applies $\mu$ after evaluating $f$), $\mathfrak{R}^*$ becomes a right vector space. When $\mathfrak{R}$ is finite-dimensional with basis $(e_1, \dots, e_n)$, $\mathfrak{R}^*$ is also $n$-dimensional, and the matrices representing linear functions are $n \times 1$ column vectors.
