For a single linear transformation $A$ over a field, complete reducibility is equivalent to the minimum polynomial being squarefree (a product of distinct irreducible polynomials). The forward direction uses Theorem 2: if $A$ is completely reducible, $\mathfrak{R}$ decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible invariant subspaces, and by Theorem 3 each irreducible component has a prime minimum polynomial, whose least common multiple is $\mu(\lambda)$ and therefore a product of distinct primes. Conversely, if $\mu(\lambda)$ is a product of distinct primes, each invariant factor is squarefree, forcing the rational canonical form to be a direct sum of irreducible summands, hence $A$ is completely reducible.
