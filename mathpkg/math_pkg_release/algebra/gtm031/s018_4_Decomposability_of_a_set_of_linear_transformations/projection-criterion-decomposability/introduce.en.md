Theorem 1 gives a fundamental algebraic criterion for decomposability: a set $\Omega$ of linear transformations admits a proper invariant direct sum decomposition if and only if there is a nontrivial projection (different from $0$ and the identity) that commutes with every element of $\Omega$. The forward direction uses the projections determined by the decomposition; the converse constructs the decomposition from the projection $E$ and its complement $1-E$. This criterion translates the geometric notion of decomposability into an algebraic condition on the centralizer of $\Omega$.
