Two invariant subspaces are $\Omega$-isomorphic if there is an $\Omega$-equivariant linear isomorphism between them. This means the action of every transformation in $\Omega$ is represented by the same matrix on corresponding bases. In terms of arbitrary bases, the matrices of each $A \in \Omega$ on the two subspaces are related by a fixed similarity transformation independent of $A$. This notion of equivalence is fundamental for the Jordan-Hölder and Krull-Schmidt theorems in the context of linear transformation sets.
