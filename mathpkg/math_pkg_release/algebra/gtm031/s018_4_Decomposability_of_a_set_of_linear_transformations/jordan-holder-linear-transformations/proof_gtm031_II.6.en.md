The proof follows from the standard Jordan-Hölder argument adapted to $\Omega$-invariant subspaces. Given two composition series

$$0 \subset \mathfrak{R}_1 \subset \mathfrak{R}_2 \subset \cdots \subset \mathfrak{R}_t = \mathfrak{R},$$
$$0 \subset \mathfrak{S}_1 \subset \mathfrak{S}_2 \subset \cdots \subset \mathfrak{S}_u = \mathfrak{R},$$

one refines both series using intersections, then applies the isomorphism theorem for $\Omega$-modules to establish a 1-1 correspondence between the factors of composition $\mathfrak{R}_i/\mathfrak{R}_{i-1}$ and $\mathfrak{S}_j/\mathfrak{S}_{j-1}$ such that corresponding pairs are $\Omega$-isomorphic. The equality $t = u$ follows from this correspondence. The argument is identical to the classical Jordan-Hölder theorem for groups, translated to the context of $\Omega$-invariant subspaces (equivalently, modules over the enveloping ring).
