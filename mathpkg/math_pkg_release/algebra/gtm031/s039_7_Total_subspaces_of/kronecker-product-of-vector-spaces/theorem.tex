\textbf{Definition.} Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be dual to $\mathfrak{R}'$ relative to the bilinear form $g$, and let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a left vector space. Consider the set $\mathfrak{P}$ of linear transformations of $\mathfrak{R}$ into $\mathfrak{S}$ which have the form
$$x \mapsto \sum_{k=1}^{m} g(x, x_k')y_k,$$
where $x_k' \in \mathfrak{R}'$ and $y_k \in \mathfrak{S}$. It is immediate that $\mathfrak{P}$ is a subgroup of $\mathfrak{L}(\mathfrak{R}, \mathfrak{S})$. If $x' \in \mathfrak{R}'$ and $y \in \mathfrak{S}$, then we define $x' \times y$ to be the linear transformation $x \mapsto g(x, x')y$ belonging to $\mathfrak{P}$. Then $\mathfrak{P}$ is a direct product group, $\mathfrak{P} = \mathfrak{R}' \times \mathfrak{S}$, of $\mathfrak{R}'$ and $\mathfrak{S}$.
