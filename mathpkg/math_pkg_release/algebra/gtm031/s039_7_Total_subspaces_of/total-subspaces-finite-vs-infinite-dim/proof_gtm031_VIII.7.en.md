**Proof.** For finite dimensional $\mathfrak{R}$, the conjugate space $\mathfrak{R}^*$ has the same finite dimension. The finite topology on a finite dimensional space is discrete, so closure equals the subspace itself. Only the whole space can be dense, hence only $\mathfrak{R}^*$ itself is total. For infinite dimensional $\mathfrak{R}$, $\mathfrak{R}_0^*$ is a proper subspace (column-finite matrices vs all linear functions) yet total as shown earlier.
