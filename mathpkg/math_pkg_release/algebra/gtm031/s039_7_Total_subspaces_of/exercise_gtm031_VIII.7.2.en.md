(Mackey) Let $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$ be as in Exercise 1. Assume, moreover, that $\mathfrak{R}'$ has a denumerable basis. Prove that $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$ have complementary bases; that is, there exist bases $(u_i)$ for $\mathfrak{R}$ and $(u_i^*)$ for $\mathfrak{R}'$ such that $u_i^*(u_j) = \delta_{ij}$ for all $i, j = 1, 2, \cdots$.
