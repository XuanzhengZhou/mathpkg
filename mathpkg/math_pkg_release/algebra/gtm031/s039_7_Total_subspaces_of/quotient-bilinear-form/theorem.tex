\textbf{Definition.} Let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a subspace of $\mathfrak{R}$. Define $\bar{g}$ on $\mathfrak{S} \times (\mathfrak{R}' / j(\mathfrak{S}))$ by $\bar{g}(x, \bar{y}') = g(x, y')$ where $\bar{y}' = y' + j(\mathfrak{S})$. This is well-defined and is a bilinear form. Moreover, $\mathfrak{S}$ and $\mathfrak{R}' / j(\mathfrak{S})$ are dual relative to $\bar{g}$.
