Let $\mathfrak{R}$ and $\mathfrak{R}'$ be as in Exercise 2, and let $(e_i)$, $(e_i^*)$ be complementary bases. Show that the basis $(f_i)$ where $f_1 = e_1$ and $f_i = e_i + e_{i-1}$ for $i > 1$ has no complementary set in $\mathfrak{R}'$.
