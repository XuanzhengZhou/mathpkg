The kernel $n$ of the canonical homomorphism $\varphi: A \rightarrow A_S$ is (Vol. I, Ch. IV, § 9) the set of all elements $b$ in $A$ for which there exists an element $s = 1 - m$ in $S(m \in m)$ such that $bs = 0$. This last relation implies $b = bm = bm^2 = \cdots = bm^n$, hence $n$ is contained in $\bigcap_{n=0}^{\infty} m^n$. Conversely, if $b$ is an element of $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} m^n$, then there exists an integer $q$ such that the ideal $mb$ contains $m^q \cap Ab$ (Vol. I, Ch. IV, § 7, Lemma 1) and therefore is equal to $Ab$ (since $b \in m^q$, all $q$); we thus have $b \in mb$, i.e., $b = mb$ for some $m$ in $m$, whence $b(1 - m) = 0$ and $b$ belongs to $n$. Since the ideal $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} m^n$, which is the closure of 0 in $A$, is also the kernel of the homomorphism of $A$ into its completion $\hat{A}$, it follows that the quotient ring $A_S$ may be identified with a subring of the completion $\hat{A}$.

From now on we simplify matters by replacing $A$ by $A'$, i.e., by assuming that $A$ is a Hausdorff space in its $m$-topology. In that case we have $\bigcap_{n=0}^{\infty} m^n = (0)$ and hence no element of $S$ is a zero-divisor in $A$. Therefore $A$ is a subring of $A_S$.
