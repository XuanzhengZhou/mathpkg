We consider the smallest segment $G^\star$ in $\Gamma^\star$ such that $G^\star \supset G$ ($G^\star =$ set of all elements of $\Gamma^\star$ which are of the form $\pm \alpha^\star$, where $0 \leq \alpha^\star \leq \alpha$ for some $\alpha$ in $G$). Then it is immediately seen that $G^\star$ is a subgroup of $\Gamma^\star$ and that it is a proper subgroup of $\Gamma^\star$, since $G$ is a proper subgroup of $\Gamma$. Hence $G^\star$ is an isolated subgroup of $\Gamma^\star$, and it is clear from the definition of $G^\star$ that we have $G^\star \cap \Gamma = G$ and that $G^\star$ is the smallest of all the isolated subgroups $H^\star$ of $\Gamma^\star$ such that $H^\star \cap \Gamma = G$.
