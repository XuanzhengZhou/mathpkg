If $R$ is an integrally closed finite integral domain, of transcendence degree $r$, then every proper principal ideal in $R$ is unmixed, of dimension $r-1$. If, in addition, $R$ is also a unique factorization domain (in particular, if $R$ is a polynomial ring), then also the converse is true, i.e., every unmixed ideal of dimension $r-1$ is principal.
