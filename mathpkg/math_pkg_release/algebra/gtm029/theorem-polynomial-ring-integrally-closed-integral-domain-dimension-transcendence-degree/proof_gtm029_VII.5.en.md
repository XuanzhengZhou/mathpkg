The first part of the theorem is a restatement of Theorem 14 in Vol. I, Ch. V, § 6. Conversely, if $\mathfrak{A}$ is an unmixed ideal, of dimension $r-1$, then all its prime ideals $\mathfrak{p}_i$ are minimal, and if $R$ is a UFD then each $\mathfrak{p}_i$ is a principal ideal $(G_i)$ and each primary ideal having $\mathfrak{p}_i$ as associated prime ideal is a power $(G_i^{\nu_i})$ of $\mathfrak{p}_i$. We have therefore, for suitable integers $\nu_i$, $\mathfrak{A} = \bigcap_i (G_i^{\nu_i})$. Since $R$ is a UFD and the $G_i$ are two by two relatively prime, it follows that $\mathfrak{A}$ coincides with the principal ideal generated by $\prod_i G_i^{\nu_i}$. Q.E.D
