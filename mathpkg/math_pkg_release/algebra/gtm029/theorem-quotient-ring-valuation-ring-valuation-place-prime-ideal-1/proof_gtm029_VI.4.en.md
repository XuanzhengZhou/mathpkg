It will be sufficient to show that every valuation ring $S$ in $K$ which contains $\mathfrak{Q}$

It has been proved (Vol. I, Ch. V, § 6, Theorem 15) that there exists an element $m$ of $R_p$ such that every element of $R_p$ may be written as $um^q$ where $u$ is a unit in $R_p$ and $q$ a non-negative integer. It follows, upon division, that every element of $K$ may also be written under the form $vm^s$, where $v$ is a unit in $R_p$ and $s$ an integer. Let $S$ be a subring of $K$ properly containing $R_p$. Then $S$ contains some element $vm^s$, with $s < 0$. Thus, since $S$ contains $R_p$, it contains $m^{-1} = (m^{-s-1}v^{-1})(vm^s)$; hence $S$ contains $m^{-n}$ for every integer $n$, and therefore also every element $um^q$ (a unit in $R_p$, $q$—any integer). It follows that $S = K$. Q.E.D
