Let $A$ be a noetherian ring, $m$ and $\alpha$ two ideals of $A$ such that $\alpha$ is closed in the $m$-topology. Then the completion of $A/\alpha$ (for its $(m+\alpha)/\alpha$-topology) is canonically isomorphic to $\hat{A}/\hat{A}\alpha$.

In fact the $(m+\alpha)/\alpha$-topology of $A/\alpha$ coincides with the $m$-topology of $A/\alpha$ considered as an $A$-module, and we thus have a special case of Theorem 6, with $E=A$ and $F=\alpha$.

We terminate this section by

THEOREM 7. Let $A$ be a ring, $m$ an ideal in $A$, $E$ an $A$-module and $F$ a submodule of $E$. Suppose that $A$ is a complete Hausdorff space for its $m$-topology, and that $E$ is a Hausdorff space for its $m$-topology. Let $\{x_1, \cdots, x_q\}$ be a finite system of elements of $F$ such that their initial forms $G(x_i)$ generate (over $G_m(A))$ the leading submodule of $F$ in $G_m(E)$. Then the elements $\{x_1, \cdots, x_q\}$ generate $F$.
