Let $A$ be an ideal in $0$, primary for $m$, let $r$ be the order of $A$, let $s$ be the degree of $c(A)$ and let

(5) $B = A: m^{r-s}$.

If $A$ is a contracted ideal then $c(A)$ is a power of $\bar{g}$ (possibly, $c(A) = 1$), and we have

(6) $A = B m^{r-s}$.

Furthermore, $B$ also is a contracted ideal, and we have $r(B) = s$, $c(B) = c(A)$.
