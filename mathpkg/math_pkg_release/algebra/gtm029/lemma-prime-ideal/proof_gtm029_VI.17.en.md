The inclusion $V(\vartheta_i) \subset M \cap L(\vartheta_i)$ is obvious. Conversely, if $P \in M \cap L(\vartheta_i)$, $P$ contains $\vartheta_i$ and hence dominates the element $P' = (\vartheta_i)_{\mathfrak{p}'}$ of $V(\vartheta_i)$, where $\mathfrak{p}' = \mathfrak{m}(P) \cap \vartheta_i$. Since $M$ is irredundant, this implies $P = P'$, and proves the first assertion. The second assertion is now pure topology. The necessity of the condition is obvious. In the proof of the sufficiency it is enough to consider the case of open sets (replace $H$ by $M - H$). In this case, since $V(\vartheta_i)$ is open in $M$ and since $H \cap V(\vartheta_i)$ is open in $V(\vartheta_i)$, $H \cap V(\vartheta_i)$ is open in $M$, whence also $H$ is open in $M$, for $H$ is the union of the sets $H \cap V(\vartheta_i)$. Q.E.D
