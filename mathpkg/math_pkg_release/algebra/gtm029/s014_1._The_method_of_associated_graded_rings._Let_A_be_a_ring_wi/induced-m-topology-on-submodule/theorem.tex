Let $A$ be a noetherian ring, $\mathfrak{m}$ an ideal in $A$, $E$ a finite $A$-module and $F$ a submodule of $E$. Then the topology induced on $F$ by the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $E$ coincides with the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology of $F$. More precisely, there exists an integer $s$ such that for every $n$,

$$\mathfrak{m}^n F \subset F \cap \mathfrak{m}^n E \subset \mathfrak{m}^{n-s} F \quad (\text{for } n \geq s).$$
