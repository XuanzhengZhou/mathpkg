The $\mathfrak{m}$-adic distance is defined by $d(x,y) = 2^{-v(x-y)}$, where $v$ is the order function. This distance satisfies the strong (ultrametric) triangle inequality, making it a non-archimedean metric (though it only defines a metric in the usual sense when the space is Hausdorff). It provides the foundation for defining Cauchy sequences and completions.
