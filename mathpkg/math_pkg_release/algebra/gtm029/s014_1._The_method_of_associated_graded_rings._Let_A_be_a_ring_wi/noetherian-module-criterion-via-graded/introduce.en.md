If the associated graded module $G(E)$ is noetherian over $G(A)$, then $E$ is noetherian over $A$. This is because for every submodule $F$ of $E$, its leading submodule is finitely generated (by the noetherian hypothesis on $G(E)$), and Theorem 7 then implies $F$ itself is finitely generated.
