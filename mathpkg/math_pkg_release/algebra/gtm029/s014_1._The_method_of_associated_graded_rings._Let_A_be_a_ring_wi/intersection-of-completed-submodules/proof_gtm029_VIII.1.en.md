Consider the mapping $g: F \oplus G \to E$ defined by $g(x,y) = x - y$ for $x \in F, y \in G$. The kernel $K$ of $g$ is the set of elements $(x,x)$ with $x \in F$ and $x \in G$, which is isomorphic to $F \cap G$. From the exact sequence $0 \to K \xrightarrow{i} F \oplus G \xrightarrow{g} E$, Theorem 11 (exactness of completion) yields the exact sequence $0 \to \hat{K} \xrightarrow{i} \hat{F} \oplus \hat{G} \xrightarrow{\bar{g}} \hat{E}$, where $\bar{g}(\xi, \eta) = \xi - \eta$ for $\xi \in \hat{F}, \eta \in \hat{G}$. Thus $\hat{K}$ is identified with $\hat{F} \cap \hat{G}$, i.e., $\hat{A}(F \cap G) = \hat{A}F \cap \hat{A}G$.
