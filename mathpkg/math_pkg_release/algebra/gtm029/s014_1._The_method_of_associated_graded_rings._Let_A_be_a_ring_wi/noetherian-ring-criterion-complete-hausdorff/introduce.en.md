A complete Hausdorff ring whose associated graded ring is noetherian must itself be noetherian. In particular, if $\mathfrak{m}$ is finitely generated and $A/\mathfrak{m}$ is noetherian, then $G(A) \cong (A/\mathfrak{m})[x_1,\ldots,x_q]/\mathfrak{I}$ is noetherian, hence $A$ is noetherian.
