A point $x$ belongs to the closure $\bar{S}$ if and only if every neighborhood of $x$ intersects $S$. The basic neighborhoods of $x$ are $x + \mathfrak{m}^n E$. Thus $x \in \bar{S} \iff (x + \mathfrak{m}^n E) \cap S \neq \emptyset$ for all $n \iff x \in S + \mathfrak{m}^n E$ for all $n \iff x \in \bigcap_{n=0}^{\infty} (S + \mathfrak{m}^n E)$.
