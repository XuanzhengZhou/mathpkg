Let $\{x_1, \ldots, x_q\}$ generate $E$ over $A$. Define the surjection $\varphi: A^q \to E$ by $\varphi(a_1, \ldots, a_q) = \sum a_i x_i$. Let $R$ be the kernel (the module of relations). By Theorem 4 (or Artin-Rees), the $\mathfrak{m}$-topology on $R$ is induced from $A^q$. Passing to completions gives an exact sequence $\hat{R} \to \hat{A}^q \xrightarrow{\hat{\varphi}} \hat{E} \to 0$. The image of $\hat{\varphi}$ is closed (as a finite $\hat{A}$-module, it is complete) and dense (since $\varphi$ is surjective). Hence $\hat{\varphi}$ is surjective, and $\hat{E}$ is generated by the images of $x_i$. The isomorphism with $E \otimes_A \hat{A}$ follows from the universal property of tensor products and the fact that $\hat{A}^q$ is the completion of $A^q$.
