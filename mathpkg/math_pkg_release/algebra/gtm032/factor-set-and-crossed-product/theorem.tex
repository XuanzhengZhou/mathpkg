Let $P/\Phi$ be Galois with group $G$. A factor set is a map $\mu: G \times G \to P^*$ satisfying $\mu_{s,t}^v \mu_{st,v} = \mu_{s,tv} \mu_{t,v}$. The crossed product $(G, P, \mu)$ is the algebra with $P$-basis $\{u_s\}_{s \in G}$ and multiplication $u_s \rho \cdot u_t \sigma = u_{st} \rho^t \sigma \mu_{s,t}$. Two factor sets $\mu, \mu'$ are equivalent if $\mu_{s,t}' = \mu_{s,t} \gamma_{st}^{-1} \gamma_s^t \gamma_t$ for some $\gamma_s \in P^*$.
