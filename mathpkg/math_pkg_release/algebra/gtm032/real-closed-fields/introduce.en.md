Real closed fields are the algebraic analogue of the real numbers. They are characterized by the property that adjoining $\sqrt{-1}$ yields the algebraic closure. Artin-Schreier proved that a field admits an ordering making it real closed if and only if $-1$ is not a sum of squares and the field has no proper algebraic extension with this property.
