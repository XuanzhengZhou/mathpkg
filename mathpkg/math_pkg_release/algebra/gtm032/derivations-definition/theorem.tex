Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an algebra over a field $\Phi$. A derivation of $\mathfrak{A}/\Phi$ is a $\Phi$-linear mapping $D: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}$ satisfying the Leibniz rule: $D(ab) = D(a)b + aD(b)$ for all $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}$. The set of all derivations forms a vector space over $\Phi$. For a field extension $P/\Phi$, derivations detect separability: $P/\Phi$ is separable algebraic if and only if the only derivation of $P/\Phi$ is the zero derivation.
