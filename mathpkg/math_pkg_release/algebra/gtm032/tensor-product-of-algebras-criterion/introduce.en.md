This theorem gives an internal characterization of the tensor product of algebras. If an algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ contains isomorphic copies of $\mathfrak{A}_1$ and $\mathfrak{A}_2$ whose elements commute, whose product of bases forms a basis, and which together generate $\mathfrak{A}$, then $\mathfrak{A}$ is isomorphic to $\mathfrak{A}_1 \otimes_\Phi \mathfrak{A}_2$. For finite-dimensional algebras this reduces to the dimension condition $[\mathfrak{A}:\Phi] = [\mathfrak{A}_1:\Phi][\mathfrak{A}_2:\Phi]$.
