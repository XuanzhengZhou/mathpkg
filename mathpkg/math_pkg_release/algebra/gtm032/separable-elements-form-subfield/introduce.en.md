The set of separable elements in any algebraic extension forms a subfield that is separably algebraically closed in the extension. The proof uses the Lemma to show that sums, products, and inverses of separable elements remain separable, by embedding any two separable elements in a finite Galois extension and applying the lemma. This establishes the separable closure as a fundamental construct.
