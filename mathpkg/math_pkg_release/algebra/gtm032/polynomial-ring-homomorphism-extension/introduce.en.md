This fundamental result states that a polynomial ring over a commutative ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is the free commutative $\mathfrak{A}$-algebra on the indeterminates $x_1, \ldots, x_r$. Any homomorphism from $\mathfrak{A}$ into a commutative ring $\mathfrak{B}$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism from the polynomial ring by specifying the images of the indeterminates arbitrarily. This universal property is the foundation for evaluating polynomials and studying algebraic relations.
