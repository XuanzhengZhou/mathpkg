In characteristic $p > 0$, an algebraic element $\rho$ is separable precisely when the tower $\Phi(\rho) \supseteq \Phi(\rho^p) \supseteq \Phi(\rho^{p^2}) \supseteq \cdots$ stabilizes immediately — that is, $\rho$ can be expressed as a polynomial in $\rho^p$. Purely inseparable elements are characterized by having minimal polynomials of the special form $x^{p^e} - \alpha$, which have only one distinct root. This structural description is essential for understanding the separable/inseparable dichotomy.
