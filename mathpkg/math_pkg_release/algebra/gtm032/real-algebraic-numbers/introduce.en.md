The real algebraic numbers form a countable real closed field that is the real closure of $\mathbb{Q}$. By Tarski's theorem, it is elementarily equivalent to $\mathbb{R}$, meaning that any first-order sentence true in $\mathbb{R}$ is true in the real algebraic numbers. This fact underlies the decidability of real algebraic geometry.
