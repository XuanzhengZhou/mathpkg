The full symmetric group $S_n$ occurs as the Galois group of polynomials over $\mathbb{Q}$ for every degree $n$. The general equation of degree $n$ (with indeterminate coefficients) has Galois group $S_n$. Since $S_n$ is not solvable for $n \geq 5$, this proves the unsolvability of the general quintic and higher degree equations by radicals.
