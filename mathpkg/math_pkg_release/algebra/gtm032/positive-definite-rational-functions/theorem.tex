Hilbert's 17th Problem: Let $f(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \in \mathbb{R}(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ be a rational function that takes only non-negative values wherever it is defined. Then $f$ can be expressed as a sum of squares of rational functions. More generally, over any real closed field, a positive definite rational function is a sum of squares.
