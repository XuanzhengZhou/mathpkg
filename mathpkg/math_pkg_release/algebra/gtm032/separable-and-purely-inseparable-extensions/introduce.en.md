Separability is a fundamental dichotomy in field theory, distinguishing extensions where minimal polynomials have distinct roots (separable) from those where they do not (inseparable). In characteristic 0, all algebraic extensions are separable, so this concept is vacuous. In characteristic $p > 0$, however, inseparable extensions arise naturally. The set of separable elements forms a relatively algebraically closed subfield — the maximal separable subextension. Purely inseparable extensions are those where no element outside the base field is separable, and they are closely related to $p$-th power iterations.
