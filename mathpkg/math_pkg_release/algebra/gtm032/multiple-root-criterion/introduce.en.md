This fundamental criterion connects multiple roots of a polynomial to its formal derivative. A polynomial has repeated roots exactly when it shares a non-trivial common factor with its derivative. For irreducible polynomials, this happens precisely when the derivative vanishes identically — impossible in characteristic $0$, but possible in characteristic $p$ when the polynomial is a polynomial in $x^p$. This distinguishes separable from inseparable extensions.
