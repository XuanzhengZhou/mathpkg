A splitting field of a polynomial $f(x) \in \Phi[x]$ is the smallest extension of $\Phi$ in which $f$ factors completely into linear factors. It is generated over $\Phi$ by all the roots of $f$. Splitting fields are the central objects of Galois theory: a finite Galois extension is precisely a splitting field of a separable polynomial. Splitting fields always exist, and any two splitting fields of the same polynomial over $\Phi$ are $\Phi$-isomorphic.
