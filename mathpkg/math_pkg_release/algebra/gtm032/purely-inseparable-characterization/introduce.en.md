This lemma characterizes purely inseparable elements in characteristic $p$. A separable element $\rho$ can be recovered from its $p$-th powers: $\Phi(\rho) = \Phi(\rho^p)$. A purely inseparable element has minimum polynomial of the special form $x^{p^e} - \alpha$, and conversely any element satisfying such an equation is purely inseparable. This gives a concrete algebraic description of inseparability.
