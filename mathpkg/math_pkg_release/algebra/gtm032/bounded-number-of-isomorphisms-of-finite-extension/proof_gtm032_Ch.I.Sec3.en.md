Let $s_1, s_2, \dots, s_r$ be distinct isomorphisms of $E/\Phi$ into $P/\Phi$. Each $s_i$ is a $\Phi$-linear map from $E$ to $P$, hence $s_i \in \mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P)$. By Dedekind's theorem, the $s_i$ are right $P$-linearly independent. Since $[\mathfrak{L}_\Phi(E, P) : P]_R = [E:\Phi] = n$ by Theorem 1, any set of right $P$-linearly independent elements has size at most $n$. Therefore $r \leq n$.
