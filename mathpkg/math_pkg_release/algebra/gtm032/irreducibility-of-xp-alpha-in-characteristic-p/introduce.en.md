In characteristic $p$, the binomial $x^p - \alpha$ is either irreducible or a $p$-th power of a linear factor. The proof uses the fact that in a splitting field, $x^p - \alpha = (x - \beta)^p$ where $\beta^p = \alpha$. If any proper factor exists, its coefficients must lie in $\Phi$, forcing $\beta \in \Phi$ and hence $\alpha$ to be a $p$-th power in $\Phi$. This lemma is fundamental for understanding purely inseparable extensions.
