This theorem establishes uniqueness of the splitting field up to isomorphism and counts the number of isomorphisms extending a given isomorphism of the base field. The proof uses induction on $[P:\Phi]$, lifting isomorphisms step by step via the extension theorem for simple algebraic extensions. When the polynomial has distinct roots, the number of isomorphisms equals the field degree — a result that characterizes Galois extensions.
