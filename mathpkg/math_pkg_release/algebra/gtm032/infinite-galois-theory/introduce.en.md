Infinite Galois theory extends the fundamental theorem to arbitrary Galois extensions by equipping the Galois group with the Krull (profinite) topology. The Galois group is then a compact, totally disconnected Hausdorff group, and the Galois correspondence holds for closed subgroups. This is essential for studying absolute Galois groups and infinite algebraic extensions.
