Artin's theorem gives a purely lattice-theoretic characterization of simple extensions: a finite extension is simple precisely when it has only finitely many intermediate subfields. The forward direction is proved by noting that each intermediate field is generated by the coefficients of the minimal polynomial of the primitive element over that field, and these are factors of the fixed minimal polynomial over the base. The converse uses a pigeonhole argument on the family of fields $\Phi(\xi + \alpha\eta)$ to show that any two generators can be replaced by a single one.
