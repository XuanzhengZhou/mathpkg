Every polynomial over a field admits a splitting field. The proof proceeds by induction on the degree of $f$: if $f$ has an irreducible factor $g$, one adjoins a single root by forming $\Phi[x]/(g(x))$, obtaining an extension where $g$ (and hence $f$) has at least one linear factor. Factoring out this linear factor reduces the degree, and the induction hypothesis completes the construction. This theorem justifies the fundamental construction underlying all of Galois theory.
