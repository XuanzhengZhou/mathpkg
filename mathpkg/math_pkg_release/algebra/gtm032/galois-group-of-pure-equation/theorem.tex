Assume the field $\Phi$ contains $n$ distinct $n$-th roots of unity. Let $P/\Phi$ be a splitting field of $x^n - \alpha$ where $\alpha \in \Phi$. Then the Galois group $G$ of $P/\Phi$ is cyclic, and its order divides $n$.

More precisely, if $\rho$ is any root of $x^n - \alpha$ (so $P = \Phi(\rho)$) and $\zeta_1, \dots, \zeta_n$ are the $n$-th roots of unity in $\Phi$, then every $s \in G$ satisfies $\rho^s = \zeta_{i(s)}\rho$. The map $s \mapsto \zeta_{i(s)}$ is an isomorphism of $G$ onto a subgroup of the (cyclic) group of $n$-th roots of unity.
