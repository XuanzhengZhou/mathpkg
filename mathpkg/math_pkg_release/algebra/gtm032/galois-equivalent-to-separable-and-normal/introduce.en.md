This proposition unifies the various characterizations of Galois extensions. From earlier results: Galois extensions are splitting fields of separable polynomials, hence normal and separable. Conversely, if an extension is both normal and separable, it is a splitting field of separable polynomials (the minimum polynomials of basis elements), hence Galois. This clean equivalence is a cornerstone of Galois theory.
