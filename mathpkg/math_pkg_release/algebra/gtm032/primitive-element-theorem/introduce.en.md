The Primitive Element Theorem states that a finite separable extension can be generated by a single element (a primitive element). The proof constructs $\theta = \xi + \gamma\eta$ for a suitable $\gamma \in \Phi$, chosen to avoid finitely many "bad" values where the linear combination fails to separate the conjugates. Since $\Phi$ is infinite, such a $\gamma$ always exists. This theorem simplifies the study of separable extensions by reducing them to the form $\Phi(\theta) \cong \Phi[x]/(f(x))$ where $f$ is the minimal polynomial.
