Let $P$ be a splitting field over $\Phi$ of a separable polynomial, and let $G$ be the Galois group of $P/\Phi$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] There is a bijection between subgroups $H$ of $G$ and intermediate fields $E$ ($\Phi \subseteq E \subseteq P$) given by $H \mapsto I(H)$ (the $H$-invariants) and $E \mapsto A(E)$ (the automorphisms fixing $E$).
\item[(ii)] $|G| = [P:\Phi]$, and for any intermediate field $E$, $[P:E] = |A(E)|$ and $[E:\Phi] = (G : A(E))$.
\item[(iii)] $E/\Phi$ is Galois if and only if $A(E) \trianglelefteq G$, in which case the Galois group of $E/\Phi$ is isomorphic to $G/A(E)$.
\item[(iv)] The minimum polynomial over $\Phi$ of any $\rho \in P$ is $\prod_{i=1}^{r} (x - \rho^{s_i})$ where $\{\rho^{s_1}, \ldots, \rho^{s_r}\}$ is the orbit of $\rho$ under $G$.
\end{enumerate}
