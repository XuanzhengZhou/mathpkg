If an element is separable over a Galois intermediate extension, then it is already separable over the base field. The proof constructs the minimal polynomial of $\theta$ over $\Phi$ as the product of the distinct Galois conjugates of its minimal polynomial over $E$. Since each conjugate polynomial has distinct roots (by separability over $E$) and distinct conjugate polynomials are coprime, the resulting product has distinct roots, proving separability over $\Phi$. This lemma is crucial for proving that separability is transitive.
