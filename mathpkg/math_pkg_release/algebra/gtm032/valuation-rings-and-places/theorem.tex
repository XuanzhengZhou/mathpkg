A valuation ring of a field $K$ is a subring $\mathfrak{o}$ such that for every $x \in K^*$, either $x \in \mathfrak{o}$ or $x^{-1} \in \mathfrak{o}$. A place of $K$ is a homomorphism $\varphi: \mathfrak{o} \to L$ where $\mathfrak{o}$ is a valuation ring, $L$ is a field, and $\varphi$ is surjective. Valuation rings, valuations, and places are equivalent notions.
