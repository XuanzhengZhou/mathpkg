A valuation $v$ on a field $K$ is non-archimedean (i.e., satisfies the ultrametric inequality $v(a+b) \geq \min(v(a), v(b))$) if and only if $v(n \cdot 1) \geq 0$ for all integers $n$. For valuations of rank 1, the non-archimedean ones correspond precisely to those whose valuation ring contains a subring isomorphic to the integers.
