Hilbert's Satz 90 (Theorem 90 in Hilbert's Zahlbericht) states that in a cyclic extension, every element of norm 1 can be expressed as $\gamma/\gamma^g$ for some $\gamma$. This follows from Theorem 18 because for a cyclic group $G = \langle g \rangle$ of order $n$, a cocycle is determined by $\mu = \mu_g$, and the cocycle condition reduces to $N_{P|\Phi}(\mu) = 1$. The result is fundamental in class field theory and in the study of cyclic extensions, particularly for Kummer theory and the construction of abelian extensions.
