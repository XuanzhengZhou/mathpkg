The tensor product of two field extensions over a common base field is a fundamental construction. When the extensions are linearly disjoint, the tensor product is again a field — their free composite. More generally, the tensor product provides a way to extend scalars: a $\Phi$-vector space $M$ becomes a $P$-vector space $M_P = P \otimes_\Phi M$ with the same dimension. This construction is essential for studying base change, for decomposing normal extensions as tensor products of Galois and purely inseparable parts, and for the general theory of field composites.
