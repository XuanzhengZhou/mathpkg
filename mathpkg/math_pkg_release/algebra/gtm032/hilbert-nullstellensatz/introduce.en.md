The Hilbert Nullstellensatz: In an algebraically closed field, maximal ideals of polynomial rings correspond bijectively to points of affine space. The geometric formulation states that the vanishing set of an ideal is empty exactly when the ideal is the whole ring. The proof uses valuation theory to extend homomorphisms, showing the power of valuation-theoretic methods.
