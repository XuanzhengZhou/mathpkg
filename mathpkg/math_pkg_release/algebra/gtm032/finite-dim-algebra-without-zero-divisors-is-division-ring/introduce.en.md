A finite-dimensional algebra over a field that has no zero divisors must be a division ring. The proof uses the fact that for any non-zero element $a$, the left multiplication map $a_L$ is an injective linear transformation on a finite-dimensional vector space, hence surjective, giving a left inverse. A similar argument with right multiplication yields a two-sided inverse. This is a key structural result for finite-dimensional algebras.
