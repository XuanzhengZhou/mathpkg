The splitting field property behaves well under base change. If $P$ splits $f$ over $\Phi$, then it also splits $f$ over any intermediate field $\Sigma$. Conversely, if $P$ already contains some roots of $f$ and splits $f$ over the subfield generated by those roots, then $P$ is a splitting field over the original base field $\Phi$. These elementary facts are used repeatedly in inductive constructions and proofs about splitting fields.
