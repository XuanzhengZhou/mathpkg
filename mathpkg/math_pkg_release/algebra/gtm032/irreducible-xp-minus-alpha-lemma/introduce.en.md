In characteristic $p$, the polynomial $x^p - \alpha$ is either irreducible or a $p$-th power of a linear factor. The proof uses the factorization $(x - \beta)^p$ in the splitting field: any proper factor would have degree $k < p$ with coefficient $k\beta \in \Phi$, forcing $\beta \in \Phi$. This lemma is crucial for studying purely inseparable extensions.
