Theorem 7 is the fundamental isomorphism theorem for splitting fields. It establishes that any two splitting fields of the same polynomial are isomorphic via an isomorphism extending the identity on the base field, proving uniqueness up to isomorphism. More importantly, when the polynomial has distinct roots (the separable case), there are exactly $[P:\Phi]$ distinct isomorphisms from $P$ into any splitting field, achieving the theoretical maximum from Dedekind's corollary. This is proved by induction on the degree, lifting isomorphisms from a primitive element step by step through the tower of extensions.
