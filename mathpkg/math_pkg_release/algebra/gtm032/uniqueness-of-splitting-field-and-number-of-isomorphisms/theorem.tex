Let $\alpha \mapsto \bar{\alpha}$ be an isomorphism of a field $\Phi$ onto the field $\bar{\Phi}$ and let $f(x) \in \Phi[x]$ be a polynomial of positive degree with leading coefficient 1, and let $\bar{f}(x)$ be the corresponding polynomial in $\bar{\Phi}[x]$. Let $P$ and $\bar{P}$ be splitting fields over $\Phi$ and $\bar{\Phi}$ of $f(x)$ and $\bar{f}(x)$ respectively.

Then there exists an isomorphism of $P$ onto $\bar{P}$ which coincides with the given isomorphism on $\Phi$. Moreover, if $\bar{f}(x)$ is a product of distinct linear factors in $\bar{P}[x]$, then the number of extensions of the given isomorphism on $\Phi$ to an isomorphism of $P$ into $\bar{P}$ is exactly $[P:\Phi]$.
