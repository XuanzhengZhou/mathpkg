Let $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, \dots, x_r]$ be a commutative polynomial ring in indeterminates $x_i$ over a commutative ring $\mathfrak{A}$, and let $s$ be a homomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ into a commutative ring $\mathfrak{B}$. If $u_1, \dots, u_r$ are arbitrary elements of $\mathfrak{B}$, then there exists a unique homomorphism $S$ of $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, \dots, x_r]$ into $\mathfrak{B}$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $a^S = a^s$ for all $a \in \mathfrak{A}$,
\item $x_i^S = u_i$ for $i = 1, \dots, r$.
\end{enumerate}

More generally, if $t_1, \dots, t_r \in \mathfrak{C}$ (a commutative ring containing $\mathfrak{A}$) and $\mathfrak{R}$ is the ideal of polynomials $f$ with $f(t_1, \dots, t_r) = 0$, then $s$ extends to a homomorphism $S: \mathfrak{A}[t_1, \dots, t_r] \to \mathfrak{B}$ sending $t_i \mapsto u_i$ if and only if $f^s(u_1, \dots, u_r) = 0$ for all $f \in \mathfrak{R}$.
