Let $E_1/\Phi$ and $E_2/\Phi$ be subfields of a common extension. They are linearly disjoint over $\Phi$ if the natural map $E_1 \otimes_\Phi E_2 \to E_1 E_2$ is injective (hence an isomorphism). For finite extensions, this is equivalent to $[E_1 E_2 : \Phi] = [E_1 : \Phi][E_2 : \Phi]$. Linear disjointness is related to separating transcendence bases.
