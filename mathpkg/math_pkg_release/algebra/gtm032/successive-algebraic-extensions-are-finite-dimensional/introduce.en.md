If a field extension is obtained by successively adjoining finitely many algebraic elements, the resulting extension is finite-dimensional, and the ring generated by these elements already equals the field generated by them. The proof uses induction and the dimensionality relation $[P:\Phi] = [P:\Phi(\rho_1,\dots,\rho_{m-1})] \cdots [\Phi(\rho_1):\Phi]$, together with the fact that a finite-dimensional integral domain over a field is itself a field. This lemma provides the converse to the elementary fact that every finite-dimensional extension is finitely generated by algebraic elements.
