The additive analogue of Hilbert's Satz 90 states that in a cyclic extension, every element of trace zero can be written as $\gamma - \gamma^g$. This follows from the triviality of the additive first cohomology group $H^1(G, P) = 0$, which is proved similarly to the multiplicative case but using the trace instead of the norm. Together with the multiplicative version, this forms the foundation for understanding the additive and multiplicative Galois cohomology of cyclic extensions, essential for Artin-Schreier theory and Kummer theory respectively.
