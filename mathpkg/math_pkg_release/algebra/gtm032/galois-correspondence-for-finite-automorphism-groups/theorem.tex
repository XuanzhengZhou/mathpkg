Let $P$ be a field, $\mathcal{A}$ the collection of finite groups of automorphisms in $P$, and $\mathcal{I}$ the collection of subfields of $P$ which are Galois and of finite co-dimension in $P$. Then the maps $G \mapsto I(G)$ and $\Phi \mapsto A(\Phi)$ are inverses, establishing a bijection between $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{I}$.

Moreover, if $G \in \mathcal{A}$ and $\Phi = I(G)$, then:
(i) $[P:\Phi] = (G:1)$.
(ii) $G = A(\Phi)$.
(iii) For any subgroup $H$ of $G$, $I(H)$ is a subfield of $P$ containing $\Phi$, and the map $H \mapsto I(H)$ is a bijection from the set of subgroups of $G$ onto the set of subfields of $P$ containing $\Phi$. Under this correspondence, $[P:I(H)] = (H:1)$ and $[I(H):\Phi] = (G:H)$.
(iv) $H$ is invariant in $G$ if and only if $E = I(H)$ is Galois over $\Phi$, and then $A_E(\Phi) \cong G/H$.
