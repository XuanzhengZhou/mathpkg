Let $\Phi$ be a field whose characteristic does not divide $n$ (characteristic 0 included). Let $P/\Phi$ be a splitting field of $x^n - 1$ (an $n$-th cyclotomic extension). Then the Galois group $G$ of $P/\Phi$ is isomorphic to a subgroup of the multiplicative group $U(n)$ of units in $\mathbb{Z}/(n)$.

In particular, $G$ is commutative. If $n = \ell$ is a prime, then $G$ is cyclic (since $U(\ell)$ is cyclic of order $\ell - 1$).
