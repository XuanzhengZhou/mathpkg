The Artin-Schreier theorem characterizes orderable fields as those in which $-1$ is not a sum of squares. It further characterizes real closed fields by two conditions: existence of square roots of positive elements, and the intermediate value property for odd-degree polynomials. This theorem is fundamental to real algebra and the solution of Hilbert's 17th problem on sums of squares.
