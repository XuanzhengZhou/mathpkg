For any two fields $E$ and $P$, the set of all homomorphisms from the additive group of $E$ to the additive group of $P$ forms an additive group $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ under pointwise addition. This group also supports a right multiplication by elements of $P$ via composition with the right multiplication map $\rho_R$ in $P$, giving $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ the structure of a right vector space over $P$. Additionally, if $B$ is a homomorphism from $P$ to a third field $\Delta$, then post-composition $A \mapsto AB$ gives a multiplication operation satisfying both distributive laws and associativity.
