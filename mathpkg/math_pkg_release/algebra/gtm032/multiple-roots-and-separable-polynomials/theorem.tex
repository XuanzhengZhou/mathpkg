Let $f(x) \in \Phi[x]$ be a polynomial of positive degree and let $P/\Phi$ be a splitting field. Write
$$f(x) = (x - \rho_1)^{k_1}(x - \rho_2)^{k_2} \cdots (x - \rho_r)^{k_r},$$
$\rho_i \in P$, $\rho_i \neq \rho_j$ for $i \neq j$. Then $\rho_i$ is a root of multiplicity $k_i$ of $f(x) = 0$. If $k_i = 1$, $\rho_i$ is a simple root; otherwise it is a multiple root.

The formal derivative $f'$ in $\Phi[x]$ is the linear map defined by $(x^i)' = i x^{i-1}$. It satisfies $(fg)' = f'g + fg'$.

Theorem 8: $f(x)$ has a multiple root (in a splitting field) if and only if $(f, f') \neq 1$, i.e., $f$ and $f'$ have a common factor of positive degree.
