The additive group $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ of homomorphisms between the additive groups of two fields can be given the structure of a right vector space over the codomain field $P$. Scalar multiplication is defined by post-composition with the right multiplication map in $P$: for $A \in \mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ and $\rho \in P$, the map $A\rho$ sends $\epsilon \in E$ to $(\epsilon A)\rho$. This enriches the algebraic structure of $\mathfrak{L}(E, P)$ and prepares for the study of linear transformations between field extensions over a common subfield.
