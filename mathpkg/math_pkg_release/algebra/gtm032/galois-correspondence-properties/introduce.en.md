The maps $I$ (invariants) and $A$ (automorphism group) between subgroups of $\operatorname{Aut}(P)$ and subfields of $P$ form a Galois connection: they are order-reversing and satisfy $G \subseteq A(I(G))$ and $\Phi \subseteq I(A(\Phi))$. The identities $I(A(I(G))) = I(G)$ and $A(I(A(\Phi))) = A(\Phi)$ imply that these maps restrict to a bijection between closed subgroups and closed subfields. A subfield is Galois precisely when it is closed, i.e., $\Phi = I(A(\Phi))$.
