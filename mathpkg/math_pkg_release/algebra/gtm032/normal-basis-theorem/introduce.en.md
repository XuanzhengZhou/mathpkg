The Normal Basis Theorem asserts that every finite Galois extension admits a basis consisting of the Galois conjugates of a single element. In module-theoretic language, this says $P \cong \Phi(G)$ as $\Phi(G)$-modules, where $\Phi(G)$ is the group algebra. The proof for infinite base fields uses algebraic independence of isomorphisms and a non-vanishing determinant argument. For finite fields (cyclic extensions), a constructive proof using the generator of the Galois group and the theory of linear transformations provides the normal basis. Normal bases are important in Galois cohomology and algebraic number theory.
