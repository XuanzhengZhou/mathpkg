The right $P$-vector space $\mathfrak{A}$ spanned by a finite group $G$ of automorphisms of $P$ satisfies all three conditions of the Jacobson-Bourbaki theorem. The dimension of $\mathfrak{A}$ over $P$ equals the order of $G$, and the associated subfield $\Phi$ (elements commuting with all operators in $\mathfrak{A}$) coincides with the fixed field of $G$. This lemma provides the bridge between the abstract Jacobson-Bourbaki correspondence and classical Galois theory for finite automorphism groups.
