Let $G$ be a finite group of automorphisms in the field $P$ and let

$$\mathfrak{A} = \left\{ \sum_{i=1}^{n} s_i \rho_i \mid s_i \in G, \rho_i \in P \right\}.$$

Then $\mathfrak{A}$ satisfies the hypotheses (i), (ii), (iii) of Theorem 2 (Jacobson-Bourbaki):
(i) $\mathfrak{A}$ is a subring of $\mathfrak{L}(P, P)$ containing the identity;
(ii) $\mathfrak{A}$ is a subspace of $\mathfrak{L}(P, P)$ as right vector space over $P$;
(iii) $[\mathfrak{A}:P]_R = (G:1)$, the order of $G$.

Moreover, the subfield $\Phi$ given by Theorem 2 (i.e., $\Phi = \{\alpha \in P \mid \alpha_R A = A \alpha_R, \forall A \in \mathfrak{A}\}$) is precisely the subfield of $G$-invariants.
