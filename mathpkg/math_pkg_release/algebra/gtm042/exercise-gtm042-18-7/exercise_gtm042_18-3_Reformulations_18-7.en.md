Let $m$ be the l.c.m. of orders of the elements of $G$. Write $m$ in the form $p^n m'$ with $(p, m') = 1$ (cf. 18.1.) and choose an integer $q$ such that $q \equiv 0 \pmod{p^n}$ and $q \equiv 1 \pmod{m'}$.

**(a)** Show that, if $s \in G$, the $p'$-component $s'$ of $s$ is equal to $s^q$.

**(b)** Let $f$ be a modular character of $G$, and let $\phi$ be an element of $R_K(G)$ whose restriction to $G_{\text{reg}}$ is equal to $f$. Show that $f' = \Psi^q \phi$, where $\Psi^q$ is the Adams operation on $R_K(G)$ defined by $(\Psi^q \phi)(s) = \phi(s^q)$.
