For each positive integer $h$, the representation $\rho^{h}$ is a degree-2 irreducible representation of the infinite dihedral group $D_{\infty}$. It is constructed by inducing the character $e^{i h \alpha}$ from the rotation subgroup $C_{\infty}$ up to $D_{\infty}$. On rotations, $\rho^{h}$ acts diagonally with eigenvalues $e^{\pm i h \alpha}$, while on reflections it swaps the two basis vectors with appropriate phase factors. These representations are the infinite analogues of the degree-2 irreducible representations of the finite dihedral groups $D_n$.
