The infinite dihedral group $D_{\infty}$ is the continuous analogue of the finite dihedral groups $D_n$. It consists of all rotations $r_{\alpha}$ about the origin (forming the circle group $C_{\infty}$) together with all reflections $s r_{\alpha}$ through lines through the origin. The fundamental relation $s r_{\alpha} s = r_{-\alpha}$ mirrors the dihedral relation in the finite case. As a topological space, $D_{\infty}$ is the disjoint union of two circles, reflecting the two cosets of the rotation subgroup.
