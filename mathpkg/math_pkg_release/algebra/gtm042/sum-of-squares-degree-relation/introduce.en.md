Corollary 2 gives two fundamental numerical identities. Part (a) states that the sum of the squares of the irreducible degrees equals the group order -- a powerful constraint that often helps determine irreducible representations. Part (b) is a column orthogonality relation, showing that the irreducible characters weighted by their degrees sum to zero on non-identity elements.
