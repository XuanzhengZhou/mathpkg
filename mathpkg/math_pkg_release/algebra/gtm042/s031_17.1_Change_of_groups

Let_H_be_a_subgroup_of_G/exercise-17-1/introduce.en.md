This exercise asks to extend the definitions of the restriction homomorphism $\text{Res}_H^G$ and induction homomorphism $\text{Ind}_H^G$ from the case where $H$ is a subgroup of $G$ to the more general case of a group homomorphism $f: H \to G$ whose kernel has order prime to the characteristic $p$. The hint refers to Exercise 7.1, which likely establishes that such a homomorphism induces well-defined maps on Grothendieck groups via the isomorphism between representations of $H$ and representations of the image $f(H)$ (since the kernel acts trivially in characteristic $p$).
