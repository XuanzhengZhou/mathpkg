Let $H$ be a subgroup of $G$. By restriction, every $k[G]$-module defines a $k[H]$-module, which is projective if the given module is projective. Passing to Grothendieck groups, we obtain homomorphisms

$$\text{Res}_H^G: R_k(G) \rightarrow R_k(H) \quad \text{and} \quad \text{Res}_H^G: P_k(G) \rightarrow P_k(H).$$
