The scalar product on complex-valued functions on a finite group is the fundamental analytic tool in character theory. It allows one to treat the space of class functions as an inner product space, turning qualitative statements about irreducible representations into quantitative orthogonality relations. This product is normalized by the group order so that irreducible characters have norm one, a property that underpins the entire structural theory of representations.
