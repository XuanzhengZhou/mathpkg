Theorem 6 is the central structural result of character theory. Not only are irreducible characters orthonormal (Theorem 3), but they actually span the entire space of class functions. This completeness has far-reaching consequences: it implies that any class function can be uniquely expressed as a linear combination of irreducible characters, and in particular that the number of irreducible representations equals the number of conjugacy classes.
