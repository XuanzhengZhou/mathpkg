Proposition 7 gives the "column orthogonality" relations for the character table. While Theorem 3 gives orthogonality of rows (irreducible characters as functions), Proposition 7 gives orthogonality of columns (conjugacy classes). These relations are dual to Theorem 3 and are essential for computing character tables: they constrain the possible character values and provide consistency checks. For the identity element, they recover the degree-sum relation $\sum n_i^2 = g$.
