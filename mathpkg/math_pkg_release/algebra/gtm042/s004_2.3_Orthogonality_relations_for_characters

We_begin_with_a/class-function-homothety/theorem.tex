Let $f$ be a class function on a finite group $G$, and let $\rho: G \to \operatorname{GL}(V)$ be a linear representation of $G$. Define the linear mapping $\rho_f: V \to V$ by
$$\rho_f = \sum_{t \in G} f(t)\rho_t.$$
If $V$ is irreducible of degree $n$ and character $\chi$, then $\rho_f$ is a homothety (scalar multiple of the identity) with ratio $\lambda$ given by
$$\lambda = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{t \in G} f(t)\chi(t) = \frac{g}{n}(f|\chi^*),$$
where $g$ is the order of $G$ and $\chi^*(t) = \chi(t)^*$ is the complex conjugate character.
