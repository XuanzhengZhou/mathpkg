Proposition 46 shows that irreducible representations of defect zero (those whose dimension is divisible by the full $p$-part of $|G|$) yield projective $A[G]$-module lattices. Specifically, if $E$ is a simple $K[G]$-module of dimension $N$ divisible by $p^n$ (the highest power of $p$ dividing $|G|$), then any $G$-stable lattice $P$ in $E$ is projective over $A[G]$. Moreover, the natural map $A[G] \to \operatorname{End}_A(P)$ is surjective with a direct factor kernel, and the reduction $\bar{P}$ is a simple projective $k[G]$-module. The Steinberg representation of a semisimple linear group over a finite field of characteristic $p$ provides a key example.
