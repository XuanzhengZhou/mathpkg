Lemma 22 of Serre's "Linear Representations of Finite Groups" establishes a hereditary property of the plus-part $P_A^+(G)$ inside the projective class group $P_A(G)$. If a positive integer multiple $nx$ of an element $x$ belongs to the semigroup $P_A^+(G)$ of classes of actual projective modules, then $x$ itself already belongs to $P_A^+(G)$. The proof is elementary: identifying $P_A(G)$ with $\mathbb{Z}^r$ and $P_A^+(G)$ with $\mathbb{N}^r$ (where $r$ is the number of isomorphism classes of simple $k[G]$-modules), the statement reduces to the obvious fact that if $n x_i \geq 0$ for all coordinates then $x_i \geq 0$.
