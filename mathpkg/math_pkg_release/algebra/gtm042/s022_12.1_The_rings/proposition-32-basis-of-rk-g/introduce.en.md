Proposition 32 generalizes the orthogonality relations of complex characters to an arbitrary field $K$ of characteristic zero. The irreducible $K$-characters $\chi_i$ form a $\mathbb{Z}$-basis of $R_K(G)$ and are pairwise orthogonal. The self-inner-product $\langle \chi_i, \chi_i \rangle = \dim_K \operatorname{End}^G(V_i)$ is an integer $\geq 1$, equal to $1$ precisely when $V_i$ is absolutely irreducible.
