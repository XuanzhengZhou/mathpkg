Proposition 33 gives a clean character-theoretic criterion: a complex representation of a finite group $G$ can be realized over a subfield $K$ (i.e., by matrices with entries in $K$) precisely when its character takes values in $K$. This is the fundamental link between the arithmetic of character values and the field of definition of a representation.
