Theorem 3 is the cornerstone of character theory. It states that the irreducible characters form an orthonormal set with respect to the Hermitian inner product $(\phi|\psi) = \frac{1}{g} \sum \phi(t)\psi(t)^*$. This orthogonality makes it possible to decompose any representation by computing inner products, and ultimately to classify all representations of a finite group.
