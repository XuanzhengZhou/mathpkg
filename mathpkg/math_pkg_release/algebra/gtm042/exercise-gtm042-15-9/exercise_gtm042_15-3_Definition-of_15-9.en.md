Suppose that $g$ is prime to $p$. Let $E$ be a free $A$-module.

(a) Let $n \geqslant 1$ be an integer, and let

$$\rho_n: G \rightarrow \mathbf{GL}(E/m^n E)$$

be a homomorphism of $G$ into the group of automorphisms of $E/m^n E$. Show that $\rho_n$ can be lifted to

$$\rho_{n+1}: G \rightarrow \mathbf{GL}(E/m^{n+1} E)$$

and that this lifting is unique, up to conjugation by an automorphism of $E/m^{n+1} E$ congruent to 1 mod $m^n$. [Use the fact that the cohomology groups of dimension 1 and 2 of $G$ with values in $\text{End}(E/mE)$ are zero.]

(b) Obtain from (a) the fact that every linear representation

$$\rho_1: G \rightarrow \mathbf{GL}(E/mE)$$

of $G$ over $k$ can be lifted, in an essentially unique way, to a representation of $G$ over $A$.
