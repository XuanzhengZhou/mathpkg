\textbf{Exercise 7.1 (Generalization of the concept of induced representation).} Let $\alpha: H \to G$ be a homomorphism of groups (not necessarily injective), and let $\tilde{\alpha}: \mathbb{C}[H] \to \mathbb{C}[G]$ be the corresponding algebra homomorphism. If $E$ is a $\mathbb{C}[G]$-module, we denote by $\operatorname{Res}_{\alpha} E$ the $\mathbb{C}[H]$-module obtained from $E$ by means of $\tilde{\alpha}$; if $\varphi$ is the character of $E$, that of $\operatorname{Res}_{\alpha} E$ is $\operatorname{Res}_{\alpha} \varphi = \varphi \circ \alpha$.
