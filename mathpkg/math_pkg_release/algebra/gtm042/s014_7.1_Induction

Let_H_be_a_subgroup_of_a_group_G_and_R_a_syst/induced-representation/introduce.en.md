An induced representation is a way of constructing a representation of a group $G$ from a representation of a subgroup $H$. If $V$ is a $\mathbb{C}[G]$-module and $W \subseteq V$ is a sub-$\mathbb{C}[H]$-module, then $V$ is induced by $W$ if $V$ decomposes as the direct sum of the translates $sW$ of $W$ by a system $R$ of left coset representatives of $H$ in $G$. This notion, developed extensively by Frobenius and Mackey, is fundamental to the representation theory of finite groups, as it allows one to build representations of larger groups from those of subgroups. The condition is independent of the choice of coset representatives $R$, making it a well-defined intrinsic property.
