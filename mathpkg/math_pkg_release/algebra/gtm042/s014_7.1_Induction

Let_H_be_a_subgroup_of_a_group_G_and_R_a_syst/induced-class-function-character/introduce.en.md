Proposition 20 establishes the fundamental relationship between induction of class functions and induction of representations. Part (i) ensures that the induced class function construction preserves the property of being a class function (i.e., being constant on conjugacy classes). Part (ii) confirms that the character of an induced representation is precisely the induced class function of the original character. This result is essential for applying character-theoretic computations to induced representations.
