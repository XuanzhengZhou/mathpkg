Indeed, when $H \trianglelefteq G$, we have $H_s = sHs^{-1} \cap H = H \cap H = H$ for all $s \in G$. Thus $\operatorname{Res}_s(\rho) = \rho$ (since the restriction is taken to the whole group $H$), and condition (b) of Proposition 23 reduces to: for each $s \notin H$, the representation $\rho$ is disjoint from $\rho^s$. Since both are representations of the same group $H$, disjointness means they have no common irreducible component. In particular, if $\rho$ is irreducible, disjointness is equivalent to $\rho \not\cong \rho^s$.
