When the subgroup $H$ is normal in $G$, Mackey's Irreducibility Criterion simplifies considerably. In this case, $H_s = H$ for all $s \in G$, and the condition about disjointness of $\operatorname{Res}_s(\rho)$ and $\rho^s$ reduces to checking that $\rho$ is not isomorphic to any of its $G$-conjugates $\rho^s$ for $s \notin H$. This corollary is the starting point for Clifford Theory, which describes the structure of representations of a group in terms of those of a normal subgroup.
