The theorem follows from Remark 2 in Section 7.1 (or from Lemma 1 of Section 3.3, which amounts to the same thing). Specifically, one has the canonical isomorphism

$$\operatorname{Hom}^H(W, \operatorname{Res} E) \cong \operatorname{Hom}^G(\operatorname{Ind} W, E)$$

for any $\mathbb{C}[H]$-module $W$ and $\mathbb{C}[G]$-module $E$. Taking dimensions and applying Lemma 2 (which identifies $\dim \operatorname{Hom}$ with the inner product of characters) yields the reciprocity formula. It is also possible to prove Theorem 13 by direct calculation using the explicit formula for the induced character.

The supplementary formula $\operatorname{Ind}(\psi \cdot \operatorname{Res} \varphi) = (\operatorname{Ind} \psi) \cdot \varphi$ can be checked by a simple calculation, or deduced from the module-theoretic formula $\operatorname{Ind}(W) \otimes E \cong \operatorname{Ind}(W \otimes \operatorname{Res} E)$, cf. Section 3.3, Example 5.
