Frobenius Reciprocity is the cornerstone theorem relating induction and restriction of representations. It states that induction and restriction are adjoint functors with respect to the natural inner product on class functions. In concrete terms, the inner product of an induced character with any character of the larger group equals the inner product of the original character with the restricted character. This reciprocity law, together with its corollaries, provides the primary computational tool for decomposing induced representations and analyzing their irreducible constituents.
