Let $H$ be a Sylow $p$-subgroup of $G$. Show that, if $E$ is a projective $k[G]$-module, then $E$ is a free $k[H]$-module (cf. 15.6), and so $\dim E$ is divisible by $p^n$. Conclude that the map $[E] \mapsto \dim E$ defines, by passing to quotients, a surjective homomorphism $\operatorname{Coker}(c) \to \mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z}$. In particular, the element $p^{n-1}$ of $R_k(G)$ does not belong to the image of $c$.
