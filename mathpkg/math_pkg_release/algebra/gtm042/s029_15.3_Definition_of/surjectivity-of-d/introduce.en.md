Theorem 33 is a fundamental result in modular representation theory, asserting that the decomposition homomorphism $d: R_K(G) \to R_k(G)$ is surjective. This means every virtual representation of $G$ over the field $k$ of characteristic $p$ can be lifted to a virtual representation over the characteristic-zero field $K$. This result is extremely useful because it allows one to study modular representations by lifting them to characteristic zero, where character theory is available. The theorem was originally stated only for sufficiently large $K$, but was later observed by Claude Chevalley and Andreas Dress to be valid in general.
