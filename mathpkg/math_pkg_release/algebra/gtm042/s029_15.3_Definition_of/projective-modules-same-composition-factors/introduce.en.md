Corollary 2 is a direct restatement of the injectivity of the Cartan map $c$ in concrete module-theoretic language. It says that the class of a projective $k[G]$-module in the Grothendieck group $R_k(G)$ (which records its composition factors with multiplicities) uniquely determines the module up to isomorphism. In other words, two projective $k[G]$-modules with the same multiset of composition factors must be isomorphic.
