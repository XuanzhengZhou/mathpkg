Assume that the order of $G$ is prime to $p$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Each $k[G]$-module (resp. each $A$-free $A[G]$-module) is projective.
\item[(ii)] The operation of reduction mod $\mathfrak{m}$ defines a bijection from $S_K$ onto $S_k$.
\item[(iii)] If we identify $S_K$ with $S_k$ as in (ii), the matrices $C, D, E$ are all identity matrices.
\end{enumerate}

(More briefly: the representation theory of the group $G$ is "the same" over $k$ as over $K$.)
