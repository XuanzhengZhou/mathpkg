7.4: Mackey's irreducibility criterion

We know that $V$ is the direct sum of the images $xW$, for $x \in G/H$. Let $s \in S$ and let $V(s)$ be the subspace of $V$ generated by the images $xW$, for $x \in KsH$; the space $V$ is a direct sum of the $V(s)$, and it is clear that $V(s)$ is stable under $K$. It remains to prove that $V(s)$ is $K$-isomorphic to $\text{Ind}_{\text{H}_s}^{\text{K}}(W_s)$. But the subgroup of $K$ consisting of the elements $x$ such that $x(sW) = sW$ is evidently equal to $\text{H}_s$, and $V(s)$ is a direct sum of the images $x(sW)$, $x \in K/H_s$. Therefore $V(s) = \text{Ind}_{\text{H}_s}^{\text{K}}(sW)$. Now it remains to check that $sW$ is $\text{H}_s$-isomorphic to $W_s$, and this is immediate: the isomorphism is given by $s: W_s \rightarrow sW$.

Remark. Since $V(s)$ depends only on the image of $s$ in $K\backslash G/H$, we also see that the representation $\text{Ind}_{\text{H}_s}^{\text{K}}(W_s)$ depends (up to isomorphism) only on the double coset of $s$.

7.4 Mackey's irreducibility criterion

We apply the preceding results to the case $K = H$. For $s \in G$, we still denote by $\text{H}_s$ the subgroup $sHs^{-1} \cap H$ of $H$; the representation $\rho$ of $H$ defines a representation $\text{Res}_s(\rho)$ by restriction to $\text{H}_s$, which should not be confused with the representation $\rho^s$ defined in 7.3.
