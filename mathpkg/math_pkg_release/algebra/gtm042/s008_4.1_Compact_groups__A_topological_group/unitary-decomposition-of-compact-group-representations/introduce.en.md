This proposition, related to the Peter--Weyl theorem, states that every Hilbert space representation of a compact group decomposes as a Hilbert direct sum of finite-dimensional unitary representations. This fundamental result implies that the study of representations of compact groups can be reduced to the finite-dimensional unitary case. Consequently, most properties of representations of finite groups carry over to compact groups with the Haar measure replacing the finite averaging operation.
