A topological group is a group equipped with a topology that makes the group operations continuous. Specifically, both the multiplication map $(s, t) \mapsto s \cdot t$ and the inversion map $s \mapsto s^{-1}$ are required to be continuous functions. This structure combines algebraic and topological properties, allowing one to study groups using tools from topology. Topological groups form the natural setting for studying representations of compact groups, where the continuity of representations is defined with respect to the group's topology.
