Let $G$ be a compact group. There exists a unique measure $dt$ carried by $G$ satisfying:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $\displaystyle\int_G f(t)\,dt = \int_G f(ts)\,dt$ for each continuous function $f$ and each $s \in G$ (invariance of $dt$ under right translation).

\item[(ii)] $\displaystyle\int_G dt = 1$ (the total mass of $dt$ is equal to $1$).
\end{itemize}

Moreover, $dt$ is also invariant under left translation:
\[
\text{(i')}\qquad \int_G f(t)\,dt = \int_G f(st)\,dt.
\]
