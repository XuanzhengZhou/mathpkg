The proof carries over without change from the finite group case (cf. Theorem 10 of Serre's text). The result follows from the uniqueness of the Haar measure: the product measure $ds_1 \, ds_2$ on $G_1 \times G_2$ is readily verified to satisfy the defining properties (i) right-invariance and (ii) total mass $1$, so by the uniqueness of the invariant measure, it must coincide with the Haar measure of the product group.
