This proposition states that the Haar measure on a product $G_1 \times G_2$ of two compact groups is simply the product measure $ds_1 \, ds_2$ of the individual Haar measures on $G_1$ and $G_2$. This result is used in the study of representations of product groups, where the representation theory of $G_1 \times G_2$ is related to those of $G_1$ and $G_2$ individually. The proof carries over without change from the finite group case.
