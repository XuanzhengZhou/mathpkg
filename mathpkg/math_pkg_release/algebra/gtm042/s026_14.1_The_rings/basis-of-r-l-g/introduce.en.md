Proposition 40 establishes that the representation ring $R_L(G)$ is a free abelian group whose canonical basis consists of the classes of simple $L[G]$-modules. This structural result is fundamental: every element of $R_L(G)$ can be uniquely expressed as an integer linear combination of simple module classes, and the positive cone $R_L^+(G)$ corresponds precisely to combinations with non-negative coefficients. More generally, the same argument applies to the category of modules of finite length over an arbitrary ring.
