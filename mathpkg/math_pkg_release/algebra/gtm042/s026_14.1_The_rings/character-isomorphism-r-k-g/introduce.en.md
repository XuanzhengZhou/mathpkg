When the ground field $K$ has characteristic zero, every $K[G]$-module has a well-defined character, and the character map is additive on direct sums. This additivity allows the character map to be extended linearly from $R_K^+(G)$ to the whole Grothendieck group $R_K(G)$, yielding an isomorphism onto the group of virtual characters (integer linear combinations of irreducible characters). This justifies the common identification of elements of $R_K(G)$ with virtual characters. In contrast, the analogous result for the modular case (residue field $k$ of characteristic $p$) requires Brauer's modular characters and is treated in Ch. 18.
