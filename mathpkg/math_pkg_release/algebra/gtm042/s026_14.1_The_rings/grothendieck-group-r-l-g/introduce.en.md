The Grothendieck group $R_L(G)$ encodes representations of a finite group $G$ over a field $L$ into a ring structure. It is constructed from the category of finitely generated modules over the group algebra $L[G]$, with addition given by direct sum and multiplication by the external tensor product over $L$. The subset $R_L^+(G)$ consists of classes of actual modules (as opposed to virtual differences), and $S_L$ indexes the isomorphism classes of simple $L[G]$-modules. When $L = K$ has characteristic zero, the character map identifies $R_K(G)$ with the ring of virtual characters.
