Theorem 2 establishes complete reducibility: every finite-dimensional representation of a finite group over $\mathbb{C}$ decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible representations. The proof proceeds by induction on dimension, using Theorem 1 to split off irreducible summands. This is the fundamental structure theorem of representation theory.
