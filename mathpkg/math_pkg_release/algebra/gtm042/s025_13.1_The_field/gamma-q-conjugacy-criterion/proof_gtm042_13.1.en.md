Follows directly from Gauss's theorem ($\Gamma_Q = (\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z})^*$) and the discussion of $\Gamma_K$-conjugacy in Section 12.4. By Gauss's theorem, the action of $\Gamma_Q$ on the $m$th roots of unity is the full Galois group, so two elements are $\Gamma_Q$-conjugate precisely when their cyclic subgroups are conjugate.
