This corollary follows from Gauss's theorem on the Galois group of cyclotomic extensions. It characterizes $\Gamma_Q$-conjugacy of group elements purely in terms of the internal group structure: two elements are $\Gamma_Q$-conjugate precisely when the cyclic subgroups they generate are conjugate in $G$. This criterion is fundamental for analyzing which class functions are rational-valued, as $\Gamma_Q$-conjugacy determines the action of the Galois group on character values.
