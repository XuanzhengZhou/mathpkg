This corollary of Theorem 29 provides a combinatorial formula for the number of irreducible representations of a finite group $G$ over the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$. Rather than counting conjugacy classes of group elements (which gives the number over $\mathbb{C}$), one instead counts conjugacy classes of cyclic subgroups. This reflects the fact that rationality forces characters to be constant on generators of the same cyclic subgroup, collapsing the usual conjugacy class count.
