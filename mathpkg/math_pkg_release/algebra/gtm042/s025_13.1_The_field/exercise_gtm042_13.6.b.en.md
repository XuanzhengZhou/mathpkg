Show that $X$ (resp. $Y$) can be identified with the set of homomorphisms from the $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra $\operatorname{Cent} \mathbb{Q}[G]$ (resp. $\mathbb{Q} \otimes R(G)$) into $\mathbb{Q}(m)$. Deduce that the $\Gamma_Q$-sets $X$ and $Y$ are isomorphic if and only if the center of $\mathbb{Q}[G]$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{Q} \otimes R(G)$.
