Theorem 30 provides a powerful vanishing criterion: if a $\mathbb{Q}$-valued class function $\theta$ on a finite group $G$ sums to zero over every cyclic subgroup, then $\theta$ must be identically zero. The proof proceeds by induction on the group order, using $\Gamma_Q$-conjugacy to equate values on generators of the same cyclic subgroup and the induction hypothesis to handle elements generating proper subgroups. This result is the key tool for distinguishing representations over $\mathbb{Q}$ by their restrictions to cyclic subgroups.
