Gauss's theorem determines the Galois group of the $m$-th cyclotomic extension over the rational numbers. It states that adjoining all $m$-th roots of unity to $\mathbb{Q}$ yields a Galois extension whose Galois group is isomorphic to the full multiplicative group of units modulo $m$, i.e., $(\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z})^*$. This result is equivalent to the irreducibility of the $m$-th cyclotomic polynomial $\Phi_m$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, a fundamental fact in algebraic number theory that underpins the analysis of rational representations of finite groups.
