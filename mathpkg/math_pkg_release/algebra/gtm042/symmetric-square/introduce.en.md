The symmetric square $\text{Sym}^2(V)$ is the subspace of $V \otimes V$ consisting of symmetric tensors (those fixed by swapping factors). It is a subrepresentation of the tensor square, with dimension $n(n+1)/2$. Together with the alternating square, it gives the decomposition $V \otimes V = \text{Sym}^2(V) \oplus \text{Alt}^2(V)$.
