This corollary provides a useful upper bound on the degrees of irreducible representations. By restricting an irreducible representation $\rho$ of $G$ to an abelian subgroup $A$, one obtains one-dimensional subrepresentations. The translates of such a line generate $V$, and there are at most $g/a$ distinct translates, yielding $\dim V \leq [G:A]$. For example, dihedral groups contain a cyclic subgroup of index 2, so their irreducibles have degree at most 2.
