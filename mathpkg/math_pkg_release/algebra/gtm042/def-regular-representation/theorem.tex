Let $G$ be a finite group. Let $V$ be a vector space of dimension $g = |G|$ with a basis $(e_t)_{t \in G}$ indexed by the elements of $G$.
For $s \in G$, let $\rho_s$ be the linear map of $V$ into $V$ which sends $e_t$ to $e_{st}$.
This defines a linear representation called the regular representation of $G$. Its degree is equal to the order of $G$.
