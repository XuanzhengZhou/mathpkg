There are finitely many prime ideals $\mathfrak{p}_1, \ldots, \mathfrak{p}_h$ of $A$ containing $p$. The quotients $A/\mathfrak{p}_i$ are finite fields of characteristic $p$, and there exists an integer $N$ such that $pA \supset (\mathfrak{p}_1 \cap \cdots \cap \mathfrak{p}_h)^N$.

The $\mathfrak{p}_i$ correspond to prime ideals of $A/pA$, which is a finite ring of characteristic $p$. The first two assertions follow from this. The third follows from the fact that $(\mathfrak{p}_1 \cap \cdots \cap \mathfrak{p}_h)/pA$ is the radical of the artinian ring $A/pA$, thus is nilpotent.
