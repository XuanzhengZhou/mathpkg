A linear representation is a way to realize an abstract finite group $G$ as a group of linear transformations (invertible matrices) acting on a complex vector space. It associates to each group element $s$ an invertible linear map $\rho(s)$ on $V$, respecting the group structure via $\rho(st) = \rho(s)\rho(t)$. This fundamental notion, due to Frobenius, connects group theory with linear algebra and is the central object of study in representation theory.
