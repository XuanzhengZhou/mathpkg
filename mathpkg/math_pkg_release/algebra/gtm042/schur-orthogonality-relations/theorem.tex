Let $\rho^1: G \to \text{GL}(V_1)$ and $\rho^2: G \to \text{GL}(V_2)$ be two irreducible representations given in matrix form by $r_{i_1 j_1}(t)$ and $r_{i_2 j_2}(t)$.

(1) If $\rho^1$ and $\rho^2$ are not isomorphic, then for all $i_1, i_2, j_1, j_2$:

$$\frac{1}{g} \sum_{t \in G} r_{i_2 j_2}(t^{-1}) r_{j_1 i_1}(t) = 0.$$

(2) If $\rho^1 = \rho^2$, then:

$$\frac{1}{g} \sum_{t \in G} r_{i j}(t^{-1}) r_{k \ell}(t) = \frac{1}{n} \delta_{i \ell} \delta_{j k}$$

where $n = \dim V_1$ and $\delta$ is the Kronecker symbol.
