Let $\varphi = f/g$. In the notation of Lemma 6, we have
$$f = \sum_C \operatorname{Ind}_C^G(\theta_C \cdot \operatorname{Res}_C^G \varphi),$$
and it remains only to show that $\varphi_C = \theta_C \cdot \operatorname{Res}_C^G \varphi$ belongs to $A \otimes R_K(C)$.

We know that $f$ is constant on $\Gamma_K$-classes (Corollary 1 of Theorem 25). So we can assume that $x$ is the $p'$-component of $y$, in which case the same argument applies as in the proof of Lemma 7.
