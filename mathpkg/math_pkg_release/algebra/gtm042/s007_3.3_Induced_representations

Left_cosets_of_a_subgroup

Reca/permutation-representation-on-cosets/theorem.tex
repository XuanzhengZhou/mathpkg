Let $G$ be a group and $H$ a subgroup. Let $V$ be a vector space with basis $(e_\sigma)$ indexed by the elements $\sigma \in G/H$. Define a representation $\rho$ of $G$ in $V$ by
$$\rho_s e_\sigma = e_{s\sigma} \quad \text{for } s \in G,\; \sigma \in G/H.$$
This is the permutation representation of $G$ associated with $G/H$. The vector $e_H$ (corresponding to the coset $H$) is invariant under $H$, giving the unit representation of $H$ on $\mathbb{C} e_H$, and this representation induces $\rho$.
