Artin's theorem states that the function $a_G$, built from the ramification data of a finite Galois extension of a complete discretely valued field, is a genuine character of a representation of the Galois group $G$. This means that for every irreducible character $\chi$ of $G$, the scalar product $\langle a_G, \chi \rangle$ is a non-negative integer. The theorem is fundamental to the study of Artin and Swan representations and is proved by reducing to the cyclic case.
