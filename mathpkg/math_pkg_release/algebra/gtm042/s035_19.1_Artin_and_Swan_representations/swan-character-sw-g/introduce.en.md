The Swan character $sw_G$ is obtained from the Artin character $a_G$ by subtracting the augmentation character $u_G = r_G - 1$, where $r_G$ is the regular representation character. For a non-identity element $s \in G$, $sw_G(s) = 1 - i_G(s)$, and $sw_G(1) = \sum_{s \neq 1}(i_G(s) - 1)$. Like the Artin character, the Swan character is a virtual character that turns out to be a genuine character of $G$.
