It is easily checked that, if $\chi$ is a character of $G$, the scalar product $\langle sw_G, \chi \rangle$ is $\geqslant 0$. Using Artin's theorem (that $a_G$ is a character of a representation of $G$), one sees that $\langle sw_G, \chi \rangle$ is a non-negative integer for all $\chi$, that is, $sw_G$ is a character of $G$.

The character $a_G$ (resp. $sw_G$) is called the Artin (resp. Swan) character of the Galois group $G$; the corresponding representation is called the Artin (resp. Swan) representation of $G$.
