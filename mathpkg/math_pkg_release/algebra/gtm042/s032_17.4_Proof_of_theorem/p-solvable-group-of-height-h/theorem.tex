A finite group $G$ is said to be \emph{$p$-solvable of height $h$} if it is a successive extension of $h$ groups which are either of order prime to $p$ or of order a power of $p$. Equivalently, there exists a normal series
$$
1 = G_0 \trianglelefteq G_1 \trianglelefteq \cdots \trianglelefteq G_h = G
$$
such that each successive quotient $G_i / G_{i-1}$ is either a $p$-group or a group of order prime to $p$.
