Theorem 37 gives a character-theoretic criterion for an element of the Grothendieck group $R_{k'}(G)$ to come from a projective $A'[G]$-module. It states that a virtual character $\chi$ corresponds to a projective module if and only if $\chi$ vanishes on all $p$-singular elements of $G$. The proof splits into necessity (showing characters of projective modules vanish off $p$-regular elements by reduction to cyclic groups) and sufficiency (using the Artin induction theorem to decompose the class and checking the condition on elementary subgroups). The third part handles descent from $K'$ to $K$ when the character values lie in the smaller field.
