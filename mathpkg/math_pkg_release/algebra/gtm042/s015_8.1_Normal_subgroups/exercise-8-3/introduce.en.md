Exercise 8.3 asks to verify the classification of the dihedral group $D_n$ within the solvable--supersolvable--nilpotent hierarchy. The dihedral group is always supersolvable (it has a cyclic normal subgroup of index 2), but it is nilpotent only when $n$ is a power of 2, in which case it is actually a $2$-group and thus nilpotent by Theorem 14.
