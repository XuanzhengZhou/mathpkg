Let $G$ be a nilpotent group. Show that, for each prime number $p$, $G$ contains a unique Sylow $p$-subgroup, which is normal [use induction on the order of $G$, and apply the induction hypothesis to the quotient of $G$ by its center, cf. ex. 8.7(b)].
