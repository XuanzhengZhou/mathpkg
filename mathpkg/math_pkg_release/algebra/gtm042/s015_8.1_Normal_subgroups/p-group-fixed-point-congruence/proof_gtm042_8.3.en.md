Indeed $X \setminus X^G$ is a union of nontrivial orbits of $G$, and the cardinality of each of these orbits is a power $p^\alpha$ of $p$, with $\alpha \geqslant 1$; hence $\operatorname{Card}(X \setminus X^G)$ is divisible by $p$. Therefore $\operatorname{Card}(X) \equiv \operatorname{Card}(X^G) \pmod{p}$.
