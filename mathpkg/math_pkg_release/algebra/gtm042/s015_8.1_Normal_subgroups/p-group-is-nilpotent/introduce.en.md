Theorem 14 states that every $p$-group (a finite group of prime-power order) is nilpotent, and consequently supersolvable and solvable. The proof reduces to showing that every nontrivial $p$-group has a nontrivial center, which follows from Lemma 3 applied to the action of $G$ on itself by inner automorphisms. This places $p$-groups within the hierarchy nilpotent $\Rightarrow$ supersolvable $\Rightarrow$ solvable.
