In characteristic $p$, the representation theory of $p$-groups collapses dramatically: the only irreducible representation is the trivial one. This stands in stark contrast to the ordinary (characteristic $0$) theory, where $p$-groups can have irreducible representations of degree equal to any power of $p$ dividing the group order. The result follows from Lemma 3 via an application of the fixed-point congruence to the representation space.
