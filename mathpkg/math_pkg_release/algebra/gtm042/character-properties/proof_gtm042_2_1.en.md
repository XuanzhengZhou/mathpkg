(i) $\rho(1) = 1$, and $\text{Tr}(1) = n$ since $V$ has dimension $n$.

(ii) $\rho_s$ has finite order; consequently its eigenvalues $\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n$ have absolute value equal to 1 (this follows since $\rho_s$ can be defined by a unitary matrix, cf. 1.3). Thus

$$\chi(s)^* = \text{Tr}(\rho_s)^* = \sum \lambda_i^* = \sum \lambda_i^{-1} = \text{Tr}(\rho_s^{-1}) = \text{Tr}(\rho_{s^{-1}}) = \chi(s^{-1}).$$

(iii) $\chi(tst^{-1}) = \chi(st^{-1}t) = \chi(s)$ by the formula $\text{Tr}(ab) = \text{Tr}(ba)$, valid for arbitrary linear mappings.
