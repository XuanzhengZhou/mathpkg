**Lemma 8.** Let $x$ be a $p'$-element of $G$, and let $H$ be a $p$-elementary subgroup of $G$ associated with $x$ (10.1). Then there exists a function $\psi \in A \otimes R(H)$, with integer values, such that the induced function $\psi' = \text{Ind}_H^G \psi$ has the following properties:

(a) $\psi'(x) \neq 0 \pmod{p}$.
(b) $\psi'(s) = 0$ for each $p'$-element of $G$ which is not conjugate to $x$.

Let $C$ be the cyclic subgroup of $G$ generated by $x$, and let $Z(x)$ be the centralizer of $x$ in $G$. We have $H = C \times P$, where $P$ is a Sylow $p$-subgroup of $Z(x)$. Let $c$ be the order of $C$, and let $p^a$ be the order of $P$. Let $\psi_C$ be the function defined on $C$ by

$$\psi_C(x) = c \quad \text{and} \quad \psi_C(y) = 0 \quad \text{if} \ y \neq x.$$
