This exercise establishes the important fact that the number of orbits of a group action can be computed as the inner product of the permutation character with the trivial character. This is a special case of Burnside's orbit-counting lemma expressed in character-theoretic language. When $G$ acts transitively, the permutation representation decomposes as the trivial representation plus a complement with no trivial component.
