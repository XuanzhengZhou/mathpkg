As in Section 10.5, Theorem 27 is obtained from a more precise result, relative to a fixed prime number $p$, namely Theorem 28 below. The proof of Theorem 28 is completely analogous to that of Theorem 18' (to which it reduces when $K$ is algebraically closed, i.e., $\Gamma_K = \{1\}$). From Theorem 28 one deduces Theorem 27 by the same argument as in the classical case: the constant function $l$ (where $g = p^n l$ with $(p, l) = 1$) belongs to the image $V_{K,p}$ of induction from $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary subgroups, and since the index of $V_{K,p}$ is finite and prime to $p$, varying $p$ over all prime divisors of $g$ shows that the image of induction from all $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups is the whole ring $R_K(G)$.
