Brauer's classical theorem asserts that every character of a finite group is an integral linear combination of characters induced from elementary subgroups. Theorem 27 generalizes this to representations over an arbitrary field $K$ of characteristic zero, with $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups playing the role of ordinary elementary subgroups. The $\Gamma_K$-elementary condition ensures compatibility with the Galois action of $K$, making these subgroups the correct class for induction over non-algebraically-closed fields. The proof follows the same strategy as in the classical case, reducing to a more precise result (Theorem 28) for a fixed prime $p$.
