Let $G$ be a finite group, $K$ a field of characteristic zero, and $p$ a prime number. A subgroup $H$ of $G$ is said to be \emph{$\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary} if it is a semidirect product $H = C \cdot P$ of a cyclic group $C$ of order prime to $p$ by a $p$-group $P$, such that for every $t \in \Gamma_K$, the automorphism $c \mapsto c^t$ of $C$ belongs to the subgroup of $\operatorname{Aut}(C)$ generated by the Frobenius automorphism $c \mapsto c^p$. Equivalently, for each $t \in \Gamma_K$, there exists an integer $\nu$ such that $t \equiv p^{\nu} \pmod{|C|}$.

(When $\Gamma_K = \{1\}$, this condition just means that $C$ and $P$ commute, so that $H = C \times P$ is a $p$-elementary group in the usual sense.)

A subgroup $H$ of $G$ is said to be \emph{$\Gamma_K$-elementary} if it is $\Gamma_K$-$p$-elementary for at least one prime number $p$.
