A $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroup generalizes the notion of a $p$-elementary subgroup to account for the Galois action of a non-algebraically-closed field $K$. While an ordinary $p$-elementary subgroup is a semidirect product of a cyclic group (order prime to $p$) and a $p$-group, the $\Gamma_K$ variant imposes an additional compatibility condition: the Galois automorphisms $t \in \Gamma_K$ must act on the cyclic factor via powers of the Frobenius automorphism $c \mapsto c^p$. This condition ensures that characters of $\Gamma_K$-elementary subgroups behave well with respect to the field $K$, and these subgroups serve as the natural class for Brauer's induction theorem over arbitrary fields of characteristic zero.
