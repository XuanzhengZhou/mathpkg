The homomorphism $d: \mathbb{R}_K(\mathbb{G}) \to \mathbb{R}_k(\mathbb{G})$ is surjective.

The proof will be given in 17.3.

Remarks

(1) This applies in particular to $k = \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}$, taking for $K$ the $p$-adic field $\mathbb{Q}_p$; the ring $A$ is then the ring $\mathbb{Z}_p$ of $p$-adic integers.

(2) Roughly speaking, the theorem asserts that every linear representation of $G$ over $k$ can be lifted to characteristic 0 if we are willing to accept "virtual representations", i.e., elements of the Grothendieck group $\mathbb{R}_K(\mathbb{G})$. This is an extremely useful result for many applications.
