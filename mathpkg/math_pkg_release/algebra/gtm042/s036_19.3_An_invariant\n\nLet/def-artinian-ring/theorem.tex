A ring $A$ is said to be \emph{artinian} if it satisfies the following equivalent conditions (cf. Bourbaki, Alg. Ch. VIII, \S 2):

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] Every decreasing sequence of left ideals of $A$ is stationary.
\item[(b)] The left $A$-module $A$ has finite length.
\item[(c)] Every finitely generated left $A$-module has finite length.
\end{enumerate}
