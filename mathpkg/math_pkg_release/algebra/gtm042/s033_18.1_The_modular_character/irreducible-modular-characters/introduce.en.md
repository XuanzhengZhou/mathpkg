The irreducible modular characters of a finite group $G$ (in characteristic $p$) are the Brauer characters of the simple $k[G]$-modules. They are the modular analogues of irreducible ordinary characters, and form a fundamental set of class functions on $G_{\text{reg}}$. By Brauer's Theorem 42, they constitute a basis of the space of class functions on the $p$-regular elements, analogous to how irreducible ordinary characters form a basis for class functions on the whole group.
