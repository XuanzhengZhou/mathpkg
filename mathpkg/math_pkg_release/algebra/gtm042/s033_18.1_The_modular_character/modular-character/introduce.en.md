The modular character (or Brauer character) $\phi_E$ is the characteristic-$p$ analogue of the ordinary character of a representation. For a $k[G]$-module $E$ in characteristic $p$, the modular character is defined only on the $p$-regular elements $G_{\text{reg}}$, where the representing endomorphisms are diagonalizable. The eigenvalues of $s_E$ are roots of unity in the residue field $k$; they are lifted uniquely to roots of unity in the characteristic-zero field $K$, and their sum defines $\phi_E(s)$, taking values in the valuation ring $A$.
