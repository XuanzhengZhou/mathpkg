Corollary 2 of Section 14.4 states that the Grothendieck group $P_A(G)$ of projective $A[G]$-modules provides a complete isomorphism invariant: two projective $A[G]$-modules are isomorphic precisely when they determine the same class in $P_A(G)$. This follows from the decomposition theorem (Corollary 1) together with the fact that the classes of projective indecomposable modules form a basis of $P_A(G)$.
