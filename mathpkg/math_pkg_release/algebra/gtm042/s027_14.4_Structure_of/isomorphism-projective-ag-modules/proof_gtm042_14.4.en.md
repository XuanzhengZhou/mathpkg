By Corollary 1, every projective $A[G]$-module decomposes uniquely (up to isomorphism) as a direct sum of projective indecomposable $A[G]$-modules. The classes of these indecomposables form a basis of $P_A(G)$. If $[P] = [Q]$ in $P_A(G)$, then the coefficients in their expansions with respect to this basis coincide, which means $P$ and $Q$ have the same indecomposable summands with the same multiplicities and are therefore isomorphic.
