For an element $x$ in a commutative ring $R$, being integral over $\mathbb{Z}$ is equivalent to the subring $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ being finitely generated as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module, and also equivalent to $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ being contained in some finitely generated $\mathbb{Z}$-submodule of $R$. The equivalence $(ii) \Leftrightarrow (iii)$ uses that $\mathbb{Z}$ is Noetherian. For $(i) \Rightarrow (ii)$, the monic polynomial relation shows $1, x, \ldots, x^{n-1}$ span $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ over $\mathbb{Z}$.
