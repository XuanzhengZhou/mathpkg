Let $x$ be an element of a commutative ring $R$. The following properties are equivalent:

(i) $x$ is integral over $\mathbb{Z}$.

(ii) The subring $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ of $R$ generated by $x$ is finitely generated as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module.

(iii) There exists a finitely generated sub-$\mathbb{Z}$-module of $R$ which contains $\mathbb{Z}[x]$.
