Every value taken by a character of a finite group is an algebraic integer. This follows because $\chi(s) = \text{Tr}(\rho(s))$ is a sum of eigenvalues of $\rho(s)$, each of which is a root of unity (since $\rho(s)^{|G|} = I$). Roots of unity are algebraic integers, and sums of algebraic integers are algebraic integers. This property is foundational for integrality arguments bounding degrees of irreducible representations.
