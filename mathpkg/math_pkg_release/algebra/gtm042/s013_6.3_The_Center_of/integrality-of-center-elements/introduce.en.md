If every coefficient $u(s)$ of a central element $u \in \text{Cent. } C[G]$ is an algebraic integer, then $u$ itself is integral over $\mathbb{Z}$ as an element of the commutative ring $\text{Cent. } C[G]$. The proof expresses $u$ as a linear combination of the class sums $e_i$ with algebraic integer coefficients, then uses that the $e_i$ span a finitely generated $\mathbb{Z}$-subalgebra of the center, making each $e_i$ integral over $\mathbb{Z}$.
