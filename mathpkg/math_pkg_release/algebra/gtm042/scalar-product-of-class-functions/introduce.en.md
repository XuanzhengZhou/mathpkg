The scalar product $(\phi|\psi) = \frac{1}{g} \sum_{t \in G} \phi(t)\psi(t)^*$ defines a Hermitian inner product on the space of complex-valued class functions on $G$. This inner product is the central tool for character theory: irreducible characters form an orthonormal basis with respect to it, and the multiplicity of an irreducible representation in a given representation is exactly the inner product of their characters.
