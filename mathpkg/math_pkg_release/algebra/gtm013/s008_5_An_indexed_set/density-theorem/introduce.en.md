The Jacobson Density Theorem states that for a semisimple left $R$-module $M$, the canonical homomorphism $R \to \operatorname{BiEnd}({}_R M)$ has dense image in the finite topology. Concretely, any biendomorphism of $M$ can be approximated arbitrarily closely (on finite subsets) by the action of some ring element $r \in R$. Equivalently, every $R$-submodule of $M^{(n)}$ is also a $\operatorname{BiEnd}({}_R M)$-submodule. This theorem generalizes the classical result that primitive rings are dense rings of linear transformations.
