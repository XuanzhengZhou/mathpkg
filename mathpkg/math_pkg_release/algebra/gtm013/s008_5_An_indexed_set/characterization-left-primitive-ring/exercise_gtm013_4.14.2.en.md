Let $Q$ be a field. For each $n \in \mathbb{N}$, each $A \in \mathbb{M}_n(Q)$, and each $s \in Q$, let $[A, s] \in \mathbb{M}_n(Q)$ be the matrix
$$[A, s] = \begin{bmatrix} A & 0 \\ s & 0 \end{bmatrix}.$$
Let $S$ be a subring of $Q$ and $R = \{[A, s] \mid s \in S, A \in \mathbb{M}_n(Q), n \in \mathbb{N}\}$.

(1) Prove that $R$ is a primitive subring of $\mathbb{CFM}_\mathbb{N}(Q)$ with $\operatorname{Cen} R \cong S$. Thus each subring of a field is the center of some primitive ring. (See Exercise (1.9).)

(2) Prove that if $S$ is not a field, then $R$ has a non-primitive factor ring.
