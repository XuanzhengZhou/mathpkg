For a ring $R$ the following statements are equivalent:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $R$ has a simple left generator;
\item[(a')] $R$ has a simple right generator;
\item[(b)] $R$ is simple and left artinian;
\item[(b')] $R$ is simple and right artinian;
\item[(c)] For some simple ${}_R T$, ${}_R R \cong T^{(n)}$ for some $n$;
\item[(c')] For some simple $T_R$, $R_R \cong T^{(n)}$ for some $n$;
\item[(d)] $R$ is simple and ${}_R R$ is semisimple;
\item[(d')] $R$ is simple and $R_R$ is semisimple.
\end{itemize}
