For a ring $R$ the following statements are equivalent:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $R$ is semisimple;
\item[(b)] $R$ has a semisimple left generator;
\item[(c)] Every short exact sequence
$$0 \to K \to M \to N \to 0$$
of left $R$-modules splits;
\item[(d)] Every left $R$-module is semisimple.
\end{itemize}
Moreover, these statements are equivalent if throughout "left" is replaced by "right".
