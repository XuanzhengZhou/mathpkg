This theorem provides the detailed internal structure of a semisimple ring $R$. It states that $R$ has finitely many isomorphism classes of simple left modules, represented by minimal left ideals $T_1, \ldots, T_m$. The trace ideals $\operatorname{Tr}_R(T_i) = R T_i R$ are simple artinian rings, each isomorphic to a matrix ring $\mathbb{M}_{n_i}(D_i)$ over a division ring $D_i = \operatorname{End}({}_R T_i)$, and $R$ is their ring direct sum. This refines the Wedderburn–Artin Theorem by explicitly identifying the simple artinian summands.
