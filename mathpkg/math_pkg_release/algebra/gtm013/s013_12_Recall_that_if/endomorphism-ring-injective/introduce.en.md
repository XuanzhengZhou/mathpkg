Proposition 18.20 characterizes the Jacobson radical of the endomorphism ring of an injective module: an endomorphism $a$ belongs to $J(\operatorname{End}({}_R E))$ precisely when its kernel is an essential submodule of $E$. This is the dual of Proposition 17.11, which states that for a projective module $P$, $a \in J(\operatorname{End}(P_R))$ iff $\operatorname{Im} a \ll P$. Together, these results reveal a deep duality between projective covers and injective envelopes at the level of endomorphism rings.
