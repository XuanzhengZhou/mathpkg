Given $R$-homomorphisms $f: M \to M$ and $g: N \to N$, their tensor product $f \otimes g$ is the induced map on tensor products. The map is well-defined because $(m,n) \mapsto f(m) \otimes g(n)$ is $R$-balanced (as $f(mr) \otimes g(n) = f(m)r \otimes g(n) = f(m) \otimes r g(n) = f(m) \otimes g(rn)$). This construction makes the tensor product into a bifunctor, covariant in both arguments, and is the foundation for the tensor functors used throughout homological algebra.
