Let $M_R$ and ${}_R N$ be modules. A pair $(T, \tau)$ consisting of an abelian group $T$ and an $R$-balanced map $\tau: M \times N \to T$ is a \text{tensor product} of $M_R$ and ${}_R N$ in case for every abelian group $A$ and every $R$-balanced map $\beta: M \times N \to A$, there exists a unique $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism $f: T \to A$ such that $f \circ \tau = \beta$. Denote $T = M \otimes_R N$ and $\tau(m, n) = m \otimes n$.
