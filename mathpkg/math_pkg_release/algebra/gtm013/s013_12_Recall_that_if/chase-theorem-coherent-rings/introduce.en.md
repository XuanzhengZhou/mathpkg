The Chase Theorem provides a beautiful characterization of left coherent rings in terms of flat modules: a ring $R$ is left coherent precisely when arbitrary direct products of flat right $R$-modules are flat. The proof is a tour de force of diagram chasing, using the Flat Test Lemma (19.17) and the Equational Criterion for Flatness (19.19). The theorem reveals a deep connection between the homological property of coherence (a finiteness condition on ideals) and the closure properties of the class of flat modules. Over a left coherent ring, the category of flat modules is a Grothendieck category.
