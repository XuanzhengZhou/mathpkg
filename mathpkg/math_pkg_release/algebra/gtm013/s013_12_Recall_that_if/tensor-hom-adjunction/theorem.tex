Given modules ${}_S U_R$ and ${}_S N$, if $f: {}_R M \to {}_R M$ is an $R$-homomorphism, then there exist $\mathbb{Z}$-isomorphisms $\phi$ and $\phi$ such that the following diagram commutes:
$$\operatorname{Hom}_R(M, \operatorname{Hom}_S(U, N)) \xrightarrow{\operatorname{Hom}_R(f, \operatorname{Hom}_S(U, N))} \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, \operatorname{Hom}_S(U, N))$$
$$\operatorname{Hom}_S((U \otimes_R M), N) \xrightarrow{\operatorname{Hom}_S((U \otimes_R f), N)} \operatorname{Hom}_S((U \otimes_R M), N).$$
