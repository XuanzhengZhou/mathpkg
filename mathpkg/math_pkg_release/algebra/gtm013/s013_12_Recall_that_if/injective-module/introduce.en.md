An injective module is the categorical dual of a projective module. While a projective module $P$ satisfies that $\operatorname{Hom}_R(P, -)$ preserves exactness of sequences, an injective module $E$ satisfies that the contravariant Hom functor $\operatorname{Hom}_R(-, E)$ preserves exactness. Equivalently, every $R$-homomorphism from a submodule into $E$ can be extended to the whole module. Injective modules play a fundamental role in the structure theory of modules over arbitrary rings, analogous to divisible abelian groups in the category of $\mathbb{Z}$-modules.
