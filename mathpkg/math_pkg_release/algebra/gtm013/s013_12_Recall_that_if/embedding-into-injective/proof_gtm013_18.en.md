Let $Q = \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ be the standard injective cogenerator of $\mathbb{Z}\mathbf{M}$. For each $m \in M \setminus \{0\}$, there exists a $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism $\gamma_m: M \to Q$ with $\gamma_m(m) \neq 0$. Define $f_m: M \to \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(R_R, Q)$ by $[f_m(x)](r) = \gamma_m(rx)$. Then $f_m$ is an $R$-homomorphism. The product map $f = \prod_{m \neq 0} f_m: M \to \prod_{m \neq 0} \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(R_R, Q)$ is a monomorphism. By Lemma 18.5, each factor is injective, and by Proposition 18.2 the product is injective.
