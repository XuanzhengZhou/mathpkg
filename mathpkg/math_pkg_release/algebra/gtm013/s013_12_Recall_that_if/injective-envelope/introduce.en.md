The injective envelope (or injective hull) $E(M)$ of a module $M$ is the minimal injective extension of $M$, dual to the concept of a projective cover. While every module is a submodule of an injective module (18.6), the injective envelope provides a minimal such embedding via an essential monomorphism. The classic example is $(\mathbb{Q}, i)$ as the injective envelope of $\mathbb{Z}$, where $i: \mathbb{Z} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Q}$ is the natural inclusion.
