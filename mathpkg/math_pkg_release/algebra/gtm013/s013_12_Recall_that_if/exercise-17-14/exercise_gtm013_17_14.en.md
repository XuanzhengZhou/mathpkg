(1) Prove that if $R$ is a ring with $J(R) = 0$, then no non-projective $R$-module has a projective cover. In particular, no finite $\mathbb{Z}$-module has a projective cover.
(2) Let $n$ be a natural number and let $p$ be a prime that divides $n$, say $p^m$ is the largest $p$-power that divides $n$. Prove that if $1 \leq k \leq m$, then the epimorphism $\mathbb{Z}_{p^m} \to \mathbb{Z}_{p^k}$ is a projective cover in $\mathbb{Z}_n\mathbf{M}$.
