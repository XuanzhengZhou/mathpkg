For an $R$-monomorphism $i: M \to E$, the following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $i: M \to E$ is an injective envelope of $M$;
\item[(b)] $E$ is injective and for every $R$-monomorphism $f: M \to Q$ with $Q$ injective, there exists a monomorphism $g: E \to Q$ such that $g i = f$;
\item[(c)] $i$ is an essential monomorphism and for every essential monomorphism $f: M \to N$, there exists a monomorphism $g: N \to E$ such that $g f = i$.
\end{enumerate}
