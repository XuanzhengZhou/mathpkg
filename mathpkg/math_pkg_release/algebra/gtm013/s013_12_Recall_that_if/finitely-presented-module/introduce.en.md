A finitely presented module is one that can be described by finitely many generators and finitely many relations. Over a noetherian ring, every finitely generated module is finitely presented. More generally, rings over which finitely generated ideals are finitely presented are called left coherent. Coherent rings are precisely the rings over which direct products of flat right modules are flat (Chase Theorem, 19.20). The concept of finite presentation is fundamental in the study of flatness, purity, and the model theory of modules.
