This lemma is a crucial step in proving that every module embeds into an injective module. It shows that by starting with a divisible abelian group $Q$ (such as $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$), one can construct an injective left $R$-module via the contravariant Hom functor $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(R_R, Q)$. The proof uses the Injective Test Lemma to reduce injectivity checking to left ideals of $R$.
