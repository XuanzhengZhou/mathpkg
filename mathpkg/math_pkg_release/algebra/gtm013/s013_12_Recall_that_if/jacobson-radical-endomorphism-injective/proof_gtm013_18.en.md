The proof is dual to that of (17.12). From (18.20), $a \in J(S)$ iff $\operatorname{Ker} a \trianglelefteq E$. Since $\operatorname{Soc} E \trianglelefteq E$, an endomorphism $a$ has essential kernel iff it annihilates $\operatorname{Soc} E$ (because any non-zero submodule of $E$ contains a simple submodule). Thus $J(S) = r_S(\operatorname{Soc} E)$. The restriction map $S \to \operatorname{End}({}_R \operatorname{Soc} E)$ is surjective (since $E$ is injective, any endomorphism of the socle extends to $E$), and its kernel is exactly $r_S(\operatorname{Soc} E) = J(S)$, giving the isomorphism.
