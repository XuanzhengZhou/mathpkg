This follows directly from the definition of the tensor product. Given an $R$-balanced map $\beta: M \times N \to A$, the universal property of $(M \otimes_R N, \tau)$ provides a unique $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism $f: M \otimes_R N \to A$ such that $f \circ \tau = \beta$, which means $f(m \otimes n) = \beta(m, n)$ for all $m \in M, n \in N$.
