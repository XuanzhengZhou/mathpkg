This proposition restates the universal property of the tensor product in its most usable form: any $R$-balanced map $\beta$ out of $M \times N$ factors uniquely through the tensor product via the canonical map $\tau(m,n) = m \otimes n$. This gives a bijection between $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(M \otimes_R N, A)$ and the set of $R$-balanced maps $M \times N \to A$, making the tensor product the universal linearization of bilinear phenomena. This property is the foundation for all computations with tensor products.
