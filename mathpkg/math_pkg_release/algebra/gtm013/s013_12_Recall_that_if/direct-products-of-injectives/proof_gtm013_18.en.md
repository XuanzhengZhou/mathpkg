The result follows from (16.11): a direct product of modules is injective if and only if each factor is injective. For direct summands, if $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$ is injective, then for any monomorphism $f: M_1 \to M_2$ and any $h: M_1 \to E_1$, composing with the inclusion $E_1 \hookrightarrow E$ gives an extension to $M_2 \to E$, whose projection onto $E_1$ yields the desired extension of $h$. Thus $E_1$ is injective.
