By the universal property of $(T, \tau)$, there exists a unique $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism $f: T \to T$ such that $\tau = f \tau$. Similarly, there exists a unique $\mathbb{Z}$-homomorphism $g: T \to T$ such that $\tau = g \tau$. Then $g f \tau = g \tau = \tau = 1_T \tau$, so by uniqueness in the universal property, $g f = 1_T$. Similarly $f g = 1_{T}$. Thus $f$ is an isomorphism.
