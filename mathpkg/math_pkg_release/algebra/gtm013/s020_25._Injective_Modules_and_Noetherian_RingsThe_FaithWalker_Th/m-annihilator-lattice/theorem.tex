The annihilator $\ell_R(X)$ in $R$ of a subset $X$ of a left $R$-module $M$ is a left ideal of $R$; such left ideals are called $M$-annihilator left ideals. The set $\mathcal{L}_R(M)$ of all $M$-annihilator left ideals, ordered by set inclusion, is a complete lattice where for each $\mathcal{A} \subseteq \mathcal{L}_R(M)$ the meet and join in $\mathcal{L}_R(M)$ of $\mathcal{A}$ are

$$\bigcap \mathcal{A} \quad \text{and} \quad \ell_R(r_M(\textstyle\sum \mathcal{A})),$$

respectively.  In general, $\mathcal{L}_R(M)$ is not a sublattice of the lattice of left ideals of $R$ since not every sum of $M$-annihilator left ideals need be an $M$-annihilator left ideal.
