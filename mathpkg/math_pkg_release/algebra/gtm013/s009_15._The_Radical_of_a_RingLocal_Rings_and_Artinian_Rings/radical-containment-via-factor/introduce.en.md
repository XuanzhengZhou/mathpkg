Corollary 15.6 states that if the factor ring $R/I$ is semiprimitive (i.e., $J(R/I) = 0$), then the Jacobson radical $J(R)$ must be contained in $I$. In other words, any ideal modulo which $R$ is semiprimitive must contain the radical.
