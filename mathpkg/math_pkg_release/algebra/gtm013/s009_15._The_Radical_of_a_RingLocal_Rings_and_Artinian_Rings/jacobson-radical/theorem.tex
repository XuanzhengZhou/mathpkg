Let $R$ be a ring. Then $\operatorname{End}(R)$ is simply the ring of right multiplications by elements of $R$. By (9.14) the radical $\operatorname{Rad}(R)$ of $R$ is a (two-sided) ideal of $R$. This ideal of $R$ is called the \textbf{(Jacobson) radical} of $R$, and we abbreviate
$$J(R) = \operatorname{Rad}(R).$$
One consequence of Theorem 15.3 is that this radical is also $\operatorname{Rad}(R_R)$; therefore there is no left-right ambiguity.
