\textbf{(a) $\Rightarrow$ (b).} Suppose $M \neq 0$ is finitely generated. Then $M$ has a maximal submodule $K$ by (10.5). By (15.5), $J(R)M \leq K$. Since $I \leq J(R)$, we have $IM \leq K \subsetneq M$, so $IM \neq M$.

\textbf{(b) $\Rightarrow$ (c).} Suppose $N \leq M$ and $IM + N = M$. Then
$$I(M/N) = (IM + N)/N = M/N.$$
If $M$ is finitely generated, then so is $M/N$, and (b) implies $M/N = 0$, i.e., $N = M$.

\textbf{(c) $\Rightarrow$ (a).} Assume (c). Since ${}_R R$ is finitely generated, $IR \ll R$. Thus $I \leq IR \leq J(R)$.
