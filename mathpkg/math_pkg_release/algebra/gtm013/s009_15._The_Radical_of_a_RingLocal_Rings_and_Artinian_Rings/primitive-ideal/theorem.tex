An ideal $P$ of a ring $R$ is a \textbf{(left) primitive ideal} in case $R/P$ is a (left) primitive ring. Similarly, a \textbf{right primitive ideal} is an ideal $P$ of $R$ such that $R/P$ is a right primitive ring. Since every simple ring is primitive, every maximal ideal is both left and right primitive. A left primitive ideal of $R$ is a two-sided ideal, but it need not be right primitive. The primitive ideals of $R$ are the kernels of the ring homomorphisms of $R$ onto dense rings of linear transformations of vector spaces.
