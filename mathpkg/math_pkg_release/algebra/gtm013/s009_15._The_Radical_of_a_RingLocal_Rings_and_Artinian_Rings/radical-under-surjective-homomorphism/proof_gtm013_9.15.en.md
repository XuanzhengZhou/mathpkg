Since $\phi$ is surjective, $\phi(J(R))$ is a quasi-regular ideal of $S$; thus $\phi(J(R)) \subseteq J(S)$ by (15.3). Conversely, suppose $\operatorname{Ker} \phi \subseteq J(R)$. If $M$ is a maximal left ideal of $R$, then $\operatorname{Ker} \phi \subseteq J(R) \subseteq M$, so by (3.11), $\phi(M)$ is a maximal left ideal of $S$. It follows that $\phi(J(R)) = J(S)$. Taking $S = R/J(R)$ with the natural projection yields $J(R/J(R)) = 0$.
