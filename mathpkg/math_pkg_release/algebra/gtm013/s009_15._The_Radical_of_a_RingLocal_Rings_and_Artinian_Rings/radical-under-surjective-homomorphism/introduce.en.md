Corollary 15.8 describes how the Jacobson radical behaves under surjective ring homomorphisms. The image of the radical is always contained in the radical of the target, and equality holds when the kernel is contained in the radical. A key consequence is that $J(R/J(R)) = 0$, so factoring out the radical yields a semiprimitive ring.
