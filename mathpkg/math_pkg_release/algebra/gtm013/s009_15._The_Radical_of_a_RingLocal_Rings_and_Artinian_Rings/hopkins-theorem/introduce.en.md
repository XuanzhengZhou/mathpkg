Hopkins' Theorem (15.20) is a remarkable result stating that for rings, the left artinian property implies the left noetherian property -- a fact that has no analogue for modules in general. Specifically, $R$ is left artinian if and only if $R$ is left noetherian, $J(R)$ is nilpotent, and $R/J(R)$ is semisimple. As a corollary, for a module over a left artinian ring, the properties of being finitely generated, noetherian, having a composition series, and being artinian are all equivalent.
