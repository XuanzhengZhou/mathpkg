A ring $R$ is called semiprimitive if its Jacobson radical $J(R)$ is zero. Equivalently, $R$ is semiprimitive if it has no non-zero quasi-regular left ideals. By Corollary 15.8, the factor ring $R/J(R)$ is always semiprimitive.
