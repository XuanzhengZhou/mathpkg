A covariant functor $F: C \rightarrow D$ is an isomorphism in case there is a covariant functor $G: D \rightarrow C$ with $FG = 1_D$ and $GF = 1_C$. If such a pair of functors exist, then $C$ and $D$ are isomorphic. Clearly isomorphic categories are equivalent, but the converse fails. For example, let $R$ be a simple module and let $C$ be the full subcategory of $_R M$ whose object class is $\{T\}$. Let $\mathcal{D}$ be a non-empty set of $R$-modules isomorphic to $T$, and let $D$ be the full subcategory of $_R M$ with object class $\mathcal{D}$. Prove that $C \approx D$ but that $C$ and $D$ are isomorphic iff $\mathcal{D}$ is a singleton.
