This exercise provides an important counterexample demonstrating that the condition "$R/J(R)$ is semisimple" alone does not guarantee semiperfectness. The ring $R$ of rational numbers whose denominators (in lowest terms) are not divisible by 2 or 3 has exactly two maximal ideals, so $R/J(R)$ is a product of two fields (hence semisimple). However, $\mathbb{Z} \subset R$ has only two idempotents, and the idempotents of $R/J(R)$ cannot all be lifted to $R$, showing that the idempotent lifting condition is necessary for semiperfectness.
