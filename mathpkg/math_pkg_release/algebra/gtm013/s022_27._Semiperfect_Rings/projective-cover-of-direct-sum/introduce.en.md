This lemma establishes that projective covers are compatible with finite direct sum decompositions. A homomorphism $p: P \to M$ is a projective cover exactly when $P$ decomposes into a direct sum whose components are projective covers of the corresponding summands of $M$. This fact is crucial for lifting orthogonal sets of idempotents modulo the Jacobson radical, as it allows one to lift decompositions term by term.
