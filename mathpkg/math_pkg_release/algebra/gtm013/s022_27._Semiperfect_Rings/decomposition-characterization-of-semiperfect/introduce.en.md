Theorem 27.12 provides a decomposition-theoretic characterization of semiperfect rings. It shows that semiperfectness is equivalent to the property that projective modules admit well-behaved decompositions complementing direct summands — a property reminiscent of the Krull-Schmidt theorem. Remarkably, it suffices to check this decomposition property for the single free module $R^{(2)}$ to conclude that every projective module enjoys it, and moreover that $R$ is semiperfect. This result, together with Azumaya's theorem, solidifies the connection between the internal idempotent structure and the external decomposition theory.
