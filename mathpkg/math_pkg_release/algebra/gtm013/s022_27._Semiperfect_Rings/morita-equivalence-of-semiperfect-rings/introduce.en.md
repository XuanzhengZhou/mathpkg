Proposition 27.14 extends the Wedderburn-Artin philosophy from semisimple rings to semiperfect rings: a semiperfect ring and its module category are completely determined by its basic ring. Specifically, any semiperfect ring $R$ is Morita equivalent to $e R e$, where $e$ is a basic idempotent, and two semiperfect rings are Morita equivalent precisely when their basic rings are isomorphic. This reduces the classification of semiperfect rings up to Morita equivalence to the classification of basic semiperfect rings up to isomorphism.
