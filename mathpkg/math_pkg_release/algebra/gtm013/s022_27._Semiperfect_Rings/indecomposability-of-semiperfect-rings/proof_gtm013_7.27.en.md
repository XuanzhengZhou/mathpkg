By (27.17, not included in the extracted text), if $e^2 = e \in R$ and $e + I^2$ is central in $R/I^2$, then $e$ is central in $R$. This lifting of central idempotents modulo ideals with square zero, combined with induction, shows that central idempotents lift uniquely modulo each $J^n$. Since $\bigcap_{n=1}^\infty J^n = 0$, a central idempotent of $R/J$ lifts uniquely to a central idempotent of $R$. Therefore $R$ has a non-trivial central idempotent decomposition iff $R/J$ does, iff $R/J^2$ does. Hence $R$ is indecomposable iff $R/J^2$ is indecomposable.
