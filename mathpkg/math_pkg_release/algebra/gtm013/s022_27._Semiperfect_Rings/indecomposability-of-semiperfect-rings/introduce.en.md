Corollary 27.18 provides a useful criterion for indecomposability of semiperfect rings whose Jacobson radical has vanishing intersection of powers (such as left Artinian rings). The ring $R$ is indecomposable as a ring if and only if $R/J^2$ is indecomposable. The proof relies on the idempotent lifting lemma (27.17, not included) which shows that central idempotents lift modulo any ideal $I$ with $I^2 = 0$, then inductively modulo $J^n$. Since $\bigcap J^n = 0$, a central idempotent of $R/J$ lifts uniquely to $R$.
