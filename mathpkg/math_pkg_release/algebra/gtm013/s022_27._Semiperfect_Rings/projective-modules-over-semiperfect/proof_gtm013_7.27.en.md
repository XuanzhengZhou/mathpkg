Let $R$ be semiperfect with basic set $e_1, \ldots, e_m$, and let ${}_R P$ be projective. Since $R$ is semiperfect, $R$ itself is a direct sum $R = R e_1 \oplus \ldots \oplus R e_n$ of primitive left ideals generated by a complete orthogonal set of primitive idempotents. Hence $R^{(A)}$ is a direct sum of copies of such primitives for any set $A$.

Since $P$ is a direct summand of $R^{(A)}$ for some $A$, we have

$$P \oplus P' \cong R^{(A)} \cong R e_1^{(C_1 \times A)} \oplus \ldots \oplus R e_m^{(C_m \times A)}.$$

Now by (27.6), each $e_i R e_i \cong \operatorname{End}({}_R R e_i)$ is a local ring. So the existence assertion of the Theorem follows from the Crawley-Jonsson-Warfield result (26.5). The uniqueness assertion follows from (12.6).
