This proposition shows that semiperfectness is a property that can be checked on the corner rings $e_i R e_i$ of a complete orthogonal set of idempotents. Since each $e_i R e_i \cong \operatorname{End}({}_R R e_i)$, this relates the semiperfectness of $R$ to the endomorphism rings of indecomposable projective summands. This result is the semiperfect analogue of the fact that a ring is a direct sum of indecomposable right ideals if and only if its corner rings are local.
