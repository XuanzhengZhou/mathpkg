This exercise shows that in the commutative case, the theory of semiperfect rings simplifies dramatically. A commutative semiperfect ring is always basic (the only idempotents are the sums of primitive ones appearing in a complete orthogonal set) and decomposes as a finite direct product of commutative local rings. This generalizes the structure of commutative Artinian rings.
