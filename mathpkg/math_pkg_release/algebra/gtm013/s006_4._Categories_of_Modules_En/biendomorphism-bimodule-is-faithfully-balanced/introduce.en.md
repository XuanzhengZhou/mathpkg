For any left $R$-module $M$, one can form its endomorphism ring $T = \operatorname{End}(_R M)$ and then the biendomorphism ring $B = \operatorname{BiEnd}(_R M) = \operatorname{End}(M_T)$. Proposition 4.12 states that the resulting bimodule $_B M_T$ is always faithfully balanced. This means the natural maps $B \to \operatorname{End}(M_T)$ and $T \to \operatorname{End}(_B M)$ are isomorphisms. In other words, the process of taking endomorphism and biendomorphism rings "stabilizes": every $T$-endomorphism comes from $B$ (by definition), and every $B$-endomorphism comes from $T$ (via the ring homomorphism $\lambda: R \to B$ and the inclusion $\operatorname{End}(_B M) \leq \operatorname{End}(_R M) = T$).
