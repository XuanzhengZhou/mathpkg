A module $_R M$ naturally gives rise to the bimodule $_R M_{\operatorname{End}(_R M)}$, where the right action is evaluation of endomorphisms. The module is called balanced if this derived bimodule is balanced, which is equivalent to the natural map $\lambda: R \to \operatorname{BiEnd}(_R M)$ being surjective. In other words, every $\operatorname{End}(_R M)$-endomorphism of $M$ is realized as left multiplication by some element of $R$. The module is faithfully balanced if $\lambda$ is an isomorphism. This concept is central to the density theorem and the structure theory of primitive rings.
