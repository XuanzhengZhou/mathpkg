Proposition 4.6 generalizes the canonical isomorphism $M \cong \operatorname{Hom}_R(R,M)$ from (4.5) to the setting of idempotents. Given a bimodule $_R M_S$ and idempotents $e \in R$, $f \in S$, the submodule $eM$ inherits an $eRe$-$S$-bimodule structure, while $Mf$ inherits an $R$-$fSf$-bimodule structure. The maps $\rho: eM \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(Re, M)$ and $\lambda: Mf \to \operatorname{Hom}_S(fS, M)$ are bimodule isomorphisms, where the Hom sets carry the bimodule structures induced by (4.4). This result is fundamental for studying idempotent-generated submodules and their endomorphism rings.
