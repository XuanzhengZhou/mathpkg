From (4.3) applied with $M = N$, we have $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M,M) = \operatorname{Hom}_{R/I}(M,M)$ as abelian groups. Since composition of endomorphisms is the same operation in both sets, the ring structures coincide. Therefore $\operatorname{End}(_R M) = \operatorname{End}(_{R/I} M)$ as rings.
