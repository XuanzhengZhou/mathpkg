A faithfully balanced bimodule is a balanced bimodule in which both natural maps $\lambda$ and $\rho$ are not just surjective but isomorphisms. This means the rings $R$ and $S$ are canonically identified with the full endomorphism rings of $M$ viewed as a module over the other ring. Key examples include the regular bimodule $_R R_R$ (by Proposition 4.11) and the biendomorphism bimodule $_{\operatorname{BiEnd}(_R M)} M_{\operatorname{End}(_R M)}$ (by Proposition 4.12).
