For two left $R$-modules $M$ and $N$, the set $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M, N)$ of all $R$-homomorphisms from $M$ to $N$ carries a natural structure of an abelian group. The group operation is pointwise addition of homomorphisms: given $f, g \in \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, N)$, their sum $f + g$ is defined by $(f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)$ for all $x \in M$. One verifies that $f + g$ is again an $R$-homomorphism, using the $R$-linearity of $f$ and $g$ together with the commutativity of addition in the target module $N$. The zero element is the zero homomorphism $x \mapsto 0$, and the additive inverse of $f$ is $-f$ defined by $(-f)(x) = -f(x)$. This abelian group structure is the starting point for the categorical study of modules.
