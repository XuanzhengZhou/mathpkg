Remark 4.13 observes that the process of taking endomorphism and biendomorphism rings stabilizes after one iteration. Starting from $R M$, one obtains $T = \operatorname{End}(_R M)$ and then $B = \operatorname{BiEnd}(_R M)$. By Proposition 4.12, $T = \operatorname{End}(_B M)$, and applying the biendomorphism construction again just returns $T$: the "tri-endomorphism ring" $\operatorname{BiEnd}(M_T)$ equals $T$. This stabilization property is one of the key features that makes the biendomorphism ring a useful invariant.
