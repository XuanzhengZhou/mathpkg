This exercise provides a concrete counterexample in the ring of column-finite infinite matrices over $\mathbb{R}$: the matrix with entries $\delta_{i,2j}$ has a left inverse but fails to be right cancellable, demonstrating that one-sided invertibility does not imply the other side's properties in noncommutative rings.
