The canonical injections $\iota_\alpha: R_\alpha \to \prod_A R_\alpha$ embed each factor ring into the product by placing an element in the $\alpha$-coordinate and zeros elsewhere. These maps preserve addition and multiplication but fail to be ring homomorphisms when the index set has at least two elements, because they do not send $1_{R_\alpha}$ to $1_R$ (the tuple of all ones).
