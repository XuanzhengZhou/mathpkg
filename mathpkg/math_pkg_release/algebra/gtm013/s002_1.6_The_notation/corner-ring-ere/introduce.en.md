For a nonzero idempotent $e$, the set $eRe = \{exe : x \in R\}$ forms a ring with multiplicative identity $e$. It is called a corner ring. Unlike subrings, the identity of $eRe$ is $e$ rather than $1_R$, so $eRe$ is generally not a subring of $R$. When $e$ is central, $eRe$ is a homomorphic image of $R$.
