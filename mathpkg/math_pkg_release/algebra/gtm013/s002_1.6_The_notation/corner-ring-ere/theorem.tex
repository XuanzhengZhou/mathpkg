Each non-zero idempotent $e$ of a ring $R$ determines a second ring, namely

$$eRe = \{exe \mid x \in R\},$$

with addition and multiplication that of $R$ restricted to $eRe$, and with identities $0 = e0e$ and $e = e1e$. If $e \neq 1$, then the ring $eRe$ is not a subring of $R$ and if $e$ is not central, $eRe$ need not be a homomorphic image of $R$.
