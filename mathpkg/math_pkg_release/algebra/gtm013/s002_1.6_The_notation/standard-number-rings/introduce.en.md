The sets $\mathbb{Z}$ (integers), $\mathbb{Q}$ (rational numbers), $\mathbb{R}$ (real numbers), and $\mathbb{C}$ (complex numbers) carry their standard ring structures. All four are commutative integral domains, with the latter three being fields. The ring of integers $\mathbb{Z}$ has the special property that its additive group is cyclic, which forces every ideal to be principal.
