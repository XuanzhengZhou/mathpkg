Let $\Gamma, \Lambda, \Omega$ be non-empty sets, and

$$A = \llbracket a_{\alpha\beta} \rrbracket \in \mathbb{M}_{\Gamma \times \Lambda}(R), \quad B = \llbracket b_{\beta\gamma} \rrbracket \in \mathbb{M}_{\Lambda \times \Omega}(R).$$

For each $\alpha \in \Gamma$ and $\gamma \in \Omega$ consider the formal series $\sum_{\beta \in \Lambda} a_{\alpha\beta} b_{\beta\gamma}$. If either $A$ is row finite or $B$ is column finite, then this series has at most finitely many non-zero terms which sum to a unique element $c_{\alpha\gamma} \in R$, and the $\Gamma \times \Omega$-matrix

$$AB = \llbracket \sum_{\beta \in \Lambda} a_{\alpha\beta} b_{\beta\gamma} \rrbracket_{\Gamma \times \Omega}$$

is called the (matrix) product of $A$ and $B$ (in that order).

Wherever this product is defined, it is associative and distributes over addition on both the right and left. If both $A$ and $B$ are column finite (row finite) then $AB$ is column finite (row finite).
