For each integer $n > 1$, the set $\mathbb{Z}_n = \{0, 1, \ldots, n-1\}$ forms a ring under addition and multiplication modulo $n$. Each element $[a]_n \in \mathbb{Z}_n$ is the least positive remainder of $a$ upon division by $n$, making $\mathbb{Z}_n$ the canonical set of representatives for the residue classes modulo $n\mathbb{Z}$.
