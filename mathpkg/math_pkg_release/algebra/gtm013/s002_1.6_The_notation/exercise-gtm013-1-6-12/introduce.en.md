The semigroup ring $RG$ (also called the group ring when $G$ is a group) is a fundamental construction that combines a ring $R$ with a monoid $G$. Elements are finitely supported functions from $G$ to $R$, with multiplication given by convolution. The ring $R$ embeds via constant functions on the identity, and $G$ embeds as delta functions, yielding a unique representation of each element as a formal linear combination.
