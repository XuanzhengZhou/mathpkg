Cayley's Theorem for rings: every ring embeds into the endomorphism ring of its underlying additive group via left (or right) multiplication. This is the ring-theoretic analogue of Cayley's Theorem for groups (embedding into a symmetric group). When the additive group is cyclic, the embedding is actually an isomorphism and the ring is commutative.
