The endomorphisms of an abelian group $A$ form a ring $\operatorname{End}(A)$ under pointwise addition and composition. This ring plays a role in ring theory analogous to that of symmetric groups in group theory: every ring embeds into the endomorphism ring of its underlying additive group (Cayley's Theorem for rings).
