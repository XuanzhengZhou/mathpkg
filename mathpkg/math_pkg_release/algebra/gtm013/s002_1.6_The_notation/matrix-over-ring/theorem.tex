Let $R$ be a ring and let $\Gamma$ and $\Lambda$ be non-empty sets. Then a $\Gamma \times \Lambda$-matrix over $R$ is simply a function $A: \Gamma \times \Lambda \rightarrow R$. For each $(\alpha, \beta) \in \Gamma \times \Lambda$ let $A(\alpha, \beta) = a_{\alpha\beta} \in R$; then $a_{\alpha\beta}$ is called the $(\alpha, \beta)$ entry in $A$ and we write

$$A = \llbracket a_{\alpha\beta} \rrbracket_{\Gamma \times \Lambda}.$$

When there is no likelihood of confusion about the sets $\Gamma$ and $\Lambda$ we may simply write $A = \llbracket a_{\alpha\beta} \rrbracket$.
