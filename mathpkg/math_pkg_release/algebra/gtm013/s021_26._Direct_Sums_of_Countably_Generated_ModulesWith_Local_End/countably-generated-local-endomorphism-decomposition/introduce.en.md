This is the main result of Section 26. It answers the open question mentioned at the beginning of the section: if $M = \oplus_A M_\alpha$ with each $M_\alpha$ countably generated and having a local endomorphism ring, then every direct summand $K$ of $M$ also admits a decomposition into terms with local endomorphism rings (specifically, into finite direct sums of the original $M_\alpha$). The proof reduces to the countably generated case using the Kaplansky-Walker theorem, then applies Lemma 26.4 iteratively along a spanning set to build the decomposition.
