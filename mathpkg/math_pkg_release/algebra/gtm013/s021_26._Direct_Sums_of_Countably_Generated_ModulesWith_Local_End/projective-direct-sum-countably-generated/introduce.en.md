Every projective module $P$ is a direct summand of a free module $R^{(X)}$, which is itself a direct sum of copies of the regular module $R_R$. Since $R_R$ is countably generated (in fact, singly generated), the free module $R^{(X)}$ is a direct sum of countably generated modules. Applying the Kaplansky-Walker Theorem (26.1) with $c = \aleph_0$ yields that $P$, as a direct summand, is also a direct sum of countably generated modules.
