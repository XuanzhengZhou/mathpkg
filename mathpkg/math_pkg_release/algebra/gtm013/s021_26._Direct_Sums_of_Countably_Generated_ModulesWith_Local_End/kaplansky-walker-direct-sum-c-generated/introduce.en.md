The Kaplansky-Walker Theorem generalizes the fact that for any infinite cardinal $c$, the property of being a direct sum of $c$-generated submodules is inherited by direct summands. Originally proved by Kaplansky for the countably generated case ($c = \aleph_0$), it was extended to arbitrary infinite cardinals by C. Walker. The proof combines Lemma 25.7 (each $c$-generated submodule is contained in a sum of at most $c$ of the $M_\alpha$) with a standard induction argument to construct an increasing chain of index subsets of cardinality at most $c$.
