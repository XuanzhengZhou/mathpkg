Corollary 26.6 extends the Krull-Schmidt-Azumaya uniqueness theorem to the setting of direct sums of countably generated modules with local endomorphism rings. It states that any two direct sum decompositions of such a module $M$ share the same indecomposable summands up to isomorphism, with the second decomposition being a coarsening of the first. This generalizes classical uniqueness results from finite to infinite direct sums under suitable countability hypotheses.
