Corollary 22.4 provides the most practical test for Morita equivalence: two rings $R$ and $S$ are equivalent if and only if $S$ is isomorphic to the endomorphism ring of some progenerator over $R$ (or vice versa). This generalizes the fact that a ring is equivalent to the matrix ring $M_n(R)$ via the progenerator $R^{(n)}$. Combined with the fact that progenerators are exactly finitely generated projective generators, this gives a concrete criterion for checking equivalence.
