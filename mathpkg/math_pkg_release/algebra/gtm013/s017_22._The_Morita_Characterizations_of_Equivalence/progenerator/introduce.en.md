A progenerator is a module that is simultaneously a finitely generated module, a projective module, and a generator. This three-in-one property makes progenerators the fundamental building blocks of Morita equivalence theory, as they allow one to transfer module categories between different rings. The regular module ${}_R R$ itself serves as the canonical example of a progenerator over any ring $R$.
