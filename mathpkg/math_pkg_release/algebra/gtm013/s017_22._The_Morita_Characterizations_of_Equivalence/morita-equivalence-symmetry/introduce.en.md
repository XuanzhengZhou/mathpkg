A key consequence of Morita's Theorem (22.2) is that Morita equivalence is symmetric: $R \approx S$ if and only if $R^{\text{op}} \approx S^{\text{op}}$. This means that left module category equivalence implies right module category equivalence and vice versa. Consequently, properties characterized by Morita equivalence (such as semisimplicity) are left-right symmetric for rings.
