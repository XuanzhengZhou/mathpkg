Let $R$ and $S$ be rings and let
$$F \colon {}_R\mathbf{M} \to {}_S\mathbf{M} \quad \text{and} \quad G \colon {}_S\mathbf{M} \to {}_R\mathbf{M}$$
be additive functors. Then $F$ and $G$ are inverse equivalences if and only if there exists a bimodule ${}_S P_R$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] ${}_S P$ and $P_R$ are progenerators;
\item[(2)] ${}_S P_R$ is balanced;
\item[(3)] $F \cong (P \otimes_R -)$ and $G \cong \operatorname{Hom}_S(P, -)$.
\end{enumerate}
Moreover, if there is a bimodule ${}_S P_R$ satisfying these conditions, then with
$$Q = \operatorname{Hom}_R(P, R),$$
we have ${}_R Q_S$ with ${}_R Q$ and $Q_S$ progenerators and
$$F \cong \operatorname{Hom}_R(Q, -) \quad \text{and} \quad G \cong (Q \otimes_S -).$$
