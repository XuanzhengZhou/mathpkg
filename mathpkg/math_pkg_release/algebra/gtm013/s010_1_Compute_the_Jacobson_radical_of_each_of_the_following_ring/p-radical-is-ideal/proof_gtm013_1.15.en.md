Since each kernel $\ker(R \to P)$ is an ideal of $R$, and the $\mathcal{P}$-radical is the intersection of all such kernels, it follows that $\operatorname{rad}_{\mathcal{P}}(R)$ is an intersection of ideals and therefore an ideal of $R$.
