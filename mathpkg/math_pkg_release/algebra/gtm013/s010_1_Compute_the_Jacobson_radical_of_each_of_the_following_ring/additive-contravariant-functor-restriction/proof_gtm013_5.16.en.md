For $f, g \in \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, N)$, additivity gives $T(f + g) = T(f) + T(g)$. Since $T$ is contravariant, $T(f)$ and $T(g)$ lie in $\operatorname{Hom}_S(T(N), T(M))$, and the equality $T(f + g) = T(f) + T(g)$ is exactly the statement that the restriction $T|_{M,N}$ preserves the group operation. Hence it is an abelian group homomorphism.
