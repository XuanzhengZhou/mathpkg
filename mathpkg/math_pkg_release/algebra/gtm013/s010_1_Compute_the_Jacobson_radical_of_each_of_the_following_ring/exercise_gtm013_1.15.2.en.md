Let $R$ be the ring of all upper triangular $\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}$-matrices over a field that are $0$ almost everywhere off the main diagonal.

(1) Show that the Jacobson radical $J(R)$ of $R$ is the subset of all strictly upper triangular matrices (i.e., all $[a_{ij}] \in R$ with $a_{ii} = 0$ for all $i$).

(2) Show that $J(R)$ is nil but not nilpotent.
