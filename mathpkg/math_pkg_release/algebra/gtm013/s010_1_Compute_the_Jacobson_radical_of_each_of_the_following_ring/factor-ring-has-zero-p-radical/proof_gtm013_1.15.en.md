Let $\bar{R} = R / \operatorname{rad}_{\mathcal{P}}(R)$. Consider the natural projection $\pi: R \to \bar{R}$. Any homomorphism $\bar{R} \to P$ with $P \in \mathcal{P}$ composes with $\pi$ to give a homomorphism $R \to P$, whose kernel therefore contains $\operatorname{rad}_{\mathcal{P}}(R)$. Hence the kernel of $\bar{R} \to P$ is trivial. Since $\operatorname{rad}_{\mathcal{P}}(\bar{R})$ is the intersection of all such kernels, it must be $0$.
