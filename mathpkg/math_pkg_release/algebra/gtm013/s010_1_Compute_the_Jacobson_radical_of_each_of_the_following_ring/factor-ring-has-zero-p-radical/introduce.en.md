For any class $\mathcal{P}$ of rings, the factor ring $R / \operatorname{rad}_{\mathcal{P}}(R)$ obtained by modding out the $\mathcal{P}$-radical itself has trivial $\mathcal{P}$-radical. This is a fundamental closure property of the radical construction: applying the radical operator twice yields nothing new. In the special case of the Jacobson radical, this means $J(R/J(R)) = 0$, i.e., the quotient is semiprimitive.
