An ideal $P$ of a ring $R$ is a prime ideal in case $R/P$ is a prime ring (see Exercise (14.10)). Thus an ideal $P$ is prime iff for each $x, y \in R$, $xRy \subseteq P$ implies $x \in P$ or $y \in P$. Note that for commutative rings this agrees with the usual definition.
