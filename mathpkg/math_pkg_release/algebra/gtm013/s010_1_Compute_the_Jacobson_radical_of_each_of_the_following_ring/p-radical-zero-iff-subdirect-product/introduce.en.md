The $\mathcal{P}$-radical of a ring $R$ vanishes precisely when $R$ embeds as a subdirect product of rings from the class $\mathcal{P}$. This gives a structural interpretation of the radical: it measures the failure of $R$ to be approximable by rings in $\mathcal{P}$. For the Jacobson radical in particular, $J(R) = 0$ if and only if $R$ is a subdirect product of primitive rings; such rings are called semiprimitive.
