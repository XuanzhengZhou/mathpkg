Let $R$ be a ring. An ideal $P$ of $R$ is a \text{prime ideal} in case $R/P$ is a prime ring. Equivalently, for all $x, y \in R$,

$$xRy \subseteq P \quad \Longrightarrow \quad x \in P \text{ or } y \in P.$$

\text{For commutative rings, this reduces to the usual condition: } xy \in P \implies x \in P \text{ or } y \in P.
