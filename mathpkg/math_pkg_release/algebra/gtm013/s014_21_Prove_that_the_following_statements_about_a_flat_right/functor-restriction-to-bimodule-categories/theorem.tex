Let ${}_R\mathcal{C}$, ${}_S\mathcal{D}$, and $\mathcal{D}_S$ be full subcategories of $R$- and $S$-modules. Let $F, F': {}_R\mathcal{C} \to {}_S\mathcal{D}$ be covariant and $H, H': {}_R\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}_S$ be contravariant additive functors. Let $\eta: F \to F'$ and $\nu: H \to H'$ be natural transformations. Finally, let ${}_R U_T$ and ${}_R V_T$ be bimodules with ${}_R U, {}_R V \in {}_R\mathcal{C}$ and let

$$f: {}_R U_T \to {}_R V_T$$

be a bimodule homomorphism. Then with the canonical bimodule structures:

(1) $F(f): {}_S F(U)_T \to {}_S F(V)_T$ is an $(S,T)$-bimodule homomorphism;

(2) $\eta_U: {}_S F(U)_T \to {}_S F'(U)_T$ is an $(S,T)$-bimodule homomorphism;

(3) $H(f): {}_T H(V)_S \to {}_T H(U)_S$ is a $(T,S)$-bimodule homomorphism;

(4) $\nu_U: {}_T H(U)_S \to {}_T H'(U)_S$ is a $(T,S)$-bimodule homomorphism.
