Two functors $F, G: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ are isomorphic, denoted $F \cong G$, if there exists a natural isomorphism $\eta: F \to G$ between them. This defines an equivalence relation on the class of functors from $\mathcal{C}$ to $\mathcal{D}$. Isomorphic functors are essentially identical in their behavior on objects and morphisms, differing only by a uniform relabeling via isomorphisms.
