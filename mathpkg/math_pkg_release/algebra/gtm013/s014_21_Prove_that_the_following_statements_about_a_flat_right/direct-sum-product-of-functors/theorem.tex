Suppose that $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}$ are categories of modules and that all direct sums and products of members of $\mathcal{D}$ belong to $\mathcal{D}$. If $(F_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ is an indexed class of additive functors $F_\alpha: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$ all of the same variance, then their \text{direct sum} and \text{direct product} are the additive functors

$$\bigoplus_A F_\alpha: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D} \quad \text{and} \quad \prod_A F_\alpha: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}$$

defined coordinatewise by

$$\left(\bigoplus_A F_\alpha\right)(f) = \bigoplus_A (F_\alpha(f)) \quad \text{and} \quad \left(\prod_A F_\alpha\right)(f) = \prod_A (F_\alpha(f)).$$
