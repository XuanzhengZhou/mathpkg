Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a full subcategory of ${}_R\mathcal{M}$ and let ${}_R U_S$ and ${}_R V_S$ be bimodules with ${}_R U$ and ${}_R V$ both in $\mathcal{C}$.

(1) If $\phi: \operatorname{Hom}_R(U_S, -) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(V_S, -)$ is a natural isomorphism, then $\phi_U(1_U): V \to U$ and $\phi_V^{-1}(1_V): U \to V$ are inverse $(R, S)$-isomorphisms.

(2) If $\phi: \operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U_S) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(-, V_S)$ is a natural isomorphism, then similarly $U$ and $V$ are isomorphic as bimodules.
