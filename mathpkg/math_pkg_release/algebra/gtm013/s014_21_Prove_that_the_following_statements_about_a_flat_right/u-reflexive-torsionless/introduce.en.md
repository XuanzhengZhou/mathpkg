A module $M$ is called $U$-torsionless if the evaluation map $\sigma_M: M \to M^{**}$ is injective, and $U$-reflexive if $\sigma_M$ is an isomorphism. Torsionlessness means that elements of $M$ can be detected by functionals into $U$, while reflexivity means $M$ is canonically identified with its double dual. These concepts generalize reflexivity from linear algebra to the module-theoretic setting.
