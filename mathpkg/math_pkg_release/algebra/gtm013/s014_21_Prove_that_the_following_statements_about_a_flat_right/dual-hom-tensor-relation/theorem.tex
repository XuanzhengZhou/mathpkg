Given modules $P_R$, ${}_T U_R$ and ${}_T N$ there is a homomorphism, natural in $P$, $U$ and $N$,

$$\eta: N \otimes_T \operatorname{Hom}_R(P, U) \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(P, (N \otimes_T U))$$

defined via

$$\eta(n \otimes_T \gamma): p \mapsto n \otimes_T \gamma(p).$$

If $P_R$ is finitely generated and projective, then $\eta$ is an isomorphism.
