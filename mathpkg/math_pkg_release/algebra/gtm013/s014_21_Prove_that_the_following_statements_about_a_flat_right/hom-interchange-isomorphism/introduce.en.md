The Hom interchange isomorphism allows swapping the order of Hom functors when the arguments belong to compatible bimodule categories. For $({}_R M, N_S, {}_R U_S)$, it provides a natural isomorphism $\operatorname{Hom}_R(M, \operatorname{Hom}_S(N, U)) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_S(N, \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, U))$. The map sends $\gamma: M \to \operatorname{Hom}_S(N, U)$ to $\eta(\gamma): N \to \operatorname{Hom}_R(M, U)$ defined by evaluating the arguments in reverse order.
