The Maximal Principle, commonly called Zorn's Lemma, is equivalent to the Axiom of Choice. It states that any non-empty partially ordered set in which every chain has an upper bound contains at least one maximal element. It is an indispensable tool in algebra for proving the existence of maximal ideals, bases of vector spaces, and algebraic closures.
