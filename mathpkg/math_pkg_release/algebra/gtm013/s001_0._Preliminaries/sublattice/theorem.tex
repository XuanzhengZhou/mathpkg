Let $P$ be a lattice. A subposet $P'$ of $P$ is a sublattice of $P$ if for every pair $a, b \in P'$, the join of $a$ and $b$ in $P$ (and the meet in $P$) belong to $P'$ and coincide with the join and meet computed in $P'$. A subposet that is itself a lattice is not necessarily a sublattice, because joins and meets in the subposet may differ from those in $P$.
