An ideal is an additive subgroup closed under left and right multiplication by arbitrary ring elements. Ideals are the ring-theoretic analogue of normal subgroups: they are precisely the kernels of ring homomorphisms. An ideal containing an invertible element must be the whole ring. The collection of all ideals of a ring forms a complete lattice under inclusion.
