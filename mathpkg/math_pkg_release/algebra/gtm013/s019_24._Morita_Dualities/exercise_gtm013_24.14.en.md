(1) Prove that every Frobenius algebra is quasi-Frobenius. (See Exercise (21.9).)

(2) Prove that every semisimple ring is quasi-Frobenius.

(3) Prove that if $R = R_1 \times \cdots \times R_n$, then $R$ is a cogenerator ring (quasi-Frobenius ring) if and only if each $R_i$ is.

(4) Prove that if $R \approx S$ (Morita equivalent), then $R$ is a cogenerator ring (quasi-Frobenius ring) if and only if $S$ is.
