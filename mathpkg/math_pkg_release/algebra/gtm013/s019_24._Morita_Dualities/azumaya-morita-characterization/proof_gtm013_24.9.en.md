By Theorem 24.8, a duality exists between ${}_R\mathbf{FM}$ and $\mathbf{FM}_S$ if and only if ${}_R R$ and $S_S$ are artinian and ${}_R U$ and $U_S$ are injective cogenerators. The additional condition that ${}_R U_S$ is balanced follows from (24.5) together with the fact that the duality restricts to an anti-isomorphism between the lattices of finitely generated submodules. The equivalence of the two directions follows by symmetry, applying the dual statement with the roles of $R$ and $S$ interchanged.

Specifically, the last statement (that ${}_R U_S$ is balanced) follows from Lemma 24.7 (Osofsky), Proposition 15.21, and the submodule correspondence established in (24.5). Since no infinite direct sum of non-zero $U$-reflexive modules exists, the finitely generated modules are precisely the $U$-reflexive ones of finite length, and the balanced property of $U$ is then deduced from the annihilator lattice anti-isomorphisms.
