Let ${}_R U_S$ define a Morita duality. Then for each $U$-reflexive left $R$-module $M$, the maps

$$K \mapsto r_{M^*}(K) \quad (K \leq M), \qquad L \mapsto l_M(L) \quad (L \leq M^*)$$

are inverse lattice anti-isomorphisms between the lattices of submodules of $M$ and $M^*$. In particular:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] For $K \leq M$, $K = l_M(r_{M^*}(K))$ and $r_{M^*}(l_M(L)) = L$ for $L \leq M^*$;
\item[(2)] The correspondence respects sums and intersections dually: $r_{M^*}(\sum K_\alpha) = \bigcap r_{M^*}(K_\alpha)$ and $r_{M^*}(\bigcap K_\alpha) = \sum r_{M^*}(K_\alpha)$;
\item[(3)] $K$ is essential in $M$ if and only if $r_{M^*}(K)$ is superfluous in $M^*$;
\item[(4)] $K$ is superfluous in $M$ if and only if $r_{M^*}(K)$ is essential in $M^*$.
\end{enumerate}
