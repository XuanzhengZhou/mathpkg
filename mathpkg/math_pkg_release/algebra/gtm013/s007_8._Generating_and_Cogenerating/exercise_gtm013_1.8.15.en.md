A ring $R$ is **left IBN** (for "invariant basis number") in case no two free left $R$-modules of different ranks are isomorphic. Thus (see Exercise (8.13)), $R$ is IBN iff $R^{(m)} \cong R^{(n)}$ implies $m = n$. Prove that:

(1) Left IBN implies right IBN. [Hint: Exercise (6.22).]

(2) If $I$ is an ideal of $R$ and if $R/I$ is IBN, then so is $R$. [Hint: Exercise (6.3).]

(3) Every field is IBN.

(4) Every commutative ring is IBN.
