A module $M$ is (finitely) generated by a class $\mathcal{U}$ of modules if there exists a (finite) indexed family $(U_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ from $\mathcal{U}$ and an epimorphism from the direct sum $\oplus_A U_\alpha$ onto $M$. This is the categorical reformulation of the notion of a spanning set: instead of requiring that elements of $M$ be linear combinations of elements from a subset, we require that $M$ be a homomorphic image of a direct sum of modules from $\mathcal{U}$. When $\mathcal{U}$ is a singleton $\{U\}$, this reduces to the existence of an epimorphism $U^{(A)} \to M \to 0$, meaning that $M$ is generated by sufficiently many copies of the single module $U$.
