(1) If $M$ is (finitely) cogenerated by $\mathcal{U}$, there exists a (finite) indexed set $(U_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ in $\mathcal{U}$ and a monomorphism $f: M \to \Pi_A U_\alpha$. Given a monomorphism $g: M' \to M$, the composition $f \circ g: M' \to \Pi_A U_\alpha$ is also a monomorphism, so $M'$ is (finitely) cogenerated by $\mathcal{U}$.

(2) For each $\alpha \in A$, since $M_\alpha$ is (finitely) cogenerated by $\mathcal{U}$, there exists a monomorphism $f_\alpha: M_\alpha \to \Pi_{B_\alpha} U_{\beta_\alpha}$ with $U_{\beta_\alpha} \in \mathcal{U}$. The product of these monomorphisms gives a monomorphism

$$\Pi_A M_\alpha \to \Pi_A (\Pi_{B_\alpha} U_{\beta_\alpha}) \cong \Pi_C U_\gamma$$

where $C = \bigcup_A B_\alpha$. Hence $\Pi_A M_\alpha$ is (finitely) cogenerated by $\mathcal{U}$.
