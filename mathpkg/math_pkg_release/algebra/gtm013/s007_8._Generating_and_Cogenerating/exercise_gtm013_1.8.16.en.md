Recall that a commutative integral domain $R$ is a **principal ideal domain** (P.I.D.) in case every ideal of $R$ is principal. Of course $\mathbb{Z}$ is a P.I.D. Essentially all results for abelian groups extend to ones for modules over P.I.D.'s. Show that standard proofs for abelian groups extend to prove the following results for modules over a principal ideal domain $R$:

(1) Every finitely spanned torsion-free $R$-module $M$ is free. (Note: "torsion-free" means $ax = 0$ for $x \in M$ and $a \in R$ implies $a = 0$ or $x = 0$.)

(2) Every submodule of a free $R$-module is free.
