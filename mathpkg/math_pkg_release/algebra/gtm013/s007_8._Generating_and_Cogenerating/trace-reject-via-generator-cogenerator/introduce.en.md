Proposition 8.20 shows that the trace and reject with respect to a class $\mathcal{U}$ can be computed using any generator of $\mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$ and any cogenerator of $\mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$. Specifically, $\mathrm{Tr}_M(\mathcal{U}) = \mathrm{Tr}_M(G)$ for any generator $ of $\mathrm{Gen}(\mathcal{U})$, and $\mathrm{Rej}_M(\mathcal{U}) = \mathrm{Rej}_M(C)$ for any cogenerator $ of $\mathrm{Cog}(\mathcal{U})$. This is a powerful simplification, reducing computation to a single module.
