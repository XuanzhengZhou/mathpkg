[Morita] Let $R$ and $S$ be rings and let ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}_S$ be full subcategories of ${}_R \mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{M}_S$ such that
$${}_R R \in {}_R \mathcal{C} \quad \text{and} \quad S_S \in \mathcal{D}_S,$$
and such that every module in ${}_R \mathcal{M}$ (respectively, $\mathcal{M}_S$) isomorphic to one in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ ($\mathcal{D}_S$) is in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ ($\mathcal{D}_S$). If $(H', H'')$ is a duality between the categories ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}_S$,
$$H': {}_R \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}_S \quad \text{and} \quad H'': \mathcal{D}_S \to {}_R \mathcal{C},$$
then there is a bimodule ${}_R U_S$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(1)] ${}_R U \cong H''(S_S)$ and $U_S \cong H'({}_R R)$;
    \item[(2)] $H' \cong \operatorname{Hom}_R(-, U)$ and $H'' \cong \operatorname{Hom}_S(-, U)$;
    \item[(3)] Every module in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}_S$ is $U$-reflexive, so that ${}_R \mathcal{C} \subseteq {}_R \mathcal{R}[U]$ and $\mathcal{D}_S \subseteq \mathcal{R}_S[U]$.
\end{enumerate}
