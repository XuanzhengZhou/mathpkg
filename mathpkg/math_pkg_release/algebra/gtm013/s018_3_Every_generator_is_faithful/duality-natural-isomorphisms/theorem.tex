Let ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}_S$ be full subcategories of left $R$-modules and right $S$-modules, respectively. Let the pair of contravariant functors
$$H': {}_R \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}_S \quad \text{and} \quad H'': \mathcal{D}_S \to {}_R \mathcal{C}$$
be a duality between ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{D}_S$. Then
$$H''H' \cong 1_{{}_R \mathcal{C}} \quad \text{and} \quad H'H'' \cong 1_{\mathcal{D}_S},$$
i.e., there exist natural isomorphisms
$$\eta: H''H' \to 1_{{}_R \mathcal{C}} \quad \text{and} \quad \zeta: H'H'' \to 1_{\mathcal{D}_S}.$$
For each ${}_R M$ in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ there is an isomorphism $\eta_M: H''H'(M) \to M$ such that for each $M_1, M_2$ in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and each $R$-homomorphism $f: M_1 \to M_2$ the diagram
$$\begin{CD}
M_1 @>{f}>> M_2 \\
@A{\eta_{M_1}}AA @AA{\eta_{M_2}}A \\
H''H'(M_1) @>{H''H'(f)}>> H''H'(M_2)
\end{CD}$$
commutes. Similar remarks apply to $\zeta$. For each ${}_R M$ in ${}_R \mathcal{C}$ and each $N_S$ in $\mathcal{D}_S$ there are $\mathbb{Z}$-isomorphisms induced by the adjunction.
