This exercise examines the relationship between generators and faithful modules. While every generator is faithful, the converse is not generally true. Part (1) gives a criterion for when a projective module is a generator. Part (2) provides a counterexample showing faithful finitely generated projective modules need not be generators. Part (3) shows that in the commutative setting, faithfulness is equivalent to being a progenerator for finitely generated projective modules.
