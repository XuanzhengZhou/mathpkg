Let $p: P \to M$ and $q: Q \to M$ be epimorphisms where $P$ and $Q$ are projective modules. If $p$ is a projective cover, then:

(1) $Q$ has a direct summand $P'$ isomorphic to $P$;

(2) The restriction $q \mid P' : P' \to M$ is a projective cover;

(3) $Q = P' \oplus P''$ where $P'' \leq \operatorname{Ker} q$.

Moreover, if $f: M_1 \to M_2$ is an isomorphism and $p_1: P_1 \to M_1$ and $p_2: P_2 \to M_2$ are projective covers, then there is an isomorphism $\bar{f}: P_1 \to P_2$ such that $p_2 \bar{f} = f p_1$.
