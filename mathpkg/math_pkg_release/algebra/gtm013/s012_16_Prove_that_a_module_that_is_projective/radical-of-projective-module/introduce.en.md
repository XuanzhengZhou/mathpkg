For a projective module, the radical coincides with the product of the Jacobson radical of the ring with the module. This result generalizes the fact that for a free module $R^{(A)}$, one has $\\operatorname{Rad}(R^{(A)}) = J^{(A)}$, and extends it to all projective modules via their characterization as direct summands of free modules.
