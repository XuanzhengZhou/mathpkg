**Proof.** By (4.11) and (13.2) there is a natural ring isomorphism

$$\\rho: \\mathbb{M}_n(R) \\to \\operatorname{End}({}_R R^{(n)}).$$

Now $J R^{(n)} = J^{(n)}$ and clearly $\\rho([r_{ij}]) \\in \\operatorname{Hom}_R(R^{(n)}, J^{(n)})$ iff $[r_{ij}] \\in \\mathbb{M}_n(J)$. Apply (17.12).

For the other assertion, recall (4.15) that there is a natural isomorphism

$$\\rho: eRe \\to \\operatorname{End}({}_R Re)$$

and clearly $\\rho(ere) \\in \\operatorname{Hom}_R(Re, Je)$ iff $ere \\in J$. Apply (17.12).
