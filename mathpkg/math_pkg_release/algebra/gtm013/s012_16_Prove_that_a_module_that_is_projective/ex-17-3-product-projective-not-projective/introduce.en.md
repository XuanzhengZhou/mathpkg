While direct sums of projective modules are projective, the same does not hold for direct products. The countably infinite product $\mathbb{Z}^{\mathbb{N}}$ of copies of the integers is a concrete counterexample: it is not a projective $\mathbb{Z}$-module, even though each factor $\mathbb{Z}$ is projective (indeed free). This exercise demonstrates a fundamental failure of completeness for projectivity under products.
