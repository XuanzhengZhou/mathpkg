A module is projective (or injective) modulo its annihilator if it becomes projective (or injective) when considered as a module over the quotient ring by its annihilator ideal. This exercise establishes equivalent characterizations, including a condition on $U^A$-projectivity (and injectivity) for all index sets $A$, connecting the modulo-annihilator property to the ability to lift (or extend) families of homomorphisms.
