Let ${}_R U_S$ be a bimodule and let $(M_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$ be an indexed set of left $R$-modules.

(1) If $(M, (q_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A})$ is a direct product of $(M_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$, then

$$(Hom_R(U_S, M), (Hom_R(U_S, q_\alpha))_{\alpha \in A})$$

is a direct product of the left $S$-modules $(Hom_R(U_S, M_\alpha))_{\alpha \in A}$.

(2) If $(M, (j_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A})$ is a direct sum of $(M_\alpha)_{\alpha \in A}$, then

$$(Hom_R(M, U_S), (Hom_R(j_\alpha, U_S))_{\alpha \in A})$$

is a direct product of the right $S$-modules $(Hom_R(M_\alpha, U_S))_{\alpha \in A}$.
