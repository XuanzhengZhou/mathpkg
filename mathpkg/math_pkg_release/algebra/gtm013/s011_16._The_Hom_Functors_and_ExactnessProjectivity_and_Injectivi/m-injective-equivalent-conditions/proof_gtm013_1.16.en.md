The proof is dual to that of Proposition 16.7. By (16.6), the sequence $0 \to Hom_R(N, U) \xrightarrow{g^*} Hom_R(M, U) \xrightarrow{f^*} Hom_R(K, U)$ is always exact. Condition (b) holds precisely when $f^*$ is epic. That $f^*$ is epic means that for each $\gamma: K \to U$, there exists $\bar{\gamma}: M \to U$ with $\gamma = f^*(\bar{\gamma}) = \bar{\gamma}f$, which is exactly the definition of $U$ being $M$-injective. Thus (a) $\Leftrightarrow$ (b).
