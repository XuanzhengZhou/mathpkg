Let $R$ and $S$ be rings and let $U = {}_R U_S$ be a bimodule. The function

$$Hom_R(U, -): {}_R\mathcal{M} \to {}_S\mathcal{M}$$

is defined on objects by $M \mapsto Hom_R(U, M)$ and on morphisms $f: {}_R M \to {}_R N$ by

$$Hom_R(U, f): \gamma \mapsto f\gamma$$

for all $\gamma \in Hom_R(U, M)$, yielding an $S$-homomorphism $Hom_R(U, f): Hom_R(U, M) \to Hom_R(U, N)$.

The function

$$Hom_R(-, U): {}_R\mathcal{M} \to \mathcal{M}_S$$

is defined on objects by $M \mapsto Hom_R(M, U)$ and on morphisms $f: {}_R M \to {}_R N$ by

$$Hom_R(f, U): \gamma \mapsto \gamma f$$

for all $\gamma \in Hom_R(N, U)$, yielding an $S$-homomorphism $Hom_R(f, U): Hom_R(N, U) \to Hom_R(M, U)$.
