A left exact functor is a functor between module categories that preserves exactness of short exact sequences at the leftmost (and middle) terms. For a covariant functor $F$, left exactness means $0 \to F(K) \to F(M) \to F(N)$ is exact whenever $0 \to K \to M \to N \to 0$ is exact. For a contravariant functor $G$, the condition becomes $0 \to G(N) \to G(M) \to G(K)$ exact. The Hom functors are the prototypical examples of left exact functors.
