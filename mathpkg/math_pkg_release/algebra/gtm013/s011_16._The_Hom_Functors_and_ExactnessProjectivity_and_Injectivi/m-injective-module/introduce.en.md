A module $U$ is $M$-injective (injective relative to $M$) if every homomorphism from a submodule of $M$ to $U$ extends to a homomorphism defined on all of $M$. This relativizes the usual notion of injectivity: $U$ is injective if and only if it is $M$-injective for all modules $M$. The $M$-injectivity concept corresponds to exactness of the contravariant Hom functor $Hom_R(-, U)$ on sequences with middle term $M$.
