Let $C$ and $D$ be full subcategories of categories of modules and let $F: C \to D$ be a covariant functor. $F$ is said to be **right exact** if for every short exact sequence in $C$

$$0 \to K \to M \to N \to 0$$

the sequence

$$F(K) \to F(M) \to F(N) \to 0$$

is exact in $D$.

In the contravariant case, a functor $G: C \to D$ is **right exact** if for every short exact sequence $0 \to K \to M \to N \to 0$ in $C$, the sequence

$$G(N) \to G(M) \to G(K) \to 0$$

is exact in $D$.
