This theorem characterizes $R$-modules that admit a $Q$-vector space structure: they are precisely the divisible and torsion-free $R$-modules. The scalar multiplication by fractions $r/s \in Q$ is well-defined because torsion-freeness ensures injectivity of multiplication by $s$, and divisibility ensures surjectivity, allowing division by $s$. This is a fundamental result connecting module theory over domains to linear algebra over their fields of fractions.
