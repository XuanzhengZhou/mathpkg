Over a commutative integral domain $R$, the torsion submodule $T(M)$ of an $R$-module $M$ consists of all elements $x$ whose annihilator $I_R(x)$ is nontrivial; equivalently, elements for which there exists a nonzero $r \in R$ with $rx = 0$. The set $T(M)$ forms a submodule of $M$, and this decomposition is fundamental to the structure theory of modules over domains.
