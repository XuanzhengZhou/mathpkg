The Prüfer $p$-group is one of the standard examples of a divisible abelian group, alongside $\mathbb{Q}$. Its divisibility follows from the fact that every element lies in some finite cyclic subgroup $\mathbb{Z}/p^n\mathbb{Z}$ and the equation $ax = b$ can be solved within a large enough cyclic subgroup.
