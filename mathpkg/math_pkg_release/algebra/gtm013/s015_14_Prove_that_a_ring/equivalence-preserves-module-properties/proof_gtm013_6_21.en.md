Each of these is simply an assertion about the lattices of submodules of $M$ and $F(M)$, which by 21.6/21.7 are isomorphic via $\Lambda_M$.

(1) $M$ is simple iff its submodule lattice has exactly two elements. Since $\Lambda_M$ is a lattice isomorphism, $F(M)$ has the same property. Semisimplicity means every submodule is a direct summand; this lattice-theoretic property is preserved under the isomorphism.

(2) $M$ is finitely generated iff its submodule lattice satisfies the ascending chain condition for finitely generated submodules. The lattice isomorphism preserves this property. Dually for finite cogeneration.

(3) The artinian (noetherian) condition is the descending (ascending) chain condition on the submodule lattice. The lattice isomorphism preserves chain conditions.

(4) Composition length $c(M)$ is the length of a maximal chain in the submodule lattice (a composition series). The lattice isomorphism sends composition series to composition series with the same length.

(5) $M$ is indecomposable iff its submodule lattice has exactly two complemented elements (0 and $M$). The lattice isomorphism preserves complemented elements.
