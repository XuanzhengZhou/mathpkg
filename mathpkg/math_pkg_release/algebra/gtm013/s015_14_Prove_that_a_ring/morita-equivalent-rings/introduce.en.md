Two rings are Morita equivalent if their categories of left modules are equivalent as additive categories. This is the central concept of Morita theory, which characterizes when two rings have "the same" representation theory. The Wedderburn Theorem for simple artinian rings can be interpreted as stating that $R$ is simple artinian if and only if $R$ is Morita equivalent to a division ring $D$. The equivalence of left module categories implies equivalence of right module categories.
