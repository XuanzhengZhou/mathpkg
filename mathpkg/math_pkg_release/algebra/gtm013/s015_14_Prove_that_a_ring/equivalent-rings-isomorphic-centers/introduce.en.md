This proposition shows that Morita equivalence preserves the center of a ring and the lattice of two-sided ideals. The proof constructs explicit lattice isomorphisms between the ideal lattices using the equivalence data. The center isomorphism follows because the center of $R$ can be identified with the ring of natural endomorphisms of the identity functor on ${}_R \mathbf{M}$, which is preserved by equivalence. This is a striking result: rings with non-isomorphic centers cannot be Morita equivalent.
