This exercise establishes an equivalence between two fundamental module-theoretic properties of a ring. A ring $R$ is von Neumann regular (every element $a \in R$ satisfies $a \in aRa$) precisely when every finitely presented left $R$-module is projective. The forward direction relies on the fact that over a von Neumann regular ring, every module is flat, and finitely presented flat modules are projective. The converse uses the characterization of von Neumann regularity via the condition that every cyclic left ideal is a direct summand.
