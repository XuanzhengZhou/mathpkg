The First Isomorphism Theorem is the cornerstone of module theory. It states that every homomorphism induces an isomorphism between the quotient by its kernel and its image. This theorem unifies the concepts of quotient modules and homomorphic images, and is used throughout algebra to identify quotient constructions with submodules of target modules.
