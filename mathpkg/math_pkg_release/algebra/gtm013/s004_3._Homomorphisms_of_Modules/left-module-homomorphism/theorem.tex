Let $M$ and $N$ be left $R$-modules. A function $f: M \to N$ is a \textbf{left $R$-homomorphism} (or $R$-linear map) if for all $a, b \in R$ and all $x, y \in M$,
$$f(ax + by) = af(x) + bf(y).$$
Equivalently, if $M$ and $N$ are left $R$-modules via ring homomorphisms $\lambda, \lambda': R \to \text{End}(M), \text{End}(N)$ respectively, an abelian group homomorphism $f: M \to N$ is an $R$-homomorphism iff for each $a \in R$ the diagram
$$\begin{CD}
M @>f>> N \\
@V\lambda(a)VV @VV\lambda'(a)V \\
M @>f>> N
\end{CD}$$
commutes.
