A pair of homomorphisms
$$M' \xrightarrow{f} M \xrightarrow{g} M''$$
is \textbf{exact at $M$} if $\text{Im}\, f = \text{Ker}\, g$. A (finite or infinite) sequence of homomorphisms
$$\cdots \xrightarrow{f_{n-1}} M_{n-1} \xrightarrow{f_n} M_n \xrightarrow{f_{n+1}} M_{n+1} \xrightarrow{f_{n+2}} \cdots$$
is \textbf{exact} if it is exact at each $M_n$, i.e., for each successive pair,
$$\text{Im}\, f_n = \text{Ker}\, f_{n+1}.$$
