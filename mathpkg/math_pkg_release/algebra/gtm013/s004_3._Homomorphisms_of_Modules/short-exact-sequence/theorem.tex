An exact sequence of the form
$$0 \to K \xrightarrow{f} M \xrightarrow{g} N \to 0$$
is called a \textbf{short exact sequence}. In such a sequence, $f$ is a monomorphism and $g$ is an epimorphism. Every short exact sequence is isomorphic to one of the form
$$0 \to M' \xrightarrow{i} M \xrightarrow{n} M/M' \to 0$$
where $i$ is the inclusion of a submodule and $n$ is the natural epimorphism. A short exact sequence $0 \to K \xrightarrow{f} M \xrightarrow{g} N \to 0$ is also called an \textbf{extension} of $K$ by $N$.
