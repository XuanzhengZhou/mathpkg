A short exact sequence is a five-term exact sequence beginning and ending with zero. It captures the idea that $M$ is built from $K$ and $N$: $K$ embeds as a submodule of $M$, and $N$ is the quotient. Every short exact sequence is isomorphic to a canonical one involving a submodule and its quotient. Short exact sequences are also called extensions, reflecting the idea of extending $K$ by $N$ to obtain $M$.
