For any two left $R$-modules $M$ and $N$, the \textbf{zero homomorphism} $0: M \to N$ is defined by
$$0(x) = 0 \in N \qquad (x \in M).$$
There is a unique homomorphism $M \to 0$ (necessarily epic) and a unique homomorphism $0 \to M$ (necessarily monic). The natural maps $M \to M/0$ and $M/M \to 0$ are isomorphisms.
