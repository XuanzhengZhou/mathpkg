Proposition 2.11 describes how submodule lattices behave under a ring homomorphism $\phi: R \to S$. When an abelian group $M$ carries both $R$- and $S$-module structures compatible via $\phi$, the $S$-submodule lattice is a sublattice of the $R$-submodule lattice, which equals the $\phi(R)$-submodule lattice, which in turn is a sublattice of the $\mathbb{Z}$-submodule lattice. This reflects the fact that having more scalars imposes finer submodule structure.
