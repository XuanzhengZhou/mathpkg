A linear combination of a subset $X$ of a module $M$ with coefficients in $A \subseteq R$ is a finite sum $\sum_{i=1}^n a_i x_i$ where $a_i \in A$ and $x_i \in X$. The set of all such combinations is denoted $AX$, extending the scalar multiplication notation. Linear combinations are the fundamental tool for describing submodules generated by subsets of a module.
