Annihilators provide a tool for translating between properties of a ring $R$ and properties of an $R$-module $M$. The left annihilator $l_R(X)$ of a subset $X \subseteq M$ consists of all ring elements that send every element of $X$ to zero, while the right annihilator $r_M(A)$ of $A \subseteq R$ consists of all module elements annihilated by every element of $A$. The maps $l_R$ and $r_M$ are order-reversing and form a Galois connection.
