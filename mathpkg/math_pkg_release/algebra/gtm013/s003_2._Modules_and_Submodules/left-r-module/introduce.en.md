A left $R$-module is an abelian group $M$ together with a ring homomorphism $\lambda: R \to \operatorname{End}^l(M)$. This endows each ring element $a \in R$ with an action on $M$ as a left group endomorphism $\lambda(a)(x)$, usually written simply as $ax$. The definition generalizes the notion of a vector space over a field to modules over an arbitrary (not necessarily commutative) ring.
