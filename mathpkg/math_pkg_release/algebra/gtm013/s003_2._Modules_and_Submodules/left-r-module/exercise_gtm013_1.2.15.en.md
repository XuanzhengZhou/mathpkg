Let $R$ be the subring of $\mathbb{Z}[X] \times \mathbb{Z}[Y]$ of all $(f, g)$ with $f(0) = g(0)$ (i.e., all pairs with the same constant term). Note that $R$ is isomorphic to the factor ring of $\mathbb{Z}[X, Y]$ modulo the principal ideal generated by $X Y$. Let $M = {}_R R$. Show that the inclusions of (2.16.2) can be strict even if we choose the $K_\alpha$'s to be annihilators of ideals and the $I_\alpha$'s to be annihilators of submodules. [Hint: Consider the submodule generated by $(X, 0)$.]
