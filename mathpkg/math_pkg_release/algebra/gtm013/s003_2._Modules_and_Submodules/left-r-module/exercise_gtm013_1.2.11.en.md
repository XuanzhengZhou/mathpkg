Let $R$ be commutative. A proper ideal $P$ of $R$ is \textbf{prime} if $a b \in P$ implies that $a \in P$ or $b \in P$. Prove that:

(1) A proper ideal $P$ is prime if and only if the factor ring $R/P$ is an integral domain. Thus every maximal ideal is prime.

(2) There exist chains of prime ideals of arbitrary length.
