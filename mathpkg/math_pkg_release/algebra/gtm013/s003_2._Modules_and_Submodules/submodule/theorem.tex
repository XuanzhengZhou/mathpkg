Let $M$ be a left $R$-module. A subset $N \subseteq M$ is a \textbf{submodule} of $M$, denoted $N \leq M$, if $N$ is a subgroup of the additive group of $M$ that is closed under scalar multiplication by $R$. Equivalently, $N$ is a submodule of $M$ if and only if for all $a, b \in R$ and $x, y \in N$,

$$ax + by \in N.$$

A submodule $N$ of $M$ is a left $R$-module in its own right. Note that it is possible for a subgroup $N$ of $M$ to be an $R$-module via some representation $R \to \operatorname{End}^l(N)$ without being an $R$-submodule of $M$.
