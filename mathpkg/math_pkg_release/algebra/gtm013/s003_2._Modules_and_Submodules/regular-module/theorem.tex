The additive group of a ring $R$ is both a left $R$-module and a right $R$-module via left and right ring multiplications. Since ring multiplication is associative,

$$a(xb) = (ax)b,$$

these left and right multiplications give $R$ the structure of a bimodule. When we speak of a ring $R$ as a module (left, right, or two-sided) over itself, we mean this \textbf{regular module} structure, denoted

$$_R R, \quad R_R, \quad \text{and} \quad {}_R R_R.$$
