The completion of a filtered object is a two-step construction: first take the colimit of the increasing filtration to obtain $X^\infty$, then take the limit of the resulting quotient system to obtain $Y = (X^\infty)_{-\infty}$. This process yields a filtered object that is always complete, regardless of whether the original filtration was. The construction is motivated by the need for completeness in Theorem 8.4: if a filtration is not complete, one can pass to its completion without changing the associated graded object.
