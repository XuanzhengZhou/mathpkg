Theorem 7.3 is the central structural result of the section on limits of Rees systems. A Rees system is a diagram constructed from an exact couple that encodes a spectral sequence. By passing to the limit (and colimit) of the successive derived Rees systems, one obtains a commutative diagram relating the cokernel of $\bar{\alpha}'$, the $E_\infty$ term, the kernel of $\alpha''$, and the target $\Gamma^+$. The exactness of the rows and columns of this limit diagram is the key to understanding when the spectral sequence converges, i.e., when $E_\infty \cong \Gamma^+$.
