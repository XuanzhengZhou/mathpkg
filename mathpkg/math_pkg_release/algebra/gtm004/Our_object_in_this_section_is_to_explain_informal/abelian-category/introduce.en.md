An abelian category is the proper setting for homological algebra. It enriches an additive category with the ability to take kernels and cokernels, and requires that every monomorphism is a kernel and every epimorphism is a cokernel (normality), and that every morphism factors as an epimorphism followed by a monomorphism. Standard examples include module categories, sheaf categories, and categories of graded modules. The concept is self-dual, making abelian categories ideal for the duality principle.
