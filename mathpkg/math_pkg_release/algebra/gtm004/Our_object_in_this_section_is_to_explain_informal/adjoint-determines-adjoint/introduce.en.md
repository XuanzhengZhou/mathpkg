Adjoint functors are essentially unique: if a functor has a left (or right) adjoint, that adjoint is determined up to natural equivalence. This is analogous to the statement that a universal object, if it exists, is unique up to unique isomorphism. In fact, adjunctions are precisely the "optimal" solutions to universal mapping problems, making this uniqueness a fundamental structural property of categories.
