In a category with a zero object, the kernel of a morphism $\sigma$ is the universal morphism whose composite with $\sigma$ is zero. It is the categorical abstraction of the kernel of a group or module homomorphism. Kernels, when they exist, are always monomorphisms — a consequence of the uniqueness in the universal property. The dual notion is the cokernel, obtained by reversing all arrows.
