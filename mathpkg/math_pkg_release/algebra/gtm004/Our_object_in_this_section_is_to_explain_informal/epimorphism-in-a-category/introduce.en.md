An epimorphism is the categorical abstraction of a surjective function, defined by right-cancellation. In concrete categories like sets or modules, epimorphisms correspond to surjective maps; however, in some categories (e.g., rings, integral domains), there exist epimorphisms that are not surjective on underlying sets. Epimorphisms are precisely the monomorphisms in the opposite category, forming the foundation of the duality principle.
