Let $F \dashv G$ with $F: \mathfrak{C} \to \mathfrak{D}$ and $G: \mathfrak{D} \to \mathfrak{C}$. Then $G$ preserves limits (products, pull-backs, kernels, equalizers) and $F$ preserves colimits (coproducts, push-outs, cokernels, coequalizers). In particular:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] If $(Y; \alpha, \beta)$ is a pull-back of $\varphi, \psi$ in $\mathfrak{D}$, then $(GY; G\alpha, G\beta)$ is a pull-back of $G\varphi, G\psi$ in $\mathfrak{C}$;
\item[(ii)] $G$ preserves kernels.
\end{enumerate}
