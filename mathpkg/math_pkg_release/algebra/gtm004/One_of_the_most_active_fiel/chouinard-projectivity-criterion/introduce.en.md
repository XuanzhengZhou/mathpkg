Chouinard's criterion (Corollary 4.4) is a striking detection result: a $kG$-module is projective if and only if its restriction to every elementary abelian $p$-subgroup is projective. It follows as an immediate corollary of the Alperin-Evens-Carlson theorem via the fact that complexity zero characterises projectivity. The result was actually obtained earlier than Theorem 4.3 and was historically surprising, showing that the seemingly large class of all $p$-subgroups can be reduced to the much smaller class of elementary abelian ones for detecting projectivity.
