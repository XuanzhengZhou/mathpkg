The limit of a functor generalizes notions such as products, pullbacks, and inverse limits to an arbitrary category. It is defined via the right adjoint to the diagonal functor, with the counit providing the universal cone. This definition, based on Section II.8, unifies many categorical constructions and is essential for the treatment of limits of spectral sequences in homological algebra.
