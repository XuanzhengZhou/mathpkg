By definition, $H^0(G, A) = \operatorname{Hom}_G(\mathbb{Z}, A)$. Since $\mathbb{Z}$ is generated by $1$ as a $\mathbb{Z}G$-module, a $G$-module homomorphism $\mathbb{Z} \to A$ is determined by the image of $1$, which must be $G$-invariant. Thus $H^0(G, A)$ is canonically isomorphic to the subgroup of invariants $A^G$. This gives the first concrete interpretation of a cohomology group.
