A (left) $G$-module is an abelian group $A$ together with a group homomorphism $\sigma: G \to \operatorname{Aut} A$. Equivalently, via the universal property of the group ring, a $G$-module is a (left) module over the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}G$. A $G$-module $A$ is called \emph{trivial} if the structure map $\sigma$ is trivial, i.e.\ if every $x \in G$ acts as the identity on $A$. Every abelian group may be regarded as a trivial left or right $G$-module for any group $G$.
