# Exercise 1.5

Prove Corollary 1.4 (every projective/injective $G$-module is a projective/injective $U$-module for any subgroup $U$ of $G$) without appealing to the theory of adjoint functors.

*Hint:* Use the fact that $\mathbb{Z}G$ is a free $\mathbb{Z}U$-module with basis a set of coset representatives of $U$ in $G$. Then a free $G$-module restricts to a free $U$-module, and a projective $G$-module (as a direct summand of a free $G$-module) restricts to a projective $U$-module.
