# Proof of Induced Isomorphism for an Exact Sequence of Lie Algebras

**Corollary 1.6.** If $\mathfrak{n} \xrightarrow{} \mathfrak{g} \xrightarrow{} \mathfrak{h}$ is an exact sequence of Lie algebras over $K$ (i.e., $\mathfrak{n}$ is a Lie ideal of $\mathfrak{g}$ and $\mathfrak{h} \cong \mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{n}$), then

$$K \otimes_{U\mathfrak{n}} U\mathfrak{g} \cong U\mathfrak{h}$$

as right $\mathfrak{g}$-modules. Here $K$ is regarded as the trivial $U\mathfrak{n}$-module via the augmentation map $U\mathfrak{n} \to K$.

**Proof.** The exact sequence $\mathfrak{n} \to \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{h}$ induces, by the universal property of the universal enveloping algebra, an algebra homomorphism $U\mathfrak{g} \to U\mathfrak{h}$ whose kernel contains the two-sided ideal generated by the image of $\mathfrak{n}$ in $U\mathfrak{g}$. More precisely, the composition $\mathfrak{n} \hookrightarrow \mathfrak{g} \xrightarrow{i} U\mathfrak{g}$ yields a map $U\mathfrak{n} \to U\mathfrak{g}$, and the ideal of $U\mathfrak{g}$ generated by the image of the augmentation ideal of $U\mathfrak{n}$ is precisely the kernel of $U\mathfrak{g} \to U\mathfrak{h}$.

Consider the tensor product $K \otimes_{U\mathfrak{n}} U\mathfrak{g}$. The left $U\mathfrak{n}$-module structure on $K$ is given by the augmentation $\varepsilon : U\mathfrak{n} \to K$, which sends all elements of $\mathfrak{n}$ to $0$. Thus

$$K \otimes_{U\mathfrak{n}} U\mathfrak{g} \cong U\mathfrak{g} / (\mathfrak{n} \cdot U\mathfrak{g})$$

where $\mathfrak{n} \cdot U\mathfrak{g}$ denotes the left ideal generated by the image of $\mathfrak{n}$ in $U\mathfrak{g}$.

By the universal property of $U\mathfrak{h}$, there is a unique algebra homomorphism $\varphi : U\mathfrak{g} \to U\mathfrak{h}$ extending the Lie algebra homomorphism $\mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{h}$. The kernel of $\varphi$ is the two-sided ideal generated by $\mathfrak{n}$. Hence

$$U\mathfrak{g} / (\mathfrak{n} \cdot U\mathfrak{g}) \cong U\mathfrak{h}$$

as algebras, and in particular as right $U\mathfrak{g}$-modules (where the right $U\mathfrak{g}$-module structure on $U\mathfrak{h}$ comes from the surjection $U\mathfrak{g} \to U\mathfrak{h}$).

Therefore $K \otimes_{U\mathfrak{n}} U\mathfrak{g} \cong U\mathfrak{h}$ as right $\mathfrak{g}$-modules. $\square$
