# Proof of Unit Embedding of a Lie Algebra into Its Universal Enveloping Algebra

**Corollary 1.3.** The unit map $i : \mathfrak{g} \to LU\mathfrak{g}$ is an embedding.

(Here $LU\mathfrak{g}$ denotes the Lie algebra associated to the associative algebra $U\mathfrak{g}$, with Lie bracket given by the commutator $[a, b] = ab - ba$.)

**Proof.** This is an immediate consequence of Theorem 1.2 (the Birkhoff--Witt Theorem).

Choose an ordered basis $\{e_i\}_{i \in J}$ of the $K$-vector space $\mathfrak{g}$. By Theorem 1.2, the monomials

$$e_{i_1} e_{i_2} \cdots e_{i_k}$$

with $i_1 \leq i_2 \leq \cdots \leq i_k$ ranging over all finite increasing sequences in $J$ form a $K$-basis of $U\mathfrak{g}$. In particular, the elements $i(e_j) = e_j$ (corresponding to sequences of length $1$) are linearly independent in $U\mathfrak{g}$.

Since $\{e_j\}_{j \in J}$ is a basis of $\mathfrak{g}$, the map $i$ sends a basis of $\mathfrak{g}$ to a linearly independent subset of $U\mathfrak{g}$. Therefore $i$ is injective.

To verify that $i$ is a Lie algebra homomorphism: By the definition of $U\mathfrak{g} = T\mathfrak{g}/I$, where $I$ is the ideal generated by $x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [x, y]$, the canonical map $\mathfrak{g} \to T\mathfrak{g} \to U\mathfrak{g}$ satisfies

$$i([x, y]) = [x, y] + I = x \otimes y - y \otimes x + I = i(x)i(y) - i(y)i(x) = [i(x), i(y)]$$

in $LU\mathfrak{g}$. Hence $i$ is an injective Lie algebra homomorphism, i.e., an embedding. $\square$
