\begin{theorem}[Theorem 4.1]
Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an abelian category. The $Q$-processes commute:
\[
Q^{\varepsilon} Q_{\sigma} = Q_{\sigma} Q^{\varepsilon}.
\]
Consequently, the object $B_1$ obtained by first applying $Q^{\varepsilon}$ and then $Q_{\sigma}$ coincides with the object $\bar{B}_1$ obtained by first applying $Q_{\sigma}$ and then $Q^{\varepsilon}$. More precisely,
\[
B_1 = \bar{B}_1 = \gamma^{-1}(C_1) / \beta\sigma^{-1}(0),
\]
where $B_1$ is obtained by first cutting down to the subobject $\gamma^{-1}(C_1)$ and then factoring out $\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$, while $\bar{B}_1$ is obtained by first factoring out $\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$ and then cutting down to the subobject corresponding to $\gamma^{-1}(C_1)$.
\end{theorem}
