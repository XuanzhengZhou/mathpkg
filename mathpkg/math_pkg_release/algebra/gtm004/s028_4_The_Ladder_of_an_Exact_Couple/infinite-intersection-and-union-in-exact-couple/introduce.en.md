In the study of exact couples and spectral sequences, the subobjects $\alpha^{\infty}D = \bigcap_n \alpha^n D$ and $\alpha^{-\infty}(0) = \bigcup_m \alpha^{-m}(0)$ capture the "eventual" behavior of the differential $\alpha$. The infinite intersection $\alpha^{\infty}D$ consists of elements that survive arbitrarily many applications of $\alpha$, while the infinite union $\alpha^{-\infty}(0)$ consists of all elements eventually annihilated by some power of $\alpha$. These two subobjects are fundamental for expressing the $E_{\infty}$ page of the spectral sequence in concrete terms, as they replace the abstract categorical limits with set-theoretic intersections and unions in module categories.
