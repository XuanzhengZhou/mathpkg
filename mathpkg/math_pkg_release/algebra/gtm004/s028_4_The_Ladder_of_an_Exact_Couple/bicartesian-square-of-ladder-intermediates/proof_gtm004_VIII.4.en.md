# Proof of Bicartesian square of ladder intermediate objects

**Proposition 4.2.** The square

$$\begin{CD}
B_{0,1} @>{\sigma_{0,1}}>> B_1 \\
@V{\varrho_{0,1}}VV @VV{\sigma_{1,0}}V \\
B @>{\varrho}>> B_{1,0}
\end{CD}$$

is bicartesian (i.e., a pull-back and push-out).

**Proof.** The square may be written

$$\begin{CD}
\gamma^{-1}(C_1) @>>> \gamma^{-1}(C_1)/\beta\sigma^{-1}(0) \\
@VVV @VVV \\
B @>>> B/\beta\sigma^{-1}(0)
\end{CD}$$

with the obvious morphisms, and this is plainly bicartesian. $\square$
