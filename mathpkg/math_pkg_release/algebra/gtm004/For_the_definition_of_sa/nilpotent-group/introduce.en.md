A nilpotent group is one whose lower central series terminates at the trivial subgroup after finitely many steps. The nilpotency class measures how many commutator layers are needed to reach the identity. A group of class 1 is abelian. Every nilpotent group can be built up from an abelian group by a finite sequence of central extensions, which makes them amenable to inductive homological arguments and localization techniques.
