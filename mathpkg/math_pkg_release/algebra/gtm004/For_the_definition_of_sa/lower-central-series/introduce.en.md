The lower central series of a group $G$ is a descending chain of subgroups defined iteratively via commutators. Each term $\gamma^{i+1}(G)$ is the subgroup generated by commutators $[g, x]$ where $g \in G$ and $x \in \gamma^i(G)$. The series measures the deviation of $G$ from being abelian, and the successive quotients $\gamma^i(G)/\gamma^{i+1}(G)$ are always abelian and lie in the center of $G/\gamma^{i+1}(G)$.
