This theorem is a cornerstone of the structure theory of modules over principal ideal domains. It generalizes the classical fact that subgroups of free abelian groups are free abelian. The proof uses well-ordering of the index set and the fact that every ideal in a PID is principal to construct a basis for the submodule. This result implies that PIDs are hereditary rings: submodules of projective modules are projective, which in turn yields that every module over a PID has a projective resolution of length at most 1.
