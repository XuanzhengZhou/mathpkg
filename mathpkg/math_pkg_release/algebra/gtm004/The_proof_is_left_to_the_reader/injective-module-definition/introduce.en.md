Injective modules are the dual notion to projective modules, obtained by reversing all arrows in the defining diagram. While projective modules allow maps out of them to be lifted along surjections, injective modules allow maps into them to be extended along monomorphisms. This duality is the conceptual heart of Section 6, demonstrating how the process of dualization applied to the notion of projectivity yields a new and equally important class of modules. Injective modules play a central role in homological algebra, particularly in the construction of injective resolutions and the definition of right derived functors.
