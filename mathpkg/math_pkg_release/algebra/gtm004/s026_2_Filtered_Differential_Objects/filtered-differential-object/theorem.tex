Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an abelian category and let $(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ be the category of differential objects in $\mathfrak{A}$. An object $C$ of $(\mathfrak{A}, d)$ is said to be a \text{filtered differential object} if it is filtered by subobjects (of $(\mathfrak{A}, d)$)

$$\cdots \subseteq C^{(p-1)} \subseteq C^{(p)} \subseteq \cdots \subseteq C, \quad -\infty < p < \infty,$$

such that each $C^{(p)}$ is closed under the differential $d$ on $C$, i.e.,

$$dC^{(p)} \subseteq C^{(p)}.$$
