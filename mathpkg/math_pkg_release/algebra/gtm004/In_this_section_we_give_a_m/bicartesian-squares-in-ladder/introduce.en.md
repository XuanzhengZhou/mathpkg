Theorem 4.6, a consequence of Proposition 4.2, states that each square in the ladder grid connecting $E_{m,n}$, $E_{m+1,n}$, $E_{m,n+1}$, and $E_{m+1,n+1}$ is bicartesian. This structural property enables the description of $E_{\infty}$ as a double limit: $E_{\infty} = \lim_n \lim_m E_{m,n}$, where the inner limit is taken with respect to the $\sigma$ morphisms and the outer with respect to the $\varrho$ morphisms.
