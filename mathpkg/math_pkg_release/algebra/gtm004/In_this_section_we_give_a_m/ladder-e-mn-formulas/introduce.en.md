These formulas express the ladder terms $E_{m,n}$ and their limits in terms of the original exact couple data $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$. The finite term $E_{m,n}$ is the subquotient $\gamma^{-1}(\alpha^n D)/\beta\alpha^{-m}(0)$. Taking the limit over $n$ (the $\varrho$ direction) yields $E_{m,\infty}$, while the limit over $m$ (the $\sigma$ direction) yields $E_{\infty,n}$. The double limit $E_{\infty}$ is the subquotient $\gamma^{-1}(\alpha^{\infty}D)/\beta\alpha^{-\infty}(0)$, where $\alpha^{\infty}D$ is the intersection of all $\alpha^n D$ and $\alpha^{-\infty}(0)$ is the union of all $\alpha^{-m}(0)$.
