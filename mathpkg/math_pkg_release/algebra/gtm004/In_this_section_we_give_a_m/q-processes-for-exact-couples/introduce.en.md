The Q-processes are categorical constructions on a two-term sequence $A \to B \to C$ in an abelian category. Given a monomorphism $\varrho: C_1 \rightarrowtail C$ through which $\gamma\beta$ factors, the $Q^{\varrho}$-process "cuts down" $B$ via pullback to $\gamma^{-1}(C_1)$. Dually, given an epimorphism $\sigma: A \twoheadrightarrow A_1$ through which $\gamma\beta$ factors, the $Q_{\sigma}$-process "factors out" via pushout by $B / \beta\sigma^{-1}(0)$. These processes provide the fundamental mechanism for deriving successive exact couples in the ladder construction.
