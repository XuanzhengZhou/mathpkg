The trivial map from $G$ into the integers $\mathbb{Z}$, sending every $x \in G$ to $1 \in \mathbb{Z}$, gives rise to a unique ring homomorphism
$$\varepsilon: \mathbb{Z}G \to \mathbb{Z},$$
called the \emph{augmentation} of $\mathbb{Z}G$. Explicitly,
$$\varepsilon\left( \sum_{x \in G} m(x) x \right) = \sum_{x \in G} m(x).$$
