Formula (4.6) computes $H^1(G, A)$ when $A$ is a trivial $G$-module. Since $\iota^*$ is the zero map in this case, $H^1(G, A) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_G(IG, A)$. Using Lemma 4.1 ($IG/(IG)^2 \cong G_{\mathrm{ab}}$), this simplifies to $\operatorname{Hom}(G_{\mathrm{ab}}, A)$. Thus $H^1$ with trivial coefficients classifies group homomorphisms from the abelianization to $A$, providing a bridge between cohomology and classical abelian group theory.
