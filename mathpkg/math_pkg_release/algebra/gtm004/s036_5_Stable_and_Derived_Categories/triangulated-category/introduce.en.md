The structure of a triangulated category, discovered by J.L. Verdier, is the essential framework for derived categories. While the homotopy category of complexes is not abelian, it carries this triangulated structure, which provides enough formalism to develop homological algebra. The translation functor $T$ (degree shift) and distinguished triangles (generalizing short exact sequences) give triangulated categories a calculus that replaces exactness arguments in non-abelian settings. This structure is preserved under localization at quasi-isomorphisms, making the derived category itself triangulated.
