Let $\mathfrak{A}, \mathfrak{B}$ be abelian categories and let $F : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ be an additive functor. The {\it right derived functor} of $F$ in the sense of derived categories is a functor
\[
RF : \mathfrak{D}^+(\mathfrak{A}) \to \mathfrak{D}^+(\mathfrak{B})
\]
satisfying a universal property with respect to the localization functor. If $F$ is induced from a left exact additive functor $F' : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{B}$ and $\mathfrak{A}$ has enough injectives, then the $n$th classical right derived functor coincides with $H^n(RF)$, where $H^n$ denotes cohomology in degree $n$:
\[
R^n F'(A) = H^n(RF(A)), \qquad A \in \mathfrak{A}.
\]
If $F$ is not left exact, then $H^n(RF)$ corresponds to the $n$th right satellite of $F$.
