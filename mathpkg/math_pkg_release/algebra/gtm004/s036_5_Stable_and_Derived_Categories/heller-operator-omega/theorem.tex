Let $R$ be a ring and let $A$ be an $R$-module. Choose a projective presentation
\[
0 \to K \to P \to A \to 0
\]
with $P$ projective. On the stable category $\operatorname{StM}_R$, the assignment $A \mapsto \Omega(A) = K$ yields a well-defined endofunctor
\[
\Omega : \operatorname{StM}_R \to \operatorname{StM}_R.
\]
Up to isomorphism in $\operatorname{StM}_R$, the kernel $K$ does not depend on the chosen projective presentation, and a homomorphism $\varphi : A \to A'$ induces a well-defined morphism $\Omega(\varphi)$ in the stable category. When $G$ is a finite group and $k$ a field with $p \mid |G|$, the functor $\Omega$ is invertible on $\operatorname{StM}_{kG}$, yielding a nontrivial self-equivalence.
