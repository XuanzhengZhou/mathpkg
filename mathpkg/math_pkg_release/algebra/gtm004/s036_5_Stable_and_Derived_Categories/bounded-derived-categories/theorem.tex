Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be an abelian category. Consider the following full subcategories of the category $\mathfrak{C}(\mathfrak{A})$ of cochain complexes:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\mathfrak{C}^+(\mathfrak{A})$: complexes bounded below,
\item $\mathfrak{C}^-(\mathfrak{A})$: complexes bounded above,
\item $\mathfrak{C}^b(\mathfrak{A})$: complexes bounded both below and above.
\end{itemize}
Localizing the corresponding homotopy categories at quasi-isomorphisms yields the {\it bounded derived categories} $\mathfrak{D}^+(\mathfrak{A})$, $\mathfrak{D}^-(\mathfrak{A})$, and $\mathfrak{D}^b(\mathfrak{A})$, respectively. These are full subcategories of $\mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})$ and each contains the image of the embedding $J : \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{D}(\mathfrak{A})$.
