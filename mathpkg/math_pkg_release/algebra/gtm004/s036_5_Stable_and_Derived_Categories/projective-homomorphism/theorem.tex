Let $R$ be a ring and let $\mathfrak{M}_R$ be the category of left $R$-modules. A homomorphism $f : A \to B$ in $\mathfrak{M}_R$ is called {\it projective} if it factors through a projective module, i.e., there exists a projective $R$-module $P$ and homomorphisms $g : A \to P$, $h : P \to B$ such that $f = h \circ g$. The set of projective homomorphisms from $A$ to $B$ forms a subgroup
\[
\operatorname{Phom}_R(A, B) \subseteq \operatorname{Hom}_R(A, B).
\]
