Stable equivalence is a coarser notion than Morita equivalence: two rings may have equivalent stable categories even when their module categories are not equivalent. This occurs frequently in modular representation theory, where the stable category captures the essential modular structure while ignoring projective direct summands. The Heller operator $\Omega$ provides a natural example of a stable self-equivalence for group algebras $kG$ when $p \mid |G|$.
