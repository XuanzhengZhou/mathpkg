A filtered differential object is a differential object equipped with a filtration by subobjects that are each closed under the differential. This structure is one of the primary sources of exact couples in homological algebra: the filtration yields short exact sequences of successive filtration layers, which in turn produce homology exact triangles. The category $(\mathfrak{A}, d, f)$ of filtered differential objects serves as the domain for several important functors, including the associated graded functor and the $E$-term functor, both of which play central roles in the construction of spectral sequences.
