For the associated graded object $Gr(M)$ to faithfully represent a filtered object $M$, the filtration must satisfy two convergence conditions: the intersection of all filtration layers must be zero (no non-zero element belongs to every layer), and the union of all layers must be the whole object (every element belongs to some layer). When both conditions hold, each non-zero element of $M$ corresponds uniquely to a homogeneous element of $Gr(M)$, so that passage to the associated graded loses only the extension information -- the precise manner in which successive layers are glued together. These conditions are fundamental to ensuring that a spectral sequence genuinely converges to the homology of the original filtered differential object.
