# Exercise 4.6

We say that $A$ has a finite presentation if there is a short exact sequence $0 \to N \to P \to A \to 0$ with $P$ finitely-generated projective and $N$ finitely-generated. Show that

(i) if $A$ has a finite presentation, then, for every exact sequence $0 \to R \to S \to A \to 0$ with $S$ finitely-generated, $R$ is also finitely-generated;

(ii) if $A$ has a finite presentation, it has a finite presentation with $P$ free;

(iii) if $A$ has a finite presentation every presentation $0 \to N \to P \to A \to 0$ with $P$ projective, $N$ finitely-generated is finite, and every presentation $0 \to N \to P \to A \to 0$ with $P$ finitely-generated projective is finite;

(iv) if $A$ has a presentation $0 \to N_1 \to P_1 \to A \to 0$ with $P_1$ finitely-generated projective, and a presentation $0 \to N_2 \to P_2 \to A \to 0$ with $P_2$ projective, $N_2$ finitely-generated, then $A$ has a finite presentation (indeed, both the given presentations are finite).
