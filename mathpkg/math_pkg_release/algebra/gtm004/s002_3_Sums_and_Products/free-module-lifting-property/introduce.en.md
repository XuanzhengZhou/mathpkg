If $P$ is a free $\Lambda$-module, then any homomorphism $\gamma: P \to C$ can be lifted through any surjective homomorphism $\varepsilon: B \to C$. That is, there exists $\beta: P \to B$ making the triangle commute: $\varepsilon \circ \beta = \gamma$. In category-theoretic terms, free modules are projective objects. The proof uses the universal property: since $\varepsilon$ is surjective, for each basis element $s$ one can choose a preimage in $B$, then extend linearly.
