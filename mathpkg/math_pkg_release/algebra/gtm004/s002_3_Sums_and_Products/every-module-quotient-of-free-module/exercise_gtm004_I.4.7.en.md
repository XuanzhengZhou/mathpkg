# Exercise 4.7

Let $\Lambda = K[X_1, \dots, X_n, \dots]$ be the polynomial ring in countably many indeterminates $X_1, \dots, X_n, \dots$ over the field $K$. Show that the ideal $I$ generated by $X_1, \dots, X_n, \dots$ is not finitely generated. Hence we may have a presentation $0 \to N \to P \to A \to 0$ with $P$ finitely generated projective and $N$ not finitely-generated.
