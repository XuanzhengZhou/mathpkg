A spectral sequence is a powerful computational tool in homological algebra that organizes homology calculations into a sequence of successive approximations. It consists of a sequence $\{(E_n, d_n)\}$ of differential objects where the homology of each term yields the next: $H(E_n) = E_{n+1}$. In practice, one often has substantial knowledge of the early terms (say $E_2$ or $E_1$), and the limit term $E_\infty$ provides information about the homology of the original object of interest. Spectral sequences arise naturally from exact couples and from filtered chain complexes, and they play a fundamental role in algebraic topology, algebraic geometry, and group cohomology.
