Theorem 1.2 gives an explicit description of the terms $E_n$ of the spectral sequence arising from an exact couple $\{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$. The formula $E_n = \gamma^{-1}(\alpha^n D) / \beta(\alpha^{-n}(0))$ expresses each term directly in terms of the original objects $D, E$ and the structure maps, without iterative computation. The differential $d_n$ is induced by $\beta\alpha^{-n}\gamma$. The theorem also provides an associated short exact sequence $0 \to \operatorname{coker}\alpha_n \to E_n \to \ker\alpha_n \to 0$ that relates $E_n$ to the kernel and cokernel data of $\alpha$, offering structural insight into the convergence behavior of the spectral sequence.
