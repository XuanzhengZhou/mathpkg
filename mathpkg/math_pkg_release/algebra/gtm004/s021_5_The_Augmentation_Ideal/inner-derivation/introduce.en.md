An inner derivation is a derivation of the special form $d(x) = (x-1)a$ for some fixed $a \in A$. These correspond, under the isomorphism $\operatorname{Der}(G,A) \cong \operatorname{Hom}_G(IG,A)$, to those homomorphisms $\varphi: IG \to A$ of the form $\varphi(x-1) = xa - a$. The subgroup $\operatorname{Ider}(G,A)$ of inner derivations serves as the analogue of coboundaries in the interpretation of $H^1(G,A)$.
