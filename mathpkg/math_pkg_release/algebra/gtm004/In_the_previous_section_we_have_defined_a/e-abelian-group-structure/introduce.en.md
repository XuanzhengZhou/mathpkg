Since $\operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(A, B)$ is an abelian group and Theorem 2.4 provides a natural equivalence $\eta : E(-, -) \xrightarrow{\sim} \operatorname{Ext}_{\Lambda}(-, -)$, the set $E(A, B)$ of equivalence classes of module extensions inherits a natural abelian group structure. Corollary 2.5 makes this explicit: the addition (the Baer sum) can be described geometrically via pull-backs and push-outs. The neutral element is the split extension. This result is fundamental to homological algebra as it equips the classification of extensions with algebraic structure.
