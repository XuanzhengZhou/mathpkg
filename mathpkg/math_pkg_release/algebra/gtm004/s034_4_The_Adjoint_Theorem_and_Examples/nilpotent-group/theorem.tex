A group $G$ is called \text{nilpotent of class $\leq c$} if $\gamma^{c+1}(G) = 1$, where $\{\gamma^i(G)\}$ is the lower central series of $G$. The smallest such $c$ is called the \text{nilpotency class} of $G$, denoted $\operatorname{nil}(G)$. Equivalently, a nilpotent group can be obtained by a finite number of successive central extensions, starting with an abelian group.
