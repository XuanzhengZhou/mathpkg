Let $F_i: \mathfrak{C}_i \rightarrow \mathfrak{D}$ be a left adjoint of $G_i: \mathfrak{D} \rightarrow \mathfrak{C}_i$ for each $i$, and suppose that $\mathfrak{D}$ has coproducts. Define $G: \mathfrak{D} \rightarrow \prod_i \mathfrak{C}_i$ by $GD = \{G_i D\}$. Then $F: \prod_i \mathfrak{C}_i \rightarrow \mathfrak{D}$, defined by $F\{C_i\} = \coprod_i F_i C_i$, is a left adjoint to $G$. That is,
$$
\mathfrak{D}\left(\coprod_i F_i C_i, D\right) \cong \prod_i \mathfrak{C}_i(C_i, G_i D).
$$
