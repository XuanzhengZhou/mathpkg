The homology of a small category $\mathfrak{U}$ with coefficients in a functor $T: \mathfrak{U} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A}$ is defined as a special case of $J$-homology, by taking the target category to be the terminal category $\mathbf{1}$. In this setting, the Kan extension $\tilde{J}T$ is just the colimit of $T$, and the higher homology groups $H_n(\mathfrak{U}, T)$ for $n \geq 1$ measure the failure of the colimit to be exact. When $\mathfrak{U} = G$ is a group regarded as a one-object category, this recovers ordinary group homology.
