Proposition 6.6 states that the quotient of any finite-dimensional Lie algebra by its radical is semi-simple. If $\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{r}$ had a non-zero abelian ideal, its preimage in $\mathfrak{g}$ would be a solvable ideal strictly containing $\mathfrak{r}$, contradicting the maximality of $\mathfrak{r}$. This is a crucial structural result leading to the Levi decomposition.
