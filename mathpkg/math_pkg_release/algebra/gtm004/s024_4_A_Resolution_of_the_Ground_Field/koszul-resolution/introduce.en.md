Proposition 7.2 (the Koszul resolution) provides an explicit $P$-free resolution of the trivial module $K$ over the polynomial ring $P = K[x_1, \ldots, x_m]$. It is obtained as a special case of the Chevalley--Eilenberg resolution (Theorem 4.2) applied to the abelian Lie algebra $lpha$ with basis $x_1, \ldots, x_m$, since $Ulpha \cong P$. The resolution has length $m$ and is significantly simpler than the general Lie algebra case. It is the fundamental tool for proving Hilbert's chain-of-syzygies theorem.
