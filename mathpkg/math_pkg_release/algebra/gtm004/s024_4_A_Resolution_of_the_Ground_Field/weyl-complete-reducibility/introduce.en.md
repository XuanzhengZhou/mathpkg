Theorem 6.2 (Weyl's Theorem) states that every finite-dimensional representation of a semi-simple Lie algebra over a field of characteristic zero is completely reducible, i.e., splits as a direct sum of irreducible representations. The proof uses the First Whitehead Lemma: given a submodule $B \subset A$, the exact sequence $0 \to \operatorname{Hom}_K(A/B, B) \to \operatorname{Hom}_K(A/B, A) \to \operatorname{Hom}_K(A/B, A/B) \to 0$ splits because $H^1(\mathfrak{g}, \operatorname{Hom}_K(A/B, B)) = 0$. This is the Lie algebra analogue of Maschke's theorem for finite groups.
