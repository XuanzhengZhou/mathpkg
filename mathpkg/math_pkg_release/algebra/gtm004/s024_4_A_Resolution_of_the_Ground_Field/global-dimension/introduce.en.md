The global dimension of a ring $\Lambda$ is the supremum of the projective dimensions of all $\Lambda$-modules. The condition $\operatorname{gl. dim} \Lambda \leq m$ means that the $m$-th syzygy in any projective resolution is projective, which implies that every module has a projective resolution of length at most $m$. This invariant measures the homological complexity of the ring.
