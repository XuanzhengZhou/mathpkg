The radical $\mathfrak{r}$ of a finite-dimensional Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ is its unique maximal solvable ideal. Its existence follows from Lemma 6.5: the ideal generated by the union of all solvable ideals is itself solvable (finite-dimensionality ensures the union is finite). The radical plays a fundamental role in the structure theory of Lie algebras: $\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{r}$ is semi-simple, and the Levi decomposition expresses $\mathfrak{g}$ as a split extension of a semi-simple subalgebra by $\mathfrak{r}$.
