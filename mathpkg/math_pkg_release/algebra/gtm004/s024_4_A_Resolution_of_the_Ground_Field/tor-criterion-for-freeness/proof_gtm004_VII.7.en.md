# Proof of the Tor-Vanishing Criterion for Freeness

**Theorem 7.4.** Let $B$ be a graded $P$-module, where $P = K[x_1, \ldots, x_m]$ and $K$ is a field. If $\operatorname{Tor}_1^P(B, K) = 0$, then $B$ is a free $P$-module.

**Proof.** Every element of $B \otimes_P K$ can be expressed uniquely as $b \otimes 1$ with $b \in B$, where $1$ is the unity of $K$. Choose a $K$-basis $\{b_i \otimes 1\}_{i \in I}$ of $B \otimes_P K$.

Let $F$ be the free graded $P$-module generated by symbols $\{b_i\}_{i \in I}$ (with the same degrees as the $b_i$), and define a homomorphism of graded $P$-modules

$$\varphi : F \to B, \quad \varphi(b_i) = b_i.$$

Notice that $\varphi$ induces the identity map

$$\varphi_* = 1 : F \otimes_P K \to B \otimes_P K,$$

since $\varphi_*(b_i \otimes 1) = b_i \otimes 1$ by construction.

We show $\varphi$ is an isomorphism.

**Surjectivity.** Consider the exact sequence

$$F \xrightarrow{\varphi} B \to C \to 0$$

defining $C = \operatorname{coker} \varphi$. Applying $- \otimes_P K$ (which is right exact) gives

$$F \otimes_P K \xrightarrow{1} B \otimes_P K \to C \otimes_P K \to 0.$$

Since the first map is the identity, $C \otimes_P K = 0$. By Proposition 7.3 (the graded Nakayama lemma), $C = 0$, so $\varphi$ is surjective.

**Injectivity.** Consider the exact sequence

$$0 \to R \to F \xrightarrow{\varphi} B \to 0$$

defining $R = \ker \varphi$. Applying $- \otimes_P K$ and using the long exact Tor sequence:

$$\cdots \to \operatorname{Tor}_1^P(B, K) \to R \otimes_P K \to F \otimes_P K \xrightarrow{1} B \otimes_P K \to 0.$$

Since $\operatorname{Tor}_1^P(B, K) = 0$ by hypothesis and the map $F \otimes_P K \to B \otimes_P K$ is the identity (hence injective), we obtain $R \otimes_P K = 0$. By Proposition 7.3 again, $R = 0$. Hence $\varphi$ is injective.

Therefore $\varphi$ is an isomorphism, and $B \cong F$ is a free $P$-module. $\square$
