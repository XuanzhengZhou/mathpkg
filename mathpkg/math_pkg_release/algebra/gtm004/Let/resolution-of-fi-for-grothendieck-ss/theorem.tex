Given an injective resolution $I : I_0 \to I_1 \to I_2 \to \cdots$ of $A$, the complex $FI$ in $\mathfrak{B}$ admits a Cartan-Eilenberg resolution
\[
\begin{array}{cccccc}
Z_0 \to & F_0 \to & B_1 \to & Z_1 \to & F_1 \to & B_2 \to \cdots \\
\downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow \\
K_{0,0} \to & J_{0,0} \to & L_{1,0} \to & K_{1,0} \to & J_{1,0} \to & L_{2,0} \to \cdots \\
\downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow & \downarrow \\
K_{0,1} \to & J_{0,1} \to & L_{1,1} \to & K_{1,1} \to & J_{1,1} \to & L_{2,1} \to \cdots
\end{array}
\]
where each column is an injective resolution, the sequences $K_{r,s} \to J_{r,s} \to L_{r+1,s}$ are exact, and $Z_r/B_r = R^r F(A)$.
