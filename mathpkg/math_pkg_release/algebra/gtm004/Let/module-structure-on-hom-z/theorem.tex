Let $A$ be a right $\Lambda$-module and let $G$ be an abelian group. The abelian group $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$ acquires a left $\Lambda$-module structure via
$$(\lambda \varphi)(a) = \varphi(a\lambda)$$
for $\lambda \in \Lambda$, $a \in A$, $\varphi \in \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$. Similarly, if $A$ is a left $\Lambda$-module, $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$ becomes a right $\Lambda$-module.
