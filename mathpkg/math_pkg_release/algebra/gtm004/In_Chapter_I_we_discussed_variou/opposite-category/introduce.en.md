The opposite category $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ of a category $\mathfrak{C}$ is formed by formally reversing all morphisms — a morphism from $X$ to $Y$ in $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ is a morphism from $Y$ to $X$ in $\mathfrak{C}$. This construction provides the natural domain for contravariant functors: a contravariant functor from $\mathfrak{C}$ to $\mathfrak{D}$ is simply a covariant functor from $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ to $\mathfrak{D}$.
