If a category $\mathfrak{C}$ possesses a zero object $0$, then for any objects $X, Y$ in $\mathfrak{C}$, the composite morphism
$$X \to 0 \to Y$$
is called the \text{zero morphism} from $X$ to $Y$, denoted $0_{XY}$. For any $f: W \to X$ and $g: Y \to Z$ in $\mathfrak{C}$,
$$0_{XY} f = 0_{WY}, \quad g\,0_{XY} = 0_{XZ}.$$
