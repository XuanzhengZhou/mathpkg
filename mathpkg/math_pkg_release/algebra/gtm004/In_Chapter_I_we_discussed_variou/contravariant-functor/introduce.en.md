A contravariant functor reverses the direction of morphisms: it sends a morphism $f: A \to B$ to a morphism $Ff: FB \to FA$. Equivalently, a contravariant functor from $\mathfrak{C}$ to $\mathfrak{D}$ is a covariant functor from the opposite category $\mathfrak{C}^{\text{opp}}$ to $\mathfrak{D}$. A key example is the Hom-functor $\mathfrak{C}(-, B): \mathfrak{C} \to \mathbf{Set}$, where a morphism $f: A_1 \to A_2$ induces a pullback map $\mathfrak{C}(A_2, B) \to \mathfrak{C}(A_1, B)$.
