# Exercise 7.8

Show that an associative algebra (with unity) over the commutative ring $\Lambda$ may be defined as follows. An algebra $A$ is a $\Lambda$-module together with $\Lambda$-module homomorphisms $\mu : A \otimes_\Lambda A \rightarrow A$ and $\eta : \Lambda \rightarrow A$ such that the following diagrams commute:

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
A \otimes_\Lambda A \otimes_\Lambda A & \xrightarrow{\mu \otimes 1} & A \otimes_\Lambda A \\
\downarrow 1 \otimes \mu & & \downarrow \mu \\
A \otimes_\Lambda A & \xrightarrow{\mu} & A
\end{array}
$$

(associativity) and the unit diagrams expressing that $\eta(1)$ is a two-sided unit for $\mu$.
