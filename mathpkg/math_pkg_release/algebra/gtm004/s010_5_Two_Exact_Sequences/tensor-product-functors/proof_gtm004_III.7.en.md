# Proof of Tensor Product as a Bifunctor (Proposition 7.1)

For any left $\Lambda$-module $B$, $- \otimes_\Lambda B : \mathfrak{M}_\Lambda^r \rightarrow \mathfrak{Ab}$ is a covariant functor. For any right $\Lambda$-module $A$, $A \otimes_\Lambda - : \mathfrak{M}_\Lambda^l \rightarrow \mathfrak{Ab}$ is a covariant functor. Moreover, $- \otimes_\Lambda -$ is a bifunctor.

**Proof.** The tensor product $A \otimes_\Lambda B$ of a right $\Lambda$-module $A$ and a left $\Lambda$-module $B$ is defined as the abelian group generated by symbols $a \otimes b$ ($a \in A$, $b \in B$) subject to the relations:

$$
(a_1 + a_2) \otimes b = a_1 \otimes b + a_2 \otimes b,
$$
$$
a \otimes (b_1 + b_2) = a \otimes b_1 + a \otimes b_2,
$$
$$
a\lambda \otimes b = a \otimes \lambda b, \quad \lambda \in \Lambda.
$$

Given a homomorphism of right $\Lambda$-modules $\alpha : A \rightarrow A'$, define

$$
\alpha_* = \alpha \otimes 1_B : A \otimes_\Lambda B \rightarrow A' \otimes_\Lambda B
$$

by $\alpha_*(a \otimes b) = \alpha(a) \otimes b$. This is well-defined because the defining relations of the tensor product are respected. One verifies that $(1_A)_* = 1_{A \otimes B}$ and $(\alpha' \circ \alpha)_* = \alpha'_* \circ \alpha_*$, establishing that $- \otimes_\Lambda B$ is a covariant functor.

Similarly, given a homomorphism of left $\Lambda$-modules $\beta : B \rightarrow B'$, define

$$
\beta_* = 1_A \otimes \beta : A \otimes_\Lambda B \rightarrow A \otimes_\Lambda B'
$$

by $\beta_*(a \otimes b) = a \otimes \beta(b)$. This makes $A \otimes_\Lambda -$ a covariant functor.

For the bifunctor property: given $\alpha : A \rightarrow A'$ and $\beta : B \rightarrow B'$, the diagram

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
A \otimes_\Lambda B & \xrightarrow{\alpha \otimes 1} & A' \otimes_\Lambda B \\
\downarrow 1 \otimes \beta & & \downarrow 1 \otimes \beta \\
A \otimes_\Lambda B' & \xrightarrow{\alpha \otimes 1} & A' \otimes_\Lambda B'
\end{array}
$$

commutes, and $\alpha \otimes \beta = (\alpha \otimes 1) \circ (1 \otimes \beta) = (1 \otimes \beta) \circ (\alpha \otimes 1)$.
