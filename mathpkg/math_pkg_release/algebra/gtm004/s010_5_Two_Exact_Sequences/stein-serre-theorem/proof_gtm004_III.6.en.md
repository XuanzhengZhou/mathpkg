# Proof of the Stein-Serre Theorem (Theorem 6.1)

If $A$ is an abelian group of countable rank, then $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z}) = 0$ implies $A$ is free.

**Proof.** By Lemma 6.2 it suffices to show that every subgroup of $A$ of finite rank is free. Suppose $A$ is not free; then there exists a subgroup $A_0$ of finite rank which is not free (otherwise by Lemma 6.2 $A$ would be free). We will derive a contradiction by showing $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z}) \neq 0$.

Consider the short exact sequence $\mathbb{Z} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Q} \twoheadrightarrow \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ and the associated Hom-Ext sequence in the second variable (Theorem 5.2) with respect to $A$:

$$
\operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow \operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Q}) \xrightarrow{\eta_*} \operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow \operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z}) \rightarrow \operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Q}).
$$

Since $\mathbb{Q}$ is divisible (hence injective as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module), $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Q}) = 0$ by Corollary 5.6. Thus $\eta_*$ is epimorphic if and only if $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z}) = 0$.

We show that $\eta_*$ is not epimorphic by proving that $\operatorname{card}(\operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z})) > \operatorname{card}(\operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Q}))$.

Let $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ be a maximal linearly independent set of elements of $A$. Let $A_0$ be the subgroup of $A$ generated by $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$. By hypothesis (or by the reduction to the finite rank case) $A_0$ is free. Since $A$ is not free, $A_0 \neq A$.

Every homomorphism from $A$ into $\mathbb{Q}$ is determined by its restriction to $A_0$, and every homomorphism from $A_0$ to $\mathbb{Q}$ extends to $A$ (since $\mathbb{Q}$ is divisible, hence injective). Thus:

$$
\operatorname{card}(\operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Q})) = \operatorname{card}(\operatorname{Hom}(A_0, \mathbb{Q})) = \aleph_0^n = \aleph_0.
$$

Now take $b_1 \in A \setminus A_0$ and let $k_1 \geq 2$ be the smallest integer for which $k_1 b_1 \in A_0$. Let $A_1$ be the subgroup generated by $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n, b_1$. By hypothesis $A_1$ is free. But $A_1 \neq A$, since $A$ is not free. Take $b_2 \in A \setminus A_1$. Let $k_2 \geq 2$ be the smallest integer with $k_2 b_2 \in A_1$. Continuing in this way, we construct an infinite sequence $b_1, b_2, \ldots$ and integers $k_1, k_2, \ldots \geq 2$.

For each $i$, there exists a homomorphism $f_i : A \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ sending $b_i$ to $\frac{1}{k_i} + \mathbb{Z}$ (and suitably defined on the rest of $A$). These give uncountably many distinct homomorphisms (by considering all possible sign choices), showing

$$
\operatorname{card}(\operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z})) = 2^{\aleph_0} > \aleph_0 = \operatorname{card}(\operatorname{Hom}(A, \mathbb{Q})).
$$

Therefore $\eta_*$ cannot be surjective, so $\operatorname{Ext}(A, \mathbb{Z}) \neq 0$, a contradiction. Hence $A$ must be free.
