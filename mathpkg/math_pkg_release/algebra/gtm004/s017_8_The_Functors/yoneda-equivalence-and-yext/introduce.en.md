The Yoneda equivalence relation on $n$-extensions generalises the equivalence relation for $1$-extensions given in Section III.1. The primary relation $\rightsquigarrow$ is given by the existence of a commutative diagram that is the identity on $A$ and $B$; for $n \geq 2$ this relation is not symmetric, so one passes to the equivalence relation $\sim$ generated by it (allowing zig-zag chains). The set of equivalence classes is denoted $\operatorname{Yext}_A^n(A,B)$, and by Theorem 9.1 it is naturally isomorphic to $\operatorname{Ext}_A^n(A,B)$.
