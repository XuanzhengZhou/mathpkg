Corollary 4.5 is a direct consequence of the natural isomorphism $\operatorname{Ext}(\operatorname{Tor}(A', A), A'') \cong \operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, A''))$ from Theorem 4.3. When $A'$ is torsion-free, $\operatorname{Tor}(A', A) = 0$ for all $A$, so the left side vanishes for all $A, A''$, forcing $\operatorname{Ext}(A', \operatorname{Ext}(A, A'')) = 0$.

This result shows that applying $\operatorname{Ext}$ twice, with the outer argument torsion-free, always yields zero -- a useful vanishing criterion in homological computations.
