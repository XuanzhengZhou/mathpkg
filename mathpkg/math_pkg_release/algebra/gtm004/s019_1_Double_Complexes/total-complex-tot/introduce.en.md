The total complex $\operatorname{Tot} B$ collapses a double complex into an ordinary (singly graded) chain complex by taking the direct sum along each anti-diagonal $p+q=n$. The differential is simply the sum of the horizontal and vertical differentials.

This is one of two standard ways to construct a chain complex from a double complex; the other uses the direct product instead of direct sum. The total complex connects the iterated homology $H(H(B,\partial'),\partial'')$ with $H(\operatorname{Tot} B)$, typically via a spectral sequence.
