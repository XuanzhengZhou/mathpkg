The second total complex $\operatorname{Tot}' B$ collapses a double complex into an ordinary chain complex using the direct product along each anti-diagonal. This variant is particularly important in the context of Hom double complexes, where it yields the correct adjointness relation with the tensor product.

The choice between $\operatorname{Tot} B$ (direct sum) and $\operatorname{Tot}' B$ (direct product) depends on the specific application: the tensor product of chain complexes uses $\operatorname{Tot}$, while the Hom complex uses $\operatorname{Tot}'$.
