# Exercise 1.4

We define a differential right $\Lambda$-module with involution to be a right $\Lambda$-module $A$ equipped with an endomorphism $d: A \to A$ such that $d^2 = 0$ and an involution $\eta: A \to A$ such that $d\eta = -\eta d$. Given such an object $A$, show how to introduce a differential into $A \otimes_\Lambda B$ where $B$ is a differential left $\Lambda$-module, and into $\operatorname{Hom}_\Lambda(A, B)$ where $B$ is a differential right $\Lambda$-module. Suggest a definition of a chain map of differential $\Lambda$-modules with involution, and of a chain homotopy between such chain maps.
