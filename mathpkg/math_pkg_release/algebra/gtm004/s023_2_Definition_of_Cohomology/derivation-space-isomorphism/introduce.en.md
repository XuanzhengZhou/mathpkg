Proposition 2.3 establishes a natural isomorphism between the space of derivations $\text{Der}(\mathfrak{g}, A)$ and the space of Lie algebra homomorphisms from $\mathfrak{g}$ into the semidirect product $A \rtimes \mathfrak{g}$ that induce the identity on $\mathfrak{g}$. The correspondence sends a derivation $d$ to $x \mapsto (d(x), x)$ and recovers a derivation from a splitting homomorphism $f$ by composing with the projection $p_A$. This naturality in $A$ is a key ingredient in interpreting $H^1$ geometrically.
