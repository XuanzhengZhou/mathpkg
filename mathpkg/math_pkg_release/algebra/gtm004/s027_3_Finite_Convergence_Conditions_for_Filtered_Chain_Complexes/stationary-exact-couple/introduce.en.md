In the theory of spectral sequences arising from exact couples, the notion of a stationary map captures when the maps in the exact couple eventually become isomorphisms in a given direction. Given a bigraded exact couple $D \xrightarrow{\alpha} D \xrightarrow{\beta} E \xrightarrow{\gamma} D$ with $\deg \alpha = (1,0)$, the map $\alpha$ is called positively stationary if for each fixed $q$ there exists a threshold $p_0$ beyond which all components $\alpha: D^{p,q} \to D^{p+1,q}$ are isomorphisms. Negative stationarity is defined analogously for the opposite direction, and $\alpha$ is simply called stationary when both conditions hold. This property is the key hypothesis ensuring that the associated spectral sequence converges after only finitely many pages at each bidegree.
