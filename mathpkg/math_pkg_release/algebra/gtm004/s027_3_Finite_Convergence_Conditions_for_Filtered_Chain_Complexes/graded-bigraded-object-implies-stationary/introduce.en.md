Proposition 3.2 states the elementary but useful relationship between grading conditions on the bigraded objects $D$ or $\bar{D}$ appearing in an exact couple and the stationarity of the corresponding structure maps $\alpha$ or $\bar{\alpha}$. Specifically, if $D$ is positively graded—vanishing for small $p$ at each fixed $q$—then $\alpha$ is negatively stationary, and the dual statement holds for negative grading. The same relationship holds for $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{\alpha}$. This proposition is employed as a stepping stone toward the equivalence theorem (Theorem 3.3) linking stationarity of $\alpha$, grading of $E$, and stationarity of $\bar{\alpha}$.
