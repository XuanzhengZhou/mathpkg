Lemma 4.1 describes how the Ext functor interacts with direct sums and direct products. When direct sums appear in the first argument of Ext, they become direct products outside: $\text{Ext}_A(\bigoplus_i A_i, B) \cong \prod_i \text{Ext}_A(A_i, B)$. When direct products appear in the second argument, they are preserved: $\text{Ext}_A(A, \prod_j B_j) \cong \prod_j \text{Ext}_A(A, B_j)$. This duality reflects the contravariant nature of the first argument and the covariant nature of the second argument. These isomorphisms are fundamental for computing Ext groups by decomposing arguments into simpler components.
