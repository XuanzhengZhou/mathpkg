An abelian Lie algebra is one where all brackets vanish, making the Lie structure trivial. Abelian Lie algebras correspond precisely to vector spaces with zero bracket, and they play a role analogous to abelian groups in group theory. Every Lie algebra has a largest abelian quotient $\mathfrak{g}_{\text{ab}} = \mathfrak{g}/[\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{g}]$, where $[\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{g}]$ is the derived subalgebra.
