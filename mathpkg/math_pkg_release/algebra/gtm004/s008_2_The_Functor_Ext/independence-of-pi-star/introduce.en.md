Lemma 2.1 in Hilton-Stammbach establishes that the natural transformation $\pi^* : \text{Ext}_\Lambda^\varepsilon(A, B) \to \text{Ext}_\Lambda^{\varepsilon'}(A', B)$ induced by a map $\alpha : A' \to A$ does not depend on the choice of lifting $\pi : P' \to P$. The proof proceeds by showing that any two liftings differ by a map factoring through $R$, and this difference vanishes when composed with a representative $\varphi : R \to B$ in Ext. This result is a crucial step toward showing that Ext is well-defined as a bifunctor independent of projective presentation choices.
