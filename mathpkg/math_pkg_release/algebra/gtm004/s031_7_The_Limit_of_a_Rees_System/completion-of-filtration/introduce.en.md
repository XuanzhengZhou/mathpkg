The completion of a filtration is a universal construction that turns any filtered object into a complete one with the same associated graded object. The process proceeds in two steps: first taking the inductive limit to enforce left completeness (the $X^\infty$ construction), then taking the projective limit of the resulting cofiltration quotients to enforce right completeness (the $Y = (X^\infty)_{-\infty}$ construction). This ensures that one may always replace a filtered object by its completion without altering the associated graded.
