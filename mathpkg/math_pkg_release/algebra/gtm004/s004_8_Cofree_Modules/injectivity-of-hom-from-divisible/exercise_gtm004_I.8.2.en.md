# Exercise 8.2

Let $A$ be a $\Lambda$-module and let $G$ be a divisible abelian group containing $A$ as an abelian subgroup. Show that we may embed $A$ in an injective module by the scheme

$$A = \operatorname{Hom}_{\Lambda}(A, A) \subseteq \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, A) \subseteq \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G).$$

Check that the composition yields an embedding of $\Lambda$-modules.

*Hint:* Use Proposition 8.1 to interpret the first inclusion. For the second inclusion, use the fact that $A \hookrightarrow G$ induces $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, A) \hookrightarrow \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(A, G)$. Verify that the left $\Lambda$-module structures are respected at each step.
