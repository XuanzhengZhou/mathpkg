A $\Lambda$-module is called \emph{cofree} if it is the direct product of copies of the module
$$\Lambda^{*} = \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\Lambda, \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}),$$
where $\Lambda^{*}$ is made into a left $\Lambda$-module using the right $\Lambda$-module structure of $\Lambda$. This is consistent with the description of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ as a cofree abelian group, since $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{Z}}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}) \cong \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$.
