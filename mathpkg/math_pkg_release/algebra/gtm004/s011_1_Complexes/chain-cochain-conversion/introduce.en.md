The distinction between a chain complex (differential of degree $-1$) and a cochain complex (differential of degree $+1$) is purely formal. Given a chain complex $C = \{C_n, \partial_n\}$, simply reindex by $D^n = C_{-n}$ and $\delta^n = \partial_{-n}$ to obtain a cochain complex.

Conversely, any cochain complex yields a chain complex by the same reindexing. This means one does not need to develop separate theories for chain and cochain complexes; results for one translate immediately to the other.
