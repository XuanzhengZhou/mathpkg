The $n$-th homology module $H_n(C)$ of a chain complex $C = \{C_n, \partial_n\}$ is defined as the quotient $\ker \partial_n / \operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1}$. This is well-defined because $\partial_n \partial_{n+1} = 0$ guarantees $\operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1} \subseteq \ker \partial_n$.

Homology measures the deviation of the complex from being exact: $H_n(C) = 0$ precisely when $\ker \partial_n = \operatorname{im} \partial_{n+1}$, i.e., when the complex is exact at $C_n$. The collection $H(C) = \{H_n(C)\}_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ forms a graded $\Lambda$-module.
