The homology construction $H(-)$ is a functor from the category of chain complexes over a ring $\Lambda$ to the category of graded $\Lambda$-modules. It sends a chain complex $C$ to its homology $H(C) = \{H_n(C)\}$ and a chain map $\varphi: C \rightarrow D$ to the induced map $\varphi_*: H(C) \rightarrow H(D)$.

Each individual homology group $H_n(-)$ is itself a functor into the category $\mathfrak{M}_\Lambda$ of $\Lambda$-modules. This functoriality is the fundamental organizing principle of homological algebra and underpins all later constructions of derived functors.
