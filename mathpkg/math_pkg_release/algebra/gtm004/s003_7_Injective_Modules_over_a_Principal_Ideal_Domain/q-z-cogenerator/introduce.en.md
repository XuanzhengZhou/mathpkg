The quotient group $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ serves as an injective cogenerator for the category of abelian groups. It is the dual counterpart to $\mathbb{Z}$, which is projective and admits a nonzero homomorphism into any nonzero abelian group. The proof that every abelian group embeds into a product of copies of $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ constructs, for each nonzero element $a \in A$, a homomorphism $\beta_a : A \to \mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$ that does not vanish on $a$, and then combines them via the universal property of the product.
