Under the hypotheses of Proposition 7.3, let $\theta : G \to G'$ and $\bar{\theta} : G' \to G$ be the uniquely determined mutually inverse natural equivalences. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $\theta F \circ \varepsilon = \varepsilon'$ and $\delta \circ F\bar{\theta} = \delta'$, where $\varepsilon, \varepsilon'$ are units and $\delta, \delta'$ are counits.
\item[(ii)] $\theta_Y \circ \eta(\varphi) = \eta'(\varphi)$ for any $\varphi : FX \to Y$.
\end{enumerate}
Conversely, if $\theta : G \to G'$ is a natural equivalence, then an adjugant $\eta : F \dashv G$ induces an adjugant $\eta' : F \dashv G'$ via $\eta'(\varphi) = \theta_Y \circ \eta(\varphi)$, with unit and counit given by (i).
