# Exercise 9.2

Show that the square

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
A & \xrightarrow{\varphi} & B \\
{\scriptstyle \alpha}\downarrow & & {\scriptstyle \beta}\downarrow \\
A' & \xrightarrow{\varphi'} & B'
\end{array}$$

in the abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$ is commutative if and only if the sequence

$$A \xrightarrow{\{\alpha, \varphi\}} A' \oplus B \xrightarrow{\langle -\varphi', \beta \rangle} B'$$

is differential, i.e., $\langle -\varphi', \beta \rangle \circ \{\alpha, \varphi\} = 0$. Show further that

(i) the square is a pull-back if and only if $\{\alpha, \varphi\}$ is the kernel of $\langle -\varphi', \beta \rangle$,

(ii) the square is a push-out if and only if $\langle -\varphi', \beta \rangle$ is the cokernel of $\{\alpha, \varphi\}$.
