The Grothendieck group construction turns an abelian semigroup into an abelian group in a universal way. It is the group completion: every homomorphism from the semigroup to an abelian group factors uniquely through $\operatorname{Gr}(S)$. This construction is a left adjoint (to the forgetful functor) and thus an example of a universal construction. It generalizes the construction of the integers from the natural numbers and is fundamental in K-theory.
