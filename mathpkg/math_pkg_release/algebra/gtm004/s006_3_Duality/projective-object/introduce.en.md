A projective object is one that has the lifting property with respect to epimorphisms: any morphism from the projective object to the codomain of an epimorphism lifts through the epimorphism. This generalizes the notion of projective modules from Chapter I. In the category of sets, every object is projective. Projective objects are fundamental in homological algebra as they are used to construct projective resolutions for defining derived functors.
