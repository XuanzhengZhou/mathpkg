An exact sequence in an abelian category generalizes the familiar notion from module theory: the image of each morphism equals the kernel of the next. This is defined purely in terms of the categorical structures (kernels, cokernels, image factorization) without reference to elements. Exact sequences are the fundamental tool of homological algebra, encoding relationships between objects and functors.
