Let $G$ be a finite group, $k$ a field of characteristic $p$, and $M$ an indecomposable $kG$-module. Consider the class of subgroups $U \leq G$ with the property that $M$ is projective relative to $U$. A minimal such subgroup $V$ is called a {\it vertex} of $M$ (unique up to conjugacy). The restriction $M_V$ decomposes, and there is an indecomposable $kV$-module $N$, called the {\it source} of $M$, which is a direct summand of $M_V$ and is essentially uniquely determined. A vertex is always a $p$-subgroup of $G$.
