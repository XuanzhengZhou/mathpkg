Relative projectivity with respect to subgroups is a cornerstone of J.A. Green's theory of vertices and sources. A module is projective relative to a subgroup $U$ if it inherits its projectivity from $U$: any exact sequence that splits upon restriction to $U$ must already split over $G$. This notion generalizes ordinary projectivity (which corresponds to being projective relative to the trivial subgroup) and provides a finer classification of indecomposable modules. The fact that every module is projective relative to a Sylow $p$-subgroup shows that relative projectivity is always tied to $p$-local structure.
