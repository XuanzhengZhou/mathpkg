Chouinard's criterion is a striking corollary of the complexity theory: projectivity of a module can be detected by restriction to elementary abelian $p$-subgroups alone. This means that the projective modules over $kG$ are precisely those modules that appear projective when viewed from any elementary abelian $p$-subgroup. The result provides an elegant homological characterization of projectivity that reduces a global question about $G$ to a collection of local questions about its elementary abelian $p$-subgroups.
