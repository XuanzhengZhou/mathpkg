\begin{definition}[Complexity of a Module]
\label{def:module-complexity}
Let $M$ be an indecomposable $kG$-module. The \textbf{complexity} $c_G(M)$ is a numerical invariant defined via the growth rate of the minimal projective resolution of $M$. In particular:
\begin{itemize}
\item $c_G(M) = 0$ if and only if $M$ is $kG$-projective.
\item $c_G(k)$ equals the Krull dimension $c(G)$ of $H^*(G, k)$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}
