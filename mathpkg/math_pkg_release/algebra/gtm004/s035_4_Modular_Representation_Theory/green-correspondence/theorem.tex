Let $G$ be a finite group, $k$ a field of characteristic $p$, $Q$ a $p$-subgroup of $G$, and $L = N_G(Q)$ the normalizer of $Q$ in $G$. There is a bijective correspondence between:
\begin{itemize}
\item isomorphism classes of indecomposable $kG$-modules with vertex $Q$,
\item isomorphism classes of indecomposable $kL$-modules with vertex $Q$.
\end{itemize}
If $M$ is an indecomposable $kG$-module with vertex $Q$, its Green correspondent is the unique indecomposable direct summand of the restriction $M_L$ with vertex $Q$. Conversely, if $N$ is an indecomposable $kL$-module with vertex $Q$, its Green correspondent is the unique indecomposable direct summand of $kG \otimes_{kL} N$ with vertex $Q$.
