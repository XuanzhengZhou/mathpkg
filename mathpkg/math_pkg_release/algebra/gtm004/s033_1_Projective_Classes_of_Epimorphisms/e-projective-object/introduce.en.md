An $\mathcal{E}$-projective object generalizes the notion of a projective object in an abelian category by relativizing it to a given class $\mathcal{E}$ of epimorphisms. Instead of requiring lifting against all epimorphisms, one only requires lifting against those in $\mathcal{E}$. This concept is the foundation of relative homological algebra, where one develops a homology theory tailored to a specific projective class, yielding $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors and $\mathcal{E}$-satellites that refine the classical absolute theory.
