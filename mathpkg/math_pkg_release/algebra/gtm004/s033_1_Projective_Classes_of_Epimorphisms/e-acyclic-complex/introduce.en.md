An $\mathcal{E}$-acyclic complex is one whose augmentation by its zeroth homology
yields an exact sequence. When combined with the $\mathcal{E}$-projective condition,
this defines an $\mathcal{E}$-projective resolution of $H_0(K)$. Such resolutions
are the fundamental tool for computing $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors, replacing
the classical projective resolutions in the relative setting.
