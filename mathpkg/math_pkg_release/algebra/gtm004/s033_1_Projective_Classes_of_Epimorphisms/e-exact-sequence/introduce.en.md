An $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence is an exact sequence in which every morphism is
$\mathcal{E}$-admissible. This is the relative analogue of an ordinary exact
sequence. The condition ensures that the epimorphic parts of all differentials
lie in the closed class $\mathcal{E}$, which is essential for the construction
of $\mathcal{E}$-projective resolutions and the subsequent development of
$\mathcal{E}$-derived functors.
