This proposition shows that right $\mathcal{E}$-exactness is a stronger condition than mere functoriality; it forces the functor to be additive. The proof uses the fact that the zero object is characterized by the exactness of the sequence $A \xrightarrow{1} A \xrightarrow{1} A \to 0$, which is $\mathcal{E}$-exact for any projective class $\mathcal{E}$. This result ensures that the theory of $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors is developed entirely within the additive setting, consistent with the classical theory.
