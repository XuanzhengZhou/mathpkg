An $\mathcal{E}$-connected sequence of functors is the relative analogue of a (co)homological $\delta$-functor. This proposition verifies that the long sequence induced by any short $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequence is automatically a complex (differential at every position). The proof relies on naturality arguments applied to morphisms between short $\mathcal{E}$-exact sequences. The $\mathcal{E}$-derived functors of an additive functor provide an important example of an $\mathcal{E}$-connected sequence.
