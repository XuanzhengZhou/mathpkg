Let $\mathfrak{U}$ be an abelian category and $\mathcal{E}$ a class of epimorphisms in $\mathfrak{U}$. The \textbf{closure} $C(\mathcal{E})$ of $\mathcal{E}$ consists of those epimorphisms $\varepsilon$ in $\mathfrak{U}$ such that every $\mathcal{E}$-projective object of $\mathfrak{U}$ is also projective rel $\varepsilon$. Plainly $\mathcal{E} \subseteq C(\mathcal{E})$ and $C(C(\mathcal{E})) = C(\mathcal{E})$.
