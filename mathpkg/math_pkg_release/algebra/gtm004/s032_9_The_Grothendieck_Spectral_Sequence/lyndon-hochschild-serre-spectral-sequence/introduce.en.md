The Lyndon--Hochschild--Serre spectral sequence is the principal application of the Grothendieck spectral sequence to group cohomology. For a short exact sequence of groups $N \hookrightarrow K \twoheadrightarrow Q$ and a $K$-module $A$, it relates the cohomology of $K$ to that of $N$ and $Q$ via $E_1^{p,q} = H^p(Q, H^{q-p}(N, A)) \Rightarrow H^q(K, A)$. The key observation is that the $N$-invariants functor $F(A) = A^N$ sends injective $K$-modules to injective $Q$-modules, satisfying the $G$-acyclicity hypothesis. A homology version with $E^1_{p,q} = H_p(Q, H_{q-p}(N, A)) \Rightarrow H_q(K, A)$ follows by dualizing to left derived functors.
