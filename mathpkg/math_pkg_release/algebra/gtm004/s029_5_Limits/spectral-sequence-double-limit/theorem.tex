Consider an exact couple in an abelian category with the associated spectral sequence $\{E_{m,n}\}$. Then the limiting terms satisfy
$$
E_{\infty,\infty} = \lim_m E_{m,\infty} = \lim_m \lim_n E_{m,n},
$$
and the limiting and colimiting processes commute in this special case, i.e.,
$$
\lim_m \lim_n E_{m,n} \cong \lim_n \lim_m E_{m,n}.
$$
Moreover, passing to the limit from any derived couple of the given exact couple, one obtains the exact sequences
$$
\begin{aligned}
D_m &\xrightarrow{\alpha_m} D_m \xrightarrow{\beta_{m,\infty}} E_{m,\infty} \xrightarrow{\gamma_{m,\infty}} I \xrightarrow{\alpha''} I, \\
U &\xrightarrow{\alpha'} U \xrightarrow{\beta_{\infty}} E_{\infty} \xrightarrow{\gamma_{\infty}} I \xrightarrow{\alpha''} I, \\
U &\xrightarrow{\alpha'} U \xrightarrow{\beta_{\infty,n}} E_{\infty,n} \xrightarrow{\gamma_{\infty,n}} D_n \xrightarrow{\alpha_n} D_n.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact couple (1.1) ceases to be an exact couple ``in the limit'' but remains an exact sequence as above, because both limiting and colimiting processes are carried out. This theorem is valid in any abelian category in which the appropriate limits exist.
