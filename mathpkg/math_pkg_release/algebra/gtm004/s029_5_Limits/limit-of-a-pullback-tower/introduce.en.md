Theorem 5.2 characterizes the limit of a tower of pullback squares as again a pullback. Given a sequence of commutative squares each of which is a pullback, the induced map between the limits $A_{\infty} = \lim A_i$ and $B_{\infty} = \lim B_i$ together with the limiting maps to $A_0$ and $B_0$ forms a pullback square. This result is used in the theory of spectral sequences to relate the limiting behavior of successive derived couples.
