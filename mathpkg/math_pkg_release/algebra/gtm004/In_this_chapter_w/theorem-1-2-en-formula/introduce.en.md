Theorem 1.2 provides an explicit formula for the $n$-th term $E_n$ of the spectral sequence associated to an exact couple: $E_n = \gamma^{-1}(\alpha^n D) / \beta(\alpha^{-n}(0))$, with differential $d_n$ induced by $\beta\alpha^{-n}\gamma$. This formula expresses $E_n$ directly in terms of the original data $(D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma)$ without iterating the derived couple construction. The theorem also shows that $E_n$ fits into a short exact sequence relating it to the cokernel and kernel of $\alpha_n$, providing structural insight into how $E_n$ is built from subquotients of $D$.
