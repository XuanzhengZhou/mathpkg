A morphism $\Phi$ from an exact couple $\mathit{EC} = \{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$ to $\mathit{EC}' = \{D', E', \alpha', \beta', \gamma'\}$ is a pair of morphisms $\kappa : D \to D'$, $\lambda : E \to E'$ such that
$$\alpha' \kappa = \kappa \alpha, \quad \beta' \kappa = \lambda \beta, \quad \gamma' \lambda = \kappa \gamma.$$
The category of exact couples in $\mathfrak{A}$ is denoted $\mathfrak{EC}(\mathfrak{A})$, or briefly $\mathfrak{EC}$.
