Let $\mathit{EC} = \{D, E, \alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$ be an exact couple in an abelian category $\mathfrak{A}$, and let $\mathit{EC}_1 = \{D_1, E_1, \alpha_1, \beta_1, \gamma_1\}$ be its derived couple, where
$$D_1 = \alpha D, \quad E_1 = H(E, d_0), \quad d_0 = \beta\gamma,$$
and $\alpha_1, \beta_1, \gamma_1$ are defined by
$$\alpha_1(\alpha x) = \alpha(\alpha x), \quad \beta_1(\alpha x) = [\beta x], \quad \gamma_1[z] = \gamma(z).$$
Then $\mathit{EC}_1$ is an exact couple; that is, the sequence
$$D_1 \xrightarrow{\alpha_1} D_1 \xrightarrow{\beta_1} E_1 \xrightarrow{\gamma_1} D_1 \xrightarrow{\alpha_1} D_1$$
is exact.
