A covariant functor is a structure-preserving map between categories. It sends objects to objects and morphisms to morphisms while preserving identity morphisms and composition. Functors are the morphisms of the "category of categories" and are ubiquitous in mathematics: homology, the fundamental group, the abelianization of a group, and the Hom-functor are all examples. The notion of functor was introduced by Eilenberg and MacLane to formalize constructions in algebraic topology.
