The Sandwich Theorem is a major result in categorical universal algebra. Given a commutative triangle of functors where the bottom functor $V$ is algebraic, any functor $U$ sitting "above" $W$ inherits algebraicity: if $W$ has a left adjoint, so does $U$; and if $W$ is algebraic, so is $U$. This implies that homomorphisms between algebraic categories (i.e., functors over $\mathbf{Set}$ commuting with the algebraic forgetful functors) are themselves algebraic, provided the domain has coequalizers.
