[Duskin '69, Theorem 3.2.] Let $\mathcal{K}$ have finite limits and let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$. Prove that $U$ is algebraic if and only if:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $U$ has a left adjoint;
\item[(b)] $\mathcal{A}$ has separators;
\item[(c)] given an equivalence relation $p, q: A \longrightarrow B$ in $\mathcal{A}$ (see section 2.1 exercise 15) such that $(pU, qU)$ extends to a contractible coequalizer $(pU, qU, h; d, d')$ in $\mathcal{K}$, $h$ admits a unique lift which is, moreover, $\text{coeq}(p, q)$ in $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{itemize}

[Hint: a proof of the same assertion, substituting "kernel pairs" for "equivalence relations" in (c) follows the proof of 1.13 and is itself an interesting result; to prove the assertion as stated requires more work.]
