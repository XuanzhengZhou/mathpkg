Beck functors are functors that both create limits and create coequalizers of absolute pairs. They generalize algebraic functors: every algebraic functor is Beck (Proposition 1.19), and conversely, a Beck functor satisfying the solution set condition is algebraic (Proposition 1.21). The concept provides a convenient intermediate notion for proving that functors are algebraic without explicitly constructing the algebraic theory.
