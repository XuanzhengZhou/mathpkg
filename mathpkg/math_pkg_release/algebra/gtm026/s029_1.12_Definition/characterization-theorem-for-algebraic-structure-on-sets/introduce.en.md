A functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathbf{Set}$ from a category of sets with structure is **algebraic** if and only if three conditions hold: $U$ has a left adjoint, $\mathcal{A}$ has separators, and $U$ creates quotients of congruences. This theorem, proved by adapting Beck's characterization (1.9) together with results of Duskin and Linton, provides an alternative to the Beck tripleability criteria that is often more convenient for categories over $\mathbf{Set}$. The proof of necessity follows from previously established results (1.4.12, 2.1.11, 2.1.14, 2.1.22, 1.5, 1.9), while the proof of sufficiency reduces to showing that $U$ creates coequalizers of $U$-contractible pairs by constructing an appropriate congruence.
