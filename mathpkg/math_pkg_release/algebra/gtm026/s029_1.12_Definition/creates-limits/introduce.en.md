A functor $U$ creates limits if every limit diagram in the base category that lives under the image of a diagram in $\mathcal{A}$ can be uniquely lifted to a limit diagram in $\mathcal{A}$. This is a stronger condition than preserving or constructing limits. Together with creation of coequalizers of absolute pairs, it characterizes Beck functors, which generalize algebraic functors.
