This theorem (identical to 1.5.45) states that any tractable equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$ gives rise to an algebraic forgetful functor $U$ to $\mathbf{Set}$. The proof uses Proposition 1.22: $U$ creates limits (by endowing the set-theoretic limit with the unique $\Omega$-algebra structure making projections homomorphisms) and creates quotients of congruences (by showing the quotient set inherits a unique $\Omega$-algebra structure). The tractability condition ensures that $U$ has a left adjoint.
