This is the set-valued specialization of Proposition 1.21, using Theorem 1.13 instead of Beck's theorem. A set-valued functor is algebraic precisely when it creates limits, creates quotients of congruences, and satisfies the solution set condition. With a cogenerator, the solution set condition is automatic, giving a purely structural characterization of algebraic categories over $\mathbf{Set}$.
