The class of algebraic functors is not closed under composition. A canonical counterexample involves the category of torsion-free abelian groups: the inclusion $U: \mathcal{P} \hookrightarrow \mathcal{A}$ into abelian groups is algebraic (since $\mathcal{P}$ is full replete reflective), the forgetful functor $V: \mathcal{A} \to \mathbf{Set}$ is algebraic, but the composite $UV: \mathcal{P} \to \mathbf{Set}$ fails to be algebraic even though it admits a left adjoint. The failure stems from the fact that the algebraic theory for abelian groups does not restrict properly to torsion-free groups -- the free algebra on a two-element set yields elements of finite order in the tensor product.
