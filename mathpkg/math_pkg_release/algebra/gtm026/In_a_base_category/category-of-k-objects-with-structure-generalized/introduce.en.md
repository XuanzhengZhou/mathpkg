This definition extends the literal category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure (Section 3.1) to a more flexible notion: a concrete category $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ is a category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure if it is isomorphic in $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ to some literal category $\mathcal{C}$. This generalization reflects the idea that the essential structure is captured by the forgetful functor $U$, not by the specific presentation of objects as pairs $(K, s)$. The principle is that invariants in a given category of structured objects are implicit in the forgetful functor, a theme that runs throughout the book.
