A concrete category $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ over $\mathcal{K}$ is a \textbf{category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure} if there exists a literal category $\mathcal{C}$ of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure (as defined in Section 3.1) together with an isomorphism

$$(\mathcal{A}, U) \cong \mathcal{C}$$

in the category $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ of concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$. In other words, $\mathcal{A}$ is abstractly isomorphic, as a concrete category, to a literal category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure.
