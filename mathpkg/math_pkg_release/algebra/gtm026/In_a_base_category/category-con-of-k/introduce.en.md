$\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ is the category whose objects are concrete categories over a base category $\mathcal{K}$ and whose morphisms are homomorphisms of concrete categories (functors over $\mathcal{K}$). Composition is ordinary functor composition and identities are identity functors. This construction organizes all categories of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure into a single categorical framework. A notable caveat: $\text{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ is not guaranteed to be locally small even when $\mathcal{K}$ is, due to size issues with the collection of concrete categories.
