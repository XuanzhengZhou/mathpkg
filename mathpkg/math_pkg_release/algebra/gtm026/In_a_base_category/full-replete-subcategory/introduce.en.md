A subcategory $\mathcal{A}$ of $\mathcal{B}$ is full replete if it is closed under isomorphisms within $\mathcal{B}$: whenever an object of $\mathcal{B}$ is isomorphic to an object in the image of the inclusion functor, it already belongs to $\mathcal{A}$. Together with fullness and the representative property, repleteness characterizes when a subcategory inclusion is actually an isomorphism of categories: $U$ is an isomorphism if and only if $U$ is a full, representative, replete subcategory.
