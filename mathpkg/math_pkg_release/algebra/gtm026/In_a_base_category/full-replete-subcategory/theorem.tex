Let $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ be a functor. Then $U$ is a \textbf{full replete subcategory} of $\mathcal{B}$ if every object of $\mathcal{B}$ that is isomorphic to an object of the form $AU$ (for some $A \in \mathcal{A}$) is itself in the image of $U$; that is, for every $B \in \mathcal{B}$, if there exists $A \in \mathcal{A}$ and an isomorphism $B \cong AU$ in $\mathcal{B}$, then there exists $A' \in \mathcal{A}$ with $A'U = B$.
