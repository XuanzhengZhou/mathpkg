A subcategory $\mathcal{A}$ of $\mathcal{B}$ is full if every morphism in $\mathcal{B}$ between objects that lie in $\mathcal{A}$ is also a morphism in $\mathcal{A}$. In other words, the inclusion functor is full (and faithful by definition of subcategory). Full subcategories are the most common type of subcategory encountered in practice, as they are determined entirely by their objects — one simply selects a subclass of objects and retains all morphisms between them. The text notes that the image of a faithful functor need not be closed under composition, so it may fail to be a full subcategory.
