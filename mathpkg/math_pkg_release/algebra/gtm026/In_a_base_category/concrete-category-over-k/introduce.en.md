A concrete category over a base category $\mathcal{K}$ is a category $\mathcal{A}$ equipped with a faithful forgetful functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ that assigns to each structured object its underlying $\mathcal{K}$-object. This formalizes the intuitive idea of "objects with additional structure": the faithfulness condition ensures that a morphism of structured objects is uniquely determined by its underlying morphism in $\mathcal{K}$. Examples include groups (with forgetful functor to sets), topological spaces (to sets), and topological groups (to groups).
