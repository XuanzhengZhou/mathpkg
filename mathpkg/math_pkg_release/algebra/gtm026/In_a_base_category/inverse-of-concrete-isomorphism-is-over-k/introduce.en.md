If a concrete homomorphism $H: (\mathcal{A}, U) \to (\mathcal{B}, V)$ is an isomorphism of the underlying categories, then its inverse $H^{-1}$ automatically respects the forgetful functors (i.e., satisfies $V H^{-1} = U$). This means that for concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$, being isomorphic as concrete categories is equivalent to being isomorphic as plain categories — the faithfulness condition on the forgetful functors ensures that the inverse functor automatically commutes with them.
