For any collection $\mathcal{X}$ of $T$-algebras, show that the class $\mathrm{Var}(\mathcal{X})$ of all $T$-algebras satisfying all equations that hold in every algebra of $\mathcal{X}$ is a variety.

[For the following three exercises (implicit in [Birkhoff '35, page 141]) fix an algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$. A variety in $\mathbf{Set}^T$ is a collection of $T$-algebras closed under the formation of products, subalgebras, and quotients.]
