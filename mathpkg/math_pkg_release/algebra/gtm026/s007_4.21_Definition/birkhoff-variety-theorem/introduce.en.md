Birkhoff's Variety Theorem (also known as the HSP Theorem) is a fundamental result in universal algebra characterizing equationally definable classes of algebras. A class of algebras is a variety -- that is, definable by a set of equations -- if and only if it is closed under homomorphic images (H), subalgebras (S), and products (P). This theorem establishes the precise correspondence between syntactic equational presentations and semantic closure properties, and is central to the structural theory of algebraic theories developed in this chapter.
