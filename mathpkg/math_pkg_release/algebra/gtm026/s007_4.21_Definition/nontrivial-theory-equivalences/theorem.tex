Let $T = (T, \eta, \mu)$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$. The following conditions on $T$ are equivalent:

1. $T$ is nontrivial (i.e., there exists a $T$-algebra with at least two elements).
2. For each set $X$, $X\eta: X \longrightarrow XT$ is injective.
3. $T: \mathbf{Set} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Set}$ is a faithful functor, that is, whenever $f, g: X \longrightarrow Y$ are distinct functions, then $fT, gT: XT \longrightarrow YT$ are again distinct.
