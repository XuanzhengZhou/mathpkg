This proposition characterizes nontrivial algebraic theories through three equivalent conditions. A theory is nontrivial if it admits an algebra with at least two elements. Equivalently, the unit $\eta$ of the monad is componentwise injective, or the underlying functor $T$ is faithful. The proof uses cartesian powers of algebras to construct an injective map into a $T$-algebra, demonstrating the interplay between the category-theoretic and universal-algebraic perspectives on algebraic theories.
