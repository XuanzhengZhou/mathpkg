The support and arity of a semantic operation generalize the familiar notion of the number of variables an operation depends on. A subset $S \subset X$ is a support of a semantic operation $\alpha$ if the value of $\alpha$ on any $X$-tuple depends only on the coordinates indexed by $S$. The arity is the minimum cardinality of a support, representing the smallest number of variables needed to express the operation. For example, in abelian group theory, the operation sending $f: X \to Y$ to $xf + x'f$ has arity 2, with support $\{x, x'\}$. These definitions are essential for the theorem that syntactic and semantic arity coincide (5.11).
