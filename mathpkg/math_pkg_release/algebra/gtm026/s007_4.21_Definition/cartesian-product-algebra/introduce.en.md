The cartesian product algebra generalizes the familiar product of algebraic structures (such as product of groups or semigroups) to an arbitrary family of $\Omega$-algebras over a fixed operator domain $\Omega$. Given a family of $\Omega$-algebras $(X_i, \delta_i)$, the cartesian product set $\prod X_i$ is equipped with a unique $\Omega$-algebra structure that makes every projection map an $\Omega$-homomorphism. This construction is fundamental in universal algebra, as it provides the product object in the category of $\Omega$-algebras.
