Let $\Omega$ be an operator domain. Given a family $((X_i, \delta_i): i \in I)$ of $\Omega$-algebras, the cartesian product set $X = \prod X_i$ admits a unique $\Omega$-algebra structure $\delta$ such that for all $i \in I$ the projection $\mathrm{pr}_i: (X, \delta) \longrightarrow (X_i, \delta_i)$ is an $\Omega$-homomorphism, namely:

$$((x_i^1), \ldots, (x_i^n)) \delta_\omega = ((x_i^1, \ldots, x_i^n)) (\delta_i)_\omega: i \in I$$

$(X, \delta)$ is called the cartesian product algebra of the algebras $(X_i, \delta_i)$.
