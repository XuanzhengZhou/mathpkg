This lemma states that the image of an injective $\Omega$-homomorphism is a subalgebra of the codomain, and that the corestriction to the image is an isomorphism. It is a standard fact used in universal algebra to conclude that an injective homomorphism provides an isomorphic embedding onto a subalgebra. The statement is used in the proof of Birkhoff's Variety Theorem (4.22) to establish that $XT$ is isomorphic to a subalgebra of a product of algebras from the given class.
