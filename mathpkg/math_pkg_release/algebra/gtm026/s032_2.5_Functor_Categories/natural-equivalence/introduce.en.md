A natural equivalence (or natural isomorphism) is an isomorphism in a functor category. It is a natural transformation whose every component is an isomorphism in the target category. Natural equivalences are the appropriate notion of "sameness" for functors, and they play a central role in category theory, particularly in the characterization of adjunctions and equivalences of categories.
