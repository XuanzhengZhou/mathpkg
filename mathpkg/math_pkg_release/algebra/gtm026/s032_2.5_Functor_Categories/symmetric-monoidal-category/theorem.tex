A \text{symmetric monoidal category} is a monoidal category $(\mathcal{C}, \otimes, I, a, b, c)$ equipped with a natural equivalence $d$, called the \text{symmetry}, whose components are
$$
(A, B)d: A \otimes B \longrightarrow B \otimes A
$$
satisfying coherence conditions with the associativity and unit constraints. Examples include:
\begin{itemize}
\item $(\mathcal{K}, \times, 1, a, b, c, d)$ for any category $\mathcal{K}$ with finite products (the cartesian monoidal structure).
\end{itemize}
