Let $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ and $(\mathcal{A}', U') \in \mathrm{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ be algebraic, so that there exist algebraic theories $T, T'$ in $\mathcal{K}$ with $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ isomorphic to $(\mathcal{K}^T, U^T)$ and $(\mathcal{A}', U')$ isomorphic to $(\mathcal{K}^{T'}, U^{T'})$. Then $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ and $(\mathcal{A}', U')$ are isomorphic in $\mathrm{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ if and only if $T$ and $T'$ are isomorphic in $\mathrm{Th}(\mathcal{K})$.
