Let $H: \mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{L} \rightarrow \mathcal{M}$ be a ``prefunctor'' assigning to each object $(K, L)$ an object $(K, L)H$ of $\mathcal{M}$ and to each morphism $(f, g): (K, L) \rightarrow (K', L')$ a morphism $(f, g)H: (K, L)H \rightarrow (K', L')H$. Show that $H$ is a functor if and only if for every $K \in \mathcal{K}$, $H(K, -): \mathcal{L} \rightarrow \mathcal{M}$ is a functor and for every $L \in \mathcal{L}$, $H(-, L): \mathcal{K} \rightarrow \mathcal{M}$ is a functor.
