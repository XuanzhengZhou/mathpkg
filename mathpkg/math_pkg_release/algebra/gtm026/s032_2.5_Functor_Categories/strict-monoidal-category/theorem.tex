A \text{strict monoidal category} is a monoidal category $(\mathcal{C}, \otimes, I, a, b, c)$ in which the natural isomorphisms $a$ (associativity), $b$ (left unit), and $c$ (right unit) are all identity transformations. In particular, the endofunctor category $(\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K}, \mathrm{comp}, \mathrm{id}_\mathcal{K})$ is a strict monoidal category.
