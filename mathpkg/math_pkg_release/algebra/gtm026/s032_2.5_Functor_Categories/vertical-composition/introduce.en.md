Vertical composition of natural transformations defines the monoidal product on the endofunctor category $\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K}$. Given two natural transformations $\alpha: F \to F'$ and $\beta: G \to G'$, their vertical composition is defined via whiskering and satisfies the interchange law. This structure makes $\mathcal{K}^\mathcal{K}$ into a strict monoidal category where the tensor product is functor composition and the unit is the identity functor.
