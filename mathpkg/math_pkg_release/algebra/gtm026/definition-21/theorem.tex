If $\emptyset T \neq \emptyset$ then every $T$-algebra is nonempty and possesses a unique minimal subalgebra, namely the subalgebra generated by the empty set. For example, if $T$ corresponds to "rings with unit," $\{0, 1\}$ is the unique minimal subring of every ring. This situation is very uninteresting. If $T$ is the theory corresponding to "semigroups" then $\emptyset T = \emptyset$. Here it is not the case that every semigroup possesses a minimal semigroup; consider the semigroup of natural numbers greater than 0 under addition.
