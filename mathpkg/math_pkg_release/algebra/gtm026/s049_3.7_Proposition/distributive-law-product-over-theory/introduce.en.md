For any set $X$ and any algebraic theory $\mathbf{T} = (T, e, m)$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, there is a canonical distributive law of the product functor $-\times X$ over $\mathbf{T}$. The law is given at each set $Q$ by mapping a pair $(p, x)$ consisting of a $\mathbf{T}$-term $p$ on $Q$ and an element $x \in X$ to the $\mathbf{T}$-term on $Q \times X$ obtained by applying the functor $T$ to the insertion map $\mathrm{in}_x(q) = (q, x)$. This construction provides the foundation for modeling probabilistic automata and fuzzy systems where the input alphabet $X$ interacts with the algebraic structure of the transition monad.
