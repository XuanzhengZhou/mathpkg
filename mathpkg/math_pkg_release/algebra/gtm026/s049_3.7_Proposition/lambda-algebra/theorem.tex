Let $\lambda \colon TX \longrightarrow XT$ be a distributive law of the functor $X \colon \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ over the algebraic theory $\mathbf{T} = (T, e, m)$ as in 3.6. A $\lambda$-algebra is a triple $(Q, \delta, \xi)$ where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $(Q, \delta)$ is an $X$-dynamics,
\item $(Q, \xi)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra,
\end{itemize}
subject to the $\lambda$-law that $\xi \colon (QT, Q\lambda \cdot \delta T) \longrightarrow (Q, \xi)$ is an $X$-dynamorphism. Equivalently, for every $x \in X$, the transition map $\delta_x \colon Q \longrightarrow QT$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism.
