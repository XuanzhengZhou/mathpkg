A $\lambda$-algebra generalizes the concept of an $X$-dynamorphism to the setting where a monad $\mathbf{T}$ and a functor $X$ are linked by a distributive law $\lambda$. It consists of an object $Q$ that simultaneously carries the structure of an $X$-dynamics (governing input transitions) and a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra (governing the algebraic theory operations), with the compatibility condition that the $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structure map is an $X$-dynamorphism. The category $\mathcal{K}^{\lambda}$ of $\lambda$-algebras is always algebraic over $\mathcal{K}$, making it a natural setting for studying fuzzy system response where reachability and observability morphisms are homomorphisms of $\lambda$-algebras.
