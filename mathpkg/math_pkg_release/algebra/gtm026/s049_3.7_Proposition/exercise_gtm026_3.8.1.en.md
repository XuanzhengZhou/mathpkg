Let $\mathbf{T}$ be the theory for abelian groups and write a typical element of the free abelian group $QT$ as $\sum_q n_q q$ where $n_q \in \mathbb{Z}$. Let $X = X_{\Omega}$ be the input process with $\Omega_2 = \{\cdot\}$ and $\Omega_n = \emptyset$ for $n \neq 2$, so that $QX = Q \times Q$. Define $\lambda \colon TX \longrightarrow XT$ by
$$
\lambda_Q \colon QT \times QT \longrightarrow (Q \times Q)T,
$$
$$
\sum_q n_q q \;\cdot\; \sum_r m_r r \;\longmapsto\; \sum_{q,r} n_q m_r (q \cdot r).
$$

Verify that $\lambda$ is a distributive law of $X$ over $\mathbf{T}$ by checking the two axioms from Definition 3.6 (the unit axiom and the multiplication axiom).
