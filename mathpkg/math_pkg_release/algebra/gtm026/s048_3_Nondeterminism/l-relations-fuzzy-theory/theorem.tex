Let $L$ be a complete distributive lattice, that is, a complete lattice in which for each $x \in L$ the map $x \wedge (-) : L \longrightarrow L$ preserves suprema. $L$ induces a fuzzy theory $(T, e, \circ)$ as follows:

$$AT = L^A$$

$Ae : A \longrightarrow AT$ sends $a$ to its characteristic function $\chi_a : A \longrightarrow L$ defined by $a' \chi_a = 1$ (the greatest element of $L$) if $a' = a$, and $a' \chi_a = 0$ (the least element of $L$) otherwise.

Given $\alpha : A \longrightarrow L^B$, $\beta : B \longrightarrow L^C$, the composition $\alpha \circ \beta : A \longrightarrow L^C$ is given by:

$$c(\alpha \circ \beta) = \bigvee_{b \in B} (b\alpha \wedge c\beta)$$

where $\bigvee$ denotes supremum in $L$.
