The L-relations fuzzy theory, due to Goguen (1967), is induced by a complete distributive lattice $L$. For each set $A$, $AT = L^A$ is the set of all $L$-valued fuzzy subsets of $A$. The unit $Ae$ sends each element to its characteristic function, and composition of fuzzy morphisms is defined via the lattice operations: the $L$-valued relation composition uses suprema (for existential quantification) and meets (for conjunction). This construction unifies many examples of fuzzy theories, with the lattice $L$ representing the "space of truth values."
