The probability distributions fuzzy theory is a subtheory of the matrices theory of 1.3.6 that models probabilistic uncertainty. For each set $Q$, $QT$ is the set of all probability distributions on $Q$, i.e., assignments of nonnegative weights summing to 1 to the elements of $Q$. The fuzzy morphisms in this theory are row-stochastic matrices, and the fact that this forms a subtheory reduces to the well-known result that the composition of row-stochastic matrices is again row-stochastic. This theory provides the algebraic foundation for modeling probabilistic automata.
