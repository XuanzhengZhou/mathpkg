The canonical map $Q\lambda : QT \times X \to (Q \times X)T$ is a structural morphism arising when $\mathbf{T}$ is the nonempty subset theory and $X$ is a set. It sends a nonempty subset $A$ of $Q$ together with an input $x \in X$ to the subset $A \times \{x\}$ of $Q \times X$. This map is the key ingredient that allows multiple branching dynamics $\delta: Q \times X \to QT$ to be transformed into ordinary dynamics on the set of fuzzy states $QT$, and it exemplifies the concept of a distributive law of the functor $-\times X$ over the theory $\mathbf{T}$.
