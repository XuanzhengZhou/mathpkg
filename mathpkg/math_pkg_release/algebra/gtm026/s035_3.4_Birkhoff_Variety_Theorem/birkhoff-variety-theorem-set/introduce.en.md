This theorem specializes the categorical Birkhoff Variety Theorem (3.4) to the category of sets. It establishes the equivalence between the classical HSP characterization of varieties (closure under Homomorphic images, Subalgebras, and Products, as formulated in Theorem 1.4.22) and the modern categorical definition of an abstract Birkhoff subcategory. This result bridges classical universal algebra with the categorical approach, showing that the notion of "variety of algebras" in the sense of Birkhoff coincides exactly with the categorical concept developed in Chapter 3.
