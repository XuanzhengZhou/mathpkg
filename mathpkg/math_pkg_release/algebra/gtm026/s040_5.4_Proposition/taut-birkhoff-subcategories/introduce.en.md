Proposition 5.10 addresses a key simplification: when an abstract Birkhoff subcategory $\mathcal{C}$ of $\mathcal{P}$ satisfies the additional "taut" condition (Definition 2.3.37), the algebraic theory $\hat{T}$ in $\mathcal{A}$ that gives rise to $V: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{A}$ admits a very simple description. The taut condition requires that if a family of morphisms from $P$ to objects in $\mathcal{C}$ induces an optimal family in $\mathcal{A}$ after applying $U_1$, then $P$ itself lies in $\mathcal{C}$. Under this hypothesis, $\hat{T}$ is constructed by replacing the structure map $s$ with the cartesian lift $\hat{s}$ of a suitable family, and the identity on $KT$ serves as the reflection map. This mirrors the construction of the canonical lift $\bar{T}$ from 5.2.
