It suffices to show that $K\lambda: (K, s)\bar{T}_1 \longrightarrow (K, s)\bar{T}_2$ is admissible in $\mathcal{A}$ for all $(K, s)$ in $\mathcal{A}$. Using the notations of the proof of 5.2, let $(f, L, \xi_2, t) \in \mathcal{F}_2(K, s)$. Define $\xi_1 = L\lambda \circ \xi_2$. Then $(K, \xi_1)$ is a $T_1$-algebra (see the diagram above). Moreover, $K\lambda \circ fT_2 \circ \xi_2 = fT_1 \circ \xi_1 \in \mathcal{F}_1(K, s)$, so $K\lambda$ is admissible. $\square$
