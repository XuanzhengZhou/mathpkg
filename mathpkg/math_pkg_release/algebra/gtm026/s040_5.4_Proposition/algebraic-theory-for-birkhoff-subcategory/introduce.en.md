Corollary 5.11 returns to the context of Theorem 5.6 and provides an explicit description of the algebraic theory $\hat{T}$ in $\mathcal{A}$ that gives rise to the forgetful functor $V: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{A}$. The construction is a concrete instantiation of Proposition 5.10: $\hat{T}$ is defined on objects by $(X, s)\hat{T} = (XT, \hat{s})$, where $\hat{s}$ is the cartesian lift of a family of $T$-homomorphisms from $KT$ to objects in $\mathcal{C}$. The admissibility of $K\eta$ and closure under composition of admissible maps provide the unit $\eta$ and composition $\circ$ for the algebraic theory structure.
