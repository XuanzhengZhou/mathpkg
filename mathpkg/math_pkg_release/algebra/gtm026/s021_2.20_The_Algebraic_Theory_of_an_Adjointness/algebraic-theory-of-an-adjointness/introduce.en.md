Given an adjointness $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{K}, U, F, \eta, \varepsilon)$ between categories, one can construct an algebraic theory (monad) $\mathbf{T} = (T, \eta, \mu)$ on the base category $\mathcal{K}$. The functor part is $T = FU$, the unit is the unit $\eta$ of the adjunction, and the multiplication $\mu = F\varepsilon U$ is derived from the counit. This construction establishes the fundamental link between adjunctions and monads, showing that every adjunction gives rise to a monad on the category of the right adjoint.
