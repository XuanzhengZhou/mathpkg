Let $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{K}, U, F, \eta, \varepsilon)$ be an adjointness with induced algebraic theory $\mathbf{T} = (T, \eta, \mu)$ where $T = FU$ and $\mu = F\varepsilon U$. The assignment

$$A \longmapsto (AU, A\varepsilon U)$$

defines a functor $\Phi\colon \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ such that the following diagram of functors commutes:

\[
\begin{CD}
\mathcal{A} @>{\Phi}>> \mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}} \\
@V{U}VV @VV{U^{\mathbf{T}}}V \\
\mathcal{K} @= \mathcal{K}
\end{CD}
\]

where $U^{\mathbf{T}}$ is the forgetful functor from the Eilenberg-Moore category. $\Phi$ is called the semantics comparison functor of the adjointness. Roughly speaking, $\Phi$ measures the extent to which an object $A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ is the $\mathcal{K}$-object $AU$ "with algebraic structure."
