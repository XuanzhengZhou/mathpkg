Let $U\colon \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ be a functor and let $K$ be an object in $\mathcal{K}$. $U$ satisfies the solution set condition at $K$ provided there exists a small set $\mathcal{S}_K$ of pairs $(S, s)$ with $S$ in $\mathcal{A}$ and $s\colon K \longrightarrow SU$ such that for every object $A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ and every morphism $f\colon K \longrightarrow AU$ in $\mathcal{K}$, there exists $(S, s) \in \mathcal{S}_K$ and a morphism $g\colon S \longrightarrow A$ in $\mathcal{A}$ with

\[
\begin{CD}
K @>{s}>> SU \\
@V{f}VV @VV{gU}V \\
AU @= AU
\end{CD}
\]

commuting, i.e., $f = s \cdot gU$.
