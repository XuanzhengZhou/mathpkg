This proposition establishes a fundamental necessary condition for a functor to be a right adjoint: any functor $U\colon \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ that possesses a left adjoint must preserve all limits that exist in $\mathcal{A}$. The proof uses the adjointness data and the universal property of limits, leveraging the bijection between hom-sets to construct the required unique map. This result is a key tool for ruling out the existence of a left adjoint and is a special case of the more general fact that right adjoints preserve limits (and left adjoints preserve colimits).
