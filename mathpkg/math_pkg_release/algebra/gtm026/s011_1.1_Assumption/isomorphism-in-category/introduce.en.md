An isomorphism in a category is a morphism with a two-sided inverse. This generalizes the familiar notions: in Set, isomorphisms are bijections; in the category of groups, they are bijective homomorphisms; in the category of topological spaces, they are homeomorphisms (not merely continuous bijections). The fundamental philosophical principle of category theory is that isomorphic objects are abstractly the same -- categorical constructions are typically unique only up to isomorphism.
