Let $(A_i : i \in I)$ be a family of objects in a category $\mathcal{K}$. A \textbf{product} of $(A_i)$ is an object $A$ together with morphisms $p_i: A \longrightarrow A_i$ (called \textbf{projections}) such that for every object $A'$ and family of morphisms $f_i: A' \longrightarrow A_i$ there exists a unique $\mathcal{K}$-morphism $f: A' \longrightarrow A$ satisfying
$$f.p_i = f_i \quad \text{for all } i \in I.$$
