In the category of metric spaces with distance-decreasing (non-expansive) maps, products exist precisely when the sup-metric is finite on every pair of tuples. The underlying set must be the usual Cartesian product, and since projections must be distance-decreasing, the product metric is forced to be the sup-metric. This category has all finite products but only some infinite products, providing a first example where products do not always exist.
