A terminal object in a category is the empty product -- the product of an empty family of objects. By the universal property of the product, for any object $A$ there is a unique morphism from $A$ to the terminal object. In Set, any one-element set is terminal; in the category of groups, the trivial group is terminal. Terminal objects, like all objects defined by universal properties, are unique up to unique isomorphism.
