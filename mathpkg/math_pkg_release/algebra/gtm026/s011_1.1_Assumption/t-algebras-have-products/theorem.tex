Let $\mathcal{K}$ have products and let $\mathbf{T}$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$. Then the category $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ of $\mathbf{T}$-algebras and $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphisms has products. Moreover, if $(A_i, \xi_i)$ is a family of $\mathbf{T}$-algebras and $p_i: A \longrightarrow A_i$ is a product in $\mathcal{K}$ of the underlying objects, then there exists a unique $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structure $\xi$ on $A$ making $(A, \xi)$ the product in $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ with projections $p_i$.
