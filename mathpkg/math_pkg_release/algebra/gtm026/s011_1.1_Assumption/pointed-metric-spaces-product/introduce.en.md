In the category of pointed metric spaces with distance-decreasing base-point-preserving maps, every family has a product -- but the underlying set is a proper subset of the usual Cartesian product. The product set consists of those tuples whose distances from the base-point tuple are uniformly bounded. This provides an important counterexample showing that, unlike in Set or Top, the underlying set of a product in a category of structured sets need not be the usual Cartesian product.
