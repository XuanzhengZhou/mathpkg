Consider the category whose objects are metric spaces with base point $(X, d, \bar{x})$ (where $\bar{x} \in X$ is arbitrary) and whose morphisms are distance-decreasing base-point-preserving functions ($xf = x'$). Every family $(X_i, d_i, \bar{x}_i)$ has a product $(X, d, \bar{x})$ where $X$ is the subset of the usual product of the $X_i$ consisting of all tuples $(x_i)$ such that
$$\sup\{d_i(x_i, \bar{x}_i) : i \in I\}$$
is finite. The metric is
$$d((x_i), (y_i)) = \sup\{d_i(x_i, y_i) : i \in I\},$$
which is finite by the definition of $X$ and the triangle inequality. The base point is $(\bar{x}_i)$ and projections are the restrictions of the usual ones. In general, $X$ is a proper subset of the usual product set.
