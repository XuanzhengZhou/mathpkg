This proposition extends the pattern of categorical universal algebra: the category of T-algebras inherits equalizers from the base category. Given two T-homomorphisms, one takes their equalizer in the base category K and lifts the T-algebra structure to the equalizer object. The proof parallels the corresponding result for products -- the algebraic structure lifts uniquely to the limit, and the universal property in K^T follows from the universal property in K together with the T-homomorphism condition.
