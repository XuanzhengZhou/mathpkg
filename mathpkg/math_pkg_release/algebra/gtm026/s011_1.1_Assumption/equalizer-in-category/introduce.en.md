An equalizer is the categorical generalization of the subset on which two functions agree. Given two parallel morphisms $f, g: A_1 \to A_2$, their equalizer is a universal arrow $i: E \to A_1$ that equalizes $f$ and $g$. Most categories of sets with structure have equalizers: for topological spaces use the relative topology, for metric spaces restrict the metric, and for groups the equalizing subset is a subgroup. Like products, equalizers are unique up to isomorphism by the standard universal property argument.
