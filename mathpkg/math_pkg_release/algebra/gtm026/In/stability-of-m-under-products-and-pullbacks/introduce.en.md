Proposition 4.12 establishes the stability of the class $M$ under two important categorical constructions: products and pullbacks. First, an arbitrary product of $M$-morphisms (when the products exist) remains in $M$. Second, the pullback of an $M$-morphism along any morphism also belongs to $M$. Both statements are proved using the characterization of $M$ from Proposition 4.11 together with the diagonal fill-in property (4.10). These stability properties are essential for the theory of regular categories and for proving that various subcategories defined by closure conditions (e.g., Birkhoff subcategories) are well-behaved.
