An image factorization system in a category formalizes the idea of uniquely factoring every morphism into an "epimorphism part" followed by a "monomorphism part." The pair $(E, M)$ consists of two subcategories of morphisms — the $E$-morphisms play the role of surjections and the $M$-morphisms play the role of embeddings. The axioms require that every isomorphism belongs to both classes and that every morphism admits an essentially unique factorization through an intermediate object $\mathrm{Im}(f)$. This axiomatic framework generalizes the familiar epi-mono factorizations in concrete categories such as sets, groups, and topological spaces.
