This proposition states that any morphism belonging to both the $E$ and $M$ classes of an image factorization system is automatically an isomorphism. This is a formal consequence of the uniqueness clause in axiom (4.5): factor $f$ as $f = \mathrm{id} \cdot f$ (with $E$ containing isomorphisms, so $\mathrm{id} \in E$) and also as $f = f \cdot \mathrm{id}$ (with $\mathrm{id} \in M$). The uniqueness of the factorization forces $f$ to be an isomorphism. This result generalizes the elementary observation that a morphism which is both epic and monic need not be an isomorphism in a general category, but within a factorization system the additional structure guarantees the converse.
