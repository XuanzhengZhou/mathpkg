In a cartesian closed category, there is a natural isomorphism between $(X^m)^n$ and $(X^n)^m$, both being isomorphic to the exponential $X^{m \times n}$. For the category of sets (and more generally for any category with finite products), this "transpose" isomorphism $\Phi$ is defined by swapping the order of arguments: a family of $m$-tuples indexed by $n$ is equivalent to a family of $n$-tuples indexed by $m$. This isomorphism plays a technical but essential role in the diagrammatic proof of the Symmetry Proposition for $S$-$T$ bialgebras.
