The tensor product $\mathbf{Set}^S \otimes T$ of algebraic theories sits as a full replete subcategory of the product category $\mathbf{Set}^S \times \mathbf{Set}^T$. Despite the product category not necessarily being algebraic over $\mathbf{Set}$, its subcategory of $S$-$T$ bialgebras enjoys Birkhoff-type closure properties: it is closed under products, subalgebras, and quotients. The equational nature of the bialgebra condition (each $T$-operation is an $S$-homomorphism and vice versa) ensures these closure properties, making the underlying set functor a Beck functor.
