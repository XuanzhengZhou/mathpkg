An ultrafilter $\mathcal{U}$ on $X$ gives rise to a $\beta$-operation on the set $2$, i.e., a map $\tilde{\mathcal{U}}: 2^X \to 2$ that is the characteristic function of $\mathcal{U}$ itself. This operation evaluates to $1$ precisely on those subsets belonging to the ultrafilter. The non-continuity of $\tilde{\mathcal{U}}$ in the Tychonoff topology (for non-principal ultrafilters on infinite $X$) is a key counterexample in the theory of $\beta$-bialgebras.
