As an alternative measure of certainty in nondeterministic systems, one may assign probabilities: for each state $r$ in $Q$, $\lambda_r$ denotes the probability that the current state $q$ equals $r$. This leads to the probability distribution subtheory of the matrices theory, where $QT$ consists of all probability distributions on $Q$, i.e., tuples $(\lambda_b : b \in B)$ with nonnegative entries summing to $1$. Fuzzy morphisms in this theory correspond to row-stochastic matrices, and the closure under composition reduces to the fact that products of row-stochastic matrices remain row-stochastic. Every convex subset of a real vector space is an algebra over this theory.
