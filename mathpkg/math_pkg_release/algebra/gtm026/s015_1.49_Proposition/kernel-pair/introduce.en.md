The kernel pair of a morphism $f: A \to B$ is the pullback of $f$ along itself. In the category of sets, the kernel pair is precisely the equivalence relation induced by $f$ on its domain $A$, consisting of pairs $(x, y)$ with $f(x) = f(y)$. This construction generalizes the notion of kernel from algebraic categories to arbitrary categories with pullbacks. The kernel pair plays a crucial role in constructing coequalizer-mono factorizations: when both the kernel pair and its coequalizer exist, the induced map from the coequalizer to the codomain provides the monomorphism part of the factorization.
