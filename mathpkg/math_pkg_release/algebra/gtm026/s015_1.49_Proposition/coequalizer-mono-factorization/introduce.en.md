A coequalizer-mono factorization of a morphism $f$ expresses $f$ as the composition $f = p \cdot i$, where $p$ is a coequalizer and $i$ is a monomorphism. This is analogous to the familiar image factorization in concrete categories, where every map factors as a surjection onto its image followed by an injection. The dual notion is the epi-equalizer factorization, where $f = p \cdot i$ with $p$ an epimorphism and $i$ an equalizer. A category $\mathcal{K}$ is said to have coequalizer-mono factorizations if every morphism admits such a factorization.
