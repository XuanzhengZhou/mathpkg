Given morphisms $f: A \longrightarrow C$ and $g: B \longrightarrow C$ in a category $\mathcal{H}$, a **pullback** of $(f, g, C)$ is an object $P$ equipped with morphisms $a: P \longrightarrow A$ and $b: P \longrightarrow B$ such that $a \cdot f = b \cdot g$, and universal with this property: for any object $X$ and morphisms $x: X \longrightarrow A$, $y: X \longrightarrow B$ with $x \cdot f = y \cdot g$, there exists a unique morphism $h: X \longrightarrow P$ with $h \cdot a = x$ and $h \cdot b = y$. The square $a \cdot f = b \cdot g$ is called a **pullback square**, and $b$ is the **pullback of $f$ along $g$**.
