Proposition 1.49 establishes that coequalizer-mono factorizations are unique up to isomorphism. If a morphism $f$ can be factored in two ways as a coequalizer followed by a monomorphism, there exists a unique invertible mediating morphism between the intermediate objects that makes both factorization diagrams commute. This result is analogous to the essential uniqueness of image factorizations, and it underpins the well-definedness of the coequalizer-mono factorization as a categorical construct.
