The justification follows from the representation $x = \{y : y \text{ is an ordinal and } y < x\}$ (Monk 9.13 (iii)). Since every ordinal is precisely the set of all smaller ordinals, specifying $f(y)$ for all $y < x$ uniquely determines $f$ on the entire ordinal $x$.
