Russell's paradox reveals that unrestricted comprehension (forming the set of all sets satisfying a given property) leads to a contradiction. The set $R$ of all sets that do not contain themselves cannot consistently exist as a set. The standard resolution in Zermelo-Fraenkel set theory is to restrict comprehension to separation over already-existing sets, relegating $R$ to the status of a proper class.
