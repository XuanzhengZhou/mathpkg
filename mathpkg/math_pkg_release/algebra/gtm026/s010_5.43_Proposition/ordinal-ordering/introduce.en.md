Ordinals are linearly ordered by the membership relation $\in$, which coincides with the subset relation $\subset$ for ordinals. Any nonempty set of ordinals has a least element (given by the intersection of all ordinals in the set). These properties make $\mathbf{Ord}$ a well-ordered proper class, fundamental for transfinite constructions.
