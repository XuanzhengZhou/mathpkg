Given an abstract Birkhoff subcategory $\mathcal{A}$ of $\mathcal{K}^T$, Lemma 3.2 constructs a new algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}'$ and a theory map $\lambda: \mathbf{T} \to \mathbf{T}'$ that encodes the reflection of free algebras into $\mathcal{A}$. The construction uses the Kleisli extension operation: for each $X$, the reflection $X\lambda: (XT, X\mu) \to XT'$ provides the object map, while the composition $\circ$ on $\mathbf{T}'$ is defined via the unique $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism extension $\beta^{\#\#}$. This lemma establishes the fundamental correspondence between Birkhoff subcategories and quotient theories, which is the core of the abstract Birkhoff classification theorem.
