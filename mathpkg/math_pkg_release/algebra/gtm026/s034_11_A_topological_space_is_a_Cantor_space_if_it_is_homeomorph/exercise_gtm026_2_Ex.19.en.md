[Bergman '75.] Let $A$ be the category of groups but let $U: A \longrightarrow \mathbf{Set}$ be the subfunctor of the forgetful functor defined by $GU = \{x \in G : x^2 = e\}$. An involution algebra is a triple $(X, m, e)$ with $m: X^2 \longrightarrow X$, $e \in X$ subject to

$$exm = e, \quad xymym = x,$$
$$xem = x, \quad xyzmm = xzymzm,$$
$$xxm = x.$$

Prove that the algebraic completion (as in exercise 12) of $U$ is the equationally-definable class of involution algebras.
