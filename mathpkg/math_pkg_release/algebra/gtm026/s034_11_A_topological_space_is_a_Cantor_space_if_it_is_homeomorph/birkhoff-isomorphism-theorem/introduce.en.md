Theorem 3.3 is the central classification result for abstract Birkhoff subcategories. It states that every abstract Birkhoff subcategory $\mathcal{A}$ of $\mathcal{K}^T$ is categorically isomorphic to the category of algebras $\mathcal{K}^{T'}$ for a quotient theory $\mathbf{T}'$ obtained via Lemma 3.2. Consequently, $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ is algebraic over $\mathcal{K}$ in the sense of $\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$. This generalizes the classical Birkhoff HSP theorem: when $\mathcal{K} = \mathbf{Set}$, the conditions of Definition 3.1 reduce to closure under products, subalgebras, and homomorphic images, and the theorem recovers the familiar variety-theory correspondence.
