[Guitart '75.] A theory with involution in $\mathcal{K}$ is a pair $(T, I)$ where $T$ is an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ and $I: \mathcal{K}_T \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}_T$ is an involution on the Kleisli category of $T$, that is, $I$ is the identity on objects and $I^2 = \mathrm{id}$ on morphisms. A contravariant monad in $\mathcal{K}$ is a triple $(F, i, \psi)$ where $F: \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}^{\mathrm{op}}$, $Ai: A \longrightarrow AF$, $A\psi: AF^2 \longrightarrow A$ satisfy suitable axioms.

(c) Show that $(F^2, i \cdot \psi: \mathrm{id} \longrightarrow F^2, F t F: F^4 \longrightarrow F^2)$ is an algebraic theory for each contravariant monad $(F, i, \psi)$. Observe that in (b) this constructs the double power-set theory.
