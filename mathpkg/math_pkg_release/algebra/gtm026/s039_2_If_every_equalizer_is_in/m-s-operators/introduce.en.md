The operators $\mathsf{M}$, $\mathsf{S}$, and $\mathsf{P}$ act on classes of objects in a category by closing under $\mathsf{M}$-morphisms, split epimorphisms, and products respectively. Their composites $\mathsf{MP}$ and $\mathsf{SMP}$ describe how to generate a quasivariety from a given class $\mathcal{A}$: $\mathsf{MP}(\mathcal{A})$ is the ordinary quasivariety generated by $\mathcal{A}$, while $\mathsf{SMP}(\mathcal{A})$ is the smallest quasivariety containing $\mathcal{A}$ that is additionally closed under split epimorphic images.
