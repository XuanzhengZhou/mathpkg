Let $\mathcal{K}$ be the category of metric spaces with base point as in 2.1.12. Verify that $(\mathcal{K}, \mathcal{E}, \mathcal{M})$ is a regular category with respect to the following choice of $(\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{M})$:

$$\mathcal{E} = \text{coequalizers},\quad \mathcal{M} = \text{injective maps}.$$
