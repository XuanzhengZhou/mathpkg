For $(K, s)$ in $\mathcal{A}$, let $\mathcal{F}(K, s)$ denote the class of all $(f, L, \xi, t)$ such that $f : K \to L \in \mathcal{K}$, $f : (K, s) \to (L, t)$ is admissible in $\mathcal{A}$, and $(L, \xi)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra. The fibre-completeness of $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ ensures that for each $(K, s)$ this class admits an initial lift, which yields the free $\mathbf{T}$-algebra over $(K, s)$. The algebraic structure of $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ then transfers along the pullback to make $(\mathcal{P}, U_1)$ algebraic, with the left adjoint to $U_1$ constructed via the composition of the free $\mathbf{T}$-algebra functor and the initial lift in $\mathcal{A}$.
