The co-concept algorithm is the systematic rule for forming the dual of any categorical concept. Given a term naming a categorical notion, one obtains its dual by toggling the prefix "co-": if the term already begins with "co-", remove it; otherwise, prepend "co-". For example, the dual of "product" is "coproduct", and the dual of "coproduct" is "product". This convention streamlines the passage between a category and its opposite, making duality arguments transparent.
