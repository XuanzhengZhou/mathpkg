In the category of sets, coproducts exist and are given by the disjoint union construction. The $i$th injection map embeds each component $A_i$ by tagging its elements with the index $i$, producing the set $\{(i,a): i \in I, a \in A_i\}$. The universal property is straightforward: a family of functions $f_i: A_i \to B$ uniquely determines a function on the disjoint union by applying $f_i$ to the element from the $i$th component. This is the simplest concrete example of a coproduct.
