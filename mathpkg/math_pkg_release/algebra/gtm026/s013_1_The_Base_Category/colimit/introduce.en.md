A colimit is the categorical dual of a limit. Given a diagram $(\Delta, D)$ in a category $\mathcal{K}$, its colimit is obtained by first dualizing the diagram scheme $\Delta$ (reversing all arrows) and then taking the limit of the resulting diagram in the opposite category $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$. Intuitively, while a limit maps into the diagram, a colimit receives maps from the diagram. Coproducts and coequalizers are special cases of colimits (discrete diagrams and parallel pairs, respectively). The colimit is the universal cocone, characterized by an initiality property dual to the terminality property of limits.
