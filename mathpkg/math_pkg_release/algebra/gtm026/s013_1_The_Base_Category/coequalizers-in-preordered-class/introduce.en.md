In a preordered class regarded as a thin category (at most one morphism between any two objects), any parallel pair $f, g: A \to B$ must satisfy $f = g$. Consequently, the coequalizer of any such pair is trivially the identity morphism on $B$. This illustrates how coequalizers degenerate in thin categories, where the only nontrivial identifications are between objects forced to be equal by the existence of a morphism.
