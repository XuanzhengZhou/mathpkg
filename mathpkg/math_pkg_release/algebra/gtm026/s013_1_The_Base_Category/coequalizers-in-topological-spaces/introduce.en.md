The category of topological spaces has coequalizers. Given two continuous maps $f, g: A \to B$, one first computes the set-theoretic coequalizer $Q = B/R$ as the quotient by the equivalence relation generated by identifying $f(a)$ with $g(a)$. Endowing $Q$ with the quotient topology yields the coequalizer in $\mathbf{Top}$. The universal property lifting follows from the set-theoretic construction together with the universal property of the quotient topology.
