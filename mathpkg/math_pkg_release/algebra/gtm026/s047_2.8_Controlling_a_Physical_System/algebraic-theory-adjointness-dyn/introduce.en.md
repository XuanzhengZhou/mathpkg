Lemma 2.12 characterizes the algebraic theory arising from the adjunction between the category $\text{Dyn}(X)$ of $X$-dynamics and the base category $\mathcal{K}$. When $X$ is an input process, the free algebraic theory $X^@ = (X^@, \eta, \mu)$ over $X$ has multiplication $\mu$ given by the run-map extension $\mu_0^{\#}$. The semantics comparison functor $\Phi$ sends each $X$-dynamics $(Q, \delta)$ to its associated $X^@$-algebra $(Q, \delta^{\#})$, where $\delta^{\#}$ is the unique dynamorphic extension of the identity on $Q$.
