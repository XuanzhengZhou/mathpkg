A free object over an object $K$ with respect to a functor $U$ is the categorical abstraction of the notion of a free algebraic structure. The definition captures the universal property that any morphism from $K$ to the underlying object of some $\mathcal{A}$-object factors uniquely through the free object. This generalizes the construction of free $\mathbf{T}$-algebras from Section 1.4 to the setting of an arbitrary functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$, laying the foundation for the theory of adjoint functors.
