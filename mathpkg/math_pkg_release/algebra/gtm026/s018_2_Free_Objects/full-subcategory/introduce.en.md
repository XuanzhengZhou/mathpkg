A full subcategory is one in which the hom-sets between any two objects coincide with those of the ambient category. In other words, a full subcategory is determined entirely by which objects of the ambient category are included; all morphisms between those objects are automatically present. Full subcategories play a fundamental role in category theory, as many important subcategories (e.g., abelian groups within groups, compact Hausdorff spaces within topological spaces) are full.
