A subcategory $\mathcal{L}$ of $\mathcal{K}$ is \textbf{full} if for every pair of objects $L_1, L_2$ in $\mathcal{L}$, the induced map
$$
\mathcal{L}(L_1, L_2) \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}(L_1, L_2)
$$
is bijective. Equivalently, a literal subcategory $\mathcal{L}$ of $\mathcal{K}$ is full if $\mathcal{L}(L_1, L_2) = \mathcal{K}(L_1, L_2)$ for all $L_1, L_2$ in $\mathcal{L}$.
