Let $\mathcal{C}$ be the category of categories and functors. A \textbf{subcategory} of $\mathcal{K}$ is a $\mathcal{C}$-monosubobject of $\mathcal{K}$ as defined in 1.65. That is, a subcategory is given by a functor $H: \mathcal{L} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ such that for all $L_1, L_2$ in $\mathcal{L}$, the induced maps
$$
\mathcal{L}(L_1, L_2) \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}(L_1 H, L_2 H)
$$
are injective.
