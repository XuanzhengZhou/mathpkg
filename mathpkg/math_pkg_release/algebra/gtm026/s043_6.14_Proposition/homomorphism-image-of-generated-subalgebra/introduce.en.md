For a $T$-homomorphism $f \colon (X,\xi) \to (Y,\theta)$ between $T$-algebras over $\mathbf{Set}$, the image under $f$ of the $T$-subalgebra generated by a subset $A \subseteq X$ equals the $T$-subalgebra generated by the direct image $Af \subseteq Y$. In symbols, $\langle A \rangle f = \langle Af \rangle$. This result is fundamental for understanding how the subalgebra-closure operator $\langle - \rangle$ interacts with structure-preserving maps.
