For a $T$-algebra $(X,\xi)$, the set $\mathcal{O}_n(X,\xi)$ of all $n$-ary $T$-operations on $(X,\xi)$ naturally forms a $T$-subalgebra of the power algebra $(X,\xi)^{(X^n)}$. This is shown by considering the injective map $p \colon n \to (X,\xi)^{(X^n)}$ sending $i$ to the $i$-th projection; the composite $pT \cdot \theta$ is a $T$-homomorphism whose image is exactly $\mathcal{O}_n(X,\xi)$, and it sends a formal $n$-ary operation $\omega$ to the derived operation it induces on $(X,\xi)$.
