When the target algebra $(Y,\theta)$ is completely commutative, the collection of all $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphisms from any $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(X,\xi)$ to $(Y,\theta)$ naturally forms a $\mathbf{T}$-subalgebra of the power algebra $(Y,\theta)^X$. The proof relies on the fact that operations on the power algebra are computed pointwise (since projections are $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphisms) and on the complete commutativity of $(Y,\theta)$ to interchange the order of operations. This result, Proposition 6.23, is due to Freyd and Linton.
