(a) For any algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ show that every pair of $T$-algebras has a tensor product. [Hint: define $(X, \xi) \otimes (Y, \theta) = (X \times Y)T/R$ where $R$ is the intersection of all congruences of the form $R_f = \{(x, y): xf^\# = yf^\#\}$ with $f: (X, \xi); (Y, \theta) \to (Z, \gamma)$ bi-admissible.]

(b) ([Freyd '66], [Linton '66-A]). If $T$ is a commutative theory in $\mathbf{Set}$ show that $\mathbf{Set}^T$ is a closed category with the tensor product of (a).

(c) Show that the passage from $(Z, u)$ to the set of bi-admissible maps $(X, s); (Y, t) \to (Z, u)$ describes a functor $U: \mathcal{C} \to \mathbf{Set}$ such that $(X, s) \otimes (Y, t)$ exists if and only if there exists a free $\mathcal{C}$-object over $1$ with respect to $U$. Give an alternate proof of (a) using the adjoint functor theorem.
