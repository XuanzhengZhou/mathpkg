This proposition gives another sufficient condition for the existence of the tensor product $S \otimes T$. If every syntactic operation of $S$ has arity 1 (so $S$ is essentially a monoid acting on sets), then $S \otimes T$ exists for every theory $T$. The proof exploits 1.5.40, which shows that $\mathbf{Set}^S$ can be viewed as $(\Omega, E)$-alg with only unary operation symbols, and constructs an explicit $S$-$T$ bialgebra structure on $XST$.
