In a category with binary products, any three objects $A, B, C$ give rise to an associativity isomorphism $(A,B,C)a: A \times (B \times C) \to (A \times B) \times C$. This isomorphism and its inverse are natural transformations between the two ways of forming triple products as functors $\mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{K}$. The existence of such a natural equivalence is the key motivation for the pentagon coherence axiom in the definition of a monoidal category.
