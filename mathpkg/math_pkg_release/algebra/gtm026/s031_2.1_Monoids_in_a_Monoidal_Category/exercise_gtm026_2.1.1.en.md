Show that the associativity isomorphism $(A, B, C)a: A \times (B \times C) \to (A \times B) \times C$ (defined via the universal property of the product) is indeed an isomorphism. Moreover, show that both $a$ and $a^{-1}$ are natural transformations of functors from $\mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K} \times \mathcal{K}$ to $\mathcal{K}$; that is, $a$ is a natural equivalence.
