A monoid in a monoidal category $(\mathcal{K}, \otimes, I, a, b, c)$ generalizes the notion of an ordinary monoid from the category of sets. It consists of an object $K$ together with a multiplication morphism $m: K \otimes K \to K$ and a unit morphism $e: I \to K$, subject to associativity and unit coherence laws expressed as commutative diagrams. When $\mathcal{K} = \mathsf{Set}$ and $\otimes = \times$, this recovers the classical definition: $(X, m, e)$ where $m: X \times X \to X$ is a binary associative operation and $e: 1 \to X$ picks out a two-sided unit element.
