The Kleisli Composition Law expresses clone composition $\circ$ in terms of the extension operator $(-)^{\#}$ and the monoid-form structure map $\mu$. For each morphism $\beta: B \rightarrow C$, its extension $\beta^{\#}: BT \rightarrow CT$ is defined via $C\mu$, and composition with $\alpha: A \rightarrow B$ is recovered as $\alpha.\beta^{\#}$. This law provides the bridge between the clone form $(T, \eta, \circ)$ and the monoid form $(T, \eta, \mu)$ of an algebraic theory, and is the inverse passage used in Theorem 3.18.
