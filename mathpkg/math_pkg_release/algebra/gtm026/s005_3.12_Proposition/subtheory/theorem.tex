Let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$, and let $T'$ assign to each set $A$ a subset $AT'$ of $AT$. $T'$ is a subtheory of $T$ if:

\begin{enumerate}
\item For all $A$, the image of $A\eta$ is a subset of $AT'$ (thereby defining the map $A\eta': A \longrightarrow AT'$).
\item For all $\alpha: A \longrightarrow BT'$ and $\beta: B \longrightarrow CT'$, the image of
$$\left( A \xrightarrow{\alpha} BT' \hookrightarrow BT \right) \circ \left( B \xrightarrow{\beta} CT' \hookrightarrow CT \right)$$
is a subset of $CT'$ (thereby defining $\alpha \circ' \beta: A \longrightarrow CT'$).
\end{enumerate}

Clearly $(T', \eta', \circ')$ is an algebraic theory if $T'$ is a subtheory of $T$.
