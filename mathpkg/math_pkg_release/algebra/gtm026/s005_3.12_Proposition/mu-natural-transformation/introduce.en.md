This proposition establishes that the multiplication $\mu: TT \rightarrow T$ of an algebraic theory in monoid form is a natural transformation. Specifically, for every morphism $f: A \rightarrow B$ in the base category $\mathcal{K}$, the square formed by $fTT$, $fT$, $A\mu$, and $B\mu$ commutes. This naturality condition is one of the fundamental coherence axioms relating the functor $T$ and the multiplication $\mu$, and is essential for the equivalence between clone form and monoid form established in Theorem 3.18.
