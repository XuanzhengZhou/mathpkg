A monoid $(X, m, e)$ is a set $X$ equipped with a binary associative operation $m: X \times X \longrightarrow X$ with a two-sided unit $e \in X$. Express the associative law as a commutative diagram. Similarly, recognizing that $e$ is a function $e: 1 \longrightarrow X$ from the one-element set $1$, express the unit laws as a commutative diagram.
(Hint: see 3.2.3.)
