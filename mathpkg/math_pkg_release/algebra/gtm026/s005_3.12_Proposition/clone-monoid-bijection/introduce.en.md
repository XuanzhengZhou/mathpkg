This theorem establishes that the two presentations of algebraic theories — clone form $(T, \eta, \circ)$ and monoid form $(T, \eta, \mu)$ — are equivalent via a bijective correspondence. The forward direction constructs $\mu$, $fT$, and the naturality proofs from the clone operations, while the inverse direction recovers the clone composition via the Kleisli formula $\alpha \circ \beta = \alpha.\beta^{\#}$ (3.14). This result shows that Lawvere's theories and Eilenberg-Moore triples/monads are coextensive notions, unifying the two foundational approaches to universal algebra in category theory.
