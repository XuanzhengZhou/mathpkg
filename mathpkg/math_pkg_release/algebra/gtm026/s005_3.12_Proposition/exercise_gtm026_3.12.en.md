The motivation for Definition 3.2 stressed $(-)^{\#}$ as more basic than $\circ$, and an alternate definition is easily given. An algebraic theory in extension form is $\mathbf{T} = (T, \eta, (-)^{\#})$ with $T$ and $\eta$ as in 3.2, and with $(-)^{\#}$ assigning to each $\alpha: A \longrightarrow BT$ an "extension" $\alpha^{\#}: AT \longrightarrow BT$ subject to the three axioms "$A\eta.\alpha^{\#} = \alpha$," 2.14, and 2.12. Show that extension form and clone form are coextensive via the passages 2.8 and $\alpha^{\#} = \mathrm{id}_{AT} \circ \alpha$. We have preferred clone form because the associative law for $\circ$ is more natural in appearance than 2.12.
