The Ultrafilter Theory is a fundamental example of an algebraic theory (monad) in the category $\mathbf{Set}$. For each set $X$, $XT$ is the set of all ultrafilters on $X$. The unit $\eta$ sends each element to its principal ultrafilter, the functorial action $fT$ pushes ultrafilters forward along a function $f$, and the multiplication $\mu$ sums an ultrafilter of ultrafilters into a single ultrafilter via a Kowalsky-style sum. This monad captures the notion of convergence in topology and is closely related to the Stone-Cech compactification. The proof that $(T, \eta, \circ)$ satisfies the algebraic theory axioms is left as an exercise.
