A fixpoint-free action is one where no non-identity element of the acting object fixes any point. The condition that $(q, -)m$ is injective means that if two elements of $Y$ act the same way on $q$, they must be equal -- equivalently, the stabilizer of every point is trivial. This is the algebraic analogue of a free group action. When $\mathcal{K} = \mathbf{Set}$, principal objects with fixpoint-free $Y$-actions correspond precisely to $H^1$, providing a geometric interpretation of the first cohomology group.
