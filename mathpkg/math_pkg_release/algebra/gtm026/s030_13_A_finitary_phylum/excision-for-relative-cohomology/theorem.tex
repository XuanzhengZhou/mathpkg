Let $f: (X, \xi, A) \rightarrow (\bar{X}, \bar{\xi}, \bar{A})$ be a morphism of $\mathbf{T}$-algebras with distinguished subalgebras (i.e., a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism $f: (X, \xi) \rightarrow (\bar{X}, \bar{\xi})$ mapping $A$ into $\bar{A}$). If $f$ restricts to a bijection from $X - A$ to $\bar{X} - \bar{A}$, then the induced map

$$H^n f: H^n(X, \xi, A) \rightarrow H^n(\bar{X}, \bar{\xi}, \bar{A})$$

is an isomorphism for all $n \geq 0$.
