Triple cohomology (also called comonad cohomology or Beck cohomology) associates to each algebra $(X, \xi)$ for an algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$ a cochain complex built from the canonical resolution (or "bar resolution") of $(X, \xi)$. The coefficients lie in an abelian group object $(Y, \theta)$ internal to the category of $\mathbf{T}$-algebras. This theory unifies many classical cohomology theories: group cohomology, Lie algebra cohomology, Hochschild cohomology of associative algebras, and others arise as special cases for appropriate choices of $\mathbf{T}$ and $(Y, \theta)$.
