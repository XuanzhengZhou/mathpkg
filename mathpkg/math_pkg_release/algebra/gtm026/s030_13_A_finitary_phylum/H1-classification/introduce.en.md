The first triple cohomology group $H^1(X, \xi)$ classifies extensions of the algebra $(X, \xi)$ by the coefficient abelian group object $(Y, \theta)$. Concretely, $1$-cocycles correspond to $\mathbf{T}$-algebra structures on the product $Y \times X$ that are compatible with the projection to $(X, \xi)$ and the action of $Y$ on itself. Two such structures are cohomologous (differ by a $1$-coboundary) precisely when they are isomorphic via a map that preserves the projection and is compatible with the $Y$-action. This generalizes the classical fact that $H^1(G, M)$ classifies group extensions.
