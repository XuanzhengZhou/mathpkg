A **principal object** over $(X, \xi)$ is a tuple $(E, \gamma, p, m, s)$ where:

- $p: (E, \gamma) \rightarrow (X, \xi)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism;
- $m: (Y, \theta) \times (E, \gamma) \rightarrow (E, \gamma)$ is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism;
- $s: X \rightarrow E$ is a $\mathcal{K}$-morphism;

subject to the following conditions:

(1) The diagram commutes:
\[
\begin{CD}
Y \times Y \times E @>{+ \times \text{id}}>> Y \times E\\
@V{\text{id} \times m}VV @VV{m}V\\
Y \times E @>>{m}> E
\end{CD}
\]

(2) $(Y, \theta) \times (E, \gamma) \xrightarrow{m} (E, \gamma)$ is an action;

(3) The square
\[
\begin{CD}
Y \times E @>{m}>> E\\
@V{\text{pr}_E}VV @VV{p}V\\
E @>>{p}> X
\end{CD}
\]
is a pullback in $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$.

A **morphism of principal objects** is a $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphism preserving $m$ and $p$ (but ignoring the choice of section $s$).
