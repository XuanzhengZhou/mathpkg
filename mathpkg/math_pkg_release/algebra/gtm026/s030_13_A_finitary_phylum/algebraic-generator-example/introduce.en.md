The free algebra on a nonempty set is always an algebraic generator. This generalizes the fact that in many categories of algebras (groups, rings, modules), the free object on one generator is a generator in the usual categorical sense. The proof uses the adjunction between the free functor and the forgetful functor, together with the weakly algebraic property of the functor $U_3 = (-)^n$.
