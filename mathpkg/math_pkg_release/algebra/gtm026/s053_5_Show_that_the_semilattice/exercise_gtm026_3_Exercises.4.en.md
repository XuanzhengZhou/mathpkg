Let $\lambda$ be a distributive law of $X$ over $T$. Show that the unique $\lambda$-homomorphic extension of $f: A \longrightarrow (Q, \delta, \xi)$ can be constructed as the unique $T$-homomorphic extension of the unique dynamorphic extension as indicated above. Conclude that, when $\mathcal{H} = \mathbf{Set}$, if $A$ is a subset of a $\lambda$-algebra, then the $\lambda$-subalgebra generated by $A$ is just the $T$-subalgebra of the $X^@$-subalgebra generated by $A$. Can you generalize this statement to regular categories?
