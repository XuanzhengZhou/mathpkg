Prove the Yoneda lemma: given $U: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathbf{Set}$ and $A$ in $\mathcal{A}$, the class of natural transformations from $\mathcal{A}(A, -)$ to $U$ is a small set, indeed is in bijective correspondence with $AU$. [Hint: generalize 1.5.6, 1.5.7.]
