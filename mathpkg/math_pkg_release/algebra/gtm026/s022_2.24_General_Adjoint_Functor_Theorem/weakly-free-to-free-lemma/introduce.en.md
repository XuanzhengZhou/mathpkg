Lemma 2.25 provides the crucial second step in the proof of the General Adjoint Functor Theorem. Given a weakly free object $(F', \eta')$ and a set $C$ of endomorphisms of $F'$ that fix $\eta'$ under $U$, the lemma shows that taking the collective equalizer $i: F \to F'$ of $C$ (assumed preserved by $U$) yields a genuinely free object $(F, \eta)$ over $K$ with respect to $U$. The proof uses the fact that the collective equalizer $i$ is a monomorphism and that $U$ preserves equalizers of pairs to establish uniqueness of the mediating morphism.
