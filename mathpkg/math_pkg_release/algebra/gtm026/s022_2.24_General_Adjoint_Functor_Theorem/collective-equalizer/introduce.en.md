A collective equalizer generalizes the ordinary equalizer from a pair of parallel morphisms to an arbitrary set $C$ of parallel morphisms from $A_1$ to $A_2$. The morphism $i: E \to A_1$ coequalizes all pairs from $C$ jointly — that is, $ix = iy$ for all $x, y \in C$ — and is universal among such morphisms. This concept is used in Lemma 2.25 to refine a weakly free object into a genuinely free one by taking the collective equalizer of all endomorphisms $x: F' \to F'$ satisfying $\eta'.xU = \eta'$.
