Let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ be a functor, let $K$ be an object in $\mathcal{K}$, and let $(F', \eta')$ be a weakly free $\mathcal{A}$-object over $K$ with respect to $U$ with the property that the set
$$C = \{F' \xrightarrow{x} F': \eta'.xU = \eta'\}$$
has a collective equalizer $i: F \longrightarrow F'$ which is preserved by $U$ (that is $iU$ is the collective equalizer of $\{xU: x \in C\}$). Assume further that every pair of $\mathcal{A}$-morphisms has an equalizer and that $U$ preserves these equalizers. Then, for suitable $\eta$, $(F, \eta)$ is free over $K$ with respect to $U$.
