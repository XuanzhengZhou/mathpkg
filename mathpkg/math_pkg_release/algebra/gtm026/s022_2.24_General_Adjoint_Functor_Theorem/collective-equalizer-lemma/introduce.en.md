This lemma provides the second step in the proof of the General Adjoint Functor Theorem (2.24). Given a weakly free object $(F', \eta')$ over $K$, one takes the collective equalizer of all endomorphisms $x: F' \to F'$ satisfying $\eta'.xU = \eta'$. When this collective equalizer is preserved by $U$, the resulting object $(F, \eta)$ is actually free over $K$, not just weakly free. The lemma isolates the technical heart of Freyd's proof.
