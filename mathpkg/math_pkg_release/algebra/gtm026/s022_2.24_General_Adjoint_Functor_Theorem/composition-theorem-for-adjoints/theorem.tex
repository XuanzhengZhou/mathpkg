Let $U_1: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}$ and $U_2: \mathcal{B} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}$ be functors which have a left adjoint. Then $U_1U_2: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}$ has a left adjoint. Indeed, if $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}, U_1, F_1, \eta_1, \varepsilon_1)$ and $(\mathcal{B}, \mathcal{C}, U_2, F_2, \eta_2, \varepsilon_2)$ are adjointnesses, then so is
$$(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{C}, U_1U_2, F_2F_1, \eta_2.F_2\eta_1U_2, U_1\varepsilon_2F_1.\varepsilon_1).$$
Equivalently, $(U_1U_2)^* = U_2^*U_1^*$.
