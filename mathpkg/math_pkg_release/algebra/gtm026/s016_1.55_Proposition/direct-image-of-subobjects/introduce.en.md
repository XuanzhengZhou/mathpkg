In a category with coequalizer-mono factorizations, every morphism $f: A \to B$ induces a direct image operation on monosubobjects. Given a monosubobject $[i]$ of $A$, its direct image under $f$ is defined as the mono part of the coequalizer-mono factorization of the composite $i \cdot f$. This generalizes the set-theoretic direct image of a subset under a function, and the construction is independent of the choice of representative monomorphism.
