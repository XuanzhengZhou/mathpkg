A monosubobject of an object $X$ in a category is an isomorphism class of monomorphisms with codomain $X$. Two monomorphisms $i: A \to X$ and $j: B \to X$ are isomorphic if each factors through the other via an isomorphism. The class of monosubobjects carries a natural partial order $[i] \leq [j]$ defined by $i$ factoring through $j$. This abstraction captures the notion of "subobject" in a purely categorical way, without reference to elements. In the category of sets, monosubobjects correspond bijectively to subsets.
