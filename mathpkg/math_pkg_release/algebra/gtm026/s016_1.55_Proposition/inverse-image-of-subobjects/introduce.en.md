In a category with pullbacks, every morphism $f: X \to Y$ induces an inverse image operation on monosubobjects of $Y$. Given a monosubobject $[i]$ of $Y$, its inverse image under $f$ is obtained by pulling back the monomorphism $i$ along $f$. The resulting morphism $j$ is also a monomorphism (pullbacks preserve monomorphisms), and its isomorphism class defines a monosubobject of $X$. This generalizes the set-theoretic preimage operation.
