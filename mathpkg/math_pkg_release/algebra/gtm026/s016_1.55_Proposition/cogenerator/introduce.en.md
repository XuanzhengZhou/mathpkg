A cogenerator in a category $\mathcal{K}$ is an object $C$ that can separate distinct parallel morphisms via post-composition. In other words, if two morphisms $f, g: A \to B$ differ, there exists a morphism $h: B \to C$ that witnesses the difference: $f \cdot h \neq g \cdot h$. This is the dual concept of a generator. While generators allow separation by pre-composition, cogenerators separate by post-composition. The circle group $S^1$ in the category of abelian groups and any set with at least two elements in $\text{Set}$ are prototypical examples.
