In the category of sets, any set containing at least two elements is a cogenerator. This follows because if two functions $f, g: A \to B$ differ at some element $a \in A$, one can construct a map $h: B \to C$ (where $|C| \geq 2$) that separates their values at $f(a)$ and $g(a)$. Thus the cogenerator condition in $\text{Set}$ reduces to a very simple size condition.
