Let $\mathcal{K}$ be the category of topological spaces and continuous maps. Given $f:X \rightarrow Y$ in $\mathcal{K}$ show that $f$ is a coequalizer if and only if $f$ is surjective and $Y$ has the quotient topology induced by $f$ (i.e., a subset $B$ of $Y$ is open if and only if $Bf^{-1}$ is open in $X$). Establish the same result in the category $\mathcal{L}$ of metric spaces and distance-decreasing maps. Show that in both categories, $\text{eq}(f, g)$ is the subset on which $f$ and $g$ agree provided with the subspace topology; and show that $\text{eq}(f, g)$ is a closed subset in the Hausdorff case.
