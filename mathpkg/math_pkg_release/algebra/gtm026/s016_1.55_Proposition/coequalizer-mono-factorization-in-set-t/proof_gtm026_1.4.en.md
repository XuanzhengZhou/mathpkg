Let $f:(A, \alpha) \longrightarrow (B, \beta)$ be a $T$-homomorphism. Let $f = (p:A \longrightarrow C).(i:C \longrightarrow B)$ be the usual image factorization at the level of sets. By 1.4.31, there exists a unique $T$-algebra structure $\gamma:CT \longrightarrow C$ on $C$ such that $p$ and $i$ become $T$-homomorphisms. Let $a, b:E \longrightarrow A$ be the kernel pair of $f$ in $\text{Set}$. By analyzing the proof of 1.22 in the context of 1.11 and 1.14, it is clear that there exists a unique $\delta:ET \longrightarrow E$ by virtue of which $a$ and $b$ become $T$-homomorphisms, and then $a, b:(E, \delta) \longrightarrow (A, \alpha)$ is in fact the kernel pair of $f$ in $\text{Set}^T$. Crucial is the use of 1.4.29 which guarantees that $pT$ is epi. $\square$
