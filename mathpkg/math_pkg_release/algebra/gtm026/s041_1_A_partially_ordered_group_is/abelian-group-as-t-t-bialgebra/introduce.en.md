This proposition provides a key example of a bialgebra: when T is the algebraic theory of abelian groups, every T-algebra (i.e., every abelian group) is naturally a T-T bialgebra by using the same structure map for both the S and T algebra components. The verification hinges on the fact that the fundamental operations of abelian groups -- zero, minus, and plus -- are themselves group homomorphisms. This would fail for non-abelian groups, where the group operation is not a homomorphism with respect to itself.
