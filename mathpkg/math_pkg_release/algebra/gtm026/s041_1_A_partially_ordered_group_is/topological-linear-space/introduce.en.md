A topological linear space, also commonly called a topological vector space, is a vector space over the real or complex numbers equipped with a topology that makes the algebraic operations continuous. This compatibility ensures that translation and scaling are homeomorphisms, making the topological and algebraic structures work harmoniously. Topological linear spaces and continuous linear maps between them form a category that is algebraic over the category of topological spaces, as explored in the exercises of Manes' "Algebraic Theories."
