Let $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ be fibre-complete over $\mathbf{Set}$. Recall from exercise 3(b) of section 2 that $\mathcal{A}$ is a symmetric monoidal category.

Prove that $\mathcal{A}$ is closed as defined in exercise 5 of section 2. [Hint: the exponential object $B^A$ -- which we write hence as $[A, B]$ -- is the set of admissible maps from $A$ to $B$ with the "subspace of the product" structure, i.e., the restricted projections are optimal.] Observe that $[A, -] \colon \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{A}$ and $[-, A] \colon \mathcal{A}^{\text{op}} \longrightarrow \mathcal{A}$ are functorial.
