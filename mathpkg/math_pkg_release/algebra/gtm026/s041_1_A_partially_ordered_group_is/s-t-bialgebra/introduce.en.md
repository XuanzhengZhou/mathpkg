An S-T bialgebra is a set simultaneously equipped with algebra structures from two algebraic theories S and T, subject to a compatibility condition: every semantic T-operation must be an S-homomorphism with respect to the S-algebra structure. This captures the idea that the operations of one theory commute with those of the other. Examples include rings (where addition and multiplication structures interact), groups with operators, and bimodules. The question of whether the bialgebra category is algebraic over sets is delicate and depends on whether both theories have a rank.
