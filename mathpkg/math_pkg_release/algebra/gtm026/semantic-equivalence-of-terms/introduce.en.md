Given an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$, two Omega-terms $p$ and $q$ over the variable set $A$ are semantically equivalent (written $p E_A q$) if they evaluate to the same element in every $(\Omega, E)$-algebra under every assignment of variables. This captures the notion that $p$ and $q$ are indistinguishable by the equations of the theory — for instance, in group theory, $a^{-1}(ab)$ and $b$ have the same interpretation in all groups. The quotient $AT = A\Omega / E_A$ is the free $(\Omega, E)$-algebra on $A$, whose elements are equivalence classes of terms modulo the theory.
