The Hierarchy Theorem for Epimorphisms establishes a chain of implications among the three categorical notions of surjectivity: every split epimorphism is a coequalizer, and every coequalizer is an epimorphism. Thus split epimorphisms are the strongest notion and epimorphisms the weakest. The first implication is proved constructively: if $f$ has a left inverse $d$, then $f = \mathrm{coeq}(\mathrm{id}_A, f \cdot d)$. The second implication—that coequalizers are epimorphisms—follows by duality from the corresponding result for equalizers and monomorphisms (proved in 1.15).
