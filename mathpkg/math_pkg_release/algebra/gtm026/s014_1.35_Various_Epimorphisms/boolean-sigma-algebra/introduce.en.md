A Boolean $\sigma$-algebra extends the notion of a Boolean algebra by requiring the existence of suprema and infima for countably infinite families, not just finite ones. This additional completeness property is essential in measure theory, where one works with countable unions and intersections of measurable sets. A fundamental structure in measure theory consists of a set together with a sub Boolean $\sigma$-algebra of its power set.
