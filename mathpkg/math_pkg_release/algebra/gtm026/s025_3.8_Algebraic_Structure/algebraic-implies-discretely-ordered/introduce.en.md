Algebraic structure implies the discretely ordered property. In other words, if a category of $K$-objects with structure arises from an algebraic theory (i.e., is equivalent to $K^T$ for some algebraic theory $T$ in $K$), then it is necessarily discretely ordered. This establishes algebraicity as a stronger condition that entails the discrete-order behavior of fibres.
