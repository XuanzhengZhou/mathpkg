The dual of a concrete category $(A, U)$ over $K$ is obtained by passing to the opposite categories: $(A^{\text{op}}, U^{\text{op}})$ becomes a concrete category over $K^{\text{op}}$. This construction is essential for defining coalgebraic structure by duality: a structured category is coalgebraic over $K$ precisely when its dual is algebraic over $K^{\text{op}}$.
