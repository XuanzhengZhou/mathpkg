A concrete category $(A, U)$ over $K$ is weakly algebraic when its structural reflection—the free structured category generated by the concrete category as defined in section 3.5—is an algebraic structure over $K$. This provides a broader notion than being algebraic, capturing categories whose structure can be algebraically characterized after passing to the structural reflection.
