In the category of topological spaces over sets, the optimal lift of a family of maps $f_i: K \to (L_i, \mathcal{T}_i)$ is the initial (weak) topology on $K$ making all $f_i$ continuous, generated by the subbase of preimages $U f_i^{-1}$ of open sets. This is traditionally called the smallest or weakest topology with this property. The empty-family optimal lift yields the indiscrete topology, and dually the empty co-optimal lift yields the discrete topology.
