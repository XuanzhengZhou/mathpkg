An operator domain formalizes the notion of a signature in universal algebra. It consists of a family of sets $(\Omega_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ indexed by natural numbers, where each $\Omega_n$ contains the labels of operations that take $n$ arguments. For example, the operator domain for groups has $\Omega_0 = \{e\}$ (the identity element, a nullary operation), $\Omega_1 = \{i\}$ (inversion), and $\Omega_2 = \{m\}$ (multiplication). An operator domain may be viewed as a directed graph whose nodes are natural numbers and whose edges terminate at 1, foreshadowing its interpretation as a category.
