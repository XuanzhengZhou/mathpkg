Proposition 3.32 states that the property of being fibre-complete is preserved under arbitrary products in $\text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$. If $\mathcal{C}_i$ are fibre-complete categories of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure, then their product $\prod_i \mathcal{C}_i$ (constructed as in 3.7) is again fibre-complete. This is a closure property that ensures fibre-completeness is stable under product constructions within the Struct framework.
