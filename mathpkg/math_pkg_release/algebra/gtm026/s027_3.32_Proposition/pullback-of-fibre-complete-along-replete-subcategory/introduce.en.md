Proposition 3.36 states that fibre-completeness is preserved under pullback along a full replete subcategory. If $\mathcal{C} \in \text{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ is fibre-complete over $\mathcal{K}$, and $\mathcal{L} \hookrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ is a full replete subcategory, then the pullback $\mathcal{P} = \mathcal{C} \times_{\mathcal{K}} \mathcal{L}$ (which is the full subcategory of $\mathcal{C}$ on objects whose underlying $\mathcal{K}$-object lies in $\mathcal{L}$) is fibre-complete over $\mathcal{L}$. An important application: if $\mathcal{K}$ is topological spaces, $\mathcal{C}$ is topological groups, and $\mathcal{L}$ is Hausdorff spaces, then $\mathcal{P}$ is separated topological groups, which is fibre-complete over Hausdorff spaces.
