Theorem 4.15 establishes that the algebraic theory construction provides a perfect correspondence between the syntactic and semantic descriptions of finitary algebraic structures. On one side, an $(\Omega, E)$-algebra is a set equipped with operations $\omega_X: X^n \to X$ for each $\omega \in \Omega_n$, satisfying the equations $E$. On the other side, the algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$ encodes the same data algebraically: a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(X, \xi)$ interprets each derived operation (formal $\Omega$-word) as an actual operation on $X$ via the structure map $\xi: XT \to X$. The theorem asserts that these two perspectives are equivalent — the categories $\mathbf{Set}^{\mathbf{T}}$ and $(\Omega, E)\text{-}\mathbf{Alg}$ are isomorphic over $\mathbf{Set}$. This result is the culmination of the construction in Chapter 4, showing that algebraic theories faithfully capture equational varieties.
