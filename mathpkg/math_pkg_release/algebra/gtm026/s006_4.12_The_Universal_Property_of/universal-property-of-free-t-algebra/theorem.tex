Let $T$ be an algebraic theory in a category $\mathcal{K}$, let $(X, \xi)$ be a $T$-algebra, and let $f: A \longrightarrow X$ be a $\mathcal{K}$-morphism. Then there exists a unique $T$-homomorphism $f^{\#}: (AT, A\mu) \longrightarrow (X, \xi)$ such that the following diagram commutes:

$$
\begin{CD}
A @= A \\
@V{A\eta}VV @VV{f}V \\
AT @>>{f^{\#}}> X
\end{CD}
$$

Equivalently, $A\eta \cdot f^{\#} = f$. Moreover, the explicit formula for $f^{\#}$ is given by the composite

$$
AT \xrightarrow{fT} XT \xrightarrow{\xi} X,
$$

that is, $f^{\#} = fT \cdot \xi$.
