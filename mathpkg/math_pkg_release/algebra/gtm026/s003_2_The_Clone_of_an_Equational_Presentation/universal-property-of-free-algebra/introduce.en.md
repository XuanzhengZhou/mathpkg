The quotient set $AT = A\Omega / E_A$ of term equivalence classes carries a natural $(\Omega, E)$-algebra structure and satisfies the universal property of a free algebra. The insertion map sends each variable $a \in A$ to its equivalence class $[a]$, and any function from $A$ to an $(\Omega, E)$-algebra extends uniquely to an $\Omega$-homomorphism from $AT$. This means $AT$ has "just enough relations to satisfy $E$, but no more," making it the free $(\Omega, E)$-algebra on $A$.
