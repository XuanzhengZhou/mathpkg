"Lattices" constitute an equationally-definable class (e.g., see exercise 3.2.10d). A lattice is modular if $(x \wedge b) \vee a = (x \vee a) \wedge b$ whenever $a \leq b$. Prove that modular lattices constitute an equationally-definable class.
