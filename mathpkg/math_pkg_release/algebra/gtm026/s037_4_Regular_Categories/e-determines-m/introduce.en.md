In an image factorization system $(E, M)$, the class $M$ is completely determined by $E$ via a diagonal fill-in condition. Specifically, a morphism $t$ belongs to $M$ if and only if it has the right lifting property with respect to every morphism in $E$: for any commutative square with left edge in $E$ and bottom edge $t$, there exists a diagonal fill-in making the upper triangle commute. Together with the dual statement (that $E$ is determined by $M$), this shows that specifying either class uniquely pins down the factorization system.
