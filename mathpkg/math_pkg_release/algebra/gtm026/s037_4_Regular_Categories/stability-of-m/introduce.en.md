The class $M$ in an image factorization system is stable under the formation of products and pullbacks. If each morphism in a family belongs to $M$, then the product morphism induced between the products also belongs to $M$. Moreover, if a morphism belongs to $M$, then any pullback of it along an arbitrary morphism again belongs to $M$. Both statements are proved using the diagonal fill-in characterization of $M$ established in Proposition 4.11: one verifies the right lifting property of the constructed morphism with respect to every $e \in E$.
