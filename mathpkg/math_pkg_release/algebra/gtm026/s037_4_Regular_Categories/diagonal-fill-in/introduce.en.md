The diagonal fill-in property is a fundamental orthogonality-like property of image factorization systems. Given a commutative square where the left vertical map belongs to $E$ and the bottom horizontal map belongs to $M$, there exists a unique diagonal morphism $h$ making both triangles commute. This property encapsulates the fact that $E$-morphisms are "left orthogonal" to $M$-morphisms in the sense of factorization systems, and it follows from the uniqueness of $E$-$M$ factorizations together with the fact that $e$ is an epimorphism (and $m$ a monomorphism).
