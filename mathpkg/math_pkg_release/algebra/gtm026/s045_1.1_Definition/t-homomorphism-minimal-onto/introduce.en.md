This lemma establishes the fundamental relationship between minimality and surjectivity for $T$-homomorphisms. If a $T$-homomorphism is surjective and its domain is minimal, then the codomain is also minimal — minimality transfers forward along surjective homomorphisms. Conversely, if the codomain is minimal and the domain is nonempty, then any $T$-homomorphism must be surjective, meaning that minimal algebras can only receive homomorphisms that cover them entirely. The proof uses the fact that $U^T$ creates pullbacks to relate subalgebras of domain and codomain.
