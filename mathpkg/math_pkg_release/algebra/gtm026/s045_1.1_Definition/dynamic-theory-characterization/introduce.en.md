Proposition 1.12 is the central theorem of this section. It establishes that the dynamical property of the theory $T$ — namely, that the enveloping monoid $E(1T, 1\mu)$ possesses a coalescent ideal — is exactly the condition needed to guarantee that every nonempty $T$-algebra contains a minimal subalgebra and that a universal minimal $T$-algebra exists. This result connects the monoid-theoretic notion of coalescent ideals with the algebraic notion of minimal algebras, providing a complete characterization.
