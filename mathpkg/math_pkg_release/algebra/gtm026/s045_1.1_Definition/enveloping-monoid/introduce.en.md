The enveloping monoid $E(X,\xi)$ of a $T$-algebra $(X,\xi)$ is the $T$-subalgebra of the power algebra $(X,\xi)^X$ generated by the identity map $\mathrm{id}_X$. It captures the monoid of unary operations induced by the theory on the algebra. The enveloping monoid plays a central role in the study of minimal algebras because it provides a bridge between the algebraic theory $T$ and the monoid-theoretic properties of the algebra. In fact, $E(X,\xi)$ is isomorphic to the monoid $1S$ of unary operations of the theory corresponding to the Birkhoff subcategory $\mathrm{SMP}(X,\xi)$ generated by $(X,\xi)$.
