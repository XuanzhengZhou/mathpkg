A distal $T$-algebra generalizes the concept of a distal transformation group from topological dynamics to the purely algebraic setting of algebraic theories. For a dynamic theory $T$, five equivalent conditions characterize distality: the enveloping monoid consists entirely of bijections; all its elements are injective; the enveloping monoid itself is minimal; the subalgebra generated by any element in the Birkhoff subcategory generated by $(X,\xi)$ is minimal; and the subalgebra generated by any element in the square $(X,\xi) \times (X,\xi)$ is minimal. The term "distal" reflects that distinct points remain distinct under the action of the enveloping monoid.
