A $T$-algebra $(X, \xi)$ is \textit{minimal} if $X$ is nonempty and if $(X, \xi)$ contains no proper, nonempty $T$-subalgebra.

If $\emptyset T \neq \emptyset$ then every $T$-algebra is nonempty and possesses a unique minimal subalgebra, namely the subalgebra generated by the empty set. If $T$ is the theory corresponding to "semigroups" then $\emptyset T = \emptyset$, and it is not the case that every semigroup possesses a minimal semigroup.
