A theory $T$ is compactible if there exists a theory map from the theory $\beta$ of compact Hausdorff spaces to $T$. This means that every $T$-algebra carries a natural compact Hausdorff topology rendering the operations continuous. The existence of such a theory map implies that the left multiplications on the enveloping monoid are continuous, which is a sufficient condition for dynamical behavior.
