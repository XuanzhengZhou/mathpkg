A scoop of a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ consists of an object $Q$, an inclusion-like morphism $i: Q \to \bar{Q}$, and a retraction $c: \bar{Q} \to QT$ satisfying $c \cdot i^{\#} = \mathrm{id}$, where $i^{\#}$ is the $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphic extension of $i$. The concept formalizes the idea that $Q$ generates $\bar{Q}$ as a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra: in the category of sets, a scoop with the given $(Q, i)$ exists precisely when the image of $Q$ generates $\bar{Q}$. The trivial scoop $(\bar{Q}, \mathrm{id}, Qe)$ always exists.
