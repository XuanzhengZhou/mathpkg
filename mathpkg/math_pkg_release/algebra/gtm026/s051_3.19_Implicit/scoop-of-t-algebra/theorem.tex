Let $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ be a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra. A \textit{scoop} of $(\bar{Q}, \xi)$ is a triple $(Q, i, c)$ where $i: Q \to \bar{Q}$, $c: \bar{Q} \to QT$ are such that
$$c \cdot i^{\#} = \mathrm{id}.$$

By 1.4.31, if $\mathcal{K} = \mathbf{Set}$ then for each $(Q, i)$ there exists a scoop of form $(Q, i, c)$ if and only if the image of $Q$ in $\bar{Q}$ generates $\bar{Q}$ as a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra. There always exists at least one scoop, namely $(\bar{Q}, \mathrm{id}, Qe)$.
