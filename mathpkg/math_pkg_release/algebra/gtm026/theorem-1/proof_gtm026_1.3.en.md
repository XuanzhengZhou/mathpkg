Define $\langle x, X\eta \rangle = \text{prin}(x) \in XT$, where $\text{prin}(x)$ is the principal ultrafilter on $x$ (see 3.21) defined by $\text{prin}(x) = \{A \subset X : x \in A\}$. The proof that $(T, \eta, \circ)$ is an algebraic theory is left as an exercise. For $f: X \longrightarrow Y, fT$ is defined by $\langle \mathcal{A}, fT \rangle = \{B \subset Y : Bf^{-1} \in \mathcal{A}\}$. $X\mu: XTT \longrightarrow XT$ is defined by $\langle \bar{\mathcal{A}}, X\mu \rangle = \{A \subset X : \{A \in XT : A \in \mathcal{A}\} \in \bar{\mathcal{A}}\}$.
