This theorem extends Theorem 4.25 to infinitary algebraic theories, showing that every algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ admits an equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$. The operations $\Omega_n$ are indexed by $nT$ when $n \in \mathrm{Law}(T)$ and are empty otherwise, while the equations $E$ are constructed explicitly as the union of three classes $E_1 \cup E_2 \cup E_3$ encoding the monad structure. The proof constructs the presentation directly rather than relying on a Birkhoff variety argument, providing an explicit and more informative construction.
