A literal category $\mathcal{C}$ of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure is said to be small over $\mathcal{K}$ when each class $\mathcal{C}(K)$ of structures on a given base object $K$ is in fact a small set. This size condition ensures that the category of structures is locally small in the usual sense.
