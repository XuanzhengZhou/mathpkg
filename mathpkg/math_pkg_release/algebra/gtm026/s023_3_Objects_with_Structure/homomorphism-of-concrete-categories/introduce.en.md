A homomorphism between concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$ is a functor $H$ that respects the base: the forgetful functor from $\mathcal{B}$ composed with $H$ equals the forgetful functor from $\mathcal{A}$. Such homomorphisms compose and, together with identity functors, form the morphisms of the category $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$. If $H$ is an isomorphism of categories, then its inverse is automatically over $\mathcal{K}$.
