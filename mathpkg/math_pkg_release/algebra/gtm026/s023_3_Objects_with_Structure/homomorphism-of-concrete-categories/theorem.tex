A \textbf{homomorphism} $H: (\mathcal{A}, U) \to (\mathcal{B}, V)$ of concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$ is a functor $H: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}$ which is \textbf{over $\mathcal{K}$} in the sense that the diagram

$$\xymatrix{
\mathcal{A} \ar[rr]^{H} \ar[dr]_{U} & & \mathcal{B} \ar[dl]^{V} \\
& \mathcal{K} &
}$$

of functors commutes (on objects and on morphisms). Notice that if $H$ is such a homomorphism, $(\mathcal{A}, H)$ is a concrete category over $\mathcal{B}$.
