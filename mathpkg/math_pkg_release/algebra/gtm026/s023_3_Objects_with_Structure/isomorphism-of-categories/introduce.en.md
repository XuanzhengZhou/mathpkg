A functor $U$ is an isomorphism of categories precisely when it is a full, representative, and replete subcategory. This elegant characterization decomposes the notion of isomorphism into three independent properties. The text also discusses the more fundamental notion of equivalence of categories (citing Mac Lane), where a full and faithful functor that is also representative forces the two categories to have very similar internal structure.
