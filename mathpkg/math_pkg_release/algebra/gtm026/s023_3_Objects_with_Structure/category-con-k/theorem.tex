With homomorphisms of concrete categories as morphisms, and using identity functors, we get the \textbf{category $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$} whose objects are concrete categories over $\mathcal{K}$ and whose morphisms are the homomorphisms defined above. Notice that there is no guarantee that $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ is locally small if $\mathcal{K}$ is.
