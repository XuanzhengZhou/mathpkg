A concrete category over a fixed base category $\mathcal{K}$ is a pair $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ consisting of a category $\mathcal{A}$ together with a faithful functor $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$. This generalizes the earlier notion of a literal category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure: if $\mathcal{C}$ is such a literal category with its underlying $\mathcal{K}$-object functor $U$, then $(\mathcal{C}, U)$ is a concrete category over $\mathcal{K}$.
