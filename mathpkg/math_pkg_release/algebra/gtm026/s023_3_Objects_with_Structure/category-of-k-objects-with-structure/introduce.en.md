The extended definition of a category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure generalizes the literal definition from 3.1: a concrete category $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ qualifies if it is isomorphic in $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ to some literal category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure. The text observes that for functors over $\mathcal{K}$, there is no difference between isomorphisms in $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$ and isomorphisms of categories — the inverse is automatically structure-preserving.
