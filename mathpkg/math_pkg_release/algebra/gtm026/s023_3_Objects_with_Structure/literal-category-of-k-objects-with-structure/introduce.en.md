A literal category of $\mathcal{K}$-objects with structure axiomatizes the intuitive notion of "objects of $\mathcal{K}$ equipped with additional structure." Given a base category $\mathcal{K}$, such a category $\mathcal{C}$ assigns to each $\mathcal{K}$-object a class of possible structures and specifies which $\mathcal{K}$-morphisms are admissible between structured objects. The axioms ensure that identity morphisms are always admissible and that admissible morphisms compose. This construction produces a category with a canonical forgetful functor $U: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{K}$, providing the framework for all categories of structured objects considered in the text.
