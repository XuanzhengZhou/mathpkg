A contractible coequalizer is a coequalizer diagram equipped with a contraction $(d, d')$ satisfying specific commutativity conditions. Every contractible coequalizer is an absolute coequalizer, meaning it remains a coequalizer after applying any functor. These notions are fundamental to Beck's characterization theorem for algebraic functors, providing a purely diagrammatic condition that replaces the need for explicit construction of quotients by congruences.
