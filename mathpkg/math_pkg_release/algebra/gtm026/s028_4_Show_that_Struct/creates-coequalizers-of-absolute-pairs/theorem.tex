Let $U: \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{K}$ be a functor. $U$ \textbf{creates coequalizers of $U$-absolute pairs} if whenever we are given a pair of maps $f, g: A \to B$ in $\mathcal{A}$ and a morphism $h: BU \to K$ in $\mathcal{K}$ such that $(fU, gU, h)$ is an absolute coequalizer in $\mathcal{K}$, we may conclude that $h$ has a unique lift $\bar{h}: B \to \bar{K}$ in $\mathcal{A}$ such that $\bar{h}U = h$, and moreover $\bar{h} = \operatorname{coeq}(f, g)$ in $\mathcal{A}$.

Similarly, $U$ \textbf{creates coequalizers of $U$-contractible pairs} if the same holds whenever $(fU, gU, h)$ is a contractible coequalizer in $\mathcal{K}$.
