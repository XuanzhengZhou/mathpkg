A coalgebraic theory in a category $\mathcal{K}$ is an algebraic theory $\mathbf{G} = (G, \varepsilon, \delta)$ in $\mathcal{K}^{\text{op}}$. Let $\mathcal{K}$ be a category and let $K$ be an object. Assume that $K \times A$ exists for all $A$. Define a coalgebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ by $AG = K \times A$, $A\varepsilon: K \times A \longrightarrow A$ the projection, and $A\delta: K \times A \longrightarrow K \times K \times A = \Delta \times \text{id}_A$ where $\Delta: K \longrightarrow K \times K$ is the diagonal map. Show that the category of $\mathbf{G}$-coalgebras is isomorphic over $\mathcal{K}$ to $\mathcal{K}/K$.
