An absolute coequalizer is a coequalizer diagram that is preserved by every functor — not just those with special exactness properties. This is a very strong condition: it means the coequalizer is "equationally" determined and does not depend on any limit or colimit structure of the category. Absolute coequalizers play a fundamental role in Beck's characterization of algebraic functors, where the ability to lift certain absolute coequalizers determines whether a functor is monadic.
