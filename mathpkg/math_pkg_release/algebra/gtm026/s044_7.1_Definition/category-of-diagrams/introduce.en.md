For a diagram scheme $\Delta$, the category $\mathcal{K}^{\Delta}$ of $\Delta$-diagrams in $\mathcal{K}$ captures diagrams as objects and natural-transformation-like families as morphisms. The constant diagram construction embeds $\mathcal{K}$ into $\mathcal{K}^{\Delta}$ by sending each object $K$ to the diagram that is constantly $K$ with identity maps. This embedding functor is central to the study of colimits, as an upper bound of a diagram $D$ corresponds precisely to a diagram morphism from $D$ to a constant diagram.
