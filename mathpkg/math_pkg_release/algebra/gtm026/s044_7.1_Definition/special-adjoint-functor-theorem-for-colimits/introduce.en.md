This theorem provides purely structural sufficient conditions for a category $\mathcal{K}$ to possess all small colimits: it must be locally small, have small limits, be well-powered, and possess a cogenerator. The proof applies the Special Adjoint Functor Theorem to the constant-diagram embedding functor. A classical consequence is that any partially ordered set with all infima also has all suprema, recovering a familiar order-theoretic fact as a corollary of this category-theoretic machinery.
