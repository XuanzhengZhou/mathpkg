The Principle of Finitary Algebraic General Recursion states that a function $\psi$ on the set $A\Omega$ of all $\Omega$-words over $A$ is completely determined by specifying its values on the generators $a \in A$ and by giving a rule for computing $\psi$ on a compound term $p_1 \cdots p_n \omega$ in terms of the values $\psi(p_i)$ on its immediate subterms and the operator symbol $\omega$. This principle is the algebraic analog of definition by recursion in logic and set theory, and it is justified by the Uncoupling Lemma, which guarantees that every $\Omega$-word has a unique decomposition into an outermost operator and its subterms.
