For any algebraic theory $T$ in a category $\mathcal{K}$, the pair $(KT, K\mu; K\eta)$ provides the canonical example of a free $\mathcal{K}^T$-object over an object $K$ with respect to the forgetful functor $U^T: \mathcal{K}^T \to \mathcal{K}$. This concretely illustrates Definition 2.1 by showing that every algebraic theory gives rise to free algebras via its Kleisli construction, generalizing the familiar free algebra constructions in universal algebra.
