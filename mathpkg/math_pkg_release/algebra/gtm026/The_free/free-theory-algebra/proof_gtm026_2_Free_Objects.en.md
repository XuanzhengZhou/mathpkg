This is the content of Theorem 1.4.12. The pair $(KT, K\mu; K\eta)$ satisfies the universal property required by Definition 2.1: for any $\mathcal{K}^T$-algebra $(A, \xi)$ and any $\mathcal{K}$-morphism $f: K \longrightarrow A = AU^T$, there exists a unique $\mathcal{K}^T$-homomorphism $\psi: (KT, K\mu) \longrightarrow (A, \xi)$ extending $f$, namely $\psi = fT \cdot \xi$. The verification that this construction satisfies the free object universal property follows directly from the definition of the Kleisli category $\mathcal{K}^T$ and the axioms of an algebraic theory.
