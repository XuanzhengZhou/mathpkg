If two objects $(F, \eta)$ and $(F', \eta')$ both satisfy the universal property of a free $\mathcal{A}$-object over $K$ with respect to the same functor $U$, then they are isomorphic via a unique isomorphism $g: F \to F'$ that respects the universal morphisms. This is the familiar categorical fact that objects defined by a universal property are unique up to unique isomorphism.
