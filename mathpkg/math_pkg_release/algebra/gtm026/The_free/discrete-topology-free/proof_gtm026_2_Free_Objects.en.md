Let $A$ be any topological space and let $f: n \longrightarrow AU$ be any function. Since $F_n$ has the discrete topology, every function from $F_n$ to $A$ is continuous. Define $\psi = f$ as a map $F_n \longrightarrow A$; this is the unique set map such that $\eta \cdot (\psi U) = \mathrm{id}_n \cdot f = f$. Thus $(F_n, \eta)$ satisfies the universal property of Definition 2.1.
