For each set $A$, define the insertion-of-the-variables map $A\eta: A \to AT$ by $\langle a, A\eta \rangle = [a]$, where $[a]$ is the $E_A$-equivalence class of the variable $a \in A \subseteq A\Omega$. Then for every $(\Omega, E)$-algebra $(X, \delta)$ and every function $f: A \to X$, there exists a unique $\Omega$-homomorphism $f^{\#}: AT \to (X, \delta)$ extending $f$ in the sense that the diagram

\[
\begin{CD}
A @>{A\eta}>> AT \\
@V{f}VV @VV{f^{\#}}V \\
X @= X
\end{CD}
\]

commutes, i.e., $f = A\eta \cdot f^{\#}$.
