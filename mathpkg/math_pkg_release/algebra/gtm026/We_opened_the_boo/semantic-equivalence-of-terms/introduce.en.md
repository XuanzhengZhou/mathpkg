For a fixed equational presentation $(\Omega, E)$, two $\Omega$-terms $p, q \in A\Omega$ are declared equivalent under $E_A$ precisely when they evaluate to the same element in every $(\Omega, E)$-algebra under every assignment $f: A \to X$ of the variables. This semantic equivalence is the natural notion of "having the same meaning" forced by the equations $E$. The quotient $AT = A\Omega/E_A$ is then the free $(\Omega, E)$-algebra on $A$, presented by generators (the variables) and relations (the equations $E$), with just enough identifications to satisfy $E$ and no more.
