Definition 3.26 introduces extremal-state $T$-algebras: those algebras generated by their isolated elements, which are then called the states. By Proposition 3.25, the set of isolated elements forms the unique minimal generating set. This concept provides a unified framework spanning diverse structures: free monoids are extremal state (states are words of length one), convex polytopes are extremal state for the stochastic matrix theory (states are vertices), while nonzero vector spaces are not extremal state. Notably, neither quotients nor subalgebras of extremal-state algebras need inherit the property, which has important consequences for the minimal realization theory of fuzzy automata developed in this section.
