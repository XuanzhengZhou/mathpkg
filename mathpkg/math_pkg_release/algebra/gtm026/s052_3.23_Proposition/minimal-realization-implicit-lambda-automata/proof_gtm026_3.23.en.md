Using the notations of 3.18, $f$ extends to a $\lambda$-homomorphism

$$\bar{f} : (IX^{@}T, I\mu_0, Im) \longrightarrow (YX^{@}, YL, \theta_{\#})$$

as shown below (where it is easily checked that $(f^{\#})_{\#} = \bar{f} = (f_{\#})^{\#}$). Using the same reasoning as in 2.18 (2), as $X^{@}T$ preserves $E$, the $E$-$M$ image

$$IX^{@}T \xrightarrow{r^{\#}} \bar{Q} \xrightarrow{\sigma} YX^{@}$$

of $\bar{f}$ lifts uniquely to $\lambda$-algebra homomorphisms. The remaining details follow the proof of 2.18.
