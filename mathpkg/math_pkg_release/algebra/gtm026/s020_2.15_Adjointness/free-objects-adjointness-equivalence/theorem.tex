Let $U: \mathcal{A} \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ be a functor. Then the following two conditions on $U$ are equivalent:

\begin{enumerate}
\item For every object $K$ in $\mathcal{K}$ there exists a free object over $K$ with respect to $U$.
\item There exists an adjointness of form $(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{K}, U, F, \eta, \varepsilon)$.
\end{enumerate}
