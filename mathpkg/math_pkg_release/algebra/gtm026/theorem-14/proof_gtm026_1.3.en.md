The passage from $\xi$ to $X\lambda.\xi$ is injective because $X\lambda$ is an epimorphism in $\mathcal{K}$, so that $V$ is injective on objects. The diagram below shows that $f : (X, \xi') \longrightarrow (Y, \theta')$ is a T'-homomorphism if and only if $f : (X, \xi')V \longrightarrow (X, \theta')V$ is a T-homomorphism $(X\lambda$ is epi!). Therefore, $V$ is a full subcategory. Let $(X, \xi) \in \mathcal{A}$. There exists a unique T-homomorphism.
