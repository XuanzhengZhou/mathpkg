A subset $A$ of $X$ is said to be "close to" a filter $\mathcal{F}$ if $A$ contains a finite intersection of members of $\mathcal{F}$. This notion bridges the gap between filters and the topological concept of closeness: in a topological space, a neighborhood of a point is close to the neighborhood filter. The concept is used to define ultrafilter convergence.
