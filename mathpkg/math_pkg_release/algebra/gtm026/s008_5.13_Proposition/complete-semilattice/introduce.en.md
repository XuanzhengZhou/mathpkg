A complete semilattice is the strongest form of semilattice: every subset, regardless of cardinality, has a supremum. Consequently, every subset also has an infimum (given by the supremum of lower bounds). The homomorphisms are required to preserve all suprema. The distinction between complete semilattices and complete lattices lies in the notion of homomorphism: a complete semilattice homomorphism preserves suprema but not necessarily infima.
