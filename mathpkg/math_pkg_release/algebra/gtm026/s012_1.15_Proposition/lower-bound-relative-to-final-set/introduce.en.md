When a diagram scheme has a final subset of nodes, one can define lower bounds using only the nodes in that final set. The morphisms to nodes outside the final set are then uniquely determined by composition with the diagram's edges. This "restriction to $F$" provides a bijection between ordinary lower bounds and lower bounds relative to $F$, making it a convenient practical tool for constructing limits.
