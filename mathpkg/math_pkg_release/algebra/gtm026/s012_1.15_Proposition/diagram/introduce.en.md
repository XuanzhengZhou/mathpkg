A diagram in a category generalizes the notion of an indexed family of objects and morphisms. A diagram scheme $\Delta$ specifies the "shape" of the diagram via nodes and edges, while a diagram $(\Delta, D)$ assigns actual objects and morphisms in a category $\mathcal{K}$ to that shape. Diagrams are the fundamental data for defining limits and colimits, abstracting constructions such as products, equalizers, and pullbacks.
