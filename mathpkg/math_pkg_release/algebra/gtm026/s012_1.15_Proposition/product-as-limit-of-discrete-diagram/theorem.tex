Let $I$ be an arbitrary set, and define a diagram scheme $\Delta$ by $N(\Delta) = I$ and $\Delta(i, j) = \emptyset$ for all $i, j \in I$. For any category $\mathcal{K}$, a diagram of the form $(\Delta, D)$ is the same thing as an $I$-indexed family of objects of $\mathcal{K}$. For such diagrams, the notion of a limit coincides with that of a product.
