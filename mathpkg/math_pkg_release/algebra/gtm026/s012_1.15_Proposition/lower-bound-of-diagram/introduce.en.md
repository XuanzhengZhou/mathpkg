A lower bound of a diagram, also called a cone, consists of an object $L$ together with a family of morphisms from $L$ to each object in the diagram, all commuting with the diagram's edges. Lower bounds provide the raw data from which limits are defined via a universal property. The concept abstracts the idea of a "compatible family of maps" into a diagram.
