By duality, it suffices to prove the second statement. Let $(e,m)$ be an $\mathbb{E}$-$\mathbb{M}$ factorization of $g$ and let $(e_1,m_1)$ be an $\mathbb{E}$-$\mathbb{M}$ factorization of $f \circ e$. By 4.10 there exists $h$ such that $(h \circ m_1) \circ m = \mathrm{id}_C$ as shown below.

Therefore $m$ is mono and split epi, so is an isomorphism, and $g = e \circ m$ is in $\mathbb{E}$.
