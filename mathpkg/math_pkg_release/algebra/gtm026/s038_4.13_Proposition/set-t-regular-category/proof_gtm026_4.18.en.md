By Proposition 4.17, it suffices to verify that every algebraic theory $T$ in $\mathbf{Set}$ preserves surjections, i.e., $eT$ is surjective whenever $e$ is surjective. This holds by 1.4.29, which states that the functor part of an algebraic theory in $\mathbf{Set}$ preserves surjections.

The additional claim that $E$ is the class of coequalizers in $\mathbf{Set}^T$ and $M$ is the class of monomorphisms in $\mathbf{Set}^T$ follows from 1.13 and 2.1.46: in $\mathbf{Set}^T$, every surjective $T$-homomorphism is a coequalizer and every injective $T$-homomorphism is a monomorphism (and conversely).
