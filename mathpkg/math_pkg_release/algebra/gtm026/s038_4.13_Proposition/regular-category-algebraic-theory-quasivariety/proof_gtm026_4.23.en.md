The proof is incomplete in the source text. The argument proceeds by combining Proposition 4.17 (which establishes that $(\mathcal{K}^T, \mathbb{E}^T, \mathbb{M}^T)$ is a regular category) with the Quasivariety Theorem 4.21, applied to the regular category $(\mathcal{K}^T, \mathbb{E}^T, \mathbb{M}^T)$. The characterization of quasivarieties in $\mathcal{K}^T$ as full replete subcategories closed under products and $\mathbb{M}^T$-subobjects then follows directly from Theorem 4.21.
