Let $(\mathcal{K}, \mathbb{E}, \mathbb{M})$ be a regular category and let $T$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ such that $eT \in \mathbb{E}$ whenever $e \in \mathbb{E}$.

A full replete subcategory $\mathcal{B}$ of $\mathcal{K}^T$ is a quasivariety in $(\mathcal{K}^T, \mathbb{E}^T, \mathbb{M}^T)$ if and only if $\mathcal{B}$ is closed under products and closed under $\mathbb{M}^T$-subobjects, where

$$\mathbb{E}^T = \{e \in \mathcal{K}^T : eU^T \in \mathbb{E}\}, \quad \mathbb{M}^T = \{m \in \mathcal{K}^T : mU^T \in \mathbb{M}\}.$$
