Let $(\mathcal{K}, \mathbb{E}, \mathbb{M})$ be a regular category and let $\mathcal{B}$ be a full replete subcategory of $\mathcal{K}$.

Then $\mathcal{B}$ is a quasivariety in $(\mathcal{K}, \mathbb{E}, \mathbb{M})$ if and only if $\mathcal{B}$ is closed under products and closed under $\mathbb{M}$-subobjects (i.e., if $m: A \longrightarrow B$ is in $\mathbb{M}$ and $B \in \mathcal{B}$, then $A \in \mathcal{B}$).
