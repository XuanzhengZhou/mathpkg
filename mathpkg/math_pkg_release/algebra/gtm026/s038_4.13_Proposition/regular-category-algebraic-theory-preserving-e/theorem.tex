Let $(\mathcal{K}, E, M)$ be a regular category and let $T = (T, \eta, \mu)$ be an algebraic theory in $\mathcal{K}$ such that $eT \in E$ whenever $e \in E$. Define

$$E^T = \{e \in \mathcal{K}^T : eU^T \in E\}$$
$$M^T = \{m \in \mathcal{K}^T : mU^T \in M\}$$

where $U^T: \mathcal{K}^T \longrightarrow \mathcal{K}$ is the forgetful functor from the Eilenberg-Moore category of $T$-algebras.

Then $(\mathcal{K}^T, E^T, M^T)$ is a regular category.
