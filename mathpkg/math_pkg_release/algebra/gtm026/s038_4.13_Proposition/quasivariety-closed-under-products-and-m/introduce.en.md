Corollary 4.22 specializes the Quasivariety Theorem (4.21) to the common case where every equalizer belongs to $\mathbb{M}$. Under this additional hypothesis, the quasivariety condition simplifies to the classical characterization: a full subcategory is a quasivariety exactly when it is closed under products and $\mathbb{M}$-subobjects. This recovers the most prevalent definition of quasivariety in the literature as a class of algebras closed under products and subalgebras. The proof follows immediately from 2.1.22.
