Definition 4.20 introduces the concept of a quasivariety in a regular category $(\mathcal{K}, E, M)$. A quasivariety is a full replete subcategory $\mathcal{B}$ of $\mathcal{K}$ that is $E$-reflective: every object $A$ of $\mathcal{K}$ has a reflection $e: A \longrightarrow B$ into $\mathcal{B}$ whose reflection morphism $e$ belongs to the $E$ class. This generalizes the classical notion of quasivariety in universal algebra (a class of algebras closed under products and subalgebras) to the abstract setting of regular categories.
