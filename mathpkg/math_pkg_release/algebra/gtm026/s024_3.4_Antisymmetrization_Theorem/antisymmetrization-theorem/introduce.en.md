The Antisymmetrization Theorem states that the antisymmetric concrete categories form a full, reflective, and replete subcategory of $\operatorname{Con}(\mathcal{K})$, the category of all concrete categories over a fixed base category $\mathcal{K}$. This means that every concrete category $(\mathcal{A}, U)$ admits an antisymmetric reflection: a universal arrow into the antisymmetric subcategory given by a full, faithful, and surjective-on-objects functor. The theorem is proved by constructing the quotient category $[\mathcal{A}]$ under the equivalence relation that identifies objects with mutually admissible identity morphisms, and then showing this construction provides the required reflection.
