For any fixed point $x$ in a metric space $(X, d)$, the distance function $d(x, -): X \to \mathbb{R}$ is a distance-decreasing (1-Lipschitz) map into the real numbers with the usual metric. This follows directly from the reverse triangle inequality: the absolute difference between the distances from $x$ to $y$ and from $x$ to $z$ is bounded above by $d(y, z)$. This simple observation is the key to proving that $\mathbb{R}$ is both a cogenerator and an injective object in the category of metric spaces with distance-decreasing maps.
