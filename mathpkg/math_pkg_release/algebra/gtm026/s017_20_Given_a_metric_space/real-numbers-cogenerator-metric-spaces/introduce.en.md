The real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual metric serve as a cogenerator in the category of metric spaces with distance-decreasing maps. This means that points of any metric space can be separated by real-valued distance-decreasing functions. To see why, take any two distinct points $y \neq z$ in a metric space $X$; then the distance function $d(y, -): X \to \mathbb{R}$ sends $y$ to $0$ and $z$ to $d(y, z) > 0$, thereby distinguishing them. The cogenerator property follows immediately from the fact that each $d(x, -)$ is distance decreasing (proved in the preceding proposition).
