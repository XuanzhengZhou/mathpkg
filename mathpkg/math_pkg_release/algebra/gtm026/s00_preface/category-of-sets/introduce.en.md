$\mathbf{Set}$ is the prototypical category and serves as the base category for all of Chapter 1 of Manes' book. Its objects are arbitrary sets and its morphisms are all functions between sets, with the usual composition and identity functions. The entire development of algebraic theories of sets — including the construction of $T$-algebras and the equivalence with equational classes — takes place within $\mathbf{Set}$. In later chapters, $\mathbf{Set}$ is replaced by more general base categories, and the theory is extended to algebraic categories over an arbitrary base.
