An algebraic theory of sets is Manes' central abstraction that captures the notion of an equational class without explicit reference to operations and equations. It consists of a construction $T = (T, \eta, \circ)$ where $T$ maps each set $A$ to a set $AT$ of "derived operations" or "terms" on $A$, $\eta$ provides the inclusion of variables, and $\circ$ is a substitution operation. The motivating example is the theory of semigroups, where $AT$ is the set of all parenthesis-free strings $a_1 \cdots a_n$ over $A$, and the axioms of associativity and unitality for $\circ$ encode the equational logic. This formulation unifies finitary and infinitary equational classes under a single abstract framework.
