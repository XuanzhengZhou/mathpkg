A $T$-homomorphism is the morphism of $T$-algebras: a function between the underlying sets that respects the $T$-algebra structure maps. In the semigroup example, this condition reduces exactly to the usual notion of a semigroup homomorphism: $f(x_1 \cdots x_n) = f(x_1) \cdots f(x_n)$. The category of $T$-algebras and $T$-homomorphisms is isomorphic to the category of algebras for the original equational class, establishing the equivalence between the algebraic-theory approach and universal algebra.
