A $T$-algebra is the algebraic-theory counterpart of an algebra for an equational class. Given an algebraic theory $T = (T, \eta, \circ)$, a $T$-algebra $(X, \xi)$ consists of a carrier set $X$ together with a structure map $\xi: XT \to X$ that interprets the formal $T$-operations on $X$ as actual functions. The two axioms (associativity and unit) ensure compatibility with the substitution structure of $T$. For the semigroup theory, the $T$-algebra structures on a set $X$ correspond bijectively to semigroup structures on $X$ via $(x_1 \cdots x_n)\xi = x_1 \cdots x_n$.
