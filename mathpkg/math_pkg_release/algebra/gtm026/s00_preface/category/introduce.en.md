A category is the fundamental structure of category theory, consisting of objects and morphisms between them, with an associative composition law and identity morphisms. Manes gives a self-contained definition in the Preliminaries, requiring no prior knowledge of category theory. The prime example is $\mathbf{Set}$, the category whose objects are sets and whose morphisms are functions. The category concept unifies diverse mathematical structures — groups, topological spaces, algebraic theories — under a common framework, and forms the foundation for the entire book's development of algebraic theories in a category.
