\text{Let} $\mathbf{T}$ \text{be an algebraic theory in} $\mathcal{L}$, \text{let} $\mathbf{S}$ \text{be an algebraic theory in} $\mathcal{K}$, \text{and let} $H: \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathcal{L}$ \text{be a functor.}

\text{A} \emph{theory map relative to} $H$ \text{is a natural transformation}
$$
\lambda: H \circ T \longrightarrow S \circ H,
$$
\text{where} $T: \mathcal{K} \to \mathcal{K}$ \text{and} $S: \mathcal{L} \to \mathcal{L}$ \text{are the underlying endofunctors of the respective theories, satisfying the coherence conditions that make} $\lambda$ \text{compatible with the monoid (theory) structures} $\eta$ \text{and} $\circ$.

\text{When} $H = \mathrm{id}$, \text{a theory map relative to the identity reduces to an ordinary theory map} $\lambda: T \to S$.
