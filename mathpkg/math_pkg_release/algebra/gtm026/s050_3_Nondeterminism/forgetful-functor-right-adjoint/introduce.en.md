This theorem constructs the right adjoint to the forgetful functor from the category $\mathcal{K}^{\lambda}$ of $\lambda$-algebras to the category $\mathcal{K}^{\mathbf{T}}$ of $\mathbf{T}$-algebras, where $\lambda: TX \to XT$ is a distributive law of the output process $X$ over the algebraic theory $\mathbf{T}$. The right adjoint sends a $\mathbf{T}$-algebra $(Y, \theta)$ to the cofree $\lambda$-algebra $(YX_{\#}, YL, \theta_{\#})$, where $YX_{\#}$ is the free dynamics on $Y$, $YL$ is its structure map, and $\theta_{\#}$ is the unique dynamorphic coextension of the $\mathbf{T}$-homomorphic extension of the coinclusion $Y\Lambda$. This result generalizes the classical construction of cofree coalgebras over an algebra to the setting of distributive laws between algebraic theories and output processes.
