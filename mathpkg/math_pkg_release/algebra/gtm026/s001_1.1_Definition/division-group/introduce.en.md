A group can be axiomatized using a single binary operation---division---and a single equational identity. This remarkable definition, due to Higman and Neumann (1952), shows that the entire group structure can be captured by one operation satisfying one equation in Polish notation. A bijective correspondence exists between this definition and the standard one: given division $d$, one recovers inversion $i$ and unit $e$, and conversely. Notably, the empty set satisfies this definition, whereas it does not satisfy the standard one; to remedy this one may additionally require a distinguished element $e$ with $x\,e\,d = x$.
