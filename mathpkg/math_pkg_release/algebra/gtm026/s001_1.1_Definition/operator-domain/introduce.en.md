An operator domain formalizes the notion of a "signature" in universal algebra. It is a graded family of sets $(\Omega_0, \Omega_1, \Omega_2, \ldots)$ where each $\Omega_n$ contains the labels for operations of arity $n$. For example, the operator domain for groups has $\Omega_0 = \{e\}$ (the unit, a nullary operation), $\Omega_1 = \{i\}$ (inversion), and $\Omega_2 = \{m\}$ (multiplication). An operator domain may also be viewed as a directed graph whose nodes are natural numbers and whose edges all terminate at $1$, a perspective that naturally leads to viewing operator domains as categories.
