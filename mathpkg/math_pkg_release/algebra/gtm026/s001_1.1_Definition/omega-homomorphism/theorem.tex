Given $\Omega$-algebras $(X, \delta)$ and $(Y, \gamma)$, an $\Omega$-homomorphism from $(X, \delta)$ to $(Y, \gamma)$ is a function $f: X \longrightarrow Y$ such that for all $n \geq 0$, all $\omega \in \Omega_n$, and all $x_1, \ldots, x_n \in X$,
$$
f(\delta_\omega(x_1, \ldots, x_n)) = \gamma_\omega(f(x_1), \ldots, f(x_n)).
$$
For a nullary operation $\omega \in \Omega_0$, this condition reduces to $f(\delta_\omega) = \gamma_\omega$.
