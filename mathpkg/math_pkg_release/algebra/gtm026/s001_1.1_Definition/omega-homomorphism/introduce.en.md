An $\Omega$-homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between $\Omega$-algebras. It is a function between the underlying sets that commutes with all the algebraic operations: applying an operation in the source algebra and then mapping is the same as mapping first and then applying the corresponding operation in the target algebra. In the familiar case of groups, $\Omega$-homomorphisms coincide with ordinary group homomorphisms. The definition unifies the notion of homomorphism across all varieties of universal algebra.
