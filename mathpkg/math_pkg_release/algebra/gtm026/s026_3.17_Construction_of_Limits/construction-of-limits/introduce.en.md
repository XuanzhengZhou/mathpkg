The notion of a functor that *constructs limits* captures when limits in a concrete category $\mathcal{C}$ over $\mathcal{K}$ can be built by first forming the limit in the base category $\mathcal{K}$ and then equipping the limiting object with an optimal (i.e., initial) structure. Concretely, for every diagram in $\mathcal{C}$, one takes its image under the forgetful functor $U$, takes a limit cone $(K, \psi)$ in $\mathcal{K}$, and then requires that the legs $\psi_i$ admit an optimal lift to a $\mathcal{C}$-structure $s$ on $K$. The resulting cone in $\mathcal{C}$ is then automatically a limit. The dual notion, *constructing colimits*, is defined by passing to the opposite categories and requiring co-optimal lifts.
