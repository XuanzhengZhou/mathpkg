Fibre-completeness of a concrete category $\mathcal{C} \in \mathbf{Struct}(\mathcal{K})$ ensures that the forgetful functor $U: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{K}$ admits both a left and a right adjoint. The left adjoint provides the free object over each $\mathcal{K}$-object $K$, realized as the least element (the co-optimal lift of the empty family out of $K$) in the fibre lattice $\mathcal{C}(K)$. Dually, the right adjoint yields the cofree object over $K$, given by the greatest element of $\mathcal{C}(K)$.
