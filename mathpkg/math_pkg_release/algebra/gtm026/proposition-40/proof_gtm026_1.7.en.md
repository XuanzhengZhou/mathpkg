Let $(\Sigma, E)$ be a diagram in $\mathcal{H}$ and let $(L, \psi)$ be a limit of $E$. Let us write $E$-nodes as $j$ and use superscripts for $\Sigma$-diagrams to avoid notational confusion with $\Delta$-diagrams. We must show that $(\tilde{L}, (\psi^j)^\sim: \tilde{L} \longrightarrow (E^j))$ is a limit of the diagram $\tilde{E}$ in $\mathcal{H}^\Delta$. Let $(D, \Gamma^j:D \longrightarrow (E^j)^\sim)$ be a lower bound of $\tilde{E}$ in $\mathcal{H}^\Delta$. Then for each $i \in N(\Delta)$, $(D_i, \Gamma_i)$ is a lower bound of $E$, inducing unique $\Lambda_i: D_i \longrightarrow L$ such that $\Lambda_i.\psi^j = \Gamma_i^j$.
