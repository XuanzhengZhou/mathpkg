The determinant of a linear transformation measures how the transformation scales volumes. It is defined using any non-zero determinant function $\Delta$: the scalar $\det\varphi$ satisfies $\Delta(\varphi x_1,\ldots,\varphi x_n) = \det\varphi \cdot \Delta(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$. The determinant is multiplicative, and $\varphi$ is invertible iff $\det\varphi \neq 0$. For scalar multiplication, $\det(\lambda\iota) = \lambda^n$.
