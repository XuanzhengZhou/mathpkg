Taking duals of linear mappings with respect to $L(E)$ and $L(F)$ preserves exactness: the dual of an exact sequence is exact, and the dual of a short exact sequence is a short exact sequence. This is a special property of vector spaces (the functor $L(-)$ is exact). It holds because every linear function on a subspace extends to the whole space.
