Consider the set $S_n$ of all permutations of the set $\{1, \ldots, n\}$ and define the product of two permutations $\sigma, \tau$ by
$$(\sigma \tau)i = \sigma(\tau i) \quad i = 1, \ldots, n.$$
In this way $S_n$ becomes a group, called the \textbf{group of permutations of $n$ objects} (or the \textbf{symmetric group on $n$ letters}). The identity element of $S_n$ is the identity permutation.
