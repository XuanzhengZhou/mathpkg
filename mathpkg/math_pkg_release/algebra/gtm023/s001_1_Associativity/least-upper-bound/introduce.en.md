A least upper bound (or supremum) of a subset is the smallest element among all upper bounds of that subset. When it exists, it is unique by antisymmetry. The least upper bound is a central concept in lattice theory and real analysis, where the completeness property of the real numbers asserts that every nonempty bounded-above set has a least upper bound.
