A field is an algebraic structure with two compatible binary operations (addition and multiplication) where both the additive group and the multiplicative group of nonzero elements are commutative. Fields generalize the rational, real, and complex numbers and serve as the coefficient domains for vector spaces in linear algebra. The distributive law connects the two operations, enabling the rich algebraic manipulation that characterizes field arithmetic.
