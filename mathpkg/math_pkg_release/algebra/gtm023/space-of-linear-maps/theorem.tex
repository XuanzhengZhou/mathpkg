Let $E$ and $F$ be vector spaces and consider the set $L(E; F)$ of linear mappings $\varphi: E \to F$. Under the operations
$$(\varphi + \psi)x = \varphi x + \psi x, \quad (\lambda \varphi)x = \lambda \varphi x,$$
$L(E; F)$ becomes a linear space, called the space of linear mappings of $E$ into $F$. The zero vector is the linear mapping $0$ defined by $0x = 0$.

In the case $F = \Gamma$, $L(E; \Gamma)$ is denoted simply by $L(E)$ and is called the space of linear functions in $E$.
