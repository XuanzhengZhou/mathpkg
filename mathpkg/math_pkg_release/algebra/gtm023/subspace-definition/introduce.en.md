A subspace of a vector space is a non-empty subset closed under addition and scalar multiplication. Subspaces inherit the vector space structure from the ambient space. The canonical injection $i: E_1 \to E$ defined by $ix = x$ is an injective linear mapping. Important subspaces include kernels, images, and linear closures of arbitrary subsets.
