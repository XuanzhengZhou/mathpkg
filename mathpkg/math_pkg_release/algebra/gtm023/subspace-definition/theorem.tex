Let $E$ be a vector space over the field $\Gamma$. A non-empty subset $E_1$ of $E$ is called a subspace if for each $x, y \in E_1$ and every scalar $\lambda \in \Gamma$
$$x + y \in E_1$$
and
$$\lambda x \in E_1.$$

Equivalently, a subspace is a subset of $E$ such that $\lambda x + \mu y \in E_1$ whenever $x, y \in E_1$. The whole space $E$ and the subset $(0)$ are subspaces. Every subspace $E_1 \subset E$ contains the zero vector.
