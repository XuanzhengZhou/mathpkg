\textbf{Proposition III.} Let $\varrho_i$ ($i = 1, \ldots, r$) be projection operators in a vector space $E$ such that
$$\varrho_i \circ \varrho_j = 0, \quad i \neq j$$
and
$$\sum_{i=1}^{r} \varrho_i = \iota_E.$$
Then
$$E = \bigoplus_{i=1}^{r} \operatorname{Im} \varrho_i.$$

Conversely, if $E = \bigoplus_v E_v$ is a decomposition as a direct sum of subspaces with canonical projections $\pi_v: E \to E_v$ and injections $i_v: E_v \to E$, then the linear mappings
$$\varrho_v = i_v \circ \pi_v: E \to E$$
are projection operators satisfying $\varrho_v \circ \varrho_\mu = 0$ for $v \neq \mu$, and $\operatorname{Im} \varrho_v = E_v$.
