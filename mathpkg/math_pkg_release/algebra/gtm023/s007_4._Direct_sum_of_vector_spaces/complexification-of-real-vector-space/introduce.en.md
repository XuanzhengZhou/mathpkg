The complexification $E_{\mathbb{C}}$ of a real vector space $E$ is obtained by endowing the direct sum $E \oplus E$ with a complex scalar multiplication. This construction extends any real vector space to a complex vector space of the same real dimension, providing a bridge between real and complex linear algebra. Every vector in $E_{\mathbb{C}}$ can be uniquely expressed as $x + iy$ with $x, y \in E$.
