Let $S$ be an arbitrary set. There exists a vector space $C(S)$ over $\Gamma$ and an injective set map $\eta: S \to C(S)$ with the following universal property: For every vector space $F$ and every set map $f: S \to F$, there exists a unique linear mapping $\varphi: C(S) \to F$ such that the diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
S & \xrightarrow{\eta} & C(S) \\
& \searrow^f & \downarrow^\varphi \\
& & F
\end{array}$$
commutes. $C(S)$ is called the \textbf{free vector space} over $S$, and $\eta(S)$ is a basis of $C(S)$.
