Let $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$ be a direct decomposition with $\dim E_1 = p$, $\dim E_2 = q$, and suppose orientations are given on $E_1$, $E_2$, and $E$. Let $a_1, \ldots, a_p$ and $b_1, \ldots, b_q$ be positive bases of $E_1$ and $E_2$. Define

$$\alpha_{12} = \Delta(a_1, \ldots, a_p, b_1, \ldots, b_q)$$

where $\Delta$ represents the orientation of $E$. Then $\alpha_{12} \neq 0$, and the number

$$\frac{\alpha_{12}}{|\alpha_{12}|} = \pm 1$$

depends only on the orientations of $E_1$, $E_2$, and $E$. It is called the intersection number of the oriented subspaces $E_1$ and $E_2$.

Moreover,
$$\alpha_{21} = (-1)^{p q} \alpha_{12}.$$
