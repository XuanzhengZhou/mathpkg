A positive basis is one that is consistent with a chosen orientation of the vector space: a representing determinant function yields a positive value on it. The sign behavior under permutations matches the parity of the permutation, reflecting the alternating property of determinants. This yields the familiar right-hand rule in $\mathbb{R}^3$: an even permutation of a right-handed basis remains right-handed.
