The characteristic polynomial of a matrix $A$ is defined as $f(\lambda) = \det(A - \lambda J)$, where $J$ is the identity matrix. This definition is compatible with the coordinate-free definition via determinants of linear transformations: once a basis is chosen, the characteristic polynomial of the transformation equals that of its matrix. The eigenvalues of the matrix are exactly the roots of this polynomial.
