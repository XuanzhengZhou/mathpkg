In an oriented vector space, the basis deformation theorem takes a particularly clean form: two bases are deformable into each other precisely when they share the same orientation (both positive or both negative). This means the set of all bases splits into exactly two deformation classes, corresponding to the two orientations of the space. This provides a topological interpretation of orientation: an orientation is a connected component of the space of all bases.
