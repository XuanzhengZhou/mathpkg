Let $a_v$ and $b_v\;(v = 1, \ldots, n)$ be two bases of a real vector space $E$. Let $\varphi: E \to E$ be the linear transformation defined by $\varphi a_v = b_v$. Then the basis $a_v$ is deformable into the basis $b_v$ (i.e., there exist $n$ continuous mappings $x_v: [t_0, t_1] \to E$ with $x_v(t_0) = a_v$, $x_v(t_1) = b_v$, and $x_v(t)$ linearly independent for all $t$) if and only if

$$\det \varphi > 0.$$
