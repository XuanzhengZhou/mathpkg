When two oriented subspaces form a direct sum decomposition of a vector space, their orientations naturally combine to give an orientation on the whole space. The positive basis of the whole space is obtained by concatenating positive bases of the summands. This construction is well-defined: choosing different positive bases in the summands yields the same induced orientation on $E$.
