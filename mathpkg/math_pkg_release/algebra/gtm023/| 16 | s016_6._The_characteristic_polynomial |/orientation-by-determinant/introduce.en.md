An orientation on a real vector space is an equivalence class of non-zero determinant functions, where two determinant functions are equivalent if one is a positive scalar multiple of the other. Since any determinant function is a scalar multiple of any other, this partitions all choices into exactly two orientations. A basis is then called positive if a representing determinant function evaluates positively on it.
