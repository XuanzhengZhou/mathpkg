An eigenvector of a linear transformation is a non-zero vector that is mapped to a scalar multiple of itself by the transformation. The scalar factor is called the eigenvalue. These concepts are fundamental to spectral theory, as they reveal the invariant directions of a linear operator. Not every linear transformation admits eigenvectors — for example, a rotation by $90^\circ$ in $\mathbb{R}^2$ has no real eigenvectors.
