The kernel and image are the two fundamental subspaces associated with any linear mapping. Injectivity is equivalent to trivial kernel; surjectivity is equivalent to the image being the whole codomain. These concepts lead to the isomorphism theorem $E / \ker \varphi \cong \operatorname{Im} \varphi$, which is the linear algebra version of the first isomorphism theorem for groups.
