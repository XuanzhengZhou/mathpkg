Let $\varphi$ be a linear transformation of a finite-dimensional vector space $E$. The evaluation homomorphism $\Phi: \Gamma[t] \to A(E; E)$, $f \mapsto f(\varphi)$, has non-trivial kernel. The unique monic polynomial $\mu$ of minimal degree in $\ker \Phi$ is the minimum polynomial of $\varphi$.

It satisfies $\mu(\varphi) = 0$ and $\dim \Gamma(\varphi) = \deg \mu$. The minimum polynomial of the zero transformation is $t$; of the identity is $t-1$.

For any subspace $F \subset E$ stable under $\varphi$ with induced transformation $\varphi_F$, the minimum polynomial $\mu_F$ of $\varphi_F$ divides $\mu$.
