A homomorphism between partially ordered sets is an order-preserving map: it satisfies $\varphi X \leq \varphi Y$ whenever $X \leq Y$. Such maps are also called monotone or isotone. In the context of vector spaces, the map sending a subspace to its annihilator in the dual space is an order-reversing bijection.
