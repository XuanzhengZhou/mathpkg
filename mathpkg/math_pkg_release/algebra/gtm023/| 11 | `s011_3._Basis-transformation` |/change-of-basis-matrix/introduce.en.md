A change-of-basis matrix encodes the relationship between two bases of a vector space. Given bases $x_v$ and $\bar{x}_v$, the entries $\alpha_v^\mu$ express each new basis vector as a linear combination of the old ones. The inverse transformation is given by the matrix $\check{\alpha}_v^\mu$. These matrices are fundamental tools for understanding how coordinates and linear mappings transform under a change of basis.
