Let $E^*$ be the dual space of $E$, let $x^{*v}$ be the dual basis of $x_v$, and let $\bar{x}^{*v}$ be the dual basis of $\bar{x}_v$ ($v = 1 \ldots n$). Write the dual basis transformation as

$$\bar{x}^{*\varrho} = \sum_{\sigma} \beta_{\sigma}^{\varrho} x^{*\sigma},$$

where $(\beta_{\sigma}^{\varrho})$ is a regular $n \times n$-matrix. Then

$$\beta_{v}^{\varrho} = \check{\alpha}_{v}^{\varrho}.$$

That is, the matrix of the basis transformation $x^{*v} \to \bar{x}^{*v}$ for the dual space is the inverse of the matrix of the basis transformation $x_v \to \bar{x}_v$ for the original space.
