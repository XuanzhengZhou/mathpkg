Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional vector space and $\Phi$ a symmetric bilinear function. The nullspace $E_0$ of $\Phi$ is
$$E_0 = \{x_0 \in E \mid \Phi(x_0, y) = 0 \text{ for every } y \in E\}.$$
The rank of $\Phi$ is $\dim E - \dim E_0$. $\Phi$ is non-degenerate iff its rank equals $n$.

If $\varphi: E \to E^*$ is defined by $\Phi(x, y) = \langle \varphi x, y \rangle$, then $E_0 = \ker \varphi$ and the rank of $\Phi$ equals the rank of $\varphi$, which equals the rank of the matrix $(\alpha_{v\mu})$ of $\Phi$ relative to any basis. $\Phi$ is non-degenerate iff $\det(\alpha_{v\mu}) \neq 0$.
