A norm-function generalizes the notion of length or magnitude from Euclidean space to arbitrary real linear spaces. It is defined by three axioms: positive definiteness ($N_1$), the triangle inequality ($N_2$), and absolute homogeneity ($N_3$). These axioms capture the essential properties that any reasonable notion of "size" for vectors should possess.
