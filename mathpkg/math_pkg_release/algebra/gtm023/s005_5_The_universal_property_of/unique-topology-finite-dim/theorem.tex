\textbf{Proposition:} Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional vector space over $\mathbb{R}$. Then there exists precisely one topology in $E$ satisfying the conditions:
\begin{itemize}
\item[T$_1$:] $E$ is a topological vector space.
\item[T$_2$:] Every linear function in $E$ is continuous.
\end{itemize}
Under this topology, any linear isomorphism $\varphi: \mathbb{R}^n \to E$ is a homeomorphism.
