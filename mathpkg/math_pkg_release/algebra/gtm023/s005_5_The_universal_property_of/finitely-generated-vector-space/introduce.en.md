A vector space is finitely generated if it is spanned by finitely many vectors. This property is preserved under surjective linear maps, and every subspace of a finitely generated space inherits finite generation (via the surjective map given by Corollary II, §1.15). Finitely generated vector spaces are precisely those that admit finite bases.
