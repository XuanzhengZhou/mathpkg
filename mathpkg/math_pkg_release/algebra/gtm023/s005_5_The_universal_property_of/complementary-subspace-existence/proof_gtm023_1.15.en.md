We may assume that $E_1 \neq E$ and $E_1 \neq \{0\}$ since the proposition is trivial in these cases. Let $(x_\alpha)$ be a basis of $E_1$ and extend it with vectors $y_\beta$ to form a basis of $E$ (cf. Corollary II to Theorem I, sec. 1.6). Let $E_2$ be the subspace of $E$ generated by the vectors $y_\beta$. Then

$$E = E_1 \oplus E_2.$$

In fact, since $(x_\alpha) \cup (y_\beta)$ is a system of generators for $E$, we have $E = E_1 + E_2$. To show the sum is direct, suppose $x \in E_1 \cap E_2$. Then $x$ can be written as

$$x = \sum_{\alpha} \lambda^{\alpha} x_{\alpha} = \sum_{\beta} \mu^{\beta} y_{\beta},$$

whence

$$\sum_{\alpha} \lambda^{\alpha} x_{\alpha} - \sum_{\beta} \mu^{\beta} y_{\beta} = 0.$$

Since the set $(x_{\alpha}) \cup (y_{\beta})$ is linearly independent, we obtain $\lambda^{\alpha} = 0$ and $\mu^{\beta} = 0$, whence $x = 0$. It follows that $E_1 \cap E_2 = \{0\}$ and so the decomposition is direct.
