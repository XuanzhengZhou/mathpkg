Let $E_2$ be the subspace of $E$ generated by the vectors $z_\beta$. Then

$$E = E_1 \oplus E_2.$$

Consider the linear mapping $\varphi: E_2 \to E/E_1$ defined by $\varphi z = \pi z$ for $z \in E_2$.

**Surjectivity:** Let $\bar{x} \in E/E_1$ be arbitrary. Since $\pi$ is surjective, $\bar{x} = \pi x$ for some $x \in E$. Decompose $x = y + z$ with $y \in E_1$, $z \in E_2$. Then $\bar{x} = \pi x = \pi y + \pi z = \pi z = \varphi z$, so $\varphi$ is surjective.

**Injectivity:** Suppose $\varphi z = \varphi z'$ for $z, z' \in E_2$. Then $\pi(z' - z) = 0$, so $z' - z \in E_1$. But $z' - z \in E_2$, hence $z' - z \in E_1 \cap E_2 = \{0\}$. Thus $\varphi$ is injective.

Therefore $\varphi: E_2 \to E/E_1$ is a linear isomorphism. By Proposition III of sec. 1.11, the vectors $\pi z_\beta = \varphi z_\beta$ form a basis of $E/E_1$.
