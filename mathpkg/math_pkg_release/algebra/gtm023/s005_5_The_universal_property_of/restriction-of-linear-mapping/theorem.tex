Let $\varphi: E \to F$ be a linear mapping and let $E_1 \subset E$, $F_1 \subset F$ be subspaces such that $\varphi(E_1) \subset F_1$. The linear mapping $\varphi_1: E_1 \to F_1$ defined by
$$\varphi_1 x = \varphi x, \quad x \in E_1$$
is called the \textbf{restriction} of $\varphi$ to $E_1, F_1$. It satisfies $\varphi \circ i_E = i_F \circ \varphi_1$, where $i_E: E_1 \to E$ and $i_F: F_1 \to F$ are the canonical injections. Equivalently, the diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc} E & \xrightarrow{\varphi} & F \\ i_E \uparrow & & \uparrow i_F \\ E_1 & \xrightarrow{\varphi_1} & F_1 \end{array}$$
is commutative.
