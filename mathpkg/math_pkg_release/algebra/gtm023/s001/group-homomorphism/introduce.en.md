A group homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between two groups that respects the group operation. Injective homomorphisms are called monomorphisms, surjective ones are epimorphisms, and bijective ones are isomorphisms. The inverse of an isomorphism is again an isomorphism, making isomorphic groups algebraically indistinguishable.
