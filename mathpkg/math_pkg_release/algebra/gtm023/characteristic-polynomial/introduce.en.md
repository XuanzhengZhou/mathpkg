The characteristic polynomial of a linear transformation $\varphi$ is $f(\lambda) = \det(\varphi - \lambda\iota)$. It is a monic polynomial of degree $n = \dim E$. Its coefficients express the traces of exterior powers (or sums of principal minors). The constant term is $\det\varphi$, while the coefficient of $(-\lambda)^{n-1}$ is $\operatorname{tr}\varphi$. By Cayley-Hamilton, $\varphi$ satisfies its own characteristic polynomial.
