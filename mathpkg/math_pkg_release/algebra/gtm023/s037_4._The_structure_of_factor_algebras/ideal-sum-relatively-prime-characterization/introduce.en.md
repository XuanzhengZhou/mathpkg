Given a factorization $f = f_1 \cdots f_m$ in the polynomial algebra $\Gamma[t]$, one can form the corresponding ideals $I_j$ in the factor algebra $\Gamma(\bar{t}) = \Gamma[t]/I_f$ generated by the residue classes $\bar{f}_j$. This proposition characterizes when the sum of these ideals equals the full algebra $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ (namely, precisely when the $f_j$ are relatively prime) and when the sum is direct (namely, when each $f_j$ and the least common multiple of the remaining factors have $f$ as their least common multiple). The proof uses the lattice isomorphism $\Phi: \mathcal{P}_f \to \mathcal{I}_f$ between polynomial divisors of $f$ and ideals of $\Gamma(\bar{t})$.
