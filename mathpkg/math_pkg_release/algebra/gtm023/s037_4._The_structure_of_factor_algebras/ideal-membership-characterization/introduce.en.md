This corollary of Theorem II gives a computationally convenient criterion for membership in the irreducible ideal component $I_i$. An element $\bar{q}$ in the factor algebra $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ lies in $I_i$ precisely when evaluating its polynomial representative $q$ at $\bar{t}_i$ recovers $\bar{q}$ and evaluating at all other components $\bar{t}_j$ ($j \neq i$) yields zero. This is a direct consequence of the decomposition formula $\bar{q} = \sum_i q(\bar{t}_i)$ from Theorem I.
