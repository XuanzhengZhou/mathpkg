A useful criterion: an element $a$ is semisimple precisely when $f'(a)$ is invertible in the subalgebra $\Gamma(a)$ generated by $a$, where $f$ is the minimum polynomial of $a$. The forward direction follows because semisimplicity means $f$ and $f'$ are relatively prime, so Bezout's identity gives $pf + qf' = 1$, whence $q(a)f'(a) = e$. The converse uses that if $f'(a)$ is invertible, then $f$ and $f'$ must be relatively prime, which forces $f$ to have distinct irreducible factors.
