This corollary provides a clean structural characterization: a factor algebra $\Gamma(\tilde{t}) = \Gamma[t]/I_f$ is irreducible (cannot be decomposed as a direct sum of proper ideals) if and only if the polynomial $f$ is a power $g^k$ of a single irreducible polynomial $g$. This follows directly from Theorem III, since the irreducible components correspond to the distinct prime factors of $f$.
