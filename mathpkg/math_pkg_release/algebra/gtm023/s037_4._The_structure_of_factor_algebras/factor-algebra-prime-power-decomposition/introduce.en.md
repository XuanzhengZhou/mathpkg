Given the prime-power factorization $f = f_1^{k_1} \cdots f_r^{k_r}$, the factor algebra $\Gamma(\bar{t}) = \Gamma[t]/I_f$ decomposes as a direct sum of ideals $I_i$ generated by $\bar{q}_i$, where $q_i$ omits the $i$-th prime-power factor. This theorem provides explicit decomposition formulas: the unit $\bar{1}$ splits into idempotents $\bar{e}_i$ and the indeterminate $\bar{t}$ splits into components $\bar{t}_i$, with the property that any element $\bar{q}$ can be recovered as $\sum_i q(\bar{t}_i)$. The result is fundamental for analyzing the structure of $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ based on the factorization of $f$.
