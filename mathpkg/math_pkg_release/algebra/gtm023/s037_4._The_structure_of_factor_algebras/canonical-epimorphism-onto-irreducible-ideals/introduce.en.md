For each prime-power factor $f_i^{k_i}$ of $f$, one can define a homomorphism $\varphi_i$ from the polynomial algebra $\Gamma[t]$ onto the ideal $I_i$ by specifying the images of $1$ and $t$ as the corresponding components $\bar{e}_i$ and $\bar{t}_i$ from the decomposition of Theorem I. The kernel is precisely the principal ideal generated by $f_i^{k_i}$, so that $\varphi_i$ descends to an isomorphism $\Gamma[t]/I_{f_i^{k_i}} \cong I_i$. This makes the structure of each irreducible ideal component explicit as a factor algebra.
