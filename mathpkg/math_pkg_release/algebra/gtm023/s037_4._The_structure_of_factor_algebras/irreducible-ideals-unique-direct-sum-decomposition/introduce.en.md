This theorem establishes that the ideal components $I_i$ arising from the prime-power factorization of $f$ are irreducible (cannot be further decomposed as direct sums of proper ideals) and that the decomposition $\Gamma(\bar{t}) = \sum_i I_i$ is unique. The proof reduces to showing that each $\Gamma[t]/I_{f_i^{k_i}}$ is irreducible, which follows from the fact that any direct sum decomposition of this algebra corresponds to a factorization of $f_i^{k_i}$ into relatively prime factors -- impossible since $f_i$ is prime.
