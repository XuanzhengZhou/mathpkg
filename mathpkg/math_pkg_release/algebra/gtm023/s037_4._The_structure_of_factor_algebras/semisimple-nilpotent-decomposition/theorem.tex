Let $A$ be a finite-dimensional associative commutative algebra. Let $x \in A$ and let

$$f = f_1^{k_1} \cdots f_r^{k_r}$$

be the decomposition of the minimum polynomial of $x$ into prime factors. Set

$$g = f_1 \cdots f_r$$

and

$$k = \max_i k_i \quad (i = 1, \ldots, r).$$

Then there are unique elements $x_S \in A$ and $x_N \in A$ such that $x_S$ is semisimple, $x_N$ is nilpotent, and

$$x = x_S + x_N.$$

The minimum polynomials of $x_S$ and $x_N$ are given by

$$\mu_S = g \quad \text{and} \quad \mu_N = t^k.$$
