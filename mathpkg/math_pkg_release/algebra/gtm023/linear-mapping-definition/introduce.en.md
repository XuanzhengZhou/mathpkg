A linear mapping between vector spaces is a function that preserves addition and scalar multiplication. Linear mappings are the structure-preserving morphisms of vector spaces — they are precisely the set mappings that preserve linear relations. Bijective linear mappings are called linear isomorphisms. Linear mappings form the central objects of study in linear algebra; they are also called linear transformations when domain and codomain coincide.
