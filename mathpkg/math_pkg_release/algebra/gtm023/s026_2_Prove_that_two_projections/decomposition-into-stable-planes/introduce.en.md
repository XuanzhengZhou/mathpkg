Every rotation on a finite-dimensional inner product space admits a canonical orthogonal decomposition into stable subspaces of dimension at most 2. The eigenspaces for eigenvalues $+1$ and $-1$ give the 1-dimensional stable subspaces (if present), while the orthogonal complement decomposes further into mutually orthogonal 2-dimensional stable planes. In each such plane, the induced transformation is a proper rotation with a well-defined rotation angle.
