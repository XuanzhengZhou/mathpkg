A fundamental property of skew (skew-symmetric) linear transformations on a finite-dimensional inner product space is that their rank is always an even number. This follows from the fact that a skew mapping is normal, so its image is the orthogonal complement of its kernel, and the induced transformation on the image is again skew and regular. Consequently, skew-symmetric matrices always have even rank.
