A skew mapping (or skew-symmetric transformation) on an inner product space $E$ is a linear transformation $\psi$ whose adjoint equals its negative: $\tilde{\psi} = -\psi$. Equivalently, $(\psi x, y) + (x, \psi y) = 0$ for all vectors $x, y \in E$. Relative to an orthonormal basis, the matrix of a skew mapping is skew-symmetric. Skew mappings are always normal and have purely imaginary spectrum; over the reals, their only possible eigenvalue is 0.
