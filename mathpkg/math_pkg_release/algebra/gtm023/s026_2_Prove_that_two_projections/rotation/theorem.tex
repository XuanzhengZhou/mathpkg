A rotation of an inner product space $E$ is an isometric mapping of $E$ into itself. Formula (8.36) implies that
$$\left( \det \varphi \right)^2 = 1$$
showing that the determinant of a rotation is $\pm 1$.

A rotation is called proper if $\det \varphi = +1$ and improper if $\det \varphi = -1$.

Every eigenvalue of a rotation is $\pm 1$.
