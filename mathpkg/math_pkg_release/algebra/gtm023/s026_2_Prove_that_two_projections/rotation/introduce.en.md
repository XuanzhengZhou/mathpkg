A rotation on an inner product space $E$ is an isometric linear mapping from $E$ to itself, i.e., a linear isomorphism $\varphi: E \to E$ satisfying $(\varphi x, \varphi y) = (x, y)$ for all $x, y \in E$. Rotations form the general orthogonal group; those with determinant $+1$ are called proper rotations and form the special orthogonal group. Every eigenvalue of a rotation is $\pm 1$.
