The rotation vector $u$ of a proper rotation in 3-space has a clean geometric interpretation: it lies along the rotation axis, its length is the sine of the rotation angle ($|u| = \sin \theta$), and together with the orientation it determines the sign of the rotation angle. The cosine of the rotation angle is given by $\cos \theta = \frac{1}{2}(\operatorname{tr} \varphi - 1)$.
