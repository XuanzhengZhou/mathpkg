Let $\varphi$ be any proper rotation of an oriented Euclidean plane $E$. Fix a non-zero vector $x$ and denote by $\Theta$ the oriented angle between $x$ and $\varphi x$. It is determined mod $2\pi$ by the equation
$$\varphi x = x \cdot \cos \Theta + j(x) \cdot \sin \Theta.$$

Then
$$\cos \theta = \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr} \varphi \quad \text{and} \quad \sin \theta = \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr} (j \circ \varphi).$$

The angle $\Theta$ is independent of $x$ and is called the rotation angle of $\varphi$, denoted $\Theta(\varphi)$.
