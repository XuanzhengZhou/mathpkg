For a proper rotation $\varphi$ in an oriented Euclidean plane, the rotation angle $\Theta(\varphi)$ parametrizes the action of $\varphi$ on any vector $x$ as $\varphi x = x \cos \Theta + j(x) \sin \Theta$. The cosine and sine of $\Theta$ are expressed in terms of the trace: $\cos \Theta = \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr} \varphi$ and $\sin \Theta = \frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr}(j \circ \varphi)$. The rotation angle is additive under composition and independent of the choice of $x$.
