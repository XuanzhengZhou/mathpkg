A 1-parameter group of linear automorphisms is a differentiable homomorphism from the additive group $\mathbb{R}$ into the group of linear automorphisms of $E$. The defining property $\varphi(t+\tau) = \varphi(t) \circ \varphi(\tau)$ makes it a commutative group parametrized by a real parameter, with $\varphi(0) = \iota$. Such groups arise naturally as solution flows of linear differential equations with constant generators: $\dot{\varphi}(t) = \psi \circ \varphi(t)$.
