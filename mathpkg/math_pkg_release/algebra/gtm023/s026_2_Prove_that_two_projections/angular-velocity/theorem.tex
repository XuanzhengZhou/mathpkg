Let $\varphi(t)$ be a differentiable family of rotations of 3-space with $\varphi(0) = \iota$. Given a vector $x$, the orbit $x(t) = \varphi(t)x$ satisfies
$$\dot{x}(t) = u(t) \times x(t)$$
where $u(t)$ is the angular velocity vector, uniquely determined by $\psi(t) y = u(t) \times y$. The straight line generated by $u(t)$ is the instantaneous axis; the norm $|u(t)|$ is the magnitude of the velocity of a vector at distance 1 from the instantaneous axis.
