A $G$-graded vector space is a vector space equipped with a direct sum decomposition indexed by an abelian group $G$ (the group of degrees), where each summand consists of vectors assigned a specific degree in $G$. This structure generalises the familiar $\mathbb{Z}$-grading and provides the algebraic foundation for homology theory. The degree assignment must be injective across the decomposition summands, and the zero vector is declared homogeneous of every degree by convention.
