Let $E$ be a vector space with $G$-gradation $E = \sum_{j \in G} E_j$ and $H$-gradation $E = \sum_{k \in H} F_k$. These two gradations are called \textit{compatible} if each $E_j \cap F_k$ is a subspace and
$$E = \sum_{j,k} E_j \cap F_k$$
is a direct decomposition. Then the compatible $G$ and $H$-gradations determine a $(G \oplus H)$-gradation by $E_{j,k} = E_j \cap F_k$.

Conversely, suppose a $(G \oplus H)$-gradation $E = \sum_{j,k} E_{j,k}$ is given. Then compatible $G$ and $H$-gradations of $E$ are defined by
$$E = \sum_{j \in G} E_j, \quad E_j = \sum_{k \in H} E_{j,k}$$
and
$$E = \sum_{k \in H} F_k, \quad F_k = \sum_{j \in G} E_{j,k}.$$
Moreover, the $(G \oplus H)$-gradation determined by these $G$ and $H$-gradations satisfies $E_{j,k} = E_j \cap F_k$.
