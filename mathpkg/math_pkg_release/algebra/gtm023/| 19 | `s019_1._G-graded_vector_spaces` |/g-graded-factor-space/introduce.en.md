A $G$-graded factor space is the quotient of a $G$-graded vector space by a $G$-graded subspace, equipped with the unique $G$-gradation that makes the canonical projection homogeneous of degree zero. This follows directly from the previous proposition on gradations induced by homogeneous surjections, applied to the canonical projection $\pi: E \rightarrow E/F$, whose kernel $F$ is a graded subspace.
