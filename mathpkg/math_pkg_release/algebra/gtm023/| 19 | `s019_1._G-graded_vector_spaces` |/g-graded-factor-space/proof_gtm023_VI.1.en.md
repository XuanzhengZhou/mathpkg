Since $F$ is a $G$-graded subspace of $E$, the canonical projection $\pi: E \rightarrow E/F$ is a linear surjection whose kernel $\ker \pi = F$ is a graded subspace. By the proposition on gradation induced by a homogeneous surjection (applied to $\pi$, which is homogeneous of any degree with respect to the original gradation on $E$), there is a unique $G$-gradation on $E/F$ such that $\pi$ is homogeneous of degree zero. Specifically, $(E/F)_j = \pi(E_j)$ for each $j \in G$. The uniqueness follows from the fact that the degree-$j$ component of $E/F$ must be the image under $\pi$ of $E_j$.
