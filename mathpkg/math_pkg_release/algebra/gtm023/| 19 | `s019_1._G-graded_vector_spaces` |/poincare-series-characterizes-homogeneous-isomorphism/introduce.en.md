This theorem states that for almost finite positively graded vector spaces, equality of Poincaré series is equivalent to the existence of a degree-zero homogeneous linear isomorphism. In one direction, a homogeneous isomorphism of degree zero restricts to isomorphisms $E_k \cong F_k$ for each $k$, so the dimensions (and hence the Poincaré series) coincide. Conversely, if the series are equal then $\dim E_k = \dim F_k$ for all $k$, and one can construct a degree-zero isomorphism by choosing linear isomorphisms on each homogeneous component. The Poincaré series is thus a complete invariant for positively graded vector spaces up to degree-preserving isomorphism.
