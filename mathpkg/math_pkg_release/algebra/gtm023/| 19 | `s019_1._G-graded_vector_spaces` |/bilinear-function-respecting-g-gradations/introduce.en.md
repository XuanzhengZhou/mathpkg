A bilinear function $\varphi: E \times F \rightarrow \Gamma$ between two $G$-graded vector spaces respects the gradations if it pairs only vectors of the same degree nontrivially. In other words, the homogeneous components are mutually orthogonal whenever their degrees differ: $\varphi(E_k, F_j) = 0$ for $k \neq j$. This condition reduces a bilinear function on the whole space to a collection of bilinear functions $\varphi_k$ on each pair $(E_k, F_k)$, providing a clean decomposition compatible with the grading structure.
