A homogeneous linear mapping between $G$-graded vector spaces is a linear map that shifts degrees by a fixed amount $k \in G$. Specifically, $\varphi$ maps vectors of degree $j$ to vectors of degree $j+k$. The element $k$ is the degree of $\varphi$. Homogeneous maps of degree $0$ are the natural morphisms in the category of $G$-graded vector spaces, while maps of nonzero degree appear in the context of differential operators and chain maps in homology.
