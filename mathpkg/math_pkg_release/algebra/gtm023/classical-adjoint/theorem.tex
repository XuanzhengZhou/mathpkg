Let $\varphi$ be a linear transformation of an $n$-dimensional vector space $E$ and $\Delta \neq 0$ be a determinant function. The classical adjoint of $\varphi$ is the linear transformation $\operatorname{ad}(\varphi): E \to E$ defined by
$$\Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_{n-1}, \operatorname{ad}(\varphi) x) = \sum_{j=1}^{n} (-1)^{j-1} \Delta(x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_j, \ldots, \varphi x_{n-1}, \varphi x) \cdot x_j.$$

**Proposition V:** The classical adjoint satisfies
$$\operatorname{ad}(\varphi) \circ \varphi = \varphi \circ \operatorname{ad}(\varphi) = \iota \cdot \det \varphi.$$

**Corollary:** If $\det \varphi \neq 0$, then $\varphi^{-1} = (\det \varphi)^{-1} \operatorname{ad}(\varphi)$.
