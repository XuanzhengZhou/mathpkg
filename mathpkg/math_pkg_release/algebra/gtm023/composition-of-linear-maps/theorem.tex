Let $\varphi: E \to F$ and $\psi: F \to G$ be two linear mappings. Then the \textbf{composition}
$$\psi \circ \varphi: E \to G$$
is defined by
$$(\psi \circ \varphi) x = \psi(\varphi x), \quad x \in E.$$
The relation
$$\psi \circ \varphi \left( \sum_i \lambda_i x_i \right) = \sum_i \lambda_i \psi \circ \varphi x_i$$
shows that $\psi \circ \varphi$ is a linear mapping. For a linear transformation $\varphi$ in $E$, define $\varphi^k = \varphi \circ \cdots \circ \varphi$ ($k$ times) and $\varphi^0 = \iota$.
