In a pseudo-Euclidean space of maximal index $n-1$, the geometry of time-like and light-vectors is highly constrained. Two time-like vectors can never be orthogonal, and two light-vectors are orthogonal exactly when they are linearly dependent. These properties follow from the decomposition of the space relative to a fixed time-like unit vector and have important consequences for the structure of the orthogonal complement of a light-vector.
