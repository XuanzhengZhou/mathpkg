A Lorentz transformation is a pseudo-Euclidean rotation of Minkowski space, representing the symmetries of spacetime in special relativity. Orthochroneous Lorentz transformations preserve the causal structure by not interchanging the fore-cone (future-directed time-like vectors) with the past-cone (past-directed time-like vectors).
