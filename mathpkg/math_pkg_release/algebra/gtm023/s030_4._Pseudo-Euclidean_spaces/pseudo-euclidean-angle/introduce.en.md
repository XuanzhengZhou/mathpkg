In a 2-dimensional pseudo-Euclidean space of index 1, the pseudo-Euclidean angle between two space-like vectors generalizes the Euclidean angle using hyperbolic functions instead of trigonometric ones. The angle $\theta$ is defined via hyperbolic cosine and sine, reflecting the indefinite nature of the inner product. This angle is a real number ranging over the entire real line.
