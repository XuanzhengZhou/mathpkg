A linear transformation $\varphi$ of a pseudo-Euclidean space $E$ which preserves the inner product,
$$(\varphi x, \varphi y) = (x, y) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in E,$$
is called a \text{pseudo-Euclidean rotation}.

From this definition it follows that:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $(\varphi x, \varphi x) = (x, x)$, so $\varphi$ preserves the quadratic form.
  \item $\det \varphi = \pm 1$.
  \item If $e$ is an eigenvector with eigenvalue $\lambda$, i.e., $\varphi e = \lambda e$, then $(\varphi e, \varphi e) = \lambda^2 (e, e)$, so $\lambda = \pm 1$ whenever $e$ is not a light-vector. Light-vector eigenvectors may have $\lambda \neq \pm 1$.
\end{enumerate}
