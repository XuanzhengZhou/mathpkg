In a pseudo-Euclidean space, an orthonormal basis generalizes the Euclidean concept by allowing the basis vectors to have norm $\pm 1$ rather than strictly $+1$. The signs $\varepsilon_v$ encode the signature of the inner product: $+1$ for the first $s$ vectors and $-1$ for the remaining $n-s$ vectors, where $s$ is the index. Such an orthonormal basis always exists.
