A pseudo-orthogonal matrix represents a pseudo-Euclidean rotation relative to an orthonormal basis. The defining relations involve the signature signs $\varepsilon_{\nu}$ and generalize the standard orthogonality condition $A^T A = I$ to the indefinite case $A^T \mathcal{E} A = \mathcal{E}$, where $\mathcal{E}$ is the diagonal matrix with entries $\pm 1$ encoding the signature.
