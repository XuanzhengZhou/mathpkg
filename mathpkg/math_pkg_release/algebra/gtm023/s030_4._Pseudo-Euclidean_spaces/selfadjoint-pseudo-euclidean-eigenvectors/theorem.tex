Let $E$ be a pseudo-Euclidean space of dimension $n \geq 3$ and let $\varphi$ be a selfadjoint linear transformation of $E$, i.e., $\tilde{\varphi} = \varphi$. Assume that
$$(x, \varphi x) \neq 0 \quad \text{for all light-vectors } x.$$
Then there exists an orthonormal basis $e_v\ (v = 1 \ldots n)$ consisting of eigenvectors of $\varphi$:
$$\varphi e_v = \lambda_v \varepsilon_v e_v \quad (v = 1 \ldots n),$$
satisfying
$$(\varphi e_v, e_\mu) = \lambda_v \varepsilon_v \delta_{v\mu} \quad (v, \mu = 1 \ldots n).$$
