In a pseudo-Euclidean space, a subspace $E_1$ can intersect its orthogonal complement non-trivially, unlike in the Euclidean case. This criterion states that the intersection is trivial precisely when the restriction of the inner product to $E_1$ remains non-degenerate. When this holds, the space decomposes as an orthogonal direct sum $E = E_1 \oplus E_1^\perp$.
