For a linear transformation $\varphi$ whose minimum polynomial $\mu$ decomposes as a product of powers of irreducible polynomials, the ordinary (non-generalized) eigenspaces correspond exactly to the kernels of the linear factors. Specifically, if $\lambda$ is an eigenvalue, then $t - \lambda$ appears among the irreducible factors $f_i$, and the eigenspace $E_\lambda$ equals $K(f_i) = \ker(f_i(\varphi))$. This connects the primary decomposition theory with the classical notion of eigenspaces.
