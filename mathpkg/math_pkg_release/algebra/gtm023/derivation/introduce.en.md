A derivation is a linear map satisfying the Leibniz product rule. Derivations generalize the ordinary derivative from calculus. The kernel of a derivation is a subalgebra. The space of derivations is closed under the commutator bracket, making it a Lie algebra. The Leibniz formula for higher powers is proved by induction.
