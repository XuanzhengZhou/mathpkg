Combining the criteria from 10.15 and 10.16, this theorem presents the complete affine classification of quadrics. Two families of normal forms -- one for quadrics with center and one for quadrics without center -- exhaust all affine equivalence classes. The parameters $(r, s)$ (rank and index) are shown to be complete affine invariants, and explicit formulas count the number of distinct classes for each rank. This is the culminating structural result of the affine theory of quadrics.
