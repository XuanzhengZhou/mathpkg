The affine equivalence classes of quadrics in the plane ($n=2$) and in 3-space ($n=3$) are represented by the following normal forms.

\textbf{Plane} ($n=2$):

I. Quadrics with center:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $r=2$, $s=2$: $\xi^2 + \eta^2 = 1$ (ellipse)
\item $r=2$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 - \eta^2 = 1$ (hyperbola)
\item $r=1$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 = 1$ (two parallel lines)
\end{enumerate}

II. Quadrics without center:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $r=1$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 = 2\eta$ (parabola)
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{3-Space} ($n=3$):

I. Quadrics with center:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $r=3$, $s=3$: $\xi^2 + \eta^2 + \zeta^2 = 1$ (ellipsoid)
\item $r=3$, $s=2$: $\xi^2 + \eta^2 - \zeta^2 = 1$ (hyperboloid of one sheet)
\item $r=3$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 - \eta^2 - \zeta^2 = 1$ (hyperboloid of two sheets)
\item $r=2$, $s=2$: $\xi^2 + \eta^2 = 1$ (elliptic cylinder)
\item $r=2$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 - \eta^2 = 1$ (hyperbolic cylinder)
\item $r=1$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 = 1$ (two parallel planes)
\end{enumerate}

II. Quadrics without center:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $r=2$, $s=2$: $\xi^2 + \eta^2 = 2\zeta$ (elliptic paraboloid)
\item $r=2$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 - \eta^2 = 2\zeta$ (hyperbolic paraboloid)
\item $r=1$, $s=1$: $\xi^2 = 2\zeta$ (parabolic cylinder)
\end{enumerate}
