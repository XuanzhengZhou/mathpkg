Let $\Phi: E \times E \to \mathbb{R}$ and $\Psi: F \times F \to \mathbb{R}$ be two symmetric bilinear functions on vector spaces $E$ and $F$ of dimension $n$. A linear isomorphism $\tau: E \to F$ with the property that
$$\Phi(x, y) = \Psi(\tau x, \tau y) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in E$$
exists if and only if $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ have the same rank and the same index.
