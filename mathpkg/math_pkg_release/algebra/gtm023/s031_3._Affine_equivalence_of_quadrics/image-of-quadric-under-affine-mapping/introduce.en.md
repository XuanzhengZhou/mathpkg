An affine mapping $x' = \tau x + b$ transforms a quadric $Q$ into another quadric $Q'$ whose equation is explicitly determined by the inverse linear part $\tau^{-1}$ and the translation $b$. The bilinear form $\Psi$, linear form $g$, and constant $\beta$ defining $Q'$ are expressed directly in terms of the original data $(\Phi, f, \alpha)$ and the transformation parameters. This compatibility relation ensures that membership in the quadric is preserved under the affine mapping: $x \in Q$ if and only if $x' \in Q'$.
