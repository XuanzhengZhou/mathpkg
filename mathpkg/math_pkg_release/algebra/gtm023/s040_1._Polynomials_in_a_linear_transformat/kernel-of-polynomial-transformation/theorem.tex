Let $E$ be a finite-dimensional non-trivial vector space over a field $\Gamma$ of characteristic $0$, and let $\varphi: E \to E$ be a linear transformation. For any polynomial $f \in \Gamma[t]$, denote by $K(f)$ the kernel of the linear transformation $f(\varphi)$:

$$K(f) = \ker f(\varphi) = \{ x \in E \mid f(\varphi)x = 0 \}.$$

Then $K(f) \subset E$ is a subspace stable under $\varphi$. In particular,

$$K(1) = 0, \quad K(t) = \ker \varphi, \quad K(\mu) = E,$$

where $\mu$ denotes the minimum polynomial of $\varphi$.
