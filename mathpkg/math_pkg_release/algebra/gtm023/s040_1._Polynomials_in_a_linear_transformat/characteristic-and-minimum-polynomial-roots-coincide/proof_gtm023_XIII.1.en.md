The characteristic polynomial is defined as $\chi(t) = \sum_{\nu} \alpha_{\nu} t^{n-\nu}$ where the $\alpha_{\nu}$ are the characteristic coefficients of $\varphi$; equivalently $\chi(\lambda) = \det(\varphi - \lambda \iota)$. By definition, $\dim E = \deg \chi$.

Recall from sec. 4.20 that the distinct roots of $\chi$ are precisely the eigenvalues of $\varphi$. The previous theorem established that the eigenvalues are precisely the distinct roots of $\mu$. Therefore the distinct roots of $\chi$ and $\mu$ coincide.

The statement $\mu \mid \chi$ will be proved in sec. 13.20 as a consequence of the structure theory developed in this chapter.
