By definition, $f(\varphi_f) = 0$, so $\mu_f \mid f$. Hence $K(\mu_f) \subset K(f)$. On the other hand, $f(\varphi)$ vanishes on $K(f)$, so $\mu_f(\varphi)$ also vanishes on $K(f)$, giving $K(f) \subset K(\mu_f)$. Thus

$$K(\mu_f) = K(f).$$

Now $f \mid \mu$ and $\mu_f \mid f$. If $\mu_f$ were a proper divisor of $f$, then Proposition III (applied with $g = \mu_f$) would imply $K(\mu_f) \subsetneq K(f)$, a contradiction. Therefore $\mu_f = f$.
