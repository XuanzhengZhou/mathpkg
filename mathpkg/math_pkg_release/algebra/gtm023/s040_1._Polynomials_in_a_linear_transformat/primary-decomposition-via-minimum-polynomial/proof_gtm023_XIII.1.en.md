Since $\mu = f_1 \cdots f_r$ with the $f_i$ pairwise relatively prime, Corollary II to Proposition II yields

$$E = K(\mu) = K(f_1) \oplus \cdots \oplus K(f_r).$$

The second statement follows directly from Corollary II to Proposition III, since each $f_i$ is a monic divisor of $\mu$ (after normalizing if necessary).
