A field is an algebraic structure with two binary operations (addition and multiplication) satisfying the field axioms. It forms a commutative group under addition, its non-zero elements form a commutative group under multiplication, and the two operations are connected by the distributive law. Fields serve as the coefficient domains for vector spaces throughout linear algebra; common examples are $\mathbb{Q}$, $\mathbb{R}$, and $\mathbb{C}$.
