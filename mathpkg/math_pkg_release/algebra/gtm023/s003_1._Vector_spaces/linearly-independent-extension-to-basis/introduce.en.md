Corollary II is the dual of Corollary I: every linearly independent set can be extended to a basis by adding vectors. It follows from Theorem I by taking $S = E$ and $R$ as the given linearly independent family. Together, Corollaries I and II say that bases are precisely the maximal linearly independent sets and the minimal generating sets, connecting the two defining properties in a fundamental way.
