Linear dependence can be characterized concretely: for a family of at least two vectors, the family is linearly dependent precisely when one of the vectors can be expressed as a linear combination of the others. The forward direction follows by isolating a vector whose coefficient is nonzero in a linear relation. The converse is immediate: if one vector is a linear combination of others, setting its coefficient to -1 yields a nontrivial linear relation summing to zero.
