A family of vectors is linearly dependent when there exists a nontrivial linear relation among them — that is, some linear combination with not-all-zero coefficients yields the zero vector. Linear dependence captures the idea that some vectors in the family are redundant: at least one vector can be expressed as a linear combination of the others (for families of two or more vectors). A singleton family $\{x\}$ is linearly dependent if and only if $x = 0$.
