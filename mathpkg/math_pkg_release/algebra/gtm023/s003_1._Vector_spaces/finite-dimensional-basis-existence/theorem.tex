Let $E$ be a non-trivial vector space and $S = (x_1, \ldots, x_m)$ a finite system of generators.

(i) $E$ has a finite basis.

(ii) If $R \subset S$ given by $R = (x_1, \ldots, x_r)$ ($r \leq m$) is linearly independent, then $R$ can be extended to a basis of $E$ consisting of vectors from $S$ together with a finite number of other vectors from $S$; that is, there exists a basis of the form
$$x_1, \ldots, x_r, y_1, \ldots, y_s \quad \text{where } y_j \in S.$$
