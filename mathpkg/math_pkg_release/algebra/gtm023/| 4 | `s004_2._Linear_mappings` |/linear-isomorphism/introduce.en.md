A linear isomorphism is a bijective linear mapping between two vector spaces. It preserves all linear structure, meaning that the addition and scalar multiplication operations are mapped bijectively. The existence of a linear isomorphism between vector spaces $E$ and $F$ establishes that they are isomorphic -- structurally indistinguishable as vector spaces. The inverse of a linear isomorphism is itself a linear isomorphism, called the inverse isomorphism.
