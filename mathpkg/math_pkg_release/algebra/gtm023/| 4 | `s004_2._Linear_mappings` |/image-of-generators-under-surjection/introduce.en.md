This proposition states that surjective linear maps preserve generating sets: if a set $S$ generates the domain $E$, then its image $\varphi(S)$ generates the codomain $F$. The proof is straightforward: every $y \in F$ has a preimage $x \in E$ (by surjectivity), which is a linear combination of elements of $S$; applying $\varphi$ distributes over this combination, expressing $y$ as a linear combination of elements of $\varphi(S)$.
