Two vector spaces are isomorphic when there exists a bijective linear mapping (a linear isomorphism) between them. Isomorphic vector spaces are structurally identical as vector spaces: they have the same dimension and all linear-algebraic properties are preserved. Isomorphism is an equivalence relation on the class of vector spaces over a given field.
