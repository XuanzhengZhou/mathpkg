This theorem gives a clean characterization of linear isomorphisms in terms of bases. A linear isomorphism sends any basis of the domain to a basis of the codomain. Conversely, if a linear map sends some basis of the domain to a basis of the codomain, then the map must be an isomorphism. This follows from combining the facts that surjective maps preserve generating sets and injective maps preserve linear independence. The theorem is fundamental for establishing that two vector spaces with the same dimension are isomorphic.
