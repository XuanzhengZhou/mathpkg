An involution is a linear transformation $\varphi$ on a vector space $E$ whose square equals the identity: $\varphi^2 = \iota$. Equivalently, $\varphi$ is its own inverse. Involutions play an important role in the structure theory of linear transformations, for example in the decomposition of a vector space into the $+1$ and $-1$ eigenspaces of the involution.
