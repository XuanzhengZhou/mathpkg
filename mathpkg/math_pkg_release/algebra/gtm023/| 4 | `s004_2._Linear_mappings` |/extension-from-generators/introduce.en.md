Proposition I characterizes when a set map defined on a system of generators of a vector space can be extended to a linear mapping on the whole space. The extension, if it exists, is unique. The necessary and sufficient condition is that the map respects all linear relations among the generators: whenever a linear combination of generators vanishes, the corresponding linear combination of their images must also vanish. This proposition is fundamental because it reduces the construction of linear maps to verifying a condition on generators.
