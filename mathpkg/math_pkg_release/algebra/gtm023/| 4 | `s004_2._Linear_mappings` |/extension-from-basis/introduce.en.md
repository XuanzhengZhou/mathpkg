Proposition II states that a set map defined on a basis of a vector space can be extended uniquely to a linear mapping on the entire space. This is a stronger result than Proposition I (extension from generators), because a basis always satisfies the necessary and sufficient condition automatically: the only linear relation among basis vectors is the trivial one, so the condition from Proposition I is vacuously satisfied. This proposition is foundational for defining linear maps by specifying their values on a basis.
