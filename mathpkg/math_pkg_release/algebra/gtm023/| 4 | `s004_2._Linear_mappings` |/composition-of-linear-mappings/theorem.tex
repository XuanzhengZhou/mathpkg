Let $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$ and $\psi: F \rightarrow G$ be two linear mappings. The \textbf{composition} of $\varphi$ and $\psi$ is the mapping
$$
\psi \circ \varphi: E \rightarrow G
$$
defined by
$$
(\psi \circ \varphi) x = \psi(\varphi x), \quad x \in E.
$$
The composition $\psi \circ \varphi$ is a linear mapping of $E$ into $G$. If $\varphi$ is a linear transformation in $E$, we denote $\varphi \circ \varphi$ by $\varphi^2$ and, more generally, $\varphi \circ \dots \circ \varphi$ ($k$ times) by $\varphi^k$. For $k = 0$ we set $\varphi^0 = \iota$ (the identity map).
