The inverse isomorphism is the inverse of a linear isomorphism. Given a linear isomorphism $\varphi: E \to F$, its set-theoretic inverse $\varphi^{-1}: F \to E$ automatically satisfies the linearity conditions and is therefore itself a linear isomorphism. This fact follows directly from the bijectivity of $\varphi$ and the linearity axioms.
