Any set map defined on a linearly independent subset can be extended to a linear mapping on the whole vector space. The extension is not unique (unlike the basis case), because the values on a basis extension of $S$ can be chosen arbitrarily. The proof uses the basis extension theorem to embed $S$ in a basis $T$, extends $\varphi_0$ arbitrarily to $T$, and then applies Proposition II.
