Every symmetric bilinear function on a finite-dimensional inner product space can be diagonalized by an orthonormal basis. The diagonal entries are the eigenvalues of the associated selfadjoint transformation. This result is equivalent to the principal axis theorem for quadratic forms: every real quadratic form can be expressed as a sum of squares $\sum \lambda_v \xi_v^2$ in suitable orthonormal coordinates.
