The sum of two orthogonal projections is itself an orthogonal projection precisely when their image subspaces are orthogonal. In that case, the sum projects onto the direct sum of the two subspaces. This result is consistent with the geometric intuition that projecting onto orthogonal components and adding the results yields the projection onto the combined subspace.
