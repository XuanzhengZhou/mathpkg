The orthogonal decomposition theorem provides the complete spectral decomposition of a selfadjoint transformation. The space splits as an orthogonal direct sum of eigenspaces, one for each distinct eigenvalue. This decomposition yields the factorization of the characteristic polynomial into linear factors over the reals, showing that all eigenvalues are real and the algebraic multiplicity of each eigenvalue equals the geometric multiplicity (the dimension of its eigenspace).
