Every real symmetric matrix has a full set of real eigenvalues. This follows by interpreting the matrix as the representation of a selfadjoint linear transformation with respect to an orthonormal basis, and applying the spectral theorem. The result is fundamental in applications ranging from principal component analysis to the classification of quadratic forms and the study of vibrating systems.
