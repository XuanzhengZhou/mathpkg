Let $\Phi$ be a bilinear function on an inner product space $E$ and let $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$ be the corresponding linear transformation defined by

$$\Phi(x, y) = (\varphi x, y) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in E.$$

The \textbf{eigenvectors} and \textbf{eigenvalues} of the bilinear function $\Phi$ are defined to be the eigenvectors and eigenvalues of the corresponding transformation $\varphi$.
