The composition of two orthogonal projections is itself an orthogonal projection precisely when the projections commute. Commuting orthogonal projections correspond to subspaces whose associated projections are simultaneously diagonalizable. The image of the product is the intersection of the two subspaces. This result is important in the spectral theory where the product of spectral projections corresponds to intersecting spectral subspaces.
