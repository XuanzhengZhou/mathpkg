The Rayleigh quotient $F(x) = (x, \varphi x)/(x, x)$ is a fundamental tool in the spectral theory of selfadjoint operators. It maps each nonzero vector to a scalar that lies between the smallest and largest eigenvalue of $\varphi$. Its critical points on the unit sphere correspond precisely to the eigenvectors of $\varphi$, and the critical values are the eigenvalues. This variational characterization provides the standard proof of the spectral theorem for finite-dimensional selfadjoint transformations.
