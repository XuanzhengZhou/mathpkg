Prove that a regular linear transformation $\varphi$ of a Euclidean space can be uniquely written in the form

$$\varphi = \sigma \circ \tau$$

where $\sigma$ is a positive selfadjoint transformation and $\tau$ is a rotation.

Hint: Use problems 5 and 10. (This is essentially the unitary trick of Weyl.)
