Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional real vector space and let $\Phi, \Psi$ be symmetric bilinear functions on $E$ with $\Psi$ non-degenerate. Then there exists a basis $e_1, \ldots, e_n$ of $E$ such that both $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ have diagonal form. More precisely, after appropriate ordering and scaling of the basis vectors,
$$\Psi(e_v, e_\mu) = \varepsilon_v \delta_{v\mu}, \qquad \varepsilon_v = \begin{cases} +1 & (v = 1, \ldots, s) \\ -1 & (v = s+1, \ldots, n) \end{cases}$$
where $s$ is the signature of $\Psi$, and
$$\Phi(e_v, e_\mu) = \lambda_v \varepsilon_v \delta_{v\mu}$$
for real numbers $\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n$.
