This proposition handles the case where the symmetric bilinear function $\Psi$ is degenerate in the simultaneous diagonalization problem. By considering the associated linear maps $\varphi, \psi : E \to E^*$ and evaluating a determinant function on the family $\varphi x_i + \lambda \psi x_i$, one obtains a polynomial $f(\lambda)$ whose degree equals the rank $r$ of $\Psi$. The non-vanishing of the leading coefficient follows from the linear independence of $\psi x_1, \ldots, \psi x_r$ in the image of $\psi$ and $\varphi x_{r+1}, \ldots, \varphi x_n$ on the kernel. Choosing $\lambda_0$ with $f(\lambda_0) \neq 0$ ensures that the perturbed bilinear function $\Phi + \lambda_0 \Psi$ is non-degenerate, reducing the problem to the previously established non-degenerate case.
