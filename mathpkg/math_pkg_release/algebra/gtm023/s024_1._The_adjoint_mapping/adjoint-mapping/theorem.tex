Consider two inner product spaces $E$ and $F$ and a linear mapping $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$. Since inner products are defined in $E$ and $F$, these linear spaces can be considered as dual to themselves. The dual mapping $\varphi^*: F^* \rightarrow E^*$ then becomes a linear mapping of $F$ into $E$. This mapping is called the \textit{adjoint mapping} of $\varphi$ and is denoted by $\tilde{\varphi}$. It satisfies

$$(\varphi x, y) = (x, \tilde{\varphi} y) \quad x \in E,\; y \in F.$$
