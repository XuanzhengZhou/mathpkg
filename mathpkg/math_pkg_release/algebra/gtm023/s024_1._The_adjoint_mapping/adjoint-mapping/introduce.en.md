The adjoint mapping generalizes the notion of the transpose of a matrix to the setting of linear maps between inner product spaces. Given a linear map $\varphi: E \to F$ between two inner product spaces, its adjoint $\tilde{\varphi}: F \to E$ is the unique linear map satisfying $(\varphi x, y) = (x, \tilde{\varphi} y)$ for all vectors $x \in E$ and $y \in F$. This concept relies on the fact that an inner product provides a natural identification of a space with its dual, so the dual mapping $\varphi^*: F^* \to E^*$ can be reinterpreted as a map from $F$ back to $E$. The adjoint plays a fundamental role in the spectral theory of linear transformations, particularly for selfadjoint and normal operators.
