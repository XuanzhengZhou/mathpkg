Let $\varphi: E \rightarrow E$ be a linear transformation of an inner product space and let $\tilde{\varphi}$ be its adjoint. Suppose $e$ and $\tilde{e}$ are eigenvectors of $\varphi$ and $\tilde{\varphi}$ respectively:
$$\varphi e = \lambda e, \quad \tilde{\varphi} \tilde{e} = \tilde{\lambda} \tilde{e}.$$
Then
$$(\tilde{\lambda} - \lambda)(e, \tilde{e}) = 0.$$
Consequently, if $\tilde{\lambda} \neq \lambda$, then $(e, \tilde{e}) = 0$; that is, eigenvectors of $\varphi$ and $\tilde{\varphi}$ corresponding to different eigenvalues are orthogonal.
