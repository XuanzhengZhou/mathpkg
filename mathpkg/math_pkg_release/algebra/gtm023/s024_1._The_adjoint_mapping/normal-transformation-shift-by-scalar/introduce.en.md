Normality is preserved under translation by a scalar multiple of the identity. This is important because the spectral theory of normal transformations relies on studying the shifted operators $\varphi - \lambda \iota$, whose kernels yield the eigenspaces. The fact that these shifted operators remain normal ensures that all the properties of normal transformations apply to them.
