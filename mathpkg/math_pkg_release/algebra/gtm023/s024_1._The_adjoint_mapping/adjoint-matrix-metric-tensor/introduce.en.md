When a linear map and its adjoint are expressed in terms of bases that are not necessarily orthonormal, their matrix representations are related through the metric tensors (Gram matrices) of the inner products on the domain and codomain. The formula $\tilde{\alpha}_\mu^\varphi = \sum_{v,\kappa} \alpha_v^\kappa h_{\kappa\mu} g^{v\varphi}$ shows how the adjoint matrix is obtained by a contraction involving the original matrix and both metric tensors. This general relationship reduces to the familiar transpose when the bases are orthonormal.
