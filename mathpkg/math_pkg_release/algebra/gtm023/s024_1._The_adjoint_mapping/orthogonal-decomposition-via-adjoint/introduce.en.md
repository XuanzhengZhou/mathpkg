The adjoint mapping provides a fundamental orthogonal decomposition of the target space: the image of a linear map and the kernel of its adjoint are orthogonal complements. This generalizes the fact that for a matrix $A$, the column space and the nullspace of $A^T$ are orthogonal complements. The decomposition $F = \operatorname{Im} \varphi \oplus \ker \tilde{\varphi}$ is a cornerstone for understanding the structure of linear maps between inner product spaces and is essential for the spectral theory developed later in this chapter.
