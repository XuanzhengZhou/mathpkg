From the defining relation $(\varphi x, y) = (x, \tilde{\varphi} y)$ we see that $y \in \ker \tilde{\varphi}$ if and only if $(x, \tilde{\varphi} y) = 0$ for all $x \in E$, which is equivalent to $(\varphi x, y) = 0$ for all $x \in E$, i.e., $y \in (\operatorname{Im} \varphi)^\perp$. Thus $\ker \tilde{\varphi} = (\operatorname{Im} \varphi)^\perp$. Since $F$ is finite-dimensional, the orthogonal complement yields the direct sum decomposition

$$F = \operatorname{Im} \varphi \oplus (\operatorname{Im} \varphi)^\perp = \operatorname{Im} \varphi \oplus \ker \tilde{\varphi}.$$
