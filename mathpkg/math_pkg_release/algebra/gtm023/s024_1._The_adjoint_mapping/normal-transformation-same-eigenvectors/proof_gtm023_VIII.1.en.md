Since $\varphi$ is normal, $\varphi - \lambda \iota$ is also normal by the previous proposition. Applying the result that the kernels of a normal transformation and its adjoint coincide to $\varphi - \lambda \iota$, we obtain

$$\ker(\varphi - \lambda \iota) = \ker(\widetilde{\varphi - \lambda \iota}) = \ker(\tilde{\varphi} - \lambda \iota).$$

Thus $x$ is an eigenvector of $\varphi$ with eigenvalue $\lambda$ if and only if $x$ is an eigenvector of $\tilde{\varphi}$ with eigenvalue $\lambda$.
