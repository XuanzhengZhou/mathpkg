When working with orthonormal bases, the metric tensors reduce to identity matrices, and the general formula for the adjoint matrix simplifies dramatically: the matrix of the adjoint is simply the transpose of the original matrix. This is the familiar relationship from elementary linear algebra and justifies the identification of the adjoint with the conjugate transpose (or simply transpose in the real case) relative to an orthonormal basis.
