Assume that the bases $x_v$ ($v = 1, \dots, n$) of $E$ and $y_\mu$ ($\mu = 1, \dots, m$) of $F$ are orthonormal, i.e.,
$$g_{v\lambda} = \delta_{v\lambda}, \quad h_{\kappa\mu} = \delta_{\kappa\mu}.$$
Then the matrix of the adjoint mapping is the transpose of the matrix of the original mapping:
$$\tilde{\alpha}_\mu^v = \alpha_v^\mu.$$
