The adjoint operation on linear maps between inner product spaces is an involution: taking the adjoint twice returns the original map. This follows directly from the defining relation $(\varphi x, y) = (x, \tilde{\varphi} y)$ together with the symmetry of the inner product. Writing the same relation for $\tilde{\varphi}$ gives $(\tilde{\varphi} y, x) = (y, \tilde{\tilde{\varphi}} x)$, and comparing the two equations yields $\varphi = \tilde{\tilde{\varphi}}$.
