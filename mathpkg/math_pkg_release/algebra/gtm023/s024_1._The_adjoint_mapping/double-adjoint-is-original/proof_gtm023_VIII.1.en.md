The mappings $\tilde{\varphi}$ and $\tilde{\tilde{\varphi}}$ are related by

$$(\tilde{\varphi} y, x) = (y, \tilde{\tilde{\varphi}} x).$$

On the other hand, equation (8.2) gives

$$(\varphi x, y) = (x, \tilde{\varphi} y)$$

for all $x \in E$, $y \in F$. By symmetry of the inner product, $(x, \tilde{\varphi} y) = (\tilde{\varphi} y, x)$. Hence

$$(\varphi x, y) = (\tilde{\tilde{\varphi}} x, y)$$

for all $x \in E$, $y \in F$. Since the inner product is nondegenerate, this implies $\varphi = \tilde{\tilde{\varphi}}$.
