The existence of one-sided inverses characterizes injectivity and surjectivity: right inverses exist precisely for surjective maps, left inverses for injective maps. A map that is both injective and surjective (an isomorphism) has a unique two-sided inverse. This is a categorical property that holds in any concrete category.
