A normed linear space is called complete if every Cauchy sequence in it converges to a limit within the space. This property is fundamental in functional analysis: complete normed spaces are called Banach spaces. Completeness ensures that limits of "potentially convergent" sequences (those whose terms eventually cluster arbitrarily close together) actually exist in the space, making it suitable for analysis and the application of fixed-point theorems.
