Let $F$ be the subset of an associative division algebra $A$ over $\mathbb{R}$ consisting of those elements $x$ which satisfy
$$x^2 = -\gamma^2 e \quad \text{for some } \gamma \in \mathbb{R}.$$
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(i)] $F$ is a vector space.
    \item[(ii)] $A = (e) \oplus F$, where $(e)$ denotes the $1$-dimensional subspace generated by $e$.
    \item[(iii)] If $x \in F$ and $y \in F$, then $xy + yx \in (e)$ and $xy - yx \in F$.
\end{enumerate}
