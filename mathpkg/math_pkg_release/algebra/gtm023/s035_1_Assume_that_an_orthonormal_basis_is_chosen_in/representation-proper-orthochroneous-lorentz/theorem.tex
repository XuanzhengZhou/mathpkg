Every proper orthochroneous Lorentz transformation $T: S \to S$ can be represented in the form $T = T_{\alpha}$ for some linear transformation $\alpha$ of the complex plane $C$ with $\det \alpha = 1$. That is, the homomorphism

$$\operatorname{SL}(2, \mathbb{C}) \to \operatorname{SO}^+(1, 3), \quad \alpha \mapsto T_{\alpha}$$

is surjective onto the identity component of the Lorentz group.
