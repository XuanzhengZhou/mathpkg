This is the main theorem of Greub's treatment of Lorentz transformations via linear algebra over the complex plane. It establishes that the group homomorphism from $\operatorname{SL}(2, \mathbb{C})$ to the proper orthochroneous Lorentz group $\operatorname{SO}^+(1,3)$, given by $\alpha \mapsto T_{\alpha}$ where $T_{\alpha} \sigma = \alpha \circ \sigma \circ \tilde{\alpha}$, is surjective. The proof proceeds in two cases: when $\iota$ is invariant under $T$ (using the isomorphism $\Omega: Q \to S$ to reduce to the quaternionic representation), and when $T\iota \neq \iota$ (constructing a boost in the plane spanned by $\iota$ and $T\iota$ via hyperbolic trigonometry, then composing). This provides a concrete spinorial description of Lorentz transformations.
