Recall from sec. 11.14 the definition of the $4$-dimensional Euclidean space $Q$. Introduce a new inner product in $Q$ by setting

$$\langle \varphi, \psi \rangle = -\frac{1}{2} \operatorname{tr}(\varphi \circ \psi) \quad \varphi, \psi \in Q.$$

There exists a linear isomorphism $\Omega: Q \to S$ such that

$$\langle \Omega \varphi, \Omega \psi \rangle = \langle \varphi, \psi \rangle \quad \varphi, \psi \in Q.$$

Explicitly,

$$\Omega \varphi = \frac{1-i}{2} \iota \operatorname{tr} \varphi + i \varphi \quad \varphi \in Q,$$

and its inverse $\Psi: S \to Q$ is given by

$$\Psi(\sigma) = \frac{1+i}{2} \iota \cdot \operatorname{tr} \sigma - i \sigma \quad \sigma \in S.$$
