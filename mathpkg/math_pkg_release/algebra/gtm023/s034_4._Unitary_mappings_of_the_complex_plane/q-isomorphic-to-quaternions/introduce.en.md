The algebra $Q$ of complex linear transformations satisfying $\varphi + \tilde{\varphi} = \iota \cdot \operatorname{tr} \varphi$ is an associative division algebra of real dimension $4$. By a classical theorem (Frobenius), every finite-dimensional associative division algebra over $\mathbb{R}$ is isomorphic to $\mathbb{R}$, $\mathbb{C}$, or $\mathbb{H}$. Since $Q$ has dimension $4$ and is not commutative, it must be isomorphic to the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$. The above constructions -- the inner product, the decomposition $\varphi \circ \psi = -(\varphi, \psi) \iota + \varphi \times \psi$, and the Lagrange identity -- make this isomorphism completely explicit.
