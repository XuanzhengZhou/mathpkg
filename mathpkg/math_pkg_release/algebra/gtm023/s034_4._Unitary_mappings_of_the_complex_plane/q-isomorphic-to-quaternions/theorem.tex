The algebra $Q$ is an associative division algebra of dimension $4$ over $\mathbb{R}$. Therefore $Q$ is isomorphic to the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$. The isomorphism can be made explicit: the traceless subalgebra $Q_1$ corresponds to the pure imaginary quaternions $\operatorname{Im}(\mathbb{H})$, with the inner product and cross product on $Q_1$ matching the standard dot and cross products on $\mathbb{R}^3$.
