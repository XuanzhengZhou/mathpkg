We have the anticommutator formula $\frac{1}{2}(\varphi \circ \psi + \psi \circ \varphi) = -(\varphi, \psi) \iota$ and the definition of the cross product $\varphi \times \psi = \frac{1}{2}(\varphi \circ \psi - \psi \circ \varphi)$. Adding these two equations:
$$
\frac{1}{2}(\varphi \circ \psi + \psi \circ \varphi) + \frac{1}{2}(\varphi \circ \psi - \psi \circ \varphi) = -(\varphi, \psi) \iota + \varphi \times \psi.
$$
The left-hand side simplifies to $\varphi \circ \psi$, yielding the decomposition
$$
\varphi \circ \psi = -(\varphi, \psi) \iota + \varphi \times \psi.
$$
