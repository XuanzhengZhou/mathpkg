The composition of two elements in $Q_1$ splits cleanly into a scalar (inner product) part and a bivector (cross product) part: $\varphi \circ \psi = -(\varphi, \psi) \iota + \varphi \times \psi$. This decomposition is the signature identity of the quaternion algebra, expressing the product of two pure imaginary quaternions as the negative of their Euclidean dot product plus their cross product. It establishes the precise sense in which $Q_1$ models the pure quaternions $\operatorname{Im}(\mathbb{H})$.
