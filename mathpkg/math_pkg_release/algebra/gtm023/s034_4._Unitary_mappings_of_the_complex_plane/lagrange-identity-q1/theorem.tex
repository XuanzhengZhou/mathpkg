For $\varphi, \psi \in Q_1$, the following Lagrange identity holds:
$$
|\varphi|^2 \cdot |\psi|^2 = (\varphi, \psi)^2 + |\varphi \times \psi|^2,
$$
where $|\varphi| = \sqrt{(\varphi, \varphi)}$ is the norm induced by the inner product on $Q$, and $\times$ is the cross product on $Q_1$. This identity mirrors the classical vector identity $|\mathbf{u}|^2 |\mathbf{v}|^2 = (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v})^2 + |\mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v}|^2$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$.
