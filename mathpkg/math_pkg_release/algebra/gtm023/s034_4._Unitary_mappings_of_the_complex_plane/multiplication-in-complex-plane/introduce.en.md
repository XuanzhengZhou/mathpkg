Using the isomorphism $z \mapsto \varphi_z$, a multiplication is defined on the complex plane $C$ by $z_1 z_2 = \varphi_{z_2} z_1$. This satisfies $\varphi_{z_1 z_2} = \varphi_{z_2} \circ \varphi_{z_1}$, so the map $z \mapsto \varphi_z$ is product-preserving. Under this multiplication, $C$ becomes a 4-dimensional real division algebra, providing an explicit realization of the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$ directly on the complex plane.
