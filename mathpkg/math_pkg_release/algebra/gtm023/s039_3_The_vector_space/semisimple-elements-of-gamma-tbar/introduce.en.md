The subalgebra $A$ appearing in the direct sum decomposition $\Gamma(\bar{t}) = A \oplus \operatorname{rad} \Gamma(\bar{t})$ has an intrinsic characterization: it is exactly the set of all semisimple elements of $\Gamma(\bar{t})$. This establishes that the semisimple elements form a subalgebra and that every element of $A$ is semisimple, while conversely every semisimple element belongs to $A$.
