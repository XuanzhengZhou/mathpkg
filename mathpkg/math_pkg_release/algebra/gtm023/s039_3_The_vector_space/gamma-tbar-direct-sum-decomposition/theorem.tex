Let $t$ be a linear transformation and $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ the associated algebra. Let $A$ be the subalgebra consisting of the images of polynomials in the semisimple part $\bar{t}_S$, and let $\operatorname{rad} \Gamma(\bar{t})$ be the radical of $\Gamma(\bar{t})$. Then $\Gamma(\bar{t})$ is the direct sum of the subalgebra $A$ and the ideal $\operatorname{rad} \Gamma(\bar{t})$:
$$
\Gamma(\bar{t}) = A \oplus \operatorname{rad} \Gamma(\bar{t}).
$$
