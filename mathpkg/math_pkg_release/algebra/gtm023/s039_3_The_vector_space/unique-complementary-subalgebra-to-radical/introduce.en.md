The subalgebra $A$ is the unique complement to the radical in $\Gamma(\bar{t})$. Any subalgebra $B$ that also complements the radical must be isomorphic to $A$ via the quotient map, and by the characterization of $A$ as the set of all semisimple elements, it follows that $B \subset A$, hence $B = A$. This uniqueness is a Wedderburn-Malcev type result in the context of the algebra associated with a linear transformation.
