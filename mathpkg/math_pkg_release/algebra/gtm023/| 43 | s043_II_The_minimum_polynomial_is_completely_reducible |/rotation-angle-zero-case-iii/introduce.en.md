A key step in the analysis of Case III is determining that the rotation angle $\omega$ appearing in the mapping equations on $E_1$ must be zero. This follows from computing the characteristic polynomial of the induced mapping $\varphi_1$ in two ways: directly from the matrix expression (yielding a factor $t^2 - 2t\cos\omega + 1$) and from the known minimum polynomial structure (yielding $(1-t)^3$). Equating the two forces $\cos\omega = 1$, hence $\omega = 0$, which dramatically simplifies the mapping to a shear-like (parabolic) form along the light-like direction.
