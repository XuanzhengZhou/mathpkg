Case III of the classification of proper orthochronous Lorentz transformations is characterized by a minimum polynomial of the form $\mu = (t-1)^k$ with $1 \leq k \leq 4$. This means all eigenvalues of $\varphi$ equal $1$, and the transformation is unipotent. The degree $k$ measures the nilpotency of $\varphi - \iota$. The boundary case $k = 1$ yields the identity transformation, while the intermediate case $k = 2$ is ruled out by structural considerations of the Minkowski inner product, leaving $k = 3$ or $k = 4$ as the only nontrivial possibilities.
