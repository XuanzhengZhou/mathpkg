This proposition (Section 9.14) constructs the first vector in the simultaneous diagonalization basis. By introducing an auxiliary positive definite inner product, the functional $F$ becomes continuous on the compact unit sphere and attains a minimum at some vector $e_1$. The first-order condition for this minimum yields the relation $\Phi(e_1)\Psi(e_1,y) = \Phi(e_1,y)\Psi(e_1)$ for all $y$, which—since $\Psi$ is non-degenerate and $\Psi(e_1) \neq 0$—implies $\Phi(e_1,y) = \lambda_1 \Psi(e_1,y)$. This is the starting point for the iterative construction.
