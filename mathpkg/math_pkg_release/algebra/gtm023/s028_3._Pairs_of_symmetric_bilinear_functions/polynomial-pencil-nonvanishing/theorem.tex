Let $E$ be a finite-dimensional real vector space, and let $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ be symmetric bilinear functions on $E$ with $\Psi \neq 0$. Fix an auxiliary positive definite inner product $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$ on $E$ and define linear transformations $\varphi, \psi: E \to E$ by
$$\Phi(x, y) = \langle \varphi x, y \rangle, \quad \Psi(x, y) = \langle \psi x, y \rangle.$$
Let $r = \operatorname{rank}(\psi) \geq 1$, and let $\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ be a basis of $E$ such that $\{x_{r+1}, \ldots, x_n\}$ is a basis of $\ker \psi$. Define the polynomial
$$f(\lambda) = \Delta(\varphi x_1 + \lambda \psi x_1, \ldots, \varphi x_n + \lambda \psi x_n),$$
where $\Delta$ is a non-zero determinant function on $E$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $f(\lambda)$ is a polynomial of degree $r$ whose leading coefficient is
$$\Delta(\psi x_1, \ldots, \psi x_r, \varphi x_{r+1}, \ldots, \varphi x_n) \neq 0.$$
\item There exists $\lambda_0 \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $f(\lambda_0) \neq 0$, and consequently the bilinear function $\Phi + \lambda_0 \Psi$ is non-degenerate.
\end{enumerate}
