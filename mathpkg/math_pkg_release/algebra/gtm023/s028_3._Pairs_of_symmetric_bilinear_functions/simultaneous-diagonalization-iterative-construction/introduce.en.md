This theorem (Section 9.15) describes the iterative construction that yields the simultaneous diagonalization basis. After finding the first vector $e_1$ via the minimization argument, one restricts to the $\Psi$-orthogonal complement $E_1$, where $\Psi$ remains non-degenerate. Applying the same construction to $E_1$ yields $e_2$, and repeating $n$ times produces a full basis satisfying $\Phi(e_v, y) = \lambda_v \Psi(e_v, y)$. After normalization by the signature of $\Psi$, both bilinear forms become diagonal with $\Psi$ in canonical form $\operatorname{diag}(+1,\ldots,+1,-1,\ldots,-1)$ and $\Phi = \operatorname{diag}(\lambda_1,\ldots,\lambda_n)$.
