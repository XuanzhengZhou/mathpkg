This exercise asks to show that for a 2-dimensional vector space $E$, the nilpotent linear transformations form a quadratic cone inside a 3-dimensional subspace $F$ of the 4-dimensional space $L(E;E)$ of all linear transformations. Specifically, one must construct a symmetric bilinear function $\Phi$ on $F$ such that $\tau \in F$ is nilpotent if and only if $\Phi(\tau, \tau) = 0$.
