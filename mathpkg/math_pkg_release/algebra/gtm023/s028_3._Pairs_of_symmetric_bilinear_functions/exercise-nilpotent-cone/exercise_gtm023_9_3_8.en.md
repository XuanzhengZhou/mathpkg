Let $E$ be a 2-dimensional vector space, and consider the 4-dimensional space $L(E; E)$. Prove that there exists a 3-dimensional subspace $F \subset L(E; E)$ and a symmetric bilinear function $\Phi$ in $F$ such that the nilpotent transformations (cf. problem 7, Chap. IV, § 6) are precisely the transformations $\tau$ satisfying $\Phi(\tau) = 0$. (In other words, the nilpotent transformations form a cone in $F$.)
