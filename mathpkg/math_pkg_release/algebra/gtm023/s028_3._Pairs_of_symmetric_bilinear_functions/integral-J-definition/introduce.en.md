The line integral $J$ is a technical device introduced to circumvent difficulties in defining a single-valued functional $F(x) = \arctan(\Phi(x)/\Psi(x))$ on the deleted vector space. By expressing the angle change of the complex-valued map $\omega(x) = \Phi(x) + i\Psi(x)$ as a line integral, one obtains a well-defined quantity $2J = \theta(1) - \theta(0)$ that is independent of the choice of angle function $\theta$. This integral plays a central role in the proof of the simultaneous diagonalization theorem for $n \geq 3$.
