When two symmetric bilinear functions $\Phi$ and $\Psi$ are given on a vector space and $\Psi$ is non-degenerate, there exists a linear transformation $\varphi$ such that $\Phi(x,y) = \Psi(\varphi x, y)$. This lemma shows that eigenvectors of $\varphi$ corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are necessarily orthogonal with respect to both bilinear forms. This is a fundamental observation that leads to the simultaneous diagonalization criterion.
