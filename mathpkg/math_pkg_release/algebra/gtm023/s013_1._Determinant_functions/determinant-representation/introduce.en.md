This proposition shows that a nonzero determinant function serves as a universal skew symmetric $n$-linear map: any other such map into any vector space factors uniquely through it. The vector $b$ is simply the value of $\varphi$ on a basis normalized so that $\Delta$ equals $1$ on that basis. This universal property is the foundation for the coordinate-free approach to determinants.
