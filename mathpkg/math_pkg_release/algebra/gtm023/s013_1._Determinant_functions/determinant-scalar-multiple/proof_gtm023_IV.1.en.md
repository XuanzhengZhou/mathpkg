Apply Proposition III with $F = \Gamma$. If $\Delta'$ is any determinant function, then viewing $\Delta'$ as a skew symmetric $n$-linear map into $\Gamma$, there exists a unique scalar $\lambda \in \Gamma$ such that $\Delta'(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = \Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \cdot \lambda$ for all $x_i \in E$. Thus $\Delta' = \lambda \Delta$.
