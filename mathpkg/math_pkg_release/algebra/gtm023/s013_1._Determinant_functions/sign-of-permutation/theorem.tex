Let $X = \mathbb{Z}$, $Y = \mathbb{Z}$ and define $\Phi: \mathbb{Z}^p \to \mathbb{Z}$ by
$$\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = \prod_{i < j} (x_i - x_j), \quad x_i \in \mathbb{Z}.$$
Then for every $\sigma \in S_p$, there exists $\varepsilon_\sigma = \pm 1$ such that
$$\sigma \Phi = \varepsilon_\sigma \cdot \Phi$$
where $(\sigma \Phi)(x_1, \ldots, x_p) = \Phi(x_{\sigma(1)}, \ldots, x_{\sigma(p)})$. The value $\varepsilon_\sigma$ is called the \text{sign} of the permutation $\sigma$.
