This formula follows from the general result in section 4.7 on the determinant of a linear transformation on a direct sum. Given $E = E_1 \oplus E_2$ and a transformation $\varphi$ whose matrix is block diagonal with respect to a basis adapted to the direct sum, the determinant of $\varphi$ equals the product $\det \varphi_1 \cdot \det \varphi_2$ where $\varphi_1$ and $\varphi_2$ are the induced transformations on $E_1$ and $E_2$ respectively. Translating to matrices via $M(\varphi) = A$, $M(\varphi_1) = A_1$, $M(\varphi_2) = A_2$ gives $\det A = \det A_1 \cdot \det A_2$.
