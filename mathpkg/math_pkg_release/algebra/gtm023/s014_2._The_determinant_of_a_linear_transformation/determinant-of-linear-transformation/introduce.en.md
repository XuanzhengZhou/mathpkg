The determinant of a linear transformation generalizes the familiar determinant of a matrix to the coordinate-free setting of an $n$-dimensional vector space. Given a non-trivial determinant function $\Delta$ on $E$, one forms $\Delta_\varphi(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = \Delta(\varphi x_1,\ldots,\varphi x_n)$, which is again a determinant function. The uniqueness theorem for determinant functions then guarantees a scalar $\alpha$—independent of the choice of $\Delta$—such that $\Delta_\varphi = \alpha \Delta$. This scalar is defined to be $\det \varphi$.
