Let $A = (\alpha_v^\mu)$ be an $n \times n$-matrix. The determinant of $A$ is defined by

$$\det A = \sum_{\sigma} \varepsilon_{\sigma} \alpha_1^{\sigma(1)} \alpha_2^{\sigma(2)} \cdots \alpha_n^{\sigma(n)},$$

where the sum runs over all permutations $\sigma$ of $\{1, \ldots, n\}$ and $\varepsilon_\sigma$ denotes the sign of $\sigma$.

If $\varphi$ is a linear transformation with matrix $M(\varphi) = A$ relative to a basis, then

$$\det \varphi = \det M(\varphi).$$
