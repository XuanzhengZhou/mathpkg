Proposition V establishes the fundamental identities governing the classical adjoint: composing the adjoint with the original transformation (in either order) yields a scalar multiple of the identity, the scalar being the determinant. These identities are the coordinate-free analogue of the matrix identity $A \cdot \operatorname{adj}(A) = \operatorname{adj}(A) \cdot A = (\det A) I$.
