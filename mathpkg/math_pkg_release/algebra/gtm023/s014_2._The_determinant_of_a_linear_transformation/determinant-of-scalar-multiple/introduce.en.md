As a direct consequence of the definition of the determinant of a linear transformation, the determinant of a scalar multiple of the identity map equals the scalar raised to the power $n$, where $n$ is the dimension of the vector space. This follows from the multilinearity of the underlying determinant function: each of the $n$ arguments contributes a factor of $\lambda$, yielding $\det(\lambda \iota) = \lambda^n$. Two important special cases are $\det \iota = 1$ and $\det 0 = 0$.
