The classical adjoint (also called the adjugate) of a linear transformation $\varphi$ on an $n$-dimensional vector space is defined using a non-trivial determinant function $\Delta$. One constructs an $n$-linear, skew-symmetric map $\Phi$ that, by the uniqueness of determinant functions, factors as $\Phi(x_1, \ldots, x_n) = \Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \operatorname{ad}(\varphi)$. The resulting linear transformation $\operatorname{ad}(\varphi)$ plays the role of the transpose of the cofactor matrix in the coordinate-free setting.
