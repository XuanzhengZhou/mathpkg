When $\det \varphi \neq 0$, the transformation $\varphi$ is regular, and the classical adjoint relations $\operatorname{ad}(\varphi) \circ \varphi = \varphi \circ \operatorname{ad}(\varphi) = (\det \varphi) \iota$ immediately yield an explicit formula for the inverse: $\varphi^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det \varphi} \operatorname{ad}(\varphi)$. This is the coordinate-free version of the matrix formula $A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det A} \operatorname{adj}(A)$.
