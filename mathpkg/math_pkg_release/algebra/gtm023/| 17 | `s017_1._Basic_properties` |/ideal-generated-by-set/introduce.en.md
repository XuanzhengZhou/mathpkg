The ideal generated by a subset $S$ of an algebra is the smallest ideal containing $S$, obtained as the intersection of all ideals that contain $S$. For associative algebras, this ideal has a concrete description as the linear span of elements of the forms $s$, $as$, and $sa$ where $s \in S$ and $a \in A$. A principal ideal is the ideal generated by a single element.
