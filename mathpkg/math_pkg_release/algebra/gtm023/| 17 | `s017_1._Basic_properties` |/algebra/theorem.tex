An algebra, $A$, is a vector space together with a mapping $A \times A \rightarrow A$ such that the conditions $(M_1)$ and $(M_2)$ below both hold. The image of two vectors $x \in A, y \in A$, under this mapping is called the product of $x$ and $y$ and will be denoted by $xy$.

The mapping $A \times A \rightarrow A$ is required to satisfy:

$$(M_1) \quad (\lambda x_1 + \mu x_2) y = \lambda(x_1 y) + \mu(x_2 y)$$

$$(M_2) \quad x(\lambda y_1 + \mu y_2) = \lambda(x y_1) + \mu(x y_2).$$

As an immediate consequence of the definition we have that

$$0 \cdot x = x \cdot 0 = 0.$$
