For an associative algebra $A$, the map $\mu: A \to A(A;A)$ sending each element $a$ to its multiplication operator $\mu(a)$ is an algebra homomorphism. This is a fundamental structural result: it embeds $A$ into the algebra of linear transformations on its underlying vector space, showing that every associative algebra is isomorphic to a subalgebra of some $A(E;E)$.
