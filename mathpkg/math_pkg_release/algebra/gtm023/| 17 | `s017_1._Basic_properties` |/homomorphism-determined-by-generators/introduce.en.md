For associative algebras, a homomorphism $\varphi: A \to B$ is uniquely determined by its values on a system of generators $S$ of $A$. This is because every element of $A$ can be expressed as a linear combination of products of generators, and $\varphi$ preserves both linear combinations and products. This property is analogous to the fact that a linear map is determined by its values on a basis.
