If

$$x = \sum_{(v)} \lambda^{v_1 \dots v_p} x_{v_1} \dots x_{v_p}, \quad x_{v_i} \in S, \; \lambda^{v_1 \dots v_p} \in \Gamma$$

then by the homomorphism property,

$$\varphi x = \sum_{(v)} \lambda^{v_1 \dots v_p} (\varphi x_{v_1}) \dots (\varphi x_{v_p}) = \sum_{(v)} \lambda^{v_1 \dots v_p} (\varphi_0 x_{v_1}) \dots (\varphi_0 x_{v_p}).$$

Thus $\varphi x$ is completely determined by the values $\varphi_0$ on $S$.
