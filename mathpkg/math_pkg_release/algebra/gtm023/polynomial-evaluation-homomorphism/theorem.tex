Let $A$ be an associative algebra with unit element $e$ and fix $a \in A$. Then the map $\Phi_a: \Gamma[t] \to A$ defined by
$$\Phi_a\left(\sum_{v=0}^n \alpha_v t^v\right) = \sum_{v=0}^n \alpha_v a^v$$
is a homomorphism of algebras, called the evaluation homomorphism at $a$. Its image $\Gamma(a) = \operatorname{Im} \Phi_a$ is a commutative subalgebra of $A$.

If $A$ has finite dimension, then $\ker \Phi_a \neq 0$. The unique monic polynomial $\mu$ of minimal degree in $\ker \Phi_a$ is called the minimum polynomial of $a$, and $\dim \Gamma(a) = \deg \mu$.
