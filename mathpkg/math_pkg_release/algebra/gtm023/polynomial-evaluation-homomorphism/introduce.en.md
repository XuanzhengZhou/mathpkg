The evaluation homomorphism sends a polynomial $f(t)$ to $f(a)$ in an algebra $A$. Its image $\Gamma(a)$ is the subalgebra generated by $a$. When $A$ is finite-dimensional, the kernel is non-trivial and the minimum polynomial is the monic generator of this kernel ideal. This connects polynomial algebra to the structure theory of linear transformations.
