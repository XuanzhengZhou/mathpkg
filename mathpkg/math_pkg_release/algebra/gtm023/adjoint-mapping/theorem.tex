Let $E$ and $F$ be inner product spaces and $\varphi: E \to F$ a linear mapping. The adjoint mapping $\tilde{\varphi}: F \to E$ is defined by
$$(\varphi x, y) = (x, \tilde{\varphi} y) \quad x \in E, y \in F.$$

Properties:
(i) $\tilde{\tilde{\varphi}} = \varphi$
(ii) $\overbrace{\psi \circ \varphi} = \tilde{\varphi} \circ \tilde{\psi}$
(iii) $\det \tilde{\varphi} = \det \varphi$, $\operatorname{tr} \tilde{\varphi} = \operatorname{tr} \varphi$
(iv) $F = \operatorname{Im} \varphi \oplus \ker \tilde{\varphi}$ (orthogonal decomposition)

Relative to orthonormal bases, the matrix of $\tilde{\varphi}$ is the transpose of the matrix of $\varphi$.
