Negative definite and negative semidefinite bilinear functions are the natural counterparts to positive definite and positive semidefinite forms. A negative definite form assigns strictly negative values to every nonzero vector paired with itself. Just as positive definiteness characterizes Euclidean inner products, negative definiteness arises when the bilinear form is restricted to the "negative energy" subspace in indefinite metric theories.
