Let $\Phi$ be a symmetric bilinear function on a finite-dimensional real vector space $E$. Suppose that in some basis the quadratic form $\Phi(x) = \Phi(x, x)$ has the diagonal representation

$$\Phi(x) = \sum_{v=1}^n \lambda_v (\xi^v)^2$$

with $\lambda_v > 0$ for $v = 1, \ldots, p$ and $\lambda_v \leq 0$ for $v = p+1, \ldots, n$. Then $p$ is the index of $\Phi$. Consequently, the number of positive coefficients $p$ is the same for every diagonal representation of $\Phi$.
