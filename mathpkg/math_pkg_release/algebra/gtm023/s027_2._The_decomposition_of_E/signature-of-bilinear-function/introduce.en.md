The signature of a symmetric bilinear function is the difference between the dimensions of its maximal positive definite and negative definite subspaces. Expressed as $2s - r$ where $s$ is the index and $r$ is the rank, the signature captures the net "sign" of the form. Together with the rank, it provides a complete invariant for real symmetric bilinear forms (Sylvester's law of inertia).
