Let $\Phi$ be a non-degenerate indefinite symmetric bilinear function in an $n$-dimensional vector space $E$. Then $E$ can be decomposed into a direct sum of two subspaces

$$E = E^+ \oplus E^-$$

such that $\Phi$ is positive definite in $E^+$ and negative definite in $E^-$. Moreover, $E^-$ is the orthogonal complement of $E^+$ with respect to the scalar product defined by $\Phi$, and $E^+$ is a subspace of maximal dimension on which $\Phi$ is positive definite.
