Let $\Phi$ be a degenerate symmetric bilinear function in $E$ with nullspace $E_0$. Choose a subspace $E_1$ complementary to $E_0$:

$$E = E_0 \oplus E_1.$$

Then $\Phi$ is non-degenerate in $E_1$. Consequently, applying the decomposition theorem to $E_1$ yields

$$E = E_1^+ \oplus E_1^- \oplus E_0$$

where $\Phi$ is positive definite on $E_1^+$, negative definite on $E_1^-$, and vanishes on $E_0$. In a suitable basis $(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, the matrix of $\Phi$ has diagonal form

$$\Phi(x_v, x_\mu) = \varepsilon_v \delta_{v\mu}, \quad \varepsilon_v = \begin{cases} +1 & (v = 1 \ldots s) \\ -1 & (v = s+1 \ldots r) \\ 0 & (v = r+1 \ldots n). \end{cases}$$
