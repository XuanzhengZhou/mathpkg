Two bases $x_v$ and $x^{*v}$ ($v = 1, \ldots, n$) of $E$ and $E^*$ respectively are called dual if

$$\langle x^{*v}, x_\mu \rangle = \delta_\mu^v.$$

Given a basis $x_v$ ($v = 1, \ldots, n$) of $E$, there exists precisely one dual basis $x^{*v}$ of $E^*$. For any vector $x = \sum_v \xi^v x_v$ and covector $x^* = \sum_v \xi_v x^{*v}$, we have

$$\langle x^*, x \rangle = \sum_v \xi_v \xi^v$$

and the components are recovered via

$$\xi^\lambda = \langle x^{*\lambda}, x \rangle.$$
