Consider the vector space $E$ of sequences

$$(\lambda_0, \lambda_1, \dots), \quad \lambda_i \in \Gamma$$

and the subspace $F$ consisting of those sequences for which only finitely many $\lambda_i$ are different from zero (addition and scalar multiplication being defined as in the case of $\Gamma^n$). Show that the mapping $E \times F \rightarrow \Gamma$ given by

$$(\lambda_0, \lambda_1, \dots), (\mu_0, \mu_1, \dots) \mapsto \sum_i \lambda_i \mu_i$$

defines a scalar product in $E$ and $F$. Show further that the induced injection $E \rightarrow L(F)$ is surjective.
