Since $E^*$ is canonically isomorphic to $L(E)$ in finite dimensions, there exists a dual mapping $\varphi^*: E^* \leftarrow F^*$. From the relations established in section 2.28 we have

$$\operatorname{Im} \varphi = (\ker \varphi^*)^\perp \quad \text{and} \quad \operatorname{Im} \varphi^* = (\ker \varphi)^\perp.$$

From the second relation we obtain

$$\dim \operatorname{Im} \varphi^* = \dim (\ker \varphi)^\perp = \dim E - \dim \ker \varphi = \dim \operatorname{Im} \varphi,$$

whence $r(\varphi^*) = r(\varphi)$.
