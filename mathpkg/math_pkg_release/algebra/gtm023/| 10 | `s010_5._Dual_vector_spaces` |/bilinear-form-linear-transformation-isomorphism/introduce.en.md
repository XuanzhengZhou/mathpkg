In finite dimensions, every bilinear form on $E^* \times E$ corresponds to a unique linear transformation on $E$. The correspondence is given by $\Phi(x^*, x) = \langle x^*, \varphi x \rangle$, which establishes a canonical isomorphism $B(E^*, E) \cong L(E; E)$. This result provides the algebraic foundation for identifying bilinear forms with linear operators, a crucial step in the study of quadratic forms and spectral theory.
