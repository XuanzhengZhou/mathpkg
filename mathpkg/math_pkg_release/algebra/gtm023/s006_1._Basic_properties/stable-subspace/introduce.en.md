A stable subspace (also called an invariant subspace) of a linear transformation $\varphi: E \to E$ is a subspace $E_1$ such that $\varphi$ maps $E_1$ into itself. The kernel $\ker \varphi$ and image $\operatorname{Im} \varphi$ are always stable subspaces. When a subspace is stable, $\varphi$ restricts to a linear transformation on the subspace and also induces a well-defined linear transformation on the quotient space.
