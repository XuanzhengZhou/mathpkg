An exact sequence is a sequence of vector spaces and linear mappings where the image of each map coincides with the kernel of the next. This concept provides a unified language for describing injectivity, surjectivity, and isomorphism properties of linear mappings. In particular, a sequence $0 \to E_1 \to E \to E_2 \to 0$ being exact means the map $E_1 \to E$ is injective, $E \to E_2$ is surjective, and the kernel of the second equals the image of the first.
