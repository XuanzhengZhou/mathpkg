The Second Isomorphism Theorem for vector spaces provides a natural isomorphism between the quotient of $E_1$ by its intersection with $E_2$ and the quotient of the sum $E_1 + E_2$ by $E_2$. This is often called the "diamond isomorphism theorem" due to the lattice structure of subspaces. It generalizes the observation that when $E$ is a direct sum $E_1 \oplus E_2$, we have $E_1 \cong E/E_2$.
