The set $GL(E)$ of all invertible linear transformations of a vector space $E$ forms a group under composition, called the general linear group of $E$. This algebraic structure is fundamental: it encapsulates the symmetries of the vector space. The associative law holds for composition of arbitrary functions, and the identity map serves as the group identity.
