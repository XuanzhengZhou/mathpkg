A vector space over a field is the central algebraic structure of linear algebra. It combines an abelian group structure (vector addition) with scalar multiplication by field elements, satisfying natural compatibility axioms. Vector spaces generalize $\mathbb{R}^n$ and $\mathbb{C}^n$ and abstract the essential properties needed to study linear combinations, linear independence, and linear transformations.
