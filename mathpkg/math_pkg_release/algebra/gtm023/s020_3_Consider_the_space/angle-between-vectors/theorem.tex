Let $E$ be a real inner product space with inner product $(\cdot, \cdot)$ and associated norm $|x| = \sqrt{(x, x)}$. Given two vectors $x \neq 0$ and $y \neq 0$, the Schwarz inequality implies

$$-1 \leq \frac{(x, y)}{|x| |y|} \leq 1.$$

Consequently, there exists exactly one real number $\omega$ with $0 \leq \omega \leq \pi$ such that

$$\cos \omega = \frac{(x, y)}{|x| |y|}.$$

The number $\omega$ is called the \textbf{angle} between the vectors $x$ and $y$. The angle is symmetric with respect to $x$ and $y$. If $x$ and $y$ are orthogonal, $\cos \omega = 0$ and $\omega = \pi/2$. If $x$ and $y$ are linearly dependent with $y = \lambda x$, then

$$\omega = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } \lambda > 0, \\ \pi & \text{if } \lambda < 0. \end{cases}$$
