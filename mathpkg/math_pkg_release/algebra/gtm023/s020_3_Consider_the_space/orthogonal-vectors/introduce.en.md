Orthogonality is the fundamental relation in inner product spaces that generalizes the notion of perpendicularity from Euclidean geometry. Two vectors are orthogonal if their inner product vanishes. By the definiteness axiom of inner products, the zero vector is the only vector orthogonal to itself, which ensures that orthogonality is a meaningful and non-degenerate relation.
