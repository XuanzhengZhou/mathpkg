The Ptolemy inequality is a generalization of the triangle inequality in inner product spaces. It relates the six pairwise distances among three vectors and the origin. The proof uses a clever inversion trick: defining normalized vectors $x' = x/|x|^2$, $y' = y/|y|^2$, $z' = z/|z|^2$ and applying the triangle inequality to these inverted vectors yields the Ptolemy inequality. This result is named after Claudius Ptolemy and connects to the classical geometric inequality for cyclic quadrilaterals in Euclidean geometry.
