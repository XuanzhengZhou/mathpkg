Let $E$ be an inner product space of dimension $n$ and let $L(E)$ be the space of linear functions on $E$. Then there exists a linear isomorphism

$$a \colon E \xrightarrow{\cong} L(E)$$

defined by $a(x)(y) = (x, y)$ for all $x, y \in E$. That is, every linear functional on $E$ can be uniquely represented as an inner product with a fixed vector.
