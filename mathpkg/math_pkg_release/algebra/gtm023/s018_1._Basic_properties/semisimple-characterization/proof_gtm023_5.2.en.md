If $A$ is totally reducible and $\operatorname{rad} A = 0$, then by the structure theorem $A = B$ where $B$ is semisimple. Conversely, if $A$ is semisimple, then $\operatorname{rad} A = 0$ by definition and $A$ is totally reducible since semisimple algebras are direct sums of simple ideals.
