When a vector space over a field $\Gamma$ is restricted to a subfield $\Delta$, its dimension multiplies by the degree of the field extension $[\Gamma:\Delta]$. For example, an $n$-dimensional complex vector space becomes a $2n$-dimensional real vector space since $[\mathbb{C}:\mathbb{R}] = 2$.
