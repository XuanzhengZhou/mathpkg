This follows directly from the definition: $\operatorname{rad} A$ consists of all nilpotent elements. Any element of $\operatorname{rad}(\operatorname{rad} A)$ is nilpotent in $\operatorname{rad} A$, hence nilpotent in $A$, so it belongs to $\operatorname{rad} A$. Conversely, every element of $\operatorname{rad} A$ is nilpotent and lies in the ideal $\operatorname{rad} A$, so $\operatorname{rad} A \subset \operatorname{rad}(\operatorname{rad} A)$.
