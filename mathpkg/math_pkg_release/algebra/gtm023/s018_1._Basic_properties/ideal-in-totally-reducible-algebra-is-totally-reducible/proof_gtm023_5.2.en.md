Let $I'$ be a complementary ideal to $I$ in $A$, so $A = I \oplus I'$. Then $I \cdot I' \subset I \cap I' = 0$. If $J$ is an ideal in $I$, then $J \cdot I \subset J$ and $J \cdot I' \subset I \cdot I' = 0$, whence $J \cdot A \subset J$. Thus $J$ is also an ideal in $A$. Since $A$ is totally reducible, $J$ has a complementary ideal in $A$, and intersecting with $I$ yields a complementary ideal to $J$ in $I$.
