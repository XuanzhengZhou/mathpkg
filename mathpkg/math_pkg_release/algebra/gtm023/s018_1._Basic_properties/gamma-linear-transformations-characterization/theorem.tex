Let $E$ be a vector space over a field $\Gamma$ with subfield $\Delta$, and let $\varepsilon_{\alpha}: E \to E$ denote scalar multiplication by $\alpha \in \Gamma$. Then a $\Delta$-linear transformation $\varphi \in A_{\Delta}(E; E)$ is $\Gamma$-linear if and only if
$$
\varphi \circ \varepsilon_{\alpha} = \varepsilon_{\alpha} \circ \varphi \qquad \text{for all } \alpha \in \Gamma.
$$
