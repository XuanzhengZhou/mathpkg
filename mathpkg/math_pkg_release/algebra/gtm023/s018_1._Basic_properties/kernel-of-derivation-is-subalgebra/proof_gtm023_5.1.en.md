For any two elements $x, y \in \ker \theta$, we have $\theta x = 0$ and $\theta y = 0$. Then by the derivation property,

$$
\theta(xy) = \theta x \cdot y + x \cdot \theta y = 0 \cdot y + x \cdot 0 = 0,
$$

whence $xy \in \ker \theta$. Thus $\ker \theta$ is closed under multiplication and is therefore a subalgebra of $A$.
