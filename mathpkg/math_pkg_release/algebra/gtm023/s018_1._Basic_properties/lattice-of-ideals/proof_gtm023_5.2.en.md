The relation $\leq$ defined by inclusion is clearly a partial order. If $I_1$ and $I_2$ are ideals, then $I_1 + I_2$ and $I_1 \cap I_2$ are again ideals. One verifies that $I_1 + I_2$ is the least upper bound (it contains both and is contained in any ideal containing both) and $I_1 \cap I_2$ is the greatest lower bound. Hence $(\mathcal{I}, \leq)$ is a lattice.
