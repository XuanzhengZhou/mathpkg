Let $\omega$ be a fixed involution in an algebra $A$. A linear transformation $\Omega: A \to A$ is called an \textbf{antiderivation} with respect to $\omega$ if it satisfies
$$
\Omega(xy) = \Omega x \cdot y + \omega x \cdot \Omega y.
$$
In particular, a derivation is an antiderivation with respect to the identity involution $\iota$.
