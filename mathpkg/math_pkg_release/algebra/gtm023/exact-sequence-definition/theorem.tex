A sequence of linear mappings
$$F \xrightarrow{\varphi} E \xrightarrow{\psi} G$$
is called exact at $E$ if
$$\operatorname{Im} \varphi = \ker \psi.$$

Special cases:
1. $F = 0$: $0 \to E \xrightarrow{\psi} G$ is exact at $E$ iff $\psi$ is injective.
2. $G = 0$: $F \xrightarrow{\varphi} E \to 0$ is exact at $E$ iff $\varphi$ is surjective.

A short exact sequence is a sequence
$$0 \to F \xrightarrow{\varphi} E \xrightarrow{\psi} G \to 0$$
which is exact at $F$, $E$, and $G$. As an example,
$$0 \to E_1 \xrightarrow{i} E \xrightarrow{\pi} E/E_1 \to 0$$
where $i$ is the canonical injection and $\pi$ the canonical projection.
