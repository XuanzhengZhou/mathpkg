Let $G$ and $H$ be two groups. A mapping
$$\varphi: G \to H$$
is called a homomorphism if
$$\varphi(xy) = \varphi x \cdot \varphi y \quad x, y \in G.$$

A homomorphism which is injective (resp. surjective, bijective) is called a monomorphism (resp. epimorphism, isomorphism). The inverse mapping of an isomorphism is again an isomorphism.
