A group homomorphism is a structure-preserving map between two groups. It preserves the group operation, meaning the image of a product equals the product of the images. Special types include monomorphisms (injective), epimorphisms (surjective), and isomorphisms (bijective). The inverse of an isomorphism is also an isomorphism, making isomorphic groups structurally identical.
