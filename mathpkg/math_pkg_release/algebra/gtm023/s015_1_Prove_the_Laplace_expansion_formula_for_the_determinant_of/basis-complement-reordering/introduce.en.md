Given two bases of an $n$-dimensional vector space and an integer $p$ between $1$ and $n-1$, one can permute the second basis so that the first $p$ vectors of the first basis together with the last $n-p$ vectors of the (permuted) second basis form a basis, and symmetrically the first $p$ vectors of the permuted second basis together with the last $n-p$ vectors of the first basis also form a basis. This lemma is closely related to the concept of complementary subspaces and the Laplace expansion, where it ensures that certain pairs of minors correspond to non-singular submatrices.
