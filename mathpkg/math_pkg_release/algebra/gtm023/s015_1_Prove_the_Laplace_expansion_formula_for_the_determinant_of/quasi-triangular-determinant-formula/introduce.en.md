If an $n \times n$ matrix has a $p \times (n-p)$ zero block in its upper right corner, the determinant factors as the product of the determinants of the $p \times p$ upper-left block and the $(n-p) \times (n-p)$ lower-right block. This generalizes the familiar property of upper triangular matrices (where the determinant is the product of diagonal entries) to block form, and follows directly from the Laplace expansion. The formula is a special case of the multiplicative property for block triangular matrices, which plays an important role in computing determinants of matrices with zero sub-blocks and in the theory of Schur complements.
