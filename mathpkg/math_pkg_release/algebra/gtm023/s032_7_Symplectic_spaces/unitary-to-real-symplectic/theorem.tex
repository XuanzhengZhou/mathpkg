Let $E$ be an $n$-dimensional unitary space and let $E_{\mathbb{R}}$ be its underlying real vector space. Then a positive definite symmetric inner product and a symplectic inner product are defined in $E_{\mathbb{R}}$ by
$$(x, y) = \operatorname{Re}(x, y)$$
$$\langle x, y \rangle = \operatorname{Im}(x, y)$$
for $x, y \in E_{\mathbb{R}}$. These satisfy the relations
$$\langle x, y \rangle = -(ix, y)$$
$$\langle ix, y \rangle = (x, y).$$
Consequently, the transformation $x \mapsto ix$ is both symplectic and skew symplectic.
