This proposition gives an explicit and remarkably simple formula for the symplectic adjoint of a linear transformation in the special case of a 2-dimensional symplectic space. The formula $\tilde{\varphi} = i \cdot \operatorname{tr} \varphi - \varphi$ expresses the adjoint in terms of the trace and the transformation itself, multiplied by the imaginary unit $i$ when working over the complex numbers (or more generally using the complex structure compatible with the symplectic form). In 2 dimensions, the symplectic group $\mathrm{Sp}(2)$ coincides with $\mathrm{SL}(2)$, and this identity reflects the special low-dimensional isomorphism between these groups.
