This theorem establishes the fundamental necessary and sufficient condition for the existence of a symplectic structure on a finite-dimensional vector space: the dimension must be even. The forward direction follows from the fact that a non-degenerate skew-symmetric bilinear form can only exist on an even-dimensional space, as the matrix of such a form is skew-symmetric and non-degenerate skew-symmetric matrices have even rank. The converse is demonstrated by explicit construction of a symplectic form on any even-dimensional space, for instance via the standard symplectic form on $\Gamma^{2n}$.
