The dual (or transpose) of a linear mapping $\varphi: E \to F$ with respect to dual pairs $E, E^*$ and $F, F^*$ is a mapping $\varphi^*: F^* \to E^*$ satisfying the adjoint relation. For $L(E)$ (the space of linear functions), the dual mapping always exists and $\varphi^* f = f \circ \varphi$. The dual is a contravariant functor: $(\psi \circ \varphi)^* = \varphi^* \circ \psi^*$.
