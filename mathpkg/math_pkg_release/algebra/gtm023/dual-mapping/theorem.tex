Let $E, E^*$ and $F, F^*$ be two pairs of dual vector spaces and let $\varphi: E \to F$ be a linear mapping. A linear mapping $\varphi^*: E^* \leftarrow F^*$ is called dual to $\varphi$ if
$$\langle \varphi^* y^*, x \rangle = \langle y^*, \varphi x \rangle \quad x \in E, y^* \in F^*.$$

If such a $\varphi^*$ exists, it is uniquely determined by $\varphi$.

For the identity map, $\iota_{E^*} = (\iota_E)^*$. If $\varphi$ and $\varphi_1$ are inverse linear isomorphisms, then so are $\varphi^*$ and $\varphi_1^*$.
