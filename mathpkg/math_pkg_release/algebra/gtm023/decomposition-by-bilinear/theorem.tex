Let $\Phi$ be a non-degenerate indefinite symmetric bilinear function on an $n$-dimensional space $E$. Then $E$ decomposes as
$$E = E^+ \oplus E^-$$
where $\Phi$ is positive definite on $E^+$ and negative definite on $E^-$. Moreover, $E^-$ is the orthogonal complement of $E^+$ with respect to $\Phi$.

In the degenerate case, let $E_0$ be the nullspace and $E_1$ a complement: $E = E_0 \oplus E_1$. Then $\Phi$ is non-degenerate on $E_1$, so the decomposition applies there.
