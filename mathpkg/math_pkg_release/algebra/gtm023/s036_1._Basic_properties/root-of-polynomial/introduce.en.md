A root of a polynomial $f$ is an element $\lambda$ of the field such that evaluating $f$ at $\lambda$ yields $0$. The factor theorem states that $\lambda$ is a root of $f$ if and only if the linear polynomial $t - \lambda$ divides $f$. This is the fundamental connection between roots and linear factors, and it implies that a polynomial of degree $n$ has at most $n$ roots.
