The Euclidean algorithm for polynomials states that for any non-zero polynomials $f$ and $g$, there exist quotient $q$ and remainder $r$ such that $f = gq + r$ with $\deg r < \deg g$ or $r = 0$. This is the polynomial analogue of integer division with remainder and is the foundation for the theory of divisibility and principal ideals in $\Gamma[t]$.
