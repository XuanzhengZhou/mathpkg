Let $A$ be a finite-dimensional associative algebra with unit $e$, and let $a \in A$. The kernel $K$ of the evaluation homomorphism $\Phi: \Gamma[t] \to A$ ($\Phi(f) = f(a)$) is a non-zero ideal in $\Gamma[t]$. By Proposition I (sec. 12.5), there is a unique monic polynomial $\mu$ such that $K = I_{\mu}$. $\mu$ is called the minimum polynomial of $a$. If $A \neq 0$, $\mu$ must have positive degree.
