The kernel of the differentiation map $d$ consists precisely of the constant polynomials, i.e., the field $\Gamma$ itself. Consequently, two polynomials have the same derivative if and only if they differ by a constant. This is the formal algebraic analogue of the familiar calculus fact that antiderivatives are unique up to an additive constant.
