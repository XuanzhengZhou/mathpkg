The $r$-th derivative $f^{(r)}$ is defined by iterating the differentiation map $r$ times. A polynomial $f$ satisfies $f^{(r)} = 0$ precisely when $r > \deg f$. This characterizes polynomials of bounded degree as those annihilated by a sufficiently high power of the differentiation operator.
