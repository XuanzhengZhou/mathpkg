Over a field of characteristic zero (or any infinite field), the map sending a formal polynomial to its associated polynomial function is injective. This means distinct polynomials induce distinct functions. Over finite fields this fails: for example, $t^p$ and $t$ induce the same function on $\mathbb{F}_p$. The proof uses the fact that a non-zero polynomial can have at most finitely many roots.
