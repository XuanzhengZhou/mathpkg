By the first isomorphism theorem for algebras, $\Phi$ factors as $\Gamma[t] \twoheadrightarrow \Gamma[t]/\ker\Phi \xrightarrow{\bar{\Phi}} A$ where $\bar{\Phi}$ is injective (a monomorphism). If $A$ is generated by $e$ and $a$, then every element of $A$ is a polynomial in $a$, i.e., lies in the image of $\Phi$. Thus $\Phi$ is surjective, and consequently $\bar{\Phi}$ is both injective and surjective, hence an isomorphism.
