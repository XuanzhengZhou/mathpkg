Consider the mapping $\Gamma[t] \times \Gamma[t] \rightarrow \Gamma[t]$ defined by $(f, g) \mapsto f(g)$, i.e., composition of polynomials.

c) Show that the mapping is not commutative.
d) Prove that the mapping obeys the left cancellation law but not the right cancellation law; i.e., $f_1(g) = f_2(g)$ implies that $f_1 = f_2$, but $f(g_1) = f(g_2)$ does not imply that $g_1 = g_2$.
