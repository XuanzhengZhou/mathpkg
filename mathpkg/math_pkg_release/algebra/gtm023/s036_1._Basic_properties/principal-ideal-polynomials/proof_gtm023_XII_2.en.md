Let $I$ be a non-zero ideal in $\Gamma[t]$. Choose a non-zero polynomial $h \in I$ of minimal degree, and make it monic by scaling. Let $I_h = (h)$ be the principal ideal generated by $h$. Clearly $I_h \subset I$. For the reverse inclusion, let $f \in I$. By the Euclidean algorithm (Lemma I), there exist $q, r$ such that $f = hq + r$ with $\deg r < \deg h$ or $r = 0$. Since $f \in I$ and $h \in I$, we have $r = f - hq \in I$. If $r \neq 0$, then $\deg r < \deg h$ contradicts the minimality of $\deg h$. Hence $r = 0$ and $f = hq \in I_h$. Thus $I = I_h$. The monic generator $h$ is unique: if $I = I_k$ for another monic $k$, then $k = g_1 h$ and $h = g_2 k$ for some monic $g_1, g_2$, so $k = g_1 g_2 k$ implies $g_1 g_2 = 1$, and since both are monic, $g_1 = g_2 = 1$ and $k = h$.
