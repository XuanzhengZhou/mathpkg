This lemma characterizes how powers of an irreducible polynomial divide a product. It states that if $h^m$ divides $fg$, then the power $h^m$ splits between $f$ and $g$ as $h^p \mid f$ and $h^{m-p} \mid g$. This property of irreducible polynomials in $\Gamma[t]$ is analogous to the property of prime numbers in $\mathbb{Z}$ and is the key to proving uniqueness of prime factorization.
