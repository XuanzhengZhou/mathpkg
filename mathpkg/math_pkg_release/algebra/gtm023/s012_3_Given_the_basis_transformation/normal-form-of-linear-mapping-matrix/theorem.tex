Consider a linear mapping $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$. Then there exists a basis $a_v\ (v = 1, \dots, n)$ of $E$ and a basis $b_\mu\ (\mu = 1, \dots, m)$ of $F$ such that the corresponding matrix of $\varphi$ has the following normal form:
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
I_r & 0 \\
0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$
where $r$ is the rank of $\varphi$, and $I_r$ is the $r \times r$ identity matrix.
