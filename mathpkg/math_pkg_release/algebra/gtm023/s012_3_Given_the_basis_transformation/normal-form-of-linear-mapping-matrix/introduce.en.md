Given a linear mapping between two finite-dimensional vector spaces, one can always choose bases in the domain and codomain so that the matrix of the mapping takes a particularly simple block form: an identity block of size equal to the rank, surrounded by zeros. This is achieved by taking a basis of the kernel and extending a basis of the image. The normal form provides a canonical matrix representation that depends only on the rank of the mapping.
