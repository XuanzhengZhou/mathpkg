When the bases of the domain or codomain of a linear map are altered by elementary operations (swapping vectors or adding a multiple of one vector to another), the matrix of the map undergoes corresponding elementary row or column operations. Changes to the domain basis affect the rows, while changes to the codomain basis affect the columns. These four elementary operations form the foundation for reducing any matrix to its normal form.
