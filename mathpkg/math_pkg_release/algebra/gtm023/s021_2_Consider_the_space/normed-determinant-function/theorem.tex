Let \(E\) be an \(n\)-dimensional inner product space. A determinant function \(\Delta\) on \(E\) is called a **normed determinant function** if for all \(x_1, \ldots, x_n, y_1, \ldots, y_n \in E\),

$$\Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \, \Delta(y_1, \ldots, y_n) = \det \big( (x_i, y_j) \big).$$

In every inner product space there are precisely two normed determinant functions, \(\Delta\) and \(-\Delta\). If an orientation is prescribed, exactly one of them represents the orientation.
