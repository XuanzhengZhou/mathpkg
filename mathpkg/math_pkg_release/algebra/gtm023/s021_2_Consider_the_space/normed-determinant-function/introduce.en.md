A normed determinant function on an \(n\)-dimensional inner product space is a determinant function \(\Delta\) that satisfies the fundamental identity \(\Delta(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \Delta(y_1, \ldots, y_n) = \det((x_i, y_j))\), connecting the determinant to the inner product. In any inner product space there exist exactly two such functions, differing by a sign. When the space is oriented, precisely one of the two represents the given orientation, providing a canonical choice. The normed determinant function is essential for defining oriented angles and the cross product.
