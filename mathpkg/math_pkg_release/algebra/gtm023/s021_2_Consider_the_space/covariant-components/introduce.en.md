In an inner product space, the canonical isomorphism \(\tau : E \to E^*\) allows the definition of covariant components \(\xi_{\lambda}\) of a vector alongside its usual contravariant components \(\xi^{\lambda}\). The two are related by the metric tensor: \(\xi_{\lambda} = \sum_{\nu} g_{\lambda\nu} \xi^{\nu}\), where \(g_{\lambda\nu} = (e_{\lambda}, e_{\nu})\) is the Gram matrix of the basis. Covariant components have the simple interpretation \(\xi_{\nu} = (x, e_{\nu})\) — they are the inner products of \(x\) with the basis vectors. When the basis is orthonormal, the distinction between covariant and contravariant components disappears.
