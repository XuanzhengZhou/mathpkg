In an inner product space, every finite-dimensional subspace \(E_1\) admits an orthogonal complement \(E_1^{\perp}\) such that the whole space decomposes as the direct sum \(E = E_1 \oplus E_1^{\perp}\). Given an orthonormal basis of \(E_1\), the orthogonal projection of any vector \(x\) onto \(E_1\) is \(p = \sum_{\mu} (x, y_{\mu}) y_{\mu}\), and the remainder \(h = x - p\) lies in \(E_1^{\perp}\). This decomposition yields the Pythagorean relation \(|x|^2 = |p|^2 + |h|^2\), and the distance from \(x\) to the subspace \(E_1\) is \(|h|\).
