Let \(E\) be an inner product space and let \(E_1\) be a finite-dimensional subspace of \(E\). Then

$$E = E_1 \oplus E_1^{\perp}.$$

Selecting an orthonormal basis \(y_{\mu}\) (\(\mu = 1, \ldots, m\)) of \(E_1\), every vector \(x \in E\) decomposes uniquely as

$$x = p + h$$

where the orthogonal projection \(p\) of \(x\) onto \(E_1\) is given by

$$p = \sum_{\mu} (x, y_{\mu}) y_{\mu}$$

and \(h = x - p \in E_1^{\perp}\). The norm satisfies

$$|x|^2 = |p|^2 + |h|^2.$$
