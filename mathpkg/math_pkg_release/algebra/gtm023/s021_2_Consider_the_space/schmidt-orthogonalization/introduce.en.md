The Schmidt orthogonalization (Gram-Schmidt process) is a constructive algorithm that transforms any basis of a finite-dimensional inner product space into an orthonormal basis. Starting from a given basis \(a_1, \ldots, a_n\), one iteratively constructs mutually orthogonal vectors \(b_k\) by subtracting from \(a_k\) its orthogonal projections onto the previously constructed vectors \(b_1, \ldots, b_{k-1}\). Normalizing each \(b_k\) yields the orthonormal basis \(e_k = b_k / |b_k|\). This theorem guarantees that orthonormal bases always exist in finite-dimensional inner product spaces, making them a fundamental tool in linear algebra.
