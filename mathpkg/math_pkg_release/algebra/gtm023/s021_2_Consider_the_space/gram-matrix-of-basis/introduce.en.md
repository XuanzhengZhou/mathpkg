The Gram matrix of a basis in an \(n\)-dimensional inner product space \(E\) is the symmetric matrix \(g_{\nu\mu} = (x_{\nu}, x_{\mu})\) formed by taking inner products of all pairs of basis vectors. This matrix encodes the entire inner product structure: for any two vectors expressed in coordinates relative to the basis, their inner product is the bilinear form \((x, y) = \sum g_{\nu\mu} \xi^{\nu} \eta^{\mu}\). When the basis is orthonormal, the Gram matrix reduces to the identity matrix, giving the simple Euclidean formula \((x, y) = \sum \xi^{\nu} \eta^{\nu}\).
