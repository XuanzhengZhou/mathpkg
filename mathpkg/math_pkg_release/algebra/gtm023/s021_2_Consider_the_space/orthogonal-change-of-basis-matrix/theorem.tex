Let \(E\) be an inner product space and let \(x_{\nu}\) and \(\bar{x}_{\nu}\) (\(\nu = 1, \ldots, n\)) be two orthonormal bases of \(E\). Denote by \(\alpha_{\nu}^{\mu}\) the matrix of the basis transformation \(x_{\nu} \to \bar{x}_{\nu}\):

$$\bar{x}_{\nu} = \sum_{\mu} \alpha_{\nu}^{\mu} x_{\mu}.$$

Then the matrix \((\alpha_{\nu}^{\mu})\) is orthogonal, i.e.,

$$\sum_{\lambda} \alpha_{\nu}^{\lambda} \alpha_{\mu}^{\lambda} = \delta_{\nu\mu}.$$

Equivalently, the transpose of the matrix coincides with its inverse.
