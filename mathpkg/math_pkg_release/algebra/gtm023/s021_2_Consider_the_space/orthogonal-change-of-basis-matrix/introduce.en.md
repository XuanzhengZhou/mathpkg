When changing from one orthonormal basis to another in an inner product space, the transformation matrix is orthogonal: its transpose equals its inverse, meaning \(\sum_{\lambda} \alpha_{\nu}^{\lambda} \alpha_{\mu}^{\lambda} = \delta_{\nu\mu}\). This follows from the preservation of the inner product under basis transformation — the orthonormality conditions \((x_{\nu}, x_{\mu}) = \delta_{\nu\mu}\) and \((\bar{x}_{\nu}, \bar{x}_{\mu}) = \delta_{\nu\mu}\) directly imply the orthogonality relation for the change-of-basis matrix. Orthogonal matrices thus characterize transitions between orthonormal bases.
