A vector space $E$ is cyclic with respect to $\varphi$ precisely when there exists a basis $\{a_0, \ldots, a_{n-1}\}$ on which $\varphi$ acts as a forward shift: $\varphi(a_v) = a_{v+1}$ for $v = 0, \ldots, n-2$. This characterization shows that a cyclic transformation is essentially the companion matrix action. Given such a basis, every vector $x$ can be recovered from $a_0$ via $x = f_x(\varphi)a_0$, confirming $a_0$ as a cyclic generator.
