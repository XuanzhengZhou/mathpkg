Theorem I gives a precise characterization of irreducible spaces: $E$ is irreducible with respect to $\varphi$ if and only if the minimum polynomial is a power of a single irreducible polynomial ($\mu = f^k$) and $E$ is cyclic. The forward direction uses the fact that any decomposition would force the minimum polynomial to factor. The converse shows that under these conditions any decomposition would force one factor to equal the whole space.
