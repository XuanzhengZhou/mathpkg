The map $\sigma_a$ is the fundamental bridge between the polynomial ring $\Gamma[t]$ and the vector space $E$. For a fixed vector $a \in E$ and linear transformation $\varphi: E \to E$, it evaluates any polynomial $f$ at $\varphi$ and applies the resulting operator to $a$. The kernel $K_a$ of this map is an ideal in $\Gamma[t]$, and its structure determines whether $a$ is a cyclic vector. When $K_a = I_\mu$ (the ideal generated by the minimum polynomial), $\sigma_a$ induces an isomorphism $\Gamma[t]/I_\mu \cong E_a$, making $E_a = \operatorname{Im} \sigma_a$ a cyclic subspace.
