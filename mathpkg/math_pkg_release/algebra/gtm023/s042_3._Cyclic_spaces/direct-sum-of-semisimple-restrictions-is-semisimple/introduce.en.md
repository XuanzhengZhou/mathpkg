This corollary shows semisimplicity lifts from stable summands to the whole space: if each $\varphi_i$ is semisimple, so is $\varphi$. The proof uses that each minimum polynomial $\mu_i$ is a product of distinct irreducibles, so their least common multiple (which divides $\mu$) is also such a product, making $\varphi$ semisimple by Theorem I.
