Proposition I states that for a semisimple transformation, nilpotency of a polynomial evaluation forces it to be zero. The proof uses that if $f(\varphi)^k = 0$, then $E = K(f^l)$ for $l = \max(k-1,1)$, and the degree of nilpotency forces $k=l=1$, so $f(\varphi) = 0$.
