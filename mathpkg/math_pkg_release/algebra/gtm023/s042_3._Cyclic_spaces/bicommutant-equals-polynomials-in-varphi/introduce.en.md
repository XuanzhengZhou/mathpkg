Theorem III (13.22) characterizes the bicommutant $C^2(\varphi)$ as exactly the algebra $\Gamma(\varphi)$ of polynomials in $\varphi$. The inclusion $\Gamma(\varphi) \subset C^2(\varphi)$ is clear. For the reverse, the proof decomposes $E$ into cyclic subspaces, uses that the projection operators commute with $\psi \in C^2(\varphi)$, and constructs polynomial relations showing $\psi = g_1(\varphi)$.
