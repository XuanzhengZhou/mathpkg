Every linear transformation $\varphi$ can be written uniquely in the form
$$\varphi = \varphi_S + \varphi_N$$
where $\varphi_S$ is semisimple, $\varphi_N$ is nilpotent, and $\varphi_S \varphi_N = \varphi_N \varphi_S$. Moreover, $\varphi_S$ and $\varphi_N$ are polynomials in $\varphi$, and their minimum polynomials are given by
$$\mu_S = f_1 \ldots f_r$$
and
$$\mu_N = t^k, \quad k = \max(k_1, \ldots, k_r)$$
where $\mu = f_1^{k_1} \ldots f_r^{k_r}$ is the factorization of $\mu$.
