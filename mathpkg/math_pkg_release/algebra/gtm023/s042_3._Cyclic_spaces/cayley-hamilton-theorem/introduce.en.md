The Cayley-Hamilton theorem states that every linear transformation satisfies its characteristic polynomial: $\chi(\varphi) = 0$, or equivalently, the minimum polynomial divides the characteristic polynomial. The proof uses a maximal cyclic subspace $E_a$ (with $\dim E_a = \deg \mu$), applies Lemma II to get $\chi_a = \pm \mu$ for the induced transformation on $E_a$, and factors the characteristic polynomial as $\chi = \chi_a \chi_F$.
