When the minimum polynomial is completely reducible ($\mu = (t-\lambda)^k$), the irreducible polynomial $f$ has degree $p=1$, so the companion blocks are $1 \times 1$ matrices $(\lambda)$. The Jordan canonical matrix becomes a single $k \times k$ Jordan block with eigenvalue $\lambda$ on the diagonal and 1's on the superdiagonal. Over algebraically closed fields (e.g., $\mathbb{C}$), every irreducible transformation has this form.
