Theorem II (13.28) provides an equivalent geometric characterization of normal transformations: $E$ decomposes into an orthogonal direct sum of irreducible subspaces, on each of which $\varphi$ acts as a homothety (scalar multiple of an isometry). This generalizes the spectral theorem: each irreducible subspace has dimension 1 or 2, corresponding to real eigenvalues or complex conjugate pairs.
