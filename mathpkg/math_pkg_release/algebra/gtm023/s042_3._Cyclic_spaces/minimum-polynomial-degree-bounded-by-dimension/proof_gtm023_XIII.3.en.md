Proposition V implies that $\deg \mu \leq \dim E$. If $E$ is cyclic, equality holds (cf. Proposition III). Conversely, assume that $\dim E = \deg \mu$. Let $E_a$ be a cyclic subspace with $\dim E_a = \deg \mu$ (which exists by Proposition V). Then $\dim E_a = \dim E$, and since $E_a \subset E$, we have $E_a = E$. Thus $E$ is cyclic.

Finally, let $F \subset E$ be any cyclic subspace and let $\nu$ denote the minimum polynomial of the induced transformation. Then $\dim F = \deg \nu$. But $\nu|\mu$ (cf. sec. 13.2) and so we obtain $\dim F \leq \deg \mu$.
