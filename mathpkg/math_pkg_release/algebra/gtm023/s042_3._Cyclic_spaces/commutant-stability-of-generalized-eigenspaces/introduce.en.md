Proposition I (13.21) states that the generalized eigenspaces $E_i$ of $\varphi$ are stable under any transformation $\psi$ that commutes with $\varphi$. Moreover, the projection operators $\pi_i$ commute with $\psi$. This follows because the $\pi_i$ are polynomials in $\varphi$, so any $\psi$ commuting with $\varphi$ also commutes with $\pi_i$, making $E_i = \operatorname{Im} \pi_i$ stable under $\psi$.
