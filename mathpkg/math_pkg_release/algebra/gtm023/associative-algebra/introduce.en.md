Associative algebras satisfy $(xy)z = x(yz)$; commutative algebras satisfy $xy = yx$. The algebra $A(E;E)$ of linear transformations is associative but not commutative when $\dim E \geq 2$. The opposite algebra reverses multiplication order. An algebra with unit element has a unique identity; epimorphisms preserve unit elements.
