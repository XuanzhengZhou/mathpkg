A bijective linear mapping $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$ is called a linear isomorphism and will be denoted by $\varphi: E \xrightarrow{\cong} F$. The inverse mapping $\varphi^{-1}: F \rightarrow E$ is again a linear mapping, bijective and hence a linear isomorphism, called the inverse isomorphism of $\varphi$.

Two vector spaces $E$ and $F$ are called isomorphic if there exists a linear isomorphism from $E$ onto $F$.

A linear isomorphism maps bases to bases. Conversely, if $\varphi: E \rightarrow F$ is a linear mapping sending a basis of $E$ to a basis of $F$, then $\varphi$ is a linear isomorphism.
