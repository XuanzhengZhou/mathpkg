Let $E$ and $F$ be $G$-graded spaces and $\varphi: E \to F$ a linear map. $\varphi$ is called homogeneous if there exists $k \in G$ such that $\varphi(E_j) \subset F_{j+k}$ for all $j \in G$. $k$ is the degree of $\varphi$.

The kernel of a homogeneous mapping is a graded subspace of $E$. If $\varphi$ is surjective and homogeneous of degree $0$, then $F$ inherits a unique $G$-gradation such that $\varphi$ is homogeneous of degree $0$.
