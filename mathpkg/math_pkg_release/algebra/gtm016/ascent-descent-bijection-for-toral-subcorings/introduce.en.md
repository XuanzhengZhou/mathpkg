In the study of finite dimensional radical field extensions $K/k$, toral $k$-subcorings of the Hopf algebra $H(K/k)$ play a central role. Winter's Ascent-Descent Theorem establishes a Galois-equivariant bijection between toral $k$-subcorings of $H(K/k)$ and $G$-stable diagonalizable toral $k$-subcorings of $H(\bar{K}/k)$, where $\bar{K}$ is the separable closure. This allows one to pass from an arbitrary toral structure $T$ over $K$ to a simpler diagonalizable structure $\bar{T}$ over $\bar{K}$ (ascent), study it there, and then transfer conclusions back via Galois fixed points (descent). The theorem also characterizes when a $k$-torus is coradical in terms of the structure of its comultiplication.
