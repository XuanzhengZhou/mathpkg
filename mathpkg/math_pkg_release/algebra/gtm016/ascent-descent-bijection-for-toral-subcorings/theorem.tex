Let $K/k$ be a finite dimensional radical extension. Then the set of toral $k$-subcorings (respectively $k$-subbirings) of $H(K/k)$ is mapped bijectively to the set of $G$-stable (necessarily diagonalizable) toral $k$-subcorings (respectively $k$-subbirings) of $H(\bar{K}/k)$ by the mapping $T \mapsto \bar{T}$, the inverse mapping being $\bar{T} \mapsto \bar{T}^G$.

If $T$ is a $k$-torus on $K$ containing $I$ such that $\varepsilon(T) \subset k$ and for each $t \in T$ there exist ${}_i t, t_i \in T$ such that
$$\Delta t = \sum_i {}_i t \otimes_K t_i,$$
then $T$ is a coradical toral $k$-subcoring of $H(K/k)$.
