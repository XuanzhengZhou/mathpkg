Let $k$ be an infinite field, $K$ and $L$ extension fields of $k$, and $L'$ an extension of $L$. Let $\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_n$ be distinct $k$-homomorphisms from $K$ to $L$. Then for $f(X_1, \ldots, X_n) \in L'[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$, the function $f(\sigma_1, \ldots, \sigma_n)$ is $0$ if and only if the polynomial $f$ is $0$.
