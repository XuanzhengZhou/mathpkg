If $s$ is radical, then $s^{p^e} \in k$ for some $e$. For any $t = \sum a_i s^i \in k(s)$, we have $t^{p^e} = \sum a_i^{p^e} s^{p^e i} \in k$, so $t$ is radical. The separable case follows similarly.
