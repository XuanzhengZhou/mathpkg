For each prime $p_i$ dividing $G:1$, the subset $G_{p_i} = \{g \in G \mid g^{p_i^f} = e \text{ for some } f\}$ is a subgroup. For distinct primes $p, q$, elements of $G_p$ commute with elements of $G_q$. Thus the product map is a homomorphism with trivial kernel, giving $G = \prod_1^n G_{p_i}$.
