A derivation from $K$ to $L$ over $k$ is a $k$-linear mapping $D: K \to L$ such that $D(xy) = D(x)y + xD(y)$ for $x, y \in K$. The set of derivations is denoted $\operatorname{Der}_k(K, L)$. The derivations $\operatorname{Der}_k(K, K)$ are called derivations of $K/k$, and form the derivation algebra.
