Let $K/k$ be a finite dimensional radical extension of exponent one. Prove the following.

(a) If $T$ is a maximal finite dimensional diagonalizable toral $k$-subring of $H(K/k)$, then $K^T = k$.

(b) If $S$ is a diagonalizable $k$-torus in $\operatorname{Der}_k K$, then there exists a diagonalizable $k$-torus $S'$ in $\operatorname{Der}_k K$ such that $S \cap S' = \{0\}$, $T = S + S'$ (direct) is a maximal $k$-torus in $\operatorname{Der}_k K$ and, consequently, $K^T = k$ and $K = K^S K^{S'}$ (internal tensor product over $k$).

(c) For any subfield $k'$ of $K$ containing $k$, there is a subfield $k''$ of $K$ containing $k$ such that $K = k' k''$ (internal tensor product over $k$).
