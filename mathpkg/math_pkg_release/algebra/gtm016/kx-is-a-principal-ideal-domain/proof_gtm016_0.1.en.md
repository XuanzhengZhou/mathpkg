Let $I$ be a nonzero ideal of $K[X]$. Take $f(X)$ to be a nonzero element of $I$ of least degree. For any $g(X) \in I \setminus \{0\}$, by the Euclidean algorithm we can write $g(X) = f(X)h(X) + r(X)$ where $\operatorname{Deg} r(X) < \operatorname{Deg} f(X)$. Since $r(X) = g(X) - f(X)h(X) \in I$ and $f(X)$ has minimal degree in $I$, we must have $r(X) = 0$. Thus $g(X) = f(X)h(X)$ and $I$ is generated by $f(X)$.
