Since $H \subseteq H(K/k)$ is coclosed, $\Delta$ restricts to $\Delta_H: H \to H \otimes_K H$. Properties (1)-(3) of 5.3.8 hold for the restrictions as identities in $H(K/k)$. If $H$ is a $K$-subbiring, it is closed under multiplication, and $\varepsilon(xy)=\varepsilon(x)\varepsilon(y)$ follows from multiplicativity of $\varepsilon$ on $H(K/k)$. Thus $(H,\Delta_H,\varepsilon_H)$ satisfies all bialgebra axioms of B.1.
