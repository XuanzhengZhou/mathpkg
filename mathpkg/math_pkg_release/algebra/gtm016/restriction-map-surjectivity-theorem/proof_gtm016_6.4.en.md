It is clear that $K^T$ is stable under $H(K/k)^T$ and that $x \mapsto x|_{K^T}$ is a $K^T$-linear $k$-algebra homomorphism.

*(The proof continues by induction on the dimension of a coradical coalgebra $C$ over $K$, using the existence of a nonzero primitive element in $C$ to construct a derivation and a torus, reducing to the induction hypothesis via the centralizer $C^{T_\pi}$ of a diagonalizable torus $T_\pi$. The surjectivity of the restriction map follows from the fact that $\bar{H} = \bar{K}\bar{H}^{\bar{T}}$ and the Galois descent argument as in the proof of Theorem 6.3.11.)*
