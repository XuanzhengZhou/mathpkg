For a finite dimensional radical extension $K/k$ and toral $k$-subcorings $S \subset T$ of $H(K/k)$, Theorem 6.3.7 gives a fixed-field characterization of the intersection $T \cap \langle S \rangle$. The result states that an element $t \in T$ lies in the subcoring generated by $S$ precisely when $t$ acts as scalar multiplication by the counit $\varepsilon(t)$ on the fixed field $K^S$. This provides a useful criterion for detecting membership in $\langle S \rangle$ using only the action on the $S$-invariant elements of $K$.
