By Corollary 0.3.2, $|G| = p^n$. The class equation gives $|G| = |C(G)| + \sum |G:G_{x_i}|$ where each $G_{x_i}$ is a proper subgroup. Since each $|G:G_{x_i}|$ is divisible by $p$, and $|G|$ is divisible by $p$, it follows that $|C(G)|$ is divisible by $p$. Since $C(G)$ contains the identity, $|C(G)| \geq p > 1$.
