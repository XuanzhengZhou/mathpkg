Let $L/k$ be a field extension. For $x \in K$ and $S \subset K$, write $x \prec_p S$ for $x \in k(K^p)(S)$. Show that $\prec_p$ is a dependence relation in the sense of E.1.20. A subset $S$ of $K$ is \textit{$p$-independent} over $k$ if $S$ is independent in the sense of E.1.20. Show that a subset $S$ of $K$ is a basis for $K$ in the sense of E.1.20 if and only if $S$ is a $p$-basis for $K/k$. Conclude that every extension $K/k$ has a $p$-basis and the cardinalities of any two $p$-bases of $K/k$ are the same. (See 4.3.5.)
