This proposition provides a weight space decomposition of $V$ with respect to a $k$-torus $T$ in characteristic $p > 1$. Since each element $t \in T_\pi$ satisfies $t^p - t = 0$, its minimum polynomial divides the separable polynomial $X^p - X = \prod_{i \in \pi} (X - i)$, making every $t \in T_\pi$ diagonalizable with eigenvalues in the prime field $\pi$. Consequently, $V$ decomposes as a direct sum of simultaneous eigenspaces $V_\alpha(T_\pi)$ indexed by the $\pi$-dual $T_\pi^*$. This is the torus analogue of the weight space decomposition in the representation theory of algebraic groups.
