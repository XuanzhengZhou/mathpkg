The elements \(x_1^{f_1} \cdots x_n^{f_n}\) form a basis for \(K\) over \(K^G\), since

\[
K x_1^{f_1} \cdots x_n^{f_n} = g_1^{f_1} \cdots g_n^{f_n}
\]

and

\[
K = \sum_{g \in G} g \quad \text{(direct)}.
\]

Thus \(G\) is a Galois splitting group for \(K/k\) with the stated basis and identity element \(K^G = k'\).
