There is nothing to prove if $p = 1$. Thus, let $p > 1$.

($\Leftarrow$) If $K/k$ is separably generated and $y_1, \ldots, y_d$ is a separating transcendency basis for $K/k$, then $K/k(y_1, \ldots, y_d)$ is separable algebraic and $k(y_1, \ldots, y_d)/k$ is separable by Proposition 4.3.15. By transitivity of separability, $K/k$ is separable.

($\Rightarrow$) [Proof truncated in source text. The converse direction uses the $p$-basis and the criterion from 4.3.14 to construct a separating transcendence basis from the inseparability degree.]
