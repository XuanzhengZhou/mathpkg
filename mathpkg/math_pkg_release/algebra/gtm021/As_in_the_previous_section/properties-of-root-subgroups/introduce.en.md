This theorem describes the fundamental properties of root subgroups in a reductive group. Each root $\alpha$ corresponds to a 1-dimensional unipotent subgroup $U_\alpha$ isomorphic to the additive group. The Weyl group permutes these subgroups compatibly with its action on roots, and the torus acts by scaling via the character $\alpha$.
