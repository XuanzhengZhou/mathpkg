Let $\hat{W}$ be the abstract Coxeter group with the given presentation, and let $\pi: \hat{W} \rightarrow W$ be the natural surjection. Define $\varphi: W \rightarrow \hat{W}$ by $\varphi(\rho_{i(1)}\cdots\rho_{i(t)}) = \hat{\rho}_{i(1)}\cdots\hat{\rho}_{i(t)}$ whenever the first expression is reduced. Step (1) shows this is well-defined: if two reduced expressions represent the same element of $W$, their images in $\hat{W}$ coincide (proved by induction using the exchange condition). Step (2) shows $\varphi$ is surjective (its image is a subgroup containing all generators). Since $\pi \circ \varphi = 1_W$, $\varphi$ is injective, hence an isomorphism.
