(a) By Theorem 29.2(a), any subgroup $H$ of $G$ including $B$ is the union of certain double cosets $B\sigma B$. Let $I$ be the set of all $\rho \in S$ appearing in reduced expressions for such $\sigma \in W$. Then clearly $H \subset P_I$. On the other hand, Lemma B (29.3) implies that $P_I \subset H$.

(b) Since $B$ and $N$ (or $W$) generate $G$, two subgroups containing $B$ are conjugate in $G$ only if they are conjugate by some $\sigma \in W$. But then Lemma D (29.3) applies.

(c) Apply Lemma D again.

(d) Each $\rho \in I$ is a product of certain elements of $J$, so Lemma C (29.3) forces $I \subset J$.

(e) If $P_I \subset P_J$, then $W_I \subset P_J$ and then $W_I \subset W_J$ (Theorem 29.2(b)), whence $I \subset J$ by part (d).
