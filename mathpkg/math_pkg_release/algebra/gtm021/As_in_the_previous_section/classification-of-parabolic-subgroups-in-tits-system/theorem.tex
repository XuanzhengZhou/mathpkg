Let $(G, B, N, S)$ be a Tits system.
(a) The only subgroups of $G$ containing $B$ are those of the form $P_I$ for $I \subset S$.
(b) If $P_I$ is conjugate to $P_J$, then $P_I = P_J$.
(c) $N_G(P_I) = P_I$ (in particular, $N_G(B) = B$).
(d) If $W_I \subset W_J$, then $I \subset J$.
(e) If $P_I \subset P_J$, then $I \subset J$.
