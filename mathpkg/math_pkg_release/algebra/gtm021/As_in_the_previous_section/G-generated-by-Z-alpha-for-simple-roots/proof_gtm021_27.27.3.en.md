The subgroup $H$ of $G$ generated by the $Z_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Delta$) includes $T$ as well as representatives of all $\sigma \in W$ (since the $\sigma_\alpha, \alpha \in \Delta$, generate $W$). So the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{h}$ includes $\mathfrak{t}$ along with all root spaces $\mathfrak{g}_{\sigma(\alpha)}$ ($\alpha \in \Delta, \sigma \in W$). As recalled above, these $\sigma(\alpha)$ exhaust $\Phi$, forcing $\mathfrak{h} = \mathfrak{g}$ and hence $H = G$. Theorem 26.3(d) completes the proof.
