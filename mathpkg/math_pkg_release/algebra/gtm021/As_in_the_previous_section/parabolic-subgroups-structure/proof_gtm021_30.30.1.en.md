(b) The Lie algebra of $P_I$ is $\mathfrak{t} \oplus \bigsqcup \mathfrak{g}_\alpha$ where $\alpha$ ranges over some set of roots $\Theta$ including $\Phi^+$. Since global and infinitesimal centralizers of tori correspond (18.4), a negative root $\alpha$ lies in $\Theta$ if and only if $Z_\alpha \subset P_I$. By construction, if $\sigma_\alpha \in W_I$, then $Z_\alpha = B_\alpha \cup B_\alpha \sigma_\alpha B_\alpha \subset P_I$. Now $W_I$ is the Weyl group of the root system consisting of all $\mathbb{Z}$-linear combinations of $I$ in $\Phi$ (A.6). It follows that all such roots lie in $\Theta$. Conversely, if a pair $\{\alpha, -\alpha\}$ is contained in $\Theta$, then $\sigma_\alpha \in W_I$, so $\alpha$ is a $\mathbb{Z}$-linear combination of $I$.
