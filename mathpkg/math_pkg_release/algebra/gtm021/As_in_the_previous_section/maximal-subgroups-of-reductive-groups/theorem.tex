Let $G$ be a reductive algebraic group.
(a) Let $H$ be a maximal (proper) closed subgroup of $G$. Then either $H^\circ$ is reductive or else $H$ is parabolic.
(b) Each unipotent subgroup of $G$ lies in some Borel subgroup; in particular, the maximal unipotent subgroups of $G$ are precisely the unipotent radicals of Borel subgroups.
