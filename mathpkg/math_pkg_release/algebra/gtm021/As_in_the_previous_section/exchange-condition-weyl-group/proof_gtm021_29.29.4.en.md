By Lemma A of (29.3), $\rho_{i(0)}B \subset B\sigma B\sigma^{-1}B$, which (by repeated application of (T1)) lies in the union of all double cosets $B\sigma\rho_{i(n_1)} \cdots \rho_{i(n_r)}B$, where $r \leqslant t$ and $t \geqslant n_1 > n_2 > \cdots > n_r \geqslant 1$. In particular, thanks to Theorem 29.2, $\rho_{i(0)}$ is equal to one (and only one!) of the indicated elements of $W$. But $\ell(\sigma) = t$ and $\ell(\rho_{i(0)}) = 1$, so it is clear that $r = t - 1$, therefore that only one $\rho_{i(s)}$ has been omitted. It is this choice of $s$ which verifies the lemma.
