The exchange condition is a fundamental combinatorial property of Coxeter groups. It states that if multiplying a reduced word by a simple reflection on the left does not increase the length, then one factor can be exchanged. This condition characterizes Coxeter groups and is essential for proving that the Weyl group is a Coxeter group.
