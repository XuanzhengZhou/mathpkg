Let $G = \mathrm{SL}(2, \mathbb{K})$, $\Omega$ as in (28.5). Prove that $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ belongs to $\Omega$ if and only if $a \neq 0$. (More generally, when $G = \mathrm{SL}(n, \mathbb{K})$, the criterion for $x \in G$ to lie in $\Omega$ is that all diagonal minors of $x$ be nonzero.)
