We know that $B'$ is conjugate to $B$ (21.3). The theorem allows us to write a conjugating element in the form $u\sigma b$ ($u \in U$, $\sigma \in W$, $b \in B$); so $B' = u\sigma B\sigma^{-1}u^{-1}$. But $\sigma T\sigma^{-1} = T$, while $uTu^{-1} \subset B$, so the maximal torus $u\sigma T\sigma^{-1}u^{-1}$ of $B'$ is also included in $B$.
