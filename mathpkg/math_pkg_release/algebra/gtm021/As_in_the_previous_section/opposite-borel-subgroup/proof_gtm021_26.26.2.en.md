Since $\Phi = -\Phi$ (Corollary 26.2B(a)), there is a unique Borel subgroup containing $T$ whose roots are exactly the negatives of the roots of $B$. This is obtained by taking the longest element $\sigma_0$ of the Weyl group: $B^{-} = \sigma_0 B \sigma_0^{-1}$. The intersection property follows from the fact that the only characters of $T$ appearing in both the Lie algebras of $B$ and $B^{-}$ are the zero weights, which come from $\mathfrak{t}$.
