Let $X, Y$ be varieties.

(a) If $X$ is complete and $Y$ is closed in $X$, then $Y$ is complete.
(b) If $X$ and $Y$ are complete, then $X \times Y$ is complete.
(c) If $\varphi: X \rightarrow Y$ is a morphism and $X$ is complete, then $\varphi(X)$ is closed and complete.
(d) A complete irreducible affine variety is a point.
