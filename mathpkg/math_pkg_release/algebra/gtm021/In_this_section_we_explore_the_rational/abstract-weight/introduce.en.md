An abstract weight is an element $\lambda$ of the ambient Euclidean space $E = \mathbb{R} \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} X(T)$ whose pairings with all roots are integral, meaning that $2(\lambda,\alpha)/(\alpha,\alpha) \in \mathbb{Z}$ for every $\alpha \in \Phi$. This condition arises naturally from the action of the Weyl group: the reflection $\sigma_\alpha$ sends $\lambda$ to $\lambda - \frac{2(\lambda,\alpha)}{(\alpha,\alpha)}\alpha$, and integrality of this coefficient is required for consistency with the representation theory. The abstract weights form the weight lattice $\Lambda$, which contains the root lattice $\Lambda_r$ as a subgroup of finite index.
