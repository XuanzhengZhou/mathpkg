It suffices to show that $\mathrm{pr}_2: X \times Z \rightarrow Z$ is closed for all affine varieties $Z$. The morphism $\varphi$ is bijective and $G$-equivariant, so $\varphi \times 1: X \times Z \rightarrow Y \times Z$ is a bijective morphism, hence a closed map (4.2). Because $G$ acts transitively on $X$ and $Y$, both are covered by finitely many translates $x_i \cdot U$ (resp. $x_i \cdot V$) for some $x_i \in G$. It follows that $\varphi \times 1: X \times Z \rightarrow Y \times Z$ is closed. Since $Y$ is complete, $\mathrm{pr}_2: Y \times Z \rightarrow Z$ is closed. Composing, $\mathrm{pr}_2: X \times Z \rightarrow Z$ is closed, so $X$ is complete by definition.
