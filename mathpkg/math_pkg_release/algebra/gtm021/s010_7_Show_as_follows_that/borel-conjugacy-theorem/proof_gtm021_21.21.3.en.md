Let B be a Borel subgroup and S another Borel subgroup. Embed S in some T(n,K) via Lie-Kolchin. S stabilizes a full flag in V=K^n, corresponding to f in the flag variety. The stabilizer of f is solvable, of dimension at most dim S. The G-orbit of f has smallest possible dimension, so is closed (8.3), hence complete (21.1a,e). Thus G/S is complete (Lemma 21.1), hence projective (21.1d). Now B acts on G/S by left multiplication. By Borel Fixed Point Theorem (21.2), B fixes xS, i.e., B x S = x S, so x^{-1} B x is contained in S. Both are Borel subgroups, so x^{-1} B x = S.
