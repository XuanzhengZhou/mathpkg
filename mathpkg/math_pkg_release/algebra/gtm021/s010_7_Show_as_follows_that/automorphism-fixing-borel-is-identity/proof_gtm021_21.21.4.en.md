G is the union of its Borel subgroups (Theorem 22.2). Since sigma fixes every element of B, and any g in G lies in some Borel subgroup x B x^{-1} (by conjugacy), we have g = x b x^{-1} for b in B. Then sigma(g) = sigma(x) sigma(b) sigma(x)^{-1} = sigma(x) b sigma(x)^{-1}. Using transitivity of G on the flag variety and Borel Fixed Point Theorem, sigma is the identity.
