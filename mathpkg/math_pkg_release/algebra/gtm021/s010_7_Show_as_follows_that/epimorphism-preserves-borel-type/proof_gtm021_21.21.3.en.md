It suffices for H = B a Borel. B' = phi(B) is connected and solvable. The natural map G -> G' -> G'/B' induces a surjective morphism G/B -> G'/B', forcing the latter to be complete (21.1b), i.e., B' is parabolic. Corollary B says it is then a Borel subgroup. Since some Borel of G' has form phi(B), conjugacy gives the rest.
