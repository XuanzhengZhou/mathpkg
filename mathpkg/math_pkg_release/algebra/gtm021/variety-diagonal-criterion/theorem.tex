A prevariety $X$ is called a variety if it satisfies the Hausdorff axiom: the diagonal
$$\Delta(X) = \{(x, x) \mid x \in X\}$$
is closed in $X \times X$ (with the Zariski product topology).

Equivalent condition: For morphisms $\varphi, \psi: Y \rightarrow X$, the set $\{y \in Y \mid \varphi(y) = \psi(y)\}$ is closed in $Y$.
