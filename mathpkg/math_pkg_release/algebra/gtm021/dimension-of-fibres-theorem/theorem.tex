Let $\varphi: X \rightarrow Y$ be a dominant morphism of irreducible varieties, and set $r = \dim X - \dim Y$. Let $W$ be a closed irreducible subset of $Y$. If $Z$ is an irreducible component of $\varphi^{-1}(W)$ which dominates $W$, then
$$\dim Z \geqslant \dim W + r.$$
In particular, if $y \in \varphi(X)$, each component of $\varphi^{-1}(y)$ has dimension at least $r$.
