Let $G$ be an algebraic group, and let $\{Y_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a family of irreducible subsets containing $e$, stable under inversion, whose union generates $G$ as an abstract group. Let $M$ be the monoid generated by these. Then:

(a) $\overline{M}$ is a (closed) subgroup of $G$, namely the group generated by the $Y_i$.
(b) There exists a finite sequence of indices $(i_1, \ldots, i_n)$ such that $\overline{M} = Y_{i_1} \cdots Y_{i_n}$.
