If $I$ is any ideal in $K[T_1, \ldots, T_n]$, then
$$\sqrt{I} = \mathcal{I}(\mathcal{V}(I)).$$
Equivalently: Given $f(T), f_1(T), \ldots, f_s(T)$ in $K[T]$ such that $f(T)$ vanishes at every common zero of the $f_i(T)$ in $\mathbf{A}^n$, there exist $r \geqslant 0$ and $g_1(T), \ldots, g_s(T) \in K[T]$ for which
$$f(T)^r = \sum_{i=1}^{s} g_i(T) f_i(T).$$
