A derivation $\delta: E \rightarrow L$ ($E$ a field, $L$ an extension field of $E$) is a map satisfying:
$$\delta(x + y) = \delta(x) + \delta(y), \quad \delta(xy) = x\delta(y) + \delta(x)y.$$
If $F$ is a subfield of $E$, $\delta$ is called an $F$-derivation if $\delta(x) = 0$ for all $x \in F$.
The space $\operatorname{Der}_F(E, L)$ of all $F$-derivations $E \rightarrow L$ is a vector space over $L$.
