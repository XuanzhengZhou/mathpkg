Embed $G$ as a closed subgroup of some $\operatorname{GL}(n, K)$. Then $\operatorname{Ad} x(y) = xyx^{-1}$ (matrix product) for $x \in G, y \in \mathfrak{g} \subset \mathfrak{gl}(n, K)$. The map $(x, y) \mapsto xyx^{-1}$ is polynomial in the entries of $x$ and $y$, hence is a morphism. The induced map $x \mapsto \operatorname{Ad} x$ into $\operatorname{GL}(\mathfrak{g})$ is therefore also a morphism.
