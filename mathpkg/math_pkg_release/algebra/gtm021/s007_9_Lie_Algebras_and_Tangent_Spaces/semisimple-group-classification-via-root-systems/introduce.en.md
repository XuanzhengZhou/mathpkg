This theorem summarizes the classification of semisimple algebraic groups in characteristic 0, which follows from the classical Killing-Cartan classification of semisimple Lie algebras. A semisimple group $G$ is determined (up to local isomorphism) by its Lie algebra, which in turn is determined by its root system. The center $Z(G)$ must be a subgroup of the fundamental group of the root system. For example, in type $A_1$ the fundamental group has order 2, so the only semisimple groups of this type are $\operatorname{SL}(2,K)$ and $\operatorname{PGL}(2,K)$. Remarkably, the classification in prime characteristic (Chapter XI) leads to virtually the same results.
