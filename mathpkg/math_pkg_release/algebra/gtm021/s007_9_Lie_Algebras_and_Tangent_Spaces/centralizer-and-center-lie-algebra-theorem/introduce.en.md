In characteristic 0, the Lie algebra of the centralizer equals the infinitesimal centralizer (fixed points of Ad x), and the center of the group corresponds exactly to the center of the Lie algebra. Part (a) follows from the GL(n) case (Proposition 10.6) plus Theorem 12.5 on intersections. Part (b) uses separability of morphisms: $N = \operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{Ad}$ has Lie algebra $\operatorname{Ker} d(\operatorname{Ad}) = \operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{ad} = \mathfrak{z}(\mathfrak{g})$, and the inclusion $Z(G) \subset \operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{Ad}$ plus a dimension/connectedness argument gives equality.
