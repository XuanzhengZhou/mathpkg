Consider the composite $G \xrightarrow{(1, \iota)} G \times G \xrightarrow{\mu} G$. Since this sends every $x$ to $e$, its differential is zero. Using $d\mu_{(e,e)}(x, y) = x + y$, we get $x + d\iota_e(x) = 0$, hence $d\iota_e(x) = -x$.
