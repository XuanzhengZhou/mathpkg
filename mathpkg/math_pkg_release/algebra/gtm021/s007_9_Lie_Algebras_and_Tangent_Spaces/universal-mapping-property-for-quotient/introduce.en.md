This theorem establishes the quotient $G/H$ as a variety with the expected universal property. Any $G$-invariant morphism from $G$ that is constant on $H$-cosets factors uniquely through $\pi$. The proof reduces to verifying that $\pi$ is an open map and that the sheaf of regular functions on $Y$ consists exactly of those regular functions on $G$ that are constant on $H$-cosets. The theorem justifies calling $Y$ the homogeneous space $G/H$ and $\pi$ the canonical quotient morphism.
