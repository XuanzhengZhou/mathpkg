Let $H$ be a closed subgroup of an algebraic group $G$, and let $\pi: G \to Y$ be the separable, $G$-equivariant morphism whose fibres are the cosets $xH$ (constructed in Section 11). Then the pair $(\pi, Y)$ satisfies the universal mapping property:

Given any variety $X$ and a morphism $\varphi: G \to X$ whose nonempty fibres are unions of cosets $xH$, there exists a unique morphism $\psi: Y \to X$ such that $\psi \circ \pi = \varphi$.

Consequently, $Y$ is uniquely determined up to unique isomorphism, and is called the homogeneous space $G/H$.
