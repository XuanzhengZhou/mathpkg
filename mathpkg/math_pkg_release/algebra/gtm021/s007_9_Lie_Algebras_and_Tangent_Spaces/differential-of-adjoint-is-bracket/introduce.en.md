This fundamental theorem connects the adjoint representation of the group with the Lie bracket. Differentiating the group homomorphism $\operatorname{Ad}: G \to \operatorname{GL}(\mathfrak{g})$ yields the Lie algebra homomorphism $\operatorname{ad}: \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{gl}(\mathfrak{g})$ given by $\operatorname{ad} X(Y) = [X,Y]$. This cornerstone result shows that the Lie bracket encodes the infinitesimal adjoint action. The proof first handles $G = \mathrm{GL}(n,K)$ and then extends to arbitrary $G$ via embedding.
