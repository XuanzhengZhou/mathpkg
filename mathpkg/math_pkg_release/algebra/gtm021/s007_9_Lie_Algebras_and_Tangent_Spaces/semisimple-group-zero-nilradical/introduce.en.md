A semisimple algebraic group (one having no nontrivial closed connected normal solvable subgroups) has a Lie algebra that is semisimple in the usual Lie algebra sense: its center is trivial and its maximal nilpotent ideal is zero. This is significant because quite a lot is known about semisimple Lie algebras (classification by root systems, complete reducibility of representations). The theorem bridges the algebraic group notion of semisimplicity with the Lie algebra notion.
