A character of an algebraic group $G$ is a morphism of algebraic groups $\chi: G \to \mathbf{G}_m$, where $\mathbf{G}_m = \operatorname{GL}(1, K)$ is the multiplicative group. The set $\mathbf{X}(G)$ of all characters forms a commutative group under pointwise multiplication: $(\chi_1 \chi_2)(x) = \chi_1(x) \chi_2(x)$.
