Chevalley's theorem is a fundamental result in the theory of algebraic groups. It shows that any closed subgroup $H$ of an algebraic group $G$ can be realized as the stabilizer of a line in some rational representation, simultaneously at the group and Lie algebra levels. This theorem is the key step in constructing the homogeneous space $G/H$ as a quasi-projective variety: one takes the orbit of the line $L$ in the projective space $\mathbf{P}(V)$.
