This theorem refines Chevalley's construction when the subgroup $N$ is normal. It shows that $N$ can be realized as the exact kernel (not just stabilizer) of a representation, and simultaneously $\mathfrak{n}$ as the kernel of the differential. This is a crucial step toward constructing the quotient group $G/N$ as an affine algebraic group. The proof uses the character group $\mathbf{X}(N)$ and the decomposition of $V$ into weight spaces.
