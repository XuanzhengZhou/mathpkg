A topological space $X$ is \textbf{compactly generated} if it is a $k$-space: a subset $U \subset X$ is open if and only if $U \cap K$ is open in $K$ (with the subspace topology from $X$) for every compact Hausdorff subspace $K \subset X$. Equivalently, the topology is final with respect to the inclusions of all compact Hausdorff subspaces.
