A pullback (or fibered product) is the universal way to complete a commutative square on two given arrows with a common codomain. In Set, $B \times_A D = \{(b, d) \in B \times D \mid f(b) = g(d)\}$. Pullbacks are fundamental: they generalize preimages, fiber products, and induced bundles in topology. The pullback of $f: B \to A$ with itself is called the kernel pair of $f$.
