In an abelian category, the canonical monic-epi factorization of any arrow allows defining two fundamental subobjects: the image (a subobject of the codomain) and the coimage (a quotient object of the domain). These are uniquely determined up to isomorphism. In concrete categories such as $\mathbf{Ab}$, this recovers the familiar factorization $B \rightarrow B/K \xrightarrow{\cong} S \hookrightarrow C$, where $B/K$ is the coimage and $S$ is the image, linked by the canonical isomorphism between them.
