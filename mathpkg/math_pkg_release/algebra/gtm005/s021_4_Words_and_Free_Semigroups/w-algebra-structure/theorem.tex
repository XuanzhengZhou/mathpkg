For the word monad $W$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, the $W$-algebras have the form $\langle S, v_1, v_2, \dots \rangle$, a set $S$ equipped with one $n$-ary operation
$$v_n : S^n \to S$$
for each positive integer $n$, such that the structure map $\theta : WS \to S$ is given on a word $\langle s_1 \rangle \dots \langle s_n \rangle$ of length $n$ by
$$\theta(\langle s_1 \rangle \dots \langle s_n \rangle) = v_n(s_1, \dots, s_n).$$
The algebra laws imply that $v_1 = 1_S$ and that the $v_n$ are the $n$-fold iterated products derived from the binary operation $v_2$.
