The free semigroup $WX$ on a set $X$ is the collection of all nonempty finite sequences (words) of elements from $X$, with the operation of concatenation (juxtaposition). Each element $x \in X$ is wrapped in angled brackets $\langle x \rangle$ to distinguish the one-letter word from the element itself. This construction is the starting point for the word monad, a fundamental example of a monad arising from an adjunction between sets and semigroups.
