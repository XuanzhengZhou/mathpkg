A metacategory is Mac Lane's term for a category defined axiomatically without any appeal to set theory. It extends a metagraph by adding identity arrows for each object and an associative composition operation for composable pairs of arrows, subject to the unit law. The term emphasizes that the definition is purely axiomatic rather than set-theoretic.
