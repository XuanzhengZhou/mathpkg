Given two functors $U: B \to B'$ and $V: C \to C'$, their product $U \times V: B \times C \to B' \times C'$ acts componentwise on objects and arrows of the product category. It is the unique functor that commutes with the projection functors, making the product $\times$ itself into a functor $\text{Cat} \times \text{Cat} \to \text{Cat}$.
