The universal property of the product category $B \times C$ states that for any category $D$ with functors $R: D \to B$ and $T: D \to C$, there is a unique functor $F: D \to B \times C$ such that composing with the projection functors recovers $R$ and $T$. This means that to give a functor into a product category is the same as giving a pair of functors into each factor. The construction $Fh = \langle Rh, Th \rangle$ makes this explicit.
