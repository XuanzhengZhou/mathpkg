There is an evident isomorphism of categories
$$(B \times C)^{\text{op}} \cong B^{\text{op}} \times C^{\text{op}}.$$

The isomorphism sends an object $\langle b, c \rangle$ of $(B \times C)^{\text{op}}$ to the object $\langle b, c \rangle$ of $B^{\text{op}} \times C^{\text{op}}$, and an arrow $\langle f, g \rangle^{\text{op}}: \langle b', c' \rangle \to \langle b, c \rangle$ in $(B \times C)^{\text{op}}$ (where $f: b \to b'$ in $B$, $g: c \to c'$ in $C$) to the arrow $\langle f^{\text{op}}, g^{\text{op}} \rangle: \langle b', c' \rangle \to \langle b, c \rangle$ in $B^{\text{op}} \times C^{\text{op}}$.
