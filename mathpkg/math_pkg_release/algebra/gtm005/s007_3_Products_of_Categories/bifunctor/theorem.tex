A \textbf{bifunctor} (or functor of two variable objects) on categories $B$ and $C$ with values in $D$ is a functor
$$S: B \times C \to D$$
from the product category $B \times C$ to $D$.

For example, the Cartesian product $X \times Y$ of two sets $X$ and $Y$ is the object function of a bifunctor $\text{Set} \times \text{Set} \to \text{Set}$.
