A bifunctor contravariant in $B$ and covariant in $C$ is a functor $B^{\text{op}} \times C \to D$. By using the opposite category $B^{\text{op}}$ in the first coordinate, the variance is built into the domain. The most important example is the hom-functor $\hom_C: C^{\text{op}} \times C \to \text{Set}$, which is contravariant in its first argument and covariant in its second.
