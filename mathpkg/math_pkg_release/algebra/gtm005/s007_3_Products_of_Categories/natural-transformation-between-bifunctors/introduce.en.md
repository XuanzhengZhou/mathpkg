A natural transformation between bifunctors $S, S': B \times C \to D$ generalizes ordinary natural transformations to the two-variable setting. It consists of a family of arrows $\alpha(b, c): S(b, c) \to S'(b, c)$ indexed by pairs of objects, such that the naturality square commutes for every pair of arrows $(f, g)$. Equivalently, this is just a natural transformation in the functor category $D^{B \times C}$.
