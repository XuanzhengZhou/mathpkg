A full subcategory $A$ of $B$ is reflective if the inclusion functor has a left adjoint $F$, called the reflector. The unit $\eta_b: b \to Fb$ provides a universal arrow from each object $b$ to the subcategory $A$. Classical examples: Ab is reflective in Grp (via the factor-commutator group), complete metric spaces are reflective in metric spaces (via completion), and compact Hausdorff spaces are reflective in completely regular spaces (via Stone-Cech compactification). Dually, a coreflective subcategory is one whose inclusion has a right adjoint.
