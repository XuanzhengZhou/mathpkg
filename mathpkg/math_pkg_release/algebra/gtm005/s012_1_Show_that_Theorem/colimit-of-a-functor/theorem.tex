A \textbf{colimit} of $F: J \rightarrow C$ is a universal cocone, i.e., a natural transformation $\mu: F \xrightarrow{\cdot} \Delta(\operatorname{colim} F)$ such that any other cocone $\nu: F \xrightarrow{\cdot} \Delta c$ factors uniquely as $\nu = \Delta f \circ \mu$ for a unique $f: \operatorname{colim} F \rightarrow c$. Colimits are limits in $C^{\text{op}}$.
