If a functor $F: X \to A$ is a left adjoint to an additive functor $G: A \to X$ between Ab-categories, then $F$ is also additive. The proof uses the universal property of the unit of the adjunction and the additivity of $G$ to show $F(g + g') = Fg + Fg'$. Dually, any right adjoint of an additive functor is also additive. This theorem ensures that adjunctions between Ab-categories automatically respect the additive structure, which is essential for homological algebra and the theory of abelian categories.
