A full subcategory $A$ of $B$ is \emph{coreflective} in $B$ when the inclusion functor $K: A \to B$ has a right adjoint. The right adjoint is called the \emph{coreflector}. For example, the full subcategory of all torsion abelian groups is coreflective in Ab, with the coreflector sending each abelian group $A$ to the subgroup $TA$ of all elements of finite order in $A$.
