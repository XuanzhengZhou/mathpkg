A coreflective subcategory is the dual of a reflective subcategory: its inclusion functor has a right adjoint. An example is the full subcategory of torsion abelian groups in Ab, where the coreflector sends each abelian group to its torsion subgroup. Reflective and coreflective subcategories are important because they allow one to "approximate" objects of a larger category by objects of a smaller, better-behaved subcategory, with the (co)reflector providing a canonical best approximation.
