Given a functor $F: J \rightarrow C$, a \textbf{cone} over $F$ with vertex $c \in C$ is a natural transformation $\lambda: \Delta c \xrightarrow{\cdot} F$ from the constant functor. A \textbf{limit} of $F$ is a universal cone, i.e., a cone $\lambda: \Delta(\lim F) \xrightarrow{\cdot} F$ such that any other cone $\mu: \Delta c \xrightarrow{\cdot} F$ factors uniquely as $\mu = \lambda \circ \Delta f$ for a unique $f: c \rightarrow \lim F$.
