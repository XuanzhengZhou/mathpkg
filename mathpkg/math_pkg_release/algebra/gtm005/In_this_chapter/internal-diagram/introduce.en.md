A left $C$-object, also called an internal diagram, generalizes the notion of a set-valued functor $H : C \to \mathbf{Sets}$ to the context of an internal category $C$ in an arbitrary finitely complete category $\mathcal{E}$. Since there is no internal category corresponding to the universe $\mathcal{E}$ itself, one cannot define internal functors into $\mathcal{E}$ the way one defines internal functors between two internal categories. Instead, the object function $C_0 \to \mathcal{E}$ is replaced by a projection $\pi : H \to C_0$, and the arrow function is replaced by an action $\mu : C_1 \times_{C_0} H \to H$ satisfying unit and associative laws. In $\mathbf{Set}$, this recovers exactly the usual notion of a functor into $\mathbf{Set}$.
