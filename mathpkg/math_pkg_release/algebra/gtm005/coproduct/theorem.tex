A \textbf{coproduct} of two objects $a, b$ in a category $C$ is an object $a \amalg b$ (also written $a + b$) together with arrows $i: a \to a \amalg b$ and $j: b \to a \amalg b$ (the \textbf{injections}) such that for any object $d$ and arrows $f: a \to d$, $g: b \to d$, there is a unique arrow $h: a \amalg b \to d$ with $h \circ i = f$ and $h \circ j = g$. This yields a natural bijection
$$C(a \amalg b, d) \cong C(a, d) \times C(b, d).$$
