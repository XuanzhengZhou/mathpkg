The Symmetric Coherence Theorem, also known as Mac Lane's coherence theorem for symmetric monoidal categories, states that all formal diagrams built from the associativity isomorphism $\alpha$ and the symmetry isomorphism $\gamma$ commute. This means that between any two tensor words related by a permutation of their arguments, there is exactly one canonical natural isomorphism obtained by composing instances of $\alpha$ and $\gamma$. The proof reduces the problem to checking commutation for the defining relations of the symmetric group $S_n$, thereby establishing that the symmetric group acts coherently on iterated tensor products in any symmetric monoidal category.
