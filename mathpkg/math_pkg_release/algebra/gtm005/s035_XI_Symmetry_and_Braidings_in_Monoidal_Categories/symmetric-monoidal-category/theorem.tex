A \textbf{symmetric monoidal category} is a monoidal category $M$ equipped with a braiding $\gamma$ such that for all objects $a, b$ in $M$,
$$\gamma_{b,a} \circ \gamma_{a,b} = 1_{a \square b} : a \square b \to a \square b.$$
Equivalently, $\gamma^2 = 1$, so that the braiding is its own inverse. In this case, either one of the two hexagonal diagrams for the braiding implies the other.
