A symmetric monoidal category is a monoidal category whose tensor product is commutative up to a natural isomorphism that is its own inverse. Unlike strict monoidal categories where associativity can be strictified, the symmetry map cannot generally be made into an identity. The key result for symmetric monoidal categories is the symmetric coherence theorem, which states that all formal diagrams built from the associativity, unit maps, and the symmetry commute, meaning there is exactly one canonical morphism between any two tensor words related by a permutation of the factors.
