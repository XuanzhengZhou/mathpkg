The \textbf{free group monad} in $\mathbf{Set}$ is the monad defined by the adjunction
$$\langle F, G, \varphi \rangle : \mathbf{Set} \rightharpoonup \mathbf{Grp}$$
where $G: \mathbf{Grp} \to \mathbf{Set}$ is the forgetful functor and
$F: \mathbf{Set} \to \mathbf{Grp}$ is the free group functor.
Concretely, the endofunctor $T = GF: \mathbf{Set} \to \mathbf{Set}$ sends each set $S$
to the underlying set of the free group on $S$, i.e., the set of all reduced words
on the alphabet $S \sqcup S^{-1}$.

The unit $\eta_S: S \to T S$ sends each element $s \in S$ to the word consisting
of the single generator $s$. The multiplication $\mu_S: T^2 S \to T S$ simplifies
a word of words into a single reduced word by concatenation and reduction
(cancelling adjacent inverse pairs).
