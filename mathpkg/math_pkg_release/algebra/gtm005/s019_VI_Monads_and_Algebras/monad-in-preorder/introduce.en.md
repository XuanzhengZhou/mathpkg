In the simple setting of a preorder $P$ (regarded as a thin category), a monad reduces to a familiar concept: a closure operator. An endofunctor $T: P \to P$ is merely a monotonic function, and the existence of natural transformations $\eta$ and $\mu$ is equivalent to the conditions $x \leq Tx$ and $T(Tx) \leq Tx$ for all $x$. Since in a preorder all diagrams commute automatically, these two inequalities completely characterize monads. This example shows that closure operations (such as topological closure or the algebraic closure of a field) are instances of the monad concept.
