A morphism of $T$-algebras $f: \langle x, h \rangle \to \langle x', h' \rangle$ is an arrow $f: x \to x'$ in the base category that respects the algebra structure: applying $T$ to $f$ and then the structure map $h'$ yields the same result as applying $h$ first and then $f$. This is the natural notion of a homomorphism between algebras for a monad, generalizing the usual notion of homomorphism in universal algebra. Together with $T$-algebras, these morphisms form the Eilenberg-Moore category $X^T$.
