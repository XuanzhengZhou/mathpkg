Let $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$ be an adjunction with
$F: X \to A$, $G: A \to X$, unit $\eta: I_X \to GF$, and counit $\varepsilon: FG \to I_A$.
Then the composite endofunctor $T = GF: X \to X$, together with the unit $\eta: I_X \to T = GF$
and the natural transformation
$$\mu = G\varepsilon F: T^2 = GFGF \to GF = T$$
defines a monad $\langle GF, \eta, G\varepsilon F \rangle$ in the category $X$.
This is called the \textbf{monad defined by the adjunction}.
