A $T$-algebra (or Eilenberg-Moore algebra) for a monad $T = \langle T, \eta, \mu \rangle$ on a category $X$ is an object $x \in X$ equipped with a structure map $h: Tx \to x$ that is compatible with the monad structure. The compatibility is expressed by two commutative diagrams: an associative law relating $h$ to the monad multiplication $\mu$, and a unit law relating $h$ to the monad unit $\eta$. $T$-algebras generalize the notion of an algebraic structure: for example, algebras for the free group monad on $\mathbf{Set}$ are precisely groups.
