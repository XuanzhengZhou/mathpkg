The Eilenberg-Moore theorem shows that every monad $T$ on a category $X$ arises from an adjunction, namely the free-forgetful adjunction between $X$ and the category $X^T$ of $T$-algebras. The category $X^T$ has $T$-algebras as objects and structure-preserving maps as morphisms. The forgetful functor $G^T$ sends each algebra to its underlying object, while the free functor $F^T$ sends each object $x$ to the free algebra $\langle Tx, \mu_x \rangle$. This construction establishes that monads are exactly the structures that encode algebraic theories.
