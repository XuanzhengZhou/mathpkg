A monoidal category generalizes the strict monoidal category by replacing the equalities of associativity and unit laws with natural isomorphisms $\alpha$ (associativity), $\lambda$ (left unit), and $\varrho$ (right unit). These isomorphisms must satisfy coherence conditions: the pentagon axiom, which ensures that all ways of reassociating a product of four objects are equal, and the triangle axiom, which relates the unit isomorphisms to the associativity isomorphism. The condition $\lambda_e = \varrho_e$ is also required. Familiar examples include any category with finite products (taking $\square$ as the product and $e$ as the terminal object) and the category of abelian groups with the tensor product.
