A \textbf{monoidal category} is a category $B$ equipped with a bifunctor $\square : B \times B \rightarrow B$, an object $e \in B$, and three natural isomorphisms
\begin{align*}
\alpha_{a,b,c} &: a \square (b \square c) \cong (a \square b) \square c, \\
\lambda_a &: e \square a \cong a, \\
\varrho_a &: a \square e \cong a,
\end{align*}
such that the following two coherence diagrams commute.

\textbf{Pentagon axiom:} For all objects $a, b, c, d \in B$,
\[
\begin{CD}
a \square (b \square (c \square d)) @>\alpha>> (a \square b) \square (c \square d) @>\alpha>> ((a \square b) \square c) \square d \\
@V{1 \square \alpha}VV @VV{\alpha \square 1}V \\
a \square ((b \square c) \square d) @>>\alpha> (a \square (b \square c)) \square d
\end{CD}
\]

\textbf{Triangle axiom:} For all objects $a, c \in B$,
\[
\begin{CD}
a \square (e \square c) @>\alpha>> (a \square e) \square c \\
@V{1 \square \lambda}VV @VV{\varrho \square 1}V \\
a \square c @= a \square c
\end{CD}
\]

Moreover, $\lambda_e = \varrho_e : e \square e \rightarrow e$.
