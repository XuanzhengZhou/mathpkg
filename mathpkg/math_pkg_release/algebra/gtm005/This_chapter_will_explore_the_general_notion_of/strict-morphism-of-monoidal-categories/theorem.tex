A \textbf{strict morphism of monoidal categories}
$$T: (B, \square, e, \alpha, \lambda, \varrho) \rightarrow (B', \square', e', \alpha', \lambda', \varrho')$$
is a functor $T: B \rightarrow B'$ such that, for all $a, b, c, f$, and $g$,
$$T(a \square b) = Ta \square' Tb, \quad T(f \square g) = Tf \square' Tg, \quad Te = e',$$
$$T\alpha_{a,b,c} = \alpha'_{Ta,Tb,Tc}, \quad T\lambda_a = \lambda'_{Ta}, \quad T\varrho_a = \varrho'_{Ta}.$$
