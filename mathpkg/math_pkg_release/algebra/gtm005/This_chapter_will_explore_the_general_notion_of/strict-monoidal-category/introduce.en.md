A strict monoidal category is a category equipped with a strictly associative bifunctor and a strict two-sided identity object. The bifunctor, denoted generically by $\square$, assigns to each pair of objects and arrows a product object and product arrow respectively, satisfying the interchange law. The associative law holds as an equality of functors, and the unit object $e$ satisfies $e \square c = c = c \square e$ for all objects and arrows. This is the simplest version of a monoidal category, where all coherence isomorphisms are identities.
