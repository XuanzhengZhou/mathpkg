A natural transformation is a morphism between two parallel functors $S, T: C \rightarrow B$. It provides a way to translate the "picture" of $C$ given by $S$ into the picture given by $T$, requiring that all naturality squares commute. This concept is arguably the central innovation of category theory, distinguishing it from the mere study of categories and functors. The commutativity condition ensures that the family of arrows $\{\tau_c\}_{c \in C}$ is compatible with the internal structure of the source category $C$.
