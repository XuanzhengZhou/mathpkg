The category $\text{Finord}$ of finite ordinals is a skeleton of the category $\text{Set}_f$ of finite sets: each isomorphism class of finite sets contains exactly one ordinal. The inclusion $S$ and the cardinality functor $\#$ together with the natural isomorphism $\theta$ provide an explicit equivalence of categories, demonstrating that equivalence (rather than isomorphism) is the correct notion of sameness for categories. Note that $S \circ \# $ is not the identity functor on $\text{Set}_f$ since it sends each finite set to a specific ordinal of the same cardinality, yet it is naturally isomorphic to the identity.
