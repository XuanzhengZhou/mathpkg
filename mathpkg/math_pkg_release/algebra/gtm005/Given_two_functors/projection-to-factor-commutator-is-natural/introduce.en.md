The canonical projection from a group to its abelianization (factor-commutator group) is a classic example of a natural transformation. It maps the identity functor on groups to the composite of the abelianization functor with the inclusion of abelian groups back into groups. The naturality square commutes because any group homomorphism $f: G \to H$ induces a well-defined map $f'$ on the quotient by commutator subgroups, since $f([G,G]) \subseteq [H,H]$.
