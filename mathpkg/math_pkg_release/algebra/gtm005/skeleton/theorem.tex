A \textbf{skeleton} of a category $C$ is a full subcategory $A$ such that each object of $C$ is isomorphic (in $C$) to exactly one object of $A$. A category $C$ is equivalent to any of its skeletons; the inclusion $K: A \to C$ is an equivalence of categories. For example, the category of all finite sets has as a skeleton the full subcategory with objects all finite ordinal numbers $0, 1, 2, \ldots, n, \ldots$.
