(This theorem has no separate proof block in the source text. The construction follows directly from Proposition 1: for each morphism $f: p \to q$ in $P$, the natural transformation $T(f, -, -): T(p, -, -) \Rightarrow T(q, -, -)$ induces, via Proposition 1, a unique arrow $\int_c T(f, c, c): \int_c T(p, c, c) \to \int_c T(q, c, c)$. The functoriality of these arrows follows from the functoriality rule in Proposition 1. The uniqueness of $U$ follows from the requirement that it be a functor and that its object function be as specified.)
