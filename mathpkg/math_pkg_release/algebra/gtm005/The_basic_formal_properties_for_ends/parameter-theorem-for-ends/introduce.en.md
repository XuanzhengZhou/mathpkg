The Parameter Theorem shows that an end depending on an extra parameter $p$ is itself functorial in that parameter. Given a trifunctor $T(p, c, c')$ where each $p$-slice has an end, one obtains a functor $U: P \to X$ sending $p \mapsto \int_c T(p, c, c)$, with the arrow part $Uf$ defined by applying Proposition 1 (the end of a natural transformation) to the natural transformation $T(f, -, -)$ induced by a morphism $f$ in $P$. This is the analogue for ends of the fact that pointwise limits in a functor category are computed pointwise, and it justifies the integral notation $\int_c T(-, c, c)$ as an object of the functor category $X^P$.
