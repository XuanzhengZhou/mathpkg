This continuation of the Parameter Theorem reformulates the functorial dependence on the parameter $p$ by transposing the trifunctor $T$ across the exponential adjunction for categories. Instead of viewing the end as producing a functor $U: P \to X$ (Theorem 2), one views $\int_c T(-, c, c)$ as an object of the functor category $X^P$ and the universal wedge $\omega^\#$ as living in $X^P$. The components $(\omega_c^\#)_p = (\omega_p)_c$ connect the two perspectives: the $p$-component of the $X^P$-wedge is simply the $c$-component of the original $X$-wedge for the slice $T(p, -, -)$. This makes precise the integral notation $\int_c T(-, c, c)$ as a functor rather than a mere object function.
