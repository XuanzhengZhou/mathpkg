Proposition 3 shows that every ordinary limit can be expressed as an end by introducing a dummy contravariant variable: $\int_c Tc \cong \operatorname{Lim} T$. The proof observes that when $S$ is dummy in its first variable (i.e., $S(c,c') = Tc'$), the wedge condition reduces exactly to the naturality condition for a cone. This unifies the notation and theory of limits and ends, allowing any limit to be written as an integral.
