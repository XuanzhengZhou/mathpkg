Theorem 1 of Chapter IX shows that the existence of arbitrary small coproducts in a category reduces to having finite coproducts and colimits over directed preorders. Given a functor $F: J \to C$ on a discrete category $J$, one constructs an associated functor $F^+$ on the directed preorder $J^+$ of finite subsets of $J$, where $F^+(S) = \coprod_{s \in S} Fs$. Then the colimit of $F^+$ yields the desired coproduct.
