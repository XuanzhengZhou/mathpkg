The subdivision category $C^{\S}$ is a technical construction that converts the problem of computing an end into that of computing an ordinary limit. For each arrow $f: b \to c$ in $C$, it introduces an object $f^{\S}$ together with arrows from $b^{\S}$ and $c^{\S}$, capturing the dinaturality square condition. A functor $S: C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to X$ induces a functor $S^{\S}: C^{\S} \to X$, and a cone over $S^{\S}$ is exactly the same as a wedge over $S$, proving that ends are limits.
