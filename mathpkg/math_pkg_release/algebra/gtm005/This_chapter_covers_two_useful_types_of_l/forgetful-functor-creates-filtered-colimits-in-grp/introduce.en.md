Proposition 2 states that the forgetful functor from groups to sets creates filtered colimits. Given a filtered diagram of groups whose underlying sets have a colimit $S$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, there is a unique group structure on $S$ making all the structure maps into homomorphisms. The proof uses the filteredness condition to transport elements into a common group where multiplication is well-defined, and associativity follows from the same transport argument.
