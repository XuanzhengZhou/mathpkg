A dinatural transformation is an extension of the concept of natural transformation to bifunctors $S, T: C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to B$ that are contravariant in the first variable and covariant in the second. Rather than requiring separate naturality in each variable (which is impossible due to mixed variance), dinaturality imposes a single hexagonal commutativity condition. Special cases include natural transformations between a contravariant and covariant functor, and the identity transformation $1: * \to \hom(-,-)$. Dinatural transformations are essential for the definition of ends.
