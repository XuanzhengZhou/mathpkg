A functor $H: X \rightarrow Y$ is said to **preserve the end** of a functor $S: C^{\text{op}} \times C \rightarrow X$ when $\omega: e \mathbin{\dot{\rightarrow}} S$ an end of $S$ in $X$ implies that $H\omega: He \mathbin{\dot{\rightarrow}} HS$ is an end for $HS$ in $Y$; in symbols

$$H \int_c S(c, c) \cong \int_c H S(c, c).$$
