A preorder $P$ is said to be **directed** when any two elements $p, q \in P$ have an upper bound in $P$; that is, an $r$ with $p \leq r$ and $q \leq r$ (there is no requirement that $r$ be unique). It follows that any finite set of elements of $P$ has an upper bound in $P$. A directed preorder is also called a "directed set" or a "filtered set".
