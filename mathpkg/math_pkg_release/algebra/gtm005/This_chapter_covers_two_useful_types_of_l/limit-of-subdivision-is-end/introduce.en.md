Proposition 1 demonstrates that every end is manifestly a limit — specifically, a limit of a diagram in $X$ constructed from the pieces $S(b,b) \to S(b,c) \leftarrow S(c,c)$, one for each arrow $f: b \to c$ in $C$. This reduction shows that ends exist whenever the corresponding ordinary limit exists, allowing one to apply standard theorems about limits to ends.
