(Converse of the Theorem of the text.) Let $L: J' \rightarrow J$ be a functor, where $J'$ and $J$ have small hom-sets, such that for every $F: J \rightarrow X$ with $X$ cocomplete the canonical map $\operatorname{Colim} FL \rightarrow \operatorname{Colim} F$ is an isomorphism. Prove that $L$ must be final. (Hint: Use $F = J(k, -)$, $X = \text{Set}$, and Exercise 4.)
