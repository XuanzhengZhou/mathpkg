A category with a fixed set $O$ of objects can be described algebraically as an $O$-graph $A$ together with two $O$-graph morphisms: a composition $c: A \times_O A \to A$ and an identity $i: O \to A$, satisfying the associativity and unit commutative diagrams. This definition, due to Mac Lane, recasts the standard definition of a category in the language of internal structures. By replacing $\mathbf{Set}$ with another category $\mathcal{V}$ and the cartesian product with a suitable monoidal structure, one obtains the notion of a category enriched over $\mathcal{V}$, a fundamental concept in modern category theory.
