A \textbf{congruence} on a category $C$ is a family of relations $R_{a,b}$ on each hom-set $C(a, b)$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] Each $R_{a,b}$ is an equivalence relation on $C(a, b)$.
\item[(ii)] If $f, f': a \to b$ satisfy $f R_{a,b} f'$, then for all $g: a' \to a$ and $h: b \to b'$, one has $(h \circ f \circ g) R_{a',b'} (h \circ f' \circ g)$.
\end{enumerate}
In other words, the equivalence is compatible with composition on both sides.
