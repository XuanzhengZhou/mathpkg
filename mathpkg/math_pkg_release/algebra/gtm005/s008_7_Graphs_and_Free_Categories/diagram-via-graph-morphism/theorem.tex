If $G$ is any graph, a diagram of the shape $G$ in a category $B$ is a morphism $D: G \to UB$ of graphs. By Theorem 1, these morphisms $D$ correspond exactly to functors $D': C_G \to B$ via the bijection $D' \mapsto D = UD' \circ P$. This bijection

$$\mathbf{Cat}(C_G, B) \cong \mathbf{Grph}(G, UB)$$

is natural in $G$ and $B$.
