\textbf{Theorem 1.} Let $G$ be a small graph. There is a small category $C = C_G$ with $O$ as set of objects and a morphism $P: G \to UC$ of graphs such that: for any category $B$ and any morphism $D: G \to UB$ of graphs, there is a unique functor $D': C \to B$ with $(UD') \circ P = D$. Moreover, if $B$ has $O$ as set of objects and $D$ is a morphism of $O$-graphs, then $D'$ is the identity on objects.

Equivalently, $P: G \to UC$ is an initial object in the comma category $(G \downarrow U)$, where $U: \mathbf{Cat} \to \mathbf{Grph}$ is the forgetful functor. Hence $P$ is unique up to isomorphism and universal among morphisms from $G$ to $U$.
