The free category construction $C: \mathbf{Grph} \to \mathbf{Cat}$ is left adjoint to the forgetful functor $U: \mathbf{Cat} \to \mathbf{Grph}$ that sends each category to its underlying directed graph. The natural bijection $\mathbf{Cat}(C_G, B) \cong \mathbf{Grph}(G, UB)$ means that functors out of the free category on a graph $G$ correspond precisely to graph homomorphisms from $G$ into the underlying graph of $B$. This adjunction is a prototypical example of a free-forgetful adjunction in category theory.
