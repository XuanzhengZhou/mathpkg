A category can be defined internally as an O-graph equipped with composition and identity morphisms satisfying commutative diagrams. This reveals that a category is a monoid in the monoidal category of O-graphs (with product x_O). The composition sends each composable pair to its composite, while the identity assigns to each object its identity arrow. This is the starting point for enriched category theory.
