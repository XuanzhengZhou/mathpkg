For a category $C$ with small hom-sets and each object $b \in C$, the \textbf{contravariant hom-functor} is written covariantly as

$$
C(-, b) = \hom(-, b): C^{\text{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}.
$$

For a category $C$ without small hom-sets, take a set $V$ large enough to include all subsets of the set of arrows of $C$. Let $\mathbf{Ens} = \mathbf{Set}_V$ be the category with objects all sets $X \in V$ and arrows all functions $f: X \to Y$ between two such sets. Then each hom-set $C(a, b) = \hom(a, b)$ is an object of $\mathbf{Ens}$, and the two hom-functors are

$$
C(a, -): C \to \mathbf{Ens}, \qquad C(-, b): C^{\text{op}} \to \mathbf{Ens}. \tag{7}
$$

In particular, when $V$ is the universe of all small sets, $\mathbf{Ens} = \mathbf{Set}$.
