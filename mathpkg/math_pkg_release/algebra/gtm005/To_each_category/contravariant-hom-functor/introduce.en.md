The contravariant hom-functor $C(-, b): C^{\text{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}$ is the contravariant counterpart of the covariant hom-functor. For a fixed object $b$, it sends each object $a$ to the hom-set $\hom(a, b)$ and each morphism $h: a \to a'$ to the pre-composition map $h^*: g \mapsto g \circ h$. Formally, it is expressed as a covariant functor from the opposite category $C^{\text{op}}$, following the convention that all functors are covariant. Together with the covariant hom-functor, it forms the two-variable hom-functor $\hom(-, -): C^{\text{op}} \times C \to \mathbf{Set}$, a bifunctor that is fundamental to enriched category theory and the Yoneda embedding.
