The opposite category $C^{\text{op}}$ is a fundamental construction in category theory obtained by formally reversing the direction of every arrow in a given category $C$. The objects remain unchanged, but each arrow $f: a \to b$ becomes an arrow $f^{\text{op}}: b \to a$ in the opposite direction, with composition adjusted accordingly. This construction enables the duality principle: any categorical statement remains valid when all arrows are reversed, because proving it in $C^{\text{op}}$ is equivalent to proving the dual statement in $C$. The opposite category provides the natural setting for defining contravariant functors as ordinary covariant functors out of $C^{\text{op}}$.
