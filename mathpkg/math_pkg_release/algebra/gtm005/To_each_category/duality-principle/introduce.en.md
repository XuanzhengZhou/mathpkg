The duality principle is a meta-theorem that establishes a systematic correspondence between statements in a category $C$ and their dual statements in the opposite category $C^{\text{op}}$. By induction on the construction of formulas in the elementary theory of abstract categories, it shows that any statement $\Sigma$ holds for arrows in $C$ if and only if the dual statement $\Sigma^*$ holds for the corresponding opposite arrows in $C^{\text{op}}$. This principle is the logical foundation for "proof by duality" in category theory: once a theorem is proved, its dual follows automatically without additional work, simply by interpreting it in the opposite category.
