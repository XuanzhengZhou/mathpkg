A \textbf{contravariant functor} $\bar{S}$ on $C$ to $B$ is represented as a covariant functor $S: C^{\text{op}} \to B$, or sometimes as a covariant functor $S^{\text{op}}: C \to B^{\text{op}}$.

To contrast, a functor $T: C \to B$ as previously defined is called a \textbf{covariant functor} on $C$ to $B$.
