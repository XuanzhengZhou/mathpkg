This corollary provides a crisp characterization: a $W$-algebra is nothing more than a semigroup $\langle S, v_2 \rangle$ together with all its iterated operations $v_n$, where $v_1$ is forced to be the identity and each $v_{n+1}$ is obtained by iterating $v_2$. As a result, the comparison functor $K: \mathbf{Smgrp} \to \mathbf{Set}^W$ is an isomorphism of categories — it merely repackages a single associative binary operation as the full family of its derived $n$-ary operations.
