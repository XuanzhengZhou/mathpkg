A semigroup is an algebraic structure consisting of a set together with an associative binary operation. It generalizes the notion of a monoid by dropping the requirement of an identity element. Semigroups serve as the motivating example in Mac Lane's construction of the word monad $W$ and the illustration of the comparison functor between the category of semigroups and the Eilenberg-Moore category of $W$-algebras.
