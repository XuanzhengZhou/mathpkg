A final functor is the categorical generalization of a cofinal subset in a preorder. The condition that each comma category $(k \downarrow L)$ is connected and nonempty ensures that colimits over $J$ can be computed by restricting to $J'$. This concept replaces the classical "cofinal" terminology while dropping the "co" prefix since the notion is not defined by dualization. Final functors are essential for comparing colimits over different indexing categories and appear naturally when studying filtered colimits and their preservation properties.
