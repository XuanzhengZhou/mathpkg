Theorem 1 states that in any category, the existence of finite coproducts together with colimits over all small directed preorders implies the existence of all small coproducts. The proof constructs an arbitrary coproduct as a filtered colimit of finite coproducts: given a discrete diagram indexed by a set $J$, one forms the directed preorder $J^+$ of finite subsets of $J$ ordered by inclusion, and defines a functor $F^+$ sending each finite subset to the coproduct over its elements. The colimit of $F^+$ then yields the desired coproduct. This result shows that directed colimits are the essential "infinite" ingredient needed beyond finite colimits.
