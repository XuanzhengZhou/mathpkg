A dinatural transformation generalizes ordinary natural transformations from functors of one variable to bifunctors $C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to B$. While an ordinary natural transformation involves commuting squares, a dinatural transformation involves commuting hexagons that mix the contravariant and covariant arguments. This concept is essential for defining ends and coends, which are limits and colimits of bifunctors with mixed variance. Dinaturality also captures familiar mathematical phenomena such as the natural isomorphism between a Euclidean vector space and its dual, and the evaluation map $\mathrm{hom}(X, A) \times X \to A$.
