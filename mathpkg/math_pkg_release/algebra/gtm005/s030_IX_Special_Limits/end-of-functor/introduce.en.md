An end is a universal dinatural wedge from a constant functor to a bifunctor $S : C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to X$. Written with the suggestive integral notation $\int_c S(c, c)$, ends capture the idea of "integrating" over the category $C$ with the contravariant and covariant variables identified. The integral notation reflects that the bound variable $c$ is "integrated out" -- the result no longer depends on $c$. Ends unify many categorical constructions: natural transformations are ends of hom-functors, limits are ends over dummy variables, and the calculus of ends provides a powerful tool for manipulating functor categories.
