Conversely, any complete semi-lattice $Q$ carries a canonical $\mathcal{P}$-algebra structure. The algebra structure map $h : \mathcal{P}Q \to Q$ is defined by taking the supremum: $h(S) = \sup S$ for any subset $S \subset Q$. The monad unit condition $h \circ \eta_Q = \mathrm{id}_Q$ follows because the supremum of a singleton is the element itself, and the associativity condition $h \circ \mathcal{P}h = h \circ \mu_Q$ follows because the supremum of a union of sets equals the supremum of their individual suprema. Together with part (b), this establishes the equivalence between $\mathcal{P}$-algebras and complete semi-lattices.
