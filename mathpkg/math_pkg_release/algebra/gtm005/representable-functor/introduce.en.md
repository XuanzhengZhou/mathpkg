A functor $K: D \to \mathbf{Set}$ is representable if it is naturally isomorphic to a covariant hom-functor $D(r, -)$. The representing object $r$ serves as a classifying object: elements of $Kd$ correspond naturally to arrows $r \to d$. Representability connects universal properties, adjunctions, and the Yoneda Lemma.
