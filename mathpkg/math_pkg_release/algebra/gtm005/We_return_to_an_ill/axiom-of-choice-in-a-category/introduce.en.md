The categorical axiom of choice states that every epimorphism (surjection) admits a section (right inverse). In a well-pointed topos, this corresponds to the classical set-theoretic axiom of choice: for every surjective function $f: a \to b$, there exists a choice function $\tau: b \to a$ that selects a preimage for each element of $b$. This formulation makes the axiom of choice available in the categorical foundation for mathematics.
