An internal category in the category of groups is a category object where both the object of objects $C_0$ and the object of morphisms $C_1$ are groups, and all structural maps (domain, codomain, identity, composition) are group homomorphisms. The key additional condition beyond the usual category axioms is that the kernels of the domain and codomain maps commute elementwise, i.e., $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$. This commutativity condition encodes the interchange law for composition in a group-enriched setting and is essential for the equivalence with crossed modules.
