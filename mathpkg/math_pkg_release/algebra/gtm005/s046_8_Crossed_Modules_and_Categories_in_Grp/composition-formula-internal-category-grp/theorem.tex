In an internal category in $\mathbf{Grp}$ with domain and codomain homomorphisms $d_0, d_1 : C_1 \rightarrow C_0$ and identity $i : C_0 \rightarrow C_1$, the categorical composition $\gamma$ of a composable pair $a \xrightarrow{f} b \xrightarrow{g} c$ is determined by the group multiplication in $C_1$:

$$g \circ f = f \, 1_b^{-1} \, g = g \, 1_b^{-1} \, f,$$

where $1_b = i(b) \in C_1$ is the identity arrow at $b \in C_0$. Moreover,

$$1_b^{-1} \circ 1_b^{-1} = 1_b^{-1}.$$

In particular, if $b$ is the identity element of the group $C_0$, then $1_b$ is the identity element of $C_1$, and for $f \in \ker d_1$, $g \in \ker d_0$ one has $fg = gf$, which implies the commutator condition $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$.
