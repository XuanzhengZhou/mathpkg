Given an internal category in $\mathbf{Grp}$ with groups $C_0$, $C_1$ and homomorphisms $d_0, d_1 : C_1 \rightarrow C_0$, $i : C_0 \rightarrow C_1$ satisfying $d_0 i = 1 = d_1 i$ and $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$, set

$$H = \ker d_0, \quad P = C_0,$$

and let $\alpha : H \rightarrow P$ be the restriction of $d_1$ to $\ker d_0$. The sequence

$$1 \longrightarrow \ker d_0 \longrightarrow C_1 \xrightarrow{d_0} C_0 \longrightarrow 1$$

is a short exact sequence of groups, which determines an action of $P = C_0$ on $H = \ker d_0$ in the standard way (by conjugation in $C_1$). Under this action and the homomorphism $\alpha$, the pair $(H, P)$ forms a crossed module.
