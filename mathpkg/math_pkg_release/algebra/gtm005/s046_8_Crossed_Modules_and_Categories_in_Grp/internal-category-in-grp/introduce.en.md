An internal category in the category of groups $\mathbf{Grp}$ is a category object whose objects and arrows both form groups, and for which the structure maps (domain, codomain, identity, composition) are group homomorphisms. The key additional condition imposed by the group structure is that the kernels of the domain and codomain maps must commute elementwise, i.e., $[\ker d_0, \ker d_1] = 1$. This commutator condition turns out to be exactly what allows an internal category in $\mathbf{Grp}$ to be equivalent to a crossed module.
