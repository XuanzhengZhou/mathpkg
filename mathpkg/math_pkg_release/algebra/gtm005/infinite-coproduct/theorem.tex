For a set $X$ and an $X$-indexed family $\{a_x\}_{x \in X}$ of objects of $C$, an \textbf{$X$-fold coproduct} is an object $\coprod_{x \in X} a_x$ together with \textbf{injection} arrows $i_x: a_x \to \coprod_x a_x$ yielding a natural bijection
$$C(\coprod_x a_x, c) \cong \prod_{x \in X} C(a_x, c).$$
This is a universal arrow from the family $\{a_x\}$ to the diagonal functor $\Delta: C \to C^X$ (where $X$ is regarded as a discrete category).
