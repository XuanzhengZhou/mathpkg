For a set $J$ and a $J$-indexed family $\{a_j\}_{j \in J}$ of objects, an \textbf{infinite product} is an object $\prod_{j \in J} a_j$ together with \textbf{projection} arrows $p_j: \prod_j a_j \to a_j$ yielding a natural bijection
$$C(c, \prod_j a_j) \cong \prod_{j \in J} C(c, a_j).$$
A map to the product with components $f_j: c \to a_j$ is the unique arrow $f: c \to \prod_j a_j$ with $p_j f = f_j$ for all $j$.
