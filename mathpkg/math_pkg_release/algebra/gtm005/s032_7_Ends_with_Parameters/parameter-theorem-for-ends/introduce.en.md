Theorem 2 of Mac Lane (Chapter IX, Section 7) is the \textit{Parameter Theorem for ends and limits}. Given a trifunctor $T: P \times C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \rightarrow X$ such that each slice $T(p, -, -)$ has an end, the assignment $p \mapsto \int_c T(p, c, c)$ extends uniquely to a functor $U: P \rightarrow X$. This theorem parallels the familiar fact that a limit depending on a parameter is functorial in that parameter, and it follows from Proposition 1 applied to the natural transformations induced by arrows in $P$. The result justifies the notation $\int_c T(-, c, c)$ as an object of the functor category $X^P$.
