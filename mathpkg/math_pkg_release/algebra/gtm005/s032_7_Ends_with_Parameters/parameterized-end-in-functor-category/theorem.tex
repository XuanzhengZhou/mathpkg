Under the same hypotheses as Theorem 2, let $T: P \times C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \rightarrow X$ be a functor such that each $T(p, -, -)$ has an end. Define $T^{\#}: C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \rightarrow X^P$ as the image of $T$ under the standard adjunction
\[
\mathrm{Cat}(P \times C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C, X) \cong \mathrm{Cat}(C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C, X^P),
\]
given on objects and arrows by $T^{\#}(f, f') = T(-, f, f'): P \rightarrow X$. Then $T^{\#}$ has an end
\[
\omega^{\#}: \int_c T(-, c, c) \rightarrow T^{\#}
\]
in the functor category $X^P$, whose component at each $p \in P$ and $c \in C$ is
\[
(\omega^{\#}_c)_p = (\omega_p)_c: \int_c T(p, c, c) \rightarrow T(p, c, c).
\]
In other words, the parameterized end $\int_c T(-, c, c)$ is itself the end of $T^{\#}$ in $X^P$.
