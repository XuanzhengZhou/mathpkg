A \text{contravariant functor} $\bar{S}$ on a category $C$ to a category $B$ may be represented as a covariant functor $S: C^{\text{op}} \to B$, or equivalently as a covariant functor $S^{\text{op}}: C \to B^{\text{op}}$.

In contrast, a functor $T: C \to B$ as previously defined (in \S I.3) is called a \text{covariant functor} on $C$ to $B$.
