For categories that may not have small hom-sets, one can still define hom-functors by choosing a sufficiently large set $V$ (containing all subsets of the arrow set of $C$) and forming the category $\mathbf{Ens} = \mathbf{Set}_V$ of all sets in $V$. This ensures that each hom-set $\hom(a, b)$ is an object of $\mathbf{Ens}$, yielding hom-functors $C(a, -): C \to \mathbf{Ens}$ and $C(-, b): C^{\text{op}} \to \mathbf{Ens}$. When $V$ is the universe of small sets, this reduces to the usual $\mathbf{Set}$-valued hom-functors.
