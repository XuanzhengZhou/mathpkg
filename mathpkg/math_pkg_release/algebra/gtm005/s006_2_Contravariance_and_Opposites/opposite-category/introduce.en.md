The opposite category $C^{\text{op}}$ is constructed from a given category $C$ by formally reversing the direction of every arrow. The objects remain the same, but each arrow $f: a \to b$ in $C$ becomes an arrow $f^{\text{op}}: b \to a$ in $C^{\text{op}}$, and composition is defined by $f^{\text{op}} g^{\text{op}} = (gf)^{\text{op}}$. This construction is fundamental to categorical duality: any statement about $C$ can be translated into a dual statement about $C^{\text{op}}$ by reversing all arrows and the order of composition.
