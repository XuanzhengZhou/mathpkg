To each category $C$ we associate the \text{opposite category} $C^{\text{op}}$.
The objects of $C^{\text{op}}$ are the objects of $C$.
The arrows of $C^{\text{op}}$ are arrows $f^{\text{op}}$, in one-one correspondence $f \mapsto f^{\text{op}}$ with the arrows $f$ of $C$.
For each arrow $f: a \to b$ of $C$, the domain and codomain of the corresponding $f^{\text{op}}$ are $f^{\text{op}}: b \to a$ (the direction is reversed).
The composite $f^{\text{op}} g^{\text{op}} = (g f)^{\text{op}}$ is defined in $C^{\text{op}}$ exactly when the composite $g f$ is defined in $C$.
This clearly makes $C^{\text{op}}$ a category.
