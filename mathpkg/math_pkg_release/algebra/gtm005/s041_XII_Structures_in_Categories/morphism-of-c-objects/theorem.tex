Let $C$ be an internal category in a finitely complete category $E$, and let $(H, \pi, \mu)$ and $(K, \pi', \mu')$ be left $C$-objects. A \textbf{morphism of $C$-objects} $\phi : H \to K$ is a morphism $\phi : H \to K$ in $E$ such that the following diagrams commute:

\textit{Preservation of projection:}
$$
\begin{CD}
H @>{\phi}>> K \\
@V{\pi}VV @V{\pi'}VV \\
C_0 @= C_0
\end{CD}
$$

\textit{Preservation of the action:}
$$
\begin{CD}
C_1 \times_{C_0} H @>{1 \times \phi}>> C_1 \times_{C_0} K \\
@V{\mu}VV @V{\mu'}VV \\
H @>>{\phi}> K
\end{CD}
$$

When $E = \mathbf{Set}$, this makes $\phi$ exactly a natural transformation between the corresponding set-valued functors $H, K : C \to \mathbf{Set}$.
