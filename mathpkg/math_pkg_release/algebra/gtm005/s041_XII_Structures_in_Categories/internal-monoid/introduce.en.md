An internal monoid generalizes the notion of a monoid from Set to any monoidal category. In $(\mathbf{Set}, \times, 1)$, monoid objects are ordinary monoids. In $(\mathbf{Ab}, \otimes, \mathbb{Z})$, they are rings. In $(\mathbf{Vect}_k, \otimes, k)$, they are $k$-algebras. This unification is one of the triumphs of category theory.
