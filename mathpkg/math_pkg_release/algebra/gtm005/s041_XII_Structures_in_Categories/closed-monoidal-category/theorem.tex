A monoidal category $(V, \otimes, I)$ is \textbf{closed} if for each object $b \in V$, the functor $-\otimes b: V \rightarrow V$ has a right adjoint $[b, -]: V \rightarrow V$, called the \textbf{internal hom}. That is, there is a natural isomorphism
\[
\hom_V(a \otimes b, c) \cong \hom_V(a, [b, c]).
\]
