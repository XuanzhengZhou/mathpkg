This theorem reformulates the pointwise limits result as a statement about creation of limits. The discrete subcategory $|P|$ has only objects and identity morphisms, so the functor category $X^{|P|}$ is simply a $|P|$-indexed product of copies of $X$. The induced functor $i^*$ sends each functor $H : P \rightarrow X$ to its restriction to the discrete subcategory $|P|$. The theorem asserts that $i^*$ creates limits, meaning that limits in $X^P$ exist and are uniquely determined by their pointwise components in $X^{|P|}$.
