A functor preserves limits when it sends limiting cones to limiting cones, not merely limit objects to limit objects. This distinction matters: the image of a limit object under $H$ must also carry the universal property of a limit in the target category $D$, and the image of the limiting cone must realize that universal property. Preservation of limits is the key property enjoyed by right adjoints and hom-functors.
