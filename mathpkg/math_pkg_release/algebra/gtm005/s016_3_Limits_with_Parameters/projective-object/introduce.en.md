A projective object is one for which every morphism into its codomain lifts through any epimorphism. The lifting condition captures the idea that projective objects have "enough room" to factor maps through surjections. The equivalent characterization via the hom-functor — that $\hom(p, -)$ preserves epimorphisms — connects projectivity to the representable-functor perspective. In module categories, the projective objects are precisely the direct summands of free modules.
