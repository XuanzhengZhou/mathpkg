Beck's Theorem, also known as the Precise Tripleability Theorem (PTT), gives a characterization of when a category $A$ is precisely the category of algebras $X^T$ for a monad $T$ on $X$. The key condition is that the forgetful functor $G : A \to X$ creates coequalizers for parallel pairs whose images have absolute (equivalently, split) coequalizers in $X$. This theorem is fundamental in monad theory and universal algebra, showing that categories of algebras are exactly those admitting such coequalizer creation.
