Since a split fork is defined by equations involving only composites and identities, it remains a split fork under the application of any functor. Consequently, the arrow $e$ in any split fork is an absolute coequalizer: its image under any functor is again a coequalizer. This makes split forks a powerful tool for constructing coequalizers that are preserved universally.
