An arrow $e$ is called an \emph{absolute coequalizer} of $\partial_0$ and $\partial_1$ in $C$ if for any functor $T: C \to X$ (to any category $X$ whatever) the resulting fork
$$T a \xrightarrow[T \partial_1]{T \partial_0} T b \xrightarrow{T e} T c$$
has $T e$ a coequalizer (of $T \partial_0$ and $T \partial_1$).
In particular, an absolute coequalizer is automatically a coequalizer.
