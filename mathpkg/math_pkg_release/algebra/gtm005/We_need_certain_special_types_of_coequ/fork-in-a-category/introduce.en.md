A fork is a diagram consisting of three objects $a, b, c$ and three arrows $\partial_0, \partial_1 : a \to b$ and $e : b \to c$ satisfying $e \partial_0 = e \partial_1$. Geometrically, it is a cone from the parallel pair $a \rightrightarrows b$ to the vertex $c$. The fork condition is the minimal requirement for $e$ to coequalize the pair $(\partial_0, \partial_1)$, and is the fundamental setup for studying coequalizers and split coequalizers.
