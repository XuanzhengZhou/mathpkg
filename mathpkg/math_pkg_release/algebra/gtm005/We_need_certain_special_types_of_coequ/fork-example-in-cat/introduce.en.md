This example exhibits a split fork in the category $\mathbf{Cat}$ of small categories. The parallel pair consists of the domain and codomain functors $\partial_0, \partial_1 : C^2 \to C$ from the arrow category. When $C$ has a terminal object $a_0$, the splitting functors are $s : \mathbf{1} \to C$ selecting $a_0$ and $t : C \to C^2$ sending each object to its unique arrow into $a_0$. This gives a concrete illustration of how split forks arise from the existence of terminal objects.
