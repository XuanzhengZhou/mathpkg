This lemma states that when the forgetful functor $G : A \to X$ creates coequalizers (hypothesis (iii) of Beck's Theorem), comparison functors from any other adjunction giving the same monad are uniquely determined. The proof constructs $M$ on objects by taking the coequalizer of the canonical presentation, and on morphisms via the universal property. This lemma not only supports Beck's Theorem but also provides a new proof of the comparison theorem.
