A split coequalizer is simply the arrow $e : b \to c$ that appears in a split fork. It coequalizes $\partial_0$ and $\partial_1$, and the splitting data provides a right inverse $s : c \to b$ for $e$, making it a split epimorphism. Split coequalizers arise naturally in the canonical presentation of algebras over a monad.
