A category is the fundamental structure of category theory, consisting of objects and arrows (morphisms) between them. Each arrow has a domain and codomain, and there are operations of identity (assigning to each object an identity arrow) and composition (combining consecutive arrows). The composition must satisfy associativity and unit laws, making categories a generalization of algebraic structures like monoids and groups, as well as geometric and topological structures.
