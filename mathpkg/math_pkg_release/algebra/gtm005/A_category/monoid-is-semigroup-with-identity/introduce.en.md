From the categorical definition of a monoid as a one-object category, it follows that a monoid is precisely a semigroup equipped with an identity element. This is because any two arrows in a one-object category are composable, yielding an associative binary operation on the set of arrows, and the identity arrow provides the two-sided unit. This result establishes the equivalence between the categorical and the classical algebraic definitions of a monoid.
