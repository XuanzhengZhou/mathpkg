A preorder is a category in which there is at most one arrow between any two objects. This definition unifies order theory with category theory: defining $p \leq p'$ when an arrow $p \to p'$ exists yields a reflexive and transitive relation. Thus, preorders are precisely those categories that are "thin" -- having no parallel arrows between any pair of objects. When the underlying relation is also antisymmetric, the preorder becomes a partial order.
