**Theorem 2.** The forgetful functor $U: \mathbf{Grp} \to \mathbf{Set}$ lifts limits. Given a diagram $H: J \to \mathbf{Grp}$, if the underlying sets $UH_j$ have a limit $L$ in $\mathbf{Set}$, then $L$ carries a unique group structure making all projections $v_j$ group homomorphisms. Moreover, this group is the limit of $H$ in $\mathbf{Grp}$. This theorem generalizes: limits in algebraic categories are computed on the underlying sets.
