**Theorem 1 (Completeness of $\mathbf{Set}$).** The category of sets is small-complete. For any small category $J$ and functor $F: J \to \mathbf{Set}$, the limit is explicitly given by the set $\operatorname{Cone}(*, F)$ of all cones from the singleton set $*$ to $F$. The limiting cone $v$ has components $v_j(\sigma) = \sigma_j$. This construction provides the fundamental example of a complete category and serves as a template for constructing limits in other concrete categories by applying forgetful functors.
