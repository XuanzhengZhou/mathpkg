A functor $V: A \rightarrow X$ \textbf{creates limits} for a functor $F: J \rightarrow A$ if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] To every limiting cone $\tau: x \rightarrow VF$ in $X$ there is exactly one pair $\langle a, \sigma \rangle$ consisting of an object $a \in A$ with $Va = x$ and a cone $\sigma: a \rightarrow F$ with $V\sigma = \tau$, and if, moreover,
\item[(ii)] This cone $\sigma: a \rightarrow F$ is a limiting cone in $A$.
\end{enumerate}
