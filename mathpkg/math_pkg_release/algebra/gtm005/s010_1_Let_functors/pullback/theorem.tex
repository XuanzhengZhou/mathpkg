A \emph{pullback} (or \emph{fiber product}) of a pair of arrows $f: a \to c$ and $g: b \to c$ is an object $a \times_c b$ together with arrows $p_1: a \times_c b \to a$ and $p_2: a \times_c b \to b$ such that $f \circ p_1 = g \circ p_2$, and for any $x$ with arrows $u: x \to a$, $v: x \to b$ satisfying $f \circ u = g \circ v$, there is a unique $h: x \to a \times_c b$ with $p_1 \circ h = u$ and $p_2 \circ h = v$.
