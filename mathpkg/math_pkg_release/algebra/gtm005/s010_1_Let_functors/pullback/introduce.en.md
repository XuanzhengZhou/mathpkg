A pullback is the categorical formulation of the fiber product. Given two arrows $f: a \to c$ and $g: b \to c$, the pullback $a \times_c b$ is the universal object making the square commute. In $\mathbf{Set}$, it is $\{(x,y) \in a \times b \mid f(x) = g(y)\}$. Pullbacks are fundamental: they generalize inverse images, and many categorical constructions (equalizers, kernels, finite limits) can be built from pullbacks and a terminal object.
