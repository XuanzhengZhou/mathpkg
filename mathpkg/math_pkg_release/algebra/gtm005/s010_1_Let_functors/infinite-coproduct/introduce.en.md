An infinite coproduct (or small coproduct) is the dual of an infinite product. It is the colimit of a functor from a discrete category. In $\mathbf{Set}$, it is the disjoint union; in $\mathbf{Ab}$, it is the direct sum. Categories with all small coproducts are called cocomplete (when they also have coequalizers).
