Given $\alpha: \hom_D(r, -) \xrightarrow{\cdot} K$, define $y(\alpha) = \alpha_r(1_r) \in Kr$. Conversely, given $a \in Kr$, define $\tau: \hom_D(r, -) \xrightarrow{\cdot} K$ by $\tau_d(f: r \rightarrow d) = (Kf)(a)$. These are inverse bijections. For naturality: given $f: r \rightarrow r'$, the Yoneda map transforms under precomposition by $f$, and postcomposition by natural transformation between functors $K \rightarrow K'$.
