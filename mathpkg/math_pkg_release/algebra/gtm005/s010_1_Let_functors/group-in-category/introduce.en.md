A group object in a category $C$ with finite products is the internalization of the notion of a group. The group operations are expressed as arrows in $C$, and the axioms as commutative diagrams. Examples include: topological groups in $\mathbf{Top}$, Lie groups in $\mathbf{Diff}$, and group schemes in algebraic geometry. This is a fundamental example of the "categorical algebra" approach.
