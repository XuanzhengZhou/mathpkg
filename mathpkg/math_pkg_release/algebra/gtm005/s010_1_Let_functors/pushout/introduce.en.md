A pushout is the dual notion to a pullback: given two arrows with a common domain, the pushout is the universal object completing the square. In $\mathbf{Set}$, pushouts are given by disjoint union modulo the equivalence generated by $f(c) \sim g(c)$. Pushouts are used to construct adjunction spaces in topology and amalgamated free products in group theory.
