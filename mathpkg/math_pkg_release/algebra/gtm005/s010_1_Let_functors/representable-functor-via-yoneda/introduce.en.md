The Yoneda Lemma characterizes representable functors: $K$ is representable exactly when it has a universal element, i.e., an element $u \in Kr$ such that for any $x \in Kd$, there is a unique $f: r \to d$ with $(Kf)u = x$. This unifies all universal properties.
