A binary product $a \times b$ in a category is the categorical formulation of the Cartesian product. It comes with projection maps to each factor, and any pair of maps into the factors factors uniquely through the product. Products are a special case of limits (limits over discrete diagrams with two objects) and are characterized by the universal property $\hom(c, a \times b) \cong \hom(c, a) \times \hom(c, b)$.
