The kernel pair of an arrow $f$ is obtained by pulling back $f$ along itself. In a regular category, the kernel pair of a regular epimorphism is its "kernel congruence," and regular epimorphisms are precisely the coequalizers of their kernel pairs. Kernel pairs generalize the notion of kernel from abelian categories to arbitrary categories.
