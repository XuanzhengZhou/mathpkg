A monoid object in a category $C$ with finite products internalizes the notion of a monoid. It consists of an object $M$ with multiplication and unit satisfying the usual axioms diagrammatically. When $C = \mathbf{Set}$, a monoid object is an ordinary monoid. The concept generalizes to monoidal categories, where one uses the monoidal product instead of the categorical product.
