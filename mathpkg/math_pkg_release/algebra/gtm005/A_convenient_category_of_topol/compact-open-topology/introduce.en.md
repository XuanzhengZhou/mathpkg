The compact-open topology is the standard function-space topology used in algebraic topology and category theory. For two topological spaces $Y$ and $Z$, it endows the set of continuous maps $\operatorname{Top}(Y, Z)$ with a topology generated by subbasic open sets $N(C, U)$ consisting of all maps sending a compact set $C \subset Y$ into an open set $U \subset Z$. When $Y$ is locally compact Hausdorff, this topology yields the exponential adjunction $\operatorname{Top}(X \times Y, Z) \cong \operatorname{Top}(X, \operatorname{Cop}(Y, Z))$, making $\operatorname{Top}$ a cartesian closed category on this restricted class of spaces. The Kelleyfication of the compact-open topology provides the internal hom for the cartesian closed category $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ of compactly generated Hausdorff spaces.
