The inclusion functor $\mathbf{CGHaus} \hookrightarrow \mathbf{Haus}$ has a right adjoint $K: \mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{CGHaus}$, the Kelleyfication functor. Thus $\mathbf{CGHaus}$ is a coreflective subcategory of $\mathbf{Haus}$. The counit $\varepsilon_Y: KY \to Y$ is the identity on underlying sets, and any continuous map $f: X \to Y$ from a compactly generated Hausdorff space $X$ factors uniquely as $f = \varepsilon_Y \circ f'$ where $f': X \to KY$ is the same set-function.
