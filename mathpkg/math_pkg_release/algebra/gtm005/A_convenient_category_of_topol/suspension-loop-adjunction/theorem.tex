The reduced suspension $\Sigma$ and loop space $\Omega$ form an adjunction
$$\Sigma \dashv \Omega: \mathbf{CGHaus}_* \to \mathbf{CGHaus}_*,$$
that is, there is a natural bijection
$$\mathbf{CGHaus}_*(\Sigma X, Y) \cong \mathbf{CGHaus}_*(X, \Omega Y)$$
for all $X, Y \in \mathbf{CGHaus}_*$. More generally, the smash product and base-point-preserving function space yield the adjunction
$$\mathbf{CGHaus}_*(Z \wedge Y, X) \cong \mathbf{CGHaus}_*(Z, X^{(*)Y}),$$
from which the $\Sigma \dashv \Omega$ adjunction follows by setting $Y = S^1$.
