A strict morphism of monoidal categories is a functor that preserves the entire monoidal structure on the nose—the bifunctor, the unit object, and all three natural isomorphisms. Such morphisms form the arrows of the category $\mathbf{Moncat}$ of small monoidal categories. However, many natural functors between monoidal categories are not strict; for instance, the forgetful functor from vector spaces to sets does not preserve the tensor product on the nose, motivating the later development of lax and strong monoidal functors.
