Let $B$ be a monoidal category. There is a function which assigns to each pair of words $v, w$ of the same length $n$ a unique natural isomorphism
$$\phi(v, w) : v_B \xrightarrow{\cong} w_B : B^n \to B,$$
called the \textbf{canonical map} from $v_B$ to $w_B$, such that:
\begin{itemize}
\item The identity arrow $e \to e$ is canonical (between functors of $0$ variables);
\item The identity transformation $\mathrm{id}_B : I_B \to I_B$ is canonical;
\item $\alpha, \alpha^{-1}, \lambda, \lambda^{-1}, \varrho$, and $\varrho^{-1}$ are canonical;
\item The composite of two canonical maps is canonical;
\item The $\square$-product of two canonical maps is canonical.
\end{itemize}
Here $v_B : B^n \to B$ denotes the functor obtained by substituting the identity functor in each blank of the word $v$.
