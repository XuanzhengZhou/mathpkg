A $T$-algebra for a monad $T = \langle T, \eta, \mu \rangle$ on a category $X$ generalizes the classical notion of an algebra of a given type. It consists of an object $x$ (the carrier) and a structure map $h: Tx \to x$ that interprets the formal $T$-operations on $x$, subject to an associative law and a unit law expressed as commutative diagrams. Morphisms of $T$-algebras are arrows in $X$ that respect the structure maps. The category $X^T$ of $T$-algebras is also called the Eilenberg-Moore category of the monad.
