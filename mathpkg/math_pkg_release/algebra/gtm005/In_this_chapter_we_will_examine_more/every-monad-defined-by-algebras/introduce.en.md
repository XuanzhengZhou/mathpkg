Theorem 1 establishes that every monad $T$ on a category $X$ arises from an adjunction, specifically the free-forgetful adjunction between $X$ and the Eilenberg-Moore category $X^T$ of $T$-algebras. The forgetful functor $G^T$ sends each $T$-algebra to its underlying object, while the free functor $F^T$ sends an object $x$ to the free $T$-algebra $\langle Tx, \mu_x \rangle$. The counit of this adjunction is precisely the structure map $h$ of each algebra. This theorem is the fundamental result that justifies the monadic approach to universal algebra.
