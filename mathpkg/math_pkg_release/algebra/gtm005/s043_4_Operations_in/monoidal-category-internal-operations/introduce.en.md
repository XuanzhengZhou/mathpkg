A monoidal category provides the environment for defining algebraic structures (monoids, groups, rings, modules) internally. For example, a monoid in $(V, \otimes, I)$ is an object $M$ with multiplication $M \otimes M \to M$ and unit $I \to M$ satisfying associativity and unit laws.
