An injective object is the categorical dual of a projective object: every morphism from a subobject into an injective object extends to the whole containing object. This extension property is equivalent to the contravariant hom-functor $\hom(-, q)$ sending monomorphisms to epimorphisms. Injective objects are central to homological algebra, where they provide the basis for injective resolutions.
