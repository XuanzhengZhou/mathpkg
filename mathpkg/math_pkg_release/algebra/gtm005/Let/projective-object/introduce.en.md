A projective object is one for which every morphism into a codomain lifts through any epimorphism onto that codomain. This lifting property makes projectives fundamental in homological algebra: in $R$-Mod the projective objects are precisely the direct summands of free modules. The definition is equivalent to requiring that the covariant hom-functor $\hom(p, -)$ preserves epimorphisms.
