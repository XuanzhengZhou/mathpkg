A functor $T: C \rightarrow B$ is \textbf{full} when for every pair $c, c'$ of objects of $C$, the function on hom-sets
$$T_{c, c'}: \hom(c, c') \rightarrow \hom(Tc, Tc'), \quad f \mapsto Tf$$
is surjective. A functor which is both full and faithful is called \textbf{fully faithful}; in this case every such function is a bijection, but the functor need not be an isomorphism of categories, for there may be objects of $B$ not in the image of $T$.
