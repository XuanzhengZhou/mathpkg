The braid category $\mathcal{B}$ is a fundamental example in the theory of monoidal categories. Its objects are natural numbers, and its morphisms from $n$ to $n$ are precisely the elements of the Artin braid group $B_n$, with no morphisms between distinct objects. This category provides a geometric model of braiding that nearly satisfies the axioms of a symmetric monoidal category, illustrating the concept of a braided monoidal category in a concrete and visually intuitive way.
