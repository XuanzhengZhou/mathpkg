This exercise asks to combine the previous results — that $C^C$ is a strict monoidal category and that any monoidal category $M$ admits a strong monoidal functor $T: M \to M^M$ — to obtain an alternative proof of the coherence theorem for monoidal categories. The key idea is that coherence in the strict monoidal category $M^M$ can be transported back to $M$ via the strong monoidal functor $T$, providing an independent verification that all formal diagrams commute.
