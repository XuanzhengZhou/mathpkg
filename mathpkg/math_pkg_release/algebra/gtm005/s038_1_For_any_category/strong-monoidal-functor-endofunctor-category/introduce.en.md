If $M$ is a monoidal category, the assignment $a \mapsto a \mathbin{\square} -$ defines a strong monoidal functor $T: M \to M^M$, where $M^M$ is the strict monoidal category of endofunctors under composition. The monoidal structure of $T$ is witnessed by the associativity isomorphism $\alpha$ serving as $T_2$ and the inverse unit isomorphism $\lambda^{-1}$ serving as $T_0$. Notably, the coherence conditions for $T$ correspond precisely to the Mac Lane coherence axioms for the monoidal category $M$ itself.
