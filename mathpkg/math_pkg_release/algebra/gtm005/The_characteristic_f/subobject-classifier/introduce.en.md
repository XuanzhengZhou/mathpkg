A subobject classifier in a category $\mathcal{C}$ with terminal object $1$ is a universal monomorphism $\mathsf{true} : 1 \rightarrowtail \Omega$ that classifies all subobjects: every monomorphism $S \rightarrowtail X$ arises uniquely as the pullback of $\mathsf{true}$ along some classifying morphism $\chi_m : X \to \Omega$. This generalizes the role of $\{0\} \hookrightarrow \{0,1\}$ in $\mathbf{Sets}$, where characteristic functions classify subsets. The object $\Omega$ is called the object of truth values and plays a fundamental role in topos theory, enabling an internal logic for the category.
