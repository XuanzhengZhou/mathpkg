A functor $V : A \to X$ is said to create limits for a diagram $F : J \to A$ if, whenever the composite $VF$ has a limiting cone in $X$, there is a unique lift of that cone to a limiting cone in $A$. This means that limits in $A$ are "created" from limits in $X$ by uniquely lifting both the vertex object and the cone morphisms. The concept is fundamental for proving completeness of algebraic categories: one shows that the forgetful functor to $\mathbf{Set}$ creates limits, and since $\mathbf{Set}$ is complete, the algebraic category is also complete. Variants include creation of products, finite limits, and colimits.
