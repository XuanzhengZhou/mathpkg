A pushout is the universal way to complete a commutative square on two given arrows with a common domain. In Set, the pushout of $f: A \to B$, $g: A \to C$ is the disjoint union $B \amalg C$ modulo the identification of $f(x)$ with $g(x)$ for each $x \in A$. Pushouts generalize adjunction spaces in topology and amalgamated free products in group theory.
