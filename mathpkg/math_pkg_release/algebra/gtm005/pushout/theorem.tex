Given two arrows $f: a \to b$ and $g: a \to c$ with a common domain, a \textbf{pushout} is an object $r$ and arrows $u: b \to r$, $v: c \to r$ such that (i) $u \circ f = v \circ g$, and (ii) for any object $s$ and arrows $h: b \to s$, $k: c \to s$ with $h \circ f = k \circ g$, there is a unique $t: r \to s$ with $t \circ u = h$, $t \circ v = k$. The vertex $r$ is often written $b \amalg_a c$ (fibered sum) and the diagram is called a \textbf{cocartesian square}.
