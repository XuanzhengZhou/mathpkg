A metacategory is the fundamental structure studied in category theory, defined purely in terms of objects, arrows, identity arrows, and a composition operation that satisfies associativity and the unit law. This axiomatic definition avoids any dependence on set theory, treating categories as first-class mathematical entities. Every concrete example — such as the metacategory of sets with functions, or groups with homomorphisms — is an interpretation of these axioms. The prefix "meta" emphasizes that Mac Lane is defining the concept without relying on a set-theoretic foundation, distinguishing it from the later set-based notion of a category.
