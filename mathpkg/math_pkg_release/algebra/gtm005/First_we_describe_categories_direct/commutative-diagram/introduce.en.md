A commutative diagram is a powerful visual and reasoning tool in category theory. It consists of objects as vertices and arrows as directed edges, with the property that any two directed paths between the same pair of vertices compose to the same arrow. This pictorial representation vividly captures the relationships among arrows and objects, and much of the effectiveness of categorical methods derives from the ability to reason with such diagrams.
