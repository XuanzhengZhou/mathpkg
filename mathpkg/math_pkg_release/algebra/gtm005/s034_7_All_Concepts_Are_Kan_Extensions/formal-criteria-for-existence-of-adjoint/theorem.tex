A functor $G: A \to X$ has a left adjoint if and only if the right Kan extension
$$\operatorname{Ran}_G 1_A: X \to A$$
exists and is preserved by $G$. When this is the case, this right Kan extension is a left adjoint $F = \operatorname{Ran}_G 1_A$ for $G$, and the counit transformation $\varepsilon: (\operatorname{Ran}_G 1_A) G \to 1_A$ for the Kan extension is the counit $\varepsilon: FG \to 1_A$ of the adjunction.
