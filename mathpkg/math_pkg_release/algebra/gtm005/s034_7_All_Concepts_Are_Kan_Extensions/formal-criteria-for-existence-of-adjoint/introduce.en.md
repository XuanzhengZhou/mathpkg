This theorem provides a formal characterization of adjoint functors purely in terms of Kan extensions. A functor $G$ has a left adjoint precisely when the right Kan extension of the identity functor along $G$ exists and is preserved by $G$. This result further substantiates the claim that all fundamental concepts of category theory are subsumed by Kan extensions: adjunctions, like limits and colimits, can be expressed as Kan extensions satisfying a preservation condition.
