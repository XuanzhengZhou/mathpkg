\text{Dually to subobjects, in any category $A$, if $r: a \to s$ and $p: a \to t$ are two epis with a common domain $a$, write $r \leq p$ when $r$ factors through $p$; that is, when $r = r'p$ for some arrow $r'$ (which is then necessarily epi).}

\text{When both $r \leq p$ and $p \leq r$, write $r \equiv p$; this defines an equivalence relation among the epis with domain $a$, and the corresponding equivalence classes are called \textbf{quotient objects} of $a$.}

\text{In Grp, every quotient object of a group $G$ is represented by the projection $p: G \to G/N$ onto the factor group $G/N$ for some normal subgroup $N$ of $G$, and $G/M \leq G/N$ holds if and only if $M \supset N$.}
