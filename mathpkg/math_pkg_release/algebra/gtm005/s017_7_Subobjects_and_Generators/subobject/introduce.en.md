In category theory, a subobject of an object $a$ is not defined as a subset of elements, but as an equivalence class of monomorphisms with codomain $a$. Two monics $u: s \to a$ and $v: t \to a$ are equivalent if each factors through the other. This definition captures the notion of "substructure" (subgroup, subspace, subring) in concrete categories while remaining purely arrow-theoretic, making it applicable to any category. The partial order $\leq$ on subobjects corresponds to inclusion of substructures in familiar categories like Grp, Ab, and R-Mod.
