\text{Given any functor $G: A \to X$, an arrow $f: x \to Ga$ is said to \textbf{span} $a$ when there is no proper subobject $s \to a$ such that $f$ factors through $Gs \to Ga$.}

\text{Equivalently, $f$ spans $a$ if for every monic $m: s \rightarrowtail a$, the condition $f = G(m) \circ g$ for some $g: x \to Gs$ implies $m$ is an isomorphism.}
