Given two parallel arrows $f, g: a \to b$ in a category $C$, an \textbf{equalizer} of $f$ and $g$ is an arrow $e: d \to a$ such that (i) $f \circ e = g \circ e$, and (ii) for any arrow $h: c \to a$ with $f \circ h = g \circ h$, there is a unique arrow $h': c \to d$ with $h = e \circ h'$. The equalizer is thus the universal arrow to the object $\langle f, g \rangle$ (in the category of parallel pairs) from the diagonal functor.
