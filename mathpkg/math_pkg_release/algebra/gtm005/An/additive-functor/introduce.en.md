An additive functor is a functor between Ab-categories that respects the additive group structure on hom-sets: it maps sums of parallel arrows to sums of their images. This is the natural notion of morphism between Ab-categories. Additive functors automatically preserve zero arrows and, when biproducts exist, preserve biproduct diagrams. They are the enriched functors over the monoidal category of abelian groups.
