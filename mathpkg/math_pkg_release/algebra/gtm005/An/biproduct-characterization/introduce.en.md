A biproduct is the simultaneous realization of product and coproduct in an Ab-category, generalizing the direct sum of abelian groups. This proposition gives a purely equational characterization: three simple algebraic conditions on the injections and projections guarantee that an object is both a product and a coproduct. The equations $p_k i_j = \delta_{kj}$ express the familiar relations from the direct sum of modules, while $i_1 p_1 + i_2 p_2 = 1$ is the key identity linking the product and coproduct structures.
