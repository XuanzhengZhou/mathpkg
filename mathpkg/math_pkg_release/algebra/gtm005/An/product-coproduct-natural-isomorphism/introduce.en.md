When an Ab-category has biproducts, the product and coproduct functors become naturally isomorphic. The arrow $f_1 \oplus f_2$ (the "direct sum" of two morphisms) can be characterized equally well by the product universal property (via components) or the coproduct universal property (via injections). This natural identification is what makes the notation $a \oplus b$ unambiguous for biproducts: the same object serves as both product $a \times b$ and coproduct $a \amalg b$, and the mapping functions coincide.
