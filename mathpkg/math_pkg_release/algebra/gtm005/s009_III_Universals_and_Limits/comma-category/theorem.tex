Given functors $T: E \rightarrow C$ and $S: D \rightarrow C$, the \textbf{comma category} $(T \downarrow S)$ has as objects all triples $\langle e, d, f\rangle$ with $e \in E$, $d \in D$, and $f: Te \rightarrow Sd$ in $C$. An arrow $\langle e, d, f\rangle \rightarrow \langle e', d', f'\rangle$ is a pair of arrows $k: e \rightarrow e'$ in $E$ and $h: d \rightarrow d'$ in $D$ such that $f' \circ Tk = Sh \circ f$.
