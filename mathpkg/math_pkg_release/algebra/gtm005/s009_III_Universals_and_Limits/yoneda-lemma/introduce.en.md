The Yoneda Lemma is one of the most fundamental results in category theory. It states that for any functor $K: D \to \mathbf{Set}$ and any object $r \in D$, there is a bijection between natural transformations $\alpha: D(r, -) \to K$ and elements of the set $Kr$. The bijection is given by evaluating $\alpha$ at the identity arrow $1_r$ on $r$. This lemma implies that the functor $D(r, -)$ (the covariant hom-functor) is a "free" set-valued functor on one generator $1_r$, and it justifies the saying that an object is determined by the arrows into it. The Yoneda Lemma underlies the theory of representable functors, the Yoneda embedding, and much of modern category theory.
