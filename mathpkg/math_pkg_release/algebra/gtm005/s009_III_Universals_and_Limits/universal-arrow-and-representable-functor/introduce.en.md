Mac Lane demonstrates the equivalence between three fundamental notions: universal arrows, universal elements, and representable functors. A universal arrow from $c$ to $S$ corresponds to a representation of the functor $\hom_C(c, S-)$, and a universal element of $H$ corresponds to a representation of $H$. These equivalences form the conceptual backbone for understanding adjunctions.
