A universal arrow from an object $c$ to a functor $S: D \to C$ formalizes the idea of a "best approximation" of $c$ by values of $S$. It consists of an object $r \in D$ and an arrow $u: c \to Sr$ such that any other arrow $c \to Sd$ factors uniquely through $u$ via $S$. This is one of the fundamental notions of category theory, unifying free constructions, limits, adjunctions, and representable functors.
