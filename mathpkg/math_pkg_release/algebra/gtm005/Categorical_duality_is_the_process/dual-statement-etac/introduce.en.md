The dual of a statement in the elementary theory of an abstract category (ETAC) is formed by systematically reversing every arrow: domain and codomain are interchanged, and the order of composition is reversed. Atomic statements about identity arrows remain unchanged, since an identity arrow is self-dual. When the original statement involves a commutative diagram, its dual is obtained by reversing every arrow in the diagram. This operation is an involution: applying it twice recovers the original statement.
