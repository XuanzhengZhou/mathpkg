An adjoint square (introduced by Kelly) generalizes the notion of a map of adjunctions to the case where the categories themselves may differ. It consists of four categories connected by two horizontal adjunctions and two vertical functors, together with two natural transformations $\sigma$ and $\tau$ satisfying a compatibility condition expressed via a commuting diagram of hom-sets. The exercise asks the reader to re-express this condition in terms of the units and counits, and to prove that $\sigma$ and $\tau$ determine each other uniquely, generalizing Theorem 2.
