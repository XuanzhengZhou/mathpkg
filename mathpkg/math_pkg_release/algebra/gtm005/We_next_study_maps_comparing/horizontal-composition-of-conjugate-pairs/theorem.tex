Given two conjugate pairs
\[
\langle \sigma, \tau \rangle : \langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle \to \langle F', G', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A,
\]
\[
\langle \bar{\sigma}, \bar{\tau} \rangle : \langle \bar{F}, \bar{G}, \bar{\eta}, \bar{\varepsilon} \rangle \to \langle \bar{F}', \bar{G}', \bar{\eta}', \bar{\varepsilon}' \rangle : A \rightharpoonup D
\]
the (horizontal) composite natural transformations
\[
\bar{\sigma}\sigma : \bar{F}F \to \bar{F}'F', \qquad \tau\bar{\tau} : \bar{G}' G' \to G \bar{G}
\]
yield a conjugate pair for the composite adjunctions.
