This theorem provides four equivalent diagrammatic characterizations of when a pair of natural transformations $\langle \sigma, \tau \rangle$ is conjugate. Each condition involves commuting diagrams of natural transformations built from the units and counits of the two adjunctions. The theorem also establishes a bijective correspondence: each natural transformation $\sigma : F \to F'$ uniquely determines a conjugate partner $\tau : G' \to G$, and vice versa. This is proved using the Yoneda Lemma and the universal property of the counit.
