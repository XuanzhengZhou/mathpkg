Conjugate natural transformations provide a notion of morphism between two adjunctions with the same source and target categories. Given two adjunctions $\langle F, G, \varphi \rangle$ and $\langle F', G', \varphi' \rangle$ both from $X$ to $A$, a pair of natural transformations $\sigma : F \to F'$ and $\tau : G' \to G$ is conjugate if the induced maps on hom-sets commute with the adjunction bijections. This definition is fundamental to forming the 2-categorical structure of the category of adjunctions, where conjugate pairs serve as 2-cells.
