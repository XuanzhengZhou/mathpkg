A colimit (also called direct limit or inductive limit) is the universal way to map a diagram-shaped functor $F: J \to C$ into a single object. The natural transformation $u: F \to \Delta r$ consists of arrows from each $F(i)$ to $r$, compatible with the diagram's morphisms. Colimits unify coproducts, coequalizers, pushouts, cokernels, and unions as special cases for different choices of the index category $J$.
