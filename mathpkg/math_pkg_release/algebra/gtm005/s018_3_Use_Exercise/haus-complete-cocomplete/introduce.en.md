Proposition 2 establishes that the category $\mathbf{Haus}$ of Hausdorff spaces is both complete and cocomplete. The adjoint functor theorem is used to construct a left adjoint $H$ to the inclusion $\mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{Top}$, which sends each topological space to its "largest Hausdorff quotient." The forgetful functor $\mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{Set}$ also has a left adjoint (equipping a set with the discrete topology, which is Hausdorff for sets).
