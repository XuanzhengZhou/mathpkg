The left adjoint $H$, constructed via the adjoint functor theorem, sends any topological space $X$ to its "largest Hausdorff quotient" $HX$. The universal arrow $\eta: X \to HX$ is surjective, and $H$ is left-adjoint-left-inverse to the inclusion $\mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{Top}$, meaning that for a Hausdorff space $Y$, $HY = Y$ and $\eta = 1_Y$.
