This exercise shows that the Stone-Čech compactification of a completely regular space $X$ is an injection (embedding) of $X$ into its compactification. The proof uses the Urysohn lemma: for distinct points $x \neq y$ in a completely regular space, there exists a continuous function $f: X \to I$ (where $I$ is the unit interval) with $f(x) \neq f(y)$. The universal arrow approach generalizes beyond the classical restriction to completely regular spaces.
