Exercise 9.5 shows that the inclusion $\mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{Top}$ does not have a right adjoint. A right adjoint would preserve colimits, but the coequalizer in $\mathbf{Top}$ of two Hausdorff spaces need not be Hausdorff, meaning the inclusion does not preserve colimits. Consequently, the forgetful functor $\mathbf{Haus} \to \mathbf{Set}$ also lacks a right adjoint. This contrasts with the existence of the left adjoint $H$ (the Hausdorff quotient) established in Proposition 2.
