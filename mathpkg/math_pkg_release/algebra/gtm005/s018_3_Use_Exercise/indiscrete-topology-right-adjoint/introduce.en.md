The right adjoint $D'$ to $G: \mathbf{Top} \to \mathbf{Set}$ equips a set $S$ with the indiscrete (trivial) topology, where only $S$ and $\emptyset$ are open. The adjunction $G \dashv D'$ implies that $G$ preserves all colimits, which explains why the coproduct (disjoint union) of topological spaces has underlying set equal to the disjoint union of the underlying sets.
