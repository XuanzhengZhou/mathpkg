Watt's theorem states that any continuous (limit-preserving) additive functor from $R$-modules to abelian groups is representable. The proof uses Exercise 2(b) together with the special adjoint functor theorem (SAFT). This result is fundamental in the study of module categories and is closely related to the Eilenberg-Watts theorem characterizing right exact functors as tensor products.
