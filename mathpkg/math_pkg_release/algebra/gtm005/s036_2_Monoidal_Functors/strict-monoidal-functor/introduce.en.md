A strict monoidal functor is a monoidal functor in which the structure maps $F_2(a,b)$ and $F_0$ are all identity morphisms. This means the functor strictly preserves the tensor product and the unit object on the nose: $F(a \square b) = F(a) \square F(b)$ and $F(e) = e'$. Strict monoidal functors are the morphisms in the category of strict monoidal categories.
