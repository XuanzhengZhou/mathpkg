A functor is a morphism of categories — a structure-preserving map between categories. It consists of an object function mapping objects of the domain category to objects of the codomain, and an arrow function mapping arrows compatibly with identities and composition. Functors are the central notion for transporting categorical constructions from one context to another, and they form the foundation for all subsequent categorical developments such as natural transformations, adjunctions, and limits.
