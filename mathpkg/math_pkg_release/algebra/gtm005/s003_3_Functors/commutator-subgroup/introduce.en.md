The commutator subgroup (or derived subgroup) of a group consists of all finite products of commutators. It is always a normal subgroup and is the smallest normal subgroup whose quotient is abelian. Its universal property — that any homomorphism to an abelian group factors through the quotient by the commutator subgroup — makes it a fundamental construction. In categorical terms, the commutator subgroup defines an endofunctor on the category of groups.
