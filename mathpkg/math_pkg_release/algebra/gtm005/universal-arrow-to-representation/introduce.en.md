Proposition III.1 establishes the equivalence between universal arrows and representable functors. A universal arrow $u: c \to Sr$ corresponds to a representation of the functor $C(c, S-): D \to \mathbf{Set}$ by the object $r$. The notions of universal arrow, universal element, and representable functor are thus three equivalent ways of expressing the same categorical phenomenon.
