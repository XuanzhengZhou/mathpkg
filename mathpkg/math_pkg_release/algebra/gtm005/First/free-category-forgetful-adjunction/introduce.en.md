The free category functor $C : \text{Grph} \to \text{Cat}$ is left adjoint to the forgetful functor $U : \text{Cat} \to \text{Grph}$ that sends each category to its underlying graph. This adjunction is a direct consequence of the universal property established in Theorem 1: graph morphisms $G \to UB$ correspond bijectively and naturally to functors $C_G \to B$. The construction $G \mapsto C_G$ mirrors the construction of free monoids and free groups, and provides a fundamental example of a free-forgetful adjunction in category theory.
