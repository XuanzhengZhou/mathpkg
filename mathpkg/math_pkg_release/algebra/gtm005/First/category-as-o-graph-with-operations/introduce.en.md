A category with a fixed set $O$ of objects can be described purely algebraically as an $O$-graph $A$ equipped with two structure maps: a composition $c : A \times_O A \to A$ sending each composable pair $\langle g, f \rangle$ to its composite $c(g, f)$, and an identity map $i : O \to A$ sending each object $b$ to its identity arrow $i(b)$. These maps must satisfy associative and unit laws expressed by commutative diagrams, mirroring the familiar axioms of a category in a graph-theoretic language. This perspective is essential for constructing free categories and for the theory of monads.
