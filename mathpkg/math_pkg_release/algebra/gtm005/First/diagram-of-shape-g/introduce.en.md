A diagram of a specified shape $G$ in a category $B$ is defined simply as a graph morphism from $G$ into the underlying graph of $B$. By the free category theorem, this is equivalent to giving a functor from the free category $C_G$ into $B$. This correspondence elegantly unifies the notion of "diagram" in category theory: the shape is encoded by a graph $G$, and the diagram itself is either a graph morphism or the corresponding functor from $C_G$, whichever is more convenient.
