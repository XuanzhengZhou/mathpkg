A subcategory $A$ of $B$ is \textbf{coreflective} in $B$ when the inclusion functor $K: A \to B$ has a right adjoint. Example: The full subcategory of torsion abelian groups is coreflective in $\mathbf{Ab}$; the coreflector sends each abelian group $A$ to its torsion subgroup $TA$ of all elements of finite order.
