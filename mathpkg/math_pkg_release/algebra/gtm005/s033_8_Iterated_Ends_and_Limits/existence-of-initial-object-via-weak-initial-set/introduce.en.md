This theorem provides a general method for constructing initial objects in small-complete categories. If a category has all small limits and contains a small full subcategory that maps into every object (a weakly initial set), then the limit of the inclusion of that subcategory yields an initial object. The proof combines the product of all objects in the weakly initial set with an equalizer, refining the construction from Chapter V Section 6.
