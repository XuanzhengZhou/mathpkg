This theorem gives a purely limit-theoretic criterion for a functor to possess a left adjoint. The two conditions require that $G$ preserves all existing limits in $A$, and that the projection functor from each comma category $(x \downarrow G)$ has a limit in $A$. When both hold, the left adjoint is given explicitly as that limit. This is a powerful reformulation of the adjoint functor theorem in terms of limits.
