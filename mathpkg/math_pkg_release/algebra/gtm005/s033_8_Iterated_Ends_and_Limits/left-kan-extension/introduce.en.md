The left Kan extension is the dual notion to the right Kan extension. Given $K: M \to C$ and $T: M \to A$, the left Kan extension $\mathrm{Lan}_K T$ comes with a universal natural transformation $\eta: T \to (\mathrm{Lan}_K T)K$. The universal property gives a natural bijection between natural transformations out of the left Kan extension and natural transformations from $T$ into precompositions. When colimits of the appropriate shape exist, the left Kan extension can be computed as a colimit over the comma category $(K \downarrow c)$.
