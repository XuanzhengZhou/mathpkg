Let $S: P^{\mathrm{op}} \times P \times C^{\mathrm{op}} \times C \to X$ be a functor such that the ends
$$\int_c S(p, q, c, c) \quad\text{and}\quad \int_c S(p, p, b, c)$$
exist, for all $p, q \in P$ and $b, c \in C$. By the parameter theorems, regard these ends as bifunctors (of $p, q$ or $b, c$) respectively. Then there is an isomorphism
$$\theta: \int_p \left[ \int_c S(p, p, c, c) \right] \cong \int_c \left[ \int_p S(p, p, c, c) \right].$$
