(Formal criterion for a universal arrow.) Let $X$ have small hom-sets. Prove that there is a universal arrow from $x \in X$ to $G: A \to X$ if and only if (i) $X(x, G-): A \to \mathbf{Set}$ preserves all limits and (ii) $\lim_{Q: ((x \downarrow G) \to A)} Q$ exists in $A$.
