(Benabou, formal criterion for representability). Let $C$ have small hom-sets, while $*$ is the one-point set. Prove: A functor $K: C \to \mathbf{Set}$ is representable if and only if (i) $K$ preserves all limits which exist in $C$, and (ii) the projection $Q: (* \downarrow K) \to C$ of the comma category has a limit in $C$. When this is the case, the limiting cone $\lambda: r \to Q$ for this projection assigns to each $h \in Kc$ an arrow $\lambda_h: r \to c$ and $h \mapsto \lambda_h$ is a representation $K \cong C(r, -)$.
