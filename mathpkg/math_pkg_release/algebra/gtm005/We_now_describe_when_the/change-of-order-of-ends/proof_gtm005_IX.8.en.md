The result follows by applying the Fubini theorem for ends (the preceding Proposition) to the double end $\int_{\langle p, c \rangle} S(p,p,c,c)$ in two different ways. First, treating the product as $P \times C$, the double end equals $\int_p \int_c S(p,p,c,c)$. Second, treating the product as $C \times P$ with the order of variables swapped, the same double end equals $\int_c \int_p S(p,p,c,c)$. Since both iterated expressions compute the same double end, they are isomorphic. The existence conditions guarantee that both iterated ends exist, and the isomorphism $\theta$ is obtained by composing the two Fubini isomorphisms.
