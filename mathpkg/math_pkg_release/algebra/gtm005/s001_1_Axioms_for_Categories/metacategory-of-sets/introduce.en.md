The metacategory of sets, often denoted $\mathbf{Set}$, is the prototypical example of a category. Its objects are all sets and its arrows are all functions between sets, each carrying a specified domain and codomain. Composition is the usual composition of functions, and identity arrows are the usual identity functions. This example motivates the category axioms: associativity of function composition and the fact that identity functions act as units.
