The arrows-only formulation of a metacategory dispenses with objects entirely, treating them as nothing more than identity arrows. A metacategory is then described purely in terms of arrows, a designated class of composable pairs, and a composition operation. An identity is defined as an arrow that acts as a unit for composition on either side whenever composition is defined. This formulation shows that objects are a conceptual convenience rather than a logical necessity.
