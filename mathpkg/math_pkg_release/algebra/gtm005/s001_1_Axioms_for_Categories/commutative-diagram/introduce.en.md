A commutative diagram is a directed graph whose vertices are objects and whose edges are arrows of a category, with the property that any two directed paths between the same pair of vertices compose to equal arrows. Commutative diagrams are the primary visual reasoning tool in category theory, encoding equalities of composite morphisms in a geometrically intuitive form. Their effectiveness rests on the fact that the relevant relationships among arrows are displayed at a glance.
