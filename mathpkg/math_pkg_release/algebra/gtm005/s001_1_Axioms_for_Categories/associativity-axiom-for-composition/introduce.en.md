The associativity axiom for composition is one of the two fundamental axioms of a metacategory. It states that for any three composable arrows $f: a 	o b$, $g: b 	o c$, $k: c 	o d$, the two possible ways of composing them yield the same result: $k \circ (g \circ f) = (k \circ g) \circ f$. This means parentheses can be omitted, and the triple composite is written unambiguously as $k \circ g \circ f$.
