A split coequalizer is the arrow $e$ in a split fork diagram. It is a coequalizer of the parallel pair $\partial_0, \partial_1$ that comes equipped with explicit splitting data $s$ and $t$ satisfying universal equations. Because these equations involve only composition and identities, split coequalizers are preserved by all functors and are therefore absolute coequalizers. Split coequalizers provide the crucial hypothesis in Beck's theorem characterizing monadic adjunctions.
