An arrow $e$ is called an \textbf{absolute coequalizer} of $\partial_0$ and $\partial_1$ in $C$ if for any functor $T: C \to X$ (to any category $X$ whatever) the resulting fork
$$Ta \xrightarrow[T\partial_1]{T\partial_0} Tb \xrightarrow{Te} Tc$$
still has $Te$ a coequalizer of $T\partial_0$ and $T\partial_1$. In particular, an absolute coequalizer is automatically a coequalizer.
