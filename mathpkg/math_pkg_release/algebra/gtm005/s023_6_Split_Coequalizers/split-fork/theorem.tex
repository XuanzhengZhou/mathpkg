A \textbf{split fork} in $C$ is a fork
$$a \xrightarrow[\partial_1]{\partial_0} b \xrightarrow{e} c$$
with two more arrows
$$a \xleftarrow{t} b \xleftarrow{s} c$$
which satisfy the conditions
$$e \partial_0 = e \partial_1, \quad es = 1_c, \quad \partial_0 t = 1_b, \quad \partial_1 t = se.$$
We say that $s$ and $t$ split the fork. These conditions imply that $e$ is a split epi, with right inverse $s$.
