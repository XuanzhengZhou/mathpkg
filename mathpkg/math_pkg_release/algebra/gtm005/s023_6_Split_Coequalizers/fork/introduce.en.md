A fork is a diagram consisting of a parallel pair of arrows $\partial_0, \partial_1: a \rightrightarrows b$ together with an arrow $e: b \to c$ that coequalizes them, meaning $e \partial_0 = e \partial_1$. Equivalently, a fork is a cone from the diagram $a \rightrightarrows b$ to the vertex $c$. Forks provide the basic configuration for studying coequalizers, split coequalizers, and absolute coequalizers in category theory.
