Let $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle: X \rightharpoonup A$ and $\langle F', G', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle: X \rightharpoonup A'$ be two adjunctions which define the same monad in $X$. If $G$ satisfies hypothesis (iii) of Beck's theorem (on the creation of coequalizers for parallel pairs whose image under $G$ has a split coequalizer), then there is a unique comparison functor $M: A' \to A$ with $MF' = F$ and $GM = G'$.
