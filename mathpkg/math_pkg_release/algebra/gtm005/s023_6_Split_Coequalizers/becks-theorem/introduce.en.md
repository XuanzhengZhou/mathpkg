Beck's theorem provides a precise characterization of when the comparison functor $K: A \to X^T$ from a category $A$ to the Eilenberg-Moore category of algebras for the induced monad is an isomorphism. The three equivalent conditions show that monadicity is equivalent to the forgetful functor $G$ creating coequalizers for parallel pairs whose images under $G$ have either absolute or split coequalizers. The theorem is also known as the "precise tripleability theorem" (PTT) and is the fundamental result in monadicity theory.
