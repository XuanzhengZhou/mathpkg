A monoid is equivalently a category with a single object. Under this correspondence, the arrows of the category become the elements of the monoid, the identity arrow becomes the unit element, and composition of arrows becomes the monoid operation. For any object $a$ in any category, the set of endomorphisms $\hom(a,a)$ forms a monoid under composition, showing that monoids arise naturally throughout category theory.
