Every ordinal number can be viewed as a category via its natural linear ordering. Since ordinals are totally ordered sets, they are in particular preorder categories. The finite ordinals give the small categories $\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{2}, \mathbf{3}$, while the first infinite ordinal $\omega$ yields an infinite category consisting of a chain of composable arrows. This illustrates how set-theoretic ordinals naturally acquire categorical structure.
