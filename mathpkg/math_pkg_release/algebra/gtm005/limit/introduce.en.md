A limit (also called inverse limit or projective limit) is the universal cone over a diagram $F: J \to C$. The natural transformation $v: \Delta r \to F$ provides arrows from the limit object $r$ to each $F(j)$, compatible with the diagram. Limits unify products, equalizers, pullbacks, and kernels as special cases. Dually to colimits, limits are right adjoints to the diagonal functor.
