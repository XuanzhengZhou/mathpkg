A \textbf{product} of two objects $a, b$ in a category $C$ is an object $a \times b$ together with arrows $p: a \times b \to a$ and $q: a \times b \to b$ (the \textbf{projections}) such that for any object $c$ and arrows $f: c \to a$, $g: c \to b$, there is a unique arrow $h: c \to a \times b$ with $p \circ h = f$ and $q \circ h = g$. This yields a natural bijection
$$C(c, a \times b) \cong C(c, a) \times C(c, b).$$
