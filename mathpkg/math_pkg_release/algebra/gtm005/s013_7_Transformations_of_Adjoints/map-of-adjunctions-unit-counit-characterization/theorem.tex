Given adjunctions
$$\langle F, G, \varphi, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A, \quad \langle F', G', \varphi', \eta', \varepsilon' \rangle : X' \rightharpoonup A'$$
and functors $K : A \to A'$ and $L : X \to X'$ satisfying the commuting squares
$$\begin{array}{c}
A \xrightarrow{G} X \xrightarrow{F} A \\
K \downarrow \quad L \downarrow \quad K \downarrow \\
A' \xrightarrow{G'} X' \xrightarrow{F'} A'
\end{array}$$
the condition that the hom-set diagram commutes for all objects is equivalent to
$$L\eta = \eta' L$$
and also equivalent to
$$\varepsilon' K = K \varepsilon.$$
