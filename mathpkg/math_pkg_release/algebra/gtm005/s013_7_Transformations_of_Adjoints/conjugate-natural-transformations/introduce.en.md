Conjugate natural transformations provide the correct notion of morphism between adjunctions sharing the same source and target categories. When two adjunctions $\langle F, G angle$ and $\langle F', G' angle$ are given between the same categories $X$ and $A$, natural transformations $\sigma : F 	o F'$ and $	au : G' 	o G$ are conjugate if the induced maps on hom-sets are compatible with the adjunction isomorphisms. This pairing establishes a bijective correspondence: given $\sigma$, there exists a unique $	au$ making the pair conjugate, and vice versa.
