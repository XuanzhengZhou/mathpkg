The \textbf{category of adjunctions} Adj has as objects all (small) categories $X, A, D, \ldots$ and as arrows the adjunctions $\langle F, G, \eta, \varepsilon \rangle : X \rightharpoonup A$, composed as
$$\langle \bar{F}F, G\bar{G}, G\bar{\eta}F \cdot \eta, \bar{\varepsilon} \cdot \bar{F}\varepsilon\bar{G} \rangle.$$
The identity arrow for each category $A$ is the identity adjunction $A \rightharpoonup A$ (with identity functors and identity unit and counit). Moreover, each hom-set $\operatorname{Adj}(X, A)$ is itself a category: the category of adjunctions from $X$ to $A$, whose objects are these adjunctions and whose arrows are the conjugate pairs $\langle \sigma, \tau \rangle$, under vertical composition.
