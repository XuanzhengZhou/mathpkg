An adjoint square (due to Kelly) generalizes the notion of conjugate natural transformations by allowing the categories to change. It consists of four categories connected by two horizontal adjunctions and two vertical functors $H$ and $K$, together with natural transformations $\sigma : F'H \to KF$ and $\tau : HG \to G'K$ satisfying a compatibility condition on hom-sets. As in the case of conjugate transformations, each of $\sigma$ and $\tau$ uniquely determines the other.
