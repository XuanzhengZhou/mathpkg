An epimorphism is the categorical dual of a monomorphism, generalizing the notion of a surjective function. An arrow is epic if it can be canceled on the right. In Set and Grp, epimorphisms are surjective, but in other categories (e.g., Rng), there exist epimorphisms that are not surjective (such as the inclusion $\mathbb{Z} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Q}$).
