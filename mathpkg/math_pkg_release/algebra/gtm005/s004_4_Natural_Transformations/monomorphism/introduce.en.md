A monomorphism is the categorical generalization of an injective function. Rather than referring to elements, the definition uses only the composition of arrows: an arrow is monic if it can be canceled on the left. In concrete categories like Set, Grp, and R-Mod, monomorphisms correspond exactly to injective functions.
