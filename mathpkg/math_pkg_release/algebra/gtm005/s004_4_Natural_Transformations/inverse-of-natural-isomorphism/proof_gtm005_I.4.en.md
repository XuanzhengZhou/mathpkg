Since each $\tau_c$ is invertible, set $\tau^{-1}_c = (\tau_c)^{-1}$. For any $f: c \rightarrow c'$ in $C$, the naturality square for $\tau$ gives $Tf \circ \tau_c = \tau_{c'} \circ Sf$. Composing on the left with $(\tau_{c'})^{-1}$ and on the right with $(\tau_c)^{-1}$ yields $(\tau_{c'})^{-1} \circ Tf = Sf \circ (\tau_c)^{-1}$, which is precisely naturality of $\tau^{-1}$.
