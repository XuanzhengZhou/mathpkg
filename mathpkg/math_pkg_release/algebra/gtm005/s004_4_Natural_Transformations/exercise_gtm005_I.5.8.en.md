Consider the category with objects $\langle X, e, t \rangle$, where $X$ is a set, $e \in X$, and $t: X \to X$, and with arrows $f: \langle X, e, t \rangle \to \langle X', e', t' \rangle$ the functions $f$ on $X$ to $X'$ with $fe = e'$ and $ft = t'f$. Prove that this category has an initial object in which $X$ is the set of natural numbers, $e = 0$, and $t$ is the successor function.
