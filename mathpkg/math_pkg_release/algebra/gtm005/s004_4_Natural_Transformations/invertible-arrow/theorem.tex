An arrow $e: a \rightarrow b$ is \textbf{invertible} in $C$ if there exists an arrow $e': b \rightarrow a$ with $e' \circ e = 1_a$ and $e \circ e' = 1_b$. If such an $e'$ exists, it is unique and is written $e' = e^{-1}$. Furthermore, $(e_1 e_2)^{-1} = e_2^{-1} e_1^{-1}$ whenever the composite $e_1 e_2$ is defined and both factors are invertible.
