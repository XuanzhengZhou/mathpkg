The notion of a small set (relative to a chosen Grothendieck universe) provides a foundation for category theory that avoids the set-theoretic paradoxes of 'the category of all sets'. By choosing a universe $U$, 'small' means 'member of $U$', and 'large' categories like Set (the category of all small sets) can be safely discussed.
