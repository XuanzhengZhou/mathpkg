The \textbf{Elementary Theory of an Abstract Category} (ETAC) consists of statements $\Sigma$ built from atomic statements involving:
\begin{itemize}
\item $a$ is the domain of $f$ \quad ($a = \operatorname{dom} f$)
\item $b$ is the codomain of $f$ \quad ($b = \operatorname{cod} f$)
\item $i$ is the identity arrow of $a$ \quad ($i = 1_a$)
\item $g$ can be composed with $f$ and $h$ is the composite \quad ($h = g \circ f$)
\item $a = b$ (equality of objects)
\item $f = g$ (equality of arrows)
\end{itemize}
These are combined using propositional connectives (and, or, not, implies, if and only if) and quantifiers ($\forall a$, $\forall f$, $\exists a$, $\exists f$).

A \textbf{sentence} is a statement in which all variables are quantified (bound).
The abbreviation $f : a \to b$ means ``$a$ is the domain of $f$ and $b$ is the codomain of $f$''.
