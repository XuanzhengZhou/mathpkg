By Proposition 1 applied to the case $C = \mathbf{Set}$, $c = *$, and $S = K$, a universal arrow $\langle r, u \colon * \to Kr \rangle$ from $*$ to $K$ is equivalent to a natural isomorphism $D(r, -) \cong \mathbf{Set}(*, K-)$. But there is a natural isomorphism $\mathbf{Set}(*, K-) \cong K$ which sends $f \colon * \to Kd$ to $f(*) \in Kd$. Composing these two natural isomorphisms yields $\psi \colon D(r, -) \cong K$, sending $f' \colon r \to d$ to $(Kf')(u(*)) = (Kf')e$, where $e = u(*)$ is the universal element of $K$. Thus $\langle r, \psi \rangle$ is a representation of $K$.
