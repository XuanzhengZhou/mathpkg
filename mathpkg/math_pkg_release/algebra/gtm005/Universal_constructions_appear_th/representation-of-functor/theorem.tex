Let $D$ have small hom-sets. A \text{representation of a functor $K \colon D \to \mathbf{Set}$} is a pair $\langle r, \psi \rangle$, with $r$ an object of $D$ and

$$
\psi \colon D(r, -) \xrightarrow{\cong} K
$$

a natural isomorphism. The object $r$ is called the \text{representing object}. The functor $K$ is said to be \text{representable} when such a representation exists.

Up to isomorphism, a representable functor is thus just a covariant hom-functor $D(r, -)$.
