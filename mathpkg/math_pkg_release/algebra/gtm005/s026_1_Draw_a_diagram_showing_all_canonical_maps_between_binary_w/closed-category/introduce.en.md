A closed category is a symmetric monoidal category $V$ in which the monoidal product has a right adjoint in each variable: for every object $b$, the functor $- \square b: V \to V$ has a right adjoint $(\cdot)^b: V \to V$. The object $a^b$ is called the internal hom or exponential, representing the "object of morphisms" from $b$ to $a$. The prototypical example is the category of abelian groups with tensor product, where $A^B = \hom(B, A)$ is the group of homomorphisms. More generally, any cartesian closed category (like $\mathbf{Set}$) is closed. Closed categories provide the foundation for enriched category theory, where the hom-sets are replaced by hom-objects in the closed category $V$.
