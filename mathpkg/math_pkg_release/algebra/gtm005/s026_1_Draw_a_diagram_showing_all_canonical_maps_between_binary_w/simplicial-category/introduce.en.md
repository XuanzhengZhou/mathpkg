The simplicial category $\Delta$ is a fundamental structure in algebraic topology and category theory. Its objects are the finite ordinal numbers $n = \{0, 1, \ldots, n-1\}$, and its morphisms are all order-preserving (monotone) functions between them. Remarkably, $\Delta$ is a strict monoidal category under ordinal sum $+$ with the empty ordinal $0$ as unit, and it contains a universal monoid $\langle 1, 0 \to 1, 1+1 \to 1 \rangle$. This means any monoid in any strict monoidal category arises via a unique monoidal functor from $\Delta$. The category $\Delta$ also admits a geometric realization as a subcategory of topological spaces via the standard affine simplices, and its opposite $\Delta^{\mathrm{op}}$ is the domain of simplicial objects, which are central to homological algebra and homotopy theory.
