A left action of a monoid $\langle c, \mu, \eta \rangle$ on an object $a \in B$ (in a fixed monoidal category $B$) is an arrow $\nu : c \square a \rightarrow a$ of $B$ such that the diagram

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
c \square (c \square a) & \xrightarrow{\alpha} & (c \square c) \square a \\
\downarrow {\scriptstyle 1 \square \nu} & & \downarrow {\scriptstyle \mu \square 1} \\
c \square a & \xrightarrow{\nu} & a
\end{array}$$

and

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
e \square a & \xrightarrow{\eta \square 1} & c \square a \\
\searrow {\scriptstyle \lambda} & & \downarrow {\scriptstyle \nu} \\
& a &
\end{array}$$

commute. For example, $c$ acts on itself by the map $\mu : c \square c \rightarrow c$; this is the "left regular representation" of $c$.
