This corollary of the Coherence Theorem for monoidal categories states that all diagrams built from the canonical natural transformations (identity, associator, left and right unitors) and their monoidal products are necessarily commutative. The vertices of such diagrams are words representing iterated functors on powers of the monoidal category, and every edge is a canonical natural transformation between the functors at its endpoints. This result is foundational for working with monoidal categories, as it guarantees that any two ways of composing the basic structural isomorphisms yield the same result.
