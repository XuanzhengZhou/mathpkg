Proposition 2 gives a presentation of the simplicial category $\Delta$ in terms of generators and relations. The generators are the face maps $\delta_i^n: n \to n+1$ (injective, omit the $i$-th element) and degeneracy maps $\sigma_j^n: n+1 \to n$ (surjective, double the $j$-th element). The three families of relations — often called the simplicial identities — govern how these maps compose: face-face, degeneracy-degeneracy, and face-degeneracy interactions. These relations are precisely what is needed to reduce any composite of $\delta$'s and $\sigma$'s to the unique normal form established in the Lemma. The proof consists of showing that these relations suffice to put any composite into the unique form.
