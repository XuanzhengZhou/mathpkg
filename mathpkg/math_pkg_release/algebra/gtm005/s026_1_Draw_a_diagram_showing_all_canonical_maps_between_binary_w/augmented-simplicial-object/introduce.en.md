An augmented simplicial object extends a simplicial object by adding a "$(-1)$-dimensional" object $S_{-1}$ and an augmentation map $\varepsilon: S_0 \to S_{-1}$. This extra structure is crucial for homological algebra applications, where augmented simplicial objects give rise to chain complexes that are exact in positive degrees (resolutions). For example, the singular chain complex of a topological space arises from an augmented simplicial abelian group, with the augmentation map sending each $0$-simplex (point) to the generator of the free abelian group in degree $-1$. Augmented simplicial objects also arise naturally from comonads via the universal comonoid in $\Delta^{\mathrm{op}}$.
