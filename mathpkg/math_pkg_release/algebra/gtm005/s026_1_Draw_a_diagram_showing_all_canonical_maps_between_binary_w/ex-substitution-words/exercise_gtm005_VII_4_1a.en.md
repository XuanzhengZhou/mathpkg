(a) (Substitution of words in a word.) Each word $u$ of length $n$ determines a functor $u_W : W^n \rightarrow W$. If $v_1, \ldots, v_n$ are $n$ words, show that the word $u_W(v_1, \ldots, v_n)$ has length the sum of the lengths of the $v_i$, and that it corresponds (intuitively) to substituting $v_1, \ldots, v_n$, in order, for the $n$ blanks $(-)$ in the word $u$.
