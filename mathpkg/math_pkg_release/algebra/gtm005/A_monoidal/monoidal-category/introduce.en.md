A monoidal category (also called a tensor category) formalizes the notion of a category equipped with an associative binary operation (tensor product) and a unit object. The coherence axioms—the pentagon and triangle—ensure that all diagrams built from the associativity $\alpha$, left unit $\lambda$, and right unit $\rho$ commute. The standard example is the category of vector spaces over a field $F$ with the usual tensor product $\otimes$ and the one-dimensional vector space $F$ as the unit object.
