A symmetric monoidal category is a braided monoidal category where the braiding is its own inverse: swapping twice returns the original order. This is the familiar commutative structure of tensor products of vector spaces. In the symmetric case, the two hexagon axioms become equivalent, and a coherence theorem (Theorem 1) guarantees that all diagrams built from associativity and symmetry commute, governed by the symmetric group.
