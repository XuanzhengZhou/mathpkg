Theorem 1 is the coherence theorem for symmetric monoidal categories, extending Mac Lane's original coherence theorem for monoidal categories to include the symmetry isomorphism $\gamma$. It asserts that for any two ways of parenthesizing and permuting the arguments of a multi-variable functor in a symmetric monoidal category, there is exactly one canonical natural isomorphism between them built from $\alpha$ and $\gamma$. The proof reduces the problem to verifying the defining relations of the symmetric group $S_n$, using that $\gamma^2 = 1$.
