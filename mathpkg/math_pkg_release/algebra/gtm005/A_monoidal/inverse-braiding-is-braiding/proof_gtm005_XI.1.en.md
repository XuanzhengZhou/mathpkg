The first hexagon axiom (7a) for $\gamma$ states that for all objects $a,b,c$,
\[
\alpha \circ \gamma_{ab,c} \circ \alpha = (1 \square \gamma_{a,c}) \circ \alpha \circ (\gamma_{b,c} \square 1).
\]
Taking inverses of both sides (and noting that $\alpha$ is invertible) yields an equation involving $\gamma^{-1}$ which is exactly the second hexagon (7b) with $\gamma$ replaced by $\gamma^{-1}$. Similarly, the second hexagon for $\gamma$ yields the first hexagon for $\gamma^{-1}$. Since both hexagon axioms hold for $\gamma$, both also hold for $\gamma^{-1}$. Naturality of $\gamma^{-1}$ follows from naturality of $\gamma$ by taking inverses. Hence $\gamma^{-1}$ is a braiding.
