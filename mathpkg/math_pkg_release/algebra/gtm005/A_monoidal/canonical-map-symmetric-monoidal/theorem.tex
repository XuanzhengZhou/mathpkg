For a symmetric monoidal category $M$, and for any pair $(v\sigma, w\tau)$ of permuted words of the same length $n$, a \emph{canonical map} is a natural isomorphism
$$\operatorname{can}_M(v\sigma, w\tau) : (v\sigma)_M \longrightarrow (w\tau)_M : M^n \to M$$
satisfying the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The identity natural transformation is canonical.
\item All instances of the associativity $\alpha$ and the symmetry $\gamma$ are canonical.
\item The composite of two canonical maps is canonical.
\item The $\square$-product of two canonical maps is canonical.
\end{enumerate}
