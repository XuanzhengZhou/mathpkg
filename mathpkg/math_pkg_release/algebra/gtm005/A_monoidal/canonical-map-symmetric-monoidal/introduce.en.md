In a symmetric monoidal category, a canonical map is a distinguished natural isomorphism between permuted word functors that is built from the structural isomorphisms $\alpha$ (associativity) and $\gamma$ (symmetry). Theorem 1 asserts that for any two permuted word functors of the same arity, there exists a unique canonical map, establishing a powerful coherence result: all ways of rearranging parentheses and permuting arguments via $\alpha$ and $\gamma$ yield the same composite isomorphism.
