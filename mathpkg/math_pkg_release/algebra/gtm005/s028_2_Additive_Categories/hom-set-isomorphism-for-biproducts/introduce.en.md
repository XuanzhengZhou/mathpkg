This proposition establishes that in any $\textit{Ab}$-category with finite biproducts, the hom-set between two finite biproducts decomposes as a direct sum of the hom-sets between their summands. Every morphism $f : \bigoplus_k c_k \to \bigoplus_j a_j$ is therefore uniquely represented by a matrix of morphisms $f_{jk} : c_k \to a_j$, and composition of such morphisms reduces to the usual matrix multiplication. This result shows that the additive structure of the category can be fully recovered from the biproduct structure alone.
