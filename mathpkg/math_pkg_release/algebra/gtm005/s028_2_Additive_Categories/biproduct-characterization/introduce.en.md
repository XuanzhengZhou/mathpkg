In an $\textit{Ab}$-category, a biproduct of two objects $a$ and $b$ is a diagram consisting of projections $p_1, p_2$ and injections $i_1, i_2$ satisfying the equations $p_1 i_1 = 1_a$, $p_2 i_2 = 1_b$, and $i_1 p_1 + i_2 p_2 = 1_c$. This theorem shows that such a diagram simultaneously exhibits $c$ as both the product and the coproduct of $a$ and $b$, with the additional relations $p_1 i_2 = 0$ and $p_2 i_1 = 0$. The universal property of the product is then expressed by a natural isomorphism of hom-sets.
