This is Mac Lane's celebrated Strictification Theorem (also known as the Coherence Theorem for monoidal categories). It states that every monoidal category can be replaced, up to monoidal equivalence, by a strict monoidal category in which the associativity and unit isomorphisms are identities. This justifies the common practice of suppressing associativity parentheses when working in monoidal categories.
