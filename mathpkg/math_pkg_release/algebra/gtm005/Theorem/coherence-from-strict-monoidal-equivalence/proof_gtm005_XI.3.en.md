Suppose that $v$ and $w$ are two tensor words in the objects of $M$ which are related by a chain of associativity maps $\alpha$, $\alpha^{-1}$, $\lambda$, $\lambda^{-1}$, $\rho$, $\rho^{-1}$. Apply the strong monoidal functor $G : M \rightarrow S$; then $G(v)$ and $G(w)$ are corresponding tensor words in the strict monoidal category $S$. Since $S$ is strict, all its structure maps $\alpha$, $\lambda$, $\rho$ are identities, so $G(v) = G(w)$. Because $G$ is a strong monoidal equivalence, it is faithful, and therefore the original maps linking $v$ and $w$ in $M$ must compose to the identity. Thus coherence holds for associativity in $M$.
