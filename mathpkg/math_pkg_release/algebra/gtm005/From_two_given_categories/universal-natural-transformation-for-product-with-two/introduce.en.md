Mac Lane shows that the product $C 	imes \mathbf{2}$ (where $\mathbf{2}$ is the interval category $0 	o 1$) serves as a cylinder object: functors $C 	imes \mathbf{2} 	o B$ correspond precisely to natural transformations in $B^C$. This universal property makes the arrow category fundamental in the study of functor categories.
