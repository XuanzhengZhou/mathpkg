A subobject classifier generalizes the set $\{0, 1\}$ from the category of sets to an arbitrary category $C$. It consists of a distinguished truth-value object $\Omega$ and a universal monomorphism $t \colon 1 \to \Omega$ from the terminal object, such that every subobject (monomorphism) arises uniquely as a pullback of $t$. The induced classifying map $\psi \colon X \to \Omega$ plays the role of the characteristic function. The existence of a subobject classifier is one of the defining axioms of an elementary topos.
