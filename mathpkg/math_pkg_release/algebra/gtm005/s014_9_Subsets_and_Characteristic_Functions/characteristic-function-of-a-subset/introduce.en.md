The characteristic function (also called indicator function) of a subset $S \subset X$ is a map $\psi_S \colon X \to \{0, 1\}$ that encodes membership in $S$ as a binary value. This construction captures the idea that any subset can be recovered as the pullback of the inclusion of a distinguished singleton $\{0\} \hookrightarrow \{0, 1\}$. The characteristic function thus exhibits $\{0, 1\}$ as a "truth-value object" and motivates the general categorical notion of a subobject classifier.
