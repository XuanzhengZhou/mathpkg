An elementary topos is a category that behaves sufficiently like the category of sets to serve as a foundation for mathematics. The three defining axioms -- existence of all finite limits, a subobject classifier, and cartesian closure -- capture the essential structural properties of $\mathbf{Sets}$. The canonical examples are the category of sets and presheaf categories $\mathbf{Sets}^{C^{\mathrm{op}}}$. Topos theory connects category theory with logic: the subobject classifier $\Omega$ carries an internal Heyting algebra structure, and constructions such as sheaf toposes play a central role in algebraic geometry.
