A presheaf is a contravariant set-valued functor on a small category $C$. It assigns a set to each object and a restriction map to each morphism, contravariantly. The category of all presheaves $\mathbf{Sets}^{C^{\mathrm{op}}}$ on any small category $C$ is a fundamental example of an elementary topos. In geometry and topology, presheaves on the poset of open sets of a space provide the framework for defining sheaves, which satisfy additional locality and gluing axioms and are central to cohomology theories.
