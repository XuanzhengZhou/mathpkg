A strict monoidal functor is a monoidal functor where all the comparison morphisms $F_0$ and $F_2(a,b)$ are identities, meaning the monoidal structure is preserved strictly (on the nose). This is the strongest form of monoidal structure preservation, typically only possible when the monoidal categories involved are themselves strict. Strict monoidal functors are convenient for coherence theorem formulations.
