A monoidal functor is the natural notion of a structure-preserving map between monoidal categories. It consists of an ordinary functor $F$ together with a natural comparison morphism $F_2(a,b): F(a) \otimes F(b) \to F(a \otimes b)$ for each pair of objects, and a unit comparison $F_0: e' \to F(e)$. These data must satisfy three coherence conditions expressing compatibility with the associator $\alpha$, left unitor $\lambda$, and right unitor $\rho$ of the monoidal structures. Monoidal functors are the appropriate morphisms in the 2-category of monoidal categories.
