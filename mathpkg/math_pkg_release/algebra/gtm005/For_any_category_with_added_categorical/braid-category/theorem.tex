The \textbf{braid category} $B$ has as objects all natural numbers $n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$ (including zero) and as arrows the braids $n \to n$; there are no arrows $n \to m$ for $n \neq m$ (and only the identity arrow $\varnothing \to \varnothing$). This defines a monoidal category, with the box product $\square : B \times B \to B$ given by addition $\square = +$; the sum of two objects $m$ and $n$ is the usual sum of numbers, while addition of braids is the operation of laying the braids side by side.
