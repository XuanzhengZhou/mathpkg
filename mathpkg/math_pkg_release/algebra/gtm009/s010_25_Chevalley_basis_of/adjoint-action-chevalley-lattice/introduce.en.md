This proposition shows that the divided powers $\frac{(\operatorname{ad} x_{\alpha})^{m}}{m!}$ act on the Chevalley lattice $L(\mathbb{Z})$ with integral coefficients, specifically binomial coefficients. The result is foundational for defining Chevalley groups: since $L(\mathbb{Z})$ is stable under these divided powers, the exponentials $\exp(\operatorname{ad} x_{\alpha})$ act as integral matrices on $L(\mathbb{Z})$. Reducing modulo a prime $p$ then yields matrix groups over finite fields, which are precisely the Chevalley groups of adjoint type.
