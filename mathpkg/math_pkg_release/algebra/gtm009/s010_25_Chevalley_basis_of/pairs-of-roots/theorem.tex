Let $\alpha, \beta$ be linearly independent roots, and let $\beta - r\alpha, \ldots, \beta, \ldots, \beta + q\alpha$ be the $\alpha$-string through $\beta$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\langle \beta, \alpha \rangle = r - q$.

\item[(b)] At most two root lengths occur in this string.

\item[(c)] If $\alpha + \beta \in \Phi$, then
$$r + 1 = \frac{q(\alpha + \beta, \alpha + \beta)}{(\beta, \beta)}.$$
\end{enumerate}
