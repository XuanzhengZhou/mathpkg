Let $F = \mathbb{C}$. Fix a Chevalley basis of $L$, and let $L'$ be the $\mathbb{R}$-subspace of $L$ spanned by the elements $\sqrt{-1}\, h_i$ $(1 \leq i \leq \ell)$, $x_\alpha - x_{-\alpha}$, and $\sqrt{-1}\,(x_\alpha + x_{-\alpha})$ $(\alpha \in \Phi^+)$. Prove that these elements form a basis of $L$ over $\mathbb{C}$ (so $L \cong L' \otimes_{\mathbb{R}} \mathbb{C}$) and that $L'$ is closed under the bracket (so $L'$ is a Lie algebra over $\mathbb{R}$). Show that the Killing form $\kappa'$ of $L'$ is just the restriction to $L'$ of $\kappa$, and that $\kappa'$ is negative definite. ($L'$ is a "compact real form" of $L$, associated with a compact Lie group.)
