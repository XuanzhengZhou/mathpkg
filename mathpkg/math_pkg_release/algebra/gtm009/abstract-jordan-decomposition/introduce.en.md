In a semisimple Lie algebra, the Jordan decomposition of the adjoint operator $\operatorname{ad} x$ lifts uniquely to a decomposition of $x$ itself into commuting ad-semisimple and ad-nilpotent parts. This abstract Jordan decomposition is fundamental for the structure theory, allowing the definition of toral subalgebras, root space decompositions, and the classification of representations.
