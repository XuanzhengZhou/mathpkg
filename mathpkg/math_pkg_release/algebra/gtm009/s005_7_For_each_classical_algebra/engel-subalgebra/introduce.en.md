The Engel subalgebra is the generalized nullspace of the adjoint action of an element $x$ in a Lie algebra $L$. This construction provides the minimal building blocks for the theory of Cartan subalgebras, as every Cartan subalgebra arises as a minimal Engel subalgebra. The Engel subalgebra $L_0(\operatorname{ad} x)$ captures all elements of $L$ that are annihilated by some power of $\operatorname{ad} x$, and it is always a subalgebra of $L$. The name honors Friedrich Engel, whose theorem on nilpotent Lie algebras underpins the proof that Cartan subalgebras are nilpotent.
