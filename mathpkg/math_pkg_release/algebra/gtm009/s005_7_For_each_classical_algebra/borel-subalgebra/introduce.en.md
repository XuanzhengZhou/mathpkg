A Borel subalgebra is a maximal solvable subalgebra of a Lie algebra, named after Armand Borel. For semisimple Lie algebras, every Borel subalgebra is conjugate to a standard one $B(\Delta) = H + \sum_{\alpha > 0} L_{\alpha}$ relative to some Cartan subalgebra $H$ and base $\Delta$ of the root system. Borel subalgebras play a role in Lie theory analogous to that of Borel subgroups in algebraic groups. Their conjugacy (Theorem 16.4) is a fundamental structural result, and they provide the framework for defining parabolic subalgebras.
