Let $L$ be a Lie algebra.

An element $x \in L$ is called \textbf{strongly ad-nilpotent} if there exists $y \in L$ and some nonzero eigenvalue $a$ of $\operatorname{ad} y$ such that $x \in L_a(\operatorname{ad} y)$. This forces $x$ to be ad-nilpotent.

Denote by $\mathcal{N}(L)$ the set of all strongly ad-nilpotent elements of $L$, and by $\mathcal{E}(L)$ the subgroup of $\operatorname{Int} L$ generated by all $\exp \operatorname{ad} x$, $x \in \mathcal{N}(L)$. 

$\mathcal{N}(L)$ is stable under $\operatorname{Aut} L$; therefore, $\mathcal{E}(L)$ is normal in $\operatorname{Aut} L$.

For a subalgebra $K \subset L$, define $\mathcal{E}(L; K)$ to be the subgroup of $\mathcal{E}(L)$ generated by all $\exp \operatorname{ad}_L x$, $x \in \mathcal{N}(K)$. Then $\mathcal{E}(K)$ is obtained by taking the restriction of $\mathcal{E}(L; K)$ to $K$.
