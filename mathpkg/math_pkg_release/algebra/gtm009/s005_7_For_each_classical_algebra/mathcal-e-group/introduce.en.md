The group $\mathcal{E}(L)$ is a subgroup of the inner automorphism group $\operatorname{Int} L$ generated by exponentials of strongly ad-nilpotent elements. Introduced in Chapter 16 of Humphreys, $\mathcal{E}(L)$ has better functorial properties than the full inner automorphism group, making it the preferred tool for proving conjugacy theorems. When $L$ is semisimple, it turns out that $\mathcal{E}(L) = \operatorname{Int} L$. The subgroup $\mathcal{E}(L; K)$ associated to a subalgebra $K$ further refines the functorial behavior, allowing inductive arguments on algebra dimension.
