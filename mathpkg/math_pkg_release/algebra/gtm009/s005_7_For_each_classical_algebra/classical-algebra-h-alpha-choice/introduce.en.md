For each of the classical Lie algebras $A_\ell = \mathfrak{sl}(\ell+1, F)$, $B_\ell = \mathfrak{so}(2\ell+1, F)$, $C_\ell = \mathfrak{sp}(2\ell, F)$, and $D_\ell = \mathfrak{so}(2\ell, F)$, one can explicitly describe a Cartan subalgebra $H$ as the set of diagonal matrices and choose elements $h_\alpha \in H$ corresponding to a base of the root system. These elements serve as the semisimple generators in a Chevalley basis, which is used in the construction of Chevalley groups and in the study of Lie algebras over fields of arbitrary characteristic.
