Verify the assertions made about $\mathfrak{C}_o$ in (19.3). In particular, show that the commutator subspace of the Cayley algebra $\mathfrak{C}$ (spanned by all $cc' - c'c$ for $c, c' \in \mathfrak{C}$) has codimension one, with basis $(c_1 - c_2, c_3, c_4, c_5, c_6, c_7, c_8)$, complementary to the line through $c_1 + c_2$ (the identity element).
