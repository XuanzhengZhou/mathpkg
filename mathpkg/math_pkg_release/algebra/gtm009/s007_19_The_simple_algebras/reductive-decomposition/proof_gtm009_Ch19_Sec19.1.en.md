Suppose $L$ is reductive but not abelian, so $L' = L/Z(L)$ is semisimple. Then $\operatorname{ad} L \cong \operatorname{ad} L'$ acts completely reducibly on $L$ (by Theorem 6.3, Weyl's Theorem). Write $L = M \oplus Z(L)$, where $M$ is an ideal. In particular, $[LL] = [MM] \subset M$. But $[LL]$ maps onto $L'$ under the canonical projection $L \to L/Z(L)$, so $L = [LL] \oplus Z(L)$. These remarks imply the first statement.

For the second statement (that $[LL]$ is semisimple): Since $[LL] \cong L/Z(L)$ is isomorphic to the semisimple algebra $L'$, it follows that $[LL]$ is semisimple. More precisely, $\operatorname{Rad}([LL]) \subset \operatorname{Rad} L = Z(L)$, and since $[LL] \cap Z(L) = 0$, we have $\operatorname{Rad}([LL]) = 0$, so $[LL]$ is semisimple.
