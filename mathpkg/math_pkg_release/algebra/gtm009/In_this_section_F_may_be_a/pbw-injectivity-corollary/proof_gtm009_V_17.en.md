This is the special case $W = T^1 L = L$ of Corollary A. The canonical map $T^1 L \to S^1 L$ is the identity map on $L$ (since $T^1 L = L = S^1 L$ by definition), and this map is trivially an isomorphism. By Corollary A, $\pi(T^1 L) = i(L)$ is a complement to $U_0$ in $U_1$.

Since $U_0 = \pi(T^0 L) = \pi(F) \cong F$ consists of scalars, we have $U_1 = U_0 \oplus i(L)$. In particular, $i(L) \cap U_0 = \{0\}$. The restriction of $\pi$ to $L = T^1 L$ factors as $L \hookrightarrow \mathfrak{T}(L) \twoheadrightarrow \mathfrak{U}(L)$, and its kernel is $L \cap J$. But $J \subset \bigoplus_{i>0} T^i L$, so $L \cap J = 0$. Therefore $i = \pi|_L$ is injective.
