Let $L$ be a free Lie algebra on a set $X$, and let $R$ be the ideal of $L$ generated by a family of elements $\{f_j\}_{j \in J}$ ($f_j \in L$). Then the quotient Lie algebra $L/R$ is called the \textbf{Lie algebra with generators $x_i$ and relations $f_j = 0$}, where $x_i$ ($i \in X$) are the images in $L/R$ of the elements of $X$ under the canonical projection.

This provides a presentation of a Lie algebra by specifying its generators and the relations they must satisfy.
