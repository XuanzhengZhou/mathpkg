Let $V$ be a finite dimensional vector space over a field $F$. Set $T^0 V = F$, $T^1 V = V$, $T^2 V = V \otimes V$, \ldots, $T^m V = V \otimes \cdots \otimes V$ ($m$ copies). Define
$$\mathfrak{T}(V) = \bigoplus_{i=0}^{\infty} T^i V,$$
and introduce an associative product on homogeneous generators by
$$(v_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes v_k)(w_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes w_m) = v_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes v_k \otimes w_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes w_m \in T^{k+m} V.$$
Then $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ is an associative graded algebra with $1$, generated by $1$ and any basis of $V$. It is called the \textbf{tensor algebra} on $V$. The tensor algebra is the universal associative algebra on $n$ generators ($n = \dim V$): given any linear map $\phi: V \to A$, where $A$ is an associative $F$-algebra with $1$, there exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\tilde{\phi}: \mathfrak{T}(V) \to A$ extending $\phi$ and sending $1$ to $1$.

The construction extends to the case where $V$ is infinite dimensional.
