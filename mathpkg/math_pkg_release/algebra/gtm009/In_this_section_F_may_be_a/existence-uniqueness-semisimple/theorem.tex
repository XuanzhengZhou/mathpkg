\textbf{Theorem.} (a) Let $\Phi$ be a root system. Then there exists a semisimple Lie algebra having $\Phi$ as its root system.

(b) Let $L$, $L'$ be semisimple Lie algebras, with respective Cartan subalgebras $H$, $H'$ and root systems $\Phi$, $\Phi'$. Let an isomorphism $\Phi \to \Phi'$ be given, sending a given base $\Delta$ to a base $\Delta'$, and let $\pi_0: H \to H'$ be the associated isomorphism. For each $\alpha \in \Delta$ ($\alpha' \in \Delta'$) select arbitrary nonzero $x_{\alpha} \in L_{\alpha}$ ($x_{\alpha'}' \in L_{\alpha'}'$). Then there exists a unique isomorphism $\pi: L \to L'$ extending $\pi_0: H \to H'$ and sending $x_{\alpha}$ to $x_{\alpha'}'$ ($\alpha \in \Delta$).
