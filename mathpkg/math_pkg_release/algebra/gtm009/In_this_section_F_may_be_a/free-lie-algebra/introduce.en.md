A free Lie algebra on a set $X$ is the most general Lie algebra generated by $X$, satisfying a universal property: any set map from $X$ to a Lie algebra $M$ extends uniquely to a Lie algebra homomorphism. The construction uses the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ (viewed as a Lie algebra via the commutator) and takes the Lie subalgebra generated by $X$. Free Lie algebras are essential for presenting Lie algebras by generators and relations, a technique used in Serre's Theorem (Chapter VI) to construct semisimple Lie algebras from root system data.
