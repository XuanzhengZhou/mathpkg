Every Lie algebra admits a universal enveloping algebra, unique up to unique isomorphism. The existence proof is constructive: take the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ and quotient by the two-sided ideal generated by $x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy]$ to enforce the bracket relation. The uniqueness argument follows the standard pattern for objects defined by universal properties, using the unique extension property in both directions to obtain mutually inverse homomorphisms.
