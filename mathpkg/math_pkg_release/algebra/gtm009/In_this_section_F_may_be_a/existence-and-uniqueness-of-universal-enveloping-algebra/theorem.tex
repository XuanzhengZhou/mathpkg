Let $L$ be a Lie algebra over a field $F$. Then there exists a universal enveloping algebra $(\mathfrak{U}(L), i)$ of $L$, and it is unique up to a unique isomorphism.

\textbf{Uniqueness.} If $(\mathfrak{U}, i)$ and $(\mathfrak{U}', i')$ both satisfy the universal property, then there exist unique homomorphisms $\phi: \mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{U}'$ and $\psi: \mathfrak{U}' \to \mathfrak{U}$ such that $\phi \circ i = i'$ and $\psi \circ i' = i$. Then $\psi \circ \phi \circ i = i = 1_{\mathfrak{U}} \circ i$, so by uniqueness $\psi \circ \phi = 1_{\mathfrak{U}}$. Similarly $\phi \circ \psi = 1_{\mathfrak{U}'}$.

\textbf{Existence.} Let $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ be the tensor algebra on $L$, and let $J$ be the two-sided ideal in $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ generated by all
$$x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy] \qquad (x, y \in L).$$
Define $\mathfrak{U}(L) = \mathfrak{T}(L)/J$, and let $\pi: \mathfrak{T}(L) \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$ be the canonical homomorphism. Since $J \subset \bigoplus_{i > 0} T^i L$, the map $\pi$ sends $T^0L = F$ isomorphically into $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. Let $i: L \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$ be the restriction of $\pi$ to $T^1 L = L$. Then $(\mathfrak{U}(L), i)$ is a universal enveloping algebra of $L$.
