\textbf{Uniqueness.} Suppose $(\mathfrak{U}, i)$ and $(\mathfrak{U}', i')$ both satisfy the universal property of a universal enveloping algebra for $L$. Since $i: L \to \mathfrak{U}$ is a linear map with $i([xy]) = i(x)i(y) - i(y)i(x)$, the universal property of $(\mathfrak{U}', i')$ yields a unique homomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{U}' \to \mathfrak{U}$ with $\phi \circ i' = i$. Similarly, the universal property of $(\mathfrak{U}, i)$ yields a unique homomorphism $\psi: \mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{U}'$ with $\psi \circ i = i'$. Now consider $\psi \circ \phi: \mathfrak{U}' \to \mathfrak{U}'$. We have $(\psi \circ \phi) \circ i' = \psi \circ (\phi \circ i') = \psi \circ i = i'$. But the identity $1_{\mathfrak{U}'}$ also satisfies $1_{\mathfrak{U}'} \circ i' = i'$. By the uniqueness clause in the universal property, $\psi \circ \phi = 1_{\mathfrak{U}'}$. Similarly, $\phi \circ \psi = 1_{\mathfrak{U}}$. Hence $\phi$ and $\psi$ are mutually inverse isomorphisms.

\textbf{Existence.} Let $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ be the tensor algebra on the vector space $L$. Let $J$ be the two-sided ideal of $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ generated by all elements of the form
$$x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy] \quad (x, y \in L).$$
Set $\mathfrak{U}(L) = \mathfrak{T}(L)/J$, and let $\pi: \mathfrak{T}(L) \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$ be the quotient homomorphism. Observe that $J \subset \bigoplus_{i > 0} T^i L$, since each generator lies in $T^1 L \otimes T^1 L \oplus T^1 L \subset T^2 L \oplus T^1 L$. Thus $\pi$ maps $T^0 L = F$ isomorphically into $\mathfrak{U}(L)$, so $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ contains at least the scalars. Define $i = \pi|_L: L \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$.

To verify the universal property, let $j: L \to \mathfrak{U}$ be any linear map into an associative algebra $\mathfrak{U}$ such that $j([xy]) = j(x)j(y) - j(y)j(x)$. By the universal property of the tensor algebra, $j$ extends uniquely to an algebra homomorphism $\phi': \mathfrak{T}(L) \to \mathfrak{U}$ sending $1$ to $1$. For any generator $x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy]$ of $J$, we have
$$\phi'(x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy]) = j(x)j(y) - j(y)j(x) - j([xy]) = 0,$$
by the defining property of $j$. Hence $J \subset \ker \phi'$, so $\phi'$ factors through $\mathfrak{U}(L) = \mathfrak{T}(L)/J$, yielding a homomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{U}(L) \to \mathfrak{U}$ with $\phi \circ i = j$. If $\phi_1, \phi_2: \mathfrak{U}(L) \to \mathfrak{U}$ both satisfy $\phi_k \circ i = j$, then $\phi_1 \circ \pi$ and $\phi_2 \circ \pi$ agree on $L \subset \mathfrak{T}(L)$ and send $1$ to $1$. Since $L$ and $1$ generate $\mathfrak{T}(L)$, they agree on all of $\mathfrak{T}(L)$, hence $\phi_1 = \phi_2$. Thus $\phi$ is unique.
