Assume $\operatorname{char} F = 0$. The symmetric group $\mathcal{S}_m$ acts on $T^m V$ by permuting subscripts of tensors $v_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes v_m$ ($v_i \in V$). An element of $T^m V$ fixed by $\mathcal{S}_m$ is called a \textbf{homogeneous symmetric tensor} of order $m$.

Fix a basis $(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ of $V$. For each ordered sequence $1 \leq i(1) \leq i(2) \leq \cdots \leq i(m) \leq n$, define a symmetric tensor
$$\frac{1}{m!} \sum_{\pi \in \mathcal{S}_m} x_{i(\pi(1))} \otimes \cdots \otimes x_{i(\pi(m))}.$$
The images of these tensors in $S^m V$ form a basis of $S^m V$.
