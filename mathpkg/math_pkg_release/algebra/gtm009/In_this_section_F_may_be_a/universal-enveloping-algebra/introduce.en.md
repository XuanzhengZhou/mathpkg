The universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ of a Lie algebra $L$ is an associative algebra that encodes the Lie bracket as the commutator: $i([xy]) = i(x)i(y) - i(y)i(x)$. It is the "freest" associative algebra generated by $L$ subject to the commutation relations in $L$. The universal property means that representations of $L$ (Lie algebra homomorphisms into $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$) correspond bijectively to $\mathfrak{U}(L)$-module structures on $V$. The existence is proved by quotienting the tensor algebra $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ by the ideal generated by $x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy]$, while injectivity of $i$ is a consequence of the PBW Theorem.
