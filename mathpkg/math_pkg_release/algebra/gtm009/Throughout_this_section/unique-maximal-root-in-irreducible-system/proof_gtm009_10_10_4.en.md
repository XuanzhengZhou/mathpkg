Let $\beta = \sum_{\alpha \in \Delta} k_\alpha \alpha$ be maximal in the partial ordering; evidently $\beta > 0$. If $\Delta_1 = \{\alpha \in \Delta \mid k_\alpha > 0\}$ and $\Delta_2 = \{\alpha \in \Delta \mid k_\alpha = 0\}$, then $\Delta = \Delta_1 \cup \Delta_2$. Suppose $\Delta_2$ is nonvoid. Then $(\alpha, \beta) \leq 0$ for $\alpha \in \Delta_2$ (Lemma 10.1); since $\Phi$ is irreducible, some $\alpha \in \Delta_2$ must be nonorthogonal to $\Delta_1$, forcing $(\alpha, \alpha') < 0$ for some $\alpha' \in \Delta_1$, whence $(\alpha, \beta) < 0$. This implies $\beta + \alpha$ is a root (Lemma 9.4), contradicting maximality of $\beta$. Therefore $\Delta_2$ is empty and all $k_\alpha > 0$. For uniqueness: if $\beta'$ is another maximal root, then $(\beta', \beta) > 0$ and $\beta - \beta'$ would be a root (Lemma 9.4) unless $\beta = \beta'$, contradiction.
