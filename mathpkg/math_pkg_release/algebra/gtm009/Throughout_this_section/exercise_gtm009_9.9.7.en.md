Let $\alpha, \beta \in \Phi$ span a subspace $E'$ of $E$. Prove that $E' \cap \Phi$ is a root system in $E'$. Prove similarly that $\Phi \cap (\mathbb{Z}\alpha + \mathbb{Z}\beta)$ is a root system in $E'$ (must this coincide with $E' \cap \Phi$?). More generally, let $\Phi'$ be a nonempty subset of $\Phi$ such that $\Phi' = -\Phi'$, and such that $\alpha, \beta \in \Phi', \alpha + \beta \in \Phi$ implies $\alpha + \beta \in \Phi'$. Prove that $\Phi'$ is a root system in the subspace of $E$ it spans.
