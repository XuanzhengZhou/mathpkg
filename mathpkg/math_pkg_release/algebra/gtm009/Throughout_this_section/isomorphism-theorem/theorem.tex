Let $L, L'$ be simple Lie algebras over $\mathbb{F}$, with respective maximal toral subalgebras $H, H'$ and corresponding root systems $\Phi, \Phi'$. Suppose there is an isomorphism of $\Phi$ onto $\Phi'$ (in the sense of root systems), and let $\pi: H \to H'$ be the induced isomorphism. Then for any choice of nonzero $x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Delta$) and nonzero $x_{\alpha'}' \in L_{\alpha'}'$, there exists a unique isomorphism $\tilde{\pi}: L \to L'$ extending $\pi$ and sending $x_\alpha$ to $x_{\alpha'}'$ for all $\alpha \in \Delta$.
