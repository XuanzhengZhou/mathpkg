Every **toral subalgebra** of a semisimple Lie algebra is necessarily **abelian**. This lemma is roughly analogous to Engel's Theorem. The proof shows that if $[x,y]=ay$ with $a \neq 0$ for $x,y \in T$, then $\operatorname{ad}_T y(x) = -ay$ is an eigenvector with eigenvalue 0, while $x$ can be expressed as a linear combination of eigenvectors of $\operatorname{ad}_T y$, leading to a contradiction.
