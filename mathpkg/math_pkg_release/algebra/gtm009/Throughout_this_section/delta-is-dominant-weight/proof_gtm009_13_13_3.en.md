Since $\sigma_i \delta = \delta - \alpha_i = \delta - \langle \delta, \alpha_i \rangle \alpha_i$ (by Corollary to Lemma 10.2B), we have $\langle \delta, \alpha_i \rangle = 1$ for $1 \leq i \leq \ell$. But $\delta = \sum_i \langle \delta, \alpha_i \rangle \lambda_i$ by the decomposition of any weight in terms of fundamental weights (cf. (13.1)), so $\delta = \sum_{j=1}^{\ell} \lambda_j$. Since each $\langle \delta, \alpha_i \rangle = 1 > 0$, $\delta$ is strongly dominant.
