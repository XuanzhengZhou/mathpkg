If $\varepsilon_1, \ldots, \varepsilon_\ell$ is an obtuse basis of the euclidean space $E$ (i.e., all $(\varepsilon_i, \varepsilon_j) \leq 0$ for $i \neq j$), prove that the dual basis is acute (i.e., all $(\varepsilon_i^*, \varepsilon_j^*) \geq 0$ for $i \neq j$). [Reduce to the case $\ell = 2$.]
