A root system is **irreducible** if it is not a disjoint union of two proper subsets orthogonal to each other. Irreducible root systems correspond to simple Lie algebras. Their classification (types $A_\ell, B_\ell, C_\ell, D_\ell, E_6, E_7, E_8, F_4, G_2$) is a highlight of the theory.
