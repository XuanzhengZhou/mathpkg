A semisimple Lie algebra $L$ is **generated by the root spaces** corresponding to the simple roots and their negatives. This means that specifying the images of $x_\alpha \in L_\alpha$, $y_\alpha \in L_{-\alpha}$ for $\alpha \in \Delta$ determines a Lie algebra homomorphism uniquely. This fact is the crucial step in proving the isomorphism theorem.
