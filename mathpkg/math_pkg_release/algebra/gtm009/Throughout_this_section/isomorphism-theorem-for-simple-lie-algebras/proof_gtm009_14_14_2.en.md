The proof proceeds by constructing a subalgebra $D$ of $L \oplus L'$:
$$D = \{ (\bar{x}_\alpha, \bar{x}'_\alpha) \mid \alpha \in \Delta \} \cup \{ (\bar{y}_\alpha, \bar{y}'_\alpha) \mid \alpha \in \Delta \} \cup \{ (\bar{h}, \bar{h}') \mid \text{generated by } [\bar{x}_\alpha \bar{y}_\alpha] = \bar{h}_\alpha \},$$
where $\bar{x}_\alpha = (x_\alpha, x'_\alpha)$, etc. One first defines the isomorphism $\pi: H \to H'$ via the Killing form identification: $\pi(t_\alpha) = t'_{\alpha'}$. Then one constructs the isomorphism from the simple root vectors. The key step is to show that $D$ is a proper subalgebra whose projections onto both factors are surjective Lie algebra homomorphisms, making them isomorphisms.
