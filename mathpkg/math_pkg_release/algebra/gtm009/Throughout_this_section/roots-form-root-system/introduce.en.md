The fundamental theorem: the **set of roots** $\Phi$ of a semisimple Lie algebra, when embedded in the appropriate real euclidean space $E$, forms an abstract **root system** satisfying axioms (R1)-(R4). This bridges the concrete Lie algebra theory with the axiomatic theory of root systems, allowing the classification results of Chapters 10-12 to be applied. The rationality properties established in Proposition 8.4 ensure that all Cartan integers $2(\alpha,\beta)/(\beta,\beta)$ are integers.
