We know from Theorem 5.1 that $\kappa$ is nondegenerate on $L$. On the other hand, $L_0$ is orthogonal to all $L_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Phi$), according to the proposition (since $\alpha + 0 = \alpha \neq 0$ for $\alpha \in \Phi$, and $\kappa(L_0, L_\alpha) = 0$). If $z \in L_0$ is orthogonal to $L_0$ as well, then $\kappa(z, L) = \kappa(z, L_0 \oplus \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Phi} L_\alpha) = 0$, forcing $z = 0$ by nondegeneracy. Hence the restriction of $\kappa$ to $L_0$ is nondegenerate.
