A **simple Lie algebra** yields an **irreducible root system**. The proof shows that if $\Phi$ were decomposable as $\Phi_1 \cup \Phi_2$ with orthogonal components, the subalgebra generated by the root spaces for $\Phi_1$ would be centralized by those for $\Phi_2$, contradicting simplicity. Combined with the corollary for semisimple algebras, this reduces the classification of semisimple Lie algebras to that of simple ones with irreducible root systems.
