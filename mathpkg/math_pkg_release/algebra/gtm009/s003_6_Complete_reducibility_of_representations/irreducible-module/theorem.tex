An $L$-module $V$ is called irreducible if it has precisely two $L$-submodules (itself and $0$) and completely reducible if $V$ is a direct sum of irreducible $L$-submodules.

If $V$ and $W$ are arbitrary $L$-modules, their direct sum is an $L$-module via $x.(v, w) = (x.v, x.w)$ for $x \in L$, $v \in V$, $w \in W$.
