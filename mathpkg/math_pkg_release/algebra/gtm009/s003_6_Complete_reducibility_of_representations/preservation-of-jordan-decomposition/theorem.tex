Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra. An abstract Jordan decomposition $x = x_s + x_n$ ($x_s$ semisimple, $x_n$ nilpotent, $[x_s, x_n] = 0$) exists in $L$. For any (finite dimensional) representation $\phi: L \rightarrow \mathfrak{gl}(V)$, the image $\phi(L)$ (which is a subalgebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$) contains the semisimple and nilpotent parts $\phi(x)_s$, $\phi(x)_n$ of each of its elements. Moreover, $\phi(x)_s = \phi(x_s)$ and $\phi(x)_n = \phi(x_n)$.
