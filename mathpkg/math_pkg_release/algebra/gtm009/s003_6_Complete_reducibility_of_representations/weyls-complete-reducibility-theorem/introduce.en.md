Weyl's Theorem is the foundational result in the representation theory of semisimple Lie algebras, stating that every finite-dimensional representation decomposes as a direct sum of irreducible submodules. The proof uses the Casimir element to construct complementary submodules, first handling the case of a codimension-one irreducible submodule and then reducing the general case using the $L$-module structure on Hom spaces.
