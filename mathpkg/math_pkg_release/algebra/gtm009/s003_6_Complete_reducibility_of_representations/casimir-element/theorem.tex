Let $L$ be semisimple and let $\phi: L \rightarrow \mathfrak{gl}(V)$ be a faithful representation of $L$. Define a symmetric bilinear form $\beta(x, y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\phi(x)\phi(y))$ on $L$. The form $\beta$ is associative and nondegenerate. Let $(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ be a basis of $L$ and $(y_1, \ldots, y_n)$ the dual basis relative to $\beta$ (so $\beta(x_i, y_j) = \delta_{ij}$). The Casimir element of $\phi$ is:

$$c_\phi = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \phi(x_i)\phi(y_i) \in \operatorname{End} V.$$

Then $c_\phi$ commutes with $\phi(L)$ and $\operatorname{Tr}(c_\phi) = \dim L$.

When $\phi$ is not faithful, let $L'$ be the sum of the simple ideals of $L$ not in $\ker\phi$. Then $\phi|_{L'}$ is faithful, and the same construction using $L'$ yields $c_\phi$, which commutes with $\phi(L) = \phi(L')$.
