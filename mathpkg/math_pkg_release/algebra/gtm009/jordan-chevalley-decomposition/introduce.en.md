The Jordan-Chevalley decomposition expresses any linear endomorphism uniquely as the sum of a semisimple (diagonalizable) part and a nilpotent part that commute. Both parts are polynomials in the original endomorphism, which implies they preserve any subspace preserved by the original map. This decomposition is fundamental throughout Lie theory, used to define semisimple and nilpotent elements abstractly in any semisimple Lie algebra.
