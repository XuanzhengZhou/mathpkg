Let $V$ be a finite dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field $F$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] For any $x \in \text{End } V$, there exist unique $x_s, x_n \in \text{End } V$ satisfying: $x = x_s + x_n$, $x_s$ is semisimple, $x_n$ is nilpotent, and $x_s x_n = x_n x_s$.
\item[(b)] There exist polynomials $p(T), q(T) \in F[T]$ with no constant term such that $x_s = p(x)$, $x_n = q(x)$. In particular, $x_s$ and $x_n$ commute with any endomorphism commuting with $x$.
\item[(c)] If $A \subset B \subset V$ are subspaces and $x$ maps $B$ into $A$, then $x_s$ and $x_n$ also map $B$ into $A$.
\end{enumerate}

An endomorphism $x$ is called \text{semisimple} if the roots of its minimal polynomial are all distinct. Equivalently (over an algebraically closed field), $x$ is diagonalizable.
