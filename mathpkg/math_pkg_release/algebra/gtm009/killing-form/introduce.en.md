The Killing form $\kappa(x,y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\operatorname{ad} x \circ \operatorname{ad} y)$ is a canonical symmetric bilinear form on any Lie algebra. It is associative with respect to the bracket, making its radical an ideal. The nondegeneracy of the Killing form characterizes semisimple Lie algebras (Cartan's criterion), making it the central tool for the structure theory and classification of semisimple Lie algebras.
