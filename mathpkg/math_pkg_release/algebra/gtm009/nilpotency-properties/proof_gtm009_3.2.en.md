\textbf{Proof.}
(a) Imitate the proof of Proposition 3.1(a), using the descending central series.

(b) Suppose $L^n \subset Z(L)$. Then $L^{n+1} = [L L^n] \subset [L Z(L)] = 0$, so $L$ is nilpotent.

(c) The last nonzero term of the descending central series is contained in the center. Therefore $Z(L) \neq 0$.
