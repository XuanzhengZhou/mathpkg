Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra over an algebraically closed field of characteristic 0, and let $H$ be a maximal toral subalgebra (a Cartan subalgebra). Then $L$ decomposes as
$$L = H \oplus \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Phi} L_\alpha,$$
where $\Phi \subset H^*$ (the dual space of $H$) is the set of nonzero \text{roots}, and
$$L_\alpha = \{x \in L \mid [h, x] = \alpha(h)x \text{ for all } h \in H\}$$
is the \text{root space} corresponding to $\alpha$. Each $L_\alpha$ is one-dimensional. Moreover:
\begin{itemize}
\item $[L_\alpha, L_\beta] \subset L_{\alpha+\beta}$ (with the convention $L_{\alpha+\beta} = 0$ if $\alpha+\beta$ is not a root),
\item $\alpha \in \Phi$ implies $-\alpha \in \Phi$, and no other scalar multiple of $\alpha$ is a root,
\item The Killing form restricted to $H$ is nondegenerate, allowing identification of $H$ with $H^*$.
\end{itemize}
