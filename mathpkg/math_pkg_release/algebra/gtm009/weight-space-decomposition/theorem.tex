Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra over an algebraically closed field of characteristic 0, $H$ a Cartan subalgebra, and $V$ a finite-dimensional $L$-module. Then $V$ decomposes as a direct sum of \text{weight spaces}:
$$V = \bigoplus_{\lambda \in H^*} V_\lambda,$$
where $V_\lambda = \{v \in V \mid h.v = \lambda(h)v \text{ for all } h \in H\}$. The $\lambda$ for which $V_\lambda \neq 0$ are called \text{weights} of $V$. There is a unique maximal weight (the \text{highest weight}) relative to a fixed base of the root system, and the module is irreducible if and only if it is a highest weight module generated by a maximal vector.
