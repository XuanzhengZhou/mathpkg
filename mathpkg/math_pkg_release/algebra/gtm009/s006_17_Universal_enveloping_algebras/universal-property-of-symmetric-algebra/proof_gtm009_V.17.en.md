By the universal property of the tensor algebra, the linear map $\phi: V \to A$ extends uniquely to an algebra homomorphism $\tilde{\psi}: \mathfrak{T}(V) \to A$. Since $A$ is commutative, for any $x, y \in V$ we have $\tilde{\psi}(x \otimes y - y \otimes x) = \phi(x)\phi(y) - \phi(y)\phi(x) = 0$. Thus the generators of the ideal $I$ lie in $\ker \tilde{\psi}$, so $\tilde{\psi}$ factors through $\mathfrak{S}(V) = \mathfrak{T}(V)/I$, yielding the required homomorphism $\psi: \mathfrak{S}(V) \to A$. Uniqueness follows because $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ is generated by the image of $V$ together with $1$.
