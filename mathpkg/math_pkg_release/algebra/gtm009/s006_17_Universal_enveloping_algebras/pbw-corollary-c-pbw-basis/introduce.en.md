The PBW basis theorem gives an explicit vector space basis for the universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. Given an ordered basis $(x_1, x_2, \dots)$ of $L$, the set of all ordered monomials $x_{i(1)}\dots x_{i(m)}$ with non-decreasing indices, together with the unit $1$, forms a basis. This is the direct analogue of the monomial basis for polynomial rings and is the most commonly used consequence of the PBW theorem in computations within $\mathfrak{U}(L)$.
