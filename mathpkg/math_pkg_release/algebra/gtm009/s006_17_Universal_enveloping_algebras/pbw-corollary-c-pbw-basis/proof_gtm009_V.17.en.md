Let $W$ be the subspace of $T^m$ spanned by all $x_{i(1)} \otimes \dots \otimes x_{i(m)}$ with $i(1) \leq \dots \leq i(m)$. Restating the definition of $\mathfrak{S}$: the image of $W$ under the canonical map $T^m \to S^m$ is all of $S^m$, and distinct such increasing-index tensors have distinct images (modulo the commutativity relations). Thus the canonical map sends $W$ isomorphically onto $S^m$. By Corollary A, $\pi(W)$ is a complement to $U_{m-1}$ in $U_m$. Iterating this for all $m \geq 0$ and taking the union over all $m$ yields that the set of all $\pi(x_{i(1)} \otimes \dots \otimes x_{i(m)})$ with increasing indices, together with $1$, forms a basis of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$.
