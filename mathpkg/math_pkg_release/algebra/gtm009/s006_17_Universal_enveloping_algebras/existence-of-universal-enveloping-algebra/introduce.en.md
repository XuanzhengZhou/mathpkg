The universal enveloping algebra always exists and can be constructed explicitly as a quotient of the tensor algebra. One takes $\mathfrak{T}(L)$ and factors out the two-sided ideal generated by $x \otimes y - y \otimes x - [xy]$ for all $x, y \in L$. This enforces precisely the commutation relations of the Lie bracket in the associative setting. The PBW theorem later guarantees that the canonical map $i: L \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$ is injective, so $L$ embeds faithfully in its universal enveloping algebra.
