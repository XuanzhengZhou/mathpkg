The tensor algebra satisfies a universal property: any linear map from $V$ into an associative algebra $A$ extends uniquely to an algebra homomorphism from $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ to $A$. This is the defining characteristic that makes $\mathfrak{T}(V)$ the "free associative algebra" on $V$. The property is used to construct the universal enveloping algebra by factoring out the commutation relations of a Lie algebra.
