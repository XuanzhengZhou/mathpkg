Once a basis of $V$ is fixed, the symmetric algebra $\mathfrak{S}(V)$ becomes isomorphic to the familiar polynomial ring in $n$ variables. The monomials $x_{i(1)}\dots x_{i(t)}$ with non-decreasing indices, together with $1$, form a vector space basis. This identification is essential for understanding the PBW theorem, where the associated graded algebra $\mathfrak{G}$ of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ is shown to be isomorphic to a polynomial algebra.
