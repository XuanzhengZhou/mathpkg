This is the special case $W = T^1 = L$ of Corollary A. The canonical map $T^1 \to S^1$ is the identity (both are equal to $L = V$), so it sends $W = L$ isomorphically onto $S^1$. By Corollary A, $\pi(L)$ is a complement to $U_0$ in $U_1$. Since $U_0 = F\cdot 1$ and $i: L \to U_1$ is the restriction of $\pi$ to $L$, the fact that $\pi(L) \cap U_0 = 0$ means $i$ is injective.
