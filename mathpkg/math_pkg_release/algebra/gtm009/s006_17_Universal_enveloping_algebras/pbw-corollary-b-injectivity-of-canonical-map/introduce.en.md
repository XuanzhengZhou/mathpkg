A crucial consequence of the PBW theorem is that the natural map $i: L \to \mathfrak{U}(L)$ is injective. This means every Lie algebra embeds faithfully into its universal enveloping algebra. Thus, when studying the representation theory of $L$, one may safely view $L$ as a Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ (under the commutator bracket), and any identity holding in $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ restricts faithfully to $L$.
