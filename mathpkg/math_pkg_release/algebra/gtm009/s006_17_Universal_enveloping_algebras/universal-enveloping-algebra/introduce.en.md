The universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ of a Lie algebra $L$ is an associative algebra that encodes the representation theory of $L$. A representation of $L$ is equivalent to a module over $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. The defining universal property says that any Lie algebra homomorphism from $L$ into an associative algebra factors uniquely through $\mathfrak{U}(L)$. This makes $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ the "free associative algebra" on the vector space $L$ subject to the commutation relations of the Lie bracket.
