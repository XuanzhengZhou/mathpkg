Let $L$ be a Lie algebra over $F$. A \text{universal enveloping algebra} of $L$ is a pair $(\mathfrak{U}, i)$, where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\mathfrak{U}$ is an associative algebra with $1$ over $F$,
\item $i: L \to \mathfrak{U}$ is a linear map satisfying $i([xy]) = i(x)i(y) - i(y)i(x)$ for all $x, y \in L$ (i.e., $i$ is a Lie algebra homomorphism when $\mathfrak{U}$ is viewed as a Lie algebra via the commutator bracket),
\end{itemize}
such that for any associative $F$-algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ with $1$ and any Lie algebra homomorphism $j: L \to \mathfrak{A}$ (where $\mathfrak{A}$ carries the commutator bracket), there exists a unique algebra homomorphism $\phi: \mathfrak{U} \to \mathfrak{A}$ sending $1$ to $1$ and satisfying $\phi \circ i = j$.
