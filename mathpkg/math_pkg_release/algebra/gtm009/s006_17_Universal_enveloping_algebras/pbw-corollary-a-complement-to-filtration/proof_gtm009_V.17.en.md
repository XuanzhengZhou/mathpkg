Consider the commutative diagram (all maps canonical):
\[
\begin{array}{ccc}
U_m & \to & G^m \\
& & \downarrow \\
S^m & \to & T^m
\end{array}
\]
By the PBW theorem, $\omega: \mathfrak{S} \to \mathfrak{G}$ is an isomorphism, so the bottom map sends $W \subset T^m$ isomorphically onto $G^m$. Reverting to the top map, the composite $W \to U_m \to G^m = U_m/U_{m-1}$ is an isomorphism, which means precisely that $\pi(W)$ is a complement to $U_{m-1}$ in $U_m$.
