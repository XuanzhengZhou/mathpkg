Assume $\operatorname{char} F = 0$. The symmetric group $\mathcal{S}_m$ acts on $T^mV$ by permuting subscripts of tensors $v_1 \otimes \dots \otimes v_m$ ($v_i \in V$). An element of $T^mV$ fixed by $\mathcal{S}_m$ is called a \text{homogeneous symmetric tensor} of order $m$.

For a fixed basis $(x_1, \dots, x_n)$ of $V$, and an ordered sequence $1 \leq i(1) \leq i(2) \leq \dots \leq i(m) \leq n$, define a symmetric tensor
$$
\frac{1}{m!} \sum_{\pi \in \mathcal{S}_m} x_{i(\pi(1))} \otimes \dots \otimes x_{i(\pi(m))}
$$
(which is well-defined since $m! \neq 0$ in $F$).
