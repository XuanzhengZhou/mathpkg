This exercise proves that every nonzero nilpotent Lie algebra admits an outer derivation, i.e., a derivation not of the form $\operatorname{ad} z$ for any $z \in L$. Using the codimension-1 ideal from Exercise 8, one constructs a derivation that vanishes on the ideal and maps the complementary element into the centralizer. This shows that for nilpotent algebras, $\operatorname{Der} L \supsetneqq \operatorname{ad} L$, contrasting with the semisimple case where every derivation is inner.
