Prove that every nilpotent Lie algebra $L \neq 0$ has an outer derivation (see (1.3)), as follows: Write $L = K + Fx$ for some ideal $K$ of codimension one (Exercise 8). Then $C_L(K) \neq 0$ (why?), so we may choose such an $x \in C_L(K)$. Then define $\delta = \text{linear map}$ sending $K$ to $0$, $x$ to some nonzero element of $C_L(K)$; check that $\delta$ is a derivation.
