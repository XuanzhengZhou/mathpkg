This proposition establishes basic properties of nilpotent Lie algebras. Analogous to the solvable case, nilpotency is preserved under subalgebras and quotients (a). A key difference from solvability is part (b): nilpotency of $L/Z(L)$ implies nilpotency of $L$, which is not true if one replaces the center with a general ideal. Part (c) states that every nonzero nilpotent Lie algebra has a nontrivial center, a fact that is essential in the proof of Engel's Theorem.
