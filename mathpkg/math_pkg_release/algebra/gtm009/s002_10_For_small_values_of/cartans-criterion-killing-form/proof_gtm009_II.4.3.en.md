Apply Cartan's Criterion (the theorem) to the adjoint representation $\operatorname{ad} L \subset \mathfrak{gl}(L)$. The hypothesis $\operatorname{Tr}(\operatorname{ad} x \operatorname{ad} y) = 0$ for $x \in [LL]$, $y \in L$ is precisely the condition required by Cartan's Criterion applied to $\operatorname{ad} L$. Therefore $\operatorname{ad} L$ is solvable.

Now $\operatorname{Ker}(\operatorname{ad}) = Z(L)$ is abelian, hence solvable. Since $\operatorname{ad} L \cong L / Z(L)$, the solvability of $\operatorname{ad} L$ means $L / Z(L)$ is solvable. With $Z(L)$ also solvable, Proposition 3.1(b) (extension of solvable by solvable is solvable) implies that $L$ itself is solvable.
