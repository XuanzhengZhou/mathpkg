Lie's Theorem is the solvable analogue of Engel's Theorem. It states that any solvable Lie algebra of linear transformations over an algebraically closed field of characteristic zero possesses a common eigenvector. Equivalently, a solvable linear Lie algebra can be simultaneously triangularized. The theorem requires both algebraic closure (to guarantee the existence of eigenvalues) and characteristic zero (as counterexamples exist in prime characteristic).
