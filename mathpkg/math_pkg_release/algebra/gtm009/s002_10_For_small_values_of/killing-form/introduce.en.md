The Killing form is a symmetric bilinear form on a Lie algebra $L$ defined by $\kappa(x, y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\operatorname{ad} x \operatorname{ad} y)$. It is named after Wilhelm Killing and plays a central role in the structure theory of semisimple Lie algebras. The key property of the Killing form is associativity: $\kappa([x, y], z) = \kappa(x, [y, z])$, which implies that the radical of $\kappa$ is an ideal. Cartan's criterion can be expressed elegantly in terms of the Killing form: $L$ is semisimple if and only if $\kappa$ is nondegenerate.
