This technical lemma provides a trace-theoretic criterion for an endomorphism to be nilpotent. Given subspaces $A \subset B$ of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ and an element $x$ satisfying $[x, B] \subset A$, the vanishing of $\operatorname{Tr}(xy)$ for all $y$ in the set $M = \{y \mid [y, B] \subset A\}$ forces $x$ to be nilpotent. The proof uses the Jordan decomposition $x = x_s + x_n$: the semisimple part $x_s$ must vanish because its eigenvalues would otherwise contribute a nonzero trace. This lemma is the core technical ingredient in the proof of Cartan's Criterion.
