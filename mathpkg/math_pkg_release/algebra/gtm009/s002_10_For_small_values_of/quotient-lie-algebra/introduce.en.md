Given a Lie algebra $L$ and an ideal $I \subseteq L$, the quotient Lie algebra $L/I$ has the quotient vector space structure and the bracket defined by $[x+I, y+I] = [xy] + I$. This is well-defined precisely because $I$ is an ideal: the ambiguity terms $[uy] + [xv] + [uv]$ all belong to $I$. Quotient Lie algebras are the natural setting for the isomorphism theorems and provide a way to factor out ideals.
