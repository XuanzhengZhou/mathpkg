The construction of a quotient algebra $L/I$ ($I$ an ideal of $L$) is formally the same as the construction of a quotient ring: as vector space $L/I$ is just the quotient space, while its Lie multiplication is defined by
$$[x+I, y+I] = [xy]+I.$$
This is unambiguous, since if $x+I = x'+I$, $y+I = y'+I$, then we have $x' = x+u$ ($u \in I$), $y' = y+v$ ($v \in I$), whence $[x'y'] = [xy] + ([uy] + [xv] + [uv])$, and therefore $[x'y']+I = [xy]+I$, since the terms in parentheses all lie in $I$.
