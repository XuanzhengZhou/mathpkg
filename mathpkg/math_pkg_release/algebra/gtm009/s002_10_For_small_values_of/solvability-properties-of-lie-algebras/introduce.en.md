This proposition collects the fundamental properties of solvability for Lie algebras. Solvability is inherited by subalgebras and quotients (a), extensions of solvable algebras are solvable (b), and the sum of two solvable ideals is again a solvable ideal (c). These properties parallel the analogous facts for solvable groups and form the basis for the existence of a unique maximal solvable ideal (the radical).
