An ideal of a Lie algebra $L$ is a subspace $I \subseteq L$ closed under the bracket with any element of $L$. This is the Lie-theoretic analogue of a normal subgroup in group theory and a two-sided ideal in ring theory. The condition $[L, I] \subseteq I$ ensures that ideals serve as kernels of Lie algebra homomorphisms and are precisely the subspaces by which one can form quotient Lie algebras.
