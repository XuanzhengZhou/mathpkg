The isomorphism theorems for Lie algebras mirror those for groups and rings. Part (b) is the third isomorphism theorem: $(L/I)/(J/I) \cong L/J$ whenever $I \subseteq J$ are ideals. Part (c) is the second isomorphism theorem: $(I+J)/J \cong I/(I \cap J)$ for arbitrary ideals $I, J$ of $L$. These structural results are fundamental for working with quotient Lie algebras.
