This exercise gives an alternative characterization of solvability: a Lie algebra $L$ is solvable if and only if it admits a chain of subalgebras, each an ideal in the previous one, with each successive quotient being abelian. This is the Lie algebra analogue of subnormal series with abelian factors in group theory. The derived series provides such a chain, and conversely, any chain with abelian quotients implies that the derived series terminates at zero.
