A semisimple Lie algebra is one whose radical (the maximal solvable ideal) is zero. Equivalently, $L$ contains no nonzero abelian ideals. Cartan's criterion provides the fundamental characterization: $L$ is semisimple if and only if its Killing form is nondegenerate. Semisimple Lie algebras form the central objects of study in the structure theory: they decompose as direct sums of simple ideals, and over algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero they are classified by Dynkin diagrams.
