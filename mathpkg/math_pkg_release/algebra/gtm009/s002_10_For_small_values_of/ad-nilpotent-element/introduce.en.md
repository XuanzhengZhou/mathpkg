An element $x$ of a Lie algebra $L$ is called ad-nilpotent when its adjoint action $\operatorname{ad} x$ is a nilpotent linear transformation. If $L$ is nilpotent, then every element is ad-nilpotent (but the converse is not true — Engel's Theorem provides the missing direction when all elements of a Lie algebra of linear transformations are nilpotent). The concept is central to the proof of Engel's Theorem.
