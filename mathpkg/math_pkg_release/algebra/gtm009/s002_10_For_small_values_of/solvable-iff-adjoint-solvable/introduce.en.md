This exercise establishes that solvability and nilpotency of a Lie algebra $L$ are equivalent to the corresponding properties of its adjoint image $\operatorname{ad} L \subseteq \mathfrak{gl}(L)$. Since $\operatorname{Ker}(\operatorname{ad}) = Z(L)$ is abelian (hence solvable), and abelian algebras do not affect solvability/nilpotency, the properties lift from $\operatorname{ad} L$ back to $L$. This allows one to use theorems about linear Lie algebras to study abstract ones.
