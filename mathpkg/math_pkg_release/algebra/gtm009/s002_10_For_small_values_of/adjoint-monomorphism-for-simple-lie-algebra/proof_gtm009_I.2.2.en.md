If $L$ is simple, then it has no nontrivial proper ideals. The center $Z(L)$ is always an ideal of $L$. Because $L$ is not abelian (it is simple and not one-dimensional), $Z(L)$ cannot equal $L$, so $Z(L)$ must be a proper ideal. Since $L$ is simple, the only proper ideal is $0$, hence $Z(L) = 0$. But $\operatorname{Ker} \operatorname{ad} = Z(L)$ (as shown in the proposition on the kernel of the adjoint representation), so $\operatorname{ad}: L \to \mathfrak{gl}(L)$ has trivial kernel and is therefore a monomorphism.
