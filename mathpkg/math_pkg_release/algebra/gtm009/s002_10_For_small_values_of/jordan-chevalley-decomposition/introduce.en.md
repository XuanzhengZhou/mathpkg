The Jordan-Chevalley decomposition expresses any endomorphism $x$ of a finite-dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field as a sum $x = x_s + x_n$ of a semisimple part $x_s$ and a nilpotent part $x_n$ that commute. The decomposition is unique, and both $x_s$ and $x_n$ are polynomials in $x$ with zero constant term. This decomposition generalizes the Jordan canonical form and is a fundamental technical tool throughout the theory of semisimple Lie algebras and algebraic groups.
