Call $x \in \operatorname{End} V$ ($V$ finite dimensional) \text{semisimple} if the roots of its minimal polynomial over $F$ are all distinct. Equivalently ($F$ being algebraically closed), $x$ is semisimple if and only if $x$ is diagonalizable. Two commuting semisimple endomorphisms can be simultaneously diagonalized; therefore, their sum or difference is again semisimple.
