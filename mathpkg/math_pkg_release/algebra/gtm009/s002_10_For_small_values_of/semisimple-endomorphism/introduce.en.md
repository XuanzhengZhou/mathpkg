A semisimple endomorphism is a linear transformation whose minimal polynomial has distinct roots. Over an algebraically closed field, this is equivalent to diagonalizability. The class of semisimple endomorphisms is closed under sums and differences of commuting elements, a property that is crucial for the Jordan-Chevalley decomposition theorem. This concept generalizes the notion of diagonalizable matrices and plays a fundamental role in the structure theory of Lie algebras.
