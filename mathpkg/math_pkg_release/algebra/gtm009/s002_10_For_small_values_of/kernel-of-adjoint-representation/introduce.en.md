An element $x \in L$ satisfies $\operatorname{ad} x = 0$ precisely when $[x, y] = 0$ for all $y \in L$, which is exactly the definition of the center $Z(L)$. Thus the kernel of the adjoint representation is exactly the center of the Lie algebra. This gives a fundamental relationship: $\operatorname{ad}$ is injective if and only if $Z(L) = 0$.
