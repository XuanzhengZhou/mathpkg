This fundamental structure theorem states that every semisimple Lie algebra decomposes uniquely as a direct sum of simple ideals. The proof proceeds by induction on dimension, using the nondegeneracy of the Killing form: for any ideal $I$, the orthogonal complement $I^\perp$ with respect to the Killing form is also an ideal, and $I \cap I^\perp$ is solvable by Cartan's criterion (hence zero in the semisimple case). This yields $L = I \oplus I^\perp$, and the decomposition follows. The theorem reduces the classification of semisimple algebras to that of simple ones.
