The general linear algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is the most fundamental example of a Lie algebra. It is obtained from the associative algebra of endomorphisms of a vector space by replacing the usual product with the commutator bracket $[x,y] = xy - yx$. This construction motivates the entire theory of Lie algebras, as every Lie algebra can be embedded into some $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ via a representation.
