Let $V$ be a finite dimensional vector space over a field $F$. Denote by $\operatorname{End} V$ the set of linear transformations $V \rightarrow V$. Define a new operation
$$[x, y] = xy - yx,$$
called the bracket of $x$ and $y$. With this operation $\operatorname{End} V$ becomes a Lie algebra over $F$. To distinguish this Lie algebra structure from the associative one, we write $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ for $\operatorname{End} V$ viewed as a Lie algebra and call it the general linear algebra. When a basis is fixed, $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is identified with the set of all $n \times n$ matrices over $F$, denoted $\mathfrak{gl}(n, F)$.
