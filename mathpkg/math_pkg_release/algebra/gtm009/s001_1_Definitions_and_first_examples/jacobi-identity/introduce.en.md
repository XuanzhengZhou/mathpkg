The Jacobi identity $[x[yz]] + [y[zx]] + [z[xy]] = 0$ is the third and most subtle axiom of a Lie algebra. It serves as the Lie-algebraic analogue of the associative law, ensuring that the adjoint representation $x \mapsto \operatorname{ad} x$ is a Lie algebra homomorphism. Together with bilinearity and alternativity, the Jacobi identity characterizes the algebraic behavior of commutators in associative algebras.
