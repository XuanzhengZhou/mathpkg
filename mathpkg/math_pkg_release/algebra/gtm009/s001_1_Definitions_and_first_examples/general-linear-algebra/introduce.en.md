The general linear algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is the fundamental example of a Lie algebra: it is the space of all linear endomorphisms of a vector space $V$ with the commutator bracket $[x,y] = xy - yx$. When a basis is chosen, it identifies with the matrix Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(n,F)$. Every linear Lie algebra is a subalgebra of some $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$.
