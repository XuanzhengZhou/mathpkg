The Lie bracket is anticommutative: $[xy] = -[yx]$ for all $x,y \in L$. This follows directly from bilinearity and the alternating property (L2) by expanding $[x+y, x+y] = 0$. In characteristic not equal to 2, anticommutativity is equivalent to the alternating condition, but in characteristic 2 the alternating property is the stronger statement.
