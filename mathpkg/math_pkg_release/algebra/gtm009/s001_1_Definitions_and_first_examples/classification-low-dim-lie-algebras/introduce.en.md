All Lie algebras of dimension at most 2 are completely classified. In dimension 1, only the abelian algebra exists. In dimension 2, there is exactly one nonabelian Lie algebra (with bracket $[xy] = x$) in addition to the abelian one. This classification demonstrates how severely the Jacobi identity restricts possible Lie algebra structures.
