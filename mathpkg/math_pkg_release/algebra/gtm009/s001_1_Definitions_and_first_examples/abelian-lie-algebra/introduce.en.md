An abelian Lie algebra is the simplest possible Lie algebra, in which all brackets vanish. It arises naturally in the classification of low-dimensional Lie algebras: in dimension 1, every Lie algebra is abelian; in dimension 2, either the algebra is abelian or it has a unique nonabelian structure. The term comes from the analogous notion in group theory, where the commutator of any two elements is the identity.
