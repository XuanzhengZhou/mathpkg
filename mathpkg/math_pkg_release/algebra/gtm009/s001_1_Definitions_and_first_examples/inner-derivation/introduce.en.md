An inner derivation of a Lie algebra $L$ is a derivation of the form $\operatorname{ad} x$ for some $x \in L$, mapping $y \mapsto [xy]$. The Jacobi identity guarantees that $\operatorname{ad} x$ satisfies the derivation property. Inner derivations form an ideal in the Lie algebra of all derivations.
