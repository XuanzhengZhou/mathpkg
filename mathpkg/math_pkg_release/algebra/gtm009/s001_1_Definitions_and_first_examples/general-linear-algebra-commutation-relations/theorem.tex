Let $e_{ij}$ denote the $n \times n$ matrix with 1 in the $(i, j)$ position and 0 elsewhere. These matrices form the standard basis of $\mathfrak{gl}(n, F)$. Since $e_{ij}e_{kl} = \delta_{jk}e_{il}$, the commutation relations are:
$$[e_{ij}, e_{kl}] = \delta_{jk}e_{il} - \delta_{li}e_{kj}.$$
All structure constants are $\pm 1$ or $0$; in particular, they lie in the prime field.
