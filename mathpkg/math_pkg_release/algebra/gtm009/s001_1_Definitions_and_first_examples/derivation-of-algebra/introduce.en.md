A derivation of an $F$-algebra is a linear map satisfying the Leibniz product rule. Derivations generalize the notion of directional derivatives from calculus to abstract algebraic settings. The set of all derivations forms a vector space, and the commutator of two derivations is again a derivation, making $\operatorname{Der} \mathfrak{A}$ a Lie subalgebra of $\mathfrak{gl}(\mathfrak{A})$.
