The classification of Lie algebras of dimension at most 2 is straightforward: in dimension 1 there is only the abelian algebra, while in dimension 2 there are exactly two isomorphism classes -- the abelian one and a unique nonabelian one. This nonabelian 2-dimensional algebra is solvable but not nilpotent and serves as a basic counterexample in the structure theory.
