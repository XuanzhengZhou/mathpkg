**Proof.** By the definitions in (1.2), each of $B_\ell, C_\ell, D_\ell$ consists of endomorphisms of trace $0$ on a vector space $V$, so by Proposition 19.1(b) it suffices to prove that each acts irreducibly on $V$ in its natural representation.

Recall that any subspace of $V$ invariant under a subalgebra $L$ of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is also invariant under the associative subalgebra of $\operatorname{End} V$ generated by $1$ and $L$. Hence it is enough to show that all endomorphisms of $V$ are obtainable from $1$ and $L$ using addition, scalar multiplication, and ordinary matrix multiplication.

- From $1$ we obtain all scalars.
- From the diagonal matrices exhibited in (1.2) we obtain all diagonal matrices.
- Multiplying the off-diagonal basis elements (such as $e_{ij} - e_{ji}$, $i \neq j$) by suitable diagonal matrices of the form $\operatorname{diag}(0,\ldots,1,\ldots,0)$ yields all off-diagonal matrix units $e_{ij}$.

Thus the associative algebra generated by $1$ and $L$ is the full matrix algebra $\operatorname{End} V$, so $V$ is irreducible as an $L$-module. The diagonal subalgebra $H$ is clearly toral and equals its own centralizer, hence is a Cartan subalgebra. $\square$
