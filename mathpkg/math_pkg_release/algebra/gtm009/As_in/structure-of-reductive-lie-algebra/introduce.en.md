This proposition provides the structural decomposition of a reductive Lie algebra. It states that any reductive Lie algebra $L$ splits as the direct sum of its derived subalgebra (which is semisimple) and its center. The proof uses the fact that for a reductive non-abelian Lie algebra, the adjoint representation of the semisimple quotient $L/Z(L)$ acts completely reducibly, allowing one to extract the center as a direct summand.
