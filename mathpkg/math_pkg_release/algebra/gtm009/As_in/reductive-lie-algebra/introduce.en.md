A Lie algebra $L$ is reductive if its radical coincides with its center. This class interpolates between the two extremes of abelian Lie algebras (where $\operatorname{Rad} L = Z(L) = L$) and semisimple Lie algebras (where $\operatorname{Rad} L = Z(L) = 0$). The general linear Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is a key non-semisimple, non-abelian example of a reductive Lie algebra. The concept is introduced in connection with a practical criterion for semisimplicity based on irreducibility of the natural representation.
