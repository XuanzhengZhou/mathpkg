This theorem verifies that the Verma module $Z(\lambda)$ constructed by induction from the Borel subalgebra is indeed standard cyclic of weight $\lambda$. The key to the proof is that $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ is a free $\mathfrak{U}(B)$-module (Corollary D of the PBW Theorem), which guarantees that the element $1 \otimes v^+$ is nonzero and remains a maximal vector. The identification of $Z(\lambda)$ with $\mathfrak{U}(N^-)$ as vector spaces provides a concrete realization useful for further study.
