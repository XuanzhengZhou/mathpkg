This proposition collects the basic hereditary and closure properties of solvability. Solvability is preserved under taking subalgebras and quotients, and the class of solvable Lie algebras is closed under extensions and finite sums of ideals. These properties are essential for constructing the radical as the unique maximal solvable ideal.
