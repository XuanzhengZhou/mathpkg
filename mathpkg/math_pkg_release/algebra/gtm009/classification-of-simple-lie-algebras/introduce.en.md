The classification of finite-dimensional simple Lie algebras over algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero is one of the pinnacles of modern algebra. It was achieved by Killing and Cartan in the late 19th century. The complete list consists of four infinite families (the classical algebras of types $A, B, C, D$) and five exceptional algebras ($G_2, F_4, E_6, E_7, E_8$). The classification is expressed elegantly via Dynkin diagrams, which encode the Cartan matrix of the root system.
