Cartan's criterion provides a computationally effective test for solvability of a Lie algebra using traces. It states that if the trace of every product $xy$ vanishes for $x$ in the derived algebra and $y$ in $L$, then $L$ is solvable. Applied to the adjoint representation, this yields the criterion $\operatorname{Tr}(\operatorname{ad} x \circ \operatorname{ad} y) = 0$, which is the foundation for the Killing form and the theory of semisimple Lie algebras.
