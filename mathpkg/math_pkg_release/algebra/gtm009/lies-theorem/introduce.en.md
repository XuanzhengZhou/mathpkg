Lie's theorem is the analogue of Engel's theorem for solvable Lie algebras over algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero. It guarantees the existence of a common eigenvector for a solvable Lie algebra of linear transformations. A key corollary is that every finite-dimensional irreducible representation of a solvable Lie algebra is one-dimensional. The theorem requires characteristic zero (counterexamples exist in positive characteristic).
