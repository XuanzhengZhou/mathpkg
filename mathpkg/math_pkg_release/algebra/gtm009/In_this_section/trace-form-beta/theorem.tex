Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra and let $\phi: L \rightarrow \mathfrak{gl}(V)$ be a faithful representation of $L$. Define a symmetric bilinear form $\beta(x, y)$ on $L$ by:

$$\beta(x, y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\phi(x)\phi(y)).$$

The form $\beta$ is associative and nondegenerate. Its radical is an ideal of $L$. In the special case $\phi = \operatorname{ad}$, $\beta$ is the Killing form.
