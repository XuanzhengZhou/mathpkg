When $V$ and $W$ are $L$-modules, the space $\operatorname{Hom}(V,W)$ of linear maps acquires a natural $L$-module structure via the given action. This arises from the isomorphism $\operatorname{Hom}(V,W) \cong V^* \otimes W$ and the previously defined module structures on duals and tensor products. This construction plays a crucial role in the proof of Weyl's Theorem, where one studies subspaces of $\operatorname{Hom}(V,W)$ to find invariant complements.
