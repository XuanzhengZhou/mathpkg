Weyl's Theorem is one of the foundational results in the representation theory of semisimple Lie algebras. It states that every finite dimensional representation of a semisimple Lie algebra is completely reducible — equivalently, every submodule has an invariant complement. The proof uses the Casimir element to construct a complementary submodule when the quotient is one-dimensional, then reduces the general case via considerations on $\operatorname{Hom}(V,W)$. The theorem implies that the representation theory of semisimple Lie algebras reduces to the study of irreducible representations.
