An $L$-module is the module-theoretic formulation of a Lie algebra representation. A vector space $V$ equipped with a bilinear action of $L$ satisfying the Jacobi-like compatibility condition $[xy].v = x.y.v - y.x.v$. Given a representation $\phi: L \to \mathfrak{gl}(V)$, the action $x.v = \phi(x)(v)$ makes $V$ into an $L$-module, and conversely every $L$-module defines a representation. The terminology of irreducibility and complete reducibility carries over directly.
