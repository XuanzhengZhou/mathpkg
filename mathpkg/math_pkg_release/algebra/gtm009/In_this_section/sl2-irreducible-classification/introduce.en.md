This theorem completely classifies all finite dimensional irreducible modules for $\mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$. For each integer $m \geq 0$, there exists exactly one irreducible module of dimension $m+1$ (up to isomorphism). The weights of such a module form an unbroken string $m, m-2, \ldots, -m$ with each weight space one-dimensional. The module is generated by a unique (up to scalar) maximal vector of weight $m$, and the action is given by the explicit formulas of Lemma 7.2. This classification serves as the model for the highest weight theory of arbitrary semisimple Lie algebras.
