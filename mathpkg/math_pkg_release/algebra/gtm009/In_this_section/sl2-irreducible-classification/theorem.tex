Let $V$ be an irreducible module for $L = \mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$.

\text{(a)} Relative to $h$, $V$ is the direct sum of weight spaces $V_\mu$, $\mu = m, m-2, \ldots, -(m-2), -m$, where $m+1 = \dim V$ and $\dim V_\mu = 1$ for each $\mu$.

\text{(b)} $V$ has (up to nonzero scalar multiples) a unique maximal vector, whose weight (called the \text{highest weight} of $V$) is $m$.

\text{(c)} The action of $L$ on $V$ is given explicitly by formulas (a)-(c) of Lemma 7.2. In particular, there exists at most one irreducible $L$-module (up to isomorphism) of each possible dimension $m+1$, $m \geq 0$.
