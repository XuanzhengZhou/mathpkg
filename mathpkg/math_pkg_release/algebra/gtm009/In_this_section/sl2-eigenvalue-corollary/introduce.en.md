This corollary extends the classification of irreducible $\mathfrak{sl}(2, \mathbb{F})$-modules to arbitrary finite dimensional modules. By Weyl's Theorem, any module decomposes into irreducibles, and each irreducible contributes integer weights symmetric about zero. Consequently, the eigenvalues of $h$ on any module are integers, and the multiplicities of $\mu$ and $-\mu$ coincide. The number of irreducible summands can be read off from the dimensions of the $0$- and $1$-weight spaces, since each irreducible has a unique occurrence of either weight 0 or weight 1 (but not both).
