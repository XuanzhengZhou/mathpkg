The Casimir element $c_\phi$ is a distinguished element of $\operatorname{End} V$ constructed from a faithful representation $\phi$ of a semisimple Lie algebra $L$. Using the nondegenerate trace form to obtain dual bases, $c_\phi$ is the sum of products of corresponding basis elements. Its crucial properties are: it commutes with $\phi(L)$ and has trace equal to $\dim L$ (not zero). These properties make it the key algebraic tool in proving Weyl's complete reducibility theorem — a nonzero $L$-module endomorphism whose kernel provides an invariant complement.
