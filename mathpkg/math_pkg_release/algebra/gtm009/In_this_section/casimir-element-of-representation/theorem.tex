Let $L$ be a semisimple Lie algebra and $\phi: L \rightarrow \mathfrak{gl}(V)$ a faithful representation. Let $\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ be a basis of $L$ and $\{y_1, \ldots, y_n\}$ the dual basis relative to the trace form $\beta(x,y) = \operatorname{Tr}(\phi(x)\phi(y))$. The \text{Casimir element} of $\phi$ is:

$$c_\phi = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \phi(x_i)\phi(y_i) \in \operatorname{End} V.$$

It satisfies $\operatorname{Tr}(c_\phi) = \dim L$ and commutes with $\phi(L)$. When $\phi$ is not faithful, restrict to the sum $L'$ of simple ideals on which $\phi$ is faithful, and define $c_\phi$ using a basis of $L'$.
