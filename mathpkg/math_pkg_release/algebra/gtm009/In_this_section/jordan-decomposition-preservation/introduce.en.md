Theorem 6.4 establishes that a semisimple Lie subalgebra $L$ of $\mathfrak{gl}(V)$ is closed under the Jordan decomposition — the semisimple and nilpotent parts of every element remain in $L$. This result is fundamental because it implies that the abstract Jordan decomposition (defined intrinsically via the adjoint representation) coincides with the usual Jordan decomposition for any representation. The proof uses Weyl's Theorem to decompose a carefully constructed containing algebra $L'$ as $L \oplus M$ and then shows $M = 0$ by examining the action on irreducible submodules.
