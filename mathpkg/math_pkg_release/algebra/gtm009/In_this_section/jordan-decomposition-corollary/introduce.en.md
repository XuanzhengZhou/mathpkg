This corollary to Theorem 6.4 establishes that the abstract Jordan decomposition in a semisimple Lie algebra $L$ is compatible with any finite dimensional representation. If $x = s + n$ is the abstract decomposition (where $s$ acts semisimply and $n$ acts nilpotently via the adjoint representation), then under any representation $\phi$, the images $\phi(s)$ and $\phi(n)$ are respectively semisimple and nilpotent in the usual sense of linear algebra, and they commute. This ensures that the representation-theoretic and algebraic notions of semisimplicity coincide.
