The proof is standard and follows from the fact that $\phi(L)$ generates an associative algebra acting irreducibly on $V$. Let $T$ be an endomorphism of $V$ commuting with $\phi(L)$. Since we work over an algebraically closed field (or a field $\mathbb{F}$ over which $V$ is absolutely irreducible), $T$ has an eigenvalue $\lambda$. Then $T - \lambda I$ also commutes with $\phi(L)$, and its kernel is a nonzero $\phi(L)$-invariant subspace. By irreducibility, $\ker(T - \lambda I) = V$, so $T = \lambda I$.
