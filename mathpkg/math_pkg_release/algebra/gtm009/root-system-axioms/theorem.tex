A subset $\Phi$ of a Euclidean space $E$ (with inner product $(\cdot, \cdot)$) is called a \text{root system} if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(R1)] $\Phi$ is finite, spans $E$, and $0 \notin \Phi$.
\item[(R2)] If $\alpha \in \Phi$, the only scalar multiples of $\alpha$ in $\Phi$ are $\pm \alpha$.
\item[(R3)] If $\alpha \in \Phi$, the reflection $\sigma_\alpha$ defined by $\sigma_\alpha(\beta) = \beta - \frac{2(\beta, \alpha)}{(\alpha, \alpha)}\alpha$ leaves $\Phi$ invariant.
\item[(R4)] If $\alpha, \beta \in \Phi$, then $\frac{2(\beta, \alpha)}{(\alpha, \alpha)} \in \mathbb{Z}$.
\end{enumerate}

The \text{Weyl group} $W$ of $\Phi$ is the (finite) subgroup of $O(E)$ generated by the reflections $\sigma_\alpha$ ($\alpha \in \Phi$).
