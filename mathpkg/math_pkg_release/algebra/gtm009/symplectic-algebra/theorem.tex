Let $V$ be a vector space of dimension $2\ell$ over $F$, equipped with a nondegenerate skew-symmetric bilinear form $f$. The \text{symplectic algebra} $\mathfrak{sp}(V)$, or $\mathfrak{sp}(2\ell, F)$, consists of all endomorphisms $x$ of $V$ satisfying
$$f(x(v), w) = -f(v, x(w))$$
for all $v, w \in V$. In matrix terms, with respect to a suitable basis, the condition for $x = \begin{pmatrix} m & n \\ p & q \end{pmatrix}$ (where $m, n, p, q \in \mathfrak{gl}(\ell, F)$) is that
$$sx = -x^t s,$$
i.e., $n^t = n$, $p^t = p$, and $m^t = -q$, where $s = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & I_\ell \\ -I_\ell & 0 \end{pmatrix}$. The dimension of $\mathfrak{sp}(2\ell, F)$ is $2\ell^2 + \ell$.
