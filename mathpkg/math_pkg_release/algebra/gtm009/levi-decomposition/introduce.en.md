The Levi decomposition states that every finite-dimensional Lie algebra in characteristic zero is the semidirect product of a semisimple subalgebra (the Levi factor) and its radical. This reduces the study of arbitrary Lie algebras to that of semisimple algebras (classified by Dynkin diagrams) and solvable algebras. The conjugacy of Levi factors (Malcev-Harish-Chandra theorem) ensures uniqueness up to inner automorphism.
