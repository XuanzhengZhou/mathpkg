Let $\lambda \in \Lambda$. If there exists $\sigma \neq 1$ in the Weyl group $\mathcal{W}$ such that $\sigma(\lambda) = \lambda$, then
$$
\sum_{\sigma \in \mathcal{W}} \operatorname{sn}(\sigma) \, \varepsilon_{\sigma(\lambda)} = 0.
$$
In particular, the alternating sum of the formal exponentials $\varepsilon_{\sigma(\lambda)}$ over the Weyl group orbit of $\lambda$ vanishes whenever the stabilizer of $\lambda$ in $\mathcal{W}$ is nontrivial.
