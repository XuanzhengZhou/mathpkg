**Proof.** Suppose $\lambda \in \Lambda$ is fixed by some $\sigma \neq 1$ in the Weyl group $\mathcal{W}$. Since $\lambda$ lies in the closure but not the interior of some Weyl chamber, there exists a reflection $s_\alpha \in \mathcal{W}$ (for some root $\alpha$) such that $s_\alpha(\lambda) = \lambda$.

Let $W_\lambda = \{\sigma \in \mathcal{W} : \sigma(\lambda) = \lambda\}$ be the stabilizer of $\lambda$ in $\mathcal{W}$. Since $s_\alpha \in W_\lambda$ is a reflection of order 2, the subgroup generated by $s_\alpha$ has order 2. More generally, $W_\lambda$ is generated by the reflections that fix $\lambda$, and since every reflection has order 2, the group $W_\lambda$ is a finite reflection group. In particular, $W_\lambda$ has even order (it contains at least one reflection, hence a cyclic subgroup of order 2).

Now consider the alternating sum:
$$
S = \sum_{\sigma \in \mathcal{W}} \operatorname{sn}(\sigma) \, \varepsilon_{\sigma(\lambda)}.
$$

Partition $\mathcal{W}$ into cosets of $W_\lambda$: $\mathcal{W} = \bigcup_{i} \sigma_i W_\lambda$. For each coset $\sigma_i W_\lambda$, the elements $\sigma_i \tau$ (with $\tau \in W_\lambda$) all map $\lambda$ to $\sigma_i(\lambda)$. Hence:
$$
S = \sum_{i} \left( \sum_{\tau \in W_\lambda} \operatorname{sn}(\sigma_i \tau) \right) \varepsilon_{\sigma_i(\lambda)}.
$$

Since $\operatorname{sn}$ is a homomorphism, $\operatorname{sn}(\sigma_i \tau) = \operatorname{sn}(\sigma_i) \operatorname{sn}(\tau)$. Thus:
$$
\sum_{\tau \in W_\lambda} \operatorname{sn}(\sigma_i \tau) = \operatorname{sn}(\sigma_i) \sum_{\tau \in W_\lambda} \operatorname{sn}(\tau).
$$

Because $W_\lambda$ has even order and is generated by reflections, the number of elements of determinant $+1$ equals the number of elements of determinant $-1$ in $W_\lambda$. Equivalently, $\sum_{\tau \in W_\lambda} \operatorname{sn}(\tau) = 0$. Therefore each inner sum vanishes, giving $S = 0$. $\square$
