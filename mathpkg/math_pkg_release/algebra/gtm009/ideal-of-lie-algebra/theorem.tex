A subspace $I$ of a Lie algebra $L$ is called an \text{ideal} of $L$ if $x \in L, y \in I$ together imply $[xy] \in I$. Since $[xy] = -[yx]$, this is equivalent to $[yx] \in I$. Ideals play the role in Lie algebra theory which is played by normal subgroups in group theory and by two-sided ideals in ring theory: they arise as kernels of homomorphisms.
