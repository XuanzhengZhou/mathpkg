Engel's theorem is a fundamental structure theorem for nilpotent Lie algebras. It states that a Lie algebra of nilpotent endomorphisms has a common eigenvector with eigenvalue zero. Equivalently, a Lie algebra is nilpotent precisely when every adjoint operator is nilpotent. The theorem is an essential tool throughout the structure theory and holds over any field.
