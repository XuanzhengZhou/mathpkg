The \text{Dynkin diagram} of a root system $\Phi$ with base $\Delta = \{\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_\ell\}$ is a graph with $\ell$ vertices, where vertex $i$ corresponds to $\alpha_i$, and vertices $i, j$ are connected by
$$ \frac{4(\alpha_i, \alpha_j)^2}{(\alpha_i, \alpha_i)(\alpha_j, \alpha_j)} $$
edges. When roots have different lengths, an arrow points from the longer root to the shorter root. The Dynkin diagram uniquely determines the root system up to isomorphism.

The connected Dynkin diagrams (corresponding to irreducible root systems) are:
$$A_\ell (\ell \geq 1), \quad B_\ell (\ell \geq 2), \quad C_\ell (\ell \geq 3), \quad D_\ell (\ell \geq 4),$$
$$E_6, \quad E_7, \quad E_8, \quad F_4, \quad G_2.$$
