This proposition refines the orthogonality properties of the root space decomposition using the nondegeneracy of the Killing form on $H$. It establishes that nonzero root spaces are orthogonal unless the roots are opposites, that the pairing between $L_\alpha$ and $L_{-\alpha}$ is nondegenerate, and that $[L_\alpha L_{-\alpha}]$ is one-dimensional spanned by $t_\alpha$ (the element corresponding to $\alpha$ under the Killing form identification). The elements $h_\alpha = 2t_\alpha/\kappa(t_\alpha, t_\alpha)$ are the coroots, satisfying $\alpha(h_\alpha) = 2$, which are essential for the root system axioms.
