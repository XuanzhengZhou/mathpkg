The \text{radical} of a Lie algebra $L$, denoted $\text{Rad } L$, is the unique maximal solvable ideal of $L$. A Lie algebra $L$ is called \text{semisimple} if $\text{Rad } L = 0$. Equivalently, $L$ has no nonzero abelian ideals. By Cartan's Criterion, $L$ is semisimple if and only if its Killing form is nondegenerate.
