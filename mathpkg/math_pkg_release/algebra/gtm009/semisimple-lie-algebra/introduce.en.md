A semisimple Lie algebra is one whose radical (maximal solvable ideal) is zero. Semisimple algebras are the direct sum of simple Lie algebras and form the core subject of the structure and classification theory. Cartan's criterion provides the fundamental characterization: a Lie algebra is semisimple precisely when its Killing form is nondegenerate.
