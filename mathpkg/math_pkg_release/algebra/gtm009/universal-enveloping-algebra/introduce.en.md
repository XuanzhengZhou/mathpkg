The universal enveloping algebra $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ is the universal associative algebra that encodes the bracket of $L$ as the commutator. Representations of $L$ correspond precisely to $\mathfrak{U}(L)$-modules, making $\mathfrak{U}(L)$ the fundamental object connecting Lie algebra theory to associative algebra theory. The Poincaré-Birkhoff-Witt (PBW) theorem describes a canonical basis of $\mathfrak{U}(L)$.
