Theorem 21.2 is the converse to Theorem 21.1: not only must the highest weight of a finite dimensional irreducible $L$-module be dominant integral, but every dominant integral weight actually occurs as the highest weight of some finite dimensional irreducible module $V(\lambda)$. Moreover, the Weyl group $W$ acts on the weight spaces by permuting them, preserving dimensions. This theorem is the cornerstone of the classification of finite dimensional representations of semisimple Lie algebras.
