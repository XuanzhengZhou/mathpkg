Let $L = \mathfrak{sl}(\ell + 1, F)$, with $\text{CSA } H = \mathfrak{d}(\ell + 1, F) \cap L$. Let $\mu_1, \ldots, \mu_{\ell+1}$ be the coordinate functions on $H$, relative to the standard basis of $\mathfrak{gl}(\ell + 1, F)$. Then $\sum \mu_i = 0$, and $\mu_1, \ldots, \mu_\ell$ form a basis of $H^*$, while the set of $\alpha_i = \mu_i - \mu_{i+1}$ ($1 \leq i \leq \ell$) is a base $\Delta$ for the root system $\Phi$. Verify that $\mathcal{W}$ acts on $H^*$ by permuting the $\mu_i$; in particular, the reflection with respect to $\alpha_i$ interchanges $\mu_i, \mu_{i+1}$ and leaves the other $\mu_j$ fixed. Then show that $V(\lambda)$ for $\lambda = a_1\mu_1 + \cdots + a_\ell\mu_\ell$ can be realized as a submodule of a suitable tensor product of exterior powers of the natural representation.
