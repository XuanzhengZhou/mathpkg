This theorem gives a complete description of the relations that define $V(\lambda)$ as a quotient of the Verma module $Z(\lambda)$. While $Z(\lambda) \cong \mathfrak{U}(L)/I(\lambda)$ has as relations only the annihilation by positive root vectors $x_\alpha$ and the scalar action of the Cartan subalgebra, the finite dimensional irreducible quotient $V(\lambda) \cong \mathfrak{U}(L)/J(\lambda)$ has the additional relations $y_i^{m_i+1} = 0$ for each simple root $\alpha_i$, where $m_i = \langle \lambda, \alpha_i \rangle$.
