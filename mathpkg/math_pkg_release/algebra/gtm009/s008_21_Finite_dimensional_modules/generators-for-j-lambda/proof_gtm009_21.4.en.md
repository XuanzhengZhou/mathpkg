Let $J'(\lambda)$ be the left ideal generated by $I(\lambda)$ along with all $y_i^{m_i+1}$ ($1 \leq i \leq \ell$), and set $V'(\lambda) = \mathfrak{U}(L)/J'(\lambda)$. Since $J'(\lambda) \subset J(\lambda)$, there is a canonical surjection $V'(\lambda) \to V(\lambda)$. It suffices to show that $V'(\lambda)$ is finite dimensional, for then the irreducible module $V(\lambda)$, being a homomorphic image of $V'(\lambda)$, must be isomorphic to it (by comparing dimensions or by the uniqueness of the irreducible quotient).

To show that $V'(\lambda)$ is finite dimensional, it suffices to show that it is a sum of finite dimensional $S_i$-submodules ($1 \leq i \leq \ell$), because then the proof of Theorem 21.2 goes through exactly as before. For this it is enough to show that each $y_i$ is locally nilpotent on $V'(\lambda)$ (this is already obvious for the $x_i$, since we cannot have $\mu + k\alpha_i < \lambda$ for all $k \geq 0$). By hypothesis, the coset of $1$ in $V'(\lambda)$ is killed by $y_i^{m_i+1}$.

We know (Theorem 20.2) that $V'(\lambda)$ is spanned by the cosets of all $y_{i_1} \cdots y_{i_t}$ ($1 \leq i_j \leq \ell$). The following lemma (Lemma 21.4) implies that if the coset of this monomial is killed by $y_i^k$, then the coset of the longer monomial $y_{i_0} y_{i_1} \cdots y_{i_t}$ is killed by $y_i^{k+3}$. Induction on length of monomials, starting at $1$, then proves the local nilpotence of $y_i$.

To apply Lemma 21.4, take $\mathfrak{A} = \mathfrak{U}(L)$, $y = y_i$, and let $z$ be the monomial whose coset we are studying. The lemma gives an expression for $[y_i^k, z]$, which allows control of how $y_i^k$ acts on $z \cdot 1$ in the quotient module.
