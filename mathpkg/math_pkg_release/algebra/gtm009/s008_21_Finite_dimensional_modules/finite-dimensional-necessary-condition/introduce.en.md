If a finite dimensional irreducible $L$-module $V$ exists, it must be isomorphic to $V(\lambda)$ for some highest weight $\lambda$, by Theorems A and B of (20.3). Restricting to each $\mathfrak{sl}(2, F)$-copy $S_i$ associated with a simple root $\alpha_i$ forces $\lambda(h_i)$ to be a nonnegative integer. Thus the highest weight of any finite dimensional irreducible $L$-module is necessarily dominant integral.
