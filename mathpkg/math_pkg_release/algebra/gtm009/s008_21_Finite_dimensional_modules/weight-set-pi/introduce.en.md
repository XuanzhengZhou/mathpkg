The notation $\Pi(V)$ is introduced to denote the set of all weights of an $L$-module $V$. When $V = V(\lambda)$ is the unique irreducible quotient of the Verma module $Z(\lambda)$, one writes $\Pi(\lambda)$ for brevity. This notation is used throughout the study of finite dimensional modules, particularly when analyzing how the Weyl group $W$ permutes the weights of $V(\lambda)$.
