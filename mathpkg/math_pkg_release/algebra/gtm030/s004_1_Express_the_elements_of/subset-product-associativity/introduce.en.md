The product of subsets in a group is associative, which follows directly from the associativity of the group operation. Since $(ab)c = a(bc)$ for all $a \in A$, $b \in B$, $c \in C$, the sets $(AB)C$ and $A(BC)$ consist of exactly the same elements. This property is used to show that coset multiplication is well-defined for factor groups.
