$(AB)C$ is the collection of products $(ab)c$ with $a \in A$, $b \in B$, $c \in C$. Since the group operation is associative, $(ab)c = a(bc)$, and $a(bc) \in A(BC)$. Hence $(AB)C \subseteq A(BC)$. Similarly, any element of $A(BC)$ has the form $a(bc) = (ab)c \in (AB)C$, so $A(BC) \subseteq (AB)C$. Therefore $(AB)C = A(BC)$.
