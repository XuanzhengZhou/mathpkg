This exercise establishes a relationship between cosets of subgroups and their intersection. The first part shows that a coset of $\mathfrak{H}_1 \cap \mathfrak{H}_2$ can be expressed as the intersection of a coset of $\mathfrak{H}_1$ and a coset of $\mathfrak{H}_2$. The second part applies this to prove Poincaré's theorem: the intersection of two subgroups of finite index also has finite index.
