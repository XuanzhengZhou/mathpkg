A right coset of a subgroup $\mathfrak{H}$ in a group $\mathfrak{G}$ is the set obtained by multiplying a fixed element $x \in \mathfrak{G}$ on the left by all elements of $\mathfrak{H}$. The collection of all right cosets $\{x\mathfrak{H} : x \in \mathfrak{G}\}$ forms a partition of $\mathfrak{G}$. Right cosets arise naturally from the right action of $\mathfrak{H}$ on $\mathfrak{G}$ by right multiplication.
