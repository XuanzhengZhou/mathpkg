The transitivity sets (also known as orbits) are the equivalence classes of a set $S$ under the action of a transformation group $\mathfrak{G}$. They partition $S$ into disjoint subsets, each consisting of elements that can be mapped to one another by elements of $\mathfrak{G}$. When $\mathfrak{G}$ is a cyclic permutation group generated by $\alpha \in S_n$, the transitivity sets correspond to the cycles in the disjoint cycle decomposition of $\alpha$.
