This exercise establishes that three-cycles generate the alternating group $A_n$ for $n \geq 3$. Since every three-cycle is an even permutation (it equals $(a\;b)(b\;c)$), they lie in $A_n$. The converse—that every even permutation can be written as a product of three-cycles—is a key structural result about $A_n$.
