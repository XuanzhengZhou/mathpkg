In a finite cyclic group of order $r$, there is a perfect 1-1 correspondence between subgroups and divisors of $r$. Each subgroup $\mathfrak{W} \neq 1$ corresponds uniquely to the smallest positive integer $s$ such that $a^s \in \mathfrak{W}$, and the order of the subgroup is $r/s$. Extending this to include the trivial subgroup, the total number of subgroups is $d(r)$, the number of positive divisors of $r$. This correspondence makes the subgroup lattice of a cyclic group completely transparent.
