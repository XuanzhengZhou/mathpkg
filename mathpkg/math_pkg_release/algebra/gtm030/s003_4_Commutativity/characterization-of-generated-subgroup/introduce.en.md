The subgroup generated by a subset $M$ can be described constructively: it consists of all finite products of elements from $M$ and their inverses. This explicit description complements the "top-down" definition via intersection of subgroups and is essential for working with generated subgroups in practice. The collection of such products is clearly closed under multiplication and inverses, and contains $M$, hence is the desired subgroup.
