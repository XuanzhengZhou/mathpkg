A right regular element in a semigroup with identity is one that has a right inverse — an element that, when multiplied on the right, yields the identity. This concept, together with its left counterpart, leads to the notion of a unit (regular element), which is an element possessing both a left and right inverse. In a group, every element is required to be a unit.
