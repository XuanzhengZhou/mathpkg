The left regular realization of a group is obtained by mapping each element $a$ to the inverse of its left multiplication: $a \mapsto a_l^{-1} = (a^{-1})_l$. Unlike the naive mapping $a \mapsto a_l$, which reverses the order of multiplication, this corrected mapping is a genuine isomorphism. This provides an alternative proof of Cayley's theorem using left instead of right multiplications.
