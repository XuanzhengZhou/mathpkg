Two cycles $\gamma = (i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r)$ and $\gamma' = (j_1 j_2 \cdots j_s)$ are called \text{disjoint} if the sets $\{i_1, i_2, \cdots, i_r\}$ and $\{j_1, j_2, \cdots, j_s\}$ have no elements in common. A cycle of length $r$ is an $r$-cycle; a cycle of length $2$ is called a \text{transposition}.
