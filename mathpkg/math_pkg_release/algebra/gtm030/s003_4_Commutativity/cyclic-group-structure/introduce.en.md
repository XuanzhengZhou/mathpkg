The cyclic group generated by $a$ consists precisely of all integral powers $a^n$ of $a$, where $a^0 = 1$ and negative powers are defined as powers of the inverse. The familiar exponent laws $a^{m+n} = a^m a^n$ and $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$ extend to all integers, making the structure of cyclic groups particularly transparent. This explicit description allows a complete classification of cyclic groups.
