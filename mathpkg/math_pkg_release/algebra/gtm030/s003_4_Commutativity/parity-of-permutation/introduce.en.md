The parity of a permutation is a fundamental invariant: every permutation is either even or odd, depending on whether it can be expressed as a product of an even or odd number of transpositions. This is well-defined — no permutation can be both even and odd. The invariant $N(\alpha)$, defined as the sum of (cycle length minus 1) over the disjoint cycle decomposition, determines the parity: $N(\alpha)$ counts the number of transpositions in the standard factorization, and multiplying by a transposition always changes $N(\alpha)$ by $\pm 1$. The even permutations form the alternating group $A_n$, a normal subgroup of $S_n$ of index 2.
