Right multiplication by an element $a$ in a group is the mapping that sends each element $x$ to $xa$. By the right cancellation law, this mapping is injective, and it is also surjective since $b = (ba^{-1})a$. Thus each right multiplication is a permutation of the group's underlying set. The collection of all right multiplications forms a transformation group isomorphic to the original group — this is Cayley's theorem.
