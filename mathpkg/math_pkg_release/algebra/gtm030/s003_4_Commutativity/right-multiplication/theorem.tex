For each $a \in \mathfrak{G}$, we denote by $a_r$ the mapping $x \to xa$ of the set $\mathfrak{G}$ into itself. We call $a_r$ the \text{right multiplication} determined by $a$. Since the right cancellation law holds, $a_r$ is 1-1. Since any $b$ can be written as $(ba^{-1})a = (ba^{-1})a_r$, $a_r$ is a mapping onto $\mathfrak{G}$. Hence $a_r$ is in the group of 1-1 transformations of the set $\mathfrak{G}$.
