Cayley's theorem states that every group is isomorphic to a group of permutations. The proof constructs the right regular realization: map each element $a$ of the group to the permutation $a_r$ defined by right multiplication $x \mapsto xa$. The collection of these permutations forms a transformation group isomorphic to the original group. This is a foundational result showing that the abstract concept of a group is equivalent to the concrete concept of a transformation group.
