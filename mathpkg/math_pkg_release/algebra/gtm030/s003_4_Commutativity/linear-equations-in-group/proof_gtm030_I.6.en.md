For the equation $ax = b$, multiplying on the left by $a^{-1}$ gives $a^{-1}(ax) = a^{-1}b$, i.e., $(a^{-1}a)x = 1x = x = a^{-1}b$. This shows $x = a^{-1}b$ is a solution. For uniqueness, if $ax = ax'$, then $a^{-1}(ax) = a^{-1}(ax')$, giving $x = x'$. Hence the cancellation law $ax = ax' \Rightarrow x = x'$ holds. Similarly, $ya = b$ has unique solution $y = ba^{-1}$, and $ya = y'a \Rightarrow y = y'$.
