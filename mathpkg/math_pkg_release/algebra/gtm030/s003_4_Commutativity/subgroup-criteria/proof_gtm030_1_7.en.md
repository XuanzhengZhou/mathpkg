**Necessity.** Suppose $\mathfrak{H}$ is a subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$. Then by definition $\mathfrak{H}$ is closed, giving condition (1). $\mathfrak{H}$ has an identity $1'$, and since $(1')^2 = 1'$, Exercise 1 (p. 24) implies $1' = 1$, the identity of $\mathfrak{G}$, giving condition (2). For $a \in \mathfrak{H}$, there exists $a' \in \mathfrak{H}$ with $aa' = 1 = a'a$. By uniqueness of inverses in $\mathfrak{G}$, $a' = a^{-1}$, giving condition (3).

**Sufficiency.** If conditions (1)-(3) hold, then $\mathfrak{H}$ is closed by (1), the associative law is inherited from $\mathfrak{G}$, condition (2) provides an identity, and condition (3) provides inverses. Thus $\mathfrak{H}$ is a group under the induced operation, hence a subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$.
