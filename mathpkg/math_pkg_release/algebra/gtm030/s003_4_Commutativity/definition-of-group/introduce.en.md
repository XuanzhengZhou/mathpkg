A group is one of the most fundamental structures in abstract algebra. It is a semigroup (a set with an associative binary operation) that additionally possesses an identity element and in which every element has an inverse. These three axioms — associativity, existence of identity, and existence of inverses — define the class of groups. The theory of groups pervades all of mathematics, from number theory to geometry to physics.
