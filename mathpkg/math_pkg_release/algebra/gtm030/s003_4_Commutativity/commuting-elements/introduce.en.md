In a semigroup, two elements $a$ and $b$ are said to commute if their product is independent of order, i.e., $ab = ba$. This property is a fundamental condition that generalizes the commutativity of ordinary multiplication of numbers. The concept of commuting elements leads to the important class of commutative semigroups, where all pairs of elements commute.
