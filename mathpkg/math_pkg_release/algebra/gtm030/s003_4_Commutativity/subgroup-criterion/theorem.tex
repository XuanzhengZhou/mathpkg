Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be a group. The three conditions:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $a, b \in \mathfrak{H}$ implies $ab \in \mathfrak{H}$,
\item $1 \in \mathfrak{H}$,
\item $a \in \mathfrak{H}$ implies $a^{-1} \in \mathfrak{H}$,
\end{enumerate}
are both necessary and sufficient for a subset $\mathfrak{H} \subseteq \mathfrak{G}$ to be a subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$.
