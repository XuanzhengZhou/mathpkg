In an integral domain, the characteristic determines the additive order of every non-zero element. If the characteristic is $0$, then every non-zero element has infinite additive order — adding the element to itself any non-zero number of times never yields zero. If the characteristic is a positive integer $m$, then $m$ must be a prime number, and every non-zero element has additive order exactly $m$. This theorem links the purely ring-theoretic notion of characteristic to the structural property of being an integral domain.
