A principal left ideal is the smallest left ideal that contains a given element $b$ of a ring. When the ring has a multiplicative identity, the principal left ideal $(b)_l$ is simply the set of all left multiples $xb$. Without an identity, integer multiples must also be included, giving $(b)_l = \{ nb + xb \mid n \in \mathbb{Z},\; x \in A \}$. Principal ideals are the simplest type of ideals and are foundational for the study of ideal structure in rings.
