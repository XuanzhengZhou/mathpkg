For any ring $\mathfrak{A}$, the right multiplication map $a_r$ is always an endomorphism of the additive group $(\mathfrak{A}, +)$. This follows directly from the right distributive law: multiplication on the right distributes over addition. This observation is the key that links a ring's multiplicative structure to the endomorphism ring of its additive group.
