A polynomial $f(x) \in \mathfrak{F}[x]$ over a field $\mathfrak{F}$ is called irreducible (or prime in $\mathfrak{F}[x]$) if $\deg f(x) > 0$ and $f(x)$ cannot be expressed as a product $f(x) = g(x)h(x)$ where both $g(x)$ and $h(x)$ are polynomials of positive degree (i.e., $\deg g(x) > 0$ and $\deg h(x) > 0$).
