The characteristic of a field is determined by the subring generated by the multiplicative identity. This subring is either isomorphic to the integers $\mathbb{Z}$ (characteristic $0$) or to $\mathbb{Z}/(p)$ for a prime $p$ (characteristic $p$). The latter case is forced because a field cannot contain non-trivial zero-divisors. In characteristic $p$, the subring generated by $1$ is already the entire prime field.
