If $x$ and $y$ are both transcendental over the coefficient ring $\mathfrak{A}$, then the polynomial rings $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ and $\mathfrak{A}[y]$ are isomorphic. In general, for an arbitrary element $u$, $\mathfrak{A}[u]$ is a homomorphic image of $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ with $x$ transcendental, and $\mathfrak{A}[u] \cong \mathfrak{A}[x]/\mathfrak{R}$ where the kernel ideal $\mathfrak{R}$ satisfies $\mathfrak{A} \cap \mathfrak{R} = 0$. When $u$ is transcendental, $\mathfrak{R} = 0$ and the homomorphism becomes an isomorphism.
