Given a polynomial ring $\mathfrak{A}_1[x]$ with $x$ transcendental over $\mathfrak{A}_1$ and another polynomial ring $\mathfrak{A}_2[u]$, if there is a homomorphism $\sigma$ from $\mathfrak{A}_1$ onto $\mathfrak{A}_2$, then $\sigma$ extends uniquely to a homomorphism $\Sigma: \mathfrak{A}_1[x] \to \mathfrak{A}_2[u]$ sending $x$ to $u$. The extension is defined by applying $\sigma$ to each coefficient: $f(x) = \sum a_i x^i$ maps to $f^\sigma(u) = \sum a_i^\sigma u^i$. This is a fundamental structural result connecting homomorphisms of coefficient rings to homomorphisms of their polynomial extensions.
