When the leading coefficient of a non-zero divisor polynomial is a unit in the coefficient ring, a division algorithm holds in the polynomial ring. Given any dividend $f(x)$, there exist unique right-hand quotient and remainder (and similarly left-hand quotient and remainder) such that the remainder has degree strictly less than the divisor. In the commutative case, right and left division coincide.
