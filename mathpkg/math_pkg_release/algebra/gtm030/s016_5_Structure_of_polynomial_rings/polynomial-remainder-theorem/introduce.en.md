When dividing a polynomial by the linear factor $(x-c)$, the constant remainder is given by evaluating the polynomial at $c$. In non-commutative coefficient rings, right and left evaluations must be distinguished, yielding $f(x) - f_R(c) = q_1(x)(x-c)$. In the commutative case, this reduces to the classical Remainder Theorem: $f(c) = 0$ if and only if $(x-c)$ divides $f(x)$.
