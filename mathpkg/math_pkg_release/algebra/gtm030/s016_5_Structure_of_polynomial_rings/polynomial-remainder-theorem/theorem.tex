For any $f(x) \in \mathfrak{A}[x]$ and $c \in \mathfrak{A}$,
$$f(x) - f_R(c) = q_1(x)(x - c)$$
where $f_R(c) = \sum a_k c^k$ (right evaluation of $f(x) = \sum a_k x^k$) and
$$q_1(x) = \sum_{k} a_k (x^{k-1} + cx^{k-2} + \cdots + c^{k-1}).$$
If $\mathfrak{A}$ is commutative, then $f(x) = q(x)(x-c) + f(c)$.
