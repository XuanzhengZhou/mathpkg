Kronecker's construction generalizes Cauchy's definition of the complex numbers $\mathbb{C} = \mathbb{R}[x]/(x^2+1)$ to any ground field and any irreducible polynomial. Given an irreducible polynomial $f(x)$ over $\mathfrak{F}$, the quotient ring $\mathfrak{F}[x]/(f(x))$ is a field containing an isomorphic copy of $\mathfrak{F}$ and a root of $f(x)$. This is the fundamental construction for adjoining algebraic elements to a field.
