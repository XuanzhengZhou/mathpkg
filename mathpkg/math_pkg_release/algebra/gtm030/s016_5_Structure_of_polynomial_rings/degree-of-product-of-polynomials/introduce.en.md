In a polynomial ring $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ over an integral domain $\mathfrak{A}$, the degree function is additive with respect to multiplication: the degree of a product equals the sum of the degrees of the factors. The convention $\deg 0 = -\infty$ ensures the formula holds universally. This relation is fundamental to the algebraic structure of polynomial rings.
