For a field $\mathfrak{F}$, construct the polynomial ring $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ with $x$ transcendental. Then $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ is an integral domain but not a field. The field of fractions of $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ is denoted $\mathfrak{F}(x)$. Its elements are rational expressions (functions) in $x$ over $\mathfrak{F}$ of the form $f(x)/g(x)$ where $f(x), g(x) \in \mathfrak{F}[x]$ and $g(x) \neq 0$, obeying the usual rules of reckoning. The extension $\mathfrak{F}(x)$ of $\mathfrak{F}$ is called a simple transcendental extension.
