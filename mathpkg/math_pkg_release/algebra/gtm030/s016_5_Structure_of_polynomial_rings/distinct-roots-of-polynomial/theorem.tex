Let $f(x)$ be a non-zero polynomial with coefficients in a field $\mathfrak{F}$ and let $c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_m$ be distinct roots of $f(x) = 0$ in $\mathfrak{F}$. Then
$$f(x) = (x - c_1)(x - c_2)\cdots(x - c_m)g(x)$$
for some polynomial $g(x) \in \mathfrak{F}[x]$. Consequently, a non-zero polynomial of degree $n$ has at most $n$ distinct roots in any field.
