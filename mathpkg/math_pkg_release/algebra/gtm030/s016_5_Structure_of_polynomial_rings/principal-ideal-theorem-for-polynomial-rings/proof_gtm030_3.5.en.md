Let $\mathfrak{B}$ be an ideal in $\mathfrak{F}[x]$. If $\mathfrak{B} = 0$, then $\mathfrak{B} = (0)$, the principal ideal generated by $0$. Assume therefore that $\mathfrak{B} \neq 0$. Let $g(x)$ be a non-zero polynomial of least degree in $\mathfrak{B}$. If $f(x)$ is any element of $\mathfrak{B}$, write $f(x) = g(x)q(x) + r(x)$ where $\deg r(x) < \deg g(x)$, using the division algorithm (valid since $\mathfrak{F}$ is a field, so the leading coefficient of $g(x)$ is a unit). Then $r(x) = f(x) - g(x)q(x) \in \mathfrak{B}$, and since its degree is less than that of $g(x)$, minimality forces $r(x) = 0$. Hence $f(x) = g(x)q(x)$ belongs to the principal ideal $(g(x))$. Thus $\mathfrak{B} \subseteq (g(x))$. But $g(x) \in \mathfrak{B}$ gives $(g(x)) \subseteq \mathfrak{B}$. Hence $\mathfrak{B} = (g(x))$.
