For fields, the isomorphism theorem for polynomial rings (Theorem 5) can be sharpened using the fact that every ideal in $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ is principal. Any polynomial ring $\mathfrak{F}[u]$ is thus a quotient of the transcendental polynomial ring $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ by a principal ideal. This structural result is the algebraic foundation for building field extensions via Kronecker's method.
