By Theorem 5, $\mathfrak{F}[u] \cong \mathfrak{F}[x]/\mathfrak{R}$ where $\mathfrak{R}$ is an ideal in $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ with $\mathfrak{F} \cap \mathfrak{R} = 0$ and $x$ transcendental over $\mathfrak{F}$. By Theorem 6, every ideal in $\mathfrak{F}[x]$ is principal, so $\mathfrak{R} = (g(x))$ for some $g(x) \in \mathfrak{F}[x]$. Either $g(x) = 0$ (which yields $\mathfrak{F}[u] \cong \mathfrak{F}[x]$), or $g(x)$ has positive degree (otherwise $\mathfrak{R}$ would contain a non-zero constant, contradicting $\mathfrak{F} \cap \mathfrak{R} = 0$).
