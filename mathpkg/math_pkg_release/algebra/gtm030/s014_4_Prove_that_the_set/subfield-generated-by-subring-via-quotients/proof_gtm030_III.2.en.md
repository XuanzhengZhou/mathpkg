Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a field and $\mathfrak{A} \neq 0$ a subring. Define
$$S = \{ab^{-1} \mid a, b \in \mathfrak{A}, b \neq 0\}.$$

We verify that $S$ is a subfield. For addition:
$$ab^{-1} + cd^{-1} = ad(bd)^{-1} + cb(bd)^{-1} = (ad + cb)(bd)^{-1} \in S.$$

The zero is $0 = 0b^{-1} \in S$. The negative is $-ab^{-1} = (-a)b^{-1} \in S$. For multiplication:
$$(ab^{-1})(cd^{-1}) = ac(bd)^{-1} = (ac)(bd)^{-1} \in S.$$

The identity is $1 = aa^{-1}$ for any $a \neq 0$ in $\mathfrak{A}$. For any non-zero $ab^{-1}$ (so $a \neq 0$), its inverse is $(ab^{-1})^{-1} = a^{-1}b \in S$.

Now, any $a \in \mathfrak{A}$ can be written as $a = (ab)b^{-1}$ for any $b \neq 0$ in $\mathfrak{A}$, so $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq S$. Hence $S$ is a subfield of $\mathfrak{F}$ containing $\mathfrak{A}$.

Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be the subfield generated by $\mathfrak{A}$ (the intersection of all subfields containing $\mathfrak{A}$). Since $\mathfrak{G} \supseteq \mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{G}$ is a subfield, $\mathfrak{G}$ must contain all quotients $ab^{-1}$ with $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}, b \neq 0$. Thus $\mathfrak{G} \supseteq S$. But $S$ is already a subfield containing $\mathfrak{A}$, so $S \supseteq \mathfrak{G}$ by minimality of $\mathfrak{G}$. Therefore $\mathfrak{G} = S = \{ab^{-1}\}$.
