If $\mathfrak{A}$ is a non-zero subring of a field $\mathfrak{F}$, the subfield $\mathfrak{G}$ generated by $\mathfrak{A}$ consists precisely of all quotients $ab^{-1}$ where $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}$ and $b \neq 0$. This is verified by showing that the set $\{ab^{-1}\}$ is a subfield of $\mathfrak{F}$ containing $\mathfrak{A}$, and any subfield containing $\mathfrak{A}$ must contain all such quotients. This characterization motivates the construction of the field of fractions of an abstract integral domain.
