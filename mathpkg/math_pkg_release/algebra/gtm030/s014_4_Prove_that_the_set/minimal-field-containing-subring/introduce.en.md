A field $\mathfrak{F}$ is called a minimal field containing a subring $\mathfrak{A}$ if $\mathfrak{F}$ equals the subfield generated by $\mathfrak{A}$ (the intersection of all subfields containing $\mathfrak{A}$). In a minimal field extension, every element can be written as a quotient $ab^{-1}$ with numerator and denominator in $\mathfrak{A}$. This concept underlies the construction of the field of fractions as the universal minimal field extension of an integral domain.
