Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a commutative integral domain $\neq 0$. Define the set $\mathfrak{B}$ of all ordered pairs $(a, b)$ with $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}$ and $b \neq 0$. Introduce the equivalence relation
$$(a, b) \sim (c, d) \iff ad = bc.$$
Denote the equivalence class of $(a, b)$ by the fraction $a/b$. The set $\mathfrak{J}$ of all such fractions is the field of fractions of $\mathfrak{A}$, with addition and multiplication defined by
$$\frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad + bc}{bd}, \qquad \frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ac}{bd}.$$
The zero element is $0/b$, the negative of $a/b$ is $(-a)/b = a/(-b)$, and the identity is $b/b = 1$ for any $b \neq 0$. If $a/b \neq 0$ (i.e., $a \neq 0$), then its inverse is $b/a$. The mapping $a \mapsto \overline{a} = ab/b$ gives an embedding of $\mathfrak{A}$ into the subring $\overline{\mathfrak{A}}$ of $\mathfrak{J}$.
