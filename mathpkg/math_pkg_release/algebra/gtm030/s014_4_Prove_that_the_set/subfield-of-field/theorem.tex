Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be a field and let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a subring $\neq 0$ of $\mathfrak{F}$. We say that $\mathfrak{A}$ is a \text{subfield} of $\mathfrak{F}$ if the set
$$\{ab^{-1} \mid a, b \in \mathfrak{A}, b \neq 0\}$$
is contained in $\mathfrak{A}$, i.e., for every $a \in \mathfrak{A}$ and every non-zero $b \in \mathfrak{A}$, the quotient $ab^{-1}$ belongs to $\mathfrak{A}$.
