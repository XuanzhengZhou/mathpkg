A subfield of a field $\mathfrak{F}$ is a subring $\mathfrak{A} \neq 0$ that is closed under taking quotients: for any $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}$ with $b \neq 0$, the element $ab^{-1}$ lies in $\mathfrak{A}$. This concept generalizes the relationship between the integers $\mathbb{Z}$ and the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$, where $\mathbb{Q}$ is the smallest subfield containing $\mathbb{Z}$. The definition is introduced in the context of constructing the field of fractions of an integral domain.
