For any group homomorphism, the set of elements that map to the identity of the target group forms a subgroup of the domain. This subgroup is not merely a subgroup — it is invariant (normal), meaning it is closed under conjugation by arbitrary elements of the domain group. This theorem establishes the fundamental relationship between homomorphisms and normal subgroups, which is central to the isomorphism theorems of group theory.
