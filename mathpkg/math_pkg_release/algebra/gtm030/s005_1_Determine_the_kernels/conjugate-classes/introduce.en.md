The conjugate classes (or conjugacy classes) of a group are the orbits of the group acting on itself by conjugation. Each class consists of all elements that are conjugate to one another. The partition of the group into conjugate classes is a fundamental structural decomposition, and the class equation relates the order of the group to the sizes of its conjugacy classes and the center.
