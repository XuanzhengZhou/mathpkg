Let $\eta$ be a homomorphism of $\mathfrak{G}$ into a second group $\mathfrak{G}'$ and let $\mathfrak{H}$ be an invariant subgroup of $\mathfrak{G}$ contained in the kernel $\mathfrak{R} = \eta^{-1}(1')$. Then the rule $a\mathfrak{H} \rightarrow a\eta$ defines a single-valued mapping $\bar{\eta}$ of $\bar{\mathfrak{G}} = \mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ into $\mathfrak{G}'$, and $\bar{\eta}$ is a homomorphism. Moreover, the given homomorphism permits the factorization $\eta = \nu\bar{\eta}$ where $\nu$ is the natural homomorphism $\nu: \mathfrak{G} \to \mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$.
