This theorem establishes that any group homomorphism factors through the quotient group by any invariant subgroup contained in its kernel. The induced map on the quotient is well-defined because elements in the same coset of the invariant subgroup have the same image under the original homomorphism. This factorization is a fundamental step toward the first isomorphism theorem.
