The direct sum is the additive analogue of the direct product, used when the component groups are commutative and written additively. A key example is the group $\mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z} \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathbb{Z}$ (the additive group of integer lattice points) with $M = \varnothing$. The three-dimensional real vector group is precisely $\mathbb{R} \oplus \mathbb{R} \oplus \mathbb{R}$ where $\mathbb{R}$ is the additive group of real numbers.
