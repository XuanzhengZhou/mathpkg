Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be an $M$-group satisfying both the ascending and descending chain conditions for invariant $M$-subgroups, and let

$$\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \times \mathfrak{G}_2 \times \cdots \times \mathfrak{G}_s$$

and

$$\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{H}_1 \times \mathfrak{H}_2 \times \cdots \times \mathfrak{H}_t$$

be two direct decompositions of $\mathfrak{G}$ into indecomposable groups $\neq 1$. Then $s = t$ and for a suitable ordering of the $\mathfrak{H}_i$ we have $\mathfrak{G}_i \cong \mathfrak{H}_i$ and

$$\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{H}_1 \times \cdots \times \mathfrak{H}_k \times \mathfrak{G}_{k+1} \times \cdots \times \mathfrak{G}_s, \qquad k = 1, 2, \cdots, s.$$
