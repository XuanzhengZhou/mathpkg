By Fitting's lemma (Theorem 11), for sufficiently large $m$, $\mathfrak{G} = \beta_m \times \mathfrak{G}\eta^m$ where $\eta$ is nilpotent on $\beta_m$ and an automorphism on $\mathfrak{G}\eta^m$. Since $\mathfrak{G}$ is indecomposable, either $\beta_m = 1$ or $\mathfrak{G}\eta^m = 1$. In the first case, $\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}\eta^m$ and $\eta$ is onto, so by Theorem 10, $\eta$ is an automorphism. In the second case, $\mathfrak{G} = \beta_m$ and $\eta$ is nilpotent.
