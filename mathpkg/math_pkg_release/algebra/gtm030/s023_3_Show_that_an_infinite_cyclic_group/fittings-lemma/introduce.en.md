Fitting's Lemma is the key technical result underlying the Krull-Schmidt uniqueness theorem for direct decompositions. It states that a normal $M$-endomorphism of a group satisfying both chain conditions decomposes the group into a direct product of its "nilpotent part" (the kernel of a high power of the endomorphism) and its "automorphism part" (the image of that power). The proof uses the ascending and descending chain conditions to show that the sequences of kernels and images stabilize after finitely many steps.
