Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be an $M$-group and let $\mathfrak{G}_1, \cdots, \mathfrak{G}_n$ be invariant $M$-subgroups of $\mathfrak{G}$. Then $\mathfrak{G}$ is $M$-isomorphic to the direct product $\mathfrak{G}_1 \times \cdots \times \mathfrak{G}_n$ if and only if:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathfrak{G} = \mathfrak{G}_1 \mathfrak{G}_2 \cdots \mathfrak{G}_n$ (every element is a product $a_1 a_2 \cdots a_n$, $a_i \in \mathfrak{G}_i$),
\item $\mathfrak{G}_i \cap \mathfrak{G}_1 \cdots \mathfrak{G}_{i-1} \mathfrak{G}_{i+1} \cdots \mathfrak{G}_n = 1$ for $i = 1, \cdots, n$.
\end{enumerate}
