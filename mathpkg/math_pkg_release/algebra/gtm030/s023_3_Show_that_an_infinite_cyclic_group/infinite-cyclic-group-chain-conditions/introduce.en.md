An infinite cyclic group satisfies the ascending chain condition (ACC) on subgroups because every proper subgroup is finite, so any ascending chain of subgroups eventually stabilizes. However, it fails the descending chain condition (DCC) because one can form an infinite strictly descending chain of subgroups by taking $2^k\mathbb{Z}$ for $k = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$, each properly containing the next.
