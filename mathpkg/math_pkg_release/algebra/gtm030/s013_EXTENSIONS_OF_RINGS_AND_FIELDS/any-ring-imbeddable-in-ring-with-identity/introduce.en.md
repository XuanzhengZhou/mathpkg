This theorem establishes that every ring, regardless of whether it possesses an identity element, can be extended to a larger ring that does contain an identity. The proof constructs the extension explicitly as the product set $\mathfrak{B} = I \times \mathfrak{A}$, where $I$ is the ring of integers, with addition and multiplication defined componentwise. As a corollary, since the larger ring with identity is isomorphic to a ring of endomorphisms, it follows that any ring — with or without identity — is isomorphic to a ring of endomorphisms. The construction also shows that the original ring sits as an ideal inside the extended ring.
