The notion of imbedding formalizes the idea that one ring can sit inside another as a substructure. Specifically, a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is imbedded in $\mathfrak{B}$ when $\mathfrak{B}$ has a subring $\mathfrak{A}'$ that is isomorphic to $\mathfrak{A}$. The larger ring $\mathfrak{B}$ is then called an extension of $\mathfrak{A}$. This concept is fundamental to the study of ring extensions, allowing us to construct larger rings that possess properties the original ring lacks — such as the existence of an identity element.
