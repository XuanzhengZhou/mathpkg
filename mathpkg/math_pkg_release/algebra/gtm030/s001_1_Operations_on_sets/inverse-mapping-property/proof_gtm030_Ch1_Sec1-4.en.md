For any $s \in S$, let $t = s\alpha$. Then $t\alpha^{-1} = s$, so $s(\alpha\alpha^{-1}) = (s\alpha)\alpha^{-1} = t\alpha^{-1} = s$. Hence $\alpha\alpha^{-1} = 1_S$. For any $t \in T$, there exists unique $s \in S$ with $s\alpha = t$. Then $t(\alpha^{-1}\alpha) = (t\alpha^{-1})\alpha = s\alpha = t$. Hence $\alpha^{-1}\alpha = 1_T$.
