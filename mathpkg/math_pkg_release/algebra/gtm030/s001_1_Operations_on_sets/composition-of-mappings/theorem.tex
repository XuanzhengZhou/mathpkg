Let $\alpha$ be a mapping of $S$ into $T$ and let $\beta$ be a mapping of $T$ into a third set $U$. The mapping that sends the element $s$ of $S$ into the element $(s\alpha)\beta$ of $U$ is called the resultant or product of $\alpha$ and $\beta$, denoted $\alpha\beta$. By definition, $s(\alpha\beta) = (s\alpha)\beta$.
