If $S$ and $T$ are arbitrary sets, the product set $S \times T$ is the collection of ordered pairs $(s,t)$, $s \in S$, $t \in T$. The elements $(s,t)$ and $(s',t')$ are regarded as equal if and only if $s = s'$ and $t = t'$. More generally, if $S_1, S_2, \ldots, S_r$ are any sets, then $\prod S_i$ or $S_1 \times S_2 \times \cdots \times S_r$ is the collection of $r$-tuples $(s_1, s_2, \ldots, s_r)$.
