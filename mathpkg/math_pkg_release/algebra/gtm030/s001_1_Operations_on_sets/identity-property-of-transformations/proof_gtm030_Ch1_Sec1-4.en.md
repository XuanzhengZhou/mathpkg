For any $s \in S$, we have $s(\alpha 1) = (s\alpha)1 = s\alpha$ by definition of the identity mapping. Hence $\alpha 1 = \alpha$. Similarly, $s(1\alpha) = (s 1)\alpha = s\alpha$, so $1\alpha = \alpha$.
