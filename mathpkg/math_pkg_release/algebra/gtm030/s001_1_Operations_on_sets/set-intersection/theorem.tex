If $A$ and $B$ are any two subsets of $S$, the intersection $A \cap B$ of $A$ and $B$ is the collection of elements $c$ such that $c \in A$ and $c \in B$. More generally, if $\{A\}$ denotes any collection of subsets of $S$, then the intersection $\bigcap A$ is defined as the set of elements $c$ such that $c \in A$ for every $A$ in $\{A\}$.
