Theorem 2 provides a fundamental characterization of Noetherian modules: a module satisfies the ascending chain condition if and only if every submodule is finitely generated. The forward direction constructs a generating set by iteratively adding elements, using ACC to guarantee termination. The converse direction takes the union of an ascending chain, which is finitely generated by hypothesis, and shows the chain stabilizes at the step containing all generators.
