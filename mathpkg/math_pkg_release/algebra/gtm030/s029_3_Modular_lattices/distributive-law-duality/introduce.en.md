The distributive law for lattices and its dual condition are logically equivalent. If the distributive law $\cap$ over $\cup$ holds, then one can derive the dual law $\cup$ over $\cap$ through a sequence of algebraic manipulations using lattice identities, and vice versa. Consequently, the principle of duality holds without restriction for distributive lattices.
