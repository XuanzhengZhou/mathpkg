The distributive law for lattices is the natural analogue of the distributive law in rings, where the join $\cup$ corresponds to addition and the meet $\cap$ corresponds to multiplication. Important examples of distributive lattices include the lattice of all subsets of a set under set-theoretic union and intersection, and the lattice of positive integers ordered by divisibility, where join is the greatest common divisor and meet is the least common multiple.
