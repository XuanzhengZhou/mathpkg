Theorem 5 is the lattice-theoretic analogue of Schreier's refinement theorem for groups. It asserts that any two finite descending chains between the same endpoints in a modular lattice possess equivalent refinements, where equivalence means a one-to-one correspondence between the successive quotient intervals such that corresponding intervals are projective. The proof uses the Zassenhaus lemma to construct explicit refinements via the elements $a_{ik} = (a_i \cap b_k) \cup a_{i+1}$.
