An integer $b$ is called a \text{factor} or \text{divisor} of the integer $a$ if there exists $c \in I$ such that $a = bc$. Then $a$ is called a \text{multiple} of $b$, and we denote this relation by $b \mid a$. This relation is transitive. If $b \mid a$ and $a \mid b$ with $a \neq 0$, then the cancellation law implies $a = \pm b$.
