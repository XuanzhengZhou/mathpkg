A prime integer $p$ is an integer whose only divisors are the trivial ones: $\pm 1$ and $\pm p$. Primes are the multiplicative building blocks of the integers: the fundamental theorem of arithmetic states that every positive integer can be expressed uniquely (up to order) as a product of positive primes. This definition excludes $\pm 1$ from being prime, which is essential for uniqueness of prime factorization.
