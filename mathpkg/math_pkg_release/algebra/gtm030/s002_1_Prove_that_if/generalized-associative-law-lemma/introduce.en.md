This lemma is the crucial step in proving the generalized associative law for any associative binary composition. It shows that the canonical left-associated product of $n$ elements, when multiplied by the canonical product of the next $m$ elements, equals the canonical product of all $n+m$ elements taken together. By induction on the number of factors, this implies that every possible way of inserting parentheses to form a product of $n$ elements (taken in a fixed order) yields the same result. Consequently, parentheses can be omitted entirely when writing products in associative systems.
