A mapping $$a \mapsto \bar{a}$$ of a ring $$\mathfrak{A}$$ onto a ring $$\bar{\mathfrak{A}}$$ that is 1-1 and satisfies

$$\overline{a + b} = \bar{a} + \bar{b}, \qquad \overline{ab} = \bar{b} \cdot \bar{a}$$

is called an anti-isomorphism of $$\mathfrak{A}$$ onto $$\bar{\mathfrak{A}}$$. If $$\mathfrak{A}$$ is commutative, an anti-isomorphism is the same as an isomorphism.
