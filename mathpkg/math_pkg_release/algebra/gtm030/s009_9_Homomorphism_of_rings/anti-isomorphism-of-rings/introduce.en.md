An anti-isomorphism is a bijective map between rings that preserves addition but reverses the order of multiplication. It is the natural notion of "mirror symmetry" for non-commutative rings. For commutative rings, every anti-isomorphism is simply an isomorphism (since multiplication order does not matter). Anti-isomorphisms arise naturally in contexts such as the transpose operation on matrix rings and the construction of opposite rings.
