Let $$\eta$$ be a homomorphism of a ring $$\mathfrak{A}$$ into a ring $$\mathfrak{A}'$$ with kernel $$\mathfrak{K}$$, and let $$\mathfrak{B}$$ be an ideal of $$\mathfrak{A}$$ contained in $$\mathfrak{K}$$. Then the correspondence

$$a + \mathfrak{B} \mapsto a\eta$$

defines a ring homomorphism $$\bar{\eta}: \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B} \to \mathfrak{A}'$$ such that $$\eta = \nu \bar{\eta}$$, where $$\nu: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B}$$ is the natural homomorphism. Moreover, $$\bar{\eta}$$ is injective if and only if $$\mathfrak{B} = \mathfrak{K}$$.

In particular, taking $$\mathfrak{B} = \mathfrak{K}$$, we obtain the factorization

$$\eta = \nu \bar{\eta},$$

where $$\nu: \mathfrak{A} \to \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{K}$$ is the natural homomorphism onto the quotient ring and $$\bar{\eta}: \mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{K} \to \mathfrak{A}'$$ is an induced isomorphism of $$\mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{K}$$ into $$\mathfrak{A}'$$.
