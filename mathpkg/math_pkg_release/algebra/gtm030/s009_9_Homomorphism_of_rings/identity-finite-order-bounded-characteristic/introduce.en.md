In a ring with identity, the additive order of the identity element determines bounds on the orders of all elements. If the identity $1$ has finite order $m > 0$ in the additive group, then $ma = 0$ for every element $a$, so all additive orders divide $m$. Consequently, the characteristic of the ring is at most the order of the identity.
