This corollary to Theorem 4 describes how ring-theoretic special elements behave under a homomorphism. The identity of the source ring maps to the identity of the image subring. Units map to units (with inverses preserved). A division ring either collapses entirely under a homomorphism or maps onto another division ring — there is no intermediate possibility. The behavior when the identity maps to zero is particularly important: it forces the entire image to be the zero ring.
