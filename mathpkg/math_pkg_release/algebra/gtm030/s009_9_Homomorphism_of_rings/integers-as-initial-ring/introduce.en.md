The ring of integers $\mathbb{Z}$ serves as the initial object in the category of rings with identity: for any such ring $\mathfrak{A}$, the map $n \mapsto n \cdot 1_{\mathfrak{A}}$ is the unique ring homomorphism from $\mathbb{Z}$ to $\mathfrak{A}$. The kernel of this map is $(m)$ for a unique $m \geq 0$, and the image is the prime subring of $\mathfrak{A}$, isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z}$. The integer $m$ is precisely the characteristic of $\mathfrak{A}$.
