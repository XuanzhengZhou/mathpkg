A subset $A$ of a lattice $L$ is called an ideal if (1) $a, b \in A$ implies $a \cap b \in A$, and (2) $a \in A$ and $x \in L$ imply $a \cup x \in A$. $A$ is a principal ideal $(a)$ if $A$ consists of all $x \in L$ such that $x \geq a$ for a fixed $a \in L$.

Prove that $L$ satisfies the descending chain condition if and only if every ideal of $L$ is principal.

Dualize the definition of ideal and the result stated above. (The dual of an ideal is called a dual ideal.)
