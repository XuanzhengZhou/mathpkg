Theorem 9 establishes a fundamental intersection formula for independent elements in a modular lattice. If $a_1, \dots, a_n$ are independent, then intersecting two joins that share a common prefix of elements yields precisely that common prefix. This generalizes the fact that in a vector space, the intersection of two subspaces spanned by independent vectors that share some basis vectors is exactly the span of the shared vectors. The result relies on the modular law and the definition of independence.
