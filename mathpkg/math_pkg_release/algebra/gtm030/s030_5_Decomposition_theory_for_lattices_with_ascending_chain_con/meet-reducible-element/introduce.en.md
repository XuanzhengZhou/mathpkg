In a modular lattice satisfying the ascending chain condition, an element is called meet-reducible (or intersection-reducible) if it can be expressed as the meet (intersection) of two strictly larger elements. An element that is not meet-reducible is called meet-irreducible. This notion generalizes the concept of reducible ideals in Noetherian rings to the lattice-theoretic setting and serves as the foundation for decomposition theory in modular lattices.
