A meet-decomposition $a = q_1 \cap q_2 \cap \cdots \cap q_m$ of an element into meet-irreducibles is irredundant if no term $q_i$ can be omitted without strictly enlarging the intersection. In other words, each irreducible term contributes essentially to the representation. Irredundant representations are the lattice-theoretic analogue of minimal primary decompositions in Noetherian rings, and they play a central role in the uniqueness theory for modular lattices with ACC.
