The uniqueness of the field of fractions theorem states that the field of fractions of a commutative integral domain is unique up to isomorphism in a very strong sense. Given two fields each generated as a field by a subring isomorphic to a given integral domain, any isomorphism between those subrings extends uniquely to an isomorphism between the fields. This theorem justifies the standard construction of the field of fractions by showing that any construction yielding the "smallest" field containing the integral domain produces the same object up to canonical isomorphism.
