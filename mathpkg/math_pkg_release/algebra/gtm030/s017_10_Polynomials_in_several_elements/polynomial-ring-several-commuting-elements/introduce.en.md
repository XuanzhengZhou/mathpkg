Given a ring $B$ with identity and a subring $A$ containing $1$, if elements $u_1, \ldots, u_r \in B$ commute pairwise and with every element of $A$, the subring $A[u_1, \ldots, u_r]$ is defined as the smallest subring of $B$ that contains $A$ together with all the $u_i$. This construction generalizes the notion of adjoining a single element to a ring, and the elements $u_i$ need not be algebraically independent over $A$. When the $u_i$ are algebraically independent, $A[u_1, \ldots, u_r]$ is the familiar polynomial ring in $r$ indeterminates over $A$.
