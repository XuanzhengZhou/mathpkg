This proposition generalizes the one-variable fact about units of polynomial rings: if $\mathfrak{A}$ is an integral domain, the only invertible elements in the polynomial ring $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, \ldots, x_r]$ (with algebraically independent indeterminates) are the constants that are already units in $\mathfrak{A}$. For example, when $\mathfrak{A} = \mathbb{Z}$ is the ring of integers, the only units in $\mathbb{Z}[x_1, \ldots, x_r]$ are $\pm 1$; when $\mathfrak{A}$ is a field, all non-zero constant polynomials are units. The proof uses the degree in each variable and the fact that algebraic independence ensures no cancellation of leading terms can occur.
