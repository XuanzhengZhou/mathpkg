Let $B$ be a ring with an identity, let $A$ be a subring of $B$ containing $1$, and let $u_1, u_2, \dots, u_r$ be elements of $B$ that commute with each other and with every element of $A$. Then $A[u_1, u_2, \dots, u_r]$ is the totality of polynomials

$$
\sum a_{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r} u_1^{i_1} u_2^{i_2} \cdots u_r^{i_r}
$$

where the coefficients $a_{i_1 i_2 \cdots i_r} \in A$ and only finitely many of them are non-zero. Each such polynomial expression is computed using the ring operations in $B$.
