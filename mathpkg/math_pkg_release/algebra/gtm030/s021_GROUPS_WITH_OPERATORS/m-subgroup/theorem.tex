Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be an $M$-group. A subgroup $\mathfrak{H}$ of $\mathfrak{G}$ is called an \text{$M$-subgroup} if $hm \in \mathfrak{H}$ for every $h \in \mathfrak{H}$ and every $m \in M$. If $\mathfrak{H}$ is an invariant $M$-subgroup, the factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ becomes an $M$-group via

$$(g\mathfrak{H})m = (gm)\mathfrak{H}.$$
