This is the correspondence theorem for $M$-groups, generalizing the ordinary group correspondence theorem. It establishes a bijection between $M$-subgroups of $\mathfrak{G}$ containing the kernel $\mathfrak{R}$ and all $M$-subgroups of the homomorphic image $\mathfrak{G}'$. The key fact enabling this is that $\eta^{-1}(\mathfrak{H}\eta) = \mathfrak{H}$ whenever $\mathfrak{H} \supseteq \mathfrak{R}$. Applied to ring theory, this yields the standard correspondence between ideals containing the kernel and ideals of the quotient ring.
