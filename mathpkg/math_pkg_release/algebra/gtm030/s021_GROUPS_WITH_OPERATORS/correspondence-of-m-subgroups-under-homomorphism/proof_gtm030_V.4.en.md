We have seen that $\mathfrak{H} \mapsto \mathfrak{H}\eta$ is a mapping of $\{\mathfrak{H}\}$ onto the set of $M$-subgroups of $\mathfrak{G}'$. Also if $\mathfrak{H}_1$ and $\mathfrak{H}_2 \in \{\mathfrak{H}\}$ and $\mathfrak{H}_1\eta = \mathfrak{H}_2\eta$, then

$$\mathfrak{H}_1 = \eta^{-1}(\mathfrak{H}_1\eta) = \eta^{-1}(\mathfrak{H}_2\eta) = \mathfrak{H}_2.$$

Hence the mapping is 1-1.

To see that $\eta^{-1}(\mathfrak{H}\eta) = \mathfrak{H}$ when $\mathfrak{H} \supseteq \mathfrak{R}$: let $h_1 \in \eta^{-1}(\mathfrak{H}\eta)$. Then $h_1\eta = h\eta$ for some $h \in \mathfrak{H}$. Hence $h_1 = hk$ with $k \in \mathfrak{R}$. Since $\mathfrak{H} \supseteq \mathfrak{R}$, this implies $h_1 \in \mathfrak{H}$.

It is easy to verify that $\mathfrak{H}$ is invariant in $\mathfrak{G}$ if and only if $\mathfrak{H}' = \mathfrak{H}\eta$ is invariant in $\mathfrak{G}'$, and the $M$-factor groups $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ and $\mathfrak{G}'/\mathfrak{H}'$ are isomorphic under the correspondence $g\mathfrak{H} \mapsto (g\eta)\mathfrak{H}'$.
