Let $\eta$ be an $M$-homomorphism of $\mathfrak{G}$ onto $\mathfrak{G}'$ with kernel $\mathfrak{R}$ and let $\{\mathfrak{H}\}$ be the collection of $M$-subgroups of $\mathfrak{G}$ that contain $\mathfrak{R}$. Then the mapping

$$\mathfrak{H} \mapsto \mathfrak{H}\eta$$

is a 1-1 correspondence of $\{\mathfrak{H}\}$ onto the collection of $M$-subgroups of $\mathfrak{G}'$.

Moreover, $\mathfrak{H}$ is invariant in $\mathfrak{G}$ if and only if its image $\mathfrak{H}' = \mathfrak{H}\eta$ is invariant in $\mathfrak{G}'$.
