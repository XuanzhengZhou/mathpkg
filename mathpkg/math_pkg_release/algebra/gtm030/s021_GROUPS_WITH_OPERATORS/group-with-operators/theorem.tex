A \text{group with operators} (or \text{M-group}) is a system consisting of a group $\mathfrak{G}$, a set $M$, and a function $(a, m) \mapsto am$ from $\mathfrak{G} \times M$ to $\mathfrak{G}$ such that

$$(ab)m = (am)(bm)$$

for all $a, b \in \mathfrak{G}$ and $m \in M$. Equivalently, it is a group $\mathfrak{G}$ together with a mapping $m \mapsto \bar{m}$ of $M$ into the set of endomorphisms of $\mathfrak{G}$, where $\bar{m}(a) = am$.
