This corollary of the correspondence theorem is the $M$-group analogue of the third isomorphism theorem (also known as the "freshman theorem" or double quotient theorem). It states that factoring $\mathfrak{G}$ by $\mathfrak{H}$ yields the same $M$-group as first factoring by $\mathfrak{R}$ and then factoring the result by the image of $\mathfrak{H}$. The proof follows by applying Theorem 2 with $\mathfrak{G}' = \mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{R}$ and $\eta = \nu$ the natural $M$-homomorphism.
