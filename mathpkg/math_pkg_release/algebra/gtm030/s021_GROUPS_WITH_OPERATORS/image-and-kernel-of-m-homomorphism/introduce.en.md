This theorem establishes the basic properties of $M$-homomorphisms. The image of an $M$-homomorphism is closed under the operator action because $(a\eta)m = (am)\eta$, and the kernel is an $M$-subgroup because if $k$ is in the kernel then $(km)\eta = (k\eta)m = 1'm = 1'$, so $km$ also lies in the kernel. These facts mirror the ordinary group case and are essential for developing the homomorphism theorems in the $M$-group setting.
