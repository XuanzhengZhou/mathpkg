A normal series (or subnormal series) for an $M$-group is a descending chain of $M$-subgroups where each term is invariant in the preceding one. The factor groups of the series are the successive quotients $\mathfrak{G}_i/\mathfrak{G}_{i+1}$. Normal series are the basic structure used in the Schreier refinement theorem and the Jordan-Hölder theorem. As an example, for a finite cyclic group of order $n$, any factorization $n = q_1q_2 \cdots q_s$ gives a normal series whose factor orders are the $q_i$.
