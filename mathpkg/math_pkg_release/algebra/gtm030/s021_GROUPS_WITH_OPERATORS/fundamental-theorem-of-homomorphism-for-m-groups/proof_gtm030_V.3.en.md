The correspondence $g\mathfrak{H} \to g\eta$ is single-valued and defines a homomorphism $\bar{\eta}$ of the $M$-group $\bar{\mathfrak{G}} = \mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ into $\mathfrak{G}'$. The only new fact that must be established is that $\bar{\eta}$ behaves properly relative to the elements in $M$, that is,

$$((g\mathfrak{H})m)\bar{\eta} = ((gm)\mathfrak{H})\bar{\eta} = (gm)\eta = (g\eta)m = ((g\mathfrak{H})\bar{\eta})m.$$

Thus we have the factorization $\eta = \nu\bar{\eta}$ where $\nu$ is the natural mapping of $\mathfrak{G}$ onto $\bar{\mathfrak{G}}$. Also $\bar{\eta}$ is 1-1 if and only if $\mathfrak{R} = \mathfrak{H}$, where $\mathfrak{R}$ is the kernel of $\eta$. This establishes the theorem.
