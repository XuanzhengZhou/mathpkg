When $\mathfrak{H}$ is an invariant $M$-subgroup of an $M$-group $\mathfrak{G}$, there is a natural way to regard the factor group $\mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ as an $M$-group. The operator action on cosets is defined by applying the operator to a representative: $(g\mathfrak{H})m = (gm)\mathfrak{H}$. One must verify that this definition is independent of the choice of coset representative, which follows from the fact that $\mathfrak{H}$ is an $M$-subgroup. This construction makes the natural projection $\mathfrak{G} \to \mathfrak{G}/\mathfrak{H}$ into an $M$-homomorphism.
