Let $\{\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha} \mid \alpha \in J\}$ be a collection of $M$-groups indexed by $J$. The product set $\tilde{\Pi} = \prod_{\alpha \in J} \mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$ becomes an $M$-group under pointwise multiplication defined by

$$(gh)(\alpha) = g(\alpha) h(\alpha)$$

for all $\alpha \in J$. This $M$-group is called the **complete direct product** of the $M$-groups $\mathfrak{G}_{\alpha}$.
