A subdirect product is a subgroup of a complete direct product that surjects onto each factor group under the natural coordinate projections. This generalizes the observation that a subgroup of a direct product may still map onto every factor even without being the full product. The concept is fundamental in the structure theory of groups and universal algebra, providing a way to represent a group as a "dense" subgroup of a product.
