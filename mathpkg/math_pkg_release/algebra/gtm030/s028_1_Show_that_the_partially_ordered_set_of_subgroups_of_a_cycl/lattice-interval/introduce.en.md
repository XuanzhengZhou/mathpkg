In a lattice, given two elements $a \geq b$, the set of all elements $x$ satisfying $a \geq x \geq b$ forms a sublattice called the closed interval $I[a,b]$ (also referred to as a quotient). Intervals are fundamental building blocks in lattice theory, analogous to intervals in a totally ordered set, and they inherit the lattice structure from the ambient lattice.
