A lattice can be defined equivalently as a set $L$ with two binary operations $\cup$ (join) and $\cap$ (meet) satisfying $L_1$-$L_4$. The relation $a \geq b$ defined by $a \cup b = a$ (equivalently $a \cap b = b$) is a partial order, and under this order $a \cup b$ is the l.u.b. and $a \cap b$ is the g.l.b. of $a$ and $b$. The duality principle holds: interchanging $\cup$ and $\cap$ (and reversing $\geq$) yields another lattice.
