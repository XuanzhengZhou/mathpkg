Polynomial functions generalize polynomials to the setting of rings of functions. Given a commutative ring $\mathfrak{A}$ with identity, a polynomial function in one variable is obtained by evaluating a formal polynomial at the identity function $s \mapsto s$. In $r$ variables, polynomial functions form the subring $\mathfrak{A}[s_1, \cdots, s_r]$ generated by constant functions and the coordinate projection functions. A crucial difference from formal polynomials: when $\mathfrak{A}$ is finite, non-zero polynomials can induce the zero function (e.g., $\prod_{a \in \mathfrak{A}}(x - a)$), meaning the $s_i$ are algebraically dependent over the constants.
