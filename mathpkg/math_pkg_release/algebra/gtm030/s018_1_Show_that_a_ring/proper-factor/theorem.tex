If $b \mid a$ and $b$ is neither a unit nor an associate of $a$, then $b$ is a **proper factor** of $a$. In this case $a = bc$ where $c$ is neither a unit nor an associate of $a$, so $c$ is also a proper factor of $a$. If $u$ is a unit and $u = vw$, then $v$ and $w$ are units; thus units do not have proper factors.
