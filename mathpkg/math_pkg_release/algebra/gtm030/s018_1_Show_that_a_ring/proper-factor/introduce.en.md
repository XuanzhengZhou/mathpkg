A proper factor of $a$ is a divisor that is neither a unit nor an associate of $a$ -- it represents a genuine non-trivial factorization. If $a = bc$ with $b$ a proper factor, then $c$ must also be a proper factor; proper factors always come in complementary pairs. Notably, units cannot have proper factors at all, since any factorization of a unit $u = vw$ forces both $v$ and $w$ to also be units. This concept is central to defining irreducibility.
