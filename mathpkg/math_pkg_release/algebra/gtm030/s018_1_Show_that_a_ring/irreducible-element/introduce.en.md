An irreducible element is a non-unit that cannot be factored further in a non-trivial way -- any factorization $a = bc$ forces either $b$ or $c$ to be a unit. Irreducible elements are the atomic building blocks of factorization theory, playing the role analogous to prime numbers in the integers. The key question in any semigroup is whether every non-unit factors (perhaps uniquely) into irreducibles, leading to the concept of Gaussian semigroups.
