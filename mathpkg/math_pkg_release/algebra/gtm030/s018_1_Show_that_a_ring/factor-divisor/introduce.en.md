In a commutative semigroup $\mathfrak{S}$ with identity and cancellation, an element $b$ is a factor (or divisor) of $a$, denoted $b \mid a$, if $a = bc$ for some $c \in \mathfrak{S}$. The divisibility relation is transitive and reflexive. The units of $\mathfrak{S}$ are precisely those elements that divide the identity $1$, and they act as trivial factors dividing every element. This definition extends the familiar notion of divisibility from the integers to the general multiplicative setting of semigroups.
