Let $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, \cdots, x_r]$ be a polynomial ring with $x_i$ algebraically independent over $\mathfrak{A}$. Let $\mathfrak{S}$ be the subring of symmetric polynomials (those invariant under all permutations of the $x_i$). Then

$$\mathfrak{S} = \mathfrak{A}[p_1, p_2, \cdots, p_r],$$

where $p_1, \cdots, p_r$ are the elementary symmetric polynomials. Moreover, the $p_i$ are algebraically independent over $\mathfrak{A}$.
