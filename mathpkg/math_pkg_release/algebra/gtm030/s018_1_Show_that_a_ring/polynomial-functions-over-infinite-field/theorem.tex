Let $\mathfrak{F}$ be an infinite field. If $f(x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_r)$ is a non-zero polynomial in $\mathfrak{F}[x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_r]$ (with $x_i$ algebraically independent), then there exist elements $c_1, c_2, \cdots, c_r \in \mathfrak{F}$ such that $f(c_1, c_2, \cdots, c_r) \neq 0$. Consequently, the polynomial functions $s_1, \cdots, s_r$ are algebraically independent over the subring of constant functions.
