Let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a commutative semigroup with identity and cancellation. If $a \mid b$ and $b \mid a$, then $a$ and $b$ are called **associates**. The conditions for this relation are that $b = au$ and $a = bv$, whence $b = au = bvu$. By the cancellation law $vu = 1$, so $u$ and $v$ are units. Thus $a$ and $b$ differ by unit factors. The relation of associateness is an equivalence relation, denoted $a \sim b$.
