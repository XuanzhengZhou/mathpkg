Given a nonempty set $S$ and a ring $\mathfrak{A}$, the ring of functions $(\mathfrak{A}, S)$ consists of all functions from $S$ into $\mathfrak{A}$, with pointwise addition and multiplication. The constant functions form a subring isomorphic to $\mathfrak{A}$ itself. This construction generalizes the familiar rings of real-valued or complex-valued functions, and provides the natural setting for studying polynomial functions and other function algebras in abstract algebra.
