The descending chain condition (DCC) is a finiteness condition on an $M$-group $\mathfrak{G}$. It asserts that every descending sequence of $M$-subgroups $\mathfrak{G}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{G}_2 \supseteq \cdots$, where each term is invariant in the preceding one, must eventually stabilize. Equivalently, there is no infinite properly descending chain of such subgroups. The DCC is one of two chain conditions that together characterize the existence of a composition series.
