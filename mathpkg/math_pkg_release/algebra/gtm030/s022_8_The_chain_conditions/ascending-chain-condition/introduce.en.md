The ascending chain condition (ACC) is a finiteness condition on an $M$-group $\mathfrak{G}$. It asserts that every ascending sequence of $M$-subgroups $\mathfrak{G}_1 \subseteq \mathfrak{G}_2 \subseteq \cdots$, where each term is invariant in some fixed term $\mathfrak{G}$ of a normal series, must eventually stabilize. Equivalently, $\mathfrak{G}$ satisfies the ACC on invariant $M$-subgroups. Together with the descending chain condition, the ACC characterizes the existence of a composition series.
