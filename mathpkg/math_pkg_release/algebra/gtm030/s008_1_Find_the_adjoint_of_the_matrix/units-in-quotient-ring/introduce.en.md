The units in the ring $I/(m)$ of integers modulo $m$ are precisely the residue classes represented by integers coprime to $m$. This follows from the fact that $a$ has an inverse modulo $m$ if and only if $\gcd(a,m) = 1$, which is equivalent to the solvability of the linear Diophantine equation $ab - mq = 1$.
