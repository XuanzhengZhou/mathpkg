The proof of $\det(ab) = \det(a)\det(b)$ is established earlier in the text through the expansion of the determinant. Once this identity holds, if $(a)(b) = 1$, then taking determinants gives $\det(a)\det(b) = \det(1) = 1$. Hence $\det(a)$ is a unit in the coefficient ring $\Re$, with inverse $\det(b)$.
