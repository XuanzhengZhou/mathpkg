The intersection of any collection of subrings of a given ring is itself a subring. This follows directly from the definition of a subring: each subring in the collection is closed under addition, contains zero, and is closed under multiplication, and these properties are preserved under arbitrary intersections. This fact justifies defining the subring generated by a subset as the intersection of all subrings containing it.
