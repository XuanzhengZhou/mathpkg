An ideal is a special kind of subring that is closed under multiplication by arbitrary ring elements from both the left and the right. Ideals play the role in ring theory that normal subgroups play in group theory: they are precisely the subsets for which the quotient construction yields a ring structure on the coset space. The two-sided closure conditions (L) and (R) ensure that multiplication of cosets is well-defined.
