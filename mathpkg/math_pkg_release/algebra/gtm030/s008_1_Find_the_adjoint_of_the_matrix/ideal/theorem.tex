**Definition 3.** A subset $\mathfrak{B}$ of a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is called an ideal if $\mathfrak{B}, +$ is a subgroup of the additive group of $\mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{B}$ has the closure properties:
$$(L)\quad ab \in \mathfrak{B} \text{ for all } a \in \mathfrak{A} \text{ and all } b \in \mathfrak{B},$$
$$(R)\quad ba \in \mathfrak{B} \text{ for all } a \in \mathfrak{A} \text{ and all } b \in \mathfrak{B}.$$

Equivalently, $\mathfrak{B}$ is an ideal if and only if:
(1) $b_1, b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}$ implies $b_1 - b_2 \in \mathfrak{B}$,
(2) $b \in \mathfrak{B}$ implies $ab$ and $ba \in \mathfrak{B}$ for all $a \in \mathfrak{A}$.
