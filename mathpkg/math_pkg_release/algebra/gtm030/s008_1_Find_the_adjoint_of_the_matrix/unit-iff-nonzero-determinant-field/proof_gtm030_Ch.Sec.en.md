This follows directly from the theorem $\det(ab) = \det(a)\det(b)$. If $(a)$ is a unit, there exists $(b)$ such that $(a)(b) = 1$. Taking determinants gives $\det(a)\det(b) = \det(1) = 1$, so $\det(a) \neq 0$ in the field $\mathfrak{F}$. Conversely, if $\det(a) \neq 0$, then $\det(a)$ is a nonzero element of the field and hence a unit; the adjoint formula $(a)^{-1} = (\det(a))^{-1} \operatorname{adj}(a)$ provides the inverse.
