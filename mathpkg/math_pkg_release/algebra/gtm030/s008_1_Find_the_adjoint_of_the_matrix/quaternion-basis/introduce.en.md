Every quaternion can be expressed uniquely as a real linear combination $\alpha_0' + \alpha_1'i + \alpha_2'j + \alpha_3'k$ where $i, j, k$ are the three fundamental quaternion units defined as specific $2 \times 2$ complex matrices. This provides the standard representation of the quaternion algebra as a four-dimensional real vector space.
