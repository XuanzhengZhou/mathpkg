The difference ring (or quotient ring) $\mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{B}$ is constructed from a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ and an ideal $\mathfrak{B}$ by taking the additive cosets $a + \mathfrak{B}$ as elements. The ideal property ensures that coset multiplication is well-defined, yielding a ring structure on the quotient. This construction generalizes the integers modulo $n$ and is fundamental to the study of ring homomorphisms and their kernels.
