Let $\mathfrak{S}$ be a commutative semi-group with identity and cancellation law. For elements $a, b \in \mathfrak{S}$, let $(a, b)$ denote any determination of the greatest common divisor (g.c.d.) of $a$ and $b$. If $d$ is a g.c.d. of $a$ and $b$, then so is $ud$ for any unit $u \in \mathfrak{S}$. Conversely, if $d$ and $d'$ are any two g.c.d.'s of $a$ and $b$, then $d \mid d'$ and $d' \mid d$, so that $d \sim d'$ (they are associates). Thus the g.c.d. is determined up to a unit multiplier.
