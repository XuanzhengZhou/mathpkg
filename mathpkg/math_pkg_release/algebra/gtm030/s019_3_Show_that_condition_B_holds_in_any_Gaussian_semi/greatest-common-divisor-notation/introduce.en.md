The greatest common divisor of two elements in a Gaussian semi-group is unique only up to multiplication by a unit. Jacobson introduces the notation $(a, b)$ to denote any determination of the g.c.d. of $a$ and $b$. This convenient notation simplifies the statement of lemmas and theorems concerning divisibility and the interplay between greatest common divisors and the prime factorization properties captured by conditions A, B, and C.
