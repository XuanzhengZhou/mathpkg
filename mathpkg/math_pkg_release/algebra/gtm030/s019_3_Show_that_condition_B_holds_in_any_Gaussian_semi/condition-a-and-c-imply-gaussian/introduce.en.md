Theorem 1 combines the results of this section into a powerful criterion: if a commutative semi-group with cancellation satisfies both Condition A (existence of factorization into irreducibles) and Condition C (every pair of elements has a g.c.d.), then it is Gaussian, meaning the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic holds — factorization into irreducibles is unique up to order and units. The proof proceeds by showing that Condition C implies Condition B (every irreducible is prime) via Lemmas 1--4, and then appealing to the previously established fact that A and B together characterize Gaussian semi-groups. Jacobson notes that the semi-group of positive integers and the domain of integers both satisfy A (by consideration of absolute values) and C, hence are Gaussian.
