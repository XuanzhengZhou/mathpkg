Lemma 3 establishes that multiplication distributes over the greatest common divisor operation, up to associates. Concretely, multiplying a greatest common divisor of $a$ and $b$ by $c$ yields the same result (up to a unit) as taking the greatest common divisor of $ca$ and $cb$. This distributive law is crucial for the proof of Lemma 4 and ultimately for showing that Condition C implies Condition B (every irreducible is prime).
