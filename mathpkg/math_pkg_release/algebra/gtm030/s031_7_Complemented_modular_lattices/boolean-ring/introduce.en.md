A Boolean ring is a ring in which every element is idempotent, i.e., $a^2 = a$ for all $a$. This condition implies that the ring is commutative and that $2a = 0$ for all elements (every element is its own additive inverse). Any Boolean ring with identity can be turned into a Boolean algebra by defining $a \cup b = a + b - ab$ and $a \cap b = ab$, establishing a categorical equivalence between the two notions. The concept originated from the study of Boolean algebras and their ring-theoretic reformulation by Stone.
