A modular lattice $L$ with $0$ and $1$ is called \text{complemented} if for every $a \in L$ there exists $a' \in L$ such that $a \cup a' = 1$ and $a \cap a' = 0$. The element $a'$ is called a \text{complement} of $a$.

In an irreducible complemented modular lattice of finite height, the center is trivial and the lattice satisfies the covering property. Such lattices correspond to projective geometries and play a fundamental role in the coordinatization theorem.
