A complemented lattice is a bounded lattice (with least element 0 and greatest element 1) in which every element admits a complement. An element $a'$ is a complement of $a$ if their join is 1 and their meet is 0. The prototypical example is the lattice of all subsets of a set, where complement is the usual set-theoretic complement. Complemented lattices play a fundamental role in the structure theory of modular lattices and form the algebraic foundation for Boolean algebras.
