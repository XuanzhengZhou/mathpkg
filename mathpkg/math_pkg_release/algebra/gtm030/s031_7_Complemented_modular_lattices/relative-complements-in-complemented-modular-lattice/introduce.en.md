In a complemented modular lattice $L$, for any two elements $b \leq a$, a complement of $b$ relative to $a$ always exists. The proof uses the fact that $b$ has a global complement $b'$ in $L$ (satisfying $b \cup b' = 1$ and $b \cap b' = 0$). By modularity, $a = a \cap (b \cup b') = b \cup (a \cap b')$, and setting $b_1 = a \cap b'$ yields the desired relative complement. This proposition shows that complemented modular lattices are relatively complemented.
