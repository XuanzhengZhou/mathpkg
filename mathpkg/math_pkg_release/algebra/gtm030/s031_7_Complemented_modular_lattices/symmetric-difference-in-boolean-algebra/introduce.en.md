The symmetric difference $a + b = (a \cap b') \cup (a' \cap b)$ provides a natural addition operation on a Boolean algebra. It corresponds to the set-theoretic operation of taking elements belonging to exactly one of two sets. Together with the meet operation $\cap$ as multiplication, the symmetric difference makes a Boolean algebra into a commutative ring with identity, where every element satisfies $a + a = 0$. This ring-theoretic perspective is the foundation of Stone's representation theorem linking Boolean algebras and Boolean rings.
