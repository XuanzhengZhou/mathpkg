Let $\mathfrak{M}$ be a finitely generated module over a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ with a fixed set of generators $x_1, \dots, x_r$. For any submodule $\mathfrak{N}$, one defines the left ideal $\mathfrak{J}_j(\mathfrak{N})$ consisting of all coefficients $b$ that can appear as the coefficient of $x_j$ in an element of $\mathfrak{N}$ expressed as a linear combination of $x_j, x_{j+1}, \dots, x_r$. This lemma establishes that if one submodule is contained in another and their associated $J_j$ ideals coincide for every index $j$, then the two submodules are actually equal.
