Given an irredundant primary decomposition of an ideal $\mathfrak{B}$ in a Noetherian ring with distinct associated primes, the set of associated primes is uniquely determined. That is, if $\mathfrak{B}$ has two such decompositions, they must involve the same number of primary components and the same collection of prime radicals. The proof uses Lemma 2 and Lemma 3 to systematically quotient out primary components and deduce equality of the prime sets by induction on the number of components.
