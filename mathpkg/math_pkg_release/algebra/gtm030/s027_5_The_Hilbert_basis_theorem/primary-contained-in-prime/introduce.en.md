If a primary ideal $\mathfrak{Q}$ is contained in a prime ideal $\mathfrak{P}'$, then the radical (associated prime) of $\mathfrak{Q}$ must also be contained in $\mathfrak{P}'$. This is because any element of the radical is nilpotent modulo $\mathfrak{Q}$ and hence modulo $\mathfrak{P}'$, and since $\mathfrak{P}'$ is prime, nilpotence modulo $\mathfrak{P}'$ implies membership in $\mathfrak{P}'$.
