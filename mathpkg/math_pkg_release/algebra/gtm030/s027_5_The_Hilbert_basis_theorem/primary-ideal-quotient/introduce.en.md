For a primary ideal $\mathfrak{Q}$ with radical $\mathfrak{P}$, the ideal quotient $\mathfrak{Q} : \mathfrak{C}$ equals $\mathfrak{Q}$ whenever $\mathfrak{C}$ is not contained in $\mathfrak{P}$. This lemma is a key technical tool in the proof of the First Uniqueness Theorem for primary decompositions: it ensures that quotienting by certain primary ideals preserves the remaining components of an irredundant decomposition.
