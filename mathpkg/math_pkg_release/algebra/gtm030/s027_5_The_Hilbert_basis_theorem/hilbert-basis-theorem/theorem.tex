If $\mathfrak{A}$ is a ring with identity satisfying the ascending chain condition for left ideals (equivalently, every left ideal is finitely generated), then the polynomial ring $\mathfrak{A}[x]$ ($x$ transcendental) also satisfies the ascending chain condition for left ideals. Consequently, if every left ideal of $\mathfrak{A}$ is finitely generated, then the same holds for $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, x_2, \cdots, x_r]$.

In particular, if $\mathfrak{A}$ is a division ring or a principal ideal domain, then every left (right) ideal of $\mathfrak{A}[x_1, \cdots, x_r]$ is finitely generated.
