In a Noetherian ring, every irreducible ideal (one that cannot be written as a proper intersection of two strictly larger ideals) is primary. This theorem is a crucial step toward the primary decomposition theorem: since every ideal in a Noetherian ring can be expressed as a finite intersection of irreducible ideals, it follows that every ideal has a primary decomposition. The proof uses the ascending chain condition to construct the relation $\mathfrak{B} = (\mathfrak{B} : (d^r)) \cap (\mathfrak{B} + (d^{r+1}))$, showing that an ideal failing to be primary is necessarily reducible.
