If $\mathfrak{g}$ is a Noetherian ring, an element $a$ of a $\mathfrak{g}$-algebra is integral over $\mathfrak{g}$ precisely when all its powers lie in some finitely generated $\mathfrak{g}$-submodule. The forward direction follows from the integral dependence relation, which expresses $a^n$ as a linear combination of lower powers. The converse uses the ascending chain condition: the chain of submodules $(1) \subseteq (1,a) \subseteq (1,a,a^2) \subseteq \dots$ must stabilize, yielding an integral equation for $a$.
