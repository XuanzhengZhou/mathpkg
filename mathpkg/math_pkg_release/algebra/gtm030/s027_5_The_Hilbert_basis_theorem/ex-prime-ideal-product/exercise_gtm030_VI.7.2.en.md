Prove that if $\mathfrak{P}$ is a prime ideal and $\mathfrak{C}_1$, $\mathfrak{C}_2$ are ideals such that $\mathfrak{C}_1 \mathfrak{C}_2 \equiv 0 \pmod{\mathfrak{P}}$, then either $\mathfrak{C}_1 \equiv 0 \pmod{\mathfrak{P}}$ or $\mathfrak{C}_2 \equiv 0 \pmod{\mathfrak{P}}$.
