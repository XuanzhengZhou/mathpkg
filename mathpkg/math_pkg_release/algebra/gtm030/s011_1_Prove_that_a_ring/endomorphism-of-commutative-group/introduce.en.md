An endomorphism of an abelian group is a group homomorphism from the group to itself. In additive notation, this means the map respects addition: the image of a sum equals the sum of the images. The collection of all endomorphisms forms a set $\mathfrak{E}$ which, when equipped with pointwise addition and composition, becomes a ring—the endomorphism ring. This construction is fundamental in the theory of modules over rings.
