Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be an arbitrary commutative group written additively, with $+$ for the group operation, $0$ for the identity, $-a$ for the inverse, and $ma$ for the $m$-th multiple of $a$. An \textbf{endomorphism} of $\mathfrak{G}$ is a mapping $\eta : \mathfrak{G} \to \mathfrak{G}$ such that

$$(a + b)\eta = a\eta + b\eta$$

for all $a, b \in \mathfrak{G}$. The set of all endomorphisms of $\mathfrak{G}$ is denoted by $\mathfrak{E}$.
