Let $\mathfrak{G}$ be a commutative group and let $\mathfrak{E}$ be the set of all endomorphisms of $\mathfrak{G}$. Define addition $+$ pointwise by

$$a(\eta + \rho) = a\eta + a\rho$$

for all $a \in \mathfrak{G}$, and define multiplication $\cdot$ as composition (resultant):

$$a(\eta\rho) = (a\eta)\rho.$$

Then $\mathfrak{E}$, with these operations, is a ring, called the \textbf{ring of endomorphisms} of $\mathfrak{G}$.
