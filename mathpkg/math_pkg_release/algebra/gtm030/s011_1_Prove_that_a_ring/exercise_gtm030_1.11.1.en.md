Prove that a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ which possesses no proper left ideals (i.e., no left ideals other than $0$ and $\mathfrak{A}$) is either a division ring or a zero ring.

(Recall: A zero ring is a ring in which the product of any two elements is $0$; a division ring is a ring in which the non-zero elements form a group under multiplication.)
