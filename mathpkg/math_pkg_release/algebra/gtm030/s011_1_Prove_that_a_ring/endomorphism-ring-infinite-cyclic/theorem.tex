Let $\mathfrak{G} = I_{+}$ be the additive group of integers (an infinite cyclic group), and let $\mathfrak{E}$ be its ring of endomorphisms. Then $\mathfrak{E}$ is isomorphic to the ring of integers $I$ via the map $\eta \mapsto 1\eta$.

More precisely, each endomorphism $\eta$ is completely determined by the image $u = 1\eta$ of the generator $1$, and the correspondence $\eta \leftrightarrow u$ gives a ring isomorphism $\mathfrak{E} \cong I$.
