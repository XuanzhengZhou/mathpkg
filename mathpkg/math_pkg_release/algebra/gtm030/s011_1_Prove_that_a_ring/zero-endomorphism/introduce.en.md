The zero endomorphism is the simplest endomorphism of an abelian group: it maps every element to the group identity. It plays the role of the additive identity element in the endomorphism ring. Its existence is essential for establishing that the set of endomorphisms forms an additive group, which together with composition yields the ring structure of the endomorphism ring.
