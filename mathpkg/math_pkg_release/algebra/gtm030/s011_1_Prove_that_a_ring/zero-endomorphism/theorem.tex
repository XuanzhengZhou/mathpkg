The \textbf{zero endomorphism} $0$ of a commutative group $\mathfrak{G}$ is the mapping that sends every element $a \in \mathfrak{G}$ to the identity element $0$:

$$a \cdot 0 = 0 \quad \text{for all } a \in \mathfrak{G}.$$

This mapping is an endomorphism of $\mathfrak{G}$ and serves as the additive identity in the ring of endomorphisms: $\eta + 0 = \eta$ for all endomorphisms $\eta$.
