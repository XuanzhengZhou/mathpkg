In ring theory, left ideals, right ideals, and two-sided ideals are fundamental substructures analogous to normal subgroups in group theory. This proposition states that the basic set-theoretic and algebraic operations of intersection, sum, and product preserve the property of being a left or right ideal. These closure properties are essential for constructing ideal lattices and studying the ideal structure of rings.
