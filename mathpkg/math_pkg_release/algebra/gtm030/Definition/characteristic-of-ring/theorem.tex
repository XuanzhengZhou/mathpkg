If there exists a maximum $m > 0$ for the orders of the elements of the additive group $\mathfrak{A},+$, then the number $m$ is called the \text{characteristic} of the ring $\mathfrak{A}$. If no such maximum exists, the characteristic is said to be $0$.

If $\mathfrak{A}$ has an identity $1$ and $1$ has finite order $m$ in $\mathfrak{A},+$, then every element of $\mathfrak{A}$ has finite order dividing $m$, and the characteristic is $m$.
