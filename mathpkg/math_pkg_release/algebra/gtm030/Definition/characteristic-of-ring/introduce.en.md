The characteristic of a ring is the least positive integer $m$ such that $ma = 0$ for all elements $a$, if such an $m$ exists; otherwise it is $0$. When the ring has an identity, the order of the identity in the additive group determines the orders of all elements and hence the characteristic. This concept plays a crucial role in understanding the structure of rings, particularly in distinguishing rings of different characteristics.
