A mapping $a \mapsto \bar{a}$ of a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ onto a ring $\bar{\mathfrak{A}}$ is called an \emph{anti-isomorphism} if it is 1-1 and satisfies
$$\overline{a + b} = \bar{a} + \bar{b}, \quad \overline{ab} = \bar{b}\bar{a}.$$
An \emph{anti-homomorphism} is defined similarly without the 1-1 requirement.
