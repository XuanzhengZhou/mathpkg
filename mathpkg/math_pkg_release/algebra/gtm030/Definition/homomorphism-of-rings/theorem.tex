A mapping $\eta$ of a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ into a ring $\mathfrak{A}'$ is called a \emph{homomorphism} if
$$(a + b)\eta = a\eta + b\eta, \quad (ab)\eta = (a\eta)(b\eta).$$
If $\eta$ is 1-1, it is called an \emph{isomorphism}, and two rings are \emph{isomorphic} ($\mathfrak{A} \cong \mathfrak{A}'$) if there exists an isomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ onto $\mathfrak{A}'$. An isomorphism of a ring onto itself is called an \emph{automorphism}.
