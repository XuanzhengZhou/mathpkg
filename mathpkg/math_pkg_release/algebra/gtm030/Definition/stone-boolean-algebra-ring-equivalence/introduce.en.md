Stone's representation theorem establishes a bijective correspondence between Boolean algebras and Boolean rings with identity. Starting from a Boolean algebra, one defines addition as symmetric difference $a + b = (a \cap b') \cup (a' \cap b)$ and multiplication as meet $a \cdot b = a \cap b$, yielding a Boolean ring. Conversely, from a Boolean ring with identity, one defines $a \cup b = a + b - ab$ and $a \cap b = ab$ to recover the Boolean algebra structure. These two constructions are mutually inverse.
