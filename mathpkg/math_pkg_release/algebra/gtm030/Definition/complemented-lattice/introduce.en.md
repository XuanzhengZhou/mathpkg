A complemented lattice is a bounded lattice (one possessing a least element $0$ and a greatest element $1$) in which every element has a complement. The complement $a'$ of $a$ satisfies $a \cup a' = 1$ and $a \cap a' = 0$. The lattice of subsets of a set is the prototypical example: the complement of a subset $A$ is the usual set-theoretic complement.
