This theorem establishes the fundamental properties of the complement operation in a Boolean algebra. Unlike general complemented lattices, the complement in a Boolean algebra is unique, making complementation a well-defined unary operation. The mapping is an involutive anti-automorphism of the lattice, reversing order and satisfying De Morgan's laws. These properties make Boolean algebras the algebraic foundation of classical propositional logic and set theory.
