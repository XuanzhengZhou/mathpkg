The characteristic of a ring is the least positive integer $m$ such that $ma = 0$ for all elements $a$ in the ring, if such $m$ exists; otherwise the characteristic is $0$. If the ring has an identity $1$ and $1$ has finite additive order $m$, then every element has additive order dividing $m$, and the characteristic equals $m$. For example, $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ has characteristic $n$, while $\mathbb{Z}$ has characteristic $0$.
