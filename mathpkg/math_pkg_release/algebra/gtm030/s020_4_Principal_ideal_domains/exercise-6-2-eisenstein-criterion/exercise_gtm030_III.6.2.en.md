Prove the following irreducibility criterion due to Eisenstein: Let $f(x) = a_0 + a_1 x + \cdots + a_n x^n \in I[x]$ be a primitive polynomial (where $I = \mathbb{Z}$). If there exists a prime $p$ in $I$ such that
$$p \mid a_0,\quad p \mid a_1,\quad \dots,\quad p \mid a_{n-1},$$
but $p \nmid a_n$ and $p^2 \nmid a_0$, then $f(x)$ is irreducible in $I[x]$ and hence in $R_0[x]$, where $R_0 = \mathbb{Q}$ is the field of rational numbers.
