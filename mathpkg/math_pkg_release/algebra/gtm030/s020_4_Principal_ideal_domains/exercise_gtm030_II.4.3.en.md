Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a principal ideal domain and let $\mathfrak{B}$ be any commutative integral domain containing $\mathfrak{A}$. Show that, if the elements $a, b$ in $\mathfrak{A}$ have the g.c.d. $d \in \mathfrak{A}$, then $d$ is a g.c.d. of $a$ and $b$ in $\mathfrak{B}$.
