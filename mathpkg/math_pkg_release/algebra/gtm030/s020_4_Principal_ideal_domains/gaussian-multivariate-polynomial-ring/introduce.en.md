This corollary follows immediately from Theorem 4 by induction on the number of variables: if $\mathfrak{A}$ is Gaussian, then $\mathfrak{A}[x_1]$ is Gaussian, and then $\mathfrak{A}[x_1][x_2] = \mathfrak{A}[x_1, x_2]$ is Gaussian, and so on. Important examples include polynomial rings over fields and over the integers. Notably, for $r > 1$, $\mathfrak{F}[x_1, \dots, x_r]$ is Gaussian but not a principal ideal domain, showing that the class of Gaussian domains is strictly larger than that of PIDs.
