Gauss's Lemma implies that unique factorization lifts from a UFD to its polynomial ring. By induction, polynomial rings in any finite number of variables over a UFD remain UFDs. This is a fundamental theorem showing that $\mathbb{Z}[x_1, \cdots, x_n]$ and $\mathbb{F}[x_1, \cdots, x_n]$ (for any field $\mathbb{F}$) are UFDs.
