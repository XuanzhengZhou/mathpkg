Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a commutative integral domain with identity. An ideal in $\mathfrak{A}$ is called \text{principal} if it is generated by a single element. $\mathfrak{A}$ is a \text{principal ideal domain} (PID) if every ideal in $\mathfrak{A}$ is principal.

In a PID, the divisor condition A can be expressed as:
$$A'. \quad \mathfrak{A} \text{ contains no infinite properly ascending chain of ideals } (a_1) \subset (a_2) \subset (a_3) \subset \cdots.$$
