Theorem 2 states that every principal ideal domain is Gaussian (i.e., it is a unique factorization domain). The proof proceeds in two steps: first, one establishes that a PID satisfies the ascending chain condition on principal ideals, which rules out infinite strictly ascending chains $(a_1) \subset (a_2) \subset \cdots$. Second, for any two elements $a, b$, the ideal $(a, b) = (a) + (b)$ is principal, say $(d)$, and the generator $d$ is a greatest common divisor of $a$ and $b$. Together these properties imply unique factorization.
