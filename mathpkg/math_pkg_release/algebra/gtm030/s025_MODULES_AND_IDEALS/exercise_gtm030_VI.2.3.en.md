Let $\mathfrak{A}$ be a ring with an identity $1$. Show that any $\mathfrak{A}$-module $\mathfrak{M}$ permits a decomposition $\mathfrak{M} = 1\mathfrak{M} \oplus \mathfrak{N}$ where $1\mathfrak{M}$ is the submodule of elements $1x$ for $x \in \mathfrak{M}$, and $\mathfrak{N}$ is the submodule of elements annihilated by every $a \in \mathfrak{A}$.
