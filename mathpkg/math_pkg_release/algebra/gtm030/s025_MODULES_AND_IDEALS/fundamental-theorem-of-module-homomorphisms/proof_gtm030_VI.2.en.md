The concepts of homomorphism, isomorphism, endomorphism, and automorphism for $\mathfrak{A}$-modules are special cases of these concepts for groups with operators. Hence the results that we derived for these notions carry over without alteration to the module case. In particular, we know that the image $\mathfrak{M}\eta$ of a module under a homomorphism $\eta$ is a submodule, the kernel $\mathfrak{R}$ of this mapping is a submodule of $\mathfrak{M}$, and the canonical map $\mathfrak{M}/\mathfrak{R} \to \mathfrak{M}\eta$ given by $x + \mathfrak{R} \mapsto \eta(x)$ is an $\mathfrak{A}$-module isomorphism, yielding the "fundamental theorem" that $\mathfrak{M}\eta \cong \mathfrak{M} / \mathfrak{R}$. We also note that the submodules of the left module of the ring $\mathfrak{A}$ are precisely the left ideals of $\mathfrak{A}$.
