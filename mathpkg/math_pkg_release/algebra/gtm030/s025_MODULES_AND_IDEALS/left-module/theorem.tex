A \text{left module} over a ring $\mathfrak{A}$ is a commutative group $M$ (written additively) with $\mathfrak{A}$ as operator set such that for all $a, b \in \mathfrak{A}$ and $x, y \in M$:
\begin{align*}
&1_l. \quad a(x + y) = ax + ay, \\
&2_l. \quad (a + b)x = ax + bx, \\
&3_l. \quad (ab)x = a(bx).
\end{align*}
Equivalently, the mapping $a \mapsto a_l$ where $a_l(x) = ax$ is an anti-homomorphism of $\mathfrak{A}$ into the ring of endomorphisms of $M$.
