Given an $\mathfrak{A}$-module $\mathfrak{M}$ and a two-sided ideal $\mathfrak{U}$ of $\mathfrak{A}$ that annihilates $\mathfrak{M}$, one can regard $\mathfrak{M}$ as a module over the quotient ring $\mathfrak{A}/\mathfrak{U}$ by defining $(a + \mathfrak{U})x = ax$. This is well-defined because elements in the same coset of $\mathfrak{U}$ act identically on $\mathfrak{M}$. A particularly important case is when $\mathfrak{U}$ is the kernel of the anti-homomorphism $a \mapsto a_l$ from $\mathfrak{A}$ into the endomorphism ring of $\mathfrak{M}$, which consists of all ring elements that annihilate every element of $\mathfrak{M}$.
