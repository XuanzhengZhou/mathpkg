The (classical) adjoint of a square matrix $(a)$ is the transpose of its cofactor matrix. Specifically, the entry at position $(i,j)$ of $\operatorname{adj}(a)$ is the cofactor $A_{ji}$ of the element $a_{ji}$. The adjoint provides a compact way to express the cofactor expansion formulas as matrix equations and plays a central role in Cramer's rule and the theory of matrix inverses.
