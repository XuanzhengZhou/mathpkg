The proof is deferred to Exercise 3: Prove that if $\Re$ contains elements $a, b$ such that $ab \neq 0$, then $\Re_n$ ($n > 1$) is not commutative. A concrete counterexample for $n = 2$: take the matrix units $E_{11}$ and $E_{12}$, where $E_{ij}$ has entry $1$ at position $(i,j)$ and $0$ elsewhere. Then $E_{11}E_{12} = E_{12}$ but $E_{12}E_{11} = 0$, showing non-commutativity.
