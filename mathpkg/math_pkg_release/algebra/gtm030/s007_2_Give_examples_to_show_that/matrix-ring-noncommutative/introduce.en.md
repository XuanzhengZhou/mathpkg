The matrix ring $\Re_n$ over a commutative ring $\Re$ fails to be commutative whenever $n > 1$. This is because matrix multiplication is defined by the row-by-column rule, which is not symmetric. One can easily construct $n \times n$ matrices $A$ and $B$ such that $AB \neq BA$, for instance by taking matrices with a single non-zero entry in different positions.
