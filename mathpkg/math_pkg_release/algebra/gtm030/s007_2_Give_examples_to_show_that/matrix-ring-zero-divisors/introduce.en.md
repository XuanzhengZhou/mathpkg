When $n > 1$, the matrix ring $\Re_n$ contains non-zero elements whose product is the zero matrix. For example, in the $2 \times 2$ case, the matrix units $E_{12}$ and $E_{22}$ satisfy $E_{12} \neq 0$, $E_{22} \neq 0$, but $E_{12}E_{22} = 0$. Thus $\Re_n$ is not an integral domain for $n > 1$, even when the base ring $\Re$ is a field.
