A zero-divisor is a non-zero element that annihilates another non-zero element under multiplication. Left zero-divisors satisfy ab = 0 with b ≠ 0, while right zero-divisors satisfy ca = 0 with c ≠ 0. The presence or absence of zero-divisors fundamentally distinguishes integral domains from general rings.
