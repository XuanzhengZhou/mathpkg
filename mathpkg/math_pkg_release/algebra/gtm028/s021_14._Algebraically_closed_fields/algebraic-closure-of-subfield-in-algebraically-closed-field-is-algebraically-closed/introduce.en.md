This corollary states that if we take a subfield $k$ of an algebraically closed field $K$, then the set $k'$ of all elements of $K$ that are algebraic over $k$ (the algebraic closure of $k$ in $K$) is itself an algebraically closed field. The proof uses the definition of algebraic closure and the transitivity of algebraic dependence: any polynomial with coefficients in $k'$ has a root in $K$ (since $K$ is algebraically closed), and this root is algebraic over $k$ by transitivity, hence belongs to $k'$.
