An algebraic closure of a field $k$ is an algebraically closed field $K$ that contains $k$ as a subfield and is algebraic over $k$. Every element of $K$ is the root of some non-zero polynomial with coefficients in $k$. The algebraic closure is the smallest algebraically closed extension of $k$; every field possesses an algebraic closure (proved via Zorn's lemma), and any two algebraic closures of the same field are $k$-isomorphic.
