An algebraically closed field is a field that has no proper algebraic extensions. Equivalently, every non-constant polynomial with coefficients in the field has a root in the field. The complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$ form the prototypical example of an algebraically closed field, while the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ are not algebraically closed (since $x^2 + 1$ has no real root). Algebraically closed fields play a fundamental role in commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, as they provide a natural setting for studying polynomial equations and their solutions.
