This theorem gives four equivalent ways to characterize algebraically closed fields: the definition itself (no proper algebraic extensions), the property that all irreducible polynomials have degree 1, the property that every polynomial splits completely into linear factors, and the property that every non-constant polynomial has at least one root. The equivalence is proved by a straightforward chain of implications: (a) implies (b) via the existence of algebraic extensions of degree equal to the degree of an irreducible polynomial; (b), (c), and (d) are clearly equivalent; and (b) implies (a) by considering the minimal polynomial of any element in an algebraic extension.
