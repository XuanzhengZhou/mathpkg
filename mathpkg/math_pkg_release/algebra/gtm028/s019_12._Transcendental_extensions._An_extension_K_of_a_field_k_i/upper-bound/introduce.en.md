An upper bound of a subset $S_1$ of a partially ordered set $S$ is an element $c \in S$ that is greater than or equal to every element of $S_1$ with respect to the order relation $\prec$. The existence of upper bounds for all totally ordered subsets (chains) is the defining property of an inductive set, which is the hypothesis of Zorn's Lemma.
