The $n$-th symbolic power $\mathfrak{q}^{(n)}$ of a primary ideal $\mathfrak{q}$ is defined to address the fact that ordinary powers $\mathfrak{q}^n$ of a primary ideal need not be primary. It is constructed by extending $\mathfrak{q}^n$ to the localization $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ at the associated prime $\mathfrak{p}$, then contracting back to $R$. Symbolic powers preserve the primary property and the associated prime, making them a useful tool for studying primary decomposition and ideal powers in commutative algebra.
