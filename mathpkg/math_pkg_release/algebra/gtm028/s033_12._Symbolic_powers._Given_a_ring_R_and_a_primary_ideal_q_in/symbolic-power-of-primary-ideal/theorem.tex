Let $R$ be a ring with identity, $\mathfrak{p}$ a prime ideal in $R$ ($\mathfrak{p} \neq R$), $\mathfrak{q}$ a primary ideal belonging to $\mathfrak{p}$, and $n$ a positive integer. The ideal $(\mathfrak{q}^n)^{ec}$ (extension and contraction being made with respect to the quotient ring $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$) is called the $n$-th symbolic power of $\mathfrak{q}$ and is denoted by $\mathfrak{q}^{(n)}$.
