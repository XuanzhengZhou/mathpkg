Denote by $\mathfrak{p}_i$ the radical of $\mathfrak{q}_i$ and by $M$ the complement of $\bigcup \mathfrak{p}_i$. Let superscripts $e$ and $c$ denote extension and contraction with respect to $R_M$. The ideal $\mathfrak{a}^e$ in $R_M$ has the primary representation $\mathfrak{a}^e = \bigcap \mathfrak{q}_i^e$, and $\mathfrak{q}_i^e$ are primary for the maximal ideals $\mathfrak{p}_i^e$ of $R_M$ (Theorem 19, §11). Since $\mathfrak{p}_i^e$ are maximal and pairwise comaximal, the primary ideals $\mathfrak{q}_i^e$ are pairwise comaximal. By the Chinese Remainder Theorem, $R_M/\mathfrak{a}^e \cong \bigoplus R_M/\mathfrak{q}_i^e$. Taking lengths, $\ell(R_M/\mathfrak{a}^e) = \sum \ell(R_M/\mathfrak{q}_i^e)$, which is exactly $\lambda(\mathfrak{a}) = \sum \lambda(\mathfrak{q}_i)$.
