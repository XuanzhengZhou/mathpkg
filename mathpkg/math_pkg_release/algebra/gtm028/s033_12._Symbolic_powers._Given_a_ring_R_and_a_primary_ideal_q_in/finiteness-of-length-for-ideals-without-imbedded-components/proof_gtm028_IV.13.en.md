Let $M$ be the complement of the union $\bigcup \mathfrak{p}_i$ of the associated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}$. As was seen in §11, the ideals $\mathfrak{p}_i^e$ are the only maximal ideals of $R_M$, and since they are the associated prime ideals of $\mathfrak{a}^e$ (Theorem 17, §10), they are the only prime ideals in $R_M$ which contain $\mathfrak{a}^e$ (Theorem 7, §5). In other words, every prime ideal in the ring $R_M/\mathfrak{a}^e$ is maximal. On the other hand, this residue class ring is noetherian. Thus it satisfies both chain conditions (Theorem 2, §2), and admits a composition series. Therefore its length $\ell(R_M/\mathfrak{a}^e)$, that is, the length of the difference $R$-module $R_M - \mathfrak{a}^e$, is finite.
