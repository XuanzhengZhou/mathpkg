Theorem 24 establishes that for a noetherian ring $R$, any ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ without imbedded components has finite length $\lambda(\mathfrak{a})$. The proof proceeds by localizing at the complement $M$ of the union of associated primes of $\mathfrak{a}$, showing that in $R_M/\mathfrak{a}^e$ every prime ideal is maximal, and then invoking the chain conditions satisfied by noetherian rings to obtain a composition series.
