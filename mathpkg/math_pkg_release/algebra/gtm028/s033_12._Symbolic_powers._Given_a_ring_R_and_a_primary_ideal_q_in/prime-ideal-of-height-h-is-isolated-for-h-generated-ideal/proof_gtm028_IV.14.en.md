Using induction on $i$, construct $h$ elements $a_1, \ldots, a_i, \ldots, a_h$ of $\mathfrak{p}$ such that for every $i$, every isolated prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}_j$ of $R a_1 + \cdots + R a_i$ satisfies $h(\mathfrak{p}_j) \leq i$. By Theorem 30, this condition automatically holds for any choice. The construction proceeds by ensuring that at step $i+1$, the new element $a_{i+1}$ avoids all height-$i$ isolated primes of the previous ideal, which is possible by prime avoidance (Corollary 3 to Theorem 11, §6). At step $h$, $\mathfrak{p}$ becomes an isolated prime of the generated ideal.
