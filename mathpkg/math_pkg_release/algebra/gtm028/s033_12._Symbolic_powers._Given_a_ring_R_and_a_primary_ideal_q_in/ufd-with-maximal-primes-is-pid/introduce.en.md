Corollary 1 to Theorem 32 provides a clean characterization: a unique factorization domain is a principal ideal domain if and only if every proper prime ideal is maximal. In such rings, every non-zero prime ideal is both minimal and maximal. The proof proceeds by showing every prime ideal is principal, and then using minimality of principal ideals containing a given proper ideal to conclude it is itself principal.
