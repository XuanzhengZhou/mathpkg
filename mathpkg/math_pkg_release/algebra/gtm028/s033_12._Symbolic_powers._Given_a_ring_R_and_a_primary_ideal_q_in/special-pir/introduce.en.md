A special principal ideal ring (special PIR) is a PIR with a unique non-unit prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ that is nilpotent ($\mathfrak{p}^n = (0)$ for some $n$). The canonical example is $R/Rp^n$ where $R$ is a PID and $p$ is an irreducible element. Together with PIDs, special PIRs form the building blocks of all principal ideal rings via the structure theorem (Theorem 33).
