Let $R$ be a principal ideal domain. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The proper prime ideals in $R$ are those generated by irreducible elements, and they are maximal.
\item The ring $R$ is a unique factorization domain.
\item Any two non-zero elements $a$ and $b$ of $R$ have a greatest common divisor $d$, and $Rd = Ra + Rb$.
\item If $\mathfrak{a}$ is a proper ideal in $R$, then $R/\mathfrak{a}$ satisfies the descending chain condition.
\end{enumerate}
