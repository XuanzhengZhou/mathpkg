Theorem 32 summarizes the key properties of principal ideal domains (PIDs). In a PID, non-zero prime ideals are exactly the principal ideals generated by irreducible elements, and all such primes are maximal. Every PID is a unique factorization domain. The greatest common divisor of any two elements exists and is a linear combination of them. Finally, every proper quotient $R/\mathfrak{a}$ satisfies the descending chain condition on ideals.
