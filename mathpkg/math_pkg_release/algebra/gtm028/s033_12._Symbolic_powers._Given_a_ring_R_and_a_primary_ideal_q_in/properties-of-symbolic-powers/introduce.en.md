Theorem 23 summarizes the fundamental properties of the $n$-th symbolic power $\mathfrak{q}^{(n)}$ of a primary ideal $\mathfrak{q}$ belonging to a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$. Part (1) shows that symbolic powers remain primary with the same associated prime, and gives an element-wise characterization. Part (2) relates $\mathfrak{q}^{(n)}$ to the primary decomposition of $\mathfrak{q}^n$. Part (3) addresses the intersection of all symbolic powers of $\mathfrak{p}$, which is especially important in noetherian domains where $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \mathfrak{p}^{(n)} = (0)$. Part (4) establishes compatibility with multiplication of symbolic powers.
