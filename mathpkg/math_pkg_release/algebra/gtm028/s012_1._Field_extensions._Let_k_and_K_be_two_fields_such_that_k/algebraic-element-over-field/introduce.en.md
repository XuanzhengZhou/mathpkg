An element $x$ in a field extension $K/k$ is algebraic over $k$ if it satisfies a nonzero polynomial equation with coefficients in $k$. Otherwise, $x$ is transcendental over $k$. The distinction between algebraic and transcendental elements is fundamental in field theory: algebraic elements give rise to finite-dimensional extensions, while transcendental elements lead to infinite-dimensional ones (isomorphic to rational function fields).
