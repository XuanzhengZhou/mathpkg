Theorem 1 establishes the fundamental properties of the polynomial of least degree that vanishes at an algebraic element $x$. First, this polynomial is necessarily irreducible: if it factored as $f = f_1 f_2$, then $f(x) = 0$ would force $f_1(x) = 0$ or $f_2(x) = 0$, contradicting the minimality of the degree of $f$. Second, any other polynomial vanishing at $x$ must be a multiple of $f$: using the division algorithm in the Euclidean domain $k[X]$, the remainder would have smaller degree yet still vanish at $x$, forcing it to be zero.
