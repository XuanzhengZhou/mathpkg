A field extension is a pair of fields $k \subseteq K$ where $k$ is a subfield of $K$. The smaller field $k$ is called the ground field (or base field), and the larger field $K$ is called an extension field of $k$. This concept is fundamental to field theory and algebraic number theory, allowing the study of roots of polynomials by embedding the coefficient field into a larger field where those roots exist. The notation $K/k$ is commonly used to denote that $K$ is an extension of $k$.
