Let $S^\prime$ be the set of polynomials $f$ in $R[X_1, X_2, \cdots, X_n]$ which are independent of $X_n$, i.e., those mappings $f: I_n \to R$ such that $(i_1, i_2, \cdots, i_n)f = 0$ if $i_n \neq 0$. Then $S^\prime$ is a subring of $R[X_1, \dots, X_n]$ and there is a natural isomorphism
$$S^\prime \cong R[X_1, \cdots, X_{n-1}].$$
Consequently, $R[X_1, \cdots, X_n] \cong (R[X_1, \cdots, X_{n-1}])[X_n]$ as rings.
