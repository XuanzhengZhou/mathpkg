Let $S$ be the polynomial ring over $R$ with indeterminates indexed by a set $E$. For any $f \in S$, the set $\{\alpha \in E : X_\alpha \text{ occurs in } f\}$ is finite. Consequently, $f$ belongs to a finitely-generated polynomial subring $R[X_{\beta_1}, \dots, X_{\beta_n}] \subseteq S$ for some finite subset $\{\beta_1, \dots, \beta_n\} \subseteq E$, and $S$ is the union of all such finitely-generated polynomial subrings.
