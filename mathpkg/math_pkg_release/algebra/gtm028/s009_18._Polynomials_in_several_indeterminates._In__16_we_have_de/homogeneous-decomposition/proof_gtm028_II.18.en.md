Write $f$ as a finite sum of monomials: $f = \sum_{(i)} a_{(i)} X_1^{i_1} \cdots X_n^{i_n}$. For each integer $k \ge 0$, let $f_k$ be the sum of all monomials in $f$ whose total degree $\sum_i i_j$ equals $k$. Then each $f_k$ is either zero or a form of degree $k$, and $f = \sum_k f_k$. Since the degree of $f$ is the maximum $k$ for which $f_k \neq 0$, we obtain $f = f_0 + f_1 + \cdots + f_m$ with $m = \partial f$. The decomposition is unique because the monomials of a given degree are linearly independent over $R$ (each monomial is supported on a distinct $n$-tuple in $I_n$).
