Let $T_0$ be an isomorphism of $R$ onto $\bar{R}$, let $S = R[x_1, \cdots, x_n]$ and $\bar{S} = \bar{R}[y_1, \cdots, y_n]$ be polynomial rings over $R$ and $\bar{R}$ respectively. Then there exists a unique isomorphism $T: S \to \bar{S}$ extending $T_0$ and sending $x_i \mapsto y_i$ for $i = 1, \dots, n$. The extension is an isomorphism if and only if $T_0$ is an isomorphism and $y_1, \dots, y_n$ are algebraically independent over $\bar{R}$.
