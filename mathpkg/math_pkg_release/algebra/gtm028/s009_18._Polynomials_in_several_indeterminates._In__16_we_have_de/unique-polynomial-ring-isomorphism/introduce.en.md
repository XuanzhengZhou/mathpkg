Any two polynomial rings over the same coefficient ring $R$ in $n$ indeterminates are canonically $R$-isomorphic. There is a unique $R$-isomorphism sending the indeterminates of one ring to the corresponding indeterminates of the other. This justifies speaking of "the" polynomial ring $R[X_1, \dots, X_n]$ as a well-defined object up to unique $R$-isomorphism.
