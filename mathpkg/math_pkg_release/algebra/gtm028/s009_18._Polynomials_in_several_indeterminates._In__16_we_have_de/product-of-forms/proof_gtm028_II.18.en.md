If $f$ is a form of degree $d$, then every monomial appearing in $f$ has total exponent sum $d$. If $g$ is a form of degree $e$, every monomial in $g$ has total exponent sum $e$. In the product $fg$, each monomial arises as the product of a monomial from $f$ and a monomial from $g$, so its total exponent sum (degree) is $d + e$. If $fg$ is not the zero polynomial, all its monomials have the same degree $d + e$, hence $fg$ is a form of degree $d + e$. If all coefficient products cancel and $fg = 0$, the statement is vacuously true.
