If $R$ is an integral domain and $f$ is a non-zero polynomial in $n$ variables over $R$, then $f$ cannot vanish on every $n$-tuple drawn from an infinite subset $Q \subseteq R$. There exist choices of arguments in $Q$ for which the polynomial evaluates to a non-zero value. This generalizes the algebraic fact that a non-zero single-variable polynomial has only finitely many roots.
