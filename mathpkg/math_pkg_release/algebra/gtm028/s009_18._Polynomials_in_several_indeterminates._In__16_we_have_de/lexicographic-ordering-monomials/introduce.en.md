To compare monomials of the same total degree, Zariski and Samuel introduce a lexicographic (dictionary) ordering: compare the exponent of $X_1$ first; if equal, compare $X_2$; and so on. The monomial with the smaller exponent at the first position where the exponents differ is considered to come first. This ordering is a key tool for proving that the product of two non-zero polynomials is non-zero, establishing that $R[X_1,\dots,X_n]$ is an integral domain when $R$ is.
