Let $\Delta$ be a ring unitary over $R$, and fix an element $x \in \Delta$. The element $x$ is said to be \text{algebraic over $R$} if the evaluation homomorphism $f \mapsto f(x)$ from $R[X]$ into $\Delta$ is a proper homomorphism (that is, not an isomorphism). Equivalently, $x$ is algebraic over $R$ if and only if there exists a non-zero polynomial $g(X) \in R[X]$ such that $g(x) = 0$.
