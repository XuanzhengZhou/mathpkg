Let $R$ be an integral domain and let $S = R[x]$ be a polynomial ring in $x$ over $R$. An element $x' \in S$ is a transcendental over $R$ such that $S = R[x']$ if and only if $x' = a_0 + a_1 x$, where $a_1$ is a unit in $R$ and $a_0 \in R$. More precisely, if $x' = f(x)$ with $f(X) \in R[X]$ of degree $m$ and $x = g(x')$ with $g(X) \in R[X]$ of degree $n$, then $mn = 1$, so $m = n = 1$, and the leading coefficient of $f$ is a unit in $R$.
