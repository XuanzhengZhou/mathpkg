When $F$ is a field, the division algorithm of Theorem 9 makes $F[x]$ a Euclidean domain (since every non-zero element of $F$ is a unit), hence a principal ideal domain, and therefore a unique factorization domain. In $F[x]$, every polynomial has a unique monic associate, and two polynomials are associates precisely when they are non-zero scalar multiples of each other.
