An element $x$ in a ring $\Delta$ unitary over $R$ is transcendental over $R$ if the evaluation map $f \mapsto f(x)$ is an $R$-isomorphism from $R[X]$ onto $R[x]$. In this case $R[x] \cong R[X]$ as $R$-algebras, and the element behaves like a formal indeterminate — it satisfies no non-trivial polynomial relation over $R$. Transcendental elements generate the simplest kind of ring extensions: the polynomial rings.
