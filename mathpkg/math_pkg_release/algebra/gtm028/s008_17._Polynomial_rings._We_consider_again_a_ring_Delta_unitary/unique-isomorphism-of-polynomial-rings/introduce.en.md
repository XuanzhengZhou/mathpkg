This corollary states that polynomial rings in one variable over the same coefficient ring $R$ are uniquely $R$-isomorphic: there is exactly one $R$-algebra isomorphism sending the indeterminate of the first ring to the indeterminate of the second. This formalizes the idea that the name of the indeterminate is irrelevant — all polynomial rings $R[x]$ with $x$ transcendental over $R$ are canonically isomorphic.
