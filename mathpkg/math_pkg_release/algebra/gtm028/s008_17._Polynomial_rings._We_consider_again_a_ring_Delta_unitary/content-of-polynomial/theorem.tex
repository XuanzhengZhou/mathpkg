Let $R$ be a unique factorization domain and let $f(x) \in R[x]$ be a non-zero polynomial. The \text{content} of $f(x)$, denoted $c(f)$, is a greatest common divisor of the coefficients of $f(x)$. Any element satisfying this condition is a GCD of the coefficients and is determined up to a unit factor. Every non-zero polynomial can be written as $f(x) = c \cdot f_1(x)$ where $c = c(f) \in R$ and $f_1(x)$ is primitive.
