Theorem 9 establishes a generalized division algorithm for polynomials over an arbitrary ring with identity. When the divisor's leading coefficient is regular (not a zero divisor), one can always multiply the dividend by a suitable power of that leading coefficient to perform division with remainder. The quotient and remainder are unique under these conditions. This generalizes the classical polynomial division over fields, where the leading coefficient is always a unit (hence regular).
