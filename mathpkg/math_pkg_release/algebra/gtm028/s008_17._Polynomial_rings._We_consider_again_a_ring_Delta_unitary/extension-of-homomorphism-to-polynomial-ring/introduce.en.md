This theorem establishes the universal mapping property of polynomial rings: any homomorphism $T_0 : R \to \bar{R}$ can be uniquely extended to a homomorphism $T : R[x] \to \bar{R}[y]$ that sends $x$ to $y$. The extended map is defined coefficient-wise by $\left(\sum a_i x^i\right)T = \sum (a_i T_0) y^i$. The extension is an isomorphism precisely when $T_0$ is an isomorphism and $y$ is transcendental over $\bar{R}$.
