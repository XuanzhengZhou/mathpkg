This lemma states that a primitive polynomial $g(x)$ that divides a scalar multiple $b f(x)$ must already divide $f(x)$ itself. The proof uses Gauss's Lemma: the content formula $c(b f) = b \cdot c(f) = c(g) \cdot c(h) = c(h)$ (since $g$ primitive means $c(g)$ is a unit) shows that $b$ divides all coefficients of the quotient $h(x)$, so $h(x)/b \in R[x]$ and $g$ divides $f$.
