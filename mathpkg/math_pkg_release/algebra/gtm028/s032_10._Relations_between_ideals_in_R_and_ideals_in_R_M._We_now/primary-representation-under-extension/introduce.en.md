Theorem 17 describes how a primary decomposition of an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ in $R$ behaves under extension to $R_M$. The primary components that meet the multiplicative system $M$ are "lost" (their extensions become the whole ring $R_M$), while those disjoint from $M$ survive and their extensions form an irredundant primary decomposition of $\mathfrak{a}^e$. This theorem is essential for understanding how primary decomposition interacts with localization.
