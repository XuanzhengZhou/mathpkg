Theorem 21 is a converse to the fact that every ideal has a set of associated primes. It shows that for any finite set of prime ideals none of which is an isolated prime of $(0)$, one can construct an ideal having exactly those primes as its associated primes. The proof uses induction and relies on Theorem 20 to ensure that at each step the $\mathfrak{p}_n$-primary ideals are not forced to contain the previously constructed intersection.
