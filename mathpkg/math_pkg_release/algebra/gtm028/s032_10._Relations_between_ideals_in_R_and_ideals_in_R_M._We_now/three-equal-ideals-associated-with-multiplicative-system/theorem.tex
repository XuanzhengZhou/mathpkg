Let $R$ be a ring with identity and let $M$ be a multiplicative system in $R$. The following three ideals are equal:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[1)] The kernel $\mathfrak{n}$ of the canonical homomorphism of $R$ into $R_M$ (that is, the set of all elements $x$ in $R$ for which there exists an element $m$ in $M$ such that $mx = 0$).
\item[2)] The intersection $\mathfrak{n}'$ of all primary ideals in $R$ which are disjoint from $M$.
\item[3)] The intersection $\mathfrak{n}''$ of all primary components of $(0)$ in $R$ which are disjoint from $M$.
\end{enumerate}
