Theorem 18 identifies three a priori different ideals associated to a multiplicative system $M$ and proves they coincide. The kernel $\mathfrak{n}$ of the canonical map $R \to R_M$ equals the intersection of all primary ideals disjoint from $M$, which in turn equals the intersection of all primary components of the zero ideal that are disjoint from $M$. This result connects the algebraic description of the kernel of localization with primary decomposition theory.
