Theorem 20 identifies the intersection of all $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideals in a noetherian ring as exactly the intersection of those primary components of the zero ideal whose radicals are contained in $\mathfrak{p}$. The proof uses localization at $\mathfrak{p}$ and Krull's intersection theorem. This result is a key tool for analyzing the primary decomposition of $(0)$ and understanding the structure of associated primes.
