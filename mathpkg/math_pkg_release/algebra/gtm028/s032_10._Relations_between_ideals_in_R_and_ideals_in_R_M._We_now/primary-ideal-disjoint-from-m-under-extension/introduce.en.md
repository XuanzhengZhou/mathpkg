Theorem 16 studies the behavior of primary ideals under extension to a quotient ring $R_M$. If $\mathfrak{q}$ is a primary ideal disjoint from the multiplicative system $M$ with radical $\mathfrak{p}$, then both $\mathfrak{p}$ and $\mathfrak{q}$ are contracted ideals containing the kernel of localization. Moreover, $\mathfrak{q}^e$ remains primary in $R_M$ with associated prime $\mathfrak{p}^e$. This theorem is fundamental for understanding how primary decomposition behaves under localization.
