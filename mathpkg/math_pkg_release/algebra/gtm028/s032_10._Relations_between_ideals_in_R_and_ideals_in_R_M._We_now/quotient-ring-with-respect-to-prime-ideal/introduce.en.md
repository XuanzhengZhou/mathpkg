Theorem 19 specializes Theorems 15-18 to the crucial case where $M = R \setminus \mathfrak{p}$ is the complement of a prime ideal. In this setting, $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is a local ring with unique maximal ideal $\mathfrak{p}^e$, and there is a dimension-preserving bijection between prime ideals of $R_{\mathfrak{p}}$ and prime ideals of $R$ contained in $\mathfrak{p}$. This construction, called localization at a prime ideal, is one of the most important techniques in commutative algebra.
