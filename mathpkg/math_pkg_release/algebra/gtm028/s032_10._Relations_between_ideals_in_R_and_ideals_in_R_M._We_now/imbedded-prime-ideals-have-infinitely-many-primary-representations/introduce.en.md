Theorem 22 demonstrates the fundamental non-uniqueness of primary decomposition: while isolated primary components are uniquely determined, imbedded components admit infinite variation. Specifically, if an ideal has an imbedded associated prime $\mathfrak{p}$, one can produce infinitely many distinct irredundant primary representations that differ only in the $\mathfrak{p}$-primary component. The proof relies on a lemma that constructs a strictly smaller $\mathfrak{p}$-primary ideal with the same intersection with another primary ideal.
