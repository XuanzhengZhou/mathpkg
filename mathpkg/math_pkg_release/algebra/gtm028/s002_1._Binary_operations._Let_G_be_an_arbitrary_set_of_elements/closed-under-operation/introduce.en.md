A set $G$ equipped with a binary operation written multiplicatively is said to be closed under that operation if the product of any two elements of $G$ is again an element of $G$. This closure property is fundamental in algebra: without it, the operation would map some pairs outside the set, breaking the algebraic structure. When a set is closed under a binary operation, the operation becomes a genuine internal law of composition, making $(G, \cdot)$ a magma — the most basic algebraic structure.
