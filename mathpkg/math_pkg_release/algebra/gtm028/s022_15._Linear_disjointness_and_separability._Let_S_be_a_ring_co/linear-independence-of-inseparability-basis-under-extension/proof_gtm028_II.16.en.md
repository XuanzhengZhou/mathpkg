**Proof.** For any field $k'$ between $k$ and $K$ we denote by $L(k')$ the maximal separable extension of $k'(z)$ in $k'(x)$. Let $\{\xi_1, \xi_2, \dots, \xi_m\}$ be a basis of $k(x)$ over $L(k)$ (whence $m = [k(x):k(z)]_i$). It is immediately seen that $k'(x) = L(k')\xi_1 + L(k')\xi_2 + \cdots + L(k')\xi_m$. By our assumption, the $\xi_i$ must be linearly independent over $L(k')$ if $k'$ is a finite extension of $k$. Since every finite set of elements of $L(K)$ is contained in some $L(k')$, where $k'$ is a finite extension of $k$, it follows that the $\xi_i$ are also linearly independent over $L(K)$, and this proves the lemma.
