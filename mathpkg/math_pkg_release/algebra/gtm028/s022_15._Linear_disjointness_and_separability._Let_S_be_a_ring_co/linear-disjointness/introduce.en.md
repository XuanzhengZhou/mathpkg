Linear disjointness is a notion concerning two subspaces $L$ and $L'$ of a ring $S$ that contains a field $k$. The subspaces are linearly disjoint over $k$ if whenever one picks linearly independent sets from $L$ and $L'$ separately, all pairwise products of elements from the two sets remain linearly independent over $k$. This property is symmetric in $L$ and $L'$ and depends on the choice of the ground field $k$. Linear disjointness plays a central role in the theory of separable field extensions, especially in characteristic $p$.
