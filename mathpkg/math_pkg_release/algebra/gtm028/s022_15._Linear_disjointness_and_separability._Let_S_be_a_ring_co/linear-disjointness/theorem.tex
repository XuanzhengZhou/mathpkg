Let $S$ be a ring containing a field $k$ and such that the identity $1$ of $k$ is also the identity of $S$. Then $S$ is a vector space over $k$. Two subspaces $L$ and $L'$ of $S$ are said to be \textbf{linearly disjoint over $k$} if the following condition is satisfied: whenever $x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n$ are elements of $L$ which are linearly independent over $k$ and $x'_1, x'_2, \dots, x'_m$ are elements of $L'$ which are linearly independent over $k$, then the $mn$ products $x_i x'_j$ are also linearly independent over $k$.
