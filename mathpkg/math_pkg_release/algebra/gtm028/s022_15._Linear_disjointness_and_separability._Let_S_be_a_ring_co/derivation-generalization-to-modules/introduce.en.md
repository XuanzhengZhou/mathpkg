This remark notes that the definition of a derivation generalizes naturally from ring-valued maps to module-valued maps. Instead of requiring the codomain to be a ring $S$ containing $R$, it suffices that the codomain be an $R$-module $M$. The Leibniz rule $D(xy) = xD(y) + yD(x)$ remains meaningful because the products $xD(y)$ and $yD(x)$ are interpreted as the $R$-module scalar multiplication. This generalization is essential for the development of Kähler differentials and the module of differentials in commutative algebra.
