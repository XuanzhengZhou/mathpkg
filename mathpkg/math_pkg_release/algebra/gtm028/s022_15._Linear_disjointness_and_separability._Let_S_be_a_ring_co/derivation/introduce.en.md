A derivation is an additive map $D: R \to S$ satisfying the Leibniz rule $D(xy) = xD(y) + yD(x)$. Derivations generalize the notion of differentiation from calculus to arbitrary rings. Key elementary properties include $D(x^n) = n x^{n-1} D(x)$ and $D(e) = 0$ for the unit element. The kernel of a derivation is always a subring. The concept of an $R'$-derivation (a derivation trivial on a subring $R'$) is fundamental for the theory of differential fields and inseparable extensions.
