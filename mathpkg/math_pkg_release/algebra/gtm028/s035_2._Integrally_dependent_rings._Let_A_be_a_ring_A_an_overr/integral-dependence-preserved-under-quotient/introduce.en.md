This lemma establishes that the property of integral dependence between two rings is preserved when passing to quotient rings. Given an overring $A'$ integrally dependent on $A$, and any ideal $\mathfrak{J}'$ of $A'$, the quotient $A'/\mathfrak{J}'$ remains integrally dependent on the quotient $A/(\mathfrak{J}' \cap A)$. The proof is straightforward: one simply reduces an equation of integral dependence for any element $x \in A'$ modulo the ideal $\mathfrak{J}'$. The identification of $A/(\mathfrak{J}' \cap A)$ as a subring of $A'/\mathfrak{J}'$ follows from the standard canonical embedding of quotient rings (Chapter III, §5, Theorem 8).
