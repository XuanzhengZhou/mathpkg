In the study of ring extensions $A \subseteq A'$, the terminology "lies over" describes the relationship between prime ideals of the two rings under the contraction map. A prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}'$ of $A'$ is said to lie over a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of $A$ if $\mathfrak{p}' \cap A = \mathfrak{p}$. This concept is central to the Cohen-Seidenberg theory of integral extensions. Theorem 3 (the Lying Over theorem) guarantees that when $A'$ is integrally dependent on $A$, every prime ideal of $A$ has at least one prime ideal of $A'$ lying over it.
