Let $A'$ be integrally dependent on $A$, and let $\mathfrak{p}$ be a prime ideal in $A$. Then $A'\mathfrak{p} \neq A'$. Consequently, there exists a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}'$ in $A'$ such that $\mathfrak{p}' \cap A = \mathfrak{p}$. If $\mathfrak{p}$ is maximal, then any such $\mathfrak{p}'$ is also maximal.
