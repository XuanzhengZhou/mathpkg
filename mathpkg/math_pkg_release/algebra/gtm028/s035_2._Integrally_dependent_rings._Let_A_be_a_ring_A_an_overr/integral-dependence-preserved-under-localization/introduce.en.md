This lemma shows that integral dependence between two rings is preserved under localization (quotient ring formation with respect to a multiplicative set). Given an overring $A'$ integrally dependent on $A$, and a multiplicatively closed set $S \subseteq A$ not containing zero, the localization $A'_S$ remains integrally dependent on $A_S$. The proof reduces to the case where $A$ and $A'$ embed in their localizations (by first quotienting out elements annihilated by $S$ and applying Lemma 1), then divides an integral dependence equation for $x \in A'$ by $s^n$ to obtain one for $x/s \in A'_S$.
