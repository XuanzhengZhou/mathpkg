In any ring with identity, the set of all units (elements possessing multiplicative inverses) forms a group under multiplication. This unit group, often denoted $R^\times$, is a fundamental invariant of the ring. The proposition follows from the facts that the product of two units is a unit and the inverse of a unit is a unit, while the identity element 1 serves as the group identity.
