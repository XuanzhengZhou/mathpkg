A ring is an algebraic structure consisting of a set equipped with two binary operations: addition and multiplication. Addition forms an abelian group, while multiplication is associative and distributes over addition from both sides. The ring axioms unify the arithmetic of integers with the algebraic properties of matrices and polynomials, forming the foundational object of commutative algebra.
