This proposition describes how integer multiples interact with ring multiplication. The formula $n(ab) = (na)b = a(nb)$ shows that multiplying an element by an integer $n$ (which means adding it to itself $n$ times) commutes with ring multiplication in the expected way. This follows from the generalized distributive laws and is essential for computations involving both additive and multiplicative structure.
