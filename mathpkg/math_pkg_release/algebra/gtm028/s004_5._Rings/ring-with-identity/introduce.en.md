A ring with identity is a ring possessing a multiplicative identity element, denoted by 1, satisfying $1 \cdot a = a \cdot 1 = a$ for all $a$. In such rings, the notion of multiplicative inverses becomes meaningful, and elements with inverses are called units. Rings with identity form the natural setting for much of commutative algebra, including the definition of integral domains and fields.
