For a simple transcendental extension $F = K(x)$, a derivation $D$ of $K$ extends uniquely to $F$ with an arbitrary prescribed value $D'(x) = u$. This follows immediately from Theorem 39 because the only algebraic relation satisfied by the transcendental generator $x$ is the zero polynomial, so no compatibility condition constrains the choice of $u$.
