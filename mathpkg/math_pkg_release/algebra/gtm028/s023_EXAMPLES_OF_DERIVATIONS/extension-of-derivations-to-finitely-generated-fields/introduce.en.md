Theorem 39 provides the fundamental criterion for extending a derivation $D$ from a base field $K$ to a finitely generated extension $F = K(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$. The key formula expresses the action of any extended derivation $D'$ on a rational expression in terms of the coefficientwise derivative $g^D$ and the formal partial derivatives $D_i g$. The necessary and sufficient condition involves a generating set $\{f_j\}$ of the ideal of algebraic relations among the generators $x_i$, and when satisfied, the extension is unique.
