Let $K$ be a field and $F = K(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ a finitely generated extension field of $K$. The dimension $s$ of the $F$-vector space $\mathcal{D}_{F/K}$ of $K$-derivations of $F$ is equal to the smallest number of elements $u_1, \ldots, u_t$ of $F$ such that $F$ is separable algebraic over $K(u_1, \ldots, u_t)$. In particular, if $K$ is perfect, then $s$ equals the transcendence degree of $F/K$.
