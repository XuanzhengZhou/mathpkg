Although the composition of two derivations is generally not a derivation, their commutator $[D, D'] = D'D - DD'$ is always a derivation. This fact endows the space of derivations with a Lie algebra structure and connects the theory of derivations in commutative algebra to the broader theory of Lie algebras. The additive property is straightforward; the Leibniz rule holds because the cross terms $D'(x)D(y)$ and $D'(y)D(x)$ cancel.
