Let $D$ and $D'$ be derivations of a field $K$ with values in a field $L$. The composite mappings $D D'$ and $D' D$ are not, in general, derivations. However, the mapping $[D, D'] := D' D - D D'$ defined by
$$[D, D'](x) = D'(D(x)) - D(D'(x))$$
is a derivation of $K$. This derivation $[D, D']$ is called the bracket of $D$ and $D'$.
