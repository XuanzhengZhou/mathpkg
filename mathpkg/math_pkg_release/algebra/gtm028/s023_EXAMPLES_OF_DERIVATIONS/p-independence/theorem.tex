Let $F$ be a field of characteristic $p \neq 0$. A finite set of elements $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$ of $F$ is said to be $p$-independent if the $n^p$ monomials
$$x_1^{i_1} x_2^{i_2} \cdots x_n^{i_n} \quad (0 \leq i_q < p)$$
are linearly independent over $F^p$. An arbitrary (finite or infinite) subset $S$ of $F$ is said to be a $p$-independent set if every finite subset of $S$ is $p$-independent.
