For a simple purely inseparable extension $F = K(x)$ in characteristic $p$, the minimal polynomial is $X^{p^e} - y$ where $y = x^{p^e} \in K$. Applying Theorem 39, the single algebraic relation $x^{p^e} - y = 0$ yields the condition $D(y) = 0$ for extendability. Once this condition holds, $D'(x)$ is free, unlike the separable case where the extension is unique.
