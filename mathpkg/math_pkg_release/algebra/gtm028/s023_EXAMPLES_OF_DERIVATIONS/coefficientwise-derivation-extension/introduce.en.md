Given a derivation $D$ on a ring $R$, one can extend it to a derivation on the polynomial ring $A = R[X_1, \ldots, X_n]$ by applying $D$ coefficientwise. This construction is the simplest example of extending a derivation from a base ring to a polynomial algebra over it. The resulting derivation $f \mapsto f^D$ acts trivially on the indeterminates $X_i$ and applies $D$ to each coefficient.
