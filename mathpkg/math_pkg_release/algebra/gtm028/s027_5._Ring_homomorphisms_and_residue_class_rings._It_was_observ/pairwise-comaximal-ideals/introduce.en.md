Theorem 31 generalizes the Chinese Remainder Theorem from integers to arbitrary rings with identity. Two ideals are comaximal if their sum is the whole ring (generalizing coprime integers). For pairwise comaximal ideals, the intersection equals the product, and simultaneous congruences can be solved. The result about radicals shows that comaximality can be checked at the level of radical ideals. This theorem is fundamental for primary decomposition and the structure theory of Noetherian rings.
