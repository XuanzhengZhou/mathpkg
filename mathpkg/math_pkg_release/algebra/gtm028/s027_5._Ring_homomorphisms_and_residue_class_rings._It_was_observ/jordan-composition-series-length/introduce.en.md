Jordan's theorem (the first half of the Jordan-Hölder theorem) establishes that the length of a composition series is an invariant of the module: all composition series have the same number of terms. Furthermore, any normal series without repetitions can be refined to a composition series, and its length is bounded by the module's length. This justifies defining the length $l(M)$ as the number of composition factors.
