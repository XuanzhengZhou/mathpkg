Theorem 12 provides a fundamental characterization of nilpotent elements: an element is nilpotent if and only if it belongs to every prime ideal of the ring. In other words, the nilradical $\sqrt{(0)}$ (the set of all nilpotent elements) equals the intersection of all prime ideals. The proof uses Zorn's Lemma to construct, for any non-nilpotent element $u$, a prime ideal not containing $u$ — a classic application of the axiom of choice in commutative algebra.
