We may assume $\mathfrak{A} \neq R$ since $\mathfrak{A} = R$ if and only if $\mathfrak{A}T = R'$. The condition for $\mathfrak{A}$ (or $\mathfrak{A}T$) to be prime or maximal is expressed in terms of the quotient ring $R/\mathfrak{A}$ (or $R'/\mathfrak{A}T$). By Theorem 6, $R/\mathfrak{A} \cong R'/\mathfrak{A}T$ (since $R' \cong R/N$ and $\mathfrak{A}T \cong \mathfrak{A}/N$). Therefore $R/\mathfrak{A}$ is an integral domain (resp. a field) if and only if $R'/\mathfrak{A}T$ is, which by the criterion of §8 establishes the result.
