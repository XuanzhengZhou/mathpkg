Let $\mathfrak{Q}$ and $\mathfrak{P}$ be ideals in a ring $R$. Then $\mathfrak{Q}$ is primary and $\mathfrak{P}$ is its radical if and only if the following conditions are satisfied:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\mathfrak{Q} \subseteq \mathfrak{P}$.
\item[(b)] If $b \in \mathfrak{P}$, then $b^m \in \mathfrak{Q}$ for some $m$ (where $m$ may depend on $b$).
\item[(c)] If $ab \in \mathfrak{Q}$ and $a \notin \mathfrak{Q}$, then $b \in \mathfrak{P}$.
\end{enumerate}
