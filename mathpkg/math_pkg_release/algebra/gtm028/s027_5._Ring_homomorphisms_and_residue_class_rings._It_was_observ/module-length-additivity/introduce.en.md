Module length is additive with respect to short exact sequences: the length of a module equals the sum of the lengths of a submodule and its quotient. This fundamental property generalizes the dimension formula for vector spaces. The second formula $l(L) + l(N) = l(L+N) + l(L \cap N)$ is the module-theoretic analogue of the inclusion-exclusion principle and follows from the isomorphism $(L+N)/N \cong L/(L \cap N)$.
