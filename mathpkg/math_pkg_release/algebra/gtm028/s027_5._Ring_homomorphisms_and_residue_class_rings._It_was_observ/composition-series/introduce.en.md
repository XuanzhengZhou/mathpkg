A composition series is a maximal proper chain of submodules — it cannot be refined further without introducing repetitions. Equivalently, each successive quotient (difference module) must be simple, having exactly two submodules. Composition series are the building blocks of the Jordan-Hölder theorem, which asserts that the simple quotients appearing in any composition series are uniquely determined up to isomorphism and multiplicity. This generalizes the fundamental theorem of arithmetic to the module setting.
