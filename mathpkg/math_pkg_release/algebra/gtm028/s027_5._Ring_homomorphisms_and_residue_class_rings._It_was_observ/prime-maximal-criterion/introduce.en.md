The nature of an ideal $\mathfrak{A}$ is reflected in the structure of the quotient ring $R/\mathfrak{A}$. An ideal is prime precisely when the quotient has no zero-divisors (is an integral domain), and maximal precisely when the quotient is a field. Since every field is an integral domain, this immediately implies that every maximal ideal is prime in rings with identity. This criterion provides the fundamental bridge between ideal theory and the structure of residue class rings.
