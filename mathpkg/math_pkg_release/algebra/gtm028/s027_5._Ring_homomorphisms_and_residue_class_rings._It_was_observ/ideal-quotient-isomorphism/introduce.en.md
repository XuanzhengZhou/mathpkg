This corollary of Theorem 6 gives the double quotient isomorphism for rings. If $N \subset \mathfrak{A}$ are nested ideals in a ring $R$, then the quotient $\mathfrak{A}/N$ is an ideal in the residue class ring $R/N$, and the quotient of $R/N$ by this ideal is isomorphic to $R/\mathfrak{A}$. This is the ring-theoretic analogue of the third isomorphism theorem for groups.
