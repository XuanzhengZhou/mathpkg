Let $R$ be a ring with identity. Let $R_1, R_2, \cdots, R_n$ be subrings of $R$ such that
$$R = R_1 + R_2 + \cdots + R_n, \quad R_i R_j = (0) \text{ for } i \neq j.$$
Then each $R_i$ is an ideal and the sum is direct. All elements of $R_i$ are zero divisors unless $R_j = (0)$ for all $j \neq i$. There exist uniquely determined elements $e_i$ such that $1 = e_1 + \cdots + e_n$, $e_i \in R_i$, with $e_i^2 = e_i$, $e_i e_j = 0$ for $i \neq j$, and $R_i = Re_i$. Moreover, if $\mathfrak{A}$ is an ideal in $R$, then $\mathfrak{A} = \mathfrak{A}_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{A}_n$ where $\mathfrak{A}_i = R_i \cap \mathfrak{A} = R_i\mathfrak{A}$.

If $R/\mathfrak{A}$ is the natural homomorphic image, then $R/\mathfrak{A}$ is the direct sum of the ideals $R_iT$ and $R_iT \cong R_i/\mathfrak{A}_i$. If $\mathfrak{A}$ is primary in $R$ and $\mathfrak{A} \neq R$, then exactly one $R_iT$ is non-zero and all others are zero.
