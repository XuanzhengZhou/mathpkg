Theorem 18 establishes that finiteness ascends from the ring to its modules: if $R$ is a Noetherian ring (satisfies the a.c.c. on ideals), then any finitely generated unitary $R$-module also satisfies the a.c.c. on submodules. This is a cornerstone result that makes the theory of Noetherian rings so powerful — finite generation over a Noetherian ring guarantees the Noetherian property for the module.
