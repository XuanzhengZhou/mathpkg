Let $M = (x_1, \cdots, x_n)$. We proceed by induction on $n$. For $n = 1$, $M$ is cyclic, say $M = Rx$. Then the mapping $a \to ax$ is an $R$-homomorphism of $R$ (as an $R$-module) onto $M$, with kernel $\mathfrak{A} = \{a \in R \mid ax = 0\}$. Hence $M \cong R/\mathfrak{A}$. Since $R$ satisfies the a.c.c., so does $R/\mathfrak{A}$ by Theorem 16, hence so does $M$.

For $n > 1$, let $N = (x_1, \cdots, x_{n-1})$. By induction, $N$ satisfies the a.c.c. Now $M/N$ is cyclic (generated by the image of $x_n$), hence satisfies the a.c.c. by the $n = 1$ case. By Theorem 16, $M$ satisfies the a.c.c.
