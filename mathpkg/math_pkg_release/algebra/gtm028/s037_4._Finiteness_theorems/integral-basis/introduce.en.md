An integral basis is a basis of the extension field $F$ over $K$ consisting of elements integral over $A$ such that the $A$-module they span equals the full integral closure $A'$. This refines Theorem 7: while Theorem 7 guarantees $A'$ is contained in some finite $A$-module spanned by a basis, an integral basis achieves equality $A' = \sum_i A y_i$. Integral bases are fundamental in algebraic number theory; for example, the ring of integers of a number field admits an integral basis over $\mathbb{Z}$.
