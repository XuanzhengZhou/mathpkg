Theorem 8 is a separable analogue of the Noether normalization lemma. Given a finite-type integral domain of transcendence degree $d$ over a field $k$, whose quotient field is separably generated, one can find $d$ linear combinations of the generators that form a separating transcendence basis -- that is, $k[x]$ becomes integral and separable over the polynomial subring $k[y_1,\ldots,y_d]$. The proof proceeds by induction on the number of generators, using properties of minimal polynomials and the transitivity of integral dependence and separability. This theorem is a key ingredient in the proof of Theorem 9.
