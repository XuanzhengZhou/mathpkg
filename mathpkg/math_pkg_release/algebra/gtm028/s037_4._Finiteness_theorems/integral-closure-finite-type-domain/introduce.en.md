Theorem 9 is the central finiteness result of §4. It states that for a finite-type integral domain $A$ over a field $k$, its integral closure in any finite algebraic extension of the quotient field is again a finite-type $k$-algebra and a finite $A$-module. The proof reduces to the case $F = k(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$ using an auxiliary ring $A^0 = A[y_1,\ldots,y_q]$, then applies Theorem 8 (separating transcendence basis) to find a polynomial subring $B = k[z_1,\ldots,z_d]$ over which $A$ is integral and separable. Since $B$ is integrally closed and Noetherian, Corollary 1 to Theorem 7 yields the result.
