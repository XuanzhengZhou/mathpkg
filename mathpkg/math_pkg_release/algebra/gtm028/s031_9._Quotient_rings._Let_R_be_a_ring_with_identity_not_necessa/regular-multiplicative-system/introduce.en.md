A regular multiplicative system is a multiplicative system $M$ in a ring $R$ whose elements are all regular (non-zero-divisors). When $M$ is regular, the quotient ring $R_M$ embeds naturally into the total quotient ring of $R$. This is the classical case of localization where denominators are guaranteed to be non-zero-divisors.
