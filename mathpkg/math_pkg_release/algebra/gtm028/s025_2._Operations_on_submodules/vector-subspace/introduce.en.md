A vector subspace is the special case of a submodule when the base ring $R$ is a field $F$. In this setting, an $F$-submodule of a vector space $M$ is called a subspace of $M$. Every subspace is itself a vector space over the same field $F$, making the concept of subspace identical to that of a submodule in the context of vector spaces.
