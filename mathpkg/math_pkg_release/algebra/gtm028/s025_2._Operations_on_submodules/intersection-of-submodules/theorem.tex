If $L$ and $N$ are submodules of $M$, then so is $L \cap N$, consisting of those elements of $M$ common to $L$ and $N$. This operation is commutative and associative, and the module $M$ itself acts as identity for this operation, since $M \cap N = N$ for any submodule $N$. If $M_1, \dots, M_r$ are submodules of $M$, then $M_1 \cap \cdots \cap M_r$ is also denoted by $\bigcap_{i=1}^r M_i$.
