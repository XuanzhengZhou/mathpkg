The intersection of submodules of $M$ is again a submodule. This operation is commutative and associative, with the whole module $M$ serving as the identity element. Together with the sum operation, intersection forms the lattice structure of submodules: intersection is the meet (greatest lower bound) while sum is the join (least upper bound). The intersection of finitely many submodules $M_1, \dots, M_r$ is written $\bigcap_{i=1}^r M_i$.
