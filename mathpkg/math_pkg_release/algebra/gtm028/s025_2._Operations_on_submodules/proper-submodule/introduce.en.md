A submodule $N$ of $M$ is called proper if $N \neq M$ and $N \neq (0)$. In other words, proper submodules are those that are neither the whole module $M$ nor the trivial zero submodule. Every module $M$ always possesses the two trivial submodules $(0)$ and $M$; proper submodules provide the non-trivial internal structure of $M$.
