The Dedekind modular law is a fundamental identity governing the interaction between the sum and intersection operations on submodules. It states that whenever $K \supset L$, the intersection of $K$ with the sum $L + N$ can be rewritten as the sum of $L$ and the intersection $K \cap N$. This identity holds in any module and is one of the key properties that makes the lattice of submodules modular.
