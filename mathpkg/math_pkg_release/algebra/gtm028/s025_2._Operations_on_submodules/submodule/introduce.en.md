A submodule of an $R$-module $M$ is a non-empty subset $N$ that is closed under subtraction and scalar multiplication. The subtraction condition makes $N$ a subgroup of $M$, while closure under scalar multiplication by elements of $R$ makes it an $R$-module in its own right. Submodules are the module-theoretic analogue of subgroups in group theory and ideals in ring theory; indeed, every submodule is itself an $R$-module.
