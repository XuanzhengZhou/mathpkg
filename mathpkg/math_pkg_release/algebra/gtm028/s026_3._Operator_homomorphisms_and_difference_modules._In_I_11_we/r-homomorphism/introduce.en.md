An $R$-homomorphism, also called an operator homomorphism, is a structure-preserving map between two $R$-modules over a ring $R$. It generalizes the notion of a group homomorphism by additionally requiring compatibility with the scalar multiplication by elements of $R$. Condition (1) ensures that $T$ respects addition, making it a group homomorphism. Condition (2) requires that $T$ commutes with the action of each ring element $a \in R$, expressed as $(ax)T = a(xT)$. When $R$ is a field, an $R$-homomorphism between vector spaces is precisely a linear transformation.
