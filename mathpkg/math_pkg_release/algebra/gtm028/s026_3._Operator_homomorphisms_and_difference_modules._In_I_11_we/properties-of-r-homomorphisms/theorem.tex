Let $T$ be an $R$-homomorphism of an $R$-module $M$ into an $R$-module $M'$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item If $L$ is an $R$-submodule of $M$, then the image $LT$ is an $R$-submodule of $M'$.
\item If $A$ is an ideal of $R$ and $L$ is an $R$-submodule of $M$, then $(AL)T = A(LT)$.
\item The kernel $N$ of $T$ is an $R$-submodule of $M$. Moreover, if $L'$ is an $R$-submodule of $M'$, then the inverse image $L'T^{-1}$ is an $R$-submodule of $M$.
\end{enumerate}
