The correspondence theorem (sometimes called the fourth isomorphism theorem or the lattice theorem) establishes a bijection between the lattice of submodules of a quotient module $M/N$ and the lattice of submodules of $M$ that contain $N$. This $(1,1)$ correspondence is inclusion-preserving: larger submodules of $M$ correspond to larger submodules of $M'$. Moreover, passing to the quotient by $N$ commutes with this correspondence, giving natural isomorphisms $L/N \cong L'$ and $(M/N)/(L/N) \cong M/L$ (the latter expressed as $M-L \cong M'-L'$ in the notation of Zariski--Samuel).
