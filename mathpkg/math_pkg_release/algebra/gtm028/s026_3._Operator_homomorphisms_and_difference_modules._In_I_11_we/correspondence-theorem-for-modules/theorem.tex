Let $T$ be an $R$-homomorphism of a module $M$ onto a module $M'$ with kernel $N$. Then there is a $(1,1)$, inclusion-preserving correspondence between submodules $L'$ of $M'$ and submodules $L$ of $M$ containing $N$, such that if $L$ and $L'$ correspond then
$$
LT = L', \qquad L'T^{-1} = L.
$$
When $L$ and $L'$ correspond, then $T$ induces an $R$-homomorphism of $L$ onto $L'$, the modules $L - N$ and $L'$ are isomorphic, and so are the modules $M - L$ and $M' - L'$.
