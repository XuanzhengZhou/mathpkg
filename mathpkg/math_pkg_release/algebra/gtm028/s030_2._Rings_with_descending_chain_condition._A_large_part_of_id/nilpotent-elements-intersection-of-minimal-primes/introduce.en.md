This important corollary to Theorem 10 characterizes the nilradical of a Noetherian ring as the intersection of its minimal prime ideals. The minimal prime ideals are precisely the isolated primes of the zero ideal. This generalizes the classical fact that in a commutative ring, the nilradical equals the intersection of all prime ideals, by showing that only the minimal primes are needed in the Noetherian case.
