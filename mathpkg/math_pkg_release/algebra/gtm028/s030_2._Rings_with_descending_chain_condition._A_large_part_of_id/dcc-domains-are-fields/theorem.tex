Let $R$ be an integral domain satisfying the descending chain condition. If $a \neq 0$ is an element of $R$, then the descending sequence $\{Ra^n\}$ of ideals stabilizes, so there exists an exponent $n$ such that $Ra^n = Ra^{n+1}$. Hence there exists $b \in R$ such that $a^n = ba^{n+1}$, i.e., $a^n(1 - ba) = 0$. Since $R$ is an integral domain and $a^n \neq 0$, we have $1 = ba$, so $a$ is a unit. Therefore $R$ is a field.
