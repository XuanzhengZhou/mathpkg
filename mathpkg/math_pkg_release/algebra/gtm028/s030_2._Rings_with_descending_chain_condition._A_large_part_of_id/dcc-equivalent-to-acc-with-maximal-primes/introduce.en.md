Theorem 2 gives a precise characterization of Artinian rings (rings with the descending chain condition): a ring with identity is Artinian if and only if it is Noetherian and every proper prime ideal is maximal. The forward direction (d.c.c. implies a.c.c. plus maximal primes) is the nontrivial part and relies on proving that the zero ideal is a product of maximal ideals. This theorem shows that for rings, unlike modules, the d.c.c. is strictly stronger than the a.c.c.
