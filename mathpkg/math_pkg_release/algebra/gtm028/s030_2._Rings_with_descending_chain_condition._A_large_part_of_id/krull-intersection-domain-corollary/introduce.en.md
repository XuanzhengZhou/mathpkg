In a Noetherian integral domain, the powers of any nonzero ideal form a strictly decreasing chain: all powers are distinct. If $\mathfrak{m}^p = \mathfrak{m}^q$ for some $p < q$, then multiplying by $\mathfrak{m}$ repeatedly would force the intersection $\bigcap_n \mathfrak{m}^n$ to equal $\mathfrak{m}^p$, which contradicts Krull's Intersection Theorem since in a domain the only zero-divisor is $0$ and $1 \notin \mathfrak{m}$.
