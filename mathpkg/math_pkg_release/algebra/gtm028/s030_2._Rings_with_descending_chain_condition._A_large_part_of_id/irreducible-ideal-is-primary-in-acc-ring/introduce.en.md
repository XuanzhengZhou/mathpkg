In a Noetherian ring, the notions of irreducible and primary ideals coincide: every irreducible ideal is primary. The proof is by contradiction: if an irreducible ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ is not primary, there exist elements $b, c \notin \mathfrak{a}$ with $bc \in \mathfrak{a}$ and no power of $b$ lies in $\mathfrak{a}$. Using the a.c.c. on the colon ideals $\mathfrak{a} : (b^s)$, one can decompose $\mathfrak{a}$ as $\mathfrak{a} = (\mathfrak{a} + Rb^n) \cap (\mathfrak{a} + Rc)$, contradicting irreducibility.
