The associated prime ideals of an ideal are classified into two types. Isolated prime ideals are those that are minimal among the associated primes (with respect to inclusion). Imbedded prime ideals are the non-minimal associated primes. This distinction is critical for the uniqueness theory: the isolated primes and their corresponding isolated primary components are uniquely determined by the ideal, while imbedded components are not.
