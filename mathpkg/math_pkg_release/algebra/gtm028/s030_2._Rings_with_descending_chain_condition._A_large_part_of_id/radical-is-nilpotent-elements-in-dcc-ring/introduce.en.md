In an Artinian ring (a ring with the descending chain condition), the Jacobson radical coincides with the nilradical. This lemma shows that every element of the intersection of all maximal ideals is nilpotent, and conversely every nilpotent element lies in every maximal ideal. The proof uses Lemma 2 to establish that $1-xa$ is a unit for $a$ in the radical, which forces $a$ to be nilpotent via the d.c.c. stabilization argument.
