Lemma 1 of Section 4 establishes that in any Noetherian ring, every ideal can be expressed as a finite intersection of irreducible ideals. An ideal is irreducible if it cannot be written as the intersection of two strictly larger ideals. The proof uses the a.c.c. to argue that the set of ideals failing this property, if nonempty, would have a maximal element, leading to a contradiction.
