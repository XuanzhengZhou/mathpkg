Let $\mathcal{F}$ be the set of ideals in $R$ which are not finite intersections of irreducible ideals. If $\mathcal{F}$ is not empty, by the a.c.c. it contains a maximal element $\mathfrak{a}$. Then $\mathfrak{a}$ is not irreducible, so it is an intersection $\mathfrak{b} \cap \mathfrak{c}$ of two ideals strictly containing $\mathfrak{a}$. By maximality of $\mathfrak{a}$ in $\mathcal{F}$, both $\mathfrak{b}$ and $\mathfrak{c}$ are finite intersections of irreducible ideals, hence so is $\mathfrak{a}$, a contradiction. Thus $\mathcal{F}$ is empty.
