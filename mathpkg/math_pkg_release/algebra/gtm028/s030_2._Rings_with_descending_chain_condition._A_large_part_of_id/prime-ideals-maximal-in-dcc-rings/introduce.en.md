In a ring satisfying the descending chain condition, the quotient by any proper prime ideal is an integral domain that also satisfies the d.c.c. Since the only d.c.c. integral domains are fields, the quotient is a field, which means the prime ideal must be maximal. This shows that the d.c.c. forces every nonzero prime ideal to be maximal, a property that holds in Artinian rings.
