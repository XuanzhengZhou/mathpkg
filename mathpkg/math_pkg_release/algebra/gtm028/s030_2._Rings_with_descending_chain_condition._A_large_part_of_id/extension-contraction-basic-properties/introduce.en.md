The operations of extension and contraction of ideals satisfy a rich set of algebraic properties that govern how ideals behave under ring homomorphisms. These properties establish a Galois connection between the lattices of ideals of $R$ and $S$. Key facts include: extension preserves sums, products, and commutes with radical formation (with an inclusion); contraction preserves intersections and commutes with radical formation exactly. The triple compositions $e c e$ and $c e c$ stabilize after the second step.
