Let $R$ be a noetherian ring and $\mathfrak{m}$ an ideal of $R$. In order that $\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty} \mathfrak{m}^n = (0)$, it is necessary and sufficient that no element of $1 - \mathfrak{m}$ be a zero-divisor in $R$. Equivalently, $\mathfrak{m} + \mathfrak{p}_i \neq R$ for every associated prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}_i$ of $(0)$.
