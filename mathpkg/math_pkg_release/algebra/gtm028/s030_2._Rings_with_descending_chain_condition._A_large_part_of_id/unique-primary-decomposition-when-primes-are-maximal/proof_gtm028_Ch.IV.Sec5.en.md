The first assertion: in a ring where every proper prime is maximal, imbedded components do not exist (since $(0)$ being prime can only be an associated prime of itself). Thus all associated primes are isolated, and by Theorem 8 all primary components are uniquely determined.

Now let $\mathfrak{a} = \bigcap_i \mathfrak{q}_i$ be the irredundant primary representation, $\mathfrak{p}_i = \sqrt{\mathfrak{q}_i}$. If some $\mathfrak{p}_i$ is not maximal, then $\mathfrak{p}_i = \mathfrak{q}_i = (0)$, so $\mathfrak{a} = (0)$ is prime (trivial). If each $\mathfrak{p}_i$ is maximal, then the $\mathfrak{q}_i$ are pairwise comaximal, so by Theorem 31 of III, §13, $\mathfrak{a} = \prod_i \mathfrak{q}_i$.

If $\mathfrak{a} = \prod_j \mathfrak{q}'_j$ is another representation as a product of primary ideals whose associated primes are distinct maximal ideals, then by the same theorem $\mathfrak{a} = \bigcap_j \mathfrak{q}'_j$. This primary representation is irredundant by Property A (since the $\mathfrak{p}_j$ are distinct and maximal). Hence the $\mathfrak{q}'_j$ coincide with the $\mathfrak{q}_i$ up to order.
