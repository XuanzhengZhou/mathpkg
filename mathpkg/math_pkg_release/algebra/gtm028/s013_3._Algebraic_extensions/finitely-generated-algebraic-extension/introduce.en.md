If finitely many algebraic elements are adjoined to a field, the resulting extension is again algebraic and has finite degree. The proof uses induction and the multiplicativity of the degree: each step $k(x_1, \ldots, x_{i-1}) \subset k(x_1, \ldots, x_i)$ is a simple algebraic extension, hence finite, and the product of all these finite degrees gives the total degree. This is a converse to Theorem 4 (finite implies algebraic).
