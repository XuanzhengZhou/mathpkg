Theorem 23, the rank-nullity theorem, states that for any linear transformation $T: V \to W$ from a finite-dimensional vector space $V$, the dimension of the domain equals the sum of the dimensions of the kernel and the image: $\dim(V) = \dim(\ker T) + \dim(\operatorname{im} T)$. The proof constructs a basis of $V$ by extending a basis of the kernel and shows that the images of the additional basis vectors form a basis of the image. This fundamental result quantifies the trade-off between injectivity and surjectivity for linear maps.
