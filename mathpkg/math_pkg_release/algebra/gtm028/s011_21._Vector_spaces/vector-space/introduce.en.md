A vector space over a field $F$ is an algebraic structure consisting of a commutative additive group $V$ together with a scalar multiplication operation that assigns to each pair $(a, x) \in F \times V$ a unique element $ax \in V$. The scalar multiplication must satisfy four natural axioms: distributivity of scalar multiplication over vector addition, distributivity of scalar addition over scalar multiplication, compatibility with field multiplication, and the identity element acting trivially. This definition unifies the geometric notion of vectors (as in three-dimensional Euclidean space) with the abstract algebraic treatment needed for modules and linear algebra over arbitrary fields.
