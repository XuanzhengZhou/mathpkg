Theorem 22 establishes the well-definedness of dimension for vector spaces: any two bases of the same vector space have the same cardinality. For a space with a finite basis of $n$ elements, the dimension is $n$, denoted $[V:F]$ or $\dim(V)$. Such spaces are called finite-dimensional. Vector spaces without any finite basis are called infinite-dimensional, with dimension $\infty$. This fundamental result makes the concept of dimension an invariant of the vector space structure.
