In Theorem 21, take $L$ to be the empty set (which is trivially free). Then there exists a subset $S'$ of $S$ such that $L \cup S' = S'$ is a basis of $V$ and $L \cap S' = \emptyset \cap S' = \emptyset$ is empty. Thus $B = S' \subseteq S$ is a basis of $V$.
