Theorem 21 is a fundamental basis extension result: given any free (linearly independent) subset $L$ and any finite generating set $S$ of a vector space $V$, one can extend $L$ to a basis $L \cup S'$ by adjoining a suitable subset $S' \subseteq S$ disjoint from $L$. The proof selects a maximal subset $S'$ of $S$ such that $L \cup S'$ remains free, then uses the exchange property $(S_5)$ to show that this maximal free set also generates $V$. The theorem generalizes to arbitrary generating sets via Zorn's lemma.
