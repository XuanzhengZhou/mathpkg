A field of algebraic functions of $r$ independent variables over $k$ is a finitely generated extension $K/k$ with transcendence degree exactly $r$. This notion generalizes the classical concept of an algebraic function field in $r$ variables: the extension is generated by finitely many elements, precisely $r$ of which are algebraically independent (forming a transcendence basis), while the remaining generators are algebraic over the purely transcendental subfield generated by those $r$ elements. This definition serves as the starting point for the study of separably generated extensions in this section.
