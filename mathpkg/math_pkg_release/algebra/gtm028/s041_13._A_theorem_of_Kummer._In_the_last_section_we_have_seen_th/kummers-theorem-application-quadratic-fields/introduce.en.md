Kummer's Theorem provides an explicit method to determine how rational primes decompose in quadratic fields. Depending on whether the squarefree integer m is congruent to 2, 3, or 1 modulo 4, different integral bases and minimal polynomials are used. A prime p is ramified if it divides the discriminant, split if the minimal polynomial factors into distinct linear factors modulo p, and inert if it remains irreducible modulo p.
