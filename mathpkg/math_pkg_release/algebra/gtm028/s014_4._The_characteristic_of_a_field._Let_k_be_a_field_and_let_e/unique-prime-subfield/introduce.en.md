Every field $k$ contains a uniquely determined prime subfield $\Delta$, namely the quotient field of the subring $E$ of integral multiples of the identity element $e$. Since any subfield of $k$ must contain $e$ and therefore all its integral multiples, every subfield contains $E$ and hence $\Delta$. Thus $\Delta$ is the intersection of all subfields of $k$, making it the smallest and the unique prime subfield.
