A **prime field** is a field that contains no proper subfields. Equivalently, it is a field that is minimal with respect to inclusion among all fields. Every field $k$ contains a unique prime subfield, which is the intersection of all subfields of $k$. The prime field of $k$ is isomorphic either to $\mathbb{Q}$ (when $k$ has characteristic zero) or to $\mathbb{F}_p$ (when $k$ has characteristic $p > 0$). Prime fields serve as the "atomic" building blocks from which all other fields are constructed via field extensions.
