The **Frobenius endomorphism** is the map $F: k \to k$ defined by $x \mapsto x^p$ on a field $k$ of characteristic $p > 0$. It is a field homomorphism: additivity follows from the freshman's dream $(x + y)^p = x^p + y^p$, while multiplicativity $(xy)^p = x^p y^p$ is immediate. Its image $k^p$, the set of all $p$-th powers, is a subfield of $k$ because it is closed under addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. The Frobenius endomorphism is a fundamental tool in the study of fields of positive characteristic, Galois theory, and algebraic geometry over finite fields.
