With every element $a$ of $R$ we can associate a mapping $T_a$ of $M$ into itself, defined by

$$xT_a = ax, \quad x \in M.$$

Equation (1) states that $T_a$ is an endomorphism of $M$, regarded as a group. It follows that if $0$ is the zero element of $M$, then $a0 = 0$ for all $a \in R$. Similarly, $0x$ equals the zero of $M$ for all $x \in M$, and $-(ax) = (-a)x = a(-x)$ for any $a \in R$ and $x \in M$.
