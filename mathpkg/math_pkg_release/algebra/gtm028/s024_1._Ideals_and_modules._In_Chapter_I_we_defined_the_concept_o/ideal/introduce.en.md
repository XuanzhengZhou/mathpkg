An ideal is a non-empty subset of a commutative ring that is closed under subtraction and under multiplication by arbitrary ring elements. Condition (a) makes it an additive subgroup, while condition (b) together with (a) makes it a subring — but not every subring is an ideal. For instance, the integers form a subring but not an ideal of the rational numbers.
