Each ring element $a$ acts on an $R$-module $M$ as a group endomorphism $T_a(x) = ax$. The distributivity axiom $a(x+y) = ax + ay$ is exactly the condition that $T_a$ is a homomorphism of the additive group of $M$. This perspective connects module theory to representation theory, where ring elements are viewed as operators.
