(1) Since $T_a$ is a group endomorphism, it maps the zero element to zero, so $a0 = 0$.

(2) Let $0_R$ be the zero of $R$ and $0_M$ the zero of $M$. We have $0_R x = (0_R + 0_R)x = 0_R x + 0_R x$. Subtracting $0_R x$ from both sides yields $0_R x = 0_M$.

(3) From the above, $0_M = 0_R x = (a + (-a))x = ax + (-a)x$, so $-(ax) = (-a)x$. Similarly, $a0_M = a(x + (-x)) = ax + a(-x)$, so $-(ax) = a(-x)$. Hence $-(ax) = (-a)x = a(-x)$.
