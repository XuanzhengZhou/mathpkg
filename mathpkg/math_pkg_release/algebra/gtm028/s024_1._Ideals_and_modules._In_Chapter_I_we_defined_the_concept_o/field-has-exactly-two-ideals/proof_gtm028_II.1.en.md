If $\mathfrak{A}$ is an ideal in $R$ and $\mathfrak{A} \neq (0)$, then $\mathfrak{A}$ contains an element $a \neq 0$. Since $R$ is a field, $a$ is a unit, so $a^{-1} \in R$. Then $\mathfrak{A}$ contains $a a^{-1} = 1$, hence $\mathfrak{A} = R$ by the proposition that an ideal containing a unit equals the whole ring.
