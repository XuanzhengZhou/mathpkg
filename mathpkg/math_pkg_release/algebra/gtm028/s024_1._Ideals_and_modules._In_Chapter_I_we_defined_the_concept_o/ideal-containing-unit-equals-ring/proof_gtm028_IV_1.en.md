Let $u \in \mathfrak{A}$ be a unit, so $u^{-1} \in R$. For any $b \in R$, we have $b = b \cdot 1 = b(u^{-1}u) = (bu^{-1})u \in \mathfrak{A}$, since $u \in \mathfrak{A}$ and $\mathfrak{A}$ is an ideal (closed under multiplication by arbitrary ring elements). Thus $R \subseteq \mathfrak{A}$, and since $\mathfrak{A} \subseteq R$ by definition, $\mathfrak{A} = R$.
