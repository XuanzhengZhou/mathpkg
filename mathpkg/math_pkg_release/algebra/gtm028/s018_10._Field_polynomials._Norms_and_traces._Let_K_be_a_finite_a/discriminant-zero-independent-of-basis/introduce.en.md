This is an immediate consequence of the change-of-basis formula $d' = d \cdot (\det A)^2$. Since $\det A \neq 0$ for any transition matrix between bases, the factor $(\det A)^2$ is nonzero, so $d' = 0$ if and only if $d = 0$. This makes "the discriminant is zero" a well-defined property of the extension itself.
