Let $K/k$ be a finite algebraic extension of degree $n$. The norm $N = N_{K/k}$ and trace $T = T_{K/k}$ satisfy:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $N(xy) = N(x) \cdot N(y)$ for all $x, y \in K$.
\item[(b)] If $x \in k$, then $N(x) = x^n$.
\item[(c)] $T(x + y) = T(x) + T(y)$ for all $x, y \in K$.
\item[(d)] $T(cx) = c\,T(x)$ for all $c \in k$, $x \in K$.
\item[(e)] If $x \in k$, then $T(x) = n x$.
\end{itemize}
