The discriminant of a basis is the determinant of the $n \times n$ matrix whose $(i,j)$-entry is the trace of the product $\omega_i \omega_j$. It measures how the trace bilinear form $(x,y) \mapsto T(xy)$ behaves on the given basis. The discriminant is a fundamental invariant that detects separability: the extension is separable if and only if the discriminant is nonzero.
