The field polynomial of an element $x$ depends on which extension field is used to compute it. When the ambient field is enlarged from $K$ to $\Delta$, the new field polynomial is simply the $m$-th power of the original one, where $m = [\Delta:K]$. This follows from the block-diagonal structure of the representing matrix with respect to a product basis.
