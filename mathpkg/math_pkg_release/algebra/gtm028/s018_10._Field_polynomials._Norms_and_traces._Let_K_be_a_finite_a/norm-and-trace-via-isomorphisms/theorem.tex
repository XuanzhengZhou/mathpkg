Let $K/k$ be a finite algebraic extension. Let $m = [K:k]$, $m_0 = [K:k]_s$ (the separable degree), and $p^f = [K:k]_i$ (the inseparable degree). Let $K^\star$ be the least normal extension of $k$ containing $K$, and let
$$\{\varphi_i \mid i = 1, 2, \dots, m_0\}$$
be the set of $k$-isomorphisms of $K$ into $K^\star$. Then for any $x \in K$:
$$N_{K/k}(x) = \left( \prod_{i=1}^{m_0} x^{\varphi_i} \right)^{p^f},$$
$$T_{K/k}(x) = p^f \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{m_0} x^{\varphi_i}.$$
If $x$ has $n_0$ distinct conjugates in $K^\star$, each conjugate appears exactly $m_0/n_0$ times among the images $x^{\varphi_i}$, yielding the alternative expressions:
$$N_{K/k}(x) = \left( \prod_{j=1}^{n_0} x_j \right)^{p^f \cdot m_0/n_0},$$
$$T_{K/k}(x) = p^f \cdot \frac{m_0}{n_0} \cdot \sum_{j=1}^{n_0} x_j.$$
