Let $K/k$ be a finite algebraic extension and let $x \in K$. Let $f(X)$ be the field polynomial of $x$ over $k$ and let $g(X)$ be the minimal polynomial of $x$ over $k$. Then $f(X)$ is a power of $g(X)$. Moreover, $f(X) = g(X)$ if and only if $x$ is a primitive element of $K$ over $k$, i.e., $K = k(x)$.
