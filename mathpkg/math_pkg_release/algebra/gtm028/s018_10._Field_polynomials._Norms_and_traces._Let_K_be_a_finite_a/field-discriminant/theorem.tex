The \textbf{field discriminant} of $K/k$ is the discriminant $d(\omega_1, \dots, \omega_n)$ of any basis of $K/k$. By the change-of-basis formula, the field discriminant is determined only up to multiplication by the square of an arbitrary nonzero element of $k$. The statement "the field discriminant of $K/k$ is zero (resp. is not zero)" is therefore independent of the choice of basis.
