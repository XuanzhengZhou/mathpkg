The field discriminant is a well-defined invariant of the extension $K/k$ up to a square factor in $k^\times$. Its vanishing characterizes inseparable extensions (Theorem 21), making it a critical tool for detecting separability. When $K/k$ is separable, the field discriminant is nonzero and carries arithmetic information about the extension.
