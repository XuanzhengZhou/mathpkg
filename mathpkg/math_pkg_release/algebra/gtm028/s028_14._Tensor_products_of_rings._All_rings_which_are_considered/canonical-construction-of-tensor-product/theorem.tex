Let $A$ and $B$ be algebras over a field $k$. Consider the set-product $A \times B$ and let $M$ be the vector space over $k$ of all formal finite linear combinations $\sum_i c_i(a_i, b_i)$ with $c_i \in k$, $a_i \in A$, $b_i \in B$, where $A \times B$ is taken as a basis. Let $\mathfrak{R}$ be the ideal of the ring $M$ (with multiplication defined by $(a,b)(a',b') = (aa', bb')$) generated by all elements of the form

$$(a + a', b) - (a, b) - (a', b), \quad (a, b + b') - (a, b) - (a, b'), \quad (ca, b) - c(a, b), \quad (a, cb) - c(a, b),$$

for $a, a' \in A$, $b, b' \in B$, $c \in k$. Then the quotient ring $T = M/\mathfrak{R}$, together with the maps $g: a \mapsto a \otimes 1$ and $h: b \mapsto 1 \otimes b$ (where $a \otimes b$ denotes the coset of $(a, b)$ modulo $\mathfrak{R}$), is a tensor product of $A$ and $B$ over $k$. This ring is denoted by $A \otimes_k B$.
