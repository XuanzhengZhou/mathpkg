Let $R$ be an integrally closed ring, $K$ its quotient field, $K'$ a finite algebraic separable extension of $K$, and $R'$ the integral closure of $R$ in $K'$. Let $\mathfrak{p}$ be a proper prime ideal of $R$, let $\mathfrak{P}_i$ ($i = 1, \ldots, g$) be the prime ideals of $R'$ lying over $\mathfrak{p}$, and let $e_i = e(\mathfrak{P}_i)$, $f_i = f(\mathfrak{P}_i)$ be the ramification indices and relative degrees.

Let $h$ be the natural homomorphism $R \to R/\mathfrak{p}$, extended to a homomorphism of $K$ into the algebraic closure of $R/\mathfrak{p}$. For each $i$, let $h_i$ be the natural homomorphism $R' \to R'/\mathfrak{P}_i$, and let $k_i = R'/\mathfrak{P}_i$, $k = R/\mathfrak{p}$.

Then for any $x \in R'$:

$$h(T_{K'/K}(x)) = \sum_{i=1}^{g} e_i \cdot T_{k_i/k}(h_i(x)),$$

$$h(N_{K'/K}(x)) = \prod_{i=1}^{g} \bigl(N_{k_i/k}(h_i(x))\bigr)^{e_i}.$$
