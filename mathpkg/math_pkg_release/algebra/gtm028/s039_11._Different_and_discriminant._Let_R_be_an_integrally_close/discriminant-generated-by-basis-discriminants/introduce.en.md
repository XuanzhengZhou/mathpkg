The discriminant ideal of an extension of Dedekind domains is generated by the discriminants of all $K$-bases of the extension field that lie in the integral closure. When the integral closure is a free module over the base ring, the discriminant of any basis generates the discriminant ideal. This theorem connects the classical notion of field discriminant from Galois theory with the ideal-theoretic discriminant of Dedekind domains.
