The assertion is equivalent to $\mathfrak{D}_{R'_M/R_M} = \mathfrak{D}_{R'/R} R'_M$. Since the complementary module is determined by local data, we have $\mathcal{C}_{R'_M/R_M} = \mathcal{C}_{R'/R} R'_M$ (the complementary module of the localization equals the localization of the complementary module).

Taking inverses, $\mathfrak{D}_{R'_M/R_M} = (\mathcal{C}_{R'_M/R_M})^{-1} = (\mathcal{C}_{R'/R} R'_M)^{-1} = \mathfrak{D}_{R'/R} R'_M$, where the last equality uses that for a Dedekind domain, the inverse commutes with localization. Therefore the exponent of $\mathfrak{P}R'_M$ in the different $\mathfrak{D}_{R'_M/R_M}$ equals the exponent of $\mathfrak{P}$ in $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R}$.
