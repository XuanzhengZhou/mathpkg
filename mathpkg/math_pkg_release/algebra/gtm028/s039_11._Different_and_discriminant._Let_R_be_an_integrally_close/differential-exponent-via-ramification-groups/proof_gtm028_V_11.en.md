Let $u \in \mathfrak{p} \setminus \mathfrak{p}^2$ be a uniformizer for $R_\mathfrak{p}$. The complementary module is computed locally. Using the trace formula and the fact that $T_{K'/K}(R') \subset R$ for integrally closed $R$, one shows that the element $u^{1-e(\mathfrak{P})}$ (an appropriate generator) has trace not in $R$ when both conditions (a) and (b) hold, establishing $m(\mathfrak{P}) = e(\mathfrak{P}) - 1$.

If either condition fails, then every element of $\mathfrak{P}^{1-e(\mathfrak{P})}$ has trace in $R$, giving $m(\mathfrak{P}) \geq e(\mathfrak{P})$. The detailed computation uses the trace formula $T_{K'/K}(y) \equiv e(\mathfrak{P}) \cdot T_{(R'/\mathfrak{P})/(R/\mathfrak{p})}(\bar{y}) \pmod{\mathfrak{p}}$ for $y \in \mathfrak{P}^{e(\mathfrak{P})-1}$.
