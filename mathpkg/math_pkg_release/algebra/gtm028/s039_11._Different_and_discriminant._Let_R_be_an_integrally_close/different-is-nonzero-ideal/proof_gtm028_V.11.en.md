By Theorem 26, $\mathcal{C}$ is a fractionary ideal of $R'$. Its inverse $\mathfrak{D}_{R'/R} = \mathcal{C}^{-1} = \{x \in K' : x\mathcal{C} \subset R'\}$ is therefore also a fractionary ideal. Since $\mathcal{C} \supset R'$, we have $\mathfrak{D} = \mathcal{C}^{-1} \subset (R')^{-1}$. But $1 \in \mathfrak{D} \cdot \mathcal{C} \subset R'$ means $\mathfrak{D}$ is an integral ideal (contained in $R'$).

That $\mathfrak{D} \neq (0)$ follows from the separability of $K'/K$: the trace bilinear form is non-degenerate, so $\mathcal{C}$ is a proper fractionary ideal, and thus $\mathfrak{D}$ is nonzero.
