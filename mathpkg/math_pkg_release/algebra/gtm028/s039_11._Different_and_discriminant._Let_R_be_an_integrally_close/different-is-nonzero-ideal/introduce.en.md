Since the complementary module is a fractionary ideal, its inverse is also a fractionary ideal. The fact that the complement contains R' implies that the different is an integral ideal (contained in R'). The non-vanishing of the different in the separable case is crucial: it means the different properly measures ramification, and its prime factorization encodes the ramified primes of the extension.
