Let $R$ be a commutative ring. The \textbf{maximum condition} for ideals of $R$ is the property that in every non-empty family of ideals of $R$, there exists a maximal element, that is, an ideal not contained in any other ideal of the family.

A consequence of the maximum condition is that every ideal $\mathfrak{A} \neq R$ is contained in a maximal ideal of $R$, as can be seen by considering the family of all ideals $\neq R$ that contain $\mathfrak{A}$.
