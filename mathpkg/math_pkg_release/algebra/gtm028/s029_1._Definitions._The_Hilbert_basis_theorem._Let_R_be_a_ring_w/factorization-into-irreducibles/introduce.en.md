In a noetherian integral domain, every non-zero non-unit element admits a factorization into irreducible elements. The proof applies the ascending chain condition twice: first, to show that every non-unit possesses an irreducible divisor (by constructing a strictly ascending chain of principal ideals that must terminate); second, to iteratively factor out irreducible divisors until the process stops. This property is one of the three basic consequences of the noetherian condition listed at the start of Chapter IV in Zariski-Samuel.
