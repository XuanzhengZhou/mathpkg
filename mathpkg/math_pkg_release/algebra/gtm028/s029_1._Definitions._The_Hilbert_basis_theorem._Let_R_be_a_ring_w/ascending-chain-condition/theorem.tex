Let $R$ be a commutative ring. The \textbf{ascending chain condition} (a.c.c.) for ideals of $R$ is the property that every strictly ascending chain
$$
\mathfrak{A}_1 \subsetneq \mathfrak{A}_2 \subsetneq \mathfrak{A}_3 \subsetneq \cdots
$$
of ideals of $R$ is finite. Equivalently, given any ascending chain
$$
\mathfrak{A}_1 \subseteq \mathfrak{A}_2 \subseteq \mathfrak{A}_3 \subseteq \cdots
$$
of ideals of $R$, there exists an integer $n$ such that $\mathfrak{A}_n = \mathfrak{A}_{n+1} = \mathfrak{A}_{n+2} = \cdots$.
