A noetherian ring is a commutative ring satisfying any of three equivalent finiteness conditions on its ideals: the ascending chain condition, the maximum condition, or the finite basis condition. The concept is named after Emmy Noether and is fundamental throughout commutative algebra and algebraic geometry. The equivalence of the three conditions follows from the general theory of modules (theorems about submodules of a module over itself). The noetherian property is preserved under many natural operations, most notably under adjoining polynomial indeterminates (the Hilbert Basis Theorem).
