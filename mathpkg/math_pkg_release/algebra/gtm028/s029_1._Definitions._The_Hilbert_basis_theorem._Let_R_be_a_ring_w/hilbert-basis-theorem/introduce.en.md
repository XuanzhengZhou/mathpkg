The Hilbert Basis Theorem is a fundamental result in commutative algebra, stating that the noetherian property is preserved under adjoining finitely many indeterminates. Hilbert originally proved it for the case where $R$ is a field or the ring of integers; the general version for arbitrary noetherian rings is presented here. The theorem is proved by induction on the number of indeterminates, reducing to the case $R[x]$. Two proofs are given: one using the maximum condition together with the lemma on leading-coefficient ideals $L_i(\mathfrak{A})$, and another using the finite basis condition directly.
