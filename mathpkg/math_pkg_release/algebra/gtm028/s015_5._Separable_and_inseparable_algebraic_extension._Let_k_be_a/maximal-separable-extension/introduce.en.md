Within any field extension $K/k$, the elements that are separable algebraic over $k$ form an intermediate field $k_0$, called the maximal separable extension of $k$ in $K$. It sits between $k$ and the algebraic closure $\bar{k}$ of $k$ in $K$. When $K$ is algebraically closed, $k_0$ is precisely the separable closure of $k$. This notion is the key building block for decomposing any algebraic extension into a separable extension followed by a purely inseparable one.
