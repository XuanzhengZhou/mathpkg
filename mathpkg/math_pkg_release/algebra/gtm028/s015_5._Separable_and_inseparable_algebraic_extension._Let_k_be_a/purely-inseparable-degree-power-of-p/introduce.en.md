For a purely inseparable element $x$ over a field of characteristic $p$, the degree $[k(x):k]$ is always a power of $p$. Specifically, if $e$ is minimal with $x^{p^e} \in k$, then the minimal polynomial is $X^{p^e} - a$ where $a = x^{p^e}$, and the degree is exactly $p^e$. This corollary follows directly from Theorem 7 and shows that purely inseparable extensions have a very rigid degree structure — they are built by successively adjoining $p$-th roots.
