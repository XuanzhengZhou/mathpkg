This criterion provides the fundamental geometric interpretation of separability: an algebraic element is separable over $k$ precisely when it is a simple root of its minimal polynomial. Consequently, a separable irreducible polynomial has as many distinct roots as its degree, while an inseparable one has fewer distinct roots. This result connects the algebraic definition (non-vanishing derivative) with the splitting behavior of polynomials over extension fields.
