Let $K$ be an extension field of $k$ of characteristic $p \neq 0$. An algebraic element $x \in K$ over $k$ is called **purely inseparable** over $k$ if its minimal polynomial over $k$ is of the form $X^{p^e} - a$ for some integer $e \geq 0$ and some $a \in k$. An algebraic extension $K/k$ is called **purely inseparable** if every element of $K$ is purely inseparable over $k$.
