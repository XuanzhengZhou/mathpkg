This theorem provides a purely algebraic criterion for a finite extension to be separable in characteristic $p$: the extension is separable precisely when the compositum $kK^p$ (the subfield generated by $k$ and all $p$-th powers of elements of $K$) equals the whole field $K$. Geometrically, this means every element of $K$ can be expressed as a $k$-linear combination of $p$-th powers. The proof uses the fact that every element of $K$ is purely inseparable over $kK^p$, together with Lemmas 1 and 2, to show that separability forces $kK^p = K$.
