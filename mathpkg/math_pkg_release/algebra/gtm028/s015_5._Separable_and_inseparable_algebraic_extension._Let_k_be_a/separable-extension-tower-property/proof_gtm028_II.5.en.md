source_section: "5. Separable and inseparable algebraic extension"

Let $x$ be any element of $K$ and let $f(X)$ be the minimal polynomial of $x$ in $k[X]$. Since $x$ is separable over $k$, $x$ is a simple root of $f(X)$, by Corollary 2. Since $f(X)$ is also a polynomial in $L[X]$ (as $k \subset L$), it follows again from Corollary 2 that $x$ is also separable over $L$. Hence every element of $K$ is separable over $L$, so $K$ is a separable algebraic extension of $L$.
