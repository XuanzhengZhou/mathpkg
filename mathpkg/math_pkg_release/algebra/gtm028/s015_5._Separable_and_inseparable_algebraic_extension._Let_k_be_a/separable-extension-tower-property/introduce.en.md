If a field extension $K/k$ is separable, then for any intermediate field $L$, the extension $K/L$ is also separable. This "tower property" follows from the fact that the minimal polynomial of any element $x \in K$ over $k$ is also a polynomial over $L$, and separability over $k$ (detected by the non-vanishing of the derivative of the minimal polynomial over $k$) implies separability over $L$. This lemma is essential for proving that separable extensions form a distinguished class of field extensions.
