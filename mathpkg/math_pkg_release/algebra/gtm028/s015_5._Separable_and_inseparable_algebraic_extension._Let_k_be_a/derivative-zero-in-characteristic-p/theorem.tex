Let $k$ be a field and $f(X) = a_0 X^n + a_1 X^{n-1} + \cdots + a_n \in k[X]$, $a_0 \neq 0$.

If $\operatorname{char}(k) = 0$, then $f'(X) \neq 0$ whenever $n > 0$.

If $\operatorname{char}(k) = p \neq 0$, then $f'(X) = 0$ if and only if $n$ is divisible by $p$ and all those coefficients $a_i$ of $f(X)$ are zero for which $n-i$ is not divisible by $p$. Equivalently, $f'(X) = 0$ if and only if $f(X) \in k[X^p]$, i.e., $f(X)$ is a polynomial in $X^p$ with coefficients in $k$.
