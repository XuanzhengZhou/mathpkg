An irreducible polynomial $f(X) \in k[X]$ is **inseparable** if $f'(X) = 0$.

If $\operatorname{char}(k) = p \neq 0$ and $f(X)$ is an inseparable irreducible polynomial, then there exists a unique integer $e \geq 1$ such that $f(X) \in k[X^{p^e}]$ but $f(X) \notin k[X^{p^{e+1}}]$, and $f(X) = f_0(X^{p^e})$ where $f_0(X)$ is an irreducible separable polynomial in $k[X]$. The integer $e$ is called the **exponent of inseparability** of $f(X)$, and the degree of $f_0(X)$ is called the **reduced degree** of $f(X)$.
