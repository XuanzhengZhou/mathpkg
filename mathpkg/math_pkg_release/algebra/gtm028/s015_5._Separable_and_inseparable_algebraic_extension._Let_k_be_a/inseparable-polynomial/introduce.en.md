An inseparable polynomial is one whose formal derivative vanishes identically. Over fields of characteristic $p > 0$, such polynomials exist and are exactly those lying in $k[X^p]$. Any irreducible inseparable polynomial $f(X)$ can be uniquely written as $f(X) = f_0(X^{p^e})$ where $f_0$ is separable. The exponent $e$ measures "how inseparable" the polynomial is, and the reduced degree $\deg(f_0)$ counts the number of distinct roots of $f(X)$ in any splitting field. Every root of $f(X)$ then appears with multiplicity $p^e$.
