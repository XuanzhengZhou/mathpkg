source_section: "5. Separable and inseparable algebraic extension"
---

Let $x$ be any element of $\bar{k}$ and let $f(X)$ be the minimal polynomial of $x$ over $k$. Let $p^e$ be the degree of inseparability of $f(X)$, so that $e = 0$ if and only if $x \in k_0$. Then the element $x^{p^e}$ has a separable minimal polynomial over $k$ (the reduced polynomial $f_0(X)$), so $x^{p^e} \in k_0$. Consequently, $x$ is a root of the polynomial $X^{p^e} - x^{p^e} \in k_0[X]$, which shows that $x$ is purely inseparable over $k_0$. This holds for any element $x$ of $\bar{k}$; thus $\bar{k}$ is a purely inseparable extension of $k_0$. It follows that any algebraic extension $k \to \bar{k}$ decomposes as the separable extension $k \to k_0$ followed by the purely inseparable extension $k_0 \to \bar{k}$.
