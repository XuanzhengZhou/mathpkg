Consider the equation $ax = b$. Multiplication on the left by $a^{-1}$ yields $a^{-1}(ax) = a^{-1}b$. By associativity, $(a^{-1}a)x = a^{-1}b$, so $ex = a^{-1}b$, hence $x = a^{-1}b$. Thus $a^{-1}b$ is the only possible solution. Direct substitution shows that $a(a^{-1}b) = (aa^{-1})b = eb = b$, so $a^{-1}b$ is indeed a solution. Similarly, for the equation $xa = b$, multiplication on the right by $a^{-1}$ gives $(xa)a^{-1} = ba^{-1}$, so $x(aa^{-1}) = ba^{-1}$, hence $x = ba^{-1}$ is the unique solution, and substitution confirms it.
