Let $a$ be an element of a group $G$ of finite order $m$. Then:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] $m$ is the smallest positive integer such that $a^m = 1$.
\item[(2)] The cyclic subgroup generated by $a$ consists precisely of the $m$ distinct elements $1, a, a^2, \ldots, a^{m-1}$.
\item[(3)] For any integer $n$, $a^n = 1$ if and only if $m \mid n$.
\end{enumerate}
