A group is a fundamental algebraic structure consisting of a set equipped with an associative binary operation, containing an identity element, and where every element has an inverse. The three group axioms — non-emptiness, associativity, and existence of identity and inverses — together imply many important structural properties such as the uniqueness of identity and inverses, the cancellation law, and the unique solvability of equations $ax = b$ and $xa = b$. Groups serve as the foundational algebraic object in commutative algebra, number theory, geometry, and nearly every branch of mathematics.
