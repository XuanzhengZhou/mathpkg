A ring $A$ is integrally closed in an overring $B$ when no element of $B \setminus A$ is integral over $A$. In other words, $A$ is equal to its own integral closure in $B$. This property is central to the definition of normal domains: an integral domain is called normal if it is integrally closed in its field of fractions. Dedekind domains, unique factorization domains, and regular local rings are all examples of integrally closed rings.
