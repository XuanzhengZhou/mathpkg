This lemma is the core technical result used to prove that condition (c''') implies integral dependence. Given a finite $A$-module $M$ and an element $x$ such that $xM \subset M\mathfrak{q}$, the lemma constructs an explicit monic polynomial with coefficients in the ideal $\mathfrak{q}$ that annihilates $x$. The proof uses linear algebra over commutative rings: expressing the action of $x$ on generators $m_i$ of $M$ gives a system of linear equations, and multiplying by the adjugate matrix yields $d M = 0$, whence the determinant $d$ vanishes by the faithfulness condition (2').
