An element $x$ in an overring $B$ of $A$ is integral over $A$ if and only if the ring $A[x]$ is a finite $A$-module. More generally, the integral dependence condition (c) is equivalent to $A[x]$ being contained in some finite $A$-submodule of $B$ (condition c''), and also to the existence of a faithful $A[x]$-module $M$ that is finite over $A$ and closed under multiplication by $x$ (condition c'''). The cyclic proof uses a determinant argument (the Cayley-Hamilton trick) for the implication $(c''') \Rightarrow (c)$.
