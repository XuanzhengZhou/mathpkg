A field is a commutative ring in which every nonzero element is a unit and which contains at least two distinct elements (equivalently, $0 \neq 1$). The three field axioms capture precisely the conditions needed to make division by any nonzero element always possible. Fields form the natural setting for studying solutions of linear equations $ax = b$ and are the foundation of Galois theory, algebraic number theory, and algebraic geometry.
