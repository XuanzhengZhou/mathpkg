Since every nonzero element of a field is a unit, no product of nonzero elements can be zero, so a field contains no zero divisors. Combined with the existence of a multiplicative identity (axiom $F_2$), this implies that every field is an integral domain. Thus the class of fields is contained in the class of integral domains, which in turn is contained in the class of commutative rings.
