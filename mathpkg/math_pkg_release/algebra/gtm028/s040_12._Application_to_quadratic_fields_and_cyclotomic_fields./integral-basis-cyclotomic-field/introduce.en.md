This proposition collects the fundamental arithmetic data of the $p$-th cyclotomic field: its degree $p-1$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, its discriminant $p^{p-2}$, and its integral basis $\{1, z, \ldots, z^{p-2}\}$. The proof uses the factorization $p = (1-z)(1-z^2)\cdots(1-z^{p-1})$ and properties of the cyclotomic units $(1-z^r)/(1-z)$. The result shows that the only ramified prime in $\mathbb{Q}(z)$ is $p$ itself, and that the prime $p$ is totally ramified.
