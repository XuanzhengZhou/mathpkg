The Galois group of $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_p)/\mathbb{Q}$ is cyclic of order $p-1$. It contains a unique subgroup of index $2$, corresponding to a unique quadratic subfield $K$. By the discriminant tower formula, the discriminant of $K$ divides $p^{p-2}$. The discriminant formulas for quadratic fields force $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{p})$ if $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ and $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-p})$ if $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$, in both cases with discriminant $p$.
