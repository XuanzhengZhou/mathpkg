Since the Galois group of $\mathbb{Q}(z)/\mathbb{Q}$ is cyclic of even order $p-1$, there exists exactly one quadratic subfield $K$ within the $p$-th cyclotomic field. This subfield is explicitly identified as $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{p})$ or $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-p})$ depending on $p \bmod 4$. This result is the crucial link that allows the quadratic reciprocity law to be deduced from the decomposition behavior of primes in cyclotomic fields, connecting the Legendre symbol to Galois theory.
