By Euler's criterion, $\left(\frac{s}{p}\right) \equiv s^{(p-1)/2} \pmod{p}$ and similarly for $t$ and $st$. Since $(st)^{(p-1)/2} = s^{(p-1)/2} t^{(p-1)/2}$, we obtain $\left(\frac{st}{p}\right) \equiv \left(\frac{s}{p}\right)\left(\frac{t}{p}\right) \pmod{p}$. Both sides are $\pm 1$, so equality holds.
