The quadratic reciprocity law, first proved by Gauss, is one of the most celebrated theorems in number theory. It relates the Legendre symbols $\left(\frac{p}{q}\right)$ and $\left(\frac{q}{p}\right)$ for distinct odd primes $p$ and $q$, with a sign factor depending on the congruence classes of $p$ and $q$ modulo 4. The proof presented in Zariski-Samuel uses algebraic number theory: the unique quadratic subfield of the $p$-th cyclotomic field and the decomposition of $q$ in that cyclotomic field together yield the reciprocity relation.
