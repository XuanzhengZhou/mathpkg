For a prime $q \neq p$, let $f$ be the smallest positive integer with $q^f \equiv 1 \pmod{p}$. Then $q$ decomposes into $g = (p-1)/f$ distinct prime ideals in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_p)$, each with relative degree $f$. This follows because $R'/\mathfrak{Q} \cong \mathbb{F}_{q^f}$ contains primitive $p$-th roots of unity, and the Galois group acts transitively on the $g$ prime factors.
