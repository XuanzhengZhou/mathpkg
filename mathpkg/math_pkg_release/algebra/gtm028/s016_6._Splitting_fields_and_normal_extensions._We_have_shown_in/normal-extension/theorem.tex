An extension $K$ of $k$ is said to be \text{normal over $k$}, or a \text{normal extension of $k$}, if $K$ is an algebraic extension of $k$ and if every irreducible polynomial $f(X)$ in $k[X]$ which has a root in $K$ factors completely in $K[X]$ into linear factors, or---what is the same thing---if $K$ contains then a splitting field of $f(X)$ over $k$.

Equivalently: $K$ is a normal extension of $k$ if $K$ is an algebraic extension of $k$ and contains with every element $x$ also all the conjugates of $x$ over $k$.
