Let $G$ be the group of all $k$-automorphisms of $K$ and let $G(L)$ (respectively, $G(\Delta)$) be the subgroup of $G$ consisting of those automorphisms of $K$ which leave fixed every element of $L$ (respectively, of $\Delta$). It is clear that $G(\Delta)$ is a subgroup of $G(L)$. Let

$$G(L) = \bigcup_{i=1}^{n} G(\Delta)\varphi_i, \qquad
G = \bigcup_{j=1}^{m} G(L)\psi_j,$$

be the decomposition of $G(L)$ into right $G(\Delta)$-cosets and that of $G$ into right $G(L)$-cosets. Then the $mn$ $G(\Delta)$-cosets are distinct and

$$G = \bigcup_{i,j} G(\Delta)\varphi_i\psi_j$$

is the decomposition of $G$ into right $G(\Delta)$-cosets.

It is clear that the $m$ automorphisms $\psi_j$ have distinct restrictions to $L$ and that the restriction of any element $\psi$ of $G$ to $L$ coincides with the restriction of one of the $\psi_j$. Since by Corollary 4 every $k$-isomorphism of $L$ into $K$ is the restriction of some element of $G$, the $m$ restrictions $\psi_j|_L$ give all $k$-isomorphisms of $L$ into $K$. Each such $k$-isomorphism has exactly $n$ extensions to $\Delta$, corresponding to the $n$ $G(\Delta)$-cosets within each $G(L)$-coset.
