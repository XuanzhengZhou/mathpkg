For an irreducible inseparable polynomial over a field of characteristic $p$, the splitting behavior is highly structured: each root appears with the same multiplicity, and that multiplicity is a power of $p$. Specifically, if the polynomial has exponent of inseparability $e$, then each linear factor in its complete factorization over a splitting field occurs exactly $p^e$ times. This follows from writing the polynomial as a separable polynomial in $X^{p^e}$ and using the fact that the separable reduction has simple roots.
