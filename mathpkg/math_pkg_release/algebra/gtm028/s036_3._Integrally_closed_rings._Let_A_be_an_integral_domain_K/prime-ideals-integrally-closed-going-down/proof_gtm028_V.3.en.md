Let $S = (A \setminus \mathfrak{q})(A' \setminus \mathfrak{q}')$ be the multiplicatively closed set generated by the complements of the two prime ideals. Consider the localization $A'_S$. It suffices to show that $\mathfrak{p} A'_S \cap A = \mathfrak{p}$, for then any prime ideal in $A'_S$ minimal over $\mathfrak{p} A'_S$ will contract to $\mathfrak{p}$ in $A$, and its contraction to $A'$ yields the desired $\mathfrak{p}'$ (contained in $\mathfrak{q}'$ since $S \cap \mathfrak{q}' = \emptyset$).

Suppose, for contradiction, that there exists $x \in \mathfrak{p} A'_S \cap A$ with $x \notin \mathfrak{p}$. Write

$$x = a b', \quad a \in A \setminus \mathfrak{q}, \; b' \in A' \setminus \mathfrak{q}'.$$

Consider the integral dependence equation for $b'$ over $A$, and let $m(X) = X^r + e_{r-1}X^{r-1} + \cdots + e_1 X + e_0 \in A[X]$ be the minimal polynomial of $b'$ over the quotient field $K$ of $A$. By the generalization of Theorem 4 (since $A$ is integrally closed), $m(X) \in A[X]$.

Since $x = a b'$ satisfies an integral dependence equation $f(X) = X^n + q_{n-1}X^{n-1} + \cdots + q_0 = 0$ with $q_i \in \mathfrak{q}$ (because $x \in \mathfrak{p} \subset \mathfrak{q}$ and belongs to the contraction of $\mathfrak{q}$-primary elements in the localization), we factor $f(X) = g(X)h(X)$ where $g(X)$ is the minimal polynomial of $x$ over $K$. Theorem 5 implies $g(X) \in A[X]$.

Reducing modulo $\mathfrak{q}$, we have $\bar{f}(X) = X^n$. Since $\bar{f} = \bar{g}\bar{h}$ and $A/\mathfrak{q}$ is an integral domain, we conclude $\bar{g}(X) = X^r$ and $\bar{h}(X) = X^{n-r}$ (by inspection of lowest degree terms). Hence

$$g(X) = X^r + d_{r-1}X^{r-1} + \cdots + d_1 X + d_0, \quad d_i \in \mathfrak{q}.$$

On the other hand, the coefficients $d_i$ are related to the $e_j$ by $d_i = e_i a^{r-i}$ (since $x = a b'$). In particular, $e_0 a^r = d_0 \in \mathfrak{q}$. Since $a \notin \mathfrak{q}$, we get $e_0 \in \mathfrak{q}$.

Now $m(b') = 0$ implies $b'^r + e_{r-1}b'^{r-1} + \cdots + e_0 = 0$. Hence $b'^r \in \mathfrak{q} A' + \mathfrak{q}'$, which is contained in the prime ideal $\mathfrak{q}'$ (since $\mathfrak{q} A' \subset \mathfrak{q}'$ as $\mathfrak{q}'$ lies over $\mathfrak{q}$). Thus $b' \in \mathfrak{q}'$, contradicting $b' \notin \mathfrak{q}'$.

Therefore $\mathfrak{p} A'_S \cap A = \mathfrak{p}$, and a prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}'$ of $A'$ lying over $\mathfrak{p}$ and contained in $\mathfrak{q}'$ exists.
