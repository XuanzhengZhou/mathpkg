Let $f(X) \in A[X]$ be a monic polynomial such that $f(x) = 0$; such a polynomial exists because $x$ is integral over $A$. Let $m(X) \in K[X]$ be the minimal polynomial of $x$ over $K$. Since $m(X)$ divides $f(X)$ in $K[X]$, we may write

$$f(X) = m(X) h(X)$$

where $h(X)$ is a monic polynomial in $K[X]$. Both $m(X)$ and $h(X)$ are monic. Since $A$ is integrally closed, Theorem 5 (the Gauss-type lemma for integrally closed domains) implies that both $m(X)$ and $h(X)$ have all their coefficients in $A$. In particular, $m(X) \in A[X]$. Thus $m(x) = 0$ is an equation of integral dependence for $x$ over $A$.
