When A is integrally closed, any element x that is integral over A (in some overring of the quotient field K) has a minimal polynomial over K whose coefficients already lie in A. This is a strengthening of Theorem 4: not only are the coefficients integral over A, but integral closure forces them into A itself. Thus the minimal polynomial provides a canonical integral dependence equation with coefficients in the base ring.
