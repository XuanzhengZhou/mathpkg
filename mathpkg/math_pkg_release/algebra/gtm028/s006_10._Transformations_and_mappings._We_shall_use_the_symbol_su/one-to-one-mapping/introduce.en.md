A one-to-one mapping, denoted $(1,1)$, is the classical notion of a bijection: a mapping that is simultaneously onto (surjective) and univalent (injective). An essential property is that the inverse $T^{-1}$ of a one-to-one mapping is itself a mapping, and moreover it is also one-to-one. This makes one-to-one mappings the natural notion of isomorphism in the category of sets.
