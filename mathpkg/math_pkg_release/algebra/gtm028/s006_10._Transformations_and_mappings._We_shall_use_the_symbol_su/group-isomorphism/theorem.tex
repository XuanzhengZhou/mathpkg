A univalent homomorphic mapping of $G$ into (or onto) $\bar{G}$ is called an isomorphism, or an isomorphic mapping, of $G$ into (or onto) $\bar{G}$. It is clear that an isomorphism of $G$ onto $\bar{G}$ is a homomorphism of $G$ into $\bar{G}$ which is at the same time a one-to-one mapping.

If $T$ is an isomorphism of $G$ onto $\bar{G}$, then it is clear that $T^{-1}$ is an isomorphism of $\bar{G}$ onto $G$. Hence if $\bar{G}$ is an isomorphic image of $G$, then also $G$ is an isomorphic image of $\bar{G}$. We say then that $G$ and $\bar{G}$ are isomorphic groups.
