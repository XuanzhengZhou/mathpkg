A group isomorphism is a homomorphism that is also a bijection (one-to-one mapping). Isomorphic groups are algebraically indistinguishable: every group-theoretic property is preserved by isomorphism. The inverse of an isomorphism is also an isomorphism, making the relation of being isomorphic an equivalence relation on the class of all groups.
