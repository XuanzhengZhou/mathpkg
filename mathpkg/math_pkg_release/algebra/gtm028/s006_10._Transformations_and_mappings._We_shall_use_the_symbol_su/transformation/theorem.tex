Let $S$ and $\bar{S}$ be arbitrary sets of elements. By a transformation $T$ of $S$ into $\bar{S}$ we mean a rule which associates with every element $a$ of $S$ some subset of $\bar{S}$. This subset, which may be empty, will be denoted by $aT$. If $\bar{a}$ is an element of $aT$, we say that $\bar{a}$ corresponds to $a$ (under the given transformation $T$), or that $\bar{a}$ is a transform of $a$, or that $\bar{a}$ is a $T$-image of $a$.
