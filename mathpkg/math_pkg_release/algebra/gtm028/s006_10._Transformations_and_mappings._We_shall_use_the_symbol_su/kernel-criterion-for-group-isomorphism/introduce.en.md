Theorem 2 provides the fundamental test for determining whether a group homomorphism is injective (and hence an isomorphism onto its image): a homomorphism $T: G \to \bar{G}$ is an isomorphism if and only if its kernel contains only the identity element of $G$. This criterion is used very frequently throughout group theory and its applications, as it reduces the injectivity question to checking a single normal subgroup.
