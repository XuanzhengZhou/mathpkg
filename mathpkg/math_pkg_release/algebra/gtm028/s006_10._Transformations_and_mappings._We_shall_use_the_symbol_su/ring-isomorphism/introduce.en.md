A ring isomorphism is a ring homomorphism that is also bijective (univalent and onto). Isomorphic rings are algebraically indistinguishable: every property expressible in terms of the ring operations $+$ and $\cdot$ is preserved. Isomorphic images of integral domains and fields are again integral domains and fields respectively.
