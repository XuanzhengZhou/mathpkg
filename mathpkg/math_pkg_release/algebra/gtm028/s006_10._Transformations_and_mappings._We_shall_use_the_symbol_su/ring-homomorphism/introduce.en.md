A ring homomorphism is a mapping between rings that preserves both addition and multiplication. The notation $R \sim \bar{R}$ indicates that $\bar{R}$ is a homomorphic image, while $R \cong \bar{R}$ and $T: R \cong \bar{R}$ denote isomorphism. These notations are used analogously for groups, providing a uniform language for structure-preserving maps across algebra.
