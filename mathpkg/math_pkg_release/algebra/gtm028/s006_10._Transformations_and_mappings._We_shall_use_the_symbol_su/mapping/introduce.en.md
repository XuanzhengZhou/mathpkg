A mapping is a transformation that is both everywhere defined (each element of the domain has at least one image) and single-valued (each element has exactly one image). This is the standard notion of a function in modern set theory. A mapping is called onto if its image equals the entire codomain, and univalent (or injective) if distinct elements have distinct images.
