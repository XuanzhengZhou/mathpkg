The inverse transformation $T^{-1}$ reverses the correspondence defined by $T$: for each element $\bar{a}$ in the target set $\bar{S}$, the set $\bar{a}T^{-1}$ consists of all elements $a \in S$ whose $T$-image contains $\bar{a}$. This is the natural set-valued generalization of the inverse relation. Every transformation is the inverse of its own inverse, making the operation involutive.
