For a transformation $T$ of $S$ into $\bar{S}$, the inverse transformation $T^{-1}$ of $\bar{S}$ into $S$ is defined as follows: If $\bar{a} \in \bar{S}$, then $\bar{a}T^{-1}$ is the set of all elements of $S$ having $\bar{a}$ as $T$-image; that is, $a \in \bar{a}T^{-1}$ if and only if $\bar{a} \in aT$. Clearly $T$ is the inverse of $T^{-1}$.
