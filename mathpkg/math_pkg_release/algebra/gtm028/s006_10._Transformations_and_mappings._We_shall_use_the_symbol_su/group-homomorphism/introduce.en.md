A group homomorphism is a mapping $T: G \to \bar{G}$ between groups that respects the group operation: $(ab)T = (aT)(bT)$. In additive notation for abelian groups this becomes $(a+b)T = aT + bT$. Homomorphisms are the structure-preserving maps in group theory, and they underlie the entire study of group relationships, extensions, and quotients.
