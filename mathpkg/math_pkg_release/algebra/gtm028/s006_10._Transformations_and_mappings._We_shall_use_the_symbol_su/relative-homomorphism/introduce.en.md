A relative homomorphism (or $R_0$-homomorphism) is a ring homomorphism $T: R \to \bar{R}$ whose restriction to a common subring $R_0$ is the identity. This concept is fundamental in commutative algebra, especially for studying extensions of rings: an $R_0$-isomorphism is an isomorphism that fixes $R_0$ pointwise. For example, complex conjugation is an $\mathbb{R}$-automorphism of $\mathbb{C}$.
