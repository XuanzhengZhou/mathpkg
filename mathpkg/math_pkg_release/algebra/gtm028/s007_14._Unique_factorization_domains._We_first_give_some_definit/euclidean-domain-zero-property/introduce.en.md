In a euclidean domain, the zero element minimizes the Euclidean function $\varphi$ among non-zero elements. This follows from the division algorithm: applying E2 with $a = 0$ forces the remainder $r = 0$, and E1 then yields the strict inequality $\varphi(0) < \varphi(b)$. The function can always be normalized so that $\varphi(0) = 0$ while preserving E1 and E2.
