Every polynomial $f \in R[X]$ induces a function $f_{\Delta}: \Delta \to \Delta$ on any unitary ring $\Delta$ over $R$, defined by substituting elements of $\Delta$ for the indeterminate $X$. This gives meaning to the term "indeterminate" — $X$ represents a variable that can be evaluated in any ring extension of $R$. Distinct polynomials may give rise to the same function on a particular ring $\Delta$, a subtlety important in finite rings.
