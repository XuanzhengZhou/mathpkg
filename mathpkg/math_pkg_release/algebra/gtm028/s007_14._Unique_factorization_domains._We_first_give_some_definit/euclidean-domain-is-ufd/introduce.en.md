Theorem 5 establishes that every euclidean domain satisfies the conditions UF1 and UF3 (equivalently, UF1 and UF2), hence is a unique factorization domain. The proof uses induction on the Euclidean function $\varphi$ to show UF1 (existence of factorization) and uses the Euclidean algorithm to construct GCDs as linear combinations, which then implies UF3 (every irreducible is prime). This provides the chain of inclusions: euclidean domains $\subset$ principal ideal domains $\subset$ UFDs.
