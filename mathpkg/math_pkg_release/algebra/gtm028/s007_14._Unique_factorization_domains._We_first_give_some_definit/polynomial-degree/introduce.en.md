The degree of a non-zero polynomial $f = \{a_0, a_1, \cdots\}$ is the largest index $n$ for which $a_n \neq 0$. The corresponding coefficient $a_n$ is the leading coefficient. The degree function satisfies $\partial(fg) \leq \partial f + \partial g$, with equality holding precisely when the product of the leading coefficients is non-zero. When $R$ is an integral domain, this equality always holds, and the polynomial ring is also an integral domain.
