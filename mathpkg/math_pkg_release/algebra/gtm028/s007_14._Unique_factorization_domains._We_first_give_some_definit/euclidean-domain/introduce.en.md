A euclidean domain is an integral domain equipped with a Euclidean function $\varphi$ that satisfies a division algorithm (E2) and is monotone with respect to divisibility (E1). The ring of integers $\mathbb{Z}$ with $\varphi(n) = |n|$ and the polynomial ring $F[X]$ over a field with $\varphi(f) = \deg f$ are the prototypical examples. Euclidean domains form an important subclass of principal ideal domains and unique factorization domains.
