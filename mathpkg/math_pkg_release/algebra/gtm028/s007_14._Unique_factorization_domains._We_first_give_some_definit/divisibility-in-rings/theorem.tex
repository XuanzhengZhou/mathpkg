If $a$ and $b$ are elements of a commutative ring $R$ with identity, we say that $b$ \text{divides} $a$ (or $b$ is a \text{divisor} of $a$) and that $a$ is \text{divisible by} $b$ (or $a$ is a \text{multiple} of $b$) if there exists in $R$ an element $c$ such that $a = bc$. Notation: $b \mid a$, or $a \equiv 0 \pmod{b}$. The units of $R$ are those and only those elements of $R$ which are divisors of $1$.
