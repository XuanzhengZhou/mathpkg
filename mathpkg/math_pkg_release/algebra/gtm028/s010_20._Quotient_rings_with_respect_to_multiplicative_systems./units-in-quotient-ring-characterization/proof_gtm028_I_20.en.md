($\Leftarrow$) Suppose $b' \in R$ is regular and divides some element $b \in M$, say $b = a b'$ with $a \in R$. Then $b'$ is regular (otherwise $b$ would be a zero divisor). We have $1/b' = a/b \in R_M$, so $b'$ is a unit in $R_M$, and hence $b' \in M'$.

($\Rightarrow$) Conversely, suppose $b' \in M'$, i.e., $b'$ is a unit in $R_M$. Then $1/b' \in R_M$, so $1/b' = a/b$ for some $a \in R$, $b \in M$. This gives $b = a b'$ in $R$, showing that $b'$ divides $b \in M$.
