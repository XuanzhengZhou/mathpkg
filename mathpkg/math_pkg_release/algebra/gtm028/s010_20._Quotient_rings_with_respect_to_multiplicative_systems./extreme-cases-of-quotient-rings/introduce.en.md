The two extreme cases of quotient rings show that the construction interpolates between the original ring $R$ and its total quotient ring $F$. When every element of $M$ is already invertible in $R$, the localization adds nothing new. At the opposite extreme, when every regular element is allowed as a denominator, we recover the maximal ring of fractions of $R$, i.e., the total quotient ring.
