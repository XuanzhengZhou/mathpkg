This proposition establishes the transitivity of localization: forming a quotient ring of a quotient ring is equivalent to forming a single quotient ring of the original ring with respect to a suitably chosen multiplicative system. In other words, localization is an idempotent operation in the appropriate sense — any iterated localization can be collapsed into a single step. This is a fundamental property that makes the category of localized rings well-behaved.
