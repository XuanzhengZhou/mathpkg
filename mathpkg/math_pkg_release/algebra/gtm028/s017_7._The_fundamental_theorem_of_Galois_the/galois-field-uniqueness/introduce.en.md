This theorem establishes that Galois fields are uniquely determined up to isomorphism by their cardinality. Since every Galois field $GF(p^n)$ is a splitting field over $\mathcal{F}_p$ of the polynomial $X^{p^n} - X$, and splitting fields of a given polynomial are unique up to isomorphism (Theorem 12, §6), any two Galois fields with $p^n$ elements must be isomorphic. This justifies the notation $GF(p^n)$ as referring to an essentially unique field.
