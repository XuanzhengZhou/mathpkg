The number of elements of a Galois field of characteristic $p$ is always a power $p^n$ of $p$ (where $n$ is the degree of the field over its prime subfield $\mathcal{F}_p$). Moreover, if $k$ is a Galois field consisting of $m$ elements and $K$ is a finite extension of $k$ of degree $n$, then $K$ consists of $m^n$ elements.

Furthermore, the polynomial $X^{p^n} - X$ factors completely into linear factors in $GF(p^n)[X]$:
\[
X^{p^n} - X = \prod_{i=1}^{p^n} (X - \alpha_i),
\]
where $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \cdots, \alpha_{p^n}$ are all the elements of $GF(p^n)$. Consequently, $GF(p^n)$ is a splitting field over $\mathcal{F}_p$ of the polynomial $X^{p^n} - X$, and is therefore a normal extension of $\mathcal{F}_p$.
