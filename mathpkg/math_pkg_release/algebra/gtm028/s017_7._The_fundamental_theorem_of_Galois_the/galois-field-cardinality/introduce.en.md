This theorem establishes the fundamental cardinality properties of Galois fields (finite fields). A Galois field $GF(p^n)$ of characteristic $p$ contains exactly $p^n$ elements, where $n$ is its degree over the prime field $\mathcal{F}_p$. More generally, a degree $n$ extension of a Galois field with $m$ elements contains $m^n$ elements. The theorem also shows that $X^{p^n} - X$ splits completely in $GF(p^n)$, making it a splitting field and normal extension of $\mathcal{F}_p$, which yields the uniqueness of Galois fields of a given order up to isomorphism.
