A principal fractionary ideal is the simplest kind of fractionary ideal, generated by a single element of the quotient field $K$. If $x = a/b \in K$, then $Rx = \{rx : r \in R\}$ is a fractionary ideal with common denominator $b$. Principal fractionary ideals are always invertible, with inverse $Rx^{-1}$.
