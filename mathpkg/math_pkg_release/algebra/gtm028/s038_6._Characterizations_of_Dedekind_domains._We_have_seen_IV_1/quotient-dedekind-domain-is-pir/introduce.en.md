Corollary 1 states that every proper quotient of a Dedekind domain is a principal ideal ring (every ideal is principal). Two proofs are given: one using Theorem 16 via localization at the multiplicative system of elements not in any prime divisor of $\mathfrak{a}$, and a more direct proof using the fact that $\mathfrak{a}$ is a product of pairwise comaximal prime powers, reducing to the case $R/\mathfrak{p}^n$ where one shows $\mathfrak{p}^s = Rt^s + \mathfrak{p}^n$.
