Corollary 2 states that every ideal in a Dedekind domain can be generated by just two elements. The proof selects a nonzero element $a \in \mathfrak{a}$; by Corollary 1, $R/Ra$ is a principal ideal ring, so $\mathfrak{a}/Ra$ is generated by a single residue class $b + Ra$. Then $\{a, b\}$ generates $\mathfrak{a}$.
