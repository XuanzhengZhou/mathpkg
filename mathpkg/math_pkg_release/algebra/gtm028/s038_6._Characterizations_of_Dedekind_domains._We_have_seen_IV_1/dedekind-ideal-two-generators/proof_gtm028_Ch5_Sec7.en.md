We take a non-zero element $a$ in $\mathfrak{a}$. As $R/Ra$ is a principal ideal ring (Corollary 1), the ideal $\mathfrak{a}/Ra$ is principal. Let $b$ be an element of $\mathfrak{a}$ whose residue class modulo $Ra$ generates $\mathfrak{a}/Ra$. Then it is clear that $\{a, b\}$ is a basis of $\mathfrak{a}$.
