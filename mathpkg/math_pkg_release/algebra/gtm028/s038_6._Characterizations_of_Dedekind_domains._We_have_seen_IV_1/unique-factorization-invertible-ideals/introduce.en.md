Lemma 5 establishes the uniqueness of prime ideal factorization for invertible ideals. The proof proceeds by induction: from $\prod_i \mathfrak{p}_i \subset \mathfrak{q}_1$ one deduces that some $\mathfrak{p}_r \subset \mathfrak{q}_1$, and conversely some $\mathfrak{q}_s \subset \mathfrak{p}_r$, forcing equality by minimality of prime ideals. Multiplying by inverses reduces the number of factors.
