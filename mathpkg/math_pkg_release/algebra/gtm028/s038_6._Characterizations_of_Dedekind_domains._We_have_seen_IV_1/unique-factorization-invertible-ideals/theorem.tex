If $\prod_{i=1}^n \mathfrak{p}_i = \prod_{j=1}^m \mathfrak{q}_j$, where the $\mathfrak{p}_i$ and $\mathfrak{q}_j$ are invertible prime ideals and no $\mathfrak{p}_i$ is contained in any other $\mathfrak{p}_k$ (and similarly for the $\mathfrak{q}_j$), then $n = m$ and, after a suitable reordering, $\mathfrak{p}_i = \mathfrak{q}_i$ for all $i$.
