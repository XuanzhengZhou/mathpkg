Since $\prod_i \mathfrak{p}_i$ is contained in $\mathfrak{q}_1$, some $\mathfrak{q}_j$, say $\mathfrak{q}_1$, is contained in $\mathfrak{p}_1$. Similarly, since $\prod_i \mathfrak{p}_i$ is contained in $\mathfrak{q}_1$, some $\mathfrak{p}_i$, say $\mathfrak{p}_r$, is contained in $\mathfrak{q}_1$. Thus $\mathfrak{p}_r \subset \mathfrak{q}_1 \subset \mathfrak{p}_1$. From the minimality of $\mathfrak{p}_1$ we deduce that $\mathfrak{p}_r = \mathfrak{q}_1 = \mathfrak{p}_1$. Multiplying the relation $\prod_i \mathfrak{p}_i = \prod_j \mathfrak{q}_j$ by $\mathfrak{p}_1^{-1}$, we get $\prod_{i \neq 1} \mathfrak{p}_i = \prod_{j \neq 1} \mathfrak{q}_j$. The lemma now follows by induction on $n$, the case $n = 1$ being trivial.
