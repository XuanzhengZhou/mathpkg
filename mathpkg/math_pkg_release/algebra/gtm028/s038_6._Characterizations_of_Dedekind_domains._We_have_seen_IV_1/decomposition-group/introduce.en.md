The decomposition group $G_Z$ of a prime ideal $\mathfrak{P}$ in a Galois extension of Dedekind domains is the stabilizer of $\mathfrak{P}$ under the action of the Galois group. It captures how the prime ideal $\mathfrak{p}$ of the base ring splits in the extension. The fixed field $K_Z$ of $G_Z$ is called the decomposition field, and the factorization of $\mathfrak{p}$ in the extension is governed by the subgroup structure of $G_Z$ and its quotient groups.
