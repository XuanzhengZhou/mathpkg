An invertible fractionary ideal is one that has a multiplicative inverse in the monoid of fractionary ideals. This notion is central to the theory of Dedekind domains: in a Dedekind domain, every nonzero fractionary ideal is invertible, and the set of all fractionary ideals forms an abelian group under multiplication. The invertibility of ideals enables the unique factorization of ideals into prime ideal powers.
