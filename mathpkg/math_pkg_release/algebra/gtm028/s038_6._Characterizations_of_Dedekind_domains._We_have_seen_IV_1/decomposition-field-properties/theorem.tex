Let $K_Z$ be the decomposition field of the prime ideal $\mathfrak{P}$ in $R'$, $R_Z$ the integral closure of $R$ in $K_Z$, and $\mathfrak{P}_Z$ the contracted ideal $\mathfrak{P} \cap R_Z$. Then:

$\mathfrak{P}$ is the only prime ideal of $R'$ lying over $\mathfrak{P}_Z$; its relative degree is $f$ and its reduced ramification index is $e$:
$$R'\mathfrak{P}_Z = \mathfrak{P}^e.$$

If the decomposition group $G_Z$ of $\mathfrak{P}$ is an invariant subgroup of $G$, then $K_Z$ is a normal and separable extension of $K$, and the factorization of the ideal $R_Z \mathfrak{p}$ in $R_Z$ consists of $g$ distinct and conjugate prime factors, all of them with relative degree $1$.
