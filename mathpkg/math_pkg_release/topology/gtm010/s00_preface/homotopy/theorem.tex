If $f$ and $g$ are maps (i.e., continuous functions) from $X$ to $Y$, then $f$ is \textbf{homotopic} to $g$, written
$$f \simeq g,$$
if there is a map $F: X \times I \to Y$ such that
$$F(x,0) = f(x) \quad \text{and} \quad F(x,1) = g(x)$$
for all $x \in X$. The map $F$ is called a homotopy between $f$ and $g$.
