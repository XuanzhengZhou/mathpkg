If $X \subset Y$, then a map $D: Y \to X$ is a \textbf{strong deformation retraction} if there exists a map $F: Y \times I \to Y$ such that
\begin{align*}
\text{(1)}&\quad F_0 = 1_Y,\\[4pt]
\text{(2)}&\quad F_t(x) = x \quad \text{for all } (x,t) \in X \times I,\\[4pt]
\text{(3)}&\quad F_1(y) = D(y) \quad \text{for all } y \in Y.
\end{align*}
Here $F_t: Y \to Y$ is defined by $F_t(y) = F(y,t)$. The space $X$ is called a \textbf{strong deformation retract} of $Y$, and $F$ is called a strong deformation retraction of $Y$ onto $X$.
