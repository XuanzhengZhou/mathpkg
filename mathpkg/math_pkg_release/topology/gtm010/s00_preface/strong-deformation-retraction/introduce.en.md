A strong deformation retraction is a particularly well-behaved type of homotopy equivalence. Given a subspace $X \subset Y$, a strong deformation retraction continuously shrinks $Y$ onto $X$ while keeping every point of $X$ fixed throughout the entire deformation. The resulting map $D: Y \to X$ is automatically a homotopy equivalence (with inclusion $i: X \hookrightarrow Y$ as its homotopy inverse), so $X$ and $Y$ have the same homotopy type. Strong deformation retractions play a fundamental role in simple-homotopy theory, especially in the study of elementary collapses and expansions of CW complexes.
