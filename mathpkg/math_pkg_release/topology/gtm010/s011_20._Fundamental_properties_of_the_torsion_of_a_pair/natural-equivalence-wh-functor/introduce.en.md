This fundamental theorem establishes that the geometrically defined Whitehead group $Wh(L)$ of a CW complex $L$ (defined via formal deformations of pairs containing $L$) is naturally isomorphic to the algebraically defined Whitehead group $\bigoplus Wh(\pi_1 L_j)$ of its fundamental group(s). This natural equivalence justifies the entire algebraic approach: one can compute Whitehead torsion using either geometry (via cell trading and boundary matrices) or pure algebra (via matrices over the group ring). The proof uses the properties (20.1)-(20.5) to show $T_L$ is well-defined, a homomorphism, injective, and surjective.
