The torsion of a union of subcomplexes (intersecting in a common subspace over which each deforms) is the sum of the individual torsions. This additivity property is the geometric manifestation of the fact that the Whitehead group is abelian, and it is used extensively in computations. It mirrors the behavior of the Euler characteristic and reflects the deeper fact that torsion is a homomorphism from a certain geometrically defined group to the Whitehead group.
