The mapping cylinder of a composition of cellular maps is formally deformable to the union of the individual mapping cylinders, joined along the intermediate space. This result, together with (5.5), provides the essential bridge between the geometric operations of mapping cylinders and the algebraic composition of maps. It is a key ingredient in proving the equivalence between geometric simple-homotopy theory and the algebraic Whitehead torsion formalism.
