An $h$-cobordism is a manifold with two boundary components, each of which is a deformation retract of the whole manifold. The $h$-cobordism theorem (proved by Smale in the smooth category) states that if $\dim W \geq 6$ and the Whitehead torsion $\tau(W, M_0)$ vanishes, then $W$ is a product $M_0 \times I$. This theorem, which reduced the Poincaré conjecture in dimensions $\geq 5$ to a problem about the triviality of certain Whitehead groups, is one of the crowning achievements of simple-homotopy theory and earned Smale the Fields Medal.
