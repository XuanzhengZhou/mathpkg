This theorem quantifies the relationship between homotopy and simple-homotopy equivalence. The number $\nu_L$ of distinct simple-homotopy types within the homotopy type of $L$ equals the index of a certain subgroup $Wh_0(L)$ in $Wh(L)$. For lens spaces, this index can be infinite, meaning there are infinitely many lens spaces that are homotopy equivalent but not simple-homotopy equivalent. This is one of the most striking applications of Whitehead torsion: it distinguishes spaces that classical algebraic topology cannot.
