Lens spaces provide the classic example where homotopy equivalence does not imply simple-homotopy equivalence. For L = L(p; q_1,...,q_n) with p not 1,2,3,4,6, the Whitehead group is infinite while the group of self-homotopy equivalences is finite, yielding infinitely many distinct simple-homotopy types within a single homotopy type.
