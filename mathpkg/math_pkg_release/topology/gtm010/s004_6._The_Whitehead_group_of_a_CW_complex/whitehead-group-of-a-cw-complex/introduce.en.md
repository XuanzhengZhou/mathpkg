The Whitehead group $\mathrm{Wh}(L)$ of a finite CW complex $L$ encodes the obstruction for a homotopy equivalence to be a simple-homotopy equivalence. Its elements are equivalence classes of homotopically trivial CW pairs $(K, L)$ — pairs where $L$ includes into $K$ as a homotopy equivalence — modulo the relation $K \wedge K'$ rel $L$. The group operation is given by taking the union of two such pairs along their common subspace $L$, yielding a finitely generated abelian group that serves as the recipient of the torsion invariant.
