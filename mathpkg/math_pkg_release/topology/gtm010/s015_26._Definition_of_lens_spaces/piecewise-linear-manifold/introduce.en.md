A piecewise linear (p.l.) $n$-manifold is a topological space $M$ equipped with a p.l. atlas $\mathcal{A}$ consisting of coordinate charts $(U_i, h_i)$ whose transition maps $h_j h_i^{-1}$ are piecewise linear. This structure provides a combinatorial setting for studying manifolds, intermediate between the topological and smooth categories. The p.l. category is essential in simple-homotopy theory and provides the natural framework for constructing lens spaces as quotient spaces of p.l. spheres by free group actions.
