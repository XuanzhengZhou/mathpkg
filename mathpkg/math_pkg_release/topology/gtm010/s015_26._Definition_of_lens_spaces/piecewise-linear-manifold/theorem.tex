A \text{p.l. $n$-manifold} (without boundary) is a pair $(M, \mathcal{A})$ where $M$ is a separable metric space and $\mathcal{A}$ is a family of pairs $(U_i, h_i)$ such that $\{U_i\}$ is an open cover of $M$, $h_i: U_i \to \mathbb{R}^n$ is a homeomorphism onto an open subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$, and $h_j h_i^{-1}: h_i(U_i \cap U_j) \to \mathbb{R}^n$ is piecewise linear for all $i, j$. $\mathcal{A}$ is called a \text{p.l. atlas} and the $(U_i, h_i)$ are called \text{coordinate charts}.
