If $G$ is a group of auto-homeomorphisms of a topological space $M$, then $G$ acts freely on $M$ if $[x \in M, 1 \neq g \in G] \Rightarrow [g(x) \neq x]$. The orbit of $x_0$ is $G(x_0) = \{g(x_0) | g \in G\}$.

If $M$ is a p.l. manifold and $G$ is a finite group of p.l. homeomorphisms acting freely on $M$, then $M/G$ has a natural p.l. structure with respect to which the orbit map $\pi: M \rightarrow M/G$ is p.l.
