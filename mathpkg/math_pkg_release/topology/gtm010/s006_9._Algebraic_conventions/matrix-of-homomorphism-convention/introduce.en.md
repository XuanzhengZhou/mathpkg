Cohen establishes a careful matrix notation for module homomorphisms that is used throughout the algebraic chapters. A key convention to note is that composition reverses order: $\langle f_2 \circ f_1 \rangle = \langle f_1 \rangle \langle f_2 \rangle$. This is because Cohen writes ring elements on the left of module elements, and the matrix acts on the right of row vectors representing coordinates. Change-of-basis matrices and their composition rules are also formalized, providing the algebraic machinery needed for the Whitehead group and torsion computations.
