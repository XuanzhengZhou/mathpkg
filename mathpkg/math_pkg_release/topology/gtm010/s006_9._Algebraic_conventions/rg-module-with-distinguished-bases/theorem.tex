An $(R, G)$-module is a free $R$-module $M$ together with a family $B$ of "distinguished bases" of $M$ such that:
(1) If $b \in B$ and $b'$ is another basis, then $b' \in B \Leftrightarrow \tau(\langle b/b' \rangle) = 0 \in K_G(R)$.
(2) If $M = M_1 \oplus M_2$ and $b_i$ is a distinguished basis of $M_i$, then $b_1 \cup b_2$ is a distinguished basis of $M$.
(3) $B$ is non-empty.
