Suppose that $(K, L)$ is a finite CW pair. Then $K \searrow L$ --- i.e., $K$ collapses to $L$ by an elementary collapse --- iff
(1) $K = L \cup e^{n-1} \cup e^n$ where $e^n$ and $e^{n-1}$ are not in $L$,
(2) there exists a ball pair $(Q^n, Q^{n-1}) \approx (I^n, I^{n-1})$ and a map $\varphi: Q^n \rightarrow K$ such that
(a) $\varphi$ is a characteristic map for $e^n$
(b) $\varphi|Q^{n-1}$ is a characteristic map for $e^{n-1}$
(c) $\varphi(P^{n-1}) \subset L^{n-1}$, where $P^{n-1} \equiv \text{Cl}(\partial Q^n - Q^{n-1})$.
In these circumstances we also write $L \nearrow K$ and say that $L$ expands to $K$ by an elementary expansion.
