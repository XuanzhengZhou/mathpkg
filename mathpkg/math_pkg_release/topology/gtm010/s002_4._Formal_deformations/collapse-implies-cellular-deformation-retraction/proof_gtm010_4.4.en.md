Let $K = L \cup e^{n-1} \cup e^n$. By hypothesis there is a map $\varphi_0: I^{n-1} \rightarrow L^{n-1}$ such that $(K, L) \approx (L \cup_{\varphi_0} I^n, L)$. But $L \cup_{\varphi_0} I^n$ is just the mapping cylinder of $\varphi_0$. Hence, by (1.1) and its proof there is a strong deformation retraction $D: K \rightarrow L$ such that $D(\overline{e^n}) = \varphi_0(I^{n-1}) \subset L^{n-1}$. Clearly $D$ is cellular.

If $D_1$ and $D_2: K \rightarrow L$ are two strong deformation retractions and $i: L \subset K$ then $iD_1 \simeq 1_K \simeq iD_2$ rel $L$. So $D_1 = D_1iD_1 \simeq D_1iD_2 = D_2$.
