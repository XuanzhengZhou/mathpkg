An elementary expansion is the formal inverse of an elementary collapse. Geometrically, an elementary expansion of $L$ attaches an $n$-ball $Q^n$ to $L$ along a prescribed face $Q^{n-1}$ of its boundary; the remaining part $P^{n-1} = \operatorname{Cl}(\partial Q^n - Q^{n-1})$ is glued to the $(n-1)$-skeleton of $L$ via a map $\varphi_0$. The resulting complex is the mapping cylinder-like space $L \cup_{\varphi_0} Q^n$, which then admits an elementary collapse back to $L$ by collapsing the newly added ball onto $P^{n-1}$.
