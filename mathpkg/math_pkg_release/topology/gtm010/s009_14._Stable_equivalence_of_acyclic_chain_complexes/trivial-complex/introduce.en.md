A trivial complex is any direct sum of elementary trivial complexes. In the category of chain complexes with preferred bases, trivial complexes serve as the identity up to stable equivalence. Adding or removing a trivial complex via direct sum does not change the stable equivalence class. This concept is the algebraic counterpart of adding or removing pairs of cells that trivially cancel each other geometrically.
