An $(R, G)$-module is a free $R$-module $M$ together with a non-empty family $B$ of distinguished bases such that:
(1) If $b \in B$ and $b'$ is another basis of $M$, then $b' \in B \Leftrightarrow \tau(\langle b/b' \rangle) = 0 \in K_G(R)$.
(2) If $M = M_1 \oplus M_2$ and $b_i$ is a distinguished basis of $M_i$ ($i = 1, 2$), then $b_1 \cup b_2$ is a distinguished basis of $M$.

The torsion $\tau(f)$ of an isomorphism $f: M_1 \rightarrow M_2$ is $\tau(A) \in K_G(R)$ where $A$ is the matrix of $f$ with respect to any preferred bases. $f$ is a simple isomorphism if $\tau(f) = 0$.
