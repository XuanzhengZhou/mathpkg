The Whitehead group $Wh(G)$ of a group $G$ is defined as $K_T(\mathbb{Z}G)$, where $T$ denotes the group of trivial units of the integral group ring $\mathbb{Z}G$. This abelian group is the central algebraic invariant in simple-homotopy theory, measuring the obstruction to simplifying CW complexes through formal deformations (elementary expansions and collapses). The computation of Whitehead groups for various families of groups is a fundamental problem linking algebraic K-theory with geometric topology.
