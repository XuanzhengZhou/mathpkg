For the lens space $L(p; q_1, \dots, q_n)$ with universal covering $\Sigma^{2n-1}$ and covering group $G = \langle g \rangle$ of order $p$, the cellular chain complex satisfies:
\begin{align*}
\partial \tilde{e}_{2i+1} &= g^{r_{i+1}} \tilde{e}_{2i} - \tilde{e}_{2i}, \quad \text{where } r_{i+1} q_{i+1} \equiv 1 \pmod{p} \\
\partial \tilde{e}_{2i} &= \tilde{e}_{2i-1} + g \tilde{e}_{2i-1} + \dots + g^{p-1} \tilde{e}_{2i-1} \\
\partial g &= g \partial: C^k(\Sigma^{2n-1}) \to C^k(\Sigma^{2n-1})
\end{align*}
The cells satisfy $e_j^k = g^t \tilde{e}_k$ where $t q_j \equiv j \pmod{p}$.
