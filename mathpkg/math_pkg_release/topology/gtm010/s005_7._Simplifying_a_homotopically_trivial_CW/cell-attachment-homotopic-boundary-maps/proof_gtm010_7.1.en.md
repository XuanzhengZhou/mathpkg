First consider the case where $e_0 \cap e_1 = \varnothing$. Give the set $L \cup e_0 \cup e_1$ the topology and CW structure which make $K_0$ and $K_1$ subcomplexes. Since $\varphi_0|\partial I^n \simeq \varphi_1|\partial I^n$ in $L$, there is a homotopy $H: \partial I^n \times I \to L$ between them. Use this homotopy to construct a mapping cylinder-like intermediate complex that expands and collapses to both $K_0$ and $K_1$, showing $K_0 \wedge K_1$ rel $L$. The general case follows by renaming points to achieve disjointness of the cells.
