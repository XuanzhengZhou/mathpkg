This theorem justifies the equivalence between the CW and simplicial approaches to simple-homotopy theory. Any finite CW complex can be replaced, up to simple-homotopy type, by a simplicial complex of the same dimension. The proof uses induction on the number of cells, the fact that mapping cylinders of simplicial maps are triangulable, and that subdivision does not change simple-homotopy type. This means that although Whitehead originally worked with simplicial complexes, the theory extends naturally to the more convenient CW category.
