Let $a$ be a non-trivial unit of $\mathbb{Z}(G)$ and let $A = (a)$ be the one-by-one matrix with $a$ as entry. Since $G$ is abelian, $\mathbb{Z}(G)$ is commutative. The determinant operation gives a well-defined map from square matrices over $\mathbb{Z}(G)$ to $\mathbb{Z}(G)$. Operations (II), (III) and (V) preserve the determinant. Operations (I) and (IV) multiply the determinant by a trivial unit. Thus if $B$ is a matrix into which $A$ can be transformed, $\det B = g \cdot (\det A) = ga$ for some trivial unit $g$. Therefore $\det B$ is a non-trivial unit and, in particular, $B$ cannot be the identity matrix.
