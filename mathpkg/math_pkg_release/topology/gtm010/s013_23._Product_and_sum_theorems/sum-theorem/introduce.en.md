The sum theorem describes how torsion behaves when a space is decomposed as a union of subcomplexes. It plays a role analogous to the Mayer-Vietoris sequence in homology, allowing torsion computations to be broken down into simpler pieces. This is particularly useful when a complex can be built from simpler building blocks whose torsions are already known.
