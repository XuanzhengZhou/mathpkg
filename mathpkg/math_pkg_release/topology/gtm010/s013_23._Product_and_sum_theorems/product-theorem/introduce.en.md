The product theorem describes how Whitehead torsion behaves under Cartesian products. The torsion of a product map $f \times f'$ equals the sum of the torsions of the factors, each weighted by the Euler characteristic of the other factor's codomain. This explains why torsion is a "multiplicative" invariant with respect to products and why the Euler characteristic appears naturally in these formulas.
