For 3-dimensional lens spaces $L(p; q, 1)$ and $L(p; q', 1)$:
(1) $L(p; q, 1)$ is homotopy equivalent to $L(p; q', 1)$ iff $q' \equiv \pm k^2 q \pmod{p}$ for some integer $k$.
(2) $L(p; q, 1)$ is homeomorphic to $L(p; q', 1)$ iff $q' \equiv \pm q^{\pm 1} \pmod{p}$.
Thus for $n = 2$, homeomorphism and homotopy equivalence differ: the homeomorphism classification is finer and was originally proved by Reidemeister using torsion.
