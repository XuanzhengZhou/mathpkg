The 3-dimensional lens spaces L(p; q, 1) are classified up to homeomorphism by the condition q' = +/- q^{+/-1} mod p. This differs from the homotopy classification (which involves squares), showing that homotopy equivalence does not imply homeomorphism even for these simple spaces. Reidemeister's 1935 classification was a landmark achievement.
