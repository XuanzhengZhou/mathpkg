For lens spaces $L = L(p; q_1, \ldots, q_n)$ and $L' = L(p; q'_1, \ldots, q'_n)$ with $n \geq 3$:
(1) $L$ and $L'$ are homeomorphic iff they are simple-homotopy equivalent.
(2) $L$ and $L'$ are p.l. homeomorphic iff they are homeomorphic (true in all dimensions by the Hauptvermutung for lens spaces).
(3) The homeomorphism classification coincides with the simple-homotopy classification.

For $n = 2$ (3-dimensional lens spaces), the classification is more subtle and involves the theory of normal invariants.
