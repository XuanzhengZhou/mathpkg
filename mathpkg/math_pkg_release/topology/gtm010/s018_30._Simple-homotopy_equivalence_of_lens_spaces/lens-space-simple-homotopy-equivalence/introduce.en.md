The simple-homotopy classification of lens spaces refines the homotopy classification by adding the condition that the Reidemeister torsion (a product over the parameters) must match up to trivial units and squares. This is the classic example where Whitehead torsion distinguishes homotopy equivalent spaces that are not simple-homotopy equivalent.
