Let $L = L(p; q_1, \dots, q_n)$ and $L' = L(p; q'_1, \dots, q'_n)$. If $f: L \to L'$ is a simple-homotopy equivalence with $f_\#(g) = g'^a$, then there exist numbers $\varepsilon_i \in \{+1, -1\}$ such that $(q_1, \dots, q_n)$ is equal modulo $p$ to some permutation of $(\varepsilon_1 a q'_1, \varepsilon_2 a q'_2, \dots, \varepsilon_n a q'_n)$.
