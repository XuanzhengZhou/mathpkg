This is the main result of the book's lens space chapter. It shows that simple-homotopy equivalence is strictly finer than homotopy equivalence for lens spaces. While homotopy equivalence only cares about the product $q_1 \dots q_n$ up to sign and powers, simple-homotopy equivalence requires the individual parameters $q_i$ to match (up to permutation, sign, and multiplication by $a$). For example, $L(7;1,1)$ and $L(7;1,2)$ are homotopy equivalent but not simple-homotopy equivalent. The proof uses the Franz theorem, a deep number-theoretic result about roots of unity, to extract individual parameter information from the torsion.
