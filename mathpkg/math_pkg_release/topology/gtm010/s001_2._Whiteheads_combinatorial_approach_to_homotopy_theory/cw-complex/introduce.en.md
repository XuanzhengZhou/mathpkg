A CW complex is a space built by inductively attaching cells of increasing dimension. The "CW" stands for "closure-finite" (CW 3: each cell closure meets only finitely many cells) and "weak topology" (CW 4: the topology is determined by cell closures). CW complexes are the natural setting for simple-homotopy theory because their cell structure allows explicit combinatorial manipulations. Every finite simplicial complex is a CW complex, but CW complexes are more flexible and easier to work with.
