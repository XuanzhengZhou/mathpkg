An elementary simplicial collapse removes a free face from a simplicial complex. Specifically, if a simplex $aA$ meets the subcomplex $L$ only in the boundary of its free face $a\dot{A}$, the pair $(a, A)$ forms a free face that can be collapsed away. This is the fundamental operation in Whitehead's simple-homotopy theory.
