If $f: L \to L'$ is a map between lens spaces with $\pi_1 L = G = \langle g \rangle$ and $\pi_1 L' = G' = \langle g' \rangle$, then $f_\#(g) = g'^a$ for some $a$ with $(a, p) = 1$. Moreover, $f_\#$ is independent of base point choices and is a homotopy invariant. A lift $\tilde{f}$ of $f$ is $(g, g'^a)$-equivariant.
