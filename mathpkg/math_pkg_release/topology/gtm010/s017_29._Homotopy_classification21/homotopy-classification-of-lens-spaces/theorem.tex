Let $L = L(p; q_1, \dots, q_n)$ and $L' = L(p; q'_1, \dots, q'_n)$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(A)] Homotopy classes of maps $f: L \to L'$ correspond bijectively to pairs $(a, d)$ where $a \in (\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^\times$ and $d \in \mathbb{Z}$ satisfy $d \equiv a^n r_1 \dots r_n q'_1 \dots q'_n \pmod{p}$.
\item[(B)] $L \simeq L'$ if and only if $q_1 \dots q_n \equiv \pm a^n q'_1 \dots q'_n \pmod{p}$ for some $a$ coprime to $p$.
\item[(C)] The number of distinct homotopy types of $(2n-1)$-dimensional lens spaces with fundamental group $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is the number of elements in $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^\times / \{\pm a^n\}$.
\end{enumerate}
