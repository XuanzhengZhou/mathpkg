The closed $n$-dimensional ball (or disc) $E^n$ is the set of all points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean norm is at most 1. It is a compact, contractible topological space that serves as a fundamental building block in algebraic topology. Any space homeomorphic to $E^n$ is also referred to as a closed $n$-ball. The closed ball is used to define cells in CW-complexes and plays a central role in the study of manifolds, particularly in constructing triangulations and handling boundaries.
