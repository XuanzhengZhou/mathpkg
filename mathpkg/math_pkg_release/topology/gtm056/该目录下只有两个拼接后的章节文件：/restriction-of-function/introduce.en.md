Given a function $f: B \to C$ and a subset $A \subseteq B$, the restriction of $f$ to $A$, denoted $f \mid A$, is the function from $A$ to $C$ defined by $(f \mid A)(a) = f(a)$ for all $a \in A$. The restriction is simply the original function with its domain narrowed to the subset $A$, while the mapping rule remains unchanged. In topology, restrictions are used to relate continuous maps on a space to their behavior on subspaces, and they play a key role in the definition of induced homomorphisms on fundamental groups.
