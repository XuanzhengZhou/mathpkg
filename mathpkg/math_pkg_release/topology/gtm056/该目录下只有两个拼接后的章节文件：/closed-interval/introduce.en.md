The closed interval $[a, b]$ consists of all real numbers between $a$ and $b$, inclusive of the endpoints. It is a compact, connected subset of $\mathbf{R}$ and is homeomorphic to the closed 1-ball $E^1$. In algebraic topology, the closed interval $I = [0, 1]$ is the standard parameter space for paths and homotopies: a path in a space $X$ is a continuous map $f: [0, 1] \to X$, and a homotopy is a continuous map $H: X \times [0, 1] \to Y$.
