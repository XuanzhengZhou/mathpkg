The open $n$-dimensional ball is the set of all points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean norm is strictly less than 1. It is homeomorphic to $\mathbf{R}^n$ itself via the map $x \mapsto x/(1 - |x|)$ and serves as the standard model for an $n$-dimensional manifold without boundary. In algebraic topology, open balls appear as the interiors of cells and as coordinate charts for manifolds. Any topological space homeomorphic to the open $n$-ball is also called an open $n$-ball.
