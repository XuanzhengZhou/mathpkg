The $(n-1)$-dimensional sphere $S^{n-1}$ is the set of all points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ at distance exactly 1 from the origin. It is the boundary of the closed $n$-ball $E^n$ and is a compact $(n-1)$-manifold without boundary. Spheres are among the most fundamental objects in algebraic topology: the computation of their homotopy groups, homology groups, and fundamental groups (e.g., $\pi_1(S^1) \cong \mathbf{Z}$) forms the backbone of the subject. The $0$-sphere $S^0$ consists of two points, $S^1$ is the circle, and $S^2$ is the usual sphere in $\mathbf{R}^3$.
