The half-open interval $(a, b]$ consists of all real numbers greater than $a$ and less than or equal to $b$. It includes the right endpoint $b$ but excludes the left endpoint $a$. Half-open intervals are neither open nor closed in the standard topology on $\mathbf{R}$. They appear naturally in analysis (e.g., as domains of integration) and in topology (e.g., as fundamental neighborhoods in the Sorgenfrey line).
