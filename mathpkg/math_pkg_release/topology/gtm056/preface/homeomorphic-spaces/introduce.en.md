Two topological spaces are homeomorphic (of the same topological type) if there exists a continuous bijection between them whose inverse is also continuous. Homeomorphism is the fundamental equivalence relation in topology: homeomorphic spaces are indistinguishable from the viewpoint of topological properties alone. In algebraic topology, homeomorphic spaces have isomorphic fundamental groups, homology groups, and cohomology rings.
