Two spaces are said to be of the \textbf{same topological type} if they are \textbf{homeomorphic}. A homeomorphism is a continuous bijection whose inverse is also continuous. The same name (e.g., closed $n$-dimensional ball, open $n$-dimensional ball, $(n-1)$-dimensional sphere) is sometimes applied to any topological space homeomorphic to one of the standard model spaces.
