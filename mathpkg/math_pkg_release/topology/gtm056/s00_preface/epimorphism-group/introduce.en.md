An epimorphism in group theory is a surjective group homomorphism. It maps the domain group onto the entire codomain group, so every element of the target group has at least one preimage. Epimorphisms are one of the three fundamental types of homomorphisms classified in the preface, alongside monomorphisms and isomorphisms. They play a crucial role in describing quotient structures and in formulating exact sequences throughout algebraic topology.
