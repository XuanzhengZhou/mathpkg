The $(n-1)$-dimensional sphere $S^{n-1}$ is the set of all points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean distance from the origin is exactly $1$. It is the topological boundary of both the closed $n$-ball $E^n$ and the open $n$-ball. The $(n-1)$-sphere is a compact $(n-1)$-manifold without boundary and is one of the most important spaces in algebraic topology, serving as a fundamental building block for CW-complexes and as the domain of homotopy groups $\pi_k(S^{n-1})$.
