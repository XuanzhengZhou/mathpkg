The closed $n$-dimensional ball $E^n$ (also called the closed $n$-disc) is the set of all points in $\mathbf{R}^n$ whose Euclidean norm is at most $1$, equipped with the subspace topology from $\mathbf{R}^n$. It is a fundamental example of a compact manifold with boundary, whose boundary is the $(n-1)$-sphere $S^{n-1}$. The notation $E^n$ (from the German "Einheitskugel") is used throughout the book. Any topological space homeomorphic to $E^n$ is also referred to as a closed $n$-dimensional ball.
