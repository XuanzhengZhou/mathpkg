An isomorphism in group theory is a bijective group homomorphism — one that is simultaneously a monomorphism (injective) and an epimorphism (surjective). It establishes a perfect structural correspondence between two groups, meaning they are essentially the same group up to relabeling of elements. The existence of an isomorphism between groups is the standard notion of "sameness" in group theory, and isomorphisms are invertible with the inverse also being a homomorphism.
