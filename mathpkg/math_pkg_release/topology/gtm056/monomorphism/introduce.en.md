A monomorphism is an injective group homomorphism — a structure-preserving map between groups that never maps distinct elements to the same image. Equivalently, its kernel consists solely of the identity element. In the category of groups, monomorphisms coincide exactly with injective homomorphisms, making the notion both categorical and set-theoretic.
