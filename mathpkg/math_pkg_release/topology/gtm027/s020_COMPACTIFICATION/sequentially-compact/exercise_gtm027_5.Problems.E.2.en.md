# Exercise E.2 — Sequentially Compact Spaces

A topological space is **sequentially compact** iff every sequence has a convergent subsequence.

(a) Prove that every sequentially compact space is countably compact. Give an example showing that the converse fails.

(b) Prove that for first-countable spaces, countable compactness implies sequential compactness. Conclude that for metric spaces, compactness, countable compactness, and sequential compactness are all equivalent.
