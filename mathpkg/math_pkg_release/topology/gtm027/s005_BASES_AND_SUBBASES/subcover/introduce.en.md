A subcover of a cover is simply a subfamily that still covers the set. The notion of subcover is fundamental for compactness: a space is compact if every open cover admits a finite subcover. Similarly, a Lindelöf space is one where every open cover admits a countable subcover. Subcovers capture the idea of reducing a cover to a smaller, more manageable collection while preserving the covering property.
