A Lindelöf space is one in which every open cover admits a countable subcover. This property is a weakening of compactness (which requires finite subcovers). By Lindelöf's theorem, every second countable space is Lindelöf. The class of Lindelöf spaces plays an important role in general topology and is closely related to separability and metrization theorems.
