A topological space is metacompact if every open cover admits a point finite open refinement. Metacompactness weakens paracompactness by replacing the local finiteness condition with point finiteness: every paracompact space is metacompact, but the converse does not hold in general. This notion provides a useful intermediate covering property with applications in dimension theory and generalized metric spaces.
