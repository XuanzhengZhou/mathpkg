In a normal space, every point finite open cover admits a shrinking: one can select a smaller open set inside each member whose closure is contained in that member, and the resulting family still covers the space. The proof uses a maximality argument on partial shrinkings, exploiting point finiteness to guarantee the maximal family is defined on the entire cover. This generalizes the classical shrinking lemma for locally finite covers to the point finite setting.
