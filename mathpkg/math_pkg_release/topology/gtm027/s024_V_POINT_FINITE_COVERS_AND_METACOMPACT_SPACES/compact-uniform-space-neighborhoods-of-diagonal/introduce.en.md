In a compact uniform space, the uniformity is maximal: every neighborhood of the diagonal in the product topology automatically belongs to the uniformity. Equivalently, every continuous pseudo-metric on the product belongs to the gage. This result has profound consequences—it implies that on a compact space the uniform structure is uniquely determined by the topology, and that every continuous function from a compact uniform space is automatically uniformly continuous.
