Given a family P of pseudo-metrics, the gage Q it generates consists of all pseudo-metrics that are uniformly continuous relative to the generated uniformity. A pseudo-metric q belongs to Q precisely when every V_{q,s} contains a finite intersection of sets V_{p_i,r} with p_i from P. This gives a concrete, metric-flavored description of membership in the gage, making the gage a computationally useful tool for working with uniformities.
