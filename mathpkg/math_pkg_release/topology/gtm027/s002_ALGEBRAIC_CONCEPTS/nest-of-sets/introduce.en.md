A nest (or chain, or tower) is a family of sets totally ordered by inclusion. Nests are the "chains" appearing in the hypotheses of maximal principles: the Hausdorff Maximal Principle concerns maximal nests, and Zorn's Lemma applies to partially ordered sets where every chain has an upper bound. The concept is essential for transfinite constructions.
