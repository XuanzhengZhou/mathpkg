A family $\pi$ of sets is a \textbf{nest} (sometimes called a \textbf{tower} or a \textbf{chain}) iff, whenever $A$ and $B$ are members of the family, then either $A \subset B$ or $B \subset A$. This means the family $\pi$ is linearly ordered by inclusion. If $\pi \subset \alpha$ and $\pi$ is a nest, then $\pi$ is a \textbf{nest in $\alpha$}.
