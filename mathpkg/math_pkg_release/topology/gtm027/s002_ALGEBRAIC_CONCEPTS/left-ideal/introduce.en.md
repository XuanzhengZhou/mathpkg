A left ideal is an additive subgroup of a ring that is closed under left multiplication by arbitrary ring elements. Ideals are the ring-theoretic analogue of normal subgroups: they are precisely the kernels of ring homomorphisms and enable the construction of quotient rings. The distinction between left, right, and two-sided ideals is important in non-commutative ring theory.
