For every set $A$ there is a unique \textbf{cardinal number} $C$ such that $A$ and $C$ are equipollent. If $C$ and $D$ are distinct cardinal numbers, then $C$ and $D$ are not equipollent but one of the cardinal numbers, say $C$, and a proper subset of the other are equipollent. In this case $C$ is said to be the smaller cardinal number and we write $C < D$. With this definition of order the family of all cardinal numbers is linearly ordered, and every non-void subfamily has a least member.
