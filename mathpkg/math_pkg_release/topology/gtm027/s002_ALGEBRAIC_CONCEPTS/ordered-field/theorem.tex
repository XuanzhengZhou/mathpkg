A field $F$ is an \textbf{ordered field} iff there is a subset $P$ of $F$, called the set of \textbf{positive} elements, such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is closed under addition and multiplication;
\item for each $x \in F$, exactly one of the following holds: $x \in P$, $x = 0$, or $-x \in P$.
\end{enumerate}
The members $x$ of $F$ such that $-x \in P$ are called \textbf{negative}.
