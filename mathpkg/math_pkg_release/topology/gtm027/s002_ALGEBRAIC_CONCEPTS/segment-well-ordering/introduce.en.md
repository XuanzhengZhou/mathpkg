A segment (or initial segment) of a well-ordered set is a subset closed under taking predecessors: if $x \in A$ and $y < x$, then $y \in A$. Segments are used in the proof that the Axiom of Choice implies the Well-Ordering Principle: the well-ordering is built by repeatedly selecting the next element (via a choice function) from the complement of the current segment.
