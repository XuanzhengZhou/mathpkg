A normal subgroup (also called an invariant subgroup) is a subgroup $H$ of $G$ such that left and right cosets coincide: $xH = Hx$ for all $x \in G$. Normality is the precise condition needed for the set of cosets $G/H$ to inherit a well-defined group structure, forming the quotient group. Normal subgroups are the kernels of group homomorphisms.
