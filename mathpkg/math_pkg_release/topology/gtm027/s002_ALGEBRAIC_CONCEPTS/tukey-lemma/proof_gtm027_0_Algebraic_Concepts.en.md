# Proof of Theorem 25(c): Tukey Lemma

**Tukey Lemma (25c).** There is a maximal member of each non-void family of finite character.

*Proof.* The proof is based on the maximal principle (25a). Let $\alpha$ be a family which is of finite character, let $\pi$ be a nest in $\alpha$, and let $A = \bigcup \{N : N \in \pi\}$. Each finite subset $F$ of $A$ is necessarily a subset of some member of $\pi$, for we may choose a finite subfamily of the nest $\pi$ whose union contains $F$, and this finite subfamily has a largest member which then contains $F$. Since $\alpha$ has finite character and every finite subset of $A$ belongs to $\alpha$, it follows that $A \in \alpha$. Then $\alpha$ satisfies the hypothesis of (25a) and therefore has a maximal member.
