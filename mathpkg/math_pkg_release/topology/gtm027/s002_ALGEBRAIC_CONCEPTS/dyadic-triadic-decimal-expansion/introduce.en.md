Special cases of $b$-adic expansions: dyadic ($b=2$), triadic ($b=3$), and decimal ($b=10$). Dyadic expansions correspond to the Cantor set and binary representation; decimal expansions are the standard base-10 representation of real numbers. Kelley uses dyadic expansions in the proof that the power set of a countable set is uncountable (Theorem 18).
