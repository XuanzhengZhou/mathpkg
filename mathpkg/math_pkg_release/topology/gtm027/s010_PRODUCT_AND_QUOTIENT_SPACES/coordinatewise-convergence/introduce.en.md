Coordinatewise convergence (also called pointwise convergence or the simple topology) is the standard mode of convergence in product spaces. In the important special case $X^A$ — the set of all functions from $A$ to $X$ — a net of functions converges pointwise precisely when it converges in the product topology: $F_n \to f$ iff $F_n(a) \to f(a)$ for every $a \in A$. This is the weakest topology for which all evaluation maps (projections) are continuous, making it the natural choice for function spaces in many contexts.
