Convergence in the product topology is called \textbf{coordinatewise convergence}, or \textbf{pointwise convergence}. The latter term is used most frequently in the case where all coordinate spaces are identical; in this case the Cartesian product $\bigwedge \{X : a \in A\}$ is the set of all functions on $A$ to $X$, denoted $X^A$. A net $\{F_n, n \in D\}$ in $X^A$ converges to $f$ in the topology of pointwise convergence iff the net $\{F_n(a), n \in D\}$ converges to $f(a)$ for each $a$ in $A$. The product topology is also called the \textbf{simple topology} in this case.
