Let $X$ be a $T_1$-space, let $\mathcal{F}$ be the family of all closed subsets of $X$, and let $w(X)$ be the collection of all subfamilies $\alpha \subset \mathcal{F}$ which possess the finite intersection property and are maximal in $\mathcal{F}$ relative to this property. The set $w(X)$ with the Wallman topology is the \textbf{Wallman compactification} of $X$.

For each $x \in X$, let $\phi(x) = \{A \in \mathcal{F} : x \in A\}$. Then $\phi$ is a one-to-one map of $X$ into $w(X)$.
