The Wallman topology on $w(X)$ is defined by taking the sets $U^*$ (maximal families containing a closed subset of $U$) as a base. This topology is always compact, and the map $U \mapsto U^*$ is a lattice isomorphism between the topology of $X$ and a base for the topology of $w(X)$. This property is key to the fact that the Wallman compactification preserves dimension and Cech homology groups of the original space.
