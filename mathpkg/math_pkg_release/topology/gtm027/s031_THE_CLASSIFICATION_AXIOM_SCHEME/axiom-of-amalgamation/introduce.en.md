The Axiom of Amalgamation (Union) states that the union of all members of a set is again a set. This axiom ensures that taking arbitrary unions of set-sized families yields a set. Together with the Axiom of Substitution, it guarantees that Cartesian products, disjoint unions, and many other constructions produce sets from sets.
