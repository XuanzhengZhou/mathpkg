# Proof of Well-Ordering Theorem (Zermelo)

PROOF If $x \in m, m \in n, y \in p$, and $p \in n$, then either $m \subset p$ or $p \subset m$ because $n$ is a nest. Suppose $m \subset p$. Then $x \in p$ and $y \in p$ and since $p$ is a nest, $x \subset y$ or $y \subset x$.

The following theorem is the Hausdorff maximal principle. It asserts the existence of a maximal nest in any set. The proof is closely related to that of 140.
