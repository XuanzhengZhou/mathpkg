The Axiom of Regularity (also called Foundation) asserts that every non-empty class contains an element disjoint from itself. This axiom prohibits circular membership ($x \in x$) and infinite descending $\in$-chains. It implies the universe of sets is well-founded and that every set has a rank in the cumulative hierarchy. Key consequences include $x \notin x$ (Theorem 101) and the impossibility of $x \in y \in x$ (Theorem 102).
