# Proof that Every Locally Compact Hausdorff Space is Regular

Let $X$ be a locally compact Hausdorff space. By Theorem 17, the family of closed compact neighborhoods of each point is a base for its neighborhood system. In particular, every point has a base of closed neighborhoods. A topological space in which every point has a base of closed neighborhoods is regular (provided it is $T_1$, which follows from Hausdorff). More directly: for any point $x$ and any neighborhood $U$ of $x$, Theorem 17 yields a closed compact neighborhood $V$ of $x$ contained in $U$, and in particular $V$ is a closed neighborhood of $x$. Since $x$ can be separated from any closed set not containing it by taking complements and applying this property, $X$ is regular.

(Note: This is the corollary following Theorem 17; a stronger statement — Theorem 18 — is proved immediately after.) $\square$
