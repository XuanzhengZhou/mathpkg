This proposition captures a fundamental fact in general topology: a continuous bijection from a compact space to a Hausdorff space is necessarily a homeomorphism. Applied to function spaces, if a topology $\mathfrak{j}$ on a family $F$ is finer than the pointwise topology, $F$ is compact in $\mathfrak{j}$, and Hausdorff in the pointwise topology, then the two topologies must coincide. This result explains why the pointwise topology, despite being very coarse, cannot be enlarged without losing compactness whenever the space remains Hausdorff. It is a key observation motivating the study of the compact-open topology, which is genuinely larger but at the cost of compactness.
