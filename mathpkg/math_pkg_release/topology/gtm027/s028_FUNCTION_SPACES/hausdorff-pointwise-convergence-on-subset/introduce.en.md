This theorem, numbered as Theorem 2 in Kelley's Chapter 7, relates the Hausdorff separation property of the topology $\wp_A$ to the distinguishing power of the subset $A$. The first part states that $\wp_A$ is Hausdorff precisely when $A$ can tell functions apart — a natural condition since the restriction map to $Y^A$ must be injective. The second part shows that when the full pointwise topology already yields a compact space and $A$ distinguishes members, the apparently coarser topology $\wp_A$ is actually identical to $\wp$. This follows from the maximality of compact Hausdorff topologies, as the identity map from the compact $\wp$ to the Hausdorff $\wp_A$ is a homeomorphism.
