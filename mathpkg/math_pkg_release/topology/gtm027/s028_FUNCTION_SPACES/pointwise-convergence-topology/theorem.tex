Let $F$ be a family of functions, each on a set $X$ to a topological space $Y$. Then $F$ is contained in the product space $Y^X = \prod \{Y_x : x \in X\}$ where each $Y_x = Y$. The \textbf{topology of pointwise convergence} (also called coordinatewise convergence or simple convergence), denoted $\wp$, is the relativized product topology on $F$.

A net $\{f_n, n \in D\}$ in $F$ converges to $g \in F$ relative to $\wp$ if and only if $\{f_n(x), n \in D\}$ converges to $g(x)$ in $Y$ for each $x \in X$.

A subbase for $\wp$ is the family of all sets of the form
$$\{f \in F : f(x) \in U\}$$
where $x \in X$ and $U$ is open in $Y$.
