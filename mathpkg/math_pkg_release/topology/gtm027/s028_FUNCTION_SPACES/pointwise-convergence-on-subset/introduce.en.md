The topology $\wp_A$ of pointwise convergence on a subset $A \subset X$ is a coarser relative of the full pointwise topology. It requires convergence only at points of $A$, ignoring behavior outside $A$. Formally, it is the initial topology induced by the restriction map $R : F \to Y^A$, and its subbasic open sets are those that constrain function values at points of $A$. When $A = X$, we recover the standard pointwise topology; when $A$ is smaller, the topology becomes coarser. This notion is essential for comparing topologies induced by different subsets of the domain.
