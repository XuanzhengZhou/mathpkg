Closed sets are precisely the complements of open sets. By De Morgan's laws, the family of closed sets is closed under arbitrary intersections and finite unions—dual to the axioms for open sets. In the usual topology on the reals, closed intervals are closed, while open and half-open intervals illustrate the subtleties of the open/closed distinction.
