An accumulation point captures the idea that there are points of $A$ arbitrarily close to $x$, excluding $x$ itself. The behavior varies dramatically across topologies: in an indiscrete space every point is an accumulation point of every nontrivial set, while in a discrete space no point is an accumulation point of any set. In the usual topology on the reals, the accumulation points of $(0,1)$ form the closed interval $[0,1]$.
