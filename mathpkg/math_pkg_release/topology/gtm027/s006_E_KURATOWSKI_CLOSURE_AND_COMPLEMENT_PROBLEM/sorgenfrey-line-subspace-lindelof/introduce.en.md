A remarkable property of the Sorgenfrey line is that the Lindelof property is hereditary: every subspace, without exception, is Lindelof. This is a strong property not shared by the usual real line (where uncountable discrete subspaces exist). The proof involves a clever argument about the structure of open covers in the half-open interval topology.
