This classification theorem for closed additive subgroups of $\mathbb{R}$ states that the only possibilities are countable subgroups (which must be discrete, like $\mathbb{Z}$) or the whole real line. There are no closed subgroups of intermediate uncountable proper size. This follows from the topological structure of $\mathbb{R}$ combined with the additive subgroup structure.
