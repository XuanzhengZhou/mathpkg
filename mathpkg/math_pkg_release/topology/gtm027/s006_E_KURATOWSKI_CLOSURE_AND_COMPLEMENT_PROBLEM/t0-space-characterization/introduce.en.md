The $T_0$ separation axiom, due to Alexandroff and Hopf, is the weakest of the classical separation axioms. A topological space is $T_0$ if for any two distinct points, the topology can distinguish them in the sense that one point possesses a neighborhood not containing the other. This is equivalent to requiring that distinct points have distinct closures of their singletons. Every $T_1$-space is a $T_0$-space, but the converse fails. The $T_0$ axiom is the first in the hierarchy $T_0$, $T_1$, $T_2$ (Hausdorff), $T_3$ (regular), etc.
