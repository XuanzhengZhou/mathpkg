The quotient space $X/R$ of a topological space $X$ by an equivalence relation $R$ is the set of equivalence classes equipped with the quotient topology. A set of equivalence classes is open exactly when its union (as a subset of $X$) is open. This construction formalizes the idea of "gluing" points of $X$ that are $R$-related, yielding new topological spaces such as the circle from an interval or the torus from a square.
