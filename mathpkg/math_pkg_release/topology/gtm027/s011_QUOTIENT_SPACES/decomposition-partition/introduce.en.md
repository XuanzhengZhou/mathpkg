A decomposition (or partition) of a set $X$ is a collection of pairwise disjoint, non-empty subsets whose union is exactly $X$. Every equivalence relation on $X$ induces a decomposition into equivalence classes, and conversely every decomposition defines an equivalence relation. In the context of quotient spaces, the decomposition consists of the fibers $f^{-1}[y]$ of a surjective map $f$.
