# Exercise F.4 — Lower Semi-Continuity and Oscillation

A real-valued function $g$ is called **lower semi-continuous** iff $-g$ is upper semi-continuous.

If $f$ is a bounded real-valued function, let $f_- = -(-f)^-$ and let the **oscillation** of $f$, $Q_f$, be defined by

$$Q_f(x) = f^-(x) - f_-(x)$$

for $x$ in $X$. Then $Q_f$ is upper semi-continuous, and $f$ is continuous iff $Q_f(x) = 0$ for all $x$ in $X$.
