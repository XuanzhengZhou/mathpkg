# Exercise D.1 — Continuity at a Point for Partial Functions into a Hausdorff Space

Let $f$ be defined on a subset $X_0$ of a topological space $X$ with values in a Hausdorff space $Y$; then $f$ is continuous at $x$ iff $x$ belongs to the closure of $X_0$ and for some member $y$ of the range the inverse of each neighborhood of $y$ is the intersection of $X_0$ and a neighborhood of $x$.

**(a)** A function $f$ is continuous at $x$ iff $x \in \overline{X}_0$ and whenever $S$ and $T$ are nets in $X_0$ converging to $x$ then $f \circ S$ and $f \circ T$ converge to the same point of $Y$.

**(b)** Let $X$ and $Y$ be topological spaces with $Y$ regular. If $f$ is defined on a dense subset $X_0$ of $X$ and is continuous on $X_0$, then $f$ has a continuous extension to $X$ iff for each $x$ in $X$, whenever $S$ and $T$ are nets in $X_0$ converging to $x$, then $f \circ S$ and $f \circ T$ converge to the same point of $Y$.
