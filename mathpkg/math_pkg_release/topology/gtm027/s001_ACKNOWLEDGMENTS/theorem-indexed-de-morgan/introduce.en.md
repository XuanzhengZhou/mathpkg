Theorem 3 extends the De Morgan laws from two sets to arbitrary indexed families. The first part states the monotonicity of union (expanding the index set enlarges the union) and intersection (expanding the index set shrinks the intersection). The second part gives the generalized De Morgan laws: the complement of a union is the intersection of the complements, and vice versa. These are ubiquitous in topology, especially when working with bases, subbases, and closed sets.
