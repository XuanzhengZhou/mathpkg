The inverse of a relation simply swaps the coordinates of every ordered pair. This operation is fundamental: symmetry of a relation is expressed as $R = R^{-1}$, and a function is one-to-one precisely when its inverse relation is also a function. The inverse relation interacts with composition as stated in Theorem 5: $(R \circ S)^{-1} = S^{-1} \circ R^{-1}$.
