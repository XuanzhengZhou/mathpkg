Anti-symmetry prevents distinct elements from mutually relating to each other. Combined with reflexivity and transitivity, it defines a partial order — the most important type of ordering in topology. Kelley's condition $R \cap R^{-1} = \varnothing$ (for the strict order) ensures that the relation has a direction: you cannot go both forward and backward unless you stay at the same element.
