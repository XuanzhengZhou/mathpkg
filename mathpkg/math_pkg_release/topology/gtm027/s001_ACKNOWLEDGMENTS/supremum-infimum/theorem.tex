Let $X$ be a set ordered by $<$ and let $A$ be a subset of $X$. An element $x$ of $X$ is a \textbf{supremum} (least upper bound) of $A$ if and only if $x$ is an upper bound of $A$ and $x \leq y$ for every upper bound $y$ of $A$. An element $x$ is an \textbf{infimum} (greatest lower bound) of $A$ if and only if $x$ is a lower bound of $A$ and $y \leq x$ for every lower bound $y$ of $A$.
