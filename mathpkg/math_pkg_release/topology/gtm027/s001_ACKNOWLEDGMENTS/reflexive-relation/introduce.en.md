A relation is reflexive if every element (in the union of its domain and range) relates to itself. The algebraic characterization $\Delta(X) \subset R$ makes reflexivity easy to check and manipulate. Reflexivity is one of the three defining properties of an equivalence relation, and reflexive orders (where $x \leq x$ always holds) are fundamental to topology, for instance in the specialization order of a topological space.
