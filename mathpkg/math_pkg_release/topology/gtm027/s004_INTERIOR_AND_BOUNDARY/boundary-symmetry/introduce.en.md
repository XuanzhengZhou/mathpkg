The boundary operator is symmetric under complementation: a set and its complement always share the exact same boundary. This follows immediately from the definition, since the condition of being interior to neither $A$ nor $X \sim A$ is unchanged when $A$ is swapped with its complement. Consequently, the boundary of $A$ and the boundary of $X \sim A$ coincide.
