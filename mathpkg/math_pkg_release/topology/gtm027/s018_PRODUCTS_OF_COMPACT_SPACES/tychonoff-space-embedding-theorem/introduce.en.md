This theorem characterizes Tychonoff (completely regular $T_1$) spaces as precisely those that embed homeomorphically into a compact Hausdorff space. In one direction, every Tychonoff space embeds into a cube (a product of closed unit intervals), which is compact Hausdorff by Tychonoff's theorem. Conversely, every compact Hausdorff space is normal and hence Tychonoff (by Urysohn's lemma), and subspaces of Tychonoff spaces remain Tychonoff. The result shows that Tychonoff spaces are exactly the subspaces of compact Hausdorff spaces.
