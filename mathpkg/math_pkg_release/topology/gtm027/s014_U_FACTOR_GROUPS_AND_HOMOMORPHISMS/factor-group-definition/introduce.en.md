When $H$ is not just a subgroup but an invariant (normal) subgroup of the topological group $G$, the coset space $G/H$ acquires a natural group structure: the product of two cosets $xH$ and $yH$ is defined as $(xy)H$. This turns $G/H$ into a group, called the factor group or quotient group. Combined with the quotient topology, this endows $G/H$ with the structure of a topological group, as shown in proposition (b) of this section.
