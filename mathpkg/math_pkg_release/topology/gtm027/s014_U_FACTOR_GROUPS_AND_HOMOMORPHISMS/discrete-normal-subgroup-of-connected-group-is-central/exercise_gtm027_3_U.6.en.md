# Exercise U(f): Discrete normal subgroup of a connected group is central

Let $G$ be a connected topological group and let $H$ be a discrete (with the relative topology) normal subgroup of $G$. Prove that $H$ is contained in the center $Z(G)$ of $G$.

*Hint:* For a fixed element $h \in H$, consider the map $\varphi : G \to H$ defined by $\varphi(x) = x^{-1} h x$. Show that $\varphi$ is continuous, so its image is connected. Since $H$ is discrete, the image must be a singleton.
