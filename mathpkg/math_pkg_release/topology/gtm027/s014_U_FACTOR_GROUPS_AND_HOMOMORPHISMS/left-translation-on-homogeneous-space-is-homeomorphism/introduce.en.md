For any fixed element $a$ of the topological group $G$, left multiplication by $a$ induces a map on the coset space $G/H$ sending a coset $A = xH$ to $a \cdot A = (ax)H$. This map is a homeomorphism of $G/H$ onto itself, with inverse given by left multiplication by $a^{-1}$. This property is precisely what makes $G/H$ a homogeneous space: the group $G$ acts transitively on $G/H$ by homeomorphisms.
