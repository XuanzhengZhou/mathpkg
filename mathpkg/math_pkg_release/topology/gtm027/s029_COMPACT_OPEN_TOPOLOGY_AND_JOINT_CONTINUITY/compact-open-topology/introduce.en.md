The compact-open topology is one of the most important function space topologies. Given a family $F$ of functions from a topological space $X$ to a topological space $Y$, the compact-open topology is generated by the subbasic sets $W(K,U) = \{f \in F : f[K] \subset U\}$, where $K$ ranges over compact subsets of $X$ and $U$ ranges over open subsets of $Y$. This topology is finer than the topology of pointwise convergence (since singletons are compact) and is closely related to the notion of joint continuity of the evaluation map $(f,x) \mapsto f(x)$.
