Let $F$ be a family of functions, each on a topological space $X$ to a topological space $Y$. The family $F$ is **evenly continuous** iff for each $x \in X$, each $y \in Y$, and each neighborhood $U$ of $y$ there exists a neighborhood $V$ of $x$ and a neighborhood $W$ of $y$ such that $f[V] \subset U$ whenever $f(x) \in W$. Equivalently, for each $x \in X$, $y \in Y$, and neighborhood $U$ of $y$, there are neighborhoods $V$ of $x$ and $W$ of $y$ such that
$$\{f \in F : f(x) \in W\} \times V$$
is carried into $U$ by the evaluation map $(f, z) \mapsto f(z)$.
