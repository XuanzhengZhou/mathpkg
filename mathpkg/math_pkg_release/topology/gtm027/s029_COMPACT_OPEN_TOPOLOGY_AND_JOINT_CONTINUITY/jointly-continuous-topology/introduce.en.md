Given a family $F$ of functions from a topological space $X$ to a topological space $Y$, a natural question is whether $f(x)$ is continuous simultaneously in $f$ and $x$. A topology on $F$ is called jointly continuous if the evaluation map $P(f,x) = f(x)$ is continuous with respect to the product topology on $F \times X$. The discrete topology is always jointly continuous for families of continuous functions, while the pointwise topology typically is not. The compact-open topology occupies a special place: it is generally the smallest topology that is jointly continuous on compacta.
