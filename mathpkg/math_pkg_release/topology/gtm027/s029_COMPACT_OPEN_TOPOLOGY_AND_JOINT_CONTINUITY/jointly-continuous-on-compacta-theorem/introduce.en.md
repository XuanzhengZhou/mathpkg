Theorem 7.5 establishes a fundamental relationship: every topology that is jointly continuous on compacta is larger than the compact-open topology $\mathcal{C}$. Conversely, if $X$ is regular or Hausdorff and each $f \in F$ is continuous on compacta, then $\mathcal{C}$ itself is jointly continuous on compacta. Consequently, when $X$ is locally compact and regular, the compact-open topology for continuous functions is precisely the smallest jointly continuous topology.
