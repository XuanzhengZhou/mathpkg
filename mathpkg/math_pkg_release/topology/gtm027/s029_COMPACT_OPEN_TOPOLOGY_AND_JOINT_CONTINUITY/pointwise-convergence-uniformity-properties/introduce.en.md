Theorem 7.3 describes the fundamental properties of the uniformity of pointwise convergence on a subset $A$. The subbase consists of entourages indexed by points of $A$ and uniform entourages of $Y$. The topology of this uniformity coincides with pointwise convergence on $A$. A net is Cauchy precisely when it is pointwise Cauchy, and completeness of $(Y,\mathcal{V})$ together with closedness of the restriction image $R[F]$ in $Y^A$ guarantees completeness of $F$ under $\sigma_A$.
