Theorem 7.14 states that equicontinuity at a point survives passage to the pointwise closure. The proof observes that for a closed member $V$ of the uniformity, the set $\{f : f[U] \subset V[f(x)]\}$ is pointwise closed (it is an intersection over $y \in U$ of sets $\{f : (f(y), f(x)) \in V\}$), so the closure of an equicontinuous family remains equicontinuous. Consequently, the pointwise closure of an equicontinuous family consists of continuous functions.
