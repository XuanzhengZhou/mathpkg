Let $F$ be a family of functions on a set $X$ to a uniform space $(Y, \mathcal{V})$. For each member $V$ of $\mathcal{V}$ let
$$W(V) = \{(f, g) \in F \times F : (f(x), g(x)) \in V \text{ for each } x \in X\}.$$
Then the family of all sets $W(V)$ for $V$ in $\mathcal{V}$ is a base for a uniformity $\mathfrak{U}$ for $F$, called the **uniformity of uniform convergence** (or the u.c. uniformity). The topology of $\mathfrak{U}$ is the topology of uniform convergence.
