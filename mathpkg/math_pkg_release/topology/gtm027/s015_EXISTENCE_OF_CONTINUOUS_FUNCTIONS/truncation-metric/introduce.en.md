The truncation metric $e(x,y) = \min(1, d(x,y))$ produces a new pseudo-metric that generates the same topology as the original while ensuring the entire space has diameter at most one. This construction shows that boundedness in the metric sense is not a topological invariant. It is technically useful when studying products of pseudo-metric spaces, as it allows one to assume all factor spaces have diameter at most one.
