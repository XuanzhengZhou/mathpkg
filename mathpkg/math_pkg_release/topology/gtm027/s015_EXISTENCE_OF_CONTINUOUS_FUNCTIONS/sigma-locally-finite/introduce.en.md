A family is $\sigma$-locally finite if it is a countable union of locally finite subfamilies. This is the key condition in the Nagata-Smirnov metrization theorem: a $T_1$-space is metrizable if and only if it is regular and has a $\sigma$-locally finite base. The $\sigma$-discrete variant (countable union of discrete families) is equivalent by Bing's theorem.
