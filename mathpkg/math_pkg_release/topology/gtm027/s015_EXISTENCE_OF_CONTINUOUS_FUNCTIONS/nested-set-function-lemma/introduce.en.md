This lemma provides a method for constructing a real-valued function from a nested family of sets indexed by a dense subset of the positive reals. By taking the infimum of indices whose corresponding set contains a given point, one obtains a function whose sub-level sets are precisely the unions of the earlier-indexed sets. This technique is central to the proof of Urysohn's lemma, where the $F_t$ are open sets separating two closed sets.
