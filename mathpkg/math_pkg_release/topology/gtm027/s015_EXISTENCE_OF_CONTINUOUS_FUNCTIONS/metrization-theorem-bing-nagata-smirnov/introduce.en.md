The Bing-Nagata-Smirnov Metrization Theorem provides a definitive characterization of metrizable spaces. A $T_1$-space admits a metric compatible with its topology precisely when it is regular and possesses a base that decomposes into countably many locally finite (or, equivalently, discrete) subfamilies. A family is locally finite if each point has a neighborhood intersecting only finitely many members; it is discrete if each point has a neighborhood intersecting at most one member. This theorem subsumes the Urysohn metrization theorem and represents the culmination of the classical program to characterize metrizability in purely topological terms.
