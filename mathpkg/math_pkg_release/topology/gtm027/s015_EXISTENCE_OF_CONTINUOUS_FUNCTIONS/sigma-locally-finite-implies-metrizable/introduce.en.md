This is the sufficiency direction of the Bing-Nagata-Smirnov metrization theorem: any regular $T_1$-space admitting a base that is the countable union of locally finite families must be metrizable. The proof constructs an embedding into a metrizable product space using locally finite refinements and Urysohn-type functions, or equivalently constructs a metric directly via a technique of assigning "weights" to basic open sets. This result generalizes Urysohn's theorem and provides the definitive sufficient condition for metrizability.
