Let $\Omega_0$ be the set of all ordinal numbers less than the first uncountable ordinal $\Omega$, endowed with the order topology. Let $A \subset \Omega_0$ be a closed uncountable set whose complement $\Omega_0 \setminus A$ is also uncountable. Let $\Omega$ be the decomposition of $\Omega_0$ whose members are the set $A$ and all singletons $\{x\}$ for $x \in \Omega_0 \setminus A$. Then the natural projection $\pi : \Omega_0 \to \Omega_0/\Omega$ is continuous and closed, and the quotient space $\Omega_0/\Omega$ is compact, but it fails to satisfy the first axiom of countability.
