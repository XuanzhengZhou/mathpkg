This example demonstrates that a closed continuous quotient map of a nice space (the real line with its usual topology) can produce a pathological quotient space. By collapsing the integers to a single point while leaving all other points untouched, the resulting quotient space fails to be locally compact and fails the first axiom of countability, even though the projection map itself is both closed and continuous. This counterexample illustrates that quotient maps do not preserve local compactness or first countability in general.
