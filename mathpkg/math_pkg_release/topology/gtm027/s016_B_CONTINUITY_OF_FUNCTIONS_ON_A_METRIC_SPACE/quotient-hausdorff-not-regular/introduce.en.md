This proposition provides a counterexample showing that regularity is not preserved by quotient maps. By collapsing a closed subset $A$ of a suitable space $X$ to a single point, one obtains a quotient space that satisfies the Hausdorff separation axiom but fails to be regular. This demonstrates that the quotient operation can destroy regularity even when it preserves the Hausdorff property.
