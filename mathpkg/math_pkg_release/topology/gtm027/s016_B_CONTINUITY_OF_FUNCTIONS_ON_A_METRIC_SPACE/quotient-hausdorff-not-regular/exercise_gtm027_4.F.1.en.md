# Exercise F.1 — A Quotient Space That is Hausdorff But Not Regular

Using the Tychonoff Plank $X = (\Omega' \times \Omega') \setminus \{(\Omega, \Omega)\}$, let $A = \Omega_0 \times \{\Omega\}$ and define an equivalence relation $R$ on $X$ by identifying all points of $A$ to a single point. Prove that the quotient space $X/R$ is Hausdorff but not regular. (The members of the quotient space are the equivalence class $[A]$, and singletons $\{x\}$ for $x \in X \setminus A$.)
