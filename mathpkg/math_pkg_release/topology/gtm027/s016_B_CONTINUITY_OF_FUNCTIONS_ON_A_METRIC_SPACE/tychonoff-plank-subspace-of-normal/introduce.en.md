The Tychonoff Plank is the classic counterexample demonstrating that normality is not a hereditary property. Specifically, there exists a normal topological space (the product of two ordinal spaces) that contains a subspace which fails to be normal. This example shows that the separation axiom of normality, unlike some weaker axioms, does not automatically pass to subspaces.
