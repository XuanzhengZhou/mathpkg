This theorem states that any expanding function $f$ on the ordinal space $\Omega_0$ (satisfying $f(x) \geq x$) has the property that the diagonal point $(x, x)$ is an accumulation point of its graph for some $x$. The proof uses the interlacing lemma applied to a sequence defined inductively by $x_{n+1} = f(x_n)$, yielding a fixed-point-like result in the order topology. This theorem is used to establish that the product $\Omega_0 \times \Omega'$ is not normal.
