# Exercise F.2 — A Quotient Space That Is Not Hausdorff

Let $X$ be the Tychonoff Plank, and let $A = \Omega_0 \times \{\Omega\}$, $B = \{(x, x) : x \in \Omega_0\}$. Define the equivalence relation
$$S = \Delta \cup (A \times A) \cup (B \times B) \subset X \times X,$$
where $\Delta$ is the diagonal. Prove that $S$ is closed in $X \times X$, but the quotient space $X/S$ is not Hausdorff. (The members of $X/S$ are the equivalence classes $[A]$, $[B]$, and $\{x\}$ for each $x \in X \setminus (A \cup B)$.)
