This is the key theorem establishing Gelfand duality: every unit-preserving algebra homomorphism $H: C(Y) \to C(X)$ between function algebras of compact Hausdorff spaces is of the form $H = F^*$ for a uniquely determined continuous map $F: X \to Y$. The proof proceeds by observing that $H$ induces a map between the spaces of real homomorphisms (maximal ideal spaces), which correspond to the points of $X$ and $Y$.
