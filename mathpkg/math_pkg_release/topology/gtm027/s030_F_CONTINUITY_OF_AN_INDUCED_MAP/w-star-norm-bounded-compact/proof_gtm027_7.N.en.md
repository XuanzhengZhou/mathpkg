# Proof of Weak-Star Compactness of Norm Bounded Closed Sets

From the Alaoglu theorem: the unit sphere $S^* = \{f \in X^* : \|f\| \leq 1\}$ is $w^*$-compact. For any norm-bounded $w^*$-closed set $F \subseteq X^*$, there exists $M > 0$ such that $\|f\| \leq M$ for all $f \in F$. Then $F \subseteq M \cdot S^*$, and $M \cdot S^*$ is $w^*$-compact (scaling is a $w^*$-homeomorphism). Since $F$ is $w^*$-closed in $X^*$ and contained in the $w^*$-compact set $M \cdot S^*$, $F$ is $w^*$-compact.
