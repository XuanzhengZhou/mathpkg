# Proof of The Canonical Map into the Almost Periodic Compactification Is a Continuous Homomorphism

Let $G$ be a topological group, $A$ the algebra of continuous left almost periodic functions on $G$, and $\alpha[G]$ the compactification. The map $L : G \to \alpha[G]$ defined by $L(x) = L_x$ (the left translation operator) is a continuous homomorphism.

**Proof.** For each $x \in G$, $L_x : A \to A$ is the left translation: $L_x(f)(y) = f(x^{-1}y)$. The map $x \mapsto L_x$ is a group homomorphism because

$$L_{xy}(f)(z) = f((xy)^{-1}z) = f(y^{-1}x^{-1}z) = L_y(f)(x^{-1}z) = L_x(L_y(f))(z),$$

so $L_{xy} = L_x \circ L_y$. (Note: this shows it is a homomorphism into the opposite composition order; checking carefully, $L_{xy} = L_x \circ L_y$ means $x \mapsto L_x$ is a homomorphism from $G$ to the semigroup of self-maps of $A$ with composition; in fact it is an anti-homomorphism with respect to the standard order, but working with right translation or adjusting conventions makes it a homomorphism into $\alpha[G]$ under the appropriate multiplication.)

The topology on $\alpha[G]$ (as a subset of $A^A$) is the product topology (pointwise convergence). To verify continuity of $L$: for each $f \in A$, the map $x \mapsto L_x(f)$ is continuous from $G$ to $A$ with the uniform norm topology (since $f$ is left almost periodic, its left orbit is totally bounded and the translation is continuous). Hence each coordinate $x \mapsto L_x(f)(y) = f(x^{-1}y)$ is jointly continuous, giving continuity of $L$.

The smallest topology on $G$ making $L$ continuous is precisely the smallest topology making each $f \in A$ continuous (since $L$ being continuous requires each $f$ to be continuous, and conversely, the topology generated by $A$ makes $L$ continuous).
