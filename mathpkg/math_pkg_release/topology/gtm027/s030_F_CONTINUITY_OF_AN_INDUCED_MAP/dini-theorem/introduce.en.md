Dini's theorem states that for a monotonically increasing net of continuous real-valued functions converging pointwise to a continuous limit, the convergence is automatically uniform on every compact subset. The proof uses a compactness argument: for a compact set $C$, the sets $A_n = \{(x,y) : x \in C, f_n(x) \leq y \leq f(x)\}$ are considered, and their intersection is shown to be precisely the graph of $f$ restricted to $C$.
