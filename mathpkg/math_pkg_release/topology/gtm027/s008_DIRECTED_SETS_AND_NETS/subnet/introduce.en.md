A subnet generalizes the notion of a subsequence to nets. It consists of a new net indexed by a different directed set, obtained by composing the original net with a function from the new index set to the original one. Condition (b) ensures that the new index set contains arbitrarily large elements in the sense of the original directed set. The key property is that if a net is eventually in a set, every subnet is also eventually in that set. The simplest subnets are obtained by restricting a net to a cofinal subset of its index domain.
