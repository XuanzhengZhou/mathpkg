A subset $A$ of a topological space $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ is \textbf{closed} iff its relative complement $X \sim A$ is open. The complement of the complement of the set $A$ is again $A$, and hence a set is open iff its complement is closed.

The space and the void set are always closed as well as open. The union of a finite number of closed sets is necessarily closed and the intersection of the members of an arbitrary family of closed sets is closed.
