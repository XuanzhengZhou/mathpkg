The derived set of $A$, often denoted $A'$, is the set of all accumulation points of $A$. This terminology originates with Cantor and is fundamental to the Cantor-Bendixson theory of perfect sets and the classification of closed sets in Polish spaces. The derived set operator is monotone and satisfies $A'' \subseteq A'$, though it need not be idempotent in general topological spaces.
