The \textbf{closure} of a subset $A$ of a topological space $(X, \mathfrak{J})$ is the intersection of the members of the family of all closed sets containing $A$. The closure of $A$ is denoted by $A^{-}$, or by $\overline{A}$. The set $A^{-}$ is always closed because it is the intersection of closed sets, and $A^{-}$ is contained in every closed set which contains $A$. Consequently $A^{-}$ is the smallest closed set containing $A$ and it follows that $A$ is closed if and only if $A = A^{-}$.
