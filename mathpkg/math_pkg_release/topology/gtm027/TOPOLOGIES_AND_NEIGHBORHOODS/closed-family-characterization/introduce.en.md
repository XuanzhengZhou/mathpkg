This theorem provides an axiomatic description of closed sets, dual to the definition of a topology via open sets. Any family of subsets closed under finite unions, arbitrary nonempty intersections, and containing the whole space defines a unique topology by taking complements. This establishes that one can equivalently define a topological space by specifying its closed sets rather than its open sets.
