A uniformity on a set $ can be defined equivalently in terms of coverings rather than entourages. A collection $\Phi$ of covers satisfying three axioms—pairwise refinement, star refinement, and upward closure under refinement—defines a uniformity $\mathcal{U}$ via the diagonal neighborhoods $\bigcup\{A \times A : A \in \alpha\}$. Conversely, $\Phi$ recovers exactly the family of all uniform covers relative to $\mathcal{U}$. This covering approach was pioneered by Tukey and earlier by Alexandroff and Urysohn.
