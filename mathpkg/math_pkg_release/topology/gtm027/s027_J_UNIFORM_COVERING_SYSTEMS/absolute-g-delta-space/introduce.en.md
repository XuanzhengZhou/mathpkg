An absolute \delta$ space is a metrizable space that, whenever embedded in any metric space, appears as a countable intersection of open subsets. This property characterizes precisely those metrizable spaces that are metrically topologically complete. The equivalence between metric topological completeness and being an absolute \delta$ is a classical result due to Alexandroff and Hausdorff.
