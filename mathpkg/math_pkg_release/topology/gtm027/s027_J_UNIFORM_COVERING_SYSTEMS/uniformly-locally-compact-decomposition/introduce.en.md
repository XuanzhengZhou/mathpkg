Every uniformly locally compact space decomposes into a disjoint union of open $\sigmaehBcompact subspaces. As an immediate corollary, uniformly locally compact spaces are always paracompact. This decomposition result shows that uniform local compactness is a strong condition that forces the space to be a topological sum of reasonably 'small' pieces, each expressible as a countable union of compact sets.
