Every member $ of the $\sigmaehBalgebra $\mathcal{Q}$ generated by the compact open sets $\mathcal{B}$ can be represented uniquely modulo meager sets by a compact open set $. That is, the symmetric difference  \Delta B$ is meager. This representation theorem is a category-theoretic analogue of the measure-theoretic fact that measurable sets are approximated by open sets modulo null sets, and it relies on the Baire category theorem applied to the Stone space.
