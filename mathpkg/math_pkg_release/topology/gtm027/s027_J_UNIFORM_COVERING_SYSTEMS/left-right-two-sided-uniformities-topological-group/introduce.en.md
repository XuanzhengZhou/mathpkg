Every topological group carries three natural uniformities. The left uniformity is generated by entourages  = \{(x,y): x^{-1}y \in U\}$; the right uniformity uses  = \{(x,y): xy^{-1} \in U\}$; and the two-sided uniformity is the supremum of both. These uniformities encode the uniform structure of the group operations and are essential for studying completeness and completion of topological groups.
