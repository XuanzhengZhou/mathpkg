A family of vector spaces over a smooth manifold $X$ is a smooth manifold $E$ equipped with a surjective submersion $\pi: E \to X$ such that each fiber $E_p = \pi^{-1}(p)$ carries the structure of a real vector space of fixed dimension $k$, and the vector space operations vary continuously with respect to the topology inherited from $E$. This definition serves as a precursor to the full notion of a vector bundle, where one additionally requires local triviality.
