Let $\pi_E: E \rightarrow X$ and $\pi_F: F \rightarrow X$ be families of vector spaces over $X$. Then $\phi: E \rightarrow F$ is a \textbf{homomorphism} from $E$ to $F$ if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\pi_F \circ \phi = \pi_E$,
\item[(b)] $\phi$ is smooth,
\item[(c)] For every $p \in X$, $\phi|_{E_p}: E_p \rightarrow F_p$ is a linear map.
\end{enumerate}

$\phi$ is an \textbf{isomorphism} if $\phi$ is a diffeomorphism and a homomorphism.
