Lemma D deals with the deep tail of the stratification: points where all partial derivatives up to order $k$ vanish, for a sufficiently large $k$. Choosing $k > \frac{n}{m} - 1$ (or more precisely, $k+1 > n/m$), Taylor's Theorem gives a tight bound $|f(y) - f(x)| \leq K|x-y|^{k+1}$ for $x \in C_k$. Subdividing the domain $U$ into small cubes and using this estimate, the image of each subcube intersecting $C_k$ is contained in a ball of disproportionately small radius. Summing volumes shows that $f(C_k)$ can be covered by sets of arbitrarily small total volume, hence it has measure zero.
