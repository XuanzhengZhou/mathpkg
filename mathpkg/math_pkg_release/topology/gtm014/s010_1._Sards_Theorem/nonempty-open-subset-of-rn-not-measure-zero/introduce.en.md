Any nonempty open subset of $\mathbf{R}^n$ is not of measure zero. This "obvious but surprisingly complicated" result is proved by finding a compact cube inside the open set and bounding its volume away from zero using the volume of any open cube covering, via counting integer lattice points. This contrasts with the fact that individual points and many fractal-like sets are of measure zero.
