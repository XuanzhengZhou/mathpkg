Exercise 3 from Chapter VII, Section 4. This exercise asks to construct a topological immersion of the real projective plane $\mathbf{P}^2$ into $\mathbf{R}^3$ whose image is a cross-cap. This is a classical problem in topology related to the immersion of $\mathbf{P}^2$ in $\mathbf{R}^3$, connected to Boy's surface and the fact that $\mathbf{P}^2$ cannot be embedded in $\mathbf{R}^3$ without self-intersection.
