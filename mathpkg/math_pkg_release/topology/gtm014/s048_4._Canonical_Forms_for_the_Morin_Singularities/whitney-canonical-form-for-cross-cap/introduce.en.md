Theorem 4.6 gives Whitney's canonical form for cross-cap singularities of maps from $n$-manifolds to $(2n-1)$-manifolds. At a cross-cap point $x_0$, coordinates can be chosen so that $f^*y_i = x_i$ for $i = 2, \ldots, n$, with the singularity set $S_1(f)$ characterized by the simultaneous vanishing of certain partial derivatives. The theorem also characterizes the jet transversality condition $j^1 f \pitchfork S_1$ in terms of linear independence of differentials of those partial derivatives.
