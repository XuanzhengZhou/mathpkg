The $S_r$ classification provides a rudimentary stratification of the singular set of a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ by the corank of its differential. At a point $x \in X$, the map $f$ has an $S_r$ singularity if the differential $(df)_x$ drops rank by exactly $r$. The set $S_r(f)$ is the preimage under the 1-jet map $j^1 f$ of the submanifold $S_r \subset J^1(X,Y)$ consisting of 1-jets of corank $r$, first introduced in the proof of the Whitney Immersion Theorem. This concept is the starting point of the Thom-Boardman theory of singularities.
