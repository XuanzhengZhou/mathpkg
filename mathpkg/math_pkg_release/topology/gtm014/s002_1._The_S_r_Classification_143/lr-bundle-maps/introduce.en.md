$L^r(E, F)$ generalizes the space of corank-$r$ linear maps from the vector space setting to the setting of vector bundles. Given two vector bundles $E \to X$ and $F \to Y$, the fiber over $(x, y)$ consists of linear maps $E_x \to F_y$ whose corank equals $r$. This construction is needed to formulate the $S_r$ submanifolds in jet bundles: the 1-jet bundle $J^1(X, Y)$ is isomorphic to the vector bundle $\operatorname{Hom}(TX, TY)$, and $S_r \subset J^1(X, Y)$ is precisely $L^r(TX, TY)$. Proposition 1.4 applied fiberwise to vector bundles (replacing vector spaces) proves that $S_r$ is a submanifold of $J^1(X, Y)$.
