Let $F$ be a smooth real-valued function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$ such that
$$F(t, 0) = g(t)t^k$$
where $g(0) \neq 0$ and $g$ is smooth on some neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R}$. Then given any smooth real-valued function $G$ defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$, there exist smooth functions $q$ and $r$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G = qF + r$ on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$, and
\item $r(t, x) = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} r_i(x)t^i$ for $(t, x) \in \mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$ near $0$.
\end{enumerate}
