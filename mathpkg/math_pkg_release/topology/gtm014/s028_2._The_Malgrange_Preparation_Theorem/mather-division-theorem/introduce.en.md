The Mather Division Theorem is a fundamental result in singularity theory that generalizes the Weierstrass Division Theorem from the analytic category to the smooth ($C^\infty$) category. Given a smooth function $F(t, x)$ whose restriction to $x = 0$ has a zero of order $k$ in $t$, any other smooth function $G$ can be divided by $F$ with a remainder that is a polynomial in $t$ of degree at most $k-1$ with coefficients depending smoothly on $x$. Unlike the complex analytic case, the quotient and remainder are not unique in the smooth category.
