The Borel Theorem states that any prescribed sequence of smooth functions $\{f_k\}$ can be realized as the Taylor coefficients (in $t$) of some smooth function $F(t, x)$. The construction uses a smooth bump function $\rho$ and a rapidly increasing sequence $\mu_k$ to ensure convergence of all derivatives. This is a crucial technical tool for the smooth Malgrange Preparation Theorem, replacing power series arguments from the complex case.
