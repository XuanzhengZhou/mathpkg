Let $V$ and $W$ be linear subspaces of $\mathbf{R}^n$ such that $V + W = \mathbf{R}^n$. Let $g$ be a smooth function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $V$, and $h$ a smooth function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $W$, such that $g$ and $h$ agree to infinite order on $V \cap W$ (i.e., all partial derivatives of $g$ and $h$ coincide on $V \cap W$). Then there exists a smooth function $F$ defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R}^n$ such that for all multi-indices $\alpha$,
$$\frac{\partial^{|\alpha|} F}{\partial x^\alpha}(x) = \begin{cases}
\dfrac{\partial^{|\alpha|} g}{\partial x^\alpha}(x) & \text{if } x \in V,\\[8pt]
\dfrac{\partial^{|\alpha|} h}{\partial x^\alpha}(x) & \text{if } x \in W.
\end{cases}$$
