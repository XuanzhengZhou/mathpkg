The Polynomial Division Theorem is the key technical result used to prove the Mather Division Theorem. It establishes that any smooth function $G(t, x)$ can be divided by the generic monic polynomial $P_k(t, \lambda) = t^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \lambda_i t^i$ with a quotient and remainder that depend smoothly on all variables including the coefficients $\lambda$. The proof uses a generalized Cauchy integral formula (Lemma 2.3) and a delicate extension argument (Proposition 2.4) to handle the zeroes of $P_k$. Unlike the Weierstrass case, the division holds with parameters $\lambda$.
