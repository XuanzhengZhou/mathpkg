Let $F$ be a smooth real-valued function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$ such that
$$F(t, 0) = g(t)t^k$$
where $g(0) \neq 0$ and $g$ is smooth on some neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R}$. Then there exists a smooth function $q$ with $q(0) \neq 0$ such that
$$(qF)(t, x) = t^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \lambda_i(x) t^i$$
on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$, where each $\lambda_i$ is a smooth function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbf{R}^n$.
