This lemma generalizes the Cauchy Integral Formula to smooth (not necessarily holomorphic) functions using Green's Theorem. For a holomorphic function, the second (area) term vanishes since $\partial F / \partial \bar{z} = 0$, recovering the classical Cauchy formula. The area integral involving $\partial F / \partial \bar{z}$ measures the failure of $F$ to be holomorphic. This formula (also known as the Pompeiu formula) is essential for the proof of the Polynomial Division Theorem, where one must work with merely smooth extensions of functions into the complex domain.
