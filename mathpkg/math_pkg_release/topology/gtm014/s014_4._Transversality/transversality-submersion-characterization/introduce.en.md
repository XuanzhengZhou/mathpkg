This lemma gives a convenient local characterization of transversality: when a submanifold $W \subset Y$ is locally cut out as the zero set of a submersion $\phi$, then $f$ is transversal to $W$ at $p$ precisely when the composition $\phi \circ f$ is a submersion at $p$. This follows because $\operatorname{Ker}(d\phi)_{f(p)} = T_{f(p)}W$, so the transversality condition $T_{f(p)}Y = T_{f(p)}W + (df)_p(T_p X)$ reduces to checking that $(d(\phi \circ f))_p$ is surjective. Such a neighborhood $U$ with submersion $\phi$ always exists by choosing a suitable coordinate chart.
