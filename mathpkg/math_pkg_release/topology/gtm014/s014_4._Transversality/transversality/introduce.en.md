Transversality is a fundamental concept in differential topology that generalizes the notion of a smooth map intersecting a submanifold "in general position." A smooth map $f: X \to Y$ is transversal to a submanifold $W \subset Y$ at a point $x$ if either $f(x)$ misses $W$, or the tangent space of $Y$ at $f(x)$ is spanned by the tangent space of $W$ together with the image of the derivative of $f$ at $x$. When the dimensions satisfy $\dim X < \operatorname{codim} W$, transversality forces $f(X) \cap W = \varnothing$, and in general the set of transversal maps is large (a residual set in the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology), as formalized by the Thom Transversality Theorem.
