This definition refines the notion of transversality by restricting attention to a subset $W'$ of the submanifold $W$. A map $f$ is transversal to $W$ on $W'$ if the transversality condition holds at every point of $X$ whose image lies in $W'$. This localized version is useful when working with compact subsets $\overline{W}_r$ in the proof of the Thom Transversality Theorem, where transversality on each compact piece is shown to be an open and dense condition.
