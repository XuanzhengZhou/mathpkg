This theorem generalizes Proposition 4.3 from infinitesimal stability at a single point to a finite set $S = \{p_1, \ldots, p_k\}$. The key idea is to decompose the module $A_F^S$ as a direct sum $A_F^{p_1} \oplus \cdots \oplus A_F^{p_k}$ and apply Lemma 1.4 in place of the Malgrange Preparation Theorem. When $S$ is a single point, the statement reduces to Proposition 4.3. This result is a crucial intermediate step in Chapter IV toward proving the global theorem that infinitesimal stability implies stability.
