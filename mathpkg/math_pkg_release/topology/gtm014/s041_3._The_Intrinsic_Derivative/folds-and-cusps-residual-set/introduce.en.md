For smooth maps between 2-dimensional manifolds, Whitney's theorem asserts that a generic smooth map has only two types of singularities: folds and simple cusps. More precisely, the set of maps in $C^{\infty}(X, Y)$ whose only singularities are folds and simple cusps contains a residual subset (a countable intersection of open dense sets). This result is a special case of the more general Thom-Boardman theory developed later in this chapter, and its proof is deferred to the study of $S_{r,s}$ singularities in §4.
