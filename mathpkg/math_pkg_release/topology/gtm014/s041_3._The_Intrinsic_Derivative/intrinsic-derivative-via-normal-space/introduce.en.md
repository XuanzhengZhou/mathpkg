This proposition establishes the equivalence between the two definitions of the intrinsic derivative given in §3. The first, "pedestrian" definition uses arbitrary local trivializations of the vector bundles $E$ and $F$. The second, more invariant definition views $\rho$ as a map into $\operatorname{Hom}(E, F)$ and uses the canonical identification of the normal space $N_\sigma$ to $L^r(E, F)$ with $\operatorname{Hom}(K_\sigma, L_\sigma)$. Proposition 3.6 confirms that composing the derivative $(d\rho)_x$ with the projection onto the normal space yields exactly the same map as the original intrinsic derivative construction.
