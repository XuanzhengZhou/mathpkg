Proposition 3.7 relates the surjectivity of the intrinsic derivative at a point to the transversality of the vector bundle map $\rho$, viewed as a section of $\operatorname{Hom}(E, F)$, to the submanifold $L^r(E, F)$ of homomorphisms of corank $r$. This equivalence follows directly from Proposition 3.6: since the intrinsic derivative factors through the normal space $N_\sigma \cong \operatorname{Hom}(K_\sigma, L_\sigma)$, surjectivity of $(D\rho)_x$ is exactly the condition that $(d\rho)_x(T_x X) + T_\sigma L^r(E, F) = T_\sigma \operatorname{Hom}(E, F)$, which is the definition of transversality.
