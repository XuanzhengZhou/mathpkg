Clearly $\mathring{G} \cdot p$ is connected (as the continuous image of the connected set $\mathring{G}$). If $G'$ is any other connected component of $G$, then $G' \cdot p \cap \mathring{G} \cdot p = \varnothing$ or $G' \cdot p = \mathring{G} \cdot p$. Thus $\mathring{G} \cdot p$ is both open and closed in $\mathcal{O}_p$ (in the topology induced from $G/H_p$) and is therefore a connected component of $\mathcal{O}_p$. Clearly $p \in \mathring{G} \cdot p$.
