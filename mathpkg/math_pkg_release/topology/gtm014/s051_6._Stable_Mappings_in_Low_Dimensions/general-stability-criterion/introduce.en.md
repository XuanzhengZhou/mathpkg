Theorem 6.4 is the general result underlying the low-dimensional stability characterization of Theorem 6.3. It states that for smooth maps $f: X \to Y$ between $n$-dimensional manifolds that exhibit only Boardman-type singularities transversely, stability is equivalent to the normal crossing condition. The proof, sketched in the text for the case of two preimage points, analyzes the tangent spaces to singularity strata and their images, showing that the general position of image subspaces (guaranteed by Condition NC) allows simultaneous canonical coordinate choices, from which infinitesimal stability follows.
