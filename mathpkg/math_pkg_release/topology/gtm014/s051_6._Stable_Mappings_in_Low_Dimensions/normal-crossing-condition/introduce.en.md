The normal crossing condition (Condition NC) is a global geometric constraint on the singular set of a smooth map $f: X \to Y$. Given distinct points $x_{i_r}$ in singularity strata $X_{i_r}$ that share the same image $y$, the condition requires that the pushforwards of their tangent spaces $(df)_{x_{i_r}}(T_{x_{i_r}} X_{i_r})$ are in general position in $T_y Y$. This implies that $f$ restricted to each stratum is an immersion with normal crossings and that the images of different strata intersect transversely. For instance, a point cannot simultaneously be the image of an umbilic and an $S_1$ singularity.
