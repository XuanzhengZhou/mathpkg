Condition NC is a global criterion for stability. Given a map $f: X \to Y$ with $\dim X = \dim Y \leq 4$ that exhibits only the singularities on the list (6.1) and exhibits them transversely, the stability of $f$ is equivalent to the normal crossing condition. Specifically, for distinct points $x_{i_r}$ in the strata $X_{i_r}$ with common image $y$, the subspaces $(df)_{x_{i_r}}(T_{x_{i_r}} X_{i_r})$ must be in general position in $T_y Y$.

This condition implies that $f$ restricted to each stratum is an immersion with normal crossings, and that the images of different strata intersect transversely. For example, it forbids a point from being simultaneously the image of an umbilic and of an $S_1$ singularity. The proof verifies that under Condition NC, the Mather infinitesimal stability criterion is satisfied. For $S_{1_k}$ singularities, one chooses canonical coordinates (4.2) and checks that the tangent space and image space conditions from Chapter VI, Section 5 are satisfied. The general position of the image subspaces translates into the linear independence of the corresponding differentials, which in turn yields the surjectivity required for infinitesimal stability.
