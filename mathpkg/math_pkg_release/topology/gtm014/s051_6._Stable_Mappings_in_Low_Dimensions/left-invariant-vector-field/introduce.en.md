A left-invariant vector field on a Lie group $G$ is one that is preserved under all left translations: pushing $\zeta$ forward by the derivative of left multiplication $L_a$ recovers $\zeta$ itself. Given any tangent vector $v \in T_e G$ at the identity, one obtains a unique left-invariant vector field $\zeta^v$ by defining $\zeta^v_a = (dL_a)_e(v)$. These vector fields form a finite-dimensional Lie algebra isomorphic to $T_e G$ and provide the infinitesimal generators of one-parameter subgroups.
