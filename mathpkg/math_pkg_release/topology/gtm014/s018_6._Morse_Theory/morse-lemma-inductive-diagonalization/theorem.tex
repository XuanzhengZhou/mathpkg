Let $U$ be an open convex neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$ and let $f: U \to \mathbb{R}$ be a smooth function with a nondegenerate critical point at $0$. Assume that
$$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_i \partial x_j}(0) = \pm \delta_{ij}.$$
Then there exists a neighborhood $V$ of $0$ contained in $U$ and smooth functions $h_i: V \to \mathbb{R}$ ($1 \leq i \leq n$) satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] $h_i(0) = 0$ and $\displaystyle \frac{\partial h_i}{\partial x_j}(0) = \pm \delta_{ij}$;
  \item[(b)] $\displaystyle f(x) = f(0) + \bigl(\pm h_1^2(x) \pm \cdots \pm h_n^2(x)\bigr)$ for all $x \in V$.
\end{enumerate}
