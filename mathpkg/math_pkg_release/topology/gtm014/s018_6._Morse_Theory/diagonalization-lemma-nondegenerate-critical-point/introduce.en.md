This lemma diagonalizes a smooth function near a nondegenerate critical point whose Hessian is already in diagonal form $\pm I$. By induction on $r = 1, \ldots, n$, one constructs smooth functions $h_i$ such that $f(x) = f(0) + \sum \pm h_i^2(x)$, with $h_i(0) = 0$ and $\partial h_i/\partial x_j(0) = \pm \delta_{ij}$. The proof uses a clever algebraic manipulation of the quadratic expansion together with a completing-the-square technique, and is the main technical step in establishing the Morse Lemma.
