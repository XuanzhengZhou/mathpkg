The intrinsic derivative (or invariant Hessian) $(d^2 f)_p$ is the coordinate-free definition of the second derivative of a smooth function $f: X \to \mathbb{R}$ at a critical point $p$. Unlike the ordinary Hessian matrix which depends on a choice of coordinates, the intrinsic second derivative is defined as a symmetric bilinear form on the tangent space $T_p X$ using only the smooth structure. It is a fundamental tool in Morse theory, allowing the classification of critical points on manifolds without reference to local coordinates.
