A nondegenerate critical point of a smooth function $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ is a point where the gradient vanishes and the Hessian matrix is invertible. This is the fundamental concept underlying Morse theory: near a nondegenerate critical point, the function behaves like a nondegenerate quadratic form, and the index of the Hessian (number of negative eigenvalues) classifies the local topology. Nondegenerate critical points are isolated and generic among smooth functions.
