Every smooth function $f$ on a convex neighborhood can be expanded as $f(x) = f(a) + \sum g_i(x)(x_i - a_i)$ with smooth coefficients $g_i$ satisfying $g_i(a) = \partial f / \partial x_i(a)$. This is proved by integrating the derivative along the line segment from $a$ to $x$. When $a$ is a critical point, the $g_i$ vanish at $a$, allowing a further expansion to quadratic terms — the crucial step toward the Morse Lemma.
