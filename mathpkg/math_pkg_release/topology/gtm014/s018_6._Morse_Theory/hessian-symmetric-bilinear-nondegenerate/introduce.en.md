Proposition 6.6 establishes the invariant properties of the Hessian on a smooth manifold. For a function $f: X \to \mathbb{R}$ with a critical point $p$, the intrinsic second derivative $(d^2 f)_p$ is always a symmetric bilinear form on the tangent space $T_p X$. Moreover, it is nondegenerate as a bilinear form precisely when $p$ is a nondegenerate critical point. This provides the coordinate-free characterization of nondegeneracy needed for the Morse Lemma.
