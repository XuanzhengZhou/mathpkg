Given a compact finite-dimensional manifold $X$, the space $C^\infty(X, \mathbf{R})$ of smooth real-valued functions forms a Fréchet space when equipped with the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology. This extends to sections of any vector bundle $E$ over $X$, making $C^\infty(E)$ a Fréchet space, and to the set $C_f^\infty(X, TY)$ of vector fields along a smooth map $f: X \to Y$. These examples motivate the study of Fréchet manifolds, as the mapping space $C^\infty(X, Y)$ will itself be shown to be a Fréchet manifold.
