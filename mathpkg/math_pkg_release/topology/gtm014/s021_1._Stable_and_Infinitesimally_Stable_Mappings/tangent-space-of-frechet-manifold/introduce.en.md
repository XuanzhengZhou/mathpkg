The tangent space to a point on a Fréchet manifold is defined analogously to the finite-dimensional case. One considers the set $S_p(X)$ of all smooth curves through $p$ and introduces an equivalence relation via the derivatives of coordinate representations. The resulting quotient $T_p X$ carries a natural vector space structure, and smooth maps between Fréchet manifolds induce well-defined linear maps on tangent spaces. This construction is essential for the development of infinitesimal stability theory in later sections.
