When $X$ is a compact smooth manifold, the set $C^\infty(X, Y)$ of all smooth maps from $X$ to any smooth manifold $Y$ admits the structure of a Fréchet manifold. The proof uses the tubular neighborhood theorem: the graph of a map $f$ is a submanifold of $X \times Y$, and nearby maps are identified with sections of the normal bundle, which form a Fréchet space by Proposition 1.9. This result is the key geometric input that makes Mather's infinitesimal stability theory possible.
