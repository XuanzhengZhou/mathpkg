A curve on a smooth manifold $X$ based at a point $p \in X$ is a differentiable map from the real line into $X$ that sends the origin to $p$. This definition generalizes the intuitive notion of a parametrized curve passing through a specified point in Euclidean space to the setting of abstract differentiable manifolds. Curves based at $p$ form the foundational building blocks for defining tangent vectors: the tangent space $T_p X$ will be constructed as equivalence classes of such curves under a suitable tangency relation.
