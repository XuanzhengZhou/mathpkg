The differential of a smooth map $f \colon X \to Y$ at a point $p$ is a linear map from the tangent space of $X$ at $p$ to the tangent space of $Y$ at $f(p)$, defined by sending a tangent vector represented by a curve $c$ to the tangent vector represented by the pushed-forward curve $f \circ c$. This is the manifold analogue of the ordinary derivative in Euclidean space. In local coordinates $\phi$ around $p$ and $\psi$ around $f(p)$, the differential is expressed as $d(\psi \circ f \circ \phi^{-1})_{\phi(p)}$, the Jacobian matrix of the local representative of $f$.
