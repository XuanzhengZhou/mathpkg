Let $X$ and $Y$ be smooth manifolds with $W$ a submanifold of $Y$. Assume that $\dim X = \operatorname{codim} W$. Let $p$ be in $X$ and let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth. Assume that $f(p) \in W$ and $f \mathrel{\perp\!\!\!\perp} W$ at $p$. Then there exists a neighborhood $N$ of $f$ in $C^\infty(X, Y)$ (in the $C^1$ topology) and an open neighborhood $U$ of $p$ in $X$ such that if $g \in N$, then $g^{-1}(W) \cap U$ consists of one point $q$ and $g \mathrel{\perp\!\!\!\perp} W$ at $q$.

*Hint:* Use Lemma 4.3 and note the similarity with Theorem 4.4.
