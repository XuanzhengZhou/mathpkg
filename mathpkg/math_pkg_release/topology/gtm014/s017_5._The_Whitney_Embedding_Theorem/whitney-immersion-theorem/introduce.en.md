The Whitney Immersion Theorem is a foundational result in differential topology, stating that when the target manifold $Y$ has dimension at least twice that of the source $X$, the set of immersions is both open and dense in $C^\infty(X, Y)$. The openness follows from Lemma 5.5, while density is proved by a transversality argument: the codimension of each corank-$r$ stratum $S_r$ is at least $n+1$, so a generic 1-jet avoids all $S_r$ for $r \neq 0$, implying the map is an immersion by Lemma 5.1. This theorem guarantees that immersions are "generic" in the appropriate dimension range.
