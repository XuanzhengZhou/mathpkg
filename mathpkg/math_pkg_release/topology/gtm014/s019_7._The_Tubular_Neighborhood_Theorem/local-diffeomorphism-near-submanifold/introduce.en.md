This proposition states that if a smooth map $f: Y \to Y'$ restricts to an embedding on a submanifold $X \subset Y$ and its derivative is an isomorphism at every point of $X$, then $f$ is actually a diffeomorphism on some open neighborhood of $X$. The proof constructs local inverses $f_i^{-1}$ on a covering of $f(X)$, patches them together using the uniqueness of inverses on overlaps, and produces a global diffeomorphism $g$ inverting $f$ near $X$. This is a key technical lemma used in proving the Tubular Neighborhood Theorem.
