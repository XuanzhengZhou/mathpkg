A subbundle of a vector bundle $E \to X$ is a smooth submanifold $F \subset E$ such that the restriction of the bundle projection makes $F$ into a vector bundle over $X$ in its own right. In each fiber, the vector space structure on $F_x$ is the one inherited from the ambient fiber $E_x$. This definition provides the natural notion of a vector subbundle and is fundamental for constructing and analyzing substructures within vector bundles, such as kernels and images of bundle homomorphisms.
