Let $\pi_E: E \to X$ and $\pi_F: F \to Y$ be vector bundles. A smooth map $\phi: E \to F$ is a \textbf{homomorphism} of vector bundles if

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(1)] There exists a smooth function $f: X \to Y$, called the \textbf{base mapping}, such that the diagram
\[
\begin{array}{ccc}
E & \xrightarrow{\phi} & F \\
\downarrow & & \downarrow \\
X & \xrightarrow{f} & Y
\end{array}
\]
commutes.
\item[(2)] $\phi$ is smooth.
\item[(3)] For each $p \in X$, the restriction $\phi_p := \phi|_{E_p}: E_p \to F_{f(p)}$ is a linear map.
\end{enumerate}
