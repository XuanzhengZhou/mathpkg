Proposition 2.3 of Golubitsky--Guillemin states that the dimension of the local ring $\mathcal{R}_f(x)$ varies upper semi-continuously with the point $x$. The proof uses the Malgrange Preparation Theorem to express polynomials near $x$ in terms of a basis of $\mathcal{R}_f(x)$ with smooth coefficients depending on the target coordinates, then extends this representation inductively to all polynomials. An immediate consequence is that finiteness of a map germ at a point propagates to nearby points.
