Let $X$ and $Y$ be complex manifolds and let $f: X \to Y$ be a holomorphic map. If $f$ is locally finite-to-one (i.e., for every $x \in X$ there exists a neighborhood on which $f$ has finite fibers), then the local ring $\mathcal{R}_f(x) = \mathcal{O}_{X,x} / f^*(\mathcal{M}_{Y,f(x)})$ has finite complex dimension:
$$\dim_{\mathbf{C}} \mathcal{R}_f(x) < \infty$$
for every $x \in X$. In other words, a holomorphic map is finite (in the sense of the local ring) if and only if it is locally finite-to-one.
