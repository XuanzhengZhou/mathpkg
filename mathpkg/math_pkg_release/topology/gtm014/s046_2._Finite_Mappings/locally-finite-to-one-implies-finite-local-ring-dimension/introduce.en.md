Remark 2 from Golubitsky--Guillemin (Section VII.2) observes that in the complex analytic category, the converse of Proposition 2.4 holds: a holomorphic map that is locally finite-to-one automatically has finite-dimensional local ring at every point. This highlights a key difference between the smooth ($C^\infty$) and complex analytic settings -- in the smooth category, a map can be topologically finite-to-one yet have infinite local ring dimension, while complex analytic rigidity precludes this pathology. The result is due to [14].
