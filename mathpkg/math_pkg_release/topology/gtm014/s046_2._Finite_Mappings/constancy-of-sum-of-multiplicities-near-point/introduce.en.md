Proposition 2.4 of Golubitsky--Guillemin asserts that for a finite smooth map, the sum of the dimensions of the local rings over all pre-image points near a given base point is locally constant. This result captures a conservation law for multiplicities under small perturbations of the target point. Together with Proposition 2.3 (upper semi-continuity of the individual dimensions), it provides fundamental control over the behavior of the local ring along fibers of a finite map.
