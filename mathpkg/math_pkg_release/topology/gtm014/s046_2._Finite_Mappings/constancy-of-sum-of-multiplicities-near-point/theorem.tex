Let $f: X \to Y$ be a smooth map between manifolds, and let $x \in X$ be a point at which $f$ is finite. Then there exists an open neighborhood $U$ of $x$ in $X$ such that for every $y$ sufficiently close to $f(x)$, the sum
$$\sum_{z \in f^{-1}(y) \cap U} \dim_{\mathbf{R}} \mathcal{R}_f(z)$$
is constant (and equal to $\dim_{\mathbf{R}} \mathcal{R}_f(x)$ when $y = f(x)$). Here $\mathcal{R}_f(z) = C_z^\infty(X) / f^*(\mathcal{M}_{f(z)}(Y))$ denotes the local ring of $f$ at $z$.
