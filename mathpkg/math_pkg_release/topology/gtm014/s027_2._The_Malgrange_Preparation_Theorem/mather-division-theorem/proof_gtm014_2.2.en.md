This proof is word for word the same as the proof that Theorem 1.4 implies Theorem 1.2 with the single exception that smooth is substituted for holomorphic throughout. $\square$

*Note.* The Malgrange Preparation Theorem, which states that there exists a smooth $q$ with $q(0) \neq 0$ such that $(qF)(t, x) = t^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \lambda_i(x)t^i$, follows from Theorem 2.1 in precisely the same way that Theorem 1.1 follows from Theorem 1.2.
