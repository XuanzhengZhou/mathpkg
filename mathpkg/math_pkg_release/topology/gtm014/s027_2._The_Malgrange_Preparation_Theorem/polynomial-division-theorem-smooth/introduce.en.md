The smooth Polynomial Division Theorem generalizes complex polynomial division to $C^\infty$ functions. The proof replaces the Cauchy Integral Formula with a generalized Cauchy-Green formula: for smooth $F$ on $D$,
$$F(w) = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\gamma \frac{F(\eta)}{\eta-w}d\eta - \frac{1}{2\pi i}\iint_D \frac{\partial F/\partial \bar{\eta}}{\eta-w} d\eta \wedge d\bar{\eta}.$$
A careful choice of smooth extension $\tilde{G}$ vanishing to high order on the zero set of $P_k$ makes the double integral well-defined and smooth.
