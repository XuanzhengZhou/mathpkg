Let $G(t, x)$ be a complex-valued smooth function near $0 \in \mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n$. Then there exist smooth complex-valued functions $q(t, x, \lambda)$ and $r(t, x, \lambda)$ near $0 \in \mathbf{R} \times \mathbf{R}^n \times \mathbf{R}^k$ such that
$$G(t, x) = q(t, x, \lambda) P_k(t, \lambda) + r(t, x, \lambda)$$
where $P_k(t, \lambda) = t^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \lambda_i t^i$ and $r(t, x, \lambda) = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} r_i(x, \lambda) t^i$. If $G$ is real-valued, $q$ and $r$ may be chosen real-valued.
