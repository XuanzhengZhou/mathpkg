Let $V$ and $W$ be linear subspaces of $\mathbb{R}^n$ such that $V + W = \mathbb{R}^n$. Let $g$ be a smooth function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $V$ and let $h$ be a smooth function defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $W$. Suppose that $g$ and $h$ agree (with all derivatives) on a neighborhood of $0$ in $V \cap W$. Then there exists a smooth function $F$ defined on a neighborhood of $0$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$ such that for all multi-indices $\alpha$,

$$\frac{\partial^{|\alpha|}F}{\partial x^\alpha}(x) = \begin{cases}
\dfrac{\partial^{|\alpha|}g}{\partial x^\alpha}(x) & \text{if } x \in V,\\[10pt]
\dfrac{\partial^{|\alpha|}h}{\partial x^\alpha}(x) & \text{if } x \in W.
\end{cases}$$
