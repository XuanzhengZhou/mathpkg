The Polynomial Division Theorem is the smooth ($C^\infty$) analogue of the classical Weierstrass Polynomial Division Theorem. Given a smooth function $G(t,x)$ and a monic polynomial $P_k(t,\lambda)$, it provides a smooth quotient $q$ and a remainder $r$ that is a polynomial in $t$ of degree less than $k$ with smooth coefficients depending on $x$ and $\lambda$. Theorem 2.2 implies the Mather Division Theorem (Theorem 2.1) by the same argument that the complex-analytic polynomial division theorem implies the Weierstrass Division Theorem, substituting "smooth" for "holomorphic" throughout.
