Let $f: X \rightarrow Y$ be smooth and let $F: X \times V \rightarrow Y \times V$ be a $k$-deformation of $f$. Then $F$ is trivial iff there exists an open neighborhood $U$ of $0$ in $V$ and vector fields $\zeta^i$ on $X \times U$ and $\eta^i$ on $Y \times U$ (for $1 \leq i \leq k$) satisfying

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $\rho(\zeta^i) = 0 = \rho(\eta^i)$, \quad i.e., the $\mathbf{R}^k$-components of $\zeta^i$ and $\eta^i$ vanish;
\item[(b)] $\tau_F^i = (dF)(\zeta^i) + F^*(\eta^i)$ on $X \times U$.
\end{enumerate}
