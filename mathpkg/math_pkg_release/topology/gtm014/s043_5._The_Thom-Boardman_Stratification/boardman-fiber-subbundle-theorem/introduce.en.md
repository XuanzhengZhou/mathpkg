Boardman's Theorem (Theorem 5.1) proves Thom's conjecture that the inductive definition of higher-order singularity symbols can be continued indefinitely for a residual set of maps. It asserts that for any non-increasing sequence of nonnegative integers $r_1 \ge \cdots \ge r_k \ge 0$, the set $S_{r_1,\ldots,r_k}$ is a fiber subbundle of the $k$-jet bundle $J^k(X, Y)$ over $X \times Y$. This structural result is the foundation of the Thom-Boardman stratification: for maps whose jet extensions are transversal to all such subbundles (Boardman maps), the preimages form nested submanifolds of $X$ that stratify the domain by singularity type.
