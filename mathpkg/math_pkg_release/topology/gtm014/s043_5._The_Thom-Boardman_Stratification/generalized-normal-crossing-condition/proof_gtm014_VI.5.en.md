The generalized normal crossing condition is formulated for submanifolds $T_1, \ldots, T_s$ of $J^k(X, Y)$ with the property that the target map $T_j \to Y$ is a submersion. For maps $f$ with $j^k f$ transverse to each $T_j$, the pre-images $T_j(f) = (j^k f)^{-1}(T_j)$ are submanifolds of $X$. For distinct points $x_j \in T_j(f)$ with $f(x_1) = \cdots = f(x_s) = y$, the condition requires that the subspaces $(df)_{x_j}(T_{x_j} T_j(f))$ be in general position in $T_y Y$. This generalizes Condition NC to arbitrary families of target-submersive submanifolds of jet space. When the $T_j$ are taken to be the Boardman subbundles $S_{I_j}$, this recovers the original normal crossing condition of Theorem 5.2.
