A smooth map $f: X \to Y$ between smooth manifolds is called a \textbf{Boardman map} if for every $k \ge 1$, its $k$-jet extension $j^k f: X \to J^k(X, Y)$ is transversal to every universal Boardman subbundle $S_{r_1,\ldots,r_k}$ in $J^k(X, Y)$. By the Thom transversality theorem, the set of Boardman maps forms a residual subset of $C^\infty(X, Y)$.
