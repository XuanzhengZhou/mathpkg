This exercise asks to show that for $k > l$, the natural forgetful map $\pi_{k,l}: J^k(X,Y) \to J^l(X,Y)$ that discards jet information of order $> l$ makes $J^k(X,Y)$ into a fiber bundle over $J^l(X,Y)$. The fiber consists of jets of order $k$ that vanish to order $l$, which can be identified with the vector space of homogeneous polynomials of degrees $l+1$ through $k$.
