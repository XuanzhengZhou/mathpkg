This lemma constructs explicit local charts $\tau_{U,V}$ on $J^k(X, Y)$ using coordinate charts on $X$ and $Y$. In these coordinates, a $k$-jet is represented by its source point, target point, and the $m$-tuple of Taylor polynomials (without constant term) of degree $k$ of the coordinate representatives. The crucial computation shows that changing coordinates transforms these Taylor polynomials smoothly via the chain rule for higher derivatives, establishing that $J^k(X, Y)$ is a smooth manifold. The source and target maps are shown to be smooth submersions in these coordinates.
