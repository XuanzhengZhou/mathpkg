This lemma translates the inductive, coordinate-free definition of $k$-th order contact into a concrete condition on partial derivatives in local coordinates. It states that two smooth maps have $k$-th order contact at a point precisely when all their partial derivatives up to order $k$ agree at that point. The proof proceeds by induction on $k$, using the description of the differential in local coordinates as a map $(x, y) \mapsto (f(x), \sum_j \frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j} y_j)$.
