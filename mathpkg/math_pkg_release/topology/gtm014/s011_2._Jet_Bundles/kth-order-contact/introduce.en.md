The notion of $k$-th order contact formalizes the idea of two smooth maps agreeing "to order $k$" at a point. It is defined inductively: first order contact means equality of differentials, and $k$-th order contact means that the differentials of the maps have $(k-1)$-st order contact at every tangent vector. This equivalence relation $\sim_k$ partitions the set of smooth map germs at a point into equivalence classes called $k$-jets, which form the foundation of jet bundle theory.
