This proposition establishes the fundamental functorial behavior of jet bundles. Post-composition with a smooth map $h: Y \to Z$ induces a forward map $h_*$ on jets, while pre-composition with a diffeomorphism $g: Z \to X$ induces a pullback $g^*$. These operations respect composition and identities, making $J^k(X, -)$ a covariant functor and $J^k(-, Y)$ a contravariant functor on the category of smooth manifolds with diffeomorphisms. The well-definedness of $h_*$ and $g^*$ relies on Lemma 2.4, which guarantees that the equivalence class of the composite depends only on the equivalence class of the original map.
