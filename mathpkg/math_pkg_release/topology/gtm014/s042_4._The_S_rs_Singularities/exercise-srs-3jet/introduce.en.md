This exercise asks the reader to verify that the transversality condition $j^2 f \overline{\cap} S_{r,s}$ at a point $x$ depends only on the three-jet of $f$ at $x$, i.e., on the Taylor expansion of $f$ up to order 3. This is because $S_{r,s}$ is defined using the intrinsic second derivative, which itself depends on the second jet, and transversality to a submanifold defined in $J^2(X,Y)$ involves the derivative of $j^2 f$, which is a three-jet condition.
