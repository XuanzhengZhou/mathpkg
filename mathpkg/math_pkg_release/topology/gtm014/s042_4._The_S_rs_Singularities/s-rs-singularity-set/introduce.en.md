The $S_{r,s}(f)$ singularities form the second level of the Thom-Boardman stratification of a smooth map $f: X \to Y$. After the first derivative classifies points by the rank $r$ of $df$ (giving $S_r(f)$), the intrinsic second derivative further stratifies $S_r(f)$ by the rank $s$ of the associated map to $\text{Hom}(K, \text{Hom}(K, L))$. The result is that $S_{r,s}(f)$ is a submanifold of $X$ whose dimension is given by a formula involving the dimensions of $X$ and $Y$, the rank $r$, and the codimension of $S_{r,s}$ inside $S_r$.
