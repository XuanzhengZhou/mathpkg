This exercise characterizes the 3-jet of a map near a non-simple cusp point of a 1-generic mapping between surfaces. Using coordinates adapted from Theorem 2.2, the map takes the form $(x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_1, h(x_1, x_2))$ where $h$ vanishes to third order in $x_2$ at the cusp point. The exercise shows that any such map has the same 3-jet as a map of the special form $(x_1, x_2) \mapsto (x_1, x_1 \alpha(x_1, x_2))$, where the second component is divisible by $x_1$. This is an early step toward the classification of Morin singularities.
