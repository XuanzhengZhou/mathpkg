Let $V$ and $W$ be vector spaces, let $V \circ V$ be the symmetric product of $V$ with itself, and let $V \wedge V$ be the space of alternating tensors. Recall that $V \otimes V = (V \circ V) \oplus (V \wedge V)$, so there is a canonical projection $\pi : V \otimes V \to V \circ V$ whose kernel is $V \wedge V$. Consider the map

$$(4.2) \qquad \text{Hom}(V \circ V, W) \to \text{Hom}(V \otimes V, W) \to \text{Hom}(V, \text{Hom}(V, W))$$

where the first arrow is $A \mapsto A \cdot \pi$ and the second arrow is $B \mapsto \phi_B$ with $\phi_B(v)(v') = B(v \otimes v')$. Define $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)_s$ to be the pre-image under (4.2) of $L^s(V, \text{Hom}(V, W))$.

(Note that $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)_s$ is not the same as $L^s(V \circ V, W)$.)
