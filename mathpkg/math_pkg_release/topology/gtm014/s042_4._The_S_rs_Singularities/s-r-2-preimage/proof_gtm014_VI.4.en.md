The set $S_r^{(2)}$ is defined as the pre-image of $S_r$ in $J^2(X, Y)$. By Exercise 2 at the end of Section 3, the intrinsic derivative gives a map of fiber bundles $S_r^{(2)} \to \operatorname{Hom}(K \circ K, L)$. Moreover, the top arrow in this diagram is surjective, which follows from Exercise 1 in Section 3 — the last two terms on the right-hand side of equation (3.8) are completely arbitrary except for the symmetry condition. This construction provides the foundation for defining the universal $S_{r,s}$ singularities via the algebraic classification of symmetric homomorphisms.
