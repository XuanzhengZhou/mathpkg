Let $f: X \to Y$ be smooth and infinitesimally stable, and let $m = \dim Y$. For each integer $r$ with $1 \leq r \leq m+1$, there exists an open neighborhood $W_r$ of $f$ in $C^\infty(X, Y)$ (with the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology) such that every $g \in W_r$ satisfies $(\dagger)$ for all finite subsets $S \subset X$ consisting of $r$ points. The neighborhood $W_1$ of $f$ consisting of locally infinitesimally stable mappings is given by Proposition 1.10.

Consequently, the finite intersection
$$
W = \bigcap_{r=1}^{m+1} W_r
$$
is an open neighborhood of $f$ such that every $g \in W$ satisfies $(\dagger)$ for every finite subset $S \subset X$ with $|S| \leq m+1$.
