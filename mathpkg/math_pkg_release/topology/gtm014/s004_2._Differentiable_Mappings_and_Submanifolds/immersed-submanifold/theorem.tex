The image of a 1-1 immersion, made into a manifold in the following manner, is an immersed submanifold.

Let $X$ be a differentiable manifold, $Y$ a set, and $f: X \to Y$ a bijection. There is a natural way to make $Y$ into a differentiable manifold: first declare that the topology on $Y$ is the one which makes $f$ a homeomorphism, then define the charts on $Y$ to be the pull-backs via $f^{-1}$ of the charts on $X$.

**Warning:** This definition of immersed submanifold is not the same, in general, as that of a submanifold. In particular, the topology of the immersed submanifold need not be the same as the induced topology from the range.
