Definition 2.7 defines a submanifold $Y$ of a $C^k$-manifold $X$: locally, in adapted charts, $Y$ looks like a coordinate subspace $\mathbf{R}^m \subset \mathbf{R}^n$ defined by the vanishing of the last $n-m$ coordinates. This is the standard "regular submanifold" definition. A submanifold inherits its topology from the ambient manifold and carries a natural $C^k$-differentiable structure of dimension $m$ given by the restrictions of the adapted charts.
