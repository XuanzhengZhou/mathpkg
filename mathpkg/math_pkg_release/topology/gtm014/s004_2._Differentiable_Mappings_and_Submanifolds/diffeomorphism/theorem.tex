Let $\phi: X \to Y$ be a $C^k$-differentiable map between $C^k$-differentiable manifolds. If $\dim X = \dim Y = n$, $\phi$ is bijective, and the rank of $\phi$ is $n$ at every point of $X$, then $\phi$ is a diffeomorphism.

If $\phi: X \to Y$ is a diffeomorphism, then $\phi^{-1}: Y \to X$ is well-defined and is as differentiable as $\phi$ is, by the inverse function theorem.
