Definition 2.12 introduces the notion of a proper map: a continuous map $\phi: X \to Y$ such that the preimage of every compact subset of $Y$ is compact in $X$. Properness is a crucial condition in differential topology; for instance, a 1-1 proper immersion is always an embedding (Theorem 2.13), making properness a powerful tool for upgrading immersions to submanifolds.
