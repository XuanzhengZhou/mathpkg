Lemma 5.12 asserts that the tangent space to the orbit $\mathcal{D}_{\sigma}$ of a $k$-jet $\sigma$ under the action of $\operatorname{Diff}(X) \times \operatorname{Diff}(Y)$ is spanned by the infinitesimal actions of vector fields on the source $X$ and the target $Y$. Concretely, every tangent vector $v \in T_{\sigma}\mathcal{D}_{\sigma}$ can be written as the sum of a contribution from a vector field on $X$ (via $(d\gamma_{2})$) and a contribution from a vector field on $Y$ (via $(d\gamma_{1})$). The proof uses the parametrization $T$ of the jet space to represent a curve in $\mathcal{D}_{\sigma}$ and then extracts curves of diffeomorphisms via the Lie group structure of the jet groups $\mathring{\mathcal{G}}^{k}(X)_{p}$ and $\mathring{\mathcal{G}}^{k}(Y)_{q}$. This surjectivity is a key step in establishing Theorem 5.13.
