The $S_r$ classification is the most rudimentary way to classify the singularities of a differentiable mapping $f: X \to Y$. At a point $x \in X$, the singularity type $S_r$ records the corank of the differential $(df)_x$ -- that is, how many ranks the differential drops below the maximal possible rank $\min(\dim X, \dim Y)$. The set of all such singular points $S_r(f)$ is obtained by pulling back the submanifold $S_r$ of corank-$r$ jets in the 1-jet bundle $J^1(X, Y)$ via the 1-jet extension $j^1 f$. By the Thom Transversality Theorem, generically $S_r(f)$ is a submanifold of $X$ of codimension $r^2 + er$ where $e = |\dim X - \dim Y|$.
