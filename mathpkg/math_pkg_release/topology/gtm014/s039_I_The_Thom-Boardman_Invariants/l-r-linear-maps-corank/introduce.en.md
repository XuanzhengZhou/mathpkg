For a given pair of vector spaces $V$ and $W$, the set $L^r(V, W)$ consists of all linear maps from $V$ to $W$ whose corank is exactly $r$ -- that is, maps whose rank falls short of the maximal possible by $r$. This space is central to singularity theory because it models the different ways a linear map can degenerate. Using the Grassmannian $G(s, V)$ of $s$-dimensional subspaces (where $s = \dim V - r$), one can fiber $L^r(V, W)$ over $G(s, V)$ with each fiber identified with the full-rank linear maps $L^0(Q_p, W)$ on the quotient $Q_p = V/K_p$. This construction endows $L^r(V, W)$ with a natural manifold structure and generalizes to vector bundles, yielding the fiber bundle $L^r(E, F)$ whose typical fiber is $L^r(V, W)$.
