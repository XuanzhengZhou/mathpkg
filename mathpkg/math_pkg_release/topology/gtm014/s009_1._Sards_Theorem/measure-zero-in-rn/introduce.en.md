A subset $S$ of $\mathbf{R}^n$ has (Lebesgue) measure zero if for every $\varepsilon > 0$ it can be covered by a countable collection of open cubes whose total volume is less than $\varepsilon$. This is Definition 1.1(1) in Golubitsky--Guillemin and provides the Euclidean-space foundation for the concept of measure zero, which is central to Sard's Theorem.
