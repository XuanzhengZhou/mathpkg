The notion of measure zero is extended from $\mathbf{R}^n$ to differentiable $n$-manifolds by requiring that, in local coordinate charts, the intersection of the set with each chart domain maps to a measure zero subset of $\mathbf{R}^n$. This is Definition 1.1(2) in Golubitsky--Guillemin. Lemma 1.2 and Proposition 1.3 ensure that this definition is well-defined (independent of the choice of atlas).
