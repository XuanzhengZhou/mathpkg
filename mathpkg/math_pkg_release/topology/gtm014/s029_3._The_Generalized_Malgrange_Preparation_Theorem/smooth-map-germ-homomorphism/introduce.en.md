A smooth map $\phi: X \to Y$ induces a pullback ring homomorphism $\phi^*$ on the local rings of germs, sending a germ at $\phi(p)$ to its composition with $\phi$. This lemma characterizes when $\phi$ is a local diffeomorphism purely algebraically: $\phi$ is a local diffeomorphism near $p$ if and only if $\phi^*: C_q^\infty(Y) \to C_p^\infty(X)$ is an isomorphism. This algebraic criterion is fundamental for the proof of the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem, where module structures over different local rings are compared.
