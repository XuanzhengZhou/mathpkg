Nakayama's Lemma is a fundamental result in commutative algebra: if $A$ is a finitely generated module over a local ring $\mathcal{R}$ with maximal ideal $\mathcal{M}$, and $A = \mathcal{M}A$, then $A$ must be the zero module. The proof uses the "determinant trick": expressing generators in terms of elements of $\mathcal{M}$ leads to a system of linear equations whose coefficient matrix has determinant $1 + s$ with $s \in \mathcal{M}$, which is invertible in a local ring. This lemma is essential for the proof of the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem.
