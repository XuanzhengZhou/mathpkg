The $s$-fold $k$-jet extension $j_s^k f$ of a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ evaluates the ordinary $k$-jet $j^k f$ at $s$ distinct points simultaneously. It maps the configuration space $X^{(s)}$ into the multijet bundle $J_s^k(X, Y)$. This map is the central object in the multijet transversality theorem, where the condition $j_s^k f \pitchfork W$ for a submanifold $W \subset J_s^k(X, Y)$ characterizes those smooth maps whose multijet extensions are transverse to $W$.
