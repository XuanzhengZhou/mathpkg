The Multijet Transversality Theorem generalizes the Thom Transversality Theorem from ordinary jet spaces to multijet spaces. It states that the set $T_W$ of smooth maps $f: X \to Y$ whose $s$-fold $k$-jet extension is transverse to a given submanifold $W \subset J_s^k(X, Y)$ forms a residual subset of $C^\infty(X, Y)$ in the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology. When $W$ is compact, $T_W$ is in fact open. A related remark notes that if $\alpha^{(s)}(W)$ is a compact subset of $X^{(s)}$, then $T_W$ is also open. This theorem is a fundamental tool for studying the injectivity and self-intersection properties of generic smooth mappings.
