The Whitney $C^k$ topology on the space $C^\infty(X, Y)$ of smooth maps between manifolds is defined by specifying a neighborhood basis at each function $f$. A basic open neighborhood $B_\delta(f)$ consists of all smooth maps $g$ whose $k$-jets $j^k g(x)$ lie within a $\delta(x)$-ball of the $k$-jet $j^k f(x)$ at every point $x \in X$, where $\delta: X \to \mathbf{R}^+$ is a continuous positive function. This topology is finer than the compact-open topology and is particularly well-suited for studying transversality and stability of smooth mappings. For non-compact $X$, the Whitney topology does not satisfy the first axiom of countability, reflecting its fine character.
