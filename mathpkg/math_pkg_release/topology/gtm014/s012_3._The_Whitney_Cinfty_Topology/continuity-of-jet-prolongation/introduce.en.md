Proposition 3.4 of Golubitsky and Guillemin establishes that the $k$-jet prolongation map $j^k: C^\infty(X, Y) \to C^\infty(X, J^k(X, Y))$ is continuous when both function spaces carry the Whitney $C^k$ topology. This is a foundational fact needed for working with jet spaces in the Whitney topology framework, ensuring that the operation of taking $k$-jets behaves well with respect to the topological structure. The proof follows directly from the definition of the neighborhood basis via the sets $B_\delta(f)$, using compatible metrics on the jet bundles.
