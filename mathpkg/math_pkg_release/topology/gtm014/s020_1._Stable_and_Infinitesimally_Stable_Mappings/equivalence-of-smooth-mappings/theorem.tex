Let $X$ and $Y$ be smooth manifolds, and let $f, f' \in C^\infty(X, Y)$. Then $f$ is \text{equivalent} to $f'$ if there exist diffeomorphisms $g: X \to X$ and $h: Y \to Y$ such that the diagram

$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
X & \xrightarrow{f} & Y \\
{\scriptstyle g}\downarrow & & \downarrow{\scriptstyle h} \\
X & \xrightarrow{f'} & Y
\end{array}
$$

commutes, i.e., $h \circ f = f' \circ g$.
