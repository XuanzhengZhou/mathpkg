A smooth mapping between manifolds is stable if every sufficiently small perturbation of the mapping is equivalent to the original one, up to diffeomorphisms of the domain and codomain. Stability captures the idea that the qualitative singularity structure of the mapping does not change under small deformations. This notion is central to the singularity theory developed by Whitney, Thom, and Mather.
