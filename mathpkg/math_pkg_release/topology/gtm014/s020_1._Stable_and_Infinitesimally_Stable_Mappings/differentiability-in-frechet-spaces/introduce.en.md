A continuous map $f$ between Frechet spaces is differentiable at $p$ if it admits a continuous linear approximation $A_p$ (the Frechet derivative) such that the remainder is $o(|h|)$. This definition generalizes differentiability to infinite-dimensional complete metric vector spaces and is the foundation for Frechet manifold theory, which provides the geometric setting for Mather's approach to stability of smooth mappings.
