This lemma provides the crucial bound that justifies checking condition $(\dagger)$ only on subsets of size at most $m+1$ in Theorem 1.6. It shows that under the infinitesimal stability condition $(\dagger)$, no fiber $f^{-1}(q)$ can contain more than $\dim Y$ critical points. The proof is a concise dimension-count argument: if there were $m+1$ critical points, their derivative images would be proper subspaces of $T_qY$, each of codimension at least 1, and Lemma 1.7 would force their intersection to have codimension at least $m+1$, which is impossible in an $m$-dimensional space.
