This is the central theorem of the section, establishing that global infinitesimal stability is equivalent to a multijet condition $(\dagger)$ that only needs to be checked on subsets $S$ of the fiber $f^{-1}(q)$ of size at most $m+1$. The bound $m+1$ comes from Lemma 1.9, which limits the number of critical points in any fiber to $\dim Y$. The sufficiency proof splits into two parts: handling a neighborhood of the critical set $\Sigma$ using Proposition 1.5 and a partition-of-unity argument, and handling the complement $X \setminus \Sigma$ where $f$ is a submersion (hence locally infinitesimally stable by an earlier result).
