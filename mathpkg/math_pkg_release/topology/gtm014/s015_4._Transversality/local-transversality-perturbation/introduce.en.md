Corollary 4.11 refines the Thom Transversality Theorem by providing localized control over the perturbation. Given a submanifold $W$ whose source projection $\alpha(W)$ lies in an open set $U \subset X$, any smooth map $f$ can be approximated arbitrarily closely (staying within a prescribed open neighborhood $V$) by a map $g$ that is transverse to $W$ and agrees with $f$ outside $U$. This is a corollary of the proof of the Thom Transversality Theorem, where the polynomial perturbations are constructed to have compact support inside the chosen coordinate charts.
