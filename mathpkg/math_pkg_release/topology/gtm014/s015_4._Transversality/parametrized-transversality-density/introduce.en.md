Corollary 4.7 establishes a fundamental density property of transversality in parametrized families: if a $k$-jet extension $\Phi$ of a family $G$ is transverse to a submanifold $W$ of the jet bundle $J^{k}(X, Y)$, then for a dense set of parameters $b$, the individual mappings $G_b$ are also transverse to $W$. This is the basic principle underlying transversality theory — transversality of a family ensures transversality for almost all members.
