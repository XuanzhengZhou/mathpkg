The Thom Transversality Theorem (Theorem 4.9) is a cornerstone of differential topology, stating that transversality is a generic property. For any submanifold $W$ of the $k$-jet bundle $J^{k}(X, Y)$, the set of smooth maps whose $k$-jet extension is transverse to $W$ forms a residual (and when $W$ is closed, open and dense) subset of $C^{\infty}(X, Y)$. This theorem underpins the genericity of stable mappings and is proved via local polynomial perturbations and a Baire category argument.
