Let
$$a x_1^2 + 2b x_1 x_2 + c x_2^2$$
be a quadratic form whose coefficients are smooth functions of parameters $z = (z_1, \ldots, z_m)$. Suppose that when $z = 0$, this form equals $x_1^2 - x_2^2$. Then there exists a rotation
$$S = \begin{pmatrix} s_{11} & s_{12} \\ s_{21} & s_{22} \end{pmatrix}$$
whose coefficients are smooth functions of $z$, such that $S(0)$ is the identity, and in the rotated coordinates $(\bar{x}_1, \bar{x}_2)$ the quadratic form has the form $\bar{a} \bar{x}_1^2 + \bar{b} \bar{x}_2^2$, where $\bar{a}(0) = 1$ and $\bar{b}(0) = -1$.
