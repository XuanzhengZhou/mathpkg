Definition 5.9 classifies umbilics into three types based on their local ring: hyperbolic (ring (5.3)), elliptic (ring (5.2)), and parabolic (ring (5.4)). The terminology comes from the associated quadratic form on $\mathcal{M}/\mathcal{M}^2$: the hyperbolic umbilic corresponds to the indefinite form $x^2 - y^2$, the elliptic umbilic to the definite form $x^2 + y^2$, and the parabolic umbilic to the degenerate form $x^2$.
