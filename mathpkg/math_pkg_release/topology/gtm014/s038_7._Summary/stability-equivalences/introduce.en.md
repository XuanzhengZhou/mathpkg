Theorem 7.1 is the central result of Chapter VII, unifying five distinct notions of stability for smooth maps between manifolds. When the source manifold $X$ is compact, stability, transverse stability, infinitesimal stability, homotopic stability, and stability under $k$-parameter deformations are all equivalent. This theorem ties together the geometric (transverse), algebraic (infinitesimal), and deformation-theoretic perspectives on stability, forming the backbone of Mather's theory of stable mappings.
