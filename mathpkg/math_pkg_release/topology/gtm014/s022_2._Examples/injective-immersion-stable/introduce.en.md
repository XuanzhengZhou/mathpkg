A one-to-one (injective) immersion $f: X \to Y$ from a compact manifold $X$ has the property that its image $f(X)$ is an embedded submanifold of $Y$ and $f$ is a diffeomorphism onto its image. This proposition shows that any such map is stable. The proof identifies vector fields along $f$ with vector fields on the image submanifold, extends them smoothly to all of $Y$, and verifies infinitesimal stability via the decomposition $w = (df)(0) + t \circ f$.
