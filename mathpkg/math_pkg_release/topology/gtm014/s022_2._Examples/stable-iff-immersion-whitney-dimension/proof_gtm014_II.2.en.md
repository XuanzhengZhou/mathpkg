We first note that any mapping equivalent to a one-to-one immersion is also a one-to-one immersion. If $f$ is stable, then there is an open neighborhood $W_f$ of $f$ in $C^\infty(X, Y)$ in which each mapping is equivalent to $f$. By the Whitney Embedding Theorem, under the dimensional hypothesis $\dim Y \geq 2 \dim X + 1$, the one-to-one immersions of $X$ into $Y$ form a dense open set in $C^\infty(X, Y)$. Hence $W_f$ must contain a one-to-one immersion $g$, and since $g$ is equivalent to $f$, $f$ itself must be a one-to-one immersion. The converse follows from Proposition 2.3.
