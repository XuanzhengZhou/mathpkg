Let $f: X \to Y$ be a smooth map between 2-manifolds, and let $p \in S_1(f)$ satisfy condition (b):
$$T_p S_1(f) = \operatorname{Ker}(df)_p.$$
Choose a smooth nonvanishing vector field $\xi$ along $S_1(f)$ such that at each point of $S_1(f)$, $\xi$ is in the kernel of $df$. Let $k$ be a smooth function on $X$ such that $k = 0$ on $S_1(f)$ and $(dk)_p \neq 0$. Consider the function $(dk)(\xi)$ restricted to $S_1(f)$.

The point $p$ is called a \textbf{cusp point} of $f$ if the function $(dk)(\xi)$ vanishes to at least second order along $S_1(f)$ at $p$. The point $p$ is called a \textbf{simple cusp point} if $(dk)(\xi)$ vanishes to exactly second order along $S_1(f)$ at $p$.
