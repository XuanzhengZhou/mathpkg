Let $f: X \to Y$ be a smooth map between 2-manifolds with a $S_1$ singularity at $p$. Suppose condition (b) holds: $\ker (df)_p = T_p S_1(f)$. Let $\xi$ be a smooth nonvanishing vector field along $S_1(f)$ such that at each point $\xi$ lies in the kernel of $df$. By the assumption that $\ker (df)_p = T_p S_1(f)$, the vector field $\xi$ is tangent to $S_1(f)$ at $p$. 

Let $k$ be a smooth function on $X$ with $k = 0$ on $S_1(f)$ and $(dk)_p \neq 0$. The nature of the singularity at $p$ depends on the order of contact that $\xi$ has with $S_1(f)$ at $p$, measured by the vanishing order of $\xi(k)$ along $S_1(f)$. A cusp is defined by the condition that this order of contact is exactly 2, which in suitable coordinates means that the second derivative in the kernel direction vanishes while the third does not.
