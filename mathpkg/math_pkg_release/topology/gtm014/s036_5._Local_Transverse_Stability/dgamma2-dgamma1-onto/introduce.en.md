This lemma is the crucial geometric fact underpinning the equivalence between transverse stability and infinitesimal stability: the tangent space to the orbit $\mathcal{D}_\sigma$ at $\sigma$ is spanned by the infinitesimal generators of the $\mathrm{Diff}(X)$ and $\mathrm{Diff}(Y)$ actions. In other words, every tangent vector to the orbit can be represented as a pair $(\xi, \eta)$ of vector fields on $X$ and $Y$. The proof uses the explicit description of $\mathcal{D}_\sigma^{U,V}$ from Theorem 5.1 as $T(U \times V \times \mathring{\mathcal{O}}_\sigma)$, the Lie group structure of the invertible $k$-jets, and Proposition 5.5 to promote polynomial jets to global diffeomorphisms.
