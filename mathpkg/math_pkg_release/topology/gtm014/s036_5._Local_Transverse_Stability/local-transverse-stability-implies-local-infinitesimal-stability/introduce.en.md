This is the main theorem of Section 5, establishing that the geometric transversality condition (local transverse stability) implies the algebraic condition (local infinitesimal stability). The proof is a beautiful synthesis of all the machinery developed in the section: given $\tau \in J^m(f^*TY)_p$, its image $\lambda(\tau) \in T_\sigma J^m(X,Y)$ can be decomposed using transversality as $w + (dj^m f)_p(v)$ with $w \in T_\sigma \mathcal{D}_\sigma$. Lemma 5.12 then expresses $w$ via vector fields on $X$ and $Y$, and Propositions 5.10-5.11 translate this back to the condition $\tau = (df)(v_1) + f^*(v_2)$, which is precisely the condition for local infinitesimal stability (by Corollary 1.3, it suffices to check this to order $m$).
