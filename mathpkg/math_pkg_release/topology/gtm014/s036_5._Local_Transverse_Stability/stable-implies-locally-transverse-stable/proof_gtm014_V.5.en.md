Choose an open nbhd $U$ of $f$ in $C^\infty(X, Y)$ such that all $g$ in $U$ are equivalent to $f$. Let $\sigma = j^m f(p)$. By the Thom Transversality Theorem (and the fact that transversality to immersed submanifolds is still a residual condition — see Note (3) after Definition 5.6), there is a $g$ in $U$ such that $j^m g \pitchfork \mathcal{D}_\sigma$. Choose $(\alpha, \beta)$ in $\mathrm{Diff}(X) \times \mathrm{Diff}(Y)$ such that $f = \beta \cdot g \cdot \alpha^{-1}$. Then $j^m f \pitchfork \mathcal{D}_\sigma$ at $p$ since $j^m g \pitchfork \mathcal{D}_\sigma$ at $\alpha(p)$ and the action of diffeomorphisms preserves transversality to orbits.
