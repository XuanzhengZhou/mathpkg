This proposition is the source-side analogue of Proposition 5.10. The map $(d\gamma_2): C^\infty(TX)_p \to T_\sigma \mathcal{D}_\sigma$ is the infinitesimal version of the $\mathrm{Diff}(X)$-action on the jet orbit: a vector field $\xi$ on $X$ induces a path $\sigma \cdot j^k \psi_t^{-1}(\psi_t(p))$ in the orbit, and $(d\gamma_2)$ is its derivative at $t=0$. The commutative diagram expresses $(d\gamma_2)$ in terms of $\lambda$, the derivative $(df)$, and the derivative of the $k$-jet map $(dj^k f)_p$, with a minus sign reflecting the inverse in the group action. The proof involves a careful computation using $F_t = f \circ \psi_t$ as the deformation.
