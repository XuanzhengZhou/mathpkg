This proposition is a fundamental technical tool for constructing global diffeomorphisms from local data. Given a local diffeomorphism $\phi$ defined near the origin with $\phi(0)=0$ and positive Jacobian determinant, one can extend it to a global diffeomorphism that equals the identity outside a compact set. The proof proceeds in two stages: first damping the nonlinear part $\beta = \phi - (d\phi)_0$ using a bump function, then handling the linear part $\alpha = (d\phi)_0$ by connecting it to the identity matrix via a path in $\mathrm{GL}(n,\mathbb{R})$ (using Lemma 5.4) and constructing diffeomorphisms for each small step.
