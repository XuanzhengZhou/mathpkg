Let $\mathcal{D}_\sigma$ denote the orbit of a $k$-jet $\sigma \in J^k(X,Y)$ under the natural action of the diffeomorphism groups $\mathrm{Diff}(X) \times \mathrm{Diff}(Y)$ on the jet bundle. This theorem establishes that every such orbit is an immersed submanifold of $J^k(X,Y)$, even though it may not be an embedded submanifold. The proof uses the structure of the Lie group of invertible $k$-jets and constructs explicit charts showing that each connected component of $\mathcal{D}_\sigma$ is the diffeomorphic image of $U \times V \times \mathring{\mathcal{O}}_\sigma$, where $\mathring{\mathcal{O}}_\sigma$ is the connected component of the orbit under the finite-dimensional Lie group action at the fiber level.
