This lemma provides a useful criterion for an immersion of $\mathbb{R}^n$ to be a global diffeomorphism: if the map is already a diffeomorphism outside a compact set, then it is a diffeomorphism everywhere. The proof proceeds by showing the map is surjective (its image is both open and closed) and injective (the set $S$ of points where injectivity fails is both open and closed, hence empty). The Inverse Function Theorem then upgrades the bijective immersion to a diffeomorphism.
