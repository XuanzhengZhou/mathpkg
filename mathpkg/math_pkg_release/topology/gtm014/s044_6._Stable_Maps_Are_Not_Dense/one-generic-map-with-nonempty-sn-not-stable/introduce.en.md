Proposition 6.4 is the key result of this section: if a one-generic map between $n^2$-dimensional manifolds has any $S_n$ singularity and $n > 2$, then the map cannot be stable. Combined with Proposition 6.1, this proves that stable maps are not dense in $C^\infty(X,Y)$ when $\dim X = \dim Y = n^2$ for $n > 2$. The proof relies on analyzing the action of the diffeomorphism group on jet spaces and showing that certain orbits cannot be open, via Lemma 6.9.
