Lemma 6.9 is the central algebraic fact that completes the proof that stable maps are not dense. It states that the natural action of $GL(V) \times GL(W)$ on the space of symmetric homomorphisms $\text{Hom}(V \circ V, W)$ has an open orbit if and only if $\dim V = \dim W < 3$. For $n \geq 3$, all orbits are of lower dimension and therefore have measure zero, making it impossible for an open set to be covered by countably many such orbits. This lemma formalizes the obstruction that prevents the existence of open orbits in the jet space $S_n^{(2)}$ when $n > 2$.
