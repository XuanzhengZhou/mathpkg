Definition 3.9 introduces the notion of general position for a finite collection of submanifolds of a smooth manifold at a common intersection point. A family of submanifolds $Y_1, \ldots, Y_r$ of $Y$ are said to be in general position at $q \in \bigcap_i Y_i$ precisely when their tangent spaces $T_q Y_1, \ldots, T_q Y_r$ are in general position as subspaces of $T_q Y$. This is the key geometric condition that enables the simultaneous linearization of the submanifolds near $q$, as stated in Lemma 3.10.
