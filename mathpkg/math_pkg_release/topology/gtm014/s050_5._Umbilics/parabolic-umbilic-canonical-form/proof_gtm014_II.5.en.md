The proof of Theorem 5.26 is left as Exercise (13) in the text. The strategy parallels the proof of Theorem 5.10 (hyperbolic and elliptic cases): one first uses the local ring $\mathbf{R}[x,y]/(x^2, xy)$ to determine the 2-jet of $f$, then applies a Malgrange-Mather-type preparation theorem to remove higher-order terms. The transversality condition $j^2 f \pitchfork S_P$ ensures that the relevant differentials are linearly independent, which legitimizes the coordinate changes needed to bring $f$ into the stated normal form. Key ingredients include Exercise 7 part (a) for the codimension computation of $S_P$ and the general machinery of contact classes developed in Section II.3.
