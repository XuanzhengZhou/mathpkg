Theorem 5.26 establishes the canonical normal form for a parabolic umbilic singularity. When a smooth map $f: X \to Y$ between $n$-manifolds exhibits a parabolic umbilic at $x_0$ (whose local ring is $\mathbf{R}[x,y]/(x^2, xy)$) and the 2-jet is transverse to the parabolic contact class $S_P$, there exist local coordinates in which $f$ takes a specific polynomial normal form. The first two components involve quadratic and cubic terms ($x_1^2 + x_2 x_3 + x_2^2 x_4$ and $x_1 x_2 + x_2 x_5$), while the remaining components are identity maps. This theorem completes the classification of umbilics alongside the hyperbolic and elliptic cases proved in Theorem 5.10.
