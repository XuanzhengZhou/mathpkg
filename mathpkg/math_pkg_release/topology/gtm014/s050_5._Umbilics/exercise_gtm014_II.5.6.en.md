Let $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ be the mapping
$$(x_1, \ldots, x_n) \mapsto (f_1(x), f_2(x), x_3, \ldots, x_n)$$
where
$$f_1 = \alpha_1(x_1, x_2) + \cdots$$
and
$$f_2 = \alpha_2(x_1, x_2) + \cdots$$
the dots indicating quadratic terms in $x_1 x_3, x_3^2$, etc. plus higher order terms in $x_1, \ldots, x_n$. Here $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ are assumed to be homogeneous quadratic polynomials in $x_1$ and $x_2$ alone. Show:

(a) $0$ is a parabolic umbilic $\Leftrightarrow$ $\alpha_1$ and $\alpha_2$ have a common linear factor.

(b) $0 \in S_{2,1} \Rightarrow \alpha_i = c_i (s_1 x_1 + s_2 x_2)^2$, $i = 1, 2$.

(c) $0 \in S_{2,2} \Rightarrow \alpha_1 = \alpha_2 = 0$.
