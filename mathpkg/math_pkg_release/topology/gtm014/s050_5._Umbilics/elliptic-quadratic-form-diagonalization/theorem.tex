Let
$$a(x_1^2 - x_2^2) + bx_2^2$$
be a quadratic form whose coefficients are smooth functions of a set of parameters $z = (z_1, \ldots, z_m)$. Suppose that for $z = 0$ the form equals $x_1^2 - x_2^2$. Then there exists a function $\lambda$ depending smoothly on $z$ such that $\lambda(0) = 1$ and such that with respect to the coordinates
$$\bar{x}_1 = \lambda x_1, \qquad \bar{x}_2 = \frac{1}{\lambda} x_2,$$
the quadratic form has the form $\bar{a}(\bar{x}_1^2 - \bar{x}_2^2)$.
