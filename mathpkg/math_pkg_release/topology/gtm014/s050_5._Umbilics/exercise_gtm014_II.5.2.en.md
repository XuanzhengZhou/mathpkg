Let $z$ and $w$ be complex numbers. Show that the map of $\mathbb{C}^2$ into $\mathbb{C}^2$ defined by
$$(z, w) \mapsto (z^2 + \bar{z} w, w)$$
has an elliptic umbilic at $0$ when viewed as a map of $\mathbb{R}^4 \to \mathbb{R}^4$. (Hint: Compare with the canonical form (5.12).) For $w$ fixed and real, sketch the curve $u = z^2 + \bar{z} w$, $|z| = \text{const.}$, in the $u$ plane.
