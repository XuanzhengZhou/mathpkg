The local ring $\mathcal{R}_f$ of a map germ $f: (X, x) \to (Y, y)$ is the quotient of the ring $C_x^\infty(X)$ of smooth function germs at $x$ by the ideal generated by the pullback of the maximal ideal $M_y(Y)$. This algebraic object encodes the infinitesimal behavior of $f$ near the singularity at $x$. For a submersion, $\mathcal{R}_f$ is isomorphic to the ring of smooth function germs in the fiber variables; for singular maps such as folds and cusps, $\mathcal{R}_f$ recovers truncated polynomial rings that serve as complete invariants of the singularity type.
