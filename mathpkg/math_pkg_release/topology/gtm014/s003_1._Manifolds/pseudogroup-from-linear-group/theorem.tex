Let $G$ be a group of linear automorphisms of $\mathbf{R}^n$. The pseudogroup $\Gamma_G^k$ is defined as
$$\Gamma_G^k = \{\phi \in (\mathrm{diff})^k \mid \forall x \in \operatorname{dom} \phi,\; (d\phi)_x \in G\}.$$
In particular:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] If $G = \{\text{all linear maps on } \mathbf{R}^n \text{ with positive determinant}\}$, then $\Gamma_G^k = (\mathrm{diff})_0^k$ consists of orientation-preserving $C^k$ mappings.
\item[(ii)] If $G = \{\text{all linear maps on } \mathbf{R}^n \text{ with determinant equal to } 1\}$, then $\Gamma_G^k$ consists of all volume-preserving $C^k$ mappings.
\item[(iii)] Let $(\;,\;)$ be an inner product on $\mathbf{R}^n$. If $G$ is the group of orthogonal matrices relative to $(\;,\;)$ (i.e., $A \in G$ iff $(x,y) = (Ax, Ay)$ for every $x,y \in \mathbf{R}^n$), then $\Gamma_G^k$ consists of all $C^k$ isometries in $\mathbf{R}^n$.
\end{itemize}
