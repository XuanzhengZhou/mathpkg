A general method for constructing pseudogroups on $\mathbf{R}^n$ is to restrict the linear part of $C^k$ diffeomorphisms to lie in a prescribed group $G$ of linear automorphisms. The resulting pseudogroup $\Gamma_G^k$ collects all local $C^k$ diffeomorphisms whose Jacobian at every point belongs to $G$.

Three classical examples arise from this construction. Taking $G$ to be all linear maps with positive determinant yields the pseudogroup of orientation-preserving $C^k$ mappings. Taking $G$ to be the special linear group (determinant $1$) gives volume-preserving mappings. Taking $G$ to be the orthogonal group with respect to a fixed inner product yields $C^k$ isometries.
