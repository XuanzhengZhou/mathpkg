The $(n-1)$-dimensional sphere $S^{n-1}$ provides a fundamental example of a compact analytic manifold. Its manifold structure is given by two stereographic projection charts, centered at the north and south poles respectively, which together cover the entire sphere.

The transition map between the two charts is the inversion $y \mapsto y/|y|^2$ on $\mathbf{R}^{n-1} \setminus \{0\}$, which is real analytic. The atlas defined by these two charts gives $S^{n-1}$ the structure of an analytic manifold. A slight modification of one chart (flipping the sign of the last coordinate) yields an oriented atlas, making $S^{n-1}$ an oriented analytic manifold.
