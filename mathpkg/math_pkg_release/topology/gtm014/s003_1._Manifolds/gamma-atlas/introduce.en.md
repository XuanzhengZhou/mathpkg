A $\Gamma$-atlas generalizes the usual notion of a smooth atlas by replacing the pseudogroup of $C^\infty$ diffeomorphisms with an arbitrary pseudogroup $\Gamma$ on $\mathbf{R}^n$. The underlying space $X$ is required to be Hausdorff and second-countable, and the charts are local homeomorphisms from $X$ into $\mathbf{R}^n$.

Condition (a) requires that the domains of the charts cover all of $X$. Condition (b) stipulates that every transition map $\psi \circ \phi^{-1}$ between overlapping charts must belong to the prescribed pseudogroup $\Gamma$, ensuring compatibility of the local $\Gamma$-structures.
