For an immersion $\phi$, every smooth function defined near $p$ can be extended to a smooth function near $q = \phi(p)$ in the ambient manifold $Y$. This means the pullback $\phi^*$ on germs is surjective, making the finite generation claim trivial for immersions. Together with the submersion case, this completes the proof of the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem by factoring an arbitrary $\phi$ as immersion followed by submersions.
