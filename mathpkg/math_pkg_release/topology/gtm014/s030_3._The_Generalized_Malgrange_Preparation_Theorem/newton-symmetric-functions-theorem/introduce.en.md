Newton's classical theorem on symmetric polynomials extends to smooth functions: every smooth symmetric function is a smooth function of the elementary symmetric polynomials. The proof uses the Generalized Malgrange Preparation Theorem with $C_p^\infty(\mathbf{R}^n)$ as a module over $C_q^\infty(\mathbf{R}^n)$ via the map $g = (g_1, \ldots, g_n)$. A special case is Whitney's theorem: every smooth even function is of the form $g(x^2)$.
