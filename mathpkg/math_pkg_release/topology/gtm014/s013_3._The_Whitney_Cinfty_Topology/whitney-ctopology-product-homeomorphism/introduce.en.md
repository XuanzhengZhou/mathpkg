This proposition establishes that the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology on spaces of smooth maps is compatible with the cartesian product of target manifolds. Specifically, a pair of smooth maps $(f, g)$ from $X$ into $Y$ and $Z$ respectively corresponds naturally to a single smooth map $f \times g$ from $X$ into the product manifold $Y \times Z$, and this identification is a homeomorphism. The proof relies on Lemma 3.7 (fiber product neighborhood lemma for proper maps) applied to the jet bundles $J^k(X, Y)$ and $J^k(X, Z)$ over $X$. This result is fundamental for establishing the continuity of operations such as composition in the Whitney topology.
