This is a technical lemma in general topology concerning the fiber product $A \times_P B$ of two spaces fibered over a base $P$. It asserts that when $P$ is Hausdorff, locally compact, and paracompact, and the restrictions of the projections to subsets $K \subset A$ and $L \subset B$ are proper maps, any open neighborhood of $K \times_P L$ in the fiber product contains a product-like neighborhood $V \times_P W$. The lemma is a key ingredient in proving the continuity of composition and the product homeomorphism in the Whitney $C^\infty$ topology on spaces of smooth maps.
