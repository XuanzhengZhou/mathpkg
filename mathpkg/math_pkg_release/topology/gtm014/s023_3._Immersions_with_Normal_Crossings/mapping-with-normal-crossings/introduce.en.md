An immersion $f: X \to Y$ has normal crossings if the multi-jet extensions $f^{(s)}: X^{(s)} \to Y^{s}$ are transverse to the diagonal $\Delta Y^{s}$ for every $s > 1$. Here $X^{(s)}$ denotes the set of $s$-tuples of distinct points in $X$, and $\Delta Y^{s}$ is the diagonal in the $s$-fold product $Y^{s}$. This condition controls how the image of $f$ sits inside $Y$ by requiring that self-intersections be transverse and that multiple branches meet in a controlled way analogous to linear subspaces in general position.
