Within the space of all immersions of $X$ into $Y$, those that additionally satisfy the normal crossings condition form a dense subset. This is an immediate consequence of Proposition 3.2, since the set of mappings with normal crossings is dense in all smooth maps, and restricting to the subspace of immersions preserves density. Thus, normal crossings is a generic property among immersions.
