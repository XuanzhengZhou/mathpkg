If an immersion $f: X \to Y$ is stable (meaning it is preserved in form under small perturbations), then it must automatically satisfy the normal crossings condition. The proof is immediate: any map equivalent to an immersion with normal crossings is itself an immersion with normal crossings. This establishes that for immersions, stability forces the geometric regularity encoded by normal crossings.
