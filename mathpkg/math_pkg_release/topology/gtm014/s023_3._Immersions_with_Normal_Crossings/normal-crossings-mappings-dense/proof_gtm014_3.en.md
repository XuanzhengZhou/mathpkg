Let $f: X \to Y$ be an immersion. Let $\beta^{s}: J_s^{0}(X, Y) \to \cdots$ be the jet bundle map. The proof proceeds by applying the Thom transversality theorem to show that normal crossings maps are residual (and hence dense) in $C^{\infty}(X, Y)$. The multi-jet transversality condition defining normal crossings is an open condition in the jet space, and by the Thom transversality theorem, maps whose jet extensions are transverse to a given stratified set form a residual subset.
