Let $V$ be a vector space and let $H_1, \ldots, H_r$ be subspaces of $V$. Then $H_1, \ldots, H_r$ are said to be in general position if for every sequence of integers $i_1, \ldots, i_s$ with $1 \leq i_1 < \cdots < i_s \leq r$,
$$
\operatorname{codim} (H_{i_1} \cap \cdots \cap H_{i_s}) = \operatorname{codim} (H_{i_1}) + \cdots + \operatorname{codim} (H_{i_s}).
$$

\textit{Note.} In the case $r = 2$, $H_1$ and $H_2$ are in general position iff $H_1 + H_2 = V$. For
$$
\dim (H_1 + H_2) = \dim H_1 + \dim H_2 - \dim (H_1 \cap H_2)
$$
$$
= n - (\operatorname{codim} H_1 + \operatorname{codim} H_2 - \operatorname{codim} (H_1 \cap H_2)).
$$
So $\dim (H_1 + H_2) = n$ iff $\operatorname{codim} (H_1 \cap H_2) = \operatorname{codim} (H_1) + \operatorname{codim} (H_2)$.
