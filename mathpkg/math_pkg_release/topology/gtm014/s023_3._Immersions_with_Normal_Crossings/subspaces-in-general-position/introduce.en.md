A collection of subspaces $H_1, \ldots, H_r$ of a vector space $V$ is in general position when the codimension of any intersection equals the sum of the individual codimensions. This generalizes the notion of transversality to multiple subspaces: each additional subspace cuts down the dimension by exactly its codimension. For two subspaces, this condition reduces to $H_1 + H_2 = V$, i.e., the subspaces span the whole space. This algebraic definition provides the local model for normal crossings of an immersion near a point with multiple preimages.
