Mappings with normal crossings form a dense subset of $C^\infty(X, Y)$. The proof uses the jet transversality theorem: for an immersion $f$, the condition $f^{(s)} \pitchfork \Delta Y^s$ can be phrased as a transversality condition in an appropriate jet bundle, and Thom's transversality theorem implies density.
