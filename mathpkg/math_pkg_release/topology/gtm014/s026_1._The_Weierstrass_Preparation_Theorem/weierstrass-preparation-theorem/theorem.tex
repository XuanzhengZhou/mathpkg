Let $F$ be a complex-valued holomorphic function defined on a nbhd of $0$ in $\mathbf{C} \times \mathbf{C}^n$ satisfying:
(a) $F(w, 0) = w^k g(w)$ where $g$ is holomorphic near $0 \in \mathbf{C}$,
(b) $g(0) \neq 0$.

Then there exist a holomorphic function $q$ near $0 \in \mathbf{C} \times \mathbf{C}^n$ and holomorphic functions $\lambda_0, \ldots, \lambda_{k-1}$ near $0 \in \mathbf{C}^n$ such that
$$(qF)(w, z) = w^k + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} \lambda_i(z)w^i$$
and $q(0) \neq 0$.
