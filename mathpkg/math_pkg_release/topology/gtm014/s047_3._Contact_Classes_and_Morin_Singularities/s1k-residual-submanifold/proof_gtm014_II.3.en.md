Just apply the Thom Transversality Theorem. Since $S_{1^k}$ is a submanifold (in fact, an immersed submanifold) of $J^k(X, Y)$ of codimension $k$ (by Proposition 3.13 and Theorem 3.12), the Thom Transversality Theorem guarantees that for a residual set of maps $f: X \to Y$, the $k$-jet extension $j^k f$ is transverse to $S_{1^k}$. When $j^k f \pitchfork S_{1^k}$, the preimage $(j^k f)^{-1}(S_{1^k}) = S_{1^k}(f)$ is a submanifold of $X$ of the same codimension as $S_{1^k}$ in $J^k(X, Y)$, namely $k$.
