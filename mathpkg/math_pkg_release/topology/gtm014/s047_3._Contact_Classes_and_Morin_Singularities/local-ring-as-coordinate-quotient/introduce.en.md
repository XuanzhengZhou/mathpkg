This lemma provides the fundamental computational description of the local ring $\mathcal{R}_f(p)$ of a map germ at a singularity. It identifies $\mathcal{R}_f(p)$ with the quotient of the ring of smooth function germs on the source by the ideal generated by the coordinate functions of the map. This makes $\mathcal{R}_f(p)$ a concrete algebraic object — a finite-dimensional $\mathbf{R}$-algebra when $f$ is finite — that can be studied using commutative algebra. The proof combines Lemma 3.6's computation of the ideal $\mathcal{I}_p(A,B)$ with the definitions of $\mathcal{R}_{A,B}$ and $\mathcal{R}_f(p)$.
