By Definition 3.2, the local ring of the contact of $B$ with $A$ is $\mathcal{R}_{A,B} = C_p^\infty(A) / \mathcal{I}_p(A,B)$. Applying this to $A = \mathbf{R}^n \times \{0\}$ (identified with $\mathbf{R}^n$) and $B = \operatorname{graph} f$ in $\mathbf{R}^n \times \mathbf{R}^m$, we have
$$
\mathcal{R}_{\mathbf{R}^n \times \{0\}, \operatorname{graph} f} = C_0^\infty(\mathbf{R}^n) / \mathcal{I}_{(0,0)}(\mathbf{R}^n \times \{0\}, \operatorname{graph} f).
$$

By Lemma 3.6, $\mathcal{I}_{(0,0)}(\mathbf{R}^n \times \{0\}, \operatorname{graph} f) = (f_1, \ldots, f_m)$, the ideal generated by the coordinate functions of $f$ pulled back via the inclusion $i_{\mathbf{R}^n \times \{0\}}^*$.

Now, by definition of the local ring of the singularity, $\mathcal{R}_f(0)$ is defined as $\mathcal{R}_{\mathbf{R}^n \times \{0\}, \operatorname{graph} f}$ (after identifying $\mathbf{R}^n$ with $\mathbf{R}^n \times \{0\}$). Therefore
$$
\mathcal{R}_f(0) = C_0^\infty(\mathbf{R}^n) / (f_1, \ldots, f_m),
$$
as claimed.
