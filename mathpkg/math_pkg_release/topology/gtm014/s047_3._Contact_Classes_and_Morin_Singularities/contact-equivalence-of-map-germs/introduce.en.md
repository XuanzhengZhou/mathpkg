Contact equivalence of map germs is defined by reducing to the previously defined notion of contact of submanifold germs: two map germs are contact equivalent if their graphs, as submanifolds of the product manifold, have the same contact with the zero-section $X \times \{q\}$. This definition elegantly bridges the geometric theory of contact of submanifolds with the study of singularities of smooth maps. Together with Theorem 3.3, it establishes that the local ring $\mathcal{R}_f(p)$ is a complete invariant of the contact equivalence class.
