The space $H_{k,l}^n$ of $k$th-order contact elements classifies submanifold germs up to $k$th-order contact. This lemma establishes that $H_{k,l}^n$ is a smooth manifold and a fiber bundle over the Grassmannian $G_{l,n}$, with fiber given by the higher-order jet data beyond first order. For $k=1$, $H_{1,l}^n$ is diffeomorphic to $G_{l,n}$ itself. This manifold structure is essential for defining contact classes $S_\mathcal{R}$ as submanifolds of jet bundles in Theorem 3.12, which in turn is crucial for the Thom-Boardman stratification theory.
