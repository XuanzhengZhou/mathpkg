This theorem provides the fundamental geometric interpretation of the local ring $\mathcal{R}_f(p)$ of a singularity: two map germs at a point have the same local ring precisely when their graphs have the same contact with the source manifold (embedded as the zero-section in the product). In other words, the algebraic invariant $\mathcal{R}_f(p)$ completely classifies the contact equivalence class of the singularity. This bridges the geometric notion of contact of submanifold germs with the algebraic notion of local rings of map germs.
