This result provides the fundamental gluing construction for difference isomorphisms of vector bundles. Given a difference isomorphism $\alpha: \xi_0 \to \xi_1$ over a pair $(X, A)$ where $X = X_0 \cup X_1$ and $A = X_0 \cap X_1$, there exists a vector bundle $\xi$ over $X$ together with isomorphisms $u_i: \xi_i \to \xi|_{X_i}$ that are compatible with $\alpha$ over $A$. The triple $(\xi, u_1, u_0)$ is unique up to a unique isomorphism. This lemma is essential for constructing vector bundles from clutching data and is used in the proof that the $L$-cup and $K$-cup products are compatible under the natural transformation $L \to K$.
