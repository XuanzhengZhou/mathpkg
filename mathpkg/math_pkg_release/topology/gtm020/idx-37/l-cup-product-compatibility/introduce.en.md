The $L$-cup product is defined on difference isomorphism classes in $L(X, A)$ using the tensor product of complexes. This proposition states that the product operation $\alpha\beta$ respects the equivalence relation used to define $L(X, A)$ from the semigroup $S(X, A)$ of difference isomorphisms. Consequently, the product descends to a well-defined bilinear pairing on the $L$-groups, yielding a cup product structure on $L$-theory analogous to the cup product in ordinary cohomology.
