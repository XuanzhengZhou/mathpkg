The Thom space $T(\xi)$ of a real vector bundle $\xi$ is defined as the quotient of the associated disc bundle $D(\xi)$ by the sphere bundle $S(\xi)$. It collapses all vectors of length $1$ to a single base point. The Thom space is a central construction in algebraic topology, serving as the domain for the Thom isomorphism theorem which relates the cohomology of the base space to that of the Thom space. The definition is independent of the choice of Riemannian metric thanks to the alternative model $P(\xi \oplus \theta^1)/P(\xi)$.
