We have $\xi \oplus \theta^n$ isomorphic to $\xi \times \mathbf{R}^n$, where $\mathbf{R}^n$ is the $n$-dimensional vector bundle over a point. Since $T(\mathbf{R}^n) = S^n$ (the $n$-sphere), applying Proposition 1.3 yields $T(\xi \times \mathbf{R}^n) \cong T(\xi) \wedge T(\mathbf{R}^n) = T(\xi) \wedge S^n$. The smash product of a space with $S^n$ is precisely the $n$-fold suspension $S^n(T(\xi))$, giving the desired homeomorphism $T(\xi \oplus \theta^n) \cong S^n(T(\xi))$.
