If $(u, f): (E, p, B) \rightarrow (E', p', B')$ and $(u', f'): (E', p', B') \rightarrow (E'', p'', B'')$ are bundle morphisms, then the compositions define a bundle morphism
\[
(u'u, f'f): (E, p, B) \rightarrow (E'', p'', B''),
\]
which is defined to be the composition $(u', f') \circ (u, f)$ of $(u, f)$ and $(u', f')$.

The commutativity condition follows from the diagram:
\[
\begin{array}{ccccc}
E & \xrightarrow{u} & E' & \xrightarrow{u'} & E'' \\
p\downarrow & & p'\downarrow & & \downarrow p'' \\
B & \xrightarrow{f} & B' & \xrightarrow{f'} & B''
\end{array}
\]
