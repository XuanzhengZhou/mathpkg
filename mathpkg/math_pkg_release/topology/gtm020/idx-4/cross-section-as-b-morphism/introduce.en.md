The cross sections of a bundle $(E, p, B)$ admit an elegant description in the language of bundle morphisms: they are precisely the $B$-morphisms from the trivial identity bundle $(B, 1_B, B)$ to $(E, p, B)$. In other words, a cross section $s: B \to E$ is exactly the total-space component of a $B$-morphism $(s, 1_B)$. This characterization shows that every general property of bundle morphisms applies to cross sections, unifying these two fundamental notions.
