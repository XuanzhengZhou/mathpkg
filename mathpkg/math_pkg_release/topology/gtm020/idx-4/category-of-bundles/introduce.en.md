The category of bundles $\mathbf{Bun}$ is the fundamental organizing structure for the theory of fibre bundles. Its objects are all bundles $(E, p, B)$, and its morphisms are bundle morphisms $(u, f)$ satisfying $p'u = fp$. Composition is componentwise and identities are the identity maps on total and base spaces. This category provides the natural setting for studying bundle-theoretic constructions: subbundles correspond to monomorphisms, isomorphisms of bundles are precisely the invertible bundle morphisms, and functorial operations such as pullback are defined as functors on $\mathbf{Bun}$.
