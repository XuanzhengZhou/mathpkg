The identity bundle morphism on a bundle $(E, p, B)$ is given by the pair of identity maps $(1_E, 1_B)$. This is trivially a bundle morphism since $p \circ 1_E = p = 1_B \circ p$. Moreover, it is a $B$-morphism because the base map is $1_B$. The identity morphism serves as the identity arrow for each object in the category of bundles, satisfying the required composition laws with any other bundle morphisms to or from $(E, p, B)$.
