The **join** $X \ast Y$ of two spaces is a standard construction in homotopy theory, formed from $X \times [0,1] \times Y$ by collapsing the fibers at the two endpoints. When $X$ and $Y$ are pointed, the join inherits a natural base point. The **(reduced) suspension** $SZ$ is the special case $Z \ast S^0$, obtained by taking the quotient of $Z \times [0,1]$ that collapses the top and bottom copies of $Z$. These constructions are fundamental for defining the Hopf invariant in $K$-theory, where the suspension isomorphism plays a central role.
