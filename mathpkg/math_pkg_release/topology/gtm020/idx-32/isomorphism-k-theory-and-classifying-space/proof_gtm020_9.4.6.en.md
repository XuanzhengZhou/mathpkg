This follows directly from the natural splitting $K_F(X) = \tilde{K}_F(X) \oplus \mathbb{Z}$ and the corepresentation theorem $\theta: [-, B_{(F)}] \xrightarrow{\cong} \tilde{K}_F(-)$. The $\mathbb{Z}$ factor in $B_{(F)} \times \mathbb{Z}$ corresponds to the rank, giving a natural isomorphism $[-, B_{(F)} \times \mathbb{Z}] \cong \tilde{K}_F(-) \oplus \mathbb{Z} \cong K_F(-)$.
