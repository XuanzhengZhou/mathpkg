The Bott periodicity theorem provides a fundamental link between topological $K$-theory and the homotopy groups of the classical groups. Using the homotopy exact sequence of the Stiefel bundle $V_n(F^{2n}) \to G_n(F^{2n})$ and the corepresentation theorem for reduced $K$-theory, one obtains an isomorphism $\tilde{K}_F(S^i) \cong \pi_{i-1}(U_F)$ for sufficiently large $n$. This isomorphism is the key to computing the reduced $K$-theory of spheres, which exhibits periodic behavior depending on the ground field $F$ (real, complex, or quaternionic).
