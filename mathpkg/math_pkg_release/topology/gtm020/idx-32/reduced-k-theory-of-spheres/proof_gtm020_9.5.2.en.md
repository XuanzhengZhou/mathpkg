By the Bott periodicity theorem, $\tilde{K}_F(S^i) \cong \pi_{i-1}(U_F)$ for sufficiently large dimension parameter $n$. The homotopy groups of the classical groups are known:

For $F = \mathbb{R}$: $\pi_0(O) = \mathbb{Z}_2$, $\pi_1(O) = \mathbb{Z}_2$, hence $\tilde{K}_R(S^1) = \pi_0(O) = \mathbb{Z}_2$ and $\tilde{K}_R(S^2) = \pi_1(O) = \mathbb{Z}_2$.

For $F = \mathbb{C}$: $\pi_0(U) = 0$, $\pi_1(U) = \mathbb{Z}$, hence $\tilde{K}_C(S^1) = \pi_0(U) = 0$ and $\tilde{K}_C(S^2) = \pi_1(U) = \mathbb{Z}$.

For $F = \mathbb{H}$: $\pi_0(Sp) = 0$, $\pi_1(Sp) = 0$, hence $\tilde{K}_H(S^1) = \pi_0(Sp) = 0$ and similarly for higher dimensions.

For $i=0$, $\tilde{K}_F(S^0) = \mathbb{Z}$ for all cases since $S^0$ is a two-point space and the rank map gives the splitting.
