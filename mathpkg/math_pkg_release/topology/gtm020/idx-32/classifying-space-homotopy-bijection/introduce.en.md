For any finite connected CW-complex $X$, there exists a sufficiently large integer $k$ such that all homotopy classes of maps from $X$ into the classifying space $B_{(F)}$ are represented by maps factoring through a finite Grassmannian $G_q(F^{2q})$ for $q \geq k$. This means the natural inclusion induces a bijection on homotopy classes, allowing computations in $K$-theory to be reduced to finite-dimensional Grassmannians. The result relies on the compactness of $X$ and the cellular approximation theorem.
