By (2.7), for $k$ such that $\dim X \leq c(k + 1) - 2$, the following bijections are induced by inclusions:

$$[X, G_k(F^{2k})] \rightarrow \cdots \rightarrow [X, G_q(F^{2q})] \rightarrow \cdots$$

Since $X$ is compact, every map $f: X \rightarrow B_{(F)}$ has the image $f(X) \subset G_n(F^{2n})$ for some $n$ with $k \leq n$, and the function $[X, G_q(F^{2q})] \rightarrow [X, B_{(F)}]$ is surjective. Since the image of a homotopy of maps $X \rightarrow B_{(F)}$ lies in some $G_n(F^{2n}) \subset B_{(F)}$ for some $n$ with $k \leq n$, the map is also injective. Hence the inclusion induces a bijection.
