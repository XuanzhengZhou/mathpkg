A manifold is a Hausdorff space equipped with a maximal $C^r$-atlas, which provides a differentiable structure. The underlying topological space must be Hausdorff, and the maximal atlas ensures that all compatible charts are included. By convention, paracompactness is also incorporated into the definition of a manifold, which is equivalent to each connected component being separable or a countable union of compact sets.
