This theorem relates the canonical line bundles on real and complex projective spaces. Part (1) states that pulling back the complex canonical bundle along the natural quotient map yields the complexification of the real canonical bundle. Part (2) and (3) connect the second Stiefel-Whitney class, the first Chern class, and the Euler class. These relations are fundamental for understanding how characteristic classes behave under complexification and realization.
