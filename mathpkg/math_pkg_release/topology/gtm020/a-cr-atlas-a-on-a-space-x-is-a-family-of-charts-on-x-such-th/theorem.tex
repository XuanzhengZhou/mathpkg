(2) For $(U, \phi), (V, \psi) \in A$, the restriction $\psi \phi^{-1} : \phi(U \cap V) \rightarrow \psi(U \cap V)$ is a $C^r$-map (thus a $C^r$-isomorphism with inverse $\phi \psi^{-1}$).

Two $C^r$-atlases $A$ and $A'$ are equivalent provided $A \cup A'$ is a $C^r$-atlas. A maximal $C^r$-atlas $A$ is an atlas such that for every $C^r$-atlas $A' \supset A$ there is the relation $A = A'$. The reader can easily verify that equivalence of $C^r$-atlases is an equivalence relation and that every $C^r$-atlas is included in a unique maximal $C^r$-atlas. Two $C^r$-atlases are equivalent if and only if they determine the same maximal $C^r$-atlas.
