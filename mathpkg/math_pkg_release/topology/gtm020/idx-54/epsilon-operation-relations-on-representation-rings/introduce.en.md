This proposition gives the algebraic relations between the epsilon operations that connect real, complex, and quaternionic representation rings. Specifically, $\varepsilon_U: RO(G) \to R(G)$ is the complexification map on real representations, $\varepsilon_0: R(G) \to RO(G)$ is the restriction (or "realification") of complex representations, and $\varepsilon_{Sp}: R(G) \to RSp(G)$ is the symplectification map. The relations involve the Adams operation $\psi_{-1}$ which corresponds to taking the conjugate representation. These identities are fundamental for understanding how the $\lambda$-ring structures interact under change of coefficient fields.
