Exercise 1 from Chapter 3, Section 9 of Husemoller's Fibre Bundles. This exercise establishes a useful criterion for a vector bundle to be trivial: a $k$-dimensional vector bundle is trivial exactly when it admits $k$ everywhere linearly independent cross sections. This generalizes the familiar fact that a trivial rank-$k$ bundle $B \times \mathbf{F}^k$ has the $k$ constant sections forming the standard basis at each fibre.
