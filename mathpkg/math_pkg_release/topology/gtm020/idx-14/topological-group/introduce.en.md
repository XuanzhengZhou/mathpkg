Definition 1.1 from Chapter 4, Section 1 of Husemoller's Fibre Bundles. A topological group is a group equipped with a topology such that the group operations (multiplication and inversion) are continuous. This is the foundational structure upon which the theory of principal bundles and fibre bundles is built. Key examples include the general linear groups and the classical Lie groups such as orthogonal, unitary, and symplectic groups.
