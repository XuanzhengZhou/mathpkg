The projective bundle $P\xi$ associated with a vector bundle $\xi$ is formed by replacing each fibre $\mathbb{F}^n$ with the projective space $\mathbb{F}P^{n-1}$ of lines through the origin. This construction is fundamental in the theory of characteristic classes, as it provides the geometric setting in which the Stiefel-Whitney and Chern classes are defined via the Leray-Hirsch theorem. The projective bundle is locally trivial and carries a natural inclusion of the projective space fibre.
