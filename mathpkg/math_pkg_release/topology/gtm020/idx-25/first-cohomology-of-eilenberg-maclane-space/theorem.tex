The first nonzero reduced cohomology group of a $K(\Pi, n)$ is in degree $n$ where

$$H^n(K(\Pi, n), \Pi) = \text{Hom}(\Pi, \Pi)$$

which has a canonical element $\iota_n$ corresponding to the identity on $\Pi$. Using $\iota_n$, we define a natural morphism from the group of homotopy classes of maps $[X, K(\Pi, n)]$ to $H^n(X, \Pi)$ by $f \mapsto f^*(\iota_n)$, which is an isomorphism.
