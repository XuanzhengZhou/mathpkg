An Eilenberg-MacLane space $K(\Pi, n)$ is a path-connected topological space whose homotopy groups vanish in all degrees except one. The single nontrivial homotopy group $\Pi$ appears in degree $n$. For $n > 1$, $\Pi$ must be abelian; for $n = 1$, $\Pi$ can be any group; and for $n = 0$, $\Pi$ is any set. These spaces play a fundamental role in algebraic topology as classifying spaces for cohomology.
