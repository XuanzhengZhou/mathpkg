Definition 9.2 from Chapter 4 (General Fibre Bundles) of Husemoller's Fibre Bundles. A principal $G$-bundle $\xi$ over a space $B$ is called numerable if there exists a numerable open covering of $B$ on which the bundle restricts to a trivial bundle. Every numerable principal $G$-bundle is locally trivial, and every locally trivial principal $G$-bundle over a paracompact space is numerable. Numerability is a crucial technical condition that enables the classification theory of principal bundles via homotopy classes of maps into a classifying space.
