Theorem 9.8 from Chapter 4 (General Fibre Bundles) of Husemoller's Fibre Bundles. This theorem establishes the homotopy invariance property for numerable principal $G$-bundles: a numerable bundle over a product $B \times I$ is $G$-isomorphic to the product of its restriction to one end with the interval, regardless of which end is chosen. Consequently, the pullbacks along the inclusion maps $\varepsilon_0, \varepsilon_1: B \hookrightarrow B \times I$ at the two ends yield $B$-isomorphic bundles over $B$. This is a fundamental result that underlies the classification of bundles via homotopy classes of maps into classifying spaces.
