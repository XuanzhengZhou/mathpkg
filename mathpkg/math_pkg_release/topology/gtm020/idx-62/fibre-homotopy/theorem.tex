Let $p: E \rightarrow X$ and $p': E' \rightarrow X$ be two bundles over $X$.
\begin{itemize}
\item A homotopy $f_t: E \rightarrow E'$ is a \textbf{fibre homotopy} if $p' f_t = p$ for all $t \in I$.
\item Two bundle morphisms $f, g: E \rightarrow E'$ are \textbf{fibre homotopic} if there exists a fibre homotopy $f_t: E \rightarrow E'$ with $f_0 = f$ and $f_1 = g$.
\item A bundle morphism $f: E \rightarrow E'$ is a \textbf{fibre homotopy equivalence} if there exists a bundle morphism $g: E' \rightarrow E$ such that $g f$ and $f g$ are fibre homotopic to the identity.
\item Two bundles $E$ and $E'$ have the \textbf{same fibre homotopy type} if there exists a fibre homotopy equivalence $f: E \rightarrow E'$.
\end{itemize}
