Consider the set $F_n(X)$ of all fibre homotopy classes of fibrations with fibre $S^{n-1}$. The transition functions have values in the semigroup $H(n)$ of homotopy equivalences $S^{n-1} \to S^{n-1}$. As in Chaps. 3 and 4, the cofunctor $F_n(X)$ can be represented by $[X, B_{H(n)}]$, where $B_{H(n)}$ is a classifying space for the semigroup $H(n)$ (see Dold and Lashof [1]).

The inclusion $H(n) \to H(n+r)$ defines a map $B_{H(n)} \to B_{H(n+r)}$. We denote by $B_H$ the inductive limit of the sequence
$$B_{H(1)} \to B_{H(2)} \to \cdots \to B_{H(n)} \to \cdots$$

Then $B_H$ admits a natural $H$-space structure, and there is an $H$-space map $B_O \to B_H$ which is induced by the inclusion $O(n) \to H(n)$. By analogy with $\widetilde{K}O(X)$, we define $\widetilde{K}_{\text{Top}}(X) = [X, B_H]$, and $J(X)$ is identified as the image of $\widetilde{K}O(X)$ under the induced map, hence a subgroup.
