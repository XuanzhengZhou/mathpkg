If an orientable manifold $M$ admits an everywhere-nonzero vector field, then its Euler characteristic must vanish: $\chi(M) = 0$. This corollary follows immediately from the relation $e(\tau(M)) = \chi(M) \overline{\omega}_M$ (Theorem 7.2) together with the fact that the Euler class $e(\tau(M))$ vanishes when the tangent bundle admits a nowhere-zero section (by 16(8.3)). The result provides a topological obstruction to the existence of non-vanishing vector fields on orientable manifolds, and is closely related to the Poincare-Hopf theorem.
