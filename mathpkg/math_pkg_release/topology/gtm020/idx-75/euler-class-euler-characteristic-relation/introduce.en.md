For a closed connected manifold $M^n$ with a $\mathbf{Z}$-orientation $\omega_M$, there is a fundamental relation between the Euler class of the tangent bundle $e(\tau(M))$ and the Euler characteristic $\chi(M)$. Specifically, the Euler class equals the Euler characteristic multiplied by the dual orientation class $\overline{\omega}_M$. This theorem connects the differential-geometric invariant $e(\tau(M))$ (which measures the obstruction to finding a nowhere-vanishing section of the tangent bundle) with the purely topological invariant $\chi(M)$.
