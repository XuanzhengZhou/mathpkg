The fibration $SO(n) \to O(n) \xrightarrow{\det} \mathbf{Z}_2$ expresses the fact that the special orthogonal group is the index-2 subgroup of orientation-preserving transformations within the full orthogonal group. Since the fibre $\mathbf{Z}_2$ is a discrete group, its higher homotopy groups vanish, and the homotopy exact sequence immediately yields that the inclusion $SO(n) \hookrightarrow O(n)$ induces isomorphisms on $\pi_i$ for all $i \geq 1$. In other words, $SO(n)$ and $O(n)$ have identical higher homotopy groups.
