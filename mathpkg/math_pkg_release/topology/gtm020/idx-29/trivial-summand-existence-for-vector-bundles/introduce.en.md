This proposition gives the first decomposition theorem for vector bundles: when the base space dimension $n$ is small relative to the fibre dimension $k$ (specifically $n \leq ck-1$), every $k$-dimensional vector bundle splits off a trivial line bundle as a direct summand. The proof uses the connectivity of the sphere $F^k \setminus \{0\}$ (which is $(ck-2)$-connected) and obstruction theory to construct a nowhere-vanishing cross section, which then determines the trivial summand.
