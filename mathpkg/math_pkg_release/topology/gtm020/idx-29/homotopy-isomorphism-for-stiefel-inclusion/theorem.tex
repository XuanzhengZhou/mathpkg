Let $j: V_k(F^n) \to V_{k+1}(F^{n+1})$ be the map defined by
$$j(v_1, \ldots, v_k) = (v_1, \ldots, v_k, e_{n+1}).$$
Then $j$ can be viewed as the inclusion of the fibre over $e_{n+1}$ of the map
$p: V_{k+1}(F^{n+1}) \to V_1(F^{n+1})$, where $p(v_1, \ldots, v_{k+1}) = v_{k+1}$.
From the homotopy exact sequence of this fibration, the homomorphism
$$j_*: \pi_i(V_k(F^n)) \to \pi_i(V_{k+1}(F^{n+1}))$$
is an isomorphism for $i \leq c(n+1) - 3$, where $c = \dim_{\mathbf{R}} F$.
