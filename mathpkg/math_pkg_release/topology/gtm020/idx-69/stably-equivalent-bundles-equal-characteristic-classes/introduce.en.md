If two vector bundles become isomorphic after adding trivial bundles of possibly different dimensions, they are called stably equivalent. This proposition shows that the total Stiefel-Whitney class (for real bundles) and the total Chern class (for complex bundles) are invariants of the stable equivalence class. The proof follows directly from the Whitney sum formula and the fact that trivial bundles have trivial characteristic classes.
