The integral cohomology ring of the infinite complex Grassmannian $G_n(C^\infty)$ is a polynomial ring freely generated by the universal Chern classes $c_1, \dots, c_n$. Each $c_i$ is the $i$-th Chern class $c_i(\gamma_n)$ of the universal $n$-dimensional vector bundle $\gamma_n$ over $G_n(C^\infty)$. This result identifies $G_n(C^\infty)$ as the classifying space $BU(n)$ and shows that its cohomology is isomorphic to the ring of symmetric polynomials. Some expositions use this theorem as the starting point for the theory of characteristic classes.
