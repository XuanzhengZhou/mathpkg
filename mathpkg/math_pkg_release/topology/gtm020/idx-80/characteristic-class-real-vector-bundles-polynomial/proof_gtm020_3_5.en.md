By Theorem 5.2, the cohomology ring $H^*(G_n(R^\infty); \mathbb{Z}_2) = \mathbb{Z}_2[w_1, \dots, w_n]$ is a polynomial ring freely generated by the universal Stiefel-Whitney classes. Let $\gamma_n$ be the universal bundle over $G_n(R^\infty)$. For any characteristic class $\phi$, the value $\phi(G_n(R^\infty))(\gamma_n)$ is an element of $H^*(G_n(R^\infty); \mathbb{Z}_2)$, hence a polynomial $q(w_1, \dots, w_n)$. For any real $n$-plane bundle $\xi$ over $X$, there exists a classifying map $f: X \rightarrow G_n(R^\infty)$ such that $\xi \cong f^*(\gamma_n)$. By naturality of characteristic classes, $\phi(X)(\xi) = \phi(X)(f^*(\gamma_n)) = f^*(\phi(G_n(R^\infty))(\gamma_n)) = f^*(q(w_1, \dots, w_n)) = q(f^*(w_1), \dots, f^*(w_n)) = q(w_1(\xi), \dots, w_n(\xi))$. Uniqueness of $q$ follows from the algebraic independence of $w_1, \dots, w_n$ in $H^*(G_n(R^\infty); \mathbb{Z}_2)$.
