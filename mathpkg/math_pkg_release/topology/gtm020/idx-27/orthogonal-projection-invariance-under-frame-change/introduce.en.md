This proposition verifies that the orthogonal projection of a vector $x \in F^n$ onto a $k$-dimensional subspace $W \subset F^n$ is well-defined, regardless of which orthonormal basis of $W$ is used to compute it. In formulas, if two orthonormal $k$-frames $(v_i)$ and $(v'_i)$ span the same subspace and differ by the action of a unitary matrix $a \in U_F(k)$, then the projection $\sum_i \langle x \mid v_i \rangle v_i$ coincides with $\sum_i \langle x \mid v'_i \rangle v'_i$. This fact is essential for defining the projection map $\pi: G_k(F^n) \times F^n \to F^n$ in the proof of local triviality.
