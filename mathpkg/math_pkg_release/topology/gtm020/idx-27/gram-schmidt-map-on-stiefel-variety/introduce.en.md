The Gram-Schmidt map $GS$ sends any $k$-tuple of linearly independent vectors in $F^n$ to an orthonormal $k$-frame spanning the same $k$-dimensional subspace. It is constructed by the classical Gram-Schmidt orthonormalization procedure: normalize the first vector, then for each subsequent vector subtract its projections onto the previously constructed orthonormal vectors and normalize. This map is continuous and serves as a key tool in proving that the projection $p: V_k(F^n) \to G_k(F^n)$ is locally trivial.
