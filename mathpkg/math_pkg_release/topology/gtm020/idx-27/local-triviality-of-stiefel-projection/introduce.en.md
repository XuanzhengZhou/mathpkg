This theorem establishes that the natural projection $p$ from the Stiefel manifold $V_k(F^n)$ of orthonormal $k$-frames to the Grassmannian $G_k(F^n)$ of $k$-dimensional subspaces is a locally trivial fibre bundle with fibre the unitary group $U_F(k)$. The proof is elementary and geometric: for a given subspace $W$ with orthonormal basis $(v_1,\ldots,v_k)$, one defines an open neighborhood $O(W)$ of nearby subspaces $W'$ and a local section by orthogonally projecting the $v_i$ onto $W'$, then applying the Gram-Schmidt map to obtain an orthonormal frame. This produces a local trivialization $O(W) \times U_F(k) \cong p^{-1}(O(W))$.
