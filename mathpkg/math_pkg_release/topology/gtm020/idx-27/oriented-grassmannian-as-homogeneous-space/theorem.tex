For $F = \mathbf{R}$ or $\mathbf{C}$, let $SG_k(F^n)$ denote the space of oriented $k$-subspaces, that is, subspaces together with an equivalence class of orthonormal bases that differ by the action of $SU_F(k)$. There is a homeomorphism

$$\psi_k^n: U_F(n)/(SU_F(k) \times U_F(n-k)) \xrightarrow{\cong} SG_k(F^n)$$

given by associating to each coset the oriented $k$-subspace spanned by the first $k$ columns of the representative matrix. A map $SG_k(F^n) \rightarrow G_k(F^n)$ results by forgetting the orientation. Over each point of $G_k(\mathbf{R}^n)$ are precisely two points representing the two orientations of a subspace, and over each point of $G_k(\mathbf{C}^n)$ is a circle $S^1$.
