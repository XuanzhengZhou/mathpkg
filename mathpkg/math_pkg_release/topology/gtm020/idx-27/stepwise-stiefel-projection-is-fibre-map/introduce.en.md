This corollary of Theorem 3.6 states that the natural projection $q: V_{k+1}(F^n) \to V_k(F^n)$ forgetting the last vector of an orthonormal $(k+1)$-frame is a fibre map. The fibre over a $k$-frame is the unit sphere in the orthogonal complement of its span, namely the sphere $S^{n-k-1}$ ($\mathbf{R}$), $S^{2n-2k-1}$ ($\mathbf{C}$), or $S^{4n-4k-1}$ ($\mathbf{H}$). This yields a sequence of sphere bundles linking consecutive Stiefel manifolds, which is fundamental for computing their homotopy groups by induction.
