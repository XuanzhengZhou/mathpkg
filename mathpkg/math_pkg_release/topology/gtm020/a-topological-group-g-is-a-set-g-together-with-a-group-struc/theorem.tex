The above condition of continuity on $(s, t) \mapsto st^{-1}$ is equivalent to the statement that $(s, t) \mapsto st$ is a map $G \times G \rightarrow G$ and $s \mapsto s^{-1}$ is a map $G \rightarrow G$.

Examples. The real line $R$ with addition as group operation, the real line minus zero $R - \{0\}$ with multiplication as group operation, the full linear groups of nonsingular matrices $GL(n, R)$ and $GL(n, C)$, and the orthogonal, unitary, and symplectic groups $O(n), SO(n), U(n), SU(n)$, and $Sp(n)$ are all topological groups. The orthogonal, unitary, and symplectic groups are considered in detail in Chap. 7.
