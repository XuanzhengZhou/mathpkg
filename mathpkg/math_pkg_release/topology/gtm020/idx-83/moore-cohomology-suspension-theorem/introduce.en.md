This theorem is the cohomological dual of Moore's homology suspension theorem (Theorem 1.3). For a path connected space $X$ whose homology is $R$-projective of finite type, the algebra morphism $H^*(\beta): H^*(\Omega S(X)) \to H^*(X)$ factors through the tensor Hopf algebra $T'H^*(X)$ and yields an isomorphism of Hopf algebras. An important additional feature is Steenrod algebra compatibility: when $R = \mathbb{F}_p$, the mod $p$ Steenrod algebra action on $H^*(X)$ induces an action on $T'H^*(X)$, and the isomorphism preserves this action. The result follows from Theorem 1.3 by duality.
