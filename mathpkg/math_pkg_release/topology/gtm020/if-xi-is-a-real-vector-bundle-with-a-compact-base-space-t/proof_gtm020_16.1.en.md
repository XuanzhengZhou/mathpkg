The space $T(\xi \times \eta)$ is the one-point compactification of $E(\xi \times \eta)$ and $T(\xi) \wedge T(\eta)$ of $E(\xi) \times E(\eta)$. The result follows from $E(\xi \times \eta) = E(\xi) \times E(\eta)$.

The proposition holds more generally. One can construct a homeomorphism

$$f: (D(\xi \times \eta), S(\xi \times \eta)) \rightarrow (D(\xi) \times D(\eta), S(\xi) \times D(\eta) \cup D(\xi) \times S(\eta))$$

defined by the relation

$$f(x, y) = \left( \max \left( \|x\|, \|y\| \right) \right)^{-1} \left( \|x\|^2 + \|y\|^2 \right)^{-1/2}(x, y)$$
