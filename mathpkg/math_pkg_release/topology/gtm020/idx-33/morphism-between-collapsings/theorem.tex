Let $(v, f): (\xi, A, t) \rightarrow (\xi', A', t')$ be a morphism of bundles with trivializations, where $v: \xi \rightarrow \xi'$ is a vector bundle morphism, $f: X \rightarrow X'$ is a continuous map with $f(A) \subset A'$, and the trivializations satisfy the compatibility condition $t' \circ v|_{E(\xi|A)} = t$. Then there exists a unique morphism
$$(w, g): (\xi/t, u, r) \rightarrow (\xi'/t', u', r')$$
where $g: X/A \rightarrow X'/A'$ is induced by $f$, such that the diagram

\[
\begin{CD}
\xi @>v>> \xi' \\
@VuVV @VVu'V \\
\xi/t @>w>> \xi'/t'
\end{CD}
\]

commutes. Moreover, $(w, g)$ is unique with respect to this property, and if $(v, f)$ is an isomorphism with $f(A) = A'$, then $(w, g)$ is an isomorphism.
