**Proof.** The commutative diagram defines $w$ uniquely: since $u$ is a fibrewise isomorphism, we must have $w = u' \circ v \circ u^{-1}$. The map $g: X/A \rightarrow X'/A'$ is the map induced by $f$ on quotient spaces, which is well-defined since $f(A) \subset A'$. One verifies that $w$ is well-defined as a map of total spaces because the compatibility condition $t' \circ v|_{E(\xi|A)} = t$ ensures that the identification defining $\xi/t$ is respected. If $(v, f)$ is an isomorphism, its inverse $(v^{-1}, f^{-1})$ defines the inverse of $(w, g)$ by the same construction, so $(w, g)$ is an isomorphism.
