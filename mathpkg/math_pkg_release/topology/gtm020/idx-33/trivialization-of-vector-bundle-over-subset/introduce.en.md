A trivialization of a vector bundle over a subset provides a way to identify all fibres above that subset with a single fixed vector space $F^n$. This concept is fundamental in relative K-theory, as it enables the collapsing construction: by identifying fibres over a closed subspace to a single fibre, one can pass from a bundle over $X$ to a bundle over the quotient space $X/A$.
