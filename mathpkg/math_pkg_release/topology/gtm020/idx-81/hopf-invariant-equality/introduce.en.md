This proposition establishes that the Hopf invariant defined using $K$-theory coincides with the classical cohomological Hopf invariant for even $n$. The proof relies on the fact that the Chern character is a ring homomorphism, which translates the multiplicative relation $a_f^2 = h_f b_f$ in $K$-theory to the corresponding relation $(a^*)^2 = h_f^* b^*$ in rational cohomology.
