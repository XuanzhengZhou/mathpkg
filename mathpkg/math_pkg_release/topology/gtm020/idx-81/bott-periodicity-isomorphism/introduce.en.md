This fundamental theorem establishes two key results: the Bott periodicity isomorphism, which states that the Bott map $\beta \colon \tilde{K}(X) \to \tilde{K}(X \wedge S^2)$ is an isomorphism for all compact spaces $X$, and the integrality theorem, which states that the Chern character maps the reduced $K$-theory of even-dimensional spheres isomorphically onto their integral cohomology. Together, these imply $\tilde{K}(S^{2k+1}) = 0$ for all $k$, and provide the foundation for the $K$-theory of spheres.
