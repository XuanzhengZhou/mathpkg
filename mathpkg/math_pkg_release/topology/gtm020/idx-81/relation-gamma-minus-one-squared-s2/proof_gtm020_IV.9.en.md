Since $\mathrm{ch}$ is a ring homomorphism,
$$\mathrm{ch}((\gamma - 1)^2) = \mathrm{ch}(\gamma - 1) \cdot \mathrm{ch}(\gamma - 1) = c_1(\gamma)^2 = 0.$$
Since $\mathrm{ch}$ is an isomorphism on $K(S^2)$ by Proposition 9.1, it follows that $(\gamma - 1)^2 = 0$ in $K(S^2)$.
