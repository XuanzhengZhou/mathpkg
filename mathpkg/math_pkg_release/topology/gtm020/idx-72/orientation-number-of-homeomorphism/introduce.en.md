The orientation number is extended from diffeomorphisms to arbitrary homeomorphisms via the homological characterization given in Theorem 3.4. For a homeomorphism $f: U \to V$ and a point $x \in U$, the local orientation number $O_x(f)$ is defined by the action of $f_*$ on the canonical generator $\alpha_x$ of the local homology group $H_n(U, U - x)$. This definition generalizes the notion of orientation to the topological category.
