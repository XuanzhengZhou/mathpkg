The orientation number $O(f)$ behaves multiplicatively under composition of diffeomorphisms and is invariant under restriction to open connected subsets. This proposition formalizes the basic algebraic properties of the orientation number, showing that it defines a functorial invariant on the category of open connected subsets of $\mathbf{R}^n$ with diffeomorphisms as morphisms.
