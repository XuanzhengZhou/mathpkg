Let $X$ be a space with the following property $(S)$: For each vector bundle $\xi$ over $X$, there exists a vector bundle $\eta$ over $X$ such that $\xi \oplus \eta$ is isomorphic to a trivial bundle. Let $\alpha: \text{Vect}_F(X) \to K_F(X)$ be the canonical map to the group completion.

Then two vector bundles $\xi$ and $\eta$ over $X$ are $s$-equivalent if and only if $\alpha(\xi) = \alpha(\eta)$ in $K_F(X)$. Consequently, there is a natural bijection between $s$-equivalence classes of vector bundles over $X$ and elements of $K_F(X)$.
