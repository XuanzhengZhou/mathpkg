This theorem establishes the fundamental relationship between K-theory and stable equivalence of vector bundles. For a space $X$ satisfying property (S) — that every vector bundle admits a complementary bundle making the Whitney sum trivial — the canonical map $\alpha: \text{Vect}_F(X) \to K_F(X)$ from isomorphism classes to the Grothendieck group completion identifies precisely the $s$-equivalence classes.

The result shows that $K_F(X)$ (and consequently $\tilde{K}_F(X)$) precisely classifies vector bundles up to stable equivalence. This is the core insight of topological K-theory: the Grothendieck construction of the semiring of vector bundles yields an algebraic invariant that captures exactly the stable isomorphism type. The proof uses the defining properties of the completion map, particularly the relation $\alpha(\zeta \oplus \theta^q) = \alpha(\zeta)$.
