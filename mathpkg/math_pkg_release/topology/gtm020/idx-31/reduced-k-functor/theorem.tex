The reduced $K_F$ cofunctor, denoted $\tilde{K}_F$, is defined by
$$
\tilde{K}_F(X) = \ker(\text{rk}: K_F(X) \to \mathbf{Z})
$$
where $\text{rk}: K_F(X) \to \mathbf{Z}$ is the rank homomorphism sending a formal difference $\xi - \eta$ to $\dim(\xi) - \dim(\eta)$.

The cofunctor $K_F$ splits as
$$
K_F(X) = \tilde{K}_F(X) \oplus \mathbf{Z}.
$$

For a map $f: Y \to X$, the induced map $\tilde{K}_F(f)$ is the restriction of $K_F(f)$ to the reduced summand, making $\tilde{K}_F$ a cofunctor.
