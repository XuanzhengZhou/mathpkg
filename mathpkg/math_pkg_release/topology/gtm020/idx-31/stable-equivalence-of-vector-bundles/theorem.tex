Two vector bundles $\xi$ and $\eta$ over a space $X$ are $s$-equivalent, denoted $\xi \sim \eta$, provided there exist nonnegative integers $q$ and $n$ such that
$$
\xi \oplus \theta^n \quad \text{and} \quad \eta \oplus \theta^q
$$
are isomorphic over $X$, where $\theta^k$ denotes the trivial vector bundle of rank $k$ over $X$.

A bundle $\xi$ that is $s$-equivalent to the zero bundle $0$ is called $s$-trivial.

Stable equivalence ($s$-equivalence) is an equivalence relation on isomorphism classes of vector bundles. Isomorphic vector bundles are $s$-equivalent, so $s$-equivalence descends to a relation on $\text{Vect}_F(X)$.
