For two fibre bundles $$\xi$$ and $$\eta$$ over the same base space $$B$$ that are locally isomorphic (i.e., every point in $$B$$ has a neighborhood over which the restrictions of $$\xi$$ and $$\eta$$ are isomorphic), the property of being locally isomorphic is preserved when both bundles are restricted to any subset $$A \subset B$$. This is a direct consequence of the functorial behavior of the pullback with respect to restrictions and the fact that local isomorphisms are detected on open covers.
