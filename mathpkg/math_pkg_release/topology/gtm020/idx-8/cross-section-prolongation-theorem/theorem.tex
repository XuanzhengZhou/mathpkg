Let $$\xi = (E, p, B)$$ be a locally trivial bundle with fibre $$F$$, where $$(B, A)$$ is a relative $$CW$$-complex. Then all cross sections $$s$$ of $$\xi|A$$ prolong to a cross section $$s^*$$ of $$\xi$$ under either of the following hypotheses:

$$\text{(H1)}$$ The space $$F$$ is $$(m - 1)$$-connected for each $$m \leq \dim B$$.

$$\text{(H2)}$$ There is a relative $$CW$$-complex $$(Y, X)$$ such that $$B = Y \times I$$ and $$A = (X \times I) \cup (Y \times 0) \cup (Y_{n-1} \times I)$$.

Finally, if $$\dim B = \infty$$, $$F$$ is $$n$$-connected for each $$n$$, and we construct inductively cross sections $$s_n$$ of $$\xi|B_n$$ such that $$s_n|B_{n-1} = s_{n-1}$$ and $$s_{-1} = s$$. We define a cross section $$s^*$$ of $$\xi$$ by the requirement that $$s^*|B_n = s_n$$.
