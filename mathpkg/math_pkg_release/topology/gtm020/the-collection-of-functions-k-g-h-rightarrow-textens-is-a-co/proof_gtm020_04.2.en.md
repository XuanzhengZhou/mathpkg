Let $f : X \rightarrow Y$ and $g : Y \rightarrow Z$ be two maps, and let $\xi$ be a numerable principal $G$-bundle over $Z$. Then the bundles $(gf)^*(\xi)$ and $f^*(g^*(\xi))$ are isomorphic over $X$ by the construction used in 2(5.7). Therefore, we have $k_G([g][f]) = k_G([f])k_G([g])$. Since $\xi$ and $l_Z^*(\xi)$ are isomorphic over $Z$, the function $k_G(1_Z)$ equals the identity on $k_G(Z)$.
