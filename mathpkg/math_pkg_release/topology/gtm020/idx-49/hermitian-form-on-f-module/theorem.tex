A hermitian form on an $F$-module $M$ (where $F = \mathbf{R}$ or $\mathbf{C}$) is a function $\beta: M \times M \rightarrow F$ such that $\beta$ is $F$-linear in the first variable and satisfies $\beta(y, x) = \overline{\beta(x, y)}$ for all $x, y \in M$. When $M$ is a $G$-module, $\beta$ is called $G$-invariant if $\beta(sx, sy) = \beta(x, y)$ for all $s \in G$ and $x, y \in M$.
