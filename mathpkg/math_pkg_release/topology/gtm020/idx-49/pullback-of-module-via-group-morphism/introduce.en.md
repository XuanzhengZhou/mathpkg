For a continuous group homomorphism $u: G \to H$, every $H$-module $M$ pulls back to a $G$-module $u^*(M)$ with the same underlying vector space and $G$-action defined via $u$. This pullback respects direct sums, tensor products, and exterior powers, making it a $\lambda$-semiring morphism. It induces a contravariant functor $R_F(-)$ from topological groups to $\lambda$-rings, turning the representation ring into a functor on the category of topological groups.
