This theorem establishes that maximal tori in a compact Lie group are unique up to conjugation. The proof relies on the fact that tori are monic (have a dense cyclic subgroup): given a generator $a$ of a torus $T'$, the condition $G = \bigcup sTs^{-1}$ guarantees $a \in sTs^{-1}$ for some $s$, and the density of powers then forces $T' \subset sTs^{-1}$. A dimension argument then yields equality when $T'$ is also maximal. This conjugacy is fundamental to the Cartan-Weyl theory of compact Lie groups.
