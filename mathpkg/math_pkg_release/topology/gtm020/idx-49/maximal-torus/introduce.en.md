A maximal torus of a compact group $G$ is a torus subgroup whose conjugates cover the entire group. This concept is central to the structure and representation theory of compact Lie groups. Examples include the diagonal matrices in $U(n)$ and $SU(n)$. The inclusion of a maximal torus induces an injection of representation rings, and the Weyl group acts on the maximal torus, controlling which characters descend to the whole group.
