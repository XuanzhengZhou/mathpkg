A subgroup $T$ is a maximal torus of a compact group $G$ provided $T$ is a torus with $G = \bigcup_{s \in G} sTs^{-1}$. The condition $G = \bigcup_{s \in G} sTs^{-1}$ means that every conjugacy class of $G$ intersects $T$. Since $T$ is connected, this forces $G$ to be connected. Every compact, connected Lie group has a maximal torus.
