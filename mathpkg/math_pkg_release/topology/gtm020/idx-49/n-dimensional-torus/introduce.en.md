The $n$-dimensional torus $T^n = \mathbf{R}^n/\mathbf{Z}^n$ is the prototypical compact abelian Lie group. Every compact, connected, abelian Lie group is a torus. Tori play a fundamental role in the structure theory of compact Lie groups: every element lies in a maximal torus, and the classification of representations of a compact group is governed by its maximal torus and Weyl group.
