This corollary follows immediately from the semisimplicity theorem: since every $G$-module $M$ decomposes as a direct sum of simple submodules, its class $[M]$ in the representation ring is a non-negative integer combination of simple module classes. Taking formal differences, every element of $R_F(G)$ is a $\mathbf{Z}$-linear combination of classes of simple $G$-modules.
