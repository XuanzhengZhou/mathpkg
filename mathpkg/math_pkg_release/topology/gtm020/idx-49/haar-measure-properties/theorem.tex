Let $G$ be a compact topological group. There exists a unique linear functional $\mu: C(G) \to \mathbf{R}$ (the Haar measure) satisfying:

(1) If $f \geq 0$, then $\mu(f) \geq 0$, and if additionally $f(s) > 0$ for some $s \in G$, then $\mu(f) > 0$.

(2) $\mu(1) = 1$, where $1$ denotes the constant function.

(3) $\mu(L_a f) = \mu(f)$ for all $a \in G$, where $L_a f(s) = f(as)$ (left-invariance).

The Haar measure extends uniquely to a measure $\mu_V: C_V(G) \to V$ for each normed vector space $V$ via the requirement that $u \mu_V(f) = \mu(u f)$ for every $\mathbf{R}$-linear $u: V \to \mathbf{R}$.
