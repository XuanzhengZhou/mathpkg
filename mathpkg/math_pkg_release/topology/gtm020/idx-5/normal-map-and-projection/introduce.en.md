For each nonzero vector $b$ in Euclidean space $\mathbf{R}^n$, one can decompose any vector $x$ into a component orthogonal to $b$ and a component parallel to $b$. The normal map $v_b(x)$ extracts the component orthogonal to $b$, while the projection $\pi_b(x)$ gives the scalar coefficient of the projection onto the direction of $b$. These two linear functions are fundamental tools used in constructing explicit bundle isomorphisms, such as those relating tangent and normal bundles of spheres.
