For $k = 1, \ldots, 8$, let $C_k$ be the real Clifford algebra, $C_k^c = C_k \otimes_{\mathbf{R}} \mathbf{C}$ its complexification, and let $N_k, M_k, a_k$ (resp. $N_k^c, M_k^c, a_k^c$) be the ranks of the corresponding Grothendieck groups of irreducible modules (resp. complex modules) and the number $a_k$ such that $N_k \cong \mathbf{Z}^{a_k}$ or similar. Then:

\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c}
k & C_k & N_k & M_k & a_k & C_k^c & N_k^c & M_k^c & a_k^c \\
\hline
1 & \mathbf{C} & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 1 & \mathbf{C} \oplus \mathbf{C} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 1 \\
2 & \mathbf{H} & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 2 & \mathbf{C}(2) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & 1 \\
3 & \mathbf{H} \oplus \mathbf{H} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 4 & \mathbf{C}(2) \oplus \mathbf{C}(2) & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 2 \\
4 & \mathbf{H}(2) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & 4 & \mathbf{C}(4) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & 2 \\
5 & \mathbf{C}(4) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 8 & \mathbf{C}(4) \oplus \mathbf{C}(4) & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 4 \\
6 & \mathbf{R}(8) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 8 & \mathbf{C}(8) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & 4 \\
7 & \mathbf{R}(8) \oplus \mathbf{R}(8) & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 8 & \mathbf{C}(8) \oplus \mathbf{C}(8) & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} & 8 \\
8 & \mathbf{R}(16) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & 8 & \mathbf{C}(16) & \mathbf{Z} & \mathbf{Z} \oplus \mathbf{Z} & 8
\end{array}
\]

Moreover, these invariants satisfy the periodicity relations:
$$
N_{k+8} \cong N_k, \quad M_{k+8} \cong M_k, \quad a_{k+8} = 16a_k,
$$
$$
N_{k+2}^c \cong N_k^c, \quad M_{k+8}^c \cong M_k^c, \quad a_{k+2}^c = 2a_k^c.
$$
