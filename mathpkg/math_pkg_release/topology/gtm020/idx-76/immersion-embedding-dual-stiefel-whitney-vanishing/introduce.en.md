This theorem provides necessary conditions for a manifold $M^n$ to be immersed or embedded in Euclidean space in terms of its dual Stiefel-Whitney classes. For an immersion $M^n \to \mathbf{R}^{n+k}$, the normal bundle $\nu$ has dimension $k$ and satisfies $\tau(M) \oplus \nu$ is trivial, which forces the dual classes $\tilde{w}_i(M)$ to vanish for $i > k$. For embeddings, a stronger vanishing condition holds: $\tilde{w}_i(M) = 0$ for $i \geq k$, and if the normal bundle is orientable, its Euler class also vanishes. These results form the foundation for the classical obstruction theory of immersions and embeddings of real projective spaces.
