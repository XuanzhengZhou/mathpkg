The Leray-Hirsch Theorem is a fundamental result in the cohomology theory of fibre bundles. It provides sufficient conditions under which the cohomology of the total space $H^{*}(E, E_{0})$ is a free module over the cohomology of the base $H^{*}(B)$, with basis given by cohomology classes that restrict to a basis of the fibre cohomology on each fibre. The theorem is a key ingredient in the axiomatic construction of Chern classes and Stiefel-Whitney classes via the splitting principle, and it generalizes the K\"{u}nneth formula to nontrivial fibre bundles with cohomologically trivial fibres in the sense that the fibre cohomology is generated by globally defined classes.
