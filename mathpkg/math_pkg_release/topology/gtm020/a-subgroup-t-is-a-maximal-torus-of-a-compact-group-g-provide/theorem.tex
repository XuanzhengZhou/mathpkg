The condition $G = \bigcup_{s \in G} sTs^{-1}$ says that each conjugate class of $G$ intersects $T$. Since $T$ is connected, $G$ is also connected. By examples [see (8.4)], we see that a wide class of groups have maximal tori. Their importance lies in the fact that Corollary (7.7) applies to the inclusion $T \rightarrow G$, where $T$ is a maximal torus of $G$. In general, every compact, connected Lie group has a maximal torus.
