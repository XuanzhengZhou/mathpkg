Observe that $L^n(p)$ is the product of three matrices where $p_r^*(z) = \sum_{r \leq k \leq n} p_k z^{k-r}$ and $p_r^*(z) - zp_{r+1}^*(z) = p_r$.

$$\begin{bmatrix}
1 & p_1^*(z) & \cdots & p_n^*(z) \\
0 & 1 & \cdots & 0 \\
\cdots & \cdots & \cdots & \cdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 1
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
p(z) & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 1 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\
\cdots & \cdots & \cdots & \cdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\
- z & 1 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\
\cdots & \cdots & \cdots & \cdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & - z & 1
\end{bmatrix}$$
