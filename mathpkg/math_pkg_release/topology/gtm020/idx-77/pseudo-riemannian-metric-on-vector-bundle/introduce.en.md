A pseudo-riemannian metric on a smooth real vector bundle $E$ is a smoothly varying nondegenerate symmetric bilinear form on the fibres, defined globally as a bilinear map on the space of sections. The nondegeneracy condition is expressed via an induced isomorphism $\gamma: \Gamma(E) \to \Gamma(E^\vee)$ to the dual bundle. This generalizes the notion of a Riemannian metric (where the form is positive definite) and is the natural setting for defining metric-compatible connections, such as the Levi-Civita connection on the tangent bundle.
