The Chern-Simons form is a canonical differential form of degree $2q-1$ on the total space of the principal bundle associated to a flat vector bundle. It is constructed using the transgression obtained from the affine homotopy between the flat connection and the canonical trivial connection on the pullback bundle. The defining relation $\phi(K_0) = d(\text{CS}(E, \nabla))$ shows that the Chern-Simons form is a potential for the characteristic form of the flat connection. When the flat bundle is also trivial (i.e., the principal bundle admits a cross section), the Chern-Simons form can be pulled back to the base manifold, yielding the classical Chern-Simons invariants used in 3-manifold topology and quantum field theory.
