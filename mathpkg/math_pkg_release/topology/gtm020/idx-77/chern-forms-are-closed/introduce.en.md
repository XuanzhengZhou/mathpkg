This proposition establishes the fundamental fact that characteristic Chern forms $c_q(E, \nabla)$ are closed differential forms. The proof uses the Bianchi identity $dK = [\theta, K]$ and the invariance of the trace under cyclic permutations. Since the exterior derivative of $\text{Tr}(K^q)$ vanishes by the Bianchi identity, and every invariant polynomial is a polynomial in the traces $\text{Tr}(K^q)$ (over characteristic zero), it follows that $\phi(K)$ is closed for all invariant polynomials $\phi$. Consequently, each $c_q(E, \nabla)$ defines a de Rham cohomology class independent of the connection.
