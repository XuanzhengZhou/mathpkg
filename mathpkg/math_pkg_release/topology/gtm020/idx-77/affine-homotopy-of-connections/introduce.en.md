The affine homotopy $\nabla_t = (1-t)\nabla_0 + t\nabla_1$ provides the standard construction of a homotopy between any two connections on a vector bundle. Since the space of connections is an affine space, this linear interpolation is well-defined. The affine homotopy is used in the Chern-Weil theory to compute the transgression form relating the characteristic forms of $\nabla_0$ and $\nabla_1$, and in the construction of Chern-Simons forms.
