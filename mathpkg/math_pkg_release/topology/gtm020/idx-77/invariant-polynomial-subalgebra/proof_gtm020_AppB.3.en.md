This is a classical theorem of invariant theory. The proof follows from the fact that every matrix $X$ over an algebraically closed field can be conjugated to its Jordan normal form, and the diagonal entries determine the conjugacy class up to permutation. Since the elementary symmetric functions generate the ring of symmetric polynomials in $n$ variables, and every invariant polynomial restricts to a symmetric polynomial on the diagonal matrices (which form a Zariski-dense subset of all matrices), the result follows. The $c_q(x)$ defined via $\det(I + Xt)$ are invariant under conjugation and restrict to the elementary symmetric functions on diagonal matrices.
