Let $k$ be a field of characteristic zero. The subalgebra of polynomials in $n^2$ matrix elements invariant under conjugation by $\text{GL}_n(k)$ is a polynomial algebra generated by elements $c_1(x), \ldots, c_n(x)$:
$$
k[x_{1,1}, \ldots, x_{n,n}] \supset k[x_{1,1}, \ldots, x_{n,n}]^{\text{GL}_n(k)} = k[c_1(x), \ldots, c_n(x)]
$$
where the polynomials $c_q(x)$ are given by
$$
R_x(t) = \det(I + Xt) = \sum_{0 \leq q \leq n} c_q(x) t^q.
$$
In the diagonal case $x_{i,j} = \delta_{i,j}\lambda_i$, the $c_q(x)$ are the elementary symmetric functions:
$$
c_q(x_{1,1}, \ldots, x_{n,n}) = \sum_{i(1) < \ldots < i(q)} \lambda_{i(1)} \cdots \lambda_{i(q)}.
$$
