The Bianchi identity is a fundamental differential identity satisfied by the curvature form $K$ of a connection. It states that the exterior derivative $dK$ equals the commutator $[\theta, K] = \theta \wedge K - K \wedge \theta$. This identity plays a crucial role in proving that characteristic forms built from invariant polynomials are closed, which is the cornerstone of the Chern-Weil theory of characteristic classes.
