In Dold's theory, a fibration is a bundle satisfying the homotopy lifting property for all spaces. This definition is stronger than the one used earlier in Chapter 1 of the book, as it requires the lifting property with respect to arbitrary spaces, not just CW complexes or paracompact spaces. Dold proves that this property, like the section extension property, satisfies a local-to-global principle.
