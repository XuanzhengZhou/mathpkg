A weak fibration is a bundle that satisfies a relaxed version of the homotopy lifting property. The lifting $f_t$ of the homotopy $h_t$ is required to exist, but the initial lift $f_0$ need not equal the given map $f$; instead, $f_0$ and $f$ are only required to be homotopic via a homotopy that projects to the constant path. This notion is preserved under fibre homotopy equivalence and plays an important role in Dold's local-to-global theory.
