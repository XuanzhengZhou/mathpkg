A halo of a subset $A$ is a special type of open neighborhood. It comes equipped with a continuous function $f \colon X \to [0,1]$ that equals $1$ on $A$ and whose support is contained in the open set $V$. The concept of a halo is used in Dold's formulation of the section extension property, where a cross section defined on $A$ can be extended to a halo before being extended to the whole space.
