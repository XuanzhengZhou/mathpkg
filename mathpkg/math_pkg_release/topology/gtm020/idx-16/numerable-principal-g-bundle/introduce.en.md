A principal $G$-bundle $\xi$ over a space $B$ is called numerable if there exists a numerable open covering $\{U_i\}_{i \in S}$ of $B$ such that the restriction $\xi|U_i$ is trivial for each $i \in S$. This notion strengthens local triviality: every numerable principal $G$-bundle is locally trivial, and conversely, every locally trivial principal $G$-bundle over a paracompact space is numerable. The numerability condition is essential for the homotopy classification theory developed in the subsequent sections.
