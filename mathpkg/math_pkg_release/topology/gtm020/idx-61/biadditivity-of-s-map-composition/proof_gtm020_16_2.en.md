Using the coH-space structure of $S^k X$, the function

$$[S^k X, S^k Y] \times [S^k Y, S^k Z] \to [S^k X, S^k Z]$$

is biadditive for each $k$. Since the suspension $S^k X$ is a coH-space for $k \geq 2$, the set $[S^k X, S^k Y]$ carries the structure of an abelian group, and composition is additive in each variable. Specifically, for $f_1, f_2: S^k X \to S^k Y$ and $g: S^k Y \to S^k Z$, we have $(f_1 + f_2) \circ g = f_1 \circ g + f_2 \circ g$ by the coH-space distributivity, and similarly for additivity in the second argument using the suspension coordinates.

Passing to the direct limit as $k \to \infty$, the biadditivity is preserved. Thus the composition of $S$-maps $\{X, Y\} \times \{Y, Z\} \to \{X, Z\}$ is biadditive.
