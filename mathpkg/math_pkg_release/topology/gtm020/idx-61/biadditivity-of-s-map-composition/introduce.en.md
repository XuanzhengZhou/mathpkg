The composition of $S$-maps $\{X, Y\} \times \{Y, Z\} \to \{X, Z\}$ is biadditive, meaning it distributes over the abelian group structure on both sides. This follows from the coH-space structure of the iterated suspensions $S^k X$, which induces abelian group structures on the homotopy classes $[S^n(X), S^n(Y)]$ that are compatible with the direct limit defining $S$-maps. Biadditivity is an essential property for the development of the $S$-category as an additive category.
