If $u: X \wedge X' \to S^n$ is an $n$-duality between finite CW-complexes, then the group isomorphisms witnessed on spheres extend to all finite CW-complexes $Z$. The maps $u_Z: \{Z, X'\} \to \{X \wedge Z, S^n\}$ and $u_Z': \{Z, X\} \to \{Z \wedge X', S^n\}$ are isomorphisms. The proof proceeds by induction on the number of cells of $Z$, using the Puppe exact sequence for the cofibration attaching the last cell to handle the inductive step. This extension from spheres to arbitrary finite CW-complexes is a crucial step in the theory of $S$-duality.
