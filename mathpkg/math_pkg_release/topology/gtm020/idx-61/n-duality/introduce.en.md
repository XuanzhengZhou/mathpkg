An $n$-duality (or $S$-duality) between finite CW-complexes $X$ and $X'$ is an $n$-pairing $u: X \wedge X' \to S^n$ such that the induced morphisms $u_k: \{S^k, X'\} \to \{X \wedge S^k, S^n\}$ are isomorphisms for all integers $k$. This concept, independently studied by P. Freyd, generalizes Spanier-Whitehead duality by encoding duality in terms of stable maps into spheres. The pairing induces isomorphisms between the $S$-maps from $Z$ to $X'$ and the $S$-maps from $X \wedge Z$ to $S^n$, providing a powerful computational tool in stable homotopy theory.
