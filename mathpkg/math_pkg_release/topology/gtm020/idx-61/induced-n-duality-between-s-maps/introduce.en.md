Given two $n$-duality pairings $u: X \wedge X' \to S^n$ and $v: Y \wedge Y' \to S^n$ between finite CW-complexes, there is an induced group isomorphism $D_n(u, v): \{X, Y\} \to \{Y', X'\}$ defined as the composition $u_{Y'}^{-1} \circ v^X$. This isomorphism is functorial with respect to composition of $S$-maps, satisfying $D_n(u, w)(\{g\} \circ \{f\}) = D_n(u, v)(\{f\}) \circ D_n(v, w)(\{g\})$ and $D_n(u, u)\{1\} = \{1\}$. The induced duality provides a powerful computational tool that relates $S$-maps in one direction to $S$-maps in the opposite direction between dual spaces.
