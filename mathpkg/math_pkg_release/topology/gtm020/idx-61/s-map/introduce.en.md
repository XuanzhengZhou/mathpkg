An $S$-map from a topological space $X$ to $Y$ is an element of the direct limit $\{X, Y\}$ of the sequence of homotopy classes $[S^n(X), S^n(Y)]$ under the iterated suspension maps. This construction stabilizes the homotopy category by identifying maps that become homotopic after sufficiently many suspensions. The suspension functor $S$ induces an isomorphism on the set of $S$-maps, reflecting the fact that $S$-maps are inherently stable objects.
