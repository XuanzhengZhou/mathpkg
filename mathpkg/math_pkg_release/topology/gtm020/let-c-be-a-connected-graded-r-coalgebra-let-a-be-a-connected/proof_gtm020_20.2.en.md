Since $A = R \oplus I(A)$ as modules, we can iterate the above given isomorphism

$$\prod_{1 \leq k < m} I(C)^{k \otimes} \oplus (A \otimes I(C)^{m \otimes}) \rightarrow I(A)$$

whose restriction $f_{m}$: $\prod_{1 \leq k < m} I(C)^{k \otimes} \rightarrow I(A)$ is just the restriction of $f$ to this part of the tensor algebra. By the connectivity hypothesis $f_{m}$ is an isomorphism

in degrees $<m$ and hence $f: T(C) \rightarrow A$ is an isomorphism because $I(C)^{m \otimes}$ is zero in degrees $<m$. This proves the proposition.
