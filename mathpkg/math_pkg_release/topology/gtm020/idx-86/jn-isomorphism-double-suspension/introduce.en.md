This proposition establishes that the map $j_n \colon T_n \to \Omega F_n$ induces an isomorphism on the relative homotopy groups $\pi_{2np-2}(-, S^{2n-1})$ localized at $p$, via a commutative diagram connecting homology and relative homotopy through the Hurewicz homomorphism. The key observation is that since the boundary map $\partial$ from $\pi_{2np-2}(T_n, S^{2n-1})_{(p)}$ to $\pi_{2np-3}(S^{2n-1})_{(p)}$ is injective, the Hurewicz homomorphisms $w$ and $v$ are isomorphisms of cyclic groups of order $p$, forcing the entire diagram to consist of isomorphisms.
