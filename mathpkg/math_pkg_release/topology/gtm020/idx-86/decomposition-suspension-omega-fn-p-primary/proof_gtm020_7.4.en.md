The map $j_n \colon T_n \to \Omega F_n$ defines by loop multiplication a map $g_m \colon (T_n)^m \to \Omega F_n$, and this map is a surjection in homology mod $p$ in degrees $\leq m(2np-2)$. The homotopy class of $Sf$ is in $\pi_{4np-4}(S^{2n-1})$, and the suspension morphism from odd to even spheres is injective on the odd prime components
\[
\pi_{4np-4}(S^{2n-1})_{(p)} \to \pi_{4np-3}(S^{2n})_{(p)}.
\]
Hence the class of $S(f)$ is zero, and the space $S(T_n \wedge T_n)$ has the homotopy type of a wedge of four spheres. When the map $\bar{h}$ restricts to a mod $p$ homology isomorphism
\[
h \colon ST_n \vee X \to S\Omega F_n,
\]
where $X$ is a subwedge of spheres of $\bigvee_{2 \leq m} S(\bigwedge_m T_n)$. After localizing at $p$, the map $h$ is the desired homotopy equivalence.

The homology of $S\Omega F_n$ determines which dimensions the spheres appear in the wedge decomposition: namely of the form $(2np-2)i+1$ for $i \geq 2$ and $(2np-2)i+(2n-1)+1$ for $i \geq 1$.
