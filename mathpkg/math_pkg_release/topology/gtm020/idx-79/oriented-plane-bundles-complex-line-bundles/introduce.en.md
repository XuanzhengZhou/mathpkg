The isomorphism $SO(2) \cong U(1) \cong S^1$ implies that oriented real plane bundles admit a natural complex line bundle structure. This is because the structure group of an oriented real 2-plane bundle can be reduced from $GL(2,R)$ to $SO(2)$, and the identification $SO(2) = U(1)$ means that every such bundle inherits a complex structure. This fact is fundamental for relating real and complex characteristic classes, particularly for comparing Euler classes and Chern classes in low dimensions.
