Theorem 11.2 determines the reduced complex K-theory of even-dimensional spheres. The group $\tilde{K}(S^{2m})$ is infinite cyclic, generated by the Bott class $\beta_{2m}$, with the key relation $\beta_{2m}^2 = 0$. Furthermore, the Adams operation $\psi^k$ acts on the generator by multiplication by $k^m$: $\psi^k(\beta_{2m}) = k^m \beta_{2m}$. This result illustrates the power of Adams operations in K-theory computations and provides an explicit example of their action on a fundamental space. The computation follows from the formula $\psi^k(a_1) \cdots \psi^k(a_m) = k^m a_1 \cdots a_m$ and the fact that the exterior product map $\tilde{K}(S^{2m}) \rightarrow \tilde{K}(S^2 \times \cdots \times S^2)$ is injective in this context.
