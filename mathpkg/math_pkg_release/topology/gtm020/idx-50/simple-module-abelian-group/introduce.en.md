Let $G$ be an abelian group and $M$ a simple $G$-module over the complex numbers $\mathbb{C}$. Theorem 9.1 asserts that every such simple module is necessarily one-dimensional, and the action of each group element $s \in G$ on $M$ is given by multiplication by a scalar $\lambda_s \in \mathbb{C}^\times$. The map $s \mapsto \lambda_s$ defines a group homomorphism from $G$ into the multiplicative group of non-zero complex numbers. This result serves as the foundation for the classification of representations of abelian groups, leading directly to the description of the representation ring of a torus.
