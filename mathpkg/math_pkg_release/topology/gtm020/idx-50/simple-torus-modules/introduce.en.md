Theorem 9.3 classifies all simple modules of the $n$-dimensional torus $T^n = (S^1)^n$. Every simple $T^n$-module is one-dimensional and corresponds to a unique $n$-tuple of integers $(k_1, \ldots, k_n) \in \mathbb{Z}^n$. The representation $M(k_1, \ldots, k_n)$ acts on $\mathbb{C}$ via the character $(\theta_1, \ldots, \theta_n) \mapsto \exp(2\pi i \sum k_j \theta_j)$. As a consequence, the complex representation ring $R(T^n)$ is isomorphic to the Laurent polynomial ring $\mathbb{Z}[\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n, \alpha_1^{-1}, \ldots, \alpha_n^{-1}]$, where $\alpha_j$ corresponds to the one-dimensional representation with $k_j = 1$ and all other $k_i = 0$.
