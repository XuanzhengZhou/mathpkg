This follows from Proposition 10.1 together with the definition of $KO(X)$ as the Grothendieck group of real vector bundles over $X$. An oriented vector bundle is one with structure group $SO(n)$. By Proposition 10.1 and related splitting arguments, any real vector bundle can be related to oriented ones, yielding the representation of an arbitrary class as a difference of oriented bundle classes.
