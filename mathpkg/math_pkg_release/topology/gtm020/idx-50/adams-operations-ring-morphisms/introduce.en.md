Theorem 10.5 establishes the fundamental properties of the Adams operations $\psi^k$ on the K-theory groups $K(X)$ and $KO(X)$. It asserts that each $\psi^k$ is a ring homomorphism (i.e., additive and multiplicative) and that the operations compose multiplicatively: $\psi^k \circ \psi^l = \psi^{kl}$. The proof relies on reducing to the representation ring of a maximal torus via the splitting principle, where the corresponding statement holds by Theorem 9.5, and then using Corollary 10.2 to extend from oriented to all real vector bundles in $KO(X)$.
