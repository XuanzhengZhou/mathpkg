This proposition computes the multiplicity with which the trivial one-dimensional representation appears in the semispinor representations $\Delta_{2r}$ or $\Delta_{2r+1}$ of the spin group. The multiplicity depends only on $r \bmod 4$, taking values $2$, $-2$, or $0$. The negative multiplicity $-2$ indicates that when combined with the criterion from Corollary 11.7, the corresponding semispinor modules are quaternionic rather than real.
