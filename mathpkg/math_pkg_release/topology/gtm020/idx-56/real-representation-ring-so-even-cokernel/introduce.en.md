For the even special orthogonal group $SO(2r)$, the behavior of the complexification map $\varepsilon_U: RO(SO(2r)) \rightarrow RSO(2r)$ depends on the parity of $r$. The key is the splitting behavior of the middle exterior power $\Lambda^r \mathbf{R}^{2r}$: when $r$ is even it splits over both $\mathbf{R}$ and $\mathbf{C}$, yielding an isomorphism; when $r$ is odd it splits only over $\mathbf{C}$, resulting in a cokernel of order $2$.
