For $SO(2r)$, the module $\Lambda^r \mathbf{R}^{2r}$ splits over $\mathbf{R}$ and $\mathbf{C}$ if $r$ is even, and splits over $\mathbf{C}$ for $r$ odd. The morphism $\varepsilon_U$ is a monomorphism since $\varepsilon_0 \varepsilon_U = 2$ and $R(G)$ is a free abelian group. When $r$ is even, the real splitting of $\Lambda^r \mathbf{R}^{2r}$ ensures that $\varepsilon_U$ is surjective onto the generators of $RSO(2r)$, hence an isomorphism. When $r$ is odd, the failure of real splitting means $\varepsilon_U$ is not surjective, and the cokernel has order $2$.
