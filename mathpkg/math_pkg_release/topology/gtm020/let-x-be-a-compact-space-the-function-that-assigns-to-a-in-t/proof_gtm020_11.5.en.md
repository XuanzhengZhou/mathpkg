Moreover, where is the following commutative diagram:

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
U_F(n) \bmod U_F(n-k) & \xrightarrow{\theta_k^n} V_k(F^n) \\
U_F(n+1) \bmod U_F(n+1-k) & \xrightarrow{\theta_k^{n+1}} V_k(F^{n+1})
\end{array}$$

The vertical maps are inclusions or are induced by inclusion.

By taking unions we have for $n = +\infty$ a homeomorphism $\theta_k: U_F \bmod (1_k \times U_F) \rightarrow V_k(F^\infty)$, where $U_F \bmod (1_k \times U_F)$ has the inductive topology which, as the reader can easily verify, equals the quotient topology.
