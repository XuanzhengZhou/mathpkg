A \textbf{bundle} is a triple $(E, p, B)$, where $p: E \rightarrow B$ is a map. The space $B$ is called the \textbf{base space}, the space $E$ is called the \textbf{total space}, and the map $p$ is called the \textbf{projection} of the bundle. For each $b \in B$, the space $p^{-1}(b)$ is called the \textbf{fibre} of the bundle over $b \in B$.
