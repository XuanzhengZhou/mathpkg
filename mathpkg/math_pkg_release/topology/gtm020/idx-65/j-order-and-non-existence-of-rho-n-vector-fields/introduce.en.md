This theorem establishes that the vector field problem on spheres can be fully resolved by computing the order of the $J$-class of the canonical line bundle $\xi_k$ in $J(RP^k)$. The Radon-Hurwitz number $\rho(n)$ gives the maximal number of linearly independent vector fields that one might hope to construct on $S^{n-1}$. If one can prove that $J(\xi_k)$ has order exactly $c_k$, then the morphisms in the sequence $Z_{c_k} \to \tilde{KO}(RP^k) \to J(RP^k)$ become isomorphisms, and the nonexistence result follows by analyzing the coreducibility of Thom spaces.
