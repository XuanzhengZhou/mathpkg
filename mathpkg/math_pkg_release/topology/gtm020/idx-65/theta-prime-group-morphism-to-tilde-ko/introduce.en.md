This proposition constructs a canonical group homomorphism $\theta'_{k,l}$ from the representation ring $N_k$ of the Clifford algebra $C_k$ to the reduced real $KO$-theory of the relative real projective space $RP^l/RP^k$. For each $C_k$-module $M$, the image is the difference between $(\dim M)$ copies of the canonical line bundle $\xi_l$ and the trivialization $\tau_M$ of $m\xi_k$. The uniqueness of $\theta'_{k,l}$ follows from the additivity property $\tau_{M \oplus N} = \tau_M \oplus \tau_N$ and the resulting compatibility with direct sums.
