This proposition gives a geometric characterization of reducibility for $n$-dimensional CW-complexes with a single top-dimensional cell. A space $X$ is reducible precisely when there exists a map $f$ from the $n$-sphere into $X$ such that composing with the natural projection $v: X \to X/X^{n-1} = S^n$ yields a map homotopic to the identity on $S^n$. Equivalently, the top cell splits off as a wedge summand.
