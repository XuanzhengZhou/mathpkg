This theorem provides a partial converse to Proposition 6.1 by establishing the equivalence of three conditions for a vector bundle $\xi$ over a connected finite CW-complex $X$: the vanishing of its $J$-class, the $S$-coreducibility of its Thom space $T(\xi)$, and the equality of the $S$-type of $T(\xi)$ with that of $X^+$ (the base space with an added disjoint base point). In particular, $T(\xi)$ having the same stable homotopy type as $X^+$ characterizes when a vector bundle is $J$-trivial.
