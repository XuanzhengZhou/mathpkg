This proposition establishes the naturality of the $r$-duality isomorphism with respect to maps between dual pairs. Given two duality maps $u: X \wedge X' \to S^r$ and $v: Y \wedge Y' \to S^r$, and maps $f: Y \to X$, $g: X' \to Y'$ that are $r$-dual in the sense that $D_r(v,u)\{f\} = \{g\}$, the induced isomorphisms $\delta$ on stable homotopy sets commute with the induced maps $f_*$ and $g^*$. This commutative diagram expresses the functorial nature of $S$-duality.
