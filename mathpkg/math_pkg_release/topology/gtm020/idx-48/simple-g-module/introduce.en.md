A simple $G$-module is a $G$-module that contains no proper nontrivial $G$-submodules. This is the representation-theoretic analogue of an irreducible representation: the only subspaces invariant under the group action are the zero subspace and the module itself. Simple $G$-modules are the building blocks of representation theory, and Schur's Lemma describes the morphisms between them.
