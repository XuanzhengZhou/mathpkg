This proposition states that any $G$-morphism whose domain or codomain is a simple $G$-module must be either trivial or injective/surjective respectively. The result follows directly from the fact that the kernel and cokernel of a $G$-morphism are $G$-submodules, and by simplicity these can only be the zero submodule or the whole module. This is a fundamental structural property that underpins Schur's Lemma.
