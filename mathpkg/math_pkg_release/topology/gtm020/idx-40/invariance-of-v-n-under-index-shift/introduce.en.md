The element $v_n(\zeta, u)$ is defined in the K-theory ring $K(X)$ using the clutching construction with a vector bundle $\zeta$ and an endomorphism $u$. This proposition establishes that $v_n$ is independent of the index $n$, so one may simply write $v(\zeta, u)$. The invariance follows from the relation $L^{2n+2}(\zeta, p_{n+1})_+ = L^{2n}(\zeta, p_n)_+ \oplus \zeta$ together with algebraic manipulation of the terms involving the Bott element $\eta$. This result is a pivotal step in the proof of the complex Bott periodicity theorem.
