In Theorem (8.2), it is shown that isomorphism classes correspond to certain equivalence classes of maps $B \to G$. Observe that if $u(t)$ is a path in $G$ from the identity $1$ to an element $s$, then $u(t) \, c_\xi \, u(t)^{-1}$ is a homotopy from $c_\xi$ to $s c_\xi s^{-1}$. Since $G$ is path-connected, this shows that conjugation acts trivially on homotopy classes. This remark, together with Theorem (8.2), establishes the bijection.
