The universal $U_F(k)$-principal bundle over the Grassmannian $G_k(F^n)$ can be realized via the Stiefel variety $V_k(F^n)$. The associated vector bundle $\alpha_k^n[F^k]$ maps naturally to the tautological $k$-plane bundle $\gamma_{k,n}$ via a morphism $f$ that sends a $k$-frame $(v_1, \ldots, v_k)$ and coefficient vector $y$ to the subspace spanned by the frame and the linear combination $\sum y_j v_j$. The well-definedness follows from the fact that a change of basis in $U_F(k)$ transforms both the frame and coefficient vector compatibly.
