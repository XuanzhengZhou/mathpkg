By (10.1), the map $x \mapsto \alpha(x)\alpha(e_{n-1})y$ has degree $1 + (-1)^n$ and is independent of the choice of $\alpha(e_{n-1})y \in S^{n-1}$. This gives the first component of the bidegree.

For the second component, the map $y \mapsto \alpha(x)y$ for fixed $x$ is a reflection through the hyperplane orthogonal to $x$. A reflection is a linear map with determinant $-1$, hence as a map $S^{n-1} \to S^{n-1}$ it has degree $-1$. Therefore the bidegree is $(+1 + (-1)^n, -1)$.
