Let $\alpha: S^{n-1} \to O(n)$ be the reflection map defined by $\alpha(x)y = y - 2(y|x)x$, and let $\beta: S^{n-1} \to O(n)$ be the rotation-by-$180^\circ$ map defined by $\beta(x)y = 2(y|x)x - y$, where $(y|x)$ denotes the Euclidean inner product. Note that $\alpha(x)y = -\beta(x)y$. Then for any $x_1, x_2 \in S^{n-1}$,
$$R(x_1, x_2)^2 = \alpha(x_1)\alpha(x_2) = \beta(x_1)\beta(x_2).$$
