A bundle over a suspension $SB$ is called regular if it is trivial on a neighborhood of each pole (the two identification points). This condition ensures the bundle can be described by a single transition function, or characteristic map, defined on $B$. For path-connected structure groups, every locally trivial bundle over a suspension is regular, making this a useful framework for classification.
