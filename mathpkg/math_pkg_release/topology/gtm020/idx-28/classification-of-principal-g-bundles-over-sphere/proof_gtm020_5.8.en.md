Note that $S^n$ is homeomorphic to the suspension $S(S^{n-1})$. Applying Corollary (8.3) with $B = S^{n-1}$, the isomorphism classes of regular principal $G$-bundles over $S^n = S(S^{n-1})$ correspond bijectively to homotopy classes $[S^{n-1}, G] = \pi_{n-1}(G)$. Since $G$ is path-connected, every locally trivial bundle over $S^n$ is regular (the two hemispheres are contractible and the bundle is trivial over each by the homotopy invariance of pullbacks of bundles over paracompact spaces).
