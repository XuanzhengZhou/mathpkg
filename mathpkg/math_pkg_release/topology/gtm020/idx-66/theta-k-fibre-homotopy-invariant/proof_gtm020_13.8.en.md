The proof that $\theta_k(\xi)$ is a fibre homotopy invariant of $S(\xi)$ follows from the definition of $\theta_k(\xi)$ via the Adams operation $\psi^k$ on $KO(S(\xi))$. The Adams operations are defined at the level of $K$-theory, which is a homotopy invariant, and the construction of the generator $\Delta^+_{8m}$ uses only the fibre homotopy type of the sphere bundle. A fibre homotopy equivalence $S(\xi) \simeq S(\eta)$ induces an isomorphism in $KO$-theory that preserves the module structure from $KO(B(\xi))$, and hence preserves the class $\theta_k$.

The consequence for $J(2n\zeta) = 0$ follows from (13.7): if the sphere bundle is fibre homotopy trivial, then $\theta_k(2n\zeta) = \theta_k(\text{trivial bundle}) = k^n$. Comparing with the formula $\theta_k(2n\zeta) = k^n + \frac{k^n-1}{2}(\zeta-1)$ forces $\frac{k^n-1}{2}(\zeta-1) = 0$.
