Observe that $\text{Vect}_F$ is a cofunctor from the category of spaces and maps to the category of semirings (or semigroups). If $f: Y \rightarrow X$ is a map, then $\text{Vect}_F(f): \text{Vect}_F(X) \rightarrow \text{Vect}_F(Y)$ is defined by the relation $\text{Vect}_F(f)(\xi)$ equals the isomorphism class of $f^*(\xi)$ over $Y$. Here $\xi$ denotes both a vector bundle and its isomorphism class.

Similarly, the functions $K_F$ define a cofunctor. For a map $f: Y \rightarrow X$ there are the following morphisms:

$$\begin{array}{ccc}
\text{Vect}_F(X) & \xrightarrow{\theta} & K_F(X) \\
\text{Vect}_F(f) & \downarrow & K_F(f) \\
\text{Vect}_F(Y) & \xrightarrow{\theta} & K_F(Y)
\end{array}$$
