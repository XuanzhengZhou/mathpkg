This theorem provides a practical criterion for checking whether a $B$-morphism between two vector bundles is an isomorphism. Rather than constructing a global inverse, it suffices to verify that the restriction of $u$ to each fibre is an isomorphism of vector spaces. The forward direction is immediate from the definition of an isomorphism, while the converse requires constructing a global inverse whose continuity follows from the local triviality of the bundles.
