This corollary applies obstruction theory to the problem of reducing the structure group of a principal bundle. When the base space $B$ is a CW-complex and the homotopy groups $\pi_i(G/H)$ vanish for all dimensions $i$ below the dimension of $B$, every principal $G$-bundle admits a restriction to the subgroup $H$. The proof uses the cross-section criterion of Theorem 2.3 together with standard obstruction theory: the obstructions to constructing a cross section in the associated bundle $\xi[G/H]$ lie in cohomology groups $H^{i+1}(B; \pi_i(G/H))$, which all vanish under the stated hypothesis.
