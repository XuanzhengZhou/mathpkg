We need prove only that $g$ commutes with the action of $G$. Since
$$
g((y, s)H)t = ((f(y), s)G)t = (f(y), st)G = g((y, st)H),
$$
it follows that $g$ is a $G$-morphism.

The fact that $\eta[G]$ and $\xi$ are isomorphic as bundles over $B$ follows from Theorem 3.1, which establishes that the natural map from the associated bundle of a restriction to the original bundle is a homeomorphism. Combined with the $G$-equivariance shown above, this yields the $G$-space isomorphism.
