This proposition describes how transition functions behave under pullback. Given a fibre bundle $\eta$ over $B$ with atlas $\{(V_i, h_i)\}$ and transition functions $\{g_{i,j}\}$, and a continuous map $f: B_1 \to B$, the pullback bundle $f^*(\eta)$ has an atlas $\{(f^{-1}(V_i), f^*(h_i))\}$ whose transition functions are simply the pullbacks $\{g_{i,j} \circ f\}$. This naturality property makes pullback operations easy to compute in local coordinates.
