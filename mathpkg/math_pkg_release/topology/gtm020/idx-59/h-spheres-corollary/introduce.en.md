This corollary classifies which spheres admit an $H$-space structure, i.e. a continuous multiplication with a two-sided identity up to homotopy. The result follows from the Adams operations $\psi^k$ in $K$-theory and the Hopf invariant one problem. The proof shows that an $H$-space structure on $S^{n-1}$ yields a map $S^{2n-1} \to S^n$ of Hopf invariant $1$, and the Adams operations imply that such maps exist only for $n = 1, 2, 4, 8$, corresponding to spheres $S^1$, $S^3$, and $S^7$.
