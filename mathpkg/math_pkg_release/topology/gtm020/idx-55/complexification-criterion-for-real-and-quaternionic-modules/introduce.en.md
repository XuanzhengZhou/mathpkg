For a compact connected Lie group $G$, this theorem gives a criterion to determine whether a given complex $G$-module with invariant hermitian form arises from a real or quaternionic representation. The criterion is expressed in terms of the existence of additional $G$-invariant nondegenerate bilinear forms: a symmetric form characterizes complexifications of real modules, while a skew-symmetric form characterizes restrictions of quaternionic modules. The proof constructs an appropriate real or quaternionic structure on $M$ by decomposing the module into eigenspaces of a carefully normalized operator.
