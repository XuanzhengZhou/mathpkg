Each $C_n^c$-module determines a complex $\text{Spin}(n+1)$-module. Let $\Delta$ denote the $\text{Spin}(2r+1)$-module corresponding to the simple module over $C_{2r}^c = \mathbf{C}(2^r)$ of dimension $2^r$, and let $\Delta^+$, $\Delta^-$ denote the $\text{Spin}(2r)$-modules corresponding to the two simple modules over $C_{2r-1}^c = \mathbf{C}(2^{r-1}) \oplus \mathbf{C}(2^{r-1})$, each of dimension $2^{r-1}$. Specifically, $\Delta^\pm$ correspond to the module where $i^r e_1 \cdots e_{2r}$ acts as multiplication by $\pm 1$, respectively.
