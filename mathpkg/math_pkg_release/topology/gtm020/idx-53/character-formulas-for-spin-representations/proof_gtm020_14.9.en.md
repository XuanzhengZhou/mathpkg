Let $T'$, $\text{Spin}(n)$, and $\phi: \text{Spin}(n) \to SO(n)$ be the natural homomorphism. The maximal torus $T'(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ of $\text{Spin}(n)$ maps under $\phi$ to the maximal torus $T(e_1, \ldots, e_n)$ of $SO(n)$, giving the relation between the weight coordinates $\alpha_j$ for the spin group and the standard coordinates for the special orthogonal group. The formulas follow by expressing the characters of the simple Clifford modules in terms of the weight space decomposition with respect to this maximal torus, using the description of $\Delta$, $\Delta^+$, $\Delta^-$ from the simple modules over the complex Clifford algebras $C_{2r}^c$ and $C_{2r-1}^c$ (see 12(5.9) and 12(6.5)). The formulas for $\lambda_r^\pm$ in $RSO(2r)$ are then obtained by summing the appropriate exterior power characters restricted to the spin group.
