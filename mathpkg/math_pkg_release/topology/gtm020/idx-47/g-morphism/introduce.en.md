A $G$-morphism is an $F$-linear map between $G$-modules that commutes with the group action, i.e., it is $G$-equivariant. This notion defines the morphisms in the category of $G$-modules, making it analogous to the category of modules over a ring but with continuity constraints. The identity map is always a $G$-morphism, the composition of $G$-morphisms is again a $G$-morphism, and the kernel, image, and cokernel all inherit natural $G$-module structures.
