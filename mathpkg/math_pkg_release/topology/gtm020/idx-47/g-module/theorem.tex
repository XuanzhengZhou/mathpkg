Let $G$ be a topological group. A $G$-module $M$ is a $G$-space such that the action of $s \in G$ on $M$ is linear.

For $s \in G$ and a $G$-module $M$, let $s_M$ denote the linear automorphism of $M$ such that $s_M(x) = sx$. Then the relations $1_M = 1$, $(st)_M = s_M t_M$, and $(s^{-1})_M = (s_M)^{-1}$ hold. The function $(s, x) \mapsto sx$ of $G \times M \rightarrow M$ is continuous.
