This proposition establishes that the Laurent polynomial $x^m + x^{-m}$ can be expressed as an integer-coefficient polynomial in $x + x^{-1}$. The polynomials $f_m$ are closely related to Chebyshev polynomials and satisfy a simple three-term recurrence. This result is used in the computation of the representation ring of the symplectic group $Sp(n)$, where characters involve expressions of the form $\alpha_i^m + \alpha_i^{-m}$.
