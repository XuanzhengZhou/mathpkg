The weight of a monomial in $R[y_1, \ldots, y_m]$ assigns to each variable $y_i$ a degree $i$, so that the monomial $y_1^{a(1)} \cdots y_m^{a(m)}$ has weight $\sum i \cdot a(i)$. This grading is designed to interact with elementary symmetric functions: substituting $\sigma_i$ for $y_i$ transforms weight into total degree in the variables $x_1, \ldots, x_n$. The weight concept is used to keep track of degrees when expressing symmetric polynomials in terms of elementary symmetric functions.
