This proposition establishes that the orthogonal projection of a vector onto a $k$-dimensional subspace, computed via any orthonormal frame spanning that subspace, is well-defined. Given two orthonormal $k$-frames that differ by the action of the unitary group $U_F(k)$ (i.e., two frames for the same subspace), the sum of projections onto the individual frame vectors yields the same result. This is a key ingredient in constructing well-defined maps from the Grassmannian.
