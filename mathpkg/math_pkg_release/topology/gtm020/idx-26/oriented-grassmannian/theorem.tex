Let $F = \mathbf{R}$ or $\mathbf{C}$. The space $SG_k(F^n)$ of oriented $k$-subspaces is the set of $k$-dimensional subspaces of $F^n$ together with an equivalence class of orthonormal bases that differ by the action of $SU_F(k)$. There is a homeomorphism

$$\psi_k^n: U_F(n)/(SU_F(k) \times U_F(n-k)) \to SG_k(F^n)$$

A map $SG_k(F^n) \to G_k(F^n)$ is given by forgetting the orientation. For $F=\mathbf{R}$, over each point of $G_k(\mathbf{R}^n)$ are precisely two points representing the two orientations of a subspace. For $F=\mathbf{C}$, over each point of $G_k(\mathbf{C}^n)$ is a circle.
