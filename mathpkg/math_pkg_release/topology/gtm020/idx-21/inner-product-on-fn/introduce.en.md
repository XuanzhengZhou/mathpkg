The standard inner product on $F^n$ (where $F = \mathbf{R}, \mathbf{C}, \mathbf{H}$) is defined by $(x|y) = \sum x_i \bar{y}_i$ with the associated norm $\|x\| = (x|x)^{1/2}$. This inner product is the foundation for defining the classical groups $O(k)$, $U(k)$, and $Sp(k)$ as the subgroups of $\mathbf{GL}(k, F)$ that preserve it. The quaternion case requires the conjugate $\bar{x} = x_0 - ix_1 - jx_2 - kx_3$ for $x = x_0 + ix_1 + jx_2 + kx_3$.
