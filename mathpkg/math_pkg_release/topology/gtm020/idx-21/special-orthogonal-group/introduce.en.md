The special orthogonal group $SO(k)$ is the subgroup of $O(k)$ consisting of orthogonal matrices with determinant $+1$. Geometrically, $SO(k)$ corresponds to rotations in $\mathbf{R}^k$ (as opposed to reflections, which have determinant $-1$). It is the connected component of the identity in $O(k)$ and is a compact connected Lie group.
