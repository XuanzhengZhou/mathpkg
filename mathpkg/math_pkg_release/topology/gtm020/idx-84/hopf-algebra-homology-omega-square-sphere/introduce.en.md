This theorem, due to J. C. Moore, describes the full Hopf algebra structure of the mod-$p$ homology of the double loop space $\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}$ of an odd-dimensional sphere. The result decomposes the homology as a tensor product of exterior algebras on generators of degrees $2np^i - 1$ and polynomial algebras on generators of degrees $2np^j - 2$, where $p > 2$ is the characteristic of the coefficient field $k$. This calculation is fundamental for understanding the connectivity properties of the localized pair $(\Omega^2 S^{2n+1}, S^{2n-1})$ and for deriving refinements of the double suspension theorem.
