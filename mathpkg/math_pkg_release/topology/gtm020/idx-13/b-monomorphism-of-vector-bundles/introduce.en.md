A $B$-monomorphism is a morphism $u: \xi \to \eta$ of vector bundles over the same base space $B$ whose kernel is the zero bundle. Equivalently, $u$ is injective when restricted to each fibre. This generalizes the notion of an injective linear map from linear algebra to the category of vector bundles, where the morphisms are required to respect the base space.
