Let $V$ be a vector space over $F$, where $F$ denotes either $\mathbf{R}$ or $\mathbf{C}$. An \text{inner product} on $V$ is a function $\beta: V \times V \rightarrow F$ such that the following axioms hold:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $\beta(ax + a'x', y) = a\beta(x, y) + a'\beta(x', y)$
\item $\beta(x, by + b'y') = \bar{b}\beta(x, y) + \bar{b}'\beta(x, y')$
\end{enumerate}

where $\bar{x} = x$ for $x \in \mathbf{R}$ and $\overline{z} = x - iy$ for $z = x + iy \in \mathbf{C}$.
