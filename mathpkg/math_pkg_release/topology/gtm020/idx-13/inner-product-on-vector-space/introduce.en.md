An inner product on a vector space $V$ over the field $F$ (where $F$ is either the real numbers $\mathbf{R}$ or the complex numbers $\mathbf{C}$) is a sesquilinear function $\beta: V \times V \to F$. It is linear in the first argument and conjugate-linear in the second argument, meaning $\beta(x, by + b'y') = \bar{b}\beta(x,y) + \bar{b}'\beta(x,y')$. When $F = \mathbf{R}$, the conjugation is trivial and $\beta$ is an ordinary bilinear form. This notion is the algebraic prerequisite for defining Riemannian and Hermitian metrics on vector bundles.
