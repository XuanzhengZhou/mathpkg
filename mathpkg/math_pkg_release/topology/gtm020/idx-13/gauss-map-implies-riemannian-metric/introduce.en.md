This theorem establishes that the existence of a Gauss map for a vector bundle $\xi$ guarantees the existence of a metric on $\xi$. Given a Gauss map $g: E(\xi) \to F^\infty$, one defines $\beta(x, x') = (g(x) \mid g(x'))$ using the standard Euclidean inner product on $F^\infty$. Since $g$ is a continuous fibre-wise monomorphism, $\beta$ restricts to an inner product on each fibre, yielding a Riemannian (real case) or Hermitian (complex case) metric. Combined with the fact that every bundle over a paracompact space admits a Gauss map, this implies that all bundles over paracompact spaces carry a metric.
