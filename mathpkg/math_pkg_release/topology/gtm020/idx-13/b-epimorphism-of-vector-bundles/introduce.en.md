A $B$-epimorphism is a morphism $u: \xi \to \eta$ of vector bundles over the same base space $B$ whose cokernel is the zero bundle. Equivalently, $u$ is surjective when restricted to each fibre. This is the vector-bundle-theoretic analogue of a surjective linear map, adapted to the category where morphisms must be compatible with the projection maps onto the common base $B$.
