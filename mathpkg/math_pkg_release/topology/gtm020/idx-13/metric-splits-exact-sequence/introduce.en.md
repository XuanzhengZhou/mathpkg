This theorem shows that the presence of a Riemannian or Hermitian metric on a vector bundle $\eta$ forces every short exact sequence $0 \to \xi \to \eta \to \zeta \to 0$ to split. Using the metric $\beta$, one defines the orthogonal complement of the image of $u$ in each fibre of $\eta$, which provides a canonical isomorphism between $\zeta$ and a subbundle of $\eta$ complementary to $\operatorname{im} u$. The splitting morphism $w: \xi \oplus \zeta \to \eta$ is then constructed from $u$ and this orthogonal projection, yielding a direct sum decomposition $\eta \cong \xi \oplus \zeta$. This is one of the most important applications of metrics on vector bundles.
