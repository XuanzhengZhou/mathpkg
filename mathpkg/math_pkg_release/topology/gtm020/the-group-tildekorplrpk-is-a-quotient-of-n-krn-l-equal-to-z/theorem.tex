11.7 Remark. In the case of $k = 0$, $\tilde{KO}(RP^l)$ is a quotient of $Z_{c_k}$. Consequently, it is a cyclic group generated by the class of the canonical line bundle $\xi_l$. This class has an order that is a divisor of $c_l$. In summary, there is a sequence of two epimorphisms

$$Z_{c_k} = \frac{N_0}{rN_k} \xrightarrow{\theta_{0,k}} \tilde{KO}(RP^k) \longrightarrow J(RP^k)$$

If we can prove that $J(\xi_k)$ has order $c_k$, the above morphisms will be isomorphisms, and we shall have determined the structure of $\tilde{KO}(RP^k) = J(RP^k)$.

The order of $J(RP^k)$ is related to the vector field problem.
