For any nonzero vector $b \in \mathbf{R}^n$, the normal map $v_b$ and projection $\pi_b$ decompose each vector $x$ into a component orthogonal to $b$ and a scalar multiple of $b$. The normal map $v_b(x) = x - \pi_b(x)b$ gives the component perpendicular to $b$, satisfying $(b|v_b(x)) = 0$, while $\pi_b(x) = (b|x)/(b|b)$ extracts the coordinate along $b$.
