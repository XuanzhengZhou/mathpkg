Thom's theorem provides an explicit cohomological construction of Stiefel-Whitney classes. Working with $Z_2$ coefficients, the $i$th Stiefel-Whitney class $w_i(\xi)$ is obtained by applying the $i$th Steenrod square to the Thom class $U_\xi$ and then pulling back via the Thom isomorphism $\phi$. This formula connects the axiomatic characterization of Stiefel-Whitney classes to the cohomology operations of Steenrod.
