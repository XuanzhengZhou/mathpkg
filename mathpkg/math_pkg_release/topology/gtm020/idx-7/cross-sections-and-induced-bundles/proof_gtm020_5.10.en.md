*Proof.* (1) We have $p_1 \sigma(b_1) = p_1(b_1, sf(b_1)) = b_1$ and $f(b_1) = psf(b_1)$; consequently, $\sigma$ is a cross section of $f^*(\xi)$. The relation $f_\xi \sigma(b_1) = f_\xi(b_1, sf(b_1)) = sf(b_1)$ also follows.

(2) For the second statement, we have a factorization of $f_\xi \sigma$ by $f$, giving a map $s: B \to E$ with $sf = f_\xi \sigma$. Moreover, $psf = pf_\xi \sigma = fp_1 \sigma = f$ and $ps = 1_B$ since $f$ is surjective. Then $s$ is the desired cross section.
