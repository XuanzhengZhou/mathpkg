The induced bundle (also called the pullback bundle) is a fundamental construction that allows one to pull back a bundle $\xi$ over $B$ along a continuous map $f: B_1 \to B$ to obtain a new bundle $f^*(\xi)$ over $B_1$. The total space is defined as the fibre product $B_1 \times_B E$, consisting of pairs $(b_1, x)$ such that $f(b_1) = p(x)$. This construction is functorial and satisfies natural transitivity properties with respect to composition of base maps.
