The induced bundle construction $f^*: \mathrm{Bun}_B \to \mathrm{Bun}_{B_1}$ is not just an operation on bundles but a full functor. For any $B$-morphism $u: \xi \to \eta$, there is an induced $B_1$-morphism $f^*(u): f^*(\xi) \to f^*(\eta)$ defined by $(b_1, x) \mapsto (b_1, u(x))$. This functoriality ensures that the pullback operation respects composition and identities, making it a cornerstone of bundle theory.
