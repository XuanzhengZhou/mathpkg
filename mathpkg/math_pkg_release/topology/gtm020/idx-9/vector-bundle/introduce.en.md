A vector bundle is a fibre bundle where each fibre carries the structure of a finite-dimensional vector space over a field $F$ (the real numbers, complex numbers, or quaternions). The key requirement is local triviality: around every point in the base space $B$, the bundle looks like a product $U \times F^k$, and the transition to the fibre over each point is a vector space isomorphism. Vector bundles generalise the tangent bundle of a smooth manifold and provide the natural setting for studying characteristic classes and $K$-theory.
