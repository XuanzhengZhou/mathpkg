Let $T'$, $\text{Spin}(n)$, and $T \subset SO(n)$ be maximal tori such that the natural homomorphism $\phi \colon \text{Spin}(n) \to SO(n)$ maps $T'$ onto $T$. The representation ring $R\,\text{Spin}(n)$ embeds into the ring of characters on $T'$ invariant under the Weyl group. The formulas are obtained via the Weyl character formula applied to the spin representations. For $\text{Spin}(2r+1)$, the basic spin representation $\Delta$ has highest weight $(\tfrac{1}{2}, \ldots, \tfrac{1}{2})$ relative to $T'$, and its character is given by the product formula $\prod_j (\alpha_j^{1/2} + \alpha_j^{-1/2})$, which expands to the stated sum over all sign choices. For $\text{Spin}(2r)$, the two half-spin representations $\Delta^+$ and $\Delta^-$ have highest weights $(\tfrac{1}{2}, \ldots, \tfrac{1}{2}, \pm\tfrac{1}{2})$ and their characters are the sums over sign choices with product $\pm 1$ respectively. For $SO(2r)$, the representations $\lambda_r^\pm$ correspond to the two irreducible summands of the $r$-th exterior power of the standard representation, and their characters are obtained by restricting the $\text{Spin}(2r)$ characters to the torus $T$ of $SO(2r)$, yielding the sums over ordered $r$-tuples with alternating signs weighted by the sign product.
